Due to the sensitivities of the current flood events, when talking to
customers, do not use the term rejection. Use the term ineligible.
For example, “After assessing your claim we have found it does not meet
the conditions of the payment and therefore your claim outcome is
ineligible.”

DRA and NZ DRA average weekly income cut-off points

Based on the Rate Calculator Determination 2020.

Average weekly income is the Average Weekly Ordinary Time Earnings
(AWOTE) for a full-time adult in Australia according to the Australian
Bureau of Statistics.

AWOTE (average weekly income)

Table 2: customers are initially eligible for DRA or NZ DRA if their
disaster affected income is less than the fortnightly rate of the AWOTE
(average weekly income).

+-----------------------------------+-----------------------------------+
| Period                            | Current AWOTE                     |

+-----------------------------------+-----------------------------------+

+-----------------------------------+-----------------------------------+

Maximum rates

For current rate information, see:

JobSeeker Payment (JSP) - rates
These rates do not apply to DRA or NZ DRA:

-   single, 60 or over, after 9 months
-   single, principal carer of child, exempt from activity test

Rates - Youth Allowance (YA) (incl. special Rate and YDS)
These rates do not apply to DRA or NZ DRA:

-   single, principal carer of child, exempt from activity test
-   special rate of Youth Allowance

Add-on payments including the Energy Supplement are not payable for DRA
or NZ DRA.
