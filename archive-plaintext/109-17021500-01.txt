For staff in the Tasmanian Transport Scheme (TTS) only.

This document outlines the review of decision process for the TFES.

TFES review of decision

There are 2 levels of review:

-   An initial review of decision conducted by the Services Australia –
    TFES Service Delivery team
-   A claimant can request a second review if they are not satisfied
    with the outcome of the initial review, This is conducted by the
    Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development,
    Communications and the Arts

A claimant can ask for a review of:

-   claimant eligibility
-   goods eligibility
-   a claim for assistance

Review requirements and timeframes

A review of decision must be:

-   requested by the claimant or their authorised claims agent, within
    30 days of receiving notification of the original decision
-   completed by a reassessment officer within 30 days of receipt of the
    request

The reassessment officer:

-   cannot be a person involved in making the original decision. This
    includes the Quality Assurance Officer who quality checked the
    original decision
-   makes a decision in line with the TFES Ministerial Directions
-   provides the outcome of the review and reasons for the decision to
    the claimant in writing
