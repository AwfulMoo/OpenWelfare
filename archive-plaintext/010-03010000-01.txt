AIC Scheme allowances

Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme allowances are available
for primary, secondary and certain tertiary students who are unable to
attend an appropriate state school because of geographic isolation or
special health-related or educational needs.

The applicable AIC Scheme allowance for a student will normally reflect
the student's living arrangements while they are studying. If the
student:

-   boards away from home - Boarding Allowance
-   lives in a second home - Second Home Allowance
-   lives at home while studying by distance education - Distance
    Education Allowance (DED)
-   needs to board for short periods during the year (e.g. to undertake
    diagnostic testing and/or receive support for a learning disability,
    or when adverse weather conditions have caused impassable roads or
    cut off daily access from their home to school) - short-term
    Boarding Allowance
-   receives Disability Support Pension (DSP) or Parenting Payment
    Single (PPS) - Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
