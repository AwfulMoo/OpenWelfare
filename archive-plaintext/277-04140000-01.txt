This document outlines information about the collection of child support
via deduction from the payer's salary or wage. This is known as employer
withholding/linking.

On this Page:

About employer withholding (linking)

Employer withholding (EW) deductions from a payer's salary or wage are a
convenient payment method for payers who are:

-   salary or wage earners
-   receiving Parental Leave Payments (PLP) from an employer
-   contractors

Payers can make an election for EW not to apply.

To collect both ongoing liability and arrears for non-compliant payers,
use employer withholding arrears (EWA).

Roles and responsibilities

Service Officers:

-   identifying cases suitable for EW/EWA
-   contacting the payer
-   identify appropriate employer
-   processing Elections for EW not to apply
-   referring appropriate cases to Employer Services (EW)
-   request the ending of EW/EWA

Employer Services Officers (ESO):

-   checking the referral to ensure suitability for EW/EWA
-   providing advice to Service Officers where the link is not
    appropriate
-   establishing and maintaining EW link at the request of a Service
    Officer
-   amending and ending EW with the employer
