-   A principal home with no assessable curtilage refers to customers
    who are assessed under the single title rule - PLU, where extended
    land use test applies - EXT, or where curtilage is assessed under
    the grandfathered savings provisions for real estate - GRF)
-   A principal home with assessable curtilage refers to customers with
    land on one title greater than 2 hectares and do not meet the
    extended land use test or grandfathered savings provisions, or where
    the customer uses a portion of their principal home for business
    purposes)
-   For PLS-only applicants, assessable curtilage and assessable real
    estate refers to real estate that would ordinarily be an assessable
    asset if the customer received an income support payment (ISP).
-   The additional coding refers to the coding required for principal
    homes offered as security for the Scheme loan. See the Real estate
    valuation for the Scheme applications table for coding instructions.
