This document lists the types of financial investments used in income
and assets tests.

Financial investment definition

For income and asset test purposes, a financial investment includes:

-   cash
-   deposit money
-   a managed investment. This includes margin loans
-   a listed security
-   a loan not repaid in full
-   an unlisted public security
-   gold, silver or platinum bullion
-   an asset-tested income stream (short term)
-   some account-based income streams, depending on the purchase date

The Financial Industry Network Support (FINS) Bulletin has up-to-date
information about financial products. The information provided can be
different for each product. Examples of what could be provided are:

-   coding advice
-   how to assess the asset value
-   information about a change of name of the investment

The Resources page contains links to FINS and other investment
information.
