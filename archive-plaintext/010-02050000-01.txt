This document outlines the payments available for eligible ABSTUDY
students and Australian Apprentices.

Government intent - purpose of the payment

ABSTUDY is the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Study Assistance
Scheme. It is a Commonwealth payment made on behalf of the Department of
Social Services.

Payments are made to assist with increasing access and participation in
education and training for Indigenous Australians.

ABSTUDY payments for students and Australian Apprentices

The following payments are available under ABSTUDY for eligible students
and Australian Apprentices:

-   Additional Assistance - available when experiencing severe financial
    difficulties
-   Additional Incidentals Allowance - assistance for essential
    tertiary, Masters and Doctorate course costs
-   Away from Base assistance - covers the costs of travel, meals and
    accommodation to participate in compulsory activities away from the
    home or study location
-   Fares Allowance - covers the cost of travel to live away from home
    to attend study or to participate in an Away from Base activity
-   Incidentals Allowance - assistance for study expenses
-   Lawful Custody Allowance - assistance for study and course costs
    while in lawful custody
-   Living Allowance - assist in meeting the day-to-day living costs
-   Relocation Allowance - assistance with the cost of moving to another
    location to commence Masters and Doctorate study
-   Relocation Scholarship - annual payment to assist with the cost of
    establishing and maintaining accommodation away from home to attend
    a higher education course
-   Residential Costs Option - an option to help pay residential costs
    while living away from home to study
-   School Fees Allowance Group 1 - an annual payment to assist with
    school fees when living at home
-   School Fees Allowance Group 2 - an annual payment to assist with
    school fees when living away from home
-   School Term Allowance - assistance with school expenses
-   Student contributions or Tuition fees for Masters or Doctorate
    study - assistance to pay student contributions or tuition fees
-   Thesis Allowance - help with costs of presenting a thesis or similar
    course requirement

Add-on payments

-   Energy Supplement - help with energy costs
-   Pension Supplement - help with bills and medicines when of Age
    Pension age
-   Pensioner Education Supplement - help with the ongoing costs
    associated with study
-   Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA) - help with medicine costs
-   Remote Area Allowance (RAA) - payment for customers who live in a
    remote area
-   Rent Assistance (RA) - a regular extra payment for customers pay
    rent
-   Youth Disability Supplement (YDS) - an extra payment for a young
    person with a disability

Rates of payment

Student or Australian Apprentice

ABSTUDY payment rates depend on factors including whether a student or
Australian Apprentice is:

-   dependent or independent
-   living at or away from home
-   in state care
-   in lawful custody
-   a pensioner student
-   subject to the personal, partner or parental income and assets tests

Students

Payment rates for students are impacted by the:

-   type of course studied
-   student status, either full-time or part-time

See Rates and Thresholds for current and historical rates of payment.
