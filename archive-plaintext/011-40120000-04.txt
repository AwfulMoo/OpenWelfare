Policy

All Service Officers have an obligation under the Privacy Act 1988, as
well as the secrecy provisions of both the Health Insurance Act 1973 and
the National Health Act 1953 to protect information held by the Services
Australia.

When assisting members of the public over the telephone, act with care
and diligence when discussing personal information.

Service Officers must confirm that the person to whom they are speaking
is authorised to discuss the matter before proceeding

Service Officers must familiarise themselves with privacy policies and
procedures.
