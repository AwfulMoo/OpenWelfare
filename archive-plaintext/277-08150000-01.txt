This document outlines how to deal with stale cheques issued from
Australian Child Support.

Stale and unpresented cheques

A cheque is 'stale' if it has exceeded the timeframe within which the
bank in its country of issue will still honour it. That is, the time
between the date of issue and the date presented for payment.

Timeframes vary between countries.
Some of the more common countries and their timeframes are:

-   CAD: 6 Months
-   CHF: 20 Days
-   EUR: 20 Days (Austria, Belgium, France, Germany, Italy and Spain)
-   EUR: 6 Months (Greece, Ireland and Portugal)
-   GBP: 6 Months
-   JPY: 8 Months
-   NZD: 6 Months
-   PHP: 6 Months
-   TRY: 3 Months
-   USD: 6 Months
-   ZAR: 6 Months

The International Disbursement Officer actions unpresented cheques
daily.

Only authorised officers perform this process.
For more information, see References for delegations and authorisations.
