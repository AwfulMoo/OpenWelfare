This document outlines the eligibility criteria and evidence
requirements for each event under the scheme and how to register a
claimant.

About the scheme

The Disaster Health Care Assistance Scheme (DHCAS) provides financial
assistance to affected individuals for out-of-pocket health care costs
arising from specified international adverse events such as natural
disasters and acts of terrorism.

There are currently 5 active DHCAS adverse events:

-   Balimed – bombings in Kuta, Bali on 12 October 2002
-   Tsunami – Indian Ocean Tsunami on 26 December 2004
-   London – bombings in London on 7 July 2005
-   Bali – bombings in Bali on 1 October 2005
-   Dahab, Egypt – bombings in Dahab, Egypt on 24 April 2006
-   Care package

Before claiming, an applicant must register with DHCAS. The eligibility
criteria and document requirements for each of these adverse events are
in the DHCAS category and services table.

Assessments of eligibility for registration of applicant is conducted
within Medicare Provider Services and the DHCAS Programme Management
team.

Special circumstances for eligibility

If a person does not meet the eligibility criteria for any of the
events, but it would be unreasonable to exclude the person from
assistance, one of the following officers may determine that the person
shall be covered by the Scheme, subject to any reasonable conditions or
requirements specified by that officer:

-   Assistant Secretary, MBS Policy and Specialist Services Branch,
    Department of Health and Aged Care, or
-   Director, Patient Eligibility and Special Access Programs Section,
    Department of Health and Aged Care

Such a determination may specify the scope and duration of eligibility
and/or assistance.

How applicants can register

Applicants must:

-   complete the relevant DHCAS adverse event registration form
-   provide evidence as per the eligibility document evidence
    requirements for that adverse event – this can be certified copies
    of the documents
-   send the completed form and supporting documentation to Services
    Australia. For the postal address, see Disaster Health Care
    Assistance Scheme.

The Resources page contains links to the Services Australia website, the
DHCAS category and services table, registration and claim forms, and
contact details.
