This document outlines suppliers approved to supply pharmaceutical
benefits under the National Health Act 1953.

Approved suppliers

Under the National Health Act 1953, an approved supplier of
pharmaceutical benefits can be one of the following:

-   Section 90 (s90) approved pharmacist
-   Section 91 (s91) deceased approved pharmacist
-   Section 92 (s92) approved medical practitioner
-   Section 94 (s94) approved hospital authority

See the References page for a link to the National Health Act 1953.

Approved supplier responsibilities

Suppliers can only supply pharmaceutical benefit items from a properly
written prescription by a prescriber, see Pharmaceutical Benefits
Scheme - Health Professionals, or in limited circumstances, the
communication of a prescription (which must be forwarded to the supplier
later).

Suppliers are also responsible for making entries on Prescription Record
Forms (PRF) presented by patients for the purpose of reaching the PBS
Safety Net. See Eligibility for Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS).
