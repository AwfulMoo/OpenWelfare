This document outlines the process for reviewing the content of a Child
Support objection to make sure it meets the criteria for a valid
objection. The procedure is for Child Support Objections Officers at all
levels (APS4 – APS6).

What a customer can object to

A customer can object to decisions made by the Child Support officers.
They cannot object to the legislation.

The References page contains links to The Child Support Guide to provide
more information.

Types of objections

There are three types of objection:

-   General objections – Objections to general child support decisions,
    excluding objections to care decisions. They must be made in
    writing. The customer can lodge a written objection in person, by
    mail, e-mail, fax or via CSAonline using
-   Care objection - Objections to care decisions may be lodged by phone
    or in writing. For care objections lodged by phone, see Care
    objections
-   Part 6A objections – Objections to Change of Assessment
