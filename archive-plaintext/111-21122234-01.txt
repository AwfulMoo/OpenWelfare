This document describes the role and responsibilities of Services
Officers and Technical Peer Support Officers (TPSOs) when seeking or
providing technical support for boutique work in Smart Centres.

Boutique work

Technical Peer Support (TPS) for boutique work in Smart Centres is for
specialised work types without a telephony TPS channel.

Staff trained in boutique work seek support using a national SharePoint.
Technical Peer Support Officers (TPSOs) specialised in a work type,
manage the enquiries. These include but are not limited to the
following:

-   ABSTUDY
-   Apprentices
-   Automated Income Stream Reviews (AISR)
-   Assistance for Isolated Children
-   Child Support Unit
-   Complex Assessment
-   Essential Medical Equipment Payment (EMEP)
-   Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
-   Farm Household Allowance Voucher Approval
-   Manual Intervention (MIV)
-   Member of a Couple Assessment (MoCA)
-   Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT)
-   Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
-   Tertiary Access Payment (TAP)
-   Unreasonable to Live At Home (UTLAH) over 18

Note: Staff providing support for Boosting and Completing Apprenticeshp
Commencements must use the task cards on the Smart Centres Operations
Division home page.

Technical Peer Support Officers (TPSOs)

TPSOs in Smart Centres must:

-   give technical help and guidance to Service Officers using the
    national SharePoint
-   Quality On Line (QOL) check Smart Centre work as directed
-   escalate issues unable to be resolved to a Service Support Officer
    (SSO)
-   give feedback through the Technical Support Centre (TSC)

The Resources page contains information on technical escalations,
intranet links, TPSO Accountability Statement and mySupport.
