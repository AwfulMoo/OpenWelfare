This document explains Fares Allowance (FAA) for ABSTUDY. It is paid to
cover the cost of specific travel for students who need to live away
from home to attend study or to participate in an approved Away from
Base activity.

Eligibility

Students may be eligible if they are a full-time or concessional
study-load and meet one of the following:

-   secondary and tertiary students eligible for the Away from Home rate
-   independent and pensioner students who need to study away from their
    permanent home
-   Masters and Doctorate students (instead of Relocation Allowance)
-   students and part-time students to attend an approved Away From Base
    activity
-   secondary students in State Care who are eligible to live away from
    home but are not eligible for Away from Home Rate of Living
    Allowance

Non-student travellers may also be entitled to FAA.

Australian Apprentices are not entitled to FAA.

Claiming

Customers can claim for FAA before and after travel. The Resources page
shows how a customer can lodge a claim.

Customers may claim for the following travel:

-   Travel at commencement and end of study period
-   Tertiary or secondary non-schooling semester travel
-   School vacation travel
-   Compassionate travel
-   Away from Base travel
-   Examination travel
-   Graduation travel
-   Masters and Doctorate travel for ABSTUDY
-   Orientation or Special Purpose visit travel
-   Emergency and disaster travel

Note: some restrictions apply to eligibility for assistance to travel
interstate.

Rate

FAA rate is based on the most practicable and cost effective for the
journey between the permanent home and the study location.

Travel to another destination other than the permanent home or usual
study location can be available in certain circumstances. See
Determining the appropriate travel destination for ABSTUDY Fares
Allowance (FAA).

Payment

The payment may be made to the:

-   Applicant
-   Institution
-   Third party incurring the expense

The Resources page has information on how students can claim Fares
Allowance (FAA).
