5

Verbal assessments + Read more ...

If a verbal application is asked for by an authorised third party, see Accepting information from and disclosing information to Power of Attorney to confirm authorisation.

When undertaking a verbal application ask the care recipient or third party if they want to:

  • Use their asset and income details to get an aged care means assessment

  • Discuss when the care recipient’s Customer First record was last updated
    • Update new information as needed
  • If needed, use the questions on the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care as a guide while on the phone with the care recipient

Can a verbal assessment be completed?

    • Care recipient or third party wishes to complete a form, or changes to circumstances are advised, and documentation is required to make these updates:

Income support payment (ISP) care recipient and a homeowner - value of principal home + Read more ...

When a current income support recipient asks for a means assessment for future entry into care, use all income and asset details on record to complete the assessment:

  • Update any changes to income and assets:
    • See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services
    • Information must be provided to determine required coding for the principal home

To calculate the care recipient’s net share of the principal home:

  • use the total asset value of the principal home
  • less the balance of any loans or mortgages (including Home Equity Access Scheme balance) currently owing
  • determine the care recipient’s percentage of ownership

Complete a verbal means assessment when supporting documentation is not needed to confirm the value of a principal home:

  • Supporting documentation for a mortgage or a refundable entry contribution is only needed when the care recipient's 'net share' is less than the first asset threshold

  • Where the home is a retirement village unit, see Retirement villages for more information

9

Code rental income + Read more ...

Rental income received for a care recipient's principal home may be exempt from a means assessment for aged care.

An income exemption for aged care purposes may apply where the care recipient:

  • is renting out their principal home
  • is paying their accommodation costs by either periodic payment or a combination of periodic and lump sum payments, and
  • entered care before 1 January 2016 (including pre-entry leave)

Note: income support payments (ISP) care recipients who entered care before 1 January 2017, have rental income received for their former principal home exempt from the ISP income and assets test. See Vacation of principal home due to illness.

If the care recipient receives rental income for their principal home, enter rental income received on REBS screen using applicable codes. See Aged Care means assessment - Rental income from principal home.

Update the RCA means test details (RMND) screen + Read more ...

Aged care pre-entry assessments are valid for a maximum of 120 days from the date the assessment is completed. When the activity is completed, the DOE defaults to the date of receipt of the activity. If assessment is being completed at a later date, a new entry needs to be added.

To add the start date, go the RMND screen and update:

  • Enter the date assessment was completed in the Reform Enquiry Means Test DOE field
  • Key 'Y' in the Reform Enquiry Means Test Ind field
  • Key ‘RES’ in the Reform Enquiry Care Type field

  • Key 'D' to delete any previous and existing data

Finalise the RCA/NCL as means not disclosed (MND) + Read more ...

Care recipients pay the full amount for the cost of their care if they do not disclose their income. A letter will not be issued. If a request for a letter to be issued is received, advise the care recipient:

  • 'As you have elected not to disclose your means we are unable to issue you a letter until you start receiving care. You may need to negotiate the cost of your care directly with the service provider'

Ensure the RCIRC screen fields DSS Require Income Details and Date of Effect (DOE) are populated as below:

  • Key 'Yes' in the DSS Require Income Details field
  • Enter the date of receipt (DOR) of the assessment in the Date of Effect field
  • Update the DOE if needed

Navigate to the RCA Means Test Details (RMND) screen within the RCA/NCL activity and key (I) the following:

  • Upper section of RMND:

Confirm the assets outcome on RCAD + Read more ...

The RCAD screen should reflect the assets outcome of the care recipient. For care recipients with a partner this will be 50% of the total combined assets recorded, regardless of the actual ownership percentage of the asset.

Does the RCAD screen display the correct asset amount for the care recipient?

  • Yes, go to Step 7

6

Reject or withdraw a pre-entry means assessment + Read more ...

A pre-entry means assessment is rejected if:

  • an aged care RFI was issued and the care recipient has not provided documentation within 21 days (plus extra time for mail delivery), or
  • a care recipient withdraws their means assessment request

Note: if a care recipient is currently in care, a claim is not able to be rejected, but the activity can be cancelled, a Fast Note will be recorded and a Final Notification will be issued.

When the care recipient has not entered permanent residential care and has not provided requested information:

  • key 'S' in the RCA/NCL activity
  • navigate to BA screen to rejected the RCA/NCL:
    • Key 'RCA' in the SVC RSN field
    • Key 'REJ' in the Action field
    • Key 'OTH' in the Reason field
    • Enter the date of event (DOV) in the Effect Date field
    • Key 'REJ-OTH RCA/NCL REJ - failed to provide information' in the Notes field
  • attach Fast Note to claim activity and finalise via AR
    • Rejected claims – Auto text > Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care MA Rejected-OTHER
    • Withdrawn claims - Auto text > Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care means assessment Withdrawn
  • issue a letter to care recipient and nominee using the AC - Request Further Information - Final notification - CR-NM template
  • a letter is not issued for withdrawn claims
  • scan and upload the aged care final notification to Customer First. The PDF file shows in the Document Tools tab

Procedure ends here.

Attach Auto-text Fast Note to RCA/NCL activity + Read more ...

When the RCA/NCL activity coding is complete, exit and attach a Fast Note to record the assessment outcome:

  • Locate and open the Fast Note. Select Auto Text > Aged Care > Assessment > RES Care Assessment - FIN to document the means assessment outcome
  • Link to the open RCA/NCL activity
  • Complete applicable fields of the Fast Note including all relevant information, for example:
    • When a verbal assessment was completed with changes coded, read the verbal declaration and document it was agreed to
    • Document if an update was made that differs from the information lodged (for example a care recipient failed to advise of a value of home contents on the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care form but later provides a verbal valuation)

8

Assessment result (AR) + Read more ...

When the Fast Note is complete:

  • select (S) RCA/NCL activity from Activity List (AL) screen and navigate to the Assessment Result (AR) screen.
  • Check results of assessment on AR by confirming:
    • the first ATH line and RCA Benefit line contain a date equal to the DOV of the assessment
    • any arrears or overpayments that have been calculated are correct based on DOV and date of receipt (DOR)
    • the result of the RCA Income Assessment Summary (RIAS) and RCAA Assessment Determination (RCAD) screens from the DOV once means assessment completed

For care recipients currently in care who require a manual pre-entry level letter (RC1), refer to Aged care letters – creating manual letters.
