4

Manual suspension and/or cancellation using the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow + Read more ...

If a carer receives CA for more than one care receiver, it is not possible to suspend one payment of CA. In these situations, as a matter of urgency, the carer should be contacted and the required information obtained.

The Carer cancellation and suspension workflow will allow staff to:

  • cancel or suspend CP at payment level, and/or CA at payment level or care receiver level in a single activity
  • choose action on CP and not CA or vice versa. In some cases the workflow will auto-populate the reason field where it is appropriate to cancel or suspend both CP and CA
  • perform different actions at the same time, for example, cancel CA if care requirements are not met and cancel CP if the carer's activities exceed 25 hours per week

Where the care receiver is not the carer's partner or child, the care receiver's circumstances will need to be updated separate to the workflow.

The workflow can only be actioned in the carer's record. Within the workflow there will be detailed help and direct links to Operational Blueprint.

Understanding the difference between carer level and care receiver level of cancellation is extremely important when there is more than one care receiver.

What does 'suspend/cancel at carer level' mean?

  • If CP and/or CA (for all care receivers) is suspended or cancelled for the carer and all payments cease, this is called carer level
  • If it is required to suspend or cancel CP at the carer level, 'Select a reason' from the drop down box
  • If it is required to suspend or cancel CA at the carer level, 'Select a reason at the carer level' from the drop down box. This will suspend/cancel CA for all care receivers

What does 'cancel at care receiver level' mean?

  • Note: it is not possible to suspend payment at the care receiver level
  • A carer may receive CA for one or more care receivers and it may be necessary to cancel a chosen care receiver(s) only. The payment will cancel for the chosen care receiver(s) only and C A may remain current if there are other qualifying care receivers. This is called care receiver level
  • If it is required to cancel CA at the care receiver level then enter a reason from the drop down box at the 'Select a reason at the care receiver level' field

The workflow allows some current BA and CJBA codes to be selected only, manual suspension or cancellations may be required if the BA and/or CJBA code is not able to be selected within the workflow. For manual suspension and cancellations see:

  • Suspension of Carer Payment (CP)
  • Suspension of Carer Allowance (CA)
  • Cancellation of Carer Payment (CP)
  • Cancellation of Carer Allowance (CA)

The Carer cancellation and suspension workflow will recognise and prompt for hand off when a Service Officer tries to use a code that is limited to carer Processing Service use. When this occurs, the activity should be handed off to the appropriate Processing Service for assessment and completion

For a complete list of valid suspension and cancellation reasons that can be used within this workflow, see:

  • Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Carer Payment (CP)

6

Suspending or cancelling CA using the workflow + Read more ...

If a carer receives CA for more than one care receiver, it is not possible to suspend one payment of CA. In these situations, as a matter of urgency, the carer should be contacted and the required information obtained.

Go to the carer's record

  • Select 'ISS' system, with 'CDA' as the subsystem or 'PEN' system
  • Select the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow from the left hand menu list titled 'Carer'
  • Run the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow

Upon initial entry to the workflow, check that the source and date of receipt are correct. The source field will automatically populate based on the Service Officer's user profile location and the date of receipt field will automatically populate with the system date. If these are incorrect they must be changed before proceeding.

The care receiver(s) will be displayed if they are current, suspended, cancelled or conducting a new claim within the period from today to within the previous 26 weeks.

After a reason has been selected at either the carer level or care receiver level, the workflow will automatically populate the Action field with 'cancel' or 'suspend'. For example, if the reason 'GWN' is chosen at the CA carer level it will populate the reason at the CP carer level.

  • Cancellation of Carer Allowance (CA), and/or
  • Cancellation of Carer Payment (CP)

Note: if the reason 'Other' ('OTH') is coded, a prompt will appear advising the Service Officer to hand off to the Carer Processing Service if they are not attached to a Carer Processing Service in their user profile. The message prompt is 'Carer Processing Teams should only use 'OTH' as a final option where no other cancellation, suspension or change in circumstance coding is appropriate.' A manual letter must be issued after the workflow is completed.

CA and CP can be cancelled and/or suspended with a different reason and different date of effect, for example, cancel CP with reason '25H' from start date of activity 01042010 and cancel CA not living with Carer - care not in home of carer or care receiver from Date Paid to (DPT) +1 being 08042010.

For cancellations, manually code the correct Date of Effect (DOE). However, for suspensions the workflow will automatically populate the DOE field with DPT+1.

There will be workflow prompts for some of the reasons to assist with coding the correct DOE, for example, entry into permanent institution 'PMI', carer activity exceeds 25 hours per week '25H'.

Does CP also need to be suspended or cancelled?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 1 in Table 2

  • Cancellation of Carer Allowance (CA), and/or

Note: if the reason 'Other' ('OTH') is coded, a prompt will appear advising the Service Officer to hand off to the Carer Processing Service if they are not attached to a Carer Processing Service in their user profile. A manual letter must be issued after the workflow is completed.

Some advices will have the letter displayed upon 'S'electing the activity, while other activities will need to be 'S'elected from the Text Find (TF) screen.

Following the suspension or cancellation of payment, a manual letter will be required if a Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity has been created on the Activity List (AL) screen after the suspension or cancellation has been finalised.

  • If there is a MFU requiring a manual letter to be sent to the carer, go to Step 3
  • If there is no MFU, a suspension or cancellation letter will automatically be sent to the carer, go to Step 4

3

Send letter manually + Read more ...

Select the MFU activity from the AL screen and manually issue a Q134 letter to the carer. The letter must contain the following information:

  • a clear explanation of the reason the payment has been suspended or cancelled
  • the date of effect of the suspension or cancellation
  • if the payment is suspended, what the carer needs to do to have their payment restored, and the consequences for not doing this (that is, payment will be cancelled)
  • the payment type that is, Carer Payment
  • the reference to the relevant act, and
  • the carer’s review and appeal rights

4

Check carer's other entitlements + Read more ...

  • Check if the carer is eligible for CA (child) manual under standard provisions, for example, if the CP (child) is cancelled and therefore carer loses entitlement to CA (auto) but the carer is still caring, or the carer is working more than 25 hours permanently and is still providing daily care to the child care receiver, see Qualifications for Carer Allowance (CA) (child) when a customer is claiming or receiving CA only
  • Whenever CP is cancelled the workflow will display the following prompt 'Carer Payment is about to be cancelled, discuss eligibility for other income support payments and run First Contact Concessions, Carers and Seniors (FCCCS) workflow if a new claim is required'
  • If the care receiver is a child and the carer is receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) follow up if FTB is still payable. For more information see Child leaves customer's care/custody
  • If the carer and care receiver are partners, they may be entitled to be assessed as being separated from their partner due to ill health
  • After cancelling the carer's CA, check the care receiver's record and change their address if required (an Accommodation Details Module (MOD AC) can be issued if the carer is unsure of the care receiver's new address). Update the Accommodation (AC) screen and Telephone Details (TD) screen. If the care receiver has a partner, follow the instructions to code illness separated on the MS screen
  • Check if the carer may be entitled to receive any other financial assistance
