This document outlines FCC processing principles which provide the best practice guidelines for all Service Delivery staff processing FCC new claims and activities in Process Direct and Customer First.

Purpose

The FCC processing principles provide a consistent approach to manage FCC work by:

  • working with Operational Planning to ensure organisational efficiencies
  • timely completion of new claim, non-new claim, telephony and internal review work, with fewer touch points, and
  • achieving consistent, quality outcomes for customers, regardless of where they live or their demographics

The FCC processing principles sets out:

  • high level principles for the management of FCC work
  • mandated process steps for working within Workload Management (WLM)
  • cross programme business process steps for management of claim work
  • lower level programme specific business/programme process detail
  • complementary support for the prescribed workflows, guided procedures and scripts as outlined within Operational Blueprint

Overarching principles

Workload Management

Service Officers need to follow correct Workload Management (WLM) processes.

WLM contains information on how to access, reallocate, complete and change the status of work items using Temporary Allocation and troubleshooting guides.

Smart Centre staff must also follow the Business Continuity Procedures when there are disruptions due to natural disasters and failure of computer systems.

Business Imperatives and self-service

Smart Centre Business Imperatives are designed to ensure quality customer service is provided at each contact. These include checking and confirming:

  • phone details to ensure correct details are on file
  • the Customer Issues page at every contact, and
  • income estimates and payment choices for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and income estimates for Child Care Subsidy (CCS)

In addition, all Service Officers are expected to transition customers to the digital channel at every opportunity unless:

  • the customer is not eligible for self-service, or
  • the Service Officer deems it inappropriate based on the customer's circumstances. See Using self service and How customers create a myGov account and link services.

The Resources page contains links to:

  • the Business Imperatives page
  • the Services Australia Corporate Plan
  • Digital Support Resources page for additional training and support

Processing families claims in Customer First and Process Direct

Service Officers must process the following claims in Process Direct:

  • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) pre-birth
  • DAP post-birth
  • Parental Leave Pay (PPL) pre-birth
  • PPL post-birth
  • PD PPL Add Newborns
  • Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS)

All other families claims must be processed in Customer First, including:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) pre-birth
  • FTB post-birth
  • FTB/PPL combined
  • FTB lump sum claims
  • FTB Add Newborns
  • FTB/PPL combined Add Newborns
  • CF PPL Add Newborns

Add Newborn activities are created in either Process Direct or Customer First, and can only be processed in the platform they are created in:

  • Customer First PPL Add Newborns have a 10-digit work item ID
  • Process Direct PPL Add Newborns have an 8-digit work item ID

Note: some Process Direct processes may require pre-processing updates in Customer First. See relevant processing Operational Blueprint files for further information.

Operational Blueprint and Network News Update (NNU)

All Service Officers need to follow business processes, prescribed workflows and tools outlined in Operational Blueprint.

Service Officers are strongly encouraged to check each day for Current messages and OB Updates to keep informed of initiatives and changes to processes.

Staff must also regularly check NNU, FCC and/or National ICT Referral Officer (NIRO) Current Issues and Process Direct sub-site Current Issues pages for current system issues and approved workarounds. See the Resources page.

The Resources page contains links to the intranet pages, the Digital Support Resources and NNUs. It also contains information about Boutique teams.
