Assessing an RSI

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Identify response + Read more ...

Have the customer (and alleged partner if applicable) both responded?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No,
    • If pending a response from both parties, go to Step 2
    • If there has been no response by the due date from all parties to the review (including cancelled customers), assess and finalise using the information provided/on hand. Go to Step 3

2

Place intervention on hold, pending response from both parties + Read more ...

In IRS, record a contact and place the intervention on hold (HLD).

On IRCD screen, key:

  • Expected response date: the same date as other party's pending due date
  • Person contacted: 'affected partner'
  • Letter Code: the letter code of the pending request for contact/information letter for affected partner

Procedure ends here until either both parties respond or the due date.

3

Assess and determine relationship status + Read more ...

Once all information has been verified and assessed, determine the customer's relationship status.

Assess the information against the 5 factors for assessing a MOC in Member of a Couple (MoC) and Separated under one roof (SUOR) assessments.

Consider all relevant information about the customer's relationship that can be assessed and supported by evidence. Consider it in an objective and independent way.

Personal opinions, biases and moral judgements must not form any part of the decision making process.

For help assessing an Australian Defence Force member of a couple, see the Resources page for a link to ADF Pay and Conditions Manual, Chapter 8.

See Family and domestic violence if there are any concerns about family and domestic violence during the conversation.

4

Select the IRS intervention + Read more ...

Before making any updates on the customer's record, e.g. their marital status:

  • check the IRS review has a status of 'STA' (started)
  • select the review from the Review Summary (IRRS) screen
  • be in the IRS review before updating the customer's record. This is also known as being within the Review Image

This will also make sure that all updates attribute to the IRS Review.

5

Update customer circumstances + Read more ...

To update the customer circumstances, see:

  • Change in relationship status from single to partnered
  • Family assistance customer becomes partnered
  • Relationship Status for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)

When linking customers they may lose eligibility for their payment. But they may be eligible for another payment. If this is the case and the customer is on the phone, transfer them to the correct call queue or advise them to claim online. For a principle carer going from Parenting Payment Single (PPS) to JobSeeker Payment (JSP), transfer them to the job seeker queue to claim JSP.

If the customer is not on the phone and cannot be contacted after 2 attempts, place a note in a flash DOC.

Additional risk

Include all Additional Risks identified in the assessment.

See the Resources page for a link to the Assessing the Additional Risk guide.

6

Determine if there is a debt or adverse decision + Read more ...

After updating the customer circumstance/s on the customer record, determine if there is a debt or adverse decision.

There will be a Coronavirus Supplement debt if a customer:

  • has a change in circumstances (other than a benefit transfer) backdated, and
  • is no longer qualified or had received less than $1 of benefit that attracts the supplement within the Coronavirus Supplement Entitlement Period

Coronavirus Supplement debts are not to be bundled with other debts. They must be raised separately.

Arrears outside the debt period

Arrears presented outside the debt period for an RSI can be used to offset the debt or be paid to the customer if they are legally payable.

For arrears to be legally payable, the activity that has produced the arrears must have a Date of Receipt (DOR) within 13 weeks of the date of the original decision.

Use of the ADEX Scheduler is recommended. It will correctly calculate the debt amount and the amount paid to the customer when adjusting the dates.

Clearly document the decision to allow arrears to offset an overpayment in the intervention DOC.

Is there a debt or adverse decision?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3

Calculating and raising overpayments for an RSI

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Determine debt and calculate + Read more ...

All debts that can be calculated via the Centrelink system and explained using ADEX should be calculated that way. Only use MultiCal if the Centrelink system calculation is not possible or the outcome is not correct.

Check the Assessment Result (AR) screen to make sure the system has correctly calculated any potential overpayment, for example:

  • Debt schedule - do the debt dates/date of event (DOV) match the period?
  • Have the updates/coding been entered correctly?
  • Check the following to make sure the updates are as expected:
    • Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS)
    • Income Bank (NIB)
    • Employment Income Assessment (EIA)
  • Can a reassessment be made using the Benefit Action (BA) screen?
    • Use REA in relevant host system, or
    • RCR in PEN/PGA (for cancelled PEN and PGA records) and leave Reason blank

Note: check if Review of Assessment (ROA) coding is needed to generate allowable arrears. Do this when corrected income is less than declared, but the system did not generate arrears for those periods/fortnights.

If an ADEX debt calculation:

  • can be undertaken, the overpayment must be calculated with the customer over the phone where possible
  • is not possible, a MultiCal debt calculation may be required

For MultiCal debt calculation, get all information from the customer over the phone where possible to enable a debt calculation.

The CO is responsible for progressing the MultiCal activity. If they cannot action, their site is responsible for progressing the activity. The Team Leader will need to reassign the activity to another CO with the required skill set.

For more information, see:

  • Debt tools
  • Debt investigation
  • Actioning an undetermined debt on the Debt management and Information System (DMIS)
  • Using ADEX to explain a system generated debt
  • Using the correct date of effect when raising debts
  • Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts
  • Waiving Centrelink debts

2

Contact customer and alleged partner + Read more ...

If the customer is not on the phone, make 3 genuine attempts to contact the customer and the alleged partner if the alleged partner has incurred a debt.

Follow the phone contact requirements.

Record all attempts to contact the customer and partner on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for the DOC requirements.

Record contact details in IRS.

Was contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, after the third attempt, go to Step 5

3

Advise outcome + Read more ...

If the customer and/or alleged partner is responding to the Pre-adverse decision letter:

  • Record their contact on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for DOC requirements
  • Record contact details in IRS

Discuss the outcome of the intervention with the customer and/or alleged partner.

Tell the customer what information was used to calculate the outcome. This could include a combination of verification obtained from the customer or a third party.

Tell the customer:

  • Debt: 'Based on the information you have advised/ we have obtained from third party regarding your relationship and/or partner income was not taken into account in the payments made to you. This has resulted in an overpayment of your Centrelink payment of $xxxxx for the period (date to date). If you do not believe this outcome is correct, we can update your details now if you have further information available.'
  • Coronavirus Supplement debt: 'This has also resulted in an overpayment of the Coronavirus supplement of $xxxxx for the period (date to date).'

Ask the customer if they have more information they want to provide before the outcome is finalised. If they want to provide more information, a satisfactory verbal explanation for the discrepancy may be provided. Update the customer record and recalculate as required.

If the customer has more information they want to provide but cannot provide it over the phone, tell them:

  • they have 14 days to provide the information
  • the intervention will be finalised using the information available if the requested information is not provided within the expected response time

Check with the customer if they require a further call at this stage or happy to receive the accounts payable which will indicate the debt outcome.

If the customer is not satisfied with the decision and they have no more information they can provide, tell them:

  • the decision will be made using the information available
  • they should provide any extra information/ evidence that may be relevant to the decision as soon as they can. If more information is received, a reassessment will occur

Tell the customer their review and appeal rights. See the Reviews and appeals of a Centrelink decision page on the Services Australia website.

Ensure the details of the conversation with the customer are recorded in the intervention DOC.

Is the customer providing more information?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 6

4

Customer providing more information + Read more ...

COs must self-assign the work item via Work Optimiser and ensure the IRS review is in their Work Group and Position.

Record a contact and place the intervention on hold.

Once activities come due or information is provided

  • When the intervention comes due, check if more information has been provided. If so, update the customer’s record based on that information and raise debt and finalise the intervention
  • If no new information is provided, raise debt and finalise the intervention based on information previously provided

Procedure ends here.

5

Issue Pre-adverse decision letter + Read more ...

After 3 unsuccessful call attempts to the customer and/or partner to advise the outcome, issue a manual Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter to them.

This letter tells the customer what evidence has been used to calculate a debt and gives an estimated debt amount. It gives the customer a further right of reply before finalising the outcome and further actions.

See the Resources page for the Q888 template. Use the correct letter template based on the customer's circumstances.

In IRS, record a contact and place the intervention on hold (HLD) for 28 days:

  • On IRCD screen, key:
    • Expected response date: 28 days in future
    • Letter code: 'ADVERS'
    • Text: 'Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter issued'
  • On IRMR screen, self-assign the intervention and add keyword:
    • Workgroup: relevant workgroup
    • Position: COs logon
    • Keyword: 'Q888'

COs must also self-assign the work item via Work Optimiser and DOC the record. See the Resources page for DOC requirements.

Once activities come due or information is provided

  • When the intervention comes due, check if more information has been provided. If so, update the customer’s record based on that information and raise debt and finalise the intervention
  • If no new information is provided, raise debt and finalise the intervention based on information previously provided

Procedure ends here.

6

DOC all information to the customer's record + Read more ...

Record all information in the customer's intervention DOC to clearly explain the:

  • customer's circumstances
  • information relating to the compliance trigger
  • the scope of intervention
  • customer's action
  • outcomes of the intervention

If the customer is on the phone, advise them of the:

  • debt period
  • debt amount
  • reason for the debt, and
  • 'You will receive a letter with this information and instructions outlining the next steps'

All DOCs must give a clear and concise history of the customer's interactions. They must also adhere to the:

  • Online Document Recording (ODR) standards, and
  • Getting it Right (GIR) Minimum Standards

See the Resources page for DOC requirements.

7

Raise debt as per guidelines + Read more ...

Use the Debt Action script whenever possible.

Raise the debts (including partner) as per current guidelines.

Do not bundle Coronavirus Supplement debts with other debts. They must be raised separately. Waiver provisions can be applied to Coronavirus Supplement debts.

Debts less than $50 can be waived.

If deciding to waive the debt due to a vulnerability concern on the customer and/or partner record, the debt must be raised then waived.

Note:

  • The date entered into the date contact made with customer question should be the date the Debt Action Script is run
  • If the debt amount generated via ADEX differs from the final debt amount being raised, fully document the reasons for this in the stand alone debt DOC
  • Coronavirus Supplement debts are raised under S1223(1) of the Social Security Act (S1223(1) for the script
  • The reason code for Coronavirus Supplement debts should be NEP - Not eligible for payment
  • The date for Coronavirus Supplement debts should be the 'payment date'. This is the date the Coronavirus Supplement was paid in the system, not the date the customer received it

Use of the debt advice template is recommended when raising debts from verbal updates. However, the debt advice can be changed to suit the customer.

See Sending Account Payable letters for a table of debt reason codes, default text for the Account Payable (OPAOD) screen, and the rules for entering free text on the account payable letter.

See the Resources page for the Details of debt calculated DOC template.

For FTB debts

  • not related to qualification, see Reconciliation of Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
  • related to qualification, see Family Assistance debts

Deceased customers

If a debt is likely, before sending the debt advice, a social worker in Payment and Integrity decides the best way to contact the executor. See Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer.

Troubleshooting debt amount

It may be necessary to assess whether to use MultiCal to calculate a customer's potential overpayment. For what to check, see Step 2 in Table 2.

Finalising an RSI

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Document the intervention + Read more ...

Record all information in the customer's intervention DOC to clearly explain the:

  • customer's circumstances
  • information relating to the compliance trigger
  • the scope of intervention
  • customer's action
  • outcomes of the intervention

All DOCs must give a clear and concise history of the customer's interactions. They must also adhere to the:

  • Online Document Recording (ODR) standards, and
  • Getting it Right (GIR) Minimum Standards

See the Resources page for DOC requirements

2

Before finalising the intervention + Read more ...

Make sure that all actions are complete on the customer record (including any linked reviews) and (ex) partner records:

  • Check all activities on the Activity List (AL) screen are completed
  • Check all entries on the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen are completed
    Response?: field: Y or N

Check to see that all updates that have been made during the intervention process have been correctly attributed to the review within IRS. For example, all relationship status updates and suspension action have been attributed to the IRS review.

3

Use the Finalise Review script to finalise the intervention + Read more ...

The script will pause at the Attribute Result (IRAR) screen in IRS. This screen will include all activities on the customer record that have occurred over the life of the IRS review.

Select the activities that relate to the RSI process. For example, suspension, cancellation or a debt which resulted from updating the customer's marital status.

Ensure that a debt raised as part of the RSI is attributed to the review.

If the correct process to action the customer updates within the Review Image has not occurred, and the debt or activities have not attributed to the review:

  • Attribute/unattributed activities as per current guidelines. See Finalising and attributing actions to a Service Update or Review in the IRS
  • Choose an appropriate Result Reason as per current guidelines. See Calculating a Review Result and Restoration Processing in the IRS

If the customer has a linked review or (ex) partner who also has an intervention, finalise all interventions at the same time.

If required, seek help from the QDO to confirm all updates have been correctly attributed before finalisation.

Report Suspected Fraud

After completing an intervention, if the Compliance Officer identifies fraud, they must report it. See Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption.

4

Issuing advice + Read more ...

Was a member of a couple assessment conducted?

  • Yes,
    • If the decision is favourable, issue a Q536 to the customer
    • If the decision is unfavourable, issue a Q499 to the customer
    • If the decision was unfavourable for a past period and the customer is no longer a member of a couple, DOC that no finalisation letter had been issued and the reason. If the customer was advised verbally of the outcome, make sure this is in the DOC
  • No, send a Q765 to the customer if:
    • no member of a couple decision was needed and the customer was not advised verbally of the outcome, or
    • payment cancelled due to failing to respond to initial letter and no third party verification sought

If the customer has a partner or alleged partner, finalise their intervention and issue the appropriate letter.
