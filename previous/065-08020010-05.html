Documents required for the Scheme applications

For further support on scanning and classifications see Classifying Centrelink digital images via the National Queue. Cover sheets to enable auto-classification of unstructured documents are available via Digitising (Scanning) a document using an MFD.

Documents required

Table 1

Form code or document type

Requirement

Classification guideline

Completed Scheme application (claim)

All Scheme applicants must provide a completed application signed by the applicant and, if a member of a couple, their partner.

The Scheme applications can be submitted online, with assistance from a Service Officer via Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) or a paper claim using the:

  • Home Equity Access Scheme partnered application form (SA310)
  • Home Equity Access Scheme single application form (SA496)

The preferred channel is online.

If the applicant is partnered and both members of the couple want to apply for a Scheme loan, each member of the couple must submit their own application.

Scheme applications completed by another person on behalf of an applicant cannot be accepted, unless the person is:

  • a correspondence nominee for the applicant, and
  • legally appointed to represent the applicant, for example under a Power of Attorney (POA)

Online claims

The Scheme applications submitted via the online channel or ACC are automatically classified as a new claim and a Work Item created.

Paper claims

Scan the SA310/SA496 ensuring the barcode displays on the first page of the scan. The form type will be automatically identified as SA310 or SA496.

  • If a Work Item for a Scheme claim does not already exist in the system, a new claim Work Item will be automatically created
  • If a Work Item for a Scheme claim already exists, the form will be attached to the existing claim Work Item as a supporting document

Recent title search

All Scheme applicants must provide a copy of a recent title search with their application.

If the title search provided by the customer is not dated within 13 weeks of the lodgement date, the Scheme processing team will conduct a title search.

Unstructured document - classify as UNS011.

Rates notice(s)

All Scheme applicants must provide a copy of the latest rates notice for each real estate asset property offered as security for the loan.

Unstructured document - classify as UNS011.

Insurance policy

All Scheme applicants must provide a copy of the current insurance policy (ies), or certificate(s) of currency covering each real estate property offered as security for the loan.

Unstructured document - classify as PLSICT.

Loan agreement(s), contract(s) and most recent mortgage statement(s)

All Scheme applicants who advise in the Scheme claim that the real estate asset(s) being offered as security for the loan is mortgaged or encumbered, must provide copies of loan agreement(s), contract(s) and most recent mortgage statement(s) for each real estate property offered as security for the loan

Unstructured document - classify as UNS011.

Identity documents

All Scheme applicants and (if applicable) their partners must have a Confirmed Identity status.

The customer and (if applicable) their partner must supply identity documents if they do not already have a Confirmed Identity status. Note: partners must also complete linkage.

UNS011 when provided to support a claim/application, else use UNS009.

Proof of age

All Scheme applicants and (if applicable) their partners must provide proof of age if they if they have not previously done so. Generally, this will have already been completed if the customer receives an income support payment through the department. Loan-only applicants who have not previously claimed a payment or concession are most likely to need to provide proof of age with their Scheme claim. Generally, proof of age will usually be verified through the provision of identity documents.

SA310/SA496 Part A - Applicant and partner declarations and authorities

Always required unless the applicant is single and applied online or via ACC (unless they completed a verbal declaration).

If the applicant is single

That is, the applicant must supply the SA310 Part A if they:

  • apply for the Scheme using the paper form (SA310), or
  • applied via ACC and completed a verbal declaration

If the applicant is partnered

The SA310 Part A must be signed by both MoC regardless of whether the other person is applying or distribution of property ownership. This includes if they apply online.

Classify as SA310 or SA496.

SA310/SA496 Part B - Real estate details

Generally only required if the customer has claimed using the paper form and has provided incomplete information about the real estate they are offering as loan security.

Classify as SA310 or SA496.

SA310/SA496 Part C - Co-owner declaration

Required if the real estate offered as security is co-owned with a third party individual(s) other than the partner e.g. a joint tenant.

Classify as SA310 or SA496.

SA310/SA496 Part D - Individual trustee guarantee declaration

Required if the real estate offered as security for the loan is the asset of an individual private trust.

Classify as SA310 or SA496.

SA310/SA496 Part E - Corporate trustee guarantee declaration

Required if the real estate offered as security for the loan is the asset of a corporate trust.

Classify as SA310 or SA496.

SA310/SA496 Part F - Company guarantee declaration

Required if the real estate offered as security for the loan is owned by a private company.

Classify as SA310 or SA496.

Company constitution

Required if the real estate offered as security for the loan is owned by a private company.

Unstructured document - classify as PLSCCT.

Trust deed

Required if the real estate offered as security for the loan is the asset of an individual private trust or a corporate trust.

Unstructured document - classify as PLSTDD.

Tax File Number (TFN) application

Required if the applicant does not have a valid TFN.

Classify as UNS003.

Per Classifying Centrelink digital images via the National Queue, TFN applications will be escalated to the Classification Support Queue with note 'for ATO'.

Retirement village agreements

If the real estate asset offered as loan security is a retirement village unit, the agreement(s) with the retirement village are usually required. This is determined on a case by case basis.

Unstructured document - classify as UNS011.

Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf form (SS313)

Required if:

  • the Scheme applicant wants to authorise another person or organisation to make enquiries, get payments and/or act on their behalf, or
  • the Scheme application is being lodged by a person or organisation on behalf of an applicant. Completion of the Scheme applications by someone other than the applicant will only be accepted if the person lodging the claim is a correspondence nominee for the applicant in addition to being legally appointed to represent the person, for example by a Power of Attorney

Classify as SS313.

Power of Attorney documentation

Required if the Scheme application is being completed by a person or organisation on behalf of an applicant.

Completion of the Scheme applications by someone other than the applicant will only be accepted if the person lodging the claim is legally appointed to represent the applicant, such as through a Power of Attorney, in addition to being a correspondence nominee for the applicant.

Unstructured document - classify as UNS011.

Other

Additional documents relating to the real estate asset may be required to establish adequate loan security. This is determined on a case by case basis.

Unstructured document - classify as UNS011.
