1

Recording a decision to not apply compliance action + Read more ...

All job seeker compliance decisions must be recorded in the Participation Compliance workflow.

The Participation Compliance workflow intuitively supports the decision making process, identifying appropriate outcomes based on information recorded within the workflow. When unexpected outcomes are presented by the workflow on the Decision and Penalty page, the decision maker must ensure key facts and evidence collected are correctly recorded, and that they have a good understanding of the legislation and policy intent before making the decision.

Rejection codes that accurately represent the reasons why a decision has been made must be used. The decision maker is responsible for ensuring the correct rejection reason code is used and when the outcome is transmitted to the provider, it contains sufficient information to ensure the provider understands why the compliance action was rejected and any impacts on future servicing identified.

See the Resources page for a list of rejection reasons.

2

Informing the provider of the rejection reason + Read more ...

When the compliance action was generated by the provider, they are electronically notified it has been rejected, the rejection reason and any free text about why the failure was rejected.

To further assist providers to improve job seeker servicing or the quality of future reports for compliance action, the decision maker is responsible for considering whether additional free text information about why a failure has been rejected would be helpful. For further information, see Sending free text to providers when compliance action finalised.

Note: any information recorded can be obtained by the job seeker under the Freedom of Information Act 1982. The information which can be lawfully provided by Services Australia to providers is covered by Section 202 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999.

3

Key messages when rejecting compliance action + Read more ...

When the decision is made not to apply compliance action, key messages delivered to the job seeker must reinforce the expectations of active participation in return for payment to assist in preventing any future non-compliance and support the role of providers in managing compliance.

Key messages must include:

  • the reasons for the decision - why in this instance the compliance action was not applied
  • discussing with job seekers the reasons they have provided, the consideration that has been given to their personal and other relevant circumstances, what the decision was based on, and how the outcome of the decision will affect the job seeker while also reinforcing messaging about expectations and personal responsibility

4

Participation suspensions outstanding when payment cancelled or suspended + Read more ...

When a participation payment is cancelled or suspended, background processing generally finalises any outstanding compliance action that is no longer required.

Participation suspensions are automatically finalised, payments restored and related re-engagement requirements ended when a participation payment is cancelled for any reason, or payment is suspended due to:

  • Departure overseas (DOT)
  • Imprisonment (IMP)
  • Customer in compensation preclusion period (COM)
  • Income Maintenance Period (IMP) preclusion applied (PIM)
  • Income maintenance period (IMN)

5

Outstanding unemployment failures or Unemployment Non-Payment Periods (UNPP) when payment cancelled or suspended + Read more ...

When a participation payment is cancelled or suspended, background processing generally finalises any outstanding compliance action that is no longer required.

An outstanding compliance investigation is automatically finalised if the participation payment is cancelled for either of the following reasons:

  • Customer has reached Age Pension age (AGE)
  • Customer has reached Age Pension age (AGP)

An outstanding compliance investigation may also automatically finalise if the participation payment is cancelled or suspended due to:

  • Departure overseas (DOT)
  • Imprisonment (IMP), or
  • Customer in compensation preclusion period (COM)

If payment is cancelled or suspended for other reasons while a compliance action is outstanding, the compliance action will remain on the Activity List (AL) screen for up to 91 days. The system will automatically finalise the compliance action if it is no longer required or if payments are still suspended or cancelled when the review activity matures 91 days after the failure became an Active failure. Note: these activities should only be held where a compliance investigation has commenced and further evidence is required to make a decision.

A compliance investigation will be required if payment is restored or re-granted prior to the 91 day finalisation review maturing.

6

Outstanding work refusal failures and serious failures for refusing suitable work when payment cancelled or suspended + Read more ...

When a participation payment is cancelled or suspended, background processing generally finalises any outstanding compliance action that is no longer required.

An outstanding compliance investigation is automatically finalised if the participation payment is cancelled for the following reasons:

  • Customer has reached Age Pension age (AGE)
  • Customer has reached Age Pension age (AGP)

An outstanding compliance investigation may also automatically finalise if the participation payment is cancelled or suspended due to:

  • Departure overseas (DOT)
  • Imprisonment (IMP), or
  • Customer in compensation preclusion period (COM)

If payment is cancelled or suspended for other reasons while a compliance action is outstanding, the compliance action will remain on the Activity List (AL) screen for up to 91 days. The system will automatically finalise if payments are still suspended or cancelled when the activity matures or if compliance action is no longer required. Note: these activities should only be held where a compliance investigation has commenced and further evidence is required to make a decision.

A compliance investigation will be required if payment is restored or re-granted prior to the 91 day finalisation review maturing.

7

Other outstanding compliance investigations when payment cancelled or suspended + Read more ...

When a participation payment is cancelled or suspended, background processing generally finalises any outstanding compliance action that is no longer required. This includes mutual obligation failures, connection failures, No Show, No Pay failures, non-attendance failures and reconnection failures.

An outstanding compliance investigation is automatically finalised when:

  • payment is suspended due to:
    • Departure overseas (DOT)
    • Imprisonment (IMP), or
    • Customer in compensation preclusion period (COM)
  • payment is cancelled due to:
    • Customer failed to re-engage (CDR)
    • Did not contact provider to re-engage (DCP)
    • Did not meet provider requirement (TCF)
    • Provider work refusal failure (TCW)
    • Non-attendance report received (ANR)
    • Did not attend participation appointment or activity (APA)
    • Mutual obligation or work refusal penalty (TCP)
    • Unemployment failure penalty (TCU)
    • Death (DEA)
    • Customer has reached Age Pension age (AGE)
    • Customer has reached Age Pension age (AGP)

If payment is cancelled or suspended for other reasons while a compliance action is outstanding, the compliance action will remain on the Activity List (AL) screen for up to 91 days. The system will automatically finalise the compliance action if it is no longer required or if payments are still suspended or cancelled when the review activity matures 91 days after the failure became an Active failure. These activities should only be held pending the maturation of the 91 day finalisation review if evidence has been requested that is required to make a decision.

A compliance investigation will be required if payment is restored or re-granted prior to the 91 day finalisation review maturing.

8

Outstanding Capability Assessment when payment cancelled or suspended + Read more ...

When a participation payment is cancelled or suspended, background processing generally finalises any outstanding compliance action that is no longer required.

A Capability Assessment is automatically finalised when a participation payment is:

  • cancelled for any reason other than Youth Allowance Age (YAL/CAN-AGD)
  • suspended due to:
    • Departure overseas (DOT)
    • Imprisonment (IMP)
    • Customer in compensation preclusion period (COM)
    • Income Maintenance Period (IMP) preclusion applied (PIM), or
    • Income maintenance period (IMN)

If payment is re-granted or restored the Employment Services System (ESS) will automatically check if a Capability Assessment is still required and will regenerate the Capability Assessment.

For further information, see Capability Assessments.

9

Outstanding Comprehensive Compliance Assessments (CCA) when payment cancelled or suspended + Read more ...

When a participation payment is cancelled or suspended, background processing generally finalises any outstanding compliance action that is no longer required.

If payment is suspended due to imprisonment (IMP) or cancelled for any reason while there is an outstanding CCA on the record, the system will automatically reject the CCA.

A new CCA will be generated the next time a triggering failure is applied, if the conditions still exist to generate the CCA.

If for any reason the CCA is not actioned within 91 days of being generated, the 91 day Compliance Action Review will mature and automatically reject the CCA.

For further information, see Completing a Comprehensive Compliance Assessment (CCA).

10

Manual intervention when system unable to automatically finalise outstanding compliance action + Read more ...

When the system is unable to automatically finalise outstanding compliance, manual intervention may be required.

PST-skilled Services Officers are responsible for all manual intervention to finalise outstanding compliance action the system was unable to automatically finalise.

If possible, the workflow will automatically identify the appropriate rejection reason and autofill the Decision and Penalty page. The decision maker is responsible for ensuring the most appropriate rejection reason has been selected before finalising the compliance action.

Note: a decision to reject, or not apply compliance action, must only be made by a Service Officer who has responsibility for undertaking compliance investigations for the type of compliance action being considered.

For further information, see Participation Compliance Manual Follow Up Review guide.

11

Finalising an unemployment failure or Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP) when new claim will be rejected + Read more ...

An unemployment failure or UNPP is not applied if a participation payment will not be payable.

Rejection codes presented in the Participation Compliance workflow are based on the type of failure committed and information that has been recorded in the Compliance Investigation workflow, including the main excuse for the failure category and reason selected. When a new claim will be rejected, the Service Officer with a WNPPD or UNPP security resource should reject the compliance action by recording the most appropriate excuse based on the circumstances and selecting the most appropriate available rejection reason.

Note: when contact with the job seeker cannot be established to conduct the compliance investigation a decision should be made on the available evidence with the likely outcome to apply the compliance action. For further information, see Unemployment due to a voluntary act or misconduct.

A Display on Access DOC must be recorded to advise that, had the claim not been rejected, an unemployment failure or UNPP would have been fully investigated. Include in the DOC:

  • date of event of unemployment
  • date the Employment Separation Certificate (SU1) was scanned (if applicable)
  • whether unemployment ceased as a result of voluntary unemployment (including when a job seeker accepts employment and is paid relocation assistance but does not actually commence employment), or due to misconduct as a worker, and
  • any other relevant details

Create an expiry date of 12 weeks from the date the job seeker ceased employment.

Note: the potential non-payment period for:

  • an unemployment preclusion period as a result of an unemployment failure is 4 weeks, or 6 weeks when they received relocation assistance to move to take up the work
  • a UNPP is 8 weeks from the date the job seeker originally ceased employment, or 12 weeks when they received relocation assistance to move to take up the work

If the customer re-claims income support with a start date within the potential unemployment preclusion period or UNPP, a compliance investigation for unemployment due to a voluntary act or misconduct will need to be regenerated and determination made regarding an unemployment failure or UNPP before the new claim can be finalised.

Payment suspensions and rejected failures + Read more ...

A decision to reject a non-attendance failure or reconnection failure triggered by a Provider Appointment Report (PAR) will not lift a Non-Attendance Report Received (ANR) payment suspension. The provider is responsible for re-engaging job seekers following a Non-Attendance Report (NAR) and payment suspension is automatically lifted once the job seeker attends the re-engagement appointment, or the provider records the job seeker is no longer required to re-engage.

When a decision is made to reject a connection failure, No Show, No Pay disengagement failure or reconnection failure, a 'Did not attend compulsory participation appointment or activity' (APA) suspension can be lifted by a PST-skilled Service Officer if there is no other valid suspension failure.

2

Comprehensive Compliance Assessments (CCA) + Read more ...

A provider request for a manual CCA should be rejected when:

  • the provider states the job seeker requires an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) or the provider notes indicate an ESAt is the more appropriate assessment
  • it is clear the provider has selected CCA in error
  • the provider notes indicate significant confusion regarding the purpose of a CCA, or
  • the provider has not supplied sufficient information to undertake an assessment

Note: when a job seeker has an outstanding ESAt, the provider will not be able to transmit a request for a manual CCA.

An automatic CCA should be rejected when:

  • the job seeker is currently serving an Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP) or other serious failure period
  • a determination on persistence would not be possible as the job seeker's record is cancelled
  • the CCA was generated within a period of conditional waiver, or
  • there has been a nationally declared disaster and the CCA cannot be conducted

A CCA should not be rejected or finalised without penalty simply because the person is in a Compliance Activity.

Failures lodged after a CCA has commenced should only be investigated if the event date of the failure is later than the date the CCA is finalised.
