Replacement or re-issued Medicare card request - prisoner enrolled on their own Medicare card

Table 1: this table describes what to do when a prisoner submits an application form for a replacement or reissued Medicare card and they are the only person active on the card.

Step

Action

1

Documents required + Read more ...

Where a prisoner requests a replacement or reissued Medicare card, the following documents must be provided:

  • Completed Medicare enrolment form (MS004) or previous version of the Medicare enrolment form (3101), and
  • Cover letter provided by the prison on prison letterhead that confirms the prisoner’s identity. The letter must include the prisoner’s:
    • Full name (and any other names known by)
    • Date of birth
    • Medicare number (if known)

Note:

  • a replacement card is required when a valid card needs to be replaced due to the valid card having been lost, stolen or damaged
  • a reissued card is required when a Medicare card has expired and a new card has not been issued.
  • a prisoner may also request a replacement Medicare card or a reissued Medicare card over the phone, however the prisoner must be authenticated. See:
    • Authenticating a Medicare customer
    • Request over the phone

2

Locate prisoner’s existing Medicare record in CDMS + Read more ...

Service Officers will need to conduct a search for the prisoner’s Medicare record in CDMS (where the Medicare number has not been provided).

See Register a customer for Medicare in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS).

Service Officers must identify if the prisoner is on a Medicare card on their own or if they are enrolled with other family members.

If they are on a Medicare card with other family members, go to Prisoner enrolled on a family Medicare card

If Service Officers are unable to locate a Medicare record in CDMS for the prisoner, further information may need to be sought, such as:

  • Any alias names the prisoner may be enrolled under
  • Any previous addresses of the prisoner
  • Names of family members the prisoner may be enrolled with

Note: Service Officers can call the Correctional Centre to seek further advice in the first instance. Where contact cannot be made, the application must be returned with a letter.

Where a Medicare record still cannot be located, go to Prisoner has never been enrolled.

3

Issue a replacement or reissued Medicare card + Read more ...

Where the prisoner is enrolled on a card on their own, Service Officers can request a replacement or reissued Medicare card in CDMS and post the card to the address outlined on the Medicare enrolment form. This may be either the:

  • Prison address, or
  • Prisoner’s preferred mailing address

Replacement or re-issued Medicare card request - prisoner enrolled on family Medicare card

Table 2: this table describes what to do when a prisoner submits an application form for a replacement or reissued Medicare card and they are active on a family Medicare record.

Step

Action

1

Documents required + Read more ...

Where a prisoner requests a replacement or reissued Medicare card, the following documents must be provided:

  • Completed Medicare enrolment form (MS004) or previous version of the Medicare enrolment form (3101), or Application to copy or transfer from one Medicare card to another form (MS011) and
  • Cover letter provided by the prison on prison letterhead that confirms the prisoner’s identity. The letter must include the prisoner’s:
    • Full name (and any other names known by
    • Date of birth
    • Medicare number (if known)

Note:

  • a replacement card is required when a valid card needs to be replaced due to the valid card having been lost, stolen or damaged
  • a reissued card is required when a Medicare card has expired and a new card has not been issued
  • a prisoner may also request a replacement Medicare card or a reissued Medicare card over the phone, however the Medicare card cannot be sent to the prison address and the prisoner must be authenticated. See:
    • Authenticating a Medicare customer
    • Request over the phone

2

Determine if prisoner needs to be copied or transferred to a new card + Read more ...

Service Officers will need to conduct a search for the prisoner’s Medicare record in CDMS (where the Medicare number has not been provided on the prison cover letter)

See Register a customer for Medicare in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS).

Where it is confirmed that the prisoner is on a family Medicare card, the following action must be taken:

Enrolled with other adults (including persons 15 years and over)

The prisoner must be copied onto their own Medicare card where:

  • The application form indicates the mailing address is the prison address, and
  • They have not indicated that they wish to be transferred to another card

The prisoner must be transferred onto their own Medicare card where:

  • They have indicated on the application form that they wish to be transferred to their own Medicare card, or
  • The prisoner is 15 years and over and enrolled on their parent/s Medicare card.

Enrolled with children (under 15 years) with no other adults listed on the card

The prisoner must be copied onto their own Medicare card where:

  • The children are only enrolled on the prisoner’s Medicare card (for example, they are not enrolled on any other Medicare card) and
  • The card is being sent to the prison address

The prisoner can remain on a Medicare card with his/her children where:

  • The children are all under 15 years of age, and
  • The children are also enrolled on another Medicare card, and
  • The requested mailing address is either the prison address or another preferred address

Note: only where children are enrolled on more than one Medicare card can the prisoner remain on a Medicare card with their children and have the card sent to the prison address.

The prison address cannot be recorded as the mailing address for the children if they are only enrolled on one Medicare card with the prisoner.

Medicare card request - prisoner has never been enrolled

Table 3: the table below describes what to do when a prisoner has never been enrolled in Medicare.

Step

Action

1

Eligibility for Medicare must be confirmed + Read more ...

Where a prisoner has never been enrolled in Medicare, their eligibility must be determined before they can be enrolled.

2

Documents required to confirm Medicare eligibility + Read more ...

The prisoner must submit:

  • Completed Medicare Enrolment form
  • Cover letter provided by the prison on prison letterhead which includes the prisoner’s:
    • Full name (and any other names known by)
    • Date of birth
    • Incarceration date
  • Eligibility documents

Note: the required eligibility and identity documents required will be dependent on whether the prisoner was born in Australia or overseas.

If born in Australia:

  • Australian passport, or
  • Australian birth certificate or extract
  • 2 acceptable residency documents:
    • where residency documents are not available, a letter on prison letterhead which confirms the prisoners residency in Australia is acceptable (this is confirmed where the prisoner's incarceration date was more than 6 months ago

If born overseas:

  • Australian passport or Australian citizenship papers, or
  • Foreign passport or travel document issued by the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT), and
  • A valid visa, and
  • Evidence of an application for permanent residency (if applicable)

Note: where a foreign passport has been provided, Service Officers can conduct an immigration status check via Visa Entitlement Verification Online (VEVO) to confirm their immigration status in order to determine eligibility.

It is not necessary for a prisoner to provide residency documents. The letter from the Correctional Centre sufficiently satisfies Medicare residency requirements.

If the prisoner does not meet the required eligibility criteria they cannot be enrolled in Medicare.

3

Prisoner is eligible to enrol in Medicare + Read more ...

Where all required evidence has been obtained to confirm the prisoner is eligible for Medicare, they can be enrolled and be issued a Medicare card.

The prisoner’s correct immigration status must be selected from the ‘Entitlement Type’ drop down box in CDMS.

See Register a customer for Medicare in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System

The Medicare card can be sent to the mailing address outlined on the Medicare enrolment form.

Request for a prisoner’s Medicare number or a replacement Medicare card over the phone

Table 4: the table describes what to do when a prisoner, or someone on behalf of the prisoner, calls to obtain their Medicare number or to request a replacement or reissued Medicare card. A reissued Medicare card cannot be done over the phone if the card has been expired for 6 months or more.

Step

Action

1

Prisoner requests Medicare details via phone + Read more ...

Where a prisoner is able to call Medicare, they must be authenticated before information can be provided. See Authenticating a Medicare customer.

A prisoner who is enrolled on a card of their own can request:

  • Their valid Medicare number and expiry date
  • A replacement card (lost, damaged or stolen)
  • A reissued Medicare card

If the prisoner is enrolled on a Medicare card with other family members, they can request the same services outlined above, but replacement or reissued cards cannot be sent to the prison address.

If the prisoner requests to be copied or transferred onto their own Medicare card, they must be advised to complete an ‘Application to copy or transfer from one Medicare card to another form’ and provide a letter from the prison on prison letterhead (which will confirm the prisoner’s identity).

2

Request for prisoner information from a third party (other than a health professional) + Read more ...

A prisoner’s Medicare card number or requests for a replacement or reissued Medicare card cannot be provided to a third party (such as a Correctional Services employee) unless consent has been obtained from the prisoner.

Consent can only be provided if Service Officers can speak with the prisoner at the time of the third party calling. The prisoner must also be authenticated.

Alternatively, a third party may have authority to act on the prisoner’s behalf, such has holding Power of Attorney, Guardianship and Administration Order, or is their Authorised Representative. This information must have previously been notated in CDMS and the third party must be authenticated before the prisoner’s information can be provided.

Note: health professionals can confirm a patient’s eligibility (including prisoners) by calling the agency on their designated phone line.

Request from a customer who has been released from prison

Table 5: this table describes what to do when a person has been released from prison and requests Medicare enrolment, Medicare card replacement or reissue, transfer to a new Medicare card, or updates to personal details.

Step

Action

1

Medicare enrolment transactions for customers released from prison + Read more ...

Customers who have been released from prison need to adhere to the same requirements as any other member of the general public.

To have a Medicare card replaced or reissued or update personal details, the customer can call the Medicare general enquiries line and be authenticated. See Authenticating a Medicare customer.

If you cannot authenticate the customer, advise them to provide to the agency:

  • a completed enrolment form, and
  • prison release papers, or
  • an identity document

The form has details on how they can provide these documents to Services Australia.

Note: prison release papers provided by the customer are acceptable for confirming the customer’s identity and residency.

2

Confirm all details are current + Read more ...

Where a customer has been released from prison and makes contact with the agency, Service Officers should confirm all existing details are still current.

This could include:

  • Mailing address
  • Contact details
  • Bank account details
  • Group association with other Medicare records

Service Officers should also discuss Medicare online options with the customer and assist them with creating a Medicare online account if required.
