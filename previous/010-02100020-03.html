Determining ABSTUDY FAA eligibility

Fares Allowance (FAA) for ABSTUDY customers

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Initial contact with students + Read more ...

Staff can give general advice about ABSTUDY FAA, but should encourage the student or non-student traveller to test their eligibility, particularly in cases of discretionary decisions.

Is the customer requesting FAA to undertake their Australian Apprenticeship?

  • Yes, Australian Apprentices are not eligible for FAA. See Step 10 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check ABSTUDY status + Read more ...

Check if the student is current on ABSTUDY. In Customer First this will display on the Benefit Status field on the Customer Summary screen as ABY/CUR or ABY/ASS.

Is the student current on ABSTUDY?

  • Yes, Benefit Status field shows as ABY/CUR or ABY/ASS, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, ABY is not displayed in the Benefit Status field or it displays as ABY/SUS or ABY/CAN, go to Step 3

3

Secondary boarding students that have not returned to school + Read more ...

Secondary boarding school students, either suspended or cancelled Intent to Board (ITB) because of the Payment on Advice process, are eligible for FAA to travel back to school. The travel request is proof of their intention to return to school.

Is the student suspended or cancelled ITB and requesting FAA to return to school?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Graduation event + Read more ...

Fares Allowance may be approved if:

  • travel has been requested for a student who has completed secondary studies to attend a graduation event, and
  • they received ABSTUDY Away from Home assistance

Is the student requesting travel for a graduation event for Secondary Studies they received ABSTUDY away from home rate for?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Not in receipt of ABSTUDY + Read more ...

A student must be in receipt of, or approved for ABSTUDY to be eligible for FAA.

If the ABSTUDY payment has been suspended or cancelled, reasonable attempts must be made to resolve issues and to restore the ABSTUDY payment. See Restoration of ABSTUDY payments.

Note: if a secondary school student has travelled to their place of study and their ABSTUDY has since cancelled/suspended, under a duty of care, the end of term or end of year trip must be booked to travel the student home. Travel should not be booked for the student to return to study until the student's ABSTUDY has been restored.

Is the student's ABSTUDY now displaying as CURrent or ASSessed?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Check if an ABSTUDY claim has been lodged + Read more ...

To check if a claim has been lodged in Customer First, go to the Document List (DL) screen.

If a claim has been lodged the DL screen will display either a:

  • OLC claim progress DOC, or
  • Progress of ABSTUDY Claim DOC

Has a claim for ABSTUDY been lodged but not processed?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, the student is not eligible for FAA, see Step 10 in Table 2

7

Check if all supporting documents have been provided for the claim + Read more ...

If an ABSTUDY claim has been submitted and not assessed, review the Progress of ABSTUDY Claim Display on Access (DOA) DOC in Customer First to determine if all required documentation has been provided.

  • For secondary boarding students, go to Step 8
  • For secondary students in private boarding arrangements and tertiary students, go to Step 9

8

Secondary boarding students + Read more ...

Check the documentation lodged to support the ABSTUDY claim.

Has all the required evidence been provided for the claim?

  • Yes:
    • if the student is eligible for urgent processing, see Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing
    • advise the student or applicant, travel can be requested by the boarding school/hostel once the ABSTUDY claim has been assessed
    • the student is not eligible for FAA until the ABSTUDY claim has been assessed. See Step 10 in Table 2
  • No:
    • advise the student or applicant which documents are required for the ABSTUDY claim to be assessed
    • travel can be requested by the boarding school/hostel once the ABSTUDY claim has been assessed
    • annotate the Progress of ABSTUDY Claim DOA DOC
    • the student is not eligible for FAA until the ABSTUDY claim has been assessed. See Step 10 in Table 2

9

Secondary students in private boarding arrangements and tertiary students + Read more ...

Check the documentation lodged to support the ABSTUDY claim.

Has all the required evidence been provided for the claim?

  • Yes:
    • if the student is eligible for urgent processing, see Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing
    • advise the student or applicant, travel can be requested once the ABSTUDY claim has been assessed
    • the student is not eligible for FAA until the ABSTUDY claim has been assessed. See Step 10 in Table 2
  • No:
    • advise the student or applicant which documents are required for the ABSTUDY claim to be assessed
    • advise the student or applicant they can request travel once the ABSTUDY claim has been assessed
    • annotate the Progress of ABSTUDY Claim DOA DOC
    • the student is not eligible for FAA until the ABSTUDY claim has been assessed. See Step 10 in Table 2

Determining ABSTUDY FAA eligibility

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check correct education details are recorded + Read more ...

To determine the student's eligibility for FAA their study details must be checked.

In Customer First, check the correct course details are coded on the Education Details (EDC) screen. To access this screen type EDC in the Next: field.

Are the study details correct?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Update study details + Read more ...

If the student's study details are incorrect:

  • update the course details on the student's record, see Changing or correcting course of study and/or educational institution for ABSTUDY students
  • update the payment destination on the Payment Instruction (PIPI) screen where applicable to ensure payments are issued to the correct payee, see Payments to third parties and institutions for ABSTUDY

Go to Step 3.

3

Check if course is at Masters or Doctorate level + Read more ...

Students studying a Masters or Doctorate level course may not be eligible for FAA in some circumstances. To check if a student is studying a Masters or Doctorate level course, in Customer First check the Course Level field on the EDC screen.

Is the student undertaking a Masters or Doctorate level course?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Check if Relocation Allowance has been paid + Read more ...

A Masters or Doctorate student who has received Relocation Allowance will not be eligible for FAA. They are deemed to have moved their permanent home to the study location. However, they may still qualify for FAA for an Away from Base activity.

To check if Relocation Allowance has been paid, in Customer First, check the Supplementary Education Payments (EPSP) screen. To access this screen type EPSP in the Next: field.

Has the student already received Relocation Allowance while undertaking their Masters or Doctorate course?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Check Away from home rate eligibility + Read more ...

Students must meet 1 of the away from home criteria to qualify for FAA.

For secondary students:

  • In Customer First go to the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home summary (NIHS) screen to check for a current away from home (AFH) approval. To access this screen, type NIHS in the Next: field
  • If required, reassess the student's Independent/Homeless/Away from home reason and code the change from the relevant event date

For tertiary students:

  • In Customer First go to the home location on the Address History (ADH) screen and check the student's permanent home has not changed. To access this screen, type ADH in the Next: field
  • Check the study location on the EDC screen and confirm the student will be living away from home
  • Confirm the student's home location is not within reasonable travelling time or distance of their study location

Note: a student does not need to be in receipt of the away from home rate of ABSTUDY, but must meet 1 of the criteria for approval of away from home. For example, a 30 year old student whose permanent home is more than 90 minutes from the study location meets 1 of the away from home criteria, but is paid at the independent rate due to their age rather than the away from home rate.

Does the student meet 1 of the criteria for approval of the away from home rate for ABSTUDY?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 10

6

Check eligibility for the type of travel + Read more ...

Different types of travel can be accessed by students who need to live away from home to attend study or participate in an approved Away from Base activity.

Determine the reason for the travel request and check the student's eligibility and limits of entitlement for the specified travel requested, see:

  • Travel at commencement and end of study period for ABSTUDY
  • Tertiary or secondary non-schooling semester travel
  • School vacation travel
  • Compassionate travel
  • Away from Base travel
  • Examination travel
  • Graduation travel
  • Non-student travellers
  • Masters and Doctorate Relocation travel
  • Orientation or special purpose visit travel
  • Emergency and disaster travel

Note: FAA may be payable in circumstances where quarantine or self-isolation is required. Secondary school students suspended from school have no travel entitlement outside of the school vacation term travel dates. See Vacation travel for secondary school students for ABSTUDY.

Is the student eligible for the type of travel requested?

Yes, go to Step 7

No, go to Step 10

7

Check restrictions on interstate travel + Read more ...

Entitlement to interstate travel may vary according to the type, and level of study undertaken by the student.

Do the restrictions on interstate travel prevent eligibility of FAA?

Yes, go to Step 10

No, go to Step 8

8

Check travel destination + Read more ...

A student is normally entitled to travel between the permanent home and the study location. However, travel to or from another destination may be approved in some circumstances.

See Determining the appropriate travel destination for ABSTUDY FAA.

Is the travel request to or from an approved location?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

9

Determine how FAA is to be paid for ABSTUDY + Read more ...

The student has established eligibility to access FAA for ABSTUDY.

If FAA is required:

  • To be authorised and booked in advance of travel, see FAA authorised in advance of travel for ABSTUDY. Procedure ends here
  • To be reimbursed after the travel has been undertaken, see Reimbursing fares and additional costs after travel has been undertaken for ABSTUDY. Procedure ends here

10

Not eligible for ABSTUDY FAA + Read more ...

Advise the student, Australian Apprentice or non-student traveller they are not eligible for FAA.

Note: if a student has travelled to their place of study and their ABSTUDY has since been cancelled/suspended, under a duty of care, the end of term trip must be booked to travel the student home. The return to study trip must not be booked until the student is current on ABSTUDY.

If the travel request has been received through:

  • the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Call:
    • record details of discussion on a DOC
    • explain their review and appeal rights
  • the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Travel team:
    • finalise the travel booking status as Rejected in the Travel Management Application (TMA)
    • record details of the decision on the Notes tab
    • see Coding pre-booked FAA entitlements for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
