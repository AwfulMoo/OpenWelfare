Scenarios - Exceptional circumstances Activity Test determinations

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Short term crisis

Juliet leaves the family home with 2 children to move into crisis accommodation due to family and domestic violence.

Juliet is not able to undertake any recognised activities but requires access to subsidised child care to ensure the children are safely cared for, and give continuity for the children during this period, while giving time to set up a life away from the former partner.

Juliet requests an exceptional circumstances activity test result on this basis, explaining both the children will require placement in long day care each day until secure accommodation is found.

Level 2 Helpdesk considers Juliet's situation and makes an exceptional circumstances activity test determination of 120 hours per fortnight for 2 CCS fortnights.

2

Responding to emergency

James is a single parent with an activity test result of 72 hours per fortnight who volunteers with the SES. A fire is threatening the local area and James is asked to participate in fighting fires and be on call for the next week.

James makes an exceptional circumstances application outlining the requirement for constant care for 5 year old child Alice for the week so James can fulfil the SES duties and be on call. As a single parent, James has no one else who can care for Alice who is not old enough to be left alone.

Level 2 Helpdesk considers James' situation and makes a decision to increase James' activity test result to 336 hours for the fortnight (a result cannot be increased for a single week) to ensure James can be available to carry out the duties as an SES volunteer and access 24 hour child care for Alice.

3

Increase in Activity Test result

Four-year-old Alex attends child care 3 days a week. Alex has been deemed to be at risk and Alex's parent, Jean, has been granted ACCS (Child Wellbeing).

While attending a rehabilitation program for 4 weeks, Jean leaves Alex in the care of a relative. Before going into rehab Jean makes an exceptional circumstances application requesting 120 hours of care for Alex so Alex can attend each 12 hour session for the 5 days of the week, because Alex's relative works full-time and has to commute an hour each way to the child care service.

The Service Officer record the details of Jean's circumstances, and scans the letter from the rehabilitation centre onto her Centrelink record. It is then referred to the ACCS Processing Team for assessment.

Level 2 Helpdesk considers the application and increases the ACCS (Child Wellbeing) result to 120 hours per fortnight while Jean is in rehabilitation.

4

Request for more than 100 hours subsidised care

Example 1:

Marlene and Ian each work 5 days a week and are entitled to 100 hours of subsidised care per fortnight. However, their child care service charges 12-hour sessions meaning Marlene's child Anne uses 120 hours of care per fortnight.

If Marlene made an exceptional circumstance submission for additional hours of subsidised care, the activity test result must not be increased because this is a routine occurrence resulting from the business practice of the child care service.

Example 2:

Evan and Jamie both work 90 hours per fortnight in a family business, with travel of 2 hours per day = 110 hours work/travel per fortnight. As this is their normal work routine, there is no entitlement to more than 100 hours of subsidised child care, and they would be responsible for fees above 100 hours.

Example 3:

Rebecca and Allan both work 110 hours per fortnight (including travel time) running their own business. Due to one of the other family members involved in running their business falling ill, they need to work an extra 20 hours (2 days) per fortnight work each. This means they are now working and travelling 134 hours/fortnight.

After evidence is provided, both Rebecca and Allan are assessed as eligible for an additional 24 hours per fortnight (2 x 12 (work/travel)) is granted to each of them, giving them access to 124 hours per fortnight of subsidised care.

Note: the additional hours granted is for the extra hours they are required to work due to exceptional circumstances, not the total work/travel (134) they are undertaking. This exceptional circumstance Activity Test result would be limited to the time it takes for new staffing arrangements to be made to replace the ill family member.

Example 4:

Sue works 90 hours per fortnight in a family business. Sue's child, Andrew, attends a local day care centre near the family home. It takes 75 minutes to travel from the day care centre to the family business. Although this means Sue is undertaking work and travel for a total of 115 hours per fortnight, this does not warrant an increase in subsidised hours as it is not due to exceptional circumstances.

Example 5:

Rodney is a single parent who works 80 hours per fortnight, travelling 2 hours per day from the child care service to work. Rodney is entitled to 100 hours subsidised child care per fortnight.

Rodney's house is damaged in a storm making it inhabitable, requiring Rodney to move in temporarily with relatives until repairs are completed (estimated to be 3 months). This temporary relocation means Rodney now has to travel 90 minutes each way from the child care service to work.

In this situation, after evidence has been provided, Rodney is granted an additional 10 hours subsidised care (110 hours per fortnight) for 3 months, when repairs are expected to be completed. These additional hours cover the additional travel hours only due to the exceptional circumstances.

If the repairs are completed earlier than expected and Rodney moves back home before the 3-month period, Rodney should notify Services Australia and the exceptional circumstances Activity Test result will be ended.

Scenarios - Ex-partner debt exceptions (exceptional circumstances)

Table 2: this table provides scenarios of ex-partner debt exceptions (exceptional circumstances) scenarios where a customer may be eligible or not.

These amounts may not be current. They are examples only. For current payments, see Rates and thresholds.

Item

Description

1

Scenario

Customer is partnered at start of financial year but separates from 1 December 2019.

Customer's income estimate was $80,000, Ex-partner was $100,000.

Reconciliation occurs and customer's actual income was $80,000 but ex-partner's income was $150,000.

Customer contacts and queries debt as their own income was the same. Customer is advised that total income was $230,000 instead of the $180,000 estimated for part of the year.

Customer stated does not understand how their ex-partner earned so much more than the estimate they provided originally.

Treatment

Exception can be applied only for the partnered period where there is an applicable debt.

Reason

Customer stated they were unaware their ex-partner was going to earn more than the estimate that was provided when they were partnered.

2

Scenario

Customer is single at start of financial year and becomes partnered from 1 December 2019.

Customer's income estimate was $60,000, and partners was $80,000.

Customer is still in relationship at end of financial year.

Reconciliation occurs and customer's actual income was $60,000 but new partner's income was $120,000.

Customer contacts and queries debt as their own income was the same. Advised that total income was $180,000 instead of the $140,000 estimated for part of the year. Customer states that new partner has advised incorrectly their income when they provided original estimate.

Treatment

Customer is not eligible for the exception.

Reason

Customer was partnered at the end of the financial year and should have been aware of the family's circumstances to update the estimate in a timely manner.

3

Scenario

Customer is partnered at start of financial year and becomes single from 15 February 2020.

Customer's income estimate was $60,000, Ex-partner's estimate was $60,000 for period they were together.

Reconciliation occurs and customer's actual income was $85,000 and ex-partner's income was $100,000.

Customer contacts and queries debt.

Customer is advised that total income was $185,000 instead of the $145,000 estimated for part of the year they were partnered and $85,000 for the period single when they estimated $60,000.

Customer stated that does not understand how their ex-partner earned more than the estimate they provided originally.

Treatment

Exception can be applied only for the partnered period where there is an applicable debt.

Reason

Customer stated they were unaware their ex-partner was going to earn more than the estimate that was provided when they were partnered, even though the customer contributed to the debt.

4

Scenario

Customer is partnered at start of financial year and becomes single from 1 March 2020.

Customer's income estimate was $40,000, and ex-partner's estimate was $80,000 for period they were together.

Reconciliation occurs and customer's actual income was $85,000 and ex-partner's income was $80,000.

Customer contacts and queries debt.

Customer was advised that their income of $85,000, compared to their estimate of $40,000 caused the total income used for the period they were partnered to be $165,000 instead of $120,000.

Customer also has a debt for the period they were single due to her estimate of $40,000 compared to actual income of $85,000.

Treatment

Customer is not eligible for the exception.

Reason

It was the customer's incorrect estimation of their income that caused the overpayment.

5

Scenario

Customer was partnered for the whole CCS year with an estimate of $50,000 for themselves and $50,000 for their partner – a combined income of $100,000.

Customer separated on 5 July 2020, before reconciliation of CCS.

Customer contacts and queries reconciliation debt.

Reconciliation occurs and customer's actual income was $55,000 and partner's income was $62,000 – combined income of $117,000.

Treatment

Customer is not eligible for the exception.

Reason

Customer should have been aware of changes to income during the year they were partnered and updated the estimate accordingly to reduce the likelihood of a debt.

6

Scenario

Customer was partnered at the start of the financial year with an estimate of $40,000 for themselves and $60,000 for their partner.

Due to a domestic violence situation that was occurring throughout the relationship, customer separates on 2 February 2020.

Reconciliation occurs and customer's actual income was $40,000 and ex-partner's income was $110,000.

Customer was not aware of their partner's financial situation during the period they were partnered.

Treatment

Exception can be applied only for the partnered period where there is an applicable debt.

Reason

Customer stated they were unaware their ex-partner was going to earn more than the estimate that was provided when they were partnered.

7

Scenario

Customer was partnered at the start of the financial with an estimate of $60,000 for themselves and $70,000 for their partner – a combined income of $130,000.

Customer separated on 5 March 2020.

Reconciliation occurs and customer's actual income was $60,000 and partner's income was $110,000 (as the ex-partner received a large payout after 5 March 2020), resulting in a combined income of $170,000.

Customer contacts and queries reconciliation debt.

As the payout to the ex-partner was made after the separation, the customer was unable to factor it in to the estimate.

Treatment

Exception can be applied only for the partnered period where there is an applicable debt.

Reason

Customer would have been unaware of the change in circumstance that their ex-partner was going to earn more than the estimate that was provided when they were partnered.

8

Scenario

Customer was partnered at the start of the financial with an estimate of $45,000 for themselves and $80,000 for their partner – a combined income of $125,000.

Customer separated on 5 January 2020.

Reconciliation occurs and customer's actual income was $45,000 and partner's income was $130,000 (as the ex-partner commenced a job with a higher paying salary on 1 February 2020), resulting in a combined income of $175,000.

Customer contacts and queries reconciliation debt.

As the ex-partner commenced a new higher paying job after the separation, the customer was unable to factor it in to the estimate.

Treatment

Exception can be applied only for the partnered period where there is an applicable debt.

Reason

Customer would have been unaware of the ex-partner commencing a new job with a higher paying salary after the separation.
