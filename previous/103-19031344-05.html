Unincorporated entities

Examples of unincorporated entities

Examples

Example 1

The Department of Social Services is an entity that is not incorporated, it is also not an unincorporated association rather it is a non-corporate Commonwealth entity and part of the legal entity that is the Commonwealth of Australia. See information about Commonwealth entities.

Example 2

The St Aloysius Catholic College in Tasmania is an Other Unincorporated Entity but is a part of the Roman Catholic Trust Corporation of the Archdiocese of Hobart, which is a body corporate. It is therefore not an unincorporated association but simply an entity that is not incorporated because it belongs to a larger entity. See, Other entities, Religious organisations.

Example 3

Aboriginal Corporations incorporated under the Corporations (Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander) Act 2006 (CATSI Act) will be marked as an Other Unincorporated Entity on Australian Business Number (ABN) Lookup. However, if the Business name includes the phrase Aboriginal Corporation it is likely to be incorporated under the CATSI Act. This can be confirmed by searching for the business on the ORIC website. See Resources for a link. For further information see Companies.
