This page contains information relating to the zone office and Services Australia Canberra responsibilities for the evacuation and recovery centres.

Evacuation and recovery centres - zone office

Table 1.

Item

Description

1

Zone office role in the evacuation centre + Read more ...

Responsibilities of the zone office in the evacuation/recovery centre include:

  • clearly define the key contacts and their roles at the centre and ensure the Team Leader are aware of these
  • provide support to the Team Leader and their team positioned at the Centre
  • act as a coordination point for:
    • Policy / program information
    • Human Resource (HR) issues and
    • Employee Assistance Program (EAP) providers
  • organise the local Information Technology (IT)/LAN Support Officer (LSO) and Applications service desk to be on call to assist with IT issues at the Centre
  • provide updates to the affected Service Leader and the Emergency Management Section in Services Australia Canberra
  • attend meetings and phone hook ups where required
  • monitor the need for / request Telephone Lodgement and/or Emergency Stripping of the payment files if required

2

Communication coordinator + Read more ...

Responsibilities of the zone office in the coordination of communication:

  • coordinate and organise any requests for home visits if required
  • manage local media enquiries
  • coordinate delivery of required resources to the evacuation/recovery centre
  • contribute to discussions on the decision to 'pull out' of the centre
  • be prepared to manage misinformation that will occur
  • provide information to the Emergency Management Section in Services Australia Canberra for inclusion in updates to internal and external stakeholders
  • assist with enquiries from Local / State / Federal Government Ministers and Members of Parliament (MP)

3

Policy/procedure matters + Read more ...

Responsibilities of the zone office in Policy/procedure matters:

  • provide updates to policy or claiming procedures immediately to the team working in the Centre
  • keep a record of all policy updates and when they were disseminated

4

Payment information + Read more ...

Responsibilities of the zone office in relation to payment information:

  • work with the Emergency Management Section to provide clearly defined eligibility criteria for Crisis Payment (CrP), Special Benefit (SpB), Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP), ex-gratia payments and other options for current / existing customers. The Emergency Management Section will liaise with other Services Australia Canberra teams

5

Human Resources (HR) / People Management + Read more ...

Responsibilities of the zone office in relation to HR and People Management include:

  • ensure any overtime and meal allowances are paid to staff in the next available pay period
  • keep a record of staff who worked - for recognition of their efforts
  • promote the Employee Assistance Program (EAP) and organise debrief for teams after the event

The Resources page contains links to the Safety Reference Centre on the Employee Assistance Program (EAP) and to the People Reference Centre for details on overtime and meal allowances.

6

Staff scheduling + Read more ...

Responsibilities of the zone office in relation to staff scheduling include:

  • involve the social worker team
  • involve a Health and Safety practitioner
  • coordinate a back-up roster (in case of staff absences)
  • maintain a roster of staff attending the Centre noting the employment conditions listed in the certified agreement. For example:
    • staff cannot work longer than five hours without a 30 minute meal break. This period would need to be covered by other staff
    • staff must have at least eight consecutive hours off duty plus reasonable travelling time between the time they cease duty and the time they next commence duty

The Resources page contains links to the People Reference Centre for further details of employment conditions.

7

Administrative functions + Read more ...

Further administrative responsibilities of the zone office include:

  • may need to 'volunteer' services to other agencies / services
  • provide business cards for all key managers
  • maintain Administrative files for official record of events and actions taken
  • identify the costs associated with the setting up and running of the Centre and record the resource expenditure via the expenses spreadsheet
  • diligent recording of all decisions made
  • where appropriate, take photos of staff working at the Centre for future use (intranet site etc.)

8

Role of the Zone Emergency Contact Officer + Read more ...

Responsibilities of the Zone Emergency Contact Officer include:

  • liaise with the local/state contact regarding the evacuation/recovery centre location and the agency set up
  • refer to State Sub Recovery Plans
  • coordinate Team Leader / Staff / social worker selection for work in the evacuation Centre
  • coordinate resource allocation / delivery including emergency equipment (such as laptops) and Internet Remote Access Service (iRAS) with zone office
  • coordinate delivery of information to evacuation/recovery centre staff
  • coordinate dissemination of information to teams
  • provide the Emergency Management Section with details of key contacts for the event

9

Role of social workers + Read more ...

The role of social workers is to:

  • help disaster victims to access relevant income assistance
  • provide personal support, counselling and/or referral to other available services

The majority of the work undertaken by social workers is at the local or zone level, and in the immediate recovery phase.

See The Social Work Service.

Role of Services Australia Canberra + Read more ...

Services Australia Canberra will provide:

  • immediate support to the zone:
    • Services Australia Canberra will contact Service Leader Zone Emergency Contact/Team Leader on site to offer assistance with anything that may be required
    • Emergency Management will provide access to all relevant disaster information such as policies, claim forms, fact sheets, etc.
  • follow-up support to zone:
    • Services Australia Canberra will contact Service Leader Zone Emergency Contact/Team Leader on site to offer any further assistance and discuss recovery staff wellbeing
  • follow-up support to social work services
  • support to the Processing Centre:
    • Services Australia Canberra will contact the Processing Centre to ensure they are receiving claims and required information from evacuation/ recovery centre (such as Proof of Identity (POI) and other evidence)
  • coordination of lines of communication between Processing Centres and zone
  • situational reporting and briefings for Minister and executive staff
