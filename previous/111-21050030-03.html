Process Direct

Using Earnings and reporting workflow tool in Process Direct

This table describes the steps to enter Carer Payment (CP) details to the Earnings and reporting workflow tool in Process Direct.

Item

Description

1

Messages screen in the Earnings and Reporting workflow in Process Direct + Read more ...

During the Earnings and Reporting workflow in Process Direct, the following prompt will display on the Messages screen if the customer is currently receiving CP:

‘Carer Payment customers can work, study, train or volunteer for 25 hours per week (including travel time). If the customer wishes to relinquish payment, do not continue in this workflow.’

This message is provided to ensure staff are aware of the special processing that will be involved with CP customers. It also flags that if the customer is starting work and has arranged ongoing alternate care that this should be dealt with as a cancellation of payment outside the Earnings and Reporting workflow.

  • For customers who want to relinquish their Carer Payment, go to Cancellation of Carer Payment (CP). Procedure ends here
  • For customers who have ongoing employment, where the total of all carer activities will exceed 25 hours per week, on an ongoing basis, see Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities. Procedure ends here

Staff cannot update self-employment or hours for carers using the Earnings and Reporting workflow. Advise CP customers who start or change their self-employment income to use the Manage Income and Assets online service or issue a Profit and Loss Statement (SU580). If their self-employment income is already recorded, staff can update their activity hours directly via the Carer Payment Activity Summary (CPAS) / Carer Payment Activity Details (CPAD) screens. For more information, see Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities. Procedure ends here.

* Employment income paid in a foreign currency must be coded on the Foreign Income Paid Details (FEIP) screen outside the workflow. FEIP coding does not create a Paid employment activity and a workaround is required. Activity details for foreign employment income should be recorded using another activity type on CPAS, such as Voluntary work. Procedure ends here.

All CP recipients with employment income must have their income recorded as detailed in Recording and correcting employment income details.

Note: for CP (XWP) customers, this message is not displayed as they are exempt from the 25 hour rule and CP activity reporting requirements There is no restriction on the number of hours CP (XWP) carers can be away from their care receiver. Activity hours are not collected for these customers in the workflow or via self service channels. Procedure ends here for CP (XWP) customers.

2

Updating Carer Payment Activity Details in Process Direct + Read more ...

For customers receiving CP, the Carer Payment Activity Summary (CPAS) table will automatically appear.

If the customer is working or undertaking activities:

  • select the pencil for the relevant activity type the customer is undertaking. The Edit Carer Payment Activity Summary pop-up will appear:
    • enter the name of the Employer/Organisation/Institution
    • select how many weeks need to be recorded (1 or 2)
    • enter the hours and minutes undertaken. If hours vary each week, code each week separately
    • Event Date: Only one event date is required if the carer is working or undertaking an activity involving regular hours, and should generally be the Entitlement Period Start Day (EPSD). A second event date can be recorded and will default to the start date of the next week in the entitlement period (EPSD + 7). Date can also be up to 30 days in the future to cease the activity hours if not continuing
      Note: for customers reporting late, where there is a need to update the number of hours of their carer activity as a result of updated employment income, the system defaults the event date the current EPSD. Ensure the event dates in the prompt are amended to the EPSD for the outstanding period
    • select Yes or No as to whether the carer still provided care while working. If Yes, record how care was met. Select Save and Next
  • the Carer Payment Activity table will display. To view the updated activity, select the relevant twisty for the Carer Payment Activity Details (CPAD) to display. Ensure details are correct and update using the pencil if required. Select Next
  • if total activity hours exceed 25 in a week and ‘care is not met’, a message will display about ongoing entitlement. See Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities. If customer is eligible to use respite:
    • select Yes or No as to whether the customer would like to use respite or if the Carer Payment Activity Details (CPAD) need updating. Select Next
    • if Yes to updating CPAD, the Carer Payment Activity table will present. Select the pencil to make updates and Save. Select Next
    • if activity hours are still more than 25 hours where ‘care is not met’, the Temporary Cessation of care summary (TCC) table will display. To view absences taken, select the twisty for the care receiver. Select Next
      • the Temporary Absence Details (ABSN) table will display. Select to code the temporary absence if eligible and Save. See Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities. Select Next and finalise the activity

3

Care is met + Read more ...

If care continued to be provided and the employment income activity is the carer's only activity, regardless of the hours worked the customer can continue to receive CP subject to the income test.

For help, see Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities.

A prompt will display to advise the customer and ensure they are aware of the need to advise any change in care arrangements.

4

Carer's activity does not exceed 25 hours per week + Read more ...

If care is not met for any activities, but the total of all current activities is 25 hours or less per week, the CP can continue, as total activities do not exceed 25 hours per week.

For help, see Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities.

5

Carer's activity is more than 25 hours per week + Read more ...

If the total Carer activities are more than 25 hours per week, an assessment of ongoing eligibility will need to be undertaken.

Finalise the activity once the Earnings (EAPP) and CPAD/CPAS screens have been updated.

If more action is needed, a manual follow-up (MFU) activity will be created. This is actioned by a Service Officer with appropriate Carers' knowledge.

The MFU activity will appear on the Activity List (AL) screen. It will contain the text 'Review Carer Payment eligibility immediately as total hours of employment/training/study plus travel time exceeds 25 hrs per week'.

More information is included in the activity as follows:

  • cancel Carer Payment (CP) if the customer has exceeded 25 hours per week and respite has not been used; and customer has no remaining Working Credit balance
  • if CP is to be cancelled, the customer’s Working Credit balance has not yet reached zero and customer has a monthly (MTE) exception that is not met, place the customer on statement reporting
  • a future cancellation may be required after Working Credit balance has depleted to zero - for customers under Age Pension age
  • use the Temporary Cessation of Care workflow to code respite where activities are were going to exceed 25 hours on a one-off basis

Respite cannot be used if the carer is involved in activities greater than 25 hours per week on an ongoing basis. See Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities. Procedure ends here.

If more than one activity exists, the system totals all the activities based on the event dates. For example, training or study in addition to the work.

Carers are allowed to use respite options on a one-off basis if:

  • work, study, voluntary work and/or training (including total travel time) exceeds 25 hours per week on a temporary basis, and
  • care is continuing

If the customer chooses:

  • not to use respite, the Service Officer will be prompted to check details with the carer to see if they are still eligible for payment
  • to use respite, the Service Officer should leave the activity hours as a record and Temporary Cessation of Care screens will be displayed to code this and then finalise the Earnings and Reporting workflow

For help, see Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities.

Customer First

Using Earnings and reporting workflow tool in Customer First

This table describes the steps to enter Carer Payment (CP) details to the Earnings and reporting workflow tool in Customer First.

Item

Description

1

Employment income Summary Messaging + Read more ...

During the Earnings and Reporting workflow in Customer First, the following prompt will display on the Employment income Summary screen if the customer is currently receiving CP.

'CP customers can work, study, train or volunteer for 25 hours per week (including total travelling time). If the customer wishes to relinquish payment, the Earnings and Reporting workflow will create an activity, which will launch the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow.

This message is provided to ensure staff are aware of the special processing that will be involved with CP customers. It also flags that if the customer is starting work and has arranged ongoing alternate care that this should be dealt with as a cancellation of payment outside the Earnings and Reporting workflow.

Note: staff cannot update self-employment income or hours for carers using the Earnings and Reporting workflow. Advise CP customers who start or change their self-employment income to use the Manage Income and Assets online service or issue a Profit and Loss Statement (SU580). If their self-employment income is already recorded, staff can update their activity hours directly via the Carer Payment Activity Summary (CPAS) / Carer Payment Activity Details (CPAD) screens. For more information, see Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities.

If a Service Officer indicates that respite was used, a link is provided to the Temporary Cessation of Care workflow.

Note: for CP (XWP) customers, this message is not displayed as they are exempt from the 25 hour rule and CP activity reporting requirements There is no restriction on the number of hours CP (XWP) carers can be away from their care receiver. Activity hours are not collected for these customers in the workflow or via self service channels.

Employment income received by all CP customers must still be recorded. See Recording and correcting employment income details.

Procedure ends here for CP (XWP) customers.

2

Employment income Update Messaging in Customer First + Read more ...

A mandatory Carer Payment Details question set will be displayed for each employer on the Employment Income Update screen in the Earnings and Reporting workflow when a new occurrence of employment income for a CP customer has been inserted. Note: the Carer Payment Details question set is not displayed for CP (XWP) customers. Their activity hours are not assessed.

Have the employment activity hours changed since 'DDMONYYYY' (currently 'hh/mins' per week)?

This question is to determine if the carer's employment activity hours have changed since last reported. The answer is defaulted to 'Yes'. Changing to a 'No' response will inhibit the rest of the question set. Where a customer is continuing to work regular hours, there is no need to update the hours.

For customers reporting late, where there is a need to update the number of hours of their carer activity because of updated employment income, the system defaults the event date to the current Entitlement Period Start Day (EPSD). Ensure the event dates in the prompt are amended to the EPSD for the outstanding period.

How many weeks do you need to record (if the hours worked each week are different during the fortnight, select 2 weeks)?

This question allows a Service Officer to record more than one set of hours for each week that is worked. Where the hours worked are different for each week of the fortnight, the Service Officer should select '2' and two separate event dates and details can be recorded.

How many hours were worked per week (include total travel time)?

The details will be recorded as hours and minutes through the Carer Payment Details question set.

To display the Carer Payment Details question set when recording employment income:

  • save the response for the IOP or LOP details
  • select Yes at ‘Are there more employment details to declare for this employer?’
  • at Type of Employment Details Update, select Carer Activity (CPAD) only

The Carer Payment Details question set will appear automatically for customers/partners receiving CP who are eligible for a continuous income frequency of MTE or where Service Officers specifically select the ‘Carer Activity (CPAD) only’ option.

If the carer has started working but has not yet been paid, after adding the new employer details, select Carer Activity (CPAD) only as the Type of Employment Details Update.

What is the Event Date?

  • Carer Payment Details (CPAS): only one event date is required if the carer is working regular hours, and should generally be the Entitlement Period Start Day (EPSD). A second event date can be recorded and will default to the start date of the next week in the entitlement period (EPSD+7). Date can also be up to 30 days in the future to cease the hours worked if not continuing their employment

If the customer works different hours each week of the fortnight, record the hours separately for each week.

The hours and minutes should include total travel time to and from work each week. Details of this may need to be recorded in the Notes facility if an adverse decision on eligibility for payment is to be made, i.e. when time spent in employment and travelling to and from work (by itself or in combination with other activities) exceeds 25 hours per week.

Was the carer still providing care while working? For example, working from home and still able to care, Yes/No/Uncertain?

  • Yes, Care is met. This is where the care receiver is with the customer during employment and the care is still provided by the carer, consistent with the care receiver's care needs.
    For example, a carer who is working from home. A mandatory question 'Please describe how care is continuing to be able to be undertaken.' will be displayed. It will contain a free text field up to 70 characters, to enable description of how care is still being met (for example, working from home), which will be included on the DOC
  • No, Care is not met. This is where the care receiver is not with the customer during their working hours and travelling time or the care receiver is with the carer but insufficient care or supervision is provided
  • Uncertain, Care is unknown (CF/CR only). This is where it is not known whether the care receiver is with the carer during this activity, or if so, whether sufficient care is being provided. Note: it is best practice to immediately assess whether care was provided during the activity. Only answer 'uncertain' if it is not possible to clarify with the carer

3

Outcomes screen display + Read more ...

Based on the answers to the above questions and including any other activities recorded on CPAS, a range of outcomes will be advised. This will vary between advising the customer of information or urgent follow-up action.

A prompt will appear showing what activities exist on the record, with the hours for each of the following:

  • Employment income
  • Any additional employment income
  • Studying
  • Training
  • Voluntary work

The workflow will allow the hours for any of these activities to be changed or zeroed out and an event date to be entered. New activities can also be added in the workflow for any of the above activity types. However,:

  • self-employment details cannot be added using the workflow but can be changed or zeroed. Self-employment details can only be added when self-employment income has been coded on the Business Detail (BUS) screen
  • employment income paid in a foreign currency must be coded on the Foreign Employment Income Paid Details (FEIP) screen outside the workflow. FEIP coding does not create a Paid employment activity and a workaround is required – activity details for foreign employment income should be recorded using another activity type such as Voluntary work.

For partnered customers, receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and/or Child Care Subsidy (CCS), the Employment Income Outcomes screen will display the current Family Estimate in certain instances. For more information, go to Item 4 below.

4

Partnered customers receiving FTB and/or CCS + Read more ...

The Employment Income Outcomes screen will display the current Families estimate, the date last updated and the current payment choice(s), such as 'continuous adjustment,' which is mandatory for FTB, where employment income is updated under the following conditions:

  • neither the customer nor a partner has updated the Families estimate on that day or within this contact
  • the customer or partner is making a personal contact (source is personal or phone)

If the partner is making contact, this must be consistent with Partner Permitted To Enquire (PPE). If the partner contacting Centrelink is not the FTB customer, PPE must exist. If the customer's partner is PPE and contacts to provide a new estimate and could reasonably be expected to do so, the estimate can be updated.

If the above situation occurs, a message box and a prompt requiring a Service Officer response will appear:

'The customer's Families estimate may need updating if income has changed since the last estimate was provided - it will need to include employment income, income support payment and other income. Does the customer agree to update Family Estimate now? YES/NO'

  • Yes, the Earnings and Reporting workflow will launch the Family Income and Choices workflow after it has completed
  • No, advise the customer to update their Family Estimate at the earliest possible opportunity

The Families estimate will be displayed as an overall total, and will flag whether any components exist other than normal taxable income such as net rental income or non-taxable pension with a value greater than zero. If this information is available, then it will enable a user to access this component detail, for example, via a twisty.

5

Care is met + Read more ...

If care continued to be provided and the employment income activity is the carer's only activity, regardless of the hours worked the customer can continue to receive CP subject to the income test.

For help, see Carer Payment (CP) carer undertakes paid employment, self employment, voluntary work, education or training activities.

A prompt will display to advise the customer and ensure they are aware of the need to advise any change in care arrangements.

6

Carer's activity does not exceed 25 hours per week + Read more ...

If care is either unknown or not met for any activities, but the total of all current activities is 25 hours or less per week, the CP can continue, as total activities do not exceed 25 hours per week.

For help, see Carer Payment (CP) carer undertakes paid employment, self employment, voluntary work, education or training activities.

A prompt will display to advise the customer that the rate of CP is subject to an income test and they must notify of any change of care arrangements or increased working hours.

7

Carer's activity is more than 25 hours per week + Read more ...

If the total current activity is more than 25 hours per week, an immediate assessment of ongoing eligibility will need to be undertaken. The Earnings and Reporting workflow will allow the Service Officer to link to the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow.

A prompt will include details of another activity, if there is one on the Carer Payment Activity Summary (CPAS) screen, that is:

  • included in the assessment of the number of hours of activity, including:
    • any other work
    • study
    • training, or
    • voluntary work

The CPAS screen is included as a screen within the optional actions menu within the Earnings and Reporting workflow.

If more than one activity exists, the system totals all the activities based on the event dates. For example, training or study in addition to the work.

If the carer activity hours are changed, the follow-up action and prompt will not display. For example, the activity hours reduced to 25 or less per week.

Any event that exceeds the 25 hours per week, will take precedence in the Outcomes display, if care is:

  • unknown, or
  • not met

Carers are allowed to use respite options on a one-off basis if:

  • work, study, voluntary work and/or training (including total travel time) exceeds 25 hours per week, and
  • care is continuing

If the customer chooses:

  • not to use respite, the Service Officer will be prompted to check details with the carer to see if they are still eligible for payment
  • to use respite, the Service Officer should leave the activity hours as a record and Temporary Cessation of Care screens will be displayed to code this and then finalise the Earnings and Reporting workflow
  • finalise the activity once the Earnings and CPAD/CPAS screens have been updated

For help, see Carer Payment (CP) carer undertakes paid employment, self employment, voluntary work, education or training activities per week.

8

Consequential processing + Read more ...

If further action is required, an activity will be created to be actioned by a Service Officer with appropriate Carers' knowledge.

The activity will appear on the Activity List (AL) screen and will contain the text 'Review Carer Payment eligibility immediately as total hours of employment/training/study plus travel time exceeds 25 hrs per week'.

More information is included in the activity as follows:

  • Cancel CP if customer has exceeded 25 hours per week and respite has not been used and customer has no remaining Working Credit balance
  • If CP is to be cancelled, Working Credit balance has not yet reached zero and customer has and an MTE exception is not met, place customer on statement reporting
  • A future cancellation may be required after Working Credit balance has depleted to zero - for customers under Age Pension age
  • A link to the Temporary Cessation of Care workflow to code respite where activities were going to exceed 25 hours on a one-off basis

Discuss the use of respite in accordance with Carer Payment (CP) carer undertakes paid employment, self employment, voluntary work, education or training activities.

This activity should be actioned immediately and while the customer is present.
