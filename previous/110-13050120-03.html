Initiation process for Department of Home Affairs (DoHA) Interventions

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Locate the intervention + Read more ...

To locate the intervention use the National Search (IRNS) or Review Enquiry (IRRE) screen and code the following fields:

  • Review Region: MDC
  • Review Type: IMM
  • Review Reason: CCE - Carer/Care Cessation

Interventions will have status of REG (registered) and interventions in progress will have a status of STA (started).

Examining the matched data

Information about the compliance trigger is obtained from the customer's (carer)/care receiver's record or by reviewing the:

  • Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen on the carer/caree's record
  • Immigration Advised Child Movements (CHRSIM) screen on the child record via the Child Selection (CHS) screen first

2

Determine Scope period + Read more ...

The Scope period will be:

Scope start date:

  • 1 January of the most recent departure date in the current calendar year unless the most recent departure falls in the previous calendar year
  • The most recent departure date will be as per the RSIM screen on the customer and/or care receiver records or CHRSIM screen on the Child care receiver's record

Scope end date:

  • Date Paid To (DPT) on the customer's (carer) record

3

Other compliance intervention in progress? + Read more ...

Check the Review Summary (IRRS) screen to see if there is another intervention with status STA.

Contact the CO responsible for the other intervention before deciding to continue or cancel the intervention.

Is there another intervention in progress and the Department of Home Affairs intervention is to be cancelled?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No,
    • customer is non-current (cancelled), go to Step 4
    • customer is current, go to Step 5

4

For non-current (cancelled) customers + Read more ...

If the customers and or care receivers departure date is after the customers Date Paid To (DPT), there is no need to initiate the intervention:

Check the RSIM and or CHRISM screens for the customers and or care receivers departure date against the information recorded on the customers Payment Summary (PS) screen

Was the customer or care receiver's departure date after the customers DPT?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Initial checks/compare customer and/or care receiver record with match information + Read more ...

Before initiating the intervention, compare the travel data on the customer and/or care receivers Centrelink record to determine if there is a discrepancy.

Check the customer's record to determine:

  • who has travelled
  • periods when the customer and care receiver travelled without each other
  • if the departure was advised
    • what details about the travel were provided, i.e. date of departure, date of return, reasons for the travel
    • was the portability script used to record the absence from Australia
  • travel has exceeded 63 days
  • travel is continuing and respite has not been recorded, or
  • travel has ended and respite has not been recorded

The following screens can be used to help decide the periods when the customer and care receiver travelled without each other and periods of respite used:

  • Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen. This will provide details of the customers travel overseas
  • Immigration Advised Child Movements (CHRSIM) screen. This will provide details of the child's travel overseas. Note: select children from the Child Selection (CHS) screen first
  • Benefit Status line/Service Reason Summary (SRS) screen for payment type
  • Pension Status History (PNSH) screen for date of grant
  • Care Receiver Summary (CRS) screen or Link Summary (LS) screen to determine care receiver/giver
  • Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen for both customers. This will determine which customer has travelled overseas
  • Check for any coded respite days by selecting:
  • Care Receiver on the CRS screen
  • Absence Details on care receiver to determine any respite days already coded

Record all travel periods and any other known respite periods in the intervention DOC, this will assist in the conversation when contact with the customer has been made.

Should the Department of Home Affairs intervention be initiated?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Initiate intervention + Read more ...

Customer contact is initiated by an outbound call, or by sending a request to contact letter.

Initiate the IRS intervention:

  • review Reason CCE:
  • attempt to contact the customer via an outbound call:
    • if contact was successful:
      - over the phone, change the Review Method to PHO on the IRMR screen, go to Step 2 in Table 2
      - annotate DOC with details of attempt to contact the customer
    • if contact was not successful:
      - initiate the CCE intervention using the Immigration script

The Immigration script will:

  • send the request to contact letter(s) Q640:
    • if the customer is in receipt of both CP and CA, the Q640 letter must state both payments to enable suspension/cancellation of payments under the correct legislation
  • DOC the record and update the IRS intervention and place it on hold for 21 days awaiting customer contact

Nominee:

If the customer has a Correspondence Nominee (NOC) a letter will also need to be sent to the nominee, see Disclosing information to a nominee.

Note: if the CCE intervention is in relation to the Care Receiver, CO is to manually change the letter before printing and sending, see Text to input below. This can be done after the Immigration script has been finalised.

Text to input in the 'Free Text' field:

'The person you are caring for departed Australia on 'Day Month Year'

Procedure ends here until customer contacts or 21 days has been reached, see Table 2.

7

Cancel intervention + Read more ...

After analysing the customer's record, if not proceeding with the intervention as it is not relevant or already actioned, consult the Quality Development Officer (QDO).

If the QDO supports the decision to cancel the intervention, manually cancel the IRS review:

  • select the intervention with a 'C' from the IRRS screen
  • key IAP (Information Already Provided) in the Cancel Reason field
  • update the Intervention DOC with the reason for cancelling the intervention

Procedure ends here.

2

Respite education message + Read more ...

Before discussing the travel details and caring situation, CO are to provide the customer with some general information about Carer Payment and Carer Allowance and the respite/portability provisions available to them.

Suggested dialogue:

'The purpose of today's call is to provide you with some information regarding Carer Payment and Carer Allowance and to review your caring situation.

There are specific criteria to be eligible for Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance. The common eligibility criteria of these payments is that constant care and attention is being provided on a daily basis.

We understand that providing care is very demanding and carers will need to have a temporary break from the caring situation. We call this type of break or absence from care 'respite'.

A carer can take up to 63 days respite in each calendar year for taken a break from the caring situation. These days may be used for any purpose including overseas travel for either the carer or the care receiver.

If the carer is travelling overseas, there are specific portability rules around how long CP and/or CA can be paid whilst a carer is overseas taking into account the use of the allowable respite days.

CP and/or CA payments can continue to be paid if all 63 days are used within a calendar year. If a carer decides to have further breaks from care exceeding 63 days in a calendar year, they will not be eligible for CP and/or CA and their payments may be cancelled.

If an absence from care is or becomes permanent, this means that care is no longer being provided and CP and/or CA will cease to be payable.

I will provide you with more detail in regards to this as we go through and check your caring situation'.

For more information refer to the following Operational Blueprint files:

  • Temporary Cessation of Care (respite) provisions for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA)
  • Carer Allowance (CA) and Carer Payment (CP) interaction of portability and temporary cessation of care (respite) rules
  • Carer Payment (CP)
  • Carer Allowance (CA)

Go to step 3.

3

Discuss travel details + Read more ...

Cross check the customer and care receiver's information against the payment record.

Check:

  • who has travelled:
    • have they returned from overseas - refer to the RSIM screen on the customer/caree's record and or the CHRSIM screen on the child record via the CHS screen first. Check for departure and return dates
  • the current travel details with the customer:
    • as well as any previous travel/respite that may have occurred whilst the customer was current on Carer Payment within the scope period
  • any non-travel related respite they may have accessed but failed to advise of within the scope period

Get as much information as possible from the customer over the phone to assess their eligibility for payments, see suggested questions below.

Discuss travel periods and caring circumstances, see below suggested questions:

When taking information verbally from a customer over the phone, refer to Verbal updates for things to consider.

  • Please confirm who you are caring for
  • Please confirm the period of travel for yourself and/or care receiver
  • If customer or the care receiver has not returned from overseas, what is the date of their intended return?
  • What was the reason for travel?
  • If the care receiver was not travelling with the customer, who was caring for the care receiver during the customer's absence?
  • If the customer and or care receiver have returned from overseas:
    • when did the caring resume?
    • was there any other non-related respite days used since the return date? If so what were the dates/period of this respite?

If considering an extension to respite there must be a 'special reason' to help with a determination, see below suggested questions:

  • Are there any 'special reasons' that need to be taken into account?
  • When did you/care receiver leave Australia?
  • Why did you/care receiver leave Australia?
  • What were your/care receiver's intentions in regards to returning to Australia? Did you/care receiver have a planned date of return? Why was this not booked when you left Australia? What attempts have you/care receiver made to return to Australia?
  • Where are you or care receiver currently located?
  • Who are you or care receiver travelling with?
  • When do you or care receiver intend to return to Australia?
  • Have you/care receiver registered with Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) or local consulate for repatriation to Australia?

If the customer advises they or care receiver were unable to return to Australia, see below suggested questions:

  • There are no flights available, how do they know this? Who advised them of this?
  • Flights keep getting cancelled or rescheduled, can they describe what actions they have taken to book/rebook flights, which airlines, method of contact used i.e.: email/phone calls to travel agent for themselves or on behalf of the care receiver, name of the travel agent etc.
  • They have not been able to get on a repatriation flight, what type of notification was provided? For example, email, phone calls, who provided this notification?
  • They are in quarantine, if so which quarantine facility are they in?
  • How long do they expect to stay there, or how long did they stay there?
  • Does the customer have any documented evidence they could provide?

Thoroughly document all verbal information in the intervention DOC with the responses provided.

Does the customer dispute the travel data?

  • No,
    • extension to respite being considered, go to Step 5
    • no extension to respite being considered, go to Step 6

5

Extension to respite + Read more ...

If the customer exceeds the total allowable 63 days for temporary cessation of care (TCC) (respite), CP and/or CA may continue for more than 63 days if there is a 'special reason'.

To determine if a customer is eligible for an extension beyond 63 days respite CO will need to consider the following:

  • the reason for ceasing care would need to be outside the control of the customer and consistent with the need for, and provision of, personal care and attention, a 'special reason', and
  • the customer would resume providing home care at the required level after a definite period. See Temporary cessation of care (respite) for more than 63 days
  • whether portability provisions affect the Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance. See Carer Allowance (CA) and Carer Payment (CP) interaction of portability and temporary cessation of care (respite) rules

If related to COVID-19 in the 2020 calendar year, see the Resources page, COVID-19 extension to respite in the calendar year 2020 for guidance.

'Special reasons' is determined by an APS5/APS6. CO will need to consult with their Quality Development Officer (QDO) and/or Team Manager (TM) to get approval to apply an extension. Following consultation, refer to Temporary cessation of care (respite) for more than 63 days for coding requirements from Step 2 in Table 1.

Note: if at any time the QDO/Team Manager is unable to make a determination as to whether a respite extension should be applied, refer the case to the Customer Compliance Help Desk via a CADO Service Desk enquiry. See details below:

Place the intervention on Hold (HLD), see Table 1, Item 5 Service Desk escalation:

  • on the IRMR screen add the keyword 'DHASD'
  • CO must assign the work item to themselves via Work Optimiser
  • make sure the details of the conversation with the customer are recorded in the intervention DOC

Go to Step 6.

6

Notification obligations and customer service offer + Read more ...

It is important to remind the customer of their notification obligations.

This relates to how long a customer has to notify a change in their circumstances.

Recipients of CP and/or CA must observe the general 14-day notification provisions. That is, all customers generally have 14 days to notify Services Australia of a change in their circumstances. Exceptions may apply, for more information see Carer notification issues.

Failure to notify of a change of circumstances may result in an overpayment, cancellation or suspension of CP and/or CA.

Assess information + Read more ...

Before applying the temporary cessation of care provisions (respite) COs will need to be satisfied that:

  • the cessation of care is temporary, and
  • each case considered on its own merits, refer to

If the customer has ongoing entitlement to CP/CA do not cancel the payments. Any debt will need to be worked out manually for the historical period.

Debt Calculation

Use the Payment Summary Tool to help calculate any debt resulting from the above assessment.

Debts would normally occur from the 64th day of respite exceeding. This is dependent on the assessment that has been determined, that is:

  • has an extension been applied
  • do the portability rules affect the allowable days, the departure date and any return date
  • other respite days used outside of travel

If unsure on how to code historical respite days and/or calculate a debt, check with the Quality Development Officer.

Advise outcome + Read more ...

5

Determine debt and calculate + Read more ...

The debt should be raised while the customer is on the phone. There may be scenarios where the debt cannot be raised and finalised while the customer is on the phone. This should be in limited scenarios, see Table 5 for more information.

All debts that can be calculated via the Centrelink system and explained using ADEX should be calculated that way. Only use MultiCal if the Centrelink system calculation is not possible or the outcome is not correct.

Check the Assessment Result (AR) screen to make sure the system has correctly calculated any potential overpayment, for example:

  • Debt schedule - do the debt dates/date of event (DOV) match the period?
  • Have the updates/coding been entered correctly?
  • Check the following to make sure the updates are as expected:
    • Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS)
    • Income Bank (NIB)
    • Employment Income Assessment (EIA)
  • Can a reassessment be made using the Benefit Action (BA) screen?
    • Use REA in relevant host system, or
    • RCR in PEN/PGA (for cancelled PEN and PGA records) and leave Reason blank

Note: check if Review of Assessment (ROA) coding is needed to generate allowable arrears. Do this when corrected income is less than declared, but the system did not generate arrears for those periods/fortnights.

If an ADEX debt calculation:

  • can be undertaken, the overpayment must be calculated with the customer over the phone where possible
  • is not possible, a MultiCal debt calculation may be required

For MultiCal debt calculation, get all information from the customer over the phone where possible to enable a debt calculation.

The CO is responsible for progressing the MultiCal activity. If they cannot action, their site is responsible for progressing the activity. The Team Leader will need to reassign the activity to another CO with the required skill set.

Issuing a Q765 advice

A Q765 must be sent unless the intervention outcome:

  • has been advised over the phone
  • is related to a member of couple trigger and a decision letter has been issued
  • resulted in an adverse decision, for example:
    • payment/card remains suspended/cancelled
    • payment was varied
    • debt advice has been issued

A Q765 can be issued if requested by the customer.

Referral to the Policy Engagement Team

After completing the intervention, a referral to the Policy Engagement Team may be required if the customer has lost qualification outside the scope period.

Send a CADO Service Desk enquiry to the Policy Team. They will investigate the referral and engage with the appropriate teams, Case Selection or Intelligence and Investigation Branch.

2

Issue Pre Adverse Decision Letter + Read more ...

A manual Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter must be issued to the customer where there have been three unsuccessful call attempts made to the customer to advise them of the outcome.

The Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter provides the customer with an opportunity to respond and provide further information to a potential debt that they may have to repay based on information we have received.

See the Resources page for the manual Q888 template. Please make sure the correct letter template is used, based on the customer's circumstances.

Add a keyword to the IRS intervention. In Customer Record:

  • Go to Review Summary (IRRS) screen
  • 'T' the intervention (this will display the Manage Reviews (IRMR) screen)
  • In the Workgroup: field enter relevant workgroup
  • In the Position: field enter CO logon
  • Add the keyword 'Q888' - if no contact from customer and sending an initial Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter
  • Add the keyword: 'DBTHLD' - customer contact was successful either from a phone call or the customer responding to a Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter
  • Press [Enter]
  • Allocate the IRS intervention to your workgroup and position

CO must assign the work item to themselves via Work Optimiser and DOC the record. The Resources page contains a link to the documenting requirements template.

Place the intervention on HLD for 28 days pending contact from the customer. To place the intervention on hold, navigate to the IRCS screen and code the following:

  • Method type: key 'MAI'
  • Date Contacted: key 'T' for today
  • Press [Enter], when the Confirm Y/N box appears, key 'Y'
  • Press [Enter] and the IRCD screen will be displayed

On IRCD screen code the following:

  • Expected response date: 28 days in the future
  • Person contacted: enter the name of the person who will receive the letter
  • Letter code: ADVERS
  • Address: enter the address where the letter will be sent
  • Text: Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter issued

Note: if the customer has not responded to the Q888 Pre Adverse Decision Letter within 28 days, no additional phone calls are required. The debt can be raised using the available information.
