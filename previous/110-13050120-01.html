For staff conducting Department of Home Affairs Interventions.

This document outlines the process for initiating and contacting the customer (carer) for DoHA Interventions. It includes discussion, action to take if the customer does not contact, and how to assess and finalise these interventions.

Aim

The aim of these interventions is to address the ongoing eligibility of customer in receipt of Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance when the carer or care receiver travels overseas without their qualifying carer or care receiver.

The purpose of the DoHA compliance interventions is to:

  • create early, effective and high quality engagement with customers
  • educate customers about their Centrelink reporting obligations to make sure the customer is getting the correct payment and stop and/or minimise customers incurring debts

This will be done by:

  • giving an education message to the customer about caring responsibilities and respite/portability provisions available to them
  • assessing the customer's entitlement
  • explaining the outcome to the customer

Data matching

Data matching is used to match all Australian and New Zealand citizens, Australian permanent resident visa holders and holders of qualifying temporary visas who leave Australia against Centrelink customer records.

The objective is to promote greater compliance with eligibility conditions for Centrelink.

Centrelink customer information is matched against DoHA records using surname, forename, middle initial, date of birth and gender as match keys.

Compliance triggers

Carer/Care Cessation (CCE)

This intervention identifies when a carer or care receiver travels overseas for more than 30 days. The review is loaded on the carer's record and information relating to the travel is collected from the Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen. The carer may not meet the eligibility criteria to receive Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA) as respite may be exceeded if absence is temporary.

Initiation

Customer Compliance staff initiate these interventions after initial checks to make sure the information in the data match matches the customer's record.

Customer contact

The customer will be contacted by an outbound call, or via an inbound call when responding to a Request to Contact letter (Q640).

The Q640 letter requires the customer to contact within 21 days on the provided phone number to discuss their current circumstances.

If the customer fails to respond to the Q640 letter, their payment may be suspended or cancelled.

Verbal updates

When the customer responds, trusting verbal information provided by the customer is appropriate unless discussions indicate that documented verification is required.

Completing the Intervention

Once a discussion has been held with the customer and all the relevant information has been gathered, the customer's eligibility for payment must be assessed while the customer is on the phone. This is to avoid the need to make additional phone calls to the customer and issuing the Pre Adverse Decision letter.

If the update results in a cancellation and/or debt this must be fully explained to the customer. Advise the customer the reason for the cancellation/debt and how and what information was used e.g. travel dates, respite days.

Check that all activities completed as part of the Intervention are correctly attributed to the review in IRS.

Finalise the intervention after:

  • the conversation with the customer
  • delivering the education message, and
  • calculating any changes to the customer's entitlement

If the customer has had their payments cancelled due to Failure to Contact (FRC), the match travel period/s will not be applied to their record. A discussion has not be held with the customer around their circumstances to determine if there has been a loss of eligibility to CP and/or CA.

A display on access DOC will be generated for future contact with the customer to discuss their circumstances. The intervention will then be finalised.

Pre Adverse Decision Letter

There may be scenarios where the debt cannot be raised and finalised while the customer is on the phone. The customer must be given the opportunity to provide further information before the debt is finalised. If the customer is not on the phone, three call attempts are required to contact the customer, if unsuccessful a Pre Adverse Decision letter is issued.

Where the customer does not contact in response to the Pre-Adverse decision letter or fails to give more information the outcome can be finalised.
