Examples of ROI transcriptions and the care, storage and retrieval of evidence

Item

Description

1

Requesting transcriptions + Read more ...

A ROI recording must be sent for transcription if the case is to be referred for prosecution.

Four providers are contracted to provide transcription services for the agency:

  • Epiq Australia Pty Ltd
  • VIQ Solutions
  • APT Transcriptions Pty Ltd
  • Legal Transcripts Pty Ltd

Each Intelligence and Investigations Branch team has been allocated a provider to use.

Complete the transcript order form. Include the investigation team's positional email address (not the investigator's email address).

Requests can be:

  • Standard request (five business days from receipt), or
  • Urgent request (same day if delivered before 10:00am or one business day from receipt). Urgent requests should be the exception as there is an additional fee for this service

Send the transcription order form and a copy of the CD or DVD to the appropriate transcription office by registered post, or deliver personally if nearby.

Retain a copy of the order form on the investigation file, and email a copy to FCID Procurement. (FCID.BUSINESS.OPS@Servicesaustralia.gov.au).

2

Collecting transcribed records + Read more ...

Transcription providers are registered with PGP. Once the transcription provider has prepared the transcription information they will:

  • prepare an electronic copy of the transcript and email to the team’s positional mailbox

When the transcript is received the investigator is to listen to the recording and compare this to the transcription.

Transcription providers will retain a copy of the transcript for 30 days to allow for any corrections to be made.

3

Corrections to transcription + Read more ...

If the transcription requires correction, an email can be sent to the transcription provider quoting their reference number, the paragraph number and the required corrections.

Once the transcript is correct, a printed copy is to be sent to the customer within seven days using Registered Post.

4

Storage, care and retrieval of ROI evidence + Read more ...

Upon return to the office CDs or DVDs must be logged into the Exhibit Management Database (EMD) as an exhibit by the Exhibit Registrar and stored in class B containers within the secure Exhibit Room.

Electronic exhibits must be sealed in an anti-static bag. Wrap the bag in Services Australia anti-tamper tape, around the exhibit once horizontally then around the exhibit again, this time vertically to make a cross.

Do not use adhesive labels on discs. When writing on discs use a water based felt-tip permanent marker. Do not use pens, pencils and other types of markers (the solvent in some markers can affect the metal layer on the disc making it unusable).

5

Storage of ROI at completion of the investigation + Read more ...

When electronic evidence is created it should be returned to the Records Management Unit at the completion of the investigation:

CDs and DVDs containing information or evidence such as surveillance, CCTV footage, ROIs should be stored in hard plastic cases to minimise the risk of damage.

These CD/DVDs must be barcoded and security marked prior to sending to the RMU upon completion of the investigation and/or prosecution action. The Senior Investigator/Approver of Investigations is required to complete the electronic evidence barcode request template and email it to the RMU Townsville positional mailbox.

The RMU will register the items into HPECM and forward four security marked and unique barcode stickers. Upon receipt, the Senior Investigator/Approver of Investigations is to ensure that each item is packaged in its original case and the barcode and security marking stickers placed on both the media item and the cover (do not seal the plastic case with the label).

The items should then be sent in an envelope to:

RMU Victoria

71 Pound Road West

Dandenong South, VIC 3175.

When sending the items, only one interview should be included per envelope. The customer’s identifier should be written on the front of the envelope and a barcode and security classified sticker applied. Any extra copies made of electronic evidence may be destroyed via the secure digital media disposal service (‘red bin’) at completion of the case. Any barcode stickers that are not used should be disposed of in a security bin.

Once the items have been sent, an email should be sent to the records management helpdesk advising that the material has been sent.

All electronic evidence stored with RMU will be automatically provided when the relevant investigation file is requested. In accordance with the National Archives of Australia - Records Disposal Authority, a record of interview must be retained for 10 years after investigation has ceased or action completed, whichever is the later.
