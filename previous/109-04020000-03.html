Is the customer satisfied with the debt explanation at first contact?

  • Yes:

    • If they have provided more information to allow their debt to be reassessed, record an ACTDOC and refer to the relevant business area. Then go to Step 4 to discuss a pause on recovery

    • If they want to request an explanation of the debt decision, record their request. Then go to Step 4 to discuss a pause on recovery
    • If they want to apply for a formal review of the debt decision, record their application. Then go to Step 4 to discuss a pause on recovery

Discuss pausing recovery on the debt + Read more ...

Ask if the customer if they would like a pause applied to the recovery of the debt until the completion of the reassessment, explanation or review.

Would they like the agency to pause recovery on their debt?

  • Yes:
    • If they have been issued a formal account payable for the debt, go to Step 5
    • If the customer have only been sent an informal account payable notice, go to Step 6
  • No, procedure ends here

5

Customer has been issued a formal account payable + Read more ...

Give the following important information to the customer before recording the pause:

For current customers

If a current customer accepts the pause, tell them:

  • Recovery is paused for 3 months (6 months for compliance interventions)
    Note: if the customer's reassessment, explanation or review is not completed by this time and they want the pause extended, they need to contact to request an extension
  • If a debt still exists, recovery will restart from the earlier of:
    • at the end of the 3 or 6 month pause, or
    • when the reassessment, explanation or review process is completed
  • Automatic withholdings will restart from their next full fortnightly payment. They can renegotiate this based on their financial circumstances
  • They will get a refund of any money already paid if:
    • the outcome of the reassessment, explanation or review is to set aside the debt, and
    • they have no other outstanding debts
  • They will not be eligible for an advance payment while recovery of the debt is paused
  • Debts display as 'recovery on hold' in Money You Owe online service or Express Plus Mobile App while recovery paused
  • When a review is finalised, notification of the outcome will be given in writing

For non-current customers

If a non-current customer accepts the pause, advise them:

  • Recovery is paused for 3 months (6 months for compliance interventions)
    Note: if the customer's reassessment, explanation or review is not completed by this time and they want the pause extended, they need to contact to request an extension
  • If a debt still exists, recovery will restart from the earlier of:
    • at the end of the 3 or 6 month pause period, or
    • when the reassessment, explanation, or review process is completed
  • If a debt still exists and there is no payment arrangement in place:
    • further recovery action will restart immediately once the reassessment, explanation or review is completed
    • this may include referral to an External Collection Agent (ECA) if no finalised date is recorded on the APL system in the last 28 days
  • They will get a refund of any money already paid if:
    • the outcome of the reassessment, explanation or review is to set aside the debt
    • there is no money is owing, and
    • they have no other outstanding debts
  • Debts display as 'recovery on hold' in Money You Owe online service or Express Plus Mobile App while recovery is paused
  • When a review is finalised, notification of the outcome will be given in writing

Go to Step 7.

6

Customer has been issued an informal account payable + Read more ...

Give the following important information to the customer before recording the pause:

For current customers

If a current customer accepts the pause, tell them:

  • The debt will be paused for 3 months (6 months for compliance interventions). Note: if the customer's reassessment, explanation or review is not completed by this time and they want the pause extended, they will need to contact to request an extension
  • the debt due date will be changed to 28 days after the earlier of:
    • at the end of the 3 or 6 month pause period, or
    • when the reassessment, explanation or review is completed
  • If a debt still exists, the customer will be sent a formal account payable letter at the end of the pause period or after the reassessment, explanation or review is completed
    • the letter will include the outstanding debt amount and the new due date
    • the due date is the date by which the customer will need to pay the debt or set up a payment arrangement
  • In addition, the customer will receive a separate written notification of the outcome of the review
  • They will get a refund of any money already paid if:
    • the outcome of the reassessment, explanation or review is to set aside the debt, and
    • they have no other outstanding debts
  • They will not be eligible for an advance payment while recovery of the debt is paused
  • Debts display as 'recovery on hold' in Money You Owe online services or Express Plus Mobile App while recovery is paused

For non-current customers

If a non-current customer accepts the pause, tell them:

  • the debt will be paused for 3 months (6 months for compliance interventions)
  • the debt due date will be changed to 28 days after the earlier of:
    • at the end of the 3 or 6 month pause period, or
    • when the reassessment, explanation or review is completed
  • If a debt still exists, the customer will be sent a formal account payable letter at the end of the 3 or 6 month pause period or after the reassessment, explanation or review is completed
    • the letter will include the outstanding debt amount and the new due date
    • the due date is the date by which the customer will need to pay the debt or set up a payment arrangement
  • In addition, the customer will receive a separate written notification of the outcome of the review
  • They will get a refund of any money already paid if:
    • the outcome of the reassessment, explanation or review is to set aside the debt
    • there is no money owing, and
    • they have no other outstanding debts
  • They will not be eligible for an advance payment while recovery of the debt is paused
  • Debts display as 'recovery on hold' in Money You Owe online services or Express Plus Mobile App while recovery is paused

Process for Debt Recovery Officers - pause recovery

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Check for a request for a reassessment or explanation, or an application for a formal review of the debt decision + Read more ...

Has a request for a reassessment or explanation, or an application for a formal review on the debt decision been recorded?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No,
    • Check if contact with the customer is required
    • If the customer has provided/will provide new information and wants to request a reassessment of the debt decision, create an ACTDOC for the relevant business team
    • If the customer wants to request an explanation or apply for a formal review of the debt decision, see Request for an explanation or application for a formal review
    • Procedure ends here

2

Check the Debt List (OPDL) screen + Read more ...

Is the debt with an External Collection Agent (ECA)?

The debt will have a status of D-Collection Agent:

  • Yes,
    • select the debt on the OPDL screen
    • go to the Collection Agent (OPMC) screen
    • recall the debt with the reason REV (Review)
    • if there is more than 1 debt under review, repeat these steps. Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Check the Arrangement List (OPAL) screen + Read more ...

Does the customer have a payment arrangement that is:

  • Pending (PND)
  • Future (FUT)
  • Current (CUR), or
  • Broken (BKN)?

If:

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Check the Arrangement List (OPAL) screen + Read more ...

If the customer has:

  • no other debts or all debts pending reassessment, explanation or review, cease the arrangement:
    • go to the OPAL screen, select the arrangement
    • enter today's date to cease the arrangement
    • complete this process for all PND, FUT, CUR or BKN arrangements
    • if a garnishee notice is in place to recover the debt, go to Step 5. Otherwise, go to Step 6
  • other debts that are not pending assessment, explanation or review, do not cease the arrangement for those debts. Go to Step 6

5

Garnishee notice + Read more ...

Does the customer have a garnishee notice in place to recover the debt?

  • Yes, transfer an ACTDOC to the Garnishee Team (OPD)
    • use the Garnishee customer service Fast Note
    • include details of the pause on recovery in the ACTDOC
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Apply a pause on recovery + Read more ...

Do not use the Debt Recovery Script.

Note: if a temporary write off (Outcome of Review/Appeal 'ORA'), has already been applied due to a reassessment, explanation or review, offer the customer an extension of the temporary write off if the reassessment, explanation or review is still in progress.

  • Go to the Add Write-Off (OPWOA) screen
  • Apply a temporary write off using the reason code ORA (Outcome of Review/Appeal) for:
    • 6 months for all Compliance Intervention debts, or
    • 3 months for all other debts
  • Go to the Activity Finalisation (OPAF) screen
  • Select 'Y' to finalise the activity
  • Record a DOC using Fast Note
  • Use example DOC text:
    • Summary: Debt Recovery Paused Offered - Accepted
    • Text: The customer accepted the offer for the pause on the recovery of their debt.
    • Debt Id:
    • Debt recalled from collection agent: Y/N

Procedure ends here.
