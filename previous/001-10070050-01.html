This document outlines the policy and processes associated with investigating and making a decision regarding a connection failure under the Job Seeker Compliance Model. Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officers with PFDEC/WNPPD access are responsible for the connection failure decision making process.

Connection failures

A connection failure is generated when a Community Development Program (CDP) job seeker fails to comply with a connection requirement such as:

  • attend an appointment their CDP provider has booked with a third party
  • enter into a Job Plan with their CDP provider, or
  • provide sufficient job search efforts to their CDP provider

Connection failures ensure CDP job seekers comply with their mutual obligation requirements, including job search and entering into a Job Plan with their CDP provider.

Roles and responsibilities of PST-skilled Service Officers

A Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer who conducts the failure investigation is responsible for:

  • confirming the validity of the failure
  • establishing contact with the CDP job seeker when the failure is valid
  • re-engaging the CDP job seeker if appropriate
  • investigating the circumstances that led to the failure
  • providing the CDP job seeker with the opportunity to explain what occurred and gathering information and weighing evidence to establish the facts relevant to the case
  • identifying barriers and personal factors that directly and significantly impact the CDP job seeker's capacity to comply with the requirement
  • recording the investigation appropriately in the Participation Compliance workflow, recording the reason that best summarises the CDP job seeker's explanation for non-compliance with their requirement
  • assessing whether the CDP job seeker had a reasonable excuse for non-compliance
  • assessing whether the CDP job seeker made prior contact to advise of an ability to attend an appointment or any circumstances that prevented this contact from occurring
  • making the decision to apply or reject the connection failure
  • recording the decision in the Participation Compliance workflow
  • when a payment suspension (participation) 'DNA participation appointment' (APA) has been imposed, restoring the payment if:
    • the CDP job seeker has agreed to re-engage
    • it is determined re-engagement is not required
    • the failure is rejected before it is discussed with the CDP job seeker
  • ensuring payment remains suspended or a suspension is imposed when the CDP job seeker refuses to re-engage

Note: a PST-skilled Service Officer must consult with a PST-skilled Social Worker where the job seeker presents with family and domestic violence or has a current Family relationships/Domestic violence circumstance recorded. See Accessing a PST-Skilled social worker.

The investigation of the failure and the failure decision must be documented in the Participation Compliance workflow.
