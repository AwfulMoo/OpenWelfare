International estimates

This table describes the additional processes required to reconcile an estimate of child support income that includes international income amounts.

Step

Action

1

Reconciling an international estimate + Read more ...

Although international estimates are processed the same as domestic cases, several additional processes must be completed:

  • checking residency, go to Step 2
  • calculating the income to be used, go to Step 3
  • converting overseas income into Australian dollars (AUD), go to Step 5

Consult with a Service Support Officer (SSO) or Program Support Manager (PSM) for specialist advice about international cases.

Child support authorities in other countries vary in their ability to:

  • obtain/provide income information about customers
  • deduct child support payments from employers or third parties, or
  • work with Australian Child Support to manage child support cases

It is important to manage customers’ expectations with regard to their child support case if 1 or both parties live overseas. While every effort is made to manage international child support cases accurately and efficiently, there may be limitations that are beyond the control of Services Australia.

2

Check residency + Read more ...

Determine the residency of the customer during the estimate period. It is possible that a customer will be a resident of more than 1 country during a single estimate period. For residency determinations, see Residency decisions for Child Support customers.

3

Identify the income to be used + Read more ...

The following categories of income may be used to reconcile an estimate for an international case:

  • Australian income, for example, check for an Australian Taxation Office (ATO) tax return or income information using the Transaction Searching Facility (TSF)
  • target foreign income, and
  • overseas income

To calculate incomes for an international case, see Income processing for Child Support customers (international parents).

4

Contacting customers with international status + Read more ...

Contact the parent by phone to request information and evidence about their income during the estimate period to determine if the estimate requires reconciliation. For more information on international telephone calls, see Contact with Child Support customers.

The type of evidence that can be required will vary from case to case, but may include:

  • certification from employers such as a group certificate equivalent or payslips
  • a statement from a relevant government department showing payments made to the parent
  • documentation from an accountant or tax agent
  • a copy of a tax assessment issued by the relevant taxation authority in the parent’s country

Note: estimates relate to an Australian financial year for the 12 month period commencing 1 July and ending 30 June.
Financial years in other countries may be for a different period.
For example, the New Zealand financial year is 1 April to 31 March. Reconciling a 2014/15 overseas estimate (1/7/14 - 30/6/15) using the 2014/15 overseas taxable income would result in an incorrect outcome as the income periods do not align.

For information about international time zones and dialling codes see Contacting international customers.

Phone contact unsuccessful

If phone contact is unsuccessful, send a manually generated MX1-1 Request for Client to contact CSA letter with pre-approved text Estimate reconciliation more information required (International and Domestic). See Item 4 in CAL text options - Specific action pre-approved text table of Letters Cuba Process Help.

See Contact with Child Support customers.

5

Convert overseas income into Australian dollars (AUD) + Read more ...

Income earned overseas must be converted to AUD before it can be used in an estimate reconciliation. The same exchange rate for the original estimate must be used for the reconciliation. Refer to the estimate notepad for the exchange rate used for the original estimate.

See Income processing for Child Support customers (international parents) for guidance about converting income.

6

Income details available + Read more ...

Manually load the income information in the Reconciliation Calculator. Ensure Australian income is included as well as the converted overseas income.

Select 'INT' as the 'created reason' in Cuba.

Save the pended reconciling entry.

Document the income information being used. This includes the source, overseas currency amount/s, relevant period/s and the conversion rate to AUD. For example, document the monthly breakdown of NZ income provided by New Zealand Inland Revenue (NZIR) and the conversion to AUD.

Note: estimates relate to an Australian financial year for the 12 month period commencing 1 July and ending 30 June.
Financial years in other countries may be for a different period.
For example, the New Zealand financial year is 1 April to 31 March. Reconciling a 2014/15 overseas estimate (1/7/14 - 30/6/15) using the 2014/15 overseas taxable income would result in an incorrect outcome as the income periods do not align.

If the estimate does not require reconciling, go to Step 7.

If the estimate requires reconciling and phone contact is unsuccessful for customers residing overseas, or if the customer requests information in writing, issue an Interim Reconciliation letter ME4-4.

  • Highlight Reconciling entry on the Client Income window
  • Select the notepad icon document:

Subject: Estimate Reconciliation

Text: yyyy/yy estimate requires reconciliation using determined income.

Reconciliation using a determined Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI).

Examination identified that the determined ATI is higher than the expected ATI.

ME4-4 Letter sent advising of Pended Reconciliation.

  • If customer contacts confirm:
    • pended reconciliation information including deductions
    • YTD

Note: the ME4-4 allows the customer the standard domestic response time of 14 days. For response times, see Contact with Child Support customers.

If reconciliation results in a change to the assessment, go to Step 8.

If no income details are available or there is insufficient information, go to Step 10

Seek help from an SSO if needed.

7

No change to the assessment + Read more ...

  • Highlight Reconciling entry on the Client Income window
  • Select the notepad icon document:

Subject: Reco No Change Decision

Text: yyyy/yy estimate reconciled using a determined income.

Reconciliation using a determined Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI).

Examination identifies reconciliation will result in no change to the assessment.

Estimate set to recon-no change accordingly.

Pended Reconciliation entry deleted.

Legislation:<insert the appropriate legislative reference below>

  • Section 64, Child Support (Assessment) Act 1989 or
  • Section 64AB, Child Support (Assessment) Act 1989

And

  • Section 64AC, Child Support (Assessment) Act 1989, or
  • Section 64AD, Child Support (Assessment) Act 1989'

After adding the document:

  • delete the pended reconciling entry
  • select the relevant estimate in the Client Income window and open
  • set the Reconciliation Status of the estimate to Recon-No Change in the Estimate Income window

8

Change to the assessment + Read more ...

Issue an Interim Reconciliation letter ME4-4 if:

  • phone contact is unsuccessful for customers residing overseas, or
  • the customer requests information in writing

  • Highlight Reconciling entry on the Client Income window
  • Select the notepad icon document:

Subject: Estimate Reconciliation

Text: yyyy/yy estimate requires reconciliation using determined income.

Reconciliation using a determined Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI).

Examination identified that the determined ATI is higher than the expected ATI.

ME4-4 Letter sent advising of Pended Reconciliation.

If customer contacts confirm:

  • pended reconciliation information including deductions
  • YTD

Legislation:<insert the appropriate legislative reference below>

  • Section 64, Child Suppport (Assessment) Act 1989, or
  • Section 64AB, Child Support (Assessment) Act 1989

And:

  • Section 64AC, Child Support (Assessment) Act 1989, or
  • Section 64AD, Child Support (Assessment) Act 1989'

9

Request amended certificate + Read more ...

  • If the case is international and there is a change to the liability, a notice of this change must be sent to the relevant overseas authority. To request an amended certificate be issued for New Zealand:
    • create a NEW ZEALAND INFO REQUESTED intray
    • route the intray to:
      Office: International Hobart
      Stream/Team: MC S003 Cent Auth TM 1
      Position: NZ info Request
    • add a notepad to the estimate stating intray created and routed requesting an amended certificate
  • For all other countries:
    • create an INTERNATIONAL STHOLD FOLLOWUP intray
    • route the intray to MC S003 Cent Auth TM1 – Z Overseas Future Work
    • add a notepad to the estimate stating the intray created and routed requesting an amended certificate.

10

No income details are available + Read more ...

If contact has been unsuccessful there may be an opportunity to request information from the overseas jurisdiction if the customer is a resident of New Zealand during (or part of) the period of the estimate.

Residents of New Zealand

  • For a resident of New Zealand, overseas income information can be requested under the service agreement between Child Support and the Inland Revenue, New Zealand Inland Revenue Child Support (NZIR). Customers need to be on salary and wage for NZIR to provide this information, that is, not self-employed
  • Attempts to contact the customer must be done prior to requesting income information from NZIR. Request the income information required to reconcile the estimate including the year to date (YTD) period. In most cases a monthly breakdown will be required

If the request is for:

  • the monthly breakdown, complete the NZ Income request template, email it to International NZ Administration Team and include a copy to the local team leader
  • a full NZ financial year of income (that is, 01/04/YY - 30/03/YY), see New Zealand Case Management (NZ Out Case) - Requesting an Income from Inland Revenue, New Zealand (NZIR) for Child Support to request the information

Add a notepad to the estimate stating income information has been requested through International and if the customer is a:

  • payer, add the notepad to the Collection Window, see NZ Inc requests – Cuba recording
  • payee, add the notepad to Communication, see NZ Inc requests – Cuba recording

Note: an email will be sent to advise the new income. Enter the income following the instructions in the New Zealand Case Management (NZ Out Case) - Requesting an Income from Inland Revenue, New Zealand (NZIR) for Child Support, Information received from NZIR (Service Officer).

Allow 2 weeks for a response. If no response contact the International NZ Administration Team.

A response will be emailed to the Service Officer and documented:

  • For the payer - in the original Payer Collection window request, including the date NZIR replied
  • For the payee –in the original request in the Communication window, including the date it was received

Copy the response into the estimate reconciliation notepad.

Residents of other countries

No other central authority will provide income information for estimate reconciliations.

If income details are:

  • received, go to Step 6
  • not received or insufficient, go to Step 11

11

Income details not received or insufficient + Read more ...

If there is insufficient income information to determine if the estimate requires reconciliation or not, set the estimate reconciling status to Parked and clearly document the decision.

12

Outcome disputed + Read more ...

If a customer disputes the outcome of an estimate reconciliation, see Outcome disputed table in the Manual reconciliation procedure for child support Estimates (from 1 July 2011)
