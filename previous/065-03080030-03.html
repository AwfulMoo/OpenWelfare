Check if payment has been suspended or cancelled + Read more ...

Review original decision, see review of decision.

2

Payment suspended or cancelled Whereabouts Unknown (WUK) + Read more ...

As long as the customer has continued to be qualified and payable during the entire period of suspension/cancellation, their payment may be restored with full arrears.

This can occur when they contact more than 13 weeks after the decision to suspend or cancel. The customer is taken not to have received advice of the decision if they were not at their known address or the advice was returned.

The date of effect for the favourable decision (restoration) is the date of the original decision to suspend/cancel. See the References page.

Look at each customer's circumstances to determine if arrears are payable:

  • if they could be taken not to have received advice of the decision to suspend/cancel, and
  • if they retained qualification and payability during the entire period

Check contact details are correct before restoring payment. Check postal address, home address, phone numbers, etc. If the contact details before cancellation have been end dated, remove this coding.

If customer was cancelled WUK, but:

  • has not changed address, go to Step 3
  • has changed address or the address was recorded in error, go to Step 4

3

Customer was cancelled 'WUK', but has not changed address + Read more ...

Check if there is any reason to doubt that the customer is living at the address.

Reasons would include:

  • mail has been returned previously from the address
  • suspected member of a couple
  • customer cannot provide a satisfactory explanation of why mail was returned

If this is the first time mail has been returned and the customer can explain why mail was returned, and mail is not likely to be returned again, payments can be restored.

Otherwise, before restoring payment they must provide evidence they still live at the recorded address. This is because further mail to that address is also likely to be returned.

If the customer is in an aged care home:

  • check if the customer has a nominee, or
  • check the customer's RCA Institution Summary (RIS) screen. This may list the name of the aged care home where the customer lives, and
  • contact the nominee or aged care home by phone to confirm the customer is a permanent resident

If the customer does not have a nominee, discuss the benefits of having a nominee arrangement.

Is the customer required to provide information or documentation before payment can be restored?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, see Step 2 in Table 2

4

Customer cancelled 'WUK' and has changed address or the address was recorded in error + Read more ...

Update address and reissue returned correspondence. If eligible for Age Pension, restore payment using the correct date of restoration.

If the customer does not have a nominee, discuss the benefits of having a nominee arrangement.

See Step 2 in Table 2.

5

Suspended payments or payments cancelled after 13 weeks’ suspension + Read more ...

  • Determine if restoration can take place at initial contact
  • Check the customer's Document List (DL) screen and read the DOC regarding the cancellation/suspension
  • Check if the cancellation or suspension code is restorable
  • Age Pension may also be restored if payment was suspended or cancelled correctly, but for a reason that can be restored following necessary customer action
  • If the customer does not have a nominee, discuss the benefits of having a nominee arrangement

Check contact details are correct before restoring payment. Check postal address, home address, phone numbers, etc. If the contact details before suspension/cancellation have been end dated, remove this coding.

As long as the customer has continued to be qualified and was either payable and/or eligible for an employment income nil rate during the entire period of suspension/cancellation, their payment may be restored with full arrears.

Look at each customer's circumstances to determine if arrears are payable:

  • if they could be taken not to have received advice of the decision to suspend/cancel, and
  • if they retained qualification and payability (or an employment income nil rate) during the period

Is there any reason why payment cannot be restored at first contact?

Does the customer first need to provide information or documents, attend an assessment interview, or meet another requirement?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, see Step 2 in Table 2

6

Cancelled payments + Read more ...

If payments have been cancelled for the following reasons:

  • FRC (Failed to reply to correspondence) – Can be restored if the customer has contacted via any channel to query cancellation or supply any documentation (which may be via uploaded documents) within 13 weeks of the notice to cancel
  • E6F or E6P (Earnings preclude for 6 consecutive fortnights and Partner Earnings preclude for 6 consecutive fortnights):
    • Age Pension can be restored if it is payable due to a change in employment income in the entitlement period immediately following the 6th fortnight at nil rate, or an error has been corrected
    • The customer must contact within 13 weeks of being notified of cancellation E6F or E6P
    • If they contact within 13 weeks and cannot supply employment income details for all entitlement periods following the last fortnight at nil rate, go to Step 7
    • If they contact within 13 weeks and can supply employment income details for all entitlement periods following the last fortnight at nil rate, go to Step 3 in Table 2
  • RTA (Returned to Australia) payment can be restored. See Returning to Australia
  • NSR (Not sufficient residence) or NRQ (Not residentially qualified) because residence information was incomplete or incorrect and the error has now been corrected, payment may be restored. See Step 2 in Table 2
  • EXR (Exceeds income/assets thresholds due to foreign exchange rate processing) payment may be restored within a specially set up Manual Follow Up activity. See Cancellation due to foreign exchange rate processing
  • FRP (Failed to report)
    • Payment can be restored if the customer contacts within 13 weeks of being given the cancellation notice, and can supply employment details for all relevant periods. See Step 3 in Table 2
    • If they cannot supply employment details for all relevant periods, go to Step 7

For more information about whether Age Pension can be restored, check the Restoration column for the relevant cancellation reason in Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Age Pension.

If customer contacts after 13 weeks of being given the cancellation notice, unless a favourable determination has been made to restore payment they will need to reclaim Age Pension.

Note: the system will cancel the pension if the customer’s assets (ASS - Assets over limit) or income (INC - Excess income) are too high to qualify for payment. Review of decision procedures apply if a customer’s Age Pension is cancelled and they believe the decision was incorrect based on their circumstances at the time, and they:

  • have not been notified of the decision, or
  • were notified of the decision within the last 13 weeks

If a customer’s Age Pension has been correctly cancelled for these reasons and the customer’s circumstances have changed since cancellation, a re-claim is required regardless of the length of time that has passed. See Claiming Age Pension.

Procedure ends here

7

Further information or action required + Read more ...

Action the following:

  • advise customer of action required before restoration can occur, for example, what documentation is required
  • do not restore payment on initial contact
  • record details on a DOC of advice given to customer
  • if customer contacts about suspension for failing to attend a Random Sample Survey (RSS) interview, refer them to the Random Sample Surveys Officer (RSSO)

See Failure to attend a Random Sample Surveys (RSS) interview.

Procedure ends here until the customer takes the required action.

2

Restore payment + Read more ...

If suspended due to the customer being imprisoned, follow the following steps before restoring:

  • Confirm prison admission and release dates
  • Check Eligibility for Crisis Payment (CrP) for customers released from prison or psychiatric confinement
  • If partnered, update the Marital Status (MS) screen to reflect partnered status correct at date of release

Customers can request restoration at any time. They do not need to wait until their EPED. If cancelled due to whereabouts unknown WUK, as long as they have continued to be qualified and payable during the period of suspension/cancellation, their payment may be restored with full arrears. This may be the case even when they contact more than 13 weeks after the decision to suspend or cancel.

  • If payments are suspended, determine eligibility for Age Pension
  • Update any changes in circumstances, such as change of address
  • Check if partner's payment (if applicable) is also to be restored
  • If the customer's Family Tax Benefit (FTB) cancelled due to Age Pension stopping, restore FTB first if Rent Assistance is paid with the FTB

Restore Age Pension on the Benefit Action (BA) screen. If restoring the partner's record, also code details on their record.

  • Svc Rsn 'Age'
  • Action 'RES' (Restore)
  • Effect Date will default to the effect date of the suspension
  • Source: and DOR: fields before finalising the activity

Go to Step 4.

3

Restoring when cancelled for reason 'FRP' + Read more ...

If the customer is under income management (benefit status INM) and has arrears or lump sum payment entitlement, do not make any changes to their record to allow this payment. Contact the Income Management Team to allow income management to be turned on for the arrears payment before it is made. See Effect on Income Management when payment is restored or re-granted.

If the customer contacts within 13 weeks of date of cancellation and can supply all employment details for the relevant past entitlement periods, Age Pension can be restored with date of effect of date last paid to + 1.

Record all employment details via the Earnings and Reporting workflow.

Note: if the workflow is unavailable, manually update all details on the Employment Income Details (EAN) screen and ensure payments are manually stimulated via the Reporting Results (RR) screen.

Code the BA screen as follows:

  • Svc Rsn 'Age'
  • Action 'RES' (Restore)
  • Effect Date: Press [Enter] and the effect date will default or enter the effect date of the restoration in the Effect Date: field
  • Source: and DOR:
  • press [Enter]

If the customer is income managed under the Voluntary Income Management measure, and the agreement was in place for more than 13 weeks, the Voluntary Income Management Continuation Assessment page is presented. The purpose of this screen is to record whether the customer wishes to continue or terminate their VIM agreement. See Effect on Income Management when payment is restored or re-granted.

4

Are arrears due to be paid on the Assessment Results (AR) screen? + Read more ...

  • Yes, check that arrears are correct
  • No, check if customer is entitled to arrears. If system does not pay any arrears due, follow up arrears payment manually. See How to manually calculate arrears

Record details on a DOC.

In Customer First:

  • A DOC template for restorations can be found using the Fast Note workflow
  • Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > SUS/CAN/RES > RESTORE (Payment type) DOV
