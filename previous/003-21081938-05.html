Geospatial tool - 16-22 November 2021 activation and onwards

To assess if a customer lives or works in a relevant area:

  • Open the Northern Territory Live Feed App
  • Place the address given by the customer in the search box. Residential and employer addresses may need to be checked
  • Select the correct address that populates in the drop down
  • In the Layers menu on the right hand side, for:
    • Relevant periods 1 and 2 for Katherine and Robinson River and homelands, select COVID-19 Disaster Payment – NT – 16-24 November 2021
    • Relevant period 3 and onwards for Katherine, select COVID-19 Disaster Payment – NT – 25 November 2021 and onwards
  • An address must be in the blue shaded area for the customer to be eligible
  • Continue to assess the claim

To assess if a customer lives, works or was present in a relevant area:

  • Open the Northern Territory Live Feed App
  • Place the address given by the customer in the search box. Residential and employer addresses may need to be checked
  • Select the correct address that populates in the drop down

  • For relevant areas in:
    • Greater Darwin the address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible to receive a payment

Scenarios - eligibility - 16-22 November 2021 activation

Table 1

Scenario

Outcome

Unable to attend work due to exemption from mask wearing

The customer works in a café in Katherine as a casual for 25 hours per week and is currently receiving an income support payment.

The customer has an exemption from wearing a mask, however, a state public health order mandates that people working in food and beverage outlets must wear a mask.

Their employer tells the customer that they cannot attend work.

Eligible

The customer will receive $200 for each relevant period for Katherine.

Note: if there was no public health order to mandate mask wearing and this was a condition of employment imposed by the employer, the customer would not be eligible for payment.

As the Northern Territory has reached 70% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, the customer will need to lodge a new claim for each new relevant period.

Customers who lodged a full claim in relevant period 1 can use the abridged claim process for relevant period 2 (and any new relevant periods announced), if their circumstances have not changed.

Customer has been receiving JobSeeker Payment since 21 September 2021.

They normally work 16 hours per week in a local shop in the Robinson River region.

Due to the restricted movement (lockdown), the customer has lost all 16 hours per week.

The customer has reported regular earned income since their first reporting period after 21 September 2021 and meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer will receive $200 for each relevant period for Robinson River and homelands.

As the Northern Territory has reached 70% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, the customer will need to lodge a new claim for each new relevant period

Customers who lodged a full claim in relevant period 1 can use the abridged claim process for relevant period 2 (and any new relevant periods announced), if their circumstances have not changed.

Customer is receiving a zero rate of an income support payment and up until the lockdown was able to earn income working at a local café in Katherine.

The customer then started working delivering take-out food when the lockdown started.

Their income was reduced from $1000 per week to $800 per week as they lost one whole day of work on 16 November 2021.

Their income support has stayed current during this time at a zero rate, as the income has been over the threshold.

Customer resides in an eligible Local Government Area (LGA).

Eligible

The customer will receive $200 for each relevant period for Katherine.

The customer was receiving a zero rate of an income support payment at the time they are claiming. They are eligible for payments for each relevant period they meet they have lost one full day's work.

As the Northern Territory has reached 70% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, the customer will need to lodge a new claim for each new relevant period

Customers who lodged a full claim in relevant period 1 can use the abridged claim process for relevant period 2 (and any new relevant periods announced), if their circumstances have not changed.

Customer is receiving an income support payment and has recorded self-employment income as they run a hobby farm. This income has been reduced because of the restricted movement (lockdown).

Before the lockdown, the customer was receiving $100 per fortnight for their income support payment.

After the lockdown, the customer is making no employment income and has updated their income reporting.

The customer is receiving full rate of their income support payment.

Customer resides in the Robinson River region and meets all other criteria.

Eligible

The customer will receive $200 for each relevant period for Robinson River and homelands.

As the Northern Territory has reached 70% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, the customer will need to lodge a new claim for each new relevant period

Customers who lodged a full claim in relevant period 1 can use the abridged claim process for relevant period 2 (and any new relevant periods announced), if their circumstances have not changed.

Customer is receiving a Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) payment since 5 January 2021. Customer has not been working or declaring earnings from employment.

Customer was due to start work on 16 November 2021. Due to restricted movements (lockdown), they were unable to.

Ineligible

As no employment earnings were declared after 21 September 2021, they are not eligible to receive a payment.

Customer is receiving an eligible DVA payment. The customer declared employment income for five hours in one fortnight in July 2021.

The customer declares that due to the restricted movement (lockdown) they have lost more than 8 hours in the first relevant period.

The customer resides in Katherine and meets all other eligibility requirements.

Eligible

The customer will receive $200 for each relevant period for Katherine.

As the Northern Territory has reached 70% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, the customer will need to lodge a new claim for each new relevant period

Customers who lodged a full claim in relevant period 1 can use the abridged claim process for relevant period 2 (and any new relevant periods announced), if their circumstances have not changed.

Customer is receiving an income support payment while also working part time as an essential worker. Customer lives and works in the Robinson River region.

Customer has 2 children, aged 4 and 7, who ordinarily attend child care while the customer is at work. Due to the lockdown conditions, the customer’s child care centre has closed. The customer is unable to perform their work from home and unable to earn income for the period of the lockdown.

The customer would normally work 7 hour shifts 2 times per week.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer will receive $200 for each relevant period for Robinson River and homelands.

As the Northern Territory has reached 70% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, the customer will need to lodge a new claim for each new relevant period

Customers who lodged a full claim in relevant period 1 can use the abridged claim process for relevant period 2 (and any new relevant periods announced), if their circumstances have not changed.

Customer is not vaccinated against COVID-19 and their employer does not allow them to attend work

The customer is receiving an income support payment and was working 10 hours per week in the Robinson River region. The customer also lives in the same relevant area.

The customer has been told by their employer they cannot attend work, as they are not vaccinated against COVID-19.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer will receive $200 for each relevant period for Robinson River and homelands.

As the Northern Territory has reached 70% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, the customer will need to lodge a new claim for each new relevant period

Customers who lodged a full claim in relevant period 1 can use the abridged claim process for relevant period 2 (and any new relevant periods announced), if their circumstances have not changed.
