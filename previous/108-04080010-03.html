Assessing and coding exempt income

Step

Action

1

Compensation and insurance payments + Read more ...

Compensation for loss of wages is not covered in this procedure. See Treatment of compensation payments.

Compensation and insurance payments for damage to property and personal effects are not treated as income.

If the payment is invested, do not record funds in any savings/investment accounts. Money earned on the investment is not deemed under the Income Test for up to 12 months after the person receives the payment. The exemption from the Income Test deeming rules may be extended past 12 months if the customer can show they meant to spend the compensation or insurance payment on repairs within 12 months, but were not able to do so for reasons beyond their control.

See Exempt lump sums for further information about this.

Exception: If the payment is not invested, but spent for a purpose other than for which it was paid, it may become immediately assessable under the Income and Assets Tests.

Advise customer of outcome.

Procedure ends here.

2

Blocked income + Read more ...

Blocked income is not treated as income.

The term 'blocked income' is used if a customer has severely limited or no access to income they have a legal entitlement to.

If a customer has blocked income not from an overseas source, clarify through local Policy or Helpdesk contacts to ensure correct assessment.

Overseas income

In certain circumstances it may be accepted an overseas pension is not income for social security purposes, if the prospect of receiving the money is so remote the moneys are not 'earned, derived or received for the person's own use or benefit', that is, effectively 'blocked'.

See the References page for a link to the Guide to Social Security Law, section 4.3.6.10, Income from overseas pension. Overseas income is not considered blocked just because the country of payment will not allow funds to be transferred out of that country unless it is unreasonable for the customer to travel to that country, for example, medical reason preventing travel or civil unrest in that country. International Programme must determine if a payment is blocked.

See the Resources page for a link to the International Programme contacts.

Review cases where blocked income is being disregarded regularly. The timing of the review depends on the particular situation.

  • Advise customer of outcome
  • Record details on a DOC or select to add NOTES

Procedure ends here.

5

New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS) + Read more ...

NEIS payments are treated as a direct deduction for the following payments:

  • Age Pension
  • Carer Payment (CP)
  • Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
  • Parenting Payment (Single (PPS) or Partnered (PPP))
  • Partner Allowance (PA)
  • Rehabilitation Allowance
  • Special Benefit (SpB)
  • Widow Allowance (WA)

Recipients of CP, PPS and DSP can elect to remain on their Centrelink payment while participating in NEIS, up to the maximum of 52 weeks, providing all other pension/payment and NEIS eligibility criteria is met.

Effect of NEIS payment on partner's payments

If a DSP or Age Pension customer is receiving NEIS, and their partner is receiving CP (caring for partner or spouse), treat 50% of the payments made to the participant as a direct deduction against both members of the couple.

If their partner is receiving a payment other than CP, the NEIS payment does not affect their payment rate under section 1187(2) and 8(8)(t) of the Social Security Act 1991.

Updating a NEIS payment + Read more ...

In Process Direct:

  • Type Start into Super Key then select Income and Assets Update workflow from Task Selector
  • On Other Government Payments (OGP, OGPS) select to access the Create Other Government Payment options. Update the following fields:
    • Payment Type select EIS - ENTERPRISE/EMPLOYMENT INCENTIVE SCHEMES - DEEWR
    • Reference/Description code the NEIS reference number
    • Start Date code the day the payment commenced
    • Payment Amount code the rate of payment
    • Amount Frequency Code: select the frequency of payment from the dropdown list
    • Change Reason Code only if correcting existing income or coding new income with a date of event more than 17 weeks in the past
  • Select Save
  • Update Receipt Date and Channel. Select Save
  • Select Assess
  • Check the payment outcome on Entitlements (ELD). Select Finish
  • Once NOTES are complete, select Finalise. The transaction will complete and NOTES will replicate to the Document List (DL)
  • Advise customer of the outcome.

In Customer First:

Go to the Other Government Payments (OGP) screen and enter details in the following fields:

  • Payment Type: code 'EIS' for NEIS
  • Ref/Description: code the NEIS reference number
  • Event Date: code the day the payment commenced
  • Amount: code the rate of payment
  • Freq: code the frequency of payment
  • Chg Rsn: code only if correcting existing income or coding new income with a date of event (DOV) more than 17 weeks in the past
  • Source: code the information source
  • DOR: code the date of receipt of this information

Finalise activity and advise customer of the outcome.

Record details on a DOC.

Note: recipients of some income support payments may be entitled to a higher rate of income support than that paid under the NEIS Allowance, for example, JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance principal carers, single with dependent children or over 60 years of age and receiving an income support payment for more than 9 months.

These customers may be eligible to receive a top up of the remainder of their income support payment. Manual coding may be required for these customers. See the workaround to pay partial rate of income support payment entry in New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS).

7

Coding equity or merit based scholarship income + Read more ...

The gross amount of the equity or merit based scholarship is coded on the Other Income (OIN).

If completing in Process Direct type Start into Super Key select Income and Assets Update workflow

  • Lump sum and non-periodic payments:
    • coded over a 12 month period from the date the payment is received
    • code the gross amount of the scholarship using the code 'EMS' in the Type: field
    • the system automatically deducts the exempted portion from the total and applies the Income Test to the remaining amount
  • Fortnightly or periodic payments:
    • Coded over the period which the payment represents from the first day of the assessable period
    • Code 'EMS' in the Type: field and the actual amounts and frequency in the relevant fields
    • Note: when an equity or merit based scholarship is coded as a fortnightly or periodic payment, the exempt income amount is spread over the calendar year. The exempt income is divided by 26 fortnights and this is deducted from the income coded on OIN. The relevant Income Test is then applied to any remaining amounts each fortnight or period
    • Set a review for the appropriate date to cease scholarship assessment. It is important to code a review to remove recorded scholarship income

Review annual payments 12 months after their receipt. Set periodic payments for review in Customer First when they are expected to cease. Record all information regarding customer contact and scholarship on a DOC or NOTE.

Note: scholarships that are not equity or merit based must be coded separately on OIN screen as income type 'OTH'.

Procedure ends here.
