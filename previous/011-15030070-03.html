This page contains information to assist Service Officers with amending a notice of past benefits (NOPB) or notice of charge (NOCH) and to delete valid NOPB - all services taken and issue new Medicare history statement (MHS).

Service Officers must review the References and Resources pages before issuing an amended NOPB or NOCH.

Step

Action

  • Yes, Service Officer has delegation to amend, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Has the delegate approved the amendment?

  • Yes, Service Officer to process amendment, go to Step 3
  • No, do not issue amendment. The Resources page contains information on the evidence required to amend notices and delegate approval required

Procedure ends here

3

Is the case already closed in the Compensation Case Management system (CCMS)? + Read more ...

  • Yes, amended NOCH and amended NOPB (advance payment) - all services taken or issued in accordance with a completed statement by claimant to be processed manually, go to Step 4
  • No:
    • if non advance payment case with a later date of injury advised on the notice of judgment or settlement (NOJ/NOS) that will result in a debt to the compensation payer, process manually, go to Step 4
    • amended NOCH - all services taken or issued in accordance with a completed statement by claimant can be processed in CCMS, see the Amend valid notice of past benefits table to delete and reissue a verified NOCH
    • procedure ends here

4

Calculate new total for verified services + Read more ...

Using the Calculator - Manual refunds located in Tools on the Resources page, calculate the total cost of the identified (ticked) services on the completed statement by claimant:

  • Enter the Work Identification Number (WIN)
  • Enter the line number/s and benefit amounts for service/s ticked on the statement by claimant in the columns provided (all 3 columns can be used for large calculations)
  • Only include service/s ticked from the date of injury or illness (DOI) to the date of judgment or settlement (DOJ/DOS) (including any services ticked on both of these days)
  • Select the set of coloured cells that apply to the case, either:
    • APO for an amended notice of past benefits (advance payment), or
    • DEEMED NOCH for an amended notice of charge
  • Enter the DEEMED NOCH or original NOCH amount and any advance payment amount (if applicable)
  • Amended NOCH shows the verified services total and is auto-calculated from the line number/s and benefit amounts for service/s previously entered into the calculator above
  • Refund or Debt amount is auto-calculated

Note: the calculator does not take any previous payments into account, for example a voluntary recovery. If a voluntary payment is found, adjust the final refund or debt calculations accordingly. Any refund figure that shows as negative is an outstanding debt.

A copy of the calculation spreadsheet is saved to the Service Officers desktop as it must be sent to a Team Leader at Step 6 for a quality control check, go to Step 5.

5

Issue manual letter + Read more ...

Prepare manual letter

Select the appropriate manual letter depending on the case, that is, refund or debt letter and whether it is to be addressed to the claimant or compensation payer. See the Resources page for a link to manual letters.

  • The following manual letters will apply depending on the calculations at Step 4:
    • z2278 - Notice of charge (Individual)
    • z2518 - Notice of charge (Business)
  • View the NOJ/NOS document in CCMS view folder to determine where the refund (if applicable) or debt letter should be sent. An authority to refund the injured person must be on file, otherwise any refund will be returned to the compensation payer

Note: if the injured person is under 18 years of age, the Public Trustee or other Trustee may be authorised to receive the refund on the injured person’s behalf. See Minors and Medicare Compensation Recovery to determine the evidence required before proceeding.

  • if the refund or debt is for the injured person
    • open the z2278 - Notice of charge (Individual) letter
  • if the refund or debt is for the compensation payer
    • open the z2518 - Notice of charge (Business) letter
  • complete the required fields in the letter from the amounts in your calculation spreadsheet, ensuring the customer and claim information is accurate in accordance with the most recent correspondence in the CCMS view folder
  • letter is to be saved to the COMPREC letters folder by the Work Identification Number, claimant’s name and letter code as it must be sent to a Team Leader at Step 6 for a quality control check, go to Step 6

6

Prepare email template + Read more ...

Access the Referring a case to compensation Finance email template on the Resources page, and:

  • complete all fields in the 'Referring a case to compensation Finance’ email template as required. If help is required in completing the template, consult a Programme Support Officer (PSO) or Team Leader
  • change the auto populated email address to the email address recorded under Programme Support Officers (PSO) hotline
  • if funds in SAP have been allocated elsewhere rather than to this WIN:
    • in the email subject line add text 'allocation change also required'
    • in body of email at 'Reason for refund' add 'Funds currently allocated to xxxxxxx' and the reason
  • send the email template to Comp Team Leaders via Programme Support Officers (PSO) and attach
    • claimant or insurer refundable or debt letter, and
    • calculation spreadsheet, go to Step 7

7

Add a case note + Read more ...

If the Case Notes screen has not opened automatically, select the Add notes button from the list on the right hand side of the main CCMS or case enquiry screen

  • The top box shows all notes previously recorded on this case
  • In the blank bottom box, record the actions taken, information requested and from whom and reasons for a decision. Use standard case notes where possible
  • Select Add to save notes. A confirmation box will appear, select 'Yes' to add case note

Note: once the amended notice has been sent, check the case on the Case enquiry screen in CCMS to see if the case has an active status. If the case has an active status, access the Work Identification Number in CCMS and match all images to the closed case.

The Team Leader will perform a quality control check on the amendment and respond accordingly below, go to Step 8.

8

Quality control check + Read more ...

  • The Team Leader will quality control the details in the manual letter and the Service Officers calculation spreadsheet for accuracy
  • If approved, the Team Leader will forward the email to finance to issue the appropriate refund or raise the appropriate debt and will return a copy to the Service Officer. Once the Team Leader’s email is received, the Service Officer must:
    • print 2 copies of the manual letter - post 1 copy to the injured person or compensation payer and send 1 copy to be scanned to the Work Identification Number (WIN) as backlog, and
    • print 1 copy of the calculation spreadsheet and scan to the WIN as backlog
  • If not approved, the Team Leader will return the email to the Service Officer for any corrections. Once the corrections have been made, the Service Officer must repeat Steps 6 and 7 above

The Team Leader will perform a further quality control check on the amended manual letter and amended calculations spreadsheet for accuracy and then take the appropriate actions per above.
