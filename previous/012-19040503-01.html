This document outlines the eligibility and registration requirements for midwives.

On this Page:

Midwives

A midwife is a health professional who offers care to women during their pregnancy, labour and birth, and during the post delivery period. They also care for the newborn through to 6 weeks of age, including assisting with breastfeeding.

Not all midwives are eligible for Medicare. The Nursing and Midwifery Board of Australia deem a midwife as eligible, based on their qualifications and clinical experience.

Medicare eligibility and registration

To be eligible for a provider number or a prescriber number:

  • the applicant must have current registration to practise with the Nursing and Midwifery Board of Australia. This board is supported by the Australian Health Practitioners Regulation Agency (Ahpra)
  • the Profession field on the registration certificate must state 'Midwife'

The registration record must state in the 'Endorsement' section:

  • 'An endorsed midwife qualified to prescribe schedule 2, 3, 4 and 8 medicines and to provide associated services required for midwifery practice in accordance with relevant state and territory legislation', or
  • 'Endorsed as qualified to prescribe schedule 2, 3, 4 and 8 medicines required for midwifery practice across pregnancy, labour, birth and postnatal care, in accordance with relevant State and Territory legislation'

Evidence of registration may be:

  • a copy of the Ahpra registration certificate submitted with an initial provider number application
  • an existing 'shell' record in the Provider Directory System (PDS) where registration details are prepopulated
  • Ahpra Information window (if available), or
  • Ahpra website search result

Registration is recorded on the provider stem in PDS. Further confirmation of registration (such as a certificate) is not required if PDS shows a current Australian (AUS) registration in the 'Registration' section of the provider's record.

Recognition of a midwife for Medicare purposes is made by the Chief Executive Medicare (or their delegate) under the Health Insurance (Section 3C Midwife and Nurse Practitioner Services) Determination 2020 made under subsection 3C(1) of the Health Insurance Act 1973. See the References page for a link to the legislation.

Medicare Benefit Schedule (MBS) information

To access Medicare benefits midwives must:

  • have a collaborative arrangement with a medical practitioner, and
    • Services Australia does not need to sight evidence of the arrangement. More information about collaborative arrangements can be found on the Department of Health and Aged Care’s website via a link on the Resources page
  • be working in a private practice (which means they cannot provide Medicare services as an employee of a public hospital or other government funded entity), or
  • be working in a public facility which has been granted an exemption under Subsection 19(2) or 19(5) (including locations listed on COAG Direction) of the Health Insurance Act 1973, see Section 19(2) or 19(5) Directions for more information, and
  • have professional indemnity insurance. Services Australia does not need to sight evidence of this

Midwives working in public practice are eligible for a provider number for the purpose of referring and requesting only. A Service Rule is applied at public practice locations to prevent the claiming of Medicare benefits.

Enquiry escalation

The Provider Registration enquiry line is used for all enquiries relating to the processing of provider registrations.

Enquiries to this area are responded to by a Service Officer (SO). These Service Officers escalate complex enquiries to a Programme Support Officer (PSO) who will liaise with a Programme Officer (PO) if required. Enquiries requiring further legislative or policy advice are escalated to the Provider Eligibility and Accreditation team in National Office.
