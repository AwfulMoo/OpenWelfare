2

Establishment of paternity under the Family Law Act 1975 + Read more ...

The Secretary of AGD may commence paternity proceedings if it is necessary or convenient to do so for the purposes of an international maintenance agreement or arrangement. Proceedings are necessary under the Agreement between the Government of Australia and the Government of the United States of America for the Enforcement of Maintenance (Support) Obligations.

A request for establishment of paternity will typically come from an overseas authority as part of an application for an Australian administrative child support assessment. If an application is refused and the payee resides in the USA, Child Support must make a referral to AGD for the establishment of paternity.

Has an application for assessment from the USA been refused because presumption of parentage (POP) requirements for the parent in Australia are not met?

  • Yes:
    • Refer the application to AGD who will decide whether to initiate the process to establish parentage
    • Go to Step 3
  • No:
    • Procedure ends here
    • The refusal letter will inform the applicant they have the right to make their own application directly to an Australian court for a declaration under section 106A of the Child Support (Assessment) Act 1989 that a person should be assessed in respect of the costs of the child because the person is a parent of the child

3

Referring a paternity matter to AGD + Read more ...

The following information is required for referral of a paternity matter to AGD and will generally be included in the application for assessment from the overseas authority:

  • Mother’s full name, date of birth and address
  • Child’s full name, date of birth, and address
  • Particulars of the putative father (E.g. full name, alias if applicable, date of birth, last known addresses, last known phone numbers)
  • Relevant information concerning the relationship between the mother and putative father (E.g. where the parents met, how long were they in a relationship for, did they cohabit, city that the child conceived)

Child Support will need to confirm whether it has located and attempted to contact the putative father and the outcome of that contact (could not establish contact, contacted but not willing to sign a statutory declaration acknowledging paternity etc.)

The following information, if available, will also assist AGD:

  • Whether the mother was in a sexual relationship with anyone else around the time that the child was conceived
  • Letters or emails between the parties that may help to substantiate the assertion of paternity
  • A photograph of the putative father

Gather the required documents and go to Table 2.

4

UNCRAM application for adult child or spousal maintenance + Read more ...

If a payee does not have an existing overseas adult child maintenance liability and is not eligible for an Australian assessment (e.g. because the child is over 18), they can make an application for establishment of a liability in an Australian court under UNCRAM if they reside in an UNCRAM country. See the Resources page for UNCRAM details on the United Nations Treaty Collection website.

A payee may also apply for spousal maintenance under the Convention.

If Child Support receives an UNCRAM application for adult child support or for spousal maintenance, the application must be referred to AGD. AGD will commence proceedings in an Australian court on behalf of the payee.

The following information is required for referral of an UNCRAM adult child or spousal matter to AGD and will generally be included in the application received, usually from an overseas authority:

  • A cover letter/application document naming the international arrangement under which the application has been forwarded
  • Details of all parties involved
  • All relevant documentation related to the application

Gather the available documents, go to Table 2.

5

Confirmation of an overseas provisional order + Read more ...

An overseas authority or parent may send Child Support a provisional overseas maintenance order made in a court of their jurisdiction for registration or confirmation. Child Support can only register a provisional overseas order made in a reciprocating jurisdiction if an Australian court confirms it. However, an Australian court cannot confirm a provisional order if a child support assessment could properly be made.

With reference to Chapter 2.1.1 of the Child Support Guide, can an application for a child support assessment be properly made?

Note: see the References page for a link to the Child Support Guide.

  • Yes, sent the overseas authority or parent a unique letter advising:
    • Australian courts cannot confirm a provisional order if an application for an Australian child support assessment could properly be made and in this case, an application can be made
    • The provisional order has not been referred to the Australian courts
      Also advise, Child Support can accept:
    • Child Support can accept an application for a child support assessment, which may result in an Australia assessment being made
    • Child Support can accept a request to register an overseas final order, or a request to register an overseas maintenance assessment
  • No:
    • The provisional order and any accompanying letter or documentation must be referred to AGD
    • The AGD will commence confirmation proceedings in an Australian court on the behalf of the parent
    • Gather the available documents
    • Go to Table 2

6

Enforcement of a non-registerable maintenance liability that has been entered into the Child Support Register + Read more ...

A payee of an overseas maintenance liability that is not registrable can apply to Child Support for it to be entered into the Child Support Register. This includes periodic de facto maintenance, lump sum spousal, de facto or child maintenance orders, and non-periodic maintenance liabilities such as insurance, dental costs etc. These are referred to as overseas maintenance entry liabilities.

If Child Support enters these non-registrable liabilities in the Register they become a debt due to the payee (but not to Child Support or the Commonwealth). The payee can ask AGD to initiate enforcement proceedings in an Australian court on their behalf. They may send a request via Child Support.

The following information is required for referral of an overseas maintenance entry liability enforcement matter to AGD and will generally be included in the application received from the payee:

  • A copy of the order, agreement or assessment under which the liability arises
  • A copy of the Child Support letter sent to the payee advising of the liability’s entry into the Child Support Register

Gather the required documents, go to Table 2.

AGD referral back to the Child Support Program Advice Team for further action + Read more ...

AGD may need to refer a matter back to Child Support via the Child Support Program Advice Team.

A referral from AGD typically occurs when they make a decision to proceed with a paternity matter and require power of attorney to apply for a section 106A declaration that a person should be assessed in respect of the costs of the child because the person is a parent of the child.

If the customer does not provide AGD with the power of attorney, AGD will only seek establishment of paternity and not a declaration under section 106A. If a section 106A declaration is not made, child support will only become payable after parentage has been established by the court and a new application for child support has been accepted, rather than from when the initial application for child support was made.

The Child Support Program Advice Team will take appropriate action. If the matter concerns power of attorney for paternity proceedings, the matter will be referred to the relevant Program Support Manager (PSM) asking that AGD power of attorney forms be sent to the payee or the relevant overseas authority for forwarding to the payee.

These requests are emailed to the Site mailbox for Hobart.
