Responding to customers in emergency events + Read more ...

When a customer contacts to advise they are impacted by an emergency event:

  • be mindful of their situation and manage all issues with sensitivity
  • make sure their record and child support assessment:
    • are up to date, and
    • reflect their current circumstances
  • give the customer extra time to provide information or respond to decisions

2

Update customer record in Cuba + Read more ...

Update the customer's record to show the customer is impacted by a disaster. If not already showing, add:

  • a Sensitive issue indicator for Emergency Response. Make sure the most recent disaster is documented on a notepad
  • Special contact details, include the name of the disaster the customer is impacted by and the date
  • a Special Recovery Condition to prevent the agency intercepting their income tax refund

If a customer already has a special indicator on their record:

  • do not overwrite it
  • add a note to the customer's record in the Special Recovery Condition screen, to show that the customer is impacted by [name of disaster], and they have the following indicators:
    • Family Violence
    • Deceased Customers
    • Security Incident
    • Personalised Services sensitive issue indicator
    • Special Contact information

3

Encourage customer to lodge an estimate of income + Read more ...

Staff must:

  • check the Client Income screen
  • ask the customer what their current income amount is. If it is at least 15% less than the adjusted taxable income used in the child support assessment, the customer may be eligible to lodge an estimate

Endorsed estimate deeming policy

Endorsed estimate deeming policies help customers whose income has reduced due to an emergency event.

Does an endorsed estimate deeming policy match the customer’s emergency event?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the start date of the estimate or subsequent estimate is the day the estimate is lodged

4

Apply endorsed estimate deeming policy + Read more ...

If an endorsed estimate deeming policy matches the customer’s emergency event, check the customer also meets all 3 of the following criteria:

  • They live or work in an area affected by the natural disaster, as outlined in the endorsed estimate deeming policy
  • Their income has reduced as result of the natural disaster, and
  • They contact Child Support to lodge an estimate within a reasonable period

Does the customer satisfy all 3 criteria above?

  • Yes, the start date of the customer’s estimate or subsequent estimate is:
    • the date of the event that affected their income, as per the endorsed policy
    • not the date that they lodged their estimate
  • No, the start date of the estimate or subsequent estimate is the day the estimate is lodged

Endorsed estimate deeming policies apply to estimates that:

  • have already been accepted, pended, or
  • are yet to be lodged

5

Financial hardship and referrals + Read more ...

If the customer is in financial hardship:

  • encourage and help the customer to lodge a financial hardship application.
  • refer them to:
    • emergency assistance services
    • payments and services

See Emergency events for other information and payments available.

Table 2

Step

Action

1

When to remove an Emergency Response Indicator + Read more ...

Staff must remove an Emergency Response Indicator, and associated emergency event details, if the customer:

  • has an Emergency Response Indicator with a past review date, and
    • is Private Collect, or
    • is payee, or
    • has a debt of $10 or less

Staff may choose to remove the Emergency Response Indicator, and associated emergency event details, if the customer:

  • has an Emergency Response Indicator with a past review date, and
    • is a payer, and
    • has a debt of more than $10

Staff must review the customer’s circumstances to determine if the indicator must stay. Staff must use their discretion. If the indicator must stay, set the next date of review.

2

How to remove an Emergency Response Indicator + Read more ...

To remove an indicator and associated emergency event details:

  • Go to the Client Profile screen in Cuba:
    • Remove the tick next to the Sensitive Issue 'Emergency Response' (this should remove both the 'Emergency Response' and the Review date)
    • Change Special Contact to None, if applicable
  • Add Notepad with details:
    • Subject: Emergency Response Exit Strategy
    • Text: Emergency Response and Special Contact removed as part of Child Support [insert name of event and date]
    • Save and Exit

Note: customers can access the relevant Estimate Deeming Policies, whether or not they have an Emergency Response Indicator.
