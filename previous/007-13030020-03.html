Employer non-compliance

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Employers who consistently fail to accept their employer determinations + Read more ...

When an employer consistently fails to comply with employer determinations, the PPLEPT may prepare an infringement notice for consideration. E.g., an employer has failed to accept 2 or more times within the past 12 months.

PPLEPT must be confident there have been no circumstances beyond the employer’s control that have contributed to the employer failing these obligations.

Has the employer consistently failed to accept employer determinations?

  • Yes:
    • Transfer the payment responsibilities to Services Australia. This action revokes the employer determination
    • Contact the employee to advise the transfer, details of payments and arrears if applicable
    • DOC the employee's record with details of the revocation of the employer determination
    • Complete the MFU on the employee’s record
    • see Table 3, Step 1
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check if the assisted dispute resolution process is appropriate + Read more ...

Employers who do not accept their employer determination have 14 days to apply for a formal review OR they must formally accept the determination.

Have 14 days or less passed since the employer determination, or is there a formal review or appeal that has not been finalised by the ARO or AAT?

  • Yes, the assisted dispute resolution process cannot start. If there:
    • is not a formal review/appeal in progress, hold the activity until day 15
    • is a formal review/appeal in progress, procedure ends here
  • No, complete the MFU on the employee’s record. and if the employee is:
    • not known to the employer, see Table 1, Step 2 in Role of the employer in Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
    • known to the employer, see Table 2, Step 1

3

Formal review or appeal results in decision being affirmed + Read more ...

If an ARO or the AAT undertakes a review and determines the employer must be the PPL paymaster, the PPLEPT Service Officer must send the employer a written notice (Q999) requiring bank account and employee pay cycle details. Within 14 days of the Q999 being issued, the employer needs to:

  • accept their role, and
  • provide bank account and pay cycle information

If required, see Table 5, Step 1-Employer determination-ARO AAT implementation decision, Delivery of Parental Leave pay (PPL)

Has the employer accepted and responded with bank account and pay cycle details?

  • Yes, employer is compliant. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Employer failed to notify of certain events + Read more ...

If Services Australia has sent a determination to an employer, they are obliged to notify when certain events happen. If an employer does not notify Services Australia, consider issuing an Infringement notice. This applies where employers have not:

  • notified when their business is likely to cease
  • responded to an employer determination
  • provided bank account and pay cycle details
  • complied with a non-compliance notice

Has the employer failed to notify Services Australia of an event?

  • Yes, see Table 3, Step 1
  • No, see Table 2, Step 1

Initiating the assisted dispute resolution process

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Attempt first phone contact with the employer + Read more ...

Make a genuine attempt to contact the employer using:

  • existing contact numbers on the employers record
  • ABN lookup
  • google search
  • white pages, and
  • more recent/alternate contact details for the employer in the employee's claim

Where an attempt to contact the employers authorised contact person was unsuccessful, a 'Request for contact' message can be left asking the employer's authorised contact person to call the agency back.

A 'Request for contact' message can be left with:

  • business reception/front office
  • unidentified voicemail
  • an employee of the company/business when using the contact number provided on the company/business website, or on the Organisation Contact Summary (OSCS) screen

Note: when leaving a 'Request for contact' message, ensure that the relevant ABN is provided in the message. This will assist the employer's authorised contact person when re-contacting the agency. Do not provide the employer's or employee's CRN.

Commence the PPL assisted dispute resolution (ADR) process.

Has the first attempt to contact the employer's authorised contact person been successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Successful employer contact + Read more ...

Discuss the outstanding employer determination for their employee/s:

  • This includes their requirement to register and/or accept their role to deliver PPL as notified in the PPL Employer Determination letter (PPEPP003)
  • For further information to support the registration and acceptance process, see Delivery of Parental Leave Pay (PPL)

Has the employer's authorised contact accepted or agreed to accept the employer determination?

  • Yes, and the employer has:
    • accepted - Document the record. Use Fast Note > Auto Text > Families > Updates > ADR Initial - Employer Determination. Confirm all fields in the text to reflect the circumstances. Delete any information that is not appropriate, such as references to Compliance Notices. Complete the DOC
    • Complete the MFU on the employee's record
    • Procedure ends here
    • agreed to accept, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Issue compliance notice + Read more ...

Prior to issuing a compliance notice to the employer, check the employer’s record for an outstanding AAT review. If there is an outstanding AAT review, hold the compliance action until the AAT review is completed.

  • Document the employee’s record:
    • Use Fast Note > Auto Text > Families > Updates > ADR Commenced PPL Determination
    • Finalise the DOC
    • Complete the MFU
  • On the employer’s record, create the Q840 - Compliance Notice:
    • Go to the Letter Display (LD) screen
    • Select the relevant options, then Next to scroll to the next page. Select continue to confirm each option selected. Note: select Option 1 for the breach - for Section 103
  • Document the employer's record:
    • Use Fast Note > Auto Text > Families > Updates > ADR Initial - Employer Determination. Confirm all fields in the text to reflect the circumstances
    • Hold the DOC for 22 days from the date of issue of the compliance notice

Is a second contact attempt required?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, when the DOC falls due, go to Step 6

4

Attempt second or subsequent phone contact with the employer + Read more ...

Attempt to phone the employer a second time. Use all available contact numbers recorded on the system.

Has phone contact with the employer's authorised contact been successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No: in the employers record:
    • Complete ADR Initial - Employer Determination DOC
    • Use Fast Note > Auto Text > Families > Updates > ADR Second - Employer Determination. Confirm all fields in the text to reflect the circumstances
    • Hold the DOC for 22 days from the date of issue of the compliance notice.
      If allocated the held employer DOC, and 22 days or more have elapsed since compliance notice (Q840) was issued to employer, go to Step 6

5

Successful employer contact + Read more ...

Discuss the outstanding employer determination for their employee/s:

  • This includes their requirement to register and/or accept their role to deliver PLP as notified in the PLP Employer Determination letter (PPEPP003)
  • For further information to support the registration and acceptance process, see Delivery of Parental Leave Pay (PPL)

Has the employer's authorised contact accepted or agreed to accept the employer determination?

  • Yes, and the employer has:
    • accepted - Annotate the ADR Initial - Employer Determination DOC on the employer's record. Complete the DOC. Procedure ends here
    • agreed to accept.- document the employer’s record
      Complete ADR Initial - Employer Determination
      Use Fast Note > Auto Text > Families > Updates > ADR Second - Employer Determination. Confirm all fields in the text to reflect the circumstances
      Hold the DOC for 22 days from the date of issue of the compliance notice. If allocated the held employer DOC, and 22 days or more have elapsed since compliance notice (Q840) was issued to employer, go to Step 6
  • No, document the employers record:
    • Use Fast Note > Auto Text > Families > Updates > ADR Second - Employer Determination. Confirm all fields in the text to reflect the circumstances
    • Hold the DOC for 22 days from the date of issue of the compliance notice.
      If allocated the held employer DOC, and 22 days or more have elapsed since compliance notice (Q840) was issued to employer, go to Step 6

6

Timeframes on employer obligations + Read more ...

Has the employer met their obligations within the agreed timeframe?

  • Yes:
    • annotate the existing Fast Note ADR Second - Employer Determination with details on the outcome
    • Complete the DOC
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, if:
    • the employer has met, but outside the agreed timeframe, go to Step 7
    • the employer has not met the agreed timeframe, go to Step 9

7

Employer’s obligation to accept the employer determination within the timeframe + Read more ...

When an employer accepts the employer determination after the agreed timeframe, determine if an infringement notice should be issued, by considering individual circumstances such as:

  • the employer determination was sent during a Christmas shutdown period, or
  • the business has been impacted by flood or fire, or
  • if there is a history of non-compliance

Do the circumstances warrant the issue of an infringement notice?

  • Yes:
    • annotate the existing ADR Second - Employer Determination DOC with details on the outcome
    • complete the DOC
    • see Table 3, Step 1
  • No:
    • record the outcome by annotating the ADR Second - Employer Determination DOC to say the ADR has been finalised without penalty
    • complete the DOC
    • Procedure ends here

8

Has the employer responded to the Compliance Notice and complied within the 21 days? + Read more ...

  • Yes:
    • annotate and complete ADR Second - Employer Determination DOC with details on the outcome
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Consider whether further action is required + Read more ...

Where the employer has attempted to meet their obligations since the compliance notice was issued, make a genuine attempt to contact the employer's authorised contact to resolve the issue.

This may include where the employer has:

  • provided written acceptance for a business that is not registered
  • registered the business but the acceptance process has not been actioned

Following the contact attempt, has the employer's authorised contact accepted the employer determination?

  • Yes:
    • annotate and complete the existing Fast Note ADR Second - Employer Determination DOC with details on the outcome
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Revoke the paymaster role + Read more ...

Where the employer has failed to accept an employer determination or respond to a compliance notice:

  • revoke the employer determination, and
  • transfer the payment responsibilities to Services Australia.

Select Claim Contact > Paid Parental Leave Claim summary in the employee's record.

  • Go to the Paid Parental Leave Claim summary (P1CS) screen
  • Key 'P' beside the relevant claim to go to the Paid Parental Leave Payment Delivery (P1CP) screen
  • Perform action, 'revoke determination'
  • Revoke Reason, select from the drop down list
  • Date of Effect of the Paid Parental Leave Delivery Role Transfer, Date of effect (DOE) of the PPL delivery transfer
  • Complete the Source: and Receipt Date: fields and select Continue
  • Go to the Payment Instruction Summary (PINS) screen to check that the employee's nominated PPL tax rate is applied. For information on how to update tax deductions, see Managing Australian Taxation Office (ATO) tax deductions
  • Contact the employee to advise the transfer, details of payments and arrears if applicable
  • Document the employee's record:
    • Use Fast Note > Auto Text > Families > Updates > ADR Determination revoked - Employee
    • Complete the DOC. The Resources page contains a user guide with processes for revoking the employer determination and transferring the provider role to Services Australia
  • Document the employer's record:
    • Use Fast Note > Auto Text > Families > Updates > ADR - Failed to comply with Q840
    • Confirm all fields in the text to reflect the circumstances
    • Complete the DOC

Should consideration to issue an infringement notice to the employer be undertaken?

  • Yes, see Table 3, Step 1
  • No, procedure ends here

Infringement notice consideration

Table 3: this table describes the compliance action taken when an employer fails to comply with accepting the employer determination following the assisted dispute resolution for Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

Following a review of an employer determination, certain letters and timeframes must be strictly adhered to by Services Australia. Different processes apply when an employer does not comply with a non-compliance (Q840) notice.

Step

Action

1

Infringement notice consideration + Read more ...

An Infringement Notice (Q841) can only be considered when Services Australia have followed strict procedures.

Staff need to be satisfied that the correct processes have been followed by checking all required action has been taken, Including:

  • Did the employer respond to the acceptance letter?
  • Does the employer's record have evidence of recent contacts about any other case or special circumstances that may have impacted them?
  • Confirm employer has not been impacted by a disaster situation
  • Reconfirm if the employer applied for a formal review of a decision and any communication received regarding this
  • Ensure all required phone contact attempts were made
  • Ensure all required letters and notices were sent
  • Verify if any responses were received
  • Verify contact phone number and mailing address are correct using additional available resources. For example, Yellow Pages, internet, Australian Business Register (ABR)
  • Confirm the PPLEPT Service Support Officer or Team Leader are satisfied the employer contravened one or more of the penalty provisions specified under subsection 159(1) of the Paid Parental Leave Act

Note: the above action must have been followed for previous failures for employers who consistently fail to accept their employer determinations.

Have all procedures been followed correctly?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Further action required + Read more ...

An infringement notice referral cannot be considered when more action is needed.

  • Identify where the process was not followed
  • Initiate action to correct it. For example, if a Q999 was not issued following a review of decision, make sure 12 months will not pass and issue the Q999. Allow the employer to respond
  • Record the reason for the decision
  • Consider if the case is appropriate for an infringement notice. Go to Step 3

3

Timeframe + Read more ...

Check whether 12 months has elapsed since the end date of the 21 day compliance notice.

Has 12 months elapsed?

  • Yes, an infringement notice cannot be considered at all
    • Record the reason for the decision
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Preparing an infringement notice for consideration + Read more ...

The PPLEPT Service Support Officer (SSO) and Team Leader will review the case and consider whether a referral to the PPLEPT Manager is appropriate. Infringements can only be issued within 12 months of the 21 day period specified in the compliance notice issued ends.

If an infringement consideration is appropriate the PPLEPT will:

  • complete an infringement notice consideration template, and
  • provide it to the PPLEPT SSO to review and provide a recommendation to the PPLEPT Team Leader

The PPLEPT Team Leader will then make their recommendation to the PPLEPT Manager.

If the PPLEPT Manager agrees with the recommendation, the case (with all relevant letters and correspondence is referred to the Families and Child Care - Business Process Branch for coordination.

5

Referring the infringement notice consideration to the SES delegate + Read more ...

The Infringement Notice Consideration must be sent using Email Classification Offical:Sensitive to the Families and Child Care - Business Process Branch. Use the subject header: Infringement Notice Consideration - for <Employer's Name>.

The body of the email must contain:

  • Employer’s name, CRN and ABN
  • Employee’s name and CRN
  • Date Compliance Notice (Q840) was issued and due date
  • all attachments of letters and correspondence sent to the employer

The PPL Business Owners will review the case. They will forward the details if required to the Department of Social Services (DSS) for their determination.

6

Senior Executive Services (SES) Delegate referral + Read more ...

If DSS determines the case should be referred for an infringement, a referral letter will be prepared for the SES Delegate.

The SES delegate has discretion for determining if:

  • an infringement notice and penalty is to be issued, or
  • no further action is warranted

Has the SES Delegate decided an infringement notice is to be issued?

  • Yes:
    • the appropriate penalty amount will be confirmed and an invoice raised
    • Once the invoice is received it will provided to the PPLEPT by email
    • The PPLEPT will be advised to generate a Q841 Infringement Notice. (PPLEPT do not issue the Q841 to the employer at this step)
    • To manually create a Q841, see Table 4, Step 1
  • No, see Table 5, Step 2

Finalise Infringement Notice and monitor for response

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Further PPLEPT action + Read more ...

Once the Q841 Infringement Notice has been signed by the SES delegate, the Q841 and invoice will be issued to the employer. A copy of both will be provided to the PPLEPT.

Then:

  • upload the signed Q841 and invoice to the employer's record
  • update the ADR DOC on the employer record and hold the DOC for 36 days (28 days plus 8 days postage) from date infringement letter sent
  • monitor for when the DOC becomes due
  • go to Step 3

2

DSS and/or SES Delegate elects not to issue an Infringement notice + Read more ...

PPLEPT will be notified of DSS’ recommendations and/or SES decision to not issue an infringement notice to the employer.

PPLEPT will create an Infringement Warning (Q852).

  • Go to the Letter Display (LD) screen and enter the letter code Q852 and select continue
  • On the OLA Accept Service Reason screen (OASR) record service reason as PPE, select continue
  • On the LD screen, select the variables, for example Dear Sir/Madam and enter until reaching:
  • PPLSEC: and select the relevant section for non-compliance:
    • section 103 = option 1, or
    • subsection 105(3) = option 2, or
    • subsection 82(2) = option 3,
  • PPLNOT: select the relevant option:
    • Compliance notice sent = option 1, or
    • Compliance notice not sent = option 2
  • Go to the History Summary (HS) screen and select Print central
  • Issue the employer with an Infringement Warning (Q852) letter
  • Finalise ADR DOC on the employer record with full details of the outcome

Procedure ends here.

3

Infringement notice response due + Read more ...

36 days (28 days plus 8 days postage) since the day the infringement notice was sent

Note: PPLEPT should email any overdue cases (36 days has elapsed) to Families and Child Care - Business Process Branch. They will coordinate and determine whether the employer has paid the infringement.

PPLEPT will be advised of the outcome. If the infringement is:

  • paid and not complied, go to Step 4
  • paid and complied, go to Step 5
  • not paid and not complied, go to Step 6
  • not paid and complied, go to Step 7

4

Infringement paid and not complied + Read more ...

  • Annotate the ADR DOC with details
  • Update/Check ADR database details
  • Procedure ends here

5

Infringement paid and complied + Read more ...

Consider if it is appropriate to withdraw the infringement and refund the penalty. For example, did new information become available about circumstances that prevented or made it difficult for the employer to comply or attempt to comply in a timely manner?

Details provided to PPLEPT by the employer about those circumstances should be sent using Email Classification Official:Sensitive to the Families and Child Care - Business Process Branch. Use the subject header: Withdrawal of Infringement/Refund of Penalty consideration - <Employer's Name>.

Delegate decides - to withdraw the infringement

A refund request will be raised. The request will advise if the invoice is to be cancelled or voided, and include the details of the infringement.

The outcome will be notified to the PPLEPT via email. The email will include the decision reason to withdraw the infringement notice. (The PPLEPT will refer to this decision in the Q844 letter).

PPLEPT will:

  • finalise and annotate ADR DOC with details
  • When creating the Q844 check the letter content. Code the following:
    • Employee full name
    • Employee identification number (ID)
    • Claim reference ID
    • Date the infringement notice was sent
    • Free Text paragraph: Reason decision was made to withdraw the infringement (this text will be provide in the notification email to PPLEPT)
    • Select option 1 for Penalty Paid
    • Date employer paid the penalty

Infringement not paid and employer complied + Read more ...

Details provided to the PPLEPT by the employer about those circumstances must be sent to the Families and Child Care - Business Process Branch. Use the subject header: Withdrawal of Infringement consideration - <Employer's Name>.
