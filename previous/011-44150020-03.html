Documents required + Read more ...

The applicant must provide the following:

  • completed Application to copy or transfer from one Medicare card to another form
  • identification for the applicant listed as person one, which can include:
    • current Australian driver's licence
    • current Australian passport
    • Australian birth certificate
    • ImmiCard
    • current foreign passport with an Australian visa
    • Certificate of Identity

If insufficient documentation is provided, attempt to call the applicant to discuss Identity documents required and how to resubmit. If phone contact cannot be made, add processing note in PaNDA and send letter Z0137.

Where the consumer is unable to provide the required identification documents, see Alternative identity proofing requirements for Medicare.

If customer does not fall within this cohort then Service Officers must not refuse a consumers request, and must escalate the issue. See Escalation process for Medicare Eligibility.

Before processing a copy or transfer request

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Before processing a transfer or copy + Read more ...

Before processing a transfer or copy of a person to a new or existing Medicare card, Service Officers must:

  • Verify identification documents in the Document Verification System (DVS) before to processing the application
  • Ensure the person to be transferred/copied onto a card with other people have the same entitlement type. See Medicare cards
  • Transfer or copy the person before actioning any changes on the new card number
  • Ensure if there are any changes to be made to a legal name, acceptable documents supporting the change must be verified in DVS. See Change legal name on Medicare record
  • Identify if the customer has more than one Medicare card number. Their association may need to be ended on the other card. See item 3 to determine if the customer must be contacted
  • Advise the customer if they have registered their child for a My Health Record to review their Medicare Information Settings to ensure these remain appropriate
  • Advise updates made to a Medicare card appear on the digital version next time the Digital wallet is accessed
  • Discuss the family Medicare Safety Net (MSN), go to Step 2

2

Check the Medicare Safety Net (MSN) + Read more ...

When transferring or copying a person from one card to another, the Service Officer must check the MSN and update if required.

If no changes are needed or the customer does not want to update the registration:

  • key a comment in the Personal Comments field in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) and include:
    • <insert customer name> confirmed no changes required to MSN, or
    • <insert customer name> declined to update MSN

Where changes are made:

  • key a comment in the Personal Comments in CDMS and include:
    • <insert customer name> updated MSN

This could include:

  • creating a new MSN family registration
  • copying dependants to a second MSN family registration
  • adding the person to an existing MSN family registration
  • removing the person from an existing MSN family registration

Note: if transferring a person from an existing MSN family that has registered children, the Service Officer must confirm with the person if the children are to be added to the new registration. (Do not remove these children from the existing MSN registered family).

See also: Family registration and confirmation for Medicare Safety Net.

Consent from parents + Read more ...

A child must not be removed from a card without the consent of both parents. Where consent cannot be obtained by both parents, children should be left on the original parents Medicare card and copied onto the transferring parent's card.

Where a request to Transfer child/ren under the age of 15 years is received and the parent provides either of the following documents:

  • an Apprehended Violence Order (AVO) or equivalent
  • Sole Parental Responsibility

The parent must provide a completed Copy/Transfer form

The Resources page details consent requirements when a request is received to copy or transfer a child to a new or existing Medicare card. Consent is obtained by signing the 'Application to copy or transfer from one Medicare card to another' form.

Copy a child to another card

Table 4

Item

Description

1

Copy a child to an existing Medicare card + Read more ...

Where the child has:

  • been previously enrolled with the requesting parent:
    • consent from the requesting parent is required
  • never previously been enrolled with the requesting parent, the requesting parent must provide either:
    • proof of relationship with child for example, birth certificate
    • evidence child is in their care
    • consent from parent on the original card

2

Copy a child to a Medicare card that does not include parent or guardian. + Read more ...

The person requesting the copy must obtain consent from the legal parent/guardian of the child. Where consent cannot be obtained, it is necessary for the requesting person to provide documentation confirming the Australian government child is in their care.

Note: documentation provided must clearly state applicant's and child's full name.

Third party requests to copy a child onto another Medicare card may come from:

  • a government agency (such as Child Protection Agencies) see note below
  • grandparents
  • person other than a parent

Note: where documentary evidence confirms the child is in care of a Child Protection Agency, applications must be actioned by Medicare eligibility and enrolments (Health Services Delivery Division (HSDD) Eligibility team). See Child Protection Agency – Medicare card number requests for children and young people in out-of-home care (OOHC) for more information.

Where a child is in the care of a third party other than a Child Protection Agency (such as a grandparent or a person other than the parent), but consent has not been given by the parent, evidence must be sighted, such as:

  • Court order confirming they have legal guardianship, or
  • Centrelink documents confirming the third party is receiving benefits for the child

Note: do not accept documents where only the first name of the child is notated – Centrelink documents must state the child's full name.

Where the third party cannot provide a court order or acceptable Centrelink document, the third party can provide:

  • confirmation they have enrolled the child in play group, kindergarten or school
  • confirmation the child is in their care from professional members of the community who have regular contact with the family, for example, teachers, police, ministers of religion, doctors or lawyers
  • social worker reports

Note: where the third party claims they are receiving Centrelink benefits for the child, but do not have a Centrelink document with the child's full name, Service Officers can check the third party's Centrelink record for confirmation. This can only be done where the third party has given their permission for their Centrelink record to be accessed for this purpose. The customer's consent must be notated in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS).

Only in extenuating circumstances can a child be copied onto a third card, for example, the child is already enrolled on both parents Medicare card and the child is in care of a third party. Evidence (as outlined above) must be provided. This is irrespective of whether the parent has given consent. See Table 6.

Apprehended Violence Order (AVO)

Table 5

Item

Description

1

When to override the current copy/transfer policy + Read more ...

It may be necessary where an Apprehended Violence Order (AVO) is produced, to override the current copy/transfer policy which states where a child is to be transferred off a parent's Medicare card, authorisation must be received from the other parent.

2

Requests to remove a child from the other parents Medicare card + Read more ...

If a person (for example, parent, or guardian) requests a child under the age of 15 years be removed from the other parent's Medicare card this may be considered.

They must provide documents relating to the following scenarios

  • an Apprehended Violence Order (AVO)
  • Sole Parental Responsibility order

When Service Officers receive advice from a customer in the above situation to remove children from a Medicare card due to Family and Domestic violence, these applications must be escalated. All documentation including a copy of the court order or AVO must be scanned into PaNDA under work type 'Dept of Child Protection' on the MFD. Medicare Enrolment and Eligibility helpdesk will action these applications. They will make the decision on whether the child can be removed from the Medicare card in question.

The decision is based on the information provided.

The requesting applicant (for example, parent, or guardian) is contacted by Medicare Enrolment and Eligibility helpdesk and advised of the outcome, and the documentation is then forwarded to My Health Record Support for a decision on any required action with regards to the child's My Health Record.

3

Medicare Safety Net + Read more ...

Service Officers are to check the persons MSN and notate any required changes on the Medicare Enrolment form. This could include:

  • removing the person and dependants from an existing MSN family registration
  • creating a new MSN family registration, where a new MSN family registration is requested, Service Officers are to include all member names and DOB's

Third cards

Table 6

Item

Description

1

When is a child shown on a third card + Read more ...

The Medicare enrolment system currently allows a child to be shown on 2 Medicare cards but sometimes it is clear a child should be shown on a third card.

Children can be included on a third Medicare card where there are extenuating circumstances.

This could be where a child already enrolled on 2 Medicare cards, has now been placed in Out-Of-Home-Care or in the care of a grandparent or person other than the parent.

These applications must be escalated.

2

Third card requests must be approved + Read more ...

A Medicare Enrolment and Eligibility PSO must approve third card requests. The PSO must assess the request and all documents to determine if they can create a third card.

Before creating a third card check the court order as this will generally be quite specific about the responsibility, if any, of both parents or if no contact is to occur. In these cases, there is no reason for the child to remain on that Medicare card (Office of Legal Counsel has provided this policy).

Step

Action

1

Assess additional identity documents + Read more ...

Customers may come to a service centre with the issued letter and additional identity documents.

FOH Service Officers must locate the previously lodged application in PaNDA. Additional identity documents must:

  • be original, and
  • one of the documents is a photo identity document (on the approved document list)
  • the photo must provide facial verification of the customer

If the Service Officer is:

  • Satisfied with the provided documents, process the enrolment as normal
  • Not satisfied with provided documents

If the Service Officer believes the documents are suspicious, they must lodge a Fraud Tip-Off with the Intelligence and Investigations Branch.
