Assessing eligibility for PES 25% concessional study load

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Obtain study load information + Read more ...

Gather information about the study load. This information can be found in the study details section in the online claim, or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), or from Section B of the Claim for Pensioner Education Supplement (SY004).

Is the student's study load between 25% and 74% of the normal amount of full-time study?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Assess study load + Read more ...

Determine which of the following apply:

  • If the customer's study load is between 75% and 100% of the normal amount of full-time study they are a full-time student for the purposes Pensioner Education Supplement (PES). See Assessing qualifying study for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES). Procedure ends here
  • If the customer's study load is less than 25% of the normal amount of full-time study they do not satisfy the study load requirements and therefore do not qualify for PES. See Step 1 in Table 2

3

Eligible payments + Read more ...

Eligible payments from Services Australia or Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) for 25% concessional study load student are:

  • Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • Carer Payment (CP)
  • Invalidity Service Pension, or
  • Carer Service Pension

Is the customer receiving one of the eligible payments?

  • Yes, these customers may qualify for PES as a 25% concessional study load student. See Step 2 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Principal carers + Read more ...

Payments for principal carers include:

  • Parenting Payment Single (PPS)
  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP) as a single principal carer
  • Special Benefit as a sole parent
  • War Widow/er's Pension
  • Widow Allowance

Does the customer have a dependent child under 16 years of age, and receive one of the above payments?

  • Yes, these customers may qualify for PES as a 25% concessional study load student. See Step 2 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Reduced study load due to disability, illness or injury + Read more ...

Is the customer's study load between 25% and 75% because they have a disability, illness or injury which affects their ability to study full-time?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, they do not qualify as a 25% concessional study load student and therefore do not qualify for PES. See Step 1 Table 2

6

Request evidence + Read more ...

Evidence of the student's disability and the effect on their ability to successfully undertake the normal amount of full-time study for the course is required.

If the customer was undertaking a course in the previous study period and was already assessed as meeting the requirements for the concessional workload due to their medical or psychiatric condition, or intellectual disability, and the customer's circumstances have not changed, a new assessment is not required. See Step 3 in Table 2.

Ask the customer to provide a recent report or medical evidence detailing the nature of the disability (that is, how it affects the students ability to undertake full-time study) from a health professional who has detailed knowledge of their circumstances/condition:

  • If it is a medical condition, a medical practitioner
  • If it is a psychiatric condition, a psychiatrist or
  • If it is an intellectual disability, a psychologist

Record the details of discussion with the student (including the request for medical evidence) on a DOC. Procedure ends here for Service Centre and Smart Centre Call staff.

7

Make decision based on evidence + Read more ...

When evidence is available, make a decision about whether the 25% concessional study load applies.

Does the student's disability mean they are able to study at least 25% of the normal amount of full-time study but not able to study more than 74% of the normal amount of full-time study?

  • Yes:
    • tell the customer they may qualify for PES at a 25% concessional study load. Note: students who qualify for the 25% concessional study load can take up to twice the minimum allowable time to complete their course. This applies to secondary and tertiary students
    • See Assessing satisfactory progress for secondary students for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), or
    • See Assessing satisfactory progress for tertiary students for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
    • See Step 3 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

Coding PES 25% concessional study load

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Customer does not satisfy the study load requirements and therefore does not qualify for PES + Read more ...

  • Tell the customer of their review and appeal rights. See Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process
  • If the customer is claiming, reject their claim for PES
  • If the customer is currently receiving payment, cancel their PES
  • Record details of decision and any discussion with the customer on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

2

Student is qualified for a 25% concessional study load + Read more ...

Complete the following tasks:

  • Tell the student the period for which the 25% concession applies. This will be the duration for which the student has been approved by their institution to study a reduced study load
  • Tell the student that they are required to notify Services Australia of any changes to their study load within 14 days
  • Code the education details on the system:
    • for customers who are studying between 25% and 49% of a full-time load, the Participation Status: field on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen must be coded as 'U50' - 25 to 49%, no concession
    • for customers who are studying between 50% and 74% of a full-time load, the Participation Status: field on the EDC screen must be coded as O50 - 50 to 74% no concession
  • Record details of decision and discussion with customer on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

3

Student is qualified for a 25% concessional study load on the basis of their disability, illness or injury + Read more ...

Complete the following tasks:

  • Tell the student the period for which the 25% concession applies. This will be the duration for which the student has been approved by their institution to study a reduced study load
  • Tell the student that they are required to notify of any changes to their study load within 14 days
  • Code the medical condition on the Medical Conditions Details (MC) screen
  • Code the education details on the system:
    • for customers who are studying between 25% and 49% of a full-time load, the Participation Status: field on the EDC screen must be coded as C25 - 25% Concessional Student
    • for customers who are studying between 50% and 74% of a full-time load, the Participation Status: field on EDC screen must be coded as C50 - 50 to 74% concessional

Record details of decision and discussion with customer on a DOC.
