This is a Family and Domestic Violence Interaction Point. If the customer is with another person, on speaker phone, or already identified with family and domestic violence concerns, continue with the current business conversation. Otherwise, go to the Family and domestic violence procedure to conduct the risk identification and referral process.

Process when separation details are provided

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Determine action required + Read more ...

If:

  • a skilled Service Officer receives a work item relating to:
    • a customer uploading a MOD S (Work Item ZDMS_MODS - MOD S Separation Details Scan). Check the:
      - Activity List (AL) screen to identify the form code on the left had side of the activity
      - Document List (DL) screen to identify if there was a triggering event that created this activity
      - Go to Step 3
    • a customer using the Update relationship details online service, (Work Item ZCOC_SEP_GEN - Separation Details General / ZCOC_SEP_FDV - Separation Details - FDV flag / ZCOC_SEP_SUOR - Separation Details - SUOR), go to Step 3
    • a review activity for MOD S to be returned, go to Step 2
    • a review activity for documents to be returned as part of an online update, go to Step 2
  • a non-skilled Service Officer needs to confirm an online Update relationship details service has been used, see Step 9 in Update relationship details online
  • the customer attends an appointment to discuss their separation, go to Step 6

2

A review activity has matured + Read more ...

The review activity may be for the return of a MOD S form or documents to be returned as part of an online update. If the customer has previously told the agency they have separated, the activity may relate to:

  • a MOD S form and:
    • they have been issued a request for information (verbal or letter) or
    • they previously returned an incomplete MOD S, and a Q004 letter was issued
  • the online service and they have been asked to send more information following an online update. This will be actioned within the activity in Process Direct

Check the customer's record for the return of the requested information on the Document Tools screen.

If the review activity is in relation to:

  • return of MOD S, and the customer has:
    • returned the MOD S, go to Step 3
    • not returned the MOD S, suspend payment
  • return of other forms/information, and the customer has:
    • returned required forms, go to Step 10
    • not returned required form, suspend payment

3

Separation details (MOD S or online update) lodged + Read more ...

When a customer lodges separation details, check if the customer has:

  • identified family and domestic violence safety concerns / indicated they would like to be contacted to discuss their support options specific to their needs. Note: when a customer identifies safety concerns via the Update relationship details online service, the work item type is ZCOC_SEP_FDV - Separation Details - FDV flag
  • actioned all requests for additional forms
  • fully completed the MOD S form (if applicable) (including section 2) and it is clearly readable. See Resources for a link to the MOD S

Note: JobSeeker Payment (JSP) customers with a dependent child using the Update relationship details online service prior to 4 December 2021 to advise of separation are not requested to provide the details of 2 referees. An outbound contact may be required to obtain details of an independent referee, where there is not sufficient written documentation to verify that a bona fide separation has occurred already available on their record. Contact to request details of a suitable referee will be done only when staff processing the separation activity have determined sufficient written documentation is not already available.

Is the MOD S/online update complete with no further action required (including no FDV concerns)?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, if:
    • the customer identified family and domestic violence concerns but did not want to be contacted to discuss support options, record a DOC using the FDV Concerns Identified Fast Note. See Family and domestic violence. Go to Step 10
    • the customer identified family and domestic violence safety concerns and has asked to be contacted to discuss support options, go to Step 4
    • unanswered questions or clarification required (including where the customer indicated they may reconcile with their ex-partner), go to Step 5
    • for MOD S only - unsigned form, name missing or section 2 not completed/returned, go to Step 8
    • further forms required, go to Step 9

4

Customer indicated safety concerns + Read more ...

Customer has indicated safety concerns and asked to be contacted to discuss support options.

Is the outbound contact attempt successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, or the customer terminates the call, create a social work referral in Customer First
    • Select Workspace > search Social Work Referral in search field. Select Social Work Referral > FDV support required
    • Record a DOC using the FDV Concern Identified Fast Note. See Family and domestic violence
    • Go to Step 10

5

Unanswered questions or clarification required + Read more ...

Services Australia is not liable for charges/fees related to gathering information authorised under social security law.

Customers can verbally clarify details about their separation, if they wish to. Make one outbound attempt to contact the customer to clarify or get the missing details about their separation.

Is the outbound contact attempt successful?

  • Yes, and the customer agrees to give the information over the phone, go to Step 6
  • No, or the customer does not agree to give the information over the phone or terminates the call, for:
    • Clarification required due to possible reconciliation, go to Step 7
    • Unanswered questions, go to Step 8

6

Customer attends appointment or outbound contact successful

Customer indicated safety concerns + Read more ...

If the customer indicated family and domestic violence concerns follow the Risk Identification and Referral Model to provide appropriate support.

Note: in all cases where a customer has indicated family and domestic violence concerns:

  • Record a DOC using the FDV Concern Identified Fast Note. See Family and domestic violence
  • Go to Step 10

Unanswered questions or clarification required + Read more ...

Record details of all verbal customer contact in a DOC, including:

  • conversations
  • information given, and
  • information received

Text to include in the DOC

Ext Details: extra verbal information obtained about the separation

Text: verbal information obtained from customer via phone on <XX/XX/XXXX> in relation to separation. Information sought in relation to the following question: <insert question details>. Details obtained: <free text>. Responses verbally confirmed

Go to Step 10.

7

Possible reconciliation + Read more ...

  • Book an appointment to interview the customer in person or over the phone. If no appointment profile is available, issue a Q164 - Request to contact office letter, via an Online Advice
  • Record the issue of the Q164 and information to clarify with the customer in a DOC
  • Set a manual review to follow up the customer contact. Allow time for the delivery of the notice

Procedure ends here until the customer responds to the Q164.

8

MOD S incomplete - Request information in writing + Read more ...

It is a minimum requirement that the customer's name be on the form.

Where certain information is missing, it may not be possible or practical to get the information from the customer verbally or a contact attempt may have been unsuccessful.

Issue the Request customer to complete Form or Questionnaire (Q004) letter when:

  • the customer's name is missing
  • the form has not been signed
  • section 2 (referee declaration) is not completed/returned and/or signed
  • the customer refuses to provide information via phone, or
  • contact with customer has not been successful

To issue the Q004, see Creating an Online Advice (OLA), including within an existing activity, noting the following:

  • print the letter locally
  • print the incomplete MOD S and insert it with the Q004
  • set a manual review to mature when the allowable time has passed to follow up the customer contact

Procedure ends here until the customer responds to the request.

9

Further forms required + Read more ...

Additional forms are required when the customer indicates:

  • Their address or accommodation details have changed (MOD AC)
  • They are living in the same home as their ex-partner (SS293)
  • They share care of children, or number of children in care has changed (FA012)
  • There has been changes to their income and assets (MOD iA)

Where the online Update relationship details service has been used to advise of separation, the service will request any additional forms required.

Check the customer's record for any form/s that may have already been issued and/or returned.

If the customer needs to complete additional forms:

  • Make one outbound attempt to the customer
  • if contact is successful, direct them to the Services Australia website to download the forms online
  • In all cases (contact is successful or unsuccessful), issue a request. In:
    • Customer First, run the Request for Information guided procedure to send the request for the required form
    • Process Direct, request additional information
  • Do not include a reply paid envelope
  • An automatic review will be created to follow up on the return of the forms. This review will mature in 14 days on the Activity List (AL) screen. Resubmit the review activity to fall due when the allowable time has passed
  • Place the separation activity on hold

Where the customer must complete an SS293, Member of a Couple processing staff will complete the assessment. This includes deciding if the customer needs a risk-based assessment.

Procedure ends here.

10

Completed MOD S/required forms returned or online separation update completed- check if the customer's payment is suspended + Read more ...

On receipt of completed separation details, check if the customer's payment shows as suspended (SUS).

Is the customer's payment SUS?

  • Yes, do not update the MS screen and unlink the records until after:
    • investigating the reason for the suspension, and
    • restoring the payment
    • after the payment has been restored, go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 11

11

Verify the separation + Read more ...

After receipt of completed separation details, verify the separation. Once verified, the records can be unlinked. Follow relevant Operational Blueprint files below for process.

For customers in receipt of:

  • Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP), including customers partnered to a PPP recipient, see Transfer from PPP to PPS. Procedure ends here
  • Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY (ABY), see YA or ABSTUDY customer separates. Procedure ends here
  • Only receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and/or Child Care Subsidy (CCS), see FTB and/or CCS customer separates. Procedure ends here
  • Other payments, see the Verification of separation table in Change in relationship status from partnered to single, which also covers unlinking processes
