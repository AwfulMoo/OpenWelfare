4

Make at least 2 attempts to call the customer + Read more ...

Phone the customer at the most likely time and place to be contacted, as this will increase the possibility of a successful outcome.

If an interpreter is required when discussing debt repayment, see Contacting an on-demand interpreter (interpreter Connect).

At the start of a call to an interpreter, staff:

  • must read the privacy statement script to the interpreter, and
  • ask the interpreter to translate the script to the customer
  • can read the script once if all parties are on the phone at the start of the call

Customers should only be contacted by phone during the following times:

  • Monday to Friday – 8:00am to 5:00pm local time, or
  • During their normal working hours, if known

Do not make contact on weekends or National public holidays.

Make at least 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer.

Send pre-call notification SMS via Customer First, before making an outbound call. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging.

Details of all attempts must be clearly DOCumented on the customer's record, including:

  • type of number/s dialled, for example, Home (HOM), work (WRK), mobile phone (MOB), contact (CON)
  • phone number dialled
  • time of call
  • result of call. For example, who the Service Officer spoke with, details of message left, information discussed and agreement reached
  • the types of searches completed (where applicable). For example, searches to locate suitable alternate numbers and financial institution checks

Was the customer contact successful?

Yes, is the customer current or suspended?

  • Current, and:
    • agreed to repay the debt, go to Table 4 and follow the steps based on the repayment method
    • Is unable to repay the debt, go to Table 3
    • requested a review, see Review of decision for Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS) payment
  • Suspended:
    • If the customer:
      - can pay the outstanding balance within 28 days, procedure ends here
      - cannot pay the outstanding balance within 28 days, transfer the debt to the Department of Home Affairs go to Table 3

No, hold the activity until the next day and attempt 2nd contact.

Is the customer current or suspended?

  • Current:
  • If the 2nd attempt is unsuccessful, enter the 5% involuntary withholding from customer’s SRSS payment. Select the RCV activity on the Activity List (AL) screen. See Table 4 Step1 for coding
  • Create a closed Fast Note ‘Involuntary Withholding’ auto text option>Auto Text> Multicultural>SRSS Debts> Involuntary Withholding
  • Procedure ends here
  • Suspended:
    • If the 2nd attempt is unsuccessful, transfer the debt to the Department of Home Affairs, go to Table 3

6

Over recovered amount + Read more ...

The customer will no longer have a debt once the debt has been paid in full or paid off in full through:

  • a payment arrangement
  • BPAY
  • Post Billpay, or
  • voluntary withholding

Has the customer paid more than the debt amount via BPay or Post Billpay?

  • Yes,
    • tell the customer to stop making repayments
    • confirm the customer’s bank account details to deposit the over recovered amount if the customer is entitled to the refund
    • if the debt has been transferred to the Department of Home Affairs, check OPDA, the excess repayments will be transferred to the Department of Home Affairs
    • if the customer has an outstanding debt with the Department of Home Affairs, these funds will be credited against that debt
    • Go to Table 5 to action over recovered amount
  • Create an open Fast Note auto text option >Auto Txt>Multicultural>SRSS Debts>SRSS Debt Escalation for SRSS Helpdesk to follow up
  • No, Procedure ends here

7

If customer has broken arrangement + Read more ...

  • No, go to Table 3

Withholdings end date reached + Read more ...

Is the customer current on SRSS payment?

  • Yes, re-enter the previous withholding as agreed with the customer by selecting the RCV activity on the AL screen. See Table 4 for processing
  • No, go to Table 3

Payment arrangements

Table 4: This table provides the processes for making payment arrangements by SRSS withholding, SRSS voluntary direct deductions and POST Billpay or BPAY and in person.

Step

Action

1

Coding SRSS withholding + Read more ...

  • Ask the customer if they can pay in full or if they can pay more than the minimum 5 % withholding from their SRSS payment. The aim is to recover the debt in the shortest time
  • Select RCV activity on the AL screen (if there is one)
  • Go to the OPDL screen and select the debt the customer wants to repay. Note the Debt identification number (id)
  • Go to the OPAL screen and enter ‘WHS - Withholding’ in the Create new arrangement type: field
  • Enter ‘NRR’ ( Status Resolution Services) in the Withholding Type field:
  • Leave the Third-party Withholding CRN: field blank
  • Enter the debt id in the Withholding debt id: field
  • Leave the Non Standard amount: field blank
  • Enter 5% in the Non standard %: field
  • Leave the Non standard Arrears %: field blank
  • Add comments if the customer has agreed to continue repayment for other debts and include the debt identifications. This reduces the need to follow up on other SRSS debt/s
  • Create the ’SRSS Debt Repayment Arrangement’ auto text option in Fast Note > Auto Text > Multicultural > SRSS Debts > SRSS Debt Repayment Arrangement
  • Close Fast Note
  • Procedure ends here

2

Coding SRSS voluntary direct deduction + Read more ...

  • Go to the OPDL screen and select the debt the customer wants to repay. Note the Debt identification number (id)
  • Go to the OPAL screen and enter ‘VOL- Voluntary’ in the Create new arrangement type: field
  • Enter today’s date in Authority to Debtor Date field:
  • Enter the amount customer has agreed to pay in addition to the 5% in the Amount: field
  • Key DDMMYY in the Pay Start date: field
  • Select the frequency:
    • 2WE, FNY-Fortnightly
    • FWK- Every four weeks
    • MTY-Monthly
  • Enter today’s date in the Acknowledgement date: field
  • Enter the BSB number in the FI code (BSB): field
  • Enter customer’s account number and account name
  • Document that the customer has agreed to the arrangement
  • Create the ’SRSS Debt Repayment Arrangement’ auto text option in Fast Note > Auto Text > Multicultural > SRSS Debts > SRSS Debt Repayment Arrangement
  • Close Fast Note
  • Procedure ends here

3

Coding an SRSS arrangement via POST Billpay, BPAY or in person + Read more ...

  • Go to the OPDL screen and select the debt the customer wants to repay. Note the Debt identification number (id)
  • Go to the OPAL screen and enter CSH - Cash in the Create new arrangement type: field
  • Enter todays date in the Agreed Date: field
  • Enter DDMMYY in the Pay Start date: field
  • Enter the amount the customer has agreed to pay in the Amount: field
  • Select the frequency:
    • 2WE, FNY-Fortnightly
    • FWK- Every four weeks
    • MTY-Monthly
  • Document that the customer has agreed to the arrangement
  • Create the ’SRSS Debt Repayment Arrangement’ auto text option in Fast Note > Auto Text > Multicultural > SRSS Debts > SRSS Debt Repayment Arrangement
  • Close Fast Note
  • Procedure ends here

Over recovered debts

Table 5: This table provides the process for staff to action over recovered activity.

Step

Action

1

Customer current on payment + Read more ...

Check if the customer is current on payment. If not make at least 2 genuine attempts. If unsuccessful, refund to existing bank account.

2

Check over recovered amount + Read more ...

  • Check the over recovered amount on the Debt List (OPDL) and Repayment History (OPRH) screens matches the amount listed on the Open Item list
  • Check the debt identification associated with the over recovered amount has not been transferred to the Department of Home Affairs
  • Escalate to the SRSS Helpdesk if the debt associated with the over recovered amount:
    • has been transferred to the Department of Home Affairs, and/or
    • the OPDL and OPRH amount does not match the Open Item list
  • To view the Open Item list, go to Workspace > Financial Customer Care > Open Item list
  • Select Outgoing payment request
  • Select the SRSS Debt payment documents for refund; identified by a ‘red arrow’ on the left hand side. The dates should correspond with dates on the OPRH screen. Do not select anything with a lock status
  • Select correct bank account
  • Enter reason:
    • Debt over recovered, or
    • Over recovered due to review
  • Save. Once processed, the system displays a ‘blue arrow’ next to the refund amount
  • Create the ’SRSS Over recovered Debt Refund Outcome auto text in Fast Note > Auto Text > Multicultural > SRSS Debts > SRSS Over recovered Debt Refund Outcome
  • Procedure ends
