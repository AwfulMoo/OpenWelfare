How to reverse an overpayment for VAP

  • An overpayment is reversed by SAPGUI when it has been raised incorrectly or documentation is outside the National Overpayments Procedures Manual agreed to by Medicare and Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA).
  • Delegate approval from VAP Helpdesk must be obtained to reverse an overpayment

Table 3: This table contains information for VAP to reverse an overpayment to a health practitioner or facility.

Step

Action

1

VAP Overpayments Officer obtains delegate approval to reverse the overpayment + Read more ...

VAP Overpayments Officers input the follow details into HP Records Manager:

  • Record number = 17/2190382 DVA SAP Reversal Approvals
    • date
    • health practitioner or facility name
    • customer name DVXXXXXXXX
    • claim number
    • amount
    • veteran name
    • veteran number
    • reason

Reversals that are over 30 days and under $100 or an error in processing are highlighted in blue.

Reversals that are over 60 days and over $100 and requires referral to DVA are highlighted in green.

2

Delegate approval has been obtained to reverse the overpayment + Read more ...

The DVA Helpdesk delegate will check details in HP Records Manager Record number – ‘17/2190382 DVA SAP Reversal Approval’ and update DVA SAP Reversal Approvals with date and login ID approving a reversal of the overpayment.

Check DVA SAP Reversal Approval for confirmation that an approval has been granted by VAP Helpdesk. See Creating an invoice in SAP GUI table for the reversal process.

Has approval been granted by the VAP Helpdesk?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, overpayment has been recovered. Procedure ends here

3

Referring unrecovered overpayments + Read more ...

Is the unrecovered overpayment for an entity due to incorrect bank details or for a health practitioner or facility?

  • an entity, go to Step 6
  • a provider under $100, go to Step 4
  • a provider $100 and over, go to Step 5

If overpayment has been recovered, procedure ends here.

4

Health practitioner or facility overpayments for under $100 + Read more ...

Has the overpayment under $100.00 been recovered within 30 days of the initial letter of demand?

  • Yes – no further action
  • No – Reverse overpayment in SAP GUI when approval has been received from the VAP Helpdesk

5

Health practitioner or facility overpayments for $100 and over + Read more ...

For overpayments that have not been recovered within 60 days of the initial letter and second letter of demand:

  • Check documentation, ensuring that:
    • all calculations are correct
    • all data is correct
    • the letter has gone to the correct address

If the address has changed

  • Reverse overpayment in SAP GUI when approval has been received from the VAP Helpdesk
  • Scan all health practitioner or facility correspondence and supporting documentation
  • Email to VAP Helpdesk who will forward to DVA within 2 working days

Procedure ends here.

6

Entity overpayment - bank account details incorrectly registered + Read more ...

If a health practitioner or facility has received payments from Services Australia that it is not entitled to, Medicare eBusiness will request VAP raise an overpayment in SAP GUI.

All supporting information about the overpayment is emailed to the VAP Helpdesk by eBusiness.

VAP Helpdesk staff only to action the following steps.

  • create a pseudo customer number
  • add this number to the SAP DV number for incorrect bank account refunds register
  • e-mail all information and supporting documentation to the VAP Overpayments Officers

The following instructions are for VAP Overpayment Officers.

If recovery of the entity overpayment has not been received within 60 days, it is referred to the VAP Helpdesk to determine if the overpayment can be referred to Debt Recovery Parramatta (DRP) for further recovery action. Outstanding invoice is not to be reversed off SAP GUI on advice from Treasury. VAP Service Officers are to:

  • scan all correspondence and supporting documents
  • email this information to the VAP Helpdesk within 2 working days
  • procedure ends here

Quality control for VAP recovery of overpayments

Table 5: This table describes the process used by staff undertaking quality control checks of VAP overpayment recovery actions.

Step

Action

1

Decide the volume of quality control required + Read more ...

To decide the volume of quality control required:

  • Determine the total volume of overpayments actioned for the preceding month
  • Calculate 5% of the total volume of overpayments actioned. Note: fractions of a number should be rounded up to the nearest whole number

2

Select overpayments for quality checking + Read more ...

Select overpayments for quality checking from:

  • completed overpayments
  • overpayments referred to the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)
  • overpayments that have been reversed, and
  • outstanding overpayments

3

Allocate quality checking + Read more ...

The Quality Controller must be an independent Overpayments Officer who has not been involved in the recovery process.

4

Conduct and record quality control checks + Read more ...

Open the Quality Control Checklist on the Resources page.

Using the checklist, record the action taken on the recovery of the overpayment. Ensure:

  • all documentation is correct and attached to the overpayment
  • all KPIs as stipulated in the Recovery of VAP Overpayments Policy have been adhered to
  • health practitioner or facility details are verified against the DVA Provider File (VPUI), and
  • all details have been correctly entered on SAP GUI

5

Notify actioning officer of inaccuracies or errors + Read more ...

Any inaccuracies or errors are to be bought to the attention of the actioning officer and training is to be provided on the correct procedure.

6

Store quality control findings + Read more ...

Findings are kept within each site for auditing purposes. Store the Quality Control Checklist used to conduct the quality check in the site shared drive.

7

Report quality control findings + Read more ...

On the first working day of the month use the 'Quality control recovery of overpayments' email template on the Resources page to:

  • email a report of the quality control findings for the preceding month to the VAP Helpdesk, see Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP) for contact details
  • copy (cc:) the email to Site Team Leaders, see Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP) for contact details
  • use the subject line 'State QC Overpayments of Overpayments - (Site) (Month)'

Create a customer in SAP GUI

Table 6: This table contains the process for creating a customer in the ESSentials Software Application Program (SAP) Graphic User Interface (GUI) system to process Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP) overpayments. Note: before creating a new customer, perform a search to ensure the customer does not already exist. Fields do not need to be completed unless specified.

Step

Action

1

Access Customer Create: Initial screen + Read more ...

Either:

Key ‘FD01‘ in the transaction search box and select Enter , or

  • Select FD01 - Create Customer (Accounting) from your favourites list.

The Customer Create: Initial screen will display.

2

Key details - Access general data screen + Read more ...

  • Key ‘DV’ followed by the 8 character provider number in the Customer field. For example, DV1234567B. Note: if the provider number is not 8 characters, key the number 1 followed by 0s before the provider number to make a total of 8 characters.
  • Key ‘HIC’ in the company code field
  • Select Continue

The 'Create Customer: General Data' screen is displayed.

3

Complete details + Read more ...

Complete the following details in the Address tab:

  • In the Title field key the appropriate title. For example:
    • Mr, Dr (if provider)
    • leave the field blank (if clinic or medical centre)
  • In the Name field key the full name of the provider or company
  • In the Search term 1/2 field key the provider number without the DV before the number.
  • In the Street Address field key the address details including:
    • Street address and number or PO Box
    • Postcode
    • Suburb (to be written in capital letters)
    • AU in the country field
    • Region/State (to be written in capital letters)
  • In the Communication field key the telephone and fax numbers if known

Note: if PO Box, key in Name of street field and leave House number blank.

As per Australia Post standards the Suburb, Regions/State and postcode are to be printed in capitals without punctuation or underlining. To see how the customer's address will print for postage, select Preview. To return to the Create Customer: General Data screen, select Continue .

4

Check for health practitioner or facility ABN + Read more ...

Is the health practitioner or facility's ABN provided?

  • Yes, select the Control Data tab and key the health practitioner or facility's ABN in the VAT Reg. No. field.
  • No, go to Step 5

Note: this field is mandatory for health practitioners or facilities who claim Direct GST or Third party GST.

5

Access Company Code Data screen + Read more ...

Select Company Code Data.

The Create Customer: Company Code Data screen is displayed.

6

Enter Recon. Account details + Read more ...

Select the Account Management tab and key ‘13039’ in the Recon. Account field.

7

Enter terms of payments + Read more ...

Select Payment Transactions tab and:

  • Key ‘AR01’ in the Terms of payments field. Note: this details the due date for the payment
  • Tick the Payment history record checkbox

8

Enter correspondence details + Read more ...

Select Correspondence tab and:

  • Key ‘ZHIC’ in the Dunn.Procedure field. Note: this details the dunning overpayment parameters for letters
  • Key ‘01’ in the Grouping key field. Note: This groups the letters based on the claim ID

9

End process + Read more ...

Select either:

Save - the Customer Create: Initial screen is displayed, or

Cancel - the Main Menu is displayed.

Creating an invoice in SAP GUI

Table 12: This table describes how to create an invoice in ESSentials Software Application Program (SAP) Graphic User Interface (GUI) system for a Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP) overpayment.

Step

Action

1

Access Enter Company Code screen + Read more ...

Key ‘FB70’ in the transaction search box and select Enter .

The Enter Company Code or Enter Customer Invoice: Company Code (HIC) screen is displayed.

Note: the Enter Company Code screen is only displayed the first time the transaction is selected. Once the company code HIC is entered the system will always display the HIC screen.

2

Enter Company Code + Read more ...

Is the Enter Company Code screen displayed?

  • Yes, key ‘HIC’ in the Company Code field and select Continue
  • No, go to Step 3

3

The basic data tab + Read more ...

Select the Basic data tab and:

  • enter the ‘customer number’ in the Customer field. For example, DV1234567B
  • enter invoice date as yesterday’s date dd.mm.yyyy
  • enter the processing state/claim ID in the Reference field. For example, SA/Z1234
  • enter the posting date as yesterday’s date dd.mm.yyyy
  • enter the reason for overpayment in the free text field
  • select DD-MCA VAP invoices from the document type dropdown dox
  • Quality Check the address when it is displayed
  • enter the total amount of overpayment in the amount field, including GST
  • ensure calculate tax box is not ticked
  • the screen will grey out, select details once and the screen will appear normal
  • select blank from the dropdown box under calculate box

Note: the address should populate on the right, confirm the correct customer has been chosen.

4

Select Details tab + Read more ...

Select the Details tab and enter the:

  • veteran's full name in the Assign field. Ensure the first letter of an individual’s name and surname are in capitals. For example, John Smith.
  • veteran's file number in the Header Text field. For example, SX123456
  • reason code for overpayment in the Cont No. field. For example, A001
  • date of service dd.mm.yyyy for overpayment in the Ref.key 2 field. If more than 1 date, key ‘various’
  • item number for overpayment in the Ref.key 3 field. If more than 1 item, key ‘various’ or ‘audit’

Note: if there are more than 2 individuals on the invoice, use the term ‘various’.

The following text limits apply to each of the fields:

  • Assign – 18 character limit
  • Header – 25 character limit
  • Free text – 50 character limit
  • Ref Key 3 – 20 character limit

5

Enter details + Read more ...

Select the Basic data tab and go to the line data table at the bottom of the screen. Enter the following details:

  • In the column G/L acct enter the G/L account code for the type of benefit that was overpaid
    • 51111 - DVA direct benefit
    • 51112 - DVA direct GST
    • 51115 - DVA benefit 3rd party (GST free)
    • 51116 - DVA 3rd party benefit
    • 51117 - DVA 3rd party GST
  • If multiple G/L account codes are entered then enter the amount of the breakdown into the Amount in doc. curr. field

Note: proportion GST as specified in the G/L account codes if applicable.

6

Preview invoice + Read more ...

Select Simulate to preview the invoice (OP01 Letter).

Select Reset to return to the Enter Customer Invoice: Company Code HIC screen.

Note: a warning message 'Automatically created line items will be deleted' is displayed.

Close the message by selecting X or Continue .

7

Finalise process + Read more ...

Change information if required and select Post .

See Create a Dunning Run table and then Print a reminder letter in SAP GUI to obtain OP01 reminder letter.

Detail the invoice no. and date from the OPO1 letter on the overpayment form.

The invoice is saved and allocated a unique document number displayed in fine print at the bottom of the screen.
