SpB customer advises commencing or returning to work. + Read more ...

SpB customers are not covered under the Working Credit initiative as they are subject to a different income test.

Obtain the following details from the customer:

  • Date of commencement or return to employment
  • Employer’s name
  • Employer’s address and phone number
  • Gross employment income paid per week/fortnight
  • Hours per week/fortnight
  • Full time/Part time

DOC minimum standards

These only require the collection and recording of enough information about the employer (e.g. contact name and address or phone number) to allow income assessment, as well as contact with the employer if necessary.

Contact may be necessary for verification of the income details provided, or for conducting reviews resulting from Data matching with the Australian Taxation Office (ATO).

Some customers (e.g. those who work in sensitive areas like women's refuges, etc.) may not want all employment details (e.g. business name) recorded on a DOC. This does not affect the requirement to collect sufficient information for assessment or review purposes.

Has the customer commenced full time work?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Customer has commenced full time work + Read more ...

Customer no longer meets the qualification for Special Benefit as they are no longer unable to obtain or earn a sufficient livelihood.

  • Manually cancel SpB on the Benefit Action (BA) screen coding the following fields:
  • Svc Rsn: code ‘SPL’
  • Action: code ‘CAN’
  • Reason: code ‘OSA’ (other support available)
  • Effect Date: date work commenced
  • Source: 'INT/PHO' and DOR: (date of receipt)
  • Finalise activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen after checking details are correct
  • If the customer has a partner, record the customer's employment income so that any excess income is correctly assessed for the partner
    Except for partners on Partner Allowance (PA) or SpB, partners under Age Pension age may have access to working credits to offset their employment income. Their payment may remain current for up to 6 fortnights if it is reduced to nil by income. PA will auto cancel when SpB is cancelled
  • Cancellation letter will auto-generate
  • Record the decision]

See Table 2.

3

Part time or casual employment income + Read more ...

  • Code details on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen via the Earnings and reporting workflow tool. Code income as 'IOP' (Income in One Period) or 'LOP' (Longer Period).
  • If still entitled to SpB, the customer will need to lodge a Reporting Statement fortnightly via self service (online or phone) in person or in writing (SU19). Code two weekly reporting on the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen.

Assessment of income for SpB customers commencing or returning to work

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Assessment of Income + Read more ...

Is the customer single?

  • Yes, go to Step 2.
  • No, if their partner receives:
  • a pension, go to Step 3
  • Partner Allowance (PA) or SpB, go to Step 4
  • another allowance or benefit, or does not receive a payment, go to Step 5

2

Special Benefit customer is single + Read more ...

Advise customer there is no income free area for SpB, and payment is reduced dollar for dollar under the Special Benefit income test.

Customer’s entitlement = Current rate of SpB minus total of gross income from employment.

Go to Step 6.

3

SpB customer with partner on a pension + Read more ...

If below Age Pension age, partner may have access to working credits to offset their employment income.

They may remain on payment for up to 6 fortnights and retain certain benefits if their rate is reduced to nil by income and some of that income is employment income.

Advise the customer that half of the assessable income will be applied to the pension customer under the Pensions Income and Assets Tests rules, and half will reduce the SpB customer's payment dollar for dollar.

SpB customer’s entitlement = Current rate of SpB minus (total of gross income from employment divided by 2).

Go to Step 6.

4

SpB customer with partner on SpB or PA + Read more ...

Neither customer is covered by the Working Credit provisions as a different income test applies. The partner's qualification for PA depends on the customer's qualification for SpB.

The customer's SpB payment is reduced dollar for dollar under the Special Benefit income test and any excess income reduces their partner’s payment dollar for dollar.

Customer’s entitlement = Current rate of SpB minus total of gross income from employment.

Go to Step 6.

5

SpB customer with partner not receiving pension, SpB or PA + Read more ...

Partner may have access to working credits to offset their employment income.

They may remain on payment for up to 6 fortnights and retain certain benefits if their rate is reduced to nil by income and some of that income is employment income.

Income will be applied to customer's payment as a direct deduction, and any excess income will reduce partner's payment by 60c in $1.00.

SpB customer’s entitlement = Current SpB rate minus total of gross income from employment.

6

Finalise assessment + Read more ...

Finalise activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen after checking details are correct.

Record details of employment, changes to reporting details and any other relevant details on a DOC.

Is customer or their partner receiving Family Tax Benefit or Child Care Subsidy (CCS)?

  • Yes, check if the customer needs to revise their current annual income estimate and advise payment options available to help reduce the chance of an overpayment during the reconciliation process. See Updating income estimates for the current financial year. Procedure ends here
  • No, procedure ends here
