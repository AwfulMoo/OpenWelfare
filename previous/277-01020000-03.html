This page contains information about obtaining identity confirmation from parents and carers and the types of checks that may be considered. This includes identity checks for representatives and the process to follow if they refuse to provide identity.

Confirming identity

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Identity confirmation + Read more ...

When conducting identity checks, always ask the parent or carer to answer the questions. Never read out personal details such as addresses, telephone or other information available on the record.

If updates are needed, establish identity before making the updates. Confirm last known address in the system before updating new address.

Note: Tax File Numbers (TFNs) are not identity confirmation identifiers. Do not ask for a TFN from a parent or carer to identify them. Do not provide a TFN to a parent, a carer, an authorised representative or anyone else. Refer person with the TFN queries to the Australian Taxation Office. Only use TFNs for data matching purposes.

Extra identity checks for sensitive actions

Conduct additional identity checks for customers asking for action on their case that is considered sensitive. See Step 7.

Offer passwords to all customers

During customer interactions tell them they may record a password on their account:

  • as an added security measure to protect their personal information, and
  • provide an additional assurance. See Step 8

2

Conducting identity confirmation + Read more ...

Conduct identity checks at the beginning of every customer interaction, including:

  • inbound calls, go to Step 3
  • outbound calls, go to Step 5
  • face to face contact, go to Step 6
  • calls with parent or carer's representatives, see Table 2

3

Inbound calls + Read more ...

Is the inbound call transferred from Centrelink?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, follow the instructions below

Voice biometrics authentication

Voice biometrics authentication:

  • is not available for Child Support customer representatives, however if they are calling about their own case it is available
  • uses mutual customers Services Australia voiceprint on inbound calls. It removes the need to ask customers their:
    • name
    • DOB, and
    • password
  • works on transfers from Centrelink and internal transfers

If the caller is voice biometrics authenticated, confirm their:

  • postal address
  • residential address (if this differs from the postal address)
  • telephone numbers

If their response does not match, ask additional questions to confirm their identity. See Step 7 for additional identify questions. After confirming identity, open their record and update if needed.

If a customer asks for their Services Australia voiceprint to be deleted, or advises of misuse

After completing the customers query, offer to cold transfer the customer to Online support - myGov helpdesk – support hotline for customers.

Non voice biometrics authentication

If the caller is not voice biometrics authenticated, tell them to protect their privacy, their identity must be confirmed.

Obtain one of the following identification numbers from Services Australia Workspace or the caller

  • Child Support Reference Number (CSRN)
    • Only provide their CSRN after identity has been successfully completed
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • Callers may choose to provide their CRN to facilitate the identity process but are not obliged to do so. If a caller does not know their CRN this can be provided after identity checks are successfully completed
    • Child Support Customer Representatives still need to contact Centrelink for this information as we cannot be satisfied that they are also a Centrelink nominee for the customer

In addition to the CSRN or CRN or instead of, confirm the following details against Child Support records, and do not prompt the parent or carer in any way:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • postal address
  • residential address (if this differs from the postal address)
  • telephone numbers
  • password (if nominated). See Step 8 for password information

If a response does not match Child Support records, ask additional questions to confirm their identity. See Step 7 for additional identify questions.

Confirm last known address in the system before updating new address.

The customer may refuse to provide or be unable to provide:

  • a residential, or
  • postal address

When this is the case see Step 1 in the Update customer contact and tax file number information table of Updating Child Support Customer and Assessment information.

After confirming identity, open their record and update if required. Go to Step 9.

4

Transfers from Centrelink + Read more ...

When a call is warm transferred from Centrelink, confirm with the officer transferring the call that identity has been completed.

Ask the officer transferring the call, for the callers:

  • identification number (CSRN or CRN) to locate the caller's record
  • postal address
  • residential address (if this differs from the postal address)
  • telephone numbers

Check the customer detail match in the Centrelink and Child Support system.

If there is a password, obtain this prior to proceeding with the enquiry. See Step 8 for password information

If identity was not completed, go to Step 3 non voice biometrics authentication.

5

Outbound calls + Read more ...

Inform the parent or carer that to protect their privacy, their identity must be confirmed. Confirm each of the following details against their record:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • postal address
  • residential address (if this differs from the postal address)
  • password (if nominated)

If a response does not match, ask additional questions that only they are likely to know in order to confirm their identity. Go to Step 7 for information. After confirming identity, open their record and update if required.

Confirm last known address in the system before updating new address.

If the parent or carer refuses to provide identity, see Table 3.

The customer may refuse to provide or be unable to provide:

  • a residential, or
  • postal address

When this is the case see Step 1 in the Update customer contact and tax file number information table of Updating Child Support Customer and Assessment information.

6

Face to face contact + Read more ...

In the limited circumstances in which Child Support have face to face contact with a parent or carer, ask them to provide the following details against Child Support records, and do not prompt them in any way:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • postal address
  • email address
  • password (if nominated)
  • CSRN or case number

Alternatively ask for:

  • full name
  • CSRN or case number
  • photo identification

Note: if a response does not match Child Support records or if photo identification cannot be supplied, ask additional questions only they are likely to know, to confirm their identity. Go to Step 6 for information. After confirming identity, open their record and update if required. Confirm last known address in the system before updating new address.

7

Additional identity questions + Read more ...

Ask the parent or carer additional questions when they:

  • do not provide sufficient identity confirmation or
  • asking action on their case that is considered sensitive:
    • requesting a refund
    • gifting excess cash
    • discharging arrears
    • updating bank details
    • ending a child support assessment

Explain Services Australia's obligation to protect their privacy. To confirm their identity, ask additional questions about information Child Support has on record which only they are likely to know such as details about their:

  • bank account and BSB number
  • previous call (date, purpose, outcome, receipt number)
  • previous address or contact details
  • recorded email address
  • recent written communication sent to or received by the agency (for example date, content and/or outcome)
  • amount and date of last child support payment

Do not prompt them with the answer. Do not let them have more than one attempt at answering if their first answer is incorrect. Ask a different question.

If the caller's identity cannot be confirmed, the Service Officer must call the customer back on the contact numbers recorded in the record to confirm their identity. Otherwise, explain specific information about their case cannot be discussed - general child support queries can be answered.

8

Passwords + Read more ...

The Child Support system has the facility to record a nominated password up to 15 characters long.

Offer passwords to all customers

During customer interactions tell them they may record a password on their account:

  • as an added security measure to protect their personal information, and
  • provide an additional assurance

To add, update or delete a password:

  • in Cuba, see Customer records process help
  • in Pluto, see Update customer details in Pluto

Password checks must be conducted in addition to identity checks for non voice biometrics authenticated callers. If a caller (or representative, see Table 2) satisfies identity requirements but is unable to remember their password or provides an incorrect password, ask additional questions to confirm their identity. Go to Step 7 for information. Ask them to nominate another password and document why the password was changed and how the identity was verified, see Documenting Child Support information.

Do not accept offensive passwords or those that do not adhere to the Australian Public Service values. Encourage callers to change existing passwords that are considered offensive. If they refuse to do so, tell them their current password will be removed and then delete the password.

9

Update Cuba + Read more ...

Complete identity before opening the caller's records. If during identity confirmation, they provide Child Support with more recent information that is not shown on their record, ask them to verify the information saved before making any changes.

If the surname or gender of any party to the assessment (including a child) is recorded as 'unknown', request the information from the caller and update their record. See Updating Child Support customer and assessment information.

Authorised Representative identification

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Authorised representatives + Read more ...

If a customer wants to authorise a representative to speak to Services Australia about their child support case, they can provide:

  • Verbal authorisation, or
  • Written authorisation

See, Child Support representative with ordinary authority for details on:

  • Who can be authorised representative
  • What a representative can do
  • Requirements for verbal and written authorisation

2

Check authorised representative details + Read more ...

To view the details of a current authorised representative:

  • open the Customer Representative window
  • highlight the current representative record to view details
  • open the notepad to view the parent's or carer's written authorisation

Authorised representatives with a status of 'DEL' are not current. Do not disclose any details about the parent or carer or the case.

If there is no written authority documented on the parent's or carer's record:

  • do not confirm that a child support case exists
  • only speak about child support in general
  • request the caller to forward the written authority to the agency via fax, mail or email
  • offer to return their call once the written authorisation is received and documented on the record

Note: do not provide case or specific information to an alternate contact as they are not an authorised representative.

See Child Support representative with ordinary authority.

3

Identity checks for representatives + Read more ...

Individual authorised representative

Inform the authorised representative that to protect the customer's privacy, their identity, and that of the person they are representing must be confirmed. Ask the authorised representative to provide their:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • postal address
  • phone numbers

Ask the authorised representative to provide the following details for the person they are representing:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • postal address
  • contact telephone numbers
  • password (if nominated)

Organisational authorised representative

Where a customer authorises an organisation to be their representative, any current member of the organisation can act as the customer’s representative. Legal Aid, Community Legal Centres, and private debt management services are examples of organisations where different members of the organisation may contact child support as the customer representative.

Inform the authorised representative that to protect the customer’s privacy, their identity, and that of the person they are representing must be confirmed. Ask the authorised representative to provide their:

  • name
  • name of organisation (as recorded in the written authority)
  • organisational postal address (as recorded in the written authority)
  • organisational phone numbers (as recorded in the written authority)

Ask the authorised representative to provide the following details for the person they are representing:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • Child Support Reference Number or Case Number, if not already provided
  • postal address
  • contact telephone numbers
  • password (if nominated)

Confirm last known address in the system before updating new address. Ensure the details recorded in the Customer Representative window and Customer window match the information provided before proceeding with the call.

4

Representative requesting multiple parent/carer enquiries on one call + Read more ...

If a person or organisation is a representative of multiple parents/carers they must make separate calls for each parent/carer's enquiry. This is to protect the privacy of each parent/carer by ensuring that the call recording system correctly records and assigns call recordings to the correct customer.

5

Third party representatives with implied authority + Read more ...

The third parties listed below have implied authority from the parent or carer and are not required to provide written authorisation:

  • Parliamentarians (including international) and their electoral staff
  • Senators and their electoral staff
  • Commonwealth Ombudsman staff (only investigates agency administrative actions)
  • Office of the Australian Information Commissioner staff

Identity checks for representatives with implied authority

Ask the third party representative with implied authority to:

  • provide their identity and position
  • identify the constituent who has made the complaint, and
  • provide information that could only have come from the customer

Immediately warm transfer the call to the Parliamentarian and Ombudsman Advice line by selecting the 'MP' option in Services Australia Workspace.

Refusal to provide identity

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Where a person refuses to provide identity + Read more ...

Where a person refuses to provide sufficient identity, explain that establishing identity is required before Child Support can speak with them about their case.

If the person is not confident that it is Child Support contacting them during an outbound phone call, ask them to call back on 131 272 within 2 working days. Tell them that when they call back they will be asked to provide information to confirm their identity.

If the parent or carer refuses to call Services Australia or has a history of refusing to provide identity, inform them their unwillingness to provide identity may be preventing the discussion of child support matters and could result in decisions being made on their case without their input.

If the person continues to refuse to provide identity end the call in line with Contact with Child Support Customers Table 17 step 1.

The customer may refuse to provide or be unable to provide:

  • a residential, or
  • postal address

When this is the case see Step 1 in the Update customer contact and tax file number information table of Updating Child Support Customer and Assessment information.

Where there is a child support debt, explain that their unwillingness to provide identity may result in enforcement action commencing to recover overdue amounts without further contact. This action may include taking payments from their salary, Centrelink payments, tax refund, or legal action.

2

Further refusal to provide identity + Read more ...

If the customer still refuses to provide identity send a unique letter requesting contact within 7-14 days of the date of the letter. See the Creating unique letters table in Letters for Child Support customers.

The letter must advise them that:

  • when they contact Child Support they will be asked to provide information to confirm their identity, to avoid the possible disclosure of protected information to a person not entitled to receive it
  • failure to contact Child Support or unwillingness to provide identity could result in decisions being made on their case without their input, and
  • where they have a child support debt
    • if they do not contact Child Support by the date provided in the letter, action may commence to recover overdue amounts without further contact. This action may include taking payments from the customer's salary, Centrelink payments, tax refund, or legal action

Allow the customer time to respond. See Standard response times.

3

No response received by the due date + Read more ...

If there is no response to the letter by the due date, progress the outstanding child support issues and make the required decisions on their case based on the information available. Follow up on all outstanding child support matters.

If the outstanding child support matters include overdue child support amounts, initiate action to recover the outstanding child support See Debt repayment.
