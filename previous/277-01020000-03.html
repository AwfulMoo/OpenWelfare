Inbound calls + Read more ...

An inbound call will be:

  • voice biometrics authenticated, or
  • not voice biometrics authenticated

Softphone and voice biometrics authentication

  • passed authentication - shows ‘green’ with ‘Voiceprint match’
  • no authentication - shows 'amber' as 'no Voiceprint'

    • voice biometric authenticated - with password, go to Step 3

2

Voice biometrics authenticated - no password + Read more ...

Staff do not do an identity check where the caller:

  • is voice biometrics authenticated, and
  • does not have a password on the Child Support customer record

For these customers, staff are to check (do not prompt) the:

  • postal address. The suburb and state are enough if they do not know the postcode
  • residential address, if the postal address is:
    • different,
    • blank, or
    • invalid
  • phone numbers (mobile, daytime, after hours)

If their response does not match the details on record:

  • document the correct contact details
  • update the contact details
  • do not ask additional Child Support identity check questions
  • continue with the call

If the caller refuses to give their contact details:

  • discuss why it is better to make sure their contact details are correct
  • tell them about self-service options
  • document their refusal to give the specific contact detail/s, and
  • continue with the call

If a customer:

  • asks for their Services Australia voiceprint to be deleted, or advises of misuse
    • complete the customer's query
    • offer to cold transfer the customer to Online support - myGov helpdesk - support hotline for customers
  • wants to create a Services Australia voiceprint
    • tell them to go to the Services Australia website, see Resources for a link, or
    • cold transfer the customer to the relevant telephony queue

3

Voice biometrics authenticated with a password + Read more ...

Staff must complete an identity check where the caller:

  • is voice biometrics authenticated, and
  • has a password on their Child Support record

Staff are to check (do not prompt) the:

  • postal address. The suburb and state are enough if they do not know the postcode
  • residential address, if the postal address is:
    • different,
    • blank, or
    • invalid
  • phone numbers (mobile, daytime, after hours)

If their response does not match the details on record:

  • document the correct contact details
  • do not update the contact details
  • continue with the call

If the caller refuses to give their contact details:

  • discuss why it is better to make sure their correct contact details are correct
  • tell them about self-service options
  • document their refusal to give the specific contact detail/s

Staff must ask the customer for the password. If their password response:

  • matches the record password:
    • the identity check for Child Support is complete
    • continue with the call
    • update any changed contact details or password
    • give the customer their CSRN if they do not have it or ask for it. The CSRN helps when calling or using self-service
  • does not match the record password, or is unknown:
    • the identity check for Child Support is incomplete
    • document the difference in the password or unknown
    • do not update any changed contact details or password
    • do not discuss the customer details
    • ask additional questions to confirm their identity. Go to Step 10

If they refuse to give their password:

  • the identity check for Child Support is incomplete
  • document their refusal to give the password
  • do not update any changed contact details or password
  • do not discuss the customer details
  • ask additional questions to confirm their identity. Go to Step 10

    • different,

5

Warm transfer from Centrelink - voice biometrics authenticated + Read more ...

When a call is warm transferred from Centrelink:

  • they will make sure the correct customer record is given when transferring the call
  • Child Support staff may need to complete a modified identity check

Note: if the customer has a password on the record, the Service Officer must ask for the password before beginning the enquiry.

Child Support staff:

  • do not need to complete an identity check when:
    • a call is warm transferred from Centrelink, and
    • the customer is voice biometrics authenticated, and
    • there is no password on the Child Support record
  • complete a modified identity check when:
    • a call is warm transferred from Centrelink, and
    • the customer is voice biometrics authenticated, and
    • there is password on the Child Support record

Ask the transferring Officer, for the customer's:

  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • legal name
  • date of birth
  • postal address. The suburb and state are enough if they do not know the postcode
  • residential address, if the postal address is:
    • different,
    • blank, or
    • invalid
  • phone numbers (mobile, daytime, after hours)

Where the Centrelink contact details do not match:

  • document the difference
  • do not update any contact details
  • accept the transfer

Authenticated customers do not need to give their contact details.

If the Centrelink contact details do not match the Child Support details, ask the customer if they would like to update their details. If they:

  • agree:
    • document any new contact details
    • update the Child Support record contact details
    • do not ask additional Child Support identity check questions
    • continue with the call
  • do not agree, ask them what they want for their Child Support record:
    • postal address. The suburb and state are enough if they do not know the postcode
    • residential address, if the postal address is different, blank, or invalid
    • phone numbers (mobile, daytime, after hours)
    • document any new contact details
    • update the contact details
    • do not ask additional Child Support identity check questions
    • continue with the call
  • refuse to give their contact details
    • discuss why it is better to make sure their correct contact details are correct
    • tell them about self-service options
    • document their refusal to give the specific contact detail/s
    • continue with the call

If the customer has a password on their Child Support record, go to Step 9.

10

Additional identity questions + Read more ...

Ask the customer additional questions when they:

  • do not provide sufficient identity confirmation, or
  • ask for action on their case that is considered sensitive:
    • requesting a refund
    • gifting excess cash
    • discharging arrears
    • updating bank details
    • ending a child support assessment

Always ask the customer/representative for information. Do not:

  • prompt them with the answer
  • let them have more than one try at answering if their first answer is incorrect

Ask questions about information Child Support has, which only they know, such as their:

  • bank account and BSB number
  • previous call (date, purpose, outcome, receipt number)
  • previous contact details (address and phone number)
  • recorded email address
  • recent written communication sent by or received by the agency. For example date, content and/or outcome
  • last child support payment amount and date
  • Child Support Reference Number (CSRN) or Customer Reference Number (CRN), if not already provided

If their response matches the record:

  • the identity check for Child Support is complete
  • continue with the call
  • update any changed contact details
  • update any changed password details. An authorised representative cannot change a customer’s password:
    • document why the password changed, and
    • how identity was verified
  • if the customer does not know or wants their Child Support Reference Number (CSRN), give it to them:
    • their CSRN assists when calling or using self-service

If their response does not match the record, check it is the correct record. Ask (do not prompt) the customer:

  • the name of the other person they have a Child Support case with
  • details about the child/ren, including:
    • number of children
    • names
    • date of birth
  • relationship and proof of parentage details used to start the assessment/s
  • start or stop date of their assessment

Check if the customer has access to use self-service to update their details:

  • Child Support online account, or
  • Express Plus Child Support mobile app

Can the customer use self-service?

  • Yes:
    • wait for the real-time update, and
    • check their Child Support record contact details
    • continue with the call once identity is confirmed
  • No:
    • tell them no specific details about their Child Support record will be discussed
    • general child support queries can be answered
    • end call. See Contact with Child Support customers for follow up contact

Note: if suspected someone is fraudulently attempting to access another customer’s record, report it. See Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption.

3

Identity checks for Individual authorised representative + Read more ...

An authorised representative:

  • cannot be voice biometrics authenticated
  • has an individual Child Support record connected to the customer they represent

The individual authorised representative record does not identify the customer record it represents.

A search on the Child Support represented customer record will identify the authorised representative record linked to it.

To protect privacy, an authorised representative must confirm:

  • their identity, and
  • the represented customer's identity

If a customer record did not generate from Services Australia Workspace, ask the authorised representative for the customer's:

  • Child Support Reference Number (CSRN), or
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)

An authorised representative is not obliged, may not know or can refuse to give the customer's CSRN or CRN.

To help an authorised representative find the customer's CSRN or CRN, tell them it can be found on:

  • Services Australia Child Support letter - top right under the address block
  • Child Support Online Account - top right in the title bar
  • Express Plus Child Support App - via My Profile

If the customer's record does not generate from Services Australia Workspace or cannot provide their CSRN or CRN. Ask (do not prompt) the authorised representative for the customer's:

  • legal name
  • date of birth
  • postal address (if postcode is not known, suburb and state is enough)
  • residential address (if this differs from the postal address, blank or invalid)
  • phone numbers (mobile, daytime, after hours)
  • password (if nominated)

Ask (do not prompt) the authorised representative for their:

  • legal name
  • date of birth
  • postal address (if postcode is not known, suburb and state is enough)
  • residential address (if this differs from the postal address, blank or invalid)
  • phone numbers (mobile, daytime, after hours)
  • password (if nominated)

If their response matches the record:

  • the identity check for Child Support is complete
  • continue with the call
  • update any changed contact details
  • if the authorised representative did not know the customer's CSRN, give it to them:
    • the customer's CSRN assists when calling or using self-service
  • do not give the customer’s CRN to the authorised representative:
    • the authorised representative must contact Centrelink if they want to know their represented customer’s CRN
    • Centrelink determines the nominee status for Centrelink customers, not Child Support

If it is the correct record, and an authorised representative does not meet the identity check for Child Support requirements:

  • the identity check for Child Support is incomplete
  • note the change, and
  • do not update any contact details, and
  • do not discuss the customer record at this stage, and
  • tell them that verbal or written authorisation cannot be found, and
  • offer to answer general child support queries, or
  • can they put the customer on for an identity check for Child Support to give verbal authority, or
  • call/message the customer to contact Child Support to give a verbal or written authority, or
  • forward the completed written authority via fax, mail or upload with a call back request

4

Identity checks for Organisational authorised representative + Read more ...

Where a customer authorises an organisation to be their authorised representative, a current member of the organisation can act as the customer’s authorised representative.

Legal Aid, Community Legal Centres, and private debt management services are examples of organisations. In which different members of the organisation may contact Child Support as the authorised representative.

An organisational authorised representative must give Child Support the identity of:

  • their organisation
  • their individual limited identity
  • the customer represented identity

An organisational authorised representative cannot be voice biometrics authenticated.

If a customer record did not generate from Services Australia Workspace, ask the authorised representative for the customer's:

  • Child Support Reference Number (CSRN), or
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)

An organisational representative is not obliged, may not know or can refuse to give the customer's CSRN or CRN.

To help an organisational authorised representative find the customer's CSRN or CRN, tell them it can be found on:

  • Services Australia Child Support letter - top right under the address block
  • Child Support Online Account - top right in the title bar
  • Express Plus Child Support App - via My Profile

If the customer's record does not generate from Services Australia Workspace or cannot provide their CSRN or CRN. Ask (do not prompt) the organisational authorised representative for the customer's:

  • legal name
  • date of birth
  • postal address (if postcode is not known, suburb and state is enough)
  • residential address (if this differs from the postal address, blank or invalid)
  • phone numbers (mobile, daytime, after hours)
  • password (if nominated)

Ask (do not prompt) the organisational authorised representative for their:

  • name
  • organisation:
    • name
    • postal address
    • phone numbers

Organisational structure changes may have occurred since the original written authorisation.

If their response matches the record:

  • the identity check for Child Support is complete
  • continue with the call
  • update any changed contact details
  • if the authorised representative did not know the customer's CSRN, give it to them:
    • the customer's CSRN assists when calling or using self-service
  • do not give the customer’s CRN to the authorised representative:
    • the authorised representative must contact Centrelink if they want to know their represented customer’s CRN
    • Centrelink determines the nominee status for Centrelink customers, not Child Support

If their response does not match the record:

  • the identity check for Child Support is incomplete
  • document the difference
  • do not update any contact details
  • do not discuss the customer record
  • ask additional questions to confirm their identity. Go to Step 7

5

Authorised representative requesting multiple customer enquiries on one call + Read more ...

If an individual or organisation represents multiple Child Support customers, they must make separate calls for each enquiry.

This protects each customer's privacy by making sure the call recording system:

  • correctly records the call
  • the recorded call is assigned to the correct customer

6

Third party representatives with implied authority + Read more ...

The third parties listed below have implied authority and are not required to give written authorisation:

  • Parliamentarians (including international) and their electoral staff
  • Senators and their electoral staff
  • Commonwealth Ombudsman staff (investigates agency administrative actions)
  • Office of the Australian Information Commissioner staff

Identity checks for representatives with implied authority

Ask the third party representative with implied authority to:

  • provide their identity and position
  • identify the constituent who has made the complaint, and
  • provide information that could have only come from the customer

Immediately warm transfer the call to the Parliamentarian and Ombudsman Advice line by selecting the 'MP' option in Services Australia Workspace.

7

Additional identity questions + Read more ...

Ask the customer additional questions when they:

  • do not provide sufficient identity confirmation, or
  • ask for action on their case that is considered sensitive:
    • requesting a refund
    • gifting excess cash
    • discharging arrears
    • updating bank details
    • ending a child support assessment

Always ask the customer/representative for information. Do not:

  • prompt them with the answer
  • let them have more than one try at answering if their first answer is incorrect

Ask questions about information Child Support has, which only they know, such as their:

  • bank account and BSB number
  • previous call (date, purpose, outcome, receipt number)
  • previous contact details (address and phone number)
  • recorded email address
  • recent written communication sent by or received by the agency. For example date, content and/or outcome
  • last child support payment amount and date
  • Child Support Reference Number (CSRN) or Customer Reference Number (CRN), if not already provided

If their response matches the record:

  • the identity check for Child Support is complete
  • continue with the call
  • update any changed contact details
  • update any changed password details. An authorised representative cannot change a customer’s password:
    • document why the password changed, and
    • how identity was verified
  • if the customer does not know or wants their Child Support Reference Number (CSRN), give it to them:
    • their CSRN assists when calling or using self-service

If their response does not match the record, check it is the correct record. Ask (do not prompt) the customer:

  • the name of the other person they have a Child Support case with
  • details about the child/ren, including:
    • number of children
    • names
    • date of birth
  • relationship and proof of parentage details used to start the assessment/s
  • start or stop date of their assessment

On an inbound call, if a customer is concerned about providing identity:

  • end the call politely

On an outbound call, if a customer is concerned about providing identity:

Where a person refuses to provide identity

If a customer refuses or is unwilling to give sufficient identity, explain:

  • an identity check is required before Child Support can discuss their record
  • if they refuse to call Services Australia
  • if they have a history of refusing to give identity
  • that decisions can be made without their input
  • if calling about a debt not under a payment arrangement, enforcement may occur without further contact
  • end the call politely

2

Further refusal to provide identity + Read more ...

If the customer still refuses to give identity on an:

  • Outbound call - send the appropriate letter (for the purpose of the call)
  • Inbound call - send a unique letter (only for repeat refusals when a customer record is properly established)

See Step 4 in the Sending letters to Child Support customers table.

The letter must tell them that:

  • when they contact they will be asked to give details to confirm their identity. This avoids the possible disclosure of protected information to a person not entitled to get it
  • failure to contact or unwillingness to give identity could result in decisions being made on their record without their input
  • where they have a debt:
    • if they do not contact by the date provided in the letter, enforcement action may commence to recover overdue amounts without further contact. This action may include taking payments from their salary, Centrelink payments, tax refund, or legal action

Allow the customer time to respond. See Standard response times.
