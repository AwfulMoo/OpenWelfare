Discrepancy with signature date on AODR registration form

Table 1: describes what action is required where an AODR registration form has been received by mail and there is an obvious discrepancy with the signature date. AODR forms received should be signed and dated.

If the signature date is:

then:
  • the consumer's date of birth
  • Incomplete
  • not supplied/omitted

Use the date the form has been received/PaNDA DOL for the Most Recent Decision Date and Date of Last Contact.

Note: in all other cases the signature date provided on the form should be recorded.

See also: Date of last contact on the Process page.

Most recent donation decision date

Most recent donation decision date is the date the donor signed the registration form.

The most recent donation decision date must be entered when:

  • creating a new AODR registration
  • amending a Donor Only or AODR entitlement via a registration or Medicare Claim form (MS014) (an updated and more current signed consent signature date)
  • linking a donor only entitlement to a consumer record

Do not update most recent decision date an existing registration when organ donor registration enquiries are received by phone or over the counter at a Medicare Service Centre. Do not update when advised of date of death with no change to donor decision type or organs and/or tissue. In these circumstances only the Date of Last Contact is to be updated.

Note: if the form has not been signature dated, see Signature date discrepancy.

Scenario

How to action

Unsigned form

’Send z0125 letter with photocopy of the form with the incorrect boxes highlighted.

“Yes” to donate and “No”/objection to donate boxes are selected

The Organ Donor Registrant needs to select either to donate or not to donate.

  • Send z0125 letter with photocopy of the form with the incorrect boxes highlighted

Unclear what organs and/or tissues are to be donated i.e. “All” organs and some individual organs/tissues boxes selected

The Organ Donor Registrant needs to select either:

  • “All” organs and tissues, or
  • whatever organs and tissues in the 9 boxes they have decided to list for donation

Call the Organ Donor registrant to check what organs and/or tissues they want to list.

If phone call unsuccessful, send z0125 letter with photocopy of the form with the incorrect boxes highlighted.

“No”/objection and “Remove” boxes are selected

The Organ Donor Registrant needs to select:

  • “No” to register their decision not to be a donor, or
  • “remove” if they are on the register and want to be removed

Phone call to the registrant to check whether they want to be registered as a “No” decision or be removed from the register.
