Validate or revoke a written authority to release information

Table 1: this table contains the steps for Service Officers to validate a Medicare Compensation Recovery Third party authority (MO021) or to revoke existing authority.

Step

Action

1

Check Compensation Case Management System (CCMS) + Read more ...

View documents and case notes to determine if there is an existing authorised third party. To release information to a third party, a completed MO021 form must be provided by the injured person or claimant.

Is there an existing valid Third party authority already on file?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 6

Note: all existing third party authorities are valid up to and including the date the case is finalised by Services Australia, unless otherwise revoked verbally or in writing by the injured person, claimant or authorised third party, or unless the claimant has passed away.

2

Revoking third party authority + Read more ...

If the existing TPA form is being revoked, one of the following will apply:

the injured person, claimant has advised the agency that they:

  • no longer want the existing third party, and/or
  • want to authorise a different third party

or

  • the authorised third party has advised that they no longer represent the injured person or claimant

Is this revocation or an existing authority:

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, a TPA form has been received, go to Step 4

3

Revocation of an existing authority + Read more ...

The following actions must be completed immediately:

  • If the case is active on CCMS:
    • Access the Staffware Process Client and search for the WIN
    • Double click on the WIN and open the active case in CCMS
    • Remove the authority from the claimant solicitor details fields on the CCMS work form
    • Take all required actions in the designated work step and suspend the work item
    • Go to Step 10
  • If the case is suspended on CCMS:
    • Access the Case Enquiry Screen and search for the WIN
    • Select Activate case from the drop down menu in the Wake-up command box
    • Select Adhoc enquiry or data update from the drop down menu in the Wake-up step command box
    • Select Update to launch case notes
    • Request the authority be revoked on CCMS in the blank bottom box
    • Note: use standard case notes where possible
    • Select Add to save notes - a confirmation box will appear
    • Select Yes to add case note
    • Access the Follow up work to be actioned email template. The Resources page contains a link to the email template
    • Complete all applicable fields in the template requesting the authority be revoked in CCMS
    • Email template to the Team Leader

If the revocation is:

  • in writing, go to Step 10
  • verbal, procedure ends here

4

Changes to the existing Third party authority + Read more ...

Are there any changes to the existing authorised third party (change of solicitor or change of solicitor address)?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 12

5

Form version + Read more ...

From 1 April 2021, only the current form is accepted, or the previous version signed and dated prior to 1 April 2021.

See the Resources page for the current version of the MO021 form.

Has authority been given on the current MO021 form, or on the previous version that is signed and dated before 1 April 2021?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 9

6

Validate Medicare Compensation Recovery Third party authority (MO021) form + Read more ...

The following mandatory information is required in the MO021.

Injured person's details:

  • First given name and family name
  • Date of birth, and
  • Postal address

Claimant’s details (if applicable):

  • First given name and family name, or business name (if applicable)
  • Postal address
  • If the injured person is under 14 years of age:
    • They must be on the same Medicare card as claimant
    • If they are not on the same Medicare card as claimant, supporting documentation must be attached or already be on file
  • If the injured person is over 14 years of age, supporting documentation must be attached or already be on file
  • If the injured person does not have the capacity to act on their own behalf or is deceased, supporting documentation must be attached or already be on file

Note: supporting documentation can include Power of Attorney, Court order, Last Will & Testament, Probate or Commonwealth of Australia Statutory Declaration in some circumstances.

Authorised third party details:

  • Business name or solicitor's firm name (if applicable), or
  • Third party's given name and family name (if applicable), and
  • Postal address (postal address of the business or solicitor firm is not mandatory on the authority form provided that separate correspondence is enclosed with the address details of the business or solicitor's firm)

Injured person or claimant’s declaration:

  • Full name
  • Valid signature, and
  • Date the authority is made

Authorised third party declaration

  • Full name of authorised third party
  • Valid signature of authorised third party, and
  • Date the authority is made

Has all the mandatory information been completed in the MO021 form?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, see next question

Is the form complete except for claimant signature?

  • Yes, seek verbal consent, go to Step 7
  • No, the authority is invalid, go to Step 8

7

Verbal consent for an unsigned form + Read more ...

Note: form must have already been signed by the third party to be authorised.

Has the injured person or claimant provided verbal consent for the otherwise complete and current MO021 form?

  • Yes, enter a case note then go to Step 11
  • No, the authority does not meet our requirements, go to Step 9

8

Date of injury or illness + Read more ...

Confirm if the third party authority form is for this case.

Is the date of injury correct?

  • Yes, the authority is valid
    • issue a copy of any current correspondence to the authorised third party (where required), and go to Step 11
  • No, date is missing, or a different date than is recorded on this compensation case
    • contact the injured person or claimant, and
    • verbally obtain the correct date of injury or illness over the phone
    • if contact is successful and the correct date is confirmed, the authority is valid - see above
    • if contact is unsuccessful, go to Step 9

Note: the authority may relate to a different case for the same person. If so, obtain correct case details.

9

Complete z2450 letter template + Read more ...

If the MO021 form does not meet requirements, complete the z2450 - More information is needed to process third party authority form letter template.

Note: request any missing mandatory and/or required information from the person who submitted the Medicare Compensation Recovery Third party authority (MO021) form.

Select any combination of the following options:

  • Missing details of the injured person
  • The date of injury or illness is missing or does not match our records
  • Missing details of the claimant
  • Missing details of the authorised third party
  • Supporting documentation (if required)
  • Any incomplete questions
  • Completion of the injured person’s or claimant’s declaration, including:
    • Full name of injured person or claimant
    • Signature of injured person or claimant
    • Signature of injured person is who over 14 years of age, and/or
    • Date the authority is made
  • Completion of the authorised third party’s declaration including:
    • Full name of authorised third party
    • Signature of authorised third party, and/or
    • Date the authority is made
  • The request does not meet requirements - complete MO021 form

Note: a digital or hand written signature is required on the MO021 form. Only digital signatures created using DocuSign software or the inbuilt Adobe Digital ID function (available on current forms) will be accepted.

10

Print letters + Read more ...

For an invalid TPA form:

  • Print 2 copies of the completed z2450 letter (1 to send to the claimant, the other to scan to the case)
  • Print 2 copies of the invalid MO021 form
  • Post the z2450 letter and a copy of the invalid MO021 form to the person who submitted the request

For a revocation:

  • Print a copy of the revocation letter

11

Prepare documents for scanning + Read more ...

If the third party authority is:

  • Being revoked in writing by injured person, claimant or authorised third party
  • A valid MO021 form notifying a change of the authorised solicitor or authorised third party, or
  • A new valid MO021 form that needs to be added to an existing case

Title correspondence with the WIN, scanning code (01) and your Logon ID as close as possible to the top of the first page to be scanned using a black marker only. Place correspondence in the Urgent Authority Updates/Removals scanning tray.

If the third party authority is:

  • Invalid as it is missing mandatory and/or required information or does not meet our requirements and a z2450 - More information is needed to process third party authority form letter has been issued

Title correspondence with the WIN, scanning code (18) and your Logon ID as close as possible to the top of the first page to be scanned using a black marker only. Place correspondence in the Backlog scanning tray.

12

Add a case note + Read more ...

Select the Add notes button from the CCMS Case Enquiry screen.

  • The top box shows all notes previously recorded on this case
  • In the blank bottom box, record:
    • Actions taken
    • Information requested
    • Advice provided for future requests
    • From/to whom
    • Actions required, and
    • Reasons for a decision
  • Use standard case notes where possible
  • Select Add to save notes. A confirmation box will appear
  • Select Yes to add case note

Table 2: This table contains the steps for Service Officers to obtain verbal consent while in a telephone conversation. This process must be followed for every call. If the customer has limited English, an interpreter should be offered. See Contacting an on-demand telephone interpreter (Interpreter Connect). The Resources page has a link to contact details for the Language Services team.

Step

Action

1

Confirm purpose of call + Read more ...

Confirm conversation is 'regarding (Claimant’s name)’s Medicare Compensation Recovery case that may include the injured person’s personal information'.

2

Obtain verbal consent + Read more ...

Obtain claimant’s clear verbal consent to add the other person (named) to participate in the conversation for the duration of the call.

3

Case note + Read more ...

Insert standard case note in CCMS for ‘including another person during a phone call’. Refer to case notes template on the Resources page of General Information for Medicare Compensation Recovery.
