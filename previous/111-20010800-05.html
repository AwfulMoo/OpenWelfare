QMA auto sampling model

Table 1

Item

Details

1

QMA applies accuracy data

QMA automatically calculates and adjusts the sampling rate for each staff member.

QMA selects work items based on an algorithm. The algorithm uses the staff member's accuracy data from the last 20 sampled transitions under the relevant sampling variant.

Under the auto sampling model, line managers do not need to record and adjust staff proficiency levels in ESSentials to update QMA sampling rates. This includes where new staff or existing staff start new work (not yet proficient). QMA sampling will start at 100% for these staff by default.

Line managers still need to record a staff member's skill tags in Essentials. This enables correct allocation of work items through Workload Management. This includes adding accreditation of quality checkers (Quality Management Officer skill tag).

2

QMA calculates sampling rates

Staff will transition at their current sampling rate, based on their QOL proficiency level for the work that is moving into Process Direct. Current proficiency level is based on the QOL proficiency assessment criteria, outlined in Updating proficiency for QOL:

  • It is important that annual proficiency assessments are undertaken. This will ensure staff are transitioned at the correct sampling rate
  • If annual proficiency assessments are not completed, staff will commence at 100% sampling rate by default

This only occurs at:

  • the initial rollout, where the work type is not subject to a Pilot, or
  • national rollout, where the work type is subject to a Pilot,

Staff who have not gained QOL proficiency at 'Proficient' level at the time of the rollout, or are new to the work, will automatically be sampled under the default 100% sampling rate. These staff must gain their proficiency under the auto sampling model.

For example:

  • Proficient staff will start at 2% QMA sampling rate
  • All others will default to 100% QMA sampling rate

Once the work items are quality checked, the QMA sampling rate can change. As a general guide, an accuracy result of:

  • 80% and above triggers a decrease in sampling rate by 10% (down to 2%)
  • between 70 and 80% does not change the sampling rate
  • 70% and below triggers an increase in sampling rate by 10% (up to 100%)

Example

Sampling starts at 100%.

Lucy was at 100% sampling rate for QMA:

  • Lucy completes 10 Youth Allowance Student claims in Process Direct, which have been quality checked in QMA
  • Lucy had a proficiency affecting error in 1 of the 10 claims, so the overall accuracy result is 90%
  • As a result, Lucy’s sampling rate has now decreased by 10%, from 100% to 90%.

A person's accuracy rate for the particular piece of work will recalculate every time a QMA work item is quality checked in QMA. This may then trigger a sampling movement. This happens on an ongoing basis.

Note: the system applies a QMA auto-sampling rate adjustment after the QMA check is completed and the work item is finalised. The status will be Completed. If the work item is Cancelled, no sampling rate adjustment will occur.

3

Where to find accuracy results and sampling rate

Managers and line managers can access QMA data from the QMA Dashboard - Team Leader in Process Direct. This will also apply to QMO accreditation assessments. Team Leaders may need to request access to the dashboard via ESSentials.

Service Support Officer (SSO) or Quality Development Officer (QDO):

  • Access QMA data at an organisational/site level from the QMA Dashboard - Helpdesk tile in Process Direct
  • Request access in ESSentials. See Table 3

Business/Support Teams:

  • Access national QMA data from the QMA Dashboard - Helpdesk tile in Process Direct
  • Request access in ESSentials. See Table 4

If the SSO/QDO identifies a sampling data issue, they should consult their Team Leader/Quality and Performance Manager (QPM) in the first instance.

Line managers, QPM, Business/Support Teams or team leaders can contact QMA QOL Tools if they have any concerns with the sampling data.

4

Queries about sampling rate

Staff members can view their current sampling rates using the QMA Dashboard - Service Officer in Process Direct.

QMA sampling rates for work under the Skill Tag model remain aligned to the proficiency level recorded by line managers in Essentials.

If staff members think the sampling rate is wrong, they should speak with their team leader or SSO/QDO.

If the SSO/QDO identifies a sampling data issue, they should consult their Team Leader/Quality and Performance Manager (QPM) in the first instance.

The team leader or Quality and Performance Manager can review the data and contact QMA QOL Tools if needed.

The auto sampler can be adjusted for staff receiving targeted performance support, if static sampling for a specified timeframe is needed. Team Leaders/Line Managers will need to review and remove the sampling override once the targeted performance support ceases.

For more information about auto sampling override see:

  • QMA for Team Leaders
  • QMA Sampling Override tool

5

More help

Report any issues using Business As Usual (BAU) procedures.

If QMA issues are not covered by BAU procedures, contact the team leader or SSO/QDO.

Contact QMA QOL Tools when the issue is urgent and cannot be resolved by the team leader or SSO/QDO.

If applicable, escalate the issue via normal channels (ROXY or mySupport). Provide the incident number with the escalation details so the QMA QOL team can investigate.

Access to the QMA Dashboard for SSO/QDO

Table 3: Service Support Officers (SSO) and Quality Development Officers (QDO) can request access to the QMA Dashboard - Helpdesk to support staff for their organisation unit/site to:

  • undertake performance analysis and reporting requirements
  • support staff with identified learning needs
  • use the replay function on selected work items to help with QMA Disputes

    Item

    Action

    1

    Roles and Responsibilities

    To ensure the integrity of QMA Dashboard, SSOs and QDOs need to:

    • Have an appropriate business need to access and manage the QMA Dashboard data for which they are accountable
    • Only view the information that is required to undertake my role
    • Ensure data accessed and obtained from the QMA Dashboard is only used for its intended purpose, that is for performance, quality and analysis purposes
    • Privacy and secrecy of the data must be maintained at all times
    • Reports obtained from the QMA Dashboard are to be kept in a secure location and should be disposed of accordingly, where required
    • If distributing reports, it should only be for the intended audience and should be marked “Official: Sensitive”
    • If you are no longer in the SSO/QDO position and your access is still available, you must advise QMA QOL Tools to remove the access, if it hasn’t already been actioned for you

    2

    Requesting access to the QMA Dashboard

    To request access SSO/QDO must:

    • Review the:
      • Roles and Responsibilities (above)
      • QMA Dashboard Guide
      • QMA Dispute Escalation Policy
      • Aim for Accuracy Policy
    • Complete the QMA Dashboard Access Request for SSO/QDO Webform, and
    • Request access via ESSentials for Role Name: ZC.C4.CF.QMA.SAMPLING.BO

    The QMA team will review the request and approve access as appropriate. Access to the QMA Dashboard is limited to 3 months. When it expires, SSO/QDO will need to reapply in the same way. This also includes if staff lose access due to positional changes or extension of Temporary Performance Allowance (TPA).

    Note: the QMA Team does not code the ESS role access request for staff. The SSO/QDO must complete the webform and the ESSentials role request at the same time. This avoids delays in assessing the QMA Dashboard request and/or rejections.
