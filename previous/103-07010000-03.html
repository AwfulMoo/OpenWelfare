This page contains information about issuing a Payment Summary.

Customer requests a Centrelink payment summary

Step

Action

1

Customer contacts about a Centrelink payment summary + Read more ...

Is the customer contacting in relation to their own Centrelink payment summary?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 4

2

Advise the customer they can access their Centrelink payment summary through a range of self-service channels + Read more ...

Centrelink payment summaries can be requested/accessed for the last seven financial years.

A customer will not be able to access a Centrelink payment summary through self service in the following circumstances:

  • Payment summaries requested for more than seven years in the past can only be issued manually by a Service Officer in the call centre or service centre. Go to Step 5

Where one or more of the amounts on the Centrelink payment summary is less than zero (that is, a negative amount). An 'E117TX' - Negative component amount message is displayed on the Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen. Service Officers should use the Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request Fast Note in Customer First to refer these cases to the specialist debt processing team advising that the payment summary cannot be issued. See Resources for information on locating the Fast Note.

For more information, see General information on Manual Follow-up (MFU) tax activities

For all other requests, go to Step 3

3

Does the customer have access to self service channels? + Read more ...

  • Yes, direct the customer to use one of the following channels, including:
    • their online account, through myGov
    • the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app
    • self service terminals at service centres
    • the self service line, see Phone self service for customers
    • For further information, see Self service for customers
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Third party contact in relation to Centrelink payment summaries + Read more ...

See Disclosing information to third parties.

If customer is requesting information on behalf of their partner or child, see Person Permitted to Enquire and Nominees.

If information requested is about a deceased customer, advise the contact that the executor (to the estate) can request a Centrelink payment summary by contacting Services Australia once a death has been recorded. For further information, see Requests for information after someone has died.

Procedure ends here.

5

Issuing a Centrelink payment summary by a Service Officer if customer does not/cannot access their Centrelink payment summary through self-service channels + Read more ...

Check that customer is aware and encourage the use of available self-service channels for requesting a Centrelink payment summary.

If a customer does not/cannot access self service channels then navigate to the Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen in Customer First. If the required financial year is not present a search will be required:

  • Use the Line field to search by increasing the number and press [Enter]

Is there a Centrelink payment summary line present for the relevant year?

  • Yes, in the customer's record, on Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen:
    • If the Issue Date of the selected payment summary is not blank, go to Step 6
    • Select the first instance for the relevant financial year. Once selected click on Issue (I)
    • A prompt 'Issue Group Certificate/Statement (Y/N)' appears on the screen. Type 'Y' to confirm the issue of the Centrelink payment summary and press [Continue]
    • The Centrelink payment summary will have a date of issue with today's date
    • As the issue of the Centrelink payment summary is not a determinable action it does not need to be finalised
    • Record all details on a DOC
    • The Centrelink payment summary will be issued overnight and sent to the customer's preferred letter channel
    • If the customer attends a service centre and requires a copy of their Centrelink payment summary immediately, the Service Officer can create a QTAX letter and print on the same day. This option should only be used as a last resort
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

Note: the Tax Payment Summary Detail (TXGD) screen is not available in Process Direct. Payment summary details, including amendments must be viewed in Customer First.

6

Reissuing a Centrelink payment summary + Read more ...

In Customer First, go to the Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen:

  • If the required financial year is not present a search will be required. Use the Line field to search by increasing the number and press [Enter]
  • Select the first instance for relevant financial year. Once selected, click on Reissues (R) and press [Continue]
  • A prompt 'Issue Group Certificate/Statement (Y/N)' appears on the screen. Type 'Y' to confirm the issue of the Centrelink payment summary
  • The Centrelink payment summary will have a date of issue with today's date
  • As the reissue of the Centrelink payment summary is not a determinable action it does not need to be finalised
  • Record details on a DOC
  • The Centrelink payment summary will be issued and sent to the customer's preferred letter channel
  • Procedure ends here

Note: the Tax Payment Summary Detail (TXGD) screen is not available in Process direct. Payment summary details, including amendments must be viewed in Customer First.

7

No Centrelink payment summary line present on TXGS + Read more ...

If no Centrelink payment summary line is present for the relevant year on the Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen, it may be due to the following reasons:

  • There were no reportable payments made by the agency in that financial year for the customer:
    • The Service Officer should check the reportability of the payments on the Tax Year To Date (TXYTD) screen
    • If a reportable payment is displayed on the TXYTD screen and not on the Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen contact a Service Support Officer (SSO), Technical Peer Support (TPS) or Access Support Centre (ASC) for further investigation. They will escalate to the ICT Service Desk via mySupport if further action is required
  • There is an issue with the customer record, contact a Service Support Officer (SSO), Technical Peer Support (TPS) or Access Support Centre (ASC) for further investigation. They will escalate to the ICT Service Desk via mySupport if further action is required
