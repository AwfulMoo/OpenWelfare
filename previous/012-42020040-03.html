Assessing requests for dual recognition for a medical practitioner

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Assess the request + Read more ...

Dual recognition may be processed when Services Australia receive one of the following:

  • Telephone call from the medical practitioner, go to Step 3
  • Signed written request from the medical practitioner, go to Step 3
  • Email from the medical practitioner, sent from the email address listed on the medical practitioner’s stem, go to Step 3
  • Application for recognition as a specialist or consultant physician form (HW077), go to Step 2

See Perform telephone security check for Medicare health professionals for steps to perform a security check.

When taking information over the telephone, a note is required to be added to the provider’s stem confirming:

  • date and time of telephone call
  • whom the Service Officer spoke with, and
  • describes the information obtained over the telephone

2

Process Application + Read more ...

  • To process an application see Recognition as a specialist or consultant physician for Medicare

Once the application is processed, go to Step 3.

3

Check Provider Directory System (PDS) + Read more ...

Open PDS:

  • select Provider
  • select View
  • select Detailed Search
  • in the Family name field key the first 3 letters of the last name followed by a %
  • in the Given name field key the first 3 letters of the first name followed by a %
  • key date of birth in Date of Birth field
  • select Search

A list of health professionals with a matching name will display.

Select View for the matching medical practitioner.

4

Check specialty codes + Read more ...

Scroll to the bottom of the provider stem record to view the specialty codes for the medical practitioner.

Check if the PDS stem has any of the following codes registered:

  • Specialist and consultant physician specialty codes - 001-099 and 801-899, and
  • General practitioner specialty codes - 130, 132,530, 532 or 540

Medical practitioner has a specialist or consultant physician specialty code and a general practitioner specialty code:

  • Yes, eligible for dual recognition, go to Step 5
  • No, does the medical practitioner have a specialist and/or consultant physician specialty code with a 104 specialty code:
    • Yes, eligible for dual recognition, go to Step 5
    • No, not eligible for dual recognition, go to Step 6

5

Dual recognition request type + Read more ...

Request received for dual recognition via:

  • Telephone call, see Table 2
  • Signed written request, see Table 2
  • Email from the medical practitioner, sent from the email address as listed on the stem, see Table 2
  • Application for recognition as a specialist or consultant physician form (HW077), see Table 3

6

Return documentation + Read more ...

Where the request for dual recognition is received via a telephone call, advise the medical practitioner they do not meet the eligibility criteria and dual recognition will not be applied to their provider record.

A security check is required to be performed. See Perform telephone security check for Medicare health professionals for steps to perform a security check

When taking information over the telephone, a note is required to be added to the provider stem with the following:

  • confirming date and time of telephone call,
  • whom the Service Officer spoke with, and
  • describing the information obtained over the telephone

Where the request for dual recognition is received via a written request or email, select letter template Z2305 Provider Registration - Reject, and:

  • Complete appropriate fields of letter template
  • Print letter
  • Save letter as a PDF
  • Upload letter to PaNDA
  • In PaNDA, ensure the Surname First Name, Provider Number and Case ID fields are completed and correct
  • Add any appropriate notes to the PaNDA file
  • Select Complete
  • Select Save
  • Send letter

Where the Application for recognition as a specialist or consultant form (HW007) is received, the respective team processes the application. As the medical practitioner is not eligible for dual recognition, no further action is required.

Check specialty codes + Read more ...

Scroll to the bottom of the provider stem record to view the specialty codes for the medical practitioner.

Is the 165 specialty code present?

  • Yes, continue
  • No, procedure ends here

Determine if the medical practitioner has continued to meet the eligibility criteria to have the dual recognition specialty code on the provider record:

  • Specialist and consultant physician specialty codes - 001-099 and 801-899, and
  • General practitioner specialty codes - 130, 132,530, 532 and/or 540, or
  • Other Medical practitioner (specialty code 104), or
  • MedicarePlus OMPs Ongoing (specialty code 189)

Medical practitioner has a specialist or consultant physician specialty code and working in a general practitioner capacity:

  • Yes, remains eligible for dual recognition, procedure ends here
  • No, medical practitioner is not eligible for dual recognition, go to Step 5

5

Determine dual recognition end date + Read more ...

The end date used depends on how the removal request is received.

If Services Australia receive the removal request via:

  • Telephone call from the medical practitioner:
    • dual recognition removal is the date of the telephone call
  • Signed written request from the medical practitioner:
    • dual recognition end date is the date requested in the letter or the date of lodgement of the letter
  • Email from the medical practitioner:
    • dual recognition end date is the date requested in the email or the date of lodgement of the email
  • Removal of specialist or consultant physician recognition under sections 3DA, 3DC or 3E of the Health Insurance Act 1973. Does the medical practitioner have an additional specialist or consultant physician recognition on PDS:
    • Where they have additional open specialty codes as a specialist or consultant physician, no requirement to close the 165 specialty code
    • Where they are being end dated and there is no additional open specialist or consultant physician specialty codes, end date the 165 specialty code
