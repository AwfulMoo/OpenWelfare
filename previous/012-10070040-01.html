Indigenous Health Incentive (IHI) patient registration and withdrawal for Practice Incentives Program (PIP) 012-10070040

Expand all


  • Background
  • Process
  • Resources

This document outlines the process for registering or withdrawing patients from the IHI.

Aims of the Incentive

The PIP IHI aims to support general practices and Indigenous health services to provide better health care for Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander patients, including best practice management of chronic disease.

Eligible practices can register their patients for the Indigenous Health Incentive. Practices must register patients for the IHI each calendar year.

The patient registration payment is paid once per patient, per calendar year for patients registered between 1 January and 31 October. If a patient is registered for the first time in November or December of a year, they will be registered from 1 January to 31 December of that year, and for the following year. Practices will receive 1 patient registration payment of $250 in the February payment quarter of the following year.

Patient registrations

A practice can register their Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander Australian patients using 1 of the below:

  • HPOS (Health Professional Online Services), or
  • PIP IHI patient registration and consent (IP017) form

A practice can send an electronic version of the form created using their practice software. Accept these forms when:

  • The form is current
  • Its structure is the same
  • The declaration refers to Services Australia

If the form is missing any of these details, return the form to the practice. If not sure whether to accept the form, escalate to the Program Support Officer (PSO) via the Incentives Escalation Database.

When a practice uses HPOS, they do not need to send a copy of the form. However, they must keep a copy of the signed patient consent for 6 years for compliance and audit purposes.

Patient withdrawals

Patients can withdraw from the PIP IHI at any time. To withdraw they must complete the PIP Indigenous Health Incentive patient withdrawal of consent (IP029) form.

Practices can request a patient withdrawal by sending a message or email using HPOS. The request should include:

  • the patient’s name, and
  • Medicare number

They do not need to complete the IP029 form.

Note: do not use the IP029 form for a patient who wants to change their usual practice. Patients who want to change their usual practice can complete the Practice Incentives Program Indigenous Health Incentive patient registration and consent form with the new practice details.

PBS Co-payment Measure

From 1 July 2021, patient registrations for the Closing the Gap (CTG) Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) Co-payment Measure are no longer managed through the PIP IHI.

Practices must register eligible patients for the PBS Co-payment Measure online through Health Professionals Online Services (HPOS) using their Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account.

The Resources page contains links to letters and useful websites. There are also details about consent dates and signing consent forms and guidelines about warning messages.
