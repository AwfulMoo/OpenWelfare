Confirming partner details and answering TAC enquiries

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Check action needed + Read more ...

If the Service Officer:

  • has received a work item, and a Temporary Access Code (TAC) was:
    • issued, this will appear as a Partner Confirmation task within the claim, go to Step 2
    • not issued, a Partner details (MOD P) form will have been requested and must be returned as part of the claim. Continue with the claim. Procedure ends here
  • is running an ACC, go to Step 3
  • is helping the customer or their partner with an enquiry about the TAC or the partner confirmation process, go to Step 3
  • has been allocated a Request for Information review work item to follow up the return of the MOD P, see Assessing the MOD P. Procedure ends here
  • has received a MOD P, see Assessing the MOD P. Procedure ends here
  • has had contact with the partner, who has advised they are not in a relationship with the customer, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • is processing a paper claim form and the form has not been signed by the partner where required, go to Step 5

2

TAC was issued + Read more ...

If the partner has:

  • confirmed the relationship, continue with the claim. Procedure ends here.
  • not confirmed the relationship, make one genuine attempt to contact the partner. If contact was:
    • successful, see Step 1 in Table 2
    • not successful, see Step 2 in Table 3

3

ACC claim or customer/partner enquiry + Read more ...

If a TAC was issued, a Partner Confirmation task will present in the claim.

Was a TAC issued to allow the partner to confirm the relationship details online?

  • Yes, and the customer's partner:
    • can use the TAC, go to Step 4
    • cannot use the TAC, or chooses not to use online services, go to Step 6
  • No, check if confirmation from the partner is required. If partner confirmation is:
    • required, go to Step 5
    • not required, continue with the claim. Procedure ends here

4

Partner to confirm relationship online + Read more ...

Tell the customer/partner that the partner needs to go to servicesaustralia.gov.au and confirm the partnered relationship:

  • enter 'Confirm your relationship status' into the Search bar
  • select Confirm your relationship status when making a claim link from the results list
  • select the link to the Partner Confirmation Logon service
  • on the Partner Confirmation Logon page, enter the 7 digit Reference Number then the 10 letter upper and lower case Access Code provided in the customer's claim. Note: this Reference Number is not the customer or partner’s Customer Reference Number (CRN)

If the partner is unable to follow the above steps, give the Partner Confirmation Logon page URL to access.

Service Officers can view the partner’s details via the Digital Partner Confirmation (DMODP) tile in Process Direct.

If contact with the customer/partner is:

  • face-to-face. the TAC advice can be printed and handed to the customer/partner
  • by phone
    • tell them the TAC reference number, access code and instructions, or
    • print and mail the TAC advice to the customer. Note: this may delay finalisation of the claim

If running an ACC, continue the ACC workflow.

Procedure ends here.

5

No TAC issued - verbally confirm the relationship details with the partner + Read more ...

If the partner is available, their details can be confirmed verbally.

If a MOD P is a required document for the claim, Service Officers do not need to contact the partner. The MOD P collects all partner details. As both the customer and partner are required to sign the MOD P, there is no need for further confirmation of partner details.

Has the partner contacted or can they confirm their details verbally?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, and the claim is:
    • assessed under CCM, issue a Partner details (MOD P) form to the claimant using the Request and Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure
    • not assessed under CCM, issue a (MOD P) form to the claimant using the Request for Information (RFI) guided procedure

When the MOD P is returned or due to be returned, see Assessing the MOD P.

Procedure ends here.

6

TAC issued - customer unable to use TAC or online services + Read more ...

Service Officers must check if partner confirmation is required.

If partner confirmation is:

  • required, and the partner is
    • available, see Step 1 in Table 2
    • not available, contact the customer’s partner to verbally confirm the relationship. Go to Step 7
  • not required, set the Partner Confirmation task to Not Required. If completing an ACC, continue with the claim. Procedure ends here

7

Partner confirmation is required - contact the customer’s partner + Read more ...

Was contact with the partner successful?

  • Yes, to complete verbal confirmation, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Partner not using a TAC to confirm the relationship details + Read more ...

  • Set the Partner Confirmation task to Not Required. See Circumstance Change Monitor
  • Issue a Partner details (MOD P) form to the claimant. If the claim:
    • is assessed under CCM, use the Request and Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure issue a MOD P
    • is not assessed under CCM, use the Request for Information (RFI) guided procedure to issue a MOD P

Note: confirmation of the relationship by both the customer and partner is required to update and link records. If there is documented family and domestic violence on the customer or partner’s record, see Family and domestic violence.

When the MOD P is returned or due to be returned, see Assessing the MOD P.

Procedure ends here.

Confirming partner details - verbal confirmation

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Read the Privacy Statement and Declaration of false and misleading information + Read more ...

Tell the partner the agency has received information about their relationship status which needs to be confirmed.

Read the following statements to the partner before confirming that the details provided in the claim are accurate.

Privacy and your personal information

'Your personal information is protected by law (including the Privacy Act 1988) and is collected by the Australian Government agency Services Australia for the assessment and administration of payments and services. Your information may be used by the agency, or given to other parties where you have agreed to that, or where it is required or authorised by law (including for the purpose of research or conducting investigations). You can get more information about the way in which the agency will manage your personal information, including our privacy policy, at www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/individuals/privacy.'

Has the partner confirmed the relationship details?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, DOC the partner's record with details of the discussion, confirming the privacy statement and declaration has been read. See Step 1 in Table 3

2

Partner confirms the relationship details + Read more ...

Confirm that the partner’s details provided in the online claim are correct:

Read the following statement to the partner:

Declaration about false and misleading information

'Do you declare that the information you have provided is complete and correct and understand that giving false or misleading information is a serious offence?

  • Use the Verbal Confirmation of Relationship Fast Note. Select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Verbal Confirmation of Relationship DOC to:
  • DOC the partner’s record with the details of the discussion, confirming:
    • the relationship with the customer has been verbally confirmed with the partner
    • the partner details are accurate and correct, and
    • the privacy statement and declaration has been read to the partner
      Note: the DOC must not include that confirmation of relationship details was obtained as part of the claimant’s online claim.
  • Annotate the Progress of Claim DOC on the claimant’s record, confirming:
    • the relationship has been verbally confirmed with the partner
    • their details are accurate and correct, and
    • the privacy statement and declaration has been read to the partner
  • Make sure the Confirmation of relationship details task status is changed to Provided. This will allow the customer to proceed and submit their claim

If running an ACC, proceed with the assessment of the claim.

Procedure ends here.

Partner does not confirm the relationship

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Ask the Family and Domestic Violence Risk Identification Question (RIQ) + Read more ...

As the partner has not confirmed they are in a relationship with the customer, it is possible they may have some concerns for their safety.

Ask the partner the Risk Identification Question if there are no exceptions. Follow processes in the Risk Identification and Referral (RIR) Model.

If the partner is distressed or there are concerns about their immediate wellbeing or safety, follow the risk identification and referral process for an immediate referral to a social worker.

Has the partner indicated they have concerns for their safety or their family’s safety?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Check for family and domestic violence concerns - unable to contact partner + Read more ...

If the partner did not confirm the relationship when the TAC was issued and contact with the partner is unsuccessful, check the partner’s record for any information that may indicate family and domestic violence, such as:

  • recent social work intervention
  • online Document Records, such as the FDV Concern Identified (FDVCI) Fast Note
  • display on Access documents
  • relevant payments, such as a Crisis Payment for family and domestic violence
  • documents lodged by a customer (uploaded or scanned). For example, copy of a restraining order (for example, AVO, ADVO, DVO), legal documentation, relevant medical evidence.

Have family and domestic violence/safety concerns been identified?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No,
    • Contact the customer to discuss the conflicting information received from their alleged partner, and the need for a MoC assessment to determine if a MoC relationship exists
    • DOC both customer and partner records with this information
    • Issue the appropriate form and letter to the customer and partner
    • When forms are returned, see Confirming the need for a Member of a Couple (MoC) assessment
    • Procedure ends here

3

No family and domestic violence issues identified – partner contact + Read more ...

The customer and alleged partner will need to complete a Relationship Details form (SS284 or SS293) because the information they have provided about their relationship conflicts. A MoC assessment is required to determine if a MoC relationship exists.

  • Tell the alleged partner of the requirement to complete the Relationship Details form and why the form is required
  • Contact the customer to discuss the conflicting information received from their alleged partner, and the need to complete a Relationship Details form so a MoC assessment can be undertaken
  • DOC the customer and alleged partner records with this information
  • Issue the appropriate form and letter to the customer and alleged partner

When forms are returned, see Confirming the need for a Member of a Couple (MoC) assessment. Procedure ends here.

4

Family and domestic violence issues identified – partner contact + Read more ...

The alleged partner will need to complete a Relationship Details form (SS284 or SS293) because the information they have provided about their relationship conflicts with the information from the customer. A MoC assessment is required to determine if a MoC relationship exists.

  • Issue the appropriate form and letter to the alleged partner only. Do not send the form to the customer/claimant
  • Make sure the alleged partner understands that the customer will receive notification of the MoC decision. This will allow them time to prepare and ensure their safety before this occurs
  • If the alleged partner is
    • getting income support, both they and the customer/claimant will receive notification of the MoC decision
    • not getting income support, a letter with the decision or claim outcome will only be sent to the customer
  • DOC this information on the alleged partner’s record only
  • The MoC assessment may delay finalisation of the customer’s claim or processing of the MOD P
  • If the customer calls about the status of their claim/update:
    • tell them their claim/update is being assessed and will be finalised as soon as possible
    • do not disclose that an assessment of the relationship is underway
    • do not disclose that the alleged partner has safety concerns

When returned, see Confirming the need for a MoC assessment. Procedure ends here.

5

Family and domestic violence issues identified - unable to contact partner + Read more ...

The partner will need to complete a Relationship Details form (SS284 or SS293) because the information provided about their relationship conflicts with the information the customer has provided and a MoC assessment is required to determine if a MoC relationship exists.

  • Issue the appropriate form and letter to the partner only. Do not send the form to the customer/claimant
  • DOC this information on the partner’s record only
  • The MoC assessment may delay finalisation of the customer’s claim or processing of the MOD P
  • If the customer calls about the status of their claim/update:
    • tell them their claim/update is being assessed and will be finalised as soon as possible
    • do not disclose that an assessment of the relationship is underway
    • do not disclose that the alleged partner has safety concerns

When returned, see Confirming the need for a MoC assessment.
