Staff must not use the myGov Staff Linking Application to issue a linking code to themselves, their family, friends or colleagues.

For Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment, before issuing a myGov linking code, staff must make sure the customer has met the identity requirements. For self-isolation, quarantine or caring periods that start

on or after 18 January 2022, see Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment – Assisted Customer Claims

on or before 17 January 2022, see Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment in Customer First

On this page:

Centrelink Service Officer

Medicare Service Officer

Child Support Service Officer

Centrelink Service Officer

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Open the myGov Staff Linking Application + Read more ...

Only give the myGov linking code directly to the customer, not to a third party.

Follow instructions in myGov Linking Application. From the Intranet page, select myGov Staff Linking Application

Registration Information page

Read the registration information to the customer.

Offer the customer to link the following accounts in one transaction:

  • Centrelink
  • Child Support, and/or
  • Medicare

If they do not have an online account with a member service, the linking code will create one.

Customers must satisfy Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO) by providing:

  • a valid program ID, such as:
    • Centrelink Customer Reference Number
    • Child Support Customer Reference Number, or
    • Medicare card number
  • personal information relating to that program ID, such as:
    • name
    • date of birth
    • address
  • approved identity documents (original documents) or by answering further questions to satisfy PoRO

Customers satisfy PoRO by answering questions about information they have previously provided.

Verify the details separately for:

  • Centrelink
  • Child Support, and/or
  • Medicare services

Some questions may be repeated.

Note: If the Service Officer has any concerns about a customer's authority to enquire, they should request to see photo identification or ask about further PoRO questions until they are satisfied.

Select Continue to proceed.

2

Program selection page + Read more ...

Read the information to the customer. Ask what member services they would like to link.

Select the box beside each member service the customer wants to link. Enter the program ID for each:

  • Centrelink Customer Reference Number
  • Child Support Customer Reference Number, and/or
  • Medicare card number
    Medicare customers are also required to enter their Individual Reference Number in myGov

When selecting more than 1 member service to link, the customer:

  • must answer PoRO questions for each selected program
  • will get 1 linking code

When they enter the linking code in myGov, it will link all selected member services at once.

Select Continue to proceed.

3

Program summary page + Read more ...

A message will display. If it says the customer:

  • already has access to online services, tell the customer they already have access. Check if the customer wants to continue. If they agree:
    • they must complete the process again by answering the PoRO questions, and
    • their existing online account will be overwritten
  • has a locked account, they cannot continue with linking that program. Investigate the locked access and reset if appropriate
  • is unable to access online services for any program. They must provide approved photo ID or more details to record on their record. Update the customer's record before registering for that program

Thoroughly investigate the locked or unable to access account. If required, refer to a support officer.

  • select Continue to proceed. Go to Step 4
  • select Cancel to stop the process. Procedure ends here

4

Customer information page + Read more ...

Customer information will display for each member service (program) selected for linking.

Only once the initial PoRO has been correctly answered, confirm the information shown is correct.

If the customer has a placeholder address (C/O AUTO CTAT), this must be updated as part of this process to prevent future online claim issues. See Updating address details.

Check with the customer that their name and date of birth on their myGov account matches the name and date of birth shown in the myGov Linking Application.

A Centrelink record will not successfully link to their myGov account if their name and date of birth do not match.

If there is a mismatch, the customer may need to:

  • contact the original linked member service (this will be visible on their myGov account under Account Settings) to have their details corrected, or
  • provide relevant identity document/s to Centrelink before their details are updated, refer to Changing the Legal name of customers or their children

Is customer contact in person and can they provide photo ID to Fast track the process?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

If the customer is unable to satisfy initial PoRO do not proceed.

5

Centrelink service centre staff + Read more ...

Fast track registration page

Fast track the customer if they attend face to face within service centres or via Silver Service and they provide an approved photographic identity document (original document).

Approved photographic identity document face to face (original documents):

  • Australian Driver Licence
  • Valid Australian Passport (or expired up to 3 years)
  • Shooters or Firearm Licence
  • Security guard/crowd control photo Licence
  • Foreign Passport

Other accepted photographic identity documents

  • Proof of Age card,
  • Immigration card,
  • Secondary student photo ID

Note: to use these documents staff must follow the Shooters licence workaround, go to Step 10.

Check the identity document to make sure the:

  • details on the myGov Linking Application match those on the document
  • the photo matches the facial profile of the customer

Can the customer provide a photographic identity document to Fast track the process?

  • Yes, check the initial PoRO Confirmed? box displays, and select ‘Yes’ to providing photo ID to Fast track. Then select Continue to proceed. Go to Step 9
  • No, select No. Go to Step 6

6

Questions and answers (Q&A) Proof of record ownership page + Read more ...

Read the message located on the screen to the customer.

The system will:

  • ask questions about information on their record
  • base the questions on information the customer has previously provided

Do not give answers to the customer for the questions being asked and do not select answers to questions that haven’t been asked, or answered incorrectly.

Verify the details separately for their:

  • Centrelink
  • Child Support, and/or
  • Medicare services

Some questions may repeat.

Ask the questions starting from the top. The system arranges the questions in order to achieve the active registration as quickly as possible.

For each question the customer answers correctly:

  • Select Yes in the Confirmed? column
  • Select the Calculate Progress to see if the customer has answered enough questions to have full access to services.
  • Select Ask more questions to display a full list of the customer's:
  • previously recorded PoRO, and
  • identity items

Note: if using Receipt number issued within the last eight weeks, the receipt number from the current call is not to be used.

Always ask, do not tell.

Under no circumstances should a Service Officer:

  • ask leading questions
  • prompt the customer
  • give a customer hints
  • give a customer answers to the PoRO questions
  • select answers to questions that haven’t been asked

This:

  • avoids disclosure of personal information
  • avoids breaches of privacy, and
  • prevents intertwining customer records/data

Has the customer answered enough questions to have full access to services?

  • Yes,
    • select Continue
    • Once PoRO is complete for each program selecting or linking, go to Step 11
  • No,
    • if customer contact is over the phone, go to Step 7
    • if customer contact is face to face, go to Step 10

7

Contact over the phone + Read more ...

When the customer is unable to successfully complete the Q&A PoRO process:

  • approved identity documents must be DVS verified in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard in Process Direct in order to undertake the Fast track process
  • attempt to verify more than one document, if the first attempt fails

Note: this only applies where fields for DVS verifiable Commencement of Identity (Col) and Primary Use in Community (PUiC) identity documents are available.

Before coding approved identity documents in the customer's records, the staff member should:

  • ensure the record being accessed belongs to the correct person
  • ask the person to provide personal details that can be checked against the customer record by asking sufficient authentication questions to confirm PoRO
  • vary the questions

Approved identity documents are Col or PUiC identity documents

This does not include:

  • student cards
  • Proof of age cards or
  • Foreign passports
  • See Coding identity documents

Check the identity document details match those on the customer’s record.

Does the customer have a COI or PUiC document that can be coded in the relevant field in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No:
    • but the customer has verified Col and PUiC identity documents, go to Step 9
    • otherwise, go to Step 11

8

Code and verify the approved identity document + Read more ...

Code and verify the approved identity document in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard in Process Direct.

Did the customer's identity document verify?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, Go to Step 11

9

Verified CoI and PUiC identity documents + Read more ...

Follow the process below when the customer has verified CoI and PUiC identity documents and failed to successfully complete the Q&A PoRO process.

To proceed, the customer must correctly confirm their:

  • full name
  • DOB
  • address, and
  • program ID for all services selected, as an example CRN

If the customer, without prompting, can confirm:

  • what identity documents they provided, and
  • actual details of their verified Col or PUiC identity document from the Identity Confirmation Dashboard, go to Step 10

An example, their driver licence number or the actual details required to verify the document within the Identity Confirmation Dashboard.

Where the customer fails to correctly confirm their details of a verified Col or PUiC identity document, go to Step 11.

Note: when attempting or issuing a linking code via the method outlined within Step 9, Service Officers must record the details in a DOC on the customer’s record.

10

Fast track registration page + Read more ...

Code the details in the Linking application if:

  • an approved photographic identity document such as an Australian driver licence or Australian passport and,
  • the customer is face to face or,
  • approved identity documents verified in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard

Follow the process below for all other approved photo identity documents provided face to face. These are:

  • Proof of Age card (POA) (Service Centre staff only)
  • Immigration card
  • Secondary student photo ID (Service Centre staff only)

Record the following identity documents by selecting ‘Shooters licence’ as per the details below.

Other accepted photographic identity documents do not need to be verified or coded on the Identity Confirmation Dashboard.

  • Government issued POA card
    • select the Shooters-licence field
    • key 'POA' and the card number (for example, POA912345)
  • Department of Immigration issued ImmiCard
    • select the Shooters-licence field
    • key 'IM' and the card number (for example, IMPRE123456 or IMEIS123456)
  • Valid shooter or firearm licence
    • select the Shooters-licence field
    • key 'SL' and the licence number (for example, SL123456)
  • Secondary student photo identification card (under 18 years only)
    • select the Shooters-licence field
    • key 'SI' and the student number or expiry date (for example, SI31122016)

If the customer provided an approved identity document that was DVS verified through the Identity Confirmation Dashboard:

Approved identity document:

  • For Birth certificates (BC), Australian citizenship by descent certificate (RD), Australian citizenship certificate (AC), Australian marriage certificates (MC), Australian visas (VI), Certificate of identity(CI), Change of name certificate (NC), Department of Immigration issued ImmiCard (IM):
    • select the Shooters-licence field
    • key in the abbreviated document name and the document details. For example, Birth Certificate will be BC784921B and use expiry date 31/12/current year

Note: only use the Change of name certificate (NC) if the name on the certificate is reflected on the customer's record.

Select Continue to proceed. Go to Step 11.

11

Customer did not gain full access to the service/s + Read more ...

If the customer fails to gain access for the service/s they are requesting, escalation to resolve is the responsibility of that program.

Advise the customer before they can receive a linking code for the program, they must contact:

  • Medicare, or
  • Child Support to provide additional details

See the Resources page for contact details.

Note: if the customer is requesting a Medicare linking code to access their COVID-19 digital certificate, see Step 7 in Table 2 and contact Medicare Document Verification line.

For Centrelink customers

Advise the customer before they can receive a linking code, they must:

  • provide an approved photographic identity document in person at a Centrelink Service Centre, or
  • contact at another time with their correct details and approved identity document to obtain a linking code

Do not provide the customer any information about the details displayed on their record or in the myGov Linking Application.

Consider if the customer meets grounds to have their claim for payment completed by the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) process.

Where the customer has achieved full access for any member service, select Continue: a linking code will generate for any program where they have achieved full access for. Go to Step 12.

If the customer has not achieved full access to any member services, procedure ends here.

Note: if the person is suspected of fraudulently attempting to access another person's record, the Service Officer must report the suspected fraud.

This is the last chance to make any changes to the customer's registration.

Select Back to change details.

To generate a linking code and create online accounts for each selected member service (program), select Generate Linking Code.

Linking code page + Read more ...

Ask the customer to confirm they have the linking code correct.

Do not:

  • write down and give the linking code to customers, or
  • give the linking code to a third party, including nominee or if verbal permissions have been given by the customer

  • the linking code is a single use code to link their services via myGov in one online transaction
  • the linking code expires in 14 days from date of issue. Customers can get a new code should this occur
  • how to link services to their myGov account.

Note: Service Officers must advise the customer when accessing their myGov account and linking their Medicare record, that the 'Agency Reference Number' includes both their Medicare Card Number and their Individual Reference Number (IRN).

Document Verification Service (DVS) and customer consent + Read more ...

Use the DVS to verify identity documents where:

  • The customer is unable to successfully complete the Q&A PoRO process
  • The customer is at a service centre/face to face and is unable to provide approved photographic identity document
  • Staff are unable to undertake a facial comparison of the customer and their photographic identity document (for telephony staff); or
  • Staff receive an error message via the myGov Linking Application

The consent statement must be read to the customer:

'Do you understand that identification documents you provide to Services Australia will be checked with the issuing authority to confirm validity. The documents are subject to agency compliance and audit processes. In providing these documents, you consent to the agency validating the documents with the issuing authority.'

Approved Identity documents which can be verified in DVS are:

  • Australian driver licence (DL)
  • Australian Passport (current or expired less than 3 years) (AP)
  • Australian Birth certificate (BC)
  • Australian citizenship by descent certificate (RD)
  • Australian citizenship certificate (AC)
  • Australian marriage certificate (MC)
  • Australian visa (VI)
  • Certificate of identity (CI)
  • Change of name certificate (NC)
  • Department of Immigration issued ImmiCard (IM)

Note: only use the Change of name certificate (NC) if the name of the certificate is reflected on the customer's record.

Check the identity document details match those on the customer’s name and date of birth match those on the customer’s record.

If the staff member has access to the DVS tool and the DVS Processing Form, they should attempt to verify a DVS verifiable identity document.

If the staff member does not have access to the DVS tool they should contact Medicare Document Verification.

Attempt to verify more than one document, if the first document fails verification.

Did the customer give consent and their identity documents verify?

  • Yes, for Telephony and Face to Face staff, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

Thoroughly investigate the message displayed. See Access to Child Support self service - set up, checking and updates. If needed, refer to support officer.

  • Select Continue to proceed. Go to Step 4
  • Select Cancel to stop the process. Procedure ends here

4

Customer information page + Read more ...

Customer information will display for each member service (program) selected for linking.

The customer must be able to correctly confirm their name, date of birth and address (initial PoRO) to proceed with issuing a linking code.

Only once the initial PoRO has been correctly answered, confirm the details shown are correct, by selecting the check boxes.

If the Centrelink address is a placeholder address (C/O AUTO CTAT), this must be updated. Please refer the customer to a Centrelink service officer after issuing the linking code. See Resources for contact details.

If providing linking codes for Centrelink and Medicare, check with the customer that their name and date of birth on their myGov account matches the name and date of birth shown in the myGov Linking Application.

If there is a mismatch, the customer may need to contact the original linked member service (this will be visible on their myGov account under Account Settings) to have their details corrected.

As customer contact does not occur in person, the Fast track registration process is not an available option.

Select No to Fast track and proceed. Go to Step 5.

5

Questions and answers (Q&A) Proof of record ownership page + Read more ...

Read the information to the customer

The system will ask questions about information on their record. The system will base the questions on information the customer has previously provided.

Do not give answers to the customer for the questions being asked and do not select answers to questions that haven’t been asked, or answered incorrectly.

Verify the details separately for their:

  • Centrelink
  • Child Support, and/or
  • Medicare services

Some questions may repeat.

Ask the question starting from the top. The system arranges the questions in order to achieve the active registration as quickly as possible.

For each question the customer answers correctly:

  • Select Yes in the Confirmed? column
  • Select the Calculate Progress to see if the customer has answered enough questions to have full access to services
  • Select Ask more questions to display a full list of the customer's:
  • previously recorded PoRO, and
  • identity items

Note: if using Receipt number issued within the last eight weeks, the receipt number from the current call is not to be used.

Always ask, do not tell.

Under no circumstances should a Service Officer:

  • ask leading questions
  • prompt the customer
  • give a customer hints
  • give a customer answers to the PoRO questions
  • select answers to questions that haven’t been asked

This:

  • avoids disclosure of personal information
  • avoids breaches of privacy, and
  • prevents intertwining customer records/data

Has the customer answered enough questions to have full access to services?

  • Yes,
    • select Continue
    • once PoRO is complete for each program selecting or linking, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6
