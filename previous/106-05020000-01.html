This document outlines the procedures that detail the various types of waiting periods a customer may need to serve depending on the type of income support payment they receive.

Waiting periods

Once a customer is qualified for an income support payment then certain factors may affect the payability of the payment. Waiting periods may be applied which delay the commencement of payment or reduce the amount of payment for a defined period. Waiting periods and preclusion periods are generally applied because customers are expected to use their own funds to support themselves before receiving government support. There are a number of waiting periods that can be applied to different payments.

If a customer is affected by a waiting period for an income support payment, they may wish to test their eligibility for a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC).

Referral to Employment Services Providers

Job seekers claiming an income support payment with mutual obligation requirements are Fully Eligible Participant (FEP) job seekers and must be referred to an Employment Services Provider. Job seekers who are required to serve waiting periods other than an Ordinary Waiting Period (OWP), are normally eligible for employment services assistance as a voluntary participant. They should be encouraged to participate with providers who can help them with access to self-help facilities, jobsearch.gov.au and other services.

Note: job seekers serving a waiting period and participating voluntarily do not have a 6 month restriction on access to employment services by jobactive.

The Resources page contains information on how the waiting periods were affected by the coronavirus (COVID-19).
