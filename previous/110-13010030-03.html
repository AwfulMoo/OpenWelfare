Customer conversation and determining employment situation

Step

Action

1

Locate match data + Read more ...

Locate the match data to discuss the risk and continue the conversation with customer.

View the match data via the Match Data (IRMD) screen in the Integrated Review System (IRS).

Anomaly or mismatch

If there is a discrepancy between the customer's details and the match data:

  • Advise the customer that if the anomaly/mismatch is confirmed, the intervention will be finalised. If it is not confirmed, more information may be needed from them
  • Refer the case to the Service Desk, see Table 2

2

Determine employment situation + Read more ...

Use the information in the match data to start the discussions and to get an understanding of the customer and/or partner's employment situation.

  • A full investigation on the customer's record will help with the discussions
  • Use the Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen to check if the customer has been declaring earnings

The customer may have declared the matched employer under another name, e.g. a trading name.

For help with questions to use, see the Resources page for questioning techniques.

  • Do not limit enquiries to just the employer(s) from the match data. Include all relevant employers for the scope period
  • If the customer has a partner who is working, information about the partner's employer will be required

3

Determine Scope of Intervention + Read more ...

Scope start date:

  • Tax File Number Declaration Form (TDF)
    • If the customer signed a TDF due to starting new employment
      the date the employment started
    • If a TDF was signed for any other reason and the customer has been reporting employment income for that employer (e.g. they have been reporting, but a new owner of the business needed the customer to sign a new TDF
      the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) just before the TDF Sign Date
  • Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DE)
    the EPSD just before the Event Date
  • Earnings Under Declaration (EUD)
    the EPSD just before the Match Load Date

If there are multiple compliance triggers, use the earliest scope start date.

Scope end date:

  • the customer's income support payment Date Paid to (DPT) when deciding the scope period, or
  • the end date of the entitlement period (EPED) in which employment ceased if the customer is no longer working

What needs to be updated:

  • employers listed in the compliance trigger IRMD screen)
  • unverified employers on EANS screen (unmatched employers)
  • previously undisclosed employers (divulged during the conversation)

When a customer and partner both have interventions, assess them under the same scope.

Record scope details on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for the 'Initial customer discussion - ODB or IRSRFI contact made and customer has time to discuss the intervention' DOC template.

4

Notification obligations + Read more ...

Advise the customer their notification obligations.

This relates to how long a customer has to notify a change in their circumstances.

5

Customer's current employment situation + Read more ...

Verify with the customer if they are:

  • future - employed but not started yet, go to Step 6
  • not employed, go to Step 7
  • currently employed, go to Step 10

6

Future - employed but not started yet + Read more ...

If the customer has advised they have not started work, check when and if the customer and/or partner is expected to start work by asking:

  • 'When do you expect to start work?'
  • 'Do you expect to start work before you lodge your next fortnightly payment?'

If the customer is going to start work before their next fortnightly payment:

  • they must be placed on fortnightly lodgement
  • give the education message

If the customer has not started work:

  • advise them that they may be contacted in future to discuss their employment
  • check there are no wage details to assess
  • give the education message

After giving the education message, the intervention can be finalised as there are no wage details to assess.

See Table 5.

7

Not employed + Read more ...

If the customer advises they are not employed, or they do not recognise the employer in the match data, advise them that information has been received to indicate they have possibly started work.

Consider the following discussion points:

  • The agency has received information from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) that a Tax File Number Declaration Form (TDF) has been signed
  • The agency has received information from the Department of Education, Skills and Employment that the customer had gained work via Workforce Australia
  • The agency has received information that they or their partner started work with (Employer)?
  • Did they or their partner sign this Tax File Number Declaration Form/accept a position with Workforce Australia?

Check the EANS screen to see if the customer has been declaring the matched employer under another name, e.g. a trading name.

If the customer states they are not working and they have not signed a TDF, check if there is an anomaly or mismatch.

Note: a customer or their partner can sign a TDF without starting work, especially if they have signed with an employment agency.

If the customer or their partner:

  • did not start work, go to Step 8
  • started work but stopped, go to Step 9

8

Did not start work + Read more ...

There are no wage details to assess.

  • Give the education message
  • The intervention can now be finalised as there are no wage details to assess
  • See Table 5

9

Started work but stopped + Read more ...

Check if employment income has been correctly declared.

Obtain the following details from the customer:

  • names and contact details of the employer they received the income from:
    • include trading and business names of the employer
    • make sure details match the details on the IRMD screen
  • when they started work
  • dates paid
  • employment status:
    • full-time
    • part-time
    • casual

Record the details obtained in the intervention DOC.

Then see Table 3.

10

Currently employed + Read more ...

If the customer (and/or partner) advise they are currently working, check if employment income has been correctly declared.

Obtain the following details from the customer:

  • names and contact details of the employer they are receiving income from:
    • include trading and business names of the employer
    • make sure details match the details on the IRMD screen
  • when they started work (if new employment)
  • employment income details (including dates paid if income relates to post 7 December 2020 income assessment changes)
  • employment status:
    • full time
    • part-time
    • casual

If the customer or their partner is currently employed, they must be placed on fortnightly lodgement.

Record details obtained from the customer on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for the 'Initial customer discussion - ODB or IRSRFI contact made and customer has time to discuss the intervention' DOC template

Then see Table 3.

3

Earnings not declared or incorrectly declared + Read more ...

Update the customer's record to reflect the correct details.

For example, the customer has declared the net earnings instead of the gross earnings.

Take into account the following information when updating wage details:

  • Update or correct employment income based on the details obtained from the discussion with the customer
  • Update employment income while the customer is on the phone
  • Give the customer the chance to source their employment income details and to call back once they have all the details needed
  • Do not ask the customer to provide paper verification (e.g. to upload or provide payslips to the service centre or post them)
  • Ask the customer if they are happy to update their record based on the details obtained over the phone
  • Use questioning skills to obtain as much information as possible from the customer over the phone to determine when thy worked and what they were paid

The CO must be satisfied the verbal update addresses the risk and results in an accurate record of the customer's circumstances.

If the customer gets DSP or CP, check that the number of hours they are currently working does not exceed the allowable limits: Note: these rules do not apply to Carer Payment (XMP)

  • For DSP customers, see Commencing or returning to work or self-employment Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • For CP customers, see Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities
  • For CP customers where the care receiver commences or returns to work

Customer required to call back

  • There may be times when the customer cannot provide their correct employment income details in the initial phone discussion
  • Allowing the customer to call back gives them time to source the correct information

Before allowing the customer to call back, as per Step 1 above use questioning skills to obtain the required information in the initial call. This avoids the need for the customer to call back.

Does the customer need to call back to proceed with the intervention?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 6

4

Customer calling back + Read more ...

Deliver the education message and tell the customer:

  • If they fail to call back within the required time, they may be selected for a future review and could possibly incur a larger debt
  • They have 7 days to call back from the 'Expected Response Date'
  • The information they will need when they call back - pay details for employers listed in the match data and any unmatched employers
  • The 1800 number they will need to call
  • Employment income details will be updated over the phone when they call back
  • Paper verification (payslips) is not required and should not be lodged at a service centre or by post

Record contact details in IRS and place the intervention on hold for 14 days:

  • On IRCD screen, in Text field, key 'Customer to call back with further information'
  • On IRMR screen add keyword ‘CBKHLD’

After placing the intervention on hold, ‘W’ it on the Review Summary (IRRS) screen.

Record details on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for the 'IRSRFI contact - customer will call back' DOC template.

No further action until the customer calls back or the review comes DUE.

Has the customer called back?

  • Yes,
    • Follow the phone contact requirements
    • Self assign the IRS intervention via the IRMR screen and any work items associated with the intervention via Work Optimiser
    • Go to Step 6 to continue the intervention
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Customer fails to call back + Read more ...

Make one outbound call attempt to contact the customer.

Is contact made?

  • Yes, continue the intervention, go to Step 6
  • No, the customer has been given the education message and advised that if they do not call back they may be selected for a future review and could incur a larger debt. They have been given 7 days to call back with their wage details. The intervention is not finalised until day 14. This allows another 7 days for the customer to call back before finalising
    • Record details on the intervention DOC, See the Resources page for the 'Intervention finalised - current (CUR) customer failed to call back (education message delivered)' DOC template
    • Then finalise the review, see Table 5

Selecting the IRS intervention + Read more ...

7

Assess earnings + Read more ...

After obtaining all the employment information, calculate the customer's entitlement.

The assessment of employment income may include:

  • leave entitlements
  • commissions and bonuses
  • allowances

From 7 December 2020, employment income is assessed on when it is paid by the employer to their employee. This is the 'Pay Date' or 'Date Paid' on the customer's payslip. If the customer does not get a payslip, they would need to confirm this information with their employer. Even if there is a delay between when an employer pays their employee and when the employee receives the pay (due to the time it takes to transfer funds between banks), employment income is still considered paid on the date the income is disbursed by the employer.

For more information, see:

  • Recording and correcting employment income details
  • Assessment of employment income for Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) customers over Age Pension age
  • Income Maintenance Period (IMP)
  • Paid leave and redundancy payments (advised before 19 April 2010)
  • Leave and termination payments paid by an employer
  • Assessment of other ordinary income for Centrelink payments
  • Treatment of lump sums

Employment income before 7 December 2020:

  • should be assessed for the fortnight the income is paid
  • for help, view the historical version of the above Operational Blueprint. Select the Page history icon on the Page tools menu and select a Modified date before 7 December 2020

If needed, code the employment income using the Earnings Apportionment Tool (EAT) and upload EAT calculation sheets to the customer’s record:

  • Use the verification code BIR, but do not update the Actual Earnings Details (EAAD) screen if the customer provides the income details verbally
  • Code the Australian Business Numbers (ABN) number/s when known. These can be copied from the Match Data (IRMD) screen for TDF matches

Verbal updates are not verification for coding purposes. Do not update the EAAD screen, but the code BIR can be used when accepting details verbally.

For more information, see:

  • Earnings Apportionment Tool (EAT)
  • Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts

Discuss the outcome of the updates with the customer.

Has a debt been identified?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, see Table 4

8

Determine whether Recovery Fee is applied + Read more ...

Determine if a Recovery Fee should be applied.

Check the customer's record for any evidence that would support a decision not to apply a Recovery Fee.

Discuss the Recovery Fee as part of the call. However, if the call is ended and the customer cannot be contacted to discuss the Recovery Fee, issue a Recovery Fee invite to contact letter. See Recovery Fee applied to debts resulting from incorrect declaration of income and Recovery Fee Workload Management for Compliance Interventions.

Note: the system will raise any earnings debts with a period of 14 days or less on the completion of the earnings activity. The Recovery Fee is not applied to auto raised debts. The customer will receive the standard debt advice as part of the automatic process.

Customer is providing more information + Read more ...

If the customer wants to provide more information but cannot provide it over the phone, tell them:

5

Issue Pre-adverse decision letter + Read more ...

After 3 unsuccessful call attempts to the customer and/or partner to advise the outcome, issue a manual Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter to them.

This letter tells them what evidence has been used to calculate a debt and gives an estimated debt amount. It gives them a further right of reply before finalising the outcome and lets them know what will happen next.

See the Resources page for the Q888 template. Use the correct letter template based on the customer's circumstances.

In IRS, record a contact and put the intervention on hold (HLD) for 28 days, pending contact from the customer:

  • On IRCD screen, key:
    • Expected response date: 28 days in future
    • Letter code: 'ADVERS'
    • Text: 'Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter issued'
  • On IRMR screen, self-assign the intervention and add keyword:
    • Workgroup: relevant workgroup
    • Position: COs logon
    • Keyword: 'Q888'

Also self-assign the work item via Work Optimiser.

Record details on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for the 'Pre-adverse letter sent' DOC template.

When the customer calls or activities come DUE

  • If the customer calls in response to the Q888, update their record based on any new information they give, remove the Q888 keyword on the IRMR screen, raise the debt and finalise the intervention
  • If the intervention comes DUE and the customer has not called or given new information, raise the debt and finalise the intervention based on information previously provided

6

Determine debt and calculate + Read more ...

All debts that can be calculated via the Centrelink system and explained using ADEX should be calculated that way. Only use MultiCal if the Centrelink system calculation is not possible or the outcome is not correct.

Check the Assessment Result (AR) screen to make sure the system has correctly calculated any potential overpayment, for example:

  • Debt schedule - do the debt dates/date of event (DOV) match the period?
  • Have the updates/coding been entered correctly?
  • Check the following to make sure the updates are as expected:
    • Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS)
    • Income Bank (NIB)
    • Employment Income Assessment (EIA)
  • Can a reassessment be made using the Benefit Action (BA) screen?
    • Use REA in relevant host system, or
    • RCR in PEN/PGA (for cancelled PEN and PGA records) and leave Reason blank

Note: check if Review of Assessment (ROA) coding is needed to generate allowable arrears. Do this when corrected income is less than declared, but the system did not generate arrears for those periods/fortnights.

If an ADEX debt calculation:

  • can be undertaken, the overpayment must be calculated with the customer over the phone where possible
  • is not possible, a MultiCal debt calculation may be required

For MultiCal debt calculation, get all information from the customer over the phone where possible to enable a debt calculation.

The CO is responsible for progressing the MultiCal activity. If they cannot action, their site is responsible for progressing the activity. The Team Leader will need to reassign the activity to another CO with the required skill set.

For more information, see:

  • Debt tools
  • Debt investigation
  • Actioning an undetermined debt on the Debt management and Information System (DMIS)
  • Using ADEX to explain a system generated debt
  • Using the correct date of effect when raising debts
  • Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts
  • Waiving Centrelink debts

When raising a debt from verbal updates and documented verification (in limited cases), choose the most relevant option from the Debt Action script.

Select the option that relates to the main source of the information provided. If using more than one source to calculate the debt, annotate the DOC to state this.

Deceased customers

If a debt is likely, make sure before sending the debt advice that a social worker in Payments and Integrity decides the best way to contact the executor. See Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer.

Automatic debts

For earnings debts with a period of 14 days or less, the system will:

  • raise the debt on the completion of the earnings activity
  • send a standard Debt Advice
  • not create a separate DOC in relation to the debt as it is not needed
  • not apply a Recovery Fee

7

Suggested debt advice templates + Read more ...

The default text on the Account Payable (OPAOD) screen is mostly unprotected. This allows the user to give a more suitable reason for the debt when appropriate.

The Account Payable letter template reads, in part:

‘We have checked your (PAYMENT TYPE) and found we have paid you too much.’

(DEBT REASON): add the below suggested text to explain why the debt occurred. However, the information can be changed to suit the customer situation.

'This is because an incorrect amount of employment income from (employer's name) was used to work out the payments made to you.'

The debt period now pre-fills under Details of your debt with us. It is no longer necessary to add the debt period in the free text field.

Sending Account Payable letters contains debt reason codes, default text for the Account Payable (OPAOD) screen, and the rules for entering free text.

Finalising the intervention

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Document the intervention + Read more ...

It is important to record all information in the customer's intervention DOC.

Annotate the existing DOC with the agreed DOC standards upon completing the intervention. If a DOC was created to initiate contact, annotate the original DOC.

The intervention DOC must clearly explain:

  • customer's circumstances
  • information relating to the compliance trigger
  • the scope of intervention
  • customer's action
  • outcomes of the intervention

If the customer is on the phone, advise the customer:

  • debt period
  • debt amount
  • reason for the debt, and
  • 'You will receive a letter with this information and instructions outlining the next steps'

All DOCs on a customer's record are to provide a clear and concise history of the customer's interactions. They must also meet the Online Documentation Recording (ODR) standards and Getting it Right (GIR) DOC minimum standards.

See the Resources page for DOC requirements.

2

Finalise the intervention + Read more ...

Before finalising the intervention:

  • Make sure all actions required on the customer (including linked reviews) and (ex) partner records are completed. Check:
    • Activity List (AL) screen
    • all entries on the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen show Y or N in the Response?: field
  • Check that all updates have correctly attributed to the review in IRS. For example, all earning updates

Use the Finalise Review script to finalise.

The script will pause at the Attribute Result (IRAR) screen in IRS. It will include all activities on the customer record that have occurred over the life of the IRS review.

Make sure the activities that relate to the EIIP review process are selected. For example, a debt which resulted from updating the customer's employment income.

Partner Interventions

If required, finalise the partner's intervention using the Finalise Review script.

Finalise the partner intervention at the same time as the customer's intervention.

3

Sending a Q765 advice + Read more ...

Send the advice unless:

  • the intervention outcome has been advised over the phone
  • the intervention resulted in an adverse decision, e.g.
    • payment/card remains suspended/cancelled
    • payment was varied
  • debt advice has been issued

A Q765 can be sent if requested by the customer.

Record details on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for the 'Finalise review' DOC template.
