2

Scope of intervention and authority to change scope + Read more ...

If Energy Supplement has been paid, make sure the customer received their correct entitlements in the past and their record is correct.

The scope period will help set the period to be reviewed.

Debts will only need to be investigated (verification may be needed) if:

  • the matched data from the ATO has indicated the customer's annual ATI exceeds the limit for ongoing entitlement, and
  • there are no verified details recorded on the SHID screen

Determine if a potential supplement debt exists by checking the SHID screen:

  • If the customer has a Notice of Assessment (NOA) recorded on the SHID screen for the match year, no historical information or verification is needed
  • If there is no NOA on the SHID screen for the match year, there may be a potential debt. Establish the customer's ATI and relevant NOA dates for each financial year during the scope period

Scope start date

1 July of the Match Data Year.

The Match Data Year is on IRMD.

All financial years before the Match Data Year do not need to be requested. Prior checks have identified that the customer's (and partner's combined) ATI was below the limit, or the customer was not required to lodge an ITR.

If the customer's partner passed away in the scope period, assess entitlement in the scope period for both parties.

Scope end date

'Date Paid To' at the Date of Scope Assessment (DOSA).

Requests may be refined

If information has been previously verified.

Scope assessment may occur

  • at the initial phone conversation
  • when actioning a call back

This assessment is called the 'Date of Scope Assessment' (DOSA).

Updates/verification to be requested

  • for the scope period only
  • for customer/partner and/or relevant ex-partners

If a customer and partner both have interventions, assess them under the same scope.

A combination of documented verification and verbal updates can be used to finalise an intervention:

  • If the customer advises they were not required to lodge a tax return due to low income for any of the financial years in the scope period, they must provide a verbal update of their ATI for each applicable financial year
  • Documented verification: the customer or third party is required to provide documents

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Assess the customer's ongoing eligibility + Read more ...

Determine the customer's Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) for the current reference tax year. The reference tax year is usually the year before the current tax year. See CSHC income test and reference tax year.

See also:

  • Adding or updating an account-based income stream
  • Payment rates and thresholds

For the customer to remain eligible, they must provide new ATI details showing their new ATI is below the CSHC income limit if, since the match year:

  • they lodged a later Income Tax Return (ITR) and had their income assessed by the ATO, or
  • they are not required to lodge an ITR due to their low income

Documented verification is required to assess ongoing eligibility, e.g. current ITR and Notice of Assessment (NOA). Exception: customers who are not required to lodge need to provide their ATI verbally.

Is the ATI for the current reference tax year under the CSHC income limit?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check if the customer is eligible to provide an estimate of Income + Read more ...

A customer's eligibility may be based on a current year estimate of income if they can demonstrate a change in their circumstances due to an acceptable condition. See CSHC income test and reference tax year.

All estimates will require written verification and supporting documents from the customer.

Using an estimate for 2 or more consecutive years

A person may use an estimate of income for 2 or more consecutive years only if:

  • they can show the extra increase in income is of a one-off nature for each year, and
  • the change in their circumstances is acceptable and the events are unrelated

Estimate below pension income and asset limits

If a customer's new estimate is below the pension income (and asset) limit, encourage them to test their eligibility for Age Pension.

Transfer the customer to the Older Australians line after finalising the call, or advise them to go to the Payment Finder on the Services Australia website.

Is the customer eligible or do they want to lodge an estimate?

  • Yes, they will need to lodge an estimate with verification, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

7

Documented verification is required + Read more ...

If the customer has completed a tax return since the Match Data Year and advises their ATI is now below the limit:

  • ask them to provide their Individual Tax Returns and NOAs for each financial year since the Match Data Year
  • if they cannot provide verification, seek third party verification. Explain that delays may be expected when obtaining information directly from the ATO

If the customer is going to provide an estimate for the current year, ask them to provide suitable evidence to confirm their change in circumstances. See Acceptable condition for using an estimate of income for the CSHC income test.

If the customer/partner passed away after the start of the intervention, verification from third party (ATO) is required.

7

Calculate overpayment CSHC + Read more ...

The provisional debt period starts on the date of issue of the NOA that has the customer's assessable income as exceeding the relevant income limit for CSHC.

Raise debts under Seniors Supplement Payment (SSP). This Includes Clean Energy Advance (CEA), Clean Energy Supplement (CES) and Energy Supplement.

If not qualified for CSHC in a tax year, Energy Supplement payments for full quarterly supplement entitlement periods that fall within the debt period may be raised as an overpayment. See Raising Pension Supplement and SSP debts.

Use the Payment Summary tool to calculate debt. See the Resources page for a link to Calculators and Tools.

Information about supplements

SSP was introduced from 20 September 2009, It replaced Seniors Concession Allowance (SCA) and Telephone Allowance (TAL) for CSHC holders. SSP was abolished from 20 June 2015.

Before 20 June 2015, the Energy Supplement was automatically paid to CSHC holders who chose to receive SSP. If a CSHC holder declined the SSP, they would not have received Energy Supplement.

Since 20 June 2015, the only quarterly supplement is Energy Supplement. This is paid each quarter on or shortly after the test days of 20 March, 20 June, 20 September and 20 December. A quarter starts on a test day and ends the day before the next test day. Payments are within a few days after the test day.

From 20 March 2017, CSHC holders need to meet grandfathering provisions to continue to receive Energy Supplement.

For more information, see:

  • Quarterly Supplement
  • Energy Supplement for CSHC holders

Economic Support Payment

The Economic Support Payment is payment is not a recoverable debt unless it was obtained fraudulently. For example, the customer knowingly made a false or misleading statement or knowingly provided false or misleading information in order to receive payment. If fraudulent behaviour is identified, record a tip off via the Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption System,

  • Record attempts to contact on the intervention DOC

Discussion

Follow the phone contact requirements.

Discuss the outcome of the intervention, including the information used to calculate the outcome. This might be a combination of verification from the customer and third party.

Advise the customer of any debt, including:

  • debt period
  • debt amount
  • reason for the debt

Ask the customer if they have more information they want to provide before the outcome is finalised. If the customer:

  • provides more information, update their record and recalculate as required
  • has more information but cannot provide it over the phone, go to Step 9
  • has no more information:
    • use questioning skills to check they understand the decision. Clear up any misunderstanding
    • advise the customer the decision will be made using the available information
    • advise their review and appeal rights
    • advise the customer they will receive an Accounts Payable letter. This will state the required payment amount, including plans and options to pay the debt

Record details

Update contact details in IRS if necessary.

Fully record the discussion and actions taken on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for the DOC template.

10

Issue Pre-adverse decision letter + Read more ...

After 3 unsuccessful call attempts to the customer and/or partner to advise the outcome, issue a manual Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter to them.

This letter tells them what evidence was used to calculate the debt and gives an estimated debt amount. It gives them a further right of reply before finalising the outcome and lets them know what will happen next.

See the Resources page for the Q888 templates. Use the correct letter template based on the customer's circumstances.

In IRS, record a contact and put the intervention on hold (HLD) for 28 days, pending contact from the customer:

  • On IRCD screen, key:
    • Expected response date: 28 days in future
    • Letter code: 'ADVERS'
    • Text: 'Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter issued'
  • On IRMR screen, self-assign the intervention and add keyword:
    • Workgroup: relevant workgroup
    • Position: COs logon
    • Keyword: 'Q888'

Also self-assign the work item via Work Optimiser, Hold to User.

DOC the record. See the Resources page for DOC requirements.

When the customer calls or activities come DUE

  • If the customer calls in response to the Q888, update their record based on any new information they give, raise the debt and finalise the intervention
  • If the intervention comes DUE and the customer has not given new information, raise the debt and finalise the intervention based on information previously

11

Manually raise debt + Read more ...

If the customer does not respond to the Q888 Pre adverse decision letter within 28 days, no extra phone calls are needed. Raise the debt using the available information.

When raising a debt from both verbal updates and documented verification, choose the most relevant option in the Debt Action script.

Select the option that relates to the main source of the information provided. Annotate the DOC if there is more than one source of evidence used to calculate the debt.

For more information, refer to:

  • Debt investigation
  • Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts
  • General debt raising information
  • Waiving Centrelink debts

It is recommended to use the debt advice template when raising debts from verbal updates. COs can change the debt advice to suit the customer.

See Sending Account Payable letters for debt reason codes, default text for the Account Payable (OPAOD) screen, and the rules for entering free text.

Raise CEA debts only under Section 1224

  • Raise the CEA component with all other components/payments the customer has been overpaid
  • Include in the debt raising DOC
    'The CEA component of this debt has been raised under s1224. Full consideration has been given to the application of S1224 when raising this debt.
    Due to system limitations this has been applied to the system under S1223'
