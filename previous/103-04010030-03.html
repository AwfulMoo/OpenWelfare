Determining if payment destination can be changed

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Recipient identity check + Read more ...

Always complete a recipient identity check when requested to change payment destinations

Follow normal Identity Confirmation procedures and also ask the recipient to state their current payment destination details and the amount of their last payment to that account. If there are any concerns about the identity of the recipient:

  • Ask the recipient additional identity questions or to provide documents at a service centre

Where Service Officers receive calls from recipients affected by payment changes (unauthorised by them or to accounts which they do not control) report this to a Team Leader immediately.

2

Where payment is currently sent + Read more ...

Are the payments currently being sent to the parent(s)/guardian(s)?

Check the Payments Destination Summary (PAS) screen.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, see Step 1 in the Determining exemption from requirement for payments to be made to parent/guardian table

3

Education level + Read more ...

Is the student studying a secondary non-school, tertiary or Masters and Doctorate level course?

Check the current study details on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen.

  • Yes, see Step 1 in the Updating payment destination and nominee arrangements table
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Is the student independent? + Read more ...

Check the recipient's independent status on the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home (NIH) screen.

  • Yes, see Step 1 in the Updating payment destination and nominee arrangements table
  • No, see Step 1 in the Determining exemption from requirement for payments to be made to parent/guardian table

Determining exemption from requirement for payments to be made to parent/guardian

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Away from home rate + Read more ...

Is the student approved for away from home rate of payments?

Check the students away from home status on the NIH screen.

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Does the parent/guardian receive all, or any, of the away from home rate of Living Allowance? + Read more ...

A student who is approved for away from home rate has strict rules governing who the payee can be, depending on the student's living arrangements. See Payment of away from home rate for ABSTUDY to determine who can be the payee for away from home living allowances.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, advise the student that ABSTUDY provisions do not allow for the change of payee to occur for students approved for away from home rate. Record a DOC on the student's record. Procedure ends here

3

Exemptions from having payments paid to parent(s)/guardian(s) + Read more ...

ABSTUDY can be paid directly to the dependent 16-17 year old student, if:

  • the student is not benefiting from the allowances because the payee is using it for other purposes
  • the payee leaves Australia temporarily and the young person cannot gain access to ABSTUDY payments, or
  • the parent(s)/guardian(s) choose to have ABSTUDY paid directly to the young person and have authorised the change in payee either verbally or in writing. The parent/guardian does not have to explain their decision. Note: where a parent/guardian has requested payments be directed to their 16-17 year old dependant they can also request that payments revert back to their own account - no explanation or reason is needed

Has the student advised that they meet any of the above exemption requirements for ABSTUDY payments to be paid to parent(s)/guardian(s)?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 6

4

Substantiation of claim + Read more ...

Advise the student that they will be required to substantiate their claim for a payment exemption in writing where the exemption reason is:

  • the student is not benefiting from the allowances because the payee is using it for other purposes, or
  • the payee leaves Australia temporarily and the young person cannot gain access to ABSTUDY payments

Advise the student that a supporting statement from an independent person would assist Services Australia to make a decision.

Lodgement of written statements

The student can provide the written statement(s) online via the Upload documents service, mail or in person at a service centre or an ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing team. Look up local Processing Service details for: ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY PES.

5

Will payments be directed to the student? + Read more ...

The Service Officer must determine from the student's written statement, and any supporting statements, whether ABSTUDY payments can be directed to the student.

  • Yes, see Step 1 in the Updating payment destination and nominee arrangements table
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Recipient does not meet exemption requirements + Read more ...

Advise the recipient that they do not meet the requirements and that their payments will continue to be directed to their parent/guardian until such time as the student:

  • meets one of the exemption requirements,
  • turns 18 years old, or
  • becomes independent

Record details of the decision on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

Updating payment destination and nominee arrangements

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Update payment destination + Read more ...

Update a change to the payment destination via the PAS screen. See changing payment destination.

Where a 16-17 year old ABSTUDY student is independent or studying a secondary non-school, tertiary or Masters and Doctorate level course, payments must be made to a bank account in the student's name.

2

Nominee arrangements in effect from 1 January 2013 + Read more ...

  • Payment and correspondence nominees are now recognised in Section 1.6 of the ABSTUDY Policy Manual
  • The guidelines, policy and procedures for correspondence and payment nominee arrangements for ABSTUDY are based on those for income support recipients
