This page contains details for setting up new, transferring, combining and closing aged care services in the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS) and Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP). Only Subject Matter Experts (SME) or higher can do this.

New service

Table 1: this table describes how to set up a new service for home care or flexible care in the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS) and residential care in the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP).

Step

Action

1

Receiving the Department of Health and Aged Care's notification + Read more ...

Has Services Australia received the Department of Health and Aged Care's email notification via the Aged Care and Allied Health (ACAH) positional mailbox?

  • Yes, update the sales and transfer spreadsheet saved in the ACAH shared drive
  • No, procedure ends here

Note: ACAH staff can request access/directions to this drive via their Team Leader.

2

Contact the provider + Read more ...

Note:

  • Before emailing the provider a copy of the blank claim form, SME or higher must seek guidance from the Aged Care Programme Branch’s Operations Team whether the blank claim form can be sent to them
  • This can be done via the escalation process. See Team Leader for the escalation process
  • Aged Care Programme Branch’s Operations Team will advise the SME or higher of the outcome

Obtain provider contact information from Department of Health and Aged Care’s notification. If there is no contact information in the notification, search the National Aged care Provider System (NAPS). Only SME or higher have NAPS system access.

Update provider contact information on the sales and transfer spreadsheet.

Send an email to the provider, asking them to complete the following forms:

  • Application to add or change Approved Care Service’s bank details (AC015)
  • Blank claim form. The blank claim form is not an online Services Australia form and must be emailed to the provider. Alternatively, advise the new service they can submit their first claim via the Aged Care Provider Portal (ACPP)
  • Aged Care Provider Portal Register, Amend or Remove Users (AC004)
  • Aged Care Entry Record (AC021). The AC021 only needs to be completed if the provider doesn’t have ACPP access to add care recipients in themselves

3

Check if forms have been received and processed + Read more ...

Check if the following forms been received in PaNDA and processed:

  • Application to add or change Approved Care Service’s bank details (AC015)
  • Aged Care Provider Portal Register, Amend or Remove Users (AC004)
  • Aged Care Entry Record (AC021) (if applicable)

Are all required forms received and processed?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, contact the provider and request any missing forms. When all forms are returned and processed go to Step 4

4

Actioning a manual advance request + Read more ...

Has the new service requested a manual advance?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to step 6

5

Calculate manual advance + Read more ...

The calculation of the manual advance differs slightly, depending on the type of aged care service.

The calculation for:

  • flexible care, is the number of care recipients multiplied by the number of days multiplied by 90%
  • residential care services, is the number of care recipients multiplied by the default ACFI rate multiplied by the number of days multiplied by 90%

Note: from 1 February 2021 home care advances ceased. Subsidies and supplements will be paid in arrears each month.

To create and approve flexible care manual advances, see Advances in the Aged Care Management Payments System (ACMPS).

To create and approve residential care manual advances, see Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage residential care advances (Service in Context) table.

6

Update the sales and transfer spreadsheet + Read more ...

Update the sales and transfer spreadsheet as 'completed' in the shared drive.

Procedure ends here.

Transfer service

Table 2: this table describes how to process a transfer request for home care and flexible services in the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS) and residential care services in the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP).

Step

Action

1

Receiving the Department of Health and Aged Care's notification + Read more ...

A transfer can occur between two existing services when ownership is changed.

Services Australia receives the Department of Health and Aged Care's email notification via the Aged Care and Allied Health (ACAH) positional mailbox.

  • Update the sales and transfer spreadsheet saved in the ACAH shared drive
  • Check all addresses of services both old and new match our systems

Note: if the transferring services are residential care, monitor the services in ACSP to ensure the previous service submitted their final claim before the transfer to the new service takes place or any change of bank details are actioned.

Process the final claim, if not done already. See Residential Care monthly claim form for the processing steps.

2

Suspend advance and request new bank details + Read more ...

Service Officers must:

  • suspend the advance for any services transferring their ownership one month before the transfer date:
    • For flexible care, see Suspend and unsuspend advances table
    • For residential care, see Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage residential care advances (Service in Context) table
  • update the service’s bank details. This must be done after the previous owner's final claim has been approved and paid
    • Application to add or change Approved Care Service’s bank details (AC015)

3

Lift advance suspension + Read more ...

Once the service provider’s bank details are up-to-date, lift the advance suspension.

  • For flexible care, see Suspend and unsuspend advances table
  • For residential care, see Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage residential care advances (Service in Context) table

Is the previous service homecare or flexible care, and needs care recipients transferred?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Ensure all care recipients have been transferred + Read more ...

Ensure all care recipients from any closing service have been transferred over to the continuing service on the first day of the transfer.

If there are 1 or more care recipients still in care of the closing service, contact the service provider and advise them of remaining action required.

Note: if the new service provider is not an online service provider (still lodges paper claims) Service Officers must action the transfer of care recipients. Ensure the care recipients’ leave details are updated and do not overlap.

5

Update the sales and transfer spreadsheet + Read more ...

Update the sales and transfer spreadsheet as 'completed' in the shared drive.

Procedure ends here.

Combine services

Table 3: this table describes how to combine services for home care and flexible care in the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS).

Note: there is no combine functionality for residential care services in the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP). All service combine requests from the Department of Health and Aged Care and elsewhere will need to be escalated to the Aged Care Programmes Branch’s Operations (Residential Care) Team. See Team Leader for the escalation process.

Step

Action

1

Notification received from the Department of Health and Aged Care + Read more ...

Services Australia receives the Department of Health and Aged Care's email notification. This is sent to both positional mailboxes of the Aged Care and Allied Health (ACAH) Branch and the Aged Care Programmes Branch.

  • Update the sales and transfer spreadsheet saved in the ACAH shared drive
  • Check all addresses of services, both old and new, match our systems
  • For home care and flexible care services, go to Step 2

2

Combine services for home care and flexible care services + Read more ...

Service Officers must:

  • suspend the advance for the closing service
    • See Suspend and unsuspend advances table
  • contact the service provider and advise:
    • the provider can transfer care recipients via the Aged Care Provider Portal (ACPP)
    • the initial combined claim will be processed, or if the continuing service is online they can process the claim themselves
    • any held over amount for the closing service can be recovered either from the continuing service or raised as debt
  • calculate held over amounts. Service Officers must check:
    • the closing service’s held over amounts from the last two months’ claims up to the combine date
    • if any advances have been issued to the closing service after the combine date
    • include unclaimed unspent home care amounts and retrospective claims approved after the latest payment statement
  • monitor the system to ensure the care recipients from the closing service have transferred over to the continuing service

Go to Step 3.

3

Transfer of care recipients for home care and flexible care services + Read more ...

Note: if the continuing service provider is not an online service provider (still lodges paper claims) Service Officers must action the transfer of care recipients. Ensure the care recipients’ leave details are updated and do not overlap.

Ensure all care recipients from the closing service have been transferred over to the continuing service on the first day of the combining.

If there is 1 or more care recipients still in care of the closing service, contact the service provider and advise them of remaining action required.

Go to Step 4.

4

Recover outstanding amounts from the closing service + Read more ...

This step only applies to home care.

Recover the outstanding amounts from the closing service:

  • Send a formal letter to the closing service provider for the outstanding funds
  • Request a written response from the closing service provider of their agreement to pay the debt or recover the outstanding funds from the continuing service
  • Complete a Debt Advice Notice (DAN) if debt option is chosen
  • Attach the DAN, the formal letter and the closing service provider’s written response to Medicare debt recovery

5

Update the sales and transfer spreadsheet + Read more ...

Update the sales and transfer spreadsheet as 'completed' in the shared drive.

Close service

Table 4: this table describes the closure process for home care and flexible care services in the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS) and residential care services in the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP).

Step

Action

1

Notification received from the Department of Health and Aged Care + Read more ...

Services Australia receives email notification from the Department of Health and Aged Care via the Aged Care Allied Health (ACAH) Branch's positional mailbox.

  • Update the sales and transfer spreadsheet saved in the ACAH shared drive
  • For home care and flexible care services, go to Step 2
  • For residential care services, go to Step 3

2

Suspend the advances + Read more ...

A month before the closure date, suspend the advances for the closing service.

For flexible care services, see the Suspend and unsuspend advances table.

Go to Step 4.

3

Contact service providers + Read more ...

Contact the service provider and advise the closure and recovery process of (any) outstanding funds.

For residential care services, see the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage residential care advances (Service in Context) table.

Go to Step 4.

4

Calculate any outstanding amounts from the closing service + Read more ...

Note: for home care services, Service Officers must also include unspent home care amounts that have not been returned prior to closure. These are likely to be advised by the service and will not be captured in the system given the service is now closed and not lodging future claims. Service Officers must also check if any retrospective claims were approved in the system after the latest payment statement.

To calculate the held over amounts, check:

  • the closing service’s held over amounts from the last two months’ claims up to the closure date
  • any advances issued to the closing service after the closure date

5

Recover the outstanding amounts from the closing service + Read more ...

Recover the outstanding amounts from the closing service:

  • Send a formal letter to the closing service provider for the outstanding funds
  • Request a written response from the closing service provider of their agreement to pay the debt
  • Complete a Debt Advice Notice (DAN)
  • Attach the DAN, the formal letter and the closing service provider’s written response to Medicare debt recovery

6

Update the sales and transfer spreadsheet + Read more ...

Update the sales and transfer spreadsheet as 'completed' in the shared drive.
