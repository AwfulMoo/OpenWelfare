Residence and identity requirements + Read more ...

The system does not automatically assess a customer's residence status.

To avoid granting payments incorrectly, make sure all residence data is coded and manually check the customer meets the visa requirements.

Residence checks must be completed for every claim before running the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow.

Residence checks needed

  • Check the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen for citizenship, visa and/or New Zealand residence status details
    • For NZ citizens check the Visa (Sub) Class code for a 444 visa
    • Check the New Zealand residence status
      - Grandfathered (GRF) is a protected Special Category Visa (SCV) holder and should claim as an Australian resident
      - Transitional (TRA) is a protected SCV holder and should claim as an Australian resident
      - NOT is a non-protected SCV holder and should claim as a non-Australian resident
  • Check the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen for country of birth or travel documents
  • Check the Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen for travel details
  • Details on the RSLEG, RSIMME and RSIM screens must be refreshed by running the RSIMME update
  • For Australian residents (through legal permanent residence and residing in Australia) - make sure the Country of Residence (CRES) screen is coded. Note: the CRES screen does not always need to be coded with an Australian entry for non-Australian residents (temporary visa holders)
  • If the customer is on the phone, asking them the last time they travelled overseas is a good way to confirm any passport details. This will help to complete PoRO
  • If there are no residence details on the RSLEG screen, see Recording legal residence status to complete residence coding on the record
  • Check the Travel Outside Australia Summary (TOAS) screen to make sure the customer is in Australia. Note: if a customer’s first movement is an arrival in Australia, it is not displayed on this screen. See the RSIM screen instead
  • If the customer is a non-Australian resident, check they have an eligible visa
  • If the customer is an Australian resident with a visa, to check if the visa is a permanent resident visa, see Social Security Guide, 9.2, Visa subclasses & payment eligibility for visas issued after 1/9/94)

  • start date of self-isolation, quarantine or caring period

  • date of a positive COVID-19 test, if relevant
  • first day they did not work

Start date of the claim + Read more ...

For the purposes of receiving the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment, the start date of the period the customer is claiming is the start of the:

  • self-isolation
  • quarantine, or
  • caring period

Staff will use this date in the Date affected by the event field when running the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow. For first claims, this is always the start of the self-isolation, quarantine or caring period. It is rarely the exposure date or the date the customer was contacted by a required authority and advised to isolate (unless this is also the same day the customer started to self-isolate or quarantine). For a second or subsequent claims this date may need adjustment. See the relevant ‘Start date of claim’ scenarios and Table 3.

Personal instruction by a required authority and other evidence + Read more ...

If a customer advises they have received personal instruction by a required authority to self-isolate or quarantine, make sure it is because they (or a child 16 years or under in their care):

  • for self-isolation or quarantine periods that start on or before 9 January 2022 only, have tested positive with COVID-19, or
  • for any self-isolation, quarantine or caring period, are the close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19

See Positive test result for self-isolation, quarantine or caring periods that start on or after 10 January 2022, if a customer is claiming as they:

  • or a child 16 years of under in their care, have tested positive with COVID-19, or
  • are caring for a person who has tested positive with COVID-19

Note: customers self-declare they have the required evidence available, if requested. Types of evidence for customers to keep depend on the criteria and self-isolation, quarantine or caring period they are claiming. See Close contact and Positive test result. Only ask for evidence if there are concerns about the customer’s claim not being valid or genuine.

Contacts from the required authority + Read more ...
