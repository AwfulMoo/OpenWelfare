This document outlines information about a:

  • Notice of past benefits (NOPB) that becomes a Notice of charge (NOC) if it is still current when the claim reaches judgment or settlement
  • NOC that is issued after the claim has reached judgment or settlement
  • Notice of past benefits – advance payment (NOPB-AP) that is issued to the claimant after the claim has reached judgment or settlement and an advance payment has been accepted

Read references to ‘notice of charge’ as NOC and NOPB-AP unless stated otherwise.

Notice of past benefits and notice of charge

A notice of past benefits or notice of charge provides the total recoverable amount for a compensation claim.

A notice of past benefits expires after 6 months. It is not payable unless it is still valid on the date the compensation amount is fixed or the claimant submits a Section 23A statement.

A notice of charge never expires and is payable within 28 days.

After Services Australia has received a statement by claimant (or if the time to return the statement has passed) MCRS sends:

  • the compensation payer:
    • the Notice of past benefits or charge (Z2807) before judgment or settlement, or
    • the Notice of charge (Z2811) after judgment or settlement, or
    • nothing if Services Australia has accepted an advance payment, and
  • the injured person or claimant:
    • a copy of the notice that was sent to the compensation payer – if there is a valid Third party authority form (MO021) attached to the claim, the copy is sent to the authorised third party instead, or
    • the Notice past benefits – advance payment (Z2811) if the agency has accepted an advance payment – if there is a valid Third party authority form (MO021) attached to the claim, this is sent to the authorised third party instead

Note: if the agency accepts an advance payment, the compensation payer no longer has authority to access information about the claim.

See Payments, refunds and debts.

Copy of verified services attached or all services included

A list of the Medicare benefits and care costs verified by the claimant is attached if a statement by claimant was returned by the due date and is substantially correct. See Extensions, not substantially correct, decisions and reviews.

No list of Medicare benefits is attached and all services listed on the Medicare history statement will be included in the total recoverable amount if a statement by claimant was:

  • not returned
  • returned incomplete, or
  • returned after the due date (including if an extension of time to submit the statement was granted)

Note: all care costs identified in a previous notice of past benefits will be carried forward.

No statement by claimant returned – all services included

Send a notice of past benefits – all services included where the statement by claimant:

  • is not returned and no Section 23A notice has been received
  • by the due date
  • by the latest date where an extension was granted
  • following a request to provide more information - where a Z2805 – Statement not substantially correct letter was sent

All services listed on the Notice to Claimant will be taken as related to the claim. All nursing home benefits, residential care subsidies and home care subsidies identified in a previous notice of past benefits will be carried forward.

Timeframes for issue of notice of charge and notice of past benefits – advance payment

The Health and Other Services (Compensation) Act 1995 (HOSC Act) prescribes timeframes for the agency to issue a:

  • notice of charge, and
  • notice of past benefits – advance payment

An extension of time to submit a statement and the notice of statement not substantially correct process may extend timeframes in some circumstances.

See

  • References page, sections 24(4), 25(4) and 33D of the HOSC Act for more information
  • Resources page for relevant issuing timeframes and how to calculate them
  • Extensions, not substantially correct, decisions and reviews

Errors in notice of past benefits or notice of charge

Administrative errors

If an administrative error is found:

  • immediately call both the compensation payer and injured person/claimant/authorised third party, and
  • advise them a new notice will be sent as soon as possible

This will ensure correct information is used when negotiating the settlement or judgment amount and minimise rework and extra enquiries later on.

Amended NOPB or NOC requested

A request may be submitted to remove services included on a NOPB or NOC.

To amend a closed claim the Service Officer must reopen the claim and reprocess the NOJS or NORA. When the NOJS or NORA has been recaptured the Reprocess a statement button can be selected on the Review screen. This will allow reprocessing of the services on the SBC and care costs that were used to generate the NOC/NOPB-AP.

To amend a NOPB on an open claim Service Officers can select the Statement by claimant as the document type and the Reprocess a statement button can be selected.

See Extension, decisions and review for more information on amending a notice.

No valid notice of past benefits at judgment or settlement

Where no valid notice of past benefits or completed section 23A statement has been received at the time of judgment or settlement, a notice to claimant is sent to the injured person or claimant.

When accepting a section 23A statement, the amount from any expired notice of past benefits relating to the claim, becomes the amount payable in the notice of charge.

Calculation of s33D due date for notice of charge- with no extension to return statement

Under section 33D of HOSC Act, if an advance payment has been made, the date to issue a notice is 3 months from receiving the later of either:

  • A complete and valid notice of judgment or settlement from the compensation payer, or
  • The advance payment

Under section 33D of HOSC Act, there are no amounts recoverable by the agency where:

  • more than 3 months has passed since a complete notice of judgment or settlement or advance payment was received
  • no extension was granted, and
  • a notice of charge has not been issued

If the Section 33D date has passed, do not issue a Notice to claimant to the claimant/injured person, or notice of charge to the compensation payer.

Calculation of s33D due date for NOPB-AP after an extension

If an extension was granted to return a statement by claimant, the new 33D date is 28 days after the new statement return date if:

  • the new statement return date is after the original section 33D(1) date (3 months after the date of receipt of notice of judgment or settlement or advance payment, whichever is later)

For example, if the section 33D (1) date was 2 April 2020, but the extension to return the statement was granted until 10 April 2020, a NOPB-AP must be issued by 8 May 2020. That is, 28 days after the new statement return date.

Calculation of s24 (4) due date for notice of charge

Under section 24(4) of HOSC Act, where there has been no advance payment made:

  • the agency has a maximum of 3 months to issue a notice of charge to the compensation payer from the date a completed notice of judgment or settlement was received

If the s24(4) date has passed and a notice of charge has not been issued:

  • there are no amounts recoverable by the agency

When a NOPB-AP or NOC is issued

After a section 23A statement is received

A section 23A statement may be submitted by the injured person if they have not received:

  • any relevant Medicare benefits since the date on their expired notice of past benefits, or
  • a notice of past benefits

The amount payable on the NOPB-AP or NOC will either be:

  • the amount on the expired notice of past benefits, or
  • nil

After an advance payment is received

The recoverable amount is reduced by the advance payment, and a NOPB-AP will be issued to the injured person or claimant, and:

  • if there is money still outstanding, the amount is payable to the agency by the injured person within 28 days after receiving the notice, or
  • if there is a refund owed, that amount is paid by the agency to the injured person (unless an authority is received to pay a Public Trustee)

After an ad-hoc payment amount is received

The recoverable amount is reduced by the ad-hoc payment amount, a notice of charge will be issued to the compensation payer, and:

  • If there is money still outstanding, the amount is payable by the compensation payer
  • If there is a refund owed, the amount is paid by the agency as per question 38 of the MO022 form

Notice of reimbursement arrangement, no previous payments received

Where a compensation payer has notified the agency of a reimbursement arrangement, if a Notice to claimant is:

  • returned by the due date:
    • a notice of charge with an itemised list of services identified by the injured person or claimant will be issued to the compensation payer
  • not returned, or is returned after the due date
    • a notice of charge - all services included will be issued to the compensation payer. This will not have an itemised list attached

When the amount can be reduced on a NOC or NOPB-AP

Percentage of apportionment

Where the injured person contributed to their injury or illness (contributory negligence), a percentage of liability is set at judgment or settlement. This is known as an apportionment and it reduces the judgment or settlement amount.

The NOC or NOPB-AP is reduced by the percentage of apportionment attributed to the injured person. For example, if the injured person was apportioned 20% liability, the NOC or NOPB-AP amount would be reduced by 20%.

Judgment fixes the amount to be paid and is less than the NOC or NOPB-AP

Where the past Medicare expenses are fixed by court at the time of judgment this fixed amount becomes the amount payable to the agency. Where the past medical expenses are fixed by the court at the time of judgment the amount payable to the agency will be determined by the notice to claimant. The agency cannot recover more than the total fixed compensation amount.
