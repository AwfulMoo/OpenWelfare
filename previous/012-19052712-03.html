Dental practitioner and dental specialist + Read more ...

To recognise a dental practitioner, Services Australia require evidence of at least 1 of the following:

  • Ahpra registration certificate
  • Where there is an existing ‘shell’ record in PDS, registration details are prepopulated within the ‘shell’ record
  • Ahpra Information window, or
  • Ahpra website search result

To recognise a dental specialist, Services Australia require evidence of at least 1 of the following:

  • Ahpra registration certificate with:
  • Specialist recorded at Registration Type, and
  • Specific speciality details in Specialty field (for example, Orthodontics)
  • Where there is an existing ‘shell’ record in PDS, registration details are prepopulated within the ‘shell’ record and Specialist Registration details recorded in the Ahpra Information window

    • Specialist recorded at Registration Type, and
    • Specific speciality details in Specialty field (for example, Orthodontics)

Dental hygienist + Read more ...

  • An existing ‘shell’ record in PDS, registration details are prepopulated within the ‘shell’ record, and
  • Registration Type of ‘Dental hypgienist’ recorded in the Ahpra information window

Note: registration can only be established via a shell record. It is a requirement to check the Ahpra window matches the profession the provider has indicated on their application form.

Midwife + Read more ...

To recognise a midwife, Services Australia require evidence of at least 1 of the following:

  • Ahpra registration certificate
  • Where there is an existing ‘shell’ record in PDS, registration details are prepopulated within the ‘shell’ record
  • Ahpra Information window, or
  • Ahpra website search result

Note: if a midwife requests a prescriber number, the midwife must have Endorsement on their Ahpra registration certificate stating:

  • An endorsed midwife qualified to prescribe schedule 2, 3, 4 and 8 medicines and to provide associated services required for midwifery practice in accordance with relevant state and territory legislation, or
  • Endorsed as qualified to prescribe schedule 2, 3, 4 and 8 medicines required for midwifery practice across pregnancy, labour, birth and postnatal care, in accordance with relevant State and Territory legislation

Nurse practitioners + Read more ...

  • Ahpra Information window with Endorsement in Type and Nurse Practitioner in Sub Type, or
  • Ahpra website search result with Nurse Practitioner recorded under Endorsements

Note: all nurse practitioners are eligible to apply for a prescriber number.

Psychologist + Read more ...

To recognise a psychologist, Services Australia require evidence of at least 1 of the following:

  • Ahpra registration certificate with Registration Type of General
  • Where there is an existing ‘shell’ record in PDS, registration details are prepopulated within the ‘shell’ record. The ‘shell’ record must have a Registration Type of Current
  • Ahpra Information window with a Registration Type of Current
  • Ahpra website search result with a Registration Type of Current

Clinical psychologist

To recognise a clinical psychologist, Services Australia require evidence of at least 1 of the following:

  • Ahpra registration certificate with Approved area of practice - clinical psychology under Endorsements
  • Where there is an existing ‘shell’ record in PDS, registration details are prepopulated within the ‘shell’ record. The ‘shell’ record must have a registration Status of ‘Current’ and ‘Approved area of practice - clinical psychology’ Endorsement recorded in the Ahpra Information window
  • ‘Approved area of practice - clinical psychology’ Endorsement recorded in Ahpra Information window, or
  • Ahpra website search result, with Approved area of practice - clinical psychology under Endorsements

Psychologist - Non-directive pregnancy support counselling (PSC)

For a psychologist or clinical psychologist to be recognised to provide Non-directive PSC, Services Australia receives either:

  • An email notification of endorsement directly from Australian Psychological Society (APS), or
  • Certificate from APS confirming completion of training in ‘Perinatal Non-Directive Counselling Training’ issued by the Australian Psychological Society

Non-Ahpra accreditation and credential evidence

Table 4

Item

Description

1

Aboriginal Health Worker + Read more ...

For Services Australia to recognise an Aboriginal Health Worker, the health professional needs to submit certification from an approved registered training organisation (RTO) of at least 1 of the following:

  • Certificate III (equivalent or higher) in Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander Primary Health Care, or
  • Certificate III (equivalent or higher) in Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health - before 1 July 2012

A full list of current approved qualifications and RTO's is available in Aboriginal Health Worker - provider eligibility and registration.

If the certificate provided is a Certificate III or higher but is not listed in Aboriginal Health Worker Approved Courses, assess if the certificate is issued from an approved RTO. Perform an Organisation/RTO Search on Training.gov.au.

Where there is a certificate number, the certificate number is the registration number.

Where there is a document number, the first 7 document characters are recorded as the registration number. The full document number is added as a note on the health professional’s stem.

Note: where an Aboriginal Health Worker is providing services in the Northern Territory, the health professional is required to hold registration with Ahpra as an Aboriginal and Torres Strait Island Health Practitioner.

2

Accredited practicing dietitian + Read more ...

For Services Australia to recognise an accredited practising dietitian, the health professional needs to submit the following certification:

  • Dietitians Australia (DA) certificate stating:
    • Accredited Practicing Dietitian (APD), with
    • APD Registration Number (recorded as the registration number)

Note: the Dietitians Association of Australia (DAA) was rebranded and changed their name to Dietitians Australia (DA), effective 28 May 2020.

3

Audiologist (Allied Health and Dental Care Initiative) + Read more ...

For Services Australia to recognise an audiologist, the health professional needs to submit the following certification from either:

  • Audiology Australia (AA) (formerly Audiological Society of Australia ASAud) stating:
    • Accredited Audiologist, and
    • AudA ID
  • Australian College of Audiology (ACAud) with a certificate of:
    • membership as a Full/Ordinary Member or Fellow, and
    • competency showing both Hearing Rehabilitation Specialist and Diagnostic Rehabilitation Specialist

4

Audiologist/Audiometrist (Office of Hearing Services Program) + Read more ...

Do not amend of update existing Audiology or Audiometry stems created for the Hearing Services Program as this program has closed.

The Office of Hearing Services Program ceased on 23 November 2019.

5

Diabetes educator + Read more ...

For Services Australia to recognise a diabetes educator, the health professional needs to submit the following certification:

  • Australian Diabetes Educators Association of Australia (ADEA) certificate showing
    • Credentialed Diabetes Educator (CDE), with a
    • CDE Number (recorded as the registration number)

6

Exercise physiologist + Read more ...

To be recognised as an exercise physiologist, the health professional needs to submit the following certification:

  • Exercise and Sports Science Australia (ESSA) certificate as an Accredited Exercise Physiologist (AEP)
    • Certificate must state Approved with accreditation in Exercise Physiology with an ID number (recorded as the registration number)

7

Mental health nurse + Read more ...

For Services Australia to recognise a mental health nurse, the health professional needs to submit the following certification:

  • Australian College of Mental Health Nurses (ACMHN) as a Credentialed Mental Health Nurse (MHN)
    • Certificate must state Credentialed Mental Health Nurse with a Credential number (recorded as registration number)

Mental health nurse - Non-directive pregnancy support counselling (PSC)

  • To be recognised to provide PSC as a mental health nurse, Services Australia receives either:
    • an email notification of endorsement directly from Australian College of Mental Health Nurses (ACMHN), or
    • certificate from Australian College of Mental Health Nurses confirming ‘completed the online CPD program Perinatal Mental Health for Nurses and Midwives’

The health professional may also submit a copy of their certificate of course completion in Perinatal Non-Directive Counselling Training: V2 issued by the Australian Psychological Society.

8

Orthoptist + Read more ...

For Services Australia to recognise an orthoptist, the health professional needs to submit the following certification:

  • Australian Orthoptic Board (AOB) Certificate of Registration - includes Continuing Professional Development (CPD) currency from 13 November 2019 (recorded as registration number), and
  • Orthoptics Australia membership with Membership Number (recorded in Comments)

9

Social Worker + Read more ...

For the Accredited Mental Health Social Worker to provide Focussed Psychological Strategies (FPS) the health professional needs to submit a certificate issued by the Australian Association of Social Workers (AASW) confirming:

  • Current member of the AASW
  • Accreditation as an Allied Health Professional (Mental Health Social Worker), and
  • Meeting the standards for mental health set out in Practice Standards for Mental Health Social Workers 2014

For the Accredited Mental Health Social Worker to provide non-directive pregnancy support counselling (PSC), the health professional needs to submit a certificate issued by AASW confirming:

  • Current member of the AASW
  • Accreditation as an Allied Health Professional (Mental Health of Social Worker)
  • Meeting the standards for mental health set out in Practice Standards for Mental Health Social Workers 2014, and
  • A PSC certificate of completion in Perinatal Non-Directive Counselling Training: V2 issued from the Australian Psychological Society

For the Accredited Social Worker to provide PSC they must supply:

  • Membership of the Australian Association of Social Workers (AASW)
  • Certificate as an Accredited Social Worker issued by the AASW, and
  • A PSC certificate of completion in Perinatal Non-Directive Counselling Training: V2 issued by the Australian Psychological Society

Recognition of a Social Worker for Medicare purposes is made by the Chief Executive Medicare (or their delegate) under the Health Insurance (Allied Health Services) Determination 2014 made under subsection 3C(1) of the Health Insurance Act 1973.

10

Speech Pathologist + Read more ...

For Services Australia to recognise a speech pathologist, the health professional needs to submit the following certification:

  • Speech Pathology Australia (SPA) certificate showing Certified Practicing Member (CPM) with a Member Number (recorded as registration number)

Note: the date at the top of the certificate is the financial period for which the speech pathologist is registered. The date at the bottom of the certificate, is the date the speech pathologist became eligible to be recognised as a speech pathologist. Certificates may display a registration period 1 July 2019 to 30 June 2020 or an 18 month period for 2019 graduates.

Eligible non-medical professionals + Read more ...

The following non-medical health professionals are eligible to apply for a prescriber number:

Table 8

Item

Description

1

Start date action + Read more ...

For Ahpra accredited professionals, except psychologists, nurse practitioners, dental hygienists, dental therapist and oral health therapists (see Items 2, 3 and 4 in this table), record Start Date as the:

  • Date of first registration in profession on the health professional’s current Ahpra registration record, or
  • Profession Start Date displayed in the Ahpra Information window

Note:

  • Where there is an existing ‘shell’ record in PDS do not change the current registration line start date as this may cause an error to occur for this record when the Ahpra data feed is next received. Prior registration line/s can be added, if required, to the promoted ‘shell’ using the information in the Ahpra Information window
  • Where the Date of first registration in profession on the health professional’s current Ahpra registration record or Ahpra Information window is prior to the date Ahpra came into effect (1 July 2010), the registration start date is the date Ahpra came into effect, 1 July 2010

For non-Ahpra registered professionals, record Start Date as:

  • Date of registration/membership/certification on accreditation documents (if provided), or
  • Date of lodgement of initial application

2

Psychologists’ Start Date + Read more ...

For psychologists, record Start Date as per the method of viewing the registration:

  • Where the Ahpra Information window is available and the registration status is Registered - Current, registration start date is the date general registration was granted. If the date general registration was granted is prior to the registration start date generated from the shell record, a registration line is required to be added to capture the earlier date of Current registration
  • Where the Ahpra certificate supplied was generated from Ahpra Online Services (identified with Copy of Certificate of Registration at the bottom of the certificate), registration start date is Date Printed in the bottom left on the certificate
  • Where the Ahpra certificate supplied does not reference Copy of Certificate of Registration, registration start date is the start date of the registration period on the certificate
  • Where Ahpra Information window is not available and Ahpra certificate has not been supplied, registration start date is the date the Ahpra website was checked (evidence of registration is required to be uploaded to PaNDA record)

3

Nurse practitioners’ Start Date + Read more ...

For nurse practitioners, record Start Date as per the method of viewing the registration:

  • Where the Ahpra Information window is available, Endorsement under Division/Registration Type is Nurse Practitioner. The registration start date is the start date of the Nurse Practitioner Endorsement. If date Nurse Practitioner Endorsement is prior to the registration start date generated from the shell record, a registration line is required to be added to capture the earlier date of Nurse Practitioner Endorsement
  • Where the Ahpra certificate supplied was generated from Ahpra Online Services (identified with Copy of Certificate of Registration at the bottom of the certificate), reflects Nurse Practitioner recorded under Endorsements, registration start date is Date Printed in the bottom left on the certificate
  • Where the Ahpra certificate supplied does not reference Copy of Certificate of Registration, reflects Nurse Practitioner recorded under Endorsements, registration start date is the start date of the registration period on the certificate
  • Where the Ahpra Information window is not available and Ahpra certificate has not been supplied, if the Ahpra website has Nurse Practitioner recorded under Endorsements, the registration start date is the date the Ahpra was checked (evidence of registration is required to be uploaded to PaNDA record)

Table 10

Item

Description

1

Nurse practitioners and midwives + Read more ...

Nurse practitioners and midwives are only eligible for a provider number with access to Medicare benefits where they are working in private practice or the location is listed on a 19(2) or 19(5) Direction. If the requested location is not a private practice or not listed on a 19(2) or 19(5) Direction, service rule 618 is added to the location to prevent Medicare benefits being paid.

All allied health and non-medical health professionals + Read more ...

When a health professional is eligible to access Medicare benefits but does not satisfy business structure legislation by not supplying business structure and banking details, PSR 618 is added to the location to prevent Medicare benefits being paid.

PSR 618 is added to the location indefinitely.

This service rule can be deleted when business structure and banking details have been supplied for the location.
