Considerations prior to issuing a DPO

Ensure the person meets the following criteria before considering a DPO:

  • They have social security, family assistance, paid parental leave or student assistance debt/s with an outstanding amount
  • They are not currently receiving a payment from the agency
  • There is evidence of regular overseas travel
  • No satisfactory payment arrangement for the debt is in place

Complete the following steps before drafting a DPO submission:

  • Consider the person's repayment history, see Arranging cash repayments to recover debts
  • Confirm all debts are correct
  • Check the person's home, postal address and other contact details on the record are correct. Conduct all searches to obtain current details
  • Contact the person and/or nominee to discuss the repayment of their debt. Request payment in full. If this is not suitable, negotiate a payment arrangement. If the customer refuses to enter into a payment arrangement, advise the customer of the debt recovery warnings including:
    • referral to an External Collection Agent (ECA)
    • bank, wage and tax garnishee
    • civil action, or
    • placement of a DPO
  • Check all other available recovery options have been taken into consideration, including, tax, bank account, or wage garnishee. See Garnishees
  • Check if any of the debt/s are subject to an external collection agent (ECA) referral. Check the history of contact and payment in the ECA portal. Before submitting the request to issue a DPO recall all debts referred to an ECA. See General information about External Collection Agents (ECA)
  • Check the person has capacity to repay their debt or make a suitable payment arrangement. Check this through:
    • available income and asset information
    • ATO Asset and Income register
    • Equifax and RP Data Ltd for company and real estate details

Resolve outstanding issues + Read more ...

Including, but not limited to:

  • undetermined debts
  • Authorised Review Officer (ARO) or Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) implementations
  • new claims
  • outstanding reviews on the Review Summary (IRRS) screen
  • outstanding ACTDOCs
  • correspondence which has been received but not actioned

Unresolved issues may affect the decision to issue a DPO. Consider if the legislative requirements for a DPO have been met. Consider all relevant circumstances, as a DPO restricts the normal liberties of citizens, residents and visitors to Australia.

Create a document that includes the:

  • details of the investigation
  • details of conversation with the customer, and
  • the decision if not proceeding with the DPO submission

Create a DPO record + Read more ...

Store the DPO record in the Departure Prohibition Order System (DPOS) within the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS).

Create a new DPO record:

  • go to the DPO list (OPDPL) screen. Check there are no current DPO's under the relevant Act with the status 'Approved' or 'Referred'
  • go to the Add DPO (OPDPA) screen and select the relevant Debt Recovery Authority code
  • enter the Referral date which is:
    • the date of referral to the Specialised Recovery Team (SRT), or
    • the first day the DPO case was actioned by the SRT team

On the OPDPL screen, the DPO status will show as 'Referred'.

Create a DPO record for each Act covered by the DPO submission.

4

Prepare a DPO submission + Read more ...

Prepare a submission to the delegate using the DPO Submission template. Indicate if more than one DPO under different Acts is being used.

Detail how the DPO meets legislative conditions:

  • if there is 1 or more debts under the same Act, check each debt and document individually
  • if there are multiple debts under different Acts, check each debt and document individually
  • there are no suitable payment arrangements for the debt/s, and
  • a belief on reasonable grounds to make the order to ensure the person does not leave Australia without:
    • having wholly paid the 1 or more debts, or
    • there being arrangements satisfactory to the Secretary for the full repayment of 1 or more debts

State any other factors that may affect the decision. If incomplete or insufficient the officer will need to review and amend the submission.

If the submission requires National Manager approval complete a Cover Sheet and include it with the submission.

Email the submission to the SRT Team Leader with the subject heading 'FOR DECISION; DPO Submission - Debtors name - CRN.

See, Debt Programme and Compensation Recovery and Compliance Programme Branch Contacts for the SRT positional mailbox.

How to vary a DPO

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Vary a DPO + Read more ...

Any changes made to a DPO will be to correct errors on the face of the order itself, on:

  • the discretion of the Secretary
  • application by the person
  • incorrect issue date of the DPO

It is not necessary to vary a DPO due to a change in debt amount or receipt of payment.

A DPO cannot be varied to allow a person to depart Australia. A DPO can be revoked, or the person can apply for a Departure Authorisation Certificate (DAC) where necessary

Note:

  • If a DPO is issued under the wrong Act, revoke the DPO and reissue it under the correct Act.
  • If the DPO is issued to the incorrect person, revoke the DPO immediately. Issue a new DPO to the correct person

Determine if revoking the DPO is valid and preparing the submission

Table 5

Step

Action

1

When DPO can be revoked + Read more ...

A DPO must be revoked if:

  • the person no longer has any debts outstanding
  • the person has a satisfactory payment arrangement to repay their debts in full
  • the relevant debts are totally irrecoverable
  • Services Australia receives a request from the Department of Home Affairs to revoke a DPO. This is as a result of issuing a Deportation Notice
  • the person starts receiving a Centrelink payment
  • the person has passed away
  • there was use of an incorrect Act reference
  • there was an order issued to the incorrect person
  • there was an order issued with the person's incorrect date of birth

A DPO may be revoked if:

  • the person applied
  • at the discretion of the SRT
  • if the delegate considers it desirable to revoke the DPO

2

Applying to revoke a DPO + Read more ...

A person can apply to have a DPO revoked verbally or in writing. The application must:

  • state which DPO they are applying for (if they have more than 1)
  • why they believe the DPO should be revoked
  • supply supporting documentation to assist with the decision

Refer persons applying to have a DPO revoked to the Specialised Recovery Team (SRT).

For:

  • SRT staff, go to Step 3
  • All other staff, see Debt Programme and Compensation Recovery Branch

3

SRT discussion with customer + Read more ...

Discuss:

  • the persons request to have their DPO revoked
  • options the person will need to consider with their request:
    • if they can pay their outstanding debt/s in full
    • If they can make a satisfactory payment arrangement to pay off their debts in full, and
    • the possibility of issuing a Departure Authorisation Certificate (DAC)
  • advise the person of their review and appeal rights if the agency refuses their request to revoke the DPO. The normal review and appeal rights do not apply. See Review or appeal a DPO or DAC decision

Record the application and discussion on a DOC on the person's record.

4

Investigate and verify circumstances + Read more ...

The SRT should:

  • review the DPO case and in particular why the agency placed the DPO
  • review the reason for revocation
  • validate information provided by the person in their application
  • consider revoking the DPO

5

Prepare a submission to revoke a DPO + Read more ...

Use the DPO Revocation Submission template to prepare a submission to the delegate to consider revoking a DPO.

The submission may recommend the revocation of 1 or more DPOs under different Act references.

The submission must specify how the legislative provisions or policy conditions apply:

  • if the person no longer has 1 or more debts under the same Act, check each debt. Document and check them individually
  • if the person has made satisfactory arrangements to pay the 1 or more debts in full
  • document the arrangement details and timeframe
  • detail the reasons why the debts are irrecoverable
  • detail where Services Australia has received a request from the Department of Home Affairs to revoke a DPO. This is as a result of issuing of a Deportation Notice
  • detail when the person started receiving a Centrelink payment and the type of payment
  • detail when the person became deceased
  • detail the reason and findings of the person applying for revocation
  • detail why the SRT or delegate would like to revoke a DPO

Clearly state any other factors affecting the decision to issue/not issue a DPO revocation. The SRT Team Leader will return any submissions to the SRT officer for further action when:

  • they are incomplete, or
  • do not include enough reasoning as to why we should revoke the DPO

If the revocation submission is to go the National Manager or the General Manager, include the relevant cover sheet.

Email the submission and completed documents to the SRT Team Leader. Include 'FOR DECISION: DPO Revocation Submission - Debtors name - CRN' in the subject line.

See Debt Programme and Compensation Recovery and Compliance Programme Branch Contacts for the:

  • Specialised Recovery Team's, contact details, and
  • positional mailbox
  • Update the DPO DOC

  • check the content to ensure that staff have appropriately considered the circumstances of the person, and

Return the submission to the SRT if additional information is needed. The SRT will amend the submission and resubmit to the delegate.
