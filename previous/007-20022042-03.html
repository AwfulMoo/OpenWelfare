Viewing apportionment details

Step

Action

1

Reviewing apportionment of CCS hours + Read more ...

If a customer has apportioned their CCS hours for a child enrolled at 2 or more services, view details in Process Direct.

Key 'APP' in the Super Key field to go to the Child Care Subsidy Apportionment Details table:

  • Start Date: the date the arrangement started. It must be the first Monday of a CCS fortnight
  • End Date: the date the arrangement ended or is due to end. It must be the last Sunday of a CCS fortnight
  • Child CRN: the child to whom the apportionment applies
  • Enrolment ID & Apportionment Hours: the hours to be apportioned to each service. To identify the service, refer to the Child Care Summary screen and view the child’s details under the Enrolment twisty
  • Total Apportionment Hours: the customer’s hours of entitlement. The apportioned amounts cannot be less than or more than these hours
  • Apportionment Inactive Indicator:
    • Displays Yes if there has been a change in circumstances before the due start date. This means the arrangement will not be applied. For example:
      hours of entitlement changed
      one of the enrolments ended, or
      the child no longer attends 2 or more services
    • An arrangement may become inactive due to a retrospective change which takes effect before the start date of the apportionment. This means CCS will be assessed as no apportionment ever existing. The customer may incur arrears or a debt, depending on the sessions and rates of care. A reassessment will process session reports in the order received.
  • Rejection Reason Code: this only displays when the customer’s circumstances changed before or during the apportion period
    • ACTTST - Activity Test Result: a change in Activity Test result means the apportioned hours do not align with the customer’s entitlement. This may be a retrospective change. E.g. hours change from 72 to 100, or 100 to 36
    • ARRDTE - Arrangement Dates Changed: the enrolment start dates have changed which fall outside the apportionment dates
    • ENRCON - Enrolment Confirmed: the customer confirmed a new enrolment (with an existing or new service) where they already have multiple active enrolments. They will need to make a new arrangement
    • ENREND - Enrolment Ended: the enrolment for one of the services in the arrangement has ceased

2

Changes in circumstances + Read more ...

If an arrangement ends due to a circumstance change, the system adjusts the End Date to the last Sunday of the CCS fortnight in which the change occurred.

Depending on the change of circumstance, this could also result in the arrangement being made Inactive. If this occurs, it will be taken as no apportionment ever existing. This means the customer may incur arrears or a debt, depending on the sessions and rates of care. Reassessment will process session reports in the order they were received.

3

No apportionment arrangement + Read more ...

If customers do not allocate their hours of CCS for a child attending 2 or more child care services:

  • CCS entitlement will be processed in the order Attendance Reports are received from the child care service
  • This happens until the eligible hours of subsidy are used up

Recording a customer's request to apportion CCS hours

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Confirm eligibility to apportion CCS hours + Read more ...

These criteria must be met:

  • the child is enrolled at 2 or more services at the same time
  • the customer's eligible hours more than zero

Is the customer eligible to apportion hours?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, advise the customer they do not meet the criteria to apportion hours of entitlement. Procedure ends here

2

Code CCS Apportionment table + Read more ...

In Process Direct, go the Child Care Summary screen. Select the Child Care icon or key 'CCS' in the Super Key field.

Take note of the hours of entitlement and the child’s enrolment numbers for each service the child attends.

Key 'APP' in the Super Key field to go to the Child Care Subsidy Apportionment Details table:

  • Select
  • Complete the Create Apportionment popup box:
  • Start Date: cannot be a past date. It must be the first Monday of a CCS fortnight
    • End Date: advised by the customer. This must be the last Sunday of a CCS fortnight. If they do not advise a date, enter the last Sunday of the CCS year. This is the latest an end date can be coded as Apportionment arrangements cease at the end of each financial year.
      See the Resources page for a link to the Centrelink Calendar to look up the first Monday or last Sunday of a CCS fortnight
    • Child CRN: select the relevant child from the dropdown menu
    • Enrolment ID: each enrolment for the child will present. Enter the number of hours to be apportioned to each child care service. Ensure total hours = hours of entitlement
    • Select Save
  • Complete the Update Header Data. Receipt Date = date the update is being made
  • Select Save
  • Select Assess
  • Review the outcome of the assessment on the APP

3

Advise customer + Read more ...

  • The arrangement will end at the earliest of:
    • the start of the CCS fortnight when their hours of entitlement change
    • one of the child’s enrolment is ceased or withdrawn, or the customer disputes or rejects the enrolment
    • the end of the current CCS financial year
    • the date specified by the customer
    • the child’s entitlement ends (e.g. child no longer eligible as they have started secondary school)
  • If one service does not use the apportioned hours for the fortnight, any balance of hours apportioned to that service will not ‘transfer’ to any other service
  • Any unused hours apportioned to a service will not be ‘reallocated’ when CCS is balanced at the end of the CCS year
  • Cancelling the arrangement cannot be backdated. Customers must contact as soon as they no longer need the arrangement

4

Record Note + Read more ...

In Process Direct record a Note confirming details of the customer’s request to apportion hours of CCS.

The Note must also confirm:

'The customer understood their obligations to cancel the arrangement if it no longer suits their needs. They are aware unused hours apportioned to a service will not be ‘transferred’ to another service or ‘reallocated’ when CCS is balanced at the end of the CCS year.

Varying or ending apportioning of CCS hours

Table 3

Step

Action

1

When apportioning automatically stops + Read more ...

The arrangement will stop at the earliest of:

  • the start of the CCS fortnight when their hours of entitlement change
  • one of the enrolment IDs ceased or a new enrolment confirmed
  • the end of the current CCS financial year
  • the date specified by the customer
  • the child’s entitlement ends (e.g. child is no longer eligible as they have started secondary school)

2

Customer requests + Read more ...

Customers may contact to:

  • cancel their arrangement, or
  • vary their current arrangement
    • entitlement hours have changed, or
    • changing the apportionment between services

Note: before changing a current arrangement it must first be ended.

3

Ending current arrangement + Read more ...

In Process Direct, key 'APP' in the Super Key field to go to the Child Care Subsidy Apportionment Details table:

  • Select the Edit icon
  • Complete the Change Apportionment popup box:
    • End Date: key the date requested by the customer. This must be the last Sunday of a CCS fortnight. It cannot be in the past.r
      See the Resources page for a link to the Centrelink Calendar to look up the last Sunday of a CCS fortnight
    • Do not change any other information
    • Select Save
  • Complete the Update Header Data. Receipt Date = date the update is being made
  • Select Save

Is the customer varying their current arrangement?

  • Yes, code new arrangement and advise the customer. See Steps 2-4 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Finalise end of apportionment + Read more ...

  • Select Assess
  • Review the outcome of the assessment on the SSP
  • Record in a Note the details of customer’s request to end the arrangement.

5

When a past end date is required + Read more ...

As arrangement may not have been ended correctly on a date in the past. This may be due to system delays or staff error..

Escalate the record via mySupport with details of the cancellation date and why the end date is to be backdated.
