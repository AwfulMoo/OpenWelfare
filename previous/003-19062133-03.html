Eligibility criteria for Flood Education Supplement (FES)

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Qualifying payments + Read more ...

Determine if the student was eligible for a qualifying payment for at least one day during the relevant period, between 25 January 2019 and 14 February 2019 (inclusive):

For AIC:

  • Assistance for Isolated Children Basic Boarding Allowance (EBB/EBBO)
  • Assistance for Isolated Children Second Home Allowance (ESH)
  • Assistance for Isolated Children Distance Education Allowance (EDE)

For ABSTUDY:

  • ABSTUDY School Fees Allowance (Group 2) (SFA2T/SFA2B)

2

Parental home eligibility + Read more ...

Determine if the parental home is located in an affected Local Government Area (LGA):

  • Open Office Locator, see Resources for a link
  • Enter the Suburb/Town of the parental home
  • Confirm the parental home LGA

Note: Office Locator searches must not be undertaken using Postcode see Resources for a link.

Is the parental home located in an impacted LGA?

  • Yes, for
    • AIC go to Step 3
    • ABSTUDY go to Step 5
  • No, the student is not eligible for Flood Education Supplement(FES). Procedure ends here

3

Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) + Read more ...

In the parent record:

  • navigate to the Link Summary (LS) screen
  • locate the student record/s, based on a Link Type of EIC
  • place an 'L' in the field corresponding with the relevant student to navigate to the student’s record

In the student record, navigate to the:

  • Small Benefit System by typing 'SVAIC' in the Next field and 'ISS' in the System field
  • Benefit Status List by typing 'XBS' in the Next field

Was the student AIC CUR for at least 1 day between 25 January 2019 and 14 February 2019 (inclusive)?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the student is not eligible for FES. Procedure ends here

4

Confirm AIC payment type + Read more ...

To qualify for FES, the AIC student must be in receipt of Basic Boarding Allowance, Second Home Allowance or Distance Education Allowance. To determine the allowance:

  • Navigate to the Payment Summary (PS) screen
  • Select the respective AIC payment. Note: you may have to navigate to ‘months in the past’

Is the payment type 'EBB', 'EBBO', 'ESH' or 'EDE'?

  • Yes, student may be eligible for FES. Go to Step 6
  • No, the student is not eligible for FES. Procedure ends here

5

Confirm ABSTUDY payment type + Read more ...

To qualify for FES, ABSTUDY students must be in receipt of ABSTUDY School Fees Allowance (Group 2).

In the student record:

  • Navigate to Payment Summary (PS) screen
  • Check to ensure the student has been paid an ABSTUDY payment in 2019, with the payment type SFA2T or SFA2B

Has the student been paid an 'SFA2T' or 'SFA2B' payment for at least 1 day between 25 January 2019 and 14 February 2019 (inclusive)?

  • Yes, student may be eligible for FES. Go to Step 7
  • No, the student is not eligible for FES. Procedure ends here

6

Check AIC payment destination + Read more ...

Payments for FES cannot be made to a third party payee (for example, school). Where this is the case, the parent/guardian payment destination details need to be obtained.

For AIC:

  • In the Student record, enter ‘SVAIC’ in the Next field
  • Navigate to the Payment Instructions (PIPI) screen

Is the payment recipient/payee a third party?

  • Yes, and the applicant is responding to a Q888 letter issued or the applicant is eligible and has not received the FES payment, see to Step 1 in the Customer/Applicant providing payment destination details table
  • No, and the applicant is eligible and has not received the FES payment:
    • document the details of the contact by running the FES Enquiry Fast Note. Advise the applicant FES payments will be made for eligible students

Procedure ends here.

7

Check ABSTUDY payment destination + Read more ...

Payments for FES cannot be made to a third party payee (for example, school). Where this is the case, parent or student (if independent or 18 years or older) payment destination details need to be obtained.

  • In the Student record, type ‘SVABY’ in the Next field
  • Navigate to the Payment Summary (PS) screen

Does the PS screen show the ABSTUDY student received a qualifying ABSTUDY payment in 2019 with a net amount of greater than $0?

  • Yes, and the parent or student is eligible and has not received the FES payment:
    • document the details of the contact by running the FES Enquiry Fast Note
  • No, select the most recent $0 net payment and press [Enter] until the PDD screen is reached to confirm third party payee details:
    • Once confirmed, if the parent or student is responding to a Q888 letter issued or they are eligible and have not received the FES payment, see Step 1 in the Customer/Applicant providing payment destination details table

Customer/Applicant providing payment destination details

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Eligible customer/applicant contacts to provide payment destination details + Read more ...

If the payment destination details are in:

  • the AIC applicant’s parent/guardian name(s), or
  • a nominee’s name, where the AIC applicant has a nominee arrangement in place, or
  • a parent’s name for a dependent ABSTUDY student under the age of 18 years, or
  • the student’s name, for ABSTUDY students aged 18 years or older or who are independent
  • Go to Step 2

If the payment details are in any other name advise the customer/applicant Flood Education Supplement (FES) can only be paid into one of the above payment destinations. Procedure ends here.

Note: where phone contact with the customer/applicant has been unsuccessful a letter may have been issued, and a Fast Note DOC (FES - Outbound Contact) will display on the record.

2

To update payment destination for FES + Read more ...

  • Record payment destination details by running the FES - Action Required Fast Note. Note: do not close this Fast Note DOC upon completion
  • advise the customer/applicant their payment destination details will be provided to the AIC Smart Centre Processing Staff and if eligible they will be paid FES

Procedure ends here.

Processing Flood Education Supplement (FES)

Table 3: For AIC Smart Centre processing staff only

Step

Action

1

Flood Education Supplement(FES) - Action Required Fast Note allocated + Read more ...

Payment is made on the AIC/ABSTUDY student record.

In Customer First (within the student record):

  • ensure the Small Benefit System (ISS) is selected
  • go to the Next field, type 'SVAAA' and Enter
  • goe to the !FFAP screen

2

On the !FFAP screen, update the following information + Read more ...

Enter the Event ID field - select the Magnifying Glass button and code:

  • Y32 for ABSTUDY Customers
  • Y33 for AIC Customers

Note: the screen will dynamically update to display more fields.

Update the following information:

  • Select the Grant radio button (after selecting this, the screen will again dynamically display more fields)
  • Grant Reason: select No Reason Required from the drop down menu
  • Date Affected by Event: input today’s date
  • Source: PER
  • Receipt Date: Today’s date
  • Check the amount, then select Continue to go to the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen

3

Update the PAS screen + Read more ...

  • Select the FSP entry to go to the Payment Destination Overseas (PAEO) screen

On the PAEO screen, update the following information:

  • Method of Payment: AUS
  • FI Country: AU
  • FI Identifier: BSB for Payment Destination
  • Account Number: Account Number for Payment Destination
  • Account Title: Account Title for Payment Destination

Note: all other fields must be left blank.

4

Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen + Read more ...

To finalise the activity:

  • Select Continue to navigate to the Assessment Result (AR) screen
  • Create a Grant DOC, using Fast Note template - FES Payment Granted

5

Finalise in Customer First/Customer Record + Read more ...

In Customer first on the Payees record:

  • Send a FES - Q888 Grant letter
  • Use the following text for Q888 Grant letter, see Creating a Q999 or Q888 letter

Free text paragraph

  • Your payment for Flood Education Supplement

Free text 1

We have paid you Flood Education Supplement for {STUDENTS NAME} on {DATE}. The Flood Education Supplement is a one-off $1,000 payment. This payment is to help isolated families affected by the flood in North Queensland with education and related costs.

Free text 2

  • An immediate payment of $1,000 has been paid to your bank account.

Free text 3

  • For more information about this supplement, please go to servicesaustralia.gov.au and search for Flood Education Supplement.
