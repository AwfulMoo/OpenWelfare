QA checks for an EP claim + Read more ...

EP claims undergo a streamlined assessment.

  • Yes, go to Step 4

4

Conduct a full assessment of an EP claim + Read more ...

The QA process is also a compliance check for EP claims.

Conduct a full assessment of the EP claim (not a QA check). Use the Operational Blueprint for the claim type:

  • Northbound domestic claim
  • Northbound other markets claim
  • Southbound primary producer claim
  • Southbound manufactures and miners clam
  • Intrastate (KIFG) claim
  • Sportsperson or entertainer claim
  • Broodmare or horse claim

Does the full assessment show errors in the claim that are outside the EP criteria?

  • Yes, return the claim to the Service Officer, select QA back to Assessor in the TFES Assessor Tool. In the comment section of the TFES Assessor Tool enter the follow-up action, including:
  • corrections needed
  • if feedback and support to the claimant is needed enter detailed notes on the Claim Comments in myHSI check Claimant Notes and refer to a Service Support Officer or a Team Leader for exclusion from EP
    Note:     if the Service Officer is not fully trained in the claim type, the QA officer completes the above action
  • No, see Table 2

5

QA checks for fully assessed claims + Read more ...

Determine the claim type:

  • Northbound domestic claim
  • Northbound other markets claim
  • Southbound primary producer claim
  • Southbound manufacturers and miners claim
  • Southbound imported goods claim
  • Intrastate claim (KIFG)
  • Sportsperson or entertainer claim
  • Broodmare or horse claim

Service Officer must follow the relevant Operational Blueprint when completing the claim assessment.

Ensure the correct customer is claiming, for example:

  • check they are the right person
  • the signatory details (for paper claims only)

Read notes on the customer record in myHSI for customer and assessment details. Check the claim to ensure the Service Officer addressed all identified areas of concern for the claimant/claim type.

Check all details on the claim form against all the supporting documents.

Focus on:

  • If goods are sent to a distribution centre, make sure the Service Officer addressed whether:
    • the goods should be claimed as Domestic or Other Markets
    • any goods are returned to Tasmania
  • Southbound goods are eligible under the relevant P or M Scheme listing. These goods should be sent to the customer for use in primary production (P) or manufacturing or mining (M). The invoice should charge the customer
  • Northbound goods being claimed are listed on Schedule 1 of the Ministerial Directions
  • Correct Free Into Store freight rates and wharf-to-wharf freight rates were used to determine the freight paid (if applicable)
  • Rates are up-to-date:
    • Free Into Store freight rates less than 2 years old
    • wharf-to-wharf freight rates less than 1 year old
  • Relevant resources were used
  • Claim comments were completed on myHSI for any adjustments
  • All contact and correspondence with claimants/agents/transport companies/third parties is documented and uploaded to the record or claim
  • Duplicate invoices warnings have been checked and the check noted in comments
  • Note to claimant completed and uploaded to Claim Documents tab (if applicable)
  • Record on myHSI updated appropriately
  • Adjusted Lodgement Date was recorded correctly with appropriate claim comment

See Table 2.

6

QA check for claimant detail additions/updates + Read more ...

This check applies to the data entry of new claims and updates to existing claimant details.

To complete the QA check for:

  • creating a new claimant record, see Created a new claimant record table of Register a TFES claimant
  • updating existing claims see Update TFES claimant information

Check the claimant record in myHSI against the Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme - Registration and change of details (TAS020) to ensure the details are entered correctly, including:

  • claimant's registered trading name
  • ABN
  • postal address
  • contact phone numbers/fax
  • email
  • bank account details
  • signatories updated/removed
  • declaration section signed by an authorised person

If the claimant provided a Claims Agent Authorisation (TAS030), check:

  • the claims agent/claimant record has been set up correctly using correct acronym, claims agent address, and signatories
  • a correct person has signed the TAS030
  • the correct bank account has been entered on the claims agent/claimant record as per the TAS030
  • a Relationship link has been set up correctly between the claimant, the agent shell record and the claims agent/claimant record

When a new claimant is added or the signatories on an existing record change, the Service Officer creates an activity in the TFES Assessor tool for online registration. The status of this activity will be Awaiting internal advice.

The QA Officer updates the status of the online registration activity to QA back to assessor after approving the new claimant record or updates to the existing record are approved.

See Table 2.

7

QA check for claim adjustments + Read more ...

100% of all claim adjustments are quality checked.

  • Review the Underpayment or Overpayment report, which will be uploaded to the claim tab in myHSI
  • Check:
  • if the review request was made in writing and submitted within 30 days of the claim payment date
  • if the review request states why the person is making the request and is supported by appropriate documentary evidence
  • the claim adjustment was done correctly and the system updated
  • the correct procedure was followed

See Table 2.

8

QA check for claimant eligibility assessments + Read more ...

100% of all claimant eligibility assessments are quality checked.

  • Select the eligibility activity from the awaiting QA officer list in the TFES Assessor Tool
  • Determine the new claimant eligibility type
  • In myHSI, access the eligibility documents from the Claimant Documents tab
  • Check the Service Officer has uploaded the eligible/ineligible letter separately, as a PDF file
  • Check that the Service Officer has followed the correct table in the Register a TFES claimant:
    • Northbound and other markets claimant eligibility
    • Southbound Manufacturers and Miners eligibility (M Scheme)
    • Southbound Primary Producers eligibility (P Scheme)
    • Intrastate eligibility
    • Sportsperson, Professional entertainer or brood mare and foal

Did the QA check show the Service Officer followed the correct eligibility table in Register a TFES claimant?

  • Yes,
    • In myHSI, View Claimant Details screen, select Update
    • Use dropdown to change the Special Circumstance field from Pending Eligibility Checks to blank. See Step 3 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 5 in Table 2

9

QA check for assessing eligibility of southbound goods + Read more ...

100% of all eligibility assessments for adding Manufacturers and Miners southbound goods are quality checked.

  • Select the Add M Scheme Item activity from the awaiting QA officer list in the TFES Assessor Tool
  • In myHSI, access documents for the application for southbound goods from the Claimant Documents tab
  • Check the Service Officer followed Add or update southbound TFES items and suppliers

Did the QA check show the Service Officer followed the correct processes in Add or update southbound TFES items and suppliers?

  • Yes, see Step 3 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 5 in Table 2

Recording details and giving feedback

Table 2

Step

Action

1

EP claim with no errors + Read more ...

Is this activity an EP claim and the EP process was performed correctly, and non-processing errors have been identified?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 2

2

EP process followed + Read more ...

For EP claims, the QA result refers to the EP process only.

Has the Service Officer followed the steps correctly in the Express Process of TFES claims?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Record QA result + Read more ...

Go back to the activity in the TFES Assessor Tool:

  • Tick the boxes next to each Getting It Right (GIR) component
  • Under the Outcome heading, select Correct
  • Select Save QA
  • Select Send email

Go into Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme (TFES) General in myHSI:

  • From Activity List
    • Activity select all
    • Role/User select QA Officer
    • tick the Show Unassigned box and select show
  • Select the activity from the QA Activity List and approve
  • Enter comments about the QA check
  • For claims and claim adjustments, entre a comment on the Claim Comments tab
  • For claimant detail additions/updates, entre a comment on the Claimant Comments tab
  • For claimants eligibility checks, enter a comment on the Claimant Comments

Paperwork

  • If a system generated letter has printed, collect from local printer TSAPR25
  • If a Note to Claimant is uploaded to the claim in myHSI, print and send with payment letter
  • Place any claimant correspondence in an envelope in the outgoing mail
  • For claims, complete details in the TFES batch spreadsheet

Procedure ends here.

4

Record correct QA result but refer non-processing errors to Service Officer + Read more ...

Go back to the activity in the TFES Assessor Tool:

  • Tick the boxes next to each Getting It Right (GIR) component
  • Under the Outcome heading, select Correct
  • Enter details about the non-processing errors in the QA Notes section. Request the Service Officer correct the claim. Provide as much detail as possible

Go into Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme (TFES) General in myHSI:

  • From Activity List
    • Activity select all
    • Role/User select QA Officer
    • tick the Show Unassigned box and select show
  • Select Continue
  • Select Claim ID link
  • Copy the comments about the non-processing errors from the QA Notes section in the TFES Assessor Tool and enter into Comments tab

Go to Step 6.

5

Record QA error in the TFES Assessor Tool + Read more ...

In the activity in the TFES Assessor Tool:

  • Leave the box blank next to the relevant Getting It Right (GIR) component the error relates to. For example:
    • Right person
      names
      ABN
      address
      bank Details
      contact
      signatories
    • Right rates
      claim error types (listed under error type drop down list in TFES Assessor Tool
    • Right dates
      shipment date
      claim lodgement date
      southbound ineligibility date
    • Right program
      scheme
      component
  • For error codes, see the Resources page
  • For EP claims, the error should relate to the EP process only
  • In the notes section, record as much detail as possible. Include details such as the line numbers incorrect in a claim. Include any action required. Include details of all errors if more than one found
  • Record the outcome of either critical error or non-critical error
    • Critical errors affect eligibility or payments
      Example: a Service Officer mistypes the volume of the shipment. Refer this activity back to the Service Officer with feedback for them to correct
    • Non-critical errors do not affect eligibility or payments, but the Service Officer did not follow the correct process
      Example: a Service Officer chooses the incorrect freight forwarder code. Accept this activity and give feedback to the Service Officer

6

Send feedback + Read more ...

In the TFES Assessor Tool select Save QA and Send email.

This will send an email to the relevant Service Officer with information on the error.

If the activity needs to be returned for a follow-up check by the QA Checker:

  • Include this in the QA Notes section of the TFES Assessor Tool (and QA email)
  • Instruct the Service Officer to:
    • return the claim to Awaiting QA Officer status in the TFES Tool after finalising
    • make a note in the TFES Assessor Tool that this is being returned for a QA follow-up
  • This follow-up may be randomly selected for QA by myHSI. Do not process as a separate QA activity. The follow up is to ensure the issue has been rectified

Then go back into Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme (TFES) General in myHSI:

  • From Activity List
    • Activity select all
    • Role/User select QA Officer, tick the sort by box and select show
    • tick the Show Unassigned box and select show
  • Select the activity from the QA Activity List
  • Select continue
  • Send the activity back to the Service Officer
