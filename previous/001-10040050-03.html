This page contains information on establishing contact with job seekers to discuss non-compliance or potential barriers to compliance.

On this Page:

Smart Centre referring customers to the PST

Service centre referring customers to the PST

Smart Centre referring customers to the PST

Table 1:

Item

Description

1

Cold transfer the job seeker to PST + Read more ...

When a Smart Centre Service Officer identifies a job seeker needs to speak to the PST they must perform a cold transfer (that is, unannounced) to the appropriate customer-facing queue as follows:

PST Remote Customer

  • If the job seeker lives in Zone Northern Australia, or
  • If the job seeker has been referred to a Community Development Program (CDP) provider

PST National Virtual

  • If the job seeker has not been referred to a CDP provider and does not live in Zone Northern Australia

2

Smart Centre Service Officers transferring job seekers calling from a mobile to PST + Read more ...

If a job seeker is calling on a mobile phone, they may incur additional phone charges whilst waiting in the queue to speak to a PST Service Officer.

Job seekers can avoid these charges by using free phones available in the service centre.

3

Transfers from the Multilingual Phone Service (MPS) + Read more ...

MPS Service Officers have access to a direct spectrum for all job seekers who call MPS and need to be referred to PST. These calls must be cold transferred (that is, unannounced). To help the PST-skilled Service Officer identify what type of Fast Track interpreter is required, MPS Service Officers must record a detailed DOC on the job seeker's record.

Contact Team Leader for the spectrum number if required.

Service centre referring customers to the PST

Table 2:

Item

Description

1

Refer the job seeker to PST + Read more ...

When a service centre Service Officer identifies a job seeker needs to speak to the PST, they must advise the job seeker to contact PST:

  • via the self service phones on site, or
  • by directing them to call the Participation Solution Team (PST)

It is not appropriate for service centre Service Officers to use internal speed dial numbers to transfer a job seeker.

Protocols have been established for phone contact between managers/team leaders in service centres and the PST to provide alerts and manage potential customer aggression threats. For further information, see Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing.
