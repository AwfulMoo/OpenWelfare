Telehealth practitioner billing requirements

Requirements for telehealth practitioner billing

This table describes the telehealth practitioner billing requirements and related direct bill forms to use for bulk billed telehealth consultations.

Practitioner type

Billing requirements

Direct Bill (DB) form to use
  • Specialist
  • Consultant Physician
  • Psychiatrist

Telehealth consultations must be billed separately to other consultations. Only the telehealth Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) derived fee item and the associated base consultation item can be detailed on any 1 claim or account. This ensures the derived fee item number is allocated to the correct base item number.

For example, item 104 plus item 99 only.

All other services must be detailed in a separate invoice or lodged in a separate claim.

DB2-OT - Nurse Practitioners and Midwives
  • Medical practitioner
  • Nurse practitioner
  • Midwife
  • Practice nurse
  • Aboriginal health worker

There is no requirement to lodge Telehealth claims separately from other claims, as there are no derived fee items used by supporting practitioners.

Privacy note

Specialist or Consultant privacy note for email agreement process for patient signature

The following privacy notes are by the specialist or consultant issued when a patient assigns their right to a Medicare benefit to a specialist via email:

"Your personal information is protected by law, including the Privacy Act 1988, and is collected by the Australian Government Services Australia for the assessment and administration of payments and services. This information is required to process your application or claim.

Your information may be used by Services Australia or given to other parties for the purposes of research, investigation or where you have agreed or it is required or authorised by law. You can get more information about the way in which Services Australia will manage your personal information, including our privacy policy at servicesaustralia.gov.au/privacy or by requesting a copy from Services Australia."

"If you (the patient) agree to the assignment of the Medicare benefit directly to the specialist (bulk bill), reply to this email including the word 'YES' in the body of the email, and your (the patient's) name."

Example specialist email

The following is an example of an email sent by the specialist when a patient to assigns their right to a Medicare benefit to a specialist via email:

"Dear Mr Jones

Item number: 104 - Benefit amount: $71.35

Item number: 99 - Benefit amount: 35.00

Date and time of service: 01/01/2012 10:30am

Patient: Peter Jones

Provider: Jane Smith

If you (the patient) agrees to the assignment of the Medicare benefit directly to specialist (bulk bill) reply to this email including:

  • the word 'YES' in the body of the email
  • your (the patient's) name

Regards

Dr Jane Smith
