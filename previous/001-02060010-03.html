2

Eligible for DES or ADE + Read more ...

Job seekers eligible for DES do not require a referral unless they want to access jobactive. This is voluntary. However, all job seekers must be encouraged to access jobactive and have an initial appointment booked and they will be able to lodge a resume. This will assist them to access a wide range of job vacancies even if they are doing other activities.

Does the job seeker wish to access services from jobactive?

  • Yes, continue with the referral. Go to Step 5
  • No, the job seeker does not require a referral at this time. Procedure ends here

3

Eligible for Intensive Support - Transition to Work (TtW) + Read more ...

Is the job seeker eligible for TtW?

  • Yes, follow the TtW referral process
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Mutual obligation exemptions + Read more ...

Check if the job seeker has one of the following mutual obligation requirements exemptions lasting more than six weeks in the future:

  • temporary incapacity
  • jury duty
  • volunteering during a state or national emergency

Does the jobseeker have an exemption?

  • Yes, the job seeker is not required to be referred to jobactive. They will be advised by letter toward the end of their exemption to contact Services Australia to arrange for the referral to jobactive. The process is adjusted to coincide with a new expiry date where the exemption period is reduced, extended or immediately followed by a different allowable mutual obligation requirements exemption
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Further exemptions + Read more ...

Check if the job seeker has one of the following mutual obligation requirements exemptions:

  • refugees (first 6 months)
  • refugees (first 13 weeks)
  • expectant mothers
  • job seekers serving a Community Service Order
  • job seekers with caring responsibilities
  • job seekers experiencing a major personal crisis
  • job seekers experiencing major personal disruption at home

Does the jobseeker have one of the above exemptions?

  • Yes, the customer is not required to be referred to jobactive during the exemption period, however, they can choose to volunteer. If the job seeker:
    • volunteers to participate continue with the referral, go to Step 6
    • chooses not to participate, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Referral to other programs + Read more ...

Make a referral for the job seeker to another participation program if appropriate. See:

  • Skills for Education and Employment (SEE)
  • New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS)
  • Reconnect
  • Adult Migrant English Program (AMEP)

Job seekers participating in these programs must still be referred to jobactive.

A voluntary referral to jobactive can also be offered when:

  • a customer is 21 years of age and under and
    • not on income support, or
    • in receipt of a payment without mutual obligation requirements, or
    • participating in one of the approved activities/programs that fully meet their requirements
  • a principal carer or a customer assessed as having a partial capacity to work is:
    • already fully meeting their requirements by undertaking suitable paid work or self-employment of at least 30 hours per fortnight or
    • studying full-time

Does the customer need a referral or accept a voluntary referral for jobactive?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, procedure ends here

7

Is the job seeker registering, already registered or re-registering? + Read more ...

  • If the referral is being done as part of an initial registration process, the List Providers screen will be presented. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • If the job seeker is already registered but has not been referred to an Employment Services Provider (for example, responding to the 'no provider' letter as exemption from mutual obligation requirements is ending), book an appointment through the Online Diary. Note: the job seeker must receive proper notification and reasonable notice of the appointment. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • If the job seeker is re-registering and already has a jobactive organisation, determine the allowable break in registration. Go to Step 8

8

Job seeker is re-registering + Read more ...

  • If the job seeker is re-registering within their allowable break in registration (13 weeks) there is no change in the registration date and they are reconnected to the same jobactive organisation, no selection/allocation is required. No appointment is needed. The jobactive organisation will contact the job seeker. Procedure ends here
  • For job seekers re-registering more than 13 weeks after the date of inactivation the referral process begins as if the job seeker is registering for the first time. See Step 1 in Table 2

Selection and allocation of a jobactive organisation

Table 2: This table describes the process of selecting and allocating a jobactive organisation. Note: this process does not apply to job seeker selected for the online jobactive employment service. These job seekers have the referral completed by the Department of Education, Skills and Employment.

Step

Action

1

The List Providers screen + Read more ...

A list of all providers mapped to the job seeker's postcode appears on this screen. More than one page may be available and there is an ability to scroll through these selections. Providers which are full or not yet open for business will display even though they may not be selected.

This screen provides different options to display jobactive organisations that are mapped to the job seeker's postcode. There are 2 radio buttons displayed on top of the screen: Show providers with Immediate Appointments, and Show all providers. The Show Providers with Immediate Appointments button will be defaulted on this screen.

  • For RapidConnect customers: when Show Providers with Immediate Appointments button is selected, the list will display only those providers that have appointments within the system date plus 2 working days. Select the Show All Providers radio button, the list will be amended to display those providers that have available appointments within 14 days
  • For non-RapidConnect customers: when Show Providers with Immediate Appointments is selected, the list will display only those providers that have appointments within the system date plus 2 working days. By selecting the Show All Providers radio button, the list will be amended to display those providers that have available appointments within 28 days

Providers who do not have available appointments on the day or within the next 2 working days will have a status of having 'no immediate appointments sessions within 2 business days'. Job seekers should be encouraged to choose a provider with immediate appointments available on the day or within 2 working days.

Ask the job seeker to select a jobactive organisation from the list.

  • only an 'Available', 'No sessions within 2 business days', and 'No immediate sessions within 14 business days' provider with available capacity can be selected
  • a job seeker can have only one jobactive organisation
  • if the job seeker's preferred jobactive organisation is full, they will need to select another organisation

Has the job seeker selected a jobactive organisation?

  • Yes, place the mouse on the provider details and select once. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Does the job seeker wish to refine the search criteria? + Read more ...

  • Yes, press the Refine Search button. Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Refine the search for jobactive organisation + Read more ...

The job seeker can refine the search criteria to find a provider more suitable to their needs.

Refine the search by selecting one of the following fields:

  • Provider Name: field
  • Provider Postcode: field

Enter the additional search criteria and conduct another search using the 'Search' button (select once).

4

Ask the job seeker to select a jobactive organisation + Read more ...

A list of providers resulting from the refined search appears on the List Providers screen. Please note that this screen may display differently on each web service call.

Ask the job seeker to select a provider.

Only an open provider with available capacity can be selected.

Has the job seeker selected a jobactive organisation from the list resulting from the refined search (es)?

  • Yes, place the mouse on the provider details and either select once or press [Enter]. Go to Step 6
  • No, randomly allocate a provider to the job seeker. Go to Step 5

5

Random allocation + Read more ...

Random allocation uses the criteria of the job seeker's postcode to find providers with available capacity.

Press the 'random allocation' button (select once) to randomly allocate a provider to the job seeker.

Selecting the random allocation button randomly allocates a provider to the job seeker and navigates to the Available Appointments screen.

If the job seeker is not happy with the provider allocated randomly, up to 4 additional random allocations can be undertaken.

To conduct another random allocation, press the 'back to initial list' button (select once).

If the job seeker is not happy with any of the maximum 5 random allocations, a jobactive organisation will be allocated.

6

Details of the selected jobactive organisation are displayed + Read more ...

The Available Appointments screen displays the following information about the selected provider:

  • name
  • address
  • phone number
  • service status
  • session type
  • specialist status (if any)

If the selected jobactive organisation has appointments available in the next 2 working days, these appointments will be displayed first. To view available appointments that are outside the next 2 working days, select the More Appointments button.

Where appropriate, contact the jobactive organisation to request more appointments to be opened within a certain timeframe (for example, next 2 working days). Once the organisation has scheduled more appointments, view the additional appointments by selecting the 'Refresh' button on the Available Appointments screen. If the resulting jobactive organisation has no immediate appointments, a reason as to why they have been chosen in preference to an organisation with immediate availability must be given. See the Resources page for a list of acceptable reasons. Note: this functionality has been temporarily removed from the Online Diary flow until further notice.

Wherever possible, job seekers should be encouraged to accept referrals to jobactive organisations with immediate appointments or their payment start date may be affected.

Open providers will not require a reason code.

7

Make an appointment on the Online Diary + Read more ...

  • Make an appointment on the Online Diary with the selected jobactive organisation
  • Print the appointment letter and give or post to the job seeker
  • Record the details of the appointment and that verbal notification has been given in a DOC
  • If booking the appointment through Customer First, record the DOC using Fast Note. Select Auto text > Jobseekers > Update > Provider Appointment Booked

Note: the job seeker must receive proper notification and reasonable notice of the appointment.
