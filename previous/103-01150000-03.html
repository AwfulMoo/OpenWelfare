Cashless Debit Card Pre-Exit Reviews

Family Responsibilities Commission

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Role of the Family Responsibilities Commission + Read more ...

  • Upon notification from an applicable state authority, the Family Responsibilities Commission (FRC) will decide what course of action is required for the customer and their family and determine if Cashless Debit Card may be appropriate. Applicable authorities can include state education department, child protection authority, registrar of a relevant court or lessor for a tenancy agreement
  • Provide Services Australia (the agency) with a referral for the customer/s to be placed on the Cashless Debit Card, including contact details and expected duration of participation on the Cashless Debit Card
  • Advise the agency of the percentage of payments to be placed on the Cashless Debit Card. Note: this could be 60%, 75% or 90%
  • Advise the customer/s they are subject to compulsory participation in the Cashless Debit Card and explain what this means, including expected duration of Cashless Debit Card participation
  • Case manage the individual. This is an ongoing process throughout the duration of the individual's Cashless Debit Card period

2

Responsibilities of the FRC + Read more ...

The FRC will be responsible for providing Services Australia with the details of customers within the designated localities who the state has determined would benefit from being under Cashless Debit Card from 17 March 2021.

The FRC will make notifications to, and consult with Services Australia. Once the FRC refers a customer, the agency will determine if they meet all other criteria required under the Cape York Initiative. If the customer is eligible for Cashless Debit Card, it will become compulsory.

Services Australia

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Services Australia role + Read more ...

  • Verify the validity of the referral
  • Cross-reference and record information received from the Family Responsibilities Commission (FRC) regarding the referral of a customer to be on the Cashless Debit Card
  • Identify if the referred customer is currently in receipt of a trigger payment (for example, category P welfare payment, category R welfare payment) and notify the FRC
  • Once a customer has been identified, determine if the customer meets all other eligibility criteria required under the Cape York Initiative
  • Facilitate the flow of information between the FRC in relation to the referred customer for example, change of circumstances

2

Contact Officer + Read more ...

Services Australia will nominate a position as the Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO). The IMCO will be responsible for liaising with the state FRC in relation to customers referred to the agency, who:

  • may benefit from Cashless Debit Card, and
  • live within the designated localities

This is from 17 March 2021.

Personal Information Disclosure

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Identifying eligible customers + Read more ...

The FRC will provide Services Australia with details of a person to identify if they meet the requirements for consideration for Cashless Debit Card. This is a Personal Information Disclosure (PID). FRC case managers complete PID requests through the Business Hub.

2

Business Hub’s role + Read more ...

The Business Hub will advise the case manager (in real time):

  • if the referred person is currently in receipt of an income support payment
  • the customer's current address details
  • the customer's eligibility results for Cape York Cashless Debit Card
  • if the customer is already subject to Cashless Debit Card
  • any partner and child (Family Tax Benefit (FTB) only) details
  • payment history, including payment category

3

Income Management Contact Officer’s (IMCO) role + Read more ...

The Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) has a role in checking the Third Party Information Summary – Organisation (TPISO) screen for any 'unprocessed' PID activities. This will occur when an FRC case manager cannot match the customer to a record or the eligibility result is 'no' in the Business Hub. For more information, see Actioning Business Hub requests.

Cashless Debit Card Pre-Exit Reviews

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Pre-exit review + Read more ...

For Income Management Contact Officer.

A review of Cashless Debit Card must be undertaken with the referring authority (Family Responsibility Commission) approximately thirty five days before the customer's Cashless Debit Card period is due to end.

A 'CDC ends in 35 days. SO to contact referring organisation to discuss’ Work Item will present via Workload Management (WLM) to prompt a pre-exit review. This activity can be viewed and finalised within Customer First on the customer’s Activity List (AL) screen. For more information, see Workload Management (WLM) in Customer First.

Note: the Work Item must not be removed without appropriate investigation and action as it may lead to appropriate updates not being completed or impact the customer's Cashless Debit Card payments.

The purpose of this review is to discuss the customer's progress to date, and for the referring authority to advise whether Cashless Debit Card will be extended. If this is the case a new Notice to Commence Cashless Debit Card will need to be lodged through the Business Hub.

To arrange a review with the referring authority, go to Step 2.

2

Arranging a Review with the Cashless Debit Card referring authority + Read more ...

For Income Management Contact Officer.

A review can be organised either over the phone or via email using an Outlook meeting invite to the case worker at the Family Responsibility Commission.

Arrange the review as close as possible to the customer's next contact interview.

To arrange meeting through Outlook:

  • Open Outlook (positional mailbox if available)
  • Click on New Items then select Meeting from the dropdown menu

On the Meeting page:

  • Type case worker's email address in the To field
  • In the Subject field type 'Cashless Debit Card Pre-exit Review'
  • In the Location field type 'phone hook-up'
  • In the Start time enter the date and start time of the meeting
  • In the End time field enter the same date and end time of the meeting
  • In the body of the meeting invite type the following:
    • Pre-exit Review of Cashless Debit Card This meeting will be conducted via phone and I will call you on (insert number). Kind Regards'
  • Click on Send

Note: customer details are not to be disclosed in emails or meeting invites.

To conduct a review with the referring authority, go to Step 3.

3

Conducting a Review with an Cashless Debit Card referring authority + Read more ...

For Income Management Contact Officer.

Contact the Family Responsibilities Commission at the agreed time and discuss the customer's individual circumstances.

Ask what decision has been made following this review.

Note: the referring authority may not make a decision at this contact.

Possible outcomes:

continue Cashless Debit Card

  • early exit (notification from referring authority required)
  • Cashless Debit Card end date reached
  • extend Cashless Debit Card from original end date (notification from the referring authority required)

Possible actions

Take appropriate action if required, for example:

  • extend Cashless Debit Card
  • exit Cashless Debit Card early

Note: the Income Management Contact Officer may be required to update the customer’s record where automated processing does not occur. For more information see Cape York Initiative Business Hub.

4

Review of Cashless Debit Card prior to revocation of notice + Read more ...

For Income Management Contact Officer.

This is a review initiated by the Family Responsibilities Commission (FRC).

The FRC should make contact with the Cape York Cashless Debit Card Team prior to revoking a notice, to discuss the Cashless Debit Card for the customer they are wishing to revoke.

The Cape York Cashless Debit Card Team is to discuss the outcomes of Cashless Debit Card to date and the implications of the decision to revoke the customer's Cashless Debit Card.
