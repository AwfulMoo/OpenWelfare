Departure review refunds for home care

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Search for refund + Read more ...

Use the DHS Fees and Charges role in the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS).

To display the Reconciliation Menu, select Means Testing Review from the ACMPS side menu.

To view a refund triggered by a departure review, select:

  • Refund Approval Task to display the Refund Approval screen
  • Use the Search Criteria to locate the refund that needs approving or rejecting:
    • Status: select Calculated – On Hold
    • Task Type: field automatically populates with Activity for Refund Approval
    • Service BP ID: key the ID
    • Care Recipient ID: key the ID or in the Care Recipient column, select the care recipient’s name
  • Select Search

Note: do not select the Refund for delegate approval at this point.

Does ACMPS have a departure refund currently on hold?

  • Yes
    • Refund amounts $1,000 and over require checking
    • Select Care Recipient record
    • Go to Step 2
  • No
    • Refund amounts under $1,000 are considered correct and are released automatically
    • Procedure ends here

2

Investigate refund amount for departure review period + Read more ...

  • Select the Reconciliation Data tab located on the same line as the Means Testing Data tab:
    • The Reconciliation Data tab displays historical refunds and any refund due for the current departure period
    • Review Type column displays quarterly review (QR), ad hoc review or departure review
    • Reconciled Amount column. A negative amount means the care recipient has underpaid fees. The underpayment is waived
  • Select Expand All and find the current departure review period to view the:
    • Fee Period Start Date
    • Fee Period End Date
    • Applied Fee
    • Notional Fee
    • Reconciled Amount
  • Check a refund has not already paid with another review for the same dates as the current departure review. For example, with a QR or manual adjustment

3

Review total refund + Read more ...

The total refund can have multiple lines. All lines require checking to determine if the refund is correct.

Starting with the top refund line:

  • Step A - Calculate the number of days from the Fee Period Start Date to the Fee Period End Date:
    • Include both the start and end dates
    • The number of days are needed for the next steps
  • Step B - Divide the Applied Fee by the number of days in Step A
  • Step C - Divide the Notional Fee by the number of days in Step A:
    • This is the daily notional fee for the period
    • Make a note of this amount for later
  • Step D - Divide the Reconciled Amount by the number of days in Step A
  • Step E - If the refund amount is correct, the difference between Step B and Step C should equal the Reconciled Amount
  • Repeat the process for each line

4

Obtain total income details and marital status + Read more ...

In the Means Testing Data tab, expand Total Income Details:

  • Use the dates of the period calculated in Step 3 (sub Step A)
  • Locate the line(s) that cover the care recipient's income for the above period
  • Key the income amounts into the Manual Adjustment Calculator (tab) in the Aged Care Fee Calculator. If more than 1 line relates to the dates being checked, record:
    • all amounts, and
    • the start and end dates for each period/amount
  • In the Means Testing Data tab, expand Circumstance Details:
    • take note of the marital status under Object Value
  • where the care recipient has an Object Value of YES for Object Type DEPENDENTS make a note of the dependant/s

5

Determine whether refund amount is correct + Read more ...

To determine whether the refund amount is correct use the Manual Adjustment Calculator (tab) in the Aged Care Fee Calculator.

Calculate the correct income tested subsidy reduction (ITSR) by coding:

  • Name, ID numbers and care details in top line of calculator, including fee type
  • Date of Effect Start Date and Date of Effect End Date. The Total Number of days will automatically calculate
  • Non-Pen Fortnightly Income and Pen fortnightly income. Can be obtained from means test data in ACMPS
  • Marital Status:
  • In the Correct Fees column, key the Daily ITSR:
    • This is the fee calculated in the previous step
    • The Total Period ITSR will automatically calculate
  • In the Previous Fees column, key the Daily ITSR:
    • This is the Applied Fee in the Reconciliation Data tab
    • The Total Period ITSR will automatically calculate
  • Check the Date of Effect in the calculator is correct for each period calculated, to make sure the relevant threshold is applied:
    • See Department of Health – Schedule of Fees and Charges on the Resources page for further information on the thresholds if needed
  • Repeat for each change in circumstances
  • If a care recipient has multiple changes in circumstances or the reason for a refund amount is not determined:
    • Stop the refund
    • Refer for further investigation

A departure review only occurs if a provider subsidy claim is already approved for the month of the departure:

  • QR only reconciles fees for the months which have a completed and approved provider subsidy claim
  • The refund calculated for a departure review may include months that should have been included in the previous QR period
  • For example:
    • A departure review occurs in November
    • The QR prior to the departure review is for the period 1 July ending 19 September
    • The QR refund is only calculated up to 31 August
    • This occurred as the service provider did not lodge the September subsidy claim until October
    • The period from 1 September to the date of departure is reconciled in the departure review

Is the refund amount correct?

  • Yes, to approve refund, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Figures do not align + Read more ...

If the figures from Step 5 do not align consider the following:

  • The dates for the departure review (that is, the dates between the last review’s effective date and the departure date). For example:
    • the last review was the March QR
    • the commencement date for the departure review is 20 March 2016 to the date of departure
  • During the departure review period, were there any changes in the care recipient’s circumstances or a previous QR period affecting the figures? For example:
    • Did the care recipient access leave?
    • Was there a change in assistance type from CLK/DVA to self-funded retiree (SFR)?
    • Was financial hardship assistance granted?
  • Is there any information in Notes that explains the difference?

If there does not seem to be an explanation for the difference, contact Technical Peer Support (TPS) for assistance.

7

Approve or reject refund + Read more ...

  • Make a note of the ACMPS care recipient ID
  • Select the Back button on the same line as the Care Recipient Profile:
    • do not select the browser back button
  • Key the Care Recipient ID in the Search Criteria > Search
  • Under the Description column, select Refund for delegate approval
  • Select either the Approve Refund or Reject Refund button at the top of the screen
  • Select Save. A green box with a tick confirms the action was successfully completed

Letters

A letter does not immediately appear in Communication History. The letter is sent later as part of a batch job once the refund decision process is completed.

For approved refunds

  • Notes are not required
  • Procedure ends here

For rejected refunds, go to Step 8

8

Remediation of rejected refunds + Read more ...

Is remediation required for the rejected refund?

  • Yes, log the issue with ICT and add a Fast Note in Customer First detailing:
    • refund details and action taken
    • ICT SD/PT number and details logged (if applicable)
      Note: where a manual adjustment is needed, see Aged care reviews – manual adjustments for care recipients - overview
  • No:
    • Update Notes in ACMPS
    • Add a Fast Note in Customer First detailing action taken

3

Situations where a departure review refund may be generated + Read more ...

Confirm entry details by going to Care recipient summary.

A departure review refund may generate for post 1 July 2014 care recipients where:

  • a care recipient’s circumstances change and reduce the Means Tested Care Fee (MTCF) or Means Tested Contribution (MTCC) they need to pay
  • income and/or assets are corrected from date of entry and resident status is corrected
  • care recipient has accessed leave – refer to Care recipient > Event summary
  • the cost of care (Aged Care Funding Instrument - ACFI) changes, resulting in a reduction of the MTCF:
    • Go to Care recipient > Event summary and check Aged Care Funding Instrument (ACFI)
    • This will display Entry date for care recipient and any updates to changes to ACFI
  • a service provider has not lodged a valid ACFI within the required time frame:
    • Confirm entry and ACFI dates on Event summary
    • ACFI lodged between 2 and 5 months from date of entry results in a care subsidy reduction of $25 per day (may affect maximum MTCF)
    • ACFI lodged after 5 months from date of entry results in no care subsidy paid to the provider until the day prior to lodgement (and therefore no MTCF payable)
  • a care recipient was means not disclosed (MND) and the care recipient has since completed a means assessment:
    • Confirm on Means testing > Assistance type
    • Check that income/asset details display from date of entry into care by checking Means testing > Total income and Total assets
  • a care recipient has reached their annual or lifetime cap
    • MTCF is not set to $0 until a claim is lodged for the month of the cap being reached. A care recipient will be eligible for a refund from the cap date until the date their fee was adjusted. See Aged Care fees and charges - Annual and Lifetime Caps

4

Investigation of departure review refund + Read more ...

Check the:

  • refund period and
  • the dates of effect of any income and asset updates

For a care recipient who was previously Means Not Disclosed (MND), if an income and assets update and system refresh were not completed at the same time as a means assessment, aged care systems may calculate a refund based on $0 assets from the date of entry up until the coding date.

When income and assets have not applied from the correct date of effect, or there is other remediation required for coding of income and assets, including from DVA, a work item will be created to follow up. This may be a work item for remediation only (refund released) or when a refund is required to be stopped. See Profiling and manual refresh of aged care means assessment data. If DVA have completed the means assessment, use the Aged Care DVA Referral web form on the Resources page to request backdating of income and assets to the date of entry.

Calculate the correct MTCF/AC

In the Aged Care Fee Calculator:

  • Go to the Residential Care Manual Adjustment tab
  • Insert the assets and income details using details in Means Testing tab
  • Check the Date of Effect in the calculator is correct for each period calculated:
    • This ensures the relevant threshold is applied. See the link to the Schedule of Fees and Charges Post - 1 July 2014 on the Resources page for further information on the thresholds
  • Repeat for each change in circumstances

Note: when a care recipient has complex changes in circumstances or the reason for a refund amount is not determined, the refund should be stopped and referred for further investigation.

Is the aged care system calculated refund the same as the manually calculated refund?

  • Yes. Approve refund, go to Step 5
  • No. Reject refund, go to Step 6

5

Approve departure review refund + Read more ...

To review, go to the care recipient ACSP record and view from:

  • Fees and Charges > Fees and charges details for ongoing fees
  • Reconciliation > Reviews and refunds for reviews and refunds

To approve refund:

  • In ACSP, select Menu > Review for manual approval
  • List of refunds that have a status of Awaiting decision will be presented
  • In the Filter by review type field, select Departure
  • To locate refund from Refunds for manual approval screen:
    • Search by: change to Care recipient ID
    • Key care recipient ID when field appears > Search
  • Select Review ID or Status to go to Review and refund details
  • Select Approve refund
  • Add Decision notes stating why this refund is being approved
  • Select Yes

Once the refund has been approved, the status will be updated to Entitled to refund. Once the provider has completed their monthly claim it will display as Refund paid. The system issued letter does not immediately appear in Letters summary screen.

Procedure ends here.

6

Reject departure review refund + Read more ...

The decision to reject a refund will be referred to a Service Officer with a Manual Adjustment skill tag.

To refer a refund for rejection:

  • In Customer First, create a Fast Note - select Auto text, use Aged Care > Enquiry > Manual Adjustment Referral
  • In the Fast Note include reasons for referral and details of why the ad hoc review refund needs to be manually calculated/adjusted
  • Update Care recipient > Care recipient notes in ACSP with referral details including that refund is awaiting action

Note: for staff completing a Manual Adjustment, see Aged care reviews - manual adjustments for care recipient - overview.
