Category 2 (grandfathered):

  • Details of income stream product (SA330) or similar schedule completed by the provider is needed for:
    • changes to the provider name and/or product reference number following a successor fund transfer

    • changes to provider name, and/or

Income Stream details + Read more ...

Term or Relevant Number: field

The number will be on the schedule. It is the length of time for which the income stream is payable. It is based on the income stream owner's, or the reversionary beneficiary's age on the purchase date.

For an income stream purchased after 1 January 2020, extra checks needed:

  • The life expectancy factors used to calculate the relevant number changed for income streams purchased from 1 January 2020
  • Some providers may not have updated their system to include the new life expectancy tables. The References page has a link to the Table of Life Expectancy - Payments Commenced on or after 01/01/2020 or use the relevant number calculator
  • If the schedule for an income stream with a purchase date on or after 1 January 2020 does not have the relevant number or it is incorrect, the life expectancy factor for the customer's age on purchase date can be used to update the Relevant Number field. See the Reference page
  • Another option is to contact the FINS helpdesk to obtain the correct relevant number
  • Do not reject the schedule

Note: the above process is not restricted only to income streams purchased after 1 January 2020. If a schedule does not have the relevant number, or it is incorrect, use the life expectancy factor for the customer's age on purchase date to update the Relevant Number field or contact the FINS helpdesk.

When a customer with a grandfathered income stream has their income support payment suspended, the system will automatically change the Cat 2 grandfathered income stream to a Cat 9 deemed income stream.

If payment is restored from the date of suspension, the system will automatically reinstate the grandfathered status.

If the payment is restored from another date and this still means the customer receives some payment is each fortnight the grandfathered status has to be manually reinstated.

For example, JobSeeker Payment customer with an existing grandfathered income stream goes overseas for 7 days. Payments suspended the day they leave the country and restored automatically on their return. In this case, if the customer receives a part payment in the entitlement period they were temporarily suspended, they are considered to still be in continuous receipt of an income support payment and their income stream can retain the grandfathered status.

An MFU is generated if the restoration date is within 27 days of the date of suspension. The keyword for the MFU is 'RESGRA'.
