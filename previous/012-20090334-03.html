This page contains processing information about medical practitioners affected by the legislation amendments in 2010 to section 19AB of the Health Insurance Act 1973. That is, an Australian or New Zealand permanent resident, or New Zealand citizen who completed their primary medical degree at an accredited medical school in Australia or New Zealand.

On this page:

Assess an initial or subsequent provider number application

Process initial stem

Assess application to determine level of access to Medicare benefits

Create an initial or subsequent provider number application

Process change of residency status

Send a letter

Medical school accreditation + Read more ...

Confirm provider details on Provider Directory System (PDS) + Read more ...

Select View for the matching medical practitioner.

Assess the provider file and confirm:

  • Place Obtained is an accredited Australian or New Zealand medical school
  • Residency Details (if applicable) and Registration Comments (if applicable) state the medical practitioner was an Australian or New Zealand permanent resident or citizen when enrolled in an accredited medical school in Australia or New Zealand
  • Specialty Code:

6

Subject to section 19AB + Read more ...

Where a medical practitioner is recorded in PDS as subject to section 19AB and has indicated on the application for a provider number they meet the criteria to be not subject to section 19AB, update PDS.

Note:

  • Evidence of residency status at the time of enrolment in an accredited Australian or New Zealand medical school must be provided with the application for a provider number form (HW019)
  • If the medical practitioner submits a New Zealand passport and the place of birth is not a New Zealand suburb or city, check the date of issue. The date of issue must be at least 4 years prior to the medical practitioner finishing medical school

Does PDS need to be updated to record the medical practitioner is not subject to section 19AB?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, if residency details do not need to be updated, go to Table 3

7

Update stem in Provider Directory System (PDS) + Read more ...

  • Select Edit

On the Registration tab:

  • Select Amend against the first available registration line that has allowable character limitations to add a comment
  • In Comments, key **NOT SUBJ TO 19AB 010410**
  • Select Update

Select Specialty tab, if medical practitioner is:

  • An Australian permanent resident or citizen:
    • End date the 500 and 519 specialty code using date 31 March 2010. Delete the 500 and 519 if date of first registration is on or after 1 April 2010
    • If the medical practitioner was a temporary resident after 1 April 2010, end date the 500 and 519 specialty codes using 31 March 2010. Key 185 specialty code with a start date of 1 April 2010 and an end date being the day before Australian permanent residency was granted
  • A New Zealand permanent resident or citizen:
    • End date the 500 and 519 specialty codes with 31 March 2010. Delete the 500 and 519 specialty codes if date of first registration is on or after 1 April 2010
    • Add 185 specialty code from 1 April 2010 or date medical practitioner was first registered if date of first registration is after 1 April 2010
  • Select Save

Assess the application and Ahpra registration + Read more ...

Check:

  • the application received is ‘Application for a Medicare provider number and, or prescriber number for a medical practitioner form (HW019)’
  • evidence of residency status at the time of enrolment in an accredited medical school in Australia or New Zealand is supplied

    • registration number
    • registration status, and

  • Yes:
    • Select View Details against the appropriate medical practitioner, check medical practitioner eligibility (name, registration number and any registration conditions)
    • Attach a print of the Ahpra registration to the PaNDA record
  • No, go to Table 6

4

Promote shell record + Read more ...

Create the provider stem number:

  • Select Promote
  • If additional given name/s is provided on the application, key other given name/s
  • If contact details are different in PDS to the application:
    • Key suburb name or postcode
    • Select suburb and postcode from the drop down menu
    • Key street number and street name
    • Select appropriate address from the drop down menu
    • If a non-standard address is provided, select Manual Entry and key the address manually
  • Key the provider specialty code in Specialty Code field, key:
    • Specialty code 160 – Interns who were Australian permanent residents or citizens at the time they enrolled in an accredited Australian or New Zealand medical school
    • Specialty codes 162, 181 and 185 – Interns who were New Zealand permanent residents or citizens at the time they enrolled in an accredited Australian or New Zealand medical school and are currently a New Zealand permanent resident or citizen
    • Specialty codes 110, 162 – Interns who were New Zealand permanent residents or citizens at the time they enrolled in an accredited Australian or New Zealand medical school and have since obtained Australian permanent residency or Australian citizenship
    • Specialty codes 181 and 185 – New Zealand permanent residents or citizens who were New Zealand permanent residents or citizens at the time they enrolled in an accredited medical school who are not undertaking internship
    • Key Date of first Registration in Profession from Ahpra website in the Start Date field
    • End Date: no end date is required
    • Select Add New
    • Select Cancel
  • Select Save

The provider stem number is now created.

5

Create a new provider stem number + Read more ...

Record personal details

Personal tab

The applicant’s name must appear in PDS exactly as it appears on the medical practitioner’s Ahpra registration.

Key:

  • Family Name as per Ahpra registration
  • Given Name as per Ahpra registration
  • Title as per provider number application
  • Date of Birth as per the application or as per identity verification documentation (for example, passport) if there is a discrepancy in the date of birth to the application form
  • Gender as per the application
  • Select the language if provided and select Add

Residency Details

  • Select Residency Status from the drop down menu as per the evidence of residency
  • Key Date of Permanent Residency (if applicable)
  • Key passport number
  • Select Country of Issue from the drop down menu as per passport
  • Key Visa Type (if applicable)

Note: If the medical practitioner has indicated a second given name on the application but it does not appear on the Ahpra registration, key the second given name as per the application.

Issue a prescriber number

8

Record the registration details + Read more ...

Registration tab

  • Select Add New Registration
  • Select AUS from the Area drop down menu
  • Key Ahpra registration number in the Registration Number field, for example MED##########
  • Status, If the Ahpra website displays:
    • General, select Current on PDS
    • Provisional - medical practitioner trained in Australia or New Zealand, select Intern on PDS
    • Provisional - medical practitioner trained outside of Australia and New Zealand, select Provisional on PDS

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Assessing documentation + Read more ...

Medical practitioners may indicate a change of residency status on an application for a provider number and/or submission of a passport and/or visa.

Services Australia must receive visa or passport documentation prior to changing a medical practitioner’s residency status in PDS from temporary resident to Australian permanent resident or Australian citizen.

If the medical practitioner indicated a change of residency status from temporary resident to permanent resident on the application and supplied no evidence of residency status, the application is incomplete. Go to Table 6.

If a medical practitioner supplies an Australian Citizenship Certificate or Australian Passport only (and not the permanent resident visa) update PDS to reflect the applicant is no longer a temporary resident:

  • use the Australian Citizenship date/passport issue date to ensure residency status is current
  • Service Officers must contact the applicant and advise them to supply their permanent residency visa to apply the earlier date. When permanent residency is received, update PDS with the permanent residency date

When sufficient evidence is provided, record details in PDS.

Note: New Zealand passport holders who become Australian citizens may not be issued with a permanent residency visa. In this instance, use the date of Australian citizenship (from Australian passport or citizenship certificate) as the commencement date of Australian citizenship. A provider number application may not always be supplied when a medical practitioner changes residency status only.
