Assisting customers to work out the reason for their FTB/CCB reconciliation overpayment/s

Step

Action

1

Reconciliation tool and relationship status + Read more ...

Note: for Child Care Subsidy (CCS) reconciliation overpayment outcomes see Customer enquires about (CCS) reconciliation outcome.

For FTB/CCB, in Customer First, go to Workspace > Families Benefits > FTB and CCB Reconciliation. For further information, see Customer enquires about FTB reconciliation or lump sum claim.

Relationship status

What was the customer's relationship status during the relevant financial year?

  • Go to the Marital Status (MS) screen
  • Take note of customer's relationship status during the relevant financial year, including the dates of any changes and partner's name/s. This information will be required during the investigation
  • Changes in relationship status could cause a reconciliation overpayment as a result of a maintenance issues

2

Income + Read more ...

Customer's income details

Compare the customer's income estimates provided during the relevant financial year from the FAO Income Summary (FINS) screen with the Actual Taxable Income (ATI) shown on the FAO Income for Previous Year (FIPY) screen.

If the ATI is more than the income estimate, this may have caused the overpayment.

Does the customer need to update their income estimate for the relevant financial year?

  • Yes, update the estimate on the FIPY screen. See Updating previous financial year incomes for Family Tax Benefit (FTB), Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Child Care Benefit (CCB)
  • No, check with the customer that the income details are correct

Is the customer or their partner required to lodge an income tax return for the relevant financial year per ATO guidelines?

  • Yes, check the estimate is correct and that a non-lodger exemption reason is not recorded on the Non-Lodger Exemption (FNLE) screen
  • No, check the latest estimate for the relevant financial year is correct and, if needed, code a non-lodger exemption reason for the customer/ partner on the FNLE screen. See Recording not required to lodge advice and previous year income

If it appears the overpayment may have been income related, see:

  • Explaining Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation overpayments due to estimated income
  • Customer enquires about Child Care Benefit (CCB) and/or Child Care Rebate (CCR) reconciliation or lump sum claim outcome

If the customer has zero entitlement to FTB Part A and/or FTB Part B due to income and there are 2 consecutive zero entitlement results since 2009-10, the customer may be precluded from receiving FTB instalment payments.

3

Maintenance + Read more ...

Did the customer or their partner receive maintenance for any child under 18 years of age during the relevant financial year?

Note: this can apply to a child in full time secondary study up to the end of the calendar year in which the child turned 18 if the customer has applied for an extension to child support.

'S'elect the Group: field from the Mtce Ent'ment Grp Summary (MNGS) screen to display the Mtce Entitlement Group Dtl (MNGD) screen. This shows details for that payer group including the collection method, maintenance entitlement or actual amount received and the assessment method used.

  • Yes, the overpayment could be due to maintenance income if the total maintenance received for the year is above the customer's Maintenance Income Free Area (MIFA).
  • No, the overpayment is not due to maintenance income

If CS collection, did the customer and/or partner receive an arrears payment of maintenance?

Go to the CSP Disbursed Payments: Group (MNGP) screen to check if the customer and/or partner received any arrears payments of maintenance.

  • Yes, the overpayment could be due to maintenance arrears if the total maintenance received for the year is above the customer's MIFA)
  • No, the overpayment is not due to maintenance arrears

Was adequate maintenance action taken?

'S'elect the child from the MNGS screen, then 'S'elect the Maintenance Action: field to view details on the Maintenance Action Result (MNMR) screen.

  • Yes, the overpayment is not due to maintenance action
  • No, the overpayment could be due to maintenance action as this would limit FTB Part A to the base rate for the child for the period that maintenance action was not taken

To explain the overpayment further, see Explaining Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation overpayments due to child support issues.

4

Shared care + Read more ...

Was a shared care arrangement in place for any child?

Check details on the FTB Shared Care (FSC) screen.

  • Yes, confirm with the customer the date that the shared care arrangement commenced and if there were changes in the arrangement during the relevant financial year. The reconciliation overpayment could be due to a shared care assessment
  • No, the overpayment is not caused by the shared care details

Note: a problem (P24845) has been identified where shared care is not being correctly applied when a child is in the care of a parent who has transferred their Family Tax Benefit (FTB) to their current partner (step parent). See the Resources page for further information.

5

Blended family + Read more ...

Did the customer share their Family Tax Benefit (FTB) payments with their partner in a blended family?

Check Blended Families Split FTB Rate (FBSR) screen.

  • Yes, confirm the percentage and date of commencement of the arrangement with the customer. Compare the date of event and the date the activity occurred. This would indicate if the change occurred later than the event date. Any discrepancy could cause a reconciliation overpayment due to a blended family assessment
  • No, the overpayment is not caused by the blended family assessment

6

Change of care + Read more ...

Did any of the children leave the customer's care during the relevant financial year?

Go to the Child Selection (CHS) screen. 'S'elect each child. The Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen will show whether the child or children changed care in the relevant financial year.

  • Yes, check the date the child/ren left care and when the customer advised Centrelink
    • Check whether it was a temporary or permanent change in care
    • These factors could determine that the reconciliation overpayment was due to a child leaving care
  • No, the overpayment is not caused by a child/ren leaving the customer's care

7

Child study requirements + Read more ...

Children aged 16-19 must satisfy study requirements. To check for a change in child education details, in Customer First:

  • Select Workspace > Children> Child education summary
  • To view the current study status for a child, select Workspace > Children > Child information

Has there been a change to the study requirements of a child in the relevant financial year?

  • Yes, if the child ceased study for a period, the reconciliation overpayment could be due to the child not meeting study requirements and not being an FTB child
  • No, the overpayment is not caused by the child's study requirements

8

Child income + Read more ...

This only applied for the financial years 2013-14 and earlier.

Were any children employed during the relevant financial year?

'S'elect the child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen and check the Child Student/Income Circumstances (CHSI) screen for correct coding. For information about children income rules, see Family Tax Benefit (FTB) child income.

  • Yes, confirm the date the customer reported that the child was receiving income and if the amount was over the child income limit for the relevant financial year. If the amount was equal to or above this limit, the child was not an FTB child for the period the child was not in full time secondary study or the period the child was exempt from the FTB activity test. See Explaining Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation overpayments due to child income
  • No, the overpayment is not caused by the child's income

9

Rent + Read more ...

Did the customer pay rent and were there any accommodation changes during the relevant financial year?

Check the Accommodation (AC) screen. Are there any changes?

  • Yes, deleted lines on the Accommodation Summary (ACS) screen could cause the system to read a previous line, giving an incorrect reconciliation result. Go to the FAO Reconciliation & Claim Summary (FRCS) screen and 'S'elect the FTB Reco Finalised: field. Code 'B' next to a line on FTB Calculation Result (FACRF) screen and see the breakdown of the rate. If Rent Assistance (RA) is incorrect, the ACS screen will help determine if the overpayment was due to RA
  • No, the overpayment is not caused by accommodation circumstances

10

Income support payment + Read more ...

Did the customer or their partner receive income support payments during the relevant financial year?

Check the benefit status line to see if the customer is currently receiving a payment. Check the Person Details (PED) screen for any previous payments. This will help to determine periods quarantined from reconciliation (for example periods the customer was entitled to the maximum rate of FTB Part A for partnered customers and FTB Part A and Part B for single customers).

  • Yes,
    • The overpayment could be due to a delay between the suspension or cancellation of payments and the date an income estimate was provided, as FTB may have continued at the maximum rate and this period is not quarantined from income reconciliation
    • Similar to the above from 1 July 2018, if the customer and/or partner received their ISP at a nil rate due to employment income, the rate of FTB Part A becomes income tested from the commencement of the employment nil rate period, despite the customer and/or partner having 21 days to provide the income estimate. Any resulting debt adjustment is calculated as part of reconciliation
    • If a tax-free pension or benefit was received, check if the actual amount received is more than the customer's estimate as the higher amount is used in reconciliation
  • No, the overpayment is not due to income support payments

11

Child care for Child Care Benefit (CCB) + Read more ...

Did the customer have any children in child care during the financial year?

  • Yes, check the CCF Rate Explanation (REXCCF) screen for the number of children in care. Go to the Child Selection (CHS) screen and 'S'elect each child with 'Y' in CCF column. The Child in Care Details (CICD) screen will show when and where they were in care
  • No, overpayment is caused by another reason

Were the children in care at the same time for all or some of the financial year?

  • Yes, the overpayment may be due to an incorrect multiple child percentage (for example CCB was paid during the year based on 3 children in care, but the child care usage/ attendance details provided by the approved child care services indicated only 2 children in care)
  • No, overpayment is not due to an incorrect multiple child percentage

Was each CCB child a school aged child or a non-school aged child during the year?

Check details on the Child Student/Income Circumstances (CHSI) screen. A higher rate of CCB is paid for a non-school child. If this was updated to school child during the year, this may have caused an overpayment.

Have all expected child care usage statements been received from the child care service(s).

This only applies to child care periods before transition of the service to the Child Care Management System (CCMS).

Code 'U' on CCF provider line on the Child in Child Care Details (CICD) screen to go to the Child Care Usage APP (CIUA) screen.

Note: JET Child Care fee assistance (JETCCFA) is not included in CCB reconciliation.

12

Immunisation + Read more ...

Information about a child's FTB immunisation status including FTB immunisation grace periods is located on the Child Immunisation Summary guided procedure.

Did an FTB immunisation grace period occur during the financial year?

  • Yes, if the child did not meet FTB immunisation requirements before the end of the FTB immunisation grace period, the FTB Part A rate reduction due to immunisation is retrospectively applied to the days in the FTB immunisation grace period. The overpayment may be due to the customer being overpaid FTB Part A for the period
  • No, overpayment is not due to FTB immunisation
