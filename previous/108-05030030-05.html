Examples of determining DOV

Item

Description

1

Pre-birth claim + Read more ...

Scenario: Pre-birth claim lodged in the current financial year with an expected date of birth in the next financial year.

On 2 June, Claire lodges a pre-birth FTB claim for her first child due on 23 July. Within the claim, Claire provides a current year estimate of $35,000 and a next financial year estimate of $20,000.

Both estimates will need to be recorded during claim processing using the date of event (DOV):

  • 2 June (date of notification) for the current year estimate, and
  • 1 July for the next financial year estimate

Once Claire's baby is born, in either the current year or the next financial year, and the Newborn Declaration (FAO81) is provided, no changes need to be made to the DOV of the income estimates provided in the pre-birth claim.

2

Non-income related changes + Read more ...

Scenario 1

Danielle lodges a Newborn Child Declaration (FA081) on 10 December to support a claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and FTB for a child born on 28 November.

Danielle receives a phone call from the processing team to discuss Danielle's current year income estimate to ensure PPL has been included as it is a taxable payment.

Danielle provides a revised estimate. The estimate change is a non-income related change as it relates directly to the birth of the child, therefore the DOV is 28 November (date of birth).

Scenario 2

Barry is currently receiving FTB based on an estimate of $50,000 for 2 school-aged children. Barry contacts on 5 February to advise a 2 year old family member came into Barry's care on 11 January. Because of this, Barry has to reduce hours of work and provides a revised estimate of $40,000.

The estimate change is a non-income related change as it relates directly to a child coming into care. Therefore, the DOV is 11 January (date the family member came into Barry's care).

3

Income related changes

Scenario 1 - FTB + Read more ...

On 1 October, Diane lodges a claim for Paid Parental Leave (PPL) and FTB for the birth of a second child, born on 28 July. At the same time, Diane provides a new estimate.

Diane receives a phone call from the processing team to discuss Diane's current year income estimate, to make sure PPL has been included and to determine the reason for the change in income. Diane advises that the change in income is due to a change in salary from 5 September and is not related to the birth of the child.

As an income related change exists, the DOV of the income estimate is the date of notification, 1 October. The system will automatically determine the date of effect (DOE) for the birth of the baby and the income estimate.

The birth of the baby is a change in non-income related circumstances, so the DOE for the FTB rate change relating to the birth of the child is 28 July (child's date or birth).

Diane's change in salary is a change in income related circumstances not related to the birth of the child. The DOE for the income related change will depend on whether it is a favourable or unfavourable decision.

Diane lodged the claim on 1 October and the next Entitlement Period End Day (EPED) is 12 October.

  • If the change can be actioned before 12 October (EPED) regardless of whether the income estimate has increased or decreased the DOE will be 1 October (date of notification)
  • If the update cannot be actioned before 12 October (EPED) and the income estimate has increased, the DOE applies from the later of the date of notification (1 October) and date paid to plus 1 (29 September) as it is considered an unfavourable decision, that is, rate of payment has decreased
  • If the update cannot be actioned before 12 October (EPED) and the income estimate has decreased, the DOE applies from the date of notification (1 October) as it is considered a favourable decision, that is, rate of payment has increased and arrears may be payable

Outcome

  • FTB for the newborn baby is payable from 28 July based on the customers previous estimate
  • The new estimate is recorded with a DOV of 1 October (date of notification)
  • The system will determine the DOE for the new estimate and change the FTB rate from this date

Scenario 2 – CCS: + Read more ...

On 7 December 2018, Jim advises a change in estimate from $75,000 to $65,000, due to a decrease in hours.

  • The increased CCS percentage will take effect from 17 December 2018 (the start of the next CCS fortnight); Activity Test details would also have to be updated to reflect the reduced hours of activity
  • The increase in FTB will take effect from 7 December 2018, the date of notification as it is an increase in entitlement

4

Residency + Read more ...

Scenario 1

Martha arrives into Australia in 2009 and is not residentially qualified for FTB. Martha's child Clare is born on 18 May 2014. Martha was granted a Permanent Visa on 1 February 2016 and is now considered residentially qualified for FTB. On 18 February 2016, Martha lodges a FTB NCL for child Clare.

Although Martha is not residentially qualified before 1 February 2016, the income estimate is recorded with a DOV as 1 July 2015 (start of the current financial year).

The period from 1 July 2015 to 31 January 2016 will be Rejected Not Residentially Qualified (REJ-NRQ) and FTB will be granted and arrears payable from 1 February 2016.

Scenario 2

Toby arrives into Australia on 15 September 2015 and is not residentially qualified for FTB. Toby's child Isabelle was born on 28 March 2015. Toby was granted Permanent Residency on 21 December 2015 and is now considered residentially qualified for FTB. On 6 January 2016, Toby lodges a FTB NCL for Isabelle.

The DOV of the income estimate is recorded as 1 July 2015.

The period from 1 July - 20 December 2015 will be Rejected Not Residentially Qualified (REJ-NRQ) and FTB will be granted and arrears payable from 21 December 2015.

5

FTB New Claim and CCS CUR

Note: this process does not apply if the customer is eligible for restoration.

Lower estimate + Read more ...

A customer in receipt of CCS only (CCP/CUR) lodges a claim for FTB on 1 September and supplies a new lower estimate. The claim processes on 15 October. The FTB claim grants from 1 July of the current financial year with the new estimate applied from this date (1 July).

The estimate(s) given for CCS purposes already recorded for the current financial year and for a date prior to the claim is/are corrected to match the estimate supplied in the FTB claim.

  • If the lower estimate results in an increased CCS percentage, this will take effect from the start of the CCS financial year and trigger a reassessment
  • Any arrears owed will be paid to the customer, subject to session reports having been submitted
  • Activity Test details may also have to be updated where a customer has had a decrease in their income estimate

Higher estimate + Read more ...

A customer in receipt of CCS only (CCP/CUR) lodges a claim for FTB on 1 September and supplies a new higher estimate. The claim processes on 15 October. The FTB claim grants from 1 July of the current financial year with the new estimate applied from this date (1 July).

The estimate(s) given for CCS purposes already recorded for the current financial year and for a date prior to the claim is/are corrected to match the estimate supplied in the FTB claim.

  • If the higher estimate results in a decreased CCS percentage, this will take effect from the start of the CCS financial year and trigger a reassessment
  • This may result in a potential overpayment, which will be assessed in reconciliation
  • Where a potential overpayment is identified and the customer is present, discuss increasing CCS withholdings

6

REJ-INC and second claim is lodged + Read more ...

Same financial year

Customer lodges an FTB claim on 2 January 2019 (2018-19 financial year) which rejects due to income (REJ-INC).

Customer lodges a second claim on 3 March 2019 (2018-19 financial year). The DOV of the income estimate is 3 March 2019, the date of lodgement of the second claim. The gap period between 1 July 2018 and 2 March 2019 will not be paid until the customer lodges an FTB lump sum claim and the ATO provides the Adjusted Taxable Income.

Next financial year

Customer lodges a claim on 2 January 2019 (2018-19 financial year) which rejects due to income (REJ-INC).

Customer lodges a second claim on 18 August 2019 (2019-20 financial year). The DOV of the income estimate is 1 July 2019 and FTB is payable from this date.

7

FTB claim lodged following FTB CAN/INC + Read more ...

Some customers can have their FTB payment restored where FTB has CAN-INC in the current or previous financial year. They do not have to lodge a new claim. If a customer lodges a new claim and are eligible for a restoration, treat this as a request for restoration of payment.

FTB CAN-INC within same financial year

Customer's FTB has cancelled due to income (CAN-INC) on 29 July 2019. The customer then lodges an online FTB claim on 10 August 2019 providing revised income estimates for the 2019-20 financial year.

The DOV of the estimate is the Date of Receipt (DOR) of claim that is, 10 August 2019.

The non-payment period between 29 July 2019 and 09 August 2019 will not be paid until the customer contacts after the end of the financial year once actual income is known. See Assessment of income cancellation periods in Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation.

If the customer receives CCS, any change to their subsidy percentage takes effect from 12 August 2019. This is the start of the CCS fortnight after the DOV. Activity Test details may also need updating where a customer has had a change in their income estimate.

FTB CAN-INC within previous financial year

Customer's FTB has cancelled due to income (CAN-INC) on 30 March 2019 (2018-19 financial year). The customer lodges an online claim on the 10 August 2019 providing a revised income estimate. The customer has not lodged a tax return for the 2018-19 financial year.

The request is actioned by a Service Officer who assesses the claim as a request for restoration of FTB (CAN- INC in the previous financial year).

Date of Event (DOV) of new estimate is the Date of Receipt (DOR) 10 August 2019.

Date of Effect (DOE) is the date the original FTB- CAN-INC in previous financial year that is 30 March 2019.

Non-payment periods:

  • The non-payment period between the 30 March and 30 June 2019 will be assessed once actual income is known during reconciliation
  • The non-payment period between 1 July and 9 August 2019 will not be assessed or paid until the customer contacts after the end of the financial year, once actual income is known. See Assessment of income cancellation periods in Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation

FTB arrears due to previously recorded CCS income estimate(s)

FTB arrears should not be paid when the system is attempting to pay FTB arrears for the CAN- INC non-payment periods due to:

  • an existing estimate(s) given for CCS purposes, and
  • this is lower than the most recent estimate provided

Code the FYSO screen for the CAN-INC non-payment period, using Status code CAN and Reason code OTH. This will stop any FTB arrears paying for this period. The arrears will be assessed in reconciliation when actual income details are confirmed.

If the system is paying FTB arrears prior to the DOR and the previous estimate given for CCS purposes is equal to or higher than the most recent estimate provided, these arrears can be paid if the customer is eligible. It is not necessary to discuss payment choices for this period.

8

Relationship status changes

Separation + Read more ...

Samantha is currently in receipt of FTB and separates from a partner on 12 October. Samantha contacts the Smart Centre on 1 November to advise of the change in circumstances and provide a new income estimate of $50,000 as an individual (previously $70,000). FTB is reassessed from the date of separation to exclude the partner's income.

The DOV for the revised estimate is 1 November (date of notification).

From 12 - 31 October, FTB will be paid based only on the customer's previous estimate (the system will disregard the ex-partner's estimate for this period).

From 1 November, FTB will be paid based on the customer's revised estimate.

From the start of the CCS fortnight after the date of separation, CCS will be assessed based solely on Samantha’s previous estimate ($70,000). Any increase in CCS subsidy percentage due to Samantha’s new estimate will take effect from the start of the CCS fortnight after the DOV (1 November). Activity Test details may also need updating where a customer has had a decrease in their income estimate.

Separation and FTB CAN-INC

Customer is FTB CAN-INC from 2 July 2019 due to an income estimate of $50,000 and $60,000 for customer and partner. On 1 November 2019, the customer separates from the partner. Customer lodges an FTB claim on 25 November 2019 and advises an income estimate of $57,000.

As the customer is FTB CAN-INC in the current financial year, consider the FTB NCL a request for restoration of FTB.

The customer is revising their estimate at the same time as notifying the change in relationship status. Therefore, two different date of effects apply in reassessing the FTB entitlement.

After the separation has been recorded, the customer's FTB can be restored from 1 November 2019 as this is the date the customer's circumstances changed (date of separation).

The revised estimate of the customer's income is coded with a date of event (DOV) of 25 November 2019 (date of notification).

The period from 1 November 2019 to 24 November 2019 will be paid based on the previous estimate of $50,000. From 25 November 2019, the new estimate of $57,000 will be used to assess FTB payment.

Note: if the system is attempting to pay FTB arrears for the CAN-INC periods when being restored based on a previous estimate which is:

  • lower than the most recent estimate provided, the arrears should be adjusted to match the estimate most recently supplied
  • equal to or higher than the most recent estimate provided, these arrears can be paid provided they are correct

Separation - CCS CUR and FTB NCL

Melanie has a partner and has been CCS CUR since 1 July. The income estimates on file are $50,000 for Melanie and $60,000 for Melanie’s partner, a combined income of $110,000.

On 1 May, Melanie lodges a FTB NCL via the online service. Within the new claim, Melanie advises of a separation from the partner on 1 April. The income estimate provided for Melanie in the new claim is $20,000.

As Melanie is CCS CUR and has not claimed FTB previously in this financial year, the DOV for the new estimate is 1 July.

The FTB arrears will be paid for the period 1 July to 31 March based on Melanie's new estimate of $20,000 and the partners existing estimate of $60,000. From 1 April (date of separation), FTB will be paid based only on Melanie's estimate of $20,000.

Any CCS estimates on file are corrected to 1 July, resulting in a reassessment of CCS. Any increased CCS percentage will take effect from the start of the CCS financial year. This may result in arrears issuing directly to the customer, subject to the submission of session reports. Activity Test details may also need updating where a customer has had a decrease in their income estimate.

Separation – FTB claim is lodged by ex-partner following separation from FTB current customer

Kim has a partner, Tom, and receives FTB entitlement for their 3 children. The income estimates on file from 1 July are $50,000 for Kim and $60,000 for Tom, a combined income of $110,000.

On 1 May, Tom lodges an FTB NCL via the online service. Within the claim, Tom advises they separated on 1 April and both have 50% shared care for their 3 children from the same date. The income estimate provided for Tom in the new claim is $70,000.

The DOV for Tom’s new income estimate is 1 April (date of separation). This must be manually changed within the online claim.

In this instance, the income estimate must not be backdated to 1 July or to a date prior to separation. If this occurs, it will cause an incorrect reassessment of Kim’s FTB entitlement for the period they were partnered, based on Tom’s new increased estimate of $70,000.

Partnered + Read more ...

Customer is FTB CUR with an income estimate of $35,000. The customer contacts on 15 May 2015 to advise of becoming partnered on 30 March. They provide an income estimate of $45,000 for the partner and the customer revises the estimate to $30,000. There are 2 DOVs for the estimate update.

  • The partner's estimate is updated with a DOV of 30 March (date of circumstance change) as this is a non-income related change. The partner's estimate was provided only because there was a change in the relationship status
  • The customer's estimate is updated with a DOV of 15 May (date of notification) as this is an income related change
  • For CCS the DOV of the income estimate for the partner will be the date they became partnered:
    • Any change in entitlement will take effect from the start of the next CCS fortnight (Monday) after the DOV. This means a customer will be treated as single during the CCS fortnight when they became partnered. If the customer becomes partnered on the start of a CCS fortnight, it will take effect from that date
    • Activity Test details must be provided for the partner, and customer’s details may also have to be updated where they have had a change in their income estimate

Cancellation of FTB instalment payments due to 3 or more non-lodger decisions + Read more ...

Jenny is currently getting FTB for 2 children based on an estimate of $23,200 for Jenny and $16,200 for a partner.

On 10 November 2017, Jenny's FTB cancels due to 3 or more non-lodger decisions (FTB CAN-N3Y). On 15 January 2018, Jenny lodges all outstanding tax returns.

Jenny then lodges an FTB claim on 1 February 2018 with an income estimate of $26,000 as an individual and $15,500 for a partner.

The DOV of the estimate is the date of notification 1 February 2018.

Jenny's fortnightly payments can resume from the date of confirmation of the ATIs for all outstanding non-lodger decisions (15 January 2018). No arrears are payable for the FTB not received in the current year until the ATI for the current year is confirmed in the next financial year.

Override the arrears that are paying for periods in the current year on FYSO to stop them from paying, for example, 1 July 2017 to 14 January 2018.

See Cancellation of FTB Instalment Payments due to 3 or more non-lodger decisions (Sunset Clause).

10

Combined FTB and CCS claim + Read more ...

Sharni (who is not currently getting FTB or CCS) lodged a combined FTB post- birth claim and CCS new claim on 3 February 2019 for a first child, Larissa, born 15 October 2018. Larissa started in childcare on 20 January 2019.

The system will code the income estimate with a DOV of 15 October 2018 (Larissa's date of birth).

The system will assess the DOE as:

  • FTB - 15 October 2018
  • CCS - 14 January 2019 (the start of a CCS fortnight up to 28 days prior to date of lodgement

11

FTB claim lodged following FTB/CAN-6WK + Read more ...

Rachel is getting FTB for 2 children. Rachel rings to advise they will be temporarily out of the country to care for a sick parent. Rachel provides a departure date of 5 July 2019 but cannot advise when they will return.

Rachel departs Australia on 5 July 2019.

On 16 August 2019, Rachel has been out of the country for 6 weeks and is therefore no longer eligible for FTB. As a result, FTB cancels FTB CAN-6WK.

Rachel returns to Australia on 1 November 2019 and lodges a new FTB claim on 15 November 2019 (as was outside of Australia for more than 13 weeks).

The date of event (DOV) for the income estimate will be 1 November 2019, the date eligibility has resumed following Rachel (and the 2 children) returning to Australia.

The income estimate DOV, which the system populates in the workflow or online claim, must be manually changed to 1 November 2019.

Note: in this instance, the income estimate would not be backdated to 1 July 2019 or to a date before Rachel’s return to Australia. That may pay arrears or raise a debt for a period the customer has already been paid.
