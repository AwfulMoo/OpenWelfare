Abridged statements for phone claims during ACC

To be read during an ACC (as per the Declaration, privacy and acknowledgement statements step in Table 1) if staff do not have access to the Automated Messages icon in Services Australia Workspace.

‘You agree that:

  • the information you have provided is complete and correct
  • you may need to provide further information or evidence to Services Australia
  • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment is for people who are unable to work and earn an income due to self-isolating or quarantine directions, or caring for a person who is under self-isolation or quarantine directions, or caring for a person who has tested positive with COVID-19
  • Services Australia can make relevant enquiries to make sure you receive the correct entitlement
  • Services Australia may request evidence to support any claims made
  • You should retain your evidence as this request may occur following an outcome on your claim
  • You must tell Services Australia of any changes to the information above or your circumstances within 14 days
  • any overpayment will be recovered
  • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment is taxable income and you will need to include it in your Income Tax Return and any income estimate for Family Assistance payments, and
  • giving false or misleading information is a serious offence’

‘You declare that:

  • the information you have provided is complete and correct and you understand that giving false or misleading information is a serious offence; and
  • if you become aware that you were not eligible to receive this payment, you will repay the payment to Services Australia
  • to the extent you have provided personal information about another person you have obtained consent from that person, or that person’s legal guardian, to provide their information to Services Australia’

‘For the purposes of assessing your application and managing any payment, you give consent for Services Australia to:

  • collect your personal information from other Commonwealth or state or territory government agencies and organisations, including your employer, including your name, address, date of birth, telephone number, employer details and relevant health information
  • use or disclose any relevant information previously collected about you for Centrelink purposes
  • disclose your personal or relevant health information to other Commonwealth, state or territory government agencies and organisations or your employer
  • contact you via electronic messaging or online letters if you have provided a mobile phone number or email address. For Terms and Conditions go to https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/terms and conditions for electronic messaging’
