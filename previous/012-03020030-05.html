This page contains information on how Service Officers can assist in resolving Healthcare Identifier Provider - Individual (HPI-I) number enquiries from providers on the phone, after completing the required security check.

Resolve provider enquiry about HPI-I number

Resolve call from provider about HPI-I number

This table lists various HPI-I number enquiries that providers may call about, and how Service Officers can assist in resolving them.

If the enquiry is about…

then…

Information regarding the HPOS searching function to obtain Healthcare Provider Individual details

advise the provider to go to the Health Professional Online Services (HPOS) website

Note: it is not necessary for an individual healthcare provider to have a published Healthcare Provider Directory (HPD) entry for details to be visible using the new search facility.

The new menu option is Search for Provider Individual and allows for:

  • a demographic search
  • an ID number search

The ID number search for an individual can utilise a family name and either:

  • a HPI-I
  • an Ahpra Registration ID

The Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (Ahpra) Registration ID search:

  • is case sensitive
  • requires the alpha characters to be entered exactly as they appear on the Ahpra website

confirmation of a provider's own HPI-I number

this can be disclosed via telephone.

The provider can search for another provider or organisation via HPOS.

publishing details in the Healthcare Provider Directory (HPD)

consent to publish or remove can be taken over the phone.

Note: a file note must be completed.

removing the link between their own HPI-I record and an organisation

this can be performed by the Service Officer.

Note: a file note must be completed.

an Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health Practitioner or Health Worker requesting information about how they can obtain a HPI-I

Aboriginal Torres Strait Islander Health Practitioner

advise they can contact Ahpra to obtain their HPI-I

Aboriginal Torres Strait Islander Health Worker

advise they can apply directly to the HI Service as they are not eligible to register with the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health Practitioner Board. They will need to meet the requirements under the legislation to receive a HPI-I.

a member of one of the 4 new Ahpra professions (below) obtaining a HPI-I:

  • Members of the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health Practitioner Board of Australia
  • Chinese Medicine Board of Australia,
  • Medical Radiation Practice Board of Australia
  • Occupational Therapy Board of Australia.

advise caller that if they are registered with their national board, which includes registration with Ahpra, then Ahpra will have allocated them a HPI-I.

To check if they have been allocated an HPI-I, direct the caller to their national board or to contact Ahpra.

amendments to their own details such as name, date of birth, sex or qualifications (non-Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (Ahpra) providers)

direct caller to Health professionals online services to obtain the 'Healthcare Identifiers Service - Application to amend a Healthcare provider record' form (HW034).

Note: they must have proof of the change.

detail originally supplied to the HI Service by the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (Ahpra)

this can only be updated by Ahpra. Direct the caller to Ahpra to amend these details.

amendments to their own details such as phone number, other contact details or address

this can be actioned.

Note: a file note must be completed.

requesting a new Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) certificate for HI Purposes, and linking HI service to an existing Services Australia PKI

if an individual only requires access to HPOS for HI Service functions, they need to register for a PRODA account.

requesting a NASH PKI certificate or information about NASH PKI certificates

To apply for an NASH PKI certificate, the provider must complete a request in Health Professional Online Services in their organisation’s certificate tab.

If they want more information or help in requesting a certificate in HPOS:

  • direct the caller to servicesaustralia.gov.au/NASH
  • warm transfer the call to the NASH PKI team

See Authentication required to access digital health.

other matters

if the provider says the information in the record is correct, escalate the enquiry (when required) via email to the Tier 3 positional inbox
