This page contains information about checking and updating details for the ATO lump sum FTB claim on the customer's record.

Updating the customer record for ATO lump sum FTB claim

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Providing new information + Read more ...

Customers may at any time provide new information or correct details provided on their FTB lump sum claim that was lodged with the ATO before 1 July 2009. However, time restrictions apply on whether full or partial arrears can be paid.

A new claim does not need to be lodged but supporting documentation may be required.

  • If the customer wishes to include an additional child, they will need to request this in writing. If it has not already been provided to Services Australia, proof of the child's birth is required
  • Changes to other details such as estimated income and rent details may be provided by phone or online if a claim has already been lodged

2

Qualification debt identified + Read more ...

If a qualification debt is identified, the debt must be manually raised before claim details are updated.

Raise the debt but do not update claim details, if the customer:

  • advises their child's income was not below the child income limit for the relevant financial year, or
  • was not residentially qualified

3

Access ATO FTB claim information screens + Read more ...

View or update details from the ATO FTB Lump Sum Claim (ATOCLM) screen.

Some ATO information may not be disclosed: Privacy, Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) and implied authority.

If no claim details exist or the automatic process has stalled, manual intervention is required.

Protocols exist for referring cases to the Manual Intervention Team.

On the ATOCLM screen, in the Action: field next to the relevant financial year, key:

  • 'V' to view claim details before updating
  • 'P' to print claim details transferred from the ATO. Do not show or give this to the customer. It should only be used to input details where automatic processing has failed. Destroy the printed claim data after use
  • 'S' to update claim details. Key Source: and DOR: fields and press [Enter] to go to the ATO Task Selector (ATOTS)

4

Checking overpayment due to reassessment of ATO lump sum Family Tax Benefit (FTB) claim + Read more ...

Confirm ATO FTB lump sum claim declared income with the customer.

Check reconciliation result:

  • Go to the FAO Reconciliation and Claim Summary (FRCS) screen, update the relevant financial year in Income year: field, and 'S'elect a reconciliation line to display the FTB Calculation Result (FACRF) screen
  • 'S'elect a line number on the FACRF screen to display the FTB Calculation Period Assessment (FCPA) screen. Press [F7]/[F8] to scroll between calculation periods

Check if the customer's and partner's income details were used for re-calculation.

Press [Enter] to return to FRCS screen:

  • 'S'elect the first lump sum claim calculation line. The FACRF screen will display
  • 'S'elect a period line. The FCPA screen will display details of income used for the original calculation. To scroll through the pages use [PF7] and [PF8]

To check income details provided by the ATO:

  • go to the ATO Claimant Income Details (ATOCID) screen for the customer income
  • go to the ATO Spouse Income Details (ATOSID) screen for the partner income

Other screens are available for view only through the ATO Enquiry (ATOENQ) screen. See ATO lump sum FTB claim screens.

FTB Part A and Part B supplements are included in the reconciliation calculation once adequate income/not required to lodge details are available.

Advise the customer of investigation results and record details on a DOC as follows (figures used are only for example):

  • Summary line (Sum: field):
    • CUS Pho re: FTB O/P of $1,234 for 00/01
  • Text (Txt: field):
    • FTB O/P of $1,234 reconciliation overpayment explained to customer
    • CUS original ATO LSC contained only the CUS income
    • the CUS was paid a lump sum of $1,234
    • a negative adjustment occurred when (partner) lodged his ITR and the FTB/CCB was re-calculated
    • CUS was entitled to $1,234
    • discussed payment options with CUS

5

Child claim details + Read more ...

Check that the customer has included the child on their claim. If not, they are required to lodge a written request to add the child.

For a Regular Care Child, the customer may be entitled to ancillary benefits.

The ATO Dependant Summary (ATODDS) screen shows dependant details as provided by the customer on the ATO FTB lump sum claim.

Go to the Child Circumstance Summary (ATOCCS) screen. This shows lump sum claim children and children listed on the Child Selection (CHS) screen.

If the child is not listed, link or add child to the customer's record.

FAO ATO Child Circumstances (ATOCC) screen

The customer may have intended to claim for a child but did not complete the claim correctly. For example:

  • They included the child as a dependent but not in the 'Family Profiles during claim period' section. Proof of birth is not required in this case
  • A family profile period has an end date before 30 June for a child but the child was in care for the entire year

Ask the customer to provide details for all children in their care and the periods they were in care.

Record start and end dates for each separate period, separate lines for each change during the year. Periods cannot overlap. Delete any incorrect end reason codes.

Finalise activity, see Item 7 of the Additional updates to the customer record for ATO lump sum FTB claim table.

6

Child overseas + Read more ...

Select the child from the ATO Child/Child Screen Selector (ATOCCS) screen and go to the ATO Child Details (ATOCD) screen

  • If child was overseas temporarily, key the date they left and returned to Australia in From and To fields
  • If the child left Australia permanently, key the date they left Australia in the From field and check this date matches the claim period end date on the ATOCC screen

Finalise activity, see Item 7 of Table 2.

7

Multiple birth indicator + Read more ...

Select the child from the ATOCCS screen and go to the ATO Child Details (ATOCD) screen.

If the 3 or more children were born on the same day, key 'A' ('B' for a second multiple birth group) for each child in the Multiple Birth field.

This is used to assess Multiple Birth Allowance (MBA).

Finalise activity, see Item 7 of Table 2.

8

Child support details + Read more ...

Child support entitlement details

Select the child from the ATOCCS screen and go to the ATO Child Maintenance Action (ATOCM) screen.

Key the date the:

  • customer/partner became entitled to claim child support for the child (usually date of separation or child's birth)
  • customer advised they took action to obtain child support or record that they have advised they are exempt

Child support income details

From the ATO Mtce Details Selector (ATOMDS) screen, 'S'elect the person who received the maintenance income.

On the ATO Mtce Recipient Details (ATOMRD) screen, record or correct details:

  • Relevant Period
  • Total Maintenance Received: the amount the customer stated on their lump sum claim as received from the payer
  • Payer Action: is used to insert another payer or to delete all details for the payer displayed

Finalise activity, see Item 7 of Table 2.

9

Partner updates + Read more ...

The ATO Spouse Personal Details (ATOSPD) screen lists details for each partner as provided by the customer on the ATO FTB lump sum claim.

  • To add a partner
  • go to the Links Summary (LS) screen
  • key 'Y' in Add New Link: field. Press [Enter]
  • from the Other Customer Links (OCL) screen update the following fields:
    • Link type: ATP
    • From SVC Rsn: CTM
    • To: XSP
    • Start Date: (the beginning of the financial year or start date of relationship in the financial year)
    • Update Source: and DOR: fields. Press [Enter]
  • Key Customer Reference Number (CRN) of partner in Link to Person: field on the Link Person (LP) screen or search for person on the LP screen
  • When link is created put an end date of the end of the financial year or end date of relationship

To update partner's name or date of birth

'C'orrect details on the ATO Spouse Name Details (ATOSND) screen.

Finalise activity, see Item 7 of Table 2.

Additional updates to the customer record for ATO lump sum FTB claim

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Relationship and blended family details + Read more ...

ATO Fam Circumstances Details (ATOFCD) screen

This screen lists all partners, start and end dates of each relationship period, whether the partner is treated as an ex-partner, and blended family percentage.

Note the number allocated to the partner at top of screen.

  • To change details, key 'C' next to person and update details. If changing dates, change earliest periods first as periods cannot overlap. Screen will not accept overlapping periods
  • To delete details, key 'D' next to person and then confirm deletion

Blended family percentage

To check the percentage paid, select the ATO claim on the FAO reconciliation and claim summary (FRCS) screen and then select a period to go to the FTB Calculation period assessment (CPAF) screen.

If this percentage is incorrect (the customer may have indicated that the children in their care were part of a blended family when in fact the children had been in their care 100% of the time), correct the record.

See Item 7 - Finalise activity.

2

Death of customer/partner + Read more ...

On the ATO Cust/Spouse Details (ATOCSD) screen, record the date of death, name and Customer Reference Number (CRN) of the deceased.

If the deceased person was the customer's partner on 30 June of the relevant financial year:

  • key a non-lodger exemption on the ATO Non-lodger Partner Exemption (ATONLPE) screen with date of death as the date of event
  • check/update estimated income for the deceased partner on the FAO Income for Previous Year (FIPY) screen

See Item 7 - Finalise activity.

3

Customer overseas + Read more ...

On the ATO Cust/Spouse Details (ATOCSD) screen:

  • If customer was overseas temporarily during the relevant financial year, key the date they left Australia in From field and the date they returned to Australia in the To field
  • If the child left Australia permanently, key the date they left Australia in the From field and leave To date blank

See Item 7 - Finalise activity.

4

Non-lodger/late lodger details + Read more ...

Is the person who was the customer's partner on 30 June of the relevant financial year now deceased or separated from the customer?

  • Yes, key a non-lodger exemption on the ATO Non-lodger Partner Exemption (ATONLPE) screen. Update the date separated or date of death in Date of Event field
  • No, if the customer or partner is not required to lodge a tax return for the relevant financial year, key exemption on the FAO Non Lodger Exemption (FNLE) screen. For help, see Recording not required to lodge advice and previous year income

For more information, see Non-lodger and later lodger process for FTB reconciliation.

Check/update customer's estimate for person not required to lodge on the FAO Income for Previous Year (FIPY) screen.

See Item 7 - Finalise activity.

5

Income details + Read more ...

Income details can be viewed and updated on the FAO Income for Previous Year (FIPY) screen.

The customer's revised estimate must be considered reasonable for it to be used.

Wrong actual income

If a customer's Tax Notice of Assessment (NOA) shows a taxable income amount different to that transferred by the ATO and shown on the customer's record (for example no link with the ATO, wrong Tax File Number recorded), see Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) process for family assistance customers.

Partner income not displayed

It may be because partner details need to be added or updated.

Updating income details

If actual adjusted taxable income details have been transferred from the ATO for the person, amounts for these components cannot be updated. The person would need to ask the ATO to review their income tax assessment. The ATO will transfer any new income details to Services Australia to trigger an automatic recalculation of the lump sum claim.

ATO link

Unless the customer has an outstanding non-lodger debt, the link with the ATO is ended after 3 years, for example from 1 July 2009 for 2005-06. The customer will need to provide proof of actual income for the relevant year. See Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) process for family assistance customers.

Special processing rules apply for ex-partner periods where income details are revised after initial grant.

See Item 7 - Finalise activity.

6

Claim rejected due to instalments or mixed delivery in same financial year + Read more ...

Check the FTB Delivery Option (FDO).

If the customer received FTB part way through the financial year, key 'ITR' from Date Paid to + 1 (DPT).

7

Finalise activity + Read more ...

Check result on the Assessment Results (AR) screen before finalising activity.

If incorrect instalment arrears or ongoing entitlement, the claim will require manual intervention. Protocols exist for referring cases to the Manual Intervention Team.

The FTB Part A and Part B supplements are included in the calculation once actual income/not required to lodge details are available.

Debt offsetting can automatically apply during lump sum claim calculation if the customer has an outstanding Families debt. From 1 July 2009, this also includes FTB qualification and non-lodger debts.

Advise customer of the new result. An automatic letter will be sent to the customer.

Record details on a DOC.

Debt action: When claim details are updated, if the recalculation results in a debt amount different to the original debt calculated, adjust the debt amount on the Overpayment Debt Amount (OPDA) screen.
