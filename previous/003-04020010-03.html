Claiming AVTOP -primary victim claims where claimant is 16 years or older

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Is the claimant eligible for an AVTOP payment? + Read more ...

  • Are they claiming for a declared event? The Prime Minister must declare the event as a terrorist act for the purposes of an AVTOP
  • Have they lodged their claim within the claiming period? Primary victims have 2 years from the date of the Prime Minister's declaration to make a claim. For the end date of this eligibility period, refer to the appropriate event specific Operational Blueprint in Emergency management events. Claims may be lodged after the claiming period has closed. However, claimants will need to meet special circumstances to be assessed for a payment. For information on the process for claims lodged after the closing date, see Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment (AVTOP) claims lodged outside of time limit
  • Has the claimant confirmed their identity? Claimants who are 16 years and older must satisfy LOA 3 requirements.
  • Was the claimant a resident of Australia, or held a qualifying residence exemption, on the day of the terrorist act?

For the March 2019 Christchurch attacks only, customers who have been harmed, as a direct result of the declared overseas terrorist act i.e. primary victims can be an Australian citizen on the day the terrorist act occurred. The Australian residency criteria that normally applies to primary victims is not a requirement for the March 2019 Christchurch attacks.

  • Was the claimant in the place where the terrorist act occurred?
    • Claimants can provide travel documents such as a travel itinerary, smart traveller records, evidence of discussions with Australian Government officials
    • Service Officers should complete a Department of Home Affairs link check
  • Was the claimant harmed as a direct result of the terrorist act?
    • Claimants must provide medical evidence to substantiate their claim, such as a Health Professional or Allied Health Professional report
    • If the claimant has indicated this information has been previously provided, they can provide permission for this information to be used for this claim
  • Has the claimant declared that they, and/or their close family members, were not involved in the commission of the terrorist act? When the claimant lodges their claim, they make a declaration that they and their close family members were not involved in the commission of the terrorist act. This declaration may or may not be sufficient for the claim to proceed. If there is any evidence or information, from any source, that suggests any level of involvement in the terrorist act by either the claimant or any of their family members use the Payment Query Template on the Resources page to refer the claim to the Emergency Management Level 2 Helpdesk for further advice

To code the claim on the AVTOP processing tool refer to the Coding a new AVTOP claim table in the AVTOP processing task card on the Resources page.

If no, or insufficient, supporting documents have been provided for any of the eligibility criteria, contact the claimant to discuss the documents needed. See Requesting clarification and/or evidence table.

When the eligibility evidence has been received, document in the AVTOP processing tool how the claimant meets the relevant criteria. Make sure the documents are scanned onto the system.

To update the claim on the AVTOP processing tool refer to the Updating the AVTOP Processing Tool table in the AVTOP processing task card on the Resources page.

Has all eligibility criteria been met?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 7

  • No, go to Step 3

3

Social work referral + Read more ...

Agency social workers are available to provide support to AVTOP claimants and AVTOP telephony and processing teams.

  • Check the 'circumstances to refer or consult with a social worker' section on the Background page of this Operational Blueprint to determine if a social worker should be contacted to support the claimant. If any of the circumstances are applicable contact a social worker in line with the below processes
  • If at any time during the AVTOP process a claimant is distressed and requires immediate help, refer them to a social worker via Services Australia Workspace >Social Work - Disaster -AVTOP
  • For all other situations, if the claimant has indicated, either verbally or via the AVTOP claim form, they would like to speak to a social worker send an email with the following details:
    • Claimants name
    • Contact number
    • CRN (if applicable)
    • Referral reason

For contact details, see Social workers - referrals.

Unless there is an immediate need for a claimant to speak to a social worker, it is important to use the email contact process. This will make sure a social worker with the appropriate skill set can be engaged to call the claimant back.

Note: the claimant may also require a referral to an external organisation. Offer to warm transfer the claimant at the end of the phone call. The Resources page ha a link to the Services Australia website where the Payment Service Finder can be located.

4

Refer claim for Injury and Life Impact Assessments + Read more ...

The injury assessment is to determine if the claimant's injury is a direct result of the terrorist act. The life impact assessment takes into account the affect the injuries sustained from the terrorist act have had on the claimant's life. These assessments are conducted by Health and Allied Health professionals employed by the Agency.

  • Send referral to Assessment Services
    • Subject - AVTOP Injury Assessment
    • Provide CRN
  • Assessment Services have up to 13 days in which to consider the claim. Place the claim on hold for 13 business days with the relevant keyword. If not returned after this time, call Assessment Services to check status.

The Resources page has a list of keywords

  • If the Health or Allied Health Professional assessing the claim requires further supporting documents, they will send an email to the AVTOP processing team to ask for this from the claimant. The claimant should be given 14 days to supply the required documents. Put claim on hold for 14 days. For contact details, see Emergency payments - claiming - Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment (AVTOP)
  • When the documents are received, the AVTOP processing team will forward the documents to Assessment Services. Make sure the documents are scanned onto the system
  • Assessment Services have up to a further 13 days in which to consider the claim. Place the claim on hold for 13 business days. If not returned after this time, call Assessment Services to check status

For contact details, see Assessment Services.

If the claimant's injuries result in a payment of less than $75,000, Assessment Services will also conduct a Life Impact assessment.

Once the Injury and Life Impact assessment has been received, update the AVTOP processing tool. Refer to the Adding the Injury Assessment table in the AVTOP processing task card on the Resources page.

Claim granted, go to Step 5

Claim unsuccessful, go to Step 7

5

Reductions and Deductions + Read more ...

The processing team is to assess any deductions and/or reductions, see Reduction and Deductions for the Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment.

Record decision to apply reductions and/or deductions in the AVTOP processing tool.

6

Rate Calculation + Read more ...

To calculate the amount of AVTOP to be paid:

Injury assessment & life impact assessment - reductions & deductions = AVTOP final payment.

7

Quality Checking + Read more ...

Services Officers must submit all AVTOP claims for quality checking.

Contact the Service Support Officer who will manage the manual quality checking process by a proficient AVTOP QOL checker.

If the claim is not returned or released after 5 business days, contact the Service Support Officer to check status and have it actioned.

The processing team must act on any recommendations from the quality check assessment before the claim is finalised.

  • Claim granted, go to Step 8
  • Claim unsuccessful, go to Step 9

8

Claim granted + Read more ...

Document the outcome of the claim and the reason for the decision in the AVTOP processing tool and Customer First. Documentation in Customer First must be in line with GIR standards.

Note: if the claimant is 16 or 17 years old, consult with a social worker before the claim being finalised.

Scan and upload a copy of the AVTOP PV Grant letter to the customer's, and if applicable the nominee's, record. Send the AVTOP PV Grant letter to the claimant. See the Resources page for a link to all AVTOP letters.

9

Claim unsuccessful + Read more ...

Approval must be received before the unsuccessful claim can be rejected.

To gain approval use the Payment Query Template on the Resources page to escalate the claim to the Emergency Management Level 2 Helpdesk.

See Rejecting a claim table.

Claim granted + Read more ...

Document the outcome of the claim and the reason for the decision in the AVTOP processing tool and Customer First. Documentation in Customer First must be in line with GIR standards.

For children under 16 years, payment must be made to the ACT Public Trustee and Guardian.

If the child already has a payment nominee, this arrangement must be ended and the ACT Public Trustee and Guardian added as the new payment nominee. Before changing the payment nominee details, make sure any other payments the child may be receiving from the agency are not transferred to the ACT Public Trustee and Guardian.

Code the new payment nominee using the nominee workflow:

CRN: 407 172 142S Note: check bank account details associated with this CRN. BSB is 062920 (Commonwealth Bank) and Account Number is 10036944

Public Trustee for the ACT

PO Box 221, Civic Square, 2608

Use reference: 'AVTOP, beneficiary's surname'

Code a review for the following day to end the payment nominee arrangements with the ACT Public Trustee and Guardian and if relevant, manually revert to the original payment nominee details.

Tell the legal guardian that the payment will be going to the ACT Public Trustee and Guardian. If they have any enquiries, they can call the ACT Public Trustee and Guardian.

Send a signed copy of the AVTOP PV Grant letter to the legal guardian. See the Resources page for all AVTOP letters.

Scan and upload a copy of the AVTOP PV Grant letter to:

  • The customer's record
  • The legal guardians record
  • ACT Public Trustees record

Send an email to the ACT Public Trustee and Guardian with the grant letter attached. CC in the following email addresses:

  • EM.Programme@servicesaustralia.gov.au
  • Emergency.Management@servicesaustralia.gov.au

Content of the email is as follows:

An Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment (AVTOP) of {AMOUNT} was made on {DELIVERY DATE} to the ACT Public Trustee account 062920/10036944 to be held in trust for {CHILD'S FULL NAME}, date of birth {CHILD'S DATE OF BIRTH}].

{CHILD'S FIRST NAME}'s guardian details are:

{GUARDIAN'S FULL NAME}

{GUARDIAN'S POSTAL ADDRESS}.

Rejecting a claim

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Important information + Read more ...

Before rejecting a claim, 2 genuine attempts must be made to contact the claimant to:

  • Provide the claimant with a full explanation of why their claim is unsuccessful and determine if the claimant can qualify under any other criteria
  • Advise that a letter will be sent outlining the review/appeal process, and
  • Consider if the claimant may be eligible to receive other social security payments and/or if a referral to a social worker or external organisation is needed. The Resources page has a link to the Services Australia website where the Payment and Service Finder can be located

2

Approval to reject claim + Read more ...

Note: unsuccessful claims cannot be rejected until approval has been sought from the Emergency Management Level 2 Helpdesk.

Has approval been sought to start claim rejection?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, place claim on hold with the appropriate rejection keyword. AVTOP New Claim keywords are located on the Resources page
    • Once approval to reject is given, and the held claim becomes due, go to Step 3

3

Check contacts + Read more ...

Is this the first attempt to contact about rejection?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

First contact attempt + Read more ...

Was the attempt successful?

  • Yes,
    • Complete the new claim activity as 'rejected'
    • Document the claimant's record accordingly
    • Make sure the correct rejection letter is sent, go to Step 6
  • No,
    • Document the claimant's record accordingly
    • Add the AVTRJ1 keyword to the new claim work item and place work item on hold until the following day (the work item will be removed from the inbox and reallocated once off hold)

Procedure ends here.

5

Second contact attempt + Read more ...

Successful/unsuccessful (the outcome is the same regardless of whether the second attempt is successful or unsuccessful)

  • Complete the new claim activity as rejected
  • Document the claimant's record accordingly
  • Make sure the correct rejection letter is sent, go to Step 6

6

Send rejection letter + Read more ...

Finalise AVTOP on claimant's record.

Document the outcome of the claim and the reason for the decision in the AVTOP processing tool and Customer First. Documentation in Customer First must be in line with GIR standards.

If claim is unsuccessful due to:

  • eligibility requirements, send the AVTOP Reject general letter to the claimant or legal guardian
  • deductions, send AVTOP Deductions preclude payment letter to the claimant or legal guardian

Scan and upload a copy of the AVTOP PV Rejection letter to the customer's, and if applicable the nominee's, record.

See the Resources page for a link to all AVTOP letters.
