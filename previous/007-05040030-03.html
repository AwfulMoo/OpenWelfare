Notification and initial assessment

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Changes in DAP circumstances + Read more ...

Change in circumstances can be advised before or after the DAP period nominated start date, however customers must notify a change in circumstances for DAP as soon as possible.

They may contact to advise a change in circumstances online, by phone or in person.

Those who have claimed or been paid DAP may contact to update 1 or more of the following:

  • the nominated tax rate (prior to payment of DAP)
  • the DAP period nominated start date
  • expected date of birth or entry into care
  • details about having care of a child for DAP purposes
  • details of work or paid leave during the DAP period
  • DAP income test details
  • DAP work test details

Customers can advise the change in circumstances online. Encourage customers to update their tax rate, date of birth or entry into care, care arrangements and work/leave details using online services.

Note: customers cannot use online services when they have a claim status of NCL to update their income or work test nor their nominated start date. A Service Officer is required to make updates in these cases.

If allocated an ACTDOC or Work Item regarding a change in circumstances for a DAP customer, go to Step 8.

If an online update is not auto-finalised, customers will receive the message
'Your update was not able to be completed. Your information has been saved and a Service Officer will complete the update' and the Work Item will be allocated in Customer First. For DAP trained staff to action the Work Item, go to Step 5.

If the customer cannot make the updates online, go to Step 2.

2

Contact to change the nominated tax rate + Read more ...

Are they contacting to advise a change in the nominated tax rate?

  • Yes, the nominated tax rate (the default rate is 15%, however customers can nominate between 0 - 50%). See Managing Australian Taxation Office (ATO) tax deductions. Note: these changes can only be applied prior to the nominated start date (NSD). If contact is made after their NSD, changes to their tax rate cannot be made. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Change in circumstances is notified + Read more ...

For a change in circumstances to be recorded the customer must have submitted a DAP claim and the claim status must be either PAY, ASS/PND or NCL.

For claims with a DAP/REJ status, see Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Dad and Partner Pay (DAP).

If the customer:

  • advises they are working or on paid leave during their DAP period, determine if an exception or permissible purpose applies. See Eligibility for Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • advising of work or paid leave and an exception or permissible purpose applies, see Step 2 in Table 2

Is Service Officer trained in DAP claim processing?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Service Officers not trained in DAP claim processing + Read more ...

If the customer advises a change in circumstances that affects their eligibility for DAP during the DAP period, such as changes to the care they are providing for their child, or working or paid leave during their DAP period and an exception does not apply, discuss whether there is another period they meet the eligibility requirements for DAP.

If there is not another period where the customer is eligible for DAP, see Step 1 in Table 2.

If there is another period they would be eligible for DAP, and if they want to change their nominated start date to a new date:

  • complete the abridged eligibility test and where the customer has a status of:
    • DAP/ASS or PND, see Step 5 in Table 2
    • DAP PAY, include all details in the Fast Note template. Select Fast Note. Use Families > Updates > Change of Circs DAP action required. This will automatically be allocated to a trained Service Officer
    • DAP/NCL, include all details in the claim Progress DOC

Procedure ends here.

5

Contact about a change in circumstances + Read more ...

Is the customer contacting to change their nominated start date for DAP?

  • Yes, see Step 3 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Is there another period where they are eligible for DAP? + Read more ...

If the customer is advising a change in circumstances, other than a change to their nominated start date and this change affects their eligibility for DAP during their DAP period, (such as working or paid leave and an exception does not apply), discuss whether there is another period they meet eligibility requirements for DAP. If it is determined the customer is eligible to DAP for another period recommend they change their nominated start date.

Does the customer agree to changing their DAP period?

  • Yes, see Step 3 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Check DAP benefit status + Read more ...

If the customer has advised a change of circumstance for DAP and has not requested to change their nominated start date, check the DAP benefit status before making any updates.

If the benefit status for DAP displays as:

  • PAY (paid), ASS (assessed) or PND (pending), see Step 2 in Table 2
  • NCL (new claim) that has not been assessed, the change in circumstances can be completed as part of the claim processing. See Processing DAP online and assisted customer claims

8

FCC processing - ACTDOC or Work Item received + Read more ...

ACTDOC received

Upon receipt of an ACTDOC relating to a DAP claim that has not yet been assessed, process the DAP claim based on the new information provided. See Assessing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims.

If the claim cannot be processed, create or annotate a progress DOC to include the changed information and place the claim on hold for the required timeframe.

To change the nominated start date after DAP has been paid, see Step 4 in Table 2.

Workload Management

Online updates that do not auto-finalise will be allocated via a Work Item with ID ZCOC ZDAP (Family Related Change of Circumstance). See Workload Management.

Check information about the online update in the Notes section. Any urgent information or errors will appear at the bottom of this section.

If details have been updated online and have not been finalised, additional required action is detailed in the Staff Tasks section. The relevant guided procedure must be used to finalise these tasks:

  • Changing payment destination
  • Changing address (CLK)
  • Changing relationship status, dependent child or family details
  • Change to their level of care of a child

Once all Staff Tasks are completed, finalise the Work Item by changing the status from Completed to Finalised.

  • Scroll to the top of the Work Item information screen and select Save
  • Confirm changes applied successfully. A green tick will appear towards the top right hand corner of the screen
  • Select Cancel to exit Edit mode, or Back to go to the Inbox

Updating nominated start dates requests and other changes of circumstances

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Updating change in circumstances + Read more ...

Circumstance changes can be updated using the following procedures:

  • Changing DAP income
  • Changing Work Detail, including working/paid leave or work exception updates
  • Changing relationship status, dependent child or family details
  • Change to the date they have care of a child
  • Changing nominated start date

To change the nominated start date, go to Step 3.

For all other changes in circumstances, go to Step 2.

2

Update and record the change + Read more ...

Run the DAP circumstances guided procedure to update the change.

  • Discuss the change
  • Launch the DAP circumstances guided procedure
  • Select the relevant claim if prompted to go to the Claim Summary. This displays circumstance information that can be updated
  • Select Next to go to the Task Selector. This displays all circumstance tasks that can be updated. Select the appropriate task/s:
    • Claim details: expected date of birth or care date (pre-birth claims only)
    • DAP Income: DAP income estimate, can be updated once the DAP status is ASS (assessed) or PND (pending)
    • Work details: view work history test, view or update return to work, including paid leave or record a return to work exception
      Note: the return to work exception reason ‘Essential worker responding to state, territory or national emergency’ can only be recorded if the customer returned to work on or after 12 March 2020. For claims requiring a return to work exception prior to 12 March 2020, code the Paid Parental Leave Exemption (P1EX) screen using the exemption reason as DWO –-Disregard Work. The P1EX screen is not to be used for coding the DWO exception after 12 March 2020
    • Advise child not in care: update child in care details
  • Select the Launch Staff Task icon
  • On the Activity Details screen:
    • Select the Staff Tasks tab. The Staff Tasks will be displayed based on the selections made on the Task Selector
    • If a relevant task is not displayed, select Back to return to the Task Selector
  • Select the hyperlink for each task. Once inside the task, use the New button to update the change in circumstances. To update a field, select and key the changed details or use the calendar function
  • If necessary, manually change the status of workflows to Completed, Required or Not Required. This should only be done if the status of the staff workflow is preventing the claim from being processed
  • Select the Icon in the Change Status column in Staff Workflows. This will not change the status to Completed, Required or Not required
  • If the change is resulting in a favourable outcome for the customer, check that the customer's nominated DAP tax rate is applied within the activity. For more information on how to update ATO tax deductions see Managing Australian Taxation Office (ATO) tax deductions
  • Once all updates are made, to complete the DAP circumstances guided procedure, run the Finalisation guided procedure from Staff Tasks
  • If the activity has not auto completed, select the activity from the Activity List (AL) screen and finalise on the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Advise the customer of the change in circumstances outcome
  • A letter will be issued advising the change and outcome

A 2 year limiting date ensures reassessments of older DAP claims do not automatically apply a retrospective change to a customer's record. These changes must be manually coded.

Is the customer notifying a retrospective change in circumstances for a DAP child whose date of birth or entry into care is more than 2 years from today's date?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, procedure ends here

3

Change of DAP period nominated start date + Read more ...

If they are changing their DAP period nominated start date and:

  • DAP/NCL has not been completed, go to Step 4
  • DAP/ASS (assessed) or DAP/PND (pending), go to Step 5
  • DAP has already been paid and new nominated DAP period has been provided, go to Step 6

4

DAP/NCL activity has not been completed + Read more ...

Service Officers not trained in DAP claim processing

  • Complete the abridged eligibility test and include all details in a Progress DOC. Create the ACTDOC using Fast Note. Select Auto Text. Use Families > Updates > Change of Circs DAP action required. This will automatically be allocated to a trained Service Officer

FCC processing staff

  • Before changing the customer's nominated start date for DAP, complete the abridged eligibility test to confirm they will meet the eligibility requirements in the new DAP period
    • Complete the new claim activity dataload
    • Add the Claim Details task to the NCL activity. Once inside the task, update the Dad and Partner Pay period start date. The question 'Child was/will be in care every date between <date A and date B> will default to Yes
      If the customer has advised they will not have care of the child for all days, select No. This will allow the first date the child was not in the customer's care to be recorded. To update a field, select and key the changed details or use the calendar function
    • Finalise the new claim activity
    • Record the updates in the progress DOC. If the customer wants to change any information they advised in their DAP claim and the claim can be completed, the change in circumstances can be completed as part of DAP claim processing

Note: if the relevant financial year has changed, the NCL activity will need to be cancelled and a new claim indexed using the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC). Information provided in the initial claim combined with the new details provided by the customer are to be used.

Procedure ends here.

5

DAP/ASS (assessed) or DAP/PND (pending) + Read more ...

Customers can notify a change in their nominated start date by phone or in person.

Before changing the nominated start date for DAP, complete the abridged eligibility test to confirm they will meet the eligibility requirements in the new DAP period.

If the customer meets the abridged eligibility test, launch the DAP Circumstances guided procedure Claim Summary:

  • Select DAP Claim details (tab)
  • Select Next
  • Select 'Claim Details'
  • Select the Launch Staff Task icon
  • On the Activity Details screen:
    • Select the Staff Tasks tab to display tasks based on the selections made on the Task Selector
  • Select the hyperlink for Claim Details task. Once inside the task, use the New button to update the Dad and Partner pay period start date. The question 'Child was/will be in care every day between <date A and date B> will default to Yes. If the customer has advised they will not have care of the child for all days, select No. This will allow the first date the child was not in the customer's care to be recorded. To update a field, select and key the changed details or use the calendar function. Once updated select Finish
  • If necessary, manually change the status of workflows to Completed, Required or Not Required. This should only be done if the status of the staff workflow is preventing the claim from being processed
    • Select the icon in the Change Status column in Staff Workflows. This will not change the status to Completed, Required or Not required
  • Once all updates are made, to complete the DAP Circumstances guided procedure, run the Finalisation guided procedure from Staff Tasks
  • If the activity has not auto completed, select the activity from the Activity List (AL) screen and finalise on the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Advise the customer of the change in circumstances outcome
  • A letter will be issued advising the change and outcome

Customers who do not meet the abridged eligibility test will have their claim rejected, e.g. they now fail the work test, income test. Record the relevant change in circumstances so the claim rejects for the appropriate reason.

Procedure ends here.

6

DAP paid and new nominated DAP period start date provided + Read more ...

  • If the customer has provided a new nominated start date (NSD), complete the abridged eligibility test before changing the NSD for DAP. This is to confirm they will meet the eligibility requirements in their new DAP period. Check if the new DAP period is in a different financial year and ensure the customer provides the new DAP income details
  • The original DAP claim must be manually rejected reason 'OTH'. If a debt is raising as part of the activity, when the claim is finalised a debt shell will automatically create and be allocated to the appropriate debt team for investigation. Check region code for Processing Service details for: Debt Raising
  • Before finalising the new claim activity with the new NSD, check if the customer has already received DAP for the child
  • If the NSD is changing to a future date, and DAP has not been paid finalise the claim
  • If the NSD is changing to a past date, or a date in the future and DAP has already been paid, before finalising the new claim activity, check if an overpayment for the previous DAP period for the child is on the overpayment debt list (OPDL) screen and:
    • If debt has not been repaid, then the DAP arrears on the OOP screen must be adjusted accordingly to recover the debt as they have already been paid their DAP entitlement. Use Fast Note. Select Auto Text. Use Debts > DMN Raising > Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request. Add the details to the DOC including the AMR number, the Debt ID and the reason why the debt needs to be zeroed. Send to the debt team to advise that the debt is to be adjusted to finalise no debt 'FND'
    • If debt has been repaid, then the DAP arrears are to be released
  • Ensure a DOC is created stating the change in circumstance and reason for rejecting the original claim. Send a Q999 detailing the outcome
  • The initial claim can be re-indexed. See Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) for re-indexing previously rejected post and pre-birth DAP claims. The date of receipt (DOR) is the date they requested the new nominated DAP start date. See Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave payments for information on how to change the DOR for reindexed post birth claims

Note: if they are not entitled to the equivalent DAP payment that was paid in the previous DAP period for example the customer received the full DAP period initially and the new DAP period is 7 days only, they will have an overpayment. Where the customer initially received 7 days of DAP and they are now eligible to 14 days in the new DAP period, the amount on the OOP screen will need to be adjusted accordingly.

Procedure ends here.

7

Coding change in circumstances outside 2 year limiting date + Read more ...

For a change in circumstance for a DAP claim where the child's date of birth or entry into care is more than 2 years in the past. The system will only apply the reassessment on the DAP child if the user is selecting the applicable DAP child and the relevant DAP claim (e.g. when a retrospective change in circumstances needs to be coded).

Once the relevant updates have been coded on the customer's record, manually code the reassessment.

To manually code a reassessment, within Customer First select the Claim Contacts > Paid Parental Leave Claim summary option.

The PPL Claim Summary (P1CS) screen will display with the current DAP claim information. Code an 'A' to navigate to the PPL Claim Action (P1CA) screen to key the reassessment details

On the P1CA screen key the fields:

  • Action: select REA (reassessment)
  • Reason: no reason is required to perform this action
  • Effect Date: the Date of Effect of the changes to the DAP claim.
  • Notes: outline the relevant details for the cancellation

Check details before finalising activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Record details on a DOC.
