1

Mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

Job seekers will be identified as an ESL who are:

  • under 22 years of age, and
  • have not completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification

Most ESLs are required to undertake approved requirements to meet their mutual obligation requirements. Approved requirements could be:

  • full-time education or training
  • a combination of part-time education or training and part-time work for a total of 25 hours per week (15 hours for Principal Carer Parents and those with a Partial Capacity to Work of 15 to 29 hours per week)
  • other approved requirements for 25 hours per week (15 hours for Principal Carer Parents and those with a Partial Capacity to Work of 15 to 29 hours per week), or
  • Youth Activities

This does not apply to job seekers in Community Development Program (CDP) regions, as requirements in CDP are voluntary. See Community Development Program (CDP) for information about mutual obligation requirements for CDP job seekers.

All job seekers with mutual obligation requirements must have a Job Plan negotiated as soon as possible.

If a job seeker is provider managed, then the provider is responsible for determining what requirements will satisfy mutual obligation requirements and complete a Job Plan with the job seeker.

Service Officers should only negotiate Job Plans for Centrelink managed job seekers in accordance with Negotiating Centrelink Managed Job Plans.

5

Coding of the NES screen for ESLs under the age of 18 + Read more ...

Early School Leavers are required to undertake approved requirements, job search or a combination of these to satisfy their mutual obligation requirements. If the job seeker is under 18, go to the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen and in the:

  • Study Exemption Rsn field, key 'YAA' (YAL activity agreement signed)
  • Study Exemption End date field, key the YAA exemption for the maximum 6 month period. The exemption only needs to be coded once and remains effective until the job seeker turns 18 years of age, even where the exemption end date has expired. The job seeker will not suspend for reason 'Under 18 - No Exemption to Studies (NEX) on expiry of the exemption

Record a DOC on the job seeker's record. Procedure ends here.

6

Job seeker's claim shows completion of Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification + Read more ...

The job seeker must provide verification of their educational qualification. This may include:

  • a Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification certificate issued by a Senior Secondary Board of Studies or an 'equivalent qualification' such as:
    • an Australian Qualification Framework Certificate III
    • a higher qualification issued by a Registered Training Organisation or higher educational institution
    • a Certificate III or IV of General Education for Adults
    • the International Baccalaureate, or
    • other higher education pre-entry course
  • letter from the school or institution
  • other evidence (for example, Details on the job seekers record which indicate they have completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification
  • where no other evidence is available, a statutory declaration detailing the name of the course, date completed, institution and contact details

Note: a statement of attainment is generally not accepted as sufficient evidence. A statement of attainment does not confirm the job seeker successfully completed a course, it only confirms that they have completed certain units from part of that course.

Completion of Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification may not require the job seeker to have sat Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification exams. A Victorian Certificate of Education, for example, can be awarded where the student has submitted School-based Assessments for satisfactory completion of units but have not been assessed for levels of achievement in the study and have not sat examinations. The job seeker still needs to provide proof of the completion of Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification (that is, VCE certificate).

If verification is not available or unable to be obtained, then a determination can be made based on the weight of evidence as to whether it appears the job seeker has attained the required level of educational qualifications. An example of other evidence is where the Service Officer can see the job seeker has undertaken this level of study as information has been recorded previously on one of the following screens:

Process Direct:

  • Customer Study Details (EDC/EDCH) screen
  • Short Course Details (SCD/SCDS) screen

Customer First:

  • Education Course History (EDCH) screen
  • Education Course Details (EDC) screen
  • Short Course Details screen

Job seeker has provided verification, or is it apparent from their record that the job seeker has completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification + Read more ...

Go to the NES screen and:

  • make appropriate updates. Note: a warning message will display to check and update the Job Seeker Snapshot. Action as appropriate
  • key the Y12/Equiv Verified field using one of the valid types of evidence:
    • 'VEC' - Certification of achievement
    • 'VEL' - Letter from the school or institution

  • finalise the claim, the existing job seeker process is to be followed. See Applying mutual obligation requirements

8

Job seeker states they will be able to provide verification after the participation interview + Read more ...

Key on the NES screen within the new claim activity with the following:

  • Y12/Equiv Verified field, key 'NVE' (Not verified)
  • Study Exemption Rsn field, key '28D' (28 day exemption to verify Y12/Equiv)
  • end date of the study exemption in the Exemption End Date field - end date should be 28 days in the future

Finalise the claim. The job seeker will be given up to 28 days to provide verification of their qualification, however, they should provide it as soon as possible. Tell the job seeker if verification is not provided within 28 days, they will be identified as an ESL. If the MFU activity with keyword 'Y12NVE' is still displaying on the Activity List (AL) screen, do not complete this activity. New claim procedure ends here.

Note: the study exemption code of '28D' can only be keyed within the new claim activity for ESLs and the end date should be keyed for a maximum 28 days in the future. If verification is not provided by the end of the 28 day period, a different MFU with keyword '28DESL' will be generated on the AL screen and an Urgent Issue will be displayed on Customer First to prompt the Service Officer to take follow-up actions.

For actions to be taken when the MFU review matures at 28 days, see Step 2 in Table 3.

Categories of Centrelink managed job seekers + Read more ...

ESLs who have one of the following approved activities will be Services Australia managed:

  • Quarterly Participation Interviews for Partial Capacity to Work and Temporary Reduced Work Capacity (0-7 or 8-14 hours per week) job seekers
  • job seekers with partial capacity to work (PCW) or temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC) or 15-29 hours per week undertaking paid work, study or self-employment, or a combination of the 3, for at least 15 hours per week
  • principal carers undertaking paid work, study or self-employment, or a combination of the 3, for at least 15 hours per week
  • job seekers participating in Self-Employment Assistance whose Self-Employment Allowance has ceased 39 weeks after commencing the programme
  • job seekers participating in Youth Activities

Job plans must be negotiated in accordance with Negotiating Centrelink Managed Job Plans.

MFU review matures + Read more ...

When the ESLs' education qualification has been updated to Y12/13 or Equivalent (for example, Form 6 or Matriculation) via the Job Seeker Snapshot, a MFU activity with the keyword of 'Y12NVE' will be generated and display on the job seeker's record. Evidence is provided + Read more ...

If the MFU activity with keyword 'Y12NVE' is displaying on the Activity List (AL) screen, select the activity and go to the NES screen. Update the Y12/Equiv Verified field in the NES screen with the appropriate code:

  • 'VEC' - Certification of achievement

If it has been established that the Yr12/Equiv Verified field has been incorrectly updated as 'verified', a Service Officer at the APS5 level or above with the required 'NESY12' SAMS access will need to correct or delete the incorrect verified status.

4

Evidence is not received from existing job seeker + Read more ...

Check if the job seeker has provided verification of completing Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification and the details are confirmed. To do this check:

Process Direct:

  • Customer Study Details (EDC/EDCH) screen, or
  • Short Course Details (SCD/SCDS) screen

Customer First:

  • Education Course History (EDCH) screen

If so, ensure the Verification Document Code field in Process Direct or the Yr12/Equiv Verified field in Customer First on the NES screen is updated with an appropriate code. For example, 'OTH' (other evidence as appropriate).

If details are not confirmed on the EDC/EDCH or SCD/SCDS in Process Direct or the EDCH screen in Customer First, tell the job seeker of the requirement to provide original documentation to prove their educational qualification as soon as possible.

If the qualification is not verified 14 days after the Educ Level Attained field on the NES screen has been updated to Y12 Sec, a letter will be sent reminding them to verify the qualification within 14 days or their payment may be suspended.

If the ESL does not provide the requested documentation within 14 days from the date the letter is sent, the job seeker's payment will automatically suspended reason code 'QNV' (Y12/Equiv Qualification Not Verified). Payment can only be restored when the qualification has been keyed as verified.

If payment is still suspended after 13 weeks, it will be automatically cancelled reason code 'QNV'. Cancellations under this reason cannot be restored.

Note: where the job seeker is unable to provide original documentation within the required timeframe (for example, educational institution is closed for a period of time), statutory declaration can be provided. See Step 3.

If the job seeker has not provided the verification of completing Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification

  • Update Yr12/Equiv Verified field on the NES screen with 'NVE' (not verified)
  • Tell the job seeker they will be subject to ESL requirements of approved activities and/or job search to satisfy their mutual obligation requirements
  • If the MFU activity with keyword of 'Y12NVE' is still displaying on the Activity List (AL) screen, select and complete activity
  • If not connected to a Provider, refer to an Employment Services Provider and book the initial appointment within the 2 working days if possible
  • Tell the job seeker the provider will negotiate the Job Plan with them. ESLs identified as eligible must be referred to Workforce Australia - Transition to Work (TtW) (where available)
