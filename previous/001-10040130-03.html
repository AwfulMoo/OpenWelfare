Collecting evidence for job seeker compliance decisions

Table 1

Item

Description

1

General principles for collecting evidence + Read more ...

Job seeker compliance decisions must be evidence-based decisions. This means the decision must be based on the relevant facts.

All compliance investigations include evidence from both the person who reported the failure or generated the investigation, and the job seeker. Whether any further evidence is required to reach a decision, will depend on the clarity and consistency of these two forms of primary evidence.

The decision maker must be confident they have sufficient evidence to establish the facts relevant to the reported failure to make an informed decision.

Any evidence collected as part of a compliance investigation should:

  • be specific to the compliance incident that has been reported
  • focus on the issues that must be considered to make a determination under the applicable legislation and policy. For example, the nature of the non-compliance event, circumstances that affected the reasonableness of the requirement, the job seeker's capacity to comply or to make prior contact (when relevant to the decision)

The Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer or service centre Service Officers with UNPP security resource should attempt to gather sufficient evidence, complete the investigation and make a decision during the initial contact with the job seeker.

2

Collecting supplementary evidence + Read more ...

Supplementary evidence is only required when there is insufficient evidence to reach a conclusive decision without it and the further evidence or information is likely to influence the outcome of the decision.

When supplementary evidence is required, the decision maker should explore whether this information is already available online (for example, on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen) or the job seeker can immediately upload this using Upload documents.

Attempts to gather additional verbal testimony as supplementary evidence during initial contact with the job seeker should be made when this evidence will affect the outcome of the decision and:

  • it is not reasonable or practical for supplementary evidence to be immediately provided by the job seeker, or
  • the job seeker is disputing the veracity of the information supplied by the person who generated the compliance action

3

Supplementary evidence for mutual obligation failure financial penalty decisions + Read more ...

When a financial penalty for a mutual obligation failure is being considered, if supplementary evidence cannot be collected on the date of initial contact, a decision is made based on the available evidence. This is because the determination of a financial penalty in relation to a mutual obligation failure forms part of the assessment of the entitlement for the outstanding period.

The Participation Compliance workflow does not provide the option to hold a mutual obligation failure penalty investigation pending the return of evidence, nor can payment be released for the entitlement period until the determination has been finalised.

4

Supplementary evidence for unemployment failures, work refusal failures and Job Seeker Compliance Model decisions + Read more ...

When the job seeker requires time to source and supply supplementary evidence, the job seeker must be given a reasonable opportunity to provide this before a decision is made. The Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer or service centre Service Officer with UNPP security resource negotiates this timeframe with the job seeker. The amount of time is not prescriptive however two working days is generally considered appropriate.

The Service Officer must:

  • explain the benefits of providing the evidence in the agreed timeframe and the impact that failing to provide the evidence may have on the decision
  • document in the Further Evidence section:
    • what evidence was requested and why this is likely to influence the outcome of the decision
    • when it is due, and
    • the job seeker is aware a decision about the failure will be made based on the available evidence if not provided by the due date

The workflow will navigate to the Save and Exit page and automatically select the appropriate save and exit option to temporarily remove the contact trigger pending the return of evidence. For Job Seeker Compliance Model compliance investigations, the system will auto restore payment if it is currently suspended 'DNA participation appointment' (APA).

On the Activity List (AL) screen, ensure the activity has been held with an appropriate due date, generally this will be the 'Due date given + 1'.

If the job seeker does not provide the evidence by the required date the contact trigger will be reinstated and the job seeker will not be able to complete their report until they have spoken to the PST. A decision should be made based on the available information. Facts that cannot be substantiated with evidence may either be given lesser weight, or may need to be disregarded.

Types of evidence

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Types of evidence + Read more ...

Evidence collected during a compliance investigation can take different forms and may need to be collected from a variety of sources. Some evidence can carry a greater weighting than other forms of evidence.

Evidence may include:

  • reported non-compliance event, see Item 2
  • job seeker's statement, see Item 3
  • job seeker circumstances impacting capacity to comply, see Item 4
  • phone contact with provider, see Item 5
  • external third parties, see Item 6
  • job seeker's Services Australia record, see Item 7
  • Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Support Officer (SSO), social worker or specialists, see Item 8
  • job seeker behaviour and presentation, see Item 9
  • Employment Services Assessment (ESAt), see Item 10

2

Reported non-compliance event + Read more ...

The reported non-compliance event is the starting point for all compliance investigations. The report submitted by the provider, or non-compliance event generated by Services Australia is a primary source of evidence and must always be considered. The report provides information about the non-compliance event, the incident date, notification and any other relevant information available at the time the of the report.

When a non-compliance event was reported by the provider, the full report is accessed via a link within the Compliance Investigation workflow. In addition, relevant information is extracted from the report to display at appropriate pages in the workflow.

If necessary, additional information may be sourced from the Employment Services System (ESS) using the links within the Compliance Investigation workflow:

  • Job/Participation Plan
  • Provider Recorded Information
  • Online Diary
  • Job Seeker Calendar. Note: this will display for Targeted Compliance Framework compliance action only

3

Job seeker's explanation of what occurred + Read more ...

A job seeker's statement is considered primary evidence in the investigation and must always be considered when investigating a reported non-compliance event. Compliance action is not applied without first affording the job seeker the opportunity to explain the circumstances that led to the reported incident of non-compliance.

Under the principles of natural justice, all job seekers have a right to a fair hearing when an incident of non-compliance is reported. This includes providing an opportunity to:

  • explain the circumstances that led to the alleged failure to comply, and
  • explain how the circumstances impacted their capacity to comply or ability to make prior contact (if relevant)

Generally this evidence is collected verbally, with a factual summary of the conversation recorded by the Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer or service centre Service Officer with UNPP security resource.

When collecting a job seeker's statement the Service Officer is responsible for leading the discussion with the job seeker in a manner that uses:

  • open questions to encourage the job seeker to explain their version of what occurred and any factors they believed impacted compliance
  • specific questions if further clarification is required or to resolve inconsistencies
  • ensuring the conversation remains focused on the specific non-compliance event as reported

The Service Officer records a concise and factual summary of the job seeker's statement in the Compliance Investigation workflow. Any evidence the job seeker would be willing to provide to support their statements should be recorded as part of this statement.

4

Circumstances impacting compliance + Read more ...

Ongoing job seeker circumstances that had a direct and significant impact on the non-compliance event may inform the compliance decision.

When a job seeker is claiming an ongoing/intermittent circumstance directly contributed to non-compliance, check if the circumstance, impact and sufficient evidence are currently documented on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen. This screen can be accessed directly from the Compliance Investigation workflow.

If a job seeker's circumstances that impacted compliance is not recorded on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen, or there is insufficient information/evidence for the purposes of a compliance investigation, it must be gathered and documented on this screen during the investigation. Information recorded or updated on this screen during the investigation forms part of the evidence base for the investigation and can be viewed later as part of the completed summary for the compliance investigation.

5

Phone contact with provider + Read more ...

When there are discrepancies between a provider's statement and the job seeker's statement which would influence the decision, additional information/evidence may need to be considered.

The provider should always be offered the opportunity to respond to the job seeker's statement when it conflicts with theirs. A 3-way telephone conversation is generally the most effective method to clarify discrepancies, to gain insight and greater understanding about the event and ensure important and relevant information has not been missed.

When recording verbal evidence, care needs to be taken to note all factual information relevant to the decision along with the source of the evidence.

When attempts to contact the provider by phone have been unsuccessful:

  • Targeted Compliance Framework decisions are made based on the available information
  • Job Seeker Compliance Model compliance reports should be returned to the provider. Use an appropriate 'returned report' rejection reason on the Decision and Penalty page in the Compliance Investigation workflow and include specific information in the free text to the provider to clearly explain the discrepancy/disputed information that requires further clarification. Providers have 5 days to resubmit the report with the required information if they still wish to pursue compliance action under the Job Seeker Compliance Model

6

External third party information + Read more ...

Contact with third parties can be an effective way to confirm statements and information that require verification and are unable to be verified through other sources.

A third party contact:

  • is only required if it is likely to influence the decision, and
  • must be considered and weighed against all other evidence

In general, third party contacts are considered supplementary evidence. They are used if required to assist in making a determination when conflicting information has been provided by the job seeker's statement and the person who reported the incident of non-compliance or when additional information is required about how specific job seeker circumstances impact their capacity.

Third party contacts may include:

  • previous employer to gather further detail on events leading to unemployment due to misconduct as a worker or voluntarily ceasing suitable employment
  • youth workers
  • medical professionals

7

Information contained on the job seeker's Services Australia record + Read more ...

The job seeker's Services Australia record is used to gain insight into their circumstances and can also be used as evidence, or when weighing evidence.

The Compliance Investigation workflow contains links to a wide range of information about the job seeker's circumstances and links to other relevant information:

  • Recent non-compliance action, including a Capability Assessment or Comprehensive Compliance Assessment (CCA)
  • Income declared and hours worked in the two previous reporting periods
  • Services Australia Rates Estimator
  • Address History
  • View ESAt for Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) referrals
  • Recent activities and exemptions
  • Circumstances impacting compliance
  • Medical certificates

Other information available to Services Australia may also be available outside the Participation Compliance workflow, for example on the following screens:

  • Document List
  • Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen also showing hours worked in one period
  • Document Tools

8

Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Support Officer (SSO), PST-skilled social worker or specialists + Read more ...

Consultations with a PST-skilled SSO, PST-skilled social worker or specialists can assist in establishing the facts and/or in weighing the evidence.

Examples of when this contact may be appropriate include contact with a:

  • Services Australia specialists including PST-skilled SSO, PST-skilled social workers, Indigenous Service Officers (ISO), Indigenous Cultural Consultants or Multicultural Services Officers (MSO) consultation to assist with identifying how job seeker circumstances impacted their capacity to comply with a specific requirement or to make prior contact
  • Services Australia assessors or the job seeker's treating health care professionals to gather information about how their medical condition(s) impact(s) on capacity

It is preferable that evidence of this nature is gathered verbally while the job seeker is on the phone. This allows follow up questions, quicker decision making and if required, appropriate interventions to support the job seeker. However, evidence from third parties may also include written statements or other supporting documents.

Contact with specialists in regards to understanding the impact of job seeker's circumstances on capacity to comply are recorded on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen.

PST-skilled Service Officers are not required to source additional written evidence to verify the professional advice given by a Services Australia social worker or specialist in relation to a decision on a reported failure. For example, when a PST-skilled social worker provides evidence about the impact of a mental health condition on the job seeker's capacity to comply, a PST-skilled Service Officer should not seek to source a medical certificate verifying the same information.

See Accessing a PST-skilled social worker for information on when consultation with a PST-skilled social worker may be required or appropriate.

9

Job seeker behaviour and presentation + Read more ...

A record of non-verbal cues from contact with the job seeker may sometimes be considered evidence in a compliance investigation when this is relevant to the investigation.

For example, advice from the service centre about the job seeker's presentation, the job seeker's engagement in the discussion, their tone, interest in their activity or complying with their compulsory requirements or the manner in which the job seeker conducts themselves during any 3-way conversations with a provider may all be relevant facts that inform the compliance investigation outcome.

This type of evidence is only recorded when it is specifically relevant to the decision under consideration. Personal judgement will dictate when it may be appropriate to consider and record this type of information which should be written in an objective manner, based on fact, and linked to the decision under investigation. Any information recorded in the compliance investigation may be requested by job seekers under Freedom of Information (FOI).

10

Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) Reports + Read more ...

The Report Assessment Summary of certain ESAts incorporates a 'risk of non-compliance' rating and details of specific vulnerabilities or barriers that may affect capacity to comply. This rating may inform a compliance decision.

When investigating an unemployment failure, work refusal failure or Job Seeker Compliance Model compliance action, an ESAt may be initiated if the Service Officer requires assessment of a possible medical condition, such as a neurological impairment, mental illness or learning disorder. This may include circumstances where the job seeker has no insight into their condition, and when their responses to the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) do not trigger an ESAt.

When a Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer determines an ESAt is required and the ESAt is not triggered by the JSCI (or a JSCI cannot be run), a referral using the referral reason of Participation Failure Pending (PFP) should be made. This referral reason ensures:

  • the Assessor gives priority to returning the report, and
  • the report contains advice about the risk of non-compliance rating

For further information see Understanding Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) reports.

Methods for returning evidence required for an outstanding compliance action + Read more ...

For example, a temporary incapacity exemption should be assessed when the job seeker requires and a Centrelink Medical Certificate is supplied.

Assessing evidence for job seeker compliance investigation decisions

For Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officers and service centre Service Officers with UNPP security resource only

Table 4

Item

Description

1

Assessment of evidence + Read more ...

Evidence gathered as part of a compliance investigation should be assessed by considering the:

  • relevance of the evidence to the incident
  • nature of the evidence
  • source of the evidence and/or deemed reliability of the witness, and
  • credibility of the evidence, that is, how convincing it is, is it corroborated or contradicted by other evidence

The assessment of evidence is known as 'weighing' the evidence. This is an important part of the compliance investigation.

Assessing and weighing each piece of evidence allows the decision maker to determine how much impact the evidence has on the investigation.

2

Weighting of evidence + Read more ...

Evidence is weighted, or considered, to form an opinion about the probably, or most likely, version of events. As a general rule, evidence given the most weight in an investigation will be:

  • directly relevant to the incident
  • precise in nature
  • deemed to be from a reliable, trustworthy source, and
  • would be viewed as credible by others

The weight assigned to evidence will reduce for each of the above elements that is not met.

Facts that cannot be substantiated with evidence should either be given lesser weight or may need to be disregarded to form a decision.

When a claim is made that may affect the outcome of an investigation, several lesser weighted pieces of evidence may together result in an assessment this was the more probable version of events.

Evidence about a job seeker's circumstances around the time of the compliance event where the job seeker's circumstances do not directly relate to the incident under investigation may help establish the context in which the non-compliance event occurred, however, would not have the same weight as evidence that specifically relates to the non-compliance event.

If conflicting or insufficient evidence cannot be resolved by gathering further supporting evidence, weighing the evidence can assist in reaching a decision based on the 'balance of probability', such as, what is more likely to have occurred.

If this is required, the way in which evidence has been weighed must be detailed on the Decision and Penalty page of the Compliance Investigation workflow.

For further information see the Recording evidence for job seeker compliance decisions table.

The Resources page contains some examples of how evidence is weighted.

Recording evidence for job seeker compliance decisions in the Participation Compliance workflow

Table 5

Item

Description

1

General requirements for recording of evidence + Read more ...

All evidence that is relevant to the compliance investigation and decision must be documented within the Compliance Investigation workflow.

Effective documentation of evidence:

  • details the source and content of the evidence
  • includes only evidence that is relevant to the decision
  • avoids duplication of information within the workflow that does not 'add value'
  • uses simple language which others can easily understand
  • can be substantiated on review

2

Recording the job seeker statement + Read more ...

The job seeker's statements regarding their reasons for the non-compliance event and their reasons for not making prior contact are recorded on the Investigation page of the Compliance Investigation workflow, in response to the following questions:

  • Is there anything else they want to add to explain the failure / What reasons have they given for the failure?
  • Why didn't you contact your provider before you were due to attend?

The job seeker statements should be written as a concise, factual summary that accurately represents all relevant information the job seeker wanted considered as part of the decision along with any evidence they have available they could provide to support the validity of their statements. It is not appropriate for the Service Officer who is conducting the compliance investigation to record their own commentary or opinions about the job seeker's explanation as part of the job seeker statement.

Before finalising, check enough detail is included to ensure another person reviewing the case at a later date will clearly understand the job seeker's explanation of what occurred and any factors the job seeker believes impacted their capacity to comply.

3

Recording sufficient evidence or further evidence obtained + Read more ...

The Compliance Investigation workflow is intuitive; the Further Evidence section on the Investigation page is only displayed when the assessment of the job seeker's reasons for the failure and prior contact indicates substantiating evidence has been provided to form an opinion about the job seeker's reason or prior contact, or if further evidence is required to make an assessment.

The further evidence section is used to record evidence that relates to the job seeker's reason for the non-compliance event and prior contact reasons provided.

In this section, the Service Officer conducting the compliance investigation will need to record:

  • whether sufficient evidence already exists to confirm the excuse for the failure
  • whether sufficient evidence already exists to confirm the reason for not contacting or that the provider was contacted with a good reason beforehand
  • the method that will be used to collect further evidence, where possible this should be via phone contact
  • if supplementary evidence has been requested, the date this is due (work refusal failure, unemployment failure and Job Seeker Compliance Model compliance action only)
  • who needs to be contacted for supplementary evidence
  • a summary of the supplementary evidence that has been provided

Where more than 1 phone contact has occurred to gather further evidence, record the details of the primary contact as only 1 type of contact (for example, provider, employer or other) may be selected. The details of additional supplementary contacts, such as organisation/business name, contact person, contact number and a summary of the evidence provided is then captured in the 'Summary of evidence requested/provided' field.

If supplementary evidence has been requested and a due date recorded for a work refusal failure, unemployment failure and Job Seeker Compliance Model compliance action, the Save and Exit page will need to be completed before exiting the Compliance Investigation workflow.

4

Recording evidence from providers + Read more ...

When a provider reports a non-compliance event, this report is a key part of the evidence base for the decision. Key information from this report, and a link to the full report, are automatically maintained as part of the decision history.

When additional contact with the provider occurred as part of the investigation, this contact must be recorded on the Further Evidence screen in the Compliance Investigation workflow, with details of who provided the additional evidence clearly identified. A summary of all factual information relevant to the decision obtained during this contact must be included. Comments which represent an opinion should be noted as such, and only included if relevant to the decision making process.

Information sourced from the Employment Services System (ESS) that provides evidence should not be recorded on the Further Evidence screen and does not need to be recorded verbatim, instead if considered should be referenced within the decision summary.

Note: all information supplied by a provider may be requested by the job seeker under Freedom of Information unless the provider has requested this information not be released and provides valid reasons why the release should not occur.

While information from the provider forms part of the failure evidence base and elements of this may need to be referenced when summarising the decision, there is no requirement to include provider comments verbatim within the decision summary as this will already be recorded within the Compliance Investigation workflow.

5

Recording specialist and other third party contact + Read more ...

Contact with specialists and other third parties must be recorded on the Further Evidence page in the Participation Compliance workflow, with the source clearly identified.

A summary of all factual information relevant to the decision obtained during this contact must be included. Comments which represent an opinion should be avoided. When a job seeker circumstance that has an ongoing effect on the job seeker's capacity to comply is identified or requires update as a result of this contact, the Circumstances Impacting Compliance page should be accessed from the Compliance Investigation workflow and evidence recorded on this screen.

All information supplied by a third party may be requested by the job seeker under Freedom of Information unless the third party has requested this information not be released and provides valid reasons why the release should not occur.

6

Recording evidence from the job seeker's record + Read more ...

Evidence sourced from the job seeker's record is recorded on the Decision and Penalty page in the Compliance Investigation workflow. Details must include the relevant screen and the nature of the evidence.

For example, 'On the EAPP screen, the income and hours worked in the two previous reporting periods is shown confirming that the job seeker worked on the failure incident date.'

7

Decision rationale + Read more ...

The decision rationale is recorded on the Decision and Penalty page in the Participation Compliance workflow. The rationale must include the matters that were taken into account when forming the decisions and how the relevant facts were substantiated (that is, the evidence that was used to support these). The decision rationale must also clearly articulate the weight that was given to evidence.
