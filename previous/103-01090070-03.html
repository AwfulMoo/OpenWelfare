Investigating Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) Income Management Work Items

Table 1: This table describes the process when a customer has an outstanding Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) Income Management Work Item which requires investigation.

Step

Action

1

Confirm Work Item investigation required + Read more ...

A Work Item may be present on a customer's record for the following reasons:

  • a Work Item has been system generated
  • a previous Work Item was not addressed at the customer's last contact or the customer was not present
  • a Service Officer has completed an Assessment Result (AR) activity on an income managed, or potential Income Management customer's record which may impact Income Management

Work Items can be viewed:

  • on the Income Management Summary screen (under the Income Management menu). Any Work Items will present under the Work Items block
  • on the Activity List (AL) screen check for any 'Income Management/Action required' activities

Note: some Work Items also display on the Issues page.

2

Contact channel + Read more ...

Has the customer contacted Smart Centre?

  • Yes, cold transfer the customer to:
    • the Income Management line
    • the Indigenous Services queue (if appropriate), or
    • Multilingual Call (if appropriate)
    • see Resources for a link to the National Transfer numbers. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Investigate and action Work Item + Read more ...

Use the tables in this process to assist in determining appropriate action required for Work Items, relating to Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) Income Management.

  • Seek assistance from a specialised Service Officer (if available) or Service Support Officer, or
  • Contact the Access Support Centre (select Income Management option) for assistance to investigate and action the Work Item. See Resources for a link to the Access Support Centre
  • Completion of an activity relating to a particular Work Item will automatically remove the Work Item and associated INM/ACT activities on the Activity List (AL) screen. If no updates are required, and appropriate investigation and discussions held manually tick off the Work Item and record details on a DOC

Note: an unactioned Work Item will create an INM/ACT activity on the Activity List (AL) screen which cannot be cancelled.

Important:

  • Work Items must not be removed without appropriate investigation and action. Inappropriately 'ticking off' any Work Item may lead to appropriate updates not being completed or impact the customer's Income Management payments
  • Work Items should not be transferred to another office

Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) Income Management Work Items

Table 2: This table details the Work Items and messages, and appropriate action specific to Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) Income Management listed by number.

Work Item

Description and Action

I703Q1

CUS/PTR (if applicable) CDA status changed, check impact on Income Management (IM) + Read more ...

When Work Item raised

A Supporting People at Risk Income Management customer or partner's (if applicable) Carer's Allowance (CDA) status has changed.

Action Required

Review the customer’s or partner's, (if applicable) Income Management eligibility to ensure eligibility criteria is met. Regular Income Management expenses and voluntary deductions should be reviewed as the customer’s circumstances and expenses may have changed. If priority needs have changed since the last review, record the discussion on the Priority Needs Expenses workflow.

For more information see:

  • Changes to Income Management priority needs, and
  • Coding Income Management expenses

I708Q1

Supporting People at Risk IM customer's payment restored. Please review expenses + Read more ...

When Work Item raised

Supporting People at Risk Income Management customer's payment has been restored.

Action Required

Review the customer's current Income Management expenses and voluntary deductions. If priority needs have changed since the last review, record the discussion on the Priority Needs Expenses workflow.

If a cancelled payment is restored:

  • within 14 days of Income Management ending, Income Management regular expenses will continue but should be reviewed
  • more than 14 days after Income Management has ended, Income Management regular expenses will have ceased and will need to be recommenced after discussions with the customer

For more information, see Effect on Income Management when payment is restored or re-granted.

I711Q1

Supporting People at Risk - Change of rent Type has occurred + Read more ...

When Work Item raised

Supporting People at Risk Income Management customer's rent Type has changed.

Action Required

Review the customer's priority needs, Income Management expenses and voluntary deductions. If priority needs have changed since the last review, record the discussion on the Priority Needs Expenses workflow.

For more information see:

  • Changes to Income Management priority needs, and
  • Coding Income Management expenses

I712Q1

Customer is imprisoned, check impact on Income Management + Read more ...

When Work Item raised

Supporting People at Risk Income Management customer is recoded as being imprisoned.

Action Required

Complete a file assessment to review the customer’s expenses, review the payment destination (GPY) and finalise any other IM activities. For example, suspend the BasicsCard and action other IM Work Items.

For more information see:

  • Effect on Income Management when payment is suspended, on zero rate, cancelled or Unemployment Non-Payment Period applied, and
  • Admission process for incarcerated customers

I713Q1

Customer's supplementary payment cancelled. Check Expenses + Read more ...

When Work Item raised

Supporting People at Risk Income Management customer's supplementary payment has been cancelled.

Action Required

Review the customer's priority needs, Income Management expenses and voluntary deductions. If priority needs have changed since the last review, record the discussion on the Priority Needs Expenses workflow.

For more information see:

  • Changes to Income Management priority needs, and
  • Coding Income Management expenses

Supporting People at Risk IM Customer's supplementary payment suspended. Review expenses + Read more ...

When Work Item raised

Supporting People at Risk Income Management customer's supplementary payment has been suspended. If priority needs have changed since the last review, record the discussion on the Priority Needs Expenses workflow.

Review the customer's priority needs, Income Management expenses and voluntary deductions.

For more information see:

I790Q1

20% UCP Service Offer available + Read more ...

When Work Item raised

Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) Income Management customer is eligible to receive a 20% Unrestricted Cash Payment (UCP).

For more information, see Unrestricted Cash Payments (UCPs).

Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) Income Management Work Items to be actioned by an IMCO only

Table 3: This table details the Work Items and messages, and appropriate action, specific to Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) Income Management, listed by number. These Work Items are to be completed by an Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) only.

Work Item

Description and Action

I789Q1

IM ends in 35 days. IMCO to contact referring ORG if applicable + Read more ...

Income Management Contact Officers (IMCO) only

When Work Item raised

The customer's current Income Management notice will end in 35 days.

Note: a Work Item will be displayed regardless of the customer's Income Management status.

Action Required

IMCO to discuss with relevant approved referring authority whether Income Management is to continue for the customer.

The IMCO is to make an appointment with the referring organisation and, where relevant, DVA to review the customer's Income Management. The relevant approved referring authority will decide whether Income Management is to end or continue.

If the customer is not currently being income managed, the referring organisation will decide if a new notice is to be issued, for potential Income Management.

I791Q1

Revoke received for current Supporting People at Risk notice. Review IM + Read more ...

Income Management Contact Officers (IMCO) only

When Work Item raised

The referring organisation has lodged a Revocation of Income Management or Cashless Debit Card notice (revoke notice), and:

  • no new commencement notice from another authority exists and the end date for the notice is more than 28 days in the future
  • no continuation notice exists and the end date for the notice is more than 28 days in the future
  • the end date for the current notice is 28 days or less in the future

Action Required

  • If the end date of the current notice is more than 28 days in the future the customer's Income Management will automatically end in 28 days
  • If the end date of the current notice is 28 days or less in the future
  • the IMCO should liaise with the referring organisation to determine what action to take with the Income Management, as the revoke notice has been received less than 28 days before Income Management end date
  • In circumstances where the revoke notice is not automatically processed, IMCOs can manually record via the Supporting People at Risk IM assessment workflow. For more information see Actioning Unified Government Gateway (UGG) requests

Note: if the customer is eligible for Income Management under a different measure when the Supporting People at Risk Notice is revoked, Income Management will continue under the other measure. For more information, see Moving between Income Management measures.
