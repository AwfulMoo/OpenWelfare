Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) and Qualifying Residence Exemptions 005-02020020

This page contains the manual referral process to Centrelink International Services (CIS) for NARWP or qualifying residence exemptions due to being a family member of a refugee or former refugee, and information to assist with these assessments. For information on how the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) and Qualifying Residence Exemptions (QRE) were impacted by coronavirus (COVID-19), see the Resources page.

1

When request is required + Read more ...

This procedure must only be used where the customer does not already satisfy residence rules or an automatic NARWP exemption (e.g. exemptions due to being an Australian citizen or holding a visa which already gives a qualifying residence exemption are automatically assessed by the system) and when directed to by a relevant process in a 'residence assessment' procedure.

Only refer to CIS if directed by the specific residence assessment procedure. See Related links on the Background page for procedures.

Has the Service Officer been directed to this procedure by a payment specific residence assessment procedure?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, see Australian residence requirements for payment. Procedure ends here

2

Reason for request + Read more ...

Referral to CIS should only be completed when 1 of the following applies:

  • Customer has lodged a claim that is rejecting for a residence related reason
  • There is a request for explanation or application for a formal review of a decision to reject for a residence related reason
  • There is a request for a hypothetical answer where a customer has lodged a claim for a different payment (e.g. Special Benefit) when they may qualify for a more beneficial payment such as JobSeeker Payment
  • The warning message 'Customer's partner/parents arrived in Australia as a refugee. Check NARWP exemption-see OB005-02020020' was presented in Process Direct
  • Exemption needs to be ended as it does not apply to current claim

When processing a new claim recording a request for explanation or application for formal review, ensure all residence coding has been completed to enable any possible automatic exemption assessment. See Residence and Portability Screens for further information.

Does the request meet CIS referral guidelines?

  • Yes, and an exemption:
    • is required, go to Step 3
    • needs to be ended, go to Step 7
  • No, an exemption does not need to be requested as claim does not exist and/or a request for explanation or application for formal review of a previous decision is not being made. Procedure ends here

3

Family member of a refugee + Read more ...

For the purpose of the NARWP and Qualifying Residence Exemptions, family members include the following:

  • A partner of the person
  • A dependent child of the person
  • Another person who, in the opinion of the Secretary, should be treated for the purposes of this definition as a partner or dependent child of the person

When assessing whether a person was a family member of a refugee/humanitarian entrant, the relationship must be considered as at the time the refugee/former refugee arrived in Australia and their circumstances at one of the following times:

  • For Family Tax Benefit (FTB) - on the earliest date they may be entitled to receive payment (generally this would be the date customer was both physically present and residing in Australia whilst the holder of a qualifying visa), or
  • For Paid Parental Leave (PPL) - on the child's date of birth or entry into care, or
  • For Dad and Partner Pay DAP - on the DAP period start date, or
  • For Youth Allowance (YA) (student) and Austudy - from date of claim, or date the customer became a family member of the refugee (if up to 13 weeks after claim date)
  • For all other payments, on the date of claim

Exception: if the refugee or humanitarian migrant has died since arrival in Australia, the customer must have been a family member of that person when the person arrived and when the person died.

The Service Officer making the referral is responsible for making the assessment of the family relationship. This assessment including the reasons for determining why a person satisfies the definition must be fully documented in the referral to CIS.

Does the customer meet one of these reasons for an exemption request?

  • Yes, and the customer is a:
    • partner of a refugee, go to Step 4
    • dependent child of a refugee, go to Step 5
    • like a partner or a dependent child to a refugee, go to Step 6
  • No, the claimant does not meet the requirement for a referral to CIS for manual NARWP/Qualifying Residence Exemption coding. Procedure ends here

4

Partner of a refugee + Read more ...

If there is coding on Marital Status (MS) screen for a Section 24 assessment, determine if it is still valid. These assessments mean the person is being assessed as un-partnered for all social security purposes which includes NARWP/Qualifying Residence Exemptions. If a person claims payment, the partner must be reassessed and placed on the partner rate or the exemption will not be applicable to the claimant. Do not refer to CIS if the Section 24 assessment is valid. See Treatment of a partnered person as single under Section 24 new determinations.

Gather the following information to include in the referral comments:

  • Reason for request (NCL started/request for explanation/application for formal review of decision/claim for different payment)
  • Payment type
  • Date of claim (if NCL started)
  • Visa subclass held by customer
  • Visa grant date
  • Refugee's CRN
  • Refugee's name
  • Refugee's date of birth
  • Refugee's date of first arrival
  • Date became member of a couple
  • Any additional information to support your request

Go to Step 7.

5

Dependent child of a refugee + Read more ...

When assessing a person as a 'dependent child' for the purposes of eligibility, the exemption applies based on the day the situation is assessed. If the child ceases to be dependent after the claim is granted (e.g. a parent dies) the exemption will continue to apply.

To be a dependent child, the child must be an Australian resident or living with an Australian resident or SCV holder residing in Australia. There are also age specific requirements which must be met. For further information see Assessment of dependent children, additional income free area and child income under social security law.

Gather the following information to include in the referral comments:

  • Reason for request (NCL started/request for explanation/application for formal review of decision/claim for different payment)
  • Payment type
  • Date of claim
  • Visa subclass held by customer
  • Visa grant date
  • Refugee's CRN
  • Refugee's name
  • Refugee's date of birth
  • Refugee's date of first arrival
  • Date customer became a full time student
  • Reason why you have determined customer is a dependent child
  • Any additional information to support your request

Go to Step 7.

6

Treated like a partner or dependent child + Read more ...

There are very limited circumstances that would meet this exemption reason. If the person wishes to be assessed as being 'like' a child of a refugee they would generally need to be a full time student under 21 years of age. An exemption could apply where a child is orphaned and under the care of a sibling or other family member carer who is a refugee.

Gather the following information to include in the referral comments:

  • Reason for request (NCL started/request for explanation/application for formal review of decision/claim for different payment)
  • Payment type
  • Date of claim
  • Visa subclass held by customer
  • Visa grant date
  • Refugee's CRN
  • Refugee's name
  • Refugee's date of birth
  • Refugee's date of first arrival
  • Relationship to refugee
  • Why relationship is like a partner/dependent child (important to include detailed information)
  • Date customer became a full time student (if applicable)
  • Any additional information to support your request

Go to Step 7.

7

Referral request + Read more ...

Send a referral to CIS.

Process Direct

  • Select > Referral
  • Referral type: International Services (CIS)
  • Referral reason: NARWP & QRE Exemption Service Request
  • Check Payment type is JSP – JobSeeker Payment
  • Select Next
  • Referral details displays
  • Update the following options as required:
    • Vulnerable or suspended customer
    • Call customer required
    • Translation required
    • Appeal request
    • Add Additional information - include the details gathered in Step 4, 5 or 6
  • Select Next
  • Select Finish

Customer First

Create a Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Centrelink International Services > Residence > QRE/NARWP Exemption Request

Note: this Fast Note is used for multiple scenarios. Delete text in the document as appropriate and ensure all required fields are completed. Missing/incomplete information may cause QRE/NARWP exemption to be incorrectly rejected.

Put the claim/request for explanation on hold for 2 weeks. Once QRE/NARWP Exemption has been assessed, CIS will DOC the record and take claim/ request for explanation off hold to allow reallocation. Use the Status icon to change the status from on hold.

Procedure ends here.

End QRE/NARWP Exemption + Read more ...

Relationship to refugee + Read more ...

When assessing whether a person was a family member of a refugee/humanitarian entrant, the relationship must be considered as at the time the refugee/former refugee arrived in Australia and their circumstances at one of the following times:

  • For Family Tax Benefit (FTB) - on the earliest date they may be entitled to receive payment (generally this would be the date customer was both physically present and residing in Australia whilst the holder of a qualifying visa), or

  • For Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) - on the DAP period start date, or

  • For all other payments, at date of claim

Exception: if the refugee or humanitarian migrant has died since arrival in Australia, the customer must have been a family member of that person when the person arrived and when the person died.

If the family member exemption request is for:

  • Partner of a refugee, go to Step 4
  • Dependent child of a refugee, go to Step 5
  • Like a partner or a dependent child to a refugee, go to Step 6

4

Partner of a refugee + Read more ...

Check document QRE/NARWP exemption request, if the customer became a member of a couple after refugee's arrival date, exemption does not apply.

Note: Marital Status (MS) screen often does not reflect partnered status at time of refugee's arrival. Providing the requesting officer can indicate that relationship was present at time of refugee's arrival, this coding does not need to be corrected

Check MS screen to ensure customer assessed as partnered at the date of claim or at appropriate date for FTB, PPL and DAP. If the customer is currently assessed as single under Section 24 provisions, exemption does not apply

If any doubt exists, contact customer to request further information.

Does customer meet the requirements for a QRE/NARWP exemption?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Dependent child of a refugee + Read more ...

When assessing a person as a 'dependent child' for the purposes of eligibility to the exemption, the exemption applies based on the day the situation is assessed. If the child ceases to be dependent after the claim is granted (e.g. a parent dies) the exemption will continue to apply.

Note: a child can be considered independent for YA purposes and at the same time be a dependent child for NARWP/Qualifying Residence Exemption purposes.

To be a dependent child the child must be an Australian resident or living with an Australian resident or SCV holder residing in Australia. There are also age specific requirements that must be met. See Assessment of dependent children, additional income free area and child income under social security law.

The QRE/NARWP Exemption Request document should contain information on the reasons why the requesting officer is satisfied that this meets the requirements of a dependent child. If any doubt exists, contact customer to request further information.

Does customer meet the requirements for a QRE/NARWP exemption?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

6

Treated like a partner or a dependent child to a refugee + Read more ...

There are very limited circumstances that would meet this exemption reason. For example, an exemption could apply where a child is orphaned and under the care of a sibling or other family member carer who is a refugee.

QRE/NARWP Exemption Request document contains information on the reasons why the requesting officer is satisfied this meets the requirements of 'like' a partner or dependent child to a refugee. If any doubt exists, contact customer to request further information.

Note: the delegation for these decisions under Section 7(6) does not need to be referred to the CIS business manager.

Does customer meet the requirements for a QRE/NARWP exemption?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Exemption approved + Read more ...

Customer is entitled to a QRE/NARWP Exemption.

Document the decision using Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Centrelink International Services > Residence > QRE/NARWP Exemption Decision

Note: this Fast Note is used for multiple scenarios. Delete text in the document as appropriate.

Include detailed information about the reason for approval.

Was request required for residence qualification for a started NCL?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 11

8

Exemption rejected + Read more ...

Customer is not entitled to a QRE/NARWP Exemption.

Document the decision using Fast Note select Auto Text, use Centrelink International Services > Residence > QRE/NARWP Exemption Decision

Note: this Fast Note is used for multiple scenarios. Delete text in the document as appropriate.

Include detailed information about the reason for rejection.

Was request required for residence qualification for a started NCL?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 11

9

Coding decision + Read more ...

  • Go to the Residence Savings (RSS) screen
  • Update Family Member/Refugee Code with the appropriate reason:
    • FME = Family Member of a refugee
    • NOT = Not entitled to exemption
  • Code date of event:
    • For FTB - on the earliest date they qualify for the exemption and may be entitled to receive payment (generally this would be the date the customer was both physically present and residing in Australia whilst the holder of a qualifying visa, or
    • For PPL - on the child's date of birth or entry into care, or
    • For DAP - on the DAP period start date, or
    • For YA (student) and Austudy - from the later of date of claim, or date the customer became a family member of the refugee (if up to 13 weeks after the claim date)
    • For all other payments - on the date of claim

Go to Step 11.

10

Ending exemption coding + Read more ...

  • Go to the Residence Savings (RSS) screen
  • Update Family Member/Refugee Code with reason:
    • NOT = Not entitled to exemption
  • Code date of event from a date before the earlier of:
    • Date claim submitted, or
    • Expected start date
  • Do not finalise NCL activity
  • Annotate 'End QRE/NARWP exemption' DOC to advise coding has been completed

Go to Step 11.

11

Notifying decision + Read more ...

Once QRE/NARWP exemption coding/decision has been finalised, the corresponding activity (NCL/ request for explanation/application for formal review of decision) must be taken off hold to allow reallocation. Use the Status icon to change the status from on hold.
