Upgraded ESAt Fast Note/DSP Invite + Read more ...

Assessment Services determine if an ESAt can be upgraded to a JCA as there is sufficient medical evidence to support medical eligibility for DSP, including manifest. If staff receive a submitted ESAt (upgraded to JCA) and/or Potential DSP eligibility Fast Note:

  • review the ESAt to ensure it is complete and accurate. Do not accept the ESAt report

  • check and accept ESAt report

3

Reassess Rejected Claim – for reason FSD/FRC/POI/IME or GCD + Read more ...

If staff receive a Reassess Rejected Claim Fast Note, review previous claim to ensure the claim meets the requirements to reopen.

Do not reopen and reassess a DSP claim where:

  • the request is made more than 13 weeks after rejection notice given
  • not all requested information has been provided within 13 weeks of the rejection notice, or
  • the DSP claim was rejected for any other reason

If any of these occur, see Requested documentation not provided resulting in rejection of new claim for action required.

Where the claim can be reopened and reassessed, action required depends on previous rejection reason. If the claim was previously rejected for reason:

  • FSD/FRC or POI, reindex claim and review previous rejection DOC to determine action required to progress claim. Go to Table 3
  • IME, reindex claim and refer to MAT via Medical Assessment Team Referral. Use the Referral reason ‘New evidence’. Go to Table 3
  • GCD, refer to Disability Medical Assessment (DMA) appointment outcome to reopen and progress claim

6

Successful contact attempt + Read more ...

If the contact attempt is successful, tell the customer or nominee:

  • Service Australia has only received:
    • medical details section of the DSP claim form, or
    • information which indicates you may wish to make a claim for DSP
  • a complete DSP claim has not been received
  • no further action can be taken unless a complete DSP claim (pages 3 to 25) is received
  • advise the options for claiming DSP are:
    • applying for DSP online
    • Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) if the customer is unable or unsuitable to complete an online claim
    • First Contact Concessions, Carers and Seniors (FCCCS) workflow to issue the SA466 paper claim
  • annotate the DSP Claim Progress DOC with, ‘Customer has lodged MEDSA466 only/ insufficient information. Customer has been contacted and advised to complete and submit/lodge a DSP claim.’
  • update claim status to ‘Not Required’ as the MEDSA466 by itself, is not considered to be a valid or complete claim
  • consider recording an Intent to claim for vulnerable customers, if applicable
  • procedure ends here

Unsuccessful second contact attempt + Read more ...

Duplicate claim lodged + Read more ...

Claims for DSP can be submitted at any time. If a customer lodges a DSP claim within the qualification period (for example, 13 weeks) of a previously submitted DSP claim, Service Officers must determine how each claim should be progressed.

Attempt to contact the customer/nominee to establish their reason or intent in lodging a second claim.

  • ask for the claim to be reinstated
  • appeal the decision

DSP claim previously streamed

MAT assessment

  • Yes, go to Step 3

JCA reports conducted for SWS that result in an invite to claim DSP cannot be used as a ‘current and valid’ assessment for the purpose of a DSP claim. If MAT determine a JCA for SWS is current and valid, return the JCA to MAT with a request to change the outcome to JCA Referral required. See Step 9 in Table 4.

For help to determine if an ESAt/JCA can be accepted if a MAT recommends it is ‘current and valid’, refer to SSO via the Direct SSO Referral.

FTB for the child is cancelled automatically when a:

  • DSP online claim is submitted, or
  • SOA shell is created in Process Direct for DSP claim processing for a paper claim

3

DSP claim start date + Read more ...

When streaming a DSP claim, review the claim and customer circumstances to:

  • establish the potential DSP start date, and
  • update the PNA screen

If staff have used the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) to start or submit the DSP claim:

  • compare the Receipt Date on the DSP claim activity with the:
    • creation date on the DSP ACC Claim Progress DOC, and
    • Receipt Date of the MEDDSPOL in Documents tab
  • if the claim displays the incorrect submission date:
    • go to the More Options menu
    • choose the correct date
    • use Update DOR (Date of Receipt)

A system issue exists about the Date of submission. For more information, see Network News Update (NNU), Centrelink online and assisted customer claim (10999).

MEDSA466 lodged without a DSP claim is not considered a claim. The correct date of receipt (DOR) is the date a complete claim is lodged.

Review PRECLM screen to:

  • decide if there are any Pre-Claim Circumstances and Contact dates recorded, and
  • consider backdating if provisions apply for:
    • intent to claim and vulnerable customers
    • early claims. For example, when a customer lodges a claim up to 13 weeks before qualifying for payment

To ensure DSP claim is assessed from the correct start date:

  • update the PNA to correctly reflect the date of claim assessment
  • check if the customer qualifies from this date. If the PNA is backdated or changed because of an ACC error, assess all non-medical eligibility to ensure it is coded from the PNA date and consider medical eligibility from the PNA date

If the DSP Claim date on the SA479/MAT Recommendation does not match the PNA date, see the Scenarios table on the Resources page to determine if referral to MAT for reassessment is required.

4

Review MAT recommendation (SA479) + Read more ...

Use the Documents icon to review the MAT Recommendation (SA479) and medical documents supplied. Ensure all medical evidence has been considered and supports the MAT outcome. If there are any concerns with the MAT recommendation, see Step 9 in Table 4.

If the MAT recommendation indicates the customer:

  • is manifestly medically ineligible or there is a current and valid assessment that indicates the customer is not medically eligible, go to Step 5
  • is ineligible for DSP due to age, MAT will:
    • cancel the MAT Referral
    • annotate the DSP Claim Progress DOC to advise MAT assessment (SA479) has not been completed as customer’s age precludes DSP eligibility
    • go to Step 9 to review non-medical
  • is manifestly medically eligible, has a current and valid assessment that supports eligibility or a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) referral is required and the claim was submitted:
    • online or via Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), go to Step 6
    • via paper claim and scanned to the record, go to Step 7
  • has insufficient medical evidence to assess medical eligibility, see Step 1 in Table 4

Manifestly medically ineligible or reject on basis of current and valid assessment + Read more ...

Review claim details + Read more ...

The Transaction Summary (TS) screen is used to determine claim action. Review the claim - including document requests, referrals or whether the claim can be finalised. See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions.

Check the claim for the following:

  • ensure the claim is indexed on the correct record. Check the customer details in the claim against the new indexed activity to prevent multiple records and intertwined record creation
  • ensure the claim and related documents are correctly scanned and classified:
    • DSP paper claims, SA466, SA494, SA439 must be classified correctly
    • medical evidence must be separated from non-medical information, and include the eMIFE indicator. See Scanning Centrelink medical/sensitive documents using an MFD

9

Querying a MAT recommendation + Read more ...

If a Service Officer has a query or concern with the MAT recommendation about clear error(s) or omission(s) only, refer the query to MAT. Examples include:

  • not all fields in the SA479 completed
  • SA479 not uploaded (when required)
  • incorrect referral reason. For instance, SA479 indicates recommendation is for a new claim when in fact it is for an appeal
  • incorrect date of claim, or
  • no MAT Recommendation DOC recorded, or
  • MAT recommend manifest but have not assessment where CITW occurred (where applicable)

If the MAT issue can be escalated by the Service Officer:

  • run the Streaming and Progress DOC script. Select 'Policy' as the on hold reason
  • update the DSP new claim Progress DOC to advise the details of issue with the MAT Recommendation
  • ensure DSP claim and Progress DOC are on hold for 14 days
  • record a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Disabilities > Claims > Template - DSP MAT Recommendation Query > Report Incomplete
  • add details of query. For example, ‘Assessment to determine if CITW occurred in Australia is required.’
  • wait for MAT to complete the review. Once completed, go to Step 10

Any other concerns, for example issues related to sensitive information that are not to be recorded on a DOC, must be escalated to a Service Support Officer (SSO):

  • run the Streaming and Progress DOC script. Select 'Policy' as the on hold reason
  • update the DSP new claim Progress DOC to advise the details of issue with the MAT Recommendation
  • ensure DSP claim and Progress DOC are on hold for 14 days
  • refer the case to the SSO via the Direct Referral to SSO web form
  • wait for the query to be reviewed. Once reviewed, go to Step 9

10

Finalise streaming the DSP new claim + Read more ...

Service Officers are to review MAT and reviewed query.

If no further MAT referral is required:

  • update the DSP new claim Progress DOC with receipt and outcome of the query
  • take the NCL activity off hold
  • finalise the SSO enquiry in the escalation inbox

If further MAT referral is required:

  • create an open work item. Record a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Disabilities > Claims > Template - DSP MAT Recommendation Query
  • edit the Fast Note auto text as appropriate. High level information about the reason for escalation must be included in the DOC. For example, manifest eligibility reason selected in the report is inconsistent with the contents of the report
  • update the DSP new claim Progress DOC advising a new referral to MAT has been actioned and processing staff are to refer to the most recent UNSPOC/SA479 or MAT Query referral DOC for outcome
  • ensure the claim and progress DOC remain on hold
  • finalise the SSO enquiry in the escalation inbox

Note: do not include sensitive information in the DOC, such as details of medical conditions and manifest codes. Instead, email this information to the Medical Assessment Team and the processing Service Officer. The email address for MAT is listed on the Assessment Services contact details. Ensure the escalation reason is clear for someone who may not have reviewed the report.

If the SSO sends an email to the Medical Assessment Team, they:

  • are not to send the DSP MAT Recommendation Query Fast Note as this duplicates the query
  • are to continue to manage the case and not finalise the enquiry in the escalation inbox. Once a response from MAT is received, the SSO enquiry can be finalised

MAT will:

  • review the escalation request, this may include revising the original recommendation and completing a new report
  • upload the new report via Document Tools or annotate the MAT referral request DOC if no new assessment completed
  • reply to the SSO email to advise of the outcome of the query, and
  • take the NCL activity off hold
