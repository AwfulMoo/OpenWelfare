Cost effectiveness when recovering debts

Item

Description

1

Debt amount: $0.00 - $49.99 + Read more ...

Waive the debt if it is a new debt that is, or is likely to be, less than $50.00.

Negotiate the debt repayment, if the debt has already been raised and part-paid.

Note: it is not cost effective to garnishee a customer when their CRN balance is less than $200.00 unless it is a tax garnishee.

Permanently write off Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013 (PGPA), debts if there is no recovery arrangement. See Permanent write off of Centrelink debts.

2

Debt amount: $50.00 - $1,500.00 + Read more ...

Negotiate an arrangement where the customer phones the Debt Programme.

Recover the debt by:

  • withholdings if the customer is receiving a payment, or
  • cash arrangement if the customer is not receiving a payment
  • wage garnishee only after customer contact / normal debt recovery processes have been exhausted where debt balance is $200.01 or more. See Garnishees of continuing income

It is not cost effective to:

  • send an 'Ask FI' for debt information (Q608) letter, or Electronic Data Exchange (EDE) to check financial records when the debt balance is less than $1,500.00
  • issue a garnishee when the debt balance is less than $200.00 unless it is a tax garnishee

The minimum balances which must be left when issuing a garnishee on a Financial Institution (FI) are:

  • $750 for singles and members of a couple
  • $1,200 for members of a family, that is more than 2 people

3

Debt amount: $1,500.01 - $10,000.00 + Read more ...

Complete the following in sequential order. Exhaust all avenues of contact prior to implementing a recovery decision.

Debt staff:

Make Reasonable effort to contact the customer

Note: advise the customer of any data matches before taking administrative action or checking the information with a third party. See  Single Touch Payroll debt recovery data match or Tax File Number Declaration Form (TDF) Debtor matching reviews.

Action by debt staff responsible for garnishee only:

  • Look for alternate contact details
  • Where no other recovery avenue is available consider referring or re-referring the debt to an External Collection Agent (ECA)
  • Check employment detail and consider sending a DRC - ask employer for employment details (Q609) letter. See Garnishee of continuing income to recover debts
  • Check bank account details and consider sending a DRC - 'Ask FI for debt information' (Q608) letter to the customer's Financial Institution (FI) or use the Electronic Data Exchange with Financial Institutions tool remembering that:
    • It is not cost effective to complete FI checks for debts under $2,500.00 (unless information gathered and/or the customer's record indicates there may be funds available in the account for possible garnishee and no other recovery methods are available)
    • It is not cost effective to complete an FI check for a joint FI account
    • APS5 approval must be obtained prior to implementing a bank garnishee
    • See Financial Institution (FI) garnishees to recover debts

4

Debt amount: $10,000.01 and above + Read more ...

Complete all steps for $1,500.01 - $10,000.00 above, and consider legal proceedings, see Civil procedures to recover Centrelink debts.

Note: customers who meet the following criteria can be referred to the Specialised Recovery Team when:

  • the debt balance is more than $10,000, and
  • there is a capacity to either:
    • repay in full, or
    • increase the recovery rate due to significant savings or assets

Cases can be referred to the Specialised Recovery Team for consideration. Referrals can be made via email to the Specialised Recovery Team and must include a summary of information found on record outlining its suitability for case management.

Implement recovery decision

If all avenues to contact the customer have been exhausted without success, decide which recovery action should be implemented. This could be:

  • writing off the debt, or
  • placing a garnishee

See Resources page for contact to Intensive Collections
