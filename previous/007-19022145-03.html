For specialist Child Care Subsidy (CCS) reconciliation Service Officers only

This page contains information on the action required to complete Manual Intervention (MIV) checking of paused CCS reconciliation results.

On this page:

CCS reconciliation requires manual intervention check

Deceased customer/child

Step

Action

1

CCS reconciliation Work Item + Read more ...

An active work item will only be present if the CCS Reconciliation result has been referred for manual intervention. An active work item can be identified by hovering over the Work Item ID hyperlink in the ‘Pending’ reconciliation tile of the Reconciliation History tab within the CCS Reconciliation Dashboard in Process Direct.

CCS MIV Work items will be allocated automatically to specialist staff.

Note: 'CCS Disaster Affected', 'ICT intervention' and some CCS Bereavement Work items will not be allocated to staff for action.

When CCS MIV staff select Ready they may be presented with a MIV activity in the Work Item Search page depending on the skill tags they hold, and the priority assigned to the piece of work.

The description in the Work Item Type field will indicate the reason why the result has been paused.

Staff can select anywhere on the line to claim/accept the work item but do not select Assess yet.

Note: where more than one pending reason applies, it is displayed according to a hierarchy.

Work items are (in order of hierarchy):

  • CCS Reco - Deceased CUS/CHI, see Table 2
  • CCS Reco - No Valid Address, including where previous outbound contact attempt was unsuccessful and work item is now off Hold, go to Step 2
  • CCS Reco - Customer disaster affected, for more information, see Customer Enquires about CCS reconciliation outcome. Procedure ends here
  • CCS Reco - Debts > $10,000, go to Step 6
  • CCS Reco - Arrears > $10,000, go to Step 5

2

CCS No Valid Address for the Customer + Read more ...

This work item will present where the customer’s address is not recorded on the system. An address update with customer confirmation is required before the work item can be actioned.

Use Desktop Messaging to send a pre-call notification SMS to the customer before making two genuine attempts to contact the customer to confirm current address details.

Check notes and scanned documents to see if the customer has provided address details recently since the address type ended date on the Address History (ADH) screen.

Can current address be confirmed by successful outbound contact or details stored on record?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No:
    • Add a note to the record detailing the first unsuccessful contact attempts
    • Create a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on the record requesting staff update the customer’s address if they contact
    • Put the activity on hold for 14 days and resubmit to the pool

If actioning reallocated No Valid Address work item after 14 days, make two additional genuine attempts to contact the customer.

Were additional outbound attempts successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No:
    • Annotate the existing unsuccessful contact note to advise additional outbound contact attempts were unsuccessful
    • Change work item status to Hold and select Referral to policy/helpdesk as the On Hold Reason
    • Enter an On Hold Expiry Date 28 days in the future
    • Refer the case to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk for escalation via the My Level 2 Policy Helpdesk Requests Summary page. Add the following to the Description field:
      'Actioning a ‘No Valid Address’ CCS Reconciliation work item but unable to confirm address details for customer. I confirm that two unsuccessful outbound contact attempts were made at the time of actioning the work item and then another two unsuccessful attempts 14 days after the initial attempt. Policy clarification/action required.'
    • Procedure ends here

3

Address confirmed, updates to be made + Read more ...

Make necessary address updates or corrections in Customer First.

Finalise Activity in Customer First noting outcomes or payment impacts on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Run the Daily Driver by keying DDR in the Next field and selecting Enter

Clear Customer and go to Step 4.

4

Check provisional reconciliation outcome for other criteria + Read more ...

Does the provisional reconciliation outcome meet any other criteria for CCS MIV checking, such as arrears/debts > $10,000?

  • Yes, for:
    • Arrears > $10,000 go to Step 5
    • Debt > $10,000 go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 8 to assess and finalise work item

5

CCS arrears greater than threshold (Arrears > $10,000) + Read more ...

Investigate the CCS Reconciliation Dashboard for an explanation of the large arrears.

The most common reasons for large arrears are:

  • Overestimation of income - this can be checked via the Income History tab. Cross check the customer’s Income Estimates against their Actual Income and confirm overestimation.
    Scrolling through the ‘Provider Payments’ display will also indicate the week in the CCS year when the assessment changed. Select the relevant hyperlinked value, then Display Weekly Breakdown to display this new table below, Select the hyperlinked ‘difference’ figure in to display the SSP which will display the difference between the initial CCS assessment, and reconciliation. A change to CCS Subsidy from a lower percentage to a higher percentage would indicate income related arrears
  • Retrospective updates to the Activity Test result - Select the Activity History tab and select the Show Logically Deleted button to show historical data. A more detailed history of Activity test updates can be found by selecting the Show History button on the Activity Test Details table within the ‘Customer Summary’ PD home page tile. Date of Receipt of any ATD updates should also be checked. The arrears could also be explained by the Customer Entitlement row in Display Weekly Breakdown, which will highlight value changes in red between previous assessments and reconciliation
  • Annual Cap no longer applicable following Reconciliation - If a customer’s income estimate for the relevant year was above the lower income threshold plus $120,000, an Annual Cap of $10,190 per child would have been applied to their CCS entitlement during the year. If the customer used in excess of this amount per child then a retrospective assessment will take place at reconciliation if the Annual Cap was applied incorrectly based on actual income. Check Session reports to see if the annual cap was exhausted by a certain date during the year and then check the Annual Cap tab in the CCS dashboard to see whether the cap ultimately applied. Note: the annual cap applied to the 2018-19, 2019-20 and 2020-21 CCS years only
  • Retrospective ACCS grant - Customers could be entitled to ACCS from a prior date that fell within the relevant CCS year. If an ACCS application is not finalised until after reconciliation has occurred, previously reconciled CCS periods could undergo reassessment. Check the Rate row under Display Weekly Breakdown to confirm an ACCS daily rate
  • Other retrospective changes to Entitlement e.g. Marital Status

The purpose of the MIV check is not to conduct a detailed investigation or reconciliation unpack but to simply confirm correctness of the automatic CCS outcome.

A customer’s reconciliation result could be affected by one or a combination of the reasons listed.

Is the MIV officer satisfied that the large arrears are valid and can be explained by at least one of the reasons above?

  • Yes, no further investigation is necessary. The Work Item can be finalised. Go to Step 8
  • No. Record correction may be required. Go to Step 8

6

CCS debts greater than threshold (Debt > $10,000) + Read more ...

  • Underestimation of income - this can be checked via the Income History tab. Cross check the customer’s Income Estimates against their Actual Income and confirm underestimation. Scrolling through the Provider Payments display will also indicate relevant weeks in the CCS year in which the assessment changed. Display Weekly Breakdown will show a difference between the initial CCS assessment and reconciliation. A change to CCS Subsidy from a higher percentage to a lower percentage would indicate income related overpayment
  • Retrospective updates to the Activity Test result - Select the Activity History tab and select the Show Logically Deleted button to show historical data. A more detailed history of Activity test updates can be found by selecting the Show History button on the Activity Test Details (ATD) table in ‘Customer Summary’ PD (this will include retrospective updates made to ATD after the end of the relevant year). Date of Receipt of any ATD updates should also be checked (this includes checking the DOR used in the ‘header box’ table that appears after editing a line). The overpayment could also be explained by selecting the difference field in the Customer Entitlement row in Display Weekly Breakdown. This will display the SSP which will highlight value changes in red between previous assessments and reconciliation
  • Annual Cap not applied during the year but applicable following Reconciliation - If a customer’s income estimate for the relevant year was below the lower income threshold plus $120,000 there would be no limit on the amount of CCS that could be paid per child. If at reconciliation the customer’s ATI was above this threshold, an Annual Cap of $10,190 per child would be applied and any CCS paid in excess of this per child cap would be raised as an overpayment. Check Entitlement in PD to confirm the annual cap was not reached in any of the session reports, particularly towards the end of the year. Cross reference this against Display Weekly Breakdown under Usage to see if the annual cap used exceeded the cap amount for the relevant year. Note: the annual cap applied to the 2018-19, 2019-20 and 2020-21 CCS years only
  • Other retrospective changes to Entitlement - e.g. loss of care of a CCS child, marital status

The purpose of the MIV check is not to conduct a detailed investigation or reconciliation unpack but to simply confirm correctness of the automatic CCS outcome.

A customer’s reconciliation result could be affected by one or a combination of the reasons listed.

Is the MIV officer satisfied that the large overpayment is correct and can be explained by at least one of the reasons above?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No. Record correction may be required. Go to Step 8

7

CCS reconciliation result correct - Work Item to be finalised + Read more ...

Select Work Item, make any necessary updates as required, select Assess, then Finish and Finalise the activity.

Add a note explaining and the basis for the decision that the CCS result is correct.

Open the Debt Application and view the date the debt was raised.

Is the work item being finalised a Debt > $10,000 and the debt was generated between 3 April 2020 and 9 March 2021?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, procedure ends here

8

CCS reconciliation result incorrect - updates required to the Process Direct record + Read more ...

Where an automatic CCS result is found to be incorrect through MIV checking it may be necessary for MIV staff to make updates to the customer’s record.

The action taken by staff will depend on what factors were identified in the investigation.

Where customer circumstances are not recorded correctly MIV staff may update the relevant table within the Work Item activity via existing processes. See relevant Operational Blueprint files for more information.

In some cases, customer circumstances will be correctly recorded but an incorrect result will still display due to system problems. Where system issues are identified, staff should follow standard escalation protocols or instructions in NNU.

With updates completed, Finalise/Finish activity, observing any changes to assessment outcome that may have resulted from your corrective action.

Finish and leave a note.

Open the Debt Application and view the date the debt was raised.

Once corrections are completed, is the new outcome of the work item being finalised a Debt > $10,000 and the original debt was generated between 3 April 2020 and 9 March 2021?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, procedure ends here

9

Outbound contact attempts for Debts > $10,000 generated between 3 April 2020 and 9 March 2021 + Read more ...

Where a Debt > $10,000 was generated between 3 April 2020 and 9 March 2021 (during the COVID-19 debt pause), contact the customer and provide an explanation of the debt.

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer.

If contact is:

  • not successful:
    • record details of the contact attempts by annotating the Note containing the explanation of debt outcome
    • procedure ends here
  • successful, go to Step 10

10

Successful outbound contact for Debts > $10,000 generated between 3 April 2020 and 9 March 2021 + Read more ...

When discussing the debt outcome, use the below scripting to assist in guiding the conversation:

'I’m calling today to discuss your Child Care Subsidy end of year balancing. Unfortunately you were paid more Child Care Subsidy than you should have been for the 20xx/xx year.

I need to let you know the overpayment amount is $xxxxx. I appreciate that this is a large amount.

I know it’s not great news, but we are here to help. I want to take a few minutes to explain how this happened.'

  • Explain reason for overpayment and what the customer can do to reduce the chance of it happening again
  • Advise the customer of their review and appeal rights
  • If the customer requests a review of decision, let them know the process that will follow

'We’ll send you a letter shortly to provide you more information about your overpayment and when it is due. If you’re not able to pay the amount by the due date, we can work with you to pay it off over time with a repayment amount you can afford.'

Once you’ve had a chance to work out how much you can repay each fortnight, you can go to the Money You Owe page in your Centrelink Online account and update it there.'

If the customer is distressed

'I appreciate that hearing about these overpayments can be upsetting or distressing. We have support staff available that you can chat to. Would you like me to organise for a Social Worker to contact you?'

If yes, refer to Social work service referral.

Annotate the Note containing the explanation of debt outcome with the details of the discussion with the customer.

Procedure ends here.

Step

Action

1

CCS deceased customer or deceased only CCS child + Read more ...

This MIV check is to:

  • confirm that the passing of a CCS customer or customer’s only CCS child is recorded correctly in Customer First
  • confirm the correctness of the CCS reconciliation outcome
  • where the outcome is a debt, apply waiver if the customer is eligible

Once inside the work item in Process Direct, identify if the work item is for a deceased customer or deceased child:

  • Customer: the Benefit Status line will display '(deceased)'
  • Child: 'Deceased' and 'Age' will appear in red underneath the CRN and Date of Birth for the relevant child under
  • Check DL/Notes for information on who the deceased individual is. Where nothing is recorded for customer/partner

Where death has been recorded incorrectly, such as for the wrong individual or with the wrong date details, See Undoing a death action.

If the MIV Officer is satisfied that relevant death details have been recorded appropriately, no correction is necessary.

Note: if any CCS child (excluding the last CCS child) becomes deceased within 28 days of reconciliation, reconciliation will complete automatically once the 28 day bereavement period has ended and the appropriate letters will be sent. These work items will not be sent to MIV for checking.

What does the Work Item relate to?

  • Deceased customer, go to Step 2
  • Deceased last remaining CCS Child, go to Step 3
  • If both customer and child are deceased follow the Deceased customer process, go to Step 2
  • If actioning an ICT response following reconciliation outcome correction, go to Step 4
  • If response received from Enterprise Outbound advising child debt letter has been inhibited, go to Step 10

2

Date of Customer’s death + Read more ...

Is the date of death within 4 weeks?

  • Yes, record cannot be finalised until after 4 weeks from the date of death
    • Add a note that the MIV work item cannot be processed until <record date 4 weeks from date of death>
    • Place the work item on Hold until 4 weeks from the date of death and unassign the work item
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

Date of last CCS child’s death + Read more ...

Is the date of death of the child within 28 days?

  • Yes, record cannot be finalised until after 28 days from the date of death.
    • Add a note that the MIV work item cannot be processed until <record date 28 days from date of death>
    • Place the work item on Hold until 28 days from the date of death and unassign the work item
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check reconciliation/ re-reconciliation outcome correctness + Read more ...

In some cases, the reconciliation outcome will be affected by known issues. Follow procedures according to NNU or CCS Common Issues.

Is the reconciliation/re-reconciliation outcome correct?

  • Yes, and the outcome is
    • $0.00 or arrears, go to Step 5
    • a debt, go to Step 6
  • No:
    • where an automatic CCS result is found to be incorrect through MIV checking, it may be necessary to escalate the record to ICT for correction
    • go to Step 12

5

CCS reconciliation/re-reconciliation result is $0.00 or arrears + Read more ...

Select Assess, then Finish and Finalise the activity.

For a:

  • deceased child, this will generate an auto letter. Procedure ends here
  • deceased customer, a manual letter will be required. Go to Step 8

6

CCS reconciliation/re-reconciliation result is a debt + Read more ...

All debts under $50 will be auto waived by the system. Go to Step 7 for manual letter creation.

For:

  • customer related debts $200 or greater - For example: customer related debts would be caused by ATD changes, underestimation of income, or late notification of changes of circumstance, go to Step 7
  • child related debts - For example: CCS entitlement continued to be paid after the child was deceased, unrelated to customer income etc., go to Step 9
  • customer related debts under $200, go to Step 10

7

Adult debt $200 or greater + Read more ...

Navigate to the Debt List (OPDL) screen in Customer First.

Has a Deceased Estate waiver been applied to any other debts for other payments?

  • Yes, Finish/finalise Work Item. Go to Step 10 to waive debt
  • No, Finish/finalise Work Item to raise debt, Go to Step 8 to create manual letter

8

Manual letter for CCS deceased customer + Read more ...

No automatic CCS reconciliation letters will be issued to deceased CCS customers. A manual letter is required.

Note: another letter is not required where:

  • the Work Item has been created due to a re-reconciliation, and
  • the new result is no different to the original reconciliation result

Action manual letter

Send a CCS Q062 using approved text to the Estate advising of reconciliation outcome.

See Creating a Q999 or Q888 letter for detailed instruction on how to create the manual letters if required. Access the approved letter text and templates on the Operational Blueprint letters and electronic messaging sub-site.

Procedure ends here.

9

CCS Reconciliation Outcome is a debt related to a deceased child + Read more ...

In all child debt cases, the debt will be waived. The Work Item will need to be finished and finalised however before waiver can be applied. This triggers an automatic outcome letter which must be inhibited as it will contain incorrect information.

Consider the time of day this work is being undertaken.

  • If approaching close of business (COB) or towards end of shift, Hold the activity to user and resume the following working day. This is to reduce the risk of ICT not being able to inhibit the letter in time
  • If up until 1:30pm AEST and the decision is made to proceed:
    • Finish and Finalise Deceased Work Item. Do not send the reconciliation outcome letter at this stage as it will contain incorrect information
    • Escalate to ICT immediately through the MySupport – Enterprise Outbound Communications (EOC) issue webform (Note: the MySupport page may need to be loaded before clicking on the link otherwise a 400 Bad Request error presents)
    • Include instructions that CCS reconciliation outcome letter is to be inhibited as a matter of urgency as it contains incorrect information
    • DO NOT take any further action until confirmation is received that letter has been stopped.
    • Once response is received from ICT, go to Step 10

If response is not received by COB or end of shift, call the mobile number displayed on the webform under 'What you need to know' to ensure the request is actioned in time.

10

Debt to be waived + Read more ...

  • Deceased customer with debt <$200, or
  • Deceased customer with debt of any size and Estate waiver already applied to other debts on OPDL, or
  • Child debt outcome letter has been suppressed

Note: the monetary delegation guidelines under the Family Assistance Act state Service Officers can only waive debts up to $5000. Authorisation from a local EL1 is required for debts above this amount.

Open the Debt Application tool in Process Direct by either selecting the Debts icon or navigating to Superkey: DEBTS:

  • Select Launch Debt Management
  • Under the Debt Case ID column select the relevant hyperlink of the debt to be considered for waiver
  • Once on the Debt Details page, select the purple action button at the bottom right of the screen, and select waive debt
  • Select waive reason from the drop down window:
    • Full waiver
    • Outstanding balance
    • Partial waiver
  • Scroll down and enter details in Summary (the text entered here will show up in the DL summary line). Add an explanation of the waiver reason
  • Select the green Submit on the bottom right hand corner

Go to Step 11.

11

Manual Letter for reconciliation outcome + Read more ...

With debt waived, send a letter to the customer/estate with new outcome.

Action manual letter

Send a CCS Q062 using approved text to the Estate advising of reconciliation outcome.

See Creating a Q999 or Q888 letter for detailed instruction on how to create the manual letters if required. Access the approved letter text and templates on the Operational Blueprint letters and electronic messaging sub-site.

Leave a note in the record explaining the outcome and waiver details.

Procedure ends here.

12

CCS Reconciliation outcome needs to be corrected – Refer to ICT + Read more ...

The information ICT will need will vary depending on CCSMIV findings about the incorrect result.

For example, for ATD corrections, outline the period from CCS Monday to Sunday that the ATD result is not correctly applying for, include context, and what the hours should be.

  • Refer the record to ICT via ROXY DA. Include:
    • relevant context and information included in the notes, and advice corrections are required, and
    • the instruction that corrections are to be made within the Work Item but the activity is not to be finalised
  • Once ICT response is received advising the corrections are complete, go to Step 4
