Vacation of home review

Table 1. steps needed to start a vacation of home review. The reviews must be completed by Home Vacation Review (HVR) processing staff only.

Step

Action

1

Vacation of home review + Read more ...

A vacation of home review is set 6 weeks before the expiration of the exemption period.

If a review for a vacation of a principal home has matured, the Manual Follow-up (MFU) can be found on the Activity List (AL) screen.

The Review Reason will present in the activity notes, or for Vacation of Home (VOH) reviews via the code in Reason Home Vacated on the Accommodation (AC) screen.

If the vacation of home review is for one of the following reasons, go to the required vacation of home review process.

  • INH - In Nursing Home, exempt 2 yrs (for high or low level care nursing home or hostel resident), see Vacation of home to enter care situation (INH) reviews
  • CRR - Vacation of principal home to provide care, see Vacation of principal home to provide care
  • SAL - Sale/Purchase of Home Property, see Selling a home
  • PUR - intends to purchase or build another home within 12 months. see Selling a home

Procedure ends here.

For a vacation of home review reason for one of the following, go to Step 2.

  • ONH - Due to ill health (for neither hostel nor nursing home resident) - 2 year review
  • OVE - Absent Overseas - VHP Review (auto review for 12 months)
  • UNH - Home temporarily uninhabitable (auto review for 12 months)
  • OTH - Other (for example, Travel in Australia, domestic violence)

2

Work allocation + Read more ...

Has the vacation of home review work item been allocated to a HVR processing Service Officer?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, if contact is required, or documents have been provided for a vacation of home review in Customer First, create an open Work Item using Fast Note. Select Fast Note > Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets:
    • Action required - Home vacation review
    • Contact required - Home vacation review

If scanned documentation is determined to be for a Vacation of Home Review, close the scan Work Item. Include details of documentation received in the Fast Note.

Note: do not select link to existing activity or annotate the existing DOC, as this will finalise the review activity.

See Office Locator for the HVR Team (under Office Messages) for more details about possible:

  • document requirements, and
  • important contact information

Procedure ends here.

3

Current payment + Read more ...

Is the customer or partner currently in receipt of an income support payment (includes suspended payments)?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, no review required
    • Remove the reason and date home vacated from AC screen
    • Finalise the review
    • Record details in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review
    • Procedure ends here

4

Blind customers + Read more ...

Customers in receipt of DSP (Blind) or Age Pension (Blind) payments are not subject to the income and asset tests unless they are paid Rent Assistance (RA). To confirm status, check Benefit Status on Customer Summary.

Is this customer or partner in receipt of DSB - DSP (Blind) or AGB - Age Pension (Blind)?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Customer is in receipt of DSB or AGB + Read more ...

Check Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen and Accommodation Circumstances Summary (ACS) screens.

A customer does not need to disclose their income and assets if they are in receipt of a Blind pension, except for the following reasons:

  • the customer is in receipt of Rent Assistance (RA), or
  • they have a partner who is receipt of or applying for an income and asset tested payment

Is the customer or partner in receipt of DSB or AGB and is required to disclose their income and assets?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No,
    • On the Accommodation Details (AC) screen:
      Home Ownership - update based on current circumstances, e.g. to a non-homeowner
      Event Date is the end date of the exemption period
      Clear the Reason Home Vacated field
      Clear the Date Home Vacated field
    • Make sure the Blind - Income & Assets Exempt: field is coded 'Y' on the Pensions Disability Information (PDI) screen
    • Finalise the review
    • Record details in a DOC via Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review

Procedure ends here.

6

Returned to principal home + Read more ...

Customers who temporarily vacate their principal home must have the intent to return to the home or be exempt from the requirement.

Using the Reason Home Vacated and information on the customer record, determine if the customer returned to their home, before the end of exemption period. (Address Details, Return to Australia, DOCs)

Has the customer (or partner) returned to living in the principal home within 12 months of the date vacated?

  • Yes, the customer remains a homeowner
    • On Accommodation Details (AC) screen
      Home Ownership field for customer remains a homeowner
      Clear the Reason Home Vacated field
      Clear the Date Home Vacated field
    • Finalise the review
    • Record details in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Life interest in former principal home + Read more ...

Check Accommodation Details (AC) screen for:

  • LIF - Bequeathed Life Interest
  • DEE -Deemed interest code

Does the customer have a life interest or deemed interest in the former home?

  • Yes, make a referral to a Complex Assessment Officer
    • Include in the referral notes the referral is due to a vacation of home review.
    • Finalise the review
    • Record details in a DOC
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Evidence of sale of former principal home + Read more ...

If the customer has temporarily vacated their principal home they may have sold the property during the exemption period. Notification of the sale and the destination of the proceeds should have been provided.

Check for evidence in the customer record that the former home has been sold, including:

  • Accommodation Details (AC) screen - Home Ownership field was a homeowner (for example codes LHO, HOM) at the time vacated home, now a non-homeowner (code ‘NHO’)
  • Savings Summary (SVS) screen, check for significant increases in funds since the home was vacated
  • DOCs (including archived) to confirm notification of the sale and distribution of proceeds
  • Documents provided to confirm the sale of the former home

Is there evidence on record that former home has been sold and either:

  • proceeds of the sale have already been recorded? (see Selling a home)
  • required documents have been provided to confirm the sale and destination of proceeds? (see Selling a home)
  • Yes, see Table 2
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Overseas absence + Read more ...

Was the vacation of home review created due to the customer absent overseas (OVE - Absent Overseas - VHP Review)?

  • Yes, for vacation of home review reason Absent Overseas:
    • If the customer has a partner who remained in Australia and lives in the home, no review is required
      Finalise the review
      Record details in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review
    • If the customer is single, or both members of a couple have remained overseas and there is no evidence of return to Australia:
      Centrelink International Services - go to Step 10
      Home Vacation Review processing - Activity List (AL) transfer to region: WTW
      Re-categorise the Work Item Type: ZALL_INTL_VHP
      Record details in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 11

10

Overseas absence - extended exemption + Read more ...

If the customer temporarily vacates their principal home to travel overseas, their absence will be considered temporary for 12 months.

If the customer remains overseas, Centrelink International Services are to undertake the vacation of home review.

An extended exemption can be applied if all of the following are met. The customer:

  • vacated their principal home temporarily
  • has a continuing intent to return to Australia
  • is making reasonable efforts to return to Australia
  • is unable to return from overseas due to circumstances beyond their control, e.g. travel restrictions resulting from a pandemic or a major disaster

The criteria can be confirmed using DOCs detailing customer contacts, scanned documents and/or a verbal declaration of their circumstance. If a significant extension is being sought or an extra extension is being considered, evidence may be required.

Does the customer meet all criteria for an extended exemption for an overseas absence?

  • Yes, the customer continues to be a homeowner with homeownership status of 'LHO' (lives elsewhere)
    • Add a keyword OVEEXT to the review
    • Hold the review to the date the customer expects to be able to return to Australia
    • Record details in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review, including:
      Customer has been granted an extended exemption for an overseas absence as they are unable to return from overseas due to circumstances beyond their control
      The customer’s intention remains to return to Australia
      The customer is making reasonable efforts to return to Australia
      Evidence used to support an extension [Insert evidence]
      The extended exemption period expires DOV: [insert DOV]
    • Notify customer of outcome and that the exemption period only applies while they are unable to return to Australia for reasons beyond their control. DOC conversation or issue written notification
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, see Table 2

11

Home is lost or damaged + Read more ...

Was the vacation of home review reason for UNH - Home temporarily uninhabitable?

  • Yes, make 2 genuine attempts to contact to:
    • Explain the vacation of home review requirements
    • Determine if customer has returned home
    • Confirm ownership of the former principal home
    • Confirm if any insurance payments have been received for the loss or damage (notification is required, however any money received for compensation due to loss or damages is both deeming and asset exempt for the exemption period.)
    • Confirm current mailing addresses for customer (update if required)
    • Determine if an extended exemption is required for reason UNH - Home temporarily uninhabitable. Go to Step 12
  • No, go to Step 13

12

Extended exemption - home is lost or damaged + Read more ...

When the customer's principal home is lost or damaged, the exemption for the temporary absence is for an initial 12 months. An extended exemption for up to 24 months is available if the all the following are met:

  • The loss or damage was not wilfully caused by the customer
  • The customer had a continuing intention to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate a new principal home and can provide documentary evidence to support they have:
    • made reasonable attempts to obtain a new principal home. This could be to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate their principal home
    • been making these attempts within 6 months of the loss or damage of the principal home, and
    • had delays beyond their control in obtaining a new principal home

If one of the above criteria is not met, the customer cannot gain an extended exemption for up to 24 months.

Does the customer meet all the criteria for an extended exemption for a home that is lost or damaged?

  • Yes, update the details on the Accommodation (AC) screen:
    • Event Date: The date the person left their principal residence
    • Home Ownership: the customer continues to be a homeowner, with homeownership status of 'LHO' (lives elsewhere)
    • Reason Home Vacated: Select UNH - Home temporarily uninhabitable
    • Date Home Vacated: Use the expiration date of the initial 12 month review
    • Finalise the review
    • Record details via Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review, including:
      Customer has been granted an extended exemption for their former principal home as the home was lost or damaged
      The customer’s intention remains to purchase or build, repair or sell their principal home
      Verification has been provided, to confirm the customer has made reasonable attempts to obtain a new principal home build, rebuild, repair, sell or purchase a new principal home
      Verification has been provided, to confirm the customer has made those attempts within a reasonable period after selling the principal home, that is, within 6 months, and
      Verification has been provided, to confirm the customer experienced delays beyond their control in obtaining a new principal home. [insert reason]
      The extended exemption period expires DOV: [insert DOV]
      The exemption period must not exceed the date of sale of former home, plus 2 years DOV: [insert DOV]
    • Notify customer of outcome, DOC conversation or issue written notification
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, see Table 2

13

Domestic violence + Read more ...

Care needs to be taken when a vacation of home review is required for vulnerable customers.

Was the vacation of home review reason for OTH - Other, when for domestic violence?

  • Yes, when vacation of home review is required for a vulnerable customer, make 2 genuine attempts to contact to:
    • explain the home vacation review requirements
    • determine if customer has returned home
    • confirm ownership of the former principal home
    • confirm current mailing addresses for customer (update if required)
    • consider if customer may benefit from assets hardship provisions
  • No, see Table 2

Confirm and update customer circumstances

Table 2. steps needed to confirm and update customer circumstances for a vacation of home review. Processing of reviews must be completed by Home Vacation Review (HVR) processing staff.

Step

Action

1

Request for information + Read more ...

If the customer has not returned to their former principal home:

  • Make a request for information using a QSS32 - Former home (See Resources) and a Mod R - Real Estate details (Mod R)
  • CIS send a XOB140 and include in the free text ‘Our records indicate you are overseas and your former home in Australia has been given an exemption from the assets test. If you are unable to return to Australia due to circumstances beyond your control, please contact Services Australia’
  • If the customer has a correspondence nominee, copies of letters and notices sent to the customer must also be sent to the nominee
  • Place the review on hold for 14 days + delivery See Reasonable timeframe
  • Record details in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review
  • Procedure ends here, pending customer response or due date

Has a response to the request for information been returned, with the required information?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Follow up contact + Read more ...

If the requested documents have not been provided, make 2 genuine attempts to contact to:

  • explain the vacation of home review requirements
  • confirm ownership of the former principal home
  • confirm current mailing addresses for customer and nominee (update if required)
  • determine if an extension is required to provide requested documents

Has the end date of the exemption period been reached?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, hold the review until the end of the exemption period. Record details in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review

3

Details for former home + Read more ...

Does the customer still own the former principal home?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Former home not sold + Read more ...

If the former home has not been sold by the end of the exemption period, the:

  • Customer’s homeowner status should be reassessed based on their current circumstances
  • The former home is no longer the principal home and is an assessable asset

On the Accommodation Details (AC) screen update:

  • Home Ownership field as required, e.g. non-homeowner as renting
  • Event Date: is the end date of the exemption period
  • Clear the Reason Home Vacated field
  • Clear the Date Home Vacated field
  • Update partner's AC screen if relevant

If the former principal home was rented, the income on the Other Income (OIN) screen must be end dated. Update OIN:

  • Event Date: is the end date of the exemption period
  • Amount $.cc: zero

Any payments received from an immediate family member for living in the principal home are considered board or lodgings (not rental income) and are not treated as income for social security purposes. Once the temporary absence provisions stop to apply to the customer, then the property becomes a normal rental property. The board and lodging rules do not apply and the income from the immediate family member is assessable as rental income.

As the former home is no longer exempt:

  • Record the former home on Real Estate (RE) screen as an assessable asset, following the process for Assessing and coding the Real estate details Mod R
  • If receiving rental income for their former principal home, see Assessing income from real estate and timeshare
  • Event Date on the Real Estate (RE) screen is the same event date recorded on the AC screen, the end date of the exemption period
  • Finalise the review
  • Record details in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review

Procedure ends here.

5

Former home sold + Read more ...

Was the home sold during a temporary vacation of home exemption period for reason UNH, OTH or OVE and the customer intends to purchase or build another home within 12 months?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, from the date of the sale of the former principal home:
    • the customer is treated as a non-homeowner
    • the proceeds of sale are assessed as an asset and subject to deeming

On the Accommodation details (AC) screen:

  • Update the Home Ownership field with the required homeowner code, see Selling a home
  • Event Date is date of sale of the home
  • Clear the Reason Home Vacated field
  • Clear the Date Home Vacated field
  • Update partner's AC screen if relevant

If the former principal home was rented, the income on the Other Income (OIN) screen must be end dated. Update OIN:

  • Event Date: date of sale or date rent ceased if ended at an earlier date
  • Amount $.cc: zero
  • Finalise the review
  • Record details in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review
  • See Selling a home

Procedure ends here.

6

Former home sold - intends to purchase or build within 12 months + Read more ...

If the home is sold during a temporary vacation of home exemption period (UNH, OTH, OVE), and the customer has the intention to purchase or build another home a new 12 month exemption may apply from the date of the sale of the former principal home. See Selling a home.

From the date of the sale of the former principal home:

  • The customer is remains a homeowner
  • The proceeds of sale remain an exempt asset, but subject to deeming

On the Accommodation details (AC) screen:

  • Update the Home Ownership field with the required homeowner code, See Selling a home
  • Event Date is date of sale of the home
  • Reason Home Vacated: field update to SAL or PUR
  • Date Home Vacated: field update to date of the sale
  • Update partner's AC screen if relevant

If the former principal home was rented, the income on the Other Income (OIN) screen must be end dated. Update OIN:

  • Event Date: date of sale
  • Amount $.cc: zero
  • Finalise the review
  • Record details in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Home Vacation Review
  • See Selling a home

Procedure ends here.

7

Suspend income support payment + Read more ...

If the customer been issued with an appropriate request for information/action and has failed to reply within a reasonable timeframe, the payment may be suspended ‘FRC’. Refer to the payment specific process for suspension instructions, Suspension of payments (CLK)

DOC the suspension of payment and include the text:

  • Refer to the vacation of home review DOC dated (xx/xx/xx) for further information relating to this matter
  • If documents are received, scan to file, do not restore payments
  • Escalate to HVR processing

Close DOC - Home Vacation Review.

Finalise the vacation of home review, adding in the notes ‘payment suspended FRC’

If the customer has passed away when the payment is suspended, HVR processing staff are to restore payment from the date payment was suspended. Service Officer(s) in service centre or Smart Centres can then proceed with DEA action.
