This page contains examples of applying a deemed date for a claim.

Applying a deemed date of claim (DDOC) where the claim contact date is before 1 July 2018

This table describes examples of applying a DDOC.

Item

Description

1

Sandy + Read more ...

Sandy's partner Virginia attends the service centre on 2 January 2014, Sandy's 65th birthday, to find out what assistance they may be entitled to. Sandy retired last year but refused to apply for government assistance until they were entitled to Age Pension. Virginia is given an Age Pension claim and the contact is recorded. Virginia is advised to return the completed claim within 14 days. Virginia mails back the claim form on Sandy's behalf, and it arrives on 10 January 2014. The claim is assessed and determined on 11 January 2014. Virginia is (50 years old) still working about 30 hours per week and is therefore not claiming any payments.

Date of first contact = 2 January 2014

Date claim lodged = 10 January 2014

Date first qualified = 2 January 2014

DDOC = 2 January 2014

2

Franklin + Read more ...

Franklin contacts by phone on 2 August 2014 and wishes to claim Carer Payment. The contact is recorded and the claim form is sent out with an acknowledgement letter of the contact. Franklin attends the counter on 21 August 2014 and requests another claim as the first has been lost. This subsequent contact is recorded. Franklin hands in the forms on the 31 August 2014. The claim is to be granted and evidence with the claim verifies that Franklin has been caring since the first contact on 2 August 2014.

Date of first contact = 2 August 2014

Date of subsequent contact = 21 August 2014

Date claim lodged = 31 August 2014

Date first qualified = 2 August 2014

DDOC = 21 August 2014

The claim can only be backdated to the date of the subsequent contact on 21 August 2014. Although Franklin was caring continuously since 2 August 2014, a claim could have been lodged on 21 August. Franklin has not demonstrated that the caring duties had a significant adverse effect on the ability to lodge the claim within 14 days of the contact date.

3

John + Read more ...

John contacts Friday 1 August 2014 and wishes to claim Age Pension. The contact is recorded on the Claim Contact Details (CCD) screen. John turns 65 years on Saturday 2 August 2014 and lodges the claim at the local service centre on Monday 4 August 2014. The claim is assessed and determined on 8 August 2014.

Date of first contact = 1 August 2014

Date claim lodged = 4 August 2014

Date first qualified = 2 August 2014

DDOC = 4 August 2014

The correct start date for the claim is the Monday - the date the claim was lodged.

For an early claim to be taken in to account, the claim form must be lodged. If the customer had lodged their claim form on the Friday rather than having the contact recorded on the CCD screen, payment could have started from the Saturday. The CCD on the Friday cannot be considered as the customer was not qualified on that date.
