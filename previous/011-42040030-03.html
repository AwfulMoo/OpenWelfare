Checking eligibility for an Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander health assessment

Table 1: this table describes how to check patient eligibility for an Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander health assessment under items 228, 715, 92004, 92011, 92016, 92023, 93470 or 93479.

Note: items 92016,92023,93470,93479 ended 30 June 2022. Check eligibility for these items until 30 March 2023.

Step

Action

1

Tell caller the online options + Read more ...

Tell the caller their options to check a patient's eligibility:

  • Health professionals can use the MBS Items Online Checker in HPOS to see if their patient is eligible for an MBS item
  • All patients can access their Medicare claims history through their Medicare online account or Express Plus app

Patients and health professionals can follow instructions on the Services Australia website.

If they want to continue with the call, go to Step 2.

2

Complete security check + Read more ...

  • For health professionals, see Health professional or their staff call about a claim
  • For consumers, see Perform security check to authenticate Medicare record

Note: proceed only if the caller meets the security check.

Tell the caller that eligibility information provided is based on the patient’s Medicare claiming history at the time of the enquiry and is subject to change if there are claims pending.

3

Retrieve the patient’s history + Read more ...

  • Key ‘NHSI,CARDNUMBER,PATIENT’ and press [Enter]
  • Press [PF9] function key
    • The patient’s PIN displays

Note: check the patient’s history by using the PIN in all instances to ensure the complete claiming history is returned, not just the history associated with one Medicare card number.

4

Check for health assessment items on the patient’s history + Read more ...

  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/228,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/715,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/92004-92011,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93470,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93479,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]

Does patient history display a paid claim in the above item range within the previous 9 months?

  • Yes, tell the caller the patient is not eligible for the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander health assessment item
  • No, tell the caller the patient is eligible for the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander health assessment item

Remind the caller that this information is based on the patient’s Medicare claims history for today’s date. Claims that have not been processed yet may affect their eligibility.

Procedure ends here.

  • All patients can access their Medicare claims history through their Medicare online account or Express Plus app

Patients and health professionals can follow instructions on the Services Australia website.

Complete security check + Read more ...

  • For health professionals, see Health professional or their staff call about a claim
  • For consumers, see Perform security check to authenticate Medicare record

Note: proceed only if the caller meets the security check.

Tell the caller that the eligibility information provided is based on the patient’s Medicare claiming history at the time of the enquiry and is subject to change if there are claims pending.

Retrieve the patient’s history + Read more ...

  • Key ‘NHSI,CARDNUMBER,PATIENT’ and press [Enter]
  • Press [PF9]
    • The patient’s PIN displays

Check for health assessment items on the patient’s history + Read more ...

  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/228,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/715,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/92004-92023,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93470,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]

Does patient history display a paid claim in the above item ranges?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, tell the caller that the patient is not eligible for follow-up allied health services

Procedure ends here.

5

Ask which follow-up items are being checked for eligibility + Read more ...

Ask which items the caller is checking patient eligibility for:

  • For standard items, go to Step 6
  • For additional physical therapy services (items 93571-93573), go to Step 7

6

Check for follow-up allied health items on the patient’s history + Read more ...

Count the number of follow-up allied health treatment services on the patient’s claims history since 1 January.

  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/81300-81360,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93048-93061,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93546-93558,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93579-93593,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]

Does patient history display 5 paid services in the above ranges since 1 January?

  • Yes, tell the caller that the patient has reached their claiming limit and is not eligible for standard follow-up allied health services
  • No, tell the caller that the patient is eligible for standard follow-up allied health services

Remind the caller that this information is based on the patient’s Medicare claims history for today’s date. Claims that have not been processed yet may affect their eligibility.

Note: Service Officers can tell the caller how many services the patient has left.

If the patient has exceeded the maximum number of eligible services in a calendar year, the caller can be told:

  • the patient can access 5 more services in the new calendar year
  • if the patient has used all the services under the existing referral, they need to get a new referral from their GP

Procedure ends here.

7

Tell caller the patient's eligibility for RACF additional physical therapy treatment service + Read more ...

Count the number of residential aged care facility (RACF) additional physical therapy treatment services on the patient’s claims history since 1 January.

  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93571-93573,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]

Does patient history display 5 paid services in the above range since 1 January?

  • Yes, tell the caller the patient has reached their claiming limit and is not eligible for the item
  • No, tell the caller the patient is eligible for the item

Remind the caller that this information is based on the patient’s Medicare claims history for today’s date. Claims that have not been processed yet may affect their eligibility.

Note: Service Officers can tell the caller how many services the patient has left.

Procedure ends here.

Assess claim for second/subsequent health assessment item in 12 months

Table 5: this table describes how to assess a claim for item 224-227 or 701-707 for a second or subsequent health assessment item for a patient in 12 months.

Note: this process is only used if the 6-RST TD message returns while processing.

Step

Action

1

Check for text with the claim + Read more ...

Has the health professional submitted text with their claim indicating which target group the patient is in?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, reject the claim with RSN 160. Procedure ends here

2

Assess text on previous claim + Read more ...

Is there text on the restricting item on patient history?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, reject the claim with RSN 160. Procedure ends here

3

Determine target group for each claim + Read more ...

Does the text on each claim show that the patient’s health assessments were done because they are a member of 2 different target groups?

For example, the claim on history has 'intellectual disability' and the claim being processed has 'over 75'.

  • Yes, pay the claim following system instructions
  • No, reject the claim with RSN 160. Procedure ends here
