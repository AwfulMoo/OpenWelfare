This page contains information relating to payment choices for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

Select the Self-managed tab to help the customer update their payment choice online.

Select the Assisted tab to advise about FTB payment choices and to record a person's choice or to view details of an Interactive Voice Response (IVR) phone self service interaction.

Self managed

The following individuals are not able to update their payment choice online:

  • those on a manual rate or with a current nil legislative rate of FTB
  • payment nominees who are not also correspondence nominees for eligible customers

Customers who are able to access and are eligible to use the online option, see Payment Choices online service.

See the Resources page for:

  • a link to Payment choices for Family Tax Benefit information on Services Australia website, and
  • further information about payment choices

If a person is unable to use the service, select the Assisted tab for Service Officers to record details.

Is the person claiming FTB? + Read more ...

2

New FTB claims + Read more ...

If the person does not advise their payment choice when claiming FTB, try calling them to discuss their payment choices.

If they cannot be contacted, grant payment at the legislative rate of FTB Part A until they have advised their choice.

For new claims, a payment choice can take effect on or after the date shown in the Earliest Date of New Choice: field. This date is equal to the latest of:

  • 1 July of the current financial year
  • cancellation date plus one day
  • date paid to plus one day

A future date can be recorded if requested by the person.

3

At every contact with customers + Read more ...

  • For FTB instalment customers, check that their annual income estimate is reasonable. Update/request a revised estimate if necessary
  • Ensure they are aware of all FTB payment options available. They can reduce the risk of overpayment through:
    • FTB Part A choices
    • FTB Part B choices
  • Ensure they are aware of how their FTB payment choice can affect:
    • Energy Supplement (ES)
    • Newborn Supplement (NBS) and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU)
    • Rent Assistance (RA)
    • advance payments of FTB Part A
    • Centrepay deductions
    • debt withholdings

Note: a payment choice cannot be applied without the FTB customer's informed choice. They must give their consent. Only the person receiving FTB can make the payment choice. Their partner, even if permitted to enquire (PPE is coded as 'Y'), cannot make the choice on behalf of the person.

Do not:

  • persuade the person in their choice, or
  • use payment choices to offset a qualification debt (for example, child had left the person's care). Raise these debts when identified

For more information about payment choices and their effects on entitlement to payments and services, see the Resources page.

4

Message for customers when they enter into a new payment choice + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'You can change your payment option at any time. Your new payment option will only apply to the remaining part of the year. However, you cannot change the way you have already received your Family Tax Benefit entitlement. The only exception to this is if you can prove financial hardship.'

If the person is receiving FTB Part A for a child from a previous relationship, remind them that if they have not done so already, they are required to take reasonable action to obtain child support during the financial year, even if they choose to receive base or nil rate of FTB Part A. If they do not take action to obtain child support, they will not be eligible for more than the base rate of FTB Part A.

Record these conversations in the relevant DOC for the payment choice activity.

5

Date of effect and reviews + Read more ...

For current FTB customers, a payment choice applies no earlier than the date paid to, plus one day. Record a future date if requested by the customer.

The FTB Payment Choice (FPC) screen displays the earliest date the payment option can apply.

If a future date recorded is more than 28 days after the end of the customer's current pay period, an automatic review is set up. This displays on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen:

  • Activity: FTBREA
  • Activity Type: FUT (Future)
  • Activity Type Notes: Change of Circumstances
  • Status: AUT Mandatory
  • Action: REA (Reassess)
  • Date: Date of change in circumstances
  • Review Reason: Future rate calculation

If FTB is current on the review date, the payment option will automatically be applied to adjust the FTB rate from the date requested by the person.

6

Health Care Cards (HCC) + Read more ...

Customers will be issued an automatic HCC if they are entitled to the maximum FTB Part A rate based on their reasonable income estimate, regardless of their adjusted rate of FTB.

7

Determining financial hardship and requesting arrears + Read more ...

A payment choice may be revoked and FTB arrears paid if:

  • financial hardship exists, or
  • where Newborn Supplement (NBS)/Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) has not been paid due to a payment choice

The Resources page contains a definition and example of financial hardship and an example of revoking a payment choice for NBS/NBU to be paid.

Ensure the customer has a reasonable family income estimate recorded and advise them that payment of arrears may increase the chance of having to repay FTB.

Consider financial hardship on a case-by-case basis.

Before granting a payment of arrears, the customer:

  • must provide a reasonable explanation supporting they are at risk of financial hardship if the payment of arrears was not made
  • can provide details verbally, without evidence

Record assessment of financial hardship in the relevant DOC.

For coding see Table 2.

Update a payment choice using the workflow + Read more ...

  • Select Update Choice action
  • The person's current payment choice is shown. As the payment choice is updated it will display the new choice and the new adjusted FTB rate
  • If a potential overpayment is identified, the workflow displays the mandatory continuous adjustment calculation as well as FTB payment options
  • Choices need to be selected separately for:
    • FTB Part A
    • FTB Part B
    • Rent Assistance (RA)
    • Energy Supplement

  • The workflow warns if the new adjusted rate will affect current withholdings from FTB and what to advise the person

The current payment choice is displayed in the Current FTB Payment Choice section.

If they want to change or cancel their FTB payment choice, update the New FTB Payment Choice Options section including the date of effect.

Update the Does the customer wish to receive all FTB components at the fortnightly rate? field with 'Yes' if they choose this option, otherwise selecting 'No' will present the other payment choices available.

The Date of effect for the payment choice: must be no earlier than date paid to plus one day or an error will present. A future date can be recorded if requested.

5

Recording choices manually + Read more ...

Other choices are recorded on the FTB Payment Choices (FPC) screen.

Access the screen directly or 'S'elect FTB Choices from the Families Task Selector (FTS) screen and then FTB Payment Choices from the FTB Choices Task Selector (FCTS) screen.

If the person wishes to change/ revoke their current payment choice, first code 'ALL' choice on the FPC screen from date paid to plus one day, or an earlier date if arrears are requested and they are in financial hardship, or entitled to Newborn Supplement (NBS)/Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) arrears when not paid due to a payment choice. Note: if they are revoking their choice (from date paid to plus one day), they have to be advised that no arrears will be paid until after reconciliation. The Resources page contains examples of revoking a payment choice.

Code choice on FPC screen

  • Event Date: no earlier than date paid to plus one day. A future date may be recorded if requested by the person. An earlier date in the current financial year can only be coded if the customer requests arrears and meets one of the acceptable financial hardship reasons or if entitled to NBS/NBU arrears when not paid due to a payment choice.
  • Payment Choice:
    • 'RBR' - base rate FTB Part A only
    • 'RPA' - FTB Part A only
    • 'RPB' - FTB Part B only
    • 'RBB' - base rate FTB Part A and Part B
    • 'NIL' - nil FTB fortnightly
    • 'ALL' - all FTB fortnightly
  • Pay RA:
    • Defaults to Yes for choices RPA and ALL and cannot be changed for these choices. Rent Assistance (RA) will be paid fortnightly
    • For other choices, code 'Yes' if they want to receive RA fortnightly, or 'No' if they want to receive RA annually

Record details on a DOC using Fast Note - select Auto text, use Families > Updates > FTB payment choice update, and finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

An Energy Supplement payment choice can only be recorded via the Family Income and Choices workflow. View history on the Energy Supplement Quarterly Payment Choice History (FYCQ) screen.

Check if they receive FTB advances. They may wish to cancel their regular advance if their rate will reduce. Otherwise, the advance will continue and their fortnightly rate will be adjusted to cover the repayments.

Procedure ends here.

6

Customer wants to cancel FTB + Read more ...

A person receiving FTB fortnightly payments may ask to cancel FTB because they intend to claim FTB as a lump sum after the end of the financial year.

Advise them if they choose the nil FTB option, they will not need to lodge a claim at the end of the financial year. They will receive any top-up payment automatically once actual income details are available. Their estimate of income must still be considered reasonable before choosing this payment choice.

If FTB is cancelled:

  • Rent Assistance (RA) will be cancelled. RA is not payable with a lump sum FTB claim unless they are not entitled to claim ongoing FTB fortnightly payments. To be paid arrears of FTB and RA, they will need to re-claim FTB as fortnightly payments
  • They will need to lodge a claim for FTB after the end of the lodgement year and meet FTB reconciliation requirements to receive any additional FTB

If they still wish to cancel FTB to claim FTB as a lump sum:

  • Cancel FTB on the Benefit Action (BA) screen from the date of notification, reason 'CLR' (Withdrawn/voluntary surrender)
  • If they will be claiming FTB Part A as a lump sum for a child from a previous relationship, they are required to take reasonable action to obtain child support during the financial year to receive more than base rate for the child/ren

Record details of the discussion on a DOC.
