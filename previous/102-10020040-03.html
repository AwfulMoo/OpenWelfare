Coding a cancellation for AIC Scheme

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Reasons for cancellation + Read more ...

Make sure the applicant is no longer eligible for payment of Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme and the decision to cancel the payment is correct.

If cancelling the payment because:

  • the applicant or student was imprisoned or institutionalised, go to Step 4
  • the student stopped studying full time, go to Step 5
  • the student changed their term living arrangements (e.g. boarding or second home), go to Step 6
  • of any other reason, go to Step 2

2

Continuity of Schooling concession + Read more ...

An AIC applicant can remain eligible for AIC under the Continuity of Schooling concession where the student:

  • stops meeting one of the AIC conditions because of a change of circumstances, and
  • continues to attend the same school or is enrolled at the same distance education institution

Does the applicant meet the above criteria?

  • Yes, this procedure does not apply. Go to Change of Address. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Cancel the applicant's payment + Read more ...

In the relevant student(s) record(s), go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen:

  • Key 'BA' in the Nxt: field, or
  • Select it from the AIC task selector (EITS) screen

Use field help ('?') to enter the correct code in the following fields:

  • Type of benefit in the Svc Rsn: field
  • Key 'CAN' in the Action: field
  • Cancellation reason in the Reason: field
  • Press [Enter] and the effect date will default, or, enter the effect date of the cancellation in the Event Date: field
  • Enter the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn: field
  • Enter any comments in the Notes: field
  • Enter the source of the cancellation in the Source: field
  • Enter the date of receipt in the DOR: field

Go to Step 7.

4

Student or applicant imprisonment or institutionalised + Read more ...

Update the student's record:

  • Go to EIC Claim Details (EICD) screen
  • In the Event Date record the date the circumstance occurred
  • Enter Relationship to Student
  • On the Non-approval reason field: key GPS
  • Enter the source of the cancellation in the Source: field
  • Enter the date of receipt in the DOR: field
  • Key I in the Action: field

Go to Step 7.

5

Student stopped studying full time + Read more ...

Update the student's record:

  • Go to the Education Course Details (EDCH) screen
  • On the Student end date: field record the student end date
  • Enter the source of the cancellation in the Source: field
  • Enter the date of receipt in the DOR: field
  • Key C in the Action: field

Go to Step 7.

6

Change to term living arrangements + Read more ...

Update the student's record:

  • Go the Living Arrangements Details (EILA) screen
  • Record the entitlement end date
  • Enter the source of the cancellation in the Source: field
  • Enter the date of receipt in the DOR: field
  • Key C in the Action: field

7

Finalise activity + Read more ...

Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen:

  • Key 'AR' in the Nxt: field, or
  • Select [Enter] if the BA or AR screens were selected from the EITS screen

Finalise the activity on the AR screen.

Actioning AIC debts

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check for possible overpayment + Read more ...

Did the applicant get any payments they were not entitled to prior to cancellation?

  • Yes, follow up and raise overpayment. See Actioning an undetermined debt on the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS). If the change to customer's circumstances was due to Coronavirus, and the applicant notified:
    • within their notification requirements, consider a decision to waive the overpayment under 7.3.3.7 - Waiver of the right to recover a debt - of the AIC Policy Scheme Guidelines
    • outside their notification requirements, assess any overpayment as per standard debt process
    • go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

TPO contact for funds received + Read more ...

Staff can issue a debt recovery notice to recover funds where:

  • there has been an administration error
  • an applicant advised the agency of a change in circumstances within 14 days, and the funds still issue incorrectly, or
  • the TPO contacts and wishes to repay AIC funds they incorrectly received

An applicant still remains responsible for the recovery of funds from a TPO where:

  • they did not tell us of a change in circumstances within 14 days, and
  • the above does not apply

AIC Processing staff, go to Step 3.

Telephony Service Officers:

  • Send a Fast Note to the AIC Smart Centre. Select Auto text, use Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > AIC update action required
  • Include details of:
    • TPO name and Customer Reference number (CRN) of who received the funds
    • date TPO contacted
    • person who contacted
    • who the incorrect entitlement was received for
    • student’s date of birth (DOB) and CRN (if known)
    • payment type, date received, period it was for and the amount
    • reason for raising debt (that is, administration error/TPO wishes to pay)
  • Tell the TPO this will be allocated to a Service Officer to action. The Service Officer may contact them for more information

3

Actioning TPO debt recovery + Read more ...

Staff must:

  • confirm reason for the debt
  • determine who is entitled to the funds (applicant, correct TPO)
  • manually raise a debt on the TPO’s record for the amount they were incorrectly paid. Use standard debt raising processes
  • contact the TPO and tell them:
    • the agency will send them an Accounts Payable letter to recover the funds, and
    • they must advise the agency when they have returned the funds

In addition to the TPO DOC, update the details on the AIC student’s record and include the Debt ID.

4

TPO contacts to advise funds returned + Read more ...

Go to Organisation Record in Customer first

  • swap to Environment B
  • view the debt details and confirm the TPO has repaid the debt:
    • go to Customer First > Workspace
    • go to the Debts assignment block
    • select Debt Information

If the status of debt is:

  • recovered:
    • send a Fast Note from the AIC student’s record
    • use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > AIC update action required, for processing staff to determine who is entitled to the funds and reissue the funds if required
  • showing determined, the recovery of funds has not been processed. Code a manual review:
    • Service Reason: EIA
    • Review Reason: Returned Cheque
    • Due Date: 7 days
    • Source: PHO
    • Date of Receipt
    • DOC organisation contact
    • When the funds have been recovered, reissue to the correct applicant if required

5

Manual follow up + Read more ...

Check the Activity List (AL) screen.

Is there a Manual Follow Up (MFU) on the AL screen?

  • Yes, create a manual letter. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Send manual letter + Read more ...

Send a manual letter (Q999) to the applicant.

  • Give the reason(s) for cancellation of payment
  • Include their rights of review and appeal

7

Document decision + Read more ...

Record details on a DOC. Include all relevant Act and AIC Guidelines references.

If the applicant has other AIC students, complete the above procedures for each student.
