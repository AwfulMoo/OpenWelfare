This page contains information that must be applied to both the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme applicant and the student.

Meeting residence requirements for AIC

AIC Scheme requirements for applicant and student

This table describes how to identify if students and applicants meet the residence requirements for AIC Scheme.

Step

Action

1

Born overseas + Read more ...

Were the student and/or applicant born overseas?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 7

2

Applicant / student born overseas + Read more ...

Advise the customer (the applicant) that if they or the student are either a New Zealand citizen or an Australian permanent resident, they need to provide evidence of their and/or the student's New Zealand Citizenship or Australian permanent residence.

Evidence could include one of the following:

  • Australian citizenship papers
  • passport or visa showing permanent resident status and all subsequent passports
  • a New Zealand passport showing all Australian arrival and departure stamps over the past year
  • a statement from the Department of Home Affairs confirming their permanent residence or, for New Zealand citizens, dates of arrival and departure from Australia over the past year

Customer does not have evidence:

Do not ask the customer to obtain evidence as the cost of obtaining documents from Department of Home Affairs can be significant.

Services Australia should arrange to verify the information for the customer. See Commencement of Identity.

3

New Zealand citizen + Read more ...

Are the student and/or applicant New Zealand citizens?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Bona fide intention to remain settled permanently in Australia + Read more ...

Note: in addition to the definition of permanently settled, New Zealand applicants and students must satisfy one of the residency rules (waiting periods).

Are the student and/or applicant permanently settled in Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 11

5

Residence requirements + Read more ...

Are the student and/or applicant permanent residents of Australia?

  • Advise the customer they will need to provide their passport or entry documentation (or those related to the student, as applicable).
  • Ensure all relevant arrival and departure stamps and the visa have been photocopied.
  • Check the most recent visa to obtain the visa sub-class. To check the visa sub-class, see Policy for a link to Guide to Social Security Law, 9.2.
    • Yes, go to Step 7
    • No, the student and/or applicant does not satisfy the residence requirements for AIC. Procedure ends here

6

Residence in Australia + Read more ...

Student and/or applicant lived in Australia:

  • continuously for 6 months or more, or
  • if there is an absence within the six month period it must be for less than a two week period and the applicant must show that the student is still enrolled in schooling within Australia and can demonstrate continuity of residence in Australia in that period, or
  • for 12 months with no more than 2 months absence in that period, or
  • for 12 months with more than 2 months absence but can demonstrate continuity of residence in Australia in that period

Has the student and/or applicant met any of the above criteria?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, to all of the above, go to Step 11

7

Studying and residing in Australia + Read more ...

Are the student and/or applicant living in Australia during the year of study?

  • Yes, the student and/or applicant satisfy the residence requirements for AIC. Continue to process the claim. Procedure ends here
  • No, if the person is the:
    • applicant, go to Step 8
    • student, she/he does not satisfy the residence requirements for AIC

Note: there are exceptions for students eligible for AIC Distance Education Allowance (DED) who can remain eligible whilst temporarily overseas under some circumstances. See Eligibility for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme Distance Education Allowance.

There is also an exception for students participating in an international student exchange and the student:

  • remains enrolled at an Australian education institution and the overseas study is credited to their Australian studies (however, if a student is paid AIC allowances while studying overseas but then needs to repeat all or part of that study in Australia, allowances are not payable for the period of overseas study that does not count towards their Australian studies)
  • is continuing to incur the costs in Australia for which the allowance is being paid (for example, if the Australian boarding school is still charging full boarding fees while the student is on exchange)

Procedure ends here.

8

Temporary absence (less than 2 years) + Read more ...

Is the applicant's absence temporary? (less than 2 years)

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 11

9

Principal family home + Read more ...

Is the principal family home in Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 11

10

Geographical isolation rules + Read more ...

Does the family home continue to meet one of the geographical isolation rules (where applicable)?

  • Yes, the student and/or applicant satisfies the residence requirements for AIC. Continue to process the claim. Procedure ends here. Note: there is a known system problem where residency rules will cause the claim to Rejection (REJ)/ NZ student not residentially qualified (NZQ) if the student or applicant have been outside Australia during the period they otherwise qualify for AIC. This is because the system is incorrectly implementing social security residency rules that do not apply to AIC. Where the system only pays from the last arrival date in Australia, the Service Officer will need to arrange for the applicant to be paid manually any arrears owed to them before this date
  • No, go to Step 11

11

Residence requirements for AIC not met + Read more ...

The student and/or applicant does not satisfy the residence requirements for AIC.

For a new claim: If the student and/or applicant is a person who will shortly meet the residence requirements of the scheme, they should also be tested against the remaining eligibility criteria. If the student and/or applicant meet the remaining criteria, they should be advised to provide evidence of their residence at the appropriate time.

For a current customer: If immigration data received from the Department of Home Affairs datalink shows the customer has ceased to meet the residence requirements, for the:

  • student, the system will use this data to automatically cancel the AIC payment
  • applicant, immigration data does not ripple to the student's record. Service Officers must manually cancel the payment. Go to Step 12

12

Cancel the AIC payment + Read more ...

Cancel the AIC payment if the applicant ceases to meet the residence requirements.

In the relevant student(s) record(s):

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen
    • Service Reason: field, EIC - Assistance for Isolated Children
    • Action: field, CAN - Cancel
    • Reason: field, OTH
    • Effect Date: field, the permanent visa end date +1
    • Source: field, PHO
    • Receipt Date: field, the date the applicant notified
  • Select Submit Change
  • Finalise the activity via the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Record details in a DOC
  • Generate a manual Q999 letter and insert the following text:

'We can no longer pay you Assistance for Isolated Children for <student name> because you do not meet the Australian residence requirements as per Assistance for Isolated Children Policy Guideline 2.2.1 - Australian citizenship or permanent residency.'

Procedure ends here.
