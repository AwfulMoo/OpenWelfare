Scenarios for transferring from one IM measure to another

Scenario

Description

1

Transfer from CPIM to LTWPR measure + Read more ...

John has been income managed under Child Protection Income Management (CPIM) for the last 12 months. The Child Protection Authority has decided not to extend the notice to income manage John’s payments so they are due to come off CPIM.

However, as John has been in receipt of JobSeeker Payment for the last 18 months and is living in the Northern Territory, when the Child Protection notice expires John will not come off Income Management. Instead they will automatically be income managed under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR) measure.

Impact of this transfer

When John moves from CPIM to LTWPR, the percentage of their income support payment that is income managed will reduce from 70% to 50%. Failure to review John's expenses could result in John having more than 100% of income managed funds allocated, which in turn will cause expenses to be undelivered due to insufficient funds being available.

2

Transfer from LTWPR measure to CPIM + Read more ...

Sue is currently income managed under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR) measure and the Child Protection Authority has lodged a Notice to Commence Income Management with Services Australia.

When Sue attends the Initial Assessment interview, Sue is automatically transferred to Child Protection Income Management (CPIM).

Impact of this transfer

When Sue moves from LTWPR to CPIM, the percentage of the income support payment that is income managed will increase from 50% to 70%. Failure to review Sue's expenses could result in hardship for Sue if they continue to use the discretionary funds for a priority need expense which could now be met from income managed funds.

3

Transfer from DEY to NIM-CIM + Read more ...

Julie is income managed under the Disengaged Youth (DEY) measure and has just been granted an exemption under the ‘with dependent children’ criteria.

Julie’s payment nominee is income managed under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients (LTWPR) measure.

When Julie’s exemption is coded, they will be transferred to Nominee Income Management – Compulsory Income Management (NIM-CIM).

Impact of this transfer

When Julie moves from the DEY measure to NIM-CIM, there will be no change in the percentage of the income support payments being income managed. A review will allow the Service Officer to explain to Julie why they are still being income managed. Julie can choose to end the payment nominee relationship if the arrangement is voluntary and no longer suits their requirements, at which time NIM-CIM would be ended. If the arrangement is to remain in place, Julie’s priority needs should be reviewed to ensure their needs continue to be met.
