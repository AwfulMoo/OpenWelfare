Step

Action

1

Checking the form + Read more ...

Ensure the SY023 has been checked.

Also, ensure the declaration is signed by the customer or the parent if the customer is dependent and 15 years old or younger during the year of study.

Note: if the customer was given this form due to missing a section on the original claim form, the Service Officer may have highlighted the section(s) to be completed. If so, the customer does not need to complete the entire form, and only the relevant section needs to be assessed.

2

Accessing the ABSTUDY system + Read more ...

Access the ABSTUDY system by completing the following screen header fields:

  • Nxt - 'SVABY'
  • Sys - 'ISS'

This will then display (ABY) in the Act field on the screen header.

Access the customer's record.

3

Identity Confirmation + Read more ...

ABSTUDY customers are subject to Identity Confirmation and may need to confirm their identity.

4

Additional information provided + Read more ...

Check which question(s) have been answered on the form:

  • Personal details, go to Step 5
  • Personal income, go to Step 7
  • Personal assets, go to Step 9
  • Study details, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • Australian Apprenticeship (apprentice or trainee) details, see Step 2 in Table 2
  • Away from home details, see Step 3 in Table 2
  • Other Government payments, see Step 4 in Table 2
  • Independence, see Step 5 in Table 2
  • Dependent children/student details, see Step 6 in Table 2
  • Tax details, see Step 7 in Table 2

5

Personal details + Read more ...

Go to the Customer Details Task Selector (CDTS) screen and ensure the details listed on the SY023 are correctly recorded on the student's record.

Note: if the student has indicated that they are receiving government assistance for study or training, check if:

  • the student is still qualified for ABSTUDY, and
  • it would be more advantageous for the student to remain on their current payment, or transfer to ABSTUDY. This may involve comparing rates of payment and any additional benefits

7

Personal income + Read more ...

Use the Self-Employment workflow in Process Direct to add or update details for sole trader and partnership businesses.

To update in:

  • Process Direct:
    • Key Start into the Super Key and select the Self-Employment workflow from the Task Selector
  • Customer Record:
    • use the Self Employment script

Record the details of the student's or Australian Apprentice's employment income on the Employment Income Details (EAN) screen.

Update the income screens as follows. If the customer has indicated that they:

  • Have been self-employed or a subcontractor in the last 12 months, they should also complete a Business details (MOD F). When this is returned, update the information on the Business Detail (BUS) screen with the information provided in the MOD F
  • Own freehold land (not native title) or buildings other than the home they live in, they should also complete a Real estate details (MOD R). When this is returned, use the information provided in the MOD R to update:
    • the Real Estate/Business Details (REBS) screen in Process Direct or
    • the Business Details (BUS) screen in Customer Record/Customer First
    • See Assessing and coding the Real estate details Mod R
  • Have money held in superannuation or rollover funds and receive a pension from this, they should also complete an Income and Assets (MOD iA), and when this is returned, update the Superannuation/Annuities Identification (SUPI) screen. See Assessing and coding the Income and Assets Mod iA. If they have money in superannuation or rollover funds but do not receive a pension from them, simply update the SUPI screen with the details provided with the form

Coding Study and Family details

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

Study details + Read more ...

Code the education details on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen.

  • If no further questions were answered, go to Step 8
  • If other questions were answered, select the appropriate step below:
    • Australian Apprenticeship (apprentice or trainee) details, go to Step 2
    • Away from home details, go to Step 3
    • Other Government payments, go to Step 4
    • Independence, go to Step 5
    • Dependent children/student details, go to Step 6
    • Tax details, go to Step 7

2

Full-time Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

If the customer indicates they are a full-time Australian Apprentice, Services Australia will obtain the Australian Apprenticeship details from the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations.

This means that it is not essential for the customer to provide this information.

Australian Apprenticeship (apprentice or trainee) details need to be coded on the Apprenticeship Details (NAPD) screen.

The EDC screen also needs to be updated to reflect the customer's status as an Australian Apprentice.

  • If no further questions were answered, go to Step 8
  • If other questions were answered, select the appropriate step below:
    • Away from home details, go to Step 3
    • Other Government payments, go to Step 4
    • Independence, go to Step 5
    • Dependent children/student details, go to Step 6
    • Tax details, go to Step 7

3

Away from home details + Read more ...

Assess or reassess (as appropriate) the customer's entitlement to the away from home rate of ABSTUDY, and update the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen accordingly.

  • If no further questions were answered, go to Step 8
  • If other questions were answered, select the appropriate step below:
    • Other Government payments, go to Step 4
    • Independence, go to Step 5
    • Dependent children/student details, go to Step 6
    • Tax details, go to Step 7

4

Other Government payments + Read more ...

  • Check if the student is on a payment that might qualify them for ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES). If the student is currently receiving a PES qualifying payment, it would generally be more advantageous for them to claim PES with their current payment rather than transfer to ABSTUDY
  • Check if the Australian Apprentice is receiving Department of Employment and Workplace Relations' (DEWR) Living Away from Home Allowance
  • For more information, see Not qualified for ABSTUDY if receiving other assistance to study or as an Australian Apprentice

Also use the information provided here to identify if the customer may need to transfer from their existing payment to ABSTUDY.

  • If no further questions were answered, go to Step 8
  • If other questions were answered, select the appropriate step below:
    • Independence, go to Step 5
    • Dependent children/student details, go to Step 6
    • Tax details, go to Step 7

5

Independence + Read more ...

Assess or reassess the ABSTUDY student's status as independent.

  • If no further questions were answered, go to Step 8
  • If other questions were answered, select the appropriate step below:
    • Dependent children/student details, go to Step 6
    • Tax details, go to Step 7

6

Dependent children/student details + Read more ...

Investigate eligibility for Family Tax Benefit (FTB). If the customer appears eligible, see Initial contact by customers claiming Family Tax Benefit (FTB) as a lump sum for a previous year.

Has the customer provided Tax details?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Tax details + Read more ...

Input the Tax File Number (TFN) details on the Tax File Number (TFN) screen.

Note: if the customer indicated that they do not have or do not know what their TFN is, see Requesting a Tax File Number (TFN).

8

Declaration and recording action + Read more ...

  • Ensure the declaration is signed by the customer or the parent if the customer is 15 or younger during the year of study
  • Record details and action taken on a DOC
