2

Criteria for customers in State Care to receive the away from home rate of ABSTUDY Living Allowance + Read more ...

The away from home rate is determined by assessing why a customer is living away from the permanent home.

In this case, the definition of a 'permanent home' for a student in State Care who:

  • has a foster carer is the place where the foster carer normally resides
  • does not have a foster carer is the place where they normally reside

A student in State Care placed into the direct care of a boarding school does not have any place they normally reside. During school holidays these vulnerable customers are most likely placed into the temporary care of a respite carer. Students eligible for the away from home rate will continue to maintain the away from home rate while in respite care in these circumstances.

Note: as boarding schools close over school holidays the student requires travel to the respite carer, the home of the respite carer will be treated as the 'permanent home' for the purposes of Fares Allowance.

The maximum away from home rate of Living Allowance, free of the parental income test, may be paid if the carer does not receive a regular foster care or similar allowance for the customer's upkeep, and are eligible for:

  • Schooling B Award and:
    • has reached 15 years of age, or
    • they are under 15 years of age but meets one of the criteria for away from home rate
  • Tertiary Award or Masters and Doctorate Award, and
    • they have reached the minimum school leaving age or,
    • is under the minimum school leaving age, but meets one of the criteria for away from home rate

Note: Commonwealth Government payments for dependent children in general (for example, Family Assistance) and payments made by any government authority or other organisation towards the upkeep of a residential care facility do not stop them from receiving the away from home rate.

3

Customers in State Care who meet an away from home category, but are not eligible to receive ABSTUDY away from home allowances + Read more ...

Customers in state care who meet the away from home category and their carer receives regular foster care or similar allowance, are only eligible for the 'at home'' rate of payment.

Customers in this category will receive the 'at home rate'. Note: where a student or Australian Apprentice is in State Care and is 15 years or younger, and entitled to Living Allowance, they are paid at the 16-17 year old rate.

If a customer in State Care who is not eligible for an away from home category, may still be entitled to receive the following:

  • ABSTUDY School Term Allowance if aged 14 years of age or younger, or
  • Living Allowance at the at home (standard) rate if undertaking:
    • secondary level studies and 15 years of age or older
    • tertiary or Masters or Doctorate level studies and have reached the minimum school leaving age

4

Exemption from the parental income test + Read more ...

Customers in State Care are not considered to be independent, however are exempt from the parental income test if:

  • they are in the guardianship, care or custody of a Court, Minister or Department; or there is a current direction or authorisation from a court, Minister or Department placing them in the guardianship, care or custody of someone who is not their natural or adoptive parent, or
  • they stopped being subject to the direction only because of age

The following information is required when a student is in State Care and placed into the care of a person.

If the student is:

  • 16 years of age or over - as the student is the applicant, the claim for ABSTUDY can still be processed if the carer has not signed the claim form or Mod JY. Where no carer information has been received, carer information can be obtained over the phone
  • under 16 years of age - as the carer is the applicant, a signed claim form or Mod JY must be supplied. Note: this will not apply for ABSTUDY Schooling A claims where the applicant is not required to return the Customer Declaration Form (CDF)

If the student is in State Care, and is in the care of:

  • an organisation, go to Step 5
  • a person, the:
    • appropriate record for PIT linking is the carer(s) with whom the customer has been placed into the care of by the Court, Minister or Department
    • income of the carer or guardian is not taken into account when calculating the relevant rate of entitlement and therefore they will receive the full relevant rate
    • go to Step 6

5

Student placed into the care of an organisation + Read more ...

Note: a Mod JY is not required where a student is placed into the care of an organisation.

In most cases, customers in State Care would be placed into the care of a person. In some cases, customers are linked to the department.

For example, customer is placed directly into a boarding school, residential facility, only provided with respite care during school holidays, or the state government department maintains a high level of daily care and control after the customer has been placed with a foster carer etc. When this occurs, an organisation can be an applicant under ABSTUDY Policy 6.1.2 and an officer of that organisation should be able to sign the form.

Where officers or staff of the organisation have not signed the application form as 'the applicant', Services Australia staff may accept any formal correspondence returned from the organisation regarding the child's care as sufficient evidence that the information in the claim about the customer is correct.

For example, when the State Care organisation returns a letter advising that the student is boarding or planning to commence boarding at a boarding school or hostel, this is sufficient evidence that the child is in the care of the organisation. It can be accepted in lieu of the completion of the 'applicant's' signature on the ABSTUDY forms, in this instance. Where there is insufficient formal correspondence supplied to this effect, confirmation from the relevant state/territory department (verbal or written) is sufficient.

Therefore, Services Australia does not need a signature to grant the ABSTUDY for State Care customers in these situations.

In regards to the system, the State Care customer still needs to be PIT linked to a person record. See Step 3 in Table 2.

6

Rate of payment + Read more ...

Customers in State Care approved for the away from home rate will be eligible for the relevant rate of payment for their age.

Customers 15 years of age or younger and eligible for the away from home rate are entitled to be paid at the 16-17 year rate.

7

Documentation required + Read more ...

Customers currently living in State Care must provide a statement from the relevant government agency:

  • confirming that a direction or authorisation by a Minister, Court or Department is currently in effect in relation to their care
  • advising of the care arrangements, including the name and address of the person(s), government agency or other organisation authorised to care for them, and
  • advising whether the person or organisation authorised to care for them is receiving any continuous payment to assist directly with their upkeep

Note: where a State/Territory welfare department contracts out to a private provider to coordinate placement of a customer in care, the letter authorising the placement must be signed by an officer of the appropriate State/Territory authority.

8

Advice to customer + Read more ...

When granting or rejecting the claim for Living Allowance at the away from home rate, the customer should be advised to notify Services Australia as soon as possible if there are any changes to their circumstances. For example, change of address, they cease to be in State Care, or payments from the State /Territory authority start or stop.

Coding required to pay the away from home rate to a young person in State Care where a person is the carer + Read more ...

Customers in State Care can be entitled to receive ABSTUDY at either the away from home or the at home rate of payment.

Coding NIH screen

To pay the away from home rate, update the following on the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH, NIHS) screen:

If a current entry exists and the entry:

  • reflects the customers current circumstances, no update required
  • does not reflect the customers current circumstances, select the current line and update the End Date field

If a new entry is required code the following:

  • Action: 'AFH'
  • Reason:
    • 'AFN' (State Care - Foster Allowance not payable to carer, ABSTUDY away from home rate payable to customer), or
    • 'AFY' (State Care - Foster Allowance payable to carer, ABSTUDY at home rate payable to customer)
  • Start Date: the date from that the away from home is granted

Note: The End Date, End Reason and Consent to Contact: fields are not required.

If a student is undertaking an approved scholarship course, they may also be qualified for a Relocation Scholarship, see Relocation Scholarship (RS) eligibility.

Department of Education considered ABSTUDY students in State Care (not receiving Foster Care Allowance) to be similar in nature to dependent students living away from home. Scholarship processing for these students mirrors dependent away from home students. A regional classification is to be coded on the RLD screen to determine the customer's rate of Relocation Scholarship. See Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding.

Customers in State Care must be linked to the person with whom they have been placed into the care of. For more information, see Linking parent records with dependent student, Australian Apprentice and job seeker recipients or claimants - Parental Income Test (PIT).

To ensure the young person is entitled to the full rate of applicable allowance irrespective of the carer's financial circumstances, and not subject to the Parental Income Test provisions, the Foster Parent PIT Exemption Reason should display on the NHI screen. This will occur when correctly linking the carer or guardian as Foster Parent (FSP) on the PIT Parent Selection (NPPS) screen.

See Coding away from home rate for ABSTUDY for full details on coding procedures.

Record a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on the customer's record advising the student is in State Care, include the organisations name and contact details.

Note: if a State Care student meets multiple away from home criteria, the NIH screen must be coded with State Care reasons. Where required, DOC the record with the additional away from home criteria met.

Has the student ceased to be qualified for state care due to age?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Coding when a student ceases to be qualified for State Care only because of their age + Read more ...

For ABSTUDY purposes, a student or Australian Apprentice meeting the qualification for State Care but has ceased care only because of their age, can qualify for entitlements available to students or Australian Apprentices who are still formally in State Care.

  • If the customer remains living with the carer they were placed with, they will remain PIT linked to that person
  • If the customer ceases to live with a carer but was already PIT linked to State or Territory Department as a 'dummy' person record, then that link can remain current to ensure the customer continues to receive the correct rate
  • If the customer ceases to live with that carer they still need to be PIT linked to a person record so as the higher rate continues to pay. This is due to system limitations. End the PIT link to the Carer they left, and create a new link to the State or Territory Department they were previously in the care of (even if they are no longer in that department's care, this process is to ensure the customer continues to receive the correct rate)

Updates may be required to the NIH screen, go to Step 3.

3

Coding required to pay the away from home rate to customers in State Care where an organisation is the carer + Read more ...

Customers in State Care who meet the away from home category, and their carer receives regular foster care or similar allowance, are only eligible for the 'at home' rate of payment if the customer is placed into the care of an organisation. For example, a boarding school.

To pay the away from home rate, update the Independent/Homeless Away From Home Details (NIH) screen.

If a current entry exists and the entry:

  • reflects the customers current circumstances, no update required
  • does not reflect the customers current circumstances, select the current line and update the End Date field

If a new entry is required code the following:

  • Action: 'AFH'
  • Reason:
    • 'AFN' (State Care - Foster Allowance not payable to carer, ABSTUDY away from home rate payable to customer), or
    • 'AFY' (State Care - Foster Allowance payable to carer, ABSTUDY at home rate payable to customer)
  • Start Date: the date from which the away from home rate is granted

See Coding away from home rate for ABSTUDY for full details on coding procedures.

4

Applying PIT links + Read more ...

Due to system limitations, the following procedure needs to be followed in all cases where the ABSTUDY student or Australian Apprentice who is in State Care does not have a carer (person) to PIT link to.

Identify which state/territory welfare department is responsible for the customer, to ensure the organisation has not already been added as a person in all environments. Undertake a national search on the Name Search (IN) screen in the ISS (for ABSTUDY) system. Use PER as the search type.

If there’s no record or a new person will need to be created, it is preferable that it is created in the same environment as the ABSTUDY recipient:

  • enter the organisation details on the IN screen:
    • Surname of Person: field, state/territory welfare department name
    • First Name or Initial: field, Department of
    • Sex: field, Either female or male can be selected
    • Date of Birth: 29/02/1920
    • ServRsn: field, PAR
    • Activity: field, ADD
    • select Search
  • if the search has identified that ‘no record has been found’, code AP in the Nxt: field
  • update the Add Person (AP) screen:
    • Surname First Name, Sex and Date of Birth should default from IN screen
    • Address: the address of the state/territory welfare department. Note: a contact phone number is not compulsory
    • Interpreter Language: field, ENG - English
    • Indigenous Indicator: field, D
    • Note: use field help ('?') if unsure of what to code in a particular field
    • Accept (Y/N): field, code a Y for Yes, if an N for No is coded, return to the IN screen to conduct another search and repeat the above.
    • select Continue
    • when the organisation's details are recorded on the AP screen, the system will create a record for the parent, generating a CRN
    • code the tax file number exemption reason TFN request from ATO
    • finalise the PAR/ADD activity -select this activity on the Activity List (AL) screen of the record, type FIN in the Nxt: field and then press [Enter]

When the record has been added, or if a record is already created, make note of the CRN, as it will need to be used to for PIT linking. See Linking parent records with dependent student, Australian Apprentice and job seeker recipients or claimants - Parental Income Test (PIT).

This will occur when correctly linking the carer or guardian as  FSP  on the  NPPS  screen.

5

State Care MFU or Review for State Care students turning 18yrs old + Read more ...

A Manual Follow-up Activity (MFU) with the keywords U18AFH and I033YB is automatically created when the student reaches 17 years and 11 months of age. Service Officers must confirm if a foster care or similar allowance will continue to be paid from the State or Territory government. This will determine the rate of ABSTUDY Living Allowance payable from the student's 18th birthday.

The Service Officer must make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer in order to determine whether the State or Territory government will continue to pay a foster care or similar allowance on the customer's behalf. Ensure to explain the effects to the customer's rate of payment if the Foster Care Allowance has or will cease.

If the customer is unable to provide this information, Service Officers can obtain permission from the customer to contact the carer and/or state or territory department.

Coding the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen

If support will continue to be paid, or no response has been received by their 18th birthday:

  • Update to NIH screen is not required. The customer is to remain as Reason Code AFY for the period of the support
  • Record details on a DOC and cancel the MFU

If support will cease from the customer's 18th birthday, an update to NIH screen will be required.

Before starting the update, an end-date is required before a new line can be entered. Once end-dated, press [Enter] for a new NIH screen.

Code the following:

  • AFH in the Action: field
  • AFN (State Care - Foster Allowance not payable to carer, ABSTUDY away from home rate payable to customer) in the Reason: field
  • the date from which the support ceases in the Start Date: field
  • record details of action taken on a DOC and cancel the MFU
