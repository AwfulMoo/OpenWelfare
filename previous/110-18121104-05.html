Education message - business income interventions

Table 1

Item

Description

1

When to deliver

Give this education message to a customer when:

  • their declared income is greater than the match data, and
  • they are determined to be an employee rather than self-employed

2

Education message

  • 'It is important that you declare:
    • Gross income - the amount of income paid before tax or any deductions
    • rather than Net income - the amount that goes into your bank account from your employer.'
  • 'You are required to declare the amount you were paid in your reporting period.'
    You may want to give examples: Diary/Calendar, hours per day X hourly rate= good estimate
  • 'Do you understand that you are required to report your employment income fortnightly, on your lodgement date before any payments will be made?'
  • 'Do you understand that if Services Australia identifies any under declared income from personal exertion, Services Australia can impose a 10% recovery fee to the debt?'
  • 'Are you aware of the Working Credit scheme?'
    'The working credit/income bank scheme allows you to build up credits in each fortnight. When you have income from employment, you can draw on your working credits/income bank to reduce the effect of that income on your income support payment. Only income from employment can be used to affect these credits in this way'
  • 'Would you like me to send you out a form to help you keep a record of your employment?'
    • 'This information will help you record and declare your gross employment income within your fortnightly period?'
    • If the customer advises yes, issue the 'Earnings Worksheet' (SU505), and the 'Information you need to know about reporting employment and using the Earnings Worksheet' via > Mail Forms > Selections > Employment Services > Forms
    • Advise the customer not to return the forms (despite the letter advising the customer to return them)
  • 'Are you aware that we get information about your income from other government agencies, such as the Australian Taxation Office? If we get information that your income is different to what you have told us when getting a payment, we will ask you to review, update and confirm your information. Therefore, we recommend that you please remember to keep all records of your employment. This will reduce the need for you to contact your employer'
  • Explain to customer that if they have commissions, bonus payments, allowances, leave and/or termination payments etc., these may be assessed differently and it is best to contact the agency to advise

Customers unable to access bank statements

Table 2

Item

Description

1

When assistance can be provided

Services Australia can help customers in the following situations:

  • If the customer has not been able to get the documents even though they have made genuine and reasonable attempts to get them. For example, they have been unable to obtain the information because the third party no longer operates, has been uncontactable, is being uncooperative)
  • If the cost of obtaining the information would cause financial hardship to the customer
  • If it would be unreasonable to expect the customer to obtain the information given the nature of their vulnerabilities. Vulnerabilities to be considered include, but are not limited to:
    • disability or illness
    • homelessness (or risk of)
    • personal crisis, e.g. bereavement, recent trauma, family or domestic violence or other issues causing severe emotional distress
    • language and/or literacy issues
    • geographically or socially isolated, for example remote Indigenous
  • If the customer has other special circumstances considered unusual or exceptional. For example, records were destroyed in a fire

In these situations the Compliance Officer (CO) must:

  • First, check the customer's record to see if there is suitable evidence about income that can be used
  • If there is no suitable evidence, check the customer's record to see if the customer has a nominee. If they have a nominee, consider whether the nominee could help the customer obtain the required information

This help should only be provided in the situations described above. Approval must be sought from the Quality Development Officer (QDO) or Team Leader before issuing a QSS40/41.

2

Sending the request

The CO should send a request to the customer's bank to obtain bank statements.

All information must be fully documented in the Note, including the reason for this request and the approvals sought.

When approval has been granted:

  • cancel the BIBI Integrated Review ( in Customer First to enable Manual IRS Processing (if not already cancelled)
    • select Edit from the Integrated Review (ZIRV) screen in Customer First
    • select Cancelled from the Activity Status drop down menu
    • select the Manual IRS Processing Required from the Reason drop down menu
    • select Save
  • issue a QSS40/41 (whichever is applicable) via the General Review Action script
  • add the keyword 'QSS40' or 'QSS41' in the integrated review system (IRS)
  • move the held IRS activity to:
    • Workgroup: UII_(site code)
    • Position: CO's logon

3

Services Australia unable to obtain bank statements

If the agency is unable to obtain bank statements, a decision will be made by the CO, with QDO or Team Leader approval, to finalise the intervention.

Before making this decision, a QDO or Team Leader should be satisfied that no further requests of the bank are necessary.
