FTB calculations + Read more ...

Check FTB Part A was calculated based on:

  • income test exempt for periods the customer was getting an income support payment (ISP)
  • income tested for periods the customer was not getting an ISP

For information about reconciliation for ISP customers, see Reconciliation of FTB.

On the FRCS screen for the relevant financial year:

  • 'S'elect the Reco/Re-reco completed line that relates to the compliance activity and earning correction. View the result on the FTB Calculation Result (FACRF) screen
  • 'S'elect a period to view more details for the reconciliation period. View the FTB Calculation Period Assessment (FCPA) screen
  • 'S'elect FTB(A) Status to view income, maintenance income, and Part A assessment details. View the FTB Calculation Period Assessment Part A (FCPAA) screen

On the FCPAA screen, check:

  • For ISP periods, FTB was calculated as one of:
    • Auto FTB: ISP Customer

    • Auto FTB: DVA Customer

To check each period, press [PF7/PF8] to page up/down through results.

Note: from 1 July 2018, employment income nil rate periods are not ISP periods. They are subject to an income test.

7

Check the reconciliation result is correct + Read more ...

FTB/CCF debts that were raised due to losing entitlement to an ISP will now be recalculated.

The reconciliation/re-reconciliation of FTB/CCB may result in:

  • a top-up
  • nil adjustment
  • a debt

Remediation activities reinstating the ISP status should result in a positive adjustment. If a further debt is being created, check the record further to see why FTB/CCB debts are increasing. If unsure of the outcome, seek help from QDO.

When CCB reconciles or re-reconciles, CCR may also re-calculate.

For more on the changes that can trigger reconciliation or re-reconciliation, see:

  • Reconciliation of FTB
  • Reconciliation of CCB for approved care
  • Enquiries about FTB reconciliation or lump sum claim
  • Re-reconciliation of FTB
  • Re-reconciliation of CCB
  • Previous partners and Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and Child Care Benefit (CCB) reconciliation

CCB and CCR payments can offset each other. There may be arrears for CCB and a debt for CCR and due to offsetting the overall balance will be the customer impact. This is because a higher CCB rate means less out of pocket expenses, so less CCR payable.

When reassessing reconciliation results for previous years, the calculation can raise current year debts and arrears for other payments. These need to be considered.

When completing the activity, the Service Officer is determining the result is correct. Do not complete the activity if unsure the result is correct.

Is the result on the AR screen correct?

  • Yes, and at Step 1 above the FAMIV screen:
    • displayed, see Manual intervention into family assistance reconciliation
    • did not display, this can confirm an accurate outcome or there is no impact on any previous reconciliation year/s. Finalise the activity. Go to Step 9
  • No, refer to QDO for help

8

Family Income Support Override (FISPO) screen + Read more ...

Due to system limitations, there is a need to code the FISPO screen. This is a limited access screen.

  • Note the period that needs to be corrected for the relevant financial year/s
  • Note the ISP payment the customer was eligible for

Refer the above details for coding to QDO.

QDO actions

Update the FISPO screen in the FAO system in Customer Record or Customer First:

  • Start Date: the date the ISP status needs to start from
  • End Date: the date the ISP status ends
  • System: depends on what payment the customer received. Choose from the following:
    • NSS - Newstart
    • PGA - Parenting Allowance or PPP
    • PEN - PPS or DSP
  • SrvRsn:
    • NSS will be NSA, JSP, YAL
    • PGA will be PPP
    • PEN will be PPS, DSP or AGE
  • Status: CUR (so the system reads ISP current)
  • Rsn: is not needed when making the system read current
  • Source: INT and Date of Receipt: today’s date

Go to the AR screen.

Is the ISP period reading correct in the reconciliation result?

  • Yes, refer back to the Service Officer and advise them to continue to Step 4 in Table 1
  • No, seek further support by submitting an enquiry via the CADO Service Desk

Step

Action

Check if eligible hours were affected when ISP cancelled + Read more ...

Depending on the income support payment (ISP) received, customers may have been eligible for an Automatic Activity Test Result (ATD) or an Activity Test Exemption for CCS.

Check if entitlement hours were affected by ISP cancellation + Read more ...

ISP cancellation may have impacted CCS reconciliation outcome or triggered a re-reconciliation. This will show as a corresponding instance window with a (roughly) coinciding date.

No further action is needed if the customer’s CCS hours were based on actual activity participation, giving them an ATD result equal to or greater than the automatic result or exemption. In this case the ISP cancellation had no impact.

For help, see:

  • Customer enquires about CCS reconciliation outcome, or
  • Resources on the Families and Child Care Peak and Reconciliation/Payment balancing entry page

Briefly unpack the reconciliation outcome relating to the ATD result in Process Direct.

  • Provider Payments tab > Adjustment Amount > Display Weekly Breakdown > Difference
    This may provide a snapshot of a changed ATD assessment, with the new ATD result in red
  • Activity History tab
    This will also indicate what activities the customer was participating in. It may help in deciding if the customer’s CCS hours were based on an auto result, or actual participation

Has the customer been left with less entitlement because of the ISP cancellation?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Loss of Automatic Activity Test Result (ATD) or Activity Test Exemption + Read more ...

Without a reconciliation dashboard outcome, use Customer Summary tables to confirm if the customer has lost an Automatic ATD or Exemption as a result of the ISP cancellation.

Go to the Customer Summary in Process Direct.

Toggle through Previous Periods on the CCS Entitlement and Activity Test twisties to check assessment details for the CCS year(s) in question. Note the eligible hours. This can be crosschecked with coding on the ATD table.

Have the customer’s eligible hours (either Automatic ATD result or Exemption) been lost as a result of the ISP cancellation?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, when documenting the record, note there is no adverse impact on CCS so no action was taken. Go to Step 5

4

Reinstate eligible hours + Read more ...

Go to the EXC table in Process Direct.

Select Add a New Row and key the following fields:

  • Type: CCS Activity Test Result
  • Start Date: the later of:
    • the date CCS was granted, or
    • the date the qualifying ISP was granted.
  • End Date: leave blank
  • Review Date: leave blank
  • Individual CRN: Customer CRN
  • Decision: Review on Secretary’s own initiative
  • Reason:
  • Text: Income Compliance Remediation
  • Value: enter the number of automatic hours the customer should receive according to the relevant ISP

Select Save.

Select Finish and Finalise.
