Customer disagrees with urgent payment decision

Step

Action

1

Has the customer had an urgent payment request assessed today? + Read more ...

This does not include an urgent payment denied via self service due to being a request for assistance with funeral expenses or due to Centrelink delay. In these cases, no request has been processed.

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, see
    • Urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances
    • Urgent payments due to Centrelink delay
    • Urgent payments due to expenses associated with a funeral

2

Customer has had an urgent payment request assessed today + Read more ...

Customers are only eligible for one request and one review of the original request per day. If both these actions have been taken, any further requests are to be refused.

Has the urgent payment request already been reviewed today?

  • Yes, as the customer has already had the decision reconsidered, no further actions can be taken. Advise the customer to contact on another day if they still need funds. Using the Immediate Payment requests script
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Urgent payment already received + Read more ...

Has the customer been issued an urgent payment today and requesting a new payment or reconsideration of the amount they were paid?

  • Yes, as the customer has already received an urgent payment, they cannot request another urgent payment on the same day. Their request is to be refused. Advise the customer they will need to make contact another day if they still need the funds. Using the Immediate Payment requests script on the Action to be Taken screen, select the Customer does not agree with an Urgent Payment decision option. Record the outcome of their reconsideration of the decision.
  • No,
    • If urgent payment is due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances, go to Step 4
    • If urgent payment is for expenses associated with a funeral, go to Step 5
    • If urgent payment is due to Centrelink delay/holiday processing, go to Step 6

4

Check the number of urgent payments in the last 12 months + Read more ...

Has the customer already received 2 urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen circumstances within the last 12 months?

Self service rejection DOCS will be in the Notes table in Process Direct. They do not detail why the request was refused.

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for any further urgent payments until the 12 months has passed, unless they are in severe financial hardship due to extraordinary circumstances. If they:
    • are not in severe financial hardship due to extraordinary circumstances, offer other assistance as customer is not eligible for a further urgent payment Using the Immediate Payment requests script, on the Action to be Taken screen, select the Customer does not agree with an Urgent Payment decision option. Record the outcome of their reconsideration of the decision.
    • are in severe financial hardship due to extraordinary circumstances, consider a further urgent payment. Return to Urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances and assess the customer’s request using the Extraordinary circumstance criteria. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Urgent payment due to expenses associated with a funeral + Read more ...

Why was urgent payment request rejected, for example:

  • Funeral could not be verified, request customer to provide more information, go to Step 7
  • Deceased is not ‘immediate family member’ of customer. Customer is not eligible for urgent payment for this reason, Using the Immediate Payment requests script, on the Action to be Taken screen, select the Customer does not agree with an Urgent Payment decision option. Record the outcome of their reconsideration of the decision.
  • Urgent payment would have placed customer in further financial hardship, if situation has not changed, customer is still not eligible. Using the Immediate Payment requests script, on the Action to be Taken screen, select the Customer does not agree with an Urgent Payment decision option. Record the outcome of their reconsideration of the decision.

6

Urgent payment due to Centrelink delay + Read more ...

Why was the customer not paid an urgent payment, for example:

  • Was the customer offered an urgent payment at time of contact? If no, go to Urgent payment due to Centrelink Delay and assess eligibility
  • Customer requested an urgent payment but was refused. Check reason for decision then go to Step 7

7

Customer requests a reconsideration of a decision + Read more ...

Advise the customer that the decision can only be changed if it did not comply with the guidelines or if new information is provided.

Reconsiderations should be undertaken by:

  • the Local Leadership Team in the service centre if the original decision was made by a service centre staff member
  • a Telephony Technical Peer Support (TPS) officer trained in urgent payment if the reconsideration is requested when the customer is advised the outcome of their initial request
  • an APS4 staff member skill tagged for urgent payment assessments if a customer requests a reconsideration of a previous request on the same day. This cannot be the staff member who processed the original urgent payment request
  • any Service Officer at the APS4 level or above trained in urgent payments if a request for an urgent payment has been rejected by the IVR or online service

Note: if the customer is making a second or subsequent request for reconsideration, this is an automatic rejection reason. For example, they had their original request rejected, and also had that decision reconsidered. Any contact after this on the same day is an automatic rejection. See Urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances.

Action for TPS/APS4/Team Leader/Service Support Officer

Has new information been provided or did the original decision not comply with the guidelines?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No,
    • Management and staff must show strong support for the decision by not changing or overriding it
    • Using the Immediate Payment requests script, the staff member must record the outcome of the reconsideration of the decision. On the Action to be Taken screen, select the Customer does not agree with an Urgent Payment decision option. Record the outcome of the reconsideration of the decision.

8

Reconsidering decision + Read more ...

Take into account the new information when reconsidering the original decision.

Using the Immediate Payment requests script, the TPS/APS4/Team Leader undertaking the request must record the outcome of their reconsideration of the decision. On the Action to be Taken screen, select the Customer does not agree with an Urgent Payment decision option. Record the outcome of their reconsideration of the decision.

Has the decision been changed?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No,
    • Advise the customer the outcome of the reconsideration of decision. Note: a customer is only entitled to 1 request and 1 reconsideration of the original request per day
    • If the customer is not satisfied with the decision and asks to be transferred to a supervisor, advise them they have had their only reconsideration for the day and they cannot be transferred to another person
    • If the customer is not satisfied with the level of service they have received during the urgent payment request process, they may be referred to a Team Leader or Service Support Officer, or make a complaint to be followed up by the Customer Relations Unit if the customer requests this
    • Ensure any supervisor calls and/or review of decision discussions are captured in the TPS Feedback where appropriate
    • Go to Step 10

9

The original decision has changed + Read more ...

The urgent payment should be issued by the staff member serving the customer.

The Immediate Payment Requests script will return the staff member to the Action Taken screen to record the new decision.

10

Offering other assistance + Read more ...

Consider referral to other assistance or appropriate specialist if the customer is not entitled to an urgent payment.
