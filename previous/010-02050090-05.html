Scenarios for ABSTUDY Additional Incidentals Allowance payment

Item

Description

1

Diploma course + Read more ...

Ali is studying a 2 year Diploma course and has lodged a claim for Additional Incidentals Allowance in semester one.

Ali has provided receipts for all purchases but has not had the Head of the Arts faculty of the education institution sign the claim to confirm that all the costs are essential course costs in order to undertake the Diploma course.

Ali is advised in writing that the claim cannot be approved without confirmation from the Head of the Arts faculty that the course costs are essential.

2

Certificate in Adult Education at the local TAFE + Read more ...

Patience is undertaking a 6 month Certificate in Adult Education at the local TAFE. Patience buys the books on the reading list for a total cost of $194.65.

Patience contacts to apply for Additional Incidentals Allowance for the cost of the books she has purchased.

Patience is advised as the Certificate in Adult Education is a secondary level course, Additional Incidentals Allowance has not been approved.

3

18 week Certificate in Information Technology course + Read more ...

Reggie has lodged an Additional Incidentals Allowance claim for an18 week Certificate in Information Technology course.

The head of the faculty has confirmed that all the items purchased are essential course costs for all students in order to complete the course. The essential course costs total $375.00.

Additional Incidentals Allowance is not approved for Reggie as the total of the course costs have not exceeded the Additional Incidentals prescribed amount for a 18 week course.

All essential course costs less than the relevant prescribed amount are to be covered by the Incidental Allowance payment for the course, and the student's own contribution to the course costs.

4

Bachelor of Medicine at Melbourne University course + Read more ...

Rex is undertaking a Bachelor of Medicine at Melbourne University.

Rex submits a claim for Additional Incidentals Allowance in semester one for a total of $2,100 in essential course costs. Full receipts are provided and all costs have been verified by the Head of Medicine as being essential to undertake the course. The amount of essential course costs in excess of the prescribed amount for a full year course is paid to Rex.

In semester two, Rex incurs additional essential course costs associated with some new subjects and lodges a fully verified claim for another $1,311. As the prescribed amount figure has already been exceeded for the year with the semester one claim, it does not need to be subtracted from the essential course cost figure claimed for semester two. The full amount of Additional Incidental Allowance claimed can be paid up to a maximum of $2,080.00 payable in the year.

5

Bachelor of Engineering course + Read more ...

Nikara is undertaking a Bachelor of Engineering at Newcastle University.

Nikara submits a claim for Additional Incidentals Allowance in semester one for a total of $1,919 in essential course costs. Full receipts are provided and all costs have been verified by the Head of Engineering as being essential to undertake the course. The amount of essential course costs in excess of the prescribed amount for a full year course is paid to Nikara.

During the semester vacation period Nikara decides to defer from the Bachelor course and enrol in a 14 week Electrical Engineering Diploma for semester two. Nikara incurs fully verified essential course costs of $1,108 for the Diploma course.

As this is a different course to the one which Additional Incidentals Allowance was claimed for in semester one, Nikara's essential course costs must exceed the prescribed amount for the 14 week course; with the amount in excess of the prescribed amount payable to Nikara.

However, the total payable for the year for both courses combined cannot exceed $2,080.00.

6

Course duration over multiple calendar years + Read more ...

James is undertaking an 80 week online Diploma course which started on 22 October.

James submits a claim for Additional Incidentals Allowance one month after commencing for a total of $345 in essential course costs. Full receipts are provided and all costs have been verified by the Head of Faculty as being essential to undertake the course.

To assess the Additional Incidentals Allowance claim correctly, the course period during the year of assistance needs to be determined. An 80 week online course starting on 22 October runs over 3 calendar years as follows:

  • Year 1 – 22 October to 31 December (10 weeks)
  • Year 2 – 1 January to 31 December (52 weeks)
  • Year 3 – 1 January to 7 May (18 weeks)

Therefore, the following enrolment periods are used to determine the applicable prescribed amount when assessing the Additional Incidentals Allowance claim:

  • Year 1 – less than 12 week course
  • Year 2 – 24 week to one year course
  • Year 3 – 17 to 23 week course

The Additional Incidentals Allowance claim lodged in year 1 is approved for James as the total essential course costs of $345, have exceeded the Additional Incidentals prescribed amount for a course of less than 12 weeks.

7

Additional Incidentals Allowance rate calculations + Read more ...

Note: the following examples use the rates and thresholds as at 20 March 2020.

To calculate the amount of Additional Incidentals Allowance payable for the applicable course, subtract the prescribed amount (based on the course period during the year of assistance) from the amount payable (total amount of the approved items). If the amount is greater than zero, this amount is payable (up to a maximum of $2,080pa). If the amount is less than zero, no amount is payable.

Below are examples of the Additional Incidentals Allowance calculations using the above scenarios. Prescribed amounts used to assess these claims are:

  • less than 12 weeks - $153.90
  • 12 to 16 weeks - $304.90
  • 17 to 23 weeks - $609.40
  • 24 weeks to 1 year - $1,220.60

Reggie

Reggie lodged an Additional Incidentals Allowance claim for an 18 week Certificate in Information Technology course for $375.

  • Total amount of the approved items: $375
  • Prescribed amount (based on 17 to 23 week course): $609.40
  • Amount payable = -$234.40. No amount payable as this is less than zero

Rex

Rex is undertaking a Bachelor of Medicine at Melbourne University. Rex submits a claim for Additional Incidentals Allowance in semester one for a total of $2,100.

  • Total amount of the approved items: $2,100
  • Prescribed amount (based on 24 week to one year course): $1,220.60
  • Amount payable = $879.40. Full amount payable as greater than zero and has not exceeded $2,080

Rex submits another claim for Additional Incidentals Allowance in semester two for a total of $1,311.

  • Total amount of the approved items: $1,311
  • Prescribed amount: $0 (As the prescribed amount for this course has already been exceeded in the semester one claim, no further prescribed amount is deducted).
  • Amount payable = $1,311. However, as the combined total of the two claims = $2,190.40 ($879.40 + $1,311), the Additional Incidentals Allowance limit payable in a year of $2,080 has been exceeded. Therefore, the amount must be amended so it falls within the allowable limit.
  • Adjusted amount payable = $1,200.60. ($2,080 - $879.40 semester one amount paid).
