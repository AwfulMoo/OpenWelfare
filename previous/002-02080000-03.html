Farm related changes + Read more ...

Changes relating to the customer's farm or farming business, for example ownership, business structure or level of involvement in the farm may affect the customer's eligibility for FHA.

  • If the customer wants more information about the possible effect of the change on their FHA payments, transfer to Farmer Assistance hotline. See Resources for a link
  • If the customer and partner are no longer farming for example, due to the sale of the farm:
    • FHA is to be cancelled, reason BLA (not a farmer) from the date they ceased to be a farmer. See Suspending, cancelling and restoring Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
    • Any outstanding 2 or 6 weekly reports will need to be completed
  • For all other farm related changes:
    • Record details of the changes in an ACTDOC and transfer to region code FHH

Income or asset changes + Read more ...

FHA customer can update their income and assets details and view existing business details through the Manage Income and Assets option online. A work item is created as a Change of Circumstance (CoC) interaction activity and allocated to staff with FHA skills tags to process.

If a work item is allocated, a Service Officer can process the activity in Process Direct. Extra document(s) can be requested using the Request for Information function in the CoC interaction in PD

If all details cannot be updated within PD, Service Officer must cancel the CoC interaction activity in PD, code income and assets changes using the legacy screens in Customer First and DOC the record.

For coding assistance, see:

  • Assessing assets for Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
  • Assessing income for Farm Household Allowance (FHA)

The Social Service Plan (SSP) will generate as part of the activity. Finalising the activity will complete and approve the SSP.

Business Income Estimates update + Read more ...

See Business income estimates for Farm Household Allowance (FHA).

6

Overseas travel + Read more ...

See Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customers going overseas.

7

Entering permanent care + Read more ...

If the farmer enters permanent care:

  • They are unable to farm and so are no longer eligible for FHA because they cannot contribute significant labour. FHA is to be cancelled, reason BLA (not a farmer)

If the partner of the farmer enters permanent care:

  • FHA is to be cancelled, reason FAT (not meeting activity test). See Suspending, cancelling and restoring Farm Household Allowance (FHA)

For partnered customers:

  • Illness separated rates are applicable to FHA customers. See Assessment of circumstances for a couple separated due to illness

If the partner of a farmer:

  • Has taken over the farming responsibilities, they can continue to receive FHA as a farmer. Go to Step 10.
  • Has not taken over farming responsibilities, they will no longer be eligible for FHA. Cancel FHA using reason NPF (Not a partner of a farmer). See Suspending, cancelling and restoring Farm Household Allowance (FHA)

If the farmer and/or partner are no longer farming, they may wish to test their eligibility for another payment. Discuss alternate payment options with the customer and/or partner. Record details of discussion on a DOC.

8

Prison or psychiatric confinement + Read more ...

An FHA customer, whether a farmer or the partner of a farmer, is not eligible for FHA while in prison or psychiatric confinement.

If a farmer is confined and they have a partner who receives FHA as:

  • A farmer, the partner will continue to be eligible for FHA if no other circumstances have changed
  • The partner of a farmer, the partner may continue to receive FHA for a grace period of 14 weeks starting from the date of confinement. If the partner advises they have taken over farming responsibilities, they may be eligible for FHA as a farmer in their own right

Action

Send an ACTDOC to the relevant state/local Prison Servicing team for follow up with authorities about incarceration dates and actions to the record. For the details to be included in the ACTDOC and Prison Servicing team contacts, see Admission process for incarcerated customers.

Assess and record details.

FHA for the confined person must be manually cancelled, reason IMP (in gaol) or PSY (psychiatric confinement). See Suspending, cancelling and restoring Farm Household Allowance.

Upon release from incarceration or psychiatric confinement, the customer must lodge a new claim for FHA, this is regardless of the length of time they were confined.

For partnered customers:

Go to Marital Status (MS) screen.

  • In Event Date field, enter the date entered prison or psychiatric confinement
  • Marital Status field will remain as MAR, REG or DEF
  • Enter/update partner permitted to enquire status (if required)
  • In Qualifier Code field, key appropriate code:
    • If the customer is confined, GAC (in gaol) or PSC (in psychiatric care)
    • If their partner is confined, GAP (partner in gaol) or PSP (partner in psychiatric care)
  • Enter Source and DOR fields and press [Enter]

The Social Service Plan (SSP) will generate as part of the activity. Finalising the activity will complete and approve the SSP.

  • If their partner receives FHA as the partner of a farmer, the partner can continue to receive FHA for a grace period of up to 14 weeks:
  • Create a manual follow up to review the customer's circumstance 14 weeks from the date of event
  • After 14 weeks, FHA will be cancelled, reason PTG or PTP. The 14 weeks count towards the customer’s cumulative total of 1460 days on payment. Note: the grace period rules for imprisonment and psychiatric confinement do not overrule the 1460 day rule for FHA eligibility

If the partner advises they have taken over the farming responsibilities in the farmer’s absence, they can continue to receive FHA as a farmer, go to Step 10

9

Relationship status + Read more ...

If a farmer and their partner cease to be a couple (including due to the death of the farmer):

  • A customer receiving FHA as a farmer may continue to receive FHA if they still meet the farmer eligibility criteria
  • A customer receiving FHA as the partner of a farmer may continue to receive FHA for a grace period of 14 weeks starting from the date the relationship ends. They may be eligible to receive FHA as a farmer if they have taken over farm responsibilities

Update customer details. For help, see:

  • Change in relationship status from partnered to single
  • Death of a Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customer

If a farmer becomes partnered:

  • The farmer may continue to be eligible for FHA
  • Their partner may be eligible for FHA as a farmer or as the partner of a farmer. Invite claim for FHA

Update customer details. For help, see:

  • Change in relationship status from single to partnered

Before the grace period is applied for the partner of a farmer, their eligibility for FHA as a farmer in their own right should be assessed, go to Step 10

10

Reassess eligibility for FHA as a farmer + Read more ...

A customer receiving FHA as the partner of a farmer may be eligible for FHA as a farmer if they meet farmer eligibility criteria.

They do not need to submit a claim. A reassessment will be completed based on the customer's circumstances.

Note: the total cumulative period for FHA is 4 years in specified 10 year periods, regardless of the farmer/partner status of the customer. The clock will not reset if their status changes.

Record details on an ACTDOC and transfer to region code FHH.

Tell the customer they will receive a letter advising any changes to their FHA entitlement. Smart Centre Rural Processing staff will contact the customer if further information is required.

Assess eligibility for FHA as a farmer.

Use the FHA Farmer Specific Data guided procedure to record details.

11

Check for an outstanding Change of Circumstance interaction activity + Read more ...

Following the completion of a guided procedure there may be an incomplete Change of Circumstance (CoC) interaction activity needing manual action.

Search for CoC interaction activities, from the Customer Summary page:

  • Select the Cases tab
  • Select Super Case for Business Partner
  • Select Activities tab

If the customer does not have a Super Case for Business Partner, CoC interaction activities can be located:

  • From the Interaction History tab in Customer First
  • By searching the customer's CRN in Work Optimiser

Is there an incomplete CoC interaction activity?

  • Yes, action the activity. Select:
    • The customer's name in the Description field
    • Edit
    • Completed from the status drop down list
    • Save to finalise and apply the updates to the customer's record
  • No, go to Table 2

Reassess SSP + Read more ...

To create a reassessment SSP:

  • Select Change Processes from header
  • On Select Change Process pop-up, select SSP Reassessment
  • On Enter Data Required screen, enter the new date in the Date of Effect field
  • On Check Decision Units screen, select the applicable decision unit, for example:
    • farmer if customer qualifies as a farmer
    • non-farmer if they qualify as a partner
  • On Verify and Execute Change Process screen, select Execute
  • At top of pop-up window select Save and Go to Follow Up

This will create a new SSP.

Go to Step 3.

Review and approve new SSP + Read more ...

Select the new SSP.

Note: where the SSP displays ‘PD SSP30000000000’ and has not been finalised, complete the activity in Process Direct. It cannot be finalised in Customer First.

On the Services Plan screen, check the Assessment Status is Determined and the Next Decision field is blank.

Review and approve SSP.

If the result is incorrect after undoing the assessment, refer the record for further investigation.

Go to Step 4.

Record details of the reassessment on a DOC.
