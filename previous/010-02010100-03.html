This page contains additional information to assist with completing the Claim for ABSTUDY Student or Australian Apprentice in Lawful Custody (SY022).

Complex sections of the SY022

Item

Description

1

Customer details + Read more ...

Aboriginality

  • for ABSTUDY purposes, an Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander is someone who:
    • is of Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander descent
    • identifies themselves as an Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander, and
    • is accepted as such by the community in which they live
  • the declaration on the claim form will normally suffice, however for more information, see Aboriginality or Torres Strait Islander status for ABSTUDY purposes

2

Study or Australian Apprenticeship details + Read more ...

Students and Australian Apprentices must provide the name and contact details for their study or Australian Apprenticeship coordinator.

Student details

If the customer is a student they must provide full details of their course. They may also be required to provide Proof of enrolment. See Proof of enrolment (POE) in a course of study.

Australian Apprenticeship details

If the customer is an Australian Apprentice, they must provide details of their Australian Apprenticeship including:

  • date their Australian Apprenticeship started, and
  • expected end date of their Australian Apprenticeship

Services Australia will then confirm this information with the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations.

Note: the customer must be a full-time Australian Apprentice.

3

Tax details + Read more ...

Tax File Number (TFN)

  • a TFN declaration must be made by the student or Australian Apprentice
  • customers who do not have, or know their TFN, must be given a Tax File Number application or enquiry for an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander (NAT1589) form for completion and lodgement within 14 days of lodging their claim
  • see Tax File Number (TFN) for ABSTUDY for further information

4

Declaration + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice must sign the declaration in order for their claim to be accepted and determined. The signature must be signed in blue or black pen. It is not acceptable to use electronic signatures, for example 'Paint'.
