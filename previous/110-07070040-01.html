This document outlines the process for Service Officers in Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Smart Centres about assessing and processing Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) End of Year (EOY) circumstance review forms.

Review forms issued

EOY review forms for the AIC Scheme are tailored to the individual student's circumstances. Review forms are sent to the applicant in the last week in October. AIC students must confirm their isolation conditions, education details and living arrangements for the following year, to determine their eligibility for continuing payments.

The completed review forms are returned to the Student Services postal address or by fax. The form is then scanned to the student's record and its receipt date recorded.

Lodgement of EOY review forms

The date of receipt of the scanned review forms in the AIC Smart Centre is recorded and sorted into three groups:

  • Group 15: update education (NYR) details only is used where no expected changes or supporting documents required
  • Group 16: complex (CMX) assessment required is used where expected changes and supporting documents is required as the child moving from primary to secondary
  • Group 17: where the applicant is no longer entitled to benefits and parental income may be required for Additional Boarding Allowance to be paid

Failure to return review form

When the form is not returned by 31 December of the year the form was sent, payments are automatically cancelled from 1 January.

Payments can be restored and backdated if the form is received by 31 December for the year the applicant is reclaiming. The form cannot be accepted if not received by this date. AIC is not payable for that year.

Note: on 1 July 2018, the intent to claim provisions were repealed for AIC customers. This does not change the existing backdating provisions for AIC.
