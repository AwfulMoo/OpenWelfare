Disability assessment tools (CLK) 009-05000000

Expand all


  • Background
  • References

This document outlines how Disability Assessment Tools are used to reduce subjectivity in the assessment process, and to help determine whether a care receiver may qualify a carer for Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA).

Government intent

Carer payments are intended to provide income support and assistance for people who provide a substantial level of care for care receivers who have a disability or medical condition. To measure the level of care, tools are used to assess the amount of help the care receiver needs to undertake basic activities of daily living such as mobility, communication, hygiene, eating and management in a range of cognitive and behavioural areas.

These tools enable a weighted score to be calculated to determine that the care receiver is a person with a disability or severe medical condition which results in the need for a significant level of care and therefore meets the definition of a disabled person.

On this Page:

Disability assessment tools

The Adult Disability Assessment Tool (ADAT) is used to assess the disability and care needs of CP and/or CA (adult) care receivers 16 years and over. The ADAT contains a carer questionnaire and a Treating Health Professional (THP) questionnaire about the amount of help the care receiver needs to undertake basic activities of daily living such as mobility, communication, hygiene, eating and management in a range of cognitive and behavioural areas.

The Disability Care Load Assessment Determination (DCLAD) 2010 is used to assess the disability and care needs of CP and CA (child) care receivers aged under 16. The DCLAD contains the List of Recognised Disabilities, a carer questionnaire (CNA) and a THP questionnaire. A score or qualifying rating derived from the answers to the questionnaires are used as part of the determination of whether the care receiver qualifies a carer for payment.

Before the introduction of the DCLAD 2009, the Child Disability Assessment Tool (CDAT) was used to assess the disability and functional ability of CA (child) care receivers aged under 16. The CDAT used the Functional Assessment and/or THP medical report. The DCLAD 2009 assessed the disability and care needs of CP and CA (child) care receivers aged under 16. The DCLAD (2009) used the THP medical reports and the Care Needs Assessment (CNA). DCLAD 2010 was introduced on 1 July 2010 and replaced DCLAD 2009.

Re-use and re-claim provisions

Re-use provisions allow eligible Treating Health Professional (THP) medical reports and/or Care Needs Assessments (CNAs) previously supplied to be used for a subsequent claim. The online claim and First Contact Concessions, Carers and Seniors (FCCCS) workflow will identify the option to re-use this data if the most recent reports meet the re-use provisions.

Re-claim provisions allow qualifying THP medical reports and CNAs previously supplied to be used for a subsequent claim. Re-claim provisions are automatically assessed when a carer claims online or FCCCS is run.
