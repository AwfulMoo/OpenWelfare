Customer lodges a request with AAT to review an objection decision

This table describes the steps involved when a customer requests AAT review a Child Support objection decision. Where the AAT is unsure of the type of Child Support Objection decision involved they will contact Child Support via email to obtain the required information.

Step

Action

1

Request for applicant details from AAT + Read more ...

When the AAT receives an application for review they will email the relevant Appeals Support Team (AST) mailbox with a request for the applicant's details.

The following 2 emails may be received from the AAT:

  • Record of Application – advising Child Support that a customer has requested a review to commence
  • Request for Applicant Details – Request to Child Support to provide to the AAT:
    • the date of notification of the objection decision (or decision to refuse an extension of time to lodge an objection)
    • how that decision was notified (electronic or post)
    • any details of the applicant that are missing or unknown in the AAT records
    • a copy of the objection decision, and
    • details of the other party(s) to the review (or other persons who may be affected by the decision under review)

Both of these emails will include the applicant name, address, date of birth, CSRN, review number, for example 2016MC001234, objection decision relevant to the request, Child Support decision dates and registry location.

The ‘Request for Applicant details’ email will include:

  • an electronic copy of the appeal application, and
  • any documents provided by the customer at the time of lodgement

2

Appeal Support Officer (ASO) action when emails received from AAT + Read more ...

The Appeal Support Officer (ASO) managing the AST mailbox will:

  • identify the customer (if the customer cannot be identified contact the AAT for more information)
  • enter a record of the application for review in the AAT database on the AAT shared drive
  • add a document to the applicant’s Communication window including:
    • Subject heading – Request for applicant details
    • Reason: Objection
    • Category: SSAT
    • Issue: Other
  • copy both the Record of application and Request for applicant details email to the Communication window notepad, noting that it is an exact copy of the original and the number of attachments included in the email.

See Documentation Cuba Process Help.

Note: applications for review from RACS customers must be processed by an ASO with the appropriate RACS access level.

3

Identify relevant objection decision (ASO) + Read more ...

Use the information provided by the AAT to examine recently made decisions and determine if there is an objection decision relating to the review.

No relevant objection identified

If no relevant objection decision is located:

  • complete the AAT Initial information received AAT template and email this to the AAT
  • record the email sent to the AAT in the applicant’s Communication window by adding a new notepad with a heading of ‘Response to request for details’, and
  • update the AAT database

If the AAT provide further information that allows a relevant objection decision to be identified continue on with Relevant objection decision identified. If no additional information is provided, no further action is required.

Relevant objection decision identified

If a relevant objection decision is located:

  • Complete the Initial response to AAT request for applicant details template and email it along with a copy of the relevant objection decision to the AAT. Note: this must be completed within 3 working days of notification to the Registrar
  • record the email sent to the AAT in the applicant's Communication window by adding a new notepad, with the heading 'Response to request for Applicant details', and
  • update the AAT database

See Documenting Child Support information in Cuba.

If either parent is on Tracing, notify the AAT by email and undertake customer location activities. See Customer location (Tracing).

No further action is required until either:

  • the AAT notify the AST they are considering an application for an extension of time for review, go to Step 4
  • the AAT notify the AST that the review will not continue, go to Step 5, or
  • the AAT notify the AST that the review will continue, go to Step 6

4

AAT advise they are considering an application for extension of time for review (AAT EOT application) + Read more ...

If the AAT advises that they are considering an application for extension of time for review (AAT EOT application), go to file Extension of time (EOT) review lodged against Child Support decisions and see the AAT Request for Documents – subsection 93(2) table.

Note: while the AAT are still considering the AAT EOT application, the actual review of the Child Support objection decision has not yet begun. Once they have finalised the AAT EOT application, the AAT will advise by email if the review of the child support objection decision is to continue and if so, they will provide a new review number for the continuing review.

If the AAT advise that the AAT EOT application has been:

  • refused, go to Step 5
  • granted, go to Step 6

5

Review not to continue + Read more ...

The AAT will inform AST by email, when they finalise a review that cannot proceed because:

  • no relevant objection exists
  • the application for review is out of time
  • the AAT does not have jurisdiction to review the objection decision, or
  • the AAT has rejected a customer’s AAT EOT application

Document the information in the applicant’s Communication window by:

  • adding a new notepad to the existing ‘Request for applicant details’ notepad using the heading ‘Review has been dismissed’. Copy the email text into the notepad, noting that it is an exact copy of the original, and
  • finalising the review in the AAT database

No further action is required.

6

AAT advise if review is to continue + Read more ...

The AAT will advise by email if the review is to continue.

If the review will continue, but the decision being reviewed is a Child Support refusal of an extension of time to object (EOT refusal), go to file Extension of time review lodged against Child Support decisions and see the AAT Request for statement table.

If the review will continue and an ordinary objection decision is being reviewed, go to Step 7.

7

Review to continue + Read more ...

The AAT will email a 'Request for statement' to the AST mailbox as confirmation that the review will continue.

The 'Request for statement' will request documents that:

  • give reasons for the decision
  • set out the findings of fact, and/or
  • refer to the evidence on which the findings were based

Note: once the AAT sends a Request for statement, Child Support must provide all documents to the AAT within 28 days of the date of the email.

Go to Step 8.

8

Create review record + Read more ...

AST mailbox administrator will:

  • Create a new record on the SSAT Maintenance window and 'link' the objection decision to the AAT review. Creating a new SSAT Maintenance record in Cuba will automatically generate an SSAT Review Lodged intray
  • Document the 'request for statement' on the SSAT Maintenance window including the email text, and
  • Update the AAT appeals database

Creating a new AAT Maintenance record in Cuba will automatically generate a SSAT Review Lodged intray.

9

Update the Care Indicator + Read more ...

If the review relates to a care decision, with a from-date of 1 July 2010 or later, update the care indicator.

Navigate to the relevant care record/s in the Care window. Update the Care Objection Status in the bottom right hand corner to In Progress.

Unable to link appeal to an objection

In some situations, Cuba may not allow the appeal to be linked to the relevant care period. If this occurs, record a notepad in the customer communication window using the following text:

Subject: Unable to link appeal to objection

Text: Due to issues with processing care in CUBA we are not able to link care records to create the SSAT appeal registration. Please see Customer Communications window SSAT ‘electronic in’ – dated [XX/X/XXXX] for all SSAT information for appeal [appeal number].

10

Disbursement suspension + Read more ...

If the review relates to the validity of the assessment and relies on 1 of the following grounds:

  • the payer was not an Australian resident
  • the child is not an eligible child, or
  • the applicant was not entitled to make an application for the child

the payer must be contacted by phone and informed of their right to apply for the payee's disbursements to be suspended. The payer can then request suspension of disbursement of monies to the payee.

This suspension request will not always be granted. See Suspending disbursements of child support.

11

AAT File Management + Read more ...

See Management of files sent to the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT).
