Improved community access under Highly Specialised Drugs Program

Prescription forms in public hospitals

Prescription forms used in participating and non-participating public hospitals

Table 1: This table describes the prescription forms used in participating and non-participating public hospitals.

Hospital type

Forms used

Participating public hospitals

Current prescription forms approved by Services Australia.

Non-participating public hospitals HSD approved
  • Existing internal medication forms when prescribing Authority required (STREAMLINED) HSD for the listed quantities and repeats or less. Note: these forms must contain the minimum information as defined under section 40 of the National Health (Pharmaceutical Benefits) Regulations 2017. See Prescriptions and Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS).
  • Medicare Australia Authority prescription form when prescribing increased quantities and repeats and for all Complex Authority Required (CAR) medicines. Note: these Authority prescription forms are unique to non-participating public hospitals and contain wording similar to: 'PUBLIC HOSPITAL HIGHLY SPECIALISED DRUG – ONLY DISPENSE IN A PUBLIC HOSPITAL'.

Scenario – prescriber requests Authority approval for HSD prescription

Scenario of prescriber requesting Renagel tablets 800mg

Table 2: This table outlines questions and answers for the situation where a prescriber requests Renagel tablets 800mg.

Item

Question

1

How do Service Officers determine which listing is required?

Ask which of the following listing is required:

  • public hospital
  • private hospital
  • s85 listing

2

Why is it important to determine which listing is required?

If the selection of listing is:

  • correct: it ensures the accuracy of data recorded on the Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) Mainframe
  • incorrect: it raises dispensing problems with a warning displayed instructing the pharmacist to call the PBS General Enquiries line

Scenario – community pharmacy claims HSD prescription

Scenario of community pharmacy claiming public hospital HSD prescription

Table 3: This table outlines questions and answers for the situation where a community pharmacy claims a public hospital Highly Specialised Drugs (HSD) prescription using the private hospital item code, and pharmacy receives an Online Claiming for PBS (PBSOL) rejection code.

Item

Question

1

What is the prescription form type?

Ask the pharmacy what is the prescription form type to identify whether the prescription comes from a public or private hospital.

Note: public hospital prescriptions can be identified by:

  • 10 item prescription form (reformed)
  • Authority prescription form bearing ‘PUBLIC HOSPITAL HIGHLY SPECIALISED DRUG - ONLY DISPENSE IN A PUBLIC HOSPITAL’ or similar (non-reformed)

2

What if it is a Public hospital prescription (PBS reform or non-reform)?

Determine if the prescription is for Complex Authority Required (CAR) HSD or trastuzumab (Herceptin®) with approval from the Complex Drugs Branch in Tasmania. If yes, advise the pharmacy they can dispense and claim the public hospital HSD.

Is the community pharmacy acting as an agent for the public hospital?

  • Yes, advise the pharmacy they may dispense the public hospital HSD, however they cannot claim for the item. The public hospital HSD must be claimed on the PBS from the public hospital pharmacy. The community pharmacy acting as an agent must have an arrangement for reimbursement with the public hospital.
  • No, advise the pharmacy to direct the patient to the public hospital to have the prescription dispensed.

3

What if it is a Private hospital prescription?

Approval using the public hospital item code was incorrect.

Advise the pharmacist to claim the prescription as usual.

Note:

  • Escalate enquiry to a Pharmaceutical Adviser (PA) if required. See Pharmaceutical Advisers (PA) in Services Australia.
  • If the prescription is a Streamlined, no record is on the Mainframe.

4

What if it is a pharmacy claim?

Reject the prescription.

Direct the pharmacy to the hospital or State Health Department for further information.

Note: details of rejected prescriptions must be escalated to a Pharmaceutical Advisor for contact with pharmacy. See Pharmaceutical Advisers (PA) in Services Australia.
