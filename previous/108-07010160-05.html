Examples of commencement and cancellation of employment income nil rate period

Table 1: This table describes examples of commencement dates for the employment income nil rate period and when an employment income nil rate is cancelled due to loss of qualifications.

Item

Description

1

Customer's rate reduces to nil part way through an entitlement period

Employment income nil rate periods start from the Entitlement Period Start Day (EPSD) of the first fortnight where a customer would get a zero payment for that entitlement period.

The system works on a daily rate and often determines that the customer's rate is nil (on a daily basis) part way through an entitlement period. This can be because the customer's Working Credits run out part way through the period.

For example, a customer may be paid a part payment in an entitlement period because their Working Credits do not deplete until day five of the period. The 6 full fortnights of the employment income nil rate period actually start from the EPSD of the fortnight after the customer's rate goes to zero on a daily basis.

That means:

  • the customer will be paid a part rate for the first 5 days
  • they will be on a nil rate of payment from day 6 of the first entitlement period and the employment income nil rate period starts from the EPSD of the next fortnight and continues for 6 full fortnights

2

DSP customer employed at or above relevant minimum wage and working over 30 hours or more per week

Customer is no longer entitled to DSP as they are employed for 30 hours or more per week at or above the relevant minimum wage. There is no entitlement to the employment income nil rate period. Payment is suspended for 2 years when the Working Credit balance has depleted to zero.

3

DSP customer employed at or above relevant minimum wage and working less than 30 hours per week

If the customer's income is sufficient to give them a zero rate but the customer is working less than 30 hours per week, the customer and their partner will be entitled to deplete their Working Credits and then will remain current at zero rate for the employment income nil rate period.

Once the employment income nil rate period has finished, both pensions will be suspended for up to 2 years or until the income goes down and they are entitled to some payment again.

If the customer/partner has income coded as income in one period (IOP), and reports their last fortnight income late, the payment will not suspend due to system limitation.

Check the Payment Summary (PS) screen, to see if the customer has had 6 fortnights of nil payment. If so, code the income from the 7th fortnight and, if payment has not suspended automatically, follow current procedures to suspend payment manually from the EPSD of the 7th fortnight.

4

DSP customer employed at or above relevant minimum wage and working over 30 hours or more per week has partner on JobSeeker Payment (JSP) paid at the Wife Pension (WP) transitional rate

The customer is no longer entitled to DSP as they are employed for 30 hours or more per week at or above the relevant minimum wage. There is no entitlement to the employment income nil rate period. Payment is suspended for up to 2 years when the Working Credit balance is zero.

JSP customers are only eligible to be paid at the WP transitional rate as long as their partner is current. If eligible, the transitional WP rate customer may continue to be paid JSP, but at the ordinary rate of payment only. If no longer eligible for JSP, they will:

  • be able to deplete their Working Credits at the same time as the DSP customer, and
  • then be suspended for the same period as the DSP customer

Note: once a JSP customer stops to meet the provisions for payment at the transitional WP rate, they can no longer return to this rate of payment. Future changes of circumstances to impact upon this.

5

Parenting Payment (PP) customers

  • Customers are no longer entitled to PP and therefore their entitlement to the employment income nil rate period stops when their youngest dependent child turns:
    • 6 years if the customer gets PP partnered
    • 8 years if the customer gets PP single
  • For PP customers, an employment income nil rate period can continue if their relationship status changes (transfers between PP single and PP partnered and vice versa)
  • However, an employment income nil rate period ends if the customer loses qualification for their payment and transfers to another income support payment, for example, a PP single customer's youngest child turns 8 and they transfer to JobSeeker Payment (JSP)

Note: PPP customers of Age Pension age are not entitled to employment income nil rate period.

If payment has not suspended automatically, follow current procedures to cancel payment manually from the EPSD of the 7th fortnight.

6

JSP customers employment is now permanent full time and the customer reports employment income

JSP customers are not qualified for payment if they start permanent full time employment and are not entitled to the employment income nil rate period. Customers who have lost qualification to payment due to employment, are however treated as being qualified for the period it takes to deplete all their Working Credits, before cancellation. Once their Working Credit balance has been depleted and they are not entitled to get a part-rate of payment, their payment will cancel.

Note: this does not apply to JSP or YA (Other) customers who are employed under the Youth Bonus Wage Subsidy (YBWS) initiative. These customers will automatically suspend for 26 weeks instead of cancelling.

7

JSP customer starts permanent full time employment, partner is also receiving a payment in their own right, for example, Youth Allowance or Parenting Payment

JSP customer's payment will cancel as soon as Working Credits are depleted, as they are not qualified for payment if they start permanent full time employment.

If the income is sufficient for the partner's payment to reduce to zero, they will be entitled to the employment income nil rate period before cancelling.

Note: these do not apply to JSP or YA (Other) customers who are employed under the Youth Bonus Wage Subsidy (YBWS) initiative. These customers will automatically suspend for 26 weeks instead of cancelling.

If the income does not reduce their payment to zero, they will remain current receiving a partial rate.

8

Customer reaches Age Pension age

The employment nil rate period applies to all Age Pension age customers on a pension payment.

A pension customer's payment on an employment nil rate period will be deemed as suspended. Their pension payment will restore automatically when the employment income is updated and it is below the thresholds. There is no need to manually restore the payment.

If the customer is currently on the 13 week nil rate suspension period when they transfer to Age Pension this continues:

  • until the customer's payment is restored, or
  • when cancellation occurs at the end of the 13 week period

9

Income support customer entitled to add-ons

If a customer is entitled to the following supplementary payments:

  • Work for the Dole Supplement
  • Pensioner Education Supplement
  • Telephone Allowance (TAL) (if receiving TAL before the employment income nil rate period), and
  • Language, Literacy and Numeracy Supplement (LLNS)

For example if a student receiving Youth Allowance (YA):

  • reports fortnightly employment income that reduces their basic YA rate to nil, and
  • is not enough to reduce entitlement to any one of the above supplementary payments to nil
  • they will enter an employment income nil rate period from that fortnight, and
  • they will still get a payment that only includes one of the above supplementary payments

Action needed due to system limitations

Table 2: This table describes the action needed to overcome system limitations about the employment income nil rate period for DSP, Age Pension or PP customers when payments are not automatically suspending/cancelling after the employment income nil rate period.

Item

Description

1

System limitations

Currently there is a system limitation which means that when a DSP, AGE or PP customer/partner is due to suspend/cancel due to employment income precluded for 6 nil fortnights and they have income coded as income in one period (IOP), the payment will not be suspended/cancelled if the customer/partner reports their income late.

In all instances, follow current procedures to suspend/cancel payment manually from the EPSD of the 7th fortnight.

2

DSP or Age Pension due to suspend after 6 fortnights at nil rate

Check the Payment Summary (PS) screen to see if the customer has had 6 fortnights of nil payment. If so, and payment has not suspended/cancelled automatically, follow current procedures to suspend/cancel payment manually from the EPSD of the 7th fortnight.

A display on access (DOA) DOC should be created warning Service Officers of the system limitation of DSP or AGE not cancelling correctly with reason 'E6F' when income precludes payment on their 6th and final fortnight of the 'nil rate period'.

To action a DSP cancellation, see:

  • Cancellation of payments - Disability Support Pension, and/or
  • Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Disability Support Pension

To action an Age Pension cancellation, see:

  • Cancellation of Age Pension, and/or
  • Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Age Pension

3

Parenting Payment due to cancel after 6 fortnights at nil rate

For Parenting Partnered (PPP) and Parenting Payment Single (PPS), check the Payment Summary (PS) screen to see if the customer:

  • is on 6 fortnights of nil payment, and
  • has employment income of IOP

A display on access DOC should be created warning Service Officers of the system limitation of Parenting Payment (PP) not cancelling correctly with reason 'E6F' when income precludes payment on their 6th and final fortnight of the 'nil rate period'.

If the customer/partner reports for the 7th fortnight and income is too high for payment, follow current procedures to suspend payment manually from the EPSD of the 7th fortnight.

For help to action a cancellation, see:

  • Cancellation of payments Parenting Payment (PP), and/or
  • Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Parenting Payment
