5

Customer does not want to pursue claim + Read more ...

A claim for CrP-EXO is not pursued where the customer identified they have incorrectly enquired about CrP.

In all cases when the CrP is not pursued:

  • Explain to the customer there are no review or appeal rights when a claim is not pursued as it has not been formally assessed and determined
  • Clearly DOC the discussion on the customer’s record
  • Discuss alternative assistance and offer the customer the chance to test eligibility for an Urgent Payment or Advance Payment
  • It is only appropriate to withdraw claims after the customer advised/agreed they no longer want to pursue the claim

Procedure ends here.

Confirm eligibility criteria - forced to leave home and establish a new one + Read more ...

A customer cannot get a CrP-EXO if they:

  • intentionally brought about the extreme circumstance with a view to getting a payment of CrP
  • were evicted from their home due to not paying their mortgage or rent
  • had to move out because their landlord is selling the property or moving back in
  • decided to move to another home
  • are living in government housing and have applied for a transfer to a different location, but are still living at the current location or intend to return while they await the transfer (they have not established a new home)
  • received a disaster relief payment administered by the local, State or Commonwealth Government for the same extreme circumstance

Note: if the AGDRP or DRP (or any other disaster relief payment) is announced as being available:

  • Crisis Payment is only payable to people who lodged a claim for CrP before the day of the announcement of the disaster relief payment, provided they meet all other eligibility conditions.
  • Customers who have been granted CrP before the announcement could also test their eligibility for a disaster relief payment.

Some common reasons a customer may claim and be eligible for a CrP-EXO include:

  • a house fire
  • flooding (for example, due to a burst water main or random flooding event not covered by a disaster relief payment)
  • severe structural damage
  • where the customer feels unsafe (for example, due to community violence or repeated break-ins/burglaries)

To be eligible, the customer must also have established a new home or intend to.

Has the customer been forced to leave their home due to an extreme circumstance and establish a new home?

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, customer is not eligible
    • See the Resources page for a list of rejection reasons and codes
    • Select the most appropriate rejection
    • Update the manual rejection field with the appropriate rejection reason
    • Select Assess and then Next
    • Go to Step 12

Payment of Crisis Payment + Read more ...

Payment should be made via the New Payments Platform (NPP).

If NPP is unavailable, payment should be made by Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS).

  • The system will approve this within 15 minutes. To check if the SSP has been approved, see Navigating to SSP
  • Once approved, pay the Crisis Payment via RTGS. Otherwise, the payment will deliver via direct credit the following business day

  • REJ-PEC, go to Step 13
  • Grant, or reject for reason other than PEC, go to Step 14

13

Check for Social Work notes when ELD screen displays REJ-PEC + Read more ...

If the ELD screen is displaying REJ-PEC, this may mean that a CrP-EXO or CrP-EXV claim has previously been granted for the same event date. Check the Notes and DOCs for a Social work GRANT Fast Note.

Is there a GRANT Fast Note?

  • Yes, consult with social worker. If the EXO claim is for
    • a different incident, override the rejection using the Manual grant indicator on the CRISIS_EXO screen. Go to Step 15
    • the same extreme circumstance. The system will automatically reject upon finalisation. Go to Step 15
  • No, go to Step 14

14

Finalising the claim + Read more ...

  • Select Finish
  • Review the prepopulated Claim Finalisation DOC and add any additional information if needed
  • Select Finalise
  • A letter will be sent to the customer notifying them of the outcome
  • If the claim is selected for QMA, add text to the DOC:
    "Crisis Payment should be paid via NPP - QMO to action after grant."

Has the claim been granted?

  • Yes, go to Step 15
  • No, go to Step 16

15

Payment of Crisis Payment + Read more ...

Payment should be made via the New Payments Platform (NPP).

If NPP is unavailable, payment should be made by Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS).

Direct credit can also be used if neither NPP or RTGS is available.

For Farm Household Assistance (FHA) customers:

  • Approval of the customer's Social Service Plan (SSP) must occur before paying the Crisis Payment
  • The system will approve this within 15 minutes
  • To check if the SSP has been approved, see Navigating to SSP
  • Once approved, pay the Crisis Payment via RTGS. Otherwise, the payment will deliver via direct credit the following business day

16

Identify if referral to social worker is needed + Read more ...

After processing the EXO claim, if the customer needs extra support and assistance due to multiple and complex issues, complete and/or consult a Social work service referral.

Offer the referral in addition to claiming CrP-EXO, not as part of a claim for CrP-EXO. Do not create a referral from within the EXO claim, as this will place the claim on hold.
