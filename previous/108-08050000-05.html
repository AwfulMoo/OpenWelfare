Forms

Claim for Pensioner Education Supplement form (SY004)

Examples - One EdEP per calendar year or 12 month period

Example

Description

JobSeeker Payment (JSP) single principal carer in receipt of Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)

The customer is a JSP single principal carer getting PES due to undertaking a part-time Bachelor course at University. They start the course on 4 March and get PES and EdEP. If their study is continued, they will be entitled to another EdEP in 12 months. The system will generate a Manual Follow Up (MFU) in 12 months to check eligibility for EdEP.

Job seeker (short course)

Customer is a job seeker undertaking a short course. They are provider managed with approved study included in their Job Plan. They are entitled to 1 EdEP for their short course. If the customer starts another short course within the same 12 month period, they will not get a second EdEP for this course.

JSP transferring to Austudy

Customer is currently a job seeker transferring to Austudy to start a full-time Bachelor degree at University. They are entitled to EdEP due to commencing a course and transferring to Austudy. This entitles them to only 1 EdEP as Austudy is not a qualifying payment for EdEP to be paid in the future.

Parenting Payment (PP) single in receipt of PES

Customer is getting PP single and PES, undertaking a Bachelor course at University. The course started in second semester and they received a payment of PES and EdEP on 24 July. They are only eligible for 1 EdEP payment per calendar year. As a continuing student, they will be entitled to EdEP again in early January the following year. A MFU is generated to check eligibility to future entitlement.

Continuous receipt of income support payment for the purpose of attracting EdEP

Customer has been in receipt of JSP for over 12 months and undertaking an approved 'short course'. Their payments have been suspended twice with a non-payment period of 6 weeks. Regardless, they are entitled to EdEP because they have been on ISP continuously for 12 months.

Continuous receipt of income support payment for the purpose of attracting EdEP, period of non-payment longer than 13 weeks

Customer was in receipt of JSP for 48 weeks when they cancelled their payment due to gaining employment. After 14 weeks, they became unemployed and restarted on JSP. They also undertake an approved short course. Therefore, they are not entitled to get EdEP due to not being in receipt of an income support payment for 12 months or more.

As they had a break in entitlement longer than 13 weeks, a new period of continuous entitlement begins from the date their JSP became current.

Continuous receipt of income support payment for the purpose of attracting EdEP, period of non-payment less than 13 weeks

Customer was in receipt of JSP for more than 60 weeks when they cancelled their payment due to gaining employment. After 12 weeks, they became unemployed and restarted on JSP. They also undertake an approved short course. Therefore, they are entitled to EdEP as they are considered to have been in receipt of an income support payment for 12 months or more.

As the customer was receiving income support payments for a long time (over 12 months) the non-payment period can be counted as a period of income support provided it is not longer than 13 weeks. The period of up to 13 weeks can be made up of several such periods.

Continuous receipt of income support payment (which is not their current payment) continuously for 12 months

Customer was in receipt of Youth Allowance (student) for 46 weeks and transferred to JSP when their course finished. After 10 weeks of being on JSP, the customer decides to return to study for a year and claims for Austudy (student).

As the customer was in receipt of JSP immediately before their new course commences and they were in receipt of an income support payment continuously for at least 12 months, the customer is entitled to EdEP.
