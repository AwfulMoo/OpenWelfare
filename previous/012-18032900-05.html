Frequently asked questions

Item

Question and answer

1

Question: I have received a prescription for brolucizumab (Beovu) with a strength of 120mg/mL, what do I do?

Answer: Approve the prescription, 120mg/mL is another way to write 6mg/0.05mL.

2

Question: An application was approved yesterday, can I approve continuing?

Answer: Yes, there is no 'too soon' rule for the Eye Program. Service Officers are not required to check patient history.

3

Question: Can I approve 2 prescriptions for the same drug and condition on the same day?

Answer: No, same day prescribing rule applies.

4

Question: If I receive a prescription stating 'initial treatment' but no application form, what do I do?

Answer: Reject the application, an authority application form must be submitted for an initial authority request.

5

Question: If the prescription does not say initial or continuing, what do I do?

Answer: Reject as there isn’t sufficient information to process the application.

6

Question: If a prescription states multiple conditions, what do I do?

Answer: Reject as the prescriber must state the condition they are requesting authority approval for.

7

Question: If the prescription states both eyes and the quantity is only '1', what do I do?

Answer: Approve the prescription as quantity '1'. Complete OPA Q&A as 'both eyes'.

8

Question: What do I do if I receive an Eylea prescription for a 2mg strength?

Answer: Approve, 2 mg is the actual dose given to the patient.

9

Question: A prescription states the following abbreviations OU, OS or OD. How do I proceed?

Answer:

  • OU (Oculus uterque) is both eyes
  • OS (Oculus sinister) is left eye
  • OD (oculus dextrus) is right eye

10

Question: If I get the same request twice in HPOS, do I need to respond?

Answer: Reply to the first request. A second response is not required.

11

Question: Can I approve increased repeats?

Answer: No, maximum repeats are on the item code sheets.

12

Question: The prescriber has written 'diabetic retinopathy', can I accept this as DMO?

Answer: No, this a different condition from DMO.

13

Question: The prescriber has written 'diabetic maculopathy', can I accept this as DMO?

Answer: No, this can be caused by DMO. However, the prescriber must confirm that the patient has DMO.

14

Question: I am processing several applications from different prescribers located at the same clinic, can I post them all back in the one envelope?

Answer: No, Service Officers must send each prescriber’s applications separately.

Where surgeries include a return express post envelope for multiple prescribers, create individual envelopes for each prescriber. Return the sealed envelopes in the same express post envelope.
