Income Stream Reviews

Historical request considerations

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Limitations on information requests

There is a limitation on the scope of information requests. The agency has a limitation of 7 years for historical information requests. However, income stream providers may be unable to supply information outside of 5 years due to tax legislation requiring information to be retained for 5 years only.

If the commencement date of the income stream is within 7 years, request information since commencement of the product.

Otherwise, request historical information as per the 7 year scope limitation.

Example:

  • The scope start date for historical information requests within 7 years will be 1 July of the financial year 7 years before the current financial year
  • In the 2020-21 financial year, the scope start date will be 1 July 2013

2

Successor funds

When requesting historical information, the customer and their income stream provider may not be able to provide data from the actual commencement date.

Example

The customer purchased the income stream for the ABC fund managers on 1 September 2008.

ABC was sold to DGE fund manager on 4 June 2017.

DGE may not have details of 1 July account balances for periods before 1 July 2017.

This is because DGE is a successor fund. In this case, DGE can only supply data from 1 July 2017.

3

Historical income stream information for recorded product

Make sure historical information requested to help recode an income stream product is only used to help recode the product.

Note: historical income stream schedules are not needed. Providers may supply this data in any format.

Do not include additional Date of Events for recoded products. For example, for products being recoded due to a product type discrepancy.

Example

A Service Officer has identified that a market-linked income stream has been coded as a term income stream. They need the 1 July account balance to recode the product correctly.

The income stream provider supplies historical income stream schedules containing the 1 July account balance and gross annual payment for 3 years (2015-16, 2016-17 and 2017-18).

When recoding the income stream, the Service Officer does not change the date of events of the historical information. They recode the product as a market-linked income stream, inputting the 1 July account balance if needed.

4

Historical income stream information for an undeclared product

When historical information is requested to code an undeclared income stream product, make sure the current account balance date in the historical information is used as the date of event.

Example

The income stream provider supplies historical income stream schedules containing the 1 July account balance and gross annual payment for 3 years (2015-16, 2016-17 and 2017-18).

The date of event would be the current account balance of each of the schedules. In this case 1 July 2015, 1 July 2016 and 1 July 2017.

5

Determining if current or historical income stream schedules are needed

The following is general information about when current or historical information requests may be needed:

Current income stream schedules

  • Commutation of information
  • Purchase date or purchase price verification
  • Term/relevant number verification
  • Commencement date verification (DEF products only)
  • TFC New Method verification

Historical income stream information

  • Undisclosed products

Considerations when actioning income stream review handoffs

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Provider data has customer and partner data

The provider data may bundle the customer and partner's income stream information for defined benefit income streams. If the Service Officer identifies that customer and partner data is in the provider data, both are to be applied within the one update.

To access the partner's record, select at top left of Process Direct in the income stream reviews manual intervention Change of Circumstance (CoC) activity.

2

Process Direct restricting original purchase price or gross income amount fields

If the gross income amount (for existing products) or 'original purchase price' field (for new products) is greyed out when they can be changed, check with a subject matter expert (SME).

If the SME confirms these can be changed, close Process Direct. Re-enter the work item, and go straight to applying the update.

This should refresh Process Direct and allow these changes to be applied.

3

Backdating the date of event (DOV)

When backdating a DOV when recoding a product, check for other assets. For example, when backdating to the original purchase date or earliest date information is available on the customer's record,

The source funds for the purchase of the product may also need reassessing from an earlier date.

4

Open activities in ISIS

Open ISIS activities attached to a Process Direct change of circumstances (CoC) activity should not be cancelled off the Activity List (AL).

If another update is needed while the income stream review activity is pending, change the started ISIS activity to registered.

5

ISRUPM3 handoffs

These handoffs may generate in error as the automated system will confirm a 'full match' if the only difference between the provider data and the customer's record is the Product Reference Number (PRN) or Australian Business Number (ABN).

This 'full match' check also happens against the partner's record. If a customer and partner both own the same type of product where both were purchased on the same date with the same amount of money, the ISRUPM3 handoff will generate for every review period, as the system will confirm a full match on both records.

6

Revisionary beneficiary indicator in provider data

The reversionary beneficiary indicator in the provider data will only indicate Yes if the income stream reverted to the customer within the past 12 months. If the income stream reverted before 12 months, it will state No.

7

Income stream schedule has rounded original purchase price

Some income stream providers round the original purchase price of account-based and market-linked income streams in their income stream schedules. If the purchase price discrepancy is because of rounding only, make sure the electronically reported original purchase price is used in completing the review.

8

Activity List (AL) has a registered (REG) AISR activity

If a Service Officer encounters a registered AISR activity, check the Interaction History to determine if any outstanding AISR activities need completion. If there are outstanding AISR activities, complete the review if possible.

Once all reviews are finalised, any remaining registered activities on AL can be finalised.

9

Grandfathered income streams

If an income stream is currently grandfathered with reason DVA, FAM, REV, MAN or SOV on SUPV and it will need to be cancelled and recoded, escalate to a technical support officer.

10

Date of receipt when activity completed in Customer First

When an AISR activity is processed in Customer First, the date of receipt (DOR) of the activity must match the DOR of the CoC.

11

Bundled handoff activities allocated

When 2 CoCs are allocated in a bundle, action the CoC with an earlier Date of Event (DOV) first.

If the 2 CoCs relate to the same product and the product is an Account based or Market-linked income stream, the earlier CoC can be completed after the newer one.

Once the latest data has been applied, complete the earlier CoC using the following:

  • Access the CoC and navigate to SUPS
  • Select 'Edit' icon on the confirmed SUPV line confirmed from the previously processed CoC, scroll to the bottom and click save
  • This will set the data to provisional with no alterations
  • This will allow the CoC to be taken to assess and finalised
