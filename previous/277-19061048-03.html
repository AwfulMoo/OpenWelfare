This page contains information and guidance about United Nations Convention on the Recovery Abroad of Maintenance (UNCRAM) applications for establishment of an overseas maintenance liability.

On this page:

An UNCRAM application is received

Processing the UNCRAM application

An UNCRAM application is received

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Establish payee options when an UNCRAM application is received + Read more ...

Determine the following:

An UNCRAM application can only be made if the payer resides in an UNCRAM country. The United Nations Treaty Collection website lists the UNCRAM countries, see Resources for a link to the website.

Does the payer live in an UNCRAM country?

  • Yes. Does the payee have or may they be eligible to have, an Australian assessment with the payer for the children named in the UNCRAM application? The References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide 2.1.1 Applications for assessment:
    • Yes, the payee has an Australian assessment (or may be eligible), go to Step 2
    • No, the payee is not eligible for an assessment because the child is over 18 years of age, go to Step 6
  • No. Does the payee have or may they be eligible to have, an Australian assessment with the payer for the children named in the UNCRAM application? The References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide 2.1.1 Applications for assessment:
    • Yes, the payee has an Australian assessment (or may be eligible) go to Step 2
    • No, the payee is not eligible for an assessment because the child is over 18 years of age, go to Step 5

2

Reciprocating jurisdictions + Read more ...

Check Australia’s reciprocating jurisdictions, if the payee:

  • has a current Australian assessment with the payer for the children named in the UNCRAM application, or
  • may be eligible for such an assessment

The Resources page contains a link to the user guide Reciprocating Jurisdictions - A guide for Services Australia - Child Support staff. The user guide lists the reciprocating jurisdictions that cannot recognise administrative assessments. See notations at the end of the document.

Does the payer reside in a reciprocating jurisdiction that recognises administrative assessments?

  • Yes, and if the payer:
    • lives in an UNCRAM country, go to Step 4
    • does not live in an UNCRAM country, go to Step 3
  • No, and if the payer:
    • lives in an UNCRAM country, go to Step 6
    • does not live in an UNCRAM country, go to Step 5

3

Contact the payee - payer does not live in UNCRAM country + Read more ...

The UNCRAM application cannot proceed because the payer does not live in UNCRAM country.

If the payee has a current assessment explain the following:

They can apply to Child Support to transmit their current assessment to the reciprocating jurisdiction for enforcement. Note: Child Support cannot guarantee the reciprocal jurisdiction will accept the request.

See Transmittal of Child Support information.

If the payee does not have a current assessment explain the following:

  • They have the option to make an application for an assessment
  • Until they make an application for an assessment, Child Support cannot determine whether eligibility criteria will be met and the application accepted
  • If their application is accepted, they can apply to Child Support to transmit their assessment to the reciprocating jurisdiction. However Child Support cannot guarantee the reciprocal jurisdiction will accept the request

See Applications for a child support assessment.

4

Contact the payee - payer lives in UNCRAM country + Read more ...

If the payee has an assessment explain the following:

  • They can make an UNCRAM application, however the application may not be required
  • They can apply to Child Support to transmit their assessment to the reciprocating jurisdiction for enforcement. This may be a faster and easier process than seeking establishment of child support overseas via UNCRAM. Note: Child Support cannot guarantee the reciprocal jurisdiction will accept the request
  • It is up to the payee to decide whether to proceed with their UNCRAM application or apply for transmittal of their assessment

Go to Step 6

If the payee does not have an assessment explain the following:

  • Before proceeding with their UNCRAM application, they have the option to make an application for an Australian assessment. This may be a faster and easier process than seeking establishment of child support overseas via UNCRAM
  • Until they make an application for an assessment, Child Support cannot determine whether eligibility criteria will be met and the application accepted
  • If their application is accepted, they can apply to Child Support to transmit their assessment to the reciprocating jurisdiction although:
    • Child Support cannot guarantee the reciprocal jurisdiction will accept the request, and
    • if the application for assessment is refused they can proceed with their UNCRAM application. Examples of why their application for assessment may be refused are residency or lack of proof of parentage
  • It is up to the payee to decide whether to proceed with their UNCRAM application or apply for an assessment

Go to Step 6

5

The UNCRAM application cannot proceed + Read more ...

Contact the payee to advise the following:

  • The country in which the payer resides is not an UNCRAM country
  • They are unable to seek establishment of a maintenance liability in that country under the Convention
  • Child Support cannot forward their application and this decision cannot be appealed against
  • If the child they are seeking support for is over 18 years of age, they are not eligible for an Australian assessment
  • If the country in which the payer resides is a reciprocal jurisdiction that does not recognise administrative assessments and the payee does not have an assessment, they can still apply for an assessment. However, if the payer does not make payments to Child Support voluntarily, the assessment cannot be enforced in the payer’s country
  • They may wish to seek legal advice about making an application directly to a court in the country in which the payer lives

Document the conversation in the payee’s Communication window.

Procedure ends here.

6

Proceeding with an application + Read more ...

If the payee decides to proceed with applying for:

  • an assessment, see Applications for a child support assessment
  • transmittal of their existing case, see Transmittal of Child Support information
  • the UNCRAM application, see the Processing the UNCRAM application table

If Child Support decides it will forward the UNCRAM application because:

  • the payee is not eligible for an Australian assessment because the child is over 18 years of age, or
  • the reciprocal jurisdiction in which the payer resides does not recognise administrative assessments, see the Processing the UNCRAM application table

Processing the UNCRAM application

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check the information provided in the application + Read more ...

The application must be accompanied by all relevant documents. This includes, if necessary, a power of attorney authorising the overseas authority to act, or to appoint some other person to act, on behalf of the payee.

Child Support must take all reasonable steps to ensure the requirements of the law of the overseas jurisdiction are complied with.

Note: an UNCRAM application is usually prepared by the payee’s lawyer.

At a minimum, the application should include:

  • the applicant payee’s:
    • full name
    • address
    • date of birth
    • nationality
    • occupation, and
    • lawyer’s name and address, if relevant
  • the responding payer’s:
    • full name
    • addresses during the last 5 years, if known by the payee
    • date of birth
    • nationality and occupation
  • a photograph of the payee and, where available, a photograph of the payer
  • the basis of their claim and what outcome they are seeking, and
  • any other relevant information such as the financial and family circumstances of the applicant and the respondent

The applicant may also wish to provide the following details:

  • current education situation of the child and a summary of expenses on either a weekly or monthly basis
  • future education or tertiary training and likely expenses
  • payee’s income, expenses and any property owned
  • what the payee considers to be the reasonable needs of the child
  • whether the payee supports any other person
  • financial or other responsibilities the payee may have
  • relevant information, for example the financial and family circumstances of the payer

Does the application include the minimal information above?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

The application does not include the minimal information + Read more ...

Contact the payee or their authorised representative to:

  • advise the application may not be accepted by the overseas authority because of missing information
  • ask they provide the missing information within 14 days

See Contact with Child Support customers.

Note: at the end of the 14 day period the application must be progressed. Child Support must send the application to the relevant overseas authority even if it is incomplete.

3

Forward the UNCRAM application to the overseas authority + Read more ...

Identify the relevant overseas authority:

  • open the United Nations Treaty Collection website
  • from Headings, select Depositary
  • from drop down menu, select Status of treaties
  • scroll to Chapter XX Maintenance obligations - Convention on the Recovery Abroad of Maintenance - New York, 20 June 1956
  • scroll to Notifications made under article 2 (Designation of administrative or judicial authority – receiving agency)
  • locate the name and address of the overseas authority
  • if the address is not listed, the Resources page contains a copy of the Central Authority Address Listing

4

Advise overseas authority of UNCRAM application + Read more ...

Write a letter to the Overseas Authority with the heading:

  • United Nations Convention on the Recovery Abroad of Maintenance (UNCRAM)
  • payee’s name v payer’s name

Explain that Child Support is:

  • forwarding an application made by the payee for establishment of a maintenance liability in the overseas jurisdiction against the payer for the children
  • asking to be kept informed of the application’s progress, and
  • requesting they advise the reasons and return the enclosed documents, if the overseas authority is unable to act on the application

Include the payee’s affidavit and any supporting documents/evidence they have provided, with the letter.

If required, seek approval for the unique letter.

Create a notepad in the Case window with a Subject heading ‘UNCRAM application forwarded’. Copy the text of the letter into the notepad, see Documenting Child Support information.

5

Notify payee + Read more ...

Advise the payee their UNCRAM application has been forwarded to the overseas authority:

  • create MX0-1 Unique letter using free text, see Letters Cuba Process Help
  • explain the letter is to tell them Child Support:
    • has sent their application for establishment of a maintenance liability against the payer for the children to the overseas authority, and
    • asked the overseas authority to keep Child Support informed of progress and if unable to act, to advise of reasons and return the enclosed documents
    • will keep them updated of progress on the application

If required, seek approval for the unique letter, see Letters Cuba Process Help.

6

UNCRAM country subsequently establishes a registrable overseas maintenance liability + Read more ...

If an overseas court or authority makes an order, either parent may apply to Child Support for the overseas liability to be registered in Australia. If there is an Australian assessment already registered, this ends if Child Support registers the overseas liability. Note: any arrears payable under the pre-existing assessment remain payable.

For more information about registering overseas maintenance liabilities, see Registration of overseas maintenance liabilities (excluding New Zealand).
