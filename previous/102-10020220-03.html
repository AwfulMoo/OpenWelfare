Partner Allowance (PA) cancellation

Step

Action

1

Has the customer advised that they have commenced work? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Customer has commenced work + Read more ...

If a customer loses payability for Partner Allowance (PA) due to employment income, the income must be recorded correctly to ensure access to working credit and that the correct automatic cancellation reason is used.

Customers eligible for working credit should be advised to supply their income details.

  • This will allow them to utilise their Working Credit, and
  • They may remain current at a nil rate for up to 6 fortnights

They could also retain certain benefits before their payment is cancelled, as long as some of that income is employment income.

If the customer:

  • supplies income details, code on Employment Income Details (EAN) screen using the Earnings macro or scriptor. See Recording and correcting employment income details
  • refuses to supply income details, cancel payment on the Benefit Action (BA) screen 'FTE' from date last paid plus one

Procedure ends here.

3

Automatic or manual cancellation + Read more ...

  • Make sure the decision to cancel the PA is correct, as the payment cannot normally be restored
  • When making an unfavourable decision, speak to the customer:
    • explain the decision
    • give them a chance to provide more information, and
    • advise their review and appeal right
  • Cancellations can be coded either:
    • manually, on the Benefit Action (BA) screen, or
    • automatically, as a result of coding the appropriate screens
  • Determine if the cancellation should be coded automatically or manually. For help, see Cancellation, suspension and rejection reason codes for Newstart System (NSS)
  • For automatic cancellation, go to Step 4
  • For manual coding, go to Step 5

4

Automatic coding + Read more ...

An automatic cancellation occurs where details are recorded on the system (for example, assets are updated and this precludes the customer from payment). See Assessment of assets for Centrelink payments.

Go to Step 6.

5

Manual coding + Read more ...

While in the customer's record, go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen and code the following fields:

  • the payment type in the Svc Rsn: 'PTA' for Partner Allowance
  • 'CAN' in the Action: field
  • the reason in the Reason: field. Use field help (?) or see Cancellation, suspension and rejection reason codes for Newstart System (NSS)
  • press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or enter the date of effect of cancellation in the Effect date: field. Where the customer or their partner is in receipt of Family Tax Benefit (FTB), and their payment is to be cancelled for a period in the past, the actual cancellation date must be recorded for FTB purposes. (i.e. not date paid to plus 1)
  • Source: and DOR:

Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and check result, but do not finalise the activity until a DOC is completed to ensure the DOC is attached to the activity.

Record details on a DOC. Ensure the DOC covers the reasons for the cancellation, how the information was received, if written advice has been issued to customer and other relevant information.

6

Finalise activity and action overpayments if appropriate + Read more ...

  • Finalise activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • The Finished: field will be protected if there are any negative adjustments. Each negative adjustment needs the debt action field completed, via the Assessment Consequences (ASC) screen. Appropriate codes are:
    • WVD - Waive debt. See Small debt waivers
    • CAL - Calculate debt - if debt definitely exists
    • INV - Investigation needed if it is uncertain if a debt exists or not. Note: Smart Centre Call Service Officers should use this code in all cases where a debt is not waived
  • Select appropriate code and update debt action codes
    • press [Enter] to go back to the AR screen
    • make any other necessary adjustments, for example, selecting the BLD line, and finalise the activity

7

Check if a manual letter is required + Read more ...

A manual letter will be required if a Manual Follow Up (MFU) activity has been created on the Activity List (AL) screen after the cancellation has been finalised.

  • If there is a MFU requiring a manual letter to be sent to customer, go to Step 8
  • If there is no MFU cancellation, the advice will be automatically sent to the customer. Procedure ends here

8

Send a manual letter + Read more ...

  • Select the MFU activity on the AL screen and complete a Cancel pension, allowance or benefit (Q134) letter or a free text (Q999) letter
  • The letter should advise the customer of the reasons for cancellation of payment and their review and appeal rights
