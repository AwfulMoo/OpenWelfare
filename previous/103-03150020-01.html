Varying the restricted percentage for Cashless Debit Card (CDC) 103-03150020

This document outlines how Local Community Panels can consult with participants to review the percentage of funds restricted to their CDC, and how the restricted percentage is updated by the CDC Processing Team. This only applies to CDC participants in Ceduna and the surrounding region, and the East Kimberley. There are no local Community Panels for CDC participants in the Goldfields region, the Bundaberg and Hervey Bay region, the whole of the Northern Territory or Cape York.

On this Page:

Default, minimum and maximum restricted percentages

Up to 80% of regular fortnightly and 100% of lump sums and arrears of restrictable payments will be restricted and paid to the customer's CDC. The remaining 20% will be paid to the customer's normal bank account.

The minimum amount which may be restricted by Local Community Panels is 50% and the maximum amount is 80%. This is only available to participants in Ceduna and the surrounding region, and the East Kimberley region, Western Australia.

Review process

In consultation with the participant, Local Community Panels will have the ability to review an individual's impacts on the community and adjust the restriction percentage paid to the customer's CDC.

All CDC participants in Ceduna and the surrounding region and the East Kimberley region (including voluntary participants), can only have their restricted percentage changed through the Local Community Panel in their area. An application form to apply for a percentage change is available from the Department of Social Services (DSS) website. See Resources for a link to the DSS website.

The customer will receive a letter from Services Australia advising them of any changes to their restricted percentage.

Local Community Panels are not available in the Goldfields region in WA, the Bundaberg and Hervey Bay region in QLD, the whole of the Northern Territory or Cape York, so CDC participants from these regions do not have any options to vary their restricted percentage.
