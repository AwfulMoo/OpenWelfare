Reissuing rejected payments for Group Payments and reporting customers

Table 1: This table describes the procedure to reissue Group Payments and payments to those customers with reporting requirements.

Step

1

Rejected payment + Read more ...

While the payment will display as ‘RTN’ on the Payment Summary (PS) screen, the associated MFU/DOC will identify whether the payment has been rejected or returned.

When a payment is rejected and not issued to the customer, a MFU (Manual Follow up) and a DOC will be placed on the customer's record to advise that action must be undertaken.

The MFU must be finalised and the DOC completed once the payment has been issued to the customer.

Examples of MFUs and DOC text are available on the Resources tab as a guide only.

Note: This file does not cover rejected Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) or New Payments Platform (NPP) payments. To action these, please refer to one of the files that relates to these payment methods (see Related links).

2

Reissuing payments + Read more ...

Note: where the term customer is used, the following steps also apply if a request is received from a nominee.

Ensure the payment destination is correct before reissuing the payment. If it is not correct, update the details first. For more details, see Changing payment destination.

  • Reissuing a rejected Group Payment, go to Step 3
  • Reissuing a rejected payment to a customer, go to Step 4
  • Reissuing payment to a former family assistance customer who has moved overseas and closed their Australian bank account. See Step 5 in the Reissuing rejected payments table
  • Reissuing a rejected payment to an employer if a customer's Parental Leave Pay (PPL) delivery is via their employer, see Step 6 in the Reissuing rejected payments table
  • Reissuing a payment to a third party, see Step 7 in the Reissuing rejected payments table
  • Reissuing a returned payment made to a deceased customer, see Replacing payment made to a deceased customer where payment was returned from financial institution
  • Reissuing a rejected New Payments Platform (NPP) payment, see Immediate payment by payment method New Payments Platform (NPP)
  • Reissuing a rejected Real Time Gross Settlements (RTGS) payment, see Immediate payment by payment method Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS)
  • If the customer is paid outside Australia, CIS staff should see Delivery of payments to Centrelink customers outside Australia
  • If the payment is not correct, submit a 'Stop Customer Payment' webform via mySupport, requesting that the payment be held to prevent reissue
  • If the payment still rejects after required action is completed, submit the relevant payment issues webform (that is, Age Pension or Jobseeker payment issues) via mySupport for ICT attention

ABSTUDY

  • ABSTUDY payments
    • can be made to customers or third parties such as educational institutions, secondary boarding schools or hostels, and
    • are not always coded on the Payment Destination (PAS) screen
  • Requests to update payment destinations for ABSTUDY must be warm transferred to ABSTUDY - ABS Head of Queue

Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC)

  • AIC payments
    • can be made to applicants or third parties such as educational institutions, secondary boarding schools or hostels, and
    • are not always coded on the Payment Destination (PAS) screen
  • staff not skilled in AIC must place the MFU on hold and create an open Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Students, ABSTUDY and Apprentices > AIC > AIC rejected/returned payment

3

Reissue the rejected Group Payment + Read more ...

When in customer's record:

  • Check the Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen to ensure:
    • Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) Code is B
    • PEN Delivery Date is D

Note: if the customer's EPED is not already Code B, use the Change Entitlement Period workflow to change the customer's future EPED to Code B.

If the workflow is unavailable, code the new payday code on the RAD screen:

  • In the relevant system, enter RAD in the NXT: field
  • Enter the payday code B in the Entitlement Period End Day Code: field.
  • Code the Delivery Day Change Reason: field with the reason CUS (Customer Request)
  • Manually record details on a DOC

Payment will not be delivered until the next regular Thursday payday (Delivery day D).

Reissuing rejected payments

Table 2: This table describes the procedure to reissue rejected payments to customers and PPL customers where the PPL delivery is by the customer's employer. Portions of this process can only be completed by the Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT).

Action

Family assistance customer + Read more ...

Before reissuing the rejected family assistance payments, check the Payment Details (PD) screen to ensure both Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A and Part B components have been rejected.

  • If the entire payment has been rejected 'This payment has been rejected on ...' will display
  • If only part of the payment has been rejected the message will specify an amount returned. For example, $180.00 has been rejected on 28 May 2011

Have both family assistance components been rejected?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, code a manual review for 3 working days to check both payments have been rejected. Procedure ends here until manual review elapses in 3 days, then go to Step 2

Check record after manual review has elapsed + Read more ...

Have both payments rejected in full?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, try to reissue the rejected amount (as per Step 4). If the system prevents this or, the amount allowed to be reissued by the system looks incorrect, then contact ICT before reissuing the payment

3

Confirm customer's entitlement to the payment + Read more ...

  • If payment is correct and can be reissued, go to Step 4
  • If the payment looks incorrect or the system will not allow the payment to be reissued, staff must follow normal escalation procedures to have this record escalated to ICT for investigation or confirmation that the payment can be reissued
  • If the full amount of the arrears are not to be reissued to the customer, the record must be escalated via mySupport to have the payment blocked and then the payment the customer is eligible for must be reissued via PYAJ. See Using the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen

Smart Centre Telephony staff are to follow normal escalation processes for referral to Service Support Officer (SSO). See Service Officer role when seeking Technical Peer Support (TPS) in Smart Centres

4

Reissue the customer or group payment via Customer First (CF) + Read more ...

  • In the system that applies to the payment to be reissued
    • PEN - Age Pension, Disability Support Pension, Carer Payment, Parenting Payment Single
    • NSS - JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance, Widow Allowance (WA)
    • FAO - FTB
    • PGA - Parenting Payment Partnered
    • ISS - Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) - (code SVDAP in the next field to access the PS screen)
    • ISS - Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) - (code SVPES in the next field to access the PS screen)
    • ISS - Carers Allowance (CDA) - (code SVCDA in the next field to access the PS screen)
    • ISS - ABSTUDY (ABY) - (code SVABY in the next field to access the PS screen)
    • ISS - Mobility Allowance (MOB) - (code SVMOB in the next field to access the PS screen)
    • ISS - Flexible Support Payments (FSP) including ADGRP, NZ DRP and COVID-19 Disaster Payments - (code SVAAA in the next field to access the PS screen)
  • For further instructions on payments in ISS subsystems, See the How to access ISS subsystems task card on the Resources page
  • Go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen
  • Check the amount is correct
  • Select the payment that has rejected by placing a 'R' in the selection field next to the payment and press [Enter]
  • On the Issue Payment Select (IIPPRE) screen, select 'Re-deliver Rejected Payment'. Destination type will automatically default to 'Direct Credit'. Press [Enter]

On the Re-deliver Returned Payment (RDRP) screen code the following:

  • If reissuing multiple payments/components, use the next and back buttons to locate the page that has the Reissue All field. This field may not appear for all payments being reissued
  • Key 'Y' in the Reissue All (Y/N): field if reissuing all rejected
    Note: when reissuing family assistance payments that have not been received, choose either Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A or Part B or both by selecting 'reissue all' and answer 'Y'
  • Reason: 'RTN'
  • A short description as to why the payment was rejected and is now being reissued is to be entered in the Notes: field
  • Record how the customer made contact in the source field, and the date of the contact in the Date of Receipt (DOR): field and press [Enter]

Note: if the payment amount is too large (System arrears limits) to issue in 1 payment, the system will automatically break into 2 or more payments. These will issue on consecutive days.

Do not attempt to reissue large payments via PYAJ.

The MFU must be finalised and the DOC completed once the payment has been issued to the customer.

Note: a 'Returned Payment' DOC is available via Fast Note.

If a manual advice is also required, this activity will must be completed once the letter is issued. See Creating an Online Advice (OLA), including within an existing activity for more information on creating a letter.

Procedure ends here.

5

Reissue rejected payment for overseas family assistance customers + Read more ...

  • If a former family assistance customer is entitled to arrears (for example, from family assistance reconciliation) but has moved overseas and closed their Australian bank account these payments will reject
  • Centrelink International Services (CIS) staff can issue a one-off payment to an overseas address
  • A one-off payment can also be used to issue payments to overseas estates and for certain other payments if no alternative option is available. See Delivery of payments to Centrelink customers outside Australia

Is there an overseas address recorded or can it be obtained by contacting the customer overseas or a nominee?

  • Yes, see Issuing a one-off payment or arrears to a Centrelink beneficiary outside Australia
  • No, payment cannot be reissued. Record the returned payment information on a DOC and detail why the payment cannot be reissued, for example, customer has closed account, is overseas and overseas contact details are unknown

6

Re-issue Parental Leave Pay (PPL) payments if the PPL delivery is via the customer's employer + Read more ...

Check or update employer's payment destination details if required.

To re-issue a returned payment to a PPL employer:

  • Go to Organisations and Merchants in the employer record (PPE CRN)
  • Select Employer Payments
  • Select Employer payment search
  • The 'PPL Employer Payment Search' page will display
  • 'Delivery Date' will default to last 6 weeks and Environment 'N' (which is all environments). Change these details or press [Continue]
  • Tick the check box beside the 'Delivery Date' of the payment line that was returned
  • Select Reissue Returned Payments from the drop down menu and press [Submit]
  • Complete the activity
  • Create a DOC on both the employer and employee/s record
  • Procedure ends here

7

Rejected payments to third parties + Read more ...

Payments to third parties such as ABSTUDY boarding payments to schools may reject when the arrears payable to the organisation are higher than the system arrears limit.

  • Go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen
  • Check the amount is correct
  • ‘S’elect the payment that has rejected and press [Enter] and then press [Enter] again
  • On the Third Party Payment Details (PDD) screen, place an 'R' against the line to be reissued
  • Complete the Reissue All (Y/N) by placing a 'Y' in the input field, complete Notes field.
  • If the Reissue All field is not visible, press [F8] to go to the next page.
  • Ensure Source type and Date of Receipt (DOR) are complete and press [Enter]
  • If the payment is over $10,000 the system will split the amount to prevent it rejecting again
