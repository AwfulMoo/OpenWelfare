1

Youth Allowance - independent, away from home rate

Sylvia, a full-time student, is entitled to a total of $544.40 per fortnight (maximum Independent Away-From-Home rate of Youth Allowance (YA) (Student) of $537.40 plus the Energy Supplement of $7.00). However, Sylvia is paid $670 every fortnight. Sylvia's Income Bank balance is nil.

To calculate the amount payable to Sylvia, see below:

  • Sylvia's gross earned income will be $670 each fortnight
    No Income Bank reduction as balance is currently zero. No Income Bank accrual occurs as employment income is above the income free area of $452.00 per fortnight
  • Sylvia's income is greater than $542.00, and therefore each dollar of income above this value will reduce Sylvia's rate of payment by 60 cents
  • Additionally, as Sylvia's income is greater than $542, the rate reduction of 50 cents for each dollar between $452 & $542 needs to be applied also. This can be done by adding $45 to the calculated rate reduction of income greater than $542

To calculate the affecting income:

  • Affecting income = ($670 - $542) multiplied by 0.60 then add $45.00
  • $121.80 = ($670 - $542) x 0.60) + $45 = $121.80

Sylvia's new rate of payment:

  • As a result of earning $670 every fortnight, Sylvia's payment of $544.40 will be reduced by $121.80 every fortnight
  • Sylvia is entitled to receive $422.60

2

YA student transfers to YA (other) jobseeker then to YA student

Rashid is 19 and defers from a Bachelor of Engineering degree half way through the second year. Rashid attends a Services Australia office to register as a job seeker. At the time of deferral from the course, Rashid's Income Bank balance was 3,400 credits.

As soon as Rashid's activity changes from full-time student to job seeker, the Student Income Bank balance will be set to zero. However, Rashid will have Working Credit balance set to 3,400 credits.

When Rashid returns to full-time study the following year, any remaining Working Credit balance will be converted to the Income Bank. This would be to a maximum of 3,500 credits.

3

ABSTUDY, full time Australian Apprentice, single rate

Jennifer is 27 years old and a single independent full-time Australian Apprentice in receipt of ABSTUDY. The maximum rate for Jennifer's circumstances is $638.30 (ABSTUDY maximum rate $629.50 plus the Energy Supplement $8.80) per fortnight. Jennifer has recently started being paid $850 per fortnight. Jennifer's Income Bank balance at the beginning of the fortnight was $266.

Student Income Bank: Jennifer's income of $850 is greater than both the income free area and Income Bank balance ($266). Therefore, Jennifer is taken to have drawn down from the Income Bank the difference between the income and the $452 personal income lower threshold. This reduces the Income bank to zero ($266 - $452 = - $186 or zero).

Ordinary income: Jennifer had $266 in the Student Income Bank. Jennifer's income of $850 is reduced by the Income Bank balance of $266, leaving an affecting income of $584. This will result in a reduction from the maximum rate by $70.20: (($584 - $542) x 0.6 + $45).

The rate for the fortnight is $568.10: ($638.30 - $70.20).

To calculate the amount payable to Jennifer, see below:

  • Jennifer's gross earned income will be $850 per fortnight
  • Current Income Bank balance of $266 will reduce to zero, as Jennifer's income exceeds the income free area and their Income Bank Balance combined
  • No Income Bank accrual will occur in the ensuing fortnights as employment income is over the $452.00 income free area
  • After considering Jennifer's Income Bank, Jennifer's remaining ordinary income is still greater than $542, and therefore for each dollar of income above this value will reduce their rate of payment by 60 cents
  • As Jennifer's remaining ordinary income is greater than $542.00, the rate reduction of 50 cents for each dollar between $452 & $542 needs to be applied also. This can done by calculating the rate reduction of income greater than $542 and adding $45

To calculate the affecting income and Jennifer's new rate of payment:

  • Remaining ordinary income = (income bank balance - ordinary income)
  • $584 = ($266 - $850)
  • Affecting income = (remaining ordinary income - $542) multiplied by 0.60, then add $45
  • $70.20 = (($584 - $542) x 0.60) + $45

Jennifer's new rate of payment:

  • As a result of earning $850 this fortnight, Jennifer's payment of $638.30 will be reduced by $70.20
  • Jennifer is entitled to receive $568.10
  • With no Income Bank credits to consider for the following fortnight, and continuing income of $850, Jennifer's rate of payment will decrease further to $408.50 for the ensuing fortnights

4

ABSTUDY full time student ceases study then recommences

Mike is a full-time student in receipt of ABSTUDY payments. Mike has been on ABSTUDY for some time and has had no employment income to date, so the Student Income Bank balance is 11,300 credits.

Mike then withdraws from the course of study, and the ABSTUDY payments are cancelled from the last day of study. Mike works for 5 weeks, but then decides to return to full-time study. Mike reclaims ABSTUDY payments, which are granted from the date that study recommences.

As there is a gap in ABSTUDY entitlement, Mike's Income Bank balance is set to zero from the date of recommenced ABSTUDY entitlement. Mike is not entitled to carry over the previous Income Bank balance of 11,300.

If Mike had recommenced studies within 28 days of withdrawing from the previous course, Mike would be entitled to receive ABSTUDY through the gap between courses. This would mean the Income Bank balance would continue over this period, and both the Income Bank and any income from part-time employment would be taken into account when determining the rate of ABSTUDY for this time.

5

ABSTUDY full time student travels overseas temporarily

Sally is a full-time student in receipt of ABSTUDY payments. Sally has been on ABSTUDY for some time and has had no employment income to date, so the Student Income Bank balance is 11,300 credits.

Sally travels to England in the mid semester break. While overseas, Sally's ABSTUDY payments are suspended for not meeting the portability rules. As Sally's payments were suspended (and not cancelled), the student income bank balance should be restored to 11,300 credits, the amount it was immediately before payments were suspended (taking into account any income that may have normally affected Sally's payments).
