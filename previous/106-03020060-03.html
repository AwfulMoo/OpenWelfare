For processing staff only

Processing an incomplete or incorrect claim

Step

Action

1

Is the customer claiming Carer Payment (CP) or Carer Allowance (CA) and the care receiver cannot be identified? + Read more ...

  • Yes, see Rejecting a CP claim and/or Rejecting a CA claim. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Incomplete claim has been lodged for a payment administered under social security law + Read more ...

Note: claim will not be considered lodged until it is fully completed and all Required supporting documentation is supplied.

This does not include supporting documents from a third party. For example, additional medical documentation from a Treating Health Professional (THP) to support their medical report.

For information about:

  • incomplete family assistance claims, see Receipt of claims, forms and documentation for family assistance
  • ABSTUDY and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme claims, see:
    • Accepting and checking ABSTUDY claims and forms
    • Assessing ABSTUDY claims
    • Checking the Claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) (SY040)
    • Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme transfers from another payment

What details are missing from the claim?

  • Unanswered questions or clarification required
    If the customer has not completed mandatory questions or clarification/correction is required, go to Step 3
  • Unsigned claim
    • If the claim is unsigned by the customer (or their partner if both are claiming on the same form), go to Step 6
    • If a non-claiming partner has not signed the form, there is no need to request their signature if the customer has signed the form and provided all relevant partner information
  • Name missing
    If the customer has not filled out the claim with their name (or their partner's, if relevant), go to Step 7

3

Unanswered questions or clarification required + Read more ...

Processing staff are permitted to accept and use verbal information from a customer (or other person to whom the customer has given express authority) if information is missing or clarification is required in relation to a claim.

Note: separate procedures apply in relation to:

  • Requesting a Tax File Number (TFN), and
  • collection and storage of medical information

If using verbal information to assess the claim, the contact, conversations and information given must be recorded in a DOC or Note.

Customers (or their authorised representatives) are under no obligation to provide information verbally. Services Australia will not be liable for any charge or fee associated with gathering information that is authorised under social security law.

Can the information be obtained verbally?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Verbal information + Read more ...

The customer (or their authorised representative) agrees to give verbal information in relation to unanswered questions or if clarification/correction is required.

  • Advise the person that the information will be used to clarify information provided by the customer
  • Document the information provided in a DOC or Note
  • Confirm the information with the person

Verbal information used in determining a claim must be recorded in a DOC or Note. If there are concerns about the validity of the verbal information, request the information in writing.

Text to include in the DOC

Ext Details: Verbal info obtained for NCL

Text: Verbal information obtained from (for example, customer or authorised representative) via phone (XX/XX/XX) in relation to claim for (payment type). Information sought in relation to form: (form number, for example, SA369), question: (question number). Details obtained: (free text). Responses verbally confirmed. Third party name and contact details: (full name), (address), (telephone).

For help, see Creating, reviewing and deleting documents (Fast Note, Callbacks, ACTDOCs and DOA DOCs).

Procedure ends here.

5

Request information in writing + Read more ...

If it is not possible or practical to obtain incomplete information verbally:

  • print a copy of the page(s) containing the incomplete question(s)
  • ensure the name and Customer Reference Number (CRN) is present on each page
  • request information from claimant by issuing a Q004 (XOBS32 for international claims) advising the claimant to complete the missing information. Enclose a reply paid envelope. Note: if the customer has a correspondence nominee, issue the same letter to the correspondence nominee

Go to Step 8.

6

Unsigned claim + Read more ...

If the claimant has not signed the claim:

  • For Disability Support Pension (DSP) claims, see Streaming a new claim for DSP
  • For all other claims:
    • print the entire scanned claim form
    • request information from claimant by issuing a Q004 (XOBS32 for international claims) advising the claimant to sign/complete details. Enclose a reply paid envelope. Note: if the customer has a correspondence nominee, issue the same letter to the correspondence nominee

If a claimant's partner has not signed the claim, it can be accepted and processed without it. However, if the partner is also claiming a payment or concession card on the same form, they will need to sign the claim and all associated forms before the claim is assessed. Alternatively, if the Person Permitted to Enquire authority on the claim is 'no', send the partner a set of blank forms to complete and sign.

A person does not need to satisfy the claim residence rules on the date the agency receives their re-signed duplicate claim. If they were an Australian resident and in Australia on initial lodgement, or met an exception to the lodgement inside Australia rule, they have already satisfied the claim residence rules.

Special conditions apply when a carer cannot provide a signed claim for Carer Payment (CP) because the care receiver cannot sign the form. In other circumstances, if the customer cannot sign, see Claim Lodgement of Centrelink claims.

Go to Step 8.

7

Name missing + Read more ...

It is a minimum requirement that a claimant's name be on the claim form.

If the claim has been scanned to the customer record but the name details are missing:

  • check the information on the form to ensure it has been scanned to the correct customer record (for example, date of birth, contact details)
  • phone the customer and advise that they will be required to complete their name details on the claim
  • print the entire scanned claim form
  • request information from claimant by issuing a Q004 (XOBS32 for international claims) advising the claimant to complete the name details. Enclose a reply paid envelope. Note: if the customer has a correspondence nominee, issue the same letter to the correspondence nominee

8

Has the information been returned within the requested timeframe? + Read more ...

  • Yes, process and finalise claim as normal using the date of claim lodgement as the date the original claim was lodged, not the date the required information was received. Procedure ends here
  • No, see Requested documentation not provided resulting in rejection of new claim
