Table 3

Step

1

Waiving an AoS debt + Read more ...

For the Assurance of Support (AoS) Processing team and Debt Recovery team only.

See Waiving Centrelink debts for details on the process of waiving a debt, but continue below for more process and policy information for waiving an AoS debt.

An AoS debt cannot be waived due to administrative error as s1237A of the Social Security Act 1991 only applies where the payment is received by the debtor (assurer). An assurer does not receive the payment so s1237A does not apply. For instance, where an assuree had received an incorrect rate of recoverable payment, due to an administrative error. Although the error was administrative, the payment was made to the assuree rather than the debtor. As such, the debt cannot be waived due to administrative error as the assuree is not the debtor in the AoS case

The only grounds to waive an AoS debt is special circumstances under SSAct section 1337AAD. The special circumstances may apply to the assurer and/or the assuree.

SSAct section 137AAE strengthens debt recovery provisions by imposing extra rules when considering the waiver of AoS debts. These extra rules prevents a waiver of debt:

  • if the assurer/s claim they were unaware of the obligations of providing an AoS. This is because the potential assurer must attend an interview with Services Australia where they will have these obligations explained to them. They must then confirm that they understand these AoS obligations
  • amounts which are lower or equal to the bond amount irrespective of whether the AoS was provided by a single assurer or joint assurers
  • in cases where a joint assurance is provided, unless the Services Australia is satisfied that the amount of debt cannot be recovered from any or all of the assurers

If it is determined an amount needs waiving and an amount has been recovered, return the amount to the assurer. This means, if the bank guarantee was used to recover all or part of the debt, that amount will be returned

2

Distinction between an AoS debt and a debt arising from a contravention of the Act + Read more ...

For example, the assuree claims fraudulently, the assuree incorrectly declares earnings.

  • Where a recoverable payment is made to an assuree in accordance with social security law during the AoS period, an AoS debt is raised by the AoS Processing team under section 1227 of the Social Security Act 1991. Liability for repayment of an AoS debt rests with the assurer/s
  • If the assuree is paid an amount to which they are not entitled and it is determined this amount is a legally recoverable debt, the debt is raised against the assuree under the relevant legislation (for example, S1223 Social Security Act 1991). The assuree, not the assurer, is liable to repay these amounts. Normal debt raising procedures are to be followed, including consideration of non-recovery (for instance, waiver)

3

Request for an explanation or application for a formal review of an AoS debt decision + Read more ...

Check the date the debt was raised via the General Information (OPGI) screen. The date determines who provides the explanation, either the National AoS Team or the Decision Maker (DM).

The National AoS Team will give an explanation of an AoS debt raised from 15 January 2007.

For debts raised before 15 January 2007, the DM is the staff member who granted the recoverable payment for the assuree. This is because this staff member held the initial responsibility for:

  • establishing that the assurer/s was/were unwilling and/or unable to provide support to the assuree, and
  • ensuring the assurer/s understood the consequences of the assuree being paid a recoverable payment. That is, the assurer/s will accrue a debt equal to the amount of the recoverable payments made to an assuree during the AoS period

If the customer disagrees with the debt decision, see Request for an explanation or application for a formal review (CLK)/

Has returned bond been credited in error to a different debt ID? + Read more ...

In Customer First or Customer Record:

  • Go to OPDL
  • Select the debt ID for ASB
  • Go to Debt Recovery History (OPRH) screen

Check the Source field. If it shows EFT, it is likely the Commonwealth Bank of Australia (CBA) has transferred that portion of the money.

Check all other ASN debts for EFT sources to see if a bond appears to have been paid, in full or in part, to a different debt ID.

For example:

A debt ID with Rsn ASB indicates a Debt Total of $14,000 but an O/S Balance of $10,000. The ORPH shows an EFT payment of $4,000 was made. Another debt ID with Rsn ASN contains an EFT amount on the ORPH for $10,000. The $14,000 in total has been returned, but a correction to the record is needed

Has the bond been returned, in full or in part, to a different debt ID?

  • Yes, contact the relevant debt raising team (Search ‘debt’ in Office Locator). Once the returned bond amount is credited against the correct ASB debt, finalise the review.   • No, go to Step 4

Check the status of the request with the CBA + Read more ...

Services Australia does not receive any documentation about a returned bond from the CBA, only an Electronic Fund Transfer.

There is no letter or document to be found on an assurer record to confirm a bond has been returned.

  • Go to the assurer record in Customer First
  • Go to Search Outbound Correspondence, the search will default the CRN in the CRN field:
    • Search the outbound correspondence for ‘AoS Bank guarantee Release letter’. If it does not appear, select the Archive Search in the top left hand corner , above Search Criteria Search via the assurer’s CRN
  • Download and attached the AoS Bank Guarantee Release Letter to an email, send to Level 3 Helpdesk requesting an update on the progress of the bond return from the CBA
    • Assurance of support will provide an update with further instructions. For example, the CBA may request a bond release letter with a new date
  • Extend the Bond Return Review for 42 days into the future, and finalise the review.
