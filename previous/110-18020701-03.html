Step

1

Discuss match data + Read more ...

A match has occurred because the information on the customer's Centrelink record differs from the information received from a third party for example, Australian Taxation Office (ATO), Department of Education, Skills and Employment.

Check the income details on the customer's Centrelink record and discuss the discrepancy with the customer. For further information, refer to the relevant 'screen' column in Assessing and Coding Income and Assets. The Resources page contains a link to this file.

The scope start date will either be:

  • the commencement date of the income, or
  • if the commencement date is greater than 7 years, the scope start date will be 1 July of the financial year, 7 years prior to the current financial year. For example, for the 2019/2020 financial year, the scope start date is 1 July 2012

The scope end date will be determined by whether the customer is still receiving income or not. If the customer:

  • is still receiving income from self-employment or a trust, the scope end date will be Date Paid To
  • has ceased receiving income, the scope end date will be the cessation date

It is important the Compliance Officer (CO) discusses the amounts listed in any of the income fields on the Match Data (IRMD) screen.

COs should use their questioning skills to obtain as much information as possible from the customer over the phone.

If the customer indicates the information is incorrect, the CO is to thoroughly review the customer record to determine an anomaly or mismatch.

Inform the customer:

'Would you mind holding the line while I check your record'

Preliminary assessment

COs must ensure that they use all information available to determine if an anomaly or mismatch exists between the customer and the match data.

If at least 2 commonalities can be identified (for example, name and date of birth matches, or name and address matches) between the customer and the match data, it can be determined that the match data is not a mismatch and should be continued. Extra care must be taken in the case of twins or intertwined records.

Alternative spelling or misspelling of a customer's name does not automatically constitute a mismatch.

Can an anomaly or mismatch be identified from the customer record?

  • Yes:
    • Advise the customer that further investigation will have to occur and that someone will contact them to advise the outcome and next steps
    • Advise the customer that if they cannot be contacted, they will need to contact back to complete their intervention within 14 days. If they fail to contact within 14 days, we may apply the match data to their record and this may result in a debt. Go to Step 2
  • No, the intervention is to be continued. The CO must refer to the relevant table in order to assess the income:
    • Family Day Care (FDC) income
    • Trust income

2

Anomaly or mismatch identified + Read more ...

If a discrepancy is identified between the customer's details and the match data, referral to a Quality Development Officer (QDO) is required.

Prior to referral, the CO is to cancel the Integrated Review (Manual Income Confirmation) in Customer First to enable Manual IRS Processing:

  • select Edit from the Integrated Review (ZIRV) in Customer First
  • select Cancelled from the Activity Status drop down menu
  • select Manual IRS Processing Required button
  • select Save
  • add the keyword MICAMM on IRMR
  • put intervention on HLD for 14 days on the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen

The QDO is to investigate the mismatch to validate the referral. These can be located by undertaking an IRNS search using the keyword MICAMM.

Where the mismatch referral is not supported the QDO is required to:

  • delete the keyword MICAMM
  • return the referral to the CO with feedback explaining the referral error

Where the mismatch referral is supported the QDO is required to:

  • delete the keyword MICAMM
  • add the keyword AMMYES
  • ensure the intervention Notes reflect validation of the anomaly/mismatch
  • forward the following information to the Customer Compliance Service Desk via the Customer Compliance Service Desk Online Form:
    • the Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • details of the mismatch
    • any additional comments that may assist the service desk in their assessment of the review

The Resources page contains a link to the Customer Compliance Service Desk Online Form.

Additional risk

If during the intervention process it is identified that the customer has an additional risk, this must be investigated and updated as part of the current intervention.

For further information on how to action the additional risk(s), see the Assessing Additional Risk Guide table on the Resources page, which will help with the assessment.

Family Day Care (FDC) income

Table 3: This table describes the steps involved in assessing Family Day Care (FDC) income.

Step

Action

1

Confirm Family Day Care (FDC) self-employment and FDC income + Read more ...

Compliance Officers (CO) need to confirm if the customer is still a Family Day Care (FDC) Educator.

CO is to inform the customer:

'We have received information from the Department of Education, Skills and Employment, that you have been working as an educator for a Family Day Care Provider.

The information we have received indicates that for the financial year/s XXXX you worked for XXXX and charged fees whilst providing care.'

CO is to ask the customer:

  • When did you start working as a Family Day Care educator?
  • Which service providers, past and present do you have contracts with?
  • Have you told Centrelink about this business? (Check REBS, EANS, OINS to make sure business is coded)
  • Are you still working as a Family Day Care Educator? If not, when did you cease?
  • How are /were you paid? (Discuss, for example, Provider, parents, bank account, cash)

CO is to ensure that the following questions are asked for each relevant financial year.

  • What services do you provide? (Before/after school care or all day care?)
  • How many hours a day do you provide care?
  • How many days per week do you provide care?
  • Do you provide additional care during school holidays? If yes how many weeks in a year do you provide care for? Does this number vary?
  • How many children do you provide care for? Does this number vary?
  • What are the ages of the children?

2

Assets and Expenses + Read more ...

CO needs to establish what assets the customer has purchased to operate the business and what expenses are for the business.

Note: for all types of expenses it is important to ensure that the amount claimed is the business portion and not the full cost of the expense.

CO is to ask the customer:

Assets

  • Are there assets that you have purchased in order to operate your business that you have not previously advised us?

Depreciation

  • What was the cost of the item?
  • What percentage do you use the item for your business?

Food

  • Do you provide food? Is it meals or just snacks?

Household expenses

  • How many rooms are in your home? How many rooms are used for child care compared to the total number of rooms in the home?
  • Do you use any room exclusively for your business?
  • Do you own or rent your home? How much rent do you pay each week? How much mortgage do you pay per month?
  • What are your total annual household costs? (Any items that relate to home expenses should be included, for example, utilities, phone, internet, insurance etc.)

Motor vehicle

  • Do you require a car for your business?
  • Do you own or lease the car?
  • Do you drop off/pick up children?
  • What calculation method was used when claiming motor vehicle expenses? For example: log book, cents per kilometre, 12% of original value, 1/3 of actual expenses
  • Is the portion claimed the full cost or the business portion only?

3

Undeclared or previously declared income + Read more ...

CO is to check the following to see if already coded:

  • Investment Update: Current bank balances
  • Check SVS/MIS/SIS and update
  • Check REBS for self-employment

If the customer is self-employed, they must provide a MOD F and a Profit and Loss Statement (SU580) at the time of disclosure of this circumstance for each sole trader and partnership business.

Each MOD F should be returned with the required documentation (as outlined within the MOD F). For new businesses not yet recorded on the customer’s record Interim statements may be requested if the business has been operating for less than twelve months or the last statement does not accurately reflect the current business situation. Customers can obtain these forms from servicesaustralia.gov.au/customer/forms.

If the customer has a company or trust set up for their Family Day Care business, they must provide a MOD PC or MOD PT. The modules should be returned with the required documentation (as outlined within the forms). Customers can obtain these forms from servicesaustralia.gov.au/customer/forms.

If the customer/partner is paying themselves a wage from their own business the amount of wages should be added back to the business income rather than recorded on EANS (unless it is a company rather than a sole trader or partnership).

Previously declared by customer

Where customers have provided up to date information on REBS we need to advise them that we will review their expenses. If there are any discrepancies we will contact them, and they may be required to provide further verification.

4

Supporting documents + Read more ...

If documented verification is required, the customer must be given the opportunity to provide suitable verification documents before contacting any third parties.

Mandatory evidence

In addition to all verbal updates obtained policy dictates that mandatory documented evidence is also required for FDC cases as income needs to be verified.

Where the customer has advised that they have ceased being a FDC Educator, the CO is to request verification from the customer, remembering that the match year may still require verification. For further information, see FDC Cessation in Source Document Options. A link to this file can be found on the Resources page.

CO is to ask customer:

'Have you lodged your tax returns for this business with the ATO?'

If yes, request full Income Tax Returns (ITR) including balance sheets, profit and loss statement and a depreciation schedule detailing the assets purchased.

  • Please provide year ………… and …………..
  • Please provide your Education Payment History Report from ……… to ………
  • Are you registered for Self Service?

'Has your income changed since your last ITR?'

If yes, request a Profit and Loss statement and Education Payment History Report.

If the customer requires further information or they ask for the request in writing, they should be referred to servicesaustralia.gov.au/individuals/subjects/compliance-program.

If the customer informs they have not lodged their tax returns the CO is to:

  • ask the customer when they will be lodging their tax returns for the business and reason for not lodging tax returns. If the customer will be lodging their tax return, the CO is to advise the CO that they have 14 days from receiving the tax notice assessment to provide their full ITR to the agency
  • request a Profit and Loss statement and Education Payment History Report for the match year/s. If the customer requires further information or they ask for the request in writing, they should be referred to servicesaustralia.gov.au/individuals/subjects/compliance-program

See the Documentary Evidence table.

Ensure the information is fully documented under the Note tab.

The Resources page contains a link to the Documenting requirements for MIC Interventions.

Third party verification required + Read more ...

A request for third party information is only made when the customer:

  • fails to contact after the initial Income Confirmation letter has been delivered and outbound contact is unsuccessful, or
  • fails to provide suitable documentary evidence

Third party verification can be requested from the third parties or in some cases directly from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO). FDC may require both third party (including FDC Providers) and ATO verification.

Two attempts must be made to gather the required information from third parties before requesting the information from the ATO.

Note: for Family Day Care interventions, where the match data indicates the business is no longer active (Cancelled, Closed or Ceased), only one attempt is required to be made to gather the required information.

For further information on what documents are to be requested and information included in the third party requests, see:

  • Source Document Options
  • MIC Intervention text for customer and third party verification

Links to this information is available on the Resources page.

  • select Continue or press [Enter]

Unearned income verification requests + Read more ...

The following steps are a guide to use for acceptable standards of verification as determined by both the nature of the investment/asset type and preliminary customer contact. Please note that for correct coding practice, all staff must adhere to the relevant assessment and coding instructions.

Bank and organisation listing contains address details for all major financial institutions. Use the drop down menu to select the relevant bank to view the address details.

Issuing a QSS40

The CO is to:

  • Launch the General Review Action Script and select QSS40/QSS41 (dom circs)
  • Select the appropriate option from Employer Information or Financial Information
  • Select who the request relates to, either Customer or Non-customer
  • Follow prompts and key information as required
  • Click on option No to finalise letter
  • When the script stops, it will ask if another QSS40 is to be issued. Do not click Next. From here view the letter and make the necessary changes
  • Delete the automated questions and replace with the questions relevant to the third party request. See the MIC Intervention text for customer and third party verification guide on the Resources page
  • Check the letter is correct (in particular check the correct contact phone number is included in the letter)
  • Once all information has been included in the letter, bring the script up and click Next
  • Finalise the QSS40/QSS41 manually
  • The script will update IRS, record the contact and 'H'old the IRS
  • Document the relevant information in the intervention Notes

Requesting information via the Electronic Data Exchange Financial Institutions

The CO should refer to:

Is there a component of earned income?

  • Yes, go to Step 4

4

Earned income verification requests + Read more ...

The verification request can be made via the General Review Action script by selecting 'Q146 - Employer Report.

The General Review Action script will:

  • create an entry on the IRCD screen with the letter and due date (21 days)
  • place the review on 'H'old, pending return of the information requested

The CO must add the third party phone numbers located/used to the intervention Notes in case future contact is required.

Note: often the customer will have a contact telephone number for the employer which can be used to obtain the correct address. This will avoid going back to the customer if the employer does not supply the required information.

Is an ATO request for information required?

  • Yes, refer to the ATO Request for Information table
  • No, ‘W' the intervention

5

Check if the third party has returned the information request + Read more ...

Before contacting the third parties about failure to return the requests ensure:

  • the original request was sent
  • the request has not been returned

To determine if the information has been returned, check:

  • the intervention Notes to see if the documents have been returned
  • the IRCS screen to see if the Actual Resp field has been updated with a 'Y'
  • Viewing Centrelink customer's digital image

For Financial Institution Exchange (FIE) tool requests

  • check the Third Party Information Summary (TPIS) screen
  • if FIE Verification Response displays in the Information Type section, the request has been returned

Has the third party returned the information request?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No,
    • Where a second request is required, go to Step 6
    • Where the third party has not replied to the second request:
    • the CO must finalise the intervention using the best information available. See Assessing and finalising Manual Income Confirmation (MIC) Interventions

6

Further requests for verification + Read more ...

If a second request has not yet been issued, the CO is to issue another:

  • QSS40/QSS4 request, go to Step 8
  • FIE tool request, go to Step 9
  • Q146 request, go to Step 10

7

Response received to third party information request + Read more ...

Once the third party information is returned, the CO must update the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen with the actual response date.

Once the information is returned, ensure that the record is updated if there is a risk of customer incurring further overpayment while waiting for other correspondence.

If all information has been received the intervention is ready to be assessed and finalised. See Assessing and finalising MIC interventions.

8

Information has not been provided QSS40/QSS41 requests + Read more ...

Check the request was sent to the correct mailing address as outlined in Bank and organisation listing which displays all major financial institutions. Use the drop down menu to select the relevant bank to view the address details.

Where required phone the third party to ascertain the correct postal address and identify any reasons for delay in responding.

Offer to send the request by fax instead.

To issue another QSS40/QSS41 request to the third party/institution:

  • Launch the General Review Action Script and select QSS40/QSS41 (dom circs)
  • Select the appropriate option from Employer Information, Real Estate Information or Financial Information
  • Select who the request relates to, either Customer or Non-customer
  • Follow prompts and key information as required
  • Click on option No to finalise letter
  • When the script stops, it will ask if you wish to issue another QSS40. Do not click Next. From here you will view the letter and make the necessary changes
  • Delete the automated questions and replace with the questions relevant to the third party request. See the MIC Intervention text for customer and third party verification guide on the Resources page
  • Ensure the original scope dates are used
  • Check the letter is correct (in particular check the correct contact phone number is included in the letter)
  • Finalise the GSS40/QSS41 manually
  • The script will update IRS, record the contact and 'H'old the IRS
  • Document the relevant information in the intervention Notes
  • ‘W’ the intervention

Procedure ends here.

9

Information has not been provided FIE tool requests + Read more ...

For FIE tool requests, check BI Today for information on response times for requests, prior to any further contact made. The Resources page contains a link to BI Today.

  • If there are delayed processing times, extend the IRCD contact
  • If there are no delays to processing, the CO is to:
    • reissue a FIE request
    • ‘W’ the intervention

For more information see:

  • Electronic Data Exchange with Financial Institutions tool
  • contact information in Electronic Data Exchange

Procedure ends here.

10

Information has not been provided Q146 Employer Report requests + Read more ...

If there is no evidence that the Q146 has been returned contact the employer by phone to see if there is an issue providing the requested information.

All reasonable attempts must be made to try and contact or identify employers.

If a phone call to the employer has been made and two Q146 have been issued and not returned this is considered all avenues have been exhausted.

Where required reissue the Q146 Employer Report request via the General Review Action script.

Procedure ends here.

Requests to ATO

Table 10: This table describes how to issue a request for information to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) and the steps involved in actioning the intervention for both the Compliance Officer (CO) and the Quality Development Officer (QDO).

Step

Action

1

Determine if information has been requested + Read more ...

Has information been requested?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 2

2

ATO request for information + Read more ...

The Australian Taxation Office (ATO) request for information form can be used where it has been determined that tax return information is required to verify a customer's circumstances and no other option is available to obtain the information.

Income tax returns for the customer are required for assessment of self-employment/other income.

For more information, see the ATO information request form. A link to this form is available on the Resources page.

Note: this is not relevant for FIE tool requests.

3

Complete the template + Read more ...

The ATO request for information template is to be completed by the requesting CO.

  • All compulsory fields must be completed (Compliance Officer (CO) full name, customer details and CRN)
  • Request information required for the verification. If information in match is for the 2010/2011 financial year only request tax return for this financial year and subsequent years. For undeclared trust, request information for the year prior to the match year
  • Only complete the Full Income Tax Return section on the ATO request form
  • In 'other box' on ATO Request Form, insert - Require accountant details
  • The request is then emailed to the site Quality Development Officer (QDO)
  • Update IRCD screen

4

Quality Development Officer (QDO) actions + Read more ...

When an ATO request for information referral is received, QDO’s are required to:

  • Check the request and ensure all relevant details have been provided on the form, including CO details and contact number
  • Check that the information requested is required for the intervention. If not return the request back to CO
  • Once all details have been checked, the request is to be emailed to the CMC via the CCB mail box
    • Add ‘ATO request for information’ to the ‘Subject Line’
    • The email content must include:
      Customer name
      CRN
  • ‘W’ the intervention

Once the information is returned from the ATO, the CMC will:

  • Upload the information to the customer’s record
  • Update IRCD screen
  • Document the relevant information in the intervention Notes

5

Information received + Read more ...

Once the ATO request for information is returned, the CO must proceed to Assessing and finalising MIC Interventions.
