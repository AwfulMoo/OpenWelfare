Assessing a CP (Standard) claim for a customer eligible for CP (XWP)

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Check eligibility for CP under ceased Wife Pension (WP) provisions + Read more ...

If CP is currently being paid under ceased WP provisions, the benefit status line will show CAR/CUR-XWP. The benefit status line appears in Process Direct and also on the Customer Summary in Customer First.

To check CP (XWP) grandfathering eligibility when the benefit status line does not show CAR/CUR-XWP, go to the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) screen and select the grandfathering type: 2020 Former WP-CARER. If the most recent status on the Grandfathering Override (GFD) screen is:

  • GRF, the customer is still eligible for CP under grandfathering provisions. They may not currently be getting CP under grandfathering provisions
  • GRO, the customer is still eligible for CP under grandfathering provisions but the system is ignoring grandfathering eligibility and assessing CP (Standard)
  • NOT, the system has determined that grandfathering provisions stopped because the only or last payment level CA ceased and has not been restored. If CA is restored, the grandfathering status ‘NOT’ will automatically be deleted
  • BRV - CP (XWP) is continuing for the 14 week bereavement period as their last CA care receiver died. The last payment level CA has already ceased

CP claims have a keyword of CARXWP attached where the claimant has a grandfathering status of GRF, GRO or BRV.

Is the customer current on CP (XWP) and has claimed CP (Standard)?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, if the customer has claimed CP and is not CAR/CUR-XWP but grandfathering status is:
    • GRF, go to Step 3
    • GRO, go to Step 4
    • NOT, procedure ends here. See Processing claims for Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA)

2

CP (XWP) current customer + Read more ...

A submitted claim for CP (Standard) cannot be processed while the claimant is current on CP (XWP). When the claim for CP (Standard) is ready to be assessed:

  • manually cancel the CP (XWP) via Customer Summary in Process Direct with reason code OTH (Other) from Date Paid To +1 (DPT+1) or the date the claim was submitted, whichever is later
  • this will generate the warning ‘W015PN - Check whether the customer is eligible for Carer Payment (XWP)’. This warning is created to prevent the CP (XWP) being incorrectly cancelled. In circumstances where the cancellation is to allow a submitted CP (Standard) claim, to be processed, the warning can be disregarded
  • inhibit the cancellation advice in Process Direct via Z in the Go to field on the AR screen
  • if a CAR/ADV activity is generated for a manual Q134, delete it. The customer will be advised of the claim outcome automatically provided the cancellation advice was inhibited

Note: cancelling CP (XWP) does not affect the customer’s potential future entitlement to CP (XWP). Providing the carer maintains a continuous entitlement to payment level CA from 19 March 2020 without a break in payment, they retain entitlement to the grandfathering provisions. Continuous entitlement to payment level CA includes where CA has been suspended or cancelled then restored without a gap in entitlement.

3

Amend the CP (XWP) grandfathering status + Read more ...

The grandfathering status needs to be GRO to assess under non grandfathered provisions.

Only staff at the APS5 level or above can make changes to the grandfathering status. For Smart Centre staff, these requests meet the criteria for direct referral to a Senior Support Officer (SSO). Contact with a Technical Peer Support (TPS) is not needed.

To add GRO:

  • select the claim from the Inbox in Process Direct
  • go to the Grandfathering Summary/Override screen in the Circumstance Details tab and select:
    • Start Date – Date the claim is to be assessed from
    • Grandfathering Type - XWC 2020 Former WP–CARER
    • Benefit Type - CDA
    • Change Reason Code - POL
    • Grandfathering status - GRO - Grandfathered Override

4

Grandfathering status is GRO + Read more ...

Assess the claimant’s eligibility for CP (Standard). The carer and care receiver/s must meet the full CP eligibility requirements.

Process the claim for CP as per the usual process. See Processing claims for Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA).

Go to Step 5.

5

Claimant eligible for CP (Standard) + Read more ...

Is the claimant eligible for CP (Standard)?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Claimant is eligible for CP (Standard) + Read more ...

To finalise the CP (Standard) claim, see Finalise a CP and/or CA claim in Process Direct.

Note: where CP (XWP) has been cancelled to enable the CP (Standard) assessment, ensure any CP (Standard) arrears are adjusted based on CP (XWP) already paid for the period.

While paid CP (Standard), the grandfathering provisions do not apply. The loss of payment level CA (SUS, CAN or changing to HCC only) will not impact on CP (Standard). However, where CP (XWP) eligibility is lost, the grandfathering status will be changed to ‘NOT’.

CP (Standard) will be subject to current requirements that apply to CP including the 25 hour rule and care receiver assessments. If the carer loses eligibility for CP in the future, but they have continued to get payment level CA since 19 March 2020 without a break in entitlement, CP (Standard) will not be cancelled but will automatically revert back to CP (XWP), subject to carer payability for example income and asset testing and portability.

Procedure ends here.

7

Claimant is not eligible for CP (Standard) + Read more ...

Where the carer is not eligible for CP (Standard) because of the 25 hour rule, leave the provisional rejection line with the rejection reason 25H on the BA screen in Process Direct.

Where the carer is not eligible for CP (Standard), E089PN– Carer Payment is rejecting with reason (### ) will be displayed and advise staff they can get further information to assess the CP new claim or they can update the grandfathering status to pay CP under the grandfathering provisions provided they are eligible.

To request further information to assess a CP (Standard) new claim, see Requesting information (CLK).

Is further information required to assess the CP (Standard) new claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Further information is requested + Read more ...

If the claimant provides further information and they now meet the full CP eligibility requirements to be eligible for CP (Standard), process the claim for CP (standard) using existing claim assessment processes.

Note: where the carer is eligible for CP (Standard), the grandfathering provisions do not apply. The loss of payment level CA (SUS, CAN or changing to HCC only) will not impact on CP (Standard) status while the grandfather status is GRO (Grandfathered Override).

Is claimant now eligible for CP (Standard)?

  • Yes, CP (Standard) is granted. Procedure ends here
  • No, or claimant does not provide further information when required, go to Step 9

9

Check CP (XWP) continuing eligibility + Read more ...

Assess the customer’s continuing entitlement to CP (XWP).

To continue to be eligible for CP under XWP provisions, the customer must continue to get payment level CA for at least 1 care receiver.

Check the reason for rejection of CP (Standard):

  • the reason to reject CP (Standard) may not apply to payment level CA for example constant care is not required for CA
  • the Adult Disability Assessment Tool (ADAT) or Disability Care Load Assessment Determination (DCLAD) result for the CP (Standard) assessment may lead to cancellation of CA for that care receiver. Note: the claimant may get payment level CA for more than 1 care receiver

Will the rejection of CP (Standard) also cause the cancellation of the claimant’s only or last payment level CA entitlement?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 11

10

Payment level CA will cancel + Read more ...

Payment level CA will cancel when the carer is being assessed for CP (Standard) and the carer is not:

  • eligible for CP (Standard), and
  • getting payment level CA for another carer receiver

The carer’s claim for CP will be rejected and the carer is not eligible for CP (XWP) from the date from which the claim was assessed because their only or last CA has been cancelled and there is a break in continuous payment level CA entitlement.

The grandfathering status on the GFD screen will automatically change to ‘NOT’ and the customer will lose their grandfathering eligibility for CP (XWP). Using Process Direct, the AR result will show CA is cancelled and the change to NOT will be applied after the claim is finalised.

The carer will automatically be advised their CA has been cancelled. However, if the CP (Standard) claim is finalised on the same day the CP (XWP) was cancelled, a manual GMANPENMAN letter may be generated to advise the CP rejection. Where CA payment is not stripped the same day, the SO may delay finalising the CP (Standard) claim until the next day to allow an automatic CP rejection advice.

Procedure ends here.

11

Payment level CA will not cancel + Read more ...

Where the carer and care receiver/s do not meet the eligibility requirements to grant on CP (Standard), but the carer will remain eligible for CP (XWP):

  • go to the Grandfathering Summary/Override screen and delete the provisional grandfathering status of GRO for the 2020 Former WP–CARER grandfathering type
  • delete the GRO (Grandfathered Override) status line. This will reinstate the carer’s previous GRF (Grandfathered) status. CP (XWP) will become payable again, from the date from which the claim was assessed
  • to finalise the CP (Standard) claim

A letter will be issued to the customer advising:

  • the reason for rejection of CP (Standard), and
  • they will be paid CP under CP (XWP) provisions because they have maintained a continuous entitlement to payment level CA from 19 March 2020

A DOC will be generated on the carer’s record explaining the reason for not being eligible for CP (Standard).

Procedure ends here.

Restoring CP (XWP) where payment level CA is restored

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

CP XWP has been cancelled CAC + Read more ...

To remain eligible for CP (XWP), the carer must have been eligible for payment level CA continuously since 19 March 2020. It’s not necessary for CA to be paid for the same care receiver since 19 March 2020.

CP (XWP) will be cancelled ‘CAC’ and the grandfathered status on the Grandfather Override (GFD) screen changed to ‘NOT’ when the carer experiences a break in continuous payment level CA entitlement due to:

  • CA suspension or cancellation
  • a change from payment level CA to HCC only or
  • a change to zero rate CA due to ETT

Has CP (XWP) cancelled CAC?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, and CP (XWP) is suspended or cancelled:
    • O6W, go to Step 2
    • for a reason other than CAC or O6W, consider the reason for suspension or cancellation and alternative income support options if applicable. Procedure ends here

2

CP (XWP) is suspended or cancelled due to the reason O6W + Read more ...

CP(XWP) for customers who leave Australia temporarily for more than 6 weeks will generally cancel ‘CAC’ (due to CA being suspended O6W).

Manually grandfathered CP (XWP) (GRF was entered manually on the GFD screen) will be suspended O6W when CA is suspended with reason O6W.

Even if CA is restored on return to Australia, there has been a break in payment level CA entitlement and CP (XWP) eligibility is lost.

An MFU is produced when CA is stopped and CP (XWP) has been manually grandfathered. The MFU states: Check if customer is still eligible for CAR (XWP).

In these cases:

  • only staff at the APS5 level or above can make changes to the grandfathering status. For Smart Centre staff, these requests meet the criteria for direct referral to a Senior Support Officer (SSO). Contact with a Technical Peer Support (TPS) is not needed
  • to make the update, on the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) screen, select the 2020 Former WP–CARER line on the GFD screen
  • key ‘NOT’ from the date the customer’s only or last payment level CA care receiver was suspended
  • cancel CP (XWP) from the same date
  • code the ‘NOT’ status before the customer returns to Australia to prevent CP (XWP) from automatically being restored on their return
  • the customer will need to claim CP (Standard) or an alternative income support payment on return to Australia

Procedure ends here.

3

CA reinstated and continuous entitlement + Read more ...

Payment level CA is reinstated without a gap in entitlement by:

  • restoring or re-granting payment level CA from the same date it was stopped, or
  • reassessing CA HCC only to payment level, from the date it was changed to HCC only, or
  • changing CA ETT back to payment level, from the same date it went to zero rate, or
  • being granted payment level CA for another care receiver without a gap

Has payment level CA been reinstated with no break in entitlement since 19 March 2020?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

CA reinstated with no break in entitlement + Read more ...

Has the entitlement to payment level CA been re-established within 13 weeks of the date of effect of ceasing?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Break in continuous entitlement to payment level CA + Read more ...

Where there is a break in continuous entitlement to CA since 19 March 2020 the carer is not eligible to be paid under CP (XWP) provisions.

The grandfathered status of ‘NOT’ will automatically be inserted on the GFD screen.

Tell the customer they may claim CP under normal provisions.

Procedure ends here.

6

CA reinstated within 13 weeks + Read more ...

Payment level CA was reinstated within 13 weeks, and a continuous entitlement to payment level CA been re-established.

In most cases:

  • CP (XWP) will be restored automatically from date of cancellation
  • the grandfathered status of ‘NOT’ will be deleted
  • an automatic advice of the re-grant of CP (XWP) will be sent to the carer

CP (XWP) is not restored automatically if the customer was manually grandfathered (GRF was entered manually on the GFD screen). See Resources page for information on MFUs.

Procedure ends here.

7

CA reinstated outside 13 weeks + Read more ...

When entitlement to payment level CA has been reinstated 13 weeks or more after the loss of entitlement, and a continuous entitlement to payment level CA been re-established:

  • the grandfathered status of ‘NOT’ will be deleted
  • CP (XWP) will not be restored automatically
  • a MFU activity and work item will be generated to investigate the carer’s entitlement to CP (XWP)

The MFU will state “Check if customer is eligible for CAR (XWP) restoration”. See Resources for information on MFU.

CP (XWP) is not automatically restored because the carer’s income, assets or accommodation may have changed while CP (XWP) wasn’t payable. If these circumstances have not been updated recently, the carer should be contacted by phone to update their current income, asset and accommodation before restoring CP (XWP).

Restore CP (XWP) if appropriate. If restored, the customer will be automatically advised.

Assessing CP (XWP) for a customer no longer eligible for CP (Standard)

Table 3:

Step

Action

1

Check eligibility for CP under ceased Wife Pension (WP) provisions + Read more ...

To check CP (XWP) grandfathering eligibility when the benefit status line does not show CAR/CUR-XWP, go to the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) screen and select the grandfathering type: 2020 Former WP-CARER. If the most recent status on the Grandfathering Override (GFD) screen is:

  • GRO, the customer is currently still eligible for CP under grandfathering provisions but the system is ignoring grandfathering eligibility and assessing CP (Standard)

Is the customer current on CP with a grandfathering status of GRO?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the carer is not eligible to be paid CP (XWP). Assess the carer’s change of circumstances as normal. Procedure ends here

2

Check the reason for failing eligibility for CP (Standard) + Read more ...

Certain codes produce an edit when a manual cancellation is attempted on the Benefit Action (BA) screen and the carer is eligible for CP (XWP). These codes relate to care and care receiver requirements and the carer’s residence.

Is the reason to cancel CP because they have failed the 25 hour rule?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Care requirements are not met due to 25 hour rule + Read more ...

Where the carer is no longer eligible for CP (Standard) because of the 25 hour rule, code a cancellation of CP for the 25H reason on the BA screen. This can be coded using the Carer Cancellation and Suspension Workflow. The warning ‘W016PN Customer will be assessed under Carer Payment (XWP) rule’ will be produced on the AWE screen if the customer is eligible for CP (XWP).

Go to Step 6

4

Check if reason is due to care and care receiver requirements + Read more ...

CP requirements relating to care or the care receiver include constant care, temporary cessation of care (hospitalisation and respite) or failing the minimum ADAT or DCLAD requirements, and CRIA.

CP requirements relating to the carer include the pensions income and asset test, and carer in gaol or psychiatric confinement.

Is the reason due to care or care receiver requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, CP is not payable and will be cancelled as normal. Go to Step 5

5

Check payment level CA + Read more ...

The carer is still qualified for CP (Standard) but they are not payable.

If payment level CA continues, they remain qualified for CP (XWP) but CP is not payable. The 2020 Former WP-CARER grandfathering status will remain as GRO unless the carer loses qualification for CP (XWP) i.e. payment level CA is suspended, cancelled or changed to CA HCC only, and it is not reinstated without a gap.

Procedure ends here.

6

Change to CP (XWP) + Read more ...

CP will not be cancelled. The type of CP will automatically be changed to XWP:

  • the qualification reason Ex-Wife Pension will be displayed on the Care Assessment Results Summary (SCRR) screen when the activity is finalised
  • the grandfathering status of GRF will automatically be reinserted on the Grandfathering Details (GFD) screen
  • a DOC will automatically be generated with the cancellation reason for CP (Standard)
  • the carer will automatically be notified of the reason for cancellation of CP and that we can pay them under the rules for ceased Wife Pension
