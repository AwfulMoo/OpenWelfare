1

Initial reconciliation completed + Read more ...

After initial reconciliation of FTB has completed, re-reconciliation will be automatically triggered if the details used to calculate FTB entitlement for the relevant financial year are updated.

Re-reconciliation automatically compares the customer's entitlement based on the new information with the earlier reconciliation result.

Re-reconciliation will occur if, for example:

  • Centrelink receives new actual adjusted taxable income details from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO). The ATO link ends after three years unless the customer has an outstanding FTB non-lodger debt. If actual income details are required for a year for which the link has ended, for example, after 30 June 2015 for 2011-12, the customer will need to provide their Notice of Assessment (NOA) and tax return (if available). See Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) process for family assistance customers
  • The customer revises their estimate of taxable income for the relevant financial year
  • The customer advises their FTB deferred child's income was below/ not below the child income limit and this is updated on the Child Student/Income Circumstances (CHSI) screen
  • The customer advises of a return to work after caring for a child during the relevant financial year. See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part B quarantine and the reconciliation process
  • A non-lodger exemption reason is coded for the current partner or ex-partner by the end of the lodgement year
  • A customer and/or their partner lodges a tax return having previously advised they were not required to lodge a tax return, or having been treated as exempt from lodging
  • A circumstance change with a date of effect in the relevant financial year is recorded (for example, child left care)
  • A retrospective change (increase or decrease) to a child support assessment for a private collect customer is received from Child Support
  • There is a retrospective change (increase or decrease) to a child support assessment for a child support collect customer and the Maintenance Income Credit (MIC) offset amount changes
  • There is a retrospective change in a child's immunisation or health check status which results in a reassessment of the FTB Part A rate reduction
  • For periods after 1 July 2018, there is a retrospective change to a customer's Income Support Payment status, this could include removal of ISP eligibility or retrospective calculation of a nil rate period due to employment income. Both scenarios would result in the customer being subject to FTB Part A income test

Customer disagrees with actual ATO income? A customer may advise that their taxation Notice of Assessment (NOA) shows a different amount from that transferred by the ATO and shown on their customer record. This could be because the NOA only shows the customer's taxable income component.

Assessable income for Centrelink is the customer's adjusted taxable income (ATI), which includes other components such as exempt reportable fringe benefits, other reportable fringe benefits, reportable superannuation contributions and total net investment losses (for example) which are not displayed on a customer's tax NOA from the ATO.

Wrong actual income? If the taxable income on a customer's NOA shows a different taxable income amount from that transferred by the ATO and shown on the customer's record (for example, no link with the ATO, wrong Tax File Number recorded), there may be a mismatch with the Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) link. See Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) process for family assistance customers.

The amounts could also differ as a result of the customer accessing superannuation funds under the First Home Super Saver (FHSS) Scheme. For these customers, income details transferred by the ATO will automatically have amounts released under the FHSS Scheme excluded, as these do not form part of a customer's adjusted taxable income (ATI). However, the taxable income amount on their NOA maybe higher than the amount reported by the ATO via the data link on our system as the ATO does regard a portion of the released funds as taxable.

Zero entitlement? If the re-reconciliation result is that the customer had nil entitlement to FTB Part A and/or FTB Part B as actual income precluded entitlement and it is the second of two consecutive zero entitlement years from the 2009-10 financial year, the customer may be subject to the non-payment of FTB by instalments.

2

The system will automatically complete the re-reconciliation process + Read more ...

  • A previous FTB reconciliation top-up or overpayment for the same year will be included in the calculation
  • A previous FTB reconciliation overpayment for the same financial year will be adjusted rather than a new debt being created
  • Re-reconciliation can result in a top-up, overpayment or nil adjustment. An automatic letter will be sent if there is an adjustment to the previous reconciliation result
  • For customers to receive their full annual FTB entitlement, income lodgement requirements, must be met within the allowable period.
  • For the 2012-13 financial year onwards, see Assessing special circumstances extensions for families unable to meet lodgement and other specific requirements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS)
  • Debt offsetting will automatically apply from 10 June 2019. FTB top-ups, supplements, lump sum claim amounts and instalment arrears can be used to recover any family assistance, social security, student assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payment debts. This will occur even if the customer has a current payment arrangement. Previously, FTB amounts could only be used to recover FTB, Child Care Benefit (CCB) and Child Care Rebate (CCR) debts

In some cases, overpayment processing will need to be completed manually. For provisional 2016-17 re-reconciliation activities of single income support payment (ISP) customers who have now lodged a tax return, see the FTB manual re-reconciliation table.

For processing for the 2000-01 financial year, see Manual Follow-ups (MFU) for family assistance reconciliation recovery.

3

Re-reconciliation + Read more ...

Like initial reconciliation, re-reconciliation results will only be sent to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) if the re-reconciliation is triggered by the receipt of actual adjusted taxable income details.

However, this will only occur the first time that the actual adjusted taxable income details are received for the customer and/or consenting partner (that is, the result will not be sent to the ATO if the actual income details received have been amended from a previous assessment).

If a Service Officer triggered the re-reconciliation by updating the customer's record, the result will immediately move to 'Finalised'.

Any FTB top-up amount is issued to the customer by Centrelink and a letter is issued to advise the customer of the result if it changes the previous reconciliation result.

4

Viewing the results of FTB re-reconciliation + Read more ...

To manually view the result, go to the FAO Reconciliation and Claim (FRCS) screen in the customer's record.

In Customer First, for

  • 2010-11 onwards, select Workspace> Families Benefits> FTB and CCB Reconciliation. See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and Child Care Benefit (CCB) Reconciliation workflow
  • 2009-10 or earlier years, see Enquiries about Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation or lump sum claim

To view details on the Centrelink system, 's'elect the FTB reconciliation completed or finalised line from the FAO Reconciliation and Claim (FRCS) screen. See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation and lump sum claim calculation screens.

For web based users, see Enquiries about Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation or lump sum claim.

For debt offsetting help, see Debt offsetting during family assistance reassessment, reconciliation and claim processing.

5

Coding re-reconciliation and lump sum calculation screens + Read more ...

'S'elect the FTB re-reconciliation line from the FRCS screen and press [Enter] to go to the FTB Calculation Result (FACRF) screen.

The FACRF screen displays:

  • Period Begin: field displays the start date of the period and displays in reverse date order, with the latest period displaying first
  • Wks/Dys: field displays the number of weeks and number of days in the period
  • Legislative $: field displays the amount the customer was legislatively entitled to during the period
  • MCFF: field displays whether the period is affected by a More Choice for Families (MCFF) payment option
  • Paid $: field displays the amount actually paid to the customer
  • Reco $: field displays the reconciliation entitlement
  • Rereco $: field displays the re-reconciliation entitlement
  • Difference $: field displays the difference between the amount paid to the customer and their reconciliation entitlement or the difference between reconciliation and re-reconciliation amounts
    • '+' - positive adjustment
    • '-' - negative adjustment
    • '0' - nil adjustment

6

Tax return lodged late after advising they were not required to lodge + Read more ...

If the customer or their partner lodges a tax return having previously advised they were not required to lodge, or having been treated as exempt from lodging, FTB will be re-reconciled using information provided by the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) including actual income and the tax return lodgement date.

If the taxation lodgement occurs after the end of the allowable period, the customer will be assessed as a late-lodger, which may result in previously paid supplements being raised as a debt.

The reason the customer or their partner has now a lodged tax return will need to be considered to determine if the late lodger rules apply.

Service Officers should discuss with the customer the reason for taxation lodgement and any income variations. The tax lodgement requirement may be due to non income events. The customer or their partner:

  • having paid tax from income received during the relevant financial year (regardless of the amount), or
  • because they had a child support assessment for the relevant financial year

If a customer or their partner had a requirement to lodge a tax return for the financial year the requirement to lodge replaces any earlier non-lodgement notifications that have been made.

What to do:

If the customer or their partner had a requirement to lodge a tax return for the financial year:

  • They cannot be assessed as exempt from tax lodgement for FTB. The customer will therefore be a late lodger for FTB reconciliation
  • Advise the customer supplements (including SIFS) are not payable and that any debt will need to be repaid

If the customer or partner did not have a requirement to lodge a tax return for the financial year:

  • The re-reconciliation result will need to be manually corrected using the Family Assistance Supplement Reconciliation Override Screen to include the supplement
  • Check first that the customer should be treated as a late lodger. The customer is not considered to be a late lodger if all of the following are met:
    • The customer's income was below the tax free threshold
    • The customer did not have any tax withheld from any amount of income
    • If they were not receiving an ISP for the full financial year, they did not have an active child support assessment, either as a paying or receiving parent
    • The customer's actual income did not include:
      • Exempt reportable fringe benefits
      • Other reportable fringe benefits
      • Reportable superannuation contributions
      • Tax exempt foreign income
      • Total net investment losses
  • Use Fast Note. Select Auto text > Families > Reconciliation > ACTDOC Manual Correction on FRMA, include the following details:
    • Financial year requiring correction
    • Reason/s for taxation lodgement
    • CRN and name of customer/partner who was not required to lodge
    • Service reason (FTB/CCF/CCR)
    • Date and AMR of the reconciliation result that needs changing
    • Full reason the reconciliation request needs to be adjusted
  • If the customer is 'at risk' or experiencing hardship, add the 'URGENT' keyword
  • MIV staff will be able to:
    • accept the provisional re-reconciliation result
    • adjust the result to include or exclude supplements (using the Supplement Override function)
    • adjust the result using the FAO Reconciliation Manual Adjustment (FRMA) screen

See Manual intervention into family assistance reconciliation.

Note: from the 2016-17 financial year onwards, FTB Part A supplement is not payable to families with an adjusted taxable income (ATI) of more than the Part A supplement income limit for the relevant income year. A supplement override to include the Part A supplement should not be coded for 2016-17 and later financial years where the family's ATI exceeds the income limit.

The FTB Part A supplement income limit also applies to income support payment customers.
