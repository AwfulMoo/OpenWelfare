This document outlines how to determine if Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP), Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) or the associated ex-gratia payments can be paid to customers affected by activated emergency events.

Emergency payments

The following payments, administered by Services Australia, have been activated for Far North Queensland Floods, February 2019 as the federal Government has made a determination under the Social Security Act that a major disaster has occurred.

  • Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP)
  • NZ Disaster Recovery Payment (DRP) - for New Zealand non-protected Special Category visa (subclass 444) holders, that is NZ equivalent of AGDRP
  • Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA)
  • NZ DRA - for New Zealand non-protected Special Category visa (subclass 444) holders, that is NZ equivalent of DRA

Affected Local Government Areas (LGAs)

The determination will specify which LGAs will be included for eligibility for the relevant emergency payment.

The determination covers the affected LGAs of:

  • Burdekin
  • Burke
  • Carpentaria
  • Charter Towers
  • Cloncurry
  • Cook
  • Doomadgee
  • Douglas
  • Flinders
  • Hinchinbrook
  • McKinlay
  • Palm Island
  • Richmond
  • Torres
  • Torres Strait Island
  • Townsville
  • Winton
  • Wujal Wujal

The Process page contains more information on the current active emergency events LGAs.

Eligibility for payments

To be eligible to receive a payment, a customer must have been impacted on any day of the major disaster, which commenced on 25 January 2019.

For more information on assessing eligibility see:

  • Eligibility for AGDRP and NZ DRP
  • Assessing DRA and NZ DRA claims

The above links also provide information about reviews and appeals.

Lodgement of claims and claiming periods

Claims must be made within 6 months of the date the disaster was declared for the affected LGAs unless special circumstances apply. See Emergency payment claims lodged outside of the time limit.

For information on how a customer can claim, steps relating to initial contact and Callback DOCs and ACTDOCs, see Claiming emergency payments.

Claim forms are available on the Services Australia Website, see the Resources page for a link.

Staff can access the claim form from the Services Australia Forms on the intranet.

Customers can:

  • call the Australian Government Emergency Information Line on 180 22 66 to claim over the phone, or
  • post their form and supporting documents to:
    Emergency Processing Centre
    Reply Paid 7815
    Canberra BC, ACT 2610
  • lodge their form and supporting documents at their nearest service centre, agent, access point or recovery centre (if available), or
  • fax their completed form and supporting documents to 1300 727 760

If a customer provides their claim form and supporting documents to a service centre, scan this information onto the system, or fax them to 1300 727 760. See Scanning a document and Storing documents. For further information on how to manage paper claims, see Claiming emergency payments.

Note: only staff trained in processing emergency payments can process these claims.

Rejection of claims

From 22 March 2019, all claims can be rejected.

  • See the Rejecting AGDRP/NZ DRP claim table in Processing AGDRP and NZ DRP claims in Customer First
  • See the Rejecting DRA/NZ DRA claim table in Recording and coding DRA and NZ DRA claims

Payment methods

There are a range of payment methods available for customers. These include the New Payments Platform (NPP) and Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) payments. If the customer advises they are in financial hardship, NPP or RTGS may be available payment methods for them for the Far North Queensland Floods, February 2019. Discuss the preferred method of payment for the customer. NPP and RTGS will ensure the speed of the delivery of payments to customers adversely affected by the floods. Note: NPP is not available for all banks including Bank of Queensland and Suncorp, however RTGS is available for most banks. Check which payments methods are available before discussing payment options with the customer.

The AGDRP claim must be finalised before using the NPP. For further information see Immediate payment by payment method New Payments Platform (NPP)
