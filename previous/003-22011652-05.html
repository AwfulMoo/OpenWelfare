Multiple payments -example 1

A customer tested positive to COVID-19 and been in isolation for 7 days.

After 7 days, the customer is still unwell and has a video conference with their general practitioner who provides them with a medical certificate to isolate for a further 3 days.

The customer has continued to lose income, as they are unable to attend their usual shifts.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible for 2 payments

The customer makes their first claim for the initial 7 day period and is eligible for a 7-day payment of Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

The customer can make another claim for a new 7 day period. The customer is eligible for a second 7-day payment of Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment, even though they only isolated for an extra 3 days. The payment amount must be in accordance with the hours of work they missed only on those extra 3 days.

The customer must retain evidence of a positive COVID-19 test from a testing centre or health professional or registering a positive COVID-19 test with a health authority for the first 7 day period claimed.

For the second period claim, the customer must retain evidence of a medical certificate or letter from an appropriate health professional that states they are still required to isolate in the current period claimed due to the effects of COVID-19.

See Positive test result.

Multiple payments - example 2 and start date of claim

A customer attended a birthday party on 17 January 2022 at a house with a friend who later tested positive with COVID-19 on the night of 19 January 2022.

They spent more than four hours at the party. The state they live in recommends the customer isolate for 14 days.

The customer started self-isolating on 20 January 2022 after their friend told them of their positive test result.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible for one payment

The customer has spent four hours or more with a person who has tested positive for COVID-19.

The start date of the claim is 20 January 2022, the date the customer started self-isolating. Not:

  • 17 January 2022, the date the customer attended the party (exposure date), or
  • 19 January 2022, the date their friend tested positive with COVID-19

The customer can make one claim for 7 days for their isolation period. They cannot make a second claim for day 8- 14 of the isolation period. This is because customers who are self-isolating or quarantining because of being a close contact are only eligible for a second payment if a new claim reason occurs, such as:

  • testing positive with COVID-19, or
  • having to care for another person who has tested positive with COVID-19

Multiple payments - example 3

A customer tested positive with COVID-19 on 2 February 2022 and starts a period of isolation on the same day. 5 days later, on 7 February 2022, their child tests positive with COVID-19.

The parent is no longer contagious or unwell and could return to work on day 8 (9 February 2022), however must now care for their COVID-19 positive child for a period of 7 days starting on 7 February 2022.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible for 2 payments

The customer can make a claim for 2 payments, one for the first 7 days because of testing positive with COVID-19.

The second claim is for the period that starts after the day their first claim period ends (as they are caring for someone who tested positive with COVID-19).

They will have to put in a new claim (on or after 9 February 2022) and only declare hours lost for the days they care for the person in the second claim period. That is, if they lose less than 20 hours work and more than 8 in the period 9 February 2022 to 13 February 2022, they will receive $450.

Multiple payments – example 4

A customer’s housemate has tested positive to COVID-19. The customer started self-isolating on 20 January 2022 after their housemate told them of their positive test result.

The customer’s other housemate tests positive to COVID-19 on 28 January 2022. The customer continues to self-isolate.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria for both claims.

Eligible for 2 payments

The customer can make a claim for 2 payments, one for being a close contact of their housemate testing positive with COVID-19 on 20 January 2022, and one for being a close contact of their other housemate testing positive with COVID-19 on 28 January 2022.

The second claim is considered a new impact reason as the close contact name differs from the previous claim.

A customer attended their workplace where an employee later tested positive for COVID-19.

Their employer suggested that as they work close to this colleague for 2 hours, they should isolate at home and not attend work until they can get a Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) test or Rapid Antigen Test (RAT) to confirm they are not positive themselves.

When the customer lodges their claim, they have not taken a PCR or RAT test.

Not eligible

The customer does not meet the definition of a close contact.

Calculating hours lost

A customer is identified as a close contact and is required to self-isolate for 3 days. The customer would have worked for 15 hours in those three days.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer is paid one payment of $450.

While payment is made for a 7 day period, the customer was only required to self-isolate for 3 days. The payment is not subject to pro-rata.

As the period of self-isolation is only 3 days, the hours of work lost is only calculated over those 3 days.

Late claim - example 1

A customer submits a claim for the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment on 19 February 2022.

The customer was required to self-isolate due to testing positive with COVID-19 for the period 20 January to 26 January 2022.

The start date of the 7 day (claiming) period is also 20 January 2022.

The customer does not have a special reason for lodging their claim outside the start date of the 7 day (claiming) period.

Not eligible

The customer has not lodged their claim within 14 days of starting their period of self-isolation.

The customer should have lodged their claim on or before 2 February 2022.

Late claim - example 2

A customer submits a claim for the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment on 15 February 2022.

The customer started self-isolating on 1 February 2022 due to being a close contact of a person who tested positive with COVID-19.

The start date of the 7 day (claiming) period is also 1 February 2022.

The customer does not have a special reason for lodging their claim outside the start date of the 7 day (claiming) period.

Not eligible

The customer has not lodged their claim within 14 days of starting their period of self-isolation.

The customer should have lodged their claim on or before 14 February 2022

Late claim - example 3

A customer submits a claim for the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment on 15 February 2022.

The customer started self-isolating on 1 February 2022 due to testing positive with COVID-19.

The start date of the 7 day (claiming) period is also 1 February 2022.

The customer was admitted to hospital for 4 days due to the effects of COVID-19. This impacted their ability to submit their claim within 14 days of the start date of the 7 day (claiming) period.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

Although the customer should have lodged their claim on or before 14 February 2022, they meet

  • all eligibility criteria for payment, and
  • have a special reason for their late claim

Close contact - example 1

A customer hears on the radio that a restaurant she visited with her sister over the weekend is in a ‘hot spot’ and she must self-isolate or quarantine for 7 days.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Not eligible

The customer does not meet the definition of close contact, has not been informed to self-isolate or quarantine by a required authority and have not tested positive with COVID-19.

Close contact - example 2

The customer in the above scenario contacts the agency a week later. Her sister, who she visited the restaurant with, has tested positive with COVID-19. The customer lives with her sister.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer meets the definition of close contact. The customer does not need to be informed by a state or territory health official to self-isolate or quarantine.

Liquid assets - example 1

A customer lodges a phone claim on 25 January 2022. They have been self-isolating since 19 January 2022 due to testing positive with COVID-19 using a Rapid Antigen Test (RAT) on 19 January 2022.

The customer advises they have:

  • evidence of registering this test with their state health authority, and
  • $12,363.00 in a savings accounts as at 19 January 2022

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Not eligible

The customer has declared they have liquid assets of $10,000 or more on the first day of self-isolation (19 January 2022).

The customer does not meet the liquid assets test.

Liquid assets and start date of claim - example 2

A customer lodges a phone claim on 19 January 2022. They have been self-isolating since 18 January 2022 due to testing positive with COVID-19 using a Rapid Antigen Test (RAT) on 19 January 2022.

The customer advises they have:

  • evidence of registering this test with their state health authority, and
  • $ 15,000 in a joint savings accounts with their partner
  • the accounts are in joint names and they own 50% each

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer has advised they have $15,000 in a joint savings accounts.

As the savings account is in both names, only 50 % of the available funds ($7,500) can be attributed to the customer.

The customer meets the liquid assets criteria.

The start date of the customer’s claim is 18 January 2022, the date they started self-isolating. Not the date they tested positive with COVID-19.

Did not claim for a previous period - extension

A customer lodges a phone claim for a 7 day claim period starting on 1 June 2022. They have been self-isolating since 23 May 2022 due to receiving a positive test result from a testing centre.

The customer has not needed to claim for the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment as they had appropriate leave entitlements from their employer. These leave entitlements were for the period 23 to 31 May 2022.

The customer has a medical certificate from a general practitioner that states the need to continue isolating until 8 June 2022 due to the effects of COVID-19.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer is isolating for greater than 7 days. Although they did not claim the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment for a previous period, they are now claiming for a period of time after their initial 7 day requirement to isolate due to receiving a positive test result.

They must have evidence available, if requested,

Evidence is:

  • for any period in the same continuous isolation period:
    • a positive COVID-19 test from a testing centre or health professional , or
    • that they have registered a positive test result for COVID-19 with a health official, for example Services NSW (that is, evidence they have registered a positive result from a home-administered Rapid Antigen Test (RAT) with their state or territory health authority), and
  • a medical certificate or letter from an appropriate health professional that states a requirement to continue isolating for the current period claimed due to the effects of COVID-19

If the customer did claim for the previous 7 day period, see Multiple payments - example 1 and Positive test result.

Table 4

Eligibility

Reason

Example 1

A customer was granted and is receiving DRA as they have reduced hours due to a declared major disaster.

The customer:

  • is still working 10 hours a week
  • then tests positive with COVID-19 and now cannot work the 10 hours per week
  • claims for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • meets all other eligibility criteria

Eligible

DRA is not a precluding payment for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

The customer is eligible for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment, as they now cannot earn 10 hours a week of income due to testing positive with COVID-19.

Example 2

A customer was granted and is receiving DRA.

The customer:

  • has not returned to work and they are not earning an income
  • then contracts COVID-19
  • claims for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment

Not eligible

DRA is not a precluding payment for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment. However, the reason for the customer not working is due to being impacted by a declared major disaster, not due to testing positive with COVID-19. The customer is already receiving DRA.
