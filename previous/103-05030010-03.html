This page contains information to offset Family Tax Benefit, Double Orphan Pension and Carer Allowance.

Offsetting eligible payments against income support payments

This table describes the steps to follow when a customer can offset Family Tax Benefit, Double Orphan Pension and Carer Allowance against their income support payment.

Step

Action

1

Request to offset payments against income support payment + Read more ...

Customer requests or Service Officer offers to offset payments against their income support payment.

Is the customer in receipt of an eligible payment, that is, Family Tax Benefit (FTB), Double Orphan Pension (DOP), and/or Carer Allowance (CA)?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, advise customer that they are not eligible for an offset payment and record details on a DOC

2

Investigate the reason for the change + Read more ...

A change of lodgement/reporting date, Family Tax Benefit (FTB) offset date or Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) is not to be used as an urgent payment or to generate an immediate payment. Frequent changes of the EPED should not be done as this may cause corruption of the record.

Investigate the record and discuss the reasoning behind the change to payment delivery.

Does the customer have a genuine reason to change the EPED?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, advise the customer that the EPED cannot be changed, procedure ends here

3

System limitations + Read more ...

Check whether there are system limitations that prevent the changing of lodgement/reporting date/EPED.

System limitations include:

  • If the request is made in the same fortnight as the members of the couple have been linked. Generally when customers become members of a couple their payments are aligned. For exceptions to this, see the Resources page
  • If the customer's record has a Benefit Status code of 'Cancel next pay'
  • International Services (CIS) customers who are overseas long term or permanently and are paid in Environment 'I'
  • If the request is made on the customer's EPED
  • If the customer is being paid by a Group Payment Schedule
  • If the customer is being paid by cheque.

Will a system limitation prevent the changing of lodgement/reporting date/EPED?

  • Yes, advise customer that the request will be rejected and they will need to recontact in the future if they still require the change. Record the decision on a DOC. Procedure ends here
  • No, system limitations do not exist, go to step 4

4

Coding the Offset + Read more ...

Explain to the customer that all of the eligible payments (FTB, DOP or CA) will be affected, if applicable.

The Change Entitlement Period workflow can be used to offset payments if the customer receives FTB.

A change to the EPED cannot be processed on the current EPED date.

If the workflow is not available or the customer receives DOP or CA only, these will need to be offset manually using the Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen, go to Step 5.

Advise the customer of the amount that will be paid on the first offset payday, explaining it will not be a full 14 days of entitlement, then:

  • open the Change Entitlement Period workflow
  • select the New Entitlement Period End Day: field. If the date is to remain unchanged select the same day as stated above from the drop down box
  • the New Delivery Day: field will automatically default to the corresponding payday
  • the Initiated By: field will automatically default to Customer. If the date has been changed to meet operational demand, Centrelink should be selected
  • select 'Yes' in the Offset Families Payment: field (to remove the offsetting, 'No' should be selected)
  • press continue to be taken to the Assessment Result (AR) screen and finalise the activity.
  • Confirm with the customer the new offset payday, the interim payment details and the next full payday.
  • Record details on a DOC

Note: when offsetting payments, an immediate payment may be issued for any entitlement accrued to the date of the change. This amount should not be paid to the customer as an urgent payment.

5

Manual coding + Read more ...

The following manual coding is only to be used if the Change Entitlement Period workflow in Customer First is not available or not able to be used.

Explain to the customer that all of the eligible payments (FTB, DOP or CA) will be affected, if applicable.

Advise the customer of the amount that will be paid on the first offset payday, explaining it will not be a full 14 days of entitlement, then:

  • enter RAD in Next: field
  • enter Y in the Families Offset: field
  • enter AR in Next: field to go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity

Confirm with the customer the new offset payday, the interim payment details and the next full payday.

Record details on a DOC.

6

Effect of offsetting payments on Income Management + Read more ...

If a customer's payments are offset, any regular Income Management expenses must be reviewed and updated as appropriate to align with the payment delivery arrangements.

Are the customer's payments subject to Income Management?

  • Yes, check any regular expenses on the Expense Management Summary screen. To update expenses to align with the offset arrangements, see Coding Income Management expenses
  • No, procedure ends here
