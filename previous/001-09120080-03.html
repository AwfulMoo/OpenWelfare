Exemptions from mutual obligation and participation requirements for serious illness

Item

Description

1

Determining who the exemption is for + Read more ...

If the exemption is for a:

  • Job seeker, go to Item 2
  • ParentsNext participant, and you:
    • have the skills to code an exemption, go to Item 2
    • do not have the skills to code an exemption, create a Priority Fast Note for allocation to a ParentsNext skilled Service Officer. See ParentsNext exits and exemptions

2

Work Item activity + Read more ...

If the medical certificate is lodged at a service centre, ensure the medical certificate is available on the job seeker's or participant's record. If not, scan and save it. See Scanning Centrelink medical/sensitive documents using an MFD.

Once the medical certificate has been actioned, check the Digital Image Work item and all associated activities are closed. See Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images.

3

Criteria for granting the Temp Incap - Serious Illness exemption + Read more ...

A job seeker or ParentsNext participant can be granted the Temp Incap - Serious Illness exemption if they meet all of the following criteria:

  • The job seeker or participant has provided a medical certificate completed by a medical practitioner, meeting the normal requirements for the Temp Incap exemption
  • The job seeker or participant has a serious illness (see Item 4)
  • The job seeker or participant is undergoing and/or recovering from intensive medical treatment, such as chemotherapy, radiotherapy, or undertaking rehabilitation for the serious illness
  • The job seeker or participant is currently incapacitated for all work or a suitable activity of 8 hours or more per week
  • For job seekers only (not ParentsNext participants). There is no indication that the job seeker is able to participate in a suitable activity including jobactive or Community Development Program (CDP)
  • The job seeker or participant is not incapacitated for all work as a result of their drug and/or alcohol misuse (including dependency), that is, the primary medical condition listed by the treating doctor is not drug or alcohol dependency (excluding CDP participants)
  • There is little prospect of significant improvement in the job seeker's or participant's medical condition over the period stated on the medical certificate, and
  • Asking the job seeker or participant to obtain new medical evidence every 3 months and provide it to the agency would place unreasonable physical and/or mental burden and/or stress on the job seeker or participant

This exemption should not be used as a way to reduce the number of contacts with job seekers.

4

Examples of medical conditions that may indicate a job seeker is seriously ill + Read more ...

Medical conditions that may indicate a job seeker or participant is seriously ill include:

  • cancer, including leukaemia
  • severe stroke
  • acquired brain injury
  • serious burns
  • coma
  • serious physical injuries requiring long recovery periods
  • severe mental health conditions for which the job seeker or participant is receiving treatment in an institutional setting

A job seeker or participant with this type of serious illness will normally be eligible for the Temp Incap - Serious Illness exemption where they do not currently qualify for Disability Support Pension (DSP) because the condition is not fully diagnosed, treated and stabilised or is not expected to last for 2 years.

5

Completing the Medical Certificate guided procedure when there is more than 1 medical condition + Read more ...

If a job seeker or participant lodges a medical certificate that has more than 1 medical condition listed, list the primary condition first, that is, the serious illness condition for which the Temp Incap - Serious Illness exemption is to be granted.

6

How to identify job seekers granted the Temp Incap - Serious Illness exemption + Read more ...

Job seekers or participants who have been granted the Temp Incap - Serious Illness exemption can be identified in the following ways:

  • the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen will display an exemption code of ISI
  • the Medical Certificate (MC) screen will contain an S in the Incap exempt: field
  • the Medical Conditions Summary Screen (MCSS) screen will display an S in the Incap exempt: field
  • the Med Cert Accepted DOC will provide information including the decision to grant the Temp Incap - Serious Illness exemption

7

Determining periods when job seeker was previously exempt due to incapacity + Read more ...

The Medical Certificate guided procedure will display information extracted from the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen showing previous periods where the job seeker or participant was granted an exemption for INP (Incapacitated (via the MC screen) and/or ISI (Temp Incap - Serious Illness).
