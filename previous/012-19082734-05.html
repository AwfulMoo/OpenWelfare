Frequently Asked Questions about PIP QI

Table 2

Item

Question and Answer

1

Question: Who to contact for more details?

Answer: More details are found at servicesaustralia.gov.au/pip

Contact a Local Primary Health Network (PHN). They will answer questions about:

  • Guidelines
  • PIP Eligible Data Set
  • The 10 Improvement Measures
  • Data Governance Framework
  • Quality improvement activities
  • Your eligibility for a PIP QI payment

2

Question: When does the PIP QI start? Which incentives will cease and which will remain?

Answer: The PIP QI Incentive commenced on 1 August 2019.

The below 4 incentives are not available after 31 July 2019:

  • Asthma
  • Cervical
  • Diabetes, and
  • Quality Prescribing

The 8 incentives that remain are:

  • eHealth
  • After Hours
  • Rural Loading
  • Teaching Payment
  • Indigenous Health
  • Procedural General Practitioner Payment
  • Quality Improvement, and
  • General Practitioner Aged Care Access Incentive

Practices must make sure they continue to meet the eligibility rules for:

  • the PIP, and
  • any individual incentives they participate in

3

Question: Will PIP QI Incentive payments be made quarterly?

Answer: Yes. The first payment was made in November 2019.

4

Question: What does a practice need to do to be eligible to receive the PIP QI Incentive payment?

Answer: General Practices must:

  • be eligible for the PIP
  • register for the PIP QI Incentive
  • submit the PIP Eligible Data Set to their local Primary Health Network (PHN) each quarter
  • agree to ongoing quality improvement activities in partnership with their local PHN

Note: practices who have been granted an exemption by Health do not need to submit this data. This exemption is in place for a number of practices until 31 July 2020.

5

Question: What does a practice need to do to apply for an exemption from the requirement to submit their data to the PHN?

Answer: If the practice’s Clinical Information System is

  • not able to submit data using my local PHNs data extraction method; or
  • has chosen not to use the local PHNs data extraction method, they can apply for an exemption

More information is available at Department of Health and Aged Care | PIP QI Incentive guidance.

6

Question: What do ACCHS and other organisations funded under the IAHP need to do to be eligible for the QI Incentive?

Answer: Aboriginal Community Controlled Health Services (ACCHS) and other organisations funded under the Indigenous Australians’ Health Programme (IAHP) must:

  • be eligible for the PIP
  • register for the PIP QI Incentive
  • report on the national Key Performance Indicators (nKPIs) to the Department of Health and Aged Care
  • agree to quality improvement activities within their current arrangement with Health

AAHCS and other IAHP funded organisations may choose to share data with their local PHN. They do not need to do this to receive the PIP QI Incentive payment.

7

Question: What data will general practices need to submit to their local Primary Health Network (PHNs)?

Answer: They need to submit de-identified general practice data against the 10 Improvement Measures. They submit this each quarter to their local PHN.

To help get ready for the PIP QI, practices should contact their local PHN.

8

Question: What is the PIP Eligible Data Set?

Answer: This is the data collected against the 10 Improvement Measures.

General practices submit the PIP Eligible Data Set to their local PHN. They do this every quarter using the practice's clinical information system.

Aboriginal Community Controlled Health Service (ACCHS) and other organisations funded under the Indigenous Australians Health Programme (IAHP)

These organisations will continue to provide data against the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander National Key Performance Indicator (nKPIs). They submit this data using their current arrangement with the Department of Health and Aged Care.

9

Question: What are the Improvement Measures?

Answer: There are 10 Improvement Measures. These help to improve local, regional and national health outcomes.

More information is available at Department of Health and Aged Care | PIP QI Incentive guidance.

10

Question: How much will a practice receive?

Answer: Payments for the PIP QI Incentive will start from November 2019. Eligible practices will receive $5.00 per Standardised Whole Patient Equivalent (SWPE) capped at $12,500 per quarter.

See PIP QI guidelines.

11

Question: Will a practice still get a rural loading under the PIP QI Incentive?

Answer: Yes, if the main practice is located in an eligible Rural, Remote and Metropolitan Area (RRMA).

This payment will be added to the PIP QI payment.

12

Question: Does this change to the PIP affect the SIP payment?

Answer: Yes, Service Incentive Payments (SIPs) for the below Incentives will cease on 31 July 2019:

  • Asthma
  • Diabetes, and
  • Cervical

Eligible practices will still get the SIP for the Aged Care Access Incentive.

Providers whose SIP payments for Asthma, Cervical and Diabetes have been held will still be able to receive these payments until 31 July 2020.

Examples of reasons why a practice has not received a payment

Table 4

Item

Example

1

The practice has registered for QI with Services Australia before the data submission date and has received a zero payment.

  • Check the practice’s Standardised Whole Patient Equivalent (SWPE) in the practice payment advice
    • If the practice SWPE is zero they will not receive a payment until they have established a SWPE

2

The practice has registered for QI with Services Australia before the data submission date and has an established SWPE.

  • If the Transaction Search screen in the Services tab shows the error message ‘2542 – No results found. Please change the search criteria and try again’ shows, then the practice has possibly:
    • not registered with their PHN
    • not provided data to their PHN
    • provided incorrect practice ID to their PHN

The practice will need to contact their local PHN to confirm they have provided the correct details and submitted their data on time.

3

The practice has registered for QI with Services Australia after the data submission date but before the point-in-time date and has an established SWPE

  • If the Transaction Search screen in the Services tab shows the error message ‘2542 – No results found. Please change the search criteria and try again’ shows, then the practice has possibly:
    • not registered with their PHN
    • not provided data to their PHN, or not provided it on time
    • provided incorrect practice ID to their PHN

The practice will need to contact their local PHN to confirm they have provided the correct details and submitted their data on time.

Note: if the practice does not have a record in the Transaction Status column, they may receive an adjustment payment at a later stage.

4

The practice has registered for QI with Services Australia and has an established SWPE.

If the Transaction Status column shows Rejected - Invalid then Health will send another payment file at a later stage and the practice will receive an adjustment payment.
