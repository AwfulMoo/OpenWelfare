Permanent Refugee Type Visas

Refugee-type visas allowing holders or former holders a Qualifying Residence Exemption (QRE) for all payments/cards.

Note:

  • Person must not also be an Australian resident, and the visa must not have been cancelled (for example, if the person departed long enough for the visa to run out).
  • People who arrived on pre-1 September 1994 refugee/humanitarian visas of the old type also have QRE for all payments/cards.

Family members (partner or dependent child) or someone who arrived on one of those listed below (or on pre-1 September 1994 types) also have QRE, but not for the Age Pension (AGE) or Disability Support Pension (DSP).

Temporary Protection Visas

Temporary protection visas (TPVs) qualifying for Special Benefit (SpB), family assistance payments, Paid Parental Leave (PPL), Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) and Low Income Card (LIC), but which generally allow no portability because they do not allow return to Australia if the person leaves.

Exception: TPV 785 (only when granted from 16 December 2014) and SHEV 790 (when available) may be portable if travelling for an approved reason.

Temporary Partner Provisional Visas

Partner Provisional Visas (PPVs) qualifying for Special Benefit (SpB), family assistance payments, Parental Leave (PPL), Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) and Low Income Health Care Card (LIC), but which allow only limited portability for no more than 6 weeks where a person needs to go overseas due to an acute family crisis, for humanitarian purposes (to attend legal proceedings), or for medical treatment of a kind not available in Australia.
