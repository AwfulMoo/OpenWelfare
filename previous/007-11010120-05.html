Scenarios - Questions and answers about Prohibition of FTB Instalment Payments (PIP)

Item

Description

1

Question: I am a single parent on an income support payment who has not taken maintenance action - can PIP be applied? + Read more ...

Answer: PIP will not be applied to their Family Tax Benefit (FTB) because their FTB is not income tested and they do not have at least one child support child in their care.

If FTB is not being income tested or maintenance income tested, PIP is not applied, even if there are outstanding lodgement years.

Note: deciding Maintenance Action periods are considered Maintenance Income tested periods.

2

Question: I have a new partner, can PIP be applied to my payments? + Read more ...

Scenario: Mark and Michelle had a baby together last week. They have been together for one year and are applying for Family Tax Benefit (FTB). Michelle is aware that Mark has not lodged a tax return for 2004-05 when Mark was with a previous partner Joy, but Joy and Mark did not have children and did not claim or receive FTB. Does Mark not lodging an old tax return affect their claim for FTB for their new baby now?

Answer: As Michelle did not receive FTB with Mark during 2004-05, their current FTB payments or FTB new claim assessment will not be affected.

Previous partners: An ex-partner not lodging their tax return should not affect the customer's. If they become partnered to a new partner they cannot be affected by the current partner not lodging an old tax return for previous years unless:

  • they were partnered to that person for at least one day in the relevant financial year, or
  • the partner received FTB in their own name in previous year and the partner did not lodge adequate income for the year/s FTB was received

An exception to this is a blended family situation where payments may be prohibited even though they were never partnered during the relevant period.

3

Question: Can I separate from my current partner to avoid PIP? + Read more ...

Answer: Separation can only be assessed if actually separated. If they want to pursue claiming separation, this will need to be investigated further before they can be assessed as a separated person and FTB instalments restored.

See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and/or Child Care Subsidy (CCS) customer separates.

4

Question: My FTB payments are affected because my partner does not want to lodge. What can Services Australia do? + Read more ...

Scenario: Elise advises that Elise's partner Boris has not lodged for 2004-05 as Boris was involved in a business at that time and has since destroyed all papers for that period. Boris cannot be bothered trying to lodge an old return for 2004-05 as Boris thinks it will cost $3,000 for an accountant to do this. Elise explained to Boris that their FTB is affected by Boris not lodging a tax return for that year but Boris still cannot be bothered. All Elise receives to look after the children is FTB money and Elise cannot get Boris to lodge the tax return although Elise nags Boris all the time, what can Elise do?

Answer: Elise and Boris need to lodge tax returns for any outstanding year FTB was received, since 2000-01. Issues within the relationship may have an impact and Elise should be offered a referral to a social worker to discuss. Elise may want to contact the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) to see what options are available for help with lodging old tax returns and finding lost payment summaries, which may encourage Boris to get this tax return lodged. The customer can visit the ATO website for more information.

Note: be aware of family and domestic violence issues. It may be appropriate to clarify the issue to find out if concerns relate to family and domestic violence. Referral to a social worker for consideration of deferral of PIP may be appropriate.

5

Question: Do I need to lodge if I was on a Community Development Employment Project (CDEP) in the affecting PIP year? + Read more ...

Answer: CDEP wages customers may be required to lodge a tax return if they earn income additional to:

  • CDEP wages, or
  • Income earned for hours in excess of the CDEP limit (CDEP top up)

CDEP wage customers should contact the ATO Indigenous Call Centre to discuss their lodgement requirements and then tell Services Australia if they are not required to lodge a tax return. For more information, see Recording not required to lodge advice and previous year income.

6

Question: I am affected by natural disaster or bereavement and PIP has been applied. What can I do? + Read more ...

Answer: Contact Services Australia to be assessed for a deferral of PIP under special circumstances.

Contact the ATO Contact the ATO about an extension to lodge the tax returns due to the fire/flood/bereavement. The customer can visit the ATO website for more information.

If an extension is granted by the ATO, the FTB customer should provide proof of this (for example receipt number, but evidence is not required) so their record can be updated and their FTB instalments restored.

After the extension end date is reached, they should provide proof that tax returns are lodged (for example, receipt number from the ATO) or a further extension to lodge.

They should discuss any missing tax documents and what can be done about this with the ATO.

In the case of child bereavement, it is possible to grant a deferral of PIP to enable payment of the bereavement payment. When the final bereavement payment is paid PIP will automatically be reapplied. See Bereavement payment of Family Tax Benefit (FTB), for more information.

7

Question: My new partner has an FTB non-lodger debt and has been advised that unless a tax return is lodged for 2006-07 FTB will stop. Will this affect my FTB as well? + Read more ...

Scenario: John and Lisa have recently become partnered and both receive FTB as a blended family.

Answer: Yes, PIP will be applied to the blended family as a whole (so if one partner in a blended family has PIP applied, then the other partner will also be affected by PIP). Deferrals however, can be applied independently.

For example, a blended family could have one person with a family and domestic violence deferral receiving their FTB and the other with PIP applied not receiving their FTB. If John and Lisa no longer wish to be paid as part of a blended family and Lisa claims FTB for John's children, FTB payments will still be affected by PIP.

For more information, see:

  • Blended families and Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
  • Deferral of Prohibition of Family Tax Benefit Instalment Payments (PIP)

8

Question: Can I apply for an FTB Advance if PIP is applied? + Read more ...

Answer: Yes, they can apply, but the advance cannot be granted. FTB payment advances are not available to customers subject to PIP as the outstanding non-lodger debt precludes entitlement to an advance.

If PIP is applied and they are already repaying an advance payment, an advance debt will not be raised as they retain their entitlement to FTB Part A. An advance debt will be raised if they subsequently lose their entitlement to FTB Part A during the advance recovery period.

If they applied for an advance payment and PIP is to be applied (for example, not yet applied as in a grace period), assess the claim for advance payment as per normal business rules.

9

Question: I have lost my payment summaries for my previous employment. What can I do? + Read more ...

Answer: If the customer cannot find payment summaries for years outstanding for PIP or cannot locate their previous employer to request these, they can request copies of payment summaries from the ATO.

They should include as part of their request:

  • name
  • date of birth
  • Tax File Number (TFN)
  • year/s needed
  • address
  • reason for request (lost)

The request should be signed and dated. The ATO will generate the missing information and send to the customer. They may wish to contact the ATO to discuss an ATO deferral due to lodgement of tax returns being delayed due to the lost payment summaries The customer can visit the ATO website for more information.

10

Question: I lodged a lump sum claim for the 2015-16 financial year. Will I be subject to PIP if my actual income is not finalised by September 2017? + Read more ...

Answer: No, FTB lump sum claimants will not be subject to the PIP process as they were not receiving FTB fortnightly in the previous year they are claiming for.

Note: they are still required to lodge the lump sum claim and confirm their income, either by lodging a tax return or advising they do not have to lodge, by the end of the lodgement year in order to receive FTB and supplement payments, if eligible.
