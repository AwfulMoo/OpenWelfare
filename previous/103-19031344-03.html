Australian Business Number (ABN) and other entities

ABN and other entities

Search for ABN and further entity information

Australian Business Number (ABN) and Australian Company Number (ACN)

To check the ABN/ACN of a business you can conduct a search on ABN Lookup/ASIC website. See Resources for a link. + Read more ...

Note: ABN Lookup is not connected to the Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC) and may not display the same information.

If the entity is a company, search the company’s details using its name or ACN through ASIC Connect. See Resources for a link.

Ensure the ABN status is Active and not cancelled.

Ensure the ACN status is 'registered' and not 'deregistered'.

Check both ABN Lookup and the ASIC website to ensure there is no mismatch in information. For further information see Updating a Business record with an Australian Business Number (ABN).

If the business does not have an ABN and has an ACN only, ensure this is documented.

Public schools and hospitals

To code a public school or hospital determine the correct legal name of the school or hospital. + Read more ...

Business name

Complete a search on ABN Lookup using the ABN to determine the full legal name of the school or hospital. See Resources for a link.

Trading name

Use the Business application to determine the trading or registered name.

For example, the Royal Adelaide Hospital is a part of the Central Adelaide Local Health Network, which also includes the Hampstead Rehabilitation Hospital, the Queen Elizabeth Hospital, and a range of other health services. The legal entity name, however, is Central Adelaide Local Health Network Incorporated as stated in ABN Lookup and registered with ASIC. Therefore, the Royal Adelaide Hospital’s information should be recorded as follows:

Example

Business Name: Central Adelaide Local Health Network Incorporated

Trading Name: Royal Adelaide Hospital

Note: do not abbreviate the legal name as the names are used for system letters and the Organisation finder.

Trusts and Self-Managed Super Funds

To code a trust use the name of its trustee + Read more ...

Business name

For example – Smith PTY LTD ATF the Smith Family Trust

(ATF = As Trustee For)

Trading name

Use the BA to determine the trading or registered name.

Code the ABN of the Trust and the ACN of the Trustee if they are a company. If they are not a company but are a legal entity with or without an ABN – Document this in a DOA DOC.

Business Name

Trading name

Use the BA to determine the trading or registered name.

Note: only one partner needs to sign the BA. The names of the other partners can be recorded in a DOA DOC.

If the name of the partnership is too long and does not fit in the required field, code what is possible under Organisation Name and for Quality Assurance purposes create a DOA DOC explaining the correct and full name of the partnership.
