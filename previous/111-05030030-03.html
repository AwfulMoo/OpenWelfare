Conditions for transferring or referring calls to specialist staff and managers

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Transferring/referring calls to Financial Information Service (FIS) Officers + Read more ...

Calls are transferred/referred to FIS Officers when customers need advice or help with:

  • managing their personal finances
  • determining how assets and/or complex income (e.g. managed investments and shares) will affect their entitlement to Centrelink payments

2

Transferring/referring a call to a Senior Service Officer + Read more ...

  • Advice and help on returning to the workforce is needed by a customer receiving:
    • Parenting Payment Single (PPS)
    • JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
    • Carer Allowance (CA)
    • Certain sole parent special benefits
  • Customer is making a complex enquiry about medical issues affecting Disability Support Pension (DSP) or JSP
  • Customer is enquiring about training or rehabilitation opportunities

3

Reasons for transferring or referring a call to a social worker + Read more ...

  • Customer insists on speaking to a social worker
  • Customer is distressed and needs advice or help to cope with immediate personal circumstances (e.g. death in the family, money or accommodation problems, family and domestic violence)
  • A customer under 18 years of age who is enquiring about the independent homeless rate should be referred to the social worker as appropriate

In areas serviced by Youth Service Units customers under 18 years of age may be referred to these units directly or via the social worker.

4

Reasons for transferring or referring a call to a Service Centre Manager + Read more ...

  • Caller is a journalist or a member of Parliament or represents an official body (e.g. Information Commissioner, Ombudsman's Office) and their call concerns policy, procedural or staffing issues, or
  • Customer insists on speaking to the Service Centre Manager after alternatives have been refused
  • Referrals to Managers are also appropriate if the caller is calling on behalf of a community group seeking a Services Australia speaker

5

Reasons for transferring or referring a call to Status Resolution Support Services (SRRS) payment staff + Read more ...

  • Customer is identified on the Benefit Status line as a SRSS customer. Status line will display NRR/CUR
  • SRSS payments are not visible to staff who do not have access to the SAP system, so these calls must be warm transferred
  • Warm transfer call to 176 352

Process when transferring or referring calls to specialist staff

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Transferring or referring a call to a specialist officer + Read more ...

When transferring or referring a call to a specialist officer, staff will:

  • Advise the caller why the referral is necessary
  • If transferring the call, brief the receiving officer before releasing the call to them
  • If using Online Document Recording (ODR) for the referral, tell the customer that their call will be returned by the specialist officer within 24 hours
  • Record enough information on the DOC to allow the specialist officer to adequately prepare themselves. Do this while the customer is on the phone or immediately after they hang up. Include any times when the customer will not be available to take the return call

2

What not to do when transferring/referring a call to a specialist officer + Read more ...

When transferring a call to a specialist officer, staff will not:

  • become an intermediary between the customer and the specialist officer
  • release a call to the specialist officer without adequate introduction or before the officer is ready to accept it

3

Things for a specialist officer to do + Read more ...

When receiving a transferred or referred call, specialist officers will:

  • monitor their work daily and return referrals within 24 hours of the enquiry
  • make arrangements for someone to answer their phone or check AYS and action referrals if they are unable to do so themselves
  • annotate all penalty period DOCs with the time the specialist officer/Service Centre Manager contacted or attempted to contact the customer and any action that was taken

4

Things for a specialist officer not to do + Read more ...

When receiving a transferred or referred call, specialist officers will not:

  • Use the staff member transferring the call as an intermediary
  • Leave a staff member on hold under any circumstances
  • Leave a customer on hold for long periods without advising them of the progress of the investigation or offering to call them back
  • Refuse a call because they consider it inappropriate. Note: a failure in procedure can be addressed via the Staff Feedback Tool after the customer's needs are met
