Applying for Living Allowance for ABSTUDY and requesting information

Table 1

Step

Action

1

ABSTUDY student or Australian Apprentice is now applying for Living Allowance - check current course code + Read more ...

Students and Australian Apprentices (apprentices) may be eligible for Living Allowance but can choose not to receive it. They still remain eligible for ABSTUDY, and other allowances or benefits under the particular ABSTUDY award they have been granted. Their circumstances may change throughout their course or apprenticeship, and they then can apply for Living Allowance.

Check the code recorded for the ABSTUDY recipient in the Participation Status: field on the Education Course (EDC) screen for their current course.

  • see Increasing study load for ABSTUDY students for any of the following codes:
    • PTS (Part Time Student)
    • C25 (25% concessional student)
    • C66 (66% concessional student)
    • procedure ends here
  • LAW (Lawful Custody Allowance), go to Step 2
  • FTI/API (Full time student or apprentice - Incidentals Only), go to Step 3
  • FTS/APT (Full time student or apprentice who has not been receiving Living Allowance due to Means Tests), go to Step 8

2

Lawful Custody Allowance student or apprentice + Read more ...

An ABSTUDY customer who has 'LAW' coded in the Participation Status: field on the EDC screen has been in receipt of the Lawful Custody Award as they were in lawful custody for a period of more than 2 weeks and studying a suitable approved course.

If they are now applying for Living Allowance, they have most likely been released.

See New claim for Crisis Payment prior to the release of a customer in prison or psychiatric confinement.

Is the customer continuing studies in their course?

  • Yes, and student will be:
    • continuing full time, go to Step 3
    • continuing part time, see Payment of ABSTUDY when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course
  • No, student will be discontinuing studies, see Payment of ABSTUDY when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course

3

ABSTUDY student or apprentice has 'FTI' or 'LAW' coded on EDC screen and is applying for Living Allowance + Read more ...

Note: if the customer is dependent, ensure it is more beneficial to receive Living Allowance, and not Family Tax Benefit (that is paid to the parent/guardian), prior to granting Living Allowance. See Eligibility for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) individuals.

Has the customer previously lodged an ABSTUDY claim for Living Allowance, for example, SY019 - Claim for ABSTUDY, for the current course or apprenticeship and provided all information necessary to grant Living Allowance, for example, personal income and assets, parental or partner income, etc.?

  • Yes, see Step 2 in the Processing application for Living Allowance when information is provided table
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Additional Information required for Living Allowance + Read more ...

ABSTUDY customers will be required to provide additional information to be assessed as eligible for Living Allowance.

Note: Students or apprentices, who have already ‘claimed’ and are qualified for ABSTUDY, do not need to lodge a new claim. Under ABSTUDY policy, they are regarded as changing awards rather than re-claiming.

The customer can provide the additional information needed by:

  • completing the ABSTUDY Additional Information (SY023) form, or
  • providing answers to the SY023 questions verbally

For details of the information required, see Completing the ABSTUDY Additional Information (SY023) form.

5

Circumstances requiring form lodgement + Read more ...

Based on the customer's circumstances additional forms may be required.

  • For students or apprentices under 22, if it is not clear they meet requirements for independent status without undertaking a full assessment, they must also complete a MOD JY. For details see Completing Parent(s)/Guardian(s) details for the Base Tax Year and Current Tax Year (MOD JY)
  • If the student or apprentice is partnered and meets requirements for a de facto relationship for ABSTUDY purposes, they must also complete a Partner Details (MOD P) form. For details see Assessing the Partner details (MOD P)

Note: if the customer prefers to lodge a completed Claim for ABSTUDY (SY019) to claim the full-time allowance in place of the forms listed above, it will be acceptable.

Request additional information

  • If the customer is present, advise them eligibility for Living Allowance cannot be assessed until the required forms have been returned
  • Send a Request for Information (RFI) letter

Record full details on a DOC including details of forms issued and/or information provided verbally.

6

Identity Confirmation requirements + Read more ...

A customer must meet full Identity Confirmation requirements prior to granting Living Allowance. Additional procedures exclusive to ABSTUDY apply in some circumstances. See Identity Confirmation for further information.

Has the customer already met the Identity Confirmation requirements for Living Allowance to be granted?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in the Processing application for Living Allowance when information is provided table
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Relevant identity documents + Read more ...

The customer must provide the relevant identity documents. See Identity Confirmation for procedure.

See Step 1 in the Processing application for Living Allowance when information is provided table.

8

Recipient has 'FTS' coded on the EDC screen, but means test has affected eligibility to Living Allowance + Read more ...

A customer may now have an eligibility to Living Allowance as their circumstance may have changed in regards to either:

  • Personal Income or Assets, for information on what the recipient may have to supply as proof of a reduction in income, see Coding income and assets
  • Partner Income or Assets, for information on what the partner may have to supply as proof of the reduction in income see Partner Income Test for ABSTUDY customers 22 years and over or Partner Income Test for ABSTUDY customers under 22 years old, Austudy and Youth Allowance students and Australian Apprentices
  • Parental Income Test, for information on what the parent/s may have to supply as proof of the reduction in income, see Increase or decrease in parental income for Youth Allowance (YA), ABSTUDY and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme

See Step 1 in the Processing application for Living Allowance when information is provided table.

Processing application for Living Allowance when information is provided

Action

Check if additional information requested has been returned + Read more ...

Ensure the steps in the Applying for Living Allowance for ABSTUDY and requesting information table have been followed first.

  • No, and staff member is:
    • trained in apprentice processing, go to Step 3

      Procedure ends here.

3

Date ABSTUDY customer eligible to receive Living Allowance + Read more ...

The date the customer is eligible to Living Allowance is based on a number of factors. These are listed below:

  • 'FTS/APT' coded on EDC screen and reduction in means has occurred. For example, decrease in parental income
    • As the EDC screen will have 'FTS/APT' coded already, the Living Allowance will start automatically from the date the drop occurred and is coded
    • Procedure ends here
  • 'FTI/API' coded on EDC screen and student or apprentice is requesting Living Allowance
    • There is no closing date for additional information to be provided for Living Allowance to be granted. Therefore, the customter may actually be eligible for Living Allowance prior to the date of receipt of the additional information. If an earlier date is determined, then that date must be used when changing 'FTI' to 'FTS or 'API' to 'APT' on the EDC screen. Go to Step 4
  • 'LAW' coded on EDC screen and student requesting Living Allowance
    • the customer becomes entitled to Living Allowance from the date they were released from lawful custody. Go to Step 4

4

Update course or apprentice details for ABSTUDY student who is now eligible for Living Allowance + Read more ...

In order to pay the Living Allowance component update the Education Course Details (EDC) screen as follows:

  • select the course displaying as 'FTI/API' or 'LAW' from the EDCH screen
  • update the Participation Status Event Date: field to the date the recipient is eligible to Living Allowance
  • change the Participation Status: field to 'FTS/APT' (Full-time student or apprentice so Living Allowance can be paid)
  • code 'I' in the Action: field to insert the new information
  • the EDCH screen will now display two entries for the same course
  • select the original 'FTI/APT' or 'LAW' entry from the EDCH screen
  • 'C'orrect the Student End Date to the day before the date the student became eligible to Living Allowance
  • select the new 'FTS/APT' entry on EDCH
  • 'C'orrect the Student Start Date to the date the student or apprentice is eligible for Living Allowance (same date as the Participation Status Event Date)
  • finalise the activity and record details of action in a DOC

The student's or apprentice's Living Allowance will be paid from the applicable date.

5

Advise student or apprentice of notification obligations + Read more ...

Advise the customer they must notify Services Australia of any changes to their circumstances, including study, within 14 days.
