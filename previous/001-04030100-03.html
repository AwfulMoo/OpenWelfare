6

Bank accounts and balances verification + Read more ...

Customers claiming YA (jobseeker) on or after the 25 September 2020 may need to supply proof of all bank accounts they hold, including balances as part of the claim.

If the customer has claimed online, do not request proof of bank accounts. The online claim will show this as a Required task.

Customers may need to provide:

  • a bank statement to all accounts held in the customer or/and partners name, or
  • a copy of digital/online account information and transactions, and
  • evidence of all bank account balances of all accounts held in the customer or/and partners name, from the Date Last Worked (DLW) plus 1 to the claim submission date. See Step 5 in Table 2 for extra detail

Note: an ATM slip is not an acceptable supporting document.

The following customers are exempt from providing supporting documentation confirming bank account balances.

  • Customers who have been on income support payment in last 13 weeks and did not advise of any new bank accounts
  • Customers who are indigenous and living in a remote area
  • Customers transitioning from Parenting Payment or Austudy or Youth Allowance (student) to JobSeeker Payment via a transition claim

Note: bank account details may be pre-populated into an online claim and, if the customer has not zero'd off the balance or advised the account is closed, Service Officers can accept verbal confirmation that the account is closed. See Documents screen in the Request for Information guided procedure. For example, if the customer advises they closed an account within the past 12 months they should provide evidence, if they advise the account was closed more than 12 months ago, Services Officers can accept verbal confirmation.

Make an outbound call to the customer + Read more ...

9

Requesting documents + Read more ...

In some cases, extra evidence may still be needed to complete the claim. For example, information about trusts, companies or compensation.

In these cases, try to obtain the information verbally:

  • Do not call customers before 8am or after 8pm in their location of residence
  • Issue a Pre-Call SMS to let them know they will get a private number call from the agency

If contact was successful:

  • record the details of the conversation in the Progress of Claim DOC
  • encourage them to upload the information via the requested task in the online claim on the day so their claim can be processed as soon as possible

If the contact was unsuccessful or the customer is not able to supply the extra information or evidence immediately:

  • Issue a request for information via >Request Documents

If the online claim has not made a request to the customer for evidence and they do not meet any of the claim exceptions, raise an incident via Roxy and issue a RFI.

Note: documents that have been requested at Next Step in the claim should not be requested again.

Referrals (if required) + Read more ...

If a specialist assessments is required, select > Referral and complete the referral details:

  • Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) if the customer's financial arrangements are considered complex, for example, include a trust or company
    • The referral ensures income and assets tests are met
    • Unless it is clear that the claim must be rejected because basic eligibility criteria has not been met, claims requiring a CAO referral should not be finalised until the assessment has been completed
    • A single referral can be made for multiple assessment reasons
  • Social work services
  • Assessment of Care Arrangements - for staff who are not suitably trained in undertaking care assessments or if care is disputed or disagreed
  • Unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH) assessment. If a UTLAH assessment is in progress, place the claim on hold for 14 days via the Status icon
  • Australian residence assessment, only if immigration data is missing. See Referring inconsistencies in overseas departure and arrival information to Centrelink International Services (CIS)
  • Member of a Couple (MoC) - if Relationship Details (SS284) form or Relationship Details - Separated under one roof (SS293) form has been requested. This does not reflect expected processing time. The NCL activity status will update when MoC processing staff complete the assessment
  • Other referrals may need to be made in Customer First, including Compensation clearance

After a referral is made, the claim remains on hold until the assessment has been completed.

Check customer record + Read more ...

To prevent incorrect claim assessment, there may be a need to update the customer's record outside the new claim.

Updates may include correcting the previous benefit status so it displays as CAN (cancel). This will prevent incorrect arrears when finalising the claim or end dating student/apprenticeship details.

  • If the benefit status is CNP (cancel next payment):
    • go to Customer Summary tile
    • enter the customer's CRN and select the customer
    • go to Payment Summary (PS) screen via Super Key
    • Key '9999' in the Number of months in past field, Execute
    • note the date paid to plus 1
    • go to Benefit Action (BA) screen via Super Key and code cancellation
  • If the customer was a full-time student/Apprentice before claiming, or current education/apprenticeship details display on their record:
    • end date details for student payment, or
    • end date details for Apprenticeship payment

Pre-Claim vulnerable circumstance

Where the customer has pre-claim vulnerable circumstances recorded within the eight weeks prior to their intent to claim contact date the system will automatically calculate the start date.

  • Where pre-claim vulnerable circumstances are not able to be automatically determined by the system they can be manually added on the Pre-Claim Vulnerable Circumstance (PRECLM) screen in Process Direct or via the Pre-claim Circumstance Guided procedure in Customer First.
  • If the customer has a pre-claim circumstance displayed on PRECLM with a start date that is more than 8 weeks ago and no end date, consider if that pre-claim vulnerable circumstance still exists using available information. Where the vulnerable circumstance is no longer active, consider end dating the vulnerable circumstance from the associated circumstance page and regenerate the claim. Some circumstances can remain current on a customer's record indefinitely, such as an exemption from seeking child support due to family and domestic violence. In these cases, do not end date the exemption. Manually update the claim start date to the date of submission if required. See Step 2 in Table 1 in Intent to claim and vulnerable customers for more information.

After completing all updates, select > Regenerate claim.

Principal carers + Read more ...

A principal carer is a person with primary care of a dependent child under 16 years. A principal carer assessment must be made if one or both customers caring for a child are claiming income support as a principal carer.

A step parent may be a principal carer if they live as a member of a couple with the parent of the child.

A principal carer may be eligible for Parenting Payment (PP) as a more appropriate payment.

If member of a couple with a dependent child in care

  • over the age of 6 years
    • Only one member of a couple can be a principal carer at one time
    • If both claim or get a social security payment based on or affected by principal carer status, they must be advised in writing who the principal carer is
  • under the age of 6 years
    • Only one member of a couple can be a principal carer at one time
    • Continue to process the claim. Once the claim is finalised, see Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen, Table 3 for coding of the PCCD and CHOC screens

See Principal carer of a dependent child.

Parental income + Read more ...

Parental income is requested as part of the claim for dependent customers.

Parental income information is coded on the Parental Income (NHI) screen. For help, see Applying the Parental Income Test (PIT) for ABSTUDY and Youth Allowance (YA).

It is likely that more parents will indicate a substantial decrease in their income that is likely to last for at least 2 years as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic. For a current year income assessment, if a parent advises a substantial decrease in income, accept that the decrease will last for 2 years or more. Parents will not need to provide evidence of their decrease in income.

See Increase or decrease in parental income for Youth Allowance (YA), ABSTUDY and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme.
