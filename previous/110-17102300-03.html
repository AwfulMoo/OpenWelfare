This page contains the most frequently used methods to diagnose why a reporting issue/drop through via self service has occurred.

Use the drop list to select instructions on common issues.

How to diagnose a failed self service report issue

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Locating work items + Read more ...

Staff can locate work items relating to unsuccessful REI updates using the keyword ‘#REI’ via the work allocation process Workload Management. When completing the reporting activity, best practice is to always check the Customer Summary screen in Customer First. Scroll to the Incomplete/outstanding activities tab to make sure all work is complete before exiting the record.

Note: check the benefit status line for the customer. If the customer is receiving Farm Household Allowance (FHA) transfer the customer to the Farmer Assistance hotline.
The Resources page has a link to the telephone number.

See Interaction Records for more information.

2

Online Interaction Notes (Customer online Account) in Customer First + Read more ...

Staff can review how and why the activity failed or the handoff occurred by reviewing the record of the Online Interaction Summary in the Customer Online Account tile in Customer First.

Select the link to be taken to a search page that already has the customer’s CRN defaulted.

Click search and the history of the customer’s recent online interactions will be displayed.

Click on the customer’s name on the line with a Category of ‘Earning’ and the status of ‘Completed’ equal to the date the customer was trying to report

The Receipt Literal will show whether the report could not be completed due to a handoff (‘UPDT.HAND…’), or a system error being returned (‘UPDT.FAIL…’). The last three letters will indicate the failure or handoff reason.

Click on the ‘Note’ tab to see the details of the transaction and why it has failed, including an error code if applicable.

3

SSAF screen + Read more ...

Staff can review the Self Service Access Facility (SSAF) screen to help identify why a reporting issue/drop through via self service has occurred.

The SSAF screen enables Service Officers to allow and remove a customer's access to self service reporting. It also provides information about messages a customer may receive when using the screen.

The screen can be manually used to exclude customers from self service reporting.

See SSAF for reporting employment income, participation and change of circumstance details.

4

Adding notes + Read more ...

Once staff finalise an Income Security Integrated System (ISIS) activity they must add any required notes to the Interaction.

5

Partner information + Read more ...

If the customer also provides updates to their partner's information, there will be 2 ISIS activities (1 on the customer record and another on the partner record) and a single ZCOC (Change of Circumstance) Interaction alerting staff of the follow-up.

6

Accessing the Issues page in Customer First + Read more ...

Staff can access the Issues page in Customer First and determine if there is an outstanding activity preventing the customer completing their transaction.

7

Activity List (AL) screen and follow-up items + Read more ...

The AL screen may display an outstanding or started activity that needs investigating and/or finalising prior to completing a REI activity.

For claim activities, see Cancelling claim activities and re-indexing claims. To correctly re-index a new claim, staff may need to seek TSO support.

8

Benefit status and possible problems + Read more ...

Reviewing the benefit status line can indicate if there is a current suspension or cancellation of the customer's payment that could be rectified by conducting a thorough investigation and conversation with the customer (e.g. SUS/CAN).

See Restoration of payments.

9

Display on Access DOCs + Read more ...

Display on Access DOCs may provide information alerting Service Officers to any difficulties the customer may have reporting employment income.

Mutual obligation requirements - job search + Read more ...

Interaction Record Notes: Customer did not satisfy mutual obligation requirements for this reporting period.

Action: Did the customer declare employment income for this reporting period? Is the customer a principal carer and should have a Part Time Work (PTW) activity coded as their more appropriate activity? Contact the customer to update the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen.

To record an approved activity in Process Direct:

  • Key 'AEX' in the Super Key field, select [Enter]
  • The Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen will display
  • Select + Add New Row
  • The Create Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) modal will display
  • Complete the Activity Type, Start date and End date, select Save
  • Complete the Receipt Date and Channel fields, select Save
  • Select Assess and address any errors/warnings
  • Finalise the transaction by selecting Assess again, then select Finish. Record details of the update on the Finalise screen
  • Select Finalise to complete the transaction

The system will determine if a new Job Plan is to be negotiated. The customer will get the appropriate self service questions in the future.

If the reason the mutual obligation requirements are not met is reasonable (e.g., customer advised they were ill and have a medical certificate to provide), Service Officer to advise the medical certificate must be provided or record of activity completed must be provided.

Note: principal carers can have 2 fortnights approved of not meeting requirements before they have to be connected to a provider.

7

Carers (travel/activity time) + Read more ...

Interaction Record Notes: Customer was advised their report was not successful and to contact Centrelink for their report to be completed and payment made. Customer on Carer Payment (CP) and has declared hours exceeding the total travel/activity time of 25 hours.

Action: Check if customer or partner are receiving CP (XWP) - CAR/CUR-XWP (or CZR or CNP equivalent) shows on the Benefit Status line. The Assessment Type of XWP is always shown on the Care Assessment Result Summary (SCRR) screen.

If receiving CP (XWP), the customer is not required to give hours of paid employment or other activity hours. However, self-service channels still request this.

  • Finalise the report without updating CPAS/CPAD screens (the Carer Payment Details section is not displayed for CP (XWP) customers in the Earnings and Reporting tool)
  • Advise the customer they can continue to report using self-service channels but:
    • can advise in any of the self service reporting channels that their activity hours do not exceed 25 hours in either week they are reporting for. This will mean they do not need to talk to a Service Officer to complete their report
    • must still report the full amount of their or their partner's gross employment income

If not receiving CP (XWP), discuss with customer eligibility for CP and travel/activity time exceeding 25 hours and determine if they are still eligible for CP.

See Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities.

8

Disability Support Pension (hours exceed JCA) + Read more ...

Interaction Record Notes: Customer was advised their report was not successful and to contact Centrelink for their report to be completed and payment made. Customer on Disability Support Pension (DSP) and has declared hours exceeding the allowable working hours for DSP (i.e. all DSP customers can do paid employment for less than 30 hours per week without DSP being suspended or cancelled). Reports will be unsuccessful if hours for a fortnight exceed 59 hours. Note: if the type of employment income is Supported Wage System (SWS) or Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE) there is no maximum allowable hours.

Action: Discuss with customer expected duration of their employment and support available for Commencing or returning to work or self-employment Disability Support Pension (DSP).

Customer is trying to add a duplicate employer with the same ABN as an existing employer + Read more ...

  • If any duplicate employer entries are identified, determine the correct employer entry. Factors to consider are:
    • Do any of the employer entries have an Employer Verification status of STP?

What can be done to limit failed self service reports? + Read more ...

Self service reports can be unsuccessful for various reasons. On sign up for self service reporting, Service Officers should attempt to limit the amount of unsuccessful reports by checking the following on the customer's record:

  • Discuss with the customer at initial registration contact, the types of questions asked based on the items outlined in the Job Plan and requirements listed on the Activity and Exemption (AEX) screen. The customer needs to be aware of their mutual obligation or participation requirements to make sure they understand the questions they may be asked
  • Encourage customers with an active phone account to register for SMS messaging. This makes sure customers have the correct access to report a change of circumstances
  • Encourage customers to register for Online Letters (OLL). This enables customers to get their mail within 2 days and have quick access to their reporting dates
  • Check customer's Partner Permitted to Enquire (PPE) status. To make sure maximum benefit from self service, reporting customers should be encouraged to have PPE set to 'yes' if they are partnered
  • Use minimal abbreviations when coding an employer's trading name. When coding employment income for customers, make sure minimal abbreviations are used in employer's trading name etc. Make sure the employer's Australian Business Number (ABN) or Withholding Payer Number (WPN) is correct
  • Do not use 'dummy' held stimulus activities. The use of these activities prevents customers from completing their report via self service and creates additional 'drop through' work. These activities also stop transactions from other government departments coming across to the customer's record
  • Check interpreter coding to make sure details are still current. Remove unnecessary interpreter coding if appropriate
  • Make sure self-employment details are coded correctly. Use the Self Employment/Contract script for all updates to ensure appropriate reviews are created. Cease businesses where applicable instead of coding zero for income or percentage owned
  • Make sure apprentice employers have a 'Y' in the Apprentice Employer field on the Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen. This ensures the employment income coded is recognised correctly and does not 'drop through'
  • Make sure part-time work (PTW) is coded on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen where appropriate. If PTW is not coded on AEX, for customers meeting their participation requirements through part-time work, self service will ask the customer the question about job search. If the customer answers 'No' to meeting job search requirements as they are participating in an activity, a handoff/'drop through' will occur
