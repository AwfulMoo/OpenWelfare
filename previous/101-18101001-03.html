Actioning a work item or review for ParentsNext

Table 1

Step

Action

1

ParentsNext Servicing Actions + Read more ...

If:

  • undertaking a ParentsNext appointment, see ParentsNext appointments and processing
  • a customer contacts regarding ParentsNext, go to Step 2
  • actioning a work item in relation to a ParentsNext review or Manual Follow Up, See ParentsNext appointments and processing
  • the customer has a Services Australia ParentsNext appointment booked and a 'Customer's JSR is inactive - Investigation required' message appears on the Customer Issues page, go to Step 7
  • a ParentsNext provider or Account Manager contacts regarding ParentsNext servicing, if it is:
    • A ParentsNext provider, advise them they must contact their Department of Education, Skills and Employment Account Manager, procedure ends here
    • An Account Manager, advise them to contact the Department of Education, Skills and Employment directly, procedure ends here

2

Customer contacts regarding ParentsNext + Read more ...

If the customer contacts:

  • to enquire why they have been selected for ParentsNext, go to Step 3
  • to reschedule their Services Australia ParentsNext appointment, go to Step 4
  • as their Parenting Payment has been suspended under ParentsNext, go to Step 5
  • as their Parenting Payment has been cancelled, go to Step 6
  • to advise they are unable to attend their face to face appointment but can now provide a contact telephone number in order to have their appointment completed as a phone appointment, go to Step 8
  • in response to receiving a Q164 regarding study or leave with a job to return to, go to Step 9
  • to commence or cease voluntary participation, see Step 1 in the Commencing or ceasing voluntary participation table
  • to advise they are unable to participate in ParentsNext due to exceptional circumstances, for example, due to pregnancy, a temporary incapacity, serious illness, homelessness or a natural disaster for which they may be eligible for an exemption. See: ParentsNext exits and exemptions. Procedure ends here
  • with a general enquiry about ParentsNext, for example, confirming a ParentsNext appointment or their requirement to participate in ParentsNext, assist them with their enquiry. Procedure ends here
  • regarding their existing provider referral, for example to discuss their ParentsNext provider servicing arrangements, to request a transfer, to provide feedback or to lodge a complaint. Advise the customer to contact their ParentsNext provider directly or the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations. Procedure ends here

3

Customer enquires why they have been selected for ParentsNext + Read more ...

Eligibility for ParentsNext is automatically determined by the system based on the information contained on the customer's record at the time of the eligibility assessment.

Note: where a temporary address is recorded for 13 weeks or more it will be used to determine eligibility for ParentsNext.

  • Navigate to the ParentsNext Summary page to determine the customer's reason for selection. The reason will be either:
    • Early School Leaver - they are under 22 and haven't completed Year 12 or an equivalent qualification or higher
    • 2 Years on ISP - they are 22 or over, haven't completed Year 12 or an equivalent qualification or higher and have been getting income support continuously for between 2 and 4 years
    • 4 Years on ISP - they are 22 or over and have been getting income support continuously for more than 4 years (the highest level of education achieved is not relevant for this selection reason)
  • Advise the customer:
    • why they have been selected based on their circumstances
    • if they were selected for the reason of Early School Leaver or 2 Years on ISP and have completed Year 12 or an equivalent qualification advise them to provide verification prior to their appointment (they can use the Centrelink Express Plus mobile app to do this) as it may impact on their requirement to participate
    • about the ParentsNext program
    • confirm the date and time of their scheduled ParentsNext appointment
    • they will have the opportunity to provide additional information about their circumstances at the appointment
    • they will be given more information at their ParentsNext appointment

Procedure ends here.

4

Customer requests to reschedule their Services Australia ParentsNext appointment + Read more ...

If the customer requests to reschedule for a date:

  • within 2 days of their initial appointment date advise the customer they are able to do so online
  • outside of 2 days from the initial appointment date or they are genuinely unable to reschedule online, Service Officers may reschedule the appointment. ParentsNext appointments may only be rescheduled twice (including any online reschedules). Therefore, Service Officers must check if the customer has already previously rescheduled their appointment via the appointments tab on the Customer Summary page. If the customer has already rescheduled their appointment:
    • twice, advise them they must attend their next scheduled ParentsNext appointment or their Parenting Payment (PP) will be suspended
    • less than twice, reschedule the appointment, see View, reschedule or cancel an appointment in the Centrelink Appointment System for more information

Procedure ends here.

5

Parenting Payment has been suspended under ParentsNext + Read more ...

If the suspension is the result of:

  • failure to attend a Services Australia ParentsNext appointment, see ParentsNext suspension and restoration
  • compliance action submitted by a ParentsNext provider, see Targeted Compliance Framework
  • failure to respond to a Q164 for study or leave with a job to return to for greater than 12 months review, go to Step 9

6

Parenting Payment has been cancelled + Read more ...

If the cancellation is the result of:

  • Failure to attend a Services Australia ParentsNext appointment, advise the customer:
    • their Parenting Payment cannot be restored, and
    • they will need to lodge a new claim if they still require income support assistance. For more information see Claiming Parenting Payment (PP) Procedure ends here
  • compliance action submitted by a ParentsNext Provider, see Targeted Compliance Framework
  • Any other reason, see Cancellation of Parenting Payment

7

Customer with a Services Australia ParentsNext appointment booked has a 'Customer's JSR is inactive - Investigation required' message on the Customer Issues page + Read more ...

Customers selected for ParentsNext are required to attend a phone or face to face appointment with Services Australia.

If the customer does not have an active job seeker status, the message 'Customer's JSR is inactive - Investigation required' will display on the Customer Issues page.

Service Officers that are not undertaking ParentsNext appointments must not action this issue and/or refer the customer to a ParentsNext or Workforce Australia provider.

If the customer enquires about their ParentsNext appointment:

  • provide general information about the program
  • advise they will be contacted at their appointment time, where they will be further informed about the program and referred to a ParentsNext provider where appropriate

Procedure ends here.

8

Customer unable to attend their face to face appointment but can now provide a contact telephone number + Read more ...

  • update the customer's telephone number
  • cancel the face to face appointment
  • book a ParentsNext appointment via phone. For information on booking appointments, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System
  • confirm details of the new appointment with the customer and advise they will be contacted at their appointment time, where they will be further informed about the program and referred to a ParentsNext provider where appropriate
  • record a DOC detailing the customer was advised of the new appointment details

Procedure ends here.

9

Customer contacts in response to receiving a Q164 or PP suspension due to failing to respond to a Q164 + Read more ...

Where the customer has study or leave with a job to return to recorded that is greater than 12 months in duration, the details must be reviewed at the end of the 12 months. If an outbound contact was unsuccessful, the customer is sent a Q164 to contact. Where the customer fails to respond to the Q164 their payment will have been suspended.

Advise the customer:

  • They were sent the letter as they have previously advised of leave/study that was greater than 12 months
  • as the study/leave is greater than 12 months, it is being reviewed to determine if there have been any changes since they advised of the details
  • Where PP was suspended, it was because they did not respond to the letter to contact

Navigate to the ParentsNext Summary page to check the study/leave end date. For:

  • Study – select the twisty at the Study section
  • Leave – select the twisty at the Leave With a Job To Return To section

Confirm with the customer if the:

  • study/leave is still current, and
  • whether the study/leave end date remains the same as previously advised and recorded

DOC the record with details of the conversation including:

  • whether the study/leave is still current or has ended
  • where the study/leave is still current but the end date has changed, ensure the new end date is included
  • Advise the customer at the expiry of the study/leave period, they may be selected for ParentsNext again in the future

Note: ParentsNext trained Service Officers will subsequently update the study/leave details on the customer's record based on what is detailed in the DOC.

Is the customer's PP suspended for failing to respond to a Q164 – Request to contact letter sent?

  • Yes, see Restoration of Parenting Payment (PP)
  • No, procedure ends here

Commencing or ceasing voluntary participation

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Commencing or ceasing voluntary participation + Read more ...

A customer advises they wish to:

  • cease voluntary participation in ParentsNext, go to Step 2
  • commence voluntary participation in ParentsNext, do not book a future ParentsNext appointment, go to Step 4

2

Cease voluntary participation + Read more ...

Navigate to the ParentsNext Summary page to determine which organisation registered the customer as a voluntary participant. Their status reason will be either Services Australia recorded or Provider recorded.

Was the customer registered as a voluntary participant by the agency?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, Services Australia cannot exit a voluntary participant who was registered by a ParentsNext provider. Advise the customer to contact their ParentsNext provider if they wish to be exited. Procedure ends here

3

Customer registered as a voluntary participant by the agency + Read more ...

If the customer was registered as a voluntary participant by the agency, Service Officers can undertake the exit.

  • Launch the Volunteer guided procedure via the Volunteer button located under the Volunteer section heading on the ParentsNext Summary page, and navigate to the Maintain page
  • Does the customer wish to volunteer? field, select No
  • Comments field: record that the customer requested to cease volunteering
  • Navigate to the Review screen and finalise the guided procedure
  • Advise the customer they can elect to volunteer again at any time if they are eligible, and if their circumstances change they may be contacted regarding compulsory participation

Note: if there is a delay in the update correctly reassessing eligibility for ParentsNext, run the daily driver. Procedure ends here.

4

Commence voluntary participation + Read more ...

Where a customer contacts and requests to voluntarily participate in ParentsNext, they must be assisted at the first point of contact and a future ParentsNext appointment must not be booked.

The Volunteer guided procedure will only be enabled and selectable if the customer meets the eligibility criteria for voluntary participation and they have not been selected for ParentsNext as a compulsory participant.

Does the customer meet the eligibility criteria for voluntary participation?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, advise the customer they do not currently meet the eligibility criteria for voluntary participation. Procedure ends here

5

Customer eligible for voluntary participation + Read more ...

As the customer is eligible to participate voluntarily, they need to be registered via the Volunteer guided procedure. Where the Volunteer guided procedure is not enabled and selectable, the customer is not eligible to voluntarily participate.

  • Navigate to the ParentsNext Summary page
  • Launch the Volunteer guided procedure from the Volunteer button (hover the curser over the word 'Volunteer') located under the Volunteer section heading on the ParentsNext summary page and ask the customer the eligibility questions
  • Select the relevant radio button based on the customer's responses to each question

Does the customer wish to volunteer? Field: This will automatically default to 'No' if the customer's responses result in them being ineligible, otherwise select 'Yes'

  • Comments field: Record relevant notes based on whether the customer was eligible to volunteer or not
  • Navigate to the Review screen and finalise the guided procedure

Note: if there is a delay in the update correctly reassessing eligibility for ParentsNext, run the daily driver.

Go to Step 6.

6

Job seeker status + Read more ...

A customer who has elected to voluntarily participate in ParentsNext must have an active job seeker status (JSR/ACT) before they can be referred to a ParentsNext provider.

In Process Direct:

  • go to Customer Summary
  • key the customer's Customer Reference Number (CRN) in the CRN field
  • in the customer's record, select > Participation Summary

Does the customer have an active job seeker status?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Activate jobseeker ID + Read more ...

On the Job Seeker registration & provider referral information screen:

  • select Start job seeker registration to register the customer. Note: the Job Seeker Snapshot does not need to be completed
  • Job Seeker Registration No Provider Referral Required field, select No (If additional questions present, complete as per the customer's responses)
  • Select Next
  • On the Referral Determination screen, messages will display 'Customer is a ParentsNext participant, use the Online Diary to refer the customer to a ParentsNext provider'
  • Select Next

8

Previous participation + Read more ...

Where the customer was a compulsory participant within the previous six months, they will be automatically reconnected to their previous provider without the requirement to create a referral.

Was the customer a compulsory ParentsNext participant in the previous six months?

  • Yes, Go to Step 9
  • No, Go to Step 10

9

Customer compulsory ParentsNext participant in previous six months + Read more ...

In Process Direct:

  • go to Customer Summary
  • key the customer's CRN in the CRN field
  • in the customer's record, select > Participation Summary

Advise the customer:

  • of the provider's details
  • the provider will contact them directly or they can choose to contact the provider if they prefer not to wait

Note: If there is a delay in the provider details displaying, run the daily driver.

Procedure ends here

10

Refer a voluntary participant to a ParentsNext provider + Read more ...

E009JW is a generic error code that may be encountered within the Job seeker registration workflow or the Online Diary.

If an interpreter is required at the ParentsNext provider appointment, the appointment must be booked for no earlier than 7 days in the future to allow for the provider to arrange an interpreter.

In Process Direct:

  • go to Customer Summary
  • key the customer's CRN in the CRN field
  • in the customer's record, select > Participation Summary
  • select Online Diary
  • select Create new referral > SPP-Parents next provider > Create Referral. The workflow will go to the List Providers screen
  • A list of all ParentsNext providers available within the customer's employment region will appear on the List Providers screen:
    • A list of all ParentsNext providers who have appointments available within the next 2 working days will appear on the List Providers screen
    • Select the ParentsNext provider. Where multiple providers are available, ask the customer their preferred provider. If a customer is indecisive, service officers must not provide a recommendation
  • On the Available Appointments screen, select a suitable appointment (no earlier than 7 days in the future if an interpreter is required)
    • If there are no appointments available showing, manually select a date using Future Appointment Date for more appointments
  • When an appointment has been selected, the 'Appointment Detail' page will display:
    • Confirm details of the appointment with the customer
    • Additional Requirements: field, enter information the provider may need to be aware of, for example, if an interpreter is required
    • Appointment Notification: field, select Face to Face (where the Services Australia appointment was conducted at a service centre) or Phone (where the Services Australia appointment was conducted via phone)
  • Select the 'Book Appointment' button to finalise the appointment booking process
  • The Appointment Outcome page will display:
    • The details of the appointment
    • Was this appointment created in error?: field, this question should be left blank.
    • Select the Finish button
  • From the Provider Appointment page, read the customer the information contained within the message displayed
  • Select Continue to finalise the procedure
  • Advise the customer no further confirmation from Services Australia will be provided (that is, they will not receive a letter or electronic message confirming the appointment details) however they will receive a reminder about their appointment from their provider 1 day before the appointment. Based on their preferences this will either be an SMS, email or a reminder via their Workforce Australia inbox
