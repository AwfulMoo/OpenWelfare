Description

Identifying records updated via the data exchange program or auto-indexation + Read more ...

Customers who have had their foreign pensions updated through the bulk data exchange process or auto-indexation will have Data Exchange (DEX) or Consumer Price Index (CPI) appear in the Channel: field on the Foreign Pension Details (FPD) screen.

Note: channel code 'RVL' will appear after each monthly exchange rate revaluation; it may be necessary to check older dates of event to determine whether DEX or CPI updates have been made in the past.

These codes were introduced to protect and identify foreign pension rate information obtained direct from the paying authority. Only authorised users (for example, International Services (CIS)) can change amounts after these codes have been applied.

2

Date of Effect for reassessments + Read more ...

Auto-indexation increases (CPI) are applied from the 1st of the applicable month. These updates are applied prior to the monthly exchange rate revaluation process. The exception is United Kingdom (UK) increases which apply from early April each year.

The DEX update processes reassess entitlements using a Date of Receipt of 'T' for today. This means no arrears or debts will be automatically generated for DEX updates.

The 'favourable determination' rules under social security law apply in respect of Australian payments that increase whilst 'unfavourable determination' rules will apply when potential debts are investigated following DEX updates.

International Services (CIS) is responsible for investigating and raising any debts that arise from DEX updates.

3

Reviews and Appeals + Read more ...

The normal review and appeals system applies.

Customer initiated reviews relating to DEX updates are the responsibility of the customer's local service centre.

Information received via data exchange or a rate updated via auto-indexation is considered to be accurate. Customers who advise that rates of foreign pension that are being maintained are incorrect should provide suitable proof from the paying authority such as a pension statement, letter or certificate. A bank statement is not sufficient evidence of the gross rate of pension in the original currency.

However, only authorised users, for example International Services (CIS), can change amounts after DEX or CPI updates have been applied. If a customer supplies new information from the paying authority about their rate of foreign pension, details must be forwarded to CIS.

CIS is responsible for appeals relating to any debts that are raised following DEX updates.

The Resources page contains a link to CIS contact details.

4

Debt recovery + Read more ...

Standard withholdings for debts raised because of DEX updates are set at $50 per fortnight.

Customers may choose to negotiate repayments in accordance with normal debt recovery procedures.

Current DEX/CPI status for various countries

Table 2: This table describes the current status of data exchange (DEX) and auto indexation of foreign pensions (CPI) for various countries.

Item

Description

Countries A to D + Read more ...

Austria

A data exchange process (DEX) with Austria has been agreed in principle but not yet finalised. There is no expected date for implementation.

Each year, customers residing in Australia generally have any Austrian pension (Types: AGE/ INV/ SUR/ RSP/ SER/ WAR/ WID) auto-indexed (CPI) on 1 January.

Canada

A data exchange process (DEX) with Canada has been agreed in principle but not yet finalised. There is no expected date for implementation.

Each year, customers generally have any Canada Pension Plan pensions (Types: CPA/ CPI/ CPS/ QPA/ QPI/ QPS) auto-indexed (CPI) in January and any Canadian Old Age Security pensions (Types: AGE) auto-indexed on 1 January, 1 April, 1 July and 1 October.

Croatia

A data exchange process (DEX) with Croatia has been agreed but not yet finalised. Discussions are continuing to sign a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) for implementation.

Some preliminary alignment files have resulted in a number of customer updates via the DEX process.

At this time, a file of all deceased customers who were in receipt of a Croatian pension in Australia is sent to Croatia every 4 weeks.

There is no auto-indexation (CPI) for Croatian pensions.

Cyprus

A data exchange process (DEX) with Cyprus has been agreed and implemented. Discussions are continuing to extend the process.

Cyprus sends a file of Cypriot pension rates (Type: AGE/ INV/ SUR) for customers in Australia in March each year.

A file of all deceased customers who were in receipt of a Cypriot pension in Australia is sent to Cyprus every 4 weeks.

There is no auto-indexation (CPI) for Cypriot pensions.

Denmark

A data exchange process (DEX) with Denmark has been agreed and implemented. Discussions are continuing to extend the process.

Denmark sends a file of Danish pension rates (Type: AGE/ INV/ SUR) for customers in Australia in September each year.

A file of all deceased customers who were in receipt of a Danish pension in Australia is sent to Denmark every 4 weeks.

There is no auto-indexation (CPI) for Danish pensions.

Finland

A data exchange process (DEX) with Finland has been agreed and implemented. Discussions are continuing to extend the process.

The Finnish employment pension fund sends a file of Finnish pension rates (Type: ETK/ INV/ SUR) for customers in Australia and Finland in January each year. The Finnish National pension fund sends a file of Finnish pension rates (Type: AGE) for customers in Australia in February each year.

A file of all deceased customers who were in receipt of a Finnish pension in Australia is sent to Finland every 4 weeks.

There is no auto-indexation (CPI) for Finnish pensions.

Germany

A data exchange process (DEX) with Germany has been agreed and implemented. Discussions are continuing to extend the process.

Germany sends a file of German pension rates (Type: AGE/ INV/ SUR) for customers in Australia in September each year.

A file of all Australian customers residing in Germany is sent to Germany each year in September requesting updated rate information.

Note: the German authorities may require a customer's specific authorisation/consent before information can be included in the DEX process.

A file of all deceased customers who were in receipt of a German pension in Australia is sent to Germany every 4 weeks.

There is no auto-indexation (CPI) for German pensions.

There is no auto-indexation (CPI) for Ireland pensions.

Italy

Some preliminary alignment files have resulted in a number of customer updates via the DEX process.

A file of all deceased customers who were in receipt of an Italian pension in Australia is sent to Italy every 4 weeks.

There is no auto-indexation (CPI) for Italian pensions.

New Zealand

An automated liaison process (DEX) with New Zealand was implemented in July 2002. Each day, a file of specified Change in Circumstances (CICs) for mutual customers is sent by each country.

Information exchanged relates to any Australian income support payment where NZ pension (Type: NZS/ NZI/ NZV) is recorded.

There is no auto-indexation (CPI) for NZ pensions but changes in rate due to indexation are included in the CICs process.

Note: International Services (CIS) is responsible for the automated liaison process with NZ. For information, see Agreement liaisons, NZ CICs and exchange of information.

Countries U to Z + Read more ...

United Kingdom (UK)

A data exchange process (DEX) with UK has been agreed and implemented. The arrangement covers UK State Pensions only (Type: AGE).

For customers residing in Australia, the UK advises of new grants each week and changes in rates each 6 weeks as well as a complete file of all customers on an annual basis.

A file of all Australian customers residing in the UK is sent to the UK each year in June requesting updated rate information.

A file of all deceased customers who were in receipt of a UK pension in Australia is sent to the UK every 4 weeks.

Each year, customers in specified countries* generally have any UK pensions (Types: AGE/ GHB/ GRA/ INV/ SER/ SUR/ WAR/ WID) auto-indexed (CPI) in early April.

* The UK Government do not apply cost of living increases while customers are in certain countries, including Australia.

United States of America (USA)

A data exchange process (DEX) with USA has been agreed in principle but not yet finalised.

A file of all deceased customers who were in receipt of a US pension in Australia is sent to the US every 4 weeks.

Each year, customers generally have any US pension (Types: AGE/ INV/ SOC/ SUR/ WID) auto-indexed (CPI) on 1 January.

6

Other countries + Read more ...

Other agreement countries (Belgium, Chile, Czech Republic, Greece, Hungary, Korea, Latvia, North Macedonia, Norway, Poland, Slovak Republic and Switzerland) have agreed in principle to a data exchange process and discussions are at various stages of progress.

Discussions are also ongoing discussions with Sweden.

There is no auto-indexation (CPI) of pensions from these countries.
