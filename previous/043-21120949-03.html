Before assessing the claim

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Locate an allocated claim + Read more ...

Open TFES Assessor Registry in G:\TTP\TFES Tool\TFES Assessor Registries.

View the list of allocated claims:

  • select oldest claim
  • note the Claimant Code shown in the activity

2

Access claimant record + Read more ...

Open TFES General in myHSI.

Select Search for Claimant, enter the Claimant Code found in step 1. Press search and select the active CRN or Claimant code.

In the View Claimant Details screen, review the following:

  • Notes tab – read Claimant Notes including:
    • general information
    • notes relevant to horses
    • wharf to wharf rates
    • any notes relating to eligibility or Extension Of Time (EOT) requests (this could determine if the claim is to be assessed or rejected)
    • information relating to transport companies
  • Comments tab – read Claimant Comments including:
    • information relating to eligibility
    • details of recent or relevant communication with the claimant

Is there information on the comments tab and/or notes tab to indicate the claim should be rejected?

  • Yes, see Table 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Check the manual claim form is complete + Read more ...

If the claimant has lodged a claim using a manual claim form, check the claimant has:

  • used the current version of the:
    • Claim for Assistance – Domestic goods form (TAS002), or
    • Claim for Assistance – Goods going to other markets form (TAS022)
  • included:
    • supporting documentation with the claim
    • the TFES – Additional information – Horses form (TAS021)

If the claimant has used an incorrect or old form, contact them by phone or email:

  • ask them to resubmit the claim on correct form
  • tell them how to get the correct form from Services Australia website

Check the:

  • Claimant name and contact details on TAS002 or TAS022 are the same as the Claimant Details screen in myHSI
  • Declaration on the TAS002 or TAS022 has been completed and signed by an approved signatory

If details do not match, contact the claimant by phone or email, ask them for an updated TAS002 or TAS022.

  • Make 2 genuine attempts. If first contact is unsuccessful, make a second attempt within 48 hours
  • If possible, leave a message with a name and number for them to contact and a position email address
  • Tell the claimant:
    • they have 21 days to lodge the updated form
    • if the information is not received, the claim will be rejected
  • On the claim Comments in myHSI and in the Comments/Details section of the activity in the TFES Assessor Register key:
    • details of the request for information
    • date of the request
    • name of contact person
  • Change the status of the activity in the TFES Assessor tool to ‘awaiting claimant response’

4

Print the assessment sheet + Read more ...

Use the assessment sheet to record the assessment of the claim. The assessment sheet is different depending on how the claimant lodges their claim.

Manual claim:

  • The TAS002 or TAS022 form and supporting documents will be uploaded to the claim Documents tab at the time of claim registration
  • Print a copy of the claim form

Online Tasmanian bulk upload system (TBUS) claim:

  • Locate TFES Bulk Claim File in the Claim’s Supporting Documents tab by selecting the active Reference ID
  • Print the TBUS spreadsheet to record the assessment, or
  • Take a snip of the claim lines from the Claim Details screen
  • Print the snip

Online Third Party Portal claim:

  • Take a snip of the claim lines from the Claim Details screen
  • Print the snip

Assess the claim

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Additional Information form + Read more ...

Each horse claim must have a TFES - Additional information – Horses form (TAS021) included with the supporting documents for the claim.

For horse claims lodged:

  • online, the claimant uploads the TAS021 to the Supporting Documents tab
  • using a TAS002 or TAS022:
    • the TAS021 is lodged with the claim form, and;
    • uploaded with the claim form and other documents to the Supporting Documents tab by TFES admin staff when the claim is registered

Go to the Supporting Documents tab and check the claim documents.

Has the claimant included a completed TAS021?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, see Table 4

2

Check the Additional Information form is complete + Read more ...

Check the claimant has completed the TAS021, including:

  • Name of the horse
  • Where the horse was bred
  • The direction that the horse travelled, either:
    • Northbound
    • Southbound, or
    • Brood mare on a round trip
  • Completed the declaration section

Has the claimant completed the required fields on the TAS021?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

TAS021 not included or missing information + Read more ...

Contact the claimant by phone, email or both.

  • make 2 genuine attempts. If first contact is unsuccessful, make a second attempt within 48 hours
  • if possible, leave a message with a name and number for them to contact and a position email address
  • tell the claimant:
    • they have 21 days to provide a correctly completed TAS021 for each mare
    • if the information is not received, the claim will be rejected

On the Claim Comments in myHSI and in the Comments/Details section of the activity in the TFES Assessor Tool key:

  • details of the request for information
  • date of the request, and
  • claimant contact person

Change the status of the activity in the TFES Assessor tool to ‘awaiting claimant response’

Has the claimant provided the requested information within 21 days?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, see Table 4

4

Determine type of horse claim + Read more ...

The details entered on the TAS021 determines the type of claim assessment to follow.

Did the horse travel northbound?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No,
    • for southbound horses, see Table 3
    • for a brood mare on a round trip, see Step 4 in Additional information – Horse TAS021 table of Brood mare claim assessment for TFES

5

Determine eligibility of a northbound horse + Read more ...

Northbound horses must be bred in Tasmania to be eligible for assistance.

Check question 2 on the TAS021.

Was the horse bred in Tasmania?

  • Yes, for:
    • manual claims, go to Step 6
    • online claims, go to Step 8
  • No, horse is not eligible, see to Table 4

6

Determine northbound horse claim type for manual claims + Read more ...

For claims lodged using a manual claim form, check the claim form.

Is the claim lodged on a TAS002 form?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, the claim is lodged on TAS022 form. To assess a claim for horse going to other markets, see Northbound other markets claim assessment for TFES

7

Determine the type of manual claim for domestic northbound horses + Read more ...

The TFES – Claim for assistance – domestic goods form (TAS002) is used for:

  • Horses shipped from Tasmania to the Mainland of Australia
  • Horses shipped from the Mainland of Tasmania to King Island or the Furneaux Group

Check the TAS002. Is the destination shown in column D a state on the mainland of Australia?

  • Yes, assess the claim, see Northbound domestic goods claim assessment for TFES
  • No, the destination is King Island or the Furneaux Group. To assess the claim, go to Intrastate (King Island and Furneaux Group) claim assessment for TFES

8

Determine northbound horse claim type for online claims + Read more ...

For claims lodged online, check the claim details in myHSI.

In the View TFES Claim screen, under the heading Type, is the claim type TFES horse or KIFG?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, the claim type is TFES Other Markets, assess a claim for a horse going to other markets, see Northbound other markets claim assessment for TFES

9

Determine the claim type for northbound online claims + Read more ...

To determine the claim type check the View TFES Claim screen.

Under the heading Type, is the claim type is TFES Horse?

  • Yes, assess the claim. Go to Northbound domestic goods claim assessment for TFES
  • No, the claim type is KIFG, assess the claim. Go to Intrastate (King Island and Furneaux Group) claim assessment for TFES

Process southbound horse claim

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Determine the origin of the southbound horse + Read more ...

Check the origin of the southbound horse. This is found:

  • in column C of the TAS002 for manual claims
  • in View Line Item under the heading Origin for online claims

Is the origin on the mainland of Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the origin is King Island or the Furneaux Group. To assess the claim, see Intrastate (King Island and Furneaux Group) claim assessment for TFES

2

Determine the eligibility of the southbound horse + Read more ...

To be eligible for assistance horses shipped from the mainland of Australia to Tasmania must be:

  • Bred on the mainland of Australia, or
  • Imported from overseas, and:
    • shipped to Tasmania via an Australian mainland port
    • have no Australian equivalent

Check the question 2 on the TAS021.

Was the horse bred on the mainland of Australia or overseas?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, see Table 4

3

Determine the claimant category + Read more ...

To be eligible for assistance from the mainland of Australia to Tasmania the Claimant Category must be either:

  • Agriculture, Forestry or Fishing (Primary Producer)
  • Manufacturing or Mining

The claimant record must show N/A in the Ineligible Southbound.

Check the View Claimant Details screen in myHSI and record the Claimant Category.

Is the claimant eligible southbound?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, see Table 4

4

Assess the southbound horse claim + Read more ...

Using the Claimant Category from Step 3, determine the southbound horse claim type.

  • Claimant is a Primary Producer, assess the claim, see Southbound primary producer claim for TFES (P Scheme)
  • Claimant is a Manufacturer, assess the claim, see Southbound manufacturers and miners claims for TFES (M Scheme)

Reject the claim

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Claim to be rejected + Read more ...

Is the claim a manual claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the claim is an online claim, go to Step 7

2

Code the manual horse claim in the system + Read more ...

A manual claim must have the claim data entered before it can be rejected.

Access TFES General on myHSI:

  • Chose: View Activity List
  • Activity: All
  • Role/user: Assessor
  • Enter the Claim ID in the box beside: Claim ID
  • Tick box: Show Unassigned
  • Select Show
  • Claim will appear with the Status: New
  • Select Process Claim under the Activity heading
  • Select Continue this will allocate the claim to the user

Select Add Line Item

3

Add line items + Read more ...

Enter all claim lines from the TAS020 or TAS022:

  • Key data from the claim form
  • For claims with multiple lines, select Add line for each additional line
  • When all claim lines are entered, select Continue
  • Select Submit to go to Activity List

4

Activity list + Read more ...

Locate the claim in the Activity List:

  • Activity: All
  • Role/user: Assessor
  • Type the claim number into Claim ID
  • Select: Show
  • Select: Process claim
  • Select: Continue

5

Reject the claim + Read more ...

In myHSI Assessment Results, select Reject Claim from the dropdown menu.

  • Select rejection reason from the dropdown menu:
    • If Other is selected, go to Step 6
  • Select Complete
  • Go to Claim Comments tab:
    • select Add Comment
    • key details of rejection reason
  • Upload all supporting documents, including:
    • completed claim assessment form
    • copy of rejection letter
  • Put the rejection letter in an envelope and put it in mail tray
  • Update TFES Assessor Tool with:
    • details of rejection reason in Comments/Details field
    • change status of activity to completed
  • Save

Go to Step 8

6

Issue a manual rejection letter + Read more ...

When there is not a suitable rejection reason, select Other from the dropdown menu. When this option is selected, a system letter will not print.

  • Issue Manual Claim Rejection letter (Z2224), detailing the rejection reason in the explanation section of the letter
  • In myHSI Claim Comments key details of rejection reason
  • Select Complete
  • Upload all supporting documents, including:
    • completed claim assessment form, and
    • copy of rejection letter
  • Put the rejection letter in an envelope and put it in the mail tray
  • Update TFES Assessor Registry with:
    • details of rejection reason in Comments/Details field
    • change status of activity to completed
    • Save

Go to Step 8

7

Reject online horse claim + Read more ...

In myHSI Assessment Results, select Reject Claim from the dropdown menu.

  • Select rejection reason from the dropdown box:
    • If Other is selected, go to Step 6
  • Select Complete
  • Go to Claim Comments tab:
    • select Add Comment
    • key details of rejection reason
    • select Complete
  • Upload all supporting documents, including:
    • completed claim assessment form
    • copy of rejection letter
  • Put rejection letter in an envelope and put it in the mail tray
  • Update TFES Assessor tool with:
    • details of rejection reason in Comments/Details field
    • change status of activity to completed
    • Save

Go to Step 8

8

Upload supporting documents + Read more ...

Upload all supporting documents and assessment sheet to the Claim Supporting Documents tab.

Check the scanned documents are legible and complete.

Supporting documents include:

  • original TAS002, TAS022 or screen shot of claim as lodged by the claimant
  • assessed claim forms, screen shots and spreadsheets
  • invoices
  • consignment notes
  • supplier invoices
  • notes to claimant
  • emails related to claim. If the email has general information included, upload a copy to the Claimant Documents tab
  • any additional information provided by the claimant relating to the claim
  • manual rejection letter if issued
