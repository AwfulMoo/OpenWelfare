Actioning the review + Read more ...

Customers can complete the income stream review online by going to:

  • my.gov.au:
    • signing in to their myGov account
    • access Centrelink services
    • select 'You need to complete your income stream review' from the Reminders section

    • keying the One Time Access Code (OTAC). This is on their Income Stream reminder letter. The OTAC lets the customer, their nominee or a trusted third party complete the review online

Customers can ask a third party to complete the income stream review online on their behalf. For example, a financial advisor or any other person. The third party uses the OTAC.

Make sure the customer does not have any concerns about the third party seeing their Customer Reference Number (CRN). This is because the CRN is on the online form.

If the customer has a nominee, they will be sent a letter with their own OTAC.

3

Information needed to complete the review + Read more ...

The below information is needed to complete the review(s).

October review

Category 9 account-based income streams (types AIS, ALA, ALP)

  • 1 July or later account balance
    • the closing balance as at 30 June is usually the opening balance for 1 July. Therefore staff can accept the 30 June balance as the 1 July balance
  • this is the only information needed for Category 9 income streams

Category 2 account-based income streams (types AIS, ALA, ALP) and market-linked income streams (type MLI)

  • 1 July account balance
    • the closing balance as at 30 June is usually the opening balance for 1 July. Therefore staff can accept the 30 June balance as the 1 July balance
  • total gross annual income amount (for the current financial year), and
  • any commutations made since 1 July in the previous year. Check the below to see if there has been an update to the commutation:
    • the Pension/Annuities Details Variable (SUPV) screen
    • if a DOC has been recorded
  • additional information for some market linked income streams:
    • the expiry date for any market-linked income streams due to expire
    • commutations are generally not able to be made. If there has been a commutation, an income stream schedule is required to determine if it is allowable or not

April review

Category 9 account-based income streams (types AIS, ALA, ALP):

  • the most recent account balance as at 31 December or later

Category 2 account-based income streams (types AIS, ALA, ALP) and market-linked income streams (type MLI):

  • the most recent account balance as at 31 December or later in the current financial year
  • any commutations made in the current financial year

Has the customer made a commutation from a market linked income stream?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Commutation from a market linked income stream + Read more ...

The customer must provide an income stream schedule. Market-linked income streams are non-commutable. A schedule is needed to:

  • confirm the product is a market linked income stream
  • establish commutation reason, and
  • determine if the commutation is allowable or not

Check for scanned documents, go to the:

  • Documents icon in Process Direct, or
  • Document Tools in Customer First.

Has the schedule already been provided?

  • Yes, tell the customer that all the necessary information has been received. They do not need to take any further action:
    • scan the customer's schedule
    • check if the commutation is allowable or not, see Commutation of asset test exempt (ATE) income streams
    • if the customer's payment has been suspended, go to Step 7
    • update the income stream. For updates, see Step 8 in Table 4
    • procedure ends here
  • No,
    • tell the customer they must provide a schedule from their provider
    • procedure ends here until the schedule is returned

Suspended payment FSS + Read more ...

The customer will get a reminder letter(s) if they do not respond to the review. Their payment will auto-suspend FSS (No reply to income stream review) if they do not respond to these letters.

Note: due to limitations in the NSS system, payments will be manually suspended FRC (Fail to reply to correspondence) if they have not responded to the review or the reminder letter. See Cancellation, suspension and rejection reason codes for Newstart System (NSS).

    • Procedure ends here

ISRMGD and ISRMDD - significant decrease in account balance + Read more ...

ISRMGD: Cat 2 account-based income streams and market-linked income streams.

ISRMDD: Cat 9 account-based income streams.

When a customer advises of an account balance that is a significant decrease, they must give further details:

  • determine if the decrease is outside the threshold
  • discuss with them what has occurred with the funds
  • ask for further documents if the customer's verbal details are not enough

If supporting information is required, see Step 3 in Table 5.

Once other updates are complete, see Step 8 in Table 4.

4

ISRMGI and ISRMDI - significant increase in account balance + Read more ...

ISRMGI: Cat 2 account-based income streams and market-linked income streams.

ISRMDI: Cat 9 account-based income streams.

When a customer advises of an account balance that is a significant increase check the increase:

  • determine if the increase is outside the threshold
  • confirm where the funds came from. Additional funds cannot be added to an existing income stream product. If this has happened, the change is assessed as a full commutation and purchase of a new income stream

If the reason for the increase is identified from details on the customer's record, update their record. See Step 8 in Table 4.

If there is insufficient information on the record, contact the customer.

If contact is unsuccessful on all available numbers:

  • document the date and time of the attempted contact
  • place the work item on hold
  • try to make contact a second time at a different time of day. Try all available numbers and document the second attempt
  • if still unable to contact, ask for supporting information. See Step 3 in Table 5

If contact is successful and:

  • the details from the customer are not enough, ask for supporting information. See Step 3 in Table 5
  • a new income stream has been purchased, ask for a schedule for the new product. See Step 4 in Table 5
  • the increase is from investment returns, update the record. See Step 8 in Table 4

ISRMSC - customer has a payment status of suspended + Read more ...

The update could not complete because the customer's payment is suspended.

Service Officers must check to see if:

  • they can restore the customer's payment, and
  • the income stream update are complete

For help with restoring a payment, use the list of codes in the relevant payment below:

  • Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Age Pension
  • Cancellation of Austudy or Youth Allowance (YA) students and Australian Apprentices
  • Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Carer Payment (CP)
  • Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Newstart System (NSS)
  • Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Parenting Payment (PP)

Can the payment be restored?

  • Yes, restore the payment:
    • add a new entry to the Income Stream Details table (SUPV) by selecting the Add a new line icon on the right of the table
    • update details on the SUPV screen as per the information provided
    • the date of event (DOV) is the date of receipt (DOR) for either the date of the DOC on the Document List (DL) screen for the online updates, or the DOR of the scan
    • go to Step 13
  • No, place the review on hold until the customer's payment is due to be cancelled by the system. For further help, contact Technical Peer Support (TPS). Procedure ends here

ISRDBT + Read more ...

  • Key SUPS in Super Key to go to the Income Stream (SUPS, SUPI, SUPV) table. Delete any provisional data applied by the system
  • Take note of the data applied in the update. This will need to be re-applied using the same DOV, which will match the Date of Receipt (DOR)
  • Delete the provisional data in CoC if the activity is processed in Customer First or Process Direct
  • The handoff occurs when the auto-update applied by the provider data results in a big change to the customer's rate
  • Re-apply the customer’s supplied data using the original DOR as DOV
  • Make sure the provider data is reasonable and the rate change and any related debts or arrears are appropriate
  • If the income from the income stream has reduced significantly, check the customer's record for documents to determine the cause. For example, the customer's income may have reduced due to:
  • a partial commutation of a defined benefit income stream, or
  • a court order if the ex-partner is entitled to part of the income stream

Is there enough information to account for the customer’s update?

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No, request an income stream schedule. Place the activity on hold pending return of information

October review

For grandfathered account-based income streams (category 2) or market-linked income streams, update the below fields as needed:

  • Current account balance, key the later of the new 1 July account balance or the current account balance

9

Market linked income stream (MLI) in the final year + Read more ...

Due to a known system issue, the minimum and maximum calculation during the final year for MLI is not correct (NNU 12279).

If the error 'E022SN - The Gross Annual Income Amount must be between minimum ####.## and maximum ####.##' is displayed, complete the following workaround:

  • open the existing SUPI entry by selecting the Edit icon next to the product listed under the Income Streams (SUPS, SUPI, SUPV) table
  • note the product reference from the SUPI screen
  • cancel the current product by selecting OTH - Other in the Product Cancellation Reason field
  • Save
  • check the data shows nil income and asset value in the Income Stream Details (SUPV) table
  • make sure the latest line on SUPV is today’s DOV

Code new entry on Other Assets (OAS) screen:

  • Type: OTH
  • Description: SUPS MLI PRN (insert product reference number)
  • DOV: today
  • Value: if the product is not Section 9A/B/BA compliant, code the current account balance as the value. If the product is Section 9A/B/BA compliant code half the current account balance as the value.
  • Ownership: 100% CUS

Code new entry on Other Income (OINS) screen:

  • Type: OTH
  • Description: SUPS MLI PRN (insert product reference number)
  • Reference: leave blank
  • DOV: today
  • Value: code assessable income - In the final period, this is calculated by the Gross Income less the deductible which is ((purchase price - commutations/relevant number)
  • Frequency: ANN

Once finalised, DOC the record and code a manual review for the expiry date. See the Resources page for the DOC templates.

A Manual review must be set to expire all market linked products in the final year.

Go to Step 10.
