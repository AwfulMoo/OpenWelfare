This page contains information on the role of staff within service centres for handling repayment of the Scheme loans and related enquiries. It also contains information for the Home Equity Access Scheme National team on their role in administering repayments including issuing of receipts.

On this page:

BPAY® payments

Credit or debit payments via Centrelink online account

Cheque or money order payments

Update payment details and issue receipt for loan payments

BPAY® payments

Table 1

Item

Description

1

BPAY payments + Read more ...

  • Are made via a financial institution's telephone or internet banking service
  • Can be made 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
  • Using a credit card is not allowed by some financial institutions
  • Takes approximately 2 to 5 working days; including the day of payment, to be credited to Service Australia’s account

To make a BPAY payment, customers need to know:

  • the Home Equity Access Scheme Biller Code: 257071, and
  • their own Payment Reference Number. Customers can get this through the Home Equity Access Scheme Summary page in their Centrelink online account

2

Troubleshooting + Read more ...

Unable to process a BPAY payment using telephone banking or internet banking

Tell the customer they must be registered with the financial institution for telephone or internet banking.

Check the customer has:

  • the correct Biller Code
  • the correct Payment Reference Number
  • sufficient funds in the bank account

Australia Post provide a similar service to BPAY called Billpay. Billpay uses a 4-digit code where BPAY uses a 6-digit code. Where the customer is attempting to pay using BPAY with a Billpay code the payment will not work.

If the above does not apply, tell the customer to contact their financial institution to get help.

Payment made by BPAY and has not been credited to the customer's account

Tell the customer to contact their financial institution and request a trace on the BPAY payment, if:

  • no payment has been received
  • at least 5 working days has passed since the customer made the payment
  • the customer has a receipt number, and
  • the funds have been debited from the customer's account

Payment made via BPAY was credited and reversed

If the payment cannot be located, escalate for CAO investigation.

Use Fast Note – select Auto text, use Complex Assessment > Request for CAO action > CAO Home Equity Access Scheme

For Service Officers, the procedure ends here.

3

Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) + Read more ...

Check Manual Follow-Up (MFU) and Essentials for unallocated money.

Where a payment issue occurs, a MFU activity generates in ISIS and allocates via Workload Manager (WLM).

The MFU displays a document number in the customer’s record.

See the Resources page for MFU warnings, errors and actions.

Note: when following MFU instructions and allocating unallocated money, follow the repayment hierarchy and allocate in order of costs, interest, then principal.

Credit or debit payments via Centrelink online account

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Help customer make credit or debit payments + Read more ...

Credit or debit card payments can be made through the Home Equity Access Scheme Summary page in the customer’s Centrelink online account.

Loan amounts repaid via credit or debit card usually clear the following business day. However due to evening cut off if a customer makes a repayment after 6pm, the amount won’t come through until after 2 business days have passed.

2

Check payment status + Read more ...

A customer’s online account displays information to advise them a payment is still in process and not yet processed

Tell the customer to:

  • access their Centrelink online account, see Using Centrelink self service for help
  • select Payments and Claims
  • select Manage Payments
  • Home Equity Access Scheme summary page displays
  • To make a repayment:
    • select Credit/Debit
    • select either pay total or pay other amount (for other amount, enter the amount)
    • confirm information for repayment is correct
    • enter card details

3

Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) + Read more ...

Check the Manual Follow-Up (MFU) and Essentials for unallocated money.

If a payment issue occurs, a MFU activity generates in ISIS and allocates via Workload Manager (WLM).

The MFU displays a document number in the customer’s record.

See the Resources page for MFU warnings, errors, actions and refunds.

Note: when following MFU instructions and allocating unallocated money, follow the repayment hierarchy and allocate in order of costs, interest, then principal.

Cheque or money order payments

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Repayment enquiry + Read more ...

Discuss the electronic repayment options with the customer:

  • BPAY® payments
  • Credit or debit payments via Centrelink online account

If the customer cannot use BPAY or a credit, or debit card then payment by cheque or money order is available.

Tell the customer to:

  • make the cheque/money order payable to ‘Collector of Public Money - Services Australia’
  • post the cheque/money order to Home Equity Access Scheme National

2

Repayment at a service centre or by mail + Read more ...

  • Scan cheque/money order to record and create an ACTDOC using Fast Note: use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Complex Assessment > Request for CAO action > CAO Home Equity Access Scheme
  • Create a DOC of the date of cheque payment and amount received
  • Send the cheque/money order via internal mail to Home Equity Access Scheme National (include information to identify to the customer when sending via internal mail)
  • Tell the customer they will receive a receipt after their repayment has been banked and their loan balance has been updated

Update payment details and issue receipt for loan payments

For the Home Equity Access Scheme National team only

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Payment received at Home Equity Access Scheme National + Read more ...

If the cheque or money order is not scanned and/or a DOC is yet to be created, the Home Equity Access Scheme National staff must:

  • scan the cheque or money order to the customers record, and
  • create a DOC on the customer’s record

Complete the Repayments pro forma and allocate the money to the loan components in order:

  • Costs
  • Interest, then
  • Principal

If the customer has nominated any or all of the funds to be allocated towards a particular component, for example Interest:

  • allocate to Interest first, and
  • then follow the hierarchy above

Note: depending on the date of receipt for the cheque/money order, an adjustment may be required for the loan interest. Calculate the adjustment before the pro-forma is complete, to ensure the correct amounts are captured for banking and receipting.

If the customer is repaying in full and wants the secured property released, see Settlement of Home Equity Access Scheme loan amounts for more details.

2

Manual receipt for the Scheme + Read more ...

  • Log into SAP GUI and open YFR_PLS – Manual Receipt PLS
  • Some fields pre-populate
  • Enter as follows:
    • Document Type E9
    • Posting Date defaults to today – (change if required)
    • Date Received defaults to today – (change if required)
    • CRN – required
    • Loan Number – required
    • Cheque number – required
    • BSB number - required
    • Remittance Source CUS, do not change
    • Remittance Mode CHQ, do not change
    • Agent Name – enter the cheque drawer name (customer or bank name on cheque)
  • Select Process Repayment

Note: if any of the entries fail, a warning message appears. Enter through the warning messages to update the data.

3

Process payment + Read more ...

If no validation errors occur the Process Payment screen displays.

Enter the distribution of the receipt in the below fields:

  • Total Amount
  • Principal
  • Interest
  • Cost

Note: the Total Amount should match the combined Principal, Interest and Costs. The cost centre fields for each of the component amount fields can be amended in case the payments are being processed in a location other than Glen Waverley which has the default cost centre of 2002577.

Staff must select Receipt Required to print a receipt locally to post to the customer.

Enter details of the customer and comments in the text fields.

The below fields are mandatory:

  • Name
  • Street
  • Suburb
  • State
  • Post-code
  • Text, reference text appears on the receipt to advise the customer what the payment is for. For example, Home Equity Access Scheme – one off payment

Keep the receipt to update the customer’s record.

4

Receipt balancing and banking + Read more ...

Balance multiple cheques or money orders before depositing at the bank. This ensures the total amount of the cheques, equals the total amount banked in SAP GUI.

Note: there is significant rework if the amount banked in SAP GUI does not match the amount deposited.

After exiting YFR_PLS:

  • Enter ZX21
  • Select the green tick, this opens Receipt Balancing
  • Add up the total amount of all the cheques and money orders
  • If the total amount does not equal the report total, fix any errors
  • When the total amount of the cheques equals the Receipt Balancing report total, print a copy of the report
  • After printing the report select Confirm

After exiting ZX21:

  • Enter ZX22 and select the green tick, this opens Receipt Banking
  • Select the box next to your logon and select the green tick Generate
  • Select the green tick Confirm Banking
  • In the dialogue box that appears, select the green tick

5

Banking + Read more ...

  • Complete the Administered Bank Deposit slip with details of cheques
  • Deposit cheques/money orders at the Commonwealth Bank of Australia

6

Update customers record for loan details + Read more ...

Go to the to the HEAS Details (PLS) screen:

  • If adjustments are required use the last date displayed
  • If no adjustments are required, insert the date of receipt of the cheque as the date of event. The interest amount will update

Update the following against each component:

  • Receipt Amount (Recpt Amt) - the amount allocated to each component
  • Receipt ID (Recpt ID) - is the number printed on the customer’s receipt
  • External Receipt ID (External Recpt ID) - is the cheque or money order number
  • Chnl (Channel) - is CHQ
  • Finalise the activity

Annotate the DOC with:

  • details of the final figure
  • details of any discrepancies in amounts, and
  • receipt numbers

Close the DOC.

Place the receipt in a window envelope and mail to the customer.

7

Refunding over repaid monies + Read more ...

If a customer makes a cheque or money order payment that is more than the amount owing, a refund is required:

  • Allocate over repaid monies to the Principal component
  • Before coding the receipt details on the HEAS Details (PLS) screen, change the Principal amount to equal the amount of Principal banked
  • Make any other changes if required
  • Update the receipt details, and finalise

Go to Refunding debt repayments:

  • Resources page > Forms, and complete a Notice to prepare a Refund Account (SF030) form
  • Use Cost Centre Code 2000497 and GL Code 70515
  • CAO to print the form, sign and date (APS 5 or above)
  • Spending delegate to approve, sign and provide position details (can be APS 5 or above)
  • Scan the signed form and save as a PDF
  • Email to Financial Shared Services to arrange the refund with a request to confirm when the refund is sent
