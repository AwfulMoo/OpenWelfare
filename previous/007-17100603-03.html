Residency assessment + Read more ...

The customer's Australian residence qualification will be automatically assessed based on the residence information recorded. Residency information is recorded on the relevant customer's record.

Residence information must still be recorded if the claim is to be rejected for a reason other than residence. Otherwise, the system will override any other rejection reason and the claim will reject for a residence related reason.

See:

  • Residence assessment for customers claiming FTB, PPL and DAP
  • Residence and Portability screens

The new claim process will attempt a datalink with the Department of Home Affairs. If successful, the datalink will automatically record a customer's visa and movement information from 1 September 1994, and grants of Australian citizenship where available.

Check the assessment and ensure PPL is only granted when the claimant has been residentially qualified for every day from the date of birth. Where the assessment result is incorrect, the details must be investigated through normal escalation processes, for example, referral to NIRO or SSO.

PPL checks + Read more ...

PPL Residency

A datalink with the Department of Home Affairs will, where possible, automatically populate residence details for customers from 1 September 1994 to date, as well as citizenship and movement information for the customer if it is available. For a Services Australia customer, the Department of Home Affairs will advise of any future changes to their visa subclass or if they, their partner or their child leaves or returns to Australia.

Service Officers should check the assessment and ensure the PPL period is only granted when the primary claimant has been residentially qualified for every day from the date of birth. For children that were born or adopted:

  • before 1 July 2020, failure to meet this will result in full rejection of PPL
  • on or after 1 July 2020, failure to meet this may result in component rejection of the PPL period only. The customer may be eligible for Flexible PPL days if they were residentially qualified on the child’s date of birth or adoption but lost eligibility before their PPL period start date

Where the assessment result is incorrect, the details must be investigated through normal escalation process, for example, referral to National ICT Referral Officer (NIRO) or Service Support Officer (SSO).

If the customer meets eligibility requirements for PPL due to having an exemption for the Newly Arrived Residents Waiting Period (NARWP), the exemption will be applied for any Flexible PPL days that are connected to their PPL period. If they have claimed any Flexible PPL days separately, their residency status will be checked the day before the claimed dates. These customers are encouraged to connect all of their Flexible PPL days to their PPL period to ensure that any changes to their exemption do not adversely impact their eligibility for Flexible PPL days.

Income Support Payment (ISP) customers claiming PPL/DAP + Read more ...

PPL and DAP is treated as income for calculating the rate of payment for ISPs.

This will result in customers (and/or their partners):

  • receiving a reduced rate of income support payment when they are getting PPL/DAP
  • entering a 6 fortnight nil rate period (only applicable where the customer/partner also has other income), or
  • losing their entitlement to their income support payment and any associated concession cards (that is cancellation of income support payment)

Note: no additional coding is required by staff for PPL and DAP payments to affect the customers rate of ISP. They will automatically be treated as ordinary income.

If PPL/DAP is granted from a past date, this may cause an ISP overpayment. Before finalising the claim attempt to make 1 outbound contact to affected customers to discuss potential payment impacts claiming PPL/DAP will have on their income support payment. If appropriate the customer may change their nominated start date to another date in the future to prevent being overpaid their income support payment.

If an overpayment is incurred:

  • it will be automatically calculated on the AR screen. PPL arrears and ISP overpayments will automatically offset to reduce the overpayment amount before a debt shell is created

the debt shell created will also need to be actioned as part of this process. See Raising ISP debts for customers granted PPL and DAP

Customers and/or their partners whose income support payment has cancelled due to PPL/DAP income may be able to have their payment resumed, if they have regained entitlement to an income support payment at the end of the PPL or DAP period.

To follow up customer's income support payment entitlement at the end of the PPL or DAP period, staff processing post birth PPL or DAP claims need to create a review DOC on the customer's (and/or their partner's) record using the Fast Note template Families>Claims>PPL/DAP income and income support payment, in cases where:

  • the customer's (and/or their partner's) income support payment has cancelled due to PPL or DAP income
  • the customer (and/or their partner) have entered a 6 fortnight nil rate period, or
  • the PPL or DAP nominated start date is in the future

Add the appropriate keyword based on PPL or DAP and the payment type as per the keyword information provided on the Resources page.

Select DOC on AL screen and enter the PPL or DAP end date + 1 in the Resubmit date field to put the started DOC activity on hold until the end of the PPL or DAP period.

See: Resumption of JobSeeker Payment, Youth Allowance, Parenting Payment, Farm Household Allowance or Austudy after Parental Leave Pay (PPL) or Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)

Note: Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customers may still be entitled to receive a part rate of FHA during their PPL/DAP entitlement periods, regardless of the rate paid. Special circumstances - Family and Domestic Violence + Read more ...

If the customer indicated they did not meet the work test due to family and domestic violence, the Service Officer must:

  • check for evidence of employment and then
  • refer the claim to a social worker

The social worker will assess whether the family and domestic violence affected the customer’s ability to work during their work test period. The social worker may contact the customer to discuss the circumstances over the phone. If there is enough evidence already on the customer’s record, the social worker may complete a file assessment.

Once the social worker has completed their assessment, they will:

  • annotate the social worker referral DOC with the outcome, and
  • take the claim off hold to be reallocated to a Service Officer for processing

Note: due to privacy and safety concerns, do not use the term ‘family and domestic violence’ on the customer’s record. Instead, use the phrase s 24B(2)(a) of the Paid Parental Leave Rules 2021. For example, the social worker assessment will advise that the customer meets/does not meet the requirement per s 24B(2)(a) of the Paid Parental Leave Rules 2021. Fast notes for grant/reject/NEF claims must also refer to this section and not ‘family and domestic violence’.

The Service Officer will then receive the claim and complete their assessment, with regard to the social worker assessment and evidence of employment.

When the Service Officer has made a determination, this needs to be update it within the PPL Work Test guided procedure. The Service Officer will also need to confirm that the social worker assessment has been completed by selecting ‘Yes’ for the ‘SWO Assessed’ question. The claim outcome fast note must not refer to family and domestic violence. Select the ‘meets requirement per s 24B(2)(a) of the Paid Parental Leave Rules 2021’ option.

Child of multiple birth + Read more ...

PPL can only be paid for 1 child from a multiple birth. Where the customer has already received PPL for 1 of the children from the multiple birth the PPL claim for the subsequent child in most cases should be rejected.

When linking the child on the Manage Dependant child workflow, only select one of the children from the multiple birth to be linked to the PPL pre-claim. If more than one child is selected, an error will present when attempting to finalise the claim.

However, in the rare event there is a significant delay between the births of the children it may be possible for PPL to be paid for more than 1 child. These cases must be referred to level 2 Policy Helpdesk with all additional evidence for investigation.

Note: multiple births being treated as separate birthing events do not apply to FTB and do not impact on the rates of NBS/NBU and MBA.

If the customer:

  • has already been paid for 1 child from the multiple birth any subsequent claims for other children must be rejected

  • had a significant delay between the birth, they must provide additional evidence to support their claims before the claim for the subsequent child can be assessed
    • attempt to contact the customer to request the evidence regarding the separate births and any other information that is required for the claim to be assessed.     • When the additional evidence has been provided the case must be referred to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk, see Resources for a link to the online webform.
    • When Level 2 Policy Helpdesk have provided a response regarding the customers eligibility to PPL for the child the claims can be assessed

Debt offsetting - FTB

From 10 June 2019, FTB top-ups, supplements, lump sum claim amounts and instalment arrears can be used to recover any family assistance, social security, student assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payment debts. This will occur even if the customer has a current payment arrangement. Previously, FTB amounts could only be used to recover FTB, Child Care Benefit (CCB) and Child Care Rebate (CCR) debts.

Debt offsetting - PPL

PPL arrears can be used to recover determined or due PPL debts, and offset PPL or ISP overpayments that were generated in the same activity. These amounts will be offset against each other so the payment does not issue and subsequently raise a debt on the same day.

Note: PPL offsetting applies only to payments of PPL or overpayments of an income support payment (ISP) that result from the customer getting PPL for the same past period. This is because PPL is treated as ordinary income for calculating the rate of ISP.

PPL arrears offsetting will occur even if the customer has a current payment arrangement. Any residual amount will then be paid to the customer. See Parental Leave Pay debt raising, debt offsetting and recovery.
