Contacting customers in Category 3a to advise eligibility for settlement payment

For Prison Liaison Officers (PLO) only

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Referrals to PLO or Prison Servicing Unit for the relevant state or territory + Read more ...

To locate the responsible Service Zone for the prison, refer to the Correctional facilities Intranet page. To find out more about servicing arrangements with a corrections facility, contact the responsible Service Zone.

Referrals to start this process will be provided to the relevant Service Zone by the Debt and Integrity Project Branch via the Face to Face Strategic Support and Coordination Team.

Can the customer be contacted directly by phone?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Direct conversation with the customer + Read more ...

If direct contact with the customer is successful:

  • follow the phone contact requirements
  • confirm they are comfortable with their personal information being disclosed as part of the interaction

Suggested dialogue:

'Could I please confirm with you that this conversation is private and you are happy for me to disclose your personal information?'

Is the customer willing to provide personal information?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • Advise the customer (suggested dialogue):
      ‘I will only give you detailed information at your request. You are eligible to receive a settlement payment as part of the Income Compliance Class Action. To register you to receive the settlement payment I need to confirm some information with you'
    • If the customer does not wish to participate in discussion with the PLO:
      'As you do not wish to continue with this conversation, we may not be able to pay the settlement payment into an appropriate account'
    • Go to Step 9

3

Facilitated interaction with the customer + Read more ...

If contact with the customer is facilitated through corrections staff or a conversation that is not private.

Tell the customer that as the conversation is not private, their personal information will only be disclosed at their request.

Suggested dialogue:

'As this conversation is not private, I will only give you detailed information at your request. You are eligible to receive a settlement payment as part of the Income Compliance Class Action. In order to register you to receive the settlement payment I need to confirm some information with you'.’

Is the customer willing to participate in discussion with the PLO?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • Advise the customer (suggested dialogue):
      'As you do not wish to continue with this conversation, we may not be able to pay the settlement payment into an appropriate account’
    • Go to Step 9

4

Explain their class action settlement + Read more ...

Suggested dialogue:

'You were previously refunded money relating to your Income Compliance Debt(s) that was originally calculated using ATO averaged income. We no longer use the averaging of ATO information.

'You are eligible to receive a settlement payment from the Income Compliance class action. The settlement payment is in place of interest you could’ve earned on the debt repayments you made.

'Services Australia has identified group members and has categorised each debt. You will be eligible to receive a settlement payment if:

  • your debt was based partially or fully on ATO income information, or
  • our debt was recalculated based on information you gave us, like payslips, and you had paid back more than the recalculated amount

'People who paid us back more, and were without their money for longer, will get larger payments. The amount you will receive depends on when you paid money towards eligible debts, how much you paid, and when you receive your refund. We expect to begin making settlement payments in early 2022.

'As you have already received a refund, you have been identified under category 3a as your Income Compliance Debt originally calculated using averaged ATO income was recalculated using payslips or bank statements and you had overpaid the recalculated amount. This means you will be eligible to receive a settlement payment.

Dispute options

'Once you receive your categorisation correspondence, if you don’t agree with the category of your debt/s, you can ask for a review. You can also ask for a review if we haven’t included a debt you think should be eligible.

If asked about other Settlement Distribution Scheme Categories, see Categorisation of group members' debts.

5

Confirm their postal address + Read more ...

The Federal Court of Australia has approved the income compliance class action settlement.

Services Australia will send correspondence in relation to settlement payment through myGov, or by post to the customer’s last known postal address.

Confirm the customer’s postal address details where the agency can send settlement payment correspondence.

Suggested dialogue:

'As you are eligible to receive a settlement payment, can I confirm your preferred postal address that the agency can send letters to.

'If you are unable to confirm, is there another person you would like us to contact, who may be able to provide postal address details?'

  • Yes, make note of the address details. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Confirm their existing payment destination is correct + Read more ...

Advise the customer:

'As you are not currently on a payment, I need to confirm your bank account details before the settlement payment can be processed. Could you please confirm that the last bank account which you received a Centrelink payment into is still active and the settlement payment can be paid into that account?'

Can the customer confirm and/or update bank account details?

  • Yes, confirm with the customer that the settlement payment will be processed into the nominated account. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Ask if someone else could provide details + Read more ...

Ask the customer if there is someone who could provide the details needed to process their settlement payment

If the customer has a nominee on the record, confirm the arrangement.

Suggested dialogue:

‘Can I confirm if you have a current nominee arrangement and that you are happy for me to contact your nominee to collect this information?'

Is the nominee arrangement still current?

  • Yes, and the customer:
    • consents for the nominee to be contacted, go to Step 9
    • does not give consent for the nominee to be contacted, go to Step 8
  • No, cancel the nominee arrangement. Go to Step 8

8

Nominee arrangement not current or contact not supported + Read more ...

If the customer does not have a nominee on record, check if there is someone else they would like the agency to contact.

Suggested dialogue:

'Is there another person you would like us to contact, who may be able to act on your behalf to provide bank account details?

'The account details must be in your name. If not, the agency will try to pay the settlement payment into an appropriate account'.

‘If you authorise for the settlement payment to be paid into another person’s account, we will attempt to contact them to confirm if they agree to act and/or receive the payment on your behalf’.

9

Record the outcome + Read more ...

Record details on a DOC on the customer's record. See the Resources page for the template.

Obtain and record any nominee and authorised contacts as accurately as possible.

Procedure ends here.

Obtain details to register customer for class action settlement payment

For staff trained in processing class action settlements

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check ACTDOC to determine action + Read more ...

Check the ACTDOC with the details provided by the customer.

  • If the customer asked Services Australia to contact:
    • their nominee to obtain the information needed to register for the customer’s settlement payment, see Table 3
    • an authorised person to obtain the information needed to register for the customer’s settlement payment, see Table 4
  • If the customer provided the information needed to register for their settlement payment, see Table 5
  • If the customer, nominee, or authorised person has already spoken with the PLO/ Compliance Officer and completed the refund task, they will automatically be registered for settlement payment. No further action is needed. Procedure ends here

To contact their nominee

For staff trained in processing class action settlements

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Locate nominee details + Read more ...

In Customer Record, find the nominee and their phone number:

  • go to the Nominee Link Summary (NOLS) screen
  • ‘S’elect the nominee to go to the nominee’s record
  • go to the Telephone Summary (TDS) screen

Review nominee status.

Check nominee status to confirm the nominee arrangement is current and should not be removed.

2

Contact the nominee + Read more ...

Contact the customer's nominee to:

  • confirm they are still the customer’s nominee and have authority to act on their behalf
  • advise them the person they are a nominee for is entitled to a refund and advise the next steps

Make 3 genuine attempts to contact the nominee on all available numbers. Follow the phone contact requirements.

If unsuccessful, do not leave a voicemail.

Record in a Note all attempts to contact the nominee. Include unsuccessful attempts.

Is contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, after 3 genuine attempts, email the following details via a Quality Development Officer to DIP.BUSINESS.PROCESS.DESIGN:
    • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • Reason for the referral: Attempted contact to the nominee of the incarcerated customer was unsuccessful
    • Procedure ends here

3

Conversation with nominee + Read more ...

Tell them:

‘Services Australia is contacting you to confirm if you are the nominee for (Customer Name). Can you confirm that you are still the nominee and wish to act on behalf of the customer?’

Does the nominee still have authority to act on behalf of the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • DOC the conversation on the customer’s record
    • Escalate the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via a Quality Development Officer to DIP.BUSINESS.PROCESS.DESIGN
    • Include the escalation reason: Incarcerated customer’s nominee no longer has authority to act on behalf of the customer.
    • Procedure ends here

4

Nominee is still current and has authority to act + Read more ...

Tell them:

‘Centrelink has changed how we use employment income information from the Australian Tax Office (ATO) in Income Compliance reviews.

'(Customer Name) previously had debt/s raised using averaging of ATO information. We no longer do this and have previously paid the applicable refund. (Customer Name) is also eligible for a settlement payment relating to this debt/s.

'As (Customer Name) has not received a payment from us in a while, we need you to confirm or update their contact, address and bank account details.

Check the nominee's addresses (including postal address), and phone numbers (including mobile number) and record their current contact details using the Change in Contact Details workflow in Customer First.

Can the nominee confirm or provide the customer's bank account details?

  • Yes, see Table 5
  • No, if the customer:
    • has verbally confirmed the settlement to be paid to the nominee, go to Step 5
    • has not confirmed the settlement to be paid to the nominee:
      DOC the conversation on the customer’s record
      Escalate the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via a Quality Development Officer to DIP.BUSINESS.PROCESS.DESIGN
      Include the escalation reason: Incarcerated customer’s does not authorise nominee to receive refund
      Procedure ends here

5

Customer has verbally confirmed for the settlement payment to be paid to nominee + Read more ...

Confirm if nominee consents to receive settlement payment on behalf of the customer.

Obtain nominee bank details.

Document conversation and details accurately.

Explain to the nominee, Centrelink will send them a letter confirming they have agreed to receive a payment of behalf of the customer.

Code the arrangement and process the Class Action Settlement Task, see Table 5.

  • Yes, go to Step 4

    • DOC the conversation on the customer’s record

4

Authorised person agrees to act on behalf of the customer + Read more ...

Tell them:

‘Centrelink has changed how we use employment income information from the Australian Tax Office (ATO) in Income Compliance reviews.

'(Customer Name) previously had debt/s raised using averaging of ATO information. We no longer do this and have previously paid the applicable refund. (Customer Name) is also eligible for a settlement payment relating to these debt/s.

'As (Customer Name) has not received a payment from us in a while, we need you to confirm or update their contact details, address and bank account details.

Confirm the authorised contact’s address (including postal address), and phone numbers (including mobile number) and record their current contact details in the existing ACTDOC work item.

Can the authorised person confirm or provide the customers bank account details?

  • Yes, see Table 5
  • No, and the customer:
    • has verbally confirmed the settlement to be paid to authorised person, go to Step 5
    • has not confirmed the settlement to be paid to authorised person
      Escalate the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via a Quality Development Officer to DIP.BUSINESS.PROCESS.DESIGN
      Include the escalation reason: Incarcerated customer does not authorise person to receive payment on behalf of the customer
      Procedure ends here.

5

Customer has verbally confirmed for the settlement payment to be paid to authorised person + Read more ...

Confirm if authorised person consents to receive settlement payment on behalf of the customer.

Obtain authorised persons bank details.

Confirm authorised person address and current contact details.

Document conversation and details accurately.

Explain to the authorised person, Centrelink will send them a letter confirming they have agreed to receive a payment of behalf of the customer.

Code the arrangement and process the Class Action Settlement Task, see Table 5.

For staff trained in processing class action settlements

Table 6

Step

Action

2

Confirm appropriate person + Read more ...

Before progressing with the reason for the call, confirm the person on the phone is the appropriate person.

Locate the record of the incarcerated customer the person is calling about.

Check the ACTDOC relating to Prison Liaison Officer (PLO) outbound contact:

  • If a refund was paid, check they were the person who received the income compliance refund - customer/ nominee/ authorised person
  • If a refund has not been paid as PLO could not get in contact with customer to confirm their payment destination and details, process refund for the customer

Is the person on the phone the appropriate person?

  • Yes,
    • If the person wants to update their contact number or address details, go to Step 3
    • If there is no change to the person's details, go to Step 3
  • No,
    • If the person calling is not the customer or the nominee/ authorised person the refund was issued to, tell them:
      ‘Services Australia has paid the refund to the nominee/ authorised person as advised by the customer. This person is also the authorised person for settlement payment’
    • Do not disclose personal Information of the refund recipient to the caller under any circumstances
    • Procedure ends here

3

Check reason for call + Read more ...

The person may be responding to the Categorisation letter they received, or they may have been contacted by Services Australia.

To help with the conversation, view the Categorisation letter in Outbound Correspondence in Customer First.

Note: if an incarcerated customer is on the phone, be sure to confirm/ update postal address for sending settlement correspondence. DOC and update record.

If the:

  • customer/ nominee/ authorised person wants help to register the incarcerated customer, progress the registration
  • customer is already registered and the customer/ nominee/ authorised person wants to update the payment destination, update details via the Payment tab
  • customer is already registered and the customer/ nominee/ authorised person wants to lodge a dispute, lodge a query on the customer's behalf

For the rest of the process, see Processing class action settlements.
