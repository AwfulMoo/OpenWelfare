This document outlines the process for determining when to raise a Coronavirus Supplement (CVS) payment debt.

Coronavirus Supplement (CVS)

On 27 April 2020, the Government introduced the CVS to provide support to Australians impacted by the Coronavirus Pandemic. The CVS ceased on 31 March 2021. See Coronavirus Supplement.

CVS debts

Eligibility for the CVS is dependent upon payability for the eligible supplement ISP including any eligible add-on payments, during the customers ISP entitlement period.

CVS debts occur when the customer:

  • has a change in circumstances other than a benefit transfer backdated, and
  • the person was no longer in receipt of a qualifying income support payment

Where a customer transfers from a CVS eligible income support payment (ISP) to a CVS non-eligible ISP, the customer will receive net arrears, and any residual excess CVS received is not recoverable. For more information, see Coronavirus Supplement.

When investigating a CVS debt, if the corresponding income support payment debt is Finalised No Debt the corresponding Coronavirus Supplement debt for the same period, should also be Finalised No Debt.

Raise the corresponding income support payment debt prior to the Coronavirus Supplement being raised.

If the ADEX identifies a CVS debt, however the customer retained some eligibility for income support payment for the customer entitlement period, determine if the customer was eligible to receive that CVS payment.

A debt may occur when CVS is incorrectly paid to an applicant/guardian for a dependent ABSTUDY customer where a change in care has occurred. For more information, see Coronavirus Supplement.

Customer transferring to a non-eligible CVS payment

Customers transferring from a CVS eligible payment to a non-eligible CVS payment after the ICT implementation on 8 August 2020, will get net arrears. They will get a net arrears if the negative adjustment for their CVS eligible payment including CVS is lower than the amount payable for the non-eligible CVS payment.

See Coronavirus Supplement for further information.
