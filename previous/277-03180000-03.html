Identifying a WA ex-nuptial child support case

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Cuba Indicators for identifying a WA ex-nuptial case + Read more ...

A WA ex-nuptial case can be identified using the indicators in:

  • Call Notification toolbar via Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone
  • Case Liability Rates for Case window
  • Child Support Period window, or
  • Case Summary window

2

Call notification toolbar via Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone + Read more ...

Where the caller has entered their reference number in the IVR, Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone will identify WA ex-nuptial cases by displaying ‘WAExnup’ on the Call Notification toolbar.

3

Case Liability Rates for Case window + Read more ...

In the Case Liability Rates for Case window there is an indicator field under the child support period details box that will have a 'Y' indicator if the case is a WA ex-nuptial case.

4

Child Support Period window + Read more ...

In the Child Support Period window there is a WA ex-nuptial indicator column. If the field is populated with a Y indicator, then the case is a WA ex-nuptial case.

5

Case Summary window + Read more ...

In the Case Summary window locate the WA Ex nup check box within the Latest Child Support Period field. It will be checked if the case has been identified as a WA ex-nuptial case.

6

Care window + Read more ...

In the Care window there is an indicator field in the box at the top left of the window that will have a 'Y' indicator if the case is a WA ex-nuptial case.

Late care change notified on WA ex-nuptial case

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Change in child support care levels + Read more ...

WA ex-nuptial customers are now treated the same as all other customers for current child support events.

When the care date of effect legislative amendments commenced on 1 July 2018 they did not apply to WA ex-nuptial children until 15 May 2019. On this date the WA parliament passed legislation adopting the care date of effect legislation.

If a care change occurred or is reported on or after 15 May 2019, the new care date of effect rules apply.

The system will be enhanced to automatically treat WA ex-nuptial customers correctly where care is late notified. Until this enhancement occurs manual workarounds are required to ensure the child support assessment is correct.

Customer notifies late care change – WA ex-nuptial case:

  • Process the care as usual, see Change in child support care levels (post 1 July 2010)
  • Pend all letters
  • If a NOA-01 letter has generated, for both customers:
    • update the status of the NOA-01 to Deleted, and
    • set all other letters to Pend in both the Suppressed letter list and the Letter list to ensure the correct letters are kept for review

2

Script explaining the next steps to the customers + Read more ...

Read this script:

‘Due to a legislative amendment, when we are told about a care change more than 28 days after it happened, we must apply this information in the assessment in different ways.

A decrease in care will be backdated to the date of the care change and an increase in care will be applied from the date we are notified.

When the care decision has been made, your case will be referred to a specialised team. This team will review the assessment from the date the care changed and make any corrections required.

This may mean there will be a delay in receiving your new assessment letters.

Once your case has been reviewed, letters will be issued to advise you of any changes from the date the care changed.

If you would like any information on your current assessment, log into your Child Support account online through your MyGov account and select current assessment.’

3

Record the discussion + Read more ...

Document the conversation with the customer, including that the script was read to them. See Documenting Child Support information.

4

Future management + Read more ...

Late notified care changes on WA ex-nuptial cases are identified by reports. They are referred to the Solutions Gateway Team (SGT) for action.

No further action is required by the Service Officer.
