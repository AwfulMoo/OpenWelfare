Weekly Exchange

Required tasks when information received from NZIR

This table describes the process CA Service Officers follow when managing the transfer of information between NZIR and Child Support.

Step

Action

1

Weekly Exchange NZIR to Child Support + Read more ...

The following spreadsheets are emailed to Child Support from Inland Revenue, New Zealand (NZIR) each week. This is known as the ‘weekly exchange’:

  • Income requests – go to Step 2
  • Determination of Habitual Residency (Customer Location/Pre-transmittal) – requests for confirmation of payers habitual residency in Australia and other information to ensure NZIR have an accurate assessment, go to Step 3
  • NZIR BAU ‘In’ registrations – requests for Child Support to register NZIR assessment information and commence collection or enforcement, go to Step 4
  • NZ funded registrations – additional requests for Child Support to register NZIR assessment information and commence collection or enforcement funded via ‘Fee for Service’ arrangements, go to Step 5
  • General Withdrawals – confirmation that collection is no longer required and case is to be withdrawn and returned to NZIR, go to Step 6
  • Amended certificates – advice regarding a customer’s change in circumstance, go to Step 7
  • Potential Returns – Request for new information, go to Step 8
  • Case Returns (No Longer Habitually Resident, Customer Deceased, Non-traceable, Non-collectable), go to Step 9
  • Request for Enforcement Updates, go to Step 10
  • Request for Amended Certificate, go to Step 11
  • Request for General Withdrawal, go to Step 12

Each spreadsheet is saved to a specific folder in the Hobart shared drive folder Int in the folder titled Int/NZ.

2

Income Requests (NZ In Cases) + Read more ...

NZIR seeks income information for a payer in Australia on a case NZIR has transmitted to Child Support

Save spreadsheet

  • open the ‘Incomes Request to DHS dd.mm.yyyy’ spreadsheet
  • save the spreadsheet in the folder titled Income Requests from NZIR (Int/NZ/20YY – Exchange NZIR to DHS) with the file name ‘Income Requests to DHS rec’d dd.mm.yyyy (xx)’

3

Actioning Customer Location (pre-transmittal) requests from NZIR + Read more ...

NZIR send a weekly quota of 70 requests to Child Support to ascertain customer’s habitual residency and associated information. Pre-transmittal is predominately a tracing request (customer location information), however it includes a preliminary habitual residency decision (that is, the decision is pended), income details, case information and a collectability rating.

Insert a column to the left and insert a heading of 'Status' and delete the column with the heading of 'CNC Case Number'. Note: if these columns are not inserted/deleted as per these instructions, the macro will not run correctly.

Save the ‘70 cases sent dd.mm.yyyy’ spreadsheet in the folder titled Int/NZ/20YY – NZIR to DHS - Tracing Request Rec'd - Pre-transmittal with the file name ‘Pre-transmittal received ddmmyyyy’.

To complete pre-transmittal requests, see Customer location (Tracing) (CS).

4

Actioning BAU ‘NZ In’ registrations (Transmittals) + Read more ...

NZIR send a weekly quota of 35 cases to Child Support to be registered for collection.

The spreadsheet should be checked to ensure the 'Arrears as at' date is the last date of the previous month and the monthly ongoing date is from the first day of the current month. If these dates appear to be incorrect, either email or phone a Team Leader in NZ to ask them to re-send the sheet if it needs to be updated. Note: both the 35 BAU and 15 NZF cases (see Step 5) are received on the same spreadsheet. The NZF cases are highlighted in a contrasting colour for identification as NZF.

Save the ‘dd.mm.yyyy (BAUnn)’ spreadsheet in the folder in Int/NZ/20YY NZIR to DHS-Reg Rec’d BAU titled Weekly Spread Sheets (located in the folder titled BAU NZ In Regos in the current year folder) with the file name ‘BAU In Rego month yyyy’. Once saved, delete the 15 NZF highlighted cases.

Send the Operations Support Business Analyst an email via the Integrated Workload Planning and Information mailbox. Advise the weekly exchange registrations are saved and ready. Note: the email is sent at Step 5 to include reference that both BAU and NZF cases are ready for allocation – do not send 2 separate emails.

Allocate cases (Operations Support Business Analyst)

When notification of the BAU ‘In’ registrations spreadsheet is received:

  • run the macro stored in the folder titled NZ Handbook (located on Hobart’s shared drive folder titled BST Segment – Current)
  • save the macro results in the folder titled NZ BAU In Rego NEW dd.mm.yyyy (located in the folder titled BAU NZ In Regos in the current year folder)
  • create an International CO/101/AGR rcvd intray for each customer

To process the registration, see New Zealand ‘In’ case management – Registrations between NZIR and Child Support.

5

Actioning NZ funded registrations + Read more ...

NZIR send an additional weekly quota of 15 cases to Child Support to be registered for collection under fee for service. Note: both the 35 BAU and 15 NZF cases (see Step 4) are received on the same spreadsheet. The NZF cases are highlighted in a contrasting colour for identification as NZF.

Save the ‘dd.mm.yyyy (NZFnn)’ spreadsheet in the folder in Int/NZ/20YY/NZIR to DHS/Reg Rec’d NZF titled Weekly Funded In Rego top up (located in the folder titled Funded In Regos in the Weekly Spread Sheets folder) with the file name ‘NZF In Rego month yyyy’. Once saved, delete the 35 BAU cases.

Send the Operations Support Business Analyst an email via the Integrated Workload Planning and Information mailbox. Advise the weekly exchange registrations are saved and ready. The email should be referenced for both BAU and NZF cases – no need to send 2 separate emails.

Allocate cases (Operations Support Business Analyst)

When notification of the NZF In Rego spreadsheet is received:

  • run the macro stored in the folder titled NZ Handbook (located on Hobart’s shared drive folder titled BST Segment – Current)
  • save the macro results in the folder titled NZF Registrations rec’d dd.mm.yyyy (located in the folder titled Funded In Regos in the current year folder in the Weekly Funded In Rego top up folder)
  • create an International CO/101/AGR rcvd intray for each customer

To process the registration, see New Zealand ‘In’ case management – Registrations between NZIR and Child Support.

6

General Withdrawals received (NZ In cases) + Read more ...

Withdrawals are notified by NZIR on the spreadsheet titled ‘Withdrawals'.

Complete the following in order:

  • open the ‘Withdrawals’ spreadsheet
  • insert two columns to the left of the sheet and title the headings ‘Status’ and ‘Maint $$ Discharged’
  • sum total the bottom of the ‘Maint $$ Discharged’ column:
    • click the Autosum button on the toolbar, and
    • press Enter
  • reformat the ‘Maint $$ Discharged’ column so it is in currency with two decimal places:
  • highlight all values in the column
  • click the Currency Style button on the toolbar, and
  • save the spreadsheet in the folder titled current year (located in the folder Int/NZ/20YY - NZIR to DHS/Wdrls Rec’d titled Withdrawals) with the file name as ‘Withdrawals to DHS dd/mm/yy’

7

Amended certificates – advice regarding a customer’s change in circumstances + Read more ...

Amended certificates are provided by NZIR on the spreadsheet titled ‘ACERTS to DHS dd.mm.yyyy’.

The spreadsheet should be checked to ensure the 'Arrears as at' date is the last date of the previous month and the monthly ongoing date is from the first day of the current month. Also check the payment Schedule date the NZ has processed. If these dates do appear to be incorrect, either email or phone a Team Leader in NZ to ask them to re-send the sheet if it needs to be updated.

Complete the following in order:

  • open the ‘ACERTS to DHS dd.mm.yyyy’ spreadsheet
  • insert 3 columns on the far left and title the headings 'CSO' ‘Status’, and 'Notes'
  • save the spreadsheet in the folder titled Acerts (Int/NZ/20YY/NZIR to DHS/) with the file name ACERTS to DHS rec’d dd.mm.yyyy (xx)

To process amended certificates, see New Zealand Case Management (NZ In Case) - Amended Certificates from Inland Revenue, New Zealand (NZIR) to Child Support.

8

Potential Returns (NZ Out – Australian Cases) + Read more ...

NZIR is seeking further information from Child Support when considering returning a case as Non Collectable, Non Traceable, Customer No Longer Habitually Resident and Customer Deceased.

Save spreadsheet

  • open the ‘Potential Returns dd.mm.yyyy’ spreadsheet
  • save the spreadsheet in the folder titled Potential Returns from NZIR (Int/NZ/20YY– Exchange NZIR to DHS) with the file name ‘Potential Returns rec’d dd.mm.yyyy (xx)’

9

Case Returns to Child Support (NZ Out – Australian Cases) + Read more ...

When NZIR has exhausted all collection avenues, is not able to locate the customer, the customer is deceased or the customer is no longer habitually resident of NZ.

Save spreadsheet

  • open the ‘CASE RETURNS to DHS dd.mm.yyyy’ spreadsheet
  • insert 1 column on the far left and title the heading 'Receipted'
  • save the spreadsheet in the folder titled Case Returns from NZIR (Int/NZ/20YY – Exchange NZIR to DHS) with the file name ‘CASE RETURNS TO DHS rec’d dd.mm.yyyy (xx)’

10

Request for Enforcement Updates (NZ In Cases) + Read more ...

NZIR seeks an update on enforcement from Child Support on a case NZIR has transmitted to Child Support (NZ In Case)

Save spreadsheet

  • open the ‘Enforcement Updates to DHS dd.mm.yyyy’ spreadsheet
  • save the spreadsheet in the folder titled Enforcement Updates from NZIR (Int/NZ/20YY – Exchange NZIR to DHS) with the file name ‘CASE RETURNS TO DHS rec’d dd.mm.yyyy (xx)’

11

Request for Amended Certificate (NZ Out – Australian Cases) + Read more ...

NZIR is requesting an Amended Certificate from Child Support on a case Child Support has transmitted to NZIR

Save spreadsheet

  • open the ‘Request for Amended Certificate dd.mm.yyyy’ spreadsheet
  • save the spreadsheet in the folder titled Request for Amended Certificate from NZIR (Int/NZ/20YY – Exchange NZIR to DHS) with the file name ‘Request for Amended Certificate rec’d dd.mm.yyyy (xx)’

Request for General Withdrawal (NZ Out – Australian Cases) + Read more ...

NZIR is asking Child Support to withdraw a case that has been transmitted to NZIR. This is generally where the case maintenance arrears and associated penalties have been paid in full.

Managing the returns process

Required tasks for the returns process

This table describes the process CA Service Officers or Quality Improvement Officers follow as part of the returns process relating to the transfer of information between NZIR and Child Support.

Step

Action

1

Reporting for ‘In’ exchange – including NZF Master List (Quality Improvement Officer with CA Service Officer) + Read more ...

All data received as part of the In exchange from NZIR must be recorded into the ‘Weekly Exch Summary 20YY' spreadsheet in the folder titled In&Out Exch Summary (located in Hobart’s shared drive folder titled Int/NZ/ current year folder).

Note: the password to edit the spreadsheet is ‘BBCSA’.

There are several tabs to this spreadsheet, however you only need to fill in the ‘Received – NZ In Exchange’ tab with the relevant details.

2

Provide NZIR with confirmation + Read more ...

Child Support provides NZIR with confirmation once the following have been completed:

  • Determination of Habitual Residency (Pre-transmittal/Customer Location) requests – confirmation of payers habitual residency in Australia and other information to ensure NZIR have an accurate assessment,
  • BAU ‘In’ registrations and NZ funded registrations – Child Support have registered NZIR assessment information and commenced collection or enforcement
  • Withdrawals – confirmation that collection is no longer required and case has been deregistered (ended) and returned to NZIR,
  • Amended Certificates – Confirmation that Acert has been processed and reasons for Acerts not processed.
  • Potential Returns – confirmation of whether new information is available to assist in collection maintenance debt or whether no new information is available
  • Case Returns – confirmation the case has been withdrawn
  • Enforcement Updates – confirmation of any available enforcement updates on a case NZIR has transmitted to Child Support
  • Request for Amended Certificate – confirmation if an Amended Certificate will be sent or whether a withdrawal will be sent.
  • Request for General Withdrawal – confirmation if a withdrawal will be sent or an Amended Certificate is required and will be sent.
  • Request for Incomes – confirmation of incomes will be sent for each request made by NZIR.

3

Withdrawals + Read more ...

There are no restrictions on how many Withdrawals are reported back to NZIR.

To provide NZIR with confirmation that the cases have been deregistered:

  • attach the ‘COMPLETED Withdrawal to DHS ddmmyyyy’ spreadsheet and send to NZIR via secure messaging with the subject heading ‘Withdrawal confirmations’, and
  • after the last spreadsheet for the month has been completed and sent back to NZIR, rename the Month yyyy folder to include the word ‘COMPLETED’

This is the end of the process.

4

Determination of Habitual Residency Responses (Customer Location/Pre-transmittal) (Quality Improvement Officer) + Read more ...

Vetting of the sheet is done by the Quality Improvement Officer prior to sending back to NZIR to ensure:

  • all requests have been actioned
  • comments are appropriate
  • CSID’s are removed for Habitual Residency response of NO
  • CSID’s are provided for Habitual Residency response of YES
  • All incomes provided as requested
  • other anomalies that may appear incorrect

To provide NZIR with confirmation that payers are habitually resident in Australia, once 70 (or less) pre-transmittal requests have been entered onto the ‘Pre-transmittal’ spreadsheet:

  • open the ‘Pre-transmittal received ddmmyyyy’ spreadsheet in the folder titled Pre-transmittal; rename the spreadsheet ‘Pre-transmital req rec'd ddmmyyyy reply sent ddmmyyyy (70)’
  • attach and send to NZIR via secure messaging with the subject heading ‘Pre-transmittal confirmations’, and
  • after the last spreadsheet for the month has been completed and sent back to NZIR, rename the Month yyyy folder to include the word ‘COMPLETED’

This is the end of the process.
