Determine if estimate is the most appropriate option + Read more ...

An income estimate is an election that a parent can make to have their assessment based on their expected Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) for a year of income, or for part of a year of income.

Lodging an estimate may not be the most appropriate option when a customer's income has changed.

Discuss the customer’s circumstances to help them decide whether an estimate is an appropriate option.

Consider whether Post Separation Income may be an option rather than an estimate – Refer Post separation income for Child Support parents. To help with the discussion see Conversation Guide – Table 1 Estimate Best Option and Conversation Guide – Table 3 Estimate process.

Tell the customer that if they elect to estimate their income:

  • the assessment will be calculated on the income they tell Child Support they expect to earn between the start of the estimate and the end of the financial year. Payments (ongoing or lump sum) under the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS), the National Redress Scheme or withdrawals under the First Home Super Saver Scheme are not considered as income for Child Support purposes
  • they need to notify Child Support of any changes to their income during the financial year
  • the estimate will be reconciled after the end of the financial year, which could result in a child support debt if the estimate is not accurate. If a debt is raised when the estimate is auto reconciled as a result of tax lodgement, their tax refund will be automatically intercepted and applied to the debt

If a customer's income varies during the financial year, lodging an estimate may result in a higher assessment than if an estimate was not lodged. Circumstances where an estimate may not be the most appropriate option include:

  • Customers with contract or seasonal work who experience income fluctuations during the financial year. If they expect their taxable income to be similar to the ATI used in their assessment, lodging an estimate may not be appropriate
  • Customers who cease employment but expect to obtain new employment within a short period with income similar to, or more than, the relevant ATI
  • Customers expecting redundancy and who are yet to receive an employment termination payment (ETP), or customers who are expecting to receive a lump sum payment from another source. Explain the taxable component of the lump sum will be included in their estimated ATI if received during the estimate period and the consequences if this occurs. Ensure the customer understands they have the option of lodging the estimate after the lump sum is received
  • Customers whose child support case is due to end before the end of the financial year who would prefer to avoid potential retrospective changes (due to estimate reconciliation) after their case has ended
  • Customers who have lodged multiple estimates or previous estimates have been inaccurate. For more information, go to Step 6

Note: customers may advise that the income used in their assessment is wrong or has changed. However the ATI is not just based on taxable income. They may not understand the inclusion of other component amounts. For example, taxable income is $40,000 but ATI is $60,000 as it includes $20,000 for a Reported Fringe Benefits Total (RFBT). References has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.4.4 Child support income for an explanation of ATI.

  • information that may contradict the information supplied by the customer

Use information available from sources such as:

  • Client Benefits window

  • the contradictory information
  • under estimating can create a debt in the future and penalties may also apply

It may be appropriate to request more evidence to support the accuracy of the estimate.

Criteria to be met for an estimate to be lodged + Read more ...

For an estimate to be complete the customer must provide:

  • an indication of the case(s) the election will apply to.
    Note: reverse accounts in Cuba do not represent separate cases. If the customer has a role of both payee and payer in 1 case, the estimate must be recorded for both roles in Cuba
  • the estimated ATI that is, the taxable income and other component amounts for the financial year, or the remainder of the financial year
  • the YTD ATI, except when the estimate is to commence at the start of a new financial year or it is a subsequent estimate in the financial year
  • the reason for lodging the estimate, which is an explanation of why their income has reduced or changed. Providing the reason for the estimate helps in determining the accuracy of the estimate. However, if a reason is not provided, and the estimate is valid and accurate in all other aspects, then the estimate cannot be refused

Some customers may elect to provide their ATI components as single amounts for example Taxable income $32,400pa plus reportable fringe benefits $8,700pa. The estimate cannot be refused if the customer does not or will not provide details such as the name of employer, frequency of income, etc. However, Service Officers must be satisfied with the accuracy of the YTD ATI and the estimated ATI before accepting the estimate.

The customer may not have all information required to make the estimate complete. For example, they do not have other component details. In this case tell the customer:

  • what information is required to finalise the estimate
  • the date the information is required within 14 days
  • if the information is not provided by the advised date:
    • the estimate will be rejected as it will be incomplete and the current assessment will continue
    • if they disagree with the decision they can lodge a new estimate when they have all the relevant information which will delay the start date

If the final decision to reject the estimate as incomplete has not been explained, contact the customer to explain the decision before rejecting the estimate. See Contact with Child Support customers.

Note: there may be times when the customer cannot provide their YTD ATI information, for example, they are incarcerated and do not have the information. It may be appropriate to request the information from the employer by issuing a S161 notice.

If the estimate has been lodged in writing or via online contact the customer to clarify missing details before rejecting the estimate.

If the customer cannot be contacted by phone, send a Request for Client to Contact CSA (MX1-1) letter. Include the appropriate text so the customer understands what is required, see Letters Cuba process help, Create a CAL.

Incomplete estimate + Read more ...

If the customer cannot be contacted or does not respond to Request for Client to Contact CSA letter, reject the estimate as incomplete. See Step 4 in Table 7.

A customer cannot object to this decision. They will need to contact Child Support to lodge a new estimate. This estimate can only be taken from the date they make the new election.

Note: an estimate cannot be refused because the YTD ATI is considered too low. The estimate cannot be treated as incomplete or invalid if YTD ATI is provided. Discuss with the customer how the YTD ATI has been calculated and the impacts of providing an inaccurate YTD ATI when reconciliation occurs.

9

Valid estimate + Read more ...

An estimate election will be valid if:

  • There is a current child support assessment (stage 2 case)
  • There is no Income Amount Order in force relating to the estimating parent on the day of the election or for any part of the remainder of the financial year for any children of the assessment. For example, if an agreement or order sets a periodic amount for 1 child but a younger child is assessed under the formula the customer would be unable to estimate their income for use in the formula for the younger child. An estimate election can be made the day after the Income Amount Order ceases to apply. For more information, References has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.5.1: Income estimates for a year of income
  • The first election (the annualised amount) is not more than 85% of the person's ATI for the LRYI. If more information is required about Taxable incomes, go to Step 10
  • The YTD ATI has been provided. The YTD ATI is not required when an estimate is to commence at the start of a new financial year or it is a subsequent estimate

Note: if a customer is prevented from lodging an estimate because there is an Income Amount Order in force, consider other options that may be available. For example a Change of Assessment application may be appropriate, there may be an unemployment clause in an agreement, Court Order that could be invoked. Discuss options with the customer.

10

Taxable income + Read more ...

To lodge an estimate, the current child support assessment must be based on:

  • a taxable income as assessed by the ATO (or an overseas authority where the customer is resident in a reciprocating jurisdiction or in the case of a receiving parent who is resident in a non-reciprocating jurisdiction where the claimed for child/ren of the case are still eligible, see Step 3 in Table 3) or,
  • an ITD provided by the customer, that Child Support is satisfied is accurate

Note: payments (ongoing or lump sum) under the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS), the National Redress Scheme or withdrawals under the First Home Super Saver Scheme are not considered as income for Child Support.

If the assessment is not based on an ATO assessed income or an ITD, offer the customer the option to provide an ITD. For more information, see Income processing for Child Support customers.

For more information about the validity requirements of estimates, References has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.5.1: Income estimates for a year of income.

If the estimate is the first election in the financial year, go to Table 2. If the election is a subsequent estimate, go to Step 11.

11

Subsequent estimates + Read more ...

A customer can advise Child Support that their initial estimate is no longer accurate by making a subsequent estimate at any time during the financial year. A subsequent estimate can also be replaced at any time during the financial year with another subsequent estimate.

A subsequent estimate can be accepted after the year of income has ended if the estimate has not been reconciled and the new estimate election is more than the previous estimate election.

When taking a subsequent estimate, the following must be obtained and recorded:

  • date of the election (notification date) when the customer advised Child Support of a change in their circumstances and made a subsequent estimate
  • date of event when the current estimate (initial or subsequent estimate) became inaccurate

Income details must be taken from the date of event. The start date (effective date) of a subsequent estimate is:

  • Date of event if the subsequent income estimate is higher than the preceding estimate. The exception is when the subsequent estimate decreases the assessment, for example paying parent’s estimate changes their liability from a fixed annual rate (FAR) to an annual rate less than FAR. In this circumstance the date of the election will be used
  • Date of the election if the subsequent estimate is less than the preceding estimate. The exception is when the subsequent estimate increases the assessment, for example, a paying parent’s estimate changes their liability from a minimum annual rate to a FAR. In this circumstance the date of event will be used

In some cases, Cuba is applying the incorrect date of effect when applying an amended estimate of income. The errors have occurred where a customer has provided an update (a higher estimate figure) to an existing estimate of income. Cuba is incorrectly using the notification date, rather than the date of event.

Check the case liability rates to ensure the correct start date is being applied.

If Cuba does not apply the correct start date, delete the amended estimate and re-key with the same information and dates. Do not alter the dates. If this correction is not done at the same time the amended estimate is taken, contact the customer to explain the correction and impact on their assessment.

Where there are several subsequent estimates loaded after the affected estimate, delete these entries to allow for deletion and re-keying of the affected amended estimate. Then re-key subsequent estimates using the original information. Check letters for both the estimating and non-estimating parent to ensure only relevant letters issue.

The customer may be disadvantaged if there was a delay between the date of event and the date of election, and the subsequent estimate is lower than the preceding estimate, and the income earned between the date of event and the date of the election is different to the regular income after the date of election. For example the customer received a lump sum payment after the event date but before the date of the election, but they will only receive fortnightly Centrelink or salary income after the election date.

Escalate these cases to a Specialised Assessments Program Support Manager before accepting the subsequent estimate.

If the calculations for the subsequent estimate show that the new income is lower, identify the income the customer estimates they will receive from the date of the election to the end of the financial year. This is the amount of the income estimate election.

If a subsequent estimate increases the assessed rate above the minimum rate, a 28 day 'grace period' will apply before the increased assessed rate will be applied. For more information References has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.4.12: The Minimum Annual Rate of Child Support.

Note: YTD ATI is not required for a subsequent estimate. If a customer becomes aware that the YTD ATI provided in their initial estimate is incorrect, they may elect another amount at any time until their estimate has been reconciled. See Table 8.

12

Estimates and lump sum amounts + Read more ...

If the customer advises of a lump sum amount in the estimate period, that amount is automatically applied evenly across all the days in the remaining period up to 30 June.

If a subsequent estimate is accepted, a portion of the lump sum is left from the prior estimate that has not been included or used in the assessment. Use the Conversation Guide – Table 4 Lump Sum to tell the customer they have the option to carry the unused portion into their subsequent estimate. The Conversation Guide - Lump Sum and Estimate documentation macro include a tool to assist with calculating lump sum apportionment.

Discuss the impact at reconciliation including:

  • creation of additional outstanding child support liability or an overpayment of child support
  • estimate penalties being applied

Multiple estimates may be affected

Lump sums are generally payments received on 1 single date, however this term can also include periodic amounts confined to a specific date set within the estimate period. For example a contract for $850 per week for the period 1 November 2018 - 31 March 2019.

Record the reason for estimate + Read more ...

The customer should advise why their income has changed. Reasons for lodging an initial estimate may include:

  • loss of job
  • reduced hours
  • part time hours
  • loss of or decrease in overtime
  • commencement or changes of Parental Leave Payments

Record the YTD and estimate income amounts + Read more ...

The information belowpopulates relevant letters sent to the customer.

Payments (either ongoing or as a lump sum) received by customers under the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS), the National Redress Scheme or withdrawals under the First Home Super Saver (FHSS) Scheme are not considered as income for Child Support purposes. If a customer includes these payments as an income source or it is found the customer is receiving these payments:

  • document for information only
  • do not include in any income calculations

YTD amount

The YTD amount is the customer’s ATI received from the start of the current financial year and the day before the estimate is lodged.

Record each type of income in this period. Note: the YTD Calculator is only enabled for estimates after 01 July of a Financial Year.

See Resources for links to National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) and National Redress Scheme.

Is the estimate to start on the first day of a new child support period?

The YTD information is from 1 July to the day before the estimate’s start date.

If the customer is unable to confirm their YTD at the time of the estimate election:

  • record as much information as the customer is able to provide
  • ask the customer to call back within 14 days with the YTD income details

An estimate:

  • cannot be refused because the YTD is considered too low
  • can be refused if satisfied that the YTD ATI provided by the customer is more than the amount likely to be the total of the actual income component amounts for this period (that is the YTD is overstated)

Tell the customer the YTD ATI will be used when reconciling the estimate and must be accurate. If the customer becomes aware the YTD ATI provided in their initial estimate is incorrect, they may notify another amount at any time before the estimate is reconciled.

An example of when customers may inadvertently overstate the YTD is when they have received lump sum payments from their previous employer. There are 4 types of lump sum payments and customers often advise the total as their YTD whereas not all these amounts are included in a customer's ATI.

Lump sum payment D in the customer's payment summary is the tax-free component of a genuine redundancy payment or an early retirement scheme payment and is not taxable (and not included on a tax return) and does not form part of the ATI.

Lump sum B in the customer's payment summary is the amount paid for unused long service leave that accrued before 16 August 1978. Only 5% of this amount is included as assessable income on a tax return and forms part of the ATI. See Table 5

If no YTD income is provided, do not accept the estimate as it is incomplete (outlined in Step 7 in Table 1.

Estimate Income:

This is the customer's estimated ATI for the financial year or the remainder of the financial year if the election is after 1 July. If the customer is unsure of their taxable income and/or other component amounts they should check information with the ATO, their accountant or other relevant sources. The Services Australia website publishes a guide with information about Centrelink payments and whether they are taxable or non-taxable. If the customer is unsure of whether their Centrelink or DVA payment is a specified tax-free pension or benefit, they should check with Centrelink or DVA to confirm whether it fits the definition. For more information, References has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.4.4.10 Adjusted Taxable Income.

See Derived income for Child Support customers for more information about DVA income and Direct requests for information from Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) (CS) for information about the process for making, a request or to see a list of payment names included in the data exchange (only incomes that are included in a parent’s ATI are part of the data exchange, DVA payments which are not a component of ATI are not provided).If information needs to be requested about Centrelink income, see Manual information request about a Centrelink customer record.

An estimate can be refused if satisfied the customer's actual taxable income and other component amounts (that is their adjusted taxable income) for the estimate period is likely to be higher than the amount estimated (that is the estimated ATI is understated). See Table 5.

Total Net Investment Loss + Read more ...

The Income details group box includes fields for both Net Rental Income and Net Financial Investment Income. Document the total of all rental income less rental expenses and all financial investment income less allowable investment related expenses. The net amount (profit or loss) is recorded in the respective field. If there is a net loss (for either) the value must be recorded in the respective field as a negative. The losses will be automatically included in the Total Net Investment Loss field.

When a customer provides their taxable income, it may already include an adjustment for net rental property and/or investment losses. If the customer confirms the amount they provided as their taxable income includes any adjustments for the rental and or financial investment income and expenses, explain that Child support still needs to obtain and record the following information:

  • their rental income and associated rental expenses (rental interest deductions, other rental deductions, capital works deductions)
  • their total investment income and investment related expenses

Note: the customer will also need to provide their taxable income without the adjustment for rental and/or financial investment income/expenses so this can be recorded accurately.

Select the Calculate button to display the ATI. Calculate can be selected at any time however any subsequent changes to amounts keyed will require the Calculate button to be reselected.

When all relevant information has been recorded in the calculator select Ok. This will return Cuba to the Estimate Income window.

Confirm with the customer the estimated ATI and YTD ATI figures calculated are consistent with their expectation. It is their election that is being taken; the customer must understand how the figures have been calculated and confirm those figures as their estimate election.

6

Cuba warning messages + Read more ...

YTD

Cuba will display a message if the YTD ATI provided by the parent is zero or greater than the expected YTD by 10% or more. The 'expected YTD' is calculated by pro-rating the LRYI for the days between the start of the financial year and the day before the estimate start date.

If the start date of the estimate election is 1 July, then the YTD will be zero.

If the YTD ATI is zero or is 10% higher than expected, confirm with the customer if the YTD ATI amount provided is correct and seek an explanation of the amount. If the customer advises that the YTD ATI is correct, consider their explanation. If their explanation is not consistent with the information known, request evidence from the customer to support their claim.

If evidence is required, go to Step 2 in Table 5.

If the start date of the estimate election is 1 July then the YTD will be zero as there is no YTD period.

85% rule

The annualised amount of the first estimate election, must be 85% or less of the customer’s ATI for the LRYI. Cuba performs the 85% validity check against the LRYI used in the assessment.

Cuba will display a message after Calculate is selected. If the estimate is greater than 85% of the LRYI confirm with the customer that the amount provided is correct.

If the amount is correct, the estimate will be made invalid, go to Step 3 in Table 7.

FAR/MAR

Cuba will display a message if an estimate leads to a fixed annual rate (FAR) or minimum annual rate (MAR) assessment. Customers may make an application for:

  • FAR not to apply, or
  • MAR to be reduced to nil

Cases end before the end of the estimate period

Cuba will display a message if a case is expected to end during the financial year. This is to prompt the Service Officer to discuss the matter with the customer. Tell the customer that all the income earned within the financial year will be included in the estimate reconciliation, even the amount that is earned after the case ends.

For example, customer’s case will end 15 November 2021, they lodge an estimate of income 01 October 2021. The customer has an increase to their income between the period where their case ends (15 November 2021) and then end of the financial year (30 June 2022).

Customer will be unable to lodge a subsequent estimate after 15 November to reflect their increased income. The estimate will still be reconciled based on the taxable income for the full 2021/2022 financial year (Actual ATI). The reconciling income will be applied for the period the estimate was used in the assessment until the case ends (1 October 2021 to 15 November 2021).

  • add a corrected CSP
  • add back any other correct CSPs that came after the CSP you corrected
  • both cases should now display the same CSPs, from the one when the estimate started, and up to, and including the latest CSP
