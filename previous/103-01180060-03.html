Is the customer eligible for VWPR Income Management? + Read more ...

Customers may be identified for Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) Income Management because they:

  • have been assessed as a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient by a social worker, or
  • receive the Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH) rate of payment, are under 16 years and receive Special Benefit, or under 25 and recently released from prison or psychiatric confinement

Conducting the Initial Assessment interview

Table 3: This table contains steps to conduct the Initial Assessment interview.

Step

Action

1

Conduct Initial Assessment interview + Read more ...

During the interview the following must be covered:

  • Explain Income Management and how delivery of their payments will change
  • Discuss why Income Management is being put in place, VWPR Youth - the customer has automatically been identified for Income Management because they are not a full time student or apprentice, and not participating in Voluntary Income Management and are:
    • aged under 16 years and in receipt of a Special Benefit
    • 16 years of age or over and in receipt of an independent rate of Youth Allowance, Disability Support Pension or ABSTUDY Living Allowance due to meeting the Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH) criteria, or
    • under the age of 25 and received a Crisis Payment due to release from prison or psychiatric confinement within the last 13 weeks, and
    • Social Worker assessed - a social worker has determined the customer suitable for Income Management. If the customer does not agree with the social worker's decision, refer the customer to a social worker who can revoke the Notice
  • Explain how long Income Management will be applied to their payment:
    • Social Worker assessed - the end date of the Notice can be found on the Income Management Summary screen under 'Notice/agreement'
    • VWPR Youth - UTLAH and SpB-SUY: for a one-off period of 12 months. Note: customers may be identified for another 12 months if they meet the Crisis Payment (CrP) criteria
    • VWPR Youth - CrP: for a period of 12 months and can be eligible multiple times under this category
  • Explain what happens if the customer moves out of an Income Management area
  • Discuss how income managed money is accessed. For more information see Coding Income Management expenses
  • Explain what income managed money can be used for, that is, School Meals Program (if applicable), food, rent/housing, utilities, clothing, medical needs. See Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management
  • Explain what goods cannot be purchased from income managed funds (that is, prohibited goods and services - alcohol, home brew concentrate, home brew kits, tobacco products, pornographic material or gambling)
  • Explain how money can be allocated to BasicsCard:
    • Residual Payment method (default), or
    • they choose the Partial Payment method, and
    • immediate transfers (where Income Management funds are available)
  • If applicable, discuss changing existing Centrepay deductions to an income managed expense. Explain the impact of Income Management on any ongoing Centrepay deductions
  • Review and discuss any existing Rent Deduction Scheme payment and whether the customer would like to pay their State Housing Authority (SHA) rent from:
    • their income managed funds
    • their discretionary funds, or
    • a combination of both
  • Explain how money can be accessed for Income Management customers accessing funds while travelling
  • Explain how customers can contact Services Australia, including self service, in regards to Income Management and BasicsCard. See Income Management and BasicsCard self service options. Offer to register the customer for self service
  • Explain how common changes in circumstances may affect Income Management and the importance of notifying the agency of any changes in their expenses
  • Advise customer of impact on expense payments if income support payments are reduced due to earnings from employment or other income, and the priority needs payment order when there are insufficient funds to cover an expense
  • Explain how lump sum payments such as Advances, arrears and Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation payments are 100% income managed
  • Advise customer when Income Management ends, all Income Management expenses will stop. They must make arrangements to continue paying these expenses themselves, or contact the agency to discuss alternative payment options (for example, Centrepay)
  • Advise customer of their right to review Income Management decisions

2

Customer at risk + Read more ...

As a part of the assessment process, if a customer talks about suicide, self-harm or harn to others, they should be referred to a social worker immediately.

Service Officers should refer a customer to a social worker if they think Income Management would place the customer's mental, physical or emotional wellbeing at risk, which includes any situation where a customer:

  • cannot meaningfully engage with Services Australia due to mental health issues
  • does not have the capacity to comprehend the operation of Income Management
  • is experiencing serious instability in their housing or living situation and Income Management would substantially impair their ability to meet housing costs

For more information on when Social Work referrals can be made for both Income Managed and non-Income Managed customers, see the Resources tab in Social Work service referral.

Continue workflow: Income Management Interview + Read more ...

Interview Details

  • Logon ID interviewer: field. If this is not the Service Officer who conducted the interview, change it to the logon ID of this person
  • Date: field. Displays as today's date
  • Channel: field. Select an option:
    • Call Centre
    • Community Visit
    • Service Centre, or
    • Processing Centre
  • Location: field. Defaults to CSC if interview conducted in a service centre or will not display if interview conducted by Smart Centre

Interpreter Requirements

Details of interpreter requirements must be recorded for interview purposes.

Where no information is recorded on the Other Contact Details (OCD) screen, the Service Officer must exit the assessment workflow and update the OCD screen before proceeding with the interview. See Booking an interpreter for an appointment

Customer's Spoken Language: This will default to the interpreter language recorded on the OCD screen.

  • Interpreter Required, this will default to:
    • Yes, if the OCD screen indicates an interpreter is required
    • No, if the interpreter language is recorded as English, and cannot be changed. If an interpreter is needed for the interview, the Service Officer must exit the workflow and update the OCD screen as appropriate
  • Is an interpreter being used? This will only present if the customer's language is not English. Select Yes, No, Not Available or Not Required from the dropdown menu as appropriate
  • Reason: If an interpreter is not available, not being used or not required, the applicable reason the interview continued is to be selected from the drop down menu:
  • No:
    • Appointment not booked
    • Conflict of interest
    • Customer declined an interpreter
    • Customer wishes to use agent or family member
    • Deaf or hearing impaired
  • Services Australia does not have an interpreter who speaks the customer's language
  • Not available:
    • Interpreter did not attend interview
    • Interpreter service for that language not available
    • Same sex interpreter not available
  • Not Required:
  • No contact required
  • Speaks and understands English
  • More information: If an interpreter is Not Available, Not being used, or Not required, additional information can be recorded in addition to the standard reasons selected above. For example, where 'Customer wishes to use agent or family member' is selected, the agent or family member's details can be recorded in this field

It is not recommended the interview proceed without an interpreter.

Confirm the customer has been read the Privacy Notice and the Third Party Disclosure Information by selecting the Yes radio button. This must be read and understood by the customer before proceeding.

Select Continue.

7

Changing Centrepay / Rent Deduction Scheme payments to Income Management expenses + Read more ...

Centrepay:

Discuss with the customer whether current Centrepay deductions should be:

  • converted to an Income Management expense
  • maintained as they are
  • varied,
  • or ceased

Search the Income Management Third Party Organisation (TPO) using Office Locator to determine the preferred payment methods for the TPO. Check for any payment notes that might impact converting the deduction from Centrepay to Income Management.

To change a Centrepay deduction to be paid in full or partially from income managed funds:

  • obtain any additional information, such as account details, from the Centrepay deduction to be cancelled or varied
  • action Centrepay deduction as appropriate:
    • cease the deduction where it will be paid in full from income managed funds, or
    • vary the deduction as discussed with the customer. For more information see Starting, changing and cancelling a Centrepay deduction at the request of a customer
  • code new Income Management expense

Rent Deduction Scheme:

Discuss with the customer whether they would like their rent to continue being paid from discretionary funds, changed to an Income Management expense or a combination of both. For more information see Updating an existing Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) deduction for a customer who has commenced Income Management.

8

Continue workflow: Code expenses + Read more ...

As per the priority needs discussion with the customer during the interview, set up the regular expenses to be paid from Income Management funds.

If there are offset payments note the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED), as shown on Expense Management Summary screen. Offset payments affect how expenses are coded. The next EPED date will be the start date for each expense allocation.

For new expenses:

  • select Add regular expenses from the Please select the expense you wish to add: dropdown list
  • for details on how to code expenses, see Coding Income Management expenses
  • for details on how to code School Meals Program expenses, see Payment of School Meals Program (SMP) expenses from Income Management funds or Centrepay

For customers who wish to pay their expenses via BasicsCard and choose Partial Payment method, regular expenses must be set up once the assessment is finalised.

Note: if the customer is being changed from 'Auto' to 'Manual' Income Management, any existing Income Management expenses set up at the time Income Management commenced can be viewed under the 'Regular Expenses' section. Expenses should be reviewed and updated as appropriate. For details on how to code changes to existing expenses, see Coding Income Management expenses.

When all expenses have been added, select the Priority Needs link.

Finalising the Income Management interview process

Table 4: This table outlines the steps for finalising an Initial Assessment interview.

Step

Action

1

Continue workflow: Priority Needs discussion result + Read more ...

The results of the priority needs discussion must be recorded on the Priority Needs Expenses screen.

If this is the first discussion being held, an answer Yes or No must be given to each of the Priority Needs types. If priority needs have been recorded previously, two questions will be displayed:

  • 'Is a discussion being held?' Select Yes as a discussion must be recorded as part of an Initial Assessment interview
  • 'Are there changes?' Select Yes or No as appropriate. If the answer is Yes then a change must be made to either the priority expense/s need/s and/or how the expense/s is/are being met

Priority Needs Expense Category

Customer Response: This field records how the customer will or will not meet the priority need. The field appears only after Yes or No is selected against the priority expense type, and appears against each expense type field when selected. When the field appears, a payment method type must be selected.

  • Yes, select the appropriate value:
  • No, select the appropriate value:
    • Not applicable to customer
    • Not met

If this is not the first discussion, Service Officers can view historical discussions under the Priority Needs Discussion History block. Past discussions can be selected and viewed but the details are protected.

Select Continue to be directed back to the Expense Management Summary screen.

2

Finalise workflow: Income Management Summary screen + Read more ...

If there are Work Items requiring action, these will be displayed on the Income Management Summary screen in the Existing Work Items section.

If Work Items cannot be actioned within the workflow, investigate and action while the customer is still present, once the workflow has been finalised. If the customer is not present, the Work Items should be left for a Service Officer to action at the next customer contact. For more information on work items, see Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients (VWPR) Income Management Work Items.

Review the Income Management Summary screen to confirm:

  • Income Management has been correctly applied ('Qualification Details' block), and
  • payments and expenses are correctly reflected in the 'Fortnightly (14 days) balance details' block

Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page.

The workflow is completed. Manually record a DOC with the following information:

  • Customer has been advised and understands:
    • the Privacy Notice and Third Party Disclosure
    • the impact on Income Management if they are/become, or have/appoint, a payment nominee
    • the review and appeal process
    • they can request an exclusion from Income Management and the exclusion criteria (VWPR Youth customers only)
    • they can request a review of their circumstances at any time by a Services Australia social worker
    • if applicable, why they have been transferred to this measure of Income Management and the impacts of transferring between measures
  • Any other information required under the guidelines for adding a DOC to a customer's record
  • Any other relevant information, for example, what communication products were given to the customer

After finalising the assessment workflow, Service Officers must update the Office Code (OC) screen to align Service Reason PQN with the customer's correct Office Code. If the Office Code is not updated, all automatically generated Work Items and IM reviews will be assigned to the recorded Smart Centre and display as outstanding activities.
