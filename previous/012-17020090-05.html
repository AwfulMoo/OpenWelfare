Business rules for convalescent care

Table 1

Item

Rule

1

Eligible Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) clients may be paid for up to a total of 21 days of convalescent care per financial year.

2

In the event a claim is submitted for a future end period, payment must be held until the account period has elapsed. If the patient is deceased in the interim period, the claim is to be adjusted accordingly.

3

All periods of convalescent care in excess of 21 days per financial year required prior financial authorisation by the DVA.

4

Claims for payments against item NH50 will be auto matched to a prior approval record with a treatment type of Nursing Home (NH).

5

These items are to be paid at a daily rate and can be bulked.

6

Accounts are to be received on a provider invoice.

Business rules for community care

Table 2

Item

Rule

1

The specific fees are for daily care packages and income tested fees, if applicable.

2

The fees are to be paid by the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) and billed by the service provider to Services Australia on a monthly basis and paid in arrears.

3

The items are to be bulked and paid at a daily rate in arrears.

4

All claims are to be auto matched to a DVA PARS record with a treatment type of Nursing Home (NH).

5

Accounts are to be submitted to Services Australia on the service providers own letterhead.

Business rules for transition care

Transition care business rules

Table 3: This table describes a list of Services Australia's business rules for transition care Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

Item

Rule

1

All periods of transition care require a Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) prior approval to be created. Claims that do not have a prior approval are to be rejected.

2

The service provider may submit their account to MA monthly in arrears during the episode of transition care or at the end of the period of transition care depending on the length of the episode. The claim should include the name of the veteran, their date of birth and DVA file number. If the patient is deceased in the interim period, the claim is to be adjusted accordingly.

3

Eligible DVA clients may be paid for up to a total of 12 weeks for each episode of transition care. There is no limit to the number of transition care episodes per eligible DVA client.

4

One extension of no longer than 42 days per episode of transition care may be approved in exceptional circumstances and following repeat Aged Care Assessment Team assessment.

5

Claims for payments against items NH09, NH10 and NH11 will be auto matched to a prior approval record with a treatment type of NH.

6

These items are to be paid at a daily rate and can be bulked.

7

When claiming manually it is preferred a completed D1217 Claim for Treatment Services voucher is forwarded, however this is not mandatory. It is acceptable for a service provider to forward an invoice only.

Business rules for Short term restorative care (STRC)

Table 4

Item

Rule

1

All periods of STRC require a Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) prior approval to be created. Claims that do not have a prior approval are to be rejected.

2

STRC will be delivered by approved service providers through the Department of Health and Aged Care, after an Aged Care Assessment Team (ACAT) assessment. Upon the completion of the delivery of an episode of service, the provider can claim the daily care fee for the full period of that service via VAP. Each provider must be registered with VAP in order to claim.

3

VAP will pay the daily care fee on behalf of DVA. Payment is to be calculated on the following basis:

  • (Daily Fee) x (Number of Days STRC provided);
  • Payment must not include leave days, but will include weekends;
  • Each episode must not exceed 56 days of provided STRC;
  • Each episode may include up to 7 leave days, so that the elapsed time of a full episode of STRC may be up to 63 days (56 days of service + 7 days of leave);
  • Each episode may include days of service in a different setting, but must not exceed 56 days in total (28 days in-home, 28 days in hospital);
  • Each client may be approved for an episode of STRC each 6 months (max 2 episodes per calendar year);
  • Leave days will not be identified by a particular item

4

Rejecting claims

  • Scenarios where a claim may be rejected include:
  • Dates do not match the Prior Approval;
  • No Prior Approval exists for the claim;
  • DVA Client not eligible;
  • Provider attempts to claim prior to a period of STRC being concluded (pre-payments);
  • Provider attempts to claim leave days;
  • Provider attempts to claim more than 56 days;
  • Provider does not have Specialty Code;
  • The claim is incomplete.

5

Claims for payments against items NH12 and NH13 will be auto matched to a prior approval record with a treatment type of Nursing Home (NH).

6

When claiming manually it is preferred a completed D1217 Claim for Treatment Services voucher is forwarded, however this is not mandatory. It is acceptable for a service provider to forward an invoice only.
