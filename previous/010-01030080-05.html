FAQ

Following are some common queries received from Australian Apprentices.

Additional assistance for Australian Apprentices

Item

Examples

1

Question: A customer cannot afford to live on their wages. How can they apply for extra money? + Read more ...

Answer: A full-time Australian Apprentice customer may be entitled to ABSTUDY, Austudy or Youth Allowance as an apprentice. Payment is subject to a personal income test and parental income test depending on the customer's circumstances. If the customer is undertaking a part-time apprenticeship, they may be qualified for payment as a job seeker to supplement their wages.

2

Question: A customer has had to move away from home and now cannot afford to live on their apprenticeship wages. Is there any help available? + Read more ...

Answer: Australian Apprentices may obtain additional assistance from an Apprenticeship Network provider by applying for a Living Away From Home Allowance (LAFHA). Apprentices need to contact their local Apprenticeship Network provider to check eligibility.

A full-time Australian Apprentice customer may be entitled to ABSTUDY, Austudy or Youth Allowance as an apprentice. Payment is subject to a personal income test and parental income test depending on the customer's circumstances. If the customer is undertaking a part-time apprenticeship, they may be qualified for payment as a job seeker to supplement their wages.

Note: if an apprentice claims a LAFHA payment, they are not entitled to receive an income support payment from Centrelink, and vice versa.

3

Question: Can a customer apply for rent assistance? + Read more ...

Answer: If the customer is in receipt of income support from Centrelink they may be eligible for Rent Assistance (RA).

4

Question: Where can an employer find information on wages that apply to apprentices, trainees, workers, etc.? + Read more ...

Answer: Additional information can be obtained from Fair Work Ombudsman (FWO) in the relevant State/Territory through the Industrial Relations Authority. This phone number is available in Fair Work Ombudsman.

5

Question: Does the Australian Government provide any financial assistance for the employment of an Australian Apprentice? + Read more ...

Answer: Assistance may be available. Contact an Apprenticeship Network provider for additional information by going to the Australian Apprenticeship Support Network search page.

6

Question: Is there any assistance for the employment of an Indigenous Australian? + Read more ...

Answer: The Department of Education, Skills and Employment has an Indigenous Employment Line available to obtain information on Indigenous Programs.
