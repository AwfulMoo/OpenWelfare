Establishing a net market value + Read more ...

For an existing property:

See Maintaining the value of real estate assets on customer records, for details of how the value of a current customer's real estate is indexed.

Procedure ends here.

For a new or unrecorded property:

Where the customer advises of a new real estate asset, a net market value of the customer's real estate must be established by directing the customer to the relevant online service or issuing the customer with the following forms:

  • Real Estate form (MOD R), or
  • Income and Assets Update (SA220m) for Age Pension and Carer Payment (CP) customers (and Disability Support Pension (DSP) customers from mid-January 2010)
  • Business details form (Mod F). (Where the customer's real estate is used for business purposes a Mod F and a Mod R must be issued)
  • The Rural Property Questionnaire (Q454) is used in conjunction with the Mod R for medium to large farm properties. It collects more information from the customer for the valuer, to allow them to more correctly value the property from the roadside. Generally, if the property operates as a commercial enterprise, issue the Q454 in addition to the Mod R. The Q454 is not needed for smaller properties such as hobby farms with minimal farm improvements or bush blocks. Where the whole property is exempt because it is the principal home, or it is an exempt farm asset, do not issue a Q454 for that property. If required, issue the Rural Property Questionnaire (Q454)

Note: if a customer is conducting a business from their principal home, only the portion used exclusively for business purposes is assessed as an asset. Where part of the home is used for both domestic and business purposes, the Assets Test exempt status of the customer's principal home is retained, so a Mod R is not required.

If the customer is receiving a Blind Pension, only issue a Mod R and/or Mod F if they are being assessed for rent assistance or have a partner and the partner is not on a means test free payment. In these cases, details are still needed to determine rent assistance or the partner's rate.

Effect of new valuations + Read more ...

A valuation that is higher than a previous valuation or a customer's estimate is applied from the first payday after it is received.

When a valuation is lower than a previous valuation or a customer's estimate, apply the date of effect rules.

Exceptions are where:

  • there is evidence the customer deliberately misrepresented details of the asset: the value may be applied from a date earlier than the valuation date
  • the customer applied for a formal review of a current valuation. In this case, the review of decision date of effect rules apply

The payday definition is in Section 23 (1) on the References page.

Only raise a debt if a person has deliberately misrepresented the details of the asset. Where a debt shell is created due to a property value not being updated before the next payday after the valuation is received, this debt should be waived.

Do the home and curtilage rules apply to the real estate? + Read more ...

From 1 January 2007 the Assets Test assessment for the principal home and curtilage was amended to a single title rule. There are 2 curtilage categories used to determine a customer's allowable house and curtilage concession, private land use test and extended land use test. A grandfathered savings provision for real estate will grandfather concessions for real estate to 2 groups of existing customers on payments continuously from prior to 1 January 2007.

Customer has elected to have the proceeds of the sale of their principal home exempted and the temporary home still assessed as an asset + Read more ...

The exemption of the sale proceeds is applicable for an initial 12 months and, in certain circumstances, may be extended up to a maximum of 24 months from the time of sale. The extended exemption may apply to all income support payments.

To gain an extended exemption of up to 24 months for the principal home sale proceeds, the customer must have a continuing intention to apply the proceeds of the sale to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate a new principal home and have:

  • Made reasonable attempts to obtain a new principal home. This could be to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate their new principal home, and
  • Been making those attempts within a reasonable period after selling the principal home, .that is, within 6 months, and
  • Experienced delays beyond their control in obtaining a new principal home

If one of the above criteria is not met, the customer cannot gain an extended principal home sale proceeds exemption for up to 24 months.

  • Change the address on Address (AD, ADS, ADH). For more information, see Changing address
  • If the customer is sharing with another person, who is not a close family member or partner, send a SS284 or SS293, if required
  • Code the full proceeds of the sale on the Savings Summary (SVS, SVP, SVDI), either a Deposit Accounts (SVDI) or a Savings Bank Account (SVP). Note: ensure the proceeds are only recorded once, so the asset is only counted once
  • If the customer has elected to have the proceeds of the sale of their principal home exempted and the temporary home still assessed as an asset, code the amount of the funds intended for purchasing or building another home as an exempt asset in the Assets Test Deduction Amount: field

Any money intended for purchasing or building another home can be exempted from the Assets Test for an initial 12 months with a possibility of an extension for another 12 months. However, regardless of the Assets Test exemption, the funds are assessed under the deeming provisions. If the customer uses part of the proceeds of sale to purchase a block of land or other assets to build a new home, these assets are also exempted under the Assets Test. This is provisional upon the joint value of the land and money exempt for the building of the new principal home does not exceed the total value from the proceeds for the sale of the old principal home. Adjust the balance of the proceeds of sale for the SVDI or SVP account and record details of the purchase of the block of land in your activity finalisation NOTE.

A system error may be present if changes are attempted to Real Estate Business (REBS) details and no updates have been made since the customer’s relationship status had changed. This will happen if the customer is single and the real estate is still recorded as partly or fully owned by the partner.

If this error is presented when attempting to apply changes to the Real Estate Business (REBS) details:

  • screen dump all, including historical pages, on the Real Estate (RE) screen, relating to the impacted property or business
  • delete the relevant entry in the Real Estate and Business (REBS) and in the same activity recode all relevant information including historical entries and the correct % ownership
  • Look at ex-partner’s record to check if property is still being assessed and if updates are needed. Do not use the information supplied by the customer to update the ex-partner’s record

Establishing a net market value + Read more ...

For an existing property:

See Maintaining the value of real estate assets on customer records, for details of how the value of a current customer's real estate is indexed.

Procedure ends here.

For a new or unrecorded property:

Where the customer advises of a new real estate asset, a net market value of the customer's real estate must be established by directing the customer to the relevant online service or issuing the customer with the following forms:

  • Real Estate form (MOD R), or
  • Income and Assets Update (SA220m) for Age Pension and Carer Payment (CP) customers (and Disability Support Pension (DSP) customers from mid-January 2010)
  • Business details form (Mod F). (Where the customer's real estate is used for business purposes a Mod F and a Mod R must be issued)
  • The Rural Property Questionnaire (Q454) is used in conjunction with the Mod R for medium to large farm properties. It collects more information from the customer for the valuer, to allow them to more correctly value the property from the roadside. Generally, if the property operates as a commercial enterprise, issue the Q454 in addition to the Mod R. The Q454 is not needed for smaller properties such as hobby farms with minimal farm improvements or bush blocks. Where the whole property is exempt because it is the principal home, or it is an exempt farm asset, do not issue a Q454 for that property. If required, issue the Rural Property Questionnaire (Q454)

Note: if a customer is conducting a business from their principal home, only the portion used exclusively for business purposes is assessed as an asset. Where part of the home is used for both domestic and business purposes, the Assets Test exempt status of the customer's principal home is retained, so a Mod R is not required.

If the customer is receiving a Blind Pension, only issue a Mod R and/or Mod F if they are being assessed for rent assistance or have a partner and the partner is not on a means test free payment. In these cases, details are still needed to determine rent assistance or the partner's rate.

Effect of new valuations + Read more ...

A valuation that is higher than a previous valuation or a customer's estimate is applied from the first payday after it is received.

When a valuation is lower than a previous valuation or a customer's estimate, apply the date of effect rules.

Exceptions are where:

  • there is evidence the customer deliberately misrepresented details of the asset: the value may be applied from a date earlier than the valuation date
  • the customer applied for a formal review of a current valuation. In this case, the review of decision date of effect rules apply

The payday definition is in Section 23 (1) on the References page.

Only raise a debt if a person has deliberately misrepresented the details of the asset. Where a debt shell is created due to a property value not being updated before the next payday after the valuation is received, this debt should be waived.

Do the home and curtilage rules apply to the real estate? + Read more ...

From 1 January 2007 the Assets Test assessment for the principal home and curtilage was amended to a single title rule. There are 2 curtilage categories used to determine a customer's allowable house and curtilage concession, private land use test and extended land use test. A grandfathered savings provision for real estate will grandfather concessions for real estate to 2 groups of existing customers on payments continuously from prior to 1 January 2007.

6

Customer has elected to have the proceeds of the sale of their principal home exempted and the temporary home still assessed as an asset + Read more ...

The exemption of the sale proceeds is applicable for an initial 12 months and, in certain circumstances, may be extended up to a maximum of 24 months from the time of sale. The extended exemption may apply to all income support payments.

To gain an extended exemption of up to 24 months for the principal home sale proceeds, the customer must have a continuing intention to apply the proceeds of the sale to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate a new principal home and have:

  • Made reasonable attempts to obtain a new principal home. This could be to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate their new principal home, and
  • Been making those attempts within a reasonable period after selling the principal home, .that is, within 6 months, and
  • Experienced delays beyond their control in obtaining a new principal home

If one of the above criteria is not met, the customer cannot gain an extended principal home sale proceeds exemption for up to 24 months.

  • Change the address on the Address Details (AD) screen. For more information, see Changing address
  • If the customer is sharing with another person, who is not a close family member or partner, send a SS284 or SS293, if required
  • Code the full proceeds of the sale on either the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen or the Deposit Accounts (SVDA) screen. Note: ensure the proceeds are only recorded once, so the asset is only counted once
  • If the customer has elected to have the proceeds of the sale of their principal home exempted and the temporary home still assessed as an asset, code the amount of the funds intended for purchasing or building another home as an exempt asset in the Assets Test Dedn Amt $: field

Any money intended for purchasing or building another home can be exempted from the Assets Test for an initial 12 months with a possibility of an extension for another 12 months. However, regardless of the Assets Test exemption, the funds are assessed under the deeming provisions. If the customer uses part of the proceeds of sale to purchase a block of land or other assets to build a new home, these assets are also exempted under the Assets Test. This is provisional upon the joint value of the land and money exempt for the building of the new principal home does not exceed the total value from the proceeds for the sale of the old principal home. Record details of the purchase of the block of land in a DOC and adjust the balance of the proceeds of sale on SVDI or SVDA.

  • Gross Annual Income $: '0'
  • Annual Interest $: '0'
  • Allowable Income Dedns $ pa: '0'
