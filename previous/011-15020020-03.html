This page contains the steps for Service Officers to action cases in all queues in the Compensation Case Management System (CCMS) and determine if a minor is entitled to a refund when they are the injured person.

Actioning a case for a minor in all queues in the CCMS

Table 1: This table describes the process for Service Officers to action cases in all queues in the CCMS when a minor is the injured person.

Step

Action

1

Check injured person is a minor + Read more ...

Is the injured person under 14 years of age (a minor)?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 6

2

Check Medicare card details + Read more ...

Is the minor on the same Medicare card as their parent or guardian?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Review legal documentation + Read more ...

Is there legal documentation received such as a Commonwealth of Australia Statutory Declaration or a Guardianship or Court order to verify the claimant can act on behalf of the minor?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No:
    • Do not process the work form
    • Ask for any required documents. Authority to release information and acting on a person’s behalf for Medicare Compensation Recovery contains details of the required documentation
    • Suspend the case in CCMS, before releasing the work form
    • Go to Step 9 to add a case note
    • Procedure ends here

4

Review Claimants details + Read more ...

Does the claimant name in CCMS match the parent or guardian on the Medicare card?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No:
    • Re-route the case to the Supervisor queue to update the Claimant Details field. The workflow will stop here
    • Go to Step 9 to add a case note before releasing the work form
    • Go to Step 7 to continue processing the work item

Note: if the Service Officer is not trained in the Supervisor queue, email Programme Support Officer (PSO) or Team Leader with details of the required actions to update the Claimant Details field

5

Process the work item + Read more ...

When the minor is listed on a parent or guardian’s Medicare card, all Medicare Compensation Recovery case documents (including the notice to claimant) may be sent to the parent or guardian

  • Process the work item
  • Issue documents to the parent or guardian, as applicable
  • Go to Step 9 to add a case note before releasing the work form

Procedure ends here.

6

Injured person over 14 + Read more ...

If the injured person is over 14 years of age:

  • Re-route the case to the Supervisor queue to update the Claimant Details field. The workflow will stop here
  • Go to Step 9 to add a case note before releasing the work form, and
  • Go to Step 7 to continue processing to work item

Note: if the Service Officer is not trained in the Supervisor queue, email Programme Support Officer (PSO) or Team Leader with details of the required actions to update the Claimant Details field

7

Select case in the Compensation Case Management System – Team 1 Supervisor + Read more ...

Process supervisor form details

  • Select Super Options? - Select Yes and Press [Enter]
  • Reset current case document dates? - Select No and Press [Enter]
  • Reset Class action? Select No and Press [Enter]
    Note: this field is only used to identify Advance Payment Option (APO) or Non Advance Payment Option (Non-APO).
  • Reset voluntary recovery amount? - Select No and Press [Enter]
  • Delete current documents? - Select No and Press [Enter]
  • Change the compensable person or claimant name? - Select Yes and Press [Enter]

Note: do not change the Compensable persons details, Press [Enter] through these fields without changing.

If the claimant’s name is being updated to the parent or guardian, go to Step 8

Claimant details - Over key claimant name fields

  • Claimant Family name field - Over key the family name (if required) where the minor has a different surname to the parent or guardian previously listed, Press [Enter]
  • Claimant Given name field - Over key the given name to the minor’s first name, Press [Enter]
  • 2nd initial name field - Over key the initial to the minor’s initial in this field, Press [Enter]
  • Change processing centre? - Select No and Press [Enter]
  • Create an ad-hoc letter? - Select No and Press [Enter]

Can the claimants address be verified?

  • Yes:
    • If a change of address has been reported (via phone or in writing), or if other agency records have been updated with new address details the Address field box will appear. New address information can be entered
    • Go to Step 9 to add a case note before releasing the work form
  • No:
    • The workflow will stop here
    • Go to Step 9 to add a case note before releasing the work form

8

Update claimant’s name to parent or guardian + Read more ...

Claimant details

  • Claimant Family name field - Over key the family name (if required) to the parent or guardian listed, Press [Enter]
  • Claimant Given name field - Over key the given name to the parent or guardian’s first name, Press [Enter]
  • 2nd initial name field - Over key the initial to the parent or guardian’s initial in this field, Press [Enter]
  • Change processing centre? - Select No and Press [Enter]
  • Create an ad-hoc letter? - Select No and Press [Enter]

Can the claimants address be verified?

  • Yes:
    • If a change of address has been reported (via phone or in writing), or if other agency records have been updated with new address details the Address field box will appear. New address information can be entered
    • Go to Step 9 to add a case note before releasing the work form
  • No:
    • The workflow will stop here
    • Go to Step 9 to add a case note before releasing the work form

9

Add a case note + Read more ...

If the case notes screen has not opened automatically, select the Add notes button from the list on the right hand side of the main CCMS screen

  • The top box shows all notes previously recorded on this case
  • In the blank bottom box use standard case notes to record:
    • The actions taken
    • The information requested and from whom
    • The reasons for a decision
  • Select Add to save notes. A confirmation box will appear, select Yes to add case note
  • Press [F4] to release the work item, and
    • If this is the end of the process, case will suspend indefinitely
    • To update the claimant details fields, select case in Compensation Case Management System - Team 1 Supervisor then return to Step 7, and
    • If further actions are required in other CCMS work queues, process case until further actions are complete

Determining entitlement to refund for a minor

Table 2: This table is a guide used by Service Officers to determine the entitlement to a refund for any payment for a case involving a minor.

Step

Action

1

Refund to a minor + Read more ...

If the refund is from a notice of charge paid, see:

  • Actioning a notice of judgment or settlement table
  • Notice of judgment or settlement for Medicare Compensation Recovery

Where the injured person is a minor who is entitled to a refund from an intended advance payment, issue the refund in the minor’s name. Send the refund to the claimant (parent or guardian) or the authorised financial administrator, usually the Public Trustee.

Note: update the Claimant Name field to the minor’s name in the CCMS - Team 1 Supervisor queue before any refund is actioned. See Step 2 in the Actioning a case for a minor table.

2

Verify entitlement to an advance payment refund + Read more ...

When Services Australia has not been notified who is administering the minor's financial affairs, verify who is legally entitled to receive an advance payment refund by telephoning the:

  • compensation payer (or their solicitor), or
  • the claimant’s authorised third party (authorised solicitor)

The Public Trustee in the minor's State or Territory can only be contacted if documents have been received (including the notice of judgment or settlement) confirming the Public Trustee is involved.

Note: if documents have not been received this is a breach of privacy.

Do not process a refund before receiving supporting documents. Refer to Payments, refunds and debts for Medicare Compensation Recovery.

Go to Step 3.

Note: if the Service Officer is unsure about these requirements, email Programme Support Officer (PSO) or Team Leader or contact the PSO hotline.

3

Issuing advance payment refunds after supporting documentation is received + Read more ...

In cases where there is no Public Trustee, issue an advance payment refund in the minor’s name, to be sent to the:

  • Claimant (normally a parent or guardian)
  • Legal representative (including a Power of Attorney or Litigation Guardian)
  • Financial administrator or executor (normally appointed by the courts), or
  • Minor, where no legal representative exists

Refer to Payments, refunds and debts for Medicare Compensation Recovery for more detailed information about when Electronic Funds Transfer banking details are required, or when a cheque may be issued.
