Example of a manual adjustment after deleting a sanction for residential care

Table 2

Item

Description

1
  • The Aged Care Quality and Safety Commission’s email sent to Services Australia on 12 September 2022 advising service XYZ is sanctioned from 1 September 2022 for 6 months
  • Jane Doe is listed in the Aged Care Quality and Safety Commission’s email as a care recipient who entered residential respite care before sanction’s start date and then became a permanent care recipient at the same service after the start date
  • The Service Officer registers both the sanction and positive manual adjustment details in ACSP
  • The positive manual adjustment is in respect to care recipient Jane Doe for the September 2022 claim
  • The Aged Care Quality and Safety Commission sends the email request to delete the sanction via email on 29 September 2022 advising the incorrect advice was sent and the service shouldn’t have been sanctioned
  • The Service Officer deletes the sanction record in ACSP and does further checks in ACSP for manual adjustments made during the sanction period
  • As a result, the Service Officer sees there is a positive manual adjustment for Jane Doe
  • The residential care service is not entitled to the positive manual adjustment as the residential care service will automatically receive payment for Jane Doe after the sanction record deletion
  • The Service Officer rejects the positive manual adjustment to ensure the residential care service receives the correct payment
  • The Service Officer must add a care recipient note detailing the action taken in relation to any manual adjustment changes
