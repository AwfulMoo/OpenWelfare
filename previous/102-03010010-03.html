1

Customer notifies a change in address + Read more ...

If the change in address is due to separation, see Change in relationship status from partnered to single.

When it is established that a customer's address must be updated, then all current addresses (including postal address) and telephone details (including mobile phone numbers) must be checked and updated online or if required via the Change in Contact Details workflow.

If a customer has been selected for a Rent Assistance (RA) Profile Review, address updates must be done as part of the review. While a RA Profile Review is outstanding, customers and staff will be blocked from updating the address outside the review.

If a job seeker has given their Employment Services Provider their new address details, the Employment Service Provider will advise the job seeker they will need to contact Services Australia to update the home address.

When the job seeker contacts Services Australia to update the details staff must:

  • Confirm the home address details
  • Access the relevant Customer First (CF) Action Issue
  • Check the Online Diary for scheduled appointments. If appointments need to be rescheduled due to the change of address, update the Online Diary
  • Select the Change in Contact Details hyperlink

The Service Officer also needs to determine if there are any potential impacts on eligibility, mutual obligation requirements and/or ongoing entitlement. For further information on how updating a change of address can interact with a job seeker's jobactive organisation, see Job seeker change of address. This may include the consideration of Moving to an Area of Lower Employment Prospects (MALEP) employment related exclusion period.

Advise the customer these updates can be made online.

Note: the following Income Management customers cannot update their Home/Temporary/Term address via online services:

  • Disengaged Youth (DEY)
  • Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR)
  • Voluntary Income Management (VIM)
  • Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR)

These customers will be required to contact the agency for updates to be made.

There are specific qualification criteria in relation to where the carer and care receiver are living. When a carer or care receiver notifies a change of address, continued eligibility for payment needs to be confirmed. See Change of address for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) for more information.

Updating change of address details

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Customer's address to be updated + Read more ...

If the customer has indicated they no longer have a home address or that their new accommodation is temporary or unstable, consider recording a homelessness indicator. If the customer already has a homelessness indicator on their record, review and update the homelessness indicator to reflect the change in circumstances.

For correspondence to be sent, Services Australia must be provided with an address where a customer can receive mail. Before updating a customer's residential address to no fixed address, staff must ensure an alternate postal address has been provided, see Where customers are unable to provide a residential address.

Note: accommodation details (for example, rent paid or ownership) are only required when the customer is receiving a Social security benefit, Social security pension, ABSTUDY and/or Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

Although it is not mandatory to update the Accommodation Details (AC) screen, or Subscriber details on the Telephone Details (TD) screen, for customers not receiving a Social security benefit, Social security pension, ABSTUDY and/or Family Tax Benefit (FTB), if the information is available it can be recorded.

If customer is moving to a remote area, assess entitlement to Remote Area Allowance (RAA).

When updating the address, check if there is a current postal address as this will need to be end dated if no longer required. Suggestion: using yesterday's date will ensure outgoing correspondence is sent to the correct address.

Check if the customer:

  • Acts as a nominee for another person. If so, update their nominee details. Note: do not change the customer's address (for example, postal address) to that of the nominee's unless the customer and the nominee reside at the same address. If it is a court appointed arrangement and the nominee does not want the customer to receive their correspondence, suppress the customer's copy without changing the postal address
  • Is the applicant for an ABSTUDY student (for example, for Schooling A payments), as the address details will need to be updated on that customer's record as well
  • Resides in an identified community area. Care should be taken when updating an address for customers living in an Indigenous community, see ABSTUDY customer who has returned to live in an Indigenous Australian community
  • An ABSTUDY student who is independent or on the away from home rate and returning to the parental home, see Returning to the parental home when in receipt of the away from home rate of ABSTUDY
  • Is assessed as Separated Under One Roof (SUOR) - if a customer currently assessed as SUOR advises they have moved out of the shared residence, the 'SUR' Relationship Qualifier Code must be ended on the Marital Status (MS) screen on the customer's record. See the Process page of Member of a Couple (MoC) and Separated under one roof (SUOR) assessments for information on how to end the 'SUR' qualifier code

Note: if the customer is in receipt of ABSTUDY as a secondary school student with living away from home entitlements, do not code any change of circumstances on the customer's file. Send an ACTDOC with details to the relevant ABSTUDY Processing Service. For further information, see Cessation of the ABSTUDY away from home rate. Procedure ends here.

Is the address update required due to allocation of a Future Address – Sharer Details DOC?

  • Yes,  go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Future Address – Sharer Details work items + Read more ...

Where an address change is advised within an early claim for YA, system limitations may prevent these updates being made at the time the claim is processed. Under these circumstances, a Future Address – Sharer Details DOC is created for allocation via workload manager to process the address update at the time the change will occur.

Service Officers allocated a Future Address – Sharer Details work item must action these updates in Customer First:

  • go to the Document List (DL) screen to review the  Future address – Sharer details  DOC Sharer information
  • check the record to see if the customer has told the agency about any change to their accommodation circumstances since their claim lodgement
  • contact the customer to confirm the sharer details are still correct, prior to updating the record

Was contact with the customer successful?

  • Yes, update the address as advised by the customer, go to Step 4
  • No, update the address with the details contained in the DOC, go to Step 5

Note: after updating the address, ensure the DOC is completed.

4

Update address details and preferred service centre by using the Change in Contact Details workflow + Read more ...

Points to consider when answering questions in the workflow:

  • Has the MS screen been updated with a relationship qualifier or should a previously coded qualifier be ended prior to coding Accommodation Details (AC) screen? This must be done if a customer is:
    • Partnered and separating temporarily (that is, going into temporary accommodation)
    • Partnered and separating permanently (that is, relationship has broken down)
    • Assessed as SUOR and is no longer sharing with their former partner. The 'SUR' qualifier code must be ended on the MS screen. See the Process page of Member of a Couple (MoC) and Separated under one roof (SUOR) assessments for information on how to end the 'SUR' qualifier code
  • For customers receiving a Social security benefit, Social security pension, ABSTUDY and/or Family Tax Benefit (FTB), code the AC screen regardless of whether the customer is paying for accommodation or not, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA).
  • Exceptions: No AC screen updates are required where:
    • a student with a term addresses ('TER') contacts to advise a change in parental home details only, or
    • a customer has moved into a Temporary address ('TEM') and the reason the customer has moved does not entitle them to receive RA for their temporary accommodation, see Temporary accommodation and Rent Assistance (RA)
  • Corrections to records, street name, number, etc.
  • Note: for Special Benefit (SpB) customers receiving free board and/or lodgings - code the Type of Rent: field with one of the following: 'FBL' (free board and lodging), 'FBD' (free board), or 'FLD' (free lodging), to allow the system to calculate the correct rate of payment
  • If rent is or was being paid, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA)
  • Office Code (OC) screen if changed
  • Telephone Details (TD) screen. For more information (including telephone subscribers), see Updating telephone details and/or paying Telephone Allowance (TAL)
  • When updating the address details for an ABSTUDY or Youth Allowance (YA) customer (students only) who is temporarily not living at their permanent home, update:
    • the Address Type: field as 'TER' (Term Address Type) on the AD and AC screens with the temporary away from home address
    • their phone number at the address where they are living while studying/looking for work/ new apprentice as a Contact number 'CON' on the TD screen
    • this will leave the permanent home details (usually the parents' details) as the home address
    • Note: when a YA customer permanently moves away from the parental home then the place of the new residence is to be coded as 'HOM'
  • Updating the Regional Location Details (RLD) screen when the parental home changes. YA and ABSTUDY customers who are full-time students in an approved scholarship course may also be eligible to receive Relocation Scholarship. For details on coding the RLD screen, see Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding
  • When a YA or ABSTUDY customer permanently moves away from the parental home then the place of the new residence is to be coded as 'HOM'
  • YA and ABSTUDY customers leaving the parental home, whether temporary or permanently, may be entitled to a higher rate of payment. For more information, see:
    • ABSTUDY customer leaving parental home or
    • Youth Allowance (YA) customer leaving the parental home
  • Family assistance customers who are renting should be advised to provide verification even if their income estimate is currently too high to receive RA. This is to ensure that, after the Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation process occurs at the end of the financial year, they receive arrears of any possible RA entitlement they may have in their top up payment
  • If the customer has other addresses recorded on their Address History (ADH) screen, check if other addresses require updates. If so, go back to the top of this step
  • Go to Step 5

5

Update address details and preferred service centre manually + Read more ...

Update address details by accessing the following screens via the Customer Details Task Selector (CDTS) screen:

If unsure of code, use field help ('?')

  • Address Details (AD) screen:
    • Ensure Is RA being claimed on this Address?: field is coded with 'Y' so the system will assess if the customer is eligible for Rent Assistance (RA)
    • If customer and partner are not living together but are still classed as a couple, the Marital (MS) screen needs to have been updated with a relationship qualifier
    • Check the Make Partner the same: field is coded accordingly. Note: if the change in address is due to separation, see Change in relationship status from partnered to single
    • Ensure screen is correctly coded for when an ABSTUDY or Youth Allowance (YA) customer leaves home
  • Telephone Details (TD) screen. For more information (including telephone subscribers), see Updating telephone details and/or paying Telephone Allowance (TAL)
  • Office Code (OC) screen if changed
  • Accommodation Details (AC) screen regardless of whether customer is paying for accommodation or not. Exceptions: No AC screen updates are required where:
    • A student with term addresses ('TER') contact to advise a change in parental home details only
    • The customer has moved into a Temporary address ('TEM') and the reason the customer has moved does not entitle them to receive RA for their temporary accommodation. See Temporary accommodation and Rent Assistance (RA)
  • Ensure MS screen has been updated with a relationship qualifier prior to this step if customer is:
    • partnered and separating temporarily (that is, going into temporary accommodation)
    • separating permanently (that is, relationship has broken down)
    • Where the relationship qualifier is no longer applicable (that is, customer assessed as SUOR advises they have moved out of the shared residence). The 'SUR' qualifier code must be ended on the MS screen. See the Process page of Member of a Couple (MoC) and Separated under one roof (SUOR) assessments for information on how to end the 'SUR' qualifier code
  • If rent is or was being paid, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA)
  • For Special Benefit (SpB) customers receiving free board and/or lodgings - code the Type of Rent: field with one of the following: 'FBL' (free board and lodging), 'FBD' (free board) or 'FLD' (free lodging) to allow the system to calculate the correct rate of payment
  • If the customer is moving to a home they own, or they are purchasing, the AC screen still needs to be updated to reflect home ownership after the AD screen has been updated. See Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA)
  • If the customer is not paying rent, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA)
  • Coding the Regional Location Details (RLD) screen for changes to the parental home address. RLD screen to be updated as follows:
    • Event Date: field, code the date of the parental home address change
    • Address Date: field, code the date of the parental home change
    • Address line: field code the new home details and post code
    • Complete the Source: and DOR: fields
    • Action: field, code 'I' (insert)

Note:

  • Family assistance customers who are renting should be advised to provide verification even if their income estimate is currently too high to receive RA. This ensures that, after the Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation process occurs at the end of the financial year, they receive arrears of any possible RA entitlement they may have in their top up payment
  • If the customer has other addresses recorded on their Address History (ADH) screen, check if other addresses require updates. If so, go back to the top of this step

Check if customer acts as a nominee for another person. If so, update their nominee details as well. Note: do not change the customer's address (for example, postal address) to that of the nominee's unless the customer and the nominee reside at the same address. If it is a court appointed arrangement and the nominee does not want the customer to receive their correspondence, suppress the customer's copy without changing the postal address.

Special considerations affecting a change of address

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Income Management - customers identified for or current on IM who move out of a declared Income Management area + Read more ...

Current Income Management customers

Customers whose payments are income managed and who move indefinitely or permanently outside of a declared Income Management area will continue to have their payments income managed. The duration of Income Management will depend on the measure applicable to the customer.

For more information about portability of Income Management, see Customer moves out of an Income Management area.

In all cases, customers must have their priority needs reassessed to ensure they meet their current requirements. For more information see:

  • Changes to Income Management priority needs
  • Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management
  • Coding Income Management expenses

Customers profiled but not current on Income Management

Customers who have been identified for but have not yet commenced Income Management may still have their payments income managed if they move out of a declared Income Management area during their Income Management engagement period.

For more information about portability of Income Management, see Customer moves out of an Income Management area.

Service Officers not trained in Income Management:

Service Officers are to complete all non-Income Management business associated with the customer's contact, ensuring all relevant advice regarding the change of address has been given. Once finalised, transfer the customer unannounced to the Income Management line. The Resources page has a link to the National Transfer numbers.

Service Officers are to seek assistance from a specialised Service Officer (if available) or Service Support Officer (SSO), or contact the Access Support Centre (select Income Management option 7.) Specialised staff will assist Service Officers to provide correct advice to the customer.

2

Customers updating their address to a CDC location + Read more ...

When updating a customer’s address to a CDC location, ensure the correct address type is being recorded appropriate to the customer’s situation, temporary or permanent.

Customers who move permanently to a CDC location may result in the customer becoming eligible for the CDC if they meet other eligibility criteria. Service Officers must advise the customer their new address is within a CDC location and they may be identified for the CDC if they meet other eligibility criteria.

If customers would like to discuss CDC eligibility, refer them to the CDC Hotline.

Note: updating or deleting a home or term address for the purposes of avoiding the CDC will not automatically end a customer’s participation on the CDC.

Extra points to consider for ABSTUDY and YA customers + Read more ...

When updating a YA or ABSTUDY customer's change of address update the AC screen and, where appropriate, the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen. This is essential to ensure the correct rate of payment is paid to the customer.

Check the following to ensure correct rate of pay (for example, at home or the away from home rate) is being made to customer.

  • For a customer moving out of the parental home, see
    • Away from home rate for ABSTUDY
    • The away from home rate for Youth Allowance (YA) customers
  • For a customer returning to the parental home, see
    • Returning to the parental home when in receipt of the away from home rate of ABSTUDY
    • Returning to the parental home when independent or qualified for the away from home rate of Youth Allowance (YA)
    • Cessation of the ABSTUDY away from home rate
    • Commencement, cessation and coding of the away from home rate for Youth Allowance (YA)
    • Qualification for the payment of Rent Assistance (RA)

Note: customers who are independent under State Care, unreasonable to live at home and disadvantaged will generally lose qualification to independent status if they move to the home of a parent.

  • For customers moving from one parent's home to live with another parent, see Changes which may affect the Parental Income Test (PIT)
  • For customers who are full-time students and receiving the away from home rate advising a change to their parental home address, the Regional Location Details (RLD) screen may also need updating, Updating the RLD screen when the parental home changes. For details on coding the RLD screen, see Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding

YA and ABSTUDY customers who are full-time students in an approved scholarship course may also be receiving Relocation Scholarship.

  • For independent customers, consider whether they are an accommodated independent person. See The away from home rate for Youth Allowance (YA) customers
  • For independent ABSTUDY customers, review their circumstances to identify any issues associated with independence, such as homelessness. See Reviews of independence for ABSTUDY
  • If the customer also changed school/education institution see Changing or correcting course of study for Youth Allowance, Austudy and Pensioner Education Supplement students

See Step 1 in Table 6.

Extra points to consider for carers + Read more ...

  • A review must be carried out if either the carer or care receiver notifies of a change of address, to ensure the constant care or supervision criteria are still being met. See Change of address for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) for more information
  • For CP, if the carer and care receiver are living in different homes, the time involved for the carer in travelling between the 2 homes, the level of care provided and the accessibility of the carer to the care receiver must be considered
  • For CA, in general, the carer and care receiver must reside together, unless certain exceptions are met. Check if the specific qualification criteria are still met

Extra points to consider for DSP customers + Read more ...
