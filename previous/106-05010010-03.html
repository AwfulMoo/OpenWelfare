Calculating the start day

Step

Action

1

Claim submitted + Read more ...

If a claim for a Social Security payment and/or concession card has been submitted, a claim will not be considered lodged until it is fully completed and all required actions are completed.

This includes supplying all mandatory supporting documentation.

This does not include 'supplementary tasks', such as supplying a rent certificate for Rent Assistance.

Online claims cannot be submitted until all required documents are provided. For Carer claims this includes medical reports. See Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM).

2

Determine appropriate action + Read more ...

Depending on the claim and the customer's circumstances, different start dates may apply for those who are:

  • claiming a concession card, go to Step 4
  • lodging an early claim, see Early claims. Note: early claim provisions do not apply to concession card claims
  • claiming soon after their partner's claim
  • transferring from one payment to another
  • claiming or receiving Parenting Payment (PP)
    • receiving PP and their relationship status changes, they may need to transfer to a different rate of payment (Parenting Payment Partnered to Parenting Payment Single and vice versa), see Claiming Parenting Payment (PP) which details the date of effect of the rate change,
  • claiming soon after child birth
  • incapacitated
  • claiming soon after death of their partner
  • claiming after admission to residential aged care
  • claiming ABSTUDY
  • claiming Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) or ABSTUDY PES
  • claiming Age Pension where the customer qualifies but is not yet payable, claim held until payable. See: Assessing Age Pension claims and transfers claiming Youth Allowance (YA) (students) and elects to claim from a later date. See Calculating the start day for Austudy and YA students and Australian Apprentices claims

If none of the above circumstances applies and the customer is claiming a payment, go to Step 3.

3

Calculate start date for payment + Read more ...

Services Australia can deem a vulnerable customer as having submitted a claim on an earlier date. See Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.

Customers may qualify for a start date of payment:

  • before the submission date if backdating provisions apply, or
  • from the date the claim was submitted if the customer qualifies and the payment was payable on the date of submission, or
  • at a later date if an early claim or an exclusion period applies. See the Resources page for examples of payment at a later date and RapidConnect scenarios and payability start dates

Note: there are administrative processes to only consider the customer's claim as having been made from a particular point in time (as this is the most beneficial outcome for the customer). This may occur for qualifying customers not yet payable, but who become payable within 13 weeks of submitting the claim.

See:

  • Assessing Age Pension claims and transfers
  • Calculating the start day for Austudy and YA students and Australian Apprentices claims

Procedure ends here.

4

Calculate start date for concession card (CSHC, LIC, FST or EHC) + Read more ...

If the customer is qualified on the date the claim for a concession card was submitted and the claim is complete, the start day is the date the claim was submitted, unless backdating provisions for a medical service or treatment apply.

Claims for the following concession cards may be backdated for a medical service or treatment situation if continuous eligibility can be established throughout the entire period between the date of medical service or treatment and the date of claim:

  • Low Income Health Care Card (LIC)
  • Health Care Cards (HCC) for foster children
  • Ex-Carer Allowance (child) Health Care Card (EHC)

If the customer is not qualified until a later time, the claim will be rejected as early claim provisions do not apply to concession card claims.
