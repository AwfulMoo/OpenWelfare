Customer contacts about a Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment claim decision

Step

Action

1

Is the Service Officer trained in Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No,
    • complete a cold (unannounced) transfer to 'Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment' in Services Australia Workspace
    • procedure ends here

2

Authenticate customer + Read more ...

Complete Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO). Do not give hints or prompts that help the customer answer the questions.

Was the customer authenticated?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No,
    • no details can be given/acted upon
    • procedure ends here

3

Second explanation of decision + Read more ...

Once a customer has received an explanation of decision, there are no further review options.

In the Document List (DL), the outcome of the review will be recorded in a CUS ODM APL APL EXP SET ASIDE or AFFIRMED DOC.

Has the customer already had an explanation of decision by a Suitably Skilled Officer?

  • Yes, there are no further appeal options, annotate the original Suitably Skilled Officer review DOC with the customers call. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Discuss the claim outcome + Read more ...

Discuss the claim outcome with the customer using any of the following documents for the claim period.

  • on or after 18 January 2022:
    • claim details in Process Direct
    • any Notes or DOCs on the customer’s record about their claim
  • on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 17 January 2022:
    • the customer’s Claim for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment claim form (EM252) or Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Verbal Claim Fast Note
    • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Granted or Rejected Fast Note
    • other DOCs on the customer's record about their claim
  • on or before 8 December 2021:
    • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Verbal Claim Fast Note or claim form
    • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Granted or Rejected Fast Note
    • other DOCs on the customer's record about their claim

Note: if the claim was rejected due to ‘fraud’ tell the customer

  • ‘Your claim was ineligible as eligibility could not be established.’

Do not

  • tell the customer their claim was rejected due to ‘fraud’
  • read any Notes or DOCs on the record to the customer

Is the customer satisfied after the discussion?

  • Yes,
    • create a DOC on the record outlining the conversation with the customer
    • procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Customer requests an explanation of the decision + Read more ...

If the customer does not understand or agree with the decision after discussing the claim outcome (other reasons), they can request an explanation which will be provided by a Suitably Skilled Officer:

  • Open Customer Record and search for the record
  • While in the customer's CRN, launch the Script Selector using the link at the top left of screen
  • Key 'Review' in the Search field, then run the Internal Review/Explanation script to record the request for an explanation
  • Create new or select existing case screen:
    • select Add new explanation or review
    • in the Date Client Queried Decision field, enter the date the customer queried the decision
    • select Next

  • Select Path screen, select:
    • I am registering a new request for explanation
    • Next

  • Customer Circumstances screen, select:
    • No to ‘Do any of the following apply to this customer?’
    • Next

  • Administrative Details screen, enter details as follows:
    • Source of Request: select the correct option from the drop down
    • Date of Decision: enter date the claim was finalised
    • Decision Maker’s Logon: enter logon of original claim decision maker, or if it was an automated decision the user should enter their own logon
    • Payment Type the Decision has been made against: select Ex-gratia One Off Payment
    • Disaster ID: for Event codes and workflows for self isolation, quarantine or caring starting:
      On or after 18 January 2022, see Resources in Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Claims in Process Direct
      On or before 17 January 2022 see Resources in Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Claims in Customer First
    • Process Type: select the appropriate option from the drop down
    • Which Office/Team made the Decision? Regardless of who made the original decision select for:
      Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment claims: TEP
    • Date Decision Queried: should pre-populate from the Date of Decision field
    • Reason for Contact: select the appropriate option from the drop down
    • Special Case Indicator: leave blank
    • Select Next

  • Original Decision screen, select:
    • Either Rate or Rejection
    • Next

  • Reason for Decision screen, select:
    • Disaster qualification not met
    • Next

  • Reason for Explanation screen:
    • record the customer’s reason for request in free text field
    • select Next

  • Transfer to relevant SME team or continue Explanation screen, select:
    • No, the Service Officer is referring the request for explanation to a Suitably Skilled Officer
      To which site do you want to transfer the request:
      Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment claims: TEP (this should auto-populate)
    • Select Next - Script creates an ACTDOC that will show on AL
    • Advise customer of case reference number that will display in the script
    • Select No if asked if partner wants to request an explanation to review
    • Select OK on the script has ended screen
    • Open Customer First and search for the record
    • Go to AL screen and select 'O' to add a keyword to the activity
      Add Keyword: 'PLDPSME'. This must be added to make sure these work items allocate to a Service Officer with the skills to complete these reviews
      Go to the activity on the AL screen and select K to check the keyword PLDPSME is on the activity
      - If the keyword PLDPSME is visible, select Next, procedure ends here
      - If the keyword PLDPSME is not visible, go back to AL and select O to add the keyword PLDPSME, select Continue, procedure ends here

Suitably Skilled Officers who are allocated the request for an explanation, go to Step 6.

6

Completing the request for explanation of a claim outcome + Read more ...

Open Customer Record, and search for record.

To understand and explain the decision, use for self-isolation, quarantine or caring periods starting:

  • on or after 18 January 2022:
    • claim details in Process Direct
    • any DOCs on the customer’s record about their claim
  • on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 17 January 2022:
    • the customer’s Claim for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment claim form (EM252) or Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Verbal Claim Fast Note
    • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Granted or Rejected Fast Note
    • other DOCs on the customer's record about their claim
  • on or before 8 December 2021:
    • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Verbal Claim Fast Note or claim form
    • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Granted or Rejected Fast Note
    • other DOCs on the customer's record about their claim

If the customer is not on the phone, make 2 genuine attempts to contact them to explain the claim outcome decision.

Is contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 10

7

Contact successful + Read more ...

If there are concerns about the validity of information provided by the customer, do not change the decision:

  • tell the customer their claim cannot proceed as eligibility cannot be established
  • do not tell the customer their claim has been escalated because of possible fraud
  • do not use the term 'fraud'
  • submit a Fast Fraud tip-off form

Has the customer provided new information that changes the original decision, or was the original decision incorrect?

  • Yes,
    • run the Internal Review/Explanation script to finalise the explanation
    • in the explanation of decision DOC staff must document:
      background information
      why the decision was changed
      the additional information used to change the decision
      if the customer has been contacted
      claim periods and event codes
    • Legislative/Policy References screen: enter S32B of the FF (SP) Act 1997
    • Reference source: from the drop down box select Operational Blueprint
    • go to Step 8
  • No,
    • the initial decision is correct and cannot be changed
    • the customer has no further review or appeal rights
    • run the Internal Review/Explanation script to finalise the explanation
    • in the explanation of decision DOC staff must document:
      background information
      why the decision was not changed
      if the customer has been contacted
      claim periods and event codes
      decision made under Section 32B of the Financial Framework (Supplementary Powers) Act 1997
    • Legislative/Policy References screen: enter S32B of the FF (SP) Act 1997
    • Reference source: from the drop down box select Operational Blueprint
    • procedure ends here

8

Explanation changes decision + Read more ...

The outcome of the explanation has resulted in a change to the original decision.

If the customer has been:

  • underpaid and needs a top-up in payment, for example paid $450 but should have been paid $750, go to Step 9
  • initially rejected - the Suitably Skilled Officer investigating the decision must implement the new decision, go to Step 11

9

Customer was underpaid + Read more ...

If the outcome of the explanation is to pay the customer a top-up as they were underpaid, run the Fast Note workflow in Customer First (no contact with Technical Peer Support is needed). Use:

  • Location: Customer First > Workspace > Documents and Correspondence > Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Emergency > Pandemic Leave Disaster > PLDP TOP UP Request
  • Service Reason: does not need to be changed
  • Select Confirm once the text in the Fast Note drops into the text box
  • Complete the template with the information needed
  • Document completion: make sure this is set to No to allow the Fast Note to automatically allocate to Level 2 Helpdesk for resolution

10

If unable to contact the customer + Read more ...

  • Run the Internal Review/Explanation script to finalise the explanation. The script will issue a Q449 letter advising that contact was unsuccessful and if they would like to talk about the decision they can contact Services Australia
  • When preparing the Q449 letter:
    • select Program, and enter Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment in Program Name
    • the options are based on details captured by the script. There should be no need to change these values. Check and update if necessary by typing DATA in the Command: field
      for example, the letter may show the incorrect phone number. Typing DATA in the Command: field lets staff update it to 180 22 66 in the GEN.ENQ.PHONE.INIT.OFF field

11

Initially rejected and claim to be granted + Read more ...

For self-isolation, quarantine or caring periods that start on or before 17 January 2022 - Customer First claims

If a Customer First claim was initially rejected and the Suitably Skilled Officer investigating the decision must implement the new decision:

  • re-run the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow to update the decision and pay the customer
  • see Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Claims in Customer First

For self-isolation, quarantine or caring periods that start on or after 18 January 2022 - Process Direct claims

If a Process Direct claim was initially rejected, the Suitably Skilled Officer investigating the decision must implement the new decision by re-indexing it in Process Direct.

  • Select the Customer Summary tile
  • On the Search Customers screen, enter the Customer Reference Number (CRN) in the CRN field and select Go
  • Select the
    • CRN link from the Search Results
    • Transactions icon
    • All Transactions button
    • Transaction ID of the completed claim that needs to be reassessed
  • This will display the Claim Application page
  • Select

Did the Re-index Claim icon display in ?

  • Yes, do not use Assisted Customer Claim (ACC)
    • Select Re-index Claim
    • The claim will open for processing.
    • To finalise (grant) the claim, see Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Claims in Process Direct. Procedure ends here
  • No,
    • Check if the claim has already been re-indexed and a new Change of Circumstance (CoC) Re-index claim transaction ID is available on the customer's record. See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions
    • Check the Status of the finalised claim being re-indexed. See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions.
    • Claims with a Not Required status cannot be re-indexed.
    • Run an ACC using relevant information in the Claim Summary to change the decision and pay the customer.
    • To run an ACC, see Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment - Assisted Customer Claims.
    • Procedure ends here.
