What's the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program Round 2?

It is a one-off grant to support travel agents and tour arrangement service providers who have lost income because of the impacts of COVID-19.

It is to help travel agents and tour arrangement service providers stay viable so they can keep paying refunds and credits to Australian consumers unable to travel because of COVID-19.

The Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister, Minister for Infrastructure, Transport and Regional Development, Treasurer, and Minister announced this grant opportunity for Trade, Tourism and Investment, on 11 March 2021.

You must read both the Round 1 and Round 2 COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program Guidelines on the GrantConnect website (grants.gov.au) before you make a claim.

Is my business eligible for the grant?

Your business may be eligible if you received a payment in Round 1 of the program and also meet the eligibility requirements for Round 2, these include:

  • Your application must be submitted by someone who is listed as an ‘eligible associate’ on the Australian Business Register (to decide if someone is an ‘eligible associate;)
  • Round 1 turnover figure was not based in any part on Total Transaction Value (TTV)
  • The business is solvent and intends to continue to operate for the April to June 2021 quarter, and
  • The business will make best endeavours to retain its staff; and to meet the business’ obligations to process refunds and travel credits to Australian consumers

What is a travel agent or tour arrangement service provider?

A travel agent is an entity that, on a commission basis, mainly operates a business that:

  • sells travel products either directly or on behalf of third parties to consumers, and
  • coordinates reservation bookings, confirmations and payments for travel products either directly or on behalf of third parties for consumers

For the purposes of the grant, a person is not a travel agent if they are only a shareholder of the business.

A tour arrangement service provider is an entity that, on a commission basis, mainly operates a business that:

  • sells travel packages either directly or on behalf of third parties to consumers, and
  • coordinates the booking reservations, confirmations and payments for travel packages either directly, or on behalf of third parties for consumers

For the purposes of the grant, a tour arrangement service provider does not include:

  • a person or entity who operates the day-to-day running of a tour or tourism activity, such as a tour guide

When can I apply?

You must apply for Round 2 between 2 May 2021 and 11:59 (AEDT) 12 June 2021, or until the program funds run out.

It is best to get your claim in as soon as possible. Please note, no late lodgement will be accepted for Round 2.

We can also only accept one claim per ABN.

Can I apply for Round 2 if the business was rejected for Round 1?

To apply for Round 2 you will need to have been determined as eligible and received a payment for Round 1 of the program.

The only exception is if your business did not receive a payment due to being assessed on a TTV of above $20 million but with a GST turnover of below $20 million.

If you are part of this category, you will have the opportunity to be considered for a payment under Round 2 by an exceptional circumstances request, you must email Consumer Travel Support.

How do I request my application be considered under exceptional circumstances?

To request exceptional circumstances, you should email Consumer Travel Support in the first instance.

Austrade will decide if your application will be considered under exceptional circumstances.

What if I was paid in Round 1 using TTV but I would have been entitled to a greater amount over both Rounds 1 and 2 had my claims been assessed using turnover as defined in Round 2 of the guidelines?

If an applicant’s combined Round 1 and Round 2 payment based on turnover (not TTV) would have been higher than only a Round 1 payment based on TTV, they can request their case be considered under exceptional circumstances.

Applicants wanting to have their case considered under exceptional circumstances should email Consumer Travel Support.

What does ‘turnover’ mean?

Turnover is defined in Round 2 Guidelines. Applicants should be directed to these, to make sure currency of the definition for the purposes of this program.

Turnover is total business income (not profit), less any:

  • GST included in sales to your customers
  • sales that are not for payment and are not taxable
  • sales not connected with an enterprise that is part of your business
  • input-taxed sales you make
  • sales not connected with Australia

What does ‘Total Transaction Value (TTV) mean?

TTV is the value of all air tickets, cruise fares, accommodation, car hire, tours and other related travel services booked by the agent.

Similar to a real estate agent, this does not represent the income of the business that provides the services. It is also not taxable income for business purposes.

How do I claim for Round 2?

To make a claim you must be an associate or authorised person for your business on the Australian Business Register (ABR). You should check your details on the ABR are up to date before you start your claim.

Go to servicesaustralia.gov.au/travelsupportprogram, then How to claim section. You must then follow the steps in order.

  • Read both Round 1 and Round 2 COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program Guidelines on the GrantConnect website (grants.gov.au)
  • Start the claim by selecting the ‘apply for a service’ option
  • Complete the online application process including a self-declaration about the eligibility requirements
  • Submit the claim
  • Be willing to lodge any documents as requested by Austrade as part of their verification process (they will notify you if this is needed)

Can I start an application if I am still waiting on the outcome of an appeal?

Yes, you can.

When you start your application, you will be asked if you currently have a pending appeal. If you answer yes, your application will be put on hold until the appeal has been finalised as the outcome of the appeal may affect the amount your business is eligible for under Round 2.

To be eligible for Round 2, you must have qualified for and received a payment under Round 1 of the program.

Can my application be processed faster if I am in financial hardship?

There are no provisions in the guidelines for financial hardship claims.

Both Services Australia and Austrade will do/are doing everything possible to process claims as quickly as possible.

How long will it take for my application to be processed?

It depends on the business’ eligible assessed turnover as determined in Round 1 of the Program:

  • less than $500,000 - Services Australia will endeavour to finalise claims within 4 weeks from date of receipt
  • $500,000 and above - Austrade will endeavour to assess applications within 8 weeks, once the outcome has been determined, Services Australia will endeavour to make a payment within 2 weeks

Please note, if you are waiting for an outcome of an appeal for Round 1 of the Program, your Round 2 application will not be assessed until the outcome has been decided.

How will I know when you have stopped taking claims?

We will carefully monitor funds paid under the program. If program funds run out before 12 June 2021 and you have submitted your application, we will let you know.

Information will also be published on our website at servicesaustralia.gov.au/travelsupportprogram and on Business Hub.

Can I submit my claim in one go, from start to finish?

Yes, you can.

You will need to apply using the Business Hub. All information is on servicesaustralia.gov.au/travelsupportprogram. Go to the How to claim section. You must then follow the steps in order.

What if I don't have access to online services?

You need to make a claim for the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program online on our Business Hub.

If you were the applicant who lodged Round 1 claim, you should still have access.

If you do not have access to online services or are a new applicant, you can get support to make your claim online. Go to servicesaustralia.gov.au/travelsupportprogram, and then to the ‘How to claim’ section.

What if I cannot complete the online application as it will not let me select certain responses?

There are known display issues if you use Internet Explorer as your web browser. We recommend you use Google Chrome to get the best experience when completing your claim in Business Hub.

Do I need to provide anything when I claim?

No.

No documents are needed at the time of making the online application.

We will ask you to verify your business details and confirm that you were eligible and received a payment under Round 1 of the Program.

You should keep a copy of your application and any supporting documents.

Note: Austrade may ask you to provide documents as part of their verification process. They will contact you and outline what is needed/how to provide it if needed.

Do I need a new ATO certificate?

For Round 2 you do not need a Certificate from the ATO.

For businesses with a turnover of $500,000 or more, Services Australia will send Austrade a copy of the certificate lodged for Round 1 with your claim for assessment.

How much can I get under the program?

It depends on your business’ turnover and the amount you received under Round 1 of the Program.

For Round 2 of the Program, there are 9 grant amount tiers, these are as follows, for eligible business with a turnover of between:

  • $50,000 and $99,999 will get $7,500
  • $100,000 and $199,999 will get $15,000
  • $200,000 and $299,999 will get $25,000
  • $300,000 and $399,999 will get $37,500
  • $400,000 and $499,999 will get $45,000
  • $500,000 and $599,999 will get $50,000
  • 1,000,000 and $1,999,999 will get $62,500
  • $2,000,000 and $2,999,999 will get $75,000
  • $3,000,000 and $20,000,000 will get $100,000

How will you pay me?

If the grant is approved it will be transferred as a one-off lump sum into your nominated Australian bank account. It must be the account associated with your ABN.

Can my accountant or tax agent lodge the application for me?

Your accountant or tax agent cannot make a claim on behalf of the business unless they are also listed as an associate or registered person in the Australian Business Register.

What if I forgot my login details for PRODA?

  • Please call 1800 700 199 and select Option 1 for help with PRODA, or
  • Go to Services Australia and search for PRODA

What if I see a different name during my application process to the business I wish to claim for?

Sometimes there are multiple business names recorded under the one Australian Business Number (ABN). When you applied for the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program Round 1, you chose a business as part of the business registration, linking and starting the claim process.

If you get to the end of the claim process and there is another business name (instead of the business you wish to claim for) you should not be concerned as:

  • the verification process that takes place is made under the ABN (not the business name)
  • we will use information received from your first application to confirm information about the ABN

What if I disagree with a decision?

If you do not agree with the decision, you can ask for the decision to be reviewed. Your review will be assessed by either Services Australia or Austrade depending on the business’ eligible assessed turnover decided under Round 1:

  • If turnover is less than $500,000, you can request that a Services Australia Subject Matter Expert (SME) review your claim decision
  • If turnover is $500,000 and above, you can request that an Austrade SME review your claim decision

Can I access the Exceptional Circumstances Review process if I am unhappy with a decision?

Austrade may waive one or more of the eligibility criteria in exceptional circumstances.

Applicants wishing to have their case considered under exceptional circumstances should email Consumer Travel Support in the first instance.

Note: Round 2 payments are calculated based on the eligible assessed turnover amount determined under Round 1 of the Program. Requests for turnover to be assessed using TTV over another reporting method in order to receive a higher grant payment:

  • will not be considered, and
  • will not be considered as an exceptional circumstance

What if I disagree with the review outcome?

If you do not agree with the recommendation Services Australia or Austrade has made as part of your review, you may complain to other regulatory bodies, including the Commonwealth Ombudsman. The Ombudsman will not usually look into a complaint unless the matter has first been raised directly with the relevant Commonwealth entity.

The Commonwealth Ombudsman can be contacted on:

  • Phone (Toll free): 1300 362 072
  • Email
  • Website: www.ombudsman.gov.au

What if I put the wrong bank account number in my claim?

Bank account details (as well as other claim information) cannot be updated once the claim has progressed to the ‘Review and Hold’ step.

If a bank account number or any other content needs to be updated, Services Australia will need the correct information from you.

Contact us on 1800 560 774, 8am to 5pm Monday to Friday.

In some circumstances, the claim may need to be cancelled and started again.

What is the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program Round 2?

The COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program Round 2 is a one-off grant to help travel agents and tour arrangement service providers who have lost income because of the impacts of COVID-19.

It is to help travel agents and tour arrangement service providers to stay viable so they can keep paying refunds and credits to Australian consumers unable to travel because of COVID-19.

An applicant must read both the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program Guidelines for Round 1 and Round 2 on the GrantConnect website (grants.gov.au) before they make a claim.

Note: Round 1 of the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program started on 14 December 2020 and closed midnight 13 March 2021.

Is there a way to see the status of a claim?

You can see the status of the claim in the Work Items box in Process Direct.

Search for claims that have a status of submitted.

To view other claim status, change Status to:

  • Approved
  • Rejected
  • Under Assessment

Ask the applicant if they have a CRN, this would have been provided to them by:

  • Business Hub during the Round 1 application process
  • Letter (about the decision from Round 1)

How do I find a claim in Process Direct if I do not have the CRN?

You can search for organisation records using the Organisation Summary tile from the Home page in Process Direct.

  • Select Organisation Summary
  • Select Search For – Organisation
  • Select ID Type – ABN (Insert ABN in blank field)
  • Select Go (or hard enter)
  • Click anywhere on the Search Results bar
  • Select Transactions
  • Select Applications
  • Select Claim Number (in blue)

Who processes Round 2 claims?

It depends on the business’ eligible assessed turnover as decided in Round 1 of the Program:

  • less than $500,000 - Services Australia will make the assessment
  • $500,000 and above - Austrade will make the assessment. Services Australia will refer these claims to Austrade for assessment and will need to implement Austrade’s decision

How will I know if the claim has been sent to Austrade for assessment

The claim status will be changed from ‘submitted’ to ‘under assessment’.

Within the claim workflow, the radio button ‘email sent to Austrade’ will also be selected.

Applicant is requesting a timeframe for the claim to be finalised

It depends on the business’ eligible assessed turnover as decided in Round 1 of the Program:

  • less than $500,000 - Services Australia will endeavour to finalise claims within 4 weeks from date of receipt
  • $500,000 and above - Austrade will endeavour to assess applications within 8 weeks, once the outcome has been determined, Services Australia will endeavour to make a payment within 2 weeks

Note: if the business is waiting for an outcome of an appeal for Round 1 of the Program, their Round 2 application will not be assessed until the outcome has been decided.

What if an applicant is enquiring about Round 2 but I cannot see a claim for Round 1?

If you cannot find a record of a claim for Round 1 using both the CRN and Organisation Summary search options:

Tell the applicant that only businesses who lodged a claim, were eligible and received a payment under Round 1, will be eligible to test their eligibility under Round 2.

What do I tell an applicant if they are waiting for an appeal decision for Round 1?

Services Australia is working closely with Austrade to finalise all appeals.

Applicants can submit their application for Round 2 while the appeal is still being processed.

Note: their Round 2 application will not be assessed until the outcome of their Round 1 claim has been determined.

Will the applicant need to get another ATO Certificate or lodge a previous one?

No.

The ATO certificate issued was to support assessment against Round 1 eligibility criteria specifically.

The turnover used to decide the Round 1 payment will be the basis of any Round 2 payment.

If the business’ turnover was $500,000 or more a copy of the ATO Certificate lodged to support the Round 1 claim will be sent to Austrade to help them in their verification assessment of the Round 2 claim. Austrade may contact the applicant to request further documents.

Business’ that received an updated ATO certificate after they submitted their Round 1 claim will have an opportunity to provide this directly with Austrade as part of their verification assessment.

Does the applicant need to lodge supporting evidence as part of their claim application for Round 2?

No.

The online claim process will not ask the applicant to lodge any documents. They will answer a series of eligibility questions and make a self-declaration only.

Services Australia will send a copy of their Round 1 ATO certificate to Austrade when we refer the claim for Round 2 across.

Austrade may need further information to complete their additional financial verification. If they do, they will contact the applicant and tell them of what documents are needed and how to provide them.

What does the self-declaration contain?

The self-declaration will need the applicant to answer if the business:

  • was eligible for, and received a Round 1 payment;
  • Round 1 turnover figure was not based on TTV
  • is solvent and intends to continue to operate for the April to June 2021 quarter, and
  • will make best endeavours to retain its staff; and to meet the business’ obligations to process refunds and travel credits to Australian consumers

When policy refers to annual turnover, what is the period it is referring to?

The reference period for annual turnover in Round 2 is the same as Round 1:

  • the 2019 calendar year if they are a monthly or quarterly BAS reporter, or
  • the 2018-19 financial year if they are an annual BAS reporter

How can I tell if the business is assessed as an annual BAS lodger?

Round 1 ATO Certificates issued to this cohort will have different descriptions for both the BAS and turnover amount fields than all others, as follows:

  • 2018-19 income year business activity statement has been lodged
  • 2018-19 income year GST turnover (G1 on business activity statement – GST exclusive amount) as at 14 December 2020

Round 1 claims were processed as per any other claim. See the FAQs for Round 1 for more details.

What bank account will the payment be made to (if successful)?

Successful businesses will have their amount paid into the same Australian bank account (in the business name) that their Round 1 payment amount was made into.

They will be provided with an opportunity during the online application to reconfirm or change their bank account details.

The grant will be paid as a one-off lump sum payment only.

What should I do if the bank account name is not in the business name?

Services Australia does not have an automatic mechanism to verify if the bank account details is in the business’ name. As part of assessing the claim, a Service Officer will check:

  • BSB lookup to confirm Australian bank account
  • Check the name of the account declared by the applicant – this should be the business name associated with the ABN

For individual/sole traders, the name of the bank account may be either the applicant’s personal name or a business name (ABN lookup would confirm if business was an individual/sole trader).

For businesses, the name of the bank account should be declared as a business name. In instances where a business, other than individual/sole trader, has a personal name for the bank details, escalate the claim to the Level 2 Helpdesk for advice, using the payment query template.

Applicant has received a notification advising they have a letter however they are not able to view the letter.

The notification applicants receive, states ‘to view this letter, please go to the Services Australia website and log in to our business online services’.

Make sure applicants are logging into the Business Hub account (using their individual PRODA account) and not their Centrelink Business Online Services account.

Note: whilst most businesses will receive the decision letter in Business Hub, Individual/Sole Traders will not. For them, it will be available in their myGov account.

Do I contact an applicant with a decision after hours? For example, I am processing claims on the evening shift.

No.

Do not contact applicants to tell them of their claim decision when processing on the night shift.

In these cases, follow the agreed process to enable a Service Officer on day shift to contact the applicant with an unsuccessful claim outcome.

Why would I need to review a claim that is under $500,000 when payments are automated?

The automation of payment cannot occur where the applicant advises the business has:

  • an appeal outstanding for Round 1 (it will need staff to hold the claim pending outcome of review) and/or
  • changed their bank account information (it will need staff to check the bank account details)

I am trying to finalise a claim and have received this error message in Process Direct – ‘Application not finalised. Call to ISIS Payment Interface failed. Please try again later.’

Escalate any instances of this error message to Level 2 Helpdesk for further investigation, using the payment query template. Include details of:

  • Business name and CRN
  • Name and logon of staff member who received message
  • Include a screenshot of the error message

What if the applicant states the business was rejected under Round 1 due to turnover amount >$20 million

Businesses in Round 1 who did not receive a payment because it was assessed on a TTV of above $20 million, but with a GST turnover of below $20 million, will have the opportunity to be considered for a payment under Round 2 by an exceptional circumstances request.

Tell the applicant to send an exceptional circumstance request to Austrade by emailing Consumer Travel Support.

What if the applicant states the business was granted under Round 1 using a turnover amount that was based in any part on Total Transaction Value (TTV)?

They will not be able to lodge a claim for Round 2.

If they believe their business received a payment based on TTV in which was less than they would otherwise have received if the payments for Round 1 and 2 were calculated on turnover, they can make a request for Austrade to consider their case under exceptional circumstances.

Tell the applicant to send an exceptional circumstance request to Austrade by emailing Consumer Travel Support.

What if the applicant disagrees with the decision?

The first step is explain the decision to the applicant.

If the applicant disagrees with the decision, they can ask for the decision to be reviewed. Decisions will be reviewed based on the following breakdown:

  • For businesses with an eligible assessed turnover decided under Round 1 totalling:
    • Less than $500,000 – they can request that a Services Australia Subject Matter Expert (SME) review the claim decision
    • From $500,000 and above - they can request that an Austrade Subject Matter Expert (SME) review the claim decision
  • Applicants also have access to an Exceptional Circumstances Review by Austrade. Austrade may waive one or more of the eligibility criteria in exceptional circumstances. Applicants wishing to have their case considered under exceptional circumstances should email Consumer Travel Support

Note: requests for turnover to be assessed using TTV over another reporting method in order to receive a higher grant payment:

  • will not be considered, and
  • will not be considered as an exceptional circumstance

What if the applicant misses the deadline to lodge a claim?

All claims must be submitted in full before the Round 2 closing date and time:

11:59 (AEDT) on 12 June 2021

No late lodgement will be accepted for Round 2.

What should I do if I identify there is more than 1 claim made for an ABN?

Escalate immediately to Level 2 Helpdesk for advice, using the payment query template.

Does the same applicant who lodged the Round 1 claim have to lodge a claim for Round 2?

No.

The same applicant can lodge both Round 1 and Round 2 but in those cases where the business circumstances have changed, another applicant is able to lodge a Round 2 application.

If they do not already have a PRODA account and are linked to the Business’ PRODA account, they will need to complete those steps before they can start the application process.
