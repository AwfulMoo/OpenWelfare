3

Customers experiencing a vulnerable circumstance + Read more ...

A customer is considered to be experiencing a vulnerable circumstance if, within the 8 weeks immediately before they contacted to enquire about making a claim, they are or have been:

  • subject to family or domestic violence
  • homeless
  • hospitalised or suffering from a temporary incapacity arising from a medical condition
  • released from prison or psychiatric confinement
  • experiencing unusually high stress associated with a relationship separation
  • a new parent of a child born
  • affected by the death of an immediate family member
  • a humanitarian entrant to Australia
  • usual place of residence is in a remote area and the customer is physically present in a remote area
  • a person whose principal place of residence was lost or sustained major damage as a result of an extreme circumstance, or
  • subject to other special circumstances beyond the person's control. See Table 1 on the Resources page for a complete list of pre-claim vulnerable circumstances

The Resources page has examples of vulnerable circumstances.

Note: where a Child Care Additional Subsidy (CCAS) flag of ACCS (Child Wellbeing) is present on the customer's record (or has been within the previous eight weeks), a pre claim vulnerable circumstance of Domestic Violence is automatically applied. No other CCAS flag type will be recognised as a pre-claim vulnerable circumstance.

Did the customer experience a vulnerable circumstance within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, record a DOC and tell the customer or third party the claim must be submitted as soon as possible to be paid from the earliest date. Procedure ends here

Recording the initial contact + Read more ...

Existing pre-claim vulnerable circumstances are highlighted:

  • on the Pre-Claim Circumstance section of the Claim Summary screen
  • as warning banners in Process Direct
  • on the Pre-Claim Circumstance tab of the CRN/BP screen, and
  • as a warning message on the SOA screen in Customer First

6

Subsequent contacts to support a claim for a vulnerable customer + Read more ...

If a customer cannot submit their claim for a payment or concession card within 14 days of their first contact, they may be eligible for an extension of time to submit. A customer may be granted an extension of up to 13 weeks to submit their claim from the date of the first contact for a medical condition or due to special circumstances.

Extensions are granted based on the customer's individual circumstances.

Medical condition extensions must be supported by a medical certificate. The customer must submit a medical certificate as evidence to support the extension when submitting their claim. The medical certificate is to confirm that the customer, or their partner, or the person for whom they provide care, had a medical condition which:

  • was continuous between the day of the contact and the day the claim is submitted, and
  • had a significant adverse effect on the ability to submit the claim within 14 days after the contact date

Special circumstances can include, but are not limited to:

  • A customer having practical difficulties in meeting the 14 day time frame as a result of living in a remote area, or
  • A customer residing in a country that Australia has a social security agreement with, and there are reasons why the customer could not submit within the 14 day period, or
  • An emergency interfering with a customer's plans to submit within the 14 day period, such as a natural disaster

When an extension is granted:

  • Tell the customer:
    • what evidence will need to be provided with their claim to support a medical condition extension
    • they must submit their claim by the end of the extension period, to be assessed from the first date of contact
  • Record the contact with the customer by creating a closed DOC using the Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Generic > Claims > ITC vulnerable customer extension:
    • Select (tick) Display on access box
    • Select Confirm
    • DOA information will present for example: Reason SCC – Special Contact Considerations and Expiry Date equal to 182 days from date of DOC
    • Select Confirm
    • Edit auto text. Note: when recording the vulnerable circumstance do not include details of a sensitive or personal nature, including medical abbreviations. See Online Document Recording (ODR)
    • Select Continue
  • Expire any previous current DOA DOCs on the customer record relating to this intended claim

When an extension is not granted, record the reason(s) in a DOC and tell the customer they must submit their claim as soon as possible to be paid from the earliest date.

Was the customer qualified at the date of first contact + Read more ...

Where the customer was qualified on the contact date and submitted the claim within 14 days of that contact, or an extension has been or may be granted, the customer's deemed date of claim is the date of that contact.

Where the system is unable to determine the date of the date of circumstance, it will default to the contact date, including the submission of a claim. Service Officers can manually enter a line on the PRECLM screen with the correct details.

The customer must also have experienced a vulnerable circumstance at any time within the 8 week period leading up to and including the contact date.

6

Claims submitted outside of 14 days of the first customer contact + Read more ...

A customer may be granted an extension of up to 13 weeks to submit their claim from their contact date, if:

  • the customer, their partner or a person whom they care for suffered from a continuous medical condition and this medical condition adversely affected the customer's ability to submit a claim within 14 days (and sufficient evidence has been provided), or
  • the customer was granted an extension to submit their claim due to special circumstances that made it unreasonable for them to submit their claim

An extension due to a medical condition of the customer, their partner or the person they are caring for does not apply to Crisis Payment. However, an extension due to special circumstances can apply to Crisis Payment.

Is there evidence on the customer's record (DOC, started claim activity or uploaded documents) that indicates either a medical condition or a special extension date has been or may be granted?

  • Yes,
    • go to Step 7 if the contact date was the first contact
    • go to Step 8 if the contact date was subsequent to the first contact
  • No, go to Step 9

7

Date of first contact is the date of claim + Read more ...

The DDOC is the date of the first contact recorded on the customer's record on the CCD screen or within the DOC or date the claim was started or documents were uploaded.

Consider if backdated start date rules apply. See Start Day.

Procedure ends here.

8

Date of claim is the earliest subsequent contact date on which the customer was qualified + Read more ...

The DDOC is the date of the earliest subsequent contact date that the customer is qualified on, provided the claim was submitted within 14 days (or up to 13 weeks later, where approved). Make sure the customer experienced a vulnerable circumstance at any time within the 8 week period leading up to and including this contact.

If the customer was released from prison and there is a DOC advising of their pending release date and the customer claims within 14 days of their release date, their claim for JSP or YA (job seeker) can be backdated to their release date under Backdating provisions for claim lodgement.

Once the deemed date of claim is established, apply backdated start date rules where applicable.

Adjust the assessment date or start date where applicable to make sure the correct start date is applied.

Procedure ends here.

9

Vulnerable circumstance no longer applies + Read more ...

If the customer has a pre-claim circumstance displayed on PRECLM with a start date that is more than 8 weeks ago, consider if that pre-claim vulnerable circumstance still exists using available information.

Where the vulnerable circumstance no longer applies, and the vulnerable circumstance is more than 8 weeks in the past the system should no longer recognise the dates on the PRECLM or !CUPC screens. The PRECLM or !CUPC screens are read only. Service Officers can manually change the claim start date on the NDC screen if required. DOC the record accordingly.

Some circumstances can remain current on a customer’s record indefinitely, such as an exemption from seeking child support due to family and domestic violence. In these cases, do not end date the exemption. Manually update the claim start date to the date of submission if required.

10

Intention to claim rules do not apply + Read more ...

The claim is considered to be made on the date it was submitted, unless other deemed start date claim provisions apply.

Before considering if this is an early claim, consider first if the customer is qualified for another payment. See Incorrect claims and Inappropriate claims.

Backdated start date provisions may also apply. See Start Day.

If these do not apply, see Early claims.

Procedure ends here.
