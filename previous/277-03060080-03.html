Manage issues after the care determination has been made

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Manage issues + Read more ...

After the care decision has been finalised, review the customers' relevant child support and family assistance assessments to identify if the change in care has caused any other impacts that need to be addressed.

2

Check data transfer for mutual customers + Read more ...

For mutual customers, data exchange will occur overnight to transfer the information to the customer’s relevant assessments. Check the case the day after the care determination has been processed to ensure the change in care data has been exchanged and reflects correctly on the customers’ relevant assessments.

For care determinations that were finalised in Cuba, access the record of each customer who is receiving family assistance payments for the child and check:

  • the care details have populated on the customer’s record, and
  • the event date is the same

For care determinations that were finalised in CF, open the Care and External Information Care windows in Cuba for each customer and check:

  • reference numbers
  • child’s name and date of birth
  • child’s gender
  • date of notification
  • date of event
  • type of care
  • percentage of care for the mutual customer
  • additional carers indicator (if applicable)
  • disputed care indicator (if applicable)
  • objection/review indicator (if applicable)
  • relationship of the customer

If the data has transferred correctly, go to Step 5.

If the data exchange has failed, go to Step 4.

3

Data exchange issues - Centrelink care intrays in Cuba + Read more ...

In the event Centrelink care-related information for a mutual customer does not transfer automatically to the customer’s Cuba record, a care intray will generate indicating further action is required, see Care intrays Cuba Process Help.

If a care intray appears on the customer’s Cuba record, action the care intray. Otherwise go to Step 5.

4

Data exchange issues -Alignment of Care Manual Follow Up (MFU) activities in CF + Read more ...

For CF, an MFU activity will be generated on the customer’s record indicating that child related information from Cuba has not transferred correctly and further action is required. Action any MFU activities that are created on the customer’s record, see Alignment of Care Manual Follow-up (MFU) activities.

Go to Step 5.

5

Identify impact on relevant assessments + Read more ...

Review and collate the effects that the change in care has had on the customers’ current assessments.

For child support customers, check for:

  • a payee overpayment, see Overpayments and payee debt (CS)
  • a retrospective liability increase for the payer, see Debt repayment (CS)
  • changes to prescribed NAPs, see Non Agency Payments (NAPs) (CS) , Prescribed NAPs
  • the customers’ roles changing, see Reverse child support cases

For Centrelink customers check for:

  • an overpayment
  • a rate reduction of FTB or other Centrelink payment
  • a cancellation of FTB or other Centrelink payment
  • an arrears payment

6

Contact customers + Read more ...

Contact each customer the day after the decision has been made to:

  • explain the decision
  • discuss the identified impacts of the care change
  • advise the review and appeal rights or objection rights

7

Impact Assessment Tool + Read more ...

The Impact Assessment Tool must be used by Integrated Care (IC) Service Officers that have not yet demonstrated a strong understanding of cross program issues.

Open the Impact Assessment Tool macro to identify associated customer entitlements or benefits such as:

  • Family Assistance
  • Income Support payments
  • Childcare
  • Medicare basics
  • Child Support
  • relevant referrals to community or government support services

Document all recommendations from the Impact Assessment Tool.

For Cuba:

  • detail the customers that the Impact Assessment Tool has been completed for and note that the details of recommendations can be found in the individual’s communication notepad, and
  • use the communication notepad to provide details of the Impact Assessment Tool outcomes

For CF, use the Macro File Note to detail the Impact Assessment Tool outcomes.

See Documenting Child Support information.

Step

Action

1

Customer responds to notification of care determination + Read more ...

If a customer contacts the agency about:

  • the care determination and is dissatisfied with it, or believes the agency has made a mistake or has applied the law incorrectly, go to Step 2
  • a review of a care determination they have lodged, go to Step 3

2

Customer responds to care determination + Read more ...

If a customer contacts the agency in response to the care determination and is either dissatisfied with the care determination or believes the agency has made a mistake or applied the law incorrectly, identify the reasons and try to resolve the issue by explaining how the decision was made.

Note: if one party involved in the care decision is a mutual customer, a review of the decision can be requested through Child Support Objections or Centrelink review and appeal process. If the party requesting the review is a non-Centrelink customer, and the care decision has no effect under the Family Assistance law; Child Support must review the decision. See Internal review process for care decisions for procedures that need to be followed.

Depending on the specific issues, see customer referral guidelines for Child Support staff for appropriate options for the customer including:

  • a referral to an external service provider
  • a transfer to Child Support Operations business area
  • a transfer to Centrelink
  • escalation through the Complaints process
  • lodgement of an objection of a Child Support care decision, or application for a forma; review of a Centrelink care decision

If the customer advises they want to pursue an objection or apply for a formal review of the care decision, they can do so verbally with either Child Support or Centrelink. If they choose:

  • Centrelink, record the application for a formal review
  • Child Support, transfer the customer to the Objections team, see Customer referral guidelines for Child Support staff, Objections

If there has been another care change since the care determination was made, treat this as a new change in care notification and go to Integrated care - Notification and recording of change in care.

If the customer states that the care percentage recorded on their assessment is different to the care determination letter, this may indicate a keying error has occurred:

  • for Child Support seek technical support to consider correcting the error to amend the care percentage, see Technical support in Child Support and Correcting errors on Child Support cases
  • for Centrelink, if the percentage on FSC screen is different to that stated on the decision letter, check the decision document:
    • if the customer has received a FTB rate letter which states a different percentage to that which is on the FSC screen, check the AX screen, the customer’s percentage may be different to that which is recorded. The customer’s actual percentage is different from the payment percentage. For example, the actual care percentage is 35% so the customer is entitled to 25% FTB. See Assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • if there appears to be an error in the keying, correct the error

Document the discussion in the Care window notepad in Cuba or CF, using the appropriate file note macro, see Documenting Child Support information.

3

Customer has lodged an application for a formal review with Centrelink + Read more ...

If a customer contacts the agency about a review, check that the Appeal indicator has a status of ‘Y’ and that a review is in progress:

  • for Cuba, view this in the External Information Care window
  • for Centrelink, view this on the COAD screen. When the application is recorded, a document will be updated to reflect the reason for the review with reference to the original decision

Open the Objections window in Cuba to check if there is a Child Support objection in progress. If there is no objection in progress, the review is being managed by Centrelink.

Note: if the Appeal Related indicator has a status of Y and the customer states that they have not lodged a review, the other party may have initiated this. For reviews lodged with:

  • Child Support, rransfer the customer to the appropriate team to discuss the review in progress
  • Centrelink, advise the customer an Authorised Review Officer will be in contact to discuss the review in progress

Centrelink will make a formal decision for FTB customers only. Determine whether the customer is currently a mutual customer to help manage the customer’s options.

Document the discussion in the Care window notepad in Cuba or use a file note macro in CF.
