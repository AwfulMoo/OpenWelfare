Investigating debts

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Identify the debt + Read more ...

Question the suspected debt. Consider:

  • What action created the debt?
  • Is the debt recoverable and cost effective?

Determine if a debt has occurred. Consider:

  • the legislative basis for the debt
  • if there is sufficient evidence to confirm the debt

DOCument the investigation, even if the debt is not identified yet. Include details such as:

  • evidence about the debt
  • the location of evidence
  • the review on which the debt is based (if applicable)

Do not use abbreviations or jargon.

Good documentation helps pursue recovery and to obtain evidence in future if required. See Online Document Recording (ODR) and Creating, reviewing and deleting documents (including Fast Notes and DOA DOCs) for DOC minimum standards.

2

Available evidence and obtaining further information + Read more ...

See Table 2 in Safeguarding customer privacy when raising and recovering debts, before issuing any debt notices. Take the appropriate actions to protect the customer's privacy.

Before calculating the correct customer entitlement, further evidence may be required to substantiate a debt. Do not request further information unless it is necessary to accurately calculate the customers' entitlement. See Acceptable evidence for verifying income when investigating debts and Table 3.

Evidence is not limited to documentation scanned to the customer's record. Staff must use all sources of evidence when raising/verifying a debt, and consider all details available on a customer's record as correct. Staff can only consider details inaccurate or incorrect if there is evidence that indicates the update is wrong.

When requesting information from customers or third parties, make a reasonable effort to contact the customer. If unsuccessful, use the Request for Information guided procedure or standard Online Advices (OLA).

For a notice to be valid it must be in writing and include:

  • details of the information/action required
  • the time allowed to respond. For example at least 14 days after the deemed receipt
  • the consequences of non-compliance
  • the reference to the relevant Act that the request has been made under

Scan any paper evidence to the record. This establishes an audit trail in an electronic format.

The Resources page contains lists of suitable letters.

3

Check and validate the debt + Read more ...

The minimum standard is to be able to reconstruct the debt.

When raising a debt using ADEX, ensure that the system debt amount is correct. This includes checking:

  • if the debt is in a category for which a system debt calculation is not appropriate
  • the Payment Summary (PS) screen for one-off payments
  • ADEX for any related activities and related debts

If the original decision cannot be validated, the debt should be finalised ('no debt').

4

Online Advice (OLA) + Read more ...

See Table 2 in Safeguarding customer privacy when raising and recovering debts, before issuing any debt notices. Take the appropriate actions to protect the customer's privacy.

To send an OLA to the customer/third party:

  • go to the Letter Display (LD) screen
  • code the required letter code in the Letter Code: field, press [Enter]
  • code 'DMN' in the Please enter the required Service Reason: field, press [Enter] and follow the prompts in the letter

Record details on a DOC and resubmit DOC/activity or a manual review to follow up the return of the notice.

There is a guided procedure in Customer First to help send and record information.

5

Work Bonus + Read more ...

The Work Bonus is a discount available to the assessment of employment income earned by a pensioner of Age Pension age except for Parenting Payment Single customers.

The Work Bonus is applicable to the following payments if the customer is Age Pension age:

  • Age Pension
  • Carer Payment
  • Disability Support Pension

It is also available to Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) pensioners who are of Service Pension age and in receipt of:

  • Income Support Supplement (ISS)
  • Service Pension (DVA)
  • Age Pension (AGC)

While a customer is paid under the transitional rules they are not entitled to the Work Bonus, however the Work Bonus will still be used in the comparison calculations and partners may still benefit by the reduction in total income. For more information see:

  • Work Bonus and balance for pensioners of Age Pension age
  • Transitional rules for pension customers who were on payment at 19 September 2009
  • Pension income and assets tests

6

Pensioner Earnings Credit Scheme (historical information 1990 - 1997) + Read more ...

The Pensioner Earnings Credit Scheme is no longer current and was abolished on 20 March 1997. Earnings Credits accrued prior to this date must be included in debt calculations if the customer was entitled to them. For the Pensioner Earnings Credit Scheme:

  • MultiCal is capable of taking into account Earnings Credit from January 1993 onwards for both Pensions and Newstart
  • The Earnings Credit history for NSA customers is usually available via the USB Earnings & Casual Income Details (UEC) screen and the 'Income History' report in the Archive System (CAR). This is not the case for Pension customers because the Earnings Credit information was not transferred across with the introduction of the Pensions Common Platform

For Rates of entitlement and examples of calculations see National Instructions, 1988 - 1995, The Earnings Credit Scheme for Job Search, Newstart and Sickness Allowance. The Resources page contains a link to the National Instructions.

For Rates of entitlement and examples of calculations see National Instructions, 1988 - 1995, The Earnings Credit Scheme for Pensions. The Resources page contains a link to the National Instructions.

Note: apply the 'best case scenario' to debt calculations if there is insufficient information (and no way to obtain the information required) to properly assess the Earnings Credit entitlement.
