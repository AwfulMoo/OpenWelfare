Create a new claim + Read more ...

  • Allied Health Care Claim Details, or

  • Select Update Claim Now
  • Select Pay Claim

This will generate a payment advice

If a processing error is made, refer to a Team Leader who can reverse the claim status using their supervisor access and the Updating Status in SAP Processing System. Once the claim is reversed, search for the claim from the home page by selecting List and continue processing.

  • details of each injury and/or condition sustained as a direct result of the event and requires ongoing treatment, therapy and/or products
  • a full list of recommended treatments, therapies and/or products over the next 12 months including:

  • confirmation that the treatment, therapies and/or products recommended are directly related to care for the injury(ies) and/or condition(s) arising from the adverse event
  • referrals to other health professionals, specialists or suppliers
  • any other information they consider relevant

Assess and process a travel and accommodation claim

Table 4: how processing staff assess and process a new claim for travel and/or accommodation.

Step

Action

1

Assess travel claim is related to treatment + Read more ...

It must be confirmed that travel costs are directly related to the claimant attending an appointment with the treating health care provider. This can be confirmed by checking:

  • that the claim documentation submitted at the same time as the Disaster Health Care Assistance Scheme Motor Vehicle and/or Accommodation Out-of-Pocket Travel Application form has occurred on the same date, or within a day of the appointment with the health professional
  • that the treating health professional has made a notation on their receipt that the goods and/or services provided are related to the adverse event

If the date(s) for the goods/services being claimed on the Disaster Health Care Assistance Scheme claim form and the above Application form do not correlate, return all documentation to the customer with a DHCAS claim needs more information letter requesting the claimant verify the dates.

Accommodation can only be claimed when the claimant lives in a different town or region, 50km+ from the health provider. Accommodation required for more than 10 consecutive days must be pre-approved.

2

Process a travel claim form for DHCAS + Read more ...

Complete the DHCAS checklist.

Costs incurred for travel using a private vehicle will be paid at the same rate as an employee of Services Australia. Air travel costs are to be paid at economy rates.

  • Does the claim form contain the following information?
    • customer details
    • mode of transportation details
    • journey details (from start to finish)
    • receipts for travel if transport is by air, rail, taxi or public transport
    • health care provider name and full address
  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, if the claimant has not supplied a completed Disaster Health Care Assistance Scheme Motor Vehicle and/or Accommodation Out-of-Pocket Application form:
    • complete the DHCAS claim needs more information letter, attaching the original claim to the letter by merging the files
    • send the letter to the claimant
    • add a notation on the claim outlining the steps taken to request the completed Disaster Health Care Assistance Scheme Motor Vehicle and/or Accommodation Out-of-Pocket Application form
    • pend the claim awaiting a response

3

Calculate travel distance and amount payable for transport by a private motor vehicle + Read more ...

In calculating the total distance travelled by the claimant, a specific calculation must be used incorporating the standard corporate rate per kilometre.

For example:

  • claimant total distance travelled is 50km (25km each way)
  • the corporate rate per km is 0.62c

The calculation will be:

  • 50km x 0.62c per km = $31.00

The kilometres must be confirmed as being correct before the claim can be paid.

To do this:

  • go to online maps
  • type in the address of the claimant
  • type in the address of the health care provider
  • select Get Directions
  • directions page will display
  • the directions page will calculate the fastest route that can be taken from the claimant’s residential address to the health care provider’s address
  • the online map gives the most direct route with the amount of kilometres it takes to get there

If the claimant has provided a different distance to the one in the online map, use shorter distance.

For example, the claimant is claiming a distance of 100 kilometres, however the online map states it is 75 kilometres round trip from the claimant’s residential address to their health care provider’s location and return to their residential address. In this circumstance always use the shorter distance.

Do not calculate any distances of travel that the claimant has not supplied on the claim form.

Payments will not be paid for non-related detours made whilst travelling.

Amounts payable for air, taxi or public transport with a fixed out-of-pocket amount are processed based on the invoice provided. Distances and allowances do not need to be calculated. Air travel costs are to be paid at economy rates.

4

Calculate the amount of reimbursement for air travel if the airline ticket is for First Class or Business class + Read more ...

  • Go to the website for which the airline ticket was purchased, for example Qantas, Virgin
  • Select the same day of the week and time that the airline ticket was purchased for, for example Tuesday 6:30am Sydney to Brisbane
  • Select the same day of the week and time for the return travel. These prices need to be added together and averaged to get the amount of reimbursement. For example, claimant travels with Qantas from Sydney to Brisbane and back to see a medical specialist. They depart on a Tuesday at 6:30am and return on the same day at 5:30pm. The example below shows the pricing for travel on a Tuesday from Sydney to Brisbane and return travel on the same day over 4 weeks:
    • 6:30 am Sydney to Brisbane - $170.00
    • 5:30 pm Brisbane to Sydney - $170.00
    • 6:30 am Sydney to Brisbane - $135.00
    • 5:30 pm Brisbane to Sydney - $170.00
    • 6:30 am Sydney to Brisbane - $135.00
    • 5:30 pm Brisbane to Sydney - $170.00
    • 6:30 am Sydney to Brisbane - $170.00
    • 5:30 pm Brisbane to Sydney - $170.00
    • Total $1290.00 ÷ 4 = $322.50

$322.50 is used as the calculated average amount to reimburse an economy class air fare for the trip (to and from the appointment).

The calculation has been done this way to work out as accurately as possible the reimbursement as airline prices for previous travel dates can’t be looked up.

5

Process a travel accommodation claim + Read more ...

Complete the DHCAS checklist.

The checklist must include the travel calculation for out-of-pocket costs. The claim must be processed under the Allied Health Care Claims Details tab in the Disaster Health Care Assistance processing system.

For Accommodation claims, check that the claim is for room accommodation only. Accommodation costs claimable do not include costs for food or beverages (including mini bar).

If all the documentation submitted is correct, follow standard DHCAS processing requirements, see Table 1.

Assess and process a mental health care claim

Table 5: how processing staff assess a mental health care claim, add/update the DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions List and request a Mental Health Care Report.

Step

Action

1

Assess a mental health care claim + Read more ...

Search the claimant’s file for the claimant’s DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions List

The DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions List (was called ‘counselling sessions list’) is used to record the details for each mental health care session claimed. This will help identify the need for a Mental Health Care Report.

Does the claimant have a Mental Health Care Sessions List in their file?

  • Yes, open the DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions List - confirm how many sessions have already been claimed and the date of service of their last session
    • Within 12 months: if the date of their last session was within the past 12 months and there are still sessions that can be claimed, proceed with processing the claim and updating the DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions list
    • Over 12 months: if the date of their last session was over 12 months ago, refer claim to Team Leader to escalate to the Programme Management team for review and assessment. This is because the new sessions may not be related to a condition that has arisen as a direct result of the relevant adverse event
      If a customer has been approved for 3-4 sessions per year, then the provider reminder letter will not be generated within the 12 month period. Letter will be generated after the 10th or 12th session
    • If there are no mental health care sessions left, go to Step 3
      The number of mental health care sessions provided before a report is requested does not change should the claimant change providers. For example, if the claimant changes providers after 5 sessions their first session with the new provider is counted as session 6. Then once the total number of sessions reaches the required number (10 or 12), a Mental Health Care report must be requested from the current provider.
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Add a new DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions List to a claimant’s file + Read more ...

If a claimant is claiming counselling, psychological or psychiatric sessions for the first time a new DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions List must be added to their file. Due to the length of time since the dates of the DHCAS events, refer the claim to a team leader first as this may need to be escalated to the Programme Management team for review and assessment.

Example of a first time claimant: a young child whose parent(s) were killed in a DHCAS adverse event. The young child is now a young adult and requires counselling sessions to deal with grief. A new claimant will need to provide a referral from their general practitioner (GP) for counselling services.

A current letter from an on-going claimant’s Mental Health Professional must be attached to the claimant’s file. The Mental Health Professional letter must be current within the last 12 months.

If the claim is approved to go ahead:

  • open a new DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions List template
  • save the template to the claimant’s file
  • enter all the required information asked for on the DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions List
  • save the list
  • continue with processing the claim, see Step 5 in Table 1

3

Request a DHCAS Mental Health Care Report + Read more ...

When a claimant has reached their maximum approved sessions with a mental health care professional a Mental Health Care Report is required:

  • complete and send the following letters:
    • Counselling report- patient notification DHCAS We are requesting a report from your Medical Specialist letter
    • Counselling report – provider DHCAS Request for a Patient counselling report letter
  • add a note to the DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions list in the claimant’s file highlighting the date that the report has been requested
  • save a copy of each letter to the claimant’s file
  • pend the claim awaiting a response in PaNDA

The DHCAS Mental Health Care Report provided needs to include the below information so that a claim can be assessed:

  • confirmation that the counselling, psychological or psychiatric sessions are related to the care of a condition arising directly from the adverse event
  • a statement of the services provided to date, for example individual, group
  • issues being addressed, for example Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD), depression, specific phobia
  • an outline of existing treatment/care plan and its objectives or milestones
  • progress to date (including number and types of sessions)
  • likely future progress and an estimate of how many additional sessions may be needed and
  • any further information that is considered relevant

Note: a maximum of 12 sessions can be approved before a further report is required.

4

Update the DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions list + Read more ...

On receipt of the Mental Health Care Report from the Mental Health Professional, forward the report via email to National Office Programme Management using the email classification: SENSITIVE/PERSONAL

The Programme Management team will either make a determination or escalate to the Health Professional Advisory Unit (HPAU) for assessment.

  • pend the report awaiting a response from Programme Management’
  • update the DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions list in the claimant’s file to detail that the report from the Mental Health Professional has been received and forwarded to the Programme Management team
  • Programme Management will review the mental health care report

A response will be received from the Programme Management team once the request has been considered against the information provided. The response will detail if the claimant has been ‘Approved’ or ‘Rejected’ for further claims.

Has the request for further sessions been approved or rejected?

Approved

  • Update the claimant’s DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions list to reflect the approval of further sessions
  • Merge a copy of the pended Mental Health Care Report response from the Mental Health Professional and the Programme Management Team’s/HPAU recommendations
  • Move the file to the claimant’s file
  • Search for any other mental health claims for the claimant that have been ‘Pended’, the claim(s) can continue to be processed
  • Open the claim in PaNDA
  • Update the claimant’s DHCAS Mental Health Care Sessions list to reflect the services that are now payable from the claim
  • Continue with processing the claim, see Step 5 in Table 1

Rejected

  • Programme Management will send a letter to the claimant outlining the reasons why the claim was rejected
  • Programme Management will cc the DHCAS email inbox with the rejected response and include a copy of the reject letter sent to the claimant

4

Delete a claim - Team Leaders only + Read more ...

Deleting a claim will remove it from the claimant’s records.

A claim can only be deleted when the status is Unpaid. If a claim has a status of Wait, go to Step 3 to change to Unpaid.

  • highlight the claim and select Delete
  • return message Claim has been deleted will confirm update
  • claim is returned to Service Officer for processing

5

Finalise a claim + Read more ...

All claims processed through the DHCAS system are considered to be in Wait until a Team Leader actions the release of the claim.

The release of a payment does not result in the forwarding of a benefit to the claimant. This is done through the Payment Run process actioned through Finance. Release for Payment in the DHCAS system is for updating the status of the claim only to identify that the claim has been processed and approved for payment. This also provides an audit trail for the program.

  • highlight the claim and select Release for Payment
  • return message Claim has been set to status of Paid will confirm update
