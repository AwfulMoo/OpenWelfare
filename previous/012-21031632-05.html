AHHCCS overview

Category

Description

Eligibility

(members)

A claim may be eligible if it is made against a person who is 1 of the following:

  • an allied health professional
  • a midwife engaged as an employee to practise as a midwife

They must be:

  • Registered with the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA)

Indemnified under an insurance policy with an eligible Medical Indemnity Insurer (MII) or Medical Defence Organisation (MDO)

Eligibility

(Applicant)

All participating MIIs and MDOs are eligible

Notification Date

The Date of Notification (DoN) of the incident/claim must be on or after 1 July 2020.

Criteria
  • The Date of Incident (DoI), or series of incidents, must have occurred in the course of, or in connection with:
    • practice by the practitioner of their allied health profession
    • practice by the midwife engaged as an employee
    • that occurred on or after 1 July 2020

(incidents that occurred before I July 2020 may be eligible under the HCCS)

  • the incident must have occurred within Australia or one of its external territories
  • the applicant (insurer) must have made a payment, or be liable to pay a payment in relation to the claim
  • the payment must be made in line with the insurer’s indemnity policy issued and within the ordinary course of business
  • the insurer must not have been notified of the occurrence of the incident, nor claim against or by the person in relation to the incident before 1 July 2020

the claim amount must exceed the threshold of $500,000.00

What is not payable?

Applicants cannot claim an amount if:

  • the incident occurred before 1 July 2020

  • another party has responsibility to pay it
  • it is an insurer to insurer payment
  • the cost was not incurred in relation to the incident
  • the settlement or judgment amounts are still within the appeal period or have been appealed or stayed
  • it’s a GST components of a defence costs that is not exempt
  • it relates to incidents that occur partly or fully within the course of treating a public patient in a public hospital. If it occurs fully, the application is rejected, If partly, the total application payment must be reduced proportionately
  • the incident/s occurred outside of Australia and external territories

Clarification

An allied health professional is a person who practices an allied health profession, other than the medical profession – see the AHHCCS Speciality Codes table’.
