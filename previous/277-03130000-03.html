Initial investigation

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Case allocated and identify if there are FDV concerns + Read more ...

When the CTP team leader allocates a case, the Financial Investigator reviews the case to determine if family and domestic (FDV) concerns have been raised. Check for a Sensitive Issues indicator and the referral notepad.

Have FDV concerns been raised?

  • Yes,
    • lock only the parent disclosing FDV concerns
    • contact must be made with the parent disclosing FDV concerns to explain the CTP process before any further review is conducted

  • No, go to step 2

2

Initial investigation – lock customers and conduct searches + Read more ...

If the CTP review is to continue the Financial investigator will:

  • lock both customers immediately in accordance with the Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support and
  • begin the preliminary assessment of the customer’s financial circumstances

Conduct a brief analysis of both customers’ financial circumstances. This includes checking:

Case details:

  • notes and communication records
  • correspondence received
  • income details
  • current assessment details
  • details recorded in the External Information window, and
  • previous decisions made in relation to:
    • change of assessment
    • objections, see Objections Cuba process help and
    • Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT)

See the External searches guide for information about:

  • general ATO searches. Note: an electronic ATO legal notice must be issued before conducting any ATO searches, see Legal notice for Child Support access to ATO data and systems. See:
    • customer and related business entities’ tax lodgements
    • any link the customer has to business entities
    • tax file number (TFN) declarations, and
    • Centrelink benefits
  • specialised searches are not required at this stage, unless:
    • other Cuba/ATO searches indicate there may be important information that is only available through a specialised search, and
    • this information is required when speaking to the customers

Suspicious Matters Report (SMR) from AUSTRAC

SMRs from AUSTRAC must never be disclosed. This means they must not be included when making a submission, they cannot be disclosed to any court or tribunal and are not admissible as evidence.

Review AUSTRAC search results to ensure they do not contain an SMR or Suspect Transaction Report (SUSTR), these must not be recorded in the system. If a record needs to be removed, take immediate action by sending an email the National Intensive Collection Services.

Full details regarding AUSTRAC disclosure of information can be found on the Resources page.

Upload external search results

Use the Document Upload Request macro to upload external search results into Cuba.

Add a note in the Payer Collection/Capacity to Pay window indicating the search has been conducted and is available in View Correspondence. Not all documents and searches are able to be stored in CUBA. These are saved as a .pdf document in an electronic file in the CTP folder in the NTD drive under the relevant year. See COA Electronic File Management for more detail on the naming convention and types of searches to save, a link is located on the Resources page.

Consider administrative remedy + Read more ...

Based on the circumstances of the case, consider if there is an administrative remedy available and be sure to include the identified options in the customer discussions.

Note: if the administrative remedy results in an assessment that accurately reflects the customers’ circumstances the CTP investigation can be finalised.

CTP timeframes and correspondence

Is the customer incarcerated?

  • Yes, advise the customer:
    • the agency has information that the assessment is inaccurate and the payer is no longer/was not earning an income
    • if the customer has no information to indicate financial resources or higher capacity a Change of Assessment application will be made by the Registrar to reduce the assessment
    • they will receive a letter summarising the details used in the recommendation and a right of response form
  • No, advise the customer:
    • they will receive a CTP introductory letter and the Statement of financial details form
    • they are required to return the Statement of financial details form and any supporting evidence within 14 days
    • you will contact them in 7 days to confirm they have received the Statement of financial details form and that they will be providing a response
    • the CTP process can take approximately 79 days

Possible CTP outcomes

Is the customer incarcerated?

  • Yes, discuss potential CTP outcomes, including:
    • referring the case to Change of Assessment
    • if the customer enquiries, discuss joint election to cease the review and/or child support agreements. These outcome will not be actively pursued for this customer type
  • No, discuss potential CTP outcomes including:
  • referring the case to Change of Assessment
  • administrative remedies, such as child support agreement, income correction or ending the assessment
  • both customers jointly electing to withdraw from the CTP process
  • a determination to cease the CTP investigation and not take any action

Advise the customers that if both parents jointly elect to withdraw from the CTP process, the payee must not be in receipt of an income tested pension benefit or allowance and if the payee elects to end the assessment, there may be adverse consequences for their Family Tax Benefit. Offer to transfer the payee to Centrelink to discuss their individual circumstances. See Ending a Child Support assessment for more information.

Objection rights

There are no objection rights to a CTP review, as there has been no decision. Once COA accepts a recommendation, any resulting decisions do have objection rights. Any subsequent Part 6A objection decision also has objection rights and the matter may be reviewed by the AAT.

Open exchange of information

Explain how open exchange of information relates to the CTP process:

  • once a case is referred for a change of assessment or the customers lodge an objection to a change of assessment decision, ‘open exchange of information’ procedures apply
  • open exchange of information does not apply to correspondence or information gathered prior to a case being referred to COA
  • if a Part 6A objection decision is reviewed by the AAT, all correspondence and information (even if these were received prior to a case being referred to Change of Assessment) are subject to open exchange of information
  • for more information, see Open exchange of information for Child Support customers.

Required financial information

Ask the customer to provide:

  • relevant financial information (identified through the customer discussion planner), and
  • any other information that would give a clearer picture of their financial circumstances

Use the information gathered in the initial investigation phase to guide the conversation. Advise the customer it may be necessary to send section 161 notices to external parties to request the financial information, if:

  • they do not provide the requested information, or
  • the information provided is not sufficient to make a recommendation

In addition to the information that they have provided, ask the customer to complete the Statement of Financial Details (SFD) and attach evidence to support their claims.

If either customer requests that you speak to a representative (for example, tax agent or accountant):

  • ensure they are confident the representative can provide adequate information about their financial circumstances
  • ask them to contact their representative in advance to advise they will receive a call regarding the customer’s finances
  • ask that they inform their representative that Child Support may ask them to provide evidence regarding the customer’s financial circumstances (for example, balance sheets or profit and loss statements)
  • advise if the customer’s representative (for example, tax agent) charges the customer for information they provide, Child Support are not liable for these charges

Note: send a section 161 notice if the customer’s representative asks for the request in writing. See Child Support’s information gathering powers.

Change of assessment process

Inform the customers about the change of assessment process:

  • although the CTP process only relates to initiating applications under reason 8A, the Decision Maker (DM) may also consider other change of assessment reasons when making a decision
  • the customers may respond to the registrar’s application with, or without a cross application for change of assessment under any of the 10 reasons. The customer may also lodge their own change of assessment application at any time. It is not necessary to wait until the CTP investigation is finalised
  • for more information, see Change of assessment in special circumstances (CS).

Table 4

Action

1

Financial Investigator analysis + Read more ...

Review all the gathered information (for example, ATO search results) to determine if the assessment reflects the customer’s true financial capacity to pay. Weigh all available information and evidence and consider if:

  • the customer’s financial resources are substantially different to their adjusted taxable income
  • the customer’s financial resources are fairly represented in the assessment
  • there is a potential administrative remedy that can be undertaken

Note: unlike COA, CTP cannot make a recommendation to change the assessment based on a customer’s capacity to earn. Therefore only analyse the information that relates to the customer’s actual financial resources. See:

  • Calculating financial capacity for business owners

If there isn't enough information to make a recommendation, conduct further investigations.

Recommendations and outcomes

Table 6

Step

Action

1

Make a recommendation + Read more ...

Make the CTP recommendation once all of the financial information has been gathered and analysed, and information has been clarified with the customers. The recommendation will form the basis for the CTP report.

The recommendation must aim to reflect the customers’ true financial circumstances as determined through analysis. If their financial resources are significantly different from what is reflected in the assessment, first consider if this can be addressed through an administrative remedies (for example, income correction or estimate review/reconciliation) go to Step 2. Otherwise, Financial Investigators would generally recommend setting the adjusted taxable income at a more reflective amount. Remember, the customer’s financial capacity is not just based on their adjusted taxable income alone - also consider the customer’s property and financial resources.

For information about issues to consider when determining a customer’s financial circumstances, see the Child Support Guide 2.6.14: Reason 8 – a parent’s income, property, financial resources, or earning capacity.

A link is on the References page and Calculating financial capacity for business owners.

If both customers’ incomes, assets and financial resources are fairly represented in the assessment, Financial Investigators would usually make a recommendation to not take any further action.

Note: where for example, the customers have jointly elected to withdraw from the CTP process or an administrative remedy has corrected the assessment, Financial Investigators may be able to make a recommendation prior to gathering and analysing all of the financial information.

Potential CTP recommendations or outcomes include:

  • administrative remedies. Go to Step 2
  • initiating a change of assessment process. Go to Step 3
  • the customers jointly elect to withdraw from the CTP process. Go to Step 4
  • taking no further action (NFA). Go to Step 5

2

CTP recommendation - administrative remedies + Read more ...

Where an administrative remedy will reflect the customer’s true financial capacity, this is the most desirable outcome. Administrative remedies include (but are not limited to):

  • correcting incomes, see Income Processing for Child Support customers
  • child support agreements, see Agreements between Child Support customers
  • estimate reviews or reconciliations, see Child support estimates review (post 1 July 2010) or Child support estimates – Reconcile (from 1 July 2011)
  • the payee electing to end the assessment, see Ending a Child Support assessment

Go to Step 7.

3

CTP recommendation - initiate change of assessment process + Read more ...

When recommending a COA, consider whether it is appropriate to make a change because of 1 or both customers’ financial details. Determine whether to recommend a change to the customers’:

  • adjusted taxable income(s), or
  • annual rate of child support

This recommendation can only be made if the customers’ income, property or financial resources are not fairly reflected in the assessment.

If it is determined that the customer’s financial resources are higher than what is reflected in their adjusted taxable income, this will usually lead to a recommendation to set their adjusted taxable income (that is, not the annual rate) at an appropriate amount. When making this recommendation, carefully weigh the evidence to determine the customer’s true financial resources.

If the customer has high financial resources, Financial Investigators may also consider recommending setting an annual rate, however, this is rarely appropriate. For example, if the customers have made a child support agreement in principle but have not been able to formalise it, then it may be appropriate to recommend setting the annual rate at the ‘agreed’ rate.

Financial Investigators must also consider whether the child support assessment is ‘unfair’ because the customer’s financial resources are not reflected in the child support assessment. For example, if the customer’s financial resources are high, but setting the customer’s adjusted taxable income would not lead to a significant change to the annual rate, the assessment would not be considered ‘unfair’. This would generally lead to a ‘no further action’ recommendation. If this is the case, go to Step 5.

Note: if assistance is required in making a recommendation, discuss this with the team leader or a Program Support Manager.

For information to consider when making a recommendation, see:

  • Calculating financial capacity for business owners, see the Resources page for a link
  • the Child Support Guide 2.6.6: Registrar-initiated change of assessment and the Child Support Guide 2.6.14: Reason 8 - a parent’s income, property, financial resources, or earning capacity, see the References page for a link

4

CTP outcome - joint election to cease the review + Read more ...

Note: the process for joint election to cease the review is not always required in cases with identified family and domestic violence concerns. Discuss with CTP team leader to determine required action.

The customers may lodge a 'joint election' to cease the CTP process at any time if the payee is not receiving any income tested pensions, benefits or allowances. This is an administrative process and not legislative until after the S98M have been issued. If both parties wish to cease the review after the S98M letter has been issued, a Joint Election to Withdraw can be made directly to the COA DM under S98P(1)

The customers’ election can be lodged over the telephone or by using the Joint election to stop a change to your Child Support Assessment being initiated (CS2899) form, the Resources page for a link

Document the customers’ election in the capacity to pay window using the appropriate macro in the CTP tool suite, see Documenting Child Support information in Cuba.

If a joint election is received, check if the payee is in receipt of an income tested pension benefit or allowance.

See Centrelink – Child Support information exchange.

If the payee:

  • is not in receipt of these benefits, the election must be accepted. Go to Step 7
  • is in receipt of these benefits, the election must be rejected, send the COA17-2 To both clients - Joint election to discontinue RICA has been rejected letter to both customers

If the election is disallowed, determine which other outcome is appropriate. If there is:

  • sufficient information to make another determination, go to Step 1
  • more information required, go to Step 1 in the Further investigation in CTP process table

A joint election to cease the review does not prevent Financial Investigators from implementing an administrative remedy. Go to Step 2 to check if any are appropriate.

  • there is no appropriate administrative remedy, and
  • the assessment correctly reflects the customers’ financial capacity

Go to Step 7

8

Submit CTP report + Read more ...

Once the recommendation has been written and/or added to the CTP report, check with the team leader if it needs to be submitted for approval. The team leader has discretion to determine if an approval is necessary based on the team and individual needs at the time. If the team leader advises approval is not required, see Finalise CTP report.

If the team leader confirms the report needs to be submitted, advise them of the case number or the customer child support reference numbers (CSRN) so they can review the report. For recommendation to COA, email a copy of the report to the team leader.

If the team leader approves the recommendation, finalise the report. See Finalise CTP report.

If the team leader does not approve the report:

  • seek feedback on what their concerns are
  • discuss the various options until both agree to an appropriate CTP outcome
  • re-submit the report with the agreed changes
  • finalise the report once it is approved, see Finalise CTP report

If the report is not approved because there is insufficient evidence or more information is required, conduct further investigations.

5

Issue S98M letter + Read more ...

The S98M letters must be issued to both parties.

Choose the relevant letter

  • UQ2.02 - S98M Considering a change to your assessment - Payer letter
  • UQ2.01 - S98M Considering a change to your assessment - Payee letter, and
  • UQ2.04 – S98M Considering a change to your assessment (Incarcerated) - Both parents

Edit the relevant letter by including:

  • customer and case specific information - ensure to follow the instructions as outlined in ‘red’ and delete these instructions before sending the letters
  • a summary of relevant information consisting of the ‘Conclusion’ and ‘Other reasons’ sections of the CTP report in Cuba. The same summary must be sent to both parties (the customer specific information will be different)

As the same summary is provided to both parties, special guidelines must be followed when completing the report and drafting the letters:

  • ensure the facts of the investigation have been verified by discussing these with the customers throughout the CTP process wherever possible
  • provide enough detail that will allow both customers to respond to the recommendation and participate in the change of assessment process
  • do not include personal opinions or assumptions
  • do not unnecessarily include the names of third party individuals – if this is not possible, describe the third parties by their relationship to the customer (for example, the customer's accountant)
  • it is not necessary to protect the names of companies, trusts and business structures, but there may not be a need to include these in the conclusion

Once the letter has been edited:

  • copy and paste the main body of the document into a unique letter for both customers, see Letters Cuba process help
  • obtain approval for the letters from the team leader
  • print the letters locally and attach the Response Form - We are considering changing your Child Support Assessment (CS2898), a link is located on the Resources page
  • send the letters

Other CTP outcomes and customer contact + Read more ...

With the exception of no further action outcomes, finalise the report by changing the status in the CAPACITY TO PAY – CTP Finalisation dialogue box in the Capacity to Pay window to Finalised.

If a joint election to cease the review has been accepted, paste the UQ2.03 - Withdraw from change of Assessment accepted - both letter (JER acceptance letter text) into an MX0-1 - Unique letter for each customer to communicate the decision, see Letters Cuba process help and Letters for Child Support customers.

For all other outcomes, where the customers’ assessment needs to be amended, record the change in Cuba by following the relevant procedural instruction.

If the outcome is recorded as ‘no further action’, customers must be contacted before finalising the CTP report.

Contact the customers

Contact both customers and inform them of the outcome from the CTP investigation, see Contact with Child Support customers. Advise the customers:

  • if the assessment liability rates have changed
  • what the ongoing liability rates are and when the next payment is due
  • if any arrears have been created

If the payee collects the child support privately and there are no outstanding child support payments, go to Step 8.

If the case is registered for collection and the ongoing liability has changed, obtain a commitment from the payer to pay the new ongoing amount.

If the payer has outstanding child support, negotiate a payment arrangement. See Debt repayment (CS).

Note: if the outcome is ‘no further action’ and the customers provide new information, determine if the CTP outcome should change. If there needs to be a change to the outcome, make another recommendation. Alternatively, if the customers have raised other change of assessment reasons throughout the review, advise they can apply for a change of assessment themselves to have these other reasons addressed.
