This document outlines information about eligibility and registration requirements for mental health nurses.

On this Page:

Mental health nurses

Mental health nursing is a specialised field of nursing which focuses on working with people to meet their mental health recovery goals.

This qualification is issued to registered nurses who demonstrate they meet specific criteria in relation to:

  • registration
  • education
  • experience, and
  • professional development

It is valid for 3 years.

Disputes about removing a registration

Direct any disputes about a registration to the Australian College of Mental Health Nurses (ACMHN). The Resources page has the contact details.

Non-directive pregnancy support counselling (PSC)

Mental health nurses can also undertake additional training in pregnancy support counselling (PSC) with the Australian College of Mental Health Nurses (ACMHN).

This endorsement is recorded on their Medicare Provider Directory System (PDS) record.

The ACMHN advises Services Australia of mental health nurses who have completed this training and been granted a certificate of completion. A health professional may also submit their certificate of completion in Perinatal Non-Directive Counselling Training: V2 to Services Australia confirming their endorsement. Endorsement is then recorded on PDS.

This qualification does not have an end date.

Services Australia eligibility and registration

To be registered with Services Australia, an applicant must:

  • be recognised as a Credentialed Mental Health Nurse by the Australian College of Mental Health Nurses (ACMHN)
  • provide a copy of the ACMHN certification with the initial application for a provider number

Peak processing periods are December to April.

Non-directive Pregnancy Support Counselling (PSC) endorsement

In order for a mental health nurse to be recognised as trained in PSC:

  • The ACMHN advises Services Australia the details of who has completed the training
  • A health professional may also submit a copy of their certificate of course completion in Perinatal Non-Directive Counselling Training: V2 issued by the Australian Psychological Society
  • Registration is recorded on the Medicare provider stem in the Provider Directory System (PDS)
  • Further confirmation of completion (such as a certificate) is not required if PDS shows a current Australian (AUS) registration in the Registration section of the provider's record

Enquiry escalation

The Provider Registration enquiry line and email inbox, managed by Medicare Provider Services (MPS), are used as the front door for all enquiries relating to the processing of provider registrations.

Enquiries to this area are responded to by a Service Officer trained in all aspects of provider registration. These Service Officers escalate complex enquiries to their team leader or a Programme Support Officer (PSO) who will liaise with the Provider Eligibility and Accreditation team in National Office for assistance if required.
