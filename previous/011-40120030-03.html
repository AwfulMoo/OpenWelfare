Complete a warm transfer

Service centres receiving calls from Smart Centres

Call transfer guide

Complete a warm transfer

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Advise caller of warm transfer + Read more ...

Tell the caller you are not the right business area but will help them find the right person or business area (not a voicemail).

2

Initiate transfer + Read more ...

Call the right person or business area.

Is contact made?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, return to the caller and explain that the:
    • right area cannot be found, or
    • transfer number is busy or diverted to voicemail. If the caller is happy to go to voicemail, transfer them. If not, offer to take a message. Ensure the enquiry is followed up

3

Complete transfer + Read more ...

  • Summarise the caller's enquiry
  • Advise that security checks are complete
  • Transfer the call

Service centres receiving calls from Smart Centres

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Record details of caller + Read more ...

Ask for the name of the staff member transferring the call and record it for reference.

2

Accept call + Read more ...

Calls can be accepted when:

  • a customer:
    • has been asked to contact the service centre
    • asks to speak to the Service Officer who originally provided the service
    • has lost personal property
  • it is a personal call

Call transfer guide

Table 3

Note: cold transfer calls to the relevant queue unless told to warm transfer below.

Item

Enquiry type and Service Officer action

1

Newborn enrolment, safety net and basic Medicare public enquiries + Read more ...

Enquiries about:

  • Medicare Newborn enrolment
  • Medicare Safety net
  • other basic Medicare Public enquiries

Transfer the call by selecting Medicare Basic Enquiries in Services Australia Workspace.

2

Customer updates, new card, copy/transfer, claims, banking updates and more + Read more ...

Enquiries about:

  • banking updates Service Officer is unable to action
  • copy/transfer
  • address updates Service Officer is unable to action
  • new card request Service Officer is unable to action
  • temporary card
  • claims enquiry
  • safety net
  • checking the progress of an application
  • unable to pass security check
  • notification of a deceased member
  • other Medicare enquiries Service Officer is unable to action

Transfer the call by selecting Medicare General Enq in Services Australia Workspace.

If the customer:

  • is unable to pass the security check do not warm transfer the call
  • wants to contact the area directly, give them the Medicare Public general phone number 132 011

3

Linking Centrelink services, mismatched name or linking code error + Read more ...

Enquiries about:

  • linking Centrelink services
  • mismatched name on Medicare and myGov
  • linking code error

If the issue is:

  • myGov login:
    • transfer the call to the myGov Helpdesk option 1
  • name discrepancy:
    • follow the Medicare name change guidelines
  • reporting error RFM39 or RFM51 when linking Medicare:
    • transfer the call by selecting Medicare General Enq in Services Australia Workspace

4

Unable to pass security check or generate linking code + Read more ...

Caller is unable to:

  • pass security check
  • generate linking code as not enough information

Select Medicare document verification in Services Australia Workspace to enable identification document to be verified in Document Verification System (DVS).

5

AIR/COVID enquiries from the public + Read more ...

For all other AIR/COVID enquiries from members of the public the Service Officer is unable to action:

  • transfer the call by selecting AIR Public Enquiries in Services Australia Workspace
  • if the customer wants to contact the area directly, tell them to call the AIR public phone number and select option 2

6

AIR/COVID enquiries from providers + Read more ...

For all other AIR/COVID enquiries received from providers the Service Officer is unable to action:

  • transfer the call by selecting AIR Provider in Services Australia Workspace
  • if the customer wants to contact the area directly, tell them to call the AIR public phone number and select option 3

7

International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificates + Read more ...

For all enquiries about International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificates:

  • transfer the call by selecting International Certificate in Services Australia Workspace

8

Defence force personnel or Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) + Read more ...

For all enquiries from Defence force personnel or Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA):

  • transfer the call by selecting AIR-DVA_Defence_Hubs in Services Australia Workspace
  • if the customer wants to contact the area directly, tell them to call the AIR public phone number and select option 4

9

PRODA, HPOS and AIR site + Read more ...

For all other enquiries about PRODA, HPOS and AIR site:

  • transfer the call by selecting AIR Internet Helpdesk in Services Australia Workspace
  • if the customer wants to contact the area directly, give them the AIR Internet Helpdesk phone number

10

Medicare eligibility and enrolments + Read more ...

If Service Officers are unable to resolve the customer's enquiries about Medicare eligibility and enrolments including:

  • new enrolments from overseas
  • applicants for permanent residency
  • Reciprocal Health Care Agreements (RHCA) customers
  • returning residents from overseas
  • New Zealand citizens enrolling for first time
  • Ministerial Orders
  • children born overseas
  • Child Dental Benefits Schedule (CDBS)
  • complex new enrolments

Contact Local Peer Support (LPS). They will decide if the Service Officer can warm transfer the customer to LPS.

11

All other Medicare 2 or Medicare 1 enquiries + Read more ...

For all other Medicare 2 or Medicare 1 enquiries contact the relevant LPS, see Tier 0 technical support - self-sufficiency.
