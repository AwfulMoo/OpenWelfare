Initial checks

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Check for exclusions + Read more ...

Check if there is a need to conduct a MoC assessment:

  • when a customer contacts to discuss Relationship Details - Separated Under One Roof (SS293) form or Relationship Details (SS284) form as a required task for an online claim
  • before issuing SS293/SS284 forms when a customer advises a change to their living arrangements, or
  • after SS284/SS293 forms are lodged
  • when allocated a Disclosed Relationship - follow up required work item

Exclusions from this process:

  • customer advises they are partnered (Note: this does not include when a customer has disclosed a past relationship during the assessment of the CrP claim, i.e. a Disclosed Relationship - follow up required work item)
  • section 24 assessments and reviews
  • customer and sharer/other person are claiming or have a current Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) only
  • customer and sharer/other person are claiming or have a current Commonwealth Seniors Health Care Card (CSHC) only
  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and/or Child Care Subsidy (CCS) only customer separates, with no Separated Under One Roof (SUOR) triggers
  • customer or partner is imprisoned or in psychiatric confinement
  • customer or partner is separated due to illness
  • customer or partner is in approved respite care
  • customer or sharer/other person has passed away. Note: if the MoC assessment was triggered before they passed away, an assessment for the past period may still be required. This is to check that correct payments were made to the person who continues to receive payments

Does one of the above exclusions apply?

  • Yes, close any open MoC work items related to the exclusion. Do this via the Activity List (AL) screen or Interaction History in Customer First, or the Transactions icon in Process Direct. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check for streaming DOCs + Read more ...

Check the Document List (DL) screen on the customer and sharer/other person's records (if they have one) for any of the following:

  • PD Referral for MOC only if there is an open Customer Referral work item in Interaction History. This means that documents are ready for assessment
  • MoCA Progress DOC if it includes 'Reviewed as per OB 106-19071608 - MoC assessment required'
  • SUOR Progress DOC if it includes 'Reviewed as per OB 106-19071608 - MoC assessment required'
  • MoC - No assessment required DOC unless it relates to a different circumstance or sharer/other person
  • MOC Hardship DOC if it includes 'Reviewed as per OB 106-19071608 - MoC assessment required'

Are any of the above DOCs present?

  • Yes, if the DOC is:
    • no MoC assessment needed, close any related open MoC work items on both the customer and sharer/other person records. This includes the SS284 or SS293 scan activities. Do this via the AL screen or Interaction History in Customer First, or the Transactions icon in Process Direct. Procedure ends here
    • any other DOC and the Service Officer is not a MoC processing officer, go to Step 3
    • any other DOC and the Service Officer is a MoC processing officer, see the Check risk-based MoC assessment exemption criteria Table
  • No,
    • if a MoC referral is required because the partner has not confirmed the relationship, see Step 1 in Table 5
    • otherwise, go to Step 3

3

Check for safety concerns or hardship indicators + Read more ...

The customer's record must be checked to determine if they have indicated there are safety concerns. This includes where they indicate there are safety concerns if the SS284/SS293 form was to be issued to the other person/ex-partner. Check the following information (but not limited to) on the customer's record:

  • For new claims: the online claim and responses to the Living Arrangements question set
  • For customer initiated change of circumstances online workflows (that is customer has used the Update address, accommodation or contact details online workflow): the online update and responses to the Living Arrangements question set
  • For staff assisted workflows (Change in Contact Details and Address workflows): responses to the Living Arrangements question set
  • DOCs on DL screen in Customer First
  • Notes screen in Process Direct
  • Document Tools

Note: if safety concerns have been identified, see family and domestic violence procedure to conduct the risk identification and referral process.

Are there safety concerns or is the customer in hardship?

  • Yes, and the customer has:
    • an open Urgent MoCA required - Financial Hardship or Urgent Moc required - Safety Concerns DOC, or
    • a PD referral for MoC with referral reasons: MoC/SUOR Financial Hardship referral or MoC/SUOR Safety Concerns (FDV) referral, or
    • an Immediate MoC assessment DOC on their record, procedure ends here
    • a Disclosed Relationship - follow up required work item and DOC on their record. Go to Step 6
    • an existing MoC/SUOR DOC type on their record, e.g. MoCA progress DOC, see Step 2 in Table 5
    • no MoC/SUOR DOC type on their record, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check for fraud or compliance activity + Read more ...

A MoC assessment may not be needed if there is current or previous fraud or compliance activity. Check it relates to the customer and sharer's/other person's relationship.

Check the DL and AL screens on the record of the customer and sharer/other person's (if they have one).

  • Check for a DOC on the DL screen with any of the following in the subject line:
    • Compl TIP Rvw or COMPLXXX Review – IRSRFI sent
    • Random Sample Survey
    • FCI Intelligence and Investigations Administrative Investigation (Display on Access DOC)
    • Mandatory review of XXX (customer's payment type)
  • An outstanding review on AL screen region code TQI, MND or TRI

Are any of the above DOCs or review activities present?

  • Yes, and there is:
    • an outstanding review activity on the AL screen for region code TQI, MND or TRI, close all open MoC work items. Record receipt of the SS293 or SS284 forms on the compliance DOC. Procedure ends here
    • no outstanding activity on the AL screen, see Check risk-based MoC assessment exemption criteria Table
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Check for claim or payments + Read more ...

A MoC assessment is:

  • Not needed if:
    • both the customer and sharer/other person are not claiming or getting a payment that may be affected by a MoC assessment. For example, a customer lodged a claim that has been/will be rejected for another (non-MoC related) reason, withdrawn or cancelled and neither person is claiming or getting other payments
    • forms were issued incorrectly
  • Needed if the customer or sharer/other person get a payment that may be affected by a MoC assessment
    For example, a customer's claim for Parenting Payment Single has been/will be rejected, a MoC assessment is still needed if they are already getting JobSeeker Payment. Check the DL screen for a recent claim rejection DOC. Check for any other payments the customer gets

Is the customer or sharer/other person claiming or getting a payment?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, or if rejecting the claim for another non-MoC related reason, reject the claim first, then see Step 1 in Table 4

6

Disclosed Relationship - follow up required work item + Read more ...

A Disclosed Relationship - follow up required work item (ZIAM_MOCSWO_MFU) is created when a single customer claiming Crisis Payment (extreme circumstances family and domestic violence) has disclosed a past relationship during the assessment of the CrP claim.

When allocated a Disclosed Relationship - follow up required work item:

  • Issue appropriate form and letter
    • issue the form to the customer only as there are indications of family and domestic violence
    • do not use the Mail Forms guided procedure to issue relationship details forms. It will not issue a letter with enough detail for the agency to take further action. For example, suspending payment if they do not return the form
  • Create a review activity on the customer's record. Set this for the allowed time to follow up the return of the form
  • Close the Disclosed Relationship - follow up required DOC
  • Record a new Fast Note DOC on the customer's record with details of the above action taken

Procedure ends here until form is returned.

Check for a MoC decision made in the last 13 weeks + Read more ...

3

SUOR only - check for previous SUOR + Read more ...

An assessment review is not needed if there was a SUOR assessment or review in the appropriate timeframe, as per the SUOR review guide and no change in circumstances are advised.

Check details on the records of the customer and sharer/other person (where they have one).

For SUOR customers, the record will show a:

  • SUR (Separated Under One Roof) relationship qualifier code in the Qualifier Code field on the Marital Status (MS) screen
  • future review on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen
  • Member of a Couple Decision DOC on the DL screen for an initial assessment
  • SUOR Review decision DOC on the DL screen for a review

Is SUR coded on the MS screen?

  • Yes,
    • If SUOR assessed or reviewed in the appropriate timeframe as per the SUOR review guide and no change in circumstances have occurred, see Step 1 in Table 4
    • If no SUOR assessment or review in the appropriate timeframe as per the SUOR review guide or a change in circumstances advised, conduct SUOR review. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check for further streaming questions + Read more ...

A MoC is not needed when the customer indicates on the SS284 or SS293 forms or in an online claim/SUOR review:

  • they are partnered to someone else, whether living in the same household or not (check SS284 or SS293 at Q6 in the 2107 form version. Note: this question is not asked in the 2104 form version)
  • the other person/ex-partner is the partner of anyone else, whether living in the same household or not (check SS284 or SS293 at Q7 in the 2107 form version or Q8 in the 2104 form version)
  • they describe the relationship with the other person/ex-partner as 'partner' (married, registered or de facto) (check SS284 or SS293 at Q8 in the 2107 form version or Q6 in the 2104 form version)
  • they or the other person/ex-partner lives alone in a fully self-contained area such as a granny flat, caravan or converted garage. Check SS284 or SS293 at Q9 in the 2107 form version or Q7 in the 2104 form version. A self-contained area is a place with private or separate sleeping, cooking and bathroom facilities

The MS screen on the sharer/other person's record (if they have one) can be checked to confirm if they have a partner.

Do any of the further streaming questions apply?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Check if dependent YAL, DSP under 21 or ABSTUDY customer + Read more ...

For new claims

Dependent customer claims are assessed on parental income details. They cannot be assessed as a member of a couple.

Check Document Tools to see if a Parental Income Details (MOD JY) form has been lodged or if parents have completed their parental income details online. See Advise parental income online service.

If the customer is independent, a MoC assessment may still be needed if they are single, share their accommodation and the LA question set has triggered the need for a MoC assessment.

Current YAL/ABY customers are dependent if:

  • the customer is under 22 years, and
  • the Independence/Homeless/Away From Home Summary (NIHS) screen does not contain a line indicating the customer has been assessed as independent (IND or HOM).
    Note: a customer may be getting the higher 'away from home' rate and this will show as AFH (away from home) code on the NIHS screen

Current DSP customers are dependent if:

  • they are under 21 years, and
  • the Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen shows NID, NLE or UTN independent living code. Refer to Assessing payment rates and independence for DSP customers under 21 years

Is the customer or sharer/other person a dependent YAL, DSP under 21 or ABY customer?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, go to Step 6

6

MoC assessment only - check MoC triggers + Read more ...

There is a low risk the customer and sharer/other person are a couple if the only MoC triggers are:

  • they previously shared an address, and/or
  • they have a joint bank account just for paying rent and/or utilities

To find MoC triggers, check the responses to the Living Arrangements question set. See the Resources page for help.

Confirm verbally, or check the SS284 form for responses to Q14 (2107 form version) or Q13 (2104 form version). Make sure they only ticked they have a joint account for 'Shared Rent and/or utilities only'.

Are one or both of the above the only MoC triggers present?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Check if the customer and sharer/other person are no longer living together + Read more ...

A MoC assessment is not needed if the customer:

  • claims a payment;
    • then advises they no longer share with the person the MoC assessment was triggered for, and
    • this change was within 14 days of their claim lodgement
  • is getting payments and is issued an SS284 or SS293;
    • then contacts to advise they no longer live with the person, and
    • they stopped living together within 14 days of the date they started living together

Check the DL screen for DOCs for changes since the MoC assessment triggered.

Check the Living Arrangements question set for updates to sharer details since the MoC assessment triggered.

Do either of the above circumstances apply?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Check if customer or sharer/other person is under the age of consent + Read more ...

If a customer or the sharer/other person is under the age of consent, they cannot be a member of a couple under social security law.

Is the customer or sharer/other person is under the age of consent?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No,
    • if the customer has contacted to discuss SS293 or SS284 forms for an online claim, see Step 1 in Table 3
    • if not, go to Step 9

9

Check if separation details are required + Read more ...

A MOD S or an update using the Update relationship details online service may be required for customers:

  • getting a payment, and
  • currently recorded as partnered

When a MOD S is or is not required.

If separation details are required, they must be provided before a MoC assessment can be undertaken.

Are separation details required?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No,
    • MoC processing staff, see Check risk-based MoC assessment exemption criteria Table
    • Non-MoC processing staff, see Step 1 in Table 5

10

Check if separation details have been provided + Read more ...

A customer may have provided separation details using the Update relationship details online service or a Separation Details (MOD S) form.

Check for separation details using:

  • The Update relationship details online service, check Process Direct > Customer Summary > Transactions > All Transactions
    • Scroll through recent transactions to find the online service update. Customers that have advised of separation and are SUOR, transaction notes will appear as:
      Submitted dd/mm/yyyy Received dd/mm/yyyy – Customer Update – Advise Separation – Separation – Under One Roof
    • Select the transaction hyperlink to open Customer and Activity Information
    • Open the online separation form using
  • A MOD S form, check Customer First > Document Tools for scanned MOD S

Has the customer provided separation details?

  • Yes,
    • MoC processing staff, see Check risk-based MoC assessment exemption criteria Table
    • Non-MoC processing staff, see Step 1 in Table 5
  • No,
    • Issue an RFI letter (generated through the Request for Information guided procedure), requesting the Separation Details (MOD S) form. The MOD S is requested by selecting the Form request - Customer option on the Available Documents list and recording the form or module name in the Additional Options section
    • Code a manual review, allowing extra time for mail delivery. State 'MOD S requested' and add keyword MOCPRG
    • Record a DOC using Fast Note. Select Auto text use Assessments and Reviews > Separated Under One Roof > SUOR Progress. Include action taken
    • Close all other associated open MoC work items on both the customer and ex-partner's records. This includes the SS293 scan activities via the AL screen

SS293 or SS284 forms are a required task for an online claim + Read more ...

Check if the customer has a suitable reason for sharer/other person not providing the form + Read more ...

Suitable reasons include but are not limited to:

  • safety concerns. The customer can indicate this through the claim process. This stops the form being requested for their sharer
  • the sharer/other person is temporarily overseas
  • the sharer/other person refuses to complete and return the form in the allowed time

Use discretion when deciding if a customer has a suitable reason for sharer/other person not providing the form.

Safety concerns + Read more ...

Do not contact the sharer.

  • Record a DOC on the customer's record stating why the sharer's form has not been provided
  • Change the SS284 or SS293 form task for the sharer to Not required. See Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM)
  • Advise the customer to continue the claim without submitting the SS284 or SS293 forms for the sharer

Note: customers may indicate safety concerns for themselves or their family due to family and domestic violence. See Family and domestic violence to conduct the risk identification and referral process.

Procedure ends here.

Other suitable reason + Read more ...

  • Issue the SS293 form and letter to the sharer outside the claim process. Do not use the Mail Forms guided procedure to issue relationship details forms. It will not issue a letter with enough detail for the agency to take further action. For example, suspending payment if they do not return the form
  • Record a DOC on the customer's record stating why the sharer/other person's form is not provided, and a confirmation that a formal request was sent via mail
  • Change the SS284/SS293 form task for the sharer to Not required. See Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM)
  • Advise the customer to continue the claim without submitting the SS284 or SS293 forms for their sharer. Advise the customer a separate request for the form will be issued to their sharer

Procedure ends here.

MoC referral or assessment

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Check for forms from the customer and/or the sharer/other person + Read more ...

In limited circumstances, a formal request may need to be issued if:

  • there is no evidence of receipt of forms on either sharer/other persons or customer's record, or
  • a formal request for the form has not been triggered for the customer and/or the sharer/other person

If relevant, check the response to this question in the Living Arrangements question set: 'Are you concerned about your safety if forms are issued to any of these people?' If the response is 'Yes', any Relationship Details forms triggered are only issued to the customer, forms must not be issued to the sharer/other person.

Check the customer and sharer/other person's record (if safety concerns have not been raised) for the formal request, and/or receipt of the forms
Note: a request sent using the Request for information is considered to be a formal request.

Does a formal request need issuing?

  • Yes,
    • If a new claim has been lodged, and a change of circumstances means a MoC/SUOR assessment is now required, request the information within the claim. Procedure ends here until the extra information is submitted to support the claim
    • If the assessment is not related to a new claim, issue the appropriate form and letter. Do not use the Mail Forms guided procedure to issue relationship details forms. It will not issue a letter with enough detail for the agency to take further action. For example, suspending payment if they do not return the form
    • Go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Consider eligibility for priority processing + Read more ...

Check if criteria are met for immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing.

If the criteria are not met, but the customer is in genuine financial hardship or there are indications of family and domestic violence, a request for urgent processing may be appropriate.

MoC hardship provisions align with new claim financial hardship provisions. This is the case whether or not the MoC assessment relates to a new claim.

Have criteria been met for immediate processing of the MoC assessment and new claim?

  • Yes, see Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing. Procedure ends here
  • No, and there is:
    • genuine hardship or indications of family and domestic violence, go to Step 3
    • no genuine hardship or indications of family and domestic violence, go to Step 4

3

Urgent assessment due to financial hardship or family and domestic violence

If there are indications of family and domestic violence, offer a referral to a social worker if this has not already occurred.

Process Direct + Read more ...

  • Select > Referrals
    • Referral Type: select Member of a Couple
    • Referral Reason: select
      MoC/SUOR Financial Hardship referral, or
      MoC/SUOR Safety Concerns (FDV) referral
    • In the Document List, check the box against all documents provided
    • Notes: state why an assessment is needed. This will show in the Customer Referral work item, not on the DL screen. State the reason even after selecting the Referral Reason above
