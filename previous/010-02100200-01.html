This document outlines details of Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel for ABSTUDY.

ABSTUDY Fares Allowance (FAA)

Orientation or Special Purpose Visit travel is available to assist eligible secondary or tertiary ABSTUDY students who have been approved to live away from home and are moving to their new boarding or term location for the first time.

Travel assistance in the form of FAA may be given to the student and/or their parent or guardian or a representative from their home community with significant ties to the student, for the entry, orientation or adjustment of the student so that the boarding/away from home placement may be effective.

Eligibility criteria for Orientation travel

Students who are eligible for FAA may have access to Orientation travel if:

  • a secondary school or boarding facility requires prospective students to attend interviews or other selection procedures prior to acceptance
  • the student is going away to board for the first time, and is from:
  • a remote Aboriginal community, or
  • a town in a remote location and it is likely that the student would experience serious problems of adjustment
  • the student is intending to study in an approved tertiary course of at least one year's duration at the study location, and has completed secondary studies in the previous year

If a student or non-student traveller has incurred accommodation costs during their stay, these costs may also be met.

Eligibility criteria for Special Purpose Visit travel

Secondary students who are eligible for FAA may have access to Special Purpose Visit travel if:

  • a student in a boarding placement is under threat of expulsion because of serious problems of adjustment or similar circumstances and a visit (sometimes known as pastoral care) is expected to stabilise the situation, or
  • a secondary school student will be participating in a school event, such as but not limited to, a graduation, school play, award ceremony, teacher/parent interview or sporting event

If a student or non-student traveller has incurred accommodation costs during their stay, these costs may also be met.

Access to Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel

Students are able to access both Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel where entitlement for each journey has been met.

Where approval has been given for Special Purpose Visit travel, entitlement is limited to 3 return journeys, per student per year, between the home community and term address. These can be used by either the student, family member or community member.

There are no limits on Orientation travel.

Note: where a student is already attending school and requires FAA to attend an interview or other selection criteria at a different school or boarding facility, FAA can be approved from their current study location, not just from the home community.

A secondary school may require prospective students to attend interviews, transition programs or other selection procedures, in the years prior to the student commencing. These procedures can include screening or selection interviews as part of the offer of a scholarship, for example The Sporting Chance Programme. This is acceptable and the student can be approved as long as:

  • the secondary school or boarding facility provides details of the interview, transition or orientation program, and
  • the student would qualify for FAA to study at the secondary school if accepted

A secondary boarding provider may conduct orientation or transition programs to assist with student adjustment to living away from home. Such programs may include requirements for students and their approved traveller to stay on or near the boarding facility or school for up to a week.

If a student or non-student traveller has incurred accommodation and meal costs during their stay, these costs may also be met.

FAA for the journey can be authorised in advance or reimbursed after the journey has taken place.

An Australian Apprentice is not entitled to FAA for the Australian Apprenticeship.

Boarding facilities

A secondary boarding facility may need to conduct an interview or selection exercise in their own right and it may not be possible at the time of claiming to confirm the school the student will be attending. If this is the case, the claim can be accepted without study details. They must advise their study details as soon as possible, otherwise their ABSTUDY may be rejected after 14 days of the interview taking place.
