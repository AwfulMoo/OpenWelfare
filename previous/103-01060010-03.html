Actioning death of an income managed customer

Process when an income managed customer dies

Step

Action

1

Notification of the death of an Income Management customer + Read more ...

Determine appropriate action required relating to the death of an Income Management customer:

  • Initial contact advising the death of an Income Management customer, go to Step 2
  • Evidence to support the previous notification of death of an Income Management customer is being lodged, go to Step 4
  • Details of a customer's Legal Personal Representative (LPR) are being lodged, following previous notification of death, go to Step 6
  • The Zone has received a request from IMPACT to investigate a customer's LPR, go to Step 10

To determine whether a customer's payments are, or were being income managed:

  • INM will display on the Benefit Status line where Income Management is still in place
  • The Historical Information twisty on the Income Management Summary screen will show if Income Management has recently ended. Also review the Document List (DL) for any DOCs regarding the death of the customer

If the customer's payments are/were not income managed, this procedure does not apply. See Initial contact after someone has died. Procedure ends here.

2

Confirm the death of the deceased + Read more ...

Confirmation can be accepted where the deceased customer's full name is provided along with 2 of the following:

  • Deceased customer's last address in full
  • Date of birth and age at death
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Partner's name (if partnered or widowed).

Can the third party supply enough information to confirm death?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Death cannot be confirmed at initial contact + Read more ...

Action any outstanding Work Items and activities on the Activity List (AL) screen.

Obtain all relevant information from the third party, including their own name, address, contact number and relationship to the deceased.

  • Obtain executor details, if known
  • Obtain the name of the funeral director or hospital where the customer passed away, if known

  • In the deceased customer's record run the Notification of Death guided procedure. The guided procedure will generate a DOC to the customer's local service centre. If the guided procedure is unavailable, record all information in a DOC and transfer the DOC to the customer's service centre with the keyword 'ACTDOC'

Note: where multiple notifications are received in relation to the death of the same customer do not re-run the guided procedure. Check the record and annotate the original DOC to state details are correct and who advised.

  • Procedure ends here

  • If there is doubt about the identity of the deceased, contact the funeral director or hospital where the customer passed away. If this is not an option and contact with relatives is proposed, social worker involvement may be necessary. Where appropriate, send Request for Verification (SS257) form
  • Where death cannot be confirmed suspend payment until the death can be confirmed. The suspension date of event should be the unconfirmed date of death or date paid to, whichever is the later.
    • Select the suspension option in the Notification of Death guided procedure, or
    • Manually code the suspension with reason 'Other' (OTH)
    • Record a DOC on the customer's record, ensuring that it meets Online Document Recording (ODR) standards for significant decisions and including what action is required to confirm the death
    • Record a manual review for 14 days from suspension to check if death has been confirmed
    • Do not issue a suspension letter

Note: it is imperative the death is confirmed and actioned as a matter of urgency.

Procedure ends here until death is confirmed. Refer to Initial contact after someone has died for any further action required.

4

Outstanding work + Read more ...

Before the Notification of Death guided procedure is run, the following is to be actioned on the deceased customer's record:

  • From the Income Management menu go the customer's Income Management Summary screen and manually tick off any outstanding Work Items. Note: as 'Work Item I098Q1 - IM account over $3000.00' cannot be manually ticked off, refer to Generic Income Management and BasicsCard Work Items to remove this Work Item
  • Go to the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen to confirm the payment destination recorded for Service Reason GPY matches the customer's most recent payment type, including where the customer has a payment nominee. (Check the Nominee Links Summary (NOLS) screen to make sure the Nominee status is still current)
  • To update the payment destination, select the GPY Service Reason to go to the Payment Destination Details (PAD) screen where the payment destination details can be recorded

Go to Step 5.

5

Notification of death action + Read more ...

Further action/coding may be required on the customer's record depending on the payment they were receiving. Use the correct procedure for the payment type or circumstances of the customer to record the death action. For a link to the appropriate procedures, see Initial contact after someone has died.

Complete the death action, as instructed in the appropriate procedure, using the Notification of Death guided procedure and include the following information in the Notification of Death DOC:

  • Name and contact details of the customer's Legal Personal Representative (LPR). If details are known, advise the third party that documentary evidence will need to be provided, such as a copy of the customer's will or evidence of the enduring power of attorney
  • If LPR details are not known or the customer died intestate (that is, without a will), include details of the person/s likely to be finalising the estate or affairs of the customer (which may include family members)

Go to Step 7.

6

Documentary evidence including LPR + Read more ...

When documentary evidence of the Legal Personal Representative (LPR) or details of the person/s finalising the estate or affairs of the customer (which may include family members) are received, these are to be scanned onto the customer's record.

Send an ACTDOC to IMPACT (Region Code IMT) advising information has been received and scanned and where residual funds are to be directed. Include the following as appropriate:

  • LPR instructions of how funds are to be disbursed
  • Name and contact details of a person likely to take responsibility for the customer's affairs (closing bank accounts, arranging and paying for the funeral etc.). This may be a family member
  • If the customer passed away in hospital, name and location of the hospital, including a contact number and invoice or reference numbers if applicable
  • Details of the funeral home if known
  • Name and contact details of the person/organisation paying for the funeral/transport costs
  • Any other additional costs to be incurred in relation to the funeral (for example, headstones or totem poles) which can be included as a funeral expense. Costs of this nature will need to be confirmed and receipts/invoices lodged by the person acting on the customer's behalf
  • Names of any immediate relatives (partner or children) surviving the customer, if the information is not readily available on their Centrelink record
  • Account details where residual funds are to be paid or invoices for payment

Procedure ends here.

7

Auto disbursement of residual funds + Read more ...

When the Notification of Death guided procedure is completed, the following updates are made automatically:

  • All regular expenses will end immediately
  • If the customer had an active BasicsCard, the account will be closed and any balance re-credited to the customer's Income Management account
  • The Income Management account balance will be paid immediately as a lump sum to the customer's (or their payment nominee's) last known payment destination
  • If the death occurred during a period of disbursement (that is, customer was having funds paid out in instalments), the remaining balance will be paid immediately as a lump sum to the customer's (or their payment nominee's) last know payment destination
  • Work Item 'I037Q1 - Death action on INM record. IMPACT action required' will be created. IMPACT will investigate expenses paid to Third Party Organisations (TPO) within the last 12 weeks to determine whether a recall of any funds is required

After the balance has been paid out, any subsequent credits into the Income Management account will remain in the account and will require follow up by IMPACT to determine the most appropriate disbursement method. Work Item I103PI customer deceased. IMPACT Team action only is generated to alert IMPACT of such an event.

Have funds been automatically disbursed?

  • Yes:
    • Procedure ends here
    • Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Residual funds cannot be automatically disbursed + Read more ...

Follow up is required by the Income Management Payments and Contact Team (IMPACT) if the deceased customer's residual Income Management funds cannot be automatically disbursed because:

  • funds have been returned from a financial institution due to the customer's account being closed, or
  • Services Australia has been advised the customer's bank account has been closed

Send an ACTDOC to IMPACT (region code IMT) requesting disbursement of residual funds be followed up.

Procedure ends here.

9

Debts due to late notification of death + Read more ...

For the Income Management Payments and Contact Team (IMPACT) only

Where auto disbursement of residual funds has not occurred, any funds incorrectly credited to a customer's Income Management account can be used to offset a debt incurred due to the late notification of death if:

  • funds have been credited to a customer's Income Management account after their date of death
  • a debt has been raised, and
  • funds are still available in the Income Management account

The Debt Recovery Team will send an email to IMPACT requesting any available incorrectly credited funds be used to offset the debt. See 'Existing Centrelink debts' in Table 5 on the Process page of Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management.

10

Disbursement of residual funds when LPR is not known + Read more ...

For the Income Management Payments and Contact Team (IMPACT) only

If residual funds cannot be automatically disbursed and details of the Legal Personal Representative (LPR) are not known, IMPACT will send an 'Income Management Deceased Customer Referral Report' to the positional mailbox of the relevant Zone for follow up:

The zone will advise IMPACT how funds are to be disbursed as instructed by the LPR or appropriate person/s finalising the estate or affairs of the customer (which may be a family member). Disbursement may include one or more of the following:

  • Payment to the LPR's bank account
  • Payment to Third Party Organisations (TPO) in settlement of the customer's outstanding bills, after sighting invoices
  • Payment for expenses directly related to the funeral of the customer (this does not include expenses incurred by people attending the funeral). This may be paid directly to the TPO or reimbursement to a person who initially paid those fees where evidence is sighted
  • Payment to a person who is 'carrying out appropriate activities' in relation to the customer's estate, which may include family members

Record details on a DOC.

Note:

  • If the Zone has exhausted all avenues of locating a customer's LPR or appropriate person/s finalising the estate, they can request IMPACT transfer the undisbursed funds to a General Ledger account, or
  • If a response is not received from the Zone, IMPACT can make a determination to transfer the funds to the General Ledger account

For more information, see Transferring undisbursed funds to the General Ledger Account. A valid claimant can still access these funds at a later date.
