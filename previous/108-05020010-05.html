Income protection or sickness accident policies - not paid from superannuation

Lump sum arrears payments

From 7 December 2020
  • Payment is apportioned for the period it represents up to a maximum of 52 weeks from the date the customer is entitled to receive the payment. That is, if the lump sum payment is for a 6 month period it is apportioned over 26 weeks
  • Example, on 9 December 2020, James received a lump sum payment from his sickness and accident insurance policy of $5,000 for the period 1 January 2020 to 3 March 2020 (9 weeks). The $5,000 is apportioned and assessed as income from 9 December 2020 for a period of 9 weeks

Pre 7 December 2020

Lump sum arrears were assessed as income in the fortnight of receipt, when:

  • the customer was not able to advise the details of the arrears payment from an income protection or sickness accident policy until they received it, and
  • the lump sum was not received until after the claim was finalised
  • Example: On 9 October 2020, James received a payment from his sickness and accident insurance policy of $5,000 for the period 1 January 2020 to 3 March 2020. The $5,000 was assessed as income for the entitlement period in which the payment was received

Only in cases where the customer notified of the lump sum payment prior to receipt, was it apportioned across the past period it represented, that is,

  • when the customer knew the amount involved and the date of entitlement to the payment in advance
  • Example: On 3 June 2019, Peter was advised by his insurance company that on 30 June 2019 he would receive a payment of $10,000 for the period 1 January 2019 to 28 February 2019. The $10,000 payment was apportioned across the period from 1 January to 28 February 2019

Income protection or sickness accident policies - paid from superannuation

Lump Sum Arrears payments

TDP lump sum arrears
  • It is common for a member of a superannuation fund to receive a lump sum payment of arrears prior to commencing regular ongoing payments
  • The lump sum arrears are assessed as income from the date of notification from the fund that the TPD pension has been approved
  • Example: On 9 December 2019, Nick applies for TPD benefit from his superannuation fund. On 18 May 2020. Nick is advised by his fund that his claim is successful and he will receive a lump sum payment of $12,000 and ongoing payments of $475.00 per fortnight starting on 1 June 2020. On 25 may 2020 he receives a lump sum payment of $12,000 for the period 9 December 2019 to 17 May 2020 (24 weeks)..
    The ongoing payments are assessed as an income stream from 18 May 2020 (notification date)
    The lump sum payment is
    • apportioned over a 24 week period, and
    • assessed as income from 18 May 2020 for 24 weeks.

TTD lump sum arrears

When a lump sum arrears payment is made prior to commencing a regular ongoing income stream:

  • the assessable amount is the gross less any adjustment for offsets applied by the payer
  • it is:
    • assessed as income for the period it relates to, and
    • applied from the date the person lodged their claim for the income stream
  • Example: On 1 August 2017, Peter applies to his superannuation fund to start his income stream. Peter is eligible to receive his income stream from that date. The superannuation fund starts Peter's income stream on 1 October 2017 and pays him an arrears lump sum of $4,000 for the period from 1 August to 30 September. The $4,000 is assessed as income from 1 August to 30 September because Peter's income stream date of claim is a notifiable event
