Bank account details provided on Bank Account Details Collection form

Table 2: this table outlines what to do when a customer provides bank account details on a Bank Account Details Collection form.

Step

Action

1

Check form is completed and signed + Read more ...

Has the form been completed and signed by the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Form not completed + Read more ...

If the form is:

  • Not completed, go to Step 3
  • Signed by a third party (except for Power of Attorney, Guardianship, Authorised Representative or Administration orders), the form must not be processed. Send form back to the customer together with letter z0834 Bank account details registration – could not be processed. Procedure ends here

Note: a customer's signature does not need to be legible to be acceptable. Customers can use any type of symbol as their signature, for example, an X. Forms must not be processed where there is a clear difference in names. For example, customer's name is Smith, but the form is signed Jones.

Bank account details provided on a Medicare claim form

Table 3: this table outlines what to do when a customer provides bank account details on a Medicare Claim form.

Step

Action

1

Check form completed + Read more ...

Has the form been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, see Incomplete or incorrect Medicare claims forms. Procedure ends here

2

Key bank account details + Read more ...

Key bank account details in NPBI screen to record either:

  • P - the bank account details are stored Permanently and overwrite previously stored bank account details in CDMS
  • T - the bank account details are stored Temporarily and are used for that claim only. Bank account details must be stored temporarily where the:
    • customer has indicated they are to be used for that claim only
    • details are for a business or organisation who do not hold financial management

Note: to update the bank account details for everyone on a Medicare card, the 'Bank Account Details Collection' form must be completed and signed by each individual aged 14 years and over.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, see the verbal bank account details provided table

Update other changes + Read more ...

Store form + Read more ...

Bank account details provided verbally or on a Medicare form

Table 5: this table describes what to do when a customer provides bank account details verbally, either over the phone or at a service centre, or where they are provided on a Medicare form and an error is displayed.

Step

Action

1

How is the customer providing their bank account details? + Read more ...

  • Over the phone or verbally in a service centre, go to Step 2
  • Using a Medicare form and an error is displayed indicating the bank account details provided are invalid. Attempt to call the customer at least once. If the customer:
    • can be contacted, go to Step 2
    • cannot be contacted, record the contact attempt, including phone number used, on the CDMS Personal tab and send the form back to the customer together with the appropriate standard letter. Procedure ends here

See the Resources page for a link to standard letters on the agency's intranet.

2

Public security check + Read more ...

Service Officers are to perform a public security check. See Authenticating a Medicare customer.

Has the customer passed the security check?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, ask them to provide their bank account details either by:
    • accessing their Medicare online account via myGov
    • using their Express Plus Medicare app
    • by attending a Service Centre with acceptable identification
    • by calling back later with the required information
    • by completing a Bank account details collection form
    • procedure ends here

The Resources page contains a link to the myGov website and the Bank account details collection form.

3

Security check passed + Read more ...

If the customer has been contacted or is contacting:

  • due to an error indicating the bank account details provided are invalid, go to Step 8
  • to provide their bank account details or are present at a service centre, go to Step 4

4

Privacy note + Read more ...

Service Officers on the general enquiries line are not required to read out the privacy note, see Privacy note and principles on the Background page for more information. Service Officers must tick the box under the Essential and Privacy information to store bank account details in CDMS.

All other Service Officers are to read the privacy note in CDMS under Essential and Privacy information to the customer and tick the box in CDMS to acknowledge the customer understands the privacy information.

The Resources page contains a copy of the privacy note.

Is the customer making a claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Customer making a claim for a Medicare benefit + Read more ...

If the customer is making a claim for a Medicare benefit:

  • key “NPBI, card number” and press [Enter]
  • go to Step 6

6

Update or record bank account details + Read more ...

Update or record bank account details in CDMS.

Service Officers must ask the customer to provide the following information and key their responses into CDMS in the appropriate fields:

  • BSB number
  • Account number
  • Account name

Confirm the following with the customer:

  • Repeat the BSB number, ask the customer to confirm these details are correct
  • Repeat the account number, ask the customer to confirm these details are correct
  • Ask the customer 'Do you understand that giving false or misleading information is an offence?'

Service Officers must record comments in CDMS, confirming that:

  • the privacy note was either
    • read to the customer, or
    • not read to the customer as the call came through the general enquiry line
  • the customer accepted the bank declaration, and
  • the customer confirmed their BSB and account number
  • Select Validate, OK, Validate, Update to confirm details updated

Note: it is critical to ensure the correct person has been selected when updating bank account details within CDMS. These details are linked to the individual and not the card.

If the customer is making a claim, key bank account details in NPBI screen as appropriate, either:

  • T (temporary)
  • P (permanent)

Can the bank account details be successfully stored?

  • Yes, and they are:
    • not making a claim, procedure ends here
    • are making a claim, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Complete Medicare claim + Read more ...

Continue assessing the Medicare claim, see Claims processing in Medicare for assistance.

Where a customer is making a claim at a service centre, the bank account details are included on the printed receipt.

Service Officers must circle the bank account details and ask the customer to check the bank account details before signing the printed office receipt.

Process ends here.

8

Bank account details not able to be successfully stored + Read more ...

Advise the customer that the system is unable to accept the bank account details due to the account:

  • being with a certain financial institution, for example, credit union
  • having restrictions on Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT) deposits
  • being a credit card account
  • being an overseas account

Ask the customer whether their financial institution has changed its BSB or if they wish to provide alternative bank account details.

Do they want to supply new details?

  • Yes, return to Step 5
  • No, advise customer valid details are required for payment of a claim.

Where the claimant does not have valid EFT details stored in CDMS or cannot provide valid temporary or permanent EFT details at the time the claim is submitted, the payment for the claim will be held until EFT details have been provided to Medicare.

Bank account details provided on Public/State Trustee letter for FMO customers

Table 6: this table describes what to do when a Public/State Trustee, organisation or administration customer is under a Financial Management Order and provides bank account details.

Step

Action

1

Check the letter is complete + Read more ...

Does the letter include the following?

  • Trustee letterhead
  • Trustee reference
  • Full name and DOB of the customer
  • Financial Management Order

Does the letter contain all of the above details?

  • No, got to Step 2
  • Yes, got to Step 3

2

Letter not complete + Read more ...

If the letter is incomplete:

  • send back to the customer with the letter z0834 Bank account details registration – could not be processed

Procedure ends here.

3

Record or update bank account details + Read more ...

Record (or update) bank account details in CDMS.

Key:

  • BSB code
  • Account number
  • Account name, Ref
  • Select Medicare Enrolment Form under the Notification Source
  • Add comments to Personal tab: bank details added from Public Trustees notification

Record confirmation in the customer's personal record in CDMS:

  • Scroll to the end of the Essential and Privacy information
  • Tick, if the application has acknowledged they understand the essential privacy information
  • Validate, Validate, Update

Procedure ends here.
