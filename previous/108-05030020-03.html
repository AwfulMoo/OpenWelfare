Annual income estimates

Table 1: This table describes the assisted processing instructions for updating income estimates in the current financial year.

Step

Action

1

Advising annual income estimate + Read more ...

Some customers do not need to provide an estimate. See Effect of income support entitlement on Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for help if needed.

An income estimate and/or FTB payment choice may be updated at any time during the financial year:

  • via self service:
    • logging into their Centrelink online account via myGov
    • using the Access to Centrelink self service – set up, checking, locking and resetting
    • telephone self service, or
  • in writing via Request for confirmation of family income details 2022-2023 financial year Family Tax Benefit/Child Care Subsidy (FA078). Note: the FA078 can only be issued to customers via the Family Income and Choices workflow. Customers must be FTB-CUR, CAN-OIS or CAN-ENR to be issued a FA078
  • in writing via a requested Partner Details (MOD P) form, where the relationship status of an ISP customer who also receives FTB and/or CCS has changed from single to partnered

If the customer provides a reasonable estimate, record it even though it may not be used to assess the customer's FTB rate.

Customers must estimate their income as accurately as possible. They:

  • must not be encouraged to 'over-estimate' to avoid overpayment
  • must not be encouraged to leave an under-estimate and choose a payment choice
  • must estimate their Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) components as accurately as possible so their entitlements are correctly assessed, and
  • must be offered an FTB payment choice to avoid any potential overpayment

Discuss FTB payment choices when updating income estimates. Give customers the option of choosing FTB at nil rate or claiming FTB as a lump sum each financial year.

Do not encourage customers to choose FTB at nil rate to reduce the number of FTB lump sum claims lodged.

Only apply a payment choice with the FTB customer's informed consent. Customers can view or update their payment choice at any time via the self service options, phone self service for FTB, Express Plus Centrelink mobile app or the Payment Choice online option. See Payment choices for FTB

Check the benefit status line:

  • If FTB is cancelled, go to Step 2
  • If FTB is current go to Step 3
  • If CCS only customer, see Step 1 in Table 4

FTB is cancelled + Read more ...

Where the new estimate provided is reasonable and under the income test for FTB Part A or FTB Part B:

Service Officers not trained in families restorations

Transfer the person to the Families and Parenting queue to complete the manual update. Procedure ends here.

Families and Parenting Service Officers

  • Where the customer is receiving CCS, make sure the correct DOV is used when updating the income estimate, as backdating DOV will also affect CCS. For more information see Date of event (DOV) for family assistance income estimates
  • If FTB is CAN-OIS in the current financial year, FTB can be restored using the Family Income and Choices workflow. Go to Step 3
  • If FTB is CAN-ENR in the current financial year, FTB must be manually restored, go to Step 1 of the Manual processing instructions table
  • If FTB was cancelled in the previous financial year or cancelled in the current financial year for a reason other than CAN-OIS or CAN-ENR, and they wish to commence receiving FTB as an instalment payment, record the estimate and payment choice manually. See Step 1 of the Manual processing instructions table

Note: if FTB is cancelled and a manual restoration is not appropriate (for example, due to income estimate, cancellation period or customers request), clearly DOC the record stating why the payment was not restored. Depending on when/if the customer wishes to re-commence receiving instalment payments, they may have to lodge a new claim. Procedure ends here.

Continue workflow - Current Financial Year Income Estimate Update + Read more ...

This page shows messages to be read to the customer to help them provide an accurate income estimate. More information is available about each income component to help in the discussion.

Record details for each income component. Indicate if the person (or their partner) will get any of the income components in the financial year. If yes, code the amount estimated by them.

Partnered income support payment (ISP) customers

For partnered ISP/FTB mutual customers, the following cannot be recorded for the ISP recipient:

  • a $0 estimate or
  • an estimate that is less than the ISP amount paid year to date

This ensures that the ISP is included in the estimate and reduces the risk of an FTB overpayment.

Is the customer requesting that their taxable income estimate be less than their taxable ISP year to date figure because when they lodge their tax return, business or investment loss will reduce their taxable income?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 8

6

Partnered income support customers + Read more ...

Customer requests their taxable income estimate be less than their taxable ISP year to date figure because when they lodge their tax return, business/investment loss reduces their taxable income.

Tell the customer:

  • while they receive an income support payment, the family income estimate will not reduce their amount of FTB Part A, however the income estimate can reduce the Part B rate
  • any business or investment loss for FTB purposes will be included when tax returns are lodged and FTB is balanced after the end of the financial year, and
  • to receive their full annual FTB entitlement, they must meet income lodgement requirements within 12 months of the financial year ending

If the customer’s Part B rate is reduced due to income and they request the business/investment loss is applied to their ongoing FTB Part B rate, check for evidence of the business loss on the Real Estate and Business (REBS) screen.

Is the loss clear on the REBS screen:

  • Yes, or credible evidence has been provided, go to Step 7
  • No,
    • Losses for ISP purposes and taxation purposes can differ
    • Before the loss can be applied to their ongoing rate of FTB, the customer will need to provide evidence of their expected loss. This may be a recent profit and loss statement or evidence of a previous year loss that will apply to the current financial year
    • Request evidence from the customer and DOC the discussion. The customer can use the Upload documents service to provide evidence. Once they have provided evidence, the customer will need to re-contact
    • The customer may also need to have their business income updated for their ISP. See Assessing and coding the Business details for sole traders and partnerships Mod F
    • Procedure ends here

7

Referral to Helpdesk + Read more ...

Advise the customer:

  • by including the potential taxation loss to their FTB taxable income estimate before the end of the financial year, they will have an increased risk of FTB overpayment should their business profit improve, and
  • balancing of FTB will take longer as it will require additional checking

If the customer would still like the loss included to their ongoing rate of FTB, refer a request to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk with the following information:

  • 'Customer has requested FTB rate adjusted to account for business/investment loss; however an estimate lower than ISP year to date cannot be recorded. The customer is aware of the risk of including the potential loss before the end of the financial year'. Include:
    • Customer CRN
    • Who the estimate is for(customer and/or partner)
    • Amount of loss the customer expects over the course of the financial year
    • ISP year to date amount
    • Evidence used

The helpdesk will make a decision on each individual case and provide instruction to the referring Service Officer to:

  • finalise the assessment, and
  • contact the customer with the outcome

See Resources for a link to the helpdesk.

Estimate is not reasonable + Read more ...

The customer has provided a verbal estimate that is unreasonable.

  • Discuss the estimate immediately with them and request the estimate be changed
  • If they provide a new reasonable estimate, go to Step 10
  • If they refuse to change the estimate, use the Family Income and Choices workflow to record the estimate as unreasonable and finalise the workflow

Smart Centre Processing

The customer has provided an FA078 or MOD P form with an unreasonable estimate.

Make 1 attempt to contact them on the phone number(s) provided. If phone contact is:

  • made with them, discuss the estimate and request they change the estimate:
    • If they refuse to change the estimate, use the Family Income and Choices workflow to record the estimate as unreasonable and finalise the review
    • If they provide a new reasonable estimate, go to Step 10
  • not made with them, use the Family Income and Choices workflow to record their estimate as being unreasonable and finalise the review

The person's FTB payments will automatically cancel. A letter will be generated advising them of the reason for cancellation and requesting they contact Services Australia to discuss further.

If the customer is receiving CCS, Service Officers are not to update the customer’s estimate if it is assessed as unreasonable. Record a Note detailing the estimate advised and that it was not recorded as it was unreasonable. If evidence is provided to support the new estimate, it can be updated if it is at least equal to or greater than any ISP payments made financial year to date.

Do not cancel CCS if an estimate is unreasonable. Service Officers must report suspected fraud when they believe the customer is providing false or misleading information to fraudulently increase their entitlement. The Resources page has a link to the Fraud intranet home page and reporting form.

  • if after the end of the financial year they (or their partner) are not needed to lodge a tax return, they must advise this online in the app or by phone

Finalise workflow + Read more ...

Record any additional notepad entries.

On the Assessment Confirmation page, click Continue to display the Assessment Results (AR) page.

Check the results to make sure payment outcomes are as expected.

The workflow will automatically produce a DOC. If the ‘Do not restore’ option is selected within the workflow, annotate the DOC to provide the reason for not restoring FTB.

Finalise the activity.

Tell the customer of their new ongoing FTB rate.

If FTB reduces to zero due to income (CZR-ITH or CZR-IEU), tell the customer that:

  • FTB has reduced to a zero rate due to income, however the payment remains current
  • they must notify of any changes to their family circumstances so their information is up to date when their entitlement is checked after the end of the financial year
  • income must be confirmed by 30 June following the end of the financial year. This is done by lodging tax returns or advising if they (or their partner) do not need to lodge
  • if the customer has a current withholding arrangement in place and FTB is reduced to nil due to income, an alternate repayment arrangement will need to be negotiated. See Procedures for arranging withholdings to recover debts
  • the balance if their advance payment (if any) will be recovered separately as a debt

If the customer is within an income support payment (ISP) employment income nil rate period and provides an estimate over the threshold to get payment, they will not cancel due to excess income (CAN-INC). Rather, their FTB will remain current at zero rate (CZR-INR).

If their family's financial situation changes, they may provide a revised estimate, which would apply from, the date advised. They may become entitled to FTB/CCS after the end of the financial year once actual income is known and FTB Supplements are included.

Check eligibility for income support and other products.

Does the customer get CCS?

  • No, procedure ends here

Customer advises an income estimate + Read more ...

The customer may provide estimates:

  • verbally
  • via the Request for confirmation of family income details 2022-2023 financial year form (FA078), or
  • via the Partner Details (MOD P) form

For Family Tax Benefit (FTB)/ Child Care Subsidy (CCS), an income estimate (or payment choice for FTB) may be updated at any time during the financial year, via self service, Centrelink online account through myGov, Express Plus Centrelink mobile app, or telephone self service. This includes customers with a status of FTB CAN-OIS or CAN-ENR.

Some customers do not need to provide an estimate. If a reasonable estimate is provided, it must be recorded even though it may not be used to assess their FTB rate.

  • They must estimate their Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) components as accurately as possible so their entitlements are correctly assessed. Offer the customer FTB payment choices to avoid any potential overpayment

Single income support payment customers affected by mandatory continuous adjustment may wish to provide a revised estimate in order to recalculate any amount withheld due to mandatory continuous adjustment. This estimate will:

  • not be used to assess their FTB unless they are in an Employment Income Nil Rate period
  • apply to Child Care Subsidy

If FTB is CAN-ENR (estimate not reasonable) or the Family Income and Choices workflow is not available, go to Step 2

5

Record estimate manually (continued) + Read more ...

From the FAO Income Task Selector (FITS) screen:

  • 'S'elect all FAO income estimate component screen(s) needed from FITS and press [Enter]. Note: if an amount is currently recorded for a component of Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI), it continues to be assessed unless updated (code $0 if the customer advises $0)
  • If a screen does not exist for the current financial year, code 'I'nsert in the Action: field and press [Enter]. Code the relevant financial year in the Financial Year: field

Code the following details in the customer/partner fields:

  • Event Date: field - Date of event (DOV) for family assistance income estimates. Note: if the customer also gets CCS and a backdated DOV is used, this may result in an arrears payment to the customer or a potential overpayment generating
  • Amt A$: field - code the estimated amount in Australian dollars (must be in whole dollars). Note: a $0 estimate cannot be recorded for income support payment customers. The estimate must include taxable income and tax-free pensions/benefits and other taxable income for the full financial year
  • Type: field - is the type of income supplied. Valid value is 'EST'imate
  • From: field - is the source of the estimate. Valid value is 'CUS'tomer

When all fields are updated, press [Enter] to go to the next selected screen. It may be necessary to press [Enter] twice to get the system to update the estimate.

For more information, see Assessment of adjusted taxable income for family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payments.

Customer advises an income estimate + Read more ...

4

Recording updates to income estimate + Read more ...

Navigate to the FAO Taxable Income (FTI) table by entering FTI in the Super Key field.

Select to record the income component, and complete the Create FAO Taxable Income page:

  • Start Date field: the system will default to today’s date, use the date the customer notified of the change
  • Income Amount field: enter the amount of the income component to be updated in whole dollars
  • Financial Year field: select the relevant year from the dropdown menu. For example, select 2023 for the 2022-23 financial year
  • Income Type field: select the income component from the dropdown menu:
    • Taxable income
    • Exempt Reportable Fringe Benefits
    • Other Reportable Fringe Benefits
    • Foreign income
    • Deductable Child Maintenance expenditure
    • Total Net Investment Losses
    • Tax free pensions/benefits
    • Reportable superannuation contributions
    • Tax exempt foreign income
      Discuss all income components recorded on the FTI table with the customer.
      If an income component is already recorded for $1 or more, and the revised amount is now $0, a new row is to be created with the income amount entered as ‘0’. Service Officers are not to edit or delete the existing line.
      Note: Service Officers do not need to record ‘0’ income for components which haven’t been recorded previously or are already recorded as ‘0’
  • Income source field: will default to Customer Provided (no change needed)
  • Income Status field: will default to Estimated Income (no change needed)
  • Select Save
  • Receipt date field: date of event
  • Channel field: select the appropriate reason for the request from the drop down list
  • Select Save

The new row created in the update will be used to re-assess the customer’s CCS percentage entitlement.

Note: if the customer is partnered, select the partner from the Relations menu to update their income details.

Once all required income components have been updated:

  • Select Assess
  • Check the outcome of the activity is correct on the Assessment Results (AR) screen and select Finish to complete the activity
  • For CCS purposes, the income estimate will take effect from the start of the next CCS fortnight
  • Tell the customer of their new CCS subsidy percentage as a result of their updated income estimate and the date this will take effect
  • If they have increased their estimate, tell the customer they can increase their CCS withholdings to reduce the risk of an overpayment at reconciliation
  • Go to Step 5
