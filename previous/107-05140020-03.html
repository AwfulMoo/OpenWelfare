Refer Work Items administered by specialist teams + Read more ...

Some Work Items are administered by specialist teams.

  • Refer the Work Item directly to the team where possible
  • Alternatively, email Workforce Strategy, Analytics & Reporting (WSAR). WSAR will allocate the Work Item to the specialist team to action

Use the debt benefit type, debt status, and/or repayment to identify Work Items requiring referral.

These include:

  • NRR - Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS):
    • refer NRR Work Items to the SRSS Team by re-categorising the Work Item to ZIAM_NRR_OVR
    • if unable to re-categorise, email the CRN and Work Item ID to WSAR
  • FHA - Farm Household Allowance (FHA):
    • refer FHA Work Items by emailing the CRN and Work Item ID to WSAR
  • AGD - Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP):
    • refer AGD Work Items by emailing the CRN and Work Item ID to WSAR
  • JB1 and JB2 - Job Commitment Bonus (JCB):
    • refer JB1 and JB2 Work Items by emailing the CRN and Work Item ID to WSAR
  • XGD - New Zealand Ex-Gratia Allowance:
    • refer XGD Work Items by emailing the CRN and Work Item ID to WSAR
  • CCS - Child Care Subsidy (CCS):
  • Service delivery partner DMOs will need to refer CCS Work Items to APS DMOs due to delegation limitations. Email the CRN and Work Item ID to WSAR
  • Any debt benefit type with a 'Civil Action' status:
    • debts with this status are case managed by Intensive Debt Collections (IDC) DMOs. Review the Display on Access (DOA) DOC. Refer the Work Item to the relevant team by adding the ISIS Activity Keyword to the Activity on the Activity List (AL) screen
  • DVA - Department of Veterans Affairs:
    • refer the Work Items which relate to a DVA debt or repayment by emailing the CRN and Work Item ID to WSAR

Check the Debt List (OPDL) screen to identify the benefit type for which the debt was raised, as well as the debt status.

Is the Work Item administered by a specialist team (which is not the Debt Management Officer's own team)?

  • Yes, refer the Work Item. The Resources page contains referral details
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Refer Work Items relating to Bankruptcy + Read more ...

Take care with Work Items which relate to Bankruptcy and Debt Agreements. Refer the Work Item to a Bankruptcy Officer in the Collections Administration Team if the repayment is a:

  • dividend
  • repayment on a debt written off under:
    • Bankruptcy Pending (BRP)
    • Bankruptcy Awaiting Estate (BRT)
    • Bankruptcy Discharged, No Further Estate/No Fraud (BRD), or
    • Debt Agreement Current (DAC)

Check the following screens to identify the debt repayment and write off status:

  • Debt List (OPDL)
  • Arrangement List (OPAL)
  • Repayment History (OPRH)
  • Write - off (OPWO)

Note: on occasion a garnishee payment will show on a record where there is no garnishee arrangement. These payments are dividends paid by an Administrator due to a bankruptcy or debt agreement. They are coded as a garnishee due to a system limitation. Refer these to a Bankruptcy Officer.

Is the repayment a dividend, or is the repayment on a debt written off under Bankruptcy?

  • Yes, refer the Work Item to a Bankruptcy Officer. The Resources page contains referral details
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Consider the transfer reason + Read more ...

Check the OPDL screen. Over recovered debts display as a negative balance and have a status of D-Over recovered.

  • If the debt is over recovered, go to Step 4
  • If an identified system issue resulted in the repayment incorrectly applying to one debt when it should be on another debt, go to Step 11

4

Over - recovered - Investigate the over recovered debt + Read more ...

View the following screens to identify previous actions which relate to the over recovered debt:

  • Document List (DL)
  • Payment Summary (PS)
  • Debt List (OPDL)
  • Debt Action Summary (OPAS)
  • Debt Amount (OPDA)
  • Repayment History (OPRH):
    • calculate the repayments
    • the repayments should equal the debt amount (original, adjusted or reconciled) and the over recovered amount
    • for example, if the debt amount is $500 and the debt is over recovered by $100, the repayments should equal $600

Note: some debts are raised in Process Direct (PD), such as Child Care Subsidy (CCS):

  • The debt will display on the OPDL screen in DMIS
  • The over recovered amount and repayments will not display in DMIS
  • Instead, view the Repayments screen in PD (access the record > select the Debts icon > view the Debt List > select the Repayments icon)

5

Over - recovered - Identify other outstanding debts + Read more ...

Check the OPDL screen. It is possible to transfer an over recovered amount to another debt to reduce the outstanding balance of that debt.

Are there are other outstanding determined debts?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Over recovered - identify other pending Family Assistance Office (FAO) debts + Read more ...

It is possible to hold an over recovered Work Item to allow the FAO Non-Lodger or Reconciliation process to finalise.

For example there may be a:

  • potential Family Tax Benefit Non Lodger Debt (FTB NLD), or
  • potential Family Tax Benefit Reconciliation Debt (FTB FRC)

To identify a potential FTB NLD:

  • on the OPDL screen, check that the:
    • debt benefit type of the over recovered debt is FTB
    • debt reason for the over recovered debt is FRR, FRC or FRA
  • 'S'elect the over recovered FTB debt
  • in the Nxt field, key OPAOD
  • press [Enter]
  • on the OPAOD screen, check the financial year of the debt
  • in the Nxt field, key FRCS
  • press [Enter]
  • read the displayed message and press [Enter] again
  • on the FRCS screen, enter the relevant financial year in the Financial year: field
  • press [Enter]
  • review this screen to identify if the most recent calculation status is Potential Non Lodger with an 'incomplete' result

To identify a potential FTB FRC:

  • on the OPDL screen, check that the over recovered debt is an FTB NLD
  • in the Select View field, key PRK (or key OPDLP in the Nxt: field)
  • press [Enter]
  • review this screen to identify parked or held FTB FRC debts:
    • disregard the FTB FRC debt if it has a QTN status (do not transfer to a Quarantined debt)
  • if no debts display, the customer has no parked or held debts. A message will also display at the top of the screen to advise the customer has no parked or held debts

To identify a recently zeroed NLD, indicating a potential FTB FRC:

  • on the OPDL screen, check that the over recovered debt is an FTB NLD
  • 'S'elect the FTB NLD debt
  • in the Nxt field, key OPDA
  • press [Enter]
  • debt adjustments will display with a reason, amount and date. More than one debt adjustment can occur on a debt
  • review this screen to identify if the FTB NLD was zeroed in the previous 7 days

Is there a potential FTB NLD or FTB FRC?

  • Yes, and there is a:
    • potential FTB NLD, go to Step 7
    • potential FTB FRC, go to Step 8
  • No, there are no other outstanding or pending debts, see Refunding debt repayments

7

Over - recovered - Hold the Work Item pending the Potential Non Lodger process + Read more ...

If there is a Potential Non-Lodger with an 'incomplete' result:

  • record a DOC using the Refund Held - Potential Non Lodger Fast Note
  • hold the Work Item (via the Activity List (AL) screen) until the to 20 October of the current year

Procedure end here.

8

Over - recovered - Hold the Work Item pending the FAO Reconciliation process + Read more ...

Holding the Work Item instead of refunding will reduce recovery re-work. The DMO should only hold for one 7 day period.

View the DL screen. Check to see if there is a DOC advising that the Work Item was previously held pending the FAO reconciliation process.

Does the record indicate that the Work Item was previously held for 7 (or more) days?

  • Yes, see Refunding Debt Repayments
  • No:
    • hold the Work Item for 7 days
    • record why the Work Item was held on a DOC
    • procedure ends here

9

Check the debt hierarchy + Read more ...

The debt repayment hierarchy determines the order in which debts are repaid when a customer has more than one outstanding debt.

View the order in which repayments will be applied to each individual debt:

  • go to the OPDL screen
  • in the Select View field, key 'ILY'
  • press [Enter]
  • review and transfer as per the debt payment hierarchy:
    • a number will display on the far right side of each debt, indicating the order of hierarchy
    • if there are 3 debts numbered from 1 to 3, transfer to debt number 1 first and then to debt number 2

10

Identify if recovery is suspended on the other debt(s) + Read more ...

Consider the debt payment hierarchy reviewed in Step 9.

It may not be suitable to transfer a repayment to a temporarily or permanently written off debt.

Go to the OPDL screen. Check the status for the debts that the repayments will go towards.

Are any of the debts written off?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 12

11

Identify if it is suitable to transfer to the written off debt + Read more ...

It is suitable to transfer repayments to debts with the following write off codes:

  • Deceased Awaiting Estate (DDE)
  • Deceased Insufficient Estate (DEC)
  • In Prison (PRI)
  • Not Cost Effective (NCE)
  • Outcome of Review/Appeal (ORA)
  • Overseas (DOS)
  • Whereabouts Unknown (WUN)
  • Exclude from Tax Garnishee Process (TAX)

Carefully consider the customers circumstances before transferring repayments to debts with the following write off codes:

  • Short Term Hardship (STH)
    • suitable to transfer except where there is evidence on the record indicating that the customer is experiencing real financial hardship due to exceptional and unforeseen circumstances
  • Debt Agreement - Current (DAC)
    • the administrator must be advised of the debt amount changing. Refer this Work Item to a Bankruptcy Officer
  • Disaster Temporary Write Off (DIS)
    • suitable to transfer except where there is evidence on the record indicating that the customer is experiencing real financial hardship due to exceptional and unforeseen circumstances
  • Bankruptcy Pending (PRP)
    • refer this Work Item to a Bankruptcy Officer
  • Non-Lodger Separated (NLS)
    • suitable to transfer except where there is evidence on the record indicating that the customer is experiencing real financial hardship due to exceptional and unforeseen circumstances. See Table 1, step 16 of Investigating a Refund of debt repayments for further information

It is not suitable to transfer repayments to debts with the following write off codes:

  • Outcome of Review/Appeal (ORA) - stay orders only
  • Bankruptcy Awaiting Estate (BRT)
  • Bankruptcy Discharged, No Further Estate/No Fraud (BRD)
  • Insolvency - Temporary (IRT)
  • Insolvency - Discharged (IRD)
  • Irrecoverable At Law (IAL)
  • Statute Barred (STB)

Is it suitable to transfer the repayment to the written off debt?

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, and there:
    • is another suitable debt to transfer the repayment, go to Step 12
    • are no other suitable debts to transfer the repayment, see Refunding Debt Repayments

12

Review the repayment source, debt type and transfer rules + Read more ...

DMOs do not have the authority to transfer all repayment sources from one debt (original) to another debt (destination).

Check the OPDL screen to identify the benefit type of the original debt.

Check the original debt's OPRH screen to identify the repayment source(s) to transfer.

Check the OPDL screen to identify the benefit type of the destination debt.

Check the Transfer Rules Table on the Resources page to identify the relevant rule.

Note: some debts are raised in Process Direct (PD), such as Child Care Subsidy (CCS). The debt will display on the OPDL screen in DMIS. The over recovered amount and repayments will not display in DMIS. Instead, view the Repayments screen in PD (access the record > select the Debts icon > view the Debt List > select the Repayments icon).

Can the repayment be transferred?

  • Yes, and the customer's permission is:
    • needed, go to Step 13
    • not needed, go to Step 14
  • No, there are no suitable debts to transfer the payment to, see Refunding Debt Repayments

13

Obtain permission from the customer + Read more ...

Make a reasonable effort to contact the customer. Record attempts to contact the customer and the outcome on a DOC. It can be in a single DOC, or by annotating the transfer or refund DOC.

If the contact attempt is successful, ask the customer if they approve the transfer.

Does the customer approve?

  • Yes, go to Step 14
  • No, see Refunding Debt Repayments

If the contact attempt is unsuccessful, refund the amount. See Refunding Debt Repayments.

14

Identify which process to follow + Read more ...

Identify which system the original debt was raised in and which system the destination debt was raised in. This will either be the Debt Management Information System (DMIS) or Process Direct (PD). Transfer the repayment in the system that the original debt was raised in.

Note: currently only Child Care Subsidy (CCS) debts are raised in Process Direct. Proceed in DMIS for all other debt benefit types.

To transfer the amount from the customer's:

  • DMIS debt to their other DMIS or PD debt, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • PD debt to their other DMIS or PD debt, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • DMIS debt to the third party's DMIS or PD debt, see Step 1 in Table 4

Transfer debt repayments in the Debt Management Information System (DMIS)

Table 2

Step

Action

1

View the Debt Transfer Details (OPRTD) screen in DMIS + Read more ...

In DMIS:

  • access the customer's record
  • view the OPDL screen
  • 'S'elect the debt that has a repayment for transfer
  • in the Nxt field, key OPRTD
  • press [Enter]
  • the Debt Transfer Details (OPRTD) screen for that debt will display

2

Update the OPRTD screen to identify the transfer action + Read more ...

The OPRTD screen will display options to transfer or refund a repayment.

On the OPRTD screen:

  • view the Debt ID to ensure it is the correct debt to transfer from
  • in the To DMIS Debt or Process Direct field, key Y
  • press [Enter]

3

Update the OPRTD screen to identify the destination debt ID + Read more ...

The destination debt ID (where the amount will be transferred to) can be keyed or selected.

  • To key:
    • in the To Debt field, key the destination debt ID
    • press [Enter]
    • go to Step 4
  • To select:
    • press [Enter] to navigate to the Debt Transfer List (OPTL) screen
    • select the destination debt
    • press [Enter] to navigate back to the OPRTD screen
    • go to Step 4

4

Update the OPRTD screen to identify the transfer details + Read more ...

On the OPRTD screen:

  • in the Transfer source type field, key one of the following to identify the repayment source:
    • WHH to transfer a withholdings repayment
    • TGN to transfer a tax garnishee repayment
    • ESS to transfer a repayment receipted via ESSentials (excluding tax garnishee)
    • ALL to transfer any repayment source (from WHH, TGN and ESS)
  • In the Amount field, key the amount to be transferred
  • In the Trnsfr Reason field, key the reason for the transfer

To confirm the OPRTD entries, press:

  • [Enter] to confirm source, amount and reason
  • [Enter] again to navigate to the Receipt Selection List (OPRSL) screen

5

View and update the OPRSL screen + Read more ...

The OPRSL screen will display the repayments (on that debt) in descending order from the latest receipt date. The selection of repayment sources is automatic based on the source type selected in Step 4. It is possible to change automatically selected sources.

On the OPRSL screen, view the automatically selected repayment source receipts.

Do the repayment source receipts need to be changed?

  • Yes, change the selections:
    • unselect the repayment source receipts
    • a message will appear advising 'selected receipts total less than amount requested'
    • select other repayment source receipts until the total meets the amount entered on OPRTD
    • press [Enter]
    • in the confirm field, key Y
    • press [Enter] to confirm and navigate back to the OPRTD screen
    • go to Step 6
  • No:
    • in the confirm field: key Y
    • press [Enter] to confirm and navigate back to the OPRTD screen
    • go to Step 6

6

Confirm the transfer + Read more ...

On the OPRTD screen:

  • in the confirm transfer details field: type Y
  • press [Enter]
  • the transferred repayment will now display on either the DMIS OPRH screen or the PD Repayments screen for the destination debt

7

Document the transfer + Read more ...

Record the details on a DOC. Multiple transfer and refund actions can be in one DOC. Include the following content:

  • Ext Detail field:
    • Transfer: From DMIS to DMIS, or
    • Transfer: From DMIS to PD
  • Content field:
    • the amount of the transfer
    • debt ID of the original and the destination debt
    • repayment source
    • details of any contact with the customer

Procedure ends here.

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Select the amount for reversal + Read more ...

If the repayment was transferred to a third party's debt, a reversal is not possible.

If the repayment was transferred to the customer's Process Direct debt, a reversal is only possible if it is still displaying as 'to send'.

If the repayment was transferred to the customer's DMIS debt, a reversal is possible.

  • access the customer's record
  • view the Debt List (OPDL) screen
  • 'S'elect the debt that has a repayment for reversal
  • press [Enter] to navigate to the OPRH screen

  • 'S'elect the negative value amount for reversal (the source code will be Blk to Oth Debt or Blk to PD debt)

On the OPRTD screen:

  • in the Review Selected Receipts field: key Y
  • press [Enter] to view the OPRSL screen
  • review the selected source receipts
  • press [Shift] and [F3] to view the OPRTD screen

3

Confirm reversal + Read more ...

On the OPRTD screen:

  • in the Confirm Reversal field, key Y
  • press [Enter] to view the OPRH screen
  • check the message 'reversal successfully complete' displays
  • ensure the reversal amount has changed to $0.00

Note: if the reversal is not correct, do not continue to make further adjustments and reversals. Seek assistance before continuing.
