Reasons for Nominee Arrangement to be coded

A voluntary (VOL) nominee arrangement is one where the customer has requested the appointment of a person or organisation to be their nominee.

An involuntary (INV) nominee arrangement is one where a court, guardianship board, administrative tribunal or Attorney-General's office has appointed a nominee to manage the care recipient's affairs. An involuntary arrangement may also be created through a Power of Attorney approved by a court when the customer has lost the capacity to manage their own affairs.

Each code is predetermined to be either a voluntary (VOL) or involuntary (INV) arrangement. The voluntary/involuntary requirement has a subsequent impact on whether advices are sent to both the care recipient and the nominee or just the nominee.

A nominee under an involuntary arrangement must specifically request letters are not sent to the care recipient.

Note: see Power of Attorney, Guardianship, Administrative Orders and Authorised Representative for Medicare for extra coding information.

POA and medical evidence - extra information

Table 4

Type

Evidence

General power of attorney (POA)

General POAs cease to have effect after the customer loses decision-making power.

If medical evidence (for example, a medical certificate), is provided to Services Australia with the POA document and it states the care recipient:

  • still has decision-making power but unable to sign due to a physical incapacity
    • accept the POA
  • has lost decision-making power/is mentally incapacitated
    • the POA is no longer effective
    • do not accept the POA

Enduring power of attorney (EPOA)

If an EPOA document states it is active regardless of the care recipient's decision-making capacity:

  • certificate of medical incapacity is not needed

If an EPOA document only comes into effect if the care recipient loses decision-making capacity:

  • Services Australia must receive medical evidence with the EPOA document. For example, a medical certificate

If the medical certificate states the care recipient:

  • still has decision-making power, and
  • unable to sign due to a physical incapacity
    • do not accept the POA
  • has lost decision-making power/is mentally incapacitated
    • accept the POA

Forms for the Aged Care program

Table 5

Form title

Description

Aged Care Appointment of a Nominee form (AC019) - AC019-1407 or lower

Aged care recipients (residential care or home care) used this form to authorise another person or organisation to act on their behalf with Services Australia as a nominee.

Aged Care Request for a nominee for Department of Veterans' Affairs customers (AC019) - AC019-2011 or higher

DVA aged care recipients (residential care or home care) or Aged care grandfathered nominees use this form to authorise another person or organisation to act on their behalf with Services Australia as a DVA aged care nominee.

Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf form (SS313) - SS313-2011 or higher

Residential care or home care recipients (excluding DVA aged care recipients) can use this form to authorise another person or organisation to enquire, act or update on their behalf for Centrelink payments and services including aged care.

Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care form

These forms are used to give the care recipient's income and/or assets details for an aged care means assessment.

Post 5 December 2020, care recipients can use the Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf form (SS313) section in these forms to authorise another person or organisation to act on their behalf with Services Australia as a nominee.

Note: pre 5 December 2020 Aged Care Appointment of a Nominee form (AC019) was used.
