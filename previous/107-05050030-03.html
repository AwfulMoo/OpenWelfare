Garnishee of compensation payment

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Check authority of debt + Read more ...

Check the authority of the debt on the General Information (OPGI) screen.

Does the customer have a debt raised under the 'Authority' PGPA/FMA?

See Recovery of Financial Management and Accountability Act 1997 (FMA) overpayments.

  • Yes, exclude debts raised under the PGPA or FMA Acts from the garnishee amount. Go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Contact the customer + Read more ...

Make a reasonable effort to contact the customer prior to garnishee.
Note: if leaving a message, see Safeguarding customer privacy when raising and recovering debts.

Can the customer be contacted by phone?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Seek approval + Read more ...

Seek approval from an APS5 or APS 6 in the Compensation Team.

DOC the approval on the record.

Go to Step 4.

4

Undertake the garnishee + Read more ...

Do not include the social security charge in the garnishee amount.

Points to consider before placing a garnishee.

On the Ongoing periodic payment screen:

  • Leave a minimum weekly balance for debtors when garnishing

A reasonable weekly balance for day to day living costs for debtors are:

  • Single and member of a couple - $750
  • Members of a family (more than two people) - $1200

Lump sum payments:

  • Consider 10% of the economic loss amount

The CRT can negotiate with the customer to recover an outstanding Income Support Benefit (ISB) and /or Clean Energy Advance (CEA) debt from their lump sum settlement, prior to the 28 days recovery period.

Record the discussion in a DOC.

Go to Step 5.

5

Manually update the customer's record + Read more ...

Go to the Activity List (AL) screen:

  • Key 'S' in the DMN/RCV 'No Arrangement' activity field
  • Press [Enter]
  • Access the (OPAL) screen, if there is no 'No Arrangement' activity
  • Press [Enter]
  • Key 'GRN' (for garnishee) in the Create new arrangement type: field. Press [Enter]

6

Complete the Garnishee Details (OPGD) screen + Read more ...

  • Use field help ('?') for assistance
  • Complete all fields
  • The total amount to be garnisheed (this may not be the balance outstanding) should be coded in the Notice Amount: field on the OPGD screen
  • Do not press [Enter] when coding is completed, press Home
  • in Nxt key 'LD', press [Enter]

Does the third party being issued with the garnishee notice recognise the customer by another name or alias?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 2 in Table 2

Customer aliases and finalising the garnishee

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Customer aliases + Read more ...

Include the customer's other known names or alias if:

  • they are recognised by another name and
  • the compensation payer does not recognise the customer's main name

When creating the garnishee notice, add the customer's alias:

  • In COMMAND: key 'DATA'
  • In CLIENT.FULL.NAME: insert the customer's alias after the customer's full name
  • On the Garnishee Details (OPGD) screen in Comments record the details of the customer's alias
  • Update the Customer Person Detail Summary (CPDS) screen if it does not show the alias

Record details of where this information was obtained on a Fast Note.

2

Finalise the garnishee + Read more ...

Produce the 'DRC - Lump sum or third party garnishee and advice to debtor' (Q442). See Creating an Online Advice (OLA), including within an existing activity.

When letter is complete:

  • Key 'OPAF' and press [Enter]
  • Key 'Y' in the Finished?: field and press [Enter]
  • Clearly explain the garnishee reason in a Fast Note

Once the activity is finalised, print the Q442 letters. Send the customer's copy to the last known address of the customer, including addresses that are end-dated.

Send the compensation payer the:

  • Q442 Garnishee letter
  • Compensation charge notice
  • Q921 cover letter

3

Check the tax record is correct + Read more ...

Where a charge or income debt has been recovered, ensure the customer's Payment Summary is adjusted and sent to the customer. For more information see Adjusting amounts on a Centrelink payment summary.

Is the matter urgent?

  • Yes go to Step 4
  • No, procedure ends here

4

Send a letter via desktop faxing + Read more ...

While in the customer's record in Customer First:

  • In Nxt key 'HS'
  • Select Continue, the History Summary (HS) screen will show
  • Key 'C' next to the letter to be printed
  • Press [Enter], the status of the letter will change to GE (Generated)
  • Key 'P' against the letter to desktop fax
  • Key 'Y' in the Final
  • In Printer key 'emailme', press [Enter]

Staff will get an email with the subject 'PDF of letter':

  • Open the email and select Forward
  • Delete all the text in the body and subject of the email
  • Do not delete the attachment
  • Key the fax number followed by @smtpfax.internal.dept.local, for example: 1300651387@smtpfax.internal.dept.local
  • Enter suitable Subject details, for example Charge Notice
  • Enter suitable message details. For example, the claim officer's name, the claim number and related message as per the fax header
  • Select Send
  • Select Email Classification OFFICIAL: Sensitive if sending customer details
  • The compensation payer will get:
    • a fax cover sheet, and
    • the attached letter
