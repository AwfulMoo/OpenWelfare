Request for a temporary card

Table 1: Participant is requesting a temporary cashless debit card.

Step

Action

1

CDC cardholder contacts or presents requesting a temporary card + Read more ...

A CDC participant and/or a CDC participant's payment nominee may contact to request a temporary card if:

  • they want to bring forward their CDC start date, or
  • they have commenced on the CDC before their personalised card has arrived, or
  • at any time they require a temporary card after their CDC start date, e.g. personalised card is lost, damaged or stolen

 

2

Confirm the cardholder does not have access to their personalised card   + Read more ...

Before issuing a temporary card, if the cardholder advised they have lost their card, ensure it is lost and not just misplaced.

There is no limit to the number of temporary cards a CDC participant can be issued.

Cardholder should be reminded that:

  • the personalised card will have the participant's name on it and, if applicable, their nominee's name
  • it has their BSB and account details on the back of the card
  • the temporary card does not include any of this information
  • they need to keep their personalised card safe and PIN secure
  • the personalised card is not disposable and should be kept
  • if they are issued a temporary card, any direct debits they had set up using their previous card details will cease to be paid (arrangements set up with BSB and Account Number will remain in place), and
  • if they locate the previous card, it will no longer be active

Does the cardholder have access to their personalised card?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Cardholder knows where their personalised card is + Read more ...

If the cardholder can still access their personalised card, a temporary card should not be issued. Issuing a temporary card will close the active personalised card.

Advise the cardholder they will need to continue to use their personalised card. If they are in hardship and need access to funds urgently while they do not have access to their card, see Transferring Cashless Debit Card (CDC) funds.

Note: if the cardholder is still waiting on their initial or personalised replacement card, issuing a temporary card will not affect the inactive card.

Procedure ends here.

4

Customer contact channel + Read more ...

For Service Centre Staff and Remote Servicing Teams Only, go to Table 2

For Remote Servicing Team where there is no connectivity, go to Table 4

For CDC Hotline Staff, this includes where a customer is calling from an Agent Site, go to Step 5.

5

Temporary card availability + Read more ...

Participants contacting via phone to enquire about a temporary card should be advised where they can access temporary cards.

Indue:

  • all service centres throughout Australia
  • participating Agents in the Northern Territory and Cape York
  • selected Australia Post Outlets and Local Partners

TCU:

  • all Northern Territory service centres, and some select bordering service centres
  • participating Agents in the Northern Territory
  • TCU Branches

Participants contacting from a participating Agent site requesting a temporary card should be advised to ask the Agent Specified Personnel for a temporary card. The participant will need to contact the card issuer to activate the card.

If the temporary card has already been activated and the participant wants to bring forward their CDC start date, see Cashless Debit Card (CDC) - changing initial waiting periods.

Confirming customer identity and card issuer

Table 2: How to confirm the participant's identity and their card issuer

Step

Action

1

Confirm participant or authorised payment nominee identity + Read more ...

Confirm the participant's or authorised payment nominee's identification before proceeding with any card activation workflows. It is essential that this step is carried out thoroughly and carefully to reduce the risk of issuing funds to third parties in error.

If issuing a temporary card to the participant’s payment nominee, request the nominee provide the recorded password stored on the Nominee Relationship Summary screen (NORS) to confirm their relationship in addition to confirming their identity.

Issuing a temporary card to a third party in error can result in hardship for the participant and financial loss to Services Australia.

If in any doubt it is not the participant and/or they are being taken advantage of, do not issue the temporary card and consult a social worker, or the Welfare Quarantining Programme Management team for further assistance.

Extra checks to ensure a temporary card should be issued to a CDC participant

Check:

  • the person’s ID
  • any other supporting ID checks
  • any Display on Access (DOA) DOCs or other DOCs, which indicate that there are financial vulnerabilities or potential fraud occurrences against the participant and ensure the temporary card is being issued to the correct participant
  • if necessary make contact with someone in the local community to confirm that the participant has travelled into town
  • where the card was last used

Note: issuing a card to a third party will provide access to the participant's quarantined fund. This could result in hardship. If a service officer has concerns someone is fraudulently trying to access another participant’s account they should refer to the department’s fraud processes. See Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption.

To issue a current CDC participant or payment nominee with a temporary card, go to Step 2.

2

Check the participant is currently on the CDC + Read more ...

If the customer is Income Managed, do not issue a temporary card.

Income managed customers who have consented to transition to the CDC and are within their initial waiting period may request a temporary card to enable them to bring forward their CDC start date. See Cashless Debit Card (CDC) - changing initial waiting periods.

To confirm if the participant is on the CDC, check the Debit Card Trial Summary screen (Q2SU) in Customer First or the Cashless Debit Card Summary screen (CDCS) in Process Direct:

  • If the status is On DCT the participant is currently on the CDC.
  • This screen will also confirm their quarantining percentage and their CDC account details.

To confirm that the participant is in their initial waiting period check Q2AS in Customer First:

  • The screen will display an information message stating the date the customer is due to commence on the program

Is the participant currently on the CDC or in their initial waiting period to transition to the CDC?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, procedure ends here

3

Confirm the participant’s Card Issuer + Read more ...

Process Direct:

  • If the participant is on the CDC, card issuer details can be found on the Cashless Debit Card Summary screen (CDCS) in Process Direct.
  • If the participant is in their initial waiting period, account details can be found on the CDC Current Account (CDCAH) or Cashless Debit Card Summary screen (CDCS) screens.

Customer First:

  • If the participant is on the CDC, card issuer details can be found on the Debit Card Trial Summary screen (Q2SU).
  • If the participant is in their initial waiting period, account details can be found:
    • Request Funds Transfer workflow
    • cancel the workflow after providing participant their CDC account details

Card issuers can be identified through their BSB:

  • Traditional Credit Union 704 119
  • Indue 704 772

If the CDC account is with:

  • TCU, go to Step 4
  • Indue, see Table 3, Step 1

If a cardholder has presented to an Agent site and they do not know who their card issuer is, they can call the CDC Hotline who will advise them who their card issuer is.

4

Issuing a TCU temporary card + Read more ...

The ‘Issue Temporary Card’ workflow cannot be used to issue a participant a temporary TCU card.

The cardholder must be provided with a temporary card and associated PIN Mailer.

For further details on accepting and issuing a temporary TCU card please refer to the Temporary TCU CDC Inventory Taskcards on the Resources tab.

If the customer has requested a temporary card to support bringing their start date forward, after you have issued the temporary card and the card is active, see Cashless Debit Card (CDC) - changing initial waiting periods.

Procedure ends here for TCU participants.

Issuing and activating a Cashless Debit Card

Table 3: Issuing a temporary cashless debit card to a participant who has presented at a service centre or a remote servicing team.

Step

Action

1

Run the Issue Temporary card workflow. + Read more ...

In Customer First Workspace, go to Cashless Debit Card and select Issue Temporary Card.

An authentication screen will display:

  • If the participant is the CDC cardholder, the ‘customer authentication’ screen will display
  • If the payment nominee is the CDC cardholder, the ‘payment nominee authentication’ screen will display.

Who is the cardholder of the temporary card?

  • The participant, go to Step 2
  • The participant's payment nominee, go to Step 3

2

Authenticating the participant + Read more ...

If the participant has a DOA DOC present, confirm all DOAs have been read and acknowledge any potential financial vulnerabilities by marking the tick box.

Confirm if the participant's record is password protected before they can continue.

Once confirmed, the system will request how the participant has been authenticated. Select the method/s. The participant must be fully authenticated to continue workflow.

The read DOA DOCs and Password confirmation options will only appear if there are any present on the participant's record.

Once the participant is fully authenticated go to Step 4.

3

Authenticating a CDC participant's payment nominee + Read more ...

If the participant has a DOA DOC present, confirm all DOAs have been read and acknowledge any potential financial vulnerabilities.

Confirm the nominee's password that is recorded under the 'Nominee provided identification' section and only select the Details confirmed box if the nominee was successfully authenticated.

Once the nominee is fully authenticated, go to Step 4.

4

Does the participant have funds available? + Read more ...

Staff can view the participant's CDC transaction history including their current balance by selecting View CDC Transaction History.

If participants can still access their personalised card (i.e. left at home and won’t be able to access for a number of days) staff should determine if the participant needs a temporary card. Staff should check:

  • how much the participant has available in their CDC account
  • if the participant is going to receive their ISP again before accessing their personalised card again
  • if the participant is expecting a transfer from another CDC participant directly into their CDC account

If the participant has no funds available, and are unlikely to be paid again prior to getting access to their personalised card, recommend that the participant waits until they can access their card again.

Issuing a temporary card will close the personalised card and request a new card to be sent by the card issuer to the participant.

Does the participant require a temporary card to be issued?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, procedure ends here

5

Has the cardholder received and activated their initial personalised card before requesting a temporary card to be issued? + Read more ...

If the cardholder has not yet received their initial personalised card, a copy of the card issuer's Conditions of Use (CoU) booklet must be provided to the participant along with the temporary card. A link to the card issuer's website where the CoU can be found is located on the Resources tab.

Note: there is no limit to the number of temporary cards that can be issued. However, it is in the cardholder’s interest to hold on to their personalised card and minimise the amount of temporary cards they access.

Based on the participant’s response, indicate Yes or No in the workflow.

Go to Step 6.

6

Does the cardholder wish to request a new personalised card? + Read more ...

Ask the cardholder if they would like a new personalised card to be sent by the card issuer.

Is a new personalised card required?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, after selecting No, the address selection will be greyed out. Go to Step 8

Note: sometimes a new personalised card will be issued by the card issuer system regardless. For example, if the participant already has an active personalised card then it must be cancelled to enable the activation of a temporary card.

7

Where is the personalised replacement card being sent? + Read more ...

Ask the cardholder where the new personalised card is to be sent.

Address registered with the card issuer

This is the address the participant has on file with the card issuer.

If Service Failed presents under the Indue address, select Retry to try the link between Services Australia and Indue again. If this fails to provide a response, a message will present advising to select No to the previous question to continue the issue temporary card workflow.

Address recorded with the agency

This is the participant’s current postal address on file. If there is no postal address on file, the participant’s residential address will display.

If the personalised card is to be sent to the participant's address recorded with the agency, the workflow will present a pop up asking if the participant consents to share their address with the card issuer. Selecting Yes will also update the participant’s address on file with the card issuer. If the participant does not consent to having their agency address sent to the card issuer, select No. Selecting No means a personalised card will not be issued to the participant.

Select the address where the participant would like their personalised card to be sent.

Go to Step 8.

8

Select the reason a temporary card is required + Read more ...

  • Lost: the participant no longer knows where their card is, or the participant has not yet received their initial personalised card.
  • Stolen: the participant advises the card has been stolen. If they believe there has been unauthorised activity on their account, they will need to contact the card issuer for disputed transaction support. The card issuer may request a police report to support the participant's application.
  • Damaged: EFTPOS and ATMs can no longer accept the card

Go to Step 9.

9

Collect the temporary card and activate + Read more ...

Collect a temporary card from the box of cards in the office or assigned to the Service Officer. Each site should have a stock of cards kept in a secure location.

Enter unique the 11 digit Card ID (XXXX XXXX XXX), located on the back of the temporary card. Click Activate Card button.

A message will display after the card has been activated

Note: as the card is a Visa card, the number on front of the card must not be recorded anywhere in the agency’s systems.

Go to Step 10.

10

Issuing Temporary Card Receipt (SS525) + Read more ...

Issuing a temporary card receipt (SS525) is no longer required for both RST and service centre staff.

Staff won’t be able to proceed without selecting ‘print receipt’. Please select the ‘print receipt’ button within the workflow to produce a receipt, and dispose of it in a secure waste bin.

Click the checkbox ‘I acknowledge the Temporary Card and Temporary Card receipt have been signed by (customer name)’, then the Continue button.

If this is the first card the participant has received, provide a Conditions of Use booklet.

Go to Step 11.

11

Setting a secure Personal Identification Number (PIN) + Read more ...

The cardholder is responsible for choosing their PIN, however staff can recommend to the participant that the PIN:

  • does not start with a zero. PINs beginning with zero cannot be created
  • is unique to them and easy to remember.
  • is not sequential (i.e. 1234)
  • is not any of the number sequences printed on the temporary card

The cardholder will need to enter their PIN and then confirm their entry, by entering their selected PIN again. If a different PIN is entered the second time, an error will present. Once the same PIN has been entered twice correctly, select Assign PIN.

Once the card has been activated, a change of their PIN can be completed by accessing their online account with the card issuer. See General CDC support for more information.

Go to Step 12.

12

Finish issuing temporary card. + Read more ...

Select I acknowledge the Temporary Card and Temporary Card receipt has been signed by the participant, to finalise the workflow.

Note: the Temporary Card Receipt is no longer required to be printed and signed by the participant.

It is important to remind the participant:

  • keep the card safe and don't let anyone else use it
  • don't tell anyone your PIN or let them see it
  • don't write your PIN down
  • they will need to arrange for any previous Direct Debit arrangements to be set up to the BSB/account number of new card details
  • if they locate the previous card, it will no longer be active

For any ongoing card transaction support the participant should contact the card issuer.

Procedure ends here.

Resetting a PIN

Table 5: This table explains the process of resetting a CDC PIN

Step

Action

1

Run the Change or Reset PIN workflow + Read more ...

In Customer First Workspace go to Cashless Debit Card and select Change or Reset PIN.

An authentication screen will display. If the participant has a DOA DOC present, confirm all DOAs have been read and acknowledge any potential financial vulnerabilities by marking the tick box.

Confirm if the participant's record is password protected before they can continue.

Once confirmed, the system will request how the participant has been authenticated. Select the method/s. The participant must be fully authenticated to continue workflow.

The read DOA DOCs option will only appear if there are any present on the participant's record.

Once the participant is fully authenticated go to Step 2.

2

Check the card details + Read more ...

Before allowing a PIN to be changed, check the following:

  • The card number matches the card number displayed
  • The name on the card matches the person requesting the change. Temporary cards are not embossed with the participant’s name and will display the name on card as ‘Temporary card’. Ensure appropriate ID checks are completed
  • The card status is ‘Active’. Only cards with a status of ‘Active’ are permitted to change PINs

Note: if the cardholder advises their new PIN does not allow purchases to be made, it may be possible the PIN associated with the card has been blocked due to six unsuccessful attempts. If this is the case, the PIN block will be removed at midnight each night. Alternatively, they can contact the card issuer.

Do the card details match?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, PIN cannot be reset. Procedure ends here

3

Reset the PIN + Read more ...

The cardholder must enter and re-enter the PIN using either the PIN pad or numerical keys on the keyboard.

PINs must meet the following criteria:

  • 4 characters in length
  • Characters must be numeric
  • Must not begin with a ‘0’
  • Both the ‘New PIN’ and ‘Confirm PIN’ fields must match

As the PIN is being keyed, the numbers are not displayed on the page. The PIN is not stored on the participant's record. Services Australia will validate the PIN before sending it to the card issuer.

To finalise the PIN reset, complete the Processing Declaration and select ‘Finalise’.

It is important to remind the cardholder:

  • keep the card safe and don't let anyone else use it
  • don't tell anyone your PIN or let them see it
  • don't write your PIN down

Procedure ends here.
