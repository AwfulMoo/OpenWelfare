4

Assessment of customer's circumstances + Read more ...

APS4 staff or below must consult with a Service Support Officer (SSO) or Team Leader to determine if the request meets the criteria for immediate processing.

Immediate processing criteria

  • The customer is vulnerable and/or has complex needs and further delay could worsen their situation
  • The customer has been assessed as in hardship and previous escalations for urgent processing have not been actioned, or
  • Failure by Services Australia to respond quickly and effectively will present a significant risk to the customer or the agency

Financial hardship provisions do not apply to:

  • Customers transferring from one income support payment to another. Unless, their previous income support payment was cancelled more than 14 days ago
  • Dependent Youth Allowance (YA) students, job seekers, Australian Apprentices and ABSTUDY Apprentices, living with parent(s)/guardian(s) or away from home

Does the claim meet the criteria for immediate processing?

  • Yes;
    • Ensure customer contact details are correct and advise the customer the claim will be escalated for priority processing
    • Refer to Team Leader/SSO as per local site escalation process, see Table 2
  • No, the claim does not meet immediate processing criteria. See Request for urgent processing of a new claim when experiencing financial hardship

Identify if the claim is an Age Pension combined partner online claim + Read more ...

Combined Age Pension claims (sometimes referred to as 'dual claims') allocate as a single work item and can only be assessed in the Process Direct (CPTv5) by Service Officers with the appropriate Age Pension combined partner online claim skill tags.

It is important that Age Pension combined partner online claims are not processed as 2 separate single claims as the data for the partner claim may be lost.

To identify if the Age Pension claim is a combined online claim in Customer First or Customer Record:

  • check for keyword DUALCLM, or
  • view the claim Note or AGE OLC Claim Progress DOC where the words 'Awaiting partner review' display within the text

Is the claim an Age Pension combined partner online claim awaiting assessment?

  • Yes;
    • If the customers meets the immediate claim processing new claims criteria complete the Workload Management webform with the wording ‘AGE DUALCLM’ in the “outline of the workload issue”. See the Resources page for a link to the webform. Procedure ends here
    • If the customers meet the immediate claim processing and the claim is a reassessment of a rejected claim, go to step 5
  • No, go to Step 5

  • Customer First via Request and Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure > Review Claim > Select the relevant claim

1

Escalation protocols + Read more ...

This process ensures the timely management of aggressive customers that are in contact with, or need to contact, the Participation Solutions Team (PST). It includes arrangements for customers who are already in a Restricted Servicing Arrangement (RSA) and Personalised Services customers.

Protocols exist for phone contact between managers or Team Leaders in service centres and PST:

  • to provide alerts, and
  • manage potential customer aggression threats

This process also includes how to identify customers following incidents of aggression using the designated PST phones who are unknown to service centre staff.

Note: do not use this process if a customer has been on the phone for a long time, or if they advise that the call dropped out. These customers will need to call back to have their compliance action investigated.

This process:

  • does not replace the normal practices for responding to customer aggression
  • assists in de-escalating or preventing potentially harmful aggressive events

Apply the usual response to inappropriate behaviours as per Services Australia's customer aggression guidelines. The Resources page contains a link to this document.

2

On site PST self service phone enquiry where potential or actual aggression is evident to a PST Service Officer + Read more ...

PST staff should alert their Team Leader or manager that an aggressive event is taking place or has the potential to take place.

PST Team Leaders or managers assesses the situation and should immediately notify the appropriate service centre manager by phone. If the service centre manager is unavailable, PST will advise the next most senior responsible officer available.

3

On site aggressive or potentially aggressive customer who needs to contact PST + Read more ...

If a service centre manager or Team Leader identifies the need to defuse an aggressive customer incident where there is an outstanding compliance action, the service centre manager will phone a PST Team Leader or manager to arrange a warm hand-off to an appropriate PST staff member.

A list of PST Team Leaders or managers can be found within Office Locator. See Resources page for instructions and a link.

If the PST does not satisfactorily resolve the matter and there is risk of further aggression, immediately advise the service centre manager by phone.

4

Customer unknown to the service centre commits an act of aggression during or following contact with PST + Read more ...

When a customer displays aggressive behaviour after contact with PST, determine the customer's identity in order to take the appropriate follow up action.

Service centre managers should phone a PST Team Leader/manager and provide the following information:

  • The self service phone number from which the call was made
  • The time the call was answered in PST (as close as possible)

A list of PST Team Leaders or managers can be found within Office Locator. See Resources page for instructions and a link.

The PST team leader/manager will then check with all teams across the PST network if any staff are aware of the particular case so that further information (including Customer Reference Number (CRN)) can be provided.

Where the identity is still unknown, initiate a call trace process to identify the Service Officer who took the call and then follow up with them to see if they can provide the relevant customer information.

Where confirmation of the identity is still not possible, request a Customer Record Access Monitor (CRAM) report on the Service Officer's record for the day and timeframe provided to identify the relevant customer.

Once the customer is identified, the PST team leader/manager will follow up with the service centre manager to discuss next steps.

5

PST arrangements for customer with Restricted Servicing Arrangement (RSA) and Personalised Services + Read more ...

Customers who have a current RSA involving a One Main Contact (OMC) should have any required PST contact facilitated by the OMC where possible.

The OMC should phone a PST team leader/manager to

  • discuss servicing strategies
  • identify a staff member to conduct the compliance investigation, and
  • work out a way to engage the job seeker (either a call back or wherever possible an immediate transfer)

A list of PST Team Leaders or managers can be found within Office Locator. See Resources page for instructions and a link.

The OMC can discuss the reason the RSA was imposed (where relevant), and any insight into known customer issues or barriers. It may be appropriate that the OMC remain engaged on the line while the investigation takes place.
