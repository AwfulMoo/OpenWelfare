Initial eligibility assessment

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Course is a compulsory part of activity test requirements or mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving, or about to start receiving government income support where study in the course (makes up part or all) is a compulsory part of the activity test/mutual obligation requirements, for example, JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) as a job seeker?

  • Yes, and they are applying for:
    • ABSTUDY, they are ineligible for any ABSTUDY assistance. If the customer wants to transfer to ABSTUDY, see Assessing a claim for ABSTUDY Schooling B Award, Tertiary Award and Masters and Doctorate Award. Note: if the student starts receiving one of these other forms of government assistance after starting their course of study while receiving ABSTUDY, the student retains eligibility for the Incidentals Allowance already paid. See Step 9 in Table 3
    • ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Course is not part of mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving, or about to start receiving government income support where study in the course does not form part or all, or is a voluntary part, of their mutual obligation requirements, for example, JSP or YA as a job seeker?

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to ABSTUDY Part-time Award entitlements. Check the other general ABSTUDY eligibility criteria. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Part-time Award. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Other payments for study + Read more ...

Is the student receiving, or about to start receiving payments for study under the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme, Austudy, YA, Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), Language Literacy and Numeracy Supplement (LLNS), Veterans' Children Education Scheme (VCES), or Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act Education and Training Scheme (MRCAETS)?

  • Yes,
    • The student is ineligible for ABSTUDY assistance for any period where they are receiving the payments for study
    • Only one type of income support benefit is payable
    • Ensure the best choice for the student. For more information on helping customers decide which payment is most beneficial to them, see Identifying the most beneficial payment for students or Australian Apprentices
    • Note: if the student starts receiving one of these other forms of government assistance after starting their course of study while receiving ABSTUDY, the student retains eligibility for the Incidentals Allowance already paid. See Step 9 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Social security pension or Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) Service Pension or Veteran Payment + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving or about to start receiving a social security pension or a DVA Service Pension or Veteran Payment?

  • Yes, the customer is not entitled to the ABSTUDY Living Allowance from the start date of the social security pension or DVA payment, but may be entitled to ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) or the ABSTUDY Part-time Award (if their workload is insufficient to qualify for PES):
    • Check the other general ABSTUDY eligibility criteria
    • See Qualifying payments for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and ABSTUDY PES and ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Part-time Award.
      Note: receiving a DVA disability pension does not preclude eligibility for Living Allowance. These disability pensions are treated as income under the personal income test. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Age Pension and study + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving Age Pension and studying?

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to ABSTUDY Part-time Award entitlements. Check the other general ABSTUDY eligibility criteria. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Part-time Award. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Defence Force Income Support Allowance (DFISA)-like payment + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving a DFISA-like payment under the Veterans' Entitlements Act 1986?

  • Yes, the customer may qualify for the ABSTUDY Part-time Award. Procedure ends here
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

Eligibility assessment for Australian Apprentices

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

Is the customer a full-time Australian Apprentice?

Note: participants in Indigenous Australian Government Development Programme (IAGDP) jointly administered by the Department of Education, Skills and Employment are employed by Australian Government departments and agencies as Australian Apprentices.

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3

2

Living Away From Home Allowance (LAFHA) recipient + Read more ...

Is the customer an Australian Apprentice receiving the LAFHA?

  • Yes, the customer is not entitled to ABSTUDY assistance for any period for which LAFHA was paid. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Training assistance + Read more ...

Training assistance from an employer for formal study includes:

  • costs associated with doing the course or training away from the home location (such as paid study leave, payment of travel, meals or accommodation), or
  • a school based traineeship or apprenticeship

Is the customer receiving training assistance from an employer for formal study?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Australian Apprentice receiving other assistance + Read more ...

Generally, customers receiving training assistance for formal study are not eligible for ABSTUDY. The exceptions to this are:

  • Participants in a school based traineeship or apprenticeship
  • Australian Apprentices receiving training assistance for formal study if they meet the criteria for Australian Apprentices under the Tertiary Award

Is the customer eligible for ABSTUDY assistance?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Full-time Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

Australian Apprentice customers may be entitled to ABSTUDY assistance with the following allowances depending on their circumstances:

  • Living Allowance (ABSTUDY)
  • Incidentals Allowance
  • Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA)
  • Remote Area Allowance (RAA)
  • Rent Assistance (RA)
  • Additional Assistance
  • Lawful Custody Allowance

Note: ABSTUDY assistance cannot duplicate assistance from other sources, like an employer.

Procedure ends here.

6

Not eligible for ABSTUDY assistance + Read more ...

Is the customer current on ABSTUDY?

  • Yes, cancel ABSTUDY from the start date of the other assistance to study. See Cancellation of ABSTUDY payments for more information. Procedure ends here
  • No, tell the customer they do not meet the eligibility criteria for ABSTUDY. Check eligibility for an alternative payment and record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

Eligibility assessment for students with other assistance

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Career Trackers Indigenous Internship Program + Read more ...

Some students enrolled in the Career Trackers Indigenous Internship Program receive an Indigenous Cadetship Support (ICS) scholarship.

Is the student an intern through the Career Trackers Indigenous Internship Program?

  • Yes, and an Indigenous Cadetship Support (ICS) scholarship:
    • is received by the student, go to Step 9
    • is not received by the student, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Student receiving other assistance for training or study + Read more ...

Generally, students receiving training assistance for formal study, including assistance from an employer are not eligible for ABSTUDY. Training assistance from an employer includes paid study leave or payment of travel, meals and accommodation costs.

The exceptions are:

  • People doing a training program placement
  • For employees of organisations registered by the Australian Charities and Not-for-profit Commission (ACNC) and/or the Office of the Registrar of Indigenous Corporations (ORIC):
    • the value of fares, meals and accommodation is assessed on a dollar for dollar basis
    • employment income is assessed under the personal income test
  • Scholarships, cadetships or similar assistance from an employer are assessed under the personal income test. Note: this does not include government employees receiving scholarships from their agency to study
  • Indigenous Youth Mobility Pathways Project (IYMP) delivered by the National Indigenous Australian Agency (NIAA) - No direct financial support is provided, only supported accommodation. Note: the accommodation support is assessed as duplicate assistance, therefore will preclude eligibility to rent assistance

See the Resources page for links to the ACNC and ORIC websites.

Is the student receiving any training assistance for formal study?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 8

3

Receiving or about to start receiving a scholarship or cadetship + Read more ...

Is the student receiving or about to start receiving a scholarship or cadetship?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Scholarships and cadetships + Read more ...

Receiving a cadetship does not preclude ABSTUDY eligibility, however, income received must be assessed under the personal income test.

In general, receiving a scholarship does not preclude ABSTUDY eligibility. Most scholarships are assessed as income under the personal income test.

However, there are exceptions to this rule.

Note: a student is not considered an employee of a government agency if they are an employee for work experience only.

Is the customer an employee of a government agency and receiving a scholarship from the agency to study?

  • Yes, students who are employees of a government agency and receiving a scholarship from that agency to study are ineligible for ABSTUDY. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Training program placement + Read more ...

Is the customer doing a training program placement?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Other assistance for training or study + Read more ...

Is the student receiving training assistance from an employer (including paid study leave or payment of course fees, travel, meals or accommodation costs)?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Assistance from exempt organisation + Read more ...

See the Resources page for links to the ACNC and ORIC websites. These websites have an Organisation Search function to check if an organisation is registered.

Is the study assistance from an organisation registered by the Australian Charities and Not-for-profit Commission (ACNC) and/or the Office of the Registrar of Indigenous Corporations (ORIC)?

  • Yes, any assistance from the employer in the form of fares, meals and accommodation is assessed on a dollar for dollar basis. Employment income is assessed under the personal income test. Details about the assistance (for example, study leave, amount paid for fares, meals or accommodation costs) provided by the third party organisation can be obtained over the phone from the student. Go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

See the Resources page for links to the ACNC and ORIC websites. These websites have an Organisation Search function to check if an organisation is registered.

8

Student eligible to ABSTUDY assistance + Read more ...

The student may be entitled to ABSTUDY assistance with the following allowances depending on their circumstances:

  • Living Allowance (ABSTUDY)
  • Incidentals Allowance
  • Additional Incidentals Allowance
  • Fares Allowance (FAA)
  • Away from Base assistance
  • Relocation Scholarship (RS)
  • Student Start-up Loan (SSL)
  • Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA)
  • Remote Area Allowance (RAA)
  • Rent Assistance (RA)
  • Additional Assistance
  • Lawful Custody Allowance

Note: ABSTUDY assistance cannot duplicate assistance from other sources, such as from an employer.

Procedure ends here.

9

Not eligible for ABSTUDY assistance + Read more ...

Is the customer current on ABSTUDY?

  • Yes, cancel ABSTUDY from the start date of the other assistance to study. See Cancellation of ABSTUDY payments for more information
  • No, tell the customer they do not meet the eligibility criteria for ABSTUDY. Check eligibility for an alternative payment and record details on a DOC
