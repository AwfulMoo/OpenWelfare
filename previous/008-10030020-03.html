Issuing an electronic version of Mobility Allowance Review form

Table 1: This table describes the process to issue an electronic version of the Mobility Allowance Review form.

Step

Action

1

Electronic Version of the Mobility Allowance Review + Read more ...

If the customer requests an electronic version of the MOB Employment/Training review form be emailed to them for completion using their assistive technology:

  • confirm the customer's current email address and update if required
  • advise the customer the fillable Review of Mobility Allowance form (MA003Y) form will be emailed to them
  • advise the customer they must not return the fillable MA003Y via the return email. The form must be completed, printed, signed and lodged via normal procedures
  • email the Accessible Letters Team and advise a customer is requiring a fillable MA003Y form and include the following details:
    • customer's name
    • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • customer's email address
  • create a DOC on the customer's record stating the date the customer contacted and requested an electronic version of the MA003Y be emailed to them. Include a note to say an email has been sent to the Accessible Letters Team

Note: customers can register to receive alternative letter formats. See Alternative letter formats for customer with a disability or medical condition affecting their vision for more information about how to register a customer for this service.

2

Accessible Letters team + Read more ...

The Accessible Letters team is to email an electronic version of the Review of Mobility Allowance MA003Y form to the customer’s email address from the Accessible Letters Team mailbox.

  • Check the return date for the MOB/ETR Employment/Training review on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen
    • ‘V’iew the activity
    • Advise customer to return the MOB/ETR Employment/Training review form by the due date
  • Annotate the form request DOC on the customer's record noting:
    • the date the electronic form MA003Y was emailed to the customer
    • customer has been advised the date the form is to be returned

3

Reminder MOB Review form received by Accessible Letters team + Read more ...

If a reminder Review of Mobility Allowance form (check History Summary (HS) screen if form is a reminder form) is received at the Accessible Letters Manager mailbox, the Service Officer is to forward the original email to the customer’s email address.

  • Check the return date for the Reminder MOB/ETR Employment/Training review on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen
    • ‘V’iew the activity
    • Advise customer to return the MOB/ETR Employment/Training review form by the due date
  • Annotate the form request DOC or create a new one if there is no original DOC noting:
    • the date the reminder electronic form MA003Y was emailed to the customer
    • customer has been advised the date the form is to be returned

Checking customer's response to questions

Table 2: This table describes the process for checking the customer's response to questions on the review form and updating the relevant screens with the information on the review form.

For AMW processing only

Step

Action

1

Status of review Activity + Read more ...

If the status of the review activity is:

  • Current (MOB/CUR), go to Step 4
  • Suspended - did not return review form (MOB/SUS-SNR), go to Step 2
  • Cancelled - did not return review form (MOB/CAN-CNR), go to Step 3

2

Suspended - did not return review form (MOB/SUS-SNR) + Read more ...

If the review form has not been returned within 21 days of issue, an automatic second request is sent allocating another 21 days. If the review is not returned within the additional 21 days, MOB is automatically suspended.

If the review form is returned within 21 days of the date of suspension, it will be allocated to AMW for review to be actioned and possible restoration of MOB. Go to Step 4

If the review form is not returned within 21 days of the date of suspension, MOB is automatically cancelled.

Procedure ends here.

3

Cancelled - did not return review form (MOB/CAN-CNR), + Read more ...

If the customer has completed and returned the review form within 13 weeks of the cancellation date, and has been entitled to MOB since the date of cancellation, activity to be allocated to AMW. Review to be actioned with possible restoration of MOB. Go to Step 4

If the customer has not been eligible for MOB since cancellation or the cancellation date is more than 13 weeks in the past, the customer must re-claim. Procedure ends here.

4

Check for vehicle assistance + Read more ...

Has the customer been given a car by the Department of Veterans' Affairs?

  • Yes, no longer eligible for MOB. Insert a new line on the MOCD screen:
    • Event Date: field, date the change occurred
    • Vehicle Assistance Received: field, type 'Y'
    • Press [Enter] and go to Step 7 in Table 4
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Satisfy the travel test + Read more ...

Does the customer continue to satisfy the travel test?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, no longer eligible for MOB. Insert a new line on the MOCD screen:
    • Event Date: field, date the change occurred
    • Failed Travel Test: field, type 'Y'
    • Press [Enter] and go to Step 7 in Table 4

6

Job Plan with an Employment Service Provider + Read more ...

Is the customer looking for work under a Job Plan with an Employment Services Provider?

  • Yes, for customers on the higher rate of MOB and customers who are volunteers, the Job Plan and suitable job search activities will be automatically verified by the system and this will trigger the appropriate rate of Mobility Allowance (from 1 July 2015). Go to Step 4 in Table 4
  • Yes, customers who are subject to Mutual Obligations and are eligible for the standard rate of MOB will need their Job Plan checked for suitable job search activities and details entered on the Employment and Training details screen, go to Step 7.
  • No, go to Step 4

7

The Employment/Training (ETS) and Employment Income Summary(EANS) screens + Read more ...

Navigate to the Employment/Training Summary (ETS) screen by either:

  • selecting the MOB/ETR activity from the AL screen and following the preselected screen flow, or
  • typing ETS in the Nxt: field and press [Enter]

Note: the Employment/Training Details (ETD) screen is displayed if 1 or more employment or training activity has been recorded previously.

If the customer advises they have commenced working and are in receipt of Disability Support Pension (DSP), Jobseeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) or Parenting Payment (PP) they may be entitled to the higher rate of MOB. Details of their employment income and hours should already be recorded as part of the reporting requirements for their income support payment. If not, go to Assessing and coding employment income for further information.

8

Compare the activity/activities on the ETS screen with the customer's responses to questions on the Review of Mobility Allowance (MA003) + Read more ...

Has the customer indicated a change to the previously approved and verified activity/activities?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, details do not have to be re-verified, see Step 4 in Table 4

9

Determine if the customer has commenced or ceased: + Read more ...

  • receiving JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) and satisfying Mutual Obligation Requirements
  • receiving JSP, YA (job seeker), Parenting Payment (PP) or Disability Support Pension (DSP) and qualifying for the higher rate of MOB
  • studying and receiving Austudy
  • undertaking job search activities as part of a program with an Employment Services Provider
  • undertaking Disability Employment Services (DES - Disability Management Service program)

Customers on higher rate MOB who, due solely to an increase in hours/income, are no longer eligible for an income support payment will continue to receive the higher rate of MOB while they continue to satisfy the basic eligibility criteria for MOB.

Has the customer commenced or ceased any of the above?

  • Yes, use '?' field help to update or record the details on ETD or EANS screen. See Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3

Ceased an approved activity + Read more ...

Do any current activities have either 'N' or 'I' in the 'Details Verified?' field?

Click here to see what the ETS screen looks like when verification action is outstanding on the activity.

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, see Step 3 in Table 5

7

Verification Action is outstanding. Do not complete the MOB activity + Read more ...

  • Verification process: Either the Mobility Allowance Verification of Work (including voluntary work), and looking for work activities (MA005(E)M) or Mobility Allowance Verification of Training (MA005(T)M) form is automatically issued to the customer overnight. The customer has:
    • 21 days to return the initial form
    • 21 days to return a reminder form before MOB is suspended
    • 21 days of suspension before MOB is automatically cancelled (with no period of grace)
  • Resubmit the activity for an appropriate date in the future. MOB will automatically supsend/cancel if the customer does not return the verification form.
  • When the verification form (or other appropriate information) is returned, go to Step 8

Note: if the activity is completed by mistake, pending outstanding verification action, then payments under a period of grace (POG) will commence. This must not occur as there is no legislative basis to pay a POG as a result of unverified hours. For more information about the period of grace, see Cancellation of Mobility Allowance (MOB).

8

Verification form returned and the employment, training or voluntary work details can be verified + Read more ...

Check the payment status of Mobility Allowance (MOB):

  • If status is current (MOB/CUR), see Step 1 in Table 4
  • If status is suspended (MOB/SUS-SVR), see Step 2 in Table 4
  • If status is cancelled (MOB/CAN-CVR), and the cancellation date is within 13 weeks:
    • If the customer has completed and returned the review form within 13 weeks, and has been entitled to MOB since the date of cancellation, restore MOB and see Step 1 in Table 4
    • If the customer has not been eligible for MOB since cancellation or the cancellation date is more than 13 weeks in the past, the customer must re-claim. Record the details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

3

Undertaking sufficient verified hours + Read more ...

Navigate to the ETS screen and check the Customer Results: field. This field should show if the customer either satisfies or is exempt from the 32 hours rule.

Is the customer undertaking sufficient verified hours in an approved activity for MOB?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 7

4

Review earlier than 12 months + Read more ...

Is there any information received as a part of this review which would indicate the customer should be reviewed earlier than 12 months?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Change in the employment, training, voluntary work or other activity details + Read more ...

Does the information relate to a change in the employment, training, voluntary work or other activity details?

  • Yes, record the date of the expected or known change in the 'Expected End Date' field on the relevant ETD screen. This will automatically set up a FAL activity to issue a review when the activity matures. Go to Step 6
  • No, record a future Manual Review, to mature on the appropriate date. Go to Step 6

6

Making a favourable decision + Read more ...

The customer will continue to receive Mobility Allowance as a result of the review.

Finalise the activity via the AR screen. This will automatically mature and finalise the Service Profiling activity on PQSS.

If there is information from the review indicating the customer’s employment income and hours varies from the information currently reported for their income support payment, the Employment Income Summary (EANS) and Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen are to be updated. See Assessing and coding a correction to previously declared employment income. Note: MOB is not income tested therefore any changes to employment income and/or hours should be reported as part of their income support payment reporting requirements.

Document the result on the customer’s record including actions undertaken, outcome and reason for ongoing eligibility (e.g. verified employment with sufficient hours, current Job Plan including job search activities).

Procedure ends here.

7

Making an unfavourable decision + Read more ...

Customer no longer qualifies for MOB.

At least 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer must be made to advise if an unfavourable decision is to be made. For more information, see Making an unfavourable decision.

Record all details on a DOC and finalise the activity via the AR screen. This will automatically mature and finalise the Service Profiling activity on PQSS.

Customer’s payment is placed into a Period of Grace (POG) and an automatic advice is issued advising they no longer qualify.

If the customer is receiving the higher rate of MOB, depending on the qualifying activity they were undertaking, their payment will either cancel at the end of the POG or there will be a reassessment to determine if they still qualify for the standard rate.

If the customer is receiving the standard rate of MOB, their payment will cancel after the relevant period of grace (POG) and an automatic advice will be issued.

Customers need to advise if they commence or recommence a qualifying activity during their POG as their payment can be restored.

For more information about the relevant period of grace, see Cancellation and Period of Grace for Mobility Allowance (MOB).
