Integrated Activity Management (IAM) activities for processing compensation reviews

Item

Description

1

Preliminary notice not issued (CMM/CLR(NOT)) + Read more ...

The activity will produce every 21 days when a preliminary notice is required.

Once all details have been returned from the customer:

  • update the Compensation Payer Details screen
  • where applicable send the preliminary notice

A preliminary notice is not to be sent:

  • without the correct reference number
  • to the defendant solicitor
  • where periodic payments are ongoing
  • to an overseas liable party, or
  • when the compensation recipient is not receiving a CAP

See Resources for when to send or not to send the preliminary notice.

The activity will complete once the notice issues.

See Actioning a compensation clearance for more details.

2

Review compensation entitlement (CMM/CLR) + Read more ...

This activity will produce every 12 months once a preliminary notice has been issued.

Contact the compensation payer to:

  • review the progress of the claim, and
  • update the record as required

If the inquiry shows settlement is expected in more than 12 months:

  • on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen, adjust the date that the next CLR activity review is produced

See Manual compensation finalisations for more details.

3

Second letter will be issued in 7 days (CMM/UNR) + Read more ...

See Locating and actioning compensation charge activities outstanding for 35 days (UNR) for more details.

4

Check tax adjustment (CLI/MFU) + Read more ...

See Processing Manual Follow-up (MFU) tax activities for compensation debts for more processing information.

5

Clear to pay (NSS PEN PGA/CLE) + Read more ...

The Compensation Management System (CMS) will generate a NSS, PEN, PGA 'clear to pay' activity, and send to the customers processing service centre.

This activity is information for Service Officers in a service centre to action.

Registered NSS, PEN, PGA/CLE activities are produced to tell the Service Officer that a compensation clearance has been given for the customer and/or their partner.

Cancel the activity and proceed with any outstanding new claim (NCL).

6

Compensation settlement paid to customer (CMM/REA) + Read more ...

The CRT has recorded a gross settlement specified amount and date. The customer will usually get a lesser net amount several weeks later.

This action is for Service Officers in service centres.

When a lump sum settles and the compensation recipient and/or their partner is in receipt of a compensation affected payment (CAP), The Compensation Management System (CMS) generates a CMM/REA activity.

The activity will automatically be sent to the customers service centre after the completion of the lump sum settlement. Six weeks after the settlement date, service centre staff should:

  • contact the compensation recipient and/or their partner to obtain the net amount
  • update the record with how the funds have been invested, see Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services

7

Periodic charge repaid (CMM/MFU) + Read more ...

CMM periodic charge repaid.

This activity is for CRT.

In the Compensation Management Summary screen, finalise the claim on the Finalisation/Reactivate screen if the:

  • compensation recipient/partner has not received compensation affected payments after the period covered by the debt, and
  • compensation is not likely to be paid for earlier periods

If there is ongoing compensation on the Period Payment Details screen, the claim cannot be finalised until it ceases. Complete the MFU activity without further action

See Finalisation of compensation claims (CLK) for more details.

8

Failed auto finalisation (CMM/AFL) + Read more ...

This activity auto completes except for a failed auto finalisation.

Check if finalisation is still required and if so:

  • complete the Finalisation screen, or
  • delete the activity

9

No reasonable action taken (CMM/NRA) + Read more ...

No action taken by the compensation recipient in 28 days.

NRA review will complete.

See Actioning a compensation clearance for more details.
