This page outlines the process for dealing with a Level 1 complaint.

A Service Officer refers to Service Officers (SO1-SO5) a Service Officer (SO6) will be referred to as a Complaints Officer or Service Officer - Escalated Complaints Team throughout this procedure.

Assessment and management

Action

1

Receiving a Complaint call + Read more ...

Complaints can be received:

  • via the Feedback and Complaints line
  • as part of business as usual

Complaint received about Centrelink or Medicare

  • If a call is received and the customer wants to lodge a complaint about Centrelink or Medicare:
  • cold transfer the call to the appropriate service delivery brand via the Feedback and Complaints line
  • procedure ends here

General enquiry received on the Services Australia Feedback and Complaints line

General enquiries are contacts from customers that are not intended to be a complaint, compliment or suggestion.

  • Advise the Feedback and Complaints line is for the lodgement of complaints and feedback

Complaint received on general enquiry line

  • If a customer calls the Child Support general enquiry line and the call is not a general enquiry but a customer complaint:
  • record the call as a Level 1 complaint and manage accordingly

2

Identify complaint issues and lock customer + Read more ...

  • Identify the customer’s issues. Ask the customer to provide specific information relevant to their complaint including dates, names and information they were provided
  • Apologise to the customer if appropriate, for any errors made or their perception of a negative experience
  • Ask if they have a desired remedy or outcome. This assists in managing the customer’s expectations and determining options available
  • If the complaint was made in writing or online, contact the customer to clarify the issues raised

Acknowledge all complaints and provide an overview of the complaints process. Advise the customer their issues will be investigated in accordance with the Customer Complaints and Feedback Policy.

If the complaint was received online, acknowledge receipt of the complaint via the customer's preferred channel within 3 days.

When customers lodge a complaint, they sometimes use language which does not align with language used internally by the agency. For example, a customer says they wish to lodge a formal complaint this should be managed as a Level 1 complaint if it does not meet any of the 8 Complaint Escalation Reasons. Everyone in the agency is authorised to receive and finalise complaints.

Lock customer or allocate to owning officer

Level 1 complaints are common activity under the Customer Management Approach (CMA). If the customer is not locked to a Service Officer lock the customer in accordance with CMA and manage the customer complaint to resolution.

If the customer is locked to another Service Officer

Service Officer who receives the complaint:

  • has ownership of the complaint
  • manages the complaint to resolution
  • does not lock the customer

If the customer is locked to a Specialist area

Service Officer who receives the complaint advises the customer:

  • they are managed in another business area
  • due to the particular specialisation, that area is best placed to manage their issues

Cold transfer the call using the following Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone options:

  • CS_Complaint1 IntMS
  • CS_Complaint1 IntNCReg
  • CS_Complaint1 IntNCGen

How to manage the complaint

  • If support from a team leader or SSO is required, go to Step 3
  • Where a decision is required concerning complaint or objection, go to Step 4
  • For staff behavioural complaints, go to Step 5
  • All other complaints, go to Step 6

Advise the customer on their call being answered, they have ‘been transferred to make a complaint’.

If the complaint is about the Officer, the customer is locked to or about the officer managing the call, go to Step 9.

3

Team Leader and SSO support + Read more ...

A Level 1 complaint can be resolved with or without assistance from a Team Leader or Service Support Officer (SSO),

Team Leaders perform an important role in Level 1 complaint management. This role includes:

  • managing and finalising complaints about the behaviour of an individual staff member
  • providing advice and assistance to Service Officers to resolve complaints. This may include taking over a call when a customer requests to speak to a manager
  • listening to call recording of Child Support calls to manage behaviour complaints and to assist Service Officers to resolve complaints
  • monitoring Level 1 complaints (received via phone and written/online) being managed within their team to ensure that they are finalised within 10 working days

Note: there is no longer a need to offer a team leader call back to a customer who wants to speak to a manager. If a Team Leader or site leadership backup is not immediately available to take the call, escalate to Level 2. Advise the customer their call will be transferred to a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team.

If the customer does not accept the Level 1 outcome, this meets escalation reason 8. Warm transfer the customer via the Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone to the escalated Level 2 complaints team. Advise the customer their call is being transferred to a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team.

4

Complaint or objection + Read more ...

If a customer disagrees with a decision and requests the decision be reviewed, explain the decision.

If the customer still wants to proceed with a review of the decision, discuss if:

  • an objection is a suitable option (objection options include an appeal via the Administrative Appeals Tribunal), or
  • there are administrative options that will resolve the customer’s issue

If an objection is an appropriate option, advise of objection rights for the customer and whether the objection needs to be made in writing. See Referrals to General, Care and Part 6A Objections in Customer referral guidelines for Child Support staff

When a customer is dissatisfied with the service provided around a decision, ask questions to ascertain if they are concerned with the decision outcome or the service provided during the decision-making process. Service issues may include the explanation, behaviour of staff, the process or the length of time taken to make a decision.

If the customer's concern is:

  • a service issue they can lodge a complaint rather than an objection
  • the outcome of the decision they should consider lodging an objection

The customer can lodge both an objection and a complaint if they:

  • are dissatisfied with a decision, believe the agency has made a mistake and want it reviewed, and also
  • express dissatisfaction with the service provided with the decision, or the service provided in the objection process

5

Complaints about staff behaviour + Read more ...

A behavioural complaint is a complaint about the manner in which an individual staff member interacted with a customer, not about a technical decision or process.

When a customer makes a behavioural complaint:

  • ask the customer for specific details - what was said and how that led to the complaint
  • create a Level 1 complaint. See Complaints handling Cuba Process Help. This will automatically create a Step 1 complaint intray
  • use the Complaints Handling macro to document the complaint. Ensure that sufficient information is obtained to enable the behavioural complaint to be investigated, but minimal information about the Service Officer (first name, team and site only) is documented in Cuba
  • consider a well-placed and appropriate apology. The NSW Ombudsman guide to an apology (Apologies - a practical guide) explains that an apology should be well placed (timed appropriately - not delayed where possible) and appropriate (in some occasions sympathy and an explanation is more appropriate)
  • explain that complaints about staff are taken seriously. Services Australia intends to provide a high quality service in accordance with the agency’s service commitments to every customer
  • explain that the complaint will be provided to the staff member’s manager to review and discuss
  • thank the customer for taking the time to provide feedback
  • explain the agency does not provide customers with details of feedback provided to staff
  • focus on what can be done to resolve other aspects of their complaint, for example, a decision, delay or process also included in the complaint
  • explain they will not get a call back or a response because behavioural complaints are managed internally

At the end of the call, the Service Officer confirms the above:

  • If the customer is satisfied:
    • Service Officer who took the complaint routes the Step 1 complaint intray to the owning Team Leader (who manages the staff member the complaint is about) and emails the Team Leader to notify of the complaint.
      Note: check Team Leader availability via Microsoft Teams
    • Service Officer documents Team Leader and team details on the intray
    • Service Officer, the behavioural complaint procedure ends here
  • If the customer is not satisfied and requests a call back from the Team Leader:
    • Document these details for the Team Leader to action
    • Advise the customer they will receive a call back from the owning Team Leader within 10 working days to discuss the outcome of their complaint.
  • Owning Team Leader will:
    • review the complaint
    • pass on feedback where appropriate
    • finalise the Level 1 complaint in Cuba

If the customer has raised multiple complaint issues including behaviour:

  • Service Officer will:
  • route the Step 1 complaint intray to the Team Leader who manages the staff member the behaviour complaint is about
  • create a Complaint follow up intray to ensure the other complaint issues are followed up by the relevant Service Officer
  • investigate every other issue and record the outcome in the complaint notepad. This may include contacting the customer to provide an outcome

The Team Leader reviewing the behavioural complaint, is responsible for recording the remedy and outcome of the behaviour complaint and finalising the complaint in Cuba. The Step 1 complaint intray is in the owning Team Leader’s Cuba position and they are responsible for making sure the complaint is finalised, including following up with the Service Officer about any outstanding complaint issues. Any feedback that occurs as a result of a complaint should not be documented in the complaint record.

Transferring the behavioural complaint call directly to a Team Leader if the complaint is about the staff member taking the call or another person in their team

If the customer wants to complain about the staff member taking the call or an officer in their team it may be appropriate to offer an apology as this could satisfy the customer and not require to the call to be escalated. If the call is escalated:

  • record a Level 1 complaint
  • refer the customer to the relevant Team Leader

If the Team Leader and/or site leadership backup is unavailable to take the call, escalate the complaint to the Level 2 Escalated Complaints Team. Advise the customer their call is being transferred to a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team. The Level 2 Officer will finalise the Level 1 complaint as ‘escalated’ and link it to the Level 2 complaint.

The Team Leader actions the behaviour complaint by:

  • reviewing the customer issues
  • listening to relevant call recordings (mandatory)
  • reviewing whether the agency’s service commitments have been met
  • providing any required feedback to their staff
  • finalising the Level 1 complaint, and
  • documenting their investigation
  • calling the customer if a call back has been requested

The Team Leader will record the following information into the Level 1 complaint notepad:

  • Date Reviewed - date the call recording was reviewed
  • Person completing - team leader first name/team/site
  • Feedback provided - yes/no
  • Outcome Summary - either upheld or not upheld and any comments

Once all issues have a remedy and outcome the owning Team Leader who has the Step 1 Complaint intray finalises the complaint in Cuba.

Complaints about Team Leaders

If a complaints is about a Team Leader:

  • advise the customer their call is being transferred to a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team
  • transfer the call to the Level 2 Child Support Escalated Complaints and Feedback Team through the rolodex function in Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone

6

Assess the complaint + Read more ...

Assess the complaint to determine what other support is required. For additional information about managing a specific type of complaint or elements of a complaint, select the appropriate link(s) from the list below:

  • Escalation to Level 2 complaint, see Step 4 in the Contact the customer table
  • Complaints relating to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) that cannot be resolved administratively, including complaints about Business Activity Statements (BAS), tax refund intercepts (TRIP), incomes and complaints with cross agency issues which will require collaboration and liaison with the ATO, escalate to Level 2 via escalation reason 5, see Step 4 in the Contact the customer table
  • Sensitive complaints, including from vulnerable customers
  • Customer aggression
  • Immediate threat of media escalation
  • Market and social research
  • Privacy incidents
  • Personalised services customers
  • Complaints about staff behaviour
  • Complaints from non-customers
  • Transgender, intersex and non-binary complaints

Managing complaint elements

Table 2: This table describes how different types of complaints and/or elements of a complaint are managed. A complaint may contain multiple types and elements.

Complaint type or element

Description

Aggression

Customer aggression + Read more ...

If a customer is displaying aggressive behaviour and there is difficulty in determining the issues or how they are best resolved. For strategies see Customer aggression - Prevention and management.

If the customer has advised they do not want to be contacted by telephone, send a letter to confirm any further issues. See Letters for Child Support customers.

Sensitive

Sensitive complaints, including from vulnerable customers + Read more ...

Sensitive complaints are urgent or high profile issues where the agency's actions may have or could harm a vulnerable customer. Sensitive complaints also include issues which could adversely affect the reputation of the agency.

Vulnerable customers are those customers who are more susceptible to disadvantage due to their personal circumstances.

If a customer is distressed, determine the source and offer referrals such as Family Relationship Advice Line, parent support or source other appropriate referrals from the community services directory.

If a complaint is sensitive or having an impact on a vulnerable customer, assess whether to escalate the complaint to Level 2 under:

  • reason 3 - the complaint is fundamentally sensitive
  • reason 5 - complex, cross programme or multiple issues

Child Support staff must identify customers who may benefit from management by Personalised Services. Discuss with a Team Leader to determine suitability for the referral.

Staff must not remove their headset before ending a phone call, see Contact with Child Support customers, Ending phone calls.

Media escalation

Media escalation + Read more ...

When managing a Level 1 complaint a customer may indicate they intend to speak with the media. This in itself is not an escalation reason. Discuss the reasons the customer wants to go to the media. Attempt to resolve their dissatisfaction.

If the customer confirms they will not be contacting the media, retain the Level 1 complaint but notify relevant manager. The manager will assess the matter and may choose to refer details to the Media Section.

If the customer indicates they have already spoken to the media, escalate to a Level 2 complaint under reason 6 - Evidence of media involvement.

For more information see Escalation Reasons.

The Complaints Officer will record a Level 2 complaint and finalise the Level 1 complaint. The Complaints Officer will review the situation and determine if it should be escalated to the agency’s Media Section.

For the Service Officer, procedure ends here.

Market and Social Research

Market and Social Research + Read more ...

A customer may indicate they intend to complain about contact regarding Market and Social Research that has been undertaken by the agency. Escalate the complaint to Level 2 by warm transferring the customer to the Complaints team via Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone (Reason 3 Complaints Escalation Reasons)

Record a Level 1 complaint in Cuba using the following Category and Issue:

  • Category: Service Channel
  • Issue: Survey

The Service Officer from the Escalated Complaints Team will finalise the Step 1 complaint as 'escalated'.

For the Service Officer, procedure ends here.

Privacy

Privacy incidents + Read more ...

If any part of the complaint relates to an alleged privacy incident, see the Privacy intranet page.

A privacy incident does not meet an escalation to Level 2. Follow the privacy guidelines. Immediately refer the matter to the Privacy Team

Failure to report a privacy incident in a timely manner can result in serious consequences. Timely action can reduce the risk of physical harm to customers or other negative consequences.

PS

Personalised Services (PS) customers + Read more ...

Service Officer receives a complaint from a PS customer

A Service Officer may receive a call from a customer who is managed by PS as they have not entered their reference number.

All customers managed in PS have a Case Manager (PSSO) and require intensive customer management, so it is extremely important that these customers are transferred to PS. Advise the customer they can lodge a complaint with PS. Cold transfer the customer to PS, using the Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone selecting - Complaint1PS.

If the customer is agitated or distressed transfer the call as a warm transfer.

If the call is from a customer who has the Restricted Services indicator in Cuba - 'Restricted Service - Trf ALL calls to x404020’ do not engage the customer, transfer the call immediately to this extension and email the PS officer to notify of the customer’s contact. No identity checks or explanation to the customer should be conducted.

For questions or support in managing this transfer speak to Team Leader. The Team Leader can seek support from a PS Team Leader.

PSSO receives a complaint from a PS customer

A PSSO who receives a Level 1 complaint will take ownership of the complaint and manage to resolution. This may require seeking technical support from a Service Support Officer or a PS Team Leader talking to the customer.

If the complaint is regarding an issue which arose prior to the case being managed in PS, the PSSO will liaise with other business areas as appropriate and respond to the customer (as per the PS approach of single point of contact).

If feedback is required for a Service Officer who provided a service prior to the customer being managed in PS, this will be provided by the owning Team Leader. The PSSO should send feedback to the Team Leader via the Staff Feedback Tool.

Non-customers

Complaints from individuals who are not customers + Read more ...

Child Support complaints from individuals who are not customers should be escalated as Level 2 complaints to the Escalated Complaints team (Reason 5 - Complex, cross programme or multiple issues escalation). Advise the customer their call is being transferred to a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team.

A non-customer is a person:

  • with no child support case
  • who may have been misidentified as a Child Support customer, and
  • who has been affected by the actions of Child Support, such as mistakenly receiving Child Support correspondence

A non-customer is not:

  • a new customer calling to register a new case
  • a person calling to advise of the death of a child support customer
  • a customer representative
  • an employer of a Child Support customer

Note: if a caller is not an authorised representative and is calling to lodge a complaint on behalf of a customer, advise the process for becoming an authorised representative.

If a caller advises they do not have a child support case, search Cuba to confirm this. This is because the caller:

  • may be unaware they have a child support case
  • may mistakenly consider themselves to be a non-customer when they in fact are, for example their case is private collect, or the case has ended

If the caller is not a customer and they want to lodge a complaint warm transfer them to the Complaints team.

Gender

Transgender, intersex and non-binary complaints + Read more ...

When assisting transgender, intersex and non-binary customers, be conscious of the agency's Service Commitments which state in part, 'We will listen and work with you to understand your individual and cultural needs'. For example, while a customer may be regarded under the Social Security Act as the gender they were at birth, staff should address the customer by their preferred name or title.

If a complaint is about the age, sex, religion, race, sexual orientation, gender identity, intersex status, marital or any other personal characteristic (perceived or known) of agency staff, staff must treat it with sensitivity and with regard to anti-discrimination principles. This may likely be a trigger to seek support from the Team Leader or site back up, or to make a referral to a Level 2 complaint under escalation reason 3 if the complaint cannot be managed by the Level 1 service officer.

Record a Level 1 complaint in Cuba using the following Category and Issue:

  • Category: Other
  • Issue: Survey

If the complaint meets an escalation reason refer to Level 2 by warm transferring the customer to the Complaints team via Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone. Advise the customer their call is being transferred to a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team.

The Service Officer from the Escalated Complaints Team will finalise the Step 1 complaint as 'escalated'.

For the Service Officer, procedure ends here.

Children

Complaints from children + Read more ...

Cases involving parents and carers under the age of 18 are managed by the Parent Support Team, who are responsible for managing all other aspects, from case registration until the parent or carer turns 18.

Complaints from children are considered sensitive in nature, and must be managed accordingly.

Staff are not expected to be experts in child safety. The role is to identify child safety concerns and respond appropriately. In all interactions, staff must be alert and responsive to listen, observe and ask questions in relations to child safety concerns.

When assisting individuals under 18, be aware of the agency's Child Safe Policy. 'We are all responsible to help children feel safe when interacting with us, and take their concerns seriously'.

If a child is in immediate danger or risk of harm:

  • A delegate must call 000
  • All other staff must contact a member of the leadership team immediately

Escalation

If the complaint meets an escalation reason:

  • advise the customer their call is being transferred to a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team
  • warm transfer the call to the Level 2 Complaints Officer via the Escalated Complaints SharePoint page

Call unable to be warm transferred

If unable to warm transfer the customer:

  • advise the customer they will receive a call back within 24 hours from a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team
  • Email the Escalated Complaints mailbox with the subject 'Urgent Complaint Call back request for parent/carer under 18'. Include the customer's reference number, contact details and a brief summary of the complaint
  • record a level 1 complaint
  • document details of the complaint issue and why the customer was not warm transferred
  • route the Step 1 complaint intray immediately to:
    • Office: Client and Community Branch
    • Steam/Team: CR SC02 COM MEL TM1
    • Position: A - Ref to Level 2

Urgent or high profile Escalation

Urgent, high profile issues or incidents are referred to the Escalation Coordination team. The escalation process is mandatory, and must be followed correctly. A National Manager must clear all escalations before being sent to the team. See Risk identification and management of threats to the safety or welfare of a child.

Investigate the complaint

Table 3: This table describes the steps in investigating and responding to a Level 1 complaint.

Step

Action

1

Assess urgency of the complaint issues + Read more ...

Determine if the customer complaint requires immediate action or escalation such as:

  • a security incident where the customer is threatening harm to themselves or others and has not withdrawn the threat when challenged
  • The customer has contacted the media or media organisation has made the complaint
  • an issue that will have a major impact on 1 or more customers, for example a significant privacy incident which needs to be resolved urgently
  • the outcomes of the complaint may influence another pending decision for example Change of Assessment (COA) or Objection
  • a data integrity issue which is/will have a significant impact on customers

In the event that prioritisation of a new complaint may impact on the timely resolution of existing complaints, advise the Team Leader of the priority action being taken and discuss the management of work on hand. It may be necessary for some work to be reallocated to ensure existing commitments are met.

Ensure the customer is kept regularly updated on the progress of their complaint by making contact at least every 5 working days.

Once the complaint issue(s) are identified and prioritised:

  • make a commitment to investigate the issues
  • advise the customer their complaint is being investigated as a Level 1 complaint
  • give the customer an expected timeframe for resolution

2

Record complaint + Read more ...

Record the Level 1 complaint, see Recording Child Support complaints and compliments in Cuba and Complaints handling Cuba process help and a Step 1 Complaint intray will automatically generate.

If a complaint meets an escalation reason the Level 2 Service Officer finalises the processing of the Level 1 complaint as 'escalated'. See Recording Child Support complaints and compliments in Cuba.

Record the Level 1 complaints but do not finalise the complaint. Advise the customer their call is being referred to a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team.

3

Employer Services complaints + Read more ...

An employer or third party may complain about their interaction with Child Support or its officers. Record complaints made by an employer on the organisation’s Cuba record rather than the associated parent record (unless the employer is complaining as a representative on behalf of the parent). Record Step 1 complaints from organisations in the Communications window selecting STEP 1 COMPLNT as the Reason.

Note: Employer Services do not undertake regular and/or direct contact with payers or payees and are exempt under the Customer Management Approach. If an employer or third party is complaining as a representative on behalf of the parent or is themselves a parent, the Employer Service Officer refers the call to the owning business line following the Customer referral guidelines for Child Support.

Complaints from employers or third parties may also meet the criteria of the Complaints Escalation Reasons. For more information go to Step 4 in Table 1.

4

Recording compliments + Read more

If a customer gives a compliment about a Service Officer, the service provided by Child Support or a product record the compliment in the Communications window as follows:

  • Type: choose the method the compliment was received
  • Reason: Provide Information
  • Category: Communication
  • Issue:
    • Compliment Service Officer - a compliment about a specific Service Officer
    • Compliment CSP - a compliment about Child Support services
    • Compliment Prod - a compliment about a product

Record the compliment in the Staff Feedback Tool. Include the name of the staff member’s manager and your SSM in the manager field. Select the Request Reply box. For more information, see Staff Feedback Tool.

5

Investigate issues + Read more ...

Wherever possible investigate the issues raised while the customer is on the phone. Consider all the issues and any other relevant concerns identified. This includes any issues that have not been identified by the customer but could impact on or contribute to the level of their dissatisfaction. Use available resources to assist in the investigation including:

  • System records
  • Child Support Guide
  • Operational Blueprint documents
  • Operational Messaging
  • Cuba Helps
  • Technical Support
  • Network News Update

If a resolution is achieved during the call, finalise the complaint in real time. See Recording Child Support complaints and compliments in Cuba.

Explain to the customer their complaint has been resolved and no further action will be taken.

6

Matter not resolved during initial contact + Read more ...

If the matter cannot be resolved during the initial contact, confirm the customer’s issues and advise the issues will be investigated and they will receive a call back. Provide the customer with the date they will next be contacted. This should be no more than 5 working days after the complaint is lodged. If the customer says they do not want to be called back, ask if they want a written response upon resolution of the complaint. Document the action required to provide an outcome.

Additional support and information can be accessed after the call from:

  • Service Support Officer
  • Team Leader for advice on managing the customer
  • Specialist areas

The owning Service Officer is responsible for undertaking all necessary actions to resolve the complaint where it cannot be resolved in real time. Use the Complaints Handling macro to record an investigation plan in Cuba. See Recording Child Support complaints and compliments in Cuba.

Contact the customer at least every 5 days to provide an update on the progress of their complaint. Use a client call back intray to ensure customer contact is made as agreed.

Throughout the management of a complaint, customers should be advised of the expected timeframe to finalise their matter and subsequent escalation options should they remain dissatisfied with the outcome.

Examples

If the Service Officer is waiting on:

  • technical advice to progress resolution of the customer’s complaint issues, advise the customer of the anticipated time frame for this
  • a CIF to be actioned to finalise a complaint:
    • advise the customer their matter has been escalated to the appropriate area for resolution, however it may take some time to be resolved
    • close the complaint
    • Consider creating a client call back intray to monitor the progress of the CIF and keep the customer informed or ask the customer to call back for updates

Documentation

All documentation relating to the complaint, including the investigation findings, call reviews and contact with the customer must be documented in the complaints notepad.

7

Resolution + Read more

A Level 1 complaint is considered to be resolved if:

  • the customer has been advised of the complaint outcome
  • the customer has been advised of any suitable options for escalation
  • all reasonable actions have been taken to resolve the issue
  • the customer needs to go to court, apply to the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT), lodge forms or attend to other matters outside Child Support control to resolve their specific issues

Finalise complaint

Table 4: This table describes the process for finalising a Level 1 complaint.

Step

Action

1

Contact customer + Read more ...

Summarise the complaint and inform the customer of the remedy, discuss the investigation and how the conclusions were reached. Discuss any available administrative options.

If the customer's complaint was about issues impacting customer service delivery, advise the customer the feedback will be provided for the purposes of Business improvement.

Advise the customer of the findings of the investigation into their complaint by advising of actions taken to remedy the situation.

Where an error has been identified or the service commitments not met, consider offering an apology.

If the customer remains dissatisfied with the response or remedy to their complaint, advise the customer of their further escalation options within the agency.

See Recording Child Support complaints and compliments in Cuba - Recording complaint outcomes for information about classifying complaint categories and issues.

If phone contact is unsuccessful, or the customer has requested a written response:

  • send a unique letter in Cuba (the Complaints Outcome letter previously issued from Cuba is no longer available), see Letters Cuba process help, create a letter using fill-in fields and Letters for Child Support customers
  • in the letter, explain the outcome of the investigation and advise the customer to call if they need further information. Note: all written communication must be in accordance with the Services Australia Writing Style Guide

Refer the customer to any support services that may be appropriate. See Social worker and other service referrals for child support customers.

3

Record outcome + Read more ...

Classify the outcome of each qualitative issue in Cuba as upheld or not upheld, withdrawn or escalated. See Recording Child Support complaints and compliments in Cuba - Recording complaint outcomes.

If the complaint includes Categories and Issues finalised with the combinations of upheld, not upheld and withdrawn the overall status of the complaint will be partially upheld.

A customer may elect to withdraw a complaint at any time during the process. Withdrawal means the customer asked the Service Officer not to proceed with the complaint investigation.

See Recording Child Support complaints and compliments in Cuba - Recording complaint outcomes, for a full description of complaint outcomes.

The Source of Issue is the team where the complaint originated from, that is the team of the person the complaint is about, not where the customer is currently allocated or the team that received the complaint.

4

Further escalation + Read more ...

If the customer is not satisfied at the completion of the Level 1 complaint warm transfer them to the Escalated Complaints Team via Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone. During the warm transfer provide the Complaints officer with:

  • a summary of the customers complaint(s)
  • the level 1 outcome provided
  • the outcome the customer is hoping to achieve, and
  • any other concerns that may impact on resolution

The Service Officer documents the complaint discussions with the customer in the Level 1 complaint notepad. Do not finalise the Level 1 complaint.

For the Service Officer, procedure ends here.

The Complaints Officer will finalise the Level 1 Complaint as 'escalated'. Create a Level 2 complaint, and manage the Level 2 complaint.

Call unable to be warm transferred

If unable to warm transfer the customer:

  • advise the customer they will receive a call back in 2 working days from a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team
  • record a level 1 complaint
  • document details of complaint issue and why the customer was not warm transferred
  • route the Step 1 complaint intray immediately to:

Region: National Office

Office: Client and Community Branch

Stream/Team: CR SC02 COM MELB TM1

Position: A - Ref to Level 2

Note: complaints are best resolved via the phone. Intray referrals should only be made if the customer cannot talk to a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team at that time. The customer must be advised to expect a call back from a Service Officer in the Escalated Complaints Team.

If call wait times are being experienced when referring a complaint consider offering a call back to the customer and refer the Level 1 intray to the above position for Level 2 complaint management.

5

Unlocking customer + Read more ...

If phone contact is unsuccessful, or the customer has requested a written response, send a Complaint Outcome letter explaining the outcome of the investigation and advising the customer to call if they need further information. See Letters Cuba process help, create a letter using fill-in fields.

The customer can only be unlocked when they have been advised of the outcome of the complaint. Where the customer has not been verbally advised of the outcome of the complaint following appropriate attempts, auto-unlock for 14 days. Minimum collection activity must be actioned before unlocking. See Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support.

6

Feedback to individuals + Read more ...

Feedback must be provided for all upheld and partially upheld complaints within 14 days of the complaint being finalised.

Complete the Staff Feedback Tool to provide feedback to the relevant Team Leader about their Service Officer’s management of the customer/case, see Staff Feedback Tool.

Issues or errors that are not a part of the complaint or positive feedback can also form part of the feedback to the Team Leader. Complete the Staff Feedback Tool for each Service Officer involved or multiple complaint issues.

A Complaint Feedback intray is automatically created when the complaint is upheld and finalised. The Service Officer deletes the Complaint Feedback intray once they have provided all required feedback via the Staff Feedback Tool.

7

Business Improvement - Reporting potential systemic issues + Read more ...

Child Support is committed to ensuring customers receive the best service possible. Complaints can help business areas within Child Support to improve the services provided. Resolving a person’s complaint is not the last step in the process. The complaint may point to a systemic or administrative problem within a business area or the customer may suggest ways to improve child support legislation or policy.

Systemic issues are those that affect customer service delivery for multiple Child Support customers. Systemic issues relate to internal or external policies, procedures or legislation that affects Child Support service outcomes or work practices or tools that do not support positive service outcomes.

  • Discuss any potential systemic issue identified from the complaint with the Team Leader. Send an email to Multicultural and Tailored Services Branch Systemic Issues with the subject line 'Systemic Issue Notification' to advise of the system issue. Provide a summary of the identified issue and customer details in the email
  • If the complaint is about child support legislation or policy, advise the customer they can contact the Department of Social Services to provide feedback. Customers can visit the Department of Social Services website for contact details.
    Note: only discuss current legislation policy with the customer. Services Australia cannot provide comment on pending or potential matters such as legislative changes or Parliamentary Inquiry recommendations
  • Email business improvement ideas to Child Support Smart Centres Innovate team
  • Consider using the feedback button in the Operational Blueprint page if feedback relates to a particular process
