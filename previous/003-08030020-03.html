5

Customer qualified for other payments + Read more ...

Customer may be qualified for JSP, Parenting Payment (PP) or Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) but not payable due to any of the following:

  • failure to enter into or comply with an Job Plan
  • is subject to a mutual obligation failure post-cancellation non-payment period
  • is subject to a serious failure period for persistent non-compliance
  • voluntary unemployment and subject to an unemployment preclusion period or post-cancellation non-payment period or Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP)
  • unemployment due to misconduct and subject to an Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP)
  • refusal of a job offer or failure to start suitable work and subject to a work refusal post cancellation non-payment period or serious failure period
  • failure to provide requested information
  • failure to comply with a notification requirement
  • Seasonal Work Preclusion Period (SWPP)
  • Move to Area of Lower Employment Prospects (MALEP)

Declaring a major disaster + Read more ...

Disaster payments can only be paid where the relevant Minister has made a determination by gazetted notice that a disaster is a major disaster for the purpose of the Social Security Act 1991.

Has the disaster been gazetted by the relevant Minister?

  • Yes, go to Step 4

4

Determine if the customer is unable to earn a sufficient livelihood for themselves and their dependants + Read more ...

It is not enough that the customer is not earning a sufficient livelihood. They must be unable to earn a sufficient livelihood and the situation must be unavoidable.

Service Officers should exercise their discretion and carefully examine the customer's circumstances when making the decision.

Although payments under the Paid Parental Leave scheme (Parental Leave Pay (PPL) or Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)) are not treated as income, they may be included when assessing if a customer is unable to earn a sufficient livelihood.

Is the customer unable to earn a sufficient livelihood for themselves and their dependants?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 9

5

Available funds over $5,000 + Read more ...

Does the customer have more than $5,000 in available funds?

While the SpB Available Funds Test has not been determined at this point, if the customer has more than $5,000 in available funds:

  • further evidence may be needed to determine the customers circumstances, and
  • any committed funds that might impact on the available funds test

Financial investments such as shares should be included in the Available Funds Test.

A customer may receive one-off support payments from the Australian Red Cross or a one-off immediate needs support package from the State Government. If the one-off payment puts the customer’s available funds above $5,000, any funds over $5,000 may be considered committed funds (and excluded from the available funds test) if the customer can provide evidence of intended immediate future expenditure and these are deemed as exceptional or unforeseen expenses.

Discretion should be applied to these vulnerable customers.

Any remaining funds will be included in the available funds test.

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Sufficient support from other sources + Read more ...

Discretion should be exercised to determine whether the customer has a reasonable means of support, from any source in Australia or overseas.

Victims of trafficking must provide a Red Cross supporting letter confirming their Red Cross living allowance has stopped.

Can the customer obtain sufficient support from other sources?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Avoidable financial hardship + Read more ...

Use discretion. Consider whether the customer could have avoided financial hardship and whether they knew at the time of taking that action (such as a voluntary act or misconduct) or of spending/disposing of funds that they would be placing themselves in hardship.

Examples include:

  • persevering with unprofitable business venture
  • being voluntarily unemployed/serving an UNPP
  • spending on unnecessary items
  • disposing of money by gifting or other means without adequate return

Bank statements may be necessary to better understand the customer’s situation.

Has the customer knowingly contributed to their financial hardship?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Customer may qualify for SpB + Read more ...

If the customer has:

  • lodged a claim for SpB refer to Progress of claim or to determine if the claim should be escalated see Request for urgent processing of a new claim when experiencing financial hardship. Procedure ends here
  • not lodged a claim, tell them to lodge a claim for SpB. See Step 1 in Table 3

9

Customer may not qualify to receive SpB + Read more ...

If the customer has not lodged a claim, it is not possible to determine their eligibility unless a claim is lodged.

Tell the customer to test their eligibility by lodging a claim.

Record a DOC on the customer's record with details of the conversation.

If the customer is enquiring about a previously lodged SpB claim, see Progress of claim.

2

Online claim, Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) or paper claim + Read more ...

The easiest way to claim SpB is online. Customers must be registered with myGov and have a linked Centrelink online account.

ACC should be used for customers deemed unable or unsuitable to complete an online claim or nominees.

The SpB online claim and ACC are located under:

Payments and Claims>Claims>Make a Claim>Other payments>Special Benefit>Apply for Special Benefit

Note: non-resident parents or guardians claiming on behalf of an Australian citizen or permanent resident child cannot claim online. They must complete the paper Claim for Special Benefit (SU004). The claim must be completed in the child's name and lodged on the child's record, not the parent's record. See Adding a customer to the system if the child does not have their own record.

Tell the customer to lodge claim with all supporting documents as soon as possible. This will make sure the payment can start from the earliest possible date.

If the customer is under minimum school leaving age, or in crisis and has not yet seen a social worker, refer them to a social worker immediately.

Eligible customers will receive payments or concession cards from the date they submit a complete claim with all relevant supporting documents. Different processes apply for customers in vulnerable circumstances.
