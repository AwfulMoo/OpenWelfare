Processing multimatch MFU activities – CIS staff

A multimatch manual follow up (MFU) activity occurs

Table 1: This table describes the steps to action a customer record when an MFU activity for a multimatch occurs. For detailed information about CIS, go to International Branch homepage.

Step

Action

1

Department of Home Affairs datamatch + Read more ...

When a Service Officer is either actioning a new claim or other activity relating to a Social Security or family assistance payment or a concession card, an attempt to exchange information with the Department of Home Affairs may occur.

If the exchange is expected but is not successful because the attempt has located more than one record which matches the customer's details, then a Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity is created. This MFU will not prevent continued action on the customer's record.

For New Claim activities Centrelink International Services (CIS) will advise the referring office when these activities are finalised.

Is the Service Officer in the Residence Assessment Team in CIS?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2 table
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Proof of residence from the Department of Home Affairs + Read more ...

Is the customer requiring proof of residence from the Department of Home Affairs?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the activity can be finalised without waiting for the MFU to be finalised by CIS. Procedure ends here

3

Urgency of new claim processing + Read more ...

Is the new claim urgent? (for example, prison release, family and domestic violence situation, natural disaster or hardship)

  • Yes, contact the CIS Residence Assessment Team directly to request the MFU to be actioned immediately. Go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check for the finalisation of the MFU + Read more ...

Continue checking the claim regularly to determine whether the MFU has been finalised by CIS.

  • A DOC will be created on the record by the CIS Service Officer when correct details have been located and updated and the MFU has been finalised
  • When the MFU has been finalised and details provided, continue processing the claim

Procedure ends here.

Processing multimatch MFU activities – CIS staff

For Centrelink International Services (CIS) staff only

Table 2: This table describes the steps for CIS staff to process an MFU activity when a multimatch occurs.

Step

Action

1

CIS staff only + Read more ...

Confirm keyword priority of customer's record.

Service Officer to check the customer's Centrelink record and the Department of Home Affairs database for information to assist them when contacting the customer.

If a match is expected in an activity that is not a new claim and multiple matches are returned, an MFU with the keyword MULIRN will be created for action by the CIS team. This MFU will not prevent continued action on the customer's record.

For New Claim Activities a secondary keyword 'NCLM' identifies the MFU as high priority as the information is required to assist in resolution of the new claim and must be completed before the review is finalised.

Non-Claim Activities will have a secondary keyword of 'PORTM' (arising from portability action). These are given high priority and will not prevent the related activity from being completed.

Non-Claim Activities will have a secondary keyword of either 'DATAM' (arising from a datamatch). These are given medium priority and will not prevent the related activity from being completed.

For Non-Claim Activities which have not had a secondary keyword assigned, they are given low priority and will not prevent the related activity from being completed on the customer's record.

Has the MFU been given a High or Medium priority or has an urgent referral been received from a service centre/Smart Centre Call?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, provide all details received in a DOC and continue with the activity. The CIS team will investigate and update.

2

Check for Immigration record + Read more ...

Check the Department of Home Affairs database for the customer's correct immigration record based on the information provided by the customer and the Service Officer's investigation.

Has the correct matching details been found for the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Contact the customer to verify details + Read more ...

Contact the customer and confirm identity and if an interpreter is required. Ask the customer if they are able to confirm the held information on file relates to them.

Ask the customer questions to ascertain the customer’s main residence and that we have these correctly recorded on the Customer Personal Details (CPD) screen.

Has the customer provide information to assist checking the Department of Home Affairs database?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, ask the customer to provide more information to help assist in investigation. Go to Step 4

4

Matching immigration record not found + Read more ...

If a match is not found, the CIS Service Officer should:

  • check the customer details as coded by the Service Officer
  • verify any doubtful or imprecise details with the customer, and
  • search the Department of Home Affairs database again with all confirmed information widening the search parameters

Has a correct match been found for the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Matching immigration record not found + Read more ...

  • Did the Service Officer incorrectly code details on the record which incorrectly created a status where an immigration record was expected?
  • This could include:
    • Country of Birth
    • Has the customer travelled since 1994
    • Has the customer changed their name, that is, marriage, by law
    • Has the customer been correctly linked to a third party- carer/caree, partner, child etc?
  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Department of Home Affairs record still not located + Read more ...

  • Check if the customer has any travel documents (for example, other passports) which are not already recorded on the record.
  • Check the Department of Home Affairs database for variations by widening the search options, if new details provided.
  • Even though a multimatch has occurred, it is possible that none of the records matching to the customer are correct.

Has the correct immigration record been found for the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Match incorrectly expected + Read more ...

  • Correct the coding that incorrectly created the multimatch status: For example, the Service Officer incorrectly recorded that the customer has travelled since 1 September 1994 on the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen.
  • Go to the Immigration Link Details (RSIML) screen
    • Code 'No' in the IRN expected: field
    • Record the date of the activity in the Date: field
    • Set the Immigration match result: field to CIS Staff No Match (CNM)
  • Select the MFU from the Activity List (AL) screen and finalise the review
  • Create a DOC advising the outcome of the investigation
  • Transfer DOC to the originating office

Procedure ends here.

8

Match expected but not found + Read more ...

If the match expectation appears correct but a match is not found then in low priority cases it may be expedient to set the IRN indicator to MAX - A partial search has failed to identify a valid IRN or MAY.

Future manual search may be required and finalise the MFU. These cases can be identified and investigated further when CIS resources permit.

If the match expectation appears correct but a match is not found then:

  • Go to the Immigration Link Details (RSIML) screen and code the customer's verified immigration reference number in the IRN:
    • Set IRN Indicator to CNM (CIS staff-No match)
    • Select the MFU from the Activity List (AL) screen and finalise the review
    • Create a DOC advising the match has not been found and the MFU has been finalised

Procedure ends here.

9

Correct IRN match found + Read more ...

  • Go to the NU screen and code the IRN.
  • Go to the Immigration Link Details (RSIML) screen and code the IRN indicator:
    • Set IRN indicator to CPM (CIS staff - Perfect Match)
    • Select the MFU from the Activity List (AL) screen and finalise the review
    • Create a DOC advising the match has been found and the MFU has been finalised
    • For new claims transfer the DOC back to the office processing the claim, for any other activities to be completed
    • Finalise the DOC
