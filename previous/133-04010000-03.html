Viewing advance details and applying for an advance payment online

Table 1: Staff can view Staff Training Simulations on the Digital Support Products sub-site.

Item

Description

1

Accessing Advance Payments online + Read more ...

If the customer is using:

  • their Centrelink online account, see Centrelink online account help - Apply for an advance payment
  • Express Plus Centrelink mobile app, see Item 2

2

Applying for an advance payment using Express Plus Centrelink mobile app + Read more ...

The Advance Payments service will display the last 2 years of the customer's previous advance applications. This includes:

  • income support payment
  • Mobility Allowance (MOB)
  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB)

Customers can also view their advance eligibility and apply for an advance.

The mobile app view of Advance Payments will show:

  • If the customer is ineligible for an advance payment. Customers can select View details for information about each advance type
  • The next available advance date if the customer is:
    • eligible today. No date will display
    • not eligible today. Where the system can determine a future date, this date will show. It will also display a disclaimer that the date may change if the person's circumstances change
    • not eligible today. Where the system cannot determine a future date, a message will display the reasons for this. For example, they may have an outstanding debt
  • Current advances: This will display an advance a customer is currently repaying:
    • payment type
    • balance outstanding
    • total paid
    • total amount advanced
    • advance start date
    • fortnightly repayments
  • Previous advances: This will display the last 2 years of advance applications including:
    • payment type
    • application status (current, rejected, finalised)
    • application date

To help a customer apply for an advance, see Item 3.

3

Applying for an advance + Read more ...

A customer can select Apply for advance to begin their application for an advance. Eligible customers may apply for multiple advances, but must complete one application at a time.

An information bar will display if the customer is not eligible for one or more advance types.

The online application will assess the customer's eligibility based on their responses.

During the application, the customer will:

  • Select the advance type they wish to apply for. For FTB advances, customers can select either a One off or Regular advance
  • Advise how much money they have left over each fortnight after paying normal expenses. This will determine if the customer can afford to repay the advance. The amount offered will not exceed the amount they can afford to repay or the maximum amount available. If the customer cannot afford to repay the minimum advance repayment the advance will reject (excluding MOB advance)
  • Provide a start date when applying for an FTB regular advance
  • Enter the amount they are applying for. This amount must be within the displayed minimum and maximum amounts (excluding MOB advance)
  • Advise if they would like a single payment or 2 instalments. This is for social security entitlement advances. If the customer wants 2 instalments, they must specify the:
    • amount of the first instalment, and
    • date for the second instalment. The date of the second instalment must be within 6 months from the date of grant

Where the customer is under Income Management, it will advise that the advance will be 100% income managed.

Once processed, the customer will see:

  • Details of:
    • amount they have to repay
    • final payment date
    • effect on their future payments
  • Information on financial management support services. This occurs when it is their fifth or subsequent FTB advance request within the last 6 month. See Item 6 in the Problems and information - online advance not available or not paying table

4

Processing times + Read more ...

Advance payments will:

  • go into the same bank account as the customer's regular payment as an immediate payment, and
  • use the standard processing times for their payment type

A message will advise the customer that the money will deposit into their account within:

  • 1 to 2 working days for an income support payment advance, or
  • 2 working days for a Family Tax Benefit (FTB) advance

5

Advance rejected + Read more ...

The system will:

  • advise of the rejection reason and
  • record a DOC

Family Tax Benefit (FTB)

The system will offer information on financial management support services if the advance rejects for any of the following reasons:

  • due to hardship (HAR)
  • less than the minimum amount is available (LMA)
  • reduced or suspended recovery in place for any advance payment (RSR), or
  • the customer is repaying an FTB advance payment granted more than 1 year prior (ROA)

See Item 6 in the Problems and information - online advance not available or not paying table.

Mobility Allowance (MOB)

A Manual Follow-up (MFU) will display on the customer's record for a Service Officer to investigate any follow up actions.

Codes

Rejection reasons for advance payments will record on the Centrelink system for customers who are not eligible for an advance. The online service will display:

  • the reasons why the application rejected,
  • a receipt number, and
  • details of the appeals process

6

DOCs + Read more ...

The system will create a DOC when:

  • the system grants an advance
  • the system rejects an advance request
  • the system advises the customer they are ineligible for at least one advance type
  • a request to cease a regular advance via online services was unsuccessful

Making a full or partial repayment online

Table 2: Staff can view Staff Training Simulations on the Digital Support Products sub-site.

Step

Action

1

Accessing Advance Payments online + Read more ...

The Advance Payments summary page will display current advances. For a current advance, the screen will display:

  • payment type
  • balance outstanding
  • total paid
  • total amount advanced
  • advance start date
  • fortnightly repayments

If the customer is using:

  • their Centrelink online account, see Centrelink online account help - Make an advance repayment
  • Express Plus Centrelink mobile app
    • Select the Advance Payment tile, or
    • Select Advance Payments > View/Apply/Manage Advance Payments from the left menu
    • Go to Step 2

2

Customer selects the advance they want to make a payment towards + Read more ...

Customer will have the following options to select from:

  • Make a payment
  • Adjust repayment
  • Discontinue advance (for FTB regular advances)

To make a repayment, the customer should select Make a payment.

3

Make a payment screen + Read more ...

The system will provide the customer with 2 payment options. Customers can select the payment option they prefer:

  • Credit card or debit card, the following message will display:
    • 'You can use credit or debit card to make a payment. By using your credit card, you may be charged additional fees or interest by your bank. You should check this before making a payment'
    • Go to Step 4
  • Post Billpay, the following message will display (for Centrelink online account only):
    • 'You can pay via Australia Post’s Billpay service to make a payment in person at any post office with cash, cheque, a payment from your bank account or Paypal. If payment is made by cheque, you will need to wait 7 working days before you can apply for a new advance'
    • Go to Step 6

4

Credit or debit card payments + Read more ...

Payment information.

The customer will see the payment amount they have nominated to pay.

Customer will need to enter the following information:

  • Cardholder name
  • Card number
  • Expiry date
  • Security code (CCV)
  • Confirmation - 'I confirm the payment details entered are correct'

If all details are correct, they can select Pay now.

5

Receipt + Read more ...

The system will display a Receipt number and advise:

  • if payment has been successful
  • the amount paid and payment date
  • the payment option used
  • details of the credit or debit card used

If the payment is unsuccessful, the customer can try again.

If payment is successful, and the customer meets all eligibility requirements, they can apply for a new advance payment.

Procedure ends here.

6

Post Billpay + Read more ...

Make payments at an Australia Post outlet.

Repayments made by cheque may prevent a customer claiming a new advance for up to 7 working days. This allows enough time for the payment to clear.

Express Plus Centrelink mobile app

A Post Billpay barcode will display on the screen along with:

  • Next steps
  • Biller code
  • Reference number

For the repayment process to be finalised, the customer must make their repayment via Post Billpay.

The system will record the repayment on the customer’s record immediately (with the exception of cheque repayments).

Once payment occurs, go to Step 7.

7

Post Billpay payment completed + Read more ...

Once the payment is successful, customers can apply for a new advance payment if they meet all eligibility requirements.

Increase or decrease regular repayment amount online

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Accessing Advance Payments online + Read more ...

Centrelink online account

  • Select Advance Payments from the homepage under Favourite services. Customers can customise their Favourite services
  • Advance Payments is in the default list
  • If the customer has removed it:
    • select Menu from the top left corner of the homepage
    • select Advance Payments

Express Plus Centrelink mobile app

  • Select the Advance Payment tile, or
  • Select Advance Payments > View/Apply/Manage Advance Payments from the left menu

2

Customer wishes to change their repayment amount + Read more ...

Customer selects Adjust repayments.

The Adjust repayments page will display:

  • a drop down list of current advances
  • the current repayment amount - this may be different to the minimum repayment amount if the customer has made a change
  • the outstanding balance amount
  • the final repayment date - the system works this out using the current repayment amount
  • the new repayment amount - the customer will need to enter the new repayment amount
  • a calculate button to show how the change will impact the repayment date

When the customer enters a new repayment amount, 'Will you have enough money left over to cover your routine expenses?' will display.

The customer answers Yes/ No and selects Next.

Does the customer select Yes?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the customer will go to the Receipt page. This will advise the customer that the update was unsuccessful. Go to Step 7

3

Repayment amount change + Read more ...

If the customer wants to modify the repayment amount, if the amount is to be:

  • increased, go to Step 4
  • decreased, go to Step 5

4

Customer wishes to increase their repayment amount + Read more ...

The customer can increase their repayment amount to double the minimum repayment amount.

For example, if the minimum repayment amount is $38.50, the maximum repayment amount can be $77.00.

This limit applies to customers updating their repayment amount using:

  • Centrelink online account
  • Express Plus Centrelink mobile app

Service Officers can increase repayments but need to make sure the amount will not cause the customer financial hardship.

Go to Step 6.

5

Customer wishes to decrease their repayment amount + Read more ...

A customer can decrease their repayment online where they have previously increased the repayment amount.

For example, the current repayment amount is $51.45. The customer advises that they cannot afford these repayments. The minimum repayment amount is $38.50. The customer will be able to reduce their repayment amount to the minimum repayment amount of $38.50.

This limit applies to customers updating their repayment amount using:

  • Centrelink online account
  • Express Plus Centrelink mobile app

Service Officers can decrease or suspend repayments.

6

Review and submit + Read more ...

The Review and submit screen will display:

  • advance type
  • outstanding amount
  • the new repayment amount
  • repayment start date
  • final repayment date
  • that the customer has answered Yes to having enough money left over to cover their routine expenses
  • the declaration for the customer to read and accept

7

Receipt + Read more ...

Update successful

The Receipt page will display if the change was successful. This will show the:

receipt number

  • outcome of their request to change their repayment amount
  • advance type
  • outstanding amount
  • new repayment amount
  • repayment start date
  • final repayment date

Update unsuccessful

The system will advise the customer, 'Your change to your repayment could not be processed'.

The Outcomes heading will advise the customer they will not have enough money left over to cover their regular expenses.

Issues and troubleshooting - online advance options not available or not paying

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Customer is unable to use this service + Read more ...

The customer is not eligible to use this service.

The Advance payment service is available to customers who:

  • register online and
  • receive a social security entitlement, Mobility Allowance (MOB) or Family Tax Benefit (FTB) payment

Tell the customer why they are not eligible.

2

Customer is unable to see previous advance information + Read more ...

The customer has not applied for an advance within the past 2 years.

Check the Advance Summary (AVCS) screen. Tell the customer of the last advance application made. Explain that only the last 2 years of income support payment, MOB and FTB advance applications will display.

3

Existing activities + Read more ...

Customers will not be able to process their advance online if there is an activity on their record. This includes:

  • started (STA)
  • registered (REG) or
  • submitted (SUB)

A message will display advising they will need to contact Services Australia to process their advance.

4

Service did not pay advance + Read more ...

The customer is not eligible for an advance. Check the Advance Request (AVR) and Document List (DL) screens and advise the customer. See:

  • Advance payments for non-pension payments
  • Advance payments for pension customers or
  • Advance payments of Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
  • Lump sum advance payments of Mobility Allowance (MOB)

5

The service advises the customer to apply in person or by phone + Read more ...

To meet the 'duty of care' advance payments provision, the system will consider the customer to be 'at risk'. These customers will need to have a face-to-face or telephone contact before receiving an advance.

This ensures that these customers are accessing advance payments:

  • after a thorough assessment of their individual circumstances and
  • are aware of the impact on their future payments

This group is at a higher risk of encountering problems with budgeting. Explain to the customer that the online option is not available to them. The following customer groups must discuss their application for an advance with a Service Officer. This may be face to face or by phone, depending on their situation:

  • Customers under 18 receiving the independent rate of ABSTUDY or Youth Allowance (YA) must apply in person at a service centre. Exceptions apply for rural/remote customers
  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customers under the age of 18 applying for an FTB advance payment
  • Customers receiving an income support payment with a weekly payment frequency. Exceptions apply for rural/remote customers
  • Customers who have a nominee cannot use self service channels:
    • If the arrangement is voluntary, contact the nominee to make sure:
      • the information the customer advises is accurate and
      • the customer has the capacity to repay the advance without placing themselves in financial hardship
    • If the nominee arrangement is involuntary (e.g. under Guardianship Act or court order), the nominee (correspondence and/or payment) must approve the customer applying for an advance

Customers cannot use self service channels to apply for a:

  • Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA) advance,
  • Farm Household Allowance advance or
  • Special Employment Advance (SEA)

Rurally isolated customers may apply via the telephone for an advance of YA, ABSTUDY or weekly payments. To lodge their application via telephone, they can access the Contact us link on the Services Australia website.

For more information, see Advance payments for non-pension payments.

6

The service advises the customer is not eligible for standard advance + Read more ...

General reasons

The customer may not be eligible if they:

  • do not receive an eligible income support payment
  • are not in Australia when they lodge the application
  • have an outstanding Commonwealth debt
  • will suffer financial hardship due to the reduction amount required to recover the advance
  • do not have the minimum advance amount available
  • have not been in receipt of an income support payment for 3 months. This includes short periods off payment immediately before they lodge the application

Reasons specific to a non-pension advance

  • Received an income support payment advance in the previous 12 months
  • Currently repaying an existing advance payment (not including other types of advance payments for example FTB)

Reasons specific to a pension advance

  • Received an advance payment more than 12 months ago and has not repaid the full amount

Action

  • Check the customer is getting an eligible income support payment and the rate they are receiving
  • Check the Advance Payment Overview in Customer First. Select Workspace > Payments & Services > Advance Payments for information about entitlement to future advances

For more payment specific information, see:

  • Advance payments for non-pension payments
  • Advance payments for pension customers

7

The service advises the customer is not eligible for FTB advance + Read more ...

The customer may not be eligible if they:

  • do not receive FTB Part A for an FTB child
  • do not qualify for the base rate of FTB A for a child under the age of 18
  • have an outstanding Commonwealth debt
  • have not repaid any FTB advance payments granted more than 12 months ago. This does not include a balance of less than $0.05
  • will suffer financial hardship due to the reduction amount required to recover the advance
  • have reduced or suspended recovery in place for a current advance
  • do not have the minimum advance amount available
  • are requesting an advance amount that would exceed the FTB advance payment ceiling
  • have applied for a regular FTB advance when an outstanding regular FTB advance payment is in place:
    • Customers can only have one regular advance payment type in place at any time
    • A rejection message will display to advise this

Approved Care Organisations (ACO) are not eligible for an FTB advance payment.

Action

  • Check if the customer is getting FTB Part A and the rate they are receiving
  • Check the Advance Payment Overview in Customer First. Select Workspace > Payments & Services > Advance Payments for information about entitlement to future advances

For further information, see Advance payments of Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

8

The service offers the customer information about financial management services + Read more ...

All channels will display messages offering financial management support services to FTB customers if:

  • the customer is not eligible for an FTB advance payment on the grounds of hardship
  • the minimum advance amount is not available to the customer
  • the customer has a reduced or suspended recovery arrangement in place
  • the customer is still repaying an FTB advance granted more than one year ago, or
  • the customer has made four or more FTB advance payments claims within the previous 6 months

9

The service advises the customer has already been paid a regular FTB advance + Read more ...

Possible cause

Customer has not fully repaid a regular advance that is already in place.

10

The service advises the customer is not eligible for advance of Mobility Allowance (MOB) + Read more ...

Possible cause

The customer may not be eligible if they:

  • do not receive MOB
  • are applying less than 11 months after their last advance
  • are likely to leave Australia for 6 weeks or more within the next 6 months
  • have a future cancellation of MOB within the next 6 months
  • are receiving a funded package of support from National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS)
  • are not doing an activity of:
    • employed and earning money
    • self-employed
    • vocational training or study (including Life Skills training)
    • voluntary work
    • job search activities
    • Green Army Programme
    • Disability Employment Service activities

Action

  • Check the customer's payment status
  • Check the Advance Payment Overview in Customer First. Select Workspace > Payments & Services > Advance Payments for information about entitlement to future advances
  • Check customer's employment/training details

For more information, see Lump sum advance payments of Mobility Allowance (MOB).

11

Other issues using advance payment services + Read more ...

For help with troubleshooting other issues customers experience using the advance payment online service, see Troubleshooting Centrelink self service.

Advance payment option using phone self service

Table 6

Step

Action

1

Phone self service + Read more ...

To use this service, customers must register with an active phone account.

Income Managed customers can apply for an advance payment. The advance payment will be 100% income managed.

The Advances phone self service application is available to customers receiving:

  • income support payment, go to Step 2
  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB), go to Step 3

2

Income support payment + Read more ...

Customers who receive an income support payment can:

  • Hear details about an income support payment advance received within the last 12 months. This includes:
    • the amount of the advance (including each instalment if they received it in 2 instalments)
    • when it was paid
    • the repayment amounts, and
    • any outstanding balance
  • Hear about future advances. If the future date:
    • can be determined, the customer will hear when they will be next able to receive an advance
    • cannot be determined, the customer will hear the reason for this. Where there are multiple reasons, the service will advise of 1
  • Apply for an advance. Customers:
    • can choose to receive their advance in 1 or 2 instalments
    • must satisfy the Hardship Test

Some customers are unable to apply for an advance via self service. These include customers:

  • with a nominee
  • on weekly payments, or
  • on Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY under 18 years of age

Independent YA and ABSTUDY customers under 18 years of age must apply in person at a service centre. Exceptions apply for rural/remote customers.

Procedure ends here.

3

Family Tax Benefit (FTB) + Read more ...

Customers who receive FTB can:

  • Hear details about an FTB advance they received within the last 2 years. This includes:
    • the amount of the advance
    • when it was paid
    • the repayment amounts and
    • any outstanding balance
  • Hear about future advances. If the future date:
    • can be determined, the customer will hear when they will be next able to receive an advance
    • cannot be determined, the customer will hear the reason for this. If there are multiple reasons, the service will advise one
  • Hear how to access financial management support services
  • Apply for an advance, including:
    • the choice of a regular advance (that is, automatically assessed every 6 months) or
    • a one- off advance

Customers who choose to receive a regular advance

Customers can provide a date in the future to start receiving their regular advance. This can be up to 182 days in the future. The system will complete an eligibility check on the date of:

  • application (the date the customer requests the advance)
  • assessment

Customers must remain eligible to:

  • receive their first regular advance payment (up until the date of assessment)
  • continue receiving their regular advance the customer (each time the regular advance is assessed)

The amount of a regular advance is always the minimum FTB advance amount.
