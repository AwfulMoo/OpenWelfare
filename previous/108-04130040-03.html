Business activity or address update + Read more ...

For customers and/or their partners with protected information, apply the Restricted Access Protocol. For more information, see Restricted Access and eligibility.

Real Estate/Business (REB) activity + Read more ...

Activities with the keywords 'REBCLN' and a secondary keyword of 'FACSIA' are to be actioned by Service Officers in Real Estate Verification Processing Services only.

Activities with the keywords 'REBCLN' and a secondary keyword of 'NOTFACS' are to be actioned by a Service Officer in service centres.

An exception to this is for customers with an office code identified as a Centrelink International Services (CIS) region code. These will be action by CIS.

As part of data cleansing, a compulsory REB activity to follow up an invalid address for a real estate asset located in Australia will be created for any of the following:

  • an address for a real estate asset is created or updated
  • the address is invalid
  • the real estate asset is a current real estate asset
  • the real estate asset is not linked to a business
  • the customer and/or their partner has a current entitlement to a Department of Social Services (DSS) asset-tested payment other than Special Benefit, and
  • the customer is not being paid under Asset Test Hardship provisions, and
  • are not in a bereavement period applicable to real estate data cleansing

Initial investigation + Read more ...

Contact with the customer must not be made before reviewing the customer's and/or partner's record to determine if the real estate data can be updated from the information already available.

Real estate information currently on the customer's and their partner's record can be found on the following screens:

  • Real Estate/Business Summary (REBS) screen
  • Real Estate/Business Identifying (REBI) screen
  • Real Estate/Business Details (RE) screen
  • Real Estate Valuation Information (REVI) screen
  • also check any DOCs for information on real estate

Examples of possible investigations:

  • % ownership is greater than '0' but the value of the property is '0'. Check the system to confirm if the asset has been sold and correctly recorded
  • Exempt real estate asset due to vacation of home or Retirement Assistance for Farmers scheme
  • Overseas property recorded on the real estate details screens with Australia as the country. These should be on the Foreign Income and Assets (FID) screen
  • Time share assets should be recorded on the Other Assets (OAS) screen not on any real estate screen

  • No, go to Step 5

Contact customer + Read more ...

Prior to contact check the customer's record to ensure they are not in a bereavement period applicable to real estate data cleansing since the activity was first generated.

Contact the customer or their nominee, if applicable, via phone or mail (Q999) advising them Centrelink is currently checking and updating real estate assets records and ascertain the correct details for the customer's and/or their partner's real estate. Note: as most of these customer's will be Age Pension age, the Service Officer should exercise maximum sensitivity.

Was the information available immediately via phone contact or is further investigation required or a Q999 was sent to the customer?

  • If details can be updated immediately, go to Step 6
  • If further investigation or Q999 sent:
  • Set a review for 14 days for Q999's return or further investigation. For assistance, see How to code and action a manual review
  • Record details on a DOC
  • When information is supplied to allow an update on the customer's and/or their partner's record, go to Step 6

Correct or update the customer's and/or their partner's record as required + Read more ...

  • If the property has been sold, go to Step 7
  • If the customer volunteers additional information on improvements to real estate, e.g. vacate land now has house on it or value has increased, see Step 1 of the Improvements to real estate table
  • Customer has undeclared real estate, see Step 2 of the Improvements to real estate table
  • Property is incorrectly recorded or there is insufficient information on real estate screens e.g. ownership %, address incorrect or insufficient, time share property, see Step 3 of the Improvements to real estate table

7

Property has been sold + Read more ...

If the customer and/or their partner advise they have sold their real estate asset, the disposal or reinvestment of the proceeds can result in changes to the customer's income and assets.

It is important to establish whether there has been any change to the customer's circumstances that may have an impact on their Centrelink entitlement. However, it is not appropriate to send a letter to ask the customer how they have disposed of the proceeds. This constitutes an invasion of privacy, because not all of the information would be relevant to Centrelink.

  • Send an Income and Assets Update (SA220) to the customer to determine their current entitlement. If the Activity Registration (AYR) screen is used to issue the SA220, this will set up a review for its return. For help, see Bereavement reviews (Table 3)
  • Record details on a DOC

When SA220 is returned, see Step 3 of the Improvements to real estate table.

Until SA220 is returned the procedure ends here.

Property is incorrectly recorded on real estate screens + Read more ...

Real estate may be incorrectly recorded in various ways e.g. ownership incorrect, address incorrect or insufficient, overseas real estate, time share. Real estate information will also need to be recorded if the customer's relationship status has changed since the Real Estate (RE) screen was last updated.

  • If % ownership is the only update required e.g. property has been sold, update as needed. For coding assistance, see Assessment and sale of real estate and timeshare asset. Go to Step 8

  • Overseas property has Australia as the country. Correct the Real Estate Detail (RE) screen with '0' % ownership. Update real estate details on Foreign Income and Assets (FID) screen. Note: ensure the same date of effect is used for both updates

For coding assistance, see Assessment and sale of real estate and timeshare asset. Go to Step 8.

  • Time share assets should be recorded on the Direct Investment (SVDI) screen not on any real estate screen. Correct the RE screen with '0' % ownership. Update time share on the SVDI screen. Note: ensure the same date of effect is used for both updates. For coding assistance, see Assessing real estate and timeshare as an asset. Go to Step 8
  • Address details are incorrect or insufficient on the Real Estate/Business Identifying (REBI) screen, go to Step 4

Updating address details + Read more ...

  • Existing real estate record - If real estate is currently on the customer's and/or partner's record, use the Real Estate Update script to update the record. Note: real estate information will need to be recorded if the customer's relationship status has changed since the Real Estate (RE) screen was last updated
  • New real estate record - If real estate is not already on the customer's and/or partner's record, code the new information using the Real Estate Update script

For all customers, if the customer purchased the property within the last six months and has provided proof of purchase, or the property is an exempt asset, valuation is not required.

See Maintaining the value of real estate assets on customer records for information about when a valuation is required.

Services Australia must be able to precisely define the location of a real estate asset in a manner which is understood by both customers and external organisations in order to assist with an assessment, for example, by verifying nature of interest (ownership) through various state land title offices.

The real estate must have a site location address with minimum requirements for coding to ensure it can be identified. The address can be:

  • Urban address - this refers to areas suitable for or occupied by private homes i.e. not used for primary production
  • Rural address - this refers to areas suitable for or used for primary production
  • Complex address - this allows identifying a location in a complex site for example, within a retirement village and/or part of a large unit complex

Is the customer's and/or partner's real estate an urban address, rural address or a complex address?

  • urban address, go to Step 5
  • rural address, go to Step 6
  • complex address, go to Step 7

5

Urban address + Read more ...

An urban address identifies, labels or locates real estate such as houses (including rural home sites on less than two hectares), flats, units schools, parks, businesses, reserves, caravan parks, addresses accessed by water, public amenities in urban areas, shopping centres.

Urban areas are traditionally subdivided into lots. Sites within urban areas are usually identified by a site address which has address numbers allocated according to the typical subdivision lot size for that area. Numbers are allocated for anticipated future lots. Lots are combined for large areas hence ranged addresses numbers. A suffix (e.g. A, B) on the address number usually indicates insufficient numbers were available for newly subdivided land, where expected number has been allocated.

While most councils in Australia have adopted national address standards, there is no requirement for councils to renumber existing urban properties and some legacy addresses will present a problem. In addition, for new subdivisions or in other instances where road numbers have not yet been allocated, allowances must be made to record a lot number instead of an address number. For real estate assets, this lot number may be both a lot and plan number/s.

An urban address for a site located in Australia must be:

  • Unit Number (optional)
  • Level Number (optional)
  • Number or Number Range or Lot Number
  • Building Name (optional)
  • Private Street Name (optional)
  • Utility Name (optional)
  • Road Name
  • Suburb/Locality
  • State/Territory
  • Postcode

Example:

Address Line 1: 234 High St or 2/234 High St

Line 2: leave blank

Town/Suburb/Line 3: Balwyn

Postcode: 3103

State: VIC

Country: Australia

The urban address for a real estate asset located in Australia may have a unit number if:

  • the real estate asset is a town house (THS), or
  • the real estate asset is a self-contained unit attached to a home (SCF)

The urban address for a real estate asset located in Australia must not have a Level Number, Unit Number or Building Name if the real estate asset is a vacant block (VAC).

For both existing and new real estate, update the information using the Real Estate Update script.

Go to Step 8

6

Rural address + Read more ...

A rural address will most likely be used for real estate which is used for, or intended to be used for primary production purposes. A rural address may also be used for bush blocks.

Rural areas currently use a mixture of property names, lot numbers and delivery points (e.g. Roadside Mail Bag (RMB), Rural Delivery (RD) numbers) as addresses. There is often either a need for local knowledge to locate the property or else the address is for a mail delivery point which may not be on or near the property. Note: postal delivery services must never be recorded in the address field for a real estate asset e.g. RMB or RD.

In recent years, local, state and federal government authorities have been cooperating to address all rural properties with a unique number determined by the distance of that property's (main) egress from the logical starting point (datum point) of the road, with odd numbers to the left, even to right. Not all states have completed this unique numbering. Customers who own rural properties which have been allocated an address under the Rural Addressing project would have been notified of their new address by their local council or shire authority. The 'new' address would also be included in the rates notices.

This address should be used in preference to all legacy addresses. However, as rural addressing is not yet complete in all states, some allowance has to be made for legacy addresses by allowing for recording of lot numbers for instances where numbers have not yet been allocated. The address field must also allow for lot and plan numbers.

The rural address for a site located in Australia must be:

  • Property Name (optional)
  • Number or Lot Number, do not use RMBs
  • Road Name
  • Locality
  • State/Territory
  • Postcode

Example:

Address Line 1: Barwick Ridge Farm

Line 2: Lot 29 Richardson Rd

Town/Suburb/Line 3: Barwick

Postcode: 3199

State: VIC

Country: Australia

For both existing and new real estate, update the information using the Real Estate Update script.

Go to Step 8

7

Complex address + Read more ...

The most common complex sites are industrial parks, caravan parks, shopping centres, universities, major hospitals and larger retirement villages.

A complex address is used to identify a location in a complex site. A complex site has its own site address. Premises (land and buildings) on these sites have their own internal address to allow one to find their specific location on that site.

A complex address for a site located in Australia must be:

  • Address-site Name (optional)
  • Number or Range of numbers
  • Road Name
  • Suburb/Locality
  • State/Territory
  • Postcode

Example:

Address Line 1: Unit 23, Blue Hills Retirement Village

Line 2: 234 High St

Town/Suburb/Line 3: Balwyn

Postcode: 3103

State: VIC

Country: Australia

For both existing and new real estate, update the information using the Real Estate Update script.

  • the script will record details on a DOC
