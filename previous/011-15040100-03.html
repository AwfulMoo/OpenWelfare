Action a notice of judgment or settlement (NOJS)

Table 1: this table follows on from an enquiry case.

Step

Action

1

Evaluate - select document type + Read more ...

  • Select Notice of Judgment or Settlement as the document type from the Please choose the document type dropdown menu
  • Select the relevant toggle icons
  • Select continue

Note: if the claim requires registration or updating the claim will flow to that process before the Notice of Judgment or Settlement can be processed. See Search for or update an existing claim or register a new claim.

2

Assign work item + Read more ...

  • A message displays stating Are you sure you want to process a Notice of Judgment or Settlement? Would you like to continue?
  • Select Yes

3

Capture NOJS details + Read more ...

  • Enter the Date document received
  • If applicable enter the Date the notifiable person advised the injured person of potential debt liability (S22)
  • Select the relevant option from the Was compensation fixed by dropdown list
  • Enter the Date of judgment or settlement
  • If applicable select Is there a date the NP is required to pay the compensation and enter the date into the field generated
  • Enter the Total amount of compensation
  • If applicable select the:
    • Was the compensation based on apportioned liability - and enter apportionment percentage into the field generated
    • Does the amount of compensation fixed redeem liability for periodic payments
    • Were past expenses fixed under judgement
    • Do past expenses awarded represent the Medicare recovery amount
    • Enter Amount of past medical expenses awarded and/or Amount of past care expenses awarded - one must be entered if either or both past expenses toggle icon is selected
    • Is any amount payable to Centrelink - enter the amount into the field generated
    • Has the notifiable person indicated Advance payment intent - enter the amount into the field generated
    • Has the notifiable person advised the injured person in writing of AP
    • Does the notifiable person authorise payment of any excess amounts from failed advance payment to the injured person
  • Select Continue

For more details about:

  • Date the notifiable person advised the injured person of potential debt liability (S22), go to Step 4
  • Do past expenses awarded represent the Medicare recovery amount / Enter Amount of past medical expenses awarded and/or Amount of past care expenses awarded, go to Step 5
  • Is any amount payable to Centrelink, go to Step 6
  • Has the notifiable person indicated Advance payment intent, go to Step 7
  • Has the notifiable person advised the injured person in writing of AP, go to Step 8

Note: based on toggle icons selected additional fields may appear about the individual toggle icons.

4

Date the notifiable person advised the injured person of potential debt liability (S22) + Read more ...

  • This information is not compulsory
  • Notifiable person(s) are not required to provide supporting documents unless they choose to
  • If available, add the date to the field in MCRS
  • If the question is not answered, leave blank
  • Go to Step 9

5

Do past expenses awarded represent the Medicare recovery amount + Read more ...

If past expenses were fixed, select Were past expenses fixed under judgment.

Do the amounts represent the Medicare recovery amount?

  • Yes, select Do past expenses awarded represent the Medicare recovery amount
  • No,
    • do not select Do past expenses awarded represent the Medicare recovery amount
    • Staff must record the amount listed on the Notice of Judgment or Settlement (NOJS) in Amount of past medical expenses awarded and/or Amount of past care expenses awarded
    • Only complete the relevant field as specified in the NOJS or accompanying documentation
    • If only one field contains an amount fixed under judgment or settlement, or the Do past expenses awarded represent the Medicare recovery amount is not selected, MCRS will issue a notice to claimant (NTC)

Go to Step 9.

6

Amounts payable to Centrelink + Read more ...

This information is not compulsory.

If the question is answered on the Notice of Judgment or Settlement (NOJS) enter the amount in the relevant MCRS field. If the question is not completed leave the field blank.

Advise compensation payers, 'DO NOT Delay in submitting NOJS waiting on Centrelink information.'

Notifiable person’s intent to make advance payment (AP)

The agency will accept the notifiable person’s intent to make an AP if:

  • Do you intent to make an advance payment is answered Yes
  • An Amount of payment equal to 10% of the total amount of compensation is recorded on the NOJS under the intent question
  • An Amount of payment equal to 10% of the total amount of compensation has been received in SAP
  • Attached documents (such as the deed of release) have evidence of the intention to make an advance payment

Go to Step 9.

7

Notifiable person’s intent to make AP + Read more ...

The agency accepts the notifiable person’s intent to make an AP if:

  • Do you intent to make an advance payment is answered Yes
  • An Amount of payment equal to 10% of the total amount of compensation is recorded on the NOJS under the intent question
  • An Amount of payment equal to 10% of the total amount of compensation has been received in SAP
  • Attached documents (such as the deed of release) have evidence of the intention to make an advance payment

Go to Step 9.

8

Notifiable person advised injured person of AP intent + Read more ...

This question on the NOJS must be completed, or the attached documentation must show the injured person was advised of the intention to make an AP before settlement.

If this question is not answered, or is answered No, the AP fails. Make sure the field in MCRS is selected, or not selected in accordance with the form/documentation.

Go to Step 9.

9

Assess NOJS details - S23A statement + Read more ...

MCRS displays the most recent Notice of past benefits.

If a Medicare Compensation Recovery Section 23A statement form (MO023) is received:

  • select Has a S23A Statement been provided
  • if the MO023 is complete, select Is S23A complete
  • if accepting the MO023, select Do you accept the S23A

Note: if the MO023 is received but is incomplete or not accepted and there is no valid Notice of past benefits, a Notice to claimant is issued if a current one has not already been sent.

See Process correspondence – Medicare Compensation Recovery.

10

Assess NOJS details - Payment details + Read more ...

Merlin payment details display will present if funds have been received and allocated to the WIN in SAP.

Check for payments in SAP. If the money found in SAP does not match the amount of money displayed in the Merlin File, see Table 2.

If money has been received that relate to the claim, even if not yet allocated to the WIN:

  • Select Has any amount been received for this claim in SAP
  • If payment meets the AP criteria select Do payment, document and claim details meet AP criteria
    • Enter Advance payment amount
    • Enter Advance payment SAP document # (receipt #)
    • Enter the Date advance payment received
    • Enter the Compensation payer SAP unique account number
  • If payment is not an intended AP or does not meet the AP criteria do not select Do payment, document and claim details meet AP criteria
    • Enter Ad-hoc payment amount
    • Enter Ad-hoc payment SAP document # (receipt #)
    • Enter the Ad-hoc payment date
    • Enter the Compensation payer SAP unique account number
  • Select Continue

Note: depending on the toggle icon selected fields will display as ad-hoc or advance payment.

If no money has been received:

  • Enter the Compensation payer SAP unique account number
  • Select Continue

11

Review NOJS details + Read more ...

  • Review the data entered
  • Select Edit to change any details if required
  • Select Reprocess SBC if available and required - SBC Capture displays. See Statement by claimant and care costs – Medicare Compensation Recovery.
    Note: use this function if an amendment pre or post claim finalisation requires processing
  • Select Submit

The next workflow will change depending on the fields entered in previous steps. A message displays advising what action is required, for a:

  • Issue a Notice to claimant, see Notice of past benefits request and notice to claimant. Procedure ends here
  • Issue a Notice of charge (where a valid Notice of past benefits exists, claim settled by judgment and Medicare recovery set by past expenses awarded, or a S23A is accepted), go to Step 12
  • Issue Z2810 notice where an advance payment has failed, go to Step 13
  • Issue Z2809 notice where a Notice of judgement or settlement has failed, go to Step 14
  • Issue a Z2812 notice if the claim is a 33K, go to Step 16
  • Issue a Z2812 notice if the claim is a 24(4), go to Step 17

12

Valid Notice of past benefits exists, claim settled by judgment and Medicare recovery set by past expenses awarded, or a S23A is accepted + Read more ...

If a valid Notice of past benefits (NOPB) exists:

  • Message displays
  • Select Yes to proceed to Notice of charge (NOC) summary, see Notice of past benefits and notice of charge – Medicare Compensation Recovery

If claim has settled via judgment and Medicare recovery set by past expenses awarded:

  • Message displays Case settled by judgment and fixed costs awarded. Close claim on fixed costs NOC
  • Select Yes to proceed to NOC summary, see Notice of past benefits and notice of charge – Medicare Compensation Recovery
    • If the claim is an AP and the Merlin file does not match the advance payment amount the claim needs action by Compensation Finance. Go to Step 1 in Table 2

If a S23A is accepted without :

  • Message displays NOPB not issued and S23A accepted, thus issue the NOC
  • Select Yes to proceed to NOC summary, see Notice of past benefits and notice of charge – Medicare Compensation Recovery
    • If the claim is an AP and the Merlin file does not match the advance payment amount the claim needs action by Compensation Finance. Go to Step 1 in Table 2

If a Medicare Compensation Recovery Section 23A statement form (MO023) is accepted with an expired NOPB:

  • Message displays Expired NOPB extended by S23A. NOPB becomes NOC
  • Select Yes to go to the NOC summary, see Notice of past benefits and notice of charge – Medicare Compensation Recovery
  • If the claim is an AP and the Merlin file does not match the advance payment amount, the claim needs action by Compensation finance. Go to Step 1 in Table 2

13

Failure of advance payment + Read more ...

  • Message displays [Issue 2810] This claim is a failed AP. (additional messages may display depending on the circumstances of the claim)
  • Select Yes

Claims with:

  • a valid NOPB go to the NOC summary, see Notice of past benefits and notice of charge – Medicare Compensation Recovery
  • no valid NOPB go to the see Notice of past benefits request and notice to claimant. Procedure ends here

Note: Z2810 notice is sent to the notifiable person to notify them of the failed AP with a copy sent with the Z2803 letter to the claimant.

14

Non-compliance of Section 23 (NOJS) + Read more ...

  • Message displays [Issue Z2809] Notifiable person has failed to comply. S29(3) applies. (additional messages may display depending on the circumstances of the claim)
  • Select Yes

Claims with:

  • a valid NOPB go to the NOC summary, see Notice of past benefits and notice of charge – Medicare Compensation Recovery
  • no valid NOPB go to the Notice to claimant work step, see Notice of past benefits request and notice to claimant. Procedure ends here

Note: Z2809 notice is sent to the notifiable person to notify them that S29(3) applies with a copy sent with the Z2803 letter to the claimant.

15

Statement by claimant (SBC) work item to process + Read more ...

If MCRS determines there is an open SBC work item to process, a pop-up message displays after Submit on the Review screen. This will direct the work flow to the SBC Capture screen.

See Statement by claimant and care costs – Medicare Compensation Recovery.

16

Claim is S33K + Read more ...

  • Message displays S33K applies, thus issue NOC
  • Select Yes
  • If the Merlin file matches the total amount of payment, the claim automatically reconciles with finance
  • If the Merlin file does not match the advance payment amount the claim needs action by Compensation finance. See Step 1 in Table 2
    Note: Z2812 notice is sent to the claimant
  • Go to Step 12

Note: if funds are to be refunded via EFT, complete the EFT to Finance (valid advance payments) SharePoint form. The Resources page has a link to the form.

17

Claim is S24(4) + Read more ...

  • Message displays S24(4) applies, thus issue NOC. See Notice of past benefits and notice of charge – Medicare Compensation Recovery
  • Select Yes

Note: Z2812 notice is sent to the claimant.

Merlin file does not match the advance payment + Read more ...

When the Merlin file does not match the advance payment details (+/- $10) the claim is marked as complex and action by Compensation Finance is needed. See Payments, refunds and debts – Medicare Compensation Recovery.

Request for override + Read more ...

  • Select Send

The Resources page has a link to the SharePoint form.
