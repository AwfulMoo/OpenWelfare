Customer contact via the Outbound Dialler or Request for information letter

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer contact + Read more ...

EIIP policy is that there is no need for documented verification (payslips) to complete these interventions.

A customer may be:

  • called using the Outbound Dialler, or
  • sent a Request for information (IRSRFI) letter asking them to call

For all EIIPs, the Review Method in the Integrated Review System (IRS) will be either PHO or FIL.

Has contact been made?

  • Yes,
    • For the initial call, go to Step 2
    • If the customer is calling back to provide wage details, see Assessing and finalising EIIPs. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

2

Introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose + Read more ...

Follow the phone contact requirements.

  • All inbound callers will hear this IVR message before transferring to a Compliance Officer (CO):
    'Welcome to the Services Australia Customer Compliance Line. This call may be recorded or listened to for quality and coaching purposes'.
  • If making an outbound call, the CO must introduce themselves, tell the customer they are calling from Services Australia and say:
    'before we proceed with this call I need to inform you that this call may be recorded for quality assurance and training purposes'.

After the IVR, the CO just has to introduce themselves, and:

  • locate the customer's record and authenticate the customer
  • confirm the customer’s home and postal address and phone details are correct. Record any updates via the Change in Contact Details workflow
  • use interpreter service if needed. Check the Customer Summary screen via Workspace to locate the interpreter indicator
  • be aware of procedures for:
  • customers at risk, referrals to a Social Worker in Payment and Integrity
  • identifying vulnerable customers
  • bereavement
  • advise the customer of the purpose of the intervention
  • check if the customer requires a nominee
  • encourage the customer to access services online via myGov

3

Attempts to contact and authenticate the customer

If a third party answers an Outbound Dialler call + Read more ...

  • Do not initiate the intervention. Leave IRS as 'REG'
  • If the customer is not available, confirm they can be reached on the number dialled and say that the agency will call back later
  • Record details on a DOC. See the Resources page for the 'Outbound dialler (OBD) - contact made but unable to continue the intervention' DOC template
  • Select the appropriate call result on the Services Australia Workspace
  • The Outbound Dialler will make more attempts to contact the customer

If phone contact details are not correct: + Read more ...

  • End the number on the Telephone Details Summary (TDS) screen
  • Do not initiate the intervention. Leave IRS as 'REG'
  • Record details on a DOC. See the Resources page for the 'Outbound dialler (OBD) - contact made but unable to continue the intervention' DOC template
  • Select the appropriate call result on the Services Australia Workspace

Payment and Integrity Operations Strategy will run a script at the end of the Outbound Dialler campaign to issue a request for information letter.

If the customer cannot be authenticated + Read more ...

If the customer has been contacted by the Outbound Dialler and cannot be authenticated:

  • Do not initiate intervention. Leave IRS as 'REG'
  • Record details on a DOC. See the Resources page for the 'Outbound dialler (OBD) - contact made but unable to continue the intervention' DOC template
  • Select the appropriate call result on the Services Australia Workspace
  • Remind the customer of their notification obligations
  • Advise the customer they will receive a request for information letter
  • No further CO action needed

Payment and Integrity Operations Strategy will run a script at the end of the Outbound Dialler campaign to issue a request for information letter.

4

Does the customer want to proceed with the conversation? + Read more ...

Select the appropriate call result on Services Australia Workspace if it was an Outbound Dialler contact.

  • Yes, see Table 2
  • No, and they have been contacted by:
    • a Request for information letter (inbound call) and the customer does not have time to discuss, go to Step 5
    • the Outbound Dialler and the customer does not have time to discuss, or is unsure of the identity of the caller from Services Australia, go to Step 6

5

Customer receives IRSRFI letter (inbound call), has no time to discuss + Read more ...

As the customer has called, this situation should be limited.

Advise the customer

  • The intervention is to:
    • give them information about declaring income
    • give them information on their obligations
    • avoid overpayments, and
    • ensure they receive the correct entitlement
  • If they fail to call back within the required time, they may be issued another notification to update their details via their normal channels
  • The Expected Response Date they need to call back by. If the customer has less than 7 days, it may be appropriate to extend the date
  • The information they should have ready when they call back. This includes pay details for employers listed in the match data

DOC intervention with the details given to the customer, including the DUE date.

6

Customer contacted by the Outbound Dialler, has no time to discuss/unsure of identity of caller from Services Australia + Read more ...

Action required

  • Do not initiate the intervention. Leave IRS as 'REG'
  • Select the appropriate call result on the Services Australia Workspace if the contact was made by Outbound Dialler

If the customer fails to contact, and the intervention is still REG, Payment and Integrity Operations Strategy will issue an IRSRFI letter at the end of the Outbound Dialler campaign.

Procedure ends here.

7

Customer fails to contact + Read more ...

If the customer fails to contact after being contacted by the Outbound Dialler:

  • Payment and Integrity Operations Strategy will run a script at the end of the Outbound Dialler campaign to issue an IRSRFI letter
  • no CO action is needed at this point

If the customer fails to respond to an IRSRFI letter and the intervention is DUE:

  • Make one attempt to call the customer
  • If contact is successful, continue the intervention. See Table 2
  • If unsuccessful and the customer's record is no longer current:
    • Record information in the intervention DOC, See the Resources page for the 'Intervention finalised - non-current customer failed to respond to IRSRFI letter' DOC template
    • Finalise the review, see Assessing and finalising EIIPs
  • If unsuccessful and the customer's record is current:
    • issue an indirect message to the customer by letter and finalise the intervention. The letter will advise the customer to update their details via their normal channels for reporting
    • Document the actions taken, see the Resources page for the 'Intervention finalised - current (CUR) customer failed to respond to IRSRFI letter' DOC template

Education message

At this stage, the education message has not been delivered.

If the education message has been delivered and the intervention is DUE, see Step 5 in Table 3 of Assessing and finalising EIIPs.

Procedure ends here.

8

Customer contacts after intervention finalised + Read more ...

Discuss with the customer their employment circumstances. Update details if necessary if they have the information available now.

  • If updates are recorded:
    • advise the customer the outcome of the assessment
    • deliver the education message
  • If the customer does not have any details now:
    • advise them they can call their Benefit Payment line to correct/update their employment income once they have the information
    • deliver the education message

DOC the intervention with the discussion. See the Resources page for the 'Customer contact after intervention finalised' DOC template.

Procedure ends here.

Initiating EIIP customer and partner interventions

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Initiating the intervention + Read more ...

Before starting the EIIP conversation with the customer, the IRS intervention must be initiated STA (Started).This will make sure that all updates to the customer record can attribute to the intervention.

If the customer has been:

  • contacted by the Outbound Dialler, go to Step 2
  • sent a Request for information (IRSRFI) letter, go to Step 3

2

Initiation - customer contacted by Outbound Dialler + Read more ...

If the IRS review has not been initiated, it will show:

  • Review type: EII
  • Review method: PHO or FIL
  • Review status: REG
  • Review origin: TDF or DE or EUD

To initiate the intervention, record contact details in IRS.

Go to Step 4.

3

Initiation - inbound contact or successful outbound call attempt + Read more ...

As an IRSRFI letter has been issued to the customer, the intervention has already been initiated.

Record inbound contact details in IRS.

4

Does the customer have a partner? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, see Assessing and finalising EIIPs. Procedure ends here

5

Partner intervention + Read more ...

It is important to take into account the partner's circumstances.

Initiating the partner's intervention will make sure all updates to customer and partner records can attribute to the interventions.

This is done by updating IRCD for the partner record:

  • from the customer's record, key 'SR' (swap record) in Next: to go to the partner's record
  • go to the Review Summary (IRRS) screen and 'K' the partner's EIIP
  • the Review Contact Summary IRCS screen will present
    • if the intervention needs to be initiated (create and close the contact on the IRCD screen), or
    • if it is already STA, close the contact on the IRCD screen
  • from the partner's record, key 'SR' in Next: to go back to customer's record

Manual partner interventions

Do not load a manual partner intervention if the partner was not getting a Centrelink payment during the determined scope period of the intervention.

If the customer has a partner and no partner (PTR) intervention is loaded, and the customer received a Centrelink payment, create a manual partner review (MPR). See Adding a Manual Partner Intervention.

6

Partner contacts + Read more ...

If the partner's intervention:

  • does not contain match data, there is no need for the EIIP conversation
  • does contain match data, undertake the EIIP process with both customer and partner before completing the interventions
    • This may or may not be at the same time
    • The education message must be given to both before completing the IRS interventions
    • The CO who undertakes the second contact is responsible for completing both IRS interventions

If the partner calls in response to the IRSRFI letter, and has no match data:

  • Update the partner's IRCD screen. Key 'Y' in the Expected Response Date field.
  • Advise the partner that the customer still needs to contact for the early intervention discussion

See Assessing and finalising EIIPs.
