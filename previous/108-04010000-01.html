Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services 108-04010000

Expand all


This document outlines how to record and update income and asset details for new and existing income support payment customers, aged care means test assessments and certain concession cards.

Recording details

The income and assets of a customer (and their partner) must be recorded when:

  • a claim is made
  • an aged care means assessment has been requested
  • a current customer has a change to their circumstances. Note: where there is a small change to asset values, determine whether the customer is required to notify

Update the details once all information needed for the update is available. Income and assets updates for a claim for payment are to be completed within the new claim activity. Refer to the relevant Operational Blueprint for details of how to locate and process claims.

Changes to a customer’s income or assets are applied from the date of event (DOV). Generally, this is the date the change actually happened to the customer. Examples where the DOV is the date of the actual change include the date:

  • shares or other assets were purchased or sold
  • of a change to a customer’s bank account
  • a customer made a gift

In some instances, circumstances such as a customer depreciating their assets or the application of relevant legislation may result in a different DOV. See Process and Reference tabs for further information about coding changes and dates of event.

Do not delete the details unless it is clear that they have been recorded in error, for example, doubled-up.

Date of event, date of receipt

To ensure payment correctness with a change in income and assets:

  • all changes related to the event should be updated in the same activity,
  • using the date of receipt the agency first became aware of the event. See Date of receipt
  • using the same date of event (DOV)

For example: Managed Fund is closed on 04/09/2019 however proceeds are not deposited into bank account until 10/09/2019. Code the DOV as 10/9/2019 for both closure of the managed fund and update to the bank account balance.

Income being maintained for 52 weeks, for example, lump sum remuneration, must not be zeroed until the 52 weeks has passed, even if the source of income has ceased. If recorded as type 'OTH' with a frequency of ANN on the Other Income (OINS) screen, the system will set it to zero after 52 weeks.

The auto-review is only for Pension customers. For other customers:

  • Code a review for the applicable date in the future to review or delete the income, using a review reason of INC on the Review Registration (RVR) screen
  • For example, if the income was received on 1 May 2017 and to be assessed for 6 weeks, the review should be set for 12 June 2017 to zero off the income at the end of the 6 week period

For more information, see Verifying and recording other income on the Other Income (OIN) screen.

If the customer still owns the income and/or asset and they are advising of a change, see the Process page for coding details.

Some fields will not allow coding of decimals. In this case, round down to the nearest whole number. Complete any calculations using the total amounts before rounding down. For example, apportioning mortgage or loans only round down the result.

Information required

This depends on the payment or service the customer is claiming or getting, and the type of income or asset being updated.

Information requirements are in the relevant coding procedure for the income or asset type.

If more information is needed to complete the update, see Requesting information.

These processes will generate a letter and update the activity status and the customer's online account.

Exempt income and assets

An exempt asset is one that is disregarded under the assets test. Its value does not matter.

An exempt asset is disregarded but may have assessable income. For example, the proceeds from the sale of the principal home are an exempt asset but the proceeds when invested are deemed for income purposes.

There are 4 broad categories of exempt income. Although exempt income is disregarded as income:

  • the amount received may be an assessable asset, and
  • if the funds are invested, the deeming provisions apply

National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) funds received from the National Disability Insurance Agency (NDIA) which are deposited into an account specifically for managing the customer's NDIS plan are exempt from the income and asset test and deeming. This includes funds received periodically or as a lump sum and any interest accrued. NDIS funds do not need to be reported. If reported, they must not be taken into account in the income and asset assessment.

Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) are income if the child was born or entrusted to care on or after 1 October 2016. PPL and DAP are treated as exempt income if the child was born or entrusted to care before 1 October 2016.

While exempt income for income support payments, certain assets such as the principal home and the Refundable Accommodation Deposit (RAD) for example, are not exempt for aged care means assessments.

Unfavourable decisions

When making an unfavourable decision, speak to the customer:

  • explain the decision
  • give them a chance to provide more information or evidence
  • advise their review and appeal rights, and record an application for a formal review of the decision if required

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer before finalising the decision.
