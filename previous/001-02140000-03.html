Providers, eligibility and referrals for CDP

This table describes information on providers, eligibility and referrals for CDP.

Item

Description

1

CDP providers + Read more ...

There are 60 designated CDP regions. Each of these regions will generally only have 1 to 2 CDP service providers, resulting in a streamlined employment-servicing arrangement through a single point of access.

CDP providers are able to make commercial agreements with a range of external services to provide the assistance they need to deliver a successful employment, participation and community-development service in their region.

2

Job seeker registration under CDP + Read more ...

The job seeker's eligibility type for CDP employment services will be determined by the Department of Education, Skills and Employment. The following job seeker registration types will result:

  • RCV - Remote Community Volunteer
  • RFE - Remote Fully Eligible
  • NRRR - No Remote Referral Required

These eligibility types will be determined by the Department of Education, Skills and Employment based on the answers provided on the Job Seeker Registration screen.

Remote Fully Eligible (RFE)

  • Job seekers who have been determined as being Fully Eligible for CDP Services will be referred to a CDP provider
  • Job seekers who submit a new claim for a participation payment and reside in a CDP region will be referred to the CDP provider in their region by the Department of Education, Skills and Employment as part of the online claim process. Their CDP provider will contact them to arrange an initial appointment
  • A Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) is not mandatory for RFE job seekers except for:
    • job seekers flagged as Vulnerable Youth or Vulnerable Youth (student); or
    • job seekers who are 55 years or older and Centrelink managed

Remote Community Volunteer (RCV)

  • RCV job seekers cannot be referred to a CDP provider by Services Australia. Job seekers wishing to be registered as a RCV should contact their local CDP provider

If a Service Officer attempts to refer a RCV job seeker via the Online Diary, the error message 'E009JW - 0; Centrelink cannot refer RCV Job seeker' will appear. Do not refer to the ICT Application Helpdesk.

  • A JSCI cannot be completed by Services Australia for RCV job seekers
  • RCV job seekers are not subject to RapidConnect
  • A CDP appointment is not required for job seekers who are RCV

Where a CDP provider referral is required, refer to the Online Diary for the processes.

3

No provider referral required + Read more ...

Services Australia will be able to register job seekers, who need to be registered in order to access other assistance, but do not require referral to a CDP provider.

The following customers are the only groups that can be registered without a provider referral:

  • Customers who require a Department of Education, Skills and Employment record for purposes of accessing referral to a Job Capacity Assessment (e.g. Disability Support Pension (DSP) claimants, Special Disability Trust)
    Note: an active or existing Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) is not required for this referral
  • Full-time students applying for supplementary allowance determination and other cohorts as required
  • Full-time students receiving Youth Allowance (YA) or Austudy who are transferring to a job seeker payment, such as JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and contact prior to ceasing studies but within four weeks of their Student End Date (SED)
  • Customers who require a Job Plan, including:
    • Principal Carer Parents who are fully meeting their part-time mutual obligation requirements who do not choose to volunteer for CDP services
    • people with a Partial Capacity to Work (PCW) who are fully meeting their part-time mutual obligation requirements who do not choose to volunteer for CDP services and whose Department of Education, Skills and Employment record is no longer active from the time JCA was completed
    • PCW 0-14 hours per week who are considered volunteers in CDP services, who have not chosen to volunteer, but have a quarterly contact (QTI) as their compulsory mutual obligation requirement whose Department of Education, Skills and Employment record is no longer active from the time JCA was completed, and
    • customers in receipt of an income support payment that have no mutual obligation requirements who do not choose to volunteer with employment services and want to apply for Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work) will need to be registered as NSSRR/NRRR job seekers in order for a voluntary Job Plan to be negotiated prior to applying for ACCS (Transition to Work)
  • Customers participating in New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS)
