This page contains information about how income support payment customers can transfer to Age Pension.

Select the relevant tab:

  • Self-managed tab explains how to help customers complete the transfer online
  • Assisted tab explains how to identify if the customer should complete the transfer online and helping customers with enquiries about transferring to Age Pension

Self-managed

Helping customers complete the transfer to Age Pension online

This table contains information for Service Officers to help customers access their Centrelink online account to submit a transfer to Age Pension online.

Step

Action

1

Check access to online account + Read more ...

Check the customer is registered with an active Centrelink online account.

Customers who do not have an active account must confirm their identity at their local service centre to register for a Centrelink online account.

Advise them how they can create a myGov account and link it to their Centrelink online account. This will also enable them to receive Centrelink mail online through the myGov Inbox.

To start + Read more ...

Customers must:

  • sign into myGov to access their message in their inbox which contains a direct link to their online transfer task
  • sign into myGov account to access their linked Centrelink online account and select the Age Pension transfer task on the landing page, or

They can also access the Transfer to Age Pension service by selecting the Make a Claim menu option, select Get started under the Older Australians category and then Transfer to Age Pension.

Customers over Age Pension age can access the Transfer to Age Pension online by selecting Claims under Payment and Claims.

The transfer does not have to be completed in one session. Customers can save their responses and continue later by selecting the My online claims link.

Note: for income support recipients over Age Pension age, see Claiming Age Pension.

Claim navigation page + Read more ...

The Claim navigation page displays the sections of the claim required to be completed. The sections must be completed in order and the next section cannot be started until the previous section is complete.

Each section contains modules with relevant questions. On each question page, the customer can select Previous to go back to the previous question.

When each module is completed and the end of the section is reached, the customer is returned to the Claim navigation page to select the next section.

As each section is completed, the customer has the option to return to a previously completed section to change their answers by selecting the Edit option. They can then navigate directly to the module they wish to update by selecting the relevant tab.

Customers can use the dots on the side navigation of each page to navigate between pages in the online claim. If a question is answered incorrectly, a red cross will appear in the dots on the right side of the page. Customers can select the red cross and it will take them directly to the page containing the error.

Customers can read the Oliver Digital Assistant text for help with the questions on the current page. The Oliver text can be selected to launch interactive Oliver.

The transfer does not have to be completed in one session. Customers can leave the Claim navigation page at any time and the responses will be automatically saved to continue with their claim later.

Customers must answer all compulsory questions before they can submit their application to transfer.

Transfer process

Customers turning Age Pension age + Read more ...

Customers eligible to complete the online transfer to Age Pension can access this service 91 days before reaching Age Pension age. Customers should be encouraged to complete their transfer to Age Pension online.

91 days before reaching Age Pension age the following will occur:

  • If the customer is subscribed to online services, they will be sent a letter to their myGov inbox to complete the transfer. The letter will include a hyperlink to the agency's website and direct link to their online transfer task
  • If the customer is not subscribed to online letters or Electronic Messaging, they will be sent a letter and a Transfer to Age Pension (SA383) form by mail, with the option to complete the transfer online rather than return the form

If the Transfer to Age Pension has not been completed online/returned 28 days before reaching Age Pension age and the customer is:

  • subscribed to online services they will be sent a reminder letter to their myGov inbox
  • not subscribed to online letters but to Electronic Messaging, they will be sent a reminder SMS or email depending on their preferred communication method
  • not subscribed to either online letters or Electronic Messaging, they will be sent a reminder letter by mail

If the customer has not completed the online task/returned the SA383 form upon reaching Age Pension age, their (and their partner's) payment will be suspended, or cancelled.

If the customer reaches Age Pension age and their current payment suspends or cancels, the Transfer to Age Pension task will remain available on the Centrelink online account landing page for up to 13 weeks after the suspension or cancellation date.

Note:

  • payment (including the partner's) should only be restored once the customer has submitted the online transfer, via the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) workflow, or lodged a SA383 including required documentation
  • identified Indigenous customers will not receive a reminder nor have their payment suspended if they do not respond, except for single Disability Support Pension (DSP) customers who receive Rent Assistance as they must advise if they are sharing accommodation so it can be determined if they are better off remaining on DSP

Go to Step 5.

Customers over Age Pension age + Read more ...

If customer over Age Pension age contacts and one of the following applies:

  • a Manual Follow-up (MFU) has matured and is visible on the Activity List (AL) screen,
  • the customer has now or is within 13 weeks of 10 years qualifying Australian residence, or
  • the customer is a woman whose partner died (see Status (MS) screen for WID relationship status), and
    • both the woman and her partner were living permanently in Australia when her partner died, and
    • she has lived in Australia for the 104 weeks immediately prior to today's date (or will have the required residence within a few weeks) as per Australian Historical Residence (RSAHR) screen, then record details on a DOC on their record,
  • the customer was getting Carer Payment (XWP), and
    • their only or last payment level CA will stop due to temporary cessation of care, or
    • their CA and/or CP allowable portability period ends before 1 July
  • they were receiving Widow Allowance (WA) or Partner Allowance (PA) immediately before turning Age Pension age and have not yet transferred to Age Pension

Go to Step 6.

If the customer has not contacted and a Manual Follow-up (MFU) has matured or they'll lose eligibility for current payment, issue a request to contact the agency Q164 and record reasons / details on a DOC, procedure ends here.

If the customer is not yet residentially qualified (i.e. is within 13 weeks of qualification), record details on a DOC, procedure ends here.

Note: WA customers that will not be residentially qualified on 1 January 2022 will have a residence exemption applied to the Additional Residence Details (ARD) screen when staff process the transfer to Age Pension. These customers will be transferred to Age Pension on 01 January 2022.

The Claim navigation page displays the sections of the transfer required to be completed. The sections must be completed in order. The next section cannot be started until the previous section is complete.

This page displays:

  • Claim status
  • Claim number
  • Advises the customer the date by which to submit their transfer/claim

Customer Declaration

Where ACC is used to support the claim process, encourage the customer to submit the claim using their Centrelink online account or the Centrelink Express Plus mobile app so that the customer can monitor the progress of their claim through the Claim Tracker.

Note: Centrelink Express Plus mobile apps are not available to nominees acting on behalf of their principals. If a customer is willing or able to do so remind them that they need to submit the claim or it will expire after 13 weeks.

If the customer declines or is unable to submit their claim online:

  • Read the customer the verbal declaration script
  • Tick the box on the Review your claim page if they accept
  • This will confirm that the declaration script has been read, and that the customer accepts it

Once the verbal declaration is accepted and ACC submitted a DOC containing 'Staff assisted verbal declaration accepted - Yes' will display in:

  • Process Direct notes
  • Customer First Super Case
  • the Customer Claim Summary

If the customer is unable to provide a verbal declaration, have the customer complete a CDF:

  • Print a Customer Declaration Form (CDF) using the template in the Resources page
  • The claim must not be submitted without the CDF being completed and signed
  • If present, ask customer to sign the CDF
  • Where the customer cannot sign the CDF:
    • Send a Q004 to customer with a Customer Declaration Form (CDF) using the template on the Resources page
    • As this cannot be added as a required document on Next steps, record a detailed note on the customer's record stating the Q004 and CDF was issued. Include the expected due date
  • Where the customer has declined to accept the verbal declaration and a signed CDF has been provided, tick the Declaration box on the Review your claim page and annotate the DOC that the verbal declaration was declined, yet as a CDF has been signed and scanned to record the claim can be submitted by the Service Officer

If the customer requests a copy of their claim summary, this can be printed once the claim has been submitted in Process Direct via the print icon in the claim overview within ACC.

WA and PA customers who decline to transfer, record a new DOC with the heading 'Transfer to Age Pension declined' and details of the discussion.
