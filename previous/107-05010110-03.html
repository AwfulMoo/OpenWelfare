For Debt Management Officers (DMO) only.

This page contains information on how to assess a customer's financial circumstances for debt recovery purposes.

On this Page:

Assess the customer's financial details for debt recovery purposes

Financial information assessment

Assess the customer's financial details for debt recovery purposes

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Debt Recovery script + Read more ...

Use the Debt recovery script when actioning a debt.

Get details from the customer about their current financial situation.

Do not encourage or suggest solutions for raising funds that would place the customer in financial hardship. For example, telling a customer to take out a loan to pay a debt in full.

Ask the customer about all sources of income or assets they have access to. Negotiate arrangements for the debt based on total household income. Exclude partner income if there is a Family and Domestic Violence determination. In these cases, the debt can be temporarily written off.

Check customer's details on the system for information on their financial circumstances. See Outbound customer contact and gathering information when recovering debts.

Do not conduct a financial assessment if the customer and partner (where applicable) have no:

  • income
  • assets, or
  • access to other sources of income

Accept the customer's repayment offer or consider other action in these cases. DOC the reasons for the decision. Include why the customer does not have access to other sources of income or assets.

Is the customer required to provide financial information?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Follow the appropriate recovery procedure + Read more ...

  • Arranging cash repayments to recover debts
  • Third party withholdings for debt recovery
  • Procedures for arranging withholdings to recover debts
  • Temporary write off of debts
  • Unique recovery actions

Procedure ends here.

3

Collect and assess information + Read more ...

  • To collect the financial information, go to Step 4
  • To assess the financial information, go to Step 7

4

Financial details over the phone + Read more ...

If the customer does not have the details when you call, arrange:

  • to call back the next working day
  • an agreed time when they will have the details

Will the customer provide their financial details over the phone?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 5

5

The customer must continue payments + Read more ...

Tell the customer that a Financial Information (SS245) form can be sent to them to complete. The customer must continue payments until the form is returned. Resources has a link to the form.

Will the customer provide their financial details over the phone?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Decide an interim repayment arrangement or consider other recovery action + Read more ...

The interim repayment amount should be:

  • the minimum offer able to be accepted without obtaining financial information or
  • a repayment amount based on the customer's basic income and expense details:
    • use the financial Information Calculator determine the customer's excess income
    • the repayment amount will be two-thirds of their excess income

Code the interim payment arrangement and record the details on a DOC, see:

  • Arranging cash repayments to recover debts or
  • Procedures for arranging withholdings to recover debts

Send a manual advice:

  • DRC - Request financial circumstances (Q312) and
  • Financial Information (SS245) to request the financial information

Advise the customer a letter will be sent to confirm the interim repayment amount/frequency as well as the Q312

Resubmit a DOC or code a manual review to follow up the receipt and assessment of the SS245 form

When the SS245 is returned, go to Step 7

If other recovery action is to be undertaken, see Writing off Centrelink debts

7

Complete the online Financial Information Calculator + Read more ...

  • Enter information into the online calculator
  • Check the system for any Centrelink payments made to the customer, partner and/or child/ren
  • Enter any payments into the Family Income box in the calculator

A link to this calculator is on the Resources page.

Financial information assessment

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Verify financial information + Read more ...

  • If expenses appear overstated or unreasonable, ask the customer to verify (for example, receipts or accounts)
  • Request evidence to verify expenses or income only where there is good reason. See Outbound customer contact and gathering information when recovering debts
  • If needed, contact the third party to verify expenses. If this needs to be done in writing, code a manual review for the return of the information

Things to consider

  • Food, clothing expenses, etc., will vary according to the customer's family or living situation
  • If a customer is partnered, there may be circumstances where there is no sharing of finances
  • If assessing a partnered customer on their income alone, assess only their share of household expenses
  • If the customer has children aged 16-18 years, they may receive Youth Allowance (YA). Take this into account as reduced expenses for the children, not as extra household income

2

Excess income calculation + Read more ...

The online calculator calculates excess income. It is the income remaining after deducting expenses.

Is the excess income $15 per fortnight or more?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Excess income is $15 per fortnight or more + Read more ...

Tell the customer the repayment amount will be $X (where $X is two-thirds of their excess income)

Has the customer accepted the repayment amount?

  • Yes, complete the arrangement by:
    • Arranging cash repayments to recover debts
    • Procedures for arranging withholdings to recover debts or
    • Placing garnishees for Centrelink debts
    • Go to Step 8
  • No, tell the customer of possible consequences
    • Go to Step 8

4

Consider the customer's circumstances + Read more ...

Is the customer paying more to other creditors, which reduces the amount they can pay us?

  • Yes, tell the customer that the arrangement not to recover is for 3 months only. The customer will need to:
    • reduce other payments so they pay a fair amount to all creditors (including the agency)
    • renegotiate and repayment contracts with their creditors
    • for more information, see Reviewing cash instalment and withholding arrangements. Go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Agreed period of non-payment + Read more ...

Is there an agreement for a period of non-payment?

In certain cases an automatic review is generated for non-standard withholding rates.

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, code a manual review for 3 months' time to follow up increasing the repayment amount. Complete the arrangement by:
    • Arranging cash repayments to recover debts or
    • Placing garnishees for Centrelink debts
    • Go to Step 8

6

Period of non-payment has been agreed to + Read more ...

Tell the customer that you are deferring recovery on the grounds of financial hardship for the agreed period.

  • Action a temporary write off of the debt due to short term hardship for the agreed period. Send a Q246 letter for current customers or a Q313 letter for non-current customers. For more information, see Temporary write off of debts
  • Go to Step 8

7

Where the customer's excess income is less than $15 + Read more ...

  • Tell the customer that you are deferring recovery on the grounds of financial hardship
    • Send a Q246 letter for current customers, or a Q313 letter for non-current customers
    • Record all details on a DOC
    • For more information, see Temporary write off of debts
  • A customer may insist on making payments, despite evidence they may not be able to afford them. Accept the amount offered. Tell the customer to contact immediately if they need the arrangement changed. Complete the arrangement by:
    • Arranging cash repayments to recover debts
    • Procedures for arranging withholdings to recover debts or
    • Placing garnishees for Centrelink debts
  • For current customers, the Service Officer must DOC they have advised the customer:
    • the reduced arrangement is for 'X' months, and
    • the arrangement will revert to the standard rate after that time
  • For non-current customers the Service Officer must DOC they have advised the customer:
    • the reduced arrangement is for 'X' months
    • to make contact to enter into an arrangement at that time or the debt may be referred to an External Collection Agent (ECA)

Note: Tax returns will not be automatically garnished for Short Term Hardship (STH) and Prison (PRI) Temporary Write-Off customers.

  • Short Term Hardship (STH) and Prison (PRI) customers will not be sent a Tax Garnishee Warning letter
  • Do not use the write-off code 'TAX' when assessing financial hardship

8

Record all details on a DOC + Read more ...

  • Copy and paste the result information from the online Financial Information Calculator onto the DOC. Include income and asset information and calculation outcome
  • Include all information about the customer's circumstances and verification obtained. Include details of money owed to other creditors (such as, name of creditor and current balance owed)
  • Include all offer information (as some customers may insist on making higher payments, despite evidence they are unable to afford them)
  • Include details of the payment arrangement or garnishee if applicable
  • DOC your discussion with the customer advising them we will contact them to review their financial situation in 'x' months. This will ensure a review of the customer's situation occurs after the period ends
