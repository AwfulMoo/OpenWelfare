Verifying a job seeker's participation in voluntary work + Read more ...

A job seeker must prove they are doing voluntary work using the Verification of Voluntary Work (SU462) form. This form must only be issued to the job seeker once the organisation has been approved. It is completed by both the job seeker and voluntary work organisation where the job seeker is volunteering.

Has participation in voluntary work been proven?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, voluntary work cannot be included in the Job Plan as an approved activity. Procedure ends here

Note: where the organisation has not been approved (or is pending approval) and/or participation has not been verified, tell the customer they can volunteer with the organisation but the voluntary work will not contribute to their mutual obligation requirements.

4

Voluntary work as an approved activity + Read more ...

To be included in the Job Plan as an approved activity, voluntary work must:

  • occur without payment other than for out of pocket expenses
  • have a community focus
  • not be undertaken in the job seeker's own home
  • not primarily promote a particular religious or political view
  • not involve working for the job seeker's or a family member of the job seeker's own organisation
  • not involve violence towards people or damage to property
  • not undertake tasks which would normally be done by a paid employee, including a casual or part-time paid employee
  • not reduce hours usually worked by a paid employee or reduce overtime of an existing worker

When assessing voluntary work as an approved activity for job seekers aged 55 and over, the above points must be considered, as well as whether the organisation is or can be approved for voluntary work.

Note: if the job seeker must attend a 'base' or like place, and is participating in a work-like activity for the duration of their time on call, these hours may count towards their voluntary work hours.

If the job seeker is going about their daily life, without participating in any work-like activity while on call, these hours cannot be counted towards their voluntary work hours.

If the voluntary work placement is not approved, the job seeker's record should include a DOC to advise why the voluntary work activity has not been approved. Ensure the job seeker is verbally told of the decision.

If approving the voluntary work activity, the Service Officer will need to:

  • Select the Approved Voluntary Work organisation details workflow, and add the details of the voluntary work organisation (including their Organisation ID number/LAD number)
  • For Disability Employment Services (DES) provider managed job seekers, go to Step 5
  • Negotiate a Job Plan with the job seeker that reflects the activities approved, as fully meeting the job seeker's mutual obligation requirements. The job seeker will now be Centrelink Managed
  • Select the VWK (voluntary work) activity in the Job Plan so that activity start and end dates are included
    Note: the Job Plan will reflect the new dates on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen once accepted by the job seeker. For job seekers who are not DES participants, do not update AEX prior to negotiating Job Plan and it being accepted
  • Select other appropriate activities in the Job Plan where a combination of activities are being approved, e.g. VPE (voluntary and part-time work combined). If the job seeker is doing a combination of voluntary work and self-employment, use both codes 'SEL' and 'VWK'
  • When updating the Job Plan, remove any Employment Services Provider compulsory activities. If the updates will make the job seeker Centrelink managed, the Service Officer does not need to contact the Employment Services Provider to tell them of the changes to the Job Plan
  • If the AEX screen is not automatically updated after negotiating the Job Plan, manually code the details ensuring the corresponding activities and dates are recorded on the AEX screen
    Note: VWK and other approved activities cannot be coded on the AEX screen for more than 12 months at a time. This ensures the approved activity is reviewed and a new Job Plan is negotiated every 12 months if the voluntary work activity is continuing
  • Close any Digital Image Work items associated with the voluntary work activity. See Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images

Note: do not update the Job Plan if the customer is a Disability Employment Services (DES) participant.

Smart Centre Service Officers who are suitably skilled may negotiate a Job Plan with the job seeker over the phone. For assistance, see Step 1 in Table 2 of Negotiating Job Plans.

Tell the job seeker:

  • they fully meet their mutual obligation requirements as they are doing an approved activity
  • they must remain connected with their Employment Services Provider, and must be available and willing to do other suitable paid work offered to them, or accept offers of additional hours of suitable paid work. They must also attend all referrals to suitable job interviews
  • if the voluntary work activity has been recorded for 12 months continuously, their participation in approved activities and their job plan will be reviewed annually if still participating in the activity. For more information on annual reviews, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • they must tell Services Australia of any changes about their participation in an approved activity within the required timeframe
  • any changes may affect their mutual obligation requirements

Is the job seeker due to report?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, procedure ends here

  • Close any Digital Image Work items associated with the voluntary work activity. See Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images

Mutual obligation requirements not met

Table 2: this table outlines the process when eligible job seekers choose to use voluntary work to satisfy their mutual obligation requirements, but do not meet their requirements.

Step

Action

1

Reasonable excuse for not meeting requirements + Read more ...

Note: where the job seeker has a previously approved voluntary work activity coded on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen and the voluntary work organisation has contacted to advise the job seeker is not meeting their voluntary work duties, go to Step 5.

Reasonable excuses

The only excuses accepted as reasonable are:

  • customer ill or injured
  • sick children
  • a cancelled shift
  • short term reduction in hours due to public holiday
  • pupil-free days
  • failure of special disability related transport

A reasonable excuse is only acceptable where there is an expectation that working hours will return to normal in the next fortnight.

Does the job seeker have a reasonable excuse for not meeting their requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 4

2

Third consecutive reasonable excuse + Read more ...

Is this the third consecutive reasonable excuse?

  • Yes, the job seeker can no longer meet their mutual obligation requirements with voluntary work, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

First or second consecutive reasonable excuse + Read more ...

If this is the first or second consecutive reasonable excuse, the Service Officer should code the reason in the Earnings and Reporting workflow and process the job seeker's payment as usual. Payment will not be affected.

Revert their reporting requirements to fortnightly if they are a mature age 55+ job seeker and had previously been placed on variable reporting.

The Service Officer should advise the job seeker that a maximum of 3 consecutive reasonable excuses may be given. However, on the third fortnight the job seeker must choose a new activity and be referred to, or reconnected with an Employment Services Provider.

Procedure ends here.

4

Job seeker does not have a reasonable excuse + Read more ...

If the job seeker does not have a reasonable excuse, or has already provided 3 consecutive excuses for failing to meet their requirements, the job seeker must choose another activity type, as they can no longer meet their obligations with voluntary work.

  • Investigate other approved activities for the job seeker. If the job seeker is not doing another activity or combination of activities that can be approved by Centrelink as fully meeting requirements, end date the voluntary work activity coding on the AEX screen
  • If the customer is a mature age 55+ job seeker, revert their reporting requirements to fortnightly if they had previously been on variable reporting. This shows the provider the job seeker is not meeting requirements through an approved activity and ensures the job seeker will be engaged by the provider in a timely manner. The job seeker's record will display as provider managed when the Department of Education, Skills and Employment send an advice that the job seeker has started or restarted in Employment Provider Services
  • Provider managed job seekers:
    • must be reconnected immediately to their Employment Services Provider or another appropriate provider (if not already active with a provider) to update their Job Plan. Procedure ends here
  • For Centrelink managed job seekers:
    • if they are and will remain Centrelink managed, the Service Officer must renegotiate the Job Plan to remove voluntary work as a compulsory activity and add appropriate activities
    • process the outstanding report. Procedure ends here

5

Voluntary work organisation notifies that the job seeker failed to meet agreed voluntary work requirements + Read more ...

In cases where a job seeker is not meeting their requirements with voluntary work, the voluntary work organisation may contact Services Australia of a job seeker's failure to meet agreed voluntary work activity requirements via:

  • their local service centre
  • the Services Australia website
  • telephone 131 524
  • mail: Centrelink Reply Paid 7800 Canberra BC ACT 3610

See Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption.

Reviewing voluntary work as an approved activity

Table 3:

Step

Action

1

Annual review of voluntary work as an approved activity + Read more ...

Where the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen is updated to record the voluntary work type of VWK or VPE coded as an approved activity for 52 weeks, a Voluntary Work 12 Monthly Review (VWR) is automatically generated for 48 weeks in the future to alert Service Officers to review the job seeker's voluntary work.

Once the date of the review is reached, an appointment will be automatically booked and a letter sent to the job seeker advising details of the appointment and of the need to provide details of the voluntary work organisation at the interview.

If a job seeker fails to attend the interview, make 2 genuine attempts to contact them and if they cannot be contacted, their payment should be suspended with the reason 'Failed to Attend Interview' (FAI).

Following successful contact with Services Australia, reschedule the interview and when the job seeker attends their appointment, payment can be restored. Service Officers must record a DOC with reasons for non-attendance.

If an appointment cannot be booked within 4 weeks of the review date, the system creates a Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity prompting manual action for the VWR to prompt Service Officers to manually book an appointment.

At this review, the Service Officer should do the following:

  • Check if the job seeker is between 55 and 59 and has been getting their current income support payment for more than 12 months. These job seekers can now fully meet their mutual obligation requirements solely with voluntary work. There is no longer a minimum of 15 hours paid work for these job seekers
  • Check the job seeker intends to, and can continue with voluntary work to fully meet their mutual obligation requirements
  • Discuss the suitability of the job seeker's current activities
  • Re-verify the job seeker's participation in the voluntary activity with their current volunteer organisation or link the job seeker to a different organisation via the Approved Voluntary Work organisation details workflow if required
  • If the voluntary work placement or organisation is not approved. The job seeker's record should include a DOC advising the reason the voluntary work activity has not been approved. Ensure the job seeker is told verbally of the decision
  • If the voluntary work will no longer be an approved activity, Services Australia must end date the voluntary work activity on the AEX screen
  • Investigate other approved activities for the job seeker if required
  • If the job seeker will not be doing voluntary work, either alone or combined with part-time paid work (including self-employment) for at least 30 hrs per fortnight, advise of the requirement to undertake suitable job search activities. The job seeker must be immediately reconnected to their Employment Services Provider or referred to an appropriate provider if not already active with a provider and must attend all appointments
  • Renegotiate the Job Plan to continue voluntary work or combinations of other approved activities for up to another 12 months where the activities will continue to fully meet the job seekers requirements (such as, self-employment, part-time work, or a combination with voluntary work). If voluntary work or other approved activities are not approved, do not update the job seekers job plan. The job seeker will become provider managed and the job plan negotiated by the job seekers Employment Services Provider
  • Update the start and end date for approved activities on the AEX screen as required, (dates should align with Job Plan)
  • Close any Digital Image Work items associated with the voluntary work activity. See Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images
  • Update any other details for the job seeker (for example: changes to income or investments) as required
  • Record outcomes of the review on a DOC

Note: the start date on the AEX screen should be the date coding is occurring to ensure details transfer correctly to the Department of Education, Skills and Employment.
