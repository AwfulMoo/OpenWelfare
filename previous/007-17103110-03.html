Updating school status details for CCS

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer advising of child's school status + Read more ...

It is the customer's responsibility to advise when their child starts primary or secondary school. This will ensure they continue to be eligible for CCS and/or they receive their correct entitlement.

Has the customer's CCS been cancelled due to child education level not met?

  • Yes, a new claim is required, unless the customer has requested an exemption for the child before the CCS was cancelled. Changing or correcting the education status will not automatically reinstate the customer's CCS. Invite the customer to re-claim CCS. See Step 10 in Table 2
  • No, and the child is (or will be) in:
    • primary school, go to Step 2
    • secondary school, go to Step 3

2

Child is attending primary school + Read more ...

A child is considered to be attending primary school if they:

  • have turned 6 years of age
  • attend the year of school before Grade 1 (or would be if they were not absent or on holidays)
  • attend primary school (or would be if they were not absent or on holidays)
  • are home schooled as recognised in the State or Territory where the child resides

Is the child attending primary school?

  • Yes, the hourly rate cap for outside school hours care service will apply, even if the child attends a centre based child care. Go to Step 4
  • No, the hourly rate cap for centre based child care will apply, even if the child attends an outside school hours care service. Go to Step 4

3

Child is attending secondary school + Read more ...

A child is considered to be attending secondary school:

  • they attend Years 7 to 12 in all states and territories
  • for special schools, education is provided to students aged:
    • 12 to 21 in all states and territories except South Australia
    • 13 to 21 in South Australia

Go to Step 4.

4

Recording school status + Read more ...

Encourage customers to update their child's school status online.

To check the customer's online updates, in Process Direct:

  • go to the child record whose details are to be updated
  • go to the Child Education Details (CED) screen via the Super Key field

Has the customer updated the child's school status online correctly on the CED screen)?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Update status in Process Direct + Read more ...

Select the Add icon to record new education details, or the Edit icon to correct existing details:

  • If the child has not started school:
    • Start date: the child's date of birth
    • Education level: select Has not started studies from the drop down menu
      This can be coded from when the child turns 3 years old
    • No further details are required/enterable
  • For Primary School:
    • Start date: the date the child did or is expected to start primary school. Note: this is not the start of the calendar year. See the Resources page for a link to Term Dates for public schools for a guide to help customers with the correct start date.
      If the customer has advised they are delaying the child's entry into primary school, start a new line with the new primary start date. This lets the customer access additional pre-school hours (if applicable) when the child is repeating preschool
    • Education level: select Primary education from the dropdown menu
    • No further details are required/enterable
  • For Secondary School:
    • Start date: the date the child did or is expected to start secondary school. Note: this is not the start of the calendar year. See the Resources page for a link to Term Dates for public schools for a guide to help customers with the correct start date
    • Education level: select Secondary education from the dropdown menu
    • Disabled child: select Yes or No as appropriate (leave blank if not known)
    • Adult available to care: select Yes or No as appropriate (leave blank if not known)
    • Unable to be left alone: select Yes or No as appropriate (leave blank if not known). Note: Yes confirms the child cannot be left unsupervised

Has the customer indicated the child has a disability or is not able to be left at home unsupervised?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Finish activity + Read more ...

Once school status details have been updated:

  • Select Assess to save the update
  • Complete the Update Header Data with Receipt Date and Channel details
  • Select Save
  • Go back to the customer's CRN by selecting them from the Relationship menu
  • Select Assess to display the Assessment Results screen
  • Ensure Assessment Result details are correct, including any changes to the child's CCS eligibility and any potential arrears or negative adjustments under Planned Payments
  • If the outcome is:
    • as expected, select Finish > Finalise
    • not as expected, do not finalise the activity. Place the activity on Hold and escalate via TPS, SSP, or Roxy
  • Take note of the activity ID to record in Notes
  • Re-access the record and record a Note. Detail the update or changes made, and any potential arrears or negative adjustments as a result of the activity

Age and school status exemptions for CCS

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Customer asks about age and school status exemption, OR work item ZCOC_CCS_EXCEPTIONAL is allocated + Read more ...

To action a ZCOC_CCS_EXCEPTIONAL work item, go to Step 5.

If a customer contacts the agency in relation to an age and school status exemption:

  • go to the Transactions tab to check the record for an indexed CCS exceptional circumstances activity, and
  • check previous documents on the record

If CCS is:

  • current and:
    • an exemption activity is 'in process' on the Transaction tab, go to Step 2
    • no exemption activity is 'in process' on the Transaction tab, go to Step 3
  • cancelled EDL (child education level not being met), go to Step 10

2

Exemption assessment already indexed on the customer's record + Read more ...

If the customer contacts about an existing school status or age and school status exemption:

  • Check DOC List/Notes. If there is a note from Level 2 Policy Helpdesk advising further evidence is required tell the customer what is needed
  • Encourage them to upload the documents to their online services
  • Tell them they will be advised of the outcome in writing

Procedure ends here.

3

Check if the customer is not subject to an age and school status exemption + Read more ...

The customer can apply for an exemption if:

  • the child cannot be left unsupervised, and
  • there is no suitable adult over the age of 18 available to care for them

The child must also be:

  • 13 years or under attending secondary school
  • 13 years or under with a disability and attending secondary school
  • 14 - 15 years with a disability attending secondary school, or
  • 16 - 18 years with a disability attending secondary school in exceptional circumstances

Before actioning the referral, confirm with the customer if they may meet basic eligibility.

Is basic eligibility criteria met?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, tell the customer a child over the age of 14 years or attending secondary school is not eligible for CCS unless a school status or age and school status exemption exists

Document the discussion

  • Select Notes > and key the following in the relevant fields:
  • Category: select 'Claim' or 'Change of Circumstances' as appropriate
  • Summary, key 'School status or age and school status exemption not sought'
  • Content: copy, paste and complete the following text:
    • Customer's request for a school status or age and school status exemption has been discussed, customer is not seeking an exemption:
    • Start date: [key start date - usually first day the child commenced secondary study]
    • Child's name: [key name of the child the circumstances relate to]
    • Details: [key summary of the customer's circumstances and why an exemption was requested]
    • Reason: [key reason why customer is not seeking an exemption]

Procedure ends here.

4

Discuss evidence requirements with the customer and create CCS exceptional circumstance activity + Read more ...

Tell the customer the evidence requirements, based on the age of the child. Encourage them to upload the appropriate evidence online. See the Resources page for the requirements. Note: decisions on exemptions are not able to be made without evidence requirements.

When requesting documents, issue a Q888. See letters for Centrelink > Families > Child Care Subsidy and Additional Child Care Subsidy).

Index a new CCS exceptional circumstance. In Process Direct, select (top right hand corner):

  • in the Task Selection field, key 'child care' and select the Child Care Subsidy Circumstance radio button
  • under 'Related Tasks' tick the CCS Exceptional Circumstance check box
  • select Start to view the Create Tasks page:
    • Date of Receipt defaults to today's date
    • select Child Care Subsidy from the dropdown list
    • select Create

Go to Notes to create a referral note. Select and key the following in the relevant fields:

  • Category, select 'Claim' or 'Change of Circumstances' as appropriate
  • Summary, key 'School status or age and school status exemption request'
  • Content, copy, paste and complete the following using information provided by the customer:
    • Customer has requested a school status or age and school status exemption:
    • Child name:
    • Child age and birthdate:
    • Secondary school start date: [key the date the child did or is expected to start secondary school]
    • Education level: [key the year level the child is commencing or has commenced]
    • Disabled child: [Yes/No]
    • Exceptional Circumstance: [details are required for children 16, 17 and 18 years only]
    • Evidence requested: [key details or delete if evidence already provided]

Note: as part of a new CCS claim, the system will determine if an age and school status exemptions may be applicable as part of an online, or staff assisted claim. Where a claim is lodged and a school status exemption is requested for a child 13 years or younger attending secondary school who is unable to be left at home alone, the system will check if the claim is suitable for straight through processing and:

  • the document will be taken at face value, there is no need for staff to verify the document
  • the system applies the exemption until the child turns 14 based on details in the Child Education Details table
  • no exceptional circumstances coding is required
  • procedure ends here

If the claim is not suitable for straight through processing, the activity will be allocated to a skilled Service Officer for exemption management and/or data validation.

Procedure ends here.

5

Work Item falls due + Read more ...

Once the matured Work Item is allocated for processing, check if evidence has been provided by selecting to check within the activity.

Has the required evidence been lodged to determine the customer's suitability for an age and school exemption?

  • Yes:
    • select Status and to update the claim or Change of Circumstances status
    • from the dropdown list, select On hold
    • key a future end date of 2 business days
    • Save. Go to Step 6
  • No, and:
    • the customer has failed to respond to a previous request for evidence. Staff not trained in CCS processing can allocate the activity back to the general pool, procedure ends here. For Smart Centre Processing Staff, if there is insufficient information to determine an age and school status exemption, CCS cannot be approved for the child. Go to Step 8
    • a request for evidence has not been previously issued, ask the customer to provide more information. In Process Direct, select Request documents and complete the relevant details. Annotate details of the request in the school status or age and school status referral Note
    • update the claim or Change of Circumstance activity status. Select Status > from the dropdown list, select On hold. Insert an end date allowing extra time for mail delivery. Procedure ends here

Note: when requesting documents, issue a Q888. See letters for Centrelink > Families > Child Care Subsidy and Additional Child Care Subsidy).

6

Refer to Level 2 Policy Help Desk for assessment of child age and school status exemption + Read more ...

Before referring the case to the Level 2 Policy Help Desk for assessment, Service Officers must check:

  • a Commonwealth statutory declaration has been supplied (State statutory declarations or statements are not sufficient), and
  • the Commonwealth statutory declaration includes why it is unreasonable for the child to be left unsupervised

Feedback may be provided to Services Officers who advise that evidence has been provided but it does not include:

  • information about the child's secondary school commencement date
  • a Commonwealth statutory declaration form with details:
    • explaining why the child cannot be left unsupervised
    • confirming there is no suitable adult over the age of 18 (this includes all other support networks, not just the parent/s or carers) available to supervise the child

Complete the web form fields as follows:

  • CSAQuery: 'Families, Childcare and Parenting'
  • CSAEnquiry: 'Childcare Subsidy'
  • Service/Payment Type: 'Child Care Subsidy'
  • Enquiry Description: 'Customer is seeking a school status or age and school status exemption. Evidence has been provided and scanned onto the customer's record. Refer to Notes dated [dd/mm/yyyy] for full details.'

Annotate the existing Note: by ensuring the referral lists the Service Officer's Team Leader in the Additional Contact Name field.

7

Implementing the Level 2 Policy decision + Read more ...

Level 2 Policy Helpdesk will determine if a customer is eligible for the child age and school status exemption and refer this back to the staff member.

If a response from Level 2 Policy has been received, the Service Officer is to:

  • locate the claim or Change of Circumstances activity, and
  • assign it to themselves

All work must be finalised as soon as a resolution/response is received.

The Service Officer needs to make any required updates based on the Level 2 Policy Desk response.

If the Service Officer cannot action the required updates, it is the responsibility of the Team Leader listed as the additional contact to allocate to another appropriately skilled Service Officer to make the relevant update.

Has the Level 2 Helpdesk approved a child age and school status exemption?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Age and school status exemption not approved + Read more ...

A customer's exemption request cannot be approved in cases where:

  • insufficient or no evidence has been provided, or
  • the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk has not approved the request

To action these, select the activity from the Process Direct Inbox, and (top right corner) to display the icon ribbon.

Select Notes and highlight the Notes with the summary 'School status or age and school status exemption request'.

In the Annotation field, select , copy and paste the following relevant information:

'Evidence not provided:

Customer has not provided evidence within 14 days of making an school status or age and school status exemption request. Request for exemption cannot be assessed.

Exceptional circumstances activity has been cancelled.'

'Exemption not exemption approved by Helpdesk:

Child's circumstances have been assessed and an school status or age and school status has been rejected.

Reason: [key details as advised by Level 2 Helpdesk if not already included in the Note]

Exceptional circumstances activity has been cancelled.'

To finalise:

  • select Save. If the customer:
    • lodged a claim for CCS, process claim and allow CCS to reject for relevant child
    • has requested an exemption before cancellation, select Status > from the dropdown list, select Cancelled > Save
  • exit from the customer's record

Note: under no circumstances code a zero link on the EXC table.

Procedure ends here.

9

Age and school status exemption approved + Read more ...

If the age and school status exemption outcome has been approved by the Level 2 Policy Help Desk, the action will differ based on the age of the child.

In the customer's record, go to the Exceptional Circumstances table by keying EXC in the SuperKey field and select icon to create a new line. Complete the 'Create Exceptional Circumstance' box. For children:

  • 15 years and under:
    • Type: select Child Age or School status from the dropdown menu
    • Start date: the date the child age and school status exemption is to apply from. This must be a Monday. The later of the date the child started secondary school, or the date the exceptional status started)
    • End date: cannot be after the child's 16th birth date (a maximum of 2 years)
    • Review Date: up to 24 months. Only code if documentary evidence states condition is not permanent, or they are in the process of assessing the child for the condition
    • Child CRN: for the child the exemption relates to
    • Decision: select the relevant reason for the decision from the dropdown menu (usually Initial Claim Determination or Review initiated by individual)
    • Reason: select the relevant reason from the dropdown menu. If Other is selected, a free text field will display to insert details as to why the exemption was granted
  • 16 to 18 years:
    • Type: select Child Age or School status from the dropdown menu
    • Start date: the date the child age and school status exemption is to apply from. This must be a Monday. The later of the date the child started secondary school, or the date the exceptional status started. It cannot be before child's 16th birth date
    • End date: cannot be after the child's 19th birth date or the end of the school year, whichever comes first (a maximum of 3 years)
      If the child is 16 - 18 years of age attending secondary school, has a disability and turns 19 before the end of the school year, the exemption end date must be the CCS Sunday before their 19th birthday. CCS will cancel COA (Child over age) from this date
    • Review Date: up to 24 months (Only to be coded where documentary evidence states condition is not permanent, or they are in the process of assessing the child for the condition)
    • Child CRN: for the child the exemption relates to
    • Decision: select the relevant reason for the decision from the dropdown menu (usually Initial Claim Determination or Review initiated by individual)
    • Reason: select Other, a free text field will display to insert details as to why the exemption was granted
    • Text: Over 16 assessment - child with a disability

Select Save.

Once the age and school status exemption outcome has been recorded (via Exceptional Circumstances):

  • select Assess to save the update
  • complete the Update Header Data with Receipt Date and Channel details, Save
  • select Assess, the Assessment Results screen will be displayed
  • go to the Child Education Details (CED) screen via the Super Key field to check if child's education details need to be updated. If required, update details as outlined in Step 5 in Table 1
  • select Assess to view the Assessment Results screen
  • ensure Assessment Result details are correct, including child eligibility and any arrears or negative adjustments as displaying under Planned Payments. If the outcome is not as expected, do not finalise the activity. Place the activity on hold and escalate for assistance as per usual business processes
  • if the outcome is as expected, select Finish > Finalise. Take note of the activity ID
  • open the customer's record again, select Notes and highlight the Notes with the summary 'School status or age and school status exemption request'

In the Annotation field, select , copy and paste the following relevant information:

'School status or age and school status exemption approved:

Child's circumstances have been assessed and an school status or age and school status has been approved.

Exceptional Circumstances (EXC) and Child Education Details (CED) have been updated.

Evidence provided: [detail what evidence was provided (NDIS number, medical evidence), period of exemption etc.].

Policy referral number: [key policy referral number]'.

Document the record to note if the child still needs access to child care after the exemption expires, a new application is required.

Note: evidence that is still on file can be used for further applications.

Procedure ends here.

10

CCS is cancelled EDL (child education level not being met) + Read more ...

If the customer's CCS is cancelled EDL, and:

  • an exemption activity is 'in process' on the Transaction tab:
    • check all other CCS eligibility
    • use Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) to restore the CCS. See Claiming Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS)
    • Procedure ends here
  • no exemption activity is 'in process' on the Transaction tab the customer will need to reclaim CCS:
    • Invite the customer to lodge a claim and document the record
    • Procedure ends here
