7

Over recovered debt and other pending Family Assistance Office (FAO) debts + Read more ...

It is possible to hold an over recovered Work Item to allow the FAO Non-Lodger or Reconciliation process to finalise.

For example there may be a:

  • a potential Family Tax Benefit Non Lodger Debt (FTB NLD), or
  • potential Family Tax Benefit Reconciliation Debt (FTB FRC) is not yet finalised

To identify a potential FTB NLD:

  • on the OPDL screen, check that the:
    • debt benefit type of the over recovered debt is FTB
    • debt reason for the over recovered debt is FRR, FRC or FRA
  • 'S'elect the over recovered FTB debt
  • in the Nxt field, key OPAOD > [Enter]
  • on the OPAOD screen, check the financial year of the debt
  • in the Nxt field, key FRCS> [Enter] > read the displayed message > [Enter]
  • on the FRCS screen, key the relevant financial year in the Financial year field > [Enter]
  • review this screen to identify if the most recent calculation status is 'Potential Non Lodger' with an 'incomplete' result

To identify a potential FTB FRC:

  • on the OPDL screen, check that the over recovered debt is an FTB NLD
  • in the Select View field, key PRK (or key OPDLP in the Nxt: field) > [Enter]
  • review this screen to identify parked or held FTB FRC debts:
  • disregard the FTB FRC debt if it has a QTN status (do not transfer to a quarantined debt)
  • if no debts display, the customer has no parked or held debts. A message will also display at the top of the screen to advise the customer has no parked or held debts

To identify a recently zeroed NLD, indicating a potential FTB FRC:

  • on the OPDL screen, check that the over recovered debt is an FTB NLD
  • 'S'elect the FTB NLD debt
  • in the Nxt field, key OPDA > [Enter]
  • debt adjustments will display with a reason, amount and date. More than one adjustment can occur on a debt
  • review this screen to identify if the FTB NLD was zeroed in the previous 7 days

Is there a potential FTB NLD or FTB FRC?

  • Yes, and there is a:
    • potential FTB NLD, go to Step 8
    • potential FTB FRC, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

Over recovered - review the garnishee of continuing income + Read more ...

A debt may over recover from a garnishee of continuing income.

Alternatively, a debt may over recover from a debt adjustment rather than the garnishee of continuing income.

A garnishee payment may show on a record where there is no garnishee coded. These payments are dividends paid by an Administrator due to a bankruptcy or debt agreement. They are coded as a garnishee due to a system limitation.

Check the following screens for:

  • a garnishee, view Activity List (OPAL) screen and Repayment History (OPRH)
  • a debt adjustment, view Debt Amount (OPDA) screen

If the record indicates that the garnishee is recent or current, and the garnishee repayment caused the debt to over recover:

  • phone the employer and request they cease sending payments
  • send a cease garnishee and advise customer (Q614) letter to request they cease sending payments, and
  • to proceed with the refund to the customer see Step 1 in Table 3

If the record indicates that:

  • the garnishee is old and an adjustment caused the debt to over recover, to proceed with the refund, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • there was never a garnishee arrangement, refer to a Bankruptcy Officer. The Resources page contains referral details

Note: there are limits to who can amend a garnishee of continuing income arrangement:

  • If this step is outside of the debt staff members work area, create an ACTDOC using the Garnishee Customer Service Fast Note
  • The team allocated to garnishee Work Items will send the Q614 and proceed with the refund

Over recovered - review the cheque and credit card repayment + Read more ...

There have been known incidents where some customers are using fraudulent credit cards and cheques to make repayments. Take extreme care.

Do not action over recovered debt activities where the repayment was made by cheque or credit card in the last 7 days. This includes when the customer requests a refund. If the payment dishonours or rejects, the customer is not entitled to refund.

Was the repayment made in the last 7 days?

  • Yes, hold the Work Item (via the Activity List (AL) screen) for 7 days from the date of the payment
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

13

Over recovered - review the External Collection Agency (ECA) repayment + Read more ...

A debt may over recover by a repayment made to an ECA.

The ECA can remit payments to Services Australia for any recall reason other than FIN (view on the OPMC screen). Investigation is only required by the ECA Relationships Team where the payment is extremely unusual, for example, but not limited to:

  • the last payment received was 5 years ago and now another payment has been received, without explanation
  • the customer has been making payments of $50 every week and the account is now finalised. A payment is then made of $200. Investigate to determine the origin of the payment. For example, has the customer actually paid the $200 or has the payment been inadvertently allocated against the incorrect record?

Alternatively, a debt may over recover by a debt adjustment rather than the repayment. This situation will not require more investigation.

Check the following screens for:

  • a debt adjustment, view OPDA
  • an ECA repayment, view OPRH
  • the ECA referral status, view OPMC

If the record indicates that the ECA repayment caused the debt to over recover:

  • access the ECA portal (or consult with a team member/leader who has access)
  • view notes regarding a claim or investigation into the repayment
  • view the repayment date to check if a mixture of repayments caused the debt to over recover. For example a tax garnishee and an ECA repayment receipted within a short period of time
  • if there are no notes regarding a claim or investigation, and a mixture of recent repayments over recovered the debt, proceed with the refund. See Step 1 in Table 3
  • if there are notes regarding a claim or investigation, and/or the repayment is unusual (see examples above) , refer to the ECA Relationships Team. The Resources page contains referral details

If the record indicates that the repayment is old and an adjustment caused the debt to over recover, proceed with the refund. See Step 1 in Table 3.

15

Hardship - review the request to refund a garnishee repayment + Read more ...

The principles of natural justice apply to each customer. Customers can experience exceptional and unforeseen circumstances which can cause them real financial hardship. In these cases, a full or partial refund of a repayment may be considered.

This is a re-assessment of the customer's circumstances, not a review of the decision.

It is a requirement that customers provide documentary evidence of hardship to support a refund of a garnishee repayment.

To proceed with an assessment of the customer's circumstances, see Garnishee customer does not agree with decision.

When the request has been assessed and approved, proceed with a refund. See Step 1 in Table 3.

16

Hardship - confirm + Read more ...

Debt staff must use discretion when making the decision to refund a repayment.

Debt staff must consider:

  • the reason for the request
  • the repayment source:
    • it is unusual for a customer to request a refund for an amount they chose to pay. For example, cash, and payments made via Australia Post
    • it may be suitable to discuss the customer's request in more detail and request documentary evidence if their verbal reasoning is not sufficient
  • the repayment date:
    • a customer may call to request a refund for an amount they paid months ago
    • it would be reasonable to refuse a refund for repayments received more than 2 weeks ago
  • if the customer has a history of requesting refunds:
    • a customer may call every 3 months to request a refund because their non-standard withholdings arrangement ended
    • the decision remains discretionary and all of the customer's circumstances need to be taken into account prior to making a decision to approve or refuse a refund

Process the refund when:

  • the customer is experiencing exceptional and unforeseen circumstances which caused them real financial hardship
  • recent and unexpected standard withholdings impact on the customer's ability to pay their living expenses

Can a refund be processed?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, tell the customer they do not qualify for a refund of the repayment. DOC the decision

17

Administrative error or business need + Read more ...

Multiple debts - Customers with multiple debts can only ask to pause recovery on the debt/s that are pending the outcome of a reassessment, explanation or review. Standard debt recovery processes apply to all other debts. Check OPRH to confirm the amount withheld has been applied to the debt under review. If not, a refund is not applicable.

Reassessment, explanation or review - If customer requests a pause, recovery is paused for 3 months (6 months for compliance interventions). If the customer's reassessment, explanation or review is not completed by this time and they want the pause extended, they need to contact to request an extension.

An administrative error may require a refund because:

  • withholdings were not cancelled when a write off was coded or
  • a pause (temporary write off) on recovery requested pending the outcome of an appeal was not applied, and
  • the customer only has one determined debt, being the debt under review

Has an administrative error been identified requiring a refund?

  • Yes:
    • obtain direction and approval from leadership (APS5 level and above) before refunding. If approved, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No:
    • tell the customer they do not qualify for a refund of the repayment
    • Record details on a DOC of the decision
    • Procedure ends here

Confirm the refund method + Read more ...

Refunds are issued in:

  • Debt Management Information System (DMIS) in Customer First using the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen, including:
    • OVO, when refunding a debt repayment via direct credit
    • RRF, when refunding the recovery fee component via direct credit
    • ROI, when refunding an interest charge component via direct credit

    • EFT, when refunding a debt repayment via direct credit

Refunds not payable through DMIS or PD require a Notice to prepare a Refund Account (SF030).

Use RFM in DMIS or PD when processing the following manual refunds via direct credit or cheque:

  • third party withholdings to third parties (not the debtor)
  • of withholdings used to repay an Other Government Department (OGD) debt
  • for deceased customers
  • on an organisation record
  • when the customer's benefit type is rejected. For example, their benefit line displays NSA/REJ
  • when there are system limitations. For example, refunds for SHC

If the method used to pay the refund is:

  • direct credit (via the DMIS PYAJ screen, PD Customer Refund Action or an SF030 form):

    • the address will need to be confirmed

Confirm and update the customer's bank account details + Read more ...

Direct credits paid through PYAJ or the Customer Refund Action are paid to the bank account listed on the Payment Destination (PAS) screen:

  • a customer can have a different bank account for each benefit type (NSS, FAO)
  • the Resources page contains a list of payment systems and their linked benefit types
  • the benefit type of the debt on OPDL will determine what bank account is used
  • for example, a FAO FTB NLD debt will go to the FAO bank account
  • Debt Staff must check that the bank account for the relevant benefit type is the most recent one

Confirm and update the customer's bank account details prior to refunding via direct credit:

  • view the OPDL screen for the debt benefit type which has a repayment to refund
  • view the Payment Summary (PS) screen to identify the customer's most recent bank account and any returned payments:
    • more investigation may be required prior to refunding if there are rejected and returned payments. See Replacement payments
    • if paying third party withholdings via RFM, review the PS screen on the third party's record to identify the correct bank account details

  • view the PAS screen to identify the correct benefit type for the refund
  • update the details if it is not the most recently used bank account on PS or Document List (DL). See Changing payment destination
  • if paying third party withholdings via RFM, review the PAS screen on the third party's record to identify the correct bank account details
  • do not record a third party's bank account details on a customer's record
  • view the DL screen to check for documented details of a new bank account

Do not include multiple requests in the same form.

To prepare a refund request using an SF030:

  • complete the SF030 Notice to prepare a Refund Account online, including the:
    • Amount
    • Method (Direct Credit, cheque)
    • Name of customer, if deceased, address to 'the Estate of customer's name'
    • Address of customer (cheques will be sent to this address)
    • Document ID (if issued to a third party, for example Workers Compensation)
    • Income support payment (benefit type related to the over recovered debt) Note: refunds less than $10 are automatically transferred to the Trust Fund. If required, use the Trust Fund - Other Trust Monies (TFOTM) option
    • Customer Reference Number
    • Bank/Financial Institution (if Direct Credit)
    • Account Number (if Direct Credit)
    • BSB Number (if Direct Credit)
    • Reason for refund
  • print the form and complete the remaining sections:
    • obtain and write the GL Code of the benefit type of the raised debt. The Resources page has the GL Codes
    • obtain and write the cost centre code 1000298
    • name of debt staff member, signature and date signed
    • name of spending delegate (APS5 or above), signature, date signed and position details
  • scan the signed form and save as a PDF
