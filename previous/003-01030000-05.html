AGDRP - Abridged statements for phone claims during ACC

Table 1 – the recordings for Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP).

Recording 1 - AGDRP

Privacy Notice

Privacy and your personal information

The privacy and security of your personal information is important to us, and is protected by law. We need to collect information about you so we can process and manage your applications and payments, and provide services to you. If you do not provide this information, we may not be able to process your claim, determine your eligibility for a payment or provide other services to you. We’ll also use information you have previously provided to Services Australia for the purpose of assessing this claim and administering this emergency payment.

We only share your information with other parties where you have agreed, or where the law allows or requires it. If you choose to use your digital identity, we will also collect information from, and disclose information about you to your identity provider to allow us to verify your identity electronically. If you do not allow us to collect this information, you may need to provide identification documents in person at one of our service centres.

We may share your information with Australian and state government departments and organisations, including as part of the emergency response and recovery effort for this disaster.

More information about how we handle your personal information can be found on our https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/individuals/privacy and Centrelink’s Personal Information Policy web pages. Our Privacy Policy also contains information about how you can access and correct your information and how to make a privacy complaint.

By continuing, you acknowledge that you have heard and understand this privacy notice. Do you wish to continue?

Honesty Statement

Services Australia has advanced methods of detecting false claims for payments. Our https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/organisations/about-us/our-commitments/strategies/fraud-control-strategy contains information about our Fraud Control Framework.

By continuing you acknowledge that:

  • the information you will provide in this claim is complete and correct

By continuing with this claim, you also acknowledge and understand that:

  • this payment is for people adversely affected by a disaster and not for minor damage or inconvenience
  • information previously provided by you to Services Australia may be used for the purposes of assessing this claim and administering this payment, including requesting evidence of your eligibility after you have received a payment
  • Services Australia can make relevant enquiries to make sure you receive the correct entitlement
  • Services Australia may request evidence to support any claims made in this application (this may occur once you have already received a payment)
  • you must tell Services Australia of any changes to this information or your circumstances within 14 days (this includes your address, a change of relationship, or circumstances which may affect your payment)
  • any overpayment will be recovered
  • giving false or misleading information is a serious offence

Do you acknowledge and understand this statement and wish to continue?

Customer Declaration

You declare that:

  • the information in this claim for Disaster Recovery Payment is complete, and reflects my current personal circumstances
  • I have read the terms and conditions for Electronic Messaging and online letters if I have provided a mobile phone number or email address
  • I will notify Centrelink of any changes to this information within 14 days of the change/s occurring
  • I have heard and understood the Privacy Notice

Do you understand and acknowledge this declaration and wish to continue with this claim?

Helping customers provide income details - DRA/NZ DRA online claims

Table 5

Item

Description

1

Payment destination

If the customer:

  • receives regular payments and wants to change their payment destination to an account they have not used before, make sure they can confirm their current payment destination. This is to avoid potential fraud known as ‘payment hijacking’
  • has a payment nominee, a message says payment will go to the nominee. If the customer does not want the payment to go to a voluntary payment nominee, cancel the nominee arrangement in Customer First. Then continue’ the started claim
  • advised in the Contact requirements section they want to appoint a payment nominee, tell them they will need to provide their own bank details until the nominee appointment is confirmed. If the customer does not want to continue with the claim until then, tell them they can continue with the claim later (within 13 weeks or it will cancel). Procedure ends here

2

Savings summary

Cash on hand

  • Includes any money in their wallet, stored at home (for example, in a safe), or in a safety deposit box.
  • Does not include any money being held to meet day-to-day expenses or payment of outstanding bills

Bank accounts

  • The payment destination bank account and any open accounts on the customer’s record will present to edit the balance. This should be the balance on the date their income reduced. They may be able to access the details in their online banking (statements or transaction history)
  • Customers should also add any other on-call deposits with banks, credit unions and building societies with credit funds in which they have a share

3

Lost income details

Date income reduced as a direct result of the disaster.

Amount of reduction (all, part, self-employed running at a loss and will incur further loss, have not lost any income),

If the customer says they have not lost any income, discuss their situation further to check if DRA/NZ DRA is suitable for them.

If the customer confirms they have not lost income from any sources as a direct result of the disaster, tell them they would not be eligible and if they continue with the claim, it will be rejected. If they:

  • Do not want to continue with the claim
    • Tell them to exit the claim and cancel it
    • record details of discussions with the customer, including why they are not eligible
    • Check if they may be eligible for AGDRP/NZ DRP (if activated, and if no claim granted or lodged)
    • Procedure ends here
  • Want to continue with the claim
    • Make sure details they give about their income are recorded to support the Service Officer processing the claim
    • Tell the customer they can continue with details in the online claim. However, their claim will be assessed on criteria for loss of income. Based on this conversation, the claim will be rejected when processed
    • Record details of the conversation a DOC to assist the Service Officer processing the claim

4

Employment income details

Tell the customer to provide the employment income, noting it may be for an employer they no longer work for since the disaster, or a new employer.

  • Employer’s details
    • name, address, ABN, telephone
    • type of work (seasonal, part-time, casual, full-time)
    • if income was impacted
  • Gross fortnightly income
  • Returned to work
    This refers to the customer returning to work after any period of not working as a direct result of the disaster
  • Planned leave
    This relates to the employer for which updates are being made, not ‘any’ employer.
    The 13 weeks referred to in this question is taken from the date the customer advised their income reduced
  • Gross income
    Fortnightly (when prompted based on the customer’s responses)
  • Gross income
    When prompted, this refers to the 8 weeks just before the disaster event.
    If the customer advised regular fortnightly income in the previous question, they will need to halve that amount and record a weekly amount in each of the 8 weeks
  • Expected to earn during this period
    This refers to the expected income for the 13 weeks from the date the customer’s income reduced
    For example, if the customer earns the same amount each week, it would be weekly income x 13, or if the same amount each fortnight, fortnightly income x 6.5
  • Expected income if the disaster didn’t happen
    If the customer indicated they would not have expected to continue earning the same amount had the disaster not occurred (this could be due to an expected wage increase or reduction), the expected income to be used is for the 13 weeks from the date their income reduced. They will also need to explain why their income would have differed and provide evidence. For example, expecting a promotion or an existing plan to reduce part-time hours

5

Self-employment details

  • Self-employment details
    Name, address, ABN, telephone, date business started, industry, if still operating
  • Operating at profit/loss
    Net income requested is for the 8 weeks just before the disaster. Customers have the option to provide profit/loss details for a period longer than the 8 weeks. If they select loss, they do not need to use a negative sign before the dollar amount
  • Expected profit/loss
    Net income requested is for the 13 weeks from the date the customer's income reduced. If they select loss, they do not need to use a negative sign before the dollar amount
  • Expected income if the disaster didn’t happen
    If the customer indicated they would not have expected to continue earning the same amount had the disaster not occurred (this could be due to an expected increase or reduction), the expected income to be used is for the 13 weeks from the date their income reduced. They will also need to explain why their income would have differed and provide evidence. For example, seasonal work going into high season

6

Income as an employee of a Trust or Company

This question refers to customers who are a shareholder or beneficiary of a company or trust, and are paid wages for remunerative work as an employee of that company or trust.

If the customer only receives directors fees or dividends from a trust or company, they should select Next to go to the next question.

  • Company or Trust details
    Name, ABN, telephone, ownership percentage, date business commenced
  • Paid as an employee?
    If no, the customer will be taken to the end of the question set
    If yes, they will need to confirm if they declare the income paid in their personal tax return and if the company will declare their income as an employee in their tax return
  • Depending on responses, the customer will be asked to provide their usual fortnightly gross income, and a history of income if income has reduced
  • Gross income
    Fortnightly (when prompted based on customer’s responses)
  • Gross income
    When prompted, this refers to the 4 weeks just before the disaster (noting this is usually 8 weeks for customers)
    If a customer advised regular fortnightly income in the previous question, they will need to halve that amount and record it in each of the weeks
    Customers can choose to give income for a period other than the 4 weeks. This may be the case if they work seasonally for the company/trust
  • Expected income
    When prompted, this refers to the expected income for the 13 weeks from the date the customer's income reduced
  • Expected income if the disaster didn’t happen
    If the customer indicated they would not have expected to continue earning the same amount had the disaster not occurred (this could be due to an expected increase or reduction), the expected income to be used is for the 13 weeks from the date their income reduced. They will also need to explain why their income would have differed and provide evidence. For example, seasonal work about to enter high season

7

Income Protection Insurance

Customers are asked whether they have income protection insurance, and whether they have or will be making a claim.

In response to a successful claim, the customer needs to select:

  • Payments have started, or
  • Payments have not started
  • Tell the customer they will also need to provide details of the start date, period of payments and payment amounts.

8

Interest in real estate (in or outside Australia)

When customers select this option, they will be asked if they receive any income from those properties. This includes rental income, non-rental income (such as agistment fees, hobby farming), or income from the property they live in (for example, Airbnb, renting a granny flat to a non-family member). If they select ‘no’, they will go to the next question in the claim.

If the customer does receive income, and they should only be listing properties they own or partly own, they will need to provide relevant information:

Although the following information is requested, tell the customer the information they provide will not be used for any purpose other than this claim. Note: even though this information is not required for claim assessment, the customer cannot skip these questions.

Pending system enhancements, information currently requested (but not relevant) includes:

  • Type of property
  • Property value
  • Building summary

Property ownership

Although it asks for property ownership, only ‘you’ is selectable from the dropdown menu. This is because only the customer’s interest can affect eligibility.

Property financials

  • Rental income
    Amount and frequency. If only rented on holidays the number of weeks rented will be prompted
  • Non-rental income
    Source (for example, horse agistment, hobby farming, animal boarding); amount and frequency of income
  • Issues that affect the property
    The customer should respond ‘no’, unless the property was within an impacted area and income has been impacted by the disaster which has impacted the rental income

Staff will need to request:

  • the income from real estate for the 8 weeks before the disaster, and
  • the expected income for the 13 weeks after the disaster (or income loss)

9

Investments

As all income (except for excluded income) is used to compare the customer’s income to the AWOTE, the customer needs to provide details of all their investments.

They should only list those details in which they have a share. If investments are in their partner’s name only, they do not need to provide details.

  • Share Details
  • Managed Investments
  • Income Stream products in Australia

10

Other income

  • Overseas income
  • Any other income

  • Payments have started, or
  • Payments have not started

Ask the customer to provide details of the start date, period of payments and payment amounts. (This information is not currently requested in the claim). Record the details provided by the customer to include in a DOC to support the Service Officer processing the claim.

Tell the customer they will need to provide evidence of their income protection insurance payments so their claim can be assessed.

8

Interest in real estate (in or outside Australia)

Tell the customer they need to advise whether they own or partly own any property inside or outside Australia.

Then ask the customer if they receive any income from:

  • those properties. Income includes rental income, non-rental income (for example, from horse agistment fees, hobby farming), or
  • the property they live in if they own or partly own it (for example, Airbnb, renting a granny flat to a non-family member)

If the customer does not receive income, the claim will go to the next question.

If the customer does receive income from properties they own or partly own, they will need to provide relevant details.

Although the following details are requested, tell the customer the information they provide will not be used for any purpose other than this claim. Pending system enhancements, details requested (but not relevant) include:

  • Type of property
  • Property value
  • Building summary

Property ownership

Although it asks for property ownership, the only drop down option is ‘you’. This is because only the customer’s interest can affect eligibility.

Property financials

  • Rental income
    Amount and frequency. If only rented on holidays the number of weeks rented will be prompted
  • Non-rental income
    Source (for example, horse agistment, hobby farming, animal boarding); amount and frequency of income
  • Issues that affect the property
    The response should be ‘no’, unless the property was within a declared disaster area and income has reduced as a direct result of the disaster

Ask the customer if their rental/non-rental income reduced as a direct result of the disaster.

If the income has reduced as a result of the disaster:

  • Ask the customer for their income
    • in the 8 weeks before the disaster, and
    • for the 13 weeks from the date of the disaster/date income reduced
  • Record the details in a DOC to help the Service Officer processing the claim
  • Tell the customer they will need to provide evidence of the change in income so their claim can be assessed

9

Other income circumstance

As all income (except for excluded income) is used to compare the customer’s income to the AWOTE, they will need to provide details of all their investments and other income.

They only need to advise income in which they have a share. If investments are in their partner’s name only, they do not need to provide details

Investments include:

  • share details
  • Managed Investments
  • Income Stream products in Australia

Other income includes:

  • Overseas income
  • Any other income - a list displays to prompt the customer

Ask the customer if there has been a change in their investment or other income as a direct result of the disaster. In most cases, there would be no change. For example, if there was a reduction in share value as result of the disaster, this would be an indirect impact and therefore not counted as a loss of income.

If the income has reduced as a result of the disaster:

  • Ask the customer for their rental income
    • in the 8 weeks before the disaster, and
    • for the 13 weeks from the date of the disaster/date income reduced
  • Record the details in a DOC to help the Service Officer processing the claim
  • Tell the customer they will need to provide evidence of the change in income so their claim can be assessed
