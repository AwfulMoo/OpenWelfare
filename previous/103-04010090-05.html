This page contains scenarios of the Residential Costs Option (RCO). These examples show how the RCO may or may not be advantageous to the student. It also contains examples to assist when determining whether RCO is payable to someone who has already received a Relocation Scholarship for the same period.

Rent assistance

Rent Assistance is only calculated for the time a student is paying residential costs. Therefore, when considering whether the RCO is more beneficial this needs to be taken into account when calculating the entitlements. Generally a student will only pay residential costs for the period of study and not during the vacation periods. Therefore, whilst a student can be paid the away from home rate during the vacation period, they do not continue to receive rent assistance during that period.

Assessing student accommodation for RCO - Choice 1

Bowie is 18 years old and approved to live away from home. Bowie is in first year of university study. Bowie’s base rate of ABSTUDY Living Allowance is $462.50 per fortnight. Bowie is also eligible to receive Rent Assistance for the periods when living in a residential facility. Currently the residential costs are $350 per week.

For Bowie to assess whether the RCO is the most suitable option, the following must be considered:

Choice 1 - No Residential Costs Option

Bowie can access Living Allowance and Rent Assistance (for the time Bowie pays 'rent' to the residential facility - Bowie is 'on-campus' for 36 weeks or 18 fortnights).

In the first year of study it is more beneficial if Bowie does not claim the RCO.

However, in the second and subsequent years of study, the RCO may be more beneficial depending on whether Bowie qualifies for the regional rate of Relocation Scholarship. Note: the rate of an Indigenous CAS does not decrease after the first year and is usually more beneficial than the RS.

Using the above figures (note: these will change with CPI and other increases) if Bowie is qualified for the regional rate of Relocation Scholarship in the second year of study, the total for No RCO would be $19,135.00. If Bowie is not qualified for the regional rate of Relocation Scholarship, the total for No RCO would be $17,048.80. Let's assume Bowie does not qualify for the regional rate of Relocation Scholarship. As both of the No RCO totals are less than what Bowie would receive under the RCO option, it would be more beneficial if Bowie accepted the RCO in the second and subsequent years of study.

Entitlement assessment scenarios

Table 3: This table describes scenarios of the Residential Costs Option (RCO) and these scenarios highlight how the RCO may or may not be advantageous to the student.

Item

Description

Scenario 1

Deborah is an ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) student who is approved to live away from home

Deborah is an ABSTUDY PES student who is approved to live away from home. Deborah is staying at a hostel that charges $40 per week and is not eligible for the Relocation Scholarship.

  • If Deborah pays the fees the ABSTUDY PES of $62.40 per fortnight will be received, but Deborah will have to pay $80
  • If Deborah chooses the RCO there will be a nil entitlement to PES but the hostel fees will be covered. Deborah will not be out of pocket

Deborah must decide which option is better.

Scenario 2

Isaac is accepted into a course at the University of Western Australia. Isaac applies for a position at St Catherine's College and is successful

However, Isaac decides to try living in a private accommodation for semester one. Isaac is paid the Relocation Scholarship in February as part of the entitlements. After three months in private accommodation Isaac realises moving into a residential college would allow more success in the course. Isaac moves into St Catherine's College in June. Isaac is not eligible for RCO because of being offered a place in February prior to receiving the Relocation Scholarship payment and choosing not to accept the offer.

Scenario 3

Lilly was paid a Relocation Scholarship and then accepts a position

Lilly is in the second year of a course at Melbourne University. Lilly applies for a position at Trinity College but is unsuccessful due to a long wait list. Lilly is paid a Relocation Scholarship in February. Trinity College offers Lilly a position when one becomes available in June. Lilly accepts the offer. Lilly is eligible for RCO because of being offered the position after the Relocation Scholarship was paid.

Scenario 4

Edward is in receipt of the Indigenous Student Success Program (ISSP) for accommodation costs, living in an on campus residential college and considering taking up RCO

Edward is in the first year of a Bachelor of Paramedicine at the Australian National University and is living on campus. Edward is in receipt of the ISSP to assist with costs of living away from home. Receipt of the ISSP for accommodation costs precludes Edward from qualification for RCO.

Edward assesses the most beneficial financial outcome is to ask the university to cancel the ISSP from the beginning of the year and take up RCO instead. Edward provides a confirmation of retrospective ISSP cancellation and RCO is now payable.

Scenario 5

Dianne is in receipt of a Commonwealth Accommodation Scholarship and is considering moving into a residential college and taking up RCO

Dianne is in the first year of a Bachelor of Science at University of Sydney and is living with a relation in private board. Dianne is in receipt of an Indigenous Commonwealth Accommodation Scholarship (CAS) to assist with costs of living away from home in Bourke. Dianne wants to move into a residential college on campus. Receipt of an Indigenous CAS precludes Dianne from qualification for RCO. Dianne assesses the most beneficial financial outcome is to ask the university to cancel the Indigenous CAS from the beginning of the year and take up RCO instead. Dianne provides a confirmation of retrospective Indigenous CAS cancellation and RCO is now payable.

Scenario 6

Charlotte has applied for an accommodation scholarship and is moving into campus accommodation and taking up RCO

Charlotte is starting the second year of a Bachelor of Education at University of Southern Queensland. Charlotte has decided to live on campus from the start of her second year of study. Charlotte is now living on campus, and has applied for a Rural Residential Scholarship of $3,350 which contributes to the accommodation costs. Charlotte is granted the Rural Residential Scholarship. As the Rural Residential Scholarship is not considered an equivalent Commonwealth scholarship, it does not preclude Charlotte from RCO, however it will reduce the total amount of residential costs payable by $3,350. Charlotte is eligible and takes up RCO.

Scenario 7

Jamison has applied for RCO whilst studying a distance education/correspondence course

Jamison has commenced a year-long course at Marr Mooditj Training and applies for ABSTUDY. Whilst the course is a distance education/correspondence course that is studied externally, Jamison has chosen to move into a residential campus affiliated with the education institution and apply for RCO. Even though the away from home rate is not normally paid when studying a distance education/correspondence course, Jamison has been approved the away from home rate under the Home Conditions criteria, and is therefore eligible to RCO.

Scenario 8

Jasmine is receiving RCO and ABY Living Allowance payment suspended Departure Overseas (DOT) for a period charged by accommodation provider

Jasmine is studying a Bachelor of Arts at the University of Technology Sydney and is currently taking up RCO. Jasmine goes on an overseas holiday during the semester break and returns home after the first week of semester 2. ABSTUDY is suspended DOT for the period of the overseas absence. Jasmine provides an invoice from the accommodation provider for residential fees for the full semester 2 period, As Jasmine has received at least $1 of Living Allowance during semester 2, RCO is payable for the full Semester 2 period.

Scenario 9

Rose is receiving RCO and ABY Grant Date is after the accommodation provider’s first date of the period charged

Rose is studying a Bachelor of Veterinary Biology / Bachelor of Veterinary Science at Charles Sturt University, Wagga Wagga campus and is currently taking up RCO. Rose will be residing on campus at the Butler Halls First Year College. The period charged on the invoice supplied is 2 weeks prior to the ABSTUDY grant date. As Rose is to receive at least $1 of Living Allowance during the period charged by the accommodation provider, RCO is payable for the full period.

Scenario 10

Change of Residential College prior to end of contract

Amy lives in a residential college whilst studying a Bachelor of Engineering at the Australian National University. At the end of Amy’s 3rd year of study, Amy has chosen to complete her studies at another campus closer to home. The charges at the new residential provider overlaps the current one by 4 weeks. The residential college charges a contract break fee of $750 and the remainder of the 4 weeks residential charges of $2300. Amy is unable to pay the whole amount and requests ABSTUDY assistance. Amy has negotiated with the college and reduced the residential charges down to 2 weeks with a balance of $1150. Amy applies for ABSTUDY Additional Assistance. Additional Assistance can be approved as Amy and her family would be placed in significant financial hardship and risk of withdrawing from studies to cover these costs.

Scenario 11

Jake receives an amount of Living Allowance once he commences living on campus

Jake has recently been assessed for ABSTUDY. Due to parental income Jake is not entitled to receive Living Allowance whilst residing in the family home. Jake contacts to discuss the eligibility criteria for RCO. Jake meets away from home conditions under the travel time and access criteria. Jake advises the on campus, residential fees are $400 per week. Once the AC screen is coded with the residential details it is determined that Jake is eligible to receive $14.00 of Rent Assistance per fortnight. As Rent Assistance is deemed an amount of Living Allowance, Jake is eligible for RCO. Jakes fortnightly payment whilst in receipt of RCO will remain at $14 as this is less than the RCO allowance of $41.60.

Scenario 12

Payment of RCO during the vacation break

Sam had eligibility for RCO assessed at the start of the school year for the full year, and will remain at the residential college over the vacation break. As Sam’s contract has already been negotiated with the provider and assessed as meeting eligibility for RCO, Sam will be eligible for residential fees for the whole period being charged. This includes any semester or vacation breaks.

Scenario 13

Payment of RCO for State Care customers who has ceased being in State care only because of their age

Dylan is living on campus whilst studying a Bachelor of Science at the University of Western Australia. Dylan contacts to discuss the eligibility criteria for RCO. Dylan has ceased being in State care only because of their age. Dylan will be eligible to take up RCO. Dylan takes up RCO.

As Dylan has ceased being in State care only because of their age, there is no need for Dylan to meet one of the criteria for approval of Away from Home entitlements to be eligible for RCO.
