Customer request to complete business income reconciliation (BIR) + Read more ...

Customers can request BIR be completed if they believe they may be entitled to a top-up for the relevant financial year.

Complete the following checks on the customer’s record:

  • the customer had an estimate of greater than zero for the relevant financial year. If the estimate was nil or a loss, they are unlikely to be owed a top-up from BIR. For help finding the estimate, see Business income estimates for Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
  • if BIR is pending for any other years to the one the customer is requesting, check whether all documents for these years are available and advise the customer we will process these at the same time
  • the required financial documents have been supplied for BIR to be completed. For more information, go to Step 4

Once the checks have been completed:

  • advise the customer:
    • the BIR process will be completed over the next 24 months
    • 'Your entitlement to a top-up for the (relevant) financial year will be confirmed once reconciliation has been completed. Please be aware, the outcome may not be a top-up as expected and could result in an overpayment or no change to your original entitlement. If the result is an overpayment, you may be eligible for a waiver and you will be advised by letter once this is completed'
  • if the customer wants to have their request for BIR completed now, use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Rural > Reconciliation > Customer request for BIR to be completed. Transfer the open DOC to region code FHH

4

Financial documents required + Read more ...

FHA customers need to provide all of the following documents for their actual business income to be assessed:

  • their personal income tax return
  • the farm and non-farm business income tax return(s) if different to their personal tax return. For example partnership, trust or company tax return
  • financial statements for each business, including:
    • profit and loss statement
    • balance sheet
    • depreciation schedule
  • their partner's personal income tax return (if they are partnered)

See Resources for a document checklist to help farmers.

5

Separated customers + Read more ...

Customers who have separated from their partner may be unable to provide all the information required to complete business income reconciliation (BIR). The customer will still be required to provide documentation for BIR to be completed.

Was the customer involved in the farming business?

  • Yes, the customer will need to supply their individual tax return as well as a copy of the business tax return. This could be a partnership, trust or company tax return
  • No, if the customer was receiving FHA as the partner of a farmer
    • if the customer is not required to lodge a tax return, see Table 2 for the documents required to verify the exemption
    • if the customer has income from other sources, they are required to provide their individual tax return to verify they did not have any primary production income distributions

Note: the Income Tax Return (ITR) Review Case will not be set to a status of complete until BIR is also undertaken for the ex-partner.

If the customer's situation is complex and does not align with the above scenarios, complete the following hand off:

  • Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Rural > Reconciliation > BIR separated cannot supply documents
  • change the service reason to FHA
  • the additional keyword BIR will be automatically added and Fast Note transferred to region FHH

Due to the possible complexity, seek policy advice from Portfolio Operations on how BIR is actioned.

If a non-lodger debt has been raised and the customer is requesting a reassessment, see Table 4 Step 3.

8

View business income reconciliation result + Read more ...

To view the results in Process Direct in the Super Key field, key FBI to navigate to the Farm Business Income screen. Select the financial year to view.

If estimated business income used during the financial year was:

  • more than assessed actual business income, a top-up payment is calculated for the difference between the customer's FHA entitlement and what they were paid
  • less than assessed actual business income, a recoverable debt is calculated for the difference between the customer's FHA entitlement and what they were paid
  • equal to assessed actual business income, the result is nil adjustment

If the customer provided revised estimates during the year, separate adjustments are shown for the period each estimate was used. If the customer was paid a manual rate at any time during the financial year, the reconciliation will need to be manually calculated and applied. A separate adjustment will be shown for the period of the manual rate. The overall result for the financial year is the sum of all adjustments.

If the customer requests an explanation or wants to apply for a formal review of the business income reconciliation (BIR) outcome, go to Step 9.

9

Customer requests an explanation or applies for a formal review of the business income reconciliation outcome + Read more ...

If the customer requests an explanation or applies for a formal review of the business income reconciliation outcome, see Request for an explanation or application for a formal review.

If the customer is:

  • applying for a formal review, the application for formal review will be referred directly to an ARO
  • requesting an explanation:
    • add the additional keyword of BIR to the appeal DOC
    • transfer the DOC to region code FHH

Note: customers with an existing RCN debt will be automatically reviewed under the Farm Household Support Amendment (Debt Waiver) Act 2021 legislative change. They do not need to request a review.

3

ATO exemption granted + Read more ...

If the ATO has granted the customer an exemption from lodging a tax return, the customer is not required to lodge a tax return for FHA and will not be subject to the business income reconciliation process.

Only FHA customers paid as the partner of a farmer and not involved in a business may not have to lodge a tax return. All farmers are required to lodge a tax return. This is because a business is not exempt from lodging an income tax return. Sole trader and partnership businesses must lodge a tax return even if the income is below the tax-free threshold.

If the partner of the farmer advises they are not required to lodge:

  • check the customer's record to confirm there are no sources of income. For example, business income, earnings, income from investments and lump sums
  • ask the customer whether they have received advice from the ATO or their accountant that they do not need to lodge

Has evidence of the ATO exemption been provided and scanned to the customer record?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, an exemption cannot be granted until the ATO evidence has been provided
    • if the customer's ITR Review Case is to be cancelled, go to Step 5
    • otherwise, procedure ends here

4

Record ATO exemption in Process Direct + Read more ...

Run the FHA BIR workflow to record the exemption.

In the Farm Business Income Actual subtask, select Add then:

  • enter zero as the Net Actual Income
  • Date of Event - 1 July of the relevant financial year
  • select the relevant Financial Year
  • Exemption Date - enter the date the exemption was provided
  • select the Exemption Provided checkbox
  • select Assess
  • in the Update Header Data pop-up enter:
    • Receipt Date - the date the customer notifies
    • Channel - the channel the information was received
  • select Save
  • check the status of the Income Tax Review (ITR) Review Case for the relevant financial year has automatically set to Complete (not required)
  • Note/DOC the customer’s record to confirm the details of the exemption

Go to Step 6.

5

ITR Review Case to be cancelled + Read more ...

Financial documents on customer record + Read more ...

Financial documents may include:

  • personal income tax returns for customer and partner
  • business income tax returns for all farm and non-farm businesses the customer/partner is involved in
  • business financial statements for each business the customer/partner is involved in, including profit and loss statements, balance sheets and depreciation schedules

Non-farm income: before 16 December 2019, non-farm directly related businesses were non-farm income and assessed separately. If the customer has sources of non-farm business income that has been included as farm income in the tax return and profit and loss (for example, contracting, harvesting), a separate profit and loss is only required where a potential BIR top up exists and separating the income without allowing expenses will affect the entitlement to the potential top up.

Depreciation:

  • Sole traders and partnerships BIR for 2014-15 through 2019-20: SBE depreciation is an allowable deduction
  • Trust and Companies BIR for 2014-15 through 2016-17: SBE depreciation is an allowable deduction
  • 2017-18 through 2019-20: where an estimate is for 2017-18 or later financial years and was received before 16 March 2017, or later but not assessed by a CAO. SBE will be an allowable deduction for the entire assessed BIR year
  • Only where the customer may have an entitlement to a potential BIR top up, ask them to provide the amount of depreciation the business would have been able to claim under Division 40 of the ITAA 1997. See Business deductions

4

Assess actual business income + Read more ...

See Assessment of income and assets from business structures for Centrelink payments.

Consistent with social security law, to calculate a person's ordinary income, expenses incurred in deriving business income can be deducted. The amount of the deduction is limited to the amount that relates to the business. See Business deductions for more information and links to relevant policy.

If a person also operates a non-farm business, the permissible deductions from business income specified in section 1075 of the Social Security Act also apply. The non-farm business income will be recorded as a separate figure in the BIR process.
Non-farm businesses that are not necessarily related cannot be offset against each other. Unrelated fields of activity must be treated as separate sources of income.

The customer's tax returns may also reveal personal income which has not been advised and which needs to be assessed and recorded for the relevant period. See Assessing income for Farm Household Allowance (FHA).

5

Has the Off-Farm Income Offset (OFIO) been applied on the customer's record during the financial year? + Read more ...

Assess eligibility for the off-farm income offset based on actual business income for both customer and partner (if applicable).

Note: the amount of the allowable deduction may have changed if the actual business income is different to the business income estimate.

During the BIR process, the allowable deduction already recorded will be reviewed to ensure the correct amount of OFIO is used. See Off-farm income offset.

The system will reassess the customer's rate of FHA for the period the offset was applied, based on the actual allowable deduction.

Depending on the difference between the estimated business income and the actual business income, a debt shell or an arrears payment may be generated.

Note; for the 2019-20 financial year the off-farm income offset applies up to and including the 15 December 2019. From the 16 December 2019, the farm business loss assessment applies.

6

Checking for outstanding FHA rate affecting activities + Read more ...

Undertake an investigation of both the customer and partner records (if applicable) to determine if any rate affecting circumstances requires correction. Take any corrective action before continuing with the reconciliation process.

For example:

  • outstanding activities on the Activity List (AL) screen
  • identify and process any other rate affecting activities that may impact the relevant financial year
  • approve any outstanding Social Service Plans and check the outcome
  • action all Change of Circumstance (CoC) Activities outstanding. See Change of circumstance for FHA

Note: where corrective action is completed causing an overpayment the debt tag will generate overnight. The debt must be investigated before further action.

7

Check for undetermined debt shells + Read more ...

If a debt shell has been generated for any period in the financial year being reconciled, action this before further reconciliation processing can occur.

Note: do not complete BIR on the same day when a debt is raised for the same financial year you are completing BIR.

If a non-lodger debt (NLG) has been resolved, do not action the BIR request until the NLG debt has offset and shows as Finalised No Debt (FND).

See:

  • Investigating and assessing FHA debts
  • Actioning an undetermined debt on the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS)

1

Nil rate reconciliation debt + Read more ...

Customers with a Nil rate pre-commencement debt will have the choice to 'trade' available clock days to reduce or waive their debt. Some customers may choose to continue to receive FHA rather than reduce their debt by trading days.

This applies if the debt has been repaid in full or partially repaid.

Customers can choose to have their:

  • BIR debt fully waived (if they have enough available days to trade)
  • BIR debt partially waived (by trading some of their available days), or
  • No debt waiver applied and will retain the days available on their clock

The trade calculation is a standard $45 per day regardless of rate of payment received. This means for every clock day a customer chooses to trade in against their existing RCN debt, the debt balance will reduce by $45. For example, $900 debt divided by $45 equals 20 clock days.

If by trading clock days, the customer reduces the original debt amount and they have fully or partially repaid the debt, any over recovered debt amounts will be refunded to the customer.

If the customer chooses not to trade clock days or does not have enough clock days available to waive the debt, they must pay any outstanding balance.
See Recovery of debts pending a reassessment, explanation or review of decision.

The system will determine cancellation reason CAN O4Y (already paid 4 years of FHA) if an FHA CUR customer depletes all available clock days to 'trade in' to reduce their BIR debt.
