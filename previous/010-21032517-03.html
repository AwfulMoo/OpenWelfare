2

Customer contact + Read more ...

If a customer contacts about their TAP review advise them to submit their review online, unless:

  • self-service channels are currently unavailable, confirmed by a Network News Update (NNU) bulletin (see the Resources page for a link), or
  • the Service Officer determines the online transaction is not appropriate to be self-managed by the customer in that instance

Customers can access the TAP review via their:

  • Online services > Tasks > Academic reassessment task for TAP online review
  • Express Plus App > view outstanding Task

If the customer:

  • has completed the review, go to Step 3
  • is unable to complete the review online, go to Step 4

3

Customer contacts after completing review + Read more ...

Where a customer contacts after completing a TAP online review. Check the completed review:

  • open Process Direct
  • key the student’s CRN in Inbox
  • check the status of the TAP – Online review
  • where the status is draft, advise the student that the review is incomplete. They need to complete the review online. Do not select the review
  • where the review status is In Process, On Hold or For Manual Action, select the review and check the Tasks icon. If there is:
    • outstanding tasks, advise the customer the tasks required to finalise the review
    • no outstanding tasks, advise the customer the review is being processed and they will be told of the outcome when completed
  • advise the customer they may be contacted if more information is required

If unable to resolve a customer’s TAP review enquiry, see Customer requires call back table in Tertiary Access Payment (TAP).

4

Staff assisted review + Read more ...

If the customer is unable to submit the review via an Online Service Officers can complete the review verbally:

  • In Process Direct:
  • Select Customer Summary
  • CRN: field, key in the customer's CRN
  • Select Go
  • Select the relevant customer from the search results
  • Select the Transactions icon and All Transactions
  • Select the transaction ID hyperlink for the TAP – Online review
  • Go to the Status icon and change the status of the review to For Manual Action

Note: Service Officers must not apply this status update to any other type of activity, for example new claims etc.

Once the TAP review is initiated, ask the customer if:

  • they are undertaking the same course that was originally claimed for
  • they are studying full-time
  • they are not undertaking full-time studies due to:
    • a medical condition, or
    • an approved concessional study load
    • a temporary medical incapacity
  • they have relocated from the family home (if they are subject to Parental Income Test)
  • their term or home address has changed since they claimed the TAP

Record the answers to the above questions in Notes.

If the customer has indicated:

  • a temporary incapacity, they will need to provide a Centrelink Medical certificate
  • a decrease in study load due to the education provider requirements, they will need to provide a letter from the education provider to support approval of concessional study load

In all TAP reviews the customer must provide proof of enrolment, identifying their Education Provider and verifying their name and study load dated on or after the relevant assessment date.

For:

  • non TAP trained staff, procedure ends here.
  • TAP trained staff, go to Table 2 Step 4

Processing a Tertiary Access Payment (TAP) online review for TAP Processing skilled staff only

Table 2

Step

Action

1

TAP customer is an ABSTUDY customer + Read more ...

Only ABSTUDY skilled Service Officers complete TAP reviews for ABSTUDY customers.

Is ABSTUDY current, recently cancelled or is there an ABSTUDY claim pending outcome?

  • Yes, and Service Officer is:
    • YA/TAP skilled, go to Step 2
    • ABY/TAP skilled, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Service Officer not ABSTUDY skilled + Read more ...

Where a TAP review for an ABSTUDY customer is allocated to a Service Officer who is not ABSTUDY skilled:

  • select Keyword
  • select Add
  • in search: field, enter ABYEEED - ABSTUDY potential backdating
  • select the  ABYEEED  keyword from the search results list
  • select Status
  • select Edit
  • select Status:  On Hold
  • in On Hold Reason:  select Refer to CALO
  • in On Hold Expiry Date:  enter today’s date +1
  • select Save

Workload Manager will allocate the review with the keyword  ABYEED  to dual ABSTUDY and TAP skilled Service Officer for assessment.

Procedure ends here.

3

Review lodgement + Read more ...

If the customer:

  • has completed the review and
    • provided all the requested documents, go to Step 5
    • not provided the requested documents, go to Step 4
  • has failed to respond to a Request For Information (RFI), go to Step 4

4

Document lodgement + Read more ...

If the customer has not provided requested documents, and the review was submitted:

  • less than 28 days before the relevant Assessment date on TPCD in the past, place the activity on Hold via the Status icon:
    • On Hold Reason: field, Customer to Provide Information
    • On Hold Expiry Date: field, 29 days from the relevant Assessment date
  • 28 days or more past the relevant Assessment date on TPCD, code the relevant Course verification field on the TAP Circumstance Details (TPCD) screen as 'N'o. Go to Step 17

5

Pre-assessment checks + Read more ...

Complete the pre-assessment checks using the icons on the Circumstance Data screen including:

  • the TAP Review Overview, to view a summary of information provided by the customer
  • Documents for any scanned documents provided by the customer
  • Tasks to view Required Tasks and supporting documents
  • Document List (DL) screen using Super Key
  • Notes to view comments made by a previous Service Officer
  • Activity List (AL) using Super Key to view outstanding activities

Note: where the customer has submitted the TAP review verbally, the information must be recorded in Notes and manually coded on the relevant screens during assessment.

Are further documents required to make an assessment?

  • Yes, and the documents were
    • not requested as part of the review go to Step 6
    • requested as part of the review Course verification field on the TAP Circumstance Details (TPCD) screen as 'N'o. Go to Step 17
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Requesting Documents + Read more ...

To request additional documents:

  • select the More Choices menu
  • select Request Documents:
    • Benefit: field, Tertiary Access Payment
    • Benefit Status: field, Continuous
    • Authority To Request: field, Tertiary Access Payment Guidelines for VET and NUHELP Applicants
    • Information to be supplied within: field, 14 days
    • select the relevant form or document required for assessment
    • where proof of enrolment is required select Other and add text ‘Confirmation of your enrolment identifying your Education Provider dated on or after (insert either first or second assessment date, for example 11 April 2022) including your current study load and name ’ to the customer task
    • select  Finish

When issuing RFIs for TAP, the Authority To Request  field will default to ‘Tertiary Access Payment Guidelines for VET and NUHEP Applicants’. The letter will display the correct policy reference ‘Tertiary Access Payment Guidelines’`

Until the task becomes due or documents are supplied, procedure ends here. .

7

Assess and code the review + Read more ...

Assess and code the TAP review:

  • select the following from the Task Selector in the SAP Warnings and Errors (SWE) screen to go through the screens that require updating:
    • Regional Location Details (RLD)
    • Education Course Details (EDC)
    • TAP circumstance data (TPCD)

Note: if provisional data has uploaded from the online review, the relevant Task Selectors will automatically display a flag.

For:

  • first assessment date review, go to Step 8
  • second assessment date review, go to Step 9

8

Regional Location Details (RLD) screen + Read more ...

If the customer’s family home address has changed since granting the TAP claim to pending (PND) status but before their course start date, add the new address on the RLD screen:

  • Event Date: field, record the date the family home address changed
  • Address Reason: field, TAP
  • Address Date: field, for a:
    • Dependent customer course start date
    • Parental income exempt customer 6 months prior to the commencement of their relevant course of study
  • Address:  field for a:
    • Dependent customer, parents home address
    • Parental income exempt customer the customer’ address 6 months before they started their current approved course
  • Address Country Code: field, AU

Where the TAP family home  is overseas the full address is not required . Enter Overseas into the Country: field.

Address Latitude and Address Longitude fields can be triggered by selecting on the latitude box. This generates a response in the Regional Classification field.

Update the campus if the customer has changed education providers or campus.

Go to Step 9.

9

Education Course Details (EDC) screen + Read more ...

A customer may be eligible for TAP if they meet all the eligibility criteria and they:

  • change their TAP approved course at any time throughout the year, or
  • ceases studies after their first assessment date

Has the customer said they have a change in course or study load?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 12

10

Eligibility when students changes course or ceases studies + Read more ...

If the student changes from one TAP approved course to another TAP approved course:

  • for TAP/CUR (second assessment date), go to Step 11
  • for TAP/PND (first assessment date):
    • leave original course coded, and
    • assess TAP online review under the original course. Document the reason for the decision. Record the new course after review is finalised. Go to Step 12

Student ceases or defer studies

When the course end date is after first assessment date, assess the TAP online review. Keep the current course dates coded. Document the reason for the decision. Go to Step 12

When the course end date is in the past, if:

  • EDC is not updated:
    • keep the current course dates coded
    • go to Step 12
  • if EDC is updated:
    • re-code the student end date as the original date within the TAP online review
    • go to Step 12

Note: where a TAP CUR customer changes their course, TPCD will continue to display the name of the course for the first TAP instalment.

11

Customer advises new study details or change in study load + Read more ...

The customer must be undertaking an approved course and full-time study load to remain eligible for TAP. See Assessing and finalising claims for the Tertiary Access Payment (TAP).

Students not undertaking a full time study load may still be eligible for the TAP if they are:

  • undertaking an approved concessional study load of at least 66%, in line with the YA concessional rules. See Assessing concessional study loads for Youth Allowance (YA) or Assessing the two-thirds study load concession for ABSTUDY, or
  • unable to study due to a medical condition and meet the incapacity rules in line with YA. See Youth Allowance (YA) student or Australian Apprentice becomes incapacitated

If the customer becomes incapacitated, see Youth Allowance (YA) student or Australian Apprentice becomes incapacitated.

12

TAP Circumstance Details (TPCD) screen + Read more ...

Update the Course Verification field on the TPCD screen:

  • 'Y'es, where the student:
    • has provided Proof of Enrolment dated on or after their course verification dates, and
    • is studying in an approved course on their first or second assessment date, and
    • studying full time, or an approved concessional study load or approved temporary incapacity
  • 'N'o, where the student has:
    • changed to an unapproved course, or
    • reduced their study load to below 75% (without an acceptable), or
    • deferred or ceased studies before their first or second assessment date

Note: record first assessment date review outcome in the Course Verification 1 field, and the second assessment date review outcome in the Course Verification 2 field.

For updates to the TPCD screen, select the customers course being assessed for TAP from the drop down selection box in the Course Name field, as this displays as blank.

If the customer’s circumstances have changed update the Course approval: field, as appropriate.

If assessing the first assessment date review, make sure the second assessment date remains pre-populated with P – Pending.

For TAP CUR customers, only the Course approval and Course Verification 2 fields can be updated.

Is the review for the 1st assessment date?

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No, go to Step 14

13

Relocation – TPCD screen + Read more ...

If a change of address has been supplied, update the address.

Determine if the customer has relocated to be closer to their place of study. Check if the customer has either:

  • a term address
  • a home address that differs from the Family home address

Does the customer have a current term address recorded or a new home address that differs from the Family home address recorded on the RLD screen?

  • Yes, check the Relocation Indicator and Excess Travelling Time are coded as 'Y'es, then go to Step 14
  • No, check:
    • documents icon for new address details
    • transactions icon for a CoC address activity
    • notes icon for documented change of address
    • go to Step 14

14

Outcome of review or update to study details MFU + Read more ...

If the customer:

  • is eligible and the payment is cancelled go to Step 15
  • is eligible and payment is Pending (PND) or Current (CUR) go to Step 15is ineligible go to Step 17

15

Restore payments + Read more ...

If the customer has been cancelled for a restorable reason, restore the customer's record

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen:
  • Benefit Type Code: field, TAP - Tertiary Access Payment
  • Benefit Action: field, RES - Restore
  • Event date: field, the date the payment was cancelled

Go to Step 16.

16

Eligible for TAP + Read more ...

Assess and Finalise the review:

  • Correct any errors identified in the Message Log on the SWE screen
  • Select Assess to go to the Entitlements (ELD) screen
  • Make sure the outcome is correct on the ELD screen, including the payment amount:
    • first instalment of $3,000
    • second instalment of $2,000 (outer regional, remote and very remote customers only)
  • ELD screen will present as:
    • TAP CUR (Current), and
    • TAP OOP (One off payment)
  • Select Finish
  • Record relevant details in the Review Finalisation DOC. If the student changes, ceases or defers studies after the first or second assessment date:
    • document the student has changed, ceased or deferred studies after first or second assessment date reason in the Finalise text box
  • Select Finalise to complete the review
  • Select OK. If the student changes, ceases or defers studies after the first or second assessment date:
    • update the Education Course Details (EDC) screen with the new course end date. See Coding new study details
  • Ensure any relevant documents are marked as Completed

Procedure ends here.

17

Not eligible for TAP + Read more ...

The customer is not eligible for TAP.

The payment should automatically reject or cancel when all necessary updates are coded on the relevant screens.

If the system does not automatically cancel or reject, manually update the Benefit Action (BA) screen:

  • Select Add button to create a new entry and update:
    • Benefit Type Code:  field, TAP
    • Benefit Action:  field, if the customer is not eligible to TAP from the first assessment date review select REJ-rejection. If customer is ineligible for TAP from the second assessment date review, select  CAN - cancel
    • Reason: field, select the appropriate reason from the drop down menu. See the Rejection and Cancellation reason codes for TAP table in Assessing and finalising claims for the Tertiary Access Payment (TAP)
    • Event Date: field, will auto-default to the TAP assessment review date
    • select Save
  • Correct any errors identified in the Message Log on the SWE screen
  • Select Assess to go to the Entitlement (ELD) screen
  • Check the outcome is correct on the ELD screen
  • Select Finish
  • Record details in the Review Finalisation DOC  
  • Select Finalise to complete the review

Go to Step 18.

18

Education course details (EDC) screen updates after TAP review completion + Read more ...

This process only applies to customer who:

  • changed courses to an unapproved course,
  • reduced their study load to below 75% (without an acceptable reason for a concessional study load, or verified temporary incapacity), or
  • never commenced the course

The EDC screen will require corrective action in Customer First for confirmed data after completion of the TAP review for the above scenarios.

Check the customer’s record to see if they are in receipt of another student payment. Where applicable, take corrective action appropriately, see:

  • Payment of Youth Allowance (YA) and Austudy when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course
  • Customer will not commence full-time study for Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy and Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
  • Changing study load from full-time to part-time or concessional study load for Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy or PES
  • Payment of ABSTUDY when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course
  • Changing or correcting course of study and/or educational institution for ABSTUDY students

Procedure ends here.

Processing a TAP Manual Follow up (MFU)

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Access and code the MFU + Read more ...

The system will create the MFU TAP reviews -TAP Study update, if a change to the EDC screen is made on a student’s record with a status of TAP/CUR, or TAP/PND.

To access and code in Process Direct:

  • From the Task Selector in the SAP Warnings and Error (SWE) screen, select the following screens:
    • EDC
    • TPCD

2

Education Course Details (EDC) and TAP Circumstance Details (TPCD) + Read more ...

Check the latest update on the EDC screen, this should be the latest course details provided by the student.

Is the latest course entry on the TPCD screen the same as the latest entry on EDC?

  • Yes, and the latest course is:
    • a TAP approved course, go to Step 5
    • not a TAP approved course, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

TPCD - Course Name + Read more ...

Update the TPCD screen:

  • Course Name: this field will display as blank. Update the course name by selecting the most recent course on the  EDC  screen from the drop down in the Course Name: field
  • Assessment Date: will update based on the course selected

Where a TAP CUR customer changes their course, TPCD will continue to display the name of the course for the first TAP instalment.

4

TPCD - Course approval + Read more ...

Update the course approval field as appropriate:

Course approval:

  • Yes, select Y
  • No, select N

5

Course Verification + Read more ...

Where the customer has changed their course of study, or study dates and the verification date is in the past, the system will not automatically issue a TAP review.

If the changed Course Verification dates are:

  • in the future, leave the Course Verification fields as Pending. Go to Step 6
  • in the past, and:
    • verification documents are provided, update the Course Verification field(s) as appropriate. Go to Step 6
    • verification documents were requested and not provided, update the Course verification field to N - No
    • verification documents have not been requested, Services Officers must not complete the MFU and issue a request for the customer to provide verification documents. See Step 4 in Table 1 to identify which documents are required.

6

Finalise MFU + Read more ...

Go to the SWE screen. Message Log displays any errors with existing coding. Correct errors before assessing the MFU.

Select Assess to go to the Entitlement (ELD) screen. On the ELD screen, select Next. Check the outcome is correct.

Select Finish.

Record details in the Finalisation DOC.

If there were no circumstances changed within the activity, change the status of the MFU to cancelled.
