Adjusting debts that are fully or partly recovered

If a decision has been made to adjust a debt:

  • the Debt Amount (OPDA) screen allows a change to the debt amount for debts with a Finalised - Recovered (FRE) status. There is no need to contact Compliance and Debt Operations(CDO). If the amount is:
    • reduced, the debt status will change to over-recovered. The customer will be entitled to a refund
    • increased, this will return the debt to a determined status
  • and the customer is entitled to a refund, see Item 3 in Table 6

If there is no entitlement to a refund then no further action is required. For example, the debt amount has changed however not fully recovered and a payment arrangement is in place.

Advance payments and debts

Advance payments are repaid from a customer's ongoing payments. They do not become a debt unless:

  • the customer's entitlement ceases before the advance has been fully repaid, or
  • the level of advance repayment is insufficient to repay the advance in a reasonable time

It will become a debt where:

  • the customer's FTB reduces substantially
  • the customer's rate does not change for 2 consecutive fortnights, and
  • the repayment of the advance will take an unreasonable amount of time. Equal to or more than 2 years, or 731 days

To qualify for an advance payment, a customer must not owe money to the Commonwealth. This includes:

  • money that is being recovered or may be recovered by withholdings from their Centrelink payments, and
  • some debts that have been temporarily written off

The Write-off (OPWO) screen will have the reason for the write.

Temporary written off debts

  • For Social Security advances, if the customer has a debt temporarily written off they are not entitled to an advance payment. This includes those with non-lodger debts temporarily written off under the separated couples rule
  • For FTB advances, if the customer has a debt temporarily written off they will not be entitled to an advance. The exception to this is where the debt is temporarily written off due to:
    • an ex-partner non-lodger, or
    • bankruptcy

Permanently written-off debt

A debt permanently written off is no longer recoverable through deductions. The customer is entitled to an advance if they meet other qualifications.

The Debt Programme is unable to provide any advice about qualification for an advance.

Customers can make a repayment to finalise the debt to be eligible for an advance.

Issuing an advance payment when the customer owes a balance of less than 5 cents

This may cause automatic rejection of any advance application.

Override the rejection in the workflow if the balance is less than 5 cents:

  • The advance can be paid if other conditions are met. The override option does not apply to advances of:
    • FTB
    • Special Employment Advance (SEA), or
    • Pharmaceutical Advance (PhA)
  • The debt will be finalised when the outstanding rounded amount is withheld on the customer's next payday

Compensation payer debts

The insurer pays compensation payer debts. They do not disqualify a customer from an advance payment. The Advance Payment cluster cannot distinguish insurer debts from customer debts. It will reject advance payment applications if insurer debts are outstanding.

The Advance Payments workflow has an option to override rejections caused by insurer debts.

Note: there is no override option for FTB, SEA or PhA Advances. These cannot be granted if there is an outstanding debt.

Repayment for an advance

For information about accepting a cash repayment for an advance, see Lump sum repayment of advance payment.

A pension customer can receive multiple advance payments. This may result in several debts if the customer ceases to be entitled to any payments.

For more information, see:

  • Advances, or
  • Hardship Assistance Service Level 2 Helpdesk. See the Resources page.

Bankruptcy - customer advising of bankruptcy

Bankruptcy may affect recovery of Centrelink debts. Ask the customer for a convenient daytime contact phone number and update the Telephone Details (TD) screen if necessary.

If the customer is present at a service centre, call Debt Recovery. Allow the customer to speak to a Debt Recovery staff member.

If the customer is on the phone, transfer the call to Debt Recovery.

  • If the Debt Recovery line is not available, obtain the:
    • customer's bankruptcy number
    • date of the bankruptcy, and
    • the name of the Trustee/Administrator
  • Prepare an ACTDOC using the keywords 'BRT' and 'ACTDRU'
  • Transfer the DOC to region code NDR

See the Resources page for links to the Debt Recovery contact details.

Note: the Bankruptcy Team (Collection Administrations Team) are solely responsible for Bankruptcy processing.

Broken recovery arrangements

If the customer does not pay the amount by the agreed date plus 7 days:

  • a Payment Not Received letter issues
  • the letter asks the customer to pay the amount within 7 days

If the customer does not pay the outstanding amount within 14 days from the due date:

  • the arrangement status will change from current (CUR) to broken (BKN)
  • a DMN/RCV Broken Arrangement activity will generate
  • the activity will assign via Customer First Workload Manager (WLM)

For how to action a broken arrangement, see:

  • Actioning broken regular/irregular cash arrangements
  • Actioning broken voluntary deduction (direct debit) arrangements
  • Actioning non-compliant and broken garnishee recovery arrangements

Claims - direct debit/BPAY® claims

Treat refunds of BPAY® payments with caution. Services Australia and the customer's financial institution have the ability to reverse BPAY payments. If this occurs, the customer may:

  • receive 2 refunds, and
  • incur another debt for the reversal amount

If a customer asks to have a BPAY® payment refunded to them, they must lodge a claim with their financial institution to have the money returned.

For all other BPAY® enquiries, transfer the customer to Debt Recovery.

Debt repayment hierarchy

The debt repayment hierarchy determines the priority for the allocation of debt repayments.

The repayment hierarchy is bypassed for payments made directly to:

  • a specific debt through internal manual repayment processing
  • compensation
  • Paid Parental Leave
  • collection agent repayment processing

Payments for a specific debt are applied based on the Debt ID used when the customer makes the payment.

The order repayments are applied is stored in and automatically controlled by Debt Management and Information System (DMIS). This determines the order in which debts are repaid when a customer has more than 1 outstanding debt.

Repayments are currently applied in the following category order:

Family assistance (including Child Care Subsidy) reconciliation debts where the due date is in the past are collected first.

They are followed by all other debts, excluding:

  • Public Governance, Performance and Accountability (PGPA) Act 2013 debts
  • Debts temporarily written off (voluntary and automatic withholdings payments against these debts may be accepted and some write-off reasons will automatically end)
  • Family assistance debts held for the reconciliation process
  • Debts raised by other government departments
  • Debts raised internationally (for example, New Zealand Government debts)
  • Family assistance reconciliation debts where the due date is in the future
  • Debts that have been previously finalised (creating an over-recovered debt), in the following priority order:
    • Fully recovered
    • Waived
    • Finalised as not a debt
    • Transferred to Department of Veterans' Affairs for recovery
    • Permanently written-off
    • Included in the family assistance reconciliation process
  • Potential debts requiring assessment
  • Compensation insurer debts

Debts finalised as no-debt, waived or deleted on the same day as the debt was raised, are excluded from the recovery hierarchy. Payments are not credited to those debts.

Deductions from payment hierarchy

This hierarchy controls the order in which deductions happen from a customer's payment.

  • Payment penalty
  • Immediate payment recovery
  • Advance recovery
  • Withholdings for debt recovery
  • Financial Supplement Loan
  • Child support liability or child support overpayment
  • Tax
  • Income Management
  • Rent Deduction Scheme
  • Centrepay

Note: where two debts in the same category exist, withholdings will apply to the debt with the earliest raise date.

Departure Prohibition Orders (DPO) and Departure Authorisation Certificates (DAC)

If an active DPO is in place, there is an indicator DPO Status - Active in Customer First:

  • on the Customer Summary page (click on the CRN/BP link), and
  • key 'OPDL' in Next to go to the Debt List screen

A DPO alert will also display on the Money You Owe section of:

  • myGov, and
  • Centrelink Express Plus app

A person enquiring about a DPO or a DAC should call the DPO team on the number provided in the notices sent to them.

Warm transfer the call to the Departure Prohibition Order (DPO) Team if it is about:

  • the customer's current DPO or DAC
  • the recovery of the customer's debt, or
  • the customer does not agree with a DPO or a DAC decision

If the DPO team is unavailable:

  • complete the DPO Fast Note template, and
  • advise the person they will receive a call back within 24 hours

Note: do not record an application for a formal review. The process for these decisions is significantly different compared to other decisions. An application for a formal review of any other debt decision may still be actioned normally.

4

External Collection Agents (ECA) - debt needs to be varied

Where the status of a debt is DMC on the Debt List (OPDL) screen, staff can vary the debt amount without a debt recall. An exception to this is debts with reason codes FRR, FRC, FRA or NLD.

The calculated debt amount can be adjusted using the Debt Amount (OPDA) screen.

Debts that are:

  • no longer recoverable, are recalled from the ECA with debt status RND (recall no debt)
  • fully recovered, are recalled from the ECA with debt status FIN (debt finalised)
  • varied above $0, the new debt amount will be advised to the collection agent

Internal reviews and pausing recovery during the process

Customers can ask for a pause on recovery when they request an explanation or apply for a formal review of the debt decision. If the person has more than 1 debt, the pause only applies to the debt/s being explained or reviewed. This applies when the customer asks for:

  • an explanation by a Subject Matter Expert (SME), or
  • a formal review by an Authorised Review Officer (ARO)

For more information see Recovering debts pending review of decisions.

This pause extends to a debt decision under external review by the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) (first review). If a debt decision has already had an AAT first review, recovery cannot be paused unless the agency is issued with a Stay Order by the AAT second review. For more information see Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process.

13

Multiple records

A customer may have more than one Customer Reference Number (CRN). A debt may exist on another record for the same customer. Investigate the possibility of multiple CRNs:

  • The MML screen in Customer First uses customer information, for example name, date of birth, address to identify probable multiple customer records
  • Key 'MML' in the Next field. Probable matches appear in order from 'excellent' to 'possible'
  • For further information, see Centrelink Multiple Match List (MML) screen

If multiple records for the customer appear, refer the CRNs to Data Quality Unit (DQU) prior to any corrective action occurring.

Create a Fast Note, select Auto Text > Data Quality > Updates > Multiple CRN Referral (either child or customer):

  • select Confirm
  • update details of the multiple CRNs in the text box and include other relevant information
  • select Continue and mark as 'Urgent' to ensure the file is actioned within 24 hours

The Fast Note will complete and transfer a DOC to the DQU (office code NA8).

DQU will:

  • identify the primary record
  • link the records, and
  • record which record to use on a DOC

See Types of Centrelink records

If a debt on the multiple record is not finalised then DQU will ACTDOC the appropriate Debt Raising Team to have the debt re-raised on the primary record.

After the Debt Raising Team has actioned the record, send an ACTDOC to the Debt Recovery Team (NS7) requesting further recovery action on the record, including the transfer of any payments already recovered on the subject debt to the primary record.

14

No arrangement activity

A no arrangement activity will generate when the debt balance is over $1,500.00 or the customer is deceased, and:

  • the debt is due and payable with no arrangement put in place
  • the temporary write off period expires
  • the debt is returned from an ECA with no other arrangement put in place
  • the current arrangement is cancelled with no new arrangement/write off in place
  • a waiver is reversed on a fully repaid/waived debt
  • a fully repaid debt is amended to have a higher balance
  • any write off is manually ended with nothing else coded in its place
  • a broken (BKN) arrangement is ceased

All no arrangement activities are actioned the same way.

See Outbound customer contact and gathering information when recovering debts.

  • Paid Parental Leave (PPL) Employer debts, refer to the PPL Employer Team
  • All other types of organisations including for ABSTUDY and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme, refer the caller to Compliance and Debt Operations

Prison

Imprisonment

In most cases, recovery of debts are deferred while a customer is imprisoned. The exception is when a customer can continue to pay their debt.

A deferred debt appears on the Debt List (OPDL) with a status of D - Pend Rcvr Actn

Services Australia data matches with state and territory corrections services to determine imprisonment and release. If a customer is matched, recovery of their debt/s will be temporarily written off or restarted automatically. For current customers, a compliance review is generated.

Action is needed when an incarcerated or recently released customer who has not been data matched correctly is identified:

  • If the debt has not been written off, and should be:
    • send an ACTDOC using the keywords 'ACTDRU' and 'PRISON' to Compliance and Debt Operations. See Item 7 in Table 6
    • The ACTDOC must request a temporary write off of the debt
    • Include details of the customer's incarceration, and release date if known
    • Prison Liaison Officers (PLO) and authorised Compliance Officers have the delegation to temporarily write off debts via the Debt Action script
  • If the debt is referred to an External Collection Agent (ECA), PLO's can recall the debt using the Collection Agent (OPMC) screen before actioning the temporary write off

In most cases, imprisoned customers are not eligible for payments. See Prison admission process and claims during incarceration

Prison release

In most cases, upon release, customers are eligible for a further 28 day deferral of recovery. PLOs can use the Debt Action script to apply a temporary write-off using the code STH. This allows the customer to receive their full entitlement for 2 pay periods.

PLO's will advise the customer there are a number of options available to repay their debt. They can negotiate a preferred payment arrangement option. If a PLO is not available prior to release, the customer can access the Money You Owe option online or call Compliance and Debt Operations to organise repayment.

If a customer has had interest charged while imprisoned, a Debt Recovery officer can apply an exemption from interest charge for the period of imprisonment. This may not be available in all cases. See Viewing and updating Interest Charge (IC) via Financial Customer Care in Customer First for how to apply an exemption.

Tax garnishee - customer contact regarding garnishee of their tax return

Customers are known as a client of interest (COI) where they:

  • no longer receive a payment
  • have a debt, and
  • do not have an arrangement

Data matching with the Australian Tax Office (ATO) Taxpayer Records system identifies COIs.

When a COI lodges their tax return, the ATO may need to send some or all of their tax refund to repay a debt owed. This action is the 'tax garnishee process’.

Before referring to Compliance and Debt Operations, determine if:

  • the tax refund was 'garnisheed' as part of the tax garnishee process, or
  • ‘off-set' as part of the family assistance payment reconciliation process

Direct all enquiries about family assistance reconciliation off-sets to Families. See Debt offsetting during family assistance reassessment, reconciliation and claim processing.

To check if the tax refund was 'garnisheed':

  • go to Debts Menu Heading in Customer First and select to expand menu items
  • select ATO Garnishee Summary screen
  • select Transaction Type Request Garnishee Amount to go to the Garnishee Transaction Details screen
  • garnishee amount (if any) will display under Requested Amount heading, or
  • go to the History Summary (HS) screen and check a Tax Garnishee Notice (GNOTDMNTGR) letter has been sent

If a customer expresses hardship at having their tax refund garnisheed, refer the customer to Compliance and Debt Operations.

10

Waiver of debt

Waiver or part waiver of a debt means the right to recover the amount waived is relinquished. No recovery action is possible on the waived amount unless there is sufficient reason to believe the waiver was incorrect. Refer these cases to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk for further investigation and advice.

A debt which has been:

  • Fully waived, will display as 'F-Waived' in the Status field on the Debt List (OPDL) screen. No further recovery action is possible
  • Partially waived, will display the partial waiver amount on the Waiver (OPWA) screen. Normal recovery action continues

See Waiving Centrelink debts.

11

Waiver of debts when repayments have been made

The action required depends if the debt has been fully recovered or partly recovered.

Debt is fully recovered

A fully recovered debt requires adjustment by Compliance and Debt Operations before waiver is completed. Send an ACTDOC to Compliance and Debt Operations - see Item 7 in Table 6

A Debt Recovery officer will:

  • adjust the debt by $0.01c on the Repayment History (OPRH) screen, and
  • refer the ACTDOC back to the requesting area to complete the waiver

This will allow the staff member to amend the debt on the Add Waiver (OPWAA) screen. Reverse the $0.01c adjustment before finalising the debt.

Debt is partly recovered

If the decision is to waive a partially recovered debt, code the waiver. There is no need to contact Compliance and Debt Operations.

If repayments were made towards the debt being waived the customer may be entitled to a refund if there are no other debts. See Item 16 in Table 4.

12

Withholdings - customer wishes to change withholding amount

Accept the customer's offer if it meets or exceeds the acceptable withholding level. Where the offer is not acceptable, Compliance and Debt Operations will use the Financial Information Calculator to assess the customer's full financial situation and determine an appropriate repayment amount.

Note: manually ending withholdings will not prevent withholdings from reapplying automatically. The following decisions can only be made by Compliance and Debt Operations staff:

  • temporary suspension of withholding, or
  • deferring recovery (non recovery) of a debt

The Debt repayment guided procedure or Debt recovery script must be used when actioning a debt.

13

Withholdings - determining when non-standard withholdings will start

Non-standard withholdings will start from the later of:

  • the next payment, or
  • if the customer's debt is not yet due, the first payment after the due date

By Law, Services Australia cannot apply automatic withholdings before the debt is due and payable.

To determine if the new arrangement will apply to the next payment, 'S'elect the next payment from the Payment Summary (PS) screen.

If 'THIS IS THE NEXT PAYMENT AND CANNOT BE ALTERED' displays on the Payment Delivery (PD) screen, the new withholding arrangement will take effect from the following payment.

Refer the customer to Compliance and Debt Operations.
