Answering calls in Centrelink 111-05010000

This document outlines procedures for staff during initial phone contact with customers and the public.

Greetings and authentication

When answering a call a Service Officer can use a best practice greeting phrase or an appropriate greeting of their own, for example:

  • 'Hello, this is [Service Officer’s name], can I start with your name please?'
  • ‘Hi, my name is [Service Officer’s name], may I start with your Centrelink reference number please?’
  • ‘Hi, this is [Service Officer’s name], how can I assist you today?’
  • ‘Hello, my name is [Service Officer’s name], how can I help you today?’

The Service Officer can combine the greeting and the name confirmation request in the one sentence if they wish. It is also acceptable if the customer volunteers their name as part of their own greeting.

The Service officer must:

  • confirm the name of the caller before proceeding, regardless of the authentication level achieved on Services Australia Workspace. This is to ensure that Customer Overview is displaying the correct record
  • authenticate the customer correctly. See Authenticating a Centrelink customer

Confirming customer phone numbers

This may be completed in the IVR. The Update Phone Number instructions must be checked on Customer Overview to confirm the action required. This is a Business Imperative.

Customer Overview displays cards with a Green icon for successful interactions that need no further action:

  • Phone number confirmed
    The phone number the customer is calling from has been checked against the number on the customer record
  • Updated phone number
    The customer has successfully updated their phone number in the IVR

Customer Overview displays cards with an Amber or Red icon if Service Officer action is needed:

  • Phone number check required
    The IVR could not confirm the customer’s phone number. Phone details must be checked and updated if necessary
  • Please update Phone
    The IVR has received new telephony details from the customer. However, the update was not completed as the customer did not have the required level of authentication
  • Attempt to update phone number failed
    There was a technical error in the IVR. Phone details must be checked and updated if necessary
  • An alternate method
    All contact details must be updated from claim information in new claims and scanned images

Confirming family assistance income estimates

If the customer has Voice Print Authentication this may be completed in the IVR. Staff must check the income estimate instructions on Customer Overview to confirm if action is required. This is a Business Imperative.

Customer Overview displays cards with a green icon for successful interactions that need no further action:

  • Income estimate confirmed: the customer has confirmed the income estimate recorded and no further action is needed

Customer Overview displays cards with an amber or red icon if Service Officer action is needed:

  • Income estimate update required: the customer has indicated they would like to update their family assistance income estimate. The income estimate must be updated via normal processes
  • Income estimate update failed: there was a technical error in the IVR. The income estimate should be checked and updated via normal processes if required

Reviewing Display on Access (DOA) DOCs

Service Officers must read and comply with all DOA DOCs at every customer contact. It is possible to access DOA DOCs in Customer First and Process Direct.

These alerts display immediately when a customer record is accessed and help to provide a better customer service. This is a Business Imperative.

When accessing a customer record, staff must:

  • check the Document List (DL) screen for DOA DOCs
  • open and read each DOA DOC on the customer's record
  • follow the directions and information in each DOA DOC

Staff must read and adhere to DOA DOCs to avoid:

  • placing customers affected by family and domestic violence at risk of further harm
  • exposing customers and the agency to fraudulent activity
  • inadvertent disclosure of a customer’s personal information
  • staff being exposed to extreme customer aggression
  • staff being unaware of relevant critical information such as bereavement periods and Managed Service Plans

Checking the Customer Issues page

Service Officers must check the Customer Issues page in Customer First at every customer contact. Customer Overview will warn if there are issues on the Customer Issues page. This is a Business imperative.

The Customer Issues page is used to ensure any mandatory actions or handoffs are handled correctly.

Any payment related issues must be actioned if the Service Officer has the capability.

The Customer Issues page can be accessed in Customer First in the Quick Links (left panel), in the Other Links block on the Workspace screen, or at the top right corner of the Customer Summary (CRN/BP) screen.

Any actions outside the Service Officer's skills must be handed off to the relevant queue or business area, and the customer advised why their call is being transferred.

Promoting self service

Self service is the preferred way for customers to do business with the agency and for most customers is the quickest and easiest option. Self service is available through mobile apps, online accounts and phone self service.

Staff are expected to:

  • transition customers to self service at every opportunity, unless the customer is not eligible or the staff member deems it inappropriate
  • educate customers about self service options
  • encourage eligible customers to enrol a voiceprint
  • troubleshoot self service issues and, where possible, transition the customer back to self service to complete their business
  • escalate self service issues unable to be resolved through troubleshooting

This is a Business Imperative.

Applying first contact resolution principles and call quality standards

Service officers must apply first contact resolution principles:

  • assist the customer with their enquiry
  • offer a complete solution to a customer's enquiry
  • avoid call transfers unless necessary

Service officers must maintain call quality standards at all times.

Privacy

Privacy and confidentiality provisions administered by the agency must be followed at all times. This includes times when a Service Officer may accidentally access a record of a person they know.

If during the course of their duties, staff inadvertently access the information of a person in whom they have a personal interest, they must record the incident on the Inadvertent Access Register. This was previously known as the Oops register.

Call transfers from Child Support

Child Support will provide the necessary identifying details to locate the correct customer record when transferring the call. Note: if the customer has a password on their record, check this before proceeding with the enquiry.

The Resources page contains intranet links to privacy and secrecy and information release.
