Change of carer, temporary or permanent absence of care

Table 1: This table describes information for Service Officers on the Temporary Cessation of Care (TCC) provisions process for Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA) with respect to change of carer and temporary or permanent absence of care.

Step

Action

1

Change in care receiver/s during the current calendar year + Read more ...

Has there been a change in care receiver/s during the current calendar year?

  • Yes, and:
    • the carer is on the phone, go to Step 2
    • the carer has presented at a Service Centre, go to Step 3
    • the case has been referred to a specialist officer or processing centre, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

2

Change in care receivers for current calendar year + Read more ...

As the system may not allocate TCC (respite) days correctly, refer the case to a Service Support Officer (SSO). SSO go to Step 4.

3

Change in care receivers for current calendar year + Read more ...

As the system may not allocate TCC days correctly, a hand-off needs to occur to the Access Support Centre (ASC). The Resources page contains a link to Access Support Centre contact details. ASC go to Step 4.

4

Change in care receivers for current calendar year + Read more ...

Will the expected number of TCC days for all care receivers exceed 63 days in the current calendar year, and TCC days have been recorded for the previous care receiver(s)?

  • Yes, and the carer is entitled to 63 days TCC for the current care receiver. Note: due to system limitations, when a carer has a change of care receiver(s), the system will use the balance of TCC days coded for the previous care receiver(s) and not allocate a new 63 days balance for the new care receiver(s). This is not correct and is due to current system limitations. Contact the Carers Level 2 Policy Help Desk via the Online Query Form. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Identify the reason for the absence + Read more ...

If the care receiver is hospitalised, in respite, or in gaol or psychiatric confinement because they have been charged with an offence, go to Step 6.

If the carer is absent:

  • because they are in gaol or psychiatric confinement and they have been charged with an offence, this is not TCC. Run the Suspension and Cancellation workflow. Procedure ends here
  • because they are in hospital, go to Step 6
  • for a reason other than they are in gaol or psychiatric confinement because they have been charged with an offence, go to Step 6

6

Determine if cessation of care is temporary + Read more ...

Is the cessation of care temporary?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Cessation of care is permanent + Read more ...

When the cessation of care is permanent, cancel the CP and/or cancel the CA.

Note: care requirements do not apply to CP (XWP) but still apply to the carer's CA. If the carer is getting CP (XWP), their CP will be auto-cancelled when their only or last payment level CA is suspended or cancelled for any reason. A carer loses their qualification for CP (XWP) permanently if payment level CA is not reinstated without a gap in entitlement.

TCC provisions and coding

Table 2: This table describes information for Service Officers on the TCC provisions process for CP and/or CA with respect to hospitalisation provisions and coding of absence of care.

Step

Action

1

Check care receiver’s admission to hospital + Read more ...

Has the care receiver been/or is about to be, admitted into hospital?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 4

2

Hospitalisation provisions + Read more ...

Hospitalisation provisions do not apply if the carer is hospitalised. Respite provisions may apply.

Do hospitalisation provisions apply?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Code the hospitalisation care absence + Read more ...

Code all absences using the Temporary cessation of Care workflow tool.

If the tool is unavailable or not working, manually code the Absences Details (ABSN) screen with a one-off absence or a regular absence.

Note: absences are 24 hour periods, midnight to midnight.

Tell the carer they must advise Services Australia within 14 days of the care receiver returning home or leaving the hospital.

If coding the absence manually, go to Step 8.

4

Code the absence + Read more ...

Code all absences using the Temporary Cessation of Care workflow tool. Absences are 24 hour periods, midnight to midnight.

Note: if the absence from care was due to the carer going overseas without the care receiver, there is a current system limitation, which continues to count TCC after expiration of portability.

Where the carer's payments will suspend after 42 days the TCC end date should reflect only 41 days if care is provided on the day of departure and TCC will commence from the following day. Code respite with start date equal to day after departure and end date equal to the end of the limited portability period. Therefore the TCC period will be 41 days provided the carer has this number available. For guidance on the interaction between Portability and TCC and correct recording procedures, see Carer Allowance (CA) and Carer Payment (CP) interaction of portability and temporary cessation of care (respite) rules.

See the Resources page for an example.

If the tool is not available or not working, manually code the absence on the Absences Details (ABSN) screen. Code a one-off absence or a regular absence.

If none of the following scenarios apply, go to Step 8:

  • carer is imprisoned. This is considered a respite absence and the carer's qualification for payment may continue. Go to Step 5
  • CA (manual) child care receiver undertakes education, training or treatment and is absent from the care situation. The carer may remain current at 'nil rate' for up to 13 weeks, as long as the care receiver is expected to return to care within that period. For CA (auto), the carer loses eligibility for CA immediately when they lose eligibility for their associated CP (child)
  • care provider and care receiver are partnered, but are separated due to respite care. A respite care couple are entitled to be paid at the single rate of CP until the respite care ends. A respite care couple is where 1 partner has:
    • entered approved respite care, see Legislation in the References page, and
    • has remained, or is likely to remain, in that care for at least 14 consecutive days

5

Determine the length of absence due to the carer being in gaol or psychiatric confinement because they are charged with an offence + Read more ...

Determine the length of the absence (due to the carer being in gaol or psychiatric confinement because they are charged with an offence). If a carer is in periodic detention or weekend detention for a whole 24 hour period from midnight to midnight, they lose qualification for CP and/or CA as TCC cannot be used for this absence and therefore, daily care is not being provided. The 'length of the absence' in periodic or weekend detention cases is the period over which the detentions will occur (for example, 6 months), not the length of each individual weekly detention.

If the length of the absence is for:

  • 13 weeks or less, go to Step 6
  • more than 13 weeks, go to Step 7

6

Confinement is for 13 weeks or less + Read more ...

In the carer's record, select 'Carers' in the left hand menu and run the Carer Cancellation and Suspension workflow:

  • suspend using the Reason code 'Imprisonment' ('IMP'). This will produce a letter to the carer advising of the suspension. Note: the suspension should be taken from Date of Effect + 1 as the carer does not lose entitlement until he/she has spent the whole day (midnight to midnight) imprisoned
  • if the date of confinement is in the future, the payment is not able to be suspended using the Carer Cancellation and Suspension workflow. The Service Officer will need to code a manual review on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen that will fall due on the date the carer enters gaol. When the review falls due, the Carer Cancellation and Suspension workflow can then be used to suspend payment
  • defer any Medical Reviews or Circumstance Service Updates during the period of suspension
  • a review will be created on the FAL screen for 13 weeks from the date they were confined
  • restoration in this case is from the date they are released. The review is deleted. If the review matures and the carer is still confined, the payment should be cancelled from the date they were suspended from and a letter should be produced advising them of the cancellation

Go to Step 8.

7

Confinement is for more than 13 weeks + Read more ...

In the carer's record, select ‘Carers’ in the left hand menu and run the Carer Cancellation and Suspension workflow:

  • Cancel, using the Reason code 'Imprisonment' ('IMP'). This will produce a letter to the carer advising of the cancellation. Note: the cancellation should be from Date of Effect + 1 as the carer does not lose entitlement until he/she has spent the whole day (midnight to midnight) imprisoned. If a carer is in periodic detention or weekend detention for a whole 24 hour period from midnight to midnight, they lose qualification for CA and/or CP as respite cannot be used for this absence and therefore, daily care is not being provided
  • if the date of confinement is in the future, the payment is not able to be cancelled using the Carer Cancellation and Suspension workflow. Service Officers are to code a manual review on the FAL screen that will fall due on the date the carer enters gaol. When the review falls due, the Carer Cancellation and Suspension workflow can then be used to cancel payment
  • carers cancelled with the reason 'IMP' must lodge a new claim if they wish to receive payment once they have been released from confinement, as they cannot be reinstated

8

Finalise activity via the Assessment Results (AR) screen + Read more ...

Manually key 'AR' in the Nxt field, to go to the AR screen. Otherwise the Activity List (AL) screen will display and the started activity will need to be selected from this screen.

A DOC will be created by the workflow in either the carer's or care receiver's record. Add any other relevant details including the dates of confinement and the name of institution if known.
