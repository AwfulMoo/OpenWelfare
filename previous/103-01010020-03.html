Cape York Initiative

+ Read more ...

Under the Cape York Initiative, there are two possible outcomes for customers who have a payment nominee:

  • If the payment nominee is an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes, the customer is not eligible for Income Management. The Cape York Initiative Team is responsible for ineligibility coding - see Nominee Income Management. It is at the discretion of the Family Responsibilities Commission if they choose to revoke the current Notice
  • If the payment nominee is not an excluded payment nominee, and the customer meets all other eligibility criteria, the customer will be income managed, with the payment nominee being responsible for determining allocations from the Income Management account of the principal

A review of the payment nominee arrangement must be completed for customers who have been referred to Income Management under the Cape York Initiative and have a payment nominee in place, regardless of whether the nominee is an excluded payment nominee.

Note: termination of a payment nominee arrangement may result in a customer again becoming eligible for Income Management under the Cape York Initiative, where that payment nominee was an excluded payment nominee.

Cape York Initiative + Read more ...

A customer referred for Income Management under the Cape York Initiative who is a payment nominee for another person must have their payment nominee appointment reconsidered to determine if the arrangement is appropriate and in the best interests of the principal.

If the appointment is deemed appropriate:

  • the customer that is income managed under the Cape York Initiative can continue to be a payment nominee
  • the principal will be income managed under Nominee Income Management - Cape York (NIM-CY), unless they are eligible under another measure in their own right, and
  • the principal's income support payments will continue to be managed by the customer as the payment nominee (that is, the applicable percentage paid to the principal's Income Management account and the discretionary funds paid to the payment nominee)

  • If the payment nominee is not an excluded payment nominee and the customer meets all other eligibility criteria, the customer will be income managed, with the payment nominee being responsible for determining allocations from the Income Management account of the principal

Customers who are paid instalments of Youth Allowance (YA) on behalf of a Youth Allowance principal, and are on Child Protection Income Management, will not have these instalments income managed (unless the Youth Allowance principal is income managed in their own right).

Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) automatic triggers

+ Read more ...

Under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) measure, there are two possible outcomes for customers who have a payment nominee:

  • If the payment nominee is an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes, the customer is not eligible for Income Management. For more information about ineligibility coding, see Nominee Income Management
  • If the payment nominee is not an excluded payment nominee, and the customer meets all other eligible criteria, the customer will be income managed, with the payment nominee being responsible for determining allocations from the Income Management account of the principal

A review of the payment nominee arrangement must be completed for customers who have been referred to Income Management under the VWPR measure by a social worker and have a payment nominee in place, regardless of whether the nominee is an excluded payment nominee.

Note: termination of a payment nominee arrangement may result in a customer again becoming eligible for Income Management under the VWPR measure where that payment nominee was an excluded payment nominee.

Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) notice + Read more ...

A customer referred for Income Management under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) measure by a social worker, must have their payment nominee appointment reconsidered to determine if the arrangement is appropriate and in the best interest of the customer.

Customers identified for Income Management under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) measure, and are a payment nominee, should have the payment nominee arrangement reviewed.

If the appointment is deemed appropriate:

  • the customer that is income managed under the VWPR measure can continue to be a payment nominee, and
  • the principal's income support payments will continue to be managed by the customer as the payment nominee (that is, the full amount paid directly to the payment nominee where the principal is not on Income Management, or if the principal is on Income Management, the relevant percentage paid to the principal's Income Management account and the discretionary funds paid directly to the payment nominee)

Disengaged Youth (DEY)

+ Read more ...

Under the Disengaged Youth (DEY) measure, there are three possible outcomes for customers who have a payment nominee:

  • If the payment nominee is an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes, the customer is not eligible for Income Management. For more information about ineligibility coding, see Nominee Income Management
  • If the payment nominee is not an excluded payment nominee, and the customer meets all other eligibility criteria, the customer will be income managed, with the payment nominee being responsible for determining allocations from the Income Management account of the principal
  • If the payment nominee has an exemption from Income Management then this exemption will also apply to the principal if they would have been income managed under Nominee Income Management - Compulsory Income Management (NIM-CIM)

Note:

  • Termination of a payment nominee arrangement may result in a customer again becoming eligible for Income Management under the DEY measure where that payment nominee was an excluded payment nominee
  • Customers on Nominee Income Management under the Disengaged Youth measure are not eligible to apply for an exemption

Disengaged Youth (DEY) + Read more ...

Customers identified as eligible for Income Management under the Disengaged Youth (DEY) measure, and are a payment nominee, should have the payment nominee arrangement reviewed.

If the payment nominee arrangement is to continue:

  • the customer that is income managed under the DEY measure can continue to be a payment nominee
  • the principal will be income managed under Nominee Income Management - Compulsory Income Management (NIM-CIM), unless they are eligible under another measure in their own right
  • the principal's payments will be managed by the customer as the payment nominee (that is, 50% paid to the principal's Income Management account and 50% paid directly to the payment nominee, or applicable percentage if the principal is income managed under another measure in their own right)
  • if the payment nominee has an exemption from Income Management then this exemption will also apply to the principal if they would have been income managed under NIM-CIM

Voluntary Income Management (VIM)

+ Read more ...

A customer who has an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes is not eligible to enter a Voluntary Income Management Agreement.

For more information about terminating a Voluntary Income Management agreement as the customer is no longer eligible, see Nominee Income Management.

However, if the payment nominee is income managed themselves, a Voluntary Income Management Agreement can be entered into.

Note: customers who are on Nominee Income Management are not eligible to enter a Voluntary Income Management Agreement.

A review of the payment nominee arrangement must be completed for customers who request to enter a Voluntary Income Management Agreement and have a current payment nominee in place, regardless of whether the nominee is an excluded payment nominee

Voluntary Income Management (VIM) + Read more ...

There is no restriction for customers who are payment nominees and want to enter into a Voluntary Income Management Agreement, however, where the customer was previously an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes, the principal may now be eligible for Income Management.

A review of the payment nominee arrangement must be completed for customers who are payment nominees and are requesting to enter a Voluntary Income Management agreement.

If the appointment is deemed appropriate:

  • the customer that is income managed under Voluntary Income management can continue to be a payment nominee, and
  • the principal's income support payments will continue to be managed by the customer as the payment nominee (that is, the full amount paid directly to the payment nominee where the principal is not on Income Management, or if the principal is income managed, the relevant percentage paid to the principal's Income Management account and the discretionary funds paid directly to the payment nominee)

BasicsCard

+ Read more ...

A payment nominee can decide to have the principal (customer) issued with the BasicsCard. The system identifies that a customer has a payment nominee and will bring up an 'assign card' screen at the end of the 'authentication screen'. Here the payment nominee can choose to have the BasicsCard issued in the principal's (customers) name rather than their own.

Should the payment nominee decide to have the card assigned to the principal (customer), the principal will need to sign the BasicsCard receipt.

Note: if an under 18 year old Youth Allowance customer is income managed in their own right, and wishes to have the BasicsCard issued in their parents name and the parent approves then the under 18 year old Youth Allowance customer must appoint their parent as a formal payment nominee.

BasicsCard + Read more ...

Customers who are payment nominees and on Income Management may hold two BasicsCards, one for themselves and one for the principal for whom they are the nominee (if they have been issued with the principal's BasicsCard).

Regular allocations

+ Read more ...

A payment nominee can control one off and regular allocations on behalf of the customer (principal).
