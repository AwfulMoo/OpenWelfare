This procedure is for the assessment of the vacated home to provide care to a person other than their partner in a care situation + Read more ...

  • For respite care, see Temporary change of address to an aged care home for respite care

2

Care for person other than their partner for over 14 days + Read more ...

Customer has vacated their principal home, for a period longer than 14 days, to care for a person other than their partner in a care situation. If the care situation is for less than 14 days record details on a DOC, no further action is required.

For long term care situations due to the customer's and/or their partner's illness, see Vacation of principal home due to illness.

A customer is in a care situation if the customer is providing a substantial level of care to the caree. Determine what evidence is required to verify if a substantial level of care is being provided.

It is not intended that medical evidence is required for the majority of assessments. Can verification of a substantial level of care be provided by any of the following:

  • The carer is paid Carer Allowance (adult) or Carer Payment (adult) in respect of the caree, or
  • The caree is paid Disability Support Pension and their medical condition indicates they require assistance, or
  • The caree is over Age Pension age and regarded as frail, or
  • The caree had an Aged Care Assessment Team (ACAT) assessment that indicates care is needed on an ongoing basis?
    • Yes, no further verification is required to determine a substantial level of care is being provided to the caree. Go to Step 5
    • No, go to Step 3

3

Other evidence + Read more ...

Is there sufficient other evidence to verify the customer has vacated their principal home for a care situation? For example, the caree can provide medical evidence that verifies they require care in their home because of their illness. This could be an ongoing illness or long term care is required after the caree is discharged from hospital.

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Insufficient evidence to support caree requires care due to ill health + Read more ...

Request the customer provide:

  • supporting evidence from a treating doctor or relevant health professional that the caree requires care
  • code a manual review and requested evidence to be returned within 14 days
  • record the details of action taken to date on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

5

Other accommodation or interests + Read more ...

Does the customer have a granny flat interest or reside in a retirement village or a sale leaseback arrangement?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Granny flat interest, retirement village or sale leaseback arrangement + Read more ...

Granny flats, retirement village units and sale leaseback arrangements are referred to as special residences.

The home ownership status of a customer or their partner residing in a special residence does not depend on who holds title to the premises. It is based on the amount of entry contribution paid. If more than the extra allowable amount was paid for their interest in a special residence then they are assessed as homeowners.

For customers vacating a retirement village, ongoing home ownership status depends on whether or not their entry contribution is refunded to them. Please contact the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk for assessment on a case by case basis.

For a partnered customer vacating a special residence the Social Security Act sections 1153 - 1157 defines the home ownership status of each member of the couple. For assistance in interpreting the Act, contact the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk.

7

Is the customer single or partnered? + Read more ...

  • Single
    • for non-homeowners, go to Step 8
    • for homeowners, go to Step 9
  • Partnered
    • if partner, moves with customer, and the customers are home owners, go to Step 9
    • if partner moves with customer and the customers are non-home owners, see Step 6 of the Partnered customers and treatment of principal home table
    • if partner is residing in principal home, see Step 1 of the Partnered customers and treatment of principal home table
    • if partner has previously vacated principal home, see Step 2 of the Partnered customers and treatment of principal home table

8

Customer who is a non-homeowner + Read more ...

Advise the customer they will continue to be assessed as a non-homeowner and therefore continue to have the higher assets limit.

If the absence is temporary then Rent Assistance (RA) may continue to be paid on their former residence. If the absence from their former home is not short term clarify why they are continuing to rent the property and whether anyone else will be paying rent at the property.

  • Run the Change in Contact Details workflow to update customer's change of address
  • If customer is paying rent at the new address, request rent verification if required

See Step 3 of the Recording and finalising the activity table

9

Customer who is a homeowner + Read more ...

A person's former principal home is exempt from the Assets Test for 2 years from the date it was vacated by the customer to enter a care situation. Customer continues to be assessed as a homeowner during this period.

The customer may be eligible for Rent Assistance (RA) during this period. For more information, see Qualification for payment of Rent Assistance (RA).

  • Run the Change in Contact Details workflow to update new address
  • Run the Change in Contact Details workflow or update the following fields on the Accommodation (AC) screen:
    • Event Date: enter date of move
    • Home Ownership: change to 'LHO' (home owner lives elsewhere)
    • Type of Rent: enter details only if the customer is paying rent at the new address
    • Actual Rent Amount: details are updated only if the customer is paying rent at the new address. Enter the net amount paid for accommodation after any subsidies, rebates, amounts paid by business, etc
    • Freq: details are updated only if the customer is paying rent at the new address. Identifies the frequency with which the customer pays the amount entered in Actual Rent Amount: field
    • Rent Verification: details are updated only if the customer is paying rent at the new address. Indicates whether customer has provided evidence or why details not verified
    • Accommodation Circumstance: details are updated only if the customer is paying rent at the new address. Used to record if customer is to be classed as a sharer in relation to the sharer provisions for RA
    • Govt Sub-tenant: details are updated only if the customer is paying rent at the new address. This field indicates whether the customer is or is not a sub-tenant in accommodation where the primary tenant pays a subsidised rental for public housing. Customers who are Government sub-tenants will not receive RA
    • Reason Home Vacated: needs to be recorded when customers leave their principal home and may be entitled to RA and/or keep the home exempt from the Assets Test. Enter 'CRR' for providing care in a private home
    • Date Home Vacated: enter date home vacated
    • Complete footer details Source:, DOR: and Action: fields

Is rental income being received for the principal home?

  • Yes, see Step 1 of the Recording and finalising the activity table
  • No, see Step 3 of the Recording and finalising the activity table

Partnered customers and treatment of principal home

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

Customer's partner is residing in principal home + Read more ...

The former principal home remains an exempt asset and the customer and their partner continue to be assessed as homeowners while the partner still resides in the principal home.

  • Run the Change in Contact Details workflow to update customer's new address as postal address if customer wants their mail sent there, otherwise record circumstances on a DOC
  • If a temporary address is being recorded ask the customer how long they estimate they will be absent from the principal home to provide care and record this information as the end date for temporary address. Advise customer to notify Centrelink if their circumstances change
  • The carer is not entitled to illness separated status when the person they are providing care to is not their partner
  • If the customer is liable to pay rent whilst providing care see Rent Assistance (RA) for care receivers
  • Advise customer/third party about appointing a nominee

Is rental income being received for the principal home?

  • Yes, see Step 1 of the Recording and finalising the activity table
  • No, see Step 3 of the Recording and finalising the activity table

2

Customer's partner not residing in principal home + Read more ...

The customer is entitled to a 2 year exemption when vacating principal home to provide care for a person who is not their partner.

Is the customer a homeowner or non-homeowner?

  • Homeowner, go to Step 3
  • Non-homeowner, go to Step 6

3

Partnered customer, homeowner, partner not residing in principal home + Read more ...

The carer is not entitled to illness separated status when the person they are caring for is not their partner but may be entitled to illness separated if their partner had vacated the home at an earlier time due to illness. For more information on the partner vacating home due to their illness, see Vacation of principal home due to illness.

Is the customer currently receiving a single rate due to their partner vacating their home previously due to illness?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Partnered homeowner, on single rate, partner not residing in principal home + Read more ...

The customer is entitled to a 2 year exemption when vacating principal home to provide care for a person who is not their partner.

  • Run the Change in Contact Details workflow to update customer's address
  • Run the Change in Contact Details workflow to update the following fields on the Accommodation (AC) screen:
    • Event Date: enter date of move
    • Home Ownership: do not change, customer continues to be assessed as a home owner
    • Type of Rent: enter details only if the customer is paying rent at the new address
    • Actual Rent Amount: details are updated only if the customer is paying rent at the new address. Enter the net amount paid for accommodation after any subsidies, rebates, amounts paid by business, etc
    • Freq: details are updated only if the customer is paying rent at the new address. Identifies the frequency with which the customer pays the amount entered in Actual Rent Amount: field
    • Rent Verification: details are updated only if the customer is paying rent at the new address. Indicates whether customer has provided evidence or why details not verified
    • Accommodation Circumstance: details are updated only if the customer is paying rent at the new address. Used to record if customer is to be classed as a sharer in relation to the sharer provisions for RA
    • Govt Sub-tenant: details are updated only if the customer is paying rent at the new address. This field indicates whether the customer is or is not a sub-tenant in accommodation where the primary tenant pays a subsidised rental for public housing. Customers who are Government sub-tenants will not receive RA
    • Reason Home Vacated: needs to be recorded when customers leave their principal home and may be entitled to RA and/or keep the home exempt from the Assets Test. Enter 'CRR' for providing care in a private home

Date Home Vacated: enter date home vacated.

Complete footer details Source:, DOR: and Action: fields.

Is rental income being received for the principal home?

  • Yes, see Step 1 of the Recording and finalising the activity table
  • No, see Step 3 of the Recording and finalising the activity table

5

Partnered homeowner, not entitled to a single rate, partner not residing in principal home + Read more ...

The customer is entitled to a 2 year exemption when vacating principal home to provide care for a person who is not their partner.

  • Run the Change in Contact Details workflow to update customer's address
  • Update customer's new address as postal address if customer wants their mail sent there, otherwise record circumstances on a DOC
  • If a temporary address is being recorded ask the customer how long they estimate they will be absent from the principal home to provide care and record this information as the end date for temporary address. Advise customer to notify Centrelink if their circumstances change
  • Do not update the AC screen. The system will not allow a change if the customer's marital status is not updated as illness separated. The carer is not entitled to illness separated status when the person they are providing care to is not their partner
  • Advise customer/third party about appointing a nominee

Is rental income being received for the principal home?

  • Yes, see Step 1 of the Recording and finalising the activity table
  • No, see Step 3 of the Recording and finalising the activity table

6

Partnered customer, non-homeowner, partner not residing in principal home + Read more ...

Advise the customer or their partner they will continue to be assessed as a non-homeowner and therefore continue to have the higher assets limit.

Customers receiving RA will have their RA ceased at their former principal home 14 days after the date they enter the care situation. For members of a couple if the absence is temporary then RA may continue to be paid on their former residence. Contact the Rent.Assistance.Servicedesk@humanservices.gov.au for more information.

  • Run the Change in Contact Details workflow to update customer's change of address. If both parties are living at separate addresses the postal address is to be updated
  • If the couple are not residing together do not update the AC screen. The system will not allow a change if the customer's marital status is not updated as illness separated. The carer is not entitled to illness separated status when the person they are providing care to is not their partner
  • If customer is paying rent at the new address, request rent verification if required

See Step 3 of the Recording and finalising the activity table.

Recording and finalising the activity

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Rental income being received + Read more ...

Issue a Module R - Real estate details (Mod R) when documents returned.

Code details of the former principal home on the Real Estate/Business Identifying (REBI) screen regardless if the customer is entitled to an exemption or not.

  • On REBI, code Real Estate/Business Type: field with the appropriate code:
    • for 2 year exemption code 'EXE'
    • if home is not exempt, code the appropriate real estate type using help field if required, for example '?'

Using the correct codes above will ensure the asset value will not be used in the customer's assessment if an exemption applies.

  • Code the Address:, Suburb/PC: fields in the usual manner

For help with coding the other fields, see:

  • Assessment and sale of real estate and
  • Assessing income from real estate and timeshare

Or use Help ('?') option.

Press [Enter] to be taken to the Real Estate (RE) screen.

2

Update RE screen + Read more ...

Complete the following fields on RE screen:

  • Event Date: code appropriate event date. Note: date of event for EXP cannot be prior to 1 July 2005 and for EXA it cannot be prior to 1 July 2004
  • % Income Owned: code as the percentage owned by the customer, for example, 100% if sole owner
  • %Asset owned: code as the percentage owned by the customer, for example, 100% if sole owner
  • Current Market Value: code as '1' or the amount nominated by the customer if given not required if home is exempt
  • Gross income: code gross income
  • Asset Valn Source: code as 'CLI'
  • Allowable Income Dedns$ pa: the allowable deductions from the gross income, not including the interest paid for loans or mortgages. Details are usually obtained from the rental property schedule of the business, a personal ITR or a profit and loss type statement

3

Other action possibly required + Read more ...

  • Update any other changes to the customer's circumstances, for example, updating the customer's income and/or assets
  • If someone is receiving Carer Allowance (CA) or Carer Payment (CP) for customer, update their record as required
  • If any documentation or forms were requested, for example, for customers not entitled to an exemption a Mod R may to issued or an SA220 when full accommodation details are not known, set a review in 14 days for its return
  • If the customer or their partner has vacated a granny flat in which they held an interest, contact the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk for possible reassessment of the deprivation that occurred when the granny flat right was created
  • Update any other changes to the customer's circumstances for example, updating the customer's income and/or assets
  • Record details on a DOC
  • Finalise activity via the Assessment Results (AR) screen
