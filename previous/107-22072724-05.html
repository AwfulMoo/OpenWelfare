Money you owe service – Services Australia Website

General information relating to the application of a DIS write off / debt pause

Table 1

Item

Description

1

SMS messaging when debt pause ends

Customers who used self-service to pause their debt in Money you owe, will receive an SMS message 6 days prior to the debt pause ending.

2

Non-standard withholdings will be reinstated at the end of a debt pause

Customers with non-standard withholdings who pause their debt in Money you owe will have their non-standard withholdings automatically re-instated at the end of the debt pause. The non-standard withholdings will be reapplied for a period of 3 months.

3

Eligible customers can access the disaster/emergency functionality in Money you owe twice in a 12 month period

Eligible customers can access the enhanced Money you owe disaster/emergency functionality twice in a 12 month period, up to a maximum of 3 months on each occasion. Customers seeking to pause their debt repayments for a third or subsequent time will need to contact Services Australia for staff assistance. Staff are to assist the customer according to their circumstances. If the customer is:

  • still located in a disaster affected location, apply a further DIS write off, or
  • not located in a disaster affected location, assess the customers’ financial circumstances and apply a STH temporary write off if required

5

Involuntary recovery action regardless of application of a pause

Customers who are located in a declared disaster affected area, regardless of whether they have a debt pause applied or not, will not:

  • have a general interest charge applied
  • be referred to an external collection agent (ECA) for the duration of the disaster event (6 months)
  • be subject to tax return garnishee

6

Debts with an ECA at the time of the declared disaster / emergency event

Debts with an ECA at the time of a declared disaster / emergency event, will not be automatically recalled to the agency. If affected customer’s request, recall their debt/s from the ECA using the code RDP and apply a DIS write off.

Exemptions to the debt pause in disaster affected postcodes

Table 2: Exemptions apply to some debts where debt recovery continues.

Exemption type

Reason

Fraud and serious non compliance

Work relating to fraud and serious non-compliance will continue to maintain the integrity of the welfare system

Assurance of Support

Debt pause does not apply to an assurer with a bond in place, as recovery action can be taken against the bond. The debt pause will apply to an assurer if no bond is available

New Zealand Pension Arrears

Debt activity is part of agreement with the New Zealand government, suspension of debt action requires agreement from New Zealand government authority

Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA) Clearance (customer and/or partner)

These debts are raised to offset DVA impact payments. DVA has continued to make these payments. Debts should continue to be raised to make the adjustments to customer’s payment to minimise overpayment and future debt to the customer

Bankruptcy

Customers declaring bankruptcy must be notified of the outcome of their debt, and the implications on bankruptcy claims
