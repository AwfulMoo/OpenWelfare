Acknowledgement emails

Generating monthly MI acknowledgement emails

Table 1: This table explains how Registering Officers are to generate acknowledgement emails to Medical Indemnity Insurers (MII) and Medical Defence Organisations (MDO) by the 10th business day of the month.

Step

Action

1

Acknowledgement emails + Read more ...

Acknowledgement emails are generated monthly, following the registration of all MI applications in HP Records Manager. The acknowledgement emails are sent to relevant MII/MDOs as confirmation that Services Australia has received the MI application(s).

This process must be completed by the 10th day of each month.

2

Identify registration details + Read more ...

Locate application details to draft MI application acknowledgement emails:

  • open the Claims on Hand spreadsheet
  • select the appropriate month by clicking on the monthly ‘Tab’ at the bottom of the spreadsheet
  • navigate to the MI mailbox > Stationery folder > Acknowledgement email template
  • use the information from the relevant fields when completing the email template

3

Check HP Records Manager records + Read more ...

Check the information against HP Records Manager:

  • in the Search By field, select ‘Record Number’ from the drop-down menu
  • in the Equal To field insert the complete Application Reference Number (ARN) number that appears on the letter (for example, ARN1600A)
  • right click on the ARN container record
  • select Properties to display the claim details

Note: if the information does not match, retrieve the physical case file and confirm where the error has occurred. Update the Claims on Hand spreadsheet or HP Records Manager where required. Update the letter with the correct details as per the application form submitted.

Repeat this process for each MDO/MII.

4

Check ARN reflects correct MDO/MII + Read more ...

Always ensure the ‘Alpha’ in the ARN corresponds with the correct MDO/MII letter (for example, 12222-1M-H).

Note: ensure the correct and appropriate acronyms appear. Refer to Insurer TRIM Identifiers.

5

Review information + Read more ...

Review all the information in the MI application acknowledgement emails, ensuring the content matches the Claims on Hand spreadsheet data:

  • organise for a team member to undertake QA and confirm the:
  • date to be sent is correct
  • recipient’s name is correct
  • details of each application against HP Records Manager (ARN, MDO/MII reference number, member name and patient name)

Note: if the information does not match, retrieve the physical case file and confirm where the error has occurred. Update the Claims on Hand spreadsheet or HP Record Manager where required

Once the QA process has been completed:

  • send the emails to the relevant MDO/MII. Note: classify all outgoing emails to MDOs/MIIs as ‘For official use only’
  • update the Claims on Hand spreadsheet to confirm the acknowledgement emails have been sent
  • add the relevant email to the corresponding HP Records Manager container, and
  • save a copy of the email in the appropriate folder in the shared drive

Section 38, withdrawal or deemed not payable letters

Processing section 38, withdrawal or deemed not payable letters

Table 2: This table explains the process of sending, receiving, reminding and requesting an extension for the section 38, withdrawal or deemed not payable letters sent to Medical Indemnity Insurers (MII) and Medical Defence Organisations (MDO) at the end of each month.

Step

Action

1

Send letters for section 38, withdrawals or claims deemed not payable + Read more ...

Scan an electronic version of each of the signed section 38, withdrawal and deemed not payable letters and save in relevant ARN folder in the shared drive:

  • navigate to the MI mailbox > Stationery folder > MI Applications Further Information Required email template. Open the email template and update the red text in the email
  • attach the appropriate section 38/withdrawal/deemed not payable letter and send an email to each MII/MDO with the email classification as ‘For official use only’
  • print each email on pink paper for each MI application
  • place the original section 38/ withdrawal/ deemed not payable letter(s) and email printouts in the relevant paper based corporate files
  • save the scanned section 38/ withdrawal/ deemed not payable letters into the relevant ARN folders in HP Records Manager
  • update the General Tab in HP Records Manager for each MI application to reflect the appropriate ‘Application Status’ and ‘Section 38 Sent’ and ‘Section 38 Due’ fields
  • file emails in appropriate folder in the Medical Indemnity mailbox, and
  • archive corporate files accordingly. For:
    • section 38 letters, store corporate files containing section 38 letters on site, ready for retrieval when section 38 responses are received. See Finalising assessment of Medical Indemnity (MI) payment application
    • withdrawal and deemed not payable letters, store corporate files containing withdrawal letters on site for 3 months before archiving. See Step 3

Open the Claims on Hand spreadsheet in the shared drive and update:

  • each MI application for the relevant month to either ‘38 sent’, ‘withdrawn sent’ or ‘not payable sent’ depending on the outcome, and
  • the ‘Section 38 Register Tab’ to reflect the current section 38 data. See the Resources page for assistance

2

Section 38 response received + Read more ...

Before an MI section 38 response can be assessed, register receipt of the section 38 response:

  • update the Claims on Hand spreadsheet for the relevant month. See the Resources page for assistance, and
  • update the section 38 register worksheet
  • compile the supporting documentation file, and
  • folio relevant pages

Note: to ensure the correct processes have been followed, see the Register Medical Indemnity application table in Medical Indemnity (MI) Schemes registration.

3

Update HP Records Manager + Read more ...

Once the paper file has been compiled, update the basic details of the Section 38 in HP Records Manager.

  • Open HP Records Manager via Start > All Apps > HP Records Manager:
  • ensure the Search By field displays 'Record Number'
  • in the Equal To field, search for the claim’s Application Reference Number (ARN) number
  • right-click on the application file relating to the section 38 response received
  • scroll down and right-click on Properties > General tab, update :
    • Application Status field to ‘PENDING ASSESSMENT’
    • Status Updated field, key today’s date
    • Section 38 Received, key date section 38 received

Note: if financials have changed, update the relevant financial details tab for the relevant scheme. Advise the Assessing Officer (AO) handling the application the section 38 response has been received.

4

Email reminder for section 38 responses + Read more ...

The day before the monthly section 38 responses are due, identify the MDOs or MIIs who have not yet responded:

  • open the Claims on Hand spreadsheet for the relevant year
  • select the S38 tab
  • filter the ‘Section 38 response received’ column to show all blank fields. Note: check to ensure nothing has been received beforehand

Send a reminder email for all applications still waiting on a section 38 response for the months' due date:

  • navigate to the MI mailbox > Stationery folder > Medical Indemnity – Outstanding Section 38 Response – ARNxxxx-xx-x’ email template
  • forward the open message and populate the relevant fields in red
  • remove the ‘FW:’ and email classification from the subject line with the relevant ARN number

In the MI shared drive, locate and open the ‘Address and email details for insurers’ document and in the:

  • To field, add the relevant MDO/MII recipients
  • Cc field, add the Medical Indemnity mailbox
  • proof read the email and provide the email to another Programme Officer to be checked for accuracy and corrections before sending
  • select the ‘For official use only’ classification
  • check the email for accuracy and send

In the MI mailbox, file all emails under the appropriate scheme > Insurer > Section 38 folder:

  • in the Claims on Hand spreadsheet, update the section 38 register with the date the reminder was sent
  • print emails and place on top of the corporate file

MDOs/MIIs usually respond to the MI reminder email by:

  • sending documentation relating to the section 38 letter
  • requesting an extension, go to Step 5
  • withdrawing their application, go to Medical Indemnity (MI) refunds and withdrawals

5

Section 38 request for extension + Read more ...

Emails received via the MI mailbox from MDOs/MIIs requesting an extension to respond to the Section 38, are to be forwarded to the delegate to seek approval for the extension and cc the MI mailbox and supervisor.

Add a brief description of the extension detailing the following:

  • ARN Number
  • date section 38 letter sent
  • due date
  • why the letter was sent
  • reason for extension

Attach the section 38 letter from the relevant ARN folder, located in the MI shared drive.

Once the delegate has responded to the email, reply to the MDO/MII advising them of the delegate’s decision and cc the MI mailbox. If approved, the new section 38 response date will be the following month's due date.

Note: before sending the email, remove any personal identifiers such as doctor’s names for privacy reasons and select the ‘For official use only’ classification.

In the MI mailbox, file all emails under the appropriate scheme > Insurer > Section 38 folder.

Update the section 38 register in the Claims on Hand spreadsheet.

Print emails and place on top of the relevant corporate file.

Archive MI corporate files

Archiving MI corporate files

Table 3: This table explains the process of archiving MI corporate files at the end of each month.

Step

Action

1

Prepare files for scanning + Read more ...

To prepare the files for scanning:

  • retrieve the paper claim files from the storage cupboards. Note: maximum weight 8 kilograms per box
  • remove and discard any staples or clips from the paper file (they may obstruct the scanner)
  • check and folio all pages within corporate folder

2

Scan claim files + Read more ...

To scan the claim files:

  • remove all claim documentation from the corporate folder. Note: keep each page in order
  • if files contain multiple applications, scan each application separately
  • applications are identified with a claim number for example: ARN145-1U-I, ARN145-2U-I, ARN145-3U-I, ARN145-4U-I to indicate there are 4 parts to this file. Note: each application will be separated by a coloured divider
  • on the scanner, select colour and double-sided scanning for all applications
  • place 1 application at a time in the document feeder. For example: ARN145-1U-I
  • return application to the corporate folder, ensure pages are placed in the correct numerical order
  • on top of paper file, add the ‘Attachment 1 – Scanning Confirmation’ page. Do not folio this page

3

Electronic Storage + Read more ...

Open the scanned application and check that all the scanned claim pages have been scanned.

If the application is scanned in 1 part, save the file in the relevant Application Reference Number (ARN) folder on the shared drive. Save file name with the correct ARN number and add ‘SCANNED’. For example, ‘ARN1234-1A-H SCANNED’

Complete the ARN details in the spreadsheet located in the shared drive Medical Indemnity > Scanned MI claims for Archive. Go to Step 4.

If the application was scanned in multiple parts, combine the application into a single PDF and save the multiple parts on the desktop by folio numbers. For example, 205-199, 198-150,149-90, 89-40, 39-1.

Note: access to Adobe Acrobat DC is required to combine the application.

When combining PDF files, open Adobe. Select:

  • the first document, then select the last page in the document
  • Tools > Organise pages > Insert > From file > Next document in folio sequence > Location After > OK

Continue until the multiple documents are in a single PDF.

If the file is in the correct order, select 'Save As' and select the ARN folder located on the shared drive and name the file with the correct file number.

Note: the name of the electronic scanned application must be changed to reflect the full application number. The full application number is not located on the file label, but is inside the file cover on the ‘File Note’ this is always the first document in the file. The application number format indicates the number of parts to the file. For example, 4 parts would read ARN145-1U-I, ARN145-2U-I, ARN145-3U-I, ARN145-4U-I.

Note: complete the Application Reference Number (ARN) details in the spreadsheet located in the shared drive Medical Indemnity > Scanned MI claims for Archive.

4

Quality Assurance (QA) + Read more ...

Place the scanned corporate files into the QA drawer, for the QA Officer to check the scanned documents.

Once QA has been completed, the corporate file will be put into the Quality Check (QC) drawer, for the QC Officer to complete a brief check.

The QA and QC Officer will sign and date the ‘Attachment 1 – Scanning Confirmation’ and place in the approved drawer.

5

Send the archive boxes to Records Management + Read more ...

Assemble an archive box as per the instructions on the flat pack box.

Note: to order archive boxes contact the Record Management Helpdesk.

Collect all ‘approved’ corporate files from the ‘approved’ drawer.

All scanned documents need to be added to HP Records Manager before being placed in the archive box and sent to Records Management.

Right click the scanned document within the ARN folder in the shared drive, send to HP Records Manager, select MI Document, and key relevant ARN number (example ARN145-1U-I) in ‘ARN Application’ field and OK, place file into the archive and update MI claims for Archive spreadsheet with relevant details.

6

Complete Courier Form + Read more ...

Complete the Adhoc Courier Request Form and follow the instructions to send the boxes containing corporate files to archive.

Note: boxes with corporate files in them should be left out overnight, before leaving for the day, check that archive boxes have been collected. If not, place boxes in a secure cupboard or compactus. Place boxes in the mail room the next day for collection.

Before leaving for the day, check that archive boxes have been collected. If not, place boxes in a secure cupboard or compactus. Place boxes in the mail room the next day for collection.
