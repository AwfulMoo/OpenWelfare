Additional MCRP dissolution information

MCRP dissolution Q and A

This table contains information regarding the changes that will be made to multiple MBS items when the MCRP dissolves on 1 November 2018. The changes relate to the Medicare Claims Review Panel (MCRP), including those for ophthalmology and plastic and reconstructive surgery.

Item

Description

1

Question: How did this change come about?

Answer: This change was in response to recommendations made by the MBS Review Taskforce. The change forms part of the 2018-19 Budget.

2

Question: What impact will this have on MCRP applications that have already been approved?

Answer: Any claim that has already received MCRP approval but has not yet been performed will need to be performed no later than 31 October 2018 to be eligible for a Medicare rebate.

Any services that have been approved by the MCRP but are not performed by 1 November 2018 will be subject to the new rules applying to the relevant MBS items.

3

Question: When do applications for the current MCRP items need to be submitted?

Answer: MCRP claims can be submitted for pre-approved services but all MCRP services must be performed no later than 31 October 2018.

Applications for claims to MCRP items for services performed after 31 October 2018 will not be accepted.

Any service for MCRP items with a date of service before 1 November 2018 will require approval before a Medicare benefit can be paid.

4

Question: What if a service is performed before 1 November 2018 but has not been approved by the MCRP?

Answer: The MCRP will continue to accept applications for claims to MCRP items for which the service was performed before 1 November 2018.

5

Question: For a service that has been performed but previously rejected by the MCRP, can the decision be appealed after 1 November 2018?

Answer: The MCRP will accept appeals on decision made for services performed before 1 November 2018. Any claim resubmitted for an appeal after 1 November 2018 must include additional clinical information/ photographs to enable the MCRP to determine if the claims meets the relevant MBS item descriptor.

6

Question: Where can practitioners and patients find out more about the changes to the MCRP items?

Answer: Any questions regarding the policy changes to MCRP and plastic surgery MBS items can be sent to the Department of Health and Aged Care, MBS Policy and Medical Specialist Services team

7

Question: Will there be further information available?

Answer: Additional information on these changes is available on MBS Online.

See MBS Online for further details.
