Role and responsibilities of the Housing Authority for RDS

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Housing Authority (HA) role + Read more ...

The Housing Authority (HA) is responsible for processing new deductions and varying current deductions for customers wanting to participate in the Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS). Calculation of the payment amount with the customer prior to the customer signing the form and the instruction being processed is also a HA responsibility.

HAs are also responsible for ensuring that their staff understand their obligations, particularly in regard to privacy and confidentiality, under the Crimes Act 1914, Privacy Act 1988 and Social Security Act 1991, as set out in the RDS Agreements.

HAs will also advise customers whose rent deduction type is 'OHO' (Other Housing Organisation) to include the advice of rent paid form as an alternate means of having Part B of the Rent Certificate (SU523) completed.

2

Housing Authority forms + Read more ...

HAs have their own deduction authority forms which are used as consent for deductions. The agency does not have a form for RDS. Customers should be directed to their HA for the commencement and variation to deductions.

Managing RDS deductions

Item

Description

1

The agency's role + Read more ...

National Business Gateway Confirmation Services provide a helpdesk support service to the HAs for issues and queries regarding the scheme.

Zone staff and Service Officers may need to answer enquiries from HA staff and customers on a case by case basis to discuss or resolve problems and issues. Service Officers are also responsible for the promotion of RDS. See Publicity of RDS.

2

Publicity of Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) + Read more ...

Service centres are responsible for promoting the RDS to customers who receive payments from which RDS deductions can be made, and for providing information to customers about the scheme.

Service centres are to display RDS promotional material where space permits, and Service Officer should promote the scheme to customers who are eligible to participate.

3

Voluntary nature of RDS + Read more ...

The voluntary nature of RDS is consistent with the 'inalienability' provisions of the Social Security Act 1991, which protects social security payments from being accessed by third parties (such as creditors) against the customer's wishes.

The voluntary nature of the scheme is also consistent with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) set out in schedule 1 of the Privacy Act 1988 (Commonwealth).

The customer and the agency can cancel an RDS deduction at any time. See Terminating a Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) deduction.

HAs are also able to cancel a deduction. The agency is advised in these cases via electronic data exchange from the HA which automatically amends the customer's record.

4

Priority order of deductions + Read more ...

A hierarchy is applied to all deductions from Centrelink payments. This is as follows:

  • weekly payment shortfall
  • weekly payment carryover
  • advance payment recovery
  • overpayment
  • Financial Supplement Loan (FSL)
  • child support liability
  • voluntary tax payments
  • government housing deduction (RDS)
  • other deductions (including Centrepay)

There is also a sub-hierarchy applied to Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) deduction types. This is as follows:

  • Rent (RNT) or Other Housing Organisation (OHO)
  • Housing Authority Loan (LON)
  • Housing Authority Arrears (ARR)
  • Other Housing Payments (OHP)

5

Limits to number of deductions + Read more ...

The number of HA deductions a customer can have under the Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) is determined by the following:

  • There can only be one current housing payment instruction for rent or loan for a particular benefit group current at a time
  • A customer can only have one current instruction for each deduction type (Rent, Loan, Arrears, Other), per benefit group, for any one HA
  • There can only ever be one current Rent of Loan deduction per benefit group
  • There can be more than one current Arrears or Other Housing Payment (OHP) instruction, but these have to be for different HAs or different benefit groups
  • When there is more than one ARR or OHP the following hierarchy must be applied:
    • HA in the state or territory where the customer resides
    • By date of effect, with the earliest date first

6

Cancelled Centrelink payments + Read more ...

When a customer's Centrelink payment is cancelled or suspended, the RDS deduction instruction is kept current for the standard 'grace' period (13 weeks for all payments). If the customer returns to payment within that period the deduction instruction will be re-activated automatically and pay to the HA from the customers next pay day.

The deduction that recommences will not include any arrears that may be owed.

If the customer does not return to payment within the grace period of 13 weeks, the deduction will cancel. The HA will receive a termination advice (ITI) on their next deduction report for the instruction.

7

RDS liaison/help + Read more ...

Service Officers who have enquiries relating to the system should contact National Business Gateway - Customer Confirmation Services helpdesk to see if the situation can be resolved. See the Resources page for a link to the RDS contacts and the mailbox address.

This mailbox is monitored daily, and the helpdesk will aim to provide a response within standard response times.

There is an Account Manager available in every State/Territory who is responsible for liaison and communication between the Service Centre, National Support Office and the Housing Authorities.

See the Resources page for a link to the contacts for RDS who can provide further assistance.

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Privacy provisions + Read more ...

Once the customer is signed up by the HA a range of information can be shared between the agency and the HA in connection with a customer's involvement in RDS.

2

Type of information which may be exchanged + Read more ...

All information contained in the deduction consent forms used by HAs can be exchanged between the agency and the HA. This includes details of a customer's:

  • type of payment and Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • name, address, telephone number and date of birth
  • type and amount of deduction(s)

If considered appropriate, other information given to the agency by HAs may be acted upon, even if not specifically intended by the customer for that purpose.

For example, a customer applies to a HA to participate in the RDS. As a tenant in government housing, they are not eligible for Rent Assistance (RA). However the customer then attends a Service Centre and claims RA on the basis that they are in private rental accommodation at a different address. In this situation the agency is entitled to act upon the information provided by the HA.
