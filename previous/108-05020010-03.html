Code other income on Other Income (OIN, OINS). It will be included in the assessment of the customer's rate of payment.

Is this an income protection or sickness policy paid from a superannuation fund?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No,
    For income protection or sickness policies that are not paid from a superannuation fund, a Compensation and damages form (MOD C) must be completed. When returned, request a compensation clearance. The Compensation Recovery Team will advise if the income should be coded on Other Income (OIN, OINS) screen. Do not update until this advice is received

This cannot be done at a Smart Centre Call.

  • If the customer is advising of a change to the amount of payments already recorded on OIN/OINS, see Assessment of income for Centrelink payments. This contains information on income protection or sickness accident policies
  • If a compensation payment is already coded on OIN/OINS, and the amount does not need updating then procedure ends here
  • If the Compensation Recovery Team has already approved the payment(s) to be recorded as Other Income, go to Step 5

For information on the treatment of a lump sum arrears payment from an income protection or sickness accident policy (not paid from superannuation), see Resources.

3

Salary continuance or income protection payments from superannuation + Read more ...

The following benefits paid from a superannuation fund are not treated as compensation:

  • salary continuance
  • income protection
  • disability income benefits

If unsure, check with the Level 2 Policy helpdesk.

Record these payments on OIN/OINS. They include:

  • regular ongoing payments
  • lump sum arrears, paid for a period before the start of regular ongoing payments, and
    • assessed from the date of receipt over a period of not more than 52 weeks
    • the assessment period is determined by the past period for which the lump sum was paid. For example, a lump sum payment of income protection arrears for a 6 month period is assessed for 26 weeks from the date of receipt of the payment

The assessable amount is the gross income less any adjustments for the offsets applied by the insurance company.

Before commencing the update, check Income Streams (SUPS, SUPI, SUPV). In the past, these payments were recorded as product type DIS.

Does a SUPS record already exist?

  • Yes:
    • Edit the appropriate record on SUPS, SUPI, SUPV and move it to OIN, OINS when the customer provides an update to the payment
    • Edit the product on SUPS, SUPI, SUPV
    • In the Product Cancellation Reason: field, code 'OTH'
    • Update Header Data with Receipt Date and Channel and select Save
    • The payment is then moved to OIN, OINS in the same activity
    • Go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Royalties, commissions or contract payments + Read more ...

Royalties received by an individual from a community, for example, royalties paid to an indigenous community, are treated as employment income if they are then distributed to individuals in that community. The income is coded as annual (ANN) and assessed for 52 weeks from the date received.

For customers who are commission or contract workers, or receive royalties and are considered to be self-employed:

  • The assessed income is the net income after allowable expenses have been deducted
  • If the customer receives lump sums from a number of contracts with the same company each instance of income will need to be coded with a unique Description.

For commissions, contracting and royalties income recorded on OIN, OINS (type ROC) the income is assessable for 52 weeks and coded with a Frequency of ANN.

If the income is a one off and received as a lump sum it will be assessed as income for a period of 52 weeks from the date it was able to be received. The frequency will be ANN.

If income is not a one off and received as a lump sum:

  • for pensions strike an annual net rate of income, taking into consideration any other income from the same source, and coded with a Frequency of ANN
  • for allowances, maintain the net income as an annual (ANN) amount

If the customer is eligible for the Work Bonus the percentage of their income that has been earned from personal exertion will need to be coded. For both employment income and self-employment, the default Work Bonus Income %: percentage is to be coded as 100%.

Go to Step 5

5

Coding advised income + Read more ...

For information on the treatment of lump sum arrears payments from income protection and sickness accident policies (not paid from superannuation), see Resources.

Note: a customer could receive more than one payment of the same type from the same provider. An example of how this should be recorded is:

  • Code the first amount on the OIN screen (for example, Grant or Scholarship - xxx University - $1,000 - ANN - with correct DOV)
  • Code the second amount on the OIN screen with a slightly different description name (for example, Grant or Scholarship - xxx University 2 - $1,000 - ANN - with correct DOV)

This will ensure the system correctly assesses both payments.

If the customer is unable to provide the information needed to complete the OIN, OINS update, go to Step 7

Go to OIN, OINS and code:

  • Type: enter the appropriate type Code or 'OTH'. For example for salary continuance or income protection payment from superannuation, code 'OTH'
  • Description: free text to give a description of the other income (30 character limit). For salary continuance or income protection payment from super enter the type of payment and source
  • Reference: enter any reference number for the income type/ source (12 character limit)
  • Start Date: date of event for the change or start of an assessment period
  • Gross Amount $: amount of other income
  • Freq: the frequency the customer receives the amount of other income:
  • For lump sum payments or lump sum arrears of salary continuance or income protection payments from superannuation which are assessed over the period to which the payment relates, use a frequency appropriate to the period of payment (for example, 2WE, MTH, etc.)
  • Due to a known system error, avoid using frequency ‘One’. Use other frequencies as long as the amount and frequency produce correct rate.
    For example, the customer receives $200 each week, code, for the period of payment, Amount $: '400' and Freq: '2WE'
  • Work Bonus %-if the other income type is eligible to have the Work Bonus applied, the Work Bonus income % will display:
  • If the customer is eligible for Work Bonus the percentage of their employment or self-employment income from royalty, commission or contract income (type ROC) that has been earned from personal exertion is to be recorded
  • The default is to code 100%
  • Select Save
  • Update Header Data with Receipt Date and Channel and select Save

Note: if a customer already has an assessment for an amount from the same source on the OIN screen:

  • Do not code another on that line, unless
  • The assessment of the previous coding has stopped

For example:

  • If there is coding from 1 January 2020 to be assessed for 1 year, and
  • Another payment has been received on 1 June 2020, then
  • Create a new line for the 1 June 2020 payment to record cessation of payment. At Income Ceased (end dated), code:
    • Start Date as the date of change
    • Amount as zero
  • Select Assess
  • Check Entitlement (ELD) and select Finish
  • Notes are auto-populated with the income and asset update and is replicated in Customer Record/Customer First
  • Select Finalise

6

Set up a review + Read more ...

Code a review to check or zero the income after the period the income is to affect the customer.

  • For pensioners:
  • a manual review for 52 weeks is not needed if income is coded as 'ANN' on OINS using the 'PEN' system, as the system automatically creates the review for 12 months to cease the assessment
  • If the income is not coded as 'ANN', use the help facility ('?') in the RevRsn: field to find a valid reason code for the review
  • For more information, see How to code and action a manual review
  • For other customers:
  • code a review for the applicable date in the future to review or delete the income, using a review reason of INC on the Review Registration (RVR) screen
  • For example, if the income was received on 1 May 2017 and is to be assessed for 6 weeks, the review should be set for 12 June 2017 to zero off the income at the end of the 6 week period

Check that any income coded previously using an ongoing assessment code (for example, MTH, ANN, etc.) on OIN has been reduced to $0 from the end of its assessment period.

7

Insufficient information + Read more ...

  • Issue Income and Assets form (MOD iA)
  • If customer has minor changes in circumstances, record available details on NOTES with a resubmit date of 14 days and ask customer to lodge details in writing
  • Advise customer to provide details within 14 days
  • Record details on a NOTE
  • Code a review with a resubmit date of 14 days for the service centre to follow-up

Procedure ends here until the information needed is available.
