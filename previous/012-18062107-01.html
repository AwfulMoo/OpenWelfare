This document outlines information about eligibility and registration requirements for psychologists.

Psychologists

Psychologists work in diverse areas such as:

  • providing assessment and therapy to clients
  • helping facilitate organisational or social change
  • conducting psychological research
  • administering psychological tests to individuals or groups.

For Medicare purposes, eligible psychologists can be either (or both) a:

  • psychologist
    • has a degree in psychology and holds general registration with the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (Ahpra)
  • clinical psychologist
    • has general registration with an additional endorsement indicating ‘Approved area of practice – clinical psychology’ or ‘Endorsement for Clinical Psychology’
    • provides advanced expertise in the assessment and treatment of mental illness and behavioural problems

Note: for the purposes of this document, the generic use of the term psychologist covers both psychologists and clinical psychologists.

Disputes about removing a registration

Direct any disputes about registration to Ahpra. The Resources page has the contact details.

Non-directive Pregnancy Support Counselling (PSC)

Psychologists can also undertake additional training in non-directive pregnancy support counselling (PSC) with the Australian Psychological Society (APS).

Unlike clinical psychologist endorsement, PSC endorsement is not recorded on a psychologist’s Ahpra registration, only on their Medicare Provider Directory System (PDS) record.

The Australian Psychological Society (APS) advises Services Australia if a psychologist has undertaken PSC training and have been granted a certificate of completion. Endorsement is then recorded on PDS.

Medicare eligibility and registration

To be registered as a psychologist with Medicare, an applicant must:

  • be currently registered to practice with the Psychology Board of Australia. This is a board supported by the Ahpra

Evidence of registration may be at least 1 of the below:

  • Copy of the Ahpra registration certificate with the Initial application for a provider number
  • Registration details prepopulated within the ‘shell’ record in PDS
  • Ahpra Information window (if available), or
  • Ahpra website search result

Note:

  • Psychologists are required to renew their registration annually on 30 November
  • Medicare benefits cannot be paid for services provided or requested when they are not registered with Ahpra
  • Gaps in registration results in gaps in eligibility to access Medicare

Endorsement

  • Clinical psychologist
    • Ahpra registration will have Approved area of practice – clinical or Endorsement for Clinical Psychology displayed in Endorsement section
  • Non-Directive Pregnancy Support Counselling (PSC)
    • The Australian Psychological Society (APS) advises the Services Australia the details of who has completed this training
    • Registration is recorded on the Medicare provider stem in the PDS

Further confirmation of completion (such as a certificate) is not required if PDS shows a current Australian (AUS) registration in the Registration section of the health professional’s record.

Enquiry escalation

The Provider Registration enquiry line and email inbox, managed by Medicare Provider Services (MPS), are used as the front door for all enquiries relating to the processing of provider registrations.

Enquiries to this area are responded to by a Service Officer trained in all aspects of provider registration. These Service Officers raise complex enquiries to a Programme Support Officer (PSO) for assistance. Where required, the enquiry will be escalated to a Programme Officer (PO), who will liaise with the Provider Eligibility team in National Office for assistance.

The Resources page contains a quick reference guide, contact details, links to an example certificate and external websites.
