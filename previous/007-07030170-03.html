2

Processing PPL/DAP claims outside of claim lodgement timeframes + Read more ...

In most cases PPL and DAP claims must be lodged within 12 months of the date the child was born or came into care as part of the adoption process.

Additionally, for children that are born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020:

  • PPL claims can be lodged by a secondary or tertiary claimants up to 24 months after the date the child was born or came into care as part of the adoption process
  • Flexible PPL days can be accessed up to 24 months after the date the child was born or came into care as part of the adoption process. However to have access to a balance of Flexible PPL days, the primary claimant must claim PPL:
    • within 12 months of the date the child was born or came into care as part of the adoption process, and
    • be eligible for Flexible PPL days

In limited circumstances, a claim for PPL or DAP may need to be processed outside of claim lodgement timeframes.

  • A review of decision has resulted in a favourable decision for the customer
  • A Ministerial decision which has resulted in a changed outcome
  • Act of Grace ruling which has resulted in a changed outcome, or
  • The customer lodged a claim inside of the claim lodgement timeframes, however a system issue was identified that resulted in the claim not able to be granted
  • Correction to incorrect data, resulting in an inappropriate rejection or non-effective claim

Generally this will be following a review of decision of a rejected or not effective claim.

Does the claim meet the criteria for a PPL or DAP claim to be processed outside of claim lodgement timeframes?

  • Yes, go to Step 3.
  • No,
    • Override referral is not required. Continue claim processing. See Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Parental Leave Payments
    • Procedure ends here

3

Create new claim using Assisted Customer Claim (ACC)/Social Online Application (SOA) workflow + Read more ...

To process a claim outside the lodgement timeframes a manual claim must be indexed in either Customer First or Process Direct.

Customer First

Using Customer First, a new claim must be processed and created using the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) workflow and the details provided by the customer in their original claim must be used for the ACC.

  • Date of Receipt (DOR): date of the review of the decision (do not backdate the DOR)
  • Source: as appropriate
  • All information provided in the customer’s original claim must be used. Note: to enter the customer’s nominated start date, always select Other date from the drop down box and manually enter the date of the review of decision, as the start date of the PPL. Selecting date of claim will cause the claim activity to reject
  • Any additional information known as a result of the review must be updated in the new claim activity e.g. customer returned to work or transferred primary care in their original PPL period
  • (For PPL only) Employer details must be recorded. However an employer determination will not be made and PPL will be delivered by the agency in all override cases
  • For children born on or after 1 July 2020, do not include any connected or Flexible PPL days in the ACC new claim. This will result in an error when attempting to process the override information

After all ACC actions have been completed and all claim information has been updated, complete the Finalise Claim workflow.

The PPL/DAP claim warning ‘E913P1 - Claim for child over 12 months in the past’ will be presented on the Assessment Warning (AWE) screen.

Process Direct

Using Process Direct, a new claim must be created and processed using a new Social Online Application (SOA) shell.

To create a blank SOA shell for a claim:

  • Select SOA Shell from the Process Direct Landing page
  • Key the appropriate details in these fields:
    • Benefit Type : Paid Parental Leave or Dad and Partner Pay
    • Customer CRN
    • Date of Receipt (DOR): date of the review of the decision (do not backdate the DOR)
  • Select Create Claim
  • All information provided in the customer’s original claim must be used
    Note: to enter the customers nominated start date, always select Other date and manually enter the date of the review of decision as the start date of the PPL. Selecting date of claim will cause an error
  • Any additional information known as a result of the review must be updated in the new claim activity e.g. customer returned to work or transferred primary care in their original PPL period
  • (For PPL only) Employer details must be recorded. However an employer determination will not be made and PPL will be delivered by the agency in all override cases
  • For children born on or after 1 July 2020, do not include any connected or Flexible PPL days in the new claim. This will result in an error when attempting to process the override information

After all information has been completed and all claim information has been updated, assess the Claim.

The PPL/DAP claim warning ‘E913P1 - Claim for child over 12 months in the past’ will be presented on the Entitlement (ELD) screen.

5

Processing PPL claims under exceptional circumstances for an Additional Primary Claimant + Read more ...

The primary claimant will most often be the birth mother or the initial primary carer of an adopted or surrogate child.

Sometimes, PPL may be payable to a new primary claimant after the birth mother/primary carer has received PPL payments.

Example

  • The birth mother or initial primary carer is no longer able to care for the child, and
  • additional circumstances have been met, and
  • evidence has been provided by a new primary carer, and
  • all other PPL eligibility is met

Does the claimant meet the exceptional circumstances criteria?

  • Yes, ensure that updates have been made to the Child in Primary Care screen and the Flexible PPL days for each carer:
    • For the previous primary carer check or code the Child in Primary Care screen with Primary care ‘No’ and Reason ‘OTH’ from the date the child left their primary care. This should be done in a separate activity, see Customer notifies a change of circumstances for Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
    • For the previous primary claimant and/or secondary claimants, check or code the Flexible PPL days as withdrawn with reason ‘OTH’ using the PPL circumstances guided procedure, see Customer notifies a change of circumstances for Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
    • For the new primary carer code ‘Yes’ from the date the child entered their primary care within the PPL claim
    • Go to Step 6
  • No:
    • Override referral is not required. Continue claim processing. See Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Parental Leave Payments
    • Procedure ends here

  • if they received FTB Part B for the same period PPL is being paid

Manual action is not required and all letters relating to overpayment must not be inhibited.

7

Override referral + Read more ...

Authority to record an override is restricted to a Families Override Officer. The override must be recorded before the claim can be finalised. There are a number of different override options available:

  • O1Y (Over 1 Year) for PPL claims (children born or adopted before 1 July 2020 only)
  • FLO (Flexible Override) for PPL claims (children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020 only)
  • APC (Additional Primary Claimant) for PPL claims (children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020 only)
  • OTH (Other) when no other code can be applied for DAP or PPL (children born or adopted before 1 July 2020 only)
    Note: OTH is only available in Process Direct in limited circumstances

Service Officers in:

  • face to face, complete a Families Override Request
  • smart centres, complete a Direct referral to SSO

See the Resources page for a link to the Families Override Contacts Program webform(s), to create a referral.

Include the following information in the referral to the Families Override Contact:

  • type of override required (O1Y/FLO/APC/OTH)
  • reason for the override (e.g. review of decision)
  • PPL/DAP start and end date. It is essential the correct dates are recorded on this screen. This will calculate the rate of payment to be issued to the customer. Note: if the customer is claiming any connected Flexible PPL days these can be within the total start and end dates
  • all dates/range of dates nominated by customer for all Flexible PPL days (not connected) to the PPL period

Note: where more than one override needs to be recorded for the same child e.g. transfer portion of PPL to the customer's partner, record an override on both PPL records. Start dates and end dates will need to be entered for each customer, and must meet eligibility rules, i.e. no break in payment for the PPL period.

DOC the record with details of the claim and override decision, and the above information.

Note: any inappropriate referrals will be returned to the initiating Service Officer.

Once the Families Override Officer has coded the override, go to Step 8.

8

Claim activity returned + Read more ...

Once the Families Override Contact Officer has recorded the Override screen/s, the claim activity will be returned to the initiating Service Officer to finalise and review the claim activity.

Contact the customer to:

  • confirm their bank account details, and update if necessary
  • advise them that the agency will issue the PPL payment to them
  • advise them that their payment is being issued in the current financial year and they may need to check if requested tax deduction is sufficient

If the customer has received NBS and NBU for the child or if they were paid FTB Part B during their PPL period or on any Flexible PPL days, advise them that they may have been overpaid.

They will receive more information about this when the overpayment is raised.

Within the new claim activity, check the result.

In Customer First

Go to the Claim Summary (P1CS) screen and select the PPL Period Calculation (P1RCI) screen to confirm the payment amounts and dates. Place an ‘R’ next to the claim line and then ‘I’ to navigate to the P1RCI.

Has the customer previously been paid PPL/DAP for this child and a debt shell been raised due to a previous rejection activity?

  • Yes. The customer’s PPL/DAP arrears must be adjusted for the same period in which a debt shell has been raised after the rejection activity has been finalised. To reduce the arrears within a new claim:
    • On the Assessment Results (AR) screen, on the left hand menu select the relevant payment type
    • Select Rate Details (RATS)
    • Select relevant periods on the Daily Rate Component (RAC) and key ‘0’ in the Man Adj $ field and ‘ADJ’ in the Man Adj Rsn field
    • Navigate to the AR screen to check the outcome. Once all required actions have been updated, the override recorded and all claim information has been updated, the claim can be finalised. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 9

In Process Direct

After selecting Assess, go to the PPL Calculation Summary (P1RCS) screen to confirm the payment amounts and dates for PPL/DAP.

Has the customer previously been paid PPL/DAP for this child, and a debt shell is on the AL/OPDL due to a previous rejection activity?

  • Yes. The customer’s PPL/DAP arrears must be adjusted for the same period the debt shell has been created for. To reduce the arrears within a new claim:
    • Key ‘RAC’ in the SuperKey field
    • On the Customer Daily Rate Component (RAC) screen, go to the relevant date range for the debt that was generated by the rejection activity, key ‘0’ in the $ Man Adj field and ‘ADJ’ in the Man Adj Reason field for the relevant dates
    • Select Update to save the updates
    • Navigate to the ELD screen to check the outcome. Once all required actions have been updated, the override recorded and all claim information has been updated, the claim can be finalised via the entitlement (ELD) screen. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Finalise Claim + Read more ...

In Customer First

The Finalise Claim workflow is run once all required actions have been taken, the override recorded and all claim information has been updated. The workflow presents any errors or warnings on the Assessment Warning and Error (AWE) screen. These may need to be actioned before the claim can be finalised.

Once all errors have been rectified, the workflow will present the Assessment Results (AR) screen where the new claim can be finalised.

After selecting Finalise and Continue, select Finish in the top right corner to complete the workflow and return to the Online Claim.

The Finalise Claim workflow will now show as Completed in the Staff Workflows.

For more information on finalising an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), see Viewing and processing online and assisted customer claims.

Add a DOC to the record, which includes information about the override and eligibility checks that have been completed.

In Process Direct

Once all required actions have been taken, the override recorded and all claim information has been updated, the claim can be finalised via the entitlement (ELD) screen.

Add details of the override in the automatic NOTES/DOC.

Inhibited letters and issuing a Q999 letter + Read more ...

PPL and DAP letters will be automatically inhibited as a part of the claim activity. Where the customer also received FTB Part B during the PPL period or Flexible PPL days, an FTB letter will also generate. This letter should not be inhibited. A Q999 letter should always be sent to the customer as a notice of the decision to grant the claim. See the Resources page for approved letter text. Letters will need to be issued in Customer First.

Authorised Review Officers (ARO) and the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) will issue all relevant letters in cases reviewed by them. No additional advices need to be issued when finalising the claim in those cases.

Procedure ends here.

Flexible PPL days only (no PPL period) - APC + Read more ...

This process is also used when an APC is claiming additional available Flexible PPL days after their initial PPL claim has been granted and coded on the P1FOV.

Customer First:

Select the PPL new claim activity, navigate to the P1FOV screen by placing a ‘Y’ in the Add/View/Withdraw Flexible Days at the bottom of the P1OV screen.

Update:

  • Override Reason: 'APC'
  • Period Start date:
  • Period End Date: (Note: if entering a single Flexible day the end date must be the same as the start date)
  • Notes: (mandatory) - details of override reason/decision

Process Direct:

  • Select the SOA shell activity
  • Key 'P1OV' in the SuperKey field to go to the PPL/DAP override screen

Update:

  • Select Add/Change Override
  • Override reason: ‘APC’
  • Suppress letter: BTH (customer and employer)
  • Paymaster: FAO (this will default)
  • Notes: mandatory details of override decision
  • Select Flexible Days only

Add Flexible days that need to be added:

  • On the Add Flexible section
  • Date Selection:
    • select Single Day if adding one day or multiple days that are not consecutive, or
    • select Date Range if adding more than one day in a row
  • Enter the Start Date either manually or via the calendar then select Add Day(s)
  • If there are more days to add, repeat for each new day
  • For Date Range select a start date and an end date and then select Add Day(s)
    Note: date range will include weekend days

Select Save at the bottom of the page.

On the P1OV screen, the PPL day count will reflect what has just been entered. Check the PPL day count is correct before continuing.
