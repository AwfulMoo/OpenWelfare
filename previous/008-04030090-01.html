This document outlines information about the Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE), previously known as Disability Supported Employment (DSE), Business Services or sheltered employment.

Services provided by ADE

ADE employ and support people with disabilities, injuries or illnesses often in specialist working environments.

The ADE program is targeted at customers with disabilities, injuries or illnesses that are permanent, or likely to be permanent, and result in a need for ongoing support services to gain and maintain employment or access supported employment.

Supported employment services approach

The supported employment services approach may include:

  • Mobile work crews usually operating as small businesses that employ people with disabilities to work in crews to carry out contract work such as gardening or cleaning
  • Enclaves are small groups of people with disabilities who are employed by the business service and who work in specialist units within a commercial business. Workers receive continuing support to help them with their tasks and to help them integrate with other workers. Note: an enclave may also operate under the open employment service approach
  • Small businesses operating as businesses employing small groups of workers with disabilities to carry out specific activities such as maintenance, assembly work or coffee shops. Specific businesses may be set up to provide employment opportunities for people with disabilities

Eligibility assessment for ADE

Services Australia plays no part in assessing eligibility for ADE, although a customer's likely suitability for ADE services may be indicated in a current and valid Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) or Employment Services Assessment (ESAt). The Assessor's referral recommendation (including ADE) will be displayed in the JCA or ESAt outcomes DOC after the report is accepted, and can also be viewed in the Recommendations section of the JCA or ESAt report.

Customers living in a National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) launch site (who meet the relevant age criteria for that site) should be referred to the National Disability Insurance Agency (NDIA) for all enquiries about possible eligibility to ADE

Customers not living in a NDIS launch site, the assessment of eligibility for ADE rests with the employer.

Customers without mutual obligation requirements

Customers without mutual obligation requirements and not living in a NDIS launch site may be interested in referral to ADE. These customers should discuss this with the local ADE employer.

If the employer agrees to accept the customer, they should notify Services Australia about the referral. Service Officers are required to code the referral on the customer's record if the customer is claiming or receiving an income support payment. No further action is required.

Job seekers with mutual obligation requirements

ADE is an approved activity, which means that customers receiving a participation payment such as Jobseeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) satisfy their mutual obligation requirements while they are participating in the program.

Customers with mutual obligation requirements and not living in a NDIS launch site should only be referred to ADE if this is recommended by a current and valid JCA. This is because:

  • referral to and participation in ADE becomes an activity to meet the customer's mutual obligation requirements, and needs to be manually included by a Service Officer in the customer's Job Plan, and
  • a decision by Services Australia to require the customer to participate in this service should be based on an expert recommendation that this is the most suitable service for the customer's needs

Referral to ADE

An Assessor conducting a JCA or ESAt may recommend referral to ADE. If the Assessor connects the customer to an ADE employer, the ADE referral needs to be coded on the customer's record after the JCA or ESAt report is submitted. Recording of ADE referrals is a manual process as the information is not auto populated.

Customers who hold temporary visas are not eligible for long-term or resource intensive programs such as ADE, see Employment services for visa holders.

Customers who are not in a National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) launch site can register directly with an ADE employer at any time. Service Officers can assist the customer by providing information about local ADE employers.

Customers who live in a NDIS launch site and meet the relevant age criteria for that site, any ADE referral is managed by the NDIA. Customers who are in a NDIS launch site and meet the relevant age criteria for that site and are interested in a referral to ADE should be referred directly to the NDIA.

Placements in ADE are manually coded by using the Referral Summary workflow. This is a record of whether the customer is participating in ADE, based on advice from the provider or NDIS.

DSP customers with participation requirements

DSP customers will automatically be excluded from having participation requirements if they are participating in ADE.

Upon cessation of ADE, if the customer meets the DSP participation requirements criteria they may be required to attend DSP participation interviews and sign a DSP Participation Plan.

Community Development Program (CDP)

If the customer lives in a Department of Education, Skills and Employment designated remote area, they are referred to a Community Development Program (CDP) provider. The CDP provider will provide similar services as ADE. The activities will be recorded in the customer's Job Plan.

Employer Electronic Reporting (EER)

Customers employed with an ADE employer may have their employment income reported through the Employer Electronic Reporting (EER) process. ADE report customer's employment income electronically to Services Australia (NBG/Online Support) either weekly or fortnightly depending on the employers pay cycle, via a secure transfer mechanism. The data is supplied in a format that can be automatically applied to the corresponding record, and correct payments can be made.

All employment income information provided by the ADE employer are fully verified (FVE). Services Australia can receive employment income information from ADE employers any day in the fortnight because of the Electronic Earnings Reporting (EEO) channel type. This means that employment income does not have to be updated on Entitlement Period End Date (EPED).

Customers are required to complete the Employer Reporting - Customer Consent (SS407) form to allow the ADE employer to provide the customer's employment details through EER. There will be a FLASH DOC on every customer's record participating in EER. This alerts Service Officers not to update or alter the customer's employment information.

Note: ADE employers are responsible for submitting the Customer Consent form. If forms are issued directly to a customer, tell them to lodge the form with their employer.

The ADE employer signs a Privacy and Security Agreement, to allow Services Australia to receive this information. The stimulus (STM) is put to OFF because the customer no longer has to report. This process saves the customers from reporting employment income individually and reduces the need for reviews. Questions relating to employer reporting and the coding of customer records should be directed to the National Business Gateway.

BSWAT payment scheme

The Business Service Wage Assessment Tool (BSWAT) payments scheme was established for supported employees with an intellectual disability, working in an Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE) who previously had their wages assessed under BSWAT.

The Resources page contains contact details for National Business Gateway.
