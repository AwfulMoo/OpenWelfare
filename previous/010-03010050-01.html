Completing the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Organisation details (SY067) 010-03010050

This document outlines information about how to complete the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Organisation details (SY067) which is used by a non-government organisation together with the Claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) (SY040) to apply for AIC for a student.

When to use this form

Claims for AIC can only be accepted from an organisation that does not receive a foster care or similar allowance for the student.

The organisation must have full responsibility for the upkeep of the student. This can either be due to:

  • placement of the student in their care by a state/territory authority or court, or
  • where the student's parents are not involved in decisions about the care of the student, do not provide any financial support for the student and are either unable to be contacted to complete the form or are unwilling to do so.

If the organisation is accepted as the applicant, the income test for Additional Boarding Allowance may be waived.

Organisation details

An organisation may receive AIC for a student who:

  • normally lives at an institution but the institution is geographically isolated from an appropriate state school
  • resides full-time at an institution because of the nature and extent of a disability or illness and the institution has special facilities to cater for the disability or illness.

An organisation may also receive AIC (as a non-applicant) on behalf of a parent/guardian applicant for a student who boards there during the school term if a geographic isolation rule is met with regard to the student's access to an appropriate state school from their parents' home.

Who should complete this form

Along with the Claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) (SY040) the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Organisation details (SY067) must be signed by the chief officer (or other officer with power to act on behalf of the organisation). By signing Claim form (SY040), the officer accepts the applicant notification responsibilities on behalf of the organisation and responsibility for any overpayment which may occur.

The Resources page contains links to forms for an organisation to claim AIC.
