3

Student has ceased study + Read more ...

A student may end their course of study by:

  • completing the course, or
  • choosing (or being compelled) to cease study before completing the course

Completing the course

This normally means that the date study ended was anticipated and recorded by Services Australia. Any Services Australia administered payments for students would cancel automatically from the day following the appropriate student end date (except for recipients of Youth Allowance (YA) 22 years of age as well as AIC and ABSTUDY secondary school students). Go to Step 4.

Choosing (or being compelled) to cease study before completing the course

This will usually not have been anticipated by Services Australia and the Service Officer would have to take active steps to avoid an overpayment. For further information, see:

  • Payment of ABSTUDY when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course
  • Payment of Youth Allowance (YA) and Austudy when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course
  • Deferring or discontinuing study before end of course for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme

If a YA (Student) or ABSTUDY student ceases full-time study they must notify the agency and register as a job seeker within 14 days in order to receive backdating of JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or YA (Job seeker) if they are eligible. Failure to notify cessation of study within 14 days will result in cancellation of YA and ABSTUDY from study end date + 1 day. Payment of JSP/YA (Job seeker) may only commence from the date the claim was submitted.

If a tertiary or secondary non-school student receiving ABSTUDY discontinues full-time study in their course but is not claiming payment of YA (Job seeker) or JSP, ABSTUDY is cancelled from the study end date plus 1 day. This is regardless of when they advised the change or when Services Australia became aware of this.

A debt may be generated for students. See Raising debts for students. There is no change to policy or process for customers advising an intention to study. See Satisfying the activity test for customers who are intending to study for Youth Allowance (YA).

Extra checks need to be undertaken whenever a Services Australia scholarship recipient is advising a change to a study end date. For more information, see: Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding.

Procedure ends here.

4

The student has completed the course (or was expected to have completed the course) and is not intending to return to study in the next available study period + Read more ...

If the student has completed the course (or was expected to have completed the course) they will usually have been sent an:

  • 'You need to act now' letter for Austudy and YA customers, or
  • Information required for continuation of Pensioner Education Supplement or ABSTUDY (SY075) for ABSTUDY and Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) customers)

prior to the anticipated student end date.

If an AIC student has completed the study year of their course (or is expected to complete the study year) the applicant (usually a parent/guardian) will usually have been sent an AIC End of Year circumstance review prior to the anticipated student end date.

Compare the pre-printed course end date on the form/letter or as coded on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen and the actual course end date as reported by the customer. Extra checks need to be undertaken whenever a Services Australia scholarship recipient is advising a change to a study end date. For more information, see: Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding.

If the student's current course or study in their current course is:

  • finishing earlier than anticipated, go to Step 5
  • finishing later than anticipated, go to Step 6
  • finishing on the date anticipated, go to Step 9

5

Course or study in course finishing earlier than anticipated + Read more ...

Where the customer’s course will finish earlier than the date shown on the EDC screen:

  • Update the EDC screen to correct the:
    • Student End Date field, and
    • Inst Course End Date field (if the Inst course end date has changed) with the earlier date (no verification is required for this update).
      Note: ABSTUDY secondary school students or AIC students remain eligible until 31 December even where the course finishes earlier than anticipated (see Step 6 in this table)

Where the customer’s study in their course will finish earlier than the date shown on the EDC screen, only the Student End Date field should be updated. The Inst Course End Date should remain the date the course will finish, despite the student finishing study earlier.

  • For Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), the assessment must be done by Smart Centre Processing. Look up local Processing Service details for: PES - Pensioner Education Supplement (DEEWR). Procedure ends here
  • For YA and Austudy customers, update the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen to record the customer as 'Not a Student' (NST) from the date recorded on the Student End Date field + 1 day
  • Investigate the possibility of an overpayment. If necessary, see Raising debts for students
  • If a YA (student) or ABSTUDY student ceases full-time study (including when they finish their course), they must notify Services Australia and register as a job seeker within 14 days in order to receive backdating of JSP or YA (Job seeker) if they are eligible for those payments. Failure to notify cessation of study within 14 days will result in cancellation of YA or ABSTUDY from study end date + 1 day. Payment of YA (Job seeker) or JSP may only commence from the date the claim was submitted. A debt may be generated for students who fail to notify cessation of studies

See Step 1 in Table 2

6

Allowable time assessment + Read more ...

Before extending the student end date on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen, Service Officers should consider the allowable time rules.

Note: AIC eligibility is not affected by allowable time or previous study rules.

Will the projected 'new' student end date still be within the allowable time end date? See Determining allowable time/reasonable time for a course of study.

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, an allowable/reasonable time assessment must be completed:
    • For YA, see Disregarding previous study for Youth Allowance (YA)
    • For Austudy or PES, see Disregarding previous study for ABSTUDY, ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement, Austudy and Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
    • For ABSTUDY, see Determining progress and duration of assistance for ABSTUDY

If the assessment cannot be completed at first contact as further information is required or the Service Officer is not trained, send an ACTDOC with the details to the relevant processing team:

  • For YA and Austudy, this is SNN (Student National Network)
  • For PES, this is APT (National PES Processing)
  • For ABSTUDY or ABSTUDY PES, this is ABS (National ABSTUDY Processing)

If the allowable time assessment can be completed at the first point of contact, and the student is still eligible for payment, go to Step 7.

7

Continuing/intending student assessment + Read more ...

Students who are continuing the same course in the next available study period, must meet the continuing/intending student rules.

Does the student meet the continuing/intending student rules?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, and:
    • the customer is under 22 years of age and wants to continue to receive YA as a job seeker, see Student to job seeker transfers
    • the customer is under 22 years of age and does not require further assistance from Services Australia, go to Step 10
    • the customer is 22 years of age or over and requires further assistance, refer the customer to the Payment and Service Finder (see Resources for a link) and advise the customer to complete a new claim as soon as possible
    • the customer is 22 years of age or over and does not require further assistance from the agency, go to Step 9

8

Continuing entitlement + Read more ...

Does the customer have continuing entitlement as a student for their current course?

Note: ABSTUDY secondary school students and AIC students who complete the school year are entitled to payment up to 31 December of that year.

  • Yes, update the EDC screen by changing the Inst Course End Date and Student End Date fields to the new date
    • For YA or Austudy students only, go to the NES screen. If NST is coded, delete the NST code
    • Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen (if required)
    • Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here
  • No, see Step 1 Table 2

9

Course finishing on the date anticipated + Read more ...

No additional coding of the EDC screen is required and the student payment will cancel automatically the day after the student end date.

For YA or Austudy only students, go to the NES screen. If NST is not coded, code the fields on the NES screen with:

  • DOV: the customer’s last date of full-time study plus one
  • Student Status: NST
  • Educ Level Attained: as per advised
  • Year 12/Equiv Verified: NVE (Not verified) (unless already verified)
  • Finalise the activity on the AR screen

Note: if the course finished on the expected date, the student is not required to return the Information required for continuation of Pensioner Education Supplement or ABSTUDY (SY075). However, if the student requires further assistance, they may contact to discuss their options.

Procedure ends here.

10

Cancellation of payment + Read more ...

If the student will not qualify for payment after their existing student end date, and they do not meet the continuing/intending student rules, their payment must be cancelled.

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen and update the fields with:
    • Svc Rsn: select the appropriate service reason
    • Action: CAN
    • Reason: NST(Not a Full-Time Student)
    • Effect Date: student end date + 1 day
  • For YA or Austudy only students, go to the NES screen. If NST is not coded, code the fields on the NES screen with:
    • DOV: the customer’s last date of full-time study plus one
    • Student Status field: NST
    • Educ Level Attained field: as per advised
    • Year 12/Equiv Verified: NVE (Not verified) (unless already verified)

Finalise the activity on the AR screen and clearly document the reason for the decision on the customer file.

Procedure ends here.
