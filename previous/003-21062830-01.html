This document explains eligibility, payment rates and claiming options for the COVID-19 Disaster Payment - New South Wales.

Due to changes in the relevant areas deemed a COVID-19 hotspot for different parts of New South Wales since August 2021, staff must carefully check the impact reason and relevant area a customer is claiming under.

The claiming period for this event has ended. Staff will only assess online claims for this event that were lodged before the claiming period ended or phone claims where special circumstances apply.

For staff not trained in processing claims, complete a cold (unannounced) transfer to 'COVID-19 Disaster Payment' in Services Australia Workspace.

If a customer calls about the status of their claim or automatic payment, see Status of claim - customer calls to enquire for what to tell the customer.

All New South Wales online claims that do not meet eligibility requirements can be rejected, except when the customer has a pending Income Support Payment.

To receive a payment

To receive a payment a customer must:

  • be unable to attend work and therefore unable to earn their usual income because of a state public health order for restricted movement, and
  • meet the general eligibility criteria

The 'unable to attend work and therefore unable to earn their usual income' criteria is different for:

  • relevant period 1 and 2 Sydney and Greater Sydney claims
  • relevant period 3 and onwards Sydney and Greater Sydney claims, and All Other Areas in NSW claim

General eligibility

For online claims for this payment, do not contact customers to confirm online claim details unless advised to do so by the Level 2 Helpdesk.

Do not follow this procedure for any other event.

A customer qualifies if they meet all of the following on the same day for at least 1 day in the relevant period:

  • an Australian resident (through legal permanent residence and residing in Australia) or a holder of a visa class permitted to work in Australia, (to check these, see Resources for visa subclasses), and in Australia at the time of their claim
  • be at least 17 years old
  • impacted as they either:
    • live or work in a relevant area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement that is a deemed a COVID-19 hotspot by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer, or
    • were present in a COVID-19 hotspot at the time the restricted movement was declared and are now subject to a second public health order that restricts their movement in the state or territory they are currently in, or
    • live or work in a state or territory that has an area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer
  • for relevant period 1 Sydney and Greater Sydney claims only, declared they have liquid assets of less than $10,000. If a customer has $10,000 in a joint bank account, their accessible asset is $5,000. (No liquid asset criteria applies for relevant period 2 and onwards Sydney and Greater Sydney claims or All Other Areas in NSW claims)
  • would have worked on or after the eighth day of a state public health order for restricted movement in Sydney and Greater Sydney or would have worked on or after the first day of a state public health order for restricted movement in All Other Areas NSW
  • not received the COVID-19 Disaster Payment for the same relevant period, or part thereof
  • has not received or will not receive an income support payment (including a zero rate) for any day in the COVID-19 Disaster Payment relevant period they are claiming for
    Note: if a customer chooses to cancel an income support payment to receive the COVID-19 Disaster Payment, this should be for a date before the relevant period starts. 'Zero rate' does not include customers with a pending, assessed (ASS) or suspended (SUS) status. See Income support payment customers
  • has not received or will not receive any of the following payments, for any day in the COVID-19 Disaster Payment relevant period they are claiming for:
    • state pandemic payment
    • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
    • state small business payment
    • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
    • Parental Leave Pay (PPL),
      Note: staff must consider the period a payment was paid for not the date the customer received a payment
  • not receiving pandemic-related paid leave entitlements, or will use them up during the same relevant period, and
  • for any day in the COVID-19 Disaster Payment relevant period they are claiming for, their employer has not received or will not receive the Retaining Domestic Airline Capability (RDAC) payment on their behalf. Note: staff must consider the period the payment was paid for, not the date the employer received the payment on the customer’s behalf

To receive a payment, customers must also be unable to attend work and therefore unable to earn their usual income due to a state public health order for restricted movement.

The 'unable to attend work and therefore unable to earn an income' criteria is different for:

  • relevant period 1 and 2 Sydney and Greater Sydney claims
  • relevant period 3 and onwards Sydney and Greater Sydney claims and All Other Areas in NSW claims

To be eligible for the COVID-19 Disaster Payment after New South Wales reaches 80% full vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, a customer must:

  • have received a COVID-19 Disaster Payment in:
    • Sydney – relevant period 16
    • Greater Sydney – relevant periods 16
    • All Other Areas in NSW – relevant periods 14, and
  • continue to meet the eligibility criteria

A customer who is in gaol or imprisoned is not eligible for the COVID-19 Disaster Payment.

Where a customer’s employer has not allowed them to attend work and earn their usual income, as they are not vaccinated against COVID-19, the customer may be eligible if they meet all eligibility criteria.

Self-employed customers can have the same work and home address

Customers involved in a trust or company

Customers who only derive their income from a trust or company (distributions/dividends) are not eligible:

  • a customer must be working and due to the restricted movement be unable to work and therefore unable to earn an income
  • distributions/dividends will not count as employment as the restricted movement is not affecting their income

Loans made to a private trust or private company must not be considered liquid assets:

  • if customers are involved in private trusts or private companies, the liquid assets of the entity must not be considered when assessing their eligibility for this payment
  • do not refer these claims to a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO)

Company directors

A customer who is a company director, even when they draw a wage from the business, cannot receive the COVID-19 Disaster Payment if the business has received a state small business payment.

The customer may be eligible to receive the COVID-19 Disaster Payment if:

  • the business has not received a state small business payment,
  • the customer draws a wage from the business, and
  • all other eligibility criteria is met

For help assessing eligibility:

  • see the scenarios
  • contact Technical Peer Support

3 impact reasons

Due to changes in the relevant areas deemed a COVID-19 hotspot for different parts of New South Wales since August 2021, staff must carefully check which impact reason and relevant area a customer is claiming under.

COVID-19 hotspot areas are:

  • decided by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer, and
  • not the same as ‘stay at home orders’ which are decided by state/territory governments

Customers claiming the COVID-19 Disaster Payment can claim under 1 of 3 impact reasons.

Impact reason 1

Customers who live or work in a relevant area deemed a COVID-19 hotspot can apply under the following reason if they are unable to work and earn their usual income:

  • live or work in a relevant area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement that is a deemed a COVID-19 hotspot by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer

Impact reason 2

Customers who were present in a relevant area deemed a COVID-19 hotspot can apply under the following reason if they are unable to work and earn their usual income:

  • were present in a COVID-19 hotspot at the time the restricted movement was declared and are now subject to a second public health order that restricts their movement in the state or territory they are currently in

Impact reason 3

In NSW, customers not living or working in a relevant area deemed COVID-19 hotspot can apply under the following third reason if they are unable to work and earn their usual income:

  • live or work in a state or territory that has an area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer
    Note: for online claims, this shows as ‘I am impacted by a COVID-19 restricted movement/lockdown, but do not live, work and was not present in a COVID-19 hotspot area’

  • can attend work and earn their usual income, and
  • are not eligible to receive a payment

Reasons a person may be unable to attend work and earn their usual income

A person may be unable to attend work and earn their usual income for a number of reasons related to the restricted movement conditions. This includes where customers:

  • are not permitted or unable to work from home
  • cannot attend work due to caring responsibilities
  • are not vaccinated against COVID-19
  • have an exemption from wearing a mask, however, cannot work because of a state public health order mandating mask wearing (this must be because of a state public health order and not because of a condition an employer has placed on a person’s employment)

Unable to attend work and therefore unable to earn their usual income means the customer, in the relevant period, must have lost at least:

  • 8 hours of work, or
  • 1 full day of work

A full day of work does not need to be at least 8 hours.

For example:

A customer normally works a 3 hour shift during the relevant period and cannot attend that shift because of the restricted movement. The customer meets the 'unable to attend work and therefore unable to earn their usual income' criteria as they have lost 1 full day of work in the relevant period

If a customer does not lose 1 full day of work, they must lose at least 8 hours of work in the relevant period. For example:

  • A customer normally works 1 x 10 hour shift during the relevant period. They work 5 hours of that shift. The customer does not meet the 'unable to attend work and therefore unable to earn their usual income' criteria as they have not lost at least 8 hours of work or 1 full day of work in the relevant period
  • A customer normally works 2 x 10 hours shifts during the relevant period. They work 5 hours each day of those 2 shifts. The customer does meet the 'unable to attend work and therefore unable to earn their usual income' criteria as they have lost 10 hours of work in the relevant period

The payment rate the customer receives is based on hours of work lost during the relevant period, not hours the customer would have worked if the restricted movement did not occur.

Due to changes in the relevant areas deemed a COVID-19 hotspot for different parts of New South Wales since August 2021, staff must carefully check which impact reason and relevant area a customer is claiming under.

Staff must use the Geospatial tool to determine if a customer lives, works or was present in an area deemed a COVID-19 hotspot.

If a customer is claiming under the impact reason 'live or work in a state or territory that has an area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement' the customer's residential and/or employer address must be in New South Wales.

Present in

For the purposes of the COVID-19 Disaster Payment - New South Wales, the eligibility criteria covers whether a customer lives, works or has been present in (visited) a relevant area. The area must be:

  • 'declared a COVID-19 hotspot' and
  • under period of restricted movement

A second public health order:

  • is a state or territory public health order that restricts a person's movement, due to them being in a COVID-19 hotspot that was also under a restricted movement order at the time they were there, even if they are now in another location
  • includes stay at home orders, self-quarantine or mandatory (supervised) quarantine
  • can differ depending on the home state or territory

Claims are assessed for the existing relevant periods for the Sydney and Greater Sydney events.

The address the customer was present in within the COVID-19 hotspot area will be checked using the Geospatial tool to make sure the relevant area was 'declared a COVID-19 hotspot' and is under a period of restricted movement.

Insufficient appropriate leave or pandemic related paid leave entitlements

To get the COVID-19 Disaster Payment, a customer must have 'insufficient appropriate paid leave entitlements (including any special pandemic leave) or pandemic related paid leave entitlements, or will use them up during the same relevant period'.

Appropriate leave includes paid special pandemic leave.

Insufficient appropriate leave means the customer:

  • has no appropriate leave entitlements under a relevant industrial instrument or by agreement with their employer including any special pandemic leave
  • does not have enough days of appropriate leave to cover the relevant period, or
  • the employer is unable to pay appropriate leave entitlements because of financial hardship

Appropriate leave does not include:

  • annual leave
  • long service leave
  • unpaid leave
  • another leave type they are not entitled to access, such as paid sick and carer's leave or compassionate leave when they have been stood down without pay by their employer under the Fair Work Act

Customers do not need to take:

  • accrued leave entitlements such as:
    • annual leave
    • long service leave
    • unpaid leave
  • leave they are not entitled to access if they have been stood down without pay by their employer under the Fair Work Act, for example:
    • paid sick leave
    • carer's leave
    • compassionate leave

However, if a customer is receiving any of the above leave types, including pandemic related leave entitlements, during the entire relevant period, they:

  • have not lost income, and
  • are not eligible to receive the COVID-19 Disaster Payment

For unpaid leave, while the customer may have lost income, they would not meet the 'would have worked' criteria. That is, if a person is on unpaid leave, they would not have worked even if the restricted movement did not occur.

If appropriate leave or pandemic related paid leave entitlements do not cover the entire relevant period, the customer meets this eligibility requirement.

For example, if a customer has appropriate leave or pandemic related paid leave entitlements that cover 3 days of the relevant period, they meet this eligibility requirement. Note: the customer must meet all eligibility requirements for at least 1 day in the remaining period.

A customer will not meet this eligibility requirement if appropriate leave or pandemic related paid leave entitlements they have received or will receive cover the full relevant period.

Payment amount - relevant period 1 and 2 Sydney and Greater Sydney claims

For relevant period 1 and 2 Sydney and Greater Sydney claims only, the customer must have lost at least 1 full day of work.

If the customer meets all eligibility criteria, the payment amount is:

  • $325 if they have lost less than 20 hours of work in the relevant period
  • $500 if they have lost 20 hours or more or work in the relevant period

The payment cannot be paid pro rata. The customer must be paid the full amount of $325 or $500.

Payment amount - relevant period 3 and onwards Sydney and Greater Sydney claims and All Other Areas in NSW claims

For relevant period 3 and onwards Sydney and Greater Sydney claims and All Other Areas in NSW claims, the customer must have lost at least 8 hours of work or a full day of work.

Payment rates depend on the relevant period a customer is claiming for:

If the customer has lost:

  • at least 8 hours and less than 20 hours of work, or a full day of work in the relevant period, they will receive $375 or $450, depending on the relevant period
  • at least 20 hours or more of work in the relevant period, they will receive $600 or $750, depending on the relevant period

The payment cannot be paid pro rata. The customer must be paid the full amount of $375/$450 or $600/$750.

For payment rates for each relevant period, see the Resources page.
