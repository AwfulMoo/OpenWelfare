Process in assessing Crisis Payment (CrP) due to extreme circumstances family and domestic violence

Step

Action

1

Claim due to extreme circumstances + Read more ...

Note: if claim is for Crisis Payment (CrP) for Other Extreme Circumstance (EXO), see New claim for Crisis Payment Other Extreme Circumstances.

Customer claiming Crisis Payment (CrP) for extreme circumstances family and domestic violence (EXV) has been referred to the social worker.

If possible, the Service Officer who referred the customer should check:

  • the common criteria for CrP are met
  • the customer is in severe financial hardship
  • the customer was in Australia at the time of the extreme circumstance
  • no more than four CrP EXV/EXO have been paid to them within the previous 12 month period
  • they have not intentionally brought about the extreme circumstance to obtain a CrP

If the customer has attended a service centre, the service officer should warm hand off the customer to the social worker.

For online or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) CrP claims or if the customer has completed the SU510, the Service Officer should tell the customer to upload all available verification to their record immediately if it is safe to do so. If the customer is unable to upload verification, the social worker can obtain verbal verification of the customer's circumstances from third parties, using verbal declarations and/or make a professional assessment based on the information available.

For all claims for CrP relating to family and domestic violence, the social worker needs to:

  • review the details within the claim and, if possible discuss what has happened to determine if it meets the definition of family and domestic violence
  • determine if the customer made contact within seven days after the extreme circumstance occurred
  • check that everyone is now safe, and
  • provide immediate support and counselling to the customer, and refer for appropriate community support and services, depending on the person's circumstances

Note: if the customer requests no social work contact, a social work referral and social work contact is still needed for the purposes of the assessment. When contacting the customer, the social worker must confirm if it is safe for the customer to discuss their circumstances.

2

Determine if a claim has been lodged + Read more ...

A CrP claim is considered lodged if:

  • the customer submits an online claim
  • an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) is completed on behalf of the customer
  • the customer lodges or uploads a SU510

Once the CrP claim is lodged, the possible outcomes to the claim are:

  • granted
  • rejected, or
  • not pursued

Once the claim is lodged, but before the claim determination, the customer can withdraw the claim.

If it is determined during the social work interview that the CrP EXV claim is to be withdrawn as the customer requests to withdraw their claim, the social worker must:

  • complete the Crisis payment EXV Claim Withdrawn Fast Note, answering all fields and make sure the Fast Note is closed
  • complete the social work referral work item as this will allocate the claim to a Crisis Payment skill tagged Service Officer for processing
  • tell the customer they have completed a request to withdraw their claim, and once processed, they will not have review and appeal rights as no social work determination was made

The claim is considered 'not pursued' for SWIS purposes. Clearly DOC this on the customers record.

See New claim for Crisis Payment under extreme circumstances family and domestic violence, Resources page, for more information.

No current claim for CrP

'Crisis Payment Enquiry' is used if there is no current claim for Crisis Payment on PD at the time of the social work assessment.

Either:

  • In discussions with the social worker the customer advises they not want to proceed with a Crisis Payment claim, or
  • The social worker was unable to make contact with the customer. This will occur in situations where a Service Officer (by SW Line or WLM) has referred the customer to a social worker and no online claim or ACC has been completed prior. As there is no claim lodged, no decision can be made. The customers contact is considered a 'Crisis Payment Enquiry' only.

3

What is the customer's situation? + Read more ...

  • victim of family and domestic violence who is leaving home, go to Step 4
  • victim of family and domestic violence who is remaining at home, go to Step 5
  • alleged or actual perpetrator of family and domestic violence who is forced to leave home, go to Step 6

4

Customer is the victim of family and domestic violence and is leaving home + Read more ...

Note: in this situation, a family member is defined as:

  • Partner including husbands, wives, boyfriends or girlfriends
  • Ex-partner including ex-boyfriends, ex-girlfriends, ex-husbands or ex-wives
  • Carers or paid support workers
  • Parent, guardian or other family members
  • Child
  • Other people the customer lives with or sees often, whether inside or outside the home

Discuss the customer's situation including:

  • Assess and determine the claimant's eligibility for CrP under the family and domestic violence criteria - has the claimant left home because of family and domestic violence (extreme circumstance) and contact within 7 days after the extreme circumstance occurred
  • Has the customer found alternative accommodation, or do they need any help with this
  • Check if there is a third party, who can verify the circumstances. In some cases the person may have left without police involvement and verification of the circumstances need to be sought from a third party such as:
    • doctors,
    • domestic violence services,
    • legal services,
    • crisis workers,
    • other household members,
    • community agencies,
    • schools, and/or
    • child care services
      Note: if unable to obtain third party verification, professional discretion should be utilised and documented in the report
  • Check if verification of severe financial hardship has been uploaded to the record. If not, a professional assessment of the customer's financial hardship should be conducted inclusive of a verbal declaration received from the customer, and documented in the grant/reject Fast Note and SWIS report.
  • Does the customer need to have their personal details updated by the Service Officer, as it does not match information provided at the time the claim was lodged. For example updating their relationship status and current address
  • Does the customer need to be referred to other services?

See Eligibility for Crisis payment (CrP) on the Background page for the definition of ‘partner’ and/or ‘family member’.

Go to Step 7.

5

Customer is the victim of family and domestic violence and is remaining at home + Read more ...

Note: in this situation, a family member is defined as:

  • The partner or ex-partner
  • Parent or guardian
  • Sibling
  • Child
  • Grandparent

Discuss the customer's situation including:

  • Assess and determine their eligibility for CrP under the family and domestic violence criteria – has the claimant remained in the home after experiencing family and domestic violence (extreme circumstance) and contact within seven days after the extreme circumstance occurred
  • If the actual or alleged perpetrator (defined as an immediate family member) has left or been legally prevented from returning to the home
  • Check if there is evidence that the actual or alleged perpetrator is legally prevented from returning to the home. For example, an Apprehended Violence Order (AVO) with an exclusion provision, that prohibits the actual or alleged perpetrator (from living in the family home with the claimant (protected person). In some cases the family member may leave without police involvement and verification of the violent relationship will need to be sought from a third party such as:
    • doctors
    • domestic violence services
    • legal services
    • crisis workers
    • other household members
    • community agencies
    • schools
    • child care services
  • Check if verification of severe financial hardship has been uploaded to the record. If not, a professional assessment of the customer's financial hardship should be conducted inclusive of a verbal declaration received from the customer, and documented in the grant/reject Fast Note and SWIS report
  • Does the customer need to have their personal details updated by the Service Officer, as it does not match information provided at the time the claim was lodged? For example, updating their current address.
  • Does the customer need to be referred to other services?

Go to Step 7.

6

Customer is the actual or alleged perpetrator of family and domestic violence and has left the home + Read more ...

Note: in this situation, a family member is defined as:

  • Partner including husbands, wives, boyfriends or girlfriends
  • Ex-partner including ex-boyfriends, ex-girlfriends, ex-husbands or ex-wives
  • Carers or paid support workers
  • Parent, guardian or other family members
  • Child
  • Other people the customer lives with

Discuss the customer's situation including:

  • Assess and determine the claimant's eligibility for CrP under the family and domestic violence criteria - has the claimant identified as the alleged or actual perpetrator of family and domestic violence (extreme circumstance) and within 7 days after the extreme circumstance occurred
  • Check if there is evidence that the actual or alleged perpetrator (claimant) is legally prevented from returning to the family home. For example, an Apprehended Violence Order (AVO) with an exclusion provision that prohibits the claimant from living in the family home with the protected person
    • Note: this should be obtained before payment can be made. There is currently no discretion for social workers to use their professional judgement in cases where there is no evidence of police involvement and no evidence of being legally prevented from returning home
  • Verify that the actual or alleged perpetrator actually lived with the victim in the family home immediately before being removed
  • Check if verification of severe financial hardship has been provided or uploaded to the record. If not, a professional assessment of the customer's financial hardship should be conducted, inclusive of a verbal declaration received from the customer, and documented in the grant/reject Fast Note and SWIS report
  • Does the customer need to have their personal details updated by the Service Officer, as it does not match information provided at the time the claim was lodged? For example, updating their current address
  • Does the customer need to be referred to other services?

7

Outcome + Read more ...

Determine if the claimant qualifies for CrP under the family and domestic violence provisions.

After the claim determination is made:

  • Record the determination using the relevant grant, reject or not pursued Fast Note. Select Auto text. Use Social Worker > Claims
  • If using contingency arrangements, go to Step 10
  • If unable to contact customer, go to Step 8
  • If the customer has or intends to set up a new home, but the address does not match details the customer gave in the claim, the social worker will record the new home and postal address in the grant/reject Fast Note. If a customer says they are living in shared accommodation, ask them the sharer question set and record their answers in the grant, reject, not pursued or withdrawal Fast Note under ‘Other information/further assessment by SO’. This will allow the Service Officer to update accommodation details for the customer when processing the CrP claim
  • Make sure the correct home and postal address details are given and nominee or PPE arrangements are reviewed and correctly recorded. It is important to make sure the safety of the customer (and their dependents) particularly in circumstances where the customer has separated or when family and domestic violence issues are identified
  • Complete the CrP disclosed relationship - follow up required Fast Note if the customer advises of a previously undisclosed relationship. Select Auto text. Use Social Worker > Claims
  • Crisis Payment Outcome - Granted
    • Contact the ‘Crisis Payment Finalisation SWO USE ONLY’ using the 'Crisis Payment Claim SWO Assessed' iWS Softphone transfer option or spectrum 178 911. Once the Service Officer has answered the call and assigned the claim to themselves, complete the social work referral work item. This will allow the claim to be processed
    • Tell the customer of the claim outcome, procedure ends here
  • Crisis Payment Outcome - Rejected
    • Complete the social work referral work item. This will allow the claim to be allocated to a skill tagged Service Officer for processing
    • If the claim needs immediate processing, contact the ‘Crisis Payment Finalisation SWO USE ONLY’ using the 'Crisis Payment Claim SWO Assessed' iWS Softphone transfer option or spectrum 178 911. Once the Service Officer has answered the call and assigned the claim to themselves, complete the social work referral work item. This will allow the claim to be processed
    • Tell the customer of their review and appeal rights. Go to Step 9

8

Unable to contact + Read more ...

Sufficient information to make a determination

If the social worker is unable to establish contact with the customer and there is sufficient information to grant the customer's claim, the social worker can undertake the assessment as a file assessment and recommend a grant without making contact with the customer. When the claim is finalised, the customer will be sent a letter advising of the outcome of the CrP claim, if it is safe to do so. Consideration of the customer's specific circumstances is essential including (not limited to) PPE and relationship status, address and bank account details.

Insufficient information to make a determination

When a social worker has made 2 unsuccessful contact attempts to the customer over more than one day, the claim is to be rejected due to insufficient information. The social worker must complete a rejection SWIS report. Copy and paste the following approved text in the body of the report:

'Claim for crisis payment is rejected. Family and domestic violence circumstances could not be sufficiently assessed because attempts to contact (customer name) about their claim were unsuccessful. Refer to crisis payment reject Fast Note dated (the date).'

9

Appeals process + Read more ...

If the Service Officer rejects the claim and the customer requests an explanation of the decision:

  • an appeal script is to be completed, and
  • the explanation of the decision must be allocated to a Subject Matter Expert (SME)

The claim needs to be allocated to a social worker inside of the claim by re-indexing the claim, because the:

  • Service Officer's original decision is overturned, or
  • customer provides new details about the same incident

See Cancelling claim activities and re-indexing claims.

If a social worker rejects the claim and the customer requests an explanation of the decision:

  • an appeals script is to be completed, and
  • the explanation of the decision must be allocated to a SME

When appealing a social work decision:

  • run the appeals script
  • key CRI in to the Payment type the decision has been made against field
  • select specialist in the Process Type field

Irrespective of who the decision maker is, if the customer applies for a formal review of the decision:

  • an appeal script is completed, and
  • the formal review will be allocated to an Authorised Review Officer

For more details, see Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process.

10

Contingency Arrangements + Read more ...

In incidents where Process Direct (PD) or Assisted Customer Claims (ACC) is not available, Social Work Services Branch will tell social workers when they must undertake contingency arrangements.

Social Work Services Branch will be responsible for providing details on contingency processes to make sure that there is no delay to a customer's claim for Crisis Payment. See Resources for Contingency Fast Notes.
