Assessing SSL eligibility

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Check for an eligible payment type + Read more ...

Students may be entitled to a Student Start-up Scholarship (SSS) from the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) when they receive assistance from the DVA under the:

  • Veterans' Children's Education Scheme (VCES), or
  • Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act Education and Training Scheme (MRCAETS)

These students must contact DVA for more information.

Is the customer receiving Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy, or ABSTUDY Living Allowance?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No:
    • The student is not eligible for a Student Start-up Loan (SSL)
    • Record details on a DOC
    • Procedure ends here

2

Check student status + Read more ...

Check the student:

  • is enrolled at an approved higher education institution (found on the National Course Approvals sub-site) and
  • is undertaking an approved scholarship course with a code on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen of either:
    • MST
    • HGA
    • HGB
    • TGA
    • TGB
    • UPC (ABSTUDY only)
    • DOC (ABSTUDY only)
    • TSC (scholarship approved TGC level courses) or
    • SSN (scholarship approved SNS level courses)

When a TGC or SNS student enquires about scholarship eligibility, check the National Course Approvals sub-site to determine whether the course is an approved scholarship course. If the course is an approved scholarship course, update the Course Level: field on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen to TSC or SSN (as applicable), when they have:

  • a participation status code that equates with a full-time or concessional study load enrolment (APC, C25, C66 or FTS), and
  • started their course

Does the student meet the required student status?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No:
    • The student is not eligible for SSL
    • Record details on a DOC
    • Procedure ends here

3

Claim time limit + Read more ...

There are 2 loan periods:

  • 1 January to 30 June
  • 1 July to 31 December

The student must submit their claim for the loan before:

  • the last day in the loan period, or
  • 35 days before the end of the course, when the course ends in the loan period

Students cannot apply for an Student Start-up Loan (SSL) where the loan period has ended.

Students can apply in advance for 1 or more future loan periods. Where a student submits a claim before the start of the loan period, they are deemed to have submitted their claim on the first day of that loan period.

Where a customer submits a second online claim, the SSL questions may not always appear. Contacting the customer to obtain the information may be required.

Has the student met the time limit?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • The student is not eligible for SSL
    • Record details on a DOC
    • Procedure ends here

4

Check Tax File Number (TFN) + Read more ...

Has the student provided a valid TFN to Services Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No,
    • The student is not eligible for SSL
    • SSL applications rejected for this reason will be auto reassessed if the TFN is provided within 13 weeks, even if the loan period has ended
    • Record details on a DOC
    • Procedure ends here

5

Check receipt of payment on Qualification Test Day (QTD) + Read more ...

The loan periods each year are:

  • 1 January to 30 June, and
  • 1 July to 31 December

For each loan period, the Qualification Test Day (QTD) is the earliest of the following dates:

  • the day the claim for SSL is determined. It may be the same as the student claim if SSL was part of it
  • the last day of the course where it ends in the loan period, or
  • the last day of the loan period

On the student’s QTD, the student must receive:

  • Youth Allowance (YA) as a student
  • Austudy
  • ABSTUDY Living Allowance
  • Youth Disability Supplement (YDS) component of YA/AUS/ABY, or
  • Energy Supplement component of YA/AUS/ABY

Earliest date is the date the claim was processed and payments have not started

The system checks the record each day after the QTD for receipt of a qualifying payment. This will continue until the end of the loan period or the last day of the course (if the course ends in the loan period and no further approved scholarship course is coded).

Students will not qualify for SSL when they only receive:

  • ABSTUDY Incidentals
  • Rent Assistance (RA) and/or
  • Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA), or
  • other supplements

Did the student receive a qualifying payment on the relevant day?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No,
    • The student is not eligible for SSL
    • Record details on a DOC
    • Procedure ends here

6

Check eligibility for relevant loan period + Read more ...

Students may qualify for a SSL or SSS under:

  • DVA entitlement schemes Veterans' Children Education Scheme and Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act Education and Training Scheme, or
  • Commonwealth Education Costs Scholarship (CECS)

Check if the student:

  • received one of these payments for the same loan period, or
  • qualifies for SSS from one of these providers in either the 6 months:
    • immediately before their SSL QTD, or
    • after their SSL QTD

Do any of the above apply?

  • Yes,
    • The student is not eligible for SSL
    • Record details on a DOC
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, student meets eligibility criteria for SSL

7

Student Start-up Loan eligibility status Summary (!SSLS) pages + Read more ...

Information can be viewed in Process Direct, however, this screen is not fully operational. Customer First must be used to complete SSL update activities and assessments.

To view the student’s status in:

  • Process Direct, type ‘SSLS’ into the Super Key field
  • Customer First, type ‘!SSLS’ in the next field

In Customer First, the 'Eligibility Status' column on the !SSLS/SSLS page is automatically updated for each loan the student has accepted. The 'Eligibility Status' column will:

  • be blank when:
    • the loan decision is 'Accepted' and the loan has been paid, or
    • eligibility for the loan has not been determined
  • display 'Ineligible - Debt Raised' when the student was paid the loan and subsequently became ineligible
  • display 'Not Paid' when the student applied for the SSL and is not eligible. One of the following reasons will display:
    • NQI - Not qualified for student Income Support Payment
    • NQP - Income Support Payment not current or assessed
    • NQC - Not enrolled in a qualifying course
    • NSD - No study details
    • NQT - Not in a qualifying institution
    • NFT - Not in full time studies
    • NTL - Time Limit Rules Not Met
    • NSR - Study Requirements Not Met
    • GFR - Grandfathered on SSS
    • CEC - Receiving CECS
    • TNP - TFN not provided
    • MM1 - TFN mismatch

Claiming methods + Read more ...

Students may apply for the SSL through the preferred method of self service.

Self service is the preferred channel for customers to conduct their business with the agency. Customers who are not eligible to register for self service or deemed inappropriate based on their circumstances can claim over the phone.

How is the student claiming the SSL?

  • Self service:
    • Student online claim, go to Step 2
    • Manage Study Details online service, go to Step 3
    • Apply for/Manage Student Start-up Loan online service, go to Step 4

2

Student online claim + Read more ...

The following information is completed in the student online claim.

The student is asked 'Do you wish to claim SSL?' If the student selects:

  • Yes,
    • they will be asked to tick the loan periods they would like to claim SSL for (based on study dates and details entered in the online claim)
    • the service will total the amount the student is applying for at the bottom of the screen. Go to Step 5
  • No, they proceed to the next claim question. Procedure ends here

3

Manage Study Details online service + Read more ...

Where a student updates their existing education details, or add a new approved scholarship course, they will receive a message 'Do you wish to claim SSL?'

If a student accepts or declines the SSL in the 'Manage Study Details' online service, they are unable to vary their decision using the 'Apply for/Manage Student Start-up Loan' online service until the Manage Study Details update is completed, processed, or their course has started.

If the student selects:

  • Yes,
    • they will be asked to tick the loan periods they would like to claim SSL for (based on study dates and details entered)
    • the service will total the amount the student is applying for at the bottom of the screen. Go to Step 5
  • No, they proceed to the next question in the online service. Procedure ends here

4

Apply for/Manage Student Start-up Loan online service + Read more ...

Students can access the online service to:

  • apply for their SSL once their student claim has been processed and they have reached the course start date
  • decline a loan before the SSL has been paid
  • look at the payment status of their loans
  • view the history of their SSL loans

The student can change their decision if the SSL payment has not been made and the loan period has not ended.

5

Online claims acknowledgement of the Terms and Conditions + Read more ...

Students must read the Terms and Conditions of the SSL. The student must:

  • understand the Terms and Conditions of accepting the SSL
  • agree to the Terms and Conditions of the loan, and
  • confirm they understand they must repay this loan to the Australian Taxation Office

The student must also accept the Terms and Conditions:

  • 'I confirm I have read and I understand the Terms and Conditions of accepting a Student Start-up Loan'
  • 'I acknowledge that the Student Start-up Loan must be repaid to the Australian Government through the taxation system'
  • Acknowledgement is made via Checkbox - Online claim, Apply for/Manage Student Start-up Loan online service

If the student does not confirm they accept the Terms and Conditions in their claim, they are deemed to have declined the loan.

Go to Step 8.

6

Student claims over the phone + Read more ...

Where a student requests an SSL over the phone, the student must be read the Terms and Conditions of the SSL.

If the student is unable to apply for an SSL using online services, DOC the reason why.

Does the student understand the Terms and Conditions of accepting the SSL?

  • The student must agree to the Terms and Conditions of the loan to proceed
  • If the student does not accept the Terms and Conditions, they are deemed to have declined the loan and their application cannot be accepted

Does the student acknowledge that the Student Start-up Loan must be repaid to the Australian Government through the taxation system?

  • The student must confirm they understand they must repay this loan to the Australian Taxation Office for this box to be ticked

The student is also required to accept the Terms and Conditions:

  • 'I confirm I have read and I understand the Terms and Conditions of accepting a Student Start-up Loan'
  • 'I acknowledge that the Student Start-up Loan must be repaid to the Australian Government through the taxation system'
  • Acknowledgement is made verbally over the phone

The student's acceptance or non-acceptance of the SSL Terms and Conditions must be recorded in Customer First on the !SSLS screen.

If the student accepts the Terms and Conditions, DOC the student's record stating they have understood and accepted the Terms and Conditions. If the student does not confirm they accept the Terms and Conditions in their claim, they are deemed to have declined the loan.

7

Assessment and Tax File Number (TFN) matching + Read more ...

Self Service claims

The loan details are automatically added to the new claim in Customer First on the !SSLS and !SSLD screens.

SSL claims taken over the phone

Service Officers must manually enter the SSL claim via phone by coding:

  • !SSLS screen for students accepting loans for the duration of the course:
    • Select 'Accept all loans for course' radio button
    • Date Advised: this is the date the student contacted and claimed over the phone
    • Channel: phone
    • Terms and Conditions: Select this box for students who have accepted the Terms and Conditions verbally
  • !SSLD screen (by updating the individual loan period from the !SSLS screen) for students accepting loans for a specific loan period:
    • select the loan period from !SSLS that requires updating
    • select 'Update Loan Period'
    • Loan Decision: Accept for the periods SSL claims and Decline for the periods SSL not claimed
    • Date Advised: this is the date the student contacted and claimed over the phone
    • Channel: phone
    • Terms and Conditions: Select this box for students who have accepted the Terms and Conditions verbally

At the Assessment Results (AR) screen for each claiming method, the system will check the loan application to ensure the student is completing an approved scholarship course and their TFN is matched with the ATO.

TFN matching

The TFN matching process can be initiated either:

  • Automatically as part of the SSL new claim, assessment and TFN change processes when:
    • a TFN exists in the agency's system
    • a student changes course details and a TFN exists in the agency's system, or
    • at any point when the student provides their TFN and has accepted the SSL
  • Manually via the !SSLATO screen when the loan is accepted in the !SSLS screen

Verification codes

The !SSLATO screen shows the current status of the TFN match:

  • Matched - TFN Verified by the ATO
  • Not Initiated - TFN has not been supplied or matching process has not been initiated
  • No Match - TFN has not matched with information held by the ATO
  • Pending - Waiting on TFN verification with the ATO

Before finalising a claim that includes SSL, check that the TFN match on the !SSLATO screen has been completed.

Do not finalise a claim with TFN Pending. Close the record and then go back in. Continue checking the record until a verification code other than PND is returned.

If verification code MIS is returned, the student will need to contact the Australian Taxation Office to confirm their Tax File Number. The ATO website will have more information for the student.

If the student contacts to advise the TFN is correct:

  • They must provide written verification from the ATO (such as Tax Notice of Assessment or TFN enquiry letter)

If a system issue is identified escalate to Level 2 ICT Helpdesk.

8

SSL assessment letters + Read more ...

One of the following assessment letters will issue to the student:

  • SSL Grant Letter if the student qualifies for SSL
  • SSL Rejection Letter if the student qualifies for student payment but does not qualify for SSL, the loan will be automatically rejected. The letter will include an explanation of the decision
  • Benefit and SSL Rejection Letter if the student does not qualify for a student payment, their claim is rejected:
    • The system automatically records the loan rejection decision on the SSLS and SSLD screens in Customer First
    • A loan rejection paragraph is not added to the letter

9

Payment and review of SSL + Read more ...

Loans for one period

Once an SSL claim is finalised, a review is created for the Student Start Date if the student has not commenced their course. The loan will be paid on the first pay day after a full entitlement period. This will occur after the Student Start date if the student qualifies.

Loans for duration of the course

Students selecting this option have elected to receive all SSL's for the duration of the course. Future reviews are set up for 1 January and 1 July to test the student's eligibility for SSL. The payment will be made on the first payday after the review date once the student meets all the eligibility criteria.

Loans for future periods cannot be issued early.

Loan has not automatically paid

Do not attempt a manual workaround.

Thoroughly investigate the record, if satisfied the student is eligible and the loan, has not issued automatically, escalate to Technical Peer Support (TPS) who will refer to Level 2 ICT helpdesk via My Support.

See, Service Officer role when seeking Technical Peer Support (TPS) in Smart Centres.

2

Student wants to change decision + Read more ...

Students with pre-accepted loans for their course can stop/decline future SSL payments at any time.

If a student has declined a loan, they can apply for one at any time provided they meet the time limit and eligibility requirements.

A loan application cannot be made for a loan period once that loan period has passed.

If a student wants to change their decision they can do this by using the 'Apply for/ Manage Student Start-up Loan' online service.

Students can change their loan decision as long as a loan has not already been paid for the period. A loan can be declined up to payment strip on the day of the customer's Entitlement Period End Date (EPED).

3

Reasons for a SSL debt + Read more ...

A SSL that has been paid becomes a Social Security/ABSTUDY debt and is recoverable by the agency.

It is not a loan recovered by the ATO if any of the following apply:

  • the student was not qualified for a student income support payment in relation to the loan period for which the loan was paid
  • student income support payment was not payable in relation to the loan period for which the loan was paid
  • the student does not meet the applicable SSL study requirements for the loan payment

If a student did not meet SSL study requirements because of exceptional circumstances beyond their control then they may not incur a debt. For more information see Student Start-up Loan (SSL) Study requirements.

4

Raising a Social Security/ABSTUDY SSL debt + Read more ...

If a SSL becomes a Social Security/ABSTUDY debt after being transferred to the ATO, the amount returned to the agency is minus any accumulated CPI adjustment. This amount is recoverable a debt to the Commonwealth. The loan needs to be returned to the agency before recovery can commence.

The full amount of the original SSL payment must be raised as a debt. It is not possible to raise a SSL debt for a proportional amount.

SSL Debt Benefit type codes:

  • SLA - SSL Austudy
  • SLB - SSL ABSTUDY
  • SLY - SSL Youth Allowance

SSL debts cannot be raised manually. If a student is not eligible for SSL, a reassessment on their record must occur to raise the SSL as a debt shell.

If the SSL is transferred to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO), and an overpayment of SSL is created on the student's record, the system will retrieve the SSL back from the ATO automatically prior to raising the debt on the 21st day. SSL debts are raised automatically by the system. An appropriate letter is sent to the student.

If an activity on a student's record creates an SSL debt, the staff member:

  • becomes the decision maker. Debt Management Branch do not raise or reassess SSL debts
  • will be responsible for undertaking a review of decision, if the student requests a review
  • reassess the SSL debt, if it is determined the student is entitled to the SSL.

5

Student asks for a review of a SSL debt + Read more ...

Where an SSL debt has been raised or debt shell created and the student contacts asking for an explanation of decision, Service Officers must:

  • Discuss the decision with the customer
  • Allow new information to be given
  • Correct any errors and do a reassessment. See, Reviews and appeals

If the customer does not understand or does not agree with the decision, see Request for an explanation or application for a formal review.

6

Restoring entitlement + Read more ...

Where an SSL debt has been raised incorrectly, Service Officers must:

  • Investigate which activity triggered the SSL debt. For example:
    • the course end date is incorrect
    • an incorrect cancellation has occurred, or an incorrect cancellation date of effect
    • the EDC entry has been deleted
  • If the coding can be corrected by updating the study details on the EDC screen, this will restore SSL entitlement automatically and change the debt status to 'Finalised - No debt'. This will:
    • send a letter to the student advising they have no debt
    • set up an activity on the FAL screen to transfer the loan to the ATO

Service Officers must check the !SSLS screen to see if the loan has been changed from 'Accepted' to 'Unknown' due to the deletion of the course, or a change of course coding. If so, this can be updated by selecting the loan period, selecting 'Update Loan Period' and re-coding the acceptance with the exact details previously coded.

If these fixes do not work and the student's entitlement to the SSL has been established, refer the case to Technical Peer Support Officer (TPS). If unable to resolve after further investigation refer the record to Level 2 Policy Help Desk. Include:

  • an explanation of why the student is entitled to the SSL, and
  • that the entitlement cannot be restored and the loan sent to the ATO so it can be repaid via the Tax system

See the Resources page for a link to Level 2 Policy Help Desk.
