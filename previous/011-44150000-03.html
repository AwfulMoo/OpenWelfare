Options for requesting a replacement or duplicate Medicare card

Table 1

Item

Action

1

Requests via telephone + Read more ...

The caller must:

  • be over 15 years of age
  • be listed on the card, and
  • complete a public security check

If the card has an expiry date of less than 6 months, a replacement card can be re-issued.

If the expiry date in CDMS is greater than 6 months see New card requests for cards expired for over 6 months table for more information.

2

Requests via online services + Read more ...

The member of the public must be registered for a Medicare account online.

Members of the public can order a maximum of 2 replacement Medicare cards online within a 12 month period. If additional cards are required, the member of the public must:

  • submit a written request
  • call the Medicare general enquiry line, or
  • visit a service centre

3

Requests in person at a service centre + Read more ...

The person must provide identity documents. See Document types for Medicare eligibility and enrolments for more information

If they cannot provide identity documents they must pass a public security check.

4

Requests via written correspondence + Read more ...

A signed written request can be submitted identifying information including:

  • name
  • Medicare number
  • signature
  • a certified copy of the applicant’s identification

Note: a person requesting a Medicare card must be over the age of 15 and be listed on that card.

New card requests for expired cards

Table 2

Note: a new card can be issued when a person is quoting an expired Medicare number and it can be confirmed that a new card has been issued in CDMS and sent to their current address.

Step

Action

1

Check CDMS current issue details + Read more ...

On the CDMS Home page:

  • select Inquire Consumer Details
  • key the Medicare card number in ID Number field.
  • select Group
  • select Current Issue Details

Has a new card been issued?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 5

2

Determine if card has been issued to an old address + Read more ...

  • Select Interactions tab to will show when a Medicare card was sent and to what address

Is the address different to what is recorded in CDMS?

  • Yes, go to Issue a replacement or duplicate Medicare card table
  • No, address is the same, go to Step 3

3

Check to see if there is a return mail indicator present + Read more ...

  • On the CDMS Home page, select Inquire Consumer Details
  • Key the Medicare card number in ID Number field
  • Select Group
  • Select Address Details tab

This will show in the field Type of Mail Returned.

Is the return mail indicator present?

  • Yes,
    • confirm that the address recorded in CDMS is correct
    • remove the indicator (see remove a return mail indicator table in Amend customer details in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System for Medicare for instruction). Note: if there is a return mail indicator and the address is updated, this will automatically remove the indicator. This will need to be updated before issuing a Medicare card
    • go to the Issue a replacement or duplicate Medicare card table
  • No, go to Step 4

4

No return mail indicator present + Read more ...

If the applicant is in a service centre, go to Step 6

If the applicant is contacting via telephone advise that they must attend a service centre, as it may be necessary to provide documentation confirming they are residing in Australia. Procedure ends here.

5

Card expired for less than 6 months + Read more ...

Issue a replacement card. Advise the caller that they may view a digital copy of their Medicare card using the Medicare Express Plus App.

Card expired for more than 6 months

Inform the applicant that they must attend a service centre as it may be necessary to provide documentation confirming they are residing in Australia.

6

Determine if the applicant has been residing outside of Australia + Read more ...

Has the applicant been residing outside of Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Issue a replacement or duplicate Medicare card table

7

Determine how long applicant has been residing outside of Australia + Read more ...

Less than 5 years for Australian citizens or less than 12 months for permanent resident or New Zealand citizen

Applicants are still regarded as a resident for Medicare purposes, go to Issue a replacement or duplicate Medicare card table and record the date of departure in the comments field at Personal level.

Service Officers cannot refuse to issue a Medicare card. See Ministerial orders and Medicare eligibility for more information.

More than 5 years for Australian citizens or more than 12 months for permanent resident or New Zealand citizen

It needs to be determined if the applicant has returned to Australia to reside. They must provide evidence in order to re-enrol in Medicare. Service Officers should not update any personal details held in CDMS until they have proven residency.

Note: where the applicant is a government employee or a member of the Australian Defence Force and has been absent from Australia for more than 5 years they remain eligible for medical benefits incurred in Australia. They are not required to satisfy the usual process for residency, providing they show documents to support they are a government employee or a member of the Australian Defence Force.

Issue a replacement or duplicate Medicare card

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Issue or replace a card + Read more ...

Before issuing a new or replacement Medicare card for a consumer, check all CDMS Group comments to identify if a BI flag comment exists.

If no comment exists, go to Step 2.

If a comment exists see Suspected Medicare fraud and Business Integrity (BI) flags.

2

Issue card in CDMS + Read more ...

On CDMS home page:

  • select Amend on consumer details
  • key the Medicare card number in ID Number field
  • select Group

Note: a Medicare Payment Integrity (MPI) restriction may be present preventing amendment. If this occurs, please contact HSDD Eligibility.

3

Issue either a replacement or duplicate card + Read more ...

Select Card Requests tab.

Tick the required checkbox to request:

  • replace Current Card
  • duplicate Current Card, or
  • duplicate Replacement Card

Select Replacement Reason from the drop down box.

Select Card Request Source from the drop down box.

Select Validate, Validate, Update to confirm the details displayed.

Note: a duplicate card should only be issued where there is more than 1 person over the age of 15 years enrolled on the card.

If an error message displays Replacement not Allowed see remove a return mail indicator table in Amend customer details in the CDMS for Medicare for instruction.

Replacement of a RHCA card

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Medicare card is due to expire in more than 1 month + Read more ...

The Service Officer must:

  • complete a public security check
  • check the address is still current
  • issue a replacement Medicare card

2

Medicare card is due to expire in less than 1 month + Read more ...

The Service Officer must:

  • complete a public security check
  • change the address to
    • PO Box 1001
    • Tuggeranong DC ACT 2901
  • issue a replacement Medicare card

Note: this must be keyed exactly as is. Do not include Production Control or any other alpha, numeric symbol, C/- or C/O in the embellishment line.

Advise the caller that a Medicare will not be sent.

3

Medicare card has expired + Read more ...

Where the RHCA card has expired, a replacement Medicare card must not be issued over the telephone.

Service Officers must advise the caller to attend a service centre with their passport, valid visa and other relevant documents.

Note: if the caller is unable to attend a service centre, due to a remote location or medical reason a signed written request may be submitted with certified copies of the passport and valid visa and a completed Medicare enrolment application.

For more information see Reciprocal Health Care Agreements (RHCA) eligibility for Medicare and Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS).

Extending a Medicare card

Table 5

Step

Action

1

On the CDMS home page + Read more ...

  • select Amend on consumer details
  • key the Medicare card number in ID Number field
  • select Group

Note: a Medicare Payment Integrity (MPI) restriction may be present preventing amendment. If this occurs, see comments in Group or contact HSDD Eligibility.

2

Extend card and request replacement card + Read more ...

Select Card Requests tab.

In Group Eligibility End date field:

  • key the allowable extended date
  • tick the checkbox to request
  • replace Current Card
  • select Replacement Reason from the drop down box
  • select Card Request Source from the drop down box
  • select Validate, Validate, Update to confirm the details displayed
  • select Comments
  • add a comment as to why you are extending the card

Note: this date will now show in Entitlement, a new entitlement does not need to be added or amended.

Stopping a Medicare card

Table 6

Step

Action

1

Applicant has provided insufficient documents + Read more ...

If an applicant is eligible for Medicare but has been enrolled in Medicare without providing sufficient documents a Service Officer can stop the Medicare card.

Advise the customer that the Medicare card will not be issued until the required documents have been provided.

2

Stopping the Medicare card + Read more ...

Provide detailed comments in the Group Comments field in CDMS, outlining the documentation required to remove the stop.

Once the customer has provided the required documents, the Service Officer can remove the stop on the Medicare card.

Medicare card issued in error

Table 7

Step

Action

1

Medicare card has been issued in error + Read more ...

If the:

  • Service Officer has created a duplicate Consumer ID (no programs attached) go to Step 2
  • applicant is already enrolled and a duplicate PIN has been created, go to Step 3
  • card has been issued in error and the applicant is eligible for Medicare, go to Step 4
  • applicant has been enrolled in error and is not eligible for Medicare, go to Step 5

See Duplicate PIN and Consumer IDs in Medicare for more details.

2

Duplicate Consumer IDs + Read more ...

Contact the HSDD Eligibility Team - Medicare Public to request the Consumer ID be deleted.

3

Duplicate PIN + Read more ...

See Duplicate PIN and Consumer IDs in Medicare process for more details.

4

Issued in error and eligible for Medicare + Read more ...

Extract the card by completing a card extraction form no later than 12 noon (AEST) the following day. The form is available on the Resources page.

The extracted card will not need to be returned to the Service Office as the error must be rectified immediately.

If the cut-off time of 12 o’clock is missed, a Medicare card will be issued and sent to the person. In this case the Service Officer must:

  • contact the group contact of the Medicare card concerned
  • apologise for the error related to the enrolment and advise the group contact they will receive an incorrect Medicare card
  • advise the group contact this card must not be used, and must be destroyed

5

Enrolled but not eligible + Read more ...

The consumer must have a consumer end date keyed and a comment put in Personal CDMS. Extract the card by completing a card extraction form no later than 12 noon (AEST) the following day. The form is available on the Resources page.

If the error was identified after the card extraction timeframe, contact the group card holder to inform them they will be receiving a card and advise them they are not eligible.

Change the group contact in CDMS

Table 8

Step

Action

1

Access Amend Consumer Details page + Read more ...

On the CDMS Home page:

  • select Amend Consumer Details link
  • key the Medicare card number in ID Number field
  • select Group
  • select Name/Address tab

2

Determine group association with card + Read more ...

In the Contact Name change the details of the group contact as required.

Is the group contact associated with the card?

  • Yes,
    • select the Group Association checkbox
    • key the reference number of the customer in Contact Reference Number
    • select Get Details

The mailing address automatically populates in the address fields.

  • No,
    • untick the Group Association checkbox
    • remove the reference number
    • key customer's name in the Contact Name field (Surname,comma,title, initial e.g., Smith,Mr J)
    • complete all required Address fields

3

Further group contact amendments + Read more ...

Make any other amendments in the remaining fields, as required:

  • Scroll down and select Validate. An information message displays advising if the amendments were acceptable or not
  • Select OK to acknowledge the message. If not acceptable, update the amendments as required
  • Scroll down and select Validate
  • When the details are correct, select Update

The Confirm Amended Card Details screen displays the updated details.
