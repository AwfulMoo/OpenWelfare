Determining the date a claim is 'made' and start day

To be paid an Australian social security payment, a person must generally make a claim. A claim is 'made' by being 'lodged' (that is, received) but may be deemed or 'taken to be made' on a different date, for example, early claims.

For claims made after 1 July 2018, the 'intent to claim' provisions apply only to customers in vulnerable circumstances. See Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.

Policy advice is that a person may meet the residence rules for claims on either the date the claim is 'made' or the date the claim is 'taken to be made' taking into account CCD, DOCs or early claim, as well as any other backdated Start Day rules. For example, lodged within 14 days of their partner or within 4 weeks of childbirth.

Note: subsection 16(7) of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 allows a delegate at the appropriate level to determine that a person may make a claim in another way.

In addition:

  • All agreements allow the date a claim is lodged with the agreement authority in the agreement country to be used as the date the claim is lodged with Services Australia
  • Some agreements allow the date of a claim for the other country's pension to be used as the date of lodgement of a claim for the corresponding Australian payment, subject to meeting specific criteria

Accordingly, for claims under agreements, the date the claim is 'made' (that is, lodged/received) may be any of the following:

  • The date the corresponding foreign pension claim is lodged in the agreement country
    Note: only for claims under those agreements with the relevant provision
  • The date the Australian claim is lodged with agreement country authority in the agreement country - Overseas Lodgement Date (DOL)
  • The date the Australian claim is lodged (received) in Australia - Date of Receipt (DOR)

If the customer needs to use an agreement to be paid, the date cannot be earlier than the date the agreement started.

Staff should first check if the customer meets the residence rules for claims under the agreement on the earliest possible date the claim was 'made' under the agreement according to the points above:

  • Check if the claim can be 'taken to be made' on a different day from the date the claim was actually 'made' (as above), including early claim, CCD, claim after partner's claim, claim after giving birth
    For claims made after 1 July 2018, the 'intent to claim' provisions apply only to customers in vulnerable circumstances. See Intent to claim and vulnerable customers
  • Check if the customer meets the residence rules for claims under the agreement on each date in chronological order and either was qualified and payable on that date or became qualified and payable within 13 weeks of that date (early claim)

Users must code the DOL in the agreement country and DOR in Australia correctly as part of the claim. Users can manually shift the Start Date on the Pensions Assessment (PNA) screen if needed for a deemed claim or to apply the 'claim for one = claim for other' provision of an agreement. If the Start Date is moved from DOL/DOR, the Date of Event for agreement coding on the Additional Residence Detail (ARD) screen also be moved to match the Start Date.

System processing will then determine if the customer meets all the requirements for payment under the agreement in sequence and grant the claim from the earliest date the person became qualified.

The customer may simply not meet the all the requirements for payment and should be rejected.

Date examples

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Using the date of lodgement in the agreement country

This is not a deemed claim scenario. The customer meets the rules on the date of lodgement in the agreement country.

Scenario:

  • Customer residing in and present in Canada at all dates
  • Turns Australian Age Pension age on 1 September 2017
  • Lodges Australian Age Pension claim in Canada on 1 October 2017
  • Australian claim is received in Australia on 1 June 2018

Result:

Customer granted from 1 October 2017:

  • DOL is 1 October 2017
  • DOR is 1 June 2018
  • Start Date is 1 October 2017

Reason:

The current agreement with Canada started on 1 January 2003 and does not include the 'claim for one = claim for other' provision.

The customer was resident and present in Canada on 1 October 2017 so meets the residence rules for claims under the agreement on the date the claim was 'made' using the date of lodgement in the agreement country.

System processing will automatically set the Start Date to DOL.

2

Using the date of receipt in Australia

This is not a deemed claim scenario. The customer does not meet the rules on the date of lodgement in the agreement country but does meet the rules on the date of receipt in Australia.

Scenario:

  • Customer residing in and present in Canada at all dates
  • Lodges Australian Age Pension claim in Canada on 1 October 2017 (DOL)
  • Turns Australian Age Pension age on 1 March 2018
  • Australian claim is received in Australia on 1 June 2018 (DOR)

Result:

Customer granted from 1 June 2018:

  • DOL is 1 October 2017
  • DOR is 1 June 2018
  • Start Date is 1 June 2018

Reason:

The current agreement with Canada started on 1 January 2003 and does not include the 'claim for one = claim for other' provision.

The customer was not qualified on 1 October 2017 and did not become qualified within 13 weeks, so cannot use the date of lodgement in the agreement country or the early claim provision. There was no contact about a claim (CCD) with Services Australia.

The customer was resident and present in Canada on 1 June 2018, so meets the residence rules for claims under the agreement on the date of receipt in Australia.

System processing will assess entitlement on DOL then automatically set the Start Date to DOR.

3

Using the 'claim for one = claim for other' provision

This is a deemed claim scenario where the agreement includes the 'claim for one = claim for other' provision (foreign pension (FP) claim = Australian (AU) claim) and the customer meets the rules on that date.

Scenario:

  • Customer residing in and present in Belgium at all dates
  • Turns Australian Age Pension age on 1 July 2015
  • Lodges Belgian Old Age Pension claim on 1 August 2015 and declares an 'affiliation' with Australia
  • Lodges Australian Age Pension claim in Belgium on 1 February 2017
  • Australian claim is received in Australia on 1 March 2018

Result:

Customer granted from 1 August 2015:

  • FP claim = AU claim is 1 August 2015
  • DOL is 1 February 2017
  • DOR is 1 March 2018
  • Start Date is 1 August 2015

Reason:

The agreement with Belgium started on 1 July 2005 and includes the 'claim for one = claim for other' provision. The provision applies without limit if the customer declares an affiliation with Australia on the Belgian pension claim.

Customer declared an affiliation with Australia when the Belgian pension claim was lodged on 1 August 2015 and the agreement was in force so the 'claim for one = claim for other' provision applies.

The customer was resident and present in Belgium on 1 August 2015 so meets the residence rules for claims under the agreement on the date the claim was 'taken to be made' using the 'claim for one = claim for other' provision.

The Start Date on the PNA screen must be manually moved to 1 August 2015.

4

Using the date of lodgement in the agreement country and CCD provisions

This is a deemed claim scenario where the customer contacts Services Australia about a claim.

Note: for claims made after 1 July 2018, the 'intent to claim' provisions apply only to customers in vulnerable circumstances. See Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.

Scenario:

  • Customer residing in and present in Estonia on all dates
  • Turns Australian Age Pension age on 1 May 2018
  • Customer contacts Services Australia on 7 May 2018 requesting help to make a claim
  • Lodges Australian Age Pension claim in Estonia on 2 July 2018
  • Australian claim is received in Australia on 1 November 2018

Result:

Customer granted from 7 May 2018:

  • CCD is 7 May 2018
  • DOL is 2 July 2018
  • DOR is 1 November 2018
  • Start Date is 7 May 2018

Reason:

The agreement with Estonia started on 1 January 2018.

As the claim was lodged after 1 July 2018, the CCD provision only applies if the customer is considered 'vulnerable'. Customers outside Australia are considered vulnerable and meet the special circumstances, which allows the extension of the CCD from 14 days up to 13 weeks.

The customer was qualified and payable on the CCD and lodged in Estonia more than 14 days but not more than 13 weeks after CCD.

As the customer was in Australia, was qualified on 7 May 2018 and lodged in Estonia within 13 weeks of the CCD, this means the CCD provision applies and the claim may be 'taken to be made' on 7 May 2018.

The customer was resident and present in Estonia on 7 May 2018 so meets the residence rules for claims under the agreement on the date the claim is 'taken to be made' using the CCD provision.

The Start Date on the PNA screen must be manually moved to 7 May 2018.

5

Using the date of lodgement in an agreement country and early claim provisions with lodgement rule policy

This is a deemed claim scenario where the customer lodges an early claim but does not meet the lodgement rules on both dates.

Scenario:

  • Customer residing in and present in Cyprus
  • Lodges Australian Age Pension claim in Cyprus on 1 July 2017
  • Goes to live in France from 1 August 2017
  • Turns Australian Age Pension age on 1 September 2017
  • Australian claim is received in Australia on 1 July 2018

Result:

Customer granted from 1 September 2017:

  • DOL is 1 July 2017
  • DOR is 1 July 2018
  • Start Date is 1 September 2017

Reason:

The agreement with Cyprus on 1 January 1993.

The customer was not qualified on 1 July 2017 but became qualified within 13 weeks, so the early claim provision applies and the claim may be 'taken to be made' on 1 September 2017.

The customer was resident and present in Cyprus on 1 July 2017, so meets the lodgement rules on DOL but was residing in France on 1 September 2017, so did not meet the lodgement rules on that date.

Policy is that a customer can meet the lodgement rules on the date the claim is made or the date the claim is taken to be made.

Customer meets the residence rules for claims under the agreement on the date the claim may be 'taken to be made' on 1 September 2017 using the early claim provision and lodgement rule policy.

The Start Date on the PNA screen must be manually moved to 1 September 2017.

6

Using the date of receipt in Australia and CCD provisions with lodgement rule policy

This is a deemed claim scenario where the customer contacts Services Australia and lodges but does not meet the lodgement rules on both dates.

Note: for claims made after 1 July 2018, the 'intent to claim' provisions apply only to customers in vulnerable circumstances. See Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.

Scenario:

  • Customer residing in and present in Latvia
  • Lodges Australian Age Pension claim in Latvia on 1 March 2018
  • Turns Australian Age Pension age on 1 August 2018
  • Customer travels to New Zealand and contacts Services Australia on 1 September 2018 about the claim
  • Returns to Latvia on 1 October 2018
  • Australian claim is received in Australia on 1 November 2018

Result:

  • DOL is 1 March 2018
  • CCD is 1 September 2018
  • DOR is 1 November 2018
  • Start Date is 1 September 2018

Reason:

The agreement with Latvia started on 1 January 2013.

Customer was not qualified on 1 March 2018 and did not become qualified within 13 weeks so cannot use DOL or early claim provision.

As the claim was lodged after 1 July 2018, the CCD provision only applies if the customer is considered 'vulnerable'. Customers outside Australia are considered vulnerable and meet the special circumstances, which allows the extension of the CCD from 14 days up to 13 weeks.

As the customer was qualified on 1 September 2018 and lodged in Australia within 13 weeks, this means the CCD provision applies and the claim may be 'taken to be made' on 1 September 2018.

Policy is that a customer can meet the lodgement rules on the date the claim is made or the date the claim is taken to be made.

The customer was not present in Latvia on 1 September 2018, so did not meet the lodgement rules on CCD but was resident and present in Latvia on 1 November 2018.

Customer meets the residence rules for claims under the agreement on the date the claim may be 'taken to be made' on 1 September 2018, using the CCD provision and lodgement rule policy.

The Start Date on the PNA screen must be manually moved to 1 September 2018.
