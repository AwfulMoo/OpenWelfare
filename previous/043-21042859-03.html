Determine if claim can be Express Processed (EP)

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Locate an allocated claim + Read more ...

Open TFES Assessor Tool.

View the list of allocated claims:

  • Select oldest claim in allocation
  • Note the Claimant Code is shown in each activity

2

Locate claim in myHSI + Read more ...

Open myHSI to locate the claim in system. Under My Tools:

  • select Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme (TFES) General
  • select Go

3

Claim type + Read more ...

The express process must only be applied to claims lodged online.

Check the Claim Details screen, under the Online heading. If the claim is:

  • a manual claim, Online will be N
  • an online claim, Online will be Y

Is this an online claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, this claim cannot be processed under EP, go to Step 8

4

Determine if the claim is for Southbound imported goods + Read more ...

Claims for southbound imported goods must not be express processed.

Check the Claim Details screen, under the Type heading.

If the claim is for Southbound imported goods, Type will be TFES Imported Goods

Is this claim for Southbound imported goods?

  • Yes, this claim cannot be processed under EP, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Determine if the claimant is on the approved claimant list + Read more ...

For initial inclusion on the Approved Claimant List, claimants must:

  • submit at least 25 claims per year
  • have history of accurate claims
  • have 100% accuracy on 5 most recent, fully assessed claims

Claimants included on the Approved Claimant List will be subject to quality assurance.

Is the claimant on the approved claimant list?

  • Yes, go to Table 2
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Claim amount + Read more ...

Check the Claim Details screen, under the Assist heading.

Is the claim $5000 or less?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, this claim cannot be processed under EP, go to Step 9

7

Number of paid claims + Read more ...

Check the Claims tab in the View Claimant Details screen.

Has the claimant had at least 5 claims paid?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, this claim cannot be processed under EP, go to Step 9

8

Check the claimant notes + Read more ...

Check the Notes tab in the View Claimant Details screen.

Is the claimant ineligible for EP?

  • Yes, this claim cannot be processed under EP, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Table 2

9

Unable to EP claim + Read more ...

Do a full assessment.

Determine the claim type:

  • TFES Northbound Domestic
  • TFES Other Markets
  • TFES Southbound M Scheme
  • TFES Southbound P Scheme
  • TFES Sportsperson and Entertainer
  • TFES Brood Mare
  • King Island Furneaux Group
  • Furneaux Group Additional Assistance

Has formal training been completed in this claim type?

  • Yes, assess the claim, see
    • Northbound domestic goods claim assessment for TFES
    • Northbound other markets claim assessment for TFES
    • Southbound manufacturers and miners claims for TFES (M Scheme)
    • Southbound primary producer claims for TFES (P Scheme)
    • Horse claim assessment for TFES
    • Sportsperson and Entertainer claim assessment for TFES
    • Brood mare claim assessment for TFES
    • Intrastate (King Island Furneaux Group) claim assessment for TFES
    • Furneaux Group Additional Assistance claim assessment for TFES

  • No, ask a Team Leader to reallocate the claim

Express Process (EP)

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Supporting documents + Read more ...

Are the supporting documents attached?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, claim cannot be processed under EP. Go to Step 7

2

Claimant name on invoices + Read more ...

Is the claimant name printed on all invoices?

Note: for claims over 20 lines, only check 10% of the invoices

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, claim cannot be processed under EP. Go to Step 7

3

Invoices numbers + Read more ...

Are all invoice numbers correct?

Note: for claims over 20 lines, only check 10% of the invoices.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, claim cannot be processed under EP. Go to Step 7

4

Claim received within 6 months of shipment + Read more ...

Are the shipment dates within 6 months of the receipt date of the claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Extension of time (EOT) + Read more ...

In Claimant notes, is there an EOT covering the shipments?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, claim cannot be processed under EP. Go to Step 7

6

Duplicate invoice warning + Read more ...

Are there any duplicate invoice warnings in the claim comments?

  • Yes, claim cannot be processed under EP. Go to Step 7
  • No, go to Table 3

7

Claim does not meet EP criteria + Read more ...

Is the mistake due to a keying error, e.g. typo in invoice number, which is not payment effecting and can be easily fixed?

  • Yes, go to Table 3
  • No, claim cannot be processed under EP. See Step 9 in Table 1

Accept the claim

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Access the claim in myHSI + Read more ...

In TFES General on myHSI locate the claim in the Activity List by selecting:

  • Activity - All
  • Role/user - Assessor
  • Key the claim number into Claim Id
  • Select - show
  • Select - Process claim
  • Select - Continue

Do any lines need to be amended?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Amend a claim line + Read more ...

Take a screen print of the original claim summary screen before making changes.

  • Select the Claim Id number
  • Select the claim line number to be changed
  • Select Update
  • Select the field to be update and make changes
  • Select Update
  • Record details of the amendment in the Claim Comments tab

Repeat for each claim line to be amended, go to Step 3

3

Accept the claim + Read more ...

In the Claim Comments tab:

  • key ‘This claim has been processed under the express process and is subject to standard QA protocols. Payment amount $xxx’
  • key date of lodgement in Adjusted Lodgement Date field:
    • Select a Reason for the Adjusted Lodgement Date from the dropdown menu
    • Select Save
  • in Assessment Results:
    • Select Accept from the dropdown menu
    • Select Complete

Completed claims may be randomly selected for quality assurance (QA). If the claim has been selected, a message will appear stating the claim has been selected for QA.

Has the claim been selected for QA?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Claim Pending Payment + Read more ...

  • If amendments were made, upload the original screen print to the Supporting Documents tab.
  • Put the payment advice letter in an envelope, with note to claimant if applicable, and place in the mail tray

Add claim details in the TFES Batch spreadsheet in R:\TAS\HOB-188-Collins\Transport_Resources\STATISTICS & REPORTS\Batch Statistics:

  • Select claim type tab at bottom of spreadsheet
  • Select Save before entering the following information:
    • Claim ID in column A
    • Claimant Name in column B
    • Amount of Assistance in column C
    • Select Save after entering information
    • Check the documents have been uploaded and are legible

Update TFES Registry Tool:

  • Tick the EP or Approved Claimant indicator
  • Change the activity status to Completed

Process ends here.

5

Claim pending QA + Read more ...

  • All EP claims are subject to 2% QA
  • The payment letter will not print when the claim is selected for QA
  • If amendments were made, upload the original screen print to the Supporting Documents tab

Update TFES Assessor Tool:

  • Tick the EP or Approved claimant indicator
  • In Comments/Details field:
    • key information relating to claim, and changes made
    • change the activity status to Awaiting QA office

The QA officer selects the claim from the TFES Assessor tool to complete the quality assurance process.

See Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme (TFES) quality checking for details on what happens if the QA check indicates the claim is correct or incorrect.
