This page contains the process for checking a home care monthly claim form is complete and how to process a claim.

Processing a home care monthly claim form

Step

Action

1

Assessing the claim channel + Read more ...

Home care claims can be submitted via the following channels:

  • Online via the Aged Care Provider Portal
  • Manual via email or posting the claim to Services Australia

Is this a manual claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No,
    • This is an online claim. For online claims, the claim registration process is automatically done as part of the claim finalisation
    • Service Officers must approve the finalised claim. See the Claim approvals table in Maintain claims in the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS)

2

Check fields completed + Read more ...

Note:

  • A monthly claim form cannot be submitted until the last day of the calendar month
  • If the claim form is submitted too early, the claim is not acceptable for processing
  • Parts 5 and 6 are available and required to be completed from the September 2021 claims onwards

Service Officers must check the following sections for completion.

Part 1 - Certification of Service Provider/Authorised Agent

  • Name of person authorised to sign
  • Signature of the authorised person
  • Date

Part 2 - Care recipients details

  • Care recipient ID
  • Care recipient surname
  • Care recipient first name
  • Gender
  • Date of birth

Part 5 - Commonwealth portion of unspent funds

  • Care recipient ID
  • Care recipient surname
  • Care recipient first name
  • Gender
  • Date of birth
  • Departure date
  • Care recipient Opt in Note this field is only available for claim periods of 1 September 2021 till 28 February 2022
  • Commonwealth portion of unspent home care amount (must be in dollar and cent format)

Part 6 - Invoice amount

  • Care recipient ID
  • Care recipient surname
  • Care recipient first name
  • Gender
  • Date of birth
  • Invoice amount (must be in dollar and cent format)

Is the claim form completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Contact service if required + Read more ...

Service Officers must contact the home care service if:

  • any of the information in Step 2 is missing, and/or
  • the claim was submitted too early

Contact can be made via email or telephone, advising:

  • what section on the form has not been completed, and
  • request the home care service to resubmit the completed claim form

Procedure ends here until the required details are provided. Note: once the resubmitted application is received, it will be treated as a new application.

4

Registering a home care manual monthly claim form + Read more ...

Register the claim in Aged Care Management and Payment System (ACMPS). See the Register claims and cancel registration table in Maintain claims in Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS)

Go to Step 5.

5

Processing Part 5 of the manual claim for existing care recipients + Read more ...

Note:

  • The Care recipient Opt in field is only available for claim periods of 1 September 2021 till 28 February 2022
  • Service Officers can disregard the Care recipient Opt in field for claim periods after 28 February 2022. Service Officers only need to create the Register reported Commonwealth unspent amount event

This step is in relation to Part 5 of the manual claim.

For care recipients whose

  • Commonwealth portion of unspent home care amount field is completed, create Register reported Commonwealth unspent amount event. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal for home care purposes - (Service in Context) table
  • Care recipient Opt in and the Commonwealth portion of unspent home care amount fields are completed, opt the care recipient in as part of creating the Register reported Commonwealth unspent amount event. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal for home care purposes - (Service in Context) table
  • Departure date and the Commonwealth portion of unspent home care amount fields are completed, create Departure event and Register reported Commonwealth unspent amount event. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal for home care purposes - (Service in Context) table

Go to Step 6.

6

Processing events for new and existing care recipients + Read more ...

With the exception to Part 5, this step is in regards to all remaining parts of the manual claim.

Service Officers can commence:

  • creating events for new care recipients as indicated on the claim form. See the Creating events table
  • updates to existing care recipient details as indicated on the claim form. See the Updating events table

Go to Step 7.

7

Finalising a home care manual monthly claim form + Read more ...

Service Officers can finalise the monthly claim form. See the Finalising claims table.

Go to Step 8

8

Approving a home care manual monthly claim form + Read more ...

A different Service Officer to the one who finalised the monthly claim form can approve the claim. See the Approve/reject events outside 2-year period in ACMPS table.
