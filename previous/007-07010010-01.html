Claiming payments for children entrusted to care as part of the process of adoption or surrogacy 007-07010010

This document outlines how to claim payments for adopted children or who enter care as part of surrogacy.

Making a claim

Customers with children in their care due to adoption or surrogacy arrangements may claim:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
  • Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
  • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP), or
  • Child Care Subsidy (CCS)

FTB can include Newborn Supplement (NBS) and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) if customers are not eligible for PPL.

An adoptive parent and a commissioning parent are different to foster parents and relatives with long term care. Services Australia needs evidence is required to confirm the care of the child/ren.

Adopted children

Customers can claim PPL and DAP for an adopted child if they are initially entrusted to the customer's care for the purpose of adoption before they turn 16 years of age. They can also claim FTB for the child.

If PPL is not payable and the customer:

  • is eligible, and
  • claims FTB Part A (adopted child any age) within 12 months of the child entering care, they will be assessed automatically for NBS and NBU

For a second or subsequent child entrusted under an adoption process, the lower rate of NBS is payable. The higher NBS rate is payable if multiple children are entrusted under the same process of adoption.

Adoption is a legal process and the customer needs to provide proof of adoption from an authorised party.

To be eligible for PPL or DAP for an adopted child, the child must have been placed in care by the authorised party for the purpose of the adoption. E.g. PPL and DAP are not payable if:

  • the child was initially placed with the claimant as a foster child, and
  • the process of adoption commenced after this date

If a child is adopted, more than one person may be eligible and payable for PPL/DAP or FTB (including NBS/NBU). For example, for PPL one claimant may be the birth mother and another claimant the adoptive parent.

Surrogacy arrangements

Customers can claim PPL, DAP and FTB for children entering care as part of a surrogacy arrangement.

For NBS eligibility, assess using the entrusted to care (non-parent) criteria.

Surrogacy is an agreement between the surrogate birth mother and the commissioning parent/s. Customers need to provide proof of the surrogacy arrangement following the birth of the child. This includes proof that the birth mother of the child has relinquished care of the child.

To be eligible for PPL or DAP, the surrogacy arrangement must be agreed before the child is conceived.

In surrogacy arrangements, more than one person may be eligible and payable for PPL/DAP or FTB (including NBS/NBU) for the child. For example, for:

  • PPL, the birth mother is able to claim PPL in the period following the birth to enable time off work for maternal recovery
  • FTB (including NBS/NBU), or DAP, any claimant meeting eligibility requirements including having care of the child may still be paid. For example
    • one claimant may be the biological father/partner of the surrogate and claiming for a period before the child has left the care of the surrogate birth mother, and
    • another claimant might be the partner of the commissioning parent

Kinship care

Indigenous and Torres Strait Islander customers may have a child entrusted to their care as part of their traditional law. For PPL and DAP, these care arrangements are treated the same as a foster care child.

Torres Straight Island customers who have a child or children entrusted to their care as part of a kinship care arrangement are required to provide evidence if required to support the change of care arrangement. This would include Family Court documents such as a parentage order or adoption papers.
