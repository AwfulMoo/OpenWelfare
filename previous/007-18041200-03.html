Family assistance

  • Estimate/payment choice for FTB/CCS: although an estimate for the partner cannot be coded until the records are linked, advise the customer they should provide an estimate and/or revise their payment choice as soon as possible to reduce the risk of an overpayment, as their current FTB/CCS rate will continue until either:

1

Benefit Transfer (BTR) from PPS to PPP + Read more ...

Benefit transfers must be processed in Customer First. They must not be processed in Process Direct as the outcome will not be accurate.

The records must be linked within the BTR activity. If both customers are receiving PPS, this activity will cancel PPS for the other member of the couple.

Do not link customer records while PPS is suspended, unless with a BTR activity in which restoration of the suspended record(s) must also be completed.

If the records have been linked incorrectly outside the BTR activity and PPS has cancelled, a new claim can be processed if the date of effect of the cancellation is correct (see the Resources page). In all other cases refer to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk. The Resources page has a link.

The partner is required to confirm the relationship before the benefit transfer can be finalised. Confirmation can be provided by 1 of the following methods and must have been received before the claim can be submitted online or via the ACC:

  • a MOD P signed by both parties
  • verbally
  • online using a temporary access code (TAC). Note: to view if the partner has verified the relationship online via the TAC:
    • Open Process Direct and select Customer Summary

    • Open Digital Partner Confirmation (DMODP) tile to view if the partner has confirmed the relationship

3

Employment income + Read more ...

The customer's employment income details up until the start date of PPP must be recorded before processing the BTR activity. Any employment income details for both the customer and their partner from the date became partnered up until the date of claim submission, is recorded within the BTR activity.

Employment income details for both the customer and their partner received after the date of claim submission is recorded after the BTR activity is finalised.

Note: for PP customers (under Age Pension age), an employment income nil rate period can continue if their relationship status changes and their PP is reassessed (transfers between PPS and PPP).

See Recording and correcting employment income details for further assistance.

4

Check family assistance + Read more ...

  • If the customer is also receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) or Child Care Subsidy (CCS), their partner must be linked to update estimate and continue payment
  • If the customer has reconciled with the person who pays maintenance:
    • To end a current Child Support assessment/collection, issue the Ending a Child Support Assessment (CS1671), refer the customer to Child Support
    • Ensure the payer's Customer Reference Number (CRN) has been updated on the Maintenance Entitlement Group Summary (MNGS) screen. See Maintenance Action Test (MAT) codes
  • Check/update customer's income estimate/payment choice for FTB/CCS. The estimate must include PPS or any other payments received, employment income and any other components of adjusted taxable income (ATI) (received or expected) for the financial year for both members of the couple. If either member of the couple is eligible for income support payment, including PPP, the expected payments must also be included in the estimate
  • If the customer and/or their partner are CCS current, check to see if either of their activity test needs updating. See Activity Test for Child Care Subsidy

Advise entitlement to FTB Lump Sum Advances. For more information about reassessing FTB/CCS, see Family assistance customer becomes partnered.

5

Check start date/arrears + Read more ...

The system should determine the correct date of effect (DOE). Ensure the correct DOE of the rate decrease from PPS to PPP has been assessed. See Resources for common scenarios for confirming correct DOE and a manual workaround if issues are encountered.

If the customer has been paid PPS since the PPP start day, the system should automatically adjust the PPP to deduct the amount already paid. If necessary, check details on the Benefit Transfer (BTR) screen.

System Issue: If there is an earlier CCD date recorded or if the PPP start date is amended for any reason, this may result in an incorrect payment to the customer. It is therefore very important to check that the start date for PPP and the end date of PPS match before finalising the activity.

If the start and end dates match, go to Step 6.

If the start and end dates do not match:

  • Print the PPP 'claim summary'
  • Cancel the claim
  • Run a new ACC for the PPS customer:
    • ACC via the ACC desktop icon and entering the customer's CRN
    • Select Make a new claim when ready to start
    • From the Make a claim page, select Start a new claim, then select Get started under the Families life event followed by Apply for Parenting Payment

In the streaming questions: ensure the Date of Receipt (DOR) is changed before selecting Claim now.

  • The DOR is the date the customer advised Services Australia of their relationship status change. The system should then determine the correct date of effect.
    See Resources for common scenarios for confirming correct date of effect

Continue to answer ACC questions using 'claim summary' from previous claim and submit again to process. Inhibit letters and required documents to ensure no further requests are sent out to the customer.

Ensure start and end dates are checked again, go to Step 6.

If the customer is also receiving FTB, remind them that they will also need to provide an updated FTB estimate separate from their income updates for Parenting Payment.

Restore PPS + Read more ...

As this is a benefit transfer activity, there is no need to cancel PPS before processing the PPP claim. If PPS is suspended for any reason on or after the date the customer became partnered and is eligible to be restored, restore PPS in the PEN system within the benefit transfer activity via the Benefit Action (BA) screen after data load has been completed and before finalising the claim.

If PPS is suspended for any reason from a date before the date the customer became partnered, see Restoration of Parenting Payment (PP).

The restoration will need to be actioned in Customer Record due to the inability to swap to the PEN system while in the BTR activity in Customer First.

10

Manually changing the status of workflows + Read more ...

Service Officers can change the status of the workflow by selecting the Change Status toggle icon in line with the relevant workflow to change the status from:

  • Required to Not Required
  • Completed to Required, or
  • Completed with errors to Completed

Circumstances which may warrant a change of status from Completed to Required:

  • During a claim assessment, before running the Finalise Claim workflow, details already coded in any workflow require correction
  • When running Finalise Claim, an error is identified and the specific workflow is not automatically re-opened
  • QOL decision outcome of not passed and workflow(s) must be re-run to code or correct required information, as identified in the QOL decision. Steps:
    • via Workspace, go to Activity List (AL) screen, select the claim activity and select U (undo submitted activity not passed in QOL) option and Continue
    • enter the Social Application (claim workflow)
    • identify the workflow(s) that must be re-run and select the Change Status toggle icon to update workflow status to Required
    • select/run the relevant workflow(s)
    • once all required workflows have been completed, select the Change Status toggle icon in line with the Finalise Claim workflow
  • Select/run the Finalise Claim workflow to finalise the claim and complete the workflow

11

Manual updates resulting from data load + Read more ...

Some information from the customer's online claim may not be automatically uploaded into the new claim activity when the data load workflow is run if:

  • it is deemed complex and needs Service Officer discretion/assessment, or
  • the data provided by the customer conflicts with existing data on their record

Note: to identify if a referral is required to the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO), see Identifying and making suitable referrals to the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO)

Any updates not completed in the staff workflows can be manually actioned:

  • launch Workspace
  • select the new claim activity from the AL screen
  • make any required updates
  • return to the online claim using the Search Online Claims functions under the Claims Online heading in Workspace

12

Finalise workflow + Read more ...

The Finalise Claim workflow is run once all the required actions have been completed and all claim information has been updated.

The workflow presents any errors or warnings on the Assessment Warning and Error (AWE) screen. These may have to be actioned before the claim can be finalised.

Once all errors have been rectified, the workflow will present the Assessment Results (AR) screen where the new claim can be finalised.

After selecting Finalise and Continue, select Finish in the top right corner to complete the workflow and return to the Online Claim.

The Finalise Claim workflow will now show as Completed in the Staff Workflows.

The Social Application (claim workflow) status will automatically update to Approved, but there may be a delay of up to 30 minutes before this update occurs.

The Social Application status can also be manually updated to Approved, once the Finalise Claim workflow is completed, select:

  • User Status tab
  • Set New Status
  • click on grey box next to Approved
  • select Save

No automatic claim finalisation DOC is created once the claim has been finalised.

If transfer to PPP:

  • can be completed, go to Step 13
  • cannot be completed, go to Step 14

13

PPP transfer can be completed + Read more ...

  • Record details of the decision to transfer from PPS to PPP as per section 500(1) of the Social Security Act 1991 on a DOC. This includes where the transfer activity can be completed but PPP is rejected as the customer is no longer qualified for PP, or PP at the PPP rate is not payable. Create the DOC by manually selecting the Grant/Reject PPP NCL Fast Note from the Documents & Correspondence assignment block within Workspace. Go to Fast Note > Auto text > Parents > Claims > Grant/Reject PPP NCL
  • Estimate/payment choice for FTB/CCS: the estimate must include any income support payments received, employment income and any other components of adjusted taxable income (ATI) (received or expected) for the financial year for both members of the couple. If either member of the couple is eligible for income support payment, including PPS, the expected payments must also be included in the estimate. For more information, see Income support customers and FTB/CCS
  • If the customer and/or their partner are CCS current, check to see if either of their activity test needs updating. See Activity Test for Child Care Subsidy
  • Stimulate final payment of the previous income support payment if necessary on the Review Results (RR) screen

Customers can report online immediately following PPP being granted once their first reporting date falls due.

If payment has been backdated and the customer is owed payment for 2 fortnights or more, the customer must be contacted to ensure any income received by the customer or their partner is declared and updated. The outstanding payments must then be stimulated by the Service Officer (via RR screen) to prevent PP cancelling overnight.

Check the MAS screen in the PEN system to see if the customer was receiving a manual rate of payment of PPS. If the customer was on a manual rate, end date this with the date prior to the cessation of the PPS. See Non NSS systems coding for manual rate of payment, Step 8 in Table 1.

If with the customer, advise of the following:

  • New rate of payment
  • Any ParentsNext participation requirements or Voluntary participation with Workforce Australia
  • Energy Supplement is paid if the customer is eligible. If a frequency change is requested, check/update Energy Supplement payment frequency option
  • Check family assistance entitlement and invite claim (if appropriate). If the customer has separated from the parent of a child in their care, check if they need to provide child support details and take maintenance action
  • Reporting requirements. Customers on 2 weekly reporting will need to report to be paid. Discuss the priority reporting line and self service options for reporting. Remind them to also keep FTB estimate up to date even though they are reporting their income as these are separate assessments
  • Automatic issue of Health Care Card (HCC) or (Pensioner Concession Card in some circumstances) and entitlement to Commonwealth Health concessions
  • Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work) provides additional help with the cost of child care while the customer is undertaking an approved activity

PPP transfer cannot be completed + Read more ...

A PP benefit transfer cannot be rejected as the customer is already current on PP. The online claim is a means of collecting the required information to process the benefit transfer which is a change of circumstances. The following process needs to be followed if the benefit transfer is unable to be completed:

  • Cancel the claim activity on the Activity List (AL) screen
  • Within the online claim, change all workflow status's to Not required. Any information that needs to be updated such as contact details will need to be updated in a separate activity
  • From the User Status tab, select:
    • Set New Status
    • Completed
    • Save - the customer's online service will now show the claim as Completed
  • Suspend PPS for reason FRC. For more information on suspending PPS, including detail on action to be taken and when a letter should be manually issued, see Suspension of Parenting Payment (PP)
  • Record the details of the decision on a DOC via Workspace > Documents and Correspondence > Fast Note > Auto text > Parents > SUS/CAN/RES > PP Cancelled/Suspended
    • Add the following information to the suspension DOC
      'Customer lodged online claim (include ID number) to transfer to PPP. The benefit transfer was unable to be completed. If the customer provides the required documents or information to enable the transfer to be completed, re-index and regenerate the claim in Customer First'
    • List all information required for a reassessment
      Note: when the claim is regenerated, the customer's online view will again show the claim status as 'submitted'
  • Estimate/payment choice for FTB/CCS: the estimate must include any income support payments received, employment income and any other components of adjusted taxable income (ATI) (received or expected) for the customer. See Updating income estimates for the current financial year to send a FA078. If the customer fails to provide an income estimate within 21 days for themselves and partner, their FTB will be cancelled. For more information see Family assistance customer becomes partnered
  • If the customer and/or their partner are CCS current, check to see if either of their activity test needs updating. See Activity Test for Child Care Subsidy
