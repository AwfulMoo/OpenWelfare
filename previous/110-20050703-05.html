Income compliance program - recording customer interaction

General FAQ

Table 1

Item

Question

1

The class action has been settled. What does this mean?

The hearing for the Income Compliance class action settlement was held on 6 and 7 May 2021.

The court approved the Income Compliance class action settlement on 11 June 2021.

The agency is working with the court, Gordon Legal and the settlement implementation plan to deliver settlement payments to registered eligible group members as soon as possible.

Letters were issued between 29 October 2021 - 19 November 2021 about:

  • eligibility for a settlement payment based on the debt categorisation
  • how a group member can update payment details
  • how to lodge a query or dispute

Letters will be issued by mid-2022 to eligible registered group members on how payments are calculated and the settlement amount to be received.

You do not need to call or visit us unless requested to via correspondence.

2

What is the settlement distribution scheme?

The scheme is a requirement of the settlement agreement. It explains how the Commonwealth will:

  • work out who is eligible for a settlement payment
  • find and notify group members of the settlement outcome and their eligibility
  • calculate and distribute settlement payments

3

What does the $112 million consist of? Is this interest, compensation or both?

The settlement payment is not compensation like WorkCover. In the settlement of the class action it was agreed that the amount of $112 million (minus Gordon Legal's court approved costs) would be paid to eligible group members in addition to the refund amounts, to settle all claims.

Under the terms of settlement, we’ll make payments similar to interest to eligible group members.

4

Can I object to the settlement?

The objection phase is closed.

The agency sent a letter to all group members with more information about the proposed settlement. This included information on how to lodge an objection.

Section 26 to 31 of the notice provided details about the objection process, including the objections closing date of 4:00 pm Friday 5 March 2021.

5

If I am a group member who objected to the settlement but the settlement is approved, will I still get a settlement payment?

If you did not opt out of the settlement and you are an eligible group member you will still get a settlement payment.

The final opt-out process closed at 4:00 pm on Friday 17 September 2021. After this date group members are bound by the terms of the settlement. No further opt-out requests can be lodged.

6

How do I know if I am eligible and will get a settlement payment?

Group members are eligible if they have paid money towards a debt that was based in full, or part, on averaged ATO income information.

Not every group member will be eligible under the settlement.

Group members will fall into separate categories, see Table 5 Categorisation of Group members’ debts.

7

How can I find out what category I am in?

Debt/s have been assigned a category. Letters were issued between 29 October 2021 - 19 November 2021.

The letter explained:

  • what category your debt is in
  • whether or not you are eligible for a settlement payment
  • how to lodge a dispute if you believed your debt is in the wrong category
  • the deadline to lodge a dispute, and provide or update your bank details

The dedicated phone line is available for group members to request information and record or update their details:

  • transfer the call using Softphone to Compliance-ClassActionSettlement Line or
  • give the compliance number 1800 171 846

8

Can a group member make a claim for compensation?

Group members who did not opt out

Group members who did not opt out are prevented under the settlement from making further claims for compensation (or otherwise) if:

  • the nature of the claim is in the same nature as the claims made in the proceeding, or
  • arise out of the same subject matter

This comes under the Scheme for Compensation for Detriment caused by Defective Administration (CDDA scheme).

The group members were sent a letter telling them that the claim for compensation has been finalised.

If group members have more questions call the Customer Compensation team on 1800 995 496.

Group members who opted out

These customers are able to make a claim for compensation through the CDDA Scheme. They are not bound by the settlement.

Customers can make a claim on the Services Australia website - Claiming compensation from us.

9

Will group members not eligible for payment under the settlement be contacted?

All group members have been notified of their debt/s category/s and eligibility for a settlement payment. This includes those who are not eligible for payment under the settlement.

10

If I am eligible how much will I receive?

Settlement payments will be made to registered group members. People who paid back more and were without this money longer will get larger payments.

Payments will be calculated in a similar way to an interest payment. The agency will use interest rates set by the court to calculate each group member's portion.

Payments are not the same as a refund. Most of the refunds referred to in the settlement have already been paid to eligible people.

The amount received depends on when you paid money on the eligible debts, how much you paid and when you were refunded.

Once all other steps in the Implementation plan have been completed, payments will be sent to group members' bank accounts. Payments needing manual processing or tailored servicing may take longer.

For more information on timeframes, see Table 6 Implementation Plan timeframes.

11

When will I receive the money?

We expect to start making payments by mid-2022. These will be spread out over a number of months.

For more information on timeframes, see Table 6 Implementation Plan timeframes.

12

How will you make sure that the settlement distribution is fair?

The Commonwealth has appointed an independent Scheme Assurer to make sure the settlement distribution is equitable and completed in line with the settlement agreement.

The Scheme Assurer and Gordon Legal will review the assessment of the debts to make sure debts are in the right category.

The Scheme Assurer and Gordon Legal will also confirm key steps of the Implementation Plan are complete before it can progress to the next step.

For more information on timeframes, see Table 6 Implementation Plan timeframes.

13

What is the implementation plan?

There are a number of steps that need to happen before we can make settlement payments. The Implementation Plan gives a timeframe for each step.

To view a summary of this timeframe see Table 6 Implementation Plan timeframes.

14

I saw the class action settlement on the news. I have some questions?

See the class action query script.

15

What is the difference between class action and income compliance refunds?

The class action and income compliance refunds are 2 separate processes.

The class action:

  • includes people who had debts raised under the Income Compliance Program, regardless of how the agency calculated the debt

Income compliance refunds:

  • apply to recovered debts raised using averaged ATO income information

Not everyone who got a class action notice will be eligible:

  • for a refund, or
  • will receive a settlement payment

Settlement payments are not the same as a refund. Most of the refunds referred to in the settlement have already been paid to eligible people.

16

What if I am a representative of a customer's deceased estate? (e.g. next of kin, executor or administrator)

Transfer the customer using Softphone to Compliance - ClassActionSettlement - Executor.

17

The notice says that Services Australia will develop a web portal and help line for group members. Where can I find this information?

Categorisation letters and payment letters advise group members about:

  • the web portal
  • dedicated phone line for group members to ask for information
  • how to lodge disputes
  • how to record or update banking details

18

Is my settlement payment taxable?

The payments are tax exempt. Group members do not have to report the settlement amounts in their individual income tax returns. They will not have any deductions available to them for any costs associated with the receipt of the payment.

19

Do I have to include the settlement payment when reporting my income to Centrelink?

No, the settlement payment does not need to be reported to Centrelink in your income reporting.

20

Does my settlement payment count as an asset?

It may count as an asset, for example if you invest the money.

Staff queries FAQ

Table 3

Item

Question

1

How should staff reference the class action

The agency is referring to the class action as the 'income compliance program class action'.

Customers may have heard of this being referred to as:

  • robodebt
  • robodebt class action
  • robodebt letter, or
  • robodebt legal notice

2

How do staff know if a customer was sent the class action notice?

Categorisation letters were sent over a 3 week period.

Once the letter was processed the Class action settlement DOC displays on the customer's Centrelink Document List (DL) screen.

3

How can staff view or reissue the categorisation letter to customers?

Non-trained staff are NOT to reissue the letter.

View the letter in outbound correspondence.

If the customer asks for a copy of the categorisation letter transfer using Softphone to Compliance-ClassActionSettlement Line or provide Compliance number 1800 171 846.

4

How many reviews can a group member have?

The categorisation review period is now closed.

The payment review period will open once payment letters have been issued.

After an initial review by our specialist officers (Tier 1), the group member can request an escalation to the Scheme Assurer (Tier 2) for an independent review.

Once a QDO (Quality Development Officer) has finalised the decision from the Scheme Assurer there are no further options to dispute the Scheme Assurers decision.

Suggested script to help with the conversation:

  • ‘The categorisation of your debt/s has been reviewed by our specialist officer. Your query was then escalated to the Scheme Assurer for an additional independent review
  • ‘The decision from the Scheme Assurer about your debt category has been finalised
  • ‘There are no further options available to dispute the independent Scheme Assurer’s decision. We acknowledge this may not be the outcome you had hoped for
  • ‘You may contact Gordon Legal, who are the solicitors for the Representative Applicants and group members. They will be able to provide more information and assistance about the settlement distribution scheme’

5

What information can I give a customer if they ask about the income compliance refunds and they are part of the class action?

Repayments from debts raised using income averaging of ATO data will be returned directly to customers and will not be allocated to other Centrelink debts.

I have other outstanding debts with Centrelink. Can the refund be paid directly off these debts? Why not?

You can use your refund to pay off other debts by making a cash payment via Money you owe.

6

What can I tell an executor or administrator of a customer's estate?

If a deceased customer's next of kin, executor or administrator contacts about the class action notice, please transfer using Softphone to Compliance-ClassActionSettlement-Executor.

7

I missed the cut off date for making a dispute of my categorisation, and I want to dispute now.

Advise the customer:

The closure date for lodging a dispute regarding your categorisation letter was set in line with the court directions. The closure date of 10 December 2021 has now passed therefore no further requests can be lodged and your debt/s remain in the same category/s.

  • You can contact Gordon Legal who are the solicitors for the Representative Applicants and group members. They will be able to provide more information and assistance about the settlement distribution scheme.

8

I missed the cut off date for registering and I want to register now

Tell the customer:

  • The closure date for registering for payment was 11 February 2022 as advised in your categorisation letter sent in November 2021
  • This date was set in line with the court directions, was agreed to by Gordon Legal and the scheme assurers
  • Under the settlement terms, there are no appeal options if you haven’t registered in time. You cannot be included in the calculation and therefore will not receive a settlement payment
  • Note: these deadlines do not apply to outstanding income compliance refunds. If you are a former customer who is eligible for a refund and have not provided us with your bank details yet, please contact the Income Compliance line on 1800 171 846

You can contact Gordon Legal, the solicitors for the representative applicants and group members, for more information and assistance.

9

How can I update my payment destination

Eligible registered group members can update their payment destination within the class action settlement by contacting Income Compliance staff on 1800 171 846.

Note: bank account details are not to be updated manually via the Payment Destination Summary screen (PAS) or in the payment destination workflow. This will NOT update payment destination for the settlement payment. Eligible registered group members must have payment destination updated within the class action settlement transaction.

Categorisation of group members' debts

Table 5

Category type

Eligibility and outcome

Category 1

Not eligible:

Group members whose debts were based on averaged ATO income information and have made no repayments will:

  • have their averaged debt zeroed
  • not get a refund or a settlement payment

Scenario:

Customer notified of debt but they DID NOT make any repayments to this debt. The debt has been zeroed.

The customer is not eligible for disbursement as no monies were paid to or held by the agency for any period of time.

Category 2

Eligible:

Group members whose debts were wholly or partially based on averaged ATO income information and had made a repayment towards those debts will receive a settlement payment.

This payment is in addition to the refund already received.

Scenario:

Customer notified of debt AND made repayments to this debt.

This debt has been refunded and zeroed.

The customer is eligible for disbursement based on:

  • the amount paid towards the debt
  • the timeframe these monies were held by the agency
  • when the monies were refunded

Category 3

Eligible:

Group members whose debts were initially based on averaged ATO income information, but were recalculated using evidence such as:

  • payslips or
  • bank statements,
  • and had overpaid the recalculated debt

These group members will receive a settlement payment for the overpaid amount of their recalculated debt, whether the overpayment was refunded or applied to pay a valid debt.

This group will not receive an additional refund under the government's announcement of 29 May 2020. Any original overpayment would have been refunded at the time of recalculation.

Scenario:

Initial ATO based debt $950, after recalculation with evidence debt dropped to $800 AND customer had paid above the $800.

The correct debt has been repaid and the customer is eligible for disbursement based on the amount paid above the $800, the timeframe these monies were held by the Agency and when the monies were refunded.

Not eligible:

Group members whose debts were initially based on averaged ATO income information, but were recalculated using:

  • payslips or
  • bank statements,
  • and had not overpaid the recalculated debt

These group members will not receive a refund or a settlement payment.

Scenario:

Initial ATO based debt $950, after recalculation with evidence debt dropped to $800 AND customer paid the $800 or less.

The customer is not eligible for disbursement as no monies above the recoverable amount were paid to or held by the agency for any period of time.

Scenario:

Debt initially generated based on ATO income information, but customer gave supporting evidence:

  • payslips for the debt to be recalculated, and
  • had made repayments to the value of the recalculated debt or less

The customer is not eligible for disbursement as no monies above the recoverable amount were paid to or held by the agency for any period of time.

Category 4

Not eligible:

Group members whose debts were not based on averaged ATO income information.

These group members will not receive a refund or a settlement payment.
