Assessing eligibility for SEE

Step

Action

1

Identifying language, literacy numeracy and digital literacy needs for job seekers + Read more ...

Language, literacy, numeracy and digital literacy needs may be identified through:

  • job seeker's self-identification
  • job seeker requiring interpreter assistance during contact
  • responses to Questions in the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) which relate to language, literacy and numeracy ability
  • the Service Officer, who can manually trigger an MFU
  • the Service Officer's observations and discussions with the job seeker, at any contact
  • interrogation of system and job seeker history
  • the manner in which a job seeker completes forms
  • successive appointments missed by the job seeker
  • the job seeker having previously been unsuccessful in education, training or employment
  • the job seeker contacting Services Australia for an explanation of advices
  • the job seeker asking the reception staff for help in completing forms or using self-serve terminals

Is the job seeker potentially suitable or likely to benefit from participating in Skills for Education and Employment (SEE)?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here

2

Currently participating in Adult Migrant English Program (AMEP) + Read more ...

Open the Referral Summary.

Is the job seeker currently placed in Adult Migrant English Program (AMEP)?

  • Yes, and the job seeker is eligible for referral to SEE.
    If they commence in SEE, they will be automatically exited from AMEP. If the customer:
    • still wants to be referred, go to Step 3
    • wants to stay in AMEP, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Eligibility criteria for SEE + Read more ...

From 01 July 2021, customers are eligible for SEE if all of the following apply. They are:

  • of working age (between 15 years and Age Pension age), and
  • registered with Services Australia or an Australian Government employment service. This includes customers who are not receiving an income support payment (volunteer job seekers), and
  • deemed suitable for training, without any barriers that would prevent successful participation, and
  • either an Australian citizen, or permanent resident, or have working rights in Australia

Note: the Services Australia system does not currently support referrals to SEE for all eligible customers listed above.

For details on making a SEE referral, and options for those who cannot be referred due to current system limitations, see Referral to Skills for Education and Employment (SEE).

4

Job seekers not eligible for SEE + Read more ...

Job seekers are not eligible to undertake the SEE program if they:

  • are in New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS)
  • are in Adult Migrant English Program (AMEP)
  • are a current holder of a skilled migrant visa, or a secondary visa holder (dependent) of a person who holds a skilled migrant visa
  • have completed the SEE program and the last program exit date is within 12 weeks of the next referral date
  • have been exited or suspended from the program within the last 12 weeks. Where a client is transferring to a new provider:
    • this rule does not apply to job seekers who are relocating
    • job seekers, who have withdrawn from SEE before completing the training, may recommence training within 12 weeks of the withdrawal date by contacting their previous SEE provider directly
    • the job seeker's eligibility resumes on conclusion of the 12 week waiting period

The Resources page contains a link to a list of skilled migrant visas.

Does the job seeker meet eligibility criteria for Services Australia to make the referral to SEE?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, and:
    • the reason for not meeting the eligibility criteria is that the job seeker is not registered as a volunteer job seeker, go to Step 5
    • for all other customers, procedure ends here

5

Check eligibility for registration as a volunteer job seeker + Read more ...

If the customers is not registered as a job seeker, determine if they are eligible and willing to be referred to employment services as a volunteer job seeker.

Volunteer job seekers can access a maximum of 12 months of employment services. However, they can remain in SEE until they complete the activity. If the customer is not eligible for referral or does not want to be registered as a volunteer job seeker, they cannot be referred to SEE.

Does the customer wish to register for employment services as a volunteer job seeker?

  • Yes:
    • register them as a volunteer job seeker, then
    • go to Step 6
  • No:
    • the customer is not eligible for SEE
    • procedure ends here

6

Job seeker is eligible for SEE + Read more ...

Record an ACTDOC for the Service Centre to action. Use Auto text. Select Nosub > Manual action Doc > Generate Document Content. Use See Referral in the Extra Details field.

Include 'The customer would benefit from referral to either SEE or AMEP program. Please make the referral' in the document content.

Record a referral to SEE.

When a referral is flagged as part of a claim:

  • record an open DOC with the keyword 'SEE'
  • this is flagged for follow up when finalising the claim
