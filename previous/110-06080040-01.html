Manually loading a Partner Review in the Integrated Review System (IRS) 110-06080040

For service centre, zone office and Review staff.

This document outlines information about how a review may be required to confirm partner details as part of a customer's eligibility and entitlement to Centrelink payments. Note: where the term 'review' is used, this also applies to interventions.

On this Page:

Manually loading Partner Reviews

As this procedure relates to manually adding a Partner Review from within the Integrated Review System (IRS), it is only applicable for Review Types which are processed in IRS, such as Compliance or Fraud reviews.

If the partner is in receipt of payment in their own right then a separate review should be conducted and a separate result claimed on IRS. A Partner Review should only be loaded if the information received is likely to impact the partner's or ex-partner's payment(s). The Partner Review should be created as soon as action is commenced on the original review in order to attribute the results of any actions. If the Partner Review has not been created before action is commenced, attributable results will not be able to be claimed.

Partner not in receipt of Centrelink income support payment

Where the partner is not in receipt of a Centrelink income support payment, details should still be confirmed to ensure that there are no changes in circumstances that will affect the primary customer's payment. Any changes made due to the partner's circumstances where the partner is not a customer, will need to be recorded as results for the original review.

A Partner Review must not be initiated if the partner is not a customer, or did not receive entitlement during the period in question. There is no legal basis to review a person who is not a Centrelink customer in their own right. Another person's circumstances may be investigated as part of a review of the entitlements of a Centrelink customer, even though that person is not a customer, if that person's circumstances could affect the entitlement of the Centrelink customer.

Service Updates and Program Reviews

Service Updates and Program Reviews will have a Secondary Partner Service Update or Review automatically loaded to the IRS when the customer being reviewed has a current partner. Secondary Service Updates or reviews are identified in IRS by a Secondary Review Type of 'PPL' which can be used as part of any search criteria on all IRS search screens to identify all Secondary Partner Service Updates or Reviews associated with the search.

To create a Partner Review in the IRS, it must have the same Review Type as the primary review. This must be created by accessing the New Review (IRNR) screen. The manual Partner Review must have a Review Reason of 'MPR' (Partner Review - manual).

It is not appropriate to use the manual partner code of 'MPR' to load a new review to IRS where the review is for another reason other than a manual partner review. For example, loading an 'MPR' when the customer has no linked partner.

Note: not all reviews processed in IRS are subject to manually adding a Partner Review.

The Resources page contains the Austrac Access and Disclosure document and a table of types of Partner Reviews which can be loaded to IRS.
