Customer enquiries for early release of superannuation

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Service Australia’s role - issue eligibility letters + Read more ...

Services Australia's only role is to issue an eligibility letter to the customer to confirm if they meet the income support requirements for the early release of superannuation under financial hardship grounds.

Service Officers should always use either of the following to assess the customer’s record and issue an eligibility letter:

  • the Issue Superannuation Advice workflow in Process Direct
  • the Issue Super Advice guided procedure in Customer First

Manual issue of eligibility letters

An eligibility letter should only be issued manually if the workflow in Process Direct or the Guided Procedure in Customer First are unavailable, or a system error has returned an incorrect result. A Note/DOC must be added to the customer's record explaining why a manual letter was required.

Before manually issuing a letter (Q230 or Q251), Service Officers must check to confirm that the customer meets the income support requirements. For more details, see Step 1 in Table 2.

Note: an incorrect letter can cause consequences for the customer when they lodge an application with their super fund, including rejection of their application. This is common when the super fund uses CCeS and the information returned through the service does not match the information on the customer’s letter.

2

Check for involuntary nominee arrangement + Read more ...

Check the Nominee Link Summary (NOLS) screen.

If the customer does not have a nominee arrangement, go to Step 3.

If they have a nominee arrangement, check the Nominee Relationship Summary (NORS) screen.

Does the customer have CRT (Court appointed involuntary nominee arrangement) or PAI (Power of Attorney involuntary nominee arrangement) recorded as Reason for Arrangement?

  • Yes,
    • Contact the nominee to get approval to issue an eligibility letter. A copy of the letter must be sent to the nominee and their name included in the DOC
    • If unable to contact the nominee or they do not give approval to issue an eligibility letter, tell the customer their nominee will need to request the eligibility letter. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Reached preservation age and retired + Read more ...

Check the customer's preservation age. See Preservation age for access to superannuation on the Resources page.

Has the customer reached their preservation age?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, assess their eligibility for early release. To do this in:
    • Process Direct, go to Step 5
    • Customer First, go to Step 6

4

Check if permanently retired + Read more ...

Is the customer permanently retired?

  • Yes,
    • They may be able to access their superannuation under the normal release conditions
    • Tell the customer to contact their superannuation fund
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, assess their eligibility for early release. To do this in:
    • Process Direct, go to Step 5
    • Customer First, go to Step 6

5

Assess eligibility in Process Direct + Read more ...

  • Key START in the Super Key
  • Select the Issue Superannuation Advice workflow from the Task Selector
  • Select Start
  • The Issue superannuation advice summary screen will show the customer’s eligibility information and any letters to be generated
  • Select Source from drop down list
  • Select Print location:
    • Central, or
    • Local. Update the Printer field (this field only shows where ‘local’ print location is selected)
  • Select Print to finalise the workflow
  • Once finalised the Snapshot page opens

Procedure ends here.

6

Assess eligibility in Customer First + Read more ...

Run the Issue Super Advice guided procedure. If the guided procedure is not available, see Step 1 in Table 2.

Did the Summary screen on the last page of the guided procedure advise the customer meets the income support requirements?

  • Yes:
    • select Finish. An eligibility letter will be issued to the customer
    • see Step 5 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

Manually calculate continuous period and cumulative period and issue eligibility letter

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check eligibility + Read more ...

Go to the Pensions General Information (PNGI) screen. Check the result in the Early Release super Eligible field.

The field will show one of the following codes:

  • Y - customer is receiving an eligible income support payment, and has been continuously for the last 26 weeks
  • F - customer is receiving an eligible income support payment, and has been so for a cumulative period of 39 weeks after reaching their preservation age plus 39 weeks
  • B - customer meets both criteria
  • N - customer does not meet either criteria

Does the code Y, F or B show on the PNGI screen?

  • Yes, manually issue the relevant eligibility letter/s Q230 or Q251 within the existing activity. Go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check the customer's payment history + Read more ...

If a customer has multiple or intertwined Customer Reference Numbers (CRNs), the below checks should be conducted across both records.

A qualifying payment ending on a multiple or intertwined record and starting on the primary record on the same day has been continuously paid for the purposes of the below checks.

When calculating cumulative periods, total payment periods across all records. If a Q230 or Q251 OLA letter is issued based on checks conducted across multiple or intertwined CRNs, note this on a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on all records.

A customer has met the income support requirements for early release of superannuation if they:

  • are getting a qualifying payment at the time of their application, and
  • have been getting a qualifying payment for a continuous period of 26 weeks immediately before applying for early release of superannuation

Review the customer's payment and eligibility history. Some screens that may be helpful are:

  • Payment summary (PS)
  • Allowance/Benefit History (ABH)
  • Pension Status History (PNSH)
  • Overpayment Debt List (OPDL)
    • Check for overpayments or debts that indicate the customer was not entitled to any payment for a period
    • Do not consider debts still under investigation or incomplete reviews
  • Workspace>Payment List for Farm Household Allowance
  • Rate Explanation (REX) screen
  • Documents List (DL)

'Receiving a qualifying payment' includes the following situations:

  • getting at current zero rate (CZR):
    • only if it is because an Adjusted Disability Pension from the Department of Veterans’ Affairs was in the income test (CZR-DFISA before 1 January 2022)
    • CZR for any other reason means the continuous period is not met. This includes an 8 weeks non-payment period
    • See the Resources page for more details about the policy about CZR and the 14 day rule
  • there has been a change to the type of payment, as long as all payments are qualifying payments, and there is no gap in entitlement
  • the suspension and restoration of the same payment has resulted in a gap in entitlement of less than 14 days. See the Resources page for examples of the 14 day rule

With the above exceptions, a customer does not meet the '26 weeks continuous' period rule if they:

  • have a gap in entitlement greater than one day, for example, cancellation and re-claim
  • have been found to be ineligible for payment, for example, undeclared income

A 'continuous' period ends on the last day the customer was entitled to payment. A new continuous period starts on the next day the customer is entitled to get a payment.

Has the customer received a qualifying income support payment for a continuous period of 26 weeks immediately before claiming for early release of superannuation?

  • Yes:
    • issue Q230 letter within the existing activity
    • go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Check preservation age + Read more ...

If the customer is at least 39 weeks older than their preservation age, they can use the 'cumulative period of 39 weeks' rule to meet the income support requirements.

See the Resources page for a table of preservation ages.

The customer does not need to be current on a qualifying payment when making the application.

Is the customer at least 39 weeks older than their preservation age?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No:
    • the customer does not meet the income support eligibility
    • issue Q247 non-eligibility letter within the existing activity
    • go to Step 5

4

Cumulative period + Read more ...

Has the customer received an eligible income support payment for a total of at least 39 weeks at any time since reaching preservation age?

  • Yes:
    • issue Q251 eligibility letter within the existing activity
    • go to Step 5
  • No:
    • issue Q260 non-eligibility letter within the existing activity
    • go to Step 5

5

Add a Note/Doc on the customer’s record + Read more ...

Record the reason in a Note/DOC to clearly explain why the workflows in Process Direct and Customer First were not used and a manual letter was required.

Go to Step 6

6

Finalise the query + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • they should provide the eligibility letter to their superannuation fund to support their application for an early release of super on financial hardship grounds
  • Services Australia has no involvement in determining whether or not the customer is in financial hardship, or in deciding if superannuation monies are able to be released. The customer's super fund will make these decisions
  • the Q230 and Q251 eligibility letters are only valid for 21 days after issue. If the customer's circumstances change during this time, the fund may take these changes into account
  • they may need to pay tax on any early withdrawal of superannuation funds if they are below preservation age. The ATO website has more details
  • money withdrawn from superannuation is not assessed as income. If it is used to buy an asset or generate income, further assessment may be needed

If the customer has not met income support requirements, there are very limited compassionate grounds that may apply. See the Resources page for a link to Accessing your super on compassionate grounds on the ATO website

If the customer does not know which superannuation fund they are with:

  • the ATO may help through SuperSeeker
  • a former employer may know

Procedure ends here

Confirming Q230/Q251 letters with superannuation funds

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Superannuation fund contacts to verify a Q230/Q251 letter + Read more ...

Superannuation funds may contact Services Australia (by phone or email) to confirm information on a letter before making a decision about the early release of a customer’s superannuation. This commonly occurs if the super fund has used Centrelink Confirmation eServices (CCeS) to check the customer's details and there is a discrepancy between the result returned through the service and information on the letter.

Staff must

  • not give information to the super fund that breaches a customer’s privacy
  • only confirm information given by the super fund
  • not give new information

2

Confirmation questions + Read more ...

Ask the superannuation fund for the following information (if not already provided):

  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Full name of customer
  • Address of customer

3

Search for a Q230/Q251 letter on the customer’s record + Read more ...

Find the customer’s record and check if the letter was sent using either the:

  • History Summary (HS) screen, or
  • Archiving and Culling Engine (ACE)

Is the information given by the superannuation fund correct?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Information given by superannuation fund matches the record + Read more ...

Tell the super fund that information is correct as per our records and DOC the following:

  • Super fund [name of fund] contacted by [phone / email] to verify Q230/Q251 letter
  • Enquirer's name [name] and contact number [number]
  • Date of letter advised [DD/MM/YYYY]

Procedure ends here.

5

Information given by superannuation fund does not match the record + Read more ...

Tell the super fund the information given does not match the letter on the agency's records. Do not give the super fund the correct information.

As the superannuation fund has enquired because the information in the Q230/Q251 could not be verified, report the discrepancy as necessary:

  • If a system issue has been identified, raise an incident for further investigation
  • If Service Officers suspect fraudulent activity has occurred see Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption.
