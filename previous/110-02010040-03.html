Registering return of AUS220

It is important to register the return of automatically issued forms to ensure the customer's payments are not suspended inappropriately. If already suspended, the customer's payment must be restored.

To register system issued forms in Customer Record:

  • Go to the Bulk Data Entry (BRET) screen
  • Enter the Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Leave the Action: field blank
  • Press [Enter]

Check for completeness

Check the form has been fully completed, dated and signed by the customer (and partner if required) or nominee. Check that the form is date stamped and that the customer has supplied relevant supporting documentation.

If the form is incomplete:

  • Send a manual advice (Questions for client (XOBS32) letter) to the customer stating the incomplete questions that must be answered
  • Code a manual review with keyword AUS220 to fall due after 28 days
  • Record all details on a DOC and complete the activity

Updating the customer record

If a system issued form has been registered it will create an activity on the Activity List (AL) screen in Customer Record.

If the form was manually issued, check the Future Activity List (FAL) screen. If there is a review for the return of the AUS220:

  • 'S'elect the review activity and ensure the date of receipt is noted at the bottom of the screen when the review is selected. Press [Enter]

If there is no activity on the AL screen, manually start an activity to update the customer record.

For information and assistance with updating the customer's income and asset details, see A-Z on the Information requested in an AUS220 form table.

Finalising all activities and reviews

Once the customer record has been updated, any resulting overpayment must be investigated and a debt raised where appropriate.

Check the customer CRN in Work Optimiser and make sure to complete any further work items relating to the issue and return of the AUS220.

Assessing an AUS220

Coding and assessing an Income and Assets Review

Table 3: This table contains actions for staff when assessing information provided by the customer in an overseas Income and Assets Review (AUS220) form.

Item

Description

Address

Update the customer's address and/or homeowner status if it differs from what is recorded. See the following for help if needed:

  • Changing address or rent details
  • Changing address (CLK)
  • Permanent vacation of principal home
  • Vacation of principal home due to illness
  • Selling a home
  • Purchasing another residence
  • Assessing a life interest in a home

Approved deposit funds, deferred annuities, superannuation funds

'Simple' overseas superannuation (for example, Private employer superannuation) is coded on the Foreign Income/Asset Details (FID) screen with 'SUP' in the Type: field.

Bonds, debentures and unsecured notes

Overseas bonds, debentures and unsecured notes are coded on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen, with 'OSA' in the Type: field to generate the exchange rate option. A description of the investment (for example, bond, debenture, unsecured note) should be recorded in the Account Number: field.

Business

For business assessments, contact the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) if assistance is required.

Cash on hand

Any overseas cash on hand the customers may have apart from monies for regular day-to-day expenses should be recorded on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen with 'OSA' in the Type: field to generate the exchange rate option.

Employment

Income from overseas employment must be coded in the appropriate currency on the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen, with 'EAN' in the Type: field.

Finalising the review
  • When all updates have been made, finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen. This will show if there is an overpayment or if the customer is entitled to arrears.
  • Record a detailed DOC of the updates and the outcome.
  • If the system shows that an overpayment has been incurred, raise the debt created as a result of a reassessment in the host system.

Gifts

Code the amount deprived on the Gifted Assets (GIFTS/GIFT) screen in Australian Dollars (AUD) (converted from overseas currency, if necessary) from the date of gifting.

Household contents and personal effects

If the household contents or personal effects are provided in currency other than Australian dollars, record the asset value on the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen by coding the following:

  • Type: field, code 'OAS' (Other assets) and
  • Description: field, enter details of the asset

Income streams

Overseas income stream products are not subject to the income stream rules. The gross amount of income is recorded on the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen with 'OIN' in the Type: field.

The asset value of overseas income stream products are assessed as for Australian assets that do not comply with the income stream rules.

Code Australian income stream products with a deductible amount (for example, immediate annuities) on the Pensions/Annuities Identification Detail (SUPI) and Pensions/Annuities Summary (SUPV) screens in AUD with a 6 month manual review of the exchange rate.

Automatic reviews are in place for Australian income stream products. If the customer has given a new schedule, or it is a new product:

  • Update the record with the details provided.
  • Request the schedule for new products. For assistance, see:
    • Adding or updating an account-based income stream
    • Adding or updating a defined benefit income stream

Life insurance policies

If the customer has an overseas life insurance policy with a surrender value, code the surrender value as an asset on the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen, with 'OAS' in the Type: field and product details in the Description: field

If the customer is unable to provide the surrender value, calculate the asset amount to be maintained using the following formula:

Number of years policy held x annual premiums paid = asset value

If the policy has been surrendered, the asset amount should be zeroed on the FID screen and the amount of profit calculated by subtracting total premiums paid from the lump sum received by the customer.

Managed investments

Overseas managed funds are registered in the Managed Investment (MIN) system when a customer advises Centrelink of the overseas managed investment details.

The unit prices are reviewed twice yearly in line with the CPI updates. The values are all converted from the foreign currency to Australian Dollars (AUD).

For a new investment, it is important to:

  • obtain the managed investment documentation, which includes:
    • the full name of the fund
    • the country and if possible
    • the product code, for many countries this will be the International Securities Identifying Number (ISIN)
  • email information about the product to the Financial Industry and Network Support (FINS) helpdesk

If a customer has a retail product that has not yet been registered on the MIN product database:

  • enter the managed investment on the customer's record using the Unregistered Managed Investments (MIUS) screen to ensure that the customer receives the correct rate of payment
  • record a manual review on the system for one month so that when the product has been registered on the MIN product database the investment can be removed from the MIUS screen and re-recorded on the Managed Investment Product List (MIP) screen

For more information, see Managed investments - adding a new investment.

Motor vehicles, boats or caravans

These details are recorded on the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen. Code:

  • Type: field, 'OAS' and
  • Description: field, enter details of the vehicle

Update the record if the details on the AUS220 are different from what is coded on the customer's record.

If a customer living long-term overseas has in-Australia details still coded on the Other Assets Summary (OASS) screen, check that these details are still current and adjust if necessary.

Other assets

These details are recorded on the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen. Code:

  • Reason code: field with 'OAS' and
  • Description: field with details of the asset

Update the record if the details on the AUS220 are different from what is coded on the customer's record.

Other income (apart from employment and real estate)

Check the Foreign Income/Assets/Pension Summary (FIPS) screen.

If the income is already coded, but details have changed, select the appropriate line to go to the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen and make changes as required.

Overseas pensions

The Foreign Pension script will assist with coding.

If the Foreign Pension script is available, code all other changes advised on the AUS220, then run the script.

If the script is not available, code the overseas pension details manually. See Foreign Pension coding for further information.

Payments from Australia in respect of veterans

The income is shown on the customer's Veterans' Affairs Pension Summary (DVAS) screen.

See Income from Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) for assistance, if required.

Private company

If the customer has already disclosed that they are involved in a company, no further action is required.

If the customer has not previously disclosed to Centrelink they are involved in a Private Company, issue a Private Company (Mod PC) form, allowing 28 days for the overseas customer to return the form. The Resources page contains a link to the form.

On return of the Mod PC the case should be referred to the Complex Assessments Officer (CAO) for the initial assessment.

For further information, see Assessing and coding the Private Company details from the MOD PC.

Private trust

If the customer has already disclosed that they are involved in a private trust, no further action is required.

If the customer has not previously disclosed to Centrelink they are involved in a Private Trust, issue a Private Trust (Mod PT), allowing 28 days for the overseas customer to return the form. The Resources page contains a link to the form.

On return of the Mod PT the case should be referred to the Complex Assessments Officer (CAO) for the initial assessment.

For further information, see:

  • Trusts and companies assessment and
  • Trusts

Real estate

If the customer owns overseas real estate other than their principal home, or a property larger than 2 hectares, the details should be recorded on the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen, with 'REA' in the Type: field. The assessable asset value and income from the real estate should be coded.

Asset value

A valuation of overseas property may be required, see Valuation of real estate and other assets.

Income value

The customer is required to provide their latest tax return for Centrelink to determine the net income the customer receives from their real estate.

A Real Estate Details (Mod R) form may need to be issued to the customer if details of the real estate have not been previously declared. The Resources page contains a link to the Mod R.

If a tax return is not immediately available the 'one third rule' is used as an interim measure until either the income tax return for the person or business that owns the property (including the rental property schedule showing rental income and deductions) or financial statements such as a Profit and Loss Statement are available.

The resulting amount is the net rental income to be coded as income on the FID screen.

If the overseas customer owns real estate in Australia, details should be recorded on the Real Estate/Business Summary (REBS) screen.

For further information on coding real estate in Australia, see:

  • Assessment and sale of real estate and timeshare asset
  • Assessing income from real estate and timeshare and
  • Assessing and coding the Real estate details Mod R

Relationship status

Check that the relationship status identified on the form matches the details on the customer's record.

Savings/investments (apart from managed investments or shares)
  • Compare the details on the AUS220 with the customer's Securities and Investment Summary (SIS) screen. Update any changes.
  • Foreign savings details should be coded in the overseas currency on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen with 'OSA' in the Type: field.

If the customer's record shows that they have savings accounts/investments in Australia, but information given in the AUS220 indicates that they no longer have an account in Australia, it may be necessary to contact the customer to clarify details.

Shares

If the customer advises of shares that have not previously been recorded:

  • obtain the share documentation, which should include:
    • the full name of the company/share
    • the country in which it is listed and if possible
    • the stock exchange code
  • send an email request to the Financial Industry and Network Support (FINS) helpdesk for the share to be added to the Security and Investment (SIN) system

If the share is unlisted (not listed on a stock exchange):

  • refer it to the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO)
  • code the shares on the Unlisted Public Securities (SIUS) screen until the shares are listed to ensure that the customer receives the correct rate of payment

The share prices are automatically reviewed twice yearly in line with the Consumer Price Index (CPI) updates. The values are all converted from the foreign currency to Australian Dollars (AUD).

Overseas shares recorded on the SIN system can be located on the Select Product (SIP) screen by their name, not by their foreign stock exchange code. It is best to use the first 3 letters of the company name.

When the product has been selected from the SIP screen, share details are recorded on the Listed Securities (SILS) screen in AUD.

If overseas shares have previously been recorded on the SIUS screen, the shares should be set up on the SIP screen and the details transferred to the SILS screen. The Service Officer should ensure that the dates of effect are the same.
