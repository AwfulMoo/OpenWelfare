Jointly owned products + Read more ...

  • Yes, check the details coded on SUPI. Make sure the:
    • purchase price, income amounts and residual capital value reflect the customer’s share of ownership
    • income stream is coded with the same details on the other owner’s SUPI screen. Note: if income in the data contains an odd number of cents, round down for both customer and partner. Go to Step 4

Check if the handoff has been created due to a commutation from the income stream product. A keyword of ISRLLTCOM will be on the record and the Notes will state that a commutation needs more investigation.

Is the activity related to a commutation handoff?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Discrepancy other than commutation + Read more ...

Check if any of the following fields have discrepancies:

  • Product type
  • Purchase date
  • Original purchase price (whole dollar difference only)
  • Residual capital value

Note: a difference in original purchase price and residual capital value may be caused by joint ownership of the product not previously being taken into consideration when initial coding was completed. If this is the case, the product must be cancelled and re-coded, see Step 1 in Table 2 for instructions.

If the discrepancy is due to:

  • indexation (ISRLLTIND, ISRLLTDAT) details only, see Step 4 in Table 2
  • product was incorrectly cancelled (ISRLLTCAC) , go to Step 6
  • relevant number (ISRLLTTRC) go to Step 8
  • Section 9A/B/BA compliant (ISRLLT9AB) go to Step 13
  • Income frequency (ISRLLTFRE) go to Step 15
  • Income amount (ISRLLTINC) go to Step 16
  • Product could not be matched to an existing income stream (ISRLLTNMF, ISRLLTNPA), go to Step 17
  • product type only including historical product code, go to Step 18
  • product being undeclared (ISRLLTNEW) or reversionary (ISRREV), and
    • product is Lifetime Income Stream, see Adding or updating a lifetime income stream
    • product is Life Expectancy Income Stream, see Adding or updating a life expectancy income stream
    • product is Term income, see Adding or updating a term income stream
  • ABN and/or Provider name with no other discrepancies, go to Step 20
  • other fields listed above (ISRLLTMUL, ISRLLTCBI, ISRLLTUPD), see Step 1 in Table 2

If no data discrepancies are found, go to Step 20.

6

Provider's income stream data potentially cancelled in error + Read more ...

Compare the details of the income stream in the provider data to the cancelled product.

Check if any of the below details match:

  • purchase date
  • relevant number
  • original purchase price
  • provider name

Check the customer’s record for documentation that may assist in reactivating the product. Documentation may include Income Stream Schedules or Statements.

Is there enough information on the record to confirm the product is still current?

  • Yes, reactivate the product, go to Step 7
  • No, request more information from the customer. See Step 1 in Table 5

7

Reactivate the product + Read more ...

  • Key 'SUPS' in Super Key to go to the Income Stream (SUPS, SUPI, SUPV) table
  • Press the Edit icon for the product that was cancelled in error
  • Change the Product Cancellation Reason so the field is blank and press Save
  • Enter data for any missing DOVs from available documentation on SUPV since the product was cancelled
  • Apply the provider data. See Step 1 in Table 4

8

Relevant Number requires investigation + Read more ...

This handoff was created because the Relevant Number on the customer’s record does not match the Relevant Number in the provider data.

  • For Term and Life Expectancy Income Streams this must be a whole number
  • It must always be the number that applied at the commencement date and must not be changed following reversion to a reversionary partner or beneficiary

Note: for Term Income streams the Relevant Number is the Term of the product and chosen at commencement. If the product is a Term Income Stream go to Step 12

  • Check the relevant number and decide if the correct life expectancy has been used.
  • Refer to life expectancy tables or use the relevant number calculator

Does the relevant number in the provider data and/or income stream schedule match the life expectancy table for either the customer or partner?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Check if relevant number is higher + Read more ...

Is the relevant number in the provider data higher than the life expectancy table for the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, use the relevant number in the life expectancy table in the review. Alert AISR Level 1 about the mismatch as contact with the provider may be required. If the relevant number in the life expectancy table is:
  • already coded on the customer’s record, go to Step 21
  • not coded on the customer’s record, see Step 1 in Table 2

10

Check for schedule on customer’s record + Read more ...

Check for an income stream on the record. Was a schedule found?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, request a schedule. See Step 1 in Table 5

11

Determine action required + Read more ...

Does the relevant number on the schedule match the provider data?

  • Yes, use this relevant number when updating the product. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, escalate to AISR level 1 or FINS Level 2 helpdesk. Contact with the provider will be needed

12

Investigate term number difference + Read more ...

Check the record for an income stream schedule for the product. If the term number:

  • matches the schedule and the number is:
    • a whole number, see Step 1 in Table 2
    • not a whole number, escalate to AISR Level 1 or FINS Level 2 Helpdesk
  • does not match the schedule, escalate to AISR level 1 or FINS Level 2 helpdesk. Contact with the provider will be needed
  • if no schedule is on the record, see Step 1 in Table 5

13

This handoff was created as the Compliance Indicator on the provider data does not match the customer’s record.

Investigation is required to determine the correct status of the product compliance to Section 9A/B/BA. See the References page for more information.

Check the customer’s record for a schedule. If it cannot be located, request one from the customer. See Step 1 in Table 5

Does the compliance indicator on the schedule match the provider data?

  • Yes, go to Step 14
  • No, escalate to AISR level 1 or FINS Level 2 helpdesk. Contact with the provider will be needed

Determine the reason for compliance indicator change + Read more ...

Investigation will be needed to determine if the product was coded incorrectly or if the compliance indicator has changed due to the product being commuted.

Has a previously unrecorded commutation occurred on the product?

  • Yes, see Step 1 of Table 3
  • No, the product compliance code was coded incorrectly. Proceed to Step 1 in Table 2

15

Income frequency requires investigation + Read more ...

This handoff has been created because the payment frequency on the record does not match the provider data. For example the provider data may state the Income Frequency is ‘2 monthly’ and the provider data is ‘2 weekly’.

Investigate the record and confirm the Income Frequency requires correction.

Does the Income frequency differ and no other discrepancies exist?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, go to Step 21

16

Income amount requires investigation + Read more ...

This handoff has been created because the payment amount on the record does not match the provider data.

Does the Income Amount require correction and no other discrepancies exist?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, go to Step 21

17

No match found + Read more ...

This handoff was created because no match could be made with an existing product.

Investigate the record for a product that already exists with outdated provider information as a result of a Successor Funds Transfer (SFT). The SFT could cause a change in ABN and potentially Product Reference Number (PRN).

Is there a product on the record that matches the provider data?

  • Yes, correct SUPI to make sure details match provider data and see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, request more information from the customer. See Step 1 in Table 5

18

Investigate product type discrepancy + Read more ...

The product types for a:

  • lifetime income stream can be LIS, ANL, ARL, and ARP
  • life expectancy income stream which complies with Section 9B can be LIE, ANN, ARS, and ART
  • term income stream which does not comply with Section 9A/B, can be TIS, ANN, ARS and ART

The old product type codes were not converted to the new codes. The assessment is not affected but any product with an obsolete product type must be updated to current product codes to prevent manual handoffs as part of the Automation of Income Stream Reviews process.

Is the product type in the provider data a Term Income Stream?

  • Yes, and the product is recorded as:
    • a lifetime or life expectancy income stream on the customer's record. Note: make sure historical product type codes are considered. For example, ANN, ARS and ART are obsolete codes for non-complying term products and can be updated. Go to Step 7
    • a different product type stream, a schedule is needed. Check the record for an existing income stream record
      If a schedule on the record is found and confirms the provider’s product type is correct, see Step 1 in Table 2
      Otherwise, see Step 6 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

19

Term income stream incorrectly coded as lifetime or life expectancy income stream + Read more ...

The income stream product needs further investigation to check the provider data is correct. Term income streams are not Section 9A/B/BA compliant. Therefore, they cannot be asset test exempt.

Investigate the record to confirm if the provider data may have been reported incorrectly. This includes checking for existing income stream schedules.

If there is an issue, check the Process Direct - Current Issues > AISR – AISR Provider data error page. Search for the provider name as instructions may have been published for any known discrepancies. If there is not enough information to say if the provider data is correct, request an income stream schedule, see Step 1 in Table 4.

Was there an issue with the provider data?

  • Yes, and the Process Direct - Current Issues > AISR - AISR Provider data error page:
    • has advice, action the update as per the instructions on this page
    • does not contain advice and a schedule has been obtained, which confirms the discrepancy in provider data, refer to Level 2 Policy Help Desk (FINS) for contact with provider
  • No, and provider data is correct, the income stream needs to be recoded. See Step 1 in Table 2

20

Change data as needed + Read more ...

Key 'SUPS' in Super Key to go to the Income Stream (SUPS, SUPI, SUPV) table.

Change the incorrect data on SUPI and SUPV as needed. Make sure the Provider Name, Reference Number and ABN match Provider Data.

If income details need updating and the product is jointly owned, make sure the gross income is halved.

If needed, due to the previous indexation coding not being applied to the customer’s record and gross income being out of date, key the provider data as a new entry on the Income Stream (SUPS, SUPI, SUPV) table. Make sure the date of event (DOV) is the date of receipt (DOR) of the activity.

If an error generates stating:

  • ‘Next indexation date must be greater than today’s date’, change the Year field so that the Next Indexation Date is within 1 year in the future
  • ‘Next indexation date cannot be more than a year in the future’, create a new SUPV entry with today’s date and replicate the latest data. Code the indexation information in this entry

Note: where there is a later DOV than the provider data DOR, consider whether this later DOV needs to be removed or amended. If in doubt, consult with a Technical Peer Support (TPS) officer.

Check if the next indexation date of the provider data has occurred since the DOR of the activity. If so, the provider data is outdated.

Does the provider data contain outdated data?

  • Yes, go to Step 21
  • No, finalise the activity. Procedure ends here

21

Determine indexation method + Read more ...

Check the indexation method in the provider data.

If the indexation method is:

  • Blank, no more action is needed. Make sure any subsequent SUPV entries include the same information as the provider data and finalise the activity. Procedure ends here
  • CPI, go to Step 22
  • FIXed, go to Step 23

23

Fixed income + Read more ...

Income will need manual calculation based on the indexation %.

Note: if the indexation rate is greater than 5%, the product cannot be section 9A/B/BA compliant.

Example:

Provider data entry on SUPV for 01/05/2020:

  • gross income = $100
  • the anniversary date of the product = 01/06/2020
  • indexation % = 2

The calculation needed would be as follows:

  • $100 x 1.02 = $102
  • DOV of update is 01/06/2020

For help with fields that need updating for a product, see:

  • Adding or updating a term income stream
  • Adding or updating a lifetime income stream
  • Adding or updating a life expectancy income stream

See Step 2 in Table 4.

1

Cancel and recode income stream + Read more ...

Check if existing entry has expired incorrectly. This will determine the event date for the activity. An income stream product may have expired incorrectly, when:

  • a Lifetime Income Stream (LIS) has been coded as a Term Income Stream (TIS) or Life Expectancy Income Stream (LIE)
  • an incorrect term or relevant number was coded for a TIS or LIE product

Before coding, make sure the correct date of receipt (DOR) is used for the activity. If a Request for Information (RFI) was issued about this Coding Accuracy check, and it was provided:

  • within 13 weeks of the original Change of Circumstances (CoC), the DOR is the date on which the agency first became aware of the CoC or notifiable event
  • outside 13 weeks, the DOR is the date the schedule was provided

See Date of receipt for more information.

Cancel the product

  • Open the existing SUPI entry
  • Before cancelling the product, note the product name on SUPI and the most recent SUPV data on the customer’s record
  • Cancel the current product by selecting OTH - Other in the Product Cancellation Reason field, including to override an incorrect EXP - expiry reason
    • if processing in Customer First, key Source and DOR as needed
    • in the Action field, key ‘C’
  • Event Date is:
    • Date of Receipt of the CoC, or schedule if supplied outside of 13 weeks, or
    • expiry date if the product was expired incorrectly, for example, the most recent anniversary of the purchase date
  • Check if there are any SUPV lines after the Event Date. If so, make sure all lines after this Event Date are deleted or zeroed off
  • Select the Add new line icon on the right of the Income Stream (SUPS, SUPI, SUPV) table
  • Code the details under the income stream details column, ensuring all fields match the provider data:
    • check if the original purchase price can be coded. If not, cancel the update, conduct a SAP refresh and try again
    • if the income stream is jointly owned, make sure the original purchase price is halved. Make sure the product has also been coded on the partner’s record
    • use the product name of the previous (now cancelled) product entry
  • Make sure the correct Section 9A/B/BA Compliant Indicator is coded

Recode SUPV + Read more ...

As the customer’s record has more recent data, recode this information.

Update the Superannuation payment details:

  • Event Date is:
    • the Date of Receipt of the activity or CoC, or
    • expiry date if the product was expired incorrectly
  • Code the original purchase price as per the provider data. If the income stream is jointly owned, make sure:
    • the original purchase price is halved
    • if the product has a Residual Capital Value, this is halved
    • the product has also been coded on the partner’s record
  • Code the gross income amount and income frequency as per the most recent entry on SUPV of the now cancelled product. If the income stream is jointly owned, make sure the gross income amount is halved
    Note: if the data contains an odd number of cents, one partner’s income will be 1 cent higher
  • Save

Note: if the income increased after the Date of Receipt of the provider data, change the cancellation event date and SUPV date on the new product to match the most recent DOV on SUPV of the cancelled product.

Check the indexation information in the provider data and on the customer’s record.

If the indexation:

  • details have not yet been coded, check the provider data to determine if indexation information needs to be applied
  • method is blank, information does not need to be applied
  • information needs updating, select the SUPV entry created on the Income Stream Details table and code the following fields:
    • Indexation Method
    • Next Indexation Date - next future review date. The review occurs each year (usually on the anniversary of the purchase date)
    • Indexation Percent for FIXed method only

If an error generates stating:

  • ‘Next indexation date must be greater than today’s date’, change the Year field so that the Next Indexation Date is within 1 year in the future
  • ‘Next indexation date cannot be more than a year in the future’, create a new SUPV entry with today’s date and replicate the latest data. Code the indexation information in this entry

Action

1

Apply the provider data + Read more ...

Code the provider data. Update the Superannuation payment details:

  • Event Date is:
    • the Date of Receipt of the activity, or
    • expiry date if the product was expired incorrectly
  • If not already coded, code the original purchase price. If the income stream is jointly owned, make sure:
    • the original purchase price is halved
    • if the product has a Residual Capital Value, this is halved
    • the product has also been coded on the partner’s record
  • The gross income amount and income frequency are as per the provider data. If the income stream is jointly owned, make sure the gross income amount is halved. Note: if the income amount is for the previous year, the new income for the current year is needed. If more details are needed, see Step 1 in Table 5
  • Save

Check the indexation information in the provider data and on the customer’s record.

If the indexation:

  • details have not yet been coded, check the provider data to determine if indexation information needs to be applied
  • method is blank, information does not need to be applied
  • information needs updating, select the entry created on the Income Stream Details table and code the following fields:
    • Indexation Method
    • Next Indexation Date - next future review date. The review occurs each year (usually on the anniversary of the purchase date)
    • Indexation Percent for FIXed method only

If an error generates stating:

  • ‘Next indexation date must be greater than today’s date’, change the Year field so that the Next Indexation Date is within 1 year in the future
  • ‘Next indexation date cannot be more than a year in the future’, create a new SUPV entry with today’s date and replicate the latest data. Code the indexation information in this entry

Check the Product Reference Number and ABN. If there is a discrepancy, change the incorrect data on SUPI as needed.

2

Finalise activity + Read more ...

Finalise and DOC the activity.

If the activity was completed in Customer First:

  • access the CoC and navigate to SUPS
  • select ‘Edit’ icon on the confirmed SUPV line confirmed from the previously processed CoC, scroll to the bottom, click Save
  • this sets the data to provisional with no alterations

This allows the CoC to be taken to assess and finalised.

Procedure ends here.

Request an income stream schedule + Read more ...

Request an income stream schedule. A historical information request cannot ask for information before 1 July within 7 years (agency’s limitation) from the date of request. See general considerations on the Resources page for more on this limitation.

In some cases, the customer may not be able to supply all the requested information. Reasons include:

  • the request exceeds the timeframe that documents need to be kept under taxation legislation (5 years)
  • the income stream is now managed by a successor fund

If requesting historical income stream information, make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer to advise why the historical information is needed.

See the AISR provider data issues page for advice on the need to request a schedule. If an information request has been sent because of a data integrity issue not yet addressed, consult AISR Level 1 Support Helpdesk.
