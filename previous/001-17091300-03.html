Accessing the Compliance Investigation workflow + Read more ...

Selecting the failure from the Participation Compliance workflow page will open the Compliance Investigation workflow and display the Key Information page.

Links to other pages in the workflow will display in the side menu. These include the Other Related Information page, which will enable CDP job seeker information to be reviewed while working through the investigation and includes (if appropriate) links to:

  • Provider Report
  • Job Plan
  • Provider Recorded Information
  • Online Diary
  • Job Seeker Calendar
  • Recent non-compliance
  • Services Australia Rates Estimator
  • Address History
  • View ESAt
  • Recent activities and exemptions
  • Circumstances impacting compliance

3

Customer contact + Read more ...

A contact trigger is recorded when the No Show, No Pay failure is generated, preventing the CDP job seeker from reporting until contact is made with the PST regarding the failure.

A No Show, No Pay failure cannot be investigated without talking to the CDP job seeker, unless it has been established the failure is invalid.

Considerations when investigating a No Show, No Pay failure

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Key Information + Read more ...

Relevant CDP job seeker information and details of the failure will be presented on the Other related Information screen.

A 'View Circumstances' link is provided to access the Circumstances Impacting Compliance page. This must be viewed to determine if there is a current family relationships/domestic violence circumstance recorded. If there is, a PST-skilled social worker consult must be done as part of the investigation. See Accessing a PST-skilled social worker

Natural justice requires the CDP job seeker to be given an opportunity to:

  • explain the reasons that prevented them from attending the job interview and/or
  • discuss any factors they believe impacted their behaviour, or ability to control their behaviour

All decisions must be based on fact, which is substantiated with suitable evidence as required. The Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer is responsible for ensuring there is enough evidence to establish the facts of the case.

This may include further contact with an employer, the CDP job seeker's provider, a specialist or third party, or obtaining and considering other evidence the CDP job seeker may provide. For further information, see Gathering information and weighing evidence to establish facts for a compliance investigation.

A summary of relevant information provided by the CDP job seeker that relates to the non-compliance event must be recorded in the Compliance Investigation workflow.

The PST-skilled Service Officer (WNPPD) will be prompted to:

  • record if an interpreter was used or not
  • check and update CDP job seeker phone and email details

2

Commence the investigation + Read more ...

Navigate to the Investigation page.

The Investigation page will be pre-populated with details of the failure.

The PST-skilled Service Officer with Participation Failure Decision (PFDEC) or 8 Week Non-Payment Period Decision (WNPPD) security resources, will be prompted to:

  • read the privacy statement to the CDP job seeker
  • outline the failure under investigation
  • access and review the Provider Report

Check if the provider has discussed the failure with the job seeker. If appropriate, record the excuse the CDP job seeker gave the provider in the free text field.

Discuss with the CDP job seeker their explanation and record any additional information provided in the appropriate free text field.

Consider the reasons given.

Note: a PST skilled Service Officer must consult with a PST-skilled Social Worker where the job seeker presents with family and domestic violence or has a current Family relationships/Domestic violence circumstance recorded. See Accessing a PST-skilled social worker.

Select the most appropriate response to the question:

  • 'Does the job seeker's statement suggest ongoing circumstances directly contributed to the failure?'

If the answer is 'Yes' or 'Unsure', a link to the Circumstances Impacting Compliance page will allow the page to be viewed and circumstances updated if required. See Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirement.

Go to Step 3.

3

Select an appropriate Job Seeker Reason + Read more ...

Record the CDP job seeker's main excuse for the failure (or the reason the Service Officer has determined is the main reason) on the Investigation page.

The categories displayed will only include options appropriate to the failure type.

  • Select a category from the drop down list
  • Select a reason from the drop down list that best describes the reasons the CDP job seeker has given

Additional information may display depending on the responses. Additional information may include:

  • Details of reported income
  • Medical certificates and exemptions
  • Rate Estimator

Is the CDP job seeker a principal carer?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Principal Carers + Read more ...

For CDP job seekers who are principal carers, use the Rate Estimator to determine financial suitability (see Financial suitability test for suitable work for principal carers) and record the following:

  • Net fortnightly income excluding employment costs and income for the job
  • Net fortnightly income including employment costs and income for the job
  • Is the job seeker (more than $50.00 per fortnight) financially better off if they accept the job?

Go to Step 5.

5

Consider the reasonable excuse or other guidelines + Read more ...

See:

  • Failing to attend or satisfactorily participate in a job interview
  • Assessing reasonable excuse for non-compliance with mutual obligation requirements

Is this an acceptable reason that warrants further investigation?

Note: when certain excuses are selected, the answer to this question will be set to 'No'.

  • Yes or Unsure, further investigation is required. Go to Step 6
  • No, further investigation is not required. See Step 1 in Table 3

6

Direct and significant impact on the failure + Read more ...

Does the excuse indicate it had a direct and significant impact on the failure?

  • Yes or Unsure, further investigation is required. Go to Step 7
  • No, further investigation is not required. See Step 1 in Table 3

7

Avoidable failure + Read more ...

Does the excuse indicate it was within the job seeker's control and they could have avoided the failure?

  • Yes, further investigation is not required. See Step 1 in Table 3
  • No or Unsure, further investigation is required. Go to Step 8

8

Sufficient evidence + Read more ...

Does sufficient evidence already exist to confirm the excuse for the failure?

  • Yes, further evidence is not required. See Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Further evidence is required + Read more ...

When further evidence is required to reach a decision, the Service Officer needs to ensure evidence collected is specific to the incident, and will influence the outcome of the decision under the applicable legislation and policy.

When the Service Officer selects either:

  • No - Phone call required to confirm the excuse for failure, or
  • No - Have requested job seeker provide evidence

the following may display:

  • Evidence due date
  • Who do you need to contact about the job seeker's excuse

Summary of evidence requested/provided

The summary of evidence free text field should be used, when presented, to document the evidence provided or requested.

When evidence has been requested, a future date is set for its return, using the Save and Exit workflow. Select 'Pending evidence from customer' option, and enter due date for the requested evidence. This will remove the contact trigger and ensure it is re-instated on the Evidence due date + 1.

Is evidence required and a future date is set for the return?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3

5

Reject the failure + Read more ...

Using the responses provided in the Compliance Investigation workflow, the decision to reject a failure will be automatically selected on the Decision and Penalty page.

PST-skilled Service Officers must be satisfied the decision is correct under the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999. If not satisfied the decision is correct, review the answers recorded within the Compliance Investigation workflow for correctness.

PST-skilled Service Officers must ensure the appropriate rejection reason is recorded.

When rejecting a PR as it does not contain the required information, or clarification is needed from the provider (and contact was unable to occur), use one of the following rejection codes:

  • IVA - Returned for update - insufficient evidence/documentation, or
  • IVH - Returned for update - incorrect incident date

For all other rejections, select the appropriate rejection reason from the drop down list.

Clearly record an explanation for rejecting the compliance action in the free text area provided.

Record further information for the provider to explain the reason for rejection, if appropriate.

Note: any information recorded can be obtained by the CDP job seeker under the Freedom of Information Act 1982. The information which can be lawfully provided by Services Australia to Employment Services Providers is covered by Section 202 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999.

Go to Step 8.

6

Failure to be applied + Read more ...

Using the responses provided in the workflow, the decision to apply a failure will be automatically selected on the Decision and Penalty page.

PST-skilled Service Officers must be satisfied the decision is correct under the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999. If not satisfied the decision is correct, review the answers recorded within the Compliance Investigation workflow for correctness.

Inform the CDP job seeker of the failure decision and their review and appeal rights.

Note: any information recorded can be obtained by the CDP job seeker under the Freedom of Information Act 1982. The information which can be lawfully provided by Services Australia is covered by Section 202 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999.

The PST skilled Service Officers record a response to:

  • Have you verbally notified the job seeker of the failure decision and appeal rights?
  • Have they indicated they intend to immediately appeal the decision?

Does the CDP job seeker want apply for a formal review of the decision?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

CDP job seeker wants to apply for a formal review of the decision + Read more ...

The following message will display:

'The job seeker has requested a review of the decision to apply the failure. Ensure appropriate action is taken to record the request for the review.'

See Non-compliance with compulsory requirements - review and appeals.

8

Existing circumstances impacting compliance require review to confirm they are up to date + Read more ...

The PST-skilled Service Officer will be required to complete any outstanding reviews or assessments of existing circumstances impacting compliance. See Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirement.

9

Participation Compliance workflow submitted to QOL + Read more ...

Due to system limitations, Quality On Line (QOL) may not display all workflow pages if the failure investigation is completed over more than 1 contact.

Any pages not displaying in QOL can be viewed through the Participation Compliance workflow by selecting the Date of Event (DOV) of the submitted failure.

10

Restoration + Read more ...

Check if CDP job seeker's payment needs to be restored.

A payment suspension (participation) will be imposed at the time an appointment related connection failure is generated unless there is another suspension in place at that time.

The Participation Compliance Workflow will display a message and a Manual Follow Up (MFU) will be generated to prompt restoration of the CDP job seekers payment when a payment suspension (participation) has been imposed and the CDP job seeker:

  • has agreed to re-engage, or
  • is not required to re-engage (including when the failure is rejected as invalid and no contact has been made with the CDP job seeker)

If the CDP job seeker has refused to re-engage then the suspension will continue, however, the CDP job seeker will still be issued with a re-engagement requirement.

If a CDP job seeker terminates a call, or the call is disconnected, before Services Australia can discuss re-engagement, their payment should remain suspended.

See Restoring payment where a payment suspension (participation) has been imposed for Community Development Program (CDP) job seekers.

11

Comprehensive Compliance Assessment (CCA) will be generated due to the application of the failure + Read more ...

When the following message presents: 'The application of this failure will result in the generation of an automatic CCA. Based on the CCA guidelines in Operational Blueprint, does the CCA need to be generated immediately?', a response must be selected from either:

  • 'Yes - generate CCA immediately'
  • 'No - system should generate CCA overnight'

If a CCA is generated immediately, it will be displayed on the Participation Compliance Workflows page.

For more information, see Generating and preparing for a Comprehensive Compliance Assessment (CCA).
