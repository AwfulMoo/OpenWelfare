This page contains information on how to convert old national currencies to or from Euro, examples of Euro conversions and a table of the irrevocable exchange rate for each country and date the currency changed. It also contains a link to International and DVA Programmes and the Services Australia website.

On this Page:

How to convert old national currencies to or from Euro

Conversion:

  • to EUR is done by dividing the amount in the old currency by the irrevocable exchange rate
  • from EUR is done by multiplying the Euro amount by the irrevocable exchange rate

Note: conversions must also take into account any changes in the rate of foreign pensions, for example, regular increases for cost of living.

Examples of Euro conversions

Example

Description

1

Converting Slovakian Koruna (SKK) to EUR

On review, a customer declares that they have received a Slovakian Old Age pension (AGE) since 1 July 2008. The notice of grant provided only indicates the original rate of pension of 4500.00 Slovakian Koruna (SKK) per month. The:

  • Relevant date for Slovak Republic is 1 January 2009
  • Irrevocable exchange rate for Slovak Republic is 30.1260
  • Conversion from SKK to EUR is 4500.00 SKK/30.1260 = 149.37 EUR
  • Slovakian AGE is coded as:
    • 4500.00 SKK from 1 July 2008, and
    • 149.37 EUR from 1 January 2009

2

Converting EUR to Latvian Lats (LVL)

The Latvian authorities advise Services Australia of a customer's previously undeclared Latvian Old Age pension (AGE) that was granted from 1 August 2013 but only provide the current rate of 180.00 EUR per month effective from 1 January 2014 and not the rate in Latvian Lats (LVL) from 2013. The:

  • Relevant date for Latvia is 1 January 2014
  • Irrevocable exchange rate for Latvia is 0.7028
  • Conversion from EUR to LVL is 180.00 EUR x 0.7028 = 126.50 LVL
  • Latvian AGE is coded as:
    • 126.50 LVL from 1 August 2013, and
    • 180.00 EUR from 1 January 2014
