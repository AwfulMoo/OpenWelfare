This document outlines information on passwords including how to place a password on a customer record, what to do when a password is recorded and how to delete a password.

Creating passwords

A customer may request a password on their record. Passwords are recorded via the:

  • Display on Access (DOA) DOCument, or
  • DOC screen alert message facility

Advise the customer to provide a password that does not contain common information such as:

  • family names, and
  • birthdates

Action required when a password is recorded

If there is a password on a customer's record the Service Officer must ask the customer to provide it each time they contact Services Australia. The password is recorded as an alert on the customer record as a Display on Access (DOA) DOC.

If there is one DOA DOC present:

  • it will auto display when the Service Office enters the record

If there are multiple DOA DOCs present:

  • access the 'Display on access documents' heading
  • the Document List (DL) screen will display a list of DOA DOCS
  • check the DL screen for a password when accessing a customer's record

A password does not replace authenticating a customer. The password is in addition to authenticating a customer. The password will provide extra assurance for customers that Services Australia is not giving out personal information.

Note: failing to ask the customer for their password may lead the customer to believe Services Australia is not taking their privacy seriously and that their details may be accessed by an unauthorised third party. A Service Officer can access the record in the course of their duties.

A customer can amend or delete a password after being authenticated. The customer's request and the changes made should be noted in a DOC on their record.

If a customer has been authenticated via Voice Biometrics technology or the Interactive Voice Response (IVR) process, the Smart Centre Service Officer must ask the customer to provide their password.

Nominee passwords on a customer's record

A nominee will be asked to provide a mandatory password which they must supply each time they contact the agency on behalf of the customer. The nominee's password is situated on the Nominee Relationship Summary (NORS) screen and therefore does not need to be recorded on a Display on Access DOC.

If the nominee forgets their password the Service Officer should attempt to identify the nominee using alternative methods before disclosing information to the nominee.

If the nominee cannot be identified using an alternative method they must attend a Service Centre with adequate proof of identity, after which the nominee's password can be disclosed or changed.
