Common enquiries

Common enquiries about Medicare Aftercare services

This table describes information relating to Medicare Aftercare services common enquiries.

Item

Description

1

What changes to Aftercare were introduced from 1 November 2017?

Answer: From 1 November 2017, Medicare benefits are payable for Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) general practitioner (GP) attendance items performed during an aftercare period, where the procedure was performed by another medical practitioner.

2

Can a GP receive a Medicare benefit for the aftercare provided for a procedure performed by another medical practitioner?

Answer: Yes. GPs providing post-operative treatment to a patient during an aftercare period are eligible for Medicare benefits. This rule applies only in the circumstance whereby the GP did not perform the initial procedure requiring post-operative treatment.

Normal aftercare rules still apply when the GP who rendered the initial procedure provides the post-operative treatment.

3

Can a GP receive a Medicare benefit for the aftercare provided for a procedure performed by the same GP?

Answer: No. Normal aftercare rules still apply when the GP who rendered the initial procedure provides the post-operative treatment under an attendance item.

4

Does aftercare apply to all surgical procedures? + Read more

Answer: No. The medical practitioner determines each individual aftercare period depending on the needs of the patient. The amount and duration of aftercare following a procedure may vary between patients for the same procedure, as well as between different procedures.

QITI outlines if there is an aftercare period set against an item. Aftercare days are only set as a guide against an item. This is internal information and the aftercare days should not be given to members of the public or practitioners.

5

Where an attendance is not related to aftercare, what is an acceptable notation?

Answer: All medical practitioners who perform a procedure must provide sufficient information on the account where an attendance is not for routine aftercare.

Acceptable notations include wording similar to:

  • Not Normal Aftercare
  • NNAC
  • NNA
  • Not related to procedure

6

Where can I find more information on aftercare?

Answer: You can find more information on aftercare in the Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) explanatory notes. You can download the latest version of the MBS from MBS online and then search using 'aftercare'.
