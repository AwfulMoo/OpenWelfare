Determining whether an Activity Agreement is required

Step

Action

1

Activity Agreement negotiated in the online claim + Read more ...

Has the student completed an online claim, and an Activity Agreement is available to view in the claim summary in Process Direct?

  • Yes, go to Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Student returning to study + Read more ...

Is student returning to study a new tertiary course?

  • at the same level as a course they have previously studied but not completed, or
  • the same type of course (for example, Bachelor of Arts) at a different institution?

Note: if a student returns to the same course at the same institution this procedure does not apply, see Determining allowable time/reasonable time for a course of study

Service Officers are not required to undertake an allowable time assessment once the negotiation of an Activity Agreement has commenced and/or is in place.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, this procedure does not apply

3

Course duration + Read more ...

Failure in a course is when a student is precluded by the institution from completing the course, for any reason.

Is the course duration 12 months or less?

  • Yes, students can complete any number of courses at the same level if they are 12 months or less in duration. An Activity Agreement is not required. Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • If the student withdrew from their previous course, go to Step 4
    • If the student failed their previous course, go to Step 5

4

Student withdrew from their previous course + Read more ...

Did the student withdraw from their previous course at the same level for reasons beyond their control?

  • Yes, see Disregarding previous study for Youth Allowance (YA). Procedure ends here
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

5

Students failed their previous course + Read more ...

Students who fail their first course and enrol in a second course are not required to enter into an Activity Agreement, these students are considered to be making satisfactory progress if they complete the second course in the minimum time.

Where a student fails to successfully complete the second course they may only be considered to be making satisfactory progress if the reason for failing the second course was for reasons beyond their control.

If the student is undertaking a third or subsequent course and has not been affected by reasons beyond their control, they are not deemed to be making satisfactory progress. These students would be required to test their eligibility as a job seeker and determine if their study can form part of their mutual obligation requirements.

If a customer indicates in the online claim or ACC that they have previously attempted, but not completed one course at the same tertiary level as their current course, additional questions will be asked to capture:

  • if the customer withdrew from or failed the course, and
  • whether the withdrawal or failure was due to exceptional circumstances

These questions are to determine whether a customer must enter into a YA Activity Agreement, and if so the online claim or ACC will display a personalised agreement with a declaration and allow the customer to agree or disagree.

If the customer indicates they have withdrawn from 2 or more courses at the same level, the claim is unable to identify if the student is returning to a course they have previously studied, therefore processing staff will need to manually determine if an agreement is required.

Steps to negotiate an Activity Agreement

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Contact student + Read more ...

Was the student able to be contacted or have they attended the Service Centre?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Discuss Activity Agreement with the student + Read more ...

Advise student the reason they are required to negotiate an Activity Agreement is:

  • they have previously discontinued study without sufficient reason, and
  • they are at risk of not completing current study and not satisfying the activity test for full-time Youth Allowance (YA) students

Explain the requirements to enter into Activity Agreement and their rights and obligations:

  • they can request a variation or renegotiation of an Activity Agreement at any time
  • they can apply for a formal review of any decision in relation to the Activity Agreement, see Steps in the review and appeal process
  • they must notify Services Australia of changes in their circumstance, and they must comply with the terms of the Activity Agreement

Negotiate the terms of the Activity Agreement, taking into account the person's capacity to comply with the agreement and their needs. See References.

Go to Step 3.

3

Issue Activity Agreement + Read more ...

Open an SY103 form available from the Resources page. Update the form with the student's name, study details and dates as follows:

  • Start date: the date the student commenced study in their current course
  • End date: the estimated current course end date if completed within the minimum time

This form should then be printed, signed and provided to the student.

Claims must not be held awaiting the return of a YA Activity Agreement. Finalise the claim and set a review for 22 days to check if the Activity Agreement has been returned or uploaded by the student. Use an appropriate review reason, for example: FOR - return of form/correspondence.

Note: if contact with the student was unsuccessful, issue the Student Activity Agreement with a written request for information. See Requesting information (CLK). When completing the letter, select the relevant options:

  • change the Print Status to Print Locally
  • select Form request - Customer from the document list
  • under Additional Options, select the form SY103 from the drop down menu
  • a DOC will be created on the customer's record

YA student requests to consider the terms of the Activity Agreement

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Activity Agreement is a digital Activity Agreement generated within the online claim + Read more ...

For YA tertiary students, who advise within the online claim:

  • they have only one previous incomplete tertiary course, and
  • they have not withdrawn or failed due to exceptional circumstances

they will be subject to a YA Activity Agreement within the online claim. If a YA Activity Agreement is required, the system will display a personalised agreement with a declaration and allow the customer to agree/disagree. All information is available within the Claim Overview in Process Direct.

Is there a YA Activity Agreement within the online claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Accepting the digital Activity Agreement within the online claim + Read more ...

Has the student accepted the digital Activity Agreement within the online claim?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Signing the Activity Agreement + Read more ...

Has the student signed and returned the Activity Agreement within 22 days?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Concerns with the Activity Agreement + Read more ...

If the student indicates concerns or expresses the need to consider the terms of Activity Agreement further, discuss the issues with the student. If they still maintain they wish to consider it further (for example, consult a third party before signing), the Service Officer should offer 'think time'.

Does the student want think time?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Activity Agreement terms not accepted in online claim + Read more ...

The student will need to be contacted to discuss the reason for the Activity Agreement and the requirement for the student to be making satisfactory progress in their course.

Will the student now accept the terms of the agreement?

  • Yes, send a completed SY103 and place the claim on hold for 14 days. Advise the student to upload the document if they have access to online services.
    Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Set a review + Read more ...

If the student has not agreed to the Activity Agreement within 22 days and has requested 'think time', set a review for 2 working days and allocate the review to Region code SNN. Use an appropriate review reason, for example: FOR - return of form/correspondence.

Note: payments will continue until the review date.

If the student is returning the Activity Agreement after think time, or has contacted again to advise they will not accept the terms go to Step 7.

7

Student now willing/unwilling to sign + Read more ...

Is the student now willing to sign the Activity Agreement?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No:
    • advise the student that although they are not willing to sign the activity agreement, they are still required to be making satisfactory progress in their course.
      Advise them that their previous study cannot be disregarded, and will be counted towards the allowable time assessment, which will have an effect on how long they will be eligible to receive payment for their current course
    • work out the allowable time and code the Allowable Time End Date, see Coding Study Details
    • record the discussion/updates in a DOC
    • procedure ends here

Recording details of a new Agreement or updating an existing Agreement

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Record details in Manage Activity Agreements for Students screen + Read more ...

Access screen via Customer First menu under Study> Student Activity Agreement.

  • If recording a new Activity Agreement, go to Step 2
  • If updating an existing Activity Agreement already on the student's record, go to Step 3

2

New Activity Agreement + Read more ...

To record details for a new Agreement:

  • complete blank fields under heading Activity Agreement Details
  • select Agreement Reason of Other
  • complete fields for Signed Date, Start Date and End Date as required.
    Note: period of new agreement cannot overlap existing agreement. Due to system limitations within the Student Activity Agreement workflow, the following dates should be coded as a workaround (where applicable):
    • the start date no earlier than the date the agreement is signed
    • the end date no later than 2 years from the start date
  • complete Notes field with reasons for Student Activity Agreement. (Information in this field will be transferred to DOC)
  • complete Source and Receipt Date fields
  • select Add button
  • finalise the activity via the Assessment Results (AR) screen

Procedure ends here.

3

Existing Activity Agreement + Read more ...

To update record for existing agreement

  • on Manage Activity Agreements for Students screen (accessed via Student Activity Agreement workflow), select required agreement from list under Existing Activity Agreement Summary
  • information for selected agreement will auto-populate and display under heading Activity Agreement Details
  • edit required fields and update notes by adding free text to Annotations field. (Information will be transferred to existing DOC)
  • enter the Source and Receipt Date
  • select Update button
  • to delete existing agreement, select Delete button
  • finalise the activity via the Assessment Results (AR) screen

Note: Clear button can be used when adding details for new record or updating an existing student Activity Agreement. When this button is selected, it will clear all fields displayed under Activity Agreement Details.
