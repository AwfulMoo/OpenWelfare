Providing options for debt recovery + Read more ...

If the debt may not be recovered from current or future claims, contact the claimant and advise:

  • of the overpayment
  • the debt amount raised

Possible recovery options

Advise claimant of possible recovery options:

  • offset against a current claim in part or in whole. For further information, see Item 1 of the Offsetting a debt against a claim/s, setting a repayment schedule and setting a review table
  • offset against future claim or claims in part or whole. For further information, see the Offsetting a debt against a claim/s, setting a repayment schedule and setting a review table
  • issue invoice for payment in full or by instalments via BPAY/cheque. For further information, see Item 1 of the Raising an invoice, checking an account status or invoice repayment table

Note: if the claimant would like to repay debt by instalments or partial withholdings, advise the claimant to put the request in writing with a proposed schedule within seven days of receiving the invoice.

No current or future claims

When there is no current or future claims, an invoice must be issued. See Item 1 of the Raising an invoice, checking an account status or invoice repayment table.

Recovery of TFES debt

Offsetting a debt against a claim/s, setting a repayment schedule and a setting review

Item

Description

1

Offsetting a debt against a claim + Read more ...

The debt will already exist on claimant record:

  • assess and complete new claim
  • the system will ask if a repayment should be applied, select Yes
  • a prompt will then ask if the repayment is partial or whole, select the relevant option
  • enter amount to be applied. Note: if whole repayment is applied an amount is not required to be entered, the system will automatically deduct the full amount
  • advise Debt Portfolio Holder of the offset of debt via e-mail
  • complete the TFES Overpayment Report found in the TTP Resource Guide. Print out the report and place in the SSO approvals tray for Quality Assurance (QA)
  • once activity is approved, send Overpayment for Assistance letter (Z2220), found in the TTP Resource Guide, to claimant via email or post stating the amount of the debt recovered and any amount remaining if applicable

2

Setting a repayment schedule/partial withholdings from future claims + Read more ...

Claimant must request repayment by instalments in writing, outlining the proposed amount, repayment schedule and reasons.

  • consider any risks to recovery, see Step 1 of the Determining the appropriate debt recovery method table
  • update claimant notes in myHSI with subject ‘Debt Recovery’
  • update Register Database in the TTP Resource Guide
  • provide proposal via e-mail for SSO or Team Leader approval

When returned by SSO or Team Leader

  • update claimant notes in myHSI with determination
  • create a claim review with due date for full recovery. See Item 3 of the Offsetting a debt against a claim/s, setting a repayment schedule and setting a review table
  • if an invoice is required for claimant to make BPAY/cheque repayments, raise an invoice for claimant. See Item 1 of the Raising an invoice, checking an account status or invoice repayment table

3

Setting a review + Read more ...

In myHSI, create a new activity:

  • go to myHSI>Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme (TFES) General, on the left hand menu select Create new activity
  • Activity type, select Claim review
  • Claim Review Type, select Overpayment Review
  • enter Claim ID and select Add

From Activity List action as follows:

  • select appropriate Overpayment Review activity from Activities List, record:
    • what action is being taken
    • details of what email or letter was sent to the claimant
    • details of the amount that is to be paid and the due date
  • Save the activity, but do not submit
  • go back to the Activity List, select appropriate Overpayment Review activity again and assign to Debt Portfolio Holder

The Debt review will remain open for the Debt Portfolio Holder to check repayment at 14 days, end of month and 30 days as appropriate.

The Resources page contains a link to myHSI.

Raising an invoice + Read more ...

Assessor action

  • create a Debt review activity noting that an invoice is to be raised and reason for debt, See Item 3 in the Offsetting a debt against a claim/s, setting a repayment schedule and setting a review table
  • refer to any notes or comments documenting conversations with claimant
  • assign activity to Debt Portfolio Holder

Check an account status or invoice repayment + Read more ...

The Debt Portfolio Holder, SSO or Team Leader to regularly check, at 14 days, end of month and 30 days as appropriate, SAP GUI to determine whether manual repayments have been made by claimant.

  • open SAP GUI and go to FBL5N screen
  • enter the customer account number. This is provided by Accounts Receivable when invoice is raised and is recorded in the debt review or comments
  • change company code to 'CENT', select All Items

Note: any repayments will have a DOC type ending in 9 and a DOC number starting with 200.

  • If repayments made, Debt Portfolio Holder will update claimant debt in TFES General and document that repayment has been actioned
  • If debt fully recovered, any reviews will be finalised, notes updated and relevant paperwork finalised

Requesting a waiver/write off and non-recovery of TFES debt

Waiver/write off and non-recovery

Table 4: This table provides the processes for requesting a waiver/write off from the Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development and potential non recovery.

Item

Description

1

Debt waiver + Read more ...

Waiving a debt means relinquishing the right to recover the overpaid amount. A debt waiver can be considered where:

  • there has been extraordinary administrative error
  • the debt is small, unable to be recovered by withholdings
  • the claimant is bankrupt or under administration
  • the claimant no longer exists

Note: debt waivers will rarely be approved as no future recovery action can be taken if the claimant’s situation changes.

2

Debt write off + Read more ...

A debt write off is where recovery action for the debt is suspended. A written off debt may be reactivated at a later time if the claimant’s circumstances change.

Examples may include trading out of administration or the claimant begins claiming again after a period of time.

3

Requesting a waiver or write off + Read more ...

Waiver and write off requests must be referred to the Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development.

  • the request can be initiated either by the claimant or by the Tasmanian Transport Program
  • requests from claimants must be in writing detailing the reasons for waiver/write off, together with any supporting evidence
  • refer requests for waiver/write off to the SSO or Team Leader for action

4

Processing a request for a waiver/write off + Read more ...

Document the request in claimant notes, including:

  • Debt ID
  • details of any conversations or documentation provided by the claimant, and
  • any supporting evidence provided or discovered

SSO or Team Leader will complete a Request to write-off a debt form, found in the Financial forms index, and forward to the Programme Team via e-mail for consideration. The Programme Team will then forward to Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development for a decision. The Resources page contains a link to the Financial forms index.

If approval is received, the Debt Portfolio Holder will action:

  • If an invoice has already been raised, send write off approval to Accounts Receivable by email. They will finalise the account and confirm nil recovery. The Debt Portfolio Holder will document the outcome on the claimant record
  • If no invoice was raised or confirmation received from Accounts Receivable, the Debt Portfolio Holder will complete the recovery action on the claimant record and document any action undertaken on the claimant record in the debt comments section

Note: if waiver or write off is not approved, see the Raising an invoice, checking an account status or invoice repayment table.

5

Non-recovery + Read more ...

If an invoice has been raised, Accounts Receivable will monitor repayment and issue written reminders at appropriate intervals. If not recovered they will contact Tasmanian Transport Program Team to discuss options.

If we are awaiting recovery by offsetting debt against future claims but the claimant fails to lodge within 30 days, the Debt Portfolio Holder will initiate the invoice procedure. See the Raising an invoice, checking an account status or invoice repayment table.
