Scenarios - checking if verbal details previously provided by the customer are suitable for Family Day Care

Table 1: How Compliance Officers (CO) check if the verbal information can be used to update the record.

Item

Examples

1

Scenario 1

  • The customer contacts about their Family Day Care (FDC) intervention
  • The CO successfully establishes details relating to the FDC business
  • The CO records the information against the required questions for each financial year

It is determined that the information recorded on the customer record is suitable for an update on the customer record. The CO would calculate the business percentage using the A x B X C = D formula for assessing child care business income.

2

Scenario 2

  • The customer contacts about their Family Day Care (FDC) intervention
  • The CO is unsuccessful in establishing details relating to the FDC business
  • The CO records the information against some of the required questions

It is determined that the information recorded is not suitable for an update to the customer record.

Assessing the Educator Payment History Report (EPHR) from Family Day Care Provider

Table 4

Item

Assessment

1

The EPHR divides the total child care fees billed by the Educator into two columns, 'total fees' and 'administration levy'.

When using the EPHR as a source document for business income assessment, the Educator's gross income is the 'total fees' plus the 'administration levy'.

Note: the administration levy is an allowable deduction for Child Care Business. The accumulative amount of the administration levy can be deducted from the Educator's gross income for income tax purposes only.
