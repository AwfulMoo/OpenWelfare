Assessing independence when a customer is/was married or a member of a couple 001-04060020

This document outlines how to assess if a customer is eligible for independent status for Youth Allowance (YA) or the independent rate of Disability Support Pension (DSP).

Definition of independence

A customer is considered independent if they are or have been:

  • legally married
  • in a registered relationship
  • in a de facto relationship for a minimum of 12 months, or
  • in a de facto relationship for a minimum of 6 months, in special circumstances

This includes living (or having previously lived) as a member of a couple for a continuous period of at least 12 months or more (or 6 months in special circumstances - this can include the death of a partner or when the customer may have left an abusive relationship).

Both members of the couple must be over the age of consent in the state or territory where the couple resides and it must not be a prohibited relationship under section 23B of the Marriage Act 1961, for example, a relationship between close blood relatives.

If a YA customer is considered independent under this criterion, they are not subject to the Parental Means tests.

Assessing relationship status for independence

The length of a relationship excludes any periods the young person or their partner were under the age of consent in the state they were living.

A young person must not be assessed as a member of a couple if they are under the age of consent, or not legally married or in a registered relationship. This means that the customer and their 'partner' must be assessed under single qualification and payability conditions. They should not be linked to their 'partner' now or previously.

If the relationship as a member of a couple started while living outside Australia, the age of consent is the age that applied in the State or Territory where they first lived on arrival or return to Australia.

Forms used to assess a customer's relationship status are:

  • Relationship Details (SS284)
  • Relationship Details - Separated under one roof (SS293)

Temporary separations and breakups interrupt the continuity of the relationship, unless the customer can prove the continuous nature of the relationship during those separations.

Automatic assessments of independence when a customer is/was married or a member of a YA couple

Certain YA customers, under 22 years of age in some circumstances, may have their independence automatically assessed within their online claim.

If current or previous relationship details on the YA customer's file indicate they are independent, the independence coding will pre-fill when processing their claim.

In these situations, it is not necessary for the customer to supply further information or verification. The Parental Income Details (MOD JY) will not be requested at the Next Steps page.

Youth Disability Supplement

Youth Disability Supplement (YDS) is available to:

  • DSP customers under 21 years
  • YA/ABSTUDY customers under 22 years who have been assessed as having a partial capacity to work of less than 30 hours per week

Unfavourable decisions

When making an unfavourable decision, speak to the customer:

  • explain the decision
  • give them a chance to provide more information, and
  • advise their review and appeal rights
