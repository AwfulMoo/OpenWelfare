Authenticating a customer for Medicare Compensation Recovery

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Authenticating a customer + Read more ...

Note: case note standards must be met including recording the caller’s first name as a minimum, or full name if injured person/claimant. All case notes must be generated using the Compensation Recovery Case Notes Macro.

Identify the caller. If the caller is:

  • the injured person or claimant, go to Step 2
  • the authorised third party, go to Step 3
  • the insurer/compensation payer, go to Step 4
  • the insurance company/compensation payer’s solicitor, go to Step 5

2

Injured person or claimant + Read more ...

When a claimant is acting on behalf of an injured person, first confirm we have evidence of their authority to act.

See Authority to release information and acting on a person's behalf for Medicare Compensation Recovery and Minors and Medicare Compensation Recovery.

Ask caller 4 Category A- Mandatory questions:

  • What is the injured person’s full name?
  • What is the injured person’s date of birth?
  • What is the injured person’s Medicare card or our reference number (WIN)?
    Note: Medicare card numbers can be checked with our records e.g. systems or correspondence received
  • What is the injured person’s residential address? (current or previous)

If the caller has:

  • not answered at least 3 mandatory questions correctly, do not proceed. Ask them to obtain their information and call back. Procedure ends here
  • answered 4 mandatory questions correctly, ask 1 additional Category B question (see below)
  • answered 3 mandatory questions correctly answered, ask 2 additional Category B questions (see below)

Category B- Additional questions:

  • What was the date of injury?
  • What is our reference number (WIN) OR Medicare card number OR authorised third party reference number? (Ask for one of the reference numbers which has not been answered as a Mandatory question)
  • What is the compensation type? (e.g. Motor Vehicle Accident (MVA), workers compensation, public liability etc)
  • What is the injured person’s telephone number?
  • What is the injured person’s postal address?
  • What is the injured person’s email address?
  • What is your authorised third party’s full name or business name?
  • What is the business name of the insurer/compensation payer?
  • What is the business name of the compensation payer/insurer’s solicitor or agent?

If the caller is

  • unable to answer the additional category B questions, tell the caller to obtain their information and call back. Procedure ends here
  • able to answer the additional category B questions, proceed with their enquiry. Procedure ends here

Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone Skills - announced, unannounced and cold call transfer procedures

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Unannounced Transfers (Cold) + Read more ...

In some instances it may be appropriate to instantly transfer a caller to another business line without introducing the caller first. This is referred to as an unannounced or cold transfer.

Does the call need to instantly transfer to another line?

  • Yes:
    • with the caller on the line (not on hold) select instant call transfer
    • search for and select the required number or programme
    • select instant call transfer
    • call will transfer and softphone will drop into 10 secs of After Call Work (ACW)
    • procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Announced Transfers (Warm) + Read more ...

An announced transfer (warm) is when the call is introduced by the first Service Officer to another Service Officer or other person receiving the call. All call escalations should be warm transferred.

To warm transfer a call, Service Officers must:

  • with the caller on the line (not on hold) select start a consultation
  • search for and select the required number or programme
  • select initialise a voice consultation. The first caller will be placed on hold and a new call will be started
  • to connect the two calls select instant call conference
  • introduce the caller to the Service Officer or the other person taking over the enquiry
  • end the call
  • call will transfer and softphone will drop into 10 secs of After Call work (ACW)

Procedure ends here.

Compensation Recovery case note macro process

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Access the Medicare Compensation Recovery Case Notes Macro + Read more ...

The macro is located on the Medicare Compensation Recovery SharePoint page.

Access the macro:

  • select 'I agree' to the cover page conditions
  • select Work Type: Telephony
  • select Type of Call: Inbound or Outbound radio button
  • go to Step 2

2

Determine who the caller is + Read more ...

  • Is the caller an injured person? - go to Step 3
  • Is the caller a parent/guardian of injured person? - go to Step 4
  • Is the caller an injured person’s solicitor? - go to Step 5
  • Is the caller an insurer? - go to Step 6
  • Is the caller an insurer’s solicitor? - go to Step 7
  • Is the caller another party enquiring about Compensation Recovery? - go to Step 8

3

The caller is an injured person + Read more ...

  • enter the Injured Person’s full name
  • select Injured Person radio button
  • confirm if Customer Authentication was completed successfully
  • go to Step 9

4

The caller is a parent/guardian of injured person + Read more ...

  • select Parent/Guardian radio button
  • enter the Parent or Guardian’s full name
  • confirm if Customer Authentication was completed successfully
  • go to Step 9

5

The caller is an injured person’s solicitor + Read more ...

  • enter the Caller’s name
  • select Injured Person’s Solicitor radio button
  • enter Solicitor Firm’s name
  • enter Solicitor Suburb
  • confirm if Customer Authentication was completed successfully
  • confirm if Authority was verified
  • enter the folder line number the Authority is located in
  • go to Step 9

6

The caller is an insurer + Read more ...

  • enter the Caller’s name
  • select Insurer radio button
  • enter name of Insurance Company
  • enter Insurer Suburb
  • confirm if Customer Authentication was completed successfully
  • enter Folder Line number of documents used to complete Customer Authentication
  • go to Step 9

7

The caller is an insurer’s solicitor + Read more ...

  • enter the Caller’s name
  • select Insurer Solicitor radio button
  • enter name of Insurer Solicitor's Firm
  • enter Insurer Solicitor's Suburb
  • confirm if Customer Authentication was completed successfully
  • enter Folder Line number of documents used to complete Customer Authentication
  • go to Step 9

8

The caller is another party enquiring about Compensation Recovery + Read more ...

  • enter the Caller’s name
  • select Other radio button
  • enter Caller’s Business Name
  • enter Caller’s Business Suburb
  • confirm if Customer Authentication was completed successfully
  • enter Folder Line number of documents used to complete Customer Authentication
  • go to Step 9

9

Do details need to be updated? + Read more ...

Select if details were updated Yes/No radio button:

  • Yes, enter the details to be updated in the free text box
    • go to Step 9

Note: this function will not update details in CCMS

  • No, radio button was not selected
  • go to Step 10

10

Action Taken + Read more ...

  • select the appropriate radio button corresponding with primary action taken on the call
    • if selecting Extension Granted, enter original due date and macro will calculate the new due date
    • if selecting NOPB Amount Provided, enter the amount in the Amount free text box
  • go to Step 10

11

Reason for the call + Read more ...

Service Officer should enter in the details of the call in the free text box.

  • go to Step 11

12

Is Follow Up Required? + Read more ...

Select Yes/No radio button:

  • Yes, enter the follow up action required

Note: this function will not send a Follow Up email.

Services Officers will need to access the Follow Up template either at the top of the macro or on the Operational Blueprint letters and electronic messaging site.

  • No, go to Step 11

13

Generate case note + Read more ...

  • select Generate Case Notes
  • copy the generated case note into the Case Notes Viewer on CCMS

End of Procedure.
