This page contains information relating to the process involved when a customer requests to vary the repayments of an advance request. This process does not apply to advance payments for the Home Equity Access Scheme.

Varying repayments of advance payments

Initial assessment of request to vary repayments

Table 1: This table describes the process involved when a customer requests to vary the repayments of an advance request.

Step

Action

1

Requests to vary the repayments of an advance payment + Read more ...

Note: variations to the repayments cannot be completed on the same day as the advance is granted.

  • If the customer can use Centrelink online services or Express Plus Centrelink mobile app to make changes. See Advance payment options online. Procedure ends here
  • If the customer wants to vary repayments for a Mobility Allowance (MOB) advance payment advise them that repayments cannot be changed. Procedure ends here
  • If the customer is not able to apply for a new advance because of an existing reduced or suspended repayment arrangement, ensure that they are no longer in financial hardship before proceeding. Go to Step 7
  • For all other requests, if the customer wants to:
    • decrease their advance repayments, go to Step 2
    • suspend their advance repayments for a short period, go to Step 3
    • increase their repayments, go to Step 7

2

High rate of recovery and wants to reduce rate + Read more ...

Does the customer already have a higher rate of recovery and wants to reduce their repayment rate to above or equal to the standard rate of recovery?

  • Yes, the amount should be reduced without question. Hardship provisions do not apply therefore the customer should not be asked hardship questions:
    • To complete the update in Customer First, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via workflow in Customer First table
    • To complete the update in Process Direct, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via Process Direct table
    • For online channel, advise the customer they are able to decrease their advance repayments via their Centrelink online account or Express Plus mobile app also
  • No, go to Step 3.

3

Released from prison and meets financial hardship criteria for Crisis Payment + Read more ...

Has the customer been released from prison and meets the financial hardship criteria for Crisis Payment?

  • Yes, the customer is eligible for a reduction in, or suspension of, the recovery of an existing advance payment for 4 weeks:
    • To complete the update in Customer First, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via workflow in Customer First table
    • To complete the update in Process Direct, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via Process Direct table
  • No, severe financial hardship due to special and unusual circumstances which could not have been foreseen at the time they were originally granted the advance can be considered. Note: if the customer is requesting a suspension of repayments and they are in a full-time residential program at a rehabilitation centre and that centre is appointed as the correspondence nominee, the centre can be contacted by phone to confirm financial hardship. Go to Step 4

4

Special and unusual circumstances + Read more ...

Establish if there are special and unusual circumstances making it difficult for the customer to make repayments at the current rate.

Have there been special and unusual circumstances which could not have been foreseen?

  • Yes,
    • for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) advances, go to Step 5
    • for Social Security Entitlement advances, go to Step 6
  • No, continue at current rate. This decision still needs to be recorded via the Advance Payment workflow (using the Modify button):
    • To complete the update in Customer First, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via workflow in Customer First table
    • To complete the update in Process Direct, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via Process Direct table

5

FTB advances: Establish severe financial hardship + Read more ...

Deduct reasonable expenses from the customer's gross fortnightly income to determine net fortnightly income.

Reasonable expenditure includes, but is not limited to: rent, groceries, electricity, minimum loan repayments, school fees, medical costs, etc.

The rate of family assistance and Social Security Entitlement payments a family group would be paid (if eligible), may be used as a benchmark when comparing the family's income and expenditure.

  • The family's gross income should include FTB prior to any existing FTB advance deductions
  • If the family's income is greater than the benchmark amount, it may be relevant to examine whether their reported expenditure is reasonable

Is the net fortnightly income less than the standard deduction for the outstanding FTB advance?

  • Yes, the customer is considered to be in severe financial hardship. Repayments can be reduced or suspended:
    • To complete the update in Customer First, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via workflow in Customer First table
    • To complete the update in Process Direct, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via Process Direct table
  • No, continue at current rate. This decision still needs to be recorded via the Advance Payment workflow (using the Modify button):
    • To complete the update in Customer First, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via workflow in Customer First table
    • To complete the update in Process Direct, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via Process Direct table

6

Social Security Entitlement advances: Establish severe financial hardship + Read more ...

Deduct reasonable expenses from the customer's fortnightly income.

Reasonable expenditure includes, but is not limited to:

  • general living expenses such as rent, groceries, electricity, minimum loan repayments, school fees, etc.
  • unusual or unexpected expenses such as medical costs, essential repairs, etc.

Is the remaining fortnightly income less than the standard deduction for the outstanding Social Security Entitlement advance?

  • Yes, the customer is considered to be in severe financial hardship. Repayments can be reduced or suspended:
    • To complete the update in Customer First, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via workflow in Customer First table
    • To complete the update in Process Direct, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via Process Direct table
  • No, continue at current rate. This decision still needs to be recorded via the Advance Payment workflow (using the Modify button):
    • To complete the update in Customer First, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via workflow in Customer First table
    • To complete the update in Process Direct, see Step 1 in the Advance repayments variations system coding via Process Direct table

7

Customer wants to increase rate of repayment of advance + Read more ...

  • For non-pension customers, advise the customer they are only entitled to 1 advance each 12 months even if the current advance is paid off sooner. Exception: any Parenting Payment recipient who applies for an advance within 28 days of separating from their partner may receive an advance payment regardless of when they received their last advance, as long as any earlier advance has been repaid
  • A pension customer may receive up to 3 advance payments in a 13 fortnight period depending on any other advances made in the last 13 fortnights and any monies owed from previous advance payments received. Paying an outstanding advance early will not benefit the customer if the advance is less than 13 fortnights old. Where multiple advances exist, it is recommended that advances over 13 fortnights old, or the oldest advance that is under 13 fortnights old, be recovered earlier than advances granted more recently
  • A Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customer can receive 1 regular advance payment and 1 or more one-off FTB advance payments as long as the total amount of the advance payments does not exceed their maximum FTB advance payment amount. Repaying an existing regular advance payment early will not entitle the customer to another regular advance sooner as they are only payable every 182 days (26 weeks)

An additional FTB advance payment cannot be received if:

  • there is any amount outstanding on an FTB advance payment granted more than 12 months previously, or
  • the amount available to be advanced (that is, the gap between the customer's maximum FTB advance payment amount and the total of the existing outstanding FTB advance payments) is less than the minimum advance amount
  • advise the customer they can increase their advance repayments via their Centrelink online account or Express Plus mobile app

Coding of request to vary repayments - via Process Direct

Advance repayments variations system coding via Process Direct

Table 2: This table describes the system coding involved when a customer requests to vary the repayments of an advance request.

Step

Action

1

Run the Manage Advances action in Process Direct to modify the advance repayment + Read more ...

From the Process Direct Launchpad:

  • Select the Customer Summary tile
  • Search for the customer
  • On the Personal Information screen, select the More Options button
  • Select Manage Advances

Run the Manage Advances action in Process Direct

  • Select the current advance payment from the dropdown list
  • From the Action dropdown list, select Adjust Repayment

On the Adjust Advance Repayment screen, complete

  • Contact method: select from dropdown list
  • How much can you afford to repay each fortnight?: code the new amount the customer has advised they want to pay
  • Thinking about your current fortnightly income and your expenses, such as rent, food, transport, medication, etc, how much do you have left at the end of every fortnight?: code the amount customer advises

If increasing repayment amount, go to Step 2.

If decreasing repayment amount or suspending recovery, go to Step 3.

2

Continue workflow: Record customer's responses + Read more ...

Accept the request to increase the repayment rate of the advance ensuring that the increase would not result in hardship for the customer.

Discuss with the customer if they can afford to increase repayments and input their responses.

Based on the discussion with the customer, the workflow will allow the following options:

  • amend the amount left at the end of each fortnight
  • reduce the amount of increased repayment amount
  • continue with the existing repayment agreement, or
  • reject the request for an increase

Complete the question: To increase your repayment I need to be sure it will not put you in financial hardship?

  • Yes, accept the increase and submit the activity. Go to Step 4.
  • No, do not accept the increase. Reject the request. Go to Step 4

3

Continue Workflow: Decrease repayment amount + Read more ...

If the repayment amount is less than the current repayment amount, further questions will need to be answered.

The request can also be rejected at this point if the customer does not meet the requirements of the financial hardship test at Step 5 - FTB advance or Step 6 - Social Security Entitlement advance.

Note: advance payment recovery cannot be reduced or suspended for greater than 3 months. The workflow will not allow the coding of a date more than 3 months in the future. If, for any reason the decreased rate needs to be for more than 3 months, the new date cannot be coded until the initial date has passed.

The service will offer FTB customers a referral to Financial Management support services.

Complete the following questions:

  • To be able to reduce your repayments, you must be in severe financial hardship due to a special and unusual change in your circumstances
    • if no reject request as customer is not in hardship
  • When will you be able to increase your repayment to the standard amount?
    • date to be entered
    • Note: repayments can be reduced for no more than 3 months

Submit the request.

4

Workflow continues: Finalise and issue receipt number + Read more ...

Advise the customer of the outcome. If the customer disagrees with the outcome, advise them they may request a review of the decision.

If the repayment amount has been reduced, the customer should be advised that once the new repayment arrangement has ceased the advance payment should be repaid as soon as practicable.

Offer a receipt number to the customer.

The workflow ends.

5

Notes and future review automatically created + Read more ...

The workflow will have automatically created a Note recording the necessary information:

  • new recovery amount
  • date of expiry of the automatic review
  • amount of standard recovery rate to be restored once the automatic review has expired
  • confirmation that the customer is aware that the decrease is for a set period of time and upon expiry that their repayments will return to the standard rate
  • reason for rejection, if appropriate

Reviews when a repayment is decreased/suspended

The workflow will have automatically coded a future activity (review) to automatically restore the repayments to the standard repayment rate, after the end date agreed when the repayment rate was reduced or suspended.

Over recovered Advance Payment

Advance payment has been over recovered and customer is owed money

Table 3: This table describes the system coding required when an advance payment has been over recovered and the excess funds need to be repaid to the customer.

Before any action is taken, check the customer record for any work items that relate to advance payments and complete these first.

Step

Action

1

Customer contacts to advise they have made a lump sum repayment but fortnightly repayments are still being taken out of their fortnightly payment + Read more ...

Check for the receipt

In Process Direct, from the Customer Details page:

  • in the bottom right hand corner of the screen, select the More Options () icon
  • from the list, select Manage Advances.

From the Overview tab

  • Open the Recent Lump Sum Payment item
  • Check the Recent Lump Sum Payment table for the receipt
  • The Recent Lump Sum Payment table displays all payments that have been received for advances. Payments made by credit/debit card will display the Payment type of GEP. Payments made at an Australia Post outlet will display the payment type of AUSPOST>

Is the receipt showing on the table?

  • Yes, make a note of the advance start date and amount paid, go to Step 2
  • No, see Step 5 in the Adjusting advance payments where payment has not been applied table (Table 6) in Lump Sum repayment of advance

2

Check balance of current advance + Read more ...

Open the Current Advance item, locate the advance by matching the effect date in this table with the start date as shown for the receipt in the Recent Lump Sum Payment table.

Click on the arrow beside the relevant advance effect date to view the transaction history.

Check if there is a payment showing that matches the amount that was recorded in the Recent Lump Sum Payment table (for example, is there a matching amount with a transaction type of credit/debit card, Post Billpay or Staff Adjustment).

  • If payment is not showing, see Step 3 in the Adjusting the balance of an advance payment table (Table 5) in Lump Sum repayment of advance
  • If payment is showing, the payment has been credited to the advance. Go to step 3

3

Process work items + Read more ...

Where the system has continued to recover repayments from the customer’s payments after the credit card or cash repayment was made, the advance will be over recovered. There will be an E054AV Balance variation exceeds outstanding advance balance in Customer First that needs to be completed to align the balance and/or repayment history in CF and PD so they match.

  • Take note of the information within the note details
  • Return to Activity Details via the back button
  • Change the Status of the work item to 'Finalised' via the drop down list, then click on the Save button
  • The work item will auto update to Completed
  • If after the work item is finalised the status updates to 'For Manual Action', complete all manual updates then 'Complete' the work item
  • In Process Direct, check the advance has updated correctly. Manual action may still be required

Manual Action:

  • In Process Direct, in Manage Advances
    • Click on the twisty next to Recent Lump Sum Payments to see all the receipts
    • Look for the coinciding receipt id
    • Take note of the start date and advance balance to identify the advance payment and status (should be RECMAT - reconciled and matched)
    • Check Current Advance and Previous Advance table to identify the advance
    • Click on the twisty next to the correct advance to see the transaction history

All work items need to be processed to ensure all updates are made.

Only once this is completed can Service Officers navigate to Process Direct and click the SAP Refresh button (spinning arrows) in the top right hand corner of the customer’s record. Wait 5 minutes for the balance and/or repayment history in CF and PD to match and then proceed.

4

Balance showing as over recovered - update advance balance + Read more ...

In the Current Advance item, for the relevant advance, select the drop down list under Action and select Adjust Balance. On the Adjust Advance Balance page, open the Adjust Balance item and complete the following fields:

  • Contact method
  • Balance adjustment reason, select System Fault
  • ICT contacted and advised outstanding balance to be updated, select No
  • ICT Problem Number - SP11000
  • What is the reason the outstanding balance is being modified? (free text field) – Advance Payment over recovered (ensure further details are included)
  • Enter new balance - this will be zero

Then Submit the change.

5

Check advance has been successfully updated + Read more ...

As the advance balance has been repaid in full, the advance will show in the Previous Advance table with the repayment showing with payment type of 'Staff Adjustment'

Excess repayments are to be reissued to the customer via the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen.

6

Repay funds to customer using Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen + Read more ...

Payments made using the code AVR will not be included as income received from the agency, as they are purely a refund of payments.

Payment to be made by PYAJ

  • If a payment needs to be made by PYAJ but the Service Officer does not have access, the request must be escalated to the appropriate staff member
  • Staff need to follow normal escalation procedures to have this payment made. For service centre and Smart Centre staff, this is a staff member at the APS 5 level or above, e.g. Service Support Officer. For Smart Centre staff, see Service Officer role when seeking Technical Peer Support (TPS) in Smart Centres
  • A DOC must be created detailing why the payment must be made via PYAJ

Access to the PYAJ screen has been limited to:

  • service centre and Smart Centre Telephony officers at the APS 5 level or above
  • ICT Service Desk staff
  • payment helpdesk staff
  • debt recovery staff

7

Processing information for staff with PYAJ access + Read more ...

Go to the PYAJ screen in the relevant system. This will display the Payment Adjustment Warnings (PAWS) screen with the following information:

Warning: use of the PYAJ screen is monitored and any payments issued using this screen must be documented

  • Enter 'S' to view help
  • Do not use this screen to do any of the following:
    • issue a top-up payment when a customer changes their Entitlement Period End Date (EPED)
    • reissue a returned Direct Credit payment
    • issue arrears after review of a penalty
    • make Bereavement Payments
    • pay Farm Help Re-establishment Grant - this should only be paid through Essentials
    • workaround system faults unless instructed by the ICT Service Desk
  • Complete the Do you want to continue? (Y/N): field
  • If, after reading the warning, the decision is made not to proceed, code 'N'. Procedure ends here

If the action does not involve any of the above, code 'Y'. The PYAJ screen will display. Go to Step 8

8

PYAJ screen + Read more ...

Code the following fields:

  • Reason Code = 'AVR' (Advance Payment Refund)
  • Service Reason = the payment type of the advance payment
  • Period Date (from & to): - The date cannot be a date in the future. The date in the Period Date: field must be earlier or equal to the date in the to: field.
  • Component: = Basic
  • Amount: = the amount of the over recovered repayments.
  • Reason for Payment: - Customer made lump sum repayment of advance. Repayments continued to be made from customer’s fortnightly payment. Advance has been finalised. Excess funds to be returned to the customer.
  • Source:
  • DOR: = Date of receipt (date of customer contact)

Press [Enter] to continue.

9

Payment Adjustment Confirmation (PYAJC) screen + Read more ...

The system will display the PYAJC screen.

Confirm that the details of the payment are correct then, in the Submit? (Y/N/Q): field, enter 'Y', 'N' or 'Q'

10

Finalise activity + Read more ...

The Assessment Results (AR) screen should be carefully checked before finalising any activity.

Annotate DOC to confirm payment has been made.

11

Customer needs money urgently + Read more ...

Payments will be made according to the payment delivery instructions which currently exist on the customer's Payment Destination (PA) screen.

To alter the payment method, reissue the payment via the Issue Future Payment (IFP) screen.

See Urgent Payment due to Centrelink delay

Advance repayments variations system coding via workflow in Customer First

Step

Action

1

Run workflow to modify the advance repayment + Read more ...

Run the Advance Payments workflow.

  • Select the advance payment by clicking on the date link in the Effect Date: field
  • Select Modify and complete the 3 fields on the Advance Payment Application page:
    • Channel contacted by
    • Rate of recovery of advance payment
    • Change to the outstanding balance

  • Select Channel contacted by
  • Code Rate of recovery as $0
  • Select No to changing the outstanding balance

  • Select Channel contacted by
  • Rate of recovery - code the amount nominated by the customer
  • Select No to changing the outstanding balance

If the rate of recovery is to be reduced:

  • Select Channel contacted by
  • Rate of recovery - enter the amount determined from the severe financial hardship test shown at Step 5 - FTB Advances or at Step 6 - SSEP Advance. Where multiple advances exist, it is recommended for recovery of the most recent advance to be adjusted rather than older advances
  • Select No to changing the outstanding balance

The workflow will display a prompt to determine if the repayment amount will put the customer in hardship.

  • If increasing repayment amount, go to Step 2
  • If decreasing repayment amount or suspending recovery, go to Step 3

  • amend the amount left at the end of each fortnight
  • reduce the amount of increased repayment amount
  • continue with the existing repayment agreement, or
  • reject the request for an increase

Will the increase put the customer into hardship?

  • Yes, do not accept the increase. Reject the request. Go to Step 4
  • No, accept the increase. Go to Step 4

3

Continue Workflow: Decrease repayment amount + Read more ...

Further fields appear to record amount of money left over at the end of the fortnight and when repayments will increase back to the standard rate. The request can also be rejected at this point if the customer does not meet the requirements of the financial hardship test at Step 5 - FTB advance or Step 6 - Social Security Entitlement advance.

Note: advance payment recovery cannot be reduced or suspended for greater than 3 months. The workflow will not allow the coding of a date more than 3 months in the future. If, for any reason the decreased rate needs to be for more than 3 months, the new date cannot be coded until the initial date has passed.

The workflow will offer FTB customers a referral to Financial Management support services.

4

Workflow continues: Finalise and issue receipt number + Read more ...

Complete activity notes on the Assessment Results page and select continue to complete activity.

Advise the customer of the outcome. If the customer disagrees with the outcome, advise them they may request a review of the decision.

If the repayment amount has been reduced, the customer should be advised that once the new repayment arrangement has ceased the advance payment should be repaid as soon as practicable.

Offer a receipt number to the customer.

Give the customer the choice of receiving an immediate printed receipt or a letter by mail or online letter advising them of the decision and information of their advance.

If, for whatever reason, a customer is not handed their immediate receipt/notice before they leave the office, the receipt is to be placed in the classified waste and not posted. A copy of the receipt/notice can be printed at a later date. The receipt/notice (NSSRCSLT) is stored on the History Summary (HS) screen for 28 days and can be reprinted from here. After 28 days the receipt/notice is archived into the Customer Archive Retrieval (CAR) system.

The workflow ends.
