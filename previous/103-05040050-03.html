Transferring balance from a finalised advance to a current advance + Read more ...

Where a customer has made a repayment against an incorrect advance and this has finalised the advance payment, the advance payment will need to be restored and the balance transferred.

To be eligible for a balance transfer/advance restoration, a customer must:

  • Request the transfer within 24 hours of making the payment (working days)
  • The repayment has finalised the advance (it has been fully paid off)
  • The balance change reason was lump sum repayment
  • An advance of the same type has not been granted since the payment was made
  • There is an available advance to transfer the payment to

Table 3: This table describes the process to follow when a customer requests a refund of a lump sum payment as they were not eligible for another advance payment. Before any action is taken, check the customer record for any work items that relate to that repayment and completed these first.

Step

Customer has requested a refund of lump sum payment + Read more ...

  • If customer is contacting to request refund, go to Step 2

2

Initial customer contact + Read more ...

If a customer has requested refund of their lump sum repayment, check the following all apply:

  • has the request been made within 24 hours of the payment being made (consider working days only when looking at the time frame)
  • has the customer been paid another advance payment of the same type since repayment was made (for example, was FTB advance paid off and a new FTB advance granted)
  • will the customer be in severe financial hardship if the payment is not refunded

If request is not made within the above time frame or customer has been paid another advance, the customer is not eligible for a refund unless the refund is part of a system issue (this includes where the customer has made the repayment twice due to a system delay in crediting the first lump sum).

If the customer is eligible for a refund, the customer should be advised that:

  • money cannot be repaid until Services Australia has received the funds, this may take up to 7 days
  • in the case of lump sum refunds, the payment will be issued back onto the credit card used to make the repayment
  • in the case of a part payment refund, the funds will be sent to the financial institution account where the customer's regular fortnightly payment is made (note this is a refund of a lump sum and not of over-recovered regular payments)
  • they will be contacted when refund has been finalised
  • an urgent payment will not be paid due to having to wait for the refund to be processed

Does customer wish to proceed with refund?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, record the details on a DOC. Procedure ends here.

4

Initiate request for refund + Read more ...

Service Officers can only initiate a request for a refund, they cannot make the actual refund. Special access is required to actually refund the funds to the customer and specialist staff process this.

In Process Direct, from the Snapshot page:

  • in bottom right hand corner of screen select the More Options () icon
  • from the list, select Manage Receipts

On the Pending Payment table

  • A warning message of 'You have no authorization for PPR table' will be displayed, no action is required in relation to this message and this will not prevent staff from following the steps below to initiate the request for refund activity
  • Identify and select the correct advance repayment
  • Select the More Options icon tile in the right corner of the screen. This will display the Request Refund task
  • Select the Request Refund task and the refund request will be initiated, and will create work item/manual follow up for 7 days' time
  • The work item may not show until the refund is ready to be actioned
  • Please only select the Refund Request once, otherwise multiple work items will be created

In Customer First

  • Record all details in a DOC
    • State whether it is a full or partial refund of the lump sum. If a partial refund, include the amount the customer is requesting, for example 'Partial refund of $25.45 requested'
    • Why the customer is requesting the refund
    • Customer has contacted within 24 hours (this relates to business days, not weekends)
    • Customer understands that money cannot be reissued until it has been received by Services Australia and this may take up to 7 days
    • Customer has not been granted a new advance of the same type
    • The payment will be issued to the customer's regular payment destination
    • Customer contact details
    • Finalise the DOC
  • When the work item matures (this can take up to 7 days for the payment to be received by Services Australia), the specialist staff will use this information to determine the customer’s eligibility to a refund

Procedure ends here.

5

Issue Refund - work item matured + Read more ...

Specialist access is required for this task

Work Item (ZADV_CLM_RERE) will mature after 7 days.

Before payment can be issued the following rules must be met:

  • Customer has requested refund within 24 hours
  • The payment has been received and reconciled against advance balance. The following statuses are considered reconciled and can be seen in the Status column in the Recent Lump Sum Payment table on the Overview page:
    • RECMAT - Exact match with payment receipt from ESSentials,
    • RECDIFF - Match with payment receipt from ESSentials with different amount
    • RECCLO - Manually matched
  • The balance of the advance has been reduced to zero
  • The customer's benefit status is current (payment cannot be cancelled)

Lump sum repayment of an advance payment

Table 6: This table describes the process to follow when a customer makes a lump sum repayment of an advance payment in a service centre.

Step

Action

1

Advance repayments + Read more ...

Note: Cash repayments should only be accepted where there is no other way for the customer to make the lump sum repayment, for example, they do not have a credit or debit card and there is no Australia Post outlet nearby.

The Request Advance Details (AVR) screen cannot be used to record a lump sum repayment of an advance payment. The Advance Payments workflow must be used.

If the customer wants to:

  • pay a lump sum off the outstanding balance of their advance payment, or
  • pay off their advance payment in full

advise the customer of the Current Balance of the advance payment and discuss the amount of the lump sum they wish to pay.

Discuss the reason for making the lump sum advance repayment.

The payment can be made by either cash, or cheque.

  • If paying by cash, the customer must have the correct change, as Service Centres do not have change available
  • If paying by cheque, the cheque should be made out to 'Collector of Public Money, Services Australia'

Note: ensure the amount of the repayment is not greater than the amount outstanding. Repayments that are higher than the amount outstanding, even by a few cents cannot be correctly recorded. See the Resources page for examples of situations when this may occur.

Advise the customer to take the lump sum payment into their local Service Centre.

Accept the repayment. Ensure the customer is certain they wish to proceed with the repayment, as once the outstanding balance of the advance payment shows as fully recovered the transaction cannot be reversed.

Outstanding advance payment balances will need to be modified using the Advance Payment workflow.
