How to conduct the Identity Confirmation process

Step

Action

1

Lodging identity documents + Read more ...

A customer or their nominee can lodge identity documents:

  • at a service centre
  • through an Agent site (Silver Service remote areas only)
  • via video chat online
  • online via upload documents
  • over the phone
  • by post (return documents to customers by registered post)

When a customer or nominee is lodging documents:

  • authenticate the customer
  • ask the nominee to authenticate themselves and the customer
  • select the correct record
  • check the claim is subject to Identity Confirmation. See the Resources page for more information
  • check the legal name or date of birth has not changed. See:
    • Changing the Legal name of customers or their children, or
    • Incorrect date of birth recorded
  • use Silver Service for rural customers at Agent site

2

Check Identity status + Read more ...

Check the customer's Identity Status under the Snapshot icon in Process Direct.

The Identity Confirmation Dashboard shows the Identity Status for Services Australia and/or Digital Identity.

If the person’s Identity Status is:

  • Confirmed, the process is complete. No further action required. Procedure ends here
    Note: this includes Confirmed (transitional) customers where the identity status is confirmed with no identity documents coded
  • No Linkage, the customer has lodged all required identity documents and does not need to complete linkage for their payment type, go to Step 7
  • Not Confirmed or Identity Status is not displayed
    • for partners of a Parenting Payment or Low Income Health Care Card customer, go to Step 6
    • for all other customers, go to Step 3
  • Ceased, go to Step 9

For more information see Resources Payments and services that require Identity Confirmation.

3

Accepting identity documents + Read more ...

When accepting identity documents, check that:

  • the person has a photographic identity document (required for linkage - face-to-face, Silver Service and video chat only)
  • documents are original, unaltered
  • foreign language documents are translated to English by Language Services
  • all name variations are recorded on the Customer Person Details Summary (CPDS) screen
  • digital driver licence presented has a refreshed application. See the Resources page for digital driver license security and refresh features

eligible customers can lodge identity documents online, see Upload documents

4

Code identity documents + Read more ...

The customer must give consent to use the following procedure.

If the customer refuses consent, do not use the Identity Confirmation Dashboard. See Coding Identity documents for the process and email details to the DVS Operations team.

Following permission, code the relevant document details through the Identity Confirmation Dashboard, see Coding identity documents.

Is Identity Confirmation complete?

  • Yes, click the Next button until the FINISH option presents. Select FINISH to confirm the customer's identity. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Providing more identity documents + Read more ...

If the customer needs more time to provide identity documents, complete assessment via the Identity Confirmation Dashboard.

For CCM aligned claims, once linkage is complete:

  • there is the option to upload remaining identity documents
  • an identity task will display in the online claim. It will display as a Required task on the Next Steps page
  • when the Identity Status is Confirmed, the outstanding task in the claim will be updated to Provided. This action will allow the claim to be finalised

Note: when the customer attends the service centre or video chat to complete linkage, staff must record identity document details through the Identity Confirmation Dashboard.

6

Coding identity documents to achieve Identity Status of No Linkage + Read more ...

This applies to:

  • partners of a Parenting Payment or Low Income Health Care Card customer, and
  • care receivers who are required to meet a no-linkage Identity confirmation status

To achieve Identity Status of No Linkage:

  • code the partner or care receiver’s identity documents through the Identity Confirmation Dashboard. See Coding identity documents
  • after verification, follow these steps to change the Identity Status from Not Confirmed to No Linkage:
    • select the Claim Details button from the Overview page
    • read the consent statement to the customer
    • code the Claim ID
    • select the Show Tasks button
    • complete the identity documents Required Task
    • select Finish
    • check the status is changed to No Linkage

If a care receiver or partner needs to meet identity requirements but cannot provide all the required identity documents, consider the following operational files:

  • Supporting Identity Confirmation for customers unable to attend in person
  • Alternative Identity

Procedure ends here.

7

Linkage + Read more ...

To complete linkage, the Service Centre, Silver Service or Video chat Service Officer must:

  • check the document is an approved photographic identity document
  • first look at the customer and then the image on the approved photographic identity document
  • consider changes that may have altered a customer’s facial features, such as:
    • the ageing process, e.g. wrinkles, skin folds, receding hairline
    • hairstyles or hair colour
    • wearing or not wearing glasses
    • facial hair
  • complete a visual comparison of customers who wear a facial covering

If the image on the document matches the customer’s facial features, check the Photo ID box in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard.

Is Linkage complete?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No,
    • if linkage photo document is expired, go to Step 8
    • if a link between the person and the identity cannot be made, go to Step 9
    • if the customer is unable to attend in person at a service centre or Agent site, or cannot complete linkage via video chat, see Supporting Identity Confirmation for customers unable to attend in person

8

DVS verified photo document is expired + Read more ...

An approved photographic identity document can be used for linkage if it was previously DVS verified and has expired before linkage is completed in person or via Video chat.

Service Centre or Video Chat staff only must check details that the expired photo document matches the DVS verified document in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard.

Is there a match?

  • Yes, complete linkage as per Step 7
  • No, do not use the expired identity document
    • if the current photo document of the same type is provided, tick photo check box after match
    • if a different photo document is provided
      - ask for original document to be deleted from the Identity Confirmation Dashboard, see Identity SME escalation process
      - code new photo document and tick photo check box after match
    • if the customer does not own another photo document
      - complete linkage using Knowledge Based Questions & Answers using the myGov linking tool

9

Cannot establish linkage or Identity Status is ceased + Read more ...

This applies when:

  • unable to complete the link between the person and the identity document presented. For example, the customer's facial features do not match the photographic identity document, or
  • ceased displays as the Identity Status in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard

In these cases:

  • do not accept additional documents by phone
  • ask if a different approved photo ID can be provided
  • do not check the Photo ID box in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard
  • scan identity documents and DOC what has taken place
  • do not let the customer know they are under question
  • do not grant pending claims
  • check if there is a DOC with a prior history
  • if unsure, check with the Identity Subject Matter Expert (SME), see Identity SME escalation process
  • if suspicious, follow process to Report Suspected Fraud
  • notify the Identity Program Helpdesk if the identity status is ceased
