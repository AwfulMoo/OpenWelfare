Permanent vacation of principal home

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer advises they are travelling + Read more ...

A customer may advise they plan to, or have started to travel the country in mobile accommodation. For example, in a caravan, campervan or mobile home.

For customers:

  • permanently vacating their principal home, go to Step 2
  • temporarily vacating their principal home, see Step 1 in the Vacation of principal home to travel table

2

Permanently left the principal home + Read more ...

Check the Accommodation Details (AC) screen.

Is or was the customer recorded as a homeowner for the principal home they left or will leave on commencement of travelling?

  • Yes, and customer has:
    • sold the principal home, go to Step 3
    • not sold the principal home go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 7

3

Sold home and moved into mobile accommodation + Read more ...

Does the customer intend to buy another home with the sale proceeds?

  • Yes, customer remains a homeowner
    • The portion of the sale proceeds to be used to purchase a new home are not counted as an asset for an initial 12 months. An additional 12 months exemption may be granted if certain criteria are met. For more information see Selling a home
    • The mobile accommodation is an assessable asset
    • The customer may be eligible to receive RA based on the costs of the temporary accommodation (for example, site/mooring fees paid)
    • See Step 6 in the Vacation of principal home to travel table
  • No:
    • The proceeds of the sale of the former principal home are assessed as an asset
    • The mobile accommodation is considered to be the customer’s new principal home
    • If the customer owns the mobile accommodation, go to Step 5
    • If the customer does not own the mobile accommodation, go to Step 6

4

Not sold the former principal home and moved into mobile accommodation + Read more ...

  • The mobile accommodation becomes the new principal home
  • The former principal home is an assessable asset. Issue a Mod R
  • If rent is received for the former principal home it is assessable income

Does the customer own the mobile accommodation?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Owns the mobile accommodation + Read more ...

  • The mobile home is the customer’s new principal home
  • The customer is a homeowner
  • If the mobile home is listed on Other Asset Summary (OASS) an update is needed
  • The customer may be eligible to receive RA based on the costs associated with the new principal home, for example site/mooring fees paid
  • Record details on a DOC. Suggested text for the DOC: 'Rent Assistance is payable while travelling. Customer/and partner are homeowners, eligible for RA while they travel. The mobile accommodation has been assessed as the principal home because the customer has permanently left the former principal home. The vacation of the principal home commenced on DD/MM/YY Social Security Act Section 11(7)'

See Step 5 in the Vacation of principal home to travel table

6

Does not own the mobile accommodation + Read more ...

  • The mobile home is the customer’s new principal home
  • The customer is a non-homeowner from the date they vacated or sold their former principal home
  • RA may be payable on accommodation costs. This would include the rental costs of the mobile accommodation and the site/mooring fees
  • Record the information on a DOC. Suggested text for the DOC: 'Rent Assistance is payable while travelling. Customer/and partner are non-homeowners eligible for RA while they travel. The hired mobile accommodation has been assessed as the principal home because the customer has permanently left the former principal home. The vacation of the former principal home commenced on DD/MM/YY. Social Security Act Section 11(7)'

See Step 5 in the Vacation of principal home to travel table

7

Non-homeowner + Read more ...

If the customer:

  • owns the mobile accommodation, they:
    • are now a homeowner
    • may qualify for RA for the costs associated with the new principal home, for example site/mooring fees
  • rents the mobile accommodation and will travel around the country, they:
    • are a non-homeowner
    • may qualify for RA for amounts paid for site/mooring fees and the hire of the mobile accommodation

See Step 5 in the Vacation of principal home to travel table.

Vacation of principal home to travel

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Temporary absence + Read more ...

The customer advises they have temporarily vacated their principal home because they will be travelling the country.

Is the customer a homeowner?

Check the Accommodation Details (AC) screen.

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Customer is a homeowner + Read more ...

The customer:

  • Continues to be assessed as an ineligible homeowner, and
  • Is not eligible to receive Rent Assistance (RA) until the 12 month continuous absence period from the principal home has elapsed

Where the customer intends to return to the principal home it remains an exempt asset for up to 12 months. This exemption applies even if the customer indicates that their absence may be more than the 12 month period.

Record any rental income for the principal home on the Other Income (OIN) screen

  • Record a Display on Access (DOA) DOC with the following suggested text: 'Homeowner, temporarily absent for less than 12 months, continuously. Rent Assistance is not payable while travelling. Customer/and partner are ineligible homeowners for RA. Vacation of the principal home while travelling is temporary and RA cannot be paid until the customer has been continuously absent from the principal home for 12 months. The temporary vacation of the principal home commenced on DD/MM/YY Social Security Act Section 11(7)'

At the end of the 12 month absence period, the house will be assessed as an asset.

If the customer:

  • Is living in a mobile accommodation they own and the 12 month absence period has elapsed:
    • they will still be assessed as a homeowner, and
    • the mobile accommodation (caravan, motor home, boat) needs to be removed from the customer's assessable assets (OASS screen)
    • they may be paid RA if they pay rent/site/mooring fees above the threshold
    • go to Step 5
  • Sells their home during the absence with the intention of purchasing a new home
    • The portion of these sale proceeds to be used to purchase a new home are not counted as an asset for an initial 12 months. An additional 12 months exemption may be granted if certain criteria are met. For more information see Selling a home
    • The mobile accommodation is an assessable asset
    • The customer may be eligible to receive RA based on the costs of the temporary accommodation (for example, site/mooring fees paid)
    • Go to step 6
  • Temporarily moves into another property they own:
    • the property they moved into will still be assessed as an asset
    • their principal home is exempt from the asset test for the 12 month continuous absence period

Tell the customer:

  • They must advise Services Australia of any changes in their circumstances. This includes advising when they move back or return for a short period to their principal home
  • If they return to their principal home, the 12 months starts again

At the end of the 12 month absence period, finalise the Display on Access (DOA) DOC on the record.

Procedure ends here.

3

Customer is a non-homeowner + Read more ...

If a customer and/or partner continue to rent their principal place of residence while they are temporarily travelling:

  • RA can continue to be assessed on that property
  • RA will not be assessed for the accommodation they stay in while travelling

Note: the 2 amounts cannot be combined.

RA is generally only payable when the accommodation being rented is regarded as the customer's principal home.

Rent paid for temporary accommodation does not qualify for RA except in limited circumstances.

Advise the customer:

  • Their absence (regardless of the intended duration) will be considered temporary unless the customer states a definite intention not to return to the principal place of residence to live
  • The vacated residence will continue to be treated as their principal home
  • They must advise Services Australia of any changes in their circumstances. This includes advising when they move back into their principal home
  • Record a Display on Access DOC with the following suggested text: 'Rent Assistance is not payable on fees paid while travelling. Customer/and partner are non-homeowners still paying rent for the temporarily vacated home. RA is assessed on the principal home which they have vacated temporarily while they travel. First date of absence from principal home is DD/MM/YY Social Security Act Section 11(7).'
  • When the customer moves back into their principal home, finalise the Display on Access DOC

4

Non-homeowner temporarily leaves principal place of residence + Read more ...

Run the Change in Contact Details workflow to update the address.

  • The new address should be recorded as a ‘TEM’ address
  • Do not alter the 'HOM' address when the customer has temporarily vacated their principal place of residence and they intend to return
  • Provided customer continues to pay rent for their principal place of residence, RA can continue to be assessed on that property and an update to AC is not required. Procedure ends here

5

Update the Address Details (AD) screen + Read more ...

Run the Change in Contact Details workflow to update address detail.

Ask the customer how long they will stay at their current address:

  • If Less than 12 months and they have moved to a RAA specified area:
    • The address should be recorded as a ‘TEM’ address to avoid incorrectly paying RAA to customers (including travellers), whose usual place of residence is not in the qualifying zones
    • If the customer no longer has a home address (home has been sold/permanently vacated), end-date the 'HOM' address
    • A new concession card may be sent unless issue of the card is suppressed. Update the concession card preferences if required.
    • Customer may be paid RA if they pay rent/site/mooring fees above the threshold at their current address
    • Complete the update of the Accommodation Details (AC) screen
  • If Less than 12 months and they have not moved to a RAA specified area:
    • The address should be recorded as a ‘HOM’ address
    • A new concession card may be sent unless issue of the card is suppressed. Update the concession card preferences if required.
    • Customer may be paid RA if they pay rent/site/mooring fees above the threshold at their current address
    • Complete the update of the Accommodation Details (AC) screen
  • If more than 12 months:
    • The customer qualifies for the payment of Remote Area Allowance (RAA) if their usual place of residence is within a RAA specified area
    • The address should be recorded as a ‘HOM’ address
    • A new concession card may be sent unless issue of the card is suppressed. Update the concession card preferences if required
    • Customer may be paid RA if they pay rent/site/mooring fees above the threshold at their current address
    • Complete the update of the Accommodation Details (AC) screen

6

Proceeds from sale of home are exempt and moved into mobile accommodation + Read more ...

Run the Change in Contact Details workflow to update the address.

  • The address should be recorded as a ‘TEM’ address, RAA will not pay if the customer moves to a RAA specified area
  • If the customer no longer has a home address (home has been sold), end-date the 'HOM' address
  • A new concession card may be sent unless issue of the card is suppressed. Update the concession card preferences if required.
  • Customer may be paid RA if they pay rent/site/mooring fees above the threshold at their current address
  • Complete the update of the Accommodation Details (AC) screen
