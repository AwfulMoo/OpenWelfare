Check patient eligibility for mental health planning and treatment services

Step

Action

1

Advise caller of online options + Read more ...

Advise the caller of their options to check a patient’s eligibility:

  • GPs can use the patient care plan history search in HPOS to view their patient’s care plan history
  • Health professionals can use the MBS item online checker in HPOS to see if their patient is eligible for an MBS item
  • All patients can access their Medicare claims history through their Medicare online account or Express Plus app
  • Remind the caller that the information provided is based on the patient’s Medicare claims history at the time of enquiry

Patients and health professionals can follow instructions on the Services Australia website.

If they want to continue with the call, go to Step 2.

2

Complete security check + Read more ...

  • For health professionals, see Perform security check when a health professional or their staff call about a claim
  • For consumers, see Authenticating (security check) a Medicare customer

Note: proceed only if the caller meets the security check.

Advise the caller that the eligibility information provided is based on the patient’s Medicare claiming history at the time of the enquiry and is subject to change if there are claims pending.

3

Retrieve the patient’s history + Read more ...

  • Key ‘NHSI,CARDNUMBER,PATIENT’ and press [Enter]
  • Press [PF9]. The patient PIN is displayed

Note: check history by PIN in all instances to ensure the complete claiming history is returned, not just the history associated with 1 card.

4

Check for paid GP MHTP items on the patient’s history + Read more ...

  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/2700-2712,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/2715-2717,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/272-277,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key 'NHSI,PIN,ITEM/281-282,SORT/DOS' and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/92112-92114,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/92116-92120,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/92122-92126,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/92128-92132,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/92134-92135,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93400-93411,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93421-93423,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93431-93442,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93451-93453,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]

Note: patients are also eligible when referred by consultant psychiatrists and paediatricians. For more information, see MBS Note MN.6.3 in the Resources page

Is the eligibility check for a plan or review item?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the eligibility check is for a treatment item, go to Step 7

5

Review result of patient history search for MHTP item + Read more ...

Did patient history check in Step 4 display a paid claim for a plan or review item?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No,
    • Advise the caller that the patient is eligible for a mental health planning item
    • Remind the caller that this information is based on the patient’s Medicare claims history for today’s date. Claims that have not been processed yet may affect their eligibility
    • Procedure ends here

6

Advise caller of the patient’s eligibility for MHTP item + Read more ...

Check QITI for information about claiming frequencies for MHTP items.

Are any of the plan or review items on history within the claiming frequency period for the item they want to claim?

  • Yes, advise the caller that the patient is not eligible for that item
  • No, advise the caller that the patient is eligible for that item

Note: Service Officers can advise the caller when the patient’s last plan item was if they ask.

Remind the caller that this information is based on the patient’s Medicare claims history for today’s date. Claims that have not been processed yet may affect their eligibility.

Procedure ends here.

7

Continue patient eligibility check for mental health treatment item + Read more ...

Did patient history check in Step 4 display a paid claim for a plan item?

  • No, go to Step 8
  • Yes:
    • For individual treatment item, go to Step 9
    • For group treatment items, go to Step 10

8

Check if the patient has a referral from a psychiatrist or paediatrician + Read more ...

Ask the caller if the patient has a referral from a psychiatrist or paediatrician.

Note: a patient can be eligible for treatment items on referral from a paediatrician or psychiatrist under items 104-109, 293-370 or 110-133. The Resources page has a link to MBS Notes MN.6.3 and MN.7.1 for more information on those eligible psychiatric or paediatric services.

Does the patient have a referral from a psychiatrist or paediatrician?

  • Yes,
    • For individual treatment items go to Step 9
    • For group treatment items, go to Step 10
  • No, advise the caller that the patient is not eligible for mental health treatment items as there is no current mental health planning item on the patients history. Procedure ends here

9

Check for paid individual mental health treatment items on the patient’s history + Read more ...

Count the number of services on the patient’s claims history since 1 January.

  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/283-287,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/371-372,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/941-942,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/2721-2735,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/80000-80015,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/80100-80115,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/80125-80140,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/80150-80165,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/91166-91176,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/91181-91188,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/91818-91821,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/91842-91845,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/93287-93386,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]

Does patient history display less than 20 paid individual treatment items since 1 January?

  • Yes, advise the caller that the patient is eligible for individual mental health treatment services under a MHTP
  • No, advise the caller that the patient is not eligible for an individual mental health treatment service

Note: if the caller asks, Service Officers can tell them how many services the patient has had under their care plan and how many they have left.

Remind the caller that this information is based on the patient’s Medicare claims history for today’s date. Claims that have not been processed yet may affect their eligibility.

If the patient has exceeded the maximum number of eligible services in a calendar year, the caller can be advised the patient can access more services in the new calendar year. Service Officers can also advise the caller that if the patient has used all the services under the existing referral, they need to get a new referral from their GP.

Procedure ends here.

10

Check for group mental health treatment items on the patient’s history + Read more ...

Count the number of paid group mental health treatment services on the patient’s claims history since 1 January:

  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/80020-80021,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/80120-80121,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/80145-80146,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]
  • Key ‘NHSI,PIN,ITEM/80170-80171,SORT/DOS’ and press [Enter]

Does patient history display less than 10 paid group treatment items since 1 January?

  • Yes, advise the caller that the patient is eligible for a group mental health treatment service
  • No, advise the caller that the patient is not eligible for a group mental health treatment service

Remind the caller that this information is based on the patient’s Medicare claims history for today’s date. Claims that have not been processed yet may affect their eligibility.

Note: if callers ask, Service Officers can tell them how many services the patient has had under their care plan, how many they have remaining, or when they would next be eligible.

If the patient has exceeded the maximum number of eligible services in a calendar year, the caller can be advised the patient can access more services in the new calendar year. Service Officers can also advise the caller that if the patient has used all the services under the existing referral, they need to get a new referral from their GP.

Procedure ends here.
