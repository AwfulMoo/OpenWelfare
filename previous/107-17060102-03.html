Person applies for a Departure Authorisation Certificate (DAC)

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Requesting a DAC + Read more ...

If a person contacts to apply for a DAC, record the following information on a DOC, the:

  • requested dates of travel
  • destination Country
  • reason for travel, including relevant information which will assist in the verification of travel reason, for example
    • if the person requests to travel for a dying relative, request the treating doctor's details
    • if the DAC is for work purposes they will need to provide employer or business contacts for verification

Did the person provide all relevant information?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, advise the customer the application process ceases until they provide the required information. Procedure ends here

2

Code a new DAC application + Read more ...

To code a new DAC:

  • navigate to the DAC List (OPDAL) screen
  • in the Create new DAC for ACT field: enter the required Act code (FAO, SSA, STU or PPL). It must matches the relevant DPO on the DPO list (OPDPL) screen
  • enter the Application Date on Departure Authorisation Certificate Details (OPDAD) screen

Note: complete these steps for each current DPO the person has.

The DAC will show on the OPDAL screen as 'Appl Recvd'

Annotate the DOC with the relevant details.

3

Investigate and verify person's circumstances + Read more ...

The DPO team should:

  • review the DPO case and in particular why the DPO is in place
  • review the reason for travel
  • validate travel reason provided by the person in their DAC application
  • consider requesting security
  • consider revoking the DPO

The above information will determine if the DAC submission will be approved or refused.

4

Prepare a DAC submission + Read more ...

Use the DAC Submission template to prepare a submission for the delegate to issue or not issue a DAC. If the person has more than one DPO, the DAC submission must recommend DACs be issued under the Different Act references.

The submission must specify how the application has met the following legislative conditions:

  • it is likely the person will depart and return to Australia within a period the delegate considers appropriate, and
  • it is likely the delegate will revoke the DPO in respect of the person within an appropriate period, and
  • if it is or is not necessary for the person to give security for the person's return to Australia

Clearly state any other factors that may affect the decision to issue or not issue a DAC. If the submission is incomplete or insufficient the DPO Officer will receive the submission back for further action.

Email to the DPO Team Leader with the subject heading 'FOR DECISION: DAC Submission - Person's name - CRN'.

If the person is expecting to travel within 72 working hours, mark the DAC as Urgent.

5

Code the DAC submission + Read more ...

Upon emailing the submission to the team leader, record the details in the Departure Prohibition Order System (DPOS):

  • navigate to the OPDAL screen
  • select the appropriate DAC
  • enter the DAC submission date on the OPDAD screen

The status on the OPDAL screen will show as 'Submitted'.

6

DPO Team Leader actions + Read more ...

The Team Leader will:

  • review the DAC submission for accuracy
  • ensure the content has all necessary considerations made in relation to the individual circumstances of the person, and
  • check the submission meets the policy and legislative conditions

If the team leader supports the submission, the documents should be emailed to the appropriate delegate for their consideration.

Delegate (EL1 and above):

  • Issue a DAC without security

Delegate (SES Band 2 and above only):

  • Issue a DAC with security. The delegate will also specify the amount of the security required

7

Delegate (EL1) actions + Read more ...

Based on the officer's recommendation, the delegate considers the recommendation and decides whether to issue a DAC or not.

  • If the DAC is approved for issue:
    • print, complete and sign the Delegate's Decision section of the submission
    • scan and email the Delegate Decision section of the submission to the Debt Management Departure Prohibition Order Team. For contact details, see Debt Programme and Compensation Recovery (DPCR) and Compliance Programme Branch contacts go to Step 11
  • If the submission to issue the DAC requires security:
    • update with the security recommendation and email the Director for their endorsement, go to Step 8

Note: if the delegate requires more information, return the submission to the DPO team outlining the additional information required. The DPO team will amend the submission. The updated submission will be sent to their team leader to forward back to the delegate for decision.

8

Director actions + Read more ...

The Director will review the DAC submission.

  • If the Director supports the submission:
    • print and sign the Endorsement section of the submission
    • email the submission, scanned endorsement section and NM/GM cover sheets to the National Manager for review
    • go to Step 9
  • If the submission is unsupported or requires more information:
    • return the submission to the DPO team for further investigation and information
    • procedure ends here

9

National Manager actions + Read more ...

The National Manager will review the DAC submission.

  • If the National Manager supports the submission:
    • print and sign the Endorsement section of the submission
    • scan and email the signed submission to the delegated General Manager for their decision
    • go to Step 10
  • If the submission is unsupported:
    • return the submission to the DPO team for further investigation and information
    • procedure ends here

10

General Manager actions - DAC decision + Read more ...

Based on the DPO Officer's recommendation, the delegate must:

  • consider the recommendation and decide whether to request security
  • determine the security amount
  • print, complete and sign the Delegate's Decision section of the submission
  • scan and email the Delegate's Decision section of the submission to the Debt Management Departure Prohibition Order Team. For contact details, see Debt Programme and Compensation Recovery (DPCR) and Compliance Programme Branch contacts

Note: if the Delegate requires more information, return the submission to the DPO team outlining the additional information required. The DPO team will amend the submission. The updated submission will be sent to their team leader to forward back to the delegate for a decision.

Go to Step 12.

11

Record DAC decision + Read more ...

Document the DAC decision once approved or rejected by the delegate. Include the date of decision and the delegate details.

Record the details in the DPO System:

  • navigate to the OPDAL screen
  • select the relevant DAC
  • update the DAC Decision: field with 'Approved' or 'Rejected'
  • enter the 'DAC Decision Date' and the 'Delegate AGS'
  • enter the DAC Depart and Return dates

The status of the DAC on the OPDAL screen will change to reflect the DAC decision ('Approved' or 'Rejected').

If requesting security the Security Sts: field will display as 'Pending'.

The Rsn for Decision: field will only populate when the DAC is Rejected.

Record the DAC decision details and the reason in the DOC.

Note: code a MFU review for the DAC Start Date + 10 days if this is in the future. See How to code and action a manual review. The system should automatically create an MFU for the DAC return date + 7 days

Is security required to issue the DAC?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in table 3
  • No, see Step 5 in table 3

Extending the DAC return date

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Requesting a substitute DAC return date + Read more ...

A person can apply to have the return date on their DAC substituted for a later date. A person must apply for a DAC either in writing or verbally.

Ensure to record the following information in a DOC, the:

  • proposed new return date
  • the reason for requesting a variation of travel. Include relevant information that assists in verifying the reason, for example:
    • if the person requests to travel for a dying relative, request the treating doctors details
    • if the person requires a DAC for work purpose they will need to provide business contacts for verification

Extend the return date if the delegate deems the change appropriate. Only an appropriate delegate can reject an application for a DAC.

The person may need to provide further security before extending the return date. Even if there was no initial requirement for security.

Security will be forfeited if,

  • the delegate does not approve the request,
  • and the debtor does not return by the due date

Discuss the reasons for the delayed return with the person. Depending on the reason the person provided, this may affect future DAC applications

Note: you cannot make changes to a current DAC, however the person can request a new DAC.

Does the person wish to request to have the return date on a DAC extended?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here

2

Investigate and verify the person's circumstances + Read more ...

The DPO team should:

  • review the DPO case and in particular why the DPO was originally put in place
  • review the reasons for travel
  • review the reasons why the person has requested to substitute the return date of the approved DAC
  • validate information provided by the person in their DAC application
  • consider if the request requires security

This helps to determine approval or refusal of a submission to substitute the DAC return date.

3

Prepare a DAC change of return date submission + Read more ...

Use the DAC change of return date template to prepare a submission to the delegate.

Include a National Manager (NM) and General Manager (GM) cover sheet (if required) with the completed submission. Email to the DPO Team Leader with the subject heading

'FOR DECISION: DAC change of return date Submission - Person's name - CRN'.

See Debt, Review and Appeals Division contacts for Debt Management Officers for contact details for the Debt DPO Team and the DPO Team positional mailbox.

4

DPO Team Leader actions + Read more ...

The Team Leader will:

  • review the DAC change of return date submission for accuracy
  • check the content ensuring all necessary considerations have been made in relation to the individual circumstances of the person
  • check the submission meets the policy and legislative conditions

If the team leader supports the submission, email the documents to the appropriate delegate for their consideration.

Delegate EL1 and above actions:

  • Issue a change of return date without security

Delegate (SES Band 2 and above only) actions

  • Issue a DAC change of return date with security. The delegate will also specify the amount of the security required
  • Refuse a DAC change of return date application

5

Delegate (EL1) actions + Read more ...

Based on the recommendation, the delegate considers and decides whether to approve or refuse the DAC vary return date.

If approved without security:

  • print, complete and sign the Delegate's Decision section of the submission
  • scan and email the Delegate Decision section of the submission to the Debt Management Departure Prohibition Order Team. For contact details, see Debt Programme and Compensation Recovery (DPCR) and Compliance Programme Branch contacts
  • go to Step 9

If refused or requires security:

  • update the submission with the security recommendation. Email to the Director for their endorsement, go to Step 6

6

Director actions + Read more ...

The Director will review the DAC change of return date submission.

  • If the Director supports the submission:
    • print and sign the Endorsement section of the submission
    • email the submission, scanned endorsement section and NM/GM cover sheets to the National Manager for review
    • go to Step 7
  • If the Director doesn’t support the submission requiring more information:
    • return the submission to the DPO team for further investigation and information
    • procedure ends here

7

National Manager actions + Read more ...

The National Manager will review the DAC change of return date submission.

  • If the NM supports the submission:
    • print and sign the Endorsement section of the submission
    • scan and email the signed submission to the delegated General Manager for their decision
    • go to Step 8
  • If the NM doesn’t support the submission:
    • return the submission to the DPO team for further investigation and information
    • procedure ends here

8

General Manager actions - substitute DAC return date decision + Read more ...

Based on the DPO Officer's recommendation, the delegate must:

  • consider the recommendation and decide whether to request security or not
  • make a decision to vary the DAC return date or not
  • print, and sign the Delegate's Decision section of the submission
  • scan and email the Delegate's Decision section of the submission to the Debt Management Departure Prohibition Order Team. For contact details, see Debt Programme and Compensation Recovery (DPCR) and Compliance Programme Branch contacts

Note: return the submission to the DPO team if it requires additional information. The DPO team will amend the submission and resubmit to the delegate.

9

Record DAC change of return date decision + Read more ...

Document the DAC change of return date decision once approved/rejected by the delegate. Include the date of decision and the delegate details.

Record the details in the Departure Prohibition Order System (DPOS):

  • navigate to the DPO List (OPDPL) screen
  • select the relevant DAC
  • complete the 'Vary Decision', 'Vary Decision Date', 'Vary Delegate AGS', and if approved 'Vary Return Date' fields

Record the DAC decision details and the reason in the DPO DOC.

Is security required to vary the return date of the DAC?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, see Step 5 in table 3

Security required to substitute DAC return date + Read more ...

Obtaining security, issuing a DAC and notification of decision

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Security payment + Read more ...

An SES Band 2 or above delegate will determine if a person is:

  • required to provide security
  • the amount required
  • the date the security is payable

The agency requires payment of the security before issuing the DAC.

Once Services Australia receives the security. Update the submission and submit to the EL1 for approval.

The SES Band 2 (or above) delegate may refuse the DAC if the person refuses to provide security.

Note:

  • Security arising from a loan is not acceptable
  • Where a person is able to give appropriate security, the preferred option is for the funds to be used to reduce the balance of the debt. This will usually justify revoking the DPO
  • The only method of security payment is via a direct deposit

Do not issue the DAC until:

  • the receipt has been provided confirming payment of the security, and
  • approval to issue the DAC has been received

2

Direct Deposit + Read more ...

The DPO team contact the person and advise:

  • the amount of security required
  • the date the security is to be paid by

Advise the person they must pay the security before the DAC can be issued.

The DPO team will provide the person the bank account details to deposit security.

Record the security information in the DPO DOC.

Payment of security

  • The person will need to deposit the funds into the specified bank account. Advise the DPO team when the funds have been deposited
  • The DPO team will email treasury(with a cc to Debt Management Collection Administration team). See Treasury Helpdesk (within the Financial Services team) for contact details. The email will advise:
    • a security payment has been requested
    • the amount
    • the person's Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • The Debt Management Collection Administration team monitor the bank account. They will email the DPO team when the security payment has been received

When the Debt Management Collection Administration team advises that payment has been received, go to Step 3.

3

Record receipt of security + Read more ...

The DPO team will update the DAC record in DPOS once advised of the receipt of security payment:

  • select the DAC from the DAC List (OPDAL) screen
  • change the Security status: field to 'Received'
  • enter the 'Security Amount'
  • enter the 'Security Paid Date'

4

Return submission to EL1 delegate for approval

The DPO team will update and email the submission to the EL1 delegate for the DAC to be issued.

EL1 delegate

The delegate will:

  • confirm the security has been received
  • email the DPO Team to advise the DAC 'with Security' notice can be issued. For contact details, see Debt Programme and Compensation Recovery (DPCR) and Compliance Programme Branch contacts

5

Issue a DAC notice and covering letter + Read more ...

The notice must be issued as soon as practicable after the decision to issue has been made.

To issue a manual letter and cover letter:

  • In the person's record, enter 'LD' in the 'Next field'
  • If approving the DAC:
    • Select Q858 and complete the required fields (only select any options that refer to a security if appropriate when completing this letter)
    • Issue DACs for all corresponding DPO notices under the different Acts
    • Complete the Q862 covering letter if more than 1 DAC is to be issued under different Acts. DACs can be sent together with a single covering letter
  • If rejecting the DAC:
    • Select Q859 and complete the required fields. Do not select any options that refer to a security when completing this letter
  • Print a draft copy of the DAC notice/s and provide to the Director to quality check
  • Update the DAC Notice/s if required and print a final copy
  • The notice and covering letter (if required) must be sent by registered mail

Record the notification details in the DPO system:

  • navigate to the OPDAL screen
  • select the DAC that the notice was sent for
  • enter the 'Customer Notification Date'

Note: if the person has a correspondence nominee, send a copy of the DPO notice to the nominee by registered mail. See Obligations of nominees.

6

Notify person of the DAC decision + Read more ...

Contact the person once the DAC notice has been issued.

If the person is registered for Online letters they can view their notice via Online Letters in myGov the day after the final print.

When contacting the person, ensure they are:

  • advised a DAC has been issued or not and what this means
  • aware of their review and appeal rights

Record the customer notification details in the DPO system:

  • navigate to the OPDAL screen and select the relevant DAC
  • enter the 'Customer Notify Date'

Note:

  • the normal review and appeal rights do not apply. See Review or appeal a Departure Prohibition Order (DPO) or Departure Authorisation Certificate (DAC) decision
  • the DPO team should make genuine attempts to call the person by phone to advise the DAC notice has been issued

Record the contact in a DOC.

Was the DAC approved?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, procedure ends here

7

Notify Australian Federal Police (AFP) + Read more ...

Upon approving the DAC, notify the AFP as soon as practicable via email.

If the customer is expected to travel within 24 hours, the request email must state the matter is urgent. The DPO team should phone the AFP to advise that an urgent request has been emailed.

Complete the Centrelink DAC email template, attach a copy of the DAC Notice/s and email to the AFP.

Record the information in a DOC.

Check the nationality of the person, see Immigration record of the person as an Australian citizen.

Record the AFP notification details in the DPO system:

  • navigate to the OPDAL screen and select the DAC
  • enter the 'AFP Notify Date'

Is the person an Australian citizen?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Notify the Department of Home Affairs + Read more ...

If the person is not an Australian citizen, send a copy of the DAC notice to the Department of Home Affairs by email.

Record the contact on the DPO DOC.

9

Record confirmation from Australian Federal Police (AFP) + Read more ...

The AFP will email the DPO team to confirm the DAC is registered.

Record the confirmation from the AFP on the DPO DOC.

Note: if confirmation is not received with 3 days of travel, the DPO team should contact the AFP to follow-up

Has the person returned to Australia following issuing of the DAC?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, procedure ends here until person's return to Australia

10

DAC Return date + Read more ...

The Department of Home Affairs will advise the AFP when the person returns to Australia.

AFP will email the DPO team with the same advice and to advise a new expiry date for their DPO.

Record the information from the AFP in the DPO System:

  • on the Departure Authorisation Certificate Details (OPDAD) screen enter the 'Actual Return' date
  • on the Departure Prohibition Order Details (OPDPD) screen enter the new 'Expiry date'

If there is more than 1 DAC they all need to be updated.

Update the DPO DOC with the information provided by the AFP.

The person's DPO remains in place. The AFP will stop them if they attempt to leave Australia without applying for a new DAC.

Refunding a security bond

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Determine if a security requires refunding + Read more ...

Action is needed when the person has returned to Australia or the 'Date of return' as specified in the DAC has been reached. The DPO team will determine if the security should be:

  • returned to the person. or
  • forfeited to consolidated revenue

2

Has the person returned to Australia by their 'due date' + Read more ...

AFP will email the DPO team to advise when the person has entered Australia. The DPO team will also monitor the person's return:

  • via Manual Follow-up (MFU) and/or
  • system checks
  • a Department of Home Affairs search if the return date has passed

Refund the security if:

  • the person has returned to Australia on or before the date specified in the DAC, or
  • the person did not depart Australia within the specified period

Has the person returned to Australia by the due date?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Person has not returned to Australia by due date + Read more ...

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the person to advise them that their security may be forfeited.

If phone contact is not successful, send the person a letter asking them to contact.

Discuss if there were any exceptional circumstances that prevented the person from returning to Australia by the required date. Determine the reason the person did not return to Australia or apply for a DAC substitute return date. For example, a natural disaster. Verify the reason.

Based on the discussion, submit a recommendation to the delegate (SES band 2) or above for a decision to:

  • return the security to the person, or
  • forfeit the security to consolidated revenue

Note: the recommendation is that the person forfeits their security if there is no contact within 14 days.

Enforce the delegate's decision. Decision options may include:

  • forfeit security
  • security refunded

Record discussion in DOC.

  • To forfeit the security, go to Step 5
  • To refund the security, go to Step 4

4

Security to be refunded + Read more ...

Refund the security into the person's nominated bank account using a Notice to prepare a Refund Account (SF030) form.

  • Prepare a refund request using the SF030. Complete the form online:
  • SF030 needs to be approved by DPO Team APS5 or above
  • The 'GL code' and 'Cost Centre' details need to be completed
  • Send the refund request to the Shared Services Accounts Payable Branch
  • Record details on a DOC, including:
    • the amount and date the refund was processed
    • any other applicable information, for example, refund via SF030
    • if the refund is via Direct Credit include the account details
  • Shared Services team will email the DPO team when the security has been processed

Contact the person to notify them the refund has been processed. The funds will be paid into their nominated bank account.

Record the security refund details in the Departure Prohibition Order System (DPOS):

  • select the appropriate DAC from the DAC List (OPDAL) screen
  • navigate to the Departure Authorisation Certificate Details (OPDAD) screen
  • enter the 'Security Return Date'

See the Resources page for a link to the SF030 form. Procedure ends here.

5

Security is forfeited + Read more ...

Where the security is to be forfeited the DPO team is sends an email to treasury. Include instructions to transfer the security payment to consolidated revenue. The email must include:

  • the person's name
  • CRN
  • the amount

Record the details of the security forfeit in the DPO system:

  • select the DAC from the OPDAL screen
  • enter the 'Security Forfeit date'
  • notify the person in writing (Q999) that the security has been forfeited

Update the DPO DOC and fully document the decision to pay the forfeited security payment to consolidated revenue. Include the information confirming the person failed to return to Australia by the return date specified in the DAC.

Expiring a DAC

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Expiring a DAC + Read more ...

Expire a DAC when a person:

  • does not leave Australia during the DAC period
  • a person returns to Australia by the 'DAC Return date'
  • a person does not return by the DAC return date

The Department of Home Affairs will advise the AFP of any inward activation (return) to Australia of a person.

AFP will email the DPO team advising the person has returned to Australia andthe new 'Expiry date' for the DPO.

  • If the person has returned to Australia, record the information from the AFP in the Departure Prohibition Order System (DPOS):
    • navigate to the DAC List (OPDAL) screen
    • select the DAC
    • enter 'Actual Return date'
    • once confirmed the DAC status on the OPDAL will display as 'Expired'
    • navigate to the DPO list (OPDPL) screen
    • select the DPO
    • enter the new 'APF Alert Expiry date'
  • If the person has not returned to or did not depart Australia:
    • navigate to the DAC list on the OPDAL screen
    • enter 'e' on the relevant DAC row
    • the status of the DAC on OPDAL will display as 'Expired'

Note: a Department of Home Affairs search for departure and arrival information, along with the AFP email.
