2

Customers not currently in receipt of payments or services + Read more ...

Updating relationship details for customers wishing to claim, but not currently receiving Centrelink payments or services is not required. If a new claim for income support or another payment/concession card is being lodged, updates will be done as part of the new claim.

Note: if the ex-partner is in receipt of payments or services, an online update of their relationship details or MOD S is required from the ex-partner whom is required to advise a change of circumstances within 14 days. A Request for Information can be sent from the ex-partner's record.

If the customer notifies of a separation via a MOD S, their record must still be updated. If the contact is by phone, the same procedures apply as if they were receiving payment or services. Advise the customer to lodge a MOD S. Verification of the separation is not required for customers not currently receiving Centrelink payments or services.

Does the customer intend to claim a government payment from Services Australia?

  • Yes,
    • check PPE authority and if the customer wishes to update their PPE to no ('N'), see Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) authority
    • direct them to lodge a claim online. Separation details will be captured where applicable as part of the new claim. If required, the separation will also be verified. See Claiming income support payments from Centrelink
    • record details on a DOC
  • No, and the customer has:
    • already lodged a MOD S, see Assessing the Separation details MOD S and online updates
    • not lodged a MOD S, go to Step 4

Note: a MOD S is not required for parents/guardians of dependent YA/ABY customers who have separated where the parents/guardians are not receiving an income support payment. See Changes which may affect the Parental Income Test (PIT) for further information.

3

Customer advises their relationship status will change in the future + Read more ...

If a customer advises their relationship status will change at a future date, do not code the change.

As the customer is separating from their partner, other changes related to the customer's privacy/security should be discussed to determine if immediate updates are required.

  • Advise the customer that if they believe their ex-partner knows their Self Service PIN or Password they should access the Self Service Channel to change these details as soon as possible
  • If the customer has their ex-partner appointed as their nominee, ask if they wish to cancel the nominee arrangement
  • If the customer has payments made to an account which their ex-partner has access to, or a joint account, it may be appropriate to change their payment destination also
  • Advise the customer the Person Permitted to Enquire authority will automatically cease once the separation has been coded. If the customer wishes to update their PPE to no ('N') immediately, see Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) authority
  • Direct the customer to advise of the separation through their Centrelink Online account via Menu > My Details > Personal and contact details>Update relationship details service. If the customer cannot use online services the customer can go to the Services Australia website to download the Separation Details (MOD S) online. If the customer indicates they cannot download the form, issue one via the Workspace > Customer Communication > Mail Forms workflow

Procedure ends here.

4

Check nominee arrangement and/or PPE authority + Read more ...

Check the customer's record to determine if nominee arrangement and/or PPE authority exists.

Nominee arrangement

If the customer has their ex-partner appointed as their nominee, ask if they wish to cancel the nominee arrangement. After the nominee arrangement has been cancelled, check if PPE authority exists and needs to be updated.

PPE authority

If PPE authority exists, Service Officers should also take the opportunity to establish if the customer's address and/or accommodation details need to be updated due to separation. This update should be done within the same activity as a temporary qualifier code is required to update the customer's address independently.

Is a change of address and/or accommodation details due to separation required?

  • Yes, if:
    • PPE authority exists, PPE authority should be updated within the same activity as the change of address. Go to Step 5
    • PPE authority does not exist. Go to Step 5
  • No, if PPE authority exists, advise the customer the Person Permitted to Enquire authority will automatically cease once the separation has been coded. If the customer wishes to update their PPE to no ('N') immediately, see Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) authority. Go to Step 6

5

Change of address and/or accommodation details due to separation is required - record a temporary qualifier code + Read more ...

If a customer separated from their partner and advises of a change of address and/or accommodation details, the customer remains linked until they provide separation details and their separation is verified. Separation details can be provided via the online Update relationship details service or MOD S.

Code, a Tmp Sep/Left Home qualifier on the MS screen to ensure address and/or accommodation details for the customer can be updated independently.

To update the customer's address and/or accommodation details, navigate to the MS screen.

Key 'MS' in Next: field to access new screen for updating:

  • Event Date: field, enter the date the customer separated
  • Marital Status: field, restate their previous partnered marital status
  • PPE Cust and PPE Ptnr: fields, update to N
  • Qualifier Code: field, select Tmp Sep/Left home
  • Source: field, select relevant source of information
  • Receipt Date: field, date the information was received. See Date of receipt
  • Select Continue to go to the Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen
  • Go to Step 6

6

Update any other change of details for the customer if required + Read more ...

If the customer advises other circumstances have changed as a result of separating, they must be updated before updating their relationship status and unlinking the records.

Do not update the customer's relationship status at this time as this will occur after separation has been verified.

Update any other changes, including Office Code (OC) screen, by selecting the appropriate screens from the CDTS screen.

If the customer:

  • has had a change in address and/or accommodation details:
    • on the Address Details (AD) screen, update the address, ensuring the correct start date is recorded. The start date is the date the customer advises they have moved
    • record 'No' in the Make partner's address the same as the customer's: field to ensure the address update is not applied to the ex-partner's record
    • if the customer is separated but living under the same roof as their ex-partner, ask the customer if they wish to provide a different postal address
    • on the Accommodation History (ACS) screen, select the 'provisional activity' via the Event Date that has defaulted from the date entered on the MS screen when the Tmp Sep/Left home qualifier code was recorded. Note: where the separation date is different to the date the customer moved address, an AC update for both the separation Event Date and address Event Date will be required
    • ensure Accommodation Details for field is Customer
    • the code of Separated due to ill health will display in the Rent Verification: field. This will need to be updated or Rent Assistance (if applicable) will stop. See Determining proof of rent for the Verification field on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen
    • review and update all accommodation details as required. For more information, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA)
  • is caring for a dependent child, see Principal carer of a dependent child:
    • for change of care, see Change of Care for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • if the customer is also receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB), see Maintenance Action Test (MAT) for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customers
    • if the customer has a child attending approved child care and is not CCS current, see Eligibility for Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • has payments made to an account which their ex-partner has access to, or a joint account, it may be appropriate to also change their payment destination
  • has changed their email address, see Adding, changing or removing an email address
  • believes their ex-partner knows their Self Service PIN or Password they should access the Self Service Channel to change these details as soon as possible
  • go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and check results (that is, debts/arrears) prior to finalising the activity
  • check if the customer's entitlement is affected by the move, for example, a job seeker with mutual obligation requirements moves to an area of lower employment. See Moving to an Area of Lower Employment Prospects (MALEP) employment related exclusion period
  • record details on a DOC

Customer's relationship status has changed from separated to divorced + Read more ...

If a customer has been separated for 12 months or longer and they advise they are now divorced, a request for verification of divorce is not required unless there is doubt about the customer's relationship status.

The date of event is the date the divorce became absolute. This is generally 1 month after the decree nisi, but may vary by court order. This will be noted on the decree.

  • To update the customer's Marital Status (MS) screen from separated to divorced, key 'MS' in Next: field, to access new screen for updating:

11

Carer Allowance (CA) only customer advises of separation + Read more ...

For customers in receipt of only CA and neither they or their ex-partner receive an income support payment, it is not necessary to obtain a MOD S and/or written documentation to verify that a separation has occurred.

The following information on the customer's record can be updated when they advise of separation verbally or from documentation provided, for example, via online claim:

  • update the MS screen to reflect the change in relationship
  • update any other change in circumstances (including new income details) as advised by the customer (for example, change of address)

Where the customer advises they live under the same roof as their ex-partner, an SS293 - Relationship Details - Separated under one roof form must be requested:

  • issue the appropriate form and letter
  • code a manual review, allow extra time for mail delivery
  • DOC the record with action taken. Include in the DOC the date the customer indicated they have separated from their ex-partner.
    Note: it is important to record this date as a MOD S will not be required and the SS293 form/s does not capture the date of separation

Action

3

Unlink records and update any other change of details for the customer if required + Read more ...

To protect vulnerable customers, those considered to be at risk of family and domestic violence may have their relationship status updated immediately. When speaking to a customer about their separation, tell them that if their partner is in receipt of a payment and/or has an online account, their partner's marital status will automatically update to a non-partnered (for example, separated) relationship status. Their partner will be able to view the status in their Centrelink online account and Express Plus Centrelink App and changes to their rate of payment. Giving the customer this information allows them to make an informed decision of the risk and make any necessary safety preparations.

As information will be taken verbally, the MOD S and online update will not be required. Verification of separation after the records have been unlinked may need to be followed up if there is insufficient written documentation to verify that a bona fide separation has occurred on the customer's record.

Note: The process of 'unlinking' records prior to obtaining verification of separation is only required for current customers, who are linked to their partner.

To avoid further risk to the customer

When updating the relationship and completing the 'unlinking' activity on the Marital Status (MS) screen:

  • care must be taken when updating the customer's address
  • ensure updates do not flow onto their ex-partner's record

Seek technical support during this process as required.

  • Go to the MS screen
  • Key 'MS' in the Next: field to access a new screen for updating
    • Event Date: field - record the date of separation
    • Marital Status: field - select separated marital status
    • PPE Cust and PPE Ptnr: field - update to 'N'
    • Qualifier Code: field - leave blank
    • Source: field - select relevant source of information
    • Receipt Date: field - date the information was received, see Date of receipt
  • Select Continue, the Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen is displayed
  • 'B' (both) must be changed to 'S' (customer only) in the following fields to ensure updates do not flow to the ex-partner's record:
    • address
    • accommodation
    • telephone

To update any other changes, including Office Code (OC) screen:

  • select the appropriate screens from the CDTS screen
    Some other screen names on the CDTS screen may default to 'B' for updates for both customer and partner. This must be changed to 'S' to ensure only the customer's record will be updated

If the customer:

  • has had a change in address /accommodation details:
    • on the Address Details (AD) screen update the address
    • record 'No' in the Make partner's address the same as the customer's: field to ensure the address update is not applied to the partner record
    • if the customer is separated but living under the same roof as their ex-partner, ask the customer if they wish to provide a different mailing (postal) address. Note: as the customer is SUOR, a Relationship Details form – separated under one roof (SS293) should be triggered from the AC update; if not, manually issue one. The SUOR assessment must still be conducted on return of the SS293
    • on the Accommodation History (ACS) screen, select the 'provisional activity' via the Event Date that has defaulted from the date entered on the MS screen
    • ensure Accommodation Details for field is Customer
    • the code of Unlink of two customers - ULK will display in the Rent Verification: field. This will need to be updated or Rent Assistance (if applicable) will stop. See Determining proof of rent for the Verification field on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen
    • review and update all accommodation details as required. For more information, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA)
  • has not had a change in address /accommodation details:
    • even where there have been no changes to the address/accommodation details, an update to the Accommodation Details (AC) screen will still be required for customers in receipt of payments that attract Rent Assistance
    • on the Accommodation History (ACS) screen, select the 'provisional activity' via the Event Date that has defaulted from the date entered on the MS screen
    • ensure Accommodation Details for: field is Customer
    • update the Rent Verification: field from Unlink of two customers - ULK to either the same code that existed prior to ULK (for example, VEF/VEC/VED) or the most relevant code for the customer's current circumstances. If the previous code was Verified electronically - VEE, update this to Verified (Rent Declaration) - VED. For more details, see Determining proof of rent for the Verification field on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen
  • is caring for a dependent child, see Principal carer of a dependent child:
    • for a change of care, see Change of care for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • if the customer is also receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB), see Maintenance Action Test (MAT) for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customers
    • if the customer has a child attending approved child care and is not CCS current, see Eligibility for Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • has had a change in income and/or assets as a result of the separation, see Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services
  • has payments made to an account which their ex-partner has access to, or a joint account, it may be appropriate to change their payment destination
  • has changed their email address, see Adding, changing or removing an email address
  • believes their ex-partner knows their Self Service PIN or Password, advise them they should access the Self Service Channel to change these details as soon as possible
  • go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and check results (that is, debts/arrears) before finalising the activity
  • check if the customer's entitlement is affected by the move, for example, a job seeker with mutual obligation requirements moves to an area of lower employment, see Moving to an Area of Lower Employment Prospects (MALEP) employment related exclusion period
  • record all details on a DOC

13
