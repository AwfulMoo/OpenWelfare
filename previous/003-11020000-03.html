Referrals to Financial Wellbeing and Capability Activities

Item

Description

1

Financial counselling + Read more ...

Financial counselling helps people with complex personal financial issues such as debt collection, fines and related legal issues, and is tailored to the needs of the customer's financial circumstances.

The customer should be advised that the information they provide to, and discuss with, the financial counsellor is private and totally separate from their business with Services Australia.

A referral must be offered when:

  • customer asks for assistance with financial issues or seeks referral for assistance
  • any situation where help may benefit the customer
  • repeated requests for an urgent payment have been made
  • repeated requests for an advance payment of social security or Family Tax Benefit (FTB) have been made
  • the customer has had four or more FTB advance payments granted or rejected within the previous 6 month period
  • a request for reduced or suspended repayment of existing advance/s is made
  • FTB advance application is rejected based on non-affordability or hardship (rejection reasons: 'HAR', 'LMA' 'RSR' and 'ROA')

Note: if a customer applies using self service channels for a Family Tax Benefit (FTB) advance payment, they will be provided with details about how they can get information on Financial Management Support Services. Both the online workflow and IVR will provide a link to the Services Australia webpage with the IVR also offering a transfer to speak with a Service Officer.

Referral to financial counselling

Offer to warm transfer the customer to the National Debt Helpline for financial counselling. Using Interaction Workspace (iWs) Softphone:

  • select the Instant call transfer button, and then
  • Nat Debt Helpline [state]

For example, Nat Debt Helpline NSW - choose the referral queue for the state or territory the customer lives in.

Alternatively, customer can call National Debt Helpline on 1800 007 007 (open 9:30am – 4:30pm Monday to Friday).

It is important to know that although called the National Debt Helpline, financial counsellors will assist with a variety of issues, not only those related to debt.

Customers can also find a financial counsellor in their local area using the National Debt Helpline’s website, ‘Find a financial counsellor’.

This can be a good option for finding face-to-face financial counsellors, or people who specialise in particular cultural backgrounds. For example, the Indigenous Consumer Assistance Network supports Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people in far north Queensland and the Torres Strait.

Record the referral in a DOC.

MoneySmart + Read more ...

5

No Interest Loans Scheme (NILS) + Read more ...

NILS is a loans option provided by community organisations to low income earners who are holders of Pensioner Concession Cards (PCCs) or Health Care Cards (HCCs). The loans are to assist people to purchase essential goods and services such as household items like fridges and washing machines and medical procedures.

Loans are not considered for food, rent, bond, electricity bills, phone bills, debt consolidation, car repairs, or other recurrent expenses. These loans are an alternative form of credit provision and should not be confused with emergency relief.

For contact details, see No Interest Loans Scheme (NILS).

The Resources page has a link to the NILS website.

Record the referral in a DOC.

6

Saver Plus Program + Read more ...

The Saver Plus Program assists families on low incomes to develop a savings habit, build assets and improve financial capability. Participants set a savings goal and receive support and education to help them achieve it. When they reach their goal, ANZ back matches their savings, dollar for dollar, up to $500.

Saver Plus is available from 60 locations across Australia.

Customers may be eligible to join Saver Plus if they or their partner:

  • have a Health Care Card (HCC) or Pensioner Concession Card (PCC)
  • are at least 18 years old
  • have some regular income from work, and
  • are a parent or guardian of a child at school or are attending or returning to vocational education

For Saver Plus Program contact numbers see Saver Plus.

The Resources page has a link to the Saver Plus Program website.

Record the referral in a DOC.
