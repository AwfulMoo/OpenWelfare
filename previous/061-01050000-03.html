Check if customer or child unable to return to Australia + Read more ...

Consider if the customer or child is unable to return to Australia because of a reason specified under section 1218C of the Social Security Act 1991, section 54 of the Farm Household Support Act 2014 or sections 24 (7) and 85EE (3) of the Family Assistance Act or the ABSTUDY Policy Manual:

  • A serious accident involving the person or a family member of the person

  • The person's involvement in custody proceedings in the country in which the person is located
  • A legal requirement for the person to remain outside Australia in connection with criminal proceedings (other than criminal proceedings in respect of a crime alleged to have been committed by the person)
  • Robbery or serious crime committed against the person or a family member of the person
  • A natural disaster in the country in which the person is located
  • Political or social unrest in the country in which the person is located
  • Industrial action in the country in which the person is located
  • A war in the country in which the person is located

8

Request evidence + Read more ...

In order for assessment to be made, the customer must provide evidence to support their request for a portability extension. Examples of acceptable verification include (but are not limited to):

  • Written confirmation from the treating doctor verifying:
    • hospitalisation of the customer (or the customer's family member) or a serious medical condition that prevented the patient returning to Australia. Note: a condition that simply requires treatment and does not prevent travel is not enough to justify an extension of portability
    • the date the event occurred to prove that it was unexpected and occurred before the end of the portability period, and
    • the date that it is expected the customer/family member will be able to travel
  • A death certificate for a family member of the customer
  • Legal documents verifying:
    • custody proceedings
    • a requirement for the customer to attend criminal proceedings as a witness
    • that a serious crime has occurred against the customer or a family member

In the case of a customer or child receiving financial assistance under the Medical Treatment Overseas Program (MTOP), either because they are receiving treatment or are accompanying such a person, documentary evidence is required from the Department of Health and Aged Care. Evidence will give details of who has been approved for financial assistance and the period for which they are travelling.

In the case of a severely disabled Disability Support Pension (DSP) customer who is substantially dependent on a family member who has been posted outside Australia temporarily for work, documentary evidence is required confirming the family member is continuing to work for the same employer outside Australia and the period of the posting.

Customers who are a member of the Defence Forces being deployed overseas will need to provide verifying documents from their commanding officer including the expected date of return to Australia.

Customers who are an Australian Federal Police officer travelling overseas for peacekeeping or capacity building purposes will need to provide documents from the Australian Federal Police confirming their travel overseas including the reason for the travel and the expected date of return to Australia.

It may be necessary for the Service Officer to directly contact the doctor or the hospital involved (in a medical case).

However, with a natural disaster in the country, such documentation may not be required, as it may have been reported in the Australian media or on the internet.

In some cases, evidence may be provided by officers in overseas consulates who are assisting customers.

  • No, advise the customer to obtain relevant evidence. Advise them that if they are registered for online services they can use Upload documents to provide the evidence. 2

Determining an end date + Read more ...

An end date is required for the discretionary portability extension. For example, in the case of illness the delegate needs to know when the doctor expects the patient to be physically able to return to Australia, or if that is not known, when the case will be reviewed. The delegate would use the date the doctor is intending to review the case as an end-date.

The end date must be coded by the delegate. A review is therefore unnecessary as, if the customer or child has not returned to Australia by this date, the customer's payment will automatically suspend or cancel in the case of Family Tax Benefit (FTB). Child Care Subsidy (CCS) will decrease to a zero rate.

In the case of an Age Pension or Disability Support Pension (DSP) recipient who has indefinite portability, their pension will proportionalise from the portability extension end-date based on their Australian Working Life Residence (WLR) and/or will lose entitlement to other add-on payments.

3

Granting a discretionary portability extension + Read more ...

This decision may only be made by a Service Officer with appropriate delegation in CIS.

The delegate must use the Portability Extension Processing script where possible to record the decision. Search for Portability Extension Processing in the Script Selector in Customer Record.

If manually recording the decision DOC due to a script problem or for payments that are not included in the script (CCS, FHA, DOP, ABSTUDY), it must contain a summary line recommending a decision to grant the extension.

Note: where customers receive Family Tax Benefit (FTB), Child Care Subsidy (CCS), Parental Leave Pay (PPL) or Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) in addition to their primary benefit, there should be a separate DOC for that benefit as it is covered under different legislation.

The DOC must be detailed and cover:

  • the customer's payment type
  • why the customer has a limited portability period, for example, DSP not severely disabled
  • the customer or child's date of departure and the portability period initially allowed for the customer or child, including end date
  • why the customer or child is unable to return to Australia prior to the end of the portability period and the relevant legislation which covers the event
  • detailed information of the verification received. For example, authority who provided it, date of documentation, contents which verify the reason for extension
  • the date of the event which prevented the customer or child's return to Australia, and where required under the legislation, whether the event occurred during the person's limited portability period
  • the length of extension recommended, including the start and end dates of the extension period and why the customer or child's circumstances meet the criteria in the legislation

Rejecting the portability extension request + Read more ...

This decision may only be made by a Service Office with appropriate delegation in CIS.

Record a rejection DOC on the customer's record using the Portability Extension Processing Script in Customer Record if:

  • there is no listing of the situation as an acceptable extension reason according to section:
    • 1218AB, 1218C or 1218D of the Social Security Act, or
    • 1220A (1B) or 1220B (1B) of the Social Security Act, or
    • 1216 (2) of the Social Security Act, or
    • 24 or 85EE of the Family Assistance Act, or
    • 54 of the Farm Household Support Act, or
    • 46 of the Paid Parental Leave Act
  • the situation cannot be proven, or
  • the event did not occur in the limited portability period, or
  • the event did not occur or begin during the initial period of 26 weeks of a temporary absence where the Age Pension or Disability Support Pension (DSP) customer is portable indefinitely

If manually recording the DOC due to a script problem, or for payments that are not included in the script (such as CCS, FHA, DOP, or ABSTUDY), it must contain a summary line recommending a decision to reject the extension.

Note: where the customer receives multiple payments, ensure the corresponding Act references are included in the DOC.

The DOC must be detailed and cover:

  • the customer's payment type
  • whether the customer does have a limited portability period, for example, DSP not severely disabled
  • the customer's or child's date of departure and the portability period initially allowed for the customer or child, including the end date
  • why the customer has advised they are unable to return to Australia prior to the end of the portability period or within 26 weeks for a customer on a payment with indefinite portability, and whether it is an acceptable reason as per the relevant legislation
  • whether the customer will go proportional based on their AWLR after 26 weeks
  • whether the customer will no longer be eligible to retain add-on payments as a result of losing their main payment, or where their payment proportionalised after 26 weeks
  • whether acceptable verification was received and if not, details of the evidence provided and why it was not suitable
  • where required under the legislation, the date of the event preventing the customer or child's return to Australia and whether it occurred during the person's limited portability period, and
  • why the customer or child's situation fails to meet the criteria in the legislation

9

Check the New Zealand Age customer has the correct Long Term Country Code recorded + Read more ...

Check the RSS screen to ensure that the correct Long Term Country Code is currently recorded for the customer. A customer will ordinarily be paid using the same rate calculation method after leaving Australia or New Zealand temporarily, for up to 26 weeks. After this time, the Long Term Country Code is automatically updated by the system, changing the rate calculation method used to determine the customer’s rate.

If an extension is granted, this would enable the customer to continue to be paid the same rate calculation method for a period beyond 26 weeks, to an agreed extension end date.

If the Long Term Country Code has already been changed/corrected prior to the extension being granted, the system will not automatically change it back to the correct Long Term Country Code. As such, this will need to be manually updated by the user after coding the extension and prior to finalising the activity.

In addition, as the PGR/AAG review to change the Long Term Country Code will already have run, the system will not automatically change this at the end of the extension

  • No, go to Step 11

Manually update the Long Term Country Code and insert review for the extension end date + Read more ...

The Long Term Country Code will need to be updated on RSS to ensure the customer receives the benefit of the portability extension. This may be as simple as deleting the most recent entry created by the PGR/AAP review, however care should be taken to ensure this is correct.

As the customer’s payment may have also SUS-PPR (Proportional on Zero Months) at 26 weeks, the payment may need to be restored. If this is the case, the user will need to code the restoration on the BA screen from the initial suspension date.

In addition, as the PGR/AAG review that would review the Long Term Country Code at the end of the portability period has already matured, a manual review is required to prompt a check of the customer’s location. The review should be set up as follows:

  • RVR screen
    ServRsn: AGE
  • RevRsn: OSR
    Due Date: extension end date
    Keyword: NZEXT
    Text: Customer's portability extension expires. Check if customer has returned to their country of residence. If not, update the Long Term Country Code on RSS
