2

Requirement and timeframes for Job Plan + Read more ...

All job seekers with mutual obligation requirements must negotiate and agree to a Job Plan within:

  • 4 business days of their first regular payment, or
  • within 14 days of a change of circumstance

If incomplete within 28 calendar days, the job seeker's income support payments will be suspended.

A job seeker with an exemption from their mutual obligation requirements cannot negotiate a compulsory Job Plan.

Does the job seeker have mutual obligation requirements?

  • Yes:
    • and their payments are suspended as they have not replied to correspondence about a Job Plan, go to Step 8
    • for all other job seekers, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 10

3

Under 18 and at risk + Read more ...

Is the job seeker under 18 years and do they have 'At Risk' issues?

  • Yes, see Eligibility and registration for employment assistance as a Vulnerable Youth (VY) or Vulnerable Youth (Student) (VY(S)). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Remote job seekers + Read more ...

Is the job seeker a remote job seeker?

  • Yes, and the job seeker is:
    • in a Community Development Program (CDP) region and is provider managed, the CDP provider is responsible for negotiating the Job Plan. Procedure ends here
    • in a CDP region and is Centrelink managed. Centrelink is responsible for negotiating the Job Plan. Arrange a phone interview to discuss their Job Plan. See Step 1 in Table 3
    • managed by Centrelink and does not live in a CDP region. Arrange a phone interview to negotiate a Job Plan. See Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Waiting period or Income Maintenance Period (IMP) + Read more ...

Does the job seeker have a waiting period or IMP of more than 14 days from the date of interview?

  • Yes:
    • The job seeker does not need to agree to a Job Plan immediately. The job seeker can agree to a Job Plan now or at the end of the waiting period
    • An action item will generate for Centrelink managed job seekers at the end of their waiting period or IMP. Service Officers must negotiate a Job Plan with the job seeker at their next contact
    • If the job seeker does not negotiate and agree to a Job Plan within 28 calendar days after starting on an income support payment, their payment may be suspended. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Break in income support payment + Read more ...

Has the job seeker had a break in their income support payment?

  • Yes:
    • If there is a Job Plan from before a break in payment, it remains current until another Job Plan is created
    • When the job seeker resumes payment, negotiate a new Job Plan based on their current circumstances. Go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Determining who is responsible for negotiating the Job Plan + Read more ...

The Participation Summary, Key Information and Job Plan/Summary pages in Process Direct show if the job seeker is:

  • provider managed, or
  • Centrelink managed

Centrelink managed

Service Officers need to negotiate a Job Plan with Centrelink managed job seekers, see Step 2 in Table 3.

Provider managed and not participating in an approved requirement that fully meets their mutual obligation requirements

The provider or Online Services will negotiate the Job Plan. For new claim job seekers this will usually occur at the initial interview with their provider or Online Services. Refer the job seeker back to their provider or Online Services. Procedure ends here.

Provider managed or in Online Services and doing an approved requirement which will fully meet their mutual obligation requirements

Job seekers who are provider managed and are fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements by doing approved activities, will become Centrelink managed.

Note: for Online Services job seekers the Job Plan will only be presented to them once they complete the Career Profile, unless exempt from RapidConnect.

Is the job seeker eligible to become Centrelink managed, and able to provide all the information and verification needed?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, tell the job seeker they must provide verification of the approved requirement. Do not update the AEX screen or negotiate a Job Plan

8

If a Job Plan is not negotiated + Read more ...

A job seeker's income support payment will be suspended Failure to Respond to Correspondence (FRC) if they do not have a Job Plan:

  • negotiated within 4 business days of the date their first regular income support payment, or
  • within 28 days of becoming subject to mutual obligation requirements (for Parenting Payment Single (PPS) job seekers), or
  • within 14 days of a change in circumstances, or
  • agreed within 4 days of negotiating and submitting their Job Plan in their online account

If a job seeker contacts about an outstanding Job Plan, check if the job seeker is Centrelink or provider managed, and if a new Job Plan is pending negotiation on the Job Plan Status page. Go to Step 7 to determine who is responsible for negotiating a Job Plan.

Are they a provider managed job seeker?

  • Yes, the provider will negotiate the Job Plan. Make sure the referral of the job seeker to a provider and an appointment has been booked within 2 working days, before restoring job seeker payment. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Centrelink managed and payment suspended FRC + Read more ...

Tell the job seeker that their failure to agree to the Job Plan has resulted in suspension of their payment. Agreeing to the Job Plan will allow restoration of their payment.

Job seekers can restore their payment by completing the outstanding 'agree to Job Plan' task in their online account.

Service Centre Service Officers, see Step 1 in Table 3

Smart Centre Service Officers who are suitably skilled may negotiate a Job Plan with the job seeker by phone, see Step 1 in Table 3

Otherwise, the Smart Centre Service Officer must:

  • check Office Locator and determine if it is appropriate to refer the job seeker as a walk-in or book a scheduled appointment
  • where appropriate, book a Compulsory Participation appointment, and
  • if appointment booked, tell the job seeker of the time, date, and purpose of the appointment
  • tell the job seeker they must notify the agency if their contact number changes so they can be contacted
  • tell the job seeker they must negotiate and agree to a Job Plan before their income support payments can be restored

Record a DOC confirming the advice given, and any other relevant information disclosed by the job seeker.

If, after 13 weeks of suspension, a jobseeker still does not have a current Job Plan their payment will auto-cancel.

Procedure ends here.

10

Voluntary Job Plan + Read more ...

The job seeker may volunteer to participate

Job seekers who do not have, or are exempt from mutual obligation requirements, may volunteer for Employment Provider Services. Procedure ends here.

Job seeker is applying for Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work)

The job seeker must be doing an Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work) approved requirement that is included in a Job Plan. A Job Plan must be negotiated to include an ACCS (Transition to Work) approved requirement as a voluntary requirement. See Step 1 in Table 3.

Note: the job seeker may volunteer to connect with an Employment Service who will negotiate a Job Plan. Procedure ends here.

Activity and Exemption Summary + Read more ...

Before negotiating a Centrelink managed job plan, check the Activity and Exemption (AEX) screen reflects the job seeker participation activity.

In the jobseeker's record, key 'AEX' in the Super Key, select [Enter]

The Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) will display

If the details recorded on the AEX screen do not reflect the job seekers current circumstances:

  • update the approved activity, and
  • the relevant start and end dates up to a maximum of 12 months (whichever is the lesser)

An Action Item may display on the Participation Summary screen advising that the AEX screen has been updated and/or a Job Plan needs to be negotiated to reflect the job seekers current activity.

After coding the Activity and Exemption (AEX) screen, do not attempt to negotiate a Job Plan until the job seekers record changes to Centrelink managed. The exception to this is job seekers participating in a Disability Employment Services (DES) program and doing paid work. See Paid work as an approved activity.

3

Negotiate the Job Plan + Read more ...

Voluntary Job Plans

Voluntary Job Plans may also be negotiated for Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work) purposes. The steps below for negotiating compulsory Job Plans for job seekers with mutual obligation requirements are only relevant for voluntary Job Plan purposes in as much as they describe the Job Plan workflow and finalisation process. This includes the option for the job seeker to finalise the Job Plan via their online account (not including the Centrelink app).

For job seekers seeking a voluntary Job Plan, go to Step 4.

Compulsory Job Plans

Explain the purpose of the interview, and tell the job seeker about the requirement to enter into a Job Plan, their rights and obligations in relation to the terms of the Job Plan, and information about compliance:

  • Restate the job seeker's mutual obligation requirements
  • In accordance with the job seeker's participation category, discuss with the job seeker the requirement that is included in the plan as well as their options for overcoming any employment barriers. Make any relevant referrals to appropriate programs or activities
  • If the job seeker needs to negotiate a Job Plan as part of a new claim but the new claim cannot be finalised, the Service Officer may choose to negotiate a pre grant Job Plan if they are confident the job seeker will be Centrelink managed and they are confident which participation category the job seeker will fall into

Note: if a Service Officer prints the Job Plan for the job seeker, the system will check if there is a preferred written language other than English recorded on the Other Contact Details (OCD) page. It will check the language recorded against available translations of the 'Information you need to know' and if appropriate, a link displays to the Service Officer allowing them to print a translated copy of the 'Information you need to know' part of the Job Plan. The English version of this information will continue to form part of the Job Plan.

Go to Step 4.

4

Begin a Job Plan or complete a started Job Plan + Read more ...

Service Officers can not negotiate a Job Plan for Provider Managed job seekers. The organisation responsible for the ongoing management of the job seeker is to negotiate the Job Plan.

Update the AEX screen with the current approved activity before starting the negotiation of a Job Plan. The Job Plan Status page will show a message to remind Service Officers of this requirement.

To begin or continue a started Job Plan:

Process Direct

For job seekers who submit an online claim or a Service Officer has completed the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) workflow:

  • once in the claim, select > Participation Interview > Job Plan > Negotiate Job Plan

For job seekers currently receiving a payment (including volunteer job seekers claiming ACCS):

  • select Customer Summary from the Process Direct landing page
  • key the job seeker's CRN, select Go
  • in the job seeker's record select > Participation Summary > Job Plan > Negotiate Job Plan

The Overview page will display.

Complete the following questions:

  • Do you wish to create/update Job Plan?
  • Do you wish to clean slate the customer's Job Plan?

Use the Next button to go through the following Job Plan screens:

  • The Participation Activities page will display. Jobseekers with compulsory participation requirements must have at least one compulsory activity included in the Job Plan. The compulsory activity will be automatically generated from the coding already completed on the AEX screen
  • The Summary page will display to confirm the activities included in the job seekers pending Job Plan
  • The Finalise page enables the Service Officer to:
    • indicate if an interpreter was used when negotiating the Job Plan
    • print a PDF copy of the Job Plan for the job seeker to sign if the Job Plan is negotiated in person or they are from a remote area
    • specify the job seeker has an online account and wishes to agree to the Job Plan online;
    • indicate if a job seeker has signed their Job Plan, if the job seeker has declined to agree to their Job Plan online
    • finalise the Job Plan

Once finalised, the details of the Job Plan will be sent to the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations.

Note: some Centrelink Managed job seekers signed up for Online Services who are fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements through part-time work can have their Job Plan updated automatically when the requirement end date is reached by completing an Online Task. These job seekers can use the 'View and Agree Job Plan' online service to approve their Job Plan.

Where a Centrelink managed job seeker attempts to update their approved requirement and/or Job Plan online and the requirement fails to update, the system will generate a 'Change of Circumstances' work item.

If allocated, investigate the reason automation has failed. Once resolved, from Customer First, complete the COC activity:

  • select the 'Online MOR & Job Plan or AEX Review' COC Interaction from the Interaction History
  • change the status from 'For manual action' to 'Completed'

If the job seeker has an online account, they can agree to the Job Plan via their online account. The Job Plan will be available in their online account immediately, and they will have 4 business days to agree to the Job Plan.

If the job seeker wants to finalise their Job Plan:

  • in person, go to Step 5
  • using their online account, go to Step 8

5

Job Plan is finalised in person + Read more ...

Issue the printed Job Plan to the job seeker and explain their rights and obligations:

  • They can request a variation or negotiation of their current Job Plan at any time
  • They can apply for a formal review of any decision in relation to the Job Plan
  • They must notify the agency of changes in their circumstances
  • Job seekers with mutual obligation requirements must comply with the terms of the Job Plan
  • Job seekers who are participating voluntarily will not be subject to compliance action
  • Answer any questions the job seeker may have
  • Ensure the job seeker signs the Job Plan

Note: for remote job seekers where the Job Plan was negotiated over the phone, print 2 copies of the Job Plan. These should be sent with a Request for Information (RFI) letter requesting one copy be signed and returned by the job seeker. If the job seeker fails to return the Job Plan, see Refusing to enter into a Job Plan or Participation Plan. Procedure ends here.

Has the job seeker signed the Job Plan?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, and the job seeker:
    • is refusing to enter an agreement, go to Step 6
    • has expressed concerns and would like to think further before signing the Job Plan, go to Step 10

6

Job seeker refuses to enter into compulsory Job Plan + Read more ...

If the job seeker continues to refuse to enter into a Job Plan, despite being advised that it is a compulsory requirement to receive a participation payment, the job seeker must be told:

  • refusing to enter into a Job Plan means they are not qualified for payment, and
  • cancellation of payment will occur because of their refusal to enter into a Job Plan

If the job seeker:

  • subsequently advises they are willing to enter into the Job Plan, go to Step 7
  • subsequently expresses concerns and would like to think further before signing the Job Plan, go to Step 10
  • still refuses to agree to the Job Plan, cancel the payment FSA 'Failed to Sign Activity Agreement (Job Plan) and record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

7

Job Plan is signed by job seeker in person + Read more ...

Sign the Job Plan as the delegated Service Officer and complete the following:

  • Give (fax/post for remote job seekers) the signed Job Plan to the job seeker
  • Return to the Job Plan workflow in Process Direct to finalise the Job Plan. Select Yes to Has the job seeker signed the Job Plan?
  • Key the date signed in the required field (for remote job seekers, the date signed will be the date the Job Plan was generated)
  • A copy of the Job Plan is not to be scanned to customer record
  • Select Finish

Procedure ends here.

8

Job seeker agrees, disagrees or refuses to agree to a Job Plan in their online account + Read more ...

The Job Plan is available to finalise in the online account immediately (this cannot be done in the Centrelink app). The job seeker does not have to wait to receive notification. Job Plans negotiated over the phone should be finalised online while the job seeker is still on the phone.

In their online account, the job seeker has the option to:

  • agree to their Job Plan
  • disagree with their Job Plan, for example if there is an error
  • refuse to agree to the Job Plan
  • do nothing

Job seeker agrees

Agreement with the Job Plan online is the same as signing the Job Plan. It becomes current and the job seeker must do the requirements in the Job Plan. Go to Step 9.

Job seeker disagrees

If the job seeker disagrees with their Job Plan, a WAP/MFU activity will be created along with an associated action item on the Participation Summary > Key Information page. This is to alert a Service Officer to contact the job seeker to re-negotiate their Job Plan. The WAP/MFU cannot be cancelled.

A work item 'Online-MOR & Job Plan or AEX Review' will also be created and allocated with notes in the interaction history for Service Officers to contact job seeker and discuss circumstances.

Job seeker refuses to agree in their online account

If the job seeker refuses to agree to the Job Plan in their online account, payment will either reject or cancel. The WAP/PLN activity will remain on the job seeker's record.

A work item 'Online-MOR & Job Plan or AEX Review' will also be created and allocated with notes in the interaction history for Service Officers to contact job seeker and discuss circumstances.

Job seeker does nothing

If the job seeker has an online account and wishes to agree to the Job Plan via their online account, they must agree to their Job Plan within 4 business days of submission of the Job Plan to the online account. If they have not agreed and there is no plan on the job seeker's record, the No Job Plan process will apply.
Note: the No Job Plan process does not apply to voluntary job seekers or if they do not have compulsory mutual obligation requirements.

If the job seeker:

  • after 2 days, has not agreed to their Job Plan in their online account, a reminder will be sent to the job seeker if they are registered for Electronic Messaging
  • after 4 days, does not agree to their Job Plan, it will trigger the Job Plan Review (JPR) and the Job Plan Suspension (JPS) review process. They will be sent a letter 14 days after the JPR is triggered, telling them to contact the agency to negotiate a Job Plan
  • responds to the letter, check the status of the Job Plan in their online account via Customer Online View in Customer View. Tell the job seeker of the required action to finalise the plan (for example, sign in person at a service centre or use their online account)
  • requires a restoration, they must negotiate and agree to a new Job Plan

If necessary, assist the job seeker to finalise their Job Plan via the job seeker's online account.

Note: the WAP/PLN activity will stay on the job seeker's record and they will be able to agree to their Job Plan in their online account.

If the job seeker does not respond to this letter, their payment will suspend 14 days later. The reason will be Failing to Reply to Correspondence. If they still do not have a current Job Plan after 13 weeks, their payment will auto-cancel.

The Resources page has information about the 'View and Agree to Job Plan' service.

9

Automatic Job Plan updates + Read more ...

Some Centrelink Managed job seekers signed up for Online Services who are fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements through part-time work can have their Job Plan updated automatically when the requirement end date is reached by completing an Online Task. These job seekers then use the 'View and Agree Job Plan' online service to approve their Job Plan.

Where a Centrelink Managed job seeker attempts to update their approved requirement and/or Job Plan online and the requirement fails to update, the system will generate a 'Change of Circumstances' work item. If allocated, investigate the reason automation has failed. Once resolved, complete the COC activity:

  • select the 'Online-MOR & Job Plan or AEX Review' COC Interaction from the Interaction History
  • change the status from 'For manual action' to 'Completed'

Procedure ends here.

10

Think time + Read more ...

If the job seeker has concerns or expresses the need to think further about the terms of the Job Plan, discuss those issues with them.

If the job seeker still wants to think further about the terms (for example to consult with a third party before signing), offer think time to the job seeker.

Remote job seekers

Arrange a phone interview for 2 days' time and either submit the Job Plan to the job seeker's online account or fax/post a copy of the draft Job Plan if required, to the job seeker to think about the terms being negotiated.

Note: the Job Plan may, if necessary, be held pending for up to 21 days where job seeker is considered remote.

Non-remote job seekers

For non-remote job seekers:

  • make an appointment or tell them to return in 2 working days' time
  • create a manual review if no appointment was booked
  • give a copy of the draft Job Plan to the job seeker to think about the terms being negotiated
  • alternatively, submit the Job Plan to the job seeker's online account for finalisation after they have had think time, avoiding the need for a further appointment

All job seekers

Tell the job seeker that failure to enter into a Job Plan within 2 days may result in payment suspension.

Do not finalise the Job Plan on the system until it has been returned and signed.

When to cancel a started Job Plan + Read more ...

It is only appropriate to cancel a Job Plan where a pending plan has been started but not finalised and:

  • the job seeker is now provider managed or
  • the job seeker's participation requirements have changed or
  • the job seeker's circumstances have changed and they no longer need a Job Plan (e.g. transferred to a non-participation payment or the job seeker is exempt)

When the Job Plan is cancelled, all updates are deleted and the job seeker's previous plan is reinstated. Note: a started Job Plan can only be cancelled once per day.

Is it appropriate to cancel the started Job Plan?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, negotiate and complete the started Job Plan as per Table 3

2

How old is the started Job Plan + Read more ...

Started Job Plans that are over 60 days old will need to be referred to ICT as Service Officers will be unable to cancel or delete these plans. Note: started Job Plans for job seekers who are now on a non-participation payment are automatically cancelled after 60 days and should not require further action.

Has the started Job Plan been outstanding for 60 days or more?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Cancelling the started Job Plan + Read more ...

From Process Direct:

Go to > Participation Summary > Job Plan

  • Select Negotiate Job Plan
  • Select the Delete Job Plan button on the Overview page
    • This will present a warning
      'Department of Employment edits control the deletion of a started (pending) Job Plan. Users may not delete two subsequent pending Job Plans on the same CRN. If you decide to delete this Job Plan now, no Services Australia user will be able to cancel a subsequent Job Plan for this customer.'
  • Select the appropriate reason for cancellation
  • Select Confirm

This will complete the cancellation process.

4

Refer to ICT + Read more ...

Raise a mySupport ticket via Roxy if a started Job Plan cannot be cancelled because:

  • of the age of the plan
  • a plan has already been cancelled and this is a second cancellation
  • there is a system issue/edit

Contacting the job seeker + Read more ...

Does the job seeker have contact details recorded?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

First outbound attempt + Read more ...

When it is necessary to contact a job seeker, make every reasonable and genuine effort to ensure contact is successful.

If the contact number on the job seeker's record is a mobile number, create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). After creating the SMS, wait 1 to 2 minutes before making the outbound attempt.

  • Yes, see Step 2 in Table 3
  • No,
    • First attempt to contact a job seeker is unsuccessful, hold the work item until the next calendar day (Go to Step 8 for details)

    • Second outbound attempt, go to Step 4

4

Second outbound attempt + Read more ...

If the first attempt to contact the job seeker is unsuccessful, make a second genuine attempt in line with current processes.

If the contact number on the job seeker's record is a mobile number, create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). After creating the SMS, wait 1 to 2 minutes before making the outbound attempt.

Was the contact successful?

  • Yes, see Step 2 in Table 3
  • No, after two unsuccessful attempts to contact job seeker, go to Step 5

5

Issue a Q164 letter after second unsuccessful contact attempt or where no contact details recorded for job seeker + Read more ...

Issue a Q164 letter asking the customer to contact Services Australia by phone within 14 days:

  • after a second unsuccessful outbound contact attempt, or
  • where there are no contact details or incorrect details are recorded (for example, the job seeker's phone number is not registered)

The customer has 14 days to respond after they receive the letter. Add days to allow extra time for mail delivery. Hold the work item until the day after the allowable time has expired (see Step 1 in Table 5 for details).

If the job seeker:

  • contacts because of the suspension, restore payment and action outstanding activities. see Step 2 in Table 3
  • fails to contact, go to Step 6
  • requires a manually issued Q164 letter, go to Step 9

6

Job seeker fails to respond to the Q164 letter + Read more ...

If the job seeker fails to contact by the notification provision date +1 after the Q164 is issued, determine if suspension action can be taken. Before suspending a customer's payment, see Suspension of payments (CLK) for information about factors to consider.

If it is appropriate to manually suspend a customer for Failing to Respond to Correspondence (SUS-FRC):

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen:
    • Key the payment type as the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn field
    • Key the action to be taken in the Action field
    • Key the suspension reason 'FRC' in the Reason field
    • Press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or key the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date field
    • Complete the Source field and the date of receipt in the DOR field
  • Include the following details on a DOC (where relevant):
    • Decision made, payment type and the Act/Guide reference the decision was based on, see Creating, reviewing and deleting documents (including Fast Notes and DOA DOCs) and Online Document Recording (ODR)
    • Date of effect of the decision
    • Reasons for the decision, including why the person was not qualified or why the payment is not payable, for decisions made under section 80
    • Follow-up action required on contact with the customer
    • If contact with the assessing Service Officer is required before restoration can be done or whether Smart Centre Telephony staff may restore the payment
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity

Where the job seeker's payment has:

  • been suspended for failing to contact:
    • no further action is required by Services Australia
    • hold the work item for 13 weeks from the suspension date. Do not cancel the work item
  • not been suspended as it is not appropriate to suspend the job seeker's payment for failing to contact:
    • undertake job seeker contact where appropriate, and
    • hold the work item as per the Q164 process. Do not cancel the work item

Note: go to Step 8 for how to hold a work item.

7

Complete Interaction Record + Read more ...

Complete the COC Interaction to prevent re-allocation of the activity.

  • Select the 'Online-MOR & Job Plan or AEX Review' COC Interaction from the Interaction History, and
  • Change the status from 'For manual action' to 'Completed'

Procedure ends here.

8

Hold a Work Item + Read more ...

Where a staff member determines a Job Plan (CoC Interaction) cannot be completed on allocation, staff must follow these instructions to hold the following work items correctly:

  • Job Plan update failed
  • Payment restore action failed
  • Customer refuses Job Plan
  • Customer disagrees with Job Plan

In Customer First, CoC Interaction Records are listed on the Customer Summary screen.

  • Select the CoC Interaction Record by clicking on the ID
  • Select Edit from the top menu
  • Go to the Dates tab and click on Expand
  • Find the On Hold Expiry Date line, update the hold date by clicking on the calendar icon in the date column and select the next calendar day
  • Select the activity status and change status to On Hold from the drop down list
  • Select Save from the top menu

Do not place Interaction Record on hold without an expiry date. This will place the work item on Hold indefinitely, resulting in the item never reallocating.

If the job seeker has a correspondence nominee, issue the same letter to the correspondence nominee.

To finalise the letter:

  • Complete the required fields in the letter by selecting each appropriate option. Press [Continue/Enter]. Use [Previous] and [Next] buttons to go through the letter until all fields are completed
  • View letter variables by keying 'DATA' in the Command field. Press [Enter] to display the Variable Data (LDD) screen. If 'free text' is required, key this on the LLD screen
  • Key or amend details as required, for example, address, contact details
  • When completed, key 'FIN'alise in the Command field and press [Enter]
  • The system will prompt if fields still need to be completed. To view these fields:
    • Go to the LDT screen, key 'OPT' in the Command field and press [Enter]
    • Make any updates and key 'FIN' in the Command field and press [Enter]
