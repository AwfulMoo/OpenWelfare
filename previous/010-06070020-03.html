Assessing the activity test for a YA student continuing/intending to study

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer is intending to study and will be claiming/is receiving YA + Read more ...

Does the YA customer meet the definition of a continuing/intending student:

  • Yes, and
  • knows the course they are intending to commence, or knows their first course preference if they have applied for more than 1 course, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • is unsure of the course they will commence, see Step 2 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

New student current on YA or JobSeeker Payment (JSP) + Read more ...

Customers who are not continuing/intending students are considered to be ‘new students’ and only satisfy the activity test requirements for students from the date they commence/resume full-time study.

Is the new student customer currently receiving either YA or JSP?

  • receiving YA as a student, go to Step 6
  • receiving JSP or YA as a job seeker, go to Step 3
  • not receiving YA or JSP, assess their YA claim. Procedure ends here

3

New students do not satisfy the activity test as full-time students until their course commences + Read more ...

These customers must satisfy mutual obligation requirements by undertaking other approved activities, no matter how short the period is before they commence their course.

Is the customer under 22 years of age?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Customer is under 22 years of age + Read more ...

Advise customer they can satisfy YA mutual obligation requirements by undertaking approved activities, for example, voluntary work, training course or job search.

Note: customers who have not completed Year 12 (or equivalent) or a Certificate III or higher qualification may undertake other approved activities of at least 25 hours per week to satisfy their mutual obligation requirements. See Mutual obligation requirements for Youth Allowance job seekers under 22 years of age who have not completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification.

If the customer is required to satisfy mutual obligation requirements by job search, they are required to look for any suitable work which will not affect their ability to commence their course (for example, temporary, part-time or casual work).

See Coding new study details.

Procedure ends here.

5

Customer is 22 or over + Read more ...

Advise customer they must remain on JSP and satisfy the JSP mutual obligation requirements during the period prior to commencing their course.

They can satisfy mutual obligation requirements by looking for casual, temporary or part-time work that will not affect their ability to commence their course.

Advise the customer to lodge an online claim within 13 weeks of intending to study YA.

6

Customer receiving YA (student) + Read more ...

If a YA student cannot be considered a continuing/intending student, end the course of study.

See Student to job seeker transfers.

Coding the education details and intention to study for a YA student

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Record course details for a YA customer who is a continuing/intending student + Read more ...

Run the Update Study Details guided procedure

  • Ensure the Participation Status Event Date is:
    • the Student End Date + 1 from the previous line of Study Details for YA students continuing from 1 course to a new course, or
    • the deemed date of claim if processing a YA new claim, or
    • the course start date for current YA job seekers
  • If the course details have been provided for a new claim, and the customer is under 22 years of age, determine whether the customer wishes to apply for YA and satisfy mutual obligation requirements by undertaking approved activities until their course commences. See Mutual obligation requirements for Youth Allowance job seekers under 22 years of age who have not completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification
  • If a new claimant only wishes for payment to be granted from the course start date, ensure the claim is being assessed from the course start date (taking into account any relevant waiting periods that may apply)
  • Record details of customer's intended course on a DOC

Note: if the student has left the course and started a new course, the original course must be end dated. The end date will be the date the customer left the course. Procedure ends here.

2

Record the intention to study + Read more ...

Run the Update Study Details guided procedure and complete only the following fields:

  • Participation Status Event Date - key the previous course end date + 1
  • Participation Status - key:
    • 'INS' if the customer is intending to study at secondary level
    • 'INT' if the customer is intending to study at tertiary level
  • Inst Course Start Date - key the same date as the Participation Status Event Date field
  • Inst Course End Date - key the day before the expected start date of the intended course. Note: this date can be amended if required, provided the date is not after the normal course start date for the institution. For example, if the customer misses out on first round university offers but expects to pick something up in the second round, or is investigating other options such as TAFE or private colleges

Finalise the activity.

Record details of customer's intended course on a DOC.

The system will issue a letter and set up a review for 3 weeks prior to the end of the intention period, when a Course details required for Youth Allowance letter will be sent to the student for further study details. For further details on the letter, see Processing end of course and intention to study reviews for Austudy and Youth Allowance (YA).

Note: when full course details are provided, study details are required to be recorded via the Update Study Details guided procedure.
