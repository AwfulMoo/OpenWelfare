Check quarterly review (QR) refund amounts for home care recipients (post 1 July 2014)

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Situations where a QR refund may be generated + Read more ...

Income tested fee (ITCF) refunds may generate for home care recipients where:

  • a care recipient’s circumstances change and reduces the ITCF they need to pay
  • a care recipient has a period of leave (social or hospital) greater than 28 days
    • During the leave the service provider subsidy is reduced by 25%
    • Care recipients cannot be asked to pay more than the subsidy payment (cost of care)
    • Home care recipients should not be charged for home care services while accessing transitional or residential respite care
    • See Temporary leave from Home Care Packages on the Resources page for further information
  • a care recipient's status changes from self-funded retiree to an income support recipient
    • This changes the annual/lifetime cap amounts
  • a care recipient's status was means not disclosed (MND) and the care recipient has since disclosed their means
  • means test data was updated in ACMPS after the date of departure
    • Departure reasons may include death, transfer to another home care service provider or admission to residential care
  • incorrect fees were charged due to data transfer issues between Customer First and ACMPS

Was a list of ACMPS Care Recipient IDs provided for the initial check?

  • Yes, in ACMPS access a care recipient record then, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Manual search for refund + Read more ...

Use the Services Australia Fees and Charges role in ACMPS:

  • Select Means Testing Review from the side menu to display the Reconciliation Menu
  • Select Refund Approval Task to view a refund triggered by QR. The Refund Approval screen displays
  • Use the Search Criteria below to locate the refund needing approving or rejecting:
    • Status: select Calculated – On Hold
    • Task Type: field will automatically populate with Activity for Refund Approval
    • Service BP ID: input ID
    • Care Recipient ID: input ID
    • Select the Search button
  • If the search is not completed using a Care Recipient ID, select a care recipient name displayed in the Care Recipient column

Note: do not select the Refund for delegate approval link at this point.

3

Confirm entry date and total income details + Read more ...

In ACMPS:

  • Select the Assessments & Events tab
  • Check the Entry and Departure Details section for an entry date
  • Select the Means Testing Data tab
  • Check the ACMPS Total Income details section for any updates made to income
  • Check Fees and Charges to confirm ongoing fees that have been set

Note: if there are any large unexplained fluctuations or reductions in income, check Customer First to confirm all updates have been actioned correctly.

Is the correct income information displaying?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 7

4

Investigate QR refund period + Read more ...

Check if ACMPS has already paid a QR refund for the same period:

  • Select the Reconciliation Data tab located on the same line as the Means Testing Data tab
    • If it does not display on this line, use the File icon (far right hand side) to select Reconciliation Data from the dropdown menu
  • The Reconciliation Data tab displays historical QR periods and current QR period refunds due
    • Review Type: shows the review type that calculated the refund. For example, QR or departure review
    • Status: displays as Refund paid or Not entitled to refund
    • Reconciled Amount: a negative amount shows the care recipient has underpaid their fees
  • Find the current QR period and select the arrow beside Review Type to view the:
    • Fee Period Start Date
    • Fee Period End Date
    • Applied Fee
    • Notional Fee
    • Reconciled Amount
  • QR only checks the months with an approved provider subsidy claim, so the refund calculated may not include all months in the QR period. For example:
    • QR period 1 July to 19 September calculates a refund only up to 31 July because the August and September provider subsidy claims are not lodged/finalised
    • The following QR will include August and September (if the provider claims are lodged and finalised)
  • If a refund has automatically paid earlier, the Status column in Reconciliation Data displays Refund paid

Check if a manual adjustment refund has already paid for the same period:

  • Select Payments Additions from the sidebar (do not select Payment Additions under the Create heading on the sidebar)
  • Input Service ID
  • Under Category Type select Claims then select Search
  • Select the relevant claim month the refund was paid, then select Payment ID
  • Select Entitlement Items and select care recipient
    • Check all pages as refunds can appear on the last page. The Entitlement Description will show as Refund
  • Select the arrow under Actions to display the amount

Check if a Financial Hardship supplement was paid:

  • Select Hardship & Income Suspension tab
    • This displays financial hardship supplements paid during the QR period under review

Check if leave was taken:

  • Select the Assessments & Events tab
  • Expand Leave Details to view any leave dates and leave types taken during the QR period under review
  • See Aged Care Reviews – Manual adjustments for home care recipients for further information regarding leave if required

Check the Notes tab for any record of a manual adjustment or financial hardship grant already paid for the same adjustment period.

In Customer First:

  • Check the Document List (DL) screen for any record of a manual adjustment or Financial Hardship grant already paid for the same adjustment period
  • Check for any change in circumstances that may affect the refund. For example, a change in Assistance Type from CLK/DVA to SFR

Is this QR refund the same as a refund already paid via a previous QR or manual adjustment?

  • Yes,
    • No further refund is due
    • To reject the refund, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Check the refund is correct + Read more ...

In ACMPS:

  • Check the Means Testing Data tab to expand:
  • Circumstance Details to get details of marital status and dependants
  • Total Income Details to get income details for the relevant dates

In the Aged Care Fee Calculator (available on the Resources page) navigate to the Home Care Manual Adjustment Calculator tab.

In the Care Recipient Profile section, add all care recipient details.

In the Home Care section complete the following fields:

  • A new line is required for every:
    • Refund line (in the ACMPS Reconciliation Data tab) of the expanded QR refund under investigation
    • Change in income noted in the ACMPS Means Testing Data tab
    • Period of leave taken
  • Date of Effect Start Date and Date of Effect End Date
    • This automatically calculates and populates the Total Number of days field
  • Total Fortnightly $ Income
    • Or complete the Total Annual $ Income field. This automatically calculates the fortnightly income
  • Marital Status
  • In the Corrected Fees column, add the Daily Income Tested Fee
    • This is the fee calculated in the previous step
    • The Total Period ITF field automatically calculates
  • In the Previously Calculated Fees column, add the Daily Income Tested Fee
    • This is the Applied Fee in the ACMPS Reconciliation Data tab
    • The Total Period ITF field automatically calculates
  • Check the Date of Effect in the calculator is correct for each period calculated
    • This ensures the relevant threshold is applied. See the Schedule of Fees and Charges on the Resources page for further information on the thresholds if required
  • Repeat for each change in circumstances
  • If leave (for example, Hospital, Transition Care, Residential Respite Care or Social Leave) was taken the ITCF may be reduced
    • See Temporary leave from Home Care Packages on the Resources page for calculation assistance and Aged Care Reviews – Manual adjustments for home care recipients for further information regarding leave

Note: when a care recipient has complex changes in circumstances or the reason for a refund amount is not determined, the refund is stopped and referred for further investigation.

Does the calculated refund amount match the QR refund under investigation?

  • Yes, to approve refund, go to Step 6
  • No, leave refund as Calculated – On Hold, go to Step 7

6

Approve the refund + Read more ...

Update the QR spreadsheet with outcome of Approved. Notes are not needed in ACMPS or Customer First when a refund is approved.

In the ACMPS side menu:

  • Select Means Testing Review
  • Select Refund Approval Task
  • Search the QR refund using the below Search Criteria:
    • Status: select Calculated – On Hold
    • Task Type: field will automatically populate with Activity for Refund Approval
    • Care Recipient ID: input ID
  • Select the required refund line
  • Under the Description column select the Refund for delegate approval:
    • select Approve Refund
    • select Save
    • A green box with a tick shows successfully saved

Note: the refund letter is not visible straight away. The system updates overnight and the letter then shows in the Communication History tab.

Procedure ends here.

7

Reject the refund + Read more ...

Reject the refund amount

In the ACMPS side menu:

  • Select Means Testing Review
  • Select Refund Approval Task
  • Search the QR refund using the below Search Criteria:
    • Status: select Calculated – On Hold
    • Task Type: field automatically populates with Activity for Refund Approval
    • Care Recipient ID: input ID
  • Select the refund line
  • Under the Description column select Refund for delegate approval:
    • select Reject Refund
    • select Save
    • A green box with a tick shows successfully saved

Update the QR spreadsheet with the outcome of Rejected.

In ACMPS add Notes detailing the rejected refund including:

  • <Month Year> QR refund of <$0,000.00> and letter series <insert letter codes>rejected
  • Whether the refund has been referred for further investigation
  • Add SD/PT number if a mySupport referral was completed. The Resources page contains a link

For remediation of incorrect QR refunds, see Aged Care Reviews – Manual adjustments for home care recipients.

Check quarterly review (QR) refund amounts for residential care (pre 1 July 2014)

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Situations where a QR refund may be generated + Read more ...

Income tested fee (ITF) refunds may generate for pre 1 July 2014 care recipients where:

  • a care recipient’s circumstances change and reduce the ITF they need to pay
  • the cost of care (Aged Care Funding Instrument - ACFI) changes, resulting in a reduction of the ITF
    • Check current ACFI on Client Profile (screen 2.6)
  • a service provider has not lodged a valid ACFI within the required time frame
    • Confirm dates of ACFI on Client Profile (screen 2.6)
    • ACFI lodged between 2 and 5 months from date of entry results in a care subsidy reduction of $25 per day (may affect maximum MTCF)
    • ACFI lodged after 5 months from date of entry results in no care subsidy paid to the provider until the day prior to lodgement (and therefore no ITF payable)
  • a care recipient's status changes from self-funded retiree to an income support recipient
  • a care recipient's status was RCA Rejected - means not disclosed (MND),the care recipient has since completed a means assessment (backdated)
    • Confirm on View Assistance, Income and Asset Details (screen 2.11)
    • Check that income/asset details in SPARC from date of entry into care
  • incorrect fees were charged due to data transfer issues between aged care systems

2

Obtain admission details + Read more ...

    • Admission From date or Pre-entry leave from date
    • RACS ID
    • Departure date (if applicable)
    • Centrelink ID and/or Veterans’ Affairs ID

Note: care recipients who entered care prior to 1 July 2014 are charged fees from the pre-entry date (if applicable).

Check quarterly review (QR) refund amounts for residential care (post 1 July 2014)

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Situations where a QR refund may be generated + Read more ...

Means tested care fees (MTCF) or accommodation contribution (AC) refunds may generate for post 1 July 2014 care recipients in SPARC where:

  • a care recipient’s circumstances change and reduce the MTCF or AC they need to pay
  • income and assets are corrected from date of entry and resident status is corrected
  • the cost of care (Aged Care Funding Instrument - ACFI) changes, resulting in a reduction of the MTCF
    • Check current ACFI on Client Profile (screen 2.6)
  • a service provider has not lodged a valid ACFI within the required time frame
    • Confirm dates of ACFI on Client Profile (screen 2.6)
    • ACFI lodged between 2 and 5 months from date of entry results in a care subsidy reduction of $25 per day (may affect maximum MTCF)
    • ACFI lodged after 5 months from date of entry results in no care subsidy paid to the provider until the day prior to lodgement (and therefore no MTCF payable)
  • a care recipient was means not disclosed (MND) and the care recipient has since completed a means assessment
    • Confirm on View Assistance, Income and Asset Details (screen 2.11)
    • Check that income/asset details in SPARC from date of entry into care
  • a care recipient has reached their annual or lifetime cap
    • MTCF is not set to $0 until a claim is lodged for the month of the cap being reached. A care recipient will be eligible for a refund from the cap date until the date their fee was adjusted. See Aged Care fees and charges - Annual and Lifetime Caps for more information
  • incorrect fees were charged due to data transfer issues between aged care systems

2

Obtain admission details + Read more ...

In SPARC:

  • From Client Profile (screen 2.6) select and expand the ADM P line to view Client Admission/Discharge Expansion screen
  • Make a note of:
    • Admission From or Pre-entry leave from (where applicable) date
    • RACS ID
    • Departure date (where applicable)
    • Centrelink ID and/or Veterans' Affairs ID

Note: care recipients who entered care prior to 1 July 2015 are charged BDF fees from the pre-entry date (if applicable).

3

Investigate QR refund details + Read more ...

Select View Means Testing Fees and Refunds (screen 2.30) and enter Client Number.

Investigate the refund and check:

  • The refund period
  • Refund start date
  • Previously calculated fees compared to updated fees
  • Ongoing fees that have been set
  • Press [Shift F3] to access the Browse Assistance, Income and Asset Details screen
    • This shows the history of assessed income and asset amounts used to calculate the MTCF
    • Check any updates to assets and/or income are in SPARC from the correct date/s
    • Press [Shift F1] to access Browse Income Sources
    • A Y in the Superceded column shows new information replaced the previous income/assets and the MTCF was recalculated

Has this refund calculated after an update to income and assets? Note: this includes means assessments completed for a care recipient previously MND.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 6

4

Refund paid after update to income and assets + Read more ...

View Browse Assistance, Income and Asset Details (screen 2.11 F5) and confirm the income and assets that have been updated.

When income and assets have been coded from the date of entry into care, a check is required to see if the resident status of a care recipient has been corrected.

This includes:

  • Care recipients who were MND and are now low means
  • Care recipients who were originally low means and the means assessment has now been corrected and they are not low means

Has this update corrected the resident status for a care recipient?

  • Yes,
    • go to Aged Care correction to resident status
    • procedure ends here
  • No, and refund is less than $10,000
    • no refund checking required
    • go to Step 8
  • No, and refund is over $10,000
    • continue with checking of refund
    • go to Step 5

5

Confirming dates of effect for income and asset update + Read more ...

Check the refund period and the dates of effect of any income and asset updates.

If an income and assets update and system refresh were not completed at the same time, SPARC may calculate a refund based on $0 assets from the date of entry up until the coding date.

When income and assets have not applied from the correct date of effect, or there is other remediation required for coding of income and assets, including from DVA, a work item will be created to follow up. This may be a work item for remediation only (refund released) or when a refund is required to be stopped. See Profiling and manual refresh of aged care means assessment data. If DVA have completed the means assessment, use the Aged Care DVA Referral web form on the Resources page to request backdating of income and assets to the date of entry.

Where a refund was calculated based on an incorrect date of effect:

  • Use the Aged Care Fee calculator on the Resources page to check the correct MTCF/MTC based on income and assets
  • Refer to the refund and check how many days the refund was calculated based on $0 assets
  • Multiply the number of days in the refund by an the average correct MTCF/MTC rate
  • The result is the difference between the correct refund amount and the incorrectly calculated refund amount

Does this calculation produce a result that is $5,000 or less?

  • Yes or unsure, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 8

6

Check if a refund has already paid for the same QR period + Read more ...

Check if a previous QR refund or Manual Adjustment refund has already paid for all or part of the QR period under investigation.

In SPARC:

  • Select View Means Testing Review Fees and Refunds (screen 2.30). Where an MTCF refund has previously been paid it will appear under the heading MTSR Refund
  • Check Client Notes (screen 20) for records of manual adjustments already paid or financial hardship assistance granted

In Customer First check the Document List (DL) screen for any previous actioned manual adjustments or financial hardship assistance granted.

Is this QR refund the same as a refund that has already been paid via QR or manual adjustment?

  • Yes or unsure, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Check refund is correct + Read more ...

Calculate the correct MTCF/AC

In the Aged Care Fee Calculator:

  • Navigate to the Residential Care Manual Adjustment tab
  • Insert the SPARC assets and income details from Step 3
  • Check the Date of Effect in the calculator is correct for each period calculated
    • This ensures the relevant threshold is applied. See the link to the Schedule of Fees and Charges Post – 1 July 2014 on the Resources page for further information on the thresholds
  • Repeat for each change in circumstances

Note: when a care recipient has complex changes in circumstances or the reason for a refund amount is not determined, the refund should be stopped and referred for further investigation.

Is the calculated refund the same as the manually calculated refund?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, and SPARC is:
    • MTF refund only - more than the correct refund and the difference is more than $5,000 from the correct refund, go to Step 8
    • MTC refund – more than the correct refund, go to Step 8
    • MTF and MTC -less than the correct refund, go to Step 9
    • MTF refund only - more than the correct refund and the difference is less than $5,000 from the correct refund, go to Step 9
  • Unable to calculate, go to Step 8

9

Release refund + Read more ...

When initial checking has been completed in SPARC prototype, update the QR spreadsheet with your logon and result of check:

  • Release refund with no further action:
    • Release: Y, Hold: N, Rem Only: N
  • Release refund with a work item for remediation:
    • Release: Y-R, Hold: N, Rem Only: Y
  • When Remediation required, complete: Error Origin, Category and Notes to state why remediation is required, including:
    • Updating/correcting the care recipient’s income and/or assets
    • Incorrect ongoing fees
    • other details (including when further refunds are due)
  • Initial checking procedure ends here

For remediation of incorrect QR refunds, see Aged Care Reviews - Manual Adjustments for Post 1 July 2014 Residential Care.
