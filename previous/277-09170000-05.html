Customer contact - initial discussion checklist

The initial discussion with the applicant should occur within 2 working days of receiving a potential objection. The respondent should be contacted within 2 working days of contacting the applicant but where possible, on the same day as the applicant contact. Use the Objection First Contact macro to discuss to following information.

Initial discussion checklist

Table 1: This table provides a checklist for the initial customer discussion.

Item

Description

1

Confirm the Applicant’s intention (applicant only)

Discuss with the applicant:

  • their correspondence or objection form has been received
  • what they want to achieve by sending in the correspondence and if an administrative options is more suitable
  • their intention to lodge an objection

2

Administrative Options (applicant only)

Discuss appropriate options with the applicant to resolve their issues, these may include:

  • error correction- simple factual errors and some more complex errors can be fixed without the need for an objection
  • complaint - if the customer is unhappy with the service provided rather than the actual decision, lodging a complaint may be a more suitable option. See Managing customer complaints and feedback.
  • new administrative action- other administrative action such as reviewing a payment arrangement may resolve the customer’s concern
  • new decision- if the customer’s circumstances have changed it may be more appropriate for a new decision to be made
  • change of assessment - if the customer has special circumstances a change of assessment application may be a better option. See Child Support Guide 2.6: Change of assessment in special circumstances

3

The Objection Process (applicant and respondent)

Explain to the respondent:

  • an objection has been received from the other parent
  • the decision, subject to the objection
  • the grounds of the objection
  • they will receive a copy of the objection and will be made aware of any new information provided by the applicant
  • the applicant may withdraw their objection at any time before the decision has been finalised
  • possible outcomes, including potential contrary or adverse decision
  • they are encouraged to provide a response either verbally or in writing as quickly as possible but have 28 days if needed. (90 days for customers in a reciprocating jurisdiction). See Extension of time calculator macro
  • their response and any information provided may be discussed and/or sent to the other customer
  • the respondent can lodge their own objection at any time, however:
    • if the respondent objects outside the 28 day timeframe (90 days for customers in reciprocating jurisdictions) they will need to request an extension of time for objection to proceed
    • if the objection is about a care percentage decision (including date of event of a care change) the respondent can object at any time, however if they object outside the 28 (90) day timeframe, any change to the care percentage will only apply to the assessment from the date the objection was lodged, unless there were special circumstances that impacted on the respondent's ability to lodge an objection earlier

4

Open Exchange of Information (applicant and respondent)

Explain to both customers:

  • when information is used by Child Support to make the decision, it is provided to the other party for comment
  • information is usually exchanged by providing the other party with copies of documents, but information can also be provided verbally if it is more efficient to do so
  • Child Support must exchange information with parties to ensure customers who may be adversely affected by the information, have the opportunity to comment before the decision is made
  • if they do not want the other party to see information provided to Child Support, they should either not include it or delete/edit the specific details from the documents they provide. Deleted information will not be available to the decision maker for consideration when making the decision. The decision will be based on information that was made available to both customers

For further information see Open exchange of information for Child Support customers

5

Evidence Requests (applicant and respondent)

Discuss with both customers:

  • the evidence needed to support the customer’s claims and any other facts that need to be established
  • the need for information to be provided as soon as possible so that it can be considered in the decision
  • agreement on how and when the customer will provide the required evidence and what we will do if it is not provided
  • possible outcomes if the evidence is not received
  • Child Support may contact third parties to seek information

6

Further Appeal Rights (this discussion is optional at the initial call stage)

Advise both customers:

  • if they do not agree with the objection decision the customer can apply to the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT)
  • if they disagree with the outcome of the AAT first review the customer may seek an AAT second review of the decision if the decision is about:
    • care percentage
    • date of effect of an AAT first review decision relating to a care percentage, or
    • an AAT decision to refuse an application for extension of time to apply for an AAT first review
  • if they disagree with an outcome of the AAT first review for any decision other than those listed immediately above, the customer may appeal to a court with jurisdiction on a point of law

Note: different arrangements apply for review of objection decisions for WA ex-nuptial cases from 1 July 2015. Seek assistance from a Service Support Officer (SSO) for further assistance with review options in these cases.

Completing the Statement of Reasons macro - General Objections

The statement of reasons report generated by the macro is completed and sent to the customers to inform them of the objection decision and explain how the decision was reached. The length of the report will vary according to the complexity of the decision.

The report should be as succinct as possible without omitting relevant information.

The statement of reasons report must be written using plain English, short sentences and no jargon.

The first time a shortened phrase is used spell it out in full and give the shortened form in brackets, for example Change of Assessment (CoA). Once it has been written in this way, use the acronym in the document.

See the General Objections Guide to Writing a Statement of Reasons and Services Australia Writing Style Guide for more information.

The statement of reasons must be written using the General Objections macro and include:

Statement of Reasons report

Table 2: This table provides details about what must be included in the Statement of Reasons report by using the Statement of Reasons macro.

Item

Description

1

Decision under review

State why the applicant objected to the decision.

Briefly describe the original decision including the:

  • date of decision
  • decision type
  • child/ren subject to the decision, if relevant
  • outcome of the decision

2

Summary of objection decision

Outline the result of the objection including the:

  • outcome of the objection, for example allowed, partially allowed, disallowed
  • change to be made
  • impact on the assessment
  • date decision is effective from, if relevant

3

Evidence considered in the decision

It is not a requirement to copy or quote the evidence as both customers should have been provided with a copy of that evidence as part of the objection Open Exchange (OEI) process.

Evidence supplied by the parents and gathered during the investigation can be summarised in the section below:

'We Considered the Following Evidence in the Decision'

Use bullet points to summarise the evidence.

Note: document any evidence requested from the parties but not provided in the report. Use quotation marks when direct quotes are used.

4

Reasons for the decision

Explain how the facts of the case in consideration of the material facts that were analysed to make the decision.

Detail all steps in the reasoning process taken to make the decision, including the relevant facts and evidence. Any other factors considered in making the decision should also be noted. This will provide the reader with an understanding of how the decision was reached and provide details of the conclusions.

The actual decision should also be articulated here as well as under its own heading at the beginning of the report.

5

Appeal rights for the decision

The covering letter for the statement of reasons must explain that either parent may apply to the AAT for a review of the decision and a timeframe for seeking a review.

Different arrangements apply for review of objection decisions for WA ex-nuptial cases from 1 July 2015. Seek assistance from a Service Support Officer (SSO) for further assistance with review options in these cases.

6

Final Checklist

Before sending the statement of reason report to customers:

  • proof read for spelling and grammatical errors
  • check that the correct names, dates and figures are used consistently throughout the report
  • check the tone of the report is respectful and does not show bias to either customer
  • check that the reasons for the decision are easy to understand

Note: a decision maker may become overly familiar with the report and read information into the content that is not there. It may also be harder for the person who wrote the report to pick up errors. Ask a peer or Team Leader to act as a ‘second set of eyes’ to review the report.
