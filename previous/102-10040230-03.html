This page contains information for restoring Partner Allowance (PA) and Widow Allowance (WA) payments following suspension or cancellation.

On this page:

Making a decision on whether PA and WA can be restored

Restoring PA and WA within 13 weeks of suspension or cancellation

Making a decision on whether PA and WA can be restored

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Check reason for suspension + Read more ...

Investigate the record. There may be more than one reason for a suspension or cancellation.

Suspension due to non-lodgement of TTA or SA383

13 weeks prior to Age Pension age, eligible customers are invited to submit the online transfer to Age Pension or return the SA383 form depending on their channel choice. If the customer does not submit the transfer online, via ACC or return the completed SA383, their (and their partner's) payment may be suspended from the date they turn Age Pension age.

If the customer (or their partner) is over Age Pension age, superannuation products must be provided before their payments can be restored. If they advise they do not have any superannuation investments, their payment can be restored.

Note: PA and WA will cease on 1 January 2022. To avoid a break in payment, customers will need to transfer to Age Pension on or before this date.

Suspension FRC or FSD

Customers may be sent a Request for information or be requested to provide information as part of a Transfer to Age claim. If the recipient and/or their partner fails to provide the requested information/action within the time allowed to respond, their payment may be suspended.

All other suspensions

For all other suspension/cancellation reasons, Service Officers must determine if the payment can be restored at initial contact.

For suspension codes, see Cancellation, suspension and rejection reason codes for Newstart System (NSS).

2

Determine period of suspension/cancellation + Read more ...

Check customer's record to see if payment is still suspended and for how long.

This information is found by accessing the Service Reason Summary (SRS) screen and 'S'electing the suspended payment. This will flow to the Allowance/Benefit History (ABH) screen. Alternatively, check the Event Summary (ES) screen and find the suspension activity.

  • If the period is more than 13 weeks, go to Step 3
  • If less than 13 weeks, see Step 1 in Table 2

3

Suspension/cancellation period is more than 13 weeks + Read more ...

PA and WA payments can no longer be granted. Where the payment has been cancelled, the customer will need to test their eligibility for a different payment.

Advise customer of claiming procedures. See Contact in relation to an intended claim.

Restoring PA and WA within 13 weeks of suspension or cancellation

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Suspension/cancellation period is less than 13 weeks (3 months) + Read more ...

Determine and/or check:

  • Check the reason for suspension/cancellation via the SRS screen
  • Check the list of suspension codes, find the relevant code and check if restorable. See Cancellation, suspension and rejection reason code for Newstart System (NSS)
  • Check the Document List (DL) screen in the customer's record and read any DOCs regarding the suspension/cancellation
  • Check the record to make sure everything that is required for restoration has been lodged/actioned

Is the customer required to provide any information or documentation or meet any other requirement before payment can be restored? Or is there any other information that prevents customer's record being restored and actioned at first contact?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Do not restore on initial contact + Read more ...

WA or PA is suspended for non-lodgement of a Transfer to Age Pension request

  • Service Officer's must discuss transferring to Age Pension. PA and WA payments will cease on 1 January 2022. To avoid a break in payment, customers will need to transfer to Age Pension on or before this date
    • If the customer wants to transfer to Age Pension, complete an ACC at the first point of contact and advise what documentation is required. See Transfer to Age Pension
      Go to Step 3
    • If the customer declines to transfer to Age Pension, superannuation products must still be provided before the payment can be restored. Advise the customer WA and PA payments will cease on the 01 January 2022
      If the customer does not have any superannuation investments, their payment can be restored
  • Please annotate the 'Urgent WDA/PA Discussion REQ' display on access DOC with details of the discussion. If there is no display on access DOC, record a new DOC with details of the discussion

All other reasons

Do not restore payment. Advise the customer what is required for restoration. Payment cannot be restored until all information has been provided and/or action has been undertaken as required and has been verified.

Record details on a DOC including advice given to the customer. If there is an 'Urgent WDA/PA Discussion REQ' display on access DOC on the record please annotate this with details of the discussion.

Procedure ends here.

3

Restore Payments + Read more ...

PA and WA can only be restored where the customer has continuous entitlement. If there has been any break in entitlement during the suspension period, payment cannot be restored. The customer will need to test their eligibility for a different payment.

Confirm with the customer if there have been any changes of circumstances they should advise - update accordingly.

TTA lodged and has declined to transfer to Age Pension

Service Officers must discuss transferring to Age Pension. PA and WA payments cease on 1 January 2022. To avoid a break in payment, customers will need to transfer to Age Pension on or before this date. If the customer wants to transfer to Age Pension, complete ACC at the first point of contact. See Transfer to Age Pension.

Income management

If the customer is under income management (they have 'INM' on the benefit status line) and they have an arrears or lump sum payment entitlement, do not make any changes to the customer's record to allow this payment to be made. Contact the Income Management Team to allow income management to be turned on for the arrears payment before it is made.

Rent assistance paid with FTB

If the customer's Family Tax Benefit (FTB) is cancelled as a result of the income support payment being suspended/cancelled restore FTB first where Rent Assistance is paid with the FTB.

To update record:

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen
  • Enter the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn: field and enter the action 'RES' in the Action: field
  • Press [Enter] and the effect date will default or enter the effect date of the restoration in the Event Date: field
  • Complete Source: and DOR: fields
  • If the customer is income managed under the Voluntary Income Management measure, and the agreement was in place for more than 13 weeks, the Voluntary Income Management Continuation Assessment page is presented. The purpose of this screen is to record whether the customer wishes to continue or terminate their VIM agreement. Refer to Effect on Income Management when payment is suspended, on zero rate, cancelled, or Unemployment Non-Payment Period applied
  • Annotate the 'Urgent WDA/PA Discussion REQ' display on access DOC or record a new DOC including action taken and advice given to customer, and of any changes in circumstances that have been recorded
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Check if customer is entitled to any arrears and that they appear correctly on the AR screen, and
  • Finalise the activity

The payment will be restored from the date of effect and a letter will be generated advising the customer that their payments have been restored.
