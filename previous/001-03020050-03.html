Access to Workload Management and Work Items + Read more ...

Staff access Workload Management capability and/or Work Items through:

  • Worklist (Work Optimiser)
  • Inbox (Process Direct)

The business process and associated business rules determine how Work Items are managed and actioned.

To receive a Student to Job seeker Transfer (SJT) appointment

Select the Ready button at the top right corner of the Work Inbox.

Bundling: at times staff may receive multiple Work Items, referred to as a 'bundle' or 'bundle allocation'. Bundled Work Items display in the Process Direct Inbox with a unique Bundling ID. For more information, see Workload Management.

Go to Step 3.

3

First call + Read more ...

Service Officers must attempt all available telephone numbers.

See Calling a customer or returning a customer's call for more information.

Was the first call successful?

  • Yes, select Answered from the First Call drop down box
    This will automatically update the appointment status to Completed:
    • for students under 22 years, see Step 3 in Table 3
    • for students 22 years and over who have taken the option of the JobSeeker Payment (JSP) streamlined claim process, see Participation Interview
  • No, go to Step 4

4

First contact call attempt unsuccessful + Read more ...

On the Change Appointment screen, select Not Answered (either message left or no message left) from the First Call drop down box.

This will automatically:

  • populate the outcome on the Change Appointment screen, on the Notes tab and in the Progress of Claim DOC
  • un-assign the appointment Work Item with the reason SCR - Second Attempt Required

If all contact numbers are disconnected, go to Step 6.

Go to Step 5.

5

Second call successful + Read more ...

Make an outbound call attempt using all available telephone numbers.

See Calling a customer or returning a customer's call for more information.

Was the second call successful?

  • Yes, select Answered from the Second Call drop down box
    This will automatically update the appointment status to Completed:
    • for students under 22 years, see Step 3 in Table 3
    • for students 22 years and over who have taken the option of the JSP streamlined claim process, see Participation Interview
    • for all other student to job seeker transfers, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Second call unsuccessful + Read more ...

If the second call is unsuccessful, select Not Answered (either message left or no message left) from the Second Call drop down box.

This will automatically:

  • populate the outcome on the Change Appointment screen, on the Notes tab in Process Direct and annotate the Progress of Claim DOC
  • update the status of the appointment to Cancelled - Did Not Attend

For students 22 years and over, their JSP streamlined claim will automatically reject with the reason 'Failed to Attend Interview' (REJ-FAI). The customer must lodge a new online claim. See Claiming JobSeeker Payment (JSP).

For students under 22 years, their Youth Allowance (YA) will automatically suspend with the reason 'Failed to Attend Interview' (SUS-FAI).

If the customer contacts after the suspension, see Table 3.

Note: the suspension/rejection will occur later in the day once you have cleared the record and not as part of the process to cancel the appointment - Did Not Attend.

Assessing a transfer from student to job seeker

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Customer contact + Read more ...

If the customer:

  • has deferred or discontinued study before the end of their course, go to Step 3
  • was issued a Course End Date review and has contacted before their student end date, go to Step 4
  • was issued a Course End Date review and has contacted after their Student End Date (SED), go to Step 2
  • is contacting to advise they will not start full-time study for Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy and PES, see Customer will not commence full-time study for Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy and Pensioner Education Supplement (PES). Procedure ends here
  • has contacted as part of a Centrelink Academic Reassessment Transformation (CART) review and has requested further income support, go to Step 5

2

Customer issued a Course End Date review and has contacted after their Student End Date (SED) + Read more ...

Customers under 22 years who contact:

  • within 14 days after their SED may have had their payment suspended or cancelled if they did not attend a Student to Job seeker Transfer (SJT) appointment. If the customer has now contacted and wants to keep getting income support and is no longer eligible as a student, restore the payment if required and see Step 3 in Table 3
  • more than 14 days after the SED are not eligible for retrospective assessment. For these students, where the payment has not auto cancelled, go to the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen and update fields as follows:
    • Event Date SED + 1
    • Student Status 'NST' Not a Student
      Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen and update fields as follows:
    • Svc Rsn YAL
    • Action CAN
    • Reason NST
    • Effect Date SED + 1 day
  • Finalise the activity via the Assessment Results (AR) screen. YA should then cancel with the reason 'Not a Full-time Student' (CAN-NST). Advise the customer a debt may be generated for payments received when not qualified as a student. If the customer needs further assistance, they must submit a claim for another income support payment. Procedure ends here

AUSTUDY and YA students 22 years and over who contact and wish to keep getting income support and are no longer eligible as a student, must submit a claim for another income support payment. If the customer submits their new claim:

  • within 14 days of ceasing study, they can be paid from SED + 1 day
  • more than 14 days after ceasing study, they can only be paid from the later of the date they submit their claim or the first day they become eligible for the new payment

SUS-PCD status: customers with an intention to study period coded whose end date has passed will be suspended for failure to provide course details (SUS-PCD). Their payment will then CAN-PCD automatically 13 weeks after the suspension date. The SUS-PCD period can affect processing other types of claims (e.g. job seeker claim). So it is important to update the NES screen with the event date SED + 1, Student Status 'NST' Not a Student and cancel the student CAN-NST, from the date the customer no longer had an intention to study where they advised within 13 weeks of the suspension decision.

For ABSTUDY

  • If still studying part-time, the student may be eligible for ABSTUDY Part-time Award
  • If no longer studying, identify if the customer is eligible for other income support as a job seeker.
    Note: ABSTUDY Living Allowance secondary school students have payment entitlement up to 31 December each year. These customers must be advised of the option to stay on ABSTUDY and recontact to claim a job seeker payment within 14 days from 31 December. If wishing to transfer to a job seeker payment immediately (before 31 December) they must be advised activity test requirements must be satisfied as part of the new job seeker payment, and an ABSTUDY debt may result
  • Secondary boarders getting ABSTUDY boarding instalment payments have entitlements paid to the boarding institution on their behalf. These students must stay on ABSTUDY until 31 December and cannot transfer to another income support payment until 1 January. If a secondary boarder getting ABSTUDY boarding instalment payments meets hardship provisions, they may be entitled to claim ABSTUDY Additional Assistance for financial assistance until they qualify for other payments from 1 January. These customers must recontact within 14 days from 31 December to claim for a job seeker payment
  • ABSTUDY Living Allowance tertiary students are paid up to the date they cease study. Transfer provisions do not apply to ABSTUDY students, under Section 23 of the Social Security Act. If the customer claims JobSeeker Payment (JSP) the date the customer submits their claim will be their start date

Procedure ends here.

3

Student End Date (SED) changing + Read more ...

If the customer has contacted within their notification period to advise they will cease study before their SED and are:

  • under 18 years, see Step 4 in Table 3
  • 18 to 21 years, see Step 5 in Table 3
  • 22 years or older, see Step 6 in Table 3

Note: ABSTUDY secondary school students and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme students who complete the school year are entitled to payment up to 31 December of that year. The system recognises this, but the exact Inst Course End Date and Student End Date must be entered on the system to record their actual study period.

4

Customer has a current Course End Date review and has contacted before their Student End Date (SED) + Read more ...

Current Social Security Law allows customers getting Austudy or Youth Allowance (YA) (full-time student) to lodge a claim to transfer to a job seeker payment up to 28 calendar days (4 weeks) before their SED. If the customer contacts within this period and confirms they will not continue studying full-time, take the following steps as appropriate.

Check the SED from the Education Course History (EDCH) screen. If the customer confirms they will not continue studying full-time after their SED and wants to transfer to a job seeker payment; they can be registered as No Stream Services Referral Required (NSSRR) job seekers.

  • For YA (full-time student) under 22 years transferring to YA (Job seeker), see Step 1 in Table 3
  • For full-time students transferring from Austudy or YA to JobSeeker Payment (JSP), see Step 2 in Table 3

5

Completed the Centrelink Academic Reassessment Transformation (CART) review and has advised they need further income support + Read more ...

If the customer has completed their CART Information review within 14 days of review initiation and has advised they need further income support, and they are:

  • under 22 years, see Payment of Youth Allowance (YA) and Austudy when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course, and:
    • have been contacted, run the Looking for Work Guided Procedure in Customer First
    • have not been contacted, update the study details as needed, this will create a Manual Follow-up (MFU) for their office to action
  • 22 years or older, they will have been advised in the CART online review to refer to the payment finder

Assessing eligibility according to age requirements

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Youth Allowance (YA) (Student) under 22 years transferring to YA (Job seeker) contacting up to 28 days before Student End Date (SED) + Read more ...

  • Tell the customer to complete the 'Confirm course end date' task if it is available and book their 'Student to Job Seeker' participation appointment. If the task is not available book this appointment for the customer
  • If the customer will stay on Youth Allowance (YA) but will change their activity type from full-time student to an alternative activity type (for example, job search, incapacitated etc.) upon completion of studies, run the stand-alone Looking for Work workflow to register the customer as No Stream Services Referral Required (NSSRR) or NRRRR. The Set registration to No Provider Referral Required field must be recorded using the reason 'FTS transfer to JSK within 4 weeks of SED' and complete the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI)
  • If the Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) referral is triggered as part of the Looking for Work (LFW) workflow, do not make the referral at this point
  • At the 'Student to Job seeker' appointment the Service Officer will complete appropriate referrals

Procedure ends here.

2

Austudy or YA (Student) 22 years or older transferring to JSP contacting up to 28 days before Student End Date (SED) + Read more ...

A JobSeeker Payment (JSP) streamlined claim can be done through the customer's Centrelink online account. This is accessed via the 'Confirm course end date' task presented on the home screen, or via the 'My Details > Manage study details' option from the main menu.

The task will present for eligible customers and is only available until the recorded SED. After this time if the customer wants to keep getting assistance, they must submit an online claim for JSP.

Customers with a study details Manual Follow-up (MFU) or a new claim activity on their file are not presented with the task and must submit a JSP online claim.

If the customer confirms they wish to claim JSP, as part of the task they need to schedule a Student to Job seeker Transfer (SJT) participation appointment. Appointments are offered from the first available date after the SED. To be eligible for continuing payment, the customer must attend the SJT appointment within 14 days of their SED.

Advise the customer their Austudy or YA will automatically cancel from their SED +1.

Procedure ends here.

3

Transfer to job seeker for YA students under 22 years at the 'student to job seeker appointment' or on restoration of suspended YA payment + Read more ...

If the customer ceased study and failed to notify cessation of study within 14 days, see the following procedures first:

  • Payment of ABSTUDY when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course, or
  • Step 1 in Table 2 of Payment of Youth Allowance (YA) and Austudy when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course

If the customer is:

  • under 18 years, go to Step 4
  • aged 18 to 21 years, go to Step 5
  • transferring to job seeker aged 22 or over, go to Step 6

4

YA customer under 18 + Read more ...

YA job seekers must undertake approved activities and/or job search to meet their mutual obligation requirements.

Has the customer completed Year 12 (or equivalent) or a Certificate III or higher? (If required, warm hand off to the Youth and Students (YAS) queue).

  • Yes:
    • End the course of study, if required
    • On the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen, ensure the Student Status 'NST' Not a Student
    • If there is no record of Year 12 (or equivalent) or a Certificate III or higher, ensure the Educ Level Attained field is updated correctly. Also, ensure the Y12/Equiv Verified field is coded with an appropriate verification code if the details are confirmed via the Education Course History (EDCH) screen or by evidence provided by the customer
    • Check for Away From Home coding on the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen as customer may have been granted Away From Home rate to study and this may need to be ceased or recoded for job seeker. See Commencement, cessation and coding of the away from home rate of Youth Allowance (YA)
    • If not already run, run the standalone Looking for Work workflow. Check and update the current job seeker registration and check the customer is a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP) and the No Stream Services Referral Required (NSSRR) flag is removed
    • If the job seeker is a Centrelink managed job seeker, see Negotiating Job Plans
  • No:
    • End the course of study, if required
    • On the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen, ensure the Student Status is 'NST' Not a Student
    • If the job seeker has not completed Year 12 (or equivalent) or a Certificate III or higher they will be identified as an early school leaver and must undertake approved activities and/or job search to satisfy their mutual obligation requirements
    • Check for Away From Home coding on the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen as customer may have been granted Away From Home rate to study and this may need to be ceased or recoded for job seeker. See Commencement, cessation and coding of the away from home rate of Youth Allowance (YA)
    • In Customer First, run the standalone Looking for Work workflow and make an Employment Services Provider appointment
    • If the customer is a Centrelink managed job seeker, see Negotiating a Job Plan

Note: as a system requirement, all job seekers under 17 years and 9 months who have not completed Year 12 (or equivalent) or a Certificate III or higher must have an exemption coded to qualify for YA. The system displays a warning message for these customers when no study exemption has been coded and their claim will reject or payment will cancel.

  • End the course of study, if required
  • On the NES screen, ensure the Student Status is 'NST' Not a Student, code Study Exemption Rsn 'YAA' (YAL activity agreement signed)
  • On the Study Exemption End date field, key the YAA exemption for the maximum 6 month period. The system will automatically extend the Exemption End Date to the day before the customers 18th birthday. A warning will show on the Assessment Warning and Error (AWE) screen 'W764NS - The Exemption End Date has been defaulted to the day before the customer's birthday'. No further updates will be required before customers 18th birthday

Record a DOC on the job seeker's record.

Procedure ends here.

5

YA customer 18-21 years + Read more ...

YA job seekers must undertake approved activities and/or job search to meet their mutual obligation requirements.

Has the customer completed Year 12 (or equivalent) or a Certificate III or higher? (If required, warm hand off to the Youth and Students (YAS) queue).

  • Yes:
    • End the course of study, if required
    • On the NES screen, ensure the Student Status is 'NST' Not a Student, Educ Level Attained and Y12/Equiv Verified fields are coded
    • Check the Student End Date (SED) on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen
    • Check for Away From Home coding on the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen as customer may have been granted Away From Home rate to study and this may need to be ceased or recoded for job seeker. See Commencement, cessation and coding of the away from home rate of Youth Allowance (YA)
    • If not already done, run the Looking for Work workflow to register the customer as a job seeker and refer them to an Employment Services Provider if appropriate
    • If the customer is a Centrelink managed job seeker, a Job Plan must be negotiated and completed
    • Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • End the course of study, if required
    • On the NES screen, ensure the Student Status is 'NST' Not a Student
    • Check for Away From Home coding on the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen as customer may have been granted Away From Home rate to study and this may need to be ceased or recoded for job seeker. See Commencement, cessation and coding of the away from home rate of Youth Allowance (YA)
    • Run the standalone Looking for Work workflow
    • Check and update the current job seeker registration and ensure the customer is a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP) and the No Stream Services Referral Required (NSSRR) flag is removed
    • Check and update the JSCI as appropriate
    • Make an Employment Services Provider appointment. If the customer is a Centrelink managed job seeker, a Job Plan must be negotiated and completed
    • Check the system has automatically changed the activity type of FTS to JSE on the AEX screen at the SED+1
    • If the need for an ESAt is triggered and is required when the Looking for Work workflow was completed, advise the customer (where applicable) to get appropriate medical evidence as soon as possible. Evidence must not be supplied later than the lodgement date of their first Reporting Statement, to allow the ESAt referral to be made
    • If the job seeker has already completed Year 12 (or equivalent) or a Certificate III or higher and details are confirmed via the EDCH screen, ensure the Y12/Equiv Verified field on the NES screen is updated with an appropriate code. For example, 'OTH' (other evidence as appropriate)
    • If the job seeker has not already completed Year 12 (or equivalent) or a Certificate III or higher they will be identified as an early school leaver and they will need to undertake study, training, job search or other approved activities to satisfy their mutual obligation requirements. Refer the job seeker to a provider. Ensure they are Stream B as a minimum

Procedure ends here.

6

Customer is 22 years or older + Read more ...

  • If the student payment currently has a status of SUS-PCD:
    • The payment must be manually cancelled to allow the new income support claim to run smoothly
    • End the course of study, if required
    • Code the NES screen with the Event date SED + 1, select the Student Status as 'NST' (Not a Student)
    • Code a cancellation reason of 'NST' (Not a Student) on the Benefit Action (BA) screen
    • The date of event is the date after the customer became aware they were no longer returning to study
  • Advise they will need to lodge an online claim for income support payment

If the customer is:

  • unsuitable or unable to complete an online claim, check Locator for the customer's local service centre for further information to complete an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC)
  • unable to submit their claim due to vulnerable circumstances, see Intent to claim and vulnerable customers
  • a Centrelink managed job seeker, a Job Plan must be negotiated. Negotiate and finalise a Job Plan and advise of mutual obligation requirements in the same contact wherever possible
