Decision Support Tool (DST) questions

The DST is an optional tool to help staff work out if a customer is self-employed or an employee. Customers do not complete the DST.

It uses questions to help staff weigh up all the facts and decide each case on its own merit. It can be used to provide a written history of staff decisions.

DST questions

Table 1: This table contains the questions asked on the DST. The questions help Service Officers to work out if a customer is an employee or self-employed.

Item

Assessment questions to determine if a customer is an employee or in business

Customer response

(circle as appropriate)

1

Does the customer have control over the work (for example, making decisions of what, how, where and authority to set working hours)?

Yes/No

2

Does the customer supply most or all of the plant, equipment or materials (for example, paint, timber, telephone costs)?

Yes/No

3

Does the customer have a responsibility for losses or damages caused by the activity?

Yes/No

4

Does the customer have control of the use of any profit?

Yes/No

5

Is the basis for calculating the payment for work performed, based on a contract for service rather than a contract of service? (contract FOR service = to produce a result; contract OF service = for labour) for example, Payment is based on a result rather than an hourly or daily rate?

Yes/No

If all of the above questions were answered 'Yes' then the customer is more likely to be self -employed.

Otherwise continue with the next lot of questions.

Item

Assessment questions to determine if a customer is an employee or in business

Customer response

(Circle as appropriate)

6

Does the customer have individual contracts for more than one person, business or company?

Yes/No

7

Does the customer lodge a Business Activity Statement (BAS)?

Yes/No

8

Does the customer advertise for their goods or services?

Yes/No

9

Does the customer engage others to do work?

Yes/No

If 6 or more of the above questions were answered 'Yes' then the customer is more likely to be self-employed.

Other supporting evidence: for example, does not receive payslips, does not receive a group certificate, has an ABN.
