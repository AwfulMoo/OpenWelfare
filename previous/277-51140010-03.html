This page contains information for Service Officers to assist the customer to complete the Details of your child support (FA003) form.

Information required on the FA003

This table describes information required to complete the Details of your child support (FA003) form.

Item

Description

1

Who should complete the FA003? + Read more ...

  • If the Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customer is entitled to apply for child support or is receiving child support, they should complete the FA003
  • If the FTB customer's current partner (opposite-sex or same-sex) is entitled to apply for child support or is receiving child support, they should complete the FA003
  • If both are entitled to apply for child support or are receiving child support for the child, they should each complete an FA003

2

Current relationship status + Read more ...

From 1 July 2009, both opposite-sex and same-sex relationships are recognised by Services Australia.

Customers with children from same-sex relationships - may be required to take reasonable action to obtain child support.

Customers need to indicate their current relationship status and also state the date of the change if they are no longer partnered or have become partnered.

3

Name of other parent + Read more ...

The person completing the form needs to provide details of the child's parent from whom they are entitled to obtain child support.

If they are entitled to apply for child support from more than one person, a separate FA003 needs to be completed.

Parent means:

  • a natural parent
  • an adoptive parent, or
  • a person who is legally responsible for a child born through an artificial conception procedure or if a surrogacy court order is in place

They need to state the parent's full name and date of birth, their previous relationship and the date they separated (or never lived together).

If the other parent is deceased, they need to state the date of death and the name of each child the deceased is a parent of. They do not need to answer any further questions. They should go straight to Your statement.

4

Child details + Read more ...

There is space on the FA003 to provide details for two children. If the customer has more than two children from this previous relationship, they should provide details for the other children on a separate sheet of paper.

For each child from this previous relationship, they need to state:

  • the child's full name and date of birth and provide the following information
  • whether or not they have taken action to obtain child support for the child. If they have not taken action, they need to explain why
  • if they have a court order or an agreement for child support registered in a court they should attach a copy if they have not already. If they have not notified Child Support, they need to do so
  • if they have a child support agreement accepted by Child Support, they should attach a copy if they have not already
  • if they do not have an agreement accepted by Child Support or a Child Support assessment, they need to call Child Support
  • if they have any other written agreement for child support they should attach a copy if they have not already, or provide details if unable to attach a copy

5

Accommodation details + Read more ...

In relation to the other parent listed on the form:

  • If the customer owns the home they are living in (includes paying it off):
    • Do they own the home jointly with the other parent? If yes, what is the percentage they own?
    • If there is a mortgage, what is the total amount of the regular mortgage payments? Does the customer pay the total amount? If not, what is the amount they pay and the amount the other parent pays?
  • If they pay rent, board or lodgings for the home they are living in, does the other parent pay any of the rent/lodgings for them? If yes, what amount is paid by the paying parent?
  • If the other parent solely owns the home, they need to estimate the market value of the accommodation (if they can), and also state how much they pay for the accommodation (if any)

6

Cash child support/ spousal maintenance not collected by Child Support + Read more ...

The customer should only provide details of the amount they receive (do not include amounts owed, or amounts received by their current partner) from the other parent listed on the form:

  • the amount they are entitled to receive (the customer can check Child Support letters, or details can be viewed on the Annual Mtce Entitlement for child: field on MNCD screen after selecting the child from the MNGS screen)
  • the amount they actually receive
  • the date they started receiving the payment
  • the customer needs to include separate details for them (spousal maintenance) and for each child (child support)

Non-cash child support/spousal maintenance

The other parent may purchase items or pay expenses that benefit the child. Not all non-cash benefits are assessed as child support.

If the customer has received non-cash benefits as child support/spousal maintenance from the other parent, they need to include separate details for them and for each child:

  • what the non-cash benefit is
  • its value
  • the date they started to receive it

For help, see Non-cash maintenance income assessment for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)

7

Capitalised maintenance + Read more ...

Capitalised maintenance is child support that is not a regular or periodic payment and is worth more than $1500 or a one-off transfer of an asset.

It may be provided as a lump sum payment, or as part of a property settlement (e.g. home, car, business.)

The customer should only include amounts they have not already included. Amounts under $1500 should be included under the cash or non-cash child support/ spousal maintenance questions.

If the customer has received any capitalised maintenance from the paying parent, they need to include separate details for them and for each child (as appropriate):

  • describe the type of capitalised maintenance they have received, and the date they received it
  • the amount and whether or not it was paid under a court order or agreement (if yes, they should provide a copy)
  • the period it is for (the period normally ends when the child turns 18)

Example

In a formal property settlement, half the family home was given to the customer specifically for child support for three children. The value was $60000. It was finalised on 1 January 1998.

Write: $20000 as amount for each child, paid under court agreement, period for each child = 1 January 1998 to the date each turns 18.

See Assessing capitalised maintenance income for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

Lump sum agreement with Child Support

If the customer has a lump sum agreement that has been approved by Child Support, they need to include separate details for them and for each child (as appropriate):

  • the amount
  • if paid under a court order or agreement, the period it is for and the amount

They should attach a copy of the agreement if they have not already provided it.

See Child support agreements and Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

8

Child disability expenses + Read more ...

Child support received for expenses due to a child's disability or learning difficulty are not counted as maintenance income.

These can include medical and pharmaceutical expenses, cost of equipment, home improvements, transport, etc. See Assessing child support received for child disability expenses for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

If the customer receives any child support from the paying parent for expenses due to a child's disability or learning difficulty, they should provide details for each relevant child.

If any of the child support for these expenses is:

  • collected by Child Support, they need to state:
    • the total monthly Child Support entitlement (the customer will find this on the Child Support letter, the Service Officer can view on the MNCD screen, after selecting the child from the MNGS screen)
    • the part ($value) that is provided for the child's disability or learning difficulty
  • received directly from the other parent (privately), they need to state any amounts previously included under other questions

9

Customer's statement and form lodgement + Read more ...

The customer should tick the boxes to indicate any documentation they are providing (e.g. statement for additional children, court order or agreement).

The customer must sign and date the form.

The form can be lodged with family assistance:

  • in person (offer an appointment)
  • by mail
  • by fax

Upon receipt of FA003, see Receipt of claims, forms and documentation for family assistance.
