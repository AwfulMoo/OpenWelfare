2

Crisis Payment (CrP) Claims + Read more ...

A customer may advise they are or were partnered during a claim for CrP, even though they are single on their record and are paid the single rate.

The claim for CrP can be assessed before a MoC/SUOR assessment. CrP eligibility is not affected by relationship status.

Whether or not CrP is granted, a MoC/SUOR assessment is needed for the past period if they:

  • are currently in a relationship not yet disclosed and recorded, or
  • were in an undisclosed relationship for more than 14 days before lodging the CrP claim

The presence of family and domestic violence may indicate a person is not a member of a couple and must be considered when making a retrospective assessment.

See New claim for Crisis Payment under extreme circumstances family and domestic violence for next steps.

Procedure ends here.

3

Relationship decision for new claim + Read more ...

A decision about a customer's relationship status must be made before finalising the new claim.

If a new claim is granted first, complete the MoC assessment retrospectively. This may result in an overpayment for the customer. National feedback should be lodged to alert incorrect processes being followed to avoid the potential for further occurrences and overpayments to customers.

If a SS284/SS293 form is lodged with a new claim, see Confirming the need for a MoC assessment.

After confirming the need for a MoC assessment:

  • MoC Processing staff, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • New claim processing or service delivery staff:
    • before coding a referral, see MoC referral or assessment table
    • referrals created in Process Direct, will hold the claim for 42 days pending the MoC assessment outcome. This does not reflect expected processing time
    • the NCL activity status will update when MoC processing staff complete the assessment
    • procedure ends here

4

Single customer is now living with their ex-partner (SUOR) + Read more ...

There are procedures for a customer experiencing family and domestic violence. If the customer has indicated concern for their or their family's safety refer the matter to a social worker if this has not occurred.

To check if safety concerns have been raised, review the customer's record for:

  • responses to The Living Arrangements question set
  • scanned information and forms
  • the Document List (DL) screen

If speaking to the customer at the time:

  • tell them that because they are sharing accommodation with their ex-partner, there may be a need for a SUOR assessment
  • ask them - 'Would they be concerned about their safety if forms were issued to their ex-partner?'

Are there safety concerns?

  • Yes, do not contact or issue an SS293 form to the ex-partner. Go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Single customer advises SUOR + Read more ...

Confirm that an SS293 form is needed for a SUOR assessment.

Update the Accommodation Details (AC) screen.

Record the customer’s sharer details (ex-partner) and complete the Living Arrangements question set which will trigger an SS293 form.

There are limited circumstances where the system will not automatically navigate to the Living Arrangements question set following an update to the customer’s accommodation details, however an SUOR assessment will be required. Customers in receipt of only:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and/or Child Care Subsidy (CCS) will require a manual issue of the SS293 form/s
  • Carer Allowance (CDA) will require a manual issue of the SS284 or SS293 form/s.

Note: sharers details cannot be updated for a change of address occurring in the future. Tell these customers to re-contact once they have physically moved address.

If it has been confirmed a SUOR assessment is needed and the LA questions cannot be used:

  • Issue the appropriate form and letter. Do not use the Mail Forms guided procedure to issue relationship details forms. It will not issue a letter with enough detail for further action. For example, suspension if they do not return the form
  • Create a review activity on the customer's record. Set this for the allowed time to follow up the return of the form
  • Record details on a DOC using Fast Note. Confirm the form/s were sent to both the customer and ex-partner, or just to the customer (if there are safety concerns). Select Auto text use Assessments and Reviews > Separated Under One Roof > SUOR Progress
  • Include in the DOC the date the customer advises their ex-partner has moved
  • If the customer has identified safety concerns, include this information in the DOC

Procedure ends here until the return of the relevant SS293 form/s.

6

Customer reconciled - SUOR no longer applicable + Read more ...

If a customer currently assessed as SUOR advises they have reconciled with their ex-partner, they are now partnered and their records need to be re-linked.

  • Check the FAL screen and cancel future SUOR review activity if present
  • Record a DOC using Fast Note. Select Auto text use Assessments and Reviews > Separated Under One Roof > SUOR Review not required
  • Document both the customer and partner records, explaining the reason that SUOR has ceased.
    Note: the DOC title on the customer's record will display as SUOR no longer applicable
  • To action updates on the customer's record and link the partner, see Change in relationship status from single to partnered

Note: as the customer’s relationship status will be updated to partnered, there is no need to separately remove the SUR relationship qualifier code from the MS page.

Procedure ends here.

Check risk-based MoC assessment exemption criteria

Table 3: for Member of a Couple (MoC) processing staff only.

Step

Action

1

Conduct risk-based MoC assessment exemption checks + Read more ...

At least one person (customer or other person/ex-partner) must have lodged a completed SS293 or SS284 form. All available information must be used.

A risk-based MoC assessment exemption criteria is met when:

  • one of the low risk exemption criteria are met, and
  • no strong indications of a MoC relationship exist

Note: do not pursue the form from the other person/ex-partner if:

  • one party has lodged the form
  • the customer falls into one of the low risk exemption criteria, and
  • there are no strong indicators present

The 'MoC assessment trigger' is the point in time the SS293 or SS284 form/s were requested due to a change of circumstances or new claim lodgement.

2

Low risk criteria 1 - subsequent change of circumstances within 13 weeks of original MoC assessment trigger? + Read more ...

A change of circumstances may include:

  • either party moving out of the address, or
  • subsequent advice that the customer and other person/ex-partner are now partnered

Note: if either person advises they are now partnered to someone else (not each other), a full MoC assessment may still be required for the retrospective period when the customer and other person/ex-partner stated they were single, depending on the date the relationship commenced.

Has a subsequent change of circumstances occurred within 13 weeks of the initial MoC trigger date?

  • Yes, additional checks to determine if there are strong indications of a MoC relationship are required. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Low risk criteria 2 - Separation details (MOD S) form (with a referee declaration included) received + Read more ...

This criteria only applies when a paper MOD S with a referee declaration included is used to advise of separation as the Update Relationship details online service does not include a referee declaration.

Check Customer First > Document Tools for scanned MOD S.

Has a referee completed Section 2 of the MOD S, confirming the customer has separated from their partner?

  • Yes, additional checks to determine if there are strong indications of a MoC relationship are required. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Low risk criteria 3 - customer has separated for the first time and triggered a SS293 + Read more ...

This criteria only applies to SS293 form lodged which has been triggered from a new claim or advice of separation within the Update Relationship details online service.

To check for online separation details:

  • Go to Process Direct > Customer Summary > Transactions > All Transactions
  • Scroll through recent transactions to locate the online service update. Customer that have advised of separation and SUOR, the transaction notes will appear:
    Submitted dd/mm/yyyy Received dd/mm/yyyy - Customer Update - Advise Separation - Separation - Under One Roof
  • Select the transaction hyperlink to navigate to Customer and Activity Information
  • Open the online separation form via the Open right slider

After checking for online separation details:

  • check the Marital Status (MS) screen in the customer and ex-partner’s record and Document Tools to determine if:
    • the customer and ex-partner have a history of separating and reconciling, and/or
    • they are currently recorded as partnered, or were recorded as partnered at the time of the MoC trigger
  • Separation details via the Update Relationship details online service has been received by either customer or other person/ex-partner for the same MoC trigger, and
    • Responses on SS293 form(s) are:
      Q32 in the 2104 form version or Q33 in the 2107 form version- both the customer and ex-partner (if ex-partner form received) have stated there is no possibility of them getting back together, and/or
      Q34 in the 2104 form version or Q35 in the 2107 form version- the customer and ex-partner have taken steps to formally terminate the relationship. For example, a divorce or revoke a registered relationship, if applicable

Note: if there is any doubt about the validity of the separation details provided by the customer via the Update Relationship details online service, for example, the customer has provided the ex-partner or a close family relative, such as mother as the referee, a full MoC assessment is required.

Is the customer and ex-partner SUOR for the first time?

  • Yes, additional checks to determine if there are strong indications of a MoC relationship are required. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Low risk criteria 4 - future plans to live separately + Read more ...

Check the form(s) to confirm the customer and other person/ex-partner’s response for how long, and the reason they will continue to live together.

For:

  • SS293 form/s, see Q29 in the 2104 form version or Q30 in the 2107 form version
  • SS284 form/s, see Q27 in the 2104 form version and Q28 in the 2107 form version

Future plans to live separately may include plans for one person to move out, or building a self-contained dwelling on the property.

As part of assessing whether the customer meets this criteria, the timeframe and reason for continuation of sharing arrangements should be specific and reasonable:

  • SS284 forms, a reasonable timeframe for sharing is less than 12 months in the future
  • SS293 forms, SUOR is a reviewable circumstance, therefore the timeframe for sharing may be longer, as long as the reason supplied for continuing to live together is clear

If the customer is current on payment and is recorded as partnered, a MOD S is needed before the customer can be unlinked from their partner.

Note: PPP customers that separate and lodge an online claim for PPS, do not need a MOD S.

See Change in relationship status from partnered to single.

Have future plans to live separately been advised and the timeframe is reasonable?

  • Yes, additional checks to determine if there are strong indications of a MoC relationship are required. Go to Step 6
  • No, a full MoC assessment is required, See Step 1 in Table 4

    • If contact is successful, offer a referral to social worker services for support if this has not already occurred

    • If a correspondence nominee exists, ensure a copy of the decision letter is issued to the appointed nominee. See Nominees

  • No family and domestic violence concerns exist:
    • Issue a Q536 letter
    • If a correspondence nominee exists, ensure a copy of the decision letter is issued to the appointed nominee. See Nominees
    • Once the letter has been issued, see Step 9 in Table 5

No, see Step 9 in Table 5

At risk assessment for family and domestic violence + Read more ...

There are procedures to follow for a customer who is experiencing family and domestic violence.

Never contact the alleged perpetrator of family and domestic violence. This may be an ex-partner or another person who may be in a relationship with the customer.

If a customer indicates they have safety concerns, see the risk and referral process if this has not been done.

Are there indications of family and domestic violence?

  • Yes, go to Step 2

2

Indications of family and domestic violence + Read more ...

The presence of family and domestic violence may indicate a person is not a member of a couple and must be considered when making a decision about a customer’s relationship status. To reach a favourable decision (the customer is not a MoC) assessors must be satisfied the customer is not in a MoC relationship with the other person/ex-partner.

If any of the 5 factors are present, FDV may be the affecting the customer's inability to change their circumstances and must be considered. For example, but not limited to:

  • joint accounts may exist due to financial abuse
  • domestic duties such as cleaning, washing etc. may not be split due to the fear of repercussions

If a customer is an FDV situation still considers themselves partnered and will not separate from their partner, this may result in an adverse decision. A social worker should be consulted where the decision maker is considering an adverse decision.

See Background Family and Domestic violence section to determine how the presence of family and domestic violence can be identified.

Information provided by the customer may be very sensitive. It must not be made available to the other person/ex-partner.

As contact must not be made with the alleged perpetrator of family and domestic violence, complete the MoC/SUOR assessment using other means such as Centrelink records, social worker recommendations and if required, contact with the customer's nominated referee.

The customer must be advised that if the other person/ex-partner is a current Centrelink customer, the agency is required to send them a letter notifying the outcome of the MoC assessment. This will let the customer plan for the arrival of the letter in case they are concerned it may affect their safety.

    • indications of family and domestic violence, an assessment can be made from the customer's form alone. See Step 1 in Table 5
    • no indications of family and domestic violence, go to Step 4

Other person/ex-partner has not returned SS284/SS293 + Read more ...

Make 1 genuine attempt to contact them to obtain the required information over the phone. Do not contact the other person/ex-partner if the customer has identified safety concerns against the person for whom the assessment is being conducted.

If contact is successful

Read the following privacy statement and declaration:

Making a decision about a MoC assessment - consider the 5 factors + Read more ...

When deciding whether a customer is a MoC, the Social Security Act 1991 has 5 factors to consider. See the Resources page for information on the 5 factors.

For further information on recording key information, use the MoC decision support template on the Resources page. The details can be transferred into the MoCA Decision script.

Base the decision on all available information, such as:

  • SS284/SS293 form(s)
  • Centrelink records of the customer and the other person (if they have a record), including the DL screen and scanned documents
  • Phone interview
  • External sources if required, such as contact with third parties, for example, social worker or referee. Do not access social media accounts belonging to the customer or other person unless it is part of a compliance investigation

Assess each case on its own merits. Consider sensitivities around sexual orientation, cultural issues, ethnicity, religious beliefs and other vulnerable groups.

After examining the 5 factors and verifying any details, consider all aspects of the case to decide whether the customer is a MoC or not.

Aspects to consider:

  • Is there any family and domestic violence identified? The presence of family and domestic violence may indicate a person is not a member of a couple and must be considered when making a decision about a customer’s relationship status. This includes retrospective assessments required due to claims for Crisis Payment (CrP) as a result of family and domestic violence
  • If additional information was requested which has not been provided (for example, the customer failed to respond to a Q164 request or referee has failed to respond to Q270 request), is there enough information to make a decision?
  • How compelling is the evidence?
  • Is it from an objective, reliable, qualified or credible source?
  • Does the information come from a source first hand or a third party who does not have knowledge first hand of the situation (hearsay)?
  • Does the person providing information have a vested interest in the matter, or is there any malicious intent evident?
  • Does one piece of information support or conflict with other information?
  • Do indicators that the customer is a MoC outweigh the indicators that the customer is not a MoC?
  • Is the customer living with the other person/ex-partner for specific reasons such as work or health?
  • Has the customer admitted they are in a relationship with the other person/ex-partner?
