This page contains information on how to finalise an assessment, complete a Quality Assurance (QA) check of the assessment process and make a recommendation to the delegate for initial, subsequent, S38 response and refund applications, submitted via the MIOC system.

Quality Assurance checks

Finalising the assessment of an MI payment application

Table 1: this table explains how an Assessing Officer (AO) determines the outcome of their assessment and makes a recommendation to the delegate.

Step

Action

1

Determine outcome of the assessment + Read more ...

Once assessment of the application is complete, determine the recommendation to be made to the delegate:

  • Approve – if accepted by the delegate, this application will be paid/recovered. Go to Step 4
  • Deem not payable - if accepted by the delegate, this application will not be paid and a deemed not payable letter will be sent to the applicant. Go to Step 2
  • Request further info – if accepted by the delegate, this application will not be paid/recovered and a Request for further information (S38) letter will be sent to the applicant. Go to Step 3

When the End of Month (EOM) batch runs, notification emails (which are auto populated from the system), will be sent to the applicant’s HPOS mailbox advising them of the outcome of their application.

For Deem not payable and Further info required outcomes, the email will have the relevant letter attached. These letters are manually prepared and uploaded to the application in MIOC.

Payment applications that have an Approve outcome will be paid and the applicant will receive a notification email into their HPOS mailbox with a payment advice of all applications paid.

Refund applications that have an Approve outcome will be recovered, in the form of a deduction from the total payment being made to the Insurer, for the corresponding pay cycle (or delayed for up to 3 pay cycles if insufficient funds are available). Details of the recovery will be included in the payment advice email sent to the applicant’s HPOS mailbox.

2

Deem not payable action + Read more ...

If the application does not meet legislative requirements and the AO deems it not payable, a Deemed not payable letter needs to be drafted for the delegate.

The letter template is stored on the Letters and Electronic Messaging sub-site. To create a Deemed not payable letter:

  • Open the Deemed not payable letter (Z2681)
  • Save the template as ‘Deemed not payable_ARN[Application number]-[Insurer identifier number and letter]-[Scheme identifier letter]’, for example ‘Deemed not payable_ARN6000-1U-H’, in the relevant application folder

Populate the template as required:

  • Enter the date <DD Month CCYY> the Deemed not payable letter is to be sent (this needs to be the EOM date, being the 25th of each month or 18th for December). This date is determined by the MIOC system as a default date (that can be changed by the internal user, if required).
  • Our Reference, key ARN allocated to this payment application
  • Your reference, key the insurer’s claim reference number (noted in the Claim details section of the payment application)
  • Recipient details, key details of the addressee as per the ‘Address and email details for insurers’ document in the MI shared drive:

G:\med_indemn\Claims by ARN\Address and email details for insurers

  • Complete Dear <Title> <First name> <Surname> as provided on the ‘Address and email details for insurers’ document
  • Key the Insured person’s name
  • Complete the scheme name in full and the abbreviation in brackets
  • Complete the paragraph <We wrote to you on …> if a S38 More information required letter was sent to the insurer, note the date the S38 letter was sent. Where an S38 response was received, note details of the information provided
  • Complete the paragraph <We have reviewed …> and enter the scheme name in full and include abbreviation in brackets
  • Enter the <rule/section etc> of the Act that has not been met and <enter details of issues identified resulting in claim deemed not payable>
  • Enter the <First name> <Surname> of the person who will be approving the letter
  • Proof read the letter ensuring all text is the same font, variable fields (in red) have been completed, brackets (< or >) and instructional text (e.g. options in blue) has been removed and all text is black in colour
  • A quality assurance check, see Table 2, and delegate sign off of the letter will be required prior to making a recommendation to the delegate in MIOC. The Deemed not payable letter uploaded to MIOC for delegate approval must be the finalised version of the letter
  • Save the signed letter

Go to Step 4.

3

Further information required action + Read more ...

If further information is required to make a decision about the application, a S38 Further information required letter needs to be drafted for the delegate.

To create a S38 Further information required letter:

  • Open the S38 Further information required letter (Z2680)
  • Select OK to open the letter template
  • Save the template as ‘S38 Further information required_ARN[Application number]-[Insurer identifier number and letter]-[Scheme identifier letter]’, for example ‘S38 Further information required _ARN6000-1U-H’, in the relevant application folder

Populate either letter template as required:

  • Enter the date <DD Month CCYY> the section 38 letter is to be sent (this needs to be the EOM date, being the 25th of each month or 18th for December). This date is determined by MIOC as a default date (that can be changed by the internal user, if required).
  • Our Reference, key ARN allocated to this payment application
  • Your reference, key the insurer’s claim reference number (noted in the Claim details section of the payment application)
  • Recipient details, key details of the contact person as per the ‘Address and email details for insurers’ document in the MI shared drive:
    G:\med_indemn\Claims by ARN\Address and email details for insurers
  • Complete Dear <Title> <First name> <Surname> as provided on the ‘Address and email details for insurers’ document
  • Key the Insured person’s name
  • Complete the details required in either paragraph Option 1, if it is the first request for information, or Option 2, if it is the second request for information. Delete whichever does not apply.
  • Complete the What you need to do paragraph with details of what the applicant needs to provide. Select either Option 1 or Option 2 for details on how to respond. Delete whichever does not apply.
  • Enter the due date for the S38 response in the <request due date> field. This date is determined by MIOC as a default date (that can be changed by the internal user, if required).
  • In MIOC, select Recommended action and in the pop up box select Request further info. The default Due date will be displayed (and can be changed here). The S38 response date noted on the letter must be the same as the Due date in MIOC. The date for the S38 response will be auto populated in the notification email sent to the Insurer.
  • Complete the <First name>, <Surname> of the person who will be approving the letter
  • Proof read the letter ensuring all text is the same font, variable fields (in red) have been completed, brackets (< or >) and instructional text (e.g. options in blue) has been removed and all text is black in colour
  • A quality assurance check, see Table 2, and delegate sign off of the letter will be required prior to making a recommendation to the delegate in MIOC.
  • Save the finalised/signed letter in the relevant application folder in the MI shared drive.

Go to Step 4.

4

Make a recommendation to the delegate + Read more ...

Based on the AO’s findings and assessment of the application, the AO will need to make a recommendation to the Approver (delegate), regarding the outcome of the assessment.

Where a recommendation of Approved has been determined, a QA check may be required prior to the recommendation being made to the delegate in MIOC. Where a recommendation of Deem not payable or Further info required has been determined a full QA check of the assessment process must be completed prior to the recommendation being made to the delegate in the MIOC system. See Table 2.

In MIOC, select Actions from the drop down menu at the top of the application. Select Recommended action and in the pop up box select the appropriate recommended action and complete the comments fields.

If you select Deem not payable:

  • Enter Comments (a maximum of 1000 characters between all users) with information you want to make the Approver (delegate) aware of and refer to the detailed case notes elsewhere in the application if relevant
  • Select a Reason from the drop down list for why the application has been deemed not payable
  • Upload the finalised Deemed not payable letter (created at step 2) by selecting Choose file, and navigate to the application folder
  • Select Recommend action

The application should now have a Recommended action status of Deem not payable in the Approvals screen. The assessment process has now been finalised and the application is ready for approval by the delegate.

If the AO selects Request further info

  • Enter comments (a maximum of 1000 characters between all users) with information to make the Approver (delegate) aware of and refer to the detailed case notes elsewhere in the application if relevant
  • Upload the finalised S38 Further information required letter (created at step 3) by selecting Choose file, and navigate to the application folder
  • Leave the default Due date as it is, unless there is a different due date noted in the letter. If a different date is used in the letter it is important to update the Due date in the modal as this is the date that will auto populate in the notification email that the letter will be attached to
  • Select Recommend action

The application should now have a Recommended action status of Request further info in the Approvals screen. The assessment process has now been finalised and the application is ready for approval by the delegate.

If the AO selects Approve

  • Enter comments if necessary (a maximum of 1000 characters between all users), however, this is optional for an approved application
  • Select Recommend action

The application should now have a Recommended action status of Approved in the Approvals screen. The assessment process has now been finalised and the application is ready for approval by the delegate.

Update the ‘Recommendation’ made column of the ‘MIOC Work on Hand’ document located in the shared drive at:
G:\med_indemn\Claims by ARN\Work on Hand

1

QA checks + Read more ...

Once the application has been assessed and a recommendation determined, it may be necessary for a Quality Assurance (QA) check to be undertaken by another Assessing Officer (AO) to determine if they agree with the original recommendation. A QA check requires the second AO to undertake a comprehensive assessment of the application in order to determine if the original recommendation is appropriate and accurate.

To view and quality assure an application:

  • Open the Medical Indemnity Online Claiming (MIOC) system:
  • From the Home screen > select Approvals from the top task bar
    Note: AOs can also refine the list of applications using the filters (e.g. Insurer, application type, scheme etc.) above the list of current applications
  • Select an application with a recommended action status of Quality assurance
  • Select the Quality assurance radio button under Recommended action to refine the list
  • Select View for the application required

To assess a High Cost Claim Indemnity Scheme (HCCS) Application, see High Cost Claim Indemnity Scheme (HCCS).

To assess a Run-Off Cover Indemnity Scheme (ROCS) Application, see Run-Off Cover Indemnity Scheme (ROCS).

To assess an Incurred But Not Reported Indemnity Scheme (IBNR) Application, see Incurred But Not Reported Indemnity Scheme (IBNR).

To assess an Allied Health High Cost Claim Indemnity Scheme (AHHCCS), see Allied Health High Cost Claim Indemnity Scheme.

If during the QA check the second AO finds errors or discrepancies in relation to the original assessment, the errors/discrepancies should be discussed with the first AO and if necessary the Approver (delegate). Once an agreement has been reached, the AO must update all relevant documentation as required and a recommendation made to the Approver.

Note any comments, issues or updates in the ‘MIOC Work on Hand’ document located in the shared drive at:
G:\med_indemn\Claims by ARN\Work on Hand

Assisted

Manually finalising an assessment

Step

Action

1

MI assessment file note + Read more ...

To create an MI assessment file note for:

  • an initial payment application:
    • open the file note template for the appropriate MI scheme located in the Claims by ARN folder
    • save file note by selecting File > Save As > select the applicable claim folder from Claims by ARN > key file name using the standard naming convention File Note_ARNXXXX-XX-X
    • go to Step 2
  • a subsequent payment application:
    • open the most recent file note for the claim located in the Claims by ARN folder
    • select File > Save As > select the applicable claim folder from Claims by ARN, create a new sub folder for the latest payment application > key file name using the standard naming convention File Note ARNXXXX-XX-X updating with the relevant payment application number
    • review the file note to confirm the details in the summary are correct
    • update the Application Reference Number (ARN), payment application number and amount sought
    • refer to the claim file and list all previous amounts that have been paid and applicable pay run dates
    • go to Step 4
  • for a Section 38 response:
    • open the previous file note for the claim located in the Claims by ARN folder
    • select File > Save As > select the applicable claim folder from Claims by ARN, create a new sub folder for the latest payment application > key file name using the standard naming convention File Note ARNXXXX-XX-X adding S38 response at the end
    • go to Step 4

2

Key claim details into file note template + Read more ...

Complete the following fields of the file note template:

  • ARN, key the relevant Application Reference Number (ARN), this is can found either on the assessment worksheet and on the registration worksheet
  • Linked Files, determine if this is a linked claim by checking the registration or assessment worksheets. If the claim is:
    • linked, key the ARN number(s) of the linked claim
    • not linked, key ‘N/A’
  • Plaintiff, key the plaintiff’s name(s).If there is more than 1 plaintiff, list them all. If the claim is on behalf of the plaintiff, key the representative, for example legal guardian or executor of estate. Note: the plaintiff may be referred to as a ‘Respondent’, ‘Applicant’ and ‘Claimant’. Refer to the supporting documents provided with the claim for the correct terminology
  • Defendant, key member’s name. Where other defendants are listed as parties to the claim, their details must be noted as ‘name (first defendant)', 'name (second defendant)', 'name (third defendant)' as relevant. Refer to the court documents to determine if there are other parties
  • Speciality Code, key the member's speciality code located on the payment application and registration worksheet (see ‘Description’)
  • Date of Notification, key the date of notification and folio number. Refer to the relevant supporting documentation for the correct date
  • Date of Incident, key the date of incident or date range of a series of related incidents and folio number. Refer to the relevant supporting documentation for the correct date.
  • for Run-Off Cover Indemnity Scheme (ROCS) claims, key the ROCS eligible date and ROCS category
  • Application number, for an initial payment application, key 'This is the first application pertaining to this claim.'
  • Amount sought, key the total amount claimed in the payment application (including any claim handling fee)

3

Summary + Read more ...

The summary briefly outlines the medical treatment and other details that lead to the claim. Start each paragraph with the significant date(s). Key the dates in full and bold. Provide a brief description of the details of the alleged negligence of the defendant and resulting injuries suffered by the plaintiff. Use footnotes to define medical terms and include hyperlinks where necessary.

See the Resources page for an example of the summary section in the file note.

4

Assessment of the claim + Read more ...

The ‘Assessment of the claim’ section identifies:

  • associated problems
  • inconsistencies
  • action that has been taken on the claim (for example, legal or policy advice, Section 38), and
  • the outcome of these actions

This part begins with a sentence outlining any settlement or judgment that has occurred within the claim including apportionment details. See the Resources page for an example.

Based on the assessment worksheet and file note, determine an outcome of the claim based on the findings throughout the assessment. This will form the basis of the recommendation to the delegate, to:

  • deem the claim payable
  • request a letter under Section 38 of the Act be sent to obtain further information, or
  • deem the claim not payable

At the end of the file note in the ‘Assessment of the claim’ section, key the following statements for each recommendation:

  • to deem the claim payable:
    • key, 'Assessment of the claim has shown the required criteria have been met and the necessary documentation and financial information has been provided’
  • to request a letter under Section 38 of the Act be sent to obtain further information:
    • key ‘A section 38 letter is required’
    • note the details which will be requested in the section 38 letter
  • to deem the claim not payable, go to Step 8

For subsequent payment applications

  • update the existing filing note based on the above. Go to Step 5

For section 38 responses:

  • continue with the File Note noting details of receipt of the S38 response
  • note what information or documentation has been provided and whether it meets the requirements. Go to Step 5

5

Claim recommendation to delegate + Read more ...

Once the claim has been assessed, the AO must make a recommendation to the Executive Level 2 (EL2) delegate within the section to:

  • deem the claim payable:
    • key, 'I recommend you approve payment of $xxx.xx as all legislative requirements have been met.'
  • request a letter under Section 38:
    • include the recommendation ‘I recommend you sign the attached section 38 letter requesting further information.’
  • deem the claim not payable:
    • include the recommendation ‘I recommend you sign the attached letter advising the insurer that this claim has been deemed not payable.’

Once the file note is complete:

  • check and proofread all documentation is completed during assessment and select ‘save’
  • print a copy of the file note and put on corporate file and folio
  • complete a covering sheet as appropriate (that is, Section 38 Letter Required, Payment Summary, Deemed Not Payable Letter Required)

To prepare:

  • a section 38 letter, go to Step 6
  • a ‘Deemed not Payable’ letter, go to Step 7
  • the file for handover, go to Step 8

6

Complete the section 38 letter + Read more ...

A section 38p letter request can be issued for both initial and subsequent payment applications.

Where assessment of a payment or refund application results in further information being required to satisfy that all eligibility requirements of the Act have been met, the delegate may send a request under section 38 of the Act seeking further information. This request must be made in writing.

To complete the section 38 letter, open the section 38 template for the relevant MI scheme in the Claims by ARN folder, located in the MI shared drive. Save the file in the relevant ARN folder as follows:

  • first s38 letter sent, save filename as '[SCHEME] S38 letter [ARN]'. For example, 'HCCS S38 letter ARN2226-2A-H'
  • second s38 letter sent in relation the payment application, save filename as '[SCHEME] S38 letter [ARN] (2nd)'. For example, ‘HCCS S38 letter ARN2226-2A-H (2nd)’

Update the following fields (highlighted in red):

  • enter date the section 38 letter is to be sent
  • Our Reference, key ARN allocated to this payment application
  • Your reference, key the insurer’s claim reference number (noted on the initial payment application)
  • recipient details, key details of the contact person as per the ‘Address and email details for insurers’ document in the MI shared drive
  • Dear [key contact person], as provided on the ‘Address and email details for insurers’ document
  • date payment application received, key date the payment application was received by Services Australia as per the Registration worksheet or date stamp on the payment application
  • member name, key the member’s name

Go to the second paragraph of the letter:

  • key the details of any issues or discrepancies identified during assessment of the claim and what information is being requested

Note: if more than 1 section 38 letter has been issued in relation to the payment application, include the date the previous section 38 letter was issued.

In the due date field, key the due date for the section 38 response.

Note: the due date must be at least 28 days from the date on the section 38 letter.

Check that the details of the delegate are current and correct.

Save and print the letter:

  • review the letter for spelling and grammatical errors
  • affix a 'sign here' sticker next to the signature block for the delegate
  • Paper clip the letter to the front of the corporate file. Go to Step 8

  • Our Reference, key ARN allocated to this payment application
  • Your reference, key the insurer’s claim reference number (noted on the initial payment application)
  • recipient details, key details of the contact person as per the ‘Address and email details for insurers’ document in the MI shared drive
  • Dear [key contact person], as provided on the ‘Address and email details for insurers’ document
  • date payment application received, key date the payment application was received by Services Australia as per the Registration worksheet or date stamp on the payment application
  • member name, key the member’s name

Go to the second paragraph of the letter:

  • update the template with application specific information including which section of the MI Act has not been met and why

Check that the details of the delegate are current and correct.

Save and print the letter:

  • review the letter for spelling and grammatical errors
  • affix a 'sign here' sticker next to the signature block for the delegate
  • Paper clip the letter to the front of the corporate file. Go to Step 8

8

File handover + Read more ...

Complete the appropriate cover sheet and all relevant fields:

  • Payment Summary
  • Section 38 letter required
  • Deemed Not Payable letter required

Paper clip the cover sheet to the front of the corporate file.

Navigate to HP Records Manager > open HP Records Manager > in the Search By field, select Record Number > in the Equal To field, key the relevant ARN (for example, 'ARN1000-1X-Y') > [Enter] > right click on the record > select Properties:

  • for a payable claim:
  • for Application Status select Pending Delegates approval > for Status Updated select today’s date > OK
  • for a Section 38:
    • for Application Status select Pending Section 38 > for Status Updated select today’s date > OK
  • for a claim deemed not payable:
    • for Application Status select Rejected > for Status Updated select today’s date
    • Rejected Application Reason, select the applicable rejection reason
    • Rejected Application Value, key the total deemed not payable amount
    • select OK

Claims on Hand (amending any details as required)

For a payable claim:

  • locate the payable claim
  • update the status to ‘Assessed’
  • update the outcome to ‘Pay’
  • in the Assess Staff column, select AO initials
  • save and close the spreadsheet

For a section 38:

  • update the status to ‘Assessed’

Place the file in the Quality Assurance (QA) cupboard.

9

Quality checks and obtaining delegate approval + Read more ...

Once the AO has assessed the claim, a Quality Assurance (QA) check is required from another AO.

The second AO is required to:

  • undertake a QA of the MI payment application to determine if they agree with the AO recommendation
  • do a final proofread to ensure formatting, spelling, grammar and financials are correct. If unsure, refer to the Services Australia Writing Style Guide on the Resources page

If during the QA the second AO finds errors or issues in relation to the MI payment application assessment, discuss them with the first AO and if necessary the EL1 Assistant Director. Once agreement has been reached, all relevant documentation must be amended as required.

Once the second AO is sure that the MI payment application has been assessed correctly and the documentation is correct:

  • complete the Assessment Checklist
  • select and initial the QA box on the covering sheet
  • update the Claims on Hand
  • place the file in the EL1’s cupboard for EL1 Assistant Director to conduct a third and final Quality Check (QC) before the claim is handed to the delegate for their consideration

The EL1 checks the entire claim including the payment application, the eligibility for payment, HP Records Manager, the registration and assessment worksheets, the file note and the recommendation.

If any errors are identified at this point the claim is then handed back to the first AO. Once the first AO has amended any errors, the claim undergoes the third QC by the EL1 again.

Once the EL1 endorses and agrees with the recommendation, the EL1 will sign the file note and coversheet, update Claims on Hand to note that the MI payment application has been QC’d and the corporate file is then handed over to the EL2 Delegate, Director of the MI team for approval.

If the Delegate agrees with the recommendation, they will sign the assessment worksheet on the file and coversheet. If the delegate does not agree with the recommendation, the delegate will discuss with the EL1 for resolution of the payment application.

The MI payment application is then ready for either:

  • payment, go to  Processing pay run for Medical Indemnity (MI) payment application, or
  • correspondence, to be sent on the date of the letters. See Monthly Medical Indemnity (MI) administrative process
