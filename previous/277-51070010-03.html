This page contains information on assessing and recording information provided on a Details of your child support (FA003) form for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customers.

Record details of your child support (FA003) form

This table describes the steps involved in processing the Details of your child support (FA003) form.

Step

Action

1

Has form been returned and completed correctly? + Read more ...

Check that all relevant questions have been answered and the form signed. A separate form is needed for each previous relationship. For blended families, both the customer and their current partner will need to complete one. For help, see Completing the Details of your child support (FA003).

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, phone the customer and discuss the Maintenance Action Test (MAT) requirements. If customer needs to take action to obtain child support, advise details and offer a warm transfer to Child Support
    • If the offer to lodge a child support application is accepted, go to Step 4
    • If the customer cannot be contacted, record details on a DOC. Note: where there is a requirement to obtain child support, a Maintenance Action Requirement letter will automatically issue to the customer if they are within the 13 week (91 day) 'DMA' (Deciding Maintenance Action) grace period when the FTB claim is granted or at completion of a circumstance change
    • If the customer does not wish to take action, record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

2

Group children on customer's record + Read more ...

Once the customer's record has been created and all children linked to the customer, check that all children are placed in a correct group on the Maintenance Entitlement Group Summary (MNGS) screen.

When adding a child, the preference is to place the child:

  • in a group with the other parent's name against it (option 1)
  • in a group with any full sibling (option 2), or
  • in a new group if the above options do not exist

3

Is action to obtain child support needed? + Read more ...

See Maintenance Action Test (MAT) for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customers.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, advise customer they are not required to apply for child support for the relevant child/ren. Check and, if needed, update the MAT code for each child to show why the customer doesn't need to obtain child support. Note: If they are entitled to apply for child support they may, for example a non-parent carer may want to seek maintenance from the child's parent/s but this is not a requirement to satisfy the MAT. Go to Step 6

4

Check child support action + Read more ...

Has the customer (or current partner) taken appropriate action to obtain child support yet?

Note: initial action must be taken within the 13 week (91 day) timeframe to take action to obtain child support for FTB Part A to continue at more than base rate.

  • Yes, check and update the MAT code if necessary to reflect the action the customer (or current partner) has taken
  • No, advise customer of the MAT and what they need to do to continue to be paid more than the base rate of FTB Part A for child. Customers may need to apply for a child support assessment. Update the MAT code for each child to show what action is being taken
    • Where there is a requirement to obtain child support, a Maintenance Action Requirement Notification letter will automatically issue to the customer if the customer is within the 13 week (91 day) 'DMA' (Deciding Maintenance Action) grace period when the FTB claim is granted or at completion of a circumstance change

5

Private child support + Read more ...

Entitlement and disbursement information is electronically transferred from Child Support to Centrelink for mutual customers.

From 1 July 2012, customers with a private collect Child Support assessment are deemed to be receiving their full child support entitlement. Private collect customers that collect more than the child support assessed amount will only need to have this information recorded in limited situations.

Does the customer have a formal arrangement for child support and the amount is collected privately? (Child support assessment or child support agreement, or a court order or court registered agreement).

  • Yes, check that the details are provided in the private maintenance income section. Request a copy of the court order/agreement or contact Child Support to clarify details of an agreement registered with them. If the customer is not receiving their full child support entitlement, they may wish to apply for Child Support collection or apply for a partial or full exemption from obtaining child support. For further information, see:
    • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customer not receiving their full entitlement privately
    • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customer has a court order or court registered agreement
    • Child support agreements in Maintenance Income Test (MIT) for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Note: any non-cash component needs to be recorded for both private and Child Support collection customers
  • No, check that the customer has provided details of any child support they receive privately

6

Does customer (or partner) receive private maintenance income? + Read more ...

Child support income received for a child support child is assessed under the Maintenance Income Test (MIT). Child support received before completion of the Child Support assessment is recorded as voluntary maintenance income once the customer's record is unlinked from the paying parent's record.

  • Yes,
    • assess non-cash maintenance income
    • for a child support agreement, any non-cash component needs to be recorded for both private and Child Support collection customers
    • record an exemption for maintenance income received for a child's disability. See Assessment of child support received for child disability expenses for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • record private maintenance income on Centrelink systems
  • No, Child Support will pass information to the FAO system about entitlement and amounts collected

7

Record details and finalise activity on assessment + Read more ...

Record details on a DOC and finalise activity on Assessment Results (AR) screen.

If FA003 form is lodged with Family Tax Benefit claim or Parenting Payment claim, place with claim on the customer's file. Otherwise batch store the form.
