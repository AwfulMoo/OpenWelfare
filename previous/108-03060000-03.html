Investigate change of DFISA rate

Step

Action

1

Customer contacts about a change in their DFISA rate + Read more ...

For periods of entitlement before 1 January 2022, DFISA will be automatically recalculated when there is a change in certain circumstances affecting the rate of the customer's pension or benefit. This rate recalculation occurs on the Services Australia system.

When there is a DFISA rate change, the new amount(s) will be transmitted to DVA, where the customer's DFISA payments are adjusted and paid by DVA accordingly. DFISA is generally paid by DVA fortnightly on the DVA payday. DFISA adjustments can result in a debt or an arrears payment with DVA.

DFISA may still be payable even if a customer's income support rate is nil. If their DFISA rate is greater than zero, the customer will be regarded as being current on their income support payment (CZR - Current Zero Rate), and all normal obligations will apply in order to remain eligible for that payment.

Check the customer's record for a change of circumstances which has led to the rate change.

Screens to check initially are:

  • DFISA Summary (DFS)
    • The customer's rate of DFISA can be viewed on the DFS screen, and is shown as a daily rate
    • Multiply the daily rate by 14 to obtain the fortnightly rate, which will enable Service Officer to verify with the customer the DFISA rate changes they are querying
    • the DFS screen also allows Service Officer to identify which activity number (AMR) caused the change to the DFISA rate for a particular date of effect
  • Event Summary (ES)
    • Search for the activity number (AMR) identified in the DFISA Summary screen on the ES screen, select and press Enter
    • Type 'Y' in the 'View all screens' box to view what changes were made within the activity that caused the DFISA rate change
  • Document List (DL)
    • On the DL screen, check any relevant DOCs relating to the activity to find additional information regarding the cause of the DFISA rate change

Was the cause of the DFISA rate change found and is customer satisfied with the explanation?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Investigate DFISA rate change further + Read more ...

Check the following screens:

  • Rate explanation
    • This may help confirm exactly where a rate change occurred
    • Check Pensions Rate Calculation (PRC) or Factors Affecting Rate (PFAR) screens (pensions); Rate Explanation (NREX) screen
  • Income and assets summary
    • This summary can assist in identifying any change to the amount of income and assets that caused the DFISA rate change
    • Check Pensions Income and Asset Summary (PIAS) screen (pensions system); Newstart Income Summary (NIS) screen (Newstart system)
  • Employment Income Summary (EANS)
    • Changes in employment income can cause a change to the rate of DFISA
    • Check EANS screen
  • Other income and assets
    • See Income & Assets Main Menu (IMM) for a full list of screens to help investigation
  • Marital status (MS)
    • A change in relationship status can cause a change to the rate of DFISA
    • Check MS screen
  • Accommodation Summary (ACS)
    • Check the ACS screen for a change to accommodation circumstances
    • Changes to DFISA as a result of a change to rent assistance require a complex calculation which should be transferred to the DVA Clearance Team (SCS)
  • Customers on stimulus reporting
    • Check Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) and Reporting Regime Summary (RPRS) screens for changes to reporting regimes
    • For stimulus reporters, a zero DFISA rate is transmitted to DVA at the start of every entitlement period before 1 January 2022
    • This occurs in the background and the DFISA Summary (DFS) screen will show that DFISA is still being paid
    • The customer may receive a letter from DVA advising that their DFISA has stopped - in this situation, DFISA will be calculated, transmitted to DVA, and paid when the customer reports at the end of the entitlement period
  • Transitional rate customers
    • Check the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) screen to see whether the customer has recently moved from transitional rules to the current means test (new rate)
    • If so, this requires a complex calculation which should be transferred to the DVA Clearance Team

Is Service Officer able to explain the DFISA rate change to the customer and is customer satisfied with the explanation?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, need further assistance to investigate the DFISA rate changes, or customer wants a detailed rate explanation. Refer to DVA Clearance Team
    • Announce transfer the call via Services Australia Workspace option DFISA
    • Service centre staff call the DVA Clearance Team

3

DVA Clearance Team + Read more ...

Investigate rate change.

Is further follow-up required with the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) Income Support Team?

  • Yes, contact DVA ISP via Services Australia Workspace option LPOOL to DVA
  • No, provide detailed explanation. Procedure ends here
