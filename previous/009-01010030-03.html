Carer contacts about their CP and/or CA claim + Read more ...

Check for a claim

For online claim, check Claim in Progress Notes in Customer First via CRN/BP hyperlink > Cases tab > Super case for business partner hyperlink > Notes tab:

  • use Search Online Claims under Workspace > Claims Online to find the online claim. View outstanding actions required by the customer to have their claim assessed under the Customer Task heading. See Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM)
  • If the claim status is showing as Draft, the customer has started the Online Claim but the claim has not been submitted. Tell the customer to return to their Centrelink online account and submit the claim. Do not change the claim status from Draft
    Note: if the Claim Submission Date reads 00:00:0000, the online claim has not yet been submitted

For paper claim, check Document Tools in Customer First for a scanned paper claim or documentation

Note:

  • If a CP and/or CA claim has been submitted and the care receivers’ POI is displayed as a supplementary task and POI is not provided, request the care receivers’ POI and place claim on hold. Only reject claim ‘Failed to Reply to Correspondence’ (FRC) if the carer has not replied to the request for further information
  • When more information is given verbally by the carer to support their claim, update Notes in Process Direct with the details provided

If a claim is allocated in Process Direct and has a status of 'in process' and has not been submitted, Service Officers will be required to:

  • Check all required documents have been lodged
  • When all required documents have been lodged, contact the carer and encourage them to submit their claim online
  • If the carer declines to submit an online claim, a Service Officer must use ACC to submit a claim on behalf of the carer. A verbal declaration is required to be accepted prior to the claim being submitted. The Service Officer must:
    • read the verbal declaration to the carer, ensuring they accept the statement ‘by ticking the Declaration box on the Review your claim page, the Service Officer is confirming that the declaration script has been read to and accepted by the customer’

Once the verbal declaration is accepted and ACC submitted a DOC containing this information will be displayed automatically on claim submission. This DOC will display in:

  • The Notes section of Process Direct and Super Case (notes) of Customer First
  • The Customer Claim Summary

If the verbal declaration is unable to be made, the Service Officer must do 1 of the following:

  • invite the customer to log into their online services, accept the customer declaration and submit their claim
  • Print the Customer Declaration Form (CDF) via the printer icon on the right of the Review your claim page. The claim must not be submitted without the CDF being completed and signed
    • Ask the carer to sign the CDF and scan the CDF to the carer's record
  • Send the CDF to the carer and ask them to sign the form. Once the verbal declaration is accepted and ACC submitted, a DOC containing this information will auto-display on claim submission. See Claiming CP and/or CA

Tell customer of timeframes for claim outcome

Use the following script:

‘Thank you for lodging your xxxxxxxx claim. Please be assured we are processing claims as quickly as possible to manage the high demand across the business. We will let you know the outcome as soon as it is complete. If you have been requested to provide information, it is important that you do so as soon as possible so as not to delay your claim. Thank you for your patience.'

For claims that are outside timeliness standards, consider if escalation is required.

A claim (Social Application) is created in Process Direct when an online claim is submitted or when paper claim details are entered using ACC. A status of Completed means the claim has been processed and finalised.

New paper claim scanned - no progress on record.

Check the Document Tool in Customer First for the claim documents to determine if they have been scanned incorrectly.

If there is a scan available and no claim activity and/or Progress of Claim Notes on the record:

  • Refer to Carer Processing (CD1)
  • Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Carers > Update > CA Update - Action Required. Complete all fields. Indicate 'No Progress Doc on record'/No Progress Notes available in Process Direct or 'No CP/CA NCL activity on record'
  • include details of why the carer made contact

Tell the carer:

  • 'Your claim has been referred for processing. A Service Officer will contact you if they need further information. Calls from us will display as a private number. Please answer this call.'

Claim rejected

If the carer is enquiring about the rejection of their claim which occurred:

  • within the last 13 weeks, go to Step 4
  • more than 13 weeks ago, they can request an explanation or apply for a formal review of the decision. See Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process. Procedure ends here

2

Initial assessment + Read more ...

Initially, claims will be assessed for immediate eligibility, the requirement to provide more information and/or referral to a specialist.

Carers may be asked to supply more medical or non-medical information at the initial assessment and will be asked to supply this within 14 days.

If a claim is submitted under an exception category, the claim cannot be rejected until 22 days has been reached. If the claim is presented via WLM and 22 days has not been reached, the claim will need to be held until day 22 before it can be rejected.

The claim can be rejected at any time when:

  • a claim has been submitted
  • documents lodged are inappropriate or not relevant to the claim
  • the claim has not been lodged under an exception category
  • the carer has failed to respond to a request for information

For requests sent by Australia Post, a reasonable time to comply is 22 days (14 days plus an extra 8 days) from the date of the original notice.

The original documents can be requested again with the claim placed on hold for 14 days when:

  • a claim has been submitted, and
  • documents lodged are not relevant to the claim
  • the carer believed they were lodging the correct information

If the care receiver's POI is a supplementary task for a submitted CP and/or CA claim and the POI is not provided, it must be requested and the claim placed on hold. Do not reject the claim 'FRC' unless the carer has failed to reply to the request for further information.

Note: in some cases, when a carer uploads a care receiver's POI, this displays on the care receiver's record. Check the carer receiver's record before rejecting the claim.

Example:

  • Day 0: Carer submits a complete claim with all required documents scanned to the carer's record
  • Day 5: Claim allocated to a staff member who identifies the carer has not lodged the correct documents. If the documents provided:
    • are what was requested, process the claim
    • are incorrect but relate to the documents requested, ask for the relevant information and allow 14 days
    • are incorrect and do not relate to the documents requested, reject the claim 'FRC'. Do not wait until day 22. If the rejection reason is 'FRC' or 'FSD', a decision DOC must list all the documents not provided at the time of the rejection

Check the CP/CA New Claim (NCL) progress DOC or Notes in Customer First. Tell the carer what is outstanding or must be provided (if applicable).

If the carer is calling to advise they have more information to support their claim, provide the following information:

  • 'Lodge your forms and/or documents online via Upload documents or at your local service centre as soon as you can. Identity Documents must be confirmed at your local service centre only. Providing the information as early as possible means your claim can be considered without further delay.'

Note: Urgent or Financial Hardship claim referrals cannot be completed if it has been determined the claim is not ready to process (not all outstanding documents have been returned).

3

Claim ready to be processed + Read more ...

If the carer is enquiring about the progress of their claim following lodgement, provide the following information:

  • 'Thank you for lodging your Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance claim. Please be assured we are processing claims as quickly as possible to manage the high demand across the business. We will let you know the outcome as soon as it is complete. If you have been requested to provide information, it is important that you do so as soon as possible so as not to delay your claim. Thank you for your patience.’

Note: check the documents provided by the carer. If the carer has provided all requested documents, take the claim off hold and change the status to ‘In progress’ to allocate the claim for processing (via Workload Management).

4

Claim rejected in the last 13 weeks + Read more ...

If the claim was rejected FRC, FSD or POI

The claim can be re-opened and the customer could be paid from the original start date if all of the following are met:

  • the claim was rejected because the customer did not supply the documentation requested, and
  • the customer was entitled to Carer payment and/or Carer Allowance at the time they made the claim or early claim provisions apply, and
  • the customer provided all the requested documentation within 13 weeks from the date the notice was given to reject the claim

Create an open work item. Use Fast Note - select CAR for Carer Payment or CDA for Carer Allowance, select Auto text use Generic > Claims > Reassess Rejected Claim Template > Confirm > Edit auto text > Transfer to: CD1 for CP and/or CA claims > Continue.

Change of circumstances

If the claim was rejected for reasons other than FRC, FSD or POI, check if there has been a change in circumstances since rejection which will allow the claim to be granted. For example, reduced income, further medical evidence.

If there has been a change which will allow the claim to be granted:

  • create an open Work Item
  • use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Carers > Update > CA Update/CP Update - Action Required. Complete all fields. Applicable keywords will be included
  • ensure Fast Note is with region CD1, noting the carer's circumstances have changed and contact is required to discuss matter

Note: if the change in circumstances is within 13 weeks of when the claim was lodged, the early claim provisions may apply.

Tell the carer:

  • 'A Service Officer will contact you, calls from us will display a private number. Please answer this call.'

No change in circumstances

Check the rejection DOC or Notes in Customer First. Discuss the decision with the carer, including their review and appeal rights.

If the carer does not understand or does not agree with the decision, they can request an explanation or apply for a formal review of the decision. See Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process.

Procedure ends here,

5

Financial hardship/ 'special circumstances' + Read more ...

Consider 'Special Circumstances' for CP and/or CA claims when the carer or care receiver has a terminal condition or has recently passed away.

When a carer indicates they are experiencing financial hardship and requests urgent processing of a new claim, conduct an assessment for financial hardship.

  • Carers getting another income support payment are not considered to be in hardship nor the claim urgent
  • While CA is not considered an income support payment for 'hardship' provisions there are exceptions to this and it may be considered under 'special circumstances' provisions. An example of this is when the carer or care receiver is terminally ill or has recently passed away
  • Carers who have income from employment/self-employment or another source are generally not considered to be in hardship

See the Resources page for an example of a Progress of Claim DOC.

When reviewing the carer's record, determine if an alternate income support payment should be offered.

If the claim has been streamed, check the carer's record and Progress of Claim DOC/Notes to determine if all the requested information/forms have been have been returned to allow urgent finalisation. This can include:

  • Completed Claim
  • Medical Reports
  • Income and Asset Form (SA369) and associated modules
  • Internal referrals available and finalised

All scanned documents can be viewed via Document Tools on the carer's record.

If all of the requested information is not available on the carer's record at the time of assessing financial hardship, tell the carer the claim will be given priority when all of the requested information has been provided.

Offer the carer a referral to community agency for assistance due to financial hardship if required.

All required information is available

Carers who are not eligible for an alternative income support payment and meet financial hardship criteria can be referred for urgent processing provided the claim is actionable, that is, all information is available.

Create an open work item. Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Generic > Urgent Claim Processing > Carer Payment (if request is for Carer Allowance, add this in the free text section of the Fast Note) > Confirm

If required tell the carer:

  • 'Your claim has been referred for urgent processing. A Service Officer will contact you when finalising your claim to advise of the outcome. Calls from us will display as a private number. Please answer this call.'

A work item for urgent claim processing must not be created in the following scenarios:

  • carer is current on another income support payment. These claims are not considered to be in hardship or urgent as they are in receipt of another income support payment
  • carer is not current on another income support payment and the claim is within KPI and has no hardship indicator

Note: financial hardship provisions do not apply to carer’s transferring from 1 income support payment to another, unless their previous payment was cancelled more than 14 days ago.
