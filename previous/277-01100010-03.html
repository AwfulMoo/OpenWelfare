1

Customer or authorised representative contacts Child Support + Read more ...

When a customer or representative contacts Child Support by phone or face-to-face:

  • provide an effective introduction. See Contact with Child Support customers
  • conduct an identity check, see Identity Confirmation for Child Support, and
  • check if the customer is managed by another area

If the customer has been delivered to the wrong business area, for example due to not entering their CSID:

  • encourage them to enter their CSID when prompted so they will be automatically directed to the appropriate area in future
  • tell them the call will be transferred to the appropriate area, and
  • cold transfer the call to the correct area using the routing tab in Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone, ensuring the 'POI done' checkbox is selected

In some situations a warm transfer may be more appropriate, examples are:

  • Managing escalated customers
  • Ensuring customers are not repeatedly cold transferred between areas
  • To prevent customers having to repeatedly explain the purpose of their call

For more about what can be exchanged with an authorised representative, References has a link to the Guide 6.3.3 Authorised Representatives.

Correspondence received

When a customer or representative sends written correspondence via fax, email or post, undertake the following:

  • Review recent case events. Scrutinise the correspondence received ensuring it is correctly categorised. See Handling mail received in Child Support
  • Contact the customer or representative if necessary to discuss or clarify issues they have raised. Re-categorise the correspondence if appropriate
  • Address all matters raised in the correspondence. If a letter has not been issued as a result of the actions, contact the customer or representative to discuss further. See Contact with Child Support customers

Delete intray after all issues raised have been addressed.

Do not re categorise or delete correspondence received intrays inappropriately. Doing this may have a detrimental effect on customers and cause distress. It can result in financial impacts and cause delays in processes such as Change of Assessment (CoA) and Part6A Objections.

Go to Step 2.

2

Identify presenting issues + Read more ...

Common and Specialised Issues

Identify why the customer has contacted Child Support. Use the list of Common and specialised activities to determine the issues to be managed or if referral to a specialist area is required. See Common and specialised activities for Child Support staff.

Issues must be managed at the point of entry wherever possible, regardless of whether the customer is already locked to another Service Officer.

Where there are multiple issues, the appropriate approach depends on the combination of common and/or specialised issues:

  • multiple common issues are managed by the owning business area. This is generally where the customer first presents
  • combination of common and specialised issues:
    • if the customer is already locked to a specialist area, the specialist area will manage all specialised and common issues, including Minimum Collection Activity
    • if the customer is not locked to a specialist area, common issues will be managed at the point of entry, including Minimum Collection Activity. Referral to the appropriate specialist area can occur once common issues have been progressed as far as possible in real time
    • specialist areas will take on outstanding common activities once a customer is locked to that area
  • multiple specialised issues: a collaborative customer management approach applies between Service Officers in each specialist area

Where a customer has multiple specialised issues, go to Step 4.

Note: sometimes customers present issues that Child Support is unable to help with, however these issues may hinder the customer's ability to manage their child support. Service Officers are responsible for identifying these customer issues and providing appropriate external referrals to organisations that can assist the customer. See Social worker and other service referrals for child support customers.

If a customer identifies a non-child support related issue, consider if they should be referred to 1 of the external referral options identified on the Customer Support/Referral Guide. This may occur before or after the child support issues are addressed.

See Customer Risk Identification Model for conversational and administrative cues to help identify the most appropriate referral option.

Correspondence received

  • Where alternative customer contact arrangements, special or preferred contact preferences has been provided update the Profile window
  • When contact with the customer or representative is necessary to discuss or clarify issues they have raised, see Contact with Child Support customers
  • If unable to contact customer, send MX1-1 Request for client to contact CSA Letter
  • If customer wants written correspondence only, send a unique letter. See Step 2 Table 3 in Letters for Child Support customers

Do not re categorise or delete correspondence received intrays inappropriately. Doing this may have a detrimental effect on customers and cause distress. It can result in financial impacts and cause delays in processes such as Change of Assessment (CoA) and Part6A Objections.

Has an error been identified?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, see the Customer Management Approach (CMA) process table

3

Error identified + Read more

When speaking with a customer there may be occasions where a Service Officer identifies an error, or that another Service Officer has not correctly followed the CMA. In these circumstances, the priority is to manage and resolve the customer issues at the point of entry wherever possible.

Where an error is identified the Service Officer will undertake the following:

  • apologise for any errors that have occurred
  • if the customer wants to make a complaint, follow the guidelines in the Managing customer complaints and feedback , and
  • take all steps to progress and/or resolve the issue in real time wherever possible

Where the issue is unable to be resolved in real time:

  • if the customer is already locked to another Service Officer, the Service Officer that identifies the error will take all necessary information and refer any follow up to the locked Service Officer for action
  • if the customer is not locked to a Service Officer, the Service Officer will lock the customer and manage all outstanding issues, including resolution of any errors as per Correcting errors on Child Support cases. In this situation, customers will not be locked back to the previous Service Officer

Internal Feedback

Where it is considered that the previous Service Officer would benefit from receiving feedback, staff can choose to send using the feedback tool. Before sending feedback, consider how it will contribute to improved customer service into the future.

4

Undertake collaborative customer management + Read more ...

Undertake a collaborative approach to customer management which involves

  • determining customer ownership, go to Step 5
  • transferring customer ownership to relevant specialised areas, go to Step 6
  • providing a holistic service, go to Step 7

5

Collaborative customer management - Determine customer ownership + Read more ...

Where there are multiple specialised issues, the following areas will generally retain ownership due to the nature of the issue or circumstances of the customer. In priority order these areas are:

Business Area

  • Deceased Customer Management - payee and/or payer parent will stay locked
  • Personalised Services - payee and/or payer will stay locked
  • Litigation - payee and payer will stay locked
  • Departure Prohibition Order Team - payer will stay locked
  • Intensive Collection Services - payer will stay locked
  • New Customers (pending registration) - payee and payer will stay locked

If none of the above areas need to retain ownership, the customer should be locked to the area where the first specialised issue was received.

6

Collaborative customer management - Transferring customer ownership between specialised areas + Read more ...

Ownership can also be transferred by agreement if this is likely to result in a better service and/or customer outcome. Consider the following when determining whether to transfer ownership:

  • amount of contact the issues are likely to generate - where 1 specialised issue is likely to generate significant customer contact, consider locking the customer(s) to the specialist area responsible for that issue, to minimise the number of call transfers required when the customer contacts Child Support
  • length of time the issues will take to resolve - where 1 issue is likely to take a longer period of time to resolve, consider locking the customer to the specialist area responsible for that issue, to minimise changes in ownership as other issues arise and are finalised

Where it is in the best interest of the customer to be locked to another specialist area, contact the other area, by phone wherever possible, to determine who will retain customer ownership. This can be done at any time. If agreement about customer ownership cannot be reached, escalate the issue to the Team Leader.

Note: customers must not be advised of, or involved in, discussions about ownership.

7

Collaborative customer management - Provide a holistic service + Read more ...

When retaining customer ownership and a second (or subsequent) specialised issue arises, advise the customers:

  • they may need to be referred to another area with specialised skills and knowledge to address the other issue. This means they may deal with more than 1 person to provide the best possible service, and
  • when they call in future they will be directed to the same area if their CSID is entered

Review the customer's circumstances and if any related or underlying issues are identified consult with the other specialist area to determine the best way to manage the customer.

Note: where a customer is managed by Personalised Services (PS), PS will always co-ordinate the liaison with other business areas to determine the best way to manage the customer issue.

Customer Management Approach (CMA) process

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Complete Minimum Collection Activity + Read more ...

During every interaction, check income, care levels, and payment up-front to determine if the right amount of child support is being paid.

Complete Minimum Collection Activity and use the Collection and debt macro in all situations where:

  • income is unreliable, and/or
  • there is a payer debt with no payment arrangement, or
  • there is an overpayment with no payment arrangement and Service Officer is speaking with the overpaid customer

Minimum Collection Activity includes:

  • discussing the importance of a correct assessment and updating incomes and care where new information is available, and
  • negotiating debt repayment with paying parents
  • gathering collection information from receiving parents where collection is a presenting issue, or
  • negotiating repayment of recoverable overpayment

This is the Minimum Collection Activity that all staff complete in real time or by simple follow-up action.

Some staff may undertake more than Minimum Collection Activity in certain situations for example as part of pro-active collection work.

2

Check income + Read more ...

Check the customer income to determine if it is reliable. A reliable income is defined as any ATO issued income.

If the income is reliable, confirm that it is still accurate. Go to Step 3.

If the income is not reliable:

  • discuss the current income being used for the assessment
  • discuss the importance of a reliable income including:
    • making sure the right amount of child support is paid/received, and
  • impacts of not lodging tax returns on time, for example inability to backdate a lower income, possible over/under payment from higher incomes
  • gain a commitment from the customer to lodge their tax return, and
  • update the income in Cuba, or if necessary, ask the customer to call back with income. See Income processing for Child Support customers

Note: where the customer has called specifically to address incomes, take action to resolve the customer's issue.

3

Check payment of child support and debts + Read more ...

If there is child support debt or an overpayment without a payment arrangement, determine the role of the customer:

  • payer, go to Step 4
  • payee, go to Step 5

4

Customer has a role as payer + Read more ...

If the payer has:

  • a child support debt, follow the process in Debt repayment (CS)
  • overpaid child support, follow the process in Information for payers about child support overpayments

A past role reversal could result in an overpayment on a payer's record. Is this the case?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Customer has a role as payee + Read more ...

If the payee:

  • has an overpayment debt, follow the process in Child support overpayments and payee debt
  • is owed child support and enquiring about the progress of collection action, to gather collection information using the Collection and debt macro

Use discretion to determine if Level 1 searches are required.

If new collection information is available, take action based on 1 of the following:

  • Payer not locked:
    • if the information is likely to lead to a collection outcome, lock the payer
    • if the information is unlikely to result in a collection outcome, there is no need for further action
  • Payer locked to another Service Officer:
    • if the information is likely to lead to a collection outcome undertake appropriate investigation and actions
    • if the information is unlikely to result in a collection outcome, there is no need to refer the information for further action

If new collection information is not available, no further collection action is required.

Manage customer expectations:

  • payer, go to Step 6
  • payee, go to Step 7

6

Manage customer expectations - payer + Read more ...

Manage customer expectations regarding the outcome of Minimum Collection Activity

Always use the Collection and debt macro.

If the payer has:

  • overpaid child support, follow the process in Information for payers about child support overpayments
  • a child support debt, explain the following
  • the need to continue to pay their child support in full and on time
  • payment arrangement details for the ongoing liability and any debt
  • consequences of not paying their child support on time (LPP and possible enforcement actions)
  • any follow up required on their part, and
  • the need to contact Child Support if there are any changes to their circumstances

Go to Step 8

7

Manage customer expectations - payee + Read more ...

Manage customer expectations regarding the outcome of Minimum Collection Activity

If a payee is owed child support, explain the following:

  • Child Support's commitment to collecting their child support
  • when Child Support is unable to take collection action due to no new information
  • follow up on collection information and likely timeframes that will apply
  • any follow up required on their part, for example calling back to provide income details
  • the need to contact Child Support if they have any new collection information, or changes to their circumstances
  • Child Support's commitment to monitor for new information and take action if possible

If a payee owes an overpayment debt, explain the following:

  • If they have an ongoing entitlement to receive child support we will automatically recover the overpaid amount from that entitlement
  • If they do not have an ongoing entitlement, any payment arrangement details
  • Consequences of not paying, soon we will use enforcement powers to collect overpayments
  • any follow up required on their part, and
  • the need to contact Child Support if there are any changes to their circumstances

8

Document Minimum Collection Activity + Read more ...

Document the outcome of Minimum Collection.

  • Payers in the Collection Window:
    • Category: Level 1
    • Action: payee/payer Contact
    • Outcome: payee/payer successfully contacted
  • Payees in the payee/payer Communication window or if an arrangement is negotiated in the receiving Payment Arrangement window

Document the detail of any other actions taken in the relevant Cuba windows, for example Level 1 searches, payment arrangements and income updates.

See Documenting Child Support information.

9

Progress issues and real time management + Read more ...

Progress the customer's issues in line with the relevant Blueprint document.

Real time management

Real time management is when an issue is progressed and/or completed while the customer is on the phone. Address customer issues in real time wherever possible.

If the issues:

  • can be addressed in real time, see the Finalise issues table
  • cannot be addressed in real time, go to Step 10

10

Customer locking + Read more ...

If an issue cannot be addressed in real time and a customer requiring locking is already locked to another Service Officer:

  • check the notes on the case, including the Collection Window
  • take all relevant information and complete as much action as possible in real time, including pending any actions in Cuba
  • clearly document actions and decisions, and
  • refer any follow up action to the locked Service Officer via an appropriate intray

Note: it may be determined that it would provide a better customer service outcome to lock the customer(s) to the Service Officer for follow up, rather than referring follow up to the locked Service Officer. If this occurs, lock the customer(s) to the Service Officer and notify the other Service Officer by phone where possible. See Transfer or lock a customer Cuba Process Help.

If the customer is not locked to another Service Officer, lock the customer(s) needed to interact with to make a decision.

Locking where customers belong to different business areas

If both parents need to be locked for the same issue and 1 party is already locked to a specialist area, the issue will be managed by that specialist area. Where there is more than 1 specialist area, collaborative customer management applies.

To refer follow up work to another business area:

  • Create a Client Contact Required intray on the customer. Set the due date for 2 working days. Include a notepad detailing follow up action required, for example Pending care, pending NAP
  • If the other customer is already locked to a Service Officer, lock the customer to the same Service Officer, who will be responsible for finalising the issue
  • If the other customer is not locked to a Service Officer, refer the intray to the relevant position of the owning business area. Lock the associated customer to the same position so that both customers and all relevant intrays are identified and allocated to a Service Officer in the owning business area. For the referral reasons see Customer referral guidelines for staff

See the Finalise issues table.

Finalise issues and unlock customer

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Finalise issue + Read more ...

Before a customer issue is finalised, ensure:

  • Minimum Collection Activity has been completed
  • all the presenting issues within the Service Officer's scope have been finalised
  • all appropriate referrals to external providers have been provided, and
  • any decisions made have been verbally explained to all impacted customers

If a customer does not agree with the decision and would like it reviewed, explain the reason for the decision and discuss appropriate options to resolve their issues, these may include:

  • new administrative action, other administrative action such as reviewing a payment arrangement may resolve the customer’s concerns
  • new decision, if the customer’s circumstances have changed it may be more appropriate for a new decision to be made
  • change of assessment, if the customer has special circumstances a change of assessment application may be a better option. See Change of Assessment - Discussion macro
  • error correction, simple factual errors and some more complex errors can be fixed without the need for an objection. See Correcting errors on Child Support cases
  • complaint, if the customer is unhappy with the service provided rather than the actual decision, lodging a complaint may be a more suitable option. See Managing customer complaints and feedback
  • an objection to the decision, if certain this is the best option for the customer. See Objections to Child Support decisions

2

Finalise all issues within scope + Read more ...

Finalise all issues within scope, according to the relevant Blueprint document and/or the Finalisation Guidelines for the Service Officers particular business area.

3

Check if other specialised issues are still outstanding + Read more ...

If there are other specialised issues outstanding:

  • contact the Service Officer in the other specialist area by phone to explain that the customer will be transferred and to discuss any in progress actions, and
  • lock the customer(s) to the Service Officer managing the outstanding specialised issue

4

Unlock customer + Read more ...

If the customer is locked, unlock as follows:

  • If the decision has been verbally explained, unlock using Ownership Rules. See Transfer or lock a customer Cuba Process Help
  • If the decision has not been verbally explained and appropriate contact attempts have been made:
  • confirm that a letter has been sent to all impacted customers advising of the decision, and
  • auto-unlock to allow time for the customers to contact Services Australia to discuss the decision

See Contact with Child Support customers.

5

Correspondence received + Read more ...

When a customer or representative sends in written correspondence via fax, email or post, undertake the following:

  • Address all matters raised. If a letter has not been issued as a result of the agency’s actions, contact the customer or representative to discuss further. If the customer or representative refers to multiple issues ensure each issue is referred to the relevant team. See Contact with Child Support customers
  • Delete intray after all issues raised have been addressed and communicated with the customer if required
  • Do not re categorise or delete correspondence received intrays inappropriately. Doing this may have a detrimental effect on customers and cause distress. It can result in financial impacts and cause delays in processes such as Change of Assessment (CoA) and Part6A Objections
