This document outlines information about the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) physiotherapy services programme for Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

On this Page:

Physiotherapy services

DVA provides funding to eligible DVA health card holders (the veteran community) that covers physiotherapy services that can only be provided to entitled persons by registered physiotherapists. Physiotherapy services claimed must be in accordance with the patient's assessed clinical need.

Services Australia provides claim processing and payment services to facilitate this programme.

Eligibility to receive DVA physiotherapy services

Members of the veteran community are eligible to receive DVA funded physiotherapy services based on the type of DVA health care card they hold:

DVA Gold Card and Totally Permanently Incapacitated (TPI) Gold Card holders

DVA funds most physiotherapy services necessary to meet a clinical need.

DVA White Card holders

DVA funds physiotherapy related to the card holders accepted disabilities only and treatment may require prior financial authorisation before it is commenced.

Patient Eligibility for VAP contains the steps to follow to verify a DVA health card holder’s eligibility before paying or rejecting physiotherapy services when the system returns 2-CHK-ELIG message.

DVA health card holders should check that their physiotherapist provider accepts the DVA Gold Card and White Card before commencing treatment.

Eligibility to provide DVA physiotherapy services

Only a physiotherapist who is registered with Services Australia (they have a Medicare provider number) at the time of the service is able to provide services to eligible DVA health card holders.

DVA service providers agree to provide services to DVA health card holders through a payment arrangement which means the card holder does not pay for those services.

The Resources page contains a link to ‘Notes for Allied Health Providers – Physiotherapists’ on the DVA Website. This contains detailed eligibility requirements and also information for Physiotherapy services.

DVA fee schedule (item numbers) for physiotherapy services

The DVA Physiotherapy Schedule of Fees contains a range of items available to eligible DVA health card holders for both professional attendances in rooms or at a place other than rooms (domiciliary visits) such as a patients home, residential aged care facility or institution.

Items in this fee schedule are in the range PH10-PH99.

The Resources page contains a link to the physiotherapy Schedule of Fees.

DVA prior financial authorisation requirements for physiotherapists

Physiotherapy service providers may be required to seek prior financial authorisation from DVA before providing certain physiotherapy services. The fee schedules contain information on which items require prior financial authorisation.

To request financial approval, physiotherapy service providers must contact DVA before administering services to be able to claim payment by completing a Chiropractic/Osteopathy/Physiotherapy Prior Financial Approval Request form. The Resources page has a link to the form on the DVA Website.

DVA physiotherapy services business rules

These business rules are issued by DVA and are used by Services Australia Service Officers processing claims.

  • Subsequent consultations cannot be claimed on the same day as an initial consultation for the same patient. Only 1 subsequent consultation per entitled person can be provided each day
  • 2 consultations cannot be claimed on the same day, for the same patient (with the exception of physiotherapy services PH23 and PH27 where treatment, not exceeding 5 days, is required for a short term acute onset condition. When claiming, providers should indicate AM or PM next to each consultation or the time of each consultation)
  • Extended consultations are to be claimed for treatment of 2 acute and unrelated areas. Treatment of related areas, or treatment of multiple, chronic musculo-skeletal conditions, should not be claimed as an extended consultation
  • Concurrent physiotherapy and chiropractic services for the same condition for any entitled patient is not permitted. The system does not support this DVA business rule, health care providers are required to deliver treatment and meet the accountability requirements as set out in Notes for Allied Health Providers. The Resources page contains a link to the Notes for Allied Health Providers
  • Lymphoedema Management is claimed with item PH41. There is a limit of 20 services for a patient per calendar year. Prior approval is not required
  • Group physiotherapy can be provided using item PH50 and cannot be claimed on the same day as any other physiotherapy item for the same patient
  • Aquatic physiotherapy can be provided using item PH60 and PH61, however, travel cannot be claimed. PH60 and PH61 cannot be claimed on the same day as any other physiotherapy item for the same patient. Only 1 aquatic physiotherapy consultation per patient can be provided each day
  • Treatment in residential aged care facilities (RACF) high care and public hospitals requires prior approval.
  • Treatment for White Card holders must be related to an accepted disability
  • Providers are to comply with billing arrangements as outlined in the 'Notes for Physiotherapists' available on DVA’s Website. The Resources page contains a link to the website

DVA physiotherapy services limitations and restrictions

Physiotherapy and chiropractic treatment or physiotherapy and osteopathic treatment cannot be provided for the same condition at the same time.

A complete list of business rules associated with DVA physiotherapy services can be found in the explanatory ‘Notes for Allied Health Providers Section 2(f) for Physiotherapists'. The Resources page contains a link to the Notes.

Claim lodgement information

DVA physiotherapy services claims can be lodged in 3 ways. The Resources page contains a link to claim choices for health professionals on the Services Australia website.

Online claiming

Physiotherapy service providers can submit electronic claims for processing without the need to send any paperwork to Services Australia.

Paper copies of forms do not need to be retained if claiming online. However a copy of the voucher should be provided to the DVA health card holder. Service providers should be sure that they can, from other means of record keeping, satisfy any request from Services Australia or DVA for evidence of service and details of treatment.

The DVA health card holder should be provided with a record of the treatment provided.

DVA Webclaim

DVA Webclaim is a real-time web based electronic claiming channel that allows health care providers to submit electronic claims via the internet, without the need to send any paperwork. It is accessed via the Health Professionals Online Services (HPOS) portal.

Paper copies of forms do not need to be retained if claiming via DVA Webclaim. A copy of the voucher should be provided to the DVA health card holder. Service providers should be sure that they can, from other means of record keeping, satisfy any request from Services Australia or DVA for evidence of service and details of treatment.

Manual (paper based) claim

DVA physiotherapist providers are required to lodge both a ‘Health Practitioner Service Voucher’ form and a ‘Claim for Treatment Services Voucher’ form. These fillable forms can be printed out and are available from the DVA Website. Physiotherapist providers can use their own invoice forms for submitting accounts if they can provide the same level of information as required by the DVA forms. After completion, service providers should post the forms to Services Australia for processing as per the instructions on the form. The Resources page contains a link to the website.

Enquires escalation

The VAP National Enquiry Line (Tier 1) is used as the ‘front door’ for all VAP physiotherapy services enquiries. VAP trained Service Officers will help with the enquiry or escalate to the appropriate department for resolution.

The Resources page contains links to the Department of Veterans’ Affairs Website and question and answers for common enquiries relating to physiotherapy services.
