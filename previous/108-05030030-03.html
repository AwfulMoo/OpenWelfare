1

Pre-birth FTB claim (FTB/CCS not current) + Read more ...

The correct DOV will pre-fill into the online claim.

  • Where the claim lodgement and the child's expected date of birth is in the current financial year, the DOV is the date of notification
  • Where the claim is lodged in the current financial year but the child's expected date of birth is in the next financial year, the DOV is:
    • the date of notification for the current financial year estimate, and
    • 1 July for the next financial year estimate

For an example, see the Resources page

There are no pre-birth claims for Child Care Subsidy (CCS).

Newborn Child Declaration (FA081) provided after pre-birth claim lodged

The DOV of the income estimate provided in the pre-birth claim can remain as this date when processing the FA081. It does not need adjusting.

If restoring FTB, see Step 4 in Table 2 for the DOV to use when:

  • income support has not stopped
  • the customer has remained eligible for FTB since the date of cancellation, and

2

Post-birth FTB claim (FTB/CCS not current) + Read more ...

For most post-birth claims, the DOV of the income estimate can remain as the original date used in the online claim. The system will insert the DOV.

This also applies when a coding an FA100 in the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) workflow. The DOV of the estimate pre-filled by the workflow does not have to be changed in most cases.

If the system does not insert an original date, the DOV of the income estimate for the majority of post-birth claims is the later of:

  • an eligible child entering care
  • 1 July of the current financial year

Example: Post-birth claim lodged 10 August 2019 for an eligible child who entered care on 2 August 2019. The DOV for the income estimate is 2 August 2019 as this is the latest date between the date the child entered care and 1 July of the current financial year. See the Resources page for examples.

The DOV of the income estimate may need to be updated if a customer:

  • lodges an FTB claim following separation and their ex-partner received FTB in the current financial year. Change the DOV pre-filled by the workflow or online claim to the later of:
    • date of separation, or
    • date an eligible child entered care
  • lodges an FTB claim following CAN-INC and is not eligible for restoration. Change the DOV to the date the new claim was lodged
  • lodges an FTB claim following CAN-ENA or CAN-OIS and is not eligible for restoration. Change the DOV to Date Paid to + 1 day (DPT+1)
  • returns to Australia more than 13 weeks after departure and lodges an FTB claim. Change the DOV pre-filled by the workflow or he online claim, to the later of:
    • date the customer/child returns to Australia, or
    • date an eligible child enters care
  • claims FTB instalments after the cancellation of their current partner's FTB in the same financial year. Go to Item 3

See the Resources page for examples.

Note: see Step 4 in Table 2 if:

  • a claim has been lodged
  • the customer has remained eligible for FTB since the date of cancellation, and
  • FTB cancelled no earlier than 1 July of the previous financial year

3

Partner claims FTB after customer's FTB cancelled or rejected + Read more ...

Income related cancellation

If a person lodges a claim for FTB after their partner's FTB cancels or rejects due to income (CAN-INC, REJ-INC), assess the claim based on the claimant's previous FTB circumstances only.

The DOV of the claim estimate will depend on when the partner's FTB cancelled or rejected. If the partner's CAN-INC or REJ-INC applied:

  • on 1 July of the current financial year. the DOV is the later of:
    • 1 July of the current financial year, or
    • date an eligible child entered care
  • after 1 July of the current financial year, update the pre-filled DOV to the earliest possible date the claimant can be granted from. This is usually the date the partner's FTB was cancelled

Example: Mary's FTB cancelled due to income from 2 Dec 2019. Peter (Mary's partner), lodges an FTB claim on 30 January 2020. Peter has never claimed or been in receipt of FTB. As Mary has already received FTB up to and including 1 December 2019, the earliest date Peter can be granted FTB is 2 December 2019. The DOV of Peter’s claim estimate should be no earlier than his earliest possible date of grant, 2 December 2019.

Non-income related cancellation

If a partner lodges a claim for FTB after a customer’s FTB has been cancelled for a non-income related reason, the DOV of the estimate must be no earlier than the date of grant.

Example: Emily’s FTB cancels from 14 October 2019 due to a lone overseas absence. John has never been in receipt of FTB. John claims FTB and is eligible from the date Emily’s FTB had cancelled (14 October 2019). The DOV must be corrected from 1 July 2019 to 14 October 2019.

When correcting the DOV from 1 July to date of grant, the 'E135FI-ISP client's estimate needs Taxable income and Tax Free pension' error may present and stop the claim from being finalised. This will only occur if the customer or their partner was in receipt of an income support payment (ISP) since 1 July and the amount of ISP received from 1 July to cancellation is more than the estimate already recorded for 1 July. In this case, staff must manually process the claim as follows:

For combined FTB/PPL claims:

  • Keep a copy of the Review Claim information open from 'Search Online Claims'
  • Cancel the online FTB/PPL NCL on AL
  • Index and manually process the FTB claim, see Manual Processing of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) claims
  • Reindex the PPL only claim by running Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) and inserting the PPL information from 'Review Claim'. See Online or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) for family assistance, Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • Once it is finalised, select the claim from 'Search Online Claims' and update the claim status by selecting the User Status tab, select Set New Status and change to Completed
  • To monitor the frequency of this workaround, send an email to Families and Child Care - Business Process Branch with the following in the subject line:
    • E135F1 Manual Claim - CRN: xxxxxxxxxx

For FTB only claims:

  • Keep a copy of the 'Review Claim' information open from 'Search Online Claims'
  • Cancel the online FTB NCL on AL
  • Index and manually process the FTB claim, see Manual Processing of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) claims
  • Once it is finalised, select the claim from Search Online Claims' and update the claim status by selecting the User Status tab, select Set new Status and change to Completed
  • To monitor the frequency of this workaround, send an email to Families and Child Care - Business Process Branch with the following in the subject line:
    • E135F1 Manual Claim - CRN: xxxxxxxxxx

Child Care Subsidy impacts

If the claimant or their partner receives or has received CCS in the current financial year, after updating the estimate DOV as above, go to Item 7.

4

Reclaiming FTB after initial claim rejected due to income + Read more ...

If a claim for FTB rejects due to income (REJ-INC), the customer may lodge a second claim with a revised income estimate.

If the customer lodges a second claim in the:

  • same financial year as the REJ-INC determination,
    • they may be entitled to FTB instalments based on the revised estimate
    • the DOV of the estimate is the date of lodgement of the second claim
    • FTB can only be paid from the date of lodgement of the second claim
    • if FTB arrears are generating for a period prior to the date of lodgement of the second claim due to a previously recorded CCS income estimate that is lower than the most recent estimate provided, code the FYSO screen for the REJ-INC period using Status code REJ and Reason Code OTH. This will stop incorrect arrears being paid. The arrears can be assessed when actual income is confirmed and the customer has lodged a lump sum claim or requested a reassessment within the relevant time frame
  • financial year after the REJ-INC determination
    • they may be entitled to FTB by instalment from 1 July
    • the DOV of the estimate is 1 July for this claim

For examples, see the Resources page.

For any entitlement to a past period (for example, the REJ-INC period), the customer must lodge a lump sum claim when their actual taxable income (ATI) is known.

5

Pre-birth FTB claim (FTB current) + Read more ...

The DOV is the date of notification.

For examples, see the Resources page.

There are no pre-birth claims for CCS.

6

Post-birth FTB claim (FTB current) + Read more ...

Non-income related change

If the estimate change is a result of the child entering the customer's care, for example, PPL/DAP or employer paid maternity leave, backdate the DOV of the estimate to the date of birth/entry into care.

If backdating FTB in the post-birth claim and CCS is based on an estimate, check the date of effect (DOE) of the existing estimate. If the DOV of the claim estimate is earlier than the date of the existing estimate in the current financial year:

  • FTB arrears may be assessed based on a non-claim estimate and the estimate may need correcting
  • to avoid FTB arrears based on a non-claim estimate:
    • note the date the claim will be assessed from in the current financial year
    • check for any estimates recorded for a later date and before the FTB new claim was lodged
  • correct any estimates found from the date of birth/entry into care (or 1 July if this was not in the current financial year) up to and including the DOR of the new claim so they match the estimate supplied in the new claim

Do not adjust arrears based on an estimate with a Date of Receipt (DOR) after the customer submitted their FTB claim. These are the most recent updates.

Coding implications:

  • If the customer receives CCS and the DOV is backdated for FTB, the DOE for CCS will be the start of the CCS fortnight after the DOV used for FTB purposes
  • A decrease in estimate may result in CCS arrears issuing to the customer
  • An increase in estimate may result in a potential CCS overpayment
  • Arrears/overpayments will depend on whether session reports have been received and processed for the past periods
  • If a potential overpayment is identified and the customer is present, discuss increasing CCS withholdings

Income related change

If the estimate change is a result of a change in salary or other source of income for the current financial year, the DOV for the estimate is the date of notification.

Code the date of notification as the DOV. The system will calculate the correct Date of Effect (DOE).

If the revised estimate:

  • increases the customer's FTB rate (favourable decision), the system DOE will be the date of notification
  • decreases the customer's FTB rate (unfavourable decision), the system DOE will be the later of the date of notification and date paid to plus 1 day
  • changes the customer’s CCS entitlement, the system DOE will be the start date of the next CCS fortnight

Activity Test details may need updating where there is a change in income estimate

See the Resources page for examples.

7

New FTB claims, current or previous CCS only customer or partner (FTB not current) + Read more ...

This process does not apply if the customer is eligible for restoration. This includes new claims following CAN-INC, see Step 4 or Step 5 in Table 2.

If a customer or their partner is or was getting CCS only and claims FTB for the same financial year:

  • the system will insert the DOV of the income estimate
  • the DOV must remain as the original date applied in the claim, unless criteria in Item 2 or Item 3 above are met
  • as the customer or their partner is CCS/CUR, an existing estimate would be recorded

If backdating a claim for FTB and CCS is based on an estimate, check the date of effect (DOE) of the existing estimate. If the DOV of the claim estimate is earlier than the date of the existing estimate in the current financial year:

  • FTB arrears may be assessed based on a non-claim estimate
  • To avoid FTB arrears based on a non-claim estimate:
    • note the date the claim will be assessed from in the current financial year
    • check for any estimates recorded for a later date and before the FTB new claim was lodged
    • correct any estimates found so they match the estimate supplied in the new claim

Do not adjust arrears based on an estimate with a Date of Receipt (DOR) after the customer submitted their FTB claim. These are the most recent updates.

The system applies the correct DOE for the estimate for each payment as follows:

  • For CCS, the DOE is the start of the CCS fortnight after the DOV for the FTB claim:
    • backdating an income estimate DOV for FTB will reassess CCS
    • a lower estimate may result in a CCS arrears issuing
    • a higher estimate may result in a potential overpayment
    • arrears or overpayments will depend on whether session reports have been received and processed for the past periods
      Note:       Activity Test details may also have to be updated if there is a change in income estimate
  • For FTB, the system DOE is the date the claim is being assessed from

To reduce risk of overpayment, the customer can make a payment choice for FTB or choose to increase their CCS withholdings.

For examples, see the Resources page.

8

New CCS claims (regardless of FTB status) + Read more ...

Process new claims for CCS using the actual date of claim lodgement, which is the date of receipt (DOR) of the claim.

The DOV of the income estimate is the date from which CCS is granted. This may be up to 28 days before date of lodgement. This is an automated process and not subject to change.

Note: do not follow this step if a customer lodges a combined CCS and FTB claim. See Item 1 or Item 2 above for an FTB claim pre-birth or post-birth claim.

For more information, see Changes in circumstances and date of effect for CCS and Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS)

9

Blended families + Read more ...

For a new blended family assessment:

  • an estimate can be coded on each of the customer and partner records
  • the DOV of the estimate is the start date of the blended family assessment

10

New claim after cancellation due to FTB CAN-N3Y + Read more ...

A customer reclaiming FTB after their FTB cancelled due to 3 or more non-lodger decisions (FTB CAN-N3Y) is eligible to receive fortnightly payments when their non-lodger debts are resolved.

This is the date the customer and their partner's (if applicable):

  • Actual Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) for outstanding non-lodger decisions is confirmed. Go to the FIPY screen to view date of lodgement which is the date:
    • the ATO received their ATI
    • they advise they do not have to lodge a tax return
  • outstanding non-lodger debts are repaid

Granting in the same financial year FTB CAN-N3Y occurred

+ Read more ...

The DOV is the later of DOR of the claim or the date their non-lodger debt is resolved.

  • No FTB arrears are payable for the current year:
    • prior to the date income was confirmed, or
    • all non-lodger debts were repaid

Payment for this period cannot be made until the customer's (and partner's if applicable) ATI is confirmed in the next financial year

  • On the FYSO screen, code REJ (status code) OTH (reason code) to stop:
    • any arrears payable from the date FTB CAN-N3Y prior to the date income confirmed for customer and partner. Check date of lodgement on FIPY screen or lodgement exemption on FNLE screen, or
    • all non-lodger debts are repaid

Granting in a subsequent financial year after FTB CAN-N3Y occurred

+ Read more ...

The DOV is the later of 1 July of the relevant year or the date their non-lodger debt is resolved.

See Cancellation of FTB Instalment Payments due to 3 or more non-lodger decisions (Sunset Clause).

Action

General rule for revised estimates + Read more ...

Code the date of notification as the event date (DOV). The system will calculate the correct Date of Effect (DOE).

Family Tax Benefit

If the revised estimate:

  • increases the customer's FTB rate, the system DOE is the date of notification
  • decreases the customer's FTB rate, the system DOE is the later of the date of notification and date paid to plus 1 day

For an automatic uplift, the DOE of a default estimate is the Schedule Application Date on the FAO Income Uplift Details (FIUD) screen. This date is at least 14 days after notifying the customer in writing of the default estimate and the proposed uplift.

Child Care Subsidy (CCS)

The DOE for CCS is the start of the next CCS fortnight after the DOV.

For an automatic uplift, the DOE of a default estimate will be:

the start of the CCS financial year (the first Monday of a financial year) if provided on or before that date, or

the start of the next CCS fortnight if provided after the start of the CCS financial year

2

Employment income nil rate period + Read more ...

If the estimate is provided:

  • during the employment income nil rate period (before income support stops), the DOV is the date of notification
  • after income support stops, the DOV is the FTB date paid to plus 1 day when restoring FTB

3

Income support stops CAN-OIS (except after employment income nil rate period) and Estimate Not Reasonable CAN-ENR + Read more ...

If FTB has not yet cancelled, code the estimate DOV from date of notification. Note: if the FTB does not automatically restore when coding the estimate, see Restoration of FTB

If FTB has cancelled in the current financial year, restore and code a new estimate using date paid to plus 1 day (DPT+1).

Coding Implications:

  • If the customer receives CCS and the DOV is backdated for FTB, the DOE for CCS will be the start of the CCS fortnight after the DOV
  • A lower estimate may result in an arrears issuing to the customer
  • A higher estimate may result in a potential overpayment
  • Customers can also choose to increase their CCS withholdings to reduce the risk of an overpayment where there has been an increase in their estimate
  • Arrears or overpayments will depend on whether session reports have been received and processed for the past periods

Activity Test details may also need updating where there is a change in income estimate.

4

Restoring FTB (except after CAN-OIS, CAN-ENA, CAN-ENR or CAN-INC) + Read more ...

The DOV of the income estimate is the date of notification.

Use the last reasonable estimate to work out FTB for any period since date of cancellation. If the income has changed as a direct result of the change in circumstance, code the DOV of the income estimate as the date the change in circumstances occurred.

Coding implications:

  • If the customer receives CCS and the DOV is backdated due to it being a change in circumstances, the DOE for CCS will be the start of the CCS fortnight after the DOV
  • A lower estimate may result in an arrears issuing to the customer
  • A higher estimate may result in a potential overpayment
  • Arrears or overpayments will depend on if session reports have been received and processed for the past periods

Activity Test details may also need updating where there is a change in income estimate.

Example

If the customer has a newborn and family income decreases as the customer or partner ceases work to care for the newborn, the DOV of the new estimate is the baby’s date of birth.

However, if the customer has a newborn and at the same time, the partner gets a pay rise, the DOV of the new income estimate will be the date of notification.

To reduce the risk of overpayment, (if applicable), the customer can apply a payment choice (FTB) and/or increase their CCS withholdings.

For more information, see Restoration of FTB.

For examples, see the Resources page.

5

Restoring FTB following CAN-INC + Read more ...

Depending on the circumstance that resulted in the income cancellation, customers may need to lodge a new claim to have FTB restored. See Restoration of FTB for information on restoration criteria for FTB CAN-INC.

New claim lodged

If FTB is eligible for restoration, treat the new claim as a request for restoration of payment.

  • Process the claim, make sure the date of event (DOV) of the estimate is the date the estimate was provided
  • If the claim activity is applying an incorrect DOV for the income estimate and it cannot be manually changed within the activity, cancel the claim on the AL screen and complete a standard BA restoration activity. Make sure:
    • the DOR is the date the claim was lodged
    • the DOV of the income estimate is the date the estimate was provided
    • any changes advised within the claim are updated inside the restoration activity

See Restoration of FTB for more information.

Required to lodge a new claim

The date of event of the new estimate must be the lodgement date of the new claim. See Online or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) for family assistance, Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)).

If the customer is eligible for restoration and:

  • FTB CAN-INC is within current financial year
    • The DOV of the income estimate is the date of notification of the revised estimate
    • If there is a previous estimate provided for CCS that is lower than the most recent estimate provided, FTB arrears may be paying for the CAN-INC period. If arrears are paying, code the FYSO screen for the CAN-INC non-payment period, using Status code CAN and Reason code OTH. This will stop FTB arrears paying for this period. The arrears will be assessed in reconciliation when actual income is confirmed and the customer has lodged a lump sum claim or requested a reassessment within the relevant time frame
    • If there is a previous CCS estimate that is equal to or higher than the most recent estimate provided, FTB arrears may pay prior to DOR. Identify the reason for the arrears and confirm the customer is entitled before paying. No discussion around payment choices for this period or retrospective correction of estimates is necessary
    • Assessment of the non-payment period from the date of cancellation to the date of restoration cannot be finalised until after the end of the financial year, when actual income is known. See Assessment of income cancellation periods in FTB reconciliation
    • If the customer is receiving CCS, any change to their subsidy percentage will take effect from the start of the CCS fortnight after the DOV. Activity Test details may need updating when a customer has had a change in their income estimate (For example, may have started or ceased work)
  • FTB CAN-INC is within previous financial year and still cancelled
    • To restore payment, a new claim activity must be coded using the date of notification as the date of receipt (DOR)
    • DOV of new estimate is the DOR in the current financial year
    • Date of effect (DOE) is the date of original FTB CAN-INC in the previous financial year

Non-payment periods:

  • FTB entitlement for the CAN-INC non-payment period in the previous financial year will be assessed once actual income is known during reconciliation
  • The non-payment period between 1 July and date of restoration in the current financial year is not be paid until the customer contacts after the end of the financial year once actual income is known. See Assessment of income cancellation periods in FTB reconciliation.

6

Restoring FTB following CAN-ENA + Read more ...

If FTB cancelled ‘estimated income not accepted’ (CAN-ENA), code the estimate with DOV = Date Paid to + 1 day (DPT+1) when restoring FTB. This will assess any arrears on the revised and reasonable estimate.

Coding implications:

  • If the customer receives CCS and the DOV is backdated for FTB, the DOE for CCS will be the start of the CCS fortnight after the DOV
  • A lower estimate may result in an arrears issuing to the customer
  • A higher estimate may result in a potential overpayment
  • Customers can also choose to increase their CCS withholdings to reduce the risk of an overpayment where there has been an increase in their estimate
  • Arrears/overpayments will depend on whether session reports have been received and processed for the past periods

Activity Test details may also need updating if there is a change in income estimate.

7

Relationship status changes + Read more ...

Become partnered

Where a customer advises they have become partnered, either verbally or via a completed Partner Details (MOD P) form:

  • FTB is reassessed from the relationship start date to include the partner's income
  • The DOV of the income estimate for the partner is the date they became partnered
  • If the customer chooses to also update their estimate, the DOV for their income estimate is the date of notification
  • FTB will cancel INP (income not provided) from the date they became partnered if the partner's estimate is not recorded
  • For CCS the DOV of the income estimate for the partner will be the date they became partnered
    • Any change in entitlement will take effect from the start of the next CCS fortnight (Monday) after the DOV. Treat the customer as single during the CCS fortnight when they partnered. If the customer becomes partnered on the start of a CCS fortnight, it will take effect from that date
    • Activity Test details must be provided for the partner, using the date they became partnered as the DOV. Customer’s details may also need updating if they have had a change in their income estimate. See Activity Test for CCS
    • The Date of Receipt (DOR) for CCS activity test details updated in Process Direct must be the date the customer advised they became partnered. This is especially important where the activity test update occurs on a different date to the marital status update completed in Customer First

Note: if both the customer and partner got FTB before becoming partnered, and they do not want to be paid as a blended family, see FTB or CCS customer becomes partnered for how to code the care details.

Separated

  • FTB is reassessed from the relationship end date to exclude the partner's income
  • If the customer chooses to update their estimate, the DOV for the income estimate is the date of notification
  • Do not code nil income for the ex-partner, their income is still taken into account until customer and partner records are unlinked
  • CCS is reassessed from the start of the next CCS fortnight (Monday) after the date they separated (DOV):
    • Treat the customer as partnered during the CCS fortnight when they separated. If the customer separates on the start of a CCS fortnight, it will take effect from that date
    • The customer’s Activity Test details may also have to be updated where they have had a change in their income estimate
