4

Voluntary request letter + Read more ...

A Voluntary Request Letter (IMS002) must be used if there is no legislative basis to exercise the coercive information-gathering power to obtain information or documents from third parties as detailed in Part 5 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999.

It is also to be used to request additional information or documents to assist the Commonwealth Director of Public Prosecutions (CDPP) when the power cannot be exercised.

Note: when requesting information from Commonwealth, State or Territory Governments, it is preferable that any existing disclosure mechanism be used to gather information first, as a matter of courtesy.

The Voluntary Request Letter (IMS002) is available from within the Investigation Toolkit.

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Australia Post + Read more ...

Australia Post may be contacted to obtain information on post office box or mail redirections for the customer or their partner.

The Australia Post Information Request form contains options to seek the following information:

  • details of the holder of a PO Box,
  • mail monitoring,
  • details of persons receiving mail at an address, and
  • details of any mail redirections for an address

Refer to Requesting information from Australia Post in the Investigation Toolkit.

2

Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) + Read more ...

The AEC no longer responds to requests under sections 192 or 196 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999.

Until further notice, do not send requests for information to the AEC.

3

Australian Taxation Office (ATO) + Read more ...

To request information:

  • Complete the appropriate ATO Information Request. This is available in the Investigation Toolkit
  • Email the template to the ATO using the email address on the ATO Information request

5

Australian Transaction Reports and Analysis Centre (AUSTRAC) + Read more ...

AUSTRAC is Australia's anti-money laundering regulator and specialist financial intelligence unit.

Financial Transaction Report (FTR) information which may be made available includes:

  • significant cash transaction reports
  • international currency transfer reports
  • suspect transaction reports
  • international funds transfer instruction

Online TRAQ Enquiry System (TES)

Intelligence and Investigations Branch staff may be authorised to use TES to obtain AUSTRAC information online.

6

Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) + Read more ...

Intelligence and Investigations Branch staff can request information from the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) by emailing the Passports Intelligence Unit. Refer to the DFAT Request for Information/Affidavit task card in the Investigation Toolkit.

If no response is received to an initial request within 2 weeks, send the email from the Intelligence and Investigations Branch Relationships mailbox with the details of the original request. The Branch Relationships team will liaise with DFAT to prioritise the outstanding request.

7

Department of Home Affairs + Read more ...

The Department of Home Affairs provides information on the movement of people in and out of the country, as well as visa information.

For more information about the Department of Home Affairs requests, refer to the Passenger card request guidelines in the Investigation Toolkit.

8

Medicare information held by Services Australia + Read more ...

The Medicare program may be contacted to provide information on:

  • address history at a particular date
  • enrolment details
  • details of family nominated for the purpose of the Medicare Safety Net
  • any other Medicare registration details as needed

Complete and issue the Centrelink Request for Medicare Information. This is available in the Investigation Toolkit.
