Acceptance of supporting documentation

Action

1

Responsibility of the determining officer (delegate)

The determining officer (delegate) must decide whether supporting documentation/evidence provided is acceptable and/or if further information should be requested. The information provided in the examples of supporting documents tables is to be used as a guide in making this determination.

For assistance with:

  • documents not listed - if the information being sought is not listed then request the customer to provide all the relevant documentation they have
  • unusual cases - follow normal escalation procedures as per individual payment processing procedures where further information for acceptable documentation in unusual cases is required. For Smart Centre staff, see Service Officer roles when seeking Technical Peer Support (TPS) in Smart Centres

Where the supporting documentation/evidence supplied requires further explanation as to why it was accepted (or not requested/provided), the determining staff member must include full details in the decision DOC to support that decision.

A to C examples of supporting documents for different circumstances

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Aboriginality

The customer's or nominee's declaration on the claim form will normally be sufficient to assess a customer as an Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander person.

2

Age

The age of all customers must be verified, except for those in receipt of family payments, Parenting Payment (PP) and Carer Allowance (CA). Proof of age is different to Proof of Birth (POB) for identity purposes because some, but not all, proof of age documents can be used for identity.

Service Officers must check that the details on the document provided match the details recorded on the claim and the customer record.

Note: a customer’s official date of birth (DOB) for Centrelink purposes is the date indicated by:

  • State or Territory Registrar of Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) if born in Australia, or
  • Department of Home Affairs if born outside Australia

Only the dates identified on documents issued by these agencies can be accepted as the official DOB - see Verifying the date of birth or age of a customer or child.

For Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme claims, proof of age is required before benefits can commence for students who have not qualified for AIC in a previous year.

Note: if Family Tax Benefit (FTB) is/has been paid for the student, then no proof of age is required.

Otherwise the normal requirement is for a customer's:

  • Birth certificate, or
  • Valid Australian passport

If neither is available, acceptable alternatives include:

  • official citizenship or residency papers (such as an Australian visa or ImmiCard) which show the customer’s date of birth, or

If a customer is unable to supply proof of age, details of this must be recorded in a DOC.

3

Accommodation - residential aged care

  • Aged care accommodation payments agreement for residential aged care facility. This should include the detail of any daily fees paid or lump sum amounts including date paid. See Aged care fees and charges - accommodation payments
  • Contract or agreement with retirement village. If an entry contribution was paid this should include the amount paid and date of entry. See Assessment of entry contribution

For verification for Rent Assistance purposes - see ‘Rent Assistance (RA)’ in L to R examples.

4

Approved Activity

Job seekers with mutual obligation requirements who are already engaged in an activity and want to have it approved and included in a Job Plan may need to provide evidence. Youth Allowance (YA) job seekers who have not attained Year 12 (or equivalent) or a Certificate III or higher qualification must undertake approved activities to meet their mutual obligation requirements.

5

Assets

Life insurance policies

The latest statement for each life insurance policy that shows the ownership and the surrender/withdrawal value for that policy.

Foreign assets

A current statement or verification documents for each asset held outside Australia showing the applicable foreign currency value and/or balance.

Other assets

Customers may need to supply evidence of the value or sale of assets where the Service Officer has doubts about the stated value. For verification of investments, see 'Investments' in D to I examples.

6

Away from home

It is necessary to verify that a dependent customer is genuinely required to live away from home for a specific period of time, for the purpose of study, training, or searching for work. See The away from home rate for Youth Allowance (YA) customers.

Customers who claim that their parents' home is an inadequate place for study may need to supply a floor plan of the house, or other evidence depending on their circumstances. See Assessing parental home inadequate for study, work or job search for Youth Allowance (YA) at the away from home rate.

Customers who claim the away from home rate of ABSTUDY will need to provide evidence supporting their circumstances. This may also need to be supported by an Authority in the community.

7

Bank accounts and balances

Customers submitting new claims for income support payments or services may need to supply supporting documents of all income and assets held:

  • by them
  • by their partner
  • jointly

If supporting documents from a financial institution (bank, building society or credit union) are required as part of the claim, they must show:

  • account holder names
  • account number
  • BSB or name of financial institution
  • the current account balance

Note: Supporting documents that do not include some of the information may be acceptable, as long as the delegate is satisfied that the information is true and correct.

Exceptions for customers reclaiming

Some exceptions may apply to former customers who are reclaiming a payment or concession. These can include:

  • when they are close to the asset limit
  • they have previously been rejected because they were over the asset limit
  • if there is a significant change in circumstances

In cases such as these, they may be exempt from verifying their income and assets if they acknowledge in their claim that the information is still true and correct.

Service Officers can process these claims without asking for further bank account verification, unless there is doubt about the validity of the information provided in the claim. When in doubt, make a request for information to the customer.

See:

  • Requesting information (CLK)
  • Verifying income and assets

Claims subject to Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP)

JobSeeker payment, Youth Allowance, and Austudy are all subject to a LAWP. If they are claiming one of these payments, see Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP).

Claims not subject to LAWP

In new claims for payments that are not subject to LAWP, the documentation does not have to include the exact balance declared within the claim. However, the delegate must be satisfied that the supporting documents show the overall financial position the customer has declared in their claim.

If the delegate conducts verification then the delegate has three courses of action:

  • they accept the customer declaration, no further action is required
  • the account balance differs from the declared financial position in the claim, the account balance can be corrected as long as the supporting documentation is either dated before or on the date of the claim
  • they are not satisfied the declaration reflects the customers financial position at time of claim, an updated statement or transaction record must be requested

If undertaking verification, staff should also check if any withdrawals do not appear to be for usual living expenses.

Term deposits

The supporting documents required for term deposits differ to other bank accounts and must include:

  • current balance of the term deposit
  • customer’s name

The latest statement should confirm the current balance. A reinvestment or rollover statement is acceptable, if an automatic reinvestment has occurred.

For overseas financial institutions supporting documentation is required that shows the applicable foreign currency value, current account balance and the account holder details, i.e. account name, account number and financial institution.

Direct Investments

For any Debentures or Bonds the latest statement or internet printout showing the current balance and ownership of the investment.

8

Business (Sole trader or partnership)

  • Sole traders and those involved in a partnership are required to:
    • complete a Mod F, or
    • where applicable advise self-employment details in their online claim, and
    • provide necessary supporting financial documents. See Assessing and coding the Business details for sole traders and partnerships Mod F
  • Documents may include the following:
    • Profit and loss statement
    • Balance sheet
    • Depreciation schedule
    • Livestock Trading Account
    • Individual Income tax returns (ITRs) and the taxation notice of assessment
    • Partnership Income tax returns (ITRs) and taxation notices of assessment
    • Any Notes to the Accounts
    • Partnership Schedules for investment loans and bank accounts
    • Partnership agreements

Refer to Documents required to assess a sole trader business and Documents required to assess a partnership for more information.

An interim profit and loss statement can be provided if the customer has just started a new business, or recently had a significant change in the business (such as an unexpected downturn, winding down to retirement or recently ceased working in the business).

D to I examples of supporting documents for different circumstances

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)

See Income - Other government payments (within Australia) in D to I examples.

2

Employment income

Ceased work

Customers applying for a payment that requires verification they have ceased work need to verify that they are unemployed through one of the following ways:

  • Employer Separation Certificate (SU1)
  • Letter from employer including - date last worked, reason for ceasing work and leave entitlements paid, the amount of the person's final payment, the amount of any money still owed by the employer to the person, any personal compensation details
  • Phone call to employer to obtain date last worked, reason for ceasing work and leave entitlements paid, the amount of the person's final payment, the amount of any monies still owed by the employer to the person, any personal compensation details
  • Note: Service Officers should let the employer know that information obtained over the phone will be confirmed in writing. Follow up contact by sending a letter to the employer. Use a Q999 (free text) letter:
    • restate the relevant details provided by the employer
    • advise employer to contact if any material information is incorrect
  • If the customer was self-employed, a letter from their accountant or solicitor detailing that the business has ceased operating should be provided

There may be situations where this verification may not be required, see Employment Separation Certificate (SU1).

Overseas work: for a customer who ceased work overseas, a letter or statement from their employer providing details of their income, cessation date, reasons etc. and any holiday or other entitlements is suitable. If the customer is not able to provide such a letter, then a declaration statement should be done instead.

Note: verification of unemployment may also allow the correct application of non-payment and other waiting periods that may be applicable.

Still working

If a customer and/or their partner is still employed full-time, part-time or casual then request the following:

  • two most recent payslips; or
  • letter from employer stating number of hours worked and gross amount paid for the two most recent pay periods; or
  • employment letter of offer or contract stating number of hours worked and gross amount paid

The Income and Asset form (Mod IA) and some online claims only requests the two most recent payslips. Subsequent and ongoing employment periods do not need verification, and details of the employment income can be reported verbally. If the customer is unable to be contacted by phone, or if there is doubt over the employment details or income amount, then formal verification may still be requested where necessary.

This applies to new claims where additional employment periods have elapsed between claim lodgement and finalisation.

Note: evidence of employment income is not required for job seeker claims as these customers are automatically profiled to report their employment income, any change of circumstances and mutual obligation activities before any payment is released.

For sales commission a document giving details, including the amount and date of receipt, is required.

Note: where a customer and/or partner are currently receiving an income support payment, documentation may be requested to assist in coding of the income, even though it may not be required for verification purposes.

Overseas work for foreign employment income, the documents should:

  • show the income amount received in foreign currency, rather than Australian dollars, and
  • be issued by the overseas person, organisation or authority making the payment

Salary sacrificing/salary packaging

For salary sacrificing into superannuation, a statement from the employer, on the employer's letterhead, advising the amount of the contributions which have been salary sacrificed and the name of the superannuation fund and member to which the payments are made.

For salary sacrificing or salary packaging for benefits other than superannuation:

  • payslips are acceptable evidence as long as they show the total gross income, that is, the amount before any salary sacrifice deductions
  • if the payslips do not show this or, if it is unclear whether the payslip shows the non-grossed-up amount, the customer should provide:
    • a copy of the salary sacrificing agreement, or
    • a statement from their employer on the employer's letterhead advising the type and amount of benefit received and whether or not the amount is grossed up or non-grossed up

See Assessing salary sacrificing/salary packaging arrangements for employment income

For employer provided fringe benefits paid in addition to a wage or salary, the customer should provide verification in the form of:

  • a letter from their employer, on the employer's letterhead, stating:
    • the type and amount of the benefit received, and
    • whether or not the amount is grossed-up or non-grossed-up
  • a recent payment summary that reflects the current financial year arrangement
  • their latest income tax return that reflects the current financial year arrangement

See Assessing fringe benefits for Social Security income test purposes.

3

Home Equity Access Scheme (the Scheme)

The Scheme applicants are required to provide the following documentation in relation to each Australian real estate property they offer as security for the loan:

  • a copy of a recent title search and the latest rates notice
  • a copy of the current insurance policy, or certificate of currency covering the property
  • any loan agreement(s), contract(s) or mortgage statements in relation to the property
  • a copy of the company constitution, or memorandum and articles of association, if the real estate is the asset of a private company
  • a copy of the trust deed for the trust acting as guarantor/guarantee

The following Parts A-F of the SA310 claim form will need to be completed by the applicant where applicable:

  • Part A - Applicant and partner declarations and authorities
    • to be completed and signed by the applicant (and partner if applicable)
  • Part B - Real estate details
    • to be completed and signed by the applicant to support the SA310 claim
  • Part C - Co-owner declaration
    • to be completed and signed by the co-owner before a witness
  • Part D - Individual trustee guarantee declaration
    • to be completed and signed by the trustee
  • Part E - Corporate trustee guarantee declaration
    • to be completed and signed by the corporate trustee
  • Part F - Company guarantee declaration
    • to be completed and signed by the director/s of the company

See Claiming a Home Equity Access Scheme loan.

4

Identity documents, Commencement of Identity and Proof of Arrival (POA)

Customers are required to provide acceptable identity documents when claiming a Centrelink payment or service that require Identity Confirmation. In some circumstances, the customer's partner will also need to confirm their identity. See Identity Confirmation.

Identity Status is located in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard or the Snapshot icon in Process Direct (PD).
For a list of Identity documents, see Identity documents A to Z.

Where a customer is experiencing genuine difficulty in providing all identity documents required to achieve an Identity Status of Confirmed, complete the Alternative Identity in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard in PD. See Alternative Identity.

Where a customer is experiencing genuine difficulty in providing an Australian Birth Certificate as a Commencement of Identity document, birth verification from the Registry of Birth, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) can be collected by Services Australia via the Request for Verification workflow in the PD Identity Confirmation Dashboard. BDM provide verification of birth only, not a birth certificate.

If a customer needs help to apply for a birth certificate, download the application form from the relevant BDM website. Photocopy and certify the required documentation and remind the customer to include payment with the application for the certificate.
For more information, see Commencement of Identity.

Where a customer is experiencing genuine difficulty or inability in providing documents to satisfy Proof of arrival (POA) requirements, see Verifying and recording Proof of Arrival (POA).

When International Services (CIS) return POA information it is proof of arrival. It cannot be used for identity outside Services Australia. It may be used, but not always, to satisfy residence requirements. It cannot be coded in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard in PD, but can contribute to an Alternative Identity assessment.

5

Incapacity

For incapacity claims (including Youth Disability Supplement (YDS)):

  • an original medical certificate (photocopy not acceptable), preferably a Centrelink medical certificate (SU415)
  • if a non-Centrelink medical certificate is used, phone the doctor/specialist to request extra information as per Centrelink medical certificate

Note: encourage medical practitioners to use the Centrelink certificate (SU415) and arrange for a supply of the forms to be sent, if required.

6

Income

Foreign income and pensions

Evidence of all employment income received from outside Australia showing the gross amount of income in the foreign currency amount and frequency of payment. For example, pay slip or other document.

Document for each foreign pension showing the gross amount of income in the currency it is paid in and frequency of payment.

Evidence documents should be issued by the overseas person, organisation or authority making the payment. For more information on ‘Foreign income and pensions’ see Foreign income and assets and Foreign pension coding.

Income Streams

See ‘Superannuation and income streams’ in S to Z examples.

Other government payments (within Australia)

Letter or other document showing the reference number and details of the other government payments received including the amount and frequency. This does not include Centrelink payments.

For more information on Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) payments, see Income from Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA).

Other income

If a customer is receiving income from a source other than employment request the following:

  • a letter or statement from person or organisation they are receiving income from
  • documents that show details of the amount of income, frequency of payment and details of when the income is expected to cease (if applicable)

This includes the receipt of royalties, lump sums, inheritances, prizemoney, winnings, match or sporting payments, scholarships, bursary or educational assistance, ex-gratia payments, income from non-discretionary life interests, director’s fees, profit sharing, board and lodgings or regular periodic gifts.

Customers will also need to supply supporting documents showing any lump sum payments (excluding compensation, damages or insurance) received in the last 12 months (or 14 months for LIC claims and renewals).

For more information for specific types of income, see Assessment of income for Centrelink payments.

7

Independence for Youth Allowance

Some customers may be required to provide evidence to support their claim of independence.

Examples of supporting documents that may be requested are listed below. See Independence for Youth Allowance (YA) customers.

Note: this is not an exhaustive list and Service Officers may request other types of evidence in order to make a correct decision. In some circumstances, it may not be necessary to ask the customer to supply any supporting documents, as the Service Officer is able to make a decision based on existing information on the customer, parent/guardian or sibling record.

Member of a Youth Allowance (YA) Couple:

  • marriage certificate
  • joint bank accounts. Photocopy even if opened less than a year, as these accounts can be used as evidence for assessing independence at a later date
  • life insurance documents at least 1 year old or 6 months in exceptional cases
  • lease agreement in both names at least 1 year old or 6 months in exceptional cases
  • dependent partner claim for taxation

Person aged 25 years or more:

  • Birth certificate
  • other suitable documents showing person's age

Orphan:

  • Death certificates
  • documents from State/Territory welfare department

Refugee:

  • Group 1.3 entry permit (permanent resident, refugee or humanitarian)

Person in State Care:

  • letter / document from State/Territory welfare department

Unreasonable to Live at Home:

  • Youth Allowance: Statement for Parents (SY016)
  • letters and reports from State/Territory Welfare departments, police, etc.

Self-supporting:

  • Group certificate(s)
  • letter from employer(s)
  • pay slips

8

Investments

If a customer has managed investments, shares or superannuation then request the following.

Managed investments

  • latest statement from fund manager. For portfolio products, this should show the current balance and for unitised products a breakdown of the investment choices and number of units owned. See Managed investments - adding a new investment

Public company shares and options

  • for shares and options listed on the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX), or other stock exchange within Australia, the latest CHESS Holding statement or Issuer Sponsored Holding statement. See Shares and securities
  • for shares listed on a stock exchange outside Australia, the latest share certificates or statements showing number of shares and type of shares held. See Shares and securities
  • for shares not listed on a stock exchange, the latest share certificates or statements showing number of shares and type of shares held and the company or co-operative financial report. See Shares and securities

Private company shares

See 'Private companies' in L to R examples.

Margin loans

  • a current statement providing details for each margin loan showing the current market value of the portfolio and the current loan balance

Funeral bonds or investments

  • a statement outlining the details of each funeral bond or investment including purchase price and the purchase date

Note: if there have been changes since the date of issue of statement, a more recent statement should be requested.

For customers claiming a payment that is subject to a Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP), details of all investment balances should be requested from the date the customer ceased work or study as well as at the date of claim. If the claim is not processed immediately the Service Officer may decide to request more recent details of an account if they consider the details supplied may not reflect the current balance.

9

Isolation

Evidence may be required to support a family's claim for AIC based on isolation. Continuing claims where there has been no significant change in circumstances need provide no new evidence. All other claims must provide evidence.

If the claim is based on adverse weather conditions, then request:

  • a statement from the school showing the actual dates of absences due to adverse conditions in previous year for either the student, a sibling or a neighbouring student, or if this is not available
  • a report stating that the road or waterway in question was impassable on particular dates and has not been significantly upgraded since that time. The reports will be accepted from either: Shire Engineer/Clerk, Bus Company Proprietor, or the Local/State/National Roads, Water and/or Land Management Authority

If the claim is based on parents being unable to transport their child(ren) to/from school, then request:

  • documentation confirming that parent(s) is/are medically unfit or legally unable to drive

If the claim is based on frequent moves or absences, then request:

  • a statement from the customer showing details of travel movements for the past 12 months and planned travel movements in the year of study, and
  • supporting evidence from employer(s) or other authorities

See also Special Needs Assessment for AIC below.

Managed Investments

See 'Investments' in D to I examples.

5

Means of support

For Age Pension and Carer Payment (CP), information about the customer's circumstances prior to the start of their claim is not required and should not be requested.

For other payments including Austudy, Disability Support Pension (DSP), JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Parenting Payment (PP), Partner Allowance (PA) and Youth Allowance (YA), evidence of how the customer has supported themselves is required if it has been more than 3 months since they last worked.

Note: an Employment Separation Certificate (SU1) is still required where the customer has ceased paid work within the last 12 months. See Employment Separation Certificate (SU1).

Evidence can be provided by any of the following methods:

  • 3 months of prior bank statements as proof of self-supporting from previous savings
  • verbal statement may be obtained. This should be clearly documented on the customer’s record
  • issue manual letter Q144 - Questionnaire regarding the customer's means of support since they were last employed. Hold the relevant claim activity or where this is not possible, set up a review to follow up the return of this information

Where the determining officer deems other evidence as acceptable, see Other examples of supporting documents not listed in Other examples.

6

Parental Income

For customers claiming Youth Allowance (YA) and ABSTUDY proof of parental income is required. For AIC, proof of income is only required for customers applying for the Additional Boarding Allowance. See Parental Income Test (PIT).

Check for existing data in the parent(s) and sibling(s) records to update base tax year.

If the parent(s)/guardian(s) is/are currently receiving a payment through the Family Assistance Office (FAO):

  • check the FAO income for the previous year (FIPY) screen in the parent(s)/guardian(s) record
  • If actual income is available, this is evidence for the purposes of the PIT
  • It is important that the information from the Family Assistance record has been verified by the Australian Taxation Office (ATO)

If the customer has a sibling receiving YA or ABSTUDY, check the sibling's record for verified parental income details from their Household Income and Assets Details (NHI) screen.

If information is not available in existing records, request the following for both parents:

  • Income Tax Notice of Assessment for the Base Tax Year
  • if this is not available, the following may be used as verification:
    • a letter from the customer's accountant/tax agent stating that 'Income received by you (and/or your partner), is not expected to exceed $xxxxx', or
    • if the parents received a taxable pension, benefit, payment or allowance from Services Australia or Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) for the full base tax year, a statement of benefit from that department or agency, or
    • PAYG payment summaries and/or statements of benefits must be supported by a statutory declaration (or accountant certification) confirming that these show the full amount of taxable income received by the parent(s)/guardians(s) during the base tax year, or
    • where the parents received overseas employment income, then similar proof of income is required
    • copy of personal income tax return

If the parent(s)/guardian(s) is/are not required to lodge an income tax return, a non-lodgement advice recorded on the FAO non-lodgement enquiry (FNLE) screen for the purpose of Family Assistance, is not sufficient evidence for PIT purposes.

Note: an income tax return is only accepted as an estimate of parental taxable income. An income tax return may be used to confirm reportable superannuation contributions and net investment/business losses.

Customers who claim between September and December must supply both the Base Tax Year and the following financial tax year at the time of claim. Attempt to obtain both financial years at the time of claim when assessing and coding parent's taxable income.

Use the Annual Parental Income review (SY012 (A)) for following financial year. See Assessing Youth Allowance (YA) and ABSTUDY annual Parental Income Test (PIT) reviews.

If the SY012 (A) collection data tax year information is not available, allow the claim to finalise on base tax year information and the Annual Parental Income review (SY012 (A)) will issue after the claim is granted.

Where the customer is applying for a Current or Reverse Current Income Assessment, similar proof of income for the Base Tax Year + 1 is required. However, customers can supply an estimate of income while waiting for proof to become available.

7

Parental Income Test (PIT) Exemptions for AIC Scheme/ ABSTUDY

Where the customer is applying for a PIT exemption on the basis of:

  • receiving a current income support payment - no evidence is required
  • receiving a current DVA pension - request a statement of benefit or letter from DVA outlining the name and status of the pension
  • where the student is in State care, request a statement from the relevant government agency outlining the care arrangements and confirming that a foster care or similar allowance is not being paid for that student
  • a Health Care Card issued due to receiving maximum rate of FTB A

8

Pension Bonus Scheme

A Centrelink Evidentiary Certificate or Pension Bonus Scheme work record for the customer’s employment to verify they have worked a minimum of 960 hours for each 12 month accrual period or suitable similar documents from the employer. For example, payslips, timesheets or letter from employer.

If the customer is self-employed, verification must show that the work was gainful. For example, individual income tax returns, partnership tax returns (if necessary) and a letter from the customer stating the dates and nature of the work performed.

Note: by itself, a letter from an accountant is generally not sufficient. A Pension Bonus Scheme work record document is available from the Services Australia website.

If the customer is using their partner’s work to meet the work test, they will need to provide evidence for the partner’s work. Note: the partner must also be a registered member of the Pension Bonus Scheme for the customer to use their work to meet the work test.

9

Private companies

Assessment for private companies require a completed MOD PC. The company’s financial statements that verify the financial position of the company will be required. Examples include:

  • Latest company income tax return
  • Profit and loss statement
  • Balance sheet
  • Depreciation schedule
  • Latest personal income tax return
  • Partner’s personal income tax return (if applicable)

For more information, see - Documents required to assess a private company.

10

Private trusts

See 'Trusts' in S to Z examples.

11

Real estate

A completed Real Estate MOD R is required for each of the properties that the customer owns (other than the family home). Supporting documentation will need to be supplied. Examples include:

  • rates notice (required for valuation purposes)
  • mortgage papers including the security of the loan and the current balance (will have an impact on the final asset value to be assessed)
  • tax return including rental property schedule (this will give information on any rental income received and deductions)
  • rental contract (only to be used when tax return is not available)
  • proof of 20 year attachment for home properties subject to extended house and curtilage

A loan agreement showing the security of each loan and a statement showing the current amount owed must also be supplied to allow a loan to be recorded against a property.

Where the home property is being assessed as it is over 2 hectares (5acres), and is on more than one title or is used for business, a MOD R and supporting documents should be requested.

See Assessing and coding the Real estate details Mod R and Assessing house and curtilage.

12

Relationship status

Evidence requirements to verify changes in relationship status may vary depending on whether the customer and/or partner are currently in receipt of any payments or concessions.

Verification of Partner details

Where a customer advises within their new claim that they are partnered, this relationship status must be verified with both parties, before the customer’s claim can be assessed and the linking of the customer's and partner’s records (where applicable).

Note: partner verification is not required in some situations, see Change in relationship status from single to partnered > Circumstances when forms may not need to be issued.

Verification can be provided by the partner verbally or by:

  • using the Temporary Access Code (TAC) provided at the end of the relevant online claim or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), or
  • signing the customer's paper claim form where there is provision for a partner signature, or
  • completing a Partner details (MOD P) form

Where the customer indicates within their online claim or ACC that they are able to provide accurate information about their partner, they will be issued a TAC for their partner to verify relationship details before being able to submit their claim. Otherwise, the customer must download a Partner Details (MOD P) form which is to be completed and submitted with their claim. The MOD P will be set as a 'Required Task' and the claim cannot be submitted unless the MOD P has been provided.

Customers will be exempt from completing all required tasks before submitting their claim where they meet current Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM) claim submission exceptions or where certain vulnerabilities exist such as family domestic violence (FDV)

Where there are any concerns or risk of family and domestic violence, see Family and domestic violence.

See Resources for links to online forms.

Note: additional documentation may be necessary where there is any doubt about that customer's circumstances.

Rent Assistance (RA)

Generally customers are required to provide verification of their rent details unless specifically exempt from the requirement. Verification requirements are the same for people who are paid Rent Assistance (RA) under Social Security Law or Family Assistance law.

See Rent Assistance (RA) verification for circumstances where rent verification is not required.

If verification is not provided, payment of RA will not be made or will cease. Rent verification documents must be provided to Services Australia within 14 days of the customer receiving the request for verification.

Customers must provide one of the following in order to verify their rental details:

  • completed Rent Certificate (SU523HD) See Rent Assistance (RA) verification
  • a full lease agreement and a completed SU523HD signed by the customer. If a full lease agreement is supplied the landlord does not have to complete the honesty declaration on the Rent Certificate. See Rent Assistance (RA) verification
  • Rent Declaration (SU336) supported with other forms of evidence. This form is only to be used in limited circumstances, for example, when the landlord's confirmation cannot be obtained. It may also be used for customers travelling around Australia and who move every few days. See Rent Assistance (RA) verification

Note for new claims: if the customer provides a formal lease/tenancy agreement with the claim and all the SU523HD details are available in the claim and supporting documents, a SU523HD will not be required and the rent verification field should be coded as 'VEF' Verified (Formal).

The information in the claim and supporting documents must contain sufficient information so that the sharer and the net rent rules can be applied. See Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA).

If the rent paid by the other occupants has not been ascertained, then the net rent rules cannot be correctly applied and a SU523HD is required.

Residence in Australia

All customers, whether they were born in Australia or overseas, must provide proof that they are a current Australian resident (or special category visa (SCV) holder or the holder of 1 of the certain temporary visas that qualify customers for some payments) that allows a person to lodge a valid claim and help to qualify for payment. Residence requirements are different from Proof of Arrival (POA) requirements for Identity Confirmation. One or more of the following can be provided:

  • Overseas passport with entry visa
  • Australian passport
  • Australian Citizenship papers
  • Other entry documentation

These documents may also satisfy the Proof of Arrival requirements for identity.

If the passport is provided to meet residence requirements for family assistance, scan the first page only, showing country of issue and passport number.

Note: while the document provided may prove that the customer is a current resident of Australia, it does not necessarily prove that the person has satisfied any qualifying residence period or newly arrived resident's waiting period.

YA job seeker and qualifications

  • not attained Year 12 (or equivalent), or
  • a Certificate III or higher, or
  • not provided evidence of their Year 12 (or equivalent) or Certificate III or higher qualification when requested to do so

Superannuation and income streams

Superannuation

Details about a customer’s (or partner’s) superannuation is only required if the owner of the investment is over Age Pension age. See Assessing superannuation.

For a Self-Managed Superannuation Fund (SMSF) or Small APRA Fund (SAF) the customer must provide the latest annual member statement and the annual financial returns for their superannuation fund.

For other superannuation investments (non-SMSF or SAF) the customer must provide the latest statement showing the following:

  • the name of the fund manager
  • full name of the investment product
  • the SPIN or APIR codes
  • the value of the investment or the number of units owned (if a unitised product)

Most providers issue an annual statement and the most recent statement should be provided by the customer. Further documentation should only be requested where:

  • the statement is more than 12 months old at time of claim lodgement, and
  • the customer confirms a more recent statement is available

However, if there has been a change in the superannuation investment since claim lodgement, or there is doubt about the accuracy of the information provided by the customer, further documentation can be requested.

Note:

  • it is not deemed to be a change in circumstance where the customer has made contributions to their superannuation fund due to employment since the date the statement was issued
  • if all or part of a superannuation investment has been converted into an income stream - see income streams below

Income streams

For income streams, including defined benefit superannuation pensions, the customer must provide the latest:

  • Centrelink/DVA schedule
  • similar schedule completed by the income stream provider or trustee of SMSF/SAF showing the income stream details including current gross income and account balance (if applicable) of the product
  • Defined Benefit schedule for defined benefits, or
  • details of Income Stream form (SA330) completed and signed by the income stream provider or trustee of the SMSF/SAF

Note:

  • a more recent schedule/statement should be requested, if the document provided by the customer is more than 12 months old

for SMSF, documents must not be requested more than once per year.
