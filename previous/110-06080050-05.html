A to C list of attribute rules

This table contains a list of attribute rules relating to Service Updates or Reviews in the Integrated Review System (IRS) beginning with letters A to C.

Attribute Rule

Description

Actions which are not attributable to a Service Update or Review

The following actions are excluded from attribution regardless of whether or not they are identified as part of a review:

  • Addition of newborn child
  • Age related changes
  • Changes to deeming rates
  • Auto or bulk updates to Shares/Managed Investments database, or Superannuation
  • Consumer Price Index (CPI) updates
  • Changes to income or Rent Assistance (RA) deeming or asset thresholds
  • Actions to apply deeming rate changes
  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A and Part B income related changes (except FTB RA)
  • Foreign Pension re-index and Foreign Exchange Rates
  • Actions to apply income in one period (IOP/LOP) which impact the customer's current rate
  • One off Payments

Administrative action suspensions or cancellations

Administration action suspensions or cancellations such as a cancellation 'WUK' (Whereabouts Unknown) are not attributable to a review unless they were directly triggered by the review.

Auto processed 'WUK' cancellations are only attributable to the review if the cancellation was triggered by the automated Return to Sender process‚ and the returned letter is a Business Integrity (BI) or Compliance Risk Branch letter. The Document Tools facility links to scanned images.

Age related suspensions or cancellations

Age related cancellations should never be attributed to a review if the cancellation is triggered by the customer's age and not from information identified as part of the review.

AGD (Doesn't meet age requirements - Auto) cancellations which occur by the changing/adding of a course end date are only attributable to the review if they were directly triggered by the review.

System generated AGD cancellations are not attributable as they are triggered by the course end date being reached which is not related to the review.

Attributing actions to a partner review

The same attribution rules apply to a partner review.

Consequential activities will not be automatically attributed to the partner review. The Service Officer must manually attribute the activity if they determine that the consequential action relates to the review.

After the partner review has started, a contact letter or advice can be issued on the primary review which also relates to the partner's circumstances.

Any Review Contact Period present on the primary review can be used to assess whether an action was indirectly triggered on the partner's record.

Attributing an indirectly triggered action to a partner review based on a contact issued as part of the primary customer's review must be recorded on a DOC on the partner's record.

Attributing upward variations

Rate increases should be attributed to a review if the review directly triggered that action.

Compliance/Service Officer should not avoid attributing an upward variation to a customer's rate because it will impact any downward variation savings that the review would realise.

The Compliance/Service Officer does not need to investigate or attribute a rate increase which has been indirectly triggered by the review unless it is a restoration, grant or re-grant action. It is unlikely that the review triggered the customer to obtain a higher rate of payment as this action would most likely have occurred regardless of the review.

A voluntary compliance cancellation is attributable to more than one compliance or fraud review

The cancellation should be attributed to the first review started.

The only exception to this is where the cancellation reason is not appropriate to attribute to a particular Review Type. For example, where the payment is cancelled due to a relationship status change then it would be appropriate to attribute the cancellation to a TIP 'MOC' (Member of a Couple) review.

Note: this rule is to cater for those scenarios when the cancellation is not attributed to any review. It does not replace the rule where IRS attributes some cancellations to the first review finalised.

The General rule to determine if the review triggered the action still applies.

Cancellations automatically attributed to a Service Update or Review

Some cancellation actions where the customer has failed to respond or attend an interview may be attributed to the first review finalised.

This will only occur where the cancellation action has not been identified as part of any review or review process.

Cancellation has been automatically attributed to a Review by IRS

  • A Compliance/Service Officer must determine whether it is appropriate to claim the cancellation based on the Compliance and Fraud attribution rules. The Resources page contains a link to the rules
  • If it is not appropriate to claim the cancellation it must be unattributed from the review before the review is finalised
  • See Suspension and Cancellation reasons which are subject to automatic attribution by IRS

Cancellation has been automatically attributed to a Service Update

  • Service Profiling wait 42 days from the date the cancellation was processed before auto attributing the cancellation to a Service Update. This provides a grace period for fraud and compliance staff to manually attribute the cancellation to a review

Cancellations for failing to lodge a Reporting Statement or other forms which stimulate payment

Customer is a fortnightly lodger

The cancellation is attributable to the review if

  • the customer's DTL date is after the start of the review
  • the customer's DTL date is the same date as the start of the review and the customer was aware of the review on the day the review started, or
  • the customer's DTL date was prior, or equal to, the start of the review but late lodgement rules apply, and
  • the cancellation (or the trigger for the cancellation) occurred within a Review Contact Period

Note: cancellations for DNL (Did Not Lodge) will be excluded from attribution if the customer is a fortnightly lodger and the DTL date is prior to the start date of the review.

Customer is not a fortnightly lodger

The cancellation is attributable to the review if:

  • the customer's DTL date is after the start of the review
  • the customer has likely received the review letter and
  • the customer has not lodged since the review started

Note: the cancellation action is likely to fall outside the Review Contact Period however as long as the above rule is satisfied it is deemed that it is acceptable to claim this cancellation action.

Cancellations for voluntary surrender where customer has high employment income

Automatic cancellations

Customers on JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance with high employment income are automatically cancelled when paid employment income precludes payment after six fortnights at nil rate. If the customer has permanent full time work they are not eligible for the six fortnights at nil rate, however working credits are taken into account prior to the automatic cancellation.

Manual cancellations

A customer in the above circumstances can also request to voluntarily surrender their payment and be cancelled manually by a Compliance/Service Officer.

If the customer is not in permanent full time employment, they must be advised that they may have entitlement to six fortnights at zero rate. The customer must also be advised that they can access working credits which may allow them to receive further payments.

These conversations must be included in a DOC to support that consideration has been given to the customer's future entitlement prior to the cancellation.

A cancellation for voluntary surrender which is directly processed by Business Integrity staff can only be attributed if the discussion on future entitlement has been DOC'd as per the above circumstances.

Cancellation of the customer's payment was triggered by another review or review process

Some suspension or cancellations which were directly triggered by another review or process can be considered for manual attribution in some circumstances:

  • the suspension or cancellation must not be attributable to a started review which directly triggered that action
  • the trigger for the action (for example, failure to attend) must have occurred within a Review Contact Period and
  • there must be a valid argument that the suspension or cancellation was indirectly triggered by the review, for example, not a cancellation which is not relevant to the review

Compensation rate variations and debts

Customer's rate decreases due to recording of compensation

Rate decreases due to the recording of compensation are only attributable to a review if the update was directly triggered by the review, or identified by the review and was processed by a Compensation team on behalf of the Compliance/Service Officer.

The recording of Compensation causes a debt to be created

  • Compensation debts are excluded from attribution if the Debt Reason identifies the debt as compensation related. IRS auto excludes the following compensation debts:
    • 'CMA'
    • 'CMB'
    • 'CMC'
    • 'CMM'
    • 'CPR'

An update to the customer's record and a compensation update are included in the same activity

  • If the compensation update was directly triggered by the review then both the compensation and other update are attributable to the review
  • If the compensation update was not directly triggered by the review then the activity is not attributable to the review. Recording the updates separately will allow the other action to be attributed

A Compensation cancellation occurs during the Review Contact Period

  • A Compensation cancellation can only be attributed to a review if it was directly triggered by the review, or indirectly triggered within a Review Contact Period, and the reason for the review was to assess entitlement based on compensation income

Customer was current at gross zero rate immediately prior to the suspension or cancellation

Attributing a suspension or cancellation action

A suspension or cancellation is not attributable to the review if the customer received a gross nil payment immediately prior to that action, unless:

  • the review directly or indirectly triggered the action to reduce the customer's gross rate to zero, and
  • the action which dropped the customer's rate to zero is attributable to the review

Customer was paid zero entitlement immediately prior to the review start

The customer has not received any entitlement during the Review Period

  • The suspension or cancellation is not attributable to the review as no entitlement was being paid throughout the review period

The customer's entitlement resumed after the review was started

  • If a customer or third party contact was undertaken after entitlement resumed then the suspension or cancellation may be attributable to the review, subject to the other attribution rules being met
  • If a customer or third party contact was not undertaken after entitlement resumed then the suspension or cancellation is not attributable to the review

Downward Variation (DVR) result is calculated

The review result of 'DVR' may not be correct and is an alert to a Compliance/Service Officer to identify if a restoration should be attributed to the review, or if there is a system fault which needs to be reported.

Restoration occurred within the Applicable Restoration Period (ARP)

  • The restoration must be manually attributed to the review by the Compliance/Service Officer

Restoration occurred outside of the ARP

  • If the customer's payment was current when the review was finalised then the DVR result is correct

Restoration occurred within the ARP and is not available to attribute to the review

  • Unattribute the suspension or cancellation action as the savings are not correct. If the customer was restored at a reduced rate then contact a Senior Practitioner or a Quality Development Officer (QDO). Ensure that the suspension or cancellation is attributed to the review before it is reported

Restoration occurred within the ARP and is attributed to the review but there is no expected UVR (Upward Variation) amount associated with the restoration action

  • Unattribute the suspension or cancellation action as the savings are not correct. If the customer was restored at a reduced rate then contact a QDO or a Senior Practitioner. Ensure that the suspension or cancellation is attributed to the review before it is reported

If the payment was not restored and the ARP has ended

  • The result should be 'SUS' or 'CAN' however the DVR result can be accepted as long as the savings are correct

If the ARP has not ended at the time the review is being finalised

  • As ARP has not been invoked any restoration, grant or re-grant of an alternative payment will not attributed to the review. Contact a Senior Practitioner or a Quality Development Officer (QDO)

Due to lodge (DTL) date is equal to the Review Start Date

Review is started by a written contact

  • Cancellation actions cannot be claimed if the trigger for the cancellation (the decision to not lodge) occurred before the customer had likely received the review letter. It is acceptable to argue that the customer would likely have received the review letter on the day following the start of the review, but not the same date as the start of the review. Note: Late Lodgement rules still apply

Review is not started by a written contact

  • It is acceptable to argue that the customer was aware of the review which triggered the non-lodgement

Employment income is continuous – not Income in One Period (IOP) or Long Period (LOP) related

Recording 1WE/2WE continuous employment income prior to 7 December 2020 and entitlement continues

  • For employment income after 7 December 2020 changes, the customer must qualify for an exemption for 1WE/2WE income to continue. If the customer does not qualify for an exemption, any 1WE/2WE continuous income must be zeroed off and the customer placed onto reporting. See Resources in Recording and correcting employment income details
  • The customer must have verified ongoing employment income at an unchanging rate before employment income updates can be attributed to the review. For Employment Income Interventions (EIIP) income paid does not need to be verified. The employment income update must drop the customer's current payment to a lower rate than the payment made immediately prior to the update. Note: where the IOP or LOP for the preceding payment is recorded in the same activity as the employment income update, work back to the last payment the customer received which was not impacted by the employment income activity
  • Verification of employment income must be through an employer or payslips and documented
  • The attribution assessment of ongoing employment income at an unchanging rate is based on the customer's base rate. This then excludes overtime and shift work where the customer's ongoing rate may not be known at the time of the assessment. Service Officers should make this decision based on the information to hand and not issue a separate request to the employer to obtain this information

The customer contacts to advise ongoing employment income at an unchanging rate

  • The rate variation caused by ongoing employment income at an unchanging rate is not attributable unless the customer's employment income paid has been verified through payslips or an employer

Recording ongoing employment income which trigger the cancellation of the customer's payment due to employment income precluding payment

  • The cancellation is attributable to the review if the ongoing employment income are verified, through payslips or an employer, as ongoing employment income at an unchanging rate
  • The customer must have been paid entitlement for the period immediately prior to the cancellation action. If the customer was not paid entitlement immediately prior to the cancellation action, then the Pay rate zero immediately prior to a SUS or CAN action rule applies

Employment income is Income in One Period (IOP) or Long Period (LOP) related

Employment income (IOP or LOP) is recorded during the review period

  • The rate variation caused by the recording of IOP or LOP employment income is not attributable to a review
  • There are no exceptions to this rule even if the customer declares IOP or LOP employment income after the start of the review

Note: activities which contain IOP or LOP updates are excluded from attribution should not be available for attribution. See Recording of employment income and IOP or LOP exclusions for further details.

The recording of IOP or LOP employment income creates a debt

  • The debt is attributable to the review if the update which created the debt was triggered by the review

Examples of a cancellation action being indirectly triggered by the review contact

Example 1:

Customer contacts the Smart Centre Call to cancel entitlement

  • The Tax File Number Declaration (TDF) review was started on 1 February and the Review Contact Period end date is 23 February
  • The customer contacts the Smart Centre Call to advise return to work and requests cancellation of payment on 10 February

Example 2:

Second Contact Letter is issued

  • The TDF review started on 1 February. The first Review Contact Period was 1 February to 23 February
  • A second contact letter is issued on 23 February and the Review Contact Period is 23 February to 17 March
  • The customer contacts the Smart Centre Call to voluntarily cancel payment and requests cancellation of payment on 8 March

Example 3:

Customer's Payment is Cancelled DNL (Did Not Lodge)

  • The TDF review started on 1 February and the Review Contact Period is 1 February to 23 February
  • The customer lodges a Reporting Statement (Application for Payment) (SU19) via self service (online or phone), in person or writing form on 6 February then ceases to lodge
  • The customer's payment is cancelled DNL on 4 March

Although the date the cancellation was processed was outside of the Review Contact Period, the cancellation was triggered by the customer not lodging on 20 February which was within the Review Contact Period.

Examples of cancellation actions not triggered by the review contact

Example 1:

Failure to attend occurred before the review start

  • The TDF review started on 1 February and the Review Contact Period is 1 February to 23 February
  • The customer is cancelled on 10 February for a failure to attend a seminar on 30 January

Example 2:

Cancellation is directly related to another review

  • The TDF review started on 1 February and the Review Contact Period is 1 February to 23 February
  • The customer's payment is cancelled 'Not a Student' as part of a started Centrelink Academic Reassessment Transformation (CART) review
  • Although the cancellation may be available to attribute to the TDF review, the cancellation was directly triggered by the CART review and should be able to be manually attributed to the CART review at finalisation of that review

Note: if the correct process is followed and actions are created after selecting the review, then these actions will already be attributed to the right review and not available to attribute elsewhere.

Example 3:

Cancellation not within a Review Contact Period

  • A Cash Economy (CSH) review started on 1 February when a customer contact letter was issued. The Review Contact Period is 1 February to 23 February
  • There is no response to the contact and a letter is issued to a government Housing Authority on 10 March. No Review Contact Period is set up for this type of contact
  • The customer's payment is voluntarily cancelled on 20 March by the customer's local Service Centre
  • Although the cancellation may be available to attribute to the CSH review, the cancellation was indirectly triggered and did not occur within a Review Contact Period

Family assistance debts

A family assistance qualification debt has a Debt Reason code of 'FRR' (FAO Re-reconciliation) or 'FRC' (FAO Reconciliation)

  • The debt is not attributable to the review as it has been identified by the FAO system as a debt which will be included as part of family assistance reconciliation or re-reconciliation
  • IRS will exclude the debt from attribution if the Debt Reason code is 'FRR' or 'FRC'

A qualification debt is for a period within the current financial year

  • The debt raised for the current financial year is attributable to a review
  • Note: raise the debt with a different Debt Reason other than 'FRR' or 'FRC' or it will be excluded from attribution

A qualification debt is not for a period within the current financial year

  • The debt is not attributable to the review because it will be included as part of family assistance reconciliation or re-reconciliation

A qualification debt covers more than 1 financial year

  • The debt is not attributable to the review if it includes any element of a previous financial year
  • The debts should be raised separately, for each financial year, to ensure that the family assistance debt for the current financial year is attributable to the review. Note: care must be taken to ensure that the debt reason is not 'FRR' (FAO reconciliation) or 'FRC' (FAO reconciliation) or it will be excluded by IRS

The debt is an income related debt

  • The debt is not attributable to the review

Family assistance rate variations

Family assistance rate changes other than Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Rent Assistance (RA) cannot be attributed to a review regardless of whether the update was qualification related.

This is a technical constraint caused by the FAO system which identifies all FAO activities as reconciliation related therefore they are excluded from attribution by IRS.

General rule to determine if an action is attributable to the review

The general rule for determining whether an action can be attributed to a review is where the review:

  • directly triggered the action, or
  • indirectly triggered the action and the trigger for that action occurred within a Review Contact Period and is not directly attributable to another started review or review process

The action must also meet additional rules outlined in the Resources page as there are some circumstances where an action, such as a suspension or cancellation should not be included as part of a review result.

Note: restoration, grant or re-grant actions should only be attributed to a review if the preceding suspension or cancellation is attributed to the review.

General rule to determine if an action related to a review

A compliance or fraud review is conducted because there is a justified, documented risk of incorrect payment which is a result of fraud, misrepresentation, error or omission on the part of the customer, rather than a system, coding or assessment error.

The savings associated with a compliance or fraud review is based on the savings the review has realised with updates to the customer's payment which relate solely to this risk.

The general rule for determining whether an action is attributable to a review is:

  • the action relates to the reason for the review
  • the action was directly triggered or indirectly triggered by the review
  • the information which changed the customer's rate was not known prior to the start of the review, and
  • the action directly relates to fraud, misrepresentation, error or omission on the part of the customer

Late Lodgement rules

The customer has lodged late for two paydays immediately prior to the review start

  • The exact number of days that the customer has last late lodged can be taken into account to determine whether the suspension or cancellation is attributable to a review
  • The cancellation is only attributable if the customer has likely received the review letter before the extended DTL has been reached
  • Where the number of days for late lodgement are different, the most recent number of days the customer has late lodged must be used to extend the DTL date

The customer has lodged late but has not lodged late for the two paydays immediately prior to the review start.

  • The suspension or cancellation is not attributable to the review

More than the Maximum Rate results

A 'more than maximum rate' savings can be calculated if more than one 'SUS' or 'CAN' action is attributed to the review without the restoration action which occurred between these actions.

This is identified by the Review Result displaying savings which were more than the customer's fortnightly entitlement amount.

  • Attribute the restoration which occurred prior to the second suspension or cancellation action, or
  • Unattribute the first suspension or cancellation action so that the most recent suspension or cancellation action is attributed to the review

Recording of employment income and IOP or LOP exclusions

Recording of e employment income

Despite how the customer's employment income is recorded on the system (IOP, LOP or any other code), the employment income update is only attributable to a review if the customer has verified on-going employment income where the base rate is unchanging. For Employment Income Interventions (EIIP and EIIM) employment income does not need to be verified.

Employment income is not attributable to a review.

Evidence of the verified employment income must be documented. Note: the base rate can be used if the customer is subject to shift work or overtime which is not always known at the time the determination is being made. This decision is based on the information already to hand, and must not be obtained separately via a second contact to a third party.

IOP or LOP Exclusions

IOP or LOP is recorded in an activity which has another attributable rate change

  • IOP or LOP is an excluded action therefore any other customer rate changes which are included in that activity will not be attributable to the review. Create the action in a separate activity

IOP or LOP is included in an activity which cancels the customer's payment

  • The cancellation action is not available for attribution to the review as the cancellation was processed from within an activity that was excluded from attribution
  • The cancellation action must be processed in a separate activity for it to be available for attribution

IOP or LOP is included in an activity but did not impact the customer's next future payment at the time the update was recorded

  • The activity will be excluded from attribution but should be available to attribute. Report these to the Senior Practitioner or Quality Development Officer (QDO)

Third Party debts

Third Party debt is created from within the review by selecting the review before performing the action

  • The third party debt is attributable to the review. If the debt is created in a different environment than the review then the debt will not be able to be attributed. This is a technical constraint

The Third Party debt was manually created

  • The Third Party debt will not be able to be attributed to the review as there is no link back to the originating review
