Eligibility for northbound assistance and transhipment

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Liable to pay shipping costs + Read more ...

Claimants must have incurred and paid the cost of shipping before submitting a claim for assistance.

Note: the exception to this rule is where goods are shipped to the mainland for the purpose of transhipment and an agreement exists between two parties. The seller may claim the assistance, even though the buyer has incurred and paid the cost of shipping.

Does the claimant have the responsibility of bearing the cost of shipping?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the claimant is not eligible to claim assistance. Procedure ends here

2

Previous assistance paid + Read more ...

Has assistance been paid to any other person or business in regards to the shipment of goods currently being claimed?

  • Yes, the claimant is not eligible to claim assistance. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Eligible goods + Read more ...

The claimant can only make a claim for assistance for eligible goods. See the Resources page for a link to the agency's website for a list of eligible northbound goods They must be produced or manufactured in Tasmania for:

  • permanent use or sale on mainland Australia, or
  • shipping to mainland Australia for transhipment with the purpose of shipping to other markets

Note: the following are not eligible northbound goods shipped loose in a ship's hold (bulk cargo) that are not packages or have any form of unitisation

Is the claimant making a claim for assistance for eligible goods?

  • Yes,
    • for shipment to other markets, go to Step 4
    • for domestic shipments, go to Step 5
  • No, the claimant is not eligible to claim assistance. Procedure ends here

4

Transhipment + Read more ...

Assistance may be payable for eligible northbound goods with a final destination other than the mainland.

To be eligible for assistance for transhipment, goods sent to mainland Australia must be off-loaded from one ship. The goods are then loaded onto the same or different ship or aircraft for carriage to their final destination. At the time of claiming, the following do not need to be known:

  • final destination of the goods, and
  • date the goods are transported to other markets

Does the shipment of goods meet the eligibility requirements for transhipment?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the claimant is not eligible to claim assistance for transhipment. Procedure ends here

5

Claimant is eligible for assistance + Read more ...

The claimant can submit a claim for northbound assistance or for the expanded component of the scheme.

See the Resources page for a link to the agency's website for claim forms and instructions on how to submit a claim.

Engaged in the primary production industry + Read more ...

The claimant must be engaged in the agriculture, forestry or fishing industry in Tasmania.

Is the claimant engaged in the primary production industry?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the claimant is not eligible to claim southbound assistance for primary industries. Procedure ends here

4

Eligible goods + Read more ...

The claimant can only make a claim for eligible southbound goods. The goods being shipped need to be used as:

  • material inputs in the primary production industry, or
  • are machinery, implements, or equipment for use in this industry

Goods can also be classed as eligible if used by a person as an input into a process that is carried out in the course of activities listed in Division A of the Australian and New Zealand Standard Industrial Classification (ANZSIC) 2006. The process must be:

  • used in the course of the claimant's main business activity, and
  • used as an input to an item that is an output of the claimant's main business activity

Goods must not be listed as ineligible southbound goods.

Are the goods being shipped eligible goods?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the claimant is not eligible to claim southbound assistance for primary industries. Procedure ends here

Claimant is eligible + Read more ...

The claimant can submit a claim for southbound assistance for primary producers.

Eligibility for King Island and Furneaux Group Intrastate (KIFG) assistance

Does the claimant have the responsibility of bearing the cost of shipping?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the claimant is not eligible to claim assistance. Procedure ends here

2

Previous assistance paid + Read more ...

Has assistance been paid to any other person or business in regards to the shipment of goods currently being claimed?

  • Yes, the claimant is not eligible to claim assistance. Procedure ends here

3

Eligible goods + Read more ...

To be eligible to make a claim for intrastate assistance, the goods being shipped must be eligible goods. The goods must be produced or manufactured on the main island of Tasmania for permanent use or sale on King Island or the Furneaux Group and vice versa.

To be eligible the claimant must be engaged in:

  • the manufacturing industry - raw materials or equipment for use by the claimant in the claimants manufacturing processes
  • the mining industry - raw material or equipment for use by the claimant in the claimants production processes
  • the agriculture, forestry or fishing industry - material inputs to, or machinery, implements and equipment for use in, that industry

Goods can also be classed as eligible if used by a person as an input into a process that is carried out in the course of activities listed in Division A of the Australia and New Zealand Standard Industrial Classification (ANZSIC 2006). The process must be:

  • used in the course of the claimant's main business activity, and
  • used as an input to an item that is an output of the claimant's main business activity

Goods must not be listed as ineligible intrastate goods.

Are the goods being shipped eligible goods?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the claimant is not eligible to claim assistance. Procedure ends here

4

Claimant is eligible + Read more ...

Eligibility requirements for brood mares and foals

Table 7

Step

Action

1

Liable to pay shipping costs + Read more ...

Claimants must have incurred and paid the cost of shipping before submitting a claim for assistance.

Does the claimant have the responsibility of bearing the cost of shipping?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the claimant is not eligible to claim assistance. Procedure ends here

2

Previous assistance paid + Read more ...

Has assistance been paid to any other person or business in regards to the shipment of goods currently being claimed?

  • Yes, the claimant is not eligible to claim assistance. Procedure ends here
  • No,
    • for trips between Tasmania and mainland Australia, go to Step 3
    • for trips between KIFG and the main island of Tasmania, go to Step 4

3

Round trip between Tasmanian and mainland Australia + Read more ...

Assistance cannot be paid for brood mares until a round trip has been completed.

Note: foals do not need to make a round trip, however they must be transported to Tasmania before the age of 6 months. Assistance may be paid if the foal is the progeny of a brood mare who is on mainland Australia for stud purposes. The brood mare does not need to accompany the foal.

Has the brood mare made a round trip between Tasmania and mainland Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the claimant is not eligible to claim assistance Procedure ends here

4

Transportation from King Island and the Furneaux Group (KIFG) + Read more ...

Assistance may be claimed for a brood mare shipped from KIFG and the main island of Tasmania for stud purposes.

Note: foals may be eligible for assistance, as long as they are transported to KIFG before the age of 6 months. The foal must be the progeny of a brood mare who is on the main island of Tasmania for stud purposes. The brood mare does not need to accompany the foal.

Has the brood mare been shipped to the main island of Tasmania from KIFG for stud purposes?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the claimant is not eligible to claim assistance. Procedure ends here

5

Claimant is eligible + Read more ...

The claimant can submit a claim for assistance for the transportation of brood mare.

See Resources page for a link to the agency's website for claim forms and instructions on how to submit a claim.
