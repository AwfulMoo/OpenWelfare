3

Sale of home and purchase of another home - exemptions + Read more ...

If the customer sold their home and is planning to purchase another home, but they have not yet purchased another home, they are still a homeowner during the initial 12 months until they move into their new home.

An additional 12 months exemption may be available only if the customer has a continuing intention to apply the proceeds of the sale to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate a new principal home and they:

  • have made reasonable attempts to obtain a new principal home, and
  • have been making those attempts within a reasonable period (i.e., 6 months) after selling the principal home (evidence may be required), and
  • have experienced delays beyond their control in obtaining a new principal home

Where a customer is determined to be eligible for an additional 12 month exemption, to code the extended exemption period see Table 3, Step 4.

During the 12 or 24 month exemption period:

  • The value of principal home sale proceeds that are intended to be used to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate a new principal home is exempt under the assets test
  • The full amount is still deemed
  • The customer is considered a homeowner

2

Loan agreement + Read more ...

Money loaned by a customer is an assessable asset. The value is the amount owed to the customer but does not include any interest payable on the loan. Loans are subject to deeming provisions.

Only those payments received within the initial 12 months of the sale are exempt from the Assets Test if:

  • a customer has sold their principal home by instalments or the payment is deferred, and
  • they intend to use the funds from this sale to purchase or build another home within 12 months

Code the loan details on customer's Direct Investment (SVDI) screen. If customer is entitled to any asset exemption as per note above, insert this value in the Assets Test Dedn Amt: field.

For more information about how to code loans, see the Detail section of Loans.

Sale agreement only + Read more ...

Does the sale agreement state interest is payable on the outstanding balance?

4

Valuation + Read more ...

The payments paid and/or the payments expected to be paid under the sale agreement will be multiplied by a discount factor to determine if a valuation is required.

The result is compared to the current market value of the property to determine if deprivation has occurred.

The discount factor will depend on:

  • the upper deeming rate at the date of the agreement
  • the term, in years, for the repayment
  • whether the purchase price is paid in regular instalments or as a single payment

Is the outstanding balance to be paid in a single payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, in equal instalments, go to Step 6

5

Single payment + Read more ...

Calculate discount factor

1 - (N x R) + {N x R x (N - 1) x R/2}

N = term for repayment in years

R = upper deeming rate at the date of agreement, for example 6% is equal to 0.06 at the date of the agreement.

Multiply the amount to be received by the result of the above calculation that is the discount factor. This gives the estimated current value.

See Step 1 in the Home ownership and deprivation assessment table.

6

Equal instalments + Read more ...

Calculate discount factor

1 - (N x R/2) + {N x R x (N - 1) x R/4}

N = term for repayment in years

R = upper deeming rate at the date of agreement, for example 6% is equal to 0.06 at the date of the agreement.

Multiply the total amount to be received by the result of the above calculation; that is, the discount factor. This gives the estimated current value.

2

Determine deprivation + Read more ...

If the property appears to have been sold below its true market value a valuation is required (if not already received) as deprivation may apply.

Deprivation is determined by obtaining an actuarial valuation and an approved valuation and calculating any difference:

  • Request an actuarial valuation to obtain the true value of the purchase price
  • To establish the actual value of the home, request a valuation of the property from an authorised valuer unless a valuation has already been completed. See Valuation of real estate and other assets at new claim
  • When the valuations are returned, assess if deprivation has occurred. The deprivation amount is the difference between the approved valuation from an authorised valuer and the actuarial valuation

Did the customer gift the property or sell it for less than its value?

  • Yes, deprivation may have occurred. Record Gift as per Assessing deprivation/gifting. Go to Step 3
  • No, include the details in a DOC at the end of the process. Go to Step 3

Customer's new accommodation + Read more ...

What is the customer's new accommodation situation?

  • If the customer:
    • is currently in an aged care home (that is, no change to address required) and has sold their principal home,
    • any exemption from the Income Test and Assets Test no longer applies unless the partner was still residing in the home at the time of the sale, and/or
    • the partner is entitled to an exemption for the principal home

  • Has purchased another home, see Purchasing another residence. Procedure ends here
  • Intends to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate a new principal home within 12 months, go to Step 4

4

Treatment of sale proceeds where customer intends to purchase or build another home within 12 months + Read more ...

Obtain details from the customer of the expected date of completion/finalisation of sale and expected total cost of the new home.

Note: the start date for the assessment of sale proceeds depends on the circumstances of the sale, it may not always be the same as the settlement date. See Resources for examples.

Code the proceeds of sale, depending on where they have been invested as follows:

Bank account

  • If the funds have been deposited into an existing account, locate the account on Savings Summary (SVS) screen
  • If the funds have been deposited into a new account, add a new account Select Product (SVP) screen
  • Code the investment details on the Deposit Accounts (SVDA) screen, including the Asset test deduction amount: field with the funds to be used for the new house purchase, build, repair or renovate a new principal home

Managed funds or shares

  • Add new managed investments on the Managed Investment Product List (MIP) screen
  • Add new shares on the Select Product (SIP) screen
  • Code the investment details including the Asset test deduction amount: field with the funds to be used for the new house purchase, build, repair or renovate a new principal home

Loans to other parties

  • The customer must state they will partially or fully recover the loan within the exemption period and use the funds for their new principal home
  • Code the loan details on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen, including the Asset test deduction amount: field with the funds to be used for the new house purchase, build, repair or renovate a new principal home

Income Streams

If the purchase date of the income stream is on or after 1 January 2015, the income stream will be subject to the deemed income provisions, however the asset test exemption can still apply. The customer must commute part, or all, of the income stream and use the funds for their new principal home prior to expiration of the exemption period.

The customer must state they will partially or fully commute the income stream within the exemption period and use the money to purchase another principal home.

  • Account-based income streams subject to deeming, for example, Cat 9 non-grandfathered products, cannot be coded on the Pension/Annuities Identification (SUPI) or Pension/Annuities Details Variable (SUPV) screens where an exemption for principal home sale proceeds is applicable. This is because there is no asset test deduction facility on the SUPV screen
  • To provide the customer with an asset test deduction for the sale proceeds, but still apply deeming to the account balance for income test purposes, code the account-based income stream on the Deposit Accounts (SVDA) screen with the Investment Type as ‘OTH’. Code the amount intended to be used to purchase a new principal home in the Assets Test Deduction Amt: field
  • If any funds remain in the income stream after the purchase of the new principal home, cancel the Direct Investments (SVDI) record. Recode the income stream as normal on the SUPI/SUPV screens

See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services for more detail

Go to Step 5.

5

Purchasing or building a new home + Read more ...

  • If the customer already owns the land (outright or mortgaged) on which they intend to build their principal home, the land is usually no longer assessable. For an example of a situation where the land previously owned may not be fully exempt, see the second example in the table in the Resources page
  • If the land is recorded on the customer’s record, the asset value must be amended
  • On the Real Estate/Business Identifying (REBI) screen, update:
    • Sold/Gifted/Ceased: field with the ‘no assessable asset value’ option
  • On the Real Estate Details (RE) screen, code:
    • % Asset Owned: field as '0', and
    • Current Market Value $: field as '0'
  • The maximum total asset value, which may be exempt, is the value of the proceeds of the sale of the old principal home. The Resources page contains examples of the assessment of land pre and post 1/7/2007
  • If the customer purchases a block of land to be used to build their new principal residence, with the proceeds of the sale, the block of land is not assessable and does not need to be coded on the Real Estate/Business Summary (REBS) screen
  • If a review is needed (e.g. for earlier date of purchase or completion of construction) before the auto 12 month review set up per AC screen coding, set review (code - 'PUR') to follow up asset exemption
  • Advise the customer to provide details of any payments made for the deposit on their new home or construction costs so their investments can be adjusted. Updates can be made to the savings account as it decreases in value when the customer uses the funds, for example progressive payments for a house being built. Remember also, to adjust the Asset test deduction amount: field on the SVDA screen by the equal value. See the Resources page for examples.
    Note: funds held in a solicitor's trust accounts, pending the signing of a legally binding unconditional contract of purchase, are considered financial investments of the customer. Code funds on the SVDI screen until settlement, type 'Other'. Code the balance and the Asset Test Deduction Amount
  • Update home ownership on Accommodation (AC) screen. Code:
    • Reason Home Vacated: field using ‘SAL’
    • Date Home Vacated: field
    • Home Ownership: field using 'LHO'
  • If applicable, update rent details and request appropriate rent verification. If coding Home Ownership: field (LHO) see Qualification for payment of Rent Assistance (RA) and Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance
  • Customers who have sold their home and purchased a mobile home to live in may be homeowners or non-homeowners, see Assessing caravans, boats and park homes
  • Record details on a DOC using Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Assessments and Reviews > Income and Assets > Sold principal home

For information on possible entitlement to Rent Assistance, see Qualification for payment of Rent Assistance (RA).

Procedure ends here.

Unable to access sale funds due to temporary legal restriction + Read more ...

When a customer’s principal home is sold they may be unable to access the funds for legal reasons. This can be the result of situations such as a pending property settlement following the breakdown of a relationship or the settlement of an estate.

From the date of sale of the home until the date a legal settlement is reached, if the customer does not have a legal entitlement to the proceeds of the sale, a nil income or asset value is assessed for the duration of this period. For this period the customer is assessed as a non-homeowner.

Has the customer’s share of the proceeds from the sale of the home been determined?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, update their accommodation details, record the details of the sale (including where the funds are being held) and details of the expected court or hearing date in a DOC
    • Set a manual review to follow up the property settlement
    • Procedure ends here

8

Proceeds of sale after legal restriction ceased + Read more ...

If:

  • Sale of the home occurred less than 12 months ago: depending on what the customer intends to do, treat the funds as per Steps 4 (intend to build/purchase), 5 (already owns land and intends to build) or 6 (no intention to build/purchase). However, the 12 month asset test exemption period begins from the date of sale so the customer may only benefit from the balance of the remaining months. While the asset test exemption is in place the customer is assessed as a homeowner
  • Sale of the home occurred between 12 and 24 months ago: depending on what the customer intends to do, treat the funds as per Steps 4 (intend to build/purchase), 5 (already owns land and intends to build) or 6 (no intention to build/purchase). However, the 24 month asset test exemption period begins from the date of sale so the customer may only benefit from the balance of the remaining months. While the asset test exemption is in place the customer is assessed as a homeowner.
  • Sale of home occurred more than 24 months ago: the customer’s share of the funds are treated as an asset and deeming applied from the date the customer is entitled to receive the funds. An asset test exemption is not applicable and the customer is assessed as a non-homeowner. Go to step 6

10

Extension of exemptions + Read more ...

Is the customer seeking an extension to the asset tests exemption for up to an additional 12 months?

An additional 12 months exemption may be available only if the customer has a continuing intention to apply the proceeds of the sale to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate a new principal home and they:

  • have made reasonable attempts to obtain a new principal home, and
  • have been making those attempts within a reasonable period (i.e., 6 months) after selling the principal home (evidence may be required), and
  • have experienced delays beyond their control in obtaining a new principal home

The customer must also provide evidence of this.

Has an extended extension been granted?

Yes, apply the extended exemption See Purchasing another residence > Granting an extended exemption:

  • Advise the customer that the extension has been extended for up to 12 months, and they have an obligation to advise within this time period when they have purchased, or completed building, rebuilding, repairing or renovating a new principal home
    • Finalise the review
    • Record details in a DOC
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 12

12

Failed to purchase or build a new principal home + Read more ...

If the customer has failed to purchase or build a new principal home by the end of exemption period:

  • the customer is treated as a non-homeowner
  • proceeds of sale are assessed as an asset and subject to deeming

On the Saving Summary (SVS) screen, select the account(s) where the proceeds of the sale where recorded and remove any Asset test deduction amount.

  • the Event Date is the end date of the exemption period.

If land either intended or in the process of building a home is recorded on the Real Estate/Business Identifying (REBI) screen as ‘no assessable asset value’. Record the land on Real Estate (RE) screen as an assessable asset if it was previously recorded with no assessable asset value.

  • the Event Date is the end date of the exemption period.

On the Accommodation details (AC) screen:

  • update the Home Ownership field with, the required non-homeowner code
    • the Event Date is the end date of the exemption period.
    • clear the Reason Home Vacated field
    • clear the Date Home Vacated field
  • update partner's AC screen if relevant
  • finalise the review
  • record details in a DOC using Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Sale of principal home review

Procedure ends here
