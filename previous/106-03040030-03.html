Managing the receipt of an incorrect claim

Step

Action

1

Claim is lodged + Read more ...

Customer lodges a completed claim for a social security payment or a supplementary payment. Accept and register the lodgement of the customer's claim form. Is the customer qualified for the payment they are claiming?

  • Yes, the claim should be processed as a new claim, see New claim coding and assessment for Centrelink payments. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

The customer is not eligible for the payment they are claiming + Read more ...

  • Advise the customer that they may not be eligible for the payment they are claiming
  • Manually reject the incorrect claim with the required rejection code and record the details of the rejection on a DOC. See Rejecting a Centrelink claim
  • Note: where an online claim is deemed to be a claim for a different service reason, that is, an indexed 'YAL' claim should be processed as an 'AUS' claim, the claim may be re-indexed. A new claim is not needed unless further information is required

Is the customer eligible for a different type of payment or benefit?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Issue claim forms or direct to the Services Australia website for the appropriate payment + Read more ...

  • After identifying the most appropriate payment for the customer to claim, issue the required claim forms or direct the customer to complete their claim online.
  • Request that the customer lodge the correct claim form with the required supporting documents as soon as possible. See Documents required for Centrelink new claims
  • From 1 July 2018, eligible customers will receive payments or concession cards from the date they actually submit a complete claim with all relevant supporting documentation. Note: different processes apply for customers in vulnerable circumstances, see Intent to claim and vulnerable customers. If it is possible the customer is in vulnerable circumstances all contacts about a claim need to be recorded in a DOC.
  • Record the details of the incorrect claim on a DOC

Go to Step 5.

4

The customer is not eligible for any other payments or benefits + Read more ...

Customers who are not qualified for other payments or benefits, should be offered an appropriate referral to either a Centrelink or an external specialist where required.

For more information, see Specialist officers and other internal supports for customers

Procedure ends here.

5

Correct claim is lodged + Read more ...

If the first (incorrect) claim is for a social security payment other than a supplementary payment then the second claim must also be for a social security payment.

If the first (incorrect) claim is for a supplementary payment, the second claim must also be for a supplementary payment.

  • Upon receipt of the completed correct claim, ensure that the claims are of a similar nature. Check the correct claim against the incorrect claim to compare questions. If there are new or different questions on the correct claim, ensure the customer has completed those questions
  • If the subsequent claim was lodged more than 13 weeks after the initial claim, the customer should be asked to provide proof as to why they could not lodge the correct claim earlier
  • For more information, see Sighting, recording and returning original documents
  • Note: if the customer cannot supply reasonable proof, the start day for the correct claim will be deemed to be the date of lodgement of the correct claim. There is a different assessment of start date where claims for family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payments have been lodged
  • Make a note on the correct claim 'refer to incorrect claim for (payment type) lodged on (date)'
  • Ensure that the customer signs the correct claim, and provides the required documentation for the correct payment. Note: online claim do not need to be signed, they are considered electronically signed once submitted be the customer

6

Calculating the start day for the correct claim + Read more ...

  • The start day of the correct claim may be backdated to the date of lodgement of the incorrect claim where the customer has:
    • lodged a subsequent claim for a social security payment of a similar nature, or
    • lodged a subsequent claim for a supplementary payment
    • lodged the subsequent claim within a 13 week period, or supplied proof as to why it was not lodged within the 13 weeks
    • met all the qualification criteria for the correct payment for the time period between the two claims
  • An earlier start date may apply where the claim meets backdating provisions for claim lodgement
  • Before finalising the new claim activity, record the determined start date on the appropriate start day screen for the customer's payment type
  • Finalise the new claim and record the details of the decision on a DOC
