This document outlines the process for staff in International Services (CIS) to finalise coding of embargoes and reconcile embargo schedules that are sent by foreign pension authorities.

On this Page:

Recovery by embargo

Some agreements provide for one country to withhold amounts from any lump sum arrears associated with the grant of pensions and send them to the other country to recover a debt that arises because of the arrears amount. This is referred to as an embargo. Information on outgoing embargoes under the Agreement with New Zealand is contained in another procedure. See New Zealand Agreement outgoing embargo - action for International Services (CIS).

Embargo types

There are 2 types of embargoes.

For an indirect embargo country:

  • The foreign pension authorities send the notice of grant to CIS and request the amount of the Australian debt to be withheld from the arrears
  • CIS code the ongoing pension, calculate the arrears debt and send a request to the other country to withhold that amount from the arrears
  • The foreign pension authorities send the requested amount to CIS in an agreed schedule and send any remaining amounts to the customer

For a direct embargo country:

  • The foreign pension authorities send the notice of grant to CIS
  • CIS code the ongoing pension
  • The foreign pension authorities arrange to send the whole amount of the arrears to CIS in an agreed schedule
  • CIS then code and calculate the arrears debt
  • CIS recover the debt from the foreign pension arrears and send any remaining amount to the customer

Note: if the embargo action fails, CIS raise the arrears debt in accordance with normal debt procedures.

Recording embargoes

Arrears debts and embargoes are managed in the Foreign Pension System (FPS) with automated reviews to track the progress following the initial coding of the grant of a foreign pension, see Foreign Pension System (FPS) status and reviews.

Details of recovery of debts by embargo are coded on separate screens in FPS rather than in the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS). Embargo action generally requires the adjustment of DMIS activities.

Exchange rates and embargo

Exchange rates are used to convert foreign currency to Australian dollars in assessing amounts included in the income test, see Foreign currency and exchange rates. These are different from the exchange rates used by other countries to convert foreign currency to Australian dollars when sending embargo amounts to Australia.

This means that there is always a difference in the amount requested by us and the amount received from the other country. Where the difference is only due to these different exchange rate conversions, policy advice is that the debt is considered fully repaid and no further action should be taken. For this reason embargo coding generally requires the adjustment of the calculated arrears debt amount to match the amount actually received.

Reconciliation of embargoes

Foreign pension authorities send bulk amounts of all monies withheld under embargo to us on an agreed cycle, usually each month. The Finance Team in Zone Tasmania confirm receipt of the monies to CIS.

The foreign pension authorities also usually send a schedule with details of individual amounts for all customers included in the payment for that cycle. Once the schedule is received, CIS code the embargo on the system and arrange to pay any excess amounts to the customer. CIS then send lists of all action taken to the Finance Team who reconcile the monies received.

Failed embargoes must be recorded and raised in DMIS in accordance with normal debt procedures.

The Resources page contains links to the International Services homepage and International Programme homepage. It also contains a link to ESSentials Support Online for the procedure to pay excess amounts to customers.
