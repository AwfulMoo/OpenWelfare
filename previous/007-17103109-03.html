Establishing a CCS link with the AIR

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Check CCS link with the AIR + Read more ...

When assessing CCS, ensure the outcome is correct before finalising the claim. If the assessment result displays a CCS status of 'not eligible' and a reason of 'immunisation not met' check that a successful AIR link has been established.

Note: if the customer is also claiming or receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB), see Immunisation requirements for FTB Part A from 1 July 2018.

To check the CCS-AIR link status:

  • Within the claim activity:
    • From select the child
    • From go to the Child Immunisation Details (CID) table
  • Standalone update (Change of circumstance):
    • From select the child
    • In Super Key, key 'CIM' to go to the CIM screen

If the child record is linked with the AIR, the following will display:

  • Status: 'Yes' or 'No' status
  • Reason Code: 5 digits, starting with a '10’
  • AIR Response: 'A' (Accept)

If the child record has not linked with the AIR, the following will display:

  • Reason Code: 5 digits, starting with a '9', ‘8’ or ‘7’
  • AIR Response: of R (Reject) or W (wait)
  • Status: no information will exist

Has the child record linked with the AIR?

  • Yes, continue processing the claim and assess CCS based on the immunisation status provided (either a 'Yes' or 'No'). Note: if message SR210 MY CHILD CARE: Child DOB mismatch between CLK and AIR presents after linking, continue processing claim and go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Child record not linked with AIR + Read more ...

If the child's record is not linked with AIR, and the AIR has:

  • provided a W (wait) response and warning 'SR212: MY CHILD CARE: WAIT for AIR manual matching to complete', go to Step 5
  • returned a R (reject) response 'SR215: MY CHILD CARE: Immunisation status not received. Eligibility for (Child Name) may cease' and:
    • the child is under 3 months of age, go to Step 3
    • the child is over 3 months of age, go to Step 7

Note: if message SR210 MY CHILD CARE: Child DOB mismatch between CLK and AIR presents after linking, continue processing claim and go to Step 8.

3

Child/ren under 3 months of age + Read more ...

Children aged 3 months and under meet immunisation requirements and CCS will grant without the AIR immunisation status. Once the child is over 3 months of age, a link with the AIR is needed to get the child's immunisation status and determine ongoing eligibility for CCS.

If a link cannot be made for a child that is 3 months of age or less, do not delete the ‘R’ rejected link from the CID screen within the claim. Continue processing.

The AIR link request may fail due to:

  • using the parent's Medicare number (from FA081)
  • guessing the Individual Reference Number (IRN)
  • the child's Medicare number is recorded without the IRN
  • the child's name or date of birth is recorded differently in Medicare
  • no Medicare record exists because the child is not enrolled and no immunisation information has been recorded by a vaccination provider

If any of this information is available, an attempt can be made to establish a link inside a claim, after initial attempt is unsuccessful. For details go to Step 7.

If still unsuccessful continue processing claim.

To request a link after CCS has been granted, go to Step 4.

4

Setting up a link after CCS granted + Read more ...

If a customer is CCS current for a child 3 months of age or under, and is providing the child’s Medicare details, record the details on the child’s Child Immunisation Details (CIM) screen in Process Direct.

There are separate AIR links for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and CCS. For CCS, a link to the AIR is established using the CID/CIM tables in Process Direct. Updating the FTB Medicare Information (MEI) screen in Customer First will not establish a link to the AIR for CCS.

In Process Direct:

  • From select the child
  • In the Super Key field, key 'CIM' to go to the CIM screen
  • Select and code the child’s Medicare number and IRN
  • Select the Get AIR Data button
  • If the AIR link is successful, a reason code starting with '10' will display
    • Save the AIR immunisation status details (this may be a status of 'Yes' or 'No') and Finalise the transaction
    • A confirmed line will display on the CIM table in the child’s record
  • if the AIR link is not successful, a reason code starting with a 9, 8 or 7 will display, and an AIR Response of either:
    • W, go to Step 5, or
    • R, go to Step 7

5

Waiting for the AIR response + Read more ...

If the AIR system cannot respond straight away, the Status column will be blank and the AIR Response will display W (wait). When waiting on a response from the AIR, this message displays within a claim: 'SR212: MY CHILD CARE: WAIT for AIR manual matching to complete'.

  • If the AIR gives a W response, place the claim or transaction on hold for 2 business days. This lets the AIR attempt to manually complete the link
  • State the hold reason as ‘Referral to policy/helpdesk’
  • Record progress in Notes
  • After 2 business days, if:
    • the warning still presents, go to Step 6, or
    • an AIR link has been established, assess the claim based on the information transmitted. See Processing CCS claims

6

Claim allocated after hold and W response is still present + Read more ...

Refer the record to a Centrelink AIR Liaison Officer if:

  • the claim has come off hold
  • it has been allocated to a Service Officer, and
  • the W response is still present

Include the following details in any escalations to Centrelink-AIR Liaison staff:

  • Parent CRN:
  • Affected Child:
  • Claim ID: (if applicable)
  • CCS Issues page checked: YES/NO
  • Issue:

Centrelink AIR Liaison staff will troubleshoot the linking request.

Hold the claim for a further 2 business days and document the record with the action taken.

Procedure ends here.

7

Unsuccessful link with the AIR + Read more ...

If an R (reject) Reason Code is returned when attempting a link to the AIR in Process Direct, hold the cursor over the reason code and hover help text will display. For more information, key the Reason Code into Roxy Digital Assistant. Roxy's response may help to troubleshoot the link request.

There are many reasons why a link with AIR may be unsuccessful, such as:

  • the child’s details may differ between Centrelink and Medicare (spelling or positioning of name, date of birth (DOB) etc.)
  • using the parent's Medicare number (from FA081)
  • guessing the Individual Reference Number (IRN)
  • recording the child's Medicare number without the IRN
  • the child's name or date of birth is recorded differently in Medicare
  • the child's Medicare number was not provided
  • no Medicare record exists because the child is not enrolled and no immunisation information has been recorded by a vaccination provider
  • the child’s hyphenated name recorded in the Centrelink record may incorrectly have a space recorded before or after the hyphen
  • there may be a system issue, check Network News Updates (NNUs)

If the information is now available, an attempt can be made to establish a link inside a claim, after initial attempt is unsuccessful.

If still unsuccessful, refer the record to a Centrelink AIR Liaison Officer who can confirm the child record exists in Medicare (CDMS) and check the Medicare number and personal details.

Include the following details in any escalations to Centrelink AIR Liaison staff:

  • Parent CRN:
  • Affected Child:
  • Claim ID: (if applicable)
  • CCS Issues page checked: YES/NO
  • Issue:
  • CC your team leader/Service Support Officer/s in the email to ensure response is received/actioned
  • Use Email Classification Official: Sensitive

Hold the claim for 14 days using reason ‘Customer to Provide Information’ to allow time for Medicare enrolment to be completed, if:

  • no Medicare record exists because the child is not enrolled and they recently arrived in Australia, or
  • a customer indicates on the FA081 to enrol the child in Medicare. Ensure there is an open MAENROL document on file in Customer First. If required recreate the MAENROL activity for Medicare enrolment. See Processing proof of a child's birth for the process and scenarios where this may be appropriate

If child details (name or DOB) are corrected in Customer First due to a difference between Medicare and Centrelink, complete a SAP refresh and select Regenerate Claim. A new AIR link request will automatically occur when the regenerated claim is processed.

If none of the above reasons apply, try again to link:

  • From select the child
  • Select My Child Care > Child Immunisation Details (CID) table
  • Select
  • Add the child’s Medicare number (10 digits) and Individual Reference Number (IRN) (1 digit) and select Proceed

Note: the Proceed button can be selected to attempt a link without the Medicare details. Immunisation status will be provided if an AIR link for the child already exists.

If unable to establish a link after following these steps, refer the record to a Centrelink AIR Liaison Officer. Centrelink AIR Liaison staff will troubleshoot the linking request.

8

Child DOB Mismatch between Centrelink and AIR + Read more ...

If the message SR210 MY CHILD CARE: Child DOB mismatch between CLK and AIR presents this should not delay claim processing.

This issue occurs because of a difference between the Child Date of Birth (DOB) on the Centrelink (CLK) and Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) systems.

To action, confirm the child’s DOB and full name by checking scanned verification documents (birth certificate, FA081, passport, etc). If the error is in:

  • Centrelink records, complete correction. See Incorrect date of birth recorded for assistance
  • Medicare Details, send details to Centrelink AIR Liaison, using Fast Note:
    • Select Auto Text use Families > Enquiry > Child DOB Discrepancy to record the information provided by the customer
    • The Fast Note will automatically add the correct keyword and the open DOC will be allocated to the Centrelink AIR Liaison via WLM. Staff must not add/change keywords. Fast Note should include:
      Parents Customer Reference Number (CRN)
      Name and CRN of child effected, with spelling of name confirmed
      Correct child DOB as confirmed by contacting the parent/guardian
      Medicare number, including child’s Individual Reference Number (IRN) if available
      Note: if scanned verification documents are unavailable to confirm the child's DOB, ask the customer to upload a copy
    • See Processing CCS claims for further details.

Customer contacts due to cancellation or rejection (Not Eligible - IMM) + Read more ...

Go to Child Care and select the Entitlement twisty to see why CCS was cancelled or rejected for a child.

  • If cancellation was due to the child not meeting immunisation requirements, and:
    • CCS is Not Eligible-IMM (the only or last child eligible for CCS), or

2

CCS cancelled due to failure to meet immunisation requirements + Read more ...

If a customer contacts advising the child met immunisation requirements before the Immunisation Grace Period (IGP) ended, check if the decision was correct:

  • Refer the customer to their myGov account to check the child’s proof of vaccination. The Resources page links to help for customers on how to get a statement
  • If the statement advises the child does not meet immunisation requirements, refer the customer back to their vaccination provider to ensure:
    • the child is fully immunised or on an approved catch up schedule, and
    • their AIR record is up to date
  • Tell the customer to lodge a new claim once the child meets immunisation requirements
  • Do not refer the customer to the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR)

Note: if the child did not meet Immunisation requirements before the end of the IGP and CCS cancels, the customer will need to lodge a new claim. This may result in a Gap in entitlement depending on when the child met immunisation and when the new claim was lodged.

To identify a period of cancellation due to a child not meeting immunisation requirements

  • Go to Child Care and select the Entitlement twisty:
    • The CCS child will have a Status of Not Eligible and a Status Reason of IMM
    • Where the CCS child has a current Status of Eligible, select the Previous Period button to view previous periods where the customer may have a gap in entitlement
    • Take note of the ‘Not Eligible IMM’ start date
  • From select the child
  • In the Super Key field, key 'CIM' to go to the CIM screen
  • If the immunisation status is Yes and:
    • the Last Immunisation Update Event (LIUE) date provided in the LIUE Date column is prior to the ‘Not Eligible IMM’ start date, CCS can be re-granted. Go to Step 8
  • The customer states the LIUE date provided by the AIR is later than the date their child met requirements or the child met requirements before cancellation but the most recent immunisation status is No, go to Step 3

See the Resources page for scenarios.

Centrelink AIR Liaison has responded + Read more ...

  • met immunisation requirements on or before day 63 of their Immunisation Grace Period (IGP), re-grant CCS. Go to Step 8
  • did not meet immunisation requirements on or before day 63 of their IGP, the cancellation is correct. Advise the customer a new claim is needed. Procedure ends here

5

Claim rejected due to the child/ren failing to meet immunisation requirements + Read more ...

Children must meet immunisation requirements at the time a claim for CCS is determined (date of claim processing). If the child does not meet the immunisation requirements on the date of determination, CCS will reject (Not Eligible IMM). The customer must re-claim CCS when the child meets immunisation requirements.

Customer contacts advising their child met immunisation requirements on the date of determination (date claim processed):

Investigate the record:

  • From select the child
  • In Super Key enter 'CIM' to go to the CIM screen

If the child record has not linked with the AIR, the following will display:

  • Reason Code: 5 digits, starting with a '9', '8' or '7'
  • AIR Response: 'R' (Reject)
  • Status: no information will exist

If the child record is linked with the AIR, the following will display:

  • Status: 'Yes' or 'No' status
  • Reason Code: 5 digits, starting with a '10'
  • AIR Response: 'A' (Accept)

Has the child record linked with the AIR?

  • Yes, and the status is No:
    • Refer the customer to their myGov account to check the child’s proof of vaccination. The Resources page links to help for customers on how to get a statement
    • If the statement advises the child does not meet immunisation requirements, refer the customer back to their vaccination provider to ensure the child is fully immunised and their AIR record is complete
    • Do not refer the customer to the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR)
    • Advise the customer once the Immunisation History Statements shows the child is up to date, lodge a new claim. Record a Note on the customer's record advising of the discussion. Procedure ends here.
    • If the status is No but the customer insists the child met immunisation requirements on the date the claim was determined, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 6

8

Re-granting CCS + Read more ...

Cancellation due to not meeting immunisation requirements

If it has been confirmed the child met immunisation requirements before cancellation, re-grant CCS. The customer does not have to lodge a new claim.

  • Complete an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) or Add child claim for Child Care Subsidy using the 'Not Eligible IMM' start date as the Date of Receipt (DOR) for the claim
  • Note: if the DOR is greater than 12 months in the past, see the Process Direct - Current Issues page for the approved workaround
  • Ensure all information provided in the claim is current based on the customer's circumstances

Finalise the ACC and submit. See Processing CCS claims to process the claim

Claim rejection due to not meeting immunisation requirements

If Centrelink AIR Liaison determines the child was immunised at date of claim determination and the customer's CCS claim was incorrectly rejected. The customer does not need to lodge a new claim.

  • Complete an ACC using the same Date of Receipt of the claim that was incorrectly rejected
  • Ensure all information provided in the claim is the same as the initial claim lodged

ARO's do not have to refer to the Helpdesk for Immunisation exception coding as part of their review process.

Finalise the ACC and submit. See Processing CCS claims to process the claim.

Immunisation day one IGP notification was not sent

Where a customer contacts to query their CCS cancellation or gap in entitlement, or has lodged a subsequent CCS claim and has a gap in entitlement, navigate to:

  • the HS (Customer Letters) table in Process Direct, and
  • the CORRO (correspondence) table in Process Direct, and
  • the outbound correspondence tool in Customer First (check both the current and archive sections) to see if an immunisation day one IGP notification letter was issued, and to note the date
  • confirm the reason CCS cancelled was due to immunisation requirements not being met
  • check the Entitlement table on the Child Care Summary page to note the start and end dates of any gap in eligibility.

If customer was not sent a day one IGP notification, has a ‘Yes’ immunisation status and has a gap in entitlement, complete a referral to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk requesting an immunisation exception.

All referrals must include:

  • Reference Check: Request immunisation exception – day one IGP letter not sent
  • Customer CRN:
  • Name of Child/ren:
  • Start date of gap period:
  • End date of gap period:
  • Confirmation that the HS in Process Direct and the outbound correspondence tool in Customer First has been checked to identify that the day one IGP letter was not issued

Put a Note on the record to advise referral to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk has been completed. If this is part of a claim, place the claim on hold for 7 days pending response from Level 2 Policy Helpdesk.

A response will be returned to the initiating Service Officer for action. If the case is:

  • approved, the initiating Service Officer must apply the decision. Go to Step 9
  • rejected, the customer is not entitled to an immunisation exception. Annotate the original Note stating the reason for rejection. Procedure ends here

Exemption from immunisation requirements

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Immunisation exemptions + Read more ...

Medical exemptions

All medical immunisation exemptions are recorded and managed by the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR). CCS immunisation requirements are met if one of the following exemptions are recorded on the AIR:

  • The child has a medical exemption due to medical contraindication or natural immunity. These are assessed by a vaccination provider and recorded on the AIR
  • A vaccine is temporarily unavailable

If there is a medical reason why a child cannot be fully immunised, refer the customer to their child’s general practitioner to discuss an exemption.

Procedure ends here.

Secretary exemptions

In extremely limited cases, a child may also meet the immunisation requirements if the Secretary determines an exemption from immunisation applies.

If the customer is seeking a Secretary determination exemption from immunisation, go to Step 2.

2

Determining the appropriate Secretary or Ministerial determination + Read more ...

Note: if a Secretary determination has been applied for the relevant child/ren for FTB, this decision can be applied for CCS for the same period without assessment/approval by the Level 2 Policy helpdesk. See the Document List (DL) screen in Customer First (CF) for FTB Secretary exemption decision details DOC.

A Secretary determination exemption is only available for children who do not meet the immunisation requirements because of any of the following reasons:

  • Permanent Humanitarian Visa Holder, go to Step 3
  • Refusal of consent to vaccination, go to Step 4
  • Family violence, go to Step 5
  • Unacceptable risk of harm to child or another person, go to Step 6

Do not escalate applications for an exemption request to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk for any other reason.

Overrides due to a Secretary determination exemption from immunisation or Ministerial determination are only applicable in extremely limited circumstances.

For Level 2 referrals or outcomes, see:

  • Referral to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk, go to Step 7
  • Decision from Level 2 Policy Helpdesk
    • Approved, go to Step 8
    • Rejected, go to Step 9

3

Permanent Humanitarian Visa Holder + Read more ...

From 7 December 2019, Humanitarian exemptions for FTB are automatically applied to a customer’s record. This means there will be no Secretary exemption decision DOC on the DL. To check if a customer has been granted a Humanitarian exemption, in Customer First refer to the Child Immunisation Summary (Families Benefits) guided procedure.

If an exemption has been applied for FTB, record a Secretary determination exemption for Immunisation for CCS. Go to Step 8.

The requirements for this exemption is:

  • the individual whose eligibility for CCS is being assessed, is the holder of a permanent humanitarian visa, and
  • no more than 6 months have passed since the child's first entry to Australia

Eligible visa subclass types include: 200, 201, 202, 203, 204 and 866.

Note: the exemption will apply for a maximum period of 6 months from the date the child first entered Australia. When the exemption ends the child will be placed into an immunisation grace period if they are still not up to date.

Procedure ends here.

4

Refusal of consent to vaccination + Read more ...

The child is in the care of a person whose eligibility is being assessed, and:

  • neither the individual nor the individuals partner has legal authority to make decisions about the medical treatment of the child, and
  • if the child is aged under 14 years, a person (other than the child) with legal authority to make decisions about the medical treatment of the child has refused, or failed within a reasonable time, to give consent to the individual taking actions to allow the child to meet the usual immunisation requirements, or
  • if the child is aged at least 14 years, the child has refused, or failed within a reasonable time, to give consent to the individual taking actions to allow the child to meet the usual immunisation requirements

Evidence must include:

  • Formal care:
    • Child Protection Order, and
    • a letter from Child Protection Agency stating that parent has not provided consent to the vaccination of the child
  • Informal care:
    • a letter from the GP stating the parent attended and the parent has not provided consent, and
    • a Commonwealth statutory declaration from the carer that includes the parent was unable to be contacted or parent was contacted and did not give consent
  • Biological parents:
    • valid Court Order covering the period the exemption has been requested for and containing a clause regarding the child's medical treatment. The clause should stipulate that neither parents have legal authority to make decisions about the child's medical treatment without consent from the other parent

Note: in all the above circumstances, if the child is over 14 years, evidence must also include a letter from medical practitioner, on appropriate letter head stating:

  • the child has attended the practitioner, and
  • has refused to consent

If a customer's circumstances indicate a Secretary determination exemption may apply due to Refusal of consent to vaccination and evidence has been provided which is scanned on the customer's record, refer the case to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk, go to Step 7.

5

Family Violence + Read more ...

If any of the following individuals would be subject to family violence if the individual took action to allow the child to meet the usual immunisation requirements:

  • An individual who has care of the child
  • The child
  • A member of the immediate family of the individual or the child

If the exemption request is due to family violence, refer the Customer to a social worker in the Carer Specialised Assessment Team (CSAT). To complete the referral, in Customer First create a Fast Note with the keyword IMMEX. Advise the customer a social worker will contact them for more information.

The Resources page has an example.

Note: only a social worker from the CSAT can undertake a family violence assessment.

CSAT team: once the exemption assessment is complete, document the record. Include:

  • child/ren (full name and date of birth) the exemption is requested for
  • payment type exemption is granted for i.e. FTB and/or CCS
  • start and end date of the exemption period for the payment type (exemption can only be granted for a maximum period of 12 months)
  • the social workers name, and
  • team (CSAT)

If a referral is required, submit a Level 2 Policy Helpdesk referral, go to Step 7.

6

Unacceptable risk of harm to child or another person + Read more ...

A Secretary determination exemption is available if immunisation of the child would result in unacceptable risk of physical harm to the:

  • child, or
  • person administering a vaccination to the child

For example - Behavioural or developmental disorder: A child with a disruptive behavioural disorder or developmental disorder who reacts with aggression or defiant behaviour when faced with a medical procedure.

This risk must be determined by all of the following:

  • the Department of Health and Aged Care
  • the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer, and
  • endorsed by the Chief Medical Officer

A referral to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk should not be made when:

  • a child had a reaction to administered vaccines, or where the parent assesses there may be a reaction, or
  • a child has a medical condition, or has had an illness and cannot receive their vaccination, or
  • the customer has not been able to arrange a suitable appointment with the Vaccination provider, or their general practitioner (GP) and the child's immunisation has become overdue

If the customer is seeking an exemption for one of the above circumstances, then refer them to their GP.

When a customer meets the criteria for an exemption due to an unacceptable risk of physical harm to the:

  • child, or
  • person administering a vaccination to the child

Supporting evidence is not required. These cases must be referred to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk who will give advice on how customers can get this determination, go to Step 7.

7

Referral to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk + Read more ...

Only cases that meet one of the Secretary determination exemption categories should be referred to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk.

Referrals that do not meet one of the Secretary determination exemption categories below will be returned to the referring officer.

Complete a referral to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk. See the Resources page for a link to the Level 2 Policy Help Desk.

In the Enquiry Description on the webform, the following information must be included:

  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Affected Child/ren (full name and date of birth)
  • Which Secretary determination exemption is to be assessed:
    • refusal of consent to vaccination,
    • family violence or
    • unacceptable risk of harm to child or another person
  • Payment type (FTB and/or CCS)
  • Start date for the period of exemption
  • End date for the period of exemption
  • Background information supplied by the customer
  • Evidence provided: YES/NO (if required for exemption reason)
  • Date of any additional information on the customers record to support exemption

Record a Note advising a referral to the Level 2 Policy Help Desk has been completed.

A response will be returned to the initiating Service Officer for action. If the case is:

  • approved, the initiating Service Officer must apply the decision. Go to Step 8
  • rejected, the initiating Service Officer must make outbound contact with the customer to advise of the outcome, record a Note on the customer record and issue customer correspondence. Go to Step 9

8

Recording Secretary determination exemption from immunisation + Read more ...

To record the Secretary determination exemption from immunisation:

Within a CCS claim:

  • Go to My Child Care
  • Select the Customer Details twisty
  • Select the Exceptional Circumstances (EXCIRC) twisty
  • Select
  • Complete the ‘Create Exceptional Circumstances’ page
    • Type: Immunisation
    • Start Date: start date for the exemption, as noted on the approval
    • End Date: end date for the exemption, as noted on the approval
    • Review Date: leave blank
    • Child CRN: select the relevant child
    • Decision: select the appropriate reason for the request from the dropdown list
    • Reason: select the appropriate reason for the exemption
    • Select Simulate > Assess
    • Review the Assessment Results (AR) screen
    • Continue processing the claim. Once the claim is finalised, go to Step 9

Standalone update (change of circumstance):

  • In the Super Key field, key 'EXC' to go to the Exceptional Circumstances (EXC) table
  • Select and complete the ‘Create Exceptional Circumstance page’:
    • Type: Immunisation
    • Start Date: start date for the exemption, as noted on the approval
    • End Date: end date for the exemption, as noted on the approval
    • Review Date: leave blank
    • Child CRN: select the relevant child
    • Decision: select the appropriate reason for the request from the dropdown list.
    • Reason: select the appropriate reason for the exemption
    • Select Save > Assess
    • Review the Assessment Results (AR) screen
    • Select Finalise to complete the activity

Once exemption is recoded, go to Step 9.

Document decision + Read more ...

Record a Note on the record, and include the following details:

  • Summary field:
    • CCS immunisation exemption
  • Content field:
    • Child/ren name/s: XXXX
    • Period assessed: XXXX to XXXX

Issue customer correspondence in Customer First

A manual Q065 letter must be issued to the customer advising the outcome of the Secretary determination exemption decision.

When creating the Q065:

  • Check the letter content to confirm the decision to grant or reject the Secretary determination exemption is correct:
    • Grant = 1
    • Not eligible = 2
    • Reviewed = 3
  • The following details may need to be keyed, as required:
    • Full name/s of the customer’s child/ren
    • For payments impacted by the exemption, code the payment name, payment period start date and payment period end date
    • First name/s of the customer’s child/ren
    • Start and end dates for overriding the original decision

The Q065 must be printed locally.

Note: if the customer has a correspondence nominee, issue the same letter to the correspondence nominee.
