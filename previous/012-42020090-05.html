Frequently asked questions

Table 6: This table contains frequently asked questions.

Step

Action

1

Question: If a GP application form was received by Services Australia before COB 15/06/2021, can it be processed?

Answer: No, it is not possible to process an application form for recognition or removal of a GP after COB 15/06/2021.

2

Question: If a medical practitioner has applied to Ahpra to be registered as a Specialist in General practice, can they also send their application to Services Australia to be processed as a specialist general practitioner?

Answer: No, this cannot be processed in advance. Once the medical practitioner is registered as a Specialist in General practice with Ahpra, we will then be notified of their eligibility through the daily data feed and their recognition will be automatically processed.

3

Question: If a medical practitioner has overseas qualifications, should their period of registration as a specialist general practitioner be limited (such as end dated)?

Answer: No. A medical practitioner’s recognition as general practitioner for Medicare purposes is only dependent on the medical practitioner’s registration with Ahpra. It is not affected by the medical practitioner’s place of qualification.

4

Question: If a medical practitioner’s recognition as a general practitioner (GP) has been removed because of their registration with Ahpra, is there any way they can appeal to the agency to be recognised as a GP for Medicare purposes?

Answer: No. Services Australia does not have any discretion to otherwise recognise a medical practitioner as a general practitioner for Medicare purposes. Where a medical practitioner does not hold the required registration to be recognised as a GP for Medicare purposes, they should be directed to Ahpra.

5

Question: If the system has recognised or removed a medical practitioner’s GP status, is it possible to view and/or re-issue the letter sent by the system?

Answer: Yes, where these letters have been sent through the Provider Directory System (PDS), these will be viewable in the PDS Correspondence screen.

6

Question: How does a medical practitioner contact Ahpra to make an enquiry about recognition?

Answer: They can contact Ahpra by:

  • completing a web form enquiry on the Ahpra website, or
  • calling Ahpra

Question

Answer

Can I be re-included on the Vocational Register?
  • Due to changes in legislation Services Australia can no longer re-include medical practitioners on the Vocational Register
  • If a medical practitioner wants to be recognised as a GP for Medicare purposes they must be registered with Ahpra as a specialist in General practice
  • If they are not, tell them they must contact Ahpra

See FAQs for how to contact Ahpra.

How do I become recognised as a General Practitioner for Medicare purposes?

As of 16 June 2021:

  • They will need to apply for recognition as a specialist in General practice with Ahpra
  • Once they are recognised as a specialist in General practice:
    • this information will be sent by Ahpra to Services Australia, and
    • we will process their recognition as a specialist General Practitioner for Medicare purposes

Can I still be recognised as a General Practitioner by providing the application forms:

  • HW075 (Application for recognition as a General Practitioner; Fellows of the Royal Australia College of General Practitioners), and/or
  • HW076 (Application for recognition as a General Practitioner Fellow of the Australia College of Rural and Remote Medicine)?

These forms are obsolete and are no longer used to apply for recognition as a GP.

To be recognised as a GP for Medicare purposes:

  • They need to be registered with Ahpra as a specialist in General practice
  • If they are not, tell them they must contact Ahpra

See FAQs for how to contact Ahpra.

Will Ahpra recognise me/my qualifications?

Medicare is unable to comment on Ahpra's assessment or processes.

  • Direct any queries to Ahpra

See FAQs for how to contact Ahpra.

I am a specialist in a General Practice with Ahpra.

What is required from me to become registered as a GP for Medicare purposes?
  • Unless otherwise advised, they do not need to do anything
  • Medicare will communicate relevant updates about their registration

Do I satisfy 19AA if I am registered as a Specialist in General Practice with Ahpra?

They will satisfy section 19AA for Medicare purposes:

  • once Services Australia has received their information, and
  • they are recognised as a specialist GP with Ahpra

Note: a provider may also satisfy Section 19AA through:

  • specialist or consultant physician registration, or
  • an approved 3GA placement
