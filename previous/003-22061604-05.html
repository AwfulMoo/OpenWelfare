Date of eligibility

Table 2: This table outlines when the claiming period begins for each category of Crisis Payment (CrP).

Extreme circumstance

Date of eligibility

Family and Domestic Violence - victim left home

The customer has left the home and has established or intends to establish a new home.

7 day period and deemed claim provisions apply from this date

The date the customer decides they cannot return to their previous home (for example, following a period of trauma or because it is unsafe).

Family and Domestic Violence - Victim remains in home

Customer chooses to remain in the home after the removal of a family member due to family and domestic violence.

7 day period and deemed claim provisions apply from this date

The date the family member was removed from the home.

Family and Domestic Violence – Alleged perpetrator left home

The customer has left the home or been removed and has legally been stopped from returning.

7 day period and deemed claim provisions apply from this date

The date the customer was legally stopped from returning home.

Other Extreme Circumstances

Customer is forced to leave their home and establish a new home.

7 day period and deemed claim provisions apply from this date

The date the customer decides that they cannot return to their home because of the extreme circumstance having occurred.

Release from Prison or Psychiatric Confinement

The customer is released from prison or psychiatric confinement after 14 or more days as a result of being charged with an offence

7 day period and deemed claim provisions apply from this date

The date the customer was released from prison or psychiatric confinement.

Humanitarian Entrants

Customer has arrived in Australia as a Humanitarian Entrant for the first time.

7 day period and deemed claim provisions apply from this date

The date the customer arrived in Australia.

National Health Emergency

Customers are required to enter quarantine or self-isolation, or caring for someone who has had to enter quarantine or self-isolation.

14 day period and deemed claim provisions apply from this date

The date the customer entered quarantine or self-isolation, or the date the person the customer is caring for had to enter quarantine or self-isolation.

Note: the exception to this rule is for periods starting on or between 1 July 2022 and 19 July 2022. The customer has until 2 August 2022 to register their intent to claim. The deemed claim provisions apply from the date of contact on or before 2 August 2022.

The decision to permanently leave and establish a new home may happen after the day the customer physically left the original home.

For example, the customer may have left the original home, been hospitalised or traumatised, and after thinking about matters then decides to permanently leave and establish a new home.

Staff should carefully judge cases involving trauma to determine when the person made an informed decision that they could not return home. Consider each case on its own merits.
