This page contains information on assessing claims for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and/or Family Tax Benefit (FTB) from birth mothers who relinquish the care of their child.

PPL and FTB claims from birth mothers relinquishing care of child

Step

Action

1

Birth mothers who relinquish the care of their child may be eligible for PPL and/or FTB + Read more ...

FTB can include Newborn Supplement (NBS) and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) if the customer is not eligible for PPL.

Has the customer lodged a claim for payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

How to claim + Read more ...

Encourage customers to complete an online claim for family assistance and PPL. This is the preferred service offer. It gives customers a streamlined experience.

Help the customer register for online services if necessary. Ensure they can access an 'active' online account before directing them to claim online.

If it is unreasonable for the customer to claim online, a Claim for Parental Leave Pay and Family Tax Benefit (FA100) can be issued by a Service Officer.

Extra assistance is available to help remote Indigenous customers make a claim.

3

Primary carer + Read more ...

Will the customer be the primary carer of the child after the birth before they relinquish care of the child?

  • Yes, they may be eligible for PPL and FTB for the child. Note: the birth mother/primary carer may receive either PPL or NBS for the newborn child. Customers cannot be paid both PPL and NBS for the same child. Go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Cessation of care of the child + Read more ...

Birth mothers who cease to have care of their child immediately after birth may be eligible for PPL for maternal recovery purposes.

Birth mothers may receive PPL up to 18 weeks following the birth of their child if they:

  • nominate a start date no later than the child's date of birth
  • claim and provide relevant documents within the 28 days following the birth

This includes Flexible PPL days available for children that are born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020. As the birth mother cannot receive PPL more than 18 weeks after the child’s birth, they should be advised to connect all of their Flexible PPL days to the end of their PPL period.

If they claim and provide relevant documents more than 28 days following the birth, the PPL period may only start from the date all information is received. This will result in them receiving less than the full 18 weeks of PPL.

Note: if the birth mother returns to work before the end of the PPL period it will cease. See Parental Leave Pay (PPL) customer returns to work.

Has the customer lodged an effective claim and provided all relevant documents within 28 days of the birth of the child?

  • Yes, confirm the customer has selected the child's date of birth as their nominated start date for PPL. Go to Step 5
  • No, the customer may not receive the full 18 weeks PPL. Confirm the customer has selected the date of claim as their nominated start date. If the customer has selected a later date, try to call the customer as a later start date will prevent them receiving the full 18 weeks of PPL. Go to Step 5

5

Return to work + Read more ...

Has the customer returned to work since giving birth to the child?

  • Yes, they may not be eligible for PPL. See Parental Leave Pay (PPL) customer returns to work. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Child details + Read more ...

Children must be named, but if care of the child has been relinquished as part of the process of adoption or a surrogacy arrangement and the child was never in the care of the birth mother, a claim can be accepted for the child without a name being given.

Has the customer provided a name for the child?

  • Yes, add the child to the customer's record with the child's name details provided. Go to Step 7
  • No, add the child to the customer's record with a first name and surname of 'surrogate' (this applies for both adopted and surrogate children). Go to Step 7

7

Check/complete coding + Read more ...

For claim processing requirements, see Assessing family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme claims.

Once this has been completed select the new claim activity from the Activity List (AL) screen and complete any further coding required.

'S'elect the child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen. On the Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen, select the following screens to check/update details:

  • On the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen, code 'ICC' (In Customer Care) in the Care Status: field.
  • Child in Primary Care (!CiPC) screen. This page should be updated for PPL claims with:
    • Date of event
    • Carer: claimant
    • In Primary Care: No
    • Reason: Surrogacy/Adoption. This will still allow the payment to issue.

There is no need to update:

  • Child Relationship (!CHRE) screen
  • Care Alignment Details (!CADDS) screen
  • Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM) screen
  • Medicare Information (MEI) screen

Note: A surrogacy arrangement includes the formal surrogacy agreement or supporting evidence of a surrogacy agreement. If you are unable to make a determination using the information provided, access technical support who may determine that a referral to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk via the Online Form is required. See the Resources page for a link to the form.

8

Refer to Families and Child Care Portfolio Operations + Read more ...

Once the claim has been assessed for the child, refer the child's Customer Reference Number (CRN) on the birth mother's record to Families and Child Care Portfolio Operations.

Insert 'New adopted child record' in the subject line.

The CRN will be promoted to a 'PER'son record (if it was a 'CHI'ld record) and will remain visible. Action by Families and Child Care Portfolio Operations will prevent any future use of that CRN.

Referral for other issues:

Any other CRN issues regarding pre-adoptive care, child names, aliases and privacy issues should be referred to the Families and Child Care Helpdesk.
