Determining whether a participation failure is applicable for Youth Allowance or Austudy students 010-07020010

  • Background
  • Process Summary
  • Process

Expand all


This document explains the action should be taken when a student does not meet the participation related requirements of their payment and how to determine if, after signing a Student Activity Agreement, a participation failure has been committed by a student.

Circumstances when a participation failure applies

Normally, where an Austudy or Youth Allowance (YA) customer has failed to attend an appointment about their Activity Test requirements, the customer may be subject to a participation failure if they do not have a reasonable excuse.

ABSTUDY, Australian Apprentices and trainees

This procedure does not apply to:

  • ABSTUDY customers who are assessed under a specific breach framework
  • Australian Apprentices receiving YA or Austudy because they are exempt from the student Activity Test at all times
  • trainees who are doing a course which does not qualify them for payment as a full-time student or an Australian Apprentice. This last group are regarded as job seekers and are required to comply with activities negotiated in a Job/Participation Plan

Examples of when a student may incur a participation failure

  • failure to comply with the terms of a previously negotiated Student Activity Agreement in relation to secondary school attendance
  • failure to attend a participation related service profiling appointment
  • failure to attend an appointment booked to enable an Student Activity Agreement to be negotiated

Examples of when a student will not incur a participation failure

  • not enrolled in and/or undertaking full-time (or qualifying) study
  • not enrolling in and/or undertaking the course of study following an intention to do so
  • not commencing the course of study by the Friday of the second week of classes
  • for continuing students, not enrolling in the course when the next enrolments are accepted
  • for a YA student, refusal to sign a Student Activity Agreement
  • failure to attend a non-participation related appointment. Suspension is the appropriate option in this case
  • leaving employment voluntarily or through misconduct and claiming a payment as a student
  • Generally, where a student fails to satisfy the Activity Test when they no longer study full time, this will result in the loss of qualification and a resultant debt, rather than the application of a participation failure

Penalties related to earnings

If a student:

  • refuses or fails to correctly declare earnings from employment
  • recklessly or knowingly gives false or misleading information about earnings

A debt will be raised for any overpayment and a 10% recovery fee may be applied. A participation failure or loss of qualification for payment is not applicable in these circumstances

Unfavourable decisions

When an unfavourable decision is to be made, a Decision Maker (DM) must provide the customer with an explanation of the decision and an opportunity to provide further information or evidence relevant to the decision. The DM must also advise the customer of their review and appeal rights. This includes unfavourable decisions made by Service Officers within Smart Centre Call.

If a DM is not speaking with the customer at the time of making an unfavourable decision, they are required to make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer before finalising the decision.

For more details, see Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process.
