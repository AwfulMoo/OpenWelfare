Customer contact

Table 1: Customer calls to enquire why they have not received the DRA (Top-up).

Step

Action

1

Payment of the DRA (Top-up) + Read more ...

The DRA (Top-up) has been activated for customers who:

  • were eligible to receive DRA or NZ DRA for the New South Wales Floods - February 2022, and
  • resided or worked in an eligible area at the time of the New South Wales Floods – February 2022

An eligible area is the local government area (LGA) of Lismore.

When a customer calls to enquire why they have not received a payment of the DRA (Top-up):

  • Complete Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO)
  • Do not give hints or prompts that help the customer answer the questions
  • Check if the customer has received a payment of the DRA (Top-up)
    • In Customer First type DL in the Next field to go to the Document List (DL) screen
    • Locate the DOC titled CUS CLM EXG FSP claim
    • Click on the hyperlink for the CUS CLM EXG FSP claim DOC
    • Review the information in the DOC
    • To confirm the amount the customer has been paid, type PS in the Next field to go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen
    • Using the drop down for Display payments for, select
      - FSP
      - continue
    • Click on the hyperlink for the FSP payment that matches the date in the CUS CLM EXG FSP claim DOC
    • The Payment Details (PD) screen will display
    • Select Continue to show the Pymt Dtls Cmpnt (PDC) screen
    • If the customer has received a payment of the DRA (Top-up) this will show as follows:
      - Qualifier Code (known as the event code), Component OT1 and payment amount of $350

For customers who have:

  • been granted DRA or NZ DRA for the New South Wales Floods - February 2022, go to Step 2
  • not yet claimed DRA or NZ DRA for the New South Wales Floods – February 2022, go to Step 4
  • been paid a payment of the DRA (Top-up) as expected, tell them to check their bank account or contact their financial institution. Go to Step 6

2

Customer has already been granted DRA or NZ DRA + Read more ...

If a customer has been granted DRA or NZ DRA for the New South Wales Floods – February 2022 and meets the eligibility criteria for the DRA (Top-up), they will receive a payment of DRA (Top-up) See When a customer will be paid.

To check if a customer meets the eligibility criteria for the DRA (Top-up):

  • close the customer’s record in Customer First
  • go to the customer’s claim in Process Direct. Note: staff should not have the customer’s record open in Customer First and Process Direct at the same time, as this can cause errors
  • select and to view the Claim Summary in a new window. If it is blank, press [F5] to refresh the page
  • check the Claim Summary

If the customer:

  • does not meet the eligibility criteria for the DRA (Top-up) go to Step 4
  • meets the eligibility criteria for the DRA (Top-up) and has not received a payment of the DRA (Top-up) as expected, go to Step 3
  • meets the eligibility criteria for the DRA (Top-up) and is not yet due to receive a payment of the DRA (Top-up):
    • tell the customer they will receive the payment as soon as possible and to call back if they have not received it as outlined in When a customer will be paid. No more action is needed by staff to escalate the customer’s query as the payment should be made in due course
    • go to Step 6

3

Customer has not received a payment of the DRA (Top-up) as expected + Read more ...

For when customers should receive their DRA (Top-up), see When a customer will be paid.

If the status line shows DRA/CAN-EDR - this may have stopped the final automatic payment from occurring:

  • check the Payment Summary (PS) screen to see the value of DRA (Top-Up) customer has received in both their arrears payment and weekly ongoing top ups
    • if a customer has received $4,550 in total (equivalent to the amount of 13 x $350), tell the customer they have received their full entitlement to the DRA (Top-Up)
      - DOC the record with details of discussion
      - procedure ends here
    • if a customer has not received a total of $4,550 (equivalent to the amount of 13 x $350), tell the customer this will be escalated for investigation and payment (if eligible) as soon as possible
      - run the Fast Note below
      - the Level 2 helpdesk will determine the action needed

If currently in Process Direct, select the Home button at the top left hand side to exit out of the transaction and the customer’s record. This will go back to the Process Direct Landing Page.

Go to the customer’s record in Customer First. Note: staff should not have the customer’s record open in Customer First and Process Direct at the same time, as this can cause errors.

Run the Fast Note workflow:

  • Select Auto Text template options
  • Category 1: Emergency
  • Category 2: Claim
  • Template: Non-receipt of DRA Top-up
  • Select Confirm
  • Complete all questions in the Fast Note
  • Answer all questions/prompts
  • Do not close the Fast Note
  • The Fast Note will be allocated to Emergency Management Programme to determine next steps.
  • Procedure ends here

4

Customer has not received DRA or NZ DRA + Read more ...

When a customer will be paid a payment of the DRA (Top-up) depends when the DRA or NZ DRA claim was granted. See When a customer will be paid.

If the customer has:

  • not yet claimed DRA or NZ DRA:
    • determine the best claiming channel for the customer’s circumstances. See Claiming emergency payments
    • procedure ends here
  • claimed DRA or NZ DRA and the claim has not been finalised
    • process the claim and document the decision as per steps in:
      - Processing DRA claims in Process Direct (DRA claims), or
      - Processing DRA or NZ DRA claims in Customer First (NZ DRA claims)
    • if the customer meets all eligibility criteria for DRA or NZ DRA, tell them:
      - their claim for DRA or NZ DRA has been assessed
      - a notice of decision will be sent to them
      - they will be automatically assessed for the DRA (Top-up)
      - they will receive their first payment of the DRA (Top-up) in due course. See When a customer will be paid
      - Go to Step 6
    • if the customer does not meet the eligibility criteria for DRA or NZ DRA
      - do not reject the claim in Process Direct unless formal approval has been provided to reject DRA and NZ DRA claims. See Rejecting claims in New South Wales Floods - February 2022.
      If the claim needs to be placed on hold, as formal approval has not been given to reject DRA and NZ DRA claims, see Processing DRA claims in Process Direct (DRA claims only) or Processing DRA or NZ DRA claims in Customer First (NZ DRA claims only)
      - Go to Step 5

5

Customer is ineligible for the DRA (Top-up) + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • the reason they are ineligible (this includes where staff identify the customer did not meet the eligibility criteria for DRA or NZ DRA)
    • do not use the terms ‘rejection’ or ‘rejected claim’
  • if they do not agree, they can request a review of their original DRA or NZ DRA claim

Go to Step 6.

6

Document call with customer + Read more ...

Tell the customer, for more financial help they can visit the Help in an emergency page for more details, and use the Payment and Service Finder to check other payments that may be suitable for them.

If the customer may be eligible for the Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP) or New Zealand Disaster Recovery Payment (NZ DRP) and has not yet claimed, see Claiming emergency payments.

Customers can be directed to their local evacuation/recovery centre for information about:

  • state government grants
  • housing and accommodation
  • health and wellbeing
  • clean-up and waste removal
  • managing contamination and material assistance

See Resources for Disasters (community support Queensland) and Disasters (community support New South Wales).

Customers who need personal support and counselling should be warm-transferred to the Social Work Disaster Line. Be clear that social workers cannot change payment decisions. See Referring a customer during a declared disaster or emergency.

Add a DOC to the customer’s record in Customer First using the Fast Note workflow:

  • Location: Customer First > Workspace > Documents and Correspondence > Fast Note
  • Service Reason: select AGP – AUS Gov Disaster Recovery (for DRA) or EXG - Emergency Exgratia (for NZ DRA) as appropriate
  • Document Completion: select Yes, otherwise the Fast Note will not close and creates a new work item
  • Extra Details to enter a title for the DOC. Type in this field: DRA Top-up enquiry
  • Clearly document:
    • what was discussed with the customer
    • what they were advised, and
    • any actions taken on the customer’s record

If staff have identified the customer did not meet the eligibility criteria for DRA or NZ DRA, go to Step 7.

Otherwise, procedure ends here.

7

Customer was not eligible for the initial payment of DRA or NZ DRA + Read more ...

If staff identify the customer did not meet the eligibility criteria for DRA or NZ DRA:

  • See DRA or NZ DRA incorrectly paid in Managing DRA and NZ DRA payments after grant
  • Procedure ends here

  • DRA (Top-up) was not processed automatically
  • Rate of payment LGA, and if an arrears payment or weekly payment is being paid. For example, LGA Lismore, 2 x $350 (arrears payment included); TOTAL = $700
  • Finalise the activity on the Assessments Results screen making sure the outcome is correct before selecting Continue

  • incorrect payment amounts show
