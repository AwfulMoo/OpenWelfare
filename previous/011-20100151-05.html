Subsequent request for Medicare history statement telephony calls and responses

Call is about

Suggested response

Non-compliance with the Health and Other Services (Compensation) Act.

To make sure we are using our time in the most efficient way and to improve our customer service, we are focusing our effort in a slightly different way.

Until recently we have processed each request in order of date received, to ensure procedural fairness. Moving forward we will be focusing on claims that have reached judgment or settlement.

This change will enable more staff to be re-directed to work on new registrations and cases that have reached settlement.

In a practical sense this means the below:

  • Any initial requests for Medicare history statements and associated Notices of past benefits will be processed
  • Any subsequent requests for Medicare history statements and associated Notices of past benefits for the same cases will be processed when the notifiable party has formally advised us of judgment/settlement
  • We will consider requests to process otherwise if exceptional circumstances apply

Why can an email response be sent advising that we are unable to process subsequent requests but not for any other reason?

The email response advising that we are not processing subsequent requests contains no identifying information or personal details. We are currently unable to send requests for more information or other correspondence via email due to the personal information which may be included.

Have received the letter/email/auto response confirming a subsequent request will not be actioned and wanting to know why.

This change will mean we can re-direct our staff to processing cases that require a new registration or have proceeded to settlement. This will better enable us to register cases and process refunds more quickly.

Why are new registrations being processed but not subsequent requests?

New registrations are being processed as this ensures that an initial Notice of past benefits has been issued on all compensation cases advised to the agency. The decision to limit this to one Notice of past benefit before settlement will assist in re-directing staff to cases which have reached settlement and processing refunds to our customers in a timely manner.

A request submitted before the change but the Medicare history statement has not been issued.

We will assess the request after we receive it. A Service Officer will then register a new case and issue a Medicare history statement if there is no case registered. If the request is for an existing case you will be notified that we are unable to process your request via post or email depending on how the request was submitted.

Insisting that a subsequent request be actioned due to it being an exceptional circumstance.

We understand that in some cases a Notice of past benefits is required. We are currently focussing on cases which have reached settlement or are new registrations. Requests which do not meet these requirements may be assessed on a case by case basis, can you please tell me more about why your case requires urgent attention?

Service Officer to see Table 5 on the Process page of Assess Medicare Compensation Recovery correspondence to determine if case meets requirements for processing under exceptional circumstances.

Advising that case is about to settle documented court or mediation date.

Send us an email with ‘Mediation/trial date set’ in the subject line.

Include the date of the trial or mediation in the email. Do not include personal/identifying information in the subject line.

Please do not call to follow up after the email is sent. We will contact you if we require further information.

A Medicare history statement was issued prior to the change and there has been a previous Notice of past benefit issued. What happens now?

Medicare history statements previously issued, completed and returned to the agency will be processed in due course. Please do not submit copies of the same document as this may delay processing further.

The Notice of past benefits has expired but a new one is required e.g. as it effects settlement negotiations. How can this information be found now?

If the initial Notice of past benefits has expired and an amount repayable to Medicare is required to help in settlement negotiations the injured person may access their Medicare record via MyGov or the Express Plus Medicare mobile app.

The history includes events from the customer's previous and current Medicare cards within the last 3 years and it may be used to help determine further services about their injury.

See the Process page of Medicare claims history and claims history statement online for how to guide customers to download Medicare history from their myGov account or advise customers to go to Medicare online account on the Service Australia website.

Caller is asking to speak to a ‘manager/supervisor’.

If the call is about:

  • the process, transfer the call to the Program Service Officer (PSO) hotline. Contact details contains a link
  • a Service Officer, transfer the call to the Team Leader
