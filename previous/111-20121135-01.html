This document outlines mandatory information for Quality Checker accreditation for staff in the Health Service Delivery Division.

On this page:

Role of a Team Leader

Team Leaders give approval for a Service Officer (SO) to start the accreditation process. The Team Leader must ensure the:

  • SO is proficient in the work type they will be checking prior to completing quality checker accreditation process
  • SO meets the minimum standard for each requirement in the quality checker prerequisites table on the Resources page
  • reaccreditation process is followed

Role of a Quality Checker

Quality Checkers must maintain up-to-date business and technical knowledge in the work types they quality check. Quality Checkers must not check their own work.

Quality checkers must:

  • undertake quality checking training (where applicable) and be accredited before conducting quality checks
  • ensure consistent assessment of work items in line with Operational Blueprint procedures
  • identify and record errors correctly and complete corrective action where required. Analysing errors helps to identify:
    • trends
    • weakness in processing
    • gaps in business rules
    • system faults
    • issues relating to environmental factors
    • limitations in training or development opportunities
  • give consistent and constructive feedback to staff
  • work with leadership and program teams to ensure quality checks are consistent
  • provide relevant feedback about systems, procedural, policy and work flow issues to the relevant team
  • complete reaccreditation every 12 months

Providing Feedback

Prior to becoming a Quality Checker, the Service Officer must fully understand the feedback process. Resources contains more information about providing feedback.

The Quality Checker must give feedback using the method outlined in the quality checking procedure.

Feedback must be provided for every identified error. The feedback must:

  • describe the error identified, and what was incorrect
  • explain the impact of the error
  • explain the action that should have been taken to correct the error
  • include any relevant reference material that supports the action, such as Operational Blueprint details if available
  • be to the point, non-judging and constructive

The feedback function can be used for positive feedback, but only when the outcome of the check is error free.

Service Delivery Quality and Support team

The Service Delivery Quality and Support team:

  • provide support and oversee the accreditation and reaccreditation process
  • provide data/reports on quality checking
  • maintain the Quality Checker accreditation register
  • support quality checking in the Quality and Support Tool (QST) by providing QST training and access

Reaccreditation

Quality Checkers must complete reaccreditation every 12 months. The Service Delivery Quality and Support Team send a reminder email to the Quality Checker and their Team leader 1 month prior to the accreditation end date.

The Quality Checker must:

  • complete the reaccreditation assessment
  • return the completed assessment to the Service Delivery Quality and Support team for checking

The Service Delivery Quality and Support team will update the Quality Checker accreditation register with the date reaccreditation is completed.

If the Quality Checker fails to complete the reaccreditation process by the due date they will no longer be an accredited Quality Checker. Access to quality checking work will be removed. To regain Quality Checker accreditation after accreditation has lapsed, the Service Officer must complete the full accreditation process.
