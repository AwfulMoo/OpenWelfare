Income support payment (ISP) care recipient and a homeowner - value of former principal home + Read more ...

If a means assessment form is not received, gather information about the occupancy and value of the care recipient's former principal home.

Call the care recipient, nominee or power of attorney (POA). Ask for an estimated valuation of the former principal home and the care recipient's net share of their former principal home.   • use the total asset value of the former principal home
  • less the balance of any loans or mortgages (including pension loan scheme balance) currently owing

Determine the care recipient's percentage of ownership.

Note:

  • Proof of identity (POI) requirements are the same for both inbound and outbound phone calls. See Calling a customer or returning a customer's call

  • The balance of any pension loans scheme is on the Loans Summary (PLSS) screen
  • When the home is a caravan or mobile home, see Assessing caravans, boats and park homes

Illness separated + Read more ...

The notification of a couple who are separated due to illness must be considered where the care recipient (or their partner):

  • is receiving an income support payment (ISP)
  • has vacated the former principal home, and
  • is a member of a couple

In Customer First, the Marital Status (MS) screen must be updated to Illness Separated before the RCA/NCL activity is completed:

  • if the care recipient or their partner is receiving an ISP, and
  • notification of illness separation was received before the date of receipt (DOR) of the auto RCA/NCL activity. See Assessment of circumstances for a couple separated due to illness
  • an Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care was lodged before entry into care. This activity cannot be used to update a care recipient to Illness Separated as notification cannot be received before an event, or
  • entry details cannot create an entry event (means assessment RCA/NCL)
  • In Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP):
    • Care Recipient tab, select Event Summary
    • Created date will show when entry was coded by provider
  • In SPARC
    • Check Client Profile (2.6) screen to confirm DOV of entry into care
    • Press [F11] on this screen to show the date that SPARC entry details were updated
    • Use the date plus 1 day SPARC was updated with entry details as the DOR for the activity

Is this care recipient (or their partner):

  • receiving an ISP, and
  • there has been a notification of an entry into care that is different to the DOR being used for this means assessment?
    • Yes, the RCA/NCL is to be cancelled and the coding of care recipient being separated due to illness is to be completed in a separate activity. When a RCA/NCL activity is cancelled, reindex. See Re-indexing claims
    • No, illness separated coding should be completed within the RCA/NCL activity

Cod employment income – ISP care recipients + Read more ...

Employment income reporting requirements for a care recipient (or partner) in receipt of an ISP:

  • Statement reporter:
    • have employment income
    • must advise of income, hours worked and any other changes on their reporting day
  • Notification reporter
    • no employment income or stable employment income and exception is met

  • Refer to Reporting overview

6

Cod employment income – self funded retirees + Read more ...

Care recipients not receiving an ISP:

  • Are not able to update their income each fortnight
  • Have their income assessed in the same way as a continuous income exception
  • Income is coded a 1WE (weekly) or 2WE (fortnightly), if income is variable will be averaged over a suitable period

See Determining the Date of Event for employment income for help coding income.

Accommodation payments + Read more ...

Care recipients in residential care must provide details of lump sum accommodation payments made to the aged care service. This includes Refundable Accommodation Deposits (RAD) and Refundable Accommodation Contributions (RAC).

If a lump sum accommodation payment has been paid by a care recipient, enter the current balance on the Exempt Accommodation Amounts (EXIA) screen. See Aged care fees and charges - accommodation payments.

9

Code rental income + Read more ...

Rental income received for a care recipient's former principal home may be exempt from a means assessment for aged care.

An income exemption for aged care purposes may apply where the care recipient:

  • is renting out their former principle home, and
  • is paying their accommodation costs by either periodic payment or a combination of periodic and lump sum payments, and
  • entered care before 1 Jan 2016 (including pre-entry leave)

Note: income support payment (ISP) care recipients who entered care before 1 January 2017, have rental income received for their former principal home exempt from the income and assets test. See Vacation of principal home due to illness.

If the care recipient receives rental income for their former principal home enter rental income received on REBS screen using applicable codes. Aged Care means assessment – Rental income from principal home.
