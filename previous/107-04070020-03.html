Apportioning debts between employers and employees

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Identifying and recovering the debt + Read more ...

The Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) are responsible for:

  • Identifying undetermined debts from PPL paid to the employer, and
  • issuing a Q318 letter to the employer

The Compliance and Debt Operations staff are responsible for:

  • raising employee debts (once identified by PPLEPT)
  • recovery of any debt raised

2

Sending the Q318 letter + Read more ...

A Q318 letter must be sent to the employer to find out who the debt should be recovered from.

Make 2 attempts to contact the employer before sending the Q318 if exceptional circumstances exist. This may include death of the PPL claimant.

If contact is:

  • successful, ask the employer to return the information as a priority
  • unsuccessful, send the Q318 letter to avoid a delayed response

Use the Debt Script to send a Q318 letter to the employer and resubmit the activity for 15 days. All requests for information from an employer must be in writing and should not be obtained over the phone.

The letter has the:

  • Date
  • Employee name
  • Employee reference (if provided by the employer)
  • External claim reference ID
  • Period that the debt relates to
  • Estimated amount of the debt
  • Amount paid to the employee

The notice will tell the employer:

  • to stop paying PPL to the employee or third party
  • they may need to amend their payroll records to make sure the employees payment summary is correct

Record a DOC on the employer's record in Customer First with the request for the employer to provide documents.

Go to Step 3

3

Create a task in Process Direct (PD) for the employer to upload the Q318 response + Read more ...

Staff create a task by selecting:

  • the Business Hub - Request Documents tile. Insert the Organisation CRN and select Search
  • the organisation CRN. The CRN must be 10 consecutive characters with no spaces. For example, 408XXXXXXA
  • select the Claimants of PPL tile
  • select the relevant employee that the documents relate to, from the column on the left hand side
  • select Tasks
  • Request Debt Investigation Q318
  • Paid Parental Leave Act 2010 for the Authority to request
  • 14 days for Information to be supplied
  • Incomplete Application for Required for
  • No for Staff Notes
  • Other documents for Claimant from Available Documents
  • the right arrow
  • Not Applicable for Provider Name
  • Add as no additional details are required
  • Finish

After the task is created:

  • an outstanding task generates for the employer in Business Hub
  • the employer gets an email notification. It says they can provide the requested documents through the Upload Requested Documents tile in Business Hub

Go to Step 4

4

Has the Q318 letter been returned? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, raise the debt against the employer. See Recovering debts from organisations

5

When the Q318 is received + Read more ...

The PPLEPT will:

  • upload the Q318 letter to the Employer's record
  • close the scan activity
  • change the debt activity hold date, to today and add keyword 'Q318'

Has the new debt activity been allocated?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, procedure ends here

6

Has the employer paid the PPL to the employee? + Read more ...

If the employer has received:

  • PPL but has not paid any amounts to the employee or the Australian Taxation Office (ATO), go to Step 7
  • PPL and paid all of the PPL to the employee (including withheld and Pay As You Go (PAYG) amounts), go to Step 8
  • PPL and paid some of the PPL to the employee (including withheld and PAYG amounts), go to Step 9

7

Employer has received PPL but has not paid any amounts to the employee + Read more ...

Raise the debt against the employer.

See Recovering debts from organisations.

Procedure ends here.

8

Employer has received PPL and has paid all the PPL to the employee (including withheld and PAYG amounts) + Read more ...

Raise the debt for the gross amount against the employee.

The debt notice (Account Payable) explains to employees that they can ask for a refund of the PAYG withholding amount included in the debt. They can request this from either:

  • their employer, or
  • the Australian Taxation Office (ATO)

This will depend on:

  • the date the employee gets the debt notice, and
  • if they take action before or after the end of the financial year

See Step 1 in Table 2.

9

Employer has received PPL and some of the PPL has been paid to the employee (including withheld and PAYG amounts) + Read more ...

Raise a debt against the:

  • employer for all amounts not paid to the employee
  • employee for the gross amounts provided to them (including withheld and PAYG amounts)

Debt notices will be issued to the:

  • employer (Request for Refund) - the notice will remind the employer to fix their payroll records
  • employee (Account Payable) - the notice will explain that they may be able to request a refund of the PAYG withholding amount included in the debt

See Step 1 in Table 2.

Table 2

Step

Description

1

Debt actioned + Read more ...

If the debt was actioned:

  • before the end of the financial year, go to Step 2
  • after the end of the financial year, but with no ATO Notice of Assessment (NOA) issued, go to Step 3
  • after the end of the financial year, and an ATO end of the financial year, and an ATO NOA has been issued, go to Step 4

2

Debt actioned before the end of financial year + Read more ...

Services Australia will recover the gross amount of PPL overpaid to an employee. This includes Pay as you Go (PAYG) amounts withheld.

The employee has the right to a refund of the amount of PAYG withheld. They can ask for a refund of PAYG withheld from either the employer or the ATO.

The employer must refund the PAYG amount to the employee before the end of the financial year in which the overpayment occurs when:

  • the employer becomes aware that the employee received an overpayment of PPL, or
  • the employee asks for a refund of the withholding amount

The employer must refund the PAYG amount to the employee even if they have paid this to the ATO. If this is the case, the employer can:

  • recover the refunded amount from the ATO as a debt, or
  • offset or reduce another payment to the ATO by the refunded withholding amount

The employer will need to include adjustments in their payroll records.

On their income tax return, the employee will include:

  • the total PAYG withheld, less any refunded amount, and
  • the total taxable income from their payment summary, less any overpaid PPL

Procedure ends here.

3

Debt was actioned after the end of financial year + Read more ...

PPL paid by Employer

If the overpayment occurred in a financial year prior to the year that it was identified, the employer does not need to refund the PAYG withholding amount.

Services Australia will:

  • recover the gross amount of the overpayment from the employee, and
  • tell them to recover the overpaid PAYG from the ATO

The employee will:

  • be able to show the debt notice (Account Payable) to the ATO as evidence of the overpayment
  • provide the amended total taxable income information from their revised payment summary or the debt notice in their income tax return

If the employee's income assessment for the previous year is not complete, they will get the benefit of the overpaid PAYG withholding amount in the calculation of their tax bill or refund.

They may ask for a payment arrangement with Services Australia that will allow them to repay the PAYG amount after they have recovered it from the ATO.

PPL paid by Services Australia

If Services Australia has paid the employee, the agency will issue an amended payment summary once:

  • the debt is raised, and
  • repayment has been received

The payment summary will display on the Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen. The ATO will automatically get the new tax information electronically.

Procedure ends here.

4

Debt was actioned after the end of the financial year and ATO Notice of Assessment (NOA) has been issued + Read more ...

The employee should lodge an amended assessment application with the ATO using:

  • the revised payment summary, or
  • the debt notice (Account Payable)

The ATO will:

  • subtract the gross amount of overpaid PPL from the employee's taxable income, and
  • fix their final income tax calculation for the relevant income year

This may result in an additional refund depending on the employee's tax liability.

Once fixed, the employee needs to tell government agencies such as Services Australia their revised income details. This may affect their eligibility for payments.

If the overpayment of PPL spans two financial years, the above processes apply to the portions of debt that attribute to each financial year.

Procedure ends here.
