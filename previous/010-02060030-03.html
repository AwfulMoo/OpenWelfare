Address Details (AD) screen

Recording the customer's address + Read more ...

The customer's address details are recorded on the AD screen. This information assists with assessment of eligibility for:

  • Remote Area Allowance (RAA)
  • ABSTUDY Fares Allowance
  • on-going qualification for away from home rate

To facilitate correct payment of away from home entitlements for ABSTUDY, the following coding is required for this screen:

Secondary school student

A secondary school student is entitled to away from home rate allowances (Living Allowance, Rent Assistance (RA) and Remote Area Allowance (RAA)) across vacation periods, even when the student returns to the permanent home for the holidays. The AD screen must be coded to match the eligibility period for away from home rate to ensure qualification for RAA matches the eligibility period for away from home rate.

If the student's home address is in a RAA specified area, record only a Home (HOM) address on the AD screen. Do not record the Term (TER) address. This facilitates correct payment of RAA for the full entitlement period.

To ensure the term address is recorded on the customer's record, a Display on Access (DOA) DOC can be created. If the student wants to receive mail at their term address, code a POS (postal) address with the term address details.

If the student's home address is not in a RAA specified area, record a HOM address and details of the location the student is residing whilst studying as a TER address.

Tertiary and secondary non-schooling students

The customer's address details coded must include the student's actual HOM address as well as the TER address at the study location. Tertiary students are subject to normal Remote Area Allowance entitlement provisions.

Australian Apprentices

Australian Apprentices who need to live away from home can have a TER address coded.

Accommodation (AC) screen

Recording living arrangements + Read more ...

The customer's living arrangements whilst living away from the permanent home are recorded on the Accommodation (AC) screen.

Access to Rent Assistance (RA) is determined by the customer's accommodation circumstances as recorded on the AC screen.

Note: the Address Type code of HOM (home) or TER (term) used on the Address Details (AD) screen, is the applicable address for matching rent assistance details on AC screen. To facilitate correct payment of away from home entitlements for ABSTUDY, the following coding is required for this screen:

Secondary school students

A secondary school student is entitled to away from home allowances across vacation periods even when the student returns to the permanent home for the holidays. The AC screen must be coded to match the eligibility period for away from home rate to ensure qualification for Remote Area Allowance (RAA) matches.

Note: for customers whose HOM address is in a RAA specified area, rent details are linked to the HOM address.

The key fields on the AC screen need specific coding to ensure rent assistance is paid correctly for an ABSTUDY secondary school student in a:

Private Board Arrangement:

  • Address Type: TER (or HOM if permanent residence is in a RAA area)
  • Event Date: code the commencement date of the board arrangement
  • Home Ownership: NHO (Non-Homeowner)
  • Type of Rent: BOA (Board and Lodging) or LOD (Lodging Only)
  • Sharer Status: See Sharer Status field on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen to determine sharer status and coding required
  • Gross/Total Household Rent: Enter the amount of the total rent paid for the property and the frequency of payments in the Freq field
  • Net Rent Amount: Enter the amount of rent paid by the customer and the frequency of payments in the Freq field
  • Rent Verification: COA (Change of Address) - if evidence is required, this will allow the Rent Certificate to be issued, or VEC (Verified Rent Certificate) - if a Rent Certificate SU523HD has been provided
  • Note: for students boarding privately, the AC screen is coded from the date the student commences in the private board arrangement, however, for students under 16, this may result in the payment cancelling or rejecting Doesn’t meet Age Requirements (AGD). To prevent this, a workaround is required; refer to the Resources tab for an example.

Boarding School, approved ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, or Hostel

  • Address Type: TER (or HOM if permanent residence is in a RAA area)
  • Event Date: first date of eligibility for away from home rate. If the student's first date of attendance is not yet known, enter the first date of the term entitlement period in which the away from home rate is being claimed
  • Home Ownership: NHO (Non-Homeowner)
  • Type of Rent: BOA (Board and Lodgings). Note: where a scholarship covers 100% of a student's boarding costs, code NRP (No rent paid)
  • Sharer Status: XSH (Exempt Sharer)
  • Gross/Total Household Rent: leave blank
  • Net Rent Amount: code the annual board charge of the school/hostel. Ensure the actual net amount charged to the student by the school is coded. For example, a student may receive a scholarship that covers all or most of the boarding fees, therefore, code only the amount charged after the scholarship has been deducted. Leave blank if Type of Rent is NRP
    Note: If the annual board charge for the relevant academic year is not yet known, use the most recent charges listed on the institution record
  • Freq: ANN (Annually), or leave blank if Type of Rent is NRP
  • Rent Verification: EXB (Exempt - Customer in Boarding Style accommodation)

Note: if actioning Group 3 or Group 5 End of Year Manual Follow-ups (MFU), a new accommodation screen should be inserted and the event date should be 1 January of the new academic year and not the first date of eligibility for away from home rate. See Actioning Reviews and Manual Follow-ups (MFUs) for more information.

Payment on Advice

ABSTUDY secondary school students boarding at a school, in an approved ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, or at a hostel that is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Standard Hostels Agreement must have their payments delivered to the school or hostel.

Payments are delivered as an ABSTUDY boarding instalment with the first instalment usually delivered to the school or boarding institution on the designated default pay date in the fourth week of the school term in each state or territory, providing the school or boarding institution has confirmed that the student has commenced. The second instalment is delivered automatically to the boarding institution near the end of the school term without a requirement for confirmation from the school of student attendance.

Payment on Advice is:

  • the term used for the process that requires schools and boarding institutions to advise Services Australia of term commencement dates for ABSTUDY away from home students to receive the first ABSTUDY boarding instalment
  • unavailable for students who are not paid via boarding instalment

OTAC Release Dates

A Payment on Advice request is sent to the school or boarding institution (based on where the student resides) in the second week of each school term. The request contains a One Time Access Code (OTAC) for schools/boarding institutions to log into the Education Institution eService and provide term commencement dates for ABSTUDY students. In the eService, schools/boarding institutions can view their estimated ABSTUDY boarding instalment payments for students and the delivery date for these entitlements.

A student processed as eligible for the away from home rate after the SAS extraction date for the Payment on Advice request for the term, will need to have their commencement status checked manually with the school or hostel. See Payment on Advice for ABSTUDY away from home rate for the SAS extraction dates.

Third Friday

ABSTUDY secondary school students are entitled to the away from home rate from the first day of the term entitlement period in which the student is eligible if they:

  • are boarding at a school, in an approved ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, or
  • are boarding at a hostel that is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Standard Hostels Agreement and
  • have commenced study at the school or hostel by the third Friday of the term

If a student does not commence by the third Friday of term:

  • the school or hostel is only eligible for payment from the date the student commenced at the boarding school or hostel
  • and it was due to circumstances beyond their control, they are treated the same as those who did commence by the third Friday. See Calculating the commencement date of away from home rate for ABSTUDY

Calculating the student start date on the EDC screen

The OTAC release dates can be found in the resources tab of Payment on Advice for ABSTUDY away from home rate. Within this document is the SAS extraction date for each term and state. This date is very important when choosing which date to code in the student start date field on the EDC screen. The SAS extraction date is the date used to compile the payment on advice list that is sent to schools and hostels. If a claim is granted for the current term entitlement period after the SAS extraction date, then that student will not appear on the Payment on Advice list.

If a claim is being assessed before the SAS extraction date in the current term, the student start date should be coded as the first day of the term entitlement period.

Note: Term 1 will need to be coded as the course start date because it is not possible to code 1 Jan.

If a claim is being assessed after the SAS extraction date in the current term and no first day of attendance has been provided from the school or hostel for the terms then the student start date should be coded as the first day of the next term entitlement period. Once the first day of attendance has been provided, the EDC can be updated with the date the student commenced so that the correct payments are issued. The school may need to be contacted or a manual request sent to collect this information.

Coding for Term 4 differs slightly when processing after the SAS extraction date. The coding will depend on if ABSTUDY is going to continue in the next calendar year. If the student is continuing study in the next calendar year, study will need to be coded for both calendar years. Term 4 should be coded from the start of the term 4 entitlement period. The EPDC should be coded from 1 January to avoid payments being issued. If the student is not continuing in the next calendar year, the claim cannot be granted until the term 4 first day of attendance is confirmed with the school.

When assessing previous terms, or having been provided with the first day of attendance to pay arrears in the current term, the student start date on EDC will need to be calculated using the third Friday rule.

In some cases multiple EDC screens may be needed if there has been a gap in entitlement.

If there is a gap in entitlement and the student was previously attending the same school in the same calendar year then a new EDC screen will need to be inserted under the same course ID. This can be done by selecting the existing course, adjusting the participation status event date field to the first day after the gap in entitlement and coding 'I' in the Action field. The student start and end dates within each EDC screen will need to be adjusted to ensure that it reflects the gap of entitlement.

Claim lodged to access Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel

Claim lodged to access Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel + Read more ...

Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel is available if:

  • a secondary school or boarding facility requires prospective students to attend interviews or other selection procedures prior to acceptance
  • the student is intending to study in an approved tertiary course of at least 1 year's duration at the study location, and has completed secondary studies in the previous year

In order to claim this type of travel, a student must be in receipt of ABSTUDY or an ABSTUDY claim needs to be lodged to assess their future entitlement for ABSTUDY assistance to live away from home to study.

Students applying from away from home rate due to a scholarship

Where qualification for the away from home rate is based on the student being awarded a scholarship in the year of studies and:

  • scholarship details are provided:
    • Code the NIH screen with the applicable Start/Reject Reason based on the scholarship
    • Code all other relevant screens as normal
    • Generate a review activity, see below Code a review section
  • scholarship details are not provided:
    • Code the NIH screen Start/Reject Reason with AET
    • Code the Education Course Details (EDC) screen with the hostel Education Institution code
    • Code $0.01 in the Annual Fees/Tuition Amount field on the EYSB screen
    • On the Payment Instructions (PIPI) screen, leave the School Fees Allowance (SFA-Boarding) component unallocated
    • Code the Education Payments Start Date (EPDC) screen from the start of the next term entitlement period to avoid incorrect payments going to the school or hostel
    • Code all other relevant screens as normal
    • Generate a review activity

Code a review

  • After the claim is finalised, code a manual review with the 'BOARDER' keyword for 14 days from the interview date, or if the claim is for studies in the following calendar year, 1 February in the year of study
  • Record scholarship information in the review notes, that is, scholarship details required or provided
  • Ensure desktop fax for granted/rejected boarders is sent to the boarding school/hostel, along with the request for approved scholarship confirmation
  • When the review matures:
    • If the scholarship details have not been provided by the time of the review, contact the school. If unable to contact, reject ABSTUDY using reason code 'Failed to Supply Documents' (FSD)
    • If scholarship details have been provided, ensure the NIH is corrected to ASP or ASF where AET has been coded
    • School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen is updated with the correct board and tuition amounts, and the AC screen reflects the correct board paid. See Assessing scholarship income

Students applying for away from home rate and not in receipt of a scholarship

A secondary boarding facility, such as a hostel, may need to conduct an interview or selection exercise. When this occurs it may not be possible to confirm which school the student will be attending. In these cases, the claim can be accepted and temporarily coded without school details to enable travel bookings:

  • Code the NIH screen with the applicable Start/Reject Reason
  • Code the Education Course Details (EDC) screen with the hostel Education Institution code
  • Code $0.01 in the Annual Fees/Tuition Amount field on the EYSB screen. Leave the School Fees Allowance (SFA-Boarding) component unallocated on the Payment Instructions (PIPI) screen
  • Code the Education Payments Start Date (EPDC) screen from the start of the next term entitlement period to avoid incorrect payments going to the school or hostel. Note: use discretion when determining the next term-entitlement period. Ensure the student is given 14 days to provide the school details after the interview and a further 7 days to process a review. For example, if the interview is in August and the student is looking to start in the following year, EPDC would be coded from 1 JAN of the following year
  • Code all other relevant screens as normal
  • After the claim is finalised, code a manual review 14 days from the interview date with the 'BOARDER' keyword
  • Ensure desktop fax for granted/rejected boarders is sent to the hostel
  • When the review matures:
    • If the school's details have not been provided, contact the applicant. If the school's details are still unknown or staff are unable to contact the applicant, reject ABSTUDY using reason code 'Failed to Supply Documents' (FSD)
    • If the school's details have been provided, ensure the EDC screen is updated with the correct Education Institution code. Code the EYSB screen with the correct tuition fees and allocate accordingly via the PIPI screen

Payment of Supplementary Allowances to students via the Supplementary Education Payments (EPSP) screen

Coding for supplementary services and concessions + Read more ...

ABSTUDY students approved for away from home rate can access additional supplementary services and concessions, other than those intended to assist with living costs whilst studying away from home.

Note: Australian Apprentices are not entitled to these additional payments.

Most supplementary allowances are paid via the EPSP screen. Payments issued through the EPSP screen are paid in a lump sum to either the student, or a nominated third party payee.

Frequency of payment of away from home allowances is determined by the allowance type and payee.

Supplementary allowances relevant to students who are living away from home are:

Secondary school student

Payment of School Fees Allowance (SFA) (Group 2) to secondary boarders in the following circumstances, are payable via the EPSP screen:

  • Students in private board
  • Students in a hostel which is not a signatory to the ABSTUDY Standard Hostels Agreement

See coding the payee for ABSTUDY away from home entitlements.

Tertiary and secondary non-schooling student

Payment of the lump sum of Residential Cost Option fees to the residential college for tertiary students is payable via the EPSP screen.

Payment of SFA (Group 2) or Residential Cost Option must have the full charge for the entitlement period recorded.

The correct payee must be determined by completion of the Payee field.

Note: if fees are unknown, contact the school to obtain the amount charged for that student.

Payment of School Fees and Boarding allowances for secondary boarders using the School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen

Coding for Living Allowance and School Fees Allowance + Read more ...

Payment of Living Allowance and School Fees Allowance (SFA) for some secondary school students is determined by details coded on the EYSB screen. This screen is used to pay Living Allowance and SFA for students whose living arrangements are as follows:

  • Boarding at a school
  • Approved ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement
  • Boarding at a hostel which is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement

It is also used to pay Living Allowance only, for students who are boarding privately with payments of Living Allowance to be delivered to the board provider, or at a hostel that is not a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement.

Note: SFA for students in private board arrangements, or in a hostel that is not a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement, is paid via the EPSP screen.

This screen needs the board charge and school fee charge (where applicable) to be obtained prior to processing, otherwise the transfer of excess School Fees Allowance to assist with boarding shortfall does not occur correctly. If fees are unknown, contact the school to obtain the amount charged for that student.

It will be necessary to request board and school fee charges each year for hostels and boarding schools who accept ABSTUDY boarders.

Note: some students may be in receipt of a scholarship that contributes to all or some of the Board and/or Fees, therefore, ensure the amount coded on the EYSB screen is the net amount of the school charges after the scholarship have been deducted. For example, if the school charge is normally $10,000 per year for Board and a scholarship contributes $4,000 per year to the Boarding fees, the correct amount to code in regards to Board is the net amount of $6,000.

This ensures that the correct amount is paid to the Boarding School/Hostel, and any excess Living Allowance is paid back to the parent/guardian/student.

To facilitate correct payment of away from home entitlements for ABSTUDY, key coding requirements on the EYSB screen are:

  • Event Date: date boarding payments are to commence
    • For students in a boarding school, ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, or a hostel which is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement: code the commencement date of the away from home rate. If the student's first date of attendance is not yet known, enter the first date of the term entitlement period in which the away from home rate is being claimed
    • For private boarders: code the commencement date of the boarding arrangement
  • Annual Fees/Tuition Amount: annual school fee charge. If school fee charges are nil, enter the amount as $0.01. Leave blank for private boarders
  • Boarding School/Hostel Action:
    • For students boarding at a hostel which is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement code AHO
    • For students in a boarding school, including an approved ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement code ASC
    • For private boarders code NPB
  • Annual Boarding School/Hostel Amount: annual board charge, leave blank for private boarders
  • Actual Term Start Date: first date of school term in which the student commenced at the away from home rate
  • Weekly Private Board Amount: code the amount requested by the applicant to be paid to the Private Board Provider (PBP) as a weekly amount. If the applicant has requested the student's entire ABSTUDY entitlement to be paid to the PBP, ensure an amount is coded that is equal to or higher than the student’s rate of payment
    Note: leave blank for students in a boarding school, ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, or a hostel which is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement
  • Scholarship Contribution Amount: the amount a school or scholarship provider contributes to an approved scholarship for the away from home rate

If granting ABSTUDY for the new academic year where the boarding and tuition fees are known, code the boarding/tuition fees accordingly.

If granting ABSTUDY for the new academic year where the boarding and tuition fees are unknown:

Where the current year boarding or tuition fees are unknown:

  • code the most recent charges listed on the institution record
  • set a review for 1 February with the following keywords where applicable:
    • GRP 3 if student is ABSTUDY current, under 16 years old and boarding at secondary school or hostel
    • GRP 5 if student is ABSTUDY current, 16 years old or more and boarding at secondary school or hostel
    • add an additional keyword of AHO if the student is boarding at a hostel
    • Education Institution Code, for example, 28195

If there are no previous charges listed on the institution record:

  • contact the institution to get details verbally
  • use details from the provider's website if not able to contact the provider, or
  • issue a Request for Information (RFI) asking for evidence of fees, when neither of the options above are available

Note: if the student is boarding at a hostel, include both the hostel and school institution codes.

This review will be picked up with End of Year (EOY) processing. See Actioning Reviews and Manual Follow-ups (MFUs) associated with EOY processing.

Coding on the School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen to allow transfer of excess School Fees Allowance (SFA) to 'top-up' Living Allowance shortfall

Coding for transfer of excess SFA + Read more ...

Secondary school students boarding at a school, an approved ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, or at a hostel that is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement, can qualify for any excess SFA to be transferred to assist with any shortfall in Living Allowance. This is known as 'top-up' and is not available to students in any other living arrangement.

Where a student's total annual entitlement of combined Living Allowance, Rent Assistance and Remote Area Allowance is insufficient to meet the board charge of the school or hostel, any excess SFA, up to the maximum available, is transferred across to top-up the shortfall in board.

The system automatically determines top-up eligibility and amount, based on the coding on the EYSB screen.

Mandatory coding of the actual board and school fee charges is needed to allow top-up to occur where applicable.

Note: both board and school fee charges must be obtained prior to the first ABSTUDY boarding instalment being paid. If the school fee charge is not coded, the system will assume that no school fees are charged and therefore the full amount of SFA will be available as a top-up towards boarding fees. This may mean that insufficient SFA is available to pay the school fees.

See the Resources page for examples of how the system automatically calculates top-up based on the coding of board and school fee charges on EYSB screen.

Coding on the School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen for Hostels that are not signatory to the Standard or National Hostel Agreement

Coding for Hostels that are not a signatory of the ABSTUDY Standard or National Hostel Agreement + Read more ...

The student’s living allowance entitlement for the whole calendar year is used to help meet the annual amounts charged by the hostel for board, even though the entitlement may relate to periods prior to or after the school year.

Entitlements calculated are based on a daily rate and must be made fortnightly in arrears directly to the hostel to cover the boarding costs.

To facilitate this payment insert a new EYSB screen:

  • Event Date: start of Living Allowance entitlement period associated with boarding at the Hostel. That is, if the customer is eligible to have payment backdated to 1 Jan, the event date would be 1 Jan rather than the date they commenced boarding
  • Annual Fees/Tuition Amount: leave blank
  • Boarding School/Hostel Action: leave blank
  • Annual Boarding School/Hostel Amount: leave blank
  • Actual Term Start Date: first date of school term in which the student commenced at the away from home rate
  • Weekly Private Board Amount: calculate the annual boarding fees and convert it to a weekly amount

Coding of the School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen when a student has been granted a scholarship that reduces or waives their fees

Coding for scholarships that reduce or waive fees + Read more ...

A student who is living away from home may be granted a scholarship that waives or reduces their student fees or boarding and lodging costs. The EYSB screen must be coded in either instance. The amount a school or scholarship provider contributes towards a scholarship is recorded at the Scholarship Contribution Amount field on the EYSB screen

See Assessing scholarship income for correct coding.

Coding the payee for ABSTUDY away from home entitlements

Coding the payee + Read more ...

Payment of away from home entitlements to the correct payee is governed by the coding used on the record. See payment of away from home rate to determine who the correct payee is based on the customer's study level or Australian Apprenticeship, living arrangements and age.

To facilitate correct payment of away from home entitlements for ABSTUDY, the following coding is required:

Tertiary and secondary non-schooling students and Australian Apprentices

Payee: Customer

Screen: Payment Destination Summary (PASB) screen

Note: if the student has elected to accept the Residential Cost Option:

  • payment of residential fees to the college is paid via the Supplementary Education Payments (EPSP) screen
  • reduced rate of Living Allowance is directed to the student

Secondary school students

Boarding at a school, approved ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, or hostel which is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Standard Hostels Agreement:

  • Payee: school or hostel
  • Screen: School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen
  • once the EYSB screen is coded, see Directing payments to third party payees

Boarding privately or in a hostel which is not a signatory to the ABSTUDY Standard Hostels Agreement

Private boarding charges must be advised prior to coding.

  • Payee: Parent or student depending on the student's age, and/or the private board provider if requested. The private board provider can only be paid up to the maximum entitlement

If payment is directed to a private board provider and there is residual entitlement, it can be paid to either the parent or the student, depending on the student's age.

  • Screen: to pay directly to the parent/student code the Payment Destination Summary (PASB) screen
  • To set up the payment instruction for payments directed to private board provider, code the EYSB screen
  • If school fees need to be paid, pay via the EPSP screen, see ABSTUDY School Fees Allowance (SFA) (Group 2)
    • If school fees are unknown, contact the school to obtain the amount charged for the student. If contact is unsuccessful, do not code the EPSP screen. Set a review for 14 days to follow up obtaining school fees charges, or if assessing a claim for studies in the following calendar year, set a review for 1 February in the year of study
  • Once the EYSB and the EPSP screen (if necessary) is coded, see Directing payments to third party payees

Directing payments to third party payees

Rules for determining appropriate payee and payment frequency + Read more ...

Strict rules determine the appropriate payee and frequency of payment of away from home rate of ABSTUDY. Correct coding of the customer's record facilitates the correct payment delivery.

An ABSTUDY customer can choose to direct payment of allowances to a third party. A third party payee can be either a person or an organisation.

Allowances coded on either of the following screens need the third party payee details to be linked to the payment:

  • Supplementary Payment (EPSP) screen
  • School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen

For instructions on linking third party payees to allowances, see ABSTUDY payments to third parties and institutions.

To facilitate correct payment of away from home entitlements for ABSTUDY, the following key coding is required for this screen:

  • Ensure individual allowances are linked to the correct payee. A different payee may exist for each allowance
  • Ensure any payments to an organisation are linked to the organisation record, rather than create a manual payment direction. See creating and updating organisation records

Checking assessment outcome at the Assessment Results (AR) screen

Checking the outcome on the AR screen (Customer First) or ELD screen (Process Direct) + Read more ...

Upon completion of coding for the away from home rate, the AR/ELD screen will display the outcome of the assessment. The information displayed on the AR/ELD screen should be checked to ensure the assessment has produced the correct outcome.

The AR/ELD screen displays the student's entitlements, including:

  • allowance type
  • period of entitlement for each allowance
  • total amount of each allowance for the entitlement period (including any arrears owing)
  • end date of eligibility

Check the display to ensure correct entitlement is payable before finalising the assessment. A quick calculation may be useful to determine whether the coding has correctly released all necessary payments. The process to calculate entitlements is:

  • add up total annual entitlement of Living Allowance, Rent Assistance and Remote Area Allowance (where eligible) based on age and living circumstances
  • apply applicable means test to entitlements to determine adjusted total entitlement of boarding allowances and School Fees Allowance (SFA) (for secondary school level students)
  • for secondary school boarders - match total adjusted entitlements (boarding and school fee) against board and school fee charges of school/hostel or private board provider:
    • if board entitlement is in excess of board charge, residual boarding entitlements payable to student or parent
    • if boarding entitlements are insufficient to cover board charge, determine if excess SFA exists
    • if SFA is in excess of school fee charge, transfer unused SFA up to maximum available to meet shortfall in board charge
    • if SFA is insufficient to meet the school fee charge, full charge cannot be met
  • determine if correct payee and payment frequency will occur

If correct complete the activity on the AR/ELD screen, and record all details of the assessment on the new claim DOC.
