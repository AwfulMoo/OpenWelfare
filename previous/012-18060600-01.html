This document outlines information about eligibility and registration requirements for social workers.

Social workers

Social workers have a dual focus on both assisting with and improving human wellbeing and identifying and addressing any external issues that may impact on or may create inequality, injustice and discrimination.

Common issues addressed by social workers include:

  • mental health and physical issues
  • gambling and substance abuse
  • poverty
  • unemployment and homelessness
  • family violence
  • trauma
  • offending behaviour

For Medicare purposes, social workers are categorised into 3 types according to qualifications and credentialing. These types determine which Medicare services they can provide:

  • Accredited Mental Health Social Workers (AMHSW) qualified to provide focussed psychological strategies (FPS) services under Medicare only
  • An AMHSW qualified to provide both FPS and non-directive pregnancy support counselling (PSC) services under Medicare only
  • Accredited Social Workers (ASW) qualified to provide PSC services under Medicare only

Disputes about removing a registration

Direct any disputes about registration to the Australian Association of Social Workers (AASW). The Resources page has the contact details.

Focussed psychological strategies (FPS)

Accredited Mental Health Social Workers (AMHSWs) have advanced training that qualifies them to use a range of focussed psychological strategies including:

  • cognitive behavioural therapy
  • relaxation strategies
  • skills training (including parenting skills)
  • problem solving
  • anger management
  • stress management
  • social skills

See Focussed psychological strategies (FPS) - Allied Health

Non-directive pregnancy support counselling (PSC)

This is a form of counselling based on the idea that some individuals can resolve their own problems without being provided with a solution by the counsellor.

The service involves the counsellor undertaking a safe, confidential process that helps the patient explore concerns they have about a pregnancy.

Additional information about the PSC initiative is available on the Department of Health and Aged Care’s website. See the Resources page for a link, as well as a fact sheet about the program.

Both Accredited Mental Health Social Workers (AMHSW) and Accredited Social Workers (ASW) can undertake PSC training through either:

  • Australian Psychological Society (APS), or
  • Australian College of Mental Health Nurses (ACMHN)

A certificate is issued upon completion of the course, and provides access to Medicare benefits specific to this type of counselling.

Medicare eligibility and registration

To be registered with Medicare, an Accredited Mental Health Social Worker (AMHSW) or Accredited Social Worker (ASW) must meet the eligibility criteria.

The following documentation must be supplied with Initial provider number applications:

  • For the AMHSW to provide Focussed Psychological Strategies (FPS) they must supply a certificate issued by the Australian Association of Social Workers (AASW) confirming:
    • Current member of the AASW, and
    • Accreditation as an Allied Health Professional (Mental Health Social Worker), and
    • Meeting the standards for mental health set out in Practice Standards for Mental Health Social Workers 2014
  • For the AMHSW to provide Non-directive pregnancy support counselling (PSC) they must supply a certificate issued by the Australian Association of Social Workers (AASW), confirming:
    • Current member of the AASW, and
    • Accreditation as an Allied Health Professional (Mental Health of Social Worker), and
    • Meeting the standards for mental health set out in Practice Standards for Mental Health Social Workers 2014, and
    • A PSC certificate of completion in Perinatal Non-Directive Counselling Training: V2 issued from the Australian Psychological Society
  • For the ASW to provide Non-directive pregnancy support counselling (PSC) they must supply:
    • Membership of the Australian Association of Social Workers (AASW), and
    • Certificate as an ASW issued by the AASW, and
    • A PSC certificate of completion in Perinatal Non-Directive Counselling Training: V2 issued by the Australian Psychological Society

Recognition of a Social Worker for Medicare purposes is made by the Chief Executive Medicare (or their delegate) under the Health Insurance (Allied Health Services) Determination 2014 made under subsection 3C(1) of the Health Insurance Act 1973.

See Resources page for a link to allied health professional recognition information.

Enquiry escalation

The Provider Registration enquiry line and email inbox, managed by Medicare Provider Services (MPS), are used as the front door for all enquiries relating to the processing of provider registrations.

Enquiries to this area are responded to by a Service Officer trained in all aspects of provider registration. These Service Officers escalate complex enquiries to a Programme Support Officer (PSO) who will liaise with the Provider Eligibility team in National Office for assistance if required.
