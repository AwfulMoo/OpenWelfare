Reason for suspension + Read more ...

CA may be suspended if the carer is temporarily not qualified for payment or the payment is not payable as a result of:

  • changes in either the carer or care receivers circumstances
  • failure to comply with the requirements for CA, or
  • information is required to ensure that CA is directed correctly (such as a valid address or bank account details)

Is it appropriate to consider suspending payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, procedure ends here

4

Contact with the carer + Read more ...

CA must not be suspended or cancelled if it is just suspected that a carer is no longer eligible for payment or if they have unverified information (e.g. a tip-off) which suggests they may not be eligible. The carer must be asked to provide information, no matter how significant the evidence. A decision cannot be made without giving customers the chance to address adverse information.

If the carer has a correspondence nominee, copies of letters and notices must also be sent to the nominee. CA must not be suspended if contact with the nominee has not been made or attempted.

Has the carer been issued with an appropriate request for information/action?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Request for information + Read more ...

Information must be requested from the carer via a notice under the appropriate section of the Social Security (Administration) Act; section 63, 67, 68, or part 5, division 1.

Requests for information must include:

  • details of the information/action required
  • the timeframe for response (noting that this must be reasonable)
  • the consequences of non-compliance, and
  • the reference to the relevant Act under which the request has been made

Issue a written request for the required information and set a manual review to reassess after reasonable timeframe has expired. See Requesting information (CLK).

If the carer has a correspondence nominee, copies of letters and notices must also be sent to the nominee.

Procedure ends here until the information has been provided and or the review for the return of information has matured.

Issue a manual letter if required (Q134).

6

Response/non-response to request for information + Read more ...

Has the carer responded to the request for information/action within the reasonable timeframe allowed?

  • Yes, cancel the manual review for the return of information and go to Step 8
  • No, and:
    • the request was 'returned to sender', see Correspondence returned to Centrelink 'Whereabouts unknown'. Procedure ends here
    • the review has matured without a response, go to Step 7

7

Carer has not responded to request + Read more ...

If a manual review activity falls due and the carer has not responded to the request for information, check if special circumstances (such as vulnerability indicators) exist to warrant an extension of time to respond.

Do special circumstances exist?

  • Yes, resubmit the request for information. DOC the reason for extension including special circumstances, etc. Set a new review date. Procedure ends here until response is received or new review date is reached
  • No, the carer has failed to reply to correspondence, see Step 1 in Table 2

8

Carer responds to request for information, update record + Read more ...

Update the carer or care receivers record with the information provided. If the carer's circumstances preclude payment of CA, payment will be automatically suspended or cancelled.

Record a DOC on the carer's record as per Getting it Right (GIR) DOC Minimum Standards.

1

Decision to suspend CA + Read more ...

Reasons to suspend CA include:

  • a direct credit payment was returned from the financial institution
  • the carer failed to reply to correspondence
  • the carer is in prison or psychiatric confinement in connection with an offence

If the carer is imprisoned or placed in psychiatric confinement for 13 weeks or less, payments are suspended from the date of confinement. If the carer is still in prison or psychiatric confinement after 13 weeks, the suspended payment will auto cancel from the date of confinement.

Is the decision to suspend the payment correct?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, record a DOC on the carers record providing information on why a manual suspension is now not required. See:
    • Online Document Recording (ODR) for Act References
    • Creating, reviewing and deleting documents (including Fast Notes and DOA DOCs)
    • Procedure ends here

Suspend CA + Read more ...

Update the carer or care receiver's record with relevant information to enable the system to make an automatic determination where possible.

  • the death of the care receiver, see:

3

The cancellation and suspension workflow + Read more ...

If a carer receives CA for more than 1 care receiver, it is not possible to suspend 1 payment of CA. In these situations, as a matter of urgency, contact the carer and obtain the required information.

The Carer cancellation and suspension workflow will allow Service Officers to:

  • cancel or suspend CA at payment level or care receiver level
  • in some cases the workflow will auto-populate the reason field where it is appropriate to cancel or suspend both CP and CA
  • perform different actions at the same time, for example, cancel CA if care requirements are not met and cancel CP if the carer's activities exceed 25 hours per week

Where the care receiver is not the carer's partner or child, the care receiver's circumstances will need to be updated separate to the workflow.

The workflow can only be actioned in the carer's record. The workflow contains detailed help and direct links to Operational Blueprint.

The Carer cancellation and suspension workflow will recognise and prompt for hand off when a Service Officer tries to use a code that is limited to carer Processing Service use. When this occurs, hand the activity off to the appropriate Processing Service for assessment and completion

For a complete list of valid suspension and cancellation reasons that can be used within this workflow, see:

  • Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for CA
  • Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for CP

The workflow allows some current BA and CJBA codes to be selected only, manual suspension may be required if the BA and/or CJBA code is not able to be selected within the workflow.

To use the Cancellation and Suspension workflow, go to Step 4.

To record a manual cancellation or suspension, go to Step 5.

4

Using the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow + Read more ...

In Customer First:

  • run the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow, see Carer cancellation and suspension workflow
  • check that the source and date of receipt are correct. The source field will automatically populate based on the Service Officer's user profile location and the date of receipt field will automatically populate with the system date. If these are incorrect they must be changed before proceeding
  • the workflow will automatically create a DOC using the Notepad function with suspension and/or cancellation reason, DOE, section of Act. Check the details of the DOC and record any additional notes including arrears details, overpayment amount, sent for investigation, etc. into the notepad which will then be added to the DOC

Note: if the reason 'Other' ('OTH') is coded, a prompt will appear advising the Service Officer to hand off to the Carer Processing Service if they are not attached to a Carer Processing Service in their user profile. The message prompt is 'Carer Processing Teams should only use 'OTH' as a final option where no other cancellation, suspension or change in circumstance coding is appropriate.' A manual letter must be issued after the workflow is completed.

For suspensions, the workflow will automatically populate the DOE field with DPT+1.

Workflow prompts for some of reason options will assist with coding the correct DOE, for example, entry into permanent institution 'PMI', carer activity exceeds 25 hours per week '25H'.

Note: if a carer receives CA for more than 1 care receiver, it is not possible to suspend 1 payment of CA. In these situations, as a matter of urgency, contact the carer and obtain the required information.

In the event the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow is unavailable, manual suspension of CA will be required.

Is manual suspension of CA required?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Manually code CA suspension + Read more ...

In the carer's record in the ISS system, key:

  • 'BA' in the Nxt field
  • 'CDA' in the Svc Rsn field
  • 'SUS' in the Action field
  • the reason code in the Reason field. For a full list of valid reasons, key a question mark ('?') in the Reason field. See also Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Carer Allowance (CA)
  • the relevant date in the Date of Effect field

Press [Enter] to go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

6

Finalise the activity + Read more ...

Check the details of the DOC created by the workflow. The workflow will automatically create a DOC using the Notepad function with suspension reason, DOE, section of Act. If a manual suspension was performed ensure all the details are recorded on a DOC.

Record any additional notes including arrears details, overpayment amount, sent for investigation, etc. into the notepad which will then be added to the DOC.

Check the Assessment Warnings and Error (AWE) screen and action any warnings or errors if necessary.

Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

7

Check carer's other entitlements + Read more ...

If the carer is receiving CP, check if they remain eligible or if this should be suspended also.

If the care receiver is a child and the carer is receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) follow up if FTB is still payable. For more information see Child leaves customer's care/custody.

If the carer and care receiver are partners, they may be entitled to be assessed as being separated from their partner due to ill health.

Check if the carer may be entitled to receive any other financial assistance.

8

Check whether a suspension needs to occur for the carer's partner + Read more ...

This would be relevant if the carer's partner is receiving a payment and the suspension reason also applies to the partner. For example, if the partner's direct credit payment was also returned.

If appropriate, suspend the partner's payment. See the 'Related links' section of Suspension of payments (CLK) for links to information about the suspension process for a variety of payments.

See Step 1 in Table 3.

After suspending CA

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Manual letters + Read more ...

If a manual Q134 letter is required following the suspension activity, a Manual Follow Up (MFU) activity will be generated, usually overnight, and allocate for action.

Has a manual suspension letter already been issued to the carer at the time of suspension?

  • Yes, finalise the MFU and go to Step 2
  • No, send a manual letter. Select the MFU from the Activity List (AL) screen and manually issue a Q134 letter to the carer. The letter must include the following information:
    • payment type
    • decision
    • date of effect of the suspension
    • a clear explanation of the reason the payment has been suspended
    • a clear explanation of what the carer must do to have their payment restored and the consequences for not doing this (that is, payment will be cancelled)
    • a reasonable timeframe to take required action to avoid cancellation
    • their review and appeal rights
    • the relevant section of the legislation under which the decision was made

2

Set up review + Read more ...

If the CA has been suspended manually, set up a manual review via the Review Registration (RVR) screen to check if the carer qualifies for CA and the payment is payable. This should mature at the time the decision to suspend must be re-examined - usually 13 weeks later.

Update the suspension DOC on the carer's record with the date the review falls due.

See How to code and action a manual review.

If a vulnerable or at risk customer remains without income support payment for a minimum of 6 weeks due to an automatic suspension or cancellation, the customer will require manual intervention. Customers who are overseas permanently are not eligible for this initiative. Cancel any MFUs generated for customers in this situation.

3

Carer contacts due to the suspension, or review date is reached + Read more ...

Review date reached

If the review due date has been reached and no action has been taken on the carers record, review the carers qualification and payability for CA. The criteria listed below are relevant to determining ongoing eligibility.

Carer contacts due to the suspension

If a carer contacts regarding the suspension, check the DOC recorded with suspension details and ascertain if the carer:

  • has already complied with the action required or whether they can do so now
  • is not required to supply documentation and/or
  • does not need to attend the service centre and that the CA is able to be restored

Does the carer meet all of the above criteria and is eligible for payment?

  • Yes, and there is no note on a DOC to prevent restoration, restore CA
  • No, to 1 or more of the above, tell the carer of the action required prior to restoration of CA
