Rejecting a claim for CP

For Carer Processing Service staff

Step

Action

1

Care receiver cannot be identified + Read more ...

Is the carer claiming CP and the care receiver cannot be identified?

  • Yes, make extensive efforts (phone calls, Q164 letter to the carer, searches on the system). If the care receiver cannot be identified:
    • set the new claim to ‘Not required’
    • complete a DOC stating that it has been determined the claim is rejected 'FRC' and due to system restraints the new claim activity is cancelled, and
    • send a manual letter to the carer. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check decision to reject claim for CP + Read more ...

If the carer withdraws their claim, see Withdrawal of claims.

Ensure decision to reject the claim for CP is correct.

Ensure the carer does not meet the qualification and/or payability provisions for CP and the decision to reject the claim is correct:

  • consider entitlement to an alternative payment
  • tell the carer of possible alternative entitlement and give them any relevant publications for payments and concessions. See Initial contact by a carer

Record all information provided by the carer to allow the system to auto-reject the claim. If a claim cannot auto-reject by the system during the new claim procedure, reject the claim manually.

If the carer has submitted the carer questionnaire but not a medical report, record the carer questionnaire rather than rejecting the claim FRC due to the non-return of the medical report. However, if the carer is already in receipt of CA, reject the claim FRC, otherwise CA will be cancelled from the date the claim is submitted and a debt incorrectly raised.

Note: if the carer is receiving a disability payment from the Department of Veterans' Affairs, do not reject their claim based on income before checking whether they may be entitled to receive Defence Force Income Support Allowance (DFISA). The system will be able to identify these carers if the claim is correctly coded including DVA Disability payment details.

Exceptions to rejecting the claim for income and assets may apply if the carer has applied for a loan under the Home Equity Access Scheme. Contact the Home Equity Access Scheme National team using Fast Note before rejecting the CP claim based on income and assets. Stream the claim and place on hold accordingly. See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services.

To determine if carer assessment details are required for Home Equity Access Scheme purposes, use Fast Note - select Auto text, use > Complex Assessment > Request for CAO action > CAO Home Equity Access Scheme.

When employment income precludes payability and it is clear that the customer will become payable within 13 weeks of the date they lodged the claim, the start date should be moved instead of rejecting. See Processing a claim for Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA)

Will the claim auto-reject?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Record residence information + Read more ...

The carer's residence qualification will be auto-assessed as long as the residence information is correctly recorded.

Note: residence information must still be recorded if the rejection is for any other reason. If residence details are not recorded, the system will override any other rejection and the claim will reject for a residence related reason.

4

Reject the claim + Read more ...

To reject carer’s claim, record all information provided by the carer. This allows the system to auto-reject the claim. If the system cannot auto-reject the new claim, reject the claim manually.

To reject the carer's claim, navigate to the Carer's record in the PEN system, go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen. In the:

  • Svc Rsn field, key 'CAR'
  • Action field, key 'REJ'
  • Reason field, key the relevant reason code (use field help '?' for relevant rejection reasons)
  • Date of Effect field, key the relevant date

Press [Enter] to open the Assessment Result (AR) screen. If rejection reason is FRC, add a decision DOC that lists all documents not provided at the time of the rejection/cancellation.

Note: carer's record status must be either NCL (New Claim) or ASS (Assessed) for the rejection activity to be accepted.

When rejecting a claim FRC, record all information provided by the carer before rejecting the claim. It is not necessary to request more information before rejecting the claim, e.g. bank statements. See Processing claims for Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA).

Care receiver POI may be stored on the care receiver’s record instead of the carers. Service Officers must check this before rejecting a claim due to care receiver POI not provided.

When rejecting a claim, a Service Officers Interview (SOI) is required before the claim is finalised to explain the adverse decision, see Step 6.

5

Reject claim after grant of another income support payment (ISP) + Read more ...

Access the Carer's record in the PEN system, navigate to the Benefit Action (BA) screen. In the:

  • Svc Rsn field, key 'CAR'
  • Action field, key 'REJ'
  • Reason field, key the relevant reason code (use field help '?' for relevant rejection reasons)
  • Date of Effect field, key the relevant date. This will be 1 day after the grant of the other ISP. Note: a DOC must be added to explain why this date was used

6

Service Offer Interview (SOI) + Read more ...

Before finalising a rejection for CP, make 2 genuine attempts to contact the carer/nominee to explain the reason(s) for rejection, give information about their options for reassessment or to request an explanation or apply for a formal review of the decision. This includes claims finalised during overtime. Consider local time zone differences. See Calling a customer or returning a customer’s call.

Successful contact attempt

Service Officers must follow the appropriate protocols when calling a customer.

When the call is answered and it is clear it is the customer, Service Officers can begin by stating:

  • their name
  • that they are calling from Services Australia (or Centrelink)
  • that the call may be recorded for training and quality purposes

Service Officers must confirm the identity of the care or correspondence nominee to ensure that the person on the phone is the person to whom the information relates.

Staff can explain:

  • (if the contact is during overtime), that they are working outside normal business hours to action their claim. Ask if it is convenient for them to discuss the outcome now
  • the reason for the call and the reason for rejection. Tell the carer/nominee the steps in the reassessment of claims following rejection and their review and appeal rights

If the carer/nominee says it is not a convenient time for them, tell them:

  • the claim will be finalised and a written advice issued, and
  • to contact the agency if they need a more detailed explanation after receiving the letter

Unsuccessful contact attempt

If the number called has an answering machine or voicemail:

  • leave a short message identifying the Service Officer and that the caller is from Services Australia
  • leave an appropriate phone number for the customer to return the call. Choosing to leave a direct, queue, or other phone number is a business decision

Staff may leave a voice message even if the customer’s name is not in the answering message. See Privacy concerns when calling customers, returning calls or leaving messages on answering machines.

If the first contact attempt is unsuccessful but the phone numbers are still current, the work item will need to be 'Held to user'.

National Support Services

If a carer discloses they may be at risk of harm, give them information on support services if required. See the Resources page in Rejecting a Carer Allowance (CA) claim. If this occurs during normal business hours, transfer to a Social Worker.

Note: if the carer is in immediate danger, a delegate must call 000.

After the SOI

Run the Fast Note - Carers > Claims > CP/CA NCL SOI Contact on the carer's record

In the free text section, record the first SOI attempt - date, phone numbers and time of day.

Document any relevant extra information provided by the carer/nominee on the carer’s record. Include if they will be lodging more information, e.g. for a rejection.

Annotate this Fast Note with all further SOI attempts.

SOI exemptions

For CP new claims actioned (weeknights and weekends), Service Officers must review the carer's record and determine if an SOI exemption may apply.

Examples of when it may not be appropriate to attempt an SOI outside business hours include:

  • adversely impact the carer's wellbeing
  • bereavement cases
  • clearly documented CRU complaints
  • likely to result in political or media attention
  • if it is clear from the carer's record, they require an interpreter for Services Australia interactions

In these exception cases:

  • clearly detail on the record why an SOI was not attempted (use Progress Note or DOC)
  • apply any income and assets updates
  • hold the claim and any other related activities to self until the next business day adding an SOI keyword to the claim activity

The claim will present to the Service Officer the next business day for another SOI attempt.

SOI and quality checking

SOI calls should be attempted before finalising a claim, whether or not the claim goes to QMA.

If the QMO decision is significantly different (e.g. grant rather than reject, the QMO will try to contact and tell the customer the new decision.

If the QMO identifies that the SOI process has not been followed but the activity is otherwise correct, the QMO will complete the SOI before claim release, and give national feedback to the processing Service Officer.

7

Finalise activity + Read more ...

To finalise the activity:

  • record the details on a DOC
  • finalise activity via Assessment Results (AR) screen, and
  • tell carer of the outcome

8

Issue manual letter if required + Read more ...

Check if the rejection reason requires a manual letter. A manual letter will be required if a Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity has been created on the AL screen after the rejection activity is finalised.

Note: the MFU may not generate straight away. Sometimes it generates overnight.

Check AL screen.

Is there an MFU to send a manual letter to the carer?

  • Yes:
    • 'S'elect the MFU activity from the AL screen
    • in Nxt, key 'LD', press [Enter]
    • in the Letter Code field, key the letter number 'Q134', press [Enter]
    • complete relevant details, giving reasons for the rejection
    • tell the carer of the steps in the review of decision process
  • No, the carer will get an auto-letter. Procedure ends here
