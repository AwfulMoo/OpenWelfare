Item numbers

Table 1

Item no.

Description and restrictions

UP01

LMO - Initial Incentive and Program Enrolment with a Practice Nurse

GP initial payment with Practice Nurse:

  • is an incentive item that can be claimed on the first day the veteran enters the program
  • can be claimed only once per veteran, regardless of a change in provider or practice nurse, or where a participant ceases to be a participant and later re-enters the program
  • the Date of Service is the date the participant enters the program
  • is only payable once on initial admission to the Coordinated Veterans' Care Program
  • a previous payment for a customer of UP01 or UP02 (to any provider in respect of a patient) will cause a claim to reject

UP02

LMO - Initial Incentive without Practice Nurse Coordinator

GP initial payment without practice nurse:

  • is an incentive item that can be claimed on the first day the veteran enters the program
  • can be claimed only once per veteran, regardless of a change in provider or practice nurse, or where a participant ceases to be a participant and later re-enters the program
  • the Date of Service is the date the participant enters the program
  • can only be paid once ever
  • cannot be paid if a UP01 has been paid

UP03

LMO - Completion of 90 day Period of Care – Review of Care Plan and Eligibility with a practice nurse

GP quarterly care payment with practice nurse:

  • can only be paid if item UP01 or UP02 has previously been paid
  • UP03 is for a 3 month period of care, and can only be paid once every 3 months
  • the Date of Service for a UP03 is the date that the period of care commenced, but it can only be claimed after that 3 month care period has finished. A UP03 will be auto-rejected RSN 759 if it is claimed before the last day of the care period
  • If UP04 has been paid, a UP03 can only be paid after the 3 month care period of the UP04 has finished
  • cannot be paid within 28 days of a UP05 or UP06

UP04

LMO - Completion of 90 day Period of Care – Review of Care Plan and Eligibility without a practice nurse

GP quarterly care payment without Practice Nurse:

  • can only be paid if item UP01 or UP02 has previously been paid
  • UP04 is for a 3 month period of care, and can only be paid once every 3 months
  • the Date of Service for a UP04 is the date that the period of care commenced, but it can only be claimed after that 3 month care period has finished. A UP04 will be auto-rejected RSN 759 if it is claimed before the last day of the care period
  • if UP03 has been paid, a UP04 can only be paid after the 3 month care period of the UP03 has finished

UP05

CVC Initial Care Coordination

Community nursing initial payment:

  • Is only claimed by a DVA contracted Community Nursing provider.
  • Is only paid once for each eligible person irrespective of whether it is paid to the same or different providers
  • Cannot be claimed if a claim has been made for Item UP01 (to any other provider in respect of a patient)
  • This item cannot be paid unless a claim for UP02 has been paid
  • The date of service can be up to 90 days after the date of service for a previous claim for UP02 (to any provider in respect of a patient)

UP06

Community Nursing Subsequent Care

Community nursing subsequent care (28 day period) payment:

  • Is only claimed by a DVA-contracted Community Nursing provider
  • A claim for the Initial Assessment and Program enrolment either Item UP01 or Item UP02 must have been made (to any provider in respect of a patient)
  • The date of service for this item must be at least 90 days after the date of service of any previous claim for UP03 (to any provider in respect of a patient)
  • The date of service for this item must not be more than 180 days after the date of service of the last claim for any of UP01, UP02, UP03, or UP04. If there has been no prior claim for any of those items with a date of service 180 days or less from the date of service of a claim for this item, the claim will be rejected
  • Only 13 of these claims can be made in any 12 month period

UP10

Coordinated Veterans' Care (CVC) Prior Approval Item (Community Nursing)

  • This item may only be paid if DVA prior approval has been granted
  • Can only be paid if a UP 01 or UP02 has been paid. Will cover circumstances where there is a change from practice nurse to community nurse, and community nurse to community nurse. Does not cover change in LMO or change from practice nurse to practice nurse
  • Amount of payment variable and determined by DVA
  • Maximum payment amounts will apply

UP11

Coordinated Veterans' Care (CVC) Prior Approval Item (GP/LMO)

  • This item may only be paid if DVA prior approval has been granted
  • Can only be paid if a UP01 or UP02 has been paid. Will cover circumstances where there is a change from community nurse to practice nurse or from an LMO to another LMO with a different practice nurse
  • Amount of payment variable and determined by DVA
  • Maximum payment amounts will apply

Frequently asked questions about CVC

Table 3

Item

Question and Answer

1

Question: What is the CVC Program?

Answer: The Coordinated Veterans' Care Program (CVC) is a health program designed to reduce unplanned hospitalisation. It is available to eligible Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) gold card holders who have chronic disease and complex care needs. From 1 July 2021, White Card holders with one or more accepted mental health condition/s are eligible to participate.

Provider enquiries about the Coordinated Veterans' Care Program, including patient eligibility, should be directed to the CVC Helpline (External contacts - VAP).

2

Question: Who provides the services in the CVC Program?

Answer: General Practitioners (GPs), along with Practice Nurses or DVA Contracted Community Nurses, work closely with participants to manage their health conditions, and to plan and coordinate their ongoing care.

3

Question: How long can a participant remain in the program?

Answer: It is expected that most participants who are enrolled in the CVC program will stay on the program indefinitely.

4

Question: What if the participant passes away during the program?

Answer: If a participant passes away partway through a period of care, the provider is entitled to claim the full amount for the quarterly care payment.

5

Question: Can a participant transfer care during the program?

Answer: Although a participant in the program can change providers there are rules that apply to the transfer of care of the patient:

  • GP to GP - Where a participant changes GPs after starting the program, the new GP:
    • cannot claim the initial payment (UP01 or UP02)
    • can only claim for a quarterly care payment (UP03 or UP04) where the quarter commenced after the expiry of the previous GP’s quarterly care period
  • Practice Nurse to Community Nurse or Community Nurse to Practice Nurse:
    • a change from a Practice Nurse to a Community Nurse, or vice versa, can occur after discussions with the GP

6

Question: Can UP items be claimed at the same time as consultations, care plan items, health assessments or other MBS items?

Answer: Yes. The UP item benefits are paid in addition to all existing MBS items such as consultations or chronic disease management items. Care provided under the CVC Program is not a replacement of the continued interaction between the GP and the patient.

7

Question: Is a VAP incentive item payable in association with UP items?

Answer: No. VAP incentive items are not payable for any of the new CVC UP items. However, VAP incentive items will continue to be paid in association with LMO items that are claimed by a GP under the existing rules.

8

Question: Is the provider required to indicate the accepted mental health condition when submitting claims for a CVC item?

Answer: No. The claim can continue to be assessed based the conditions listed on the veterans file. If ‘all mental health conditions’ ‘treatment only’ or ‘provisional’ is noted on the clients file or next to the mental health condition this relates to treatment covered under NHLC and not CVC.

Note: Refer to the list of accepted mental health conditions under the CVC programme in section Eligibility to receive CVC services.

Frequently asked questions about CVC Quarterly Care Payments

Table 4

Item

Question and Answer

1

Question: What date of service should be used when claiming item CVC Quarterly Care payments UP03?

Answer: The first day of the period of care must be used as the date of service for item UP03.

2

Question: Why was item UP03 rejected with RSN 759?

Answer: UP03 must be submitted at the end of the quarterly care period. If the claim is lodged before the end of this period, the item will be automatically rejected with RSN 759 'Item cannot be claimed until the last day of period of care'.

3

Question: Do I need to claim health assessment or a care plan item/s prior to commencing the CVC program?

Answer: No, an MBS health assessment or a care plan item/s is not required to be present on the veteran’s history prior to claiming for CVC items. MBS and CVC items are not restrictive.

4

Question: What is the procedure if I have incorrectly claimed CVC items UP01 and UP03 (with Practice Nurse) instead of items UP02 and UP04 (without Practice Nurse) respectively?

Answer: If an overpayment has occurred, you are required to refund any overpaid benefit. The veteran's history will be adjusted to display the correct item/s, allowing correct processing of future claims.

5

Question: Why was item UP06 - Community Nursing Subsequent Care rejected with reason code 160 'Maximum number of services for this item already paid'?

Answer: UP06 cannot be paid within 3 months of both items UP01 GP Initial payment with Practice Nurse or UP03 GP Quarterly Care payment with Practice Nurse. In addition, only 1 item UP06 is payable every 28 days.

6

Question: Can a GP/LMO provider claim a standard consultation item for each consult with a patient in relation to the CVC program?

Answer: The UP item benefits are paid in addition to all existing MBS items such as consultations.
