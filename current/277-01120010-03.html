Notification that a child support payee is deceased

Table 1: this table describes the initial and immediate steps when notification of a payee's death is received.

Step

Action

1

Notification of death + Read more ...

If the customer is Indigenous, obtain permission before mentioning the deceased by name. Check the indicators on the Customer window for an Indigenous indicator.

Deceased payees are managed within the Deceased Customer Management (DCM) team. If advised of a payee's death, warm transfer the call to the DCM team using Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone.

Child Support can be notified of a customer's death by:

  • Centrelink, go to Step 2
  • the payer or a third party, go to Step 3

2

Centrelink notification + Read more ...

When Centrelink are informed of a mutual customer's death the information is automatically uploaded to the Deceased Client window and an intray is generated.

Note: Centrelink notification alone is not sufficient proof of a customer's death, DCM Service Officers must obtain additional proof. Go to Step 4.

3

Notification from payer or third party + Read more ...

When a payer or third party notifies of a payee's death, obtain the following information:

  • name and contact details of the caller, if a third party
  • sufficient detail to identify the deceased, such as name, address and date of birth
  • date of death
  • country/state/town the death occurred in
  • if the payee had a will
  • name and contact details of the Executor or person who will be finalising the affairs of the deceased
  • the name of the funeral director
  • where the children are residing, and
  • if the caller does not know these details, try to obtain contact details of another person who may

Note: if the caller is a third party do not disclose any information about either parent or the case to the caller. Accept and record any information they provide.

If the caller is upset, check if there is someone available that the caller would prefer Child Support to speak with or if they would like to continue the call at a later date.

If the caller wishes to continue the call at a later date, document the conversation on the Deceased Client window and offer to transfer them to the Family Relationship Advice Line or Social worker and other service referrals for child support customers. For more information, see Products and Services Cuba Process Help. Go to Step 4.

When speaking to a person who now has care of the children, advise them they can lodge an application for child support.

If the caller is the payer and wishes to continue the call, explain:

  • once the death has been confirmed, the case will be ended from the day before the date of death
  • any child support debt or overpayment on the case and if the amount is likely to change
  • options available for addressing the child support debt or overpayment. See Step 5 in Table 2

Support services are available and, if appropriate, offer to transfer them to the Family Relationship Advice Line or Social worker and other service referrals for child support customers. See Products and Services Cuba process help.

4

Immediate action required + Read more ...

When notification of death is received the follow these steps for all cases until proof of death is received and the case is finalised:

  • lock the deceased customer and any associated customers
  • record the death in the Deceased Client window. See Deceased customer management Cuba Process Help for more information
  • do not review or reconcile any estimates. If a review or reconciliation is underway suspend activity until a case management strategy is decided
  • add a Sensitive Issue Indicator to the deceased customer and all associated customers, see Table 2 in Sensitive issue management Cuba Process Help
  • if the payee owes has an overpayment or consolidated revenue debt add a Special Recovery Condition
  • if notification of death is received via Centrelink exchange of information or through the business (for a customer who uses Centrelink services) and more information is required, check Customer First for the required information
  • suspend payee overpayment summaries in the Payment Options window

See the Deceased Customer Management Key Performance Indicator Checklist located on the Resources page of the Deceased Child Support Customer Management procedure.

Is the payee involved in an International case (identified by an 'INT' indicator on either of the parent's customer screens)?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No. If the case is:
    • private collect with no arrears, procedure ends here
    • a single collect case (or private collect with arrears), go to Step 6

5

International case + Read more ...

Go to the Case screen to see what type of application the case is based on.

If the case type is ASSESSMENT, action the deceased notification in the usual way. Go to Step 6.

If the case type is INTERL CO/AGR or INTERL ASSMNT, contact the Central Authority Team:

  • create a STAKEHOLDER FOLLOW UP intray
  • reset the intray due date to 2 business days from today's date (instead of 14 days)
  • add the Service Officer's contact details to the intray notepad with the reason for contacting the Central Authority Team, for example DCM International case – INTERL CO/AGR or INTERL ASSMENT), and
  • route to MC S003 Cent Auth Tm1-none

A Central Authority Service Officer will work collaboratively with the Child Support Service Officer and undertake any necessary liaison with the Overseas Authority. For example, the Overseas Authority may assist Child Support in establishing proof of death and if there is a will.

The Overseas Authority must be consulted on whether collection of any arrears is required and the date the case should be ended.

The DCM officer will be kept informed and all actions will be recorded on the customer’s Cuba record.

Procedure ends here.

6

A single case - Collect, or Private Collect with arrears + Read more ...

If the case is a single case - Collect, or Private Collect with arrears:

  • suspend payer statements, in the Payment Options window
  • place all payments on hold
  • suspend all collection action (stopping any EW and Centrelink deductions), and
  • suspend the accrual of late payment penalties, see Table 3 in Penalties Cuba Process Help, and
  • record a Special Recovery Condition on the payer, to:
    • generate a TRIP exception, should their tax refund be intercepted, and
    • prevent automatic Centrelink deductions. See Tax Refund Intercept Process (TRIP) exceptions Cuba Process Help

7

Multi case - Collect or Private Collect with arrears + Read more ...

If the case is a Multi case - Collect or Private Collect with arrears, place a hold on the case with the deceased payee.

8

Obtain proof of death + Read more ...

Proof of death must be obtained before processing the terminating event. The following can be used as proof of death:

  • External Searches Guide - search by the state the customer died in
  • funeral notice from a newspaper - citing the customer's name, date of birth, date of death or other identifying detail
  • confirmation from the Public Trustee, Coroner's office, hospital or Police
  • death certificate from the Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM), or
  • confirmation from the Department of Foreign Affairs, if the person died overseas

Note: due to the cost involved, obtaining a death certificate from BDM should only be pursued if no other proof can be obtained. BDM take a minimum of 6 weeks (12 weeks where there is a Coroner's enquiry) to produce a death certificate.

Proof of death should generally be in writing. If unable to obtain written evidence, or unable to obtain any proof of death but believe the customer is deceased, escalate the query, via a SSO, to a Program Support Manager to assist in the decision making process. See Technical support in Child Support.

In the event an Indigenous customer passed away in a remote location, the community manager or elder may be able to assist. Contact the local Indigenous Relations Officer who will draw on their networks to progress the matter.

Centrelink notification alone is not sufficient to process a customer's death. Additional proof must be obtained.

Once the proof of death has been obtained, see Table 2.

Step

Action

1

Process death + Read more ...

Once proof of death has been obtained process the death in the Deceased Client window. See Deceased customer management Cuba process help.

Before running Eligibility:

  • update care records, if the payer now has care of the children change the care to reflect this
  • if there is an A18 application, change the last day of secondary education to the date/day before the date of death
  • correct incomes - check if there are any estimates or provisional incomes and correct if possible. See Income Processing for Child Support customers, Child support estimates review (post 1 July 2010) or Child support estimates - Reconcile (from 1 July 2011)
  • suspend any future LPP (if this is the payer's only child support case. See Penalties Cuba Process Help
  • if an executor has been identified, ensure they have been added as the customer representative, and
  • check if a Security Incident Report is appropriate - a security incident report should be completed when the death of the customer was due to homicide or suicide. The completed security report should be sent to the team leader

Once these actions are complete, run Eligibility.

If Cuba is making unexpected changes, do not save eligibility, see Eligibility window help. If unable to correct the Eligibility changes, seek technical support from a Service Support Officer (SSO).

2

Customer contact + Read more ...

Each call must be approached in a sensitive and considerate manner. Each customer contact will differ so gauge the situation at the time of the call. If unsure how to proceed, speak to a Team Leader or Service Support Officer (SSO).

Before contacting the payer, determine if there has been:

  • previous contact regarding the death, or
  • no previous contact regarding the death

3

Previous contact + Read more ...

If Child Support has already spoken to the payer about the death, confirm:

  • the case has ended and the date it ended from
  • letters will be issued confirming the case has been ended, and
  • any amounts owing and the options available for addressing any child support debt or overpayment. For available options, go to Step 5

Once the conversation has been documented, go to Step 5.

4

No previous contact + Read more ...

The payer may not be aware of the payee's death, approach the conversation with extreme caution.

If Child Support has not had any contact with the payer since the payee's death allow a reasonable amount of time (14 days from date of death) before contacting the payer. Before contacting the payer check previous communications to see if there are any records indicating that the customers were in contact with each other. Decide if the payer is likely to be aware of the payee's death.

Contact the payer and advise that Child Support has been notified of the death of the payee and that their child support case has ended.

If the payer is upset, check if there is someone available the payer would like Child Support to speak with or whether they would prefer to continue the call at a later date. If the payer wishes to continue the call at a later date document the conversation in the Deceased Client window and offer to transfer them to the Family Relationship Advice Line or Social worker and other service referrals for child support customers. See Products and services Cuba process help. Once contact has been documented, go to Step 5.

If the payer wishes to continue the call, provide this advice:

  • the case has ended, from the day before the date of death
  • any amounts owing and the options available for addressing any child support debt or overpayment. For available options go to Step 5
  • the support service available and, if appropriate, offer to transfer them to the Family Relationship Advice Line or Social worker and other service referrals for child support customers. See also Products and services Cuba process help

5

End case + Read more ...

Before ending the case and issuing letters, consider if the case is:

  • Private Collect (no arrears), go to Step 6
  • Collect or Private Collect with arrears, go to Step 7

6

Private Collect (no arrears) + Read more ...

If the case is private collect and there are no arrears or other outstanding issues, such as consolidated revenue debt, excess cash or overpayments use the DCM letters macro to issue a letter to the payer advising that the case has been finalised. In this circumstance, there is no need to locate or contact the Executor for the payee's estate.

If the case is Private Collect but has a consolidated revenue debt, excess cash or overpayment go to Collect or Private Collect with arrears below.

7

Collect or Private Collect with arrears + Read more ...

If there are any arrears, or other outstanding issues, check the notes in the Deceased Client window to determine if the payee had a will and if they are:

  • testate - died with a valid will, see Table 3
  • intestate - died without a valid will, see Table 4

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Collection options for testate payees + Read more ...

Use the DCM letters macro to issue a letter to the payer advising that the case has been ended and detailing any outstanding issues.

The payee's Will should name an executor.

The Executor must supply a copy of the Will, or letters from the court naming them as the Executor or Administrator before any information can be released to them. Child Support does not require Executors to produce a grant of probate. Once the Executor has supplied proof of Executorship record them as the payee's representative.

Once the Executor has been recorded as a representative, check if the case has any of the following issues:

  • child support owed to payee, go to Step 2
  • overpayment, go to Step 3
  • consolidated revenue debt, go to Step 7
  • returned disbursements, go to Step 8

For these options to be pursued with an executor, Child Support must have received proof of Executorship and recorded the Executor in the Customer Representative window.

If an executor has been identified use the DCM letters macro to issue a letter to the Executor advising that the case has been ended and detail any outstanding issues.

2

Child support owed to payee + Read more ...

Contact the Executor and ask if they want the arrears collected or if they would like to opt out and discharge arrears.

If the Executor requests collection, or chooses not to discharge the arrears Child Support must pursue the arrears. Disburse any monies collected to the Executor via a bank account, supplied by the Executor, or a cheque. When collecting arrears, follow the process in Debt repayment.

If the children are now in the care of the payer contact the payer and explain the Waiver process and how they can apply.

Note: to disburse to the Executor, remove any holds previously placed on the case and any special recovery conditions created to generate a TRIP exception.

Before commencing collection action, determine the care arrangements for the child/ren.

3

Overpayment + Read more ...

Where there is an overpayment to the payee check if the overpayment can be recovered.

If the overpayment is not a debt to the Commonwealth (not a recoverable overpayment) follow the process in Child support overpayments and other payee debt.

If the overpayment is recoverable, the payer will have 2 options to ask Child Support:

  • not to recover the overpayment, or
  • pursue collection from payee’s estate

Contact the payer and ask which action they wish to take. If the payer asks Child Support not to recover the overpayment:

  • the payee (or their estate) does not have to repay the monies overpaid
  • the payer will no longer be entitled to the amount, and
  • after a cooling off period, the decision to ask Child Support not to collect the overpayment cannot be reversed. The overpayment cannot be reclaimed later

Does the payer want the overpayment to be collected:

  • Yes:
    • ensure a special recovery condition is recorded on the parent with an end date of 31/12/4000
    • in the notepad, document details of the overpayment and date of death
    • go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Payer decision - overpayment not collected by Child Support + Read more ...

If the payer has requested that Child Support not collect the overpayment, see Table 2. Managing a payer decision in Information for payer’s about a payee overpayment.

After the cooling off period has expired, has the payer contacted Child Support within the required timeframe to change their decision?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No:
  • contact the payee's Executor
  • advise that the amount is no longer owed
  • create unique letters to both the Executor and payer advising that the overpayment has been waived and that the case has been finalised. See Overpayments Cuba Process Help

5

Payer decision - overpayment collected by Child Support + Read more ...

Upon death, a person's assets cease to belong to them. Any assets are held in trust as part of their estate, which is administered by an executor.

The payee must have an Executor or Administrator to collect any monies. If there is no executor or administrator, see Table 4.

If the overpayment is recoverable and the payer requests collection:

  • advise that Child Support will attempt to recover the overpayment from the payee's estate and will disburse monies if they are collected
  • obtain the payer’s bank details to allow future disbursement

Contact the Executor and explain:

  • how the overpayment occurred
  • that the payer has requested collection
  • the option of applying for a waiver of the overpayment

The usual administrative enforcement options (such as TRIPs and s72 notices) only apply to funds held for, or on behalf of, a child support debtor. Because the assets no longer belong to a child support debtor (the payee in this instance) normal enforcement options must not be used post death. Child Support cannot collect from bank accounts, superannuation, and life insurance policies etc. because the funds/assets are not being held in the payee's name. Similarly, notices cannot be issued against an executor or estate because they are not a child support debtor.

6

Contact the Executor of the payee's estate + Read more ...

Contact the Executor

Call the Executor and, if Child Support has not spoken to them already, advise:

  • that there is an overpayment or consolidated revenue debt that needs to be paid out of the estate
  • they are required to lodge any outstanding tax returns for the payee, which may change the debt
  • try to gauge an expected timeframe for payment, and
  • advise they can apply for a waiver on the payee's behalf

Issue Proof of Debt notice

Once the Executor has been spoken to, issue a Proof of Debt notice and use the DCM letters macro to create a letter detailing the amount owed and any outstanding tax returns.

Create intray and monitor for payment

Create an intray and monitor for payment over a 60 day period. If payment is received use the DCM letters macro to create a letter for the Payer and Executor advising that the arrears have been paid and that the case has been finalised. See Letters Cuba Process Help, Create a unique letter.

If no payment is received, contact the Executor again and ask if any funds are likely to be available. If funds are likely to become available create a subsequent intray and continue to monitor for payment.

If funds are not likely to become available:

  • ensure the Executor is aware of the option to apply for a Waiver, and
  • recontact the payer to explain the situation and discuss the possibility of gifting

If unable to collect the debt address the overpayment, using 1 of the above options, prior to finalising and unlocking the case.

Note: estates can take a long time to be finalised, sometimes several years.

Document all calls, decisions made and ensure appropriate letters are issued, see Documenting Child Support information.

Once monies are received from the Estate they should be disbursed to the payer.

7

Consolidated revenue debt (testate payee) + Read more ...

If the payee has a consolidated revenue debt follow the process detailed in Step 5 and Step 6.

The Executor, or Child Support on behalf of the Executor, can apply for a Waiver of the consolidated revenue debt.

8

Returned disbursement + Read more ...

Check if the payee is still entitled to receive the funds, that is, that there is no overpayment on the case. If there is:

  • an overpayment, refund the returned disbursement (or part thereof) to the payer. See Excess cash on a child support customer's account
  • no overpayment, disburse the funds to the Executor, via a bank account supplied by the Executor or by cheque

Table 4

Step

1

Collection options for intestate payees + Read more ...

If the payee is intestate, there are limited avenues available to finalise any outstanding child support issues as there is no one who can legally make decisions on their behalf.

If no executor details have been provided, attempt to locate an executor before pursuing the options for an intestate payee.

To locate an executor:

  • contact the payee's next of kin, family or the payer and ask if they are aware of an executor
  • contact Centrelink to find out who reported the death and if they know who the executor is, and
  • contact the Public Trustee in the state where the deceased resided, (unless the deceased resided in Western Australia or Tasmania, where a fee is charged)

If an executor is located from the above searches, go to Table 3.

If an executor is not located, consider if there are any possible issues on the case including if child support is owed to payee. When an intestate payee has child support owed to them, the options are:

  • disbursement, go to Step 2
  • waiver, go to Step 3
  • non-pursuit, go to Step 6
  • letters of administration, go to Step 4
  • Public trustee, go to Step 5

If the payee has:

  • an overpayment, go to Step 7
  • a consolidated revenue debt, go to Step 9

2

Disbursement + Read more ...

If the payee is intestate and there are funds to be disbursed to the payee consider if it is:

  • an ongoing disbursement, or
  • a returned disbursement

Ongoing disbursement

If the customer is intestate, check with the person finalising their affairs if the payee's bank account remains open. If the bank account is open, remove the holds and continue to disburse.

If the payee's bank account has been closed, Child Support has no avenue of disbursement. Suspend collection and explore other options such as Waiver and Non-pursuit.

Returned disbursement

If a disbursement previously issued to the payee has been returned, check if the payee is still entitled to receive the funds. If the payee has an overpayment, refund the returned disbursement, or part thereof, to the payer. See Excess cash on a child support customer's account.

If the payee does not have an overpayment, check with the person finalising the payee's affairs if the payee's bank account remains open. If the bank account remains open remove the holds and re-disburse the funds.

If the payee's bank account has been closed, determine if the funds were paid before the payee's death. Any funds that were paid prior to the payee's death may be paid out to a person the Finance Minister considers appropriate, see section 74, Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013. If appropriate, escalate the matter to Program Advice.

If the funds were paid after the payee’s death or their bank account has been closed, Child Support has no avenue of disbursement and the funds will have to remain as 'undisbursed', unless letters of administration are obtained.

3

Waiver + Read more ...

If Child Support has not already done so, contact the payer and explain the Waiver process.

If the payee is intestate and all of the following criteria are met, refer the debt for a bulk waiver:

  • no letters of administration had been granted
  • the children would not benefit from any funds collected (no avenue for disbursement)
  • the children were either over the age of 18 or being financial supported by the paying parent, and
  • the debt amount owed is under $20,000

If the case meets the above criteria, follow the process detailed in Waivers for Child Support customers and recorded in the Bulk Waiver spreadsheet.

If any of the above do not apply and the payer is not going to apply for a waiver, consider non-pursuit of the debt. Go to Step 6.

4

Letters of Administration + Read more ...

The payee's family, or other party, can apply for Letters of Administration to administer the payee's estate. Letters of Administration:

  • are dealt with in the Supreme courts. Costs and processes may vary slightly in each state
  • are costly. The high cost associated with application fees may be greater than the value of the payee's estate, meaning it may not be financially worthwhile applying
  • may be applied for by a family member or other party. In this scenario, create an intray and monitor over a 60 day period. If Letters of Administration are:
    • received within 60 days, treat the Administrator in the same manner as an Executor and go to Table 3
    • not received within 60 days, contact the person who was considering applying and check if that is still their intention

If no one is going to apply for Letters of Administration discuss applying for a Waiver with the payer.

Note: administrators perform a similar role to that of an executor but distribute the assets of the deceased according to intestacy laws.

5

Public Trustee + Read more ...

If the payee has significant assets, their family may be able to approach the Public Trustee to administer the estate. The requirements of the Public Trustee will vary from state to state, however generally the assets must be reasonably significant and the Public Trustee will charge a percentage of the estate for their services.

If the estate is taken on by the Public Trustee treat them in the same manner as an executor and follow the process detailed in Table 3. Note: a deceased customer's estate cannot be referred to the public trustee.

Indigenous estates are handled differently to non-Indigenous estates. If the customer is Indigenous contact the relevant state's Public Trustee to determine their requirements for accepting a referral to administer the estate.

If the payee's estate is not going to be referred to the Public Trustee, ensure Child Support has discussed applying for a Waiver with the payer. Service Officers must address the arrears before unlocking the customers.

6

Non-pursuit + Read more ...

Only consider non-pursuit if the debt cannot be addressed.

7

Overpayment + Read more ...

If the payee has an overpayment, check if it is recoverable. If the overpayment is not a debt due to the Commonwealth (not a recoverable overpayment), follow the process in Child support overpayments and payee debt.

If the overpayment is recoverable contact the payer and ask if they would like to waive the overpayment, clearly explaining that:

  • this decision means the payee (or their estate) does not have to repay the monies overpaid
  • the payer will no longer be entitled to the amount, and
  • the decision to waive the overpayment cannot be reversed (the overpayment cannot be reclaimed later)

If the payer requests collection of the overpayment, go to Step 8.

Remove overpayment

If the payer confirms they do not want the overpayment collected, create a unique letter to the payer advising that the overpayment has been removed from Child Support records and the case has been finalised.

8

Collection + Read more ...

If the payee is intestate, collection of the overpayment is unlikely and give realistic expectations to the payer.

Advise the payer that there are no options for collection, unless the payee's family apply for Letters of Administration or refer the estate to the Public Trustee and that Child Support will not be pursing the debt. See Non-pursuit of individual child support debts.

Remove the overpayment from Cuba using the ‘Gift’ functionality. This action can be reversed should letters of Administration be granted and Child Support is made aware.

9

Consolidated revenue debt + Read more ...

If the consolidated revenue debt meets the following criteria:

  • the payee is intestate
  • no letters of administration have been granted
  • the children would not benefit from any funds collected (no avenue of disbursement)
  • the children were over the age of 18 or being financially supported by the payer at the time of the payee's death, and
  • the debt falls under $20,000
  • the debt can be referred for bulk waiver

Consider Non-pursuit of individual child support debts if:

  • there is no executor or person handling the payee's estate
  • none of the above applies
  • there are no options for collection (as the payee's family will not apply for Letters of Administration or refer the estate to the Public Trustee)

1

Unlocking the case + Read more ...

Before unlocking the case:

  • address the debt
  • address/remit any penalties, and

See the Deceased Customer Management Key Performance Indicator Checklist located on the Resources page.
