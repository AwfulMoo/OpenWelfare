3

Determine if customer has been targeted for FTB Income review + Read more ...

In Customer first, navigate to the Service Strategy PQSS screen and check if customer has been targeted for an FTB Income Review.

On the PQSS screen, if a customer has been targeted for FTB Income Review, this will display as:

  • FTB ATO Data Discrepancy >= $1000 (697)

Note: 696 Reviews (FTB ATO Data Discrepancy <= $1000) have been removed from allocation. However if the PQSS screen displays:

  • only a 696 review, send an email to Business Process Branch team including the CRN
  • a 696 and a 697 review:
    • advise of the 696 review via email to the Business Process Branch team including the CRN, and
    • action the 697 review

Customers can have numerous review types listed on the PQSS screen. Staff will need to determine if the customer has been targeted for FTB Income Reviews by searching the PQSS screen for either of the above review types with a status of Not Started (NST) or Started (STA).

Where a customer does not have any current reviews outstanding, the PQSS screen will display warning of ‘1002PQ - There are no Current Service Components for this customer’.

If there is no FTB Income Review present on the record, ask the customer the reason for their call.

If the customer is calling:

  • because they received a letter from the Australian Taxation Office regarding an adjustment to their income due to Department of Veteran’s’ Affairs superannuation payment, handoff via email to the Business Process Branch team. Include the following details:
    Subject Line: DVA Customer Contact
    Body of email:
    DVA customer contacted FTB income review phone line, please follow up.
    Customer CRN:
    Preferred contact number:
  • for any other reason, advise the customer to contact the relevant Main Business Line

If a FTB Income Review is present on the record, go to Step 4.

4

Commence review + Read more ...

Staff will commence review processing by opening the ‘FAO Review Platform’ tool and selecting ‘Process Reviews’ to be allocated a Customer Reference Number (CRN).

Navigate to the Service Strategy (PQSS) screen to confirm the review is displaying.

Change the Status field to STA on the PQSS screen. Finalise and complete this activity.

Note: if there is a started claim or activity on the customer’s record preventing review commencement, email the details to the Workload Management Team.

Has the customer and/or their partner claimed an Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP) or been identified as living in an affected area?

  • Yes, refer to the Emergency Event on the FTB Income Review SharePoint page (this page is accessible by Income Review staff only)
  • No, go to Step 6

Has the customer been affected by a bereavement in the last 28 days?

  • Yes, navigate to the Service Strategy (PQSS) screen and set service action 689 ‘Customer unable to be contacted’ to Competed (COM) in the STS: field to complete the review. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Disputing supplied data + Read more ...

Australian Taxation Office (ATO) pre-fill data is only one component of the FTB Income Review. If the customer disputes the information provided by the ATO, make a referral to the ATO Liaison officer for follow up.

If the customer is contacting as a result of:

  • FTB being cancelled
  • a Q999 being sent, or
  • re-contacting to provide further information

See Step 4 in the Finalising the process table.

6

Comparing pre filled information + Read more ...

Go to the FAO ATO Income Summary (FAIS) screen and the income summary screens for other income such as investments and self-employment income (PGNIA, NIA, PIAS).

Select customer and/or partner from the list and navigate to the FAO ATO Income Details (FAID) screen to compare the customer provided income with the ATO pre-fill information. Additional information will be recorded on the 'ATO Pre-fill Update' DOC.

The ATO pre-fill information includes Payment Summary (PAYG) and Annual Investment Income (AIIR) only. If the customer has received any income support, this will display separately on the Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen and should be added to the pre-fill information for comparison.

Go to Step 7.

7

Comparing pre filled information + Read more ...

Employment and all other income earned total

Total the amount of the employment income and all other income earned. Check for other income, for example, income from investments (SVS, SIS, IMPS and REBS screens). Combine this with the taxable income recorded on the TXGS screen.

Code this amount into the taxable income field on the FAO Income for Previous Year (FIPY) screen. If a tax free pension is recorded on the TXGS screen, this must also be included in the tax free pensions and benefits field on the FIPY screen.

Note: for customers/partners with joint accounts, the interest displayed is 50% of the total interest earned.

Proceed to the Activity Results (AR) screen, and then navigate back to the FAO Reconciliation and Claim Summary (FRCS) screen to see if there is any change to the customer's reconciliation result. Do not complete the activity.

Does the activity show:

  • a negative or positive reconciliation adjustment of $50 or more, or
  • a substantial difference in the incomes that indicates the customer is not estimating correctly, or
  • the customer should be lodging a tax return?
    • Yes, go to Step 8
    • No, go to Step 9

8

Determining correct income threshold where a person may be required to lodge a tax return + Read more ...

Some customers can have a higher income threshold of $20,542, if they meet the following conditions:

  • in receipt of an income support payment (ISP) for at least 300 days (non-lodgement reason of 'GPA')
  • total taxable income below $20,542 - this includes both ISP and earnings
  • no tax paid
  • no business or investment income
  • no personal or employer reportable superannuation contributions
  • no reportable fringe benefits
  • no foreign income or tax exempt foreign income, and
  • were not a liable parent or customer parent for child support purposes, or
  • if they were a liable parent or customer parent for child support purposes, they received ISP for 365 days

Does the customer and/or partner fit into this category?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, see Step 1 in the Finalising the process table

  • Complete the relevant service action by entering 'COM' in the STS: field for the following:

  • For all other service actions, enter the code 'NAT' (No Action Taken) in the STS: field

Determine if electronic message is required + Read more ...

Prior to any outbound phone call attempt, send the 'Pre-Call' SMS notifying that Centrelink will be calling from a blocked number. This Desk 'Pre-Call' service is automated using the 'Desktop Messaging' workflow - Customer First > Workspace > Desktop Messaging.

4

Customer discussion + Read more ...

Advise the customer the reason for the contact.

Suggested script

'You (and/or your partner) advised the agency that you were not required to lodge a tax return and at that time provided details of your income for the 2019-2020 financial year. This income was used to determine your entitlement to Family Tax Benefit.

Part of the process of the agency accepting your declaration is that we may review data held by the Australian Taxation Office such as payment summaries lodged by employers against the information you have provided to us.

You have been selected for a review as there was a difference between the income you provided and information provided to us by the Australian Taxation Office. We will review the information provided by the Australian Taxation Office to see if any changes or updates are required and I will then advise you if there is anything else you need to do.'

Does the customer have all the information required to discuss review at time of contact (For example, income information, payment summaries, etc.) or agree to the information from the ATO being used, or contacting as a result of their FTB being cancelled?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No:
    • bring the assessment activity back to registered by selecting Cancel an activity then Cancel Provisional Data and Mark as Registered
    • add the keyword IREV, and
    • place the review on hold for 28 days (for remote/indigenous customer, hold until 31 May 2021)
    • Advise the customer to contact within 14 days or their FTB will be cancelled issue Q999 via the ‘FAO Review Platform’ tool
    • go to Step 5

5

Issue Q999 + Read more ...

Further actions

  • Hold the REG activity for 28 days on the AL screen using keyword IREV (for remote/indigenous customers, hold until 31 May 2021)

If the customer does not contact as a result of the Q999, go to Step 8

6

Topics for discussion + Read more ...

If FTB is CAN-FRC, it can be restored. See Restoration of FTB.

Note: the restoration activity must be completed prior to and separately to the review activity to prevent negative adjustments affecting the customer's arrears and ongoing rate of FTB.

Issues to discuss

Discuss relevant issues below using the information gathered from the FAO ATO Income Details (FAID) screen, the Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen and associated DOCs:

  • Interest earned
  • Capital gains
  • Deductions the customer might have if they were to lodge a return
  • Shares and investments
  • Gross earnings versus net earnings
  • Any other pensions or benefits
  • Other relevant discussion points
  • Does the customer have a valid reason for the difference?
  • Did the customer check with the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) before providing the advice?

See the discussion points on the Resources page.

From the discussion with the customer and/or partner, assist them to understand if they need to change their income declaration.

  • Where the customer agrees to change their income declaration and/or it is determined they must lodge a tax return, go to Step 9
  • If after discussion with the customer, the income and not required to lodge declaration appear reasonable, go to Step 9
  • Where the customer disagrees with ATO data, place the review on hold and email the ATO Liaison Officer to check the details, go to Step 7

    Customer's CRN: (enter CRN)

    Customer DOB:
    Customer TFN:
    Customer Address:

    ABN:
    Regards,'

If the information provided by the ATO Liaison Officer confirms the ATO details are correct, contact the customer to discuss. If the customer still disagrees with the information, advise them to contact the ATO directly to update details. The customer should be advised to visit the ATO website for more information. Do not make any changes to the FAO Income for Previous Year (FIPY) screen without the customer's consent.

  • For customer review, update review to 694 - Partial Customer Data Missing on ATO Rec
  • For partner review, update review to 705 - Partial Partner Data Missing on ATO Rec
  • For customer and partner review, update both the above outcomes
  • Record details on a DOC
  • Hold the DOC activity on the AL screen and change it to REG. Add keyword IREV so the review will come DUE if no response is received from the TAP team

Completing the Service Action + Read more ...

The service action must be completed before updating the FIPY or FAO Non-Lodger Exemption (FNLE) screens if required.

Navigate to the PQSS screen and complete the corresponding service action that reflects the outcome of the review by entering 'COM' in the STS: field. Set the remaining service actions to 'NAT' (No action taken). Note: if the review is for both the customer and the partner, a service action must be completed for both.

To complete customer service actions, go to Step 10.

To complete partner service actions, go to Step 11.

10

Completing Customer Service Actions + Read more ...

Customer details:

  • 687 - Customer income updated prior to review
    • DOC record: Previous year income updated prior to review - income discrepancy less than $1000. No further action required
    • Check if customer has updated income. If updated and variance for the customer is greater than or equal to $1,000, set service action to 'NAT', otherwise set service action to 'COM'. 'NAT' remaining customer service action. If discrepancy is as a result of missing ATO data for the customer, then 'COM' service action
  • 688 - Customer discrepancy of income rate <$50
    • The change in income result was no impact on payment. No further action required
    • Check customer's FAID screen data and confirm via provisional activity the variance
    • If potential variance for the customer is >= $50, set service action to 'NAT'
    • If variance for the customer is < $50, set service action to 'COM'
    • 'NAT' remaining customer service action
  • 689 - Customer unable to be contacted
    • If the customer was not contactable within 21 days, no further action required, 'COM' after 28 days
    • If the customer was contactable, then 'NAT' the service action
    • 'NAT' remaining customer service action
    • 'NAT' should also be used in exceptional circumstances such as when the customer is in prison or when the partner cannot be contacted
  • 690 - Customer income updated - NRTL revoked
    • DOC record: Income details received from ATO. Customer has confirmed income details advised by ATO. FIPY updated accordingly and FNLE coding has been deleted as it has been determined that the non-lodger declaration should not be accepted due to income above tax free threshold. Customer advised to lodge Income Tax Return with ATO by 30 June in accordance with FTB lodgement requirements. No further action required
    • If previous year income has been updated for the customer and NRTL is revoked, set this service action to 'COM' and remaining customer service action to 'NAT'
    • If this service action does not apply, set to 'NAT'
  • 691 - Customer income updated - No change to NRTL
    • DOC record: 'Income updated and no change to NRTL. Income details received from ATO. Customer has confirmed income details provided by ATO. FIPY updated accordingly. No further action required'
    • If previous year income has been updated for the customer and NRTL not revoked, set this service action to 'COM' and remaining customer service action to 'NAT'
    • If this service action does not apply, set to 'NAT'
  • 692 - Customer income not updated - NRTL revoked
    • DOC record: 'No change to income and NRTL revoked. Income details received from ATO. Customer has confirmed income details provided by ATO. FNLE coding has been deleted as it has been determined that the non-lodger declaration should not be accepted. Advised customer to lodge Income Tax Return with ATO by 30 June 2019 in accordance with FTB lodgement requirements. No further action required'
    • If previous year income has not been updated for the customer and NRTL is revoked, set this service action to 'COM' and remaining customer service action to 'NAT'
    • If this service action does not apply, set to 'NAT'
  • 693 - Customer income not updated - No change to NRTL
    • if previous year income has not been updated for the customer and NRTL is not revoked, set this service action to 'COM' and the remaining customer service action to 'NAT'
    • if this service action does not apply, set to 'NAT'
  • 694 - Partial customer data missing on ATO record
    • DOC record: 'ATO data did not contain all information required. TAP investigation determined (insert result). Customer advised to contact the ATO should they wish to dispute the information. No further action required'
    • If discrepancy is as a result of missing ATO data for the customer, then 'COM' service action
    • If customer ATO data is 'Not Missing', then set service action to 'NAT'

Are updates required for the customer's partner?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, are changes required to the FIPY or FNLE screen?
    • Yes, go to Step 12
    • No, go to Step 13

    • DOC record: 'Partner income not updated - No change to NRTL Review result (insert outcome). No further action required'
    • If previous year income has not been updated for the partner and NRTL is revoked set this service action to 'COM' and remaining partner service action to 'NAT'. If this service action does not apply, set to 'NAT'
  • 705 - Partial partner data missing on ATO Rec.
    • DOC record: 'ATO data did not contain all information required. TAP investigation determined (insert outcome). I have advised the customer/partner to contact the ATO (if required). No further action required'
    • If discrepancy is as a result of missing ATO data for the partner then 'COM' service action
    • If partner ATO data is 'Not Missing' then 'NAT' service action

Are changes required to the FIPY or FNLE screen?

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, go to Step 13

12

Code the relevant family assistance screens + Read more ...

After the service action has been completed, code all other relevant family assistance screens. The coding must be done in this order.

The changes must be agreed to by the customer as the Australian Tax Office (ATO) data cannot be used by itself without customer agreement. The ATO data is only a trigger for the review.

Code change to income on the FAO Income for Previous Year (FIPY) screen and, where necessary, delete the FNLE screen coding (for example, if it is identified the customer must lodge a tax return).

If the 'Not required to lodge' is revoked, discuss any debt raised with the customer and confirm the customer must complete an income tax return. The customer must be advised to lodge their income tax return with the ATO by 30 June 2021 in accordance with FTB lodgement requirements. If customers fail to lodge they may:

  • not receive their supplement or top up payments
  • have a debt raised for supplements previously paid
  • incur a non-lodger debt

If, as a result of the review, the customer incurred an underpayment or overpayment, code the amount in the Adjustment: field on the FAIS screen.

13

Finalise the review + Read more ...

End date Display on Access DOC on customer's record (if applicable).

Complete review and advise customer of any changes to reconciliation results. Explain any arrears or overpayment amounts resulting from the review. Document this conversation.

Record details on a DOC using Fast Note - select Auto text, use Families > Updates > FTB Income Review Completed.

Example

  • Summary line: FTB Income Review completed
  • Text: FTB Income Review completed resulting in reconciliation debt/arrears/no adjustment for the 201X/1X financial year. Customer included their FTB entitlement during their non-lodger declaration resulting in overpayment of $xxxx. Please see ATO Pre-fill Updated doc dated DD/MM/YYYY

Note: if overpayment debt has been raised as a result of the review, the debt is not payable until 28 days after the issue date of the Account Payable/overpayment advice. However, a customer may choose to set up withholdings before this due date. Refer to Procedures for arranging withholdings to recover debts.

If the customer is subject to Income Management (INM), see Identifying and assisting income managed customers.

Procedure ends here.
