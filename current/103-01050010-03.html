3

Urgent payment requests for income managed customers + Read more ...

An urgent payment will be income managed at the percentage applicable to the customer's Income Management measure. For example:

  • Child Protection and Supporting People at Risk measures 70% of the urgent payment will be income managed, while the remaining 30% will be paid to the customer as discretionary funds
  • Cape York Initiative 60%, 75% or 90% of the urgent payment will be income managed while the remaining percentage will be paid to the customer as discretionary funds
  • Other Income Management measures 50% of the urgent payment will be income managed while the remaining percentage will be paid to the customer as discretionary funds:
    • Disengaged Youth
    • Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients
    • Voluntary Income Management
    • Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients (VWPR)

4

Authenticate customer/nominee + Read more ...

Authenticate the customer or nominee.

For face-to-face contact where the customer cannot be authenticated, Alternative Identity Confirmation may be used in extreme circumstances.

Can the customer/nominee be authenticated?

  • Yes:
    • nominee or third party is applying on behalf of the customer, go to Step 5
    • customer is making the application, go to Step 6
  • No, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the urgent payment request. See Processing urgent payment requests

5

Nominee or third party requesting urgent payment on behalf of customer + Read more ...

A correspondence nominee is the only type of nominee or third party that can apply for an urgent payment on the customer's behalf. The payment can only be made to the customer when a request is received from a correspondence nominee.

Consider the effect of the nominee or third party arrangement.

Is the nominee a correspondence nominee?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, an application cannot be made. Procedure ends here

6

Investigate alternative assistance + Read more ...

Discuss other assistance options with the customer or nominee before proceeding with an urgent payment request.

Is an alternative assistance option available to the customer?

  • Yes, urgent payment is not required
  • No, go to Step 7

7

First request today + Read more ...

A customer is entitled to one urgent payment and one review of an urgent payment request per day.

Is this the first request for urgent payment today?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No:
    • If a request has already been made today and the customer disagrees with the decision, they can ask for the decision to be reviewed. See Customer disagrees with an urgent payment decision
    • If a request and review of that decision has already been made today the request is denied. See Processing urgent payment requests

8

Customer receiving an eligible payment + Read more ...

Is the customer requesting the urgent payment from an eligible pension or allowance?

  • Yes, see Table 2
  • No, an urgent payment cannot be made for an ineligible payment type. The customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the urgent payment request. See Processing urgent payment requests

3

How urgent payment rules work for income managed customers + Read more ...

Explain to the customer:

  • The minimum amount required to satisfy the customer's immediate hardship need is payable as an urgent payment
  • The urgent payment will be income managed at the percentage applicable to their measure of Income Management
  • The income managed portion of the urgent payment will be credited to their Income Management account and will be available for immediate use (on finalisation of the urgent payment activity)
  • The amount of the urgent payment (total of income managed and discretionary funds) will be deducted from their next payment
  • As their next income support payment will be reduced, there may be insufficient income managed funds available to cover future regular expenses. Service Officers are to view the Expense Management Summary screen and view the percentage of fortnightly unallocated funds and fortnight (14-days) balance details to determine how the urgent payment is likely to impact future allocations

The Service Officer should consider the above when determining the customer's request for an urgent payment.

To explain how urgent payments are calculated for:

  • the Child Protection or Supporting People at Risk measures, go to Step 4
  • the Cape York Initiative, go to Step 5
  • all other Income Management measures, go to Step 6

4

Child Protection and Supporting People at Risk measures + Read more ...

Only 30% of the urgent payment will be paid as discretionary funds. The balance of the payment is credited to the customer's Income Management account and is available for immediate use.

Service Officers must ensure the customer is aware the total amount of funds paid to the customer and their Income Management account will be deducted from their next payment. This may result in insufficient funds being available to meet future regular expenses. Service Officers must therefore use caution to ensure the customer is not placed in future financial hardship.

All urgent payments are limited to a maximum payment of:

  • $200, for urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances, or
  • $1,000 if the payment is due to Centrelink delay

To calculate the amount to be recorded in the Immediate Payment requests script (30% of the urgent payment), multiply the total amount of the urgent payment being granted by 0.3.

For example, to grant an urgent payment of:

$200: 200 x 0.3 = 60. Key $60 into the Immediate Payment requests script. This will result in $60 being paid as discretionary funds and $140 to their Income Management account

$1,000: 1,000 x 0.3 = 300. Key $300 into the Immediate Payment requests script. This will result in $300 being paid as discretionary funds and $700 to their Income Management account

See Table 3.

5

Cape York Initiative + Read more ...

Only 40%, 25% or 10% (depending on the customer's income managed percentage - 60%, 75% or 90%) of the urgent payment will be paid as discretionary funds. The balance of the payment is credited to the customer's Income Management account and is available for immediate use.

Service Officers must ensure the customer is aware their next payment will be reduced by the total amount paid as discretionary and income managed funds. This may result in insufficient funds being available to meet future regular expenses. Service Officers must therefore use caution to ensure the customer is not placed in future financial hardship.

All urgent payments are limited to a maximum payment of:

  • $200, for urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances, or
  • $1,000 if the payment is due to Centrelink delay

To calculate the amount to be recorded in the Immediate Payment requests script (for example, 10%, 25% or 40% of the urgent payment), multiply the total amount of the urgent payment being granted by 0.1, 0.25 or 0.4 respectively. The amount keyed into the Immediate Payment requests script will depend on the percentage the customer is being income managed.

For example, customers income managed at:

  • 60%, to grant an urgent payment of:
    • $200: 200 x 0.4 = 80. Key $80 into the Immediate Payment requests script. This will result in $80 being paid as discretionary funds and $120 to their Income Management account
    • $1,000: 1000 x 0.4 = 400. Key $400 into the Immediate Payment requests script. This will result in $400 being paid as discretionary funds and $600 to their Income Management account
  • 75%, to grant an urgent payment for:
    • $200: 200 x 0.25 = 50. Key $50 into the Immediate Payment requests script. This will result in $50 being paid as discretionary funds and $150 to their Income Management account
    • $1,000: 1000 x 0.25 = 250. Key $250 into the Immediate Payment requests script. This will result in $250 being paid as discretionary funds and $750 to their Income Management account
  • 90%, to grant an urgent payment for:
    • $200: 200 x 0.1 = 20. Key $20 into the Immediate Payment requests script. This will result in $20 being paid as discretionary funds and $180 to their Income Management account
    • $1,000: 1000 x 0.1 = 100. Key $100 into the Immediate Payment requests script. This will result in $100 being paid as discretionary funds and $900 to their Income Management account

See Table 3.

6

Other Income Management measures + Read more ...

Only 50% of the urgent payment will be paid as discretionary funds to customers income managed under the following measures:

  • Disengaged Youth
  • Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients
  • Voluntary Income Management
  • Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients

The balance of the payment is credited to the customer's Income Management account and is available for immediate use.

Service Officers must ensure the customer is aware of the total amount of discretionary funds to be paid and the amount to be income managed from their next payment will be reduced and this may result in insufficient funds being available to meet future regular expenses. Service Officers must therefore use caution to ensure the customer is not placed in future financial hardship.

All urgent payments are limited to a maximum payment of:

  • $200, for urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances, or
  • $1,000 if the payment is due to Centrelink delay.

To calculate the amount to be recorded in the Immediate Payment requests script (50% of the urgent payment), multiply the total amount of the urgent payment being granted by 0.5.

For example, to grant an urgent payment of:

  • $200: 200 x 0.5 = 100. Key $100 into the Immediate Payment requests script. This will result in $100 being paid as discretionary funds and $100 to their Income Management account
  • $1,000: 1,000 x 0.5 = 500. Key $500 into the Immediate Payment requests script. This will result in $500 being paid as discretionary funds and $500 to their Income Management account

See Table 3.

Step

Action

1

Check the number of urgent payments in the previous 12 months + Read more ...

Check the customer’s record to determine how many urgent payments have been made in the previous 12 months. Check the Display on Access (DOA) DOC and the Document List (DL) for details.

Note: the Immediate Payment requests script is incorrect as it includes all urgent payments made, not just those for Exceptional and unforeseen circumstances.

  • Exclude urgent payments due to:
    • Centrelink delay
    • costs associated with a funeral
    • holiday processing

In the previous 12 months if the customer has had:

  • 2 or more urgent payments, assess eligibility for an urgent payment if the customer is in severe financial hardship due to extraordinary circumstances. Procedure ends here
  • less than 2 urgent payments, go to Step 2

2

Severe financial hardship + Read more ...

Ask the customer about their current financial circumstances to determine if they are in severe financial hardship.

Is the customer experiencing severe financial hardship?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the urgent payment request. See Processing urgent payment requests

3

Exceptional and unforeseen circumstances + Read more ...

An urgent payment is not to be made for regular expenses where the circumstances are not exceptional and/or could be foreseen (for example, the customer has been evicted from their rental accommodation due to not paying their rent) or the customer has solely contributed to the delay in payment.

Is the customer's financial hardship due to exceptional and unforeseen circumstances?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the urgent payment request. See Processing urgent payment requests

4

Accrued entitlement meets immediate need + Read more ...

The Immediate Payment requests script will calculate the available funds (the accrued entitlement).

An urgent payment is paid in arrears and is limited to the number of days between the 'date paid to' and the date on which the payment is being processed, less deductions for debt repayments, tax, Centrepay, immediate payment deduction etc. to ensure that enough funds are left to meet these commitments. The available amount, determined by the system, cannot be overridden.

If the customer's accrued entitlement does not meet their immediate need, the urgent payment request will not be approved.

5

Negotiate the amount to be paid + Read more ...

The amount payable is the smallest of:

  • available funds calculated by the system
  • the amount the customer requested
  • the minimum amount it will cost to meet the customer's immediate hardship need, or
  • the maximum amount of $200

Before offering an urgent payment consider:

  • Is the amount being offered the minimum required to meet the customer's immediate need?
  • If paid, how will the customer meet their regular ongoing expenses such as rent, food, utilities on their next payday? Reduce the amount offered or deny the request if the customer is not able to meet their regular ongoing expenses
  • Does the customer history indicate they are struggling to meet regular ongoing expenses or appear to be in a crisis cycle? Consider a referral to Financial Wellbeing and Capability Activities or weekly payment assessment with an Authorised Weekly Payment Officer (AWPO)

Discuss with the customer and confirm they understand:

  • any money the customer receives for urgent payment will reduce their next fortnightly payment, and
  • how they intend to meet their regular expenses with the reduced funds received on their regular payday

While the system will allow as little as $1 to be paid, when determining the amount of the urgent payment consider:

  • most stores require a minimum purchase of between $10 to $15 to be made before EFTPOS can be
  • ATMs issue a minimum of $20
  • Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) cards can only be issued in denominations of $20 or $50 as these are the amounts available at ATMs

Will receiving the urgent payment extend or make the customer's financial hardship worse?

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the payment. See Processing urgent payment requests
  • No, see Processing urgent payment requests
