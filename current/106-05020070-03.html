Student assessment for Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP)

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Determine if student is exempt + Read more ...

A student may be exempt from the LAWP if one of the following applies.

  • The student has served a LAWP within the last 12 months (check the Start Date Calculator (NDC) screen to see if a LAWP has been served within the past 12 months)
  • The student is transferring from one payment to another within a 14 day period
  • The student has self-served the LAWP
  • The student is undertaking a rehabilitation program, an activity under the Community Support Program, or formal vocational training in a labour market program approved by Centrelink

Is the student exempt from the LAWP?

Yes, the LAWP does not apply. Procedure ends here

  • No, go to Step 2

2

Severe financial hardship + Read more ...

The LAWP may be waived in full or in part when the student is in severe financial hardship as a result of unavoidable or reasonable expenditure.

For more information refer to Policy.

Is the student in severe financial hardship?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, apply the Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP). Procedure ends here

3

Unavoidable or reasonable circumstances + Read more ...

Unavoidable or reasonable expenditure by a customer serving a LAWP or an Income Maintenance Period (IMP) includes:

  • reasonable cost of living, and
  • other unavoidable or reasonable expenditure, and
  • for Youth Allowance (YA) and Austudy only, other upfront study expenses from liquid assets are allowed for funds that are required for education purposes over a 12 month period

Were the circumstances that caused the hardship unavoidable or reasonable?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, apply the Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP). Procedure ends here

4

Calculation + Read more ...

Calculate the total unavoidable or reasonable expenditure.

Request these details from customer, as well as any evidence to support this expenditure and calculate amount customer wishes to be disregarded from liquid assets. For students, upfront study expenses are also able to be included where funds are required for education purposes over the next year.

Note: only reasonable expenditure supported by evidence (for example, receipts) is to be included in this calculation.

5

Has the student already served part of their LAWP? + Read more ...

  • Yes, see Step 1 in the Calculate student expenditure to assess LAWP table
  • No, see Step 3 in the Calculate student expenditure to assess LAWP table

Calculate student expenditure to assess Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP)

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

Reasonable cost of living + Read more ...

Calculate the student's reasonable cost of living for the period they have served

For a single person, this would be the rate of the appropriate allowance that would have been payable to the person had they not been subject to the IMP or LAWP; for a member of a couple this amount is doubled.

Casual earnings need to be taken into consideration as to how they would have affected the student's rate.

2

Calculation + Read more ...

Add the total unavoidable or reasonable expenditure obtained from Step 4 in the Student assessment for LAWP table to the reasonable cost of living obtained from Step 1.

3

Student subject to income maintenance period + Read more ...

If the student is also subject to an Income Maintenance Period (IMP) add the student's leave entitlements to their other liquid assets.

4

Unavoidable or reasonable expenditure + Read more ...

Subtract the unavoidable or reasonable expenditure (from Step 2) from the student's total liquid assets (from Step 3).

Is the result obtained greater than a fortnight's allowance?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

If the result is greater than a fortnight's allowance, the LAWP cannot be waived + Read more ...

  • Code the LAWP amount on the Start Date Calculator (NDC) screen in the LAWP Amt: field
  • Code the commencement date for the LAWP in the DLW/DCS/Release Date: field. The date for a full-time student will be the date commenced study
  • Record the details of the LAWP on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

6

If the result is less than a fortnight's allowance, waive the LAWP from the date that the student applied for hardship + Read more ...

  • Code the LAWP amount on the NDC screen in the LAWP Amt: field
  • Code the commencement date for the LAWP in the DLW/DCS/Release Date: field. The date for a full-time student will be the date commenced study
  • To waive, code 'Y' in the (LAWP) Waive: field

Record the details of the waiver of the LAWP on a DOC.
