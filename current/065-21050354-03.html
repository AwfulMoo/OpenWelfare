Receiving and checking the new and existing RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance

Table 1: This table describes how the Aged Care Payments team (Health Service Delivery Division (HSDD)) receives and checks new and existing Refundable Accommodation Deposit (RAD)/Daily Accommodation Payment (DAP) financial hardship assistance requests with an effective start date prior 1 August 2022. This process:

  • is only to be undertaken by Subject Matter Experts (SME) and above
  • applies to permanent residential care recipients who have entered into residential aged care service from 1 July 2014 and care recipients who have opted into the post 1 July 2014 means assessment arrangements

Step

Action

1

RAD/DAP hardship for periods prior to 1 August 2022 – manual adjustment + Read more ...

Any RAD/DAP hardship assistance granted for periods prior to 1 August 2022 are paid as manual adjustments by the Aged Care Payments team (HSDD).

Any subsequent review or correction to RAD/DAP hardship assistance for periods prior to 1 August 2022 are also completed as manual adjustments. From 1 August 2022 onwards, hardship supplements are automatically paid in the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP).

Example: care recipients granted RAD/DAP hardship assistance and coded in the ACSP on 15 September 2022 for an effective start date of 1 March 2022

  • RAD/DAP hardship assistance is coded in ACSP from 1 March 2022
  • Supplement is paid manually to the service for period 1 March 2022 to 31 July 2022
  • Supplement are paid automatically from 1 August 2022
  • A review of hardship changes to the RAD/DAP hardship reduction amount
  • Details from 1 March 2022 to 31 July 2022 are referred to HSDD for a manual adjustment to be paid for this period
  • ACSP updated coding will then adjust any RAD/DAP hardship assistance supplement that is paid from 1 August 2022 onwards

2

AC RAD DAP manual tracker spreadsheet + Read more ...

Open the AC RAD DAP manual tracker spreadsheet and check the following fields are completed:

  • Logon
  • CRN
  • Care recipient ID - ACMPS ID
  • SPARC ID
  • RACS
  • Care recipient full name
  • Start Date - this is the start date for RAD/DAP hardship assistance. If a RAD/DAP hardship assistance is being changed or revoked, this will be the original start date for relevant hardship period
  • End Date - this will be the end date up to which the earlier RAD/DAP hardship is being changed - latest date of 31 July 2022
  • Old fee reduction amount:
    • New grant – code NA
    • review - original fee reduction amount that was calculated/advised
  • New fee reduction amount: if hardship has been ceased or revoked, this will be $0.00
  • Comments:
    • New grant RAD/DAP hardship: Hardship granted for <fee type>. See ACMPS for hardship calculator results <for RAD DAP add – Current agreed RAD DAP is $amount>
    • Review of existing RAD/DAP hardship: Add comments stating why RAD/DAP hardship needs manual adjustment. For example, review of decision, cessation, revocation, early lodgement for continuation of hardship

Service Officers from the Aged Care Payments Team must cross check the spreadsheet against the ACSP to ensure all information is correct.

If any of the above information is missing or incorrect:

  • highlight the incorrect information advising the reason why they cannot action the RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance; and
  • return the spreadsheet to the Smart Centres Aged Care team via email

Once the Aged Care Payments team (HSDD) receives the amended spreadsheet, the manual adjustment process can be done. See Step 3 in Table 2.

Coding RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance applications

Table 2: this table contains information on how RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance requests are coded. This also includes coding any changes to existing requests.

Step

Action

1

Determining how the RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance is coded + Read more ...

  • The Smart Centre Aged Care team must code RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance grants (including continuations) from 1 August 2022 in the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP)
  • Manual adjustments are required for RAD/DAP hardship assistance granted for periods up until 31 July 2022
  • Existing RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance grants, with an effective start date prior to 1 August 2022 and are ongoing post 1 August 2022, are automatically added in to the ACSP from 1 August 2022 and onwards

Does the RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance require coding in the ACSP?

  • Yes, Go to Step 2
  • No, Go to Step 3

2

Coding RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance in the ACSP + Read more ...

This step applies to the Smart Centres Aged Care team. The Service Officer must review the current and existing RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance in the ACSP. To manage care recipients with either new or existing RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance see the Managing residential care financial hardship assistance in Aged Care Staff Portal (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table.

Once the care recipient’s RAD/DAP financial hardship has been processed in the ACSP, the payment will be made automatically to the aged care service.

Procedure ends here.

3

Determine manual adjustment type + Read more ...

Note: this process is only undertaken by Subject Matter Experts (SME) and above from the Aged Care Payments Team.

The Smart Centres Aged Care team must request the Aged Care Payments team (HSDD) to action manual adjustments for any period prior to 1 August 2022.

The Aged Care Payments team (HSDD) must code manual adjustments for the following RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance types:

  • Care recipients:
    • granted with a new RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance with an effective start date prior to 1 August 2022; or
    • with existing RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance with an updated end date that is prior to 1 August 2022; or
    • new end date is an extension to the original end date, a positive manual adjustment is required up until 1 August 2022

If RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance’s new end date is:

  • earlier than the original end date - a negative manual adjustment is required
  • an extension to the original end date - a positive manual adjustment is required up until 1 August 2022

Once the SME has determined the manual adjustment type, the SME can commence the manual adjustment process:

Does the RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance meet the manual adjustment criteria?

  • Yes,
    • SME can code in the manual adjustment amount for the relevant period required
    • See Step 2 in the Register a manual adjustment for residential care table
    • Once the manual adjustment has been completed, add a care recipient note containing the care recipient ID number, the period of the manual adjustment and the reason for the adjustment. See Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)).
  • No,
    • There may be incorrect information on the spreadsheet
    • The HSDD must contact the Smart Centres Aged Care team via email and advise the reason why a manual adjustment cannot be done. See Table 1.
    • Once the issue has been identified and corrected, the Aged Care Payments team (HSDD) can code the manual adjustment for the relevant RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance
    • See Step 2 in the Register a manual adjustment for residential care table
    • Once the manual adjustment has been completed, add a care recipient note containing the care recipient ID number, the period of the manual adjustment and the reason for the adjustment. See Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC))

Aged Care means assessments team

Table 3: this table describes how the Aged Care Payments team (HSDD) action RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance related issues.

Step

Action

1

RAD/DAP financial hardship issues + Read more ...

Service Officers may receive queries about the RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance requests about:

  • overlapping dates for existing and new requests
  • the payment amount or the payment period differing to what was advised in the RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance notification letter
  • the non-payments of the RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance

2

Escalation to the Smart Centres Aged Care team + Read more ...

Service Officers must escalate the RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance issues to the respective Team Leader for further clarity. Once the Team Leader has clarified the identified issue, the Team Leader must escalate to the Smart Centres Aged Care team via email.

If an aged care service contacts Services Australia to dispute the outcome of the care recipient’s RAD/DAP financial hardship assistance application, they must advise the aged care service to contact the relevant care recipient and for them to contact the Aged Care means assessments team.

3

Issue resolved + Read more ...

Service Officers:

  • are advised by Smart Centres Aged Care team that the identified issue/s has been resolved via email
  • must contact the aged care service via email or telephony advising the issue has been resolved
