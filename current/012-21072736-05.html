FAQ - COVID-19 General Practice Incentive payment

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Question: Is it important which order the first and second-dose assessment services are billed?

Answer: Yes. The first-dose assessment service must be billed first.

2

Question: Is there a different assessment service item number if the patient does not get the vaccine?

Answer: No. The GP will bill either a first-dose or second-dose assessment service whether or not the vaccine dose is given.

3

Question: Will the practice get a payment if a GP is registered with the practice after the assessment services were billed?

Answer: Yes, if the GP registration start date is before the date of the first-dose assessment service.

4

Question: Does the same GP have to bill the first and second-dose assessment services for the practice to get a payment?

Answer: No, but the following criteria apply:

  • the assessment services must be billed at the same practice
  • the GPs must be registered on the PIP profile with a start date before the date of the first service
  • the services must be billed in the correct order

5

Question: Will the practice get a payment if the GP is now non-current on the PIP profile?

Answer: Yes, if the GP was current at the date of the first and second-dose assessment services.

6

Question: Will the practice get a payment if they are approved for the PIP after the first-dose assessment service is billed?

Answer: No. The practice must be approved for the PIP before the first-dose assessment service is billed.

7

Question: My practice did not get a payment for some of the second dose assessment items provided this quarter. Can you check if the service/s was processed by the point-in-time date?

Answer: In accordance with Services Australia’s privacy policy, we cannot investigate services provided to individuals to determine the eligibility or timing of payments. Where all the requirements for payment have been met, payments will be made with the next PIP quarterly payment after successful processing of the second-dose assessment service.

8

Question: My practice billed both MBS items correctly. Why have I not received a payment for some patients?

Answer: Payments may be impacted by a patient’s decision to visit more than one general practice for vaccination services. In accordance with Services Australia’s privacy policy, we are unable to provide advice on the payment recipient of individual payments, or on how services provided to the patient by other practices may have impacted a practice meeting the requirements to receive the $10 payment for that patient. Where all the requirements for payment have been met, payments will be made with the next PIP quarterly payment after successful processing of the second-dose assessment service.

Scenarios where a practice may get a lower/later COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive payment

Table 2

Scenario

Example

Eligibility details

Scenario A - Practice gave one first-dose assessment and one second-dose assessment

The patient had their first dose at their first appointment.

The patient returned to the same practice for another appointment and the second dose was given.

The practice is eligible to receive the incentive payment as they have provided both first-dose and second-dose assessment services to the same patient.

The practice must be the first eligible practice to process both assessment services successfully.

Scenario B – The patient attended the practice once for either the first or second dose of the vaccine

The patient attended the practice once for either the first or second dose.

The practice billed either the first-dose assessment service or the second-dose assessment service, but did not bill both.

The practice is not eligible to receive the incentive payment as they have only provided one assessment service to the patient.

Scenario C – Practice gave multiple first-dose assessments, no second-dose assessments

The patient has a fever at their appointment for a first dose of the vaccine.

The GP deems them unsuitable for the vaccine and bills a first-dose assessment service.

The patient returns for another appointment and receives their first dose.

The GP claims another first-dose assessment service. No second-dose assessment service has been provided.

The practice is not eligible to receive the incentive payment as they have only provided a first-dose assessment service to the patient.

They will become eligible to receive the incentive payment if they are the first practice to successfully bill a second-dose assessment service for the same patient.

If this was a mistake and the practice intended to provide a first-dose and second-dose assessment service, they must correct their billing with Medicare Provider Enquiries. See Contact details.

Scenario D - Practice gave multiple second-dose assessments, no first-dose assessments

The patient attends the practice for the first time, for their second dose of the vaccine. As the patient has a fever, the GP deems them unsuitable for the vaccine and claims a second-dose assessment service.

The patient returns for another appointment and receives their second dose.

The GP bills another second-dose assessment service. No first-dose assessment service has been provided.

The practice is not eligible to receive the incentive payment, as they have only provided a second-dose assessment service to the patient.

The practice is not paid for multiple second-dose assessment services for the same patient.

If this was a mistake and the practice intended to provide a first-dose and second-dose assessment service, they must correct their billing with Medicare Provider Enquiries. See Contact details.

Scenario E - Practice gave multiple first-dose assessments and multiple second-dose assessments

The patient does not receive the vaccine at both appointments for the first and second dose of the vaccine.

The GP deems them unsuitable and bills both first-dose and second-dose assessment services at each appointment.

The patient returned for subsequent first and second dose appointments and the first and second doses were given to the patient.

The GP bills both first and second-dose assessment services again at the subsequent appointments.

The practice is eligible to receive one incentive payment for this patient as they have provided both first-dose and second-dose assessment services.

The practice is not paid for multiple second-dose COVID-19 vaccine suitability assessment services for the same patient.

Scenario F - Practice gave first-dose and second-dose assessment services, but had an error billing one/both items

The patient had their first dose at their first appointment.

The patient returned for another appointment and second dose was given.

The practice has an error with their practice software and accidentally bills the second-dose assessment service first.

The practice is eligible to receive the incentive payment, as they have provided both a first-dose and second-dose assessment services to the same patient.

The practice will not be paid for this patient until they correct their billing with Medicare Provider Enquiries. See Contact details.

Scenario G - Practice gave first-dose and second-dose assessment services, but delayed billing the items

The patient had their first dose at their first appointment.

The patient returned for another appointment and second dose given.

The practice does not bill the second-dose assessment service until after the point-in-time for PIP.

The practice is eligible to receive the incentive payment, as they have provided both first-dose and second-dose assessment services to the same patient.

If the second dose was billed after point-in-time, the practice will not be paid for this patient until the next quarterly payment.

Scenario H - Practice gave first and second-dose assessments, but another practice was first to give the same patient the first and second-dose assessments

The patient had their first-dose assessment at Practice A but were deemed unsuitable. Practice A bills a first-dose assessment service.

The patient then went to Practice B for another first-dose assessment and were given the first dose. Practice B bills a first-dose assessment service.

The patient went to Practice B for a second-dose assessment but were deemed unsuitable. Practice B bills a second-dose assessment service.

Patient returned to Practice A again for a second-dose assessment and their second dose was given. Practice A bills a second-dose assessment service.

Practice B is eligible to receive the incentive payment, as they were the first practice to provide both a first and second-dose assessment service.

In scenarios where multiple practices provide assessment services to the same patient, the payment will be made to the first practice to bill both the first and second-dose assessment services.

Scenario I - Practice gave first-dose assessment service, then transferred patient to a General Practice Respiratory Clinic (GPRC) for their second-dose assessment

The patient had their first dose of the vaccine at their first appointment at the practice.

The practice was low on vaccine supply and transferred the patient to a nearby GPRC for their second-dose assessment.

The practice is not eligible to receive the incentive payment, as they have only provided a first-dose assessment service to the patient.

Scenario J - Practice gave the first-dose assessment at their main PIP practice location, and a second-dose assessment at a location registered in PIP as a practice branch of the main location

The patient had their first dose at the practice location closest to their home.

They booked their second dose through the same practice, at the practice location closest to their workplace.

The practice is eligible to receive one incentive payment as 2 practice locations registered under a single PIP practice ID have provided both first-dose and second-dose assessment services to the same patient.

Scenario K - Practice gave first-dose and second-dose assessment services, but the GP providing the assessment services was not registered on the PIP profile

The patient had their first and second dose of the vaccine at the same PIP registered practice.

The GP providing one or both of the services was not registered in PIP as an Associated GP at the time of the first and/or second-dose assessment service.

The practice is eligible to receive the incentive payment as they have provided both first-dose and second-dose assessment services to the same patient.

To be eligible for payment the GP must be registered on the practice’s PIP profile with a start date prior to the date the first-dose assessment service was provided.

Practices will not receive incentive payments for any patients that were provided first-dose and/or second-dose assessment services by a GP who was not registered in PIP at the time of service.
