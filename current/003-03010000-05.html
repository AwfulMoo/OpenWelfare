Evidence of income

Included income types

Table 1: this table outlines evidence to request or minimum details needed to code different income types during the claim process, or when an online claim may not have sufficient information to make an assessment.

Item

Description

1

General information

This table contains:

  • income to consider when assessing income loss. This is income a customer earned, received or derived before or after the disaster
  • information or evidence from the customer to document, or scan onto their record
    Note: If information contained in an online claim or Assisted Customer Claim is sufficient to make an assessment, and there are no concerns about the validity of the claim, no additional evidence is required
  • minimum details to code relevant income screens. This may help when a customer self-declares their income but there is a need to code this income due to impacts on payment rate calculations

If the customer made a self-declaration of income and lodged no documentary evidence, coding the relevant income screens is not needed unless the income means the customer's total earned income may exceed the average weekly income amount.

If a customer’s partner is receiving another income support payment, request and code income details as per standard process to make sure the customer’s partner receives the correct entitlement. See the relevant Operational Blueprint file that relates to the partner’s income support payment type.

Use the information from the customer (Process page, Table 3) to decide the period to use when calculating their fortnightly average income before the disaster. For example:

  • 4 to 8 weeks, or
  • a different period for customers with irregular income, for example, self-employed or seasonal workers

2

Employment income

  • $ gross amount - calculate a fortnightly average

Examples of evidence:

  • payslips
  • letter from an employer and/or prospective employer
  • bank statements
  • tax returns

Payslips are the best form of evidence to determine gross employment income and any deductions such as salary sacrifice amounts. If a customer who is not self-employed provides bank statements as evidence, this will show their net income. Tell the customer the Australian Taxation Office's (ATO) tax tables may help to determine their gross income. The tax tables do not take into account amounts that have been salary sacrificed. The customer will need to provide this information.

A link to the ATO website can be found under the External Website heading on this page. Tell the customer to search for ‘tax tables’ and make sure the correct table is selected, such as weekly, fortnightly or monthly.

Document for each employer:

  • employer's name
  • employer's ABN (if known)
  • employer's address
  • employer's telephone number
  • name of industry for example, Agriculture - banana industry, Retail - clothes
  • type of employment for example, full-time, part-time, seasonal, casual, self-employed, company, trust

3

Self-employment income

  • $ taxable (not gross) - calculate a fortnightly average

A sole trader's taxable income is:

  • the gross income earned by the business (total turnover)
  • less any allowable deductions incurred in running the business

A sole trader's net business income is their taxable income, for DRA/NZ DRA purposes. See Assessing sole trader income and Business deductions.

When looking at a partnership structure, look at the portion of the business that the customer owns. See Assessing partnership income.

Examples of evidence:

  • bank statements
  • tax returns
  • profit and loss statements. This includes the Profit and Loss Statement (SU580)

4

Business, trust, private trust income

DRA/NZ DRA:

  • is a payment to an individual
  • is not a payment to a business, company or private trust
  • can only be paid to an individual if they have lost income from remunerative (paid) work
  • cannot be paid to an owner, director or a shareholder if a business, company or private trust has only lost revenue due to a disaster

The customer must show they:

  • earn, derive or receive amounts (either as a wage or salary) from remunerative (paid) work being undertaken as an employee of the business, company or private trust
    • ask the customer, ‘how much income did you declare as a wage or salary from the business, company or private trust on your personal tax return?
  • have suffered a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster as an employee
  • meet all other eligibility criteria

The customer must provide documentary evidence. For example:

  • a position description or statement outlining the roles and responsibilities associated with their employment
  • invoices detailing work undertaken, number of hours worked by an hourly amount, GST deducted, net amount payable
  • copy of their individual tax return from the most recent financial year that supports they earn a wage or salary from the business, company or private trust
  • bank statements that support regular periodic payments from a business, company or privacy trust bank account into their personal bank account

Amounts drawn from business or company bank accounts

A reduction in amounts drawn from a business or company bank account after a disaster is not enough to show a loss of income as an individual.

If a customer draws amounts from a business or company bank account they must show that the drawings are:

  • for remunerative (paid) work, that is a salary or wage,
  • not for their personal use or living expenses

The customer’s personal income tax return will show if the drawings were assessed as salary.

Do not use amounts drawn from a company account for personal use or living expenses as remunerative income when assessing income loss.

5

Planned leave or termination payments

  • type of leave for example, annual, sick, maternity, long service
  • start date and end date of the leave
  • if leave is paid leave
  • $ amount – calculate a fortnightly average
  • $ amount of any termination payment

A customer does not need to access/exhaust any leave entitlements.

An income maintenance period does not apply for DRA or NZ DRA.

Any lump sum leave or termination payment received by a customer should not be averaged out over the period the payment was for.

When calculating a customer's disaster affected income, include the amount in the fortnight it was received.

6

Income protection policy and/or Landlord protection insurance

  • name of insurance company
  • if customer intends to lodge a claim
  • if customer is not lodging a claim why not
  • date income will/has started
  • $ amount - calculate a fortnightly average

7

Rental properties

  • previous year's tax return
  • current rental property statements
  • $ amount - calculate a fortnightly average

8

Shares, options, rights, convertible notes or other securities listed on the Australian Stock Exchange or stock exchange outside Australia

This does not include managed investments or investments purchased with a margin loan.

For each investment:

  • name of company
  • number of shares or other securities
  • security code (if known)
  • country if not Australia
  • customer's share %
  • $ amount – calculate a fortnightly average

9

Shares, options or rights in public companies NOT listed on a stock exchange

This does not include managed investments or investments purchased with a margin loan.

For each investment:

  • name of company
  • type of shares, options or rights
  • number of shares, options or rights
  • current market value
  • customer's share %
  • $ amount – calculate a fortnightly average

10

Managed investments, e.g. personal investment plans, life insurance bonds, friendly society bonds in and/or outside of Australia

Do not include conventional life insurance policies or investments purchased with a margin loan.

For each investment:

  • name of company
  • name of product
  • type of product/option
  • number of units
  • APIR code (if known)
  • current market value
  • currency if not in AUD
  • customer's share %
  • $ amount – calculate a fortnightly average

11

Income stream products (only if customer is drawing an income from this product)

For each income stream:

  • name of product provider/Self-Managed Super Fund/Small APRA Fund
  • type of income stream
  • product reference number
  • customer's share %
  • $ amount – calculate a fortnightly average

12

Money invested in or income from other investments or sources

In addition to others already declared. Does not include income from real estate in or outside Australia, private trusts, private companies or business, social security payments, or an account used exclusively for funding from the National Disability Insurance Scheme

For each income source:

  • type of investment/income
  • name of organisation/company
  • currency if not in AUD
  • customer's share %
  • $ amount – calculate a fortnightly average

Direct result scenarios

Table 2: see the Operational Instructions for the event for more examples.

Scenario

Eligible or not eligible

Reason

Customer works in the disaster affected area. As a direct result of the disaster, the customer’s workplace was damaged and the customer is now unemployed while their workplace is being repaired. All other eligibility criteria are met.

Eligible

Customer works in the disaster affected area and has suffered a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster.

Customer works in the disaster affected area. As a direct result of the disaster, the customer’s workplace was destroyed. The employer has indicated that it will be 8 weeks before the business reopens, at which time the customer will be able to resume work. The customer is taking all of their annual leave at half pay to cover the 8 week period during which he will be without work. All other eligibility criteria are met.

Eligible

Customer works in the disaster affected area and while they still have some income, they have suffered a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster.

Customer lives outside the disaster affected area. They had two jobs, one in a disaster affected area from which they derive 45% of their income, and the other in an area that was not affected by the disaster from which they derives the remaining 55% of their income. The customer lost their job in the disaster affected area because of the disaster causing significant damage to their workplace. The customer’s other job was not affected by the disaster. All other eligibility criteria are met.

Eligible

Customer worked in the disaster affected area and has suffered a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster.

Customer owns a small business that is in a disaster affected area, which suffered no damage and could operate as usual. However, the majority of the customers are isolated due to the disaster and cannot physically access the business. The customer has had a profit down turn of 95% and is not meeting business costs. The weekly wage the customer derives from his business has consequently been reduced. All other eligibility criteria are met.

Eligible

While the customer’s business was not physically damaged, the customers were physically unable to access the business as a direct result of the disaster. The customer has experienced a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster, as they derive a wage from the business as an employee.

Customer owns a sandwich shop in the disaster affected area. It was damaged as a direct result of the disaster and needs to shut down for four weeks during the repairs. The customer has lost all income because of the disaster. All other eligibility criteria are met.

Eligible

Customer has experienced a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster.

The customer is a transport operator who provides services into and out of the disaster affected area. The customer and their vehicle are trapped in the disaster affected area and cannot source alternative work. The customer has a log book showing their activities before the disaster and a schedule for work that has been interrupted due to the disaster. Customer has no other source of income. All other eligibility criteria are met.

Eligible

Customer has experienced a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster and the customer can verify income before the disaster.

Customer derives an income from a retail business in the disaster affected area. The business’ stock was spoiled as a direct result of the disaster. The customer cannot operate the business until replacement stock has been obtained and the customer is without any other source of income. All other eligibility criteria are met.

Eligible

Customer works in the disaster affected area and has suffered a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster.

Customer lives outside the disaster affected area but works seasonally during crop harvests in the disaster affected area. The customer has a letter of engagement from a primary producer sent before the disaster confirming their employment in the coming harvest season and bank statements confirming their income in previous seasons. The customer was to begin work a month from the date of the disaster, but will no longer needed as the primary producer’s crops were destroyed in the disaster. The customer cannot source alternative work. All other eligibility criteria are met.

Eligible

Customer has suffered a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster. Customer will become eligible from the date they were to start work. Customer has evidence of past work in the area and of their intention to work in the disaster affected area again.

Customer works as a tradesperson in the disaster affected area. The customer’s company vehicle including all their tools was destroyed. Without the tools, the customer is unable to work until they have been replaced. All other eligibility criteria are met.

Eligible

Customer works in the disaster affected area and has lost income as a direct result of the disaster.

The customer derives income from a bed and breakfast in the disaster affected area. Road closures resulting from the disaster have left the bed and breakfast physically isolated, preventing guests from travelling to the bed and breakfast. The customer has records demonstrating the bookings, which have had to be cancelled. These losses are not fully offset by the extra income from guests who have had to extend their stay because of the road closures. All other eligibility criteria are met.

Eligible

Customer works in the disaster affected area and has lost income as a direct result of the disaster.

The customer derives income from a hotel in the disaster affected area. The disaster caused damage to several of the rooms, and bookings have had to be cancelled as a result. All other eligibility criteria are met.

Eligible

Customer works in the disaster affected area and has lost income as a direct result of the disaster.

Customer was seriously injured as a direct result of the disaster and was admitted to hospital. The customer provided evidence of hospital admission. As a result, the customer was unable to attend their place of employment and suffered a loss of income.

Eligible

Customer works in the disaster affected area and has lost income as a direct result of the disaster. The customer provided evidence of hospital admission

Customer has lost their casual job as a direct result of the disaster. Customer started a full time job the day after they lost their casual job.

Not eligible

Customer has not experienced a loss of income.

Customer works as an office worker and has been underperforming in their position. Customer has had three warnings to improve performance, but has failed to meet the performance requirements. Customer was stood down from their position after the disaster.

Not eligible

Customer cannot demonstrate a clear causal link between the loss of income and the disaster.

Customer works in a business affected by the disaster and has been laid off work for a period of four weeks. Customer has four weeks of annual leave, which they are taking during this period. At the end of the 4 week period, they will be able to return to work.

Not eligible

Customer has not experienced a loss of income.

Note: a customer does not need to access/exhaust their leave entitlement.

An emergency services volunteer has been deployed to help with clean-up efforts. The volunteer continues to receive their full weekly wage. The customer’s employment has not been impacted by the disaster.

Not eligible

Customer has not experienced a loss of income because of the disaster. Customer’s employment was not directly affected by the disaster.

A customer chooses to stay at home to defend their principal place of residence from a bushfire

The customer’s place of employment has not been closed and the customer could attend work if they chose to do so. The customer experiences a loss of income. As the customer is a casual worker, they cannot access any leave entitlements.

Not eligible

Customer’s loss of income is not as a direct result of the disaster.

Customer lives and works in a disaster affected area. The customer’s children attend a childcare centre that was rendered inaccessible as a direct result of the disaster. The customer needs to stay home to care for the children. As a result, the customer had to take leave from employment but was not entitled to any leave payments resulting in a total loss of income.

Not eligible

Customer’s loss of income is not a direct result of the disaster.

Customer lives and works in a metropolitan disaster affected area. Their private vehicle was damaged in the disaster. The customer claims that they are unable to get to work until the car is repaired. Public transport continues to operate in disaster affected areas.

Not eligible

Customer is able to return to work by alternative means of transport. They have not suffered a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster.

Customer owns and operates a hotel in the disaster affected area. The hotel remains accessible and was not damaged in the disaster. Following the disaster, there has been a downturn in the local tourism industry and the hotel’s occupancy rate is far lower than is usual for this time of year.

Not eligible

Customer’s loss of income is not a direct result of the disaster.

The customer is a self-employed business person who lives in the disaster affected area and supplies exclusively to the local businesses. Due to the disaster, the local businesses have experienced a sharp downturn in demand and have subsequently reduced their orders. The customer has found new customers but their income has still been reduced by 65%.

Not eligible

Customer’s loss of income is not a direct result of the disaster.

The customer is a transport operator who provides services into and out of the disaster affected area. The customer’s vehicle is undamaged and all roads are open however, the disaster has reduced significantly the demand for their services. Customer has a log book showing their activities before the disaster and a schedule for work that has been interrupted due to the disaster. Customer has no other source of income.

Not eligible

Customer has experienced a loss of income but it is not as a direct result of the disaster.

The customer had work available to them and was able to get to work but chose to stay at home to defend their property from the impacts of the disaster. Because of defending their property, they lost income from work.

Not eligible

Customer has experienced a loss of income but it is not as a direct result of the disaster.

DRA and Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment

Table 3

Scenario

Eligible or not eligible

Reason

Example 1

A customer was granted and is receiving DRA as they have reduced hours due to a declared major disaster.

The customer:

  • is still working 10 hours a week
  • then tests positive with COVID-19 and now cannot work the 10 hours per week
  • claims for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • meets all other eligibility criteria

Eligible

DRA is not a precluding payment for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

The customer is eligible for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment, as they now cannot earn 10 hours a week of income due to testing positive with COVID-19.

Example 2

A customer was granted and is receiving DRA.

The customer:

  • has not returned to work and they are not earning an income
  • then contracts COVID-19
  • claims for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • meets all other eligibility criteria

Not eligible

DRA is not a precluding payment for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

However, the reason for the customer not working is due to being impacted by a declared major disaster, not due to testing positive with COVID-19. The customer is already receiving DRA.
