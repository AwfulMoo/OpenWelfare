Smart Centres

This page contains information on Customer Overview for Smart Centre staff.

On this page:

How to access Customer Overview

Table1

Item

Description

1

Customer Overview location

Service Officers can access Customer Overview through Services Australia Workspace. Customer Overview requires the latest version of Services Australia Workspace to display.

Customer Overview is configurable and must be in the collapsed mode, must be pinned to display.

2

Access to Customer Overview

For Customer Overview access, Service Officer logons must be included in the Genesys Access Group.

A Service Officer can request access through their Team Leader.

3

Customer information on Customer Overview

Customer Overview requires a Customer Reference Number (CRN), or Customer Access Number (CAN) to be entered in the Interactive Voice Response (IVR) during an active call.

Service Officers can also enter the customer's CRN/CAN in Customer First to fill the customer’s information while on an active call.

When the CRN/CAN has changed within Customer First, the Customer Overview screen will fill the new customer's details.

If no recent contacts exist, Customer Overview will display No recent customer history.

Customer Overview icon descriptions

Table 2

Icon

Description

Blue indicates an event was outbound to the customer.

Grey advises that the outcome is unknown.

Green, customer has successfully self-served.

Maroon implies that the event or activity originated from the customer.

Amber indicates something that requires Service Officer action - usually something that happened in the IVR

Red indicates that the desired outcome was not achieved.

Profile Header information

Table 3. The Profile Header provides Service Officers of any indicators relating to the customer. These indicators allow Service Officers to tailor the conversations they have with customers.

Profile header assists by:

  • showing if the customer is located in a remote area, has a preferred language (other than English) or if they currently have a nominee
  • provides a view of what self service the customer is registered for and if there are any issues blocking their ability to self service

    Item

    Icon

    Description

    Remote

    Remote

    Remote icon only displays if a remote indicator is present on the customers record. Mouseover shows ‘town and state’.

    Language

    Language

    Language icon only displays if a preferred Language is present on the customers record. Mouseover shows ‘preferred language’ (if not English).

    Nominee

    Nominee

    Nominee icon displays if the customer is or has a nominee. Mouseover shows nominee type: ‘Payment’, ‘Correspondence’ or ‘Payment and Correspondence’.

    Voiceprint

    Voiceprint

    Voiceprint icon displays when the customer has an active Voiceprint. Mouseover shows ‘Active’ or ‘Locked’ and last login date.

    Online

    Online

    Online icon displays when a customer is registered for a Centrelink Online Account. Mouseover shows ‘Active’ and last log on date or ‘Locked’ and the reason for the ‘Locked’ status.

    App

    App

    App icon displays when a customer is registered for the Express Plus Centrelink App. Mouseover shows ‘Active’ and last log on date or ‘Locked’ and the reason for the ‘Locked’ status.

    Locked status

    Amber icon will replace Voiceprint, Online or App icons when account is locked.

Banner information

Table 4. Banners alert Service Officers to important information about the customer.

Banners assist by:

  • alerting Service Officers that a customer has a special circumstance such as a Display on access DOC that must be viewed before interacting with the customer
  • providing a banner to alert Service Officers to any agency priority such as COVID 19 Stimulus payments

    Item

    Icon

    Description

    Display on Access (DOA) Banner

    Warning displays in the Banner display format if one or more Display On Access (DOA) DOCs on file. DOA banners do not provide information about the DOA specifics.

    Restricted Access Banner

    No information will display on Customer Overview, including staff with Restricted Access to the record.

    Managed Service Plan Banner

    Managed Service Plan Banner displays when an active MSP is in place. MSP banners do not replicate information displayed in Customer First.

    Special Banner

    Special banners are used to alert Service Officers or provide direct access to resources. Special banners are only displayed when required. Time frames will vary.

Alerts information

Table 5. Alerts provide the Service Officer with a view of what is affecting the customer in order to provide a better interaction.

Alerts assists by:

  • displaying if the customer has any issues present on their issues screen
  • advising if the customers payment is currently suspended or recently cancelled
  • providing the date of an upcoming or missed appointment
  • showing the status of a customer claim and when it was submitted
  • showing if the customer is due to report or have missed their reporting day
  • showing the customers debt situation and if any debt arrangements are due to expire

    Item

    Icon

    Description

    Reporting due today

    Reporting due today

    Displays on the day reporting is due only.

    Reporting overdue

    Reporting overdue - 11 Feb

    Displays until overdue report is submitted.

    Missed Appointments

    Missed provider appointment 24 Feb

    Displays for 14 days after the event or until re-engaged appointment is booked. Alert displays the date and type of

    appointment e.g. ServAU or Provider.

    Upcoming Appointment

    Upcoming Serv AU appointment 24 Feb 10:30am

    Displays for 7 days prior to the event. Alert displays the date, time and type of appointment. e.g. ServAU or Provider.

    Active Debt

    Active debt

    If there is an active debt, one of three debt alerts show. Only one debt alert displays, even for multiple debts.

    Active Debt - alert shows if there is an active debt/s and the other two debt alerts are not appropriate.

    Active Debt - repayment arrangement ending

    Active debt - repayment arrangement ending soon

    Alert shows if a debt arrangement plan is due to end within 14 days.

    Active Debt - repayment arrangement ended

    Active debt - repayment arrangement ended

    Alert shows if debt repayment plan has ended and not rearranged in the last 28 days.

    Claim Submitted

    New PPS claim submitted 02 Feb - on hold

    Claim alerts displays when a claim is submitted until it is finalised. Claims older than 12 months will display with the year present on the alert.

    Combined claims (e.g. CP - Carers payment and CDA - Carers Allowance) with a single claim ID display with a comma separating the type of claim in a single alert. If one claim is finalised prior to the other, only the current outstanding claim will display.

    Rejected Claim

    New PPS claim submitted 02 Feb - Rejected

    Alert displays for 28 days after the event.

    Suspended Benefit

    YAL Suspended - YBS 02 Feb

    Suspended benefit status displays for 28 days. Alert displays the payment type, reason and date for suspension.

    Cancelled Benefit

    YAL Cancelled - ATR 12 Feb

    Cancelled benefit status displays for 28 days. Alert displays the payment type, reason and date of cancellation.

    Issues

    Issues on file

    Only displays when Issues present on file.

    If no issues are present on the issues page, this alert will not display.

Smart IVR Cards information

Table 6. Smart IVR cards display the personalised messages and activities the customer has undertaken in the Smart IVR prior to reaching a Service Officer.

Smart IVR cards assist by:

  • showing the outcome of the Update Phone number activity the customer has completed and if any further checks are required by the Service Officer
  • showing the outcome of the Confirm Family Assistance Income estimate card
  • showing the personalised messages the customer has been reminded of, or asked to action. This saves the Service Officer from repeating messages or activities already undertaken by the customer

    Item

    Icon

    Description

    Update Phone Number

    Update Phone Number cards display for the current call only. There are 3 different icons that display with descriptions of the

    outcome or process to be taken. Card are grey in colour.

    Smart IVR Reminder Messages

    IVR Reminder Messages display for the current call only and only if the customer has been played IVR messages. There are 2 different icons that display with descriptions of the outcome or process to be taken. Card are grey in colour.

    Confirmed Families Income estimate

    Families income estimate cards display for the current call only. There are 3 different icons that display with descriptions of the outcome or action to be taken. Cards are grey in colour.

Cards information

Table 7. Most information on Customer Overview display as cards. Cards display the interactions between a customer and the agency in a chronological order for the last 28 days. (With the exception of SMS and push notifications displaying for only 7 days.) Cards aggregate for the same activity type undertaken of the same day.

Cards assists by:

  • displaying letters and correspondence the customer has received from the agency and a view of the letter as the customer views it (PDF)
  • displaying the interactions the customer has had in the Smart IVR prior to reaching a Service Officer. This save the Service Officer from repeating messages or completing activities the customer has already undertaken or attempted to self-serve
  • displaying the previous interactions the customer has had with the agency, such as Service Centre visit, online and app activities, phone calls and push notifications highlighting the journey the customer has already been on

    Item

    Icon

    Description

    Correspondence Cards

    Online letter

    (28 day display)

    Letter cards will display the issue date shown on the letter. Letter titles can be clicked to produce a PDF version of the letter and titles have been modified to display a meaningful title.

    Paper letter

    (28 day display)

    Letter cards will display the issue date shown on the letter. Letter titles can be clicked to produce a PDF version of the letter and titles have been modified to display a meaningful title.

    Email

    (28 day display)

    Email titles can be clicked to produce a PDF version the customer has received.

    SMS

    (7 day display)

    SMS titles can be clicked to produce a PDF version the customer has received.

    Push Notifications

    (7 day display)

    Displays activity title on card that the customer has been reminded to complete.

    Telephony Cards

    Inbound call

    (28 day display)

    Only previous calls will be shown. Not the current active call.

    Scheduled call-backs and place in queue

    (28 day display)

    Place in Queue (PIQ) calls and Scheduled callback calls (SCIQ) display on the current call to provide Service Officers the reason for

    call.

    Self Service Applications and Microservices

    (28 day display)

    Self Service Application and Microservices cards display when the customer has entered the App or Microservice within the IVR.

    • Green - indicates the transaction was successful
    • Grey - indicates the outcome is unknown
    • Red - indicated an unsuccessful transaction

    Abandoned and congestion

    (28 day display)

    Abandoned calls display when the customer ends the call while in the Queue. Congestion cards display if the customer is played a message in the IVR to call back another time as all our Service Officers are busy. This happens if the customer is not eligible to be routed to Self Service options based on the reason for their call and/or authentication level and calls are not being routed to Service Officers.

    Online and Mobile Applications

    Online

    (28 day display)

    Details of the activity undertaken are displayed in the card title for single transaction and ‘multiple activities’ if more than one activity on the same day.

    • Red - indicated activities did not complete.
    • Grey - indicates mixed outcomes from activities
    • Green - indicates completed activities

    Card expands to display time and title of activities with an indicator of the outcome.

    Mobile Application

    (28 day display)

    Details of the activity undertaken are displayed in the card title for single transaction and ‘multiple activities’ if more than one activity on the same day.

    • Red - indicated activities did not complete
    • Grey - indicates mixed outcomes from activities
    • Green - indicates completed activities

    Card expands to display time and title of activities with an indicator of the outcome.

    Service Centre visit

    Service Centre visit

    (28 day display)

    Title advises location of visit. Clicking on the title enables an expanded view providing further information - timestamp in local time, the queue name and the contact type of an adhoc Service Centre visit.

How to access Customer Overview

Description

Customer Overview location

Access to Customer Overview

Service Officers can request access to Customer Overview through their Team Leader.

3

Customer information on Customer Overview

Customer Overview requires a Customer Reference Number (CRN) or Customer Access Number (CAN) to populate customer information.
