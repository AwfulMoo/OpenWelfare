Roles and Quality standards for debt staff

Item

Description

1

Role of non-debt staff in identifying debts + Read more ...

Most debts occur automatically due to:

  • a reassessment, or
  • a review

Some debts are identified and created manually. Staff should identify debts quickly and accurately and record them in the correct system.

Staff may identify a possible debt triggered by:

  • a change in circumstances
  • reviews
  • data-matching programs
  • public information
  • automatic system tags
  • prepayments
  • advance payments
  • compensation grants
  • Assurance of Support

Where a potential debt is identified, and a debt shell is not automatically generated as part of a reassessment or review activity, record details on a minimum standard ACTDOC. Transfer the ACTDOC to the relevant Debt Raising Team who will undertake further investigations.

Look up local Processing Service details for: Debt Raising to determine the region code.

2

Role of debt staff when recovering debts + Read more ...

To recover debts as soon as possible without placing customers in severe financial hardship.

This can involve:

  • informing customers of self service options to set up a repayment arrangement
  • negotiating repayments, considering the Customer Focus statement
  • deciding the best recovery action considering cost effectiveness
    • this may include choosing not to pursue recovery
    • see Managing recovery and non recovery of Centrelink debts
  • advising customers of their review and appeal rights
  • liaising with government departments and organisations
  • reporting and raising risks about safety, privacy, self-harm and aggression

Note: during initial negotiations, staff should advise customers of their legal obligation to notify any change of address within 14 days while a debt is owed, even if they no longer receive a payment.

All debt staff are also required to be aware of quality service statement for recovery.

3

Role of debt staff when investigating debt + Read more ...

The role of debt raising staff in investigating debts is to:

  • gather evidence/documentation and/or utilise information already available to determine if a recoverable debt exists. For details on recoverable debts, see Finalised 'no debt' (FND) or 'zeroing' debts that are not legally recoverable
  • start an investigation after identifying a possible debt
  • gather and examine:
    • documentation/evidence from the customer, employer etc.
    • payment history records
    • source documents (claim forms, etc.)
    • record the start and end dates of the debt period

Any action taken should be recorded on a DOC.

4

Role of debt staff in calculating debts + Read more ...

The role of debt raising staff in calculating debts is to:

  • accurately calculate the debt amount in a timely manner using ADEX. MultiCal is the next preferred method. Sometimes it might be necessary to calculate a debt manually
  • calculate the amount of the debt as soon as all the evidence and source documents are obtained. Calculations include determining:
    • the rate payable throughout the period
    • changes in circumstances
    • actual amounts paid throughout the period
    • any withholding adjustments
    • any tax adjustments, see Adjusting amounts on a payment summary

There are tools available to assist in calculating debts.

5

Role of debt staff in raising debt and advising customer + Read more ...

The role of debt staff when raising debts is to:

  • raise a recoverable debt quickly and accurately
  • determine if a 10% Recovery Fee should apply based on evidence
  • inform the customer when required
  • discuss recovery where possible
  • issue an account payable letter

See Safeguarding customer privacy when raising and recovering debts, before contacting a customer or issuing a debt notice. Take the appropriate actions to protect the customer's privacy.

This a duty of all debt staff.

6

Customer focus when recovering debts + Read more ...

Debt staff interact with a diverse range of people and businesses including:

  • customers, their families, friends and representatives
  • colleagues, supervisors, managers and subordinates
  • other Centrelink sections, units and programs
  • government departments
  • members of Parliament
  • employers, solicitors, accountants, police, etc.

A key part of the department's focus is to treat all people fairly, with respect, empathy, courtesy and understanding.

Listening to customers and treating them with respect and courtesy is:

  • in line with the quality service statement for recovery
  • assists in negotiating an acceptable payment arrangement more successfully
  • achieves an increased commitment by the customer to repay and finalise their debt/s

See Safeguarding customer privacy when raising and recovering debts, before contacting a customer or issuing a debt notice. Take the appropriate actions to protect the customer's privacy.

This a duty of all debt staff.

7

Customer contact times + Read more ...

Unless a customer has requested contact outside these times, debt staff must only phone customers:

  • at home:
    • Monday - Friday, 8:00 am - 5:00 pm
    • Weekends - no contact
    • Public Holidays - no contact recommended
  • at work: during customers' normal working hours if known, or 9:00 am - 5:00 pm if not known

Note: be aware of time-zones. All contact times apply to local time where the customer lives.

8

Quality service statement for debt staff + Read more ...

Debt staff have a duty to recover money owing as soon as possible while providing excellence in customer service.

  • Staff will professionally represent the interests of policy departments by negotiating tailored repayments enabling effective and timely debt recovery
  • Staff will treat customers with fairness, respect and sensitivity
  • Staff will provide accurate, consistent and up-to-date information in a way that is easy to understand
  • Staff will improve communications and correct mistakes or issues as soon as they arise
  • Staff will provide prompt and efficient service and will be accountable for the quality of work and continuous improvement

9

Recovery practices - Stage 1 + Read more ...

Stage 1 - Basic Approach

  • Debt staff should assume that the customer wants to repay the debt to the best of their ability
  • To recover debts as quickly as possible without causing severe financial hardship
  • Empathy can reduce customers anguish, stress on staff and time spent negotiating a repayment arrangement
  • If trust is established customers may respond more positively, and be more likely to commit to an acceptable payment arrangement

10

Recovery practices - Stage 2 + Read more ...

Stage 2 - Expectations

  • Expectation is the debt will be paid in full by the due date
  • If the customer cannot do this, they should contact Services Australia or use self service to make an arrangement
  • Decisions based on individual circumstances are vital for successful recovery. This cannot be done if payment in full is not first raised with the customer
  • If a customer's finances improve and they are able to pay their debt in full or increase their instalment arrangement we would expect them to do so

For more information, see Negotiation of debt repayments with customers.

11

Working within the guidelines - Stage 3 + Read more ...

Stage 3 - How to work within these guidelines:

  • Account Payable notices are non-judgemental and account-like in nature
  • Make reasonable effort to contact the customer to negotiate a suitable outcome for recovery
  • All negotiations are to start from an expectation of payment in full. There are some exclusions so staff should be careful to apply the correct guidelines. Any legislative powers, for example, garnishee, are to be used only where negotiation is unsuccessful or cannot be arranged, for example the customer cannot be contacted
  • The procedural guidelines will identify whether a decision is discretionary. There should be no belief that discretion may be used unless stated
