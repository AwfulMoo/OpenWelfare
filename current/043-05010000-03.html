TT Line claims

Assessing claims for TT Line

Table 1: This table details the process for assessing claims received from TT line.

Step

Action

1

Claim received + Read more ...

  • A hard copy of the claim will be received by post. Ensure the hard copy is signed and witnessed by an authorised person from TT line
  • Check vehicles and rebate are correct against voyage details and shipment sheet

2

Claim spreadsheet + Read more ...

  • An excel file will also be emailed to the Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme (TFES) Support inbox. Save a copy of the excel spreadsheet as the 'claim original'. This is found and saved in the Bass Strait Vehicle Equalisation Scheme folder
  • Open saved 'claim original' and save another copy as 'claim copy' in the same folder
  • Close the original

3

TT Line Summary Template + Read more ...

Open the TT Line Summary Template found in the Bass Strait Vehicle Equalisation Scheme folder:

  • Copy the full template by highlighting the entire document, for example use [Ctrl A] and then [Ctrl C]
  • On the claim copy, select the last tab (sheet 1) and paste template into cell A1 of this worksheet, for example use [Ctrl V]
  • Rename tab to TT Line Summary

4

TT Line Summary sheet + Read more ...

Complete the fields in the TT Line Summary sheet from the spreadsheets provided with the claim form.

  • Number of Actual Bookings completed = Number of Vehicles from the actual Voyage details
  • Less Advance Previously Paid = Amount paid in $ on the previous Voyage details, plus current schedule bookings = Scheduled Voyage details in $
  • Actual rebate applicable = $ from the Actual Voyage Details

Note: the total rebate claimed should match the amount claimed by TT Line on the reimbursement claim form.

Ensure to check for any driver declarations and adjust if necessary.

See Step 1 of the TT Line payment procedure table.

TT Line payment

TT Line payment procedure

Table 2: This table details information on how to process a TT Line claim.

Step

Action

1

Request to Pay form + Read more ...

Complete the Request to Pay form for TT Line found in the Bass Strait Vehicle Equalisation Scheme folder.

Print one copy of the completed form:

  • Sign the completed form as the 'Requesting Officer'
  • The form must also be signed by the Team Leader in 'Authorisers Signature'

Once signed make a printed copy of the Request to Pay form and the top three pages of the claim.

2

Completed forms to be taken to Finance Team + Read more ...

The original of the Request to Pay form and copies of the three pages of the claim to be hand delivered to the Finance Team at Corporate Services, Hobart.

Note: as documents need to be scanned by the Finance Team, attach documents together using a paper clip only.

3

Tasmanian Transport Team action + Read more ...

The Tasmanian Transport Programs (TTP) team is to keep a copy of the Request to Pay form and originals of all other documents.

Documents to be filed in TT Line folder in compactus.

4

Rejected amounts + Read more ...

If there are any rejected amounts, send Bass Strait Passenger Vehicle Equalisation Scheme (BSPVES) rejection letter (Z2219) to TT Line notifying them of the details. The Z2219 is located in the Bass Strait Vehicle Equalisation Scheme folder.

See Step 1 of the TT Line recording a claim table.

SeaRoad claims

Assessing claims for SeaRoad

Table 4: This table details the process for assessing claims received from SeaRoad.

Step

Action

1

Hardcopy of claim + Read more ...

A hardcopy of the claim will arrive via post and should contain the relevant documentation:

  • two invoices - BSPVES Rebate Invoice and Administration fee invoice
  • Voyage Details Sheet
  • Shipments Spreadsheet
  • Signed and Witnessed Declaration

Other Documents received are:

  • Shipments for month spreadsheet
  • Drivers Declarations
  • SeaRoad Consignment Note
  • Airline Ticket (not compulsory to include)

2

Driver check + Read more ...

Check drivers against Shipments Spreadsheet received from claimant.

For any drivers from a previous month, verify they have not been paid by checking claimant file.

3

Driver details + Read more ...

Check driver details:

  • Vehicle type (make sure this is a passenger vehicle, not a prime mover or truck on commercial trip)
  • Vehicle registration number (ensure this is the same on the declaration form and SeaRoad consignment note)
  • Check the date the vehicle was shipped and the flight date of the passenger. If the passenger flew:
    • within a few days the claim can be approved
    • a week before or after the shipping date an explanation should be provided
  • Check the driver's declaration box or ring SeaRoad to follow up any discrepancies of 14 days or more

4

Voyage details + Read more ...

Check voyage details match number of shipments on spreadsheet.

Note: caravans are inclusive of the car and should not be included in the 'Motor Cars' column of the spreadsheet.

5

Administration fee invoice + Read more ...

The administration fee invoice should be calculated at a weekly rate and invoiced monthly.

Ensure the correct number of weeks is invoiced noting: March, June, September and December will be invoiced for a five week period, all other months should be invoiced at four weeks.

See Step 1 of the SeaRoad payment procedure table.

SeaRoad payment

SeaRoad payment procedure

Table 5: This table information on how to process a SeaRoad claim.

Step

Action

1

Searoad Request to Pay form + Read more ...

Complete the SeaRoad Request to Pay form and the Administration Fee form found in the Bass Strait Vehicle Equalisation Scheme folder:

  • Print both forms, sign the completed form as the 'Requesting Officer'
  • Both claims must also be signed by the Team Leader in 'Authorisers Signature'

Once signed make a copy of both Request to Pay forms and the top four pages of the claim.

2

Completed forms to be taken to Finance Team + Read more ...

The original copy of the Request to Pay form and copies of the four pages of the claim are to be hand delivered to the Finance Team in Corporate Services, Hobart. Keep the Request to Pay form and the Administration Fee form separate.

Note: as these documents are to be scanned by the Finance Team, attach documents together using paper clip only.

3

Tasmanian Transport Team action + Read more ...

Tasmanian Transport Program (TTP) team must keep a copy of the Request to Pay form and originals of all other documents.

Documents are to be filed in the SeaRoad folder in the compactus.

See Step 1 of the SeaRoad recording a claim table.

SeaRoad claim recording

SeaRoad recording a claim

Table 6: This table shows details on how and where to record a claim once processed.

Step

Action

1

Bass Strait Passenger Vehicle Equalisation Scheme (BSPVES) spreadsheet + Read more ...

Go to the BSPVES expenditure spreadsheet.

On the expenditure tab scroll down to the relevant claim number and add:

  • Date Range
  • Amount including the administration fee (excluding GST)

Note: cross check the date range for the correct number of weeks claimed against the administration fee.

2

Monthly report tab + Read more ...

On the monthly report tab:

  • under the relevant claim number enter data regarding the vehicles from the 'Voyage Details' sheet
  • transfer the total to the total paid column
  • enter the administration fee (exclusive of GST)

Note: SAP payment details will be entered into the spreadsheet by the Service Support Officer (SSO).

3

Action once information in expenditure spreadsheet + Read more ...

Once information is added to the spreadsheet and the documentation hand delivered to Finance Team in Corporate Services, Hobart, send an e-mail to Tasmanian Transport Programme Team advising the amounts paid.
