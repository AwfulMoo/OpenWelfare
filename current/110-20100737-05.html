Social work referral

Item

Description

1

Sensitive customer indicators

Use judgement when reviewing the record. Consult with team leader before escalating.

Age at the time of death

Younger deceased customers under 30 are more likely to have died in sudden or traumatic circumstances (e.g. car crash, industrial accident etc.).

Medical conditions

See MC screen.

Some medical conditions may represent particular vulnerabilities the customer had at the time of their death.

Customers with mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety should be considered sensitive.

Previous documents indicating social work contact

See DL screen

Documents may show that the customer received social work assistance from the agency. If so, there may be relevant information on their circumstances recorded in the Social Work Information System.

Income Management/Cashless Debit Card arrangements

If the record shows the customer was on income management, these should be flagged as sensitive for further review.

Other reasons

Deceased customer has other sensitivities which warrant social work referral. If escalated, these reasons must be provided.

2

Executor or administrator identified at risk of harm during call

Tell the person:
‘I will get someone trained to help you’

Refer the call to the Social Work Line (warm hand-off) on 171 522. Select Option 1 for priority access to a social worker.

For more information, see Customers talking about suicide or self-harm,

Once referred to the Social Work Line, escalate the following via email to CUSTOMERCOMPLIANCE.SERVICEDESK@servicesaustralia.gov.au and DL.CMC.WLMMGT:

  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Amount of refund payable
  • Details of executor or administrator and their relationship to the deceased (if known)
  • Reason for escalation
    • Executor or administrator was referred immediately to social work for a risk of harm support, or
    • Phone call highlights sensitivities which meant refund couldn’t occur (give reasons

This will ensure the refund process can be progressed after Social Work Line help.

3

Sensitivities identified - no immediate risk of harm during call

If sensitivities are identified during the call but there is no immediate risk of harm, the refund can still be processed.

Offer the person social work referral or community options.

If the person accepts the social work referral:

  • Refer the call to a Payment and Integrity social work if available
  • If they are not available, use the Social Work guided procedure to refer the person using the referral reason Grief and Loss

For more information, see Social Work Referrals and Social Work Role in Payments and Integrity.
