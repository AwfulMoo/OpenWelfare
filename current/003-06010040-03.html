If a child is in immediate danger or risk of harm you must act, and a delegate must call 000. If you have concerns about a child’s safety, conduct the risk and referral process.

Note: it is acknowledged that each interaction with a customer talking about suicide or self-harm or harm to others is different. Therefore, Service Officers are to follow the steps outlined below to the best of their ability. If unable to follow the steps in order because of the customer’s presentation and needs, the Service Officer is expected to undertake each action in an order that best meets the needs of the customer, to make sure they are safe.

Step

Action

A customer talks about suicide or harming themselves + Read more ...

  • remain calm
  • listen carefully and do not dismiss any information they share
  • allow the customer time to express themselves
  • say 'I will get someone who is trained to help you'

For an interpreter, phone an 'on demand' interpreter. For contact details, see Language Services.

If the malicious call handling (MCH) button is available on the telephony, use the MCH process in these circumstances before the caller ends the call. See the Responding to phone threats to harm staff or agency property table in Customer aggression - Response. See the Resources page for a link to the iWS Softphone Quick Reference Guide.

2

Refer the customer to Social Work Services + Read more ...

Warm transfer the call to a social worker on the Social Work Line. Choose 'option 1' from the IVR list for priority access to a social worker.

Go to Step 4.

If the call cannot be transferred or occurs outside business hours:

Go to Step 3.

3

Consider transferring to triple zero (000) + Read more ...

Ask the customer for consent to contact police or ambulance calling triple zero (000). Does the customer consent?

  • Yes, ask them for:
    • their location
    • phone number, and
    • their consent to share this information with police or ambulance. When transferring the call to police or ambulance, give the customer’s location and phone number. See Table 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Escalate to Team Leader/Manager + Read more ...

Notify a Team Leader/Manager of any critical incident involving suicide or self-harm. Contact a Team Leader or Manager immediately to consider a release of information if:

  • a customer terminates the call before they can be warm transferred to a social worker
  • a customer declines a referral to police or ambulance or other support services (or withholds consent to release their location and phone number)

  • Contact the service centre social worker and ask them to see the customer immediately

Let the customer speak to another social worker using a phone in a private area. This may be:

  • another social worker in the area (for contact numbers, see the Resources page)

    choose 'option 1' from the IVR list for priority access to a social worker

If the customer cannot be referred to a social worker by phone:

Support them to call a relevant support service. See:

  • Resources page for relevant contact numbers

Go to Step 3.

Getting support after a difficult situation + Read more ...

Contact with customers talking about suicide and self-harm can be distressing and it is normal to have a physical or emotional response.

Staff affected by a difficult situation should speak to a Team Leader or Manager who can provide support.

Releasing information in the public interest for customers in Australia - Urgent + Read more ...

Centrelink

  • Service Managers, Team Leaders, Service Support Managers, Assessors (APS6) and Social Workers (Social Workers APS5 and above), have delegation to release information if it is necessary to prevent or lessen a threat to the life, health or welfare of a person to relevant authority
