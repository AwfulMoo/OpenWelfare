This page contains additional information to assist with completing the Claim for ABSTUDY Remote Areas (SY092).

Complex sections of the SY092

Item

Description

1

Identity confirmation + Read more ...

Customers claiming ABSTUDY may be required to provide Identity Confirmation. However, some ABSTUDY customers may not be required to provide Identity Confirmation in some circumstances.

2

Customer details + Read more ...

Aboriginality

For ABSTUDY purposes, an Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander is someone who:

  • is of Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander descent
  • identifies themselves as an Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander, and
  • is accepted as such by the community in which they live

The declaration on the claim will normally suffice, however for more information, see Aboriginality or Torres Strait Islander status for ABSTUDY purposes.

Address

The permanent family home address must be provided.

3

Student details + Read more ...

The customer is required to provide details of their study. The customer can obtain this information from their education institution.

Unable to provide school details

A secondary boarding facility may need to conduct an interview or selection exercise prior to accepting a student. Therefore, it may not be possible at the time of claiming to confirm the school the customer will be attending. If this is the case, the claim can be accepted without study details. The customer must tell Services Australia their study details as soon as possible.

4

Living away from home + Read more ...

For ABSTUDY, living away from home means that the customer is not living at the permanent family home to study. This can include:

  • boarding in another person's home, or
  • boarding at a hostel or secondary boarding school

A dependant customer must be living away from home as a result of one of the circumstances listed in this section to qualify for the away from home rate of ABSTUDY. The form advises the appropriate statements, documents or letters required to support this claim.

Parents of students living in private board accommodation during term must complete all the Away from home details question set. If parents are unable to complete all the required questions, issue the ABSTUDY request for private board provider details form.

Rent Assistance may be payable depending on the customer's circumstances. For further information, see Qualification for payment of Rent Assistance (RA).

5

Parent/Guardian details + Read more ...

If parents/guardians are unable to complete these questions or would prefer to provide their details separately, a MOD JY must be issued. The Resources page contains a link to this form.

Parental Income Test (PIT) exemption

The income test for the customer's parents/guardians is waived when a PIT exemption for ABSTUDY can be applied.

Customers who claim between September and December must supply information for both BASE and current tax years. See Completing Parent(s)/Guardian(s) details for the Base Tax Year and Current Tax Year (MOD JY).

6

Payment details + Read more ...

ABSTUDY payees

There are specific payee requirements for ABSTUDY payments. See How and to whom ABSTUDY payments are made.

Customers who have genuine barriers in providing bank account details can be granted a 28 day grace period. See Payment destination for more information.

7

Tax File Number (TFN) + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice must make a TFN declaration if they are 16 years of age or over.

Customers who do not have, or know their TFN, must be given a Tax file number application or enquiry for Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander (NAT1589) form for completion and lodgement within 14 days of lodging their claim.

See Tax File Number (TFN) for ABSTUDY for further information.

8

Statement/declaration + Read more ...

The student must sign the declaration if they are 16 or turning 16 years of age during the study year.

The student's parent/guardian and their partner (if applicable) must sign the declaration in order for their claim to be accepted and determined.

Note: in certain circumstances, only one parent/guardian signature is necessary to assess the customer's entitlement.

Students turning 16 years of age who are already receiving the living away from home rate of ABSTUDY are not required to sign the declaration.
