Basic Boarding Allowance (BA) and Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA)

Step

Action

1

Applying for Boarding Allowance (BA) and Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA) + Read more ...

Customer is applying for the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme BA and ABA or has provided evidence of boarding fees.

Provide general advice (based on information given by the customer) over the phone. Encourage the customer to test their eligibility, particularly in cases of discretionary decisions.

Are general AIC Scheme eligibility criteria met for the applicant and student?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, tell the applicant they are not eligible for AIC Scheme allowances for the student. Check eligibility for other payments for students. Procedure ends here

2

Approved boarding arrangement + Read more ...

Is the student living away from home in an approved board arrangement during the school term?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Disruption by COVID-19 + Read more ...

Was the customer's boarding arrangement disrupted by COVID-19:

  • Yes, in:
    • Term 2, 3 or 4 2020 or Term 3 or 4 2021, go to Step 4
    • Term 1 or 2 2021, go to Step 5
  • No, advise the applicant they are not eligible for BA for the student. Check eligibility for other AIC Scheme allowances:
    • Second Home Allowance
    • Distance Education Allowance
    • Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
    • Procedure ends here

4

Boarding arrangements disrupted by COVID-19 in Term 2, 3 or 4 2020 or Term 3 or 4 2021 + Read more ...

Students who meet the following conditions may be eligible for assistance:

  • approved for boarding entitlements in the term immediately prior to the impacted term
  • their board provider holds a boarding place for them for the impacted term
  • they intend to return to board as soon as possible, and
  • the school/board provider continues to support the student study remotely using other methods, such as online

Did the student meet the above conditions?

  • Yes:
    • The student remains eligible for boarding entitlements under Section 5.2.1.4, Coronavirus special provisions for boarding students in the Assistance for Isolated Children Scheme Guidelines. See the References page for a link
    • Go to Step 7
  • No:
    • AIC is not payable
    • Check eligibility for other payments
    • Procedure ends here

5

Boarding arrangements disrupted by COVID-19 in Term 1 and 2 2021 + Read more ...

Students unable to attend face to face studies, due to COVID-19 impacts may be eligible to be paid once the:

  • COVID-19 impacts are confirmed, and
  • the student begins study on campus

Evidence can include one of the following:

  • A letter from the board provider or advice from the education provider website confirming reduced capacity at boarding place
  • A medical practitioner confirming health related impacts. For example, an autoimmune disorder
  • State or territory education department websites confirming school closures
  • State or territory websites confirming travel restrictions

Is the student unable to attend face to face studies due to COVID-19 impacts?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No:
    • AIC is not payable
    • Check eligibility for other payments
    • Procedure ends here

6

Boarding arrangements disrupted by confirmed COVID-19 impacts in Term 1 or 2 2021 + Read more ...

If the applicant is already receiving AIC, suspend the AIC payment through the Benefit Action (BA) screen:

  • Reason: field, OTH - Other
  • Event Date: field, as appropriate:
    • Term in Advance already paid: Term 1 – 1 April, Term 2 – 1 July, Term 3 – 1 October
    • Term in Advance not already paid: Term 1 – 1 January, Term 2 – 1 April, Term 3 – 1 July, Term 4 – 1 October
    • Fortnightly entitlements: Date paid to +1

Advise the applicant the payment has been suspended due to the student not attending away from home, face to face (boarding) studies at the start of Term 1 or 2 2021.

  • The payment can be restored, once the:
    • student has returned to away from home, face to face (boarding) studies, and
    • COVID-19 impacts have been confirmed
  • Issue a manual cancellation letter Q134 to the applicant, see Creating a Q134 letter. Reason for Decision text:
    • 'the student has/will (delete as appropriate) not returned to face to face boarding studies within 14 days of the school start date'
  • Once the applicant confirms the student has returned to away from home, face to face boarding studies, and the evidence has been provided confirming COVID-19 impacts:
    • restore the AIC payment, and
    • expire the DOA DOC on the students record

Note: if the student does not return to away from home, face to face studies in Term 1 2021, but does return to away from home, face to face studies in a later term for example Term 2, 3 or 4, 2021, cancel the payment.

If the applicant has lodged a claim for AIC, reject the claim OTH - Other (AIC Scheme guidelines 5.2). See Rejecting an Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme claim.

  • Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the applicant to tell them:
    • the claim has been rejected as the student is not attending away from home, face to face (boarding) studies at the start of the relevant term
    • the claim can be reassessed, once they contact to advise the student has returned to away from home, face to face (boarding) studies, and
    • Once the COVID-19 impacts have been confirmed:
  • Issue a manual rejection letter Q134 to the applicant. See Creating a Q134 letter. Reason for Decision text:
    • 'the student has/will (delete as appropriate) not returned to face to face boarding studies within 14 days of the school start date'
  • Once the applicant confirms the student has returned to away from home, face to face boarding studies in the relevant term during 2021, and the evidence has been provided confirming COVID-19 impacts:
    • reassess the AIC claim, and
    • expire the DOA DOC

Note: if the student does not return to away from home, face to face studies in Term 1 2021, but does return to away from home, face to face studies in a later term, for example Term 2, 3 or 4, 2021:

  • reassess the claim from the date the student returns to face to face studies
  • the applicant does not need to lodge a new claim

Record details in a Display on Access (DOA) DOC, via the Fast Note guided procedure:

  • Category 1: Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices
  • Category 2: AIC
  • Template: AIC Remote Learning (COVID-19)
  • select Confirm
  • record all relevant details and fields
  • select Complete

Procedure ends here.

7

Responsible for the costs of maintaining a residence + Read more ...

Are the student's parents or other dependent sibling/s bearing the costs of maintaining the residence or location where the student lives during the study term?

  • Yes, the student is not in a boarding arrangement and the applicant is not eligible for BA/ABA for the student. However, the family may be maintaining what is in effect a second home - check eligibility for AIC Scheme Second Home Allowance. See the Resources page for examples of Boarding Allowance versus Second Home Allowance
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Disability Support Pensioner (DSP) or Parenting Payment Single (PPS) + Read more ...

Is the student receiving DSP or PPS?

  • Yes, and the student is studying:
    • at the primary or equivalent ungraded level, the applicant is not eligible for BA for the student, however, the AIC Scheme Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) is payable
    • at the secondary or tertiary level, they are not eligible for the AIC Scheme but may be eligible for PES
    • procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

9

In State care including foster care + Read more ...

Is the student in State care (for example, foster care)?

  • Yes, a foster care allowance (or similar) is being received by the applicant for the student and the AIC applicant is:
    • a person, Basic BA is payable only
    • an organisation, AIC is not payable. Check eligibility for other payments. Procedure ends here
  • No, the applicant is eligible for BA for the student, go to Step 10

10

Additional Boarding Allowance + Read more ...

Has the applicant indicated they are applying for ABA and all relevant information has been provided, including Parental Income Test (PIT) data?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, only BA is payable. Go to Step 15

11

Actual boarding charges + Read more ...

Where boarding charges are left blank, an attempt must be made to contact the applicant by phone to obtain this information verbally. DOC the conversation or contact attempt on the customer's record.

Did the applicant provide boarding fees on their claim, review or verbally?

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, only BA is payable:
    • in the Living Arrangement Details (EILA) screen, key $1 in the Annual Boarding Fees field
    • go to Step 15

12

Verifying boarding charges + Read more ...

Some government residential schools/colleges have boarding charges less than the combined annual rate of Basic BA and maximum ABA. This means the level of the charges could affect the amount of ABA payable. Private boarding schools and government agricultural schools/colleges generally have higher boarding charges that attract the maximum ABA.

Boarding charges usually increase each year, but boarding schools will sometimes reduce boarding charges for each additional boarding child enrolled in a family. Some schools may provide information on their website about fees and discounts. Service Officers must check the school's website to attempt to validate boarding costs stated by the applicant, where the amount stated exceed the maximum annual rate of Basic BA less $250 for incidentals.

For the current rate of Basic BA, see Rates and thresholds > Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) - Basic Boarding Allowance (BA).

Does the annual actual boarding charges for the student, advised by the applicant, exceed the maximum annual rate of Basic BA, less $250 for incidentals?

  • Yes, and the provider's website:
    • lists boarding charges that exceed this threshold, accept the lower amount advised by the applicant. Go to Step 14
    • lists boarding charges that are below this threshold, basic BA is payable only. Accept the lower amount as displayed on the website. Ask the applicant to provide verification of boarding charges. Go to Step 13
    • does not state boarding charges, basic BA is payable only. Update the Living Arrangement Details (EILA) screen, key $1 in the Annual Boarding Fees field. Go to Step 13
  • No, Basic BA is payable only. Accept the boarding charges stated by the applicant. Go to Step 13

13

Request applicant to verify boarding fees + Read more ...

Verification of boarding fees must be requested where the applicant:

  • has not provided boarding fees on their claim, review or verbally
  • has provided boarding fees that exceed the maximum annual rate of Basic BA, less $250 for incidentals, and the provider's website
  • does not state boarding charges, or
  • lists boarding charges that are less than the amount stated by the applicant

On the student's record, send a Q999 AIC - Additional Boarding Allowance - Request for Boarding Fees letter to the applicant.

Record details of request and contact attempt/s on a DOC.

Pending verification, Basic BA is payable only. Go to Step 15.

14

Parental Income Test + Read more ...

Does the income of the parent comply with the Parental Income Test?

  • Yes, go to Step 16
  • No, Basic BA is payable only. Go to Step 15

15

Basic BA payable only + Read more ...

Tell the applicant to lodge a Claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) (SY040) if they have not already applied.

BA can be paid for the full calendar year. Pro-rata entitlement applies if a student is not eligible for the full year. For example, for short-term boarding or where eligibility commences during the year.

For AIC Scheme rate information, see Rates and thresholds > Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC). For students boarding:

  • full-time - at least 4 nights per week, the maximum rate is payable
  • part-time - 3 nights per week, the rate is based on the number of nights boarding per week. For example, if boarding 3 nights per week, 3/7 of the maximum rate is payable
  • short-term, the rate is based on the actual period boarding. BA is paid as a lump sum, either at the start of the boarding period if known, or after the boarding period ends

BA is paid:

  • in Term-in-advance instalments for eligible students boarding at a boarding school or school term hostel
  • fortnightly in arrears, for eligible students boarding privately

Record details on a DOC.

16

ABA annual rate + Read more ...

The maximum rate of ABA payable is determined by the Parental Income Test.

The system will auto-calculate ABA entitlement. This is done by adding the ABA annual rate payable (based on the Parental Income Test) to the BA entitlement and comparing with the level of annual actual boarding charges, plus $250 and paying the lesser amount.

Note: the student's actual BA and ABA entitlement cannot exceed the maximum rate of the combined BA and ABA. See Rates and thresholds > Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC).

17

BA and ABA payable + Read more ...

Tell the customer to lodge a Claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) (SY040) if they have not already applied.

BA/ABA can be paid for the full calendar year. Pro-rata entitlement applies if a student is not eligible for the full year. For example, for short-term boarding or where eligibility commences during the year.

For AIC Scheme rate information, see Rates and thresholds > Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC). For students boarding:

  • full-time - at least 4 nights per week, the maximum rate is payable (BA plus ABA rate subject to the Parental Means Test)
  • part-time - less than 4 nights per week, the rate is based on the number of nights boarding per week. For example, if boarding 3 nights per week, 3/7 of the maximum rate is payable
  • short-term, the rate is based on the actual period boarding. BA and ABA is paid as a lump sum, either at the start of the boarding period if known, or after the boarding period ends

BA/ABA is paid:

  • in Term-in-advance instalments for eligible students boarding at a boarding school or school term hostel
  • fortnightly in arrears, for eligible students boarding privately

Record details on a DOC.
