Common Medicare ART services enquiries

Table 1: this table contains common enquiries about Medicare benefits for Assisted Reproductive Technology (ART) services.

Item

Description

1

Will a claim for item 13200 with a date of service AFTER the date of service for item 13202 in the same calendar year attract a Medicare benefit?

Answer:

No, item 13200 can only be used as the initial superovulated treatment cycle in a calendar year where the cycle results in successful oocyte retrieval. When the first cycle for the calendar year is cancelled prior to oocyte retrieval, item 13202 should be used for the initial superovulated treatment cycle.

If item 13202 is claimed with a date of service prior to item 13200, 13202 becomes the initial cycle and item 13200 cannot be claimed with a later date of service.

If the date of service for item 13200 is before the date of service for item 13202, override the restriction with processing indicator PI 45. Note: if item 13202 is within 26 days before item 13200, the claim would also need text or notation indicating item 13200 was a ‘short cycle’ to be payable

2

Can items 13212 (oocyte retrieval) and 13215 (transfer of embryos or both ova and sperm) be billed as outpatient?

Answer:

Yes, items 13212 and 13215 can be billed as either an inpatient or out patient service.

3

Can a claim for a global item be paid within 26 days of the previous global item?

Answer:

Yes, if appropriate text has been provided, text or notations stating ‘short cycle’ or similar wording, restrictions can be overridden using PI 45. If no text is provided, reject all items in the claim with reason code (RSN) 308.

4

Are Medicare benefits payable for a donor under ART items 13200, 13201 and 13212 where a recipient has not yet been identified?

Answer:

No, the identification of a medically infertile recipient is a precondition of the provision of ART services under Medicare. If no recipient has been identified, then Medicare benefits for ART services, including MBS items 13200, 13201 or 13212, are not available to a donor donating their ova.

5

Can item 13218 be paid when it restricts with item 13212 on history and consequently item 13251 also on history?

Answer:

There is a current system restriction in place as the descriptor for item 13218 states ‘excluding a service to which item 13200, 13201, 13202, 13203, 13212 applies'.

If item 13212 has been performed within 26 days of the next treatment cycle commencing, in this case item 13218, the system looks backwards and forwards for all associated item numbers and the return message 6-RST ADJ will appear.

If it can be determined that item 13212 was performed in association with the patient’s previous cycle and was claimed in conjunction with an item 13200 or 13201, the restriction can be overridden and pay item 13218 with PI 42.

Once the restriction between 13212 and 13218 has been resolved, the return message 6-RST TD will generally appear. Item 13218 will restrict with item 13251 on history which was performed prior to the item 13218. As the item was performed in association with the patient’s previous cycle the restriction can then be overridden with PI 45.

Note: if the item 13218 has been claimed less than 26 days from the previous global item that item 13212 is related to, ‘short cycle’ text will be required.

If item 13218 has been claimed more than 26 days from the global item the items 13212 and 13251 relates to, short cycle text would not be required.

6

Is a surrogate’s antenatal care, pregnancy care and post-natal care covered by Medicare?

Answer:

Yes, Medicare benefits are payable in relation to medical examinations or tests where the medical practitioner considers them to be clinically relevant for the person. A clinically relevant service is defined as a medical service that is generally accepted in the medical profession as being necessary for the appropriate treatment of the patient.

If the patient who becomes pregnant is eligible for Medicare, then clinically relevant services for the care of that patient would attract a Medicare rebate. Only the services relating to IVF are not covered by Medicare.

Common ART return messages

Table 2: this table lists return messages and their definitions applicable to claims for ART services.

Return Message

Description

3-AGE REST

Return message: 3-AGE REST

Displays when the item is restricted to a specific age group in QITI. For ART services, this restriction is not legislated and only there as a guide. If no text indicates the patient and service in the claim is correct, phone the health professional’s practice to verify.

If supportive text or phone verification is provided, override with PI 25.

6-CLMINTRP

Return message: 6-CLMINTRP

Displays when any assisted reproductive services item appears in a claim. This ensures the Service Officer uses a supervisor payment screen to assess the claim and consider all restrictions carefully.

6-RST ADJ

Return message: 6-RST ADJ

Displays when there is an item on the patient’s history that restricts with one or more items on the screen. The item on the patient’s history has a lower schedule fee than the item in the claim. Details of the restrictive item on the history displays at the bottom of the screen.

In this situation, further investigation is required to determine if the restriction displayed can be overridden, such as supporting text (e.g. ‘short cycle’).

If supportive and acceptable text has been provided, override with PI 42.

6-RST TD

Return message: 6-RST TD

Displays when there is a time restriction between items (including the same item where applicable). Details of the restrictive item on history displays at the bottom of the screen.

In this situation, further investigation is required to determine if the restriction displayed can be overridden, such as supporting text (for example, ‘short cycle’).

6-RST TD commonly appears due to the 26 day restriction period with ART services. If it is evident that the patient is undergoing a second treatment cycle within 26 days based on appropriate text being provided, claims can be overridden and paid with PI 45.

Where short cycle text is required to be provided for a claim to be paid, the service should be rejected with reason code 308 to identify to the health professional what is necessary.

6-ASC NOT

Return message: 6-ASC NOT

Displays when benefit is only payable for the highlights item where an associated (pre-requisite or co-requisite) item is either present in the claim or on the patient’s history.

If 6-ASC NOT is returned when an online claim has been transmitted by a health professional, check if there are other transmissions for the same patient by keying F2. If there are other transmissions, process all associated claims for the patient as some pre-requisite items may be split into separate claims.

Note: if the claim is for a bulk billed item, keying F2 will not display other transmitted claims. Instead, pend the claim and check back later that same day to see if the pre-requisite claim has been transmitted.

If at the end of the day there is no associated item present on the patient’s history, reject using reason code 550.

6-RST GT 2

Return message: 6-RST GT 2

Displays when an item on the screen is restrictive with at least 2 other items. The 2 other items may not restrict with each other.

8-RST CLM

Return message: 8-RST CLM

Displays when there are 2 restrictive items on the screen. The system automatically pays one item and sets the fee for the other item to zero.

8-RST HIST

Return message: 8-RST HIST

Displays when there is an item on the patient’s history that restricts with an item on the screen. The item on the patient’s history has a higher schedule fee than the item on the screen. The system automatically sets the fee for the item on the screen to zero.

8-CONFIRM

Return message: 8-CONFIRM

Family Safety Net confirmation

Manual claims – Do not process the claim. Service Officers are required to make 2 attempts by telephone to confirm the Medicare Safety Net family composition. Where contact cannot be made, Service Officers are required to return the claim with a Medicare Safety Net confirmation letter and form.

Online claims – Service Officers are required to make 2 attempts by telephone to confirm the Medicare Safety Net family composition and leave a comment in CDMS to confirm your attempts. Where contact cannot be made on the first occasion, the Service Officer is to park the claim and attempt on day 2. If unsuccessful after second call, process claim by selecting F9 and acknowledge the warning.

Common ART rejection reasons

Table 3: this table lists rejection reasons and the steps the health professional should take to resolve them.

Rejection Reason

Description

308

IVF service – conditions not met – no benefit payable

If there is a restriction occurring for item 13201, 13202 or 13218 because it has been claimed sooner than 26 days from the last global item, reason 308 signifies to health professionals that more information is required in order for the benefit to be payable.

Health professionals can note ‘short cycle’ on the account in these circumstances.

179

Benefit not payable - associated service already paid

The health professional should check that the date of service is the first day of a treatment cycle. Each treatment cycle ends no more than 30 days after this date.

When the date of service of a subsequent global item is sooner than 26 days, health professionals can note ‘short cycle’ on the account.

160

Maximum number of services for this item already paid

Item 13200 or 13202 can be used for the initial cycle in a calendar year. However if item 13202 has the earliest date of service then item 13200 cannot be claimed during that calendar year. Item 13201 is for subsequent cycles.

550

Associated service not claimed - no benefit payable

  • For item 13201 the health professional should check that either 13200 or 13202 has been claimed as the first cycle in the calendar year before billing 13201 for subsequent cycles
  • For items 13212, 13215 or 13221 the health professional should check the date of service. Each treatment cycle ends no more than 30 days after the date of the global item. When any of these item numbers have a date of service 30 days or more after the global item, health professionals can note ‘long cycle’ on the account
