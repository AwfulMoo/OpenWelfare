This page contains information on how to perform a security check when a provider calls the Veteran's Affairs Processing (VAP) National Enquiry Line, responding to provider requests for duplicate statements, and referrals and escalations.

Provider security check for VAP

Table 1: This table outlines how Service Officers and Programme Support Officers perform a security check when contacted via the VAP National Enquiry Line by a health professional or their delegate, practice staff or authorised representative from a hospital, residential care facility or approved provider organisation.

Step

Action

1

Advise security check requirement + Read more ...

Advise the caller a security check must be conducted before releasing any information.

Request caller details:

  • provider number
  • full name of the provider
  • address for the provider number supplied (the nearest corner and suburb are acceptable)

If the caller identifies themselves as DVA staff, no personal information of a veteran, provider or service is to be released. DVA staff should be contacting their own DVA General Enquiry line. Services Australia can assist DVA staff with general enquiries where the VAP Operator does not need to check any client or provider information on the system.

2

Check information provided + Read more ...

Check that the information provided matches details held in the VAP provider directory system on the Mainframe Customer Information Control System (CICS) screen by keying 'VPUI' and the provider number stem.

Are details correct?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the Service Officer should advise the caller they cannot give them any information as they did not meet the requirements of the security check. If the caller disputes this, the call should be transferred to the Team Coach. Procedure ends here

3

Identify if caller is the provider + Read more ...

Identify if the caller is the provider, or another person (practice staff, delegate or authorised representative of a hospital, residential care facility or approved provider organisation).

Is the caller the provider?

  • Yes, proceed with the enquiry. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Caller is not the provider + Read more ...

If the caller identifies as practice staff or delegate, ask the caller:

  • 'Do you have permission from the provider to request information about the claim?’

If the caller identifies as being from a hospital, residential care facility or approved provider organisation, ask the caller:

  • 'Are you an authorised representative of the organisation?'

Does the caller have permission or are they an authorised representative?

  • Yes, proceed with call. Procedure ends here
  • No, the Service Officer should advise the caller they cannot give them any information. If the caller disputes this, the call should be escalated to the Team Coach. Procedure ends here

Issuing duplicate statements to a DVA provider or their delegate

Table 2: This table describes the steps to follow to check that a statement can be issued to a provider, and the exceptional circumstances when a duplicate statement can be faxed by a Programme Support Officer (PSO).

Step

Action

1

Ensure provider cannot access via electronic channel + Read more ...

Is the provider able to access statements via an electronic channel such as online claiming or DVA Webclaim?

  • Yes, advise the provider how they can access statements. See DVA Webclaim for Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP)
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check date of issue + Read more ...

Check the date of issue for the requested statement.

Has a reasonable delivery time elapsed (approximately 10 working days)?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, advise the provider to wait several more days. Procedure ends here

3

Confirm address + Read more ...

Confirm the address where the original statement was sent. This may involve checking any pay group links. Advise the provider.

If an address change is required, advise the provider of action required by them to update their mailing address and redirect the provider to appropriate area (Provider registration or DVA for DVA only providers).

If an address change has been completed after the original statement was issued, a manual statement will need to be produced and the address amended. See Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP) for contact details if needed.

For escalation details, see Item 1 in Referrals and escalations table.

4

Create duplicate statement + Read more ...

Create a VPSI document

  • In Mainframe Customer Information Control System (CICS) screen, key the control line VCEI, PROVIDER#,CLAIMID
  • Press [Enter]

Claim details history will display.

  • Over key VCEI with 'VPSI'
  • Add 'DOL=ddmmyy' to the end of the control line
  • Add ',D' to the end of 'DOL=DDMMYY,D'
  • VPSI,PROVIDER#,CLAIM ID,DOL=DDMMYY,D and press [Enter]

Claim Statement will display.

For more information on actioning duplicate provider statements, see Payments for Veterans’ Affairs Processing (VAP).

  • For single and multiple statements, see the Reissue single and multiple statements table
  • For reissue of statements using VCEI, see the Access claims over 6 months for a provider table

5

Faxing duplicate statements + Read more ...

Programme Support Officers only

Copy claim statement into fax template:

  • open the fax duplicate statement template. See Resources page for a link.
  • copy entire statement from Mainframe
  • paste into the fax duplicate statement template

To add multiple pages of the statement to the template, in Mainframe go to the relevant page of the Claim Statement:

  • highlight the required lines to copy
  • select Edit > Copy Append from the dropdown list
  • repeat if required for each additional statement page on screen
  • paste at the end of the fax duplicate statement template

Confirm the provider’s fax number and print and send duplicate statement.

Protocols for referrals and escalations for VAP

Table 3: This table contains protocols and links to contact details for referrals and escalations for Veterans' Affairs Processing.

Item

Description

1

VAP enquiries and escalations + Read more ...

For contact details, see Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

National Enquiry Line (Tier 1)

This is the ‘front door’ for all VAP programme enquiries. VAP trained Service Officers will help with the enquiry or escalate for resolution.

VAP Programme Support Officers (PSO) (Tier 2)

VAP PSOs provide help with complex procedural, administrative or operational enquiries for the VAP Programme.

VAP Policy Section (National Office) (Tier 3)

Tier 3 administers the VAP programme governance, policies, procedures and responds to complex enquiries.

All enquiries should originate from the VAP Tier 1 or Tier 2 above where possible. See Resources page for a link to the VAP helpdesk enquiry form which can be used for Tier 3 referrals via email.

2

VAP Allied Health, Hospital or Medical enquiries + Read more ...

Refer to VAP National Enquiry Line.

For contact details, see Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

3

VAP complaints and feedback) + Read more ...

VAP Service Officers should follow agency procedures for Managing customer complaints and feedback.

4

Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) + Read more ...

For contact details, see Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)

5

DVA Webclaim + Read more ...

  • Simple enquiries should be referred to the VAP National Enquiry Line (Tier 1). For example:
    • lodging a simple claim
    • error messages
    • using the email function
    • Provider Statements
    • Claim History Reports
  • Complex enquiries unable to be resolved by the VAP National Enquiry Line should be referred to a VAP Programme Support Officer (PSO) (TIer 2)
    • technical issues
    • lodging a complex claim
    • resolving complex error messages
    • advice on mandatory field requirements
  • Enquiries unable to be resolved should be forwarded to the VAP Policy Team (National Office) (Tier 3). They do not respond to external enquiries
    • complex enquiries and technical issues
    • product and policy enquiries

For contact details, see Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP). See Resources page for a link to the VAP helpdesk enquiry form which can be used for Tier 3 referrals via email.

When responding to technical enquiries, staff should try to determine if the problem relates to DVA Webclaim or to the Health Professionals Online Service (HPOS). If the issue is with HPOS, the enquiry should be referred to the HPOS support area. For contact details, see Health Professionals Online Service (HPOS).

Overpayments and recovery (VAP) + Read more ...

14

Provider eligibility and registration (VAP) + Read more ...

DVA and Medicare providers

  • Refer simple eligibility and registration enquiries from health professionals to the VAP National Enquiry Line (Tier 1)
  • Refer complex eligibility and registration enquiries from health professionals to the Tier 2 Provider Registration team

For contact details, see Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

DVA only providers

Some providers are registered by DVA (called DVA only providers) and are not eligible for Medicare benefits. DVA maintains a database (VPUI) containing information such as provider addresses, specialty codes and registration details. DVA only provider numbers are most commonly 8 digit numbers beginning with 9.

Refer eligibility and registration enquiries to DVA for resolution, e.g. change of address, other updates.

For contact details, see Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA).
