Customer ceases or reduces work in the 2 year suspension period for Disability Support Pension (DSP) 008-03100020

Expand all


This document outlines what to do when a customer has ceased work or reduced their hours of work and their DSP is suspended, or cancelled deemed suspended, because they had returned to work. It also helps to identify if DSP can be restored or the customer needs to reclaim.

Mobility Allowance (MOB)

Check eligibility for MOB when a customer contacts in relation to reporting employment income, changing jobs or starting work.

See Change of circumstances for Mobility Allowance (MOB) customers.

Eligibility if working up to 30 hours per week

DSP customers may work up to 30 hours per week and stay on DSP as long as they remain eligible, including meeting the income test.

There may be some exceptions to this rule for people paid under an international agreement, or for people outside Australia.

Before 1 July 2012, DSP was suspended or cancelled if the customer was working 15 hours a week or more, and:

  • they were granted DSP on or after 1 July 2006, or
  • a transitional DSP customer had their DSP reviewed between 1 July 2006 and 30 June 2012

Restoring payment

When DSP is suspended, or cancelled deemed suspended, due to returning to work, the customer's payment may be restored within 2 years of the date of effect (DOE) of the suspension or cancellation if:

  • they cease work
  • they reduce their hours below 30 hours per week, or
  • their employment income no longer precludes them from payment

All other eligibility requirements for DSP must be met to restore payment. For example, the income and assets tests.

DSP is restored under the eligibility criteria that applied to the person at the time of suspension. For example, granted under the 30 hour or 15 hour rule.

Customer must have notified starting work

To be eligible for restoration of payment within 2 years, the customer must have notified Services Australia they have started work. When the customer notifies of their return to work, DSP is suspended/cancelled with one of the valid return to work reason codes.

If the agency was made aware the customer had returned to work without the customer advising, or before they advised (and outside 14 days), DSP is cancelled using a different reason code. If the customer does not have a valid return to work code, DSP cannot be reinstated.

If a DSP customer did not notify their return to work and then ceases work, they need to lodge a new claim if they wish to retest their eligibility for DSP.

Alternative payment while DSP suspended/cancelled

During the suspension or deemed suspended period, customers can receive an alternative income support payment if:

  • they are not qualified for DSP due to the number of hours they are working, and
  • their income does not preclude them from another payment

This payment must be cancelled when DSP is reinstated.

Customer has employment income with reduced hours

When the customer has employment income and still qualifies for DSP:

  • If under Age Pension age, Working Credit may enable the customer to keep some of their income support payment while they are working
  • If over Age Pension age and getting a pension (excluding PPS), they may be eligible to the Work Bonus
