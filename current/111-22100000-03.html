Business rules for the Quality Call Framework Sampling Policy

This table has details about the business rules that relate to the Quality Call Framework Sampling Policy.

Item

Description

1

Business Rule: Recorded Call Sampling Requirements + Read more ...

If call recording is implemented, a minimum of 30% of the sample size for each branch (in each quarter), must be evaluated by listening to recorded calls.

2

Business Rule: Meeting Sampling Requirements + Read more ...

Business teams must document if sampling requirements for 9 calls per quarter per staff member are not met. To meet document requirements, business teams can prepare the document at any stage during a quarter. For example, at the beginning of the quarter (pre-determined approach), during the quarter (on a continuous basis) or at the end of the quarter (capturing information on where requirements were not met).This document must:

  • provide information on the sampling quarter, the business team and its scope of work
  • provide information on the number of evaluations completed for each staff member and the variance to the sampling policy and the reason/s
  • identify, evaluate and factor in risks associated with the business teams’ decision to not undertake the quality checking sampling requirements
  • describe other quality assurance processes that are in place if relevant
  • describe the business team’s plan/strategy to meet future Quality Call Framework sampling requirements
  • be endorsed by the business team’s National Manager
  • be retained by the business team in accordance with Services Australia's Records Management policy, see References for a link

Note: when considering using the Quality Call Standards to evaluate calls outside of the Quality Call Framework scope, business teams should:

  • consider any workplace relations implications, and
  • first consult with the Workplace Relations Branch

3

Business Rule: Definition of ‘customer’ for Quality Call Framework in scope calls + Read more ...

Inbound and outbound calls that are in scope for Quality Call Framework evaluations are calls where an interaction takes place between a Services Australia staff member and an external caller/customer. The term ‘customer’ can be used generically to refer to all the members of the community the agency provides services to. While not an exhaustive list, customers for Quality Call Framework evaluation purposes include:

  • a recipient of a payment/service or their representative (a staff member who is a recipient of a payment could be a customer if making a call about their payment - ‘staff as customers’)
  • a past recipient of a payment/service
  • a potential recipient of a payment/service (that is, individuals or their representatives enquiring about the possibility of receiving a payment/service)
  • a customer of Child Support or their representative
  • a member of the public, for example, reporting suspected fraud
  • a customer, external to the agency, who is transferred to gain technical advice, for example, to a technical support officer. (This does not include a staff member speaking to a technical support officer to gain advice only)
  • a third party, for example:
    • a call to an employer or an insurer where a staff member may be requesting information
    • a call to another government agency or community service worker to support individuals with payment-related matters. This does not include calls booking an appointment, for example, with an interpreter service or reception
    • a call to a medical provider
