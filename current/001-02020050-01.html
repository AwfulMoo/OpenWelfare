Eligibility and registration as a No Stream Services Required (NSSRR)/No Remote Referral Required (NRRR) job seeker 001-02020050

This document explains details about eligibility and registration for No Stream Services Referral Required (NSSRR) job seekers.

Definition of NSSRR

NSSRR customers need to be registered as job seekers in order to access other help, but do not want to volunteer to participate in jobactive. As a result, they are not referred to a provider and do not have access to Employment Provider Services.

Job seekers in Community Development Program (CDP) are not eligible for NSSRR, however they may be eligible for No Remote Referral Required (NRRR).

Eligibility is determined by the Department of Education, Skills and Employment system.

Eligibility for registration as NSSRR/NRRR job seeker

Customers eligible to be registered as NSSRR/NRRR include:

  • customers who need a Department of Education, Skills and Employment record in order to be referred for an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) such as customers claiming or receiving Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • full-time students applying for a supplementary allowance determination
  • full-time students receiving Youth Allowance (YA) or Austudy who are transferring to a job seeker payment, such as JobSeeker Payment (JSP), and contact prior to ceasing studies but within 4 weeks of their Student End Date (SED)
  • principal carers with long term exemptions from their mutual obligation requirements (greater than 16 weeks) who do not choose to volunteer with employment services
  • certain customers receiving JSP, YA (job seekers), Parenting Payment (PP) (with mutual obligation requirements) who are meeting their requirements and do not wish to volunteer for employment services, but require a Job Plan as part of their eligibility for income support payment. This includes:
    • principal carers already meeting their mutual obligation requirements
    • people with a Partial Capacity to Work of 15 hours or more per week who are fully meeting their participation requirements
    • people with a Partial Capacity to Work of 0-14 hours per week who are considered volunteers in employment services
    • customers in receipt of an income support payments that have no mutual obligation requirements and who do not choose to volunteer with employment services are assessed as eligible for child Care Subsidy (CCS). If they want to apply for Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work) they will need to be registered as NSSRR/NRRR job seekers in order for a voluntary Job Plan to be negotiated prior to applying for ACCS (Transition to Work)
    • New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS) participants presenting to Services Australia after the 39 weeks of participation in the Scheme to claim income support

Management of registration as NSSRR

Customers are registered for NSSRR/NRRR using either the Participation Interview standalone Looking for Work (LFW) workflow, and selecting appropriate responses. However, if a job seeker has already connected to a provider, they cannot also be registered as NSSRR/NRRR.

An NSSRR/NRRR registration lasts as long as it is appropriate, depending on the job seeker's circumstances. If job seekers do not meet the criteria for NSSRR/NRRR registration at any time, such as a principal carer no longer meeting their mutual obligation requirements, the NSSRR/NRRR flag must be removed. The customer's registration is changed to Fully Eligible Participant (FEP)/Remote Fully Eligible (RFE) and they are referred to a provider, if suitable. NSSRR applies only to the current period of registration and the flag must be removed before re-registration, even if it continues to be NSSRR.

Where a NSSRR job seeker chooses to be assessed to determine eligibility for additional services and allowances, a Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) is to be completed so the job seeker can be referred to a suitable provider. A JSCI is not necessary for NRRR job seekers except where an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) is required.
