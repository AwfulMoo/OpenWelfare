FAQs - reporting via self service options

Item

Description

1

Question: What happens if the customer cannot complete their usual report on time? + Read more ...

Answer: Customers cannot report before their reporting date, unless their payment is cancelled before the reporting date.

If the customer cannot report online, through the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app, by phone or in person by 5 pm (local time) on their reporting date, or they report late, their payment may be delayed.

The customer cannot report online if it is more than 14 days after their usual reporting date, as their payment would have cancelled.

If the customer cannot report online because their payment is cancelled, they must contact Services Australia to query whether their payment can be restored or whether they need to reclaim their payment.

See Restoration of payments.

2

Question: What happens when a customer's payment has been granted or restored and there are outstanding reports? + Read more ...

Answer: When a customer has had their payment granted or restored, they will be able to report online or through the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app where there are up to 6 outstanding reporting periods.

If the customer cannot report because there are more than 6 outstanding reporting periods, they must attend a service centre or call Services Australia for a Service Officer to process the reports.

3

Question: Does the customer need to report the hours worked? + Read more ...

Answer: If the customer is receiving 1 of the following payments, they must include hours worked:

  • Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP)
  • Parenting Payment Single (PPS)
  • Carer Payment (CP)
  • Mobility Allowance (MOB)
  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
  • Special Benefit (SpB)
  • Youth Allowance (YA) (job seekers only)

Carer Payment customers must provide the number of hours and minutes worked each week, plus travel time.

When reporting hours online, the customer must round the hours up to the nearest whole hour, e.g. 4.75 hours worked is reported as 5 hours. The process of rounding hours does not affect the rate of income support payment.

DSP customers only must round down to the nearest hour, e.g. 4.5 hours worked is reported as 4 hours. The process of rounding hours will not affect the rate of DSP.

When reporting hours worked online, the service can accept up to 336 hours.

See Recording and correcting employment income details and Digital Support Products, for demonstration.

4

Question: How does the customer know Services Australia has recorded details? + Read more ...

Answer: Once the customer has submitted their details online, they get a receipt which shows:

  • a receipt number
  • the next payment amount
  • the date of their payment
  • the next reporting date
  • their student Income Bank balance or Working Credit balance, if applicable
  • any Services Australia appointments due for the customer

They should print the receipt or record the receipt details in case they need to contact the agency about their payment.

See Digital support products.

5

Question: What should the customer do if someone else knows their password? + Read more ...

Answer: The customer should contact the agency immediately.

It is the customer's responsibility to keep their password secret to ensure they get the correct payment.

The agency assumes the customer has authorised any transaction using their credentials to access their online account.

If the customer suspects someone else knows their password, they should ring the Online Services Support Hotline or complete the Online Services Help form on the Services Australia website and enter their contact details and a brief description of the problem.

Service Officers can lock the customer's online access in certain circumstances.

For more information, including contact details for the helpdesk, see Troubleshooting Centrelink self service.

6

Question: Can customers record income or hours from self-employment or sales commission in this service? + Read more ...

Answer: No, income or hours from self employment cannot be recorded in the REI service.

Job seekers who advise they are self-employed, must first be assessed by a Service Officer about whether they are considered self-employed under the agency's definition of self-employment.

Additionally, customers are unable to report online for self-employment/sales commission or contract income that has been assessed and recorded as other income on the Other Income Summary (OINS) screen.

For self-employment: Once an initial assessment has occurred and the customer is determined to be self-employed, details of their self-employment income should be recorded on the Real Estate/Business Summary (REBS) screen. Customers will not be able to declare self-employment income and/or make changes to existing self-employment income via their online account.

After the initial assessment has been completed, a hand-off will only occur if the customer advises the income that has been coded and assessed is incorrect, regardless of whether the customer is scheduled to provide a profit and loss statement or not.

The customer can report any employment income via their online account.

For sales commission or contract payments: When a new source of contract or commission income is advised, an initial assessment is required by a Service Officer to determine assessment of income.

If received for a period equal to or less than 2 weeks, these should be added to any other income for this employer and recorded as Income in 1 period (IOP) on the customer's Entitlement Period End Day (EPED). Commission income paid for a period longer than an entitlement period must be coded with the frequency of Long Period (LOP). These updates can be reported by the customer as employment income online.

For more information on coding of contract or commission income, see Income for an independent contractor and commission income.

7

Question: Can manual rate customers use self service to report income? + Read more ...

Answer: If any manual rate customer reports via self service (Centrelink online accounts, Express Plus Centrelink mobile app or phone) they will successfully submit their report., However this will result in a hand-off/'drop through' for a Service Officer to amend and adjust the rate of payment appropriately.

See Manual rates of payment.

8

Question: Can a customer add a new ADE employer or SWS employer? + Read more ...

Answer: No, they cannot add a new Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE) or Supported Wage System (SWS) employer to their record. Eligible customers are able to report income for existing ADE employers or SWS employers.

Notification reporters with existing SWS, ADE or Disability Employment Services (DES) employer will not be able to access the Update employment income service.

See Coding income from the Supported Wage System (SWS) and Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE) referrals. Employer Electronic Reporting DOC details and actions for staff to undertake.

9

Question: Can a Service Officer see previous REI transactions completed by the customer? + Read more ...

Answer: Yes, by reviewing the Online Interaction Summary in the Customer Online Account tile in Customer First.

10

Question: Can a customer see future reporting dates and reporting periods? + Read more ...

Answer: Yes, the customer can view up to 6 future reporting periods for the next 12 weeks. They can access this on the Next reporting periods page in their Centrelink online account. The reporting periods are automatically updated at the time the page is accessed. It will always display reporting periods for 12 weeks from the date the page is viewed.

If holiday reporting arrangements are in place, the page will display the correct permitted to report dates, which may be earlier than the customer's regular reporting date. In these cases the permitted to report dates will be highlighted.

11

Question: Can a customer correct their reported income if they make a mistake? + Read more ...

Answer: Yes, customers on statement reporting can correct their reported income online if there are changes to their income, or they made an error in their initial report. They can access this service by selecting Update employment income in their Centrelink online account.

Customers can make an online correction to their (or their partner's) employment income reported in the last six reporting periods.

Customers can correct reported income for their partner where they have Partner Permitted to Enquire (PPE) recorded, unless their partner:

  • has mutual obligation requirements, or
  • is receiving Carer Payment

Customers cannot use the service to correct their reported income if they are:

  • receiving Carer Payment, or
  • making a correction to an income type other than income from employment (for example, Supported Wage System income)

Note: previously confirmed employment income from a Single Touch Payroll (STP) employer cannot be corrected by customers. They will need to provide evidence of any changes for assessment via staff assisted channels.
