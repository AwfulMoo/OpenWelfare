This document outlines information about how to complete the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Current Tax Year Assessment (SY042).

On this Page:

SY042 form

The Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Current Tax Year Assessment (SY042) only applies to assessing eligibility for Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA). The SY042 is completed if the income of the AIC applicant or their partner has either dropped substantially from the base tax year or has increased by more than 25% over the base tax year and is also more than 25% higher than the parental income free area.

Current financial year

The current financial year is the year following the base tax year. The base tax year is the financial year ending on 30 June in the calendar year prior to the year of study.

If exact current year income figures are not known, an estimate can be used. Actual income is checked with the Australian Taxation Office after the end of the year and an AIC debt can result if actual income is higher than the estimate used.

Income decreased

A current income assessment can be applied if the income is reduced for at least two years because of any of the following:

  • permanent invalidity or retirement
  • natural disasters such as drought, bush fire or other circumstances beyond the control of the parent or guardian
  • unemployment for an extended period or retrenchment
  • any other circumstances causing hardship

The death or separation of a parent or guardian does not normally require assessment using the current tax year, unless the remaining parent (applicant) also suffers a significant and sustained fall in income. Where the death or separation of a parent or guardian occurs after 1 January of the year of study, only the income of the remaining parent or guardian for the base tax year is taken into account.

Seasonal variations where income normally fluctuates (e.g. in the case of primary producers) are not a basis for current income assessment unless the reduction in income is likely to last for at least two years.

The assessment applies from the later of 1 January in the year of study or the date of the drop in income.

Income increased

If a reverse current income assessment is necessary, entitlement is reassessed from 1 October of the year of study.

Completing the form

The form must be signed by both the applicant and their partner, if applicable. If they are applying for a reverse current income assessment, they should lodge the form within 14 days of becoming aware of the increase in their income.
