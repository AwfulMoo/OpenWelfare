Eligibility for ACCS (Grandparent)

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Eligibility for Child Care Subsidy (CCS) + Read more ...

To be eligible for ACCS (Grandparent) customers must be eligible for CCS.

Is the customer current on CCS or eligible for CCS?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ACCS (Grandparent). Procedure ends here

2

Definition of grandparent + Read more ...

A grandparent or a great grandparent is defined as someone who is:

  • the biological grandparent/great-grandparent of the child, or
  • a grandparent/great-grandparent as a result of:
    • adoption
    • step-parent relationships (including former partners)
    • artificial conception procedures
    • de facto relationship, or
    • surrogacy arrangements

Is the customer a grandparent?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ACCS (Grandparent). Procedure ends here

3

All or at least 65% ongoing care + Read more ...

Grandparents must have all or at least 65% of the ongoing daily care for the child.

The percentage of care is determined using the same rules as applies for Family Tax Benefit (FTB). For more information, see Notification and assessment of shared care arrangements for FTB.

In most cases, a grandparent will not be considered as the principal carer of the child if they reside in the same home as the biological parent. The Resources page has scenarios when a grandparent may or may not be considered the principal carer for ACCS (Grandparent).

Does the customer have 65% or more care of the child?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ACCS (Grandparent). Procedure ends here

4

Substantial autonomy of day-to-day decisions about the child’s care + Read more ...

In addition to providing 65% or more daily ongoing daily care, a grandparent must have substantial autonomy for the day-to-day decisions about the child’s care, welfare and development. This means they generally have the power to make decisions without consulting with the child’s parents, when the child is in the grandparent’s care.

Does the customer have substantial autonomy of day-to-day decisions?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ACCS (Grandparent) Procedure ends here

5

Eligible income support payments + Read more ...

Is the customer, or their partner, in receipt of an ACCS (Grandparent) eligible income support payment?

  • Yes, the customer meets the eligibility criteria for ACCS (Grandparent), subject to the provision of supporting evidence. See Table 2
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ACCS (Grandparent). Procedure ends here

Evidence requirements for ACCS (Grandparent) applications

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Proof of relationship + Read more ...

If a grandparent relationship has already been established with Services Australia the customer is not required to provide evidence again as part of an application for ACCS (Grandparent).

If the relationship has not previously been determined, evidence required includes, but is not limited to:

  • checking records and scanned documents (including on the grandchild’s parent's record) to determine the relationship
  • Family law order or custody order
  • Parenting plan or order
  • Birth certificates of the grandparent’s own children and their children
  • A combination of birth certificates and marriage certificates for step-grandparents
  • Adoption papers to prove relationship
  • Artificial conception or surrogacy documentation, where the child has not been adopted
  • Commonwealth statutory declaration, only accepted where formal evidence cannot be obtained

2

Percentage of care + Read more ...

If conclusive evidence has previously been provided confirming the level of care, there is no need for customers to provide evidence again, as long as the evidence is accessible to process the application.

However, evidence of care is required if:

  • the Service Officer is not satisfied with information on hand
  • the evidence previously provided is not accessible from the customer’s record
  • the evidence has expired (e.g. court order has expired) or is no longer current (e.g. percentage of care has changed from the original court order), or
  • care arrangements have changed since care was previously determined

For further information about evidence of care, see Notification and assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

3

Substantial autonomy to make decisions about the child + Read more ...

Evidence to support substantial autonomy of decision-making is required if this cannot be established by other evidence already on hand and which can be accessed to process the ACCS (Grandparent) application.

  • Evidence can include: Family law order
  • Custody order
  • Parenting plan or order
  • Foster care placement
  • Court order for graduated return to care where child is being integrated back into the family
  • Where formal care arrangements do not exist, a letter from child’s school, General Practitioner or other third party (who has regular dealing with the family and can independently verify the circumstances) stating that the grandparent has responsibility for the day to day decisions about the child’s care, welfare and development
  • Commonwealth statutory declaration, only accepted where formal evidence cannot be obtained

Where formal documentation does not confirm autonomy of care, the following types of third party evidence can be considered on a case by case basis:

  • letter from the child’s General Practitioner, or other allied health provider, stating the grandparent is responsible for the child’s medical care and expenses
  • letter from a pharmacist stating the grandparent pays for prescriptions for their grandchild and/or seek advice for managing their grandchild’s health
  • letter from the child’s school stating that the grandparent is the person to call regarding decisions about their grandchild’s schooling, including the child’s wellbeing while at school
  • receipts from medical expenses, travel, school uniforms etc.

4

Commonwealth statutory declarations as evidence + Read more ...

Commonwealth statutory declarations should only be considered acceptable evidence for a 12 month period. This is to ensure reasonable time is given for grandparents to obtain additional evidence. It is a requirement that reasonable steps to obtain other evidence are made within the timeframe.

If a Service Officer determines it is still unfeasible for formal evidence to be obtained, the Commonwealth statutory declaration can continue to be used as supporting evidence. Such situations should be assessed on a case by case basis.

5

Single forms of evidence + Read more ...

In some cases, a single piece of evidence is sufficient to confirm grandparent relationship, care arrangements and substantial autonomy of decision-making. For example a child custody and parenting arrangement order which outlines the customer’s care arrangements and relationship to the child.

In such cases, separate evidence is not required to confirm relationship and principal care arrangements.

Completing an application for ACCS (Grandparent)

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Options to apply for ACCS (Grandparent) + Read more ...

Grandparents can apply for ACCS (Grandparent) at the same time as lodging a claim for CCS, or separately where they are already receiving CCS.

The preferred method for lodging an application for ACCS (Grandparent) is via online services, as this is the most convenient and efficient method for lodgement and assessment of claims.

  • Online claim for CCS/ACCS (Grandparent), go to Step 2
  • Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) for CCS/ACCS (Grandparent), go to Step 3
  • Standalone application for ACCS (Grandparent) for CCS current customers, go to Step 10

In extremely limited circumstances (for example, Remote Servicing Team has no internet connectivity, lack of interpreter availability to assist with a verbal claim), customers can lodge an offline (paper) form. See the Resources page for a link to FA084 Claim for Child Care Subsidy, which includes the option to Apply for ACCS (Grandparent).

2

Online Claim + Read more ...

Customers can apply for ACCS (Grandparent) at the same time they complete a CCS claim. For information about claiming CCS see Claiming CCS and ACCS.

If the customer indicates they are a grandparent as part of their CCS claim, the CCS claim will present the customer with the option to claim ACCS (Grandparent).

After completing the CCS/ACCS claim, the Next steps page displays:

  • a list of Required tasks, which will include any evidence requirements for ACCS (Grandparent)
  • the date by which the claim must be submitted before it will expire
  • a link to Upload documents via an Upload button against each Required task

All Required tasks must be provided before the customer can Submit their CCS/ACCS claim.

Customers can access their online claim to return to the Next steps page at any time before it is submitted.

Customers have the option to:

  • go Back to the Review your claim page
  • Save their claim

Until all required documentation has been provided, and the claim submitted, it is deemed no claim has been lodged or decision made. This means a customer’s CCS/ACCS payment can only be backdated from the date the claim is submitted. This is not appealable.

Started claims will automatically expire after 13 weeks.

3

Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) for CCS/ACCS (Grandparent) + Read more ...

An ACC is available for customers who:

  • are not eligible to register for self service or
  • a staff member has deemed online services to be inappropriate based on their circumstances

An ACC can also assist Indigenous customers living in remote communities who have language or literacy barriers affecting their capacity to use online claims or forms.

Before using the ACC workflow to complete a verbal claim, or enter details from an offline (paper) claim, Service Officers must update the customer's personal details via the Change in Contact Details workflow as these questions are not asked in the ACC. If the ACC started before the workflow was run, the claim can be saved and the updates made outside the claim before submitting it. Updates made using the Change in Contact Details workflow will appear on the Review and Submit page in the ACC.

For information about claiming CCS via an ACC, see Claiming CCS and ACCS.

If the customer indicates they are a grandparent within the CCS claim, the ACC will present the question set for ACCS (Grandparent).

If there is a started CCS claim on the Customer Claim Summary, the claim can be:

  • continued by selecting the pencil icon under the Actions column
  • cancelled by selecting the trash icon under the Actions column

'Channel Hopping' within the ACC workflow allows:

  • the customer to start a claim online and have it continued by a Service Officer, or
  • a Service Officer to help a customer with starting a claim, which would then be completed by the customer online (an active Centrelink online account is required)

4

Completing the ACC workflow + Read more ...

Service Officers must read the eligibility message to the customer and ask if they want to be read the full privacy agreement. The customer must agree to the privacy agreement prior to commencing the claim

Service Officers must ask the customer streaming questions on the Check Eligibility screens to check if they may be eligible for CCS.

If answers indicate the customer may be eligible for CCS, select Claim Now when ready to start.

Continue through the remaining question sets. If the customer is unable to answer mandatory questions, the claim workflow can be saved and continued at a later time.

5

ACCS (Grandparent) specific questions + Read more ...

If the customer indicates their (or their partner’s) relationship to the child is ‘Grandparent’ when updating Child details, the following questions will present:

  • Do you wish to be assessed for Additional Child care Subsidy (Grandparent)?
  • Do you responsible for the day to day decisions about [child]?

If the customer does not wish to apply for ACCS (Grandparent), they will still be asked about responsibility for the day-to-day decisions of the child as they may be eligible for an Activity Test exemption for the grandchild.

6

Review and submit claim + Read more ...

Check that all information is correct on the Review your claim page.

  • If answers need to be corrected, select the relevant Update button
  • If personal details need to be corrected, exit the ACC and update using the Change in Contact Details workflow. This information will then populate into the claim

Verbal claim declaration

Does the customer agree to a verbal declaration script being read to meet their signature requirements?

  • Yes, Service Officers must select the my obligations hyperlink (bottom of review page) and read the claim declaration to the customer. Select the ‘I have read, understood and accept my obligations’ check box. Go to Step 7
  • No, a Customer Declaration Form (CDF) is required. The CDF must be printed by selecting the printer icon on the right of the Review and submit page, and issued to the customer to sign and return. Advise the customer the claim will not be taken as submitted until the signed CDF is returned, and any supporting documentation (if applicable) is provided
    • Run the Request and Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure to request the claimant return the signed CDF within 14 days.
    • Ask claimant to sign CDF. Scan CDF to their record

7

Next Steps + Read more ...

This page lists:

  • information or tasks required for the claim to be submitted
  • bank account and TFN details will display as supplementary tasks
  • the date by which the claim must be submitted before it expires
  • links to download or print required forms

Advise the customer of documents or evidence requirements, particularly in relation to ACCS (Grandparent), explaining their claim cannot be lodged until the evidence is provided. Where the customer has not agreed to the verbal declaration, they will also need to provide a signed CDF before the ACC can be submitted.

An ACC can be saved and will remain in draft for 13 weeks, after which time it will expire and a new claim (ACC) will need to be initiated.

Is documentation/evidence required?

Note: if customer states evidence of relationship, care arrangements and/or substantial responsibility of decision has been provided previously, go to Step 8.

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, Select Save:
    • To attach documents for a saved claim, see Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM). Once all documentation has been provided, the claim can be submitted. If not provided within 13 weeks, the claim will expire. Go to Step 9

8

Uploading documents or searching for existing evidence + Read more ...

Encourage customers to access their Centrelink online account or the Centrelink Express Plus mobile app to upload documents and then submit their claim.

On the Next Steps page in the Documents and forms section there is an Upload documents button.

If the customer states evidence of relationship, care arrangements and/or substantial responsibility of decision making has been provided previously, access the customer’s record in Process Direct and select Documents. Investigate if documents are available to support the ACCS (Grandparent) application.

Has required documentation/evidence been provided and is accessible?

  • Yes, go to Step 9.
  • No, select Save. An ACC cannot be submitted if required documentation has not been provided. Advise the customer their date of claim will only take effect from the date they provide their documentation/evidence. Also advise the customer their started claim will expire in 13 weeks, after which time they will need to initiate a new claim. Procedure ends here.

9

Submit claim via ACC + Read more ...

If it is determined sufficient evidence is available and accessible to support the CCS/ACCS (Grandparent) claim, Service Officers are to Continue the ACC, and select the ‘Provided’ check box as appropriate

The ACC can be submitted where the only outstanding information are bank account and/or TFN details. Remind customers to provide their details within 14 days. They can provide this information verbally, or via their online account or the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app as it will display as an outstanding task. If details are not provided within 28 days, their claim will be not effective and they will need to lodge a new claim.

Once a claim is submitted, customers subscribed to Electronic Messaging will receive a Notification confirming the submission and reminded to monitor their claim tracker in their online account or Express Plus Centrelink mobile app.

The Submitted page will display:

  • Claim ID
  • Date submitting for processing
  • Estimated Completion Date (ECD) which can be advised to the customer
  • A ‘withdraw claim’ option
  • Tabs that link to Notifications, Tasks & Review claim details information

All CCS/ACCS (Grandparent) combined claims are allocated to appropriately skilled Service Officers for processing. See Processing ACCS (Grandparent) applications.

10

ACCS (Grandparent) standalone application + Read more ...

The 'Grandparent' service will display in the Child Care Subsidy submenu in a customers online account, if they:

  • are CCS current
  • indicated in their CCS claim that they have a relationship of ‘grandparent’, and

have 65% or more care of the grandchild

Customers will be asked if they wish to be assessed for ACCS (Grandparent). If they answer Yes or No, the following questions will present based on their circumstances to confirm:

  • which children they wish to apply for ACCS (Grandparent), and
  • they (or their partner) are substantially responsible for the day to day decisions about the care of the child

Note: if the customer answers ‘no’ to applying for ACCS (Grandparent), they will be assessed for an Activity Test exemption for the grandchild/ren nominated.

Verbal applications for ACCS (Grandparent) are not available. If a CCS current customer with a grandparent relationship contacts wishing to apply for ACCS (Grandparent), they are to be directed to online services and given the necessary assistance to lodge the application.
