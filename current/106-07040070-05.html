FAQ - Commencement of Identity

Item

Description

1

Question: Customer says their birth was never registered. How can they satisfy proof of birth requirements?

Answer: If the customer has previously claimed a Centrelink payment, check if documentation has already been provided on the customer's file. If documentation has not been provided and the customer has been authenticated, assess the claim using Alternative Identity procedures.

2

Question: What is an appropriate Request for Verification (SS257) referral?

Answer: Financial Hardship

Use the Request for Verification referral workflow in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard when the customer is in financial hardship and unable to provide this document:

  • Customer has liquid assets/funds of less than 1 fortnight of maximum income support payment:
    • For example, John is claiming a payment and needs to provide a CoI document. John advises he lost his birth certificate when he moved house and does not have the available funds to purchase a birth certificate
  • Customer has recently had a significant life event occur leaving them in financial hardship, such as domestic violence, extreme personal circumstances
    • For example, Sarah is required to provide a CoI document for her Parenting Payment Single (PPS) claim. Sarah recently left her home due to domestic violence and advises she is not able to return to her primary residence to obtain her CoI document or access funds to purchase a new birth certificate. Due to Sarah’s circumstances the agency would not expect Sarah to return home to retrieve the document or put Sarah in any further financial hardship
  • Customer is a young person who is in financial hardship, for example UTLAH
  • Customer is in financial hardship following prison release

Answer: Mobility Issues:

  • Customer is unable to obtain this document themselves due to mobility barriers, for example, customer is living in a nursing home or institution. Use the Request for Verification referral workflow in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard

Answer: Intellectual impairment:

Any customer who identifies to have an intellectual impairment and is unable to provide their birth certificate. Use the Request for Verification referral workflow in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard

3

Question: When not to send a Request for Verification (SS257) referral?

Answer: Customer has the document at home, does not want to go home to retrieve.

  • For example, Jack advises he has recently applied for his birth certificate for his Low Income Health Care Card (HCC) claim. Do not send an SS257 referral, as the customer can provide the document. Use the Identity Confirmation Dashboard and code Commencement of Identity 'YY - will provide later’ to apply a review period.
  • For example, Stacey is claiming JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and advises that her birth certificate is somewhere at home, but she is not sure where as she has recently moved and is still unpacking. Stacey has advised she has the document at home and can provide it. Do not send an SS257 referral. Use the Identity Confirmation Dashboard and code Commencement of Identity 'YY - will provide later’ to apply a review period.

Answer: Customer has a nominee who could provide the document on their behalf.

  • For example, Jane is claiming Youth Allowance while living away from home for study. Jane’s birth certificate is at her mother’s (nominee) house. Do not send an SS257 referral as Jane should ask her mother to provide her original birth certificate to her local service centre. Use the Identity Confirmation Dashboard and code Commencement of Identity 'YY - will provide later’ to apply a review period.

Answer: Customer asks the agency to obtain a physical certificate.

  • For example, Karlee is claiming Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and has requested assistance in obtaining her birth certificate for a housing application. Do not send an SS257 referral as this process will only provide verification that a birth is valid, and does not provide a birth certificate

Assist the customer by providing contact details of their relevant Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages. See Resources page for the link

4

Question: What if a customer's citizenship is only recorded on parents' citizenship papers?

Answer: If the child's name appears on the parent's certificate, it can be accepted for both parent and child. The same certificate number can be used for all on the same certificate.

5

Question: Does confirmation of arrival have to be obtained for a customer who was born overseas to Australian parents and whose birth was registered at the Australian embassy?

Answer: The customer needs to provide confirmation of arrival, not confirmation of birth as the customer was born overseas. If the customer has a Citizenship by descent document, this can be coded as an Australian Citizenship certificate. If a Citizenship by descent certificate is not available, a Citizenship Details Request form can be sent to the Department of Home Affairs by Centrelink International Services (CIS) or the Random Samples Teams only.

6

Question: When not to send the 'Verification of Australian Visa' Fast Note to the DVS Operations team?

Answer: Customer has the document at home, does not want to go home to retrieve.

  • Do not send Fast Note Visa verification referral to the DVS Operations team, as the customer can provide the document. Use the Identity Confirmation Dashboard and code Commencement of Identity ‘YY - will provide later’ to apply a review period

Answer: Customer was born in Australia

  • Customer could have an Australian Birth Certificate, ask customer for the Australian Birth Certificate, see Process Page, Step 7 for coding instructions

Answer: Customer is now an Australian Citizen

  • Customer could have an Australian Citizenship Certificate, ask customer for the Australian Citizenship Certificate, see Process Page, Step 7 for coding instructions

Answer: Customer is applying for COVID-19 Disaster Payment or Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment

  • Customer has applied for a COVID-19 Disaster Payment or Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
