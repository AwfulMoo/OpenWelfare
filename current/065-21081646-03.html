Referring customers to an ACSO

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Determine if an ACSO is at the customer’s local office + Read more ...

Check the list on the Services Australia website, or Office locator under ‘Specialists’ to find if there is an ACSO at the customer’s local or nearby office.

Is there an ACSO located at the customer’s local office, or another nearby office they are happy to travel to?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 4

2

Check the reason for referral + Read more ...

A staff member can refer a customer to an ACSO or a customer can ask for a referral.

ACSOs provide end to end services to support customers navigate My Aged Care. They also provide information and support customers to understand their financial situation and their options in relation to costs of care.

They can help customers:

  • understand, access and navigate My Aged Care services
  • understand the costs and financial options related to aged care

ACSOs are not financial planners, advisors or counsellors. They do not:

  • advise people about how to invest their money
  • deal with other agencies or businesses on behalf of people
  • make decisions about eligibility for payments, or aged care or update customer income and asset information

See Identifying a customer in crisis or needing special help if the customer is in immediate financial crisis. It may be appropriate to consult with an ACSO onsite.

3

Appropriate reasons for referral to an ACSO + Read more ...

Find out why they are contacting. Ask them 'So that I can refer you to the most appropriate person, what would you like help with today?'

Appropriate reasons for a referral to ACSO include:

  • Seeking information about the types of aged care services such as:
    • help at home (Commonwealth Home Support Programme, Home Care Packages)
    • short term care (respite, restorative and transition care)
    • aged care homes (permanent residential aged care)
  • Eligibility checks for and referring customers to a My Aged Care assessment
  • reviewing customer My Aged Care records and service referrals where needs have changed
  • Helping customers to:
    • appoint a representative for My Aged Care
    • appoint an authorising person or organisation to enquire or act on their behalf for Services Australia
  • Helping eligible customers in crisis to connect to needed services
  • Planning for or entering aged care (or on behalf of someone entering or in aged care), and wanting to understand:
    • how the aged care system works
    • how to navigate the aged care system
    • the meaning of financial words used in aged care
    • aged care fees and costs
    • how aged care may affect their government payments
    • different options to pay for aged care based on their circumstances
    • options if they are thinking of selling or transferring property
  • Help to understand their finances and aged care options if their partner has died

Note: the customer must agree to the referral to an ACSO.

See Identifying a customer in crisis or needing special help if the customer is in immediate financial crisis. It may be appropriate to consult with an ACSO onsite.

Does the customer meet the above criteria?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

No referral, offer other options + Read more ...

The customer does not require a referral to an ACSO or there is no ACSO available at their location.

A warm transfer is required to one of the following depending on the customer’s enquiry. If the enquiry is about:

  • care, medical assessment, and/or Home Care Packages, warm transfer the customer to My Aged Care. See the Warm transfer to My Aged Care table
  • aged care fees or means test assessment, refer the customer to the Services Australia Aged Care means assessments line. For Smart Centre staff transfer call via iWS. See Aged care - External contacts for the number.
  • financial information and options, consider booking an appointment with a Financial Information Services (FIS) Officer. Note: FIS appointments must only be booked by a FIS Officer, once they check the appropriate service channel.
  • other circumstances, or the customer is vulnerable, consider other specialist referrals. For example, a Social work service referral

Procedure ends here.

5

Make referral to an ACSO + Read more ...

All staff can make referrals to an ACSO.

When to book an ACSO appointment depends on the customer’s circumstances and immediate need for services.

Book an appointment if:

  • the customer requests to see someone face-to-face
  • they can return / travel to an ACSO Service Centre and the appointment is timely
  • there is an ACSO in their local area. If no ACSO in the customers local area consider booking and appointment with a FIS officer see Financial Information Service (FIS)

If the customer needs to speak to someone straight away, go to Step 6.

If the customer requests to book an ACSO appointment, go to Step 7.

6

Warm transfer + Read more ...

Reasons for transferring to My Aged Care may include but not limited to:

  • customer requests to speak to My Aged Care
  • customer needs more help than you can provide
  • customer does not want to have a face-to-face appointment with an ACSO
  • no ACSO located in the customers area
  • customer is unable to travel to a Service Centre for face-to-face appointment
  • customer needs immediate help, for example has had a fall and are in hospital

Does the customer need a warm transfer to My Aged Care?

  • Yes, warm transfer the customer to my Aged Care. See the Warm transfer to My Aged Care table in My Aged Care General Service Offer (MACGSO)
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Booking ACSO appointment + Read more ...

ACSO appointments are booked via the Centrelink appointment system in Customer first. See Creating an appointment.

  • Select Book and Manage Appointments in Workspace
  • The Appointments screen will display. Any existing appointments will also show under Booked Appointments
  • There are options to create, reschedule or cancel an existing appointment

Note: use the correct Centrelink Appointment System time display.

  • Select Create

8

Appointment Details screen + Read more ...

The customer's full name and CRN will pre-fill:

  • Select Aged Care Specialist Officer – Initial from the Appointment Type dropdown list
    • Note: do not select the other Aged Care options: ‘Aged Care - My Aged Care general - Short, Medium or Long’ appointments. These appointment reasons are for service centre staff to provide general help through the face-to-face My Aged Care General Service Offer, not ACSOs
  • Appointment Channel will pre-fill. This can be changed if business rules allow
  • Select Service Reason > Aged Care Specialist Officer Interview
  • Specify whether the customer will need an interpreter. If booking a double length appointment for a customer with an interpreter, send an email to the Language Services interpreter feedback mailbox. Include the:
    • CRN
    • location
    • date, and
    • time of appointment. This will ensure only one interpreter is booked
  • What to Bring will auto-fill based on the appointment type. Delete and add documents from this as necessary
  • After keying all data, select Next

9

Appointment Booking screen + Read more ...

If the Appointment Channel is Face to Face, the Centrelink Appointment System will:

  • default to the customer’s closest service centre, and
  • show the first available date and time for an ACSO appointment when selected

If appointment details do not display or need updating:

  • Select the customer's preferred date
  • Select the office location. This will show available appointment times
  • If no appointment is available at the location on the chosen day, use the Find button to search. Search for the next available appointment in weekly increments
  • Select the customer's preferred appointment time from the list
  • After keying all data, select Next

10

Confirm the customer's ACSO appointment details + Read more ...

The Confirm screen will display all the customer's appointment details.

Enter Appointment Notes detailing the reason/s ACSO appointment requested. This helps the ACSO to prepare for the appointment.

Select the Finish button to complete the booking.

Notify the customer verbally if the appointment is within 5 days. Make sure to advise the time, date, location and anything they need to bring with them.

If any information needs to be changed, go to the correct page using the Previous and Next buttons below the appointment workflow diagram.

When and how to use the ACSO callback fast note

Table 3

Step

Action

1

When to use the Aged Care Specialist Callback request + Read more ...

Use the Aged Care Specialist Callback request fast note in the below instances:

  • Where the customer or their nominee/representative has already had an initial appointment with an ACSO
  • The customer or their nominee/representative need to communicate important information to the ACSO that was not discussed at their appointment

2

Send an Aged Care Specialist Callback request fastnote + Read more ...

On the customer’s or their nominee/representative’s record create an Aged Care Specialist Callback request fastnote.

  • Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Aged Care > Aged Care Specialist Officer > Aged Care Specialist officer Callback request

Ensure to include the below information in the call back requests:

  • who asked to speak to the ACSO (customer/nominee/representative)
  • what office the ACSO is located at, and
  • reason for contact
