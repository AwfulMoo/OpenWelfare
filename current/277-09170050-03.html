Make objection decision

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Make decision + Read more ...

Make a decision on an objection within 60 days of lodgement. The timeframe is extended to 120 days when 1 or both customers live in a reciprocating jurisdiction.

Make a decision to allow, part allow or disallow the objection. See References for a link to the Child Support Guide 4.1.6: Making a decision to allow or disallow an objection.

If an administrative change, change of assessment application, further objection or Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) appeal is lodged on the case before the objection decision has been made. Liaise with the other decision makers and Team Leader to agree on an issue resolution strategy.

2

Analyse and apply the rules to the evidence + Read more ...

Consider all relevant evidence, legislation and policy. Make a decision based on:

  • the facts of the case which have been established on evidence
  • the relevant legislative provisions of the Child Support Acts
  • any applicable policy including the objectives and principles of the Child Support scheme

See principles of administrative decision making for more information about what to consider when making an administrative decision.

For more information on Care objections see the Care considerations for care percentage decisions table.

For more information on Part 6 objections see the Part 6A considerations table.

3

Contact customers to discuss likely outcome + Read more ...

Personalised Services managed customer

Immediately contact the Personalised Services Case Manager (PSCM) and inform them of the likely Objection decision. Determine who will discuss the outcome of the Objection with the customer. It may be necessary for a 3 way conversation to ensure the issue is appropriately and sensitively managed.

All other customers

If the objection is unlikely to result in a contrary decision, contact both customers to advise of the likely outcome. Make 3 contact attempts to each parent within 48 hours on all available numbers at different times of the day prior to finalising the objection,

Explain once the decision has been written and processed, the Statement of Reasons (SoR) will be sent to all parties.

Ask the customer if a post decision call is required, if a change is made. If so, create a Client Call Back or Part 6A Call Back intray with a notepad detailing the request.

If both customers have already been advised the objection was likely to be disallowed and no further information was provided. Only contact the customers again, if a debt is likely to be raised due to the objection decision.

If a debt is likely to be raised, explain to the customer they will be contacted to negotiate the debt once the decision has been processed.

Contrary outcome

If the objection results in an outcome contrary to that sought by the applicant:

  • review objection notes to ensure all relevant information and evidence have been considered, and
  • both parties had an opportunity to comment on the information available.

Do not contact the customers prior to finalising the decision to discuss the likely outcome of the objection. This is to ensure that the decision equally considers both parties’ interests and the applicant is not given an unfair opportunity to withdraw the objection to avoid a contrary decision. In these circumstances both parties will be notified about the outcome of the objection after the decision has been finalised. See Step 9 in Table 4.

Record the OBJ customer contact.

4

Write the Statement of Reason + Read more ...

Use the Statement of Reasons macro to write the Statement of Reasons report. This report is sent to notify customers of the objection decision and explain how it was reached.

Write a concise report using only the relevant facts. See General Objections - Guide to Writing the Statement of Reasons for guidance on how to write the report.

If a customer is managed by Personalised Services, send a copy of the decision by email to the PSCM before sending it to the customers. See Personalised Services.

Note: from 17 February 2015, Service Officers (SO6) determining Part 6A Objections have the power to allow an objection and to make a new decision under Part 6A of the Child Support (Assessment) Act. The role of ‘EL1 Delegate’ has been abolished. Until the Part 6A Objections Statement of Reasons template is updated, the ‘recommended by’ signature block will need to be deleted and then modified as follows:
Approved by:
[insert Objections Service Officer name]

Care considerations for care percentage decisions

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Care considerations for care percentage decisions with notification dates on or after 1 July 2010 (3 March 2011 for WA Ex nuptial) + Read more ...

For care percentage decisions with notification dates on or after 1 July 2010 (3 March 2011 for WA Ex nup) to determine:

  • if a new care determination can be made, go to Step 2
  • the date of effect of the decision, go to Step 3

2

Determine if new care determination can be made + Read more ...

For care percentage decisions made after 1 July 2010 (3 March 2011 for WA ex nup) a new care determination can only be made where the existing determination can be revoked. The new care determination commences the day after the existing care determination is revoked.

Circumstances where an existing care determination will be revoked are:

  • he new care percentage determination would change the cost percentage and any interim care period has ended
  • one of the parents who was previously assessed to have at least regular care of the child is now determined to have less than regular care despite the child being made available by the other parent
  • the new care percentage determination would cause a change to the existing care percentage but not the cost percentage and any interim care period has ended

3

Date of effect + Read more ...

If the objection was lodged within 28 days of the person being notified of the care percentage decision, then the objection decision will replace the original care percentage decision.

If the objection was lodged outside the 28 day timeframe (90 days for customers in a reciprocating jurisdiction), the customer may ask Child Support to consider special circumstances that impacted on their ability to lodge the objection earlier. See Child Support objection received out of time - Extension of time and Special Circumstance application, Care decisions.

When determining the date:

  • If the customer was notified of the care percentage decision, generally use the letter issued by Child Support as the first time the customer is notified, even when Centrelink made the original care decision
  • Centrelink notifies shared care decisions in writing to their customers in very limited situations. They do not always notify the other party of the outcome of an original care decision if that person is not getting FTB for the child or the decision is not changed.
  • Therefore, the first notification of a care decision a customer may receive may be the Child Support letter
  • If the customer indicates that Centrelink notified them of the decision before they got the first letter from Child Support (e.g. they were notified by phone) then that date is the date of notification

4

Care suspension periods and objections + Read more ...

From 1 July 2018, a care suspension period may apply if we are advised of a change in care outside of 28 days but within 26 weeks of the care change, for more information see Notification of change in child support care (post 1 July 2010).

Note: care suspension periods do not apply to WA Ex-nuptial children if the Agency was notified of the care change before 15 May 2019.

If an objection is lodged outside 28 days (or 90 days for a customer in a reciprocating jurisdiction) and there are no special circumstances, the objection decision will apply from the date the objection was lodged even if the care determination from the objection would otherwise change the decision about suspension of the assessment.

If an objection is lodged outside 28 days (or 90 days for customer in a reciprocating jurisdiction and there are no special circumstances and the original care determination:

  • suspended the assessment, the objection decision will apply from the date the objection was lodged, even if the care determination from the objection would not have suspended the assessment. The suspension period will remain unchanged
  • did not suspend the assessment, the objection decision will apply from the date the objection was lodged. Even if the care determination from the objection would have given rise to a suspension period if it had been the original decision, a suspension will not be applied as there is no period of time when all parents/cares have less than 35% care in the assessment

See the Resources page for examples of care suspension periods and objections.

5

Care terminating events and objections + Read more ...

From 1 July 2018, a care terminating event will occur if Services Australia is notified of a care change more than 26 weeks after the date of event and as a result of the care change the care level for all carers reduces below 35%. For more information, see Notification of change in child support care (post 1 July 2010).

Note: care terminating events do not apply to WA Ex-nuptial children if the Agency was notified of the care change before 15 May 2019.

If an objection is lodged outside 28 days (90 days for customers living in a reciprocating jurisdiction) and there are no special circumstances, the objection decision will apply from the date the objection was lodged even if the care determination from the objection would otherwise change the decision about the terminating event.

If an objection is lodged outside 28 days (90 days for customers living in a reciprocating jurisdiction) and there are no special circumstances and the original care determination:

  • terminated the assessment the objection decision will apply from the date the objection was lodged, even if the care determination from the objection would not have caused the assessment to terminate.
    • If a new application for a child support assessment has not been received and accepted, the new care determination will have no effect until an application for child support is made and accepted
    • If a new application for child support assessment has been made and accepted subsequent to the assessment terminating, the objection care determination will apply from the date the objection was lodged
  • did not cause a care terminating event, the objection decision will apply from the date the objection was lodged. A care terminating event will not occur as there will not be a period of time greater than 26 weeks where all eligible cares ceased to be eligible cares, that is care reduced below 35%

See the Resources page for examples of care terminating events and objections.

3

Multiple cases + Read more ...

Where a customer has multiple cases and the Part 6A objection decision has increased their adjusted taxable income, a capacity to pay referral should be made to ensure fairness and equity for the child support payable in all of their active cases. Refer to the capacity to pay referral guidelines.

Where the objection decision has decreased their income, discuss with the customer about making a COA application or potentially objecting to any existing COA decision on their other active cases if they want this income reflected in the child support assessment.

See Table 4.

Reason 2 + Read more ...

If a decision is made to change the assessment under Reason 2, to determine if the decision should be sent to Centrelink, see COA Reason 2 Decision and potential FTB implications.

5

Referrals to Capacity to Pay + Read more ...

If the applicant withdrew their Part 6A objection and the Part 6A Objections Officer formed the opinion that the income used in the current assessment does not accurately represent customer’s income, property or financial resources (potential COA Reason 8) a capacity to pay referral should be made to ensure the assessment is fair and equitable.

See the capacity to pay referral guidelines.

 

Finalise and process objection decision

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Finalise objection + Read more ...

If the customers are case managed by another area, inform the case manager by a General Follow Up intray with the date set for 2 days' time with a notepad of the outcome. Send an MX14-1 Outcome of objection (non-NAP) letter to both customers and attach the Statement of Reason to each letter. See Letters Cuba Process Help, Create a letter using fill-in fields.

If the decision has changed since advising the customers of the likely outcome and the new decision does not result in a contrary decision, contact both customers again prior to sending MX14-1 Outcome of objection (non-NAP) letter and Statement of Reason. See Contact with Child Support customers.

If the decision has changed and the new decision results in an outcome contrary to that sought by the applicant, do not contact the customers prior to finalising the decision. In these circumstances both parties will be notified about the outcome of the Objection, after the decision has been finalised as in Step 9.

2

Process objection + Read more ...

To process an objection:

  • Update the Objection Maintenance window to document the objection outcome
  • Save the Statement of Reasons in the Objection Maintenance window notepad with the relevant subject heading ‘Allowed/Part Allowed or Disallowed Objection’ decision
  • Make the required changes in Cuba to give effect to the objection decision
  • Reference the decision in the relevant Cuba window. For example, a note on the Non Agency Payment (NAP) window summarising the changes from the objection relating to a NAP
  • Delete the MAC 1-4 Change to annual rate letter which automatically generates when a formula modification has been processed in Cuba. This letter provides incorrect information about the customer's appeal rights. Assessment letters will generate with the MX14-1 Outcome of objection (non-NAP) letter if the decision letter is generated on the same day. See Objections Cuba Process Help
  • Delete all outstanding intrays relating to the objection. See Intray management Cuba Process Help
  • Take follow up action in accordance with the Common and specialised activities for Child Support staff
  • Check whether processing the objection has caused a role reversal to occur. If it has, see Managing reverse child support cases for important information about collection options and ensuring the case is accurate
  • Update all electronic files to ensure they contain the relevant documents
  • For International cases where the liability is transmitted to an overseas jurisdiction create an International Sthold follow up intray with a 4 day due date. Include the details of the outcome of the objection decision in the notepad. Route the intray to:
    • Office: International Hobart
    • Stream/team: MC SOO3 CENT AUTH TM1
    • Position: A- TRANSMITTAL REVIEW

3

General Objections + Read more ...

For general objections if disbursements have been suspended, they must remain suspended and the customer locked to the Objection Officer until the appeal period of 28 calendar days/90 days for customers in a reciprocating jurisdiction (plus postage time of 12 business days) has passed. If during this time the customer requests a review of the decision by the AAT, the customer will continue to be managed by the Objections Service Officer until the AAT decision is finalised. See Suspending disbursements of Child Support and the Child Support Guide 5.5.4: Suspending payments to payees for more information.

The timeframes for determining if an objection has been received in time should be based on where the applicant lives, not the postal address. Where the applicant lives will not always match a person’s Residency as recorded in the Residency window.

Note: the additional 12 days for postage is not added to the timeframe if the applicant received notification of the decision via CSAonline.

4

Finalise objection where the applicant had no intent to lodge an objection + Read more ...

If the applicant did not intend to lodge then it is not an objection. To finalise this matter:

  • create a new notepad in the Objection Maintenance window with the subject heading 'No Intent to Object' and paste the document generated from the Objection First Contact macro
  • delete the objection record that was created for the potential objection in the Objection Maintenance window
  • delete all outstanding intrays relating to the objection
  • undertake follow up action in accordance with the Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support

5

Finalise objection as improperly made + Read more ...

An objection has not been properly made if:

  • it is not from a person who may object against a decision which carries objection rights
  • it is not against a decision which carries objection rights
  • it was lodged out of time and an extension of time has not been granted

If an objection was not properly made:

  • create a new notepad in the Objection Maintenance window notepad with the subject heading ‘Improperly made Objection’ and document the reason why it is unable to be considered
  • update the Objection Maintenance window by marking the objection as invalid
  • send an MX0-1 unique letter using the Objection not accepted (including special circumstances) template to the applicant. See Letters Cuba Process Help, Create a Unique letter
  • undertake follow up action in accordance with the Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support

6

Finalise objection due to offensive or Family and Domestic Violence (FDV) content + Read more ...

To finalise an objection that is improperly made due to offensive or FDV content:

  • in the Objection Maintenance window, create a new notepad with the subject heading 'Improperly made Objection decision' document the reasons for not being able to consider the objection
  • contact the customer within 2 working days of receiving advice from Program Advice to advise:
    • their objection was improperly made due to offensive content
    • they may lodge another objection without the offensive content
    • the objection must still be received within 28 days of notification of the original decision as no special dispensation applies if the objection was improperly made
    • if applicable, they need to include an extension of time request
  • send an  MX0-1 unique letter advising the customer the objection cannot be accepted because it contains offensive content and as a result it is improperly made
  • undertake follow up action in accordance with the Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support

7

Finalise withdrawn objection + Read more ...

If the applicant elects to withdraw the objection, ensure they are aware of the consequences of the withdrawal. See Contact customer regarding Child Support objection, Contact Applicant table, Step 5.

The applicant will be given a 2 business day cooling off period before the withdrawal is actioned in Cuba. They are advised that the onus is on them to contact the Objections Service Officer within this period if they change their mind. The Objections Service Officer should create a 48 Hr Cooling Off Period intray to monitor the cooling off period.

In the Objection Maintenance window, document the discussion with the customer. Ensure the notepad includes their reasons for the withdrawal, explanation provided about the consequences of withdrawal and the cooling off period.

If, at the conclusion of the 48-hour cooling off period, the applicant has not cancelled their withdrawal:

  • in the Objection Maintenance window, using a new subject heading 'Withdrawn Objection’, document finalisation of the withdrawal
  • update the Objection Maintenance window as withdrawn
  • send a MX0-1 unique letter using the Objection withdrawn template
  • if the respondent has been contacted about the objection, inform them about the withdrawal and send a UQ50.11 - Objection withdrawn - respondent letter
  • check the file contains all the documents used in the decision making process and have been stored in accordance with the electronic filing principles
  • undertake follow up action in accordance with the Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support

8

Feedback to the original decision maker + Read more ...

Feedback must be provided to the original decision maker in 100% of Part 6A Objections finalised with the exception of invalids. General objections must provide feedback where the following is identified by the officer undertaking the review:

  • misapplication of law
  • incorrect finding of fact
  • procedural error (including insufficient contact, investigation or follow up)
  • several instances of grammatical or other typographical errors

Feedback should also be provided if it would benefit the recipient and when it is identified that a Service Officer provided exceptional customer service, or performed their role well and achieved outcomes for the customer and Child Support.

To provide feedback:

  • complete the Staff Feedback Tool
  • attach the finalised objection report
  • email the feedback summary to Team Leader within 2 business days of finalising the objection

Document details of the feedback provided including the reason the decision was changed, in the Objections Maintenance window notepad with the subject heading 'Feedback provided'. See Documenting Child Support information.

Team Leaders:

  • review the feedback form to ensure it accurately records the issues
  • forward the feedback to the original decision maker’s Team Leader within 2 business days

9

Contact customer to inform Objection decision finalised + Read more ...

See Contact with Child Support customers to contact the customer.

Where the objection results in a decision that is contrary or adverse to the outcome sought by the applicant, the objection decision must be finalised before the customer is informed of the outcome. This means attempts to contact the parties about the decision should only commence the day after the decision has been processed and the letters issued. All contact attempts should be finalised within 3 days of the decision being finalised.

If either party is being managed by a Personalised Services Case Manager (PSCM), immediately contact the PSCM and inform them about the objection decision. Determine who will discuss the outcome of the objection with the customer. It may be necessary for a three-way conversation to occur with the customer so that the issue is appropriately and sensitively managed. The same notification timeframes apply to customers managed by Personalised Services.

Minimum collection activity must be undertaken if a debt has been raised due to the objection.

Use the Statement of Reasons (SoR) to:

  • explain the decision
  • address the issues and concerns raised (to prevent subsequent reverse workflow from complaints, appeals to the AAT or lengthy repeat phone calls from customers)
  • ensure customers understand this is not an opportunity to have the decision reviewed or changed

Note: an Objections Service Officer is not required to send a separate ‘Please Contact’ letter if attempts to contact the customer(s) fail.

  • Unlock customers after these steps are completed by selecting ' Ownership Rules ' in the Customer Transfer window

Part 6A Objections

Must also discuss:

  • If a customer has multiple cases and not all have been considered through the Objections process, where their adjusted taxable income is likely to increase, discuss with the customer that a ‘Capacity to Pay’ referral may be made to ensure fairness and equity in child support payments across all active cases
  • If the objection decision is likely to decrease their income, discuss with the customer a COA application or potentially an objection to an existing COA decision will be necessary on each of their other active cases if they want this income reflected in the child support assessment

If further action is required for administrative issues (such as non-agency payments, changes in care and estimates of income) identified during the Objections process and not taken into account in the decision, document in an Objections notepad and create a Part 6A General Follow Up intray which auto routes for SAS allocation with a notepad detailing the follow up action(s).

Customers can be unlocked by Part 6A Objections Service Officers when the:

  • decision has been completed by the Objections Service Officer
  • outcome has been explained to both customers
  • minimum collection activity has been undertaken
  • Statement of Reasons has been issued, and
  • the case has been finalised in Cuba

Centrelink care decisions

Table 5: finalising Centrelink Secretary initiated or Authorised Review Officer reviews of a care decision and subsequent review by the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT). This table does not apply to AAT review decisions made following a Child Support objection.

Step

Action

1

Centrelink finalised review of care decisions (Care objections) + Read more ...

Care percentage objections or review decisions made for mutual customers and other affected parties must be applied to both Child Support and Centrelink records and assessments. When Centrelink makes a review decision for a mutual customer, Child Support must apply that decision to each relevant child support assessment.

When Centrelink has made the decision, a Care - Review of Decision Rcvd intray should generate when:

  • a formal review by a Authorised Review Officer (ARO) or a Secretary initiated review by a Subject Matter Expert (ODM) has been finalised
  • an AAT first review of a Centrelink decision has been finalised
  • an AAT second review of a Centrelink decision has been finalised

There will be times when a Case Review Required intray generates instead for these decisions. It may also generate if a Secretary Initiated review of a care percentage decision is made by a Service Officer outside the appeals system.

If Child Support becomes aware that a Centrelink review of a care percentage or an AAT review of a Centrelink decision has been finalised, this should be investigated and the decision applied to the Child Support assessment following the same processing steps.

For more information, see Care intrays Cuba Process Help - Care - Review of Decision Rcvd Intray help or Care intrays Cuba Process Help - Care - Case Review Required Intray.

If a non-mutual customer requested a review or appeal and/or no record is found in Cuba, locate or request the date the review was lodged with Centrelink and/or the AAT. See Centrelink - Child Support information exchange for more information.

2

Decision changed by a Centrelink review + Read more ...

If the application for a formal review was lodged. or the Secretary initiated review decision was made:

  • within 28 days of the customer being notified of the original care decision (or 90 days for customers in a reciprocating jurisdiction), go to Step 3
  • outside 28 days of the customer being notified of the original care decision (or 90 days for customers in a reciprocating jurisdiction), go to Step 4

3

Formal review application, or Secretary initiated review decision, made within 28 days (or 90 days) of original care decision + Read more ...

The Centrelink review decision will take effect from the date the original decision took effect.

The exception will be if the review decision changes the date of event. In that case, the new date as determined by the review will apply.

If the application for an AAT first review of a Centrelink decision was made within 28 days of the person receiving notification of the review decision (90 days for a person in a reciprocating jurisdiction), the AAT first review decision will apply from the date the original decision took effect.

Using the unique letter function in Cuba, update the following letter templates with the appropriate information and send to the customers:

  • UQ51.05 - Care Objection percentage decision - Applicant
  • UQ51.06 - Care Objection percentage decision - Respondent

See Finalise an objection table for information about updating the date these letters were sent in Cuba.

See Letters Cuba Process Help, Create a Unique letter and Objections Cuba Process Help.

4

Formal review application, or Secretary initiated review decision, made outside 28 days (or 90 days) of original care decision + Read more ...

The Centrelink review decision will take effect from the date the application for a formal review was made or the Secretary initiated review was decided.

If the application for AAT first review of a Centrelink decision was made outside 28 days (90 days if the customer resides in a reciprocating jurisdiction) of the customer being notified of the review decision, the AAT first review decision will take effect from the date the AAT first review was requested.

The customer who applied for the Centrelink formal review or a subsequent AAT review can request Child Support consider special circumstances. Special circumstances cannot be considered for a Secretary initiated review of decision. Refer to 4.1.8, Care Percentage Decisions.

Create a unique letter using pre-approved text UQ51.03 - Objection to care percentage review - Applicant, addressed to the customer who requested the Centrelink formal review or the subsequent AAT review. including a standard timeframe for response.If a customer contacts Child Support to discuss a special circumstances application for the review, the customer will be referred via warm transfer to objections who will review the special circumstances and make a decision. See Child Support objection received out of time - Extension of time and Special Circumstance application. The decision for these cases should be finalised within 28 days of the applicant asking Child Support to consider special circumstances.

If a customer does not contact Child Support within the specified time to lodge a special circumstances application, the Service Officer will finalise the care records by applying the Centrelink review or the subsequent AAT first review decision from the date the customer applied for the review.

When a decision about special circumstances is made, advise both customers of the decision and their AAT review rights in writing. See UQ51.07 - Care objection decision date of effect - Applicant letter or UQ51.08 - Care objection decision date of effect - Respondent.

5

Making an objection decision when Centrelink is reviewing or has reviewed the same care percentage decision + Read more ...

If a Centrelink formal review by an ARO is in progress before a customer lodges an objection with Child Support for the same care percentage decision and the customer has not withdrawn their Child Support objection, Child Support is still required to make an objection decision.

However, as Child Support is unable to change the care percentage determined by the Centrelink review, it will be appropriate to 'hold' the objection until the outcome of the review is known.

If the customer has requested an explanation of a care decision from Centrelink and then lodges an objection for the same care decision with Child Support, the objection will continue and the care decision can be changed if appropriate. Notify the Centrelink Officer that an objection will be undertaken, so the decision should not be changed as a result of the Centrelink explanation process.

When Centrelink has finalised the formal review by an ARO or a Secretary initiated review by a SME, the care percentages determined by the Centrelink review must also apply to the child support assessment (subject to the date of effect requirements above). At the same time, the pending Child Support Objection has to be finalised and both parties informed of the decision.

Inform both parties about the objection outcome by sending an MX14-1 Outcome of objection (non-NAP) letter. In the Statement of Reasons use the standard text available on the Child Support Specialised Assessments (SAMP) page under Objection Tools>Objection Letters>Finalising 'duplicate' objections for care percentage decisions
