Medical certificate renewal and extending temporary incapacity exemptions

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Medical certificate renewal + Read more ...

If the job seeker:

  • has a current temporary incapacity exemption for a serious illness, or they have had one in the last 14 days, see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements for serious illness
  • is lodging a new medical certificate, does not have a current incapacity exemption and has not had one in the last 14 days, see Assessment of a medical certificate to determine exemption from mutual obligation requirements for a temporary incapacity
  • is receiving JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and lodging a medical certificate (including previous incapacity ending in the last 14 days), see Assessing medical evidence for JobSeeker Payment (JSP)

Otherwise, go to Step 2.

2

Lodgement of medical certificate + Read more ...

Has the job seeker lodged a new medical certificate to extend their temporary incapacity exemption?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, procedure ends here until the customer lodges a new medical certificate. As no incapacity exemption has been applied, the customer needs to meet their mutual obligation or participation requirements once their current incapacity exemption ends

3

Incomplete or unclear medical certificate + Read more ...

If the medical certificate does not contain all the required information, run the:

  • task selector in Process Direct (if relevant training has been undertaken), or
  • Medical Certificate guided procedure in Customer First

Complete the questions covering an incomplete medical certificate.

Once run, if the job seeker has:

  • lodged in person, return the medical certificate and advise they will need to ask their treating health professional to complete a new medical certificate
  • not lodged in person, a letter is automatically sent to the customer advising the medical certificate is incomplete

If the medical certificate is complete but there is a need to clarify the content:

  • Contact the medical practitioner via phone to check the relevant information
  • Record details of the additional information provided on a DOC including contact details
  • If the medical practitioner has completed an old version of the medical certificate, tell the treating health professional they can now access the Centrelink Medical Certificate (SU415) on the Services Australia website, or explain how to order the latest version. See Centrelink Medical Certificate (SU415 or SU683)
  • If the treating doctor is unable to be contacted or further information is required, escalate to appropriate level for advice on whether the information on the medical condition is sufficient to determine an incapacity exemption (for example, Service Support Officer, Technical Peer Support or Level 2 Helpdesk). Note: do not place the medical certificate on hold. Medical certificate must be actioned

Suspected fraud

If the available information suggests the job seeker has supplied medical evidence that was not completed by a treating health professional or has been amended after a medical practitioner completed it, contact the medical practitioner to clarify.

If the medical practitioner advises they never issued the certificate or the information is different, discuss with immediate supervisor and complete a Suspected Fraud referral if appropriate. Escalate to Level 2 Helpdesk if further assistance is required.

Process the medical certificate as per normal procedures. Do not document specific details of the allegation(s) on the job seeker's record.

Is all the required medical information available to make a decision about renewing a temporary incapacity exemption?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, procedure ends here until further medical information is provided

4

Review medical certificate details + Read more ...

Review the customer's medical certificate lodgement history. Check:

  • details of the medical conditions previously coded, and
  • the Unfit to work/study from and to dates for the last (most recently) coded medical certificate

Assess the lodged medical certificate and check the following:

  • Condition type:
    • Temporary (TMP)
    • Permanent (PRM)
    • Recurring (including exacerbation of permanent condition) (REC)
    • Note: if the condition is not listed as 1 of the above 3 types, Service Officers are to either contact the medical practitioner for clarification or seek relevant advice (for example, from Technical Peer Support, Team Leader)
  • Prognosis:
    • how long the medical practitioner estimates the condition(s) will affect the job seeker's capacity to work or study (this may be 'uncertain' or 'unknown' and applies to TMP/ PRM/ REC)
  • If more information is needed to make a decision, consider:
    • any current and valid assessment of the job seeker's work capacity, in a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) or Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) report
    • previous medical certificates coded on the customer's record
    • any evidence the temporary incapacity may have been primarily caused by, or be attributable to, the person's drug or alcohol dependency or misuse

5

Determine if a further period of temporary incapacity exemption can be granted + Read more ...

A further period of temporary incapacity exemption can be granted if the job seeker:

  • is still unable to work or otherwise meet their mutual obligation requirements for 8 hours or more per week due to an incapacity, and
  • the incapacity is temporary. This includes temporary exacerbations of permanent conditions

Points to consider:

  • All medical conditions listed on the medical certificate, except where:
    • The primary condition listed is wholly or mainly due to drug and/or alcohol dependency or misuse and the job seeker is not a Community Development Program (CDP) participant. A temporary incapacity exemption cannot be granted for this medical certificate. This is whether or not there is a secondary temporary condition not related to drug or alcohol for which an exemption may be applied. If the medical certificate does not differentiate between primary and secondary, determine if the customer's incapacity is wholly or mainly due to drug or alcohol dependency/misuse. This can be based on previous medical details provided by the customer and/or a current and valid ESAt or JCA report. If unsure, seek relevant advice (e.g. from the customer's treating doctor, Service Support Officer, Technical Peer Support)
  • If all medical conditions, including temporary, have a prognosis of 24 months plus, a temporary incapacity exemption cannot be granted
  • If multiple medical conditions are listed, including temporary and permanent, a temporary incapacity exemption can be granted if the job seeker is unable to work or otherwise meet their mutual obligation requirements for 8 hours or more per week due to at least one of the temporary or exacerbated/recurring conditions
  • All medical condition(s) listed are already included in a current and valid ESAt or JCA report. Information within the report can be used unless the new medical evidence indicates the previously assessed medical condition(s) has changed (for example, existing spinal condition has worsened or treatment has changed). New conditions are to be assessed based on the latest medical evidence supplied
  • A temporary incapacity exemption is based on the job seeker having a temporary incapacity that prevents them from undertaking work or otherwise meet their mutual obligation requirements for 8 hours or more per week. The temporary incapacity exemption is not based on a particular medical condition. If consecutive medical certificates list different conditions, a temporary incapacity exemption can still be granted

6

Code medical certificate + Read more ...

Suitably skilled Service Officers are to code the medical certificate using the:

  • task selector in Process Direct (if relevant training has been undertaken), or
  • Medical Certificate guided procedure in Customer First

Complete all relevant fields within the workflow including the following if required:

  • Event Date the same as the Unfit to work/study/participate from date unless it is a future date. If it is a future date, record the Event Date as today's date (the date the medical certificate is being recorded). Note: this is not the Date of Receipt unless the date of receipt is today
  • Treating Health Professional's Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (Ahpra) provider number: leave blank if not provided

7

Unfit to work/study from date + Read more ...

Use the from date on the new medical certificate, except where one of the following apply:

  • The Unfit to work/study/participate from date overlaps with the previous medical certificate. Code the medical certificate Unfit to work/study to date +1. Note: do not overlap the dates
  • Use the previous date Unfit to work/study/participate to date +1 when all of the following apply:
    • There is a gap of less than 14 days between the previous Unfit to work/study/participate to date and the from date on the new medical certificate
    • The Service Officer is satisfied the person has remained incapacitated throughout the period
    • The new medical certificate was lodged within 14 days of the previous expiry date

Note: if there will be a gap in coverage, the customer may have mutual obligations requirements for the gap period.

8

Unfit to work/study/participate to + Read more ...

Use the 'to date' on the medical certificate, except where one of the following apply:

  • The Unfit to work/study/participate from date has been adjusted:
    • The to date then needs to be the lesser of the date noted on the new medical certificate or 13 weeks (91 days) after the adjusted from date. Note: this cannot exceed 13 weeks, which is 91 days (that is, the from date plus 90 days)
    • If adjusted, the Unfit to work/study/participate to date must never exceed the end date noted on the medical certificate
  • The Unfit to work/study/participate to date on the medical certificate exceeds 13 weeks (91 days). Manually adjust the to date to equal 13 weeks (91) days from the from date coded. Note: this cannot exceed 13 weeks, which is 91 days (that is, the from date plus 90 days).
    Serious illness exemptions can be coded for a period greater than 13 weeks. See Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements for serious illness

9

Complete the coding + Read more ...

  • Determine if a serious illness exemption can be granted by completing the questions
  • Determine if an incapacity exemption is to be granted or not. See the Resources page on coding the 'Not Exempt' option
  • Date of Receipt (DOR): the date the medical certificate was initially received/scanned.
    Note: this field is auto populated if the task selector in Process Direct is used. As it cannot be changed, it is to be disregarded when QOL is undertaken as per the Background of Quality On Line (QOL)

Granting exemption after not granted for previous medical certificate

If a customer has a medical certificate coded where a temporary incapacity exemption was not granted, and they submit a new medical certificate where an incapacity exemption is to be granted:

  • Code the incapacity exemption as per the details on the new certificate. Do not amend the details of the non-incapacity exemption already coded on the Medical Conditions (MC) screen
  • Overlapping the dates is not an issue if applying an exemption and the decision for the previous medical certificate was to not exempt. However, if the from and to dates on the new medical certificate are the same as the previous medical certificate coded, the Event Date must be the date the information is being coded.

See the Resources page for coding examples.

Medical certificate assessment follow up actions

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Work Item activity + Read more ...

If the medical certificate is lodged at a service centre, ensure it is available on the job seeker's record. If not, scan and save it. See Scanning Centrelink medical/sensitive documents using an MFD.

If assessed and completed in Process Direct, the work item will close.

If assessed and completed in Customer First, the work item is not closed by the system. Manually complete the Change of Circumstance activity.

If the task selector is started in Process Direct but not completed, the Work Item will remain open. Check and update the work item as required.

2

Determine if an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) is required + Read more ...

ESAt referrals are not applicable for ParentsNext participants.

Once a decision is made to grant or not grant a temporary incapacity exemption, consider if an ESAt referral is appropriate to reassess the job seeker's work capacity.

An ESAt may be considered if the medical certificate and/or other available information indicates the job seeker:

  • has permanent medical condition(s) that impact their ability to work
  • has medical condition(s) that are medium or long term (24 months) in nature, including episodic or chronic conditions (for example, schizophrenia, psychosis, depression and anxiety)
  • cannot be granted a temporary incapacity exemption and may have a temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC) of less than 30 hours per week
  • has medical condition(s) that appear to be no longer of a temporary incapacity nature based on multiple medical certificates being lodged for the same condition

A referral to an ESAt should not proceed where the job seeker:

  • had medical conditions assessed through a previous ESAt that is still valid (for instance, no significant change in medical conditions)
  • the conditions are temporary (less than 13 weeks)
  • is a compulsory ParentsNext participant

Use the Online Diary to initiate an ESAt referral if required.

3

Consider recording a new job seeker circumstance + Read more ...

A medical certificate may indicate there are personal circumstances that can directly affect the job seeker's ongoing capacity to comply with their mutual obligation requirements. These job seekers may require additional support.

Update the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen where there has been a change to the job seeker's circumstances (even if a temporary incapacity exemption is not granted) that:

  • potentially impacts the job seeker's ability to participate on an ongoing basis, and
  • puts the job seeker at a higher risk of non-compliance (for example, a drug or alcohol dependency)

See Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirements.

4

Consider updating the Job Seeker Snapshot + Read more ...

Invite the job seeker to update the Snapshot if the medical evidence reveals a change in their circumstances not previously included.

Note: only update the Job Seeker Snapshot if the customer is in the service centre or on the phone.

5

Inviting a claim for Disability Support Pension (DSP) + Read more ...

Only invite job seekers to claim DSP if there is clear evidence they are likely to meet all eligibility criteria. It is not appropriate to invite a claim for DSP simply because a job seeker has a permanent condition.

Has the decision been made to invite a claim for DSP?

  • Yes, see Claiming DSP
  • No, or if eligibility is unclear based on the medical evidence, go to Step 6

6

Connection with Employment Services or ParentsNext Provider + Read more ...

For job seekers

Ensure the job seeker is connected to an appropriate provider (if not already referred) if:

  • an incapacity exemption has not been granted, and
  • the job seeker does not have
    • a temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC), or
    • a partial capacity to work (PCW) of 0-14 hours per week

Determine if the job seeker is subject to modified mutual obligation requirements. This is normally assessed from the job seeker's most recent ESAt.

See:

Approved mutual obligation requirements for customers assessed as having a partial capacity to work (PCW)

Approved mutual obligation requirements for customers assessed as having a temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC)

For ParentsNext participants

If an incapacity exemption has not been granted and the parent:

  • has not yet been referred to a ParentsNext provider, ensure they have a future ParentsNext appointment booked
  • has already been referred to a ParentsNext provider, it will be the responsibility of the ParentsNext provider to re-engage the parent. The agency is not required to undertake any follow up at the end of the exemption

7

Job seeker contact and key messages after assessment of medical certificate + Read more ...

When a temporary incapacity exemption is:

  • not granted, an automatic notification is sent to the job seeker (check the letter has been issued)
  • is granted, an automatic notification is not sent to the job seeker

If the assessment is completed without the customer present, make 2 genuine attempts to contact the job seeker to advise the outcome. Do this whether or not an exemption has been granted.

Discuss the following points with the job seeker, if appropriate:

  • Need to notify if there is a change in their circumstances
  • If not granted a temporary incapacity exemption, they will get notification they must continue to meet their mutual obligation or participation requirements. If they are unable meet their mutual obligation requirements (e.g. due to their illness or injury), they must contact their provider as soon as possible to explain why they cannot attend
  • If granted a temporary incapacity exemption and they are still unable to work or meet their mutual obligation requirements of 8 hours or more per week when their current medical certificate ends, they need to lodge a new one. An assessment will then be done to determine if a further temporary incapacity exemption can be granted

If applicable:

  • ensure the Job Plan for Centrelink managed job seeker's reflects their current circumstances, taking into account any PCW or TRWC or refer the job seeker for an ESAt if appropriate
  • negotiate a new Job Plan if required

Ending an exemption early based on new medical evidence

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Notification to end exemption + Read more ...

Job seekers who notify they no longer need an exemption from their mutual obligation or participation requirements must provide evidence from their medical practitioner. It needs to state they now have capacity to undertake work or other mutual obligation requirements for at least 8 hours or more per week.

Has new medical evidence been provided?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here as no change can be actioned without updated medical evidence

2

End exemption + Read more ...

Once medical clearance is received:

  • Manually update the Unfit to Work/Study/Participate To date on the Medical Conditions (MC) screen based on the new medical certificate. This will then auto-update the exemption end date on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen
  • For job seekers:
    • Connect or reconnect the job seeker with their provider, unless the job seeker indicates they will be meeting their requirements in a way that will make them Centrelink managed
    • If the job seeker will be Centrelink managed, negotiate a new Job Plan with the job seeker
  • For ParentsNext participants, if the parent:
    • has not yet been referred to a ParentsNext provider, the system will automatically book a new ParentsNext appointment at the end of the exemption so the parent can be referred to a ParentsNext provider
    • has already been referred to a ParentsNext provider, it will be the responsibility of the ParentsNext provider to re-engage the parent at the end of the exemption period. The agency is not required to undertake any follow up at the end of the exemption
  • Record a DOC confirming the advice given and any other relevant information disclosed by the job seeker or participant
