Pend return error messages and resolutions

Table 1

Return message

Description and resolution

4-PAR NFND

No prior approval or referral record covering the item being claimed exists for the date of service

See the Resources page of Prior Financial Authorisation (PARS Approval) for Veterans’ Affairs Processing (VAP) for more information.

4-PAR NMCH

The service is not automatically matching to the approval record

See the Resources page of Prior Financial Authorisation (PARS Approval) for Veterans’ Affairs Processing (VAP) for more information.

7-Not GSTR

The provider does not have an ABN registered in the ABN repository

7-NO RCTIA

The provider does not have a Recipient Created Tax Invoice Agreement signed with DVA

4-NOT SPEC

Provider rendering the service does not have the qualifications required for the item claimed recorded on the provider file

Resolution:

Check keying. Is keying correct?

  • Yes, resolve all other pend return messages in the claim and refer the claim to the VAP team leader
  • No, amend any errors and continue processing

Note: the VAP team leader, or nominated Service Officer contacts VAP Helpdesk and arranges for the provider file to be corrected. Do not reject with RSN code S 141 unless advised to do so by the VAP Helpdesk.

4-PAR FEE

Attempt was made to manually match a community nursing item to a prior approval record

Resolution:

  • Do not force-match the community nursing item to a prior approval record as an overpayment will occur
  • Resolve all other pend return messages in the claim
  • Refer claim to VAP team leader

Note: the VAP team leader or nominated Service Officer contacts VAP Helpdesk and arranges for the prior approval record to be corrected. This allows the services to be auto-matched. If PARs approval has not been updated reject the claim with RSN code S 279 once it reaches KPI

6-EPC NOT

The associated clinical care or personal care item number for the same 28 day period has not been claimed

Resolution:

When this message displays reject the service item S 129.

6-NURS TD

More than one item has been claimed in the allowed claiming period

This message is displayed when more than 1 item has been claimed in the allowable claiming period (28 days, 56 days or 84 days).

Note: do not override the 6-NURS TD restriction just because there is a PARS record for that item. There needs to be comments that specifically state DVA authorise more than 1 claim to be paid in that period.

Resolution:

  • Press PF2 to check PARS for a relevant prior approval record that specifically states that DVA authorises payment of additional services within the allowable claim period
  • If a relevant prior approval record exists that:
    • contains specific comments to authorise additional services, key PI 45 against each approved service
    • does not contain comments to authorise additional services, reject each service with RSN code S 263

If there is no prior approval, reject service with RSN code S 263

Business rules community nursing

Table 2

Item

Rule

1

The provider specialty code C83 205 will be used for DVA-contracted community nursing (CN) providers extending their current Deed of Standing Offers past 1 October 2014.

2

CN providers are paid through a Schedule of Item Numbers and Fees.

3

A referral is required from a General Practitioner (GP), Specialist (treating doctor in hospital), hospital discharge planner, Nurse Practitioner, or Veterans’ Home Care (VHC) assessment agency, on admission per episode of care.

4

An episode of care is defined as the period between the date of admission to and the date of discharge from community nursing services. There may be several episodes of care in one year, or an episode of care may be indefinite.

5

Payments are based on an ‘episode of care’ which is divided into 28-day claim periods.

6

CN providers may be paid an additional travel allowance when claimed through NA10.

7

If an entitled person transfers between CN providers, the new CN provider is required to obtain a referral.

8

Only one item from either the Clinical Care Core (NL01 - NL29) or Personal Care Core (NP01 - NP31) items may be claimed in a 28-day claim period per provider (maximum 2 providers in a claim period). Where appropriate and in accordance with the business rules, add-ons may be claimed in addition to the core item.

9

An add-on may be claimed based upon the visit count or upon the time (hours and/or minutes). Given the visit bandings from the opposing core and add-on schedules do not align, there will be instances where the bandings 'overlap', these will be monitored through DVA post payment monitoring (PPM) of MDS, rather than through item restrictions.

10

Additional/other items may be claimed with core and exceptional case items as required, in accordance with the business rules.

11

Classifications (item numbers) can change during an episode of care.
