Date of effect rule - Changes in activity level

Table 1

Item

Scenario

1

Notification at time of change + Read more ...

On 19 July 2018, Sue accessed their online account and updated their combined hours of activity from 40 hours per fortnight to 50 hours per fortnight as at 23 July 2018. As a result, Sue’s subsidised hours increased from 72 to 100 hours per fortnight.

The increase in eligible hours takes effect from 30 July 2018, being the start of the CCS fortnight after Sue’s activity hours increased.

2

Notification within 28 days of change + Read more ...

Peter contacts the agency on 27 July 2018 to advise their hours of study increased from 15 hours per fortnight to 36 hours per fortnight from 9 July 2018. As a result, Peter’s subsidised hours increased from 36 to 72 hours per fortnight.

The increase in eligible hours takes effect from 16 July 2018, being the start of the CCS fortnight after Peter’s activity hours increased.

3

Favourable determination + Read more ...

Jane started volunteering at the local primary school 10 hours a fortnight from 15 August 2018. Jane contacts the agency on 12 November 2018 to advise a change to their activity details. Jane was already doing paid work for 40 hours per fortnight. By adding the 10 hours of volunteering to her paid work hours Jane is now eligible to receive 100 hours of subsidy per fortnight.

The date of event for the new activity will be 15 August 2018, and the increase in eligible hours will apply from 22 October 2018 (the earliest CCS Monday in the last 28 days).

4

Unfavourable determination + Read more ...

Assad ceased studying 13 October 2018. Assad contacts the agency on 13 December 2018 to advise of this change and advise they is not engaged in any other recognised activities.

The date of event will be 13 October 2018, and the reduced hours will take effect from 22 October 2018. This means Assad received CCS for the period 22 October to 13 December to which they was not entitled, and an overpayment will be raised.

5

Commencing or increasing hours of paid work – prompt notification + Read more ...

Max contacts on 12 September 2018 to advise they will be starting a new job on 17 September (increasing his Activity Test result from 36 to 100 hours per fortnight).

Because Max notified as soon as they became aware of the change, the date of effect will be 27 August 2018. This is the start of the CCS fortnight before the fortnight in which Max started the new job.

6

Commencing or increasing hours of paid work – late notification + Read more ...

Lily contacts on 5 September 2018 to advise they started paid work on 1 August (increasing the Activity Test result from 24 to 100 hours).

Due to s105C applying in relation to notification rules, the new Activity Test result of 100 hours per fortnight will only apply from 13 August 2018. This is the earliest CCS fortnight in the last 28 days.

Had Lily notified the agency of the new employment in the CCS fortnight when starting work, or up to 28 days before starting, the increased Activity Test result would have taken effect from 16 July 2018.

Date of effect rule - Children enter care

Table 2

Item

Scenario

1

CCS start date is after the date child entered care + Read more ...

Martin lodges a claim for Child Care Subsidy 14 October 2019 advising that child Kyra has entered care 11 October 2019. When the claim is finalised on 25 October, the CCS start date for Kyra is 21 October 2019 - the first CCS Monday after the child has entered care.

2

CCS start date equals the date child entered care + Read more ...

Sue lodges a claim for Child Care Subsidy 16 December 2019 advising that child Jane entered care on 2 December 2019. When the claim is finalised on 7 January 2020, the CCS start date for Jane is 2 December 2019 as it is the first Monday of a CCS fortnight.

Date of effect rule - Changes of marital status

Table 3

Item

Scenario

1

Customer separates + Read more ...

Omar and Amal have been partnered since the beginning of the CCS year. Amal is claiming CCS for their children, which has been calculated on a combined income of $175,000. Amal contacts 17 September 2019 to advise Amal and Omar separated 13 September 2019. The new CCS percentage based on Amal's income only takes effect from 23 September - the first CCS Monday following the change in circumstance.

Where the change in marital status is the first Monday of a CCS fortnight, the change will take effect from that date.

2

Customer becomes partnered + Read more ...

Gene has been single since the beginning of the CCS year. Gene has been receiving CCS at 85% for 72 hours per fortnight.

Gene contacts on 3 January 2020 to advise Gene has become partnered from 10 October 2019. Gene's partner's income of $80,000 is added using a Date of Event of 10 October 2019 (the date of becoming partnered) and Paid Work of 80 hours per fortnight with a ATD Date of Receipt of 3 January 2020 (the date the customer notified of the change).

The change in marital status and the newly combined family income estimate for CCS will take effect from 21 October 2019 - the first Monday of the CCS fortnight following the change in circumstances.

Due to late notification, the partner's Activity Test Result will only take effect from 16 December 2019 - the earliest CCS Monday up to 28 days prior to date of notification. Gene would have nil hours of entitlement from 21 October to 15 December 2019 as there would be no activity recorded for the partner.

As a result of the late notification, an undetermined debt due to both income and Activity Test would be generated in the case.

Where the change in marital status is the first Monday of a CCS fortnight, the change will take effect from that date.

Date of effect rule - Higher CCS (Multiple Child Subsidy) changes

Table 4

Item

Scenario

1

Child turns 6 + Read more ...

Grant has 3 children under 6 years who attend child care (Sonya 5, Jake 3, and Billy 2).

Sonya is the Standard Rate Child and receives the income tested rate of CCS of 50%. Jake and Billy are the Higher Rate Children and receive the higher rate of CCS of 80%.

Sonya turns 6 on 12 April 2022 (part way through a CCS fortnight) and:

  • will no longer be considered a child in the MCS group from the following CCS Monday, and
  • will continue to receive the income tested rate of CCS of 50%

From the first CCS Monday after Sonya turns 6 (18 April 2022):

  • Jake will be assessed as the Standard Rate child and will begin receiving the income tested rate of CCS (50%)
  • Billy will continue to be a Higher Rate Child receiving the higher rate of CCS (80%)

2

Customer advises child no longer using child care + Read more ...

Tania has 2 children under 6 years who attend child care (Francine 4 and Brandon 2).

Francine is the Standard Rate Child and receives the income tested rate of CCS of 35%.

Brandon is the Higher Rate Child and receives the higher rate of CCS of 65%.

Tania advises on 18 May 2022 that:

  • Francine is no longer using child care, and
  • there is no intention for her use child care again

Francine’s CCS is cancelled.

Brandon will continued to be assessed as the Higher Rate Child until the Monday after Francine’s CCS is cancelled. From 30 May 2022, Brandon:

  • will now be assessed as the Standard Rate Child, and
  • will begin receiving the income tested rate of 35%

3

Standard Rate Child leaves care + Read more ...

Jeff and Joanne have 3 children under 6 years, all attending child care (Blake 4, Sean 3, and Jess 3).

Blake is the Standard Rate Child and receives their income tested rate of CCS of 70%,

Sean and Jess are the Higher Rate Children who receive the higher rate of 95%.

Blake leaves Jeff and Joanne’s care on the 10 October 2022. From the first CCS Monday after Blake leaves care (17 October 2022), Sean:

  • will be assessed as the Standard Rate Child, and
  • will begin receiving the income tested rate of CCS (70%)

Jess will continue to be a Higher Rate Child receiving the increased entitlement of 95%.
