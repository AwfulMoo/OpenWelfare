Advance granted on incorrect record

Table 1: This table describes the steps to follow when an advance payment has been granted on an incorrect record.

Refer all Home Equity Access Scheme cases to the Home Equity Access Scheme National team.

Step

Action

1

Advance payment on incorrect record + Read more ...

Has an advance payment been created on an incorrect record?

  • Yes, and:
    • It is the same day and Payment strip has not occurred and payment has not been issued to the customer payment destination, go to Step 2
    • Payment strip has occurred and payment has been sent to the customer’s financial institution account, go to Step 3
  • No, procedure ends here

2

Advance granted on incorrect record - error identified on same day as grant. + Read more ...

Advances incorrectly recorded on customer's records must be actioned prior to processing cut off on the same day they are created whenever possible.

Requests to have an advance payment deleted also needs to be reported via the Stop Payment request form.

Escalate to ICT Service Desk

Escalate directly to the local Service Support Officer (SSO) or Team Leader (TL) for immediate action as per local protocols. For Smart Centre staff, see Service Officer role when seeking Technical Peer Support (TPS) in Smart Centres.

Once the SSO or TL is satisfied all action has been taken and the payment must be stopped, the request must be escalate to ICT Service Desk using the mySupport webform 'Stop Customer Payment'

To refer the activity to ICT:

  • Go to mySupport, complete the Stop Payment Request form
  • Search using key word 'STOP' to locate the correct webform
  • Complete and submit the Stop Payment Request form taking note of any receipt or reference code produced

'Stop Payment' DOC

Create a 'Stop Payment' DOC on the customer's record explain why they are not entitled to the arrears payment. For audit purposes, the ICT Service Desk needs this DOC.

It must include the following details:

  • Payment type/component
  • Amount to be removed/stopped
  • Period of the payment to be removed/stopped
  • The reason the stop payment is needed. Note: recording 'customer not entitled' as the stop payment reason is not sufficient. The DOC must specify why the customer is not entitled, and
  • The relevant Operational Blueprint file which supports the decision not to pay any unpaid arrears payment (if applicable)

An example 'Stop Payment' DOC

Advance payment granted on incorrect record, payment to be STOPped

DSP advance payment was granted for $850 on 13 March 2021. Advance payment was granted on wrong customer record. Payment needs to be stopped before issue to customer and advance payment needs to be deleted from customer record. Customer has been contacted and advised that this error has occurred and customer does not wish to receive advance payment. Request made to ICT to stop payment and deleted advance – SD 2222222

When mySupport request has been lodged

Once mySupport interaction has been lodged, record the relevant interaction number in the 'Stop Payment' DOC.

3

Advance has been created on an incorrect record and payment has been issued + Read more ...

Service Officers should contact the customer to establish if they wish to keep the advance and the impact of doing so.

Does the customer wish to keep the advance?

  • Yes, DOC the customer's record stating that the advance was granted in error, the customer has been contacted and advised that they wish to retain the advance payment. Procedure ends here
  • No, if the advance has been issued to the customer’s account, the advance payment must be recalled from the customer’s financial institution.
    • If any repayments have been made, the advance must be returned by the financial institution before the repayments can be refunded
    • The customer must be warned that if the funds have not been returned, Services Australia may raise a debt against them to recover the original funds where a case of fraud is not identified or advance recovery will be recommenced from their payment
    • Go to Step 4

4

Code the request for a refund of payment from the financial institution + Read more ...

  • While in the customer's record, go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen by typing 'PS' in the Nxt: field.
  • Select the payment to be refunded by typing 'F' next to the payment line. This is the Request Refund of Payment (RFR) option. This will display the Request For Refund (RFR) screen. The reason for refund field will be defaulted to '3rd'
  • Enter the source of the information in the Source: field
  • Enter the date of request in the DOR: field
  • Enter Yes/No/Qol in the Finished field
  • Press [Enter] to complete this activity
  • Ensure the Request for Refund/Recall (RFR) activity has been automatically generated on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen to check in 14 days' time and again in 28 days, that the original payment has been returned. These activities will normally not show until daily driver (DDR) has been run on the record
  • DOC the customer records will full details of all actions taken including the conversation with the customer, that future review has been created and advance deletion will need to be requested if payment has been returned. If the payment is not returned, a debt activity will automatically be created.
  • Create a future review for 28 days for the return of the payment and for the advance deletion to be requested.

If the activity has been selected by Quality Online (QOL), the future activity may not generate immediately. In these cases:

  • Note the Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Check the FAL screen the next day. Procedure ends here

5

Suspend recovery of the advance payment + Read more ...

Run the Manage Advances action in Process Direct to modify the advance repayment.

In Process Direct:

  • Select the Customer Summary tile
  • Search for the customer
  • On the Snapshot screen, select > Manage Advances

From the Manage Advances page:

  • Select the current advance payment from the dropdown list
  • From the Action dropdown list, select Adjust Repayment

On the Adjust Advance Repayment screen, click on the twisty next to Adjust Repayments and then complete:

  • Contact method: field, select from dropdown list
  • How much can you afford to repay each fortnight?: field, code the rate of repayment as $0 to suspend recovery
  • Thinking about your current fortnightly income and your expenses… field, code the amount as zero
  • As you are requesting to reduce your repayment, are you in severe financial hardship due to a special and unusual change in your circumstances
    • If No, reject request as customer is not in hardship
  • When will you be able to increase your repayment to the minimum repayment amount?
    • Enter date
    • Note: repayments can be reduced for no more than 3 months
  • A review will automatically be created

If Process direct is unavailable, see Changes to the repayment rate of an advance payment for instructions on using Customer First.

6

DOC record + Read more ...

Ensure customer record is fully DOC'd, to assist staff member who actions the return of the advance payment.

Advance granted from a fraudulent claim

Table 2: This table describes the steps to follow when an advance payment has been granted from a fraudulent claim.

Refer all Home Equity Access Scheme cases to the Home Equity Access Scheme National team.

Step

Action

1

Fraudulent advance claim reported + Read more ...

Has a fraudulent advance claim been reported on a customer record?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Advance payment obtained fraudulently without customer consent to report the fraudulent activity for investigation. Procedure ends here

2

Fraudulent advance investigation + Read more ...

Has the investigation been completed?

  • Yes, and grant has been due to an act of fraud, ensure
    • all repayments have been refunded to the customer, Check DL screen to see if payment has been refunded and if not follow, Over recovered Advance Payment table in Changes to the repayment rate of an advance.
    • future repayments have been suspended. See Changes to the repayment rate of an advance for more information
    • a detailed DOC is placed on the customer record to advise of actions taken and reason for deletion
    • a debt is to be raised against the incorrect party if details are known
    • Go to Step 3, to report the advance to ICT for deletion action
    • Note: Mobility Allowance Advance Payment data cannot be deleted from the AVCS screens
  • Yes and grant has been determined not to have been due to fraud, advance payment recovery should be recommenced. Go to Changes to the repayment rate of an advance
  • No, investigation is not complete, no action can be taken to delete the advance until the investigation is completed. Procedure ends here until investigation complete

3

Report the activity to ICT via mySupport + Read more ...

Advances incorrectly recorded on customer's records must be actioned prior to processing cut off on the same day they are created whenever possible.

In Smart Centres, staff should use the normal escalation process. See Service Officer role when seeking Technical Peer Support (TPS) in Smart Centres.

To refer the activity to ICT:

  • Go to mySupport, complete the Stop Payment Request form
  • Search using key word 'STOP' to locate the correct webform
  • Complete and submit the Stop Payment Request form taking note of any receipt or reference code produced

'Stop Payment' DOC

Create a 'Stop Payment' DOC on the customer's record explain why the advance payment needs to be deleted. For audit purposes, the ICT Service Desk needs this DOC.

It must include the following details:

  • Advance Payment type
  • Amount to be removed/stopped
  • Start date of the advance
  • The reason the stop payment is needed. Note: recording 'customer not entitled' as the stop payment reason is not sufficient. The DOC must specify why the customer is not entitled, and
  • The relevant Operational Blueprint file which supports the decision to have the advance deleted

An example 'Stop Payment' DOC

Advance Payment to be deleted after fraud investigation is complete

Age Pension (use correct payment type) Advance payment was granted for $1000 on 14 March 2021. Customer contacted to advise that advance was not requested by them and that their online account had been hijacked by their nephew. Investigation has been completed and fraud has been established. Repayments totalling $200 have been reissued to customer and future repayments have been suspended. Advance payment needs to be deleted from customer record. Request made to ICT to delete advance – SD 111111.

When mySupport request has been lodged

Once mySupport interaction has been lodged, record the relevant interaction number in the 'Stop Payment' DOC.

ICT Contact + Read more ...

Deletion of advance by specialist ICT staff

Table 3: This table describes the steps to be followed by specialist ICT staff to delete the advance from the customer record once the fraud investigation has been completed and mySupport ticket has been placed.

Step

Action

1

Advance payment delete function + Read more ...

For Specialist staff only

Access to the advance payment delete function is limited to specialist staff with the correct ISP access level or specialist staff within the Retirements Level 2 ICT Support or the Pension Production Support Level 3 ICT Helpdesk.

Requests must be referred to these staff to process as per existing ICT referral process.

  • From the Advance Payment Summary (AVCS) screen, 's'elect the correct advance
  • On the Request Advance Details (AVR) screen, the Delete button will be enabled (for ICT Service Desk staff only)
  • 'S'elect the Delete button. The Delete Advance Payment (DAP) screen will be displayed. Deletion reasons are:
  • User Error
  • Fraudulent Claim

Note: when deleting an advance, only the advance data will be logically deleted.

Once advance data has been deleted, transfer task to Payment Level 3 helpdesk to remove payment files.

2

Deleting the advance payment files + Read more ...

For ICT Service Desk staff only

If the payment is yet to be stripped, contact the Payments Helpdesk to delete the payment using the Delete PLOO (PAYDLT) screen once the advance data has been removed.

If the payment has been returned, block the returned payment. Go to Step 3.

3

Examples of recording information in a DOC + Read more ...

For ICT Service Desk Staff only

Once the activity for deleting the advance has been finalised an automatic DOC will be created. The free text section of the DOC needs to include sufficient information to enable staff to answer any customer enquiries or any investigations to be undertaken.

Example of a DOC for instances of User Error

Summary Line: Advance payment deleted.

XXX (advance benefit type, that is, FTB, DSP) Advance granted on DD/MM/ YYYY for $NNNN.NN has been deleted due to being granted on an incorrect record.

Payment was not received by the customer.

<Free text>-------------------------------------------------------------

Example of a DOC for instances of Fraudulent Claim

Summary Line: Advance payment deleted.

XXX (advance benefit type, i.e. FTB, DSP) Advance granted on DD/MM/YYYY for $NNNN.NN has been deleted due to a fraudulent claim. Payment was not received by the customer. Investigation being undertaken.

<Free text>-------------------------------------------------------------

4

Procedure if recovery of advance has already commenced + Read more ...

For Specialist staff only

If the recovery for the advance has already commenced, advise the Service Officer to refund any recovered amounts using the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen. Procedure ends here.
