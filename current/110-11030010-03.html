Search tools and systems available when gathering information

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Customer records and files + Read more ...

The Centrelink system contains customer records in relation to social security and family assistance payments.

Digital images of scanned documents (claims, forms and documents provided by customers) can be viewed in the customer record in Customer First. See Locating digital images.

Customer files can be requested from the Records Management Unit (RMU) using the Records Management service request webform. The Resources page contains a link to the form.

2

Archived information + Read more ...

The Customer Archive Retrieval (CAR) system allows staff to retrieve, view and print archived Centrelink customer data that has been stored off-line. Records that pre-date CAR, dating back to approximately 1978, may be available on microfiche. These may be retrieved by contacting the relevant State Microfiche Officer.

The Archiving and Culling Engine (ACE) application provides the facility to archive data from any source system, hold it in an archive storage database, and retrieve the archived data over Services Australia intranet.

3

Intelligence Query (iQ) Tool + Read more ...

The Intelligence Query (iQ) Tool allows users to interrogate historical internal and external data to build an historical intelligence view of customers.

4

Online Search (OLS) facility + Read more ...

OLS draws on current and historical Centrelink customer data as well as limited data from the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) and Medicare (HIC) as user access permits.

Users can:

  • search for information, i.e. names, addresses, birth dates or bank account details
  • access Historical Family Allowance (HFA) system that holds details of endowees (and their dependents) paid Family Allowance between 1975 and 1988

For more information, see Online Search (OLS) facility.

Self service and CCTV evidence

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Investigator role and support in relation to self service evidence + Read more ...

Investigators are not expected to be technical experts but should have a reasonable understanding of self service concepts, self service options available to customers, and applications used to administer and deliver self service options.

Investigators should also have sufficient knowledge to request and understand the evidence and present it in court if needed, to make sure that all relevant evidence is obtained for an investigation and to comply with Services Australia disclosure requirements.

2

Phone Self Service evidence – retrieval and verification + Read more ...

IVR recordings can be retrieved using the Archiving and Culling Engine (ACE). For information about retrieving and saving IVR recordings, known IVR issues and missing data, see Using ACE to access archived Report Employment Income (REI) IVR recordings.

3

System and Expert Assistance + Read more ...

The Intelligence and Investigations Branch Governance and Relationship Management team:

  • identifies the expert witness
  • liaises with the expert witness regarding availability to attend court
  • drafts witness statements and arranges for the expert witness to complete and sign
  • provides the signed witness statement to the investigation team, and
  • make sure the expert witness is advised of any changes to court dates or if they are no longer needed

4

Online Services evidence + Read more ...

If the Commonwealth Director of Public Prosecution (CDPP) advises that the matter has been contested and expert witnesses are needed, the investigation team must:

  • complete ‘Attachment A – Request for Expert Witness Statement’ which is available in the Investigation Toolkit
  • send using email from the INTELLIGENCE.INVESTIGATIONS.RELATIONSHIPS team mailbox as soon as possible

The Intelligence and Investigations Branch Governance Relationship Management team will contact the relevant expert and provide a draft statement in Word format to the witness. Following any necessary amendments, the statement is signed and a scanned copy provided to the investigation team in PDF format. The original is retained by the expert to be produced in court, or provided to the CDPP before court at the request of the CDPP.

Other evidence will show that the customer accessed the self service option. Check Customer Records Access Monitor (CRAM), XPAMR, Receipt Confirmation (RC) and other relevant screens.

5

Requesting CCTV footage + Read more ...

If CCTV is needed, staff can request the footage at the commencement of the intelligence assessment or investigation to make sure any available footage is retained and stored before deletion off the system.

To request CCTV footage staff must:

  • Complete the request template
  • Get supervisor’s approval, with a valid business reason for the request
  • Send an email to the local authorised person through the team’s positional mailbox. CCTV footage request guidelines are available in the Investigation Toolkit. If the authorised person is unavailable and the request is urgent, staff can email the request to an authorised person in another site

Note: If the supervisor is listed as a person who can access the Samsung Smart View CCTV system, they cannot sign the request template. There must be a separation of duties between the approver and the actioner, this is to make sure accountability and transparency. In these circumstances, staff must seek alternative approval from another supervisor onsite.

On receipt of CCTV footage

  • analyse CCTV information
  • place CCTV footage in a secure location
  • update the investigation record

6

Storage of self service and CCTV evidence + Read more ...

When the investigation or prosecution is completed, all self service documents must be:

  • electronic evidence bar-coded
  • security classified
  • locally stored pending its eventual transfer to the Records Management Unit (RMU)

Electronic evidence includes DVDs, Compact Discs (CDs), any other electronic evidence, and any documents relating to self service.
