Checking the residential care monthly claim form

Table 1: this table describes how Service Officers check the form is complete.

Step

Action

1

Channel option + Read more ...

Residential aged care services can lodge the monthly claim form via the following channels:

  • online via the Aged Care Provider Portal (ACPP), or
  • manually via post or email with the Aged Care Entry Record (ACER) (AC021) form

Is this a manual claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No,
    • This is an online claim
    • Online claims are automatically registered through ACPP as part of the claim finalisation process
    • Service Officers must approve the finalised claim via the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP). See Step 7 in the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal home page table

2

Checking the manual claim form for completion + Read more ...

Note: a monthly claim form cannot be submitted before the completion of the claim month.

Service Officers must check the following sections for completion.

Certification of Proprietor/Authorised Agent

  • Name of person authorised to sign
  • Signature of authorised person
  • Date
  • Contact phone number

Care recipient details

  • Care recipient ID
  • Care recipient surname
  • Care recipient first name
  • Gender
  • Date of birth
  • Concessional status
  • Pensioner
  • Liability

3

Contacting the aged care service + Read more ...

Note: highlight where the aged care service has not completed mandatory fields on the manual form (for example, claim form not signed)

If any of the information in Step 2 is missing, Service Officers must contact the aged care service via email or telephone, advising:

  • what section on the form has not been completed, and
  • request the aged care service resubmit the completed claim form

Managing residential care events

Table 2: this table describes how to manage residential care events.

Step

Action

1

Event options + Read more ...

Note:

  • Aged care services can register residential care entry events via the Aged Care Provider Portal (ACPP) for online claims. See the Register individual events in ACPP table
  • An Aged Care Entry Record (ACER) (AC021) form is required to manually update entry events
  • Only the Department of Health can register or update an extra service place against an aged care service
  • Services Australia can only update an Extra service place at the care recipient level after receiving the aged care service’s advice with Department of Health’s approval

Service Officers must check the AC021 form for completion before registering the entry event. See the Aged Care Entry Record (ACER) (AC021) form for the process.

Service Officers must go to the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP) to manage residential care events.

To manage:

  • an entry, leave or departure event – see the Registering events in the Aged Care Staff Portal (Service in Context) table
  • an oxygen event, see the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage Oxygen events in residential care (Service in Context) table
  • an enteral feeding event, see the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage enteral feeding events for residential care (Service in Context) table
  • an extra service place event, see the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage service approved places (Service in Context) table

Once Service Officers have registered the relevant residential care events in the ACSP, the claim can be finalised for the relevant claim month. See Step 7 in the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal home page table.
