Actions to prevent fraud

This table highlights key areas of concern about fraud and emergency payments. Staff must continue to follow processes, including fraud processes, in the Operational Blueprint for the relevant payment.

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Be aware of warnings signs indicating possible fraud + Read more ...

Some customers will contact in their attempts to fraudulently obtain emergency payments.

They may do this to:

  • have a new Customer Reference Number (CRN) created under a false identity or under other people’s identities
  • obtain the CRN and linking code to claim online
  • check the status of their claim
  • have self-service unlocked
  • complete an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) as they have exceeded the online withdrawn claim limit
  • request a re-issue of a payment

If the customer has provided a CRN, staff must thoroughly review the customer’s record and all Display on Access Docs (DOA) DOCs.

In Process Direct:

  • Select the Notes icon from the menu bar
  • Select the arrow twisty Display on access documents. This will show all DOA DOCs on the customer’s record

In Customer First:

  • In Next key DL and then press [Enter]
  • DOA DOCs will show under Display on access documents

If there is no DOA DOC indicating fraud is suspected, look for warning signs. There may be other warning signs that a claim may be fraudulent. It may be an odd feeling or inconsistencies in what the customer says.

Some warning signs include if the customer:

  • calls from a blocked number or a different number than recorded on the record
  • cannot provide the password on a DOA DOC
  • has trouble answering Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO) questions or failed Interactive Voice Recognition (IVR) authentication
  • there are DOCs on the record (on the DL/Notes screen) indicating a fraudulent claim or compromised record, for example:
    • DOA DOC, Pre-payment integrity check, Scams and Identity Theft Helpdesk (SAITH), Locked Self-Service, Claim progress
    • FRD keywords attached to the claim, such as FRDASS/FRDCHK
    • DOA DOC titled ‘Caution Emergency Payments’
  • inconsistencies in claim details
  • wants to claim for a new address in a declared local government area (LGA) for an emergency event
  • has provided a payment destination in the claim that differs from regular payment destination
  • multiple bank accounts used across different emergency payment claims including:
    • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
    • Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP), and
    • Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA)
  • insists on needing their claim to be assessed urgently
  • indications on the customer’s record of incarceration
    • payments may be suspended or cancelled with reason imprisonment ‘IMP’
    • for example the benefit status on the customer’s record may show JSP/SUS-IMP
  • has contact phone numbers that are currently linked to other customers
    • check SMS/Email Search
  • has multiple withdrawn claims or exceeded online withdrawn claim limit
  • is unable to claim online because of unauthorised access has claimed a payment before that was previously rejected because of the validity of the claim or the customer's identity
  • is recently cancelled from an income support payment and is now claiming Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • has a conflicting story about how they have been adversely affected by a major disaster
    • for example, claiming to be a delivery driver but unable to provide drivers licence details
  • the customer calls from an overseas phone number reporting to be in an affected area
    • for example the number the customer is calling from is longer than 10 digits
  • is calling and saying they have never dealt with Centrelink and need a CRN to claim and they:
    • cannot provide details of children, parents, partner
    • cannot upload or provide details of at least one verifiable identity document, such as a Medicare card, driver’s licence, birth certificate or passport. These documents are all verified through the Documentation Verification System (DVS)
    • can only provide identity documents such as a Bank card or Proof of Age card. These documents are not verified through DVS

Do not grant the payment if there are any concerns about the validity of the claim or the customer's identity. Staff must:

  • make sure they are satisfied that the person making the claim or on the phone is who they say they are, before making any updates
  • ask questions and using the resources available to confirm the information a customer is giving
  • record a DOC in circumstances where information received from or provided to a customer will need to be documented
    • this documented information builds a history of advice and actions taken when interacting with customers (information related to suspected fraud that cannot be recorded on the record can be recorded in a tip-off)
  • process in line with current business processes

2

There is a DOC/Note on the record from Real Time Analysis or about potential fraud + Read more ...

Staff may place a DOA DOC on a customer’s record with important instructions for other staff accessing it.

Some claims may have a warning or EMG claims held by Fraud DOC indicating the claim has been quarantined from processing for a pre-payment integrity check by Real Time Analysis.

Staff must:

  • check the claim for a warning or DOC/Note indicating:
    • the claim has been held for a pre-payment integrity check
    • the customer may be victim of identity fraud
    • the customer is under current fraud investigation
    • concerns of fraud
  • thoroughly review all these DOCs/Notes and DOA DOCs to decide what action can be taken before making any updates or actioning any work items
  • follow the instructions in the DOC/Note

When a pre-payment integrity check:

  • is in progress, the claim must not be actioned unless a DOC states ‘claim cleared of detected risk’ from Real Time Analysis
    • leave the claim status as ‘In Progress’ as this will make sure that the claim is allocated to specialist fraud staff
  • has been completed, the DOC will be annotated:
    • Integrity Profiling on claim determined the claim is not valid. Real Time Analysis recommends the claim to be rejected. The claim must be rejected ‘Qualification not met’
    • Analysis of claim has been completed. Cleared of detected fraud risk only. The Service Officer must undertake a full assessment of the claim based on the eligibility criteria for the specific payment

Tell the caller:

‘Services Australia is currently experiencing high demand due to the current natural disasters. All claims are being assessed as soon as possible. You will be notified through your myGov account or via mail once your claim has been finalised.’

For more details, see the relevant claims processing Operational Blueprint.

3

Customer does not have a record + Read more ...

Some fraudulent claims occur because of fabricated records being created, highlighting the need for care to be taken when establishing a person's identity.

Staff must make sure they are satisfied that the person making the claim or on the phone is who they say they are, before creating a new record or issuing a linking code.

Proof of Record Ownership cannot be completed on a new CRN. It is more critical that customers provide verifiable identity details when a CRN is created. Take extra care to make sure that identity requirements are met when creating a new record.

Some key indicators from recent fraud activity to be aware of when creating a new record include:

  • The customer's phone number does not show and says 'anonymous'
  • The customer provides a different phone number for their record creation saying they are using someone else's phone for the contact. Check SMS/Email Search for links to other customers
  • Customers who say they are Australian born but do not have a Customer Reference Number (CRN):
    • ask where their parents were born
    • if in Australia, then may have a child record for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) purposes
  • The customer:
    • struggles to answer simple PoRO questions such as Date of Birth (DOB), partners or children’s name or DOB - do not continue with the call
    • provides:
      - proof of age or photo ID card and bank cards as their only identity documents, or
      - their Drivers Licence fails DVS and the customer advises it recently expired or license was suspended
    • will say they have a friend or partner with them who also needs to claim and passes the phone to them
    • seeks the alternative identity process and cannot provide at least one verifiable document

Note: it may be reasonable for an individual to have one of these indicators. However, the more indicators a person has, the greater the identity fraud risk.

For more details, see:

  • AGDRP, NZ DRP, DRA and NZ DRA - Claiming emergency payments
  • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment - Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment - Assisted Customer Claims

4

The customer has trouble answering PORO questions + Read more ...

Never give the customer details for them to confirm. Scammers collect these details so they can impersonate customers and access their record.

  • Determine Proof of Record Ownership by asking multiple PoRO questions in addition to CRN, name, date of birth and password:
    • Name and date of birth of parents, partner, children
    • When they travelled overseas last (check the RSIM screen)
    • Email and telephone number
    • Last known payment with Centrelink
  • Confirm that the customer does not have an existing record, see Searching for a customer on the system
    • Search with minimal fields, such as the surname and date of birth only
    • Check spelling, and ask if they have gone by any other names
    • Check if the customer's mobile phone number has been recorded on another record by conducting a SMS/Email Search
  • Staff with Online Search Facility access can perform additional searches for the customer
    • Customers born in Australia should have a Medicare (HIC) record on OLS
  • Be careful when coding the Identity Confirmation Dashboard. DVS does not verify documents such as:
    • proof of age card
    • photo ID card
    • bank cards
    • shooter or firearm licence
  • Identity requirements vary for emergency payments. Staff must make sure they follow the correct processing Operational Blueprint for the relevant emergency payment when creating a new record and issuing a linking code

If the customer has trouble answering PoRO questions, or there are doubts:

  • offer to call the customer back as per authenticating a Centrelink customer process or,
  • tell them to attend a service centre with photo identification
  • do not update the record or release any information if you doubt you are speaking to the customer or their authorised representative

See the Resources page for a link to the DVS verified documents list.

If there are concerns about the customer's identity, stolen or lost identity documents or unauthorised access, see Identity fraud.

5

Customer advises someone else has accessed their account + Read more ...

See Identity fraud, if a customer:

  • advises a claim, was lodged without their consent
  • reports unauthorised changes to their record, such as address or payment destination updates

If the customer advises they cannot access their self-service it may be because of unauthorised access.

A customer or staff member may have locked self-service because of unauthorised access. Before making any updates, including unlocking the self-service:

  • thoroughly review all DOA DOCs and DOCs to understand what action can be taken
  • be satisfied that the person attending the office or on the phone is who they say they are
  • ask questions and using the resources available to confirm the information a customer is giving
  • record a DOC with the actions taken

For more details, see Access to Centrelink self service - set up, checking, locking and resetting.

6

Customer is unable to lodge an online claim + Read more ...

Customers may not be able to claim online if:

  • they have exceeded the maximum number of claims allowable to be withdrawn for the same event
  • their self-service has been locked

Customers cannot claim:

  • AGDRP, NZ DRP, DRA of NZ DRA if they have withdrawn 2 claims for the same event online
  • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment if they have withdrawn 2 claims online

Complete all pre-ACC checks including:

  • make sure that the person making the claim or on the phone is who they say they are, before making any updates
  • check for any DOA DOCs and DOCs with warnings
  • review previous claims, including withdrawn claims
  • ask the customer why they withdrew their claims
  • record a DOC

Assess the claim as usual, checking eligibility criteria.

See:

  • Claiming emergency payments
  • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment - Assisted Customer Claims

7

Customer has recently cancelled their income support payment and is claiming Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment + Read more ...

Some customers deliberately cancel their income support payments (ISP) in order to claim Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment. They may attempt to have their payment restored after the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment has been granted.

If a customer has voluntarily cancelled their income support payment (CAN/CLR) in the 28 days before the start date of the 7 day period they are claiming Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment and has not reported employment or self-employment income in their last payment period, they may need to provide evidence that they would have worked in the period. See, Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment claims in Process Direct.

Payment cannot be restored unless the customer remains eligible for the whole period covered by the restoration. To make sure the correct payment is delivered, any changes of circumstances must be updated as part of the activity to restore the customer's record.

For more details, see Restoration of payments (CLK).

If the customer advises they did not make these updates, see Identity fraud.

8

SMS/Email search + Read more ...

In Customer First:

  • Key 'SMEM' in the Next field
  • Key the customer's mobile phone number in the SMS number field
  • Press [Enter]
  • If the phone number provided by the customer is already recorded, a list of CRNs will show
  • If checks and searches return no existing customer record, staff must be satisfied the caller is who they say they are and the information is genuine before adding a customer to the system
  • If there are concerns about the customer's identity, stolen or lost identity documents or unauthorised access, see Identity fraud

9

Verify information or evidence + Read more ...

If there any concerns about the validity of the claim:

  • Use the resources available to confirm the information a customer is giving
  • Tell the customer:
    • 'We are not denying your claim, we just need to verify a few more details before proceeding'
    • 'You understand that giving false or misleading information is a serious offence, you understand that we can make relevant enquiries to ensure you receive the correct entitlement'
  • Make sure the customer has legal access to the bank account recorded for payment destination in the claim
    • It must be in their own name, or their name must appear on the joint account
  • Make sure the residency requirements are correct
    • To avoid granting payments incorrectly, make sure all residence data is coded and manually check the customer meets the visa requirements as per current disaster coding and assessing processes
  • Request evidence
    Staff must check the Operational Blueprint for the payment for relevant examples. These may include:
    • photos of damage
    • invoices
    • payslips to verify employment
    • letter from employer
    • proof of direction from relevant authority
    • lease agreement
    • phone or utility bill
  • DOC the record if the claim must not be granted because of concerns about the validity of the claim or the customer's identity
  • Do not grant the payment. Claims can be rejected as per processes in the Operational Blueprint for the relevant payment
  • Claims must be assessed against the eligibility criteria for the payment
  • Ask questions such as the following, noting this is not a full list. Do not prompt or provide customers with the answers to questions:
    • AGDRP/NZ DRP
      - 'How deep was the flood water in the house?'
      - 'Did you have to replace your contents or repair damage?'
      - 'Could smoke or ash damage be fixed with cleaning?'
      - 'Is the interior or exterior of your home damaged?'
      - 'Was there anything else on your property that was damaged?'
      For customers where the ACS screen shows they rent:
      - 'Was the landlord/property manager advised of the damage?'
      For owners:
      - 'Have you made an insurance claim for the damage?'
    • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
      - 'Can you work and earn your usual income?'
      - 'Do you or someone that you care for, have evidence of a positive test result for COVID-19 from a testing centre (this includes pathology centres) or health professional)?'
      - 'Do you have paid leave to cover the period of self-isolation/quarantine?'
    • See the relevant Operational Blueprint for the payment for more questions
  • Factors to consider:
    • Has the claimant voluntarily cancelled their Income Support Payment (ISP) before submitting a Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment claim?
    • Was the claimant declaring earnings for the employer referenced in the claim before the ISP cancellation?
    • Are details from recent claims consistent with current claim details?
    • Are there any scanned documents with evidence of employment?
    • Is the Australian Business Number (ABN) valid and consistent with the employer details provided? Check ‘ABN Lookup’ online
    • Check if the employer’s mobile phone number has been recorded on another record by conducting a SMS/Email Search in Customer First. It may be linked to themselves or close family member instead of their employer
    • Has there been concerns about the validity of the claim and the customer been raised? Check the DOCs on the DL/Notes screen for:
      - Claim progress notes
      - Pre-payment integrity checks
      - Scams and Identity Theft Helpdesk (SAITH) contact
      - Locked self-service and unauthorised access
      - Fraud Investigation

The customer must have legal access to the account. It must be in their own name, or their name must appear on the joint account.

If staff have any concerns about the payment destination:

  • check the Payment Summary (PS) screen and DOCs/Notes to identify any significant discrepancies in payment destination or warnings of unauthorised payment destination updates
  • check scanned Documents for documents demonstrating account ownership, for example, bank statements
  • make sure the customer is who they say they are and they have legal access to the account before changing payment destinations
  • offer to call the customer back, or attend a service centre with photo identification and/or proof of account ownership

For more details, see Payment destination.

10

Check for false images for AGDRP/NZ DRP/NHE claims + Read more ...

Documents provided as supporting evidence can be found in the 'Documents' tab in Process Direct or 'Document Tools' in Customer First.

Staff who have web access are able to conduct their own internet search or reverse image search to check and identify if the image provided by the customer was sourced from the internet.

Internet Search

  • Search the term, which matches what you are looking for. For example, flood house damage or fire damage
  • Select the option for Images at the top of the page
  • Identify the image in the search results
  • Find the source webpage by right clicking on the image and selecting Open Link

Reverse Image Search

  • Open the image uploaded by the customer
  • Snip the image using the Windows Snip and Sketch application
  • Save the image as a PNG, JPEG/JPG, GIF, TIFF, BMP or Web file
  • Search using an image in Google and Bing Visual Search by dragging the image into the search field
  • Identify the image in the search results
  • Find the source webpage by selecting the image or link which the image appears in

Note: the reverse image search function would be unsuitable in the instance where the image/s has personal information or an opinion that could identify an individual.

To identify if an AGDRP/DRA image has been identified as being false, search the agency's false image repository:

  • Open the false image repository intranet page
  • Select the link for the relevant disaster:
    • Fire/smoke/ash damage
    • Flood/water/rain/cyclone damage
  • Search the images using keywords (for example, ceiling, mould, carpet) or navigate between the image groups to visually search

11

The customer changed their address to a disaster declared area + Read more ...

Customers may give an address to support their claim for an emergency payment.

Be aware of customer's changing their address to a declared LGA for the emergency event.

Staff can perform checks to assess the validity of claimed location. Check:

  • claimed address in recent previous claims, including withdrawn claims
  • DOCs/Notes for 'In person' contacts during the period of claimed emergency event
  • updated accommodation is consistent with previously verified accommodation on the ACS/AC screen/s and Documents
  • if the customer is calling from an overseas phone number reporting to be in an affected area

Staff can search Google maps for the address to assess if the address is authentic:

  • Open Google maps on the internet browser
  • Search the customer's 'principal place of residence' using:
    • full street address (including street number)
    • street name and suburb only
  • View the address in:
    • Google Street View
      - drag and drop the yellow Pegman onto the map address
    • Satellite map
      - select 'Satellite' from the Layers selection box

12

Potential fraud - unsure if to report + Read more ...

When assessing a claim, staff may suspect a customer is acting fraudulently if they:

  • have or want to change their address to one within a declared area just after a disaster event or moved interstate to a state or area not resided in previously
  • receive regular payments and want to change their payment destination to an account they have not used before (referred to as 'payment redirection')
  • have submitted inconsistent responses to how they were adversely affected
  • have contact phone numbers that are currently linked to other customers' records
  • have claim/s for the same event that were previously rejected as they did not meet the definition of 'adversely impacted' and their claim now has different impact reasons
  • have claim/s that were previously rejected because of the validity of the claim or the customer's identity and no further evidence has been provided

Before submitting a fraud report, first try the steps above to verify the information provided. Contact the customer to clarify any concerns and to request evidence if appropriate.

After contacting the customer and reviewing evidence (if applicable), if:

  • the Service Officer is unsure whether to report a situation that they think may be suspected fraud, contact Technical Peer Support (TPS)
  • fraud concerns have been resolved, continue processing the claim as per current process
  • the Service Officer believes the customer has acted fraudulently report it

13

Report the suspected fraud + Read more ...

Staff should report suspected fraud if they:

  • have concerns about the identity of a person making a claim, or
  • suspect the customer is acting fraudulently when claiming an emergency payment and checks completed indicate fraud may be occurring

Important points when reporting suspected fraud:

  • Do not investigate the record any more than is needed to complete regular duties
  • Do not search the system for customer information in order to complete the report
  • Only provide details in the report that were received or became apparent during the interaction with the customer or processing of the claim
    • For example, the documents did not verify via DVS, the inconsistencies in information that was provided by the customer, the actions taken

The claim should be rejected as per current process.

If the customer deliberately provided false information, report it:

  • The Fast Fraud tip-off form is used to report suspected fraud related to an emergency payment
  • The Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption form can be used to report suspected fraud and corruption by a customer, staff members, or an individual or business outside of the agency
  • For more details, see Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption
  • Tell the customer their claim cannot be granted as eligibility has not been established
  • Do not:
    • tell the customer their claim will be escalated because of possible fraud
    • grant the claim
  • Complete a DOA DOC as per processes in:
    • Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment claims in Process Direct
    • Processing AGDRP and NZ DRP claims in Process Direct
    • Processing Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) claims in Process Direct
    • Processing DRA and NZ DRA claims in Customer First
