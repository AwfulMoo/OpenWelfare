Commencement or return to self-employment

Table 1: This table describes the steps for cancelling income support payments, requesting information relating to assessing business income, recording business details and assessing unemployment status.

Step

Action

1

Recording business details + Read more ...

Note: the script 'Self Employment/Commission/ Contract' is the preferred method for recording business details. Continue with manual coding below if script is not available.

Assistance with business

If the customer wishes to have assistance with starting their own business, see New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS).

Does the customer wish to cancel their payments?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Customer wishes to cancel payments + Read more ...

Where the self-employment income is known or a reasonable estimate can be provided, information is coded on the Business Detail (BUS) screen. For manual coding details, See Step 3 in the Coding self-employment and finalising assessment table.

Income support payments will cancel automatically where income is above the threshold. If customer also has employment income they may be eligible for:

  • the accrual and depletion of working credits and/or
  • 6 fortnights at nil rate of payment

Where income is unknown, a reasonable estimate cannot be provided, or the income did not preclude payment:

  • for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance (YA), cancel payment using reason 'EMP' via the Benefit Action (BA) screen
  • for Partner Allowance (PA), Widow Allowance (WA) or Austudy, cancel payment using reason 'OTH' via the BA screen and issue a Q134 explaining the reason for cancellation

Date of effect is the date self-employment started.

Before finalising activity, check if the customer is eligible to receive a Special Employment Advance (SEA). A customer can only receive one of these payments. SEA applications can only be taken at a service centre.

Procedure ends here.

3

Sole trader and partnerships + Read more ...

  • For sole trader and partnership business structures, issue customer with a Module F (Mod F) - Business details
  • For trusts, issue customer with a Module PT - Private trust (Mod PT)
  • For companies, issue customer with a Module PC - Private company (Mod PC)
  • Set up a manual review for its return
  • Check if customer is on variable reporting. If so, remove customer from variable reporting
  • Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here until modules returned
  • Upon return of form, go to Step 4

4

Module has been returned + Read more ...

  • For sole trader and partnership businesses, assess the income from self-employment. See sole traders or partnerships
  • For trusts and companies:
    • The case must be referred to a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) to assess the customer's involvement in the business
    • Ensure the relevant documents and modules are scanned for processing. Run the CAO referral script or CAO Referral Guided Procedure and forward it to the region code CAO
    • CAOs will assess the income from involvement in a business using the financial documents and the Mod PT/Mod PC. The CAO will then index the business and code the income and assets for these businesses using a unique entity record in Environment A, which is linked to, and sends information to, the customer's record for assessment purposes
  • For job seekers, if the customer's income reduces their rate to nil and:
    • is from self-employment only, coding the self-employment income details will cancel the customer's payment immediately. See Step 2 in the Coding self-employment and finalising assessment table
    • some of that income is employment income the customer can use their working credits to offset their employment income

The customer may also be able to remain current at nil rate for up to six fortnights and retain certain benefits (if a customer has been assessed as having a partial capacity to work, they may also retain their Pensioner Concession Card (PCC)).

Note: the customer cannot use their working credits to offset income from self-employment.

If the customer is currently receiving:

  • WA or PA, go to Step 5
  • YA (full-time student) or Austudy, go to Step 6
  • JSP or YA (job seeker), go to Step 7

5

WA or PA + Read more ...

  • Advise customer of income test
  • Advise customer if their WA or PA payment is cancelled and they wish to reclaim, they must claim another income support payment such as JSP
  • See Step 2 in the Coding self-employment and finalising assessment table

6

YA (full-time student) or Austudy + Read more ...

  • Check if the customer continues to be enrolled in and undertaking a full-time study load
  • Advise customer that their YA or Austudy payments will continue if they continue to satisfy the Activity Test as a full-time or concessional study load student
    • If customer has ceased to study full-time code the Education Course Details (EDC) screen appropriately. Payments will auto-cancel. Procedure ends here
    • If customer is still studying full-time, and their only income is from self-employment, see Obtaining an estimate of business income for the process to follow. If the customer has employment income as well then they must be coded as a two weekly reporter. Advise customers that they cannot declare their self-employment income via self-service. See Step 2 in the Coding self-employment and finalising assessment table

7

Determine whether the customer is unemployed + Read more ...

To establish this, consider the following facts:

  • Hours spent working in the business and activities conducted
  • Are they continuing to look for full-time work while operating the business, and if so what type?
  • Does the customer intend to resume self-employment?

8

Unemployment determination + Read more ...

If the customer is:

  • Not unemployed, see Step 1 in the Coding self-employment and finalising assessment table
  • Unemployed, see Step 2 in the Coding self-employment and finalising assessment table

Coding self-employment and finalising assessment

Table 2: This table describes the coding required relating to the customer's employment status and the coding of self-employment details. Portions of this process are for Complex Assessment Officers (CAOs) only.

Step

Action

1

Not unemployed + Read more ...

Take the following action:

  • Advise the customer of their mutual obligation requirements. See Applying mutual obligation requirements
  • Cancel the customer's JSP or YA as they are not satisfying their mutual obligation requirements. See Coding a cancellation
  • Advise customer of their continued entitlement to a Health Care Card (HCC) for six months (if they have been in receipt of payments for 12 months)
  • Advise customer to recontact Services Australia as soon as possible for advice on reclaiming if the business does not succeed

If the customer:

  • has a partner on income support, reassess partner's entitlement, then go to Step 2
  • does not have a partner on income support, record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

2

Unemployed + Read more ...

Complete the following tasks:

  • Advise customer how income from self-employment affects their payments. Staff can access the Combined Rate Estimator in Customer First. For help, see Online estimator options
  • Advise customer how to declare income from self-employment. The customer must still fulfil their usual reporting requirements if they also have employment income
  • If in receipt of JSP or YA (other) advise the customer that they must satisfy their mutual obligation requirements. If in receipt of Youth Allowance (student) or Austudy, remind the customer of their continued obligation to satisfy their respective Activity Tests
  • Advise the customer that if they are in receipt of JSP/YA (other) with a partial capacity to work, they may satisfy their mutual obligation requirements if they are working a minimum of 15 hours per week and declaring at least the minimum wages for this period. Note: their work capacity may be reviewed if they work more than 60 hours per fortnight over a period of three consecutive fortnights
  • If the customer is the principal carer of a dependent child, they may satisfy their compulsory participation requirements if they are working a minimum of 15 hours per week (or more than 30 hours per fortnight) and declaring at least minimum wages for this period
  • Input a review to follow-up profit and loss statements. See Setting up Manual Reviews
  • If the customer has a partner on income support, reassess partner's entitlement

3

Code income from self-employment + Read more ...

For sole traders and partnerships, if customer has already earned self-employment income, code this income, if known, on the Business Detail (BUS) screen. Go to Step 6.

For trusts and companies, the case must be referred to a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) to assess the customer's involvement in the business. Ensure the relevant documents and modules are scanned for processing. Run the CAO referral script or CAO Referral Guided Procedure and forward it to region code CAO. Go to Step 7.

4

Check REBS + Read more ...

Go to the Real Estate/Business Summary (REBS) screen

Are there any business income and assets already entered on the REBS screen?

  • Yes, 's'elect the appropriate line and press [Enter]. Go to Step 5
  • No, code the Real Estate/Business Identifying (REBI) screen with the business type and address details. The system will present the Business Detail (BUS) screen after the REBI screen has been updated. Go to Step 5

5

BUS screen + Read more ...

  • Enter the date the change in circumstances is to be applied in the Event Date: field
  • If the business owns real estate, code 'Y' in the Link New Real Estate?: field
  • Enter the source of the business valuation in the Valuation Source: field
  • Enter the value of customer's assets from the business in the Net Assets Customer $: field
  • If the customer's partner has assets in the business, enter the value of these assets in the Net Assets Partner: field
  • Enter the total income from the business in the Income p.a. $: field
  • If the customer's partner has assets in the business, enter:
    • Both the percentage income for the customer and the percentage income for the partner in the % Income (C/P): field, and
    • Both the percentage earned by the customer and the percentage earned by the partner in the Income Earned (C/P)?: field
  • Enter the source of the information in the Source: field
  • Enter the date of receipt of information in the DOR: field

6

What to do next + Read more ...

  • If the business is linked to real estate code the Real Estate Detail (RE) screen, then finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • If not linked to real estate, finalise the activity on the AR screen
  • Record details in a DOC

7

Code income from business + Read more ...

For Complex Assessment Officers (CAOs) only

  • Code the income and assets for the business(es) on the entity record(s) in Environment A
  • For coding details, CAOs should see Assessment of income and assets from trusts and companies post 1 January 2002
  • Record details in a DOC
