Notifying stakeholders in Services Australia

Table 1: when and how staff must escalate incidents of customer aggression and counterproductive behaviour to other business areas in Services Australia (the agency).

Item

Action

1

When to escalate to the Operations Centre + Read more ...

Customer aggression incidents that may require escalation include the following:

Customer aggression

This includes:

  • Serious verbal abuse or threats
  • Physical violence
  • Property damage
  • Police or ambulance involvement
  • Site closure
  • Code Grey or Code Black procedure

Staff welfare

This can be any issue or incident that may affect one or more agency staff, such as:

  • Being harassed by a customer outside of work hours
  • Needing an ambulance at work

Community or external events and threats

These include:

  • Bomb threat
  • Suspicious substances, such as white powder
  • Site evacuation
  • Code Grey or Code Black procedure
  • Security breach

Media

This is any issue or incident which appears in the media, or has the potential to do so.

If an incident disrupts service delivery, complete a business disruption notification. See When to escalate a business disruption.

Note: when in doubt, discuss with a Team Leader or Manager. The Team Leader or Manager can email the Ops Centre for advice. The team is available 24 hours, 7 days a week for urgent enquiries and escalations.

2

Completing the Escalation Template + Read more ...

Notify all escalations to the Ops Centre using the Escalation Template.

The Escalation Template requires the following details:

  • type of incident
  • location
  • date and time
  • a description of the incident. Include background information that may be relevant or have triggered the incident, such as a Managed Service Plan history
  • action taken in response to incident
  • support offered to affected staff
  • identification or agency reference numbers
  • customer’s identity
  • executive and onsite contact for further information

The relevant National Manager must approve the completed Escalation Template before sending it to the Ops Centre.

See the Resources page for a link to the Escalation Template and contact details for Ops Centre escalation team.

3

Security incidents + Read more ...

A security incident can be an actual occurrence or a threat that something will occur. It can happen face to face, over the telephone, online, on social media, and in writing.

Examples include:

  • assault (actual or threatened), including hitting, spitting and pushing
  • threats to go to a site with a weapon
  • damage to property as a result of customer aggression
  • asset and information incidents:
  • loss or compromise of customer information
  • unauthorised access to an agency’s premises
  • theft or loss of Australian Government property

When a security incident involves customer aggression, document it in in the Customer Incident Management System (CIMS) or Customer Incident Recording Tool (CIRT). See Customer aggression - Reporting and recording incidents.

CIMS and CIRT will generate an automatic email to Security when an incident is recorded, and any of the following apply:

  • it is of moderate or serious severity, and it contains an actual or threat of assault or self-harm, or actual damage to property
  • any of the following checkboxes have been selected:
    • release of information to police
    • release of CCTV footage
    • security adviser required
    • code black
    • code grey
    • site closed or police notified

When Security receives the email, they will contact either the Regional Security Advisor (RSA) or local representatives before taking any action.

See the Resources page for a link to the Security Hub and RSA contact details.

4

Threat relates to National Security or refers to terrorist organisations - escalate to Security Branch + Read more ...

If a threat relates to National Security or refers to terrorist organisations:

  • the staff member must tell their manager or team leader
  • the manager or team leader must report the incident to Security Branch
  • The Security Branch will assess the risk and advise what to do next

Note: if there is an immediate threat to anyone’s safety, manage the situation in line with existing processes and protocols, including:

  • contacting the police
  • contacting the Security Hotline

See Resources for links to security incident reporting and RSA contact details.

5

Malicious Call Trace (MCH) in the non-customer facing environment – Services Australia Workspace users + Read more ...

Use the MCH process for any matter that should involve the police, such as:

  • bomb threats
  • threatened violence
  • concern for a customer's wellbeing

Start the MCH process in Services Australia Workspace. For more information, see Responding to phone threats to harm staff or agency property.

See the Resources page for a link to the Services Australia Workspace Quick Reference Guide.

6

Social work referrals + Read more ...

A customer involved in an incident of aggression or counterproductive behaviour may require social work assistance. If this is identified, consider making a referral to Social Work Services. See Social work service referral.

Suicide or self-harm concerns

If a customer discloses suicide or self-harm concerns through any channel, refer them to Social Work Services for assistance.

  • for Centrelink and Medicare, see Customers talking about suicide or self-harm.
  • for Child Support, see Contact with Child Support customers Table 16

Other referral reasons

See more information about other social work referral reasons:

  • for Centrelink, see Social work service referral
  • for Child Support, see Social worker and other service referrals for Child Support customers

7

When to escalate a business disruption + Read more ...

A business disruption is anything that interrupts normal operations or processes and affects the agency’s ability to deliver services. It can be a temporary or long-term interruption.

Business disruptions may include:

  • a localised incident causing limited access to services
  • evacuation or office closures, such as lockdowns, Code Black, or bomb threats

For any business disruption, notify the Operations Centre (Ops Centre) using the Business Disruption Advice notice. Send the notice within 15 minutes or as soon as it is practically safe to complete the report.

Complete the report on the Business Disruption Notification page in SharePoint. See Resources for a link.

See the Resources page for further information about business disruption and notification processes.

8

When to tell the Digital Media and Media teams + Read more ...

If a customer says that they are recording an interaction with any type of device, or threatens to go to the media or social media, notify a manager. Do this even if it is considered unlikely the customer will follow through on the threat.

The manager will assess the threat and may decide to refer details to the Media Section.

See the Resources page for a link to media protocols.

See Recording interactions for what to do when people are making a recording or taking photos during interactions.

All staff

Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE) or Employment Services Providers + Read more ...

When a customer is an active job seeker and is involved in a threatening, aggressive or violent incident, Services Australia must disclose information to DESE or Employment Service Providers.

Immediate notification required

Staff must immediately tell a manager when a job seeker is aggressive (through any channel) if either of the following apply:

  • The customer says that they will go immediately to their Employment Service Provider's office
  • The customer is close to the provider's office

The manager must call the provider to tell them about the incident and the customer’s likely attendance. If the manager is unavailable, the staff member must call.

The provider’s telephone details are located on the customer’s record in Customer First. For service centres, local service providers’ contact details are recorded in the site’s Emergency Response Procedures - Aggression (ERPA) key contacts page.

See the Resources page for a link to the Emergency Response Procedures – Aggression (ERPAs) page.

If the customer makes a threat, get help to escalate the issue. See Customer aggression - Response.

Automatic notification with CIMS

The Customer Incident Management System (CIMS) automatically sends information to DESE whenever an incident is recorded involving a registered job seeker.

The details sent are:

  • Local contact User ID
  • Incident ID
  • Incident status
  • Incident time and date
  • Type and nature of incident
  • Severity level of incident
  • Emergency services notified
  • Site Security/Safety events
  • Incident Description
  • Details of the customer’s Managed Service Plan, if they have one. See Customer aggression - Managed Service Plan (MSP)

Department of Education, Skills and Employment and Services Australia staff

Receiving and managing advice from the Department of Education, Skills and Employment + Read more ...

Employment Service Providers (ESPs) may experience aggressive behaviour when they deal with a Services Australia customer. This includes threats made to Services Australia staff or service centres. ESPs have been asked to escalate these threats. This is to keep staff and other customers safe.

  • First, they should try to call the Services Australia service centre closest to them or the threat to tell them about the risk. This allows the service centre to do their own risk assessment and decide how to follow up
  • If they cannot contact the service centre or they are unsure who to call, they should call the Security Hotline. The Regional Security Advisors (RSAs) will escalate the issue appropriately
  • In an emergency, ESPs should call 000

When an Employment Services Provider records an incident in DESE’s system, it will automatically send information about it to CIMS. Services Australia staff can view recent and older incidents. See Customer aggression - Reporting and recording incidents.

When a job seeker contacts Services Australia to discuss the relationship with their Employment Services Provider, encourage them to talk with the provider first. Staff should only tell customers to contact DESE’s customer service directly in limited circumstances. For more information, see Breakdown of the relationship between a job seeker and their Workforce Australia Employment Services Provider.
