Adjust an underpaid AMD

Table 1: This table describes the process to adjust an underpaid amendment (AMD) for Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

Step

Action

1

Check item number + Read more ...

Check the item number involved is not listed under Category specific AMD procedures.

2

Determine the correct fee + Read more ...

Consult Medicare's payment enquiry system, Operational Blueprint or the Medical Benefits Schedule (MBS) to determine the correct fee.

3

Check patient history for amount already paid + Read more ...

Check patient history to determine the amount already paid and subtract this from the amount required.

Note:

  • When Goods and Services Tax (GST) is applicable, calculate using pre-GST amounts.
  • Changing items from GST to non-GST or vice versa cannot be performed through the system - a file fix is required to change these items. See also: Request a file fix for claims in Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP) for file fix procedures.

4

Key the claim + Read more ...

Data enter the claim; key the adjusted amount in the Amount field and press [Enter]

5

Does the adjustment item restrict with the service already paid + Read more ...

The adjusted item may restrict with the service already paid on history.

Override the restriction with the appropriate PI code 43, 45, 58 or 61.

When the restriction cannot be overridden, request a restriction lift from the VAP Helpdesk.

When a restriction lift cannot be performed, a manual cheque needs to be issued to pay the adjusted amount.

See also: Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP) for contact information.

6

Ensure the amount is correct + Read more ...

Ensure the correct amount appears in the PAID field.

When the correct amount does not display in the PAID field, refer to a Programme Support Officer (PSO).

7

Finalise the claim + Read more ...

Key AMD in the RSN field and press [Enter].

Adjust a travel not paid AMD

Table 2: This table describes the process for amendment (AMD) adjustments involving travel not paid KM (an allowance by DVA for kilometres travelled) or OT80 (a Remote Area Allowance paid to Occupational Therapists) for veterans.

Step

Action

1

Check patient history for item + Read more ...

Check patient history to determine the consultation item the travel is required for. The consult item will need to be keyed to enable payment of travel.

Note: when travel has been partially paid a file fix is required and a subsequent manual cheque is to be issued.

2

Key the item and travel details + Read more ...

At data entry, key:

  • the consultation item and travel
  • key ? in the Amount field for the consult item and press [Enter]

3

Key the amount + Read more ...

In pend, 1-INV CHG return message appears, key 01 (1c) in the Amount field for the consult item and press [Enter].

Note: for those items that attract GST key 5 (5c) in the Amount field.

4

Does the item restrict with the service already paid? + Read more ...

The consultation item may restrict with the service already paid on history. Override the restriction with the appropriate PI code.

When pend return message is:

  • 6-SUS DUP, key PI 43 and press [Enter]
  • 6-RST TD, key PI 45 and press [Enter]

The error message 8-BEN INCR is displayed.

5

Override the restriction + Read more ...

Key PI 58 and press [Enter].

1- INV FEE is displayed.

6

Finalise the claim + Read more ...

Key:

AMD in the RSN field, 01 (1c) in the Fee field for the consult item and press [Enter]

Note:

  • for those items that attract GST, key 5 (5c) in the Amount field
  • the system will default the fee for the consultation item to 5c. The travel will pay as normal.

Adjust payment of services made by Medicare to an eligible DVA client

Table 12: This table describes the process to adjust a payment that has been made by Medicare where the client is eligible to be paid by DVA.

Step

Action

1

Check veteran's history + Read more ...

Check the veteran's file number to identify that the service has been paid by Medicare. This will be identifiable by checking the file number against which the service has been processed.

Key VHOI, veteran's File Number, ITEM number and Press [Enter]

Press [PF10] to show the File No that payment was made to:

  • 10 digit Medicare number indicates payment was made by Medicare
  • veteran's file number indicates payment made by DVA

2

Check veteran's eligibility + Read more ...

Check the veteran's eligibility to ensure that the veteran is either:

  • Gold Card holder
  • White Card holder with accepted disabilities that relate to the service being provided
  • Has prior DVA approval for service to be paid by DVA

Key: VEXI, veteran's File Number and press [Enter]

Go to Step 3.

3

Gold Card holder + Read more ...

Is the veteran a Gold Card holder?

  • Yes, go to Step 6.
  • No, check for White Card accepted disabilities. To check White Card accepted disabilities, press [PF10], go to Step 4.

4

White Card holder + Read more ...

Does the veteran have a White Card with accepted disabilities?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, check for DVA Prior approval to the service being provided, press [PF2] and locate corresponding approval, go to Step 5.

5

DVA prior approval + Read more ...

Does the veteran have DVA prior approval for service to be paid by DVA?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, the veteran is not a Gold Card holder, does not have the accepted disabilities or prior DVA approval, then they are not eligible to claim through DVA. Advise the provider that no further action is required.

6

Provider to refund money + Read more ...

If the veteran is eligible for DVA payment and payment has already been made by Medicare, the provider is required to refund the money paid by Medicare for the DVA eligible service.

Advise the provider that they will be required to refund the money back to Medicare, along with an explanation as to why the money is being refunded.

Once Medicare receives the money from the provider, the services will be removed from the patient’s history.

7

Provider to resubmit claim to VAP + Read more ...

Once the VAP Service Officer can confirm that the service no longer appears on the veteran's history, the provider may resubmit their claim to VAP for full payment of the services by DVA.

8

Partial Payment of Surgery Items + Read more ...

Normal business rules apply when providers make claims for multiple service items with both Medicare and DVA (for example, a provider is unable to claim the same service from both Medicare and DVA. If the service forms part of a multiple procedure, the notation of times/areas are still required).

Where an item is submitted to DVA and has previously been paid by Medicare, it should be rejected RSN 258 - Medicare benefits paid; no separate DVA attendance evident.

Where a part of multiple surgery items have been claimed by Medicare and the veteran is eligible for DVA, refer to Steps 1-4 above and advise the provider to resubmit the claim in its entirety to VAP. If the provider submits their claims online, they must not resubmit their claim until it has been removed from the veteran's history (see Steps 6 & 7).

Where the veteran is a White Card holder and the services paid by Medicare are not covered as part of the veteran’s acceptable disabilities, then payment can remain with Medicare. DVA services may only be paid if they are covered by the veteran’s acceptable disabilities.

9

Medicare and DVA same day attendance claims + Read more ...

Where a patient’s treatment is covered under a veteran's White Card holder’s acceptable disabilities, attendances are payable under DVA.

Where a patient’s treatment is not covered under a veteran's White Card holders acceptable disabilities, attendances are payable under Medicare.

Where there are two separate attendances, a second consultation can be paid.
