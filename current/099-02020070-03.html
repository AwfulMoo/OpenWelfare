Actioning the death of the partner of a PA customer

Step

Action

1

Notification of death received + Read more ...

When a person contacts to advise about the death of a customer, staff must offer:

  • A social worker referral in the first instance or at any time the notifier becomes or shows signs of distress during the contact. See Referring customers to a social worker for more information
  • The 'Needing help after someone has died? (HD001)' pamphlet. Issue if accepted

Consider and respect the following cultural issues when being advised of the death of an Indigenous customer.

  • The name of the deceased person cannot be mentioned by immediate family members. This means immediate family members may have to ask other relatives/friends to call on their behalf or attend the interview with the notifier, because they cannot say the name of the deceased
  • In this situation, it may also be offensive to the person for the Service Officer to say the name of the deceased customer, if unsure, Service Officers should sensitively ask the person if it is all right for them to use the deceased customer's name. If the person is not comfortable with this, avoid using the deceased customer's name wherever possible. See Cultural Considerations when someone dies

Does the customer's record indicate that they were income managed, as shown by INM on the benefit status line?

  • Yes, follow process to update record for death of an income managed customer. Return to this procedure and go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Confirm the death of the deceased customer + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure in the deceased customer's record to determine if death can be confirmed.

Confirmation of death can be accepted where the customer's full name is provided together with 2 of the following items:

  • deceased customer's last address in full
  • date of birth and age at death
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • name of partner

Confirm if the person notifying of the death is the executor of the estate. If the informant is not the executor, but knows the executor details, obtain the:

  • name and address of the executor, and
  • name of the funeral director or hospital

See Confirmation of the death of an adult or child.

Nominee arrangements and Power of Attorney (POA) stop at the time of death. Any correspondence, including the payment summary and possible debt recovery action from a deceased estate, will be sent to the:

  • executor, or
  • administrator of an estate

Services Australia's ability to finalise any outstanding matters will be impacted if executor or administrator details are:

  • incorrectly recorded, or
  • not recorded when details have been provided

Where executor details are not known at the time of death notification, leave the executor details fields in the Notification of death guided procedure blank.

Note: executor details can be recorded manually on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen if details become known later.

Can informant supply enough information to confirm death?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Death cannot be confirmed + Read more ...

If the death cannot be confirmed:

  • use the Notification of death guided procedure to obtain and record all relevant information from the informant including their own name, address, contact number and relationship to the deceased, and if known, details of the executor, the name of the funeral director, and the name of the hospital
  • the guided procedure will not record the death, but will record details on a DOC which creates an open work item to be allocated to a Service Officer with the required skill set to confirm the death
  • if the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item. Use Fast note - select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED

Where multiple notifications are received in relation to the death of the same person do not re-run the guided procedure. Check the record and annotate the original DOC to state details are correct and who advised.

If there is doubt about the identity of the deceased, contact the funeral director or the hospital where the deceased passed away. If this is not an option and contact with relatives is proposed, social worker involvement may be necessary. Where appropriate, send Request for Verification (SS257) form.

Where death cannot be confirmed and the customer's whereabouts cannot be established, payment is to be suspended until the death can be confirmed. The suspension date of event should be the unconfirmed date of death or the date paid to plus one (DPT + 1), whichever is the later:

  • select the suspension option in the Notification of death guided procedure, or
  • manually code the suspension for reason 'OTH' (Other)

Record a DOC on the customer's record, ensuring that it meets GIR DOC Minimum Standards for significant decisions and include what action is required to confirm the death.

Do not issue a suspension letter. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension.

Note: confirm and action the death as a matter of urgency.

When death is confirmed, go to Step 4.

4

Death is confirmed + Read more ...

A death action cannot be processed with activities on the Activity List (AL) screen. The Notification of death guided procedure will provide a prompt if there is an activity on the AL screen.

Note: if a deceased customer's record was suspended while the death was being confirmed and their death has now been confirmed, manually check for started activities on the AL screen.

Where possible ensure any activities are completed and run the daily driver (DDR):

  • if there is a 'STA' debt activity on the AL screen, create an open work item for region XWA advising them of the death and requesting the debt activity be completed. Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use > Debts > DMB Raising > Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request. See Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer
  • for other outstanding activities, immediately contact the team or Service Officer who needs to complete the outstanding activity. If unsure who to contact, refer to existing escalation channels

Have started activities been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

When it is not possible to complete an activity + Read more ...

If there is an outstanding activity that the Service Officer is unable to complete, suspend the deceased customer's payment (if not already suspended).

The suspension option is not available in the Notification of death guided procedure if there is an activity preventing the death action:

  • manually suspend using the reason 'OTH' (other) pending completion of the outstanding activity
  • the suspension date of event is the date of death or date paid to plus 1 (DPT + 1), whichever is the later
  • record a DOC on the customer's record, ensuring that it meets Online Document Recording (ODR) standards for significant decisions and include what action must occur so the death can be actioned
  • as the customer's death has been confirmed, no letter must be sent. It is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension

If an open DOC/work item for the death notification has not already been created, either:

  • run the Notification of death guided procedure to generate an open work item for the relevant staff to complete the death action once the outstanding activities have been completed, or
  • if the guided procedure is not available create an open work item. Use Fast note - select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED

When activity is completed, go to Step 6.

6

Check if restoration is needed + Read more ...

Is payment suspended?

  • Yes, restore payment if suspended. Ensure that no arrears are issued. Inhibit the restoration advice, as it is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Was the deceased customer due to report + Read more ...

Was the deceased customer due to report, or lodge a reporting statement?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 10

8

Deceased customer was due to report + Read more ...

Are the deceased customer's employment income details for the reporting period in which they passed away known, or can the person notifying of the death provide the customer's employment income details for the last reporting period?

  • Yes:
    • use the Earnings and Reporting tool to process the reporting statement. Note: the date of lodgement on the Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen may need to be changed first to the day before the date of death. For more information see Changing Entitlement Period End Dates (EPED)
    • go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Stimulate the payment + Read more ...

Stimulate the payment using the Earnings and reporting workflow tool.

Take the deceased customer off reporting by updating the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen.

10

Check payments + Read more ...

Check if the deceased customer receiving any of the following payments:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) instalments
  • Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • Ancillary benefits for a Regular Care Child
  • Paid Parental Leave (PPL)
  • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)

If yes,

  • Code the child out of care
  • See Death of a Family Assistance or Paid Parental Leave Scheme Customer Process page, Table 1 Steps 5 and 6

If this activity submits to QOL, request an urgent QOL check so the death action can be finalised.

11

Process the death action + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure to code the death action:

  • the deceased person's payment will cancel from the date of death

If the guided procedure is not available:

  • manually code the death action
  • when recording a death, the Notification handler (NOHL) is incorrectly using the date of notification as the date to increase the survivor's rate to the single rate. If coding the death action manually, the workaround for this is to record the date of receipt (DOR) as the later of date of death or DPT + 1
  • record a DOC with information about the death notification

12

Check payments made to the deceased person + Read more ...

Different processes apply when the deceased person was:

  • a pensioner
  • a long-term Social Security recipient, or
  • in receipt of Service Pension, Veteran Payment or Income Support Supplement (ISS)

Do any of the above apply?

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No, go to Step 15

13

PA qualification will continue for the surviving partner during the bereavement period + Read more ...

The survivor's rate should be immediately adjusted to the single rate from the later of date of death or date paid to +1.

Tell the customer:

  • they will need to apply for a payment in their own right, before the end of the bereavement period
  • if the customer starts work, or is currently working, working credits may enable the customer to keep some of their income support payment while they are working
  • if the customer's income reduces their fortnightly rate to nil and some of that income is employment income, the customer may remain current at nil rate for up to 6 fortnights and retain certain benefits. Note: if the customer's deceased partner had any working credits, these credits cannot be transferred to the customer
  • if the customer was a joint owner of any assets with their late partner, full ownership of the assets legally transfers to the surviving partner following the death of their partner. However, for Social Security purposes, if assets are owned in joint names with the deceased partner, the surviving partner is only assessed as owning 50%, unless the surviving partner advises that the assets have been transferred to them
  • if the surviving partner is granted a reversionary income stream, then it is to be assessed from the date that it is first entitled to be received. A lump sum arrears payment received by the reversionary beneficiary, accrued between the death of the primary beneficiary and when the reversionary beneficiary commences to receive regular payments, is assessed as income from the income stream. This means the income is assessed over the period to which it relates and may result in a debt for that period if the customer has been in receipt of an income support payment. Effective from 9 May 2018, where the assessment of the lump sum arrears result in a debt during the bereavement period for the surviving spouse, consideration should be given to waiver provisions contained under Class of Debt, Section 1237AB(1) of the Social Security Act 1991. The legislative instrument "Social Security (Waiver of Debts - Bereavement Period) Specification 2017" permits this action if all provisions are satisfied. Note: any debt outside the 14 week bereavement period cannot be waived under the legislative instrument. The legislative instrument is not retrospective. This means that any of these debts that occur before 9 May 2018 cannot be waived under class of debt waiver provisions

Offer referral to a social worker if appropriate.

14

PA customer may also be eligible for a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP) + Read more ...

A PA customer must both be qualified for a LBP and an LBP must be payable.

The PA customer is qualified for an LBP if the deceased partner was a long-term social security recipient. This means that if the deceased partner was a pensioner, in receipt of an income support benefit or allowance, or in receipt of Service Pension, Veteran Payment or Income Support Supplement (ISS), they must also have been a long-term social security recipient.

Once death action has been recorded, switch to the surviving partner's record.

The LBP is payable to the eligible surviving partner where the new single rate is less than the previous combined member of a couple rate.

Once the death action has been recorded, switch to the surviving partner's record.

If the surviving partner is entitled to an LBP, manually calculate the LBP then code and issue the payment.

If the deceased partner was receiving a pension a PEN/OTP BLS MFU will be created on the survivor's record. Note: if the deceased partner was receiving an allowance, no MFU will be generated.

If generated, cancel the PEN/OTP BLS activity from the surviving partner's record as LBP must be paid in the NSS system. In the NSS system:

  • in the Nxt field, key 'OOP'
  • in Date of Effect field, key Code date
  • in Payment Type field, key BLS
  • go to the AR screen and key 'O' beside the BRVLS/OOP line
  • on the One Off Payment (OOP) screen, key the amount of LBP
  • finalise the activity

Record LBP calculation in a DOC (or annotate Notification of Death DOC).

After death processing is completed, if the surviving PA customer is on 2 weekly reporting because their deceased partner had employment income, Service Profiling will need to be refreshed:

  • go to the Profile Summary (PQS) screen and key 'Y' in the Reprofile field
  • complete activity
  • send Q150 letter

Procedure ends here.

15

PA customer is not qualified for Lump Sum Bereavement Payment or continuation of PA during bereavement period + Read more ...

The survivor's PA will cease. PA customer is not qualified for continuation of PA during the bereavement period or LBP:

  • identify any service providers to be advised of the death
  • run the Notification of Death guided procedure to code death action (which includes executor details). If the guided procedure is not available, manually code the death action
  • tell the customer that they will need to apply for a payment in their own right
  • identify any service providers to be advised of the death including any Job Capacity Assessment referrals
  • offer referral to social worker if appropriate
  • update executor details after the death has been recorded, if required
  • record the details of the bereavement on a DOC

If the surviving partner's PA cancels immediately afterwards, as a result of the customer's death, a Q134 must be sent to the surviving partner advising of the cancellation reason, if an advice is not automatically issued.
