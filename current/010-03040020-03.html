3

Studying circumstances + Read more ...

DED may be paid in the following cases if the (primary or secondary level) student is:

  • studying from home, and
    • enrolled in a distance education school (including a school of the air)
    • undertaking a course of home schooling/education formally approved (registered) by the relevant State or Territory authority as a satisfactory alternative to the government curriculum in that state or territory
    • the student is unable to attend their local school due to behavioural or health problems (including a shorter term injury or illness) and is set work by the school to do at home
  • not studying from home but is:
    • studying or using facilities at a school that does not offer tuition at the student's level (usually enrolled in a distance education centre)
    • studying at premises not classified as a mainstream school that does not provide tuition by qualified teachers
    • studying at a Homeland Learning Centre in the Northern Territory
    • undertaking distance education study or home schooling/education, not from their principal family home, but while at the family's second family home maintained for reasons other than daily access to appropriate schooling for the student or their siblings
    • travelling overseas for less than 12 months and continues to be enrolled at an approved distance education provider and provides assurances they will continue to undertake the required school work on a full-time basis. Note: The 12 months is calculated from each departure from Australia. The student will remain eligible as long as they are not outside Australia for a continuous 12 months. See Resources page for examples.

Is the student studying by distance education through an approved distance education provider or in another acceptable arrangement as above?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 11

4

Studying arrangement + Read more ...

If the student is studying in one of the approved arrangements as per Step 3, and is:

  • registered for a full-time primary or secondary level course of home schooling/education formally approved by the relevant education authority as being a satisfactory alternative to a state/territory provided curriculum, go to Step 5
  • studying at a Homeland Learning Centre, go to Step 6
  • enrolled with an approved distance education provider, go to Step 7
  • being provided with lessons to study at home by their local school, go to Step 7

5

Home schooling/education + Read more ...

To be approved for home schooling/education, the student must:

  • meet the age requirements for registration for their home State/Territory
  • be provisionally or formally registered for home schooling/education

The Home Schooling table on the Resources page contains details of Age and Registration requirements for home schooling for each state.

Does the student meet the above requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 11

6

Eligibility for AIC DED for students attending Homeland Learning Centres (HLC) in the Northern Territory (NT) + Read more ...

Check the current home address of the applicant and student to ensure they are both living at the homeland or outstation location and not in the main community.

Check the student is attending a HLC or outstation and not the main community (hub) school.

Check the year of study of the student as some HLCs may not provide secondary level study or provide the same range of grades for primary and/or secondary level study.

Is the student attending an appropriate HLC or outstation school and living with the approved applicant in a homeland location or on an outstation?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 11

Check payment + Read more ...

AIC eligibility criteria not met + Read more ...

Check if approved study is full-time + Read more ...

Is the approved study full-time (or considered so by the education institution or authority)?

  • Yes, go to step 12
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Undertaking distance education study, home schooling/education or other approved home-based study part-time + Read more ...

Where a student with a special need has been assessed as needing to study from home, they may still be eligible for a pro-rata rate of DED where their study:

  • Comprises a combination of study at home for part of the week (through a part-time distance education enrolment or home schooling/education) and attending a local school for face-to-face tuition for other days or parts of the school week, or
  • Consists of work set by the local school to do at home for part of the school week and attending the school for face-to-face tuition for other days or parts of the school week, and
  • The distance education provider, the state/territory education authority responsible for home schooling/education or local school has agreed to, or has no objection to, part-time home study.
    See Table 4 on the Resources page for information about the policy of state/territory education authorities regarding part-time distance education or home schooling/education

Does the student fit one of the above?

  • Yes, advise the parent/guardian they will need to supply detailed verification of the relevant study loads and applicable study periods before their entitlement can be assessed. This verification must show the amount of their face-to-face and home-based study (in terms of the number of lessons, hours, days or periods spent at school and at home, and the dates or periods affected). Go to Step 12
  • No, AIC DED eligibility criteria not met, go to Step 11

DED eligibility criteria not met + Read more ...

6

Calculating the pro-rata rate of DED + Read more ...

Where the percentage of home-based study between 20 and 74.9 percent, is consistent across a term instalment period, the calculation of entitlement for that term instalment is as follows:

  • $ annual DED rate/days in the year x days in the term instalment x percentage of home-based study

Note:

  • Round to the 3rd decimal point, for example 0.3948 becomes 0.395
  • Days in the year are 365 or 366 in a leap year
  • Term instalment periods have a different number of days as follows:
    • Term 1: 90 days or 91 days in a leap year
    • Term 2: 91 days
    • Term 3: 92 days
    • Term 4: 92 days

See Table 5 on the Resources page for Examples 1 and 2.

Where the percentage of home-based study is between 20 and 74.5 percent is not consistent across a term instalment period, the calculation of entitlement should be done for each portion of the instalment period as follows:

  • $ annual DED rate/days in the year x number of affected days in that portion of the term instalment period x percentage of home-based study for that portion of the instalment period

Each portion is then added together to get the total entitlement for that term instalment period.

See Table 5 on the Resources page for Examples 3 and 4.

Complex cases should be referred to policy via the AIC Level 2 helpdesk.

7

Coding requirements + Read more ...

As DED is paid by term instalments, the pro-rata rate can only be applied by adjusting the term instalment amount on the One Off Payment (OOP) screen.

  • 1. Code the AIC Claim as required for the family/child’s circumstances. See Assessing the new claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) (SY040)
  • 2. On the Assessment Results (AR) Screen, 'S'elect the appropriate term instalment payment line (these will need to be actioned individually) and type 'OOP' in the Nxt: field.
  • 3. Adjust the payment amount on the relevant line on the OOP screen to the calculated amount for that term
  • 4. Repeat Steps 2-3 for each other affected term
  • 5. Finalise on the AR screen
  • 6. Record the following information on a DOC:
    • reason(s) the customer is eligible for AIC
    • verification documentation supplied
    • calculations used for pro-rata rate
    • any other relevant information (for example, information provided by the customer)

Note: the OOP screen may not allow adjustment of future entitlement. In this case, payments may need to be term by term. If so:

  • end date Living Arrangements Details (EILA) screen with the current 'paid to' date (to prevent the auto issue of incorrect payment amounts)
  • DOC that only the relevant terms have been paid
  • create a review 'RVR' for the next term instalment due date
  • advise the applicant payments can only be made term by term
  • finalise on the AR screen

2

Special coding requirements + Read more ...

As these claims have unique circumstances that only apply to these cases, follow the instructions and special coding requirements below:

  • Address - Check the current home address of the applicant and student to ensure they are both living at the homeland or outstation location and not in the main community
  • Education Course Details (EDC) screen:
    • Institution - when coding the EDC screen, there is no institution code for each individual HLC. Where a student is attending a HLC, the EDC screen must be coded with the collective 'Outstation School' or 'Homeland School' code related to the relevant remote community school administering that HLC (and in most cases a number of other HLCs). For example if the student is attending Irrultja HLC, that is administered by and attached to Arlparra School, code Institution: 83934 'Arlparra Outstation Schools'. Do not use the institution code for the remote community school that is different to the outstation/homeland schools code (in this example 86040 'Arlparra School (Utopia)'
    • Comments: on the Comment line enter the name of actual HLC the student is attending
  • Living Arrangements Details (EILA) screen:
    • Check the year of study of the student as some HLCs may not provide secondary level study or provide the same range of grades for primary and/or secondary level study. Code an end date (of 31 December of the relevant year) on the EILA Screen to reflect the level of study or range of grades known to apply to the particular HLC. If the student is a primary student, code an end date (31 December) on EILA Screen for the last year of primary schooling as the student may change to ABSTUDY for secondary study
    • Code the Living Arrangement as DEO - Distance education other
  • Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
    • As all homeland or outstation locations are geographically isolated from the student's nearest appropriate state school, the EIID screen should be coded with the default distance of 56 km. Even though technically not an appropriate state school (that is, all NT remote community schools have a limited program), use the remote community school institution code as the 'Nearest Approp Govt School'. Using this school is a coding compromise as ascertaining which full program primary or secondary schools in the NT are the actual nearest appropriate state schools to the HLCs would not be simple and the distances to those schools would far exceed 56 km in all cases
  • Complete all other coding and DOC requirements for the claim
  • On the DOC on the student's record, enter the name of the HLC they are attending and its remote community school. For example, advise "student attending Irrultja HLC one of the Arlparra Outstation Schools administered by Arlparra School"

For details of the appropriate codes and year levels of the HLCs, see Table 7 on the Resources page.
