General information about Centrelink deductions

Table 1

Item

Description

1

What can be collected from Centrelink deductions + Read more ...

Centrelink Income Support Payments (ISP) deductions

Centrelink deductions can be used to collect:

  • child support (ongoing or arrears)
  • overpayments, and
  • payments due under a section 143 recovery order

Centrelink deductions cannot be used to collect:

  • penalties
  • costs
  • fines, or
  • Spousal maintenance

Parental Leave Payment (PLP) deductions

PLP deductions can be used to collect:

  • payer consolidated revenue debt and
  • spousal maintenance

PLP deductions cannot be used to collect:

  • penalties
  • costs, or
  • fines

PLP deductions are collected from Centrelink under a different legislative provision than deductions from Income Support Payments (ISP). Therefore, Child Support can collect different amounts from PLP and different types of debt.

Payments that can be deducted

Deductions can only be made from a pension, benefit or from PLP.

The allowed Centrelink pensions and benefits are listed in the Child Support Guide, 5.2.5: Collection from social security pensions and benefits. See the References page for a link.

Note: deduction requests and s72A notices cannot be issued to Centrelink for ABSTUDY payments.

Cuba and Family Tax Benefit (FTB) deductions

Centrelink Deductions through Cuba cannot be used to collect from Family Tax Benefit.

See Recovering a child support debt from a Family Tax Benefit (FTB) to collect from FTB payments.

2

Advantages of Centrelink deductions + Read more ...

There are a number of advantages of Centrelink deductions:

  • Payment of deductions is prompt - they are sent on a daily basis
  • Automatic suspension period:
    • If the customer no longer receives a pension or benefit the deduction is active but suspended for 2 weeks
    • If the pension or benefit resumes in this period the deduction resumes
    • If the pension or benefit does not resume, the deduction request will stop automatically
  • If the customer moves from a pension or benefit to another, the deduction automatically transfers to the new pension or benefit
  • The deduction stops automatically once the child support (maintenance) debt is paid

3

Limit to deduction amount + Read more ...

Section 20 of the Child Support (Registration and Collection) Regulations 2018 sets the amount that can be deducted from ISPs per week. See the References page for a link to the Regulations.

If the Child Support Period commenced in 2019, this amount is 3 times the minimum weekly rate of $8.34 per week, or $25.01 per week. This amount is increased periodically to take inflation into account.

If the Child Support Period commenced in an earlier year, see the Basic Values for that year to determine the deduction amount.

Customers receiving ISP who have overdue child support will automatically have 3 times the minimum weekly rate of child support deducted.

Note: PLP is not an ISP and is not subject to Section 20. For deduction amounts, go to Step 6.

4

Deduction amount concession + Read more ...

Customers on an ISP who have overdue child support that are having difficulty meeting other life costs can seek a deduction concession, as long as they are not assessed to pay at least 3 times the minimum annual rate.

Income Support Payment (ISP) concession for Centrelink deductions outlines how to assess and action applications from a customer to have the amount of deduction reduced.

Note: if a customer is receiving PLP and wants to adjust the amount of PLP deductions being made for their arrears, they are not required to seek an ISP Concession. If their ongoing liability is less than the amount Child Support can collect from Centrelink, they can negotiate a payment arrangement.

5

Parental Leave Payment (PLP) deductions + Read more ...

Deductions can be requested from customers who receive PLP from Centrelink. The 2019 maximum gross amount of PLP a customer can receive is $740.60 a week, equivalent to the weekly national minimum wage. This amount will be indexed at the start of each financial year. The maximum deduction is the PLP maximum gross amount minus the Protected Earnings Amount (PEA).

Centrelink will not make any deductions if the customer’s PLP instalment is less than the PEA. If the customer is already receiving an ISP when they commence receiving PLP, Centrelink deductions can be requested from both entitlements.

If the customer is receiving PLP from their employer, see Employer Withholdings (linking) for Child Support customers.

Note: Centrelink may make other deductions, for example tax from PLP which could affect the deduction amount.

6

Automatic and manual deductions + Read more ...

Deduction requests can occur automatically or manually.

Automatic deduction requests are made by Cuba according to certain rules, without prompting by an officer. Many customers have Centrelink deductions start through an automatic request.

Manual deduction requests require an officer to use Cuba to send the request to Centrelink. For:

  • Automatic Centrelink deductions and how to stop them, see Table 2
  • Manually starting or stopping Centrelink deductions, see Table 3
  • Actioning Centrelink Deduction intrays, see Table 4
  • Resolving general problems with Centrelink deductions, see Table 5

Conditions when automatic deductions will commence + Read more ...

Cuba and Centrelink's computer systems exchange information to establish which customers will automatically have deductions made from their Centrelink benefit.

Automatic deductions will commence for all customers who meet at least 1 of the following conditions:

  • the case has an ongoing liability
  • the customer has a child support debt of at least $25
  • the customer has no debt offset in any case
  • the customer owes an overpayment of at least $25
  • the customer is in receipt of certain Centrelink benefits or Parental Leave Payment (PLP) (Cuba will seek to collect 100% of any PLP entitlement)
  • the customer does/does not have a Special Recovery Condition of hardship, stay order, or customer agreement, (a special recovery condition will only prevent disbursement, not collection) and the case has an ongoing liability or arrears registered for collection

Note:

  • For Child Support liabilities and arrears, automatic Centrelink deductions will commence 7 days after the amount becomes due
  • For overpayments, automatic Centrelink deductions will commence 21 days after the overpayment is created

Once a customer is determined to meet the requirements a batch process sends a deduction request to Centrelink to commence deductions

If the customer is receiving PLP, the maximum deduction will be automatically requested if:

  • the ongoing liability is equal to or higher than the maximum deduction amount, or
  • the ongoing liability is less than the maximum deduction amount, but the customer has arrears which makes their account balance more than the maximum deduction amount

The PLP deduction amount can be manually adjusted. PLP is not an ISP and therefore an ISP concession is not applicable. If the customer wants to reduce their PLP deductions for their child support arrears, they must negotiate a payment arrangement. See Debt repayment.

To amend Centrelink deductions, see Centrelink Deductions Cuba Process Help.

Note: in certain circumstances, payments from PLP and Centrelink benefits may be deducted simultaneously. See Centrelink Deductions Cuba Process Help.

2

Preventing automatic deductions + Read more ...

It may be appropriate to prevent Cuba from making automatic deduction attempts because of the customer’s circumstances, for example due to an agreement with the customer.

Record a special recovery condition to prevent automatic deduction attempts.

In the Special Recovery Condition window select:

  • Reason - hardship, stay order, or client agreement
  • Finalisation date - based on the reason for preventing automatic deductions
    • If automatic deductions should never be established for the customer, key a finalisation date of 31/12/4000
    • See TRIP Cuba Process Help - Record a special recovery condition.

3

Ending automatic deductions + Read more ...

Centrelink Deductions will automatically stop when:

  • the child support liability and/or arrears has been paid
  • the customer has stopped receiving a Centrelink pension, benefit or PLP

This will generate a Centrelink Deduction Terminated intray.

Where the debt has been paid and the customer has other debts, such as penalties or consolidated revenue debt (which cannot be collected via Centrelink deductions), contact the customer to negotiate payment of the remaining debt.

Where the customer has stopped receiving a pension or benefit there may be alternative collection avenues available. This may be an indication that the customer is employed.

Where the customer is deceased the refer the case to the Deceased Customer Management (DCM) team. See Terminating Events (Stage 2).

To resolve problems with automatic Centrelink deductions, see Table 4 or Table 5.

Manual Centrelink deductions

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Establishing manual deductions + Read more ...

Although the majority of Centrelink deductions are automated, it will sometimes be necessary to establish manual Centrelink or Parental Leave Payment (PLP) deductions.

Manual deductions are appropriate:

  • Where a liability is raised, not yet due and the customer requests that Centrelink or PLP deductions commence
  • Where automatic deductions have been prevented and a decision is made to commence Centrelink or PLP deductions
  • When the customer is a payee with an overpayment and requests Centrelink deductions be established immediately. Centrelink deductions for overpayments will normally commence 21 days after the overpayment is raised
  • When the customer is a payee with an overpayment and automatic deductions did not commence due to a debt offset in 1 of their cases. Note: manual deductions can still be set up in this scenario

The conditions outlined in About Centrelink deductions must be met in order to establish Centrelink or PLP deductions.

See the Client Benefits window on how to check which benefit or pension (if any) the customer receives.

2

Review the case + Read more ...

Review the case to ensure that Centrelink or PLP deductions are appropriate:

  • The debt must be correct (or corrected if possible)
  • There must be no outstanding intrays
  • Contact the Debt Enforcement Service Officer if enforcement litigation is occurring, to discuss the proposed deduction

3

Action any outstanding intrays + Read more ...

Check both customers' records for any outstanding intrays indicating pending actions that may change the liability, such as a change in care. Ensure that these issues are finalised before proceeding.

4

Correct the debt + Read more ...

Check that the debt is correct.

  • If there are outstanding NAP intrays, the NAPs must be accepted or refused before commencing deductions. See Non Agency Payments (NAPs)
  • If the liability is set by a child support assessment, ensure that the incomes being used are accurate. See Income Processing for Child Support customers
  • If the liability is set by a child support agreement, check the agreement to for unemployment clauses, or other clauses that may affect the liability. See Agreements between Child Support customers
  • If the liability is set by a court order that overrides a child support assessment, check for unemployment clauses, or other clauses that may affect the liability. See Court Varied Assessments (CVA) including lump sum orders
    • If there is an unemployment clause in the order that has not been actioned, see Stage 1-variations to activate the clause
    • If there is no unemployment clause in the order, the customer may be able to apply for a Low Income Non Enforcement Period (LINE). Contact the customer if a LINE is not in place. See Stage 1- Low Income non-enforcement periods

5

Payment arrangements + Read more ...

Check if the customer has a payment arrangement on the Customer window and in the Payment Arrangement window. See Payment arrangements Cuba Process Help on how to access these screens.

Where there is a payment arrangement in place, only request Centrelink deductions following negotiation with the customer. See Debt repayment.

6

Legal action + Read more ...

Where legal action/litigation is in progress, contact the Debt Enforcement Service Officer managing the issue to determine if Centrelink Deductions are appropriate.

7

Customer contact + Read more ...

Attempts to contact the customer must be made before taking any enforcement action, refer to Contact with Child Support customer.

Contact must be made to advise the customer:

  • of other possible payment methods, and the possibility of making a payment arrangement
  • that if the customer does not make payments voluntarily collection action will commence without further notice
  • that deductions can be made from certain Centrelink payments and PLP
  • that deductions will continue until the debt is paid, and
  • that only Child Support can start or stop deductions (Centrelink does not make this decision)

Note: customers can also make a payment arrangement when collection from PLP via Centrelink is occurring. See Debt repayment.

Check if the customer needs to update their income and if an estimate is appropriate. See Child support estimates (Post 1 July 2010).

If phone contact is not successful issue a MX1-1 Request for Client to contact CSA letter in accordance with Contact with Child Support customers with the following specific action text, as appropriate:

  • 'ask Centrelink to deduct {key amount} each fortnight from your social security pension or benefit. Please call Child Support to discuss other payment options' or
  • 'ask Centrelink to deduct {key amount} each fortnight from your Paid Parental Leave entitlement. Please call Child Support to discuss other payment options'

Document the attempted customer contact.

8

Starting manual deductions + Read more ...

The decision to commence Centrelink or PLP deductions requires the same consideration as other enforcement actions:

  • Has the customer been contacted?
  • Has the customer had the opportunity to pay voluntarily?
  • Are Centrelink or PLP deductions the most appropriate collection method for the customer?

To start manual Centrelink deductions, see Table 1 in Centrelink Deductions Cuba Process Help.

Document the decision in the Payment Options notepad.

9

Ending manual deductions + Read more ...

It may be appropriate to end manual Centrelink or PLP deductions, including when the customer:

  • gains employment whilst still being in receipt of a benefit or pension
  • becomes able to make a payment arrangement
  • is in hardship (where only arrears are being collected)

See Centrelink Deductions Cuba Process Help.

Step

Action

1

Types of failed deduction intrays + Read more ...

There are 2 intrays that generate as part of the Centrelink deduction process:

  • Centrelink Deduction Rejected intray, go to Step 2, and
  • Centrelink Deduction Terminated intray, go to Step 3

The Centrelink Deduction Rejected intray generates when a Centrelink request was unsuccessful and deductions were never made.

A Centrelink Deduction Terminated intray generates when a previously active deduction (deductions received from Centrelink) fails, or is no longer able to obtain deductions.

See Intray Management Cuba Process Help to manage intrays in Cuba.

2

Centrelink Deduction Rejected intray + Read more ...

A Centrelink Deduction Rejected intray generates when the request is rejected by Centrelink because the customer:

  • name or Date of Birth (DOB) details do not match, go to Step 3
  • is not currently receiving Parental Leave Payment (PLP) or is not on a social security pension or benefit, go to Step 4
  • is deceased, go to Step 5

3

Customer name/DOB details not matched + Read more ...

A deduction request may be rejected if 1 or more of the following does not exactly match on Centrelink and Child Support records:

  • customer name
  • date of birth
  • customer reference number

This may be the result of

  • a keying error
  • a slight variation in spelling, for example 'Stephen' rather than 'Steven', or
  • the result of the customer changing their details with Centrelink or Child Support, but not the other

On rare occasions, this is because 2 different customers have been incorrectly matched.

Carefully investigate all instances. Do not delete the intray until the issue is resolved.

To resolve mismatches:

  • Check the Cuba documentation of the original application to make sure the Customer Reference Number keyed in the Individual Customer Details window is that provided by the customer at registration
  • Check the External Information window to determine if Centrelink have updated their records and advised us of the change
  • Confirm the identity and address details on Cuba using the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) systems
  • Contact the customer to confirm their details. Customer Location (Tracing) contains information on determining current customer details, and how to confirm them
  • Submit a manual information request to Centrelink to confirm the customer’s name, address and date of birth

These steps should indicate which details are correct and which are not identical on the systems. Document any information found or changes.

Where the mismatch cannot be resolved after taking all possible action, prevent automatic deductions.

Note: the reason will be Hardship and the end date is 31/12/4000.

If a Centrelink data mismatch results from use of an alias due to family violence, seek advice from a Service Support Officer who can review the circumstances and make a case specific decision.

4

Duplicate customer + Read more ...

If the customer is incorrectly matched – that is, there are 2 different customers - then the customer record in Cuba must be corrected.

Replace the incorrect Customer Reference Number (CRN) with the customer's correct CRN, in consultation with a Service Support Officer (SSO), under the direction of a Program Support Manager. Ask an SSO for assistance before commencing this action.

5

Customer deceased + Read more ...

A deceased customer should only be managed by the DCM team. See Terminating Events (Stage 2).

6

Centrelink Deduction Terminated intray + Read more ...

A Centrelink Deduction Terminated intray is generated when:

  • an active deduction arrangement has ceased because the debt has been paid
  • an active deduction arrangement has ceased because the customer has stopped receiving PLP or a pension or benefit that can be deducted from, or
  • the customer is deceased

If the maintenance debt has been paid, but there are other debts (consolidated revenue debt or penalties), contact the customer to discuss payment of the non-maintenance debt.

If the customer is no longer in receipt of PLP or a Centrelink pension or benefit, investigate alternative collection avenues. The customer may now be employed.

If the customer is deceased refer to the DCM team and see Terminating Events (Stage 2).

Delete the intray when the issue is resolved.

Issues and troubleshooting

Table 5

Issue

Action

1

Statements + Read more ...

Generally payers are sent a payment account (or 'statement') every month. Centrelink deductions automatically stop statements issuing when the payer has an account balance of less than $20.

If a customer requests that statements be issued, this can be overridden by keying the statement function and changing the frequency from Suppress to Always. Cuba will then continue to send statements while Centrelink deductions are being made.

Note: customers with an overpayment are sent a monthly overpayment summary.

2

Client Benefits does not show the customer's benefit status- no records found or records inaccurate + Read more ...

Submit a manual information request to Centrelink to confirm that the customer is currently receiving a social security pension or benefit or Parental Leave Payment (PLP) and confirm the customer’s CRN.

If the customer is currently receiving a payment contact an Service Support Officer (SSO) for the case to be investigated which may result in a Cuba Incident Form (CIF) being required.

3

Deduction status showing as Ended but customer states deductions still occurring + Read more ...

Submit a manual information request to Centrelink to confirm the status of deductions on their records.

If deductions are continuing, report this to an SSO for the case to be investigated which may result in a CIF being required.

4

Request Status Unconfirmed for more than 1 week + Read more ...

Report this to an SSO for the case to be investigated which may result in a CIF being required.

5

Request appears active but no payments received + Read more ...

Submit a manual information request to Centrelink to verify that the customer has been receiving payments and that they have been deducting amounts.

Note the date of each deduction and the amount that was deducted for child support.

If Centrelink has made deductions but these have not been received, report this to an SSO for the case to be investigated which may result in a CIF being required.

6

Deduction request rejected - New (but a current instruction exists) + Read more ...

Submit a manual information request to Centrelink to confirm the status of deductions on their records.

If there is a discrepancy, report this to an SSO for the case to be investigated which may result in a CIF being required.

7

Deduction request rejected - customer not current on requested system + Read more ...

Submit a manual information request to Centrelink to verify the benefit status of the customer.

If the Cuba record of the customer benefits is inaccurate, report this to an SSO for the case to be investigated which may result in a CIF being required

8

Deduction request rejected - request amount and allocated amount do not match + Read more ...

Generally, this is caused when there was previously an amended deduction amount keyed.

Adjust the amended deduction amount to match the allocated amount. This will allow Centrelink Deductions to re-commence.
