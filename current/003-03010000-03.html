2

DRA and NZ DRA general eligibility + Read more ...

To receive DRA/NZ DRA, a customer must meet all the following criteria on any day of the major disaster:

  • be at least 16 years old
  • earn, derive or receive income from:
    • one of the affected areas and does so by working in that area, or resides in one of the affected areas, or
    • one of the affected industries and does so by working in one of the affected areas
  • suffered a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster
  • did not receive or will not receive for the period of time they are claiming the DRA or NZ DRA:
    • another social security entitlement, (select the Definitions icon or link on this page and search for ‘social security entitlement’)
    • a prescribed payment for DRA or NZ DRA
    • NEIS allowance
  • have a rate of payment greater than nil at the time their DRA or NZ DRA claim is finalised. A customer’s income cut off rate (income below an income threshold amount) is determined when assessing the customer’s income loss. See Table 3

Note: affected areas (Local Government Areas) and income cut-off rates are in Current emergency management events.

Office Locator is not to be used when assessing Local Government Areas.

A customer who receives DRA or NZ DRA is not automatically entitled to additional benefits, payments or supplements such as:

  • Health Care Cards
  • Rent Assistance

If a customer is already entitled, receiving DRA or NZ DRA does not affect this.

Members of a couple must:

  • lodge their own claim, and
  • be eligible for DRA or NZ DRA in their own right

DRA and NZ DRA are not subject to:

  • assets tests
  • partner income tests

Payment rates depend on a customer’s age and circumstances. See Payment rates for DRA and NZ DRA.

3

DRA additional eligibility + Read more ...

To receive DRA, a customer must meet all the following criteria on any day of the major disaster:

  • DRA and NZ DRA eligibility
  • be an Australian resident or the holder of a specified visa for DRA

To check if a visa is a permanent visa, see the References page for a link to the Social Security Guide, Chapter 9.2, Visa subclasses and payment eligibility for visas issued after 01/9/1994.

The system does not assess all factors of Australian residence for DRA claims. Code residence data and manually check that residence rules are met. Depending on where the claim is processed, see:

  • Processing DRA claims in Process Direct
  • Processing DRA and NZ DRA claims in Customer First

A customer may be eligible for both AGDRP and DRA if they are activated for the same event.

4

NZ DRA additional eligibility + Read more ...

To receive NZ DRA, a customer must meet the all the following criteria on any day of the major disaster:

  • DRA and NZ DRA eligibility
  • a New Zealand non-protected Special Category Visa (SCV) subclass 444 holder (not an Australian Resident under the Social Security Act 1991) who is living in Australia, and
  • participated in the Australian tax system for at least one year in the past 3 financial years, or before the ‘next 12 months’ date in NZ DRA tax participation in the Background page Operational Blueprint for the event

Note: participation in the Australian tax system means the customer's income is above the tax free threshold for at least one year in the past 3 financial years, or will be in the next 12 months.

To check what is above the tax free threshold, when accessing the link from the Resource page to the ‘Australian Taxation Office’s individual income tax rates’, see the Resident tax rates. The customer’s income under the column ‘Taxable income’ must show an amount of tax payable under the column ‘Tax on this income.’ For example, 19 cents for each $1 over $18,200.

The system does not assess all factors of Australian residence for NZ DRA claims.

If the online claim channel:

  • has been activated for an event, an immigration link will trigger
  • has not been activated for an event, Service Officers must request an immigration link

In all cases, Services Officers must review the relevant residence screen to make sure the customer meets the residence requirements for DRA/NZ DRA.

For more information see:

  • Processing DRA claims in Process Direct
  • Processing DRA and NZ DRA claims in Customer First

A customer may be eligible for both NZ DRP and NZ DRA if they are activated for the same event.

5

Customer under 22 years of age additional eligibility + Read more ...

A customer under 22 years of age (16 to 21 years of age) is not eligible for DRA or NZ DRA if, on the day of the determination, all the following apply:

  • wholly or substantially dependent on another person (except the person's partner), and
  • is not the parent of another person, and
  • their income in the financial year when the determination occurs will be equal to or less than $6,403

This means the customer may be eligible for DRA or NZ DRA if one of the following does apply:

  • not wholly or substantially dependent on another person (except the person’s partner), or
  • are a parent of a child, or
  • their income in the financial year when the determination occurs will be more than $6,403

6

NZ DRA claims only – Australian tax system participation + Read more ...

The customer must:

  • have participated in the Australian tax system for at least one year in the past 3 financial years, or
  • will participate in the Australian tax system in the next 12 months

Participated in the Australian tax system means a customer’s income is over the tax free threshold, or will be in the next 12 months.

Note: if a customer has allowable deductions that takes their income below the tax free threshold, contact Technical Peer Support who will escalate a payment query template to the Level 2 Helpdesk. These queries will be escalated to the Level 3 Helpdesk.

For the relevant financial years and ‘next 12 months’ date, see NZ DRA tax participation in the Background page of the Operational Blueprint for the event.

Evidence is required before granting NZ DRA

Evidence may include:

  • a previous tax return or Notice of Assessment as proof of participation in the Australian tax system in the past 3 financial years
  • a payslip or letter from their current employer that they will meet this requirement in the next 12 months

If the customer cannot provide the evidence as they have lost their records in the disaster, they can get a copy of their Notice of Assessment from the Australian Tax Office (ATO). Customers with a myGov account may be able to access this information if their ATO account is a linked service.

Also request evidence if the customer cannot show participation in the tax system for any of the following reasons:

  • their income did not meet income tax thresholds
  • they have lodged non-taxable returns or nil activity statements
  • they have not yet lodged a tax return
  • they were not required to lodged tax returns or activity statements

Evidence may include a payslip or letter from the customer’s current employer that there is a likelihood they will pay tax within Australia in the next 12 months.

When submitting a claim, customers are asked to provide this information and advised their claim cannot be assessed until they provide it. NZ DRA cannot be granted until this evidence has been provided.

To request evidence:

  • for DRA claims processed in Process Direct, see Table 4
  • for DRA/NZ DRA claims processed in Customer First, see Table 5

7

Proof of Identity (POI) requirements + Read more ...

DRA and NZ DRA can be paid before a customer has provided POI to Services Australia when:

  • Service Officers are satisfied the person making the claim is who they say they are, and
  • the customer is eligible to receive the payment

See Proof of Identity (POI) requirements for Emergency payments.

  • Processing DRA claims in Process Direct
  • Processing DRA and NZ DRA claims in Customer First

8

Direct result of the disaster + Read more ...

Direct result of the disaster needs a clear link between the disaster (the cause) and the customer’s loss of income (the effect).

Examples of direct result include:

  • physical damage to a work place such as
    • damage to commercial buildings
    • loss of stock due to a power outage
    • damage to equipment
    • damage to a residential building where a home based business is affected
  • physical destruction of, or damage to, a principal place of residence to the extent that a customer needs to find alternative accommodation
  • physical impediments such as road closures that prevent a customer from getting to their workplace
  • serious injury that a person sustained as a direct result of the disaster and because of the injury the person was admitted to hospital or under normal circumstances, the person would have been admitted to hospital. Evidence is needed for hospital admission

If the assessing Service Officer determines evidence is required to support the claim:

  • for DRA claims in Process Direct, see Table 4
  • for DRA/NZ DRA claims in Customer First, see Table 5

See the Resources page for direct result scenarios.

9

Assurance of Support + Read more ...

The following is not relevant for NZ DRA due to the nature of the visa.

A customer is not eligible for DRA if they have an Assurance of Support (AoS) in force unless they meet all eligibility criteria and the following apply:

  • the assurer is not willing or able to support the customer, or
  • it would not be reasonable for the customer to accept the support

If the customer has provided information that indicates they are not able to be supported by their assurer, annotate the Progress of Claim DOC with details provided. For approval to proceed with the claim, contact Technical Peer Support for escalation to Level 2 Helpdesk using the payment query template.

DRA is not an AoS recoverable payment.

If DRA is granted due to an administrative or customer error, any debt will be recoverable from the customer/assuree. See Raising emergency payment debts and grant and ex-gratia overpayments.

10

New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS) Allowance + Read more ...

Customers cannot receive NEIS Allowance and DRA/NZ DRA at the same time.

NEIS Allowance is paid to eligible job seekers during the first 39 weeks of operating their NEIS business. Any period of time that a participant is suspended ‘pauses’ their NEIS Allowance clock.

Discuss the best option with the customer. If the customer wants to continue with their DRA or NZ DRA claim and meets all other eligibility criteria, they will need to contact their NEIS provider to suspend their NEIS Allowance. The Department of Education, Skills and Employment will forward a notification to the agency via electronic messaging. This automatically applies to the customer’s Centrelink record. The customer will not need to contact the agency after they have taken this action.

To request the customer contact their NEIS provider:

  • for DRA claims processed in Process Direct, see Table 4
  • for DRA/NZ DRA claims processed in Customer First, see Table 5

11

Allowable absences + Read more ...

DRA and NZ DRA are payable during the following absences.

  • Temporary departure overseas (for any reason)
  • Imprisonment
  • Psychiatric confinement

For NZ DRA the customer must continue to meet the subclass 444 visa requirements.

Customers must meet notification requirements during their allowable absences.

12

Tax File Number (TFN) + Read more ...

For DRA

If a customer does not have a TFN on record and refuses to provide their TFN in writing to the agency, they are not eligible to receive DRA.

If a customer does not have a TFN on record, and meets all eligibility criteria, the claim can be granted and a TFN exemption applied if:

  • the customer cannot provide their TFN in writing with their claim, but are willing and able to do so at a later time, and
  • this will delay the grant of their claim

The customer must provide their TFN in writing within 28 days otherwise their claim may be cancelled creating a debt or overpayment.

A TFN cannot be provided over the phone and should not be entered into the:

  • Disaster Recovery workflow in Process Direct, or
  • DRA verbal claim template in Customer First

Customers can give permission over the phone for the agency to use a TFN they have provided in writing before.

Customers can upload their TFN via their online account.

To request a customer’s TFN :

  • for DRA claims processed in Process Direct, see RFI for a TFN in Processing DRA claims in Process Direct
  • for DRA claims processed in Customer First, see Table 5

For NZ DRA

The agency is unable to request or use the customer's TFN. Do not ask the customer for their TFN. An exemption from the TFN requirement must be recorded when coding claims, see Table 5, Processing DRA and NZ DRA claims in Customer First.

13

Timeframe for claiming + Read more ...

Claims should be lodged within 6 months of the date the disaster determination is signed by the relevant Minister.

See Current emergency management events for the closing date.

A claim may be lodged more than 6 months after the disaster determination is signed if:

  • there are acceptable special circumstances applying to the customer that justify a late lodgement, and
  • the claim is lodged within a reasonable period having regard to those circumstances

See Emergency payment claims lodged outside the time limit.

Income loss definitions

Table 2: for the types of income to include and for evidence of income to assess income loss (if required), see the table on the Resources page. For help with income loss definitions, contact the Technical Peer Support.

Step

Action

1

Income loss as a direct result of the disaster + Read more ...

If the amount of disaster affected income is less than the amount the customer would have earned, received or derived in the same period even if they had not suffered directly because of the disaster, the customer has suffered a loss of income.

Note: self-employed customers may already be running their business at a loss before the disaster. The customer may experience a greater income loss as a direct result of the disaster and may still meet the definition of income loss.

2

Disaster affected income + Read more ...

This is the fortnightly income that a customer has earned, received or derived, or will earn, receive or derive, in the 13 weeks after the date they suffered a loss of income as a direct result of a major disaster. That is, the actual income that will be/has been earned after the date they suffered a loss of income as a direct result of a major disaster.

A customer claiming:

  • within the 13 week period since their loss of income commenced - will only be able to give an estimate of their earnings for that period
  • outside the 13 week period since their loss of income commenced - must give the actual amount of their earnings for that period

Use:

  • gross income for customers with a salary/wage
  • taxable (net) income for customers who are self-employed
  • all income in the calculation - include earnings from employment, any other source of income and deemed income calculation from financial investments

3

Average weekly income + Read more ...

This is the average weekly ordinary earnings for a full-time adult in Australia according to the Australian Bureau of Statistics.

This is based on the Average Weekly Ordinary Time Earnings (AWOTE) for a full-time adult in Australia. The AWOTE rate:

  • is from the Australian Bureau of Statistics
  • will be applied based on the payment period and will be updated if this crosses over two calendar years
  • is located in the Operational Blueprint file for each specific event, see Current emergency management events for a list of current events
  • is a weekly rate and therefore for certain DRA and NZ DRA calculations may be required to be multiplied by:
    • 2 - for fortnightly rate, or
    • 52 - for annual rate

4

Earned, derived or received + Read more ...

  • An income amount earned, derived or received by any means, and
  • An income amount earned, derived or received from any source (if within or outside Australia)

5

Excluded income amounts + Read more ...

Exclude the following types of income earned, derived or received when calculating a customer’s income:

  • interest or investment return on an amount held in a bank account on trust for a person (the trustee), where the trustee does not, at the time the trustee’s income is being assessed, have access to the interest or investment returns earned
  • compensation payments
  • an amount paid by a company registered as a proprietary company under the Corporations Act 2001 to a person who is a director of, or shareholder in, the company other than an amount earned, derived or received by the person from remunerative work undertaken by the person as an employee of the company
  • adjusted disability pension payments
  • amounts under subsections 8(4), (5) and (8) of the Social Security Act 1991
  • all income outlined under exempt income which includes emergency and COVID-19 related payments (CDP and PLDP)

Compensation payments do not include:

  • any landlord protection insurance or similar which compensates for rental income on rental property, or
  • a payment to a person under an income protection insurance scheme, in relation to a person’s inability to earn, derive or receive income from remunerative work

The COVID-19 Disaster Payment and Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment are also excluded income amounts, as they are considered ‘emergency relief or like assistance’.

‘Emergency relief or like assistance’ is listed as an excluded income amount under subsection 8(8) of the Social Security Act.

6

Income + Read more ...

  • An income amount earned, (see Step 7 below for definition), derived or received by the person for the person’s own use or benefit
  • A periodical payment by way of gift or allowance, or
  • A periodical benefit by way of gift or allowance

It does not include excluded income amounts, (see Step 5 above for definition).

7

Income amount + Read more ...

If of a capital nature or not means:

  • valuable considerations
  • personal earnings
  • moneys, or
  • profits

8

Income loss date + Read more ...

Income loss date, means the date a customer suffered a loss of income as a direct result of a major disaster.

This date cannot be earlier than the start date of the major disaster (as per Current emergency management events).

9

Backdating + Read more ...

Backdating is available to the date of the customer’s income loss date if the loss of income:

  • is a direct result of the disaster, and
  • occurs on or after the start date of the disaster (as per Current emergency management events)

If the loss of income occurs before the disaster start date, customers will only be eligible for backdating from the start date of the disaster.

10

Claim assessment date + Read more ...

The claim assessment date is the date a decision to grant or reject a claim has been finalised.

2

COVID-19 restrictions before the disaster + Read more ...

A customer may have:

  • been impacted by COVID-19 restrictions before the disaster, and
  • does not have income for at least 4 to 8 weeks before the disaster

If the customer received the COVID-19 Disaster Payment or Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment, this is:

  • an excluded income amount, and
  • must not be included in the calculation of income loss for DRA/NZ DRA

Has the customer advised their income has been impacted by COVID-19 restrictions?

  • Yes, if the customer has:
    • returned to work since COVID-19 restrictions, go to Step 3
    • not returned to work since COVID-19 restrictions, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Income impacted by COVID-19 restrictions + Read more ...

If the customer had income impacted by COVID-19 restrictions for the entire 8 weeks before the disaster, go to Step 5.

The customer may have only returned to work for short period before the disaster occurred.

As the customer has returned to work since COVID-19 restrictions and has lost income as a direct result of the disaster, calculate an average weekly income based on the period and amount the customer advised during their claim. This period may only be a few days.

Compare it to the customer's disaster affected income to decide if the customer has suffered an income loss.

Go to Step 8

4

No work because of COVID-19 restrictions + Read more ...

If a customer has not returned to work since COVID-19 restrictions were in place, they will not meet the eligibility criteria unless they were going to return to work if the disaster did not occur. It must be clear that the customer’s income loss was a direct result of the disaster (not ongoing COVID-19 impacts).

While a customer may not have returned to work since COVID-19 restrictions were in place, they may have been scheduled to do so.

Was the customer scheduled to return to work but could not due to the disaster?

  • Yes,
    • Calculate an average weekly income based on the period and amount the customer advised during their claim. The customer must have evidence (such as a letter from an employer) that shows:
      - they would have restarted work if not for the disaster, and
      - the amount they would have earned
      Note: the evidence does not need to be provided if a customer is providing a self-declaration. A self-declaration includes where information is provided in an online claim and there is sufficient information to make an assessment
    • Go to Step 8
  • No, customer is not eligible for DRA/NZ DRA. Depending on where the claim is processed, see:
    • Processing DRA claims in Process Direct
    • Processing DRA and NZ DRA claims in Customer First

5

Income 4 to 8 weeks before the disaster + Read more ...

  • Sets a pattern of regular income before the disaster
  • Is compared to the customer's disaster affected income to decide if the customer has suffered an income loss

The customer will be asked to provide details of their income 8 weeks just before the disaster and upload evidence of that income.

Although asked for 8 weeks of income in the online claim and Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), a claim can be assessed with less than 8 weeks of verified income. For example, if the customer was working:

  • full time and earning the same wage each fortnight, they need to provide details of income for at least 4 weeks just before the disaster
  • as a casual employee with their wage changing each pay cycle, they need to provide details of income for the 8 weeks just before the disaster

For paper claims, online claims and phone claims using ACC or verbal claim template (when available for the event)

Has the customer provided details and documentary evidence of income for 4 to 8 weeks (based on whether they were earning same wage each fortnight) before the disaster?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Income for another period (not 8 weeks) before the disaster + Read more ...

  • Sets a pattern of irregular income before the disaster
  • Is compared to the customer's disaster affected income to decide if the customer has suffered an income loss

For example, the customer is:

  • a seasonal worker and has income that covers the period before and after the disaster, in the same periods in previous years
  • self-employed and has income up to 2 years before the disaster and 6 months after the disaster

For paper claims, online claims and phone claims using ACC or Verbal claim template (when available for the event)

Has the customer provided details and documentary evidence about their income for another period (not 8 weeks) before the disaster?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Reason for another period + Read more ...

Is the reason for using income for a period other than 4 to 8 weeks before the disaster acceptable?

For example, the customer is a seasonal worker or self-employed, or impacted by COVID-19 restrictions.

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, try to contact the customer to obtain information and/or evidence
    • for DRA claims processed in Process Direct, see Table 4
    • for DRA/NZ DRA claims processed in Customer First, see Table 5

8

Income 13 weeks following the income loss date + Read more ...

Would the customer continue to earn the same amount for the 13 weeks after their loss of income date if the disaster had not occurred?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No,
    • If the customer’s income was going to be higher than before the disaster and this will impact the income loss assessment, calculate an average weekly income based on the period and amount the customer advised during their claim
    • The customer must have evidence (such as a letter from an employer) that shows:
      - they would have increased hours of employment if not for the disaster, and
      - the amount they would have earned
      Note: the evidence does not need to be provided if a customer is providing a self-declaration
    • Go to Step 9

9

Documentary evidence of income + Read more ...

Has the customer already provided documentary evidence to support their online or verbal income information?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No,
    • for DRA claims processed in Process Direct, see Table 4
    • for DRA/NZ DRA claims processed in Customer First, see Table 5

10

Is there complex income? + Read more ...

Referral to a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) may be needed for customers with complex financial documents, for example relating to private trusts and private company assessments.

Do not issue MOD forms to assess income details.

Check if the customer has any income coded on Trust and Company Income & Asset (TRCIAS) screen. If any income coded on TRCIAS a referral to CAO is required to review/update details.

Is a CAO referral needed?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 12

12

Has the customer experienced an income loss? + Read more ...

Is the amount of disaster affected income less than the amount the customer would have earned, received or derived in the same period if the disaster did not occur?

Note: coding income/asset screens may be required to determine deemed income from managed investments and bank accounts. See:

  • Processing DRA claims in Process Direct
  • Processing DRA and NZ DRA claims in Customer First

Customer only needs a $1.00 reduction to have an income loss.

Has the customer experienced an income loss?

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No, the customer is not eligible for DRA/NZ DRA. See:
    • Processing DRA claims in Process Direct
    • Processing DRA and NZ DRA claims in Customer First

13

Income cut-off (income threshold) + Read more ...

Calculate the:

  • customer’s annualised disaster affected income:
    • multiply the customer’s disaster affected income by 26
  • annualised average weekly income amount. Average weekly income is in Current emergency management events
    • multiply the average weekly income by 52

If the person’s annualised disaster affected income is an amount:

  • equal to or more than the annualised average weekly income, the fortnightly rate is nil. The customer does not have an income loss and is not eligible to receive DRA/NZ DRA
  • less than the annualised average weekly income amount, the customer meets the income loss criteria

For help, see Payment rates for DRA and NZ DRA.

For claims processed in:

  • Customer First
    • Phone claims - update the Using DRA/NZ DRA verbal claim template
    • Claim forms - record details on a DOC on the customer’s record
    • Process the claim
  • Process Direct
    • Update the Progress of Claim Note
    • Process the claim

Requesting evidence - claims in Process Direct

Table 4: process to follow when evidence is required for assessment of eligibility. For help to progress any steps, contact the Technical Peer Support.

Step

Action

1

Fully review claim lodged by customer + Read more ...

Review all information provided in the Claim Summary and any evidence already uploaded.

Make sure the claim has been fully reviewed and the following has been considered to decide if customer contact is required:

  • How the disaster impacted the customer
  • Is there sufficient information in the claim about income and loss of income to be able to make an assessment?

Try to contact the customer + Read more ...

Before contacting the customer, determine what additional information or evidence is required to be able to assess the claim. All information required to assess the claim should be requested at a single contact.

Consider the following when determining what additional information or evidence may be required before attempting to contact the customer:

  • How the disaster impacted the customer
  • Additional details needed for employment types (such as self employment, seasonal work)
  • Additional details needed for other income types (including income before the disaster and disaster affected income)
  • Supporting information for relationship detail updates

When requesting proof of identity, see Customers without a Confirmed identity status table.

Issue a pre-call notification (SMS) to the customer between 8:00 am and 8:00 pm using the customer's local time to let them know a Service Officer will call them today from a private number.

Make one genuine attempt to contact the customer.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 10

3

Contact successful - information provided verbally + Read more ...

Authenticate the Centrelink customer.

Ask the customer for the required information to assess the claim. See the Resources page for examples.

Can the customer upload all the information/evidence?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Contact successful - ask for information/documentary evidence + Read more ...

Offer self-declaration of income + Read more ...

A self-declaration of income can be used to support the claim if:

  • the customer cannot lodge evidence immediately using their online account
  • there are no concerns about the validity of the customer’s claim
  • the customer is associated with a business, company or private trust, and it is clear their income is for remunerative (paid) work, such as a salary or wage

The minimum information needed for a self-declaration of income is:

  • the customer's residential address, to determine if it is in an eligible Local Government Area (LGA)
  • the name of the customer’s employer (for assurance purposes)
  • the location of the customer’s workplace, to determine if it is found in an eligible LGA
  • a statement of the customer's disaster affected income, to determine if they earn less than the income cut-off threshold
  • a statement of how much income the customer has lost due to the disaster, to establish loss of income
  • a statement of why the customer's income was affected, to establish eligibility

Any additional information provided by customer in support of their claim that has not been included in the online claim details should be recorded in a DOC on the record, confirming this was accepted as part of the self-declaration of income.
