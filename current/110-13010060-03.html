How to request documented verification from the customer or third party

Table 1: requesting documented verification within the scope of intervention an what to do when the customer or third party fails to respond.

Step

Action

1

Requesting verification of earnings + Read more ...

Only if the customer is unable to provide verification of earnings should the employer be contacted.

A request to the employer to verify income and employment details is made when the customer:

  • fails to respond to the contact letter by the due date
  • fails to provide suitable verification documents for the EIIM by the due date
  • requests the agency to obtain verification from the employer
  • Is verification to be requested from the customer or third party?
    • customer to supply verification, go to Step 2
    • third party request to be sent, go to Step 8

2

Issuing the customer a Request for Information (RFI) letter + Read more ...

A Request for Information (RFI) letter is issued when income and employment details are to be requested from the customer.

For Tip-offs issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter via the Request for Information guided procedure.

Before issuing a RFI letter via the guided procedure, the CO must fully explain to the customer the information required, including the periods the documents are required. The CO is to select the relevant 'Available Documents' from within the Request for Information letter guided procedure.

The Request for Information letter is to be issued via Workspace > Guided Procedures > Request for Information > select option 'a completed {form description} form and all supporting documents'.

Where modules/forms are being requested, the Request for Information letter must be printed locally to allow the CO to include the necessary modules/forms with the letter.

For all remaining EIIM interventions issue a Request for pay details (TF002), see Circumstance Review Action script - Earned Income Interventions (Mail based) (EIIM).

The customer must be given at least 14 days to supply the information. See Coding the Contact screen in the Integrated Review System (IRS).

Note: if customers fail to supply the requested documents by the due date their payments may be suspended and the information will be requested from their employer or payer.

Procedure ends here.

3

Customer provides verification + Read more ...

How to determine if the requested documents have been returned:

  • the returned document will be scanned to the customers record and available for viewing in the Activities tab via Customer Details page by clicking on the 'CRN/BP' hyperlink
  • See Viewing Centrelink customers digital images

Where requested documents have been returned, CO is to

  • Finalise the work item once the document has been actioned
  • Annotate the intervention DOC to indicate the documents have been returned
  • Update the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen with 'Y' in the 'Response' field and enter the 'Date of response'

Contact is not required with the employer if the customer supplies information and the Compliance Officer (CO) is satisfied that it is complete and accurate.

Has customer provided the documents requested?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 4

Note: the contact on the IRCS screen must be updated with the response and the details recorded in the DOC.

4

Customer fails to provide verification + Read more ...

If the customer has not responded in the required time, the intervention will either auto suspend or refer to a CO to determine if manual suspension is required.

The auto suspension process occurs overnight via IRS, and is determined by the customer meeting a set of required business rules.

Note: if the customer is already suspended/cancelled, two genuine attempts to contact the customer must be made. If contacts are unsuccessful, third party verification is required.

Before calling, send a desktop message. Follow the agency's phone contact requirements - introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose.

Is a manual suspension required?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Manual suspension + Read more ...

Before proceeding, the CO must be aware of the procedures to refer a customer to a social worker in Payments and Integrity and Identifying customer vulnerability and risk issues.

Manual suspension action is required if the customer:

  • does not meet the required business rules for auto suspension
  • does not provide the required documentation

Automatic overnight processing by IRS will add a keyword of 'IRSSUS' to the intervention, the IRCD screen is not updated and the intervention has a status of 'DUE'.

When the steps outlined in manual suspension action have been actioned the CO will need to suspend payment by coding the record manually or using the Circumstance Review Action script:

  • for Tip off interventions manually suspend payment 'fail to respond to correspondence' (FRC), go to Step 6
  • for all remaining EII interventions run the Circumstance Review Action script

6

Coding manual suspension + Read more ...

To manually suspend the payment, in the customer's record:

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen and code the following:
    • Key (payment type, for example, JSP) as the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn: field
    • Key the Action: SUS
    • Key in the Reason: FRC
    • Press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or enter the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date: field
    • Complete footer details, Source: field and date of receipt in the DOR: field
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen
    • Finalise the activity
  • Include the following details in a DOC:
    • Decision made including reason, payment type and Act/Guide reference (Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 - Section 80)
    • Date of effect of decision
    • Follow up action required on contact

The date of event used for manually suspending payments is the date the determination is made, that is, today. Do not use Date Paid to Plus 1 (DPT+1).

Update the letter contact on IRCD screen to 'N' the customer did not contact.

Record a new contact on the IRCS/IRCD screen to place the intervention on hold (HLD) for 28 days, pending contact from the customer. See suspension/cancellation in Coding the Contact screen in the Integrated Review System.

All significant decisions must be recorded in the Intervention DOC. The Resources page contains a link to the DOC templates for Earned Income Interventions (Mail-based) (EIIM).

Customer Compliance Division policy requires a separate DOC be created for all suspensions, cancellations and debts. See Creating documents (Fast Note, Callbacks and ACTDOCs).

If third party verification is required, go to Step 8.

If the customer contacts after suspension, go to Step 7.

7

Customer contacts in relation to suspension action + Read more ...

During conversations with the customer, the CO must be aware of the procedures to refer a customer to a social worker in Payment and Integrity and Identifying customer vulnerability and risk issues.

If the customer contacts in relation to the suspension action, follow the agency's phone contact requirements - introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose. Once the CO has assessed whether the customer has retained eligibility for the payment, then payments can be restored using the following processes:

  • for Tip-off interventions, use the Cancellation and Restorations workflow
  • for all remaining EII interventions use the Circumstance Review Action script
  • record action taken in the intervention DOC
  • discuss the customer's employment income details ensuring all the required information has been requested

When third party verification has already been issued, advise the customer that a third party request has been sent.

Procedure ends here.

8

Issuing third party request + Read more ...

When required, COs may need to verify a customer's employment income.

Note: one outbound call attempt may be made to each employer, to confirm the postal address.

The verification request can be made via the following scripts:

  • for Tip-off interventions use the General Review Action Script by selecting 'Q146 - Employer Report'
  • for all remaining EII interventions use the Circumstance Review Action script to issue a Request for Employee Information (SU658 or SU659)

Both scripts will:

  • place the intervention on hold (HLD) in IRS
  • annotate the intervention DOC

For the request to be issued to an employer the Review Status in IRS must have an overall status of HLD. If the intervention needs to be manually placed on HLD, see Coding the Contact screen in the Integrated Review System (IRS).

Q146/Q492 requests

All Q146/Q492 requests will print locally and are required to be mailed by the CO undertaking the intervention action. The Q146/492 will be listed on the HS screen.

SU658/659 requests

All SU658/659 requests are issued automatically via an overnight process. The SU658/659 will either be issued via mail or if an employer is registered for the Employer Reporting Services, it will be issued electronically.

The information from the electronic form will be recorded on a DOC.

If the Circumstance Review Action script is unable to issue the required SU658/659, the verification request can be made via the General Review Action script by selecting 'Q146 - Employer Report'.

The General Review Action script will:

  • annotate the intervention DOC
  • create an entry on the IRCD screen

Place the review on 'H'old, pending return of the information requested.

9

Third party returns information request + Read more ...

Before contacting the employer about failure to return the requested information ensure:

  • the original request was sent to the correct address
  • the request has not been returned

How to determine if the information has been returned:

The returned document will be scanned to the customers record and available for viewing in the Activities tab via Customer Details page by clicking on the 'CRN/BP' hyperlink

See Viewing Centrelink customers digital images

Where requested documents have been returned, CO is to:

Finalise the work item once the document has been actioned

Annotate the intervention DOC to indicate the documents have been returned

Update the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen with 'Y' in the 'Response' field and enter the 'Date of response

When the information is returned, ensure that the customer is not incurring any further overpayment while waiting for other correspondence. For example, coding earnings where applicable.

Has the employer supplied employment information?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Employer fails to supply information + Read more ...

If there is no evidence that the Request for employee details has been returned, the CO is to contact the employer by phone to check if there is an issue providing the requested information.

All reasonable attempts must be made to try to contact and/or identify employers.

If the employer requires more time to respond, or the information is in transit, the contact on the IRCD screen can be extended.

If required, the CO may need to reinforce the legal requirements relating to providing information under section 192 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 (SSAA).

The References page contains a link to this legislation.

If only one request for employment information has been sent, a second notice may be issued if either a new address has been obtained, or the employer advised the original request was not received.

To issue a new notice, go to Step 11.

Employer refuses to supply the information

The CO will need to assess the match data to determine if it contains gross income amounts, if, after reminding the employer of their obligations under SSAA the employer:

  • refuses to supply the information
  • the employer denies the customer works/worked for them
  • refutes the employment or contracting arrangement, and/or
  • the date that the customer was placed in employment or the type of employment

If match data contains gross income amounts, the CO will need to apportion the original match data income over the financial year or defined period of employment via the Earnings Apportionment Tool (EAT). The customer must have been given the opportunity to supply this verification at the beginning of the intervention

If match data does not contain gross income amounts, clearly note on the customer record that verification from the employer cannot be obtained so that a CO conducting an intervention in the future need not seek verification of this employment income

Note: any EAT calculation sheets used in making an unfavourable decision must be uploaded to the customer's record.

Record the name of the contact person, their telephone number and details of the phone conversation on the intervention DOC.

If it is determined that all avenues to obtain verification or contacts to the employer have been exhausted, see Step 1 in Table 2.

11

Issue a new notice to the employer + Read more ...

If an employer has advised they did not receive or lost the notice, or they have a new address, a new notice may be issued.

  • Send a new notice using the Circumstance Review Action Script or General Review Action script (Tip-offs) to the correct address
  • When sending out a new request, use the same dates as on the original request. These dates are determined at the Date of Scope Assessment (DOSA) and should not be changed
  • Manually record the first SU658/Q146 for that employer on the IRCD screen as response received 'N'

Ensure the Review Status is HLD when issuing a Request for Employee Details.

How to assess and finalise an EIIM

Table 2: This table describes the steps required to assess earnings, raise debts and finalise an Earned Income Intervention (Mail based) (EIIM).

Step

Action

1

Locate employment information + Read more ...

Employment information can be supplied verbally or via documented verification.

Verbal updates

Check the intervention DOC for the required information.

Documented verification

All returned employment information documents from the customer and/or employer will be scanned by the agency's scanning provider, if they are returned via the mail.

Once scanned, a keyword of 'IRSRTN' is automatically added to the intervention. Check:

  • the intervention DOC has been annotated to indicate the documents have been returned
  • IRCS screen to ensure the contact in the Actual Resp: field has been updated with a 'Y'
  • Document Tools in Customer First to ensure documents have been scanned

2

Assess earnings + Read more ...

When all the employment information has been gathered, the customer's entitlement must be calculated as soon as possible.

For assistance, see Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts.

Selecting the IRS intervention

Before any updates are made on the customer's record, the CO must ensure the IRS intervention has a status of 'STA'rted and the Review Image is viewed.

All verified employment income must be recorded on both the Employment Income Details (EAN) screen and the Actual Earnings Details (EAAD) screen.

Note: verbal updates are not verification for coding purposes. Do not use the code BIR and the EAAD screen is not to be updated.

The assessment of earnings from employment may include:

  • Leave entitlements
  • Commissions and bonuses
  • Allowances
  • Deductions
  • Salary sacrifice

For more information on the assessment of earnings from employment see:

  • Recording and correcting employment income details
  • Assessment of employment income for Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) customers over Age Pension age
  • Income Maintenance Period (IMP)
  • Paid leave and redundancy payments (advised before 19 April 2010)
  • Leave and termination payments paid by an employer
  • Assessment of other ordinary income for Centrelink payments
  • Treatment of lump sums

Code the earnings using the Earnings Apportionment Tool (EAT) and upload EAT calculation sheets to the customer's record.

If the assessment of earnings results in a debt, application of the recovery fee will need to be considered.

Additional Risk

Note: reminder to include all Additional Risks, if identified, in your assessment. The Resources page contains a link to Assessing the Additional Risk guide that will help with the assessment.

Is a debt to be raised?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 6

3

Determine whether recovery fee is applied + Read more ...

Determine if a Recovery Fee should be applied to an overpayment.

Check the intervention DOC and customer's record for any evidence that would support a decision to not apply a Recovery Fee. If the customer had contacted to discuss the intervention a discussion should have been undertaken with the customer around the reasons for failing to correctly declare their employment income.

This information may be used to inform the Recovery Fee decision.

Note: earnings debts with a period of 14 days or less will be automatically raised on the completion of the earnings activity. The Recovery Fee is not applied to auto raised debts. The customer will receive the standard debt advice as part of the automatic process.

The CO must fully DOC the actions taken and decisions made regarding the application of the Recovery Fee.

4

Determine debt and calculate + Read more ...

All debts that can be calculated via the Centrelink system and explained using ADEX should be calculated that way. Only use MultiCal if the Centrelink system calculation is not possible or the outcome is not correct.

Check the Assessment Result (AR) screen to make sure the system has correctly calculated any potential overpayment, for example:

  • Debt schedule - do the debt dates/date of event (DOV) match the period?
  • Have the updates/coding been entered correctly?
  • Check the following to make sure the updates are as expected:
    • Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS)
    • Income Bank (NIB)
    • Employment Income Assessment (EIA)
  • Can a reassessment be made using the Benefit Action (BA) screen?
    • Use REA in relevant host system, or
    • RCR in PEN/PGA (for cancelled PEN and PGA records) and leave Reason blank

Note: check if Review of Assessment (ROA) coding is needed to generate allowable arrears. Do this when corrected income is less than declared, but the system did not generate arrears for those periods/fortnights.

If an ADEX debt calculation:

  • can be undertaken, the overpayment must be calculated with the customer over the phone where possible
  • is not possible, a MultiCal debt calculation may be required

For MultiCal debt calculation, get all information from the customer over the phone where possible to enable a debt calculation.

The CO is responsible for progressing the MultiCal activity. If they cannot action, their site is responsible for progressing the activity. The Team Leader will need to reassign the activity to another CO with the required skill set.

For more information, see:

  • Debt tools
  • Debt investigation
  • Actioning an undetermined debt on the Debt management and Information System (DMIS)
  • Using ADEX to explain a system generated debt
  • Using the correct date of effect when raising debts
  • Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts
  • Waiving Centrelink debts

When raising a debt that has originated from both verbal updates and documented verification, choose the most relevant option from within the Debt Action script. Select the option that relates to the source of the greater portion of information provided. If required, annotate the DOC to indicate if there is more than one source of evidence used to calculate the debt.

For more information on the tools available to assist with the calculation of debts, see the Calculators and Tools located on the Resources page for a link.

The Debt Advice template is recommended to be used when raising debts from verbal updates. The information recorded in the Debt Advice can be changed to suit customer's individual circumstances.

Suggested debt advice template for verbal updates:

'The correct amount of your earnings for (employer's name) was not taken into account in the payments made to you from (debt start date) to (debt end date). This means you have been overpaid $(debt amount). We are, therefore, required to recover this amount.'

Note: sending Account Payable letters contains a table of Debt Reason Codes, default text for the Account Payable (OPAOD) screen, and the rules for entering free text on the Account Payable letter.

Deceased customers

If the customer is deceased and a debt is likely, ensure that before the debt advice is sent, a social worker in Payment and Integrity is used to decide the necessary method of contact with the executor. For the procedure on raising debts, see Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer.

5

Making an unfavourable decision + Read more ...

Before proceeding, follow the agency's phone contact requirements - introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose.

Follow the required action for making an unfavourable decisions, including:

  • annotate the existing intervention DOC making sure all relevant information is included
  • discuss the recovery fee if appropriate

Where an adverse decision results for the customer and partner, both the customer and partner must be contacted in relation to the decision.

If unable to discuss the adverse decision with both the customer and partner at the time of the phone call, the interventions may need to be held until 2 genuine attempts to contact are undertaken to both customer and partner.

If the customer has further evidence related to the decision

  • see making an unfavourable decision (CLK), Step 6 for required actions
  • annotate the existing intervention DOC making sure all relevant information is included
  • place the intervention on HLD for the agreed timeframe (preferably 7 days)
  • do not issue Request for Information (RFI)/TF002 letters requesting information as these letters attract auto-suspension actions

For evidence provided or not provided by agreed date, see Step 7 and 8 in Making an unfavourable decision (CLK).

6

Document the intervention + Read more ...

It is important that all information be recorded in the customer's intervention DOC.

Annotate the existing DOC with the agreed DOC standards upon completing the intervention. The Resources page contains a link to the DOC standards and templates.

Intervention DOC

The intervention DOC must include:

  • all the relevant details required to follow the progress of the intervention
  • any actions/updates that contributed to an overpayment
  • a record of all decisions made including the final outcome

All compulsory scripts must be run to ensure that:

  • the DOC is appropriately updated, including the Extra Details Line
  • all actions are recorded in IRS

7

Finalising the Intervention + Read more ...

Check screens

Before finalising the intervention ensure all actions required on the customer, ( including any linked reviews) and (ex) partner records are complete:

  • Check the Activity List (AL) screen to ensure all activities are completed
  • Ensure all entries on the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen are completed:
    • Response?: field 'Y' or 'N'

Review results - Attribution

It is important that the correct activities be attributed to the intervention.

For further information, see:

  • Calculating a Review Result and Restoration Processing in the Integrated Review System (IRS) for Result and Reason codes
  • Finalising and attributing actions to a Service Update or Review in the Integrated Review System (IRS)

Ensure all actions required by the CO are completed.

Finalise the intervention using the relevant script:

  • tip-off interventions are to be finalised using the Finalise Review script
  • all remaining EII interventions are to be finalised using the Circumstance Review Action script

If the customer has a linked review or (ex) partner who also has an intervention, it is important to finalise all interventions at the same time.

Issuing a Q765 advice

A Q765 must be sent unless the intervention outcome:

  • has been advised over the phone
  • is related to a member of couple trigger and a decision letter has been issued
  • resulted in an adverse decision, for example:
    • payment/card remains suspended/cancelled
    • payment was varied
    • debt advice has been issued

A Q765 can be issued if requested by the customer.

Referral to the Intelligence and Investigations Branch

Once an intervention is completed, and CO identifies non-compliance outside of the Scope of Intervention, the case can be referred to the Intelligence and Investigations Branch .
