Determining eligibility for a Health Care Card (HCC)

This table describes the steps to determine eligibility for a HCC for customers who are receiving an eligible income support payment and customers who are not receiving an eligible income support payment.

Step

Action

1

Check if customer is receiving eligible payment + Read more ...

Is customer receiving an eligible payment?

  • Yes, customer is issued an automatic HCC for a period of 12 months and is reissued every 12 months if the customer remains eligible for the qualifying payment or is in an employment income nil rate period
  • No, if customer is in receipt of
    • Family Tax Benefit, go to Step 2
    • Any other payments, go to Step 4

2

Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customer + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving maximum rate of FTB Part A paid in instalments or would except that they only have care of a Regular Care Child?

This includes customers whose FTB Part A rate is reduced only by the Maintenance Income Test.

  • Yes:
    • The HCC is issued automatically by the FAO system for a period of 12 months and is automatically reissued as long as the customer remains eligible
    • The customer's residence qualification will be automatically assessed as long as the residence information is correctly recorded
    • If the residence information is already recorded from a previous claim, no further information will be necessary. If residence information is incomplete, the datalink with the Department of Home Affairs will need to be activated to obtain the necessary information. For assistance, see Activating the Department of Home Affairs datalink and contingency procedures if datalink is unavailable
    • For a partner's details to appear on the HCC that person must satisfy the dependant requirements prescribed in Social Security (Administration) Act 1990, section 240B, and their verified residential details must also be properly recorded on the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) and Country of Residence (CRES) screens
    • If customer loses entitlement to maximum rate FTB Part A, they also lose entitlement to the HCC regardless of the expiry date shown on the HCC. Check eligibility for the Low Income Health Care Card (LIC)
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Receipt of a reduced rate of FTB + Read more ...

Check the Assessment Explanation (AXFTB) screen to view child eligibility details.

Is the customer's FTB Part A reduced from the maximum rate due to the failure of the Maintenance Action Test (MAT) for all children?

Note: if only some of a customer's children fail the MAT, the customer may continue to receive a HCC for all children including the ones who have failed the MAT.

Yes, the customer’s FTB (A) rate will be reduced to base rate for all eligible FTB children for reason 'Base No Maintenance Act', and the customer’s existing HCC may be cancelled (check MCCS for any other eligible concession): Submit a Level 2 Policy Helpdesk Enquiry including the following details for a manual override to be coded:

  • CSAQuery: select dropdown 'Families, Chidcare & Parenting'
  • CSAEnquiry: select dropdown 'Concessions'
  • Service/Payment Type: select dropdown 'Card - Health Care Card (HCC)'
  • CRN: Insert relevant Customer Reference Number
  • Reference Check: Insert 'Health Care Card (HCC) 101-06020000'
  • Enquiry Description: Insert 'FTB HCC Manual override MAT'
  • Fill out all additional fields and click 'Save'
  • DOC the customer record to advise a referral for manual override has been requested

No, and

  • the FTB rate is reduced due to the income test for FTB Part A, the customer
    • is not entitled to an automatic HCC
    • can test their eligibility for a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC)
  • the customer loses entitlement to the maximum rate FTB Part A for another reason
    • they lose entitlement to the HCC regardless of the expiry date
    • can test their eligibility for a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC)
    • Procedure ends here

4

Mobility Allowance + Read more ...

Is customer receiving Mobility Allowance (MOB) or has the customer's MOB been cancelled due to becoming a funded participant of the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS)?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

If customer is receiving MOB only: + Read more ...

  • They are entitled to a HCC for 12 months. If MOB is cancelled or rejected due to a sales tax exemption for the purchase of a new car, customer may still be eligible for a HCC

If receiving Disability Support Pension (DSP) as well as MOB:

  • they are entitled to a Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) but can also be issued a HCC (non- priority card) at customer's request

If customer's MOB has been cancelled due to becoming a funded participant of the NDIS

  • They retain entitlement to their Mobility Allowance HCC. For more information on issuing this card, see Cancellation and Period of Grace for Mobility Allowance

Procedure ends here.

6

Caring for foster child or child customer is receiving CA for + Read more ...

Is customer caring for a foster child or for a child for whom they are entitled to Carer Allowance (CA)?

  • Yes, for a:
    • Foster child, if the customer is not claiming or receiving FTB for the foster child, they may claim a HCC (not subject to an income test) that covers the child only. See Health Care Cards (HCC) for foster children. If the customer is an FTB Part A recipient and an eligible FTB child is recorded as a foster child on the Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM) screen and the customer is not eligible for an automatic FTB HCC, the child will be automatically assessed as being entitled to a FST (foster child) HCC
    • CA child, the HCC covers the child only and is automatically issued for a period of 12 months. See Health Care Card (HCC) only Carer Allowance (CA) (child). Note: Renewal is automatic as long as entitlement continues
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Ex-CA (child) care recipient + Read more ...

Is the customer an ex-CA (child) care recipient?

  • Yes, they may be entitled to an HCC. See Ex-Carer Allowance (child) Health Care Card (ECC)
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Carer Payment (CP) for short-term care + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving Carer Payment (CP) for short-term care?

  • Yes:
    • Short-term care receivers will receive a Health Care Card (HCC) with entitlement for the dates of the short term care
    • If there is an extension granted to the short-term care, the care receiver's HCC is to be reissued with an end date in line with the date the extension was granted
    • If extensions occur for a continuous period of 6 months or more, then a reassessment for long-term Carer Payment (CP) (child) will occur and a Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) will be issued to the carer, and the care receiver will retain their HCC
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Carer payment for episodic care + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving Carer Payment (CP) for episodic care?

  • Yes:
    • Episodic care receivers will receive a Health Care Card (HCC) with entitlement for the dates of the episodic care
    • If there is an extension granted for the episodic care, the care receiver's HCC is to be reissued with an end date in line with the date the extension was granted
    • If extensions occur for a total period of 6 months or more, then a reassessment to long-term mainstream care Carer Payment (CP) (child) will occur and a Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) will be issued to the carer, and the care receiver will retain their HCC
  • No, procedure ends here
