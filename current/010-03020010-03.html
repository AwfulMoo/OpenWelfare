Checking the SY040

Step

Action

1

Claim lodgement

+ Read more ...

For Service Centre staff only

A Claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) (SY040) is lodged.

Date stamp front cover of SY040 and scan it to the customer record.

Note: the customer is the person indicated at Question 6 of the SY040. A student cannot claim AIC in their own right.

If a customer has an unusual or difficult enquiry, it is best to refer them to an AIC Smart Centre on 13 2318.

2

Organisation details (SY067) + Read more ...

Check SY040 is completed correctly.

If an organisation is the applicant, they must complete an Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Organisation details (SY067) as well as the SY040.

All questions are essential unless stated otherwise.

There are very few organisation applicants for AIC but an organisation that has never claimed AIC before may contact a service centre. However, due to outreach activity by the AIC Smart Centre, organisations deal directly with the Smart Centre and receive SY040/ Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Organisation details (SY067).

3

Applicant applying on behalf of child + Read more ...

If the customer (applicant) applying on behalf of the child is:

  • a person, go to Step 6
  • an organisation, go to Step 4

4

Check organisation has answered all questions + Read more ...

Has the organisation answered all relevant questions on the SY040 and signed the claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, and the form was lodged:
    • by mail, go to Step 9
    • in person, go to Step 5

5

Check SY067 is completed + Read more ...

Has the organisation completed an SY067?

  • Yes, attach it to the claim and go to Step 9
  • No, issue the SY067 and advise the organisation to return it to the AIC Smart Centre. Go to Step 9

6

Check SY040 is completed and signed by applicant + Read more ...

Applicant is parent /guardian/other person

Has the applicant answered all relevant questions on the SY040 and signed the claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, and the claim has been lodged:
    • by mail, go to Step 9
    • in person, go to Step 7

7

Additional Boarding Allowance + Read more ...

Is the customer applying for Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA)?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Tax File Number (TFN) questions completed + Read more ...

Has the customer and their partner completed the relevant income and Tax File Number (TFN) questions and signed the form?

Note: the claim can be processed for non-means tested Boarding Allowance in the meantime.

If an organisation is accepted as an applicant, the income test for ABA may be waived.

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, forward the claim to the relevant AIC Smart Centre with a covering note so they can follow-up the missing signature and/or any other required information. Go to Step 10

9

Check all documentation lodged + Read more ...

Has the claim been signed and all of the relevant evidence and documentation been provided? These will be listed in the Information you need to know about your claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (CI023).

  • Yes, photocopy any original documents, attach them to the claim and return originals to the customer. Note: photocopies of proof of age (if required) must be certified.
  • No, forward the claim to the relevant AIC Smart Centre with a covering note so they can follow-up the missing signature and/or any other required information (including SY067 if the applicant is an organisation).

10

Document and scan + Read more ...

Record details on a DOC, include where the form was sent.

Scan the claim and any supporting documents for the relevant AIC Smart Centre for indexing and processing. A customer record may need to be added if none exists.
