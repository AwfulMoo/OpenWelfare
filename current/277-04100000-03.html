Step

Action

1

Third party collection + Read more ...

Consider third party collection if:

  • the customer has a child support debt or overpayment debt. For enforcement action on an overpayment, see Debt repayment
  • there is an identified source of funds
  • deductions from the customer’s salary is not an appropriate form of collection. For example, where the Employer Servicesteam has refused a link referral
  • deductions from Centrelink payments are not available or are insufficient to satisfy the debt
  • contact has been made/attempted with the customer to discuss voluntary debt repayment of the ongoing child support and debt
  • the customer does not agree to voluntarily and/or satisfactorily meet their child support obligations, and:
    • has been advised of the consequences of non-payment that is, enforcement action, or
    • payment and consequences of non-payment have not been discussed but the circumstances are urgent or delay will put collection at jeopardy. A Service Support Officer (SSO) or Team Leader must approve these enforcement actions. See Technical support in Child Support

Note: garnishee from other sources can occur even if employer withholding (EW) and employer withholding arrears (EWA) is in place. Investigate each case to decide if additional third party collection is appropriate.

Do not issue s72A notices to:

  • National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) specific bank accounts to collect NDIS payments made directly to NDIS participants or their nominee. These payments are not a financial resource that is available to meet a debt
  • a bank account to recover debt where a known National Redress Scheme payment has been made. These amounts are inalienable and remain protected once paid into a bank account. For more information, see National Intensive Collection Services (ICS)
  • recover relevant debts directly from a Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS) Program payment
  • customers undergoing a residency decision determination. Issuing an s72A can result in overpayments that cannot be recovered legally from the payee once the residency decision has been finalised

See References for a link to the Child Support Guide 5.2.9: Collection from third parties.

Document all discussions about debt and payment arrangements in the Collection window.

2

Identify source of funds + Read more ...

Information about funds can come from:

  • payers
  • payees
  • the External Information window
  • ATO systems
  • AUSTRAC
  • Equifax
  • employers/contractors, current and past
  • financial institutions. For example, banks, credit unions and superannuation fund managers, in limited circumstances. The References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide 5.2.9: Collection from third parties
  • accountants and solicitors
  • share registries/stockbroking firms
  • Centrelink
  • real estate and settlement agents
  • TAB
  • Cab charge
  • Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) - Military Compensation Pensions via S72A
  • Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) - certain income/disability support payments via an s72AC notice only. See Direct requests for information from Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)

The References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide 5.2.7: Collection from Veterans’ pensions and allowances.

If more information is required or work undertaken before third party collection is appropriate:

  • contact the third party via phone or issue a section 120 (s120) notice:
    • only issue a s120 notice if there is reasonable belief the third party holds funds on behalf of the customer
    • s120 notices cannot be used to 'fish' for information
    • see Child Support’s information gathering powers, Section s120/161 notice Cuba Process Help and View External Information
  • an Employer Services Service Officer may issue an EF1 to get information for establishing employer withholding (linking). See Employer Withholding (linking) for Child Support customers
  • also see the following documents:
    • Debt repayment, or
    • Centrelink deductions used to collect child support debts

Actions that must not be undertaken for collection:

  • Section 72A notices must not be issued:
    • for NDIS customers
    • to Centrelink for ABSTUDY payments
    • for unrestricted non-preserved superannuation (UNPS) unless the customer has elected to cash these amounts
    • a bank account where a Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) payment has been deposited
  • Suspicious Matter Reports (SMRs) from AUSTRAC must not be recorded on the system
    • review AUSTRAC results and if an SMR/SUSTR is found, it must be removed immediately
    • email the request to National ICS
  • Do not include SMRs when making a submission as:
    • they cannot be disclosed to any court or tribunal, and
    • are not admissible as evidence

Has a possible source of funds been identified?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, collection through third parties is not an option at this time. Procedure ends here

3

Review case/circumstances + Read more ...

When a Service Officer has located a source of funds, review the case and consider if it is appropriate to issue either:

  • s72A notice, go to Step 4
  • s72AC notice, go to Step 5

4

Section 72A appropriateness + Read more ...

In some circumstances, it is not possible to issue an s72A notice or it is precluded by law.

To decide the appropriateness of issuing a notice, the References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide 5.2.9: Collection from third parties.

If an opportunity to garnishee for a lump sum is identified instead of periodic amounts, see Customer referral guidelines for Child Support staff.

Consider collection from these sources:

  • New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS):
    • The New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS) is a Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE) program. The program is for unemployed people who wish to start their own independent business
    • NEIS income may be identified through an ATO system search or s120 response from a financial institution
    • An s72A notice may be issued to the bank account to which NEIS payments are made provided the account belongs to the customer
    • Do not issue s72A notices to DESE if NEIS payments are the only source of funds received by the customer. DESE will not comply with s72A notices in this circumstance
  • Unrestricted Non-Preserved Superannuation (UNPS):
    • Do not issue a new garnishee notice under section 72A to collect UNPS unless the customer has elected to cash these amounts
  • Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) loans and grants:
    • These payments are financial support issued to vulnerable Australians who are currently overseas and have been unable to return to Australia, either because they cannot afford flights or flights are not available. Do not issue a garnishee notice to an account holding a DFAT loan or grant

Note: banks may charge their customers a fee when they comply with an s72A notice. If a bank imposes a fee and the customer is dissatisfied they must speak with their bank.

Services Australia has no control over the imposition of bank fees. If customers wish to avoid these fees, they have the option of making voluntary payments.

Before referring a submission to National ICS ensure no outstanding decisions or other avenues of collection exist.

Is an s72A notice an appropriate option?

  • Yes, check the debt is correct, go to Step 8
  • No, document decision and pursue other collection activities

5

Section 72AC appropriateness + Read more ...

Service Officer action

Issue a direct request for information to confirm a customer with debt is in receipt of a Veterans' pension or allowance that can be garnisheed.

An amount up to 3 times the minimum annual rate of child support (calculated as a fortnightly rate) may be requested from the customers DVA pension and benefit. The amount requested addresses either the ongoing child support and/or any debt.

The References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide 5.2.7: Collection from veterans’ pensions and allowances.

Ensure the debt is correct (based on all available information)

Before issuing an s72AC notice, contact the customer. Ask them to enter a sustainable arrangement to address both ongoing and overdue child support.

Issue an s72AC notice if:

  • the customer refuses to enter an arrangement to address their child support debt, or
  • contact attempts have been unsuccessful, or
  • the customer requests that deductions start

Note: do not deduct from customer’s DVA pension or benefit if already deducting from their Centrelink benefit for ongoing or overdue child support. If the deduction from Centrelink stops, start collecting from the customer’s DVA pension or benefit.

See Contact with Child Support customers.

An s72AC notice can only reflect a periodic amount. The periodic amount requested will be determined by:

  • the type and amount of DVA pension or benefit the customer is receiving
  • the number of the customer’s collect cases, and
  • the amount of overdue child support and/or overpayment

If the customer later contacts Child Support seeking a concession to this amount, see Income Support Payment (ISP) concession for Centrelink deductions.

6

Request s72AC notice + Read more ...

To request an s72AC notice:

  • pend an s72AC request to generate a 72ACPEND intray
  • send an email outlining the collection action to National ICS

Search Officer action

Search Officer receives the email advising of the 72AC PEND intray:

  • review the case to ensure:
    • all negotiations and/or contact attempts with the customer have been made
    • debt correction has taken place
    • all other collection avenues are exhausted
    • a request for an appropriate periodic amount has occurred, and
    • Child Support is not collecting child support through a Centrelink deduction
  • when satisfied the request should proceed, finalise the request on Cuba:
    • a CE08-3 Section 72AC Notice generates and will be set to keyed
    • save and email the notice to National ICS
    • National ICS will forward the notice to the DVA income support payments team

Create a Recovery Action Initiated intray. Monitor for a response from the Search Officer to confirm deductions request outcome.

If a deduction request is established, finalise all outstanding issues while waiting for the first payment form DVA. Payments should be received within 21 days from emailing the garnishee.

See Section 72A, 72B and 72AC notices Cuba Process Help.

7

Withdrawing s72AC notice + Read more ...

Service Officer action:

If a Service Officer determines it is appropriate to withdraw the s72AC notice, send an email requesting the withdrawal of the s72AC notice to National ICS.

Search Officer action:

  • review the request. Withdraw the notice if appropriate
  • see Section 72A, 72B and 72AC notices Cuba Process Help
  • if notice is withdrawn:
    • save and email the withdrawal notice to National ICS
    • National ICS will forward the notice to the DVA income support payments team

8

Check the debt is correct + Read more ...

Review the case to see if the debt is correct.

Is the debt an overpayment?

  • Yes, ensure the overpayment is legally recoverable. Follow the process in Child support overpayments and other payee debt
  • No, check:
    • intrays indicating incomplete work, for example NAPs and estimates
    • the care level is correct in the assessment
    • assessments based on non-ATO incomes, have the best available incomes used. See Income processing for Child Support customers
    • pending Change of Assessment or Objection decisions, and
    • unreconciled estimates. If appropriate and where possible, reconcile these estimates

The Service Officer must be satisfied the debt is correct before issuing a notice to a third party. This ensures Child Support does not take money unless warranted.

In consultation with a SSO or Team Leader, issue the notice. Amend the notice once the debt is corrected if the collection notice is:

  • for ongoing periodic deductions or
  • urgent and the Service Officer has not yet finalised debt correction

See Technical support in Child Support.

Issuing the correct type of notice

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Determine appropriate notice to issue + Read more ...

Does the customer have an overpayment debt?

  • Yes, if enforcement action is required on an overpayment, see Debt repayment
  • No, consider which type of notice is appropriate

Issue the notice through Cuba to serve the notice. Consider whether it is appropriate to issue an s72A notice on an organisation with a Ceased, Insolvent or Duplicate status.

Note: a warning message will appear if the customer has a current alias. The alias may need to be included on the notice/s.

Notices for collection through third parties are issued under s72A of the Child Support (Registration and Collection) Act 1988. An s72A notice may be issued for:

  • a lump sum, go to Step 2
  • a periodic amount, go to Step 3
  • cents in the dollar, go to Step 4

2

Lump sum notices + Read more ...

Lump sum notices are appropriate when the source of funds is:

  • greater than $100. Unless the case has ended or has a low liability and arrears have accrued over a substantial period of time
  • available once only, for example, proceeds from a sale of a house, compensation payout, and
  • likely to become unavailable in the future. For example, funds in a bank account

All Service Officers manage simple lump sum opportunities, such as garnishee of bank accounts.

Other complex/urgent lump sum opportunities requiring ongoing management may be managed by the Lump Sum Team within Intensive Collection Services.

If an opportunity to garnishee for a lump sum is identified, see the Lump sum opportunity in the Customer Referral Guidelines.

Before referring a complex lump sum opportunity:

  • make at least 1 phone contact attempt to all available numbers
  • have a s120 response that identifies a lump sum opportunity no older than 4 weeks
  • issue a CAL with specific action pre-approved text - Collection/enforcement action - see Contact with Child Support customers for surface mail or online delivery response timeframes

If a complex lump sum referral does not meet the criteria, email National ICS for escalation.

Employer Service Officers (ESO) see Step 6 in Table 1 of Employer withholding reconciliation and top up for referral of lump sum opportunities.

An s72A notice may be issued for less than the amount of the outstanding debt. This may be appropriate when issuing a lump sum notice to a financial institution and:

  • the customer's bank account balance is less than the total debt or
  • where Child Support is aware that withdrawals such as mortgage payments will occur

An s120 notice can be used to establish these details.

Once an amount is applied to another party's entitlement, it cannot be refunded.

  • for payments of child support, this amount is visible on the Payee Account Summary window
  • for overpayment of child support, this amount is visible in the Payer Account Summary window

Consider issuing the notice for less than the account balance. This gives the customer sufficient funds to meet their immediate essential living expenses. Before making a decision on the amount to leave in the bank account, take into consideration: the customer’s:

  • living expenses, and
  • expected regular payments

Unclaimed funds:

S72A notices sent to financial institutions specifically to garnishee ‘unclaimed funds’ can only be issued for amount of the ‘unclaimed funds’ identified in the s120 response. Do not issue the s72A for the customer’s full debt amount.

Seek help from a SSO or Team Leader.

Note: s72A notices sent to financial institutions for money held for a customer must be issued for a lump sum amount only.

Go to Step 5.

3

Periodic amount notices + Read more ...

Issue periodic amount notices when EW is not appropriate and the source of funds

  • will replenish at regular intervals, and
  • at regular rates

Examples of EW not being appropriate:

  • the customer is a subcontractor, or
  • protected earnings amount (PEA) restrictions prevent EW due to low earnings, or
  • the customer is a payee with an overpayment

Issue a periodic amounts notice where the employer is administering Parental Leave Pay (PLP) and PEA restricts the amount collectable under EW/EWA to:

  • nil, or
  • a very low amount

Once determined that periodic payments are the most appropriate way to collect the debt, contact the customer. Negotiate a debt repayment amount within or above the acceptable range. If unable to negotiate with them, set the periodic at the upper amount of the acceptable range.

Note: if this is the customer’s only source of income, ensure they are not placed into financial hardship. Some contractors are subject to EW.

Go to Step 5.

4

Cents in the dollar notices + Read more ...

Issue cents in the dollar notices when the funds only become available at irregular intervals.

Examples of funds being available at irregular intervals:

  • customer is a casual employee and employer withholdings is not an option due to protected earnings or
  • customer lodges invoices for payment either at irregular intervals and/or for irregular amounts

Once decided that cents in the dollar is the most appropriate way to collect the debt, contact the customer. Negotiate a debt repayment amount within or above the acceptable range.

If unable to negotiate, set the cents in the dollar to the upper amount of the acceptable range.

PEA does not apply to s72A. Do not place the customer in financial hardship by excessive deductions from their only source of income.

Go to Step 5.

5

Create notice in Cuba + Read more ...

Issue an s72A notice through the Collection window. See Section 72A, 72B and 72AC notices Cuba Process Help, Issue section 72A, 72B and 72AC notice.

If unable to locate the organisation do not create or modify the Organisations on Cuba.

If a new organisation is needed for:

  • a linking request, see Employer Withholding (linking) for Child Support customers
  • any other reason, use the Add attributes request macro to request a new organisation be created

For urgent creation of new organisations and/or the addition of attributes, contact the ES queue line. Use Services Australia Workspace, select Employer Withholding in the corporate directory. The Managed Telecommunications Services Task Cards and Quick Reference Guides page contains task cards for Services Australia Workspace.

In 'special circumstances' the liability for the current month that is due but not yet payable can be included in the notice. A SSO or Team Leader must give approval.

Record decision using the Collection and debt macro, which includes the following:

  • identified or anticipated source of funds
  • contact with the customer
  • negotiation with the customer
  • reasons for the proposed issue of the notice, and
  • any other details relevant to the decision

6

Serve and monitor the notice + Read more ...

The s72A notice will automatically issue through Cuba to the third party.

In exceptional circumstances, Service Officers may need to fax the notice to ensure prompt attention. For example, where there is a risk that the funds may move.

Ensure the fax receipt shows a confirmed status.

Only fax a notice to the recipient if they have agreed to receive them in this manner. After faxing the notice, mail the original notice issued via Cuba. See Letters for Child Support customers.

Consider personal service directly on a third party where prompt action is necessary or there is some risk with compliance. The officer serving the notice should identify the served person and record all details in a sworn affidavit of service. The References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide 6.7: Service of documents.

Cuba will automatically send a copy of the notice to the customer 10 days after issuing the notice to the third party.

Note: some employers and financial institutions can elect to have their notices sent electronically via Child Support Business Online Services. To enrol an organisation, see Child Support Business Online Services.

Cuba automatically issues the following letters:

  • CE08-1 - Section 72A notice to 3rd party
  • CE011-1 - Withdraw Section 72A Notice
  • CE012-1 - Copy of section 72A notice to Payer

The References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide 6.7: Service of documents.

Monitor notice

A notice remains in force until complied with in full or withdrawn in writing. A third party may need to make 1 or more payments to comply with the notice. Sometimes a future event such as sale of property needs to occur before compliance can occur.

7

Debt changes and s72A review + Read more ...

Where a notice is in force all variations must be actioned promptly and the notice reissued accordingly.

If correcting or amending the debt:

  • withdraw the current notice, and
  • send a new notice reflecting the new arrears amount

This must occur when the notice is issued in an urgent situation without debt correction being finalised.

If the customer makes a payment, contact them by phone:

  • acknowledge the payment and
  • advise that the notice has been reissued

Discuss the:

  • outstanding debt with them and
  • possibility of a payment arrangement

Withdraw the current notice and reissue a new notice for the updated debt amount if the customer is:

  • unwilling to discuss the outstanding debt, or
  • has not complied with previous payment arrangements

A 72A Review Intray generates when there has been a reduction to the child support debt since the original notice was issued. To action check the account for any reductions.

If there has been a reduction:

  • contact the customer
  • withdraw the notice and reissue a new 72A notice

If there is no evidence of a reduction:

  • no action is required
  • delete the intray

Note: periodic amounts or cents in the dollar notices only need to be withdrawn or reissued if the variation to the debt does not relate to a payment made by the persons complying with the notice.

8

Withdrawal of notice + Read more ...

An s72A notice already issued must be withdrawn in writing after 3 months if:

  • the customer is no longer employed with the named employer:
    • and the employer verbally notifies of the unemployment. Ask the employer for the customer’s contact details and any new employment details
    • Employer Services Service Officers will complete the Employer Withholding (linking) macro and document the information in the Collection window (CW)
  • there is no activity on the bank account, unless it is a term deposit or similar that has not yet matured
  • the named third party has finalised all financial interests with the customer, for example, a property settlement

Service Officer (SO3) or above are authorised to withdraw an s72A notice. Only Withdraw a notice after considering the reason for issue and the customer’s circumstances. If unsure about a decision, seek help from a SSO.

Note: an s72A notice issued for UNPS amounts, without the customer making an election to cash these amounts must be withdrawn.

If there is evidence suggesting the third party is deducting but not remitting the deductions to Child Support?

  • Yes, consider the relationship between the customer and the third party:
    • Is the relationship between them that of a closely held entity? For example the customer has a personal relationship with the third party or holds a position within the business
    • Email the Tip off analysis unit to start a discussion about whether to withdraw the s72Abefore referring for investigation
  • No, withdraw the notice

If withdrawing and re-issuing an s72A to same third party, contact the third party to explain payment requirements.

If the s72A notice is withdrawn and the customer still has outstanding child support or an overpayment, consider further options to recover the child support debt.

Urgent withdrawal from a financial institution

For an urgent withdrawal of an S72a notice issued to a financial institution, see Contacting and issuing notices to financial institutions in Bank and organisation listing.

An s72A notice not issued through the Collection window will not generate a 72A expiry review intray. Proactively review these notices.

9

Debt paid in full or new notice required + Read more ...

If the debt has been paid in full after the notice is issued:

  • contact the customer by phone to advise the debt has been paid and
  • immediately notify the third party and withdraw the notice in writing

Note: debts paid in full due to compliance with the s72A notice do not need to be formally withdrawn if the notice has been satisfied by the party it was issued to. Withdraw the notice if the debt has been paid by a Tax Refund Intercept Process (TRIP), Non Agency Payment (NAP), account adjustment, customer etc, otherwise the third party will keep making deductions, not knowing the debt is satisfied.

Issue new notice

If the notice is issued against a future settlement, monitor the debt closer to the time of settlement.

If necessary, withdraw the original notice and issue an updated notice reflecting the updated debt balance.

If withdrawing and issuing an updated notice, call the third party to ensure they understand the new amount to withhold.

An s72A periodic notice is satisfied when the amount of the debt stated in the notice is paid in full. Where debt continues to accrue, new notices will need to be issued on a regular basis.

See Section 72A, 72B and 72AC notices Cuba Process Help.

10

S72A DDN RCD NO DEBT intray on customer’s account + Read more ...

An S72A DDN RCD NO DEBT intray generates on the customer's record when:

  • an employer reports a section 72A deduction via STP, and
  • the customer does not have a debt

Note: this can also occur when the employer reports the wrong deduction type through STP (Employer Services will have contacted the employer to rectify this).To action the intray:

  • Check an s72A was issued (in case the employer has reported the incorrect deduction type). If so, delete the intray and document why, or
  • Check the debt has been paid in full, and:
    • Confirm the amount paid via s72A in the Payer Account Summary window
    • Notify the employer and withdraw the s72A notice if required

Note: debts paid in full due to compliance with the s72A notice do not need to be formally withdrawn if the notice has been satisfied by the party. Withdraw the notice if the debt has been paid by a TRIP, NAP, account adjustment, customer etc, otherwise the third party will keep making deductions, not knowing the debt is satisfied.

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Failure to comply – by an employer + Read more ...

Contact the employer if they fail to comply with the s72A notice by the due date.

How to follow up non-compliance of an s72A notice depends on whether the employer is:

  • locked to an Employer Services Service Officer (ESO), or
  • has paying parents linked, or
  • reporting s72A deductions being reconciled by Employer Services

Check the employer record

Is the employer record locked to an ESO or are payers linked?

  • Complete a search to locate the organisation record. Open the organisation record
    • check if the details of an ESO is recorded in the Owner Details field
    • if there are no ESO details, select the close button to be navigated to the Organisation List screen
  • From the Organisation List screen:
    • look at the EW PYR column
    • a number in the EW PYR column indicates the number of payers linked
    • if this column is blank, there are no payers linked to the employer
  • From the Payer Account Summary screen:
    • go to Go/Payer EW enquiry. If this shows current s72A deductions, Employer Services manage the employer

Is the employer managed by an ESO or has payer linked for deductions?

  • Yes:
    • create an Organisation Follow Up intray
    • document a request to follow up compliance with the notice
  • No:
    • promptly make 3 contact attempts. Source alternate numbers for the responsible officers to establish the reason
    • document all contact attempts in the Collection window and Employer/Organisation Communication window

2

Failure to comply – by a third party other than an employer + Read more ...

If a third party fails to comply with the notice, make 3 contact attempts to establish the reason.

Document all contact attempts in the Collection window.

If the third party is a financial institution, see Contacting and issuing notices to financial institutions in Bank and organisation listing.

Go to Step 4.

3

Discuss compliance requirements + Read more ...

Ensure the person understands the requirements and encourage them to comply with the notice voluntarily:

  • confirm they received the notice. If not received, reissue the notice after confirming the address for service of the notice
  • ensure the person being spoken with has the appropriate authority. For example notices issued to a Pty Ltd company, speak to the Public Officer
  • ask for the name (and title if relevant) of the person being spoken with and document this information

If the person expresses concern about their obligations to the customer, tell them:

  • that under Child Support legislation, they are taken to be acting under the authority of the customer
  • the third party is indemnified for any money they pay inline with the notice
  • this means the customer cannot prosecute the third party for complying with the notice

If the person remains uncooperative, remind them of the following details:

  • they may be ordered to pay the amount they failed or refused to deduct. This includes if they no longer hold funds for the customer
  • there is a $2,100 penalty for non-compliance

4

Third party remains non-compliant + Read more ...

Refer the third party for prosecution if they:

  • will not comply with the notice
  • make it clear they will not comply, and/or
  • have passed the money to the customer

Make contact attempts with the non-compliant recipient of the following notices:

  • EF1
  • s72A
  • s120, or
  • s161

If an Organisation fails to remit in response to an s72A notice, report the failure in the Report Suspected Fraud system. See Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption.

If an organisation fails to deduct in response to an s72A notice, or has not provided information in response to an EF1, s120 or s161 notice:

  • refer the case to Employer Services if:
    • there is an indication of no intent to respond to/action the notice, or
    • the required contact attempts are unsuccessful
  • complete the Non-compliance with notice referral template. Copy the information into a General Follow Up intray (for customer) or an Organisation Follow Up intray (for employers) titled 'Non Compliance With Notice' and route to:
    • Region: NATIONAL OFFICE
    • Office: NATIONAL OFFICE
    • Stream/Team: MC SEW NAT EMP SERVICES
    • Position: A – ES LEGAL NOTICE GATE

Employer Services will:

  • reissue the notice with the appropriate signature
  • ensure the notice is correctly addressed, and
  • monitor for 14 days

If the notice is not complied with, Employer Services will:

  • report the suspected fraud
  • document the reported suspected fraud on the Client Profile window

Where the person is deducting under an s72A notice but fails to remit these deductions:

  • withdraw the s72A notice, and
  • report the suspected fraud
