The CCS reconciliation process

Results of Child Care Subsidy (CCS) reconciliation or claims should not be discussed with the child care service. Advise the service to ask the customer to contact Services Australia.

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Viewing CCS reconciliation details + Read more ...

From the Process Direct landing page, select the CCS Reconciliation Dashboard and select the applicable year to view the following information:

  • entries for each session report reconciled
  • the total reconciliation result for the full CCS year
  • estimated income provided throughout the CCS year and entitlement received
  • actual adjusted taxable income and entitlement calculated at reconciliation
  • historical activity test changes throughout the CCS year
  • status of reconciliation income obligations

See Customer enquires about CCS reconciliation outcome for more information.

Customers can view the following under Payment Balancing via their myGov account or Express Plus App:

  • the total reconciliation result for the full CCS year (CCS paid based on estimate vs CCS paid on Adjusted Taxable Income, any ACCS entitlement excluded from income review)
  • any withholdings/offsetting amounts from the reconciliation result

Customer view access to partner and ex-partner information shall align with existing CCS OLS & Nominee process.

2

Timing of CCS reconciliation + Read more ...

CCS reconciliation can occur from the 29th day after the end of the last relevant CCS year.

The reconciliation status can be:

  • Pending, for further information on possible pending reasons see Customer enquiries about CCS reconciliation outcome
  • Replaced, if re-reconciliation has occurred
  • Completed
  • Not to be reconciled, where there is a reason reconciliation could be delayed.
  • Cancelled, if income details have been cancelled by the Australian Tax Office (ATO)

3

Income confirmation requirements + Read more ...

All CCS customers must meet reconciliation requirements by confirming income. This includes confirming income for any partners or ex-partners they may have had during the relevant year.

Income can be confirmed in the following ways:

  • lodging an Income Tax Return with the Australian Tax Office (ATO), or
  • advising Services Australia they are not required to lodge an Income Tax Return and providing their actual income, or
  • submitting a non-lodgement advice to the ATO, and advising actual income to Services Australia even where the income is $0.

The income confirmation requirements apply to ALL CCS customers and their partners/ex-partners. Even customers who have received an Income Support Payment (ISP) for the full CCS year must still confirm their income with Services Australia before reconciliation can occur.

When a customer advises they are not required to lodge an income tax return, this information must be updated in the system for income requirements to be met for the individual. Customers can notify that they or their current partner are not required to lodge using Centrelink online account or Express Plus Centrelink mobile app.

Customers cannot advise their ex-partner is not required to lodge via online services; Service Officers must record on behalf of the customer.

Note: CCS only customers are unable to advise they are not required to lodge an income tax return via their Centrelink online account or Express Plus Centrelink mobile app until after the end of the relevant CCS year, for example: after 12 July 2020 for the 19/20 financial year. Service Officers can record this on behalf of the customer between 01 July and the end of the relevant CCS year where required.

See Recording not required to lodge advice and previous year income.

4

Result of CCS reconciliation + Read more ...

CCS reconciliation can result in:

  • a top up amount. After offsetting to recover any outstanding CCS, CCB reconciliation or CCR debts, any remaining top-up will be issued to the customer's nominated account
  • a nil adjustment. The customer has received their correct entitlement
  • a debt

For more information, see:

  • CCS overpayments arising from reconciliation
  • Debt recovery for Centrelink

5

Letters + Read more ...

All customers will receive a letter following completion of the initial CCS reconciliation.

Letters can be viewed from the Process Direct ‘CORRO’ screen.

CCS reconciliation notifications and/or letters (top up, nil adjustment or overpayment) include:

  • total amounts of CCS paid (either as reduced fees or directly to the customer),
  • the total amount the customer is eligible for, and
  • the balance between the two

6

Does the reconciliation period cover all child care attendance? + Read more ...

CCS reconciliation covers all customers who had at least one day of CCS entitlement (including 0%) in the relevant year.

If the customer lodged a CCS claim within 28 days of child care starting, all child care attendance should be included, as the 28 day backdating provisions will apply.

CCS is based on Monday to Sunday childcare fortnights, the CCS year ends on the Sunday of the fortnight containing 30 June.

For example: CCS for 2018-19 covered the period 2 July 2018 to 30 June 2019.

Service Officers must check:

  • the CCS reconciliation dashboard in Process Direct and select the applicable year
  • Check adequate income details have been received by selecting > Reconciliation History then > Tax return Status
  • Compare those periods with child care attendance details by selecting ‘Provider Payments’ tab
  • The customer is liable for any child care fees for attendance prior to the 28 day CCS backdated start date
  • If all periods are paid for in the CCS claim but the customer indicates the child has attended care for periods child care attendance has not been received, advise the customer to discuss the issue with their child care provider
  • If a child care provider has missed submitting child care attendance for a period outside of 28 days, they will need to seek approval from the Department of Education, Skills and Employment to submit late attendance
  • If attendance is submitted outside of 28 days, the system will automatically re-reconcile any previous CCS assessments for that relevant year
  • Record details on a DOC of any advice given or action taken

Note: in response to coronavirus (COVID-19), a $0 session fee was recorded on every current CCS enrolment on 6 April 2020 and 12 July 2020. Further updates were made on 12 October 2020 with $0 session fees recorded for children in Victoria only, who had active enrolments and had not attended child care for at least 10 weeks.

7

Dates of effect used for circumstance changes + Read more ...

CCS entitlement is calculated in a CCS fortnight, starting on the relevant Monday until the second Sunday after that day.

With any change of circumstances 3 different dates apply:

  • date of receipt (the date Services Australia is notified of the change)
  • date of event (the date the change of circumstance actually occurred)
  • date of effect (the date the changes will be applied to their payment)

The date the change takes effect will be determined by:

  • the date the customer notified of the change, and
  • if the impact of the change has a favourable or unfavourable outcome on the customer’s entitlement

See Changes in circumstances and date of effect for CCS and ACCS.

8

Deadlines + Read more ...

A late lodger process applies to CCS reconciliation. Any customer who does not meet CCS reconciliation requirements by certain deadlines will be penalised.

1st Deadline

Customers must meet reconciliation requirements by midnight 30 June of the year following the one being reconciled.

If they do not meet the requirements by this time, their CCS and ACCS entitlement (except ACCS (Child Wellbeing) will be reduced to 0% from the first CCS Monday after the 1st deadline date.

Note: on 30 June 2020, the Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE) announced that the 1st deadline date for 2018-19 financial year would be extended until 31 March 2021. This is to allow customers who have not met their CCS reconciliation requirements for 2018-19 to retain their CCS entitlement until 5 April 2021, the first CCS Monday in the fortnight following 31 March 2021.

This means that for the 2018-19 financial year, customers have until 31 March 2021 to meet their reconciliation requirements for CCS. This extension only applies to CCS and does not apply to FTB or for ATO lodgement requirements for the 2018-19 financial year.

If income requirements have not been met, a non-lodger undetermined debt will be raised which totals all CCS amounts paid during the relevant year. The customer will not have to pay the debt at this point.

For information on identifying whether a customer is affected by the 1st deadline, see What to check when CCS rate has changed.

2nd Deadline

If customers do not meet the requirements by midnight 30 June of the year following the 1st deadline, or by the 2nd deadline extension, CCS eligibility will be cancelled from the following CCS Monday. The undetermined non-lodger debt which was raised at the 1st deadline now becomes determined. The customer is required to repay the non-lodger debt amount until they confirm their family income for the relevant outstanding year.

If relevant year income is confirmed after the 2nd deadline:

  • the determined non lodger debt status is changed to Finalised-No Debt (FND) and recovery ceases
  • Reconciliation is completed for the outstanding year which may result in:
    • A nil adjustment – the customer has received correct entitlement, or
    • A debt, or
    • A top up amount, which will not be paid to the customer.
    • Any withholding amounts will also not be paid. However, the withholding amounts may be used to offset the current year reconciliation debt.

With reconciliation completed, the customer can reclaim CCS but a claim will only be deemed effective if:

  • there are no outstanding CCS debt amounts, or
  • if the customer has an adequate repayment plan in place

Note: the customer will enter an automatic repayment arrangement through the granting of the new CCS claim which will meet this requirement.

CCS rate

Results of Child Care Subsidy reconciliation or claims should not be discussed with the child care provider. Advise the provider to ask the customer to contact Services Australia.

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Factors that determine the rate of CCS payable + Read more ...

The following three factors will determine the rate of CCS a family may be entitled to receive:

  • Combined family income - see Item 2
  • CCS Activity test - see Item 3
  • Type of child care used - see Item 4

2

Income + Read more ...

A CCS customer’s percentage (%) rate is based on their family's estimated Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI). After the end of the financial year, the family's CCS rate is balanced (reconciled) using their actual ATI.

See Combined family income for CCS.

3

Activity test + Read more ...

The number of hours of subsidised child care a family can access in a fortnight is determined by an activity test. The customer (and their partner) need to participate in recognised activity/ies for a specific amount of hours, or have an exemption. See Activity Test for CCS.

Updating the activity test details for a customer will trigger a re-reconciliation.

Exceptions:

  • ACCS (Grandparent) provides a subsidy equal to the actual fee charged, up to 120% of the hourly rate cap (whichever is lower), for up to 100 hours per fortnight
  • ACCS (Child Wellbeing) provides a subsidy equal to 100% of the fee charged, up to 120% of the hourly rate cap and up to 100 hours of subsidised care per fortnight (regardless of the activity level of the customer and/or partner)
  • ACCS (Transition to Work) provides a subsidy equal to 95% of the fee charged, up to 95% of the hourly rate cap and up to their eligible subsidised hours of care

The customer's eligible hour limit is applied across all services.

If the customer:

  • uses more than 1 service, CCS is paid during the year based on the hours the customer has nominated to use at each service, or
  • has not nominated the hours with the service, services are paid on a first in first paid basis during the year

At reconciliation, the nominated CCS hours will not be reassessed up to the customer's eligible hour limit, across all services.

4

Child Care service + Read more ...

The type of child care service used will determine the maximum hourly rate cap to which a customer’s CCS percentage will be applied.

  • Centre-based long day care
  • Family Day care
  • Outside School Hours care
  • In Home care

See:

  • Entitlement for Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • In Home Care (IHC) for Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • Updating school status, and assessing age exemptions for Child Care Subsidy (CCS)

Child care attendance

Results of approved Child Care Subsidy (CCS) reconciliation or claims should not be discussed with the child care service. Advise the service to ask the customer to contact Services Australia.

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Child care session reports from services + Read more ...

  • Services transfer attendance details directly to the Department of Education, Skills and Employment who transfer details to Services Australia
  • Services Australia calculates the customer's CCS and passes the relevant amounts on to the child care provider for the CCS fortnight
  • CCS reconciliation will not wait for late submitted attendance. Services are expected to report attendance weekly. Reconciliation commences from the 29th day after the relevant CCS year. It is assumed that services will have already submitted all attendance for the year

Attendance received post-reconciliation

If a service reports attendance for additional weeks after reconciliation, any top up of CCS is paid to the customer.

Note: if a childcare service reports a change to attendance that results in a debt, the debt will be raised with the provider.

Viewing attendance details

Customers can use Online Services such as myGov, or the Express Plus App to view their childcare attendance reported by their provider.

Staff can view a customer's attendance reports in Process Direct. Use the Process Direct>Child Care >View session report twisty option to view a customer's attendance details. This is for periods prior to reconciliation.

If the child's attendance received does not match all periods attended by the child for a childcare service, the customer may dispute the attendance details, but this must be done with the childcare service.

2

Child care absences + Read more ...

  • CCS/ACCS are payable for days where: a child does not attend care,
    • up to the allowable limit per child,
    • across all child care services,
    • in each relevant financial year
  • CCS/ACCS can be paid, without evidence and for any reason, up to 42 days per child each financial year. This applies across all services that the child attends.
  • Public holidays are included in the absence count, if:
    • the child would normally have attended the child care service on that day (if it were not a public holiday),
    • and fees have been charged for that day for the child
  • After the 42 day absence allowance has been reached, additional absences are paid if:
    • evidence has been provided to the child care service, and
    • flagged as part of the attendance
  • An absence is deemed as unapproved when:
    • a customer has exceeded the 42 allowable absence day limit, and
    • no further evidence is provided
  • When a child does not attend child care for a continuous period of 8 weeks, the enrolment is automatically ceased
  • At reconciliation, the system identifies customers who have received CCS for more than the allowed absences as part of the calculations

For more information, see Absences for CCS and ACCS.

Note:

2019-20 financial year - In response to coronavirus (COVID-19) customers were entitled to an extra 20 absences for any reason from 5 April 2020. This allowed customers to access 62 paid absence days without evidence being required. Child Care services were not required to submit session reports from 6 April 2020 to 12 July 2020.

2020-21 financial year - Victorian families with children in childcare will get 30 extra absence days for the 20/21 financial year. This applies to families living in Victoria, with children that attend a Victorian childcare service. These extra days are now called special purpose absence days.

See Absences for CCS and ACCS.

3

Customer disputes child care attendance details + Read more ...

Confirm payments have been made for the relevant fortnight.

Advise the customer they must contact the childcare service.

Income reconciliation

Results of Child Care Subsidy (CCS) reconciliation or claims should not be discussed with the child care service. Advise the service to ask the customer to contact Services Australia.

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Income details used for reconciliation + Read more ...

Colour coded income source indicators are used to determine where the income used in the CCS reconciliation calculation came from.

A different income source may be received for the customer, current partner and ex-partner.

Income sources apply according to the following hierarchy:

  • Actual Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) - Actual taxable income details received from the Australian Taxation Office which include:
    • reportable fringe benefits (including any exempt reportable fringe benefits), total net rental property losses and Tax exempt foreign income
    • These income details will display in Green in the CCS Reconciliation dashboard
    • Tax Free pension/benefits, foreign income and Deductible Child maintenance expenditure will be in Black. These income components are declared by the customer and will not be received as part of an Income Tax Return
  • ATO Return Not Needed (RNN). Individuals can advise NRTL to Services Australia or the ATO, but income details still need to be advised to Services Australia to meet the reconciliation requirements.
    Once NRTL coding is complete (that means both components of the coding are complete, FNLE and FIPY), the income values will display in Purple in the CCS Reconciliation Dashboard
  • Non Lodger Exemption (NRE). If income details are provided to Services Australia as part of a NRTL advice, the income values will display in Red in the CCS Reconciliation Dashboard

2

Income reconciliation periods + Read more ...

All periods where the customer received CCS entitlement, including 0% will be subject to income reconciliation.

If the customer received Additional Child Care Subsidy during the relevant financial year, income reconciliation will not be performed for those periods. See:

  • ACCS (Grandparent)
  • ACCS (Child Wellbeing)
  • ACCS (Transition To Work)

3

Most Recent Estimate (MRE) exception + Read more ...

CCS reconciliation requires income for all ex-partners from the relevant financial year. If a customer is unable to meet income requirements by the 1st deadline only due to the inaction of an ex-partner, the customer can request a Most Recent Estimate (MRE) exception. This will allow the balancing process to complete using the most recent estimate of the ex/previous-partner's income from the 1st deadline.

Is the customer requesting a MRE exception?

Advise the reconciliation will not occur until after the 1st deadline unless the ex/previous -partner meets CCS reconciliation conditions before this date.

Note: if an MRE exception is applied and the ex/previous -partner subsequently lodges a tax return, CCS will be re-reconciled based on the new income received.

This could result in:

  • arrears,
  • a nil adjustment, or
  • overpayment

A MRE exception cannot override actual income received for the ex/previous -partner.

Does the customer accept these conditions and still wish to apply the MRE Exception?

  • Yes, see Exceptional Circumstances for CCS and ACCS for coding information
  • No, procedure ends here

Table 5

Step

Action

1

CCS manual adjustments + Read more ...

Where the CCS reconciliation result must be manually adjusted, once updated, this will prompt a re-reconciliation to occur.

CCS manual adjustments will only be undertaken by staff with specific security roles.

2

Deceased customers + Read more ...

For a deceased customer or partner actual income is converted to an annualised actual income amount. This is so the annual income test can be applied.

When a customer or partner dies during the relevant financial year, the reconciliation process remains the same except if:

  • a tax return is required to be lodged for the deceased, it will be lodged by the Estate
  • the deceased actual income is converted to an annualised actual income amount The time the deceased was alive is then assessed under the ex-partner income rules and that amount of income used in the final reconciliation amount

Note: if the partner is deceased, the annualised income figure will be compared to the estimated figure for the relevant period. Reconciliation will calculated on the most beneficial of these amounts.

See the Resources page for scenarios of how deceased income rules are applied.

3

Child immunisation status + Read more ...

CCS reconciliation will not recheck a customer's eligibility for periods a child is marked as not meeting CCS immunisation requirements.

A customer is eligible to be assessed for CCS entitlement when:

  • the child meets CCS immunisation requirements
  • CCS for the child is in an immunisation grace period
  • a CCS immunisation exemption is recorded

See Immunisation requirements for CCS.

Re-reconciliation

Results of approved Child Care Subsidy (CCS) reconciliation or claims should not be discussed with the child care service. Advise the service to ask the customer to contact Services Australia.

Table 6

Item

Description

1

What is re-reconciliation of CCS? + Read more ...

Re-reconciliation of CCS will automatically occur if:

  • the customer has received CCS, and
  • CCS reconciliation has been finalised for the customer after the end of the relevant financial year, and
  • information for the relevant financial year is updated. For example:
    • actual adjusted taxable income details are transferred from the ATO. See Item 3
    • new or corrected child care details are received. See Item 4. Note: when attendance is amended, the system waits three days before re-reconciliation occurs
    • Other details are updated. See Item 5
  • the system recalculates the customer's CCS entitlements using the new information and compares this with the result of the most recent reconciliation. The result will either be a top up, overpayment or nil adjustment
  • ACCS periods are excluded from reconciliation. For re-reconciliation to occur for these periods, entitlement to ACCS would have ceased which would then require the CCS entitlement to be re-reconciled

CCS reconciliation letters (top up, nil adjustment or overpayment) include total amounts of ‘Child Care Subsidy entitlement’

2

When does information need to be received by? + Read more ...

For time limits and date of effect of review of decisions, see Customer initiated review of decision.

Revised actual income can be transferred by the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) at any time to trigger re-reconciliation. This depends on whether the link established through Mutual Customer Identification still exists with the ATO. The link with ATO is ended after three years, for example after 30 June 2009 for 2005-06 unless the customer has an outstanding FTB non-lodger debt for the financial year.

If the link is closed, further investigation may be required. See Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) process for family assistance customers.

Updates to child care attendance details reported by an approved child care service can be received at any time and will trigger re-reconciliation.

3

Income details + Read more ...

Re-reconciliation of CCS will occur automatically if:

  • CCS reconciliation was finalised using estimated income for example: non lodger, for the customer or their ex/previous partner and actual income details are later received from the ATO for the person
  • the ATO amends a Notice of Assessment and transfers corrected actual income for the customer, current partner or ex/previous partner to the agency. ATO link closed: further investigation may be required. See Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) process for family assistance customers

For more information on when a customer wishes to revise estimated income for a previous financial year, see Item 5.

4

Child care attendance + Read more ...

Child care attendance is expected to be reported weekly by the service and then these details are applied and assessed against the customer’s record.

Processing rules apply to attendance when it is cancelled after reconciliation is finalised.

Any child care attendance submitted after reconciliation has occurred (if the customer is entitled to any further CCS entitlement) is then paid directly to the customer and not the service.

Customers registered for online services can use the 'View Child Care Details option' to view attendance details reported by their child care services.

For changes to attendance after the initial reconciliation has occurred, these changes will display differently.

Changes to attendance post reconciliation that cause a change to the outcome will be visible via the ‘Provider payments’ tab within the CCS Reconciliation Dashboard.

No new instance of a ‘Replaced’ reconciliation result will appear within the CCS Reconciliation Dashboard. Staff can still see the changes to the attendance session by selecting the most recent reconciliation result tab and accessing the ‘Provider Payments’ and seeing where the change in outcome was generated

5

Other details + Read more ...

If any of the details below are updated by a Service Officer, CCS re-reconciliation will be triggered:

  • revised estimated adjusted taxable income is recorded (for example, non-lodger update) for the customer, current partner or previous partner
  • TFN details are recorded for the customer, current partner or previous partner
  • customer is recorded as deceased. Re-reconciliation will result in an overpayment for any CCS entitlement previously calculated for the period from the Monday of the CCS fortnight following the date of death
  • child is recorded as deceased. Re-reconciliation will include all child care attendance recorded for the child and any fee reduction that applied after the child's death, or
  • income support payment is retrospectively cancelled for the customer, current partner or a previous partner (for reasons other than compensation - 'CMP'). CCS will be income tested from the Monday of the CCS fortnight following the date of effect of the cancellation and an overpayment may result. For reason 'CMP', the customer is still entitled to CCS as if income support payment was not cancelled
  • immunisation details are updated
  • child in Child Care Details updated (child entered or left customer's care). At reconciliation, the system uses child care details reported in the customer's name to determine eligibility rather than child in customer care details
  • school child status
  • activity test eligible hours

This list does not include all updates that may trigger re-reconciliation. It is important to note that although re-reconciliation is triggered, the result may not change.

6

Result of re-reconciliation of CCS + Read more ...

Re-reconciliation is an automatic process.

The result of re-reconciliation can be:

  • a top-up amount. After any debt offsetting, any remaining top-up amount is issued to the customer unless the customer did not meet the income requirements at the 2nd deadline
  • a debt
  • nil adjustment

An automatic letter is issued to the customer to advise the result, unless there is nil adjustment.

Viewing results of re-reconciliation of CCS

See Customer enquires about CCS reconciliation outcome.

If a customer does not agree with reconciliation result + Read more ...

If the customer has not had the CCS reconciliation explained to them, see Customer enquires about CCS reconciliation outcome.

The customer has review and appeal rights if they do not agree with the decision after it has been explained to them.

2

Manually adjusting the reconciliation result after review + Read more ...

Adjustments are made automatically. For example, if the service was unable to pass on the CCS fee reduction to the customer, any amounts already paid will not be factored into the reconciliation result.

If the customer's reconciliation result is incorrect due to:

  • an administrative error, check whether the account can be partially or fully waived under Administrative error waivers. Manual adjustment of the reconciliation result may not be needed
  • a system problem/issue, details of the customer's record must be referred to technical support
  • inaccurate customer details on the system, correcting the customer's details will generate re-reconciliation. Manual adjustment is not needed.
