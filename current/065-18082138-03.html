Home care means assessment process – post-entry

Table 1: This table contains the process steps to complete a post-entry home care means assessment in Customer First and Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS).

Step

Action

1

Preliminary check and dates for post-entry assessment + Read more ...

Complete the Aged care means assessment – preliminary checks to confirm the claim is ready to assess.

Determine the date of event (DOV) for a home care means assessment – post-entry.

Is this care recipient (or partner) in receipt of a means tested income support payment (ISP)?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Date of receipt for care recipient receiving income support payment (ISP) + Read more ...

Determine the correct date of receipt (DOR) to use for this means assessment. Investigate further to obtain the correct date for ISP purposes as well as the means assessment.

Does this care recipient have a status of RCA/CUR?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Date of receipt (DOR) for self-funded retiree + Read more ...

The DOR used for a means assessment is the DOR of the auto activity or the DOR of the means assessment form, whichever is on the Activity List (AL) screen.

Does this care recipient have a status of RCA/CUR?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Care Recipient is RCA/CUR (including means not disclosed) + Read more ...

Customer First record shows RCA/CUR.

Go to the RCA Circumstance (RCIRC) screen and check if the care recipient is currently in residential care.

In residential care

  • RCA/CUR-DSS or RCA/CUR-SFR:
    • Cancel RCA/HOM on AL screen
    • Key discharge details on RCIRC screen
    • Finalise via AR screen
    • Re-index new claim from correct date

Not in residential care

  • RCA/CUR-MTA:
    • Action using RCA/HOM activity
    • Delete previous entries in Reform Enquiry Means Test field on RCA Means Test Details (RMND) screen
    • Continue with the means assessment
  • RCA/CUR-SFR – care recipient is in home care with a Means Not Disclosed (MND) status of Y:
    • Cancel the RCA/HOM activity on AL screen
    • Update RMND screen MND status to N
    • Go to Benefit Action (BA) screen
    • Key Service Reason as ‘RCA’, Act as ‘REA’, Date of Effect as the date of entry
    • Continue with coding of the means assessment

See also: Cancelling claim activities and re-indexing claims.

5

Check the source of the RCA/HOM activity + Read more ...

If required, index an RCA/HOM activity. See manual processing table in Indexing a customer’s claim on the system for help.

Has a calculation of your cost of care been completed for the RCA/HOM activity?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No:
    • Verbal assessment for an ISP customer, go to Step 7
    • Auto RCA/HOM activity, go to Step 8

6

Confirm responsible agency/department for means assessment + Read more ...

See the Resources page for the Responsible Departments for Aged Care Assessments task card to determine responsible agency/department.

Which agency/department is responsible for completing a means assessment?

  • Services Australia, see Table 2
  • Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA), go to Step 12

7

Verbal assessments + Read more ...

When undertaking a verbal assessment:

  • Ask the care recipient if they want to give their income details to receive a home care means assessment
  • Advise the care recipient that maximum aged care fees may be payable if income details are not given
  • Discuss when the care recipient’s Customer First record was last updated:
    • Update new information as needed
    • Use the questions on the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care as a guide while on the phone with the care recipient (if needed)
  • Read the ‘Declaration for the person the assessment is for’ to the care recipient and record the response

Can the care recipient complete the home care means assessment verbally (see Table 3 for help)?

  • Yes, including Means Not Disclosed (MND), see Table 3
  • No, further information required, see Table 2

8

Check if care recipient is eligible for an automatic assessment + Read more ...

For care recipients with a discharge from home care coded, see Table 4.

An automatic means assessment is completed for a care recipient who:

  • had a home care means assessment for a previous home care package, and a break in care of less than 28 days, or
  • receives income support from the Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA) and eligible for an automatic assessment. See the Resources page for the Responsible Departments for Aged Care Assessments task card

For care recipients:

  • in receipt of a means tested ISP:
    • an RCA/HOM activity is created by the entry event in ACMPS
    • complete the RCA/HOM activity using the information currently held on the Services Australia or DVA record
  • with a break in care less than 28 days:
    • can use any valid means assessment when changing providers
    • see Aged care means assessment – preliminary checks for information about assessment validity periods

Does the care recipient meet any of the above criteria?

  • Yes, and:
    • Services’ Australia is responsible for the means assessment, go to Step 9
    • DVA is responsible for the means assessment, go to Step 12
  • No, go to Step 11

9

Automatic assessment failed - check the RCA/HOM and finalise + Read more ...

Finalise the RCA/HOM activity using the information held on record.

Before finalising the RCA/HOM:

  • confirm from ACMPS the date care started
  • update in Customer First:
    • RCIRC screen if updated within the activity
    • RCA Dependants (RDEP) screen if the care recipient has dependent child/ren

10

Series 2 letter process – request to lodge an Aged Care – Calculation of your cost of care + Read more ...

If a care recipient starts home care without a valid means assessment, Services Australia must issue the care recipient with a written request to lodge an Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care . Letters issue automatically from ACMPS.

If the care recipient does not respond to the written requests, the means assessment is finalised as means not disclosed (MND).

In ACMPS, check the Communication History tab for letter codes:

  • 2A Request to lodge an calculation of your cost of care - sent 3 days after the date of notification of the entry date
  • Note: apply MND 25 days plus an allowance for the letter to be received or earlier if the care recipient advises they are not disclosing their means

Did ACMPS send 2A letter to the care recipient?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, issue a manual letter and place the claim on hold:
    • 2A (initial request) – hold for 25 days from the date the letter is sent plus an allowance for the letter to be received
    • See the Aged Care Series 2 Manual Letters task card for help
    • Procedure ends here

11

Series 2 letter sent + Read more ...

Has the allowable time passed since the 2A letter was sent to the care recipient?

  • Yes, finalise the means assessment as MND, see Table 4
  • No, put the claim back on hold:
    • 2A (initial request) – hold claim for 25 days plus an allowance for the letter to be received from the date the letter is sent
    • Procedure ends here

12

Care recipient in receipt of means tested DVA payment + Read more ...

If the care recipient lodges an Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care form, send the form to DVA for completion. See the Resources page for the Responsible Departments for Aged Care Assessments task card for the process.

Check ACMPS for:

  • means assessment data provided by DVA, and
  • fees are set from the date of entry

In Customer First, reject the RCA/HOM activity:

  • Select the started RCA/HOM activity from the Activity List (AL) screen
  • Navigate to the RCA Means Test Details (RMND) screen and confirm:
    • Means Not Disclosed Date of Event field is blank
    • Means Not Disclosed Indicator field is blank
    • If the above fields are populated delete the existing information
  • Navigate to the Benefit Action (BA) screen to reject the assessment activity:
    • Key ‘RCA’ in the Service Reason field
    • Key ‘REJ’ in the Action field
    • Key ‘DVA’ in the Reason code field
    • Enter the date of admission to care in the Date of Event (DOV) field
  • Record a manual (DOC) using Fast Note Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care Assessment REJ DVA
  • Copy the Fast Note details into the Notes tab in ACMPS
  • Complete any open work items in Customer First

Outstanding information

Table 2: This table contains the process steps to complete the RCA/HOM activity when there is outstanding information.

Step

Action

1

Check Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care form for an authorised person or organisation + Read more ...

If the Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf section on the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care form has been completed, and all signatures/documentation are provided, see Appointing nominees to code a nominee. This nominee will be added to all aged care systems through the means assessment.

Refer to Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) or Update (PPU) authority if a third party has been nominated as a:

Check if additional information or documentation is required with the claim + Read more ...

  • Confirm all details in the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care are complete

  • Check all supporting documentation is:
    • provided with the claim, or
    • can be accepted verbally
  • Check the form is signed by:
    • the care recipient, or
    • an authorised third party
    • see Aged care - consent and disclosure for more information

Income and asset details supplied as part of a home care means assessment may need supporting documentation to verify the information provided is true and accurate.

For care recipients in receipt of an ISP, see Verifying income and assets for the limited circumstances when supporting documentation is required.

Is more information needed before finalising the assessment?

  • Yes, and a request for information letter has:
    • not previously sent, go to Step 3
    • previously sent, see Table 6
  • No, see Table 3

3

Care recipient discharged from care + Read more ...

Do not issue further request for information letters if:

  • the care recipient is discharged from home care, and
  • further supporting information is needed to complete the assessment

Is the care recipient discharged from care?

  • Yes, go to Table 4
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Collect outstanding information + Read more ...

Additional information can be taken over the phone if no supporting documentation is needed to complete the assessment.

Are supporting documents needed?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Collect information verbally + Read more ...

Call the care recipient, nominee or POA.

There is no obligation to provide information verbally but Service Officers must advise of the benefits and the potential result of failing to provide the information.

Can the outstanding information be collected verbally?

  • Yes, obtain the required information and see Table 3
  • No, and:
    • the care recipient does not want to disclose their means, see Table 4
    • contact is unsuccessful, or
    • the care recipient wants a written request for the information, or
    • verification is needed, go to Step 6

6

Issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter + Read more ...

  • Issue an aged care RFI letter, see Aged care letters - creating manual letters
  • Complete a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) referral (if needed)
  • Place claim on hold for 21 days plus an allowance for the letter to be received for the care recipient to respond to the request
  • Procedure ends here

Contact and address details

Table 3: This table contains the process to update contact details for a care recipient and correspondence nominee.

Step

Action

1

Dependent children + Read more ...

A care recipient is exempt from paying the home care income tested care fee (ITCF) if they have a dependent child in their care. However, income details must be maintained on the record.

If a care recipient has a dependent child in their care, on the RCA Dependants (RDEP) screen record:

  • Number of Dependants Date of Event: DOV of means assessment
  • Total Number of Dependants: enter number of dependent children

See the References page for a link to the Aged Care Act 1997 definition of a dependent child.

2

Update care recipient name and home address details + Read more ...

If the care recipient has provided a new home address, telephone details or alternate names:

  • Select the RCA/HOM activity
  • Go to the Contact Detail Task Selector (CDTS) screen
    • Select Customer Details to update alternate names on Customer Person Detail (CPD) screen
    • Select Address to update the care recipient’s home address on the Address Details (AD) screen
  • Update the contact number on the Telephone Details (TD) screen
  • Update the email address on the Email Address (EMA) screen
  • Update the office code back to the Home Office

3

Update care recipient’s postal address + Read more ...

Update a postal address in Customer First when requested by:

  • a care recipient
  • a court appointed guardian or administrator
  • a power of attorney (POA), or
  • an existing Centrelink correspondence nominee, when the postal address is not the same as their own postal address

A postal address is not updated in Customer First when requested by:

  • an existing Centrelink correspondence nominee, when the postal address is the same as their own postal address

For additional information on updating addresses (including postal) see:

  • Updating address details
  • Appointing nominees
  • Aged care request for a nominee
  • Accepting information from and disclosing information to Power of Attorney

Income and assets details

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Assets and income information + Read more ...

An Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care requests a care recipient to provide details of their income. Supporting documentation may include a history of income and assets.

A care recipient in receipt of an income support payment (ISP):

  • May choose to use the income information currently recorded in Customer First
  • If it is more than 2 years since the existing details were updated, attempt to confirm (by phone) if income or assets need updating
  • If contact is unsuccessful, accept the care recipient’s declaration of income

If the partner has recently died, do not contact the care recipient or correspondence nominee. See Bereavement reviews for further information on reviews following the death of a member of a couple.

Has the care recipient advised of any changes to assets and income information?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Select screens to update income and assets + Read more ...

Care recipients receiving ISP may provide details of changes that have occurred before the date of event (DOV) of the assessment.

Key these changes as part of the home care means assessment.

The DOV for multiple ISP updates must include:

  • the date of the change – as stated in the supporting documentation
  • the date of lodgement – if further changes have occurred

To update:

  • select the screens from Residential Care Task Selector (RCTS) screen
    • if data already exists on a screen, a Y will appear on the RCTS screen
    • check this data for accuracy

The coding of income and assets for aged care purposes aligns with the Social Security Act 1991. See the below links for further information:

  • Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services
  • Verifying income and assets
  • Financial Investments
  • Exempt income and assets for Centrelink payments
  • Assessing deprivation/gifting
  • Foreign income and assets
  • Aged Care means assessment with total assessable assets equal to or greater than $10,000,000 (and applicable assessable income)

3

Check if employment income received + Read more ...

Does the care recipient (or partner) have employment income?

  • Yes, and care recipient (or partner):
    • receives an ISP, go to Step 4
    • does not receive an ISP, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

4

Code employment income – ISP care recipients + Read more ...

Employment income reporting requirements for a care recipient (or partner) in receipt of an ISP:

  • Statement reporter
    • Must report any income each fortnight before ISP is paid
  • Notification reporter
    • ISP paid automatically and must report any income or other changes within 14 days of the change occurring

ISP care recipients (or partners) should already have employment income coded. If this is the first advice that the care recipient (or partner) receives employment income, see Recording and correcting employment income details for help.

Go to Step 6

5

Code employment income – self funded retirees + Read more ...

Care recipients not in receipt of an ISP:

  • Do not need to update their income each fortnight
  • Have their income assessed as a notification reporter i.e. must report any income or other changes within 14 days
  • If income is variable, average this over a suitable period

See Determining the Date of Event for employment income for help in coding income for notification reporters.

6

Other income sources + Read more ...

A care recipient (or partner) may have income from other sources. For example:

  • rental income for their own home or other real estate
  • income from a business – sole trader or partnership
  • an involvement in a Private Company or Private Trust (including Special Disability Trust)

Does the care recipient have income from real estate or business (sole trader or partnership)?

  • Yes to any of the above, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 10

7

Real estate and business income + Read more ...

Update the Real Estate and Business (REBS) screen if the care recipient (or partner) indicates they have:

  • rental income from real estate, including the principal home
  • income from a business partnership, a farm or from operating as a sole trader

Does the care recipient (or partner) receive rental income from real estate, including their principal home?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Code rental income + Read more ...

For help with coding rental income, see Assessing and coding the Real estate details Mod R, and Income from boarders and lodgers.

  • Add new property details:
    • Update rental income
    • Code asset value as $0
    • For care recipients in receipt of a means tested ISP, issue a Real estate details form (Mod R) (see the Resources page for a link to the form). See Requesting information (CLK) for more information
  • Update existing property details:
    • Update rental income
    • Do not change the previously coded asset value

9

Code business income + Read more ...

When a care recipient receives income from a business, any income from the business will need to be included in a home care means assessment. If the income of a business has now changed, see Changes to income and assets from a business structure.

Does the care recipient receive income from a sole trader business or a business partnership?

  • Yes, code income only then, go to Step 10
    • For sole trader business coding, see Sole traders
    • For business partnership coding, see Partnerships
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Interest in a private trust or company + Read more ...

Does the care recipient have an interest in a private trust or private company?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, see Table 6

11

CAO referral required for private trust or private company + Read more ...

If the care recipient (or partner) has an interest in a private trust or private company:

  • See Trusts or Companies for details on documents needed for and Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) referrals
  • See Identifying and making suitable referrals to the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) for information on referring to a CAO for assessment

In Customer First:

  • Use the CAO Referral guided procedure to complete in the RCA system
  • Place the claim on hold for 28 days pending return from the CAO
  • The CAO will document their assessment on the care recipient record
    • The assessment may include coding instructions for the actioning Service Officer
  • When the CAO assessment is complete, see the Table 6

Finalise the RCA/HOM activity

Table 6

Step

Action

1

Means assessment is ready to finalise + Read more ...

Was the care recipient discharged from home care before the completion of this means assessment?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 5

2

Care recipient discharged from home care + Read more ...

To retain entry information in the Customer First record, the RCA/HOM activity is finalised for all discharged care recipients.

Was the means assessment completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Care recipient discharged from home care – means assessment not completed + Read more ...

Reject the RCA/HOM activity in Customer First when:

  • a care recipient is discharged from home care before their means assessment is finalised, and
  • the means assessment was not completed
    • For example, the information/documentation needed was not lodged

To reject:

  • Key ‘S’ to select the RCA/HOM activity
  • Navigate to BA screen to reject the RCA/HOM:
    • Key ‘RCA’ in the SVC RSN field
    • Key ‘REJ’ in the Action field
    • Key ‘OTH’ in the Reason field
    • Enter the date of event/entry (DOV) in the Effect Date field
    • Notes: REJ-OTH Customer discharged from Home Care prior to means assessment completion

4

Code discharge details + Read more ...

After the RCA/HOM is finalised, record the discharge information in Customer First.

  • Navigate to the RCIRC screen:
    • Add the departure date into the Discharge Date field. This is the Departure Date recorded in the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS)
    • Key ‘?’ in the Discharge Reason field. Select the reason that most closely matches the Departure Reason coded in ACMPS
    • Complete the activity
    • Annotate the Aged Care > Assessment Fast Note with the discharge details
    • Procedure ends here

5

Finalise the means assessment + Read more ...

Finalise the means assessment as means not disclosed (MND) when the care recipient:

  • has not provided an Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care (after the initial request and reminder letter sent), or
  • has not replied to an Aged Care Request for Information (RFI) letter sent, or
  • chooses not to disclose their information

Is this assessment being finalised as MND?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Finalise the RCA/HOM as MND status + Read more ...

Before finalising the means assessment as MND, check if the care recipient has a current means assessment completed by the Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA).

In ACMPS check:

  • Means Testing Data tab
  • Means Tested Assessments tab

If the latest entry event is after a care recipient changed responsibility from DVA to Services Australia, do not complete as MND. See the Resources page for the Responsible Departments for Aged Care Assessments task card.

Note: MND care recipients pay the full amount for the cost of their care.

Finalise the RCA/HOM activity as MND:

  • Select the RCA/NCL activity
  • Go to the RMND screen and update:
    • Enter the date entered care in the Means Not Disclosed Date of Event field
    • Key ‘Y’ in the Means Not Disclosed Indicator field

7

Attach Auto-text Fast Note to claim activity + Read more ...

When the claim activity coding is complete, attach a Fast Note to record the assessment outcome.

Before finalising the activity:

  • Access the Fast Note workflow. Use Fast Note:
    • Finalised claims - Auto Text > Aged Care > Assessment > HOM Care Assessment – FIN to document the assessment outcome
    • Rejected claims – Auto Text > Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care MA Rejected OTHER to record the action taken
  • Link the Fast Note to the open RCA/HOM activity
  • Complete applicable Fast Note fields
  • Clearly document other relevant information, for example:
    • If the assessment was completed with a verbal declaration
    • If an update was made that differs from the information lodged (for example a care recipient failed to provide bank account balances on the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care but later provides a verbal balance)

8

Assessment result (AR) + Read more ...

When the Fast Note is complete:

  • Key ‘S’ to select the RCA/HOM activity from Activity List (AL) screen and navigate to the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Check results of assessment on AR:
    • the first ATH line and RCA Benefit line have a ‘Period End’ date equal to the date of event (DOV) of the assessment
    • any arrears or overpayments calculated are correct (based on DOV and date of receipt (DOR))

9

Follow up action + Read more ...

Follow up action may include:

  • In the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS), copy notes into the Notes tab
  • Update financial disclosure permission where required – see Aged care – consent and disclosure
  • Complete any progress of claim DOCs or scans
  • Contact the care recipient if the assessment results in a significant reduction to any income support payments (ISP), see Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK) for more information
  • If the assessment generates a social security debt needing investigation, clearly document the details about the potential debt for the investigating Service Officer
  • Check if further action is needed on the partner’s record, including and outstanding RCA/HOM activity
  • If the means assessment is completed verbally, issue an Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf form (SS313)
  • See Appointing nominees or Aged care request for a nominee
