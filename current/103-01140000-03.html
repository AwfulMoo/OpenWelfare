2

Customer debts - overpayment process + Read more ...

When NBGRT is notified of a potential overpayment, investigation will be undertaken and a determination of the debt will be completed.

Where required, the Service Support Officer or Team Leader will liaise with the Debt Management Branch Helpdesk and/or Income Management Helpdesk to determine if a debt is to be recovered.

Prior to the debt being raised, the NBGRT Service Officer will attempt to contact the customer to discuss the debt.

Even where a debt will not be recovered, an overpayment must be processed. Launch the IM expense overpayment workflow:

  • On the Expense Overpayment screen, select the appropriate Overpayment type from the dropdown menu:
    • Credit card
    • Money credited incorrectly

Further details will be required depending on the overpayment type selected:

  • Credit card: Credit card holder; Third Party Organisation CRN; Third Party organisation/Payee name; Date paid; Overpayment amount; Notes
  • Money credited incorrectly: Date paid; Overpayment amount; Notes
  • Select Finalise

Once an overpayment activity has been generated, a Debt Management/New DMIS Debt activity will be generated and display on Activity List (AL) screen.

Process the debt activity Guided Procedures - Raise a Debt workflow.

Go to Step 4.

3

Third Party Organisation (TPO) debts - overpayment processing + Read more ...

When the NBGRT is notified of a potential overpayment, an in-depth investigation will be undertaken and determination of the debt completed.

Where required the Service Support Officer or Team Leader will liaise with the Debt Management Branch Helpdesk and/or Deduction and Confirmation Branch -Income Management to determine the debt action required.

Prior to the debt being raised, the NBGRT Service Officer will attempt to contact the TPO to discuss the overpayment.

Even where a debt will not be recovered, an overpayment must be processed.

Recovery process has not commenced:

In some instances, a debt may be raised where no Income Management recovery processing has commenced from the customer's Income Management account. For example, where an error has occurred on the Administered Credit card account, but the customer's record has not been affected.

In situations like this where a TPO debt needs to be raised, the Debt Action script is used to create a debt activity. Go to Step 6.

Recovery process exists:

For TPO debts arising from an existing recovery process, go to the Recovery Summary screen and select Create debt.

A warning message may appear. Select Ok to continue.

Once the Create debt action has been finalised, a Debt Management/New DMIS Debt activity will be generated and display on the AL screen.

Process the debt activity to raise the debt via Debt Action script. Go to Step 4.

4

Debt Guided Procedure for Income Management Customer debts + Read more ...

When it has been determined that a Customer debt is to be raised or waived, and a debt activity has been generated, the Guided Procedures - Raise a Debt workflow must be launched and completed to finalise the debt raising process.

When a debt is to be raised, ask the customer how much they can afford to have deducted from their payments. For more information, see Procedures for arranging withholdings to recover debts.

Within Guided Procedures, select Raise a Debt to launch the workflow. Select the applicable Debt ID and complete the following mandatory Debt Details within the workflow:

  • Benefit Type: this is the IM measure the customer is on
  • Reason: select from one of the following:
    • IFO - IM payment fraudulently obtained
    • IME - IM error in payment
    • IMR - IM replacement payment
  • Section of Act: 123Income Management Debt
  • Date of Event: today’s date
  • Start Date: today’s date
  • Calculated Amount: enter the total debt amount
  • Position Responsible: Logon of person raising the debt
  • How was the debt calculated: Manually

Note: the Letter Details section will pre-populate based on the above inputs. If the text is not appropriate, NBGRT Service Officers can change the text but this must be checked and approved by a Senior Support Officer or Team Leader prior to workflow being started.

5

Debt Action script for Income Management debts + Read more ...

When it has been determined that a TPO debt is to be raised or waived, and a debt activity has been generated, the Debt Action script must be launched and completed to finalise the debt raising process.

The Debt Action script will automatically populate with details provided when the debt activity was created, and relevant pages in the script process must be updated as required.

Note: the Debt Action Script is only used to waive TPO debts. Customer debts are raised and waived through the Debt Guided Procedure.

If there is no existing debt activity, then a new one can be created from the Debt Action script:

  • Open Customer Record application (Mainframe)
  • Enter TPO CRN in CRN field
  • Go to Script Selector and select Debt Action script
  • Confirm TPO CRN in script window is correct - select Next
  • Select Action a new debt activity - select Next

Note: for TPO debts, a system generated TPO notice is not available. Issue the manual debt notice. Service Officers must use the approved Manual Debt letter provided to NBGRT.

6

Writing off debts or Waiver of debts + Read more ...

Writing off debts

Income Management debts may be written off as per current provisions.

Waiver of debts

Income Management debts may be waived as per current provisions.

For more information on all aspects of recovery, including the 5 methods of recovering debts and what to do in special circumstances (for example, when a customer is deceased), see Debt recovery for Centrelink.

Dealing with customer or Third Party Organisation (TPO) enquiries

Table 2: This table provides information about how to handle enquiries from customers and TPOs relating to Income Management debts.

Step

Action

1

Income Management (IM) debts + Read more ...

Income Management overpayments may be raised against a customer or a Third Party Organisation (TPO) for reasons which may include, but are not limited to:

  • system errors
  • administrative errors (for example, manual cheque not correctly debited from a customer's IM account)
  • incorrect credit to an Income Management account
  • payment made to an incorrect TPO
  • following an immediate re-credit of funds to a customer (for example, urgent recovery and funds returned were not sufficient to cover the re-credit)
  • breach of condition relating to crediting of account
  • customer owes money to a TPO

To respond to any Income Management debt enquiries from:

  • a TPO querying the reason for a debt repayment option or requesting a review of decision:
    • go to Step 2
    • go to Step 4
  • customers querying the reason for an Income Management debt, go to Step 5
  • customers requesting a review of the debt decision, see Request for an explanation, quality check or review (CLK). Procedure ends here
  • customers requesting information about repayment arrangements, go to Step 6

To raise an Income Management debt, or refer an overpayment for investigation, see Step 1 in Table 1.

2

Third Party Organisation debt enquiries + Read more ...

A TPO may contact to:

  • query why a debt has been raised
  • request the debt decision be reviewed, or
  • discuss repayment options

Where a representative from a TPO has called to discuss a TPO debt, the caller must be warm transferred to the National Business Gateway Recoveries Team (NBGRT). See the Resources page for a link to contact details.

Was the call transfer successful?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Attempted transfer of TPO was not successful + Read more ...

Advise the TPO they can contact the NBGRT directly. For contact details, see National Business Gateway Recoveries Team (NBGRT) in National Business Gateway.

Note: these contact details are for TPO's only and must not be given to customers.

If the TPO requests a callback:

  • On the TPO's record, send a Fast Note with details of the enquiry and the caller's contact details. Select Auto Text, use Generic > Centrepay > Recovery ACTDOC
  • If the TPO's record cannot be located, send an email to NBGRT with details of the TPO, their contact details and the caller's enquiry. For contact details, see National Business Gateway Recoveries Team (NBGRT) in National Business Gateway

Procedure ends here.

4

TPO queries Income Management debt + Read more ...

Explain the reason for the debt being raised and how funds can be repaid using electronic funds transfer or cheque.

If a TPO does not repay a debt, the agency has the following means for recovering monies owed:

  • garnishee
  • referral to an External Collection Agent (ECA)
  • legal proceedings (civil recovery)

See Recovering debts from Organisations.

After explaining the reason for the debt, is the TPO requesting a review of the debt decision?

  • Yes, see Initial contact for review request. Procedure ends here
  • No, record details of discussion on a DOC. Procedure ends here

5

Customer queries Income Management debt + Read more ...

If a customer queries why an Income Management debt has been raised:

  • Go to the Debt List (OPDL) screen and 'S'elect the relevant debt
  • The Modify/Assess Debt (OPMA) screen provides details of the start and end dates of the debt, the reason for the debt and Recovery Fee details
  • The General Information (OPGI) screen provides the above details, lists the Service Centre or debt management team responsible for raising the debt and any recovery arrangement in place
  • The Account Payable (OPAOD) screen is where detailed information about why the debt has been raised is included. This information is used for the Account Payable
  • The Document List (DL) screen will contain documents and all relevant information relating to the debt

For more information see General Centrelink debt recovery information.

If further information about the reason for the debt is required, warm transfer the customer to the NBGRT. For contact details, see National Business Gateway Recoveries Team (NBGRT) in National Business Gateway.

If a warm transfer was not successful, send a Fast Note with the details of the customer's enquiry. Ensure customer contact details are up to date. Select Auto Text, use Generic > Centrepay > Recovery ACTDOC.

After explaining the reason for the debt, is the customer requesting a review of debt decision?

  • Yes, see Initial contact for review request. Procedure ends here
  • No, record details of discussion on a DOC. Procedure ends here

6

Debt repayment options + Read more ...

Inform the customer of the debt repayment options available:

  • mail (cheque or money order)
  • telephone (Australia Post's POSTbillpay service)
  • in person at a Services Australia Service Centre or Australia Post Office (must present with bar-coded payment section on letter or payment card)
  • from online account
  • direct debit
  • BPAY, or
  • withholdings from Centrelink payments

Advise customers that information about debt repayment is available on the Services Australia website. The Resources page contains a link to the website.

If the customer wishes to arrange repayments or withholdings to repay the debt, see Procedures for arranging withholdings to recover debts.

Procedure ends here.
