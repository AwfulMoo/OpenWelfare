This page contains information to assist in determining whether payments can be redirected from parent/s to an under 18 Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY customer.

On this page:

Customer identity check + Read more ...

All Service Officers must complete a customer identity check when requested to change payment destinations.

Follow normal Identity Confirmation procedures and also ask the customer to state their current payment destination details and the amount of their last payment to that account.

Check customer's independent status + Read more ...

Payment destination details to be updated see Changing payment destination.

4

Assessment of independence + Read more ...

Is the customer in the process of being assessed for the Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH) category of independence?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, see Step 1 of the Assess if customer meets the exemption guidelines table

5

Customer in the process of being assessed for UTLAH + Read more

The assessment or recommendation of whether the YA or ABSTUDY should be directed to the young person is often made by the social worker but it can be made by the Service Officer.

If in any doubt, a Service Officer should consult with a social worker. However the YA or ABSTUDY should not continue to be paid to the parent if the Service Officer is satisfied that the payment is not being used for the customer's benefit. The Service Officer does not have to wait for the determination of UTLAH to make this decision.

Note: it is not acceptable to suspend or cancel a payment while making a decision about UTLAH or payment determination. A payment should only be suspended/cancelled if the Service Officer is satisfied that the person is not qualified for YA or ABSTUDY or the payment isn't payable.

Make a decision about where the payment will be directed and advise customer of outcome.

Do any necessary updates via the Change Payment Destination workflow.

Procedure ends here.

Assess if customer meets the exemption guidelines

Table 2: This table describes details of the process used to make a decision if a YA customer's or an ABSTUDY customer's payment could be paid directly to the customer or to the parent.

Step

Action

1

Payments paid directly to customer or parent + Read more ...

Payment can be paid directly to the dependent 16 or 17 year old customer, if the:

  • young person is not benefiting from the allowance because the parent(s) are using it for other purposes, or
  • parents choose to have payments paid to the young person, and authorises the change:
    • in writing for YA or ABSTUDY, or
    • verbally for ABSTUDY only

For YA only:

  • young person identifies as an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander who has been ceremonially admitted as an adult member of their community, or
  • parents leave Australia temporarily and the young person cannot access YA payments

For ABSTUDY only

Payments are paid directly to the dependent 16 or 17 year old if they are:

  • studying at a secondary non-school, tertiary or Masters and Doctorate level, or
  • undertaking an Australian Apprenticeship

Payments must be made to the ABSTUDY customer in these cases as they are considered to be the 'payee'. They have the option of appointing a Payment Nominee.

Note: Payments must be made to the parent/guardian for any ABSTUDY student who is of dependent status and under 16 years of age and a secondary school student. School Term Allowance payments must be made to the Applicant.

In general, an ABSTUDY student or Australian Apprentice who are in State Care receiving Living Allowance receive their allowances direct. However, the authority may request that payment be made direct to the care giver or a person nominated by the student or Australian Apprentice, if:

  • the relevant authority or an organisation approved by that authority believes that payment of allowances direct to younger students or Australian Apprentices could jeopardise the student carer or Australian Apprentice carer relationship, or
  • it is determined that the student or Australian Apprentice is unable to manage their own finances, for example a student in a special school

Note: some students who are boarding may have their payments directed to a Boarding School or Hostel that is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement. Bank details still need to be recorded for the carer or student on the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen so that residue/supplementary payments are issued correctly.

Does the customer meet the exemption guidelines for dependent YA or ABSTUDY customers under 18 having payments going to parents?

  • Yes, and the customer is receiving:
    • YA, go to Step 3
    • ABSTUDY, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Dependent customer does not meet exemption guidelines + Read more

Advise customer that they do not meet guidelines and that their payments will continue to be directed to their parent(s) until such time as they:

  • meet one of the criteria within the guidelines,
  • turn 18, or
  • become independent

Record details of the decision on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

3

YA customer meets exemption guidelines + Read more

Complete the following tasks:

  • take a statement of the customer's reasons for wanting to stop payments going to parent(s)
  • make a decision about where the payment will be directed and advise customer of the outcome
  • if the customer is current, update the payment destination via the Change Payment Destination workflow
  • if the customer has lodged a new claim, update the payment destination via the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen
  • depending on customer's reason to stop payments going to parents, a referral to a social worker may need to be considered
  • record decision on DOC

Procedure ends here.

4

ABSTUDY customer meets exemption guidelines + Read more ...

Complete the following tasks:

  • confirm the customer's reason for wanting to stop payments going to the parent(s)
  • make a decision about where the payment will be directed and advise the customer of the outcome
  • for current customers, update new payment destination details via the Change Payment Destination workflow
  • for new claims, update the payment destination details via the PAS screen
  • Correct the ABSTUDY Payee/Debtor Details (EYPY) screen
    • Update the End Date using the last Entitlement Period End Date
    • Insert a new Start Date using the current Entitlement Period End Date
  • record decision on DOC
