Pre or post judgment or settlement – evidence and delegations to amend notices

Table 1: this table outlines the evidence and delegations required to amend a notice of past benefits (NOPB) or notice of charge (NOCH) at either pre or post settlement stage.

Reason for amendment

Delegation and evidence required

There is an identified processing error and services or costs have been incorrectly included

Service Delivery APS1 and all levels above can approve to correct the error.

No evidence is required.

Service has already been paid by the compensation payer

Service Delivery APS5 or above can approve to remove the service with:

  • a copy of invoice and receipt that was paid by the compensation payer

Use the Escalation to Programme Management template to send to Team Leader.

A care cost amount (in full or part) does not relate to the compensable injury or illness

Program Management APS5 or above can approve to review the costs with:

  • Letter from treating doctor that the services specifying:
    • some or all of the care costs do not relate to the compensable injury or illness, or
    • the increase in the level of care was not related to the compensable injury or illness

Use the Escalation to Programme Management template to send to Team Leader.

Pre judgment or settlement – evidence and delegations to amend notices

Table 2: this table outlines the evidence and delegations required to amend a notice of past benefits (NOPB) at pre judgment or settlement stage.

Reason for amendment

Delegation and evidence required

Valid NOPB all services taken

On a case by case basis, Service Delivery APS5 or above can approve to process:

  • a recompleted statement by claimant with
  • written evidence from the compensation payer or claimant’s solicitor that the claim cannot be finalised without an accurate notice and must be:
    • endorsed by a judge and the date has been set or
    • endorsed by the Public Trustee

Use the Escalation to Programme Management template to send to the Team Leader.

Valid verified NOPB

Consider a review if:

  • there are processing errors, Service Delivery APS1 can approve
  • there is an invoice/receipt from compensation payer that they have paid the account, Service Delivery APS 3 can approve
  • care costs do not relate, escalate to Programme Management with the letter from the treating doctor, use the Escalation to Programme Management template to send to the Team Leader

Otherwise:

  • amend at expiry of notice or post settlement by processing new statement by claimant

Expired NOPB

Service Delivery APS 3 can approve to process a statement to remove previously verified services with a clear statement by the claimant.

If unsure, consult a Programme Support Officer or Team Leader.

Note: this is not an amended NOPB. It is completed in a statement by claimant processing.

Post judgment or settlement – evidence and delegations to amend notices

Table 3: this table outlines the evidence and delegations required to amend a notice of past benefits (NOPB) or notice of charge (NOCH) at post judgment or settlement stage.

Reason for amendment

Delegation and evidence required

Within 2 years of a judgment or settlement being fixed, a request to reduce NOPB or NOCH due to apportionment of liability to the injured person

Service Delivery APS3 can approve to reduce the NOCH by the percentage of apportionment applied where written evidence has been supplied by the compensation payer.

A change only in the date of injury or illness (DOI) at date of settlement (DOS)
  • Consider if a new case is needed, see General information for Medicare Compensation Recovery
  • If retaining the existing case and the only change is the DOI advised on the notice of judgement or settlement (NOJ/NOS) is later than the previous registered date, Service Delivery APS3 can process to remove any services prior to the new date of injury

NOCH all services taken
  • The Health and Other Services (Compensation) Act 1995 (the HOSC Act) provides for a review within 2 years of DOS
  • Service Delivery APS1 and above can process a valid statement by claimant, see Table 2 on the Process page

Verified NOCH – valid at DOS
  • No provision in the HOSC Act to review a notice resulting from a verified statement, however consideration for review will be given within 2 years of DOS
  • Injured person/claimant must apply in writing and evidence may be required

Possible scenarios

Previously verified services are clearly unrelated to the injury or illness:

  • Service Delivery APS5 can approve to process a recompleted statement by claimant

Previously verified services that might relate to the injury or illness:

  • Service Delivery APS5 can approve to process:
    • a recompleted statement by claimant
    • a statutory declaration by the claimant that the services do not relate

There has been a change in the nature of the injury or illness at DOS:

  • Service Delivery APS5 can approve to process:
    • a recompleted statement by claimant
    • a statutory declaration by the claimant to explain the change in injury/illness and that the services do not relate
    • supporting valid NOJS

Use the Escalation to Programme Management template to send to the Team Leader.

If unsure, escalate to Programme Management.

Verified NOPB – expired at DOS
  • No provision in the HOSC Act to review a notice resulting from a verified statement, however consideration for review will be given within 2 years of DOS
  • Injured person/claimant must apply in writing and evidence may be required

Possible scenarios

Previously verified services that are clearly unrelated to the injury or illness:

  • Service Delivery APS3 can:
    • process a statement by claimant requesting to remove previously verified services
    • consult a Programme Support Officer or Team Leader if unsure

Previously verified services that might relate to the injury or illness:

  • Service Delivery APS5 can approve to process:
    • a recompleted statement by claimant with
    • a statutory declaration by the claimant that the services do not relate
    • use the Escalation to Programme Management template to send to the Team Leader
    • if unsure, escalate to Programme Management

There has been a change in the nature of the injury or illness at DOS:

  • Service Delivery APS5 can approve to process:
    • a recompleted statement by claimant with
    • supporting valid NOJS
    • use the Escalation to Programme Management template to send to the Team Leader
    • if unsure, escalate to Programme Management

Determine the validity of statement by claimant

Table 4: this table helps Service Officers to determine the validity of statement by claimant to amend a valid notice of past benefits (NOPB) - all services taken, provided the required evidence has been supplied in writing by the injured person (if applicable).

Statement by claimant is….

And there are….

If substantially compliant

Returned within 3 months from the date of issue of the Medicare history statement (MHS)

Statement by claimant is valid to amend the NOPB - all services taken

Returned within 3 to 6 months from the date of issue of the MHS

no additional eligible benefits consistent with compensable injury or illness since the date of issue of MHS

Statement by claimant is valid to amend the NOPB - all services taken

Returned within 3 to 6 months from the date of issue of the MHS

additional eligible benefits consistent with compensable injury or illness since the date of issue of MHS

Statement by claimant is not valid to amend the NOPB - all services taken, unless upcoming trial date or sanctioning conference is set prior to expiry of notice

Returned more than 6 months after the date of issue of the MHS

no additional eligible benefits claimed since the date of issue of MHS

Statement by claimant is valid to amend the NOPB - all services taken

Returned more than 6 months after the date of issue of the MHS

additional eligible benefits claimed since the date of issue of MHS

Statement by claimant is not valid to amend the NOPB - all services taken
