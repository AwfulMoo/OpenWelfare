MCA EDI105 NPHEDI RPT

Table 1: this table describes how to action the MCA EDI105 NPHEDI RPT

Step

Action

1

Open email + Read more ...

Open the Provider Services Communication positional inbox.

Service Officers should open an email from the state they are in.

Are there any attachments to the email?

  • Yes,
    • Open the attachment (excel spreadsheet)
    • Go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here

2

Review error message + Read more ...

Review the error message to determine the cause for the failure. Some examples may include:

  • TRANSMISSION ****** REJECTED ****** TRANSMISSION PATIENT COUNT = 133
    • this means that the number of patients is over the maximum of 50 allowed, therefore the whole claim is rejected
  • CLAIM ALREADY PROCESSED. FUND ID = ABC12345 CLAIM ID = 12345
    • this means the claim has already been paid

The Error Message will be listed against each record in the report and will either be at:

  • transmission level only
  • voucher level only, or
  • at both transmission level and voucher level

Note: not all Error Message Information is visible in each line. The Service Officer needs to scroll across the page to view all error message information.

Is the error message enough information to determine the cause for the failure?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Insufficient information + Read more ...

If the error message is not enough information to determine the cause of the failure, call the private health insurer (PHI) to discuss. For PHI contact details see Simplified Billing claims in Medicare.

Ask them for more information such as Medicare card number, item numbers, provider numbers in order to key a dummy claim. This should identify the exact cause of the failure. The PHI need to fix and retransmit the claim. Go to Step 5.

4

Sufficient information + Read more ...

If the error message is enough information to determine the cause for failure, contact the private health insurer (PHI) to advise the details of the error. Request that the claim be retransmitted. For PHI contact details see Simplified Billing claims in Medicare.

5

Contacting private health insurers + Read more ...

Attempt to contact the PHI three times and document the following information in the EDI105 report email and save:

  • Private health insurers staff name and phone number
  • Document the details of the error
  • Time and date of 3 attempted phone calls
  • Service Officer initials

If the attempt to contact the PHI was successful and the Service Officer advised the PHI of the failed transmission and to re-transmit their claim, procedure ends here.

6

Unsuccessful contact + Read more ...

After three unsuccessful attempts to contact the PHI, complete the EDI105 email template with the following information:

  • Minor ID
  • Claim ID
  • DOL
  • Error code and explanation
  • Operator Number
  • Report Date

The EDI105 email template and secure email list is located in Outlook folder:

Provider Services Communication Mailbox > Folder> Simplified Billing EDI105 Report folder > Stationery and secure email list

Send the email to the PHI’s nominated secure email/s (BCC: DCM & PSC mailboxes) and mark as ‘OFFICIAL’.

7

Private health insurer contacts + Read more ...

If the PHI calls to discuss EDI 105 failure notification, the telephony Service Officer will email the MPS Service Officer who sent the email.

The email should:

  • ask that they make contact with the private health insurer
  • the contact phone number, and
  • person who called

The private health insurer should be advised that the operator that sent the email will contact them regarding their enquiry.

8

Send and file email + Read more ...

Send a copy of the email to the PSC mailbox and file in the EDI folder for the relevant month.

MCA NSB301 SIMPBILL DLY PEND RPT

Table 2: this table describes how to action the MCA NSB301 SIMPBILL DLY PEND RPT.

Step

Action

1

Open the report + Read more ...

Open the report in RMDS

  • Key RMDS
  • pXXXXX
  • Password and press [Enter]
  • Select option 3 and key report name “MCA NSB301 SIMPBILL DLY PEND RPT” and press [Enter]
  • Key [F8] to scroll to nominated dates:
    • Usually the current date will be at the end of the page
    • The latest report date must be used to view the most up to date status of these claims
  • Select 1 in the Action field for the nominated date and press [Enter]

2

View claims + Read more ...

View claims and ensure no claims are out of lag – after the 7 day lag time.

Manual claims must be processed by the 7th day to meet the 10 day lag time. In some cases the claim may appear on the report but to ensure the claim has been finalised before sending any notification to their Team Leader the operator must view the claim using:

  • FCEI, Minor ID, Claim ID, DOL

If the claim appears as PAID-LD or PAID-SD no further action is required.

3

Out of lag claims + Read more ...

View the claim from the above report MCA NSB301 SIMPBILL DLY PEND RPT and determine which claim is out of lag.

Manually keyed claims refer to OPER-DATE which indicates the date the operator keyed the manual claim and DOL is the date used to key the original date the claim was received:

  • Key FCEI, Minor ID, Claim ID, DOL=DDMMYY
  • View the status of the claim by referring to the bottom left hand side which indicates D/E keyed by the operator
  • If the claim appears in PEND status this must be escalated
    • the operator’s p number and source is shown in the status for the purpose of escalation

4

Email Team Leader + Read more ...

Service Officers need to email their Team Leader with the following details:

  • Minor ID
  • Claim ID, and
  • DOL

The Team Leader will send this onto the operator’s Team Leader.

5

Email template + Read more ...

The Team Leader must instruct the operator’s Team Leader to complete or delete the claim.

The operator may need to ensure that they are using the same source office code and seek advice from their Team Leader/Subject Matter Expert if they are having difficulty completing or deleting the claim.

After trying all of the above, if the operator is unsuccessful in completing or deleting the claim they must raise a file fix request. The operator needs to complete a file fix template (The template must include a screen shot of each stage up to and including the error) and send to Digital Channel Management (DCM) using the email template below:

DCM,

I would like to request a file fix to the below claim as it is abending when attempting to process/close/delete.

Please find attached completed file fix template and claim details below.

Minor ID:

Claim ID:

DOL:

Note: the file fix template is available on the Resources page.

MCA NSB 302 SB LDA AGE ANALYSIS RPT

Table 3: this table describes how to action the MCA NSB302 LDA AGE ANALYSIS RPT.

Step

Action

1

Open the report + Read more ...

Open the report in RMDS

  • RMDS
  • Key pXXXXX
  • Key password and press [Enter]
  • Key option 3 and key the report name in the report name field “MCA NSB 302 SB LDA AGE ANALYSIS RPT” and press [Enter]
  • Key in ‘1’ in the ACTION field to nominate the date and press [Enter]
    • Use [F8] to scroll to nominated date usually the current date will be at the end of the page. The latest report date must be used to view latest status of these claims

2

Report opened LDA claims + Read more ...

Report the LDA claims left opened by providing the following details:

  • Minor ID
  • Claim ID
  • DOL

Note: Latter Day Adjustments must be finalised at the end of each day unless the operator is waiting on further information to complete the LDA.

3

Review LDA claim + Read more ...

Review LDA claim on mainframe using FCEI, Minor ID and Claim ID and DOL. These must be viewed before reporting these to the operator to ensure these are still left opened and not finalised.

Note: in some cases the claim may appear on the report but in the meantime the claim may have been finalised. The 302 report is only loaded on RMDS once a day and not updated to reflect any changes which have occurred throughout the day.

4

View claim history + Read more ...

Type S to select in the action (A) field. This will provide information about the latest changes made to the claim by an operator. Keep in mind that where the claim is open and over keyed the current operator’s number making the changes will be recorded.

Note: do not open or over key the claim as this will replace the operator number’s details.

5

302 report + Read more ...

The Service Officer actioning the 302 report must email their Team Leader with the identified operator’s details of the adjustment. The Team Leader must escalate the details to the identified operator’s Team Leader by email with the following details:

Date sent:

To:

Subject: RMDS 302 – Simplified Billing LDA DAILY AGE REPORT [SEC=OFFICIAL]

Hi,

The below adjustments are appearing in the RMDS Simplified Billing LDA DAILY AGE REPORT 302, can you please have your staff members finalise/ delete?

Attach: [SCREEN SHOT of Medicare Mainframe]

The receiving Team Leader must advise their staff member to take the necessary steps to complete or delete the LDA.

Was the operator successful in completing the LDA?

  • Yes, no further action is required
    • Note: the operator may need to ensure that they are using the same source office code and seek advice from their Team Leader/Subject Matter Expert if they are having difficulty completing the adjustment.
  • No, complete a file fix template (the template must include a screen shot of each stage of the error) email Digital Channel Management and provide the following details:
    • Minor ID
    • Claim ID
    • DOL

Note: the file fix template is located on the Resources page.
