Australian Birth Certificate (BC) - 70 points or commencement of identity (COI) document (only 1 can be accepted)

Information about the Australian birth certificate used as a form of POI, how to code them, and examples of documents.

Description

Coding

Document examples

Australian Birth Certificate, in customer’s current or former name.

  • BCs from Papua New Guinea before 16 September 1975 are acceptable
  • BC from Cocos/Keeling Island post 1977
  • BC from Christmas Island post 1993
  • See the Resources page for links to the Australian State and Territory Registries of Births, Deaths and Marriages
  • Note: a Request for Verification (SS257) returned from the Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) should be coded as a Birth Certificate
  • Document Type: field
    • Australian Birth Certificate (BC)
    • Registration/Serial Number: field
    • Record the birth registration or entry number (do not confuse this with the serial, application or certificate number) and the four digit year of registration. If year of registration doesn't appear use year of birth, i.e. 123/1974
    • If the year of registration appears within the registration number it should be coded as yyyy, i.e. 123/74 should be coded as 123/1974
    • If the registration or entry number doesn't appear, code the place of registration and serial, application or certificate number and year of birth, e.g. Perth 123/01/1921x
    • If the serial number shows a letter at the end, e.g. N or C (which indicates a change of name), the coding of this letter is optional. However, where the certificate has details recorded on both sides, staff must ensure that other names listed are coded on the Customer Person Details (CPD) screen, see Aliases and other names (legal, preferred, sex and gender diverse customers, cultural considerations, etc.). Additional information regarding change of name can be found in the Resources page, in particular FAQs
  • Issued/Expiry Date: field
    • Issue date
    • If no issue date, code date of birth
  • State Issued: field
    • State or Territory of Registry
    • External Territories (EXT) is used for Australian External Territories - Papua New Guinea (pre September 1975 only), Cocos/Keeling Island (post 1977) and Christmas Island (post 1993)
  • Country Issued: field
    • Australia
  • Arrival Date: field
    • Not applicable

Not accepted

  • Birth record issued by hospital
  • Confirmation of Aboriginality or Torres Strait Islander Descent
  • Abridged Adoption
  • Maternity Allowance
  • Acknowledgement letter from Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM)
  • Notification of birth registration from Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM)
  • Certificates issued by organisations other than (BDM)
  • Certificate of Registration of birth of a child
  • Pre Adoption Birth Certificate issued under Adoption of Children Act
  • Overseas Birth Certificates. See POI documents - O, Other overseas Documents (OD)
  • Birth Certificate - Norfolk Island
  • Birth Certificate – Cocos/Keeling Island pre 1977
  • Birth Certificate – Christmas Island pre 1993
  • Certificates of Aboriginal Population Records (APRs)

Accepted

  • Birth Certificates - Australian Capital Territory (ACT)
  • Birth Certificates - New South Wales (NSW)
  • Birth Certificates - Northern Territory (NT)
  • Birth Certificate - Queensland (QLD)
  • Birth Certificate - South Australia (SA)
  • Certified Copy of Registration of Birth - SA
  • Extract - SA
  • Extract on Search - Tasmania (TAS)
  • Extract From Record - TAS
  • Birth Certificates - Victoria (VIC)
  • Birth Certificates - Western Australia (WA)
  • Extract From Birth Entry - WA
  • Record of Registry of Birth - WA
  • Letter from Aboriginal Affairs - Indigenous Australian
  • Aboriginals Preservation Act - Indigenous Australian
  • Birth Certificate - Christmas Island (post 1993)
  • Birth Certificate - Cocos/Keeling Islands (post 1977)
  • Birth Certificate – Papua New Guinea (pre-September 1975 only)

Australian Citizen Certificate (AC) - 70 points or commencement of identity (COI) document (only 1 can be accepted)

Information about the Australian citizenship certificate used as a form of POI, coding them, and examples of documents.

Description

Coding

Document examples

Australian Citizenship or Citizenship by Descent Certificate in claimant's current or former name, or verification directly from the Department of Home Affairs via the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen or Residence Verification script.

  • Many children of new citizens are included on the reverse of the parent's certificate. If applicable, Service Officers should use the parent's Serial/Registration number for each child
  • Citizenship information populated on the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen by the Department of Home Affairs datalink can be coded as 'AC', if it will not result in a points value (i.e. only for Commencement of Identity)
  • Document Type: field
    • Australian Citizenship Certificate (AC)
  • Registration/Serial Number: field
    • Register number, generally at bottom of front page, or Certificate number, on top of back page. If no number, code place of issue
    • For 'by descent' Certificates code, extract from Register of Births number, e.g. 10, number in Register e.g. 999 and place of Register e.g. London, i.e. 10/999 - London
    • If confirmed by Department of Home Affairs code DIAC confirmed'
  • Issued/Expiry Date: field
    • The date of grant of the certificate or other date advised by the Department of Home Affairs (for Department of Home Affairs confirmed customers). Where the certificate has a grant date and an oath date, record the earliest date
  • State Issued: field
    • Optional
  • Country Issued: field
    • Australia
  • Arrival Date: field
    • Not Applicable

Not accepted

  • Information from the Department of Home Affairs if Proof of Arrival has already been received, i.e. Australian Visa (VI)
  • Australian Citizenship Extract
  • Local Council Certificates
  • Local Member of Parliament Certificates
  • Certificate of Resident Status

Accepted

  • Australian Citizenship Certificates
  • Australian Citizenship Record Extract
  • Certificate of Naturalisation of an Australian Citizen
  • Citizenship by Descent
  • Evidence of Australian Citizenship
  • Evidentiary Certificate - Commonwealth
  • Extract from Register of Citizenship by Descent
  • Declaratory Certificates of Citizenship
  • Declaration of Acquisition of British Nationality
  • Christmas Island

Australian Defence Force (ADF) Identity Card (DF) - 40 points

Information about the Australian defence force identity card used as a form of POI, how to code them, and examples of documents.

Description

Coding

Document examples

Australian Defence Force (ADF) Identity Card

  • ADF cards are issued to full time and reserve members of the ADF
  • Family Identity Cards are issued to partner, spouse or children over 16
  • Some cards will have a strip 'Under 18' if the holder was under 18 when the card was issued
  • This strip will not be printed if the holder is over 18 when the card was issued. Its presence or absence does not matter for Centrelink POI purposes
  • Document Type: field
    • Australian Defence Force (ADF) Identity Card (DF)
  • Registration/Serial Number: field
    • Yes
  • Issued/Expiry Date: field
    • Expiry date (if one appears on the card). Note: if the card has an expiry date and this date has passed, the card is not acceptable
  • State Issued: field
    • Optional
  • Country Issued: field
    • Australia
  • Arrival Date: field
    • Not applicable

Not Accepted

  • White and Red cards - VIP, Visitor
    • these do not have names or photos on them
  • White and Green cards - PI (Positively identified) day pass
    • these do not have names or photos on them
  • Red series - Superseded
    • were issued to family members
  • Yellow series - Contractors, industry partners
  • Green series - Foreign military serving with ADF
  • Blue series - Public servants working on bases

Note: these, and some other cards that may be seen, do not have the words 'Australian Defence Force Identity Card' on them. If the card has 'Australian Defence Force Identity Card' on the front or back it would be 'Accepted' POI for Centrelink purposes.

Accepted

  • Current member
    • Australian Defence Force (ADF) Identity Cards - Royal Australian Navy, Australian Army, Royal Australian Air Force
    • Purple series: Current member - Royal Australian Navy, Australian Army, Royal Australian Air Force
    • Orange series - Trainees
  • Family member
    • Dependant Identity Cards - Royal Australian Navy, Australian Army, Royal Australian Air Force
    • Red series: Base Access Only - Family
    • Mauve series: National Family ID - Army, Navy, Air Force
  • Retired member
    • Retired Member Identity Cards - Royal Australian Navy, Australian Army, Royal Australian Air Force
    • Grey series: Retired member - Royal Australian Navy, Australian Army, Royal Australian Air Force

Australian Visas (VI) - 70 points or commencement of identity (COI) document (only 1 can be accepted)

Information about Australian visas used as a form of POI, how to code them, and examples of documents.

Description

Coding

Document examples

Valid Australian Visa (VI) held by the customer. May have been used for entry into Australia, or issued since their arrival.

  • VI may be coded in conjunction with a Certificate of Identity (CI) but not with a Document of Identity - Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) (DI)
  • Details provided via the Residence Verification script and visa information populated on the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen by the Department of Home Affairs datalink can be coded as VI, if it will not result in a points value (i.e. Only for Commencement of Identity (COI)). See Chapter 9 of the Guide to Social Security Law for more detail on reading Visas
  • Document Type: field
    • Australian Visa (VI)
  • Registration/Serial Number: field
    • Visa number generally starts with a letter, e.g. V999<1234567X. Code as 9991234567X
    • If a (wet) stamped visa is provided, record arrival location, e.g. Sydney, Fremantle
    • If confirmed by the Department of Home Affairs (Channel type is IMM on RSLEG or RSIMME screen) - record visa number if available or record 'DIAC Confirmed'
    • If confirmed by Centrelink International Services (CIS), record number advised in DOC. For further information, see Commencement of Identity
  • Issued/Expiry Date: field
    • Issue date. The date granted is in text in the middle of the visa
  • State Issued: field
    • National
  • Country Issued: field
    • Australia, even when issued Overseas
  • Arrival Date: field
    • Use date of grant from visa, date stamp in Passport or date check done by CIS or the Department of Home Affairs

Not accepted

  • Letter from the Department of Home Affairs advising grant of visa
  • Expired visas
  • Visa information populated on RSLEG and RSIMME screens by the Department of Home Affairs datalink
  • Information from the Department of Home Affairs if Proof of Arrival has already been received, e.g. Australian Citizenship Certificate
  • Details provided via the Residence Verification script
  • Pacific - Australia Card

Accepted

  • Letter Of Authority for Travel to Australia
  • Visas

Coding

Document examples

Mail or counter issued statement for current savings, cheque or loan account in the claimant's name, with the same address as the claim, not more than 12 months old. Financial institution details must be included.

  • A debit card statement is acceptable, as it is generally tied to a savings account

      - Commonwealth bank - 76206
      - NAB - 082996
      - Westpac - 032898
    • To code the account number for a debit card, truncate the account number at the ninth digit
  • Issued/Expiry Date: field

    • Optional

    • Australia

    • Not Applicable
