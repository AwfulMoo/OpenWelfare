Split case customers + Read more ...

If a customer is a member of a couple and would like to receive their Centrelink payment to an overseas bank account (or by cheque), and their partner is also on a Centrelink payment and remaining in Australia, further discussion with the customer and partner is needed. This includes concession card impacts for the customer remaining in Australia.

It is not possible to have one member of a couple paid every 4 weeks and the other paid every 2 weeks.

The customer and their partner will need to decide if they are both to be paid:

  • every 4 weeks. This means that:
    • Customer and partner records will need to be transferred to Environment I
    • Customer may be able to be paid into an overseas bank account, or by cheque, and their partner will continue to be paid to their Australian bank account every 4 weeks
    • The customer will become a CIS customer. Update the Office Code (OC) screen to WTW. The partner's OC will remain unchanged
    • The customer in Australia needs to be aware they will not be automatically issued with a concession card when it expires. The customer must contact a Service Centre to get an interim card
  • every 2 weeks. This means that:
    • Customer and partner records will remain in their local environment
    • Customer (or partner) cannot be paid into an overseas bank account or by cheque to an overseas address. Both will continue to be paid every 2 weeks to their Australian bank account
    • The customer will become a CIS customer. Update the Office Code (OC) screen to WTW. The partner's OC will remain unchanged

2

Cheque payment method + Read more ...

Cheques are not the preferred option as:

  • they are sent by post, which causes a delay in the customer receiving their payment and may even be lost
  • stale cheque timeframes exist for cashing cheques

The customer's home address must also be updated to the destination address for cheque payments to be delivered.

If the customer wants and is permitted to receive payment by cheque, a direct deposit exemption must be recorded.

Currencies used for International cheque payments.

Customers in the following countries can only be paid by direct deposit. They cannot be paid by cheque: Austria, Belgium, Denmark, Finland, Ireland, Kenya, Malta, Netherlands, New Zealand, South Africa and Tunisia.

Customers in the following countries cannot be paid by cheque or direct deposit: Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Russia, Sudan and Syria. The customer can choose to:

  • have their payments deposited into their or their payment nominee's Australian bank account, or
  • have payments made by cheque, sent to their payment nominee's address in another country, or
  • be paid by direct deposit into their or their payment nominee's account in another country

Go to Step 7

3

Direct deposit method + Read more ...

The benefits of overseas direct deposits:

  • quickest method for the customer to receive their payment
  • most secure delivery as payments can be traced from the agency to the customer's bank
  • cost effective for the customer

There are 3 direct deposit methods:

  • Automated Clearing House (ACH) is an electronic funds-transfer system that facilitates transactions between Citibank and the customer's bank
  • Legacy ACH is a funds transfer system between an account held by Services Australia and the customer's bank in the country of payment
    A customer in an ACH country or Legacy ACH country must have payment into an Australian or overseas bank account, unless an exemption reason exists. Make sure the account is held in the name of the customer or their payment nominee only and is recorded to match the details on the bank account
    Note: the customer may choose to receive payment by cheque to allow them time to open an account after initial departure. (Except for customers in Austria, Belgium, Denmark, Finland, Ireland, Kenya, Malta, Netherlands, New Zealand, South Africa and Tunisia)
  • Worldlink Wire (WWR) are electronic fund transfers sent through a correspondent bank or intermediary bank. It is processed manually by the receiving bank rather than sent automatically to the customer's bank account like the ACH payment method
    A customer in a WWR country may choose to have their payment deposited into an Australian or overseas bank account, or made by cheque to their home address outside Australia

If paying by overseas direct deposit, check the country on OBAD to see the direct deposit payment method and currency used.

If the country is not on OBAD, contact the CIS VSSO hub and request that country banking requirements are added to enable the recording of overseas bank details for that country, based on the:

  • international Bank Account Details (AUS178) form, or
  • bank statement the customer has given on the PAPO screen, or
  • verbal bank details (the customer must still provide written evidence of their overseas bank details)

International bank details can be accepted if the customer has:

  • completed and returned the AUS178 form relevant for their country, or
  • provided a bank statement or document which shows their bank details, including bank account number or International Bank Account Number (IBAN), and the name(s) of the account holder(s), with a request for payment to be made to that account, or
  • provided their bank details verbally. The customer must also give documents (e.g. an official bank statement) of their new payment details as soon as possible to confirm the details given verbally. Make sure that:
    • the customer is aware of the currency paid and can confirm their bank account can accept payments
    • the IBAN is validated by checking it in the IBAN Validation Tool

If the customer is paid in a currency other than their local currency (such as USD or EUR), and intermediary details have not been given, check if the intermediary details are in OBAD. If the details do not appear in OBAD, contact the CIS virtual SSO (VSSO) Hub and request that intermediary details are provided by International Program. International Program can get intermediary bank details from the RBA.

Cheque payments + Read more ...

Cheques cannot be issued to addresses in:

  • Australia, or
  • Austria, Belgium, Cuba, Denmark, Finland, Iran, Ireland, Kenya, Malta, Netherlands, New Zealand, North Korea, Russia, South Africa, Sudan, Syria or Tunisia

Check the country’s banking requirement in OBAD + Read more ...

Before coding overseas bank account details on PAPO, the country’s banking requirements must be referenced in OBAD.

Find the country's banking requirements in OBAD. This must be done to make sure:

  • all information needed by that country/bank to update the overseas bank account details has been given by the customer
  • to help with coding overseas bank account details in the correct format, and
  • to make other essential non-PAPO updates for that country when needed (for example, customer's home address has to be recorded in a specific format for payments made to the US)

Country is not in OBAD:

If the country’s banking requirements do not appear in OBAD:

Intermediary bank details have not been provided:

Coding PAPO:

Refer to #OBAD Fields Explained for detailed explanations about the fields used on PAPO.

Code all fields on the PAPO screen with the details needed for the applicable country:

  • Method of payment: one of the following:
    • DIR for an ACH country
    • LWR for a Legacy ACH country
    • WWR for a WWR country
  • FI country: the country in which the bank account is held. For example, AT for Austria, GB for United Kingdom, DE for Germany, HR for Croatia
    Note: these are the international country codes. They may differ from the country codes used by Centrelink
  • FI identifier: if the country needs an FI identifier to be recorded, code the bank/branch sort code according to the requirements for that country. Otherwise, leave blank
  • FI SWIFT/BIC: if the country needs a SWIFT/BIC code to be recorded, code the 8 or 11 character SWIFT/BIC code. Otherwise, leave blank
  • Account Number: enter the account number or IBAN according to the requirement for that country. Check the country's banking requirements in OBAD. If the account number is an IBAN, the IBAN must be validated by entering it into the IBAN Validation Tool
  • Account Type: key for USA customers only
  • Account Title: key the account title (account holder name) exactly as written on any official bank documents given by the customer. Make sure the account is held in the name of the customer or their payment nominee only
  • IBAN: is a display only field. If the IBAN is not recorded as the primary Account number for countries, the IBAN is to be recorded here. By recording the IBAN in this field, if a country changes their banking requirement from a traditional account number and mandates the use of an IBAN, it can be quickly moved from this field to the Account Number: field. No customer contact or follow up is needed. If the IBAN is needed to be recorded in the Account Number: field, the IBAN field is left blank
  • Phone number: leave blank
  • PayDtl: code with any non-account country specific information. For example, payments to Bangladesh need the nationality of the customer, Republic of Korea need the customer's phone number to be provided
  • Intermediary Details section, FI Country: and either FI Identifier or FI SWIFT/BIC needs to be recorded for those countries if the currency paid differs to the local currency. For example, payments to Chile are made in US Dollars, not in Chilean Peso
  • Source: and DOR: fields

If error E338PI – Match not found for FI Identifier appears, this means the international bank is not recorded in the RDF table. To request an update to the bank details on RDF:

  • check OSDD News for any known bank mergers. Refer to the Resources page for a link to International Program
  • if the bank is not listed, bank details will need to be added. Only CIS staff trained in updating RDF and CIS Helpdesk can do this
  • ask team leader or the CIS Helpdesk for contact details of staff with RDF update access
  • request the update

Global impacts to customers overseas payments + Read more ...

Centrelink staff or International Program may get notice of changes, which affect a group of customers whose payments are made to a certain country or bank.

For example, an overseas bank:

  • advises of a sale to another bank (merger) and customers' bank details will no longer be valid from a certain date
  • has collapsed and customers are no longer able to access their funds from this bank
  • is no longer accepting payments in the currency in which Centrelink pays customers

When this occurs:

  • There is a limited time before customers' bank details become invalid

Payment is rejected by financial institution + Read more ...

When a payment is rejected by a financial institution, a DOC is normally on the customer's record with the reason for the rejection. The DOC may be recorded by International Program or Finance Tasmania team.

Payments rejected upfront by the RBA

The RBA’s system may reject upfront by RBA’s system when:

  • ACH payment amount is over 12,000 Euro
  • WWR payment amounts are under US$25
  • certain payment requirements are not adhered to (for example, IBAN does not start with correct country code)

ACH payment limit

ACH payments are subject to a maximum limit of 12,500 Euro (approx. A$18,000) limit. This applies to every transaction. Payments above this limit will be rejected by the RBA’s system. The payment will need to be sent to the customer’s bank in smaller amounts. The RBA will notify Finance Tasmania team when a payment is rejected in this manner. Finance Tasmania team will contact CIS Helpdesk to split the amount and process via the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen. A DOC will be placed on the record to advise payment has been rejected and that CIS Helpdesk will be actioning.

There is no minimum limit on the amount that can be paid via ACH.

WWR payment limit

WWR payments are subject to a US$25 (approx. A$30) minimum limit. Where a payment has been rejected due to being under this limit and customer is paid an Australian payment regularly, SO needs to consider paying the customer periodically (for example, Every 2, 3, 4, 6 or 12 months) depending on the amount.

Customer’s payment will need to be suspended OTH and restored to release the payment at the agreed period before repeating this process until the amount every 4 weeks is greater than US$25.

  • Contact the customer and advise that they will be receiving their Australian payment periodically. Negotiate the appropriate period based on the amount customer is to receive every 4 weeks and customer’s preference. Customer can opt to be paid to an Australian bank account if they would like to receive their payment, at this rate, every 4 weeks. DOC conversation with the customer
  • Record a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on the customer’s record with subject: WWR payment limit. State reason why customer is to receive period payments, the frequency (for example, every 4 months) and that a new FAL RVR needs to be recorded every period to ensure the payment is issued to the customer
  • On the Review Registration (RVR) screen, code all required fields including:
    • RevRsn: ACC
    • Due Date: <insert agreed period minus 2 weeks before pay strip>
    • Text:
      Customer paid via WWR to <insert country>. Amount is under US$25 every 4 weeks. Customer to be paid every <insert period> months. Customer due payment <insert pay strip date>. Refer to OB: 103-04020000 Table 5, Step 5
    • Keywords: 'BAY4' and 'RGNWTW'

When the review is due:

  • Restore customer’s payment
  • Place a new review on RVR to suspend customer’s payment after the payment has been issued:
    • RevRsn: ACC
    • Due Date: <insert 5 business days after pay strip>
    • Text:
      Customer paid via WWR to <insert country>. Amount is under US$25 every 4 weeks. Customer to be paid every <insert period> months. Customer to be paid <insert pay strip date>. Please SUS/OTH. Refer to OB: 103-04020000 Table 5, Step 5
    • Keywords: 'BAY4' and 'RGNWTW'

There is no maximum limit on the amount that can be paid via WWR.

      - Record a DOC on the customer’s record providing date, time and method of contact as well as what was discussed and agreed to (return funds or raise debt
    • For the customer who did not receive payments they were entitled to due to administrative error:
      - Update PAPO with the customer’s correct overseas bank account details. Go to Step 10
      - Issue (reissue) all payments owed to the customer. Go to Step 11. If payments are unable to be reissued via the PS screen. PYAJ may need to be used is there are issues reissuing via the PS screen
      - If the customer has not been contacted, phone the customer and advise them what has occurred and/or action taken. Do not disclose any details of the customer who incorrectly received their payments
      - Record a DOC on the customer’s record advising what has occurred and what action has been take. Do not record the incorrect customer’s CRN or name in the DOC

Procedure ends here.

Overseas payment details may have been provided to Services Australia that:

  • have been incorrectly recorded on PAPO by a Service Australia officer

Before considering reissuing payment(s) to the customer, the bank must be contacted and refund requested.

Refund from the financial institution

Request a refund of payment directly from the financial institution:

  • Write to the bank. Send a XOB999 letter to the financial institution. Use the following approved text:

Payment(s) from Services Australia has been incorrectly paid to a person who is not our customer. Name of account holder: <insert full name of non-customer> holds account number <insert non-customer’s account number or IBAN> via SWIFT/FI ID <insert non-customer’s SWIFT of FI ID>.

As we do not have any contact details, we are unable to contact this person. Could you please contact person and advise they have incorrectly received payment from Services Australia.

Please contact the Reserve Bank of Australia (SWIFT: RSBKAU2S) for refund details. Please quote: INC RCP <insert customer’s CRN> in your SWIFT message to the bank.

Reissuing payment(s)

Were the bank account details incorrectly provided by the customer?

  • Yes,
    • ensure correct bank account details have been recorded on PAPO. Go to Step 10
    • if the customer is experiencing financial hardship, the payment can be reissued before the outcome of the request for a refund. However, advise the customer that a debt will be raised against them in 28 calendar days to recover the funds issued to the incorrect account. To raise a debt against the customer, see General debt raising information
    • if funds are successfully returned to Services Australia within 28 calendar days, no debt will be recorded against the customer
    • if funds are successfully returned once the customer has repaid their debt in part or full, arrears will be issued to the customer. See How to manually calculate arrears

    • issue (reissue) payment(s) owed to the customer. Go to Step 11. If payments are unable to be reissued via the PS screen. PYAJ may need to be used is there are issues reissuing via the PS screen
    • for any debt relating to an administrative error, see Administrative error waivers

9

Payment information requested from the overseas financial institution + Read more ...

An overseas financial institution may request via the RBA detailed payment information relating to a refund request.

The request will be sent to International Program who will then consider forwarding to actioning Services Australia officer to provide the required payment details.

When requested, download the Payment refund request template and complete the details in the spreadsheet.

Completing the template

The following details must be provided for every payment:

  • Payment period. Payment period is located on the Payment Details (PD) screen
  • TRN (Payment ID). The Payment ID, more commonly known as the Transaction Number (TRN) is located:
    • on the Payment Details (PD) screen, or
    • by keying 'L' beside the payment in question on the PS screen (to take you to the Payment List (PAYLIST) screen), and
    • is the LRN on the top left (without the letter), added to the customer's CRN (also minus the letter)
  • AUD amount. The Australian dollar amount issued
  • Foreign Exchange rate. The foreign exchange rate used for that payment. See Resources page for current and historical exchange rates applied to 4 weekly payments outside Australia
  • Foreign currency amount issued. The amount in the correct foreign currency amount for the country (to derive this, multiple the AUD amount with the Foreign Exchange rate)
  • Payment issued. Date payment was issued. See Resources page for Overseas Payment Schedule
  • Comments. Any comments, if required, for example, part payment owed

Staff are to provide these details to International Program.

Record all information requested in a DOC.

The customer must provide bank account details for an account they have legal access to (either solely or jointly).

Note: not every country has joint bank account facilities. The name on the bank account must include the name of the customer or payment nominee.

A customer can give their overseas payment destination details verbally. Make sure that all details are provided. This includes confirming the currency of the account. Ask the customer to provide verification of their new payment details as soon as possible, e.g. an official bank statement.
