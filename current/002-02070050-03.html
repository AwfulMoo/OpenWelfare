Issue, completion and receipt of activity supplement voucher and invoice

Completed by Farm Household Case Officers (FHCO) only

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Assessing eligible activities + Read more ...

The Farm Household Allowance Voucher for activity supplement (A2555) is issued to the customer once it is determined they have an approved eligible activity in their signed Financial Improvement Agreement (FIA). The initial FIA will auto issue the A2555 voucher if it contains an activity that is eligible for the supplement. In all other cases, the A2555 must be manually printed from Online forms when the activity is due to be completed.

If the customer has a specific course/training activity in mind, they should be advised to provide the details to their FHCO who will assess whether the activity supplement can be used to pay for the activity.

The FHCO must record the details of the activity, including the cost and qualifications of the provider in Case Notes.

If the activity is not already recorded as 'eligible for activity supplement' in the FIA, the FIA will have to be updated.

Make sure specific details relevant to the training or advice being undertaken by the customer (such as training course name or the aims to be achieved from obtaining advice) are reflected in the FIA before it is agreed. See Financial Improvement Agreements.

The A2555 voucher can be used to pay for reasonable travel and accommodation costs associated with an agreed activity in the FIA, including if the activity is free.

Note: the activity supplement is not to be used to cover the cost of activities considered Business as Usual (BAU). While an activity may be considered BAU for one customer based on their circumstances and industry, it may not be considered BAU for another customer.

If the activity is:

  • advice, go to Step 2
  • study or training, go to Step 3
  • counselling, go to Step 4

2

Advice provided by an appropriately qualified person + Read more ...

For the activity and any associated travel and accommodation costs to be eligible for the Activity Supplement, the service provider must have a tertiary qualification:

  • in a field related to the subject of the advice being provided, and
  • recognised by a professional institution

Are both requirements met?

  • Yes:
    • the FHCO can approve the activity. Go to Step 5
  • No:
    • the activity is not eligible for the Activity Supplement
    • make genuine attempts to contact the customer by phone in their preferred time if possible and explain the decision
    • tell the customer they can still participate in the training as a valid FIA activity, but the customer is liable for all expenses incurred. Note: if the customer no longer wishes to complete the activity, the FIA may have to be renegotiated
    • procedure ends here

3

Study or training as an activity + Read more ...

Evidence of individual qualifications is not required where the service provided is:

  • a Registered Training Organisation (RTO) or had non-RTO approval granted and scanned to the customer's record
  • study at University

Will the study or training activity be provided by an RTO?

  • Yes, the FHCO can approve the activity. Go to Step 5
  • No, seek approval for the activity from the Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry by completing the Non-RTO consideration template. Once the template has been completed, send it to Farm Household Case Officer for consideration

If the activity is:

  • approved:
    • upload the Delegate's decision documentation to Document Tools on the customer's record (UNS011)
    • go to Step 5
  • not approved
    • upload the Delegate's decision documentation to Document Tools on the customer's record (UNS011)
    • make genuine attempts to contact the customer by phone in their preferred time if possible and explain the decision
    • tell the customer they can still participate in the training or study as a valid FIA activity, but the customer is liable for all expenses incurred.
      Note: if the customer no longer wishes to complete the activity, the FIA may have to be renegotiated
    • procedure ends here

4

Counselling as an activity + Read more ...

If a customer has exhausted funding received through the Better Access initiative, customers are able to use the activity supplement to cover payment to continue assessing services. Customers are able to seek health services from the same providers as the Better Access initiative:

  • consultant psychiatrists
  • general practitioners (GP)
  • non-vocationally recognised medical practitioner (non-VR MPs)
  • psychologists
  • clinical psychologists
  • occupational therapists
  • social workers

The listed providers above will need to hold relevant qualifications.

Escalate the process for anything outside of this.

Will the counselling activity be provided by a qualified professional?

  • Yes, the FHCO can approve the activity, go to Step 5
  • No:
    • make genuine attempts to contact the customer by phone in their preferred time if possible and explain the decision
    • tell the customer they can still participate in the counselling as a valid FIA activity, but the customer is liable for all expenses occurred.
      Note: if the customer no longer wishes to complete the activity, the FIA may have to be renegotiated
    • procedure ends here

5

Issue activity supplement voucher + Read more ...

If the activity is not already included in the FIA, or the FIA indicates the activity is not eligible for the activity supplement, it must be added to the FIA. For help, see Financial Improvement Agreements (FIA).

Print a copy of the amended FIA and activity supplement voucher and post to the customer. See the Resources page for a link to the voucher.

The customer must agree to the new FIA before the activity supplement spending can be approved.

Note: FHCOs must not sign the A2555 voucher before sending it to customers. It must only be signed once the completed form is returned for assessment.

6

Customer submits activity supplement voucher + Read more ...

To claim the activity supplement for an activity in their FIA, the customer must:

  • complete Part A of the voucher
  • have the service provider complete Part B of the voucher
  • submit the voucher
  • provide itemised invoice(s) for the service
  • provide any associated reasonable travel and accommodation costs (with receipt if already paid) to Services Australia via Upload Documents, post or in person at their local service centre or to their FHCO
  • if the activity was receiving advice, evidence of the service provider's qualifications must also be submitted with the A2555 voucher

Upon receipt

Go to Step 7

Scan voucher and evidence and upload to Document Tools on the customer's record.
Include:

  • form type A2555
  • select Yes to Hand off for Further Action.

A follow-up activity will automatically generate and sent to the relevant processing team.

If submitted via Upload documents, the voucher is linked to the relevant activity and an Image Notification Activity ('SCN' activity) created for action.

If submitted by post, the voucher is scanned to the customer's record and an activity created in the Integrated Activity Management System to alert the relevant processing team.

View scan activities in the Activities tab. In the customer's record, select CRN hyperlink, scroll to the bottom of the page and select the Activities tab.

Procedure ends here.

7

Assessment by FHCO + Read more ...

Review the voucher and any evidence provided to ensure:

  • the voucher has been correctly completed, and agreed to by the customer and the service provider
  • the activity for which the supplement is being claimed, was included on the customer's agreed FIA before the activity was completed. The activity recorded in the FIA must be specific and include detail relevant to the study or training, advice or counselling undertaken by the customer. Including the relevant course name or the aims to be achieved from obtaining advice. Note: the increased activity supplement amount can only be used to pay for activities that started on or after 11 June 2020
  • FHA was payable when the FHCO approved use of the activity supplement for the specific activity. Note: there is no time limit on the lodgement of vouchers after FHA has ceased, other than the requirement to be submitted within 2 months from the date of the invoice
  • the customer has not already used the maximum amount
  • any training activity:
    • was provided by an appropriately qualified person or Registered Training Organisation (RTO), or
    • has been approved by the Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry and scanned to the customer's record
  • reasonable travel and accommodation costs associated with the activity were approved. Where the customer provides an invoice or receipts for actual travel/accommodation costs, the FHCO can authorise a change in the estimated costs based on the receipts. If no receipt is available and estimated fuel costs using rome2rio have been authorised, this must be clearly documented in the FHCO Invoice Authorisation Fast Note

Have the above requirements been met?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Activity supplement payable + Read more ...

If the FHCO has determined the activity supplement is payable, they must:

  • record their authorisation on the customer's record using the FHCO Invoice Authorisation Fast Note template
  • if the voucher has been provided to the FHCO:
    • complete the 'FHCO Use only information' section to confirm which activity payment is authorised for and sign the voucher
    • Scan the form and upload to Document Tools on the customer's record. Include:
      form type A2555
      select Yes to Hand off for Further Action
  • if the voucher has been scanned before being reviewed by the FHCO, the FHCO is not required to print and sign the voucher as their authorisation is recorded using the Fast Note

FHCOs must never sign a blank FIA voucher.

Note: a follow-up activity will automatically be generated and sent to the relevant processing team. An ACTDOC is not required.

Procedure ends here.

9

Activity supplement not payable + Read more ...

Do not sign the voucher. FHCO must make genuine attempts to contact the customer by phone in their preferred time if possible to advise the outcome of the claim. Document the record with the details of the decision to reject the voucher.

Scan the form to Document Tools on the customer's record.

  • form type A2555
  • select Yes to Hand off for further action.

Check invoice and documentary evidence of service provider qualifications + Read more ...

Process an activity supplement debt or refund

For Smart Centre Rural Processing staff only

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Circumstances where refund may be necessary + Read more ...

It may be necessary to raise a debt for an amount of activity supplement, which has been paid to a customer or service provider.

For example, but not limited to, this may be the case where the customer:

  • is convicted of fraud in relation to the activity supplement claim
  • or provider has been paid the amount in excess of the amount payable under the activity supplement or additional activity supplement
  • has not participated/completed the agreed activity or high value activity without reasonable excuse, but costs associated with that activity have been incurred
  • in consultation with their FHCO has changed their mind about participating in a particular activity and the service provider is willing to refund an amount of activity supplement already paid

An amount may be recovered where the customer has been reimbursed for the cost of the activity or where the service provider has been paid directly.

The amount must be recovered and, where applicable, re-credited to the customer's activity supplement balance.

If a refund of the activity supplement is required from:

  • the customer, go to Step 2
  • the service provider, go to Step 5

2

Run the Raise a Debt guided procedure + Read more ...

To raise a debt for the value of the activity supplement to be refunded:

  • go to Workspace > Guided Procedures > Raise a Debt
  • on the Select Action screen, select Add to create a new debt
  • select Next

3

Debt Details screen + Read more ...

  • Benefit Type: 'ACS'
  • Reason: 'FHA'

  • Start Date: start of debt period
  • End Date: end of debt period
  • Date of effect
  • Amount of the debt

Complete The Request to raise customer invoice and email to Accounts Receivable Team. The service provider is issued with an invoice to return the funds to the agency. See Forms (for staff) on the Resources page for a link to the Request to raise customer invoice form.

Coding assistance for the Request to raise customer invoice.

Section 1:

  • B/U Name & Email address: FHA and requesting officer's email address
  • Company Code: CENT
  • Is this unearned revenue? No
  • Is this S.74 revenue? No

Section 2:

  • provide email or postal address for where the invoice is to be issued

Section 3:

  • provide details for the reason the invoice needs to be raised

Section 4:

These details must match what was on the original request to pay.

  • Cost Centre: 1000301
  • GL (General Ledger) code:
