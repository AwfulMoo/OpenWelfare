Recording staff and/or customers in a service centre

Table 1: the process for staff when a member of the public is taking photos or recording using a phone, camera or other recording device in a Services Australia premises.

Step

Action

1

A person is seen to be recording or says they are recording + Read more ...

Tell the person:

'Services Australia does not allow you to record conversations or take photos in service centres.'

(Refer to signage if available)

'I will be unable to help you with your business until you stop recording.'

If the person:

  • complies with the request, go to Step 2
  • does not comply with the request:
    • If the person identifies they have a need to record the interaction due to reasonable adjustment for a disability, go to Step 3
    • Otherwise, go to Step 4
  • queries about Services Australia's use of recording technology, go to Step 7

2

Ask for the recording to be deleted + Read more ...

Tell the person:

'Thank you for stopping your recording. Can you please delete any recordings you have made.'

Do not:

  • ask the person to delete any recordings if this will further escalate the interaction
  • insist if the person refuses to delete any recordings
  • ask for or take the recording device from the person

If the person stops recording, ask why they are recording and address any issues if possible.

Document details of their response as part of the interaction documentation.

If the person’s decision to record is due to dissatisfaction with Services Australia decision-making or processes issue, refer to:

  • Managing customer complaints and feedback
  • Customer aggression – Prevention and Management

If it is due to reasonable adjustment for a disability, go to Step 3.

Procedure ends here.

3

Making a reasonable adjustment for customers with disability + Read more ...

Services Australia will permit a person to record where reasonable adjustment is required to support a person with disability. For example a customer who is deaf, has hearing loss or a speech disability.

Generally, reasonable adjustment should be made if stopping the recording would cause an unintentional effect of hardship or vulnerability.

In all cases, consider why the person has asked to record the interaction. When assessing reasonable adjustment requirements, the nature of the person's circumstances (for example, disability) and the impact of not recording the interaction must be significant to the person.

Consider alternative options, for example printing a hard copy of any file notes made during the interaction, should also be considered before consenting to the recording. For more information about providing customer services to customers with disabilities, see Providing services to customers with disabilities.

Staff should seek support from their manager in making this decision.

If the request to record is deemed appropriate, it is important that the location of the recording is considered to mitigate the risk of other private conversations or private activity being included in the recording. Before using an interview room for this purpose, consider the Use of interview rooms guidelines in the Emergency Response Procedures - Aggression.

If the person has a Services Australia record, document details of the interaction and their response.

4

Recording does not stop as requested + Read more ...

Tell the person:

'If you continue to record this interaction you will be asked to leave.'

If the person stops recording, go to Step 2.

If the person continues to record:

They should be asked to leave. Any APS4 or above delivering face-to-face services can direct them to leave the premises under section 12 of Public Order (Protection of Persons and Property) Act 1971. Staff should seek support from their manager.

Do not:

  • ask the person to delete any recordings if this will further escalate the interaction
  • insist if the person refuses to delete any recordings
  • ask for or take the recording device from the person

Instruct the person to leave under the Public Order (Protection of Persons and Property) Act 1971 (Cth) (PoPPA). This is a spoken direction to a person to leave a site.

'I am an authorised person for the purposes of paragraph 12(2)(c) of the Public Order (Protection of Persons and Property) Act 1971, and I am directing you to leave by virtue of the powers conferred under that Act. You will be liable to prosecution if you do not comply with this direction.'

The authorised person can use a shorter version in some circumstances as long as they achieve substantial compliance with the wording of the direction, including that:

  • they are authorised under the Public Order Act to direct that the person leaves the premises, and
  • the person will be liable to prosecution if they fail to comply.

See References page for a link to the legislation Public Order (Protection of Persons and Property) Act 1971 (Cth) (PoPPA).

If the customer:

  • continues to record and refuses to leave the premises, go to step 5
  • complies with the direction to leave, go to step 6

5

Customer continues to record and refuses to leave the premises + Read more ...

In these circumstances the local leadership/the Aggressive Response Team (ART) must:

  • advise other customers when they enter the service centre that another customer is filming or recording. Other customer can decide if they want to leave the service centre to avoid being recorded
  • move other staff in the vicinity of the recording to minimise the risk of inadvertent disclosures of official information, including information which contains personal/protected information
  • if necessary, initiate the site's Emergency Response Procedures – Aggression and alert
    • Media Branch
    • Employment Law Team
    • See the Resources page for contact details

6

Managing counterproductive or aggressive behaviour + Read more ...

Report, record and escalate all incidents of counterproductive behaviour and customer aggression see Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression

Contacting the customer after an incident, helps keep staff safe, for more information see Customer aggression - Post incident contact.

A Managed Service Plan should be considered to address any ongoing risk to staff safety. See Customer aggression - Managed Service Plan (MSP).

Procedure ends here.

7

If asked about Service Australia's use of recording technology + Read more ...

Tell the person:

Call recording

See Call Recording - information and access for more information.

Closed Circuit Television (CCTV)

'CCTV is used to help protect staff, customers, visitors and resources from criminal or anti-social behaviour. CCTV is used during the opening hours of the office. CCTV helps improve the existing security features within the site and in the collection of evidence when required.'

Images are stored for at least 30 working days and coverage includes customer entrances, waiting areas, customer service areas and interview rooms.

The Investigation Toolkit contains a link to the CCTV Guidelines, which outlines the process for requesting CCTV footage. See the Resources page for a link to the Intelligence and Investigations Policy page.

If the person has a Services Australia record, document details of the interaction and their response.

4

Third Party organisation special circumstances + Read more ...

There are circumstances where Third Party organisations (e.g. Private Health Insurance company) who contact the agency, which like Service Australian, record all of their inbound and outbound calls.

When a Third Party organisation contacts the agency and advises they will be recording an interaction with Services Australia, they must notify of their organisation’s terms of usage. Such organisations should comply with Commonwealth, state or territory privacy laws, and know and comply with any surveillance legislation applicable to their call recording practices.

If the Service Officers is satisfied with the identity of a caller from a Third Party organisation, they may proceed with the call.

For further advice and information, refer to Customer Aggression Prevention Team (CAPT) team or the Employment Law Team. See the Resources page for contact details.

5

Recording of call does not stop as requested + Read more ...

Tell the person:

'If you do not confirm that you will not be recording this conversation, I will have to end the call.'

If the person does not confirm:

'I am ending the call now.'

Politely end the call.

For more information, see:

  • Centrelink and Medicare, see Telephone Standards (CLK or MED), or
  • Child Support, see Ending customer phone calls table in Contact with Child Support customers

If the person has an Agency record, document details of the interaction and their response and record an incident in appropriate Agency system

Go to Step 8.

6

Caller says they have recorded the call + Read more ...

If a person records a phone call without telling the staff member until the end of the conversation, tell the person:

'Services Australia does not allow you to record conversations and I do not give my consent for you to record this conversation.

'Can you please delete any recording you have made.'

If the person complies with the request to stop recording, document details of their response as part of the interaction documentation. Include specific reference that consent was not given.

If the customer displays counterproductive or aggressive behaviour, go to step 8.

Call recording

Closed Circuit Television (CCTV)

Intention to or published recording of staff or customers

Table 3: the process when staff become aware that a member of the public is intending to or has published audio, visual or audio-visual recording of staff or customers. For example, on social media, websites or in hardcopy format.

Step

Action

1

Stated intention to publish + Read more ...

If a person states they intend to publish the recording, tell them:

'Services Australia does not allow you to record conversations or take photos in service centres.'

Note:

  • Do not ask the person to delete any recordings if it is assessed this will further escalate the interaction
  • If a person refuses to delete any recordings, do not insist
  • Do not ask for or take the recording device from the person
  • Point out relevant signage if available

If the person has a Services Australia record, document the details of the interaction.

If the customer displays counterproductive or aggressive behaviour, go to step 4.

2

Recording has been published + Read more ...

If a person states they have published the recording, or a Service Officer becomes aware that this has happened:

  • Service Officer should tell their manager
  • If needed, the manager can consult with the Regional Security Advisor
  • In addition, the Media Branch can be contacted to investigate removal of content and discuss if the recordings can be removed from the public domain

See Resources page for contact details

3

Removal of recording from social media platforms + Read more ...

For a recording to be removed from a social media platform, the content containing the recording must breach the relevant social media platform’s terms and conditions.

There is a greater chance that content can be successfully removed if it can be demonstrated that content falls into one or more of the following categories:

  • Content poses a security risk for staff or customers
  • Content includes sensitive customer information
  • Content includes recording of a private conversation
  • Content includes recording of a private activity

In these instances the Media Branch can be contacted for advice.

For an assessment to be made, the Media Branch will need screenshots and/or links to published recording.

See Resources page for contact details

4

Managing counterproductive or aggressive behaviour + Read more ...

If the person's behaviour escalates, follow Customer aggression - Response.

Report, record and escalate all incidents of counterproductive behaviour and customer aggression see Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression.

Contacting the customer after an incident, helps keep staff safe, for more information see Customer aggression - Post incident contact.

A Managed Service Plan should be considered to address any ongoing risk to staff safety. See Customer aggression - Managed Service Plan (MSP).

Procedure ends here.
