SEE program placement, commencement and suspension

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Placement in the SEE program - updating the Job Plan + Read more ...

When a job seeker is:

  • Centrelink managed:
    • the job seeker's record is automatically updated with placement in Skills for Education and Employment (SEE), and
    • an action item is created, advising that the Job Plan is to be updated
  • provider managed, the provider is responsible for updating the Job Plan

Job seekers must be advised of the compliance consequences of not attending the SEE program as detailed in their agreed Job Plan.

If a referral to SEE is identified for a provider managed job seeker when re-running the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI), the Service Officer should advise the job seeker to discuss participation in SEE with their provider.

2

Compliance action for customers who do not commence SEE + Read more ...

It is not compulsory for the job seeker to accept the SEE referral, but it becomes a compulsory activity, subject to compliance, when:

  • it has been recorded in the Job Plan, and
  • the plan has been signed by the job seeker

SEE is expected to start within eight weeks of the Pre-Training Assessment appointment.

The provider finalises the referral with the reason Did Not Start Program (NST), if the job seeker:

  • fails to attend training on the scheduled start date, and
  • does not commence within five business days of the scheduled start date

If the job seeker wants to start SEE any time after the first week of the scheduled start date, a new referral is required.

See Failing to attend or satisfactorily participate in a compulsory activity.

3

Suspension from SEE due to absence + Read more ...

The provider and the participant may agree to a suspension from training rather than withdrawing the participant from training entirely.

SEE participation can be suspended for the following reasons:

  • Health (HLT)
  • Personal Issues (ISP)
  • Other training /education (OTE)
  • Casual or seasonal work (AEO)
  • Cannot contact participant (CCP)

When SEE participation recommences, to cover the period of absence, the provider can:

  • increase the SEE duration, or
  • increase SEE intensity

4

Suspensions from SEE for other reasons + Read more ...

A job seeker may be suspended from training for:

  • unacceptable behaviour
  • unsatisfactory attendance, or
  • misuse of computer or internet facilities

Use code UBH for these suspensions.

Compliance action must be considered in these circumstances. See Step 2 in Table 2.

For provider managed job seekers, see Failing to attend or satisfactorily participate in a compulsory activity.

5

SEE participation information is not up to date + Read more ...

If the job seeker's:

  • SEE participation details have not been updated automatically on their record, and
  • SEE status is not the same as the SEE provider's details:
    • the update may be delayed because of an error or other technical issue, which should be investigated. Report the case to the Level 1 Helpdesk - the SEE Service Zone contact
    • the SEE Service Zone contact will ask the SEE provider to escalate the issue, if the issue cannot be resolved through error processing. The SEE Service Zone contact may report the incident to Participation Programmes

Completion or exit from SEE and compliance action

Table 2

Item

Description

1

SEE provider to monitor participation + Read more ...

A job seeker with mutual obligation requirements who has SEE in their Job Plan must meet their attendance obligations for this activity.

The provider:

  • monitors each participant's attendance
  • takes appropriate action to withdraw a participant, where
    • unsatisfactory attendance persists, or
    • explained absences are such that the participant is not adequately participating and cannot reasonably be expected to satisfactorily complete SEE
  • notifies Services Australia electronically within five working days, if participation in SEE should be suspended or finalised. This includes where SEE is completed or another outcome has occurred. For example, the customer finds employment or withdraws from SEE

2

Compliance action for unacceptable behaviour or attendance in SEE where SEE is in the Job Plan + Read more ...

For Centrelink managed job seekers, commence compliance action if:

  • the job seeker's record shows they have been withdrawn from SEE, and
  • before the withdrawal, it is clear the job seeker has been suspended due to:
    • unacceptable behaviour
    • unsatisfactory attendance, or
    • misuse of computer or internet facilities

Refer to the Referral Summary screen for the reason why SEE was suspended or finalised.

For provider managed job seekers, see Failing to attend or satisfactorily participate in a compulsory activity.

3

Recommencement of SEE within 12 weeks of a withdrawn status + Read more ...

A job seeker can recommence SEE with the same provider, up to 12 weeks from the date of suspension or finalisation.

The provider confirms the job seeker's recommencement in SEE electronically, within five working days. The recommencement displays on the job seeker's record.

A job seeker is not eligible for a referral to a new SEE provider within the 12 weeks of withdrawing from SEE:

  • Check the due date of the previous finalised referral
  • Do not record a new referral before the due date

4

SEE participant ceases to participate + Read more ...

The provider may withdraw the job seeker from SEE, if a job seeker:

  • ceases to participate
  • is absent from SEE without a suitable reason on regular occasions, or
  • fails to return to SEE within four weeks of the agreed date, following suspension

Providers should consider alternatives to withdrawal. For example, temporary suspension.

The provider must advise Services Australia electronically, within five working days of withdrawal.

5

Job Plan action when job seeker ceases SEE + Read more ...

The Participation Summary screen prompts the Service Officer to update the Job Plan, when SEE has been finalised before the end date shown.

  • Investigate the reason for non-attendance
  • Take the appropriate action, and
  • Record a DOC, with:
    • details of the non-attendance, and
    • the action taken

6

Recommencement after relocation + Read more ...

A job seeker must discuss any transfer with their provider. If a job seeker wishes to transfer to:

  • another site run by the same provider, the existing placement continues
  • a new provider, then a new referral is required. The current provider must contact the new provider to seek agreement. When agreed:
    • the initial provider finalises the referral with the reason of 'customer withdrawn (transfer)', and
    • the job seeker must attend a service centre to ask for a referral to the new SEE provider

The 12 week waiting period does not apply to transfers.

Check the due date of the previous finalised referral. Do not record a new referral before or on the due date.

7

Completion of SEE + Read more ...

Services Australia is advised automatically, within five working days, when a participant has completed SEE. The course completion displays on the job seeker's record.
