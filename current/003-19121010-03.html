Identifying and supporting older customers affected by abuse

Step

Action

1

Identifying the abuse of older customers + Read more ...

If the customer states they are being abused, mistreated, or neglected, go to Step 3.

Other signs that a customer is being abused could be:

  • Visual cues, such as:
    • Physical signs such as bruising
    • Attempts to hide injuries
    • Appearing distracted
    • Continually looking at the door
    • Mood swings
    • General emotional distress
  • Conversational cues, such as:
    • What the person says, doesn't say or even how they say it
    • Questions about their privacy
    • Whispering or speaking in a low voice
    • Complaints about how their nominee or carer is treating them
    • Comments about money missing from, or inability to access, their bank account
    • Asking for help to pay bills
    • Nominee or Power of Attorney do not want Services Australia to have any contact with the customer
  • Administrative cues, such as:
    • Setting their Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) to 'no'
    • Unwillingness to agree to a new correspondence and/or payment nominee
    • Unaware of a current payment nominee arrangement or that the payment nominee has changed
    • Nominee cancels Centrepay arrangements
    • Regular requests for an Urgent Payment or payday change
    • Suspected inappropriate use of a nominee arrangement

If any of these cues are present, or concern arises due to something the customer says, clarify if they are experiencing abuse, or are concerned about it. Go to Step 2.

2

Clarify, acknowledge and offer support to the customer + Read more ...

If abuse of an older person is suspected, use the below questions to clarify the situation. Staff should not try to clarify details with the customer if they suspect the alleged abuser is present:

  • 'Are you worried that this will make trouble for you?'
  • 'Are you afraid of what may happen if you do this?'
  • 'Are you concerned you may upset someone if you do this?'
  • 'I sense that you are worried or afraid about something. Am I right?'

If the customer confirms they are being abused, or has concerns about being abused:

  • acknowledge what they have said, and
  • offer to help them

For example

  • 'I am concerned for you. May I talk to you about what services can support you further?'
  • 'You have let me know about your concerns that if your son/daughter/neighbour/carer finds out you have been speaking with Centrelink today. Did you want to know about some of the people you can talk to about your situation?'

Go to Step 3.

3

Identify support options + Read more ...

Offer a referral to external and internal support services.

If the customer:

  • is in distress or needs support such as a Crisis Payment, go to Step 4
  • needs specialist support such as one of the below, go to Step 5
    • a Multicultural Service Officer (MSO)
    • an Indigenous Service Officer (ISO)
    • a Community Engagement Officer (CEO)
    • Aged Care Specialist Officer (ACSO)
    • a Financial Information Service Officer (FISO)
  • needs help to access other payments and services, go to Step 6

If the customer:

  • does not need any help from Services Australia see if a referral to an external service is suitable, see Resources. Go to Step 7
  • does not want any help, go to Step 7

If there are concerns about the conduct of a nominee, go to Step 4.

4

Internal referral to a Services Australia social worker + Read more ...

Social workers can:

  • offer short-term counselling, support and information
  • offer specialist assessments
  • help identify and suggest other supports and services

Always refer any of the below to a social worker:

  • Allegations that a nominee is:
    • not acting in the best interests of a customer
    • misusing the arrangement
    • using family and domestic violence, including abuse of an older Australian

A referral to a social worker may be appropriate when the customer:

  • is experiencing family and domestic violence, including abuse of an older Australia, or
  • is highly distressed and unsupported, or
  • is currently in an unsafe situation

A customer can also be referred if they need any of the below:

  • Crisis Payment
  • Child Support assessment
  • mutual obligation exemption

To refer the customer, see Social work service referral.

Additional referrals

Encourage the customer to speak to other areas of the agency for further help. For example, if they get payments from Centrelink, or have a My Health Record.

If the customer:

  • needs help from a specialist officer, go to Step 5
  • needs help from other areas within the agency, go to Step 6
  • does not need any more help, go to Step 7

5

Referrals to other specialist officers + Read more ...

Other specialist service officers can help the customer manage services and referrals for abuse.

For help from other specialist Service Officers, see the below:

  • Aged Care Specialist Officer (ACSO)
  • Financial Information Service Officers (FISO)
  • Multicultural Services Officers (MSO)
  • Indigenous Services Officers (ISO)
  • Community Engagement Officers (CEO)
  • Grandparent, Foster and Kinship Carer Advisers

Does the customer need help or more information from other areas within Services Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Internal referrals + Read more ...

With the customer:

  • discuss the available payment and service options
  • remind them to update their details with all the relevant service delivery areas

This will ensure they get the right payments and services as well as help to protect their safety.

The below is a guide for discussing the other payments and services that the customer may need to consider. This list is a guide only and there may be other payments or services for them to consider. It is important to be guided by the customer's individual circumstances.

Discuss ways the customer can contact the relevant area. Give them the phone numbers if needed.

Family and domestic violence and COVID-19 Vaccination certificates + Read more ...

If a customer is affected by family and domestic violence, and having proof of COVID-19 vaccination causes them safety concerns, there is support available. Services Australia can help customers access safer options for their circumstances, for example:

  • Add their digital COVID-19 certificate to a digital wallet. Their device may allow a customer to use a security pass code to use Apple Wallet or Google Pay. They can set up or change their security pass code through their Settings
  • A customer’s vaccination provider can print their immunisation history statement for them. Customers can also call the Australian Immunisation Register and ask Services Australia to send their proof to them. It can take up to 14 days for a customer’s statement or certificate to arrive in the post
  • If a customer is concerned about unauthorised activity to their online accounts, or the security of their Centrelink, Child Support or Medicare records, they can let Services Australia know. If someone else has access to a customer’s online accounts, they can change their passwords at any time

Centrelink + Read more ...

Payment specific:

  • Eligibility for Crisis payment (CrP)
  • Eligibility for AGDRP and NZ DRP
  • Processing AGDRP and NZ DRP claims in Customer First
  • Urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances
  • Access to Centrelink self service - set up, checking, locking and resetting
  • General Centrelink debt recovery information

Nominees:

  • Appointing nominees
  • Reviewing nominee arrangements
  • Cancelling a nominee arrangement

Account security:

  • Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) authority
  • Customer password on a Display on Access DOC
  • Centrelink customer requests a new Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Restricted Access and eligibility
  • Changing the Legal name of customers or their children
  • Aliases and other names (legal, preferred, sex and gender diverse customers, cultural considerations etc.)
  • Change in relationship status from partnered to single
  • Where customers are unable to provide a residential address

Medicare + Read more ...

Process specific:

  • Copy or transfer a person or child to another Medicare card
  • Debt recovery information for non Debt Management Officers
    • see Assistance with queries for non DMO C-M, select Family and Domestic Violence (Item 12)

Nominees:

  • Power of Attorney, Guardianship, Administrative Orders and Authorised Representative for Medicare

Account security:

  • The My Health Record system - Your Health
  • My Health Record enquiry line
  • Customer password for Medicare enquiries
  • Sensitive Information Indicators in the CDMS

Changing personal details:

If the customer has concerns about disclosing their new name or postal address to other people on their Medicare, do not update their record.

Tell the customer to get their own Medicare card and number before changing their details.

  • Amend customer details in the CDMS
  • Change legal name on Medicare record

  • Application for Child Support to be collected
  • Collecting child support payments privately and/or discharging arrears
  • Ending a Child Support assessment

7

Return to the original discussion + Read more ...

Return to the customer's original enquiry. Continue with the discussion and related actions.

  • the Employee assistance program (EAP), see Resources
