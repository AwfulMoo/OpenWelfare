Job seeker contacts regarding a Capability Assessment

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Requirement to attend a Capability Assessment + Read more ...

A Capability Assessment is triggered by the Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE) Employment Services System (ESSweb).

Employment Services Providers and ParentsNext Providers are responsible for notifying job seekers when they are required to contact Services Australia to complete a Capability Assessment. They are also required to advise the job seeker that the assessment takes approximately 30 minutes.

The date the provider has this conversation with the job seeker becomes the deemed date of notification.

Providers issue this notification verbally, job seekers do not receive an SMS or other outbound contact from Services Australia to prompt contact for the Capability Assessment.

The job seeker's payment remains suspended (SUS/TCF - did not meet provider requirement) until they contact Services Australia and complete the Capability Assessment.

Note: once the Capability Assessment is complete, the other reconnection requirements for mutual obligation failures committed while the Capability Assessment was pending may impact when payment can restart.

Participation payments are automatically cancelled for reason failure to re-engage (CAN/CDR) if a job seeker does not arrange a Capability Assessment within 4 weeks of the deemed date of notification. Job seeker payments cannot be restored following cancellation, but the job seeker may reclaim payment. If the job seeker returns to payment within 6 months, a Capability Assessment will be retriggered and must be completed before the job seeker can start reporting.

2

Job seeker contacts Services Australia for a Capability Assessment + Read more ...

Where the job seeker contacts the service centre, the Customer Details screen in the Front of House (FoH) tool will indicate if compliance action is outstanding.

Where the job seeker contacts a Smart Centre, the Participation Compliance summary will confirm if a Capability Assessment is outstanding.

Capability Assessment is outstanding

When a job seeker contacts for a Capability Assessment, they should be referred to the Participation Solutions Team.

Capability Assessment is not outstanding

A Capability Assessment will not display as outstanding where:

  • the initial transaction was not accepted through automatic validation, for example where an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) is pending, or
  • background processing has identified that a Capability Assessment is not required, or
  • demerits that contributed to the Capability Assessment being triggered have been removed

Where a Capability Assessment is not outstanding, but the job seeker has a current Targeted Compliance Framework (TCF) suspension, the job seeker will need to be referred back to their provider.

3

Job seeker granted exemption from mutual obligation requirements when they have a Capability Assessment requirement + Read more ...

When an exemption from mutual obligation requirements has been granted and a Capability Assessment exists, the Capability Assessment is placed on hold until the exemption ends.

When the exemption has ended, a contact trigger will prompt the job seeker to contact Participation Solutions Team (PST) to have a Capability Assessment.

Note: the system will automatically hold the Capability Assessment if an exemption is recorded before the Capability Assessment workflow has been accessed.

4

Job seeker is asked to provide evidence for a Capability Assessment + Read more ...

Most Capability Assessments will be completed the day the job seeker contacts Services Australia using evidence available on the record.

If the job seeker advises there are circumstances impacting their ability to meet the compulsory requirements in their Job Plan, PST-skilled Service Officers should first check the record for any evidence that can be used to substantiate their claims.

If evidence does not exist, PST-skilled Service Officers can request suitable evidence from the job seeker to support their circumstances.

If evidence (such as medical evidence that has been lodged in the last two years) already exists on the record, and supports the job seeker's disclosed circumstances impacting compliance, this should be used. The Capability Assessment outcome should not be delayed by seeking further evidence unnecessarily. If evidence is requested from the job seeker, the Capability Assessment should be held for no more than 2 days (this can be longer if required and should be assessed on a case by case basis).

Where the job seeker contacts to provide the evidence Services Australia has requested:

  • check the Participation Compliance summary to confirm if a Capability Assessment is pending
  • advise the job seeker to upload evidence or scan documents as appropriate
  • refer the job seeker to the Participation Solutions Team (PST)

5

Job seeker fails to provide evidence + Read more ...

When the job seeker has not returned the requested evidence by the due date, Services Australia will complete the Capability Assessment during the next contact based on available information.

When the job seeker contacts:

  • check the Participation Compliance summary to confirm if a Capability Assessment is pending
  • refer the job seeker to the Participation Solutions Team (PST)

This will generally mean that unsubstantiated circumstances cannot be taken into account. This will likely result in an outcome of capable, meaning the job seeker is capable to meet the compulsory requirements in their current Job Plan and will move to the financial penalty zone.

6

Job seeker provides evidence after the Capability Assessment is finalised + Read more ...

If the job seeker provides evidence after the Capability Assessment is completed, Services Australia cannot reassess the Capability Assessment.

The job seeker will need to be referred back to their provider to discuss the circumstances that have been impacting their compliance and the evidence they are now able to supply.

Note: Services Australia staff are still required to take all appropriate action for the evidence that has been supplied – i.e. coding a medical certificate, updating the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) and/or referring a job seeker for an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) as needed.

Job seeker refuses to participate in a Capability Assessment + Read more ...

If a job seeker refuses to participate in the assessment, tell them:

  • the assessment will be completed using information on their record, and
  • it could mean they move into the penalty zone where non-compliance can result in a financial penalty or cancellation of their payment

For job seekers who refuse to participate or deliberately end the call, PST-skilled Service Officers must complete the Capability Assessment using information on the record.

  • checking for medical certificates that are yet to be coded

3

Discuss the Capability Interview findings + Read more ...

The Capability Interview is conducted by the provider when a job seeker gets 3 demerits in 6 active months. In this interview the provider and job seeker review:

  • activities in the Job Plan, and
  • the job seekers circumstances

The Capability Interview ensures the provider tailors the Job Plan to ensure it is achievable given the job seeker's circumstances.

During the Capability Assessment the PST-skilled Service Officer must discuss the outcomes from the Capability Interview.

The Service Officer selects 'view summary of Capability Interview' in order to review the outcomes of that conversation. If the Service Officer needs any clarification about the details of the Capability Interview they should contact the provider.

Contact with the provider must be recorded on the Capability Assessment Outcomes page.

Note: job seekers who query the recent non-compliance and demerit accrual outlined in their Capability Interview must be referred back to their provider to discuss. See Non-compliance with compulsory requirements - review and appeals.

Go to Step 4.

4

Review the Job Plan - checking the compulsory requirements + Read more ...

Providers negotiate Job Plans with the job seeker. During the Capability Interview, the provider will have:

  • talked to the job seeker, ensuring their circumstances have been considered, and
  • made sure the requirements included within the Job Plan are reasonable and achievable

Job Plan errors

Check if the job seeker's Job Plan includes any errors such as:

  • conflicting requirements, for example, two or more compulsory activities at the same time
  • additional requirements for job seekers who are fully meeting their mutual obligations through volunteer work and/or paid work
  • activities that require:
    • a principal carer to regularly participate outside school hours or travel more than 60 minutes to and from the activity
    • taking part in a criminal activity
    • undergoing medical, psychiatric or psychological treatment
    • participation in an activity outside of Australia
    • involvement in the sex or adult entertainment industry
    • undertaking an activity contravening laws relating to discrimination or occupational health and safety
    • a job seeker to participate in an activity that would aggravate an existing illness, disability or injury, or
    • a job seeker to undertake an activity that does not provide appropriate support or facilities regarding their illness, disability or injury
    • participation in WFD (or other approved programs of work) as a compulsory activity when the job seeker receives a partial rate of income support due to the income test (either due to their earnings or their partner's)
    • a job seeker under 18 years of age to participate in WFD

Further information is available within Social Security Guide 3.11.2 Job Plans to view the types of requirements that a Job Plan must not contain.

If the Service Officer identifies one or more errors, the information relating to this is recorded on the Capability Assessment Outcomes page.

The errors must be specified through selecting the relevant requirement containing the error/reason in the toggle twisty.

The Service Officer must include information for the provider to action and negotiate a more appropriate Job Plan with suitable activities, for example, by recording the error or inappropriate term in the Job Plan.

If errors are identified in the Job Plan, the Capability Assessment outcome will automatically become 'Not Capable' and no further assessment is required.

Note: for more information, see Approved activities for Job Plans.

Go to Step 5.

5

The following circumstances have been added or updated since the Capability Interview + Read more ...

Following the workflow, the next question that presents after reviewing the Job Plan is: 'Is there anything that is making it difficult for you to meet the requirements in your current Job Plan that you haven't mentioned so far?'

The purpose of this part of the Capability Assessment is to prompt the job seeker for any circumstances impacting their capacity to comply with their requirements. These may be circumstances which:

  • are newly identified: the job seeker has not disclosed these circumstances to their provider in the Capability Interview or during other contacts, or
  • are ongoing: the circumstances may have been disclosed previously and are continuing to impact the job seeker's capacity

If the job seeker has any circumstances or barriers impacting their capacity to comply, the PST-skilled Service Officer must:

  • select 'Yes - has ongoing difficulties meeting requirements', and
  • record their statement in the free text box that presents in this section of the workflow

Completing 'Yes - has ongoing difficulties meeting requirements' and the associated free text section will progress the workflow to the Circumstances Impacting Compliance page. See Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirement on how to record circumstances.

  • Go to Step 6

If the PST-skilled Service Officer determines there are 'No' circumstances impacting the job seekers compliance, go to Step 1 in Table 5.

When 'No' is selected the workflow will bypass the Circumstances Impacting Compliance section and any new information obtained at this point will not impact the outcome of the Capability Assessment.

6

Circumstances or barriers impacting compliance identified + Read more ...

Where difficulties meeting requirements are identified the Circumstances impacting Compliance page must be updated.

Remember: PST-skilled Service Officers are able to 'hold' a Capability Assessment, allowing job seekers an opportunity to provide evidence that supports the impact their circumstances are having on their ability to meet their mutual obligation requirements. See, Holding a Capability Assessment, Step 1 in Table 3.

On the Circumstances impacting compliance page, update all relevant circumstances. This includes those with a status of 'yet to assess impact'.

Circumstances impacting Compliance with a status of 'yet to assess impact' must be updated to either:

  • confirm their impact (through the provision of suitable evidence)
  • coded as 'difficulties are point in time or short term', or
  • ended as they are 'not identified' or 'do not impact compliance'

Free text outlining what the customer states is impacting their ability to meet their compulsory requirements is to be recorded in the Describe the circumstances field. This text should be a clear, concise factual record of the circumstances impacting compliance.

If circumstances are:

  • 'yet to be assessed', 'new', or ,'ongoing difficulties', go to Step 7
  • 'not identified', or 'difficulties identified are point in time or short term', the Capability Assessment workflow will navigate to the Capability Assessment Outcome page. See Step 1 in Table 5

7

Job seeker has identified difficulties meeting requirements + Read more ...

Assessing and identifying the impact of job seeker circumstances is a key part of the Capability Assessment.

If a job seeker presents with family and domestic violence, or have a Family relationships/Domestic violence circumstance (FDV), the Service Officer must consult with a PST-skilled social worker during the Capability Assessment. See, Accessing a PST-skilled social worker.

If the job seeker discloses a circumstance (not FDV), and help is needed to assess the impact of that circumstance upon their ability to comply with their current compulsory requirements, PST-skilled Service Officers should consult with a PST-skilled SO5, social worker or other specialists. These consults can also be used as evidence to support the addition or validation of most circumstances.

Note: only circumstances with a minimum level of evidence can be used to determine a job seekers capability during the assessment. Circumstances that do not meet the minimum level of evidence should result in either the job seeker being given an opportunity to supply evidence to support the circumstance, or the circumstance is ended as evidence does not exist to support the impacts.

Contact with the job seeker's provider or other third parties may also be required. Any contact with a specialist provider, or third party should be used as evidence and must be recorded on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance page.

For more information see the Resources page of Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirement.

Is the job seeker presenting with family and domestic violence crisis or do they have a current family relationship/domestic violence circumstance?

  • Yes, a consult with a PST-skilled social worker is required, see Accessing a PST-skilled social worker
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Updating job seeker circumstances, recording capability to meet requirements + Read more ...

Updating the circumstance

PST-skilled Service Officers record the job seeker's explanation of how a particular circumstance impacts their ability to meet their requirements.

The text recorded must be relevant to both:

  • the circumstance; and
  • the requirements the job seeker is having trouble complying with

PST-skilled Service Officers must record the date the circumstance started to directly impact the job seeker's ability to meet requirements. If the date the circumstance began to impact is prior to the date the Capability Assessment is completed, additional information needs to be recorded to determine if the circumstance disclosed contributed to demerits accrued on or after that date.

Note: information on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance page is shared with the provider, so sensitive, personal information should not be recorded to protect the job seeker's privacy.

Recording capability to meet requirements

Where there are requirement/s which the job seeker finds difficult to meet, the following questions need to be considered:

  • Which compulsory requirement/s are they finding it difficult to meet?
  • How does the circumstance affect their ability to comply with the requirements?
  • What is the reason?
  • What dates/times can they attend?
  • What kinds of activities could they complete?

Click the 'Save Impacted Requirement' button to confirm the information before assessing another requirement.

Is the JSCI required to be completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 1 in Table 3

9

Updating the JSCI + Read more ...

If the Confirm change button is selected and the information recorded suggests the JSCI should be reviewed, a message will display under the Further action required section prompting the PST-skilled Service Officer to Save and Exit workflow to exit the Capability Assessment.

The PST-skilled Service Officer should run the JSCI with the job seeker, following the workflow prompts to ensure they are in the appropriate stream. See Step 6 in Table 4.

Once the JSCI has been completed, the Capability Assessment should be resumed. This ensures any change in stream servicing occurs as part of the Capability Assessment outcome.

If:

  • evidence is required to verify relevant circumstances, go to Step 1 in Table 3
  • a referral for an ESAt is required, go to Step 3 in Table 3
  • all circumstances have been identified, verified and assessed, go to Step 1 in Table 5

More information required + Read more ...

Services Australia - particularly PST-skilled Service Officers conducting a Capability Assessment - must be satisfied that the circumstances disclosed by the job seeker are impacting their ability to meet the compulsory requirements in their Job Plan.

This can mean the job seeker is given an opportunity to provide evidence to verify the circumstances disclosed. This will result in the Capability Assessment being placed on hold while evidence is obtained.

  • is relevant

If evidence cannot be supplied, PST-skilled Service Officers must update the status to 'Sometimes impacts ability to comply, requirements reasonable' and record 'evidence yet to be provided to assess impact on compliance' within 'Describe the circumstances' free text section.

3

Capability Assessment and Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) required + Read more ...

Employment Services Assessments (ESAts) are required to ensure job seekers receive the most appropriate support to help them in their transition into employment based on their circumstances.

There are two types of ESAts: medical and non-medical.

To make a referral for a medical ESAt there must be medical evidence available.

Is there appropriate evidence available to support an ESAt referral?

  • Yes, complete referral for ESAt, go to Step 4
  • No, see Step 3 in Table 4

4

Capability Assessment - ESAt referral arranged + Read more ...

A referral for an ESAt must be made before finalising the Capability Assessment with the outcome 'ESAt referral arranged'.

To make a referral to ESAt:

  • use the Save and exit workflow to save information already recorded. Selecting the reason 'check information not available in the workflow'. Exiting the workflow for this reason means:
    • suspension is maintained
    • contact trigger is maintained
  • complete the Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) Referral
  • once referral for an ESAt has been completed, the PST-skilled Service Officer must:
    • resume the Capability Assessment
    • prior to finalising check the outcome is 'Not Capable – ESAt referral arranged'
    • finalise the Capability Assessment. Procedure ends here

Saving and exiting a Capability Assessment

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Capability Assessment cannot be completed during the job seeker's contact + Read more ...

Capability Assessments that cannot be completed due to information outside the workflow needing to be checked or another workflow/assessment needs to be completed, an appropriate save and exit reason must be selected.

The reason selected also determines whether the payment suspension and/or contact trigger is lifted and whether completion of the Capability Assessment is suspended for a period of time or not.

When:

  • the call drops out or is disconnected (job seeker has not terminated the call deliberately), go to Step 2
  • the job seeker is being asked to provide evidence, go to Step 3
  • the job seeker is now exempt from compulsory requirements, go to Step 4
  • the job seeker is unable to complete the Capability Assessment on day of contact, go to Step 5
  • information outside of the Capability Assessment needs to be checked or the JSCI needs to be updated, go to Step 6
  • system issues are preventing completion of the Capability Assessment, go to Step 7

2

Call drops out or is disconnected (job seeker has not terminated the call deliberately) + Read more ...

This happens when the job seeker agrees to participate in the Capability Assessment but the call drops out, or is disconnected.

Note: this reason is different from when a job seeker refuses to participate in the Capability Assessment.

PST-skilled Service Officer should attempt to make 1 outbound call to re-engage the job seeker. If this attempt is unsuccessful the workflow should be exited using 'Contact unexpectedly terminated'. This means:

  • the payment will stay suspended
  • contact trigger is maintained, meaning the job seeker cannot report until the Capability Assessment is complete
  • free text is required to explain why the Capability Assessment has not been able to be completed
  • review date required – use today's date
  • deferred notification is not required

Procedure ends here.

3

The job seeker is asked to provide evidence + Read more ...

The Capability Assessment needs to be saved and exited when the job seeker is asked to provide evidence that will likely effect the outcome.

The amount of time required to obtain and supply evidence should be assessed on a case-by-case basis. This would generally be 2 business days, but no more than 14 calendar days unless exceptional circumstances exist.

Job seekers should be encouraged to submit their evidence using the Upload documents service.

When evidence has been requested, exit the workflow by selecting 'Pending Evidence From Customer'. This will:

  • clearly document the evidence requested and the possible impact the evidence will have on the outcome (for example medical certificate required for ESAt referral)
  • enter the job seekers next reporting date within the review date field
  • select 'exit' to end the workflow
  • assist the job seeker to finalise any outstanding reporting

Once the due date for evidence is reached, a contact trigger prevents the job seeker from reporting until the Capability Assessment is finalised.

Advise the job seeker:

  • if evidence is not provided by the due date, the Capability Assessment will be completed at their next contact and be based on the information available at that time. This may mean any additional support they require (eg. an ESAt referral) cannot be arranged. They may also move into the financial penalty zone and further non-compliance may result in a reduction or cancellation of their payment
  • they may not be able to complete their outstanding reporting, see Step 4 in Table 7

4

The job seeker is now exempt from compulsory requirements + Read more ...

The Capability Assessment will be held for the duration of the exemption. It will need to be completed once the exemption has ended.

A pending Capability Assessment is automatically put on hold if the exemption is granted prior to commencing the Capability Assessment workflow.

The Capability Assessment needs to be held manually by PST-skilled Service Officers when the Capability Assessment has been commenced before the exemption has been granted.

To manually place a Capability Assessment on hold, the PST-skilled Service Officer must:

  • select the Capability Assessment workflow
  • explain the purpose of the Capability Assessment and read the privacy provisions
  • on the Capability Assessment page, for 'Is the assessment able to be completed now?' select the 'Job seeker currently exempt' option
  • use the 'Exemption Applied' reason to Save and Exit the workflow, and
  • enter the exemption period end date (note this cannot exceed 14 weeks)

This lifts the payment suspension, and removes the contact trigger until the exemption has ended.

Exiting the workflow when the job seeker is exempt from compulsory requirements means:

  • the job seeker's payment is restored
  • the contact trigger is released allowing the job seeker to report (until the hold date has been reached)
  • free text is required to explain why the Capability Assessment has not been completed at point of contact
  • the review date is the date after the exemption ends (end date + 1)

Note: where the exemption is updated to end on a different date, the Capability Assessment workflow will need to be accessed, saved and exited again, using the new exemption end date to update the review date field.

Procedure ends here.

5

Capability Assessment unable to be completed on day of contact + Read more ...

Where possible, the Capability Assessment should be completed the day the job seeker contacts.

If the job seeker contacts and the assessment cannot be completed, Services Australia should ask the job seeker to call back by 12pm the next working day to complete it.

Because the job seeker is then able to be deemed as having made arrangements to complete their Capability Assessment, they are considered as having met their re-engagement requirements.

At the initial contact, PST-skilled Service Officers will still need to:

  • explain the purpose of the Capability Assessment, and
  • read the customer the privacy provisions
  • on the Capability Assessment page, for 'Is the assessment able to be completed now?' select the 'Can't conduct assessment today' option
  • use the 'Unable to Complete on Day of Contact' reason to Save and Exit the workflow. This will lift the payment suspension, and remove the contact trigger for the current report only
  • notify the job seeker that they need to call back by 12pm the next business day

Exiting the workflow when the Capability Assessment is unable to be completed on day of contact means:

  • the payment suspension is lifted
  • the contact trigger is released (for the current report only)
  • free text is required to explain why the Capability Assessment has not been completed at point of contact
  • review date is the next business day
  • the job seeker is notified of their requirement to call back

Procedure ends here.

6

Information outside of the Capability Assessment needs to be checked or the JSCI needs to be updated + Read more ...

This option is used to save the information already gathered and recorded before exiting the workflow.

PST-skilled Service Officers may exit the workflow to check other information in the job seeker's record, or to complete other actions like a referral for an ESAt or updating the JSCI.

Once these actions are complete, the Capability Assessment workflow should be resumed and completed with the job seeker on the phone wherever possible.

Exiting the workflow for this reason means:

  • the suspension is maintained, and
  • the contact trigger is maintained

7

Unable to complete - system issues + Read more ...

This option should only be used where the job seeker contacts to have a Capability Assessment:

  • before they are due to report, and
  • system issues prevent it from being completed

Using this option will not remove payment suspension and it will not allow the job seeker to report.

For job seekers who are due to report or who have an outstanding report to complete, PST-skilled Service Officers should use the option 'Unable to complete on day of contact' when a system issue prevents the Capability Assessment from being completed, and:

  • the workflow has been checked to make sure all relevant details have been recorded; and/or
  • there is an NNU advising records affected by the problem must be reported to mySupport; and/or
  • the record has been reported to mySupport

Procedure ends here.

Finalising a Capability Assessment - Capable

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Capability Assessment Outcome - Capable of meeting requirements + Read more ...

A capable outcome means the Capability Assessment has not identified anything impacting the job seeker's ability to meet the compulsory requirements set out in the Job Plan. The Job Plan is free from errors and the requirements contained within it are reasonable and appropriate to their capabilities.

When the outcome of capable is sent back to the Provider and accepted (via the Employment Services System (ESSweb)) the job seeker will move into the financial penalty zone.

The PST-skilled Service Officer must use the talking points presented in the workflow when the outcome of capable is reached to reinforce:

  • compliance with compulsory requirements
  • making prior contact with the provider if unable to comply with requirements, and
  • the consequences of further non-compliance with compulsory requirements

2

What happens from here? Penalties, cancellation, and reclaiming payment + Read more ...

Job seekers in the financial penalty zone who meet their compulsory requirements, or have a reasonable excuse for non-compliance, for a period of 3 active months will return to the Green Zone.

Financial Penalties

Job seekers who fail to meet their requirements without a reasonable excuse, will have a financial penalty applied, losing part or all of their payment.

A first and second mutual obligation failure can be applied in a single fortnight.

A job seeker can self-accept a first and or a second mutual obligation failure when they report.

Reclaiming payment after a third mutual obligation failure

Job seekers who incur a third mutual obligation failure, will have their payment cancelled and a four week non-payment period applied. When this occurs, the job seeker will need to re-claim payment.

For further information, see:

  • Targeted Compliance Framework financial penalties and payment cancellations
  • Fully compliant job seekers returning to the green zone

Go to Step 1 in Table 7.

Finalising a Capability Assessment - Not Capable

Table 6

Step

Action

1

What happens from here? Being found 'not capable' and returning to the green zone + Read more ...

At the end of the Capability Assessment the workflow will display important messages PST-skilled Service Officers must discuss with the job seeker.

This includes:

  • clarifying the circumstances that were disclosed, a summary of how they are expected to impact, and anything the provider may need to follow up with the job seeker at their next contact
  • reminding the job seeker their provider will contact to arrange an appointment to renegotiate the Job Plan
  • confirming with the job seeker that they are still expected to meet the requirements they are capable of meeting and the importance of notifying their provider if their circumstances change
  • discussing the return to the green zone, what that means for the job seeker and the consequences of further non-compliance

If the job seeker is not capable because:

  • the Job Plan contains requirements that are inappropriate for the job seeker's current circumstances or capacity, go to Step 2
  • the JSCI was re-run during the Capability Assessment and resulted in a change in eligibility/stream, go to Step 3
  • a circumstance has been identified and it directly impacts recent non-compliance, go to Step 4
  • an ESAt is required, go to Step 5
  • an error exists in the current Job Plan, go to Step 6

If none of the above apply, go to Step 1 in Table 7.

2

Not Capable – unable to meet one or more requirements in current Job Plan + Read more ...

This is the appropriate outcome when the Capability Assessment has:

  • identified new or updated existing job seeker circumstances directly affecting their ability to meet requirements in the current Job Plan
  • job seeker circumstances are verified with appropriate evidence
  • the impact of the job seeker's circumstances clearly indicates they are unable to meet 1 or more requirements in their current Job Plan
  • recommended how the Job Plan can be adjusted so the job seeker is able to meet their requirements by recording:
    • which requirements should be adjusted
    • why the requirements need to be adjusted, and
    • how the requirements should be adjusted

When the ESSweb receives this Capability Assessment outcome the job seeker will return to the green zone.

The provider will receive the following information when this outcome is used:

  • job seeker circumstances recorded/updated during the assessment
  • the requirement(s) in the Job Plan that require change and the reasons why

When this outcome is received, providers are required to update the Job Plan within 10 business days.

Go to Step 7.

3

Not Capable - Change in stream + Read more ...

This is the appropriate outcome when the Capability Assessment has:

  • identified a change in circumstances and the JSCI has been re-run during the Capability Assessment, and
  • the system has changed the job seeker's servicing stream as a result

When this is the Capability Assessment outcome, the job seeker will return to the green zone.

The provider will be advised:

  • the job seeker circumstances recorded/updated during the assessment including description, impact and evidence

Go to Step7.

4

Not Capable - New circumstances identified that directly impacted recent non-compliance + Read more ...

This is the appropriate outcome when:

  • the Capability Assessment has identified ongoing circumstances are impacting compliance and these were not identified or addressed at the time of the most recent Capability Interview
  • the impact of the job seeker's circumstances has been assessed and deemed as significant in terms of their ability to meet their requirements
  • job seeker circumstances have been verified with appropriate evidence

When this is the Capability Assessment outcome, the job seeker will return to the green zone.

The provider will be advised:

  • the job seeker circumstances recorded/updated during the assessment including description, impact and evidence
  • sufficient and appropriate evidence has been recorded, confirming the job seeker circumstances

Go to Step 7.

5

Not Capable - ESAt referral arranged + Read more ...

This is the appropriate outcome when:

  • the job seeker has been referred to either a non-medical or medical ESAt during the Capability Assessment, and
  • appropriate action has been taken to arrange an ESAt appointment

When this is the Capability Assessment outcome, the job seeker will return to the green zone.

The provider will be advised:

  • the job seeker circumstances recorded/updated during the assessment including description, impact and evidence
  • the ESAt referral details

Go to Step 7.

6

Not Capable - Job Plan error + Read more ...

This is the appropriate outcome when the Capability Assessment has:

  • identified a requirement in the Job Plan that contains an error, and
  • recommended how the Job Plan can be corrected by recording:
    • which requirements should be corrected or removed, and
    • why the requirements need to be corrected or removed

When this is the Capability Assessment outcome, the job seeker will return to the green zone.

The provider will be advised:

  • the requirement(s) that needs to be adjusted/removed and reason the change is required

When this outcome is received, providers are required to update the Job Plan within 10 business days.

Go to Step 7.

7

Job seekers returning to the green zone + Read more ...

When a job seeker is returned to the green zone by the ESSweb after a Capability Assessment outcome, any current demerits are reset to zero.

The provider is then responsible for re-negotiating the Job Plan with the changed circumstances and related evidence being taken into consideration.

Action

Finalising the assessment – completing the last page + Read more ...

Before finalising the Capability Assessment, the Service Officer must ensure the outcome recorded is appropriate to the circumstances presented by the job seeker during the assessment.

Where the Service Officer is uncertain about the Capability Assessment outcome they should consult with PST VSQ.

The last page of the Capability Assessment needs to be completed to include details of the Service Officer completing the Capability Assessment.

  • If additional staff were consulted during the Capability Assessment, their details (logon, surname, initial) and position should also be inserted in the Position: field on the Capability Assessment Outcome page
  • The date the Capability Assessment was conducted

Prompt: ensure existing circumstances impacting compliance are up to date + Read more ...

Other action required + Read more ...

The PST-skilled Service Officer will need to record if any of the following action will be taken because of information discussed during the Capability Assessment:

  • a tip-off is required as income has not been declared or circumstances the job seeker disclosed would affect rate of payment
  • a claim for alternative payment is appropriate based on circumstances disclosed during the assessment

4

Lifting the participation suspension + Read more ...

When ESSweb receives a Capability Assessment outcome, the re-engagement requirement to attend a Capability Assessment is automatically finalised.

  • If the job seeker has complied with all outstanding reconnection requirements, ESSweb will automatically send an electronic transaction to lift the payment suspension
  • If other reconnection requirements are still outstanding, for example as a result of failures incurred after the Capability Assessment was triggered, payment will remain suspended until the reconnection requirement is met
    • Check the Participation Compliance Workflows Summary screen, Completed/Provider to action section and where there is further outstanding compliance action with status Yet to meet requirement, advise the job seeker to contact their provider

5

Crisis interventions required + Read more ...

Once the Capability Assessment is complete, if the job seeker requires crisis intervention via social work, a general referral may be made where the following issues are identified:

  • family and domestic violence
  • mental health issues
  • customer is in crisis and has complex personal circumstances requiring immediate social work intervention
  • customer in receipt of Youth Allowance (YA), who presents with one or more risk factors listed below:
    • family and domestic violence
    • recent homelessness
    • mental health issues
    • identified as 'At Risk' by a social worker in a Display on Access (DOA) DOC

For further information, see Social work service referral.
