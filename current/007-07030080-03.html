3

Medicare enrolment only + Read more ...

Customers can enrol a newborn for Medicare online.

Customers can lodge a FA081 to enrol the child in Medicare. This includes the Medicare Safety Net and My Health Record. The FA081 must be complete and signed.

The customer can also provide other acceptable proof of birth and lodge a Medicare Enrolment Application (MS004) form. The Resources page contains a link to this form.

If the completed FA081 indicates to only register the newborn for Medicare:

  • Cancel the families scan activity in Customer First. Ensure the MAENROL DOC remains open. If the original MAENROL DOC closes:
    • create this manually in Customer First for processing by Medicare
    • ensure the Date of Receipt (DOR) matches the original MAENROL DOC. See Fast Note: Auto Text > Families > Update > Medicare Enrolment
  • Check if the customer's partner already has a claim for payments. If yes, use the proof of birth to assess the claim. Note: customers claiming PPL/DAP must advise if applied to register the birth with their state, territory or agency

Despite what the customer states on the form, Centrelink and Medicare can use this proof of birth.

Procedure ends here.

4

No claim for child on record + Read more ...

Check the FA081 to see if the customer wants to claim payments for their newborn child. Check their response to the PPL question and the Medicare-only question.

If the customer lodges a FA081 and is:

  • not FTB current (FTB/CUR or FTB/CZR) and does not have a claim, go to Step 5
  • FTB current (FTB/CUR or FTB/CZR), and
    • wants to claim PPL, go to Step 5
    • does not want to claim PPL (or rejected PPL) and has a complete FA081, reassess FTB in Customer First. Procedure ends here
    • does not have a pre-birth claim and the FA081 is incomplete or needs more information go to Step 5
    • does not have a pre-birth claim or acceptable POB (FA081 back page), go to Step 5

If the customer is not FTB current (FTB/CUR or FTBCZR) and lodges other acceptable POB, contact to discuss claim options.

In Customer First:

  • create a manual Q777 letter via the Letter Details (LD) screen. The Resources page contains the instructions and suggested letter text
  • cancel the scanned image. Record details on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

5

Customer claiming payments + Read more ...

Invite the customer to claim payments for the child.

Staff can inform a customer of contact by phone through sending a desktop message. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging.

To send a pre-call SMS, in Process Direct:

  • go to the Correspondence (CORRO) screen via Super Key
  • select the twisty below the Correspondence History table
  • in the Issue Correspondence table, select Pre Call SMS to Customer
  • select Submit
  • A Pre Call Alert SMS notification will be issued to the customer. View this in the Correspondence History table
  • Allow a lead time of 15 minutes in case there is a delay

Call the customer to discuss claim options.

If contact with the customer is successful:

  • Encourage the customer to lodge an online claim. This is the preferred service offer. Ensure customers have 'active' online account access before asking them to claim online
  • If unreasonable for the customer to claim online, issue a Claim for Parental Leave Pay and Family Assistance (FA100)
  • Extra help is available for remote Indigenous customers making a claim
  • Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) is available for families in crisis

If the customer is not available at the time of contact, check the address details and update if needed. If the customer lodges:

  • an FA081: issue a Q776 letter in Customer First using the FA081 Customer to claim Q776 guided procedure
  • other acceptable POB: issue a Q999 letter in Customer First. See Resources page for letter text

For more information, see Information requests for FTB, PPL and DAP claims and Add Newborn activities.

In Customer First:

Complete the families scan activity. Ensure the MAENROL DOC remains open. If the original MAENROL DOC closes:

  • Create this manually for processing by Medicare
  • Ensure that the Date of Receipt (DOR) matches that of the original MAENROL DOC. See Fast Note: Auto Text > Families > Update > Medicare Enrolment

Check if the customer's partner has a claim for payments. If yes, use the proof of birth for their claim. Note: customers claiming PPL/DAP must advise of application to register the birth with their state, territory or agency.

Discuss potential entitlement to another Centrelink payment (e.g. Parenting Payment (PP)) with the customer or send a Q999. See Resources page for letter text.

Record details on a DOC in Customer First.

Procedure ends here.

6

FTB/PPL claim assessed pending proof of birth

Customers using the 'Add newborn child' service online or the 'Add child' service via the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app + Read more ...

For customers whose pre-birth claim is pending proof of birth and who used the 'Add newborn child' service online or the 'Add child' service via the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app to provide child details and upload proof of birth:

  • Access the work item in Process Direct by selecting Ready via the Inbox tile or Search Work Item via the Inbox>Work Item Search
  • Complete pre-processing checks. For more information see Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments
  • Complete Add Newborn activity updates
  • Go to Step 7

Customers who have lodged an FA081 + Read more ...

If the customer has lodged an FA081, check that all questions have been answered in full. If:

  • minimal questions have been missed and the child's name has been provided, collect the missing information over the phone. Assess the Add Newborn using the new information along with the information from the initial claim
  • the customer has the original FA081, ask the customer to complete the missing questions and upload the fully completed FA081

Once the FA081 is completed in full, create a Social Application (SOA) via the Process Direct landing page. See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions for more information. The Add Newborn can be assessed in the newly created activity using the information from the FA081 along with the information from the initial claim.

Contact the customer if there are any concerns that their circumstances have changed since the initial claim was submitted.

Go to Step 7.

Lodged/uploaded FA081 with back page only, or without the back page and without child's name/details or only other acceptable proof of birth provided + Read more ...

Contact the customer and advise them:

  • to access the 'Add newborn child' service online or the 'Add child' service via the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app and answer all questions displayed, or
  • if the customer has the original FA081, complete it in full and upload all pages of the completed FA081

Note: if the customer provides the back page of the FA081 with the Doctor/Midwife section complete, but the customer section at the top of the back page is incomplete, the back page is acceptable proof of birth providing the full serial number is also included.

If the customer is not available, issue a Q777 in Customer First about completing their claim online. See approved letter text for Q777 on the Resources page.

Go to Step 7.

For PPL claims when processing the Add newborn service + Read more ...

The system checks if the Nominated Start Date (NSD) is between:

  • the Expected Date of Birth (EDOB), and
  • Date of Birth (DOB) inclusive

If it is, the system updates the NSD to the DOB. This is the earliest possible start date for that child.

Do not update in a separate activity if the NSD in the pre-birth claim falls between the EDOB and DOB.

Note: where proof of birth has not been provided, claim will automatically NEF-POB 56 days after the nominated start date.

Go to Step 7.

7

Action the Add Newborn activity + Read more ...

Prior to completing the necessary updates in the Add Newborn activity, on the Transaction Summary (TS) screen, link the child and complete any partner updates. See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions.

Action the Add Newborn activity:

  • Select Process
  • In the customer’s record, check/update the following screens from the SAP Warnings and Errors (SWE) screen:
    • Child Claim Override (CHOC)
    • Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM)
    • FAO Early Claims Summary (FECD)
    • Medicare Information (MEI)
    • For more information on updating these screens see Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments
  • In the child's record check/update the following screens from CHTS on the SWE screen:
    • Child Registration Details (CHR)
    • Child in Primary Care (CIPC)
    • Newborn Supplement Questionnaire (NBSQ)
    • Child in Care (CHC)
    • Customer Person Details (CPDS)
    • Shared Care Assessment (SCA) - FTB only
    • Care Alignment Details Screen (CADDS) - FTB only
    • Change Child (CCH)
    • Child Relationships Details (CHRE)
    • Residency task Selector (RETS)
    • Travel Outside of Australia (TOAS) (view only)
    • Residency Details (RSLEG)
    • Country of Residence (CRES)
    • Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME)
    • For more information on updating these screens see Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments

Note: as the 'Add Newborn' Service is not a claim, any duplicate 'Add newborn child' service activities completed by the customer for the same child should not be deemed not effective (NEF). Complete all updates within the first initial 'Add newborn child' service. Change the status of any duplicate 'Add Newborn' activities to 'Not Required'. See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions for more information on changing the status of a work item.

If there is a manual follow up (MFU) on the record that is preventing processing, action via the Activity List (AL) screen in Customer First.

To finalise the Add Newborn activity, see Table 2.

8

DAP claim assessed pending proof of birth

Partner is claiming FTB/PPL + Read more ...

Customers whose partner is claiming FTB/PPL for the newborn child and a DAP pre-birth claim is assessed 'pending proof of birth' do not need to provide any additional information as their claim will automatically complete when their partner's PPL/FTB claim/s are assessed.

Check the Entitlement (ELD) screen to confirm the DAP payment has finalised as part of the PPL/FTB claim processing. If this payment does not appear, see Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims.

Note: the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen for a DAP only customer should not be updated. If the CHC screen was incorrectly updated as part of the claim, delete the 'ICC' (in customer care) line.

Go to Table 2.

Partner is not claiming FTB/PPL + Read more ...

Customers whose partner is not claiming FTB/PPL for the newborn child and the pre-birth DAP claim has been finalised and have:

  • lodged a fully completed FA081 or other acceptable proof of birth, such as a birth certificate, see Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims
  • lodged an incomplete FA081 or the back page of the FA081 only, contact the customer to request a fully completed FA081 or alternative Proof of birth (for example, birth certificate) for the newborn. If the customer cannot be contacted or provide these documents, send the customer a Q777 (via the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure) in Customer First asking the relevant questions to complete the customer’s DAP claim. The Resources page contains examples of Q777 text. Note: this step is only to be used for DAP customers who have a pre-birth claim DAP/PND-POB

Procedure ends here.

9

Pre-birth claim finalised as 'NEF' + Read more ...

If the Add Newborn activity is for:

  • PPL only, status - NEF/PCB:
    • using the pre-birth claim and the FA081 form, the PPL claim can be re-indexed in Process Direct. This will be from the date the agency receives the new information. Once the claim has been reindexed, update the date of receipt using > Update DOR. For more information, see Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
  • DAP, status - NEF/POB
    • the DAP claim can be re-indexed in Process Direct from the date the agency receives the new information. Once the claim has been reindexed, update the date of receipt using > Update DOR. See Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)

FCC Processing Staff, to re-index a claim:

  • Go to Process Direct Landing Page
  • Select Inbox
  • In the Work Item Search, enter the required fields:
    • Claim ID
    • Claim in Main Category, or
    • CRN in the CRN field
  • Select Go
  • Select the claim displayed in the Work Item. Check the claim status is NEF or REJ
  • Select > Re-index claim
  • Re-index will generate a new receipt number

Note: if a claim was submitted and made Not Effective in Customer First, the claim must be re-indexed by selecting the Re-index button at the top of the Social Application to reassess the claim in Customer First.

Procedure ends here.

10

Pre-birth claim lodged, not yet processed

Paper pre-birth claim + Read more ...

Using the pre-birth Social Application (SOA) shell, the claim can be assessed as a post-birth claim using the information from the pre-birth paper claim (FA100/FA080) in addition to the FA081/Proof of birth.

Determine the correct date of receipt (DOR):

  • DOR = Child’s date of birth (DOB), proof of birth received less than/equal to 28 days after child’s DOB
  • DOR = Date proof of birth (POB) received, where more than 28 days after the child’s DOB

Go to Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments.

Online pre-birth claim + Read more ...

If the primary claimant is the biological father or partner of the birth mother and they have submitted a pre-birth claim, prior to processing, do more checks to work out if the claimant meets the exceptional circumstances criteria, see Step 2 Table 1 in Eligibility for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) as a primary claimant.

Attempt to contact the claimant to determine if the claim has been incorrectly made (for example, customer meant to claim DAP instead of PPL).

Before calling Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) may be used to advise the customer they will be receiving a phone call. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging.

If the claim was incorrectly lodged, help the claimant to withdraw the claim. See Withdrawal of claims.

Otherwise:

  • Process the pre-birth claim as a post birth claim in Process Direct
  • Change the status of the Online Pre-Birth claim to Not Required in Customer First and cancel the activity on the Activity List (AL) screen
  • In Process Direct, assess the pre-birth claim and proof of birth by creating a Social Application (SOA). See Process Direct Navigation, common screens and functions
  • Determine the correct date of receipt (DOR):
    • DOR = Child’s date of birth (DOB), proof of birth received less than/equal to 28 days after child’s DOB
    • DOR = Date proof of birth (POB) received, where more than 28 days after the child’s DOB
  • Manually enter relevant claim data using the information from the Pre-birth and post birth claims and Proof of Birth provided. See Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments

Incomplete FA081 + Read more ...

If the customer has lodged an incomplete FA081, contact them to collect the missing information. If the customer cannot be contacted, print the incomplete FA081 lodged and send this with a Q777 via the Request Documents Q777 Guided Procedure in Customer First to the customer to complete the missing questions. See the Resources page for suggested letter text.

Other scenarios + Read more ...

The Resources page contains a date of receipt matrix for all other scenarios.

Record action details on a DOC in Customer First.

4

ISP customers granted PPL/DAP + Read more ...

PPL/DAP is treated as income for ISP. Granting PPL/DAP may have resulted in a reduced ISP rate, an employment income nil rate period or ISP cancelling.

If PPL/DAP is granted from a date in the past, an ISP debt may have been calculated. Attempt to make one outbound contact to affected customers to discuss potential payment impacts claiming PPL/DAP will have on their income support payment. Where appropriate, the customer may change their nominated start date to another date in the future to prevent being overpaid their income support payment.

If contact is unsuccessful complete processing and action the debt shell. See General debt raising information.

To enable ISP entitlement to be followed up at the end of the PPL or DAP period, in Customer First create a review DOC on the ISP customer's record using the Fast Note template Families> Claims> PPL/DAP income and ISP.

Add the appropriate PPL or DAP keyword and the payment type as per the keyword information provided on the Resources page in Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments.

Select DOC on AL screen and enter the PPL or DAP end date + 1 in the Resubmit date field to put the started DOC activity on hold until the end of the PPL or DAP period.

See Resumption of income support payments (ISP) after the end of Parental Leave Pay (PPL) or Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) payments.

  • Use the 'Add newborn child' service online or the 'Add vhild' service via the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app:
    • access these claims through the Search Service Request menu in Customer First, process the Add newborn child activity and do pre-process checks

    • Use the most appropriate Fast Note to record the PPL claim outcome - auto text Families > Claims, and select the relevant claim option
