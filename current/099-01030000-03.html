This page contains information about how to manage requests for information about a deceased person, how to gather the information required to respond to the request and how to prepare and issue the correct letter.

Note: written requests for information about deceased customers are handled by National Business Gateway (NBG) staff in a dedicated processing team.

On this page:

Calls received about customers who are deceased

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Before taking the call + Read more ...

Before the Service Officer has answered the call, the message 'Deceased CRN entered' will display in the Services Australia Workspace, advising the person is calling about a deceased customer.

This is to alert the Service Officer that:

  • this is a sensitive call
  • information about the deceased person (even whether or not they were a customer) must not be provided without sufficient evidence of their authority
  • the caller may not be an executor/administrator of the estate and may be calling to seek bereavement assistance for themselves or another person

2

Death/Executor (DEA) screen + Read more ...

On the DEA screen, has the death of the customer already been notified and updated (i.e. the Date of Death and Date of Notification fields completed)?

  • Yes,
    • Is the caller identifying themselves as the executor/administrator of the deceased customer’s estate?
      • Yes, go to Step 4
      • No, go to Step 3
  • No, see Confirmation of the death of an adult or child

3

Caller is not the executor/administrator of the estate + Read more ...

Note: do not provide any information about the deceased person, including whether or not they were a customer.

Does the caller require bereavement assistance?

If unsure, ask the caller 'Do you need any information about payments or support while you are grieving?'

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, advise the caller that more information can only be provided to the executor/administrator of the deceased customer’s estate after providing sufficient evidence of their authority

For more information, go to Step 4.

4

Caller is the executor/administrator of the estate + Read more ...

Have the executor/administrator details been entered on the DEA screen?

  • Yes, check if an Executor/Administrator Request for information form (SS524), with supporting evidence, has been lodged and uploaded to the deceased customer’s record. See Locating digital images via Document Tools in Customer First. Go to Step 5
  • No, direct the caller to the Services Australia website to search for and download a copy of the Executor/Administrator Request for information form (SS524)

If the caller is also enquiring about bereavement services, go to Step 6.

5

SS524 form has been received + Read more ...

The Executor/Administrator Request for information form (SS524) will be allocated for processing. The requested information will be issued to the executor/administrator via letter.

If the executor/administrator has additional questions, advise the caller that a new SS524 will need to be lodged.

Procedure ends here.

6

Enquiring about bereavement services, including eligibility for payments after the death of a loved one + Read more ...

Direct the caller to the Services Australia website and to search 'bereavement' for the relevant pages. This will provide the person with information about what help is available after someone dies.

In most cases, they will not need to submit a claim for Bereavement Payment; the amount will be calculated after notification of death and will be paid if they are an eligible income support customer.

For more information, see About Bereavement services.

There is the option to offer the caller the services of a social worker where appropriate.

The caller may wish to obtain a copy of the Needing help after someone has died? (HD001) pamphlet, which is available at their local service centre.

Procedure ends here.

Managing requests for information about a deceased person

Table 2

Step

Description

1

Check death action + Read more ...

Has the person been recorded as deceased on the system?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, process the death action of the customer, then go to Step 2

Note: for executor requests - where a customer record cannot be located on the system, complete an outbound call to the executor:

  • Tell them that the record cannot be located. Ask if there is any additional information that they can provide to assist with record searches. For example, other names or omitted details on the form
  • If no record is found, confirm with the executor that this is the case and the procedure ends here. Verbal discussion is sufficient
  • Only complete 2 outbound call attempts to check the information/confirm no record
  • If outbound contact with the executor is unsuccessful, send a Q999 on the executor’s record, where available. See Table 5 for suggested text to be used
  • If the record is found, go to Step 2

2

Determine action required + Read more ...

A request for information from a third party about a deceased person may be received via letter or lodgement of an Executor/Administrator Request for information form (SS524). Work is allocated via Workload Manager (WLM). Third parties who make verbal requests for information about a deceased person must be advised to lodge a SS524 form with proof of their authority.

Where the work item is:

  • a scanned request for information letter or SS524 form that has not yet been actioned, go to Step 3
  • an ‘STA’ letter activity on the Activity List (AL) screen that has been drafted and requires approval, see Step 2 in Table 4
  • a follow up of finalisation of debts, see Step 3 in Table 3

If a third party requests any returned payments be issued to them, see Replacing payment made to a deceased customer where payment was returned from financial institution.

3

Determine what Service Delivery brand this request is for + Read more ...

  • Child Support information requests, see Deceased Child Support Customer Management
  • Medicare information requests, see Release of information for Medicare
  • International agreement information requests, see Agreement liaisons, NZ CICs and exchange of information
  • Aged Care Means Test Assessment (MTA) information requests, go to Step 4
  • Centrelink information requests, go to Step 5

4

Aged Care Means Test Assessment (MTA) information requests + Read more ...

Refer requests for information about Aged Care MTA to the Aged Care (RCA) team. See Office Locator for the team’s contact details. The Resources page has a link to Officer Locator.

The Aged Care (RCA) team can provide copies of any letters relating to Aged Care or manual assessments/reassessments of existing MTAs where required.

To refer the request to the Aged Care (RCA) team, create an open work item using Fast Note:

  • Select Auto Text use Aged Care > Enquiry > Aged Care Discharged CR – Man Let Req
  • Select Confirm
  • Edit auto text and include information about written request received and action required
  • Select Continue
  • Complete any existing work item associated with this request

Procedure ends here.

5

Request for information received + Read more ...

Note: these requests are generally handled by the National Business Gateway (NBG) Executor Request team. If the SS524 form has been lodged this work should flow directly to them.

Information must only be disclosed to:

  • the executor as named in the will
  • the Public Trustee
  • a court
  • the administrator of the estate
  • state and territory government departments assessing burial/funeral assistance

Is the person/organisation requesting the information in 1 of the above categories?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

Information Release Section referrals

In some specific situations, the Information Release Section will need to make a decision about whether information can be provided to the requestor.

Only refer to the following requests:

  • from police, the Coroner, a Royal Commission or a court/tribunal
  • where identity information about the deceased person is sought (such as their next of kin) and the request is made separate to any request for a statement of pension
  • from a life insurance or superannuation company requesting information in order to contact a beneficiary or determine the policy holder
  • from a state or territory government department requesting information about assets held by the deceased person to determine eligibility for funeral assistance

Does the request relate to any of the above circumstances?

  • Yes,
    • Email the request to the Information Access Team
    • Record progress on the deceased customer's record using the Deceased Customer - Executor Request Fast Note. Select Auto Text use TPO – Third Party Organisations > UPD – Update
    • Close the associated work item/s
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

International agreement countries + Read more ...

Requests from an agreement partner or approved liaison for information about the following must be referred to Centrelink International Services:

  • date of death
  • executor/administrator details
  • last known address
  • surviving partner
  • assessment of entitlement to foreign pension and any recovery debts

Does the request relate to any of the above circumstances?

  • Yes,
  • Email the request to the CIS Helpdesk
  • Record progress on the deceased customer's record using the Deceased Customer - Executor Request Fast Note. Select Auto Text use TPO – Third Party Organisations > UPD – Update
  • Close the associated work item/s
  • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Check proof of authority + Read more ...

Proof of executor or administrator authority is always required before any information is released or follow-up action is taken to contact or advise the estate on matters such as debt or payments owed.

The following documents are accepted as proof of authority:

  • executor as named in the will - copy and store the first and last pages of the deceased customer's will
  • letter from the Public Trustee or a solicitor stating they are administering the estate
  • a court order
  • letters of administration from the administrator of the estate

Note: if a letter is received from a family member or individual (who is not a solicitor) stating they are administering the estate, it is not proof and 1 of the above documents is required.

Record on a DOC on the deceased customer's record any information about disputed executor or administrator roles.

Has sufficient evidence been provided?

  • Yes:
    • record the executor or administrator details on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen and on a DOC on the deceased customer's record
    • proceed to Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Reject the request for information + Read more ...

The request for information must be rejected, as Services Australia has no authority to release the information.

Complete an outbound call to the executor:

  • tell them that as evidence of the executor’s authority is lacking, Services Australia cannot provide the information requested
  • only complete 2 outbound call attempts to advise the executor the information requested cannot be provided
  • if outbound contact with the executor is unsuccessful send a Q999 on the deceased customer’s record, see Table 5 for suggested text to be used

Record the outcome on the deceased customer's record using the Deceased Customer - Executor Request Fast Note select Auto Text use TPO – Third Party Organisations > UPD – Update.

Procedure ends here.

Gathering details for an information request

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Request for information received + Read more ...

If the request is for information about:

  • Services Australia owing money to the estate (any uncollected pension or benefit entitlement) and amount, go to Step 2
  • the deceased person owing money to Services Australia at the time of death and the amount, go to Step 3
  • if the agency will be making a claim against the estate (if there is an undetermined/determined debt) and the amount, go to Step 3
  • if there is an outstanding request for an explanation or application for a formal review of a decision or appeal, go to Step 6
  • an income and asset statement, go to Step 7
  • a statement of payment for the current or previous financial year, go to Step 8
  • any other matter, go to Step 9

2

Payments owed to the deceased customer's account + Read more ...

Gather information for the response letter. Check the customer's record:

  • Payment Summary (PS) screen for rejected or returned payments
  • Document List (DL) screen for money owed to the deceased customer
  • Outstanding activities that will result in arrears being paid
  • Outstanding claims that will result in a payment

For more information, see the relevant Operational Blueprint for the deceased customer's payment type. For example:

  • Death of a single pensioner
  • Death of a single social security benefit customer
  • Death of an income support customer or their partner

3

Check for undetermined debts + Read more ...

Undetermined debts will generally be identified by an ‘STA’ debt activity on the deceased customer's Activity List (AL) screen. However, there may be situations where a recoverable debt was finalised no debt (FND) to allow the death action to be updated. Where this occurs, the debt will not be raised until 4 weeks after the customer's death.

To identify these undetermined debts, check the deceased customer's:

  • Future Activity List (FAL) screen, or
  • Document List (DL) screen

For more information, see Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer.

Does the deceased customer have any undetermined debts?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Investigate the record + Read more ...

Additional information about the deceased customer may be required.

Obtain the total balance of all debts owed by the customer’s estate from the Debt List (OPDL) screen. This information is required before a written request for information can be sent to the customer's estate.

Is further information required from the customer’s estate?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Investigate all undetermined debts + Read more ...

Before a written response can be sent in relation to the enquiry, all undetermined debts for the deceased customer must be assessed and raised, waived or finalised 'no debt' as appropriate (see Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer):

  • Create an open work item using Fast Note with the following information:
    • Select Auto text, use Debts > DMB Raising > Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request
    • Select Confirm
    • Request urgent finalisation of all undetermined debts to enable the Providing Info to the executor about deceased (Q126) letter to be issued
      Note: do not create another open work item if there is already one on the deceased customer's record. Consider contacting the Debt Raising team to follow up finalisation of debts
  • Finalise the ‘Executor Request’ work item
  • Record an open work item using the Deceased Customer - Executor Request Fast Note. Select Auto text, use TPO – Third Party Organisations > UPD – Update
  • HOLD this work item for 14 days to follow up determination of debts
  • Procedure ends here, pending determination of debts

6

Outstanding request for an explanation or application for a formal review of decision + Read more ...

Check the deceased customer's record:

  • Document List (DL) screen for any outstanding requests for explanations, reviews or appeals
  • Appeals (APL) system for any open APL records

Only respond to the enquiry about the deceased customer's account to advise if there is/is not an outstanding request for an explanation, application for a formal review or appeal. Do not give advice about the progress or outcome. This will happen as part of the explanation, formal review or appeal process.

Go to Step 9.

7

Income and assets statement + Read more ...

For an overview of income and assets for pensions, go to the Pension Income and Asset Summary (PIAS) screen.

For income, check all associated screens in the customer’s record, such as:

  • Income from employment (EANS)
  • Profit from self-employment/business income (REBS)
  • Financial Investments (IVIS)
  • Income streams/annuities (SUPS)
  • Real Estate/Business Income (REBS)
  • Attributed Income from Trust or Company (TRCIAS)
  • Foreign Income (FIPS)
  • Other Income (OIN)

For assets, check all associated screens in the customer's record, such as:

  • Accommodation Details (AC)
  • Financial Investment Summary (IVIS)
  • Other Assets Summary (OASS)
  • Trust/Company Income/Assets Summary (TRCIAS)
  • Real Estate/Business Summary (REBS)
  • Savings Summary (SVS)

Check for exempt assets, for example non-assessable assets, on the following screens:

  • Pension Annuities Summary (SUPS)
  • Managed Investment Summary (MIS)
  • Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID)

Has other information about the deceased customer been requested?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

8

Current or previous financial year statement/income details + Read more ...

Collect the following information for the response letter. For payment details about:

  • For previous financial year/s, view previous tax year details via the Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen. Select the relevant year to get the income information, including payment start and end dates. Note: any payments made after the customer’s death will not be included in the TXGS screen, as they are not taxable. For example, final payment paid to single pensioner’s estate up to EPED in which pensioner died are not included in TXGS
  • For current financial year, refer to the Tax Year to Date (TXYTD) screen. The end date will be the date the customer was last paid to as per the Payment Summary (PS) screen. Taxable and non-taxable payments will show, plus any tax deductions and Remote Area Allowance (RAA) amounts. All payments made to the deceased customer after their death, are NOT taxable. As a result, should NOT be included in the Financial year income statement
  • Taxable and reportable tax-exempt payments or components of payments will show, plus any tax deductions and Remote Area Allowance (RAA) amounts
  • Education Entry Payment (EdEP), Quarterly Energy Supplement or any other one-off payments, view the One Off Payments (OOP) screen
  • Tax File Number or any exemptions, select the Tax File Number tab from the CRN/BP link in Customer First (the CRN/BP link may need to be selected twice to generate the Tax File Number tab)

Has other information about the deceased customer been requested?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

9

Requests for other information + Read more ...

In limited cases, the third party may request details about other customer information.

If the third party makes a broad request or for excessive amounts of information, seek technical assistance. Go to Step 11.

Check with the Information Access Team for permission to release the information, if there is any doubt whether the information can be released.

Some examples of what may be requested, and where to find the information are:

  • Next of kin/dependents, check the Link Summary (LS) screen for any partner or children. The LS screen will also display any links to carers related to the customer
  • Date and place of birth, check the Customer Personal Details (CPD) screen. The Identity Summary screen may have birth certificate details recorded; if so, the state or territory of issue will be displayed
  • Former names, check the Customer Personal Details Summary (CPDS) screen
  • Former addresses, check Address Summary (ADS) screen
  • Date of arrival in Australia, check Country of Residence (CRES), Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) and Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) and Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screens
  • Centrepay deductions, see Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer
  • Bank details, check the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen
  • Bereavement payments, check the Bereavement Payment Information (BVI) screen
  • Archived information, access Archiving and Culling Engine

10

Check all information gathered + Read more ...

Has all requested information been gathered?

Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4

  • No, go to Step 11

11

Request for large amounts of information + Read more ...

If the third party makes a broad request or for excessive amounts of information:

  • contact them to clarify what they need
  • only complete 2 outbound call attempts to clarify the information requested
  • if large amounts of information are still required, advise the requestor that they will need to lodge a Freedom of Information request
  • if the requestor cannot be contacted, reject the request:
    • send a Q999 on the deceased customer’s record. See Table 5 for suggested text
    • record the outcome on the deceased customer's record using the Deceased Customer - Executor Request Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Third Party Organisations > UPD – Update
  • if technical assistance is required, go to Step 12

12

Seek technical assistance + Read more ...

When unable to obtain details to provide the requested response, seek technical support.

For complex cases unable to be resolved after seeking technical support:

  • escalate to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk, the Resources page includes a link under Contacts
  • use the 'CSAEnquiry' type Bereavement unless another reason is more appropriate

Where required, place the work item on hold pending advice and record a DOC on the deceased customer's record, including the action that has been taken.

Once all information has been received, see Step 1 in Table 4

Preparing and issuing response letters

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Determine which type of letter to send + Read more ...

Once all the required information has been gathered, determine which letter type/s will be sent in response to the request for information about the deceased person.

Note: depending on the information requested, more than 1 letter may need to be sent.

Select the correct letter from:

  • Providing Info to the executor about deceased (Q126), used for:
    • payments owed to the customer's estate

2

Prepare advice letter/s + Read more ...

  • Do not finalise/issue the letter
  • Use the correct letter code, either Q126, QTAXCH or Q999
  • If more than 1 letter is required, create each letter separately

See Creating an Online Advice (OLA), including within an existing activity.

Is the letter being prepared a QTAXCH letter?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Prepare QTAXCH letter + Read more ...

When preparing the QTAXCH letter:

  • In the pension supplement field, for:
    • taxable payments such as Age Pension, enter the text 'taxable component included in taxable pension paid'
    • non-taxable payments such as Disability Support Pension (DSP), enter the text 'non-taxable'
  • If tax rebate codes are unknown or not applicable, record accordingly

4

Check delegation levels are met + Read more ...

Delegation to release information about a deceased person sits at the APS4 level for Smart Centre and service centre staff. See the Resources page for links to the Delegation Instruments.

Letters can be prepared by staff members at all levels, but before being issued, must be approved by a staff member with the appropriate delegation.

Smart Centre and service centre staff at the APS4 level or above have the delegation to both prepare and issue the letter without seeking further approval.

Is the Service Officer preparing the letter at the APS4 level or above?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Delegated officer to approve letter + Read more ...

Where possible, a staff member at the same site who has the appropriate delegation (APS4 level or above) must check and issue the letter.

In limited cases, where a staff member with the appropriate delegation is not available:

  • Add keyword EXCREQ to the draft Online Letter (OLA) on the Activity List (AL) screen, and
  • Record a DOC with the action taken using the Deceased Customer - Executor Request Fast Note. Select Auto Text use TPO – Third Party Organisations > UPD – Update

For the Service Officer:

  • who prepared the letter but is not delegated to approve or issue the letter, procedure ends here
  • with delegated authority to approve and issue the letter, go to Step 6

6

Approve and issue letter + Read more ...

If the approving staff member (APS4 level or above) did not prepare the letter, they must check the information to confirm the details are correct before issuing.

Issue the letter from the deceased customer's record.

To issue the letter:

  • Key 'FIN'alise in the Command field and press [Enter]. The system will prompt if any required fields are not completed - make any updates and key 'FIN'alise in the Command field then press [Enter]
  • If more than 1 letter is to be issued, print the letters locally and send together in 1 envelope
  • Once the letter is ‘FIN’alised, see Changing settings to print a letter or electronic message on a local printer to change the letter from central to local

See Creating an Online Advice (OLA), including within an existing activity.

7

Determine if 'Release of Information' web form is required + Read more ...

Scan the request for information to the deceased customer's record.

Was the request for information from the executor or administrator of the deceased customer's estate?

  • Yes:
    • Finalise any work items associated with the request
    • Either annotate the existing DOC or record a new one (where a DOC is not already recorded) using the Deceased Customer - Executor Request Fast Note. Select Auto Text use TPO – Third Party Organisations > UPD – Update
    • Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • Complete a 'Release of Information' web form. The Resources page includes a link to the web form
    • Finalise any outstanding work items associated with the request
    • Record a DOC using the Deceased Customer - Executor Request Fast Note. Select Auto Text use TPO – Third Party Organisations > UPD – Update
    • Procedure ends here

Deceased customer record not found + Read more ...

3
