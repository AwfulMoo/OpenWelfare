Letter options - Z2264 Recognition as a specialist and consultant physician is approved

Table 2

Item

Option

When to tick

1

Provisional - competent authority pathway End date letter
  • Medical practitioner who is recognised for a 2 year period with:
    • the provisional registration includes approval for the medical practitioner to work in a specialist position, and
    • the registration confirms the medical practitioner is on a competent authority pathway (CAP), and
    • any approved locations are listed
  • Recognition expiry date is the end date of specialty

2

Limited area of need END date letter
  • Medical practitioner who is recognised for a 2 year period with:
    • the limited registration includes approval for the medical practitioner to work in a specialist position, and
    • the registration subtype shows Area of Need (AoN), and
    • any approved locations are listed
  • Recognition expiry date is the end date of specialty

3

Provider does not hold Fellowship of the relevant Australasian College END date letter
  • Medical practitioner who is recognised for a 2 year period with:
    • specialist registration for the specialty requested, and
    • has not supplied evidence of Australasian college fellowship
  • Recognition expiry date is the end date of specialty

4

Change of Status under 3DB
  • Medical practitioner who is:
    • recognised as a specialist/consultant physician previously under section 3E, and
    • reapply under section 3DB after change of residency status
  • Recognition expiry date is date of permanent residency granted

5

Recognition under 3DB
  • Australian citizen/permanent resident medical practitioner who applies for recognition as a specialist/consultant physician under section 3DB

6

Recognition under 3E when recognition is open ended
  • Temporary resident medical practitioner who:
    • applies for recognition as a specialist/consultant physician under section 3E, and
    • supplied evidence of Australasian college fellowship

7

Provider subject to section 19AB
  • Medical practitioner who is subject to section 19AB

8

For 3DB/3E - if already have 19AB approval
  • Medical practitioner who is subject to section 19AB and has current 19AB exemptions at any open locations

9

Mental Health Skills Training
  • Medical practitioner who is no longer eligible to MHST, PSC or FPS (VR specialty codes 647, 648, 649 and/or 650 or non-VR specialty codes 662, 663, 664 and/or 665)
  • No longer eligible to access from date is the end date of relevant specialty codes

10

Respiratory and Sleep Medicine with training in Level I or Post Fellowship Recognition in Sleep Medicine can also be selected with Letter A or C
  • Medical practitioner who is:
    • recognised as a consultant physician in Respiratory and Sleep Medicine (specialty code 094), and
    • eligible for Sleep Apnoea recognition (specialty code 192)
  • Sleep Medicine practitioner with effect from date is the start date of specialty code 192

11

Provider recognised in the speciality of Respiratory and Sleep Medicine with no evidence of training in Level I or post fellowship recognition in sleep medicine
  • Medical practitioner who:
    • is recognised as a consultant physician in Respiratory and Sleep Medicine (specialty code 094), and
    • has not supplied evidence for Sleep Apnoea recognition (specialty code 192)

12

Provider recognised in the specialty of Diagnostic Radiology
  • Medical practitioner who is recognised as a specialist of Diagnostic Radiology (specialty code 047)

13

Provider recognised in the specialty of Nuclear Medicine
  • Medical practitioner who is recognised as a specialist and/or consultant physician of Nuclear Medicine (specialty code 048 and/or 090)

14

where dual recognition has been granted
  • Medical practitioner who is eligible for dual recognition (specialty code 165)
  • Dual recognition status effective from date is the start date of specialty
