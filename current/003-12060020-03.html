Description

1

Carer is under 18 years of age + Read more ...

Service centre social workers conduct these referrals.

These claims need a referral to a social worker before finalising a claim for CP and/or CA.

Consultation with a Social Work Manager is mandatory before:

  • contacting a young carer 13 years and under, as it is expected that in most instances alternative sources of verification will be used to assess eligibility
  • finalising a carer assessment for a young carer aged 15 years and under

The main reasons for social worker involvement in these cases are:

  • to make sure that the carer has the physical and emotional capacity to provide the level of care needed, and
  • a young carer may have difficulty accessing support services and resources. A social worker can provide information and help in this regard

If unable to contact carer, see Item 7

For carers under the minimum school leaving age, the social worker should check that the appropriate education authority has given permission for the young person to leave school.

Note: school leaving age differs depending on the state or territory in which the person resides (not the location of the school).

Social workers may contact relevant third parties for example, school, support workers, counsellors, other family members involved in the care, in order to support their assessment.

If the carer is under 18, it is mandatory for social workers to use the template to complete and upload the report. See the Resources page for a link to the template and instructions to attach a report to a customer's record.

Once the report is complete, the social worker summaries the information relating to the recommendation into CSAT Assessment Finalised Fast Note. This activity loops back to region CD1 for a delegate to make a decision about qualification.

2

Care receivers at risk + Read more ...

Service centre social workers conduct these referrals, if there are concerns the care receiver is at risk, for example,

  • family and domestic violence
  • elder abuse
  • neglect
  • the care receiver has contacted disclosing they are not being cared for

The Service Officer will refer to social work for a wellbeing review.

3

Multiple and Complex Needs + Read more ...

These referrals present in the National WLM layer.

Referrals for assessment of care situation will be made under the multiple and complex needs referral option in the following circumstances:

  • Capacity to care including, if there is concern over the capacity of the carer/s to provide the appropriate level of care
  • Multiple carer/disputed care following an investigation by the Service Officer. Disputed care situations that may be appropriate for Social Work, include those involving particularly vulnerable carer’s or care receivers, for example those with mental illness, intellectual disability or reduced cognitive capacity
  • Other complex care situations such as where the carer does not reside with the care receiver or where the carer is providing care to 2 or more disabled children. These carers do not need to provide details about the level of care they provide. In these situations, it would generally be accepted that constant care is being provided. If doubt exists about whether the claimant is personally providing constant care, further investigation in the form of a social work interview may be appropriate. These situations may be referred to a social worker for a professional assessment where the initial Service Officer investigation has not been able to ascertain the level of care being provided

In conducting Carer Assessments social workers help in providing information for:

  • the care receiver's personal care or supervision needs in the home
  • the level of care or supervision actually being provided by the carer
  • if any other person helps with the care of the care receiver, and if so whether the CP customer is personally providing constant care
  • if the carer has the physical and/or emotional capacity to deal with the care receiver's care needs
  • if there are concerns about the capacity of the carer to provide an appropriate level of care, regardless of their age, for example, the carer's own health/mental health needs

Assessment of care situations will often involve the social worker contacting both the carer and the care receiver as well as any relevant third parties in order to conduct a holistic assessment of the care situation.

Once the assessment is complete, the social worker records information in either:

  • a Social Work report which is scanned onto the record, or
  • in a DOC on the Document List

A Social Work report may be appropriate in particularly complex matters or where multiple parties have been contacted. The social worker must make sure that sufficient information is provided to help the Service Officer in making a decision about the caring situation.

If there is outstanding CA/CP claims, the social worker will incorporate information relating to the recommendation into CSAT Assessment Finalised Fast Note. This activity loops back to region CD1 for a delegate to make a decision about qualification.

Information provided by a social worker should contribute to a decision about qualification for CP, but the delegate, not the social worker, makes the decision about qualification.

CP should not be rejected or cancelled without contacting the carer/ treating health professional to discuss conflicting information.

4

Carer aged 80 or over + Read more ...

These referrals present in the National WLM layer.

A social worker should assess and document the care situation.

The main reasons for social worker involvement in these cases are:

  • to make sure that the carer has the physical and emotional capacity to provide the level of care needed, and
  • an elderly carer may have difficulty accessing support services and resources. A social worker can provide information and help in this regard

If unable to contact carer, see Item 7

Once the information has been obtained the social worker is to document on the customer's record:

  • the social work contact
  • supports provided
  • any recommendations if appropriate

If the recommendation is not to grant the social worker is to write a report using the report template located on the Resources page.

Note: if the assessment is made post grant and the social worker recommends payment the CSAT Assessment Finalised Fast Note can be completed.

5

Child care receivers with terminal conditions + Read more ...

Social workers in the Carer Specialised Assessment Team (CSAT) conduct these referrals

CP (child) for a care receiver with a terminal condition will be reviewed 24 months from the date the payment was granted, and every 12 months thereafter.

Carer Specialist Assessment Team (CSAT) social workers will sensitively review each case and provide information to help a delegate in making a decision about continuing qualification for CP.

This includes if the CP recipient is continuing to provide care and whether the care receiver's condition has changed.

For further information, refer to Terminal illness reviews for Carer Payment (CP) child.

6

Investigating additional evidence relating to assessment of care load requirements in identified CP (child) claims + Read more ...

Social workers in the Carer Specialised Assessment Team (CSAT) conduct these referrals.

The CSAT report will include the CSAT social worker’s professional recommendation, the reasons and evidence to support the recommendation. Delegation for decision making will reside with the Service Officer.

Once the report is complete, the social worker is to incorporate information relating to the recommendation into CSAT Assessment Finalised Fast Note. This activity loops back to region CD1 for a delegate to make a decision about qualification.

7

Action to be taken if a social work assessment for under 18 and over 80 years is unable to be completed + Read more ...

If a claim is received for a person under 18 or over 80 the following attempts should be made:

  • Multiple phone attempts should be made to contact the customer at various times
  • Document all attempts to contact the customer
  • If unsuccessful, a Q164 letter is to be sent to the customer requesting contact within 21 days of the date the letter was issued
  • If contact with the customer has not been received within 21 days and the customer is:
    • under 18, the social worker runs the CSAT Young Carer Assessment unsuccessful Fast Note advising the Service Officer that attempts to contact have been unsuccessful
    • over 80, in cases of post grant only, the social worker runs the CSAT Payment Suspension Fast Note for a Service Officer to suspend payment. See Suspension of payments (CLK). The Service Officer should document the record to note that the payment is not to be restored until the social work assessment has been completed

Note: if the customer contacts the social worker to arrange an interview, however does not attend - follow the above processes.

8

Action to be taken in completing a social work referral + Read more ...

Guided procedure referrals must be updated as 'completed' following the social work assessment. To complete the guided procedure:

  • Select the 'Activities' tab on the Customer Summary
  • Select the referral 'ID' and edit status to 'complete'
