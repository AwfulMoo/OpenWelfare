Temporary and Special Circumstances exemptions + Read more ...

SpB customers are subject to the same MO exemption requirements as JSP and YAL job seekers.

Verification of circumstances is required before granting a temporary or Special circumstances exemption from either MOR.

Temporary exemptions

Customers receiving SpB may be eligible for a temporary exemption if they meet certain circumstances including:

  • temporary incapacity due to illness or injury
  • serious illness
  • expectant mother
  • death of a partner

Special circumstances

There are special circumstances when an exemption from MOR may apply, such as:

  • a major personal disruption to a person's home
  • a major personal crisis
  • affected by a declared natural disaster
  • performing temporary carer duties
  • fulfilling a Community Service Order
  • performing jury duty
  • performing voluntary work during a state or national emergency

A Nominated Visa Holder granted a 070, 449, 785, 786 or 790 visa may have a temporary exemption from MOR for the first 13 weeks from the visa grant date. This exemption only applies once, not each time a new visa type is granted. Code the exemption on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen, using the code of 'ASR' in the Activity Type: field.

Does the customer meet requirements for a temporary or special circumstances exemption:

  • Yes:
    • for temporary exemptions see Mutual obligation requirements exemptions
    • for special circumstances see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances
  • No, go to Table 3

Principal Carer-related exemptions

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Customer is a principal carer of a dependent child aged under 6 years + Read more ...

A person does not have to satisfy MOR if the person has at least one child who:

  • is a dependent child of the person (within the meaning of sub section 5(2), (3) and (6) of the Social Security Act), and
  • is aged under 6 years, and
  • is not a dependent child of another person, and
  • is in Australia

Note: for this exemption, a child can only be the dependent child of one parent.

Does the customer meet the criteria above?

  • Yes, the customer satisfies the MOR
    • For customers subject to the SpB MOR (policy), make sure the Payable Under: field on the SPL Other Details (NSOD) screen is blank
    • For customers subject to the Nominated Visa Holders (legislation) MOR, code the exemption on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen, using the code of 'DCE' in the Activity Type: field. Note that due to a system limitation specifically for NVH categories, a 'DCE' principal carer exemption can only be coded on the AEX screen and can only be coded for a maximum of 13 weeks at a time. This will need to be re-coded, as applicable, to extend the exemption
    • Principal carer status should be coded on the Principal Carer Circumstances Details (PCCD) screen
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Customer is a principal carer of a dependent child with special family circumstances + Read more ...

A person does not have to satisfy MOR if the SpB principal carer is:

  • an active, registered foster carer (reason FCR)
  • providing home schooling (reason PHS)
  • facilitating distance education for their child (reason ECH)
  • caring for a large family (4 or more children all aged between 6 years and 15 years inclusive) (reason LF4), or
  • caring for a disabled child (reason CCE)

A person in other special family circumstances will be assessed on a case-by-case basis for their eligibility for an exemption from MOR (reason SCI). For more information, see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances.

Does the customer have a special family circumstance specified above?

  • Yes:
    • customer satisfies the MOR, code the exemption on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen, using the relevant reason code in the Activity Type: field. Refer to Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances for more information
    • principal carer status should be coded on the Principal Carer Circumstances Details (PCCD) screen
    • procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Principal carer subject to family and domestic violence + Read more ...

Refer to a Social Worker. An exemption can be granted to a principal carer who:

  • cares of a dependent child experiencing extreme or high stress associated with a relationship separation
  • ceased to be a member of a couple
  • has been subject to family and domestic violence, and
  • has not become a member of couple

Has the customer been assessed by a Services Australia social worker and warrants an exemption?

  • Yes:
    • grant and code the exemption on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen, using the reason code 'D26' or 'R26' in the Activity Type: field for up to a maximum period of 16 weeks. At the 13 week review AEX should be reassessed to confirm if exemption is still required. Subsequent exemptions for one or more other periods may be granted if required. Refer to Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances for more information
    • principal carer status should be coded on the Principal Carer Circumstances Details (PCCD) screen
    • procedure ends here
  • No, see Table 1 Step 5

SpB MOR for Nominated Visa Holders (NVH) and non-Nominated Visa Holders

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Customer is subjected to MOR for SpB + Read more ...

Explain MOR to the customer, including:

  • attending Employment Services Provider (ESP) appointments
  • undertaking job search or meeting a monthly points target
  • acting on referrals to jobs
  • undertaking an Annual Activity Requirement
  • participating in an activity that fully meets their MOR
  • be actively seeking and willing to undertake suitable paid work
  • lodging a Reporting Statement via Self Service (online account and phone self service), in person or in writing (SU19)

Customers confirm compliance with their MO or participation requirements when completing fortnightly reporting.

Note: NVH customers will not be paid SpB if they do not comply with their MOR without a reasonable reason. SpB recipients with MO requirements which are based on legislation are subject to the compliance arrangements set out in Division 3A of the Social Security (Administration) Act.

2

Make sure the customer is registered as a Job Seeker + Read more ...

Check the Job seeker status is Active (JSR/ACT on the benefit status line).

If the customer is not JSR/ACT:

  • select Customer Summary from the Process Direct landing page
  • enter the customer's CRN, select Go
  • in the customer's record select >Participation Summary>Job seeker registration & provider referral information>Start job seeker registration
  • Complete the Job Seeker Snapshot if the customer is present
  • Note: if unable to undertake the Job Seeker Snapshot with the customer a JSR/MFU or a JSR UPD activity will be auto generated to make sure the customer is referred to the appropriate ESP. See Job Seeker Management Activities

To be eligible for Employment Services Assistance customers must be registered as Fully Eligible Participants (FEP) in order to access the full range of employment services. See Eligibility for Workforce Australia as a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP).
