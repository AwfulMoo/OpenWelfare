Customers are able to remain on DRA or NZ DRA for the entire 13 week entitlement period, including after returning to work as long as their total income remains under AWOTE.

Review date reached + Read more ...

DRA is payable for a maximum of 13 weeks from the date the customer has or will have a loss of income as a direct result of the disaster.

The system creates a review after DRA is granted. This review:

  • stays in the Future activity list until 11 weeks after the grant date
  • shows on the Activity List (AL) screen after 11 weeks
  • tells staff 'The customer’s DRA 13 week entitlement has been ended/will soon end. You should contact the customer and invite them to consider an alternative payment'

  • for a review called Disaster Recovery Allowance/Reassess Benefit
    • this review will no longer show on the FAL screen if the payment has ended (Benefit Status line on record will show as DRA/CAN-EDR)
  • the Action Date of this review, if it shows
    • the Action Date is the date the customer’s DRA should end
  • the Benefit Status line in the Customer Summary screen

Note: staff may be allocated a DRA 11 week review after it has been placed on hold by another Service Officer. This would have occurred to make sure the customer’s DRA cancels at the expected date.

  • DRA/CUR shows on the Benefit Status line and the customer’s DRA should have ended, go to Step 5
  • a DRA 11 week review shows in the FAL screen and the customer’s DRA should not have ended yet, and DRA/CUR shows on the Benefit Status line, if the customer was granted DRA for:
    • the New South Wales Floods - February 2022 and lives or works in the local government area (LGA) of Lismore only, customer contact is needed. (The employer details will only be viewable in the Claim Summary in Process Direct). Go to Step 2

    • the New South Wales Floods - February 2022 and lives or works in the LGA of Lismore only, customer contact is needed, go to Step 2

Enquiries about transferring to another payment + Read more ...

DRA and NZ DRA are 13 week payments to help people who have had their income affected by a major disaster.

While most customers return to their employment or start new employment after the disaster, some may need extra help.

If the customer asks about transferring to another payment after their DRA or NZ DRA entitlement ends:

DRA and NZ DRA automatically cancels at the end of a customer’s 13 week entitlement period. Sometimes customers ask Services Australia to cancel their claim before the end of this period.

The payment cancellation date depends on the reason the payment was cancelled.

Note: DRA is only payable up to the day before a customer passes away. Any payments made after the date of death are calculated as overpayments and may be recovered. This does not apply to NZ DRA

If a person calls to advise a customer receiving DRA has passed away, if there is

  • evidence available on record to support death of the customer, go to Step 3
  • no evidence available, if help is needed, or the person who has passed away was receiving NZ DRA contact Technical Peer Support. If further help is needed, Technical Peer Support can escalate the claim to the Level 2 Helpdesk using the payment query template.
    • All NZ DRA queries should be escalated to the Level 2 Helpdesk using the payment query template.

2

Tell the customer about the decision + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • DRA or NZ DRA is a 13 week payment
  • they remain current and do not receive DRA/NZ DRA if their income is over the threshold, that is equal to or more than the fortnightly rate of the average weekly income amount (AWOTE)
    • see Table 1
  • they can continue to received DRA/NZ DRA even if they have returned to work as long as their income remains below the AWOTE

Will DRA or NZ DRA still be cancelled?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, DOC the discussion. Procedure ends here

3

Cancel Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) or NZ DRA + Read more ...

Cancel the DRA or NZ DRA in Customer First:

  • Change the System drop down menu to ISS
  • In Next enter 'SVAAA'

Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen:

  • For Service Reason select either:
    • DRA for DRA, or
    • EXG for NZ DRA
  • Cancel the DRA/NZ DRA from the date the customer has requested cancellation
  • Select cancellation code OTH

Note: when selecting ‘OTH’, notes are needed on the BA screen within the cancellation activity, explain the reason. For example, the customer has requested the payment be cancelled. For DRA only - a debt activity may be created if the cancellation date is in the past. Send the debt shell activity to region code XWA with keyword DRADET.

For NZ DRA only, issue a Q888 NZ DRA cancel letter.

For all claims, DOC the actions on the customer's record. The following information must be included:

  • Date of contact:
  • Reason for cancellation.
    • Detail of any debts created as a result of the cancellation
    • Act reference for:
      - DRA - Social Security Act 1991 1061KA, Qualification for Disaster Recovery Allowance
      - NZ DRA - Financial Framework (Supplementary Powers) Act 1997

Procedure ends here.
