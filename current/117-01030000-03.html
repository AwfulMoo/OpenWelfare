For Compensation Recovery Teams (CRT) only.

What to do when an interest check is received

Step

Action

1

Interest check received + Read more ...

If a document has been attached to an incorrect customer’s record, see Digital image attached to incorrect Centrelink customer record.

A compensation work item is created when a third party submits:

  • Compensation Advice of Periodic Payments (SS445) form
  • Compensation Advice of Lump Sum Payments (SS446) form
  • other supporting documents (for example, payment schedule, settlement documents)

Check the Document List (DL) screen if the request relates to a previous request for information.

If the request is not about the release of a periodic or lump sum payment (for example, solicitor seeking interest prior to settlement of a claim), go to Step 10.

Otherwise, go to Step 2.

2

Activity falls due + Read more ...

Check the DL screen for notes about attempted contact with third parties.

Has a work item fallen due after 23 days since contacts were made to request information?

Yes, and all required information:

  • has been returned. Go to Step 5
  • has not been returned:
    • Cancel the activity or work item
    • Record all action taken in a DOC including that information has not been returned
    • Procedure ends here

No, go to Step 3.

3

Is further information needed? + Read more ...

Check the documents and decide if further information is needed to assess the periodic or lump sum payment. For example, completed SS446 and supporting documents.

If an Advice of Lump Sum Payments (SS446) or Advice of Periodic Payments (SS445) form has not been provided:

  • check if the questions in the forms can be answered based on the available evidence (for example, settlement documents, letters or statements)
  • decide if more information is needed

Have all details have been provided?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Incomplete or missing information + Read more ...

Make reasonable effort to contact the third party to request the information. Attempts to contact third parties must be made over 2 or more business days.

Information that can be obtained over the phone may include, but is not limited to:

  • Date last paid weekly compensation
  • Confirmation of economic loss
  • Claim number

Note: the decision maker must decide whether to request the information in writing. For example, if there are concerns about the accuracy of information supplied verbally.

If contact with third parties by phone is successful and the information: + Read more ...

  • Can be given over the phone:
    • Record the discussion in a DOC
    • Go to Step 5
  • Cannot be given over the phone:
    • Request a completed SS446 or SS445 and missing documents to be faxed
    • Record all action taken in a DOC. For example, successful contact made with compensation payer to re-fax a completed SS446 with settlement documents.
    • Place the CMM activity on hold for 22 days
    • Procedure ends here

If contact by phone is unsuccessful: + Read more ...

  • Record all unsuccessful contact attempts in a DOC including time, dates and voicemail recipient name (if applicable) along with details of information required.
  • Place the CMM activity on hold for 22 days
  • Procedure ends here

If there is no CMM activity (for example, digital image work item): + Read more ...

  • Complete the digital image
  • Go to the Activity Registration (AYR) screen:
    • Key CMM in the Service Reason field
    • Key CLE in the Activity Type field
    • Complete the Source field (eg. FAX)
    • Complete the Date of Receipt field using the date of the digital image
    • Complete the Resubmit Date for 22 days from today
    • Enter Keyword SS445 or SS446, whichever is applicable. This will ensure the work item allocates to the correct queue
    • Transfer to Region: CTN
  • Procedure ends here

5

Determine agency interest + Read more ...

Agency interest exists if the customer and/or partner’s past or future social security payments or concessions will be impacted by the compensation payment. See Resources for a list of social security entitlements that may indicate agency interest.

Check the following screens and decide if the customer and/or partner is receiving an income support payment or concession:

  • Payment Summary (PS)
  • Benefit Status (XBS)
  • Allowance/Benefit History (ABH)
  • Pensions Status History (PNSH)
  • PPP Change History (PGCH)
  • Concession Entitlement Summary (MCCS)
  • LIC Assessment Summary (LIAS)
  • Payment List screen in Customer First for Farm Household Allowance (FHA) payments

Is the customer and/or partner receiving any payment or concession with a current (CUR) status?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Future preclusion period + Read more ...

If the request relates to arrears of periodic payments, go to Step 7.

Check the documents provided and decide if there will be a future preclusion period. Complete a provisional calculation. See Compensation lump sum preclusion periods and Calculating the compensation part of a lump sum payment.

Will there be a future preclusion period?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Determine potential debt + Read more ...

Check the start and end dates of the periodic payment or lump sum preclusion period and decide if a compensation affected payment (CAP) was received in the period.

If the customer and/or partner has:

  • received a CAP in the period, go to Step 8
  • not received a CAP in the period or has only received a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) and the request is:
    • from a compensation payer or customers solicitor, go to Step 9
    • from the defendants solicitor, go to Step 12
    • for a deceased estate, go to Step 11

8

Agency interest + Read more ...

If no compensation record exists in CMS for the incident date, create a record. See Coding compensation and damages (Mod C) to request a clearance.

If a compensation record exists in CMS and the request is about:

  • periodic payments, see Coding and raising debts for periodic compensation payments
  • lump sums, see Assessing lump sum compensation payments and calculating debts
  • judgement order, see Calculating a compensation debt for judgement

If the payments are found not to meet the definition of compensation, record the payments on the relevant screens on the customer's record. See:

  • Recording other income on the Other Income (OIN) screen
  • Assessing superannuation

Procedure ends here.

9

No agency interest and the claim has settled + Read more ...

Check the Debt List (OPDL) screen for outstanding debts and consider garnishee. See Compensation garnishees to recover debts.

Staff must:

  • Send Q999 letter to the customer’s solicitor, see Resources for text
  • Send Q928 nil recovery notice to the compensation payer
  • Do not code details onto CMS
  • Cancel the CMM/CHG activity or scanned document
  • Record all action taken in the No Interest Fast Note

If a fax number has been provided for the compensation payer and/or solicitor, fax the notices on the same day. See Faxing letters using Outlook and Customer First

Note: where a lump sum has settled for a deceased customer, and the preliminary notice was sent prior to the death of the customer, see Assessing lump sum compensation payments and calculating debts.

Procedure ends here.

10

Claim has not settled + Read more ...

When notification received from the solicitor about a claim that has not settled yet, check if the customer has receive a compensation affected payment (CAP) since their date of injury.

If the customer's solicitor is only asking for a preclusion period estimate, go to Step 13.

If the customer:

  • has received a CAP since their date of injury, consider sending a Preliminary Notice. See Actioning a compensation clearance
  • has not received a CAP since their date of injury:
    • Send Q922 Claim for compensation to the customer’s solicitor
    • Do not code details on CMS
    • Cancel the CMM/CHG activity or scanned document
    • Record all action taken in the No Interest Fast Note

11

Deceased estates + Read more ...

Do not send any letter to, or care of, the executor of the deceased estate or personal representative.

If the compensation payer is seeking clearance for a deceased estate:

  • Do not create a record in the name of the Executor of the Estate
  • Do not code details on CMS
  • Do not send an automatic release notice
  • Send Q928 nil recovery notice to compensation payer in the name of the compensation recipient
  • Cancel the CMM/CHG activity or scanned document
  • Record all action taken in the No Interest Fast Note
  • Procedure ends here

12

Defendant Solicitor + Read more ...

Is the interest check from the Defendant Solicitor?

  • Yes, see Step 3 of Release of information for a compensation interest check
  • No, procedure ends here

13

A request for an estimate is received from a solicitor + Read more ...

Contact the solicitor and refer them to the Compensation Online Estimator (CLK).
