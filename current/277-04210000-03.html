Information provided by children + Read more ...

Child Support will not attempt to obtain information from a child about any of the parents' child support issues.

Child Support will not accept statements made by a child in writing or verbally even if the statements are volunteered by the child. See References for a link to The Child Support Guide 6.2.2: Taking information from children.

4

Formal vs. informal information gathering + Read more ...

Formal information gathering means that a notice is issued in writing requiring that specified information be provided in a certain manner by a certain date. Informal information gathering does not require a formal notice to be issued. Informal information will usually be gathered via phone or by accessing electronic resources such as Centrelink.

Note: prior to accessing ATO systems directly for information relevant to a particular customer, issue an electronic ATO legal notice. See Legal notice for Child Support access to Australian Taxation Office (ATO) data and systems.

Where possible, information should be sought informally as it can be faster and easier for both Child Support and the customer. If Service Officers are concerned that a person may not respond to an informal information gathering request, or the person states that they will comply but have not after a specific time, issue a formal notice. This includes issuing a notice directly to the customer to provide the required information.

If there is a formal arrangement for requests between Child Support and a source, Service Officers must follow the established procedures. For example, Child Support will always formally seek information from a bank or financial institution by issuing a notice.

Informal information gathering via phone or electronic searches

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Informal information gathering + Read more ...

Informal information gathering will usually be via telephone or through electronic searches.

Often the best informal source is the customer. Contact the customer first unless it may jeopardise collection action. Make genuine efforts to contact the customer with methods specific to the customer. See Contact with Child Support customers.

2

Seeking information from a customer + Read more ...

When seeking information from a customer:

  • Explain to the customer why the information is required including the type of information that will be relied upon to make a decision
  • Consider the customer's cultural background and individual needs when supporting them to comply with the request for information
  • Ensure customers are given reasonable opportunities to provide relevant information taking into account individual circumstances
  • Ensure customers understand the potential consequences of not complying with the request for information, that is, a formal notice may be issued
  • Ensure all relevant available information is considered before making a decision and whether the decision is to undertake more formal information gathering or otherwise
  • Other sources that may provide information informally are the other customer in the case, employers, or business associates of the customer. As informal information gathering from the other parent or third party often occurs in a very conversational way, be careful not to give away information about the customer that would breach their privacy

If information is obtained, go to Documentation.

3

Seeking information from any other source + Read more ...

When seeking information informally from any source:

  • Allow a reasonable timeframe to provide the information
  • Be careful not to breach the customer's privacy
  • If the source is the customer and they cannot or will not provide the information, advise them the intention is to seek the information elsewhere
  • If the source refuses to provide information or is uncomfortable providing the details informally, or there is reason to believe the source will not comply with an informal request, issue a formal notice requiring the information be provided

Note:

  • Many organisations must comply with privacy legislation and issuing a formal notice ensures that the source is not in breach of their own privacy considerations
  • If seeking detailed, complex, or extensive information it is preferable to issue a formal notice so that the information required is clearly communicated to the source and the source provides the response in writing

If information is obtained, go to Documentation.

If other sources may provide information informally, continue seeking information from those sources.

Table 3

Description

1

Formal information gathering + Read more ...

Notices must be generated and issued through Cuba.

When issuing a notice, Service Officer(s) must select Melbourne Central from the Office dropdown list.

There is currently no capacity for Cuba to issue s161 notices seeking information about a child earnings (who does not have a role as a payer) in a change of assessment Reason 4 application.

The notice must be issued manually. Where a notice cannot be issued through Cuba see Letters for Child Support customers.

If a notice is issued manually the schedule sent must be copied into a notepad. Also a hard copy of the actual notice must either be:

  • batch filed or
  • stored in the file relevant to the proceedings. For example COA or OBJ

Notices may be issued by facsimile in certain circumstances. See Letters for Child Support customers if there is a need to serve a notice by fax:

  • Financial institutions must consent to a notice being served by fax, see Letters for Child Support customers
  • Employers and some financial institutions can elect to have their notices sent electronically via Child Support Business Online Services

Service Officer(s) should consider issuing a notice on the customer if they have refused or failed to comply with an informal request on this and/or previous occasions and are identified as the likely sole holder of the required information.

Set clear and reasonable timeframes for information to be provided, generally not less than 14 days. Consider the individual circumstances of the source when setting the response date. A source in a regional or remote area may need 21 or 28 days to return a response.

Allow the customer time to respond. See Standard response times.

Section 120 notices seeking information about customers are issued through the Collection window.

If information from an employer is required, use an employer questionnaire (EF1). Issuing a s120 notice to an employer could delay the linking process.

Section 161 notices are issued through the Collection/Capacity to Pay window for both payees and payers.

If issuing a s120 or s161 notice with a standard schedule will result in obtaining information that cannot be used for the purposes of collecting, making and varying child support assessments, a unique schedule should be used.

Endorsed schedules for a range of organisations are available via Cuba or unique schedules in Separated parents letter and email templates, Schedules - S161 and S120.

Clearly document:

  • reasons for issuing a s120 or s161 notice
  • reasons for requesting a search
  • the information being requested
  • whether the customer was asked to provide the information, and if not, the reason the customer was not asked, and
  • how the lead was obtained, for example, bank account details supplied by employer

Issuing a notice will generate an intray due 21 days after the notice issued. For large organisations (such as banks) it is usual to allow the full 21 day response timeframe and only contact them if no response is received after this time.

When issuing a notice, the customer must be locked to an officer as per Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support. This is to ensure that a contact officer is listed on the notice.

Note:

  • notices are tools to gather information. They must not be used or issued to intimidate or coerce a customer
  • a warning message will appear when issuing s120 and s161 notices if the customer has current aliases which may need to be included on the notice/s

The Child Support Guide outlines the information that can be sought under section 120 of the Registration and Collection Act and section 161 of the Assessment Act.

2

Requesting information from Department of Veterans' Affairs + Read more ...

When there is reason to believe a customer is in receipt of a Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) pension or benefit and attempts are being made to update the customer's income or explore avenues for collecting child support, it may be appropriate to issue a request to DVA for more detailed information. There are 2 ways this can be done:

  • Child Support can directly request information from DVA when it relates to determining the tax free pension or benefit component of a customer's adjusted taxable income (see the Child Support Guide 2.4.4: child support income) or to determine if the customer receives 1 of the 6 DVA payments from which Child Support can collect debt (see the Child Support Guide 5.2.7: Collection from veterans pensions and allowances). For direct requests, see Direct Requests for Information from Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)
  • For all other information requests, Child Support must issue a notice under s120 or s161 using the appropriate schedule

3

Schedules for requesting information + Read more ...

There are a variety of standard schedules which have been designed to request the most commonly required information.

Endorsed schedules for a range of organisations are available via Cuba or manual schedules in Separated parents letter and email templates, Schedules - S161 and S120.

If it is necessary to ask for information which is not covered by questions in the Cuba schedules, generate a unique schedule This must be authorised by TL. Document the reasons for the unique schedule in the notepad.

See Issue section 120/161 notice or External searches guide.

4

Payment for information + Read more ...

Some authorities may request payment of a fee before they comply with our request for information. These may be government authorities or 'information brokers' such as Veda Advantage. In this case, look for other ways to get the information. If unsuccessful, ask a Service Support Officer (SSO) to help decide if the information is critical enough to justify the fee.

However, not all organisations are entitled to charge a fee for complying with Child Support notices. For example, accountants, lawyers and medical professionals ordinarily charge their clients at a scheduled rate for their services. These professionals may ask Child Support to pay at a similar rate for what they see as a service. Complying with Child Support notices is their legal obligation so Child Support will not pay for their compliance. Service Officers should be careful not to make unreasonable requests for information in these cases. For example, a request for information from an accountant should not require the accountant to produce 'special purpose financial statements' – documents created solely for the purpose of complying with Child Support notice.

5

Simultaneous issue of s120 and s72A notices to banks + Read more ...

Child Support will usually seek information to identify capacity to pay to improve Child Support's negotiating power, prior to taking garnishee action.

Some financial institutions, for example, Commonwealth Bank, will advise a mutual customer that Services Australia has made enquiries which could result in the customer withdrawing funds to avoid garnishee action.

In limited circumstances where this is known to be the case, it may be appropriate to issue s72A and s120 notices at the same time to that financial institution.

Service Officers must be certain that:

  • the customer is non-compliant
  • the customer has no intention of meeting their debt responsibilities, and
  • the s72A notice should not leave the customer without access to funds to meet immediate essential living expenses and expected regular payments. Contact a SSO for support with this decision, see Technical support in Child Support

Note: the customer will be unable to access their account where the balance of the account is less than the amount requested in the notice.

Fully document the reasons for the decision and evidence that supports the decision in the s120 notepad and the s72A notepad.

Do not issue s72A notices to:

  • a bank account receiving National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) payments or which holds a known payment from the National Redress Scheme.
    This includes NDIS or Redress payments made to a nominee or beneficiary.
    See National Intensive Collection Services
  • recover relevant debts directly from a Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS) Program payment

To issue s72A notices, see Collection of child support debts through third parties (s72A and s72AC).

See References for a link to The Child Support Guide 5.2.9: Collection from third parties.

6

Formal request to attend an interview + Read more ...

Serving a notice requiring a person to attend an interview requires authorisation at the SO5 level or above. A person should only be required to attend when doing so will produce a necessary outcome that a written response would not.

The notice must clearly state the information required and give the person a reasonable amount of time to arrange their affairs to attend the interview and bring any documents that have been requested. The person should also be given the option to reschedule the interview to a mutually convenient time.

Both Child Support Regulations specify out of pocket expenses to be paid to a person attending an interview, however there is no provision for such payments if the person is the payer, the payee or their representative.

7

Change of assessment and system notices + Read more ...

Notices issued through Cuba are issued under the delegation of the Chief Operating Officer. Senior Case Officers (SCOs) are delegated to issue notices on their own authority, but that notice must be issued through Cuba.

Pre and Post-conference: SCOs must issue system generated s161 notices to gather information through Cuba. Manually issued notices must not be used except for Reason 4 Change of Assessment. See Section 120/161 Notice Cuba Process Help.

Exceptions: Reason 4 Change of Assessment

When a s161 notice is required to gather information about the income, earning capacity, property or financial resources of a child and the child does not have a role as a payer, a manual s161 notice must be issued.

When a complete response is received go to Documentation.

Follow up response to the notice + Read more ...

For a notice issued to an Employer

If a response is not received by the specified date or an incomplete response is received, check the employer record.

Allow the customer time to respond. See Standard response times.

Is the employer record locked to an ESO or has paying parents linked?

  • Complete a search to locate the organisation record. Open the organisation record
    • check if the details of an ESO is recorded in the Owner Details field
    • if there are no ESO details, select the close button to be navigated to the Organisation List screen
  • From the Organisation List screen:
    • look at the EW PYR column
    • a number in the EW PYR column indicates the number of paying parents linked

if this column is blank there are no paying parents linked to the employer

Is the employer managed by an ESO or has paying parents linked for deductions?

  • Yes,
    • create an Organisation Follow Up intray
    • document a request to follow up compliance with the notice
  • No,
    • make 3 contact attempts on all available numbers over 48 hours to discuss the notice, the obligation of the recipient and expected timeframe to respond.

Document all attempts of contact in the Collection window and Employer/Organisation Communication window.

Ensure the source understands the legal requirements in providing information and negotiate any blockers to compliance.

Compliance is not merely acknowledging a request for information. It means responding to every question/request as completely as possible.

Confirm that the source understands what information has been requested. If the notice was not clear it may be appropriate to issue another notice.

Notice not complied with

A failure or refusal to comply by a third party must be fully documented, as well as any reasons the source gave for non-compliance.

Refer the case to Employer Services, if an EF1, s72A, s72B, s120 or s161 notice has not been complied with by a third party (except for financial institutions - see below) and:

  • there is an indication of no intent to respond or action the notice, and
  • all contact attempts to discuss the notice with the source have been made and are unsuccessful
  • Complete the Non-compliance with notice referral template, see Resources>Template
  • Copy the information into a General Follow Up intray (for customers) or an Organisation Follow Up intray (for employers) titled Non Compliance With Notice and route to:
    • Region: NATIONAL OFFICE
    • Office: NATIONAL OFFICE
    • Stream/Team: MC SEW NAT EMP SERVICES
    • Position: A – ES LEGAL NOTICE GATE

Employer Services will reissue the notice to the third party with the appropriate signature, ensure the notice is appropriately addressed and monitor for 14 days.

If the notice is not complied with, Employer Services will:

  • report suspected fraud, and
  • add a note on the Client Profile window and document a report of suspected fraud has been made, see Documentation Cuba Process Help

Continue seeking information from alternate sources. See Item 1 in the Gathering information table.

9

Follow up conflicting or unclear information provided + Read more ...

If the information provided by a third party conflicts with other current information held by the agency, seek clarification from the third party/employer.

Note: this does not apply to Employer Withholding (EW) notices because ES manage all interactions with employers for EW related enquiries or follow up.

Contact the third party/employer

Check the Organisation details on Cuba Organisation screen, click on ‘contacts’. If the record indicates that this third party has elected to be ‘ESO contact only’:

  • The Service officer is to create an organisation follow up intray on the employer, which will auto-route to the owning ESO. In the notes, document clearly what information the agency holds that is contradicting the information the employer has already provided

Employer Services Action

  • The ESO will contact the Organisation's payroll officer telephone to confirm they understand what information has been requested and the expected timeframe to respond.
  • Advise the employer if appropriate, the information the agency holds which conflicts with the information they have provided. For example, the employee’s current bank statement indicates regular deposits from the employer when the employer states they have no record of the employee. If the information still conflicts refer to the Notice complied with but information still conflicts section, below
  • The ESO will document the outcome of the discussion on a Linking/Attributes response intray on the paying parent which auto routes to the owning Service Officer.

If the Organisation is NOT ESO contact only:

  • The Service Officer should contact the Organisation’s payroll officer directly by telephone to confirm they understand what information has been requested and the expected timeframe to respond. If the notice was not clear it may be appropriate to issue another notice.
  • Advise the employer, if appropriate, the information the agency holds which conflicts with the information they have provided. For example, the employee’s current bank statement indicates regular deposits from the employer when the employer states they have no record of the employee.

Recruitment agencies and large organisations may have permanent and casual employees and hold their records on different systems.

Ensure the source understands the legal requirement to provide the information and negotiate any blockers to compliance.

Document any conversation with the source even though they are expected to formally respond in writing.

Notice complied with

If the source subsequently complies with the notice, see Item 1 in the Documentation table.

Notice complied with but information still conflicts

If the Service Officer or the ESO have contacted the source and not been able to clarify the situation and the Service Officer remains dissatisfied with the response, refer the matter to the SSO to determine whether further escalation is warranted.

See Customer referral guidelines.

See Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support.

In some limited circumstances it may be appropriate to refer the matter for prosecution; see Fraud.

Document response + Read more ...

When a response is received, consider the reasons the information was requested and document all relevant details.

Example

A response is received to an s120 request to a bank seeking information to determine a customer's capacity to pay. Record the following items:

  • date of response
  • account type, numbers and balances
  • current address and contact details
  • source of any regular credits to the account and details of amounts of most recent payments
  • details of any lump sum payments including source, amounts, dates
  • details of any joint accounts or business relationships
  • details of any mortgage or loan accounts including the asset for which the loan was obtained
  • any details that suggest further investigation may be warranted, and
  • details of any spending patterns of interest, for example, regular restaurant dining, or other expenses indicating disposable income

See View, update and finalise a s120/161 response in Section 120/161 Notice Cuba Process Help.

All discussions, calculations and decisions in relation to the information obtained must be documented in the relevant notepad. The documentation must be easy for any other Service Officer to understand what has happened and why. See Documenting Child Support information.
