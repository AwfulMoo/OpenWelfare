This document outlines withholding rates for debt recovery.

Withholding arrangements

Withholdings are deductions taken from a customer's payment used to repay a debt.

Standard withholdings will apply to customers if:

  • a debt exists after the due date, and
  • no contact is made to organise another withholding rate

Standard withholdings apply the day after the debt becomes due (due date + 1 day).

A cash arrangement cannot replace withholdings. Cash payments can supplement withholdings.

For customers who are paid weekly, the withholding amount is calculated using their rate of payment for the full 14 day entitlement period.

See the Resources page for withholding hierarchy.

Family assistance

Apply the family assistance reconciliation withholding rate to Family Tax Benefit (FTB) payments if the only debts are:

  • family assistance reconciliation, or
  • re-reconciliation

Staff can accept an offer for reduced withholding rate. See Withholdings.

Family assistance non-reconciliation withholding rates

Different withholding arrangements apply if a customer has a family assistance reconciliation debt and another type of debt. This includes:

  • Qualification
  • Circumstance
  • Non-lodger
  • Non-family assistance

If the customer is:

  • getting an income support payment - withholdings apply to that payment
  • not getting an income support payment - withholdings apply at specific rates to their FTB payment

Child Care Subsidy (CCS)

The standard CCS withholding rate is 20% of their ongoing entitlement.

See Child Care Subsidy (CCS) debts for more details.

Parental Leave Pay (PPL)

Standard withholdings from PPL can only be used to recover PPL debts.

See Parental Leave Pay (PPL) debt raising, debt offsetting and recovery.

The Resources page has details to help Service Officers determine how the withholding amount is calculated.
