This document outlines how eligible customers for Income Management are identified.

On this Page:

System identification

Customers may be automatically identified for Income Management, or referred to Services Australia for Income Management. Services Australia is responsible for ensuring customers meet the basic eligibility criteria for any of the measures to which they have been referred.

The system will identify customers who live in a declared Income Management area who are:

  • eligible for Compulsory Income Management (CIM), Disengaged Youth (DEY) and Long-Term Welfare Payment Recipients (LTWPR) measures
  • eligible under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) measure because they are:
    • aged under 16 years and in receipt of a Special Benefit (SpB-USY)
    • 16 years of age or over and in receipt of an independent rate of Youth Allowance, Disability Support Pension or ABSTUDY Living Allowance due to meeting the Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH) criteria
    • under the age of 25 and receive a Crisis Payment (CrP) within the last 13 weeks due to prison release
    • customers will not be identified for VWPR if they are full time students or apprentices, or currently on Voluntary Income Management (VIM). Note: if a customer's VIM ceases, they may be income managed under this category
  • potentially eligible for Nominee Income Management (NIM)

Income Management referring authorities

Customers may be referred to Income Management by a State or Territory Child Protection Authority, a Supporting People at Risk approved referring authority or the Family Responsibilities Commission (FRC). Customers referred by one of these authorities will be income managed under Child Protection, Supporting People at Risk or Cape York Initiative measures respectively.

Vulnerable customers

Customers whose personal circumstances make them vulnerable or at risk, will, upon being determined as suitable by a Services Australia social worker, be placed on Income Management under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) measure.

Voluntary Income Management

Customers can volunteer for Income Management under the Voluntary Income Management (VIM) measure. This measure should be promoted to customers who would benefit from Income Management in areas where Voluntary Income Management is available.

Approved referring authorities and external organisations can also refer a customer to the agency for Voluntary Income Management (VIM), however the customer is under no obligation to enter into a VIM Agreement or attend any appointments.
