Role of QOL checker

Table 2

Item

Description

1

QOL checking an urgent payment activity

The reason for a decision following an Urgent Payment (UP) request must be clearly DOCumented on a customer's record.

If a UP activity has been selected for QOL checking, contact with the customer is not finished until the activity has been released and the payment (if granted) has been issued.

QOL checking will not apply to payments delivered via the NPP.

2

QOL question set

The question set for Urgent Payments has recently been changed to make sure QOL standards are met:

  • Record a DOC about the decision to grant or reject a UP and include a detailed report of the reason why the customer is requesting the payment.
    Example: For an exceptional and unforeseen circumstance, this may include
    'the customer's fridge blew up and all food has been spoilt, has used funds to buy new fridge.'
  • Urgent Payments still meet urgent QOL guidelines. A customer must remain with the service officer until after the UP activity has been checked and released by a QOL checker

The revised Urgent Payment QOL question set

When considering a UP assessment as part of a QOL check, the checker will be presented with the below options in the revised question set. If any of these are selected, the activity should be returned.

  • Amount over $200 and grant is for exceptional and unforeseen circumstances (except funeral assessments)
  • Amount over $1,000 and grant is for Centrelink delay or error
  • No decision reason recorded
  • Paid for regular and ongoing expenses, not exceptional and unforeseen circumstances
  • Extraordinary payment granted without TL/TPS approval

3

Incorrect payment reason coded

If an incorrect payment reason code is used on the Issue Future Payment (IFP) screen, the QOL activity should be returned for correction.

Due to the limit of 2 urgent payments in a rolling 12 months, it is important to use the correct code.
