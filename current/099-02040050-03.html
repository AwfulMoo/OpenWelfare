1

Services Australia has been advised of the death of the partner of a PPP customer + Read more ...

When a person contacts Services Australia to advise of the death of a customer, staff must offer:

  • a social worker referral in the first instance or at any time the person shows signs of distress during the contact
  • the pamphlet 'Needing help after someone has died?' (HD001) and if accepted, issue

Consider and respect the following cultural issues when being advised of the death of an Indigenous customer.

  • The name of the deceased person cannot be mentioned by immediate family members. This means they may have to ask other relatives/friends to call on their behalf or attend the interview with the person notifying us of the death
  • It may also be offensive to the person for the Service Officer to say the name of the deceased customer. If unsure, sensitively ask the person if it is all right to use the deceased customer's name. If they are not comfortable with this, avoid using the deceased customer's name wherever possible. See Cultural considerations when someone dies

Does the PPP customer partner's record indicate they were income managed?

Check for INM on the benefit status line.

  • Yes, follow the process to update record for death of an income managed customer. Go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Confirming death of the deceased person + Read more ...

The Notification of death guided procedure cannot be used for the death of a PPP customer's partner. This is the case whether the deceased partner was receiving a Centrelink payment or not.

Obtain all the relevant details from the informant including:

  • their name and contact number
  • relationship to the deceased, and
  • name of the funeral director (if known) or hospital

Record the details of the executor (if known).

Nominee arrangements and Power of Attorney (POA) stop at the time of death. Correspondence will be sent to the executor or administrator of the estate. This includes a payment summary and possible debt recovery action. If details are incorrectly recorded, this will affect the agency's ability to finalise required follow up items. If executor details are not known now, leave blank the executor details fields on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen. Those details can be recorded on the DEA screen later, after the death action.

Confirmation of death can be accepted if the deceased person's full name is given, plus 2 of the following items:

  • deceased customer's last address in full
  • date of birth or age at death
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • partner's name

See Confirmation of the death of an adult or child.

Can the person give enough information to confirm the death?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Death cannot be confirmed + Read more ...

If the deceased person was receiving a payment, manually suspend the payment until the death can be confirmed.

The suspension date of event should be the later of the unconfirmed date of death or the date paid to.

Manually code the suspension for reason 'OTH' (other).

Record a DOC on the customer's record. Ensure it meets Online Document Recording (ODR) standards for significant decisions and includes what action is needed to confirm the death.

Do not issue a Q134 suspension letter. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension.

Create an open work item. Use Fast Note – select Auto Text use Generic > Update > Template Name: Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED.

Note: the death must be confirmed and actioned as a matter of urgency. For Service Officers confirming the death, contact the funeral director or the hospital where the deceased passed away. If this is not an option and contact with relatives is proposed, social worker involvement may be necessary.

When the death is confirmed, go to Step 4.

4

Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) involvement + Read more ...

Income support pension payments from DVA can be found on the Department of Veterans' Affairs Summary (DVAS) screen and include:

  • War Widow(er)'s Pension
  • Age Service Pension
  • Partner Service Pension
  • Invalidity Service Pension
  • Carer Service Pension
  • Veteran Payment
  • Income Support Supplement (ISS)
  • Social Security Age Pension (paid by DVA)

Note: Disability Pension is excluded for this purpose.

Was the deceased person in receipt of a DVA pension?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Deceased customer in receipt of a DVA payment + Read more ...

Email the DVA Clearance Team (SCS) to advise that a LBP should be calculated for the surviving partner.

DVA Clearance Team (SCS) only:

Confirm the death of the customer's partner (if able) and fax through a request to the DVA for the following information:

  • date of death
  • type of DVA payment and rate of payment
  • date last paid and entitlement period end date

Record details of the bereavement on a DOC.

When information has been received from the DVA, find out the type of payment the deceased received. DVA may be responsible for paying the lump sum bereavement payment.

6

Code death action manually + Read more ...

The Notification of death guided procedure cannot be used to record the death of a PPP customer's partner.

Once the death has been confirmed:

  • if the deceased partner's payment was suspended, restore payment and determine if arrears are payable. Consider the confirmed date of death. Inhibit the restoration advice. It is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased person
  • Check if the deceased partner was receiving any of the following payments:
    • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) instalments
    • Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
    • Ancillary benefits for a Regular Care Child
    • Paid Parental Leave (PPL)
    • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • If they were, code the child out of care. See the Process page in Death of a Family Assistance or Paid Parental Leave Scheme Customer, Table 1 Steps 5 and 6

If this activity submits to QOL, request an urgent QOL check so the death action can be finalised.

Once completed, return to this procedure and continue the steps below.

  • Go to the PGA system. In the deceased customer's record, manually code the death action. Record the Date of Receipt (DOR) as the later of date of death or date paid to + 1
  • The Assessment, Warning and Error (AWE) screen will display message: 'Cust in Bereavement period and Man Rate Req'd. Please Update MAS'
  • Before finalising the activity, code a manual rate of Parenting Payment (PP) with reason 'BRV'. The manual rate is the rate of Parenting Payment Single (PPS) the customer would be entitled to

7

Record manual PPS rate for the surviving PPP customer within death activity + Read more ...

Key 'SR' in the Nxt field to swap to the PPP customer's record:

  • once in the PPP customer's record, key 'PGAS' in the Nxt field and press [Enter]
  • on the PP Assessment (PGAS) screen, update:
    • Manual Rate Customer Group field with the customer benefit type, e.g. PPN, NSS
    • press [Enter]
  • on the Manual Assessment (MAS) screen, key:
    • Effect Date, the customer's date of death + 1 day
    • Type, 'Man' (manual)
    • Manual Rate Reason, update to 'BRV' (bereavement)
    • any notes can be recorded in Notes
    • press [Enter]
  • on the W/C Rate Override (WCRC) screen, key:
    • Start Date, the start date used on the MAS screen
    • Component, either ACC (accrual), DEP (depletion) or BAL (balance)
    • Reason, 'MAN' (manual rate)
    • Change, either AUT (to end a MAN code) or MAN (manual rate). Amount, the amount to manually adjust the balance to each fortnight. This will require manual calculation of accrual / depletion amount

See Non NSS systems coding for manual rate of payment.

8

Code NMIS screen + Read more ...

Recording partner death details and if applicable, pregnancy details, on the Miscellaneous Update Details (NMIS) screen will enable a bereavement mutual obligations exemption should the customer become subject to mutual obligation requirements while in the bereavement period.

Code NMIS screen in the following fields:

  • Pregnancy details, if applicable. Note: the pregnancy bereavement mutual obligations exemption will not be enabled unless the pregnancy has been verified. Medical evidence is required, such as a letter from a doctor or registered midwife
    • Expected date of confinement, key date
    • Pregnancy verified
  • Partner death details
  • Date of partner death
  • Date of notification
  • Notified by

The end date of the bereavement period will pre-fill when the NMIS is coded with relevant details.

If the customer is already subject to mutual obligations, check the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen for bereavement exemption:

  • BPP - bereavement period exemption - partner
  • BPE - bereavement period exemption - pregnant partner

See Mutual obligation requirement exemptions.

9

Assessment Results (AR) screen + Read more ...

On the AR screen a manual rate line will display. 'S'elect the manual rate line and type 'RATS' in the Nxt field. The bereavement payment amount can be changed if it is not correct.

The Daily Rate Summary (RATS) screen will display the old and new daily rates for the customer. 'S'elect the first non-Period Total line which shows a new rate of $0.00 with a manual rate indicator of M beside the new rate.

On the Manual Rate (MAR) screen, check the amount which appears in the Ben field of the New column. If this is not the correct PPS rate, overstrike the defaulted amount with the correct daily rate.

Note: the rate of PPS does not include Pension Supplement unless the customer is Age Pension age.

Press [Enter] to navigate back to the AR screen, finalise the assessment and record any notes in the Notes field.

If the surviving partner was a joint owner of any assets with their late partner, full ownership of the assets legally transfers to the surviving partner following the death of their partner. However, for social security purposes:

  • the surviving partner is only assessed as owning 50%
  • the system will only read that the surviving partner owns 50% of the asset. This is not updated until the surviving partner advises the transfer of assets

If total combined assets (and/or income) could affect the survivor's rate, code a bereavement review for 16 weeks after date of death.

1

Paying the lump sum bereavement payment + Read more ...

The surviving PPP customer may be entitled to bereavement entitlements if:

  • they are a long-term social security recipient, and
  • their deceased partner was a long-term social security recipient or a pensioner

If the PPP customer is entitled to a lump sum bereavement payment (LBP), a BRV activity will appear on the Activity List (AL) screen:

  • manually calculate the lump sum bereavement payment. See Calculation of a lump sum bereavement payment
  • Rent Assistance (RA):
    • RA is included in the previous combined member of a couple rate for the LBP calculation when the customer and partner with a child were paying rent below the threshold to receive RA with FTB, but above the threshold to receive RA as a couple, and they received 50% of their RA each with their primary benefit
    • if the survivor is entitled to RA as part of FTB after the death of their partner (due to different threshold), then RA paid with FTB is not included in the new single rate calculation
  • select the BRV activity and complete the fields on the Lump Sum Bereavement (LSB) screen:
    • Continuity indicator: whether deceased customer was receiving continuous payments for 52 weeks or more
    • Member of a couple rate: key the fortnightly rate received by the customer prior to death including add-ons
    • Total paid in BRV: key the amount that the customer has been paid since death
    • Single Rate: key the new fortnightly single rate to be paid to the PPP customer
    • Taxable Amt of Single Rate: key only the taxable amount to be paid fortnightly
  • press [Enter]. The system will complete the remaining fields
  • check and amend if necessary the details on the LSB screen
  • check warnings and amount paid on AR screen. If all correct, finalise the activity

2

Check other payments + Read more ...

The customer remains eligible for PP at the single rate during the bereavement period following the partner's death. This rate was set manually when recording the death action.

The customer can be exempted from mutual obligation requirements for the 14 week bereavement period. If the customer is pregnant when their partner died, and the expected date of confinement is more than 14 weeks in the future, the participation exemption is extended for the duration of the pregnancy. See Mutual obligation requirement exemptions.

If the deceased customer was receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) or Child Care Subsidy (CCS) for any children, invite the new carer to claim FTB and or CCS for the children.

3

Record details on a DOC + Read more ...

Record the details of the bereavement on a DOC.

4

Employment + Read more ...

If the customer starts work or is currently working, working credits may enable the customer to keep some of their income support payment while they are working.

If the customer's income reduces their fortnightly rate to nil and some of that income is employment income, the customer may remain current at nil rate for up to 6 fortnights and retain certain benefits. Note: if the customer's deceased partner had any working credits, these credits cannot be transferred to the customer.

5

Action to take after death action is completed + Read more ...

Service Profiling

If the customer is on 2 weekly reporting because their partner had employment income, refresh Service Profiling:

  • go to the Profile Summary (PQS) screen
  • key 'Y' in the Reprofile field
  • complete activity

Income Streams

The person who purchased an income stream can nominate another person to receive the income after their death, for example their spouse or partner. This person is the reversionary beneficiary.

If the surviving partner is granted a reversionary income stream, assess from the date it is entitled to be received.

There may be a gap between the death of the original beneficiary and the date the reversionary beneficiary starts to receive payments. A lump sum arrears payment is made to the reversionary beneficiary to cover the gap. The lump sum is assessed as income from an income stream for the period to which it relates. This may result in a debt if the person has been receiving an income support payment. Waiver provisions came into effect from 9 May 2018:

  • if the lump sum arrears results in a debt during the bereavement period for the surviving spouse, and certain conditions are met
  • only debts within the 14 week bereavement period can be waived under these rules

The 9 May 2018 rules are not retrospective. Any of these debts that occur before 9 May 2018 cannot be waived using these rules.

See the References page for a link to the legislation.

Advice to service providers

Identify any service providers to be advised of the death.

6

Book an appointment + Read more ...

Book an appointment for 2 weeks before the end of the bereavement period.

The purpose of this appointment will be to run Assisted Customer Claim to start a transfer to another income support payment. Generally, this will be to PPS.
