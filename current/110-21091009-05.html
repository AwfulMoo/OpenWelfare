    Suggested dialogue

    This table is intended to help staff with the customer conversation.

    Item

    Description

    Auto registered

    If you are:

    • an eligible current customer you do not need to do anything. Your settlement payment will be paid to you automatically. This will be paid into the bank account where we pay your regular Centrelink payment
    • a former customer who is eligible for a settlement payment, and you received a refund, you do not need to do anything. Your settlement payment will be paid into the same bank account where your refund was paid

    Former customer - Not registered

    If you are a former customer who is eligible for a settlement payment, you will find an online ‘Income Compliance settlement task pending’ in your Centrelink online account.

    You will need to complete this task by confirming your phone number, address and bank account details to get your settlement payment.

    More information for eligible group members
    • The settlement payment is in place of interest you could have earned. It is based on the amount of debt repayments you made and for the period you were without the money
    • People who paid back more and were without their money for longer, will get larger payments
    • Many people who only paid back a small amount or were refunded soon after they made the payment, will receive a smaller payment
    • Some people will get less than $10 as a settlement payment

    If a customer enquires about their settlement payment eligibility
    • You need to have made repayments on an eligible debt to get a settlement payment
    • If your debt was based in full, or in part, on averaged ATO income information and you paid back some, or all, of this type of debt, you may be eligible for a settlement payment
    • You may also be eligible if all of the following applies:
      • your debt was based in full or in part on averaged ATO income information
      • your debt was recalculated based on information you gave us, e.g. payslips
      • you paid back more than the recalculated amount
    • Services Australia is aiming to deliver settlement payments as quickly as possible to eligible group members, but the settlement process will take time
    • Until the categorisation process is finalised we cannot tell you how much you will be paid. We will send you a letter in the first half of 2022 to confirm the settlement amount you will be paid
    • There is no need to contact us unless you have been requested to

    Who is not eligible for a settlement payment
    • You are not eligible if you did not repay any money towards your debt, even if it was based on averaged ATO income information. These debts were changed to zero
    • You are not eligible if your debt was calculated using averaged ATO income information and both of these apply:
      • it was recalculated based on other information you gave us, e.g. payslips or bank statements
      • the amount you have paid was less than or equal to the recalculated debt
    • If your debt was not based on averaged ATO income, you are not eligible for a settlement payment

    Pre-2015 debts

    Eligible categorisation only applies to income compliance debts raised from 1  July  2015 onwards.

    Your debt was raised on <date debt raised> which was before 1  July  2015.

    This means your debt is not eligible for a settlement payment.

    FTB debts

    Settlement payments do not apply to family assistance debts.

    Your debt was for family assistance payments. This means your debt is not eligible for a settlement payment.

    Non Income Compliance debts

    Settlement payments only apply to income compliance debts.

    Your debt was not as a result of an income compliance review.

    <Where possible, advise customer the reason for the debt>.

    Disputes

    If the customer disagrees with the categorisation decision, ask them:

    • could you please explain why you do not agree with the categorisation
    • you have been categorised under <insert category> because <advise the customer the reason for the categorisation and an explanation as to why they fall under this category>

    If the customer still does not agree with the category after the explanation of decision, see Tier 1.

    Tier 1

    If you think we have not included all of your debts or not taken into account repayments made on a debt or think your debt was based on averaged ATO income information, you have the option to lodge a dispute.

    This dispute will involve a review of the categorisation outcome (Tier 1). Once a review has been completed we will contact you to notify you of the outcome.

    You can lodge a dispute by accessing the Income Compliance settlement query menu item in your online account or I can assist you to lodge the query now.

    Tier 2

    If you still disagree with the outcome of the category decision review (Tier 1), you can request an independent review (Tier 2). The review will be completed by the Scheme Assurer who is not part of Centrelink. They are qualified to conduct a review and have been approved by the Federal Court to do this.

    There is no further course of action if you disagree with the outcome of the Tier 2 dispute.

    If a customer enquires about their payment

    Script to assist staff in taking these calls:

    • We are working to make payments as soon as possible
    • There are a number of steps that must be completed before we can start making settlement payments
    • This is a complex legal process and a number of calculations are required to make sure everyone gets their correct settlement payment
    • We apologise that at the moment we are unable to tell you how much you will be paid and when
    • We can confirm that the amount people will receive depends on when they repaid money on eligible debts, how much was repaid and when they were refunded
    • We are working hard to have each person’s settlement amount calculated correctly as soon as possible
    • We will write to you when this step has been completed to confirm your settlement payment amount

    If pressed:

    • I acknowledge that this has been a frustrating situation for you. I’m sorry you’ve had to go through this
    • For more information about how the settlement amount is calculated for eligible group members you can search ‘Class Action’ on the Services Australia website
    • If you want to see more details about the process, search ‘Implementation Plan’ on the Services Australia website
    • Once all the steps in the implementation plan have been completed, we will start making payments to everyone

    If pressed even further:

    • I am sorry it’s been a long process for you. We are working hard to finalise the class action
