This document explains the process to follow when a customer advises they receive Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) income. It shows how the different types of payments from DVA affect entitlement to payments administered by Centrelink.

Customer with income from the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)

The DVA Clearance Team:

  • has access to view the DVA customer database, which has details of most payments delivered by DVA
  • performs the clearance role for Centrelink with DVA, and
  • verifies and codes all DVA income and benefits declared by the customer on the Department of Veterans' Affairs Summary (DVAS) screen

Multiple entitlement exclusion

Payability of a benefit, pension, supplement or card, can be affected by multiple entitlement exclusion. Customers may not be able to receive a payment from both Centrelink and DVA.

Payments treated as income

Some DVA payments are treated as income for social security purposes. These payments include:

  • War Widow(er)'s Pension (WWP) (paid under VEA) and Wholly Dependent Partner (paid under Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2004 (MRCA)) are treated as income for the customer's partner
  • Temporary Incapacity Allowance (paid under VEA). Only payable for periods of incapacity before 20 September 2011
  • Dependant Allowance (paid under VEA). Only payable for periods before 20 September 2009
  • Periodic Eligible Young Person payments (paid under Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2004 (MRCA))
  • Adequate Means of Support. There have been no new grants for Adequate Means of Support since 6 June 1986, but a small number may currently still be in receipt of this payment

Payments treated as exempt income

Some DVA payments are exempt under the income test for social security purposes. These include add on payments made by DVA, which are similar to Centrelink payments and payments made to veterans for their disabilities, such as:

  • Attendant Allowance
  • Clothing Allowance
  • Compensatory in nature lump sums:
    • Eligible Young Person
    • Other Dependant
    • Permanent Impairment
    • Wholly Dependent Partner
  • The Safety, Rehabilitation and Compensation (Defence Related Claims) Act 1988 (DRCA) Supplement
  • Energy Supplement
  • Ex-gratia lump sum Prisoner of War (POW) payments
  • Gold Card Energy Supplement
  • Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2004 (MRCA) Supplement
  • Pension Supplement
  • Prisoner of War Recognition Supplement (POWR Supplement)
  • Recreation Transport Allowance
  • Rent Assistance
  • Remote Area Allowance
  • Telephone Allowance
  • Vehicle Assistance Scheme
  • Veterans' Supplement
  • Victoria Cross Allowance

From 1 January 2022, Adjusted Disability Pensions are exempt income. These include:

  • Disability Compensation Payment paid under the Veterans' Entitlements Act (VEA) 1986. For example:
    • General Rate Disability Pensions (10% to 100%)
    • Special Rate Disability Pensions (Totally and Permanently Incapacitated (TPI))
    • Temporarily Totally Incapacitated (TTI))
    • Extreme Disablement Adjustment (EDA), and
    • Intermediate Rate (IWP)
  • Specific Disability Compensation Payment Allowances (Items 1 to 15) (paid under VEA)
  • Periodic Permanent Impairment payments and Special Rate Disability Pension (paid under MRCA)

Social security Age Pension (paid by DVA)

Veterans in receipt of a Disability Compensation Payment from DVA, who are also eligible to receive a social security Age Pension (and their partners who are eligible for Age Pension), have the choice of receiving their Age Pension from either DVA or Centrelink. DVA acts as an 'agent' for the Department of Social Services and any Social Security Age Pension paid by DVA is assessed under social security law.

Until 20 March 2020, DVA could also make payments of Wife Pension to eligible spouses.

Incapacity Payments treated as compensation

Incapacity Payments made under Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2004 (MRCA) and the Safety, Rehabilitation and Compensation (Defence Related Claims) Act 1988 (DRCA) representing compensation for loss of income or earnings, are treated as compensation payments for social security purposes.

Education payments

DVA administers the Veterans' Children Education Scheme (VCES) and Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act Education and Training Scheme (MRCAETS). They are listed as prescribed educational scheme payments.

When a student receives VCES or MRCAETS, payment of any other Commonwealth income support or educational assistance is precluded under DVA legislation. Legislation administered by Centrelink differs, as it only precludes VCES and MRCAETS recipients from receiving Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy and ABSTUDY. This allows VCES and MRCAETS to be treated as income for some social security income support payments, however these customers are ineligible to receive payments from both Centrelink and DVA due to multiple entitlement exclusion rules in the DVA legislation.

Notification of DVA income

Notification is usually received either from DVA or from the customer. If a customer advises at the new claim stage that they are receiving DVA income, confirmation of the type of income and amount must be sought from the DVA Clearance Team.

DVA will notify Centrelink of a grant or change in DVA payments through the DVA clearance process. This process advises Centrelink of the payment types and amounts received by the customer. Adjustments to the customer's records are then made.

Rates are automatically adjusted for CPI for:

  • DVA Disability Compensation Payment
  • Disability Compensation Payment Allowances
  • War Widow(er)'s Pension
  • Wholly Dependent Partner payment
  • Orphan's Pension, and
  • Veterans' Supplement

There is no automatic rate adjustment for other payments, for example:

  • Service Pension
  • Veteran Payment
  • Social Security Age Pension, and
  • Income Support Supplement (ISS)

DVA income for YA, ABSTUDY and AIC Parental Income Test

A Parental Income Test (PIT) exemption may apply if a parent or guardian is receiving certain DVA payments and:

  • receives or applies for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC), or
  • has a dependent child(ren) who receive or apply for:
    • Youth Allowance (YA), or
    • ABSTUDY Living Allowance

The system will automatically apply the PIT exemption based on the DVA payment recorded on the parent's record, if that payment provides for a PIT exemption.

If the DVA payment does not provide a PIT exemption, it may be counted as taxable income (if taxable) or a tax free pension/benefit (if it is not taxable) under the Parental Income Test.

For further information, see Tax-free pensions and benefits for family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payments, and the Parental Income Test for Youth Allowance, ABSTUDY and Assistance for Isolated Children.

Family assistance

Some DVA payments are included in adjusted taxable income for Family Assistance purposes.

Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A is not income tested for customer and/or partners receiving:

  • DVA Service Pension
  • Veteran Payment
  • Income Support Supplement (ISS), or
  • Age Pension paid by DVA

These DVA payments must be coded and/or verified by the DVA Clearance Team only. The coding of these DVA payments will attract the maximum rate of FTB Part A. Incorrect coding of these payments may result in overpayments.

Customers must also include these payments in the family income estimate to determine the correct entitlement to FTB Part B and Child Care Subsidy (CCS).

When receiving a VCES or MRCAETS payment, FTB:

  • is payable when a child is aged under 16
  • cannot be paid when a child is aged 16 or over. In some circumstances it may be more beneficial if the person caring for the child claims FTB payments

Child Care Subsidy (CCS) is income tested. This means all customers need to provide an estimate of their annual income, even if receiving an income support payment.

For further information on the treatment of DVA income for Family Tax Benefit, see Updating income estimates for the current financial year.

Supplements

A number of supplements are available from DVA. They are mutually exclusive with those available from Centrelink.

Non-Taxable DVA Supplements:

These supplements are non-taxable and exempt from the income test for pensions and benefits. They are not included in the family assistance income tests or the Parental Income Test.

  • The Safety, Rehabilitation and Compensation (Defence Related Claims) Act 1988 (DRCA) Supplement - equivalent to Centrelink Pharmaceutical Allowance
  • Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2004 (MRCA) Supplement - equivalent to Centrelink Pharmaceutical Allowance
  • Veterans' Supplement - equivalent to Centrelink Pharmaceutical Allowance
  • DVA Energy Supplement - equivalent to Centrelink Energy Supplement
  • Gold Card Energy Supplement - equivalent to Centrelink Energy Supplement
  • DVA Remote Area Allowance
  • Pension Supplement (tax-exempt portion only)

Taxable DVA Supplements:

  • Pension Supplement - equivalent to Centrelink Pension Supplement; only payable with a qualifying income support payment, and includes a taxable component. Only the taxable component is included in the family assistance income tests.

A payment hierarchy for supplements is available from Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) clearances.

The Resources page has information about the different DVA payments. It has tables showing how DVA payments affect Social Security payments, DVA codes used on the DVA screen and DVA Clearance Team (SCS) contact details. There is also a link to the DVA website for their current pension rates.
