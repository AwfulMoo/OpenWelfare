1

Record is SUS-PIE and one or more entitlement periods have passed since suspension and actual income is lower than estimate, but both above the PIFA + Read more ...

Sam receives Youth Allowance (YA) and is classified as dependent. Sam's payment has been suspended because Services Australia did not receive his Parent(s)/guardian(s) actual taxable income by the estimate review date recorded on NHI.

Sam uploaded his parent(s)/guardian(s) Tax Notice of Assessment (TNOA) on 1 March is requesting payment restoration.

Key Information

  • Sam has an entitlement period code of '3' on the Centrelink Payments Calendar with his last EPED on 8 February 2022
  • Payment was suspended on 10 February
  • Sam called and uploaded the required information on 1 March
  • The income estimate and actual income are both above the PIFA
  • Estimate recorded on NHI is $70,000
  • Actual income is $62,000

As the actual income is lower than the estimate, a debt will not be generated. The Service Officer must update NHI with the actual taxable income and complete a restoration within the same activity.

Outcome

  • As the actual income is less than the estimate, the system will generate arrears from 1 January
  • Sam is only entitled to arrears from the Suspension date (10 February)
  • Service Officers must determine the rate of payment Sam was receiving prior to the actual income being provided and manually adjust the RATS screen to reflect a rate of payment with the estimate of $70,000 from 10 February until the most recent EPED, which is 22 February
  • The system will pay a rate based on the actual income for 23 February to 8 March
  • Due to a system limitation, staff cannot manually adjust a rate of payment within a current entitlement period

Example of calculation

Note: this is an example and does not reflect the correct Youth Allowance (YA) rate of payment

  • Sam's rate of Youth Allowance on an estimate of $70,000 was $350 per fortnight
  • $350÷14= $25 per day
  • The period of time from 10 - 22 February =13 days
  • $25 per day x 13 days = $325
  • Sam is entitled to arrears of $325 from 10 to 22 February

2

Record is SUS-PIE and one of more entitlement periods have passed and actual income is higher than estimate and above PIFA + Read more ...

Josh receives Youth Allowance (YA) and is classified as a dependent. His payment has been suspended because Services Australia did not receive his parent(s)/guardian(s) actual taxable income by the estimate review date recorded on NHI.

Josh contacts Services Australia and advises his parent(s)/guardian(s) Tax Notice of Assessment (TNOA) was uploaded on 13 March and is requesting his payment be restored.

Key information

  • Josh has an entitlement period code of '5' on the Centrelink Payments calendar with his last EPED on 9 February
  • Payment was suspended on 10 February
  • Josh uploaded his parent(s) TNA on 13 March
  • Josh contacted the Smart Centre on 25 March
  • Estimate recorded was $56,000
  • Actual income is $67,000

Outcome

  • as the actual taxable income is higher than the estimate, the system will generate a debt
  • as one or more entitlement period end dates (EPEDs) have passed, Josh is entitled to arrears from the suspension date (10 February)

For this scenario, two separate activities are necessary to prevent arrears being offset by an overpayment. The Service Officer must:

  • Update NHI with the actual income of $67,000
  • Finalise the activity and send the debt for investigation (INV)
  • Once the activity has been completed, restore the payment in a separate activity so the system will not offset the debt with arrears

Josh is entitled to arrears from 10 February until 12 March based on the estimate of $56,000 and from 13 March until 23 March based on the actual of $67,000.

The Service Officer should calculate the rate Josh was receiving based on the estimate and manually adjust the rate via the RATS screen to pay arrears from 10 February until 12 March.

The system will pay the correct rate from 13 March onwards.

Example of calculation

Note: this is an example and does not reflect the correct Youth Allowance (YA) rate

  • Josh's rate of Youth Allowance on the estimate of $56,000 is $420/fn
  • $420÷14= $30 per day
  • The period of time from 10 February until 12 March = 31 days
  • $30 per day x 31 days = $930
  • Josh is entitled to arrears of $930 from 10 February to 12 March
  • The system will generate the correct rate of payment from 13 March

3

Record is SUS-PIE and information is provided within the same entitlement period as the suspension + Read more ...

Sarah is receiving Youth Allowance (YA) and is classified as a dependent. Sarah's payment has been suspended because Services Australia did not receive her parent(s)/guardian(s) actual taxable income by the estimate review date recorded on NHI.

Sarah contacts Services Australia as her parent(s)/guardian(s) have not received their Tax Notice of Assessment (TNOA) and wish to provide a new estimate. Sarah has contacted with her mother on 19 April.

Key Information

  • Sarah has an entitlement period code of 'C' on the Centrelink Payments calendar with last EPED on 13 April
  • Payment suspended on 15 April
  • Sarah has called on 19 April
  • Estimate recorded is $30,000
  • New estimate provided by parents is $40,000

Outcome

In this scenario, the previous and new estimates provided are both below the PIFA, and an entitlement period end date (EPED) has not passed since Sarah's payment was suspended. This means Sarah is not entitled to arrears as she is already receiving the maximum rate, and there are no outstanding EPEDs.

The Service Officer should update NHI with the new estimate, record a new review date and restore the payment within the same activity.
