This page contains the processing steps for the cessation of ABSTUDY away from home rate.

On this page:

Change in circumstances and impact of eligibility

Step

Action

1

Change of circumstances + Read more ...

A change of circumstances has been notified in relation to a student who is currently receiving the away from home rate of ABSTUDY, which may affect their eligibility for the away from home rate. Determine whether the change in circumstance will affect the eligibility for the away from home rate.

If the student:

  • is a secondary school student moving or has moved back home who is in receipt of living away from home entitlements, go to Step 2
  • is an Australian Apprentice whose parents have moved the family home closer to where the recipient is working, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • has circumstances that made the student eligible for the away from home rate to cease (for example, racial discrimination at a closer school has now ceased or parents have moved the family home), go to Step 5
  • has ceased the study for which approval for payment at the away from home rate had been given, go to Step 6
  • has completed the study for which approval for payment at the away from home rate had been given, go to Step 7
  • is moving or has moved back home and is not in the above groups, go to Step 3

2

Secondary school student in receipt of living away from home entitlements and is moving or has moved back home + Read more ...

If the move is:

  • temporary, the student retains eligibility for the away from home rate whilst returning to the parental home for a short period for example, vacation period. Procedure ends here
  • permanent and the student has contacted Smart Centre Call or a Service Centre:
    • do not code any change of circumstances on the student's file
    • send an ABSTUDY action request Fast Note with details to the relevant ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing for action. Select Auto Text, use Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > Update > ABSTUDY Boarder action request. Include dates the student left the school, if the student completed the term/year, name of school/board provider and new school details
    • ensure student's contact details are correct
    • procedure ends here

When information is received at the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing site regarding a change in circumstances for these students, go to Step 6.

3

Temporary or permanent move + Read more ...

A student who returns permanently to the family home or returns temporarily and does not maintain their residence ceases eligibility to the away from home rate from the date they return home.

If the student has moved home:

  • permanently, their eligibility for the away from home rate ceases from the date they return home. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • temporarily, go to Step 4

4

Temporary move home + Read more ...

A secondary student who returns to the permanent home on a temporary basis:

  • If this is during their course of study (such as study vacation period), the student can continue to receive the away from home rate for the period of the return to the family home. Procedure ends here

A secondary non-schooling or tertiary student has returned home temporarily:

  • There is no clear cut distinction between maintaining and not maintaining accommodation. Each case must be judged on its own merits
  • Note: if the student is in receipt of the Residential Cost Option (RCO) and they are planning on accessing RCO again in the next available study period, they are entitled to receive the away from home rate during their temporary return home over the vacation period. Only those RCO students who plan on accessing RCO again in the next available study period are considered to be maintaining their term address

Will the student continue to maintain their accommodation away from home whilst staying at the parental home temporarily?

  • Yes, the student is still entitled to the away from home rate. Procedure ends here
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

5

Continuity of study provisions + Read more ...

See Assessing continuity of study for ABSTUDY away from home rate to determine if the continuity of study provisions apply.

Does the student meet continuity of study provisions?

  • Yes, the student is still entitled to the away from home rate under continuity of study provisions. Update the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home (NIH) screen End Date: field with the date the circumstances changed. Create a new NIH screen and update the fields as follows:
    • Action, key AFH
    • Start/Reject Reason, key APA
    • Start Date, key the day after the previous AFH reason was ceased
    • Source: and Date of Receipt, key as appropriate
    • Action, key I
    • Record a DOC on the student's record, detailing the decision. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

The end date of away from home rate + Read more ...

The end date of away from home rate for a student depends on the student's circumstances. If the student:

  • returns permanently to the family home, the student ceases to be eligible for the away from home rate from the date they return home. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • has discontinued the course of study before the course work is complete, they cease to be eligible for the away from home rate from the date the student discontinued. See Step 2 in Table 2
  • has completed the term of study, go to Step 7
  • has a change in the circumstances that gained approval for away from home rate, go to Step 8

7

A student who completes study + Read more ...

If the student has completed the term of study, the student's circumstances determine the end date to be applied.

Note: a tertiary or secondary non-schooling student is not considered to have completed a course of study at the end of each year of study in a multiple year course. Completion of a course occurs at the end of the total course period. For example, a 3 year course is completed at the end of the third year.

A secondary student:

  • who boards at a boarding school, in an approved ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, or at a hostel which is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement) and:
    • whose entitlements are paid to the boarding school or Hostel as an ABSTUDY boarding instalment, and
    • who completes a term of study is eligible to the away from home rate until the last date of the term entitlement period
  • who boards privately, or whose board entitlements are paid to a Hostel as fortnightly-in-arrears, and who completes a term of study is eligible for the away from home rate up until the last day of the school term
  • who is a non-schooling or tertiary student who completes study is eligible for the away from home rate until the last formal day of the course

Note: a student who completes all course work earlier than the formal completion date is still considered to have completed the course and is eligible for the full entitlement period. This often occurs for students in Year 11 or 12 who complete course work earlier than the official study end date, and are released from school early.

See Step 4 in Table 2.

8

Change in circumstances + Read more ...

A student or Australian Apprentice who has had a change in their circumstances which gained initial approval for away from home rate, and who is no longer eligible to away from home rate under continuity of study is eligible for the away from home rate until the date of the change in circumstances.

See Step 1 in Table 2.

No longer eligible for away from home rate

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

Student circumstances + Read more ...

If the student:

  • returns to the family home permanently, or returns temporarily and does not maintain their residence, they cease to be eligible for the away from home rate from the date they return home, go to Step 2
  • discontinues study in a course before the course work is complete, they cease to be eligible for the away from home rate from the date the student discontinues study:
    • a tertiary or secondary non-schooling student is not considered to have completed a course of study at the end of each year of study in a multiple year course
    • completion of a course occurs at the end of the total course period. For example, a 3 year course is completed at the end of the third year. Note: a student who completes all coursework earlier than the formal completion date of a course is still considered to have completed the course and is eligible for the full entitlement period
    • go to Step 2
  • is an Australian Apprentice whose parents have moved the family home closer to where the recipient is working. The recipient is no longer eligible for the away from home rate from the date of the change in circumstances. Procedure ends here

2

Student residence circumstances + Read more ...

For students residing in:

  • a boarding school, a hostel that is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement or an approved ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, go to Step 3
  • a private board arrangement or boarding at a hostel that is not a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement, go to Step 4

For tertiary or secondary non-schooling students, go to Step 7.

3

Student is residing in a boarding school, a hostel that is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement or ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement + Read more ...

Is the student's date of discontinuation of study on or after 1 January 2019?

  • Yes, students who reside in a boarding school, a hostel that is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement or ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, and discontinue study before the completion of a term are only eligible for away from home rate up to the date of discontinuation. Go to Step 4
  • No, and the student is:
    • residing in a boarding school or an ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, go to Step 5
    • residing in a Hostel, go to Step 6

4

Secondary school boarding student + Read more ...

A secondary school boarding student who discontinues study before the completion of a school term is only eligible for away from home rate up to the date of discontinuation.

Update the following on the student record:

  • correct the Student End Date on the Education Details (EDC) screen. If the student is ceasing in the current academic year, delete the following academic year if it has been inserted on EDC
  • insert a new screen on the School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen and update the following fields:
    • Event Date, key the day after the board arrangement ceased
    • Annual Fees/Tuition Amount, leave blank
    • Boarding School/Hostel Action, key No longer boarding at the school or hostel ('NPB')
    • Annual Boarding School/Hostel Amount, leave blank
    • Actual Term Start Date, leave blank
    • Weekly Private Board Amount, leave blank
    • Scholarship Contribution Amount, leave blank
  • if coded, end the term address on the Address Details (AD) screen
  • insert a new screen on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and update:
    • Event Date, key the day after the student stopped paying board or rent
    • Address Type, key Home ('HOM')
    • Home Ownership, key Non homeowner ('NHO')
    • Type of Rent, key No Rent Paid ('NRP')
    • Sharer Status, key Lives With Parents ('LWP')
  • correct away from home details on the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen by keying an End Reason and End Date. The End Date is the date confirmed for the last day of attendance
  • end Payee/Debtor details on ABSTUDY Payee/Debtor Details (EYPY) screen. For students residing in:
    • a boarding school, a hostel that is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement or ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, use the last day of the term entitlement period
    • a private board arrangement or boarding at a hostel that is not a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement, use the day the arrangement ceased

Note: the EYPY screen does not need to be end dated if new study details are to be coded for Schooling B Award. See School Fees Allowance (Group 2) for correct coding of SFA Group 2 for students in private board arrangements and hostels that are not signatories to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement

Record a DOC on the student's record detailing the reason for the cessation of away from home rate.

Go to Step 11.

5

Student is residing in a boarding school or ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement + Read more ...

Prior to 1 January 2019:

  • a school with a condition of enrolment that no refund will occur for a student who discontinues study before the completion of a term, was entitled to retain the full term payment on behalf of a student who has ceased
  • a boarding institution cannot retain payments for a student who does not commence study in a term, irrespective of the boarding organisation's enrolment and fee payment policy, see the Resources page for examples
  • whilst a boarding institution may be entitled to retain away from home entitlements paid term-in-advance for a period after the student discontinues, the student is still deemed to have ceased eligibility to away from home rate from the date their circumstances changed
  • a boarding school can only retain the correct rate of the full term payment. This means any change in a student's circumstance which changes the rate of entitlement part way through a term (for example, loss of Parental Income Test Exemption, change in Family Pool, gain a new parent) and after the term-in-advance payment has been made, must still be applied to the term-in-advance payment. This may mean that the boarding school has been overpaid. See the Resources page for an example

Update the following on the student record:

  • education details on the Education (EDC) screen, key the student end date. This date will be coded as the last day in the term entitlement period unless the boarding school has granted a refund due to ceasing early. The student end date will be the day before refund takes effect. If the student is ceasing in the current academic year, delete the following academic year if it has been inserted on EDC
  • boarding details, insert a new screen on the Boarding Details (EYSB) screen and update the following fields:
    • Event Date, key the first day of the next term entitlement period unless the boarding school has been granted a refund due to ceasing early. The event date will be the first day of the refund period
    • Annual Fees/Tuition Amount, leave blank
    • Boarding School/Hostel Action, key NPB (No longer boarding at the school or hostel)
    • Annual Boarding School/Hostel Amount, leave blank
    • Actual Term Start Date, leave blank
    • Weekly Private Board Amount, leave blank
  • if coded, end the term address on the Address Details (AD) screen
  • accommodation details, insert a new screen on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen. In the following fields:
    • Event Date, key the first date of the next term entitlement period unless the boarding school has been granted a refund due to ceasing early. The event date will be the first day of the refund period
    • Address Type, key Home ('HOM')
    • Home Ownership, key Non homeowner (NHO)
    • Type of Rent, key No Rent Paid ('NRP')
    • Sharer Status, key Lives With Parents ('LWP')
  • correct away from home details on the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen by coding an End Reason and End Date. The End Date will be:
    • the last day of the relevant term entitlement period, or
    • where a refund has been granted, the last day of the refund period
  • end Payee/Debtor details on ABSTUDY Payee/Debtor Details (EYPY) screen using the last day of the term entitlement period. Note: the EYPY screen does not need to be ended if new study details are to be coded for Schooling B Award

Record a DOC on the student's record detailing the reason for the cessation of away from home rate.

Go to Step 11.

6

Student is residing in a hostel that is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement + Read more ...

Prior to 1 January 2019, if a student leaves the hostel or ceases study, the hostel that is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement is entitled to retain payment of the away from home rate allowances as a lump sum term-in-advance payment, see the Resources page.

The amount of lump sum that the hostel is entitled to keep is determined by the date the student leaves the hostel or ceases study and when they became eligible for payments in that term.

Note: a hostel cannot retain payments for a student who does not commence study in a term, irrespective of the Hostel Agreement. See the Resources page for examples.

Whilst a hostel may be entitled to retain away from home entitlements paid term-in-advance for a period after the student discontinues, the student is still deemed to have ceased eligibility to away from home rate from the date their circumstances changed.

Note: a hostel can only retain the correct rate of the full term payment. This means any change in a student's circumstances which changes the rate of entitlement part way through a term (for example, loss of Parental Income Test Exemption, change in Family Pool, gain a new parent) and after the term-in-advance payment has been made, must still be applied to the term-in-advance payment. This may mean that the hostel has been overpaid. See the Resources page for an example.

If the student became eligible for payments from:

  • the commencement of the term entitlement period, go to Step 8
  • a date after the commencement of the term entitlement period, go to Step 9

If the student does not resume study in the term entitlement period, go to Step 10.

7

Tertiary or secondary non-schooling student + Read more ...

For students who return permanently to the family home or return temporarily and do not maintain their term residence:

  • update education details on the Education Details (EDC) screen by keying the student end date if they have ceased study
  • correct away from home details on the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen by coding an End Reason and End Date
  • if coded, end term address on the Address Details (AD) screen
  • update accommodation details by inserting a new screen on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen. In the following fields:
    • Address Type, key Home (HOM)
    • Event Date, key the day student stopped paying rent
    • Home Ownership, key Non homeowner (NHO)
    • Sharer Status, key this mandatory field as appropriate, record the known details for example, lives with parents (LWP) or not a sharer (NAS)
    • Rent verification, leave blank

Advise the student they are not entitled to RA, but they should advise if their circumstances change and they commence to pay rent.

For further information, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA).

Record a DOC on the student's record detailing the reason for the cessation of away from home rate.

If the student has commenced a new course of study, see Changing or correcting course of study and/or educational institution for ABSTUDY students.

Procedure ends here.

8

Student became eligible for payments from the start of the term entitlement period + Read more ...

If the student left the hostel or ceased study:

  • within the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the lump sum the hostel keeps is the total amount of Living Allowance the student would have been eligible for, at the away from home rate, over the period from the start of the term entitlement period to the sixth Friday of the school term
  • after the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the lump sum the hostel keeps is the total amount of living allowance the student is eligible for, at the away from home rate, for the period between the start of the term entitlement period and the earlier date of ceasing study or leaving the hostel

See the Resources page for examples.

On the Education Details (EDC) screen, correct the Student End Date. If the student ceased study or left the hostel:

  • within the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, update with the sixth Friday
  • after the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, update with the earlier of the date they:
    • ceased study or
    • left the hostel

Insert a new screen on the Boarding Details (EYSB) screen:

  • if the student ceased study or left the hostel within the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the Event Date will be the sixth Friday + 1
  • if the student ceased study or left the hostel after the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the Event Date will be the date they ceased study or left
  • Annual Fees/Tuition Amount, leave blank
  • Boarding School/Hostel Action, 'NPB' (No longer boarding at the school or hostel)
  • Annual Boarding School/Hostel Amount, leave blank
  • Actual Term Start Date, leave blank
  • Weekly Private Board Amount, leave blank

On the Address Details (AD) screen, end the term address

Update accommodation details by inserting a new screen on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen. If the student ceased study or left the hostel:

  • within the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the Event Date will be the sixth Friday + 1
  • after the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the Event Date will be the date they ceased study or left
  • Home Ownership, key Non homeowner (NHO)
  • Sharer Status, key Not a Sharer (NAS)

On the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen, correct Away from home details by keying an End Reason and End Date

On the ABSTUDY Payee/Debtor Details (EYPY) screen, end Payee/Debtor details. If student ceased or left the hostel:

  • within the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, update with sixth Friday
  • after the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, update with either the date they ceased study or the date they left the hostel, whichever is earlier

Note: the EYPY screen does not need to be ended if new study details are to be coded for Schooling B Award.

Record a DOC on the student's record detailing the reason for the cessation away from home rate.

Go to Step 11.

9

Student became eligible for payments from a date after the start of the term entitlement period + Read more ...

If the student left the hostel or ceased study:

  • within the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the lump sum the hostel retains is the total amount of Living Allowance the student would have been eligible for, at the away from home rate, from the start of payment of the Living Allowance to the sixth Friday. The Event Date is the 6th Friday +1, provided this does not extend beyond the end of the term entitlement period
  • after the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the lump sum the hostel retains is the total amount of Living Allowance the student is eligible for, at the away from home rate, over the period from the commencement of the term entitlement period and the earlier date of ceasing study or leaving the hostel. The Event date is the date the student ceases studies +1

See the Resources page for examples.

Navigate to the student record:

  • update education details on the Education Details (EDC) screen by correcting the student end date. If the student ceased study or left hostel:
    • within the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, update with sixth Friday
    • after the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, update with either the date they ceased study or the date they left the hostel, whichever is earlier
  • update the boarding details by inserting a new screen on the Boarding Details (EYSB) screen:
    • if the student ceased study or left the hostel within the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the Event Date will be the sixth Friday + 1
    • if the student ceased study or left the hostel after the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the Event Date will be the date they ceased study or left
    • Annual Fees/Tuition Amount, leave blank
    • Boarding School/Hostel Action, key NPB (No longer boarding at the school or hostel)
    • Annual Boarding School/Hostel Amount, leave blank
    • Actual Term Start Date, leave blank
    • Weekly Private Board Amount, leave blank
  • update accommodation details by inserting a new screen on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen. If the student ceased study or left the hostel:
    • within the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the Event Date will be the sixth Friday + 1
    • after the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, the Event Date will be the date they ceased study or left +1
    • Home Ownership, key Non homeowner (NHO)
    • Sharer Status, key Not a Sharer (NAS)
  • if coded, end term address on the Address Details (AD) screen
  • correct away from home details on the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen by keying an End Reason and End Date. The End date will align with the Student end date
  • end Payee/Debtor details on ABSTUDY Payee/Debtor Details (EYPY) screen. If student ceased study or left the hostel:
    • within the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, update with sixth Friday
    • after the first 6 weeks of taking up residence, update with either the date they ceased study or the date they left the hostel, whichever is earlier. Note: the EYPY screen will not need to be ended if new study details are to be coded for Schooling B Award

Record a DOC on the student's record detailing the reason for the cessation away from home rate.

Record a DOC on the student's record detailing the reason for the cessation away from the home rate.

Go to Step 11.

11

Reassessing students last day of eligibility + Read more ...

The reassessment of a student's last day of eligibility to the away from home rate may create an overpaid amount. The overpaid amount can be a:

  • student residual boarding allowance
  • ABSTUDY boarding instalment paid to the board institution
  • term-in-advance boarding allowance paid to the board institution prior to 1 January 2019, see the Resources page

Student residual board allowance

If a debt activity occurs for any residual board allowance paid on behalf of the student, raise a debt against the responsible payee see the Resources page of Raising Fares Allowance (FAA) debts. A DOC must be recorded on the student's record of action undertaken.

ABSTUDY boarding instalments

If a debt activity occurs for any portion of an instalment paid to a boarding institution on behalf of the student, a debt should be raised against the responsible organisation payee. The ABSTUDY Payee/Debtor Details (EYPY) screen will hold the organisation details and the debt activity will be automatically transferred to the debtor organisation.

Term-in-advance boarding allowance paid to a boarding institution prior to 1 January 2019

If a debt amount occurs from the term-in-advance amount paid to the boarding institution, a debt amount should be raised only for the amount that relates to the period that a boarding institution does not retain the away from home payments. Record a DOC on both the student and board organisation record of action undertaken. See the Resources page for information about term-in-advance payments.

In this circumstance, whilst the boarding institution, or Hostel, is entitled to retain the way from home entitlements already received via a term-in-advance payment for a period after the student discontinues, the student is deemed to have ceased eligibility to the away from home rate from the date their circumstances changed.

The student is not entitled to any fortnightly residual boarding allowance from the date they are deemed to have ceased eligibility for the away from home rate. Residual boarding allowance components will need to be ceased by coding Rate Component Override (EPRC) screen.

Access the EPRC screen:

  • use the field (?) help to key the relevant values:
    • start date for the override in the Start Date field (for example, student end date + 1)
    • end date in the End Date field (if non-standard rate will be for a defined period, for example, end of the term-in-advance entitlement period)
    • override type in the Component field
    • the reason in the Reason field
    • the amount in the Amount field (which will be $0.00)
    • Source and DOR fields, and press [Enter]
  • finalise activity on Assessment Results (AR) screen, ensuring that the residual boarding amount has ceased from the correct date, and
  • record a DOC advising reason for decision

Coding EPRC to cease the residual boarding allowance to a customer may result in incorrect adjustments of boarding entitlement and/or transfer of unused School Fees Allowance (SFA) (Group 2) components. These amounts will need to be adjusted via the Daily Rate Component (RAC) or One Off Payment (OOP) screens to ensure no incorrect arrears or debts are raised, see Payment of arrears after reassessment.

The student cannot access alternate ABSTUDY allowances for any period the boarding institution, or Hostel, retains ABSTUDY away from home allowances. The Resources page contains an example.

If the student has commenced a new course of study, see Changing or correcting course of study and/or educational institution for ABSTUDY students.
