3

Customer current on DVA payment. Reverse Clearance process is required + Read more ...

Complete Request for a DVA Clearance pro-forma Part A and email to DVA Income Support Team.

The pro-forma is used to advise DVA about the Centrelink new claim, to request cancellation of a current DVA payment and obtain the following details:

  • DVA debt details (if any)
  • Work Bonus Balance at date prior to Centrelink grant date
  • Any grandfathered account based income stream under pre 1/1/15 rules
  • Whether the customer is paid under Transitional rules

The date of cancellation for the DVA payment is to be negotiated with DVA, depending on the customer's individual circumstances, keeping the following principles in mind:

  • If the customer is receiving Veteran Payment from DVA:
    • Request DVA to cancel the Customer's payment at the end of the current DVA entitlement period, and grant the Centrelink payment from the following day (i.e. Date Paid To + 1), as the customer will receive a lower fortnightly rate with Centrelink than they would with DVA
    • It is a Government priority that Veteran Payment customers are not disadvantaged when they move onto a Centrelink income support payment
  • If the customer is receiving Service Pension from DVA and will be granted a Pension by Centrelink, the date of DVA cancellation would generally be backdated to the date that the customer claimed the Centrelink payment, however the start date is usually negotiated with DVA
  • If the customer is receiving Service Pension from DVA and will be granted an Allowance by Centrelink, or if Pension is not maximum rate, it may be more beneficial to the customer to request DVA to cancel their payment at the end of the current DVA entitlement period, and grant the Centrelink payment from the following day (i.e. Date Paid To + 1) because the customer would generally receive a lower fortnightly rate with Centrelink than they would with DVA

Clearances resulting from DVA grants and changes to payments under the Veterans’ Entitlements Act 1986

Table 4

Step

Action

1

DVA transmits details of a new grant or change to DVA income support payments, Disability Compensation Payments and allowances, or limiting amount deductions + Read more ...

These details are loaded automatically onto the customer's record and updated on the Veterans Affairs Pension Summary (DVAS) screen, which may cause a reassessment of the customer's entitlements.

An automatic DOC will be recorded as part of this process that will contain details of the information received from DVA including:

  • the date of receipt of clearance
  • details of the DVA payments
  • date of grant/variation of payment
  • DVA reference number

If a multiple entitlement exclusion exists, the customer's Centrelink payment will be cancelled.

For periods before 1 January 2022

Where ADP reduces a Centrelink income support payment, the system will auto calculate a rate of Defence Force Income Support Allowance (DFISA) and send the rate to DVA.

If the change creates an overpayment

In most cases the system will calculate any overpayment and load it on the Debt Management Information System (DMIS) to be sent and recovered by DVA as part of an automated recovery process. DVA will hold arrears of the granted payment pending notification of any Centrelink overpayment amount.

Once DVA has recovered a debt amount from a customer's DVA arrears, a notice is sent to Centrelink which contains details of the amount recovered. These repayment details are recorded onto the customer's debt record. The debt will be finalised automatically.

If DVA do not hold enough arrears to recover Centrelink debt, a residual debt remains on the customer record. DMIS will issue an account payable letter (SS340) to the customer.

The system may be unable to automatically calculate a debt amount. This may be due to:

  • a limiting date
  • cancelled payment record
  • manual rate case, or
  • where conflicting data exists

In these cases an MFU will be produced and a manual clearance will be required. This will be completed by the DVA Clearance Team (SCS).

If an MFU has been created, confirm the details in the DVA VIEW system, select the MFU and Code the Veterans Affairs Pension (DVA) screen with the details of the payment DVA has granted or adjusted.

The activity will automatically produce a DOC on the DL screen. Where an MFU is created for manual action, the AMR field in the DOC text with be blank.

3

Clearance process is completed automatically as a result of the coding of the DVA screen + Read more ...

Coding the DVA income will result in either:

  • the customer's Centrelink payment being cancelled and an overpayment calculated (multiple entitlement exclusion provisions exist) or
  • the customer remaining current on the Centrelink system with an adjustment or an overpayment being calculated by Debt Explanation (ADEX) (that is, DVA payment treated as income against the social security payment). For periods of entitlement before 1 January 2022, the system will calculate a daily rate of DFISA, load it on the DFISA Summary (DFS) screen and transmit the rate to the customer's DVA record. DVA will pay any DFISA payable
  • the DVA payment not affecting a customer's Centrelink payments (e.g. when the customer is asset tested, the grant date of Adjusted Disability Pension is on or after 1 January 2022, was not on Centrelink payments during the assessment period, or is under the Partner Income Free Area for an Allowee partner)

If the customer has a partner in receipt of income support payment from Centrelink, their payments may also require a reassessment. An automated clearance will be sent to DVA containing the partner's details and any overpayment amount.

When the auto clearance has been finalised, the system will calculate the overpayment amount. Details regarding the adjustment amount (including nil adjustment) will be transmitted to DVA for recovery from the arrears of DVA payment, before it is released to the customer.

The debt will be started automatically on DMIS (reason code 'DVC'), if the system has calculated the overpayment), but no advice will be issued to the customer.

4

Clearance process is completed automatically as a result of DVA recovering Centrelink debt + Read more ...

DVA system will recover Centrelink debt automatically and transmit amount details back to Centrelink customer record. If DVA held enough arrears, Centrelink debt will be finalised and show fully recovered on the Debt List (OPDL) screen. If the Centrelink debt has not been fully recovered, the residual amount will remain on the debt list.

An account payable letter will be issued to the customer for the residual debt and Centrelink debt teams will manage debt recovery.

DVA can also recover partner clearance debts from arrears using the above automatic process.

5

System unable to calculate overpayment – Manual Clearance required + Read more ...

The system is unable to calculate whether there is an overpayment if:

  • the date of effect is Before the Limiting Date (BLD)
  • the customer is currently receiving a manual rate
  • customer has a cancelled payment record
  • there is conflicting data on the DVAS screen

DVA Clearance Team has delegation and access to manage and raise manual debts for Centrelink excess payments arising from DVA clearances. These are recoverable debts due to the Commonwealth under Subsection 1223(1) of the SSA 1991.

Manual actions are required when:

  • DVA payment needs manual coding and the MFU needs to be finalised manually, go to Step 6
  • A manual calculation will be required for an affected BLD period and debt added to the MFU, go to Step 7
  • Auto Clearance failed, DVA released all held arrears prior to MFU being actioned by Centrelink and customer has an unrecovered Centrelink debt, go to Step 8
  • DVA are processing a complex claim manually and due to system limitations are unable to send clearance data to Centrelink via the data exchange, go to Step 9

A manual calculation will be required for an affected BLD period and debt added to the MFU + Read more ...

The AR screen is showing an overpayment adjustment line but also a BLD line but no amount.

Debt Explanation (ADEX) is unable to calculate full overpayment for the Before Limiting Date (BLD) period so manual calculation is required.

Do not complete the MFU at this point.

Warning: If completed, the automated clearance process would transmit incorrect data to the DVA system and all DVA arrears would be released.

To view the calculated adjustments on the AR screen, use ADEX for rate summary (RATS) by inserting 'R' on the adjustment line or 'W' on the OPDL screen.

Manual calculation using the MULTICAL debt calculator is required for the adjustment in the BLD period.

When debt is calculated, select the MFU, navigate to AR screen, insert 'T' on the BLD line, and add the adjustment amount calculated with MULTICAL.

Ensure a minus symbol '-' is added in front of the calculated amount.

Components that cross a financial year need to be entered on a separate line. BLD debt components may be added by selecting the debt from the Debt List (OPDL) screen, and navigating to the OPCA screen.

Finalise the started MFU.

This action will register the debt on the AL screen and transmit the debt details to DVA for auto recovery from DVA held arrears.

The debt will be started automatically on DMIS (reason code 'DVC' if the system has calculated the overpayment as part of the MFU), but no advice will be issued to the customer.

DVA will transmit recovery data back to the Centrelink record and debt will show fully recovered OPDL.

To view a rate summary of the overpayment, insert 'W' on the selected debt on the OPDL screen. Copy the web link generated to a blank Web page and [Enter].

If a residual debt remains following DVA recovery, Centrelink debt Teams are responsible for further recovery action.

9

For DVA Clearance Team:

Code the Veterans Affairs Pension (DVA) screen.

Any backdating to an existing DVA payment treated as income will create an overpayment to a current Centrelink benefit. If a mutually excluding payment is granted by DVA, then a cancellation of Centrelink benefit will occur, with possible overpayment.

Navigate to the assessment result AR screen.

Once DVA have been advised of a Centrelink overpayment, they will recover the amount from the customer's held DVA arrears payment. Details of the recovery will be sent back via the SNI transmission, which will then tag across to DMIS.
