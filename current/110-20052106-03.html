Check if a manual partner intervention is needed + Read more ...

Make sure a partner intervention is loaded and linked if the updates may affect the partner's payments. All updates to the customer and partner's records are then attributed to the intervention results.

  • the partner was not receiving a Centrelink payment during the period of the intervention, or

4

Assess income + Read more ...

Self-employment

For sole trader or business partnership, the best available estimate of the self-employment income is used. This estimate is based on the most recent completed tax returns, however can also be based on an interim Profit and Loss Statement (SU580) for the period from 30 March 2020 to 30 June 2020.

For the 2020-21 financial year, the business income from the sole trader customer’s personal income tax return or the business partnership tax return must be obtained.

For more information, see Raising social security debts due to self-employment or business income.

If the customer is involved in a Private Trust or Company and has provided the relevant documents, refer to a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO). Go to Step 7.

After gathering the JobKeeper Payment income information, calculate the customer's entitlement.

Changing the Social Security Income Assessment Model (CAM) was introduced from 7 December 2020. CAM applies from the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) of the customer’s entitlement period that 7 December 2020 falls in. This changed the way customers report employment income.

Customers with regular ongoing income were contacted by the agency and placed on 2 weekly reporting (STM). Exception: where the Pension Monthly Exception Rule applied.

To make sure the employment income is not assessed twice:

  • Check EANS screen for continuous employment income (e.g. 1WE or 2WE) that was used to calculate the customer’s entitlements before 7 December 2020. Zero the continuous frequency from the EPSD of the first fully verified fortnight to be coded as IOP
  • Code the continuous employment income frequency (and amount) back on the customer’s record from the first EPSD after the last fully verified fortnight of the JobKeeper updates

If multiple pay events fall in a single entitlement period, for post-7 December 2020 income for help coding the income, see Multiple pays received in a single entitlement period.

Transition period

  • 24 November 2020 to 18 December 2020 was set up as customers moved from the ‘earned’ model to the ‘paid’ model. It affected them when they reported between 7-13 December if they or their partner reported (or should have reported) employment income in their previous reporting period
  • use the online calculator for help calculate the correct amount to report during the transition period
  • the Resources page in Assessing and coding employment income has examples on how to assess employment income over the Transition Period

Note: EAT uses 4 decimal points and the Online Calculator uses 2. If any earned amount is being excluded from the paid amount to be coded in the Online Calculator, the excluded amount must match the amount that EAT has apportioned to the transition entitlement period.

If needed, use the Earnings Apportionment Tool (EAT).

  • Only payslips will be uploaded to the Actual Earnings Details (EAAD) screen
  • Do not code JobKeeper Payment income as earnings on the EANS screen for self-employed sole trader or business partnership customers if it is being paid to the business

Back pay of JobKeeper Payment

Self-assign the:

intervention DOC and any JobKeeper activities created as part of the JobKeeper intervention in Work Optimiser.

6

Calculation sheets + Read more ...

Save all EAT calculation sheets used as an .XLSX file:

  • Select File
  • Select Save As
  • browse to locate the H Drive
  • change the Save as type to Excel workbook

For multiple employers, save each calculation with the Customer Reference Number (CRN) and employer number. Example:

  • EAT sheet for first employer, save as - 123456789A01
  • EAT sheet for second employer, save as - 123456789A02

Upload the sheets to the customer's record using Document Tools. Use the reason 'DBTCAL' This includes activities selected from QOL checking.

Delete the saved files from the H drive after uploading all calculation sheets.

If a decision is returned in Quality On Line (QOL), complete the ECRM Image Deletion Request form to request removal of the calculation sheet from the customer record. This makes sure only the correct calculation sheet is attached to the customer record.

7

CAO referral for Private Trust or Company involvement + Read more ...

If the customer is involved in a Private Trust or Company, refer the CRN to a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) after obtaining the information from the customer.

Use the CAO referral guided procedure in Customer First.

Select BI Referral and select the Assessment Reason Yes - Other. This will add the BICAO keyword to the referral.

For more information, see:

  • Step 10 in Table 1 of Identifying and making suitable referrals to the CAO
  • Assessment of income from trusts and companies

Record contact details in IRS

  • place the review on hold for 30 days.
  • delete the CAOREF keyword and add the keyword BICAO
  • self-assign the intervention DOC and any JobKeeper activities created as part of the JobKeeper intervention in Work Optimiser.

See the Resources page for the DOC template - CAO referral for private trust/Private Company.

Procedure ends here until the CAO assessment is returned. Check for a CAO assessment DOC for any actions that must be followed.

Where updates made to the customer’s and/or the Trust/Company record by a CAO result in an overpayment, the debt shell is sent back to CO to raise.

8

Check if there is a debt or adverse decision + Read more ...

After updating the income to the customer record, determine if there is a debt or adverse decision.

If a debt is expected but does not display at the Assessment Results (AR) screen, check the need to use MultiCal:

  • AR screen has a BLD (before the limiting date) line. Limiting Date (LMTD) screen (in each system) for limiting dates relevant to the period
    • NSS General Information (NGI)
    • Pensions General Information (PNGI) or
    • PP General Information (PGGI)
  • If a PEN/PGA customer is cancelled, reassess the cancelled record (RCR) using the Benefit Action (BA) screen in the relevant Host System (PEN or PGA). this should result in an ADEX debt
  • Larger debt result than expected - historical rejection/cancellation of payment

Coronavirus Supplement (CVS) debts

The customer will incur a debt if they did not receive at least $1 (or less) of eligible income support payment (ISP) including add on payments in their:

  • regular entitlement period, or
  • final entitlement period or part payment period

Do not bundle Coronavirus Supplement debts with other debts. Raise them separately.

The Date of Event (DOV) of a CVS debt is the date that the CVS was paid for, this will be the amount that shows on the ADEX and the OTP date on the PS screen.

Arrears outside the debt period

The JobKeeper compliance intervention is a Secretary initiated review of decision:

  • Arrears presented outside the debt period can be used to offset the debt or be paid to the customer if they are legally payable
  • Generally, arrears are legally payable 13 weeks back from the start date of the Secretary initiated review of decision.
  • The start date of the Secretary initiated review is the date the review started, this is on the Review Details (IRRD) screen. Use this start date as the Date of Receipt (DOR) when updating the record.
  • Any arrears 13 weeks back from the DOR are legally payable and can be used to offset a debt. However, the automatic Account Payable notice must be suppressed and a manual Q417 is sent to the customer

For more information, see Actioning an undetermined debt on DMIS.

Using the ADEX Scheduler is recommended. It will correctly calculate the debt amount and amount paid to the customer when adjusting the dates.

System limitation: if a customer is entitled to less than $1 of a payment component (e.g. CES/PHA) the system rounds up and pays exactly $1. This displays on the Payment Summary (PS) screen. However, ADEX displays their exact entitlement, e.g. $0.44. Confirm what the customer was actually paid and actually received when calculating debts. It may be different to ADEX.

In the intervention DOC, clearly record the decision to allow arrears to offset an overpayment.

Is there a debt or adverse decision?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3

Calculating and raising overpayments

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Decide if Recovery Fee applies + Read more ...

Customers receiving a working age payment who incur a debt due to income from personal exertion may have a 10% Recovery Fee applied.

Do not apply a Recovery Fee if the customer:

  • has reasonable evidence supporting why they under-declared income from personal exertion
  • has a reasonable excuse for failing or refusing to provide information, or
  • can show their incorrect declaration of income was not made knowingly or recklessly

A customer may have already provided enough evidence to decide the fee should not apply. Use the information on the customer record to decide.

Consider:

  • the customer's understanding of their obligations to advise the agency of their income. For example, were they advised of their notification obligations?
  • any reasons the customer failed to declare or under-declare their earnings, e.g. were they under excessive stress due to COVID19

The recovery fee should be applied if the customer:

  • failed or refused to declare their income from personal exertion without a reasonable excuse, or
  • 'knowingly' or 'recklessly' provided incorrect information

Auto raised debts: the system will raise earnings debts with a period of 14 days or less on the completion of the earnings activity. The Recovery Fee is not applied to these debts. The customer will receive the standard debt advice as part of this process.

2

Determine debt and calculate + Read more ...

All debts that can be calculated via the Centrelink system and explained using ADEX should be calculated that way. Only use MultiCal if the Centrelink system calculation is not possible or the outcome is not correct.

Check the Assessment Result (AR) screen to make sure the system has correctly calculated any potential overpayment, for example:

  • Debt schedule - do the debt dates/date of event (DOV) match the period?
  • Have the updates/coding been entered correctly?
  • Check the following to make sure the updates are as expected:
    • Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS)
    • Income Bank (NIB)
    • Employment Income Assessment (EIA)
  • Can a reassessment be made using the Benefit Action (BA) screen?
    • Use REA in relevant host system, or
    • RCR in PEN/PGA (for cancelled PEN and PGA records) and leave Reason blank

Note: check if Review of Assessment (ROA) coding is needed to generate allowable arrears. Do this when corrected income is less than declared, but the system did not generate arrears for those periods/fortnights.

If an ADEX debt calculation:

  • can be undertaken, the overpayment must be calculated with the customer over the phone where possible
  • is not possible, a MultiCal debt calculation may be required

For MultiCal debt calculation, get all information from the customer over the phone where possible to enable a debt calculation.

The CO is responsible for progressing the MultiCal activity. If they cannot action, their site is responsible for progressing the activity. The Team Leader will need to reassign the activity to another CO with the required skill set.

For more information, see:

  • Debt tools
  • Debt investigation
  • Actioning an undetermined debt on the Debt management and Information System (DMIS)
  • Using ADEX to explain a system generated debt
  • Using the correct date of effect when raising debts
  • Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts
  • Waiving Centrelink debts

3

Contact customer (and partner if applicable) + Read more ...

Contact the customer before raising the debt if a debt shell results from completing the activity to update employment income.

Also contact the partner if they have incurred a debt.

If they are not on the phone, make 3 genuine attempts to contact them:

  • Self-assign any debt shell(s) created in Work Optimiser
  • Follow the phone contact requirements
  • Record all attempts to contact the customer and partner on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for the DOC template
  • Record contact details in IRS

Was contact successful?

  • Yes, close off the PHO contact on IRCD by coding 'Y' in the Response field and 'T' in the Date Contacted field. Then go to Step 4
  • No, after the third attempt, go to Step 6

4

Advise outcome + Read more ...

Discuss the following during an outbound call, or when the customer or partner responds to a Q888 Pre adverse decision letter.

If they respond to the Pre adverse decision letter:

  • record the contact on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for the DOC template

Update the IRCD screen before discussing any information with the partner, make sure they have permission to enquire.

Discuss the outcome of the intervention with the customer and/or partner.

Tell the customer what information was used and how it was used to calculate the outcome. This could include:

  • the assessment of Tier Rates of JobKeeper
  • what evidence was used, e.g. a combination of verification from the customer or a third party (employer)
  • how employment income is assessed now compared to pre 7 December 2020
  • how back pay of JobKeeper may have been assessed

It is important to summarise the discussion. This ensures a clear understanding of how the employment income was assessed and that all the information has been captured/discussed.

Tell the customer

  • Debt: 'Based on the information you have advised/we have obtained from third party regarding your income, this has resulted in an overpayment of your Centrelink payment of $xxxxx for the period (date to date). If you do not believe this outcome is correct, we can update your details now if you have further information available
  • Coronavirus Supplement debt: 'This has also resulted in an overpayment of the Coronavirus supplement of $xxxxx for the period (date to date)'
  • Changes to reporting: if the customer/partner has continuous employment income coded (e.g. 1WE or 2WE), code the income correctly and place the customer on STM reporting. See Recording and correcting employment income details

Tell the partner

  • Partner: 'Your partner has been selected for a compliance intervention. The employment income was not taken into account in the payments made to you, therefore you have incurred an overpayment of $xxxxx for the period from (date) to (date)'

Ask the customer if they have more information they want to give before the outcome is finalised. They may be able to verbally explain the discrepancy. Update the customer record and recalculate as required.

Tell the customer their review and appeal rights.

Is the customer providing more information they cannot give over the phone?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Customer will provide more information + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • to provide the information within 7 days (this can be extended if needed)
  • if they give information, it will be used to update their record. An accounts payable letter will be issued advising of any debt outcome
  • if they do not give more information, the intervention will be finalised with the information we have. An accounts payable letter will be issued advising of the debt outcome

Record a contact in IRS and put the intervention on hold for 14 days.

Self-assign any debt shell(s) and work items related to the intervention via Work Optimiser for 14 days by selecting actions, Hold to User.

Record details of the conversation in the intervention DOC.

Procedure ends here until activities come DUE or the customer gives more information.

When the intervention comes due:

  • update the customer’s record based on new information given, raise debt (if applicable) and finalise the intervention
  • if no new information is given, raise debt and finalise the intervention based on information previously given

6

Issue Pre-Adverse Decision Letter + Read more ...

After 3 unsuccessful attempts to call the customer and/or partner to advise the outcome, issue a manual Q888 Pre adverse decision letter to them.

Note: if the customer is no longer in receipt of a payment and has not been in receipt of a payment within the last 12 months, see Safeguarding customer privacy when raising and recovering debts.

This letter tells the customer:

  • what evidence was used to calculate the debt
  • an estimated debt amount
  • they have a further right of reply before the outcome is finalised
  • next actions and consequences

See the Resources page for the Q888 template. Use the correct letter, based on the customer's circumstances.

Record contact details on IRS and place the intervention on hold for 28 days pending contact from the customer:

  • On IRCD screen
    • Letter code: 'ADVERS'
    • Address: enter the address where the letter will be sent
    • Text: Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter issued
  • On IRMR screen
    • Add keyword 'Q888'
    • Self-assign the intervention

Self-assign any debt shell(s) and all work items related to the intervention via Work Optimiser. Place the work item on hold for 28 days by selecting actions, Hold to User.

DOC the record. See the Resources page for the DOC template.

Procedure ends here until the customer responds to the Q888, activities come DUE or more information is given:

  • If the customer responds but gives no new information, raise the debt and finalise the intervention
  • When the intervention comes due
    • update the customer’s record based on new information given, raise debt (if applicable) and finalise the intervention
    • if no new information is given, check that the updates reflect what the customer was entitled to. Raise debt and finalise the intervention based on information previously given

7

Raise debt as per guidelines + Read more ...

Use the Debt Action script if possible.

Raise the debts (including partner) as per current guidelines.

Do not bundle Coronavirus Supplement debts with other debts. Raise them separately. Waiver provisions can apply to Coronavirus Supplement debts.

Debts less than $50 can be waived.

If waiving the debt due to a vulnerability concerns identified on their record, the debt must be raised then waived.

Make sure the recovery fee question is answered.

Notes:

  • In the 'date contact made with customer' question, enter the date the Debt Action Script is run
  • If the debt amount generated via ADEX differs from the final debt amount raised, fully document the reasons for this in the stand alone debt DOC
  • Coronavirus Supplement debts
    • raise under S1223(1) of the Social Security Act (S1223(1) for the script)
    • leave the reason code NEP - Not eligible for payment
    • use the date paid in the system, not the date the customer received it

Suggested text for <DEBT REASON> field of Accounts Payable letter for ISP debt:

'This is because an incorrect amount of employment income from (employer's name) was used to work out the payments made to you from (debt start date) to (debt end date).'

See Sending Account Payable letters for debt reason codes, default text for the Account Payable (OPAOD) screen, and the rules for entering free text on the account payable letter.

See the Resources page for DOC templates:

  • Details of debt calculated (Wage/Salary earner), or
  • Details of debt calculated (Sole Trader/Ptrship)

Finalising JobKeeper compliance interventions

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Document intervention + Read more ...

Record all information in the customer's intervention DOC to clearly explain the:

  • customer's circumstances
  • information relating to the compliance trigger
  • the scope of intervention
  • customer's action
  • outcomes of the intervention

All DOCs on a customer's record are to have a clear and concise history of the customer's interactions. They must meet:

  • Online Document Recording (ODR) standards, and
  • Getting it Right (GIR) minimum standards

See the Resources page for DOC templates.

Run all compulsory scripts to make sure that:

  • the DOC is appropriately updated, including the Extra Details line
  • all actions are recorded in IRS

Annotate the DOC if one was created to initiate contact.

2

Finalise the intervention + Read more ...

Before finalising the intervention, make sure all actions required on the customer, (including any linked reviews) and (ex) partner records are completed.

Check:

  • the Activity List (AL) screen
  • all entries on the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen
  • Response?: field Y or N

Use the Finalise Review script to finalise the intervention.

Check that all updates have been correctly attributed to the review within IRS. For example, all earning updates and suspension action have been attributed to the IRS review.

The script will pause at the Attribute Result (IRAR) screen in IRS. The IRAR screen will include all activities on the customer record that have occurred over the life of the IRS review.

Select the activities that relate to the JobKeeper Intervention process. For example suspension, cancellation or a debt which resulted from updating the customer's earnings.

Make sure that a debt raised is attributed to the review. If the correct process to action the updates within the review image has not occurred, and the debt has not attributed to the review, see the Resources page for the task card.

For more information:

  • Attribute/unattributed activities as per current guidelines. See Finalising and attributing actions to a Service Update or Review in IRS
  • Choose an appropriate Result Reason as per current guidelines. See Calculating a Review Result and Restoration Processing in IRS

If the customer has a linked review or (ex) partner who also has an intervention, finalise all interventions at the same time.

If required, seek help from the QDO to confirm all updates have been correctly attributed before finalising.

3

Referral to the Policy Engagement Team + Read more ...

After completing the intervention, a referral to the Policy Engagement Team may be required if the customer has continued to incorrectly declare their income outside the scope period.

Send a CADO Service Desk enquiry to the Policy Team. They will consider the referral and engage with the appropriate teams, Case Selection or Intelligence and Investigation Branch.

4

Send a Compliance Review Finalisation (Q765) advice + Read more ...

The Finalise Review script will pause and ask if a Q765 advice is to be sent to the customer advising them the review has been finalised.

Select Yes and send the advice unless:

  • the intervention outcome has been advised over the phone
  • resulted in an adverse decision, for example:
    • payment/card remains suspended/cancelled
    • payment was varied
    • debt advice has been issued

A Q765 can be sent if requested by the customer.
