From 17 March 2021, Income Management (IM) customers based in the Northern Territory (NT) can permanently transition to the Cashless Debit Card (CDC) program. Customers on an income support payment who are living in the NT and are not currently on IM, will be able to volunteer for the Cashless Debit Card (CDC) from this date.
For more information on this process or to initiate an IM to CDC transition, see Income Management (IM) to Cashless Debit Card (CDC) Transition in the Northern Territory, which also contains staff resources and links to the transition overview Intranet page for additional information.

This document outlines how to conduct an Initial Assessment interview to place a customer onto Income Management under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) measure.

Initial Assessment interview

An Initial Assessment interview will be conducted to place a customer on Income Management under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients (VWPR) measure. This is conducted with the customer and, if applicable, their payment nominee, to determine how their income managed funds can be best used to meet their priority needs.

The interview can be conducted face-to-face or over the phone. Service Officers will explain to the customer the benefits of Income Management and provide information about other services which may assist them, including Centrepay.

Automatically triggered VWPR Youth Income Management customers may be eligible for an exclusion from Income Management. Only social workers can determine if a customer is eligible for an exclusion. At the Initial Assessment interview, Service Officers will assess if the customer should be referred to a social worker for an exclusion assessment. Customers can also request an exclusion, which must be referred to a social worker.

Failure to attend interview

If a customer does not attend an Initial Assessment interview within their Income Management engagement period. Income management will be automatically applied to their payments.

If a customer still does not attend an Initial Assessment interview 28 days after Auto Income Management commences their payments may be suspended.

Preparing for an Initial Assessment interview

To prepare for an Initial Assessment interview, Service Officers must:

  • Check whether the customer requires an interpreter, and book interpreter if required
  • Check whether the customer has an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes. If they do not have an excluded payment nominee, they are eligible for Income Management. However the payment nominee must attend the Initial Assessment interview as they are responsible for managing the customer's payments
  • Check the record for any vulnerability indicators
  • Check the customer's current rate of payment and any regular income
  • Check whether the customer has any current Centrepay deductions or Rent Deduction Scheme payments
  • Have relevant Income Management and self service communication products available for the customer
