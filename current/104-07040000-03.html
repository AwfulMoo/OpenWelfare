Immediate response and support options: + Read more ...

Depending on the seriousness and nature of the incident, consider the following:

  • Prioritise immediate safety of staff within and outside the site, this may involve:
    • stopping all customer service and face to face servicing
    • removing staff and other customers from ongoing exposure to customer aggression
    • contacting the police and Regional Security Adviser where there is a threat to a staff member, customer or third party
  • Contact a First Aid Officer (FAO) to provide first aid to any person who has been injured, if it is safe to do so. It is the decision of the FAO to call an ambulance; Services Australia will pay for any ambulance costs associated with work related customer aggression. See the Resources page for a link to the First aid page
  • Arrange for the Employee Assistance Program (EAP) provider to attend the site and be available to staff, either immediately or within 24hrs
  • Providing Psychological First Aid (emotional and practical support)
  • Arrange site coverage from another location
  • See the Resources page for a link to the Employee assistance program (EAP) page

Operational debriefing + Read more ...

Organise an operational debrief before the end of the working day. The relevant Regional Manager/Service Manager/Social Work Branch will consult to determine the availability of staff. The debrief of those involved may include the following:

  • A brief summary of the incident, providing clear information about the known circumstances and facts
  • Inviting and answering questions (where possible) - some information will not be immediately available
  • Discussing issues of concern (briefly) and demonstrating care and support
  • Drawing up a plan of action, taking into account the needs of individual staff
  • Making short-term arrangements for managing any workload issues, if required

Provide practical and emotional support to affected staff + Read more ...

  • Does each staff member feel safe?
  • Consider time out, a short break or a walk outside of the office if safe to do
  • Check-in with other staff who were not directly impacted, but who may still be affected:
    • Provide information about Employee Assistance Program (EAP) support, which can include counselling
    • See the Resources page for a link to EAP contact details
  • If the incident involved a potential transfer of a communicable disease, see the Resources page for a link to Guidelines for preventing the spread of communicable disease
  • If impacted staff are working off-site, or are managed remotely, give relevant information about the incident or injury to their line manager
    • This will enable the line manager to address any local workplace issues and/or support the staff member and monitor their wellbeing
  • Support staff to complete the necessary aggression reports as soon as possible, while events are still fresh in their mind:
    • Managers should complete the reports(s) if the staff member cannot do so
    • See Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression
  • For impacted non-APS staff, reports of aggression are recorded using the WHS Incident Form – Non APS
  • Review the personal security information on the intranet and consider if contact is required with the Physical Security team
    • They can provide staff with assistance and advice around personal safety and security
  • Consider whether a Security Guard may need to escort a staff member to their car at the end of the day
  • If a staff member does not wish to speak about the incident, tell them you are available for support any time they require
  • Acknowledge each staff member’s experience and reactions; provide reassurance that different responses to an incident are valid and normal
  • Remind staff they may experience further reactions to the incident in the coming days, weeks and months
    • This may include emotional reactions, physical symptoms and psychological changes

Psychological debriefing immediately following an incident is not the staff member’s main source of treatment and should not occur. Professional and ongoing support is available to staff via EAP, HR Support and their own treating doctor.

Short-term support

Short term considerations + Read more ...

Consider alternative duties or an alternate work location. Timeframes for breaks should be discussed and check in with staff before they return to their normal duties.

Provide visibility of the post incident response to the site. See the Resources page for a link to the Communication template for serious customer aggression incidents.

Ongoing supports and monitoring

Ongoing supports + Read more ...

Managers must make sure staff know they can seek support at any time after an incident through a number of channels, including:

  • Psychological counselling outside of EAP via Early Intervention or self-funded and free counselling services in the community
    • Staff can also be referred to counselling/psychological services by their doctor
  • Accessing helplines outside of Services Australia, such as RUOK and Lifeline
    • See the Resources page for a link to Guidelines for managers: supporting employees after a traumatic event
  • Employment Law and FOI Branch provide witness factsheets for staff asked to provide a statement, see the Legal issues – Customer aggression intranet page. Contact the Employment Law team for:
    • additional support when providing a statement
    • attending a police interview, or
    • employees seeking legal assistance in order to obtain protection orders
  • HR Support provide expert HR advice, support and services to staff and managers. See the Resources page for a link to the HR Support intranet page

Monitoring + Read more ...

Monitor changes in behaviour. Changes could be due to a delayed impact from the incident or an impact from a series of incidents. Look for any significant change in the staff member’s behaviour. This could include:

  • Absenteeism - unexplained or unusual/unplanned leave
  • Presenteeism - being at work when maybe they should be on leave
  • Increased irritability or anger, especially during customer interactions
  • Poor concentration, reduced performance or increased mistakes
  • Extreme behaviour or heightened reactions
  • Poor staff engagement or social withdrawal

Tell staff about any post incident management of the customer, such as:

  • post incident phone contact
  • warning letters issue
  • Managed Service Plan implemented

Include prevention and management of customer aggression as a regular agenda item in team meetings, this will:

  • promote the importance of recording incidents
  • provide evidence that Services Australia is actively addressing the prevention, recovery and response to customer aggression
  • promote supports available
  • provide the opportunity to discuss learnings from incidents with a focus on what staff did well in addressing the issue

Use regular coaching sessions to discuss staff wellbeing and support options. See the Resources page for a link to Mind Tools:

  • this is an external resource that all staff can access
  • it provides a range of tools and resources to assist with providing staff support and coaching

The manager may observe trends relating to the number, frequency or type of incidents occurring at a site or with individual customers. The manager should flag this with the relevant CANOC. The CANOC can provide data to:

  • support the need for increased security (for example, deploying a security guard)
  • further staff support
  • Managed Service Plan recommendations, or t
  • he need for staff training

Ask the Aggression Response Team (ART) to review and test the Emergency Response Procedures - Aggression at least annually.

Discuss with the Physical Security team any changes (other than to personnel or contact numbers).

See the Resources page for a link to the Emergency Response Aggression (ERPAs).

If an injury occurs + Read more ...

In all instances where an injury has occurred, the manager must check on the wellbeing of any injured staff or other person. This could be via:

  • a direct phone call to the person, or
  • the Emergency Contact recorded in ESSentials

Record injury in ESSentials (ESS)

Managers must make sure the staff member completes an Occupational Health and Safety Incident or Injury Report with as much detail as possible. If the affected staff member cannot complete this form due to the impact of injury, the manager should do so on their behalf.

See the Resources page for information about Occupational Health and Safety Incident or Injury reports and customer aggression reports in ESSentials.

First Aid Officer (FAO)

When it is safe, contact an FAO to help with the provision of initial health care and basic life support for:

  • staff,
  • other workers, and
  • third parties including customers, visitors and contractors

It is the decision of the FAO to call an ambulance. Services Australia will pay for any ambulance costs associated with work related customer aggression.

See the Resources page for a link to Role and responsibilities – first aid officer.

Death in a Service Australia site

It is very rare for a death to occur in a Services Australia site.

A checklist exists to support managers and supervisors in effectively responding and supporting staff should a customer or staff member die on a Services Australia site.

See the Resources page for a link to Death on a departmental site checklist - Quick Reference guide for managers and supervisors.

Notify Comcare

Managers should use the Comcare Notification Checklist to identify if Comcare needs to be notified of:

  • a serious injury/illness
  • dangerous incident, or
  • death

See the Resources page for a link to the checklist.

If unsure of obligations or need clarification, email the Work Health and Safety (WHS) Delivery team for advice. See the Resources page for a link to People Division page.

If an incident occurs outside of office hours (including weekends or public holidays), and staff are unable to contact a manager/team leader, the employee should notify Comcare on 1300 366 979. Choose to be redirected to an on-call inspector to make sure required timeframes are met.

Regional Security Advisers & Security Incident Reports

Regional Security Advisers (RSAs) + Read more ...

Regional Security Advisers (RSAs) are out posted members of the Physical Security team who can provide security advice and support following a customer aggression incident. For example:

  • liaising with the police
  • post incident reviews
  • supporting managers with advice on CCTV, duress and intruder alarm protocols
  • providing information about personal security risks

Contact local RSAs

+ Read more ...

For security advice, email the Security Hotline.

Contact the local RSA by phone, if they are not available call the Security Hotline.

In the event of an emergency, call 000.

Centrelink + Read more ...

The Customer Incident Management System (CIMS) will automatically send an email to the Physical Security team notifying them of the details of an incident when certain conditions apply.

For more information, see the Resources page for a link to the CIMS Task Card: Notification Emails in CIMS for Incidents and Managed Service Plans.

Reporting customer aggression

Reporting + Read more ...

Managers should make sure that staff understand why recording an incident is important and that recording needs to occur as soon as possible following an incident.

See the Resources page for information about the 4 WHYS of recording customer aggression.

Reporting for APS staff

Staff must record an Occupational Health and Safety Incident or Injury Report in ESSentials if they are impacted by an incident either:

  • physically, or
  • psychologically

Details of customer aggression and counterproductive behaviour need to be reported using the relevant approved system.

For more information, see Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression.

Reporting for non APS including customers, contractors or staff from other agencies

Managers should report customer aggression incidents involving third parties including labour hire staff, contactors, customers and visitors using the WHS Incident Form – Non-APS in the Services Australia Financial Management and Compliance System (FMCS).

Note: this Form replaces information previously recorded in The Visitor Accident Report and The Contractor Incident Form.

If unable to login, email the Financial Policy team.

For assistance with queries completing the form, contact the WHS Delivery team.

Incident and Injury report follow up requirements

ESSentials + Read more ...

Once an Incident and Injury report has been recorded in ESSentials, the manager will be notified via an ESSentials inbox workflow item.

  • Complete the manager/team leader section
  • Check the employee has completed all required fields
  • Offer practical and emotional support and advise of support services. (See the Resources page for a link to the Manager’s Guide: customer aggression post-incident support)
  • The ESSentials record must be actioned within 2 days

For more information about ESSentials recording, see ESSentials on the Resources page.

Customer management to reduce future risk to staff and others

Future risk + Read more ...

Review the customer’s record, including previous incidents to assess the risk posed by the customer and likelihood of future aggression.

For more information about post incident follow-up, see Customer aggression - Post incident contact.

Early intervention

The HR support teams manage early intervention + Read more ...

Contact the People Advisory Centre (PAC) promptly on 1800 906 144.

They will advise the appropriate HR Support Team to gain assistance in supporting affected staff.
