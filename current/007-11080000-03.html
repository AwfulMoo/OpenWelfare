Manual intervention into family assistance reconciliation

Note: All CCB/CCR references relate to financial years prior to 2018/2019.

Item

Action

1

Manual Intervention (MIV) team processing + Read more ...

The MIV team will monitor and process reconciliation and lump sum claim cases needing manual intervention by:

  • checking the reconciliation result and, if necessary,
  • adjusting details to complete the reconciliation activity

2

Protocols, customer contacts - outstanding manual reconciliation activity + Read more ...

If a customer enquires about their Family Tax Benefit (FTB)/Child Care Benefit (CCB) reconciliation or lump sum claim and the activity has been stopped for manual intervention, Service Officers should not contact Technical Peer Support (TPS).

Note: some activities with the manual intervention reason 'Customer may be impacted by Natural Disaster/Emergency' (#EMGDIS) may be completed by Service Officers without referring to the MIV team. See Item 9.

Before sending a request to the MIV team, check the record for a MIV Progress DOC. A MIV Progress DOC indicates the MIV team are currently working on the customer’s reconciliation outcome. Advise the customer that their reconciliation will be finalised in due course. Direct them to check their online account for updates.

If appropriate to escalate to the MIV team:

  • use Fast Note. Select Auto text > Families > Reconciliation > Outstanding MIV activity. This automatically adds the keyword ACTMIV and allocates to the MIV team to action
  • if the customer is 'at risk' or experiencing hardship, add the URGENT keyword

The customer is not to be transferred to the MIV team as they are a processing team and do not have contact numbers available. If contact with the customer is required as a result of the activity, the MIV team will contact them.

If the MIV activity is preventing another urgent activity from being completed (for example, an earnings update), do not contact TPS. Using Fast Note. Select Auto Text > Families > Reconciliation > Outstanding MIV activity. This automatically adds the keyword ACTMIV and allocates to the MIV team to action. If the customer is 'at risk' or experiencing financial hardship, the URGENT keyword should also be added.

If further information or action is required by the service centre for the MIV team to complete the activity, contact will be made by the MIV team outlining the action required.

Do not send correspondence to MIV team.

Do not cancel manual intervention activities on the Activity List (AL) screen.

3

Cases requiring manual intervention + Read more ...

All of the following manual intervention reasons can be triggered in an automatic activity, such as, receipt of income details from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) or a user generated activity as indicated below:

  • manual rate customers (Department of Veterans' Affairs saved cases and additional partners (only) in Multiple Relationship cases)
  • bereavement cases - User generated activities - Y
  • manual debt cases
  • amended debt cases - User generated activities - Y
  • manual adjustments via the Rates Component (RAC) screen - User generated activities - Y
  • manual cheque payments
  • Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen payments
  • Notice of Assessment Pre-issue cancellations
  • multiple Customer Reference Numbers (CRN)
  • blended families
  • large debts over $10,000
  • large arrears over $10,000
  • FTB Part B Quarantine multiple partner scenario
  • customer had a School Enrolment and Attendance Measure (SEAM) suspension greater than 13 weeks (SEAM ceased from 1 January 2018)
  • where an override has been coded on the FTB or CCB Ex-Partner Period Override (FEXPO or KEXPO) screen and there is a subsequent income or ex-partner period change - User generated activities - Y
  • where a customer or their partner lodges a late tax return for the 2012-13 or later financial years, having previously been reconciled as a non-lodger
  • where a late lodger assessment is made for the customer, or their partner, for the 2011-12 and later financial years and the customer had Mandatory continuous adjustment (MCA) deductions in the relevant year - User generated activities - Y
  • where information is received from the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) after reconciliation has occurred, changing the child's immunisation status from immunised to not immunised
  • where a Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS) grandfathering override of Yes is in place and the system has calculated at least one day during the entitlement year that the customer's rate of SIFS is nil
  • where the reconciliation or non-lodger outcome is a debt and the customer may be affected by declared major disaster or emergency

4

Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part B Quarantine multiple partner scenario + Read more ...

This scenario is where:

  • the customer has more than one partner in the financial year and is the lower income earner for one partner but not for the other(s); the other partner(s) was also the lower income earner
  • a return to work is recorded in relation to an eligible Part B quarantine child for more than one lower income earner (for example, the customer and a partner, both partners, or all 3)

The system will apply a potential quarantine period for the return to work recorded for each lower income earner.

Only one person's return to work in relation to a child attracts the FTB Part B quarantine. This is the return to work of the person for whom the potential FTB Part B quarantine period begins first.

Eligibility for the quarantine measure is also subject to the Part B primary earner income limit, except for periods the customer or their partner received an income support payment. If the primary earner's income exceeds the limit, the customer is not eligible for quarantining.

Manual intervention is required to override those periods that are not the first return to work using the ineligibility code 'N1R'.

The manual intervention activity is identified by:

  • Keyword: #QMP
  • Reason: QMP

5

The FTB/CCF Manual Intervention (FAMIV) screen + Read more ...

The FAMIV screen displays the manual intervention reasons relevant to a particular activity.

Where the FAMIV screen presents, any intervention reasons displayed must be actioned prior to the activity being finalised.

Note: with the exception of activities where a 'Customer may be impacted by Natural Disaster/Emergency' (#EMGDIS), only MIV staff are to action activities that trigger the FAMIV screen.

For activities requiring manual intervention, the FAMIV screen will display in the screen flow before the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

For user generated activities, the FAMIV screen will only be displayed where there are certain intervention reasons present. See Cases requiring manual intervention.

In some cases, the FAMIV screen will not always be displayed, however an adjustment may be required on the FTB Confirm Reconciliation Amount Summary (FCRAS) screen. Where the FCRAS screen is presented for an adjustment to be included in the reconciliation result, the activity must be transferred to MIV for completion.

The FAMIV screen can also be displayed by coding 'V' next to a line on the FAO Reconciliation and Claim Summary (FRCS) screen.

When the FAMIV screen or the FCRAS screen is displayed in a user generated activity, do not complete the activity:

  • Do not contact the TPS
  • Navigate to the AR screen, and select 'Do Not Finalise'
  • Place the activity on hold with a resubmit date of 7 days (21 days during the MIV peak period - 1 July to 30 September)
  • Use the Fast Note. Select Auto text > Families > Reconciliation > Outstanding MIV activity. This automatically adds the keyword ACTMIV and allocated to the MIV team to action. If the customer is ‘at risk’ or experiencing hardship, add the URGENT keyword

The MIV team will check the activity result and make any required updates.

For activities requiring urgent completion see Protocols, customer contacts - outstanding manual reconciliation activity.

6

Income support payment School Enrolment and Attendance Measure (SEAM) suspension greater than 13 weeks + Read more ...

Note: the School Enrolment and Attendance Measure (SEAM) ceased from 1 January 2018.

From the December 2010 major systems release, there have been limited cases of continued SEAM suspension periods over 13 weeks (payments automatically cancelled after 13 consecutive weeks and were subject to review and manual determinations after 13 cumulative weeks of suspension). There were also limited reconciliation periods prior to the December 2010 release that included SEAM suspension periods exceeding 13 continuous weeks. In these limited cases, the following process applied:

For reconciliation purposes, where a customer's income support payment had a SEAM suspension of 13 or more continuous weeks, the customer’s Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A and/or Child Care Benefit (CCB) were subject to the income test for the entire suspension period. This included the initial 13 week suspension if the entire period fell within the same financial year.

For suspension prior to the December 2010 release, the customer's record will have been suspended with one of the following codes:

  • 'ATF' (Attendance not satisfactory)
  • 'ENF' (Enrolment details not provided)

For suspension post the December 2010 release and up to 31 December 2017, the customer's record will have been suspended with one of the following codes:

  • 'ATN' (Attendance not satisfactory)
  • 'ENN' (Enrolment details not provided)

If the suspension spans 2 financial years, only the suspension period in the second financial year is to be income tested. The income test will not apply to FTB Part A and/or CCB for the suspension period in the first financial year, regardless of the length of the suspension. If the income support payment was suspended for less than 13 continuous weeks, the customer’s FTB Part A and/or CCB is free of the income test for the suspension period.

If a customer's payment was 'SUS/ATF' or 'SUS/ENF' anytime during the financial year and is suspended as at 30 June, manual intervention is required if the payment was not being automatically assessed. The MIV team will override the suspension period which has been assessed as 'off income support'.

Manual intervention is required to ensure that the above rules are applied correctly at reconciliation. Where required, the FAO ISP Override (FISPO) screen may be coded to correct the assessment.

7

FTB late lodger assessment where a tax return for the 2012-13 or later financial year is lodged late and where reconciliation has previously been completed based upon an estimate of income + Read more ...

From the 2012-13 financial year, where FTB is reconciled under late lodger provisions, supplements (including Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS)) are not payable (excluded from the assessment) and top ups are inhibited, that is, not paid to the customer nor are they available for debt offsetting.

Where a tax return for the 2012-13 or later financial year is lodged after the end of the allowable period and reconciliation for the relevant year has previously completed based upon an estimate of income, manual intervention may be required for some customers to determine if they are entitled to FTB supplements, SIFS or top ups calculated.

MIV staff will be able to:

  • accept the provisional re-reconciliation result
  • adjust the result to include or exclude supplements (using the Supplement Override function)
  • adjust the result using the FAO Reconciliation Manual Adjustment (FRMA) screen

Note: from the 2016-17 financial year onwards, FTB Part A supplement is not payable to families with an adjusted taxable income (ATI) of more than the Part A supplement income limit for the relevant income year. A supplement override to include the Part A supplement should not be coded for 2016-17 and later financial years where the families ATI exceeds the income limit.

8

FTB late lodger assessment for the 2011-12 and later financial years where Mandatory continuous adjustment (MCA) deductions were in place in the relevant year + Read more ...

From the June 2014 major system release if a late lodger assessment is made for the customer or their partner, for the 2011-12 and later financial years, manual intervention is required if the customer had MCA deductions in the relevant year.

Manual intervention is necessary as reconciliation calculations cannot distinguish between MCA deductions and More Choice for Families (MCFF) deductions when determining if a late lodger is eligible for a top-up.

If the customer chose to receive less than their legislative rate of FTB during the year they will only be entitled to the lesser of the entitlement based on their estimate and entitlement based on their actual adjusted taxable income (AATI). This is not a top-up.

MCA deductions cannot be paid as a top-up and will need to be inhibited.

Part A and Part B supplements, and SIFS will not be included in the customer’s annual FTB rate.

MIV staff will be required to manually check the result to ensure MCA deductions are adjusted and not paid to the customer as a top-up.

9

Customers affected by a declared major disaster or emergency + Read more ...

Where the agency applies a pause to debt raising, customers who meet the following criteria may have their FTB, CCB and/or CCR re-reconciliation activity stopped for manual intervention:

  • The customer's FTB, CCB and/or CCR re-reconciliation, Lump Sum Claim or FTB Non-Lodger activity is resulting in a new or increased debt, and
    • the customer has resided in a residential postcode affected by a declared natural disaster or emergency event for at least one day within the defined period, or
    • the customer received an AGDRP, DRP, DRA, Ex-gratia DRA or AVTOP payment within a defined period

The manual intervention activity is identified by:

  • Keywords: #EMGDIS
    The following additional keywords will also be added to the activity:
    • For FTB: #FTBEDP and/or
    • For CCB/CCR: #CCFEDP
    • Specific keywords for each natural disaster or emergency. See the Resources for a list of defined natural disasters and emergency keywords and periods

FAMIV screen display: Customer may be impacted by Natural Disaster/Emergency.

Do not complete activities stopped for:

  • Natural Disaster/Emergency, or
  • where a re-reconciliation or non-lodger debt is being generated within an activity and this MIV reason is displayed on the FAMIV screen, unless one or more of the following occurs:
    • the defined period has expired
    • it is necessary to complete the activity to enable another activity or update to be completed, for example a claim activity, or to make sure the customer’s ongoing rate is correct, or
    • the customer requests that reconciliation is completed and the debt raised before the end of the pause period

Complete the activity as per normal process if any of the above occur.

However, if:

  • the FAMIV screen displays for another MIV reason, other than ‘Cust may be impacted by Natural Disaster/Emergency’, and one of the following occurs:
    • the defined period has expired
    • it is necessary to complete the activity to enable another activity or update to be completed, for example a claim activity, or to make sure the customer’s ongoing rate is correct, or
    • the customer requests that reconciliation is completed and the debt raised before the end of the pause period
  • escalate to the MIV team to action:
    • Use Fast Note > Auto Text > Families > Reconciliation > Outstanding MIV activity. This automatically adds the keyword ‘ACTMIV; and allocates to the MIV team to action
    • If the customer is ‘at risk’ or experiencing financial hardship, add the URGENT keyword
