Carer death notification

Step

Action

1

Death of carer notified + Read more ...

When a person advises the death of a carer, the following action is to be undertaken:

  • If the person providing the information is distressed, offer a private interview before proceeding
  • Offer the services of a social worker
  • Offer the pamphlet Are you needing help after someone has died? (HD001). If accepted, issue the pamphlet which is on the intranet or can be ordered via the Services Australia website. See the Resources page for links

The following cultural issues need to be respected and considered by Service Officers when being advised of the death of an Indigenous customer:

  • The name of the deceased person cannot be mentioned by immediate family members. This means immediate family members may have to ask other relatives/friends to call on their behalf or attend the interview with the notifier, because they cannot say the name of the deceased
  • In this situation, it may also be offensive to the person for the Service Officer to say the name of the deceased person. Service Officers should sensitively ask the person if it is all right for them to use the deceased customer's name. If the person is not comfortable with this, avoid using the deceased customer's name wherever possible

See Cultural Considerations when someone dies.

Does the carer's record indicate that they were income managed, as shown by INM on the benefit status line?

  • Yes, follow process to update record for death of an income managed customer, then return to this procedure and go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Confirmation of death + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure in the deceased customer's record to determine if death can be confirmed.

Confirmation of death can be accepted where the carer's full name is provided together with at least 2 of the following:

  • deceased carer's last address in full
  • date of birth
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • partner's name, if partnered

Can the person who advised of the carer's death provide enough information to confirm the death?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Death cannot be confirmed + Read more ...

Using the Notification of death guided procedure, obtain and record all relevant information from the informant including their:

  • name
  • address
  • contact number
  • relationship to the deceased

If known, obtain details of the executor or administrator, the name of the funeral director, and the name of the hospital.

The guided procedure will not code the death, but will create a DOC on the record, which is an open work item that will be allocated to a Service Officer with the required skill set to confirm the death.

If the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item using Fast Note Select Auto Text use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED.

The Notification of death guided procedure presents a suspension option if the death is unable to be confirmed:

  • Use the reason code 'OTH' (Other) from the date of the advised date of death or date paid to plus one (DPT+1), whichever is the later
  • Ensure the DOC meets GIR DOC Minimum Standards for significant decisions and includes what action is required to confirm the death if applicable
  • Do not issue a Q134 suspension letter. Cancel the advice activity on the Activity List (AL) screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension
  • It is not necessary to code a manual review, as there is already an open DOC/work item
  • If the guided procedure is not available or the suspension option is not presented, suspend the deceased customer's payments using the Carer Cancellation and Suspension workflow or manually. See:
    • Suspension of Carer Payment (CP)
    • Suspension of Carer Allowance (CA)

Confirm the death before processing and finalising death action. For Service Officers confirming the death, contact the funeral director or the hospital where the deceased passed away. If this is not an option and contact with relatives is proposed, social worker involvement may be necessary.

When the death is confirmed, go to Step 4

4

Check if deceased carer was receiving CA or CP + Read more ...

Was the deceased carer receiving CA or CP?

  • Yes:
    • for CA only or CA and CP, go to Step 5
    • for CP only, go to Step 1 in Table 2
  • No:
    • but claim for CA and/or CP has been lodged and not yet processed, refer to Processing a claim lodged after the death of a customer
    • carer was receiving another payment type (e.g. JobSeeker Payment (JSP)) and had not made a claim for CP and/or CA; see Death of an income support customer and their partner for the relevant procedure

5

Final payment of Carer Allowance (CA) + Read more ...

CA is payable up to the date before the carer's death. Regardless of when notified, any payment of CA made from the date of death is a legally recoverable debt. See Finalised 'no debt' (FND) or 'zeroing' debts that are not legally recoverable.

Note: the exception to this is where CA (auto) is paid for a care receiver child. CA is paid until CP ceases as part of the CP bereavement process.

Was the deceased also in receipt of CP?

  • Yes, go to Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Table 3

Was the deceased person a member of a couple?

  • Yes, go to Step 2

2

Was the deceased person's partner a pensioner for Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP) purposes or a long-term social security recipient? + Read more ...

The surviving member of a couple is entitled to an LBP if, at the time of death, each member of the couple was either a pensioner for LBP purposes or a long-term social security recipient. However, where the surviving partner is not in receipt of a pension or was not a long-term social security recipient, there is no entitlement to an LBP.

  • Yes:
    • Any bereavement payments owing to the partner will be paid depending on what payment they receive. See Death of an income support customer or their partner. Go to Table 3
    • The partner is in receipt of Parenting Payment see Death of a Parenting Payment customer, their partner or their child. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Partner is not a pensioner for LBP or long-term social security recipient + Read more ...

CP is payable for the entitlement period in which the carer died and is usually credited to the account of the deceased.

The partner may be entitled to receive JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) so that they can access Partner Bereavement Payment and other bereavement provisions.

Go to Table 3.

Coding the death of a carer in receipt of CP and/or CA

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Outstanding activities + Read more ...

A death action cannot be processed with activities on the Activity List (AL) screen. The Notification of death guided procedure will provide a prompt if there is an activity on the AL screen.

Note: if a deceased customer's record was suspended while the death was being confirmed and their death has now been confirmed, check for started activities on the AL screen manually.

Where possible ensure any activities are completed and run the daily driver by keying 'DDR' in the Nxt: field and pressing [Enter].

If there is a 'STA' debt activity on the AL screen, create an open work item for region XWA advising them of the death and requesting the debt activity be completed – use Fast Note select Auto Text use Debts > DMB Raising > Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request. See Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer.

For other activities that cannot be completed, immediately contact the team or Service Officer who needs to complete the outstanding activity - if unsure who to contact, refer to existing escalation channels.

Have any started activities been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Unable to complete an activity + Read more ...

If there is an outstanding activity that the Service Officer is unable to complete, the customer's payment should be suspended if not already suspended.

The Notification of death guided procedure does not present a suspension option when activities are preventing the death from being coded.

If not already suspended, suspend the deceased customer's payments using the Carer Cancellation and Suspension workflow or manually:

  • Use the reason code 'OTH' pending completion of the outstanding activity
  • Suspend:
    • Carer Payment from the date of the advised date of death or date paid to plus one (DPT+1), whichever is the later
    • Carer Allowance from date paid to plus one (DPT+1)
  • Ensure no letter is issued. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension
  • Record a DOC on the customer's record, ensuring that it meets Online Document Recording (ODR) standards for significant decisions and include what action must occur so the death can be actioned

If an open DOC/work item about the death notification has not already been created, either:

  • run the Notification of death guided procedure to generate an open work item for the relevant staff to complete the death action once the outstanding activities have been completed, or
  • if the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item. Use Fast Note – select Auto Text, use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - Action Required

When outstanding activities are completed, go to Step 3.

3

Check if restoration is needed + Read more ...

Is payment suspended?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Restore payment + Read more ...

Restore payment if suspended:

  • Determine if any arrears are payable, taking the confirmed date of death into consideration. CP customers are payable up to the end of the entitlement period in which they died. CA customers are payable up to the day before the date of death
  • Inhibit the restoration advice - it is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer
  • Death action is to then be processed immediately

5

Check if customer due to report + Read more ...

Was deceased customer due to report or lodge a reporting statement?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 8

6

Deceased customer was due to report + Read more ...

Is the deceased customer's employment income for the last reporting period known or can the person notifying of the death provide the customer's employment income for the last reporting period?

  • Yes:
    • process the reporting statement. Note: the date of lodgement on the Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen may need to be changed first to the day before the date of death. See Changing Entitlement Period End Dates (EPED)
    • Go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Stimulate the payment + Read more ...

Stimulate the payment. See Recording and correcting employment income details.

Take the deceased customer off reporting by updating the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen.

8

Coding the death + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure (which includes executor details). If the guided procedure is not available, manually code the death action.

When recording a death for a CP customer, the Notification Handler (NOHL) is incorrectly using the date of notification as the date to increase the survivor's rate to the single rate. If coding the death action manually, the workaround for this is to record the date of receipt (DOR) as the later of date of death or date paid to + 1.

If the deceased customer received CP and the surviving partner is eligible for Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP), check the LBP amount for correctness.

Record the details of the bereavement on a DOC. Note: the guided procedure should auto-record these details.
