Calculation of arrears

Manual calculation of arrears

This table describes the steps on assessing and manually calculating arrears.

Step

Action

1

Are arrears payable? + Read more ...

Is the customer entitled to a higher payment for the period than has been automatically paid through the system?

Note: this may be identified when a reassessment is done and the correct arrears amount does not appear on the Assessment Results (AR) screen. It may be identified when the customer queries the result after notification of the decision. The Service Officer must check to see if the customer has already been paid for the period before issuing any arrears.

If necessary, seek technical support. If further assistance is required, contact can be made with the relevant Business Support Service Desk.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

No entitlement to arrears + Read more ...

If request originated from customer, advise that arrears are not payable.

Record the details on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

3

Can arrears be paid automatically? + Read more ...

Can correct payment be made by coding correct details of assessment and correct date of assessment on the appropriate assessment screen?

The Assessment Results (AR) screen will show if any arrears are being generated.

Arrears are result of review of decision

If the arrears have been generated from a review of decision, access the Review of Assessment (ROA) screen The ROA screen allows the payment of arrears where the customer is entitled.

  • enter ROA in the Next: field, or
  • select the relevant review of assessment summary from the Review of Assessment Summary (ROAS) screen

The ROA screen collects the following information:

  • start date of Review of Assessment (where applicable)
  • section of the SSAA the review is based on
  • source of the error (customer or Services Australia)
  • whether the customer was notified of the original decision
  • whether a review of this decision has been requested within 13 weeks of the original decision
  • whether, as a Delegate, the user agrees that any calculated arrears are payable to the customer (where applicable)

If arrears that the customer is entitled to cannot be calculated by using this screen, manually calculated arrears may be required.

If necessary, seek technical support to see if automatic arrears can be paid through the system.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Arrears paid automatically through the system + Read more ...

Code correct date of effect to generate arrears.

Check the correct date of effect is being used. For more information see:

  • dates of effect for review outcomes
  • customer initiated review of decision
  • Centrelink initiated review of decision

Record the details on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

5

Manually calculate arrears period + Read more ...

Determine period that the arrears cover.

For Pension customers:

  • over Age Pension age, go to Step 6
  • under Age Pension age, go to Step 7

For Allowance customers, go to Step 8

6

Pension customers over Age Pension age + Read more ...

For pension customers over Age Pension age calculate customer's entitlement for the period.

For assistance in calculating a pensioner's entitlement, see:

  • Pensions income and assets tests, and
  • Payment rates and thresholds and
  • If the customer has employment income, if eligible, apply the Work Bonus before assessment of employment income. See Work Bonus and balance for pensioners of Age Pension age.

Add amounts the customer is entitled to be paid for the period.

Multiply daily rate by number of days in the period for each component to be paid (for example, Basic, Rent Assistance, Remote Area Allowance, and Pension Supplement).

Include all add-ons if eligible. For example:

  • Energy Supplement
  • Pharmaceutical Allowance
  • Rent Assistance (RA)
  • From 1 July 2010, a customer in receipt of the minimum amount of Pension Supplement may choose to have this amount accrued for a quarterly Pension Supplement. The same option is available for customers on transitional rate. When the minimum Pension Supplement amount is paid quarterly the Payment Summary (PS) screen will show 'Reg+qtr'. The Regular Period Frequency (RPF) screen provides a history of the customer's payment frequency for the minimum Pension Supplement amount

If applicable, see also Transitional rules for pension customers who were on payment at 19 September 2009.

Go to Step 9.

7

Pension customers under Age Pension age + Read more ...

For pension customers under Age Pension age calculate customer's entitlement for the period.

For assistance in calculating a pensioner's entitlement, see:

  • Pensions income and assets tests, and
  • Payment rates and thresholds

Add amounts the customer is entitled to be paid for the period.

When calculating amounts for customers who are eligible for Working Credit check the Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS) screen. This provides a snapshot of a customer's working credits and the relevant accrual or depletion amounts at a particular point in time.

The Working Credit Balance Explanation (WCBE) screen presents specific and relevant Working Credit information to explain a customer's Working Credit balance, its current accrual or depletion rate and its effect on a customer's gross rate of payment for all Working Credit payment types.

The Working Credit Rate Override (WCRC) screen allows for manual adjustments to be made to the balance of working credits as well as the accrual and depletion amounts.

Multiply daily rate by number of days in the period for each component to be paid (for example, Basic, Rent Assistance, Remote Area Allowance, and Pension Supplement).

Include all add-ons if eligible. For example:

  • Energy Supplement
  • Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA)
  • Rent Assistance (RA)
  • From 1 July 2010, a customer in receipt of the minimum amount of Pension Supplement may choose to have this amount accrued for a quarterly Pension Supplement. The same option is available for customers on transitional rate. When the minimum Pension Supplement amount is paid quarterly the PS screen will show 'Reg+qtr'. The RPF screen provides a history of the customer's payment frequency for the minimum Pension Supplement amount

If applicable, see also Transitional rules for pension customers who were on payment at 19 September 2009.

Go to Step 9.

8

Allowance customers + Read more ...

For allowance customers calculate customer's entitlement for the period.

For assistance in calculating an allowance customer's entitlement, see:

  • Allowance income and assets tests, and
  • Payment rates and thresholds

Add amounts the customer is entitled to be paid for the period.

When calculating amounts for customers who are eligible for Working Credit, check the Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS) screen. This provides a snapshot of a customer's working credits and the relevant accrual or depletion amounts at a particular point in time.

The Working Credit Balance Explanation (WCBE) screen presents specific and relevant Working Credit information to explain a customer's Working Credit balance, its current accrual or depletion rate and its effect on a customer's gross rate of payment for all Working Credit payment types.

The Working Credit Rate Override (WCRC) screen allows for manual adjustments to be made to the working credits as well as the accrual and depletion amounts.

For customers receiving ABSTUDY, Austudy, Youth Allowance (YA) full time and concessional study load students and Australian Apprentices, see Personal Income Test and Income Bank for ABSTUDY, Austudy and Youth Allowance (YA) students and Australian Apprentices. This process will determine the amount payable for the fortnight after considering Income bank credits.

Multiply daily rate by number of days in the period for each component to be paid (for example, Basic, Rent Assistance and Remote Area Allowance).

Include all add-ons if eligible, for example:

  • Energy Supplement
  • Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA)
  • Rent Assistance (RA)

9

Subtract amount customer was paid for the period + Read more ...

Calculate how much customer was paid for the period.

Payment Summary (PS) and Payment Details (PD) screens display amount paid. Note: when the minimum Pension Supplement amount is paid quarterly the PS screen will show 'Reg+qtr'.

Subtract the amount paid from the amount entitled for each component to give the amount to be paid as manual arrears.

10

PYAJ, working credit adjustment and payments issued after 1 July 2003 + Read more ...

Code payment on Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen

Note: the PYAJ screen may only be used to issue payments which cannot be issued via any other method. Access to the PYAJ screen is limited to the APS 5/6 level.

Pension customers

Payments paid at the transitional rate of pension must be paid using a separate payment adjustment activity from payments paid at the old or new pension rate (the old rate refers to the rules applied before the new rate of pension was introduced 20 September 2009). This is because pension payments under the transitional rate are appropriated separately from those paid under the old and new rates.

From 1 July 2010, customers on the new rate of pension can elect to have the minimum pension supplement (PSMIN) paid either fortnightly or quarterly. The same option was offered to customers on the transitional rate. For customers on the transitional rate electing to receive an amount equal to the minimum pension supplement amount on either a fortnightly or quarterly basis the component code is TRMIN. Note: from 1 July 2010 TRNTX was replaced by TRMIN for both fortnightly and quarterly payment frequency.

Example for change from old to transitional rate of pension

When making a payment that includes components paid under the old rate and the transitional rate, in addition to BASIC, the payment period must be split between 2 separate PYAJ activities.

Old rate components can include:

  • Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA)
  • Pension Supplement
  • Utilities Allowance (UA)
  • Telephone Allowance (TAL)

Transitional components can include:

  • TRNTX
  • TRMIN

Use one PYAJ for the period that covers the components paid under the old rate, for example PhA, Pension Supplement, UA and/or TAL, and another PYAJ for the remainder of the period covering the components TRNTX and/or TRMIN. Include BASIC in each PYAJ activity depending on whether it was paid at the old or transitional rate.

The Resources page contains an attachment with more information on components.

Working Credit adjustments

When the system is unable to determine the amount or the working credit balance (accrual or depletion) requires a correction, code the W/C Manual Override (WCRC) screen.

Income Bank adjustments

For customers receiving ABSTUDY, Austudy, Youth Allowance (YA) full time and concessional study load students and Australian Apprentices, see Personal Income Test and Income Bank for ABSTUDY, Austudy and Youth Allowance (YA) students and Australian Apprentices. This process will determine the amount payable for the fortnight after considering Income bank credits.

Payments issued after 1 July 2003

For payments issued after 1 July 2003, the system will automatically calculate the effect on income tax calculation for the customer.
