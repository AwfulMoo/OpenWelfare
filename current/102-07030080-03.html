Determining entitlement after returning to or commencing work

Table 1: This table describes how to determine entitlement when a JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance (job seeker) customer advises they are commencing or returning to work.

Step

Action

1

Returning to or commencing work + Read more ...

If a customer contacts to tell they have commenced employment and it is not their reporting day, record a DOC with employment details. Tell the customer to report on their reporting day with all the details of their employment. Explain to them how to report correctly.

Obtain the following details from the customer:

  • date of commencement of employment
  • type of employment: part-time, casual, full-time ongoing
  • employer's name and Australian Business Number (ABN)
  • employer's phone number
  • gross employment income per week/fortnight
  • period of employment, if known
  • ensure the customer is now a 2 weekly reporter and advise the customer of any changes to their reporting requirements, for example, they are now required to report every 2 weeks

DOC minimum standards only require the collection and recording of enough information about the employer (for example, contact name and address or phone number) to allow income assessment, as well as contact with the employer if necessary. Contact may be necessary to verify income details provided or to conduct reviews resulting from Data Matching with the Australian Taxation Office (ATO). Some customers (for example, those who work in sensitive areas like women's refuges) may not want all employment details (for example, business name) recorded on a DOC. This does not affect the requirement to collect sufficient information for assessment or review purposes.

2

Partner returning to or commencing work + Read more ...

If a customer contacts to advise their partner has commenced employment, see Partner commencing or returning to work.

3

Australian Apprenticeship + Read more ...

Has the customer commenced an Australian Apprenticeship?

  • Yes, consider whether the customer should apply for another payment:
    • Claiming ABSTUDY
    • Initial contact with students or Australian Apprentices claiming Youth Allowance (YA)
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Cancel or continue payments + Read more ...

If the customer:

  • requests immediate cancellation of payment or withdrawal of a new claim, go to Step 5
  • wishes payment to continue, go to Step 6

Note: if the customer has a nominee, see Nominees.

5

Entitlement to income support payments + Read more ...

Explain to the customer that they may still be entitled to receive income support payments.

Code the employment income to allow correct reassessment of the customer's entitlement to income support, taking into account any Working Credits and any nil rate period entitlement.

Note: family assistance and income support reporting obligations are different:

  • Income support customers must report actual income paid, not just an estimate. Service Officers must discuss income reporting requirements at every opportunity and ensure actual income paid and income estimates are both updated when required
  • If either member of a couple is receiving family assistance, their estimate must include income support payments received, employment income and any other components of adjusted taxable income (received or expected) for the financial year for both members of the couple

The Resources page contains a link to a matrix, which has more details to assist with determining the appropriate action in various situations.

If the customer chooses to cancel their payments immediately without advising of their income details, tell them they will lose access to their Working Credit balance and any remaining income support to which they may be entitled.

Does the customer still wish to cancel payments immediately and declines to provide income details?

  • Yes, for customers:
    • currently in receipt of payment, see Step 3 in Table 2
    • with a new claim, see Rejecting a Centrelink claim. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Assess continuing eligibility + Read more ...

For JSP or YA job seekers, determine if the customer has ongoing entitlement to payment. They must still be able to satisfy the sufficient work test, mutual obligation requirements and continue to report on their normal reporting due date.

JSP job seekers who have returned to full-time work who may still be eligible for payment

The most common examples of full-time workers who might qualify for JSP payment without further activity requirements are:

  • job seekers with a pensioner partner (the income test for these couples results in a cut-off point for JSP which is considerably higher than applies to individuals and couples where both receive an income support payment such as JSP)
  • long term single JSP customers aged 60 or over who receive Rent Assistance (RA) (the cut-out point for such cases can also exceed full-time minimum wages)
  • JSP single principal carers (the income test for these customers may allow them to continue to be entitled to part rate of JSP after all Working Credits have been exhausted)
  • job seekers with a working credit balance at the entitlement period start date that they are paid employment income, see Step 1 in Table 2

For information on sufficient work test and mutual obligation requirements, see Paid work as an approved activity.

  • if the job seeker does not have continuing entitlement and payment has not been cancelled, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • if the job seeker does not have continuing entitlement and payment has been cancelled under reason 'FTE' (Full time employment, paid income not advised), go to Step 7
  • if the job seeker does have continuing entitlement, including where payment has been cancelled under reason 'FTE' (Full time employment, paid income not advised), go to Step 8

7

Non-ongoing entitlement for payment and payment cancelled under reason 'FTE' + Read more ...

When a provider advises a job seeker has been placed in full-time ongoing employment, an electronic transaction will be sent via the Department of Education, Skills and Employment system to Services Australia, which updates the job seeker's record.

The transaction will also automatically update the Employment Status with the 'FTO' code on the Customer Employment Status (EAES) screen.

When this occurs, record the employment income as ongoing income so the customer can access their Working Credit balance. If there is no ongoing income recorded "after three reporting periods", the job seeker's payment is auto-cancelled. After the depletion of the Working Credit, their payment will be auto cancelled.

If there is no ongoing income recorded, the job seeker's payment is auto cancelled under reason of 'FTE' from the date they commenced full-time employment. The job seeker is not able to utilise their Working Credit balance unless employment income is reported and payment is restored.

If it is confirmed that the job seeker has commenced full-time ongoing employment, to restore payment and apply Working Credit provision correctly:

  • restore the payment via the Benefit Action (BA) screen. Before finalising, code employment income from the date the job seeker is first paid and then finalise the activity
  • if payment is current, check if the job seeker is still required to report for the next entitlement period. If so, advise the job seeker of the requirement to still report on the report due date and that their payment will automatically cancel once their Working Credit is depleted
  • record details on a DOC

If the job seeker disputes that they have not commenced work or the work is non-ongoing (i.e. casual or only part-time), see Jobactive/Community Development Program (CDP) registration inactivated and cancellation of income support in error.

Procedure ends here.

8

Ongoing entitlement for payment, including where payment cancelled under reason 'FTE' + Read more ...

Restore the customer's payment after working out if they meet the sufficient work test but can still be considered unemployed.

For customers with casual or part-time employment

To restore payment for this cohort:

  • Code a new entry of 'NFT' (Not full-time ongoing employment) with the date being the day after coding 'FTO'. Do not delete the 'FTO' details from the EAES screen. An activity will be sent to the provider advising of a change in employment status for the customer
  • Process outstanding reports. Assess all employment income paid during the entitlement period as IOP or LOP. See Recording and correcting employment income details
  • Restore the payment via the Benefit Action (BA) screen and finalise the activity
  • Check and ensure that the customer's Job Seeker Registration is reactivated
  • Negotiate/renegotiate the Job Plan as appropriate. See Negotiating Job Plans
  • Tell the customer to keep reporting and to include any employment income paid on their normal reporting day. If income will be too high, the nil rate period process may apply. See Employment income nil rate period
  • Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

For customers with full-time ongoing employment who still qualify for payment

This process may apply to the following job seekers:

  • customer with a pensioner partner (the income test for these couples results in a cut-off point for JSP considerably higher than applies to individuals and couples where both receive an income support payment such as JSP)
  • long term single JSP customer aged 60 or more who receives Rent Assistance (RA) (the cut-out point for such cases can also exceed full-time minimum wages)
  • JSP single principal carers (the income test for these customers may allow them to continue to be entitled to part rate of JSP after all Working Credits have been exhausted)

To restore payment for this cohort:

  • Code a new entry of 'NFT' (Not full-time ongoing employment) with the date being the day after coding 'FTO'. Do not delete the 'FTO' details from the EAES screen. An activity will be sent to the provider advising of a change in employment status for the customer
  • Process outstanding reports. Assess all employment income paid during the entitlement period as IOP or LOP. See Recording and correcting employment income details
  • Restore the payment via the Benefit Action (BA) screen and finalise the activity
  • Check and ensure that the customer's Job Seeker Registration is reactivated
  • Negotiate/renegotiate the job seeker's Job Plan with compulsory activity 'Paid Work' and with the relevant number of working hours included. See Negotiating Job Plans
  • Once the Job Plan is completed, check that the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen is populated with the 'PTW' approved activity. Job seeker should then be 'suspended' from their services with their employment services provider and is Centrelink managed
  • Tell the customer to keep reporting and to include any employment income paid on their normal reporting day. If income will be too high, the nil rate period process may apply. See Employment income nil rate period
  • Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

Note: this procedure does not affect a customer's right to cancel their entitlement to an Allowance voluntarily.

Unfavourable decision, change to reporting or cancellation

Table 2: This table describes the process when a customer requires a change of payment frequency or no longer has an ongoing entitlement and their payment must be cancelled.

Step

Action

1

Customer does not have an ongoing entitlement or has Working Credit to deplete + Read more ...

For customers with full-time ongoing employment, code the employment income, via the Earnings and Reporting Workflow, with the following:

  • Date of Event - the date the customer was paid by their employer. If the customer has worked but not yet been paid, the Date of Event will be the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) to code hours only
  • Amount - the gross amount paid
  • Frequency field - code as the employer pay period frequency
    • Weekly (1WE) - where the customer is paid the same amount, on the same day every week
    • Fortnightly (2WE) - where the customer is paid the same amount, on the same day every fortnight
    • Income for one period (IOP) - where income can vary or first employment income amount is for less than the full entitlement period. If this occurs, code the first EPED as IOP and the following EPED as the relevant ongoing frequency
    • Long Period/Lump sum (LOP) - where income is for more than the entitlement period. Start and End dates will be required.
    • If coding hours only, select HOP to code hours worked in the entitlement period
  • Empl Sts (Employment Status) field - as 'FTO' (full-time ongoing). This can only be coded with a 1WE or 2WE frequency. Leave blank for IOP or LOP frequency
  • Hrs PF field - number of hours worked. This can be coded with 1WE or 2WE frequency. If coding income using IOP or LOP, leave blank and use frequency HOP to code hours. Ensure Empl Sts field is coded as 'FTO' (full-time ongoing). See Recording and correcting employment income details for more information
  • Income support payments will cancel automatically when the customer's Working Credit has depleted
    • If employment income precluded payment and JSP/YAL does not cancel automatically, go to Step 3

Check if the customer is eligible to receive a Special Employment Advance (SEA).

Advise the customer about providing verification of employment income.

Before a customer's payment ceases, Special Employment Advance (SEA) applications can be:

  • taken at a service centre, or
  • uploaded through the Express Plus Centrelink mobile

When making an unfavourable decision, the Service Officer must:

  • explain the decision to the customer and give them an opportunity to provide further details or evidence relevant to the decision
  • tell them about their review and appeal rights, and
  • if needed, record a request for an explanation or review of the decision

This includes unfavourable decisions made by Service Officers within a Smart Centre.

If a Service Officer is not speaking with a customer at the time of making a decision, they must make 2 genuine attempts to contact a customer before finalising an unfavourable decision.

2

Update the customer's reporting frequency + Read more ...

  • A JSP or YA (job seeker) on variable reporting will be removed from variable reporting
  • Other customers who advise that they will have varying income can be manually placed on statement reporting if required
  • If the customer is not manually placed on reporting, the Service Profiler will automatically update the customer's reporting frequency for customers who have had one or more instances of their and/or partner employment income occurrences greater than the customer's income free area in the last 12 weeks

For more information on updating reporting frequency, see Reporting overview.

3

Cancellation of payment + Read more ...

  • If the customer is a job seeker who declines to advise details of full-time employment income:
    • if cancellation has not occurred, the customer will need to be removed from variable reporting first
    • code the Customer Employment Status (EAES) screen. This will cancel payments from the date paid to plus 1 day
    • for customers commencing full-time non-ongoing, part-time or casual work, see Cancellation of JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) at customer's request
  • If the customer does not report, the payment will cancel 'DNL' 14 days after the due to report date:
    • the customer will be unable to deplete any Working Credit
    • any entitlement to 6 fortnights at nil payment will be forfeited
  • If a customer is on variable reporting and does not intend to report:
    • payment cancels from date paid to plus 1 of the last Reporting Statement lodged
    • if the customer has received any payments after that date the system will treat these as overpayments and create a debt
  • For other customers:
    • if the customer requests cancellation of their payment, but does not want to report with employment and/or income details, cancel payment on the Benefit Action (BA) screen 'customer request' (CLR). In the Effect date field, coded EPED +1 of last Reporting Statement lodged
    • the customer will be unable to deplete any Working Credit
    • any entitlement to 6 fortnights at nil payment will be forfeited
    • When coding a voluntary cancellation of payment
      select Fast Note template: CANC CLR - requested by CUS for (PAYMENT TYPE) DOV
      record all relevant details of the cancellation in the template, following the minimum standards
      attach the Fast Note to the cancellation activity by 'S'electing the CAN activity and complete the Assessment Results (AR) process.

Note: if automatic cancellation, i.e. FTO, but payment has not cancelled automatically, follow normal escalation procedures.

Before a customer's payment ceases, Special Employment Advance (SEA) applications can be:

  • taken at a service centre, or
  • uploaded through the Express Plus Centrelink mobile

Do not cancel the payment if the customer wishes to apply for SEA. Advise customer to complete SEA application (SU514) and upload through the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app.

4

Other considerations + Read more ...

  • If the customer's payment is being cancelled and their circumstances later change, they will have to lodge a new claim. JSP/YA job seeker customers can lodge the claim online or staff can complete the Assisted Customer Claim workflow
  • If the customer has a partner on income support, reassess the partner's entitlement and advise of the effect of the customer's income on their partner's continuing entitlement
  • Advise customer of possible entitlement to a Health Care Card (HCC) or Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) once payments cease
  • Parenting Payment will suspend after cancellation of their partner's payment
  • If applicable, request and update the estimate of annual income for Family Tax Benefit. Customers can give a verbal estimate. Note: if either member of a couple is receiving family assistance, their estimate must include income support payments received, employment income and any other components of adjusted taxable income (received or expected) for the financial year for both members of the couple
  • Where the customer has a nominee arrangement in place (voluntary or involuntary), contact with the nominee may be required before taking action requested by the customer. For more information, see Nominees
