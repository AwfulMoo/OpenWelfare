Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Request to contact letter + Read more ...

A request to contact letter is issued when an Employment income Intervention (Mail based) (EIIM) has been initiated.

The request to contact letter requires the customer to contact within 21 days to discuss their employment circumstances.

Has the customer contacted?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose + Read more ...

Follow the agency's phone contact requirements - introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose.

All inbound callers will hear this IVR message before transferring to a Compliance Officer (CO):
‘Welcome to the Services Australia Customer Compliance Line. This call may be recorded or listened to for quality and coaching purposes’.

The CO just needs to introduce themselves, and:

  • locate the customer's record and authenticate the customer
  • confirm the customer’s home and postal address and phone details are correct. Record any updates via the Change in Contact Details workflow
  • use interpreter service if needed. Check the Customer Summary screen via Workspace to locate the interpreter indicator
  • be aware of procedures for:
    • customers at risk, referrals to a Social Worker in Payments and Integrity
    • identifying vulnerable customers
    • bereavement
  • advise the customer of the purpose of the intervention
  • check if the customer requires a nominee
  • encourage the customer to access services online via myGov

Select the relevant script to update the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen:

  • For Tip-offs run the General Review Action Script
  • For all remaining EII interventions run the Circumstance Review Action script

Then determine the customer's employment circumstances. See Step 1 in Table 2.

3

Customer fails to respond to the request to contact letter + Read more ...

If the customer has not responded in the required time, their payments will either automatically suspend or be referred to a CO to decide if manual suspension is needed.

The auto suspension process occurs overnight via Integrated Review System (IRS), if the customer meets set business rules.

If the customer is already suspended/cancelled:

  • Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer. Send a desktop message before trying to call. Follow the agency's phone contact requirements - introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose.
  • If contact is unsuccessful, see Step 1 in Table 2

Has automatic suspension occurred or are payments already suspended/cancelled and 2 genuine phone attempts have been unsuccessful?

  • Yes, employment circumstances will need to be determined. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Manual suspension + Read more ...

Before proceeding, be aware of the procedures to refer a customer to a social worker in Payments and Integrity and Identifying customer vulnerability and risk issues.

Manual suspension action is required if the customer:

  • does not meet the required business rules for auto suspension
  • has not responded to the request to contact letter

Send a desktop message before trying to call. Follow the agency's phone contact requirements - introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose.

Automatic overnight processing by IRS will add a keyword of IRSSUS to the intervention, the IRCD screen is not updated and the intervention has a status of 'DUE'.

When manual suspension action steps are completed, the CO will need to suspend payment:

  • For Tip off interventions manually suspend payment ‘fail to respond to correspondence’ (FRC), go to Step 5
  • For all remaining EII interventions run the Circumstance Review Action script

5

Coding manual suspension + Read more ...

To manually suspend the payment, in the customer's record:

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen and code the following:
    • Key (payment type, for example, JSP) as the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn: field
    • Key the Action: SUS
    • Key in the Reason: FRC
    • Press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or enter the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date: field
    • Complete footer details, Source: field and date of receipt in the DOR: field
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity
  • Include the following details in a DOC:
    • Decision made including reason, payment type and Act/Guide reference (Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 – Section 80)
    • Date of effect of decision
    • Follow up action required on contact

The date of event used for manually suspending payments is the date the determination is made - today. Do not use Date Paid to Plus 1 (DPT+1).

Update the letter contact on IRCD screen to ‘N’ the customer did not contact

Record a new contact on the IRCS/ Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen to place the intervention on hold (HLD) for 28 days, pending contact from the customer. See suspension/cancellation in Coding the Contact screen in the IRS.

Record all significant decisions in the Intervention DOC. The Resources page contains a link to the DOC templates. Customer Compliance Division policy requires a separate DOC be created for all suspensions, cancellations and debts. See Creating, reviewing and deleting documents (Fast Note, Callbacks, ACTDOCs and DOA DOCs).

Employment circumstances will need to be determined. See Step 1 in Table 2.

Suspension action procedure ends here.

6

Customer contacts in relation to suspension action + Read more ...

When talking to the customer, be aware of the procedures to refer a customer to a social worker in Payments and Integrity and Identifying customer vulnerability and risk issues.

If the customer contacts about the suspension action, follow the agency's phone contact requirements - introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose. After assessing whether the customer has retained eligibility for the payment, payments can be restored using the following processes:

  • For Tip off interventions use the Cancellation and Restorations workflow
  • For all remaining EII interventions use the Circumstance Review Action script
  • Record action taken in the intervention DOC

If third party verification has already been issued, take the following action:

  • discuss their employment history
  • give the education message
  • advise the customer that third party request has been sent

If the customer contacts before any verification has been issued to third parties, the intervention can be conducted. See Step 1 inTable 2

Procedure ends here.

Customer conversation and determining employment situation

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Locate match data + Read more ...

The Compliance Officer (CO) is required to locate the match data in order to address the risk.

View information about the compliance trigger via the Match Data (IRMD) screen in IRS.

Tip-offs

View more information on a Tip-off compliance trigger in the Tip-off Recording System.

Identify if a Centrelink report (ORE Review) has been referred from the new system on the Match Data (IRMD) screen. The report ID will display as the match data. For example, RSF_XXXXXXXXXXXX.

On the IRMD screen it only shows a number 00001234567 and does not have RSF in front of it. When accessing the Reports of Suspected Fraud and Corruption System, change the Search Case ID, then put a percentage at the front of the number.

When viewing the Tip-off information, be aware of other details (third party information) in the Tip-off. This information may influence the customer conversation and any decisions about Scope e.g. if third party verification is required.

See the Resources page for a link to View a Suspected Fraud and Reports of Suspected Fraud and Corruption System.

Anomaly or mismatch identified

If there is a discrepancy between the customer's details and the match data:

  • Remind the customer of their notification obligations
  • See Referral for Anomalies and Mismatches for Compliance Interventions

If not relevant to the customer the intervention must continue.

Additional Risk

If identified during the intervention process that the customer has an Additional Risk, investigate and update this as part of the current intervention. For help, see the Resources page for the Assessing Additional Risks Guide.

If not relevant to the customer the intervention must continue.

2

Determine employment situation + Read more ...

Use the information listed on the IRMD screen/TORS viewer to help determine the customer's employment situation:

Do not limit enquiries to just the employer(s) from the match data, include all employers

If the customer has a partner information about the partner's employer will be required

If it is identified that employment income has been incorrectly declared (or not declared) the customer's record must be updated to reflect the correct details

If the customer is currently employed, they must be placed on fortnightly lodgement. Code 'RR' in next to go to the Report Results (RR) screen and check that reporting requirements are fortnightly

If the customer is not on the phone, a full examination of the customer's record may help address the risk.

Is the customer on the phone?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Discuss/gather employment details + Read more ...

Confirm with the customer:

  • names and addresses of all employers
  • include trading and business names of the employer
  • ensure details match the details on the Match Data (IRMD) screen
  • when they commenced work
  • dates paid
  • employment status:
  • full time
  • part-time
  • casual
  • hours worked

Confirm the information discussed with the customer with what has been advised via the Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen.

Deliver the education message.

Questioning techniques

Use questioning skills to obtain as much information as possible from the customer over the phone to determine the gross income they were paid. For any income paid monthly, or commission income, the customer must advise the start and end dates.

See the Resources page for Questioning Techniques.

4

Discuss Recovery Fee + Read more ...

Discussing the Recovery Fee at this point in the intervention process is an extension of the education message. Discuss with the customer their understanding of their obligations to advise Services Australia of their income.

During this discussion, if the customer supplies evidence about why they did not correctly declare their employment income, record this in the intervention DOC. It may be used later when deciding whether to apply the Recovery Fee.

5

Manual partner intervention required + Read more ...

If the customer has a partner, and no partner (PTR) intervention is loaded, a Manual Partner Review (MPR) may need to be created.

  • If identified during the intervention process that the intervention outcome could affect a partner's (or ex-partner's) entitlement (current or past), create a manual partner intervention on the partner's record
  • This will ensure all updates made to the customer's and partner's records are attributed to the interventions
  • Do not load a manual partner Intervention if the partner was not getting a Centrelink payment during the period of the intervention

Is a manual partner intervention required?

  • Yes, see Adding a Manual Partner Intervention
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Determine Scope of intervention or Change of Scope + Read more ...

Start Date

  • Tax File Number Declaration Form (TDF)
  • TDF sign date
  • Department of Education, Skills and Employment Event Date
  • Earnings Under Declaration (EUD)
  • Match Load Date
  • Pay-As-You-Go (PAYG)
  • 1 July of the match financial year
  • Tip-offs
  • Date created in TORS viewer (unless specific date of event identified in content of Tip-off)

If the compliance trigger or undisclosed employment extends before the Scope Start Date, information can be requested up to 12 months before the Scope Start Date.

End Date

Customer's Date Paid to (DPT) at the Date of Scope Assessment (DOSA).

Changes to scope after the original decision has been made must be endorsed by the team leader.

Is the customer already suspended/cancelled, has failed to respond to the request to contact letter and 2 genuine attempts to contact them have been unsuccessful?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Verbal updates + Read more ...

What to consider when taking verbal updates.

If the customer cannot provide the information during the initial phone conversation, they must be given the chance to source the required information. Tell the customer they must call back within the negotiated timeframe (preferably no more than 14 days) with the required information.

The following action is required for verbal updates in the EIIM process:

  • Manually close off the contact for the IRSRFI (Request to Contact letter). To do this, go to the IRCD screen and code the following:
  • Response?: 'Y'
  • Response initiated by: CUS/REP/THD
  • Person responded: CUSTOMER/NOMINEE/PARTNER
  • If the customer is calling back with the verbal update, code a new line on the IRCD screen with:
  • Method Type: PHO
  • Expected response date: Due date for response (no more than 14 days)
  • Person contacted: Customer to verbal
  • Tel/Mob/Fax: Telephone number for customer

Note: fully document verbal information in the intervention DOC.

Was the customer able to provide a verbal update that addresses the risk?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Notification obligations + Read more ...

It is important to remind the customer of their notification obligations.

This relates to how long a customer has to notify a change in their circumstances.

For customers who are notification reporters, see Reporting overview.

9

Determine what needs to be verified + Read more ...

What needs to be verified

  • employers listed in the compliance trigger (Match Data (IRMD) screen)
  • unverified employers on Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen (unmatched employers)
  • previously undisclosed employers (divulged during the conversation)

Verification to be requested for:

  • the Scope period only
  • customer/partner and/or relevant ex-partners
  • when a customer and partner both have interventions they are to be assessed under the same Scope

Requests may be refined when:

  • information has been previously verified
  • start/end dates of employment are known (and are within Scope)
  • customer/partner/ex-partner were not in receipt of income support

Note: if extra information is received outside the requested scope of intervention, the Compliance Risk Branch must code this information.

Tip-off Interventions

Review the allegation in TORS to identify the third-party income source. If there is not enough information in the allegation to identify the source, and the customer cannot provide information about the source, conduct up to 2 queries to try to identify the source. The attempts can include:

  • White/Yellow Pages search
  • Australian Securities and Investment Commission(ASIC) free company search
  • Australian Business Number (ABN) lookup website
  • other web searches e.g. Google

When not able to identify the third party income source, proceed with the information available.

10

Requesting verification + Read more ...

Is verification of employment income required?

  • Yes, customer or third party verification of employment income is required
  • No, all required information has been received verbally
