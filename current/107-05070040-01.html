For Debt Management Officers (DMO) only.

This document outlines debt recovery action when a debtor has an agreement under the Bankruptcy Act. Agreements are an alternative to bankruptcy.

Debt agreements

This is a way for debtors to negotiate a binding compromise with their creditors. It is based on their expected income from all sources, household expenses and circumstances.

The Australian Financial Security Authority (AFSA) website explains how debt agreements work.

Personal insolvency agreements (PIA)

To propose a PIA, a debtor must be unable to pay all their debts. There are no income, asset or debt limits.

The debtor appoints a trustee to take control of their property and make a proposal to creditors.

The AFSA website explains how PIAs work.

Agreement proposals

When a proposal is received, cease debt recovery.

If creditors vote to:

  • accept the proposal, all creditors receive the same amount over the same period
  • reject the proposal, restart debt recovery

Services Australia does not need to vote unless the debt amount in the proposal is much different to the debt balance.

Variation or termination

The debt administrator informs the agency of changes or default by the debtor.

The agency may need to take action for new or adjusted debts.

Debtors will not be released from the debts covered by the agreement until the Official Receiver issues a certificate.

After an agreement ends, the agency can only recover debts that were incurred by fraud.

Escalation

If a case is unusual or not covered in Operational Blueprint , discuss the case with the APS5 Bankruptcy Officer. The APS5 will refer to the relevant Helpdesk, policy team and/or Legal Services for guidance.
