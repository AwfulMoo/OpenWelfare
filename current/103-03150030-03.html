Participants who reach Upper Age Limit in the Ceduna, East Kimberley, Goldfields, Bundaberg and Hervey Bay regions only

For Cashless Debit Card Processing Team only

Table 2: How the CDC Processing team complete reviews for participants approaching Upper Age Limit and, when reached, how to action the record.

Step

Action

1

CDC participants approaching the Upper Age Limit + Read more ...

Participants will have a review placed on their record one week prior to reaching Upper Age Limit.

Upper Age Limit reviews will present via WLM. Staff are required to make two genuine attempts to contact the participant to discuss:

  • if they wish to volunteer for the CDC
  • if ending participation in the CDC, discuss alternative arrangements for any ongoing CDC deductions and a disbursement of residual funds

Was contact successful?

  • Yes:
    • Update the Debit Card Trial Notes Detail Screen (Q2NT) detailing the discussion and clearly indicate if the participant has requested to volunteer.
    • Cancel review off the Activity List (AL)
  • No:
    • Update the Debit Card Trial Notes Detail Screen (Q2NT) advising of attempt to contact
    • Select the review off the Activity List (AL)
    • From Selection Options, select the radio button (H) Hold/resubmit for action on a future date and Continue
    • From the Activity Details screen, set a review for two days after first unsuccessful contact attempt by updating the Resubmit Date and Continue

2

Upper Age Limit review + Read more ...

The Upper Age Limit review will present via WLM. Second attempt to contact the participant is required to discuss:

  • If they wish to volunteer for the CDC
  • if ending participation in the CDC, discuss alternative arrangements for any ongoing CDC deductions and a disbursement of residual funds

Was contact successful?

  • Yes:
    • Update the Debit Card Trial Notes Detail Screen (Q2NT) detailing the discussion and clearly indicate if the participant requests to volunteer
    • Cancel review off the Activity List (AL)
  • No:
    • Update the Debit Card Trial Notes Detail Screen (Q2NT) advising of attempt to contact has been unsuccessful
    • Cancel review off the Activity List (AL)

3

CDC participant reaches Upper Age Limit + Read more ...

A review via WLM will present the day the participant reaches Age Pension age.

Check the Debit Card Trial Notes Summary (Q2NS) to establish what action is required from previous discussions.

The participant has reached Upper Age Limit and requested to:

  • end participation in the CDC, go to Step 4
  • volunteer for the CDC, See Cashless Debit Card Volunteers
  • cannot be contacted to discuss volunteering, go to Step 4

4

Participant requests to end participation or could not be contacted + Read more ...

  • Go to Workspace > Cashless Debit Card > Decision Details and select End Participation
  • Description type will default to End Participation
  • Select End Reason of UAL (upper age limit reached) from the drop down menu
  • Under Additional Information select Add mandatory notes, add notes and select Continue to complete the activity
  • Email the card issuer to disburse residual CDC funds and close account. See Table 1

This will end CDC participation and issue an advice letter.

Procedure ends here.

Participants who were in the CDC temporarily or address was incorrect

Table 3: How to record details to end a person's participation in the CDC who were only in the CDC area temporarily, or had incorrect address coding making them ineligible for the CDC. Portions of this process are completed by the Cashless Debit Card Processing Team only.

Step

Action

1

Participant in CDC area temporarily + Read more ...

The CDC Processing Team will assess whether the person's participation in the CDC can be ended, taking into account address history and ties to the area, and apply End Participation coding to the record.

Recent contacts with the agency, Rent or Centrepay deductions are also taken into account.

Supporting evidence from the participant may be required to determine the correct address details before a change or end of participation in the CDC can occur. Evidence may include letters, utility bills, invoices or statements from a reputable third party. Statutory Declarations alone will not suffice as evidence.

If it cannot be satisfied that the move genuinely occurred, the participant will remain on the CDC, even if they have backdated their address, until evidence can be provided.

Note: updating or deleting home address details for the purposes of circumventing the CDC will not automatically end participation. Address updates done incorrectly in an effort to avoid the CDC place the participant at risk of overpayment for Rent Assistance or Remote Area Allowance. Service Officers must not update addresses for the purposes of ending a person's participation in the CDC without discussing with the CDC Processing Team initially.

Is it confirmed that the participant was only in the CDC area temporarily, or they moved before the CDC start date?

  • Yes, see Resources for contact details for technical support to allow them to assess the request. If the request is approved, update the address
  • No, procedure ends here

2

End participation + Read more ...

For Cashless Debit Card Processing Team only

  • Go to Workspace > Cashless Debit Card > Decision Details and select End Participation
  • Select Decision Type of IIE (Customer was incorrectly identified as eligible) from the drop down menu
  • Under Additional Information select Add mandatory notes, add notes and select Continue to complete the activity

This will end the CDC participation and issue an advice letter.

Check the PQSS screen to confirm that the participant is no longer in CDC service component 716.

The participant will now remain off the CDC, unless their home address is subsequently changed to a CDC area.

Processing requirements when participant has died

Table 4: Processing requirements when a participant has died, including if funds need to be recalled from a financial institution. Portions of this process are completed by the Cashless Debit Card Processing Team only.

Step

Action

1

Participant death is confirmed + Read more ...

The standard notification of death processes apply for CDC participants.

The agency can only confirm a person is participating in the CDC if the person contacting is an executor or administrator of the deceased person’s estate. Executor/Administrator details are recorded on the DEA screen.

If the person is requesting information about a deceased participant, see Requests for information after someone has died

If the person is notifying of the death and is not an administrator, only general information can be provided:

  • If the deceased person is a Cashless Debit Card participant, the agency will exit them from the program once the death is confirmed
  • Once the death of a Cashless Debit Card participant is confirmed, access to the card and associated accounts (including regular statements) will be suspended

Has the CDC participant’s death been recorded?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, complete the death action. For more information, refer to Initial notification of death
    • Go to Step 2

2

Code end of participation in the CDC for a deceased participant + Read more ...

For Cashless Debit Card Processing Team only

If a CDC participant dies, and the Death Action is coded as confirmed, a reassessment activity is created for the Cashless Debit Card Processing team to follow up.

To end participation for a deceased participant:

  • Select IQN/REA activity and this will navigate to the End Participation screen
  • The screen will auto-populate DCD (participant is deceased)
  • under Additional Information, select Add mandatory notes, add note and select Continue to complete the activity

Go to Step 3

3

Email the CDC card issuer + Read more ...

Email the card issuer requesting suspension of the participant’s account. See approved email templates on the Resources tab.

Note: advice letters are not generated for deceased participants.

4

Requesting funds and raising debts for payments made after death + Read more ...

If payments have been made after a CDC participant dies, the usual process for requesting funds and raising debts applies. See Requesting refunds and raising debts for payments made after death of participant.

When a request for refund from a Financial Institution is required, the funds will need to be requested from both the participant's unrestricted and restricted bank accounts separately:

  • Go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen ('Multiple Destinations' will display for payments delivered under the CDC)
  • Select the payment/s to be returned by typing 'F' next to the payment line and press [Enter]
  • Select (S) the individual payment from the PDL screen
  • The Request for Refund (RFR) screen displays showing the details of the payment to be refunded, including the type and amount of the payment, and the payment destination. Check the details to ensure the correct payment is being refunded from the relevant Financial Institution. Complete the process; see Requesting refunds and raising debts for payments made after death of participant
  • Once complete, repeat the above process to select the individual payment for the alternate Financial Institution.

Discussion about decision + Read more ...
