This page contains the process for urgent payment requests due to a delay in the delivery of a customer's payment because of a national public holiday.

On this page:

Urgent payment request

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer requests an urgent payment + Read more ...

Customer requests an urgent payment due to a delay in their payment because of a national public holiday.

Separate procedures apply for:

  • Urgent payment requests from Income Management customers
  • Urgent payment requests due to expenses associated with a funeral
  • Urgent payment due to Centrelink delay
  • Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customers. Transfer the customer to the Farmer Assistance hotline. If hotline staff are not trained in urgent payments, they should lodge a TPS request

Delay in payment due to public holiday

Has or will the customer's regular payment be delayed due to the public holiday?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, assess the customer under the usual urgent payment guidelines. See Urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances. Procedure ends here

2

Authenticate customer/nominee + Read more ...

Before assessing or issuing an urgent payment, make sure the customer record is the correct one. Ask authentication questions to verify Proof of Record Ownership (PORO). See Authenticating a Centrelink customer.

If the customer contacts in person:

  • it is preferable they also provide at least 1 photo identity document for a manual-visual comparison
  • If this is not available and the Service Officer is in doubt they are speaking with the customer, they must ask further authentication questions. Do not limit these to details a customer's friends and relatives would know, such as the customer's date of birth or how many children are in the family. The questions must be searching, but not so personal as to be intrusive. If appropriate, explain why they are being asked these questions

If the customer cannot confirm their details and the Service Officer is not satisfied they are speaking with the customer, do not release any information.

Has the customer/nominee been authenticated and PORO met?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, procedure ends here

3

Is customer requesting urgent payment of an eligible payment? + Read more ...

The customer must be receiving an eligible Centrelink payment.

An urgent payment cannot be granted for an ineligible payment type.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment:
    • offer the customer other assistance
    • then use the Immediate Payment requests script to record the details of the denied payment

4

Does the customer have a nominee or third party payment arrangement? + Read more ...

  • Yes,
    • consider the effect of the nominee or third party arrangement
    • see Table 2
  • No, see Table 2

Checking reasons for delay and payment status

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Late lodging due to public holiday + Read more ...

Does the customer need to report their employment income and is late lodging or due to report on the day before the public holiday?

  • Yes, process the late lodgement and stimulate the payment. Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Has the customer's payment been delayed for any other reason? + Read more ...

  • Yes, this procedure cannot be used:
    • Advise the customer that the national public holiday will not affect the day the payment would normally be delivered
    • If the customer still requests an urgent payment, assess under usual urgent payment guidelines. See Urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Checking the payment summary for payment status + Read more ...

Check the Payment Summary (PS) screen for status of payment.

If a date displays on the PS screen, the payment was issued via direct credit. It cannot be reissued.

Does Immediate or late stm display on the PS screen?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, this procedure cannot be used:
    • Advise the customer that the national public holiday will not affect the day the payment would normally be delivered
    • If the customer still requests an urgent payment, assess under usual urgent payment guidelines. See Urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances. Procedure ends here

4

Processing urgent payment request + Read more ...

Due to the public holiday periods delaying payment, especially for late lodgers, the customer is eligible for an urgent payment.

Use the reason code 'DEP' (Centrelink/Departmental delay) with an explanation of 'public holiday processing'.

An urgent payment due to Centrelink delay should be issued for the lesser of:

  • the amount required to meet the customer's immediate need, or
  • $1,000

Note: urgent payments for FHA customers cannot be processed using the Immediate Payment Request script.

See Processing urgent payment requests.
