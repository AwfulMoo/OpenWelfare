Initial assessment of self-employment to fully meet mutual obligation requirements

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Self-employment as an approved activity + Read more ...

A job seeker, who has mutual obligation requirements and is self-employed, may use their self-employment to fully meet their requirements.

A job seeker can fully meet their mutual obligation requirements through self-employment if:

  • the net business income is equal to, or more than, the national minimum wage (for the number of hours required to meet the job seeker's mutual obligation requirements), and
  • they are working the required number of hours in self-employment:
    • 35 hours per week for job seekers who have full time mutual obligation requirements
    • 30 hours per fortnight for principal carers, those with a partial capacity to work (PCW) of 15-29 hours per week and job seekers aged 55 and over
    • 0-14 hours per week (within their assessed bandwidth) for job seekers with a PCW or temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC)

Note: principal carers combining suitable self-employment and other paid work, or self-employment and approved study must complete a combined total of 30 hours per fortnight.

Once the self-employment (SEL) approved activity is coded on the job seeker's record, the details are sent to the Department of Education, Skills and Employment and the job seeker will be suspended from Employment Provider Services. This suspension is automatic. There is no need for the job seeker to discuss this with their Employment Services Provider. However, the job seeker may choose to volunteer into Employment Provider Services. In these cases, the job seeker must discuss this with their provider.

Note: to view the business details, see the Real Estate/Business Summary (REBS) screen.

Assessed income meeting mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

Is the job seeker’s assessed income meeting the required number of hours to fully meet their mutual obligation requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Assessed income from net business income + Read more ...

Is the job seeker's net business income equal to or more than the national minimum wage rate for the number of hours required?

That is, 30 hours per fortnight at the national minimum wage for job seekers with part-time requirements and job seekers aged 55 and over. For principal carers doing a combination of self-employment and approved study or self-employment and other paid work, the business income must be equal to the national minimum wage for the agreed hours of self-employment.

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Self-employment does not fully meet mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

If the self-employment does not satisfy the sufficient work test, the job seeker must do another approved activity entirely or may be able to combine their self-employment with another approved activity.

Note: Services Australia can approve employment activity combinations for both Services Australia and provider managed job seekers. In the case of job seekers aged 55 and over, Services Australia can approve voluntary work and employment combinations. Services Australia can also approve a combination of self-employment, paid part-time work and/or approved study for principal carers. Only an Employment Services Provider can approve voluntary work or a combination of voluntary work and other activities for principal carers under 55.

If the job seeker is not doing activities that enable them to be Services Australia managed and they are not exempt from mutual obligation requirements, they must be referred to an Employment Services Provider if they have not already been connected.

Employment Services Providers are responsible for negotiating and maintaining the Job Plan for provider managed job seeker.

Investigate other approved activities for the job seeker.

Procedure ends here.

6

Job seeker's initial assessment satisfies mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

Self-employment must equal 30 hours or more per fortnight for partial capacity to work (PCW) 15-29 hours per week, job seekers aged 55 and over and principal carers. In addition, net business income must also equal or be more than the national minimum wage per fortnight for the number of required hours.

Job seekers assessed with a PCW or temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC) of 0-7 or 8-14 hours per week may fully meet their mutual obligation requirements through self-employment or a combination of self-employment and paid work activities within their assessed bandwidth.

Principal carers can also combine self-employment and other paid work, or self-employment and study totalling at least 30 hours per fortnight to fully meet requirements, as long as the total remuneration is sufficient for the agreed hours of self-employment.

  • Record the self-employment as an approved activity on the AEX screen using the following codes:
    • For job seekers aged 55 and over doing a combination of voluntary work and self-employment, use both codes of 'SEL' and 'VWK'
    • For principal carers doing a combination of self-employment and other paid work, use the code 'PWS'
    • For principal carers doing a combination of self-employment and approved study, use the code of 'SSE'
  • The start date used for the AEX screen should be the date of coding, to ensure details are transferred to the Department of Education, Skills and Employment system
  • Negotiate a Job Plan with the job seeker that includes the agreed activity/ies and ensure it includes corresponding dates to the activity/ies coded on the AEX screen. In the Job Plan workflow, the 'Self employment' activity is located under the 'Employment' heading
  • Ensure any other non-current activities are removed from the Job Plan

Note: SEL should not be coded on the AEX screen for more than 12 months at a time and requires a new Job Plan every 12 months if the work is continuing.

Details must be recorded using the Self employment script.

Advise the job seeker they:

  • are fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements while they are doing the agreed approved activity
  • do not have job search efforts however, they still must declare their participation in the approved activity to Services Australia each fortnight
  • will be automatically suspended from Employment Provider Services, however they can voluntarily continue to participate with their provider
  • must advise Services Australia of changes about their participation in the agreed approved activity (e.g. cessation of self-employment or increase in income) within the required timeframe and how the changes may affect their mutual obligation requirements

If they are not connected to a provider or they are suspended from Employment Provider Services, they do not need to attend ongoing provider appointments.

Job seekers aged 55 and over are suspended from Employment Provider Services, however, they must stay connected to their provider and still must accept full-time, suitable paid work or an increase in their work hours and must accept all referrals to suitable job interviews.

Business income, participation not met, reasonable excuse and change in activities

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Ongoing assessment of self-employment + Read more ...

Job seekers who are self-employed and have had their business income assessed, only need to report they have participated in the activity specified in their Job Plan.

Hours of work - Self-employment must equal:

  • 30 hours or more per fortnight if the job seeker has a partial capacity to work (PCW) 15-29 hours per week, or aged 55 and over or is a principal carer
  • Within the job seekers bandwidth where they are PCW or temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC) 0-7 or 8-14 hours per week

Business income - The job seeker's net business income must be equal to or more than the national minimum wage rate for the number of hours required. If business income has been assessed and there have not been any significant changes to business activity, they only need to write 'as previously declared' in the earnings section of the Reporting Statement (SU19) form. If the job seeker is reporting via self service, their business details will be displayed and the job seeker can advise if there has been any change.

Note: any changes to business activity must be notified along with other changes during the reporting process.

Does the job seeker's self-employment income and declared hours of employment still fully meet their mutual obligation requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Mutual obligation requirements not met + Read more ...

  • Does the job seeker have an exemption from their mutual obligation requirements? If so, the Service Officer should go to 'reasonable excuse' select 'no excuse' and record the reason why 1 was not required on a DOC. Process the payment, subject to all other eligibility being met
  • If the job seeker requests an exemption from their mutual obligation requirements, ensure they provide evidence to support their claim. Consider the merits of the case and make a decision. If an exemption is granted, code it on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen. Process the payment, subject to all other eligibility being met. See Mutual obligation requirement exemptions
  • If reporting by self service and the job seeker indicates they have failed to meet their mutual obligation requirements they will need Service Office interactions before their payment is released

Does the job seeker have a reasonable excuse for not meeting their mutual obligation requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Job seeker has a reasonable excuse for not meeting the self-employment requirements + Read more ...

A reasonable excuse can only be 1 of the following:

  • sick child (or children)
  • a cancelled shift
  • short term reduction in hours due to a public holiday
  • pupil free day/s
  • failure of special disability related transport
  • with an expectation that working hours will return to normal in the next fortnight
  • If this is the first reasonable excuse:
    • The Service Officer should code the reason through the Earnings and Reporting workflow and process the job seeker's payment as usual
    • The Service Officer should advise the job seeker that a maximum of 2 consecutive reasonable excuses may be given
    • Job seekers aged 55 and over are allowed a maximum of 3 consecutive fortnights with reasonable excuses. However, on the second or third fortnight the job seeker must choose a new activity that continues to fully meet their mutual obligation requirements or be referred to an Employment Services Provider if not already connected to 1. See Employment Services Providers
    • If the job seeker remains Services Australia managed, negotiate a new Job Plan with an appropriate activity
    • Ensure any other non-current activities are removed from the Job Plan
    • Procedure ends here
  • If this is the second or third consecutive reasonable excuse, go to Step 4

4

Job seeker must choose another activity + Read more ...

After providing 2 or 3 consecutive reasonable excuses, the job seeker must choose another activity, as they can no longer meet their requirements through self-employment.

The Service Officer should:

  • process the outstanding Reporting Statement (SU19) form using the Earnings and Reporting workflow
  • end date the approved activity/ies on the AEX screen. The default of JSE should automatically apply as the approved activity
  • connect or reconnect the job seeker with their Employment Services Provider (if not already active with 1). See Employment Services Providers

Ending the activity on the AEX screen shows the provider that the job seeker is not meeting requirements through an approved activity and ensures the job seeker will be engaged by the provider in a timely manner. The job seeker's record will display as provider managed when the Department of Education, Skills and Employment sends advice that the job seeker has commenced or recommenced in Employment Provider Services.

Note: when the job seeker becomes provider managed, the Employment Services Provider is responsible for renegotiating the job seeker's Job Plan.

Procedure ends here.

5

Insufficient hours to meet requirements - no excuse + Read more ...

Job seekers who fail to declare sufficient self-employment work hours to fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements, and have no reasonable excuse, may be able to choose another activity to fully meet their mutual obligation requirements and allow them to remain Services Australia managed. They cannot meet their obligations only through self-employment.

If the job seeker is no longer doing activities that fully meet mutual obligation requirements, they must immediately resume participation with their Employment Services Provider, or be referred, if not already active with 1, and must attend all appointments. See Employment Services Providers.

Investigate other approved activities for the job seeker. If the job seeker does another activity or combination of activities that can be approved by Services Australia as fully meeting mutual obligation requirements:

  • end date the previous approved activities on the AEX screen. The default of JSE should automatically apply as the approved activity
  • otherwise, connect or reconnect the job seeker with their Employment Services Provider (if not already active with 1). See Employment Services Providers

Note: when the job seeker becomes provider managed, the Employment Services Provider is responsible for renegotiating the Job Plan with the job seeker.

Procedure ends here.

6

Meets the ongoing requirements for self-employment + Read more ...

Self-employment must equal 30 hours or more per fortnight for PCW 15-29 hrs per week, job seekers aged 55 and over and principal carers. Any self-employment within the bandwidth of a PCW/TRWC 0-14 hrs per week job seeker will fully meet requirements. If a principal carer is doing a combination of self-employment and study, or self-employment and other paid employment, the total hours between the 2 activities must be at least 30 hours per fortnight.

Advise the job seeker:

  • they are fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements as they are doing an approved activity
  • they do not need to report job search efforts when reporting to Services Australia
  • they must still be available and willing to do other paid work if it is offered to them

7

Update self-employment details + Read more ...

Are there details of the self-employment on the job seeker's record? Note: business details are on the Business Details (BUS) screen.

  • Yes, select the business details from the Real Estate/Business Summary (REBS) screen, go to Step 8
  • No, code the Real Estate/Business Identifying (REBI) screen. Once completed, the activity will flow to the BUS screen for updating, go to Step 8

8

Coding business details + Read more ...

Update the job seeker's business income and hours of employment. This should be done by using tax returns or profit and loss statements, depending on how long the business has been running for.

Code the BUS screen with the income details and ensure the hours of employment are entered in the Hours p/f (C/P) field.

Record a DOC about how the income assessment was calculated.

Procedure ends here.

Self-employment review for job seekers 55 years and over - updating the Job Plan

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Review of self-employment as an approved activity for job seekers aged 55 and over + Read more ...

If the job seeker is aged 55 and over, and doing a combination of self-employment and voluntary work for at least 30 hours per fortnight, they must attend an annual review to discuss their mutual obligation requirements. See Voluntary work as an approved activity process page for details.

If the activity type of self-employment is being reviewed, the Service Officer should check the job seeker still wants to continue with this activity.

If there are no changes:

  • extend the end date of the approved activity/ies on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen if needed
  • record the conversation on a DOC
  • if Services Australia managed (including partial capacity to work (PCW) / temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC) 0-14 job seekers)
  • review and agree on a new Job Plan that reflects the approved activity and is consistent with the dates on the AEX screen

If there are changes (e.g. job seeker has ceased self-employment, self-employment is no longer fully meeting the requirement or job seeker wants to change their activity type):

  • end date the approved activity/ies on the AEX screen. The default of JSE should automatically apply as the approved activity
  • connect or reconnect the job seeker with their Employment Services Provider (if not already active with 1). See Employment Services Providers
  • record decision on a DOC

Note: when the job seeker becomes provider managed, the Employment Services Provider is responsible for renegotiating the Job Plan with the job seeker.

Procedure ends here.

2

Previously approved self-employment (SEL) activity has expired on the AEX screen + Read more ...

Is the job seeker still meeting requirements through sufficient paid self-employment?

  • Yes, check the job seeker’s current Job Plan. If a corresponding activity in the Job Plan has:
    • expired, extend the end date of the SEL activity on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen by 28 days. Note: SEL can only be coded for a maximum period of 12 months on the AEX screen, therefore a new SEL line may need to be coded if the 12 month period has already been exceeded.
      Refer the job seeker to their local service centre for their Job Plan to be updated, by sending an ACTDOC to the service centre to advise. Procedure ends here
    • not expired, extend the end date of the SEL activity on the AEX screen to correspond with the Job Plan activity end date. Note: SEL can only be coded for a maximum period of 12 months, therefore a new SEL line may need to be coded on the AEX screen if the 12 month period has already been exceeded. Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • End date the approved activity/ies on the AEX screen. The default of JSE should automatically apply as the approved activity
    • Connect or reconnect the job seeker with their Employment Services Provider (if not already active with 1). See Employment Services Providers
    • Record decision on a DOC

Note: when the job seeker becomes provider managed, the Employment Services Provider is responsible for renegotiating the Job Plan with the job seeker.

3

Check the job seeker's current Job Plan + Read more ...

Is there a corresponding activity in the Job Plan that has expired?

  • Yes, use the Job Plan workflow to negotiate a new Job Plan including the self-employment activity. Update the AEX screen to extend the SEL activity code. Note: SEL can only be coded for a maximum period of 12 months on the AEX screen. Therefore, a new SEL line may be required if the 12 month period has already been exceeded
  • No, extend the end date of the SEL activity on the AEX screen to correspond with the Job Plan end date. Note: SEL can only be coded for a maximum period of 12 months on the AEX screen. Therefore, a new SEL line may be required if the 12 month period has already been exceeded
