Determining if the customer is a homeowner or a non-homeowner for the Accommodation Details (AC) screen

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Living in own home + Read more ...

A customer is considered to own the home they live in when they and/or their partner:

  • own the property outright
  • are purchasing the property, or
  • have an interest in the property (for example, by way of paying instalments)
  • are temporarily absent for up to 12 months for an approved reason

Note: this includes mobile accommodation such as a caravan, tent or boat, where a customer has reasonable security of tenure. See Assessing caravans, boats and park homes to determine home ownership

Does the customer and/or their partner own the home they live in?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Amounts paid for the right to live in accommodation for life + Read more ...

The customer can financially secure a right to live in their accommodation for life (for example, life interest) by:

  • paying, or agreeing to pay a lump sum amount (for example, entry contribution), or
  • transferring, or agreeing to transfer assets

Has the customer financially secured a right to live in their accommodation for life in one of the above ways?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Right to accommodation for life and homeownership + Read more ...

Eligibility for Rent Assistance (RA) relies on the determination of homeownership status. Compare the amount paid (for example, entry contribution) or value of assets transferred for the right to accommodation for life with the Extra Allowable Amount (EAA).

Does the comparison of the amount paid with the EAA determine the customer is a homeowner?

  • Yes, see Step 2 in Table 3
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

4

Other homeowner situations + Read more ...

The customer would also be considered a homeowner when they:

  • leave the principal home they own to enter an aged care or nursing home residence, and retain ownership of the vacated principal home
  • have the right to use the home they live in for life, also called a life interest. This can be established by a deceased estate or third party
  • have a deemed interest in a residence for example, the home is owned by a Trust or Company in which the customer has an interest and that interest gives the customer reasonable security of tenure
  • sell their previous principal home, intend to repurchase and proceeds are determined to be exempt assets
  • own their principal home but live elsewhere

Does the customer meet any of the above criteria?

  • Yes, see Step 3 in Table 3
  • No, see Step 2 in Table 2

Selecting an AC screen option for non-homeowners

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Non-homeowner lives in a special residence + Read more ...

Special residences include:

  • granny flats
  • retirement villages
  • sale - leaseback homes, for more information see References
  • shared equity housing

Does the customer live in a special residence?

  • Yes, the correct option is Lives in Special Residence - SRN. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Non-homeowner life interest or right to accommodation for life + Read more ...

This applies if the customer is:

  • paying for a life interest or right to accommodation for life
  • living in a private residence that is to be their principal home, and
  • assessed as being a non-homeowner

Do all of the above apply?

  • Yes, the correct option is Purchased life tenancy - PLT. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Non-homeowner in government funded aged care + Read more ...

The Aged Care Processing Team enters this code. An Aged Care Assessment may be needed. Service Officers not in the Aged Care Processing Team, see Rent Assistance (RA) for care receivers.

Is the customer a non-homeowner in a government funded bed in an accredited aged care home?

  • Yes, the correct option is Non homeowner - in government funded aged care - GFN. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Non-homeowner lives in nursing home + Read more ...

A customer is considered to live in a nursing home if they are a non-homeowner and residing in a:

  • multi-purpose service facility
  • non-subsidised (unfunded) bed, in an accredited aged care home, or
  • hospital as a nursing home type patient awaiting admission to an aged care home

Does the customer live in a nursing home?

  • Yes, the correct option is Non-homeowner - Lives in Nursing Home - NHN. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Customer is a non-homeowner + Read more ...

If none of the above circumstances apply, the correct option is Non-homeowner - NHO.

6

Other considerations + Read more ...

Was the customer previously a homeowner?

  • Yes, no matter whether they have sold the previous home or kept it:
    • an update to real estate details may be needed
    • an update to other assets may be needed for example, financial assets
    • Include asset changes on the DOC
    • go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen + Read more ...

See Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA) to finalise the update.

Selecting an AC screen option for homeowners

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Owns or is purchasing a home + Read more ...

Record the customer as a homeowner if they and/or their partner own the home they live in, even if there is:

  • a mortgage, or
  • joint ownership

This includes mobile homes, for example caravan, boat or tent if the customer has reasonable security of tenure. See Assessing caravans, boats and park homes to determine home ownership

.

Does the customer and/or their partner jointly or singly own, or purchasing the home that they live in?

  • Yes, the correct option is Homeowner - Jointly/singly owns or purchasing home - HOM. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Homeowner lives in special residence + Read more ...

Special residences include:

  • granny flats
  • retirement villages
  • sale - leaseback homes, for more information see References
  • shared equity housing

Does the customer live in a special residence?

  • Yes, the correct option is Homeowner - Lives in a special residence - SRH. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Homeowner in government funded age care + Read more ...

The Aged Care Processing Team enters this code. An Aged Care Assessment may be needed. Service Officers not in the Aged Care Processing Team, see Rent Assistance (RA) for care receivers.

Is the customer a homeowner in a government funded bed in an accredited aged care home?

  • Yes, the correct option is Homeowner - Homeowner in government funded aged care - GFH. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Homeowner lives in a nursing home + Read more ...

A customer is considered to be living in a nursing home, if they are a homeowner and residing in either a:

  • multi-purpose service facility
  • non-subsidised (unfunded) bed, in an accredited aged care home, or
  • hospital as a nursing home type patient awaiting admission to an aged care home

Does the customer live in a nursing home?

  • Yes, the correct option is Homeowner - Lives in a nursing home - NHH. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Homeowner life interest or right to accommodation for life + Read more ...

If the customer has the right to the use of a home for life, was it established by a deceased estate or a third party?

  • Yes, the correct option is Homeowner - Bequeathed life interest - LIF. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Deemed interest in a residence + Read more ...

A customer has a deemed interest in a residence where they:

  • have a right to the use of a home for life, or
  • have created a life interest or a right of occupancy in a private home for their own benefit

Does the customer have a deemed interest in a residence?

  • Yes, the correct option is Homeowner - Deemed interest - DEE. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Owns a home but lives elsewhere + Read more ...

Does the customer reside in the home they own?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, the correct option is Homeowner - Lives elsewhere - LHO. Go to Step 9

8

Owns home but no other classification codes apply + Read more ...

Use this option rarely. Customers normally fit into another category.

If none of the above circumstances apply, the correct option is Homeowner - Owns home but can't be classified by any other code - OTH. Go to Step 9.

9

Other considerations + Read more ...

Was the customer previously a homeowner?

  • Yes, no matter whether they have sold the previous home or kept it:
    • an update to real estate details may be needed. See also Permanent vacation of principal home
    • an update to other assets may be needed for example, financial assets
    • leave customer as LHO, see Temporary vacation of principal home
    • Include asset changes on the DOC
    • go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen + Read more ...

See Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA) to finalise the update.
