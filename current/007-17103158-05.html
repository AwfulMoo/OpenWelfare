Evidence requirements

Note:

  • Evidence already available on a customer's record (for example, Employment Separation Certificate, Medical Certificate etc.) can be used as evidence to support an application for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship)
  • Evidence must demonstrate the link between the event and a substantial reduction in the ability to pay for child care fees
  • Statutory declarations cannot be the sole source of evidence for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) applications, but can serve as complementary evidence to explain how an event has impacted their financial circumstances

Evidence to support ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) applications

Period of reduced evidence - COVID 19

If a period of reduced evidence is approved due to Coronavirus (COVID -19), acceptable evidence includes:

  • an Employment Separation Certificate
  • a letter from the employer that indicates they no longer have employment

The circumstance or event was either:

  • loss of employment of the individual, or a partner, other than due to resignation or retirement
  • loss of income or business failure of the individual, or a partner, due to circumstances outside of their control

Approved periods:

  • 26 March 2020 to 4 April 2021
  • 12 July 2021 onwards

Note: within the Approved periods listed above, this will apply to all government declared areas where an individual would be entitled to receive a COVID-19 Disaster Payment.

Period of reduced evidence - Disaster

If a period of reduced evidence is approved, where the customer or their partner receive an applicable government disaster payment, they will only need to show proof of this payment. No further evidence will be required to demonstrate financial hardship.

Approved disaster events:

  • Victoria Bushfires - December 2019
  • New South Wales Bushfires - September 2019
  • South Australian Bushfires - November 2019
  • Eastern Queensland Bushfires - November 2019
  • Queensland Bushfires - September 2019
  • Tasmanian Bushfires - December 2019
  • New South Wales North Coast Floods and Queensland Floods - February 2021
  • New South Wales Floods - March 2021
  • Western Australia Tropical Cyclone Seroja - April 2021
  • Victorian Storms and Floods - June 2021

Payments include:

  • Australian Government Disaster recovery payment or ex-gratia disaster recovery payment
  • Disaster recovery allowance or ex-gratia disaster recovery allowance from the Australian Government
  • State Emergency payments

To view a customer/partners proof of payment check:

  • Payment Summary (PS), or
  • Notes/Document List

In most cases, the circumstance or event was either:

  • being adversely affected by a major disaster
  • the destruction of, or severe damage to, their home

Note: if a State body granted the payment, staff may not be able to see the payment in Customer First or Process Direct. In these circumstances, request the customer to supply proof of payment. Check that the customer got the payment for a disaster event, which occurred in the past 6 months of the application.

Customers are able to receive up to 13 weeks ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) per disaster event.
