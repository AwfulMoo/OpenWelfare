All Other Areas in NSW

Due to changes in relevant areas deemed a COVID-19 hotspot since August 2021, staff must carefully check which of the 3 impact reasons and relevant area a customer is claiming under.

Customers claiming the COVID-19 Disaster Payment can claim under 1 of 3 impact reasons

  • L - live or work in a relevant area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement that is a deemed a COVID-19 hotspot by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer, or
  • P - were present in a COVID-19 hotspot at the time the restricted movement was declared and are now subject to a second public health order that restricts their movement in the state or territory they are currently in, or
  • I - live or work in a state or territory that has an area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer

Note: for online claims, this shows as ‘I am impacted by a COVID-19 restricted movement/lockdown, but do not live, work and was not present in a COVID-19 hotspot.

All relevant areas in All Other Areas of NSW stopped being a COVID-19 hotspot from 19 October 2021. The impact reasons a customer was able to apply under for any relevant period is outlined in tables 5 to 7.

For relevant periods 15 and 16, customers will only be able to apply if they received payment ($750/$450) for relevant periods 14 and continue to meet the eligibility criteria.

For relevant periods 15 and 16, customers will only be able to apply under one impact reason:

  • F - received the final payment that was released in the relevant period in which the 80% target was met. Note: while this is not 1 of the 3 impact reasons a customer can claim under for earlier relevant periods, for the 2 final relevant periods, it is the only option a customer can claim under. The customer must have previously received a payment for 1 of the 3 impact reasons to receive a payment for the 2 final relevant periods.

Geospatial tool

Due to changes in the relevant areas deemed a COVID-19 hotspot for different parts of New South Wales since August 2021, staff must use this tool to carefully check the relevant area a customer is claiming under to determine if they live, work or were present in a COVID-19 hotspot area for the relevant period.

Note: for customers outside a COVID-19 hotspot, staff need to check the residential and/or employer addresses provided for the relevant period are in New South Wales.

To assess if a customer lives, works or was present in a relevant area:

  • Open the NSW Live Feed App
  • Place the address given by the customer in the search box. Residential, employer and/or present in addresses may need to be checked
  • Select the correct address that populates in the drop down

Before all of New South Wales was deemed a COVID-19 hotspot for any claim relating to a relevant period before or including 15 August 2021:

  • Select COVID-19 Disaster Payment NSW in the Layers menu
  • To be eligible for living or working in, or present in, an area deemed a COVID-19 hotspot:
    • a relevant area in Sydney, the address must lie in the red shaded area
    • a relevant area in Greater Sydney, the address must lie in the blue shaded area
    • a relevant area in Greater Hunter (from 5 August 2021) for All Other Areas in NSW, the address must lie in the brown shaded area to be eligible
    • Blayney, Cabonne and Orange (for 21-28 July 2021), for All Other Areas in NSW, the address must lie in the purple shaded area
    • Armidale, Ballina, Byron, Lismore, Richmond Valley and Tamworth (from 9 August 2021), for All Other Areas in NSW, the address must lie in the green shaded area
    • Bathurst, Bogan, Bourke, Brewarrina, Dubbo, Gilgandra, Narromine, Walget and Warren (from 11 August 2021), for All Other Areas in NSW, the address must lie in the yellow shaded area

From 15 August 2021, all of New South Wales is deemed a COVID-19 hotspot:

  • The following maps will be automatically selected in the layers menu - staff do not need to unselect a map:
    • COVID-19 Disaster Payment NSW - August 2021 and
    • Jervis Bay - COVID-19 - Disaster Payment NSW

From 19 October 2021, all of New South Wales is no longer deemed a COVID-19 hotspot:

The following maps will be automatically selected in the layers menu – staff do not need to unselect a map:

  • COVID-19 Disaster Payment NSW - August 2021 and
  • Jervis Bay - COVID-19 - Disaster Payment NSW

The address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible.

Check the relevant area so the correct workflow is run:

  • Staff must check the relevant area by selecting:
    • the small bright blue box, then
    • in the pop up box, the white triangle arrow that points to the right
  • The relevant area will show in bold at the top of the pop up box as follows:
    • Relevant Area: Greater Sydney, or
    • Relevant Area: Sydney, or
    • August 2021 map - Relevant Area: Other NSW (for All Other Areas in NSW)
    • Not the August 2021 map - Relevant Area: All Other Areas in NSW or for the Greater Hunter, Hunter Valley & All Other Areas of NSW
  • The Local Government Area (LGA) where the address is located will show below the Relevant Area
  • To make sure the correct workflow is selected, check the relevant area (Greater Sydney, Sydney or Other NSW/All Other Areas in NSW) that shows in the pop up box
  • For Other NSW/All Other Areas in NSW select the workflow with outside relevant area
  • Continue to assess the claim

Scenarios - eligibility

Scenario

Outcome

Unable to attend work due to exemption from mask wearing

The customer works in a café as a casual for 25 hours per week.

The customer has an exemption from wearing a mask, however, a state public health order mandates that people working in food and beverage outlets must wear a mask.

Their employer tells the customer that they cannot attend work.

Eligible

The customer is eligible for the at least 20 hours or more rate of payment as they have lost 25 hours of work in the relevant period.

The customer’s rate of payment will be $750.

Note: if there was no public health order to mandate mask wearing and this was a condition of employment imposed by the employer, the customer would not be eligible for payment.

Unable to work due to restricted movement

The customer works in a café in Sydney CBD as a casual with an average of 30 hrs per week.

Due to the state public health order for restricted movement, the customer has been unable to attend their workplace for the entire period of the restricted movement. The restricted movement was for an initial 7 days and was then extended for a further 7 days.

The customer has insufficient appropriate leave to cover the extended restricted movement or relevant period.

The customer has $3,490 in their bank account and $10 cash. The customer is not receiving an income support payment or a state/commonwealth based pandemic payment for any days in the relevant period.

Eligible

The state public health order for a stay at home restricted movement was greater than 7 days.

The customer would otherwise have worked but was unable to attend work from the eighth day to the fourteenth day of the restricted movement.

The customer has lost 30 hours of work during the relevant period.

The customer has insufficient appropriate leave to cover the relevant period.

The customer is paid $500.

Unable to work due to restricted movement

The customer is an essential worker living and working in a declared COVID-19 hotspot.

The customer has 2 children, aged 4 and 7, who ordinarily attend child care while the customer is at work. Due to the restricted movement conditions, the customer's child care centre has closed. The customer is unable to perform their work from home and unable to earn income for the period of the movement restrictions.

The customer would normally work 7 hour shifts 5 times per week.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer is eligible for the at least 20 hours or more rate of payment, as they as have lost 35 hours of work in the relevant period.

The customer's rate of payment will be $750.

The customer will receive automated payments for the remaining periods unless there is a change in their circumstances.

  • automatic payments stopped, and
  • the customer will lodge a new claim for each relevant period
    • they can use the abridged claim process to lodge their claim

Sydney

  • Relevant period 18: $320

  • Relevant period 17: $450
  • Relevant period 18: $320

All Other Areas in NSW

  • Relevant period 15: $450
  • Relevant period 16: $320

Paid leave for part of restricted movement period

The customer lives in Katoomba and works in the Blue Mountains Tourism Centre part time, 5 hours per day, Monday to Friday.

Due to the state public health order for restricted movement, the customer has been unable to attend their workplace for the period of the restricted movement. The restricted movement was for an initial 7 days and was then extended for a further 7 days.

The customer has been paid 9 days of special pandemic leave by their employer starting from the first day of the restricted movement.

The customer has $8,999 in their bank account and $100 cash. The customer is not receiving an income support payment or a state/commonwealth based pandemic payment.

Eligible

The state public health order for a stay at home restricted movement was greater than 7 days.

The customer was unable to attend work from the eighth day to the fourteenth day of the restricted movement.

Although they have been paid 9 days leave from their employer, it is not sufficient to cover the entire period for the second period of the restricted movement. That is, it covers 5 days of the first week and 4 days of the second week. It does not cover 1 day.

The customer would have worked more than 20 hours (25 hours), but the appropriate leave covered 4 of 5 days they would have expected to work. Therefore, the customer only experienced a reduction in hours on one day, which equals 5 hours.

The customer is paid $325. The payment is not pro-rata.

Self-employed customer - impacted by 3 relevant periods

The customer is a self-employed gardener who lives in Randwick and works 30 hours per week (6 hours per day) at various homes across the LGAs of City of Sydney, Randwick and Shellharbour. Due to the state public health order for restricted movement, the customer has been unable to attend work, unable to work from home and unable to earn an income for the period of the restricted movement.

The customer has been impacted by the restricted movement for City of Sydney and Randwick from 23 June 2021 to 16 July 2021.

The customer was also impacted by the restricted movement for Shellharbour from 26 June 2021 to 9 July 2021.

All income has been impacted due to the fact they live in an LGA announced as restricted movement from 23 June 2021.

The customer has less than $10,000 in cash and savings. The customer is not receiving an income support payment or a state/commonwealth based pandemic payment or a small business payment.

Eligible for 2 relevant periods

The customer can only claim one payment of COVID-19 Disaster Payment for each relevant period.

Although the customer was impacted by 3 relevant periods of restricted movement under the LGAs of City of Sydney and Randwick, and a relevant period of restricted movement under the LGA of Shellharbour, the customer is eligible for only 3 payments. The customer chooses the event they would like to claim for under each relevant period.

If choosing Sydney as the first event they claim for, the customer cannot claim a further payment under the restricted movement for Shellharbour for any relevant period that overlaps.

The payments the customer can get are:

  • $500
    loss of more than 20 hours for the relevant period 1-7 July 2021 for the Sydney event
  • $500
    loss of more than 20 hours for the relevant period 8-14 July 2021 for the Sydney event
  • $600
    loss of more than 20 hours for the relevant period 15-21 July 2021 for the Sydney event
  • $600
    loss of more than 20 hours for relevant period of 22-28 July 2021 for Sydney event
  • $600
    loss of more than 20 hours for relevant period of 29 July 2021-4 August 2021 for Sydney event
  • $750
    loss of more than 20 hours for relevant period of 5-11 August 2021 for Sydney event
  • $750 for all future relevant periods up to and including relevant period 16

The customer cannot claim a further payment under the restricted movement for Shellharbour as they have made a claim for the relevant period/s already.

The customer will receive automated payments for the remaining periods unless there is a change in their circumstances.

Once NSW reaches 70% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older:

  • automatic payments will stop, and
  • the customer will need to lodge a new claim for each relevant period
    • they can use the abridged claim process to lodge their claim

Once NSW reaches 80% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, payments will reduce for 2 weeks before stopping. As Randwick is in ‘Sydney’ the customer will receive:

  • Relevant period 17: $450
  • Relevant period 18: $320

Reduced work due to restricted movement

The customer works casually in a café in Hunters Hill (Greater Sydney). They were scheduled to work:

  • 38 hours over 5 separate days between 27 June and 3 July 2021, and
  • 30 hours over 4 days for the week 4-10 July 2021

Due to the restricted movement, they were unable to work from 26 June 2021.

The movement restrictions were announced for the period 26 June 2021 to 10 July 2021 (inclusive). The customer has no leave to cover the period of movement restrictions.

The customer has less than $10,000 in cash and savings. The customer is not receiving an income support payment or a state/commonwealth based pandemic payment or a small business payment.

Eligible

The state public health order for restricted movement was greater than 7 days.

The customer was unable to attend work for 5 days in the first relevant period (27 June to 3 July 2021). As there is no COVID-19 Disaster Payment for the first 7 days of the restricted movement, there is no payment for this period.

The customer was unable to work 5 days in the second period (4-10 July 2021).

This results in reduction of 30 hours of work.

The customer is paid $500 for this relevant period.

Works but does not live in a relevant area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement that is deemed a COVID-19 hotspot

The customer lives in the LGA of Upper Hunter, which is a relevant area not subject to a state public health order for restricted movement that is deemed a COVID-19 hotspot.

They normally work as a cleaner and clean a motel in the LGA of Central Coast (part time hours of 10 per week, 2 hours per day).

Because of the state public health order for a restricted movement and, as a result the significant downturn in tourism, the customer is unable to attend the motel to work in the LGA of Central Coast. They are told they are not required to clean the motel until at least 1 August 2021.

The customer is not receiving an income support payment or a state/commonwealth based pandemic payment or a small business payment.

Eligible

The customer has been impacted by the restricted movement for Greater Sydney, reducing their hours for the relevant period by 10 hours.

The customer is eligible for 2 payments of COVID-19 Disaster Payment;

  • $375 for loss of at least 8 hours and less than 20 hours or a full day of work for relevant period 18-24 July 2021
  • $375 for loss of at least 8 hours and less than 20 hours or a full day of work for relevant period 25-30 July 2021

 

Was present in a relevant area

The customer is 22 and an Australian citizen working full time.

The customer resides in South Australia and travelled into the LGA of Waverley on 23 June 2021 to attend a work conference being held by the ICT company they work for.

The restricted movement came into effect on 23 June 2021 as the customer was on a flight home. Upon arrival in South Australia, the customer is under a second public health order to quarantine for 14 days as they had been in a hotspot (Waverley) that was now under restricted movement.

Due to the second public order, the customer will not be able to work and earn an income from 24 June 2021 to 7 July 2021.

The workplace has provided the customer with special pandemic leave for the period 24 - 30 June 2021. The customer is not receiving any other payments.

The customer has $5,500 in her savings account and no other liquid assets.

Eligible

The customer will receive assistance as follows:

  • 1-7 July 2021, $500 COVID-19 Disaster Payment as lost 40 hours of work

The customer will claim for the payment and confirm that they meet the criteria for the relevant period.

The customer does not need to lodge evidence when making the claim but does need to make a self-declaration of their circumstances and acknowledge that Services Australia can make enquiries to determine correct entitlement.

  • deemed a COVID-19 hotspot
