This page contains how the income and asset details contained in the Income and Assets (SA369) are used to assess a customer's entitlement to an Income Support Payment (ISP). For Farm Household Allowance (FHA), see Assessing assets for Farm Household Allowance (FHA) or Assessing income for Farm Household Allowance.

3

Income and Assets (SA369) + Read more ...

If not already done:

  • issue a SA369 to the customer, and
  • setup a review for return within 14 days

If customer is partnered, make sure that income and assets details are obtained for both even if the partner is not claiming.

On return of SA369 or equivalent, assess to see if any of the questions suggest more investigation or action regarding the customer's income and assets. Make sure the requested documents is supplied for each question.

When processing the SA369 if a customer and/or partner are not currently receiving any income support payments but have an existing record, make sure all income and asset categories are checked and any income and assets that no longer exist are removed (zero'd).

4

Employment details + Read more ...

Is the customer or their partner employed?

  • Yes, record employment income by selecting the employer from Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen, see Recording and correcting employment income details
    • Check if receiving fringe benefits - see Assessing fringe benefits for Social Security income test purposes. Go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Recording asset details + Read more ...

In the customer's record, go to the Other assets summary (OASS) screen.by keying 'OASS' in the Nxt: field.

Are asset details already recorded?

  • Yes, select the Description link of the type of asset to be recorded:
    • Enter the value of the asset in the Total Asset $: field
    • If the asset is owned with the customer's partner, enter the percentage owned by the customer in the % Owned Client: field and the percentage owned by the partner in the % Owned Partner: field
      Note: check that the percentage owned by the customer is correctly recorded against the asset. If customers have had a recent change in circumstances, percentages may need updating
    • Complete the Source: and DOR: fields as appropriate
    • Select the box to the left of the asset update and select Update to update the screen details
    • The system will then read the Accommodation (AC) screen to see if the customer is a home owner or not to determine the right personal assets test limits
    • Go to Step 7
  • No, go to the Other assets (OAS) screen. by keying 'OAS' in the Nxt: field. Go to Step 6

6

Recording the OAS screen + Read more ...

To record details of a new asset on the OAS screen, select the type of asset:

  • LIF = Life insurance policy
  • HPE = Home contents and personal effects
  • VEH = Motor vehicle
  • LIV = Livestock
  • BOA = Boats and accessories
  • CVN = Caravan
  • OTH = Other assets

Add as much detailed information as the field allows to help with checking the asset and the event date in the Description: and Event Date: fields.

Record the value of the asset in the Total Asset $: field.

  • Life Insurance - the surrender value is recorded. If the customer cannot provide the details, use the following formula to calculate the asset value. Number of years policy held x annual premiums paid = value maintained
  • Home contents and personal effects - the market value (not replacement cost)
  • Motor vehicle - market value, less any monies owing
  • Livestock - market value
  • Boats and accessories - market value
  • Caravan - market value
  • Timeshare (recorded as a managed investment on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen) - the legal structure of timeshare arrangements vary widely and can include a share in holiday homes/units, caravan parks, houseboats etc. Do not ask for valuations for timeshare assets. To decide the value of a timeshare:
    • If it was recently purchased, the purchase price is the asset value
    • If it was not purchased recently, the latest sale price which is given by the timeshare manager can be accepted as the asset value
  • Other assets - see Assessment of assets (CLK).

If the asset is owned with the customer's partner, enter the percentage owned by the customer in the % Owned Client: field and the percentage owned by the partner in the % Owned Partner: field.

Complete the Source: and DOR: fields as appropriate and select Save to update the screen details.

7

Record bank accounts + Read more ...

Record details of bank, building society and credit union accounts on the Deposit Accounts (SVDA) screen. SVDA is accessed by the Savings Summary (SVS) or Select Product (SVP) screens.

Note: Check that the name of the bank, type of account, account number, dollar value and ownership % are recorded as provided in the SA369.

If no record is available on the SVP screen, record on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen - see Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services.

8

Check and record income and assets + Read more ...

If the customer or partner has any of the following, record details on the system. See links for further information:

  • Bonds or debentures - see Deeming provisions. Note: do not include friendly society bonds, funeral bonds, life insurance bonds or accommodation bonds for residing in an aged care facility
  • Money on loan - see Loans
  • Shares, options, warrants, rights, convertible notes or other securities - see Deeming provisions
  • For recording - see Adding shares and securities
  • Managed investments. These include investment trusts, personal investment plans, life insurance bonds and friendly society bonds, but do not include conventional life insurance policies, funeral bonds, superannuation or rollover investments - see:
    • Managed investments - adding a new investment, or
    • Managed investments - updating existing investments
  • Funeral bonds. If the customer and their partner (if applicable) has a contract to have funeral services provided for which an agreed sum has already been paid to the provider or used to buy funeral bonds assigned to the provider request a copy of the funeral bond contract. See:
    • Exempt Funeral Investments (Funeral Bonds), and
    • Exempt lump sums
  • Money invested in superannuation (This includes complying Self-Managed Superannuation Funds (SMSF) or Small APRA Funds (SAF) - see: Assessing superannuation
  • Income streams - see: Income streams
  • Money invested or receiving income from investments not declared elsewhere on the form - see:
    • Assessment of other ordinary income for Centrelink payment
    • Assessment of assets for Centrelink payments, and
    • Financial Investments
  • Gifts in the last five years (including sold for less than their value, or surrendered a right to, any cash, assets, property or income) - see Assessing deprivation/gifting
  • Payments from another Australian government department - see:
    • Income from Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA), and
    • Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services
  • Income from an agency outside Australia - see Foreign income and assets
  • Income from boarders and lodgers
  • Lump sum payment not declared elsewhere (received in the last 12 months) - see Treatment of lump sums
  • Income from other foreign sources - see Foreign income and assets
  • Income or assets from any other source - see Assessment of assets for Centrelink payments
  • Cash holdings - see Deeming provisions
  • Own their home but live somewhere else - see:
    • Permanent vacation of principal home, or
    • Vacation of principal home due to illness

Check private company/private trust/ business involvement + Read more ...

Are the customer or their partner involved in (or have previously been involved in):

  • A private trust, if not already supplied, issue a Private Trust form (MOD PT) and record a 14 day review for return.
    On return of MOD PT, see Assessing and coding the Private Trust details from the MOD PT. Go to Step 7
  • A private company, if not already given, issue a Private Company form (MOD PC) and record a 14 day review for return.
    On return of MOD PC, see Assessing and coding the Private Company details from the MOD PC. If the Private Company is deregistered, see Identifying and making suitable referrals to the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO). Go to Step 7
  • Other business (including a farm), if not already supplied, issue a Business details form (MOD F) and a record 14 day review for return.
    On return of MOD F, see Assessing and coding the Business details for sole traders and partnerships MOD F
  • No involvement, go to Step 7
