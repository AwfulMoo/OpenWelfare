Review objection and prepare for customer contact

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Prepare for customer contact + Read more ...

When correspondence has been received and indexed as an objection, a Potential Obj Recd intray is created and routed for further action.

If the objection is lodged via CSAonline, a CSAonline reply required, CSAonline review complaint or CSAonline general enq reply intray generates.

Within 2 business days of receiving an objection intray Objections Service Officers must record the objection in the Objection Maintenance window. See Objections Cuba Process Help.

2

Review the objection correspondence + Read more ...

Review the objection correspondence and case history to identify the original decision the customer is disputing:

  • Check where and when the original decision was made if the objection relates to a care decision
  • Identify if there is a Family and Domestic Violence (FDV) indicator on any party to the objection. One or both parents may hold FDV concerns about the other parent, which may or may not be known to Child Support. The exchange of the material may have a significant impact on that person and potentially others
  • Review the correspondence provided about the objection to ensure there is no offensive, derogatory or abusive material to the other party
  • Check External Information, External Information - Care and the Care window to see if Centrelink is conducting, or has conducted, an Authorised Officer Review (ARO) or a Secretary initiated review (SIR/ODM) for the same original care percentage decision
  • This will display as APPL in the Type field of the External Information window
  • If this is unclear from the Cuba records, check Centrelink systems and/or refer via the Centrelink CSP information exchange macro to confirm the original decision review status:
    • SIR review will display as Level ODM and with an outcome of Change (C). Note an outcome of No Action (N) means no SIR review was completed
    • ARO review will display as Level ARO

If there is no Centrelink review in progress:

  • identify the relevant law and policy relating to the decision, see the relevant section of the Child Support Guide for the original decision
  • identify the facts to be established in order to make a decision
  • determine whether an objection decision can be made
  • determine whether an extension of time (EOT) is required before a decision can be made
  • identify possible administrative remedies to address the issues raised in the correspondence, including errors that may need to be corrected in accordance with the Correcting Errors on Child Support cases
  • identify if there are family and domestic violence indicators. See Sensitive issue management Cuba Process Help, Viewing a Sensitive Issue Indicator
  • check the Client Profile window to identify whether the customer has any service restrictions
  • check if the customer is currently locked to Personalised Services or Litigation, if so liaise with the owning case officer prior to commencing contact with the customer
  • check where the original decision was made if the objection relates to a care decision
  • check External Information and or the Care window to determine if Centrelink is conducting an Authorised Officer Review (ARO) or a Secretary initiated review for the same original care percentage decision
  • check if the applicant is objecting to a registered overseas maintenance liability. Section 12 allows the Registrar to provide assistance on behalf of the Australian applicant to seek a variation of an international maintenance arrangement. If the applicant requests and provides permission for Child Support to transmit their details, their objection can be transmitted to the overseas authority on their behalf. See Transmittal of Child Support information (excluding New Zealand) or New Zealand Case Management - Exchange of Information from NZIR to Child Support for more information

Use the Objection Scrutiny macro to record the information obtained from the review.

Note: if any written correspondence contains threats of harm (implied or overt), escalate this immediately to your Team Leader. See Security Incident Management for more information.

3

Additional requirements for Part 6A objections + Read more ...

If the objection relates to a change of assessment decision (Part 6A objection), also identify:

  • the change of assessment reasons in the original application
  • any new change of assessment reasons indicated in the objection
  • if summary disallow provisions apply. If so, do not contact the respondent and go to Summary disallow of Child Support Part 6A objections

4

Determine information required + Read more ...

Make notes of any information required from the customers, including:

  • any evidence needed to establish the facts or change of assessment reasons
  • any information that might clarify the grounds where it is unclear what the objection is based on
  • any other issues to discuss with the applicant and respondent in accordance with the Common and specialised activities for Child Support staff

5

Determine if objection decision can be made + Read more

Child Support must make an objection decision where an objection is lodged and the following conditions apply.

The objection:

  • is in writing, unless it relates to a care percentage decision, go to Step 6
  • is from a person who may object against a decision which carries objection rights, go to Step 7
  • is lodged within 28 days (90 days for customers living in a reciprocating jurisdiction) or an extension of time (EOT) is granted, go to Step 8
  • any accompanying documents contain no offensive content or FDV content, go to Step 9

For more information, see the Child Support Guide 4.1.4: Can an objection decision be made?

6

Objection is in writing + Read more

An objection must be in writing, either by way of written correspondence or on an Objecting to a decision form, unless it is an objection to a care percentage decision (including the date of event of a care change) that was made after 1 July 2010 (3 March 2011 WA ex-nup). See Taking care objections by phone for more information.

The written objection can be lodged in person, by mail, e-mail, fax or via CSAonline.

A representative with ordinary authority to act as a parent's representative may not lodge an objection on behalf of that customer unless the parent signs the objection. See the Child Support Guide 6.3.6 Customers’ authorised representatives.

It is not necessary to use the word 'objection' in a written objection but a customer must ask Child Support to reconsider the original decision.

7

A person may only object against a decision which carries objection rights + Read more

A customer can only object to a decision if there are objection rights to that decision. See the Child Support Guide 4.1.2: Decisions made under the Assessment Act to which a parent may object and the Child Support Guide 4.1.3: Decisions made under the Registration and Collection Act to which a parent may object.

A customer may lodge an objection when:

  • the decision has been finalised on Cuba
  • they have been informed of the decision (either verbally or in writing). Note: a customer can lodge an objection before they are informed of the decision in writing however the timeframe for lodgement of their objection commences from the date they receive the decision letter

If a decision has been made and a customer has not received notification in writing, refer the case to a Team Leader to determine if the letter should be issued by the Objection Officer or the original decision maker.

If it is unclear which decision the customer is objecting to, contact them to clarify. If the customer cannot be contacted by phone, create a unique letter and send to the customer. See Letters Cuba Process Help, Create a Unique letter.

If unable to identify what the customer is objecting to, it will not be possible to establish that an objection has been lodged against a decision which carries objection rights.

If after attempting to contact the customer, it cannot be established that they have objected to a decision which carries objection rights, finalise the objection. See Make decision and finalise Child Support objection.

Note: a customer cannot object to an objection decision. If they want a review of an objection decision they can apply to the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) for a first review. See the Child Support Guide 4.2.2: Decisions which can be reviewed by the AAT.

8

Objection received within 28/90 days of notification + Read more

The objection must be lodged with Child Support within 28 days (or 90 days for customers living in a reciprocating jurisdiction) of the customer receiving notice of the original decision, unless it relates to a care percentage decision. See the objection and response time calculator in either the extension of time calculator or Objection First Contact macro.

Add 12 business days to the start of the 28/90 calendar day timeframe to allow for postage and time for the customer to receive the decision. Also consider customers who live in remote communities with limited postal access.

When deciding if an objection has been received in time, consider where the applicant lives, not the postal address. Where the applicant lives will not always match a person’s Residency as recorded in the Residency window.

Objections to care percentage decisions made after 1 July 2010 (3 March 2011 for WA exnups) do not have a time frame requirement for lodgement. However the date of effect for objections lodged more than 28 days from notification will be the date of lodgement, unless special circumstances are met. See Child Support objection received out of time - Extension of time and special circumstance applications.

Note: the extra 12 days for postage is not added to the timeframe if the applicant received notification of the decision via CSAonline.

If the objection has not been received within the 28/90 calendar day timeframe, consider whether an extension of time is appropriate or whether special circumstances exist if the objection relates to care.

9

Offensive or FDV content + Read more ...

For information on offensive content see Open exchange of information for Child Support customers, Exchanging information Step 4.

If the objection contains offensive content, the objection may be considered to be 'improperly made’.

Note: if any written correspondence contains threats of harm (implied or overt), escalate this immediately to your Team Leader. See Security Incident Management for more information. Consider the possible relevance of the content.

Sometimes, while content in photos may be indicative of FDV, they are provided in the context of either:

  • a ground that underpins the parent’s application, or
  • as evidence as to why the parent didn’t apply earlier and why they think any decision in their favour should be backdated

However, even if the material is potentially relevant and should not be refused nor redacted, it may be appropriate to urge a customer to reframe their content if the Service Officer thinks it is drafted in a manner intended to be malicious or could have unintended consequences.

An example of content that should be exchanged due to relevancy is:

  • Evidence supporting an objection to a care decision, which supports a parent’s claim to withhold care of a child to protect them from harm. That is, where an ‘actual care in special circumstances’ decision may be appropriate.
  • Photos from websites and screen shots of text messages between an alleged sex worker and a putative client indicating services and costs. Even in a case where it is claimed FDV exists, this information may be relevant in the context of a Reason 8 COA application as to that parent’s income and earning capacity.
  • An applicant’s claim that they need additional child support to meet the costs of ongoing counselling for a child who experienced abuse when the child was in the care of the other parent.

If it is considered that an objection may contain offensive of FDV content, Objection Officers must consult Program Advice before making any decision about whether the application should be treated as not being properly made. This will ensure the policy is being applied consistently and as intended. To consult Program Advice:

  • email CS Program Advice Requests with a subject line of ‘OBJ POSSIBLE OFFENSIVE CONTENT’ or OBJ POSSIBLE FDV CONTENT outlining the potential:
    • offensive content, or
    • FDV content. FDV content decisions are made with 48 hours of recognition of the possible prejudice that could be caused by any extended delay
  • once the feedback from Program Advice has been received, document the decision, citing Program Advice's feedback in a new notepad in the Objection Maintenance window

Do not exchange documents containing offensive content with the other party, see Open exchange of information for Child Support customers.

If Program Advice support the view that the application contains offensive or FDV content, the objection is declared to be ‘improperly made’ on the basis of offensive content. A decision to not accept an application because it was not made in the manner specified by the Child Support Registrar is not reviewable. There are no objection rights.

To finalise the objection see Make decision and finalise Child Support objection, Finalise objection Step 6.

Locking customers during the objection process

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Locking + Read more ...

Customers should be locked as per the Customer Management Approach (CMA) process.

This means Objections Officers would generally lock a customer unless they are already locked to another specialist area i.e. Personalised Services, non-compliance, litigation etc.

  • Lock the applicant when allocated an objection
  • Lock the respondent after initial contact with the applicant, unless the objection is not going to proceed, for example it is withdrawn.
    • Locking a customer means the majority of intrays will route directly to the Objections Officer
    • Always remember to lock first, then route the intrays as required
    • If the customer is locked to a Service Officer in any other non-specialist area i.e. Mainstream Customer Services (MCS) or Specialised Assessment Services (SAS), lock the customer to your position in Cuba and route the intrays back to the Service Officer the customer was locked to
    • If the customer is not locked to a Service Officer, lock the customer to your position in Cuba and route the intrays to the Cuba position below

Intray Position for CMA activities:

  • Office: National Office
  • Stream/Team: MC SMCN NATIONAL MCS
  • Position: A - MCS REFERRALS

Grounds for objection unclear or objection likely to be disallowed

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Grounds for the objection + Read more

To consider the objection, it is important to understand the customer’s concerns. If the customer’s grounds are unclear, contact the customer to clarify their reasons for wanting the original decision reviewed. See Contact with Child Support customers.

An objection decision must still be made if the customer does not provide grounds for their objection or if further information cannot be obtained.

2

Objection likely to be disallowed + Read more

Where there is no reasonable basis to conclude that the original decision may be wrong, contact the customer to manage their expectations and to seek further information about the reasons for their objection.

This approach should only be taken where the original decision does not involve the application of discretion and there is no reason to doubt the facts upon which the decision was made. For example, where a customer objects to the use of an ATO issued taxable income in the child support assessment.

See Contact customer regarding Child Support objection.
