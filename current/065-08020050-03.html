Receipt of advice of settlement of a HEAS debt

Step

Action

1

Advice of settlement received + Read more ...

  • The Home Equity Access Scheme National team is responsible for assessment and administration of all HEAS cases
  • Customers can initiate settlement of their HEAS loan via their Centrelink online account. The online transactions create work items that allocate to the Home Equity Access Scheme National team
  • If a customer contacts by phone or attends a service centre with settlement advice, Service Officers must use Fast Note – select Auto text, use Complex Assessment > Request for CAO action > CAO Home Equity Access Scheme > and annotate the settlement request details
  • If the customer has provided a signed, written request confirming they would like to withdraw from the HEAS and settle their debt, staff can accept this as a formal request.

Note: if the customer is a Member of a Couple (MOC), the partner must sign the request too.

If the customer and/or their partner have not provided an acceptable written request, issue the customer with a Home Equity Access Scheme variation form SA497 to formalise their request to settle.

The Resources page has a link to the SA497 form.

2

Home Equity Access Scheme National team action + Read more ...

  • Contact the customer to confirm the settlement
  • If a formal request to settle has not been provided, tell the customer and send a Home Equity Access Scheme variation form (SA497) for completion. The Resources page has a link to the form
  • If the security property is an assessable real estate asset and has been sold:
    • update the sale price on the Real Estate Details (RE) screen with the sale value
    • tell the customer that they will be sent a letter with proposed settlement amounts, and they do not need to make early repayments to have the caveat removed (this will avoid double repayments and refunds). The caveat is removed on the date of settlement, in exchange for the amount due on settlement
  • If the customer is still in receipt of fortnightly loan payments, stop the HEAS from the last date of effect (DOE) for the current payment period:
    • go to the Loan Details (HEAS) screen
    • action as C
    • do not insert a new date
  • For online requests, check to see if any documents are required. For example, sale contract if the No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG) applies, and/or loan documents

Create a HEAS Settlement DOC using Fast Note – select Auto text > use Older Australians > Home Equity Access Scheme > HEAS Settlement, do not finalise it.

Keep the DOC open to:

  • annotate throughout the settlement process, and
  • later for the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) to complete once the loan is settled and records have been updated to reflect the zero balance

Record settlement details in the DOC:

  • the property has been sold
  • the loan is to be repaid without the sale of property
  • someone is representing the customer at settlement, include the details (if provided)
  • the proposed settlement date (if provided)

Once the above is complete, the HEAS Settlement DOC allocates to a CAO to process.

3

Assess if the No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG) applies + Read more ...

Home Equity Access Scheme National CAOs assess the NNEG.

General rules:

  • If the customer has intentionally increased encumbrances, misrepresented their circumstances or committed fraud, even if the adjusted real estate value is lower than the loan balance owing at the time of settlement:
    • the NNEG will not apply, and
    • the full amount of the debt can be recovered
  • If the NNEG applies and the adjusted real estate value is less than the loan balance owing at the time of settlement, then anything owing above the adjusted real estate value cannot be recovered
  • If the adjusted real estate value is more than the loan balance owing, the NNEG will not reduce the amount payable and the full amount of the debt can be recovered

4

Assessment of the adjusted real estate value + Read more ...

At this stage CAOs must calculate the ‘adjusted real estate value’.

The adjusted real estate value is the current market value minus any non- HEAS encumbrances or loans, and can be determined as below:

Market Value

  • If the settlement is following the sale of the security property, the market value will generally be the sale price
    • The sale price can be accepted if the CAO considers it reasonable
    • If the No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG) is to apply or could potentially apply, request a copy of the contract of sale to support any decision made
  • If the proposed settlement is by way of funds not from the sale of the security property, the market value can be:
    • The market value from the last valuation of the security property held on Real Estate and Business Summary (REBS) if it was obtained within the last 3 months, or
    • The customer’s estimated value if determined by the CAO to be reasonable
  • If either party does not agree to the value being the last recorded value, then the market value of the secured property is as determined by an accredited valuer via an on-site valuation
    • Follow the HEAS appeal and review guidelines. See Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) > Resources > Appeal codes for valid codes to include in the Internal Review/Explanation Script
    • Where a valuation has been appealed, alert the Property and Home Equity Access Scheme Legal team to place the tasking request (see Step 9) on hold until the review is finalised
    • Get an up to date onsite valuation of the security property
  • For security properties owned by a trust or company
    • The CAO must determine the value of the ‘specified property’ the trust or company guaranteed for the loan

Encumbrances

  • For any pre-existing encumbrances registered against the security property when the loan was granted
    • get the most recent loan statements available
  • Check to see if there were any additional borrowings on pre-existing loans and request details of any loan variation contracts, if available
  • Do not include any deductions from the HEAS debt
  • If there is any indication of any additional borrowings taken out against the security property
    • Request a title search to check for additional encumbrances or charges that may have been raised
    • Request a copy of any related loan contracts and loan statements

See the Resources page for examples of adjusted value calculation for No Negative Equity Guarantee.

5

Update the HEAS screen and HEAS Settlement DOC + Read more ...

Once it has been decided if the No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG) applies or not, update the HEAS Details (HEAS) screen.

Adjusted Real Estate Value

  • After the adjusted value has been determined, enter the amount in the Adjusted Real Estate Value field on the Home Equity Access Scheme Details (HEAS) screen

NNEG Ineligible Reason

  • If NNEG does not apply:
    • and the reason is the customer has intentionally increased encumbrances, and the full amount of the debt is to be recovered , key LRL (ability to recovery loan limited) in the NNEG Ineligible Reason field
    • and the reason is the customer misrepresented their circumstances, or committed fraud and the full amount of the debt is to be recovered, key MCF (customer misrepresented circumstances) in the NNEG Ineligible Reason field

Annotate full details of the adjusted value calculation and NNEG assessment on the HEAS Settlement DOC including:

  • the adjusted Real Estate Value – if applicable, or
  • the NNEG Ineligible Reason = LRL or MCF and how this decision was made, and
  • the evidence or documentation used to make the decision. Staff must attach the documents to the customers record using Document Tools in Customer First

6

Calculate the HEAS settlement sum + Read more ...

  • If the No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG) applies, the settlement sum is the Adjusted Real Estate Value
  • If the NNEG does not apply:
    • confirm the customer’s relationship status. This is to determine if a single customer has a deceased partner, that may also have a recoverable loan
    • confirm if the caveat/charge registration costs were added to the loan, and are paid. For example, if a loan was recently granted the registration costs may not have been added to the loan balance. Get these details to form part of the settlement sum
    • use the HEAS Future Settlement calculator on the Resources page
    • go to the Loan Details (HEAS) screen, enter the last Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) in the Pension Payday field
    • and there has been a repayment since the last EPED, enter the repayment amount as a negative figure next to the next Pension Payday field on the spreadsheet
    • the amount required to settle is the amount owing at the EPED prior to the date of settlement

Update the Settlement Fast Note with:

  • the total settlement amount owing and if partnered, the combined total settlement amount owing
  • a breakdown of the total settlement amount owing and if partnered, a breakdown of both settlement amounts
  • the date the settlement amount will be accepted to being the day before the next Entitlement Period End date (EPED), and repeat the same for the settlement amount owing for the following EPED

Send a Withdrawal Tasking Request and include:

  • the total settlement amount owing and if partnered, the combined total settlement amount owing
  • details of secured property
  • the date the settlement amount can be accepted to, and repeat the same for the total settlement amount owing for the following EPED
  • details of who the legal provider is required to contact for settlement of the loan

7

Issue a settlement letter + Read more ...

CAO issues a settlement letter to the customer with the settlement loan payout figure for the next 3 fortnights.

The settlement letter:

  • allows the CAO to manually add settlement projection amounts and dates of acceptance into the letter.
  • allows the CAO to manually enter the reason why the No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG) may not apply, and
  • provides repayment options such as BPAY and AusPost Billpay

CAO issues a reference number to the customer and BPAY details for a direct payment and request to remove the caveat/charge. (The Agency covers transaction costs.)

The settlement letter is issued from Home Equity Access Scheme letters (HEASL) in Process Direct, and includes:

  • the date the customer has advised they wish to settle the loan
  • loan ID
  • all addresses of property/ies involved in securing the loan
  • whether the NNEG will be applied to the loan
  • any reduction in the amount to be repaid because of the NNEG
  • the initial payout figure and when it will be accepted
  • the following 2 fortnight’s payout figures and the dates they will be accepted to
  • that there will be final costs associated with the settlement
  • that interest will continue to accrue until the loan is paid in full
  • appeal rights
  • what the customer needs to tell us

8

Issue a tasking request + Read more ...

Once a settlement date and settlement amount has been determined, issue a tasking request via email to the Property and Home Equity Access Scheme Legal team.

Include the below details about the settlement:

  • The property details including the caveat or charge number
  • The total settlement amount (both customer and partner if applicable)
  • Details about if the No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG) applies or not
  • Contact details of the:
    • Customer
    • their solicitor, or
    • other representative
  • Details of the proposed settlement date
  • Attach to the email a copy of the scanned title search obtained by the legal provider at grant
  • The title will be on the customer’s record. See the HEAS CAVEAT and Costs Note for the document ID number

The Legal Services Branch will instruct contracted solicitors to withdraw the caveat or charge in exchange for repayment of the outstanding loan balance.

The email from Property and Home Equity Access Scheme Legal, must be deleted from the HEAS mailbox.

Annotate the Settlement DOC with the referral details. Include:

  • a breakdown of the settlement amount (both customer and partner if applicable) covering the total amount
  • the Legal Explanation (LEX) number from Legal Services. Note: staff must attach a document with the email that includes the LEX number to the customers record
  • the individual components:
    • principal
    • interest, and
    • costs (if any)

9

Receipt of settlement cheque from contracted solicitors + Read more ...

Coding of the Adjusted Real Estate Value is required for all settlements regardless if the No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG) applies or not, and is done twice:

  • first when the settlement letter is sent, and
  • again after settlement funds have been received and banked

The following steps ensure the most recent loan balance is used in the calculation of the correct NNEG amount for settlement.

Update the Adjusted Real Estate Value as follows:

  • Remove the earlier coded Adjusted Real Estate Value, accept the changes, and finalise the activity
  • Recode the Adjusted Real Estate Value, accept the changes, and finalise the activity

For settlements where the NNEG does not apply:

  • Go to Repayment of Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) loans, and follow the process for general receipting and coding

For settlements where the NNEG does apply:

  • Use the last date on the Loan Details (HEAS) screen
  • Code the settlement costs to Costs, and accept the change. Do not make any other adjustments
  • Allocation of settlement funds will be in order of:
    • Costs
    • Interest, and
    • Principal
  • Code the receipts and accept the change
  • Zero any amount left owing and accept the change
  • Finalise the assessment

10

Finalise settlement + Read more ...

After all monies have been receipted and coded, finalise the settlement on the customer’s record.

  • Update details of the receipts in the HEAS Settlement DOC and close the DOC
  • Remove any details about the HEAS from the property description on REBS
  • If the security property is an assessable asset and settlement was:
    • not by sale of the property, remove the HEAS loan amount from the Mortgage/Loans field
    • by sale of the security property, do not remove the Scheme loan amount from the Mortgage/Loans field. Create an open work item using Fast Note. Select Generic > Update> Real estate action required to advise the property has sold (include the REBS property ID number, address and date of sale if known), and request they follow up the sale of real estate
  • Send a Settlement statement to the customer from Home Equity Access Scheme letters (HEASL) in Process Direct

The Settlement statement is sent once payment has been finalised and advises the customer that the loan has been zero’d.

The statement addressed to the customer and/or partner/nominee provides:

  • All transactions for the dates selected
  • Loan payments made to customer
  • Any advance payments made to customer
  • Any legal costs
  • Accrued interest
  • Repayments made
  • Adjustments that have been made
  • Outstanding balance of the loan at the time request for settlement was made
  • Closing balance

Note: the government has extended the sale of home provisions for sales completed from 1 January 2023, to:

  • extend the 12 month asset test exemption period to 24 months. This may be extended to a maximum of 36 months in extenuating circumstances
  • deem the proceeds from the sale of a primary home at the lower deeming rate, currently 0.25%

This applies to:

  • all income support payments
  • Farm Household Allowance
  • ABSTUDY, and
  • Low Income Health Care Card entitlements

See Selling a home for more details.
