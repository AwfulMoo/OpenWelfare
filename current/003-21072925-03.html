Review process

This table outlines the process to follow when a customer requests an explanation of a decision. Do not follow this process if there is an error with an automatic payment.

Step

Action

1

Is the Service Officer trained in the COVID-19 Disaster Payment + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No,
    • complete a cold (unannounced) transfer to 'COVID-19 Disaster Payment' or 'ISP COVID-19 Disaster Payment' in iWS
    • procedure ends here

2

Authenticate customer + Read more ...

Complete Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO). Do not give hints or prompts that help the customer answer the questions.

Was the customer authenticated?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No,
    • No details can be given/acted upon
    • Procedure ends here

3

Request for an explanation of a claim outcome + Read more ...

Discuss the claim outcome with the customer using:

  • if an online claim - the online claim details
  • if a phone claim - the relevant COVID-19 Disaster Pymt Verbal Claim Fast Note/ISP-COVID-19 Disaster Pymt VERBL CLM Fast Note
  • any:
    • COVID-19 Disaster Pymt/ISP-COVID-19 Disaster Pymt Granted or Rejected Fast Note
    • other DOCs on the customer's record relating to their claim

Is the customer satisfied after the discussion?

  • Yes,
    • create a DOC on record outlining the conversation with the applicant
    • procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Applicant requests an explanation of the decision + Read more ...

If the applicant does not understand the decision or does not agree with the decision after a discussion about a claim outcome (other reasons), they can request an explanation which will be provided by a Suitably Skilled Officer:

  • open Customer Record and search for the record
  • while in the customer's CRN, launch the Script Selector using the link at top left of screen
  • key 'Review' in the Search field, then run the Internal Review/Explanation script to record the request for an explanation
  • Create new or select existing case screen:
    • select Add new explanation or review
    • in the Date Client Queried Decision field, enter the date the customer queried the decision
    • select Next
  • Select Path screen, select:
    • I am registering a new request for explanation
    • Next
  • Customer Circumstances screen select:
    • ‘No’ to ‘Do any of the following apply to this customer?’
    • Next
  • Administrative Details screen, enter details as follows:
    • Source of Request: select the correct option from the drop down
    • Date of Decision: enter date the claim was finalised
    • Decision Maker’s Logon: enter logon of original claim decision maker
    • Payment Type the Decision has been made against: select Ex-gratia One Off Payment
    • Disaster ID: check the Payment Details (PD) screen for this code. See Event codes and workflows on Resources in Coding COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims
    • Process Type: select the appropriate option from the drop down
    • Which Office/Team made the Decision? Regardless of who made the original decision select for:
      -Income support payment customers - COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims: CVD
      -COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims: TEP
    • Date Decision Queried: should pre-populate from the Date of Decision field
    • Reason for Contact: Select the appropriate option from the drop down
    • Special Case Indicator: leave blank
    • Select Next
  • Original Decision: screen, select:
    • Either Rate or Rejection
    • Next
  • Reason for Decision: screen, select:
    • Disaster qualification not met
    • Next
  • Reason for Explanation: screen:
    • record the applicant’s reason for request in free text field
    • select Next
  • Transfer to relevant SME team or continue Explanation screen, select:
    • Yes, if the Suitably Skilled Officer is recording the explanation and ready to finalise
      -go to Step 5
    • No, if the Service Officer is referring request for explanation to a Suitably Skilled Officer
      -To which site do you want to transfer the request:
      Income support payment customers - COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims: CVD
      COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims: TEP
      -Add Keyword: 'COVDPSME'. This must be added to make sure these work items allocate to a Service Officer with the skills to complete these reviews.
      -Select Next
      -procedure ends here

5

Completing the request for explanation of a claim outcome + Read more ...

Open Customer Record, and search for record.

To understand and explain the decision, use:

  • if an online claim - the online claim details
  • if a phone claim - the relevant COVID-19 Disaster Pymt Verbal Claim Fast Note/ISP-COVID-19 Disaster Pymt VERBL CLM Fast Note
  • any:
    • COVID-19 Disaster Pymt/ISP-COVID-19 Disaster Pymt Granted or Rejected Fast Note
    • other DOCs on the customer's record relating to their claim

If the applicant is not on the phone, make 2 genuine attempts to contact them to explain the claim outcome decision.

Is contact successful (or is the applicant on the phone)?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 9

6

Contact successful (or applicant is on the phone) + Read more ...

If there are concerns about the validity of information provided by the customer, do not change a decision:

  • tell the customer their claim cannot proceed as eligibility cannot be established
  • do not tell the customer their claim has been escalated because of possible fraud
  • do not use the term 'fraud'
  • submit a Fast Fraud tip-off form

Has the applicant provided new information that changes the initial decision, or was the initial decision incorrect?

  • Yes,
    • run the Internal Review/Explanation script to finalise the explanation
    • in the explanation of decision DOC staff must document:
      -background information
      -why the decision was changed
      -the additional information used to change the decision
      -if the customer has been contacted
      -relevant periods and event codes
    • Legislative / Policy References screen: enter Section 32B of the Financial Framework (Supplementary Powers) Act 1997
    • Reference source: select the appropriate option from the drop down
    • go to Step 7
  • No,
    • the initial decision is correct and cannot be changed
    • the customer has no further review or appeal rights
    • run the Internal Review/Explanation script to finalise the explanation
    • in the explanation of decision DOC staff must document:
      -background information
      -why the decision was not changed
      -if the customer has been contacted
      -relevant periods and event codes
      -decision made under Section 32B of the Financial Framework (Supplementary Powers) Act 1997
    • Legislative / Policy References screen: enter Section 32B of the Financial Framework (Supplementary Powers) Act 1997
    • Reference source: select the appropriate option from the drop down
    • procedure ends here

7

Review outcome successful + Read more ...

The outcome of the review has changed the initial decision.

If the applicant has been:

  • Underpaid and needs a top-up in payment, for example paid $450 but should have been paid $750, go to Step 8
  • Initially rejected - the Suitably Skilled Officer assessing the review must implement the outcome. See:
    • Assessing COVID-19 Disaster Payment Claims, or
    • Assessing income support payment customers – COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims, and
    • Coding COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims
    • Procedure ends here

8

Customer was underpaid + Read more ...

If the outcome of the review needs the customer to receive a top-up as they were underpaid, contact Technical Peer Support who will complete a payment query template.

Procedure ends here.

9

If unable to contact the customer + Read more ...

  • Run the Internal Review/Explanation script to finalise the explanation. The script will issue a Q449 letter advising that contact was unsuccessful and if they would like to talk about the decision they can contact Services Australia
  • When preparing the Q449 letter:
    • select Program, and enter 'COVID-19 Disaster Payment' in Program Name
    • the options are based on details captured by the script. There should be no need to change these values, but check and update if necessary by typing DATA in the Command: field
      – for example, the letter may show the incorrect phone number. Typing DATA in the Command: field will allow staff to update it to 180 22 66 in the GEN.ENQ.PHONE.INIT.OFF field
