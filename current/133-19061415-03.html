Centrelink online account

This tab outlines the menu options that customers have. Their options will depend on the payments they get and their circumstances. Staff training simulations and online guides are available for some services.

Homepage

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Centrelink online account homepage

This page shows when a customer selects their linked Centrelink service from their myGov account. This can include:

  • an Acknowledgment of Country message
  • tasks they need to complete
  • a link to View Payment History
  • a link to view Next reporting date
  • a link to Make a claim or view claim status
  • Favourite services
    Four services show as default, in the following priority order and based on a customer's circumstances:
    • Upload documents (always shows)
    • Money you owe
    • Manage advance payments (only if the customer meets eligibility. If they don’t, either Manage Deductions or My profile will show in its place)
    • Manage deductions
    • My profile

Note: They can customise 3 of these. They cannot remove Upload documents.

  • main desktop menu options across the top of the page (this doesn’t show when using Centrelink online account on a mobile internet browser):
    • My details
    • My tasks
    • Payments and claims
    • My family
    • Income and assets
    • Documents and appointments
      Customers can also select the MENU icon at the top left of the page
  • Edit theme where customers can:
    • choose from either Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander artwork to customise their homepage by selecting the Edit theme button
    • change their homepage theme back to the default

At the bottom of the page, there is an Acknowledgement of Country and links to:

  • Legal Notices which include the conditions of use
  • Your Rights
  • Contact us

When the customer finishes using their Centrelink online account, they need to select Return to myGov to go back to their myGov account.

During times of increased demand on the system, the Centrelink online account homepage switches to a simplified view. This prevents tasks from showing on the homepage and instead provides a link to the My tasks page. For more information, go to Table 14 > My tasks.

Report employment income

Eligible customers use this service to report employment income for a specified reporting period to get their income support payment. The reporting task shows on the Centrelink online account homepage from the customer’s reporting due date.

Customers can use the Earnings Worksheet (SU505) to keep track of any pay events and hours worked they need to report on their next reporting due date.

Online guides

  • Centrelink online account help - Report employment income
  • Centrelink online account help - Report employment income - Carers

For more help, see Centrelink digital support products.

Customers can:

  • confirm, reject or match pre-filled Single Touch Payroll (STP) employer name and Australian Business Number (ABN) or Withholding Payer Number (WPN)
  • check, confirm or change pre-filled STP employment income data
  • add additional STP employer pay events that are not pre-filled yet
  • view the pre-filled employment cessation data reported by their STP employer when they cease work. Mutual Obligation customers may be required to provide details when the cessation reason is dismissal or voluntary
  • answer additional questions when unused leave and redundancy payments have been included with cessation details, such as the amount of days the leave covers or the average weekly wage
  • answer additional questions when unused leave on termination income is included in their pay event, but no employment cessation details are included in the STP data
  • report employment income they or their partner are paid from non-STP employers
  • advise they have met their mutual obligation, activity test or participation requirements
  • view and edit previous reports for up to 6 past reports submitted. Note: customers must contact the agency to make changes to confirmed STP income and provide evidence of the change
  • view details of their Working Credit, Income Bank or Work Bonus balance on their Report employment income receipt page
  • Principal carer customers can advise of a reasonable excuse why they are unable to temporarily unable to meet their mutual obligation requirements if they:
    • have a benefit status of CUR or SUS
    • have an approved activity code of PTW, and
    • are in receipt of one of these payments:
      JobSeeker Payment
      Parenting Payment Single
      Youth Allowance (job seeker)
      Special Benefit (who have mutual obligations requirements)
  • Note: they can select from a list of excuse reasons. If none of the listed excuse reasons apply to the customer, they can select Other and enter a reason. They are advised to contact the agency so the agency can assess them for an exemption and complete their report.

What are push notifications?

Push notifications are short messages from a mobile app that tell a user there is something that needs to be done. These notifications:

  • show on a mobile device's lock screen
  • can be turned off in the device settings if the customer does not want to get them

In Express Plus, push notifications tell customers about a task they need to complete.

If the customer selects the notification on their mobile device, it opens the app, asks them to sign in and then takes them straight to the task. This helps customers complete tasks quickly and efficiently.

A Service Officer can find out if a customer has been sent a push notification:

  • In Customer First:
    • select the 'Outbound correspondence' guided procedure
    • enter the customer's CRN in the recipient CRN field and select Search
    • the customer's correspondence shows
    • if the customer has been sent a push notification it shows under 'Distribution channel'
  • In Process Direct:
    • enter 'Corro' in the Super Key field
    • the Correspondence History screen shows
    • if the customer has been sent a Push Notification, it shows under 'Channel type'

Multiple entries for the same push notification may show in customer First and Process Direct. Each time a customer downloads the app, it creates an installation ID. Customers may install the app many times. For example if they use multiple devices or upgrade their phone.

A push notification is sent to any active installation ID. Customers do not get multiple notifications on the same device.
