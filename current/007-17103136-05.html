Shared Care - Partner check examples

Table 1

Example

Description

1

Peggy is partnered to Bart.

Peggy lodges a Child Care Subsidy claim for child Josh. Josh is coded as in Peggy’s Care on the CHC screen, however a care percentage has not been recorded for Peggy or Peggy's partner Bart on the FSC screen.

A blank or null percentage is treated as 100% by the system.

This means that Child Care Subsidy is granted because Peggy has Josh coded In Customer Care.

2

Martin lodges a Child Care Subsidy claim for child Harry. Harry is coded as In Customer Care on the CHC screen for Martin and partner, Sally.

A care percentage of 0% has been recorded on the FSC screen for Martin and no care percentage (blank) has been recoded for Sally.

Child Care Subsidy will grant because Sally has In Customer Care recorded and no percentage (Blank coding is treated as 100%).

3

Marley lodges a Child Care Subsidy claim for Mark's (Marley's current partner) child Kelly. Kelly is coded as In Customer Care on the CHC screen.

Mark does not have Kelly coded as In Customer Care on the CHC screen. Marley has less than 14% coded on the FSC screen. No care percentage (blank) has been coded for Mark.

Child Care Subsidy will be rejected as Marley has under 14% care recorded.

Example

CCS claim is allocated but cannot be actioned due to a started PPL claim on the record.

In this situation the CCS only trained Service Officer would:

  • 'unassign' the CCS claim work item using the reason “Capability Adjusted”
  • Add the NV_CLK_NCL_FCC_PPL skill tag

This means the CCS claim work item will have both the NV_CLK_NCL_CCS_GEN and NV_CLK_NCL_FCC_PPL skill tags when it is returned to the allocation pool.

This ensures that when the claim is allocated next time, it will go to a Service Officer skilled in processing both claims.
