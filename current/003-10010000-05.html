FAQs - urgent payments

Circumstances

Answer

Payment less than accrued entitlement

The available amount in the script is less than the customer's accrued entitlement: why is this and can I override the amount?

When the system assesses the available amount that can be paid as an urgent payment, it withholds the total value of deductions such as debt repayments, tax, Centrepay, immediate payment indicator etc. This leaves enough funds to meet these commitments. The available amount determined by the system cannot be overridden.

For Income Managed customers, see Urgent payment requests from Income Management customers

Service centre collection timeframe

When a customer is advised to collect a payment from the service centre, how long will it take from when the customer presents at reception until they walk out the door with the payment?

The time the customer will be at the service centre waiting to collect their payment may vary depending on operational requirements of the service centre on that day.

In general, when the customer presents at reception/to the Customer Liaison Officer (CLO), a walk-in will be booked. At the walk-in, the customer must be authenticated and PORO established and in the case of 'conditional approvals’, documentation will be verified. The payment will then be processed and issued to the customer, with instructions on how the payment is to be withdrawn.

The customer should be advised to attend the service centre during the preferred walk-in times as recorded in Office Locator. Tell the customer that they must attend the service centre on that day to receive their payment. If they do not attend on the day the payment is approved they will need to reapply.

Payment not recovered in full from the payday

Will a debt be raised if an urgent payment was issued, but due to declared earnings on the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED), it cannot be recovered in full from the payday?

If an immediate payment deduction cannot be recovered in full from the first entitlement period, it will cascade into the next until it has been repaid in full. This could be an urgent payment, anticipated payment or hardship advance etc.
Should the customer's payments be cancelled or suspended before the immediate payment deduction is recovered, the system will wait for the customer's payment to restart and then will recover.

If it is unlikely the customer's payment will restart soon, the immediate payment deduction is to be manually converted into a debt.

Two working days for payment into bank account

Why does it take 2 working days for a pension or Parenting Payment Partnered payment to be made into the customer's bank account via direct credit?

Due to the large number of payments issued via the Reserve Bank to the various banks, credit unions and building societies across Australia on a daily basis, the banking industry believes that its members do not and would not have the capacity to process and clear all of the payments within one working day.

To manage this capacity and customer expectations, it was negotiated that payments issued by the Newstart System (NSS) would be delivered in one working day. All other income support payments would be delivered in 2 and all other non-income support payments would be delivered in 3 working days.

'No more EBT's' or 'face to face only'

What happens if a DOA DOC advises 'No more EBTs' or 'Customer must only be seen face to face'?

To support local arrangements, when a customer is managed by the service centre (for example, some 'at risk' customers are managed by a local community team and/or senior staff members), the Smart Centre Service Officer should not assess the request for an urgent payment. They should instead refer the customer to their local service centre for assessment.

For more about local arrangements, check the service centre's Office Locator page. See Urgent payment request to be assessed at the service centre or by a processing team for more about when not to refer a customer to Smart Centre telephony.

An appropriate Display On Access (DOA) DOC would state that when/if the customer makes a request for an urgent payment, they are to be referred to the service centre for assessment. It will state the contact details of the staff member to refer the customer to. The DOC is also to have a reasonable expiry date or review date depending on the customer's circumstances.

An inappropriate DOA DOC will ban the customer from applying for an urgent payment, EBT card or service without reason or contact person and have an unreasonable review period. In these cases:

  • Consider the request based on the merit of:
    • information and circumstances provided by the customer, and
    • the historical information recorded on their record (for example, previous urgent payment requests and relevant risk factors)
  • Give feedback via the Staff Feedback Tool to the author of the DOC to let them know the text is inappropriate and that the DOC must be expired or replaced with appropriate text.

Payment pickup from Medicare

Can a customer pick up an EBT Card at Medicare?

Medicare services and other agencies within Services Australia will not have access to EBT cards and the extension of urgent assistance in Smart Centres will not change this arrangement.

Never refer a customer to a Medicare service or Centrelink to collect a payment.

Payment granted inappropriately

What should a staff member do if they identify that a customer has been granted an urgent payment inappropriately?

Unlike most decisions that staff make, urgent payment decisions can be very grey. For example, what one staff member believes could be foreseen, another may not. In addition, due to the sensitive nature of these types of decisions, all staff should support the decision made by the decision maker.

If a customer is unhappy with the decision, follow the reconsideration of decision process.

However, if a staff member is not complying with the guidelines, or not following Operational Blueprint procedures, feedback should be given to the staff member via the Staff Feedback Tool.

Payment and Service Finder

What if Payment and Service Finder is out of date?

If a service needs some information to be updated, use the edit link in Payment and Service Finder to update and send the appropriate information. This will be updated within 24 hours.

Social worker role

The Payment and Service Finder should be used to locate alternative assistance for customers presenting in financial hardship. Consider consultation with a social worker if:

  • the circumstances are complex, and
  • more detailed knowledge of resources is required

Repeated requests for urgent payments may indicate that a customer is having difficulty managing their finances or there are other ongoing problems. In such cases consider referring the customer to an Authorised Weekly Payment Officer (AWPO) or an external financial counselling or budgeting support service.

Consider a referral to a social worker for customers presenting with highly complex needs in line with the social work priority groups. These groups include:

  • customers at risk of suicide or self-harm
  • young people without adequate support
  • customers experiencing family and domestic violence including assessment for a Crisis Payment due to family and domestic violence

Refer the customer to a social worker if the customer displays suicidal or possible self-harming behaviours, see Customers talking about suicide or self-harm.

Consider any social worker recommendations recorded on the customer record, however the delegation to grant or reject urgent payments remains with the Service Officer and they must ensure they are following the correct procedures in approving any urgent payment. Approval to grant the payment must still be provided by:

  • Technical Peer Support (TPS)
  • a Team Leader, or
  • a staff member above Team Leader delegation (this does not include social workers)

See Customer disagrees with an urgent payment decision for how the original decision will be reconsidered based on new information provided.

To reduce the risk of the customer having unrealistic expectations when referring the customer to a social worker, be clear with the customer about why they are being referred and the role of the social worker. A social worker cannot change the urgent payment decision, but they can offer support and assistance for customers presenting with highly complex needs.

If a social work referral is appropriate:

  • refer the customer to the Social Work Line via a warm handoff
  • if unable to contact a social worker, complete a Fast Note. Select Auto Text. Use Emergency > Urgent Payment > Referral to SWO due to UP assessment

For more help, see:

  • Social work service referral
  • Referral to external support services

2

TPS, Team Leader and local Leadership Team role

Approval required - more than 2 urgent payments

Service Officers making a decision to grant a third or subsequent urgent payment to a customer within 12 months must seek approval from Technical Peer Support (TPS), Team Leader or a staff member above this level before recording the decision to approve the payment via the Immediate Payment requests script.

TPS and Team Leaders (or above) will make the decision to approve urgent payment if satisfied the customer is experiencing severe financial hardship and the documentary or third party verbal evidence provided supports the customer's claim of extraordinary circumstances.

Other responsibilities

The TPS/Team Leader/local Leadership Team is responsible for any reconsiderations of the original request, when the requested at the time the customer is advised the outcome of their initial request. It must take into consideration:

  • The customer's recent urgent payments, Change Payday and Welfare Referral History (within 12 months)
  • The circumstance of the customer's request
  • Whether all alternative assistance options have been explored and why they were not appropriate
  • Whether the request is due to exceptional and unforeseen circumstances
  • Whether the amount is the minimum required to meet the customer's immediate need
  • How the customer will meet their regular ongoing expenses like rent, food, utilities on their next payday
  • Whether the circumstances have been verified (if required), and
  • Whether the customer is struggling to meet their regular ongoing expenses or appear to be in a crisis cycle. Note: consider a weekly payment assessment with an Authorised Weekly Payment Officer (AWPO) or a referral to a Financial Management Program Support Service for financial counselling or budgeting assistance
  • A social work referral could be considered if aligned with the social work priority groups. These groups include customers at risk of suicide or self-harm, young people without adequate support or customers experiencing family and domestic violence

Note: all reconsiderations where the decision is going to be changed must have the circumstances verified.

If a customer requests a reconsideration of the outcome of a previous request on the same day, an APS4 can do this as long as they are not the original decision maker and are trained in urgent payment assessments.

The Immediate Payment requests script records the approving staff member's User ID on the customer's record as part of the urgent payment decision.

Minimum amount is established with verification

Customer asks for $40 for medication.

Customer asks for $200 fuel and food to travel to a job interview. The customer lives in Canberra and needs to travel to Sydney for the interview.

Food is not an additional cost in this circumstance and is not included.

Fuel costs to travel from Canberra to Sydney are estimated to be around $60. This amount is approved, as it meets the customer's immediate hardship need.

Examples - urgent payment requests

Table 4

Item

Description

1

Customer requests urgent payment for accommodation

The customer advises they have been living rough on a friend's couch. When they returned home today, their personal belongings were in a box at the front door and they had been locked out.

The customer phoned around and found accommodation at a caravan park but they must pay for the stay up front. Their next payday is in 3 days. The customer has no urgent payment history.

Explore other assistance. For example, emergency accommodation, staying with a friend until their payday. A referral to a social worker may also be appropriate if aligned with social work services priority groups. These groups include customers at risk of suicide or self-harm, young people without adequate support and customers experiencing family and domestic violence.

If all other assistance is exhausted, it may be appropriate to consider approving the request on the basis that

  • there was no other assistance available
  • the customer had no tenancy rights sleeping rough
  • they had tried to resolve the issue, and
  • they have no urgent payment history

The minimum amount required to satisfy the customer's immediate hardship need should be approved.

2

Customer requests a CrP NHE and is not eligible

The customer claims Crisis Payment National Health Emergency as a result of needing to quarantine/self-isolate or care for an immediate family member or member of their household. They are deemed ineligible.

Gather information to find out why funds are required during isolation. An urgent payment is not to be made for regular expenses if those expenses were foreseen. For example, a customer advises funds are required for food during isolation. Investigate further to determine what occurred with funds that would normally be spent on food.

Explore other assistance. Only consider an urgent payment when there are additional exceptional and unforeseen circumstances.
