Impact when customer relocates outside an Income Management area

Portions of this process are completed by the Income Management Policy Helpdesk only

Item

Description

1

Customer moves out of a declared Income Management area during their engagement period and advises at the time of their move + Read more ...

Child Protection and Supporting People at Risk measures

Income Management will commence unless a Revocation of Income Management is lodged by the referring authority.

Disengaged Youth, Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients, Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients and Voluntary measures

As customers must reside in a declared Income Management area to commence under these measures, Income Management will not commence.

2

Customer moves out of a declared Income Management area after commencing Income Management

Child Protection and Supporting People at Risk measurers + Read more ...

Income Management will continue unless the following occurs:

  • a Revocation of Income Management is lodged by the referring authority
  • the Notice to Commence Income Management expires, or
  • the customer is no longer eligible. For example, they no longer receive a qualifying payment, or have
  • an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes

If a customer's payment is cancelled and subsequently restored when living in a non-declared area, Income Management is reinstated if the notice is current. For more information, see Effect on Income Management when payment is restored or re-granted.

Disengaged Youth and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient measures + Read more ...

Income Management will automatically end 13 weeks from the date the customer moved outside the Northern Territory.

If a customer's payment is cancelled and subsequently restored and less than 13 weeks has lapsed since the customer moved outside the Northern Territory, Income Management will be automatically reinstated.

Note: customers may still apply for an exemption from Income Management when living in a non-declared area. For more information, see Customers requesting an exemptions from Income Management.

Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients- social worker assessed + Read more ...

Income Management will continue unless the following occurs:

  • the Vulnerable Notice expires
  • the notice is terminated by a social worker, or
  • the customer is no longer eligible. For example, they no longer receive a qualifying payment or have an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes

An activity will be generated to alert the social worker when a customer moves out of a declared Income Management area. A review will then be conducted to determine if the customer is still vulnerable. If necessary, a referral to a social worker in the customer's new location will be arranged.

If a customer's payment is cancelled and subsequently restored, Income Management is reinstated if the Vulnerable Notice is current. For more information, see Effect on Income Management when payment is restored or re-granted.

Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients - Youth + Read more ...

Income Management will continue for the remainder of the minimum 12 month period, unless one of the following occurs:

  • the customer is granted an exclusion from Income Management
  • the customer no longer has one of the automatic vulnerable triggers and requests to be exited from Income Management, or
  • the customer loses eligibility for Income Management. For example, they no longer receive a qualifying payment or have an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes

At the end of their 12 month period, the customer's eligibility will be reviewed according to the trigger in place when they moved out of the Income Management area.

Voluntary Income Management + Read more ...

Income Management will continue unless one of the following occurs:

  • the mandatory 13 week period has not been completed. Note: customers whose payments are restored will automatically have VIM reinstated if they have not completed the 13 weeks
  • the customer ends their VIM Agreement (after completing the mandatory 13 week period). Note: customers cannot enter into a new VIM agreement at a later date unless they move to a declared Income Management area and meet the eligibility criteria, or
  • the agency terminates the Voluntary Income Management Agreement if certain events occur. For example, the customer appoints an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes

3

Customer moves out of a declared Income Management area after commencing Income Management and address is updated retrospectively to a date before their engagement period + Read more ...

Child Protection and Supporting People at Risk measures

Income Management will continue until the customer is no longer eligible for the applicable measure.

For more information, see Customer moves out of a declared Income Management area after commencing Income Management.

Disengaged Youth and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients measures

Income Management will automatically end and any residual funds will be disbursed. See Disbursement interview for Disengaged Youth (DEY) and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients (LTWPR) Income Management.

Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients

Customers are not eligible for Income Management and must be manually updated as ineligible. Level 2 Security Resource is required to update a customer's eligibility.

To request the customer be coded as ineligible for Income Management:

Conduct a Disbursement interview with the customer, do not run the Disbursements workflow. See Disbursement interview for Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients (VWPR) Income Management.

Complete any processing as required such as:

  • issuing one off payments
  • closure of BasicsCard
  • set up Centrepay deductions
  • changing to Rent Deduction Scheme payments

Clearly DOC the Disbursement interview, including any additional action required.

  • Send the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via webform to the Income Management Policy helpdesk requesting the customer be made ineligible for Income Management. See the Resources page for a link to the helpdesk webform
  • The helpdesk will update the customer's eligibility and complete the Disbursements workflow as per information recorded in the Disbursement interview DOC

Launch the Vulnerable IM Assessment workflow.

Complete the Decision screen:

  • Is the customer ineligible for Vulnerable Income Management? Select Yes
  • Select Incorrectly Identified As Eligible from the dropdown menu
  • Continue workflow

Complete disbursement interview coding as per Disbursement interview DOC recorded on the customer's record.

On the Income Management Summary screen:

  • Tick off any associated Work Item
  • Select Continue then Finalise

Voluntary Income Management

As there is no engagement period for Voluntary Income Management, the change of address has no impact. See Customer moves out of a declared Income Management after commencing Income Management.

However, if the retrospective change of address results in the customer not residing in a declared Income Management area at the time they entered into a VIM Agreement they do not meet the criteria for VIM. Once all address updates have been completed, Service Officers must refer the CRN to the local Income Management Contact Officer or the Income Management Policy Helpdesk requesting the agreement be revoked.

See the Resources page for a link to the Income Management Contact Officers (IMCO) homepage and the Income Management Policy helpdesk webform.

4

Customer moves out of a declared Income Management area after commencing Income Management and address is updated retrospectively to a date within their engagement period + Read more ...

Child Protection, Supporting People at Risk and Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient measures

Income Management will continue until the customer is no longer eligible for the applicable measure.

For more information, see Customer moves out of a declared Income Management area after commencing Income Management.

Disengaged Youth and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients measures

Income Management will automatically end 13 weeks from the date the customer moved outside the Northern Territory.

For more detailed information see Customer moves out of a declared Income Management area after commencing Income Management.

5

Income Management customers moves into a Cashless Debit Card area + Read more ...

Outside the Northern Territory, Income Management is not available in Cashless Debit Card (CDC) areas.

When a customer is income managed under one of the following measures and moves permanently into a Cashless Debit Card area outside the Northern Territory, Income Management will continue until it reaches its natural end:

  • Child Protection Income Management
  • Disengaged Youth
  • Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients
  • Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients

Income Management will end when:

  • the relevant Notice expires or is revoked, or
  • an exemption or exclusion is granted (where eligible), or
  • the customer's portability period ends (where applicable), or
  • the customer no longer meets the eligibility criteria

When a Voluntary Income Management customer moves into a CDC area outside the Northern Territory, the agency will manually end Income Management as the customer no longer meets the eligibility requirements.

When Income Management ends in a CDC area outside the Northern Territory, eligible customers will be placed on the Cashless Debit Card, or they can volunteer to participate in the trial.

Note: Income Management and CDC programs run concurrently in the Northern Territory. Income Managed customers in/or who move into the Northern Territory, can choose to stay on IM or request to transition to the CDC. For More information, see: Income Management (IM) to CDC Transition in the Northern Territory.
