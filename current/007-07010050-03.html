3

Check for a Progress of claim DOC + Read more ...

For all enquiries relating to unprocessed claims, first check the Document List (DL) screen in the customer's record for a Progress of claim DOC.

This DOC provides information on where the claim is in the assessment process, and if any additional information has been requested from the customer.

The Resources page contains examples of a Progress of claim DOC.

Is a Progress of claim DOC available?

  • Yes,
    • and there is no started claim activity on Activity List (AL), go to Step 4
    • and additional information has been requested from the customer, go to Step 6
    • and additional information has not been requested from the customer:
      discuss timeframes around processing of claims, go to Step 8, or
      if the customer meets the definition of a family in crisis or is experiencing financial hardship, go to Step 10
  • No, check for a claim:
    • For online claim, check Activity List (AL) screen for a submitted online claim (OLC) View claim details using Search Online Claims under Online claims heading in Customer First Workspace. For help, see Viewing and processing online and assisted customer claims
    • For paper claim, check AL screen for a scanned paper claim. Check Document Tools in Customer First for a scanned paper claim or documentation. Paper forms will be allocated to an appropriately skilled officer. If the customer meets the definition of a family in crisis or is experiencing financial hardship, go to Step 6
    • If the claim is located, but there is no started claim activity on the Activity List (AL), go to Step 4
    • If the customer has an FTB status of CAN-OIS or CAN-INC in the current or previous financial year, determine if FTB can be restored without the need for a new claim. See Restoration of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for more information
    • If the claim cannot be located after thoroughly checking the customer's record, go to Step 5

4

Regenerate or re-index Families claim for allocation

In Customer First for FTB or FTB/PPL claims + Read more ...

Online claim

Regenerate or reindex the claim as required, then go to Step 6.

Paper claim

Manually re-index the claim:

  • In Customer First, make sure the correct System is displaying
  • Go to Name Search (IN) screen and select Continue and key:
    • customer surname
    • customer first name or initial
    • date of birth (DOB)
  • In Claims details section, key:
    • relevant service reason, e.g. 'FTB', 'DAP'
    • activity 'NCL'
    • same source as original claim, e.g. 'FA100', 'FA080'
    • same date of receipt (DOR) as original claim
  • Select Continue
  • Select the customer from the list and select Continue
  • On Person Details (PED) screen key 'Y' to confirm and select Continue
  • Go to the AL screen
    • In Notes section record why claim was re-indexed
    • Select Continue
  • An additional keyword may need to be added to the claim activity:
    • If specialist skills are required:
      Shared Care - SHARED
      FCC International (FIC) - FICREQ
      Integrated Care - ICLOCKW
    • INDOCPPL must be added if the claim is indexed as FTB only and it is identified they are also claiming PPL
    • INDOCFTB must be added when only PPL or DAP has been claimed and the customer (or their partner) is FTB current (CUR or CZR) for other children
  • Go to Step 6

In Process Direct for standalone PPL and DAP claims + Read more ...

  • Claims are allocated for processing by using the work item (claim ID) of the claim activity
  • If the claim has a status of In Process or Submission failed and no started activity on the AL screen, no further action is required
  • Go to Step 6

5

No claim on customer's record + Read more ...

If no online claim or scanned paper claim can be located, ask the customer what method they used to submit the claim (online or paper form).

If the customer mailed a paper claim form, advise them it has not yet been received:

  • For customers who meet the definition of a family in crisis or experiencing financial hardship, see Helping customers in crisis or financial hardship claim family assistance
  • For all other customers, advise the option to complete an online claim for family assistance and/or PPL, and explain Services Australia cannot process a claim until it is received. Procedure ends here

If the customer says they claimed online, ask if they have a receipt number or claim ID.

Can the customer provide a receipt number or claim ID?

  • Yes, escalate the issue to ICT via existing escalation protocols:
    • document the record with the date the customer advised they lodged the claim, the claim ID/receipt number and other relevant information
    • advise the customer to complete a further online claim for family assistance and/or PPL, to minimise any further delay
  • No,
    • document the record with the date the customer advised they lodged the claim and other relevant information
    • advise the customer to complete a further online claim for family assistance and/or PPL, to minimise any further delay.

Procedure ends here.

6

Checking for outstanding information/documentation + Read more ...

Before a claim can be actioned, all outstanding documentation or information needs to be provided by the customer.

Outstanding information/documentation can include:

  • Proof of birth (POB) - check Documents icon in Process Direct or Document Tools in Customer First. Post birth claims can only be finalised if POB has been provided, or is already coded on the customer's record. See Helping customers provide proof of a child's birth for family assistance, Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Paid Parental Leave scheme claims. Note: depending on where the POB is lodged, it can take up to 14 days for the scanned image to be available on Documents icon/Document Tools
  • Tax File Number (TFN) - (if applicable) required for most Families claims to be assessed. Update customer's record if TFN provided. See Provision of Tax File Number (TFN) for family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payments
  • Bank account details, update customer's record if current details are provided. See Changing payment destination for assistance if required
  • For claims involving shared care or change of care, check Document Tools for any evidence of care arrangements. See:
    • Notification and assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • Change of care for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • For claims involving shared care or change of care where no evidence has been provided, advise the customer of what kind of evidence can be provided
  • Outstanding Member of a Couple Assessment - check if referral to MOC processing staff is required
  • For FTB instalment claims, check that the estimate provided within the claim still reflects the customer’s circumstances and is reasonable
    • The estimate provided can be viewed using the Review Claim function
    • If the customer commenced the claim between 1 April and 30 June of the previous financial year, the system will have requested the customer’s (and partner’s if applicable) income details for that year and the next financial year
    • If the customer commenced the claim prior to 1 April, they will not have been requested to provide a new financial year estimate. Where the customer is contacting on or after 1 April, staff must obtain a new financial year estimate and include it in the Progress of Claim doc
    • If the customer indicates that the estimate has changed, update the Progress of Claim doc with the new information

If a scanned image required for a claim (e.g. claim form) is not appearing on Documents icon/Document Tools within a reasonable time from lodgement, this needs to be followed up. There are different processes depending on how and where the customer lodged the document. See Digital image not located on Centrelink customer record.

Note: Online Claims or scanned images may not be accessible during system outage.

Is further information/documentation still outstanding?

  • Yes,
    • Advise the customer their claim cannot be finalised until the information is provided. Obtain the information verbally where possible. The information can be provided via Upload documents
    • Annotate the Progress DOC (if available) with the details of information provided to the customer or create a Progress DOC for this purpose. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

No further information documentation outstanding + Read more ...

    • Go to the Activity List (AL) screen
    • Select the new claim activity and from the Selection Options select (H) Hold / resubmit for action on a future date and select Continue
    • Delete the date from the Resubmit Date and select Continue

Within timeframes

  • Advise the customer of claim processing timeframes if applicable go to Step 8

System outage

  • If a system outage is preventing the claim from being processed, go to Step 9

Family crisis/hardship

  • If the customer meets the definition of a family in crisis or experiencing financial hardship, go to Step 10

8

Timeframes for claim outcome + Read more ...

If the customer is enquiring about how long their claim will take before they have an outcome, and they do not meet the definition of a family in crisis or experiencing financial hardship, use the following script:

'Thank you for lodging your Family Assistance claim. Please be assured we are processing claims as quickly as possible to manage the high demand across the business. We will let you know the outcome as soon as it is complete. If you have been requested to provide information, it is important that you do so as soon as possible so as not to delay your claim. Thank you for your patience.'

Note: from 10 June 2019, FTB top-ups, supplements, lump sum claim amounts and instalment arrears can be used to recover any family assistance, social security, student assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payment debts. This will occur even if the customer has a current payment arrangement.

If the customer advises during the call they are a family in crisis or experiencing financial hardship, go to Step 10.

9

System outage + Read more ...

If a system outage is preventing determination on whether the claim is actionable, advise the customer:

'We are currently experiencing technical difficulties which are preventing us from accessing the information required to process your claim at this time. We will assess your claim as soon as possible. Once your claim is processed, if eligible you will be paid for the period you are entitled to. We will notify you of the outcome of your claim in writing once it has been finalised. Thank you for your patience.'

Does the customer meet the definition of a family in crisis or experiencing financial hardship?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, procedure ends here

10

Family in crisis or financial hardship + Read more ...

Note: from 10 June 2019, FTB top-ups, supplements, lump sum claim amounts and instalment arrears can be used to recover any family assistance, social security, student assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payment debts. This will occur even if the customer has a current payment arrangement.

Where exceptional and/or unforeseen circumstances apply which may cause the customer to suffer severe financial hardship, customers can request to be temporarily excluded from debt recovery prior to any money being taken from their FTB arrears, lump sum or top-up payments.

For customers experiencing financial hardship, the claim can be escalated for priority processing, go to Step 11

For customers who meet the definition of family in crisis , see Helping a customer in crisis or financial hardship claim family assistance. Procedure ends here.

11

Experiencing financial hardship - families claim escalation check + Read more ...

Check the Activity List (AL) screen.

Is there already an Urgent Claim Processing Req ACTDOC open on the AL screen requesting hardship processing of this claim?

  • Yes,
    • Tell the customer:
      'Your claim has been referred for urgent processing. Once your claim has been finalised, we will send you a letter advising the outcome'
    • Procedure ends here.
  • No, go to Step 12

12

Experiencing financial hardship - families claim escalation + Read more ...

Use the Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Families > Claims > Families Claim Escalation - Hardship to send a request for urgent processing.

Make sure the correct service reason is selected (e.g. FTB, PPL).Include all relevant details in the escalation request, for example:

  • Shared care or change of care details
  • Interpreter language if interpreter is required

Keywords ACTFAO and URGENT are automatically added. Do not need add manually.

  • For shared care claims, add keyword 'SHARED'
  • For change of care claims, add keyword 'COCARE'

For claims where both parties have indicated a Disagreement over care arrangements:

A request for urgent activity finalisation for Disagreement of care arrangements cannot be made before the request for evidence due date.

Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Families > Enquiry > Disagree Care Urgent Esc.

For claims where Disputed Care provisions apply:

A request for urgent activity finalisation for Disputed care arrangements cannot be made before the request for evidence due date.

Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Families > Enquiry > Disputed Care Urgent Esc.

Advise the customer:

'Your claim has been referred for urgent processing. Once your claim has been finalised, we will send you a letter advising the outcome'.
