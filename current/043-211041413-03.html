3

Check Claimant Category + Read more ...

To be eligible for intrastate (KIFG) assistance:

  • The goods must be eligible intrastate goods under 2.13 of the Ministerial Directions
  • The claimant must have incurred and paid the cost of shipping the goods
  • Assistance must not have been paid to any other person for the shipment of the goods

If the goods are eligible, the Claimant Category can be any one of the following:

  • Unspecified
  • Manufacturing
  • Mining
  • Agriculture
  • Forestry
  • Fishing
  • Sportsperson or Entertainer

The claimant category will be added during the registration process if the claimant has been approved:

  • as a Southbound Manufacturer or Miner, or
  • in the Agriculture, Forestry or Fishing industries,

If the Claimant Category is Unspecified, the claimant will be Ineligible Southbound. The system will automatically reject all southbound shipments.

Eligible shipments from King Island or the Furneaux Group to Tasmania may need a work-around applied if the record is Ineligible Southbound.

Change the route code to prevent the automatic rejection of the line. For shipments from:

  • King Island or the Furneaux Group to Tasmania – change from KT to TK
  • Furneaux Group to Tasmania – change from FT to TF

Claim submitted within 6 months of shipment + Read more ...

Claimants have 6 months from the date of shipment to lodge a completed claim, unless they have an approved Extension of Time (EOT). Once all required documents are received, the claim is considered complete.

  • manufacturing or mining on King Island or the Furneaux Group and;

  • agriculture, forestry or fishing industry on King Island or the Furneaux Group and;

  • liquids transported in tanks
  • goods that stow at 1.1m3/tonne or less

  • TBUS spreadsheet :

Freight Load + Read more ...

The rate of assistance is based on whether goods are shipped in

  • Full Container Load (FCL), or
  • Less than a Container Load (LCL)

Shipping units must be efficiently utilised to be considered full.

For the purpose of assessing a TFES claim, a full 6 meter container is considered to hold an average of 21 tonnes or 30 cubic meters.

If a claimant utilises at least 80% of the container with their own, eligible product, it qualifies as a full load.

Check Claimant Notes for information relating to freight load.

Review the supporting documents to determine if the goods are FCL, consider:

  • the shipping unit number
  • if the container or trailer is full
  • the weight indicates that the container or trailer used is full
  • the volume indicates that the container or trailer used is full
  • the price paid indicates that the claimant has been charged for a full container or trailer

Consider accepted transport practices and the shipping characteristics of the goods. E.G. a container full of polystyrene is very light in weight, but high in volume. If unsure, call the freight company to confirm if the container was fully utilised.

If the goods were transported in a:

  • full container or trailer load, go to Step 16
  • less than a container or trailer load, go to Step 17

Obtain missing information + Read more ...

Contact the claimant and/or freight company to ask for the missing information noted while assessing the claim.

Contact by phone, email or both.

  • If contacting a freight company, ask them to give the information via email within 2 business days
  • If contacting the claimant, by phone, email or both:
    • make 2 genuine attempt. If first contact is unsuccessful, make a second attempt with 48 hours
    • if possible, leave a message with a name and number for them to contact and a position email address
    • tell the claimant they have 21 days to provide the information. If the information is not received, the claim/claim line will be rejected.

  • On the claim Comments in myHSI and in the Comments/Details section of the activity in the TFES Assessor Registry key:
  • details of the request for information
  • date of the request, and
  • claimant contact person

  • Change the status of the activity in the TFES Assessor tool to ‘awaiting claimant response’

Note: some claim lines may be rejected and some may be accepted.

Has the claimant provided all missing information within 21 days?

  • Yes, claim check is complete, for:
    • manual claims see Table 3
    • online claims, see Table 4
  • No, reject the claim:
    • to reject a claim line on a manual claim, see Step 4 in Table 3
    • to reject a claim on a manual claim, see Step 9 in Table 3
    • to reject a claim line on an online claim, see Step 2 in Table 4
    • to reject an online claim, see Step 7 in Table 4

  • change the activity status to awaiting QA officer

The QA officer will select the claim from the TFES Assessor tool and carry out the QA process.

If the QA check is correct, the QA officer:

  • selects Approve and accepts the claim in myHSI
  • keys the QA outcome in the Claim Comments in myHSI
  • prints the Note to Claimant (if applicable)
  • posts payment advice letter with Note to Claimant
  • enters the claim details in TFES Batch spreadsheet
  • Completes the activity in the TFES Assessor tool, and
  • sends QA feedback email

If quality check shows claim is incorrect, the QA officer:

  • selects Not Approve in myHSI
  • keys details of QA outcome in Claim Comments in myHSI
  • changes the activity in the TFES Assessor tool to QA back to assessor
  • sends QA feedback email with details of error/s

If QA is returned with errors, the Service Officer:

  • corrects error/s, and
  • corrects the Note to Claimant if applicable and go back to Step 1
