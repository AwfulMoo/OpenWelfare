Basic eligibility criteria for DRA

For staff processing Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) claims only.

Table 3: This table describes the steps to assess basic eligibility for DRA for those affected by Tropical Cyclone Debbie - March 2017. Service Officers should not process a claim unless formal training has been completed. Note: if a claimant is not eligible for DRA claims are still to be recorded and the outcome noted as rejected. Claims cannot be rejected until formal approval has been provided. Staff will be notified via an Operational Message once they are able to reject payments.

Step

Action

1

Disaster determined and basic eligibility + Read more ...

The relevant Minister has made a determination under the Social Security Act that a major disaster has occurred for the purposes of DRA for people affected by the Tropical Cyclone Debbie - March 2017. To confirm that DRA has been activated for this event, check the Operational Instructions.

Is the claimant at least 16 years old, and an Australian resident or the holder of a specified visa for DRA?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, another social security payment may be payable to meet the needs of the claimant. See Rejecting a claim for DRA for Tropical Cyclone Debbie - March 2017 table

2

Age of claimant + Read more ...

Is the claimant under 22 years of age?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Claimant under 22 years of age + Read more ...

  • Is the claimant:
    • independent of all other persons (except for person's partner), or
    • a parent, and
    • received, or will receive, income of more than $6,403 in the 2016/2017?
  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, another social security payment may be payable to meet the needs of the claimant. See Rejecting a claim for DRA for Tropical Cyclone Debbie - March 2017 table

4

Declared Local Government Areas (LGA) + Read more ...

Office Locator is to be used to identify which LGA a claimant resides in, or earns, derives or receives income by working.

Service Officers are to search Office Locator by suburb name only. Searches are not to be completed on postcodes.

The declaration covers the affected LGAs of:

  • Byron
  • Gold Coast
  • Lismore
  • Logan
  • Mackay
  • Rockhampton
  • Scenic Rim
  • Tweed
  • Whitsunday

Does the claimant either reside in, earn, derive or receive income by working or living in an affected LGA?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in the income eligibility criteria for DRA.
  • No, claimants in other areas of the state affected by this disaster can test their eligibility for this payment providing they have been adversely affected by the disaster and meet the eligibility criteria. Select the 'follow up' option and refer to the Regional Office Code EMG. Procedure ends here

Income eligibility criteria for DRA

For staff processing Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) claims only.

Table 4: This table describes the steps to assess income eligibility for DRA for those affected by Tropical Cyclone Debbie - March 2017. Service Officers should not process a claim unless formal training has been completed. Note: if a claimant is not eligible for DRA claims are still to be recorded and the outcome noted as rejected. Claims cannot be rejected until formal approval has been provided. Staff will be notified via an Operational Message once they are able to reject payments.

Step

Action

1

Loss of income + Read more ...

Has the claimant suffered a loss of income as a direct result of the event?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, another social security payment may be payable to meet the needs of the claimant. See Rejecting a claim for DRA for Tropical Cyclone Debbie - March 2017 table

2

Other income support payment or precluding payment received + Read more ...

Did, or will, the claimant receive another income support payment, pension or one of the precluding payments outlined in the Operational Instructions in the 8 week period prior to their DRA eligibility start date and with an ongoing entitlement into the DRA claiming period?

  • Yes, the claimant is not eligible for DRA, other assistance may be available to meet the needs of the claimant. See Rejecting a claim for DRA for Tropical Cyclone Debbie - March 2017 table
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Income cut-off rates + Read more ...

The disaster affected income cannot exceed the relevant cut-off for the first fortnight of the period in which they are claiming. The cut-off rates are outlined in the Operational Instructions and are subject to indexation.

Calculate the disaster affected income for the first fortnight of the period for which they are claiming.

Is the claimant's disaster affected income below the relevant cut-off point?

  • Yes, the claimant is eligible for DRA. Run the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow.
    To determine the fortnightly rate of payment, go to Step 4
  • No, the claimant does not meet the qualification for the DRA, however they may reapply if their income reduces, otherwise another social security payment may be payable to meet the needs of the claimant. See Rejecting a claim for DRA for Tropical Cyclone Debbie - March 2017 table

4

Rate of payment + Read more ...

To determine the fortnightly payment:

  • Calculate the projected income for the qualifying fortnight
  • Calculate the disaster affected income for the same qualifying fortnight

Note: excluded income is not to be included when calculating the disaster affected income or projected income.

Is the difference between the projected income and the disaster affected income less than the maximum applicable rate?

  • Yes, the rate of payment is equal to the difference
  • No, the rate of payment is equal to the maximum applicable rate

Rejecting a claim for DRA for Tropical Cyclone Debbie - March 2017

Table 6: This table describes the process for rejecting an unsuccessful DRA claim for Tropical Cyclone Debbie - March 2017. Note: claims are being held for rejection until 24 April 2017, with the exception of claims not in a declared local government area (LGA). Claims not in a declared LGA cannot be rejected until 1 May 2017.

Step

Action

1

Important information + Read more ...

Prior to rejecting a claim, two genuine attempts must be made to contact the claimant to:

  • provide the claimant with a full explanation of why their claim is unsuccessful and determine if the claimant can qualify under any other criteria,
  • advise that a letter will be sent outlining the review and appeal process, and
  • consider if the claimant may be eligible to receive other social security payments and/or if a referral to a social worker or external organisation is required. The Resources page contains a link to the Payment and Service Finder

2

Approval to reject claim + Read more ...

Note: new claims taken over the phone that do not meet eligibility requirements can be rejected at that contact with the claimant (provided that approval has been given to commence claim rejections)

Has approval been given to commence claim rejections?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, place claim on hold (until relevant date) with the appropriate rejection keyword, see the Resources page for Minimum Activity Management Standards. Once permission to reject is given, and the held claim becomes due, go to Step 3

3

Check contacts + Read more ...

Is this the first attempt to contact regarding rejection?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

First contact attempt + Read more ...

Was the attempt successful?

  • Yes,
    • Complete the new claim activity as ‘rejected’
    • Document the claimant’s record accordingly
    • Ensure rejection letter has been sent
  • No,
    • Document the claimant’s record accordingly
    • Add the CON1 keyword to the new claim work item and place work item on hold until the following day (work item will be removed from the inbox and reallocate once off hold)

Procedure ends here

5

Second contact attempt + Read more ...

Successful/unsuccessful (the outcome is the same regardless of whether the second attempt is successful or unsuccessful)

  • Complete the new claim activity as rejected
  • Document the claimant’s record accordingly
  • Ensure rejection letter has been sent
