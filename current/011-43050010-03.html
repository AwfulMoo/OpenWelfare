Medicare patient claim adjustment for Tier 1

Table 1: This table describes the process for a Medicare patient claim adjustment in a Medicare Service Centre.

Step

Action

1

Enter patient information + Read more ...

Key NHSI, card number, patient's IRN or first initial and press [Enter].

2

Check RSN code + Read more ...

Check the service lines to be adjusted for RSN codes. If the reason code is:

  • between 887-899, go to Step 3
  • any other reason code, go to Step 6

3

Overpayment + Read more ...

Are the services a total overpayment?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Pay claim + Read more ...

Re-key the services on a claims processing screen and pay the claim.

Go to Step 9.

5

Adjustment claim + Read more ...

Use the [PF12] key to cancel the screen, complete the Adjustment on an adjustment Patient Claim form (available on the Resources page) and forward to the relevant area in the capital city office.

Note: one of the following must be attached to the form:

  • a copy of the original archived documents
  • letter, fax or duplicate account
  • account and receipt from the practice on letterhead stationery
  • a completed VG4 form with the amended service details

6

Confirm payment + Read more ...

Press [PF10] to confirm the cheque has been PRES or the Credit EFTPOS/EFT payment field displays a PAID status.

7

Services to be adjusted + Read more ...

Over key transaction NHSI with NHRI and press [Enter].

Select the services to be adjusted by adding OP in the field and press [Enter].

8

Incorrect information + Read more ...

Over key the incorrect information with the correct details.

Note: check all information on screen including the referral/requesting information is correct before paying the adjustment.

9

Batch documents + Read more ...

Place all documents in batched work for the day.

Medicare patient claim adjustment for Tier 2

Table 2: This table describes the process for a Medicare patient claim adjustment referred to Tier 2.

Step

Action

1

Patient information + Read more ...

Key NHSI,card number,patient's IRN or first initial and press [Enter].

2

Check reason code + Read more ...

Check the service lines to be adjusted for RSN codes. If the reason code is

  • between 887-899, go to Step 3
  • any other reason code, go to Step 6

3

Overpayment + Read more ...

Are the services a total overpayment?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Pay claim + Read more ...

Re-key the services on a claims processing screen and pay the claim.

Go to Step 9.

5

Check history + Read more ...

Check history for an 'L' indicator in the TX field.

Place the cursor under the indicator and [PF1] to locate the previous adjustment.

If the total number of adjustments performed for this claim is:

  • three or more, go to Step 6
  • less than three, go to Step 7

6

Adjustment claim + Read more ...

Use the [PF12] key to cancel the screen, complete the Adjustment on an adjustment Patient Claim form and email or fax the claim to the Medicare claims helpdesk, Policy Services Australia Canberra team for approval.

  • a copy of the original archived documents
  • letter, fax or duplicate account
  • account and receipt from the practice on letterhead stationery
  • a completed VG4 form with the amended service details

The Resources page contains a link to the claim form.

Batch documents + Read more ...

Place all documents in batched work for the day.

Patient claim LDA completed - second claim LDA required

LDA has been performed and additional LDA required

Table 3: This table describes the actions Service Officers must take where a patient claim LDA has been performed and another patient claim LDA is required.

Tier

Action

1

Service Centres + Read more ...

Service Officers in Service Centres must not adjust a previously adjusted service. An Adjustment on an adjustment for Patient Claims form must be completed and sent with either:

  • a copy of the original archived documents
  • a letter, fax or duplicate account
  • account and receipt from the practice on letterhead stationery
  • a completed VG4 form

Forward to MPS Assessing.

The Resources page contains a link to the claim form.

Note: only an initial adjustment may be processed in a Medicare Service Centre.

The Patient claim adjustment - Tier 1 table contains further information.

2

Capital city offices + Read more ...

Service Officers in capital city officers must initially only action a total of 3 adjustments on a claim, made up of the following:

  • the original adjustment (if not processed in the Medicare Service Centre); and/or
  • a further 2 adjustments on a claim where a previous LDA has already been performed

Where a 4th or further adjustment is required, an Adjustment on an adjustment for Patient Claims form must be completed and faxed or emailed with either:

  • a copy of the original archived documents
  • a letter, fax or duplicate account
  • account and receipt from the practice on letterhead stationery
  • a completed VG4 form

to the Medicare Claims Helpdesk and Support, Services Australia Canberra for processing approval.

Note: these claims can be sent via fax or email. They must not be sent via internal mail.

The Patient claim adjustment - Tier 2 table contains further information.
