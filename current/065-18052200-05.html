Supporting evidence

Table 1: This table includes the evidence needed from the aged care service to support their request to change an event outside the 70 day and the 2 year limit. Note: the 70 day limit is only applicable for care recipients whose departure date from the home care service occurred on or after 1 September 2021.

If the variation is:

Evidence needed

an Entry
  • The original Aged Care Entry Record (ACER) or the original claim containing the entry date
  • The Care Recipient Agreement containing the entry date
  • Any invoice containing the date the care recipient commenced paying aged care fees

hospital or respite leave
  • The original claim containing the hospital leave
  • Documentation from the hospital containing the hospital’s admission date and discharge dates
  • In the case of respite leave, any invoice containing the date the care recipient commenced paying aged care fees

oxygen
  • The Application for the Oxygen and/or Enteral Feeding Supplement form originally submitted to Services Australia
  • Written certification from a medical practitioner stating the care recipient has a continual need for the administration of oxygen

Enteral feeding
  • The Application for the Oxygen and/or Enteral Feeding Supplement form originally submitted to Services Australia, and
  • A copy of the written certification from a medical practitioner stating the care recipient has a medical need for the enteral feeding, and
  • Written certification from a medical practitioner or dietician stating that the dietary formula is a nutritionally complete formula

location details (viability supplement details)
  • Documentation stating the care recipient address with a reference date

Commonwealth (CW) unspent amount
  • The original claim containing the CW unspent amount

For any changes greater than 70 days after departure (home care only)
  • Evidence of the original claim/documentation submitted to Services Australia prior to the 70 day limit, or similar evidence that supports the claim of defective administration

Investigation process

Service Officer must undertake an investigation as part of their process to accept or reject an event outside the 70 day and/or 2 year limit.

Investigation may include:

  • checking for duplicate delegate activities pending approval
  • reviewing care recipients’ profiles in the aged care payment systems
  • checking previous event submission
  • checking claims and payment statements
  • checking system notes (that is, care recipient notes, service notes, provider notes etc.)
  • checking previous correspondence from the aged care service
  • checking the relevant Aged Care form, for example:
    • Aged Care Entry Records (ACER)
    • Application for the Oxygen and/or Enteral Feeding Supplement
    • Aged Care Home Packages transfer level of care for Aged Care recipients
    • Aged Care Application for the Dementia and Cognition Supplement in Home Care
  • receiving the documentary evidence from the aged care service to support the requests to change an event outside the 70 day and/or 2 year limit
