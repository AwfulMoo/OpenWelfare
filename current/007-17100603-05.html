PPL Income Details (P1INC)

Check the customer's estimate of adjusted taxable income for the relevant financial year. Indicate whether or not the estimate is reasonable, and whether verification is required/provided.

    • Exempt Reportable Fringe Benefits
    • Reportable Super Contributions
    • Total Net Investment Losses
    • Tax Free Pensions/Benefits
    • Foreign Income
    • Tax Exempt Foreign Income

Undertake further investigations and record the appropriate PPL income verification outcome for the customer.

Determine whether the customer has provided the verification documents and whether these are acceptable to meet their PPL income eligibility requirements.

Certain income test determinations cannot be changed on review. Once a person has provided their adjusted taxable income (ATI) and it is decided PPL is payable, this decision should not be overturned if later the person's ATI exceeds the income limit.

Changes to PPL income details must only be processed to rectify incorrect coding by customers and staff

As a consequence, a debt would generally not be raised following updates to the PPL income details. Note: an exception would be if the income determination resulted from the person knowingly making false or misleading representations or providing false or misleading information or documents, the person is taken not to have satisfied the income test. Such debts are recoverable and will be investigated.

Schoolkids Bonus explanation (SKBOVR)

Schoolkids Bonus was abolished from 31 December 2016.

The final instalment of Schoolkids Bonus was for the 30 June 2016 test day.

The final payment of the Schoolkids Bonus Leaver Payment was for the 30 June 2016 bonus test day and covered the 1 January - 30 June 2016 education period. It was payable if a student completed secondary school during that education period. It is not payable if a student completed secondary school on or after 1 July 2016.

Reconciliation or re-reconciliation of FTB Part A for the 2012-13, 2013-14, 2014-15 or 2015-16 financial years may result in a Schoolkids Bonus arrears payment or overpayment.

FTB lump sum grants or recalculations for the same financial years as above may also result in a Schoolkids Bonus Payment or overpayment.

  • View Schoolkids Bonus details
  • Go to the SKBOVR screen

This screen can be used to check the date the Schoolkids was issued, the education level, amount paid, and whether the rate was affected by shared care or a blended family assessment. SKBOV is accessed with the customer in context.

The screen groups test dates based on the child the SKB was paid for.

To view the FTB Schoolkids Bonus explanation for a past bonus test day, expand the dropdown for the relevant child to display a list of all past SKB test dates they have been assessed for.

The amount displayed is an indication only. The Schoolkids Bonus amount displayed will be correct in most cases but should not be completely relied on, particularly if there are children aged 16 - 18 years in the family.

In the event that a manual update to the past period is required a referral will need to be made as only authorised officers have access to edit the information in the dropdown.

To make the appropriate referral submit a Direct to SSO referral web form the Override Contacts SharePoint page.

For authorised Families override contacts follow the procedure below:

To update schoolkids bonus details:

  • Go to the SKBOVR screen
  • Click on the twisty for the relevant child
  • Select the correct Bonus Test Date (BTD)
  • Update the child details
  • Select Save

Examples - recording of care details

Table 6

Example

Description

1

Family Tax Benefit (FTB) lump sum claim

Carrie and partner lodge an FTB lump sum claim, they have not lodged a claim for FTB for the past 5 years. During coding of the claim it is noticed there is no percentage of care recorded for their son James who is in their care 100% of the time.

In this case, care does not need to be coded for James unless there has been a change in care as advised by the customer or another claimant.

2

FTB claim for newborn child

Peter and partner lodge an FTB new claim for their newborn child Sarah. This is the first time Peter has claimed FTB so Sarah is added to the system. As Sarah is in the care of parents only, the FTB Shared Care (FSC) screen must be coded to reflect 100% care.

In this case, the care must be coded even though it is for 100% care.

The intent of this requirement is to have a percentage of care for the child coded at all times. If the parents separate in the future, this means the percentage must be reviewed when completing the separation updates instead of allowing the system to assume care and send incorrect information across to Child Support and causing overpayments.

For the purpose of this instruction it is not a requirement to review and code retrospective information for children who are already on the system. It is to ensure the quality of ongoing data is correct in order to limit the use of incorrect care dates which causes information to ripple across to Child Support creating reassessments.

3

FTB claim for customer and child recently arrived into Australia

Indra is single and has recently arrived in Australia from New Zealand with 5 year old child Samuel, who has always been in Indra's care. Indra completes an FTB claim for Samuel and is eligible for FTB from the date of arrival into Australia.

As Samuel has always been in the care of Indra, the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen is left as the default date being Samuel's date of birth.

The residence screens are updated and Indra is not paid FTB prior to the date of arrival into Australia (due to ineligibility). If required, the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen can be coded to override FTB paying for periods of ineligibility due to residence requirements not met for Indra and Samuel.

Indra is given a Pseudo Deciding Maintenance Action (PDE) providing a 13 week (91 day) timeframe to take action to obtain child support.

Table 8

Example

Description

1

Separation of couple within 13 week Newborn Supplement (NBS) period

Scenario: A couple separates after receipt of Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) and shared care starts within the 13 week NBS period, the other carer is not entitled to NBU but can be eligible for NBS.

Nena and Ben are members of a couple and receive NBU and NBS for their child Joe from Joe's date of birth.

Nena and Ben separate 10 weeks (70 days) after Joe's birth and now share the care of Joe.

Ben claims FTB and the eligibility for NBU/NBS requires manual assessment. Ben is not entitled to NBU as this was paid when Ben and Nena were a couple.

Ben is eligible for NBS but the NBS period is adjusted by the 70 days they were partnered so Ben would be eligible for 21 days of NBS (91 minus 70 days).

The Service Officer must code:

  • NBS Eligible Start Date: and Days: fields to pay Ben NBS for the 21 days
  • NBU Ineligible Reason Code: as 'DIL' to prevent a duplicate payment of NBU
  • Override Reason Code: as 'DIL' and Only affect this customer: as 'Y'

2

Couple become partnered during 13 week NBS period and then separate

Scenario: A couple become partnered after customer has started NBU/NBS and separate after the end of the NBS period. The other carer claims FTB for the child and can be eligible for NBU and NBS for the period not partnered.

Tamara is single and starts to receive NBU and NBS for Billy. Tamara is eligible for 13 weeks (91 days) NBS.

Tamara becomes partnered to Simon after receiving 50 days NBS. Tamara and Simon are a couple for the rest of Tamara's NBS period.

Three weeks later Tamara and Simon separate. Simon claims FTB for Billy due to shared care.

Simon's entitlement to NBS is reduced by the number of days Simon and Tamara were partnered in the NBS period. Simon's NBS partnered period is 41 days so the NBS period is 50 days (91 minus 41 days). Simon is eligible for NBU as Simon was not partnered to Tamara on the day Tamara received NBU and they are not a couple for any day that Simon is eligible for NBS.

The Service Officer must code the:

  • NBS Eligible Start Date: and Days: fields to pay Simon for the 50 days
  • Override Reason Code: as 'DIL' and Only affect this customer: as 'Y'

The above coding will result in the NBU being assessed.

Customer was partnered for part of the partner's PPL period (13 weeks or more)

Scenario: A customer was partnered to a person for part of that person's PPL period for the relevant child. NBU and NBS may be payable to the customer or their new partner if the person had a PPL period of 13 weeks or more, and the customer and the person were partnered for less than 13 weeks of the PPL period.

Peter and Dianna are a couple and Dianna is eligible for PPL for the full 18 weeks (126 days) for their child Hannah.

Dianna has received 66 days of PPL for Hannah when Dianna and Peter separate and Peter now has shared care of Hannah.

Peter's partnered period is 66 days. Peter claims FTB and will be entitled to NBU and his individual NBS period is 25 days (91 minus 66 days).

The Service Officer must code the:

  • NBS Eligible Start Date: and Days: fields
  • Override Reason Code: as 'DIL' and Only affect this customer: as 'Y'

The above coding will result in the NBU being assessed.

4

Customer was partnered for part of the partner's PPL period (less than 13 weeks)

Scenario: A customer was partnered to a person for part of that person's PPL period for the relevant child. NBU and NBS may be payable to the customer or their new partner if:

  • the person has a PPL period of less than 13 weeks, and
  • the customer and person were partnered for part of the PPL period

Naomi and Greg are a couple and Naomi is eligible for PPL for 80 days for their child Billy.

Greg and Naomi separate after Naomi has received 66 days of PPL for Billy and Greg now has shared care of Billy.

Greg's partnered period is 66 days. Greg claims FTB and will be entitled to NBU and Greg's individual NBS period is 25 days (91 minus 66 days).

Service Officer must code the:

  • NBS Eligible Start Date: and Days: fields
  • Override Reason Code: as 'DIL', and
  • Only affect this customer: as 'Y'

The above coding will result in the NBU being assessed.

Examples of effective claims

Table 15

Item

Description

1

Current FTB customer claiming PPL for their child

30 June: Yasmin gives birth to a baby. Yasmin is receiving FTB for 2 older children.

7 July: Yasmin completes an online claim for PPL for the new child. Yasmin takes a photo of the Doctor/Midwife - Proof of Birth declaration on the back page of the FA081 and uploads it with the claim.

10 July: A Service Officer processes Yasmin's PPL claim. In the claim Yasmin has indicated the child is in both Yasmin's and partner Rodney's care. Rodney is the other parent of the new child. Yasmin did not indicate they wanted to add the child for FTB, but the Service Officer adds the child based on the information provided in the PPL claim. This ensures Yasmine receives the correct payment applicable to their circumstances.

2

Using 'Add Newborn' service to provide child's proof of birth

1 May: Paula lodged a pre-claim for PPL and FTB.

10 May: Paula's pre claim is assessed and the payments are pending proof of birth of the baby (PPL/ASS-POB and FTB/ASS-PCB).

1 July: Paula gives birth to baby Lachlan.

8 July: Paula downloads the Centrelink Express Plus application from the App store, opens the Add Newborn workflow, answers the post birth questions, enters the serial number from the form, and uploads a clear, legible image of the Doctor/Midwife - Proof of Birth declaration on the back page of the FA081 to finalise the claim for PPL and FTB.

A MAENROL DOC is recorded on Paula's record to enable Medicare to add Lachlan to the Medicare card, Medicare Safety Net and for eHealth details.

By using the Express Plus Families app, Paula does not need to complete or lodge the rest of the paper FA081 for the claim or Medicare enrolment to be completed.

Note: the above would also apply if Paula had used the Add Newborn workflow from her online services account.

3

Customer affected by a natural disaster

6 April: Jo completes an online pre-birth claim for PPL and FTB.

2 May: Jo gives birth to child Taylor.

26 May: Jo's house is flooded and everything inside is destroyed, including the Newborn Child Declaration (FA081) issued by the hospital.

28 May: Jo calls and states they are in severe hardship and explains they no longer have the form from the hospital and wants to finalise the claim for PPL and FTB.

As Jo has been affected by a natural disaster it would be reasonable for a Service Officer to contact the hospital and confirm the birth of Taylor. It would also be reasonable for the Service Officer to collect the missing information via the Assisted Customer Claim workflow. Once all the missing information has been collected and proof of birth has been confirmed for the newborn child, Jo's payment can be finalised.

Note: if a claim had not already been lodged Jo would need to sign the Families Declaration Form (FDF) or accept the verbal signature declaration for the claim to be effective

4

Remote Indigenous customer

20 April: Helen, an indigenous customer living in a remote community near the Kimberley gives birth to child Russell.

15 May: Helen mails the completed Newborn Child Declaration (FA081) to Services Australia to claim FTB for Russell.

21 May: A Service Officer contacts Helen because they have not completed an online claim or Claim for Paid Parental Leave and Family Assistance (FA100) for the claim to be effective. Helen advises the Service Officer that they do not have access to the internet and due to literacy limitations cannot understand the FA100.

Based on this information it would be reasonable for a Service Officer to complete Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) to collect the additional information from Helen and obtain a verbal signature declaration or issue a Family Declaration Form (FDF) to be signed and returned.
