Notification of death of a member of an allowee couple

Step

Action

1

Advice of the death of a member of a couple received + Read more ...

When a person contacts to advise of the death of a member of a couple where, at the time of death:

  • both were receiving a social security benefit or allowance, or
  • the deceased was receiving a benefit or allowance and the surviving partner was not receiving an income support payment

Staff must offer:

  • A social worker referral in the first instance or at any time the person shows signs of distress during the contact. See Referring customers to a social worker for more information
  • The 'Needing help after someone has died?' (HD001) pamphlet. Send to customer if accepted

Consider and respect the following cultural issues when being advised of the death of an Indigenous customer.

  • The name of the deceased person cannot be mentioned by immediate family members. This means immediate family members may have to ask other relatives/friends to call on their behalf or attend the interview with the notifier, because they cannot say the name of the deceased
  • In this situation, it may also be offensive to the person for the Service Officer to say the name of the deceased person. Service Officers should sensitively ask the person if it is all right for them to use the deceased customer's name. If the person is not comfortable with this, avoid using the deceased customer's name wherever possible. For more information, see Cultural Considerations when someone dies

Other procedures apply where:

  • the surviving partner is not in receipt of a Centrelink income support payment, see Death of a customer and their partner is not in receipt of a Centrelink or Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) income support payment
  • the surviving partner is in receipt of Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP), see Death of a partner of a Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) customer
  • the person who dies is in receipt of PPP, see Death of a customer in receipt of Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP)
  • the surviving partner is in receipt of Partner Allowance, see Death of the partner of a Partner Allowance customer
  • one member of the couple is a pensioner, see Death of a member of a pensioner and allowee couple
  • both members of the couple are pensioners, see Death of both members of a couple
  • one member of the couple is a pensioner and one is an allowee, see Death of a member of a pensioner and allowee couple

2

Income Management + Read more ...

Does the deceased customer's record indicate that they were income managed, as shown by the INM on the benefit status line?

  • Yes, follow process to update record for death of an income managed customer, then return to this procedure and go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Confirm the death of the deceased + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure in the deceased customer's record to determine if death can be confirmed.

Confirmation of death can be accepted where the customer's full name is provided together with at least two of the following:

  • deceased customer's last address in full
  • date of birth and age at death
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • name of partner

Confirmation of death also requires the following information about the informant:

  • name
  • address
  • phone number

Confirm if the person notifying of the death is the executor of the estate. If the informant is not the executor, but knows the executor details, obtain:

  • the name and address of the executor or administrator, and
  • the name of the funeral director or hospital

For more information, see Confirmation of the death of an adult or child.

Nominee arrangements and Power of Attorney (POA) stop at the time of death. Any correspondence, including the payment summary and possible debt recovery action from a deceased estate, will be sent to the:

  • executor, or
  • administrator of an estate

Services Australia's ability to finalise any outstanding matters will be impacted if executor or administrator details are:

  • incorrectly recorded, or
  • not recorded when details have been provided

Where executor details are not known at the time of death notification, the executor details fields in the Notification of Death guided procedure must be left blank.

Note: executor details can be recorded manually on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen if details become known later.

Can informant supply enough information to confirm death?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Death cannot be confirmed at initial contact + Read more ...

Using the Notification of death guided procedure, obtain and record all relevant information from the informant including:

  • their own name, address, contact number and relationship to the deceased
  • executor details, if known
  • name of the funeral director or hospital where the deceased passed away, if known

The guided procedure will not finalise the death action, but will record details on a DOC. This becomes an open work item and will be allocated to a Service Officer with the required skill set to confirm the death.

Note: where multiple notifications are received in relation to the death of the same person do not re-run the guided procedure. Check the record and annotate the original DOC to state details are correct and who advised.

If the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item, use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED.

Where death cannot be confirmed and the customer's whereabouts cannot be established, payment is to be suspended until the death can be confirmed.

  • Select the suspension option in the Notification of death guided procedure, or
  • Manually code the suspension for reason 'OTH' (Other)
  • The suspension date of event should be the unconfirmed date of death or the date paid to plus one (DPT +1) whichever is the later
  • If the guided procedure is not available record a DOC manually and ensure that it meets Online Document (ODR) standards for significant decisions and including what action is required to confirm the death
  • Do not issue a suspension letter. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension

Note: it is imperative that the death is confirmed and actioned as a matter of urgency.

For Service Officers confirming the death, contact the funeral director or the hospital where the deceased passed away. If this is not an option and contact with relatives is proposed, social worker involvement may be necessary.

When death is confirmed, go to Step 5

5

Check for started activities + Read more ...

A death action cannot be processed with activities on the Activity List (AL) screen. The Notification of Death guided procedure will provide a prompt if there is an activity on the AL screen.

Note: if a deceased customer's record was suspended while the death was being confirmed and their death has now been confirmed, check for started activities on the AL screen manually.

Where possible ensure any activities are completed and run the daily driver (DDR).

  • If there is a 'STA' debt activity on the AL screen, create an open work item for region XWA advising them of the death and requesting the debt activity be completed. Use Fast note - select Auto text, use Debts > DMB Raising > Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request. See Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer
  • For other outstanding activities immediately contact the team or Service Officer who needs to complete the outstanding activity – if unsure who to contact, refer to existing escalation channels

Have started activities been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

When it is not possible to complete an activity + Read more ...

If there is an outstanding activity that the Service Officer is unable to complete, the customer's payment should be suspended, if not already suspended

Suspend manually using the reason 'OTH' (Other) pending completion of the activity.

  • The suspension date of event should be the date of death or date paid to plus one (DPT+1), whichever is the later
  • Record a DOC on the customer's record, ensuring that it meets Online Document (ODR) standards for significant decisions and including what action must occur so the death can be actioned
  • As the customer's death has been confirmed, no letter must be sent. It is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension

If an open doc/work item regarding the death notification has not already been created:

  • run the Notification of death guided procedure to generate an open work item for the relevant staff to complete the death action once the outstanding activities have been completed or
  • if the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item. Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED

When activity is completed, go to Step 7.

Check if restoration is needed + Read more ...

Is payment suspended?

    • Ensure no arrears are issued
    • Inhibit the restoration advice, as it is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer
    • Go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 8

Deceased customer was due to report + Read more ...

Are the deceased customer's employment income details for the reporting period in which they passed away known, or can the person notifying of the death provide the customer's employment income details for the last reporting period?

  • Yes:
    • Using the Earnings and Reporting tool, process the reporting statement. Note: the date of lodgement on the Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen may need to be changed first to the day before the date of death. For more information see Changing Entitlement Period End Dates (EPED)
    • Go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 10

Stimulate the payment + Read more ...

Stimulate the payment using the Earnings and reporting workflow tool.

Take the deceased customer off reporting by updating the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen.

Check payments + Read more ...

Check if the deceased customer receiving any of the following payments:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) instalments
  • Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • Ancillary benefits for a Regular Care Child
  • Paid Parental Leave (PPL)
  • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)

If yes,

12

Process the death action + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure to code death action (which includes executor details).

  • Death action will cancel the deceased person's payment

If the guided procedure is not available:

  • Manually code the death action
  • When recording a death the Notification Handler (NOHL) is incorrectly using the date of notification as the date to increase the survivor's rate to the single rate. The workaround for this is to record the date of receipt (DOR) as the later of either the date of death (DOD) or date paid to + one (DPT + 1)
    Note: the workaround is automatically applied if the Notification of death guided procedure is used

1

Long term social security recipients + Read more ...

Were both members of the couple long term social security recipients at the time of death?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Both were long-term social security recipients + Read more ...

  • A Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP) is payable to the surviving partner where their new single rate is less than the couple's member of a couple rate at the time of death. The LBP represents the difference between these 2 rates for the duration of the bereavement period. Therefore, if the member of a couple rate of payment has continued from the date of death, the LBP will be reduced
  • When a member of an allowee couple dies the LBP will need to be worked out manually, however, no Manual Follow-up (MFU) or activity is generated on the survivor's record to do this
  • Follow the process to manually calculate the LBP:
    • go to the One Off Payment (OOP) screen
    • in the Effect Date field, code the date of death
    • in the Payment Type field, select 'bls' (bereavement lump sum)
    • press [Enter]
    • The Assessment Results (AR) screen is displayed
    • from the left hand menu select Optional Actions>Partner/Ex-Partner>Pensions/Allowances>One Off Payments (OOP)
    • Code the LBP amount in the Amount column in the Bereavement Payment line
    • press [Enter] twice to return to the AR screen
    • finalise activity and record details on a DOC

Is the surviving partner in receipt of JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • Send Q150 letter to advise of LBP
    • Go to Step 5

3

Not both long-term social security recipients + Read more ...

Lump Sum Bereavement Payment is not payable on the death of a member of an allowee couple unless both were long-term social security recipients.

Is the surviving partner in receipt of JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Surviving partner receives JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance + Read more ...

An MFU will be generated to check eligibility to Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV). The MFU will be allocated to the JobSeeker Boutique Bereavement Team (JBBT) for assessment and processing.

PBV is paid to a JSP or YA customer if:

  • the customer is receiving JSP or YA when they advise of their partner's death
  • both the customer and their deceased partner were Australian residents when the partner died
  • the customer and the deceased person were a couple when their partner died
  • the customer or their correspondence nominee has notified of the partner's death
  • the customer had not re-partnered when they notified of the death
  • the customer notified of the death within the 14 week bereavement period or within the duration of the pregnancy if the pregnancy is more than 14 weeks from partner's death

If the customer is also eligible for a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment, they must relinquish the LBP writing. This includes customers who have already been paid LBP and wish to receive PBV instead.

Is the customer entitled to PBV?

  • Yes,
    • In Process Direct, use bereavement calculator to estimate PBV entitlement
    • If PBV estimate is more than LBP, enclose Application for Partner Bereavement Payment (SA490) form with Q150 letter
    • Refer to Assessing entitlement to PBV and Calculating Partner Bereavement Payment
    • Issue Q150 to advise of LBP and invite PBV application
  • No,
    • Issue Q150 to advise of LBP
    • Go to Step 5

5

Action to take after death action is completed + Read more ...

  • Ongoing payments are subject to the lodgement of reporting statements; however, if the customer receives JSP, YA or SpB under JSP conditions, they can be exempted from mutual obligation or activity test requirements for the 14 week bereavement period. If the customer is pregnant when their partner died, and the expected date of confinement is more than 14 weeks in the future, the participation exemption is extended for the duration of the pregnancy. Refer to Mutual obligation requirement exemptions
  • If the surviving partner is on 2 weekly reporting because the deceased customer had employment income, Service Profiling will need to be refreshed on the surviving partner. Go to the Profile Summary (PQS) screen and code a 'Y' in the Reprofile field, then complete activity
  • Offer referral to a social worker if appropriate. See Referring customers to a social worker
  • Identify any service providers to be advised of the death including Assessment Services referrals
  • Record the details of the bereavement on a DOC
    Note: the Notification of death guided procedure should record all of these details automatically
  • If the surviving partner was a joint owner of any assets with their late partner, full ownership of the assets legally transfers to the surviving partner following the death of their partner. However, for Social Security purposes, if assets are owned in joint names with the deceased partner, the surviving partner is assessed as owning 50 per cent. The system will assess that the surviving partner owns 50 per cent of the asset until the surviving partner advises of the transfer of assets
  • If the surviving partner is granted a reversionary income stream, then it is to be assessed from the date that it is first entitled to be received. A lump sum arrears payment received by the reversionary beneficiary, accrued between the death of the primary beneficiary and when the reversionary beneficiary commences to review regular payments, is assessed as income from the income stream. This means the income is assessed over the period to which it relates and may result in a debt for that period. Effective from 9 May 2018, where the assessment of the lump sum arrears result in a debt during the bereavement period for the surviving spouse, consideration should be given to waiver provisions contained under Class of Debt, Section 1237AB(1) of the Social Security Act 1991. The legislative instrument 'Social Security (Waiver of Debts – Bereavement Period) Specification 2017' permits this action if all provisions are satisfied
    Note: any debt outside the 14 week bereavement period cannot be waived under the legislative instrument. The legislative instrument is not retrospective. This means that any of these debts that occur before 9 May 2018 cannot be waived under class of debt waiver provisions
  • Where the transfer of the deceased's assets and/or income to the survivor is likely to affect the survivor's rate, code a manual review for date of death plus 16 weeks. See Bereavement reviews
  • if the deceased had any working credits, these credits cannot be transferred to the surviving partner
  • Executor details may need to be updated after the death has been recorded
