Assess and process the claim + Read more ...

Claims must be rejected if they:

  • have been found to be fraudulent
  • do not have referral details

See the Resources page for how to reject these claims.

Check the service details in Mainframe match those on the invoice/receipt.

Are all details correct?

  • Yes:
    • press F9 to acknowledge any warnings
    • enter 'P' to pay
    • press Enter
    • press F2 to select the next claim which will return to Step 1 (go to Step 1)
  • No, or the details are insufficient and/or cannot be confirmed to be a minor discrepancy:
    • change payee code to ‘E' (or ‘R’ for unpaid invoices) to reject and add the relevant reason code

Common reason codes for digital self service claims are:

  • 162 (benefit has been previously paid for this service - duplicate claim)
  • 452 (Resubmit claim for this service – Image not claim related)
  • 453 (Resubmit claim for service – Claim details do not match image)
  • 454 (Resubmit claim for service – Some details not shown or unclear on image)
  • 455 (Resubmit claim for this service – Include account and receipt)
  • 621 (Item not claimable electronically)

Review and apply any other assessing restrictions by following the warning messages. Warning messages must be actioned in order from left to right. Further explanations can be viewed by placing the cursor at the beginning of the return message and pressing F1.

For a full list of reason codes see Indicators, codes, modifiers and control lines for claims processing in Medicare. Ask a team leader if help is needed or unable to action.

If the customer has not entered item numbers on the invoice, insert extra lines into the claim so the whole invoice is paid at the same time.

Note: include a comment in personal section of the claimant’s CDMS record which shows reason code used and the reason for the rejection.

The appropriate rejection letter will automatically be sent to the claimant.

8

Service Officer is unable to finalise and pay a claim + Read more ...

If unable to finalise and pay a claim, the claim can be parked until it can be resolved, or redirected to a Complex Claims Officer.

Service Officers can escalate the claim to Complex self service (CS) queue, due to complexity or if needing help. Note: only escalate once all appropriate assessing rules have been applied.

To Escalate a claim:

  • On the MQRI Screen
  • Key ‘R’ into the A: field of the relevant claim and press Enter
  • Press F10 to add text and key a brief reason for escalation and press Enter
  • Key U to update and press Enter
  • Press F2 to return to the redirection screen
  • In the Redirect Claim: field key 1 to select the Complex Self-Service queue and press Enter
  • To finalise and Confirm the Escalation press PF9

When redirecting a claim, include message text detailing the reason for the redirection.

If a claim is incorrectly redirected, the claim will be returned to the Service Officer with operator text explaining why.

To Park a claim:

  • on the MQRI Screen
  • key ‘P’ into the A: field of the relevant claim and press Enter
  • check the claim status has changed from WR to PR

To Redirect a claim:

  • On the MQRI Screen
  • Key ‘R’ into the A: field of the relevant claim and press Enter
  • Press F10 to add text and key a brief reason for redirection and press Enter
  • Enter ‘U’ to update when prompted
  • Press F2 to return to the redirection screen
  • In the Reassign to Individual: field key the operator number and press Enter
  • To finalise and Confirm the Redirection press PF9

If a claim is incorrectly redirected, the claim will be returned to the Service Officer with operator text explaining the reason why. This will be followed up with written feedback to the Service Officer, with a CC to their team leader.

Process a claim with a minor discrepancy

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Amending claim data + Read more ...

Customers submitting claims using digital self service channels occasionally mistype or omit information. This results in a minor difference between what shows in Mainframe and on the invoice.

If fraud is not suspected, Service Officers should amend the claim data to correct the error. See the Resources page for examples.

Amendments are not acceptable if the information is substantially different. See the Resources page for examples.

Amendments are also not acceptable if it is suspected the claim may be fraudulent. These claims must be referred for investigation.

2

Verify details + Read more ...

  • Verify all details in Mainframe against what is on the receipt – note any minor discrepancy/ies
  • Verify the items in mainframe - confirm any items that have not been submitted using digital self service claiming process, or need reordering
  • If the discrepancy is not minor, reject the services as per Step 7 in Process claims table
  • If fraud is suspected, it must not be paid. For instructions on how to reject these claims, see the ‘Processing issues for claims submitted using digital service channels’ table on the Resources page

3

Modifying claim lines + Read more ...

Additional lines can only be keyed where the invoices claimed are for the same provider. Where the customer has claimed multiple invoices with different providers, Service officers are only to pay the service details for one provider on the condition that the details match what the customer has keyed.

Invoices for secondary providers are to be rejected 453 as the customer is required to lodge separate accounts for each provider.

If the invoice has additional items not keyed by the customer:

  • insert a new line by keying 'I' in the A: field of the claim as shown and press F6
  • a new line will be inserted into the claim
  • key the correct claim details from the receipt into the new line

If the discrepancy is minor and the claim is not suspected to be fraudulent, modify the line. See Resources for examples of acceptable and unacceptable amendments when processing claims:

  • key ‘M’ in the A: field of the affected claim line and press F6
  • overtype information in editable field/s
  • repeat if required and press Enter to finalise claim

Note: if any inserted lines are incorrect, delete the line by keying ‘D’ in the A: field. Where a payee provider is present on the invoice, a PayP line may have to be inserted, by keying ’Y’ in the A: field:

  • correct any errors returned by the system
  • when the system displays the message 'enter P to pay', enter 'P' to pay and press Enter
  • press F2 to select the next claim

Process a claim that requires Safety Net verification (substantiation)

Table 3: this table describes how to action claims that contain a part payment, or claims that may require Safety Net verification in the NSCI screen.

Step

Action

1

Determine if Medicare Safety Net verification is required + Read more ...

Review the claim and ensure that the service details on Mainframe match the invoice image provided by the customer. Correct any minor discrepancies.

If the invoice is not paid in full and contains a part payment, go to Step 2

If the invoice is:

  • paid in full
  • contains items that were previously processed as unpaid (payee code 2), and
  • Mainframe displays a 6-SUS DUP return message, go to Step 3

2

Key a part payment in the FEE field + Read more ...

  • In the FEE field, key the part payment with a P prefix, overwriting any FEE amount that was displayed previously. For example, key P2000 if there is a part payment of $20.00.
  • Press Enter. The system will record the part payment amount and the FEE field will be reset to display the schedule fee

Note: if an incorrect part payment is keyed, repeat this step.

  • Check all return messages and finish assessing the claim

Procedure ends here.

3

Verify (substantiate) the claim in NSCI + Read more ...

  • Take note of the claim details (a temporary screenshot may assist) and clear the Mainframe screen by pressing [ESC] or [F12]. The digital self service claim will remain assigned and can be finalised after verification is complete.
  • Check the NSCI verification screen and verify the relevant service(s) if required
  • If the service was for a previous year, use the appropriate control line modifier. For example:
    NSCI,patient Medicare card number,FROM/010119 to view unverified services for 2019
  • If the relevant service does not appear in NSCI, that service was either already verified or is not eligible for Safety Net verification (for example, it may be an in-patient service or Child Dental Benefits Schedule service)
  • Return to the digital self service claim in MQRI

  • Reject the digital self service claim with RSN 151

Same day reversal of a claim

Step

Action

1

Identify record for reversal + Read more ...

In Mainframe, key NQEI,[insert claim ID here],card number and press Enter.

claim ID is either:

  • HC - MOA
  • AC - app

If the paid claim can be reversed, it will show as PAID-SD:

  • If there are multiple claims listed for that card number:
    • Key ‘V’ and press Enter to view the selected digital self-service claim
    • Press [F2] to return to the list of claims

2

Overtype NERC with NERR and press Enter.

This will remove the claim from the customer’s NHSI history. Service Officers can now reprocess.

Reassess claim + Read more ...

Key NQEI,[insert claim ID here],card number and press Enter.

claim ID is either:

  • HC - MOA
  • AC - app

4

Complete action + Read more ...

To reassess and complete the claim, overtype NEPC with NEPP and press Enter.
