Questions and answers for processing NARs

Item

Description

1

Question: What is the preferred terminology to use with customers - 'Non Attendance Report' or 'NAR'? + Read more ...

Answer: When talking to customers they should always be referred to as a Non Attendance Report.

2

Question: Can CDP job seekers complete a NAR online using self service? + Read more ...

Answer: No. There are no self service arrangements for compliance events.

3

Question: What messages will CDP job seekers receive online or via IVR when there is a NAR/ANR suspension outstanding? + Read more ...

Answer: If the only outstanding compliance event is a NAR (with 'Non-Attendance Report Received' (ANR) suspension reason), CDP job seekers will receive a message directing them to contact their provider.

If there are other compliance investigations outstanding prior to the NAR being submitted, CDP job seekers will be advised of the need to contact Centrelink first.

4

Question: Can NARs attract a financial penalty? + Read more ...

Answer: No

5

Question: Can a provider withdraw a NAR? + Read more ...

Answer: There should be no need for a provider to withdraw a NAR. Providers are able to finalise a NAR by recording an appropriate re-engagement reason, including where a reconnection appointment is no longer required.

6

Question: Will there be any indication on the 'Front of House' iPAD that the CDP job seeker is subject to a NAR? + Read more ...

Answer: They only indication that will be visible on the 'Front of house' iPAD will be a benefit status of 'SUS/ANR'.

Where a NAR is outstanding but the record is suspended for another reason, a DOA DOC will be created to alert Service Officers.

7

Question: Why doesn't it display on the 'Customer Issues' page? + Read more ...

Answer: Finalisation of a NAR is a provider's responsibility, that is, Services Australia staff are not required to discuss NARs with CDP job seekers as providers have responsibility for these.

Where a NAR is outstanding but the record is suspended for another reason, a DOA DOC will be created to alert Service Officers.

8

Question: If the NAR is invalid does that make the CAN/CDR invalid and will this only be decided by the review process? + Read more ...

Answer: This does not automatically follow.

The validity of the NAR is not relevant to a cancellation for not complying with a reconnection notice.

It is only if a subsequent review found the reconnection notice was not valid, the cancellation may be called into question.

9

Question: What happens if the CDP job seeker contacts their provider on a Thursday or Friday but a reconnection appointment is not available until after the weekend and the CDP job seeker is in hardship? + Read more ...

Answer: The CDP job seeker will need to discuss the reconnection appointment with their provider.

Where possible, a re-engagement appointment should be conducted within two working days. The availability of re-engagement appointments is a provider responsibility, and they will also retain responsibility for booking these appointments.

10

Question: What happens if there is a public holiday (including national and state based public holidays) + Read more ...

Answer: CDP job seekers will not be asked to attend appointments with their provider on a National or State public holiday that applies to their locality.

Operational Messages provide guidance for how to manage CDP job seekers who have an outstanding NAR but are unable to contact their provider and attend a re-engagement appointment due to a public holiday.

11

Question: What happens if a CDP job seeker has been unable to contact their provider to discuss the NAR? + Read more ...

Answer: Providers are responsible for re-engaging the CDP job seeker and finalising the NAR. If the CDP job seeker contacts Services Australia to advise they are unable to contact their provider, see Restoring payment where a payment suspension (participation) has been imposed for Community Development Program (CDP) participants.

12

Question: What happens where a re-engagement appointment has been booked and there is a provider shutdown due to contingency arrangements or a DEWR systems outage? + Read more ...

Answer: Department of Employment and Workplace Relations (DEWR) and National Indigenous Australians Agency (NIAA) have contingencies in place for scenarios such as this. The contingency arrangement will be based on the circumstances that caused the provider to shut down.

Where a contingency arrangement is activated in response to a natural disaster or a lockdown due to COVID-19, an Operational Message will provide guidelines regarding the handling of job seeker queries and compliance actions for impacted regions.

Where mutual obligation requirements are paused due to a COVID-19 lockdown in a CDP region or regions, see the Community Development Program (CDP) - suspension of mutual obligation requirements table in Altered processes and mutual obligation requirements for job seekers due to coronavirus (COVID-19) response.

Where a significant systems outage has occurred, Department of Employment and Workplace Relations liaises with Services Australia to advise of interim actions required pending resolution. This advice is communicated to Service Officers via an NNU or Operational Message.

13

Question: What happens if there is a breakdown in the communication/relationship between the provider and the customer? + Read more ...

Answer: Services Australia's role has not changed. The CDP job seeker will need to contact the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations to discuss their situation.

14

Question: What should I do if a jobseeker contacts to query why their payment is being suspended due to a NAR? + Read more ...

Answer: If a CDP job seeker contacts Services Australia to question why their payment is suspended due to a Non-Attendance Report (NAR), the Service Officer should explain how a NAR works and the CDP job seeker should be encouraged to contact their provider to resolve any outstanding issues so that payment can be restored as quickly as possible.

If the CDP job seeker has requested an explanation or applied for a formal review of a decision to suspend or cancel payment as a result of a NAR, the Participation Solutions Team (PST) skilled Service Officer must start the action immediately. The CDP job seeker should also be told to contact their provider to have their payment restored as quickly as possible.

15

Question: What happens if a CDP Provider is unable to finalise a NAR as the CDP job seeker has changed address and is now linked with a new provider? + Read more ...

Answer: In some limited circumstances, PST-skilled Service Officers with WNPPD or PFDEC security resources are able to manually finalise the NAR. See Restoring payment where a payment suspension (participation) has been imposed for Community Development Program (CDP) participants for more information.
