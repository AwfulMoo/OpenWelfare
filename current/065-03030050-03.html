Age Pension combined partner online claims

These combined claims (sometimes referred to as 'dual claims') allocate as a single work item. They can only be assessed in Process Direct by staff who have the appropriate Age Pension combined partner online claim skill tags.

Do not process Age Pension combined partner online claims as 2 separate single claims as the data for the partner claim may be lost.

This process only applies to a combined partner online claim awaiting assessment. If the claim has been previously assessed, e.g. the claims status is Rejected, go to Step 5.

Paper Claim for Age Pension (SA002 or SA412) lodged

When an SA002 or SA412 is scanned to the customer's record, a SOA shell work item is generated on the customer's record. This contains no claim information apart from the CRN and personal details, and receipt date. Add the claim information into the work item using the scanned claim and documentation.

If both the customer and partner have claimed on the same claim form, the single SOA shell can be transformed to a combined customer-partner claim by using > Combined claim switch >select Switch to combined claim. This action can be reversed to return the claim to a single claim if 'switched' in error.

8

Intention to claim for vulnerable customers + Read more ...

Claims are deemed to be made on the date a customer with vulnerable circumstances contacted Services Australia, providing all requirements are met (including that they were qualified on the contact date).

Check the PRECLM screen to review whether vulnerable circumstances have already been identified.

Some vulnerable circumstance reasons need to be recorded manually. Review the customer's circumstances and identify if a circumstance needs to be recorded manually. To record a circumstance manually or for more information, see Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.

Where an automatic or manual circumstance has been recorded and the vulnerable circumstance date was within 8 weeks of the contact date, Process Direct will automatically adjust the start date if there was a contact within 14 days of the claim submission date. However, when adjusting the start date Process Direct does not take into account:

  • if the customer was qualified on that date (required to fully meet intention to claim provisions)
    • contact dates more than 14 days prior to claim lodgement and intent to claim provisions to accept lodgement up to 13 weeks
    • other backdating provisions (these may apply in addition to intent to claim provisions)
    • other deemed claim provisions i.e. inappropriate claims or incorrect claims

For more information on the Pension Assessment Date see Step 3, Table 2, Step 14, Table 2 and in Step 8, Table 3.

3

Pension Assessment Date + Read more ...

The Pensions Assessment (PNA) screen displays the Age Pension Age Date and the Assessment Date. The Assessment Date automatically determines from what date the claim will be assessed. This is based on the date the claim was submitted, recorded contact dates for claimants that have vulnerable circumstances - see Intention to claim for vulnerable customers in Step 8, Table 1 and the date the customer reached Age Pension age.

The Assessment Date can be changed manually, if applicable, for example:

  • vulnerable circumstances exist for a claim submitted before 7 December 2020
  • vulnerable circumstances exist, the customer was not qualified on the contact date passed from Process Direct and was qualified on a later contact date before the claim was submitted
  • other backdating provisions apply (these may apply in addition to intent to claim provisions)
  • the customer is qualified but not payable on the date they submitted their claim but it is clear that their income or assets change will be within 13 weeks of the original claim date
  • inappropriate claim or incorrect claim provisions apply

To change the Assessment Date select the Calendar and select the new assessment date. For help with the start date, see Calculating the start day general rule.

Blind Pension (PDI) screen + Read more ...

A customer can qualify for Age Pension (Blind) if they qualify for Age Pension and can be assessed as permanently blind.

The PDI screen contains details in relation to the customer's impairment and exemptions that may apply. PDI can be selected from the Task Selector on the SWE screen or type PDI in the Super Key.

  • Event date - in most cases for new claims this will be the same date as the Pension Assessment Date and can be selected by keying the date into the field or using the Calendar
  • Blind Indicator
  • Blind income and assets exempt
  • Severely disabled code

The PDI screen is not pre-populated from the online claim. If the customer is claiming Age Pension (Blind), verification will be triggered for the customer to supply as part of the claim lodgement process. Check the verification against the claim summary and update within the claim if needed.

  • Select Add to insert a new row for coding the provisional details
  • If more than 1 date of event needs to be recorded, select Add to create additional rows
  • Provisional details can be changed by coding over the information or deleted

For more information, see Assessing Age Pension (Blind) claims.

Residence and claim lodgement + Read more ...

The customer's Australian residence qualification will be automatically assessed based on the residence information recorded.

Note: customers who have submitted an Age Pension claim and are receiving Widow Allowance (WA) or Partner Allowance (PA) immediately before reaching Age Pension age are exempt from the residence requirements for Age Pension.

Residence information must be recorded if the claim is to be rejected for a reason other than residence, otherwise the system will override any other rejection reason and the claim will incorrectly reject for a residence related reason.

For coding help, see Residence and Portability screens.

View whether the customer was in Australia on the lodgement date on the Immigration Advised Movements Screen (RSIM) screen see Residence assessment for customers claiming Age Pension.

Claim outcome + Read more ...

View the provisional results on the Entitlement (ELD) screen.

For Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) customers: if the customer is receiving a disability payment from the DVA, do not reject their claim based on income before checking whether they may be entitled to receive Defence Force Income Support Allowance (DFISA).

There is a known system issue for Age Pension customers with vulnerable circumstances - the system incorrectly grants from the Age Pension Age Date if this date falls between the contact date and the date the claim was submitted. Check if the Assessment Date has changed after selecting Assess in Process Direct. If so:

  • Check if there was a later contact on the PRECLM screen before the claim was submitted. If the customer was qualified on that contact date, make this the Assessment Date
  • Otherwise change the Assessment Date to the date of receipt of the claim (unless other backdating provisions or deemed claim provisions apply)

If the customer is also claiming Pension Bonus Payment and no basic amount of Age Pension or pension supplement basic is payable, no Pension Bonus Payment will be payable. Note: if not trained in Pension Bonus assessments, reallocate both claims as per Table 4.

  • Take care to record any employment income paid to the customer and partner in their first entitlement period if there is any possibility that employment income will preclude payability for this period. Confirm with the customer the amounts paid or expected to be paid:
    • If the reporting date is in the past, code the income and check payability. If payable, stimulate payment
    • If the reporting date is in the future, code income expected to be paid to check payability and then delete coding. If payable, allow the customer to report the exact amount on their reporting day
  • Where no Age Pension is payable, it may be possible to change the start date, or the customer may wish to withdraw both claims until an amount of Age Pension or pension supplement basic is payable. See Qualification and assessment of Pension Bonus Payment (PBP). The customer should be referred to a Financial Information Service (FIS) Officer.

Before finalising an Age Pension claim where a Pension Bonus entitlement has also been claimed, it is imperative to check rate details, including that the Age Pension basic amount or pension supplement basic amount is payable, on the following screens to ensure the expected results are reflected in the provisional data. Both Provisional and Prior data displays so the changes can be identified:

  • Pensions Income and Assets (PIAS)
  • Pensions Assessable Asset Summary (PAAS)

8

Pension Assessment Date + Read more ...

The date the customer reaches Age Pension age is displayed on the Pensions General Information (PNGI).

The Pension Assessment (PNA) screen displays the Assessment Date. This date determines the date the claim will be assessed from. The system works out this date based on:

  • the date the claim was submitted
  • recorded contact dates (if applicable for claims lodged before 1 July 2018)
  • the derived date of receipt of the claim for customers with vulnerable circumstances (for claims submitted on or after 7 December 2020), and
  • the date the customer reached or will reach Age Pension age

If the customer had a vulnerable circumstance that needs to be recorded manually, add this on the PRECLM screen in Process Direct or to the CUPC screen in Customer First via the guided procedure. This will update the PNA screen.

The Assessment Date can be changed manually if applicable, for example:

  • vulnerable circumstances exist for a claim submitted before 7 December 2020
  • vulnerable circumstances exist, the customer was not qualified on the contact date passed from Process Direct and was qualified on a later contact date before the claim was submitted
  • intention to claim provisions for vulnerable customers apply and the customer is eligible for an extension to lodge up to 13 weeks after the contact date
  • backdating provisions apply
  • the customer is qualified but not payable on the start date (e.g. the date they submitted the claim) but the customer has confirmed their income or assets will change within 13 weeks of the original claim date

Note: after the system calculates the assessment results, it incorrectly assesses the claim from the date the customer turns Age Pension age for customers with a vulnerable circumstance who turn Age Pension age between the intent to claim contact date and the date the claim was submitted. To apply the correct Assessment Date manually:

  • Check if there was another contact on the PRECLM screen before the claim was submitted. If the customer was qualified on that contact date, make this the Assessment Date
  • Otherwise change the Assessment Date to the date of receipt of the claim (unless other backdating provisions or deemed claim provisions apply)

For help, see Calculating the start day general rule.
