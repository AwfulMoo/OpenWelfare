Check the reason for referral + Read more ...

A staff member can refer a customer to FIS or a customer can ask for a referral.

FIS Officers provide education and information about financial matters. They support customers to understand their financial situation and their options. They can help customers to understand:

  • general financial literacy
  • financial options when starting or returning to work
  • options and effects on payments when receiving a lump sum
  • retirement planning, and how different retirement income products work
  • costs and financial options for aged care

FIS Officers are not financial planners, advisors or counsellors. They do not advise people about how to invest their money. They do not deal with other agencies or businesses on behalf of people. They do not make decisions about eligibility for payments, or update customer income and asset information.

See Identifying a customer in crisis or needing special help if the customer is in immediate financial crisis. It may be appropriate to consult with a FIS Officer onsite.

See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services if the customer:

  • wants to update their current income or assets, or
  • has questions about income and asset tests deeming provisions or changes in investments

2

Appropriate reasons for referral to FIS + Read more ...

Check if the customer needs a referral to a FIS Officer.

Find out why they are contacting. Ask them 'So I can refer you to the most appropriate person, what would you like help with today?'

Appropriate reasons for a referral to FIS include:

  • To better understand the following and how they work together
    • social security
    • tax
    • wages and salary
    • superannuation
    • budgeting
    • credit, loans and debt
    • investments, risks and returns
  • Considering ending or ending participation in the enhanced Income Management program
  • Considering accessing early release of superannuation
  • Starting or returning to work, and wanting to understand
    • how to budget and save money
    • superannuation and why it’s important
    • how income tax is worked out
    • different types of loans and how to use credit well
    • different types of financial investments, risks and returns
  • About to receive, or have received, a lump sum. This includes compensation, redundancy, total permanent disability, inheritance or property settlement
  • Newly separated, and wanting to understand financial matters that come with this change of circumstance
  • Planning to or about to retire, and wanting to understand options such as
    • superannuation
    • income streams
    • annuities
    • allocated pensions
    • Pension Bonus Scheme
    • Home Equity Access Scheme
  • To discuss options for retirement accommodation, granny flats and downsizing
  • To understand how to choose a financial planner or adviser
  • Planning for, entering or using aged care (or on behalf of someone entering or in aged care), and wanting to understand
    • how the aged care system works
    • the meaning of financial words used in aged care
    • aged care fees and costs
    • how aged care may affect their government payments
    • different options to pay for aged care based on their circumstances
    • options if they are thinking of selling or transferring property
  • Help to understand their finances if their partner has died

Note: In some instances, a referral to Financial Counselling may be more appropriate. Financial counsellors can provide advice, advocacy and case management, particularly to customers in financial hardship. See Resources tab for more information.

Note: the customer must agree to the referral to a FIS Officer before they are referred.

In some instances, an appointment with an Aged Care Specialist Officer (ACSO) may be more appropriate if there is one located in the customer’s area. See Aged Care Specialist Officers (ACSOs) for more information.

Does the customer meet the above criteria?

  • Yes,
    • go to Step 3
    • go to Step 4
  • No, refer the customer to a more appropriate service offer (Step 1 above)
    If the customer still asks to speak to a FIS Officer:
    • go to Step 3
    • go to Step 4

3

Customer is at a service centre + Read more ...

If referral to FIS is appropriate, and the customer:

  • does not need an interpreter, refer them to the FIS Phone Service using one of these options
    • The customer can call the Services Australia Older Australians phone number, and say Financial Information Service when asked for the reason for the call
    • Call the FIS Phone Service and transfer the call to the customer’s mobile phone. There is no need to wait for the call to connect to a FIS Officer
    • If the customer would prefer a FIS officer to contact, go to Step 5
  • needs an interpreter, refer the customer to the FIS phone service using one of these options
    • The customer can call the Multilingual Phone Service and ask to be referred to a FIS Officer
    • Connect an on-demand telephone interpreter (Interpreter Connect), then call the FIS phone service and transfer the call to the customer’s mobile phone. There is no need to wait for the call to connect to a FIS Officer
    • If the customer would prefer a FIS officer to contact, go to Step 5

Note: FIS appointments must only be booked by a FIS Officer, once they have checked the appropriate service channel.

Procedure ends here.

4

Customer is on the phone + Read more ...

If referral to FIS is appropriate, and the customer:

  • does not need an interpreter, transfer the call unannounced to the FIS phone service (do not create a transfer DOC)
    • Select FIS after clicking on the Instant call transfer button in Services Australia Workspace
    • If Services Australia Workspace is down, transfer manually to the FIS phone service
    • If the line is busy or the customer cannot wait, go to Step 5
  • needs an interpreter, connect an interpreter (on demand) before calling the FIS phone service and transfer the call to the customer’s mobile phone. There is no need to wait for the call to connect to a FIS Officer

There is no need to warm transfer the call.

Note: FIS appointments must only be booked by a FIS Officer, once they have checked the appropriate service channel.

Procedure ends here.

5

Request for contact by a FIS Officer + Read more ...

Access the customer's record:

  • create an open work item. Use Fast Note - select Generic > Enquiry > Request for contact FIS
  • record notes to explain why the customer is asking for a FIS to contact

Authenticate the customer + Read more ...

Ask the customer why they have contacted FIS.

3

Identify customer vulnerabilities + Read more ...

Customers experiencing vulnerability may need a face-to-face appointment.

If the customer identified any of the following vulnerabilities or discussion indicates these may be present, the customer may need a face-to-face appointment (where available):

  • financially vulnerable, for example, within a FIS Priority Audience
  • hearing impaired
  • experiencing family and domestic violence
  • impaired cognition, for example, significant mental health condition, acquired brain injury or dementia/Alzheimer’s
  • literacy and/or numeracy issues
  • needs an interpreter
  • experiencing bereavement
  • in distress
  • unsupported young person

4

Identify complex interactions + Read more ...

Face-to-face interviews are not necessary for all FIS discussions. They should be offered when the customer needs that interaction to understand the information that will be provided to them.

When assessing complexity, consider:

  • Any vulnerabilities the customer has that may make it harder for them to understand the information that will be provided
  • The customer’s existing knowledge about the topic
  • If the customer needs a complex level of information about the topic, or if they have a simple enquiry
  • The number and structure of the customer’s financial assets and investment products
  • If multiple topics will be discussed

The following are common FIS topics often seen as complex. This is a guide only. Other topics may be appropriate for a face-to-face interview if the customer is vulnerable or their situation is complex:

  • Farm succession planning
  • Aged care
  • Retirement planning
  • Granny flat rights/life interest
  • Trust and company complex business structures
  • Pension Bonus Scheme discussion
  • Recently separated/divorced and they have multiple properties, investments

5

Can the customer's enquiry be completed now? + Read more ...

  • Yes, complete the enquiry. Create and post a Record of Interview to the customer if needed. Procedure ends here
  • No,
    • If the enquiry is complex and if the customer agrees, offer a face to face appointment. Go to Step 6, or
    • Offer to contact the customer at a more appropriate time or give them the FIS seminar number 13 6357

6

Offer a Video Chat appointment + Read more ...

The customer:

  • accesses the appointment using their own device from home or another location of their choosing
  • will receive a confirmation letter in their myGov Inbox once the appointment is booked
  • accesses a link appointment in their myGov confirmation letter, in their Centrelink online account or Express Plus Centrelink mobile app
  • will need an internet enabled device, webcam and speakers
  • can use the Upload documents service to upload relevant information for the FIS Officer

If the customer agrees, make a video chat booking for them through Process Direct.

  • Use the Appointments icon to create Video Chat appointment:
    • Select Appointment Type Video Chat (Trial Only)
    • Select Appointment Channel Video Conference
    • Select Appointment Reason Talk to a FIS Officer
    • Select Location Canberra Virtual Service Centre
    • Customer timezone: select the customers timezone based on their location
    • Select an available time
    • Record 136 357 as the Appointment Phone Number (this is for the customer to contact if they cannot attend) and Save. Do not book an appointment less than 90 minutes ahead of the current time
    • The appointment will now show on the Booked Appointments table and the customer will receive a myGov confirmation letter

If the customer declines and asks for a face-to-face appointment at their home or nearby office, book the next available customer appointment at that office.
