This page contains further information relating to a Carer Allowance (CA) (child) turning 16 years of age.

CA (child) care receiver turns 16 years of age

This table contains information about eligibility for CA and other assistance when the care receiver turns 16 years of age.

Item

Description

1

CA (child) turning 16 years of age + Read more ...

Payment of CA (child) can continue up until the care receiver turns 16 years and 3 months or a determination has been made on a claim for CA (adult) and/or Carer Payment (CP) (adult) or another income support payment.

When a care receiver turns 15 years and 9 months a CTAT invitation letter is sent to the carer advising their CA (child) payment will stop when the care receiver turns 16 years and 3 months, or from the date a determination for CA (adult) or another income support payment is made, whichever is earlier, if the carer doesn't test their eligibility for any further payments.

The CTAT must be submitted online or lodged via paper form, before the child turns 16 years and 3 months (or 18 years if terminal). Paper forms lodged after cancellation date will be a new claim from the date of receipt.

The invitation letter also advises that the care receiver may be qualified for Disability Support Pension (DSP) and invites a claim.

The carer will also be required to advise the agency of any changes to their own circumstances.

2

Processing CA (child) turning 16 + Read more ...

When the carer returns the online or paper CTAT, the relevant processing is completed.

Determination of CA (adult) qualification is to be made as soon as the information is available, after the carer has lodged all necessary documentation, including the CTAT application, Medical Report (SA332A) and any other relevant information required to assess their entitlement. Delayed determination of the transfer may result in cancellation of existing payments.

3

CA suspended or cancelled + Read more ...

If the carer's CA payment:

  • has suspended or cancelled and the care receiver reaches the required age, the CTAT invitation will not be sent
  • is restored before the child turns 16 years and 3 months, the CTAT will automatically be sent on the day of restoration

Carers who are not eligible for restoration under CTAT need to complete a new claim for CA (adult).

4

Caring for 2 children each with a disability or medical condition where CA (child) is turning 16 + Read more ...

When a carer qualifies under these provisions, and 1 or more of the children turns 16, they will remain qualified until they turn 16 years and 3 months or a determination has been made on a claim for CA (adult) and/or CP (adult) or another income support payment, whichever is earlier.

If the carer has not taken any steps to claim another income support payment, CA (child) will cancel when the eldest care receiver turns 16 years and 3 months.

5

Child to Adult Transfer (CTAT) has not been processed + Read more ...

When the CTAT is returned, it is processed by carers or Smart Centre processing team. Processing staff can refer to Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA) Child to Adult Transfer (CTAT).

If a carer has submitted their CTAT application online and contacts about the status, Service Officers should first check that the CTAT was submitted in Process Direct:

  • search for the work item in the Inbox:
    • Main category: Claim or Change of Circumstance
    • CRN: Carer CRN
    • select the Carer-Child to Adult Transfer Review. Status will display on work item
  • when the TS screen displays, check:
    • status of the work item, and
    • date of submission

Further checks can be made by selecting:

  • Status icon
  • Tasks icon
  • Documents icon
  • check the DL screen for DOCs
