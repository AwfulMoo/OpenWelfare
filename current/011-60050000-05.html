Handle calls relating to the CTG PBS Co-payment Program

For pharmacists

Table 1: this table describes how to address call enquiries from pharmacists relating to the CTG PBS Co-payment Program.

Scenario

Advise

Action

Patient at pharmacy wanting to be removed from the CTG register

When a pharmacist is dispensing a prescription for a patient and:

  • the patient wants to be removed from the register
  • the pharmacist does not believe the patient should be listed on the register, or
  • the pharmacist believes the registration is an error

“I can only withdraw someone from Closing the Gap by their own request.

The customer will need to ask us to remove them from the register.”
  • Ask the pharmacist to provide the PBS General phone number to the customer
  • If the customer is able to speak on the phone, speak with them directly to confirm the request
  • The process to follow to allow a customer to opt-out is found on Table 2

Patient not recognised as registered or would like to be registered for CTG

When a pharmacist is dispensing a prescription for a patient and:

  • the script is annotated as CTG, but the dispensing software does not recognise the patient as being CTG-registered
  • the patient is known to the pharmacy as a CTG patient, but the dispensing software does not recognise them as being CTG-registered
  • they want to check with Services Australia if a patient is registered for CTG
  • the patient would like to return to the CTG register

“Pharmacy software will recognise if a patient is CTG- registered. If this is not the case, the person’s registration may not have been completed properly.

You can verify their status with HPOS, if your pharmacy is registered within PRODA and linked to HPOS.

The patient must return to, or call their doctor and register for CTG if no record is showing.

Their doctor or their doctor's HPOS-linked delegate can register the patient through HPOS.

The doctor can call us if they need further assistance relating to their CTG patient.
  • See contact details to provide phone numbers, website information if required
  • PRODA organisation information can be found on the Services Australia Website Links
  • For pharmacy organisation instructions, see Delegate and Pharmacy Organisation Steps to Access HPOS for Closing the Gap
  • Escalate complex enquiries using the escalation template
  • If required, lodge a customer aggression incident, by following existing Medicare escalation processes. See Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression- Medicare

Patient unknown to pharmacist

When pharmacists dispensing a prescription for a patient and:

  • they do not know the patient who has presented with a prescription that is CTG- annotated, and their dispensing software indicates they are CTG- eligible, or
  • they do not know the patient who has presented with a prescription that is not CTG- annotated, but their dispensing software indicates they are CTG- eligible

“Pharmacy software will recognise if a patient is CTG- registered.

You can verify their status within HPOS, if your pharmacy is registered with PRODA and linked to HPOS.

If the software indicates that the person is entitled to the CTG Co-payment, you are able to dispense the prescription as CTG.

This is valid, regardless if the person is known, or the prescription is annotated as CTG.”

Refer to Patient wanting to be removed from the CTG register above, if relevant.

Escalate further enquiries using the escalation template.

For PBS prescribers/Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health Practitioners

Table 2: this table describes how to address call enquiries from PBS prescribers/Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health Practitioners relating to the CTG PBS Co-payment Program.

Scenario

Advise

Action

Error code 2064- age limit when attempting to register

“You are probably in the Practice Incentives Program (PIP) tile within HPOS. CTG has moved from there and now has own tile.

CTG has no age limit. The age limit only applies to PIP/ Indigenous Health Incentive.

Please return to the HPOS landing page and access programs via the Prescriber/ Organisation Act For function. Then select the Closing the Gap PBS Co-Payment Register tile within My programs."
  • Verify that the caller is a prescriber. If they are a delegate, see below for delegate instructions
  • Advise the caller they are in the wrong HPOS tile, no age limit applies to CTG
  • Transfer the prescriber to eBusiness for assistance if the CTG tile is not visible. See Contact details for the eBusiness hotline number
  • Escalate enquiries using the Escalation template, where the caller holds a Prescriber number in PDS but cannot see the CTG tile in HPOS

Prescribers/ practitioners who have registered a patient for CTG in error

“Once they are registered, only a patient can withdraw themselves from CTG.

However, if you have just selected the wrong patient via HPOS in error, I can escalate to have this reversed.”

  • Obtain details from the prescriber including provider/ prescriber number name, contact number
  • Verify that they are not overriding another prescriber’s CTG registration
  • Advise that the patient must call us directly if they wish to withdraw
  • Obtain patient details to check against CDMS system
  • Escalate with details of why the patient is being withdrawn, using the escalation template

No Self Service access

Prescribers/practitioners wanting to issue a prescription to a CTG -eligible patient they are seeing, and:

  • cannot self-serve via HPOS due to internet connectivity issues, Services Australia system down issues
  • cannot self-serve via HPOS because their PRODA and/ or HPOS accounts are not enabled, or
  • there is no delegate in the practice who can action this in HPOS on their behalf

“As you need to issue a prescription to your patient immediately, I am able to assist you today”

“I will conduct a security check first and then check your patient’s CTG status”.
  • Complete Table 1 on the Process page
  • Transfer the prescriber to eBusiness. See Contact details for the eBusiness hotline number

Patient does not require immediate prescription

Prescribers/practitioners wanting to:

  • check the CTG status of a patient (or patients) not consulting with them, or
  • register an eligible patient, not in a current consultation but has confirmed the patient declaration for the CTG PBS Co-payment Program

“CTG registrations are now self-managed through HPOS.

Please log into your PRODA account and access HPOS, or your delegates can do this on your behalf.

You can check multiple patients at any time, and register them straight away if they are not registered.

If you need assistance to check your PRODA and/ or HPOS accounts, I can put you through to the eBusiness hotline who can help you to get set up for CTG.”
  • Transfer the prescriber to eBusiness. See Contact details for the eBusiness hotline number
  • Escalate complex enquiries using the Escalation templates
  • If required, lodge a customer aggression incident, by following existing Medicare escalation processes. See Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression- Medicare

Assistance required

Prescribers/practitioners requiring:

  • assistance to create their PRODA and/ or HPOS accounts, or
  • assistance accessing/ troubleshooting their existing PRODA and/ or HPOS accounts

“Information on PRODA and/or HPOS accounts can be found on the Services Australia website.

If you would like to speak to someone straight away, I will transfer you to the eBusiness hotline for assistance with this.”
  • Transfer the prescriber to eBusiness. See Contact details for the eBusiness hotline number

  • Escalate complex enquiries using the escalation template under Escalation templates

Escalations

Needing to escalate a system issue with HPOS due to:

  • having the correct permissions, but not being able to see the CTG tile
  • having the CTG tile but not being able to select it, or
  • having any other HPOS issues that eBusiness cannot resolve

“As you are having technical issues with self-service, I am able to assist you with your enquiry today.

I will escalate your HPOS issue and have somebody investigate this for you.”
  • Assist the prescriber to complete their transaction for CTG, within CDMS
  • Escalate a system issues enquiry using the escalation template

Frequently asked questions and answers about 1 July 2021 changes

Table 5

Item

Description

1

Question: What changed from 1 July 2021?

Answer:

  • A new centralised patient registration database for the Program, managed by Services Australia, went live
  • Any PBS prescriber or Ahpra registered Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health Practitioner registered with Medicare as a provider can register eligible Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients for the program
  • Eligible Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients can be registered for the Program no matter where they live. This registration will cover eligible patients even if they move to a different health clinic
  • Registration of eligible Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients will be via the Health Professional Online Services (HPOS)
  • PBS prescribers are no longer legally required to write or electronically print ‘CTG’ on eligible PBS prescriptions for registered Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients. However, annotation of a PBS script will help community and Section 94 Approved Private Hospital pharmacists, when dispensing the medicines, to know that the patient is registered for the program
  • Any PBS prescriber can write PBS General Schedule medicine prescriptions for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients that are registered for the Program
  • PBS prescriptions written by PBS prescribers within public hospitals are eligible to be dispensed by any community pharmacy
  • If Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients are registered for the Program the pharmacy dispensing system will automatically calculate the co-payment reduction for general schedule PBS medicines at the point of dispensing

To assist prescribers to set up their digital access see also:

Helping users create a Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account

Accessing Health Professional Online Services

2

Question: Do existing CTG patients need to be registered again in HPOS?

Answer: No, existing CTG records are visible in HPOS, and the patient registration database, from 1 July 2021.

3

Question: How do patients register for the program, or find out if they are already registered?

Answer:

  • From 1 July 2021, if a patient is unsure whether they are registered for the Program, their Practitioner or the Pharmacist will be able to check via HPOS
  • If Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients want to register for the program they will need to talk to their practitioner to see if they are eligible for registration

See also, HPOS - Find a Patient - Services Australia

4

What is the eligibility criteria for patients to be registered for the program?

Answer:

  • The eligibility criteria is a person who meets all of the following:
    • Identifies themselves to the practitioner as an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander
    • In the opinion of the practitioner, would experience setbacks in the prevention or ongoing management of a condition if the patient did not adhere to a course of treatment
    • In the opinion of the practitioner. would be unlikely to adhere to the course of treatment without assistance from this Program; and
    • Is enrolled with Medicare

5

Question: What medicines are covered under the program?

Answer:

  • The Program applies to prescriptions for PBS General Schedule medicines only. Those are PBS medicines made available under Section 85 of the National Health Act 1953 (the Act)
  • PBS medicines supplied under a special supply arrangement established under Section 100 of the Act are not covered under the program
  • PBS medicines provided to Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients on discharge from public hospitals, under the provisions of the Pharmaceutical Reform Agreements with individual states and territories, are not covered under the program

6

Question: How much will Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients registered for the Program pay for General Schedule PBS medicines?

Answer:

  • Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients who are registered for the program but do not have a Concession Card pay $6.80 for PBS General Schedule medicines
  • If registered Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients have a Concession Card they receive their PBS General Schedule medicines for free

7

Question: What are the new requirements around endorsing prescriptions with the ‘CTG’ annotation?

Answer:

  • The ‘CTG’ annotation on PBS prescriptions indicates to community pharmacists that patients are eligible for the Program. However, annotation of a PBS script will help community and Section 94 Approved Private Hospital pharmacists, when dispensing the medicines, to know that the patient is registered for the program
  • If Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients are registered for the Program, the pharmacy dispensing system automatically calculates the co-payment reduction for General Schedule PBS medicines at the point of dispensing
  • If the Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander patient is not registered for the Program (regardless if the prescription has been endorsed with CTG) the dispensing system will return an error. Refer the patient back to their practitioner to have their registration completed

8

Question: How can health services or clinics in remote areas without internet access register patients for the program?

Answer: In remote areas with limited internet access, a health service or clinic can phone Services Australia Aboriginal Health Services (option 2) to register the patient for the Program.

9

Question: Who can pharmacists direct dispensing software and/ or PBS claiming questions to?

Answer:

  • Any questions from pharmacists in regards to changes to the dispensing software should be directed to the Software Vendor
  • For help or any questions a Pharmacist may have in relation to a PBS, they can phone PBS general enquiries

10

Question: What did not change from 1 July 2021

Answer:

  • The Program continued to be for self-identified Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients only, and no other patient groups
  • Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients that had already registered for the Program continued to be registered
  • Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander patients still need to be assessed by their practitioner as being eligible for Program registration
  • Section 94 Private Hospital pharmacies still dispense PBS prescriptions covered under the program
  • Section 94 Public Hospital pharmacies still cannot dispense PBS prescriptions covered under the program
  • The program continues to apply to prescriptions for PBS General Schedule medicines only
  • Patients still need to be registered for the program before a reduced co-payment is applied to PBS General Schedule prescriptions when being dispensed

PBS Safety Net Scenarios for patients participating in the measure

Joe is a non-concessional patient. He is registered for the Program and presents you with a prescription for atorvastatin 80mg tablets. While Joe is charged $6.80 for this supply, $42.50 is printed on his ‘Safety Net’ sticker that you add to his family’s Prescription Record Form (PRF).

Joe’s mother is a concessional patient and is also registered for the Program. After dispensing her prescription for her insulin, she doesn’t have to pay for the medicine, but $6.80 counts towards her family’s Safety Net records for that calendar year. For more examples see Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) Safety Net processing and enquiries.
