Assessing satisfactory progress for PES tertiary students

Step

Action

1

Customer contacts Smart Centre Telephony + Read more ...

Is the Service Officer skilled in PES processing?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, record the details in a Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > Pensioner Education Supplement > PES Study. Update Action DOC. Procedure ends here

2

Previous study + Read more ...

Has the tertiary student undertaken previous study in their current course or in another course at the same tertiary level?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, assessment of satisfactory progress is not required as satisfactory progress is met. Go to Step 11

3

Assess previous study undertaken + Read more ...

Students are asked to provide study details in the online claim. This information can be used to determine allowable time. To access this information, go to the Claim Overview in Process Direct.

Academic transcripts may need to be manually requested if there is insufficient information, either in the online claim or from the customer's record, to calculate the allowable time accurately. If a request for academic transcripts is required, an official academic record or transcript is preferred. In very simple cases, a statement from the student may be all that is required.

When calculating the amount of time spent studying at the same tertiary level as the current course, the method of calculation depends on whether the student is:

  • currently a full-time 25% or 66% concessional study load student, and
  • at the time the previous study was undertaken was part-time, full-time, 25% or 66% concessional study load student

See Measuring time spent for tertiary student's attachment on the Resources page.

  • Look only at study at the same tertiary level as the current course
  • Completed courses count as the lesser of minimum time or the actual time it took to complete the course
  • Divide other study into its academic periods (for example, semesters, years). Most courses are divided into periods of study. In these cases, each period is considered separately. Where the course is not divided, the course is considered as a whole. See Determining allowable time/reasonable time for a course of study
  • Determine whether any of this study can be disregarded
  • For current full-time and 66% concessional study load students assess each remaining period separately to determine what percentage of a full year's study it represents
  • Whether or not any study load concessions (25% or 66%) applied for any of the previous study. Special care needs to be taken if the student is granted a 25% study load concession for the current study or was granted a concession for any of the previous study. See the Resources page
  • Where study load aggregation has previously been applied to satisfy full-time study requirements, all study periods included in the aggregation assessment are counted as full-time
  • Total the academic period percentages by adding together each individual period of study to give the total amount of study undertaken
  • For assistance in calculating complex assessments of allowable time for PES customers, contact a PES TPS

Example: A student who has undertaken 3 part-time semesters of study (25% of a full year's study load each) and a full-time semester (50%). This totals 125% or the equivalent of one and a quarter years of full-time study.

4

Total study and allowable time + Read more ...

Determine the allowable time for the student's current course and compare the total study (as calculated in Step 2) with the allowable time.

If the total previous study is:

  • less than or equal to the allowable time, go to Step 5
  • greater than the allowable time, go to Step 6

5

Less than allowable time + Read more ...

As the total previous study is less than or equal to the allowable time, the customer is making satisfactory progress.

The result of the calculation at Step 3 indicates how much longer the customer could expect payments to continue in their current course, take note of the amount of allowable time the customer has remaining.

Go to Step 7.

6

Not making satisfactory progress + Read more ...

As the total previous study is greater than the allowable time, the student has already exceeded allowable time. PES cannot be paid for any further study in this course.

PES may be payable, however, for a short period of a longer course at the same level or for a course at a different level.

Where the student does not meet the satisfactory progress rules, their record needs to be updated. If the student is

  • claiming PES, reject the claim. Procedure ends here
  • currently receiving payment, use the procedure Determining allowable time/reasonable time for a course of study to determine the date to enter in the Allowable Time End Date: field in either

Process Direct

  • Go to the Customer Study Details (EDC) screen
  • Select Edit next to the course detail
  • On the Change Education Details screen update the Allowable Time End Date: field
  • Select Save
  • Complete the Receipt Date and Channel. Select Save

Customer First

  • Go to the Education Course Details (EDC) screen
  • Enter the date in the Allowable Time End Date field
  • Check the Comments field and ensure only the exact name of the course in which the student is enrolled is recorded. This field cannot be left blank and cannot contain any information in addition to the course name. Allowable time assessments or any other comments must not be recorded in the Comments field as this information will be displayed to customers when using the 'Manage study details' online service
  • In the Source field, key the source of the information
  • In the Receipt Date field, key the day the customer provided the information
  • Go to the Action field, select 'CHG' or key 'C' to update the information recorded

Note: completion of this action will automatically cancel the customer's payment and a letter will issue to the customer advising them of the cancellation

Identify if the customer is eligible for any other income support or products

Go to Step 11.

7

Making satisfactory progress, allowable time remaining + Read more ...

Use the procedure Determining allowable time/reasonable time for a course of study to determine if the Allowable Time End Date: field should be coded. This field is located in:

  • Process Direct on the Customer Study Details (EDC) screen
  • Customer First on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen

Will the customer reach their allowable time end date before reaching their current course or student end date?

  • Yes, to update the Allowable Time End Date field:
    • in Process Direct, go to Step 8
    • in Customer First, go to Step 9
  • No:
    • The Allowable Time End Date field does not need to be coded
    • Record the findings on a DOC including details of allowable time calculations made
    • Procedure ends here

8

Process Direct - code Allowable Time End Date field + Read more ...

  • Go to the EDC screen
  • Select Edit next to the course detail
  • On the Change Education Details screen, update the Allowable Time End Date field. Note: the result of the calculation at Step 4 indicates when the customer will reach their allowable time, enter this date in the Allowable Time End Date field
  • Select Save
  • Complete the Receipt Date and Channel. Select Save
  • Select Assess and address and errors/warnings
  • Select Assess
  • Select Finish. Record details of the update
  • Select Finalise to complete the transaction.

Go to Step 10

9

Customer First - code Allowable Time End Date field + Read more ...

  • Go to the EDC screen
  • The result of the calculation at Step 4 indicates when the customer will reach their allowable time, enter this date in the Allowable Time End Date field
  • Check the Comments field and ensure only the exact name of the course in which the student is enrolled is recorded. This field cannot be left blank and cannot contain any information in addition to the course name. Allowable time assessments or any other comments must not be recorded in the Comments field as this information will be displayed to customers when using the 'Manage study details' online service
  • In the Source field, key the source of the information
  • In the Receipt Date field, key the day the customer provided the information
  • Go to the Action field, select 'CHG' or key 'C' to insert the information recorded on the existing EDC entry

10

Advise the customer of the decision in writing + Read more ...

As the customer's Pensioner Education Supplement will cancel in the future as a result of the allowable time coding, they must be advised of this in writing by sending a Q888 letter.

  • Issue a Q888 to the customer, see Creating a Q999 or Q888 letter for instructions on how to create a Q888. Include the following approved wording in the free text paragraph:
    • 'Following an assessment of your allowable time based on previous studies undertaken at the same level as the course you are doing now it has been determined we can pay you Pensioner Education Supplement until XXXX. Your Pensioner Education Supplement will cancel on XXXX because you will have reached the maximum time allowed for your course.'

11

Record the decision on a Note/DOC + Read more ...

Record details of the decision of the allowable time assessment on a Fast Note. Select Auto text option > Students, ABSTUDY > Update > Updated/Corrected EDCH Study Details.

This Fast Note should include:

  • the name, academic level and allowable time of the student's current course
  • a summary of the student's previous study, semester by semester (including at other levels of study if this is available - although it won't be counted in this assessment it may be useful if the customer applies for another course)
  • details of any study that has been disregarded and the reasons for disregarding the study
  • the total of the previous study, and
  • the period of eligibility for payment (if any) that still remains
  • If the PES payment has cancelled as a result of the allowable time coding, include the relevant Act Ref. See Recording reasons for decisions under Getting it Right (GIR)
