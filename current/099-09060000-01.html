Change of circumstances for Widow Allowance (WA) customers 099-09060000

  • Background
  • References
Expand all


This document outlines the procedures applicable when a current Widow Allowance (WA) customer advises a change in their circumstance.

Closure of Widow Allowance (WA)

From 1 July 2018, no new claims for Widow Allowance can be granted.

If a customer advises of a change in their circumstances which results in loss of entitlement, they will need to claim a different income support payment, such as JobSeeker Payment.

If the customer's circumstances change again, and as a result they meet the WA eligibility criteria again, they will be unable to reclaim WA.

Payment should not be cancelled, as the favourable decision overrides the adverse decision when a customer notifies multiple events within 14 days, and:

  • the first notifiable event results in an adverse decision, and
  • the second notifiable event results in a favourable decision which nullifies the earlier event

Notification obligations

WA customers are obliged to advise Services Australia, usually within 14 days, of any event or change in circumstances that could affect their payment.

Once a customer has signed their claim form (confirming, among other things, that they have read and understood what changes they must advise) they are bound by the notification provisions.

Notifiable events

Customers are advised what they must notify in their grant notification letter and on the back of each reassessment letter sent to them.

Following is a list of the types of changes a customer must advise. As circumstances vary for each customer, this list is not exhaustive.

Changes of circumstance that may require review or update to a customer's record include any change to:

  • income or assets, including commencing employment
  • contact details including changes to address and/or telephone number
  • accommodation details, including changes to address and rent
  • relationship status
  • residence status

At this time, consider whether this customer is eligible for the Age Pension.

Consequences if fail to notify

Widow Allowance (WA) customers may incur an overpayment if they fail to notify a change of circumstance which results in a loss of entitlement.

As WA closed to new claims from 1 July 2018, if the customer's WA is cancelled they will not be able to reclaim the payment.

If the customer still requires income support, they will need to test their eligibility for another payment such as JobSeeker Payment.

Check and update contact details

Every customer's address (including postal address) and telephone details (including mobile phone numbers) must be checked at every contact, and updated if required. Note: if the customer has authenticated themselves via IVR, address details do not need to be confirmed.
