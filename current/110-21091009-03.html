Step

Action

1

Phone contact + Read more ...

All inbound and outbound calls must be made using phone contact requirements.

Discuss the reason for the call:

If the customer:

  • wants general information:
    • answer any queries the customer has. See Income Compliance Programme Class Action for FAQs
    • discuss the information on the Services Australia website and how the customer can find out if they are eligible for a distribution payment
      - encourage the customer to change or confirm their details on their Centrelink online account
    • see the Resources page for suggested dialogue to help in the conversation
  • calls about a Class action settlement and you are not trained to assist the customer:
    • attempt to transfer the customer to the Compliance line listed in the DOC on the customer's record
  • is returning a call refer to the Notes in the Class action settlement activity or query to determine the reason for the call and proceed as required
  • needs staff help to submit a class action settlement query, tell the customer the dispute period closed on 10 December 2021. See Table 3 Staff queries FAQ Item 7 on the Resources tab of Income Compliance Programme Class Action for suggested dialogue to assist with the conversation
  • requests an independent review by the assurer, tell the customer the independent review period closed on 7 January 2022. See Table 3 Staff queries FAQ Item 7 on the Resources tab of Income Compliance Programme Class Action for suggested dialogue to assist with the conversation
  • missed the cut off date for registering, tell the customer the registration period closed 11 February 2022. See Table 3 Staff queries FAQ Item 8 on the Resources tab of Income Compliance Programme Class Action for suggested dialogue to assist with the conversation

If the executor/administrator or solicitor of a deceased customer calls and the Compliance Officer (CO) is not skilled to help them, use Services Australia Workspace to warm transfer the customer to Compliance - Class Action Settlement Executor.

3

Decide what action is needed + Read more ...

Check the interaction history and Notes in the Class Action settlement activity to decide what action is required.

  • If the Status is On Hold with Reason:
    • Refund Pending, the former customer has a Returned Repayments refund task with a status of ‘On Hold - Awaiting Contact'. The refund needs to be completed before progressing the settlement activity, see Income Compliance Refunds
  • If the Status is For Manual Action with the Reason:
    • Settlement Attribution failure, Trigger Repayment Failed or Settlement Unexpected failure, go to Step 4
    • Settlement Circumstance Update Failure, go to Step 5
    • Settlement Pay destination update failure, go to Step 8
    • Settlement Forced Manual Action, see Processing class action settlements for customers with manual exceptions
  • If the Status is In Process with the reason: Awaiting contact, complete the staff assisted workflow, go to Step 6
  • If the status is Finalised with the reason Awaiting Settlement Calculation, the customer is current and has been auto registered
  • If the customer is deceased and a third party, executor/administrator or solicitor calls, see Table 1 in Processing class action settlements for customers with manual exceptions
  • If the customer is incarcerated or the nominee/authorised person contacts on their behalf, see Processing class action settlements for incarcerated customer

Payments tab + Read more ...

Select the Payments tab to go to the most recent payment destination.

The account listed can be updated and new accounts added.

Note: if the payment destination has failed, a provisional update can display.

A warning displays if the customer has a payment nominee arrangement. The nominee can be voluntary or involuntary.

  • For involuntary:
    ‘The customer has an involuntary payment nominee arrangement with [Nominee Name] from DD MM YYYY’
  • For voluntary
    ‘The customer has a voluntary payment nominee arrangement with [Nominee Name] from DD MM YYYY. Cancel the payment nominee arrangement before adding payment destination details’

Ask the customer where they want the settlement paid.

If there is:

  • no payment nominee and a payment destination is to be added or updated, go to Step 10
  • no payment nominee and the payment destination is correct, go to Step 11
  • an involuntary payment nominee, go to Step 11
  • a voluntary payment nominee and it is to be paid to the nominee’s account details on the Payments screen, go to Step 10
  • a voluntary payment nominee, but the customer wants the settlement paid to their account:
    • exit the Class Action activity
    • in Customer Record, cancel the nominee arrangement
    • after cancelling the payment nominee and updating the payment destination, go to the Payments tab. Check the account details are correct. Then go to Step 11

Finalise class action settlement + Read more ...

To finalise, select the Finalise tab to go to the Confirm Class Action Settlement eligibility screen

  • ‘Have you confirmed all the customer's contact details are up to date?’ Yes/No
  • ‘Have you confirmed the customer's payment details are up to date?’ Yes/No
  • ‘Is the payment to be made via cheque?’ Yes/No
    If Yes, Select Cheque reason: Customer request, Deceased or Overseas
  • Does this payment need to be processed manually? No must be selected in all instances

If the customer has a payment nominee, extra questions display:

  • For voluntary payment nominees
    ‘Does the customer want to receive the settlement in their own bank account?’ Yes/No
  • For involuntary payment nominees
    ‘Have you informed the customer that the settlement will go to their payment nominee?’ Yes/No

Record all details in the Notes. See the Resources page for the template.

Select Finalise.

‘Are you sure you want to finalise this Class Action settlement eligibility? Yes/No

Once finalised, the Class Action settlement Activity will display a Status of Finalised with the Reason Awaiting Settlement Calculation. No further action is required

If there is an eligible refund pending to be paid for a deceased estate, see Processing income compliance refunds for customers with manual exceptions.

5

Check if the debt relates to an in-scope Earned Income Intervention - PYG + Read more ...

The Income Compliance review on the Integrated Review System (IRS) determines the service used to action the customer’s income compliance intervention.

Staff need to locate the review type to decide if the debt was a result of an Earned Income Intervention. This also indicates where to find information about the review on the customer’s record.

The customer can have multiple income compliance interventions. Assess each intervention.

To locate the review/s:

  • in Nxt, key 'IRRS' to go to the Review Summary (IRRS) screen
  • locate the EII Compliance review, an EII Compliance review will display IRS/EII/EMP
  • 'S'elect the review to go to the Review Details (IRRD) screen

The Review Details screen will display the review method and review origin. This helps in determining the review type:

The Review Method can display as:

  • PAYG Manual Compliance Interventions:
    • PHO
    • MAI
    • FIL
  • PAYG Online Compliance Interventions:
    • ONL
    • ONM

The review origins for both PAYG Manual Compliance Interventions and Online Compliance interventions display as:

  • PYG
  • Enhanced Data Matching (EDM). EDM is income given by the customer to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) via their Income Tax Return (ITR). This information is then given to Centrelink

Explain assurer category decision + Read more ...

Tell the customer the outcome of the category decision.

Tell the customer:

‘You asked for a review because you disagreed with the category of your debt/s. This review checked if the debt/s are part of the ‘Robodebt’ Class Action and whether you are eligible for a settlement payment.'

The Scheme Assurer is an independent party appointed to review your case. They have directed us to advise you on the outcome of this review.’

Not eligible

‘The outcome of the review is you are not eligible for a settlement payment.’

Eligible

‘The outcome of the review is you are eligible for a settlement payment. Services Australia will start making payments in early 2022.’

Tell the customer they will be issued a letter notifying them of the outcome.

See the Resources page for a Categorisation table and suggested dialogue to help with the explanation of the category decision.

If the customer still disagrees with the outcome, tell them there are no further appeal decisions available.

For complaints not relating to the Settlement Distribution scheme, see Managing customer complaints and feedback.

If the customer wants general information:

  • answer any queries the customer may have. See the Resources page of Income Compliance Programme Class Action for frequently asked questions (FAQ)
  • explain the information available about the Class Action Settlement on the Services Australia website

Record all details of the conversation in Notes. See the Resources page for the template.

The query will change to the status Complete with the reason: query completed.
