Exemptions from mutual obligation or participation requirements due to COVID-19

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Exemptions from mutual obligation or participation requirements due to COVID-19 + Read more ...

Customers must not go to a service centre to seek an exemption in areas impacted by COVID-19 or if isolating due to the virus. They:

  • should call their regular payment line if they need an exemption
  • not to go to a service centre

Service Officers may record an exemption if a job seeker advises they cannot complete activities/tasks due to the impacts of COVID-19.

If a job seeker seeks an exemption due to temporary incapacity, illness or injury not related to COVID-19 they must provide a valid medical certificate to Services Australia. For details, see Assessment of a medical certificate to determine exemption from mutual obligation requirements for a temporary incapacity.

There are temporary rules for assessing medical certificates lodged by job seekers in COVID-19 areas with reduced mutual obligation requirements. See Table 2.

An exemption is not appropriate for Centrelink managed job seekers who cannot continue with voluntary work or part time work due to workplace closures. Refer these job seekers to their Employment Services Provider.

2

COVID-19 Exemptions + Read more ...

Grant an exemption if the job seeker is:

  • isolated due to possible exposure to COVID-19
  • caring for someone following advice to isolate by a medical practitioner or government authority
  • providing temporary care for an adult or child when the following are not available:
    • child care facilities
    • day services, or
    • supported workplaces for people with disabilities or other special needs

3

Parents of school aged children required to stay home + Read more ...

An exemption is not appropriate if the job seeker is a:

  • Parent with a child undertaking school work at home due to COVID-19. This circumstance does not meet formal ongoing 'home schooling' arrangements and it is not appropriate to apply an exemption

There are reduced mutual obligation requirements for parents of children who are:

  • school age, and
  • directed by their Federal, State or Territory authorities not to attend school

Any parent that this impacts:

  • should remain connected to their Employment Services Provider. This is so they are ready to re-enter the labour market when schools re-open
  • should still attend their regular provider appointments, by phone or online
  • does not have job search requirements, and
  • does not need to participate in any other activity

Note: this is not home schooling. It is not appropriate to apply an exemption from mutual obligation requirements in this scenario. For assessing home schooling exemptions for principal carers, see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances.

4

Assessing and coding the exemption + Read more ...

The Resources page contains a list exemptions and describes when they should be applied.

5

Isolation due to possible COVID-19 exposure + Read more ...

If a customer advises they need to be isolated at home or in hospital due to possible COVID-19 exposure:

  • do not ask them to provide evidence
  • ask appropriate questions to confirm they are following advice of:
    • a medical practitioner
    • a government authority, or
    • the latest COVID-19 health alerts from the Australian Government Department of Health
  • grant the customer an exemption for the period they are isolated
  • cancel or reschedule any face-to-face appointments booked with Services Australia during the exemption period
  • tell the customer not to attend any offices until they are no longer required to be isolated, and
  • check the Travel Outside Australia Summary (TOAS) screen to confirm the day they arrived in Australia

6

More information for customers about COVID-19 + Read more ...

Tell customers:

  • with flu-like symptoms to call the healthdirect helpline on 1800 022 222
  • without flu-like symptoms to call the Australian Government's Coronavirus Health Information Line on 1800 020 080

Assessing medical certificates and incapacity exemptions for job seekers in COVID-19 areas with reduced or suspended mutual obligation requirements

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Assessing medical certificates and incapacity exemptions for job seekers in COVID-19 areas with reduced or suspended mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

This process applies only to job seekers with reduced or suspended mutual obligation requirements due to COVID-19.

For all other job seekers lodging medical certificates, follow usual process to determine if:

  • an exemption from mutual obligation requirements can be granted
  • the job seeker requires referral to an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt)

Participation requirements for all DSP customers aged under 35 are currently voluntary. There is no need for DSP customers to lodge a medical certificate while the participation is voluntary.

Job seekers who are unable to work due to a medical condition can seek a temporary incapacity exemption from their mutual obligation requirements by lodging a valid medical certificate. There are temporary rules for job seekers living in COVID-19 areas. These rules allow job seekers to be granted a special circumstances (major personal crisis) exemption where a temporary incapacity exemption cannot be granted.

Regardless of the assessment outcome for the medical certificate, job seekers in a COVID-19 area should also be referred for an ESAt where required, to allow them to access suitable payments and services.

2

Job seeker in a COVID-19 area with reduced or suspended requirements lodges a medical certificate + Read more ...

Based on the information from the treating doctor, assess if the job seeker can be granted a temporary incapacity exemption - see Assessment of a medical certificate to determine exemption from mutual obligation requirements for a temporary incapacity.

If the job seeker is eligible for a temporary incapacity exemption, an exemption can be granted an exemption for the period of incapacity on the medical certificate up to:

  • a maximum of 13 weeks as a general rule
  • 12 months if the job seeker meets the criteria for a serious illness exemption

3

Manifestly medically eligible for Disability Support Pension + Read more ...

If a medical certificate shows a job seeker is clearly manifestly medically eligible for DSP, they should be invited to claim Disability Support Pension. This includes where the job seeker is terminally ill.

See Granting Disability Support Pension (DSP) (manifest).

4

Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) referral + Read more ...

Consider if an ESAt referral is required to assess the job seeker's work capacity and support needs.

An ESAt should be considered if the job seeker does not have a current ESAt, or their circumstances have changed since their last ESAt, and available information indicates they:

  • may have an ongoing partial capacity to work, because they are unable to work at least 30 hours per week within the next 2 years
  • cannot be granted a temporary incapacity exemption and may have a temporary reduced work capacity of less than 30 hours per week
  • may benefit from referral to Disability Employment Services

5

Special circumstances (major personal crisis) exemption + Read more ...

If a temporary incapacity exemption cannot be granted for a job seeker in a COVID-19 area with reduced or suspended requirements, a special circumstances (major personal crisis) exemption may be granted for the period of the medical certificate, up to a maximum of 13 weeks. This includes where the:

  • job seeker is able to do some work of at least 8 hours per week
  • incapacity for work is clearly not temporary

Job seekers are not eligible for an incapacity exemption if their incapacity for work is mainly because of their drug or alcohol dependency/misuse. In this case, a special circumstances (major personal crisis) exemption may be considered due to COVID-19, if special circumstances not wholly or predominantly due to misuse of drugs or alcohol mean that job seekers are unable to comply with their requirements.

For more details, see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances.

Job seeker reporting

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Job seeker reporting + Read more ...

Job seekers who reside in COVID-19 areas with reduced or suspended mutual obligation requirements are still required to report but are not required to respond to the activity reporting questions in all reporting channels, i.e., online services, express plus app, IVR and staff facing workflows.

The response to the 'Have you met the requirements in your job plan for this period' question will default to Yes and will not be able to be changed when customer reports online services.

The 'Have you met the requirements in your job plan for this period' question will not be presented to customers reporting via express plus app or IVR.

Negotiating Job Plans over the phone for Centrelink managed job seekers

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Verbal approval of Job Plans for Centrelink managed job seekers + Read more ...

Verbal agreement can be accepted over the phone if the Centrelink managed job seeker:

  • negotiated their Job Plan over the phone, and
  • is unable to agree to their Job Plan online

If they can agree to their Job Plan online or in person (via signature), they must do so.

Can the job seeker agree to their Job Plan:

  • in person, go to Step 3
  • through their online account while they are on the phone, go to Step 3
  • over the phone, go to Step 2

2

Accepting and recording verbal agreement + Read more ...

  • Discuss the Job Plan in detail with the job seeker to make sure they understand the terms and their rights and obligations
  • Return to the Job Plan workflow to finalise the Job Plan
  • Select 'Yes' to Has the job seeker signed the Job Plan?
  • Key the date of the verbal agreement in the required field
  • Create a DOC using the Job Plan Verbal Negotiation Fast Note. Record the following:
    • Centrelink managed job seeker - a Job Plan has been negotiated by phone and agreed verbally under approved special arrangements in place due to the COVID-19 pandemic
    • Contact Method - Phone
    • Job Plan has been discussed - Y/N
    • Service Officer has explained obligations and customer agrees to activity/ies in Job Plan - Y/N
    • Reason job seeker cannot view/agree to Job Plan online; e.g. no internet access
    • Job Plan has been verbally accepted - Y/N
    • Job Plan mailed to job seeker - Y/N
    • Was an interpreter used - Y/N

3

Complete the update + Read more ...

  • Finalise as usual
  • Print locally and mail to the job seeker the Job Plan and 'Information you need to know'. A translation displays as a link if a preferred written language other than English is on the Other Contact Details (OCD) screen
  • The job seeker does not need to return a signed copy

4

Job seeker refuses to agree or needs 'think time' + Read more ...

If they have concerns or say they need to think more about the terms of the Job Plan, discuss those issues with them.

If they still want to think more about the terms, offer them more time. For example, they may want to consult with a third party before signing.

Follow the process in Table 3 of Negotiating Job Plans.

Employment Services Assessments (ESAt)

Table 6

Step

Action

1

ESAts + Read more ...

Due to the impacts of COVID-19, all ESAts will be booked by the Assessment Services and Smart Centres Coordination Branch and completed via phone or file assessment.

Before actioning a referral, make sure all available medical/sensitive information is scanned and attached to the record.

2

Service Officer action + Read more ...

  • Do not book ESAt appointments for job seekers via the Online Diary
  • All ESAt referrals and requests for ESAt appointments must be made via:
    • Fast Note
    • the Activity List (AL) screen in Customer First
  • The Assessment Services team will book and complete the ESAt
  • All ESAts will be completed via phone or file assessment
  • The Services Australia ESAt referral criteria remain unchanged

3

Requesting an ESAt referral + Read more ...

  • Use the ESAt referral activity process to manually recategorise the activity to ZIAM_ASB_ESAT_APT where there is an ESAt referral activity (JSR/UPD) on the Activity List (AL) screen
  • Select ESAt Appointment Required Fast Note in Workspace
  • Where the Medical Assessment Team (MAT) identify an ESAt referral is required following a Disability Support Pension rejection:
    • use the Fast Note workflow: Disabilities > Claims > Post MAT ESAt referral required
    • make sure the job seeker has an active Job Seeker Identification Number (JSID) and there are no unfinalised JCA/ESAt reports before completing the Fast Note
  • Tell job seekers their ESAt will be completed via phone or file assessment at any time. If a phone assessment is required, the job seeker will be contacted by an Assessor on their primary contact number:
    • SMS notification before the call will be sent if the job seeker has accepted this form of contact
    • the call will show as a private number on their phone
  • Contact the Assessment Services National Administration Team (ASNAT) if the job seeker:
    • does not have a telephone, or cannot communicate via telephone, or
    • is in the office and immediate advice is required

Centrelink managed customers - Quarterly Participation Interviews (QTI)

Table 12

Step

Action

1

Quarterly Participation Interviews (QTI) + Read more ...

Centrelink managed job seekers in COVID-19 areas with reduced or suspended mutual obligation requirements are not required to attend the Quarterly Participation Interview (QTI).

The system will:

  • auto COMplete existing components, and
  • exclude customers in the exclusion area from being profiled

When the postcode/state has been removed from an area with reduced or suspended mutual obligation requirements, the system will:

  • find customers that were previously excluded because of COVID-19, and
  • re-run service profiling to put them back into their 12 weekly cycle

Service Delivery will be provided a list of CRNs of auto completed components for action.

2

Impacted Quarterly Participation Interviews components + Read more ...

The impacted Quarterly Participation Interview (QTI) components are:

  • SC707 - QTI Initial Phone
  • SC708 - QTI Initial Face to Face
  • SC710 - QTI Ongoing Phone
  • SC711 - QTI Ongoing Face to Face

Exemption ending notifications + Read more ...

Centrelink managed job seekers in COVID-19 areas with reduced or suspended mutual obligation requirements won't be sent a notification to advise that their exemption is ending in the next 7 days.
