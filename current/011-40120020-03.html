Mandatory bank details check

Table 1: this table describes how to check a customer's bank details. The mandatory bank details check:

  • should occur after the customer's enquiry has been identified and resolved
  • does not apply to PBS customer interactions

Step

Action

1

Identify customer enquiry + Read more ...

Complete a mandatory security check with the customer. See Authenticating a Medicare customer.

Ask the customer to provide their password, if they have one. See Customer password for Medicare enquiries.

Has the customer's enquiry been identified and resolved?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, see procedures relevant to the customer's enquiry, for example change of address for Medicare. Note: return to this process to complete the mandatory bank details check

2

Confirmation of bank details + Read more ...

Customer bank details must be confirmed during every customer interaction and relevant comments keyed in CDMS Personal.

Ask the customer if they have confirmed their bank details recently (in the last 3 months).

If the customer:

  • has recently confirmed their details, in CDMS Personal add the comment 'Customer advises banking details are up to date'. Procedure ends here
  • has not recently confirmed their details, ask if they would like to do so now:
    • Yes, go to Step 3
    • No, they will update later. Provide self-service information, add the comment 'mandatory bank details check was completed and self-service options were offered' in CDMS Personal. Note: do not select CLAIMANT REFUSAL in the EFT Details on CDMS. Go to Step 4
  • refuses to provide bank details:
    • In CDMS, access the customer's personal record and select CLAIMANT REFUSAL as the reason for no EFT Details. Add the comment 'mandatory bank details check was completed and self-service options were offered'
    • Advise the customer of self-servicing options, go to Step 4

Note: if the claimant refusal option has been previously selected:

  • a mandatory bank details check must still be performed, and
  • comment added stating this was performed

3

Check bank details + Read more ...

Ask the customer to verbally provide the following bank account details:

  • BSB
  • Account number, and
  • Account name

If the same account details are recorded in CDMS, in CDMS Personal add the comment ‘Up to date bank details confirmed’.

To add or update bank details, go to the Bank details provided verbally table on Store, confirm and end date bank account details for Medicare EFT payments.

If the customer does not have their new/updated bank details available, provide self-service options and go to Step 4.

4

Advise self service options + Read more ...

Customer can confirm and update bank account details using:

  • Medicare online account via MyGov
  • Express Plus Medicare mobile app

See Using Medicare self service for more information.

Note:

  • In CDMS Personal add the comment 'mandatory bank details check was completed and self-service options were offered'
  • Do not select CLAIMANT REFUSAL in the EFT Details on CDMS

Make an outbound call for Medicare Public

Table 2: this table describes how to make outbound calls for Medicare Public and leave voicemails where appropriate.

Step

Action

1

Outbound contact successful + Read more ...

When the call is answered and it is clear it is the customer, the Service Officer advises their name and that they are calling from Services Australia - Program (for example, Medicare).

Service Officers:

  • must perform a mandatory security check with the customer. See Authenticating a Medicare customer
  • must ask the customer to provide their exact password, if they have one. See Customer password for Medicare enquiries
  • tell the customer that the call may be recorded or listened to for quality and coaching purposes

Note: Service Officers must not disclose the reason for the call or identify the team they work with before the security check is completed. This could reveal the type of business the person has with the agency and impact their privacy.

If the customer does not wish to complete the security check:

  • Give them the Medicare General phone number and advise them to ring back
  • Add a comment in CDMS Personal and the relevant PaNDA work item stating 'Attempted to contact <FULL NAME> to <REASON>. Customer will return call. <PaNDA ID>'

2

Outbound contact unsuccessful and leaving a voicemail + Read more ...

  • When an outbound call is unsuccessful, staff may leave a voice message regardless of whether the customer’s name is used in the answering recorded message
  • Service Officers must:
    • not disclose the reason for the call or identify the team they work with. This could reveal the type of business the person has with the agency and impact their privacy
    • add a comment in CDMS Personal and the relevant PaNDA work item stating 'Attempted to contact <FULL NAME> to <REASON>. Voicemail left. <PaNDA ID>'

Manage a PBS Authority call

Table 3: this table describes how to manage a Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) Authority call.

Step

Action

1

Standard greeting + Read more ...

When answering the call, the Service Officer can use any of the greeting phrases that end with their name, for example:

  • Hello, this is <insert name>
  • Good morning or afternoon, my name is <insert name>
  • Hi, you are speaking with <insert name>
  • PBS Authorities this is <insert name>
  • Welcome to PBS this is <insert name>

For PBS Authority calls, the following question can be included in the introduction:

  • Can I start with your prescriber number?

Note: it is no longer a requirement to state PBS Authorities in the call greeting.

2

Acknowledge prescriber's request + Read more ...

'Certainly, could I start with the prescriber number?'

Confirm prescriber's full (first and last) name if not previously provided.

Note:

  • the name must match the name shown on the Online PBS Authorities (OPA) screen. If the prescriber states they are known by another name such as their middle name, staff must explain they need to provide the name they have registered with Ahpra
  • we cannot assume a prescriber’s gender based on the sound of their voice

Staff must not proceed with the authority application if the prescriber does not provide their full name as part of the security check.

3

Permission to ask questions + Read more ...

'May I have the patient's Medicare number?'

Complete the Authority request.

For further information on authority requests, see Online Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) Authorities System (OPA System).

4

Complete the call + Read more ...

  • 'Thank you for calling'
  • 'You're welcome, thanks for your call' (if doctor has thanked you)
