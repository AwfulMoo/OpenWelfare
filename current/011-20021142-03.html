This page has how to assess claims notated with text and how to handle enquiries about Multiple Echocardiogram Services Rule (MESR).

Assessing claims notated with text

Table 1

Item

Description

1

The claim has the text/notation ‘non-contiguous body areas’ (or to that effect) + Read more ...

Is the claim for ultrasound (Category 5, Group I1) items that could be for non-contiguous body areas (for example, 55028 and 55036)?

  • Yes, the claim can be paid using PI 42. The system will apply the DIMSR and calculate the benefit amounts for each item
  • No, ‘non-contiguous body areas’ is not acceptable text. Assess the claim based on the assessing rules and information for the items being claimed. The DIMSR applies across different types of diagnostic imaging services

2

The claim has the text/notation ’contiguous body area…’ or something similar + Read more ...

Examples of acceptable text include:

  • ‘contiguous body area with different set-up requirements’
  • ‘contiguous body area different set-up’
  • ‘contiguous different set-up’
  • Anything else that indicates the health professional knows they are contiguous body areas but that there were different set-up requirements

Is the claim for ultrasound (Category 5, Group I1) items that could be for non-contiguous body areas (for example, 55028 and 55032)?

  • Yes, the claim can be paid using PI 42. The system will apply the DIMSR and calculate the benefits for each item
  • No, ‘contiguous body area with different set-up’ is not acceptable text. Assess the claim based on assessing rules and information for the items being claimed. The DIMSR applies across different types of diagnostic imaging services

3

The claim has text indicating separate times/attendances + Read more ...

Generally only 1 ultrasound service can be claimed per attendance.

If a claim is notated with separate times (for example, 9:00 am and 3:00 pm), the claim can be paid. The claim system will apply any relevant multiple services rules to all items claimed for the same day.

Note: a separate attendance is generally accepted as a minimum of 3 hours after the first attendance. If claims indicate that both services were provided at the same time, reject the item with the lower schedule fee following system instructions.

Handle enquiries about MESR

Table 2: This table describes how to handle enquiries about the MESR fee reduction from health professionals and/or their staff.

Item

Description

1

Question: Why have my benefits been reduced? + Read more ...

Answer: The Multiple Echocardiogram Services Rule came into effect in the Medicare system on 30 August 2020.

It reduces the schedule fees of stress and rest echocardiograms when they are claimed on the same day.

The echocardiogram with the highest schedule fee will stay at 100%, and the other(s) with the lower fees are reduced to 60%.

The benefit rate is then calculated based on the new schedule fees at:

  • 85% for services provided out of hospital
  • 75% for services provided in hospital

More information about the rule can be found on MBS Online (see Resources page for a link).

2

Question: Why have my benefits been paid differently between August and September (or later) 2020? + Read more ...

Answer: The legislation that supports the new multiple services rule came into effect on 1 August 2020, but the Medicare payment system changes were implemented on 30 August 2020.

Claims processed between 1 and 29 August won’t have the rule applied by the Medicare payment system, but may be subject to post-payment audit by the Department of Health and Aged Care.

Claims with a date of service from 1 August that are lodged from 30 August will have the rule applied as per the legislation.

3

Question: How will I know if my claims have had the rule applied? + Read more ...

Answer: Your Medicare statement of benefits will have reason code:

  • 440 if only the Multiple Echocardiogram Services Rule applies
  • 441 if both the Multiple Echocardiogram Services Rule and the Diagnostic Imaging Multiple Services Rule applies
