ABSTUDY payability overseas

Table 1: This table describes how to determine if ABSTUDY payments are payable overseas. Portions of this process can be completed by ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing staff only.

Step

Action

1

ABSTUDY customer advises plans to travel overseas + Read more ...

While conducting the portability interview, the Service Officer will be able to determine if the customer has to contact when they have returned to Australia, and discuss this before the customer's departure.

Check if the customer has employment income and, if yes, use the Earnings and Reporting workflow to allow changes to the event date on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen. For further information, see Recording and correcting employment income details.

The customer may contact after using the Travelling outside of Australia service within their Centrelink online account. If the online service has already keyed the absence, details are recorded in a DOC. The online service may refer the customer to contact to have their absence keyed, or explore further portability for their situation.

The following questions will determine if a Service Officer can complete a portability interview or must refer the information to a processing team or Centrelink International Services (CIS).

Note: the Portability Script - Departures and Returns cannot be used for assessing portability entitlement for ABSTUDY.

2

Is the customer leaving Australia to live in another country? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Customer is leaving Australia to live in another country + Read more ...

To be eligible for ABSTUDY, the customer must reside in Australia. Where a customer is departing Australia to live in another country, their eligibility for ABSTUDY ceases from the date of departure (or from an earlier date for a qualification reason if the student ceased study before leaving).

Is the Service Officer in an ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Team?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, send an ABSTUDY action request Fast Note to the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing team. Select: Auto Text >Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices >Update>ABSTUDY action request>Confirm.

Include the following information in the body of the Fast Note:

  • Date of departure
  • Departure is permanent
  • Destination/country (if the customer is travelling to multiple destinations, record 'various or multiple')
  • New address details if provided

If the customer has a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC), the LIC must be cancelled separately.

Procedure ends here.

4

Recording a departure to live in another country - ABSTUDY staff only + Read more ...

  • Record the change of address on Address Details (AD) screen if the customer has provided new address details. Note: it is not mandatory for the customer to provide an address outside Australia
  • Go to the Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen:
  • key the date of departure in the Event Date: field
  • key the destination country in the Country: field. Key ‘XT’ if the customer is travelling to multiple destinations or if the destination country is not provided
  • leave the Reason:, End date: and Ext Rsn: fields blank
  • select Continue
  • Update the Country of Residence (CRES) screen
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and check payment will cease from the date of departure for a residence related reason. Note: if the customer ceased study before departure, payment may cease from an earlier date with a different reason
  • Finalise the activity. Do not suppress the auto advice
  • Explain to the customer their payment will cancel and advise them of the requirement to reclaim if they return to reside in Australia
  • If the student requests written confirmation of the decision, issue a Pre Departure Interview - Portability Decision (XOB101) letter. Note: an automatic cancellation letter will be sent, it is normally not necessary to send an XOB101
  • Record details of decision on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

5

Does the customer continue to meet the ABSTUDY eligibility criteria? + Read more ...

If the customer is (or will be) no longer a full-time or concessional study load student, cancel the customer's payment from:

  • the date of departure, or
  • the date the customer ceases to meet the study requirements and general ABSTUDY eligibility criteria if this is prior to the date of departure

See Cancellation of ABSTUDY payments

Is the customer still qualified for ABSTUDY?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, complete the actions required for cancellation. See Cancellation of ABSTUDY payments. Even if the departure is temporary, it is not accepted the customer still meets the study qualifications for ABSTUDY benefits. Record the overseas absence as well. See Step 2 in Table 3

6

Reason for departure + Read more ...

If the customer is an Australian Apprentice or full-time student or concessional study load student planning to travel overseas:

  • to undertake work or activities as part of an apprenticeship, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • to undertake studies at a secondary level, see Step 2 in Table 2
  • to undertake studies at a secondary non-school, tertiary or Masters and Doctorate level, see Step 4 in Table 2

In all other cases, establish if the customer is travelling for an approved reason.

For assistance with this decision, see:

  • Overseas absences for Australian Defence Force Reserves
  • Overseas absences for acute family crisis
  • Overseas absences for eligible medical reasons

Note: evidence is required to support the reason for travelling.

If a customer has indicated their overseas travel meets the initial criteria for an approved reason, refer the case to Centrelink International Services (CIS) for assessment.

Note: CIS approval to continue payment for a specific negotiated period will usually be 2 to 3 weeks. As it would be an unusual circumstance requiring a customer's attendance overseas for the maximum portability period, it is rarely granted.

Does the customer have an approved reason for travelling overseas?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, ABSTUDY is not payable. See Step 1 in Table 3

7

Temporary absence for approved reason + Read more ...

Absence is temporary and is for an approved reason (other than overseas study/work as an Australian Apprentice or student)

  • Determine if the customer has to contact when they have returned to Australia and discuss this before the customer's departure to correctly assess their ongoing entitlements
  • Advise the customer they may meet the requirement for payment overseas but they must speak to Centrelink International Services (CIS) for a decision on their ongoing portability
  • Contact CIS with the customer's details. The customer can then talk directly to the CIS Service Officer
  • Transfer the call to CIS, and explain the customer's circumstances. The CIS Service Officer will take over and complete the portability interview with the customer. Once transferred, complete a DOC advising what action has been taken (that is, the call has been referred to CIS and details of travel, especially the reason)
  • If it is not possible to refer the customer immediately to CIS: use the Callback/Actdoc guided procedure to create a Callback:
    • Select Office: WTW
    • Select Type: CALLBACK
    • Select Category: Nosub
    • Select Item Name: Manual Callback Template
    • Record the travel details including dates, reason for travel and current telephone contact details
    • Once the Callback is created, manually assign keyword 'CALLRPG'
  • Advise the customer an officer from CIS will contact them as soon as possible

Does the customer have supporting documentation?

  • Yes, where possible, Service Officers can scan the document onto the customer's record and store the original documents
  • No, advise the customer they may be required to provide supporting documentation

Procedure ends here.

ABSTUDY approved overseas study or work

Table 2: This table describes how to determine when ABSTUDY students or apprentices can be paid for approved study or work while they are overseas.

Step

Action

1

Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

Will the customer continue to meet the eligibility criteria for ABSTUDY and their Commonwealth Registration Number remain current while overseas?

  • Yes, the customer may be paid ABSTUDY for the duration of activities such as working or training as an Australian Apprentice while overseas. Go to Step 6
  • No, the customer does not qualify for ABSTUDY assistance while overseas, even if the departure is temporary. Cancel their ABSTUDY payment from the date they last qualified as an Australian Apprentice. See Step 2 in Table 3

2

Secondary school students studying overseas + Read more ...

ABSTUDY is payable for the duration of approved overseas study if:

  • the education institution at which the student is studying is equivalent to an approved education institution in Australia, and
  • the course the student is studying is at secondary level and meets the study load provisions set down for secondary school students

Written evidence required

Customers must provide proof of the reason for their absence. Customers must also advise travel dates so the period of approved portability, including any additional travel time can be assessed, and, an overseas contact address.

Keep a copy of the documentation on file.

Can the student provide the evidence as described above?

  • Yes, the student qualifies for assistance while studying overseas. Go to Step 3
  • No, ABSTUDY payments will stop when the student leaves Australia. See Step 1 in Table 3

3

Secondary school student approved to study overseas + Read more ...

The periods for which ABSTUDY assistance is available for secondary school students studying overseas are, if the student:

  • is participating in an international educational exchange programme which is recognised by the relevant State or Territory education authority, the period of study at the overseas institution
  • accompanies a parent/guardian who is employed by the Commonwealth of Australia (for example, a member of the Australian Defence Force), the period of the overseas posting
  • is studying overseas for any other reason, ABSTUDY entitlement is for a maximum of 12 months from the first date of attendance at the overseas education institution
    Note: if a student does not return to Australia, ABSTUDY assistance ceases from the end of the payment period that includes the anniversary of the first date of attendance. Where a student does return to Australia but returns overseas to recommence secondary study, the 12 month period will recommence from the date the customer recommences secondary study at the overseas education institution

Rate and entitlements:

  • Schooling students may be assessed as independent or qualify for the Away From Home rate of ABSTUDY. This is only if their circumstances overseas would meet the requirements for these rates if the student was in Australia
  • Similarly, they may qualify for Fares Allowance if their circumstances overseas would entitle them to Fares Allowance if they experienced these conditions in Australia. Note: this refers to travel within the overseas country. Fares Allowance to travel between Australia and the overseas country is not available

Go to Step 6.

4

Secondary non-school, tertiary or Masters and Doctorate level student studying overseas + Read more ...

ABSTUDY is payable for the duration of approved overseas study or work placement if the following criteria are met:

  • The student is enrolled on a full-time or concessional study load basis in an approved course at an Australian higher education institution, which
    • approves the overseas study or work placement, and
    • will credit the results of the study or work placement at the overseas education or placement institution towards the approved course
  • The student meets the study load requirements in respect of the approved Australian course
  • A work placement (if undertaken) forms part of the student’s full-time Australian course, regardless of whether credit is given for the placement

Customers must provide proof of the reason for their absence. Customers must also advise travel dates so the period of approved portability, including any additional travel time can be assessed, and, an overseas contact address.

Keep a copy of the documentation on file.

Can the student provide the evidence as described above?

  • Yes, the student qualifies for assistance while studying overseas. Go to Step 5
  • No, ABSTUDY payments will stop when the student leaves Australia. See Step 1 in Table 3

5

Secondary non-schooling or tertiary level students approved to study overseas + Read more ...

ABSTUDY assistance is available for secondary non-schooling or tertiary level students studying overseas for the duration of the approved study.

Rate and entitlements

  • The student is automatically approved for the Away From Home rate of ABSTUDY. This must be keyed on the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen
  • The student may qualify for Fares Allowance only if their circumstances overseas would entitle them to Fares Allowance if they experienced these conditions in Australia. Note: this refers to travel within the overseas country. Fares Allowance to travel between Australia and the overseas country is not available

Go to Step 6.

6

Customer is leaving Australia for approved overseas study or work + Read more ...

Has the customer already been granted an approved overseas study or work period and are they leaving Australia again within 6 weeks to resume the same activity?

  • Yes, no new decision is required when they depart again to continue their overseas study or work. Example: a student receives a 6 month approved study period overseas and returns after 3 months for a 2 week break in Australia. When they depart again they are still within the original approved period, so no new portability decision is required. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Combining holiday and study + Read more ...

Is the customer intending to combine a holiday overseas with their approved study or work period?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3 table

8

Customer is combining a holiday overseas with approved period + Read more ...

If the holiday is:

  • before starting study/work, payment will stop from the date of departure from Australia and resumes when the overseas study or work starts, as long as it is not cancelled in the meantime. To avoid a gap in payment, encourage a student to depart Australia within a few days of their overseas study/work start date
  • after the end of the approved study/work period, payment will stop when the study/work stops

Note: it may be necessary to check approved study dates (Education Course History (EDCH) screen) to ensure continuation of payment during and following an approved overseas study period. If payment has been cancelled, it will not be possible to restore and continue payment.

The Resources page contains more information and examples on holidays combined with approved study overseas.

See Step 1 in Table 3.

Completing the portability interview and coding overseas absence

Table 3: This table describes how to complete portability interviews and coding required for ABSTUDY students going overseas.

Step

Action

1

Completing the portability interview + Read more ...

  • Ensure students and Australian Apprentices intending to study or work overseas, are aware that if the departure date changes by more than 3 days, the approved reason for travel will no longer apply and payments will cease on departure. The customer must advise the change and the reason for travel must be reassessed
  • Ask the customer if they have a contact address or phone number while outside Australia. If they do, check if they want their mail to go to their overseas address or continue to their normal Australian address. Note: ensure the customer is aware payments may be delayed if any forms are not returned
  • Ensure the customer is registered for letters online which will prevent any delay in receiving correspondence from Services Australia
  • Update any changes in rent or accommodation details
  • Ask if they wish to nominate an agent to act on their behalf while they are overseas. If so, record the details on a DOC
  • Advise that if they remain qualified to receive them, add-ons can remain payable
  • Ask the customer if any income or asset details have changed. If so, update the system with the new information
  • Check the Future Activity List (FAL) screen for any reviews due before the expected date of return. Action and finalise if possible, or tell the customer they must reply to any mail from the agency while they are overseas
  • Advise continuation of payment on their return to Australia or to contact the agency if their plans change or they are delayed overseas
  • Advise contact details for CIS for contact with the agency while overseas
  • Check if the customer has any further queries

2

ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Team + Read more ...

Is the Service Officer in an ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Team?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, send an ABSTUDY action request Fast Note to the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing team. Select: Auto Text >Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices >Update>ABSTUDY action request>Confirm

Include the following information in the body of the Fast Note:

  • Overseas payability assessment required
  • Date of departure
  • Start date of the approved study or work placement
  • End date of the approved study or work placement
  • Date of return
  • Destination/country
  • New address details if provided
  • Letter from current educational institution to support overseas study or work placement will be provided prior to departure

Note: students who travel overseas to undertake approved study or work placements must provide written advice from their Australian educational institution or State Training Authority confirming proof of the reason for their absence. Customers must also advise travel dates so the period of approved portability, including any additional travel time can be assessed, and, an overseas contact address.

Procedure ends here.

3

Code overseas absence + Read more ...

  • Record the change of address on Address Details (AD) screen, if the customer has provided new address details. Note: it is not mandatory for the customer to provide an address outside Australia
  • Record the overseas telephone number and/or email address if provided
  • Go to the Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen

Is the customer travelling for overseas study or work and have they provided acceptable supporting documentation?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, they are not travelling for overseas study or reason/evidence does not support payment under OFS provisions:
  • key the date of departure in the Event Date: field
  • key the destination country in the Country field:. Key 'XT' if the customer is travelling to multiple destinations, the destination country is unknown, or the customer is travelling outside Australia on a cruise
  • leave the Portability Reason: and End date: fields blank
  • select the Continue button

Go to Step 6

4

Travelling for overseas study or work + Read more ...

Customer is travelling for overseas study (and has provided acceptable proof of the reason for their absence).

  • On the Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen:
    • key the Source: and DOR: fields
    • key the start date of approved portability period in the Event Date: field (often the date of departure, include any additional travel time)
    • key the destination country in the Country of Location: field
    • key 'OFS' in the Portability Reason: field
    • key the last day of approved portability period in the Portability End Date: field
    • Press [Enter]

Note: the approved study period may start several days (or weeks) after departure. If there is a holiday period before the approved study, reflect this from departure date and key 'OFS' on a new line from the approved start date. There is no additional travel time allowed where study follows a holiday or a holiday follows study

Is the customer holidaying before commencing overseas study/work?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Customer holidaying before overseas study + Read more ...

On the RSCD screen:

  • key the date of departure in the Event Date: field
  • key the destination country in the Country: field. Key ‘XT’ if the customer is:
    • travelling to multiple destinations, or
    • the destination country is unknown, or
    • travelling outside Australia on a cruise
  • leave the Portability Reason:, End date: and Ext Rsn: fields blank

If the approved study period is after the departure date but within 13 weeks of departure, payment should automatically restore on the OFS start date, otherwise the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Team must manually restore payment.

6

The customer is a secondary school student receiving ABSTUDY board instalment payments + Read more ...

Secondary school students who attend a boarding school or hostel, whose allowances are paid to the boarding institution and depart overseas for a non-approved reason, are not entitled to ABSTUDY for the period they are outside Australia, regardless if they commence study within the first 3 weeks of a term. ABSTUDY is only payable for the period they are in Australia.

Prior to 1 January 2019, where the boarding school or hostel the student is attending has a no refund policy, no overpayment will be raised against the boarding school or hostel, even though the student is not eligible for ABSTUDY during the period they were outside Australia. Where the secondary student was receiving a residual payment, an overpayment may be raised for the period they are outside Australia for a non-approved reason.

7

Finalise coding + Read more ...

  • Finalise the coding on the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Return to the RSCD screen, on the blank line key the expected date of return to Australia in the Event Date: field and key 'AU' in the Country: field
  • Leave the Portability Reason:, End Date: and Ext Rsn: fields blank
  • Key the Source: and DOR: fields if required
  • Select Continue

Go to the AR screen and finalise the activity. Note: the reassessment will occur once the customer actually leaves Australia and the immigration datalink verifies the departure.

If the customer requests written confirmation of the decision, issue a Pre Departure Interview - Portability Decision (XOB101) letter.

Is the customer a secondary school student approved to study overseas?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, procedure ends here

8

Secondary school student + Read more ...

Update the education institution code on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen to Institute Code 4B551 for the period they are studying at the overseas education institution.

Is the secondary school student approved to study overseas for a reason other than accompanying a parent employed by the Australian Government or participating in an approved international exchange programme?

Yes, as the student's 12 month portability period recommences every time they return to Australia, code a manual review on the Review Registration (RVR) screen for 14 days before the approved portability period coded on the RSCD screen

When the review activity matures, check if the customer has returned to Australia and since departed overseas to recommence secondary study. If so, the student's portability period on the RSCD screen must be extended for another 12 months from the date the customer recommenced secondary study overseas

No, procedure ends here
