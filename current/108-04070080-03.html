How to assess customers residing in caravans, park homes and boats

Step

Action

1

Park or village promoted as a retirement village + Read more ...

Does the customer live in a park or village that is promoted as a retirement village? Note: this can include parks with non-moveable homes.

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Check the type of accommodation + Read more ...

Check if the customer's accommodation is actually a retirement village or a park or lifestyle village which promotes itself as a retirement village.

Check the written contract or agreement entered into by the customer.

In some cases, residential premises with similar functions to retirement villages can be treated as if they were retirement villages. This classification can only be made by a delegate in the Seniors and Means Test Branch in the Department of Social Services (DSS).

  • If it is a retirement village, see Retirement villages. Procedure ends here.
  • If it is a park or lifestyle village promoted as a retirement village (not covered by state retirement village legislation), advise customer the park is treated like a caravan park, not like a retirement village. These types of facilities may have mobile or non-moveable dwellings
  • If unsure, refer the case via the Level 2 Policy Helpdesks - Online Query Form for a decision.

3

Ownership of caravan, park home or boat + Read more ...

Is the dwelling (campervan, caravan, park home or boat), the customer lives in, owned by (or being purchased by) the customer, and do they have reasonable security of tenure?

  • Yes, go to Step 5.
  • No, and the customer:
    • Does not own the land or marina berth the dwelling is situated on, go to Step 4.
    • Owns the land or marina berth the dwelling is situated on, this is assessed on a case by case basis. Send details via the Level 2 Policy Helpdesks - Online Query Form for a decision on how to assess.

4

Customer does not own land or marina berth and is not a homeowner + Read more ...

Update change of address.

Request proof of rent and advise customer they may be eligible for Rent Assistance (RA). RA can be paid for rental of the dwelling plus any site/mooring fees paid.

For more information on updating the AC screen and requesting rent verification, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA) or Rent Assistance (RA) for travellers.

Was the customer previously a homeowner?

  • Yes, go to Step 10.
  • No, record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here.

5

Left home temporarily to travel + Read more ...

Has the customer left their principal home temporarily (for less than 12 months) to travel in a campervan, mobile home, caravan or a boat that they own?

  • Yes, go to Step 6.
  • No, advise the customer they are classed as a homeowner and the homeowner (lower) assets limits apply. The value of the campervan, caravan, mobile home, or boat is an exempt asset. Note: if the exempt asset was previously coded as an assessable asset on the Other Assets (OAS) screen, the asset value and ownership need to be updated to zero. Go to Step 7.

6

Temporary vacation of principal home + Read more ...

Customer is a homeowner of their principal residence they have temporarily vacated. Rent Assistance (RA) is not payable for the first 12 months of absence. For more information, see Rent Assistance (RA) for travellers.

The value of the dwelling (campervan, caravan, mobile home, or boat) is an asset.

If the customer is absent for more than 12 months, the home is then classed as an asset as it is no longer the principal home. The customer may then be eligible for RA for the site/mooring fees paid for their dwelling, which would then be classified as their principal home. See:

  • Temporary vacation of principal home
  • Rent Assistance (RA) for travellers

Procedure ends here.

7

Dwelling situated on land or marina berth the customer owns + Read more ...

Is the customer's dwelling (campervan, caravan, mobile home or boat) situated on land or at a marina berth owned by the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Customer owns land/mooring + Read more ...

Advise the customer the land around the dwelling (up to two hectares used for private purposes) or the marina berth is also an exempt asset.

The customer is not entitled to Rent Assistance (RA).

On the Accommodation (AC) screen, code 'HOM' (fully owns home, purchasing own home, or owns jointly with their partner) in the Home Ownership: field

For more information, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA).

Go to Step 10.

9

Customer does not own land/marina berth + Read more ...

Advise customer that Rent Assistance (RA) can be paid for fees paid for the use of a site or the right to moor a vessel. They need to provide proof of fees.

Update change of address.

On the Accommodation (AC) screen, code:

  • 'HOM' (fully owns home, purchasing own home or owns jointly with partner) in Home Ownership: field
  • 'SIT' (site fees) or 'MOO' (mooring fees) in Type of Rent: field

For full AC coding instructions, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA).

Record details on a DOC.

10

If the customer was previously a homeowner, follow-up permanent vacation of principal home + Read more ...

  • assessing income from real estate and timeshare
