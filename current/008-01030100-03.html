This page contains information to assist with registering or cancelling registration for alternative letter formats for a customer with a disability or medical condition affecting their vision.

On this page:

Reviewing customer details

Fillable forms

Reviewing customer details

Table 1: This table describes how to register or cancel a registration for alternative letter formats for a customer with a disability or medical condition affecting their vision.

Step

Action

1

Customer contacts about the alternative letters service + Read more ...

If the customer asks to:

  • participate in the service, go to Step 2
  • stop receiving letters in an alternative format, go to Step 10

2

Prerequisite for service + Read more ...

To be eligible to participate in this service a customer must have a disability or medical condition affecting their vision. Customers whose payment is suspended can also be considered for the service.

For guidance and enquiries, use Office Locator to contact the Accessible Letters Service, Canberra Smart Centre.

If a customer has a disability or medical condition affecting their vision, this will generally be recorded on the Medical Condition (MC) screen. If this is not recorded, it is not appropriate to request the customer to provide evidence of their disability or medical condition. In these cases, advice from the customer or nominee is sufficient to register the customer for the Accessible Letters Service. Do not record the medical condition or disability on the MC screen, unless it is supported by medical evidence.

Does the customer have a disability or medical condition affecting their vision?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 10

3

Advise customer of alternative format options + Read more ...

To help customers make an informed decision about accepting the service, tell them:

  • if they access this service, they must agree to have their postal address recorded as the Accessible Letters Manager’s postal address for administrative purposes. This is the only way Centrelink can identify and intercept a customer's mail for conversion to the customer's preferred format
  • after letters have been identified and converted, the original copies and reformatted letters are sent to the customer's residential or nominated postal address

Explain the alternative format options available:

  • large font (Arial size 18 pt). Go to Step 4
  • braille (can take up to 2 weeks for conversion through Vision Australia Foundation). Go to Step 5
  • phone. Go to Step 6
  • compact disc (CD) (customer must have access to a screen reader). Go to Step 7
  • fillable forms, go to Table 2

4

Large font + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • the original unformatted letter and the converted letter will be sent to them
  • that the best efforts will be made to contact them by phone if the letter has information that is considered urgent, important or affects their payment. For example, upcoming reviews, forms or appointments
  • if contacted, a receipt number is provided

Go to Step 5.

5

Braille + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • Vision Australia Foundation will convert a copy of the letter to Braille within 2 weeks
  • the original unformatted letter will be sent to them
  • the best efforts will be made to contact them by phone if the letter has information that is considered urgent, important or affects their payment. For example, upcoming reviews, forms or appointments
  • if contacted, a receipt number is provided

Go to Step 6.

6

Telephone + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • they will be contacted by phone during business hours and advised Centrelink has sent them a letter
  • the content of the letter will be read to them
  • if applicable, they will be advised of any due dates, upcoming reviews, forms or appointments
  • a receipt number will be provided
  • the original unformatted letter will be sent to them

Go to Step 7.

7

Compact disc (CD) + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • they must have access to a screen reader on their computer
  • rich text format is used
  • the original unformatted letter and the CD with the letter in rich text format will be sent to them
  • the best efforts will be made to contact them by phone if the letter has information that is considered urgent, important or affects their payment
  • if contacted, a receipt number is provided

Go to Step 8.

8

Choice of service + Read more ...

Does the customer wish to participate in the service?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

9

Updating postal address + Read more ...

Tell the customer only 1 postal address can be recorded with Centrelink.

If the customer insists on maintaining a postal address other than their residential address, record their existing postal address on the Display on Access (DOA) DOC when running the Accessible Letters Fast Note.

Update the customer’s postal address before running the Accessible Letters Fast.

Note: do not change the partner’s address.

To update the postal address in Customer First with the correct details, in the following fields:

  • Suburb or Post code, key details > Continue
  • Street address field, key ‘Care Of’ > Continue to allow 2 address type fields to populate
  • Address line 1, key Accessible Letters Manager
  • Address Line 2, key ‘P.O Box 7788’ (remove ‘Care Of’)
  • Source, key details
  • Receipt Date, key details > Continue and finalise the workflow

If the customer is eligible for a concession card, update their preferred delivery address to ensure the concession card is not sent to the Accessible Letters team.

Once the customer’s address has been updated, In Workspace > Fast Note, search for ‘Accessible letters’ and select the DOA checkbox.

Run the Accessible Letters Service - Customer In Fast Note to record the relevant details.

For customers in receipt of Disability Support Pension (DSP) (Blind) or Age Pension (Blind), use default DOA of BLI.

For all other customers, change DOA reason to SCC.

Changes will be required to the following DOA text:

  • Customer has been included in the Accessible Letters Service
  • Customer's choice is:
    • phone [delete if not applicable]
    • big font [delete if not applicable]
    • compact disk [delete if not applicable]
    • braille [delete if not applicable]
  • Customer's postal address has been changed to:
    • Accessible Letters Manager
      PO Box 7788
      Canberra BC ACT 2610
  • Document Completion is set to No
  • Does customer require their formatted letter to be sent to their own postal address if different to the home address?
    • Yes, postal address is [key address]. Note: if the customer has a partner recorded, ensure Make Partner the Same field is 'No'
    • No, if the customer does not agree to the alternative recording of the postal address, go to Step 10

10

The customer is unable/does not wish to participate in the service + Read more ...

Expire the Display on Access DOC relating to accessible letters and end date the postal address.

In Workspace > Fast Note, search for ‘Accessible letters’.

Use the Fast Note Accessible letters service - Customer not included to record the following details on a DOC:

  • Customer has not been included in the Accessible Letters service
  • Reason:
    • Customer does not have a medical condition or a disability affecting their vision [delete if not applicable]
    • Customer not prepared to change postal address [delete if not applicable]
    • If the customer is ceasing the service, record the reasons given for this
    • Select > Document Completion > Yes, to close the DOC on completion

Use Office Locator to email the Accessible Letters service outlining the reasons for the customer cancelling or declining the service.

Fillable forms

Table 2: This table describes the action to take if customer requests a fillable form.

Step

Action

1

Customer requests a fillable form + Read more ...

If the customer requests a fillable form for:

  • module and forms, go to Step 2
  • Mobility Allowance Review form, go to Step 3

Note: fillable forms cannot be signed electronically.

2

Modules and forms + Read more ...

Fillable Modules and forms can be opened from the Services Australia website.

Search forms by title or form number.

3

Mobility Allowance Review form + Read more ...

An Employment/Training review form is sent to customers in receipt of Mobility Allowance (MOB) when they are selected for review. The form includes information to contact the agency's Disability and Carers line if they would like an electronic version of the review form, which is designed to be used with assistive technology.

Direct customers in the first instance to the Services Australia website where an accessible/electronic version of the form is available.

Customers unable to access website

If customers cannot access the electronic version, email the form to them for completion using their assistive technology.

If a customer asks for an electronic version (MA003Y) to be emailed to them, send an email to the Accessible Letters Team, including:

  • Name
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Customer's email address

Update the email address on the Electronic Messaging and Letters Subscript (EMS) screen.

Create a DOC on the customer's record stating the date the customer contacted and requested an accessible version of the MA003Y.

Tell the customer a fillable form for the Mobility Allowance Review will be sent by email and must be completed, signed, printed and lodged by the due date.
