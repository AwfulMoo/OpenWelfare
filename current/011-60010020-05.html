Script to identify caller on IME enquiry line

For Services Australia to release Medicare card numbers under the secrecy provisions of the Health Insurance Act 1973, callers must answer a series of questions to identify themselves. Staff must also verify the caller's identity prior to the release of information. See the References page for a link to the act.

Note:

  • callers may not always be the pharmacy’s approved person, but they must be able to correctly respond to any questions you ask to verify the caller, including who is the approved person for the pharmacy
  • for a pharmaceutical benefit related query, general practitioners (GP) can only request to have a patient's concessional entitlement status confirmed via Medicare Provider Enquiries line – Option 5 – sub option 2 – Medicare concessional entitlement status

Table 1: this table describes the scripts Service Officers use to identify callers on the Improved Monitoring of Entitlements (IME) for pharmaceutical benefits enquiry line.

Question

Script

Action/Information

1

Welcome to the IME enquiry line, this is <your name>.

or

Welcome to the IME enquiry line, my name is <your name>.

2

Could you please tell me your name?

As part of the authentication process, I need to have your name.

Use the caller's name as appropriate for the rest of the conversation.

If caller refuses to provide name, tell them you are not able to help them.

3

Are you a:

  • Pharmacy?
  • Public hospital?

If the caller is from a pharmacy:

  • Determine if the caller is contacting about Rapid Antigen Tests (RATs)
    • If the caller confirms they are contacting about RAT eligibility, advise them:
      "Services Australia is not able to confirm eligibility for Rapid Antigen Tests over the phone. Patients will need to attend a participating pharmacy either in person, or through their carer/guardian/family member and present their concession card (physical/digital)"
  • If the caller is asking about ‘Find a Patient – HPOS’ online service, go to Table 6
  • If the call is not about RATs and for all other enquiries, go to Table 2

If the caller is from a public hospital:

  • Go to Table 3

Note: IME enquiry line can be used by staff from:

  • Approved pharmacies
  • Public hospitals participating in the Pharmaceutical Reform Agreements.

  • If the caller does not meet the above criteria they are not eligible to be assisted through the IME Enquiry line. Advise the caller they will need to get information through another channel.

Script to answer call from pharmacy on IME enquiry line

Table 2: this table describes the scripts Service Officers use to answer calls from pharmacies or pharmacy staff on the Improved Monitoring of Entitlements (IME) for pharmaceutical benefits enquiry line.

Question

Script

Action/Information

1

What is your pharmacy approval number?

Key the approval number into the RPBC screen.

2

What is the name of your pharmacy?

What is the full address of your pharmacy?

Check the name and address given corresponds to the name and address listed for the approval number on the RPBC screen.

Note: when confirming pharmacy address, 'chemist' can be accepted for 'pharmacy' and 'suite' for 'shop' etc.

Note: if the pharmacy is Pendle Hill Mediadvice Pharmacy, Blooms the Chemist Miller or Chemist Warehouse Parramatta go to question 3.

If Service Officer suspects the call is fraudulent, complete the Report Suspected Fraud form.

3

Do not prompt for unique code and password.

We can’t verify your details at this time, please call back later.

Caller must provide both unique code and password without prompting from the Service Officer. Please refer to the SharePoint provided separately for the unique codes and passwords. Speak to your Team Leader if you need access to this resource.

If the caller provides the unique code and password, go to question 4.

Do not allow the caller multiple attempts to guess details.

If code and password are not provided, end the call.

Complete the Report Suspected Fraud form.

4

Do you have the patient’s consent to get their Medicare or entitlement card details?

This is required. The patient must give consent before Medicare or entitlement card numbers are disclosed to the pharmacy. This applies for bulk patient requests also. The pharmacy must provide an undertaking they have each patients permission allowing them to contact the IME pharmaceutical benefits enquiry line on their behalf.

  • Pharmacists or pharmacy staff must get each patient's Medicare or entitlement card number to confirm the patient's eligibility for government subsidised pharmaceutical benefits. This number must match the name held in the Medicare record for that person
  • The pharmacist or pharmacy staff can phone Services Australia and get the number on behalf of the patient
  • Before a card number, card expiry date or patient name can be disclosed and stored, informed consent of the patient is required
  • Only provide details the caller has requested. Do not offer other entitlement details
  • Providing additional information that is not requested may be a privacy breach, if the patient did not give consent for the information to be disclosed

Note: Service Officers can tell the caller the options below for further help but this is not mandatory.

When a patient cannot provide a Medicare or entitlement card, they can get the number by:

  • calling and getting the number from the person who holds the card
  • calling the Medicare enquiries line 132 011 to get their Medicare or entitlement number
  • asking their pharmacist to telephone the Services Australia to get the number on their behalf
  • come back with the Medicare or entitlement number at a later time, or
  • they can get the medicine by paying the full price

5

What is the patient's name and address?

You can also request the date of birth if you need to ensure correct patient details are located.

Key the patient's details quoted by the pharmacy or pharmacy staff on the RPQN screen. If the patient is:

  • found from the search criteria
    • select the number
    • press PF9-ELIGIBILITY DETAILS
    • give the caller the Medicare entitlement number and expiry date
      Note: ensure that the end date for the card is not prior to today's date. If the end date is prior to today, a check of Medicare screens is required. See not found on the PBS system or end date is prior to today option below
    • if patient is listed on two valid Medicare cards, see Patient listed on two valid Medicare cards table
  • not found from the search criteria, either
    • check if the patient has resided at another address or is known by another name - do not disclose the address
    • ask for the patient's date of birth - do not confirm or disclose the date of birth to caller
    • check the history table
  • not found on the PBS system or end date is prior to today, either
    • check Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) if access to system is authorised for this purpose
    • contact the Programme Support Officer (PSO) line for a search to be completed
  • not found on the PBS system but has been located on the Medicare system
    • report to the PBS Help Desk
  • not found on the PBS or Medicare system
    • inform the pharmacist or pharmacy staff you were unable to find the person
      Note: concession card or Health Care Card or Pensioner Concession Card cannot be accepted to identify patients as they are not issued by Medicare and the information we have may not be up to date

6

If caller requests more than 2 individual patient requests during the call, tell the caller:

You can only request 2 individual patient details each time you call the IME line. You will need to call back if you need more patient details.

Do not put the caller back into the queue. They must ring the PBS general line again.

7

If caller requests bulk patient details, ask the pharmacy who they service, e.g., a nursing home, residential home or hospital.

If the pharmacy services a:

  • nursing home, call back on the number recorded on the Pharmacy Approvals System
  • hospital, see Table 3

Always contact on the number located on the Pharmacy Approvals System.

Never tell the caller the number you will use to call them back.

Suspected fraudulent calls

Table 4: this table describes additional questions to ask and actions to take where a call is suspected to be fraudulent. There is a standard Report Suspected Fraud form located on the intranet. Not all fields need to be completed. Refer to your leadership team for further advice.

Where possible, get both the card number and full name of the patient. This helps with any fraud investigations. Always complete a Report Suspected Fraud form when you suspected fraud.

Note: callers to the IME line do not have to be the pharmacist or the approved person. However, the caller must correctly respond to the questions asked to verify the caller, including who the approved person is for the pharmacy. Do not give any information if a call is suspected to be fraudulent. Never disclose a patient’s date of birth or address to the caller.

FAQ

Table 5: this table contains answers to frequently asked questions from staff.

Question

Response

Can Medicare card numbers be released over the phone for COVID-19 vaccination purposes?

Yes.

  • The Medicare card number can be released to pharmacies for COVID-19 vaccination purposes.
  • The caller must request the Medicare card number. Do not offer the information.

Does the pharmacy have to provide the full address as part of the authentication process?

Yes.

  • If the Pharmacy Approval System has ‘Shop 12-15, 742 Evergreen Terrace, Simpsons ACT’ the caller must provide all these details. Be sensible in your approach, for example if they offer ‘Suite 12-15, 742 Evergreen Terrace, Simpsons ACT’ this can be accepted
  • Tenancies often change the address title and the Pharmacy Approvals System may not have been updated

Can we also request the patient's date of birth (DOB) from the pharmacist, as the address is often not kept up to date or the address details may have come from stealing mail from the patient?

Yes.

  • Whilst the minimum is name and address, you can seek a DOB particularly when needing to confirm the record, e.g. changed address or under marital name etc.
  • However, under no circumstances can you disclose the DOB

Remember: Ask don’t tell.

Can I provide details when the pharmacy provides an initial e.g. Kate P Smith or P J Smith?

No.

  • The pharmacy should have access to the patient to confirm these details
  • Do not let the caller guess the initial or name

Can I provide the PBS Safety Net details or concession details when the caller requests just the Medicare number?

No.

  • The pharmacy must seek the patient’s permission before they contacts us for the patient’s entitlement information. Therefore, the onus is on the pharmacy to request the relevant entitlement information for the patient during the call
  • If the patient or pharmacy believes they may be near the threshold or have reached the threshold the pharmacist can request the details during the call
  • Providing additional information that is not requested may be a privacy breach, as the pharmacy may not have sought permission from the patient for you to disclose this information

Remember: Ask don’t tell.

If the pharmacy calls for a concession number and the Medicare number they have given has been updated, so has a different last number, can we tell the pharmacy this without them specifically asking?

No.

  • Do not provide information that has not been requested. See above regarding privacy requirements
  • Note: section 86C of the National Health Act 1953 refers to an Approved supplier (pharmacist) requesting the provision of a patients Medicare number to facilitate the supply of pharmaceutical benefits. Therefore, the pharmacy should ensure they have the correct information prior to dispensing PBS medicines

If the pharmacy calls for a concession number, and have not specifically asked for PBS Safety Net, but there is a PBS Safety Net entitlement number recorded, are we able to ask if they want that information?

No.

  • Do not provide information that has not been requested. The pharmacy must get patient consent to use the IME pharmaceutical benefits enquiry line to seek customer details. Therefore, the onus is on the pharmacy to request this information if needed
  • For further context: if a fraudulent caller is provided additional information about a patient, and all that was requested was a Medicare card number, the caller may use this information to commit fraud (e.g. identity theft for financial gain). This would have serious repercussions for the patient and Services Australia

I am concerned if I do not provide all entitlement details to the caller when they request the Medicare card details this may disadvantage the patient, as the pharmacist may not receive PBS price.

The pharmacy is best placed to request the information for the patient. A patient can submit a PBS patient refund if they were eligible for a concessional rate and the pharmacy did not request these details at the time of the call.

If the customer has a hyphenated name and the pharmacy only provides part, e.g., Smith, when recorded with PBS as Smith-Jones, can this information be released? Same with first name scenarios.

If an ePrescription is rejected in a claim for name mismatch, can the correct details be released from the IME line, or is this call to be transferred to the general line?
Note: prescription information transmitted electronically to pharmacy may be known as an eRx script, as it resides in the eRx or MediSecure exchange)

No.

  • Do not provide information that has not been requested. In this scenario you may simply say there is no matching record and suggest they ask the patient if they are or were previously known under another name
  • The IME line is available to pharmacies to ensure that pharmaceutical benefits are provided to eligible people. You are also, under the Privacy Act 1977, required to protect personal information held by the agency
  • As the pharmacy should be liaising with the patient (to access their permission to contact the IME line) they should be in a position to seek further clarification from the patient and call back the IME line

Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander customer and the pharmacy have either partial date of birth (DOB), no fixed address or part of a community. How can we help? Does the pharmacy call the Indigenous Access line for information? Can details be released if patient is identified by one-search criteria? E.g., one listing of name appears in RPQN, no match with DOB or address.

Do not release customer information if you cannot confidently identify the customer.

  • In addition to full name, at least one of address or DOB is required to identify a patient
  • Where partial details are provided, you may ask clarifying questions to seek further details, but remember: Ask don’t tell

Note: In this scenario, it may be difficult to identify the customer. You can request any additional information that the customer can provide to confirm their identity. For example, if the customer can provide a full name and valid Medicare/concession card number in the absence of address or DOB.

When a caller requests more than 2 patient details during the call, it is acceptable to transfer these callers back into the queue?

No.

The caller must call back the IME line.

Is it okay for pharmacists to contact the IME line for their own Medicare/entitlement details?

No.

Patients cannot request their own Medicare, PBS Safety Net (SN) or other entitlement details via the IME enquiry line.

If the patient has lost their SN card they may request a replacement card by completing a PBS Safety Net replacement or supplementary card application (PB018) form and sending it to:

  • Services Australia, GPO Box 9826 in their capital city.

Is it okay for patients to contact the IME line for their own Medicare/entitlement details?

No.

They should contact the appropriate program, e.g. Medicare general line 132 011.

What if a patient has lost their PBS Safety Net (SN) card, can we provide details on the IME line?

No.

If the patient has lost their SN card, they may request a replacement card by completing a PBS Safety Net replacement or supplementary card application (PB018) form and sending it to Services Australia, GPO Box 9826 in their capital city.

When verifying the pharmacy name and address, can I accept interchangeable words such as 'pharmacy' instead of 'chemist' and 'suite' instead of 'shop'?

Yes.

Can a reformed public hospital request bulk patient details?

Yes.

How do I report a suspected fraudulent call I have taken on the IME line?

Note as much information about the call as possible and complete the Report Suspected Fraud form located on intranet.

Can there be multiple approved persons for a pharmacy?

Yes.

There can be multiple owners of the pharmacy, known as the approved person, recorded on the RPBC mainframe screen. Ensure you [F8] to the end so you do not miss any information.

Script to answer queries on accessing ‘Find a Patient’ on HPOS

Table 6: this table describes the scripts Service Officers use to respond to queries about ‘Find a Patient’ on HPOS on the Improved Monitoring of Entitlements (IME) for pharmaceutical benefits enquiry line.

Question

Response

Can I still call the IME line for normal circumstances?

You can still call the IME line for normal circumstances until 30 June 2022.

Why is the IME line service only going to be available for exceptional circumstances from 1 July 2022?

To strengthen and protect the privacy of patient information.

HPOS is an online secure portal, with a 2-step digital authentication process.

What are the exceptional circumstances and how will I know if I qualify?

Only release the following information when asked, and after conducting a security check. See Table 2 or 3

  • Hospital Accident and Emergency Departments – for patients unable to provide Medicare details
  • A known outage within the HPOS system preventing pharmacies verifying customer details, including internet outages for pharmacies
  • DVA concession details
  • PBS Safety Net Concession and Entitlement card details.

Your peak body organisation will be informed.

When can I access ‘HPOS – Find a Patient’?

You can start accessing ‘HPOS – Find a Patient’ from 6 June 2021. You will need to register for a Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account to access HPOS. More information is available on the agency’s website (servicesaustralia.gov.au/hpos)

Where can I find more information about accessing ‘HPOS – Find a Patient’?

You can find more information about accessing ‘HPOS – Find a Patient’ on the agency’s website (servicesaustralia.gov.au/hpos)

HPOS is a secure platform that will allow you to verify your patient’s Medicare card details and confirm concessional entitlement eligibility.

Who can help me with registration with Provider Digital Access (PRODA)?

Phone: 1800 700 199 and select Option 1 Monday to Friday, 8 am to 5 pm local time

Email: proda@servicesaustralia.gov.au

See Health professionals contact information on the Services Australia website.

Who can help me with linking my Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account to ‘HPOS – Find a Patient’?

Phone: 1800 700 199 Monday to Friday, 8 am to 5 pm local time

Email: ebusiness@servicesaustralia.gov.au

See Health professionals contact information on the Services Australia website.

I have called the 2 numbers above and am still having problems. Who can I go to for help?

Please tell me your name, contact number and a brief description of the issue. I will arrange a call back to help you.

Action for Service Officer

Record the name of the pharmacist, contact number and a brief description of the issue then email the details to PBS Help Desk

I logged on to HPOS – Find a Patient and entered the patient’s full name and date of birth. The Medicare details check returned with no results. What can I do?
  • Ask the patient if they have used another name on their Medicare Card
  • If the patient has the Medicare express app on their mobile, they can present their Medicare card through the digital wallet
  • If the patient does not currently have the Medicare express app, the patient can download it through the app store
  • If the patient does not have access to a mobile device, they can request an interim card from a Service Centre

I logged on to HPOS – Find a Patient.

Why can’t I see the concessional card number?
  • The concessional card number is not available on HPOS – Find a Patient
  • If the patient has the Centrelink express app on their mobile, they can present their concessional card number through the digital wallet
  • If the patient does not currently have the Centrelink express app, the patient can download it through the app store
  • More information on the digital wallet – concession can be found on the Services Australia website
  • If the patient does not have access to a mobile device, they can request an interim card from a Service Centre

Infographic - Ask don't tell

Patient listed on two valid Medicare cards

A patient can be listed on 2 valid Medicare cards. This usually occurs because the patient is a child (or young adult) and it is more convenient for claiming.

If a patient is listed on 2 valid Medicare cards, check the patient's date of birth. If the patient is:

  • a child (or young adult):
    • ask the caller which person the child is with
    • determine the correct card according to the address and the person's name on the card before releasing the card details
  • an adult:
    • release the patient’s adult Medicare card details after matching the given address

If a Service Officer needs clarification because the scenario does not fit with the above criteria, they should call the PBS Help Desk for advice.
