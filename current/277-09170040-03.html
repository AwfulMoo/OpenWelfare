Gather and review evidence

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Gather evidence + Read more

It is critical to obtain evidence to support all the relevant facts of the decision. Making findings of facts which are unsupported by evidence may constitute an error of law. Requesting evidence from customers as early as possible within the 60 day timeframe (120 days for customers who live in a reciprocating jurisdiction) allows for additional information to be received and provides customers with time to comment on the evidence before making a decision.

2

Review evidence + Read more

When reviewing the evidence consider the following:

  • Is the evidence still current?
  • Have the customers provided more up to date evidence with their application or response?
  • Can more up to date information be reasonably sourced?
  • Is there evidence to support all the relevant facts of the decision?
  • Which facts in dispute will require additional evidence in order to make a finding?

Note: some searches conducted in relation to the original decision may be out dated, do not use them in the objection process. Conduct new searches as part of the evidence gathering process.

3

Source evidence + Read more ...

Gather evidence from sources including but not limited to:

  • parties to the objection
  • system searches
  • other government agencies
  • information held by Child Support
  • internet - Google, relevant websites
  • third parties, for example accountants, financial institutions
  • professional resources such as industry guides and journals

See Child Support's Information Gathering powers.

Note: ‘Suspicious Matter Reports’ (SMRs) from AUSTRAC must never be disclosed. This means they must not be included when making a submission – they cannot be disclosed to any court or tribunal and are not admissible as evidence.

You must review AUSTRAC search results to ensure they do not contain an SMR/SUSTR – these must not be recorded in the system. If a record needs to be removed, take immediate action by sending an email the National.ICS mailbox.

Full details regarding AUSTRAC disclosure of information can be found on the Resources page.

4

Care objections + Read more ...

If the objection relates to a care decision made by Family Assistance, request any information they considered in the original decision. This information should be requested as a priority from the Child Support Unit (CSU) to ensure Child Support timeframes are not compromised. See Centrelink/Child Support information exchange for more information.

When CSU/Centrelink Liaison Unit (CLU) sends information regarding:

  • a Centrelink review decision
  • an Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) AAT first review or AAT second review decision

for a non-mutual Family Assistance/Child Support customer, the care records subject to the review will be sent on the mutual customer’s Cuba record. If no objection record exists for the review decision in Cuba, request the date the review application was lodged with Centrelink. See Centrelink/Child Support information exchange for more information. Record the objection record in Cuba. See Objections Cuba Process Help.

5

Request evidence + Read more

Customers are required to provide information or evidence to support their claims, regardless of whether they are the applicant or respondent. Discuss the evidence and information required and agree on:

  • the exact evidence to be provided
  • the latest date it must be received
  • how it will be provided, in order of preference:
    • verbal
    • electronic (i.e.myGov)
    • surface mail
  • the action Child Support will take if the evidence is not provided by the agreed date

If the evidence or information is not provided by the agreed date:

  • source evidence from a third party, if possible
  • give the customer additional time to provide the information
  • make a decision based on the available evidence

If a customer does not agree to or cannot provide evidence to support their claims, advise the information may be sourced via other means. For example, if the customer refuses to provide payslips, these can be requested directly from their employer.

If a customer is noncommittal about providing evidence, consider seeking the information from third parties in the first instance. This will ensure the necessary information is available to make the decision within the legislated timeframe.

Where clarification is required from either party or a response has not been received, attempt 2 outbound calls within 24 hours using SMS prior to calling.

6

Exchange information and seek comment from customers + Read more ...

Natural justice requires customers to have an opportunity to respond to information used to establish the facts of a decision, particularly if the customer would be adversely impacted by the decision maker's reliance on the information.

Note: give customers the opportunity to validate or correct any document/information suspected of being falsified or that includes incorrect information.

When information relevant to the objection is received:

  • document the information in the Objection Maintenance window notepad with a subject heading 'Information received from [insert customer name]'. See Documenting Child Support information in Cuba
  • if provided by one of the customers, contact the other customer to discuss the information. See Contact with Child Support Customers
  • if obtained by Child Support or provided independently by a third party, make both customers aware of the relevant information
  • document customer contact (including unsuccessful attempts) and customer comments in the Objection Maintenance window notepad with a subject heading ‘Sought comment from [insert customer name]’
  • there is no legislative obligation to provide copies of the new information to each party. However, the principles of procedural fairness require that customers be made aware of and have the opportunity to comment on any information being considered by the decision maker that might be adverse to that customer’s interests

Subsequent information is not required to be physically exchanged (that is, by post or online). Open Exchange of Information requirements can be satisfied verbally however a physical exchange of actual copies or edited copies of relevant documents may still occur where:

  • there is a large amount of material or the material is quite complex and not easily explained over the phone
  • the Objections Service Officer considers it in the best interests of the customer, or
  • a customer requests it

Action

1

Provide information to other party + Read more ...

Under open exchange of information, customers may need to see the information provided by the other party.

If there is potential offensive content or Family and Domestic Violence (FDV) content, see Open Exchange of Information for Child Support customers.

  • send an MX0-1 unique letter using the UQ50.05 - Additional information received re-objection letter and attach the additional information See Letters Cuba Process Help, Create a Unique letter
  • ask the customer to contact Child Support to provide their response to the additional information
  • encourage a timely response if information is to be forwarded to Child Support
  • create an Obj Reply Req intray with a due date of either the date the information is due, or the following day. See Intray management Cuba Process Help

Contact the customer if the response has not been received by the intray due date to confirm if they are going to respond. Make 2 phone contact attempts across the day. If a new timeframe is negotiated, update the Obj Reply Req intray and the 'Sought comment from xxx' notepad. See Contact with Child Support customers

2

Contact is unsuccessful + Read more

If contact is unsuccessful:

  • send an MX0-1 unique letter using the UQ50.05 - Additional information received re-objection letter template requesting they contact Child Support by phone within five business days of receiving the letter if they want to make additional comments
  • send a copy of the information provided by the other customer, see Open Exchange of information for Child Support customers
  • create an Obj Reply Req intray with a due date in five days. If the customer has not responded by the intray due date, call them and encourage a verbal response

3

Response received + Read more ...

When the response is received:

  • send an MX0-1 unique letter using the output from the Objection letters macro
  • contact the other customer to discuss the information provided
  • if contact by phone is unsuccessful, follow the process see Open Exchange of Information for Child Support customers and use the UQ50.05 - Additional information received re-objection letter requesting they contact by telephone within five business days of receiving the letter if they want to make additional comment
  • create an Obj Reply Req intray

4

Request evidence from third parties + Read more

Third party information can be sought informally through research, phone calls or formally by issuing a notice. See Section 120/161 notice Cuba Process Help. Information should be sought from third parties in the following circumstances, but is not limited to when:

  • a customer cannot provide adequate evidence to substantiate their claims and it is likely a third party can provide information needed to establish the relevant facts
  • if it is reasonably expected the information will not be received from the customers but it is necessary to establish the relevant facts
  • the evidence provided by the customers is conflicting or equally weighted and third party information may tip the balance of probability
  • it is likely the third party evidence is more reliable or can provide more detail than that provided by the customers

Document the enquiries and the information received in the  Objections Maintenance window and the appropriate Cuba window. For example, document section 161 responses in the Issue Section 120/161 Notice window.

5

Consider late evidence + Read more

If information is provided late in the 60/120 day process, assess:

  • the relevance of the information to the decision
  • its potential impact on the outcome of the objection
  • the ability to open exchange the information with the other customer giving them reasonable time for comment

If it is necessary to delay making the objection decision, discuss this with your Team Leader. Note: it is appropriate to balance the requirement to meet the 60/120 day timeframe against the obligation to make a decision which takes account of all relevant evidence. Where there is a likelihood of a better decision being made, it may be appropriate to allow the decision to go overdue.

6

Assess evidence + Read more

Decide how much weight should be given to each piece of evidence and how it supports the facts of the decision. Influencing factors to be considered when weighting the evidence include the:

  • nature and integrity of the evidence, for example, a document versus verbal testimony
  • source of the evidence
  • age of the evidence - is it still relevant to the issue being decided

In some cases, evidence can be too evenly balanced to support a definite finding of fact. If evidence is inconclusive and no additional evidence can be obtained, the relevant legislation and policy must be analysed to determine which question(s) must be decided. Seek guidance from your Team Leader in these cases.
