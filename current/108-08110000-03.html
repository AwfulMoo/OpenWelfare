Granting and reassessment of UA payment

This table describes the steps to pay, decline and reverse the decline for the UA payment.

Step

Action

1

Payment of UA + Read more ...

Utilities Allowance (UA) is paid automatically to the same payment destination as the eligible customer's income support payment. It is usually issued on the customer's first Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) after the test days of 20 March, 20 June, 20 September and 20 December.

  • If the customer has previously declined UA payment and now wishes to accept UA payments, go to Step 3
  • If the customer wishes to decline payment for UA, go to Step 2
  • If the UA payment was not made automatically, go to Step 4

2

If the customer does not want to receive UA + Read more ...

To code the request to decline UA:

  • go to the SHC Misc. Details (SHMD) screen
  • enter 'Y' in the Suppress Payment of Utilities Allowance: field
  • record details on a DOC

If UA has been paid it will show on the Payment Summary (PS) screen, One Off Payments (OOP) screen and the Concession Summary (CCAS) screen.

Procedure ends here.

3

Reverse customers decline to UA + Read more ...

A request by a customer to decline UA can be reversed at any time. However, only future instalments can be paid that is, no arrears can be paid for any past period for which UA has been declined.

To reverse the decline payment option:

  • go to the SHC Misc.Details (SHMD) screen
  • enter 'N' in the Suppress Payment of Utilities Allowance: field. (the field can also be left blank)
  • record details on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

4

Check if the UA payment was made + Read more ...

Payment of UA will only display on the Payment Summary (PS) and Payment Destination (PD) screens after the triggers have been run on the qualification day that is, 20 March, 20 June, 20 September or 20 December.

Details and a history of UA payments can be viewed on the Concession Summary (CCAS) screen. The CCAS screen will also show payments made by the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) and payments the customer has declined.

Has the customer been paid UA?

  • Yes, advise customer when payment was made. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

UA was not made automatically + Read more ...

Qualification is determined on the test days of 20 March, 20 June, 20 September and 20 December each year and usually issued on the customer's first EPED after these test days. A customer must, on the test day:

  • be receiving Disability Support Pension (DSP) and be aged under 21 with no children, and
  • be either:
    • in Australia, or
    • temporarily absent from Australia where their test day is within the first 6 weeks of the temporary absence and the customer maintains eligibility

UA payments can be made if a customer's payment is granted or restored and payment is backdated to include the test day.

Customers on zero rate are only eligible for UA if their zero rate reason is 'DVA' (Defence Force Income Support Allowance) or 'NZP' (New Zealand agreement direct deduction).

Does the customer meet these requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, there is no entitlement to UA. Procedure ends here

6

The customer meets the qualification criteria for UA + Read more ...

Was there a started or submitted activity which may have delayed or interrupted the UA trigger or did an event occur which may have delayed issue of UA to the following EPED, such as EPED change or a change in relationship status?

  • Yes, and
    • the payment has been delayed due to an EPED or relationship status change advise the customer of the new delivery date. Procedure ends here
    • the UA trigger was delayed/interrupted advise the customer of the delivery date (check the PS and CCAS screens). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Reassessment for UA + Read more ...

Refresh the customer's payment on the Benefit Action (BA) screen. Note: if UA is also payable for the partner, refresh the partner's record as well.

Code the BA screen as follows:

  • Svc Rsn: field, enter the appropriate code for the customer's income support payment. If needed, use help '?' facility
  • Action: field, enter 'RSH' for 'Refresh'
  • Reason: field, leave blank
  • Effect Date: field, enter the test day applicable for UA
  • Notes: field, enter note on why reassessment is being done
  • Source: and DOR: enter appropriate details
  • Go to Assessment Results (AR) screen. This will show if payment is being made

Has the refresh activity paid UA to the customer?

  • Yes, finalise activity. Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Refresh did not pay UA + Read more ...

Cancel the refresh activity.

If a refresh did not pay UA, go to the OOP screen. Note: if the partner is also entitled to UA and it was not paid via a refresh activity also update the partner's OOP screen.

Update details as follows:

  • Effect Date: field, enter the test day applicable for UA
  • Payment Type: field, enter 'UAS' for UA
  • Source: and DOR: enter appropriate details
  • go to AR screen and finalise activity
  • record details on a DOC
