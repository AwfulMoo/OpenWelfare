This document outlines details about the approved activities that will satisfy mutual obligation requirements for principal carers.

Mutual obligation requirements for allowance recipients

Principal carers of dependent children who are receiving JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA) job seeker, Parenting Payment Single (PPS) when their youngest child is 6 and over or Special Benefit (SpB) need to meet part-time mutual obligation requirements, unless an exemption is appropriate. They can fulfil their requirements by:

  • meeting the sufficient work test (undertaking paid work and/or self-employment)
  • looking for work
  • participating in other approved activities
  • a combination of suitable paid work and self-employment of at least 30 hours per fortnight
  • approved study of at least 30 hours per fortnight, or
  • a combination of study and paid work (including self-employment) of at least 30 hours per fortnight

Job seekers can volunteer to undertake drug dependency treatments or interventions which may count towards meeting their mutual obligation and Annual Activity Requirements if they are provider managed.

Participation requirements

Parenting Payment (PP) customers who are participating in ParentsNext have participation requirements.

Job search or approved activities

Principal carers with mutual obligation requirements need to undertake job search and be connected with an Employment Services Provider unless they are fully meeting their requirements through an approved activity such as:

  • undertaking suitable paid work of at least 30 hours per fortnight
  • a combination of suitable paid work and self-employment of at least 30 hours per fortnight
  • undertaking self-employment of at least 30 hours per fortnight (meeting the sufficient work)
  • approved study of at least 30 hours per fortnight, or
  • a combination of study and paid work (including self-employment) of at least 30 hours per fortnight

If a principal carer is meeting their requirements they may still volunteer to be connected with an Employment Services Provider. Customers should still have an active registration.

Voluntary work

Voluntary work can be approved by Services Australia for mature age (55 years and over) job seekers, including principal carers. Other principal carers can only have voluntary work, or a combination of voluntary work and paid work or study, approved by their provider. Where voluntary work is approved by either the agency or a provider, the customer must remain connected with their provider, still be available for suitable paid work, and accept all referrals to job interviews.

Mature Age or reduced capacity to work

If the principal carer is also aged 55+ or has an assessed partial capacity to work between 15-29 hours per week, they will be placed in the principal carer participation category as it is more beneficial for the customer. Special rules apply for Mature Aged customers.

Where a principal carer has an assessed partial capacity to work or temporary reduced work capacity of 0-7 or 8-14 hours per week, the job seeker will be placed into the appropriate partial/temporary reduced work capacity participation category as it is the most beneficial.

Principal carer fully meeting requirements

If a principal carer fully meeting their requirements through part-time work, self-employment or study volunteers to participate with a provider, they will be registered as a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP), have the JSCI completed along with any subsequent JCA, and be connected to an Employment Services Provider. The job seeker will negotiate activities in their Job Plan with their provider unless they are managed by the agency.

Note: a Principal Carer in receipt of Parenting Payment Single (PPS), participating in the PaTH Internship Programme and fully meeting their requirements will continue to be provider managed.

The initial assessment of a principal carer's participation requirements is conducted through a Compulsory Participation Interview when the customer's youngest child reaches the age of 6 years.

Note: from 3 November 2018, some Principal Carers will have their approved part-time work (PTW) activity automatically updated if the job seeker:

  • is a principal carer, and
  • is in receipt of JobSeeker Payment, Parenting Payment Single, or Youth Allowance (job seeker), and
  • has declared in their last 2 consecutive fortnights that they have worked at least 30 hours each fortnight and earned income that is equal to or above the national minimum wage

A new Job Plan must still be negotiated to include the new approved part-time work activity. The job plan needs to reflect the new dates on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen.

Approved activities during the Work Experience Phase

The Work Experience Phase is a requirement for job seekers with mutual obligation requirements where a period of additional activity will be negotiated. Work Experience is undertaken with the job seeker's provider and can be satisfied through a range of activities. Work Experience activities may be undertaken outside of the Work Experience Phase, where the provider deems this to be of benefit to the customer's long term employment prospects. If a job seeker commences the Work Experience Phase, an activity will be chosen in negotiation with their provider and incorporated into their Job Plan. There is a 6 week period for job seekers to decide on and engage in their chosen activity from the time the Work Experience Phase commences. The provider will manage the referrals, commencement and compliance of the Work Experience Activity.

Allowable Breaks from employment activities

Principal carers who have approved activities of Part-time Work and Study and Paid Work (Combined) may be subject to the Allowable Break provisions over Christmas school holiday periods, if affected by employer initiated temporary closures, or reduced hours/shifts.

Community Development Program (CDP)

If a job seeker lives in a Department of Education, Skills and Employment designated remote area, they are referred to a Community Development Program (CDP) provider.
