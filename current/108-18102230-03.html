Staff must start at Step 1. The following steps are a step-by-step guide.

Receiving care

Step

Action

1

Members of a couple separated due to illness + Read more ...

Is the customer a member of a couple who is illness separated?

  • Yes
    • Customers who are illness separated are assessed individually for Rent Assistance (RA)
    • Go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

RCA/CUR on benefit status line + Read more ...

Does this customer have RCA/CUR as a benefit status?

  • Yes and it is:
    • RCA/CUR-MTA, go to step 3
    • RCA/CUR-DSS, go to step 4
  • No, go to step 7

3

RCA/CUR-MTA + Read more ...

This code appears when the customer's income and assets have been assessed for aged care purposes but they are yet to enter an aged care service.

Check the RCA Institution Summary (RIS) screen for Admission Date and Care Type.

If the customer is advising they are:

  • in respite care, go to Step 12
  • in another temporary care situation, go to Step 13
  • entering a residential aged care service, go to Step 9
  • still in their principal home and paying rent:
    • Normal RA eligibility provisions Rent Assistance eligibility criteria which apply to all customers Additional RA eligibility criteria which apply to specific customer groups Payability of Rent Assistance to customers in some situations apply
    • Procedure ends here

4

RCA/CUR-DSS + Read more ...

The customer will be either:

  • Assessed as being in a funded bed in an accredited aged care service:
    • There is no entitlement to RA. A subsidy is paid to the aged care service instead
  • Receiving home care:
    • The customer may be entitled to RA. Normal RA eligibility provisions Rent Assistance eligibility criteria which apply to all customers Additional RA eligibility criteria which apply to specific customer groups Payability of Rent Assistance to customers in some situations apply

Navigate to the RCA Institution Summary (RIS) screen. The Care Type field will advise if the customer is in Residential Aged Care or receiving home care.

If the customer is in Residential Aged Care , the Homeowner code on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen will be:

  • GFN (Govt. funded non-homeowner) or
  • GFH (Govt. funded homeowner)

If the RIS screen has the customer in Residential Aged Care, and the customer is advising:

  • they have moved out of Residential Aged Care, go to Step 5
  • the accreditation of the aged care service has changed, go to Step 6
  • they are still paying rent for their former principal home, go to Step 10
  • they are remaining in Residential Aged Care and asking about RA, advise the customer not entitled to RA. A subsidy is paid to the aged care service instead. Procedure ends here.

5

Customer has moved out of aged care and claiming Rent Assistance + Read more ...

Services Australia receives automatic advice when a customer moves out of an accredited aged care service. If the customer still has RCA/CUR the automatic advice has not yet been received.

  • Confirm the discharge date with the aged care service
  • Update the customer's address details
  • Procedure ends here

6

Status of the aged care service has changed + Read more ...

If the aged care service is no longer accredited it will not receive government funding. The customer can be assessed for RA instead.

If accreditation has been lost, Services Australia receives an automatic advice. If the customer still has RCA/CUR the automatic advice has not yet been received.

  • Confirm the date of the loss of accreditation
  • Completing the Accommodation (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance. Procedure ends here

7

Customer in an aged care service which has become accredited + Read more ...

Is the customer in a service and is advising of a change of accreditation from unfunded to funded?

  • Yes
    • Advise the customer they will no longer be entitled to RA. A subsidy to the aged care service will be paid instead
    • The customer has no obligation to advise of a change of funding for the aged care service. There is no debt from when the status of the service changed to the date RA ceased
    • See Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Customer's care location + Read more ...

Is the customer advising they have moved to an aged care service?

  • Yes and the move is:
    • for respite care, go to Step 12
    • into a transitional care program, go to Step 15
    • permanent, go to Step 9
  • No, and the move is
    • for respite, go to Step 12
    • temporary, other than respite, go to Step 13
    • permanent, go to Step 14

9

Customer move to aged care service + Read more ...

Is the aged care service accredited and the customer is in a funded bed?

  • Yes:
    • they are not entitled to RA. A subsidy will be paid to the aged care service instead
    • use the Change in contact details guided procedure to update the Address Details (AD) and the Accommodation Details (AC) screens
    • the update can be done up to 14 days before the move
    • all Service Officers can complete the updates
    • see Updating address details
    • procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 14

10

Customer still paying rent for former principal home + Read more ...

Has the customer moved to an accredited aged care service and still paying rent for the former principal home?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No:
    • see Updating address details
    • procedure ends here

11

Customer in permanent funded aged care and still paying rent for former home + Read more ...

The customer can receive RA for the former principal home for the first 14 days after admission.

If RA stops before 14 days has passed:

  • payment will need to be made using Payment Adjust (PYAJ) screen. Local escalation procedures may be in place
  • if the customer is a member of a couple contact the Rent Assistance Service Desk for advice on how much rent to pay. See Resources page for a link
  • procedure ends here

12

Respite care + Read more ...

Rent Assistance (RA) cannot be paid for respite care.

RA is payable for the principal home if:

  • the customer continues to pay rent for it
  • they received RA for that address before going into respite care
  • they have been in respite care for a continuous period of 52 days or less
    Note: the 52 days is not cumulative. It starts again each time the customer goes into respite care

Procedure ends here

13

Temporary Care other than respite + Read more ...

  • If the customer is in a Transitional care program go to Step 15

A customer can receive RA for a temporary address when they have moved for 14 days or longer to:

  • receive a substantial level of care
  • to access specific medical treatment not available near their principal home

RA is payable for the temporary address even if the customer owns their principal home.

This includes:

  • in hospital awaiting placement in an aged care service. Note: only if the customer is not in a bed funded under aged care provisions
  • a detoxification clinic, rehabilitation centre, mental health facility or similar institution
  • in a private residence

See Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance.

Procedure ends here.

14

Permanent care not in a funded bed + Read more ...

Customers living in care situations which are not a funded bed in an accredited aged care can be assessed for RA. These services are:

  • non-accredited aged care
  • an unfunded bed in an accredited aged care facility
  • National Torres Strait Islander Flexible Aged Care (NATSIFAC) program
  • other types of care facilities, such as Residential Intellectually Disabled Facility
  • a multi-purpose facility and not in the aged care service it provides

See Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance.

Procedure ends here.

15

Transitional care programs + Read more ...

Transitional care programs delivered by aged care facilities, including multi-purpose facilities are government funded under Aged Care provisions. Customers are not entitled to RA. For more information, see Transition Care Programme

Some hospitals, psychiatric facilities and disability providers also offer transitional care programs. Customers can be assessed for RA.

If an update to the customer's Accommodation Details (AC) screen is needed see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance

Procedure ends here.
