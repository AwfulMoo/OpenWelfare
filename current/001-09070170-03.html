This page contains a summary of the types of voluntary activities that may be considered by job seekers assessed as having a temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC) and are negotiating a Job Plan. A job seeker should discuss suitable activities with Services Australia or, if appropriate, their Employment Services Provider.

On this page:

Activities that may be included in Job Plans when there is a TRWC

TRWC customer's employment and study approved activities

Activities that may be included in Job Plans when there is a TRWC

Table 1: This table describes activities that may be included in a Job Plan for job seekers with a TRWC.

Item

Description

1

Community Services Order + Read more ...

A Community Services Order can be imposed by the Court (with the offender's consent) on suitable offenders, sentencing them to perform community work for a certain number of hours instead of serving time in prison. For example, community service can be done in lieu of paying a fine.

The offenders perform unpaid work at approved organisations and are supervised during their community service. These customers are not considered to be in gaol. A Community Services Order is a type of Community Based Order that can be imposed by a Court.

2

Non-Vocational activities + Read more ...

Job seekers may benefit from one of the following activities:

  • Undertaking an assessment of their present capacity or needs by a provider or organisation
  • Drug and alcohol rehabilitation
  • Intervention Non Specific
  • Parenting Skills program
  • Health Maintenance Program
  • Child Care

3

Quarterly Appointment + Read more ...

Customers with a temporary reduced work capacity of 0-14 hours per week:

  • for 12 weeks or more - must attend a quarterly (QTI) interview with Services Australia unless they are meeting their requirements through part time work or self-employment. This QTI activity must be included in the job seeker's Job Plan
  • For less than 12 weeks - see Reduced Capacity Requirements

4

Reduced Capacity Requirements + Read more ...

Customers with a temporary reduced work capacity to work of 0-14 hours per week:

  • For less than 12 weeks - must attend any Services Australia interviews when requested, unless they are meeting their requirements through part time work or self-employment. This activity must be included in the job seeker's Job Plan
  • For more than 12 weeks - see Quarterly Appointment

5

Youth Activities + Read more ...

Youth Activities allows a job seeker, up to 20 years of age, to undertake a 50 to 74% study load or a combination of activities for at least 15 hours per week as part of their Job Plan.

TRWC customer's employment and study approved activities

Table 2: This table describes the employment and study approved activities for customers assessed as having a TRWC.

Item

Description

1

Disability Employment Services (DES) + Read more ...

Disability Employment Services (DES) services work with job seekers who have disabilities to help them gain and maintain employment. This can only be a voluntary activity for job seekers assessed as having a temporary reduced work capacity of 8-14 hours per week, because a job seeker cannot be referred to this program while they have temporary reduced work capacity of 0-7 hours per week.

2

Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE) + Read more ...

Australian Disability Enterprises is an approved activity that employs people with a disability, illness or injury often in a specialist work environment.

3

Employment, including part-time work and self-employment + Read more ...

  • Job seekers are encouraged to engage in part-time work as a way to maintain skills and increase opportunities to move to full-time employment
  • Self-employment is a recognised approved activity

4

Skills for Education and Employment (SEE) + Read more ...

Temporary reduced capacity to work job seekers who have been assessed as 8-14 hours per week and identified as requiring assistance with language and literacy skills can be referred to SEE.

5

Study + Read more ...

Job seekers may be able to undertake study as an approved activity, depending on their circumstances. Part-time study can be approved as a voluntary activity.

Note: if a new claimant starts full-time study on transitional Disability Support Pension (DSP) and transfer to JobSeeker Payment (JSP) they can continue to do full-time study in lieu of job search for the remainder of the course. Any approved studies to satisfy a job seeker's mutual obligation requirements must be included in their Job Plan and also coded on the AEX screen.

See Part-time or full-time study as an approved activity for JobSeeker Payment (JSP).

6

Voluntary Work + Read more ...

Voluntary Work is available as a voluntary activity for customers who are 55 years of age or over, to work with approved volunteer organisations.
