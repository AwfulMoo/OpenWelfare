Undo Crisis Payment

Step

Action

1

Check if grant can be reversed + Read more ...

The decision to grant Crisis Payment (CrP) can be reversed for the following reasons:

  • a CrP-PRI pre-release customer was released after their expected release date
  • the customer advises they did not make the claim for CrP. Someone else has claimed using the customer’s details
  • staff incorrectly granted a CrP
    • for example, CrP was granted on the wrong customer record or the customer is clearly not entitled
  • an internal review determines the customer was not entitled to receive CrP

Does one of the above reasons apply?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here

2

CrP entitlement to be reversed + Read more ...

Access to the Crisis Payment function in Process Direct and the Crisis Payment CRIPAY screen in Customer First is limited to:

  • service centre and Smart Centre staff at the APS 5 level or above
  • ICT Service Desk staff
  • payment helpdesk staff
  • Services Officers who have been given access

Does the Service Officer have access to the CRIPAY screen/function?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, and the Service Officer:
    • needs ongoing access, go to Step 3
    • does not need ongoing access
      - DOC the record why the payment must be reversed
      - escalate following usual procedures. For service centre and Smart Centre staff, this can be to a staff member at the APS 5 level or above, for example, Service Support Officer. For Smart Centre staff, see Service Officer role when seeking Technical Peer Support (TPS) in Smart Centres. Procedure ends here

3

Access required + Read more ...

Send an email to CRISIS.PAYMENT.EMEP.PROGRAM.

  • Subject line: Access required for CRIPAY screen
  • Include in the email:
    • staff logon
    • role
    • the reason access is required
    • approval from Line Manager
    • the period access is required

Access can take several days to be granted. If urgent CRIPAY action is needed, escalate to relevant staff, for example, another Prison Liaison Officer with access or Roxy Triage staff.

Procedure ends here until access has been granted.

4

Check the income support payment + Read more ...

Was the CrP claim for a Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

FHA customer + Read more ...

In Process Direct:

  • Select the Customer Summary tile and enter the CRN
  • On the START screen. select Undo Crisis Payment, then Crisis Payment Summary, and select Start
  • Select the red arrow next to the payment to reverse, select the applicable reason
    • PRC (pre-release claim)
    • ERR (processing error)
    • REV (review)
    • FRD (fraud)
  • Select Save
  • Enter the date and channel and select Save
  • Select Assess twice and check the result on the Entitlements (ELD) screen
    • The activity should generate a negative adjustment for the CrP amount
    • Confirm this amount matches the amount from the CRISIS_PS screen
    • Escalate via Roxy if the amounts do not match
  • Go to the Assessment Consequences (ACS) screen via Super Key
  • Select the appropriate Debt Action option, for example, complete any investigation needed. Select Update and Finish
  • Check the DOC text and select Finalise

Go to Step 7.

6

All other income support payment customers + Read more ...

In Customer First:

Go to the One Off Payment (OOP) screen. Note the AMR of the CrP grant and the amount paid.

  • Go to the CRIPAY screen and key these fields:
    • Is this Insert or Delete action? key 'D'
    • Reason for using the screen? key the appropriate code and press [Enter]
      PRC (pre-release claim)
      ERR (processing error)
      REV (review)
      FRD (fraud)
    • Provide the existing activity id: key the AMR from the OOP screen. Press [Enter]
    • If the reversal reason was ERR, enter a replacement Rejection Reason as per the relevant Crisis Payment OB file
  • Check all details
  • Key 'Y' in Confirm action and press [Enter]
  • The workflow will go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Check the results
    • The activity should generate a negative adjustment for the CrP amount
    • Confirm this amount matches the amount from the OOP screen
    • Escalate via Roxy if the amounts do not match
  • Select the appropriate Debt Action option, for example, complete any investigation needed
  • Finalise the activity
  • A DOC generates. Add details about why the decision was reversed and entitlement removed
  • Annotate the original DOC from the CrP grant, to confirm the grant has been reversed
  • Check that a debt shell was created if the payment was already made

7

Check reversal reason + Read more ...

If the reversal reason was:

  • PRC, consider why it is being reversed. If there is a need to re-index or re-assess a claim, go to Step 8
  • ERR, and the reversal is processed in:
    • Customer First, the system will send a rejection letter. Procedure ends here
    • Process Direct, consider why it is being reversed. If there is a need to re-index or re-assess a claim, go to Step 8
  • REV, consider why it is being reversed. If there is a need to re-index or re-assess a claim, go to Step 8
  • FRD, report the claim through the Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption form

8

Reassess claim + Read more ...

Reassess the claim to reflect the correct entitlement.

A reassessed or re-indexed claim may need to be rejected or granted, depending on circumstances of the claim/customer.

If necessary, refer to a Service Officer with the skills to process the type of CrP claim. EXO/EXV/PRI/HUM require specific skill tags.

In Process Direct:

  • Go to the Home page and select the Social Online Application (SOA) shell tile
  • Create a Manual SOA shell for Crisis Payment (select category) with the right date of receipt
  • Edit and fill in the provisional entries on the CrP questionnaire
  • Assess the claim and check the results
  • Finalise the claim and clearly DOC why CRIPAY was used and the new outcome of the claim

Try to contact the customer to explain the outcome as required.

For full processing details, see:

  • New claim for Crisis Payment – National Health Emergency
  • New claim for Crisis Payment under extreme circumstances family and domestic violence
  • New claim for Crisis Payment Other Extreme Circumstances
  • New claim for Crisis Payment for humanitarian entrants newly arrived in Australia
  • New claim for Crisis Payment prior to the release of a customer in prison or psychiatric confinement
  • New claim for Crisis Payment after customer released from prison or psychiatric confinement

Pay top up Crisis Payment

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check if the customer is eligible for top up payment + Read more ...

A customer is eligible for a top up payment if they were on a partnered rate when assessed for Crisis Payment, but are now on a single rate on the date of event.

Was the customer paid at the partnered rate of Crisis Payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here

2

Escalate to ICT + Read more ...

The top up payment is made by ICT.

Staff need to escalate this via Roxy.

Roxy needs the following details:

  • Date of marital status change
  • Date of Crisis Payment grant
  • Date of event of extreme circumstance
  • Crisis Payment Type
  • If the Crisis Payment was EXV, the reason for grant (left home, remained in home or perpetrator)
  • Adjustment amount needed (including the calculations at how this amount was arrived at)

Procedure ends here.
