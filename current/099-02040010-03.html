Process following death of a PP child

Portions of this process can be completed by Smart Centres Families Bereavement and Double Orphan Pension (DOP) Team staff only.

Step

Action

1

Initial contact + Read more ...

Advice has been received that the child of a PP customer has died.

If a customer's only child died shortly after childbirth (not stillborn), but PP has not yet been claimed and/or granted, PP may be payable for a 14 week bereavement period if the PP claim is lodged within 4 weeks of the birth. If the customer has contacted within 4 weeks of the child's birth and cannot claim at this contact:

  • record the contact as per Intent to claim and vulnerable customers
  • advise the customer they must fully submit their claim within 14 days of the contact. This will allow the start date to be backdated to the child's date of birth

If the customer is also receiving:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB), Paid Parental Leave (PPL) or Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) for the deceased child, see Death of a family assistance or Paid Parental Leave scheme child
  • Family Assistance, the advice of the death of a child is processed by the Smart Centres Families Bereavement and DOP Team (DOP)
  • Offer the customer a referral to a social worker at any time if necessary

Obtain confirmation of death for a child before taking any action.

Smart Centres Families Bereavement and DOP team staff, go to Step 4.

Has enough information been provided?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Death cannot be confirmed at initial contact + Read more ...

If there is not enough information or there is doubt, record an ACTDOC using the Notification of death FTB/CCS/PPL - Child Fast Note. Obtaining more information now means less need for further contact at a stressful time.

Record as much of the following information as possible:

  • Full name, address, contact number and relationship to child of the person notifying the death
  • Full name, address, contact number and CRN of the customer receiving PP/FTB/PPL/DAP
  • Full name, date of birth and date of death of the deceased child
  • Name of the funeral director or hospital able to confirm the date of death
  • Whether FTB payment options were discussed and if the customer has chosen to be paid via instalments or a lump sum
  • Any other information considered to be confirmation of death for a child
  • Whether there are any doubts regarding the information provided
  • If relevant and known, name of any person charged with the death of the child/ren

The Notification of death FTB/CCS/PPL - Child Fast Note template automatically adds the keyword FAOBRV to ensure the ACTDOC is allocated to the Families Bereavement and Double Orphan Pension (DOP) team. There is no requirement to add additional keywords.

Refer complex enquiries to the DOP/Bereavement helpdesk. Procedure ends here.

3

Refer to DOP team to action + Read more ...

Using the Notification of death FTB/CCS/PPL - Child Fast Note, send an ACTDOC recording all of the following information:

  • Full name, address and contact number and relationship to child of the person notifying the death
  • Full name, address, contact number and CRN of the customer receiving PP/FTB/PPL/DAP
  • Full name, date of birth and date of death of the deceased child
  • Name of the funeral director or hospital that can confirm the date of death, if official confirmation not provided
  • If provided, details of official confirmation such as a medical declaration or death certificate. Note: additional third party confirmation is then not required. See Confirmation of death for a child
  • Whether there are any doubts regarding the information provided
  • If relevant and known, name of any person charged with the death of the child/ren

The Notification of death FTB/CCS/PPL - Child Fast Note template automatically adds the keyword FAOBRV to ensure the ACTDOC is allocated to the Families Bereavement and Double Orphan Pension (DOP) team. There is no requirement to add additional keywords.

Refer complex enquiries to the DOP/Bereavement helpdesk. Procedure ends here.

4

Check if the deceased child has a record + Read more ...

On receipt of the ACTDOC, check if the death is confirmed.

If the death is not yet confirmed, conduct enquiries to confirm the death.

When the death is confirmed:

  • In the PP customer's record, go to the Child Selection (CHS) screen in either 'PGA' (for Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP)) or 'PEN' (for Parenting Payment Single (PPS)) system and press [Enter]
  • On CHS, 'S'elect the deceased child and press [Enter]
  • On Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen, 'S'elect In Customer Care: field and press [Enter]
  • On the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen, this message will appear at top of screen if child has own record: 'Child level screen is protected because the selected child is a customer'. In this situation, no updates can be made for the child on the customer's record

Does the deceased child have their own customer record?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

The deceased child has their own record + Read more ...

In the child's record, run the Notification of Death guided procedure,

The results of the death action will 'ripple' to the PP customer's record and a review will be set for the day after the 14 week bereavement period. Go to Step 6.

If the guided procedure is not available

Go to the Death/Executor (DEA) screen on the child's record in either PGA (PPP) or PEN (PPS) system 'S'elect the Death/Executor: field from the CDTS screen.

On the DEA screen:

  • key the date of the child's death in Date of Death: field
  • key the date the notification was received in Date of Notification: field. Record the executor details, if applicable. See executor or administrator of a deceased person's estate for more information

Complete the screen footer with Source: and DOR:

Press [Enter]. A warning will appear on the Death Confirmation (DAC) screen:

"Think again, SHOULD DEATH ACTION PROCEED?"

  • Yes,
    • Key 'Y' and press [Enter]. The death action is recorded
    • Confirm the ripple details on the RIP Consequences (RMC) screen and ley 'COM' in Assessment Instruction: field
    • Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen
    • The results of the death action will ripple to the PP customer's record and a review will be set for the day after the 14 week bereavement period
    • Go to Step 6
  • No, key 'N' and press [Enter]. The death action is stopped and will return to the DEA screen. Go to Step 7

6

The deceased child does not have their own customer record + Read more ...

If a customer's only child died shortly after childbirth (not stillborn), but PP has not yet been claimed, PP may be payable for a 14 week bereavement period if the PP claim is lodged within 4 weeks of the birth. If speaking with the customer within the 4 weeks and there has been no PP claim for the child or intent to claim recorded in a DOC:

  • record the contact as per Intent to claim and vulnerable customers
  • advise the customer they must fully submit their claim within 14 days of the contact. This will allow the start date to be backdated to the child's date of birth

Code the death of the deceased child on the Change Child (CCH) screen on the PP customer's record:

  • If child already selected, type 'CCH' in Nxt: field and press [Enter]
  • Otherwise, 'S'elect child from Child Selection (CHS) screen, then 'S'elect Change Child on Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen
  • On the CCH screen, enter the child's date of death in Death Date: field. Note: the Reason: field is only updated for a stillborn child. (PP is not payable for a stillborn child)
  • Press [Enter] to go to the FTB Child Bereavement Assistance Delivery Option (FBRV) screen, where the customer's payment option for their Family Tax Benefit bereavement payment is entered in Payment Delivery Option: field. See Bereavement payment of Family Tax Benefit (FTB)

Finalise the activity via the AR screen. If the customer is receiving PP, a review will be set to mature on the day after the end of the 14 week bereavement period.

7

Last qualifying child + Read more ...

A PP customer is only entitled to payment for the bereavement period if the deceased child was their last qualifying PP child.

Is deceased child their last qualifying PP child?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Bereavement eligibility for last qualifying child + Read more ...

Death of the last qualifying PP child

The customer is eligible to receive PP for the 14 week bereavement. PP will continue for 14 weeks from the date of the child's death.

After the 14 week bereavement period has ended, the customer's PP record will be automatically cancelled with reason code 'NDE' (no dependants eligible).

Advise the customer that if they need ongoing income support, they will need to claim another payment at the end of the bereavement period.

See:

  • Transfer to JobSeeker Payment (JSP) from another payment
  • Transfer to Youth Allowance (YA) (Job seeker) from another payment
  • Transfer to Austudy or Youth Allowance (student) from another payment

Parenting Payment Single (PPS)

The customer's CRN will need to be sent to the Parenting Payment programme team mailbox if the customer is receiving PPS and:

  • their only qualifying child was under 6 years of age, and
  • they have no earnings prior to the death of their child

This is because as soon as the child death action is coded, the system will make the customer a fortnightly reporter. As long as the customer's circumstances have not changed (they have no earnings or other income), the Parenting Payment programme team will apply a work around so the customer is not required to report during the bereavement period.

Parenting Payment Partnered

The customer’s CRN will need to be sent to the Parenting Payment programme team mailbox. This is because the system calculates the 14 week bereavement period from the beginning of the entitlement period in which the child died instead of from the child’s date of death. This means the customer may not receive PPP for the full 14 week bereavement period. The Parenting Payment programme team will apply a workaround to manually pay any additional amount owing at the end of the bereavement period.

See the Resources page for a link to the Parenting and Relationship Status Programmes team for the Parenting Payment programme team’s mailbox address.

Go to Step 10.

9

Customer has other PP children + Read more ...

The customer's PP eligibility is not affected as they still have a PP child.

For PPS customers:

  • their rate may decrease if they receive income, as their income free area will reduce
  • if the customer has part-time mutual obligation requirements, an exemption may be applied for the duration of the bereavement period

10

Family assistance + Read more ...

The customer may be entitled to family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payments after the death of a child.

This includes a bereavement payment of Family Tax Benefit (FTB). The customer may choose to receive their FTB bereavement payment as instalments or as a lump sum payment.

A customer making contact about a payment must be invited to make a claim for payment, even if their eligibility to that payment is in doubt:

  • Offer the customer the option of claiming online. Note: to claim online the customer must have an active Centrelink online account
  • If it is unreasonable for the customer to claim online, offer:
    • an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), or
    • a Claim for Paid Parental Leave and Family Tax Benefit (FA100) for be issued
  • Also see Helping customers in crisis or financial hardship claim family assistance

11

Document record + Read more ...

Record the details of the child's bereavement on a DOC on the PP customer's record.

Create a Manual letter Q353 for the customer.
