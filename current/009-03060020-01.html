Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA) Child to Adult Transfer (CTAT) 009-03060020

This document outlines the CTAT process for carers in receipt of CP and/or CA when the child is turning 16 years of age (or 18 years for CP child with a terminal condition).

CTAT invitation letter

Carers are sent a CTAT invitation letter when their child care receiver turns:

  • 15 years and 6 months
  • or 17 years and 9 months (for a child with a terminal condition)

The invitation letter:

  • invites the carer to submit a transfer to CP and/or CA for an adult care receiver. Note: carers can only submit a transfer for the payment they currently receive. For example, if the carer currently only receives CA, they cannot apply to transfer to CP
  • advises the carer to submit their transfer by the due date if they want their payments to be assessed under adult provisions
  • informs the carer of the cancellation date of:
    • child payment, or
    • Health Care Card (HCC) only CA (child)

Note: prior to 1 July 2022 CTAT invitations were generally sent when the child turned 15 years and 9 months. Most carers with a child born between 1 October 2006 and 31 December 2006 inclusive were sent an invitation on 1 July 2022.

CTAT due dates

If the child was born before 1 January 2007, the CTAT will be due by the time the child is:

  • 16 years for CA Health Care Card (HCC) only
  • 16 years 3 months for CP, and/or CA

If the child was born on or after 1 January 2007, the CTAT will be due when the child is 16 years for CP and/or CA (including CA HCC only).

If the carer is being paid Carer Payment for a child with a terminal illness, the CTAT will be due when the child turns 18 years.

Short term/episodic care period

Carers who receive CP for a child with a short term or episodic condition will be invited to submit a CTAT. However the CTAT must be processed as a new claim and should only be processed when the short term or episodic medical certificate has expired.

Payment guarantee

The payment guarantee applies for carers of children born on or after 1 January 2007 (or 1 April 2005 if the carer is being paid Carer Payment for a child with a terminal illness).

If the carer submits their completed CTAT on or before the child turns 16 years, they will continue to receive CA and/or CP until the CTAT is processed, even if it is not processed until after the child turns 16 years and 3 months (or 18 years if terminal).

The payment guarantee does not apply to CA HCC only.

Outcome of CTAT for Carer Allowance (CA) Health Care Card (HCC) only

The outcome of the CTAT will be to either:

  • cancel CA HCC when child turns 16 years, 3 months and 1 day (or 16 years if born before 1 January 2007), or
  • grant CA under the adult rules from the later date of when the child turned 16 years, or CTAT was submitted

Outcome of CTAT for Carer Payment (CP) for a child with a terminal illness

The outcome of the CTAT will be to:

  • cancel CP and/or CA, or
  • continue CP and/or CA under the adult rules

If the child is born on or after 1 April 2005, the date of effect for the decision is the later date of when:

  • child turns 18 years and 1 day, or
  • the CTAT is processed

If the child is born before 1 April 2005, the date of effect for the decision is the date the child turns 18 years.

Outcome of CTAT for CP and CA payment level - child born before 1 January 2007

The outcome of the CTAT will be to:

  • cancel CP and/or CA
  • continue CP and/or CA under the adult rules

The date of effect for the decision is the later date of when:

  • child turns 16 years, or
  • the CTAT is processed

Note: CA does not have a Health Care Card for adult care receivers. Carers eligible for CP are entitled to a Pensioner Concession Card (PCC).

Outcome of CTAT for CP and CA payment level - child born on or after 1 January 2007

The carer will continue to be paid under the child rules up to and including the day the child turns 16 years and 3 months. They will continue to be paid under the child rules past this date until the CTAT is processed if they submitted the CTAT on or before the child turned 16 years.

The outcome of the CTAT will be to:

  • Cancel CP and CA
  • Continue CP and/or CA under adult rules

The date of effect for the decision is the later date of when:

  • child turns 16 years, 3 months and 1 day, or
  • the CTAT is processed

Ex Carer Allowance Health Care Card for child

The child can claim for an Ex Carer Allowance Health Care Card. They can do this even if the carer does not submit a CTAT.

Reminder message

There are 2 different reminders carers may get about their CTAT:

  • when a carer has answered the questions in their CTAT task and all required tasks have been completed but the CTAT remains not submitted. This will be sent to their myGov inbox or via SMS message
  • 2 months before the CTAT is due, carers will be automatically sent a reminder message if they have not yet submitted a CTAT. This will be sent to their myGov inbox, via SMS message or letter
