Call escalation to a Programme Support Officer (PSO)

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Advise of call transfer + Read more ...

Establish if the call is to be transferred to a PBS PSO or PBS Complex Drugs PSO.

Tell the caller they will be placed on hold while the call is transferred.

2

Initiate contact + Read more ...

Press the Conference button on the softphone.

This action automatically places the caller on hold.

Softphone option:

  • PBS PSO
  • PBS Complex Drugs PSO line

Establish if the call will be accepted by PSO.

If the PSO line is busy and customer has been on hold, see The PSO line is busy table.

If there are no PSOs available, complete the PSO Referral Form.

3

Advise and discuss issue + Read more ...

Initiate contact with the PSO and discuss issue.

Service Officers must provide:

  • name
  • position
  • the line the call originated from
  • brief description/reason for escalating the call
  • background information
  • any questions asked and the caller’s responses

4

Action according to PSO response + Read more ...

The PSO will respond by either:

  • providing advice to Service Officers to complete the call. Go to Step 6
  • suggest a conference call with the caller. Go to Step 5

5

Initiate a warm transfer if required + Read more ...

To initiate a warm transfer:

  • press the Conference button on the softphone
  • introduce caller to the PSO
  • PSO takes over the call

Note: Service Officers can remain on the line and listen in where appropriate to identify learning gaps.

6

Complete the work for the transaction if required + Read more ...

Complete the call by actioning any directions given by the PSO.

Call escalation to a Pharmaceutical Adviser (PA)

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Advise of call transfer + Read more ...

Establish if the call is to be transferred to a PA

Tell the caller they will be placed on hold while the call is transferred.

2

Contact the PA line + Read more ...

  • Press the Conference button on the softphone
  • Select softphone option PBS PA. This action automatically places the caller on hold
  • Establish if the call will be accepted by PA

Note: if unable to contact PA, terminate the transfer to PA, record the caller details and advise a call back. See Table 5.

3

Advise and discuss issue + Read more ...

Initiate contact with the PA by advising:

  • name
  • staff logon and phone number
  • the line the call originated from
  • brief description/reason for escalating the call
  • background information
  • any questions asked and the caller's responses

PAs may require:

  • patient Medicare number
  • pharmacy approval number
  • caller’s prescriber number
  • prescriber’s phone number including area code

4

Action according to PA response + Read more ...

The PA will either:

  • provide advice to the Service Officer or Programme Support Officer (PSO) to complete the call. Go to Step 6
  • speak directly with the caller. Go to Step 5

5

Initiate a warm transfer if required + Read more ...

To initiate a warm transfer:

  • press the Conference button on the softphone
  • introduce caller to the PA and transfer the call
  • PA takes over the call

6

Complete the work for the transaction if required + Read more ...

Complete the call by actioning any direction given by the PA.

Enquiry about decision to approve/reject an authority approval - Service Officers

Table 6: This table describes the process for Service Officers when an approved prescriber or patient enquires about a decision to approve or reject an authority approval.

Step

Action

1

Enquiry received about a decision to approve or reject an authority approval request to prescribe a PBS subsidised medicine + Read more ...

If the enquiry is received from:

  • a patient, go to Step 2
  • a prescriber, go to Step 3

2

Enquiry from a patient via telephone or email + Read more ...

Tell the patient to discuss the issue with their prescriber in the first instance.

If the patient has concerns regarding the outcome of the authority approval request, encourage them to talk to their approved prescriber regarding their concerns.

Tell the patient, if the approved prescriber believes they are eligible for the PBS subsidised treatment in accordance with the restriction criteria, the approved prescriber can write an authority prescription or telephone the authorities line to seek authority approval to prescribe the PBS subsidised medication.

Note: Approved prescribers are obliged to be self-satisfied that the patient is eligible to receive treatment under the PBS. An approved prescriber must determine if all elements of the restriction criteria are met as per the PBS website. If the request is submitted in writing, the approved prescriber must provide sufficient information for the Service Officer to assess the request. If the approved prescriber contacts via phone, they are required to answer any questions attached to the item.

Encourage patients to discuss eligibility including the PBS restriction criteria for the medicine in question with their approved prescriber and enquire whether they may be eligible for other suitable PBS subsidised treatments available for the condition.

If a patient disagrees with their approved prescriber in relation to eligibility for PBS subsidised medication, they may choose to seek a second opinion from an alternative medical practitioner.

Should the approved prescriber have any concerns regarding the outcome of the authority approval request, they should be encouraged to discuss these with the Service Officer at the time the request to prescribe is made. Escalate the call to a PSO or PA if necessary.

Procedure ends here.

3

Enquiry from approved prescriber via telephone regarding the outcome of an authority decision + Read more ...

The Service Officer is to:

  • provide the approved prescriber with an explanation of the decision outcome
  • advise that authority for PBS subsidised medicines can only be provided when the PBS medicine restrictions have been met

Is the approved prescriber satisfied with the response?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Approved prescriber is not satisfied with the response and requests a review of the decision - Service Officer + Read more ...

  • Locate the file in Workload Management (WLM), check the letter attached or any notes from previous Service Officers
  • Discuss the reject reasons with the prescriber as per the information received
  • If the prescriber would like to discuss further, warm transfer the call to a Pharmaceutical Advisor (PA)

Enquiry about decision to approve/reject an authority approval - PA

Table 7 This table describes the process for Pharmaceutical Advisers (PA) when an approved prescriber enquires about a decision to approve or reject an authority approval.

Step

Action

1

Approved prescriber is not satisfied with the response and requests a review of the decision - PA + Read more ...

The Pharmaceutical Adviser (PA) will:

  • Investigate the outcome of the previous decision and consider whether the original decision was appropriate including a review of any additional information or circumstances
  • Communicate to the approved prescriber an overview of the reasons the PBS medicine restrictions have not been met in the individual case
  • Outline the role of Services Australia in administering the PBS in line with the legislation and policies for which the Department of Health is responsible
  • Confirm that Services Australia has no discretion to grant special access to subsidised medicines outside of the restriction criteria
  • Use information available on the record and/or authority application
  • Provide advice of other options for exploring access to subsidised medicines. These may include:
    • approaching the sponsor pharmaceutical company
    • enquire regarding compassionate access programs
    • patient access through the public hospital
    • consulting with the approved prescriber’s professional body to lobby for a change to the restrictions
    • a private prescription may be written

Does the approved prescriber have information for exceptional circumstances that may exist for the patient to meet the restriction criteria?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 5

2

Exceptional circumstances may apply + Read more ...

If the approved prescriber presents information that demonstrates that exceptional circumstances prevent the patient from meeting the restriction criteria, the PA may consider referral to the Pharmaceutical Benefit's Branch (PBB) for consideration and advice on the intent of the restriction. PBB may escalate to the Department of Health if appropriate, where the:

  • PA is satisfied that facts are/can be substantiated. These may include a contraindication, inability to perform a required test or other legitimate compelling reason
  • Approved prescriber is prepared to submit a written request including any additional relevant evidence
  • Approved prescriber is advised that Services Australia has no administrative discretion to authorise the request nor can provide comment on the likely outcome, however, the PA will commit to escalating the request for consideration and will provide a response in due course

Is the case suitable for referral to PBB?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Referral to Pharmaceutical Benefits Branch (PBB) + Read more ...

Once the PA receives the correspondence from the approved prescriber, they must summarise the reason the case should be referred to PBB, including the details outlining why the application for authority approval was rejected. Include:

  • the specific part of the restriction that was not met
  • whether the restriction is worded clearly and whether there is room for misinterpretation (in this case the restriction text should also be raised separately to mitigate any similar cases)
  • the urgency of the issue(s) raised
  • stating reason(s) why they believe the case has merit for referral

Refer the case to the PBB via email:

  • Fill out the PA escalation form and include a copy of the approved prescriber’s case and any further documents to support their claim
  • Email the PBS Listings and Authorities Policy (LAP) team mailbox

Note: LAP do not require a copy of the prescription, the details of this should be included in the PA summary.

When LAP respond, go to Step 4.

4

Response from Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) Listings and Authorities Policy (LAP) section + Read more ...

PBS LAP section will acknowledge the escalated case. Where necessary, the PBS LAP section may seek policy advice from the Department of Health.

PBS LAP will provide a recommendation to the PA for consideration. The PA is the final decision maker and will inform the approved prescriber of the outcome of the review.

If the PA decision is to:

  • approve the authority request, the PA can provide the authority approval number at the time of the call if possible. Procedure ends here
  • reject the authority request, inform the approved prescriber that following the review of the original decision, authority approval cannot be granted. Ideally this advice is provided via telephone, but may be in writing using the authority rejection letter template, dependent on the urgency of the case. If the approved prescriber remains dissatisfied with the response or the decision provided:
    • lodge the complaint in the Customer Feedback Tool for escalation
    • tell the approved prescriber they may wish to consider approaching the sponsor pharmaceutical company for access to subsidised medications/compassionate supply, contact the local public hospital for subsidy, write their patient a private prescription or consult with the professional body to lobby for a restriction change

Procedure ends here.

5

Other alternative options + Read more ...

Where referral to the PBB for consideration is not supported by the PA, tell the approved prescriber:

  • prescribing PBS subsidised medicines must be in line with the restrictions
  • PBS subsidised medicines are available for the listed clinical indications only
  • there is no mechanism for individual patient approval for indications outside those listed
  • confirm that Services Australia has no discretion or delegation to grant special access to subsidised medicines outside of the restriction criteria
  • the approved prescriber may wish to consider approaching the sponsor pharmaceutical company for access to subsidised medications/compassionate supply, contact the local public hospital for subsidy, or consult with the professional body to lobby for a restriction change
