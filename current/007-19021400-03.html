ACCS (Child Wellbeing) reassessment request

Table 1: This table describes how to action a reassessment review request form.

Step

Action

1

ACCS (Child Wellbeing) Review of a decision form + Read more ...

Child care services seeking a review of decision of an ACCS (Child Wellbeing) determination will complete a Review of a decision form and will upload the form with support evidence to a new determination request.

Where a child care service has lodged a Review of a decision form, the request at first instance should be treated as a request for reassessment of the original decision, rather than a new determination request.

The Review of a decision form should be completed by the service with the following details:

  • Service ID: The identification number for the child care service
  • Enrolment ID: The identification number pertaining to the enrolment for the child
  • Document ID: The identification number of the original determination the service is requesting a review
  • Determination Start Date: The start date of the rejected determination the service is requesting a review
  • Date of the decision (if known): The date the service received notification of the original determination outcome
  • Why do you believe the decision is incorrect? Field allowing the service to explain why the original decision should be reviewed
  • Do you have new evidence to support the determination? Yes/No option with a prompt to upload evidence
  • Requested by: Contact details of the requesting child care staff member

Note: where the provider or customer has lodged an appeal via the Internal Review/Explanation Script and the appeal has been allocated, see Table 5.

2

View previous certificate/determination history for the child + Read more ...

Paste the Child Customer Reference Number (CRN) details in the Filter Child CRN field and select Search icon.

Review the Child Wellbeing certificate and determination history for the child to identify any non-payment periods or rejected determinations.

Note: if the childcare provider is seeking a review, though the full period has already been approved for ACCS (Child Wellbeing), see Table 3.

If the request for Review of decision is related to:

  • certificates, go to Step 3
  • determinations, go to Step 4

3

Review of certificates + Read more ...

Services Australia are not required to conduct reassessments or reviews relating to certificates.

In the event a service lodges a new determination application which includes a review request relating to a certificate:

The Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE) are responsible for assessing certificate requests where exceptional circumstances warrant more than 28 days backdating. Certificates requiring DESE assessment will be classified as work item CWB Certificate with exceptional circ (ZCOC CCS J170) and must not be processed by Services Australia staff.

If an exceptional circumstance certificate application is incorrectly allocated for assessment, ensure the work item is classified correctly and unassign the work item back to the national allocation pool. See Work Optimiser for staff for information relating to unassigning and recategorising work items.

  • the determination is to be rejected with the reason 'Evidence does not demonstrate child at risk'
  • the service is to be contacted by phone and advised that any review or appeal for certificates be directed to the Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE)
  • create notes on the service record

Finalise the Work Item.

Procedure ends here.

4

Check Original Decision Maker (ODM) + Read more ...

DESE are responsible for assessing determination applications where exceptional circumstances warrant more than 28 days backdating.

Where DESE have made the original decision, the decision is to be reviewed by DESE staff. If Services Australia have made the original decision, the review will need to be actioned by Services Australia.

Providers may lodge new determinations as part of the ACCS (Child Wellbeing) review process using a date greater than 28 days prior to the original application, when the original application was less than 28 days prior to the lodgement date. This is causing the new determination to be assigned to DESE for review as the “Approving Agency,” however Services Australia was the ODM and these reviews will need to be actioned by Services Australia. Staff may amend the work items of identified determinations to allow correct allocation (see Table 6). Where this has occurred, the amended work items when allocated will display:

  • Approving Agency - Services Australia
  • Record Status - Provisional
  • Status - Rejected

If the original determination has been determined by DESE, see Work Optimiser for staff to recategorise the work item to CWB Determination with exceptional circ. (ZCOC_CCS_J180).

5

Review notes + Read more ...

Check the notes relating to the previous determination under review. Notes can be searched using the child CRN or Document ID numbers

  • select the Notes icon
  • filter the Text field by clicking on Text which will drop down a filter box
  • in the Filter box enter the Document ID or child CRN (try searching the CRN with and without gaps for example, XXXXXXXXXA or XXX XXX XXXA) then press [Enter]
  • locate the note relevant to the previous determination

Where the review relates to:

  • the start date of an approved determination, see Table 2
  • a rejected determination, go to Step 6

6

Determine if further evidence meets requirements + Read more ...

Determine whether the supporting evidence now meets evidence requirements. See Processing Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Child Wellbeing) determinations for more information regarding evidence requirements.

If the determination was rejected due to minimum basic evidence requirements and new evidence is uploaded, and the evidence now cumulatively meets requirements, staff can retrospectively review the determination.

Example

An application for the period 10/09/2018 - 09/12/2018 was rejected as the court order was dated more than 6 months in the past.

Provider lodged a new determination for dates 08/10/2018 - 06/01/2019 (due to system backdating limitations) and uploaded an ACCS Request for review form, asking for the assessment from 10/09/2018 to be reviewed. The provider also uploaded a letter dated 10/11/2018 from state based child protection stating that the court order remains in force and the child continues to be in foster care.

Cumulatively, the evidence now meets minimum basic requirements; therefore, the original assessment and any additional evidence can be reviewed favourably and retrospectively applied.

Does the evidence provided now meet the ACCS (Child Wellbeing) evidence requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, the reassessment is to be rejected as the evidence does not meet minimum requirements. See Step 3 in Table 3

7

Review made within 13 weeks of being notified of the original decision + Read more ...

Under Family Assistance Law, the time limit for seeking a review of an original decision relating to Child Care Subsidy or Additional Child Care Subsidy is 13 weeks after the person is notified of the decision. This is unless special circumstances prevented the person from applying for a review.

The time taken to deliver the notice must be taken into account when deciding if a person has requested a review within 13 weeks of being given the notice. See Customer initiated review of decision.

Determine if the review was requested within 13 weeks of being notified of the original decision (e.g. check dates on the rejection letters issued, or notes on the record).

Was the review made within 13 weeks of being notified of the original decision?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, the reassessment is to be rejected. See Step 3 in Table 3

8

Determine if evidence supports assessment of child being at risk + Read more ...

For more information, see Processing Additional Child Care Subsidy ACCS (Child Wellbeing) determinations.

Does the evidence support the assessment of the child being at risk?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, the reassessment is to be rejected as the evidence does not support the child being assessed as at risk. See Step 3 in Table 3

Action

Provider is seeking a review of the assessment start date + Read more ...

The provider has indicated in the ACCS (Child Wellbeing) Request for review form that they are requesting reassessment to the start date of an approved determination.

If the provider is requesting the start date to apply from:

  • a later date than what was originally assessed, go to Step 2

Start date to apply from a later date than originally assessed + Read more ...

In the event that a service lodged a new determination application with the review request or if unfavourable:

  • the new determination is to be rejected with the reason 'Evidence does not demonstrate child at risk'
  • create notes on the service record

3

Start date to apply from an earlier date than original determination + Read more ...

Determinations can only be backdated 28 days from the date of lodgement.

To check the date of lodgement:

  • navigate to the Child Wellbeing (Super Key CWB), select the original determination twisty and note Related Document transaction number
  • select the Transactions icon and twist the Applications line
  • locate relevant transaction number and note Submitted date

Is the childcare service seeking a start date review more than 28 days prior to the original approved determination lodgement date (date submitted)?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Start date more than 28 days prior to the original approved determination lodgement date + Read more ...

The Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE) manage any exceptional circumstances requests for additional backdating more than 28 days prior to lodgement. If a review is received requesting additional backdating, more than 28 days prior to original determination lodgement date, see Work Optimiser for staff to recategorise the work item to CWB Determination with exceptional circ (ZCOC CCS J180).

Procedure ends here.

5

Start date less than 28 days prior to the original approved determination lodgement date + Read more ...

As the original assessment was previously approved, there is no requirement to conduct a full ACCS (Child Wellbeing) assessment.

The existing approved assessment must not be altered, regardless of the start date reassessment outcome. Any favourable coding is to be completed in the provisional determination line to both acknowledge and assess the request for reassessment.

Process the new determination in accordance with Processing Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Child wellbeing) determinations, ensuring:

  • the start date is edited to match the start date the provider is requesting the assessment to be backdated to, and
  • the Risk Weeks are edited to adjust the Risk End date to the Sunday prior to the existing approved assessment

Go to Step 6 to record reassessment notes.

ACCS (Child Wellbeing) appeal

1

Record the ACCS (Child Wellbeing) appeal + Read more ...

ACCS (Child Wellbeing) Review of decision forms are to be treated firstly as a request for reassessment of entitlement.

If a reassessment of ACCS (Child Wellbeing) has been conducted and the outcome is still unfavourable, the provider is taken to have engaged their appeal rights. Therefore, the appeal is to be recorded via the Internal Review / Explanation script in Customer Record. ACCS (Child Wellbeing) appeal allocated to ACCS Subject Matter Expert (SME)

Table 5

Step

Action

1

ACCS (Child Wellbeing) appeal source + Read more ...

As ACCS (Child Wellbeing) decisions affect both the child care service and the customer, both parties have a right of review and can appeal a decision regarding an ACCS (Child Wellbeing) determination. A customer may:

  • request an explanation of decision by an ACCS SME, or
  • apply for a formal review of decision

Has the customer lodged an application for a formal review?

  • Yes, an Authorised Review Officer (ARO) will review the decision and try to contact the customer. For more details, see Reviews by an Authorised Review Officer ARO. Procedure ends here
  • No, a request for explanation has been lodged, go to Step 2

For all other action, including withdrawal of an explanation or application for a formal review, allocation of ARO further action required work item, see SME explanations, ARO referrals and implementing ARO decisions.

2

ACCS (Child Wellbeing) explanation of decision requested by customer + Read more ...

Where the ACCS (Child Wellbeing) explanation of decision was requested by the customer:

  • Check if the Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE) made the original decision. While Services Australia does not have jurisdiction to review DESE decisions, a SME can provide an explanation of the decision and should explain there is no jurisdiction for a formal review to be undertaken. Customers will need to be referred to DESE helpdesk to engage any appeal rights. If the customer:
    • still wants a formal review, the decision must be referred to an ARO. The ARO will send the customer a ‘no jurisdiction’ letter confirming the agency has no jurisdiction to review the decision
    • does not wish to proceed with the formal review, the explanation can be withdrawn. See Request for an explanation or application for a formal review

Refer to SME explanations, ARO referrals and implementing ARO decisions for Process detail on conducting initial checks and completing an explanation of decision. There is no requirement to record progress notes on the Provider record.
