This page contains information relating to financial investments that are liable to deeming provisions. Some investments may be exempt from deeming provisions in special circumstances.

Deeming exemptions

Deeming Exemption Register

This table describes information relating to the Deeming Exemption Register that contains information about the income test and assets test treatment of investments with companies and financial institutions in financial difficulty. The register has information about these investments where an application for exemption has been granted.

Step

Action

1

Deeming exemptions + Read more ...

Deeming exemptions are provided for failed investments, not for those in short-term difficulties.

Note: payments to customers for homes purchased under the ACT Government Loose-fill Asbestos Insulation Eradication Scheme or the NSW Voluntary Purchase and Demolition Program are to have the payment exempt from deeming. For further information, see Exempt Assets.

2

New investment or change to an existing investment + Read more ...

If this is a new investment or a change to an existing investment, the customer should provide evidence, for example investment certificate, investment statement or letter(s) from the investment company or Insolvency Practitioner as evidence of investment failure.

Investment enquiries can be done over the phone, but all new investment acquisitions should be done at the Service Centre.

3

The customer contacts and advises their investments have changed and/or failed + Read more ...

Check the A-H Deeming Exemption Register and I-Z Deeming Exemption Register for the investment. Note: the name of the investment on the customer's documentation must match exactly the name on the Deeming Exemption Register. Ask the customer to provide the name as shown on the documentation for their investment.

Is the investment listed in the Deeming Exemption Register?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Investment not listed in Deeming Exemption Register + Read more ...

Does the customer wish to apply for their investment to be exempt from deeming rules?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, and
    • it is a new investment, code investment on customer's record. See Coding income and assets. Procedure ends here
    • it is an existing investment, record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

5

For a church or charitable organisation + Read more ...

If the investment is new or an addition to the customer's current investment in a church or charitable organisation (which currently has an exemption) there is no exemption available.

If the customer's current investment in a church or charitable organisation currently has an exemption, their exemption remains and any actual return they earn or receive must be declared for assessment under the Income Test. This includes interest or any benefit such as a discount in fees (i.e. valuable consideration).

If a current customer already has an exemption granted for an investment with a deeming exempt church or charitable development fund, and their partner claims a payment after 1 January 2010, the exemption will flow to the partner's record for the same investment only.

Any extra investment to the same fund or a new account/investment is deemed and requires a separate line of coding on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen.

For all other applications

All other applications for deeming exemptions are seen by the Financial Information Services (FIS) Officer, who will discuss the customer's situation and provide the application form for the customer to complete.

FIS Officer appointment required.

Make an appointment with the FIS officer

Record reasons for the FIS Officer interview on a DOC.

6

Documents required at FIS Officer interview + Read more ...

Advise customer they need to supply as much information of their financial circumstances at the interview as they can. A minimum amount of information should include:

  • investment reference number
  • name of the debtor
  • amount of the loan
  • name of investment
  • details of current status
  • any correspondence issued by the investment provider or other relevant source (for example, Administrator, Receiver or Liquidator)

The FIS Officer will explain the process and advise the customer on completion of the application for a Deeming Exemption for a Failed Financial Investment.

When the application form is completed and returned, it is scanned for processing (with the investment documentation and evidence of failure) and forwarded to the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO):

  • run the CAO referral script or CAO Referral Guided Procedure
  • transfer the DOC to region code CAO

7

Investment listed in Deeming Exemption Register + Read more ...

Advise customer the investment has already been granted a deeming exemption and the actual income received from the investment will be used to calculate assessable income.

Coding the investment

Is the investment type either debentures or loans?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, code the exempt investment on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen. Go to Step 9

8

Coding debentures and loans + Read more ...

There is currently a display problem with debentures and loans coded with a deeming exemption on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen. When a deeming exemption is coded on the SVDI screen, the exemption displays back to the first page or commencement of the investment. Note: it is only a display problem and not an assessment problem.

Although the system coding method is not wrong and the system will not assess any deemed income, due to a display problem it will not show when the deeming ceased for the investment.

Workaround till further notice:

For debentures and loans, search the Savings Summary (SVS) screen for the deeming exempt investment currently on the customer's record.

  • Zero the value or unit number to nil using the date the deeming exemption is applicable from

The deeming exempt investment needs to be recorded back on the customer's record. Go to the SVDI screen.

9

On the SVDI screen enter the following information + Read more ...

  • Investment Name: field, enter the investment name
  • Type: field, enter the investment type
  • Balance: field, enter the $ amount as previously recorded on zeroed investment
  • Interest %: field, enter the actual rate of return. Interest rate should only be coded if 'Y' is coded in the Deeming Exempt: field. Note: if interest rate is not available and customer can provide the annual income received, the percentage rate can be worked out. (For example customer has $10 000 invested and advise they have received $250. The interest rate is (250/10 000) x 100 = 2.5%.) The system then calculates the income and displays it on the Financial Investment Summary (IVIS) screen in the Deem Ex I: field
  • Event Date: field, enter date the exemption is applicable from
  • Deeming Exempt: field, enter 'Y' for yes
  • DOR: field, enter same date as the Event Date: field

Press [Enter].

The Savings Summary (SVS) screen displays showing the updated details.

Complete the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Record details on a DOC.
