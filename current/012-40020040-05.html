Description and reason for use

366

Description - Benefit paid for Rule 3 Exemption item

  • When processing paper and online pathology claims staff must:
    • Enter Reason Code (RSN) 366 on all eligible pathology item numbers that are endorsed ‘Rule 3 exempt’ or ‘R3EX’ before assessing any other pend messages

Scenarios for processing pathology claims

Table 8

Scenario

Processing action

Group P1-P8 - PA line keyed

When a pathology request (PA) line has been keyed for pathology item(s) from Group P1 to Group P8 and no error conditions apply, the system calculates the Medicare benefit payable and the payment process continues in the normal manner.

Group P1-P8 - PI 11 keyed

When processing indicator (PI) 11 (pathology service has been self-determined) has been keyed for pathology item (s) from Group P1 to Group P8 and no error conditions apply, the system calculates the Medicare benefit payable and the payment process continues in the normal manner.

Group P1-P8 item - PA line or PI 11 required

When a pathology request (PA) line or processing indicator (PI) 11 has not been keyed for a pathology item(s) from group P1 to Group P8, the system displays the message ‘4-PATH RQ’. Check keying, correct any errors and press [ENTER].

If Self Determined or deemed necessary, key PI 11.

If the request details are invalid or not supplied, reject the patient episode with RSN 137.

Group P9 item - PA line or PI 11 not keyed

When pathology item/s from Group P9 has been keyed without a pathology request (PA) line or processing indicator (P1) 11 and no error conditions apply, the system calculates the Medicare benefit payable and the payment process continues in the normal manner.

Group P9 item - PI 11 keyed

When pathology item/s from Group P9 has been keyed with processing indicator (PI) 11 and no error conditions apply, the system calculates the Medicare benefit payable and the payment process continues in the normal manner.

Group P9 item - PA line keyed

When a pathology item from Group 9 has been keyed with a pathology request (PA) line and no error conditions apply, the system calculates the Medicare benefit payable and the payment process continues in the normal manner.

Scenarios for Patient Episode Coning pathology (PEC) claims processing

Table 9

Scenario

Description

PEC - all pathology items on screen exempt from PEC rule - no history items exist for same patient episode

When pathology items that are not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule are keyed on the claims payment screen, and no pathology items relating to the same patient episode are on the patient's claims history, Medicare benefit is calculated in the normal manner.

Example:

  • all of the items are admitted patient items which are not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule
  • there are not pathology items relating to the same patient episode on the patient's claims history
  • the message 8-NOTL CHG is displayed because a notional charge has been keyed for the items involved
  • the system has calculated the Medicare benefit payable and the payment process can continue in the normal manner

Note: they system treats other items that are not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule (for example pathology services requested by specialists or consultant physicians, etc.) in a similar manner.

PEC - more than 3 pathology items on screen - no history items exist for same patient episode

When more than 3 items of pathology testing are requested by a general practitioner for a non-admitted patient, and no pathology items relating to the same patient episode are on the patient's claims history, the system automatically applies the Patient Episode Coning Rule and displays the message 8-PATH CON to advise that the Patient Episode Coning Rule has been applied.

Example:

  • five of the pathology items involved are subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule, items 65060, 66500, 69333, 69384 and 71097
  • item 73907 is a patient episode initiation (PEI) item and PEI items are not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule
  • the system calculates the Medicare benefit payment for the 3 items with the highest schedule fee, items 71097, 69333 and 69384
  • because Medicare benefits are limited to the 3 items with the highest schedule fee in a patient episode, items 65060 and 66500 have been rejected by the system with RSN 173
  • RSN 173 results in the explanation 'Patient episode coning - maximum number of services paid' printed on the patient’s claims history and Statement of Benefit

When service details have been keyed correctly and no error conditions apply, the payment process continues in the normal manner.

PEC - more than 3 pathology items on screen - no history items exist for same patient episode - notional charge involved in a patient claim

When more than 3 items of pathology testing are requested by a general practitioner for a non-admitted patient, and no pathology items relating to the same patient episode are on the patient's claims history and a notional charge has been keyed, the system automatically applies the Patient Episode Coning Rule and displays the message:

  • 8-NOTL CHG
  • 5-REQ CHG
  • 8-PATH CON

Example 1:

  • the message 8-NOTL CHG is displayed because a notional charge has been keyed for the items involved
  • four of the pathology items involved are subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule, items 65070, 66515, 69333 and 65120
  • item 73907 is a patient episode initiation (PEI) item which is not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule
  • because Medicare benefits are limited to the 3 items with the highest schedule fees in a patient episode, item 65120 has been rejected by the system with reason code 173 (patient episode coning - maximum number of services paid) and the message 8-PATH CON is displayed to advise that the Patient Episode Coning Rule has been applied
  • the message 5-REQ CHG displays because the system is not able to apportion any of the notional charge to the items that follow a rejected item

When the message 5-REQ CHG displays because an item within the notional charge has been rejected:

  • insert an item line containing details of the rejected item immediately before the pathology request (PA) line involved
  • delete the original rejected item line

This has the effect of removing the rejected item from the notional charge group and the system is then able to apportion the notional charge over all payable items.

To insert an item line in a patient claim, key the insert symbol (>) in the first character of the PATIENT field on the relevant line to key the details.

Example 2:

The insert symbol (>) has been keyed over the duplication symbol (*) on the PA line ready for the item line to be inserted. When [Enter] is pressed, the system inserts a blank item line.

Example 3:

When an item line has been inserted, the system removes all messages from the screen and converts any duplication symbols (*) on the following line to the appropriate values. Details for the rejected item can now be keyed on the blank item line.

Example 4:

To delete an item line in a patient claim, key the delete symbol (<) in the first character of the PATIENT field.

  • details of the rejected item (65120) have been rekeyed on the blank item line immediately before the PA line and the original payee code (5) has been used
  • reject code (E), reject indicator (09) and reason code (173) shown in the original rejected line have not been keyed on the item line that is being inserted
  • the delete symbol (<) has been keyed over the duplication symbol (*) on the original rejected line ready for it to be deleted

When [Enter] is pressed, the system deletes the original rejected item line and reassesses the claim.

Example 5:

  • the system has reassessed the claim and the messages 8-NOTL CHG (notional charge involved) and 8-PATH CON (Patient Episode Coning Rule has been applied) are now displayed
  • the pathology item with the lowest schedule fee (65120) has been automatically rejected by the system and reason code 173 (patient episode coning - maximum number of services paid) inserted
  • the message 5-REQ CHG is no longer displayed because the system has been able to apportion the notional charge across all of the payable items - this is possible because the rejected item is now the last item in the pathology group and can be excluded from the notional charge

After the payment process has been complete, the system inserts reason code 113 (total charge shown on account apportioned over all paid items) against all payable items included in the notional charge group on the patient's claims history and on the Statement of Benefit.

  • Note: this method of processing can be used in all situations where the message 5-REQ CHG displays and the rejected item forms part of a notional charge.

PEC - all pathology items on screen exempt from PEC rule - history items exist for same patient episode

When pathology items relating to the same patient episode are on the patient's claims history, the system displays the message 1-PREV PA and highlights the request date in the pathology request (PA) line involved. Details of the pathology item from history that relate to the same patient episode are displayed at the bottom of the screen.

Example 1:

  • all of the items on the screen are admitted patient items which are not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule
  • pathology items held on the patient's claims history for the same patient episode (that is, those associated with a PA line on history that has the same request date where the requesting provider is a general practitioner) are displayed at the bottom of the screen
  • pathology items displayed in the history segment at the bottom of the screen include items that are not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule, that is, item 73907 is a PEI item which is not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule

In this situation, the Service Officer must determine whether the pathology items on the claims payment screen and on the patient's claims history are subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule.

Because all of the pathology items on the claims payment screen are admitted patient items which are not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule:

  • key reason code CTD (continue processing the pathology claim) in the RSN field on the PA line involved

Example 2:

When [Enter] is pressed, the system calculates the Medicare benefit payable.

Example 3:

When service details have been keyed correctly and no error conditions apply, the payment process continues in the normal manner.

Note: this method of processing applies in all situations where the message 1-PREV PA is displayed and all of the pathology items on the claims payment screen are not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule.

PEC - some pathology items on screen and history exempt from PEC rule - history items exist for same patient episode - more than 3 items subject to PEC rule – item with lowest schedule fee in current claim

When pathology items relating to the same patient episode are on the patient's claims history, the system displays the message 1-PREV PA and highlights the request date in the pathology request (PA) line involved. Details of the pathology item from history that relate to the same patient episode are displayed at the bottom of the screen.

Example 1:

  • there are a total of 6 pathology items for the same patient episode, that is, items 69333, 66515, 65120 and 73915 on the screen and items 73907 and 65070 in the history segment displayed at the bottom of the screen
  • items 73915 and 73907 are patient episode initiation (PEI) items which are not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule
  • when all the PEI items are excluded from the total number of pathology items involved, 4 items remain that are subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule

In this situation, compare the schedule fees for the items that are subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule because only the 3 items with the highest fees are payable.

When considering payment of the 3 highest items, items in the history segment that have already been paid must be taken in account.

Because only the 3 items with the highest schedule fees can be paid:

  • reject the remaining item (which has the lowest schedule fee) with reason code 173 (patient episode coning - maximum number of services paid)
  • key reason code CTD (continue processing the pathology claim) in the RSN field on the PA line involved

Example 2:

  • items 69333, 65070 and 66515 are the items with the highest schedule fees and these are the items that are payable
  • item 65120 is the item with the lowest schedule fee and it has been rejected with reason code 173 (patient episode coning - maximum number of services paid)
  • reason code CTD (continue processing the pathology claim) has been keyed in the RSN field on the pathology request (PA) line involved

When [Enter] is pressed, the system calculates the Medicare benefit payable and displays the message 8-PATH CON to advise that the Patient Episode Coning Rule has been applied.

When service details have been keyed correctly and no error conditions apply, the payment process continues in the normal manner.

PEC - some pathology items on screen and history exempt from PEC rule - history items exist for same patient episode - more than 3 items subject to PEC rule - item with lowest schedule fee already paid

When pathology items relating to the same patient episode are on the patient's claims history, the system displays the message 1-PREV PA and highlights the request date in the pathology request (PA) line involved. Details of the pathology item from history that relate to the same patient episode are displayed at the bottom of the screen.

Example 1:

  • there are a total of 5 pathology items for the same patient episode, that is, item 69333, 66515, 65070 and 73907 on the screen and item 65120 in the history segment displayed at the bottom of the screen
  • item 73907 is a patient episode initiation (PEI) item which is not subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule
  • when PEI items are excluded from the total number of pathology items, 4 items remain that are subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule

In this situation, compare the schedule fees for the items that are subject to the Patient Episode Coning Rule because only the 3 items with the highest schedule fees are payable.

When considering payment of the 3 items with the highest schedule fee, items from history that have already been paid must be taken into account.

Where there is an item in the history segment with a lower schedule fee than the items on the screen, an adjustment is required.

As there is an item in the history segment with a lower schedule fee:

  • one of the remaining 3 screen items must be adjusted with the steps below the next example
  • key reason code CTD (continue processing the pathology claim) in the RSN field on the PA line involved

Example 2:

  • items 69333, 65070 and 66515 are the items with the highest schedule fees and these are the items that are payable
  • item 65120 has the lowest schedule fee but it has already been paid and is on history
  • the schedule fee ranking for the pathology items involved is 69333 ($20.55), 65070 ($16.95), 66515 ($19.80) and 65120 ($13.70)
  • because of the schedule fee ranking, item 65070 is the item that should have been paid instead of item 65120
  • a lower amount must be paid for item 65070 to take into account the benefit already paid for item 65120 - this is achieved by keying the following additional information for item 65070:
    • processing indicator (PI) 58 (use fee entered in FEE field to calculate benefit) in the PI field
    • an adjusted schedule fee amount of $3.25* in the FEE field
    • reason code 174 (patient episode coning adjust) in the RSN field
  • reason code CTD (continue processing of pathology claims) have been keyed in the RSN field on the pathology request (PA) line

The amount that must be keyed in the FEE field in an adjustment situation must be manually calculated. The calculation involved in this example are shown below:

  • rank the items in schedule fee order
  • determine the item that is to be adjusted
  • subtract the fee amount for the item already paid with the lowest schedule fee from the normal fee payable for the item to be adjusted
  • it is the fee difference amount ($3.25) in this example which is to be keyed in the FEE field of the item to be adjusted

After all of the details have been keyed and [Enter] pressed, the system calculates the Medicare benefit payable.

Note: where more than one item is to be adjusted, the process outlined above is repeated for each item involved. When selecting the item to be adjusted, make sure that the normal fee for the item being adjusted is sufficient to allow the subtraction of the fee for the item that has already been paid.

Example 3:

  • the system used the amount of $3.00 keyed in the FEE field to calculate the amount to be paid for item 65070 which is the item being adjusted
  • When manually calculating the benefit, multiply the fee difference by:
    • 0.85 for outpatient services, or
    • 0.75 for inpatient services
  • the amount paid for item 65070 is $2.55 which would match the difference in benefit that was manually calculated
  • the message 8-PATH CON displays to advise that the Patient Episode Coning Rule has been applied
  • the message 8-CHG HIGH displays to advise that the charge for item 65070 appears too high - this has occurred because of the adjusted fee amount ($3.00) that was keyed in the FEE field

If the amount being paid by the system for the item involved in the adjustment does not match the difference in benefit that was manually calculated:

  • check the manual calculation and the details keyed, correct any errors and press [Enter]
  • if the manual calculations are correct, adjust the amount keyed in the FEE field (one cent at a time) until the system arrives at the correct amount to be paid

When service details have been keyed correctly and no error conditions apply, the payment process continues in the normal manner.

Note: when an item is being adjusted and the message 8-100% CAL is displayed in conjunction with the message 8-PATH CON, it indicates that the claimant has reached their Safety net threshold and is entitled to a 100% Medicare benefit. In this situation, the amount keyed in FEE field should be changed to match the benefit difference that was manually calculated before the claim is paid.
