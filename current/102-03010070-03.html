AIC change of address

Information on changing an address for AIC applicants or students

This table describes information on changing an address for Assistance for Isolated Children Scheme (AIC) applicants and/or students. Portions of this process are done by Service Officers in Assistance for Isolated children (AIC) only. Lookup local Processing Service details for: Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC)

Step

Action

1

Change of address for applicant or student + Read more ...

AIC involves any of following payments:

  • AIC Boarding Allowance (BA)
  • Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA)
  • Second Home Allowance (SHA)
  • Distance Education Allowance (DED)
  • AIC Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)

When an Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme applicant advises:

  • a change to the student's term address only, Go to Step 10
  • a change of theirs and the student's address (for example, principal family home) complete the following:
    • for students, record the change of address in a DOC and refer the DOC to the appropriate AIC Smart Centre for action in relation to applicant and student records
    • for applicants, update the Address Details (AD) screen, Accommodation (AC) screen, and Telephone Details (TD) screen. All updates and coding on AIC records should be done via the ISS system (not NSS) with a Service reason: of ‘SVAIC’
    • Check if the applicant has any other AIC students that the change of address would affect and follow these procedures for those students as well

Note: a cancellable Manual Follow-up (MFU) will be created by the system for the AIC Smart Centre to action

2

Change to student's living arrangements + Read more ...

Changes to the student's living arrangements will generally entail a change of allowance or rate.

Contact applicant to request full details of the student's new circumstances by phone if possible or via an Online Advice (Q760). See the Resources page for link to the form. Go to Step 3.

Transfer the caller to the relevant AIC Smart Centre via internal transfer numbers in Office Locator. The Resources page contains a link to Office Locator.

13 2318 is the external number for applicants or members of the public making AIC enquiries. Procedure ends here.

3

For AIC Smart Centre Processing staff only

Action any Manual Follow-ups (MFUs) + Read more ...

Determine whether the student still meets an AIC isolation condition in relation to the new principal family home address. See Isolation conditions for AIC

Where an applicant has advised that the family has a new address (for the principal family home) and that as a result, the student no longer meets one of the AIC isolation conditions (for example family has moved to a non-isolated area), the applicant may still be eligible for payment of the student under the AIC Continuity of schooling concession. This concession applies where the student continues to:

  • attend the same school, or
  • be enrolled at the same distance education institution

Does the student still meet an AIC isolation condition in relation to the new principal home address, or satisfy the AIC Continuity of schooling concession?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

When to cancel the AIC Application from + Read more ...

Cancel the AIC application from the date the principal family home address was changed.

See Coding a cancellation for AIC.

  • Record details on a DOC including all relevant Act and AIC Guidelines references

Procedure ends here.

5

Check student's circumstances + Read more ...

If the student:

  • attends the same school or is still enrolled at the same distance education institution and has had no change in their term living arrangements, advise the applicant that the student continues to be eligible for the AIC under the continuity of schooling concession. Record decision in a DOC. Procedure ends here
  • is now studying by distance education, go to Step 6
  • is now boarding away from home, go to Step 7
  • is now living in a Second Family Home, go to Step 8

6

Check Distance Education Allowance (DED) eligibility + Read more ...

Determine whether the student is eligible for Distance Education Allowance (DED).

See eligibility for AIC - DED particularly Steps 3 to 5.

Go to Step 9.

7

Check eligibility for AIC Boarding Allowance + Read more ...

Determine whether the student is eligible for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme - Boarding Allowance (BA/ABA)

Go to Step 9.

8

Check AIC Second Home Allowance eligibility + Read more ...

Determine whether the student is eligible for AIC Second Home Allowance (SHA)

Go to Step 9.

9

Updating EILA and EDC screens + Read more ...

Update details on the EILA screen, as required.

  • Update study details on the EDC screen, if necessary
  • Finalise the activity on the AR screen
  • Record details on a DOC, including any relevant Act or AIC Guidelines references

10

Updating the TER address and Payment Instruction (PIPI) screen + Read more ...

If the student has changed the term address:

  • Update the TER address on the Address Details (AD) screen
  • Navigate to the address on the PIPI screen
    • Select the Default Payee
    • Update the Payment Instructions Payee Details (PIPD) screen with the new address and Payee and account details (if necessary)
    • Applicant Field:
      • Code a 'Y' for letters to be sent via the address coded as HOME or POS on the Address Details (AD) screen
      • Code a 'N' for a letter to be sent to the third party

Note: letters being issued to a third party will display payment dates and rates.
