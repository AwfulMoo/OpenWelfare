For the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT) only.

This page outlines the process for determining an exemption from Income Management for customers with dependent children. It explains what evidence of responsible parenting is needed in order to grant an exemption.

Exemption from Income Management

Income Management exemption for customers with dependent children

Table 1: This table outlines the second and final part of the process for determining an exemption from Income Management for customers with dependent children. It explains what evidence of responsible parenting is needed in order to grant an exemption.

Step

Action

1

Financial Vulnerability Assessment completed + Read more ...

The customer is not considered financially vulnerable based on the Financial Vulnerability Assessment.

The customer is now required to provide evidence of responsible parenting based on whether the child has reached compulsory school age for the purposes of Income Management.

To determine the type of evidence required, see Requirements for an exemption from Income Management for customers with dependent children.

Evidence required for child under school age

The customer needs to provide the following evidence:

  • participation in health related activities, and
  • for children aged 36 months or more, engagement in early education support services

These activities are not compulsory measures. They are considered indicators of responsible parenting. The purpose of this requirement is to promote the benefits of social interactions before a child attends school and the involvement of parents in this area of a child's development.

If the customer is affected by the Healthy Start for School initiative and the evidence lodged to meet the Income Management exemption requirements proves the child has completed a relevant health check since turning three, see Healthy Start for School to update the health check status.

Evidence for school age child

Advise the customer that they need to provide evidence that the child:

  • is enrolled in and attending school, and has no more than 5 absences for each of the previous 2 school terms that are not satisfactory to the person responsible for the operation of the school
  • is enrolled in and attending school, and has no more than 5 absences that are not satisfactory to a person responsible for the operation of the school, but the person is taking reasonable steps to ensure that the child attends school as required
  • is being provided home based schooling that has been approved by the state/territory Department of Education, Skills and Employment, or
  • is participating in alternative activities for circumstances where it is not reasonable for a child to be regularly attending school, or it is not reasonable for the parent to be held responsible for the child's non-participation

If the school aged child is attending school regularly when in the care of one parent, but not with the other customer (parent) applying for the exemption will need to demonstrate that they are taking reasonable steps to ensure the child attends school and have taken steps to reduce or stop the absences, for example, addressing bullying at school.

2

28 days to provide evidence + Read more ...

Advise the customer:

  • to provide the evidence within 28 days
  • that the evidence can be provided by fax or uploading the document via the Express Plus application and via the online account. See Upload documents service, and
  • that an exemption cannot be granted if evidence is not provided

Note: when a customer contacts during the 28 day period, 'Issue 001410' displays in the Centrelink system for the Service Officer to remind the customer to provide the evidence.

3

Was evidence provided within 28 days? + Read more ...

  • Yes, check that the evidence is acceptable to grant exemption. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Customer advised unable to obtain evidence + Read more ...

Was advice received from the customer that they are unable to provide evidence and they have made all attempts to do so?

  • Yes, attempt to confirm details with third party by phone. Go to Step 5
  • No, the customer does not meet the exemption criteria. Go to Step 9

5

Has the Service Officer obtained evidence from a third party? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Customer to be advised of exemption rejection + Read more ...

Prior to recording an exemption activity, Service Officers are to make at least two genuine attempts to contact by phone the customer to advise of the decision.

Is contact with the customer successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, proceed with recording of exemption decision. See Step 4 in the Actioning the decision for an exemption from Income Management table

7

Determining a special circumstances exemption referral + Read more ...

If the customer or Service Officer considers an exemption should not be refused because the reason for non-participation in health or engagement activities is due to circumstances outside of the customer’s control (for example, limited or no suitable activities in the area, religious or cultural issues, etc.), the application can be referred to the Income Management helpdesk for assessment.

Is the customer or Service Officer requesting an exemption from Income Management due to special circumstances?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, see Step 4 in the Actioning the decision for an exemption from Income Management table

8

Referring a special circumstances exemption for determination + Read more ...

Service Officers are to investigate the reason for non-compliance with health or engagement activities and obtain any evidence (for example, verbal or written confirmation from third parties/organisations, written statement from customer, etc.).

Evidence must be scanned onto the customer’s record and/or information recorded in a DOC.

Refer the Customer Reference Number (CRN), and any additional supporting information, via webform to the Income Management helpdesk. See Resources for a Link to the webform.

Has a special circumstances exemption been approved by the Income Management helpdesk?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, contact the customer to advise the special circumstances exemption has been rejected. See Step 4 in the Actioning the decision for an exemption from Income Management table

9

Eligible for exemption + Read more ...

Can an exemption be granted, for example, there are no signs of financial vulnerability in the last 12 months and acceptable evidence of responsible parenting has been provided?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in the Actioning the decision for an exemption from Income Management table
  • No, see Step 4 in the Actioning the decision for an exemption from Income Management table

Finalising the exemption from Income Management

To action the decision for an exemption from Income Management

Table 2: This table contains the final steps for the process for determining an exemption from Income Management for customers with dependent children.

Step

Action

1

Grant exemption + Read more ...

Process work item to complete workflow and grant exemption.

Launch the Compulsory IM exemption workflow.

  • The exemption applies for 12 months from the time the decision is made
  • If the customer's circumstances change during the 12 months, the exemption will still apply and it may be appropriate to refer the customer to a social worker

Does the customer have funds in their Income Management account and the exemption period is for more than 60 days?

  • Yes, conduct a disbursement interview. Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here
  • No, the Manage Expenses screen appears. Cease regular expenses. Go to Step 2

2

Service offer + Read more ...

Offer Voluntary Income Management and record outcome on the Service Offer screen. (If disbursing funds, this is done as part of the disbursement process.)

Advise the customer that if they volunteer for Income Management they can exit any time after completing the compulsory 13 weeks of Income Management.

If the customer wishes to be income managed on a voluntary basis, conduct an Initial Assessment interview for Voluntary Income Management.

3

Finalise activity + Read more ...

When the Service offer screen is updated the Manage Expenses screen displays to update.

The Income Management Summary screen will display to finalise the activity. Select:

  • the Finalise radio button
  • Continue

Record details on a DOC.

A letter is issued to the customer.

Did the customer provide evidence of regular child health checks as part of their exemption application?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, procedure ends here

4

Rejection of exemption + Read more ...

Launch the Compulsory IM exemption workflow.

  • Process the work item on the customer's record to reject the application. Note: where only partial evidence has been provided, select the 'No evidence provided' option
  • The Income Management Summary screen will display to finalise the activity. Select the Finalise radio button. Select Continue
  • Record details on a DOC
  • Advise customer of their right to request Reviews of Income Management decisions
  • Advise customers where to find more information to help with managing their finances. Customers can visit servicesaustralia.gov.au/managingmoney for useful information and resources
  • For customers who have been identified for Income Management, conduct an Initial Assessment interview

Offer further assistance where appropriate:

Did the customer provide evidence of regular child health checks as part of their exemption application?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, procedure ends here

5

Launch the Healthy Start for School Review work flow + Read more ...

Centrelink records health check details for customers receiving an income support payment at any time during the financial year in which their child turns 4 to ensure children are healthy, fit and ready to start school.

Heathy Start for School requirements are linked to:

  • the customer’s rate of FTB Part A for children who turn four on or after 1 July 2018
  • payment of the FTB Part A supplement for children who turn four during financial years 2011-12 to 2017-18

Evidence received for an Income Management exemption satisfies the health check requirements for the Healthy Start for School initiative. Run the Healthy Start for School Review workflow.

Note: the date the child completed the health check must be when the child was aged between 3 and 4 years (up until the day before the child turns 5 years).
