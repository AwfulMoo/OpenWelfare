2

Type of assets owned by the customer + Read more ...

Assets which are not real estate include:

  • the portion of a principal home used to operate a business. This is included in the assets of the business
  • assets which are recorded on Other Assets (OAS) screen. For example:
    • airplane hangars
    • boats
    • licences

Notes:

  • Assets such as marina berths or car park spaces must be recorded as real estate type OTH
  • Self-contained flats and other dwellings on the same title as the principal home which are currently rented (other than to a near relative), must be recorded as real estate type SCF:
    • Real estate assets coded as SCF are not in the risk profiling and auto-indexation cycle.
    • The nature of this arrangement is not suited to indexation
    • For more information regarding Dual Occupancy, see the Background page of Assessing house and curtilage

Does the customer own assets that are not real estate?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, only real estate, go to Step 4

3

Valuation of non - real estate assets + Read more ...

A valuation can be requested. Non-real estate assets include, but are not limited to:

  • coin collections
  • art collections
  • fishing licences or other licences
  • taxi licences
  • racehorses
  • other livestock
  • marina berths
  • plant and equipment
  • rent roll

Any evidence the customer can supply to help in determining the value of the other asset must be collected, This may help in determining if valuation is required. For example, documents relating to recent sales or private professional appraisals. Do not ask the customer to pay for new reports. It is only if they already have them.

A valuation would only be required where:

  • the customer is likely to be impacted by the assets test and/or
  • the customer's estimate of the asset value appears unreasonable

If a valuation is required for non-real estate assets, complete and submit a SA470 to the Valuation Helpdesk. See the Resources page for a link.

When the value of a non-real estate asset has been determined, see Assessing assets at their net value for coding information.

Taxi licences

The relevant state and territory transport authorities can generally be contacted to obtain the value of any taxi licence held in their respective jurisdictions. See Resources page for contact details and further information regarding each state and territory.

If the state or territory does not have a contact, email the Valuation Helpdesk to get help valuing a taxi licence. When sending a taxi licence valuation request, attach the completed:

  • Valuation Request SA470
  • Valuation Questionnaire - Taxi Licence forms

See the Resources page for the forms.

In general, if the sales/transfer data is older than 6 months, request a valuation from the Valuation Helpdesk.

Note: the Valuations Helpdesk will reply to the email with the returned valuation. Coding can then be completed.

Does the customer also own assessable real estate assets?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, asset assessment procedure ends here

4

Record or update real estate details + Read more ...

Record any new real estate details using the customer's estimated value and the valuation source code of CLI (client estimate). See Assessing and coding the Real estate forms MOD R.

If there is uncertainty about the title details provided by the customer, email the Valuation Helpdesk for guidance. A title search may be needed.

If the property is:

  • the customer's principal home, go to Step 5
  • not the principal home and:
    • not previously recorded, go to Step 6
    • new claim with a property previously recorded, go to Step 7

5

Property is the customer's principal home + Read more ...

Complete all mandatory fields in Real Estate (RE), Real Estate Valuation Information (REVI) and Real Estate/ Business Identifying (REBI) screens are completed and updated.

Select the appropriate house and curtilage type in the Curtilage Type field on the RE screen. For all new customers or a new property, this will be:

  • PLU - Private Land Use, or
  • EXT for Extended Curtilage. Where EXT has been used:
    • land title reference of the home block must be recorded in the Other Circumstances field on the REVI screen
    • note this is the title where the principal home is located
  • the Asset Valuation Source code on RE screen must be CLI

The Other Circumstances and Directions to the Property field on the REVI screen allows free text. Use this field to record any additional information, for example, 'water rights to be included in valuation'. Providing all available information will enable a successful and accurate valuation.

For help coding water assets for Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customers, see Assessing assets for Farm Household Allowance (FHA)

Note: existing customers with curtilage recorded as at 31 December 2006 were automatically assessed as curtilage type GRF. Reassess the curtilage. Update with the appropriate code.

If the customer:

  • also has property not previously recorded, go to Step 6
  • also has property already recorded, go to Step 7
  • has no other real estate, go to Step 8

6

New property not previously recorded + Read more ...

Complete all mandatory fields in RE, REVI and REBI screens.

The Curtilage Type: field must be blank if the property is not the customer's principal home.

The Other Circumstances and Directions to the property field on the REVI screen allows free text. Use this field to record any additional information, for example, 'water rights to be included in valuation'. Providing all available information will enable a successful and accurate valuation.

For help coding water assets for Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customers, see Assessing assets for Farm Household Allowance (FHA)

If the customer:

  • also has property already recorded, go to Step 7
  • has no other real estate, go to Step 8

7

New claim property previously recorded + Read more ...

If the property has previously been recorded with a valuation source code of 'NVE', 'VAL', 'ONS', 'EST' or 'IDX', and:

  • the customer or partner is not current and the new estimated value is different to the amount recorded:
    • update the Current Market Value field with the customer's estimate, and
    • change the Asset Valuation Source field to CLI in the RE screen
  • the customer or partner is in receipt of an Income Support Payment. Note: if the customer is current and a benefit transfer occurs, e.g. JSP to CAR:
    • do not update the Current Market Value or Asset Valuation Source fields unless the customer advises of significant changes to the property
    • for more information see Maintaining the value of real estate assets on customer records

If the property has previously been recorded and has a source code other than 'NVE', 'VAL', 'ONS', 'EST' or 'IDX':

  • update the Current Market Value field with the customer’s estimate
  • change the Asset Valuation Source: field to CLI in the RE screen
  • ensure all mandatory fields in RE, REVI and REBI screens are completed and updated

The Curtilage Type field in the RE screen must be blank if the property is not the customer's principal home.

Note: existing customers with curtilage recorded as at 31 December 2006 were automatically assessed as curtilage type GRF. Reassess the curtilage. Update with the appropriate code.

8

Assess the estimate for each assessable property + Read more ...

For each assessable real estate asset, is the customer's estimate reasonable?

  • Yes, and the customer is:
    • registered for the Pensions Bonus Scheme (PBS), go to Step 9
    • not registered for PBS, go to Step 10
  • No, discuss with an APS 5 or the Valuation Helpdesk:
    • If the APS 5 or above agrees the customer's estimate is unreasonable they can request a valuation within the new claim activity. Navigate to the REVI screen, enter High Priority in the Other Circumstances field and VAL on the Request Valn/Profile field
      Note: a successful request for a valuation will display a real estate valuation future activity on the FAL screen. The property ID number(s) will identify which property is involved
    • Procedure ends here until valuation returned

9

Customer claiming Pension Bonus + Read more ...

A valuation must be completed if the customer is claiming:

  • Pension Bonus Bereavement Payment (PBBP), or
  • Age Pension and have a Pension Bonus registration

The claim cannot be finalised until the valuation is complete.

  • Commence the valuation process. Within the new claim activity:
    • go to the Real Estate Valuation Information (REVI) screen
    • code the Request Valuation/Profile tab
    • code High Priority in the Other Circumstances tab
  • The automatic valuation request will be triggered when the claim is taken to the Assessment Results (AR) screen.
    Note: a successful request for a valuation will display a real estate valuation activity on the FAL screen. The property ID number(s) will identify which property is involved
  • Procedure ends here until the valuation(s) is returned

10

Property not included in the risk based model + Read more ...

  • Refer to an APS5 or above using the Services Australia Valuation Request (SA470)

Some real estate assets are not included in the risk based model:

Overseas real estate

  • Email a completed SA470 to the Valuation Helpdesk if
    • the property is in UK, USA or NZ,
    • the customer's estimate appears to be unreasonable, and
    • the customer is (or is likely to be) impacted by the assets test
  • In all other cases, accept the customer's estimate

Residential Care Assessments

See Aged Care means assessment - Home ownership and real estate estimates for more information.

Gifting

  • A valuation is required for all gifting assessments that involve real estate
  • Complete an SA470, make sure the gifting date is included, and email it to the Valuation Helpdesk

Self-Contained Flats/Other real estate:

  • Self-Contained Flats (SCF) and Other (OTH) real estate such as car parks will not be included in the risk based model
  • A valuation can be requested where:
    • the customer is likely to be impacted by the assets test and/or
    • the estimated asset value appears unreasonable
  • If a valuation is required, complete an SA470 and email it to the Valuation Helpdesk.

Undisclosed properties:

Complete an SA470 for historical valuations for undisclosed properties. Include full details of the valuations required, including historical dates, and if permission to inspect has been obtained. An APS5 or above must email the SA470 to the Valuation Helpdesk.

If the SA470 has been completed in regard to any suspected fraud or corruption:

  • advise in the subject line of the email that the request is for a fraud or corruption investigation
  • confirm if the valuation and/or Witness Statements are required
    Note: if the request includes a Witness Statement, attach the Witness Statement template relevant to the jurisdiction and the SA470 to the email request.

Other real estate assets not included in the risk based model

The following properties are not suitable for inclusion in the risk based model:

  • Real estate type OTH (other real estate assets for example car parks, marina berths)
  • Real estate linked to a business
  • Real estate not recorded on a customer record
  • Exempt real estate assets

Is the property type included in the risk based model?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, procedure ends here

11

Automatic risk profiling when claim finalised or new real estate added + Read more ...

The Valuation Source Code will be automatically update to NVE - Valuation/Estimate check is not required. The real estate value will be automatically profiled.

Estimate verification required

If an estimate verification is required:

  • the system will automatically submit the customer's estimated value for verification
  • a future activity will be on FAL screen with the property ID number(s). For example, Estimation: 2
  • If the estimate is:
    • acceptable, the Valuation Source code on the Real Estate (RE) screen will be automatically updated to EST - Estimate Verified
    • not acceptable the property will be automatically submitted for valuation

Valuation required

If a valuation is required:

  • the property will be automatically submitted for valuation
  • a future activity will be on the FAL screen with the property ID number(s). For example, Valuation: 1

Procedure ends here.

Follow up action after valuation data return

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Estimate or valuation returned + Read more ...

Estimation or valuation request has failed.

  • A Manual follow up (MFU) has been created.
  • The MFU will have a keyword which will provide information about why the request failed and what action is needed.
  • Go to Step 4

Estimation or valuation data returned.

Estimation

If the estimation verification has been returned and the estimate is acceptable the Valuation Source code on the Real Estate Detail (RE) screen will be automatically updated to EST - Estimate Verified

Valuation

The returned valuation data automatically uploads with source code VAL or ONS.

  • If there:
    • is a claim still to be finalised, go to Step 2
    • was a change to the rate of payment, go to Step 3

2

Claim not finalised: + Read more ...

When the assessed value is returned, the claim activity will be updated:

  • on the RE screen:
    • the Current Market Value, Valuers Job Number and Valuer Property Number fields will be coded
    • Asset Valuation Source field will be VAL or ONS
  • a keyword of VALRTN will be added to the new claim activity
  • status of claim will change from Hold (HLD) to Started (STA)
  • a Real Estate update DOC will be added to the record
  • the claim activity is ready for finalisation

Following claim finalisation automatic risk profiling will occur for property types house, unit and townhouse

Procedure ends here.

3

Valuation resulted in rate change + Read more ...

If a valuation has resulted in a change to the customer's rate they will be sent a rate change letter. The letter will say there has been a change to the value of real estate assets.

The customer can request an explanation or apply for a formal review.

Procedure ends here.

4

Follow up required- MFU has been returned + Read more ...

An MFU activity will have an error keyword.

Keyword REBEXP- Valuation or estimation data not received

  • The valuation or estimation data has not been received within required timeframes
  • Contact the Valuation Helpdesk for further advice
  • The Valuation Helpdesk will contact the valuation provider to confirm the expected date of receipt
  • If the helpdesk advises the valuation amount, update the record and finalise the MFU
  • If the data is received automatically after creation of the MFU, the system will automatically upload the valuation amount and finalise the MFU

Keyword ERRSLD - Property sold follow up required

  • The valuation provider has advised the property has been sold
  • Contact the customer and update the customer's record as needed

Keyword - REJISI - Insufficient information follow up required

  • There has not been enough information provided to enable the valuation provider to complete this valuation
  • Contact the Valuation Helpdesk. The helpdesk will confirm what additional information is required
  • The REVI screen on the record must be updated with the required information (the customer may have to be contacted to request/confirm required details)
  • Once the record has been updated, a manual VAL request must be re-submitted by an APS 5 or above

Keyword REJPMM - Property mismatch follow up required - returned by provider

  • Contact the Valuation Helpdesk for more information.

Keyword REJNAP - Property error

  • Contact the Valuation Helpdesk for more information.

Keyword REBIMM - Internal mismatch

  • A property mismatch can occur when the Pensions system tries to apply the returned estimation or valuation data
  • An activity will be created on the Activity List (AL) screen advising the following: Contact the Valuation Helpdesk for more information

Keyword REBUVR - Unsolicited VAL, ONS or EST response

  • A valuation response has been received with no request outstanding
  • Contact the Valuation Helpdesk
  • If a customer's relationship status changes while a valuation is in progress, only the customer's record will be updated. A new request must be submitted for the ex-partner or the surviving partner
