Screen information and navigation

Table 5: This table explains how to find and update screens in Process Direct.

Item

Description

SCREENS

Key SCREENS into the Super Key to access a list of available screens.

  • The list displays screen codes and their description
  • If necessary, use Ctrl + F to find the required screen
  • Select the relevant link to go to the individual screen

Task Selector (START)

Task Selectors contain a list of screens or a workflow that are needed to complete an update.

When processing a claim or change of circumstance transaction, Task Selectors will present on the Errors (SWE) screen. These Task Selectors will contain shortcuts to screens that are most commonly used during an assessment of the relevant work item.

When a customer’s record is accessed through Customer Summary, the Task Selector (START) screen will display. This screen contains a list of all workflows and tasks that can be actioned on a customer’s record.

Go back to the Task Selector at any time by keying START into the Super Key.

For processing advice and support on a Task Selector workflow refer to the relevant payment-related resources and Operational Blueprint procedure.

Active MSP: Yes/No

Active Managed Service Plan (MSP) header

  • If a customer has an active MSP in place, the Active MSP field displays 'Yes'. To launch the MSP, select the Active MSP hyperlink
  • If a customer does not have an active MSP, the Active MSP field displays 'No' and there will be no hyperlink available

For further details see Customer aggression - Managed Service Plan (MSP)

Add/Edit data

Data is displayed in tables, depending on the screen selected. Some screens display more than 1 related table and are known as summary screens.

Tables may contain:

  • Provisional data - details provided by the customer in their claim or coded by a Service Officer. This can be edited or deleted.
  • Confirmed data - information held on the customer’s record. This may also be updated if required, or ceased (zeroed) if it no longer applies. After updating it will display as confirmed or provisional data
  • Unconfirmed data - data loaded from the claim that requires mandatory action to determine if it is correct or should be deleted from the activity
  • Provisional Deleted data -confirmed data that has been deleted

Parent/child lines

A Parent Line is the main heading for the item, for example, the Financial Institution details.

The Child Lines are the individual entries underneath this heading, for example, bank accounts including bank balances.

To access the Child Lines, use the chevron at the left of the parent line.

Errors (SWE) screen

The SWE screen displays warnings and errors associated with the work item in the Message Log at the top of the screen. It will also present any Task Selectors associated with the work item. Once the SWE screen no longer displays any errors, the work item is ready to be assessed.

Warnings and errors

  • Errors display in red and require action prior to finalising the work item
  • Warnings display in yellow and may not require action prior to finalising the work item

Use the Message Log to select the error/warning to action or manually go to the screen that requires action/update.

Finalise activity

When finalising an activity, select Assess to go to the Entitlement (ELD) screen to view the assessment results.

The ELD screen is similar to the Assessment Results (AR) screen in Customer First.

Before finalising a transaction the common rate screens to check include:

  • Payment Summary (PS)
  • Daily Rate Component (RAC)
  • Assessment Consequences (ASC)

These screens are accessible from the Entitlement (ELD) screen, using the Super Key.

Table 6: This menu varies depending on the type of activity or update being worked on. Unless specified, these are in both CPTv3 and CPTv5.

Item

Description

Referral

The Referral workflow within a claim can create a referral for:

  • Assessment of Care Arrangements (Shared Care)
  • Complex Assessment Officer
  • International Services
  • Member of a Couple (MoC)
  • Social Worker

The Recent referrals table will help identify if a new referral is needed

To make a referral:

If a referral has been made, staff using Customer First can locate it under the Activities tab.

For further details on how to create a referral, refer to the relevant Operational Blueprint.

Request Documents

The Request Documents workflow within a claim or change of circumstance activity will send a request for information (RFI) advice to the customer

For further details, see Requesting Information (CLK).

Regenerate

Regenerate Claim will reset the claim or change of circumstance update to only contain the information provided by the customer when they submitted the claim or change of circumstance update online. This may be needed when Process Direct system fixes are released, or relationship linking was done after starting claim or change of circumstance updates processing.

Re-index Claim

Re-index Claim allows reassessment of claims that have previously been rejected or completed (for example, claims with a process status of Approved or Completed). If the original claim was an online claim, the reindexed work item will contain data load.

A reindexed claim will have a ten digit Claim ID instead of the original seven digit number.

For further details, see Cancelling claim activities and re-indexing claims.

Handover to CF/CR

Handover to CF/CR can be used when an activity must be processed in Customer First/Customer Record.

For further details, see Table 3 on the Process page.

Update DOR

Use Update DOR to change the original receipt date of the claim.

As DOR assessment rules vary, see the relevant Operational Blueprint procedure for the claim being processed.

Change Benefit

A customer may lodge an incorrect claim or there may be a benefit/payment that is more appropriate for the customer’s situation. For example, a customer lodges a claim for Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP), however can be assessed as single and eligible for Parenting Payment Single (PPS).

In some circumstances, the Change Benefit option may be used to change the claim type and assess the customer for the appropriate benefit/payment type without the need for the customer to submit another claim.

Note: Change Benefit should be used with caution. This option can only be used once and cannot be reversed once applied to a claim.

For further details, see the relevant Operational Blueprint procedure for the claim being processed.

Participation Interview

The Participation Interview workflow is used for all customers claiming a payment with mutual obligation requirements including Jobseeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) and some Parenting Payment (PP) customers.

For further details, see Participation Interview.

General UNPP

The Generate UNPP workflow is used where a claim has been lodged for a payment with participation requirements and it has been identified that the customer's unemployment is due to a voluntary act or misconduct.

The Generate UNPP workflow creates an open compliance action to be investigated by appropriately skilled Service Officer.

For further details, see Unemployment due to a voluntary act or misconduct.

Combined Claim Switch

Use Combined Claim Switch to transform a single SOA shell created for an Age Pension claim into a combined customer-partner claim and then back to a single claim if necessary.

See Processing claims for Age Pension and Pension Bonus.

Manage Correspondence

Use Manage Correspondence to send letters and customer advices. Only letters required for the assessment being undertaken will be available for selection.

For further details, see the relevant Operation Blueprint.

Manage Advances

Use Manage Advances to process an advance application for a customer or to access other advance payment information and functions.

For further details, see Advances.

Manage Receipts

Use Manage Receipts to action work items for advance payments.

For further details, see Works items for advance payments.

Selective Application Data

The Selective Application Data (SAD) function is used to apply and finalise provisional data within a Disability Support Pension (DSP) claim where there is insufficient information to assess a claim as a whole.

See Selective Application of Data (SAD).

DSP Claim Summary

Provides a summary of the information related to a Disability Support Pension (DSP) new claim including claim details, supporting evidence and any assessment actions that have been completed.

DMA Phone Appointment Request Approval

Allows the Government Contracted Doctor (GCD) Contract Management team to approve requests for Disability Medical Assessments (DMA) to be conducted by phone.

For further details, see Disability Medical Assessment (DMA) referrals.

Consent to Contact THP

Use Consent to Contact THP to record if a customer or nominee advises they do not want the Agency or Assessors engaged by the Agency to contact their Treating Health Professionals as part of the assessment of a DSP claim or a DMA Medical Review.

For further details, see Streaming a new claim for Disability Support Pension (DSP).

Release Medical Assessments

Assists Service Officers in determining whether copies of Job Capacity Assessment (JCA), Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) and Disability Medical Assessment (DMA) reports can be released to a customer or their nominee upon request.

For further details, see Release of Disability Support Pension (DSP) Medical Assessment reports.

DMA Assessment

Use DMA Assessment to view and update Disability Medical Assessment (DMA) information.

For further details, see the relevant Operational Blueprint processing instructions.

DMA Report Summary

Displays completed Disability Medical Assessment (DMA) reports.

QMA Processing

The QMA Processing option is only available to Service Officers with the Quality Management Officer (QMO) security role who are undertaking a quality check of a completed work item.

When QMA Processing is selected, the QMA Summary screen will open in a separate window and show all screens that need to be quality checked.

For further details, see Quality Management Application (QMA) for Quality Management Officers (QMO).

Streamline Rejection

CPTv3 only

Used to Streamline Reject a claim. When a claim is finalised as a streamline rejection, no updates or circumstance changes are applied to the customer’s record apart from the assessment outcome itself. Streamline rejection reasons will vary depending on the type of claim assessment being completed.

For further details, see the relevant Operation Blueprint claim procedure.

Activities

CPTv3 only

The Activities option can only be selected when in a work item processed in CPTv3.

Displays started activities present on the customer’s Activity List (AL) screen in ISIS which may prevent finalisation of the work item and allows the user to cancel them if necessary.

Process status definitions

Table 7: This table lists some of the process statuses that may display on a work item in Process Direct.

Note: not all business processes and Customer First SAP native processes may use all of the listed statuses. See relevant procedures for payment specific information.

Status

Description

Draft

The customer has started an online claim or update, but has yet to submit the claim or update. The submission date will display as 00:00:0000.

If a customer contacts about a submission with a draft status, they should be advised to return to their online account and submit the claim or update.

In Process

An online update or claim has been submitted by the customer and is ready to assess.

On Hold

A work item has been held for action at a later date. This status is manually updated. It can be used when requesting more information from a customer or third party referrals.

New claims (NCL), non-new claims (NNCL), also known as Change of Circumstance (CoC) transactions, if placed on hold for:

  • the reason System Investigation/Issue, the hold date defaults to 28 days. This date cannot be changed
  • for all other reasons, the maximum hold period is 120 days. Staff must select a time frame less than 120 days

Completed

A work item has been assessed and finalised. This status will display for claims that have been granted or rejected.

Not Required

A work item does not need assessment.

For example, after a claim rejection a customer lodges another claim. If reassessing the original claim is appropriate, staff can manually update the status of the second claim to Not Required.

Withdrawn

A work item, for example, an online claim, has been withdrawn by the customer through their online account. Staff can also update this status manually.

Submission Failed

A system error occurred between submission of an online or assisted customer claim and the claim generation.

This may be a result of conflicting activities on the customer or partner record, or record locking at the time of claim submission. Review the records to check if this is the case.

Once there are no activities preventing the claim activity from creating, update the claim status from Submission Failed to In Process. Then update as required.

If the status remains as Submission Failed, raise an incident via the digital assistant.
