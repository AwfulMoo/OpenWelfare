Process settlement payments for deceased customers

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Confirm the person + Read more ...

Before progressing with the reason for the call, confirm the person on the phone is the appropriate person:

  • Solicitor, Executor or Administrator, or
  • the person responsible for managing the affairs of (customer name)

Locate the deceased customer’s record that the person is calling about.

Note: staff must check all display on access (DOA) DOCs on DL. If a Multiple, Deleted or Intertwined Records DOA DOC is present, staff are to make sure they are in the correct record before proceeding.

Check the following screens:

  • Death/Executor (DEA). If coded, confirm the details with the person
  • Document Tools
  • Document List (DL)
  • Marital Status (MS)
  • Link Summary (LS)

Check the refund transaction in Process Direct for details in the Notes.

If a refund was paid check they were the person that received the Income Compliance refund.

If a refund was not paid or the customer passed away after the refund or categorisation, more checks need to be done to confirm they are the appropriate person.

Is the person on the phone the appropriate person?

  • Yes,
    • if the person wants to update their contact number or address details, go to Step 3
    • if there is no change to the persons details go to Step 4
  • No,
    • if the person calling is not the appropriate person the refund was issued to, tell them:
      ‘Services Australia has paid the refund to the appropriate person as we were satisfied with the documentation provided, confirming their authority to receive the payment and distribute to additional beneficiaries where required. This person is also the appropriate person for settlement payment.’
    • do not disclose personal Information of the refund recipient to the caller under any circumstances
    • record in a DOC details of the conversation. See the Resources page for the template
    • if a refund was not issued or the customer passed away after the refund or categorisation, go to Step 2

2

Establish the appropriate person + Read more ...

The person on the phone must be confirmed as Executor or Administrator first. Otherwise they must be confirmed as the person responsible for managing the affairs of the deceased estate.

Confirm that the person on the phone is responsible for managing the deceased customer:

  • ‘We are attempting to contact the person responsible for managing the affairs of <customer name>, is that you?’

Has the person been confirmed as the appropriate person?

  • Yes, and
    • customer has a Centrelink record, undertake Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO) as outlined in Authenticating a Centrelink customer
    • customer does not have a Centrelink record, obtain full name, address, phone number and Date of Birth (this is optional if the customer agrees to provide it)
    • Go to Step 3
  • No, see Table 2

3

Record executor or administrator details + Read more ...

Record the details on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen. This includes the name, address and contact details.

For more information, see Confirmation of the death of an adult or child.

4

Determine reason for call + Read more ...

The person could be calling because of:

  • categorisation letter received, or
  • agency contact

To help with the conversation the Categorisation letter can be viewed in Outbound Correspondence in Customer First.

The Categorisation letter must be re-issued at the request of the appropriate person for Returned to Sender work items and where new address details have been updated on DEA. To re-issue the letter, send the CRN to the Quality Development Officer (QDO). The QDO will enter the CRN onto a spreadsheet and email to Business Process and Service Design.

If the:

  • deceased estate is already registered and the person wants to update the payment destination, go to Step 5
  • if there is no further action needed, tell the person:
    • 'there is nothing more you need to do. Services Australia will start making payments in early 2022'
    • Procedure ends here

5

Viewing the Class Action settlement activity in Process Direct + Read more ...

In Process Direct:

  • select Home
  • select Inbox
  • from the Main Category menu
  • select Income Compliance
  • in the Customer CRN field, key the customer's Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • select Go to search
  • select the Class action settlement activity

To confirm or update the payment destination and finalise the class action settlement activity, see Step 9 in Table 1 in Processing class action settlements.

Step

Action

Search for probate notice + Read more ...

Executor, administrator or solicitor contact information may be on a probate notice. Search for a published probate notice for the state the customer last lived in.

Each state has a different probate advertisement website for listed executors progressing probate. See the Resources page for links. Note: Tasmanian searches can only be done for notices lodged from 8 November 2017.

Take extra care if the search results show multiple name matches. Use all information available to confirm beyond doubt that the search result relates to the customer.

If solicitor details are identified do a Google search to check if the solicitor or law firm is still active.

If deemed no longer active:

  • record in a DOC the results of the search. See the Resources page for the template
  • go to Step 2 in Table 3

Was a probate notice for the deceased customer identified?

    • record in a DOC the results of the search. See the Resources page for the template
    • try to locate and contact beneficiary. See Step 2 in Table 3

3

Check record for sensitivities + Read more ...

Check the deceased customer record to see if the customer has any of the following:

  • prosecution action
  • media contact
  • ministerial

If any of the above are identified escalate via email to the Compliance Helpdesk.

See the Resources page for sensitive customer indicators that need to be checked.

In Customer First, check these screens:

  • Customer Details (CRN/BP)
  • Document List (DL)
  • Intervention Notes
  • Document Tools

Check Archive and Culling Engine (ACE), see Retrieve information from ACE.

If sensitivities are identified and an escalation previously occurred for the refund process and no subsequent issues were identified, referral is not required again.

Does the record show that discussions with the executor or administrator may be highly sensitive if the agency was to make contact?

  • Yes, escalate the following details via email to Customer Compliance Service Desk and cc the Payment and Integrity Operations Strategy team. Use escalation reason Sensitivities Identified:
    • customer reference number (CRN)
    • details of executor or administrator and their relationship to the deceased customer (if known)
    • reason for escalation: as per the sensitive customer indicators
    • do not escalate via the CADO Service Desk form
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check if contacting around a sensitive date + Read more ...

Check if contact may be on or around a sensitive date. For example, a birthday of the deceased customer, date of death or an anniversary. This includes 7 days before and after the sensitive date.

Is contact on or around a sensitive date?

  • Yes, in Customer First:
    • place the work item on hold for 7 days after the sensitive date
    • on the Activity List (AL) screen select the relevant activity
    • from the Select Options menu, select (H) Hold/resubmit for action on a future date
    • in the Resubmit Date field, key the date 7 days from the sensitive date
    • when the work item becomes due, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Contact person + Read more ...

Always be sensitive when speaking to the person. If during the call:

  • the person is identified to be at risk of harm, see the Resources page for social work referral
  • sensitivities are identified but there is no immediate risk of harm, the settlement task can still be processed. See the Resources page for social work referral

Make 3 genuine attempts to contact. Record all contact attempts in a DOC. See the Resources page for the template.

Was contact successful?

  • Yes, with the:
    • solicitor, go to Step 6
    • executor or administrator of the estate, go to Step 9
  • No, for voicemail leave a message as follows:
    • ‘I am from Services Australia and I am attempting to contact the person responsible for managing the affairs of <customer name>. Please call us back on 1800 171 846’

After 3 attempts to contact the solicitor or executor, try to locate and contact beneficiary. See Step 2 in Table 3.

6

Advise solicitor purpose of call + Read more ...

‘We are attempting to contact the person responsible for managing the affairs of <customer name>, is that you?’

If the solicitor is still acting or representing the executor or administrator, record their details on the DEA screen. For more information, see Confirmation of the death of an adult or child.

If the solicitor is busy and unable to make time to discuss any details the solicitor can give permission to take information from the paralegal and/or assistant.

If this occurs, staff must document the following details on the customer’s record:

  • solicitor name
  • date/time of contact
  • confirmation that the solicitor has given permission for the paralegal and/or assistant to provide the executor information

Is the solicitor still acting or representing the executor or administrator?

  • Yes, go to Step 4 in Table 1
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Obtain and record executor or administrator details + Read more ...

Obtain the executor or administrator details. This includes their name, address and contact details.

Record the details on the DEA screen.

For more information, see Confirmation of the death of an adult or child.

8

Contact executor or administrator + Read more ...

Always be sensitive when speaking to the person. If during the call:

  • the person is identified to be at risk of harm, see the Resources page for social work referral
  • sensitivities are identified but there is no immediate risk of harm, the settlement task can still be processed. See the Resources page for social work referral

Check the Links Summary (LS) screen for the executor or administrator record to locate phone number if not already listed on the deceased record or probate.

Make 3 genuine attempts to contact. Record all contact attempts in a DOC. See the Resources page for the template.

Was contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, for voicemail leave a message as follows:
    • ‘I am from Services Australia and am calling to locate the executor or administrator of the estate of <deceased customer name>. Please call us back on 1800 171 846’

After 3 genuine attempts to contact, go to Step 2 in Table 3.

9

Advise executor or administrator purpose of call + Read more ...

Confirm that the person on the phone is the executor or administrator of the estate of the deceased customer:

  • ‘We are attempting to contact the person responsible for managing the affairs of <customer name>, is that you?’

Has the person been confirmed as the appropriate person?

  • Yes, close off the DOC and the relevant returned to sender work item and go to Step 4 in Table 1

Beneficiary indicators + Read more ...

A social work referral may be required before contacting potential beneficiaries.

If sensitivities are identified and a social worker was previously involved for the refund process and no subsequent issues were identified, referral to a social worker is not required again.

Check the potential beneficiary's record for any of the following:

  • under 25
  • over 70
  • identified vulnerabilities
  • mental health or significant health issues (MC screen)
  • recent social work contact (particularly around FDV)
  • record indicates homelessness or instability in accommodation
  • Indigenous Australian or other cultural sensitivity issues
  • on Income management/Cashless Debit Card
  • has a payment nominee
  • previous referral for social work contact for the refund process

If sensitivities are identified and a social worker was previously involved for the refund process and no subsequent issues were identified referral to a social worker is not required again.

Does the potential beneficiary have any of the indicators?

  • Yes, refer to a social worker. See the Resources page
  • No, go to Step 5

Contact potential beneficiary + Read more ...

Always be sensitive when speaking to the person. If during the call:

  • the person is identified to be at risk of harm, see the Resources page for social work referral
  • sensitivities are identified but there is no immediate risk of harm, the settlement task can still be processed. See the Resources page for social work referral

Make 3 genuine attempts to contact. Record all contact attempts in a DOC. See the Resources page for the template.

Contact potential beneficiaries in this order:

  1. Spouse/s, including de-facto, civil, domestic or significant relationship partner
  2. Children (including adopted), oldest to youngest
  3. Parents
  4. Siblings, oldest to youngest

If no beneficiaries are found in the search, an aunt or uncle may be contacted if identified during the search.

If a person is contacted

Tell them:

  • ‘We are attempting to contact the person responsible for managing the affairs of <customer name>, is that you?'

If they are managing the customer's affairs and the appropriate person has been established, go to Step 7.

If they are not managing the customer's affairs:

  • Tell them:
    • ‘We are looking for the person responsible for managing the affairs of <customer name>. As this is not you, thank you for your time today.’
  • Do not disclose any further information. This includes that the customer is deceased

For voicemail leave a message as follows:

  • ‘I am from Services Australia and I am attempting to contact the person responsible for managing the affairs of <customer name>. Please call us back on 1800 171 846’

After ending the call, record details in a Note.

Make 3 genuine attempts to contact each potential beneficiary before contact the next in the order above.

After 3 genuine attempts to contact all of the identified beneficiaries in order, record in a DOC the results of the beneficiary search. See the Resources page for the template. Go to Step 7.

Obtain and record appropriate person + Read more ...

Note: the date of birth is for documenting purposes only.

    • if there are no other beneficiaries, close off all relevant DOCs

3

Explain their class action settlement categorisation + Read more ...

Tell the customer the purpose of the call is to:

  • tell them their debt categorisation outcome
  • confirm their payment destination and discuss international bank account servicing fees
  • process the refund (if required)
  • give assistance to register and lodge a query or dispute if required

Suggested dialogue:

'Due to international mail suspension the agency was unable to issue you your debt categorisation letter.

Only use the following dialogue if refund required:

'You are owed a refund on money relating to your Income Compliance Debt(s) that was originally calculated using ATO averaged income. We no longer use the averaging of ATO information.

The Federal Court of Australia has approved the Income Compliance class action settlement.

Services Australia has identified group members and has categorised each debt.

You have been categorised under <insert category> because <advise the customer the reason for the categorisation and an explanation as to why they fall under this category>.

This means you are eligible to receive a settlement payment from the Income Compliance class action. The settlement payment is in place of interest you could’ve earned on the debt repayments you made.'

For further information to assist with the conversation, see the Resources page of:

  • Processing class action settlements:
    • suggested dialogue
    • customer categorisation explanation
  • Income compliance program class action:
    • FAQs
  • Services Australia website

‘You will need to register and confirm your details if you haven’t already.

If you think we haven’t included all of your debts, or we haven’t taken into account repayments made on a debt or think your debt was based on averaged ATO income information, you have the option to lodge a dispute. This dispute will involve a review of the categorisation outcome (Tier 1). Once a review has been completed we will contact you to notify you of the outcome.

We can assist you with registration and lodging a dispute after we have confirmed your digital correspondence and payment destination.’

4

Confirm details where digital correspondence can be sent + Read more ...

As customer resides in a country where international mail is suspended, confirm if the customer is able to provide an email address where the agency can send settlement correspondence.

Explain to the customer that sending correspondence via email is less secure and they must agree if they wish to receive correspondence via that method.

Suggested dialogue:

'As you are eligible to receive a settlement payment, can I confirm if you have an email address that the agency can send letters to? Are you agreeable to receiving correspondence by email’?

Is the customer agreeable to receiving correspondence by email?

  • Yes,
    • DOC their record stating customer confirms to receive correspondence via email. Make note of the email address details and update the record
    • Escalate the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via a Quality Development Officer to Business Process and Service Design. Include the escalation reason: International customer email correspondence
    • Go to Step 5
  • No,
    • If customer has an alternate postal address in another country to receive correspondence, record the address in DOC, go to Step 5
    • If all options to deliver correspondence have been exhausted, go to Step 5

5

Confirm with the customer whether they have a current Australian bank account that their settlement payment could be paid into.

Explain that receiving the settlement payment into an international bank account could incur servicing fees. Tell the customer that they will need to contact the agency to request reimbursement if servicing fees are applied.

Customer contacts regarding reimbursement of servicing fees + Read more ...

Confirm the details of the servicing fees applied to the settlement payment transaction.

If the fees are higher than USD30.00, the customer will need to provide evidence for reimbursement.

Evidence can be given via the following methods:

  • MyGov account
  • Mail: Services Australia, International Services PO Box 7809 CANBERRA BC ACT 2610 AUSTRALIA
  • Fax: +(61) 3 6222 2799

The customer’s full name and Customer Reference Number (CRN) must be included on the document/s they provide.

The customer will have 56 days to provide evidence.

Are the fees higher than USD30.00?

  • Yes,
    • tell the customer they will need to provide evidence for reimbursement to be paid
    • place work item on hold for 56 days
    • annotate details on DOC on the customer's record
    • once evidence is received or the due date is reached, escalate the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via a Quality Development Officer to Business Process and Service Design
    • include the escalation reason: International Servicing Fee Reimbursement Request
    • include in escalation the amount deducted through processing fees and currency and what evidence has been provided
    • on finalisation of DOC add the following Keyword: SDSINT30
  • No, annotate details on DOC on the customer's record
    • escalate the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via a Quality Development Officer to Business Process and Service Design
    • include the escalation reason: International Servicing Fee Reimbursement Request
    • include the amount deducted through processing fees and currency
    • tell the customer their request for reimbursement will be referred for processing. Suggested dialogue:
      ‘Your reimbursement request has been submitted for review and will be processed once approved.’
    • on finalisation of DOC add the following Keyword: SDSINT30
    • procedure ends here
