Approximate calculation

The legislation lays down strict figures for the period on income support and the final decision must be correct, but in most cases the outcome will be clear cut, one way or the other. Instead of calculating the exact period, which may be time-consuming for each case, use this method to make an approximation before deciding whether a more precise count is required.

Calculating method for an approximate 26 week requirement period

This table describes the steps to take to do an approximate calculation for a customer to be granted the higher income support rate of Austudy.

Item

Calculation method for 26 week requirement period

1

Calculation + Read more ...

  • Determine the date of the day immediately before the student or Australian Apprentice commences/recommences qualifying for payment
  • Subtract nine months from this date (alternatively add three months and subtract one year, whichever is easier) - this gives a date approximately 39 weeks before the course started
  • Work backwards from the course start/ restart date, counting periods when the customer was not receiving a payment and periods the customer was receiving LTIS for a previous course. This count can be approximate
  • If the count of periods off payments reaches 13 weeks before the date nine months earlier is reached then the customer is most likely not eligible for the higher rate
  • If the date is reached before the count reaches 13 weeks then the customer is most likely eligible for the higher rate
  • If the count reaches 13 weeks around the same time as the date is reached then a more accurate count will be required

Note: if LTIS was payable for more than 13 weeks for the previous course in the past 39 weeks, the customer is no longer eligible to continue to receive the higher rate.

2

Customer is starting a new course or Australian Apprenticeship + Read more ...

Each time a student starts a new course or restarts a course following a break in study, they must satisfy the eligibility criteria in order to be considered a LTIS student.

A customer may be eligible when:

  • a customer was granted LTIS for a course which was to last for 12 months. However, after less than 13 weeks of study the student withdrew from this course and started another course
  • the number of weeks for which the student was paid the LTIS rate will need to be factored into the calculation for determining if the customer is eligible to receive LTIS for another course
  • the customer may still be eligible to receive the LTIS rate as they may have NOT exceeded the 13 week count in the past 9 months

A customer is not eligible when:

  • a customer was granted LTIS for a course which was to last 12 months. However, after more than 13 weeks of study the student withdrew or finished this course and started another course
  • the customer would not be eligible for the LTIS rate as they would have exceeded the 13 week count in the past 9 months

3

Calculation not required + Read more ...

The period of time on income support is not relevant when:

  • A customer's first language is a language other than English
  • The customer undertakes an approved English language course, as their second language
