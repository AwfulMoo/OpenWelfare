Urgent payments due to Centrelink delay 003-17040700

This page has the process for assessing urgent payment requests due to Centrelink delay. The decision to approve or deny an urgent payment is made by a Service Officer at the APS4 level or above, Service Delivery Partner Service Officers or specific Smart Centre staff. These are staff fully trained in urgent payments.

Urgent payment request

Table 1: customer contact, basic eligibility checks and considering other assistance.

Step

Action

1

Is the customer on Income Management? + Read more ...

  • Yes, see Urgent payment requests from Income Management customers. Procedure ends here
  • No,
    • If a nominee or third party is applying on behalf of the customer, go to Step 2
    • Otherwise, go to Step 3

2

Nominee or third party requesting urgent payment on behalf of customer + Read more ...

A correspondence nominee is the only type of nominee or third party that can apply for an urgent payment on the customer's behalf. The payment can only be made to the customer when a request is received from a correspondence nominee.

Is the nominee a correspondence nominee?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, an application cannot be made. Procedure ends here

3

Authenticate customer/nominee + Read more ...

If the customer receives Farm Household Allowance (FHA), transfer them to the Farmer Assistance hotline. If hotline staff are not trained in urgent payments they need to lodge a Technical Peer Support (TPS) request.

Before assessing or issuing an immediate payment, make sure the customer record is the correct one. Ask authentication questions to verify Proof of Record Ownership (PORO). See Authenticating a Centrelink customer.

For face-to-face contact, it is preferable the customer provides identity documents, including at least 1 photo identity document.

Is the customer or nominee authenticated and PORO confirmed?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, if the customer or nominee cannot confirm details and the Service Officer is not satisfied they are speaking with the customer:
    • Do not release any information
    • Verbally deny urgent payment request
    • Procedure ends here

4

Check reason for the delay + Read more ...

Do not approve the request for urgent payment if the customer solely contributed to the delay in their regular payment by failing to:

  • attend an interview
  • lodge a form or medical certificate on the due date, unless prevented by the nature of their incapacity or a medical condition
  • lodge a form personally
  • lodge a completed or signed form
  • advise of a change of payment destination, or
  • advise Centrelink of any change in their circumstances required by the Social Security Act 1991

If the initial delay was caused by the customer but Centrelink then made the situation worse due to errors, delays or omissions, consider the customer's request for urgent payment.

For a customer with participation requirements, it is not Centrelink delay if they delayed contacting to have a suspension lifted after failing to attend an interview due to a reasonable excuse. The customer contributed to the delay in their payment.

Did the customer solely contribute to the delay of their regular payment?

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for an immediate payment due to Centrelink delay. explore other assistance options for the customer. Note: SRSS customers should be referred to their Home Affairs contracted Service Provider, see Department of Home Affairs contracted service provider responsibilities for Status Resolution Support Services payment. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Is the customer receiving an eligible payment? + Read more ...

The customer must be receiving an eligible pension or allowance.

If they are receiving an ineligible payment, they cannot get an urgent payment and their request should be denied.

If the customer is in hardship and their new claim assessment (including Family Tax Benefit new claim) is delayed for any reason including ICT advice, the onus is on the Service Officer to seek to have the claim processed. This might include:

  • contacting ICT to see what can be done to allow the outstanding claim to be finalised, or
  • contacting the relevant processing team if an apparent delay is there

Is the customer receiving an eligible payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the request. See Processing urgent payment requests

6

Customer is receiving an eligible payment + Read more ...

Consider the effect of:

  • customers on a Cashless Debt Card (benefit type IQN)
  • customers with a nominee or third party arrangement

Once a decision is made to approve or reject the urgent payment, see Processing urgent payment requests.
