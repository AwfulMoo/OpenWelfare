Possible residency referral - gathering relevant information about the travel

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Residency information received + Read more ...

A residency decision must be made if Child Support is advised that a customer:

  • has left or intends to leave Australia for a significant period of time, at least 6 months
  • is entering Australia, or
  • is moving from 1 overseas jurisdiction to another

The Specialised Residency Team must make the residency decision. If advice is received that a customer has left or is intending to leave Australia, determine if a referral should be made to the Specialist Residency Team.

A contact record cannot be created in Cuba where travel is more than 3 months in the future. Do not continue with referral process. Record travel information and create a Residency Follow-up intray to check travel details closer to travel date

There are limited circumstances where a referral may be appropriate when the period is less than 6 months, such as the customer indicating the move is permanent

Where either customer is overseas, new applications, restarts, initiating agreements or court orders must be referred to International New Customers. See International New Customer referrals in the Customer referral guidelines.

For Domestic New Customers residency referrals to International New Customers, see International New Customer referrals in the Customer referral guidelines.

Advice of a customer's departure, arrival or change of jurisdiction can be received:

  • verbally or in writing
  • before or after the customer has departed Australia
  • from a customer advising Child Support of their own travel
  • from another person (including the other parent) about the customer
  • from a Service Officer during business as usual processing, that is Department of Home Affairs conducted to locate customer may reveal customer no longer in Australia

A residency referral may need to be sent to the Specialist Residency Team for a residency decision if the customer:

  • resides overseas or has an overseas address, and
  • the intended stay is more than 6 months or if visiting friends or relatives the intended stay is more than 12 months, or
  • entering Australia where previous residency was overseas, or
  • is moving from 1 jurisdiction to another

For help, refer to the team's aligned Service Support Officer (SSO) who will liaise with an International SSO and escalate further if required.

2

Customer advice received by phone + Read more ...

If the customer has contacted Child Support by phone, obtain details regarding their travel or intended travel using the Residency Referral Contact dialogue box on Cuba. Record the relevant information while the customer is on the phone.

The following questions will assist with recording residency referral contact information on Cuba, see Residency Cuba process help:

  • Where do you believe you are resident? When from? Why?
  • What is your intended travel duration including intended return date?
  • What is the reason for travel, that is employment, holiday, education, visiting friends or family?
  • What is your usual country of residence? Where have you previously/currently been living, for example Australia and gone/going to China (destination country)?
  • What is the destination country or countries? What country or countries have you travelled to?
  • How can the Child Support Program reach you? What are your contact details, full address including residential and postal and any phone numbers (record contact details in the Individual Customer Details window)
  • What is the number and nationality on your passport?
  • Are you an Australian government employee? Is your spouse?
  • Is your family travelling with you?
  • Do you maintain a home in your usual country of residence?
  • What actions have you taken to dispose of your home? If you own your house what actions have you taken to maintain or sell your home?
  • Do you maintain financial ties in your usual country of residence? Do you have a bank account where you were previously/currently living? Do you maintain a business/employment/education ties in usual country of residence?
  • Have you applied for permanent residency? If so, has it been granted?
  • Have you established a home in the destination country? If not are you taking any actions to establish a home?
  • Have you established financial ties in the destination country? Do you have a bank account in the country you are/have travelled to?
  • Have you established a business/employment/education in the destination country?
  • Do you have any other relevant information?

Note: it is important to obtain overseas contact details.

3

Further information to discuss + Read more ...

While the customer is on the phone:

  • For a payee, confirm disbursement details if Australian bank account. If the customer wishes to change disbursement details to an overseas address, seek help from an International SSO, see Technical support in Child Support
  • For a payer without a debt, ask the customer about the arrangements they have made to pay their child support whilst overseas
    Note: if the customer wants further information about implications on their case regarding possible residency decision, speak to an International SSO. For help, refer the customer to the factsheets available on the Services Australia website
  • For a payer with a debt, discuss payment of the debt with the customer. If the payer refuses to enter into a satisfactory arrangement, consider a DPO referral. See Departure Prohibition Orders (DPO) for Child Support customers

Advise that if they are to be referred, Child Support's International Teams may manage their case while they are overseas.

Where the customer advises of a change of address, update the address in the Individual Customer Details window and complete the Residency Referral window. When the specialised residency team accepts the residency referral, customer management will be as per the Customer Management Approach.

Go to Step 5.

4

Customer advice received in writing + Read more ...

If the customer has contacted Child Support in writing and:

  • provided sufficient information, go to Step 5
  • not provided sufficient information to establish that they intend to be overseas for more than 6 months, or if visiting friends or relatives they intend to be overseas for more than 12 months, contact the customer to obtain the information. See Contact with Child Support customers

Unable to contact customer

If the customer cannot be contacted by phone, send the Residency Questionnaire and covering letter by mail to the customer. Allow 7 to 14 days from the date of the letter (allow more time for customers overseas, or in rural/remote areas).

Create a Residency Questionnaire Sent intray, adjust the due date accordingly. See Letters Cuba process help, create a letter using fill-in fields and Letter Details window help.

5

Conduct Department of Home Affairs search + Read more ...

A Department of Home Affairs search must be conducted where:

  • contact is unsuccessful, and the customer is likely to have left Australia
  • the customer has advised of future travel, or
  • before a referral can be made, where a customer has left Australia to confirm the customer's movement

Go to Step 9.

Otherwise, see Step 1 in the Submit residency referral table.

6

Advice received from third party + Read more ...

If information is received from the other parent, an employer, or other third party, request as much information as possible, using the travelling customer's Residency Referral Contact dialogue box on Cuba, see Steps 1 - 3.

Record relevant information while the person is on the phone.

Note: a contact record cannot be created in Cuba where travel is more than 3 months in the future. Do not continue with referral process. Record travel information and create a Residency Follow-up intray to check travel details closer to travel date.

7

Contact travelling customer + Read more ...

Contact the travelling customer to confirm the details recorded in the Residency Referral Contact dialogue box on Cuba.

If the travelling customer has new or different information, capture this information as a new contact record for the existing residency referral record, see Steps 1 - 3.

8

Unable to contact travelling customer + Read more ...

If unable to contact the travelling customer by phone, send a Residency Questionnaire and covering letter by mail. Create a letter by selecting Res 1-4 or Res 1-5 depending on the customer’s role. The questionnaire will print with the letter. See Letter Details window help. If required, print the questionnaire from Online Forms.

Note: to print the letter locally to address and post it to the customer's current location, use the Letter Preview icon. See Letters List window help.

Allow 7 to 14 days from the date of the letter (allow more time for customers overseas, or in rural/remote areas). Create a Residency Questionnaire Sent intray (adjust the due date accordingly).

If the contact is unsuccessful, and the customer is likely to have left Australia, go to Step 9.

Otherwise, see Step 1 in the Submit residency referral table.

If a change of address has been confirmed by the travelling customer ensure the new address is recorded in the Residency Referral window, and updated in the Individual Customer Details window, see Customer records Cuba Process Help, update individual customer details.

If the change of address has not been confirmed, only record the information in the Residency Referral window. Do not change the address in the Individual Customer Details window. The Specialist Residency Team will follow up and change the address once confirmed.

9

Department of Home Affairs search + Read more ...

When a customer has departed Australia or is entering Australia a Department of Home Affairs search will confirm their movement details.

To request a Department of Home Affairs search for a payee:

  • record a Level 2 search request in the Capacity to Pay window as Level 2 DIAC Search requested including the reason for the request
  • Level 2 Search intray to remain with the owning officer to monitor for response

See the External searches guide - Specialised searches Level 2 for information on requesting a Department of Home Affairs search. Create a DIMIA Search Requested intray with a 21 day due date.

If unable to contact the travelling customer, also request the customer’s PAX card from the Department of Home Affairs. This document is completed by the travelling customer when entering Australia and was completed until 30 June 2017 when departing Australia. It includes detailed information that may assist with making a residency decision.

PAX Cards for departing customers are only available where the movement was prior to 1 July 2017.

A request for PAX card details can only be made 10 or more days after the travelling customer’s arrival date.

See the External searches guide - Specialised searches Level 2 for information on requesting a Department of Home Affairs search and a customer's PAX card details. Create a DIMIA Search Requested intray (with a 21 day due date).

On receipt of the search results:

  • Record the results on the travelling customer's Residency Referral Contact dialogue box on Cuba, see Residency Cuba Process Help, record Department of Home Affairs information for a residency referral
  • Delete the DIMIA Search Requested intray
  • See Step 1 in the Submit residency referral table

Submit the residency referral in Cuba

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

Submit residency referral in Cuba + Read more ...

Once all the relevant information has been recorded on the travelling customer's Residency Referral Contact dialogue box, submit the customer referral for a residency decision.

If the referral would not normally result in a residency referral, an option on Cuba may be taken to override, forcing the customer to be referred for a residency decision. For example, the travelling customer is travelling from 1 overseas country to another overseas country. A Department of Home Affairs check did not identify any travel. If unsure of when to override and proceed with the residency referral, seek help from an International SSO see Technical Support in Child Support.

See Step 1 in the Referral to Specialist Residency Team table.

Once the residency referral has been submitted, Cuba will assess the contact data recorded for the referral to determine if the available information infers that:

  • The customer is to be referred for a residency decision. A Residency Decision Required intray (with a 28 day due date) is created, see Step 1 in the Referral to Specialist Residency Team table
  • The customer does not need to be referred for a residency decision, go to Step 2
  • The decision to refer is to be deferred until it has been confirmed if the travelling customer has returned in accordance with the initial travel intent. A Future Residency Referral intray (with a 365 day due date) is created. Go to Step 3

Note: if a residency referral has been submitted to a Team Leader (TL), and it is necessary to add additional information, request the TL reject the original submission, see Residency Cuba Process Help, Submit a Residency referral. Update with the additional information and resubmit to the TL.

2

No residency decision required + Read more ...

Where Cuba displays a message indicating that no residency decision is required:

  • advise the customer there will be no change to their residency status as the journey is of a short duration
  • offer CSAonline as an option to manage their child support while overseas, and
  • advise the customer to contact Child Support if their plans change

Child Support will not reassess the customer's residency status (there will be no change).

Procedure ends here.

3

Residency decision delayed + Read more ...

A residency decision may be delayed:

  • to allow confirmation that the customer has not returned in accordance with the travel intention communicated to Child Support, go to Step 4
  • where the travel date is a future date, go to Step 5

4

Confirm traveller has returned + Read more ...

Change the Future Residency Referral intray due date to the day after the customer is expected to return as a reminder to request a Department of Home Affairs check.

A Department of Home Affairs check is required to confirm whether the customer has returned in accordance with the initial intention communicated to Child Support. To assist with requesting a Department of Home Affairs search, see Step 9 in the Possible residency referral table.

Do not refer a Future Residency Referral intray to Specialist Residency Team

Note: after the Department of Home Affairs check is completed and the results are recorded, go to Step 1.

5

Future travel date + Read more ...

Change the Future Residency Referral intray due date to the day after the travel date as a reminder to request a Department of Home Affairs check. To assist with requesting a Department of Home Affairs search, see Step 9 in the Possible residency referral table.

Do not refer a Future Residency Referral intray to Specialist Residency Team.

Note: after the Department of Home Affairs check is completed and the results are recorded, go to Step 1.

Referral to Specialist Residency Team

Table 3:

Step

Action

1

Referral to Specialist Residency Team (Service Officer action) + Read more ...

To refer the customer for a residency decision, route the Residency Decision Required intray to the Service Officer TL.

Continue to manage the case as per Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support.

Do not refer a Residency Referral Follow Up intray to Specialist Residency Team. New applications, restarts, initiating agreements and court orders where either customer is overseas must be referred to International New Customers, see Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support.

2

Team leader/manager action + Read more ...

If a Residency Decision Required intray is received ensure all appropriate details have been recorded in the travelling customer's Residency Referral Contact dialogue box by viewing the residency referral details. See Residency Cuba Process Help, View history.

Note: a decision to approve, reject or approve override a residency referral must be made within 2 working days.

When completing the review, confirm if the following information is recorded in a separate 'contact' record?

  1. Department of Home Affairs and/or PAX card request
  2. Travelling customer (phone and/or residency questionnaire)
  3. Other customer (phone and/or residency questionnaire)
  4. Other source

If the answer is yes to at least 1 and 2, the submission can be accepted or rejected. If the submission is to be accepted, go to Step 3. If the submission is to be rejected, go to Step 5.

If the answer is no to 1 and 2, and it is possible to source information, the submission must be rejected, go to Step 5.

If the answer is no to any or all but all information has been sourced that can be, accept if the option on Cuba presents, go to Step 3.

Otherwise consider the most appropriate option, either:

  • go to Step 4 to 'approve override' the referral, or
  • go to Step 5 to reject the referral

Seek help from an International SSO if required. See Technical support in Child Support.

3

Approve (TL/manager) + Read more ...

If it has been determined that a residency decision is required, process the referral. Residency Cuba Process Help, submit referral. Route the existing Residency Referral Required intray to the Specialist Residency Team. See International Services, Residency decision required in the Customer referral guidelines.

Do not update the Client Residency window. The Specialist Residency Team will do this, if necessary, following the residency decision.

Note: this ends the process for domestic TL/Service Officer.

4

Approve override (TL/manager) + Read more ...

The decision to approve override should only occur when:

  • International SSO advice has been sought, see Technical Support in Child Support or
  • there are compelling reasons to refer, for example Complaint, COA, Ministerial, PS, Ombudsman

Document the reasons for approve override in the Residency Decision Required intray before routing to the Specialist Residency Team. See Documenting Child Support information.

Do not update the Client Residency window. The Specialist Residency Team will do this, if necessary, after the residency decision.

Note: this ends the process for domestic TL/Service Officer.

5

Reject (TL/manager) + Read more ...

If a decision is made that the residency referral should not proceed, process the decision to reject the referral, see Residency Cuba Process Help, submit referral. Route the Residency Decision Required intray back to the Service Officer to review details before resubmitting.

Procedure ends here.
