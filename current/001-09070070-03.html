1

Identifying job seekers who are meeting requirements through paid work + Read more ...

Job seekers who are doing sufficient paid work to fully satisfy their mutual obligation requirements may approach Services Australia about having their activity approved, so they do not have to participate in employment services and do other activities like looking for work.

Alternatively, Service Officers should try to identify job seekers who could possibly have paid work approved and become Centrelink managed.

When a job seeker with:

  • part-time mutual obligation requirements (principal carer, partial capacity to work (PCW), or
  • mature age 55+ job seeker PCP/PCW/TRWX/MA55+), and
  • has been declaring sufficient hours and earnings from employment to possibly fully satisfy their requirements,
  • an activity and action item will automatically generate on their record to prompt Service Officers to consider approving the activity

The action item is as follows, 'Customer's recent earnings suggest employment may be approved as meeting mutual obligation requirements. If approving, update Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen and determine if a Job Plan update is required. Do not update the Job Plan if the customer is a Disability Employment Services (DES) participant.

Job seekers with full-time mutual obligation requirements can also use work to fully satisfy their obligations but the work must be at least 70 hours per fortnight with income equal to or above the national minimum wage. If the employment is less, other activities like job search will be required. They may still qualify for payment depending on the income test applicable for their circumstances.

Note: from 3 November 2018, some principal carers will have their approved part-time work (PTW) activity automatically updated if they:

  • are a principal carer, and
  • are receiving JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Parenting Payment Single (PPS), or Youth Allowance (YA) (Job seeker), and
  • have declared in their last 2 consecutive fortnights that they have worked at least 30 hours each fortnight and earned income equal to or above the national minimum wage

Approving paid work as an approved activity or updating details once approval has been made + Read more ...

Job seekers can view scheduled appointments through their online account, but it will appear as a 'Participation Requirements Appointment'.

If the job seeker cannot accept a call from the service centre (e.g. they have inbound calling restrictions on their phone service), Service Officers should check Office Locator to decide if a walk-in is needed instead of a booked appointment. Procedure ends here

Updating details

  • a combination of:
    • paid work,
    • self-employment,
    • activities such as study or voluntary work for at least 15 hours per week (30 hours per fortnight)?

Note: for job seekers with full-time mutual obligation requirements, paid work of at least 70 hours per fortnight with income equal to or above the national minimum wage.
Job seekers with a partial capacity to work (PCW) of 0-14 hours per week can choose to do some paid work. For further information, see Negotiating Job Plans.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the paid work does not meet the requirements. The job seeker may need a referral to an Employment Services Provider if they have not already been connected. Providers are responsible for negotiating and maintaining the Job Plan for provider managed job seekers. 4

Is the job seeker earning at least the national minimum wage? + Read more ...

For ministers of religion who are either a principal carer of a dependent child or a person assessed as having a partial capacity to work (PCW), who is doing at least 30 hours of paid pastoral work a fortnight for their religious order, the amount of remuneration received does not have to be at the national minimum wage.

Note: more information about the national minimum wage is on the Fair Work Ombudsman website. Rates quoted in the information section of this site relate to workers 18 years or older. Other rates like junior rates are listed in the fact sheets for relevant years on the relevant website. See the Resources page for a link to the website.

Income provided by a partner to a member of a couple who is receiving a social security payment, does not meet the definition of income under the Social Security Act (1991). This does not preclude instances where a principal carer does employment and gets income from a business where their partner is involved in that business, as long as the activity meets the necessary criteria.

For principal carers, a combination of paid work and self-employment or paid work and approved study, totalling at least 15 hours per week (30 hours per fortnight), will fully meet mutual obligation requirements. The business income must be equivalent to the national minimum wage for the agreed hours of employment.

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

Paid work does not meet requirements + Read more ...

The paid work does not meet the requirements and cannot be an approved activity. The job seeker may need a referral to an Employment Services Provider if they are not already connected. Providers are responsible for negotiating and maintaining the Job Plan for provider managed job seeker. If Centrelink managed, the job seeker needs to choose another activity that fully meets their requirements.

6

Paid work meets requirements + Read more ...

For both Centrelink managed and provider managed job seekers, record the paid work as an approved activity.

If the job seeker has not started work yet, consider the correct start date to use when recording the approved activity, as this could affect their ability to use self service reporting. Activities can be recorded on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen, and in the Job Plan with a start date of up to 2 weeks in the future. Once the activity start date has been reached, the self service reporting system will expect the required hours and earnings to be declared for each fortnight. If the job seeker has only commenced work part way through the first fortnight, they will not normally be declaring sufficient hours or earnings, and they will be transferred to a Service Officer (if using phone self-service), or asked to contact the agency to have the report finalised.

  • For jobactive and Community Development Program (CDP) provider managed job seekers, negotiate a Job Plan with the job seeker that reflects the activities approved as fully meeting the job seeker's mutual obligation requirements, the job seeker will now be Centrelink managed
  • Use the 'Paid work' activity in the Job Plan so that activity start and end dates are included. This ensures the approved activity is reviewed at least every 12 months, and aligns with activity coding on the AEX screen
    Note: The Job Plan will reflect the new dates on the AEX screen once accepted by the job seeker. For job seekers who are not DES participants, do not update AEX prior to negotiating, and, the Job Plan been accepted. The preferred coding is to update the Job Plan first. The AEX screen is then updated if needed ('PTW' activities copy across to AEX)
  • Use other appropriate activities in the Job Plan where a combination of activities are being approved, e.g. 'Study - part-time or full-time' activity for principal carers doing an approved combination of part-time work and study
  • Record the approved paid work activity on the AEX screen using the code of 'PTW'. Note: if doing a combination of suitable paid work and self-employment, use both codes 'PTW' and 'SEL'
  • For mature age 55+ customers doing a combination of voluntary and paid work, use code 'VPE' on the AEX screen. See Voluntary work as an approved activity if the customer is attending for the annual review of their voluntary work activities
  • For principal carers doing a combination of part-time work and approved study, use code 'SPW'

Note: part-time work and other approved activities should not be coded on the AEX screen for more than 12 months at a time. A new Job Plan is needed every 12 months if the work is continuing. Ensure end date(s) on the AEX screen align with the Job Plan activity end date(s).

7

Advise the customer + Read more ...

  • they satisfy their mutual obligation requirements as they are doing an approved activity
  • they still need to report or lodge their Reporting Statement via self service (online or phone), in person or in writing (SU19) fortnightly but do not need to report job search efforts
  • once the paid work (PTW) approved activity is coded on the customer's record, the details are sent to the Department of Education, Skills and Employment and the customer will automatically be suspended from Employment Provider Services. There is no need for customers to discuss this with their Employment Services Provider. However, the customer may choose to volunteer in Employment Provider Services. In these cases, the customer would need to discuss this with their Employment Services Provider
  • Note: job seekers 55 years and over:
    • with a combination of voluntary and paid work will have a review of their participation in voluntary work after 12 months. An appointment is automatically booked for them and they must attend. See Voluntary work as an approved activity for details
    • may be eligible for variable reporting only if it is decided their income is unlikely to change and they meet all other eligibility criteria for variable reporting
    • are suspended from Employment Provider Services, and are not required to attend ongoing Employment Services Provider appointments. However, they must stay connected to their provider and must accept full-time, suitable paid work or an increase in their hours of work, and must accept all referrals to suitable job interviews
    • must tell Services Australia of changes about their participation in an approved activity within the required timeframe and how the changes may affect their mutual obligation requirements

Note: the employer or hours worked cannot be recorded through the Earnings and Reporting workflow unless the job seeker is due to lodge an SU19 and has already started their part-time hours.

The workflow allows the user to record the employer, gross income and hours of work.

Has the job seeker met their requirements?

  • Yes, complete the workflow then process the SU19. Procedure ends here
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

9

Principal carer did not declare minimum wage + Read more ...

Where a customer with 'PTW' 'drops through' to the Smart Centre Call or contacts about a failed self service reporting attempt, the following action is needed:

  • Stimulate the customer's current outstanding payment
  • Discuss the customer's regular declared income. If declared income has consistently been under the minimum wage per fortnight, go to Step 10

Note: if regular declared income is usually above the minimum wage, but their employer has not yet passed on the latest increase, tell the customer of the change to the minimum rate and discuss. Annotate the failed self service DOC with details of the discussion. Procedure ends here.

10

Declared income consistently under minimum wage + Read more ...

  • If declared income is consistently under the minimum wage per fortnight, 'PTW' must be ended on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen for the customer (Date of Effect (DOE) = date of discussion)
  • Tell the customer they must be connected to an Employment Services Provider for further assistance as the job seeker will have job search requirements. Their Employment Services Provider must negotiate a Job Plan with them
  • If a job seeker can arrange for an increase in their hourly rate of income with their employer to match the current national minimum wage, they can have 'PTW' approved again
  • Annotate the failed self service DOC with details of the discussion

11

Disability Employment Services (DES) provider managed job seekers + Read more ...

If the job seeker has not started work yet, consider the correct start date to use when recording the approved activity, as this may affect their ability to use self service reporting.

Activities can be recorded on the AEX screen, with a start date up to 2 weeks in the future.

If the job seeker has already started work, use the date of notification.

The end date should be coded as the end date for employment + 1 (if known) or no more than 12 months in the future.

  • Select PTW or EPT (Early School Leavers PTW) as the activity and code the start and end date for paid work on the AEX screen
  • Do not negotiate a Job Plan
  • Do not discuss exiting the current DES provider with the customer. DES Providers can offer Post Placement Support or Ongoing Support to customers who require additional workplace support
  • The DES provider will act on the coding of the activity on the AEX screen and will contact the job seeker to discuss whether the job seeker will keep participating in DES or not
  • If the job seeker continues in DES, the provider will update the Job Plan with the paid work activity
  • If, after talking with their DES provider, a job seeker chooses to exit DES (and is eligible to exit), the Job Plan must be updated by Services Australia at that time

Note: part-time work and other approved activities should not be coded on the AEX screen for more than 12 months at a time.

Mutual obligation requirements not met

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Requirements not met + Read more ...

Does the job seeker qualify for an exemption from their requirements?

If the job seeker asks for an exemption from their requirements, consider the merits of the case and make a decision. If granting an exemption, code it on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen.

  • Yes, go to 'reasonable excuse', select 'no excuse' and then record the reason why an excuse was not needed on a DOC. Process the payment, subject to all other eligibility being met. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Reasonable excuse for not meeting requirements + Read more ...

Check if the job seeker has a reasonable excuse for not meeting the requirements of their Job Plan. With an exception that working hours will return to normal in the next fortnight, the only acceptable responses are:

  • ill or injured
  • a sick child
  • cancelled shift
  • short term reduction in hours due to public holiday
  • pupil-free day
  • other

Note: principal carers and people assessed as having a partial capacity to work (PCW) of 15 or more hours per week:

  • Are not required to look for additional work if they were working 30 or more hours a fortnight and then have a drop in their hours that is temporary and unforeseen, and their work is likely to increase to more than 30 hours in the next fortnight
  • Whose approved activity is permanent part-time work, are not required to look for additional work if their work falls below 30 hours per fortnight for any period, if their basic rate of income support does not increase during that period. That is, they get the same level of employment income during the break

Does the job seeker have a reasonable excuse for not meeting their requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Code the reasonable excuse + Read more ...

  • Code the reason in the Earnings and Reporting workflow and process the job seeker's payment as usual. Payment will not be affected
  • Revert their reporting requirements to fortnightly if they are a mature age 55+ job seeker and had previously been placed on variable reporting
  • For:
    • mature age 55+ customers, go to Step 4
    • job seekers under 55, tell them that a maximum of 2 consecutive reasonable excuses is accepted

Procedure ends here.

4

Second or third consecutive reasonable excuse for job seeker aged 55 and over (55+) + Read more ...

After providing 2 or 3 consecutive reasonable excuses (for mature age 55+ job seekers), the job seeker must choose another activity type as they cannot meet their obligations through part-time work any longer.

Complete the following actions:

  • process the outstanding Reporting Statement (SU19)
  • end date the current approved activity on the AEX screen
  • connect or reconnect the job seeker with their Employment Services Provider (if not already active with one)
  • use the Job Plan workflow to renegotiate their Job Plan to remove the part-time work activity, and
  • include a new appropriate activity. This shows the provider the activity is not meeting requirements through an approved activity and ensures the job seeker will be engaged by the provider in a timely manner. The job seeker's record will display as provider managed when Department of Education, Skills and Employment send an advice that the customer has commenced or recommenced in Employment Provider Services

Note: when the job seeker becomes provider managed, the Employment Services Provider may renegotiate the Job Plan with the job seeker.

Procedure ends here.

5

No reasonable excuse or has ceased activity + Read more ...

If the job seeker does not have a reasonable excuse for failing to meet their requirements, they must choose another activity type, as they cannot meet their obligations through paid work.

  • Investigate other approved activities for the job seeker. If they are not currently doing another activity or combination of activities that can be approved by Services Australia as fully meeting requirements, the job seeker must be referred to an Employment Services Provider
  • End date the part-time work activity coding on the AEX screen
  • Refer the customer to their Employment Services Provider for their Job Plan to be updated. Record a DOC confirming the advice given and any relevant information from the job seeker

This shows the provider that the customer is not meeting requirements through an approved activity and ensures the provider engages the job seeker in a timely manner. The customer's record will display as provider managed when the Department of Education, Skills and Employment send advice that the customer has commenced or recommenced in Stream Services.

The job seeker must immediately resume participation with their Employment Services Provider or be referred if not already active with one and must attend all appointments.

Note: when the job seeker becomes provider managed, the Employment Services Provider may renegotiate the Job Plan with the job seeker.

Procedure ends here.

6

Review of approved activities for job seeker aged 55 and over (55+) + Read more ...

If the job seeker is 55+ doing a combination of part-time and voluntary work of at least 30 hours per fortnight, they must attend an annual review to discuss their participation. See Voluntary work as an approved activity for details.

If the activity type of paid work is being reviewed, check the job seeker still wants to continue with this activity.

  • If there are no changes, record the conversation on a DOC. Extend the end date of the approved activities as appropriate. If the job seeker is Centrelink or provider managed, renegotiate the Job Plan to reflect the new period of the approved activity
  • If the job seeker wants to change their activity type:
    • Update the coding and end date for approved activities on the AEX screen as needed
    • If the job seeker was suspended from Stream Services, they must be reconnected with their provider
    • Refer the customer to their Employment Services Provider for their Job Plan to be updated

This shows the provider the job seeker is not meeting requirements through an approved activity and ensures the provider engages the job seeker in a timely manner. The customer's record will display as provider managed when the Department of Education, Skills and Employment send an advice that the job seeker has commenced or recommenced in Stream Services.

Note: when the job seeker becomes provider managed, the Employment Services Provider may renegotiate the Job Plan with the job seeker.

Record the decision on a DOC. Procedure ends here.

7

Previously approved PTW activity has expired on the AEX screen + Read more ...

Is the job seeker still meeting requirements through sufficient paid work?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, refer the job seeker to their Employment Services Provider for their Job Plan to be updated

This shows the provider that the customer is not meeting requirements through an approved activity and ensures the provider will engage the job seeker in a timely manner. The customer's record will display as provider managed when Department of Education, Skills and Employment send an advice that the customer has commenced or recommenced in Stream Services.

The job seeker must immediately resume participation with their Employment Services Provider or be referred to a provider, if not already active with one, and must attend all appointments.

Procedure ends here.

8

Check the job seeker's Job Plan + Read more ...

Is there a corresponding activity in the Job Plan that has also expired?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, the PTW activity on the AEX screen should correspond with the Job Plan activity end date
    Note: PTW can only be coded for a maximum period of 52 weeks; therefore, a new PTW line may need to be coded on the AEX screen if the 52 week period has expired. If discrepancies are noted use Fast Note, select the Auto Text Option and select Parents>Updates>PTW extension to send an ACTDOC to the service centre

Procedure ends here.

9

Corresponding activity in Job Plan has expired + Read more ...

  • Use the Job Plan workflow to negotiate a new Job Plan including the part-time work activity
  • The AEX screen should auto-populate the PTW activity code in line with dates coded in the Job Plan
    Note: PTW can only be coded for a maximum period of 52 weeks on the AEX screen. A new PTW line may be required if the 52 week period has already exceeded
  • Smart Centre Service Officers who are suitably skilled may negotiate a Job Plan with the job seeker over the phone
  • Otherwise:
    • Book the first suitable 'Compulsory Participation Interview' available at the job seeker's local service centre. Advise the job seeker of the time, date and reason of the upcoming appointment. Record a DOC confirming the advice given and any relevant information from the job seeker. If they cannot accept a phone call from the service centre (e.g. they have incoming call restrictions on their phone service), Service Officers should check Office Locator and decide if a walk-in or another appointment type should be booked

Procedure ends here.
