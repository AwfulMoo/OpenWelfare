Acceptable documents to change a legal name at Personal level

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Acceptable documents where a customer has changed their legal name at Personal + Read more ...

At Personal a person must have their legal name recorded.

When a customer has changed their legal name, the following documents are acceptable:

  • Australian marriage certificate. Note: ceremonial certificates are not acceptable
  • Australian birth certificate
  • Australian Certificate of Name change
  • Reissued Australian birth certificate in the new name
  • Birth certificates for Northern Territory (NT) with Aboriginal Population Record (APR)

Change of name overseas: Australian visa holders who do not have Australian citizenship, who ‘return’ to Australia and ask to change their legal name must provide:

  • A foreign passport with a current Australian visa (verified)

See Escalation process for Medicare Eligibility for help.

The name must be changed at both Personal and Group unless the applicant produces documents to support a preferred name change at Group. See Table 4 Change of legal name in Personal.

If this customer is the group contact, the name of the group contact must also be changed, see Amend customer details in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) for Medicare.

Where a name has been previously changed at Group and the Comment field indicates that the documents sighted would be sufficient to allow a name to be amended at Personal (for example, a marriage certificate), a name at Personal can be changed.

See Table 4 Change of legal name in Personal.

Note: if a customer provides an Australian Passport or Australian Citizenship Certificate in their new name along with a foreign Marriage certificate or foreign birth certificate, these matters need to be escalated to Medicare Public Eligibility Helpdesk. See Escalation process for Medicare Eligibility.

Acceptable documents where a customer requests a Major Variation to change their recorded legal name + Read more ...

When a customer requests a change to their recorded legal name (major variation), the following documents are acceptable:

  • Australian birth certificate or marriage certificate
  • Australian Drivers licence
  • Australian or foreign Passport
  • Australian Certificate of Name change
  • Australian Citizenship Certificate
  • Immicard

If this customer is the group contact, the name of the group contact must also be changed, see Amend customer details in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) for Medicare.

The name must be changed at both Personal and Group unless the applicant produces documents to support a preferred name change at Group.

See Table 5 - Change of legal name in Personal.

Acceptable documents to change a preferred name at Group level

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Acceptable documents to change a name at Group + Read more ...

At Group, a person may be known by their legal name or their preferred name.

Acceptable documents to change a preferred name at Group can include:

  • Current driver's licence
  • Utility accounts (telephone, gas or electricity) no more than 6 months old - applicant's address on documents must match the address held on Medicare record
  • Current bank accounts or statements - applicant's address shown on the account must match address held on Medicare enrolment file
  • Centrelink correspondence
  • Letter from a registered medical practitioner or a registered psychologist if customer is undergoing change of gender. Letter must specify both legal name and preferred name
  • Ceremonial marriage certificate

Details of the type of document sighted to action the preferred name must be recorded in the Personal Comments field in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS).

See Table 6 Change name of customer in Group.

Change a name for a child under 15 years of age

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Change a name for a child under 15 years of age + Read more ...

A child must be known by their legal name at both Personal and Group. Note: they cannot have a preferred name (unless undergoing a change of gender).

Documents required to change the name of a child at both Personal and Group:

  • Australian Certificate of Name Change
  • Court Order which states that the child is to be known by a specific name
  • Australian birth certificate
  • Reissued Australian birth certificate
  • Foreign passport with Australian visa

Acceptable documents to change a name of a child at Group undergoing change of gender includes, but is not limited to:

  • A letter from a registered medical practitioner or a registered psychologist if customer is undergoing a change of gender. The letter must specify both legal name and preferred name
  • Centrelink correspondence

Where documentation is received and a Service Officer is unsure if it can be used to change a child’s name, contact HSDD-Eligibility or a PSO for further advice.

  • Details of the type of document sighted to action the change of name must be recorded in the Personal Comments field in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS).

Record comments + Read more ...

Register a customer with a long name in Group

Table 7

Step

Action

1

Long name + Read more ...

A name is considered long when any of the following apply:

  • the person's last name contains more than 18 characters
  • their first given name contains more than 12 characters, or
  • the combination of their first name, initial and family name have more than 25 characters (including spaces between the names)

2

Reduce a long name + Read more ...

  • Register a new customer
  • Register customer’s Medicare entitlement

After a customer’s entitlement is registered:

  • Select Group on the Registration Type screen
  • The New Medicare Card Details screen displays

The New Medicare Card Details screen is displayed. It shows how the name will look on the Medicare card. Some of the characters will be missing.

Reduce the name:

  • In the Name field (which has the long name) delete the characters at the end. Do this until the name fits in the field

Note: this does not affect the name displayed on the Medicare card.

3

Record long name + Read more ...

  • select the Long Name tab
  • key the customer’s names into each field in order as below:
    • first name
    • second initial, and
    • family name
  • select Group Contact tab

If the contact person is on the card:

  • select the Card Association box

In Contact Card Reference Number field:

  • key the customer's card reference number, e.g. 1
  • select Get Details

The remaining fields are populated with the customer's details.

If the group contact is not associated with the card:

  • key their name in the Contact Name field as:
    • last name [insert comma]
    • title [insert space]
    • initial

Complete Address field:

  • key locality or postcode
  • select the locality from drop down box
  • start keying the number and street name on the Address line
  • select the address from the drop down box

4

Comments + Read more ...

Select Comments tab:

  • key comments if required
  • select Validate

This will display the name to be embossed on the Medicare card.

5

Confirm Medicare card details + Read more ...

Select either:

  • Update to confirm the details displayed are correct
  • Change to amend the details

The new Medicare card record has been updated in CDMS. The Medicare number is displayed.

6

Complete Safety Net registration in CDMS + Read more ...

See Family registration and confirmation for Medicare Safety Net.

Shorten the name + Read more ...

  • Register a new customer to existing Medicare card
  • Register customer’s Medicare entitlement

Personal name fields

Fields in personal name do not have a character limit. The customer's first given and last name can be keyed in full.

After a customer’s entitlement is registered:

  • Select Group on the Registration Type screen
  • An error message may display as there are over 25 characters
  • Click Members tab
  • Then Members button

Reduce the name

  • In the Name field (which has the long name) delete the characters at the end. Do this until the name fits in the field.
  • Select Validate
  • Select Return

Examples

  • Daisy Rose Chrysanthemum-Petunia could be shortened to:
    • Daisy Chrysanthemum-Petu at Group level
  • Chrysanthemum Flower (first name) could be shortened to:
    • Chry Flower at Group level
