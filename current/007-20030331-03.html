Providing advice to customers about the reassessment of Parental Leave Pay (PPL)

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Flexible PPL changes + Read more ...

Flexible PPL changes affect claims for children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020. Customers will have more choice about how and when they get PPL to help them balance work and family commitments.

The changes were implemented from 14 September 2020.

Was the PPL child born or adopted before 1 July 2020?

  • Yes, the customer is not impacted by these changes. See Eligibility for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) as a primary claimant. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

PPL claim status + Read more ...

PPL new claim (NCL)

From 14 September 2020, the PPL Flexible Days guided procedure will be used within PPL new claim processing to record the customer’s Flexible PPL payment choices. If the claim was lodged before 12 September, this will need to be manually added. See Table 2.

PPL CURrent or ASSessed

  • Claims processed before 12 September 2020 were assessed using the PPL eligibility and payability rules for children that are born or adopted before 1 July 2020. From 12 September 2020, these claims will be reassessed and converted to include up to 30 Flexible PPL days
  • For current and assessed customers, the 18 week PPL period will be converted to an 18 week block of payment consisting of:
    • a 12 week PPL period, plus
    • 30 Flexible days (6 weeks)
  • Customers ineligible for PPL due to returning to work, transferring their payment, residency or losing primary care under the pre-1 July 2020 rules may also be reassessed from 12 September 2020
  • A letter will be issued to affected customer advising them of:
    • the changes to their Parental Leave Pay, and
    • how they can claim their Flexible PPL payment using the Paid Parental Leave Circumstances online service
  • To view or manage the customer’s Flexible PPL days, including claiming Flexible PPL days, see Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days

Processing PPL claims lodged before 14 September 2020

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Processing new claims for PPL that were lodged before 14 September 2020 + Read more ...

This process relates to PPL claims for children that were born on or after 1 July 2020.

To process claims for children that were born before 1 July 2020, see Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments.

Additional action is required to obtain Flexible PPL information when processing claims that were lodged before 14 September 2020 or claims that were lodged using an older version of the paper claim.

Add the PPL Flexible Days guided procedure into the staff workflows during the online claim processing in Customer First.

Is the customer claiming as a primary claimant or a secondary claimant as part of a PPL transfer?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No:
    • within the guided procedure, connect all 30 Flexible PPL days to the customer’s PPL period
    • The customer’s PPL grant letter will include information about how they can change these Flexible PPL days using the Paid Parental Leave Circumstances online service
    • Procedure ends here

2

PPL transfer + Read more ...

Primary claimants who have transferred some or all of their PPL to another person, will need to be contacted before their claims can be processed.

They can choose to:

  • connect their Flexible days to their PPL period
  • take them separately, not connected to their PPL period, including after they have returned to work and up to 2 years after their child’s birth/adoption
  • given some or all of them to another person to claim at a later time (primary claimants only)

For more information on Flexible PPL payment options, see Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days.

3

Contact primary claimant + Read more ...

One genuine attempt must be made to contact the primary claimant.

If the contact is successful use the PPL Flexible days guided procedure to record their payment option.

If the secondary claimant has not yet claimed PPL, advise the primary claimant to:

  • tell the secondary claimant to lodge their claim as soon as possible, and
  • provide them with the number of Flexible PPL days they have given them permission to claim. They will need to include this number in their claim

For more information, see Eligibility for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) as a secondary or tertiary claimant.

If the attempt to contact with the primary claimant is unsuccessful:

  • transfer the full or partial PPL period from the date nominated by the customer as per the claim
  • use the PPL Flexible Days guided procedure to record permission for another person to claim all 30 Flexible PPL days

The primary claimant will be able to remove the permission to claim the Flexible PPL days until they have been claimed by the secondary claimant. Removing permission can be done using the PPL Circumstance online service.

4

Process secondary claimant’s claim if available + Read more ...

Go to the primary claimant’s record and make note of the Flexible PPL payment choice recorded on their PPL claim. Note: the primary claimant’s claim must be processed first.

In the secondary claimant’s claim, add the PPL Flexible Days guided procedure into the staff workflows during the online claim processing in Customer First.

Transfer some or all of the PPL period

If the primary claimant has chosen to transfer some or all of the PPL period, record the PPL period transfer from the date nominated by the secondary claimant.

Note: If:

  • the primary claimant choses to keep the 30 Flexible PPL days and not give permission for another person to receive them,
  • the secondary claimants overall payment may be reduced

The Resources page contains an example.

5

Given permission for another person to claim Flexible PPL days + Read more ...

If the primary claimant has given permission for another person to claim some or all of their Flexible PPL days and:

  • contact with the secondary claimant is successful:
    • discuss the new PPL rules including the need to use the online service to access Flexible PPL days
    • Ask the secondary claimant if they know when they would like to access their Flexible PPL days. Use the PPL Flexible Days guided procedure to record the dates for these.
    • the Flexible PPL Declaration must be read and agreed to by the customer
  • contact with the secondary claimant is unsuccessful:
    • use the PPL Flexible Days guided procedure to record the payment choice.
    • If the transfer date is within the 12 week PPL period, connect all of the Flexible PPL days
    • If the transfer date is outside of the 12 week PPL period, the secondary claimant will only be eligible for Flexible PPL days.
      Record the number of Flexible PPL days as a continuous block of not connected Flexible PPL days commencing from the date the primary claimant transferred care. Do not include weekends in this block.
      The Resources page contains an example.

6

Recording PPL claim outcome + Read more ...

Record PPL claim outcomes using Fast Note. The DOC a New Claim script does not cater for Flexible PPL component outcomes.

Fast Notes are available for:

  • pre-birth and post birth claims
  • different claimant types (primary or secondary/tertiary), and
  • all PPL claim outcomes (granted, rejected, assessed, not effective)

If a PPL claim is processed as part of an FTB/PPL combined claim, use the DOC a New Claim script to record the FTB claim outcome.
