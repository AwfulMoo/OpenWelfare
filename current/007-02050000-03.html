6

Shared care percentage for social security payments + Read more ...

If Child Support have determined the percentage of care, there is no need to calculate the percentage of care. The percentage advised by Child Support can be used for the principal carer assessment and determination of Social Security payment entitlements for Income Support Payments, see Step 1 in Table 2.

If FCC Processing or Child Support staff have already determined the percentage of care for FTB purposes this percentage can be used for principal carer assessment and determination of Social Security payment entitlements, see Step 1 in Table 2.

Determine the assessment period to use. This may depend on the payment the customer is receiving.

If carers have agreed on the percentage of care, there is no need to calculate the percentage based on the agreed pattern of care or the available evidence.

To calculate the percentage:

  • add up the number of nights the child is in the customer's care during the assessment period based on the pattern of care
  • if the number of nights does not accurately reflect the time in care, the number of hours may be used. Add the number of hours for each part day the child is in care. Divide the total hours by 24 to get the number of days, and add this to the number of whole days in care to get the total days in care during the assessment period
  • divide the number of nights in care in the assessment period by the total number of days in the assessment period
  • multiply the result by 100 to get the shared care percentage
  • round to the nearest whole percentage (0.5 is rounded up) except where the percentage is close to the eligibility percentage, for example where a customer has 13.9% it is not rounded up to 14%. Exception for principal carer assessment: Percentages between 45.1 and 45.9 should all be rounded up to 46%. Having at least 45.1 of care means there is less than a 10% difference, therefore equal care policy applies. The system will only allow coding of whole numbers, so code 46%

Is the percentage of care 55% and above, or 45% and below?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No:
    • Create a Fast Note, select Auto text. Use Families > Updates > Principal Carer Determination Required
    • Go to Step 7

7

Principal carer determination manual review + Read more ...

For appropriately trained staff only.

A manual review to make a principal carer determination should be conducted if the percentage of care:

  • decreases below 55% to determine if the customer will continue to be paid as the principal carer
  • increases above 45% for a non-current principal carer, to assess a principal carer entitlement
  • see Principal carer determination in shared care arrangements

Note: where there are more than two (2) carers for a child, a manual review must be undertaken to determine who the child’s principal carer is.

These reviews also apply to any other carer where the percentage of care changes for income support customers.

Principal carer determination + Read more ...

Is the customer the principal carer of the child?

Note: if one carer's level of care is at least 10% more than the other carer's level of care, the child is a PP child of the person with the greater level of care. For example, where there is a shared care assessment and the customer has 60% care, the customer would have the greater degree of care and control of the child and therefore would be the principal carer.

  • Yes, the customer may qualify for Parenting Payment (PP) if they have a PP child, or as a principal carer for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker). Go to Step 11
  • No, if the customer is not a principal carer, they may be eligible for the 'with child' rate of benefit concession card and additional income free area for a dependent child in their care. Note: the additional income free area for dependent children is only available to pensioners paid under the transitional rules and Parenting Payment Single customers. Go to Step 10

Shared care assessment for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) + Read more ...

Has a care assessment already been completed for FTB based on this care arrangement?

  • Yes:
    • the shared care percentage is generally the same as for FTB, however may differ in specific circumstances, see Customer advises care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • see Step 1, Table 2
  • No:
    • Appropriately trained staff see Notification and assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • All other staff, see Customer advises care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)

For current FTB customers who wish to add an older child (not a newborn child), see Reassessment of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for a current customer where an additional child enters care in the current or previous financial year.

Customers can claim family assistance and PPL using the Online or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) for family assistance, Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and Dad and Partner Pay (DAP).

For more information about claiming family assistance payments, Parental Leave Pay (PPL), Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) and/or ancillary benefits for a Regular Care Child, see:

  • Claiming family assistance and Parental Leave Pay (PPL) for a newborn child
  • Claiming Family Tax Benefit (FTB) instalments for children in care other than a newborn or adopted child
  • Initial contact by customer's claiming Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)

Customers now caring for a child from a previous relationship may need to apply for child support to be paid more than base rate FTB Part A for the child.

If any of the customer's children attend child care, invite claim for Child Care Subsidy (CCS).

Foster carers who are not entitled to an automatic concession card through income support or FTB entitlement may wish to claim a Health Care Card (HCC) for a foster child. If the customer lodges a claim for family assistance, this can be used as a claim for a HCC for a foster child.

Customers caring for a Double Orphan Pension (DOP) child should be invited to claim DOP.

Note: a problem (P24845) has been identified where shared care is not being correctly applied when a child is in the care of a parent who has transferred their FTB to their current partner (step parent). This may impact on a record in multiple ways:

  • the other biological parent may have their FTB entitlement withdrawn for the shared care child, as the care percentage is being adjusted to 0%
  • the step parent may receive an incorrect percentage of care and therefore an incorrect rate of FTB
  • the care may transfer to the step parent's child support record (with incorrect child relationship details) causing inaccurate child support assessments and impacting multiple parties

The Resources page contains details of the workaround that must be applied every time care is reassessed on these records.

Finalise and record shared care arrangements

Step

Action

1

Code care details + Read more ...

Coding a change of care may cause a change of entitlement to income support payments:

  • for existing Parenting Payment Single (PPS) customers if principal carer status is lost for all children under 8 years of age, the customer will no longer be qualified. Offer the customer a Transfer to JSP if appropriate, see Transfer to JobSeeker Payment (JSP) from another payment
  • for existing JSP customers an increase in care may mean principal carer status is to be assigned. For single customers with children under 8 years of age who gain principal carer status, offer Parenting Payment Single (PPS)
  • for single customers with dependent children over 8 years of age but under 16 years of age who are principal carers, the 'with child' rate of JSP may be payable and/or the JSP single principal carer income test is applied
  • for single customers with a 'regular care' child in care, that is minimum of 14% care, who have a Court Order or Parenting Plan or written agreement for care arrangements that states they are a person the child is supposed to live with, may be entitled to the 'with child' rate
  • for partnered customers with children under 6 years of age, offer Transfer to Parenting Payment Single (PPP) from another payment
  • where the dependent child receives Youth Allowance or ABSTUDY there may be a change in parent for the Parental Means Test. See Principal carer of a dependent child

For coding reduced care, go to Step 2

For coding increased care, go to Step 3

2

Reduced care - record details for each carer + Read more ...

If percentage of care has reduced record details for losing carer first.

All children in the customers care under 16 years of age require coding if a change in care has occurred.

In the customer's record:

  • 'S'elect child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen. If the child is not listed, see Linking a child to a customer's record
  • on the Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen 'S'elect In Customer Care (CHC) screen, Shared Care (FTB) FSC screen, Shared Care (SSA) SSC screen and Override/Claim (CHOC) screen
  • check that the child is recorded as in the customer's care (ICC) on Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen. If not, code a DOV line on the CHC screen for the customer and other carer. The ICC line should be coded from the date the child entered the customer's care. If the customer is losing care of the child altogether an LCC line should be coded
  • on the FSC screen code the reduced care percentage, using the correct DOV and the correct DOR when applying care decisions and ensuring the Preliminary indicator is 'Y'
  • on the SSC screen, code the reduced shared care percentage using the correct DOV. If more than one customer is linked to the child, code the carer number for the customer in the Carer: field. Ensure the 'principal carer' indicator is correct according to the % of care
  • code Principal Carer: field on the SSC screen to show whether or not the customer is considered the principal carer of the child. Code 'No' if the child is not principally cared for by the customer. The Principal Carer: field is used to determine if the child is a PP child or not for PPP customers and principal carer status for JSP and SpB, regardless of the SSA % of care field
  • for JSP single principal carer customers, coding on the SSC, FSC, CHOC screens and PCCD screen (in the NSS system) is required to ensure the customer has the correct income test applied to their payment, has correct mutual obligation requirements and a PCC is issued. Code percentage of care and principal carer indicator 'Yes' or 'No' on the SSC screen
  • for JSP, YA (job seeker) and SpB customers, check/update the customer's principal carer status on the Principal Carer Circumstance Details (PCCD) screen (in the NSS system). If the customer is a principal carer, and there is no coding on the PCCD screen, code DOV and principal carer indicator 'Y'. If the customer is not the principal carer, and SSC screen principal carer indicator is 'N', the PCCD screen should be updated to 'N' for principal carer status
  • for a dependent child or where the 'with child' rate of JSP/SpB is payable, check that a claim line exists for the income support payment on the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen
  • for JSP, YA (job seeker) and SpB customers where they are not the principal carer but have a 'dependent child' in care (above 14%), or 'regular care' child in care, coding on the SSC screen, PCCD screen, FSC screen and CHOC screen is required to ensure the correct entitlement is assessed. Code percentage of care and principal carer indicator 'No' on the SSC screen
  • where a customer has 0% care for a child, code an 'off' line on the CHOC screen using reason 'TOP' with the correct DOV
  • for PPS customers, a child is considered a PP child regardless of coding on the SSC screen unless an override is coded on the CHOC screen. Code 'OTN' on the CHOC screen if the child is in the customer's care (but is not a PP child of the customer) so that the child is included when working out the customer's additional income free area, or to ensure PPS cancels if it is the last remaining child under 8 that was in the customer’s principal care
  • on the customer's record only, update the Care Alignment Details page for the child to 'Finalised'
  • check/ update Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM) screen if necessary
  • check/ update Child Student/Income Circumstances (CHSI) screen if necessary
  • finalise the update and record the details in a DOC

Note: CHC, FSC and SSC coding should ripple to the other carer, but will not update the rate of FTB until a care assessment is completed for the other carer. However, CHOC and PCCD coding will not ripple to the other carer. If the other carer becomes the principal carer due to the decrease in care, code a CHOC line, ensure SSC is correct, and code PCCD (in NSS system) on the other carer's record for the child/ren if not already updated.

Go to Step 4.

3

Increased care - Record details for each carer + Read more ...

If the percentage of care has increased record details for losing carer first. All children in the customer's care under 16 years require coding if a change in care has occurred.

In the customer's record:

  • 'S'elect child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen. If the child is not listed, see Linking a child to a customer's record
  • on the Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen, 'S'elect In Customer Care (CHC) screen, Shared Care (FTB) FSC screen, Shared Care (SSA) screen and Override/Claim (CHOC) screen
  • check that the child is recorded as in the customer's care on Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen. If not, code a DOV line on the CHC screen for the customer and other carer. If the customer is losing care of the child altogether an LCC line should be coded
  • on the FSC screen code the reduced care percentage, using the correct DOV and the correct DOR when applying care decisions and ensuring the Preliminary indicator is 'Y'
  • on the SSC screen, code the increased shared care percentage using the correct DOV. If more than 1 customer is linked to the child, code the carer number for the customer in the Carer: field. Ensure that the 'principal carer' indicator is correct according to the % of care
  • code Principal Carer: field on the SSC screen to show whether or not the customer is considered the principal carer of the child. Code 'No' if the child is not principally cared for by the customer. The Principal Carer: field is used to determine if the child is a PP child or not for PPP customers and principal carer status for JSP and SpB, regardless of the SSA % of care field
  • for JSP single principal carer customers, coding on the SSC, FSC, CHOC screens and PCCD screen (in the NSS system) is required to ensure the customer has the correct income test applied to their payment, has correct mutual obligation requirements and a PCC is issued. Code percentage of care and principal carer indicator 'Yes' on the SSC and PCCD screen
  • for JSP, YA (job seeker) and SpB customers, check/ update the customer's principal carer status on the Principal Carer Circumstance Details (PCCD) screen. If the customer is a principal carer, and there is no coding on the PCCD screen, code DOV and principal carer indicator 'Y'. If the customer is not the principal carer, and SSC screen principal carer indicator is 'No', PCCD screen (in the NSS system) should be updated to 'N' for principal carer status
  • for a dependent child or where the 'with child' rate of JSP/SpB is payable, check that a claim line exists for the income support payment on the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen
  • for JSP, YA (job seeker) and SpB customers where they are not the principal carer but have a 'dependent child' in care, or 'regular care' child in care, coding on the SSC screen, PCCD screen (in NSS system), and FSC screen and CHOC screen is required to ensure the correct entitlement is assessed. Code percentage of care and principal carer indicator 'No' on the SSC screen
  • where a customer has 0% care for a child, code an 'off' line on the CHOC screen using reason 'TOP' with the correct DOV
  • for PPS customers, a child is considered a PP child regardless of coding on the SSC screen unless an override is coded on the CHOC screen. If the child is:
    • now in the customer’s principal care, check coding on the CHOC screen. If there is a current override of OTN or TOP, a new line should be coded with reason End Override (EOV) to ensure the child is assessed correctly
    • still not in the customer's principle care, code 'OTN' on the CHOC screen if an OTN line is not already current, so that the child is included when working out the customer's additional income free area
  • on the customer's record only, update the Care Alignment Details page for the child to 'Finalised'
  • check/ update Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM) screen if necessary
  • check/ update Child Student/Income Circumstances (CHSI) screen if necessary
  • finalise the update and record the details in a DOC

Note: CHC, FSC and SSC coding should ripple to the other carer, but will not update the rate of FTB until a care assessment is completed for the other carer. However, CHOC and PCCD coding will not ripple to the other carer. If the other carer becomes the principal carer due to the decrease in care, code a CHOC line, as outlined above, ensure SSC is correct, and for JSP, YA (job seeker) and SpB customers code PCCD (NSS system) on the other carer's record for the child/ren if not already updated.

Go to Step 4.

4

Advise all carers of decision + Read more ...

If a principal carer determination has been made, advise all carers of the decision in writing. Use the Child - Change in Care Arrangements script to advise each carer. Consider relevant privacy issues when referring to the other carer in the letter.

Any disclosure of personal/protected information must comply with the confidentiality provisions in sections 201-208 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 and Privacy Act 1988. The References page contains links to the legislation. Where a Service Officer feels it necessary under these provisions, the name of the other carer should be replaced with the term 'other carer'. The reasons for this should be documented.

Storing evidence

Record the Customer Reference Number (CRN) of the person providing the information on each page of any documentation.

Store a copy of all physical documents used to make the decision on each carer's file.
