Send back with standard letter

Send back with standard letter

If exemption information is not supplied:

  • send the form back to the medical practitioner with a standard letter
  • record a comment on AIR Mainframe and PaNDA:
    New MEDE received on (PaNDA lodgement date) from provider (provider number), form incomplete, form and letter sent back to provider (reason as to why letter has been sent)
  • finalise the document as per WLM processing guidelines

Provider number not given

If the provider is confirmed:

  • send the form back to the provider with a standard letter

If Service Officers are unable to contact the parent/guardian or the individual by phone to verify the details of the provider:

  • send parent/guardian or individual a standard letter
  • record a comment on AIR Mainframe and PaNDA:
    New MEDE received on (PaNDA lodgement date) from provider unknown, form incomplete, letter only sent back to parent, provider number missing from form
  • finalise the document as per WLM processing guidelines

  • Record a comment on AIR Mainframe and PaNDA:
    New MEDE received on (PaNDA lodgement date) from provider (provider number), form incomplete, form and letter sent back to provider, medical practitioner signature missing from form

  • Record a comment on AIR Mainframe and PaNDA:

Send back with standard letter

Send back with standard letter

  • Send the document back to the:

    • parent/guardian or individual with a standard letter
  • Record a comment on AIR Mainframe and PaNDA:

Any state/territory based medical contraindication form

Contact parent/guardian/individual by phone and send form back with standard letter

Some state and territory governments have designed their own medical contraindication form. This is not a Services Australia form.

Service Officers must tell customers that Services Australia cannot use the state and territory COVID-19 vaccine medical contraindication forms to record any details on the AIR.

  • Send parent/guardian or individual a standard letter
  • Record a comment on AIR Mainframe and PaNDA:
    State/territory form received on (PaNDA lodgement date). Letter only sent back to individual
  • Finalise the document as per WLM processing guidelines

Incomplete or invalid IM011 forms

Assess form

Do not process an IM011 form if the provider has:

  • altered or made notations on the form
  • not ticked any of the boxes or entered any vaccine brands in the 'Other - specify' field in question 7
  • not ticked temporary or permanent in question 6, even if all other details are provided in questions 6 and 7
  • added a lab test date in question 8 but no antigen details
  • If the provider has ticked either:
    • significant immunocompromised of short duration (live attenuated vaccines only) or
    • the individual is pregnant (live attenuated vaccines only) and
    • selected other vaccines listed under the Non-live heading

Service Officers must:

  • make one attempt to contact the provider. If contact is not successful:
    • send the letter Z2334 - We need more information to process your form, and
    • select the option the form has been altered and not completed correctly
  • record a comment on AIR Mainframe and PaNDA not accept verbal updates from providers to process medical exemptions

IM011 forms with end dated speciality codes (including suspended provider)

Send back with standard letter

Service Officers should:

  • review the form, see Immunisation medical exemptions in AIR
  • add comments on Mainframe and PaNDA
  • send the following letters to the:
    • provider, Z2491- Immunisation medical exemption cannot be recorded (provider not eligible)
    • customer, Z2490 - Immunisation medical exemption cannot be recorded (provider not eligible)
  • upload copies of the letters to the PaNDA work item
  • complete the PaNDA work item

Phone enquiries

Service Officers who receive calls from a provider or customer:

  • can tell the customer:
    • the provider is currently under investigation by the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA) and their provider number has been suspended
    • the exemption from the suspended provider cannot be recorded on the AIR
    • they need to get a new immunisation medical exemption from another vaccination provider
  • must make a note on Mainframe and include:
    • the date
    • unable to process MEDE due to suspended provider, and/or
    • advised individual exemption not recorded due to provider suspension

Legislation provisions for different immunisation exemptions for the AIR

Table 5

Type of exemption

Legislation provision

Vaccination objection (formerly conscientious objection)

There is no longer provision for vaccination objection (formerly conscientious objection) to immunisation in the Act. From 1 January 2016, conscientious objections were renamed vaccination objections and are no longer a valid immunisation exemption.

Medical contraindication

An individual may have a contraindication recorded on the AIR if they are “… assessed by a general practitioner, a paediatrician, a public health physician, an infectious diseases physician or a clinical immunologist as having a medical contraindication to a vaccine, and as a result should not receive a vaccination”.

Natural immunity

An individual may have a natural immunity recorded on the AIR if they are “… assessed by a general practitioner, a paediatrician, a public health physician, an infectious diseases physician or a clinical immunologist as not requiring a vaccination because the individual has contracted a disease or diseases, and as a result has developed a natural immunity”.
