2

Genuine contact + Read more ...

The customer has received a phone call, SMS or email from Services Australia that they think is a scam, but it appears to be genuine.

Check Services Australia records to confirm if the contact was genuine and if the customer is the intended recipient.

Check for the genuine notifications via:

  • Document List (DL) screen - DOC
  • History Summary (HS) screen - letter
  • Communication History - via the Interaction History
  • Outbound Correspondence tool
  • Viewing or reissuing a letter or electronic message
  • Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging. See Table 3 on the Electronic Messaging (EM) tab
  • Self-service registration notification, see Item 10 in Table 2 of Troubleshooting Centrelink self service
  • Troubleshooting myGov

The agency regularly sends ad-hoc or pilot notifications by SMS in bulk that are not recorded in a customer's program record. Check for a supporting Operational Message for an outbound notification strategy via Operational Blueprint messaging by searching for the topic or exact content of the notification, see Operational Blueprint Home - Search function - Messaging.

Other common genuine enquires can include:

  • Market research, see Current market research - Intranet
  • Secure email service, see Cyber Security - Frequently Asked Questions > PGP
  • Employment Service Provider notification. Workforce Australia notifications are not sent by Services Australia, refer the customer to their provider or to the Workforce Australia website for information on how Workforce Australia communicates with individuals

Was the contact genuine?

  • Yes:
    • the contact is genuine and the customer is the intended recipient. Procedure ends here
    • the contact is genuine, but the customer is not the intended recipient, procedure ends here
    • the contact is genuine, but unauthorised or suspicious activity has been identified on the customers record, see Table 2
    • if unauthorised or suspicious activity has been identified and there is evidence of a Payment Redirection, go to Item 5
  • No, the contact was not genuine and could be a scam, go to Item 3
  • Unknown, if there is still uncertainty or the notification appears genuine but cannot be located in any reference material, seek assistance from a Technical Support Officer (TSO). See Table 4

3

Scam impersonating Services Australia Brand + Read more ...

The Services Australia brand is often impersonated by scammers who use a range of tactics to get what they want, which may be money, personal information or login information. Scammers may contact over the telephone and disguise (spoof) their number/s, and use automated calling techniques or recorded messages. The Resources page contains a link to the Brand intranet page.

Scammers will often develop fake websites that look genuine, encouraging customers to click on a link from an email or SMS, which will redirect the customer to the fake website.

Check to see if the Helpdesk is already aware of the scam, see:

  • Phishing scam records - Helpdesk Resources
  • Scams Awareness Hub - Intranet
  • Scamwatch

If the helpdesk requires further details about the scam:

  • Ask the customer to forward the phishing email or a screen shot of the phishing SMS, social media post to the Helpdesk email address
  • Acknowledge receipt and move the email to the 'Scams' folder
  • The Identity Security, Scams and Response Team will complete further analysis and update support material (if required). Note: If the scam is via phone, take note of the scammers methodology e.g. are they using a robo dialler and what does this message say, and include this information in the email to Identity Security, Scams and Response Team

Has the customer clicked on a hyperlink and/or provided personal information to the scammer?

  • Yes, the customer has clicked on a hyperlink and/or disclosed their personal information, go to Item 4
  • No, tell the customer the following (as appropriate):
    • the contact was a scam and they are unlikely to be at risk
    • scams target everyone. Scammers use stealth, surprise, intimidation, and clever tactics to get what they want
    • scammers use technology to disguise their phone numbers and email address to make the scam more believable
    • scammers often pretend to be from trusted organisations, including Services Australia
    • recommend the customer visit the Scamwatch website to report the scam and read the scam educational resources available. This includes visiting the 'Get Help' section of their website
    • if the customer received a scam telephone call, tell them to report the incident to their telecommunication provider
    • Procedure ends here

4

Customer has reported their personal information has been lost or stolen (including inadvertent disclosure to a scammer) + Read more ...

If the customer has clicked on a static (typically redirecting) hyperlink, but did not provide any personal information, they are unlikely to be at risk. However, some scam campaigns are designed to target specific mobile devices and clicking on the hyperlink may result in malicious software being loaded on the device. Although this is less likely, as a precaution, the customer should be encouraged to avoid using the device until it is deemed safe.

Discuss what personal information may be at risk and how this has occurred e.g. lost/stolen wallet, mail theft, remote access, sign-on credentials and/or personal information provided on a fake website. If the customer has disclosed their personal information, or they think their device has malware related to:

  • Services Australia, see Table 2
  • All other personal information, see Table 3
  • Electronic device security, see Table 3

5

Customer is concerned about unauthorised activity - Centrelink record + Read more ...

Check the customer's Centrelink records to determine if there is any sign of unauthorised activity, including access, changes, payments and claims.

Check for the following:

  • Update to payment destination that the customer did not authorise
  • A payment (such as Immediate, Crisis, Advance, AGDRP payments) has been applied for that the customer did not authorise
  • A claim has been submitted that the customer did not authorise
  • Updates to the record (such as address, contact number, email etc.) that the customer did authorise

Did any of the above occur?

  • Yes, follow the process as outlined in Identity fraud. Offer the customer additional record security, see Table 2
  • No, if appropriate, refer the customer to their payment line to discuss further. Procedure ends here

6

Customer is concerned about unauthorised activity - Medicare record + Read more ...

Check the customer's Medicare records to determine if there is any sign of unauthorised activity, including access, changes, payments and claims.

If the customer reports their Medicare claims or claims history statement has incorrect or missing information only (no other unauthorised changes on the record or evidence of suspicious activity), refer the customer to the Medicare Program. See Medicare patient claims history or claims history statement has incorrect or missing information.

Check for the following:

  • Updates to the Medicare record that the customer did not request (such as address, contact number, email, payment destination)

Did the above occur?

  • Yes:
    • Update Medicare records with the customer. See Amend customer details in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) for Medicare
    • Complete a Report Suspected Fraud
    • Offer the customer additional record security, see Table 2
  • No, if appropriate refer the customer to the relevant Medicare Program to discuss further. Procedure ends here

7

Customer is concerned about unauthorised activity - myGov record + Read more ...

Check the customer's myGov records to determine if there is any sign of unauthorised activity.

Tell the customer to check their Account history via the Account settings in their myGov account. If possible, they should do this while on the call. See Using myGov accounts. If the customer is having trouble logging in, see Table 1 in Troubleshooting myGov.

Check their history of access using the myGov Hub Support Tool. Confirm account information and verify the details in 'Summary', including the mobile number, email address, linked Member Services, sign in option, most recent login activity.

Check for the following:

  • Received an unrequested SMS security code, triggered from their myGov account
  • Unable to sign-in and have not made password or other changes
  • Suspicious activity/changes without consent
  • Member service tile is inactive when clicked
  • Another Member Service advises the customer of unauthorised linking activity

Did any of the above occur?

  • Yes, if unauthorised access or changes are identified (successful/not successful), see Table 2
  • No, if there is misunderstanding by:
    • the customer, provide assurance and education. Procedure ends here
    • a Service Officer resulting in an inappropriate referral, provide assurance and education to the customer. Escalate the misunderstanding to a TSO, see Table 4. Procedure ends here
  • Unknown, if there is still uncertainty, contact a TSO for advice, see Table 4

Other customer enquiries + Read more ...

Out of scope

For example, if the customer's enquiry relates to:

  • Lost money only
  • Online shopping, goods paid for but not received
  • Dissatisfaction with a goods or service perceived to be 'scam behaviour'
  • Checking the validity of a business or organisation
  • Fake websites and/or fake profiles (that don't use Services Australia Brand)
  • Social media interactions

Tell the customer:

  • The Scams and Identity Theft Helpdesk cannot assist. They provide advice and support for people who are affected by a scam or identity crime incident that relates to our payments and services. This includes Medicare, Centrelink, Child Support or myGov
  • There are organisations that provide support services that could help them. The Resources page has links to these organisations

Incorrect/inappropriate Helpdesk contact

If the customer's enquiry relates to:

  • their payment and they have called the incorrect phone line. See Transferring Calls. Procedure ends here
  • reporting fraud instead of a scam or identity theft incident. See Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption. Procedure ends here

Unable to determine reason for customer contact

  • Contact a TSO for advice, see Table 4

Actioning an email + Read more ...

A customer may contact the Scams and Identity Theft Helpdesk by email to:

  • to report a scam
  • seek clarification about a genuine notification
  • request help

A staff member may also send an email to the Helpdesk following contact from a customer after calling their payment line.

Email from a customer

  • Determine the nature of the enquiry
  • If appropriate, create a case record and complete all fields with details available at the time
  • Reply to (or forward if the email includes attachments):
    • To = customer's email address
    • Body = Use one of the endorsed email reply templates
  • Delete the original email and leave the reply in the sent items. Procedure ends here

Email from a staff member (requesting customer contact)

  • If appropriate, create a case record and complete all fields with details available at the time
  • Upload the email to the Case Record, go to Item 11

Email from a staff member (out of scope or incorrect referral)

  • Reply to (or forward if the email includes attachments):
    • To = staff member
    • CC = staff members team leader
    • Body = Advise why the referral is out of scope for the Helpdesk and request the Service Officer action as appropriate. If known, provide a suggestion of what team may be able to assist
  • Delete the original email and leave the reply in the sent items. Procedure ends here

Contact a TSO for advice if:

  • unable to determine what email reply is appropriate
  • further clarification is needed
  • the customer appears to be at risk

Centrelink + Read more ...

If enough personal information has been stolen/lost, the customer may be at risk of unauthorised activity on their Centrelink record. Discuss the following additional security:

  • Offer to add a verbal password, see Customer password on a Display on Access DOC
  • Offer to lock the Centrelink online service, see Access to Centrelink self service - set up, checking, locking and resetting

EXT Details: OLS LOCKED

'After speaking with the Scams and Identity Theft Helpdesk, the customer has requested their online service be locked: Unauthorised access - no consent. The customer has also requested that their online service remain locked unless they provide an approved photo identity document in person. When the customer requests the lock be removed, refer to OB 133-19061400 > Process > Resetting access'

Option 2 - The customer requests their online service can be unlocked by Smart Centre staff:

DOA Code is 'SEC' (ensure the DOA expiration date is removed)

EXT Details: OLS LOCKED

2

Medicare + Read more ...

If enough personal information has been stolen/lost, the customer may be at risk of unauthorised activity on their Medicare record. Discuss the following additional security:

  • Offer to add an MPI-2 restriction (flag)
  • Encourage the customer to request a replacement Medicare card via online services. If the customer does not use online services, a replacement card can be issued. See Medicare cards
  • Offer to lock their Medicare online service, see Access to Medicare self service - set up, checking, cancelling and enabling

Customers cannot request a new Medicare card number over the phone. They must complete the Application to copy or transfer from one Medicare card to another form (MS011). Refer the customer to the Services Australia website and search for 'copy/transfer'.

3

myGov sign in details + Read more ...

If the myGov user has disclosed or had their myGov sign in details stolen or compromised, they could be at risk of unauthorised access to their myGov account and any linked member services. The Helpdesk should:

  • Offer to revoke the myGov account. If the customer declines the offer, recommend the customer changes their myGov password, and if necessary, their Secret Questions and Answers, and/or their sign in options. Refer the customer to the myGov website Help page for more information (section 3. Manage your myGov account)
  • If Centrelink or Medicare Member Services were linked to the myGov account at the time of unauthorised or suspected unauthorised access, check records for unauthorised changes/updates
    • Centrelink, see Item 5 in Table 1
    • Medicare, see Item 6 in Table 1
  • Tell the customer to contact their other linked member services (outside of Services Australia) to identify suspicious or unauthorised access/changes and to discuss the security of those records. Procedure ends here.

3

IDCARE + Read more ...

Services Australia has a partnership arrangement with IDCARE, a not-for-profit organisation based in Queensland. IDCARE is staffed by specialist Identity Security Counsellors and Identity Security Analysts. They provide professional, free and anonymous support to members of the community who believe their personal information has been put at risk in any way (online or physical). IDCARE staff can provide advice on:

  • How to reduce identity misuse in the community
  • Engaging with Credit Reporting Agencies
  • Device security

The Scams and Identity Theft Helpdesk refers callers to IDCARE using a risk weighting system based on the type and amount of personal information that has been lost/stolen. They will also refer to IDCARE if emotional support is required irrespective of the risk weighting system.

When referring a customer to IDCARE, the Scams and Identity Theft Helpdesk staff will provide the Case ID to the customer. Customers need to quote or provide this Case ID during any interaction with IDCARE. This will help IDCARE identify that the referral was from Services Australia and the Scams and Identity Theft Helpdesk.
