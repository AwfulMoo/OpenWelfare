This page contains information about when the Income Test and Assets Test are applied and how to determine the correct rate of payment using the Income Test and Assets Test.

Application of Income and Assets Tests for blind customers

Determining the correct rate of payment using the Income and Assets Tests

Application of Income and Assets Tests for blind customers

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Circumstances when blind customers are affected by Income Test and Assets Test + Read more ...

Customers who are blind are not affected by the application of the Income Test and Assets Test for Age Pension and Disability Support Pension (DSP) rates of payment, unless:

  • they are claiming Rent Assistance (RA), or
  • their partner is on an income support payment and must disclose income and assets to calculate their partner's rate of payment

However, they can disclose their income and assets if they wish.

If the blind customer is:

  • claiming RA, go to Step 2
  • not claiming RA or RA paid with Family Tax Benefit and not disclosing income and assets, go to Step 3
  • not claiming RA or RA paid with Family Tax Benefit but wishes to disclose income and assets, go to Step 4

2

Claiming RA + Read more ...

If a blind customer wishes to test their eligibility for RA, their rate is calculated in accordance with the pension Income Test and Assets Test. The customer must provide full details of their income and assets to determine the rate payable. In order to be paid RA, a blind customer must have their income and assets coded on their record.

The rate of pension payable is the greater of:

  • the rate (including RA) that would be payable after applying both the Income Test and Assets Test as if the customer was not blind (if eligible, the transitional rules for pension customers may also apply), or
  • the rate calculated under their normal entitlement (free of the Income Test and Assets Test) if they were not claiming RA

Is the customer eligible for RA and would receive a higher rate of pension?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 3

3

No RA is being claimed with an income support payment or customer is not entitled to RA + Read more ...

The Income Test and Assets Tests are not applied to determine the customer's rate of payment.

The customer's rate of payment consists of:

  • the maximum basic pension rate
  • Pension Supplement. Note: a DSP customer under 21 years with no dependent children is not entitled to the Pension Supplement
  • Remote Area Allowance, if applicable

Record details on a DOC.

Is the partner receiving a payment that is subject to the Income Test and Assets Test?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, the customer is not required to declare their income and assets. The Blind - Income & Assets Exempt: field should be coded as 'Y' on the Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen to avoid the customer being selected for Pension Entitlement Review. Procedure ends here

4

Income and Asset details not required, but customer wishes to disclose + Read more ...

Is the partner receiving a payment that is subject to the Income Test and Assets Test?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Customers who voluntarily disclose income and assets may wish to receive and complete periodic income and asset reviews + Read more ...

Does customer wish to receive periodic income and asset reviews?

  • No, update income and asset details as disclosed. The customer is not required to declare their income and assets. Code the Blind - Income and Assets Exempt: field on the PDI screen with 'Y' to avoid the customer being selected for Pension Entitlement Review
  • Yes, update income and asset details as disclosed. The customer is not required to declare their income and assets. The Blind - Income and Assets Exempt: field on the PDI screen should be coded with 'N' as the customer wishes to be selected for Pension Entitlement Review

Record the details on a DOC. Procedure ends here.

6

Partner is receiving an income support payment + Read more ...

The customer's income and assets must be disclosed to determine the partner's entitlement.

If income and assets are not already known, issue Module IA - Additional Income and Assets (Mod IA). See the Resources page for a link to Mod IA.

When details are provided, the Blind - Income & Assets Exempt: field should be coded as 'N' on the PDI screen.

Until the income and assets details are known, the procedure ends here.

When income and assets details are known, determine the customer's entitlement. Go to Step 7.

7

Customer receiving income support payment and DVA + Read more ...

Has the customer been in receipt of one of the following payments on or prior to 20 March 1995 as well as being in receipt of Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) War Widows and Widowers Pension since 20 March 1995:

  • Age Pension
  • Carer Payment
  • Disability Support Pension, or
  • Wife Pension?
    • Yes, for single or partnered customers, see Calculating frozen rate pensions for Department of Veterans' Affairs War Widow(er) pensioners. Procedure ends here
    • No, go to Step 8

8

Income and assets details needed + Read more ...

The customer must provide details of their income, assets and rental payments otherwise RA cannot be calculated.

If income and assets details are not known, issue Module IA - Additional Income and Assets (Mod IA) to obtain the details. See the Resources page for a link to Mod IA.

When income and assets details are provided update the income and asset details. The Blind - Income & Assets Exempt: field on the PDI screen should be coded as 'N'.

Is the customer claiming RA?

  • Yes, RA is assessed under the pensions Income Test and Assets Test, see Step 1 in the Determining the correct rate of payment using the Income and Assets Tests table
  • No, procedure ends here

Determining the correct rate of payment using the Income and Assets Tests

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Any rate reduction due to the Income Test or Assets Test may have the effect of reducing the amount of RA payable + Read more ...

There are two rates of payment which need to be calculated:

  • Rate 1 = A rate of payment calculated using the customer's income, assets and rental details
  • Rate 2 = The customer's normal rate of payment without RA

2

Calculate Rate 1 + Read more ...

Determine a rate of payment with RA, if eligible, as though the customer were not blind using the customer's income and assets details and applying the relevant Income and Assets Tests.

From 20 September 2009 employment income is assessed in the fortnight of receipt. A pension customer is no longer able to average their employment income over a longer period of time.

Customer or partner who has employment income is:

  • Age Pension age, if income and assets are disclosed and they are eligible, apply the Work Bonus before applying any Working Credit entitlement for partner
  • under Age Pension age, they can accrue and deplete Working Credits if their income details are provided for the purpose of paying RA or to establish their partner's entitlement. Apply Working Credit on the customer's total ordinary income before the Income Test is applied but after any Work Bonus entitlement for the partner

Single customers

  • Income Test procedures:
    • Income Test for single pension customers, or
    • Income Test for Disability Support Pension customer who is under 21 years, with no dependent children and with affecting income at 19 September 2009, or
    • Income Test for Disability Support Pension customer who is under 21 years with no dependent children
  • Assets Test - Assets Test for single pension customers

Partnered customers

  • Income Test procedures:
    • Income Test for partnered pension customers, or
    • Income Test for illness separated or partner in prison customers, or
    • Income test for pension customers with partners on allowance
  • Assets Test - Assets Test for partnered pension customers

When the rate calculations are completed, go to Step 3.

3

Determining customer's entitlement under Rate 1 + Read more ...

  • Part 1 - first look at the customer's entitlement under the current Income Test and the current Assets Test. The customer's entitlement under the current Means Test will be the lower of these two rate calculations
  • Part 2 - if the customer is entitled to the transitional rules for pension customers, additional rate calculations are required using the transitional rules. Note: if unsure, see the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) screen. For assistance in the rate calculation, see Transitional rules for pension customers who were on payment at 19 September 2009. The transitional rate is compared to the rate payable under the current Means Test and the greater of the two applies

4

Calculate Rate 2 + Read more ...

Rate 2 is calculated free of the Income Test and Assets Test.

To calculate Rate 2, add together all of the following:

  • the maximum basic pension rate
  • Pension Supplement. Note: DSP customers under 21 years with no dependent children are not entitled to the Pension Supplement
  • Remote Area Allowance, if applicable

5

Compare the amounts calculated from Rates 1 and 2 + Read more ...

The greater of the Rate 1 or Rate 2 is the amount payable to the customer.

Record details on a DOC.

6

Reporting requirements + Read more ...

All blind customers are notification reporters unless:

  • their partner is profiled as a two weekly reporter and the customer has variable income, or
  • the customer is subject to the income or Assets Tests and they have variable income
  • see Reporting Overview

However the customer must be manually placed onto two weekly reporting on the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen. See Reporting screens

The customer can receive the Reporting Statement in an alternative letter format.

The customer and/or their partner are able to report their income through the different reporting channels.

For more information, see Partner's effect on reporting requirements.
