1

Workload Management (WLM) + Read more ...

A work item is what a staff member receives when WLM allocates work. Work items contain information about action required on a customer’s record.

For example:

  • a claim for an income support payment or concession card
  • PBS patient refund
  • a new customer registration or application for child support assessment

WLM allocates work items to staff based on:

  • Capability: skills and proficiency level (identified and recorded in ESSentials using skill tags) and the staff member’s level of delegation
  • Availability: WLM recognises availability when a staff member logs in to Customer First, Work Optimiser or Process Direct and selects Ready. Note: a Quick Ready button is also available in Work Optimiser
  • Priority: staff will be allocated work items with the highest priority they have the capability to action

There are 4 platforms for staff to receive and manage their work using WLM. For details on receiving and managing work in:

  • Customer First - see Customer First navigation, common screens and functions
  • Work Optimiser - see Work Optimiser for staff
  • Process Direct - see Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions
  • Pluto - see Manage interactions in Pluto

2

Work item creation and allocation + Read more ...

A work item creates when WLM receives information from the source system that action needs to be taken on a customer’s record. For example, when:

  • A customer lodges an online claim
  • A document is scanned to their record, or
  • A review triggers after recording a change to a customer’s circumstances

WLM uses the information from the source system to determine what kind of work it is (the work type). This is known as classification.

Each work type has WLM business rules set by the business owner. These rules are applied to each work item of the same type. The rules determine how and when the work item is allocated.

WLM business rules have 3 key components:

  • Priority of the work (when it should be allocated)
  • Capability needed to complete the work (skill, proficiency and delegation)
  • Attributes that can be applied to modify the work item, e.g. keywords

Some common examples of business rules that affect how WLM allocates work to staff:

  • Bundling: related work items may be allocated together as a 'Bundle'. A bundle may include work items a staff member can complete but does not have the skill tag for. Bundling helps staff ensure customers are not affected by unnecessary delays in processing
  • Daily limits recognise the complexity of some work items and the need to limit the number allocated to a staff member over a set time

Time zone sensitive: prevents the work item allocating outside set hours for the customer’s local time zone. Note: staff may receive work outside the approved times for contacting a customer by phone. See Calling a customer or returning a customer’s call.

To receive a new work item, select Ready from the top right of the:

  • Work Optimiser Worklist tile
  • Customer First Work Management Inbox
  • WLM Worklist screen when using the Process Direct Inbox
  • Pluto Workload Management Inbox

To receive a new work item in Work Optimiser, select Ready or Quick Ready from the bottom right of the Worklist screen.

Note: WLM allocates a work item each time Ready (or Quick Ready) is selected. Any staff unsure about when or how often to receive new work should seek technical support.

3

Capability and proficiency + Read more ...

Each work type in WLM has capabilities assigned to it. These include skills, proficiency and delegation.

Staff only receive work items if their capabilities in ESSentials match those needed to action the work item, unless they:

  • Have a Temporary Work Group applied, or
  • Are allocated a work item by another staff member
  • Have a Relationship Profile in place

Depending on staff member capabilities and business area needs, each work item will have 1 of these competency/proficiency levels recorded:

  • Entry
  • Established, or
  • Experienced

4

Work item attributes + Read more ...

Attributes are a way of exception processing. They can be used to change the:

  • capability required to action a work item
  • priority of a work item, or
  • queue of skilled staff the work item is allocated to

Attributes are often used in processes that need triage for categorisation, or that need to be varied in certain circumstances, e.g. remote processing.

Different types of attributes can be applied, including keywords, source code and status. Multiple attributes can be applied to a single work type.

Note: if staff add attributes to a work item, the attribute must match both the text and the case used in the WLM rules.

5

Work item status + Read more ...

The status reflects the progress towards completion of the related activity on the customer’s record. WLM uses the work item status to determine if it needs allocating to a staff member.

Once a related activity on the customer’s record is held or completed, information transfers from the source system to the work item. The status updates. To allow enough time for the information to transfer, WLM will not allocate any work item that has been allocated in the last 30-60 minutes.

See the Resources page for a description of all work item statuses.

6

Unassign and Reassign work items + Read more ...

When a staff member is allocated a work item, WLM records them as the Employee Responsible.

If a staff member cannot complete the work item, they can:

  • Reassign: assigns the work item to a specific staff member
  • Unassign: returns the work item to the national allocation pool

Record an appropriate reason when unassigning or reassigning a work item. See the Resources page for all reasons and when they apply.

Business owners may choose to restrict the ability to unassign or reassign work items for certain reasons. For example, Capability Adjusted or Proficiency Raised. If staff members believe these work items should be reallocated for a restricted reason, they should seek advice via their usual technical escalation process.

Unless an existing business process is in place, staff should always advise the recipient when reassigning work, and include:

  • work item details
  • a brief description of the issue and/or reason for reassigning the work
  • any recommended action

For information about unassigning or reassigning work in:

  • Customer First, see Accessing and personalising Customer First
  • Work Optimiser or Process Direct, see Work Optimiser for staff

7

Recategorisation + Read more ...

If an allocated work item has been classified incorrectly, it can be recategorised to the correct work type. For example, a Rent Certificate that is actually a Medical Certificate.

Recategorisation should only occur if initial classification was incorrect and the staff member cannot complete the work item.

If staff receive an incorrectly classified work item that:

  • They can complete (regardless of their skill tags), it is expected they complete the work
  • Is outside their capability, it should be recategorised

Centrelink only: if a digital image work item has been incorrectly categorised, use Document Tools to update the form type and recategorise the work item.

For information about recategorising work in:

  • Customer First, see Accessing and personalising Customer First
  • Work Optimiser or Process Direct, see Work Optimiser for staff

8

Temporary Work Groups + Read more ...

These can be created to define particular work types for allocation to specific staff. Work is sourced from the national allocation pool. Temporary Work Groups can be created by Capability or Work Type.

  • Work types recorded in the Temporary Work Group will allocate to staff in the group before any other work they are skilled to receive
  • Capability based Temporary Work Groups will not override skill tags in ESSentials. Capabilities coded as part of the Temporary Work Group are temporarily added to the staff member’s existing skill tags coded in ESSentials. This means that when a staff member in that group selects Ready or Quick Ready, WLM will allocate the highest priority work item matching any of the capabilities, not just those coded in the Temporary Work Group

These groups are generally created and managed by Programme and Operations Management business teams for approved WLM strategies.

Staff (and their Team Leaders) can access Staff Profile in Work Optimiser to see if they have been included in any Temporary Work Groups.

9

Conflicts of interest - Centrelink and Child Support only + Read more ...

If a staff member is allocated work for a customer they know personally, e.g. family member, friend, personal acquaintance or neighbour:

  • They can unassign or reassign the work item using the reason Conflict of Interest
  • This creates a Conflict of Interest relationship between the staff member and the customer. This displays in Staff Profile under Actor Relationships
  • No further work for this customer will be allocated to the staff member

Staff can view these relationships in their Staff Profile in Work Optimiser.

If appropriate, Team Leaders can remove a Conflict of Interest relationship from staff they line manage.

Note: recording a Conflict of Interest for WLM does not record inadvertent access to a customer’s information.

10

Restricted Access work items + Read more ...

WLM allocates Restricted Access work items to staff assigned the relevant skill tag.

Due to the sensitivity of the record, if the staff member does not have authorisation to access the customer's record, details of the customer do not display.

A staff member may be allocated a work item for a Restricted Access (RA) customer whose record they are not authorised to access. They will need to email the National Restricted Access Team.

Restricted Access relationships between a staff member and a CRN can be viewed via the Staff Profile tile under the Customer Relationships table.

11

Delegation - Medicare only + Read more ...

Work Optimiser - Medicare facilitates a delegated approval process.

This process enables work items to be sent to the appropriate person for approval, when required.

The delegated approval process is only applied to a subset of work types.
