Initiation of COR interventions

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Locate the intervention in the Integrated Review System (IRS) + Read more ...

To locate the intervention, use the National Search (IRNS) or Review Enquiry (IRRE) screen and code the following fields:

  • Review Type field: 'COR'
  • Review Reason field:
    • 'PRI' - Customer in Prison
    • 'PRM' - Customer in Prison (manual)
    • 'MPR' - Partner Review (manual)

New interventions will have status of REG (Registered). Interventions in progress will have a status of STA (Started).

Examining the matched data

For information on the compliance trigger, view the Match Data (IRMD) screen in IRS.

Information on the IRMD for COR match data includes:

  • prisoner identity details
  • prisoner identification number - also known as a MIN number
  • location
  • status
  • imprisonment start date
  • release date, and
  • alias details

Compare the match data

Compliance Officers (CO) need to Analyse the match data and the information on customer's record to decide if the intervention should be initiated.

If the customer's payment is cancelled and they were incarcerated after their last date paid to (DPT), cancel the intervention.

For more information, see:

  • Prison admission process and claims during incarceration
  • Temporary Cessation of Care (respite) provisions for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA)
  • Assessment of circumstances for customers with partner in prison

Is incarceration after the customer's DPT?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Anomaly and mismatches - contacting Department of Correctional Services (DCS) + Read more ...

Compare the DCS data with Services Australia customer data to ensure a correct match.

Note: discuss anomalies and mismatches with Quality Development Officer/Team Leader before taking any action.

Only contact DCS if needing to verify discrepancies, such as:

  • missing or invalid dates in the data that need investigating
  • the customer disputes imprisonment or the dates of imprisonment
  • identity checking is needed, e.g. date of birth, signature sample, photographs

See the Resources page for a link to Liaison Officer Contacts for Prisons and Juvenile Justice Centres.

Is the match correct?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 6

3

Other compliance intervention in progress + Read more ...

Check IRS to see if there is another intervention STArted.

Contact the CO responsible for the other intervention before deciding to continue or cancel the intervention.

Is there another intervention in progress and the COR intervention is to be cancelled?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Prison Liaison Officer (PLO) contact + Read more ...

Check whether PLO staff have already actioned the prison admission/release:

  • Check the customer record on the Document List (DL) screen for any information
  • If the customer is already being assessed by the PLO and payment is current, contact the PLO by phone to make sure the customer is entitled to ongoing payment
  • Clearly document on the record this contact, the outcome, and the decision to continue or cancel the intervention

Have PLO staff already actioned the prison admission/release?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Initiate intervention + Read more ...

Use the Corrective Services script to process all COR interventions.

Check IRMD for all names provided by Department of Corrective Services.

If there are any names/aliases in the match data not recorded on the customer's Centrelink record, they must be updated to the Customer Personal Details Summary (CPDS) screen. Correctional Services departments are a trusted third party data source.

For how to code the name(s), see Aliases and other names (legal, preferred, sex and gender diverse customers, cultural considerations etc.)

If the customer has a partner, the Corrective Services script will also initiate the partner intervention, send a letter and place the intervention on hold. The script will also add a manual partner intervention if required.

To continue the intervention, see Table 2.

6

Cancel intervention + Read more ...

Check IRMD for all names provided by the Department of Corrective Services.

If there are any names/aliases in the match data not recorded in the customer's Centrelink record, they must be updated to CPDS. Correctional Services departments are a trusted third party data source.

For how to code the name(s), see Aliases and other names (legal, preferred, sex and gender diverse customers, cultural considerations etc.)

If the decision is to cancel the intervention after analysis of the customer's record. use the COR script to cancel the COR intervention using codes:

  • IAP - Information Already Provided
  • NVM - Not valid Match
  • RRV - Recently Reviewed

Record the information in the intervention DOC.

Temporary debt write offs for incarcerated customers

Check the Debt List (OPDL) screen to see if a current debt exists. Customers currently incarcerated are to have all debts temporarily written off. See Temporary write-off of Centrelink debts.

For more information on the temporary write-off of the debt, see:

  • General Centrelink debt recovery information
  • Cancelling a Service Update or Review in the Integrated Review System (IRS)

The Resources page contains a link to the DOC templates.

Procedure ends here.

Assessing and finalising COR interventions

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Customer current on income support payment + Read more ...

If the customer is current, ensure the intervention is started before any updates to the record, Changes made when the intervention is not STA cannot be attributed and claimed in IRS.

Note: current customers include customers with suspended payments.

Selecting the IRS intervention

Before any updates on the customer's record, make sure the IRS intervention has a status of STA (started) and be in the Review Image.

Is the customer's payment current?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Suspend or cancel payment + Read more ...

Use the Corrective Services script for processing all COR interventions.

  • Change the customer's postal address to the prison address
  • Suspend or cancel the customer's payment as appropriate. Assessing a member of a couple for a customer with a partner in prison
  • Check the Debt List (OPDL) screen to see if a current debt exists
  • Customers currently incarcerated are to have all debts temporarily written off

Outstanding Job seeker participation suspension

If a job seeker participation failure prevents suspending or cancelling the payment IMP, contact the local Participation Solutions Team (PST) to have the record updated correctly. See Outstanding compliance action for imprisoned job seekers.

Mobility Allowance (MOB)

If the customer gets MOB, cancel the payment IMP via the Benefit Action (BA) screen. IMP is not a valid suspension code for MOB.

Date of release is unclear

If the date of release is unclear, in all cases treat the customer as though the incarceration period is less than 13 weeks and suspend payment if possible.

Youth Allowance (job seeker) turning 22

Cancel YA if the date of imprisonment is within 13 weeks of the customer turning 22. If only suspended, the system will transfer them to JobSeeker Payment.

Q134 MFU activities

Action MFU activities that need a manual Q134 letter to be sent to the customer after suspension. This is because the customer will not get an automatic letter advising of the decision and their review and appeal rights.

  • The text about why payments have been suspended should state:
    'people remanded in custody awaiting trial are not entitled to receive…'

Note: as customers in custody cannot be contacted by phone, there is no need to make genuine attempts to contact to discuss the adverse decision. Clearly DOC that the customer cannot be contacted by phone and proceed with the decision. The customer will receive a Q134 letter advising of the decision and their review and appeal rights.

3

Check if customer has a current carer linked + Read more ...

A check of the customer’s record is required to determine if there is a current carer linked to the customer’s record. The carer’s entitlement to Carer Payment and or Carer Allowance may be affected.

The benefit status line has codes that will identify if the customer has a linked carer. The codes will appear as:

  • CRP care receiver (Carer Payment), and/or
  • CRA care receiver (Carer Allowance)

To identify if there is a current linked carer, check the Links Summary (LS) screen to see if there is a CRP and/or CRA link with no end date recorded.

If a current link exists, the Virtual Carers nation team (CD1) need to be advised to make sure appropriate action is taken to reassess the carer’s entitlement to Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA). To access the care provider's record:

  • Key 'L' in the field next to the current care provider on the LS screen, or
  • Key the CRN of the current carer and complete the following actions:
    • on the carer's record, send a Fast Note to office code CD1 (Virtual Carers National)
      - use Fast Note - select workflow use Carers > SUS/CAN/RES > CP/CA Care Receiver in custody
    • on the care receivers record, go to the Review Summary (IRRS) screen:
      - key 'M' in the field next to the COR (Correctives) Intervention
      - on the Manage Review (IRMR) add the keyword RCVRPRI

Once the Fast Note is completed in the carer's record and the IRMR screen has been updated in the care receiver's record, go to Step 4.

4

Determine if debt exists + Read more ...

Does the COR update result in a debt for the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Raising debts + Read more ...

Before raising debts due to reassessment of the customer's entitlements, allow 21 days for the customer to respond to the request to contact letter.

After 21 days have elapsed

Use the Debt Action script to raise a debt.

As the customer is currently incarcerated, the new debt must be temporarily written off. This can be done as part of raising the debt and by using the Debt Action script. See Temporary write off of Centrelink debts.

Take appropriate action before finalising the intervention.

For more information, see:

  • Debt investigation
  • General debt raising information
  • Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts

See the Resources page for help with tools for calculating debts.

Temporary debt write offs for incarcerated customers

Customers currently incarcerated must have all debts temporarily written off:

  • Go to the Overpayment List (OPDL) screen to view all the customer’s debts
  • Any outstanding debts that are not currently written off need to be actioned, whether or not they were raised by the Compliance Risk Branch (CRB)

It is recommended to use the following debt advice template when raising debts. The information in the debt advice can be changed to suit the customer.

Suggested debt advice text

'You were admitted to <correctional-facility> on <date>. You were not entitled to receive <payment-type> for the period <date> to <date>. This means you have, therefore, been overpaid $<amount>. We are required to recover this amount.'

Sending Account Payable letters contains debt reason codes, default text for the Account Payable (OPAOD) screen, and the rules for entering free text.

Go to Step 7.

6

Making an unfavourable decision + Read more ...

Follow the agency's phone contact requirements - introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose.

Follow the required action for making an unfavourable decision. This includes attempting to contact the customer.

Annotate the existing intervention DOC making sure to include all relevant information.

Note: if appropriate, discuss the recovery fee when discussing the unfavourable decision.

If the customer has further evidence related to the decision

  • See Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK),for required actions
  • Annotate the existing intervention DOC to include all relevant information
  • Place the intervention on hold (HLD) for the agreed time (preferably 7 days)
  • Do not issue Request for Information (RFI)/TF002 letters. These letters attract auto-suspension actions

Evidence provided or not provided by agreed date

See Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK) for required actions.

7

COR update does not result in a debt + Read more ...

Before finalising the intervention, allow 21 days for the customer to respond to the request to contact letter.

After 21 days have elapsed, go to Step 8.

8

Document the intervention + Read more ...

Record all information in the customer's intervention DOC to clearly explain:

  • customer's circumstances
  • customer conversations
  • information relating to the compliance trigger
  • decisions made
  • outcomes of the intervention

Run all compulsory scripts to ensure:

  • the DOC is updated appropriately, including the Extra Details line
  • all actions are recorded in IRS

When documenting the intervention, if a DOC has already been created to initiate contact, make sure to annotate the original intervention DOC.

Annotate the existing COR DOC with the agreed DOC standards when actioning the intervention.

See the Resources page for DOC templates.

Record all significant decisions in the Intervention DOC. Customer Compliance Division policy is that a separate DOC be created for all suspensions, cancellations and debts. The Debt script will create a separate DOC.

9

Finalise the intervention + Read more ...

Before finalising the intervention, ensure all actions required by the CO are completed.

Finalise the intervention using the Corrective Services script.

If the customer has a linked review or (ex) partner who also has an intervention, finalise all interventions at the same time.

Issuing a Q765 advice

A Q765 must be sent unless the intervention outcome:

  • has been advised over the phone
  • is related to a member of couple trigger and a decision letter has been issued
  • resulted in an adverse decision, e.g.
    • payment/card remains suspended/cancelled
    • payment was varied
    • debt advice has been issued

A Q765 can be issued if requested by the customer.

Referral to the Intelligence and Investigations Branch

Once an intervention is completed, the case can be referred to the Branch if the CO identifies non-compliance outside the Scope of Intervention.
