13 week review activities and initial contact

Table 1: for all Service Officers, this table outlines the 13 week review triggers and how to help customers complete the review.

Step

Action

1

Initiation + Read more ...

SpB reviews occur every 13 weeks, triggered by an activity on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen on the customer's record.

The customer needs to complete the review and provide supporting documents by their Eligibility End Date (EED) to avoid their payment cancelling.

Check the due date of the review on the FAL screen, or the EED on the Newstart Miscellaneous Details (NMIS) screen.

If the customer:

  • has a Centrelink online account, the system sends an email, SMS or letter advising them to complete the review online. Partnered customers need to have their partner's permission to enquire to access the review online
  • does not have a Centrelink online account, or partner permitted to enquire arrangements in place, the system issues a Special Benefit Review (BC048m) form for them to complete

Nominees can access the review online for the customer. Confirm via NOLS screen.

Check the EMOL screen to see if the customer has a Centrelink online account.

Check the History Summary (HS) screen to see if the customer was sent:

  • a message or letter to complete the review online (code NSSEEDO)
  • a review form (code NSSEED)

Record details of the customer or nominee contact on a DOC that meets minimum standards.

To help the customer or nominee:

  • submit the review online, go to Step 2
  • complete and lodge the review form, go to Step 3
  • if payments have stopped, go to Step 4

2

SpB review online + Read more ...

The customer or their nominee needs to log into their Centrelink online account through myGov and complete the review.

If the customer has a partner, and their partner has:

  • given permission for the customer to enquire, their partner's details are also requested in the online review
  • not given permission for the customer to enquire, the customer will not be able to access the review online. Go to Step 3

Due date

The online review must be completed by the customer's Eligibility End Date (EED) to avoid their payment cancelling. The reminder letter issues 21 days before the EED. Online customers get an extra reminder 7 days before the EED.

After submitting the review online, the customer will need to upload requested documents.

If the EED has passed, the customer will no longer have access to the review. They will need to complete the review form (BC048). Go to Step 3.

Procedure ends here.

3

Special Benefit Review (BC048m) paper form + Read more ...

To issue or reissue form

Print a copy of the review form from the HS screen or print a blank copy from the Services Australia website.

Customers can download the review form from the Services Australia website. Search for BC048. See the Resources page for link to the form.

If completing a blank form, they will need to fill in their name, address, Customer Reference Number (CRN), the date of issue and the return date on the first page of the form.

The customer needs to complete and return the form as instructed on the front page and provide any supporting documentation requested.

Due date

Customers need to complete and return the form with the required supporting documents by the due date on the form to avoid their payment cancelling.

Form lodgement

Make sure the customer has completed the form correctly and signed it.

If they have a partner, their details are also requested on the form and they must complete and sign the form as well.

Scan the review form and relevant documents for the SpB processing team.

If payment has stopped and urgent processing is needed, go to Step 4.

4

SpB suspended or cancelled + Read more ...

SpB payments may stop with benefit status:

  • EED (Eligibility End Date reached)
  • RNL (review not lodged)
  • DNR (did not lodge review form) or
  • LAP (lapsed)

Check Document List (DL) screen to see if any extra documents requested by the SpB Processing team have been provided.

After checking information and confirming all items have been provided, create an open work item using Fast Note:

  • Select Auto Text option > Special Benefit > Reassessment of Finalised Review > Special Benefit Review Reassess
  • Complete details in the template and select Confirm

Do not add any other keywords to this Fast Note as it will impact the urgent allocation of this work.

If the customer has not submitted their online review and SpB cancels, the review will no longer be available in their Centrelink online account:

  • Issue a BC048m form
  • The customer need to complete and return the review form with required supporting documents immediately

Record details of the contact on a DOC.

If payment is cancelled for more than 13 weeks and the review form and other requested documents have not been lodged, payment is not restorable. The customer will need to re-claim. See Restoration of JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (job seeker) and Special Benefit.

Procedure ends here.

Mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

  • SpB non-NVH mutual obligation requirements based on policy (not subject to compliance action)

(Fast Note > Auto text > Special Benefit > Coding of SpB Partner Death > SPL team to code SpB Partner Death). This will generate a work item for the SpB processing team to assess the bereavement exemption from mutual obligation or activity test requirement.

7

Eligibility End Date (EED) + Read more ...

If the customer is:

  • to maintain entitlement for the next 13 weeks, extend the EED by 13 weeks
  • likely to lose entitlement in the next 13 weeks
    • Extend the EED on the NMIS screen for the day they are residentially qualified for another payment, or the next payday + 14 days for the day before they lose eligibility
    • Do not extend the EED beyond this date if the visa end date is before the next 13 week EED
      Example: for a temporary visa holder e.g. (790, 449, 785 visa) whose visa end date is before the next 13 week EED, the EED on the NMIS screen must be Visa End Date minus one day
  • eligible for another payment now or by the next EED
    • Extend the EED on the NMIS screen for the day they are residentially qualified for another payment or the next payday + 14 days
    • Do not extend the EED beyond this date if the customer will meet 10 year residence
      Example: If the customer will meet the 10 year Australian residence for AGE or serve the NARWP for JSP, confirm this information on their record to make sure they meet the residence requirements or a qualifying residence exemption for a more appropriate income support payment. See Item 13 in Table 3
    • If the customer is subscribed, send a pre-call SMS before calling them
    • Advise the customer they must apply for another more appropriate payment and that their SpB will stop the day before they become eligible for another payment. Advise the customer they can make an early claim up to 13 weeks before they become eligible. Send a manual letter
    • If unable to contact the customer, issue a letter online (preferred) or by post to advise they must apply for a more appropriate payment and their SpB will stop the day before they become eligible for another payment
    • DOC the record to clearly explain the customer is no longer entitled to SpB and has been advised to make a claim for the appropriate payment
    • System will stop SpB when EED is reached
    • If the customer is considered vulnerable, run an Assisted Customer Claim to submit the appropriate payment claim where possible

9

Restoring SpB + Read more ...

  • Restore SpB on the Benefit Action (BA) screen:
    • Service Reason: key 'SPL'
    • Action: key 'RES'
    • Reason: leave blank
    • Effect Date: key date last paid + 1
    • Source: key 'BC48'
    • DOR: key date of receipt of BC048m review form
  • Update the Miscellaneous Update Details (NMIS) screen:
    • Event Date: record the current EED if the EED is in the past, or today's date if the current EED is in the future
    • Eligibility End Date: this date cannot be more than 13 weeks from the previous end date, or more than 15 weeks from today's date
  • Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen

Document the decision

Run the Fast Note workflow Auto text > Generic > SUS/CAN/RES > RESTORE and include:

  • Special Benefit restored under SS Admin Act Section 85
  • Reason for restoration (summarise on Sum: line)
  • DOE of restoration:
  • Date Last Paid To:
  • Is Customer owed any payment: YES/NO (incl. calculations)
  • Follow-up action is required: YES/NO
  • Customer has been advised/reminded of the general notification provisions

Missing payment?

If coding has resulted in a gap in SpB payments (missing payment), go to Step 10.

To complete the review, go to Step 11.

10

SpB coding for missing payments + Read more ...

If the NMIS update activity has been finalised and there is a gap in payments, reassess the record to issue the missing payments:

  • Find the EED from the previous NMIS update and write it down.
  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen and code an SPL Reassessment DOE: from the day after the previous EED
  • This should stimulate the 'missing' payment
  • Check and adjust the arrears if necessary
  • Finalise the activity

If there are missing payments,

  • Go to the Review of Assessment (ROA) screen
  • Reassess the record and apply the most appropriate date or EED in the Start date for the Review of Decision to make sure the arrears are correct

Check the arrears equals 14 days entitlement or the remaining entitlement up to the latest entitlement period (EPED).

Finalise review and record DOC + Read more ...

Complete all Special Benefit review, ACT/CALL DOCs and digital work items.

Record details on a DOC in line with minimum standards.

    • that the SpB review indicates continuing entitlement
    • 'customer is unable to receive any other income support payment, SS Act Sect 729 applies'
    • if SpB was restored, 'restored under SS Admin Act section 85'
    • any change in circumstances
    • any customer contact
    • the new EED
  • If payment is not to continue, include:
    • all changes
    • all appropriate cancellation reasons
    • any customer contact

Make sure a letter issues to the customer to advise the outcome,

Newly Arrived Resident’s Waiting Period (NARWP) and residence + Read more ...

  • have served or are due to have served the notional NARWP or 10 years qualifying Australian residence before the next EED

    • RSLEG screen
    • Immigration Movements (RSIM) screen
    • Australian Periods of Presence (RSPAP) screens, or
  • Check if they have qualifying residence for another income support payment

If the RSLEG screen has been updated with a new visa subclass, check if the new visa entitled the customer to another social security payment.

If the customer:

  • may be eligible for another payment, see Step 7 in Table 2
  • no longer qualifies for SpB, cancel the payment from the grant date of the new visa. Update the DOC with any action taken

SpB categories.

2

ACC - Australian Citizen Child or Australian permanent resident child of non-permanent resident adult + Read more ...

  • For a child:
    • at or below school age, check the custodial parent is in hardship and has no means to adequately support the child
    • over school age, to remain on payment the custodial parent must be deemed to be homeless or at risk of homelessness
  • Check the custodial parent's residential status for any changes that may mean they are entitled to claim an income support payment. For example, parental visa status has changed allowing the parent to claim SpB. Therefore, the child is no longer entitled and the parent should claim SpB
  • Has the parent been granted a visa that would qualify them for FTB or another payment?
    • Refresh the Immigration datalink on parent’s record
    • Check custodial parent’s record if permanent/provisional or any qualifying visa is granted or already current on income support payment
    • Contact the custodial parent to advise they should claim another payment (SpB and FTB). If unable to contact, issue the relevant letter. SpB for the child will be cancelled on the date of the visa change + 14 days. This gives a buffer to the customer due to potential hardship
    • Adjust the EED to the date cancelling + 14 days to ensure the ACC payment is cancelled
    • Upon lodgement of a claim, the custodial parent can ask to have their claim assessed urgently for SpB and FTB due to hardship
  • SpB cannot be paid if the custodial parent gets Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for the child on SpB (ACC category) and can earn a sufficient livelihood from the FTB payment and/or have available funds over $5,000. In these cases, cancel the payment as the customer is not considered to be in hardship
  • Make sure the Accommodation (AC) screen is coded correctly. See SpB for ACC and Australian permanent resident children
  • Check of there are changes to child support payments and code if appropriate
  • Code the custodial parent’s available funds on the Savings Summary (SVS) screen on the parent's record
  • Code the available funds as a family unit from all sources on the NSOD screen on the child's record
  • Check child’s Tax File Number or exemption if applicable
  • Check nominee is current
  • Check the Rate Component Override (RCO) screen to make sure the customer's rate is correct. Make sure all CPI rate increases have been applied if the customer is on a manual rate. Check the correct rate is applied to siblings on SpB
  • If the custodial parent gets Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS) payment, SpB should be paid at the equivalent FTB rate
  • Check for changes in child maintenance payments via the parent/ guardian's MNGS screen, and update on the Other Income Summary (OINS) screen
  • If the custodial parent is working, income above the maximum rate of JSP plus add-ons will reduce the rate of SpB. Make sure the amounts are coded correctly on the OIN screen

For help, see SpB for ACC and Australian permanent resident children.

CFC - caring for a child with medical condition + Read more ...

Check the customer has no other means of support,

Check continuous caring responsibility.

Check if the person being cared for does not have sufficient funds to support or partially support the carer.

5

CIP - caring responsibility, not qualified for CP or other income support + Read more ...

Check if the customer is:

  • continuously providing constant supervision in the carer’s home, and
  • prevented from working for this reason

Check that no alternative arrangements for care can be made.

Check they are not qualified for Carer Payment (CP) or any other income support.

6

Nominated Visa Holders + Read more ...

Check the status of the customer's current visa type on the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen.

Current Temporary Protection or Humanitarian Visas that should be paid under this category include the visa subclasses 070, 449, 785, 786 and 790.

Before 1 January 2003, Nominated Visa Holders were granted under the TPV category.

After 1 January 2003, customers on one of the nominated visa types should be granted under one of these categories:

  • TPA - Temporary Visa Holder not of workforce age
  • TPD - Temporary Visa Holder ineligible for DSP
  • TPP - Temporary Visa Holder from 1 January 2003, activity tested customer

These customers are subject to the Nominated Visa holder (NVH) mutual obligation requirements for SpB.

7

NMA - newly arrived residents of Age Pension age + Read more ...

The NRA category is more appropriate if the customer has, before their next EED:

  • served the NARWP, or
  • 10 years qualifying Australian residence

In this case, update the NSOD screen.

8

NMN - waiting period for JSP/YA + Read more ...

Check the customer has not served the NARWP and does not have a Qualifying Residence Exemption (QRE) for JSP or YA.

For customers with mutual obligation requirements, make sure the Payable under field on the NSOD screen is coded as JSP conditions. This includes principal carers with part-time mutual obligation requirements.

Principal carer status should be coded on the Principal Carer Circumstances Details (PCCD) screen.

9

NMP - waiting period for PP/WDA/CP + Read more ...

Check the customer has not served the NARWP and does not have a Qualifying Residence Exemption (QRE) for Parenting Payment (PP) or Carer Payment (CP).

Principal carer status should be coded on the Principal Carer Circumstances Details (PCCD) screen.

For principal carers whose youngest child is:

  • under 6 years, leave the NSOD screen blank so they are not subject to mutual obligation requirements. Check if the customer is eligible for a ‘lone parent’ exemption from the NARWP
  • over 6 years, code under NMN category

10

NRA- not residentially qualified for Age Pension + Read more ...

Check the customer is not residentially qualified for Age Pension.

The NRA category is more appropriate if the customer has, before their next EED:

  • served the NARWP, or
  • 10 years qualifying Australian residence

Generally, the long term available funds test should apply to this category. Check the NSOD screen and update if needed.

11

NRD - not residentially qualified for DSP + Read more ...

Check the customer has had a claim for DSP rejected due to residency and is not residentially qualified for DSP.

To qualify under this category the customer must have a capacity to work less than 15 hours per week.

For a customer whose medical condition is not manifest, a new Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) must be completed:

  • This must be coded on the customer's record every 2 years. Otherwise, payment will auto suspend FME until a new JCA is completed
  • Check the referral summary for a current JCA report. If the last JCA report was completed over 2 years ago, refer for a new JCA after obtaining a medical report

Generally, the long term available funds test should apply to this category. Check the NSOD screen and update if needed.

If the customer has a Temporary Protection Visa (695, 449, 447, 070, 785, 786 or 790) their SpB should be assessed under the TPD category (would qualify for DSP but does not meet residence requirements).

If the customer is manifest, check DSP grant coding is correct.

12

OLT - other long term cases + Read more ...

Only use this category if no other SpB category is appropriate

This category is typically used for visa subclass 060 holders.

Check the status of the customer's current visa type on the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen.

If the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) applies to the customer, consider updating NSOD to one of the following categories:

  • NMA (newly arrived resident of Age Pension age)
  • NMN (newly arrived resident waiting period for JSP/YA)
  • NMP (newly arrived resident waiting period for PA/PP/CP)

Full-time students can only be paid if they are under 18 years of age and undertaking an approved course.

Full-time students under 16 years of age should be coded under the USY category.

13

OST - short term cases + Read more ...

Only use this category if no other SpB category is appropriate and the person is likely to need income support for less than 13 weeks.

No review forms are issued for this category as payment is set under 13 weeks and stops when the date is reached. If the customer contacts and needs more support, they must be transferred to the relevant category.

If the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) applies to the customer, consider updating NSOD to one of the following categories:

  • NMA (newly arrived resident of Age Pension age)
  • NMN (newly arrived resident waiting period for JSP/YA)
  • NMP (newly arrived resident waiting period for PA/PP/ CP)

Re-check eligibility and transfer to a long-term category or re-grant.

14

PPV - partner provisional visa + Read more ...

The applicant must be the holder of a PPV visa - subclass 309 or 820 visas.

Check the status of the customer's current visa type on the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen. If a permanent resident visa has been granted, change the customer category on NSOD screen.

If the PPV has an end date on the RSLEG screen and the customer holds a bridging visa, they may lose their entitlement to SpB.

If the customer is a principal carer with a child under 6 years of age in their care, on the NSOD screen make sure the Payable Under field is blank so the customer is not subject to mutual obligation requirements.

15

SOM - socially marginalised, unable to meet mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

This category is rarely used. Escalate details to the Level 2 Policy Help Desk before proceeding.

Check the customer continuously unable to meet the mutual obligation and reporting requirements for JSP due to a medical condition

Check the customer is not eligible for Disability Support Pension (DSP), or because of their medical condition refused to claim DSP.

Check if other assistance is provided by external agencies.

If NARWP applies, consider assessment under other most appropriate category.

16

USY - under 16 years of age + Read more ...

Check the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen is coded for seamless transfer to Youth Allowance (YA) once the customer turns 16.

If the customer is studying, obtain/request study details. Update the Education Course History (EDCH) screen.

Check customer has not met minimum school leaving age to qualify for YA.

Review coding on the AC screen. If the customer is coded as NRP:

  • follow-up current accommodation circumstances and update as appropriate
  • NRP should only be coded for temporary circumstances in which the customer is currently homeless - couch surfing, sleeping rough
  • a social worker referral may be needed if safety concerns. See Risk identification and management of threats to the safety or welfare of a child

Accept financial details provided by the USY customer, even if no bank statements are provided. This is unless there is reason to believe the customer is not in financial hardship as they:

  • have available funds above $5,000, or
  • may be receiving financial support

Conduct file audit + Read more ...

Examine the customer's information, SpB category and situation over the past 12 months on payment.

This includes checking:

  • Paper SpB reviews completed within the last 12 months, including any scanned or uploaded documents

  • The customer’s living arrangements

Details to check for ACC customers or Australian permanent resident children + Read more ...

  • NOLS: payment and correspondence nominee
  • Check POI, TFN, ACS, RCO, OIN, SVS screens
  • SpB Rate/RCO screen:
    • Check number of SpB children in the family unit
    • Only or youngest child receives the JSP 'single with dependent child' rate plus any RA payable

    • If parent receives SRSS, FTB rate with no RA
  • Check if any CPI increase to update on RCO screen or MFU to be actioned
  • Accommodation
    • (Domestic violence, crisis accommodation) youngest child can receive Rent Assistance
    • Other SpB ACC will be coded as living with parents

  • Residency
    • Australian citizenship status confirmed via Immigration datalink
    • Birth Certificate showing resident parent’s name
    • Confirmed parent’s residency on RSLEG screen?
  • Check age of child/ren
    If school age, they should not remain on SpB as they are considered a full-time student. Each state and territory is different, but 6 years of age is a good indicator of school age in most states. If the child’s SpB payment is the family’s only source of income, and not getting this payment would place the family at risk of homelessness, do not cancel payment. Record details clearly in a DOC
  • Check parental income, including other means of support (e.g. Red Cross) See examples of calculations for ACC
  • Child Support
    • Is the customer/parents receiving child support?
    • Has the custodial parent taken child support action or do they have an exemption?
  • Has the parent been granted a visa that would qualify them for FTB or another payment?
    • Refresh the Immigration datalink on parent’s record
    • Check custodial parent’s record if permanent/provisional or any qualifying visa is granted or already current on income support payment
    • Contact the custodial parent to advise they should claim another payment (SpB and FTB). If unable to contact, issue the relevant letter. SpB for the child will be cancelled on the date of the visa change + 14 days. This gives a buffer to the customer due to potential hardship
    • Adjust the EED to the date cancelling + 14 days to ensure the ACC payment is cancelled
  • Non-protected SCV
    FTB is the most appropriate payment. SpB can only be granted if the customer/custodial parent is homeless or at risk of homeless

Details to check for all SpB customers + Read more ...

  • Contact updates: address, phone, email
  • Check and encourage the customer to register for self service. They will be able to view, update, upload documents, and complete reviews online
  • Refresh RSIMME screen to confirm visa status

    See Table 4

    For example, single parent for PP or JSP, family member of refugee/former refugee
  • If SpB is granted under substantial change, is that change still applicable?
    For example:
    • domestic violence victim reconciled with partner
    • customer/partner’s medical condition improved
    • partner released from prison
    • sponsor or assurer can provide support
  • If under AOS category can the assurer now provide support?
  • Customer and partner combined funds over $5,000
    See SpB available funds test
  • Mutual obligation (TPP/NMN and other categories paid under JSP conditions)
    • JSR/Active status?
    • Connected to Employment Services Provider?
    • Job Plan in place?

    • Make sure EDC and NES are coded for USY category customers
  • Mutual obligation exemption
    Check NSOD payable condition, AEX screen

AIR manual letter returned + Read more ...

If the information received needs clarifying, contact the customer and document the outcome.

If the customer and/or partner has more than $5,000 combined available funds, use discretion to assess any committed funds. See SpB available funds test. For examples of low and high risk customers, see the Resources page. If unsure, seek clarification from the Access Support Centre before cancelling SpB.
