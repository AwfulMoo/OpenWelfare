This page contains information about the leave impacts on the aged care subsidies and supplements and links to external websites.

Leave impacts on home care subsidies and supplements

Table 1

The amount of home care subsidies and supplements is dependent on the leave type and leave duration. For home care recipients, leave balances resets at 2 specific events:

  • on 1 July each year, or
  • when their package level changes at any time

    Leave type

    The payment impacts are

    Hospital

    • For 28 consecutive days for each hospital stay, the full basic subsidy including any supplements are paid
    • From day 29, the subsidy reduces. The subsidy:
      • is payable at 25% of the basic subsidy rate
      • does not include any primary supplements*
      • continues to include other supplements

    *Primary supplements are oxygen, enteral feeding, dementia and cognition, and veterans’. Other eligible supplements are the viability and hardship supplements.

    Transition care

    • For 28 consecutive days for each period of transition care, the full basic subsidy including any supplements are paid
    • From day 29, the subsidy is reduces. The subsidy:
      • is payable at 25% of the basic subsidy rate
      • does not include any primary supplements*
      • continues to include other supplements

    *Primary supplements are oxygen, enteral feeding, dementia and cognition, and veterans’. Other eligible supplements are the viability and hardship supplements.

    Residential respite leave (respite leave)

    • Care recipients are entitled to 28 days of residential respite leave in a financial year
    • The full basic subsidy including any supplements are paid during these 28 days
    • The leave days does not have to be taken in a row
    • From day 29, the subsidy is reduces. The subsidy:
      • is payable at 25% of the basic subsidy rate
      • does not include any primary supplements*
      • continues to include other supplements

    *Primary supplements are oxygen, enteral feeding, dementia and cognition, and veterans’. Other eligible supplements are the viability and hardship supplements.

    Social leave

    • For 28 days of social leave in a financial year, the full basic subsidy including any supplements are paid
    • The leave days does not have to be taken in a row
    • From day 29, the subsidy is reduced. The subsidy:
      • is payable at 25% of the basic subsidy rate
      • does not include any primary supplements*
      • continues to include other supplements

    *Primary supplements are oxygen, enteral feeding, dementia and cognition, and veterans’. Other eligible supplements are the viability and hardship supplements.

Impact of leave on the residential subsidy and supplements

Table 2

The amount of residential care subsidies and supplements is dependent on the leave type and leave duration. For residential care recipients, leave balances only reset on 1 July each year.

Type of leave

Impact on payment of residential subsidy to the services

Hospital

  • Residential care recipients are entitled to unlimited days of hospital leave
  • For 28 consecutive days for each hospital stay, the full basic subsidy is paid
  • From day 29, the subsidy will reduce to 50% of the basic subsidy rate
  • For the 2020-2021 financial year only, the basic subsidy will not be reduced from day 29, for care recipients on extended hospital leave
  • For all other financial years, the basic subsidy is reduced to 50% for permanent residential care recipients on extended hospital leave
  • For example:
    • Extended hospital leave that commenced on 20 May 2021 financial year and ends on 17 July 2021
    • The subsidy will reduce to 50% of the basic subsidy rate for the period 1 to 17 July 2021

Transition care

  • Transition care is part of hospital leave
  • For 28 consecutive days for each transition care period, the full basic subsidy is paid
  • From day 29, the subsidy will reduce to 50% of the basic subsidy rate

Social leave

  • Care recipients are entitled to 52 days of social leave in a financial year
  • Subsidy is not payable for care recipients who exceeds the 52 days

Emergency leave
  • Permanent residential care recipients are entitled to take emergency leave without exhausting their social leave entitlements
  • Aged care services will continue to be paid residential care subsidy when care recipients are on emergency leave
