Criteria for eligibility for age requirements ABSTUDY

Table 1: This table describes determining age requirement eligibility criteria for ABSTUDY.

Step

Action

1

General eligibility criteria + Read more ...

Does the student or Australian Apprentice meet the general eligibility criteria for ABSTUDY assistance?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ABSTUDY assistance. Check entitlement to another Centrelink payment. See Identifying the most beneficial payment for students or Australian Apprentices

Note: there is no maximum age for ABSTUDY.

2

Is the student or Australian Apprentice in lawful custody? + Read more ...

  • Yes, there are no minimum age requirements for these customers. They may be eligible for the Student in Lawful Custody Award. See the Resources page for details of allowances payable. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Is the student a full-time primary student and under 14 years of age? + Read more ...

  • Yes, the student has not reached the minimum age for ABSTUDY assistance for a primary student. Advise the student to contact Centrelink again when they reach 14 years of age. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Is the student a full-time primary student who is either: + Read more ...

  • 14 years of age, or
  • 15 years of age and not in State Care?
    • Yes, they may be eligible for the Schooling A Award. See the Resources page for the details of allowances payable. Procedure ends here
    • No, go to Step 5

5

Is the student a full-time primary student and: + Read more ...

  • 16 years of age or over, or
  • 15 years of age and in State Care?
    • Yes, they may be eligible for the Schooling B Award. See the Resources page for the details of allowances payable. Procedure ends here
    • No, see Step 1 in the Education levels for eligibility assessment table

Education levels for eligibility assessment

Table 2: This table describes the steps for determining eligibility for ABSTUDY by study at different education levels.

Step

Action

1

Check student status + Read more ...

Is the student a full-time secondary student under 16 years of age and living at home or not entitled to the Away from Home or an independent (ABSTUDY) rate?

Note: ABSTUDY cannot be approved until Services Australia is satisfied the student will undertake approved secondary studies. For this reason, ask the school to clarify (in writing) the type of program the young person will undertake (e.g. mainstream subjects or a remedial/bridging program). General statements from the school such as 'secondary studies' or 'upgraded' cannot be accepted. The statement must confirm the enrolment status and study level.

  • Yes, they may be eligible for the Schooling A Award. See the Resources page for the details of allowances payable. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Is the student either: + Read more ...

  • a full-time secondary school student who is:
    • 16 years of age or over, or
    • 15 years of age and in State Care, or
    • under 16 years of age and entitled to the Away from Home rate or an independent (ABSTUDY) rate,

or

  • a full-time secondary non-school student who has either:
    • reached the minimum school leaving age for their State/Territory, or
    • been granted an exemption by the State/Territory education authority to attend a non-school institution?
  • Yes, they may be eligible for the Schooling B Award. See the Resources page for details of allowances payable. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Is the student a part-time secondary student? + Read more ...

  • Yes, they may be eligible for the Part-time Award for which the minimum age for secondary students is 18 years or more as at 1 January. See the Resources page for details of allowances payable. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Is the student a part-time tertiary student? + Read more ...

  • Yes, they may be eligible for the Part-time Award for which there is no minimum age for tertiary students. See the Resources page for details of allowances payable. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Is the student applying for assistance to attend a selection test, assessment or interview for entry to a tertiary course? + Read more ...

  • Yes, they may be eligible for the Testing and Assessment Award. See the Resources page for details of allowances payable. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

The student is a full-time tertiary student or Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

If the student has reached the minimum school leaving age for their State or Territory, or has been granted an exemption from the relevant State/Territory education authority to undertake a tertiary level course, they may be eligible to receive the Tertiary Award or the Masters and Doctorate Award, depending on their level of study.

If the Australian Apprentice has reached the minimum school leaving age for their State or Territory, or has been granted an exemption from the relevant State/Territory education authority to undertake an Australian Apprenticeship, they may be eligible to receive the Tertiary Award.

See the Resources page for details of allowances payable for the Tertiary Award and for allowances for the Masters and Doctorate Award.
