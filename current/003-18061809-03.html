For Services Australia social workers only

If a child is in immediate danger or risk of harm you must act, and a delegate must call 000. If you have concerns about a child’s safety, conduct the risk and referral process.

This page outlines the process for recording a referral to Social Work related to the National Redress Scheme.

Recording referrals in SWIS for individuals linked to the Scheme

Table 1: This table describes how a social worker records a National Redress Scheme related referral in SWIS.

Step

Action

1

Referral received + Read more ...

All referrals from the Scheme are recorded in SWIS. Before recording any information in SWIS, ask the individual if they:

  • have a Customer Reference Number (CRN). Some individuals will not have a CRN or refuse to provide it
  • consent to a referral being made in SWIS

Is the individual at imminent risk of harm or suicide?

  • Yes, see Individual is at imminent with or harm or suicide table
  • No, if the individual has:
  • a CRN and provided consent to a referral being made in SWIS, go to Step 2
  • a CRN but does not consent to a referral being made in SWIS, go to Step 3
  • does not have a CRN, go to Step 3

2

Record referral - CRN and consent provided/ + Read more ...

Record the referral, CRN and consent.

From Customer First > Workspace > Social Work Information System (SWIS):

  • Select Referrals/contacts
  • Select Create New Referral
  • Referral Source: field select DHS and Specialists/Professionals. In the drop down list select Senior Customer Service Advisor
  • Referral Issues: field select Sexual Abuse and Trauma, Other Violence and other applicable fields depending on the individual's circumstances. Note: up to 3 referral issues can be selected for each referral
  • Specialist surveys: field select Zone Survey from the drop down list
  • Select Continue

Go to Step 4.

3

Record referral - no CRN and/or consent + Read more ...

Record an anonymous referral.

From Customer First > Workspace > Social Work Information System (SWIS):

  • Select Add Anonymous Client
  • Compete the mandatory fields on the Add Anonymous Client page. Do not complete the Surname; First Name; Second Name or Initial fields regardless of whether this information is known
  • Select Continue, the Referrals/Contacts screen will display
  • Select Create New Referral
  • Referral Source: field select DHS and Specialists/Professionals. In the drop down list select Senior Customer Service Advisor
  • Referral Issues: field select Sexual Abuse and Trauma, Other Violence and other applicable fields depending on the individual's circumstances. Note: up to 3 referrals issues can be selected for each referral
  • Specialist surveys: field select Zone Survey from the drop down list
  • Select Continue

Go to Step 5.

4

Record contact - CRN and consent provided + Read more ...

Where the individual has provided their CRN and consent, record the contact.

Do not record information about the individual on their Customer First record outside of SWIS, including the Document List (DL) screen. Note: only the referral and relevant information, such as release of information, is to be stored in SWIS case notes.

From Customer First > Workspace > Social Work Information System (SWIS):

  • Select Contact Type: for example phone interview or face to face
  • Select Social Work Interventions: choose up to 3 options (as appropriate), for example Crisis Intervention, Counselling/Support or Referral Out etc.
  • Outcome of Intervention: choose up to 3 options as appropriate, for example Referral Made/Completed
  • Record any other relevant SWIS contacts
  • Record SWIS case notes, only record information that is necessary to providing social work assistance and support to the individual

Other issue/s raised

If other agency business is discussed, for example a Crisis Payment (CrP) while responding to a redress related referral:

  • Record the intervention on SWIS, and
  • Document where required. Note: do not refer to redress in any documentation outside of SWIS.

5

Record contact - no CRN and/or consent + Read more ...

Where the individual has not provided a CRN or consent, record an anonymous contact.

From Customer First > Workspace > Social Work Information System (SWIS):

  • Select Contact Type: for example phone interview or face to face
  • Select Social Work Interventions: choose up to 3 options (as appropriate) for example Crisis Intervention, Counselling/Support or Referral Out etc.
  • Outcome of Intervention: choose up to 3 options as appropriate for example Referral Made/Completed
  • Record any other relevant SWIS contacts

The system automatically generates a dummy CRN for the anonymous individual. These records are designed to be used short term for 1-2 days for recording purposes, the record should not be accessed again. DOCs or case notes should not be added to anonymous records.

Other issue/s raised

If other agency business is discussed, for example a Crisis Payment (CrP) while responding to a redress related referral, the individual must provide a CRN or consent to a CRN being created.

Has the individual provided a CRN or consented to a CRN being create?

  • Yes, see step 2 in Individual at imminent risk or harm or suicide table
  • No, the discussion cannot be documented. The agency business cannot be actioned. Procedure ends here

Individual at imminent risk of harm or suicide

Table 2: This table describes how a social worker records a National Redress Scheme referral in SWIS when the individual is at imminent risk of harm or suicide.

Step

Action

1

Individual is at imminent risk of harm or suicide + Read more ...

If an individual is assessed at risk of harm or suicide and they do not provide consent for the social worker to record on their Customer First record, the social worker is authorised to take appropriate action to prevent or lessen the serious threat:

  • attempt to locate a CRN by:
    • searching Customer First
    • contacting the DSS Redress Officers
  • contact the DSS Redress Officers to seek relevant information (for example, the individual's contact details where required)
  • record information if it is necessary to prevent or lessen a serious threat to the individual's life, health or safety

Does the individual have a CRN or is there enough information to have a CRN created?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

6

Release of information + Read more ...

Where it is necessary to prevent or lessen the threat to life, health or welfare of a person, a Service Manager, Service Leader or social worker (APS6 and above) may disclose information to police or other relevant authorities.

For more information about when information can be disclosed to third parties see:

  • Disclosing information to third parties
  • Threat to self-harm and/or to harm others. The Resources page contains an Intranet link

If the individual is at risk and their information needs to be released, complete the Release of Information form. The Resources page contains a link to the form.
