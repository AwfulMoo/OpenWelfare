Process and actioning a death notification of a student

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Death of a student + Read more ...

When a person contacts to advise of the death of a student, staff must offer the notifier:

  • the services of a social worker in the first instance or at any time the notified becomes or shows signs of distress during the contact. See Social work service referral
  • the pamphlet 'Needing help after someone has died? (HD001)'. If offer is accepted, issue using the Mail Forms workflow or manually in person

The following cultural issues need to be respected and considered by Service Officers when being advised of the death of an Indigenous customer:

  • the name of the deceased person cannot be mentioned by immediate family members. This means immediate family members may have to ask other relatives/friends to call on their behalf or attend the interview with the notifier, because they cannot say the name of the deceased
  • in this situation, it may also be offensive to the person for the Service Officer to say the name of the deceased person. Service Officers should sensitively ask the person if it is alright for them to use the deceased customer's name. If the person is not comfortable with this, avoid using the deceased customer's name wherever possible. See Cultural Considerations when someone dies

Does the customer's record indicate that they were income managed, as shown by INM on the benefit status line?

  • Yes, follow process to update record for death of an income managed customer, then return to this procedure and go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Confirm the death of the deceased student + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure in the deceased customer's record to determine if death can be confirmed.

Confirmation of death can be accepted where the student's full name is provided together with 2 of the following items:

  • deceased customer's last address in full
  • date of birth and age at death
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN), or
  • partner's name (if partnered)

Confirmation of death also requires the following information about the informant:

  • name
  • address
  • phone number

Confirm if the person notifying of the death is the executor or administrator of the estate. If the informant is not the executor, but knows the executor details, obtain:

  • the name and address of the executor or administrator, and
  • the name of the funeral director or hospital

See Confirmation of the death of an adult or child.

Nominee arrangements and Power of Attorney (POA) stop at the time of death. Any correspondence, including the payment summary and possible debt recovery action from a deceased estate, will be sent to the:

  • executor, or
  • administrator of an estate

Services Australia's ability to finalise any outstanding matters will be impacted if executor or administrator details are:

  • incorrectly recorded, or
  • not recorded when details have been provided

Where executor details are not known at the time of death notification, the executor details fields in the Notification of Death guided procedure must be left blank.

Note: executor details can be recorded manually on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen if details become known later.

Has enough information been provided to accept the confirmation of death?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Death cannot be confirmed + Read more ...

Using the Notification of death guided procedure, obtain and record all relevant information from the informant including their:

  • name
  • address
  • contact number
  • relationship to the deceased

If known, obtain details of the executor or administrator, the name of the funeral director, and the name of the hospital.

The guided procedure includes a suspension option if the death is unable to be confirmed. If the guided procedure is not available, or the suspension option does not appear, code the suspension manually.

To suspend the payment:

  • use the reason code 'OTH' (Other) from the date of the advised date of death or date paid to plus one (DPT+1), whichever is the later
  • ensure the DOC meets Online Document Recording (ODR) standards for significant decisions and includes what action is required to confirm the death if applicable
  • do not issue a Q134 suspension letter. Cancel the advice activity on the Activity List (AL) screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension
  • it is not necessary to code a manual review, as there is already an open DOC/work item

The guided procedure will not code the death, but will create a DOC on the record which is an open work item to be allocated to a Service Officer with the required skill set to confirm the death.

If the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item:

  • Use Fast Note - select Auto Text > Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED
  • Select.

Note: it is imperative that the death is confirmed and actioned as a matter of urgency. For Service Officers confirming the death, contact the funeral director or the hospital where the deceased passed away. If this is not an option and contact with relatives is proposed, social worker involvement may be necessary.

When the death is confirmed, go to Step 4

4

Is the deceased customer due to report + Read more ...

Was the deceased customer due to report or lodge a reporting statement?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Deceased customer was due to report + Read more ...

Are the deceased customer's employment income details for the reporting period in which they passed away known, or can the person notifying of the death provide the customer's employment income details for the last reporting period?

  • Yes:
    • using the Earnings and Reporting tool, process the reporting statement. Note: the date of lodgement on the Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen may need to be changed first to the day before the date of death. For more information see Changing Entitlement Period End Dates (EPED)
    • go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Stimulate the payment + Read more ...

Stimulate the payment using the Earnings and reporting workflow tool.

Take the deceased customer off reporting by updating the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen.

7

Check outstanding activities + Read more ...

A death action cannot be processed with activities on the Activity List (AL) screen. The Notification of Death guided procedure will provide a prompt if there is an activity on the AL screen.

Note: if a deceased customer's record was suspended while the death was being confirmed and their death has now been confirmed, check for started activities on the AL screen manually.

Where possible ensure any activities are completed and run the daily driver:

  • If there is a 'STA' debt activity on the AL screen, create an open work item for region XWA advising them of the death and requesting the debt activity be completed
  • Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Debts > DMB Raising > Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request. See Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer
  • For other activities, immediately contact the team or Service Officer who needs to complete the outstanding activity, if unsure who to contact to get this activity completed, refer to existing escalation channels

Have any started activities been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 8

8

When it is not possible to complete an activity + Read more ...

If there is an outstanding activity that the Service Officer is unable to complete, the customer's payment should be suspended, if not already suspended.

Suspend manually suspend using the reason 'OTH' (Other) pending completion of the outstanding activity:

  • The suspension date of event should be the date of death or date paid to plus one (DPT+1), whichever is the later
  • Record a DOC on the customer's record, ensuring that it meets Online Document Recording (ODR) standards for significant decisions and include what action must occur so the death can be actioned
  • As the customer's death has been confirmed, no letter must be sent. It is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension

If an open DOC/work item regarding the death notification has not already been created, either:

  • Run the Notification of death guided procedure to generate an open work item for the relevant staff to complete the death action once the outstanding activities have been completed, or
  • If the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item
  • Use Fast Note - select Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED

When activity is completed, go to Step 9.

9

Check if restoration is needed + Read more ...

Is payment suspended?

  • Yes restore payment if suspended. Ensure that no arrears are issued. The restoration advice must be inhibited, as it is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Death action is to then be processed immediately, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Code death action + Read more ...

To code death action:

  • Run the Notification of Death guided procedure (which includes executor details, if known) unless surviving partner is on Parenting Payment. In this case follow the procedures outlined in Death of a partner of a Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) customer then return to this procedure if the student was receiving a Financial Supplement loan (FSL)
  • If the customer was receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) or Child Care Subsidy (CCS) for any children do not unlink children before coding death action. The Notification of Death guided procedure will cancel FTB/CCS and allow children to be linked to another person once daily driver has run. Invite new carer to claim FTB for the child(ren)
  • For all other payments, if the guided procedure is not available, manually code the death action
  • When recording a death the Notification Handler (NOHL) is incorrectly using the date of notification as the date to increase the survivor's rate to the single rate. If coding the death action manually, the workaround for this is to record the date of receipt (DOR) as the later of date of death or date paid to + 1'

  • No, go to Step 13

Surviving partner's entitlements

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Long-term social security recipients + Read more ...

Were both members of the couple long-term social security recipients at the time of death, or was the deceased a long-term social security recipient and the surviving partner a pensioner?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Both members of couple are long-term social security recipients or deceased a long-term recipient and surviving partner a pensioner + Read more ...

A Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP) is payable to the surviving partner where their new single rate is less than the couple's member of a couple rate at the time of death.

The LBP represents the difference between these 2 rates for the duration of the bereavement period. Therefore, if the member of a couple rate of payment has continued from the date of death, the LBP will be reduced

When a member of an allowee couple or allowee/pensioner couple dies, the LBP will need to be worked out manually.

Is the surviving partner in receipt of JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA)?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, manually calculate and then issue the LBP:
    • go to the One Off Payment (OOP) screen
    • in the Effect Date field, code the date of death
    • in the Payment Type field, select 'bls' (bereavement lump sum)
    • press [Enter]
    • the Assessment Results (AR) screen is displayed
    • from the left hand menu select Optional Actions>Partner/Ex-Partner>Pensions/Allowances>One Off Payments (OOP)
    • code the LBP amount in the Amount column in the Bereavement Payment line
    • press [Enter] twice to return to the AR screen
    • finalise activity and record details on a DOC
    • issue Q150 letter to advise of LBP, then go to Step 5

3

Both members of the couple were not long-term social security recipients + Read more ...

Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP) is not payable on the death of a member of an allowee couple or member of an allowee/pensioner couple, unless both were long-term social security recipients or the deceased was a long-term social security recipient and the survivor was a pensioner.

Is the surviving partner in receipt of JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Surviving partner receives JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance + Read more ...

A MFU will be generated to check eligibility to Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV) and/or Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP).

PBV is paid to a JSP or YA customer if:

  • the customer is receiving JSP or YA when they advise of their partner's death
  • both the customer and their deceased partner were Australian residents when the partner died
  • the customer and the deceased person were a couple when their partner died
  • the customer or their correspondence nominee has notified of the partner's death
  • the customer had not re-partnered when they notified of the death
  • the customer notified of the death within the 14 week bereavement period or within the duration of the pregnancy if the pregnancy is more than 14 weeks from partner's death

If the customer is also eligible for a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment, they must relinquish the LBP writing. This includes customers who have already been paid LBP and wish to receive PBV instead.

Is the customer entitled to PBV and/or LBP?

  • Yes:
    • in Process Direct, select the Bereavement assistance workflow. The bereavement calculator will launch after the partner death details are recorded on the Miscellaneous Details (NMIS) screen. Note: if a customer is eligible for both LBP and PBV, the bereavement calculator in Process Direct can be used to calculate and compare both amounts
    • Issue LBP
    • If PBV estimate is more than LBP, enclose Application for Partner Bereavement Payment (SA490) form with Q150 letter
    • Refer to Assessing entitlement to a Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV) and Calculation of a Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV) for details of process
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Action to take after death processing and survivor's bereavement payment assessment (if applicable) action is completed + Read more ...

Action the following:

  • If the surviving partner receives JSP or YA jobseeker, they may need to lodge 'Application for payment' forms (SU19BP) to advise of changes in circumstances. These may be lodged online, via phone self service, faxed or posted
  • If the surviving partner is on 2 weekly reporting because the deceased customer had employment income, Service Profiling will need to be refreshed on the surviving partner. Go to the Profile Summary (PQS) screen and code a 'Y' in the Reprofile field, then complete activity
  • If the surviving partner receives JSP, YA, PP or SpB under JSP or YA conditions, they can be exempted from mutual obligation or activity test requirements during this time. The Activity field on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen is automatically updated to 'BPP' (bereavement period) when the partner's death is recorded
  • Offer referral to a social worker if appropriate
  • Identify any service providers to be advised of the death including Assessment Services referrals
  • If the surviving partner was a joint owner of any assets with their late partner, full ownership of the assets legally transfers to the surviving partner following the death of their partner. However, for Social Security purposes, if assets are owned in joint names with the deceased partner, the surviving partner is assessed as owning 50 per cent. The system will assess that the surviving partner owns 50 per cent of the asset until the surviving partner advises of the transfer of assets
  • If the surviving partner is granted a reversionary income stream, then it is to be assessed from the date that it is first entitled to be received. A lump sum arrears payment received by the reversionary beneficiary, accrued between the death of the primary beneficiary and when the reversionary beneficiary commences to receive regular payments, is assessed as income from the income stream. This means the income is assessed over the period to which it relates and may result in a debt for that period. Effective from 9 May 2018, where the assessment of the lump sum arrears results in a debt during the bereavement period for the surviving spouse, consideration should be given to waiver provisions contained under Class of Debt, Section 1237AB(1) of the Social Security Act 1991. The legislative instrument “Social Security (Waiver of Debts - Bereavement Period) Specification 2017” permits this action if all provisions are satisfied. Note: any debt outside the 14 week bereavement period cannot be waived under the legislative instrument. The legislative instrument is not retrospective. This means that any of these debts that occur before 9 May 2018 cannot be waived under class of debt waiver provisions
  • Where the transfer of the deceased's assets and/or income to the surviving pension partner is likely to affect their rate, a bereavement review will be automatically issued 16 weeks after the partner's death. See Bereavement reviews
  • If the deceased had any working credits, these credits cannot be transferred to the surviving partner
  • Executor details may need to be updated after the death has been recorded
