This page contains information to assist with the processing of Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS) standalone claims for families who are not eligible for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) or do not want to claim FTB.

Actioning a standalone SIFS claim

Action

1

Receipt of claim or documentation + Read more ...

For initial action upon receipt of a claim or documentation for a claim, see Initial contact by families claiming Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS).

SIFS is not payable to new customers from 1 July 2017 for the 2017-18 and later financial years. Grandfathering provisions apply for existing customers.

If the person has:

  • submitted an online claim for SIFS, go to Step 6
  • requested to claim SIFS after the end of the lodgement year, go to Step 4

Has a special circumstances extension been applied?

Regenerate Online Claim for allocation via WLM - see final table in Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave payments.

  • Add notes as to why the claim was regenerated. Procedure ends here for Smart Centre telephony staff

4

Request to claim after the end of the lodgement year + Read more ...

Families unable or unsuitable to complete an online claim, or where a customer wishes to lodge a SIFS claim after the end of the lodgement year:

  • Launch the Assisted Customer Claims (ACC) Workflow via the desktop icon
  • Select ‘Apply for Family Assistance Payments (including Paid Parental Leave)’
  • On the Claim Options page select: Single Income Family Supplement for the last financial year
  • Follow the screen flow to record answers to all questions per customers answers
  • On the Review your Claim screen check that information entered is correct
  • Read the verbal signature declaration to the customer and select the ‘I have read, understood and accept my obligations’ radio button and Submit
  • Advise the customer any documentation required before processing can occur

Procedure ends here for Service Centre and Smart Centre Telephony staff.

Smart Centre Processing Staff, go to Step 6

5

Customer lodges a SIFS standalone claim, and a FTB combined or FTB lump sum claim for the same financial year + Read more ...

As FTB and SIFS claims are processed in the FAO system, claims must be processed in a certain order to achieve the correct outcome for all claims.

To correctly process all claims:

  • cancel the combined FTB claim from Activity List (AL) screen
  • cancel the SIFS claim from the AL screen
  • manually index and process a FTB LSC as per Processing Family Tax Benefit (FTB) lump sum claim claims. Return to this page once completed.
  • regenerate the Combined FTB claim and process as per Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments. Return to this page once completed
  • If SIFS claim is an online claim, regenerate, go to Step 6

Start workflow - check online claim details + Read more ...

  • Select Review Claim to view the information provided in the online claim

Check the status of the claim.

If the customer has uploaded the documents, view the document from the link.

Go to Step 7

Provision of information + Read more ...

Has all information/documentation required to assess the claim been provided?

This includes:

  • all relevant questions answered on claim
  • bank account details for direct credit
  • provision of tax file numbers
  • requested documents, for example, evidence of the customer's Australian residence status, proof of birth for child if this has never been provided for the child
  • If Yes, see Step 1 in Finalising workflow and SIFS claim table
  • No, exit workflow and make a genuine attempt to contact the customer:

Desktop Electronic Messaging

  • Prior to any outbound phone call attempt, send the 'Pre-Call' SMS notifying that Centrelink will be calling from a blocked number. This Desk 'Pre-Call' service is automated using the 'Desktop Messaging' workflow - Customer First > Workspace > Desktop Messaging
  • Make 1 attempt to contact the customer by phone within a reasonable time of the SMS message being issued (if issued) to ensure appropriate timeliness standards are maintained

If details cannot be obtained by phone, run Request Documents Q777 to request the information/ documentation be provided within the required timeframes or within 28 days for Tax File Number or direct credit details.

  • Resubmit activity ensuring that the customer is given the appropriate number of days to respond after the Q777 is issued on Day 1
  • If the information/documentation is not provided by the resubmit date, process claim as normal to record all details provided, but ensure the claim is made not effective before finalising on the AR screen. The customer will then need to lodge a new claim to receive SIFS

Finalising workflow for a SIFS claim

Step

Action

1

Continue workflow - process claim + Read more ...

Based on the customer’s claim responses the workflows are determined automatically for review, update and assessment.

Select each workflow in order with a status of Required and complete the guided procedure.

Workflows can be added and edited if necessary by using the Change Status toggle item.

Grandfathering status

Check the customer has met the SIFS grandfathering provisions. Check the SIFS override (FSGO) screen.

  • If the grandfathering override status is 'Yes', and the customer's circumstances indicate the override is no longer appropriate, manual intervention will be required to correct the status. The activity will be allocated to the Manual Intervention Team (MIV) for their action. See Manual intervention into family assistance reconciliation
  • For other situations of an incorrect override status, refer the case to a Families override contact to correct the status. See Families Override Contacts

Comparing relationship details

The Manage partner details guided procedure allows partner details to be updated, for example: link or unlink partner, change relationship start dates. The status of this workflow must be Completed or Not required to run data load.

If this completes with errors, manually update the status of the workflow to Completed and launch Workspace to complete any linking/unlinking within the claim activity before running data load again.

If partner details differ to those on the customer’s record, check details with customer.

Note: in some cases it will be necessary to unlink in a separate activity before running the workflow, for example unlink from a previous partner before using the workflow to link to a new partner.

Ex-partners: If the customer had other partners during the assessment year, exit workflow and manually record ex-partner details, adding the most recent ex-partner first. Then return to the workflow.

Compare child details

Check claim information against details on the customer record.

If action is required for a child, Managed dependent children workflow is presented and can be used to:

  • identify the child is on the customer's record (child will then show as matched)
  • link/add child record if not on the customer record

2

Child has their own PER record + Read more ...

Where a SIFS only child has a PER record, data load from the claim will not complete.

To ensure the correct outcome of the claim is achieved updates need to be made in the child PER record, before the data load workflow is completed.

Make note of the AMR of the SIFS claim activity as it is required to merge data on the Ripple Manager Consequence (RMC) screen.

  • Key the Child’s CRN in the CRN/BP field to go to the child record
  • On the CHS screen and ‘s’elect ‘Self’
  • Select In Customer Care and ensure the child is in the customers care and update if required.
  • Go to Workspace and select the Child education details workflow
  • Update the education details if required as per the claim
  • Navigate to the Assessment Results (AR) screen:
    • stop at the Ripple Manager (RMC) screen to update the ripple activity to the related SIFS carer
    • change the assessment instruction for the respective customer from Complete to Merge (the AMR of the SIFS claim activity in the carer record to merge into is required, to enter into the Merge on Act id field)
    • select continue to go to the AR screen and finalise the activity on the child’s record
  • Return to the customer record

3

Continue workflow - Data load and Finalise claim workflow + Read more ...

Launch Data Load workflow:

  • select Finish to complete the data load
  • the Data Load workflow status cannot be Completed with errors.
  • If this occurs:
    • select the grey box to the left of the Data Load workflow to display errors and warnings
    • select the Change Status toggle icon in line with the Data Load workflow. Change the status from Completed with errors to Completed.

Do not run Data Load multiple times. Any errors identified will need to be addressed using Staff Workflows or Workspace outside of the online claim. The only exception is if the claim has been regenerated which will require Data Load to be run again.

Launch Finalise Claim workflow:

This is run once all the required actions have been completed and all claim information has been updated, The workflow automatically takes information from the customer’s online claim and loads it into the new claim activity on the customer’s record.

Errors

Any errors or warnings will display on the Assessment Warning and Error (AWE) screen. These need to be actioned before the claim can be finalised.

Exit the workflow to action errors.

4

Select FTB LSC activity from AL + Read more ...

Go to the Single Income Family Supplement Claim (!SIFSC) page directly, or select 'FTB SIFS Claim' from the FAO Task Selector.

Select the assessment year.

Indicate that this is a SIFS only claim (this screen is only used for SIFS only claims).

An FTB Claim line with a Reason of SIF must exist on the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen for SIFS to be paid for a non-FTB child.

Blended family details

If the customer and partner during the assessment year are a blended family couple and the customer has advised the blended family percentage of SIFS they want to be paid, record details. The partner's blended family percentage cannot be recorded until they claim SIFS.

Note: unless both customers are grandfathered for SIFS, SIFS cannot be paid where only one customer is grandfathered.

5

Finalise claim + Read more ...

Go to the Assessment Results (AR) page.

For eligibility and income test information, see Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS).

Check the result:

  • ASSessed, pending further details
  • CANcelled. SIFS granted for all or part of the assessment year. The maximum annual SIFS payment is $302.95. SIFS is calculated on a daily basis. SIFS entitlement can be automatically used for debt offsetting, per FTB reconciliation rules
  • REJected or not effective. Attempt to contact the customer to explain why their claim is to be rejected or why it is not effective. (Not effective means that the claim cannot be assessed because required information/ documentation has not been provided. If the claim is not effective for reasons other than late lodgement, the customer will need to lodge a new claim to receive SIFS)

If the claim is rejecting due to late lodgement, staff will need to have a discussion with the customer to explain the new lodgement rules and to undertake a special circumstances assessment. See Assessing special circumstances extensions for families unable to meet lodgement and other specific requirements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS).

  • Review and appeal provisions apply. SIFS is paid under family assistance law

For more information, see Cancellation and rejection codes for SIFS.

FTB Assessments

As FTB is not assessed as part of a SIFS only claim, FTB eligibility during SIFS only periods will display on the FTB Calculation Period Assessment (FCPA) and the XDS (DS List) screens as 'FTB REJ/SOC' (SIFS Only Claim).

Review the claim outcome before re-running the Finalise Claim workflow and selecting Finalise and Continue on the AR screen within the workflow to complete the activity.

Then select Finish in the top right corner to complete the workflow and return to the Social Application (Claim Workflow)

The Finalise Claim workflow will now show as Completed in the Staff Workflows assignment block.

DOC the claim outcome using Fast Note select > Auto text > Families > Claims > Grant/Reject/Assess FTB LSC.

An automatic letter will be sent to the customer to advise the result.

  • If the claim is pending for all or part of the assessment year, go to Step 6
  • Otherwise, procedure ends here

6

SIFS claim pending + Read more ...

Pending reasons are similar to FTB lump sum claim pending reasons.

Pending income

SIFS cannot be paid for a period until adequate income details are available for the customer, and their partner/ex-partner for that period.

For SIFS, adequate income means:

  • Accepted not required to lodge advice and provision of an income estimate.
  • Taxable income details electronically transferred from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) if the person is required to lodge a tax return

Tax returns must be lodged (or not required to lodge advice provided) by 30 June of the lodgement year for SIFS to be paid. Claims will be automatically rejected (late lodgement) if the ATO advise either the customer or their partner lodged tax returns after the end of the lodgement year, unless a special circumstances income confirmation extension has been granted.

Note: only taxable income (TI) is used to assess SIFS, not adjusted taxable income (ATI). The customer is asked to estimate their ATI for SIFS only claims to ensure their taxable income estimate is reasonable.

SIFS may be pending for all or part of the assessment year, for example, if income details are available for the customer but not for their partner, SIFS may be paid (CUR status) for the non-partnered period(s).

Blended families - APC (awaiting partner claim). Blended family claims cannot be finalised until each member of the couple has claimed SIFS. An auto review is set up so that if no partner claim has been received by 30 June of the lodgement year, the customer can be paid the family's full SIFS. This is a manual process of deleting blended family details from the !SIFSC screen. The customer will then receive 100% of family's SIFS entitlement.
