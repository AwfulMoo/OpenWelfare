Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Contact with the job seeker + Read more ...

Job seeker management activities such as JSR/ADD, JSR/UPD, JSR/MFU and WAP/MFU activities, are created to identify further action required to enable job seeker's to access appropriate employment services.

Work items are allocated to suitably skilled staff to prompt contact with the job seeker (where appropriate) in order to complete outstanding participation tasks that require action. These include:

  • activation of a customer's job seeker registration
  • completion of the Job Seeker Snapshot
  • referral to an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt)
  • administration and booking of ESAt appointments
  • referral to Employment Services Providers
    • Workforce Australia
    • Workforce Australia – Transition to Work (TtW)
    • Community Development Program (CDP)
    • Disability Employment Service (DES)
  • referral to the Skills for Education and Employment (SEE) or Adult Migrant English Program (AMEP)
  • updates of job seeker approved activities and negotiation of Centrelink Managed Job Plans

Staff who are allocated a work item, must check the Document List (DL) screen to determine what action is required. If a:

  • first outbound contact attempt with the job seeker is required, go to Step 2
  • JSR 1st attempt to contact DOC has already been recorded on the Document List (DL) screen, a second outbound contact attempt is required, go to Step 3
  • job seeker has contacted following receipt of a Q164 – Request to contact letter, go to Step 5
  • job seeker has not responded to a Q164 letter, go to Step 6

2

First outbound attempt to contact job seeker + Read more ...

When it is necessary to contact a job seeker, 2 reasonable and genuine attempts to contact must be made.

If the contact number recorded on the job seeker's record is a mobile number and they have registered for SMS messaging, create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). After creating the SMS, wait 5 minutes before making the outbound attempt.

Was the contact successful?

Yes, go to Step 7

No, and:

  • the job seeker is not contactable due to being incarcerated or living in a drug and alcohol rehabilitation facility:
    • Hold the work item for 13 weeks from date of incarceration or admission to drug and alcohol rehabilitation
    • Record the reason there was no contact attempt in a DOC using the Notes icon in Process Direct or Fast Note within Customer First. Procedure ends here
  • the job seeker has telephone contact details recorded:
    • Hold the work item to the next calendar day
    • Record sufficient detail regarding the outbound contact attempt using the JSR 1st attempt to contact Fast Note in Customer First. This will enable subsequent Service Officers to action the second attempt and finalise the activity
    • Select the 'Display on access' box and, when prompted, check that the Reason field displays 'OTH-Urgent free text message exists'
    • The Expiry Date field should default to the maximum date allowed (60 days from today)
    • Procedure ends here
  • telephone details are not recorded, incorrect or outdated, go to Step 4

3

Second outbound attempt to contact job seeker + Read more ...

Make a second genuine attempt to contact the customer if the first attempt was unsuccessful.

If the contact number, recorded on the job seeker's record, is a mobile number and they have registered for SMS messaging, create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). Following creation of the SMS, wait 5 minutes before making the outbound attempt.

Was the contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No:
    • close the JSR 1st attempt to contact Display on Access (DOA) DOC
    • record details of the unsuccessful contact using the JSR 2nd attempt to contact Fast Note. Ensure the 'Display on access' box is selected. When prompted, check that the Reason field displays 'OTH-Urgent free text message exists'. The Expiry Date field should default to the maximum date allowed (60 days from today)
    • go to Step 4

4

Issue a Q164 letter after second unsuccessful contact attempt or where no contact details recorded for job seeker + Read more ...

Issue a Q164 letter asking the customer to contact Services Australia by phone within 14 days:

  • after a second unsuccessful outbound contact attempt, or
  • where there are no contact details or incorrect details are recorded (for example, the job seeker's phone number is not registered)

The customer has 14 days to respond after they receive the letter.

  • Add extra days to allow time for mail delivery
  • Hold the work item until the Q164 response due date + 1 day (e.g. 14 days + mailing time = 22/29 days after the date the Q164 letter is sent)
  • Where the Q164 letter is issued due to no contact details or incorrect contact details are recorded, create a Display on Access (DOA) DOC confirming:
    • the reason the job seeker needs to be contacted
    • outstanding action(s) to be undertaken if the job seeker replies to the Q164 contact request

Procedure ends here.

5

Job seeker contacts after receiving a Q164 letter + Read more ...

If the job seeker makes contact after receiving a Q164 - Request to contact letter:

  • prior to being suspended Failing to Respond to Correspondence (SUS-FRC), action outstanding activities. Go to Step 7
  • within 13 weeks of notification of suspension (FRC):
    • restore payment, see Restoration of payments (CLK)
    • action outstanding activities. Go to Step 7

6

Job seeker fails to respond to the Q164 letter + Read more ...

If the job seeker fails to contact by the due date +1 after the Q164 is issued, Service Officers must determine if suspension action can be taken. Before suspending a customer's payment, see Suspension of payments (CLK) for information about factors to consider.

If it is appropriate to manually suspend a customer for Failing to Respond to Correspondence (SUS-FRC):

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen:
    • Key the payment type as the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn field
    • Key the action to be taken in the Action field
    • Key the suspension reason 'FRC' in the Reason field
    • Press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or key the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date field. For further information, see Suspension of payments
    • Complete the Source field and the date of receipt in the DOR field
    • Finalise the activity
  • Create a closed DOC to record the decision to suspend:
    • In Customer First, use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > SUS/CAN/RES > Suspend Cancel Reason
    • Follow-up action required on contact with the customer
    • Advise if contact with the agency is required before restoration can be done or whether Smart Centre Telephony staff may restore the payment

Where the job seeker's payment has:

  • already been suspended:
    • no further action is required by Services Australia
    • hold the work item for 13 weeks from the suspension date. Do not cancel the work item
  • not been suspended as it not appropriate to suspend the job seeker's payment for failing to contact:
    • undertake job seeker contact, and
    • hold the work item as per the Q164 process. Do not cancel the work item

7

Finalise job seeker management JSR/ADD, JSR/UPD and JSR/MFU and WAP/MFU activities + Read more ...

Go to Process Direct to complete the outstanding processing. Action all job seeker management work items in Process Direct.

Before processing the work, consider the information provided within the:

  • Display on Access (DOA) DOC, outlining previous attempts to contact and a description of the outstanding action(s) required
  • Action Item section within the Key Information tab of the Participation Summary screen
  • Message section of the relevant tabs within the Participation Summary screen
  • relevant activity on the History Activity List (HAL) screen

On successful contact with the customer, go to the Key Information tab within the Participation Summary of Process Direct to view and action all outstanding job seeker management activities:

  • Check the job seeker's current circumstances to determine whether they need to be connected with a provider or have approved activity or exemption coded. If the job seeker:
    • has an expired approved activity on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen, but are still participating in an approved activity, renew the activity by extending the dates on the AEX screen. After the AEX screen is updated, the Key Information tab will display a new Action Item to negotiate a new Centrelink Manage Job Plan if a new Job Plan is required. See Negotiating Centrelink Managed Job Plans
    • has a WAP/MFU with keyword 'WAPDHSM' assess whether job seeker can be coded as meeting mutual obligation requirements via one of the following employment related approved activities PTW, SEL, PWS, SSE, SPW, and VPE on the AEX screen and update Job Plan if required. Note: if job seeker is a Disability Employment Services (DES) job seeker only update the AEX screen as the DES provider will update the Job Plan. See Approved mutual obligation requirements for Centrelink managed Job Plans
    • remains eligible for a mutual obligation requirements exemption, see Mutual obligation requirements exemptions
    • qualifies for a deferred referral or extension, go to Deferred referral to Employment Services Providers. Record deferred referrals for customers serving a waiting period who choose not to volunteer for employment services
    • no longer qualifies for registration as a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP), inactivate their JSR via the Inactivate job seeker registration workflow from the Job seeker registration and provider referral information tab within the Participation Summary in Process Direct. This auto-completes the JSR/UPD activity
  • Check Job Seeker Snapshot result via the link from the Participation Summary > Job seeker registration & provider information tab. If the status is pending then the job seeker requires an ESAt. If an ESAt is flagged for medical reasons (or both medical and non-medical reasons) determine if medical evidence has been provided. If not, check if the job seeker has been requested to provide medical evidence within 14 days of the Job Seeker Snapshot being run on the Document List (DL) screen. Contact the job seeker and request medical evidence if it is yet to be provided, or no previous request has been made. For more information regarding management of the pending ESAt and/or subsequent appointment bookings, see Actioning a pending Job Seeker Snapshot initiated Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) activity. If the job seeker has an outstanding claim, request medical evidence (if required) and hold the work item until the expected claim finalisation date. Assessment Services staff will be allocated the work item to determine if an ESAt appointment is required to be booked.
  • Check the ESAT/JCA recommended referral outcome via the Referral Summary (RRSUM) screen in Process Direct. Locate the current referrals within the Referral Summary table and select the ESAt/JCA Report hyperlink. If the ESAt or JCA outcome is showing as referral to DES, then go to the Participation Summary > Online Diary tab, select DES - Disability Employment Service from Create new referral. See refer the job seeker to a DES provider
  • Select the Participation Action Item to make appropriate referrals. Selecting the Action item will direct the Service Officer to the appropriate screens/workflows to complete the job seeker activity. See Participation Summary > Key Information screen within Process Direct for processing guidelines and actioning issues
  • Discuss the job seeker's mutual obligation requirements including requirements to attend any booked appointments and possible consequences if they fail to attend
  • Check that all of the job seeker management activities are finalised. Selecting View details of the relevant AMR listed on the History Activity List (HAL) screen displays the completed status of the activity when processing has been finalised. Activities with a completed status are not displayed on the Activity List (AL) screen within Process Direct
  • Manually complete all Manual Follow-up (MFU) activities in Customer First
  • Close any attempt to contact Display on Access (DOA) DOCs

Where a job seeker requires an initial or re-engagement appointment:

  • Ensure the appointment is booked with the provider within the next 2 working days (if available)
  • Tell the job seeker of their appointment details verbally or in writing. Ensure the job seeker receives proper notification and reasonable notice of the appointment, and that a DOC is recorded to make sure there is a record of contact with the job seeker, and that verbal notification has been given
  • After booking an appointment, Service Officers must record a DOC using:
    • the Notes icon within Process Direct, or
    • Customer First and running the Provider Appointment Fast Note. Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Jobseekers > Update > Provider Appointment Booked

Note: only place job seeker management activities on hold where instructed in this procedure. If job seeker management activities do not finalise, only lodge a mySupport request as outlined in this procedure.

Service Officers that are allocated a Job Seeker Management Activity, see Table 2 and Table 3 for more information relevant to these work items.

JSR/ADD and JSR/UPD activities

Table 2: This table describes job seeker management activities generated on customer records. These activities need further action and cannot be cancelled.

Step

Action

1

Job Seeker Registration (JSR/ADD) activities + Read more ...

The work item ZIAM_NOJSR_GEN is created when the job seeker is receiving an income support payment with mutual obligation requirements, but does not have an active Job Seeker Registration (JSR/ACT) status.

The majority of these job seekers are required to be registered as a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP) job seeker. Job seekers in Community Development Program (CDP) regions will require registration as Remote Fully Eligible (RFE).

Once the job seeker has a JSR/ACT status, the JSR/ADD activity will auto-complete.

Note: certain job seekers can be registered as a Job Seeker Registration Only (JSRO)/No Remote Referral Required (NRRR) job seeker. For example, principal carers already meeting their requirements who do not want to volunteer for referral to employment services. Complete the Job Seeker Snapshot for these job seekers if they want to access other employment services or if they are NRRR and need an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt).

Special Benefit (SpB) job seekers granted under Job Seeker Payment (JSP) conditions and who have a Temporary Visa (TPV) class, can only be registered as volunteer job seekers.

Do not cancel this activity, unless the corresponding job seeker claim has recently been rejected, in which case it should be cancelled.

2

Job Seeker Registration/Update (JSR/UPD) activities + Read more ...

These work items are created to alert further action is required on a job seeker's record. A To Do Indicator (TDI) may be attached to a JSR/UPD activity and identifies what action is needed. Job Seeker Registration (JSR) activities are displayed on the Activity List (AL) screen. Action outstanding JSR/UPD activities via the Action Item displayed on the Participation Summary screen within Process Direct. Activities are listed in priority order and should be actioned from the highest priority, see Participation Summary screen.

Do not cancel these activities if the job seeker's benefit status line shows they are JSR/ACT or they are registered as a job seeker with the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations (DEWR) system. Attempting to cancel the JSR/UPD activity will result in an error.

Complete the relevant workflow to undertake the action appropriate for the job seeker, as indicated by the TDI attached.

If the customer does not require JSR, inactivate their JSR via Process Direct > Participation Summary > job seeker registration and provider referral information tab. This auto-completes the JSR/UPD activity.

3

JSR/UPD activity with 'JSC' To Do Indicator (TDI) and 'JSCIREQ' keyword + Read more ...

The work item ZIAM_JSCI_COM is created when the job seeker is receiving an income support payment with mutual obligation requirements, and has a JSR/ACT status, but has no active Job Seeker Snapshot on their record. An Action Item showing this requirement is displayed on the Participation Summary screen within Process Direct.

Once the Job Seeker Snapshot workflow is complete, the JSR/UPD activity with 'JSC' TDI will auto-complete, even when the Job Seeker Snapshot status is still pending. For example, where an ESAt is triggered.

Note: if the job seeker has been referred/commenced in Disability Employment Services (DES), lodge a mySupport request to cancel the JSR activity. Service Officers cannot update the Job Seeker Snapshot.

Employment Services and DES do not apply in CDP regions. The CDP provider in the region will provide all levels of servicing.

4

JSR/UPD activity with 'JSC' TDI and 'FEP' keyword + Read more ...

The work item ZIAM_JSCI_COM is created when the job seeker is receiving an income support payment that requires registration as a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP). The Job Seeker Snapshot must be completed as part of FEP registration. This activity will not appear for job seekers in Community Development Program (CDP) regions.

Register a job seeker with mutual obligations as FEP unless they are a Job Seeker Registration Only (JSRO), by being:

  • long term exempt from mutual obligation requirements, or
  • participating in an approved requirement that makes them Centrelink managed

When the Job Seeker Registration workflow is completed in Process Direct, the JSR/UPD activity will complete.

Note: if the job seeker's approved activity or exemption has expired on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen and they are participating in the requirement or are exempt:

  • do not register the job seeker as a FEP, and
  • do not refer to an Employment Services Provider

If a Job Plan has not been negotiated within the last 12 months, the Key Information tab will display a new Action Item to negotiate a new Centrelink Managed Job Plan.

5

JSR/UPD activity with 'ESA' TDI and 'ESAREFRQ' keyword + Read more ...

The work item ZIAM_JSK_ESAT is created when a Job Seeker Snapshot has been run and the job seeker's responses indicate that further assessment may be appropriate and an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) or Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) may be required. As a general principle, the ESAt flag should only remain when there is a strong need to assess a customer's:

  • capacity to work, or
  • connections to support

The Job Seeker Snapshot status will be pending until a decision is made regarding an ESAt referral.

The ESAt activity is finalised using one of the following options:

  • Copy an existing ESAt or Job Capacity Assessment (JCA)
  • Withdrawing the ESAt flag
  • Deferring the booking of an ESAt appointment
  • Booking of an ESAt appointment by Assessment Services

To manage a pending Job Seeker Snapshot due to an ESAt referral being flagged, see Actioning a pending Job Seeker Snapshot initiated Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) activity.

6

JSR/UPD activity with 'ESA' TDI and 'ESATATT' keyword + Read more ...

The work item ZIAM_ESAT_SUS is created for all job seekers with mutual obligation requirements whose payments are suspended because they failed to attend an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt)/Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) appointment. Payment is suspended with reason:

  • ES1 (failed to attend first cycle ESAt appointment), or
  • ES2 (failed to attend second cycle ESAt appointment)

This activity acts as a flag on the job seeker's record and does not require immediate action. It should not be cancelled, but may be held for a maximum of 13 weeks from date of suspension pending contact from the job seeker.

No action is required until the job seeker contacts.

Once the job seeker contacts Services Australia, arrange a new ESAt appointment via the ESAt Attendance workflow on the Process Direct Task Selector.

7

JSR/UPD activity with 'ESP' TDI and 'ESPREF' keyword + Read more ...

The work item ZIAM_JSRREF_GEN is created when the job seeker has an active JSR and Job Seeker Snapshot, but has not been connected with an Employment Services Provider. An Action Item is displayed on the Participation Summary screen within Process Direct. This activity will not appear for job seekers in Community Development Program (CDP) regions.

This activity may create for the following reasons:

  • A referral is required to Workforce Australia Services
  • A referral is required to Disability Employment Services
  • An ESAt is required, however the job seekers claim has not been finalised

It is important to determine which referral is required before commencing action.

See Step 7 in Table 1 for action required.

Once an initial appointment with a provider is booked for the job seeker, the activity will complete.

8

Job Seeker JSR/UPD activity with 'ESP' TDI and 'NSUS' keyword + Read more ...

The work item ZIAM_JSCI_ESP - ESP Suspension Ended is created to advise Service Officers re-engagement with an Employment Services Provider is required after a Workforce Australia suspension period has expired. The creation of this activity is dependent on the suspension 'Expected End Date' that is advised by the DEWR system. There is no requirement to take any action on the work item or corresponding Action Item on the Participation Summary screen within Process Direct. This is because the job seekers Employment Services Provider makes contact with the job seeker after the suspension period.

The Participation Summary screen Activity Type/Non-Applied Exemptions tab provides information on the types of activity and exemptions the job seeker is undertaking. This activity will not appear for job seekers in Community Development Program (CDP) regions.

Note: if the job seeker remains eligible for an alternative activity type/exemption, the job seeker's current circumstances must be reviewed and the appropriate process actioned. See Negotiating Centrelink Managed Job Plans for Centrelink managed job seekers and Mutual obligation requirements exemptions.

There are future changes expected to remove this activity and corresponding Action Item.

9

JSR/UPD activity with 'ESA' TDI with 'D26' parameter and 'ESAREFRQ' keyword + Read more ...

The work item ZIAM_JSK_ESAT is created when a principal carer whose special family circumstances - Family and Domestic Violence (D26) exemption has expired and they:

  • live in a Community Development Program (CDP) region and are Remote Fully Eligible (RFE), or
  • do not have a current deferred Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) referral

The activity will not be created if the job seeker has had an ESAt referral since the exemption end date or an unfinished Participation Interview.

Action required:

  • Refer the principal carer to an ESAt if they are not eligible for a further exemption
  • Go to the Online Diary within Process Direct to book an ESAt appointment using the 'VLNTR' (Volunteer) referral reason. Note: in all circumstances where a customer has experienced family and domestic violence, see Family and domestic violence to identify and offer referrals and consider a social worker referral

10

JSR/UPD with 'RJC' TDI with no parameter + Read more ...

The work items ZIAM_JSCI_GEN or ZIAM_JSCI_COM will be created when a job seeker in a Community Development Program (CDP) region requires a referral to a CDP provider. Refer the job seeker to a CDP provider if they meet all of the following:

  • are Remote Fully Eligible (RFE)
  • are not linked to an Employment Services Provider, CDP or Disability Employment Services (DES)
  • do not have a deferred referral, and
  • do not have a current exemption from mutual obligation requirements

The requirement is located within the Action Item displayed on the Participation Summary screen within Process Direct.

11

JSR/UPD with 'TW5' TDI with 'TTWREF' keyword + Read more ...

The work item ZIAM_JSCI_GEN is created when a job seeker is eligible for a referral to a Workforce Australia - Transition to Work (TtW) provider and a TtW provider is available in the job seeker's region.

Refer the job seeker to a TtW provider if they meet all of the following:

  • are TtW eligible as determined by the Job Seeker Snapshot
  • are 15 - 24 years of age
  • are an early school leaver or disengaged young person
  • meet residence or visa requirements, and
  • meet the eligibility requirements as determined by the DEWR ICT system

This requirement is located within the Action Item displayed on the Participation Summary screen within Process Direct.

JSR/MFU and WAP/MFU activities

Table 3: This table describes Job Seeker Registration/Manual Follow-up (JSR/MFU) and Working Age Participation (WAP/MFU) with 'WAPDSHM' keyword activities that can be completed manually or cancelled (if appropriate).

Step

Action

1

JSR/MFU with 'AMEP/SEE' keyword + Read more ...

Work items ZCOC_JSK_AMEP_REF or ZCOC_JSK_SEE_REF are created when:

  • a job seeker is identified as benefitting from connection to the Adult Migrant English Programme (AMEP) or referral to Skills for Education and Employment (SEE) when the Job Seeker Snapshot is run, and
  • the Service Officer confirms a referral is required

Note: ensure the job seeker meets the eligibility criteria before referring them.

Ensure the job seeker has first tested their eligibility for AMEP before considering referral to SEE.

If the job seeker is a newly arrived migrant, over 18 years of age and does not have functional English, advise them to contact their local AMEP provider.

Record the advice given to the job seeker on a DOC.

For job seekers who are:

  • born overseas, check visa and residency details on the Legal Residence (RSLEG) and the Residence Results Display (RSRD) screens before proceeding
  • eligible for SEE and require a referral in Process Direct:
    • key 'START' in the Super Key
    • from the Task Selector, select Create SEE/AMEP Referral to proceed with the Skills for Education and Employment on-line eligibility checklist
    • Note: Create SEE/AMEP Referral appears on the Referral Summary (RRSUM) screen but is not a functional referral option - only refer using the Create SEE Referral action from the Task Selector
  • not interested in proceeding with Skills for Education and Employment referral, cancel the MFU. Record a DOC stating the reason why the MFU has been cancelled
  • not eligible for a SEE referral, cancel the MFU. Record a DOC stating the reason why the MFU has been cancelled

2

JSR/MFU with 'DES' keyword + Read more ...

The work item ZCOC_JSK_DES_REF is created to alert that a job seeker receiving an income support payment with mutual obligation requirements requires a referral to a Disability Employment Services (DES) provider when all the following are met:

  • the Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) or Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) report has been accepted and submitted
  • the job seeker is not residing in a Community Development Program (CDP) region
  • the Assessor's recommendation is for a referral to a DES provider, and
  • the job seeker has not yet been referred to or connected to the recommended DES provider

Go to Process Direct > Participation Summary > Online Diary to refer the job seeker to a DES provider.

These MFU activities must be manually completed in Customer First, after the referral is completed.

3

WAP/MFU with 'WAPDHSM' keyword + Read more ...

The work item ZIAM_JSK_PLN is created to prompt Service Officers to update the customer's Job Plan where a customer may be eligible for but does not currently have one of the following approved activities recorded:

  • Part-time Work (PTW)
  • Self-Employment (SEL)
  • Paid Work and Self Employment (Combined) (PWS)
  • Study and Self Employment (Combined) (SSE)
  • Study and Paid Work (Combined) (SPW), or
  • Voluntary and Paid Work (Combined) (VPE)

If the job seeker has been referred to a DES provider, record the approved activity on the AEX screen as the DES provider will be required to update the Job Plan.

See Approved mutual obligation requirements for Centrelink managed Job Plans for more information.
