Send Australian requests to NZIR (Central Authority Service Officer) + Read more ...

There are a number of tabs on the ‘Aus Daily Exchange’ to facilitate the exchange of information, The following tabs are on the Daily Exchange:

  • High Priority Cust Location – For urgent customer location details
  • Australia and New Zealand have agreed to strict parameters for urgent requests.
    You must select a reason from the drop down list. The reasons are:
    • Ministerial/Ombudsman
      Where the query relates to either a ministerial or ombudsman enquiry or complaint
    • Domestic Violence/Safety Risk
      Where there is a threat to the customer or others’ safety
    • Follow up from weekly req
      Where an initial enquiry has not been resolved from the weekly spreadsheet - CUBA will not allow the request to be made again
    • Internal Standards – NZ use only
      This is an enquiry that relates to an internal NZID performance measure and is only for NZ use
  • Article 5 and 7 notification – Child Support confirming Australian habitual residency of the payee and start dates where both the payee and payer have cases to determine which country must end
  • Article 5.2 Habitual Residency – Child Support is seeking to confirm a payee is habitually resident in New Zealand (NZIR) are to supply full payee contact and case details in response)
  • Miscellaneous Requests – used for any communication that does not fit into any of the other categories, and
  • Cases - Unable to register. This tab is for recording cases that cannot be registered

Other communication (Central Authority Service Officer) + Read more ...

Other ad-hoc emails may have to be sent to IR.NZ as not all staff have access to the NZ Secure Mailbox. Generally, the response will come from team leaders, senior management or Policy.

All emails from staff via the NZ Admin Unit Mailbox, or from NZIR via the NZ Secure Mailbox that fall outside the daily and weekly exchange will include either an officer’s name or the type of work in the subject heading.

Scrutinise each email according to the work type and who it is addressed to and either action it or forward to the:

  • Service Officer
  • Team Leader
  • Improvement Manager, or
  • Account Manager

If the email requires action or further follow up by another NZ Central Authority Service Officer or the NZ Quality Improvement Officer, mark it with that officer’s pre-determined coloured flag. Once the action is completed, mark it as completed by right clicking twice on the flag beside the email.

Table 3

1

Weekly Exchange Child Support to NZIR (Quality Improvement Officer) + Read more ...

Email the following spreadsheets to NZIR each Friday morning by 10am.

  • Income Requests – requests confirmation of incomes for both payers and payees
  • Determination of Habitual Residency (Customer Location/Pre-Transmittal requests) – requests for confirmation of payers’ habitual residency in New Zealand and other information to ensure Child Support have an accurate assessment
  • Transmittals/Registrations – requests for NZIR to register Child Support assessment information and commence collection or enforcement
  • General Withdrawals – confirmation to NZIR that collection is no longer required and the case is to be withdrawn and returned to Child Support
  • Amended certificates – advice regarding a customer’s change in circumstance and/or new liability amounts (only for cases that have already been transmitted to NZ for collection)
  • Potential Returns – request for new information for cases being considered as Non-Collectable or Non-Traceable
  • Returns (No Longer Habitually Resident, Customer Deceased, Non-Traceable and Non-Collectable) – Notification to NZIR that further collection cannot be made and case has been withdrawn
  • Enforcement Update – request update from NZIR on collection of a case transmitted to NZIR
  • Amended Certificate Requests – Child Support request NZIR send an amended certificate where they know a payer’s circumstances have changes (NZ In case)
  • General Withdrawal Request – Child Support request a general withdrawal where the payer (in Australia) has paid their Child Support debt in full

Each spreadsheet is saved to a specific folder in the Hobart shared drive folder Int/NZ/YYYY in the folder titled using the instructions below.

2

Income requests sent to New Zealand (NZ Out Cases) + Read more ...

Generate report

To request customer incomes, generate an Income request report in Cuba.

Go to the CSA Main Menu/Management/International Request Report.

In the International Request Find window, select the Income Request transaction type and choose the dates. As the report is sent each Friday, the dates will be the previous Friday to the current Friday.

Select the Excel icon on the top left of the screen to generate the Income Request Report as an Excel spreadsheet.

Save spreadsheet

Save the spreadsheet in the share drive G/Int/NZ/YYYY/PP NZ – OUTs/Incomes requested and name the spreadsheet 'Income Requests sent to NZ ddmmyy (include the number of actual requests).

Correct any obvious errors in the spreadsheet.

Note: when generating this report in Cuba save the report immediately. Once the report is generated, the data is erased from Cuba and cannot be recovered as a subsequent report.

Modify spreadsheet

Add 'Y' to each entry in column G 'Current Benefit Income Y/N', and add 'Date Benefit Started if Y'. Remove 'Paying Parents (PP) Name' from column F and replace with 'Customers Name'.

Where an Inland Revenue Department number (IRD) has not been populated on the spreadsheet, check the customer details in Cuba and include the customer’s DOB in the IRD field as: 'DOB DD/MM/YY'.

Save and close the spreadsheet and attach into the weekly exchange.

Income Requests for Ended Cases

If a Service Officer has to request an income on an ended case, they complete the template saved in G/Int/NZ/Income requests to NZ with the relevant data.

Copy and paste this information on to the bottom of the Income Request spreadsheet to NZIR for the current week.

3

Customer Location (Tracing/Pre-transmittal) requests to NZIR + Read more ...

Child Support requests customers’ residency details and associated information from NZIR. There is a limit of 70 customer location requests per week. Requests for other information do not form part of the limit of 70.

Generate report

To collate the tracing request information, generate a Tracing Request report in Cuba.

Go to the CSA Main Menu/Management/International Request Report.

In the International Request Find window, select the Tracing Request transaction type and choose the dates. As the report is sent each Friday, the dates will be the previous Friday to the current Friday.

Select the Excel icon on the top left of the screen. This will generate the Tracing Request Report as an Excel spreadsheet.

Save spreadsheet

Save the spreadsheet in the share drive G/Int/NZ/YYYY/PP NZ – OUTs/Tracing Sent and name the spreadsheet Tracing Requests sent to NZ ddmmyy (include the number of actual requests).

Correct any obvious errors in the spreadsheet.

Note: when generating this report in Cuba, save the report immediately. Once the report is generated, the data is erased from Cuba and cannot be recovered as a subsequent report.

Request second report

If there are 70 requests in the first report, generate another Tracing Request report (after saving the first one) for the same dates. Copy and paste any extra requests into a new spreadsheet. Save this spreadsheet in the share drive G/Int/NZ/YYYY/PP NZ – OUTs/Tracing Sent choosing the next Friday’s date. Ensure these are added to the following week’s spreadsheet.

Apply filter

Before sending the tracing request to New Zealand, identify each request that is an additional request for payee incomes. Apply a filter to identify blanks in the Primary Customer Surname column. The cases displayed should only be payee income requests. Update the additional comments column with 'RP income please'. Remove all future years of income from the sheet.

Re-format all dates in the spreadsheet as some may be in American format. To do this, highlight the first column of dates (date sent) select the 'Data' tab in excel and choose 'text to columns' option. Select 'next', 'next', change the selection to 'MDY' and select finish. Follow the same process for the data in the Primary Customer’s DOB and the Other Party’s DOB.

The Taxable income year of 2012 is duplicated so the 2nd 2012 year must be changed to 2013. The following years must be updated so they run in sequential order without any duplications

Add 2 columns to the end of the existing template and give them headings of “NZIR Comments” and "NZIR Positive / Negative Result".

4

Transmittals sent to NZIR (NZ Out cases) + Read more ...

A maximum of 35 transmittals can be sent to NZIR each week. These are sent via the secure mailbox on Friday as an attachment to the Weekly Exchange Out information transfer.

Create new spreadsheet

Create a new (blank) spreadsheet and open the ‘Transmittal OUT.xls’ spreadsheet from the share drive G/Int/NZ. Copy all of the data from the Transmittal Out spreadsheet and paste it into the newly created spreadsheet.

Save this spreadsheet in the share drive G/Int/NZ/YYYY/PP NZ – OUTs/Transmittals sent with the file name ‘Transmittals sent to NZ ddmmyy.xls (xx)’ include the number of transmittals included on the sheet in brackets after the date. Check there is no missing data in the IRD column as a case cannot be transmitted without this.

Modify spreadsheet

Correct any obvious errors in the spreadsheet such as keying errors in the actual exchange rate or the exchange rate & exchange rate date in the wrong columns. Ensure children’s last names are included. Send an email to the Service Officer identifying the customer, CSID and the error advising their transaction has been removed from the spreadsheet and they must re-submit the transmittal.

Delete any blank rows. Make a note of the number of transmittals to be sent thus far, remembering there is a maximum of 35 per week. If there are more than 35, only send the bottom 35 rows and save the excess separately to be sent the following week.

Apply the following formula to calculate the $NZ amount for the last ongoing monthly amount: '=' click on the mthly amount in $AUD, then '*' then click on the exchange rate. Hit enter. This will calculate the conversion from AUD to NZD. Copy the formula down the page, then copy and paste values into the same cells to over-ride the formula calculations, but retain the data. This ensures the figures will not be adjusted or compromised if any of the other data is changed. Save the spreadsheet.

Refresh master spreadsheet

Close the saved spreadsheet, which will return to the master workbook. In the master workbook highlight rows from row 3 down to the end of the data. Once the sheet is highlighted select ‘Edit’ from the menu tool bar, select ‘Clear’ and ‘Contents’. This clears the data being sent to NZ from the workbook.

Note: do not select Delete to remove the data as formulas are linked to row 3 which create the notepad for Cuba.

Save and Close the master workbook, which is refreshed, ready for staff to enter new transmittal data.

Transmittals from the NZ Hub are collated and added to the transmittal out sheet. Open the Hub. Select Pending/Sent, select transmittal, select export.

Copy and paste all transactions onto the previously saved transmittal out sheet and save again. Roll over cases to the following week’s sheet if there are more than 35 cases. Close the sheet generated by the Hub and find it’s saved location in G/Int/NZ and delete the file.

Roll over for a new month + Read more ...

On the first working day of a new month, change the dates in the headings of the master workbook for Amended Certificates (A/Certs) and Transmittals to the next month. This includes the ‘Payments Up To’ column.

The Senior Service Officer must ensure staff have not used the previous day’s exchange rate on the 1st day of a new month. This is critical if there is an ongoing liability

Where the end of the month occurs mid-week, separate the data in the spreadsheets sent in the following weekly exchange. Enter the data for the first month as usual. Copy the row containing the headings and insert below the last entry from the first month so the two batches of data have separate headings to indicate a change of months.

If the Arrears as at date changes during the last or first week of each month and where payments are applied to customer accounts (usually from the 22nd to the 25th of the month), copy the headings row again and make a third batch of data on this spreadsheet.

Payments received from NZIR

On approximately the 22nd of each month, payments from NZ are applied to paying parents accounts. Cases with newly applied payments need to be identified separately on the amended certificate spreadsheet to New Zealand. For example, cases processed that include payments from the schedule received in the previous month: “includes payments from your MM (previous month) payment schedule or cases processed that include payments from the schedule received in the current month: “includes payments from your MM (current month) payment schedule.
