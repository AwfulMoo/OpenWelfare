To start and complete an online claim + Read more ...

To start an online claim for CCS, customers must:

  • sign into myGov and access their linked Centrelink online account

select Payments and Claims > Claims > Make a Claim from the menu, then select Start a new claim on the Online Claims page

  • from the Make a claim page, select Get started under the Families category
  • Select Apply for Family Assistance (including Paid Parental Leave)
  • the Check your eligibility for Families page will be displayed. The customer must answer the streaming questions to check they are claiming the most appropriate payment based on their circumstances
  • Select 'Next' to continue the claim process

3

Claim options for CCS + Read more ...

The Claim Options page will display. Depending their circumstances, Child Care Subsidy can be claimed as follows:

  • CCS claim - to be used by CCP-CUR wishing to have CCS for an additional child(ren)
  • FTB + CCS claim
  • FTB + CCS + PPL claim

After selecting the claim option appropriate for their needs, customers will select Claim now.

At this point customers will be asked to check their details in My Profile and confirm the information is current. Customers are required to update their personal information as this cannot be updated as part of the CCS claim.

Once the claim is started, the claim will display:

  • Claim type
  • Claim status
  • Claim number
  • Instructions to 'Submit your claim as soon as possible to be paid from the earliest date'
  • Sections of the claim required to be completed

The Resources page contains a link to the Services Australia Website online claim online guide, and the non-digital claim form.

4

Online claim status + Read more ...

Incomplete claims require additional information from the customer and submission before they will be processed. Available Actions allow a customer to select Continue with their claim or Cancel their claim.

Submitted claims are claims waiting to be processed and cannot be edited by the customer. Customers can withdraw their claim by selecting the Withdraw link from the Available Actions table. Customers can also view their submitted claim by selecting View claim.

Cancelled claims are claims which have been cancelled by the customer before the claim has been submitted. Before the claim is cancelled customers are advised that:

  • after it is cancelled, this claim cannot be viewed or continued
  • this action cannot be undone

Completed claims have been processed. The claim outcome (Granted or Rejected) must be checked in Workspace as the Online or Assisted Customer Claim workflow status will only display as 'Approved'.

Withdrawn claims are claims which have been submitted by the customer but then withdrawn if the customer chose not to pursue the claim. Customers are advised before withdrawing their online claim that:

  • after it is withdrawn, this claim cannot be reinstated
  • they cannot apply for a formal review of this decision

This will cancel the claim activity and DOC the record. An MFU may be created if the claim withdrawal could not be completed. See Cancel or withdraw an online claim.

5

Customer contact - new claim progress + Read more ...

If a customer contacts to check the progress of their new claim, in Process Direct, Service Officers should:

  • search the customer’s details in Customer Summary
  • select the Transactions icon to view outstanding claims

If the claim has been finalised select the Child Care icon and advise the customer of the outcome of their claim.

If the claim has not been finalised:

  • Select the claim number hyperlink from the Transaction page which will take you to the claim page
  • Select the Tasks icon to check for any outstanding evidence or documentation. Advise the customer if any documents are still required
  • Select the Notes icon to view any Notes associated with the claim which may help inform the customer of the progress
  • If all information is available, Service Officers trained in CCS are to attempt to finalise the CCS claim at the time of contact

For Service officers not trained in CCS, or for customers who are not a family in crisis or a family experiencing financial hardship, Service Officers are to advise:
'Please be assured we are processing claims as quickly as possible. We will let you know the outcome as soon as it is complete. If you have been requested to provide information, it is important that you do so as soon as possible so as not to delay your claim. Thank you for your patience.'

Note: staff have no delegation to process Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) claims unless they are members of an ACCSPT (ACCS Processing Team). This includes combined CCS/ACCS claims where the ACCS component is embedded within the CCS claim There is no requirement to reallocate the work item as it will be mapped to appropriately trained staff. Advise the customer that the application will be processed at the earliest opportunity.

Requests for priority processing

CCS claims can be escalated for priority processing when the claim is actionable (i.e. all information and evidence has been provided), and the customer meets the definition of a family in crisis or a family experiencing financial hardship.

  • CCS only claim: Non processing Service Officers are to access the record in Customer First. Create a Fast Note select Auto Text use Families > Claims > Families Claim Escalation - Hardship to send a request for urgent processing:
    • use the service reason CCP
    • include a summary of why processing is required urgently, including how they meet the definition of in crisis or experiencing financial hardship
    • ensure telephone details are correct should the processing Service Officer need to contact them
  • CCS/ACCS combined claims and ACCS applications: All ACCS applications (if a combined CCS claim or a standalone application) meet the definition of financial hardship and are prioritised accordingly via Workload Manger, so no additional escalations are needed for this reason.
    If the customer presents multiple indicators of financial hardship, is in crisis or a repeat caller, the claim/application can be escalated to the ACCS Processing Team for priority processing.
    Service Officers are to access the record in Customer First. Create a Fast Note - select Auto Text use Families > Claims > ACCS Claim Escalation Urgent Processing:
    • ensure the service reason CCP is selected
    • include a summary of why processing is required urgently, including how they meet the definition of in crisis or experiencing financial hardship
    • ensure telephone details are correct should the ACCS Processing Team need to contact them

Note: for customers in crisis, also refer to Helping a customer in crisis or financial hardship claim family assistance and Identifying and offering services to customers in crisis or needing special help.

Customers should be encouraged to subscribe to Electronic Messaging in order to receive targeted messaging relating to their claim progress and advised to monitor their claim tracker in their online account or Express Plus Centrelink mobile app

To view claim details

The information provided by the customer in the online claim or ACC can be viewed by Service Officers. See Process Direct for information about how to access this.

Claim Tracker

When a customer lodges a claim online they are provided with an estimated date of completion. Whenever a customer accesses their Centrelink online account, they can monitor the progress of an outstanding CCS claim via a claim tracker which is displayed on the landing page.

Customer wishes to withdraw claim

If the customer wishes to withdraw their online claim, encourage them to withdraw their own claim via online services.

To withdraw a claim on behalf of a customer see Withdrawal of claims.

Rejected claim

When the claim has been rejected the customer must be provided an explanation of this decision. It is important the customer understands the reason their claim has been rejected.

There is no backdating provision if the customer provides the additional information to support their claim within 13 weeks of the date a decision was made to reject their claim. Customers will need to submit a new claim for CCS.

If, after a thorough explanation has been provided to the customer, they want to apply for a formal review of the decision, see Review of decision.

Not effective claims

Claims are effective if all the necessary information and/or evidence is provided at the time the claim is lodged. If a claim is not effective, no decision can be made about eligibility so a review of a decision cannot be requested. The claim is taken not to have been made and a new claim will need to be lodged.

Claim streamed out early as not eligible + Read more ...

Customers who begin the Child Care Subsidy claim process online are presented with a Check Your Eligibility question set which will result in the early identification of ineligibility.

If a customer contacts regarding their streamed out claim, they must be provided with an explanation based on their circumstances. It is important the customer understands the reason why they were streamed out and should be encouraged to explore their options via Payment and Service Finder.

For more information see Eligibility for Child Care Subsidy (CCS).

My Situation - specific questions for CCS - Child details + Read more ...

Any dependent children on the customer's existing record will be displayed with the message ‘updates required’. Customers must provide details for each child including:

  • Relationship to the child
  • If CCS is being claimed for the child

Customer is claiming CCS for the child, the following information will be required:

  • If the customer intends to use child care for the child within the next 26 weeks
    • No, they will receive a message that they will not be able to claim CCS for the child. They should claim closer to the time that the child will use child care
  • The same Australian residence question set and rules asked for the customer will be asked about the child
  • When the child came into the customer’s care
  • If the customer (or their partner) have, or have had, shared care of the child since the start of the current financial year
    • Yes, further details of the shared care arrangement will be requested on the Additional child details page
  • If the child has a disability
    • Yes, further details of the disability will be requested on the Additional child details page
  • If the child has been known by any other names
    • Yes, details of known names are to be supplied
  • Medicare details:
    • No, immunisation details will be included as documentation required when the claim is finished and the customer will not be able to submit the claim until Medicare details are provided
  • Child Care Service Enrolment details:
    • Initial enrolment details will be submitted by the child care service. Customers must confirm information is correct. If any details are incorrect, this will be flagged with the child care service and the customer will need to speak with their child care service to request an updated enrolment notice be submitted
    • If enrolment details have not yet been submitted by the child care service, it will present to the customer as an action item once it is received
      Note: CCS payments cannot be made until enrolment has been confirmed.
  • Child Study details:
    • Options are ‘Not yet started school’, ‘Primary Education’, ‘Secondary education’ or ‘Exempted by a state or territory education authority’
    • Depending on the child’s age, customers will be required to enter the expected start date for primary or secondary school
    • If exemption is requested, full details will need to be provided

Note: from 27 June 2022, where a customer is partnered, and does not meet care requirements, the system will automatically check if their current partner has the child/ren coded in their care (ICC) with 14% care or more.

Customer is not claiming CCS for a child, only the Australian residence question set and rules asked for the customer will be asked about the child.

If there are other dependent children to be added or have left care, or recently died, the customer must provide full details for each child.

3

My Situation - specific questions for CCS - Activity Test + Read more ...

Customers (and their partner) must provide details of their recognised activity/ies to determine the hours of subsidised child care they are entitled to receive.

  • Are you in paid work?
    • Yes, and not in casual work the number of hours per fortnight are to be recorded
    • Yes, and the work is casual, the highest number of hours the customer is expected to work per fortnight over the next 3 months is to be used
  • Date started work
  • Are you doing other activities?
    • Yes, a list of other activities will present. Customers need to include the number of hours and the start date for each activity they are engaged in
  • Other activities
    • Yes, to performing any other activity that maintains or improves their work skills or employment prospects, customers are to include the hours, start date and details of the activity being undertaken
  • About your other circumstances
    • Yes, to disability preventing engagement in an activity, in prison or psychiatric confinement, the customer will be exempt from the Activity Test

The same question sets and rules asked for the customer will be asked about the partner (if they have one).

5

My Finances - Tax details + Read more ...

A Tax File Number (TFN) is required for the customer (and their partner, if applicable), unless previously provided.

If the customer is unable to provide a TFN for themselves and/or their partner or former partner, they will not be able to progress with their online claim.

For more information, see Requesting a Tax File Number (TFN).

TFN exemptions for partners or former partners

A customer may be exempt from providing their partner’s or ex-partner’s TFN in circumstances such as:

  • the former partner has refused to provide details
  • being unable to contact the former partner due to special circumstances (e.g. domestic violence), or
  • the partner or former partner are temporarily uncontactable

Customers will need to contact the agency to request a TFN exemption, and the claim can be finalised via an ACC, with TFN being referred to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk for assessment. See Exemption from providing Tax File Numbers when claiming Child Care Subsidy (CCS).

6

Annual income estimate + Read more ...

Customers must provide a reasonable estimate of their (and their partner's) Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) for the full financial year.

Depending on when the claim is being made, customers may be required to provide an estimate for two financial years.

The Income period selection page will display, and all income periods requiring an estimate will display as ‘updates required’.

Taxable income includes any taxable government payments. Customers not required to lodge a tax return must still include any taxable income they expect to receive for the year.

For all other ATI components, an estimated amount is only requested if the customer indicates they will receive other components and is not requested if they indicate they expect it will be nil.

CCS and ACCS processing support and escalation process

Check existing resources

When a Process Direct issue is encountered, staff must refer to existing resources prior to escalating:

  • Digital Assistant (Roxy) for automated response
  • Operational Blueprint and training resources
  • Operational Messaging
  • Network News Updates
  • Child Care Subsidy Technical Support SharePoint resources
  • Child Care Subsidy Subject Matter Expert (SME)

Escalation

If the CCS Subject Matter Expert (SME) identifies that ICT analysis is required, and the issue is encountered within a CCS Claim or an ACCS activity:

  • Follow the DA Roxy V2 Task card and raise the incident via the Process Direct Digital Assistant (Roxy)
  • When raising the incident, ensure the following information is included in the referral:
    • Exact error message presented
    • What action was being performed (for example, when/where did the error occur?)
    • Current outcome
    • Expected outcome
    • Action required by ICT
    • What has been checked
    • Steps taken to resolve
    • If the error could be fixed and whether or not the claim could be completed
    • Previous SD/IM numbers relevant to the claim (from ICT Interaction Viewer)
    • Any other relevant information, including SME consulted
    • Complete a note in Process Direct with the following text 'referred to ICT on dd/mm/yyyy’, in addition to the information included in the referral
    • Ensure the claim is assigned to the logon of the employee responsible and the work item is placed on Hold

When the activity is a Change of Circumstances or if Roxy is not available, staff must escalate using the Child Care Subsidy and Additional Child Care Subsidy Issues webform via mySupport. The Resources page contains a link to mySupport. If the escalation relates to a claim activity, the referral must include the reason Roxy was not used to raise the incident.

ICT Referrals that do not contain sufficient information will be returned un-actioned.

Urgent cases

In addition to the escalation process above, Service Officers should determine if one or more of the following circumstances are applicable:

  • Ombudsman, Ministerial, Member of Parliament (MP), Media and executive enquiry
  • Escalated complaint (Level 2 Customer Relations Unit). See Level 2 complaints for a definition
  • A family in crisis or a family experiencing financial hardship

If any of the above criteria are met, once the issue has been escalated via Roxy or mySupport, contact the ICT Service Desk to escalate. See Resources for a link to an article on how to do this.

Tell us about your finances - Tax details + Read more ...

A Tax File Number (TFN) is required for the customer (and their partner, if applicable), unless previously provided.

The Tax Details page in ACC can be bypassed by selecting the I do not have or know my (or partner’s if applicable) Number checkbox. The Service Officer can continue the claim to submission.

Note: when bypassing the Tax Details page:

  • This checkbox must only be selected if the customer advises they will provide their or their partner’s TFN within 14 days or they have requested an exemption from providing their partner or former partner’s TFN
  • If the customer knows they have a Tax File Number, but are unaware of the number, direct them to contact the Australia Taxation Office (ATO) on the personal taxation enquire line 132 861
  • If TFN (and bank account details) are the only outstanding information - advise the customer their claim will not be assessed until they provide their TFN. If not provided within 28 days, their claim will be made not effective and they will need to lodge a new claim.
  • If other documents are required to support the claim - advise the customer their claim will not be submitted until all required documents have been provided. If at that time the customer is still unable to provide TFN details, the ACC can be submitted however it will not be assessed as outlined above

For more information, see Requesting a Tax File Number (TFN).

TFN exemptions for partners or former partners

A customer may be exempt from providing their partner’s or ex-partner’s TFN if:

  • they have asked the former partner and they have refused to provide details
  • they are unable to contact the former partner due to special circumstances (e.g. domestic violence, imprisonment etc.), or
  • the partner or former partner are temporarily uncontactable

For more information see Exemption from providing Tax File Numbers when claiming Child Care Subsidy (CCS).

Tell us about your finances - Income estimate + Read more ...

Customers claiming CCS must provide a reasonable estimate of their (and their partner's) Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) for the full financial year, even if only receiving an income support payment.

Depending on when the claim is being made, customers may be required to provide an estimate for 2 financial years.

The Income period selection page will display, and all income periods requiring an estimate will display as ‘updates required’.

Taxable income includes any taxable government payments. Customers not required to lodge a tax return must still include any taxable income they expect to receive for the year.

Ask the customer if they have any income from the other ATI components. A '0' income must be recorded if the customer advised they expect to have no income from these sources.
