Override Eligibility + Read more ...

Participants who are ineligible for the reasons switched issuers in the last 60 days or switched issuers more than four times in the past year may be eligible for an override if there was an administrative error or if an ‘exceptional circumstance’ exists.

Click the View card issuer history button to view the historical data.

Have an open discussion with the participant to determine if an exceptional circumstance exists. Noting a change of mind is not always considered an exceptional circumstance.

Does an exceptional circumstance exist?

  • Yes, select ‘Yes’ to the Override Eligibility question and ‘Exceptional circumstance’ for the Override Reason. Go to Step 11
  • No, select ‘No’ to the Override Eligibility question. Procedure ends here

8

The participant is ineligible for reasons ‘not on CDC’ or ‘no issuer available for customer to switch’ + Read more ...

Before determining whether eligibility is able to be overridden, cancel the ‘Cashless Debit Card Switch Card Issuer’ workflow and investigate the record to determine the reason for ineligibility.

For participants in their initial waiting period:

  • Check the Q2AS screen in Customer First to confirm the customer is in their waiting period, and
  • Have an open discussion with the participant to determine if an exceptional circumstance exists. (Noting a change of mind is not always considered as an exceptional circumstance)

If a participant’s reason is determined to be valid to switch issuers in their initial waiting period, they will need to request to bring their CDC start date forward, (a switch cannot be processed during the initial waiting period as the participant must have started on CDC).

Does the participant want to proceed?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, advise the customer to recontact after their CDC start date. Procedure ends here.

12

Service Officer + Read more ...

The role of the Service Officer is to provide the participant with further information about what is involved in switching card issuers for the CDC. The Service Officer is required to have a sound understanding of the key differences between card issuers and the CDC program, and be able to explain these to the participant.

The Service Officer must not provide advice or a recommendation to a participant as to whether or not they should switch card issuers. Only factual information about the differences and the switch card issuer process should be provided.

See the Card Issuer Choice Document in Resources for support when having the discussion.

The following topics will be covered in the Switch Card issuer workflow :

  • future eligibility to switch
  • key similarities and differences between CDC card issuers
  • ceasing deductions with current issuer and setting up deductions under your new Cashless Debit Card issuer
  • commencing and Activating new personalised Cashless Debit Card accounts
  • disbursement of Cashless Debit Card funds
  • receiving and activating the personalised Cashless Debit Card
  • consent to switch issuers

Before finalising a switch request, participants must make an informed decision and provide consent..

If, at any stage, the customer does not want to proceed with switching card issuers please exit the workflow using the back arrow in Process Direct (not the browser).

Go to Step 13.

13

Switch card issuer discussion + Read more ...

Ensure the participant understands they cannot switch card issuers again for 60 days, and they can only switch card issuers four times every 12 months.

14

Key similarities and differences between Cashless Debit Card issuers + Read more ...

Using the Card Issuer Choice Document in Resources discuss what will change for the participant when they switch card issuers.

15

Ceasing and commencing deductions (payments from CDC account) + Read more ...

Once a participant switches card issuers, they will be responsible for ceasing any regular payments with their losing card issuer. Services Australia will stop directing payments to the account with the losing card issuer after the gaining card issuer's account has been activated.

Participants will be responsible for setting up regular payments with the gaining card issuer.

Note:

  • Centrepay deductions and Rent Deduction Scheme payments with NT Housing will continue after switching card issuers
  • Housing and Other Expense limits will transfer to the gaining issuer, participants will not need to reapply for these again

16

Commencing and activating the new CDC account + Read more ...

Once a participant consents to switch cards issuers, the gaining card issuer will mail the participant their personalised card and information on how to use it.

If the participant has a payment nominee, the personalised card and information on how to use it will be mailed to the payment nominee.

If the participant or payment nominee would like to commence with the gaining card issuer prior to receiving and activating their personalised card, they can attend selected Centrelink service centres and be issued a temporary card which will be activated.

They can also attend participating Agent sites, pick up a temporary card and contact their gaining card issuer to activate the card. See Service centres and Agents stocking temporary Cashless Debit Cards (CDC) for a full list of stockists.

The participant’s payments will continue to be paid to the losing issuer’s CDC account until:

  • a personalised or temporary card is activated with the gaining issuer, or
  • if the card is not activated within 56 days, payments will automatically be paid to the account of the gaining card issuer, from day 57. The losing card issuer account will be automatically closed after 90 days

17

Disbursement of Cashless Debit Card funds + Read more ...

If a participant switches card issuers and has money left in the losing issuer's CDC account, they can still access this money for up to 60 days.

If the participant needs a temporary card to access this money they can visit selected Centrelink service centres, participating Agent sites or card issuer branch (TCU only) to obtain one.

After 60 days, any money left over or interest earned will automatically be sent to the CDC account with the gaining card issuer.

18

Receiving and activating the personalised Cashless Debit Card + Read more ...

The card issuer will send the participant a personalised cashless debit card and information on how to activate it.

If the participant has a payment nominee, the new personalised cashless debit card and information on how to use it will be mailed to their payment nominee.

Cards can be activated:

  • online through the financial institution’s website
  • through the financial institution’s mobile App, which can be downloaded from the App Store or Google Play
  • by phoning the financial institution
  • in person at a TCU branch (only TCU customers)
  • visiting a Service Centre
  • getting help from a Remote Servicing Team

Conditions of use for Cashless Debit Cards

  • If the participant choses Indue, they will be mailed a copy of Indue’s Conditions of Use together with a personalised cashless debit card. The participant can also access a copy from Indue’s website.
  • If the participant choses TCU, they can access a copy of TCU’s Conditions of Use from their website or Services Australia’s website

 

19

Switch card issuer consent + Read more ...

If the participant wants to proceed, and understands they are not able to switch card issuers again for 60 days, and they can only switch card issuers a total of four times every 12 months, obtain their consent to switch card issuers and select the gaining card issuer from the drop down menu and finalise the activity.

To assist the participant to activate their card or access online services, provide the participant with their new pending CDC BSB and account number. To view these details:

  • for Customer First, go to Workspace > Cashless Debit Card > Card Details/History
  • for Process Direct, go to the Account History screen via CDCAH

Procedure ends here.
