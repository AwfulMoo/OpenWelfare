Initial referral and intent to suspend for Centrepay

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Referral to the Business Online and Assurance Team (BOAT) + Read more ...

If a business is contactable, but does not participate in a Centrepay assurance review, the Third Party Programme (TPP) Team may send an intent to suspend letter using standard letter text. If the business fails to respond to this letter, a referral should be made to Deduction and Confirmation Branch - Business Online and Assurance Team.

In all other circumstances, if a Centrepay business is non-compliant and suspension action is required, email a referral to the Deduction and Confirmation Branch - Business Online and Assurance Team.

The referral must include details of the non-compliance and list the clause/s of the Centrepay Policy and Terms or Centrepay Framework it has breached.

The Business Online and Assurance Team (BOAT) will make a determination:

  • if suspension action is required
  • the type of suspension applied, and
  • seek approval at the appropriate delegation to suspend a Centrepay business

BOAT will either make a determination to send an intent to suspend letter or apply an immediate suspension.

Is the decision from BOAT to initiate a suspension?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, BOAT will provide details of required action, procedure ends here

2

Initiate suspension + Read more ...

  • If intent to suspend letter is to be sent to the business, go to Step 3
  • If an immediate suspension is being applied:
    • Partial suspension, See Table 2
    • Full suspension, See Table 3

3

Letter of intent to suspend + Read more ...

BOAT will engage TPP to progress the intent to suspend. BOAT will email TPP the decision details and the intent to suspend letter text which will include:

  • any remedial actions the business is required to take to avoid the suspension, and
  • the due date for these remedial actions

4

Program Support Manager sends intent to suspend letter to business + Read more ...

To send the letter to the business:

  • complete, sign and scan the letter to the business record
  • email the signed letter directly to the authorised officer for Centrepay
  • call the business contact and verbally confirm receipt of the email
  • post the signed copy of the letter on the same day, and
  • set up a team calendar reminder for the response due date (this is to check if a response has been provided or if an extension has been requested)
  • notify BOAT when the above has been completed and advise of any updates or changes from the business

If the business requests an extension, go to Step 5

If the business does not request an extension, did it comply with the required actions by the due date?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No. go to Step 7

5

Business requests an extension of time to comply before a suspension is imposed + Read more ...

If the business requests an extension of time to comply before the suspension is imposed, it will need to do so in writing to the Program Support Manager stating:

  • its willingness to comply, and
  • the reason why it has been unable to do so within the initial timeframe provided

A Program Support Manager can approve extensions. TPP may seek additional guidance from BOAT if needed.

For extension requests, factors such as, but not limited to, the following should be considered:

  • is the timeframe reasonable for the extension requested?
  • is the reason the business is requesting an extension reasonable?
  • has a previous extension been granted?
  • number of customers impacted

TPP must email the business with the outcome of the request and if approved, the new due date.

  • DOC the business’s record with the decision.
  • Ensure all relevant communication is uploaded to the record
  • Update the team calendar reminder with the new due date (to check if a response has been provided)

Does the business comply with required actions by the due date?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Business complies with required actions by the due date (TPP) + Read more ...

  • Ensure the response from the business is provided in writing
  • A Program Support Manager should determine if the response from the business meets the required remedial actions
  • The TPP team may seek additional guidance from BOAT via email referral if it is unclear whether the business has satisfactorily complied with all required actions.

If the intent to suspend action is ceased, send an email to the business advising that no further action is required and no further action will be taken by the agency.

  • DOC the business record with the outcome
  • Upload any evidence provided by the business
  • Procedure ends here

7

Business does not comply with required actions by the due date + Read more ...

If the business fails to comply with the required actions by the due date, or it is unclear if the business has satisfactorily complied with all remedial actions, email a referral to BOAT

BOAT will confirm if a suspension will be applied, and if so, the type of suspension:

  • Partial suspension, see Table 2
  • Full suspension, see Table 3

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Actioning a partial suspension + Read more ...

Send an email to the following teams advising of the action required (select as appropriate):

2

Actioning a partial suspension - (BDT and TPP teams) + Read more ...

Business Design Team (BDT) action

Complete the following actions:

  • add the following message to the business record:
    • 'This business is currently suspended from adding new or increasing existing Centrepay deductions. Centrepay deductions can still be cancelled, suspended or varied down'
  • prevent new Centrepay deductions (to this business) from being added by the customer or the business
  • prevent the business from increasing existing Centrepay deductions or their target amounts
  • ensure existing deductions can be varied down, cancelled or have their target amounts varied down by the business
  • undertake action to ensure the business is not viewable online via Find an Organisation
  • notify BOAT and the TPP team via email when the above actions are complete

For contact details, see the Resources page.

Third Party Programmes (TPP) Team action

When BDT advises their actions have been completed, complete the following:

  • complete, sign and scan the partial suspension letter to the business record
  • email signed letter to the authorised officer for Centrepay
  • call the business contact and verbally confirm receipt of the email
  • post the signed copy of the letter on the same day
  • set up a team calendar reminder for the response due date, (this is to check if a response has been provided or if an extension has been requested)
  • update the business’s Centrepay contract status to ‘Suspended’ in the system. This will prevent the business from appearing in online searches
  • DOC the record advising the reason for suspension and due date for remedial action
  • notify BOAT via email when the above actions are complete

For contact details, see the Resources page.

Has the business complied with the requested remedial action by the due date? + Read more ...

When a suspension is applied, the business is usually required to complete remedial actions by a due date. If they do not complete remedial action, they may have their Centrepay approval withdrawn.

Program Support Manager:

  • Assess the business’s response (or failure to respond) and ensure that all responses are received in writing
  • Record the response (or failure to respond) on the business’s record
  • Email the outcome to BOAT and provide recommendations

Business Online and Assurance Team:

If the business has provided a satisfactory response and complied with the requested remedial actions, the suspension can be lifted, See Table 4.

If the business has not complied with the requested remedial actions, see Withdrawing a business’ approval to use a deduction and confirmation service

Actioning a full suspension - TPP, BDT and NBG + Read more ...

BDT action

Complete the following actions:

  • prevent new Centrepay deductions (to this business) from being added by the customer, agency staff or the business
  • restrict the ability for the business to manage Centrepay deductions through DBUS and DAPA so that they will have view access only
  • add the following message to the business record:
    • ‘Do not add any Centrepay deductions to this business. This business is currently suspended from using Centrepay'
  • undertake action to ensure the business is not viewable online via Find an Organisation
  • notify BOAT and TTP when above actions are complete

For contact details, see the Resources page.

TPP action

When BDT advise their actions have been completed, complete the following:

  • complete, sign and scan the full suspension letter to the business record
  • email signed letter to the authorised officer for Centrepay
  • call the business contact and verbally confirm receipt of the email
  • post the signed copy of the letter on the same day
  • set up a team calendar reminder for the response due date, (to check if a response has been provided or if an extension has been requested)
  • update the business’s Centrepay contract status to ‘Suspended’ in the system. This will prevent the business from appearing in online searches
  • DOC the record advising the reason for suspension and due date for remedial action
  • notify BOAT via email when the above actions are complete

For contact details, see the Resources page.

NBG action

Complete the following actions:

  • use the Centrepay workflow in Customer First, to cancel each customer’s Centrepay deduction to this business
  • inhibit the system letter that is generated
  • DOC the customer’s record with text supplied by BOAT
  • populate the manual letter template with each individual customer’s details and send to each customer
  • notify BOAT when the above actions are complete

For contact details, see the Resources page.

Removing a Centrepay suspension

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Removing a partial or full suspension + Read more ...

Business Online and Assurance Team (BOAT) is responsible for decisions to remove a Centrepay suspension and restore Centrepay approval.

If BOAT determines the suspension is to be removed, the Third Party Programme (TTP) Team and the Business Design Team (BDT) can be engaged to lift the suspension.

BOAT is responsible for supplying TPP with a ‘Suspension removed’ letter template and text.

2

Removing a partial or full suspension (BDT and TPP teams) + Read more ...

BDT Action:

  • Remove all Centrepay suspension restrictions previously applied by BDT (this may include DBUS, DAPA and RDF)
  • Remove any business messaging
  • Notify BOAT and TPP once the action is complete

Program Support Manager Action (after BDT remove restrictions):

  • Update contract status to ‘Approved’
  • Call the authorised officer and verbally confirm the suspension has ceased
  • Complete and issue the ‘Suspension removed’ letter to the business
  • Post the copy of the letter on the same day
  • DOC the business record and upload any relevant documents
  • Notify BOAT once the action is complete

Suspend access for Income Management, BasicsCard, Electronic Verification of Rent and Centrelink Confirmation eServices

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Referral to Business Online and Assurance Team (BOAT) + Read more ...

Approval and advice must be sought from the Deduction and Confirmation Branch - Business Online and Assurance Team before suspending a business's access to:

  • Income Management (IM)
  • BasicsCard
  • Electronic Verification of Rent (EVoR)
  • Centrelink Confirmation eServices (CCeS)

If suspension action is required, email a referral to BOAT. The referral should include:

  • details of the non-compliance (or other issue)
  • list the clause/s of the Policy, Terms or Framework the business has breached
  • any cross program implications

BOAT will make a determination if:

  • suspension action is required
  • the type of suspension applied, and
  • seek approval at the appropriate delegation to suspend the business

Has BOAT approved a suspension?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, BOAT will provide details of required action, procedure ends here

2

Implementing an IM, BasicsCard, EVoR or CCeS suspension (BOAT team) + Read more ...

BOAT will email details of the decision to suspend a business’s access to relevant stakeholders which may include:

  • Third Party Programmes Team (TPP)
  • Business Design Team (BDT)
  • National Business Gateway (NBG)
  • Welfare Quarantining Programme Management Team
  • Business Online and Assurance Team - CCeS, EVoR and RDS
  • BOAT Director

BOAT will contact BDT, NBG and TPP as appropriate via email to implement the suspension. BOAT will supply the suspension letter text and the actions required by each team. These actions may include:

Business Design Team (BDT)

  • Add the following message to the business record:
    • ‘This business is currently suspended from adding new or increasing existing Income Management deductions. Income Management deductions can still be cancelled, suspended or varied down'
  • Suspend the business’s access to:
    • IM
    • BasicsCard
    • EVoR
    • CCeS
  • Advise BOAT and TPP via email when actions have been completed

For contact details, see the Resources page.

Third Party Programme (TPP) team

  • Complete, sign and scan the suspension letter to the business record
  • Email the signed letter to the authorised officer
  • Call the business contact and verbally confirm receipt of the email
  • Post the signed copy of the letter on the same day
  • Update the business’s IM, BasicsCard, EVoR or CCeS contract status to ‘Suspended’ in the system. This will prevent the business from appearing in online searches
  • DOC the record advising the reason for suspension
  • Set up a team calendar reminder for the due date (to check if a response has been provided or an extension has been requested)
  • Advise BOAT via email when actions have been completed

For contact details, see the Resources page.

Note: if all existing deductions are to be ceased for an IM suspension, BOAT will arrange for this to occur.

3

Has the business complied with the requested remedial action by the due date? + Read more ...

When a suspension is applied, the business is usually required to complete remedial actions by a due date. If they do not, they may have their approval to use IM, BasicsCard, EVoR or CCeS withdrawn.

In all instances, BOAT must provide approval before a suspension is lifted and a business’s access is restored.

Program Support Manager

  • Assess the business’s response (or failure to respond) and ensure that all responses are received in writing
  • DOC the business’s record and upload relevant communication with the business
  • Refer the outcome to BOAT with recommendations

Business Online and Assurance Team (BOAT):

If the business has responded satisfactorily and complied with the requested remedial actions the suspension can be lifted. Engage BDT and TPP via email to cease the suspension and restore the business’s access.

If the issues that initiated the suspension are not able to be resolved, BOAT will determine if the business’s approval to use IM, BasicsCard, EVoR or CCeS is to be withdrawn.

If the business is to be withdrawn, see Withdrawing a business’s approval to use a deduction and confirmation service.

Appeal of suspension decision - Centrepay program only

Table 6

Step

Action

1

Appeal of decision + Read more ...

A request to appeal a decision must be made in writing within 10 business days of the business being notified of the decision to suspend a Business's approval to use Centrepay.

The request must include:

  • the reasons for seeking the review
  • any new details and evidence to support the request

The Original Decision Maker (ODM), taking into account any new details, should immediately review the decision. Staff with the appropriate delegation can vary a decision as the ODM if the business provides new or more details that change the decision. Referral to the Appeals branch is not required.

If the ODM does not vary their decision, refer to the Appeals Branch, go to Step 2

2

Refer to the Appeals Branch + Read more ...

If the original decision is not varied, an independent review of the decision is needed.

Send a request by email with all supporting documents to the Centrepay Appeals Team within 3 business days of the request being received. Scan all supporting documents to the business record.

Email should include the following details:

  • Background summary - for example:
    • dd/mm/yy - date of suspension and <reason for suspension>
    • Type of suspension
    • Timeline of events (include date/event)
    • dd/mm/yy - business lodged appeal & reason/s for lodging the appeal
  • ODM details - name, position, delegation level
  • Attachments - list all documents attached to email
  • Due date - <Request date + 20 business days>

Requests for more details may be received from the Centrepay Appeals Team.

The Centrepay Appeals Team will advise if the original decision is being varied or upheld.

If the decision is upheld, continue with the original decision to suspend the business’s approval to use Centrepay.

The Appeals Team will send the letters advising of the decision.
