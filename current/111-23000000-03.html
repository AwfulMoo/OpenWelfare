Procedures for all staff members

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Check for returned activities + Read more ...

Use QOL tools to check for returned activities.

If staff see OFFLINE or QOLABT activities in Work Optimiser, action must be taken for:

  • OFFLINE activities, go to Step 2
  • QOLABT activities, go to Step 3
  • Returned Activities and/or view feedback, go to Step 4

For more information, see Work Optimiser for Staff.

2

Check for OFFLINE activities + Read more ...

The system can revert activities to offline, which will result in a non-finalised (offline) status irrespective of the quality check being finalised in the QOL tool.

There are 2 reasons for activities going offline:

  • The activity is checked in the QOLCheck tool but went offline before completing in mainframe
  • The Daily Driver has reverted the activity status back to started (STA) after it was submitted (SUB) but before being checked in the QOLCheck tool

Offline activities can be located through the QOLCheck tool or by doing a search in Work Optimiser using the keyword offline.

Note: only staff with Work Optimiser Analyst access can check for offline activities for other staff. See Work Optimiser for Team Leaders and Analysts.

Action required for OFFLINE activities

OFFLINE activities must be actioned as the original coding/update would not have applied to the customer's record upon the activity going offline.

Before actioning the activity, staff must check the QOLCheck tool to confirm if the activity has already been QOL checked:

  • If the QOL has been completed, staff should check for feedback to ensure all feedback is addressed prior to actioning the activity
  • If the QOL has not been completed, staff will need to wait until the original activity has been QOL checked, to ensure any feedback is address before the offline activity is actioned

After QOL feedback has been addressed or where there is no QOL feedback, action the OFFLINE activity:

  • Undo the OFFLINE activity from the Activity List (AL) screen:
    • To undo the activity in Customer First, go to the AL screen and select the (U) Undo submitted activity not passed in QOL button
    • To undo the activity in Customer Record enter 'U' next to the activity on the AL screen and press [Enter]
  • Ensure the relevant coding/updates are applying within the activity
  • Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Record details in a DOC, if applicable

Note: these activities can be selected for QOL checking a second time and must be fully checked for further updates to the activity prior to it being resubmitted.

3

Action QOLABT activities + Read more ...

All QOLABT activities must be referred to ICT using the Quality Online (QOL) Function Issues form in mySupport.

Staff will need to complete the appropriate action as advised by ICT.

4

Guidelines for actioning feedback reports + Read more ...

Staff must:

  • run QOLStat ‘Step Feedback and Result Feedback reports daily
  • ensure all feedback for both released and returned activities is actioned on a daily basis

Staff should action their feedback themselves, however if assistance is needed staff must follow local procedures.

For returned activities, go to Step 5.

5

Returned activities + Read more ...

Returned activities must be reverted to a STA (started) status to enable correction to occur.

To undo a submitted activity:

  • In Customer First, select the activity from the Activity List (AL) screen then select the 'U' (Undo submitted activity not passed in QOL)and continue
  • To undo the activity in Customer Record, enter ‘U’ next to the activity on the AL screen and press [Enter]

6

Review of QOL decisions + Read more ...

Staff can request a review of a QOL check decision under the QOL and QMA Dispute Escalation Policy. Local procedures supporting the policy must be followed. See the Resources page for a link.

There is no:

  • functionality to alter the outcome of a QOL check within the QOL tool
  • national report to record the altered outcome of a changed QOL check

If a QOL decision is overturned as a result of a QOL dispute, Line Managers and/or SSO's should:

  • account for these results when manually assessing the staff member's accuracy results, and
  • adjust proficiency levels accordingly where applicable

Staff and Line Managers/SSO's must keep records of dispute outcomes for audit purposes, while also supporting learning and development discussions.

Urgent QOL guidelines

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Urgent QOL criteria + Read more ...

Urgent QOL requests are limited to these activities:

  • Bereavement Payment (LBP)
  • urgent payment/Crisis Payment, or an activity preventing an Urgent/Crisis payment from being assessed
  • the activity is rate affecting for that day’s Entitlement Period End Day (EPED)
  • the activity needs to be finalised so the customer can immediately access updated information related to their service/payment
  • the customer needs to access an Income Statement with accurate details before the end of the day. For example, they need it for a medical appointment or an emergency assistance referral
  • it is a concession card refresh and the customer needs to use their card on the same day. For example, doctor or pharmacy
  • the activity is stopping another non-urgent activity from being started (STA) on the Activity List (AL) screen. The non-urgent activity will be completed after QOL checking
  • the activity is preventing a second activity from being completed on the customer's record. The second activity is rate affecting for that day's EPED
  • the activity is preventing a new claim being finalised
  • the activity is a new claim activity and the customer is in severe financial hardship
  • the activity is preventing the customer from using self service
  • the customer is vulnerable or affected by family and domestic violence

Staff should complete activities in a method/order that reduces the need for urgent QOL requests.

2

Requesting urgent QOL assistance + Read more ...

For urgent QOL assistance:

  • Smart Centres, see Urgent QOL process for Smart Centre and Labour Hire staff
  • Face to Face and boutique teams, follow local procedures
  • Compliance and Debt Operations (CDO) activities:
    • CDO staff, contact local Quality Development Officer (QDO) for help
    • Non-CDO staff, contact the Team Leader of the staff member who submitted the activity

Guidelines for QOL checkers

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Expectations of QOL checkers + Read more ...

QOL checkers will:

  • undertake QOL checks with a consistent assessment of activities in accordance with Operational Blueprint and the National QOL Principles
  • take responsibility for completing the check of the selected activity
  • utilise local escalation protocols to complete the check if the activity is beyond the QOL checker's area of expertise
  • select only 1 activity at a time and complete the QOL check before moving onto another activity. Note: customer and partner activities can be selected at the same time where the QOL checks are related
  • not override discretionary decisions unless the relevant legislation, policy or procedure does not allow discretion
  • not use the ‘Return for correction and rechecking’ option for any activity unless instructed to do so
  • not override the outcome of the QOL check (e.g. if the QOL outcome is to 'release' and no errors were selected within the QOL check, do not override the 'release' outcome)
  • give appropriate, consistent and constructive feedback

Refer to the relevant Operational Blueprint or intranet site for local procedures for managing QOL activities.

2

Allocation of work and prioritising by QOL checkers + Read more ...

QOL checkers must prioritise their work in the following order:

  • payment affecting activities (EAN, IPI, STM)
  • activities for today's Entitlement Period End Day (EPED)
  • activities for the last 2 EPEDs
  • other activities by environment, working from the oldest

Be mindful of payment strip times in each state.

Refer to the relevant Operational Blueprint or intranet site for local procedures for managing QOL activities.

3

Checking the activity + Read more ...

Instructions on how to use the QOLCheck tool are in the QOLCheck and QOLStat Guide. See the Resources page for links to the guides.

  • When completing a QOL check the questions are presented in the QOLCheck tool as a:
    • Step question relating to a specific screen, or
    • Transaction question applied to the overall check
  • A feedback box will present when an error has been identified and must be completed before continuing the QOL check. Feedback should include references to the relevant Operational Blueprint files and policy/legislation, where applicable
  • QOLCheck does not replace reviewing updated screens in Customer First/Customer Record when completing checks
  • QOL checkers are responsible for completing the check end to end
  • If the activity is beyond the QOL checker's area of expertise they should utilise local escalation protocols to ensure the activity is completed
  • The method which an activity is completed should not impact the result of the QOL check if the correct outcome is achieved, i.e. workflow versus manual coding

For details about applying step question options for the Accommodation Details (AC) screen, search QOL tool in the A-Z of Rent Assistance terms and codes.

Before finalising the QOL check:

  • The QOLCheck tool will list the errors based on the responses chosen
  • The QOL checker should access Customer First and ensure there is no lock on the record
  • If the customer’s payment should have been stimulated within the activity, the QOL checker must do this once the QOL check has been completed

QOL checkers must ensure all checks they are responsible for are cleared by the close of the business day.

4

Activities left in the QOL tool from the previous day + Read more ...

Where a payment affecting activity is identified in the QOL tool from the previous day, the QOL checker must:

  • contact the customer
  • explain the delay in payment
  • apologise
  • offer to deliver the payment via Immediate methods of payment if required
  • document the record with the above discussion

5

Feedback standards + Read more ...

When a correction is needed, feedback must be provided in the QOLCheck tool.

The Quality Checking Feedback Policy gives the minimum standards expected when giving feedback for QOL.

Feedback must be clear, concise and have:

  • a description of the error
  • a description of what action is needed
  • the relevant resource material, and
  • QOL checker’s logon

Tips on recording feedback:

  • Do not use the [Enter] key in feedback as it creates formatting problems when QOLStat reports are extracted
  • Do not use Special Characters, including but not limited to < > & as this causes significant formatting problems that can only be fixed by ICT
  • The free text feedback field has a 596 character limit:
    • QOLCheck will allow QOL checkers to paste content that exceeds this limit
    • When a staff member runs the QOLStat Feedback Report, the feedback only shows up to the 596 character limit
  • Feedback on step questions displays in the Step Question Feedback Report of the QOLStat tool
  • Feedback on the overall check displays in the Results Feedback Report of the QOLStat tool
  • Feedback in QOL can be released under Freedom of Information

6

Contingency mode for QOL + Read more ...

Contingency Mode A is a checking mechanism completed manually through Customer First/Customer Record. It is activated when:

  • the QOL tool is unavailable, or
  • system issues severely affect the function of the QOL tool

Notice of activation is communicated through the National News Update (NNU).

Tip: there is no search function for Submitted (SUB) activities in Contingency Mode A.

To undertake checking in Contingency Mode A:

  • select the appropriate activity in Customer First or Customer Record
  • select/Type (A) Approve an activity-QOL contingency mode
  • on the Procedure and Accuracy Check (PAC) screen select view all screens
  • once all screens have been viewed, the PAC screen will display
  • key ‘Y’ (Pass) or ‘N’ (Fail)

If feedback is required then it is to be provided through the appropriate feedback channel (for example the Staff Feedback Tool or Technical Support Centre).

Guidelines for Line Managers and Service Support Officers (SSO)

Table 4

Item

Description

1

Reviews of proficiency + Read more ...

Some components of the QOL proficiency assessment process are completed through automation unless an exemption has been applied. For details on proficiency reviews (manual and automated), see Updating proficiency for QOL.

The Line Manager is responsible for:

  • assessing that the staff member has the knowledge and skills to advance or maintain their level of proficiency
  • reviewing the work performed over the proficiency period
  • updating the QSS screen to reflect changes to proficiency, where QSS automation is not appropriate

2

QOL checker accreditation and reaccreditation responsibilities + Read more ...

The Line Manager or Service Support Officer are responsible for:

  • assessing the staff member in accordance with the QOL checker accreditation criteria using the QOL checker accreditation form
  • keeping records of QOL checker accreditation and reaccreditations for potential audits
  • providing feedback to QOL checkers regarding their accreditation and reaccreditation
  • monitoring and discussing any feedback (i.e. feedback for the QOL checker may have received regarding QOL disputes or staff feedback etc.)

3

ISP responsibilities + Read more ...

Line Managers are responsible for applying for and approving QOL checker access in ISP.

4

Feedback + Read more ...

Line Managers and SSOs can run the Result Feedback and Step Feedback reports in the QOLStat tool to access staff member feedback.

To provide QOL related feedback to a staff member, in particular at another site, the Staff Feedback Tool can be used.

5

QOL monitoring and workload + Read more ...

Only staff with Work Optimiser Analyst access are able to check (using a keyword search) for 'offline', 'qolabt' and 'notalloc' activities.

For more information about Analyst access to Work Optimiser, see Work Optimiser for Team Leaders and Analysts.

6

Review of QOL decisions + Read more ...

Line Managers and SSOs can review decisions made in QOL when requested by a staff member. Note: there is no functionality to alter the outcome within the QOL tool.

See QOL and QMA Dispute Policy on the Resources page.
