For Centrelink International Services (CIS) staff only

This page contains information for Centrelink International Services (CIS) staff to determine if a customer can be paid while outside Australia for an approved reason, as well recording the decision manually if the Portability Script is not available.

Family member definition + Read more ...

A family member is defined under section 23(14) of the Social Security Act as a partner, parent, sister, brother or child of the relevant person.

Is the customer going to visit a family member as per S23 (14) SSA?

  • Yes, the customer is visiting a family member for an acute family crisis. See Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Alternative family member assessment + Read more ...

The definition of a family member may also cover someone who, in the opinion of the delegate, has fulfilled a similar role as a family member. For example, if someone was brought up by another person in a relationship that could be considered to be equivalent to a parent/child relationship. Note: this is unlikely to apply to an in-law.

The delegation for family member portability decisions is held by the Service Support Manager or Business Manager of CIS. If appropriate, make a submission to the delegate.

Consider the following:

  • actual relationship to person, for example:
    • grandparent/family friend/community member
  • nature of relationship, for example:
    • customer was brought up by the person (parent relationship)
    • customer was brought up with the person (sibling relationship)
    • customer brought up the person(child relationship)
  • dates of relationship, for example:
    • start date, how old customer was at the time
    • how long the relationship has existed or did exist

Likely outcome of discussion:

  • person is clearly not like a family member, for example:
    • customer lived in extended family with grandparent who provided occasional care and parents who provided day to day care and responsibility. No assessment required
  • person may be like a family member and customer wishes to undergo assessment
  • person could be considered to be fulfilling a role like that of a family member which requires assessment

There is no point in proceeding with a family member determination if customer would clearly not qualify.

Could the person in the AFC situation be considered as fulfilling a role similar to that of a family member?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, see Step 2 in Table 3

Customer to provide evidence for the acute family crisis (AFC) + Read more ...

In order for payment to be approved, evidence is required which supports the reason for travel. See acceptable proof of the absence to assist in advising customer what type of information is acceptable.

Once evidence has been obtained, this can be lodged via online services or at their local service centre.

There is no point in proceeding with a family member determination if the customer will be unable to provide acceptable evidence of the acute family crisis.

Can the customer obtain acceptable proof of the absence either immediately or on their return to Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, see Step 2 in Table 3

Customer to provide evidence for the acute family crisis (AFC) + Read more ...

Absence is temporary and is for an approved reason.

In order for payment to be approved, evidence is required which supports the reason for travel. See acceptable proof of the absence to assist in advising customer what type of information is acceptable.

2

Customer cannot receive payment for approved reason portability + Read more ...

The overseas absence is not for an approved reason, the person is not considered to be a family member or satisfactory evidence for an approved reason cannot be provided.

The customer's payment will stop from the date of departure, unless the customer is receiving Disability Support Pension (DSP) and still has a period of general portability available:

  • If available, run the Portability Script and include the following details of the decision in the Notes field:
    • reason why customer is travelling overseas
    • relevant names and dates of reason for travel
    • reason why payment cannot be made
    • any other information provided by customer
  • Explain the decision to the customer and advise them about the continuation of payment on their return to Australia
  • Determine if the customer has to contact when they have returned to Australia
  • If customer has income from employment income, see Recording and correcting employment income details

Is Portability Script working?

  • Yes, run the Portability Script - Departures and Returns. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

3

Absence due to the death of a family member + Read more ...

Usually payment would not be considered until evidence supporting the reason for travel has been provided, however if customer is travelling due to the recent death of a family member, payment can be considered prior to evidence being received. The reason for this is that it would be unlikely for customer to provide evidence not deemed to be acceptable on return to Australia.

Is the customer travelling due to the death of a family member?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Would customer be put in severe financial hardship with loss of payment? + Read more ...

Do not prompt the customer with this question, however if it is determined after discussion with customer that they may meet requirements, approval can be considered before evidence is received. In all cases seek Service Support Officer (SSO) advice before proceeding.

Many customers are in crisis and removing their only source of income at this time is not ideal. If the reason for travel would clearly meet grounds for payment, approval before sighting evidence can be considered in limited circumstances.

For example, customer is visiting a family member overseas who has recently been submitted to palliative care with a short life expectancy. Customer indicates they would not be able to travel overseas without their payment and would have no issue obtaining detailed evidence whilst overseas or on their return.

Is customer travelling for an approved reason and would be placed in financial hardship with the cessation of payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Assessing if payment to be made before evidence can be provided + Read more ...

Advise customer that if acceptable proof is not provided on return to Australia, an overpayment will be raised for the full amount paid to them while overseas.

Does the customer want to be paid before providing evidence?

  • Yes, customer to provide evidence on return to Australia, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Payment to be approved before receipt of evidence + Read more ...

Reason for travel supports payment overseas before evidence being received. Risk of overpayment being raised due to unacceptable evidence is minimal.

  • Determine the length of time payment is to be approved overseas. See specific negotiated period.
  • If available, run the Portability Script and include the following details of the decision in the Notes field:
    • Reason why customer is travelling overseas
    • Relevant names and dates of reason for travel
    • Reason why payment has been approved prior to evidence being received
    • Reason why determined length of time was approved
    • Risk of overpayment discussed
    • Any other information provided by customer
  • Portability Script will code the approved portability on the Customer Advised Travel Details RSCD screen
  • To ensure payment suspends after the approved portability period code the RSCD screen on a new line:
    • day after the end of the approved portability period in the Event Date field
    • AU in the Country field
  • Explain the decision to the customer, ensuring the customer is aware that payment will suspend on the last day of the specific negotiated period and advise them about continuation of payment on their return to Australia
    Note: if DSP customer has any general portability period remaining they will not suspend at the end of the approved portability period
  • Determine if the customer must contact when they have returned to Australia
  • If customer has income from employment income, see Recording and correcting employment income details

Is Portability Script working?

  • Yes, run the Portability Script - Departures and Returns. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Evidence is required prior to payment being considered overseas + Read more ...

Advise customer a decision will be made once acceptable proof of the absence has been provided. If evidence supports payment for an approved absence, back-payment will be made.

The customer's payment will stop from the date of departure, unless the customer is receiving Disability Support Pension (DSP) and still has a period of general portability available:

  • If available, run the Portability Script and include the following details of the decision in the Notes field:
    • Reason why customer is travelling overseas
    • Relevant names and dates of reason for travel
    • Any other information provided by customer
  • Explain the decision to the customer and advise them about continuation of payment on their return to Australia
  • Determine if the customer must contact when they have returned to Australia
  • Check the Future Activity List (FAL) screen for a review for the return of evidence set by the Portability Script. If one exists, this must be cancelled
  • If customer has income from employment income, see Recording and correcting employment income details

Is Portability Script working?

  • Yes, run the Portability Script - Departures and Returns. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Portability Script not working + Read more ...

Manual DOC must be added to customer record, including:

  • Date of departure and return
  • Destination country
  • Reason why customer is travelling overseas
  • Relevant names and dates of reason for travel
  • Any other information provided by customer
  • Decision (Rejected/Pre-Approved/Pending evidence)

The Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen should be coded with travel details. See Coding specialist portability assessments for Centrelink International Services (CIS) staff.

Other things to consider:

  • If an approved absence has been approved prior to evidence being provided, ensure there is a manual review coded for the return of evidence:
    • Due 2 weeks after date for planned return to Australia
    • Keyword RPG
  • Record the change of address on Address Details (AD) screen if the customer has provided new address details.
    Note: it is not mandatory for the customer to provide an address outside Australia when the period of travel is temporary
  • Record the overseas telephone number and email address if provided by the customer
  • If the customer has ceased to pay rent, board or lodgings, cease rent assistance from the relevant date. For assistance with coding, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance
  • Ask the customer if they would like to nominate an agent to act on their behalf while overseas and, if so, document the authority

Assessing the record and evidence provided + Read more ...

To approve a payment for restricted portability, all evidence the customer has provided must be examined in conjunction with information obtained from the customer record to determine whether the customer's payment may be portable for the requested approved reason.

This includes the following:

  • Check document tools for evidence.
    Note: evidence may require translation, see Translation of documents

  • Check customer's travel patterns
  • Check payment type/status at time of departure
  • Check for previous restricted portability
  • Consider contacting customer for further information if required

Submission for a family member assessment + Read more ...

Contact must be made with customer to determine whether the person who was suffering the crisis can be deemed to be like a family member.

Discuss with the customer what circumstances they consider the person fulfilled in a role similar to a family member.

Consider:

  • Actual relationship to person
    • for example, grandparent/family friend/community member
  • Nature of relationship, for example:
    • customer was brought up by the person (parent relationship)
    • customer was brought up with the person (sibling relationship)
    • customer brought up the person(child relationship)
  • Dates of relationship, for example:
    • start date, how old customer was at the time
    • how long the relationship has existed or did exist

Likely outcome of discussion:

  • Person is clearly not like a family member, for example:
    • Customer lived in extended family with grandparent who provided occasional care and parents who provided day to day care and responsibility. No assessment required
  • Person may be like a family member and customer wishes to undergo assessment
  • Person could be considered to be fulfilling a role like that of a family member which requires assessment

There is no point in proceeding with a family member determination if customer would clearly not qualify.

Family Member determination Fast Note + Read more ...

For the delegate to consider a submission it must provide as much information as possible. Discuss with the customer what circumstances they consider the person fulfilled in a role similar to a family member.

Be clear in the submission whether the recommendation is that the person is to be considered to be 'like a family member' or not 'like a family member'.

Submission and recommendation of family member determination + Read more ...

When the Family Member Recommendation DOC is on the record, contact the SSO team to facilitate the submission to the delegate.

Check the record for the return from the delegate.

Has the delegate affirmed the person is an approved family member for the purposes of an acute family crisis?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Grant application for payment under approved absence portability + Read more ...

Assessment of record and/or evidence provided does support payment overseas for an approved absence. Determine the length of time payment is to be approved. See specific negotiated period.

If decision is not fully favourable (that is, not granting for maximum period/entire absence overseas) contact must be made with customer to provide them the opportunity to supply further information or evidence prior to finalising assessment. See Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK).

Once decision is finalised:

  • In customer record, run International DOC creation script, select 'ROW - RPG assessment' to document decision. Include detailed information to support decision including:
    • Description of evidence provided
    • How evidence supports that travel was for an approved reason
    • Reason why determined length of time was approved
    • Any other factors relevant to the decision
  • Run Multi-Lingual letters script and select RAT001 to generate letter of decision
  • For information on implementing decision, see Coding specialist portability assessments for Centrelink International Services (CIS) staff

Procedure ends here.

9

Reject application for payment under approved absence portability + Read more ...

Assessment of record and/or evidence provided does not support payment overseas for an approved absence.

Contact must be made with customer to provide the opportunity to supply further information or evidence prior to finalising assessment. See Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK).

Once decision is finalised:

  • In customer record, run International DOC creation script, select 'ROW - RPG assessment' to document decision. Include detailed information to support decision including:
    • Description of evidence provided
    • Why evidence/record does not support that travel was for an approved reason
    • Any other factors relevant to the decision
  • Run Multi-Lingual letters script and select RAT001 to generate letter of decision
  • Code Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen with rejection reason either AFR or HUR or MER

1

Return of evidence review for approved reason + Read more ...

This review is created by the Portability Script - Departures and Return when travel has been selected as an approved reason. The review will become due two weeks after the planned return date to allow time for the customer to provide evidence on their return to Australia.

Action required:

  • If customer was not pre-approved for restricted portability, cancel the review
  • If customer was pre-approved for restricted portability, go to Step 2

Does evidence verify reason for travel? + Read more ...

For help with this decision, see Overseas absences for acute family crisis.

Does evidence verify the pre-approval decision?

  • Yes, create a DOC with detailed information including:
    • Description of evidence provided
    • How evidence supports that travel was for an approved reason
    • Any other factors relevant to the decision
    • Finalise review
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Evidence not provided or does not verify pre-approved payment + Read more ...

Customer has not provided evidence or evidence provided does not verify the pre-approved portability payment for an approved reason.

Contact must be made with customer to provide an opportunity to supply further information or evidence prior to finalising assessment. See Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK).

If customer cannot provide further supporting evidence then an overpayment must be created for the period already paid overseas due to an approved reason.

Note: if customer has not yet returned to Australia and there are no contact details recorded whilst they are overseas, consider placing review on hold for an additional period of time before creating an overpayment.

  • In the Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen:
    • code the Source and DOR fields
    • change approved reason coded to OTH
  • Ensure debt covers correct period and send for investigation and finalise activity
  • Add keyword of DMB101 to debt shell
