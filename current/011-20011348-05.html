Frequently asked questions

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Question: Do patients need a referral to access these services?

Answer: No, patients can access these services without a referral.

2

Question: Do patients need a mental health treatment plan (MHTP) to access these services?

Answer: No, patients can access these services without a MHTP in place.

3

Question: How many services can a patient get Medicare benefits for?

Answer: Up to 10 per calendar year. A calendar year is 1 January to 31 December (inclusive).

The 10 services can be from 1 or a combination of providers.

4

Question: Do patients or providers need to provide evidence of the patient’s eligibility in their Medicare claim?

Answer: No, supporting evidence does not need to be provided with the claim for Medicare benefits to be paid.

All Medicare claims are subject to post payment audit. Health professionals should keep adequate records, including clinical indications for treatment they provide.

5

Question: Are these items only available for people who lost property or loved ones during the bushfires?

Answer: No, the items are for any individual or family affected by the fires, including people who worked or volunteered for emergency services in response to bushfires that occurred between 1 July 2019 and 30 June 2020.

Health professionals are responsible for determining clinical eligibility and whether a patient would benefit from treatment.

6

Question: Do providers need to submit any text with their claims for these items?

Answer: Only if they are claiming multiple services on the same day for the same patient.

Claims for multiple services (different item numbers) must include text that shows the services are for separate conditions/reasons or there was a reasonable lapse of time between them.

Claims for multiple services (same item number) must include text that shows there was a reasonable lapse of time between them.

See Assessing MBS item restrictions for more information about acceptable text.

7

Question: Can patients who already have a MHTP access these services?

Answer: Yes. The 10 services under this initiative do not count toward any services provided under a MHTP or shared care plan.

8

Question: What happens when the patient reaches the claiming limit?

Answer: They can continue to see their health professional after they reach the claiming limit but they won’t be eligible for Medicare benefits.

9

Question: Can these items be claimed with bulk bill incentive items 10990 and 10991?

Answer: Yes, provided that the requirements of the items being claimed are met in full.

10

Question: Does the patient need to have an established relationship with the provider to be eligible for telehealth services?

Answer: No. Patients can have all of their services provided via videoconference as long as the requirements in the item descriptor are met.
