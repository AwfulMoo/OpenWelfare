3

Family assistance reconciliation debts + Read more ...

Standard withholding level

The following withholding rates apply when the only debt/s outstanding are:

  • family assistance reconciliation, or
  • re-reconciliation debt/s

Where the balance of the reconciliation debt/s on the day the debt/s are raised is:

  • less than $750 - $30 per fortnight
  • $750 or above - $60 per fortnight

Apply the $60 withholding rate when a customer has a new reconciliation debt raised, if:

  • there is an existing reconciliation debt, and
  • the amount added increases the amount to $750 or above

In this case it will display as non-standard on the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS).

The standard withholding rates for family assistance reconciliation debts are automatically applied to Family Tax Benefit (FTB) individuals. If the customer is not receiving FTB, the standard withholding rate will apply to their income support payments.

Customers can ask for a non-standard withholding arrangement by:

  • contacting Services Australia, or
  • via the Money You Owe option online

Procedure ends here.

4

Social Security benefit or Social Security pension + Read more ...

15% of the basic rate of payment (after the income and assets) and any supplement paid (for example, Youth Disability Supplement (YDS)).

  • + 55% of ordinary income up to the income free area, and
  • + 27.5% of ordinary income over the income free area, or
  • + 27.5% of ordinary income over the income free area up to 60% taper, where the income test for the customer's payment has a taper

Withholdings are not allowed from some Pension Supplement payment components.

Procedure ends here.

5

Family Tax Benefit (FTB) + Read more ...

  • 95% of the customer's total entitlement if they are paid the base rate Part A (which may or may not include a payment of Part B)
  • 25% of the customer's total entitlement if they are paid more than base rate Part A (which may or may not include a payment of Part B)
  • 95% of Part B if that is the customer's only payment

Note: automatic withholdings only apply to FTB payments if the customer is not getting an income support payment. The exception is when the only debt/s outstanding is/are family assistance reconciliation or re-reconciliation debt/s. If this is the case the family assistance reconciliation withholding rate will apply to FTB.

Procedure ends here.

6

Child Care Subsidy (CCS) + Read more ...

The standard CCS withholding rate is 20% of their ongoing entitlement.

Withholdings of 20% will start from the customers ongoing CSS entitlement if they have:

  • an outstanding CCS, or Child Care Benefit (CCB) / Child Care Rebate (CCR) debt, and
  • have not made an arrangement to repay

No further withholdings will be deducted if the customer has:

  • other outstanding debt(s), and
  • a withholding arrangement is already in place

The CSS debt will become part of the hierarchy of debts to recover.

Note: CCS debts can be recovered from withholdings from CCS and other payments. Withholdings from CCS entitlement cannot be used to recover a non-childcare (CCS,CCB or CCR) debt.

7

Parental Leave Pay (PPL) + Read more ...

From 5 June 2021, the standard withholding rate for PPL is 100%.

Withholding of 100% will start from a customer's ongoing PPL if they have:

  • an outstanding PPL debt, and
  • have not made an arrangement to repay

Withholding will stop if the customer:

  • has no outstanding PPL debts
  • stops receiving PPL for more than 28 days

Note: if standard withholding stops and the customer still has an outstanding PPL debt without a payment arrangement, interest will apply.

Automatic offsetting for PPL arrears can occur in the same activity before a PPL debt is determined. PPL arrears can also be offset against income support overpayment amounts that are due to payment of PPL arrears for the same period.

Before 5 June 2021, the standard withholding rate for PPL was 15%.

8

Foreign pension data exchange debts + Read more ...

The withholding rate of $50 per fortnight applies when the only debt/s outstanding is/are Foreign Pension Data Exchange debt/s.

See Non-standard recovery for debts arising from Foreign Pension Data Exchange.

The withholding rate will display on DMIS as non-standard.

Procedure ends here.

9

Pension supplement + Read more ...

  • BASIC - 15%
  • PSBAS - 15%
  • PSREM - 0%
  • PSMIN - 0%
  • TRNTX - 0%
  • TRMIN - 0%
  • Other supplements - 15%

See the Resources page for more details.

Pension Supplement payment components

The components of Pension Supplement payment which may be withheld are:

  • PSBAS - Basic Amount
  • PSREM - Remaining Amount
  • PSMIN - Minimum Amount
  • TRNTX - Transitional Base and Remaining amount
  • TRMIN - Transitional minimum amount

Do not withhold from any other payment component.

For customers who:

  • do not get any of the listed components, deduct withholdings from their gross payment
  • get only listed components, other arrangements will be needed

Standard or non-standard withholdings cannot be deducted from the quarterly supplement if the customer chooses this payment option.

Standard withholdings

  • can be deducted from:
    • PSBAS
  • cannot be deducted from:
    • PSMIN
    • PSREM
    • TRNTX
    • TRMIN

Non-standard withholdings

  • can be deducted from:
    • PSBAS
    • PSREM
    • PSMIN
    • TRNTX
  • cannot be deducted from:
    • TRMIN
    • PSMIN

Procedure ends here.

10

ABSTUDY/Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme + Read more ...

15% of the basic rate of payment and any supplement paid (for example, Youth Disability Supplement (YDS)).

Procedure ends here.

11

Carer Allowance (CA)/ Double Orphan Pension (DOP)/Mobility Allowance (MOB) + Read more ...

95%

Only apply withholdings to these payments if a customer does not get any other payment.
