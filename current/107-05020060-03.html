Customer with a DIS (disaster) write-off wants to set up a debt repayment arrangement + Read more ...

Customers may have a DIS write-off applied to their debt in certain circumstances. For example, where they have received a disaster payment.

4

Date to commence deductions + Read more ...

Ask the customer when they can start to make the payments. Payments should align with the customers pay cycle (regular instalments) or when the funds are available (lump sum).

The start date must:

  • be at least six days after today's date. This allows enough time for the financial institution to set up the deduction
  • be within 1 month. Do not code direct debit to start more than 1 month in the future. The customer will continue to accrue Interest Charge (IC) until they make the first payment. Customers can also use this as a method to avoid other recovery action such as a tax garnishee or forget about the payment if it is too far into the future
  • start after the due date of a debt. If a direct debit start date is before the due date of a debt, and a payment that is due before the due date is insufficient or not paid:
    • a 'Payment Not Received' letter does not issue
    • a broken arrangement activity does not generate
    • Interest Charge (IC) applies from the due date for non-current customer

Go to Step 5

5

Account information + Read more ...

Ask the customer for their account details, including:

  • Name of the financial institution (FI)
  • Bank State Branch (BSB) number
  • Account number
  • Name of the person(s) who holds the account
  • If they can authorise deductions from the account, and
  • If the customer's FI allows direct debits from their FI account

For a joint account:

  • Make sure the customer has the right to authorise deductions
  • If more than one signature is needed to authorise deductions, send a Direct Debit Request Information and Application (SS311(A)) form. See the Resources page for a link to the form

For a third party account:

  • Do not action the direct debit if the name of the FI account does not match the debtor

For a Business account:

  • Make sure the account and business name reflects the customer's name and that they are the sole signatory on the business account
  • Send a SS311(A) as we need a signature as proof
  • Do not accept the arrangement until the completed form is returned
  • See the Resources page for a link to the form

For a Nominee account:

  • A correspondence nominee can authorise a direct debit arrangement from a customer's FI account

Go to Step 6.

6

Discuss commitment made to pay debt + Read more ...

Repeat details of arrangement made, including amount, date and method.

Confirm the terms of their payment arrangement. For example:

'Today you have entered into a verbal agreement to repay this debt. It is important you keep to the terms of the agreement you have made today. If you are unable to make a payment at any stage please contact us straight away so we can help you.'

Staff must tell the customer about the consequences of not keeping to their payment arrangement. This includes telling them about the recovery actions that may take place under the cost effectiveness guidelines.

Do not threaten the customer with recovery action. This can escalate the situation. Explain it to the customer so they can make informed decisions.

Go to Step 7.

7

DIS write-off + Read more ...

If a DIS write-off has been applied, to set up a payment arrangement in:

  • Customer Record:
    • Run the Debt recovery script
  • Customer First, remove and re-apply the DIS write-off:
    • Go to the Write-off (OPWO) screen
    • Note the DIS write-off end date and comment
    • Remove the DIS write off
    • Run the Debt repayment guided procedure
    • Re-apply the DIS write-off end date and description on the OPWO screen
    • Record details of this action on a DOC

Note: when the write-off code is removed as detailed above, re-apply the write-off code on the same day. Where this does not happen, the customer receives a letter warning them that they have an outstanding debt and to enter into a payment arrangement.

For Child Care Subsidy (CCS) debts, see Child Care Subsidy (CCS) debts.

Go to Step 8.

8

The Direct Debit (OPVD) screen + Read more ...

Use the Debt recovery script.

Follow the manual steps when the script is not available.

On the Activity List (AL) screen:

  • 'S'elect the DMN/RCV No Arrangement activity
  • press [Enter]

If there is no DMN/RCV activity:

  • key 'OPAL' in the Nxt field
  • press [Enter]
  • In the Create new arrangement type field, key 'VOL'
  • press [Enter]

On the Direct Debit (OPVD) screen:

  • Complete the following fields:
    • Authority to Debtor Date
    • Amount $ maximum amount is $500,000 for any frequency
    • Frequency
    • Pay Start Date must be at least six days after today's date
    • Acknowledgment Date must be completed otherwise deductions will not commence
    • FI Code customer's BSB number
    • The FI Name
    • FI Address-Line 1
    • Line 2 completes when the FI Code field is coded
    • Account Number take extra care when entering these details no further checks are made by the FI. If needed, have another person check them
    • Account Title
  • Press [Enter]
  • Go to the Assessment Finalisation (OPAF) screen:
    • key 'Y' in the Finished? field. Press [Enter]
  • A direct debit confirmation letter will send to the customer. This outlines their:
    • rights and responsibilities, and
    • details of the direct debit arrangement.
  • Record details on a DOC:
    • the Section of the Act that covers the decision
    • the balance of the outstanding debt
    • the customer's financial institution name
    • Bank State Branch (BSB) number (or branch address)
    • account number
    • name of the person(s) who hold the account
    • agreed start date, frequency of deductions
    • the agreed amount of deduction
    • specific details of any warnings given
    • the customer's employment details (name and location). Record if the customer is unwilling to provide employment details
    • gross income, as detailed in the acceptable cash levels
    • any other relevant details from the discussion

Direct Debit Review (DDR)

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Direct Debit Review (DDR) + Read more ...

Allocation of the DDR Fast Note is through Workload Management (WLM). Investigate:

  • who set up the direct debit arrangement,
  • who authorised the direct debit, and
  • when the direct debit was set up

Note: the DDR Fast Note:

  • is for Payments and Integrity Operations Strategy only
  • is created for WLM allocation purposes only

Is investigation required for a DDR?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here

2

Payments and Integrity Operations Strategy team DDR email + Read more ...

Has the DDR email been received from the Payments and Integrity Operations Strategy team?

Note: the email will contain details of the actions required.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, and it has been more than 24 hrs, the Team Leader is to contact the Payments and Integrity Operations Strategy team. When response is received, go to Step 3.

3

Check the details and gather the information + Read more ...

Check the details on the customer record against the details on the DDR form. The Resources page has examples of a Direct Debit Systems Claim Request and where to locate specific information.

Locate the following:

  • CRN at the end of the Lodgement Reference number. This will be the last 9 digits plus the letter. For example VDCAN1234567891011A (the CRN is 567 891 011A)
  • The reason for the claim:
    • Client claims DDR was unauthorised. Please investigate and advise
    • Unauthorised transaction, please provide copy of authority or refund
    • Debit user unknown to customer
    • Unauthorised trans, please provide copy of authority or refund
    • Please cease direct debit

Gather evidence such as:

  • If the CRN's match. Check for the CRN under Lodgement Reference
  • If the name on the Customer Record matches the name on the claim
  • If the account information used for the direct debit matches the account details on the customer record. Check the following screens:
    • Payment Destination Summary (PAS)
    • Savings Summary (SVS)
    • Direct Investments (SVDI)
  • the Direct debit confirmation letter (recovery arrangement letter). See Viewing or reissuing a letter or electronic message. Note: where the letter cannot be located, refer to Technical Leader for assistance

Check the arrangement

Go to the Payment Arrangement List screen (OPAL):

  • Select the Voluntary Direct Debit (VOL). If the:
    • customers CRN is showing in the top right corner, this indicates the arrangement was set up by the customer online or via the App
    • staff member's logon is showing in the top right corner, this indicates the customer has called and the arrangement has been set up by a staff member.

Check the repayment history

Go to the Repayment History Screen (OPRH). Check the direct debit dates and amounts debited on the customer record, match the dates and amounts debited listed on the DDR.

4

Contact the customer + Read more ...

Make a reasonable effort to contact the customer.

Note: when leaving messages, see leaving messages on answering machines.

Did the attempt result in a successful engagement with the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, DOC the attempt using the Unsuccessful Engagement Fast Note
    • If the reason for the claim is 'Please cease direct debit', go to Step 7
    • For all other reasons, go to Step 6

5

Discuss the reason for the call + Read more ...

Safeguard the customer's privacy before discussing the reason for the call.

Advise the customer that the call: 'may be recorded or listened to for quality and coaching purposes'.

See Calling a customer or returning a customer's call.

If contact is successful:

  • Confirm with the customer the request from the financial institution
  • Provide an explanation and details of what action will be taken

If the direct debit arrangement is to continue, ask the customer if they agree to a copy of their Direct debit confirmation letter being sent to the RBA.

If the claim is supported, go to Step 7

6

Attempts to contact unsuccessful + Read more ...

Consider the evidence to determine if the customer:

  • has an outstanding debt balance
  • details (name, CRN, account details) on the customer record and the Direct Debit Systems Claim Request form match
  • set up the direct debit via their online App

If the evidence indicates the customer:

  • did not set up / authorise the direct debt, the claim is supported, go to Step 7
  • may have forgotten they had set up / authorised the direct debit arrangement, the claim is not supported. The arrangement does not end. Go to Step 8

7

DDR Claim supported + Read more ...

Cease the direct debt arrangement (if not already ended).

Complete and 'accept' the claim.

If contact with the customer was:

  • successful and the customer has an outstanding debt and still wants the direct debit arrangement ceased, cease the direct debit arrangement:
    • discuss alternate debt recovery options with the customer, see Arranging cash repayments to recover debts
    • if there is refusal to enter into an alternative recovery arrangement, advise the customer more recovery action may be taken, for example, application of interest charge (IC)
  • unsuccessful and the DDR claim was to 'cease' the direct debit, cease the direct debit arrangement

Do not refund any repayments. Services Australia Treasury will remove any unauthorised direct debit payments from the customer record.

Evidence or suspicion of fraud

Report any evidence or suspicion of fraud, even unsure. See the Resources page for a link to Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption.

Go to Step 9.

8

DDR Claim not supported + Read more ...

Where contact was unsuccessful, and:

  • the information gathered does not support the claim. For example, the evidence indicates the customer set up/authorised the direct debit arrangement, and
  • the direct debit details are correct, complete and 'refuse' the claim. Go to Step 9

If contact was successful, and:

  • the customer has confirmed they want to keep the direct debit arrangement in place, and
  • the customer has given permission to include the authority (Direct Debit Confirmation letter) in the response to the RBA:
    • print the Direct Debit Confirmation letter (the authority)
    • redact (remove) all information which does not confirm the authorisation of the direct debit (for example, debt balance), see the Resources page
    • scan the redacted letter to the customer record, go to Step 9

9

Send email response to the Reserve Bank (RBA) + Read more ...

Reply to the email from the RBA and cc the Payments and Integrity Operations Strategy team. Do not change security marker.

Include the following information:

  • If attempts to contact the customer were successful or unsuccessful
  • If Direct debit claim request is supported or not supported
  • description of the decision and what action is being authorised, for example:
    • Claim not supported, customer requests direct debit arrangement to continue. Customer has been advised to contact their financial institution to withdraw their cancellation request
    • Claim supported, direct debit ceased, claim accepted for a total of $___.__ for DD/MM/YR
    • For examples, see the Resources page
  • Where the claim is not supported, attach a copy of the redacted Direct Debit Confirmation letter (the authority). Note: when the letter is saved to enable it to be attached to the email, once attached, delete the saved copy

10

Document the actions taken + Read more ...

Annotate the Fast Note, include the following:

  • Referring financial institution name, for example, CBA, ING, NAB
  • DDR reference number
  • Reason for the claim
  • Claim outcome. That is, <supported> or <not supported>
  • Who set up the arrangement <customer via online services> or <debt recovery officer assisted via inbound customer contact>
  • actions authorised, for example, direct debit ceased, claim accepted for a total of $___.__ for DD/MM/YR
  • Close the Fast Note

For examples of Fast Notes see the Resources page.

11

Financial Shared Services - For Information only + Read more ...

  • RBA will direct debit the Services Australia account
  • Services Australia, Treasury will ask the RBA the reason for the direct debit
  • RBA will forward Treasury the email sent by the Debt Recovery Officer
  • Treasury will remove the unauthorised direct debit payments from the customer record

Procedure ends here.
