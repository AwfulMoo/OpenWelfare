3

Social Security payment ceases + Read more ...

Some Social Security payments do not allow retrospective cancellation. E.g. customers receiving Youth Allowance (YA) or Austudy, who would have been financially better off under ABSTUDY. These customers must not be retrospectively cancelled and granted ABSTUDY for the same period. ABSTUDY must not be backdated, advise the student their start date will be the date the Social Security payment ceased.

Students receiving JobSeeker payment (JSP) must have a loss of entitlement for ABSTUDY to be backdated. See JobSeeker Payment (JSP) job seekers returning to full time study.

Customers who lodge an incorrect or inappropriate claim for ABSTUDY, will have their YA, or Austudy rejected and ABSTUDY granted from their start date.

If the customer will commence study:

  • first semester between 1 January and 31 March, go to Step 1 in Table 3
  • second semester between 1 July and 31 July, go to Step 2 in Table 3
  • outside the above dates, go to Step 3 in Table 3

Calculated start date

Table 3

Step

Action

1

1 January start date + Read more ...

Use the start date of 1 January in the year the student commences studies if they:

  • are eligible for ABSTUDY, and
  • have applied by the relevant closing date

If the customer was paid Family Tax Benefit (FTB) (instead of ABSTUDY when student living at home) and they completed the school year, FTB entitlements may not always pay up to 31 December. ABSTUDY can be backdated to the student’s 16th birthday, which may create an FTB overpayment. The FTB customer and the student will need to decide which payment is more beneficial before backdating ABSTUDY. In these cases, ABSTUDY is granted from the date paid +1 of FTB. See Study requirements for Family Tax Benefit aged 16 years or over. If the FTB customer cannot be contacted, ABSTUDY is only to be granted from date paid +1, unless it is obvious that ABSTUDY would be more beneficial.

Therefore, to ensure there is no gap in payments, a customer can transfer to ABSTUDY and be paid from date paid +1 of FTB (where the secondary student is living at home), as long as they:

  • are intending to enrol in a full-time or concessional study load, in an approved course, in the next available study period, and
  • lodge a claim for ABSTUDY by the closing date, which is 31 December in the year secondary school was finished, and
  • are eligible for ABSTUDY

From 27 April 2020 customers entitled to receive ABSTUDY Living Allowance were eligible to receive the fortnightly Coronavirus Supplement (CVS). See Assessing a claim for ABSTUDY Schooling B Award, Tertiary Award and Masters and Doctorate Award for information on CVS and when a customer is in receipt of ABSTUDY.

If the secondary student is aged 16 years or over and is living away from home to study and boarding, FTB can continue to be paid in addition to the ABSTUDY.

Service Officers will not be required to manually cancel FTB. The system will automatically cease FTB for the student from the grant date of the ABSTUDY (if applicable). Where the parent has no other dependent children, FTB will auto-cancel due to reason 'No dependent eligible children' (NDE).

Record details on a DOC.

Go to Step 10.

2

1 July start date + Read more ...

Advise the student that providing they are otherwise qualified for ABSTUDY, and have applied for ABSTUDY before 31 December of the year they commenced the course, the start date will be 1 July of the year in which their study commenced.

If student did not apply for ABSTUDY by 31 December, or meet any of the late lodgement concessions, they are only eligible from 1 January of the year in which they submitted their claim.

Record details on a DOC.

Go to Step 10.

3

Start date will be first day the course commences + Read more ...

Advise the student that providing they are otherwise qualified for ABSTUDY, and have applied for ABSTUDY by the relevant closing date, their start date will be the first day the course commences.

Some students may be eligible from a date prior to the start of the course if they were undertaking study in a previous study period. For further information, see specific coding requirements for paying ABSTUDY students over a vacation break in Coding new study details.

A tertiary student, who has studied over a summer study period and then commences a Masters or Doctorate course immediately after, is only entitled to the higher Masters and Doctorate rate of Living Allowance from the start of the Masters or Doctorate course. System rules will attempt to pay the customer back to 1 January in the year of study.

Resources contains examples of entitlement start dates and system coding.

Record details on a DOC.

Go to Step 10.

4

First day commenced study + Read more ...

Advise the student that providing they are otherwise qualified for ABSTUDY, and they have applied for ABSTUDY by relevant closing date, their start date will be the first day they commenced study in their course.

Record details on a DOC.

Go to Step 10.

5

Australian Apprentices + Read more ...

An Australian Apprentice claim cannot be backdated:

  • for Australian Apprentice claims lodged prior to 1 July 2018, the ABSTUDY claim must be lodged within 14 days of the intent to claim in order to receive payment (where eligible) from the date of lodgement of intent to claim
  • from 1 July 2018, Australian Apprentices will receive payments (where eligible) from the date they actually submit a complete claim unless they are a vulnerable customer. See Intent to claim and vulnerable customers
  • Australian Apprentices may be entitled to an extension of time to lodge their claim due to a medical condition or special circumstance. See Intent to claim and vulnerable customers

Note: care must be taken that a new claim is not actually being lodged as way of providing additional information for a previously rejected claim. There is no closing date for acceptance of additional information.

Go to Step 10.

Study commenced in previous term(s) + Read more ...

Did the student commence study in a previous term(s)?

7

Check student situation in a previous term + Read more ...

Check if any of the following situations occurred during the previous term(s):

  • the applicant or the applicant's partner is in receipt of one of the prescribed forms of Commonwealth Government assistance, or
  • the applicant or the applicant's partner is taken to be receiving their income support payment during an employment income nil rate period, or
  • the applicant or the applicant's partner is the holder of a Health Care Card (HCC) or a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC), including a HCC issued on the basis of receipt of maximum Family Tax Benefit (FTB) part A.
    Note: this does not include a HCC issued in respect of a child with a disability under Carer Allowance, or
  • the student would, but for age (that is, under the minimum school leaving age), qualify for independent status as an orphan, a student whose parents cannot exercise parental responsibilities, or as a homeless student, or
  • the student is in State Care, under the minimum school leaving age and not eligible for Away from home entitlements

Did any of the above apply during the previous term(s)?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9
