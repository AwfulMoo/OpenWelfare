Role of a CLA recipient, identification, duties and eligibility

This table describes the process for qualifying and applying for CLA.

Step

Action

1

Potential CLA recipient + Read more ...

Bi-lingual staff member identified as potential CLA recipient.

2

Identified business need for a CLA + Read more ...

Manager determines there is a business need for the language skill as an identifiable and ongoing need using resources such as:

  • Multicultural Customer and Payment profiles for customer demographics
  • front of house traffic reports, and
  • Multicultural Service Officers

3

Language qualification + Read more ...

The following is accepted as proof of a language qualification:

  • a pass in the CLA Proficiency Test conducted by the National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) on behalf of the agency, or
  • NAATI certification at the certified provisional interpreter level or higher, or
  • NAATI recognition as a practicing or recognised interpreter (only where NAATI testing, or certification is not available for that language), or
  • Certificate II in AUSLAN from TAFE or State Deaf Society or equivalent by another organisation approved by the agency

The following is accepted as proof of Indigenous language proficiency:

  • a pass in the CLA Proficiency Test conducted by the National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) on behalf of the agency, or
  • confirmation from a senior Indigenous community leader or organisation of the applicant's language skills
  • relevant recognised language service, such as TAFE, Language Centre or Indigenous Interpreter Service

Does the staff member have proof of a language qualification?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Requesting a CLA test + Read more ...

  • Manager completes Request for Community Language Allowance Proficiency test (PP015) and candidate signs the form
  • Email the completed PP015 to the addresses on the PP015
  • Language Services arranges payment for the test

5

Taking a CLA test + Read more ...

Candidates can use the following resources to prepare for the test:

  • NAATI CLA practice material
  • NAATI Guide to CLA Practice Material

NAATI will contact the candidate and team leader to coordinate a test date. The test will be conducted via MS Teams.

Did the candidate pass the test?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No,
    • the agency will pay for a retest after 3 months, for a maximum of 2 retests
    • the staff member will be required to pay for re-testing if they apply within 3 months of the last test or after 3 failed attempts. The staff member's manager is responsible for organising the re-test. The agency will reimburse this cost if they pass that test

6

Apply for CLA in ESSentials + Read more ...

The candidate applies for the allowance in ESSentials:

  • with an end date being no more than 12 months from the start date, and
  • approval by an EL1 or above

7

CLA review + Read more ...

The CLA recipient's manager (or specified person) must review the allowance annually but may review the payment of CLA at any time.

The CLA recipient is required to complete Record of use of a language other than English for CLA to support the rate of allowance.

8

CLA changes + Read more ...

The CLA recipients manager should advise the recipients in writing if:

  • language skills are no longer required
  • duties have changed and the language skills are not required
  • CLA rate changes, such as CLA 1 to CLA 2
