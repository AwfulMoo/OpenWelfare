Preparing for and conducting the MoC interview

Step

Action

1

Identifying if there are family and domestic violence concerns prior to arranging a MoC interview + Read more ...

When deciding if it is suitable to interview the other person/ex-partner to obtain additional information, discretion must be exercised to make sure the contact is appropriate. For example, if a customer shows that they and/or their children are at risk of family and domestic violence and are concerned for their safety, the other person/ex-partner must not be contacted under any circumstances.

Are there indications of family and domestic violence?

  • Yes,
    • do not contact or send forms to the other person/ex-partner
    • check the customer’s record to determine if there has been recent engagement with a social worker. Refer the customer to a social worker if required, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Prepare for the MoC interview + Read more ...

MoC processing staff will attempt an outbound phone contact rather than booking an appointment. Before contacting the customer, Service Officers must make sure that they are fully prepared for the MoC interview by:

  • being aware and having an understanding of the of relevant MoC legislation - see the References page for more information
  • identify if any family and domestic violence and safety concerns have been raised that must be considered
  • having undertaken a MoC assessment against all of the 5 factors using the information and evidence obtained before the phone interview
  • being familiar with all available facts of the case
  • working out what further information is required and what questions need to be asked to obtain that information
  • having the Relationship Details (SS284) form or Relationship Details - Separated under one roof (SS293) and any other supporting evidence readily accessible during the interview
  • checking both the customer’s and the other person/ex-partner’s record to consider any recent change of circumstances or information which may be relevant to the MoC assessment

Note: care must be taken during the interview not to disclose any information to the customer which relates to the other person/ex-partner’s current circumstances and was obtained from the other person/ex-partner’s record and vice versa.

3

Customer/other person/ex-partner’s rights when being interviewed + Read more ...

When being interviewed, all parties have the right to:

  • procedural fairness
  • know the purpose of an interview and the reason why it is required
  • the use of an interpreter
  • have a third party present at the interview or to assist them to complete any form (for face-to-face interviews)
  • have an opportunity to provide their perspective on circumstances even where there is valid and supporting documentation regarding certain factors
  • have information provided to the agency remain confidential (staff must adhere to the Australian Privacy Principles)
  • have any completed forms and other sensitive documents together with the assessment details kept on their file

4

Conducting the interview + Read more ...

To conduct the interview:

  • If an interpreter is required, see Booking an interpreter for an appointment
  • Complete Proof Of Identity checks, see Authenticating a Centrelink customer
  • Tell the customer the call may be recorded for training and quality purposes, see Calling a customer or returning a customer's call
  • Tell the customer/other person/ex-partner of the purpose of the interview
  • Make clear notes of any discussions and all information provided by the customer/other person/ex-partner
  • Give the customer/other person/ex-partner time to answer the question before going on to the next question. Let them finish any explanation offered
  • Fully explore explanations given by the customer/other person/ex-partner. The interview is an opportunity for the customer/other person/ex-partner to put forward their perspective of the circumstances. Even if in the face of other information or documentation their explanation may appear to be implausible, it must be explored in a courteous fashion
  • Do not frame questions to suggest a particular answer. And do not assume a fact that the customer denies or has yet to admit in the interview. For example, if the customer only admitted cohabitation, it would not be appropriate to ask 'So, when did you start your de facto relationship?’ This question assumes that the cohabitation was a Member of a Couple relationship
  • Questioning should be fair and courteous at all times. Service Officers should not seek to discredit the customer by impeaching the accuracy, credibility and general value of the answers. Staff can ask further questions to clarify discrepancies. However, be courteous at all times

If there are indications of family and domestic violence:

  • Check the customer's record to determine if a nominee arrangement and/or PPE authority exists and if any updates are required
  • Check if the customer has a postal address and whether it needs to be updated, or if the customer would like to add a postal address
  • Remind the customer to update any passwords if they have not already done so if they think their ex-partner may know and have access to any password protected services

5

Concluding the interview + Read more ...

  • Summarise the information obtained from the interview
  • Read the summary back to the customer/other person/ex-partner to allow the opportunity to explain any misunderstandings or mistakes
  • Tell the customer/other person/ex-partner they will be notified of the outcome when the MoC assessment is completed
