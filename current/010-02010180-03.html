This page contains additional information and a table with explanatory notes to assist customers to complete the ABSTUDY Change of Circumstances/Enquiries Sheet (SY053).

ABSTUDY Form SY053

Sections of the SY053

This table describes how the ABSTUDY Change of Circumstances/Enquiries Sheet (SY053) is divided into sections and gives explanatory notes on each section to help customers complete the form.

Item

Description

1

Assisting customers to complete form + Read more ...

When assisting customers to complete a form that is used to assess their eligibility for payment, Service Officers must not make any notations, underlines or comments of any sort on a form that is to be signed by a customer and lodged with Services Australia. Instead, any notations or explanations to questions on the form should be made on a separate sheet of paper and attached to the customer's form.

2

How to complete this form + Read more ...

Customers who are issued with the form need to complete the first two questions, but the rest of the questions are optional, and filled out only as required.

Where customers wish to advise of a change in their study details only, they can view and update them by using the Manage study details online or via the Study Details section of the Centrelink Express mobile app if they are registered for Online Account and are authenticated.

3

Personal details + Read more ...

Record the details for the student or Australian Apprentice to whom the change relates. This must be completed so that changes can be made to the customer's record.

4

Enquirer details + Read more ...

  • Services Australia needs to know who is advising of the change of circumstance(s). A contact phone number, and preferred call back time should be entered. This is so that if more information is needed by the agency, they have the ability to get in touch with the enquirer
  • This section must be completed so it is known who is advising of changes. This can be particularly important in many cases, for example if an under 18 year old customer is the enquirer and is advising of changes to their bank account, this can usually only be done by the parent(s). Therefore, more information may be needed before the change can be made

5

Change of address + Read more ...

  • If the change of circumstance involves a change of address, the new address details must be inserted here
  • If Rent Assistance (RA) is being paid, a rent certificate should be issued when the change of address is processed

6

Change of bank account details + Read more ...

  • If the change of circumstance involves a change of bank account details, the new bank account details must be inserted here. Note: except in the case of dependent under 18 year olds, the customer's name must be one of the names on the bank account
  • If the customer does not know their six digit branch number (otherwise known as BSB) or their account number, they will need to ring their bank to find out what this is. The BSB can also be obtained from the CODES facility in Customer First or Customer Record. It is not the number on their card

7

Change of student or Australian Apprentice's income + Read more ...

  • If the customer's income has changed, the amount it has changed to will need to be included, along with the period the amount covers (for example, customer may know they will be paid $1500 in Semester 1)
  • If the customer doesn't know what the total income will be over a semester or year, they can specify a time frame next to where it states 'Other' (for example gross employment income declared is for the period 1 May 2020 to 14 May 2020)

8

Change of institution + Read more ...

  • If the student has changed institution or school, the old institution will need to be listed, including the last date the student attended the school. The new institution or school will also need to be listed, with the date the student commenced, and whether they commenced as a full-time or part-time student

Note: students in tertiary, non-secondary or secondary study are required to provide proof of enrolment if they are studying at a TAFE or a private provider. This can be provided with the form if available, or lodged later with Services Australia either by post, fax, using the Upload documents service, or in person. Students at high school or students undertaking a Higher Education Course at university do not have to provide proof of enrolment.

9

Discontinuation of full-time studies + Read more ...

  • If the student is no longer a full-time student, complete either the last date of full-time studies (if ceased studying) or the date they dropped to part-time (if applicable)

10

Tax details + Read more ...

  • If the customer currently has tax deducted from their payment and wants to have it ceased, select NO
  • If the customer does not currently have any tax deducted from their payment, and want to commence having tax taken out, select YES

11

Other + Read more ...

  • If there has been any other change of circumstance(s) that Services Australia should know about (that is, any change which has the potential to affect the customer's rate) then this should be clearly explained here
  • If there is not enough room a separate sheet of paper can be attached to the form

12

Declaration + Read more ...

The enquirer must sign the declaration.
