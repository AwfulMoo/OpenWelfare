Telehealth services and claiming channels

Telehealth services can be lodged via existing electronic and manual claiming channels.

Easyclaim cannot be used to lodge specialist a Telehealth bulk bill claim as the system cannot assess the derived fee item number. These claims must be lodged using another method (such as, manual or online).

Telehealth video consultations assessing information

Assessing information for Telehealth video consultations in Medicare

This table lists assessing information by topic to assist Service Officers with Telehealth claims.

Topic

Assessing information

MBS items

The MBS Telehealth consultation items are not payable for telephone or email consultations.

Bulk bill incentive items 10990 or 10991 may be billed in conjunction with the Telehealth items for medical practitioners - 2100, 2122, 2125, 2126, 2137, 2138, 2143, 2147, 2179, 2195, 2199 and 2220.

Assisted Reproductive Technology and Obstetric 'enabled' items 13209 and 16401, 16404, 16406, 16500, 16590, 16591 have existing Extended Medicare Safety Net caps. Telehealth items 13210, 16399 are also capped to maintain consistency with the existing Government policy.

Aftercare

Telehealth practitioners are subject to the same aftercare rules as practitioners providing face-to-face consultations.

Referrals

Referral requirements for Telehealth consultations are exactly the same as those for a standard face-to-face consultation.

Supporting practitioners
  • Only one Telehealth MBS item number can be claimed for a video consultation by a supporting practitioner per patient episode. If a medical practitioner and a practice nurse assist during the consultation, only one item is claimable. If a patient is billed by the two practitioners, Medicare will pay only the first account received where the service is for the same patient on the same day.
  • If the supporting practitioner is also a specialist, the supporting specialist is subject to the restrictions on eligible Telehealth areas and is only entitled to claim a medical practitioner Telehealth item. Medicare rebates for Telehealth items cannot be paid to two specialists.
  • A medical practitioner may seek assistance from another health professional (for example, a practice nurse or Aboriginal health worker) to complete the service. The initiating practitioner must be present during part or all of the consultation to be able to claim the service using the appropriate time-tiered MBS item. Time spent by another health professional called in to assist with the consultation may not be counted against the overall time taken to complete the Telehealth consultation.
  • A supporting practitioner does not need to be present for the entire consultation, only as long as is clinically relevant - this can be established in consultation with the specialist. The MBS item payable for the supporting practitioner is determined by the total time spent assisting the patient.

Multiple consultations

A patient can receive a Telehealth video consultation and a face-to-face consultation by the same (or different) provider on the same day as long as the second (and any following) consultations are not a continuation of the initial or earlier Telehealth consultation (this will be treated in the same manner as an AM/PM situation). Providers should notate the times of each consultation on the patient’s account or bulk bill voucher.

Video consultations

Telehealth practitioners may provide consultations to multiple patients consecutively during a single video link, however each patient must have their own separate consultation.

If the Telehealth consultation is interrupted part way through due to technical difficulties, the MBS rebate is payable for the period of the consultation. If the connection is re-established and the video consultation is a continuation of the original failed video consultation, only one MBS rebate is payable for all participating Telehealth practitioners.

External website

The MBS Online contains a complete list of Telehealth Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) item numbers, their descriptors and related explanatory notes.
