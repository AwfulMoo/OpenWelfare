5

Carer in gaol or psychiatric confinement in connection with an offence + Read more ...

From 27 September 2006, respite days cannot be used where the carer is in gaol or in psychiatric confinement in connection with an offence.

If a carer is in periodic detention or weekend detention for a whole 24 hour period from 'midnight to midnight', they lose qualification for CA as respite cannot be used for this absence and therefore, daily care is not being provided.

Suspend CA from the day after the carer enters custody. The carer does not lose entitlement until the absence is a full 24 hour period (midnight to midnight).

If a CA HCC- only carer is imprisoned and their partner does not provide daily care to the care receiver, cancel the card (it cannot be suspended) from the date the carer entered gaol or psychiatric care.

A 13 week review will be set up automatically once the suspension is coded.

If the carer remains in gaol or psychiatric confinement for 13 weeks or more, when the review matures it will cancel CA from the day after the date the carer had entered custody. The carer does not lose entitlement until the absence is a full 24 hour period (midnight to midnight).

If the carer has already been confined for 13 weeks or more when Centrelink is notified of the absence, then CA may be cancelled immediately, without suspension, from the day after the carer had entered custody. The carer does not lose entitlement until the absence is a full 24 hour period (midnight to midnight).

Has the carer already been confined for 13 weeks or more?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Suspend payment + Read more ...

Suspend the payment using the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow:

  • Suspend from the day after the customer has entered custody, using reason code 'Imprisonment' ('IMP'). The carer does not lose entitlement until the absence is a full 24 hour period (midnight to midnight). This will produce a letter to the carer advising of the suspension
  • Defer any circumstance and/or medical reviews during the period of suspension
  • A review will be created on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen for 13 weeks from the date they were confined. If the review matures and the carer is still confined, the payment will cancel from the date they were confined and produce a letter advising them of the cancellation
  • Restoration, if required, restore from the date the carer is released. The review will be deleted

Go to Step 8.

If the workflow is not working or available, complete manual coding. Go to Step 7 in Table 4.

7

Cancel payment + Read more ...

Cancel the payment using the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow:

  • cancel from the date that the carer is confined, using the reason code 'Imprisonment' ('IMP'). This will produce a letter to the carer advising of the cancellation
  • carers cancelled 'IMP' must lodge a new claim if they wish to receive payment once they have been released from confinement, as they cannot be reinstated
  • CA-HCC only coding must be completed manually – use code CCA
  • go to Step 8

If the workflow is not working or available, coding must be completed manually. For manual instructions, go to Step 8 in Table 4.

8

Carer in gaol or psychiatric confinement in connection with an offence + Read more ...

From 27 September 2006, respite days cannot be used where the carer is in gaol, or in psychiatric confinement in connection with an offence.

If a carer is in periodic detention or weekend detention for a whole 24 hour period (midnight to midnight), they lose qualification for CA as respite cannot be used for this absence and therefore, daily care is not being provided.

Suspend CA from the day after the carer enters custody. The carer does not lose entitlement until the absence is a full 24 hour period (midnight to midnight). A 13 week review will be set up automatically once the suspension is coded.

If the carer remains in gaol or psychiatric confinement for 13 weeks or more, when the review matures it will cancel CA from the day after the date the carer had entered custody. The carer does not lose entitlement until the absence is a full 24 hours period (midnight to midnight).

If the carer has already been confined for 13 weeks or more when Centrelink is notified of the absence, then CA is cancelled immediately, without suspension, from the day after the carer had entered custody. The carer does not lose entitlement until the absence is a full 24 hour period (midnight to midnight).

Note: this rule does not apply to Health Care Card (HCC) only CA customers. HCC only carers do not receive a payment - the HCC is issued in the child's name only and will continue to remain current, unless another carer lodges a claim.

Has the carer already been confined for 13 weeks or more?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Suspend payment + Read more ...

Suspend the payment using the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow in Customer First. For workflow instructions, see Step 5 in Table 2.

Only use manual updates if the workflow is not working.

Suspend the payment via the Benefit Action (BA) page/screen. In the following fields:

  • Svc Rsn, key 'CDA' (for Carer Allowance)
  • Action, key 'SUS' (for suspend)
  • Reason, key 'IMP' (for imprisonment)
  • Effect Date, the payment is suspended from the date following the date that the carer is confined. The carer does not lose entitlement until he/she has spent the whole day (midnight to midnight) imprisoned. Note: if the carer has already been confined for 13 weeks or more when Centrelink is notified of the absence, then CA is cancelled immediately
  • Notes, key relevant notes
  • Source, key notification source
  • Date of receipt, key date of notification

The suspension action will produce a letter to the carer advising of the suspension.

Defer any circumstance and/or medical reviews during the period of suspension.

A review will be created on the Future Activity List (FAL) page/screen for 13 weeks from the date they were confined. If the review matures and the carer is still confined, the payment will cancel from the date they were confined and produce a letter advising them of the cancellation.

Restoration. If restoration is required, restore from the date the carer is released. The review will be deleted. Go to Step 11.

Note: if a CA HCC only carer is imprisoned, cancel the card (it cannot be suspended), go to Step 10.

10

Cancel payment + Read more ...

Cancel the payment using the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow in Customer First. For workflow instructions, see Step 6 in Table 2.

Only use manual updates if the workflow is not working.

If the carer is still in gaol or psychiatric confinement after 13 weeks, CA is cancelled. The payment cannot be cancelled 'Imprisonment' ('IMP') unless the carer has already been confined for 13 weeks or more.

Cancel the payment via the BA page/screen, in the ISS system. In the following fields:

  • Svc Rsn, key 'CDA' (for Carer Allowance)
  • Action, key 'CAN' (for cancel)
  • Reason, key 'IMP' (for imprisonment) or for CA HCC only, enter CAA (care and attention not met)
  • Effect Date, if the carer has already been confined for 13 weeks or more when Centrelink is notified of the absence, then CA is cancelled immediately
  • Notes, key relevant notes
  • Source, key notification source
  • Date of receipt, key date of notification

The cancellation action will produce a letter to the customer advising of the cancellation.

Carers cancelled 'IMP' must lodge a new claim if they wish to receive payment once they have been released from confinement, as they cannot be reinstated.
