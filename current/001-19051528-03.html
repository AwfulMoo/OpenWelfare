Assessing study and continuing eligibility for JobSeeker Payment (JSP)

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Contact in relation to returning to full-time study + Read more ...

The customer has advised their intention to return to full-time study. Determine if the customer is a JSP single principal carer of a dependent child. This can be checked by the participation category of the customer on the Participation Summary screen.

Note: If the customer identifies as an Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander, they may be eligible for ABSTUDY. There is no restriction on age or the length of the course. For more information, see Eligibility for ABSTUDY, and Claiming ABSTUDY.

Is the customer a JSP Single Principal Carer?

  • Yes, see Part-time or full-time study as an approved activity for JobSeeker Payment (JSP). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

JSP customer advises they are returning to study + Read more ...

Is the customer enrolled as a full-time student?

Full-time study is defined as 75% of the normal amount of full-time study unless approved for a study load concession.

Note: Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islanders studying part-time or claiming ABSTUDY Incidentals only can test their eligibility for the ABSTUDY part-time award and allowances, see claiming ABSTUDY.

  • Yes, and they have lodged an ABSTUDY living allowance claim, see Table 2 Step 1
  • Yes, and no other claims have been lodged, go to Step 3
  • No, advise the customer that as they are considered to be a part-time student, they may still be entitled to JSP. See JobSeeker Payment (JSP) customers returning to (or continuing in) part-time study. Procedure ends here

3

Approved activity + Read more ...

Is the course an approved activity in the customer's job plan?

  • Yes:
    • under 12 months see Part-time or full-time study as an approved activity for JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
    • 12 months or longer, go to Step 4
  • No, and the course is:
    • under 12 months, see Part time or full time study as an approved activity for JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
    • 12 months or longer, go to Step 4

4

Course is 12 months or longer + Read more ...

Identify the most beneficial payment. See:

  • Assessing and coding a transfer to Austudy or Youth Allowance (YA) student claim from another payment, or
  • Identifying the most beneficial payment for students or Australian Apprentices

If the customer does not want to remain on JSP or they are not eligible, see Step 1 in Table 2.

Cancelling JSP as customer is returning to full time study

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Determine Course start date + Read more ...

See the Resources page for possible exceptions.

If the customer's course start date is:

  • today or a day in the past, go to Step 2
  • in the future, go to Step 3

2

Cancel JSP and determine if Education Entry Payment (EdEP) will be paid + Read more ...

Check if the customer is eligible for an EdEP and advise them accordingly - See Education Entry Payment (EdEP).

  • Update the following fields on the Miscellaneous Update Details (NMIS) screen:
    • Current Circumstances: field – code either 'FTS' if no EdEP is to be paid or 'FTE' if EdEP is to be paid
    • Event Date: field – type the date the customer commenced their study (DPT+1 if study approved)
  • Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen. This will automatically cancel the customer's JSP (JSP/CAN-STU-CUS COMMENCE/RESUME STUDIES *AUTO) from the day study commences, and pay the EdEP if entitled
  • Advise the customer their Health Care Card will be cancelled:
    • Customers should claim a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC)
    • LIC can be claimed separately, or within a student claim if the customer is applying for a student payment
    • If the student payment is granted, and the customer has had 8 weeks payment continuity, they will be eligible for a verbal LIC claim
  • Record a DOC detailing the decision and discussion with the customer

Go to Step 5.

3

Customer's course start date is in the future + Read more ...

If the customer's course start date is:

  • in the future and they have lodged an ABSTUDY claim:
    • place the ABSTUDY claim on Hold until the course start date
    • use reason, Future transfer
    • Procedure ends here
  • within the next 14 days, go to Step 4
  • more than 14 days in the future, advise the customer that they must contact Services Australia again by the end of their payment fortnight in which they commence studying. Go to Step 5

4

Customer's course start date is within 14 days + Read more ...

NMIS screen coding of the customer's return to full-time study cannot occur prior to the course start date. Create a future activity that will fall due at the course start date.

On the Review Registration (RVR) screen record:

  • Service Reason: JSP
  • Review Reason: STU (Follow up studies commitment)
  • Due Date: The anticipated course start date
  • Source: Enter source of information (PHO, VER, INV)
  • Date of Receipt: The date the customer advised
  • Notes: CUS will commence FTS on xx/xx/xxxx. JSP to be CAN-STU via NMIS on due date. Check EdEP eligibility. CUS has been advised that JSP will stop on this date. CUS to advise before course start date if they will not commence their studies
  • NB: Add course start date at xx/xx/xxxx
  • Keywords: JSK2FTS
  • Transfer to Region: Enter customer's home region code

Advise the customer that if their course start date changes, or they choose not to commence the course, they must contact us before the course start date they have advised in this contact to ensure their payments are not cancelled.

Go to Step 5.

5

Advise of student claim options + Read more ...

Determine the most appropriate service offer for the customer. If the customer is:

  • an Indigenous Australian, see eligibility for ABSTUDY
  • 25 years of age and over, see eligibility for Austudy
  • Under 25 years of age, see Youth Allowance (YA) qualifications for students and Australian Apprentices

Is the customer interested in testing their eligibility for a student payment?

  • No, procedure ends here
  • Yes, go to Step 6

6

Intent to Claim + Read more ...

If the customer is intending to apply for:

  • ABSTUDY, see Claiming ABSTUDY - claims can be lodged up to 13 weeks prior to study commencing
  • Austudy, see Initial contact with students or Australian Apprentices claiming Austudy
  • Youth Allowance, see Initial contact with students or Australian Apprentices claiming Youth Allowance (YA)

Advise the customer to lodge an Online Claim for YAL or Austudy within 14 days of their course commencing.
