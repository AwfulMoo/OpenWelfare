Finalised and not yet finalised claims

  • Service line information relates to a particular service, the PF keys can be used to gain further information
  • Medicare claiming history relates to services other than a particular service line

Table 1

Claim is

Description

Not yet finalised

A claim is not finalised if the Medicare benefit has not been issued, for example, where the claim has been pended.

The claim may or may not have been processed.

Finalised

A claim is considered to be finalised when either:

  • It has been assessed and a Medicare benefit has been issued for the service
  • No benefit has been paid and a statement has been issued to the claimant explaining why no benefit has been issued
  • Benefit has been held as claimant has not provided valid temporary or permanent EFT details at the time the claim is submitted

Information disclosed to claimant after security met

Table 2

When the claim is...

Information that can be released

Not yet finalised

Information that can be disclosed to the claimant at this time must be limited to the progress of the claim, such as the date of processing or the status of the claim.

Finalised

When a claim has been finalised, the claimant can be advised that a Medicare benefit has been paid or held due to no EFT details provided and no further information can be provided.

If a claimant is questioning a cheque or EFT payment, a cheque or EFT trace can be performed. See Cheque trace requests for patient claims in Medicare.

  • Parents who are claimants for their children under 14 years of age can be given service line information about a particular claim for their child
  • Service line information for a child 14 years and over is only available to the patient

Where a claimant requests information about Medicare claiming history after the claim has been finalised, advise the claimant to submit a request in writing. See also: Release of information for Medicare.
