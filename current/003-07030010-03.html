Step

Action

1

Customer contact + Read more ...

Customers affected by family and domestic violence can contact a Smart Centre or attend a service centre to claim a Crisis Payment (CrP).

Go to Step 2.

2

Pending CrP-EXV social work referral on the system + Read more ...

Check if there is a pending CrP-EXV referral.

If there is a pending CrP-EXV referral, tell the customer that a social worker will contact them as soon as possible. A text will be sent before the call if the customer is registered for pre call SMS. The call will come from a private number. Make sure their contact details are current.

Note: the system will block a referral if there is an existing one in progress, or on hold for the same type of referral. Staff can make updates to existing referrals.

If there is no pending CrP-EXV referral, go to Step 3.

3

Not claimed online but has experienced family and domestic violence + Read more ...

  • Follow the Risk Identification and Referral (RIR) model and offer a referral to Social Work Services for immediate help such as referrals to local support services. This is to be done for all cases of family and domestic violence
  • Do not ask an alleged or actual perpetrator of family and domestic violence the Risk Identification question. Other than this, the RIR Model must be followed to make sure alleged or actual perpetrators of family and domestic violence are still offered appropriate referrals
    If the customer declines a referral to Social Work Services for immediate help but want to claim a Crisis Payment due to family and domestic violence review the eligibility criteria
  • To be eligible for CrP a customer must be receiving, or have claimed and be qualified (and payable) for a:
    • Social security benefit
    • Social security pension or
    • ABSTUDY (ABY) Living Allowance
      Note: on the benefit status line in the customer's record, the primary payment must show as 'CUR' (current). See View receipt of ABSTUDY Living Allowance on the system

4

Tried to claim online but did not meet the eligibility criteria + Read more ...

If there is no pending social work referral for CrP-EXV on the system offer a referral to a social worker under Family and Domestic Violence support required.

If the customer accepts:

  • Attempt to warm hand-off customer to a social worker via the Social Work Line or follow local arrangements to refer customer to a social worker in service centre
  • If a social worker is not available:
    • in Customer First, run the Social Work Referral Guided Procedure
    • Select Family and Domestic Violence
    • Select Referral Type: Support Required
  • Record the customer’s intent to claim a CrP-EXV using the Crisis Payment - Intent to claim Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Emergency > Claim > CrP Intent to Claim
    • Clearly DOC the discussion on the customer’s record (for example, customer attempted to claim CrP-EXV online but was streamed out due to ineligibility)
    • Close the Fast Note

If the customer does not accept:

  • Record the customer’s intent to claim a CrP-EXV using the Crisis Payment - Intent to claim Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Emergency > Claim > CrP Intent to Claim
    • Clearly DOC the discussion on the customer’s record (for example, customer declined offer of referral to social worker. Customer attempted to claim CrP-EXV online but was streamed out due to ineligibility)
    • Once completed, close the Fast Note
  • Offer the customer the opportunity to test eligibility for an Urgent Payment or Advance Payment

As of 1 August 2021, the paper claim option is no longer available for CrP-EXV.

5

Social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving or has claimed a social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance?

Note: if the customer does not have a status of CUR for a social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance, check the record for a submitted income support payment claim.

  • Yes, (the customer is either receiving or has claimed) go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Customer not receiving or entitled to a social security pension, benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance + Read more ...

  • Help the customer with Payment Finder and tell them to lodge a claim for the relevant income support payment or ABY Living Allowance payment online on the day of contact
  • Tell the customer to claim CrP online after lodging the claim for an income support payment or ABY Living Allowance
  • Record the customer’s intent to claim a CrP - EXV using the Crisis Payment - Intent to claim Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Emergency > Claim > CrP Intent to Claim). Once completed, close the Fast Note
  • If more help and support is needed, offer a referral to Social Work Services. A warm transfer to the Social Work Line should be attempted first or follow local arrangements to refer the customer to a social worker. If a social worker is unavailable:
    • In Customer First, run the Social Work Referral Guided Procedure
    • Select Family and Domestic Violence
    • Select Referral Type: Support Required

Procedure ends here.

7

Does customer want to pursue CrP–EXV claim? + Read more ...

Explain to the customer that a CrP claim needs to be lodged and a referral to a social worker is needed to assess their eligibility. A social worker can let them know about other help that might be available.

Offer to help the customer lodge a CrP claim using an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC).

Tell the customer this will take about 20 minutes.

Does the customer want to claim Crisis Payment now?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 12

8

Start Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) + Read more ...

Open the ACC desktop icon.

  • Enter the customer’s CRN and select Go
  • Select Make a new claim
  • Go to the Crisis Payment tab and select Get started
  • Select Apply for Crisis Payment for Extreme Circumstances
  • Read the questions to the customer and select their answer
  • If the customer says it is not safe for a social worker to contact them, ask for an alternative contact number in the claim
    If they cannot give an alternative number, do not continue the claim. Tell the customer to call back when they are safe and ask to speak with a social worker
    Record the customer’s intent to claim a CrP-EXV using the Crisis Payment - Intent to claim Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Emergency > Claim > CrP Intent to Claim. Once completed, close the Fast Note
  • If the Service Officer is streamed out of the ACC as the customer does not meet 1 of the basic eligibility criteria, a claim will not be submitted:
    • Record the customer's intent to claim using the Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Emergency > Claim > CrP Intent to Claim
    • Clearly DOC the discussion (for example, ACC run for CrP-EXV claim but streamed out due to ineligibility)
    • Once completed, close the Fast Note
    • Offer a referral to a social worker
    • If a customer is on the phone attempt a warm transfer to the Social Work Line
      If a social worker is not available, run the Social Work Referral guided procedure in Customer First
      - select Family and Domestic Violence
      - select Referral Type: Support Required
    • If the customer is at a service centre follow local arrangements and refer to a social worker. If a social worker is not available attempt a warm transfer to the Social Work Line
      If a social worker is not available run the Social Work Referral guided procedure in Customer First
      - select Family and Domestic Violence
      - select Referral Type: Support Required

Tell the customer that for financial hardship, liquid assets such as shares will also be assessed.

Check that all information is correct on the Review your claim page:

  • If answers need to be corrected, select the relevant Update button
  • If personal details need correcting, exit the ACC and update using the Change in Contact Details workflow. This information will then populate into the claim
  • Go back into the ACC

Claim declaration/evidence requirements

  • Select the my obligations hyperlink (bottom of review page)
  • Read the claim declaration to the customer
  • If the hyperlink is unavailable, see the Resources page for a copy of the online claim declaration text
  • Select ‘I have read, understood and accept my obligations’ check box
  • Record the following information on the customer’s record via Note in Process Direct: 'Verbal signature declaration script read and accepted by customer/nominee. CDF not required’
  • Based on the customer’s answers, they may need to supply evidence of bank balances and/or evidence of the extreme circumstance (FDV). The customer should be advised to provide this evidence and submit the started claim online
  • ACC will allow evidence to be marked as received and allow the claim to lodge. This should only be done in exceptional circumstances with Team Leader approval as the customer is still required to provide evidence before the claim can be processed
  • Select Submit

Tell the customer:

  • A social worker will call them to discuss their claim as soon as possible
  • The call will come from a private number

Do not refer the customer to a social worker via the guided procedure after completing an ACC. The claim will be allocated to a skill tagged CrP EXV Service Officer who will arrange a referral to a social worker if required.

If the customer is in distress, and needs immediate social work help/support, go to Step 9.

Otherwise, procedure ends here.

9

Refer to a social worker + Read more ...

Refer to a social worker after the Start Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) has been completed.

Explain to the customer that a social worker assessment is required to determine their eligibility for CrP. A social worker can also let them know about other help that might be available.

Smart Centre staff refer through the SW line.

Service centre staff follow local arrangements to refer the customer to a social worker.

Go to Step 10.

10

Social worker available + Read more ...

Is a social worker available?

  • Yes,
    • Complete the transfer of the customer to the social worker
    • Record a DOC on the customer’s record noting a warm transfer to a social worker has taken place for a Crisis Payment EXV assessment
    • Service Officer procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 11

11

Social worker not available + Read more ...

If a social worker is not available (for example, outside business hours) make sure of the customer’s safety tell the customer:

  • a social worker will call them as soon as possible to talk about their claim
  • a text message will be sent before a social worker calls, if the customer is registered for pre-call SMS
  • the call will come from a private number

Do not refer the customer to a social worker using the guided procedure after completing an ACC. The claim will be allocated to a skill tagged CrP EXV Service Officer who will arrange a referral to a social worker if needed.

Procedure ends here.

12

Customer does not want to pursue claim + Read more ...

A claim for CrP EXV is not pursued when the customer:

  • said they do not want to speak to a social worker
  • said they do not want to pursue a claim, or
  • has incorrectly enquired about CrP

In all cases where the CrP is not made:

  • tell the customer there are no review and appeal rights when a claim is not made as it has not been formally assessed and determined
  • Clearly DOC the discussion on the customer’s record
  • If appropriate, offer the customer the opportunity to test eligibility for an Urgent Payment or Advance Payment
  • If more help and support is needed, offer a referral to Social Work Services. Follow local arrangements to refer customer to a social worker in the service centre, or attempt a warm transfer to the Social Work Line. If a social worker is unavailable:
    • In Customer First, run the Social Work Referral Guided Procedure
    • Select Family and Domestic Violence
    • Select Referral Type: Support Required

Service Officer procedure ends here.

7

Confirm eligibility criteria - customer contact within 7 days of event date (Deemed claim provisions) + Read more ...

A customer must contact Centrelink within 7 days of the date of the extreme circumstance.

After initial contact, the customer must lodge the claim within 14 days. See Contact for the purposes of intent to claim and deemed claim.

If there is information showing exceptional circumstances, which delayed the customer in lodging a claim, such as a hospital admission, refer the matter to a social worker for assessment to determine if the customer meets the allowable timeframe.

If the claim was lodged between 8 and 21 days after the event date, the following warning will display:

'SR612 - Extreme circumstance is between 8-21 days, check contact date'

Note: the event date is considered to be day 0.

When did the customer lodge the claim?

  • Within 7 days after event date, go to Step 9
  • Within 8-21 days after the event date (SR612 warning message displaying), go to Step 8

8

Customer lodged claim between 8-21 days after event date + Read more ...

Check DOCs to see if there was contact about a Crisis Payment, or seeking a payment or concession card within 7 days of the date of the extreme circumstance (event) date. If there is a record of contact, the customer satisfies the contact requirements for the CrP claim, and deemed provisions apply.

Did the customer contact within 7 days of the extreme circumstance (event) date?

  • Yes:
    • Update the Override LOD rejection field to Yes
    • Select Save
    • Go to Step 9
  • No, the customer is not eligible
    • The system will display rejection reason LOD on the ELD screen
    • Select Assess and then Next
    • Go to Step 10

9

Confirm eligibility criteria - customer in Australia + Read more ...

The customer must be inside Australia and living in Australia on the date of claim in order to be granted a Crisis Payment. The system will check if the customer is recorded as being outside Australia or living in another country and present the following warning:

‘SR598 - Customer may not be in Australia’

If this warning presents:

  • Check Travel Outside Australia (TOAS) screen to see if the customer is outside Australia:
    • Refresh the Immigration Datalink
    • If the record shows they are outside Australia, but the Service Officer believes they are in Australia, see Activating the Department of Home Affairs datalink and contingency procedures if datalink is unavailable
  • Check Country of Residency (CRES) screen to see if the customer is living in Australia:
    • If the information needs to be updated, see Coding the Country of Residence (CRES) screen

Was the customer in Australia when they lodged their claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, customer is not eligible, reject the claim:
    • Update the Manual rejection field to NIA - Not in Australia
    • Select Assess and then Next
    • Go to Step 10

Social worker referral + Read more ...

If the ELD screen is showing:

  • grant, a social worker referral is mandatory
    • a social worker must perform an assessment before the claim can be finalised
  • rejection reason NQP and a claim for social security benefit, social security pension or ABSTUDY Living Allowance is pending, a referral to social worker must be made
    • continue to assess the CrP claim should with the pending social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance claim

If urgent handoff is appropriate, see Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing to have social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance claim completed. Place the CrP claim on hold and note CrP claim is awaiting ISP updates.

Check Notes in Process Direct or DOCs in Customer First for a grant, reject, withdrawn or assessment in progress Fast Note from a social worker.

  • If the Fast Note is on the record, see Table 5.
  • If the Fast Note is not on the customer's record, a social worker assessment has not been completed yet. Check if there is a pending social work referral (CrP-EXV) on the customer's record.
  • Check existing referrals on the Activity tab in Customer First or on the Work List in Process Direct
  • If there is no social work referral pending refer the claim to a social worker:
    • Select > Referral
    • Referral Type: Social Worker
    • Referral Reason: Crisis Payment
    • Select Finish
    • This will place the claim on hold until a social worker completes their assessment

Once the social worker assessment has been completed, the claim will return to In progress and allocate for finalisation. The social worker will complete a grant, reject, or claim withdrawn Fast Note advising the outcome of their assessment.
