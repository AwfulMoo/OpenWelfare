Initiate payment procedure - batching

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Batching TFES claim + Read more ...

After a TFES claim is assessed, it goes through a daily payment initiation process.

  • Claims are displayed on the Initiate Payment screen, in myHSI > Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme TFES General
  • A maximum of 20 claims are displayed on the screen at a time
  • Claims are grouped in claim types. E.G. TFES, KIFG and Other Markets
  • Claims are displayed in Claim ID numerical order

The daily payment initiation process occurs at 3pm to allow time for approval of claims with interest payable.

Send a weekly email to the Assessor distribution list to advise the processing team the batch run time and to enter completed claim details on the TFES batch spreadsheet.

2

Responsibility for initiating payment and access needed + Read more ...

Payments are initiated by:

  • Service Support Officers (SSO)
  • Team Leaders, or
  • Manager within Tasmanian Transport Program (TTP) Team

To initiate payment, Team Leader system access is needed. Request the access via ICT Security Portal (ISP) - SP (ACF (SAMS) PROD-TFES).

3

TFES batch spreadsheet + Read more ...

The TFES batch spreadsheet located in G:\Transport_Resources\STATISTICS & REPORTS\Batch Statistics.

After a claim is complete and ready for payment, processing staff enter the claim details in the spreadsheet.

The spreadsheet has 4 tabs:

  • TFES (Domestic claims)
  • KIFG (King Island and Furneaux Group
  • OM (Other Markets)
  • IMPORTS (Imported Assistance claims)

Before initiating payment:

  • access the TFES batch spreadsheet
  • sort each tab into numerical order, to match the myHSI payment schedule
  • save the updated spreadsheet in G:\Transport_Resources\STATISTICS & REPORTS\Batch Statistics\20XX\YYYYMMDD
  • delete the daily spreadsheet

4

Procedure in myHSI + Read more ...

Claims are paid through myHSI>Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme TFES General.

Retrieve claims

  • go to myHSI>Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme TFES General
  • in myHSI>Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme TFES General, on the Activity List screen
    • select Initiate Payment from the left hand side list
  • on the Initiate Payment screen:
    • select All from the Assistance Scheme dropdown menu, and
    • select Pending Payment from the Claim Status dropdown menu
  • select the Show button

5

Check for payable interest + Read more ...

On the Initiate Payment screen check Interest Amt $. This shows interest amounts payable.

Before paying the claim, complete a number of checks.

Note: by paying the claim, the interest is also paid.

Is the claim subject to any interest amount?

  • Yes, see Table 2
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Match the Claim ID in the spreadsheet with those in myHSI + Read more ...

Each screen displays a maximum of 20 claims for payment. The claims display in order of Claim ID.

  • Match the Claim ID in the spreadsheet with those in myHSI
  • Confirm the claim ID, payee and the amount of assistance assessed
  • Tick the check box for each matching claim if correct
  • Note the number of claims to be paid for each page (batch)
  • Select Pay to process the payment
  • Select confirm/continue after reading the declaration

Repeat for each page of claims on the spreadsheet.

Claim ID is on the spreadsheet but not in the payment system

  • Access the user logon against the claim ID in myHSI
  • Contact the staff member via MS Teams
  • Tell the staff member the claim status is not pending payment, ask them to accept the claim
  • Ask the staff member to enter the claim on the TFES batch spreadsheet for the following day

Claim is in the Initiate Payment screen but not on the spreadsheet

  • Check the claim comments in myHSI, confirmation from the service officer may be needed:
    • confirm the claim has been completed, and
    • is ready to be paid
  • Add the claim to the spreadsheet
  • Provide feedback to the service officer

7

Completed Payment + Read more ...

Check the number of claims processed and paid against the number showing in payment utility (PU).

  • Go into TFES PU platform.
    • Logon (csda.gov.au)

Note: request access via emailing Tasmania Transport Programmes Team.

PU payments platform

  • select PU payment business report
  • select Technical reconciliation report tile:
    • Agency field select 1010 Centrelink administered
    • Program select TFES
    • Created Date select the Batch Payment Date
    • Select Go. This shows the number of claims scheduled to be paid and the total $ amount

Do the number of claims paid in PU and Initiate Payment on myHSI match?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Email notification of claims paid + Read more ...

Advise the service delivery team and Service Support Manager (EL1):

  • Email the details of the day’s batch payment to the Assessor distribution list, with a copy to the EL1 for the service delivery team, include:
    • the number of claims paid
    • the value of the claims paid
    • Procedure ends here

9

Further investigation + Read more ...

If the number of scheduled claims do not match the number in the Initiate Payment screen on myHSI, double check before:

  • escalating to CCeS helpdesk via a webform located in G:\NBG_ALL\Incident Management\Testing\IW.accdb
  • sending an email to Business Process Branch detailing the discrepancy. Instructions/advice will be provided

Procedure ends here.

Check claims for interest

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check for online lodgement + Read more ...

Interest is only payable if the claim and all supporting documents are submitted online.

Was the claim and all supporting documents submitted online?

  • Yes, interest may be payable. Go to next Step 2
  • No, interest is not payable. Change the ALD to ‘date of payment’. This removes the interest payable. See Table 3

2

Check the Adjusted Lodgement Date (ALD) + Read more ...

  • Check the ALD is entered correctly from the claim
  • Check requests for additional claim information. Make sure additional information was submitted online

Is the ALD correct?

  • Yes, interest may be payable. Go to Step 3
  • No, ALD must be corrected / adjusted before payment, see Table 3

3

Interest $100 or over + Read more ...

Interest is not payable if less than $100.

Is the Interest showing $100 or over?

  • Yes, interest may be payable. Go to Step 4
  • No, document a note on the Claim Comments including:
    • interest is not payable as under $100
    • the amount of interest, and
    • the claim attracted interest under $100
    • go to Step 6

4

Calculate Interest + Read more ...

The interest payable is calculated using the General Interest Charge (GIC) rates, set quarterly by the Australian Taxation Office (ATO)

Manually check the interest calculation, using the formula:

  • Claim Amount

multiplied by

  • Number of Days of interest is payable

multiplied by

  • GIC Daily Interest percentage (GIC as per ATO)

Does the manual calculation match the interest amount on Interest Amt $?

  • Yes, interest may be payable. Go to Step 5
  • No, conduct further investigation. Go to Step 8

5

Escalate possible interest payment + Read more ...

Immediately email interest calculations to Tasmanian Transport Programmes:

  • Go to Tasmanian Transport Programmes
  • Scroll down to Internal contact details
  • Use the mailbox details for Tasmanian Transport Programmes, and
  • Mark the email as ‘Urgent’

The Tasmanian Transport Programme team EL1 checks and approves the calculation.

Is the calculation correct and approved in writing?

  • Yes, pay the claim. Go to Step 7
  • No, conduct further investigation. Go to Step 8

6

Interest not payable + Read more ...

Progress the claim payment to batch payment. Interest is not paid, as it is under $100.

  • Tick the check box in Initiate Payment and select Pay
  • Document the reason interest is not payable on Comments tab

Procedure ends here.

7

Claim and interest payable + Read more ...

Pay the assistance amount and interest amount:

  • Tick the check box in Initiate Payment and select Pay
  • A comment generates with details of the interest payment
  • Check the Interest tab to ensure the interest details are correct

Is the interest amount paid showing correct?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, conduct further investigation. Go to Step 8

8

Further investigation + Read more ...

Send an email to Tasmanian Transport Programmes. A system error may need to be raised urgently.

Do not proceed with payment until advised by the Programme team.

Procedure ends here.

Return payments from batch

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Pending Payment status + Read more ...

Once a claim is assessed and accepted for payment, the status changes to Pending Payment. Claims remain in pending payment status until the payment is initiated in myHSI.

Claims can be recalled if in the Pending Payment status before the batch is run if:

  • the ALD is incorrect or needs changing
  • the claim needs readjustment before payment
  • incorrect bank details have been recorded, or
  • there is a system issue

Document the change and the reason for the change on the Comments tab.

2

Returning claim from payment + Read more ...

Service Officer action:

  • identifies that a claim needs to be returned from Pending Payment or Failed Payment
  • provides the Claim ID to SSO or Team Leader, verbally or by email and requests the claim is returned

SSO or Team Leader action:

  • SSO/Team Leader accesses Initiate Payment in myHSI>Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme TFES General
  • from the Assistance Scheme drop down box selects All
  • from the Claim Status drop down box selects either:
    • Pending Payment or Failed Payment, and
    • select Show
  • selects appropriate Claim ID, as supplied by Service Officer and select Reassess

This action removes the claim from the batch and the claim can be selected from the Activity List, in myHSI>Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme TFES General by the Service Officer and actioned as required.

Rejected SAP payment

Table 4: this table details how to action a returned Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme (TFES) payment.

Step

Action

1

Rejected payments + Read more ...

A financial institution may return a TFES payment due to a data match with the account details. E.G. an error in the BSB, account number or account title.

An email is received to notify of the rejected and returned payment.

2

Procedure for a rejected SAP payment + Read more ...

Find the most recent TAS020 on either the:

  • claimant’s Documents tab in myHSI
  • claimant’s file in R:TAS\HOB-188-Collins\ TTP Resource Guide, or
  • claimant’s paper file in the TFES compactus

Do the details on the TAS020 match the Claimant Details screen?

  • Yes, contact the claimant to confirm the details and ask for a new TAS020 if needed
  • No, update the details as per the TAS020

Note: if the most recent TAS020 is older than 5 years, contact the claimant to ask for a new one.

3

Notify payment utility helpdesk + Read more ...

Send an email with the new account details and the updated TAS020 form to:

  • Payment Utility Helpdesk
  • Cc Tasmanian Transport Programmes
  • Cc Tasmanian Transport Management, and
  • Cc Treasury Help
