Process Direct

Request information

Step

Action

1

Determine what information is required from the customer + Read more ...

  • If the information required has already been requested, go to Step 2
  • To confirm care arrangements (for OLC, ACC, paper claims and Add Newborn activities), go to Step 4
  • For help using > Request Documents in OLC, ACC or Add Newborn activities, go to Step 8
  • For help using the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure in Customer First for paper claim requests (except requests for care arrangements,) go to Step 9
  • If the information is not provided by the due date as requested, go to Step 12

2

Information was previously requested + Read more ...

If the information has already been requested twice from the customer (via Circumstance Change Monitor, Q777 or Request Documents), go to Step 11.

Check if the customer has made a genuine attempt to provide the information, or if considered at risk/vulnerable.

A genuine attempt is where the customer took steps to provide an acceptable document to support their claim, but the document is not sufficient for their claim to be assessed. See Resources page for examples.

At risk/vulnerability indicators:

  • DSP/CUR
  • homelessness/ at risk indicator on record
  • evidence of domestic violence
  • primary language other than English or interpreter required

Has the customer made a genuine attempt to provide the requested information, or considered at risk/vulnerable?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 11

3

Contact customer/send new request + Read more ...

Make a genuine attempt to contact the customer by phone to request the information. Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) can be used to inform the customer of contact by phone.

Can all the required information be obtained verbally?

  • Yes, record the details when processing the claim and document the discussion/information provided. Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • To confirm care arrangements, go to Step 4
    • For all other requests in an OLC, ACC or Add Newborn activity, go to Step 8
    • For all other requests in a paper claim, go to Step 9

4

Care arrangements to be confirmed + Read more ...

Is the information being requested from the customer in a new claim work item?

  • Yes, and it is
    • an FTB or combined FTB/PPL claim, see the Customer First tab
    • an OLC, ACC or PPL Add Newborn activity, go to Step 5
    • a paper claim, go to Step 7
  • No:
    • For an FTB or FTB/PPL Add Newborn activity, see the Customer First tab
    • If the other carer has an existing record, go to Step 7
    • If the other carer does not have an existing record but enough information to create a record has/can be supplied, create a new record and link the children to the new record. See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions. Go to Step 7
    • If the other carer does not have an existing record, and there is not enough information to create a record, go to Step 5

5

Requesting care information in a new claim or Add Newborn activity work item + Read more ...

The request for information is being made in respect of the claimant, or another carer without an existing record. A separate request will need to be sent for each carer.

Before requesting information for a child inside a new claim or Add Newborn activity, make sure all children have a confirmed or provisional link on Transaction Summary (TS). See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions.

Select the Work item.

On the TS screen, select > Request Documents

In the Maintain table, update:

  • Benefit: Benefit being claimed by the customer in context (for Add Newborns this should be FTB or PPL as appropriate)
  • Benefit Status: New Claim
  • Authority to Request: Family Assistance (Admin) Act 1999
  • Customer Required to: Mail or bring in documents
  • Request: Select first, second or third
  • Information to be supplied within: Select Other:
    • If both the customer and the other carer are subscribed to myGov Inbox insert 16 days
    • If at least one of the caring parties is not subscribed to myGov Inbox insert 22 days
    • To determine if a customer is subscribed go to the Electronic Messaging and Online Letters (EMOL) screen and check for Channel Type: myGov Inbox
    • See allow extra time for mail delivery for more details on delivery times
  • Staff Notes: record details of the request such as date of event, children and care percentage advised. These notes will display in the task details and cannot be viewed by the customer

In the Available Documents table, search and select FCC Shared Care. Once selected the Additional Details table is displayed.

In the Additional Details section:

  • Select the radio button under Request by to indicate which party initiated the Shared Care assessment. This can be either Customer or Other (a third party such as another carer)

Enter the details of each child in the Children table:

  • Select Add Child to launch the Select a Child dialogue box
  • Select the relevant child from the Child drop down:
    • Percentage of Care (%): as notified by the customer or other party
      Note: this may be left blank if unknown
    • Start date: date care arrangement commenced
    • End date: date care arrangement ended (if not ongoing)
  • Select Save
  • Complete this for every individual child to be included in the letter

If the request is being made for a caring party that does not have an existing Centrelink record, answer Yes to Is this letter being sent to a carer who does not have a record and enter the carer’s personal details including name and address. Otherwise answer No.

Do not enter free text in the free text field below the Children table.

Once all children have been added to the Children table, use to move the FCC Shared Care letter to the Selected Document table.

Select Finish

Note: there is no need to issue an FA012 locally, customers are to follow the instructions contained in the Request for Information (RFI) to access the form online.

Go to Step 6.

6

Work item held + Read more ...

The RFI will automatically place the work item On hold for the number of days selected in the Request Documents application.

Once the claimant completes the online task or the hold time expires, the claim status will change to In Process and the work item will be allocated for action. 'SHARED' keyword will be automatically assigned to the work item.

Note: If there is a corresponding NCL activity on the AL screen in Customer First, this must also be placed on hold with the same resubmit date as the Process Direct work item.

Go to Step 11.

7

Requesting care information in a non-new claim work item or paper claim + Read more ...

The request for information is being made for the other carer who has not submitted a claim, or for a claimant who has submitted a paper claim.

Open Customer Summary from the Process Direct landing page and search for the customer’s record.

Once located select > Request Documents. Update the following sections.

Basic Information:

  • Benefit: This should be customer’s benefit or the benefit of the claimant if they are not current
  • Authority to request: Family Assistance (Admin) Act 1999
  • Request: Select first, second or third
  • Mailing address to be included in the letter: Yes or No
  • Customer to contact: Call Centre
  • Information to be supplied within: Select Other:
    • If both the customer and the other carer are subscribed to myGov Inbox insert 16 days
    • If at least one of the caring parties is not subscribed to myGov Inbox insert 22 days
    • To determine if a customer is subscribed go to the Electronic Messaging and Online Letters (EMOL) screen and check for Channel Type: myGov Inbox
    • See allow extra time for mail delivery for more details on delivery times
  • Select Next

Select Documents:

  • In Available documents, search and select FCC Shared Care. Once this has been selected, it will appear in the Selected Documents table
    Note: if the FCC Shared Care document is in the Favourites table it can be added to Selected Documents directly from there without searching
  • Select Next

Additional Details

  • Select the radio button under Contact initiated by to indicate which party initiated the Shared Care assessment
  • This can be either Customer or Other (a third party such as another carer)

Enter the details of each child in the Children table:

  • Select Add to launch the Add Children dialogue box
  • Select the relevant child from the CRN drop down or manually add details in the Name and Date of birth field if the child is not linked to the customer
    • Percentage of Care (%): as notified by the customer or other party. Note: this may be left blank if unknown
    • Start date: date care arrangement commenced
    • End date: date care arrangement ended (if not ongoing)
  • Select Save
  • Complete this for every child to be included in the letter
  • Select Next

Final details

The Requesting Document List table will list all documents that have been selected on the previous screens.

In the Output Options select if the document will be printed locally or centrally. Enter any additional details that are required to the Note that has populated in the Staff Notes box. These notes will display on the Notes/DL screen in Process Direct and Customer First.

  • To preview the letter before sending select Generate Print Preview
  • Select Finalise
  • The 'SHARED' keyword will be automatically assigned to the Request for Information

Note: there is no need to issue an FA012 locally, customers are to follow the instructions contained in the Request for Information (RFI) to access the form online.

Go to Step 10.

8

Request Documents for Online and ACC and Add Newborn activities + Read more ...

Check if the customer is subscribed to channel type myGov Inbox on the Electronic Messaging and Online Letter Services (EMOL) screen.

If the customer is not subscribed to myGov Inbox use the Request Documents Q777 Guided Procedure in Customer First to request documents.

For all other Online, ACC and Add Newborn activities, in Process Direct select the Work item.

On the Transaction Summary (TS) screen, select > Request Documents

In the Maintain table, update:

  • Benefit: Benefit being claimed by the customer in context (for Add Newborns this should be FTB or PPL as appropriate)
  • Benefit Status: New Claim
  • Authority to Request: Family Assistance (Admin) Act 1999
  • Customer Required to: Mail or bring in documents
  • Request: Select first, second or third
  • Information to be supplied within: Select Other:
    • If customer is subscribed to myGov Inbox insert 16 days or 30 days when requesting bank details or TFN
    • If customer is not subscribed to myGov Inbox insert 22 days or 30 days when requesting bank details or TFN
    • To determine if a customer is subscribed navigate to the Electronic Messaging and Online Letters (EMOL) screen and check for Channel Type myGov Inbox
    • See allow extra time for mail delivery for more details on delivery times
  • Staff Notes: Yes and record details of the request. These notes will display in the task details and cannot be viewed by the customer

In the Available Documents table, search and select the relevant document. Once selected, the Additional Details table is displayed. For a list of available documents see the Resources page.

In the Additional Details section complete the variable fields as appropriate for the selected document. For a list of additional details options see the Resources page.

Do not enter free text in the free text field below

Once all variables have been completed, use to move the letter to the Selected Document table.

Select Finish

The Request for Information (RFI) will automatically place the work item On hold for the number of days selected in the Request Documents application and attach relevant keywords.

Check the Tasks icon. Where multiple requests have been sent for the same document, update the status of any duplicate requests to Not Required.

Update the Progress of Claim DOC with the information requested.

Note: If there is a corresponding NCL activity on the AL screen in Customer First, this must also be placed on hold with the same resubmit date as the Process Direct work item.

Once the claimant completes the online task or the hold time expires, the claim status will change to In Process and the work item will be allocated for action.

Go to Step 11.

9

Request Documents Q777 guided procedure + Read more ...

  • Launch the guided procedure in Customer First
  • Select Paper claim option
  • The guided procedure will only send a letter to the customer. No online task will be created
  • Update the Progress of Claim DOC with information required and contact attempts made

Timeframe

Information/documentation is generally requested to be provided within 14 days (21 days for remote area or overseas customers). Exception: 28 days is allowed for the provision for Tax File Numbers (TFN) and/or direct credit details.

The Information to be supplied within to the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure allows selection options of 22 days, 29 days, 36 days or 43 days to allow extra time for mail delivery.

Close DOC

After completing the guided procedure, close the DOC on the Activity List (AL) screen in Customer First.

Claim activities in Customer First must be held for a minimum of 22 days to allow extra time for mail delivery and for customers to respond.

The Process Direct new claim work item may be automatically placed on hold. If not, place the work item On hold via the Status icon with the same resubmit date as the Customer First new claim activity on the AL screen.

Go to Step 10.

10

Request for Information sent + Read more ...

The Request for Information (RFI) will be sent to the customer’s online Inbox or by post if printed centrally, depending on their individual preferences.

If it has been printed locally, the staff member will need to post the letter.

If there are any relevant work items on the customer’s record (such as a paper claim):

  • Place them on hold by updating the Status icon in Process Direct
  • Note: paper claim activities must be held for a minimum of 22 days to allow extra time for mail delivery and for customers to respond
  • If it is a request for shared care information:
    • Select the Keyword icon
    • Select Add
    • In the dialogue box, search 'SHARED' and select the Shared Care Claim keyword
    • Any corresponding new claim activity on the AL screen in Customer First must also be placed on hold with the same resubmit date as the work item in Process Direct

When the requested information is returned by the customer, it will be allocated for action through Workload Management (WLM).

Go to Step 11.

11

Information provided + Read more ...

Has the information been provided within the timeframe as requested?

  • Yes, upon receipt:
    • For an online claim, ACC or Add Newborn activity this will be updated automatically and the work item assigned to relevant staff
    • For a non-new claim Request for Information this will be updated automatically and the RFI assigned to relevant staff
    • For a paper claim, update the Progress of claim DOC on Notes (DL) in Process Direct
    • Continue to process the claim. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 12

12

Information not provided by due date + Read more ...

FTB, PPL and DAP claims must be made Not effective or Not required as per existing processes.

See:

  • Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
  • Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • Processing proof of a child's birth

Has an online claim or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) been submitted?

  • Yes, go to Step 6

Requesting evidence of residing in Australia + Read more ...

Has an online claim or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) been submitted?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 8

6

Request Documents Q777 guided procedure + Read more ...

Launch the guided procedure.

Is the information required for a started online claim (or ACC with PHO or INV as the source)?

Select the paper claim option if sending a request for an Add Newborn, or if the customer’s early claim has been assessed and they have lodged an incomplete Newborn Child Declaration (FA081).

  • Yes, select the online claim ID option. This guided procedure will:
    • send a letter to the customer (unless the request relates to a request for evidence of a care arrangement or evidence of residing in Australia)
    • place the Social Application (SOA) on hold - under the interaction history
    • create a DOC on the customer's record
    • create a task for the customer in their online claim or ACC
    • interact with the claim tracker to advise the customer of the progress of their claim
    • send targeted notifications to the customer
  • No, select the paper claim option. The guided procedure will only send a letter to the customer. No online task will be created. Update the Progress of Claim DOC

Timeframe

Generally to be provided within 14 days (21 days for remote area or overseas customers). Exception: 28 days to provide Tax File Numbers and/or direct credit details.

A new Information to be supplied within field has been added to the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure. Options are 22 days, 29 days, 36 days or 43 days to allow extra time for mail delivery.

Close DOC

After completing the guided procedure, close the DOC on the Activity List (AL) screen.

Manually place the claim on hold on the AL screen.

Claim activities must be held for a minimum of 22 days to allow extra time for mail delivery and for customers to respond.

Go to Step 8.

7

Claim required + Read more ...

If the customer is required to lodge a claim, staff must manually create a Q999 letter. A task should not be created for a claim request.

For example, a customer claiming FTB via lump sum claim may need to lodge an FTB instalment claim to be assessed for Rent Assistance. For example letter text, see Rent Assistance (RA) processing in FTB lump sum claims.

Once the Q999 letter has been created, manually place the claim on hold via the Activity List (AL) screen.

Go to Step 8.

8

Is the information provided within the timeframe as requested? + Read more ...

  • Yes, upon receipt:
    • For an online claim or ACC use the Request and Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure to update the status of the relevant task to Provided,
    • Update the Progress of claim DOC
    • Continue to process the claim. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Information not provided by due date + Read more ...

FTB, PPL and DAP claims must be manually be made not effective.

Note: before making the claim 'not effective', check the partner's record (if relevant). Make sure the customer has not provided the requested information on their record instead.

CCB lump sum claims will auto REJ from the day after the due date if the relevant task is listed as Required in the online claim or ACC (CCM exception may apply).

See:

  • Not effective claims or withdrawal of claim for FTB or Stillborn Baby Payment
  • Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for PPL
  • Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for DAP
