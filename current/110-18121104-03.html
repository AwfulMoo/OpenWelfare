1

Initiation + Read more ...

A request to contact letter is issued when a BIBI Intervention has been initiated.

The letter requires the customer to contact to discuss information received relating to their business income or bank interest.

The letter asks the customer to contact within 28 days.

Customers may receive an SMS reminder 5 days after they receive the contact letter. If they do not contact within 14 days, they will also be sent a reminder letter.

For current customers, the letter will advise their payments may be suspended if they do not contact.

2

Contact by customer, their partner or nominee + Read more ...

Follow the phone contact requirements.

Customer contact

  • If responding to the request to contact letter, go to Step 3
  • If responding to the Pre Adverse Decision letter, see Step 4 in Table 2 of Assessing and finalising BIBI interventions
  • If calling back to provide more information that may change the outcome before finalising the decision:
    • record contact details in IRS
    • see Table 2 of Assessing and finalising BIBI interventions

Partner contact

  • If calling to discuss the customer's intervention:
    • Advise the partner:
      'The letter we sent is to advise you that we are checking your partner’s past income information to make sure they received the right amount of payments from us.
      'We have also written to your partner about our check. We are letting you know about our check because you and your partner’s combined income was used to calculate payments
      ' Any change in your partner’s past income may affect you, and may mean we over paid you. If that is the case, you may have to pay money back. You do not need to do anything at the moment. We will let you know once our check is complete. This may take some time.’
    • Document the discussion on the partner's record
  • If calling about the Pre Adverse Decision letter, see Step 4 in Table 2 of Assessing and finalising BIBI interventions

Nominee contact

  • See Disclosing information to a nominee to determine what information may be disclosed to the nominee
  • Record on the customer's record all relevant details of the information given or received

Table 2

Step

1

Discuss match data + Read more ...

A match has occurred because the information on the customer's record differs from the information received from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO).

Check the business income or bank interest details on the customer's record. Discuss the discrepancy with the customer. For help, refer to the relevant screen column in the Assessing and Coding Income and Assets attachment on the Resources page.

The scope start date:

  • the start date of the income, or
  • if the start date is greater than 7 years, 1 July 7 years before the current financial year. For example, for the 2020-21 financial year, the scope start date is 1 July 2013

The scope end date:

  • If the customer is still receiving income from business income or bank interest, Date Paid To
  • If the customer has ceased receiving income, the cessation date

Use questioning skills to obtain as much information as possible from the customer over the phone.

2

Check anomaly or mismatch + Read more ...

If the customer indicates the information is incorrect, thoroughly review the customer record to check for an anomaly or mismatch.

Say to the customer:

'Would you mind holding the line while I check your record.'

Preliminary assessment

Use all information available to decide if an anomaly or mismatch exists between the customer and the match data.

If there are at least 2 commonalities (e.g. name and date of birth matches, or name and address matches) between the customer and the match data, it the match data is not a mismatch and should be continued. Take extra care with twin or intertwined records.

Alternative spelling or misspelling of a customer's name does not automatically mean a mismatch.

Can an anomaly or mismatch be identified from the customer record?

  • Yes, advise the customer:
    • More investigation is needed. Someone will contact them to advise the outcome and next steps
    • If they cannot be contacted, they will need to call back to complete their intervention within 14 days. If they fail to contact within 14 days, the match data may be applied to their record and this may result in a debt
    • Go to Step 3
  • No, continue the intervention:
    • To assess bank interest, go to Step 6
    • To assess business income, go to Step 4

Anomaly or mismatch identified + Read more ...

    • select Save

QDO action

Investigate the mismatch to validate the referral. These can be located by undertaking a National Search (IRNS) screen search using the keyword BIIAMM.

Is the mismatch referral supported?

  • Yes,
    • delete the keyword BIIAMM
    • add the keyword 'AMMYES'

      - details of the mismatch
      - any extra comments that may help the service desk assess the review
  • No,
    • delete the keyword BIIAMM
    • return the referral to the CO with feedback explaining the referral error

6

Discuss bank interest + Read more ...

Confirm with the customer the details relating to their investments by comparing:

  • interest income from investments assessed by the ATO, with
  • investment amounts recorded on their Services Australia record

Discuss each account listed in the AIIR Account Details section and determine if the customer has any further accounts. The outcome of the conversation must explain the Gross Interest amount listed under the ATO ITR Details section.

Tell the customer:

'We have received information from the Australian Taxation Office that you may have received some interest on a financial investment from (financial institution's name) for the (state financial year)'

Give the customer the chance to first provide a verbal update. Obtain and code all of the following details:

  • Start and End date of investment if they fall within the Scope period
  • For bank accounts that have regular balance changes:
    • CPI updates (1 January, 20 March and 20 September) for each year within the Scope Period
    • income and asset thresholds updates (1 July) within the Scope Period
    • If a balance does not change from one assessment point to the next, coding may not be needed

Information must be updated when the customer advises changes to their financial assets. If there is a small change to asset values, check if the customer needs to notify.

If the customer indicates they are unable to go online to obtain bank statements, see the Resources page for how to help the customer.

If the customer has an asset held in trust for minors (e.g. children or grandchildren)

  • obtain more details from the customer and fully document this under the Note tab. See the Resources page for the DOC template/
  • complete a referral to a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) using the CAO Referral guided procedure.

NDIS participants

  • Customers who advise they are a National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) participant and self-manage their support budgets will have funds deposited into their nominated NDIS bank account. Deposits (including interest) into this account are exempt from the income and assets test. Participants do not need to tell Services Australia about their NDIS bank account
  • If NDIS funds are being held in an account with other personal funds, the customer must notify these accounts. They must be able to differentiate the NDIS amounts (including interest paid) against the other amounts

Additional risk

If an additional risk is identified during the investigation, this must be investigated and updated as part of the current intervention. See the Resources page for help with the assessment.

Discuss business income + Read more ...

Confirm with the customer the details relating to their business income by comparing:

  • business income details provided by the ATO, with
  • business Income details recorded on their Services Australia record

Discuss the business income details for the Selection Year with the customer. The questions below will help this discussion.

Note: any details listed for the previous year will be used later in the intervention to determine the potential debt start date.

Tell the customer:

'We have received information from the Australian Taxation Office that you may have Business Income from a partnership/sole trading business.'

Ask the customer the below questions for each business. Their responses will determine the need for them to provide documentation.

  • The name and address of the business?
  • Have you told Services Australia about this business? (Check Real Estate/ Business Summary (REBS) screen, Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen, Other Income Summary (OINS) screen to make sure business is coded)
  • What date did you start operating the business?
  • Is your business still trading?
  • If the business has ceased trading:
    • When did the business cease trading (date)?
    • If sold, what was the sale price of the business?
    • What did you do with the funds received from sale of the business?
  • Do you have business partners? If yes, what is your share of the profits?
  • If a Partnership, the customer must provide the Business Partner's name and their share of the profit/loss
  • Does the business hold any Real Estate? If yes, ask the customer to complete a MOD R
  • Is the income from the business different to the information received from the ATO? If yes, and the business has been declared, check for:
    • previous ITRs and financials the customer may have provided for the Selection Year, and
    • any DOC detailing the assessment to determine if there are valid reasons for differences between the ATO information and the income recorded
  • Has the business income changed since your last ITR? If yes, ask the customer to complete a profit and loss statement for the current year’s figures alongside last year’s figures. The customer can complete a SU580
  • What date did you prepare your annual financial statement/ prepare your annual tax return? If the date of event (DOV) cannot be determined and the ITR lodgement date does not appear on the ITR, use the date on the Tax Notice of Assessment. For help, see Date of Event for assessment of business structures

The Resources page links to the MOD R and SU580.

In addition to any documents above, also request:

  • Latest personal and business/partnership Income Tax Return (ITR)
  • Tax Notice Assessment (to determine DOV when the date cannot be obtained from the customer or the ITR)
  • Depreciation schedule
  • Profit and loss statement and balance sheet
  • For farmers only, livestock trading account

Fully document details under the Note tab. See the Resources page for the DOC template.

If during the intervention process it is identified that the customer has an additional risk, this must be investigated and updated as part of the current intervention. See the Assessing Additional Risk guide on the Resources page for help with the assessment.

Assessing verbal updates - bank interest and earned income only + Read more ...

If the customer supplies a verbal update, assess whether it is acceptable and aligns with the match data.

If the discrepancy can be fully explained for all matches, and no third party verification is required, thoroughly document the explanation in Notes and finalise the intervention.

To fully explain the discrepancy, there must be no further action needed as the information has been assessed or does not need to be assessed. This must be for all matches.

If the information does not align with the match data and the reason is not clear, the information is deemed insufficient.

If the customer is unable to provide a verbal update, they must provide documentary evidence.

If a rate reduction and/or overpayment is likely, update the customer's record with a date of event of DPT+1. This ensures their ongoing payment is correct and will avoid debt shells on their record.

Is the verbal update acceptable and aligns with the match data?

Manual suspension + Read more ...

Manual suspension action is required if:

  • the initial letter was receipted, and
  • a reminder letter was issued to the customer, or a verbal warning is documented, and
  • the customer has not responded to either the request to contact letter or provided required documentation as requested

To manually suspend the payment, in the customer's record:

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen and key these fields:
    • Svc Rsn: payment type, e.g. JSP
    • Action: SUS
    • Reason: FRC
    • Press [Enter] and the date of effect will default, or key the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date field. The date of event used for manually suspending payments is the date the determination is made, that is, today. Do not use Date Paid to Plus 1 (DPT+1).
    • Complete footer details, Source and DOR
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen to finalise the activity

Update the letter contact on IRCD screen to ‘N’ the customer did not contact.

Record a new contact to place the intervention on hold.

Record suspension action under the Note tab. See the Resources page for the DOC template.

Create a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on the customer's record to ensure that if they contacts they are referred to the Compliance Risk Branch before restoring payments.

To issue first or second information request to third party + Read more ...

This is a guide to use for acceptable standards of verification based on the type of investment/asset and initial customer contact. For correct coding practice, all staff must adhere to the relevant assessment and coding instructions.

The Bank and organisation listing contains address details for all major financial institutions. Use the drop down menu to select the relevant bank to view the address details only.

To issue QSS40/QSS41:

  • Launch the General Review Action script and select QSS40/QSS41 (dom circs)
  • Select Employer Information , Real Estate Information, or Financial Information
  • Select who the request relates to, Customer or Non-customer
  • Follow prompts and key information as required
  • Select No to finalise letter
  • When the script stops, it will ask about issuing another QSS40. Do not select Next. From here, view the letter and make the necessary changes
  • Delete the automated questions. Replace them with the questions relevant to the third party request. See the Resources page for text for third party letters
  • Check the letter is correct. Check the correct contact phone number is included in the letter
  • After including all information in the letter, bring the script up and select Next
  • Finalise the QSS40/QSS41 manually
  • The script will update IRS, record the contact and 'H'old the intervention
    • Add keyword 'QSS40' or 'QSS41'
    • Move the held IRS activity to:
      Workgroup: UII_(site code)
      Position CO's: logon
  • Document the relevant information in the intervention Notes

Employment income verification requests + Read more ...

Run the General Review Action script and select Q146 - Employer Report.

The script will:

  • create an entry on the IRCD screen with the letter and due date (21 days)
  • place the review on 'H'old, pending return of the information requested

Add the third party phone numbers to the intervention Notes in case future contact is required.

Note: often the customer will have a contact number for the employer that can be used to obtain the correct address. This will avoid going back to the customer if the employer does not supply the required information.

Procedure ends here.

Contact the customer + Read more ...

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer.

Follow the phone contact requirements .

Record all attempts to contact the customer under the Note tab.

Was contact successful?

  • Yes,
    • If the customer is still eligible, restore their payments and record the action in the intervention DOC
    • If no previous contact has been made by the customer, complete the intervention with the customer on the phone. See Step 3 in Table 1
    • If a further request for documentary evidence is required, see Step 6 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 12

12

Cancel customer payments + Read more ...

Cancel the customer's payments before finalising the intervention if:

  • the customer has failed to respond in relation to suspension, and
  • attempts to contact the customer have been unsuccessful.

Update the letter contact on the IRCD screen to 'N' the customer did not contact.

Fully explain and document all actions and decisions on the customer record. See the Resources page for the DOC template.

See Assessing and finalising BIBI interventions.

13

No response after second request + Read more ...

All avenues have been exhausted if:

  • 2 requests for information have been issued and not returned, and
  • 2 attempts to contact the third party have been made

Only after exhausting all avenues is a CADO Service Desk enquiry to be sent to Policy. Before issuing the request, seek and record Quality Development Officer (QDO) endorsement on the intervention DOC.

Record contact details in IRS to place the intervention on hold. Add the keyword 'BIBISD' to the IRMR screen.

Make sure the intervention DOC reflects QDO endorsement.

Forward the following details via the CADO Service Desk webform:

  • CSAQuery: Compliance Helpdesk
  • CSAEnquiry: Compliance Helpdesk
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Main Payment type: UII
  • Reference check: no response to 2nd QSS40
  • Fully document the reasons for the referral

2

ATO request for information + Read more ...

The Australian Taxation Office (ATO) request for information form can be used if:

  • tax return information is required to verify a customer's circumstances, and
  • no other option is available to obtain the information.

Income tax returns for the customer are required for assessment of self-employment/other income.

For more information, see the Resources page for a link to the ATO information request form.

3

Complete the template + Read more ...

Complete the ATO request for information template:

  • Complete all compulsory fields (Compliance Officer's full name, customer details and CRN)
  • Request information required for the verification. If information in match is for the 2010-11 financial year only, request tax return for this financial year and subsequent years. For undeclared trust, request information for the year before the match year
  • Only complete the Full Income Tax Return section on the ATO request form
  • In 'other box' on the ATO Request Form, insert 'Require accountant details'
  • Email the request to the site Quality Development Officer (QDO)

Update the IRCD screen.

4

Quality Development Officer (QDO) actions + Read more ...

Upon receipt of an ATO request for information referral:

  • Check the request and ensure all relevant details have been provided on the form, including CO details and contact number
  • Check that the information requested is required for the intervention. If not, return the request to the CO
  • After checking all details, email the request to the Payment and Integrity (P&I) Operations Strategy team via the CCB mail box:
  • Add ‘ATO request for information’ to the Subject Line
  • The email content must include: Customer name and CRN
  • ‘W’ the intervention

When the ATO returns the information, the P&I Operations Strategy team will:

  • Upload the information to the customer’s record
  • Update the IRCD screen
  • Document the relevant information in the intervention Notes
