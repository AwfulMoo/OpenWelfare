This page has the process to follow when customers request an urgent payment due to expenses associated with a funeral. The decision to approve or deny an urgent payment is made by a Service Officer at the APS4 level or above, Service Delivery Partner Service Officers or specific Smart Centre staff. These are staff fully trained in urgent payments.

Authenticate customer/nominee + Read more ...

Before assessing an urgent payment, make sure the customer record is the correct one. Ask authentication questions to verify Proof of Record Ownership (PORO). See Authenticating a customer.

For face-to-face contact:

  • It is preferable the customer provide identity documents, including at least one photo identity document

The questions must be searching, but not so personal as to be intrusive. If appropriate, explain to the customer why they are being asked these questions

If the customer or nominee cannot confirm details and the Service Officer is not satisfied they are speaking with the customer, do not release any information. Do not run the Immediate Payment requests script. See Intertwined Centrelink records.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, deny the urgent payment request, see Processing urgent payment requests

4

Explore other assistance + Read more ...

Discuss other assistance options with the customer/nominee before proceeding with an urgent payment request.

This may be:

  • referrals to community organisations, such as the appropriate land council corporations or trust for the community
  • referrals to accommodation assistance
  • financial counselling
  • Centrepay deductions
  • weekly payments
  • Crisis Payment because of family and domestic violence or other exceptional circumstances. Note: social workers decide if a customer is eligible for a Crisis Payment because of family and domestic violence

Indigenous customers attending a funeral in NSW may be eligible for the Aboriginal Funeral Transport - Sorry Business initiative.

Note: do not refer customers to the National Indigenous Australians Agency (NIAA) for financial help with funeral expenses, as they do not provide this. The NIAA regional offices may be able to refer customers to organisations that may be able to help, but they do not provide the financial assistance themselves.

Has the customer accepted the offer of other assistance?

  • Yes, urgent payment is not required. Run the Immediate Payment requests script and select Alternative Assistance option. Procedure ends here
  • No, include in the urgent payment DOC that other assistance was offered, but refused. Go to Step 5

5

Check the customer has available funds + Read more ...

An urgent payment is paid in arrears. It is limited to the number of days between the 'date paid to' and the date on which the payment is being processed.

The available amount is less deductions, for example, for debt repayments, tax, Centrepay, immediate payment deduction. This leaves enough funds to meet these commitments.

To check a customer’s accrued entitlement:

  • In Customer First, select the correct system for the payment type
  • On the Payment Summary (PS) screen, key 'R' in the box next to the future payment, select Continue twice (in some cases a third ‘click’ may be needed)
  • If there are available funds, this will display on the Issue Future Payment (IFP) screen

Do not process the urgent payment on this screen.

Has the customer’s entitlement accrued to an amount that will meet their immediate need?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the urgent payment request. See Processing urgent payment requests

6

Is this the first request today? + Read more ...

A customer is entitled to one urgent payment request (including any via self service) and one reconsideration of an urgent payment request per day.

Check the Document List (DL) screen for staff assisted requests and self-service grants. Self-service rejections show in Process Direct in the Notes section.

Is this the first request for urgent payment today?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No,
    • If they requested an urgent payment today and they disagree with the decision, they can ask for it to be reconsidered. See Customer disagrees with an urgent payment decision
    • If the reconsideration has occurred and the decision has been overturned and customer may be eligible for an urgent payment, go to Step 7
    • If they requested an urgent payment and had the decision reconsidered today, the request is denied. See Processing urgent payment requests

Indigenous customers - gathering information to assess the urgent payment request

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Cultural awareness for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander funerals + Read more ...

Service Officers must respect and consider cultural impacts for Indigenous customers, including:

  • Immediate family members may not be able to mention the name of the deceased person. This means they may have to ask other relatives or friends to call on their behalf or attend an interview with the customer
  • It may be offensive to the customer for the Service Officer to say the name of the deceased person. If unsure, ask the customer if it is appropriate to use the deceased person's name. If the customer is not comfortable with this, avoid using the deceased person's name wherever possible
  • Cultural diversity among Indigenous families is known as 'the Extended Family'. This means being raised in an extended family system, whose significant kinship categories go far beyond the nuclear family, and reinforces Indigenous people's orientation
  • Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander customers may have to attend a number of funerals within a short time
  • Often during the mourning period, all other personal and family matters will not be a priority. For example, a customer may fail to attend an interview because the funeral is considered the priority
  • A funeral set to occur in an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander community can often be changed at the last minute due to cultural negotiations and/or conflicts. For example, discussions about the country the person should be buried in

For Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples, a funeral includes any ceremonies of cultural importance about the deceased, for example sorry business.

Sorry Business

Sorry Business is an important part of Aboriginal culture practice. It describes an important time of mourning the death of a community member and involves the whole community. The timeframe for mourning is important and can last days, months or more depending on beliefs or the status of the person who has passed away.

While Sorry Business can last many months for Aboriginal peoples, Torres Strait Islander people’s burial rituals may span up to 3 years.

Service Officers should establish:

  • details about the relationship of the customer to the deceased and the funeral itself (there is no need for written confirmation of the customer's kinship relationship to the deceased person)
  • the family relationships, kinship or cultural obligations that require the customer to attend the funeral

An Indigenous Services Officer (ISO) is unable to provide proof or confirmation of kinship and should not be contacted to do so.

Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander customers may need to contribute towards the cost of the funeral or provide support to the grieving family. They may be expected to:

  • travel to attend the funeral (and return home)
  • contribute towards the cost of the funeral
  • cover the cost of the funeral of an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander person when there are expectations of community responsibility

2

Gather information during customer contact + Read more ...

Gather information from the customer about the funeral. A funeral includes any ceremonies of cultural importance about the deceased, for example sorry business.

The funeral does not need to be unforeseen for the customer to be eligible for assistance. For example, the customer may know the date of the funeral a few days or weeks before requesting assistance.

Ask the customer for the following information:

  • name of the deceased person (being aware of the sensitivities about using the deceased person's name)
  • relationship to the customer (being aware of the meaning of 'the extended family')
  • when the funeral is being held (being aware this includes ceremonies of cultural importance about the deceased)

If the customer is:

  • a job seeker, consider if they may be eligible for a temporary exemption from any mutual obligation requirements. See Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances
  • in receipt of concession card and/or free travel pass (pension), advise their travel options

3

Verify funeral details + Read more ...

Tell the customer the funeral details must be verified.

To help with verifying the funeral details use the:

  • Indigenous Funeral Notices intranet page. Do not contact an Indigenous Service Officer (ISO) for more details about a funeral listed on the page. This page has the most up-to-date information
  • Ryerson Index
  • Obits

If the customer provides documentary evidence, such as a newspaper notice or social media post, details should be confirmed by the above sources. Extra care should be taken to make sure the immediate family requirement is met.

If the funeral is not listed on any of these pages or the customer cannot provide verification, verify the funeral details via an Indigenous community organisation:

  • Ask for the contact details of a community organisation to help with verification of the funeral arrangements (as these may not be available using online resources)
  • Do not contact the Indigenous community organisation, local community or an Indigenous Service Officer for more details about a funeral listed on the Indigenous Funeral Notices page. This page has the most up-to-date information published. If the page has TBA listed instead of date, time and location, or the customer advises a change in the details, contact should be made
  • Do not contact the funeral director to confirm or enquire about a funeral

To confirm the community organisation contact, check Office Locator for the nearest service centre and liaise with the:

  • local community, or
  • local Indigenous Service Officer (there may be a hunt group number to call) Note: they may not be aware of all the sorry business in the area, due to the large areas they are responsible for

Can the funeral be verified?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, deny the request. See Processing urgent payment requests

4

What type of assistance is required? + Read more ...

If the customer needs assistance with:

  • the cost of the funeral and other associated expenses, go to Step 5
  • travel to or from the funeral, go to Step 6

5

Assistance with cost of the funeral and other associated expenses + Read more ...

The amount paid should be reasonable, but should not place the customer in financial hardship so they cannot meet their regular expenses.

If the payment will increase the customer’s hardship, or they are not eligible for the amount requested, discuss with the customer to explain the situation before denying the request.

No limit or amount can be set for these expenses. The cost will depend on the funeral and what the customer is expected to contribute. However, make sure the amount paid does not leave the customer in further financial hardship.

6

Assistance with cost of travelling to the funeral + Read more ...

Consider issuing a reasonable amount for one way only travel.

Tell the customer to contact Centrelink to apply for another urgent payment to return home if they need funds after the funeral. This provides more assurance the urgent payment is used for travel, and avoids customers being without funds to return home. Tell the customer the potential urgent payment for the return journey may depend, at that time, on their situation and available funds for an urgent payment. The customer cannot be granted a second urgent payment on the same day for the return travel.

When deciding whether to pay for one way or return travel, consider the location of the funeral and if the customer will be able to easily make contact with Centrelink to make the second request. For example, if the funeral is in a remote area with no mobile coverage, there may be a landline at the local cooperative but this may be closed as a mark of respect.

When deciding a reasonable amount for travel, Rome2Rio can be used to calculate the travel time and cost.

Indigenous customers in New South Wales (NSW) attending a funeral in NSW (only) may be eligible for the Aboriginal Funeral Transport - Sorry Business initiative. See the Resources page for a link to the Transport for NSW website.

See urgent payment methods.

  • Yes, payment to be approved. See Table 4

    • Revisit other assistance options with the customer
    • Then deny the urgent payment. See Processing urgent payment requests

Check the number of recent urgent payments to the customer + Read more ...

3

Accrued entitlement + Read more ...

The Immediate Payment requests script will calculate the available funds (accrued entitlement). This amount cannot be overridden.

An urgent payment is paid in arrears and is limited to:

  • the number of days between the 'date paid to' and the date on which the payment is being processed
  • less deductions for debt repayments, tax, Centrepay, immediate payment deduction, to make sure that enough funds are left to meet these commitments

If the customer’s accrued entitlement does not meet their immediate need, the urgent payment request will not be approved.

To check a customer’s accrued entitlement:

  • In Customer First, select the correct system for the payment type
  • On the Payment Summary (PS) screen, key 'R' in the box next to the future payment, press Continue, then press Continue again

If there are available funds, this will display on the Issue Future Payment (IFP) screen

Do not process the urgent payment on this screen.

Has the customer's entitlement accrued to an amount that will meet their immediate need?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. See Processing urgent payment requests

4

Negotiate the amount to be paid + Read more ...

Take care when approving an urgent payment that the customer can afford to have that amount recovered in full from their next payment without it causing further financial hardship.

The amount payable is the smallest of:

  • available funds calculated by the system
  • the amount the customer requested, or
  • the minimum amount it will cost to meet the customer's immediate hardship need

There is no maximum payment limit for customers granted a payment to help with the expenses of a funeral. However, consider if it will place them in future financial hardship.

Before offering an urgent payment consider:

  • Is the amount being offered the minimum required to meet the customer's immediate need?
  • If paid, how will the customer meet their regular ongoing expenses such as rent, food, utilities on their next payday? Reduce the amount offered or deny the request if the customer is not able to meet their regular ongoing expenses
  • Does the customer history indicate they are struggling to meet regular ongoing expenses or appear to be in a crisis cycle? Consider a referral to help the customer build their financial capability or completing a weekly payment assessment

Discuss with the customer and confirm they understand:

  • any money the customer receives for urgent payment will reduce their next fortnightly payment, and
  • how they intend to meet their regular expenses with the reduced funds received on their regular payday

While the system will allow as little as $1 to be paid, when deciding the amount consider:

  • most stores require a minimum purchase of between $10 to $15 before EFTPOS can be used
  • ATMs issue a minimum of $20
  • EBT cards can only be issued in denominations of $20 or $50 as these are the amounts available at ATMs

Will receiving the urgent payment extend the customer's financial hardship or make it worse?

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the payment
  • No, the customer is eligible for an urgent payment

See Processing urgent payment requests.
