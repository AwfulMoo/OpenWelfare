For National Business Gateway Income Management Processing staff

This page contains information relating to actioning a Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) instruction. This process excludes deduction for Northern Territory Housing as these requests are managed using a separate process, specific to Northern Territory Housing.

On this page:

State Housing Authority (HA) requested Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) instruction

Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) requests for SA, WA or NSW

Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) requests for VIC, TAS, ACT or QLD

State Housing Authority (HA) requested Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) instruction

Table 1: This table describes actioning an RDS instruction for an Income Management customer from a State HA.

Step

Action

1

RDS deduction has been identified for an Income Managed customer + Read more ...

Work Item 'I034Q1 Rent deduction received from HA - review IM expenses and deductions' is on a customer’s record indicating an RDS deduction has been set up for an Income Managed customer, or customer identified through weekly HA report.

Check the customer's record to determine whether the customer has sufficient unallocated income managed funds each fortnight to pay the full amount required for the RDS deduction.

Does the customer have sufficient unallocated Income Management funds available to cover the full rental amount?

  • Where there is evidence the customer has indicated they want their RDS deduction to come from their discretionary funds and not paid from Income Management, the deduction will not be transitioned
  • Work Items generated for Northern Territory Housing should be cancelled, as these requests are managed using a separate process, specific to Northern Territory Housing
  • Customers income managed under the Child Protection or Vulnerable measures must be referred to the relevant Income Management Contact Officer for that area rather than transitioned
  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Contact Customer + Read more ...

Attempt to contact the customer by telephone.

Has the attempt to contact the customer been successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, see Step 7 in the Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) requests for VIC, TAS, ACT or QLD table

3

Contact Successful + Read more ...

Advise the customer that a RDS request has been received from their HA.

Advise the customer rent can be paid from their income managed funds, their discretionary funds or a combination of both.

There may be situations when the customer has insufficient discretionary or income managed funds alone to cover their full rent payment. In these cases it is up to the customer to decide how all of their priority needs will be met and what proportion of their rent should be paid from Income Management and what proportion should be paid from their discretionary funds.

It is essential that customers continue to pay their full rent to their HA.

Note: if necessary, with the customer's agreement, it may be necessary to modify existing expense allocations to enable sufficient unallocated funds to pay rent from Income Management funds.

  • If the customer wishes to pay rent in full from Income Management funds, go to Step 7
  • If the customer wishes to pay rent in full from discretionary funds, go to Step 4
  • If the customer wishes to pay rent from a combination of Income Management and discretionary funds, go to Step 5

4

Request to pay full amount of rent payment from their discretionary funds + Read more ...

No action is required, leave the RDS deduction in place.

Ensure the customer understands that the deduction will continue to come out of their discretionary funds which means less discretionary funds will be paid to them.

Record contact and customer decision on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

5

Request to pay rent from a combination of income managed and discretionary funds + Read more ...

If the customer wishes to have the payment of rent split between the RDS deduction and their available Income Management funds, the existing RDS deduction will need to be updated and a new Income Management expense added for the balance of the rent payment based on the customer's request.

6

Modify existing RDS deduction + Read more ...

Modify the existing RDS deduction to align with the arrangements negotiated with customer for splitting the rent payment.

For more information, see Varying a Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) deduction.

Go to Step 8.

7

Request to pay rent from income managed funds + Read more ...

If the customer wishes to pay their HA rent in full from their unallocated Income Management funds, the existing RDS deduction will need to be terminated prior to coding a new expense request for the rent payment to be made from the available Income Management funds.

Terminate the RDS deduction from the customer's last Entitlement Period End Day (EPED). For more information, see Terminating a Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) deduction.

8

Coding new expense for HA rent payment from available Income Management funds + Read more ...

For instruction received from HA for:

  • SA, WA or NSW, See Step 1 in the Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) requests for SA, WA or NSW table
  • VIC, TAS, ACT or QLD, See Step 1 in the Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) requests for VIC, TAS, ACT or QLD table

Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) requests for SA, WA or NSW

Table 2: This table describes how to complete RDS requests for South Australia (SA), Western Australia (WA) or New South Wales (NSW) Housing Authorities (HA).

Step

Action

1

Instruction received from HA in SA, WA or NSW + Read more ...

If the customer has requested to pay all or part of their rent paid from income managed funds:

  • Review existing Income Management expense allocations. It may be necessary to modify existing expenses to ensure sufficient funds are available to enable rent to be paid from Income Management funds. Any adjustments to expenses should be made in consultation with the customer.
  • For payment of rent in full, create a new expense for the full amount of the rent payable by the customer.
  • For payment of rent from a combination of Income Management and discretionary funds, create a new expense for the balance of the rent as negotiated with the customer for splitting the rent payment.

Note: ensure that the Income Management expense and the agreed RDS deduction equal the full rent due from the customer to the HA.

Code new expense for rent payment

  • Navigate to the customer's Income Management Summary menu item
  • Select Manage expense, followed by Expense Management summary
  • Under New expenses, select Add regular expense from the drop down selection
  • Select Start

2

Organisation Search screen + Read more ...

A Third Party Organisation (TPO) can be found by using their Customer Reference Number (CRN), entering the name of the organisation, or using the Services Australia Organisation Search Engine button.

To find the relevant State Housing Authority, In the TPO CRN: field enter the Housing Authority Customer Reference Number (CRN) and select Find.

Then enter:

Expense Category: field, select the appropriate option.

  • SA
    • IMR - Government Housing Rent
    • IMO - IM Govt Housing, Other Payment
  • NSW
    • IMR - Government Housing Rent
    • IMO - IM Govt Housing, Other Payment
    • IM3 - Other Housing Organisation customers
    • IMR - Government Housing Rent
  • WA
    • IMR - Government Housing Rent
    • IMO - IM Govt Housing, Other Payment
    • IMR - Government Housing Rent

State: field, select the appropriate option.

  • SA
  • NSW
  • WA

Select Continue.

3

Expense Request screen + Read more ...

TPO Name: this will default with information entered in the Expense Management TPO List

Amount: enter amount to be paid - enter the amount to come from Income Management funds

Target amount: enter amount only where the customer is required to pay a fixed total to the TPO

Start Date: enter the required start date

End Date: leave blank unless the customer nominates a specific end date

Next payment date: will default from system

Frequency: select from drop down list

Select Add new notes

In the Notes field, add the amount the customer wants to pay from their discretionary funds (if any).

If the customer is/wants to pay amounts for bond, arrears, maintenance etc. - add the details for each amount in the Notes field.

Note: ensure that the IM and discretionary amounts equal the total amount of rent that the customer needs to pay.

Select Continue.

4

Finalise activity + Read more ...

When expense allocation is completed finalise assessment.

Review the Income Management Summary screen to confirm:

  • direct credit payments are showing in the 14 day balance details
  • Expense Requests are showing as provisional under Regular or One Off Expenses
  • the available balance has adjusted correctly, taking into account any regular or one-off payments and/or requests

Select the Finalise radio button and select Continue to finalise the activity.

Record details on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

Finalise activity + Read more ...

When expense allocation is completed, finalise assessment.

Review the Income Management Summary screen to confirm:

  • payments are showing in the 14 day balance details
  • Expense Requests are showing as provisional under Regular or One Off Expenses
  • the available balance has adjusted correctly, taking into account any regular or one-off payments and/or requests

Select the Finalise radio button and select Continue to finalise the activity, workflow will then proceed to the Priority Needs Expenses screen.

5

Priority Needs Expenses screen + Read more ...

Is a discussion being held? Select yes or no.

  • Yes, see Changes to Income Management priority needs
  • No, workflow will navigate to Income Management Summary screen, check details and select Finalise

6

Payment process + Read more ...

Rent will be paid each fortnight to the HA in each state below using the following payment methods:

  • QLD - BPay
  • VIC - Scheduled Transfer
  • ACT - BPay
  • TAS - no current payment options from Income Management. Discussions to take place between Income Management processing staff, State Account Manager and Deduction and Confirmation Branch if a request is made

Record details on a DOC, procedure ends here.

7

Contact not successful + Read more ...

The deduction will commence automatically via RDS using the customer's discretionary funds if the customer has not been able to be contacted.

Create a DOA DOC with the following text:

'IM customer has a discretionary Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) deduction to their State Housing Authority (HA). Attempts have been made to contact customer to discuss whether deduction should be paid from discretionary or Income Management funds. As unable to contact customer discretionary RDS deduction has been left in place. If customer contacts please assess allocations & code accordingly ensuring the current RDS deduction in place has been ceased (OB: 108-08130030). Please annotate & expire DOA DOC once appropriate action has been taken.'
