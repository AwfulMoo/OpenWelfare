6

Customer contacts about suspension action + Read more ...

Payment cannot be restored unless the customer remains eligible for the whole period covered by the restoration. To avoid incorrect arrears, update the customer's record for any changes of circumstance. e.g. employment income, including JobKeeper.

If the suspension action was outside the JobKeeper compliance intervention, contact with the relevant team might be required before restoring payments.

Restore and code any relevant employment income for any entitlement periods covered by the restoration period:

  • Restore the customer's payments
  • Select the review in IRS before making any updates to the customer record. This is also known as being within the 'review image'. See the Resources page for the task card
  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen and code these fields:
    • Svc Rsn: key the type of benefit (e.g. JSP/PPS)
    • Action: key 'RES'
    • Reason: leave blank
    • Press [Enter]. In most cases, the Effect Date: will default to the date of suspension. If it does not, key the date the customer was suspended from
    • Source: and DOR:
  • If relevant, launch the Earnings and Reporting workflow via Customer First Workspace within the restoration activity:
    • code any employment income relevant to the restoration period
    • complete any outstanding report
  • Check the result on the Assessment Results (AR) screen before finalising the activity. If the customer is entitled to arrears, check the amount to be paid is correct
  • Annotate the existing intervention DOC, with the conversation and action taken. See the Resources page for the 'Customer payment restored DOC' template

After restoring payment, continue with the intervention.

Locate match data + Read more ...

Check if there are any anomalies or mismatches with the match data information.

Check if EER customer

Use details listed on the Employment Income Summary (EANS) to check if:

  • the customer is employed with an Australian Disability Enterprise (ADE) employer, and
  • their employment income is reported through the Employer Electronic Reporting (EER) process

If both of the above apply, refer the intervention to the CADO Service Desk. Procedure ends here

Check match data

Locate the details received from the ATO.

Go to the Match Data (IRMD) screen while in the review. Key 'IRMD' in the Nxt: field or key 'M' next to the review on the Review Summary (IRRS) screen.

Interventions will have a keyword of Tier 1 or Tier 2 added to the IRS review. This keyword will help identify the tiered amount of JobKeeper Payment received.

Discuss the match data details with the customer to confirm a link between the customer and the employer details listed in the match data.

Check if the:

  • customer is or was receiving income from the matched employer, (wage or salary earner), or
  • match data relates to self-employment. This could include sole trader, member of a partnership or through a private trust or private company

If the customer says they do not know the employer or they have never worked for the matched employer, check if the customer may know the matched employer under another name, e.g. a trading name:

  • Check the ABN Lookup guide for other trading names the matched employer may be trading under. This may help the customer recognise the match data
  • Review the customer record to see if there is any information to support the match data

When considering if a customer may be self-employed:

  • check if the Legal Name/Trading Name on the IRMD screen matches the customer's name, or the business address is the same as the customer's
  • some sole traders do not register under a Trading Name and will have the match data showing the ABN only. If the details on the IRMD screen have an ABN but no Legal/Trading name, the customer is likely to be a sole trader

Is there a link between the customer and the matched employer?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, there has been a possible identity mismatch. See Step 1 in Table 3

2

Determine employment circumstances + Read more ...

JobKeeper Payment is assessed differently depending on the customer's employment circumstances. Use questioning skills and investigate the customer's record to determine their employment circumstances.

Determine if the customer is employed as a:

  • wage/salary earner (Employee), or
  • self-employed as a:
    • sole trader
    • member of a partnership, or
    • private trust or private company

If the customer has said they are self-employed, use the following information to help decide the type of self-employment.

Check the Match data:

  • If the Trading Name displays 2 names (e.g. Jane Citizen and John Citizen) this can indicate that the customer is involved in a partnership
  • Involvement in a private trust can be identified by the Legal Name or Trading Names including the words Family Trust, The Trustee for, Unit Trust or As Trustee for (ATF), or
  • For a private company, the Legal Name or Trading Name can include the words Proprietary Limited, PTY LTD or P/L at the end of the name

Check the following screens to see what has been coded:

  • For an employee, check EAN/EANS screen
  • For self-employed as:
    • a sole trade, check Real Estate and Business Summary (REBS) screen
    • member of a business partnership, check REBS screen
    • private trust or private company, check both the Link Summary (LS) and Trust/Company/Income/Assets Summary (TRCIAS) screens

Although a customer may run a business as a sole trader, they may be working as an employee and not self-employed. For example:

  • a contractor can be considered self-employed or an employee depending on their circumstances
  • if the customer receives group certificates, they are generally considered an employee

The Decision Support Tool (DST) helps to decide the customer's employment status. Only use it for customers who identify as a sole trader or member of a partnership. Do not use it for customers involved in private trusts or private companies. The DST uses questions to help staff weigh up the facts and decide each case on its own merit.

Record the employment circumstances decision on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for the 'Self-employed/employee decision support tool question' DOC template.

3

Questions to determine scope period + Read more ...

Establish the period the customer received the JobKeeper Payment:

  • Use the match data as a guide to start the conversation with the customer
  • Use questioning skills to gather more information from the customer about their circumstances.

Questions may include:

  • When did your JobKeeper Payments start/cease?
  • Were you physically working with your employer or just getting JobKeeper Payment income from them?
  • Were you still receiving your regular wage before getting JobKeeper Payment or did you temporarily cease work before receiving JobKeeper Payment?
  • Do you know why the JobKeeper Payments ceased?
  • Has the business closed down or re-opened after a COVID related shut down?
  • Have you been employed since the xx/xx/xxxx (match data start date)?
  • If the customer states they did not get JobKeeper Payment:
    ‘Do you remember completing an 'ATO JobKeeper Employee Nomination Notice?’
  • Did you get a significantly larger pay or lump sum, which may have been JobKeeper Payment back pay?

4

Determine scope period + Read more ...

Scope start date:

  • the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) of the first period that JobKeeper Payment was paid for, or
  • the date they started on income support payments if that date is after the JobKeeper Payment start date

For interventions loaded from April 2021:

The start date on IRMD will show a date of 28 September 2020 onwards. The Scope start date for these interventions will be the EPSD that this Match Start Date listed in the IRMD screen falls into.

The keyword Tier 1 or Tier 2 will be added to the IRS review. Some of these customers may have received JobKeeper or employment income from their employer before 28 September 2020. For these customers, the intervention only looks at JobKeeper and employment income from the change in Tier amounts, so from September 2020 onwards.

Scope end date:

  • the end date (EPED) of the entitlement period in which the customer received the last JobKeeper payment, or
  • the end date (EPED) that falls after 28 March 2021, or
  • the end date (EPED) of the entitlement period in which employment ceased if the customer is no longer working

When the match data dates can be used to determine Scope:

The dates within the match data can be used in these circumstances:

  • customer fails to respond to a request to contact
  • customer is unable to provide more information to determine a customer specific scope period, or
  • customer states they never received JobKeeper payments including those who advise they ceased work before the match data start date

What needs to be updated:

  • JobKeeper employer listed in the compliance trigger (IRMD screen)
  • Record all information obtained during the conversation with the customer, including the reason for the scope start date being applied in the intervention DOC. See the Initial customer discussion DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements.

Deliver the education message and finalise the intervention if the customer says they were employed and receiving JobKeeper Payment, but:

  • they ceased employment permanently before claiming income support payment, and
  • there is appropriate supporting evidence on the customer's record, e.g. an Employment Separation Certificate scanned to their record as part of their new claim for payment

Deliver the education message + Read more ...

Deliver the education message to the customer.

If required, place the customer on fortnightly reporting. See Reporting requirements for job seekers.

Disability Support Pension (DSP)/Carer Payment (CP) customers

If the customer got extra income on top of the JobKeeper Payment for hours worked, determine:

  • if the customer receives Disability Support Pension (DSP) or Carer Payment (CP), and
  • that the number of hours working does not exceed the allowable limits

For DSP customers, see Commencing or returning to work or self-employment DSP.

For CP, see:

  • Carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities
  • Care receiver commences or returns to work

If the customer is:

  • self-employed (including sole trader, involved in a business partnership, private trust or private company), see Step 1 in Table 4
  • an employee, go to Step 6
  • an employee but advises they did not get the JobKeeper Payment as they ceased work before the match data date, third party verification is required. See Step 7 in Table 8

6

Obtain employment income information + Read more ...

Obtain the following from the customer:

  • name and contact details (phone, address) of the employer they are receiving income from, including the JobKeeper Payment:
    • include trading and business name of the employer
    • make sure details match the details on the IRMD screen
  • the period the income and/or JobKeeper Payment was paid by the employer (e.g. daily/ weekly/ fortnightly/ monthly) in the scope period
  • amount paid for each period

Consider the following in the discussion:

  • If the customer received allowances, back-pay, termination payments, and bonus or commission payments
  • Summarise and document details to make sure:
    • there is a clear understanding on how the employment income will be assessed
    • the information has been captured/discussed with the customer

If the customer says there were periods in the scope period they did not get any employment income, including JobKeeper.

Determine why the customer did not get any income during this period.

Use sound questioning skills to have an effective discussion with the customer to get the required information verbally, for example:

  • Did the customer take unpaid leave?
  • Was the business shut down over the Christmas/New Year’s period?

If the customer’s verbal response is reasonable, DOC the information. There is no need to seek documented information from either the customer or employer for these periods.

7

Assessing JobKeeper Payments + Read more ...

From 7 December 2020, employment income is assessed once it is paid by an employer to their employee. This is the 'Pay Date' or 'Date Paid' on the customer's payslip.

If the customer does not get a payslip, they would need to confirm this information with their employer. Even if there is a delay between when an employer pays their employee and when the employee receives the pay, (due to the time it takes to transfer funds between banks), employment income is still considered paid on the date it is disbursed by the employer.

Note the following JobKeeper Payments as per the ATO:

  • before 28 September 2020, minimum $1,500 pf
  • from 28 September 2020 until 3 January 2021:
    • Tier 1: minimum $1,200 pf
    • Tier 2: minimum $750 pf
  • from 4 January 2021 until 28 March 2021:
    • Tier 1 minimum $1,000 pf
    • Tier 2 minimum $650 pf

Note: the employer determines the Tier rate and notifies the ATO.

If required, place the customer on fortnightly reporting. See Reporting requirements for job seekers.

Is the amount paid for each period less than the JobKeeper minimum fortnightly rates?

  • Yes, see Step 2 in Table 5
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Verbal updates + Read more ...

Assess if the verbal updates are reasonable:

  • they align with the match data and the scope period, and
  • the customer can provide all the necessary information verbally

Use sound questioning skills to have an effective discussion with the customer to get the required information verbally:

  • Do you have your wage details available and can you tell me the amounts/periods you were paid over the phone?
  • I understand that you're no longer working for that employer, but do you have, or can you get the information and provide it to us verbally?

The customer may not be able to provide all the employment income information at the time of the call. They may be away from their home and can provide the information later. In this scenario, consider sending a QSS32 to the customer.

Note: even though a QSS32 will be issued, tell the customer they can call back and give the information verbally.

If during questioning the customer cannot provide all the required information, especially if they are no longer working, issue a QSS40 to the employer.

To help decide whether to finalise the intervention or issue a third party request:

  • See the Resources page for scenarios
  • This document helps decide whether to send a QSS32 or a QSS40 or if the review can be finalised using information on the customer’s record

If the information given does not align with the match data and the reason is not clear, documented evidence should be requested. This makes sure the risk or discrepancy has been addressed.

Can the customer provide a verbal update that is reasonable and aligns with the match data?

  • No, based on the discussion with the customer:
    • If a QSS32 is required, see Step 2 in Table 5

Business is coded on REBS screen + Read more ...

Customers who are self-employed and received JobKeeper Payment through their sole trader or partnership business will only need to provide documented evidence of a change of circumstances if there was a change to the overall profit of their business when compared to the income details recorded on REBS. This is because JobKeeper Payment is included as business income.

Check if the assessable income coded in the scope period accurately reflected their business income. If not:

  • issue a Request for Information (RFI) to the customer using the RFI guided procedure in Customer First, and
  • request the customer’s 2019/20 and 2020/21 personal income tax returns. Go to Step 6.

3

Business is coded on the LS or TRCIAS screen + Read more ...

Customers who were paid JobKeeper payment through their involvement in a private trust or company and received JobKeeper Payment through their involvement need to provide documented evidence of a change of circumstances if there was a change to the overall profit of the business when compared to the income details recorded on REBS. This is because JoKeeper Payment is included as business income.

Check if the assessable income coded reflects their current business income.

Issue a Request for Information (RFI) to the customer using the RFI guided procedure in Customer First. Request the following documents:

  • The 2019/20 and 2020/21 company / trust tax returns
  • most recent individual tax return (and partner if required)
  • Business Profit and Loss Statement

The customer is to include any JobKeeper Payments they received as part of the gross income on the profit and loss statement.

Note: the RFI does not send forms to the customer. The Centrelink Profit and Loss Statement (SU580) is not suitable evidence for private companies or private trusts.

Go to Step 6.

4

Business is not coded on the customer record (REBS screen) + Read more ...

As the business has not previously been coded on the customer record, determine if the assessable income coded reflects their current business income.

Issue a Request for Information (RFI) using the RFI guided procedure in Customer First, requesting the following documents:

  • Business Details (MOD F)
  • Personal income tax returns for the 2019/20 and 202/21 financial years (and partner’s personal tax return if customer is partnered)

The customer is to include any JobKeeper payments received as part of the gross income on the profit and loss statement.

Note: the RFI does not send forms to the customer. Advise the customer they can download the MOD F from the Services Australia website.

Go to Step 6.

5

Business is not coded on LS or TRCIAS screen + Read more ...

  • Private Trust (MOD PT) or Private Company (MOD PC) form
  • most recent Trust or Company tax return
  • most recent individual tax return (and partner's, if required)

The customer is to include any JobKeeper payments received as part of the gross income on the profit and loss statement.

Note: the RFI does not send forms to the customer. Advise the customer they can download the MOD PC/PT on the Services Australia website. The Centrelink Profit and Loss Statement (SU580) is not suitable evidence for private companies or private trusts.

Go to Step 6.
