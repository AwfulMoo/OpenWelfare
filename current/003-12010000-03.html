Referral to a social worker + Read more ...

Before referring the customer to a social worker, check the customer’s record to see if there is a current social work referral.

Is there a referral for the same customer issue?

  • Yes, add a Fast Note to the customer’s record documenting their contact in Customer First. Tell the customer that a social worker will be in contact as soon as possible
    • Do not create a new referral
    • Procedure ends here

2

Discuss the reason for a referral to a social worker with the customer + Read more ...

Customer referral reasons can include:

  • suicide or self-harm, see Table 2
  • family and domestic violence support, including requests for Crisis Payment, or Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)/Parental Leave Pay (PPL) special circumstances FDV social work verification, see Table 3
  • request for face-to-face social work appointment ,go to Step 3
  • experiencing other multiple and complex issues, go to Step 6
  • unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH) assessment needed and they are under 18, go to Step 8
  • support during a declared disaster or emergency, see Table 4
  • under 16 and does not have support, refer the customer to a social worker now
  • under 18 and does not have support, go to Step 7
  • social casework referral, see Table 5
  • National Redress Scheme, see Social work role in the National Redress Scheme
  • referral from Participation Solutions Team, see Accessing a PST-skilled social worker
  • help assessing care situations, see Social worker assessment of care situations for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) claims
  • needing to talk to a social worker in Integrity and Information, see Social workers role in Integrity and Information

3

Customer requests face-to-face general social work appointment + Read more ...

If the customer is talking about suicide or self-harm, connect them to a social worker now. See Table 2.

Is the customer under 16?

  • Yes, see Assisting a customer aged under 16. Procedure ends here
  • No,
    • Consult with a social worker before booking
    • If unable to consult with a social worker, record the reason for social worker involvement when booking the appointment
    • Go to Step 4

4

Other referral reasons + Read more ...

Check if the customer needs a referral to a social worker.

  • Find out why the customer is contacting. Ask them:
    'So I can refer you to the most appropriate person, what would you like help with today?'
  • Check the customer's record for:
    • recent social worker contacts or outstanding issues
    • any documents the social worker will need, for example, a child's birth certificate
    • an existing social work referral on the Activity tab in Customer First or on the Work List in Process Direct
  • If there is a referral pending:
    • Tell the customer a social worker will call them as soon as possible. They will call from a private number. The social worker will send a text before they call if the customer is registered for pre call SMS
    • Check the customer’s correct contact details are recorded
    • Create a DOC on the customer’s record to note the contact
  • If there is no existing referral, check the customer needs social work services, for example:
    • referral for an exemption from seeking child support for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • Crisis Payment (CrP) claim
    • Cashless Debit Card (CDC)
    • Income Management (IM)
    • Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances
    • Deferral of the Prohibition of FTB payments (PIP) due to family and domestic violence
    • Reviewing nominee arrangements
    • Delaying notification of a debt to an assurer while the assuree seeks refuge from family and domestic violence

Does the customer need a social worker?

Note: the customer must agree to the referral to a social worker before referring them.

  • Yes,
    • If they are contacting to provide more information for a current social work assessment, go to Step 5
    • If they need to speak to a social worker for a new referral reason, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 9

5

Customer providing more information for a current social work assessment + Read more ...

Send a Microsoft Teams message to the assessing social worker.

If the social worker is available, warm transfer the customer.

If the social worker is not available, does the customer need help straight away?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No,
    • DOC the record to advise customer contact and extra information provided
    • Send a message to the assessing social worker confirming contact
    • Tell the customer the extra information has been provided to the assessing social worker who will follow up
    • Procedure ends here

6

Referral for multiple and complex needs + Read more ...

This referral is for unsupported customers who cannot be referred to a community service and need specialist intervention.

Note: multiple and complex needs refers to individuals or families experiencing a combination of vulnerabilities. This may include mental health, personal crisis, homelessness, disability and complex caring issues. Inappropriate referrals will be returned to the Service Officer.

  • Check if a face-to-face social worker is available. If not, call the Social Work Line
  • Use the Interaction Workspace (iWS) to call the Social Work Line

Warm transfer the customer to the social worker.

Have these details ready:

  • Customer name
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Payment type
  • Reason for referral. If the customer does not want to say, tell the social worker this before putting the call through
  • Get the customer's phone number in case the call drops out during transfer

Service Officers can decide not to stay on the line with the customer, especially if there is a long wait. If not staying on the line, complete a DOC on the customer's record with details of the reason for referral.

If no social worker is available by the Social Work Line, go to Step 7.

7

Record referral in Customer First guided procedure + Read more ...

Make every effort to connect the customer with a social worker at the time of contact. Only use the guided procedure when there is no available social worker.

To create a referral in Customer First:

  • Select Workspace
  • Search Social Work Referral in search field
  • Run the Social Worker Referral guided procedure
  • Select the most appropriate referral option

Tell the customer

Make sure the customer's phone number is correct.

Tell the customer a social worker will contact them. Tell them the call will come from a private number. The social worker will send them a text before they call if the customer is registered for pre call SMS.

Procedure ends here.

8

Refer a customer aged under 18 for UTLAH assessment + Read more ...

Social workers undertake UTLAH assessments for young people under 18 years.

If the customer is aged:

  • 15 years or under, refer them to a social worker now. The social worker will undertake a risk assessment and organise for a local UTLAH assessment
  • 16 or 17 years, they must have an appointment with the social work under 18 UTLAH team for the assessment
    • Check the customer has booked this appointment online, or
    • Book a social work under 18 UTLAH appointment for the customer in the Centrelink Appointment System in Customer First. See:
      Unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH) initial contact for Youth Allowance (YA), Disability Support Pension (DSP), Special Benefit (SpB) or Tertiary Access Payment (TAP) or
      ABSTUDY UTLAH

Procedure ends here.

9

Other referrals + Read more ...

If appropriate, refer the customer for:

  • Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing
  • Mutual obligation requirements exemptions
  • Urgent Payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances
  • Weekly payments
  • Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Temporary Financial Hardship) eligibility and applications
  • New claim for Crisis Payment Other Extreme Circumstances
  • Eligibility for Crisis Payment (CrP)
  • Exemptions from seeking child support for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for parentage unknown
  • Re-engaging job seekers - Targeted Compliance Framework
  • Assessing unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH) for customers claiming or receiving Youth Allowance (YA), Disability Support Pension (DSP), Special Benefit (SpB) or Tertiary Access Payment (TAP)
  • Specialist officers and other internal supports for customers
  • External support service. Help the customer find the right service using the Payment and Service Finder

Procedure ends here.

If the customer is not in immediate danger or at risk of harm + Read more ...

If the customer is seeking immediate help

Discuss a referral to the following services by warm transfer:

  • 1800 RESPECT:1800 737 732
    Support for all people impacted by sexual assault, domestic or family violence and abuse
  • Family Relationship Advice Line: 1800 050 321
    Service for all people affected by relationship or separation issues
  • MensLine Australia: 1300 789 978
    Counselling service offering support for men
  • Men’s Referral Service: 1300 766 491
    Behaviour change support for men who use family violence.

If the customer asks to speak with a social worker

Make every effort to connect the customer directly with a social worker at the time of contact.

Only use the Social Work Referral guided procedure when there is no available social worker.

If no social worker is available by the Social Work Line and the customer would still like contact from a social worker:

  • Check if there is an existing social work referral on the Activity tab in Customer First or on the Work List in Process Direct
  • If there is a referral pending:
    • Update additional notes in the original referral in Customer First. Do not create a new referral
    • Tell the customer that a social worker will call them as soon as possible
    • Tell the customer the social worker will send them a text before they call if the customer is registered for pre call SMS. The call will come from a private number.
    • Check the customer’s correct contact details are recorded
    • Create a DOC on the customer’s record to note the contact
  • If there is no existing referral:
    • Use Customer First Social Worker Referral guided procedure (see Step 6 in Table 1)
    • Select FDV support required and record the request
    • Tell the customer a social worker will contact them. The social worker's call will come from a private number
    • Make sure the customer's phone number is correct

Table 4

Step

Action

Disaster or emergency + Read more ...

Social workers are available to Australians affected by declared disasters and emergencies.

Social workers can provide personal support and counselling and help with referrals to material aid and community services.

Is the customer in crisis or do they need immediate social work help?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Type of claim + Read more ...

If the claim is for:

  • Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP) or NZ DRP where an immediate family member was:
    • not killed as a direct result of the disaster, go to Step 3
    • killed as a direct result of the disaster, go to Step 5
  • Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) or NZ DRA, go to Step 3
  • Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment (AVTOP), go to Step 5

3

Referrals during an AGDRP/NZ DRP/DRA/NZ DRA claim + Read more ...

Does the customer want a social worker to contact them when claiming AGDRP/ NZ DRP or DRA/ NZ DRA?

Note: customers do not need to speak to a social worker to claim a payment.

  • Yes, if the customer:
    • can wait on the phone to be transferred, go to Step 4
    • cannot wait on the phone to be transferred, create a referral to a social worker using the contingency sw disaster referral Fast Note
    • check if there is an existing social work referral on the Activity tab in Customer First or on the Work List in Process Direct. If there is a referral pending:
      - update additional notes in the original referral. Do not create a new referral

      - create a DOC on the customer’s record to note the contact
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, procedure ends here

Staff working from home or in evacuation, recovery or Mobile Service centres can use 02 8440 2753. NOTE that this number is for staff use only and is not to be given to customers, their representatives or external agencies.

Referring a customer for social casework

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Social casework + Read more ...

Social casework is planned, intensive intervention over a period for unsupported customers under the age of 25, customers experiencing family and domestic violence and other priority groups.

Is the customer eligible for social casework?

  • Yes, if:
    • a Service Officer is referring an unsupported young person, go to Step 2
    • a social worker is referring an unsupported young person, go to Step 3
    • a social worker is referring a customer experiencing family and domestic violence, and/or customers thinking about suicide and/or self-harm, go to Step 4
    • the customer presents with difficult or challenging behaviours, go to Step 6
  • No, consider a referral to a welfare agency

2

Service Officer referral in Customer First guided procedure + Read more ...

The customer must agree to the referral before the Service Officer refers them for social casework.

If the Service Officer wants to discuss the case with a social worker, consult using local arrangements.

If there is no need for a case discussion, refer the customer using:

  • Customer First - Social Worker Referral guided procedure (see Step 6 in Table 1)
    • Select Referral for Social Casework. Notes must be recorded to support the referral
    • Select the customer's zone from the drop down menu. Selecting the correct zone will assign the referral to the right team. To find a customer's zone
      - go to Office Locator
      - enter the customer's home office or Regional Office Code (ROC)
      - select Office Hierarchy

Make sure the customer's contact details are correct.

Tell the customer a social worker will contact them as soon as possible. Tell them the call will come from a private number. The social worker will send them a text before they call if the customer is registered for pre call SMS.

Create a DOC on the customer’s record to note the contact.

Procedure ends here.

4

Social worker referral to social casework for people experiencing family and domestic violence and/or people thinking about suicide and/or self-harm + Read more ...

People experiencing family and domestic violence (FDV) and people thinking about suicide and/or self-harm referrals are received by:

  • WLM, or
  • warm transfer from social workers

Note: Service Officers must not directly refer these customers for social casework.

Social workers should first consider available external services to avoid service duplication.

Social workers may create the referral as below, or proceed with the casework themselves.

To create a Social Casework FDV or people thinking about suicide and/or self-harm referral:

  • Run the Fast Note workflow on the customer's record
  • Key 'Social Casework Referral – SWO use only in search box. Select Search
  • The text auto-fills. Select Confirm
  • Add keyword at the bottom of the DOC for the customer's local region:
    • SWOCNSW - Central New South Wales
    • SWOCQLD - Central Queensland
    • SWOSEVIC - East Victoria
    • SWONA - North Australia
    • SWONNSW - North New South Wales
    • SWONQLD - North Queensland
    • SWONVIC - North Victoria
    • SWOSA - South Australia
    • SWOSNSW - South New South Wales
    • SWOSQLD - South Queensland
    • SWOSYD - Sydney
    • SWOTAS - Tasmania
    • SWOWA - Western Australia
    • SWONWVIC - West Victoria
  • Complete the DOC details. Select Continue
  • Do not complete the DOC

5

Record referral is SWIS + Read more ...

Social Work Information System (SWIS)

Record as an additional contact under the current referral. Include:

  • Contact type: select the field that describes the reason for contact with the customer
  • Social Work Intervention: select Referral Out and other interventions if needed
  • Referral Out Reason: select Social Casework
  • Outcome of Intervention: select Social Casework

Social Casework Reporting Tool

If referring a customer for social casework, there is no need to record the referral in the Social casework commenced with a customer reporting tool on the SharePoint page. This tool is only used when starting a new social casework referral with a customer.

Procedure ends here.

6

Referring people with difficult or challenging behaviours + Read more ...

Note: Service Officers must not directly refer these customers for social casework.

A social worker must be consulted about these referrals to social casework. This can be through the:

  • Zone panel or Customer Conduct Team
  • Managed Service Plan (MSP)
  • Personalised Services team
  • Service Centre Manager

The social worker will decide whether to accept or decline a referral.

Consider a referral to Personalised Services for customers who may benefit from case management. For details about the management of customer aggression, see About customer aggression.
