Customer contact

Parts of this process are for Jobseeker Bereavement Boutique Team (JBBT) processing staff only.

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Reason for customer contact + Read more ...

Customer has contacted to:

  • Advise of death of partner, go to Step 2
  • Request reassessment of PBV paid at non-pregnant rate, go to Step 3
  • Query a change in PBV entitlement after reporting, see Table 3 Step 5

2

Customer contacts to advise of a death + Read more ...

Where a customer contacts or has completed an Advice of Death (SA116) form to advise of the death of their partner, complete the Notification of Death guided procedure.

If a customer advises of their partner's death in a new claim for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA), see:

  • Processing JobSeeker Payment (JSP) claims
  • Processing Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) claims
  • Assessing and coding student and Australian Apprentice claims for Youth Allowance (YA)

If a current JSP customer has notified of the death by phone or in person, on completion of the death action a Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity will be created. Where the death action is unable to be completed, complete the PBV Enquiry Escalation Fast Note. Eligibility for PBV will then be assessed by an appropriately skilled Service Officer and the customer will be contacted where required.

See Table 2.

3

Customer has received PBV and later advises of pregnancy + Read more ...

A customer who has received PBV at the non-pregnant rate may be reassessed for PBV at pregnant rate, while they are still pregnant. For the reassessment to occur, a medical certificate or letter from the customer's doctor needs to state the expected date of the customer's confinement (due date for baby's birth). This will determine if the customer was pregnant when their partner died.

Has customer provided acceptable medical evidence of the pregnancy duration?

  • Yes:
    • Record the PBV Enquiry Escalation Fast Note requesting the PBV be reassessed under the pregnant rate
    • The existing PBV payments need to be adjusted to pay the difference between the non-pregnant rate and the pregnant rate. This will be paid as an immediate payment
    • For processing instructions, see Processing Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV) claims
      • Using Customer First, issue a Q950, advising of the net arrears resulting from the reassessment
      • Select relevant paragraphs for any extension of the bereavement exemption for mutual obligation or activity test requirements
  • No, advise the customer to provide a letter from their doctor or medical certificate that documents the expected date of confinement. DOC the record. Procedure ends here

Determining relationship status

For Jobseeker Bereavement Boutique Team (JBBT) processing staff only.

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Determine whether an existing member of a couple + Read more ...

Were the customer's and the deceased partner's records linked at the time of the partner's death?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, and
    • the customer has previously contacted and has provided evidence of the relationship, go to Step 6
    • this is the initial contact, go to Step 2

2

Check payment + Read more ...

If the customer is receiving:

  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP), go to Step 5
  • Youth Allowance (YA) and is over 22 years of age, go to Step 5
  • Youth Allowance (YA) and is under 22 years of age, go to Step 3

3

Determine if claimant is an independent YA customer + Read more ...

YA customers under 22 years of age may be considered dependent or independent.

If they are independent:

  • they are not subject to the Parental Means Test, and
  • when they become a member of a couple their record will be linked to their partner's

If they are dependent and have a partner:

  • they are still subject to the Parental Means Test and
  • their records are not linked to their partner's file (because the system will pay an incorrect rate). Once they meet the rules to be independent due to being a member of a couple their records will be linked

Navigate to the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen to determine if the customer has a current independence reason coded.

Does the customer have a current independence reason coded?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Determine if the customer could be considered a member of a couple + Read more ...

Service Officers will need to determine if the customer could have been considered a member of a couple at the time of their partner's death.

Check the customer's record to determine if evidence of their relationship has already been provided. This could include documents uploaded as a part of a new claim, or a Display on Access DOC which indicates an assessment was done as either part of a new claim or the customer has previously advised they were partnered but did not meet the rules to be assessed as independent due to being a member of a couple.

Does evidence on the customer's file indicate they can be considered to have been a member of a couple with the deceased at the time of their death?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Request evidence of relationship + Read more ...

Advise the customer they will need to provide evidence that they were a member of a couple with the deceased person at the time of their death. It is important to provide the customer with examples of acceptable evidence. Advise the customer they should contact Services Australia after they have provided evidence so that it can be assessed. Evidence may include:

  • insurance policy
  • lease agreement
  • dependent spouse rebate
  • statements from their parents

Clearly document the customer's file with the reason for the evidence request.

6

Evidence provided + Read more ...

Has the customer provided satisfactory evidence that they were a member of a couple with the deceased at the time of their death?

  • Yes, and they are receiving:
    • JSP or YA at the independent rate, see Step 1 in Table 3
    • YA at the dependent rate, go to Step 7
  • No, inform the customer they are not eligible for PBV unless they can provide satisfactory evidence. Do not update the Miscellaneous Update Details (NMIS) screen, record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

7

Assess member of a couple + Read more ...

Determine whether they should be assessed as a member of a couple due to special circumstances for independence purposes. If:

  • Yes, to ensure PBV is calculated based on the independent rate of payment, code the independence. See Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, the customer may still be eligible for PBV, however assessed on the dependent rate of YA. See Step 1 in Table 3

Assess entitlement to Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV)

For Jobseeker Bereavement Boutique Team (JBBT) processing staff only.

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Initial assessment + Read more ...

PBV is paid to the surviving JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) member of a couple if all of the following apply:

  • The customer is receiving JSP or YA when they advise of their partner's death.
    For a new claimant, the customer:
    • needs to be eligible for JSP or YA when they advise of partner's death, and
    • when the claim is granted, JSP or YA is payable
  • For current customers, their payment must be payable, that is, not current with a nil rate as determined on the Entitlement Period End Day (EPED)
  • Both the customer and the deceased partner were an Australian resident when the partner died
  • The customer and the deceased person were a member of a couple when their partner died
  • The customer has not re-partnered when they notified of the death
  • The customer, their nominee or a third party notifies of the death within the 14 week bereavement period or within the duration of the pregnancy if the pregnancy is more than 14 weeks from partner's death

If the customer is also eligible for a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP), they must relinquish the LBP in writing. This includes customers who have already been paid LBP and wish to receive PBV instead.

Dependent YA customers whose partner has died are also eligible for PBV, even though their records may not be linked.

Is the customer eligible for PBV?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, other bereavement assistance may be available:
    • LBP, see Assessing entitlement to Lump Sum Bereavement Payment
    • Exemptions from some waiting periods for new claimants, see Income Maintenance Period (IMP), Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP), Ordinary Waiting Period (OWP) and Seasonal Work Preclusion Period (SWPP)
    • Exemptions from mutual obligation and activity test requirements, see Mutual obligation requirement exemptions
    • exemptions from the activity test for YA (student) customers when their partner dies

2

Eligibility for both LBP and PBV + Read more ...

Some customers may be eligible for both PBV and Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP). They are only entitled to receive one or the other. If a customer is eligible for both bereavement payments, a Manual Follow-up (MFU) will be generated on their record after their partner's death action is done, advising staff of the next steps required.

Is the customer eligible for both LBP and PBV?

  • Yes, and
    • Partner Bereavement Payment application (SA490) has been returned, go to Step 4
    • all other circumstances, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Calculate LBP and PBV + Read more ...

The customer must be issued LBP immediately.

Launch the bereavement calculator and code relevant fields for PBV and LBP. Note: the calculator can provide comparative non-pregnant and pregnant rates of PBV. Refer to Calculation of a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment for coding details.

If the customer asks how much their LBP and PBV amounts would be:

  • provide estimated comparative amounts by using the bereavement calculator in Process Direct
  • advise the customer that these amounts are estimates only and may alter if the customer's circumstances change, such as receipt of earnings

If the customer is:

  • Eligible for PBV only, process payment. See Processing Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV) claims
  • Eligible for PBV and LBP, go to Step 4
  • Not eligible for PBV, go to Step 5

4

Customer eligible for PBV and LBP + Read more ...

If the customer is eligible for both PBV and LBP, they are not eligible to receive PBV until they have made a written request to relinquish their LBP entitlement. This includes cases where:

  • customer has already received LBP, and
  • the customer who is eligible for LBP has not yet received it

Inform the customer:

  • they must make a written request to give up their LBP entitlement, before they can be assessed for PBV
  • advise the customer that this can be done on the SA490 form

For processing instructions, see Processing Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV) claims.

5

PBV amount is not payable + Read more ...

If PBV is not paid after the customer's EPED, because the customer is now in receipt of a nil rate, i.e. CZR:

  • Create a Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Generic > Enquiry > Review of NIL PBV
  • Advise the customer that they will be contacted with the result of the review

Note: this Fast Note is only to be used where PBV is not paid as expected, when the original assessment was to pay PBV. If the customer was assessed as being ineligible for PBV and queries this decision, the usual review of decision procedures apply.

Other bereavement assistance may be available if PBV amount is not payable:

  • Lump sum bereavement payment
  • Exemptions from some waiting periods for new claimants, see:
    • Income Maintenance Period (IMP)
    • Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP)
    • Ordinary Waiting Period (OWP)
    • Seasonal Work Preclusion Period (SWPP)
  • Exemptions from mutual obligation and activity test requirements, see:
    • Mutual obligation requirement exemptions and
    • Study exemptions for ABSTUDY and Activity Test exemptions for Austudy and Youth Allowance (YA)
  • If the customer has requested PBV after receiving LBP, and PBV is:
    • less than LBP, a PBV debt will result
    • equal to LBP, no PBV will be paid
  • Advise the customer that a PBV amount cannot be paid, include the reason and ask if they wish to withdraw their application
  • If the customer:
    • still wishes to proceed with the PBV application, see Processing Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV) claims
    • agrees to withdraw their application, DOC the record with the decision. Procedure ends here
