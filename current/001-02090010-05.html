Department of Employment and Workplace Relations

Eligibility for SEE in various circumstances

Item

Description

1

Job seekers serving an Income Maintenance Period (IMP) + Read more ...

Job seekers serving an Income Maintenance Period (IMP) on nil rate are not subject to mutual obligation requirements. If they meet all the eligibility criteria, they may volunteer for SEE, despite not receiving income support.

2

Issues that may impact on the capacity to learn + Read more ...

If the Job Seeker Snapshot indicates an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) is required, or the Service Officer believes there may be barriers to the job seeker's ability to participate in SEE, then a referral to an appropriate Specialist Officer should be made. For example, Employment Services Assessor, Social Worker, or Service Support Officer.

Alternately, an ESAt may recommend that a job seeker be considered for SEE. The Service Officer must check:

  • that a referral would be appropriate, and
  • if participation in SEE can be at the same time as other activities the job seeker is undertaking

If the referral is appropriate, the Service Officer needs to arrange an appointment with the job seeker to discuss a referral to SEE.

3

Holders of temporary or skilled migrant visas + Read more ...

Job seekers must be eligible for registration for employment services through Workforce Australia, Community Development Program (CDP), Disability Employment Services (DES), Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE), ParentsNext (PNX), or Workforce Australia - Transition to Work (TtW), in order to qualify for Skills for Education and Employment (SEE).

Job seekers are not eligible to be referred to SEE, if they are:

  • a current holder of a skilled migrant visa, irrespective of whether the visa is permanent or temporary, or
  • a secondary visa holder of a person who holds a skilled migrant visa

For further information, see:

  • Common types of Australian visas
  • Employment services for visa holders

4

Assessing 'working rights in Australia' + Read more ...

Visas are issued to non-Australian citizens to travel to and enter Australia, and/or remain in Australia. Certain visas allow a person to have working rights in Australia. This means they:

  • are legally allowed to work (and look for work) in Australia, and
  • can register for employment services assistance

To check if a person holding a visa has working rights in Australia, Service Officers must check the Department of Home Affairs website visa information page.

See also Employment services for visa holders.

5

Assessing the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) + Read more ...

A customer may be eligible for SEE while they are serving the NARWP.

If the NARWP has been recorded, it appears on the Periods of AU Presence while AU Resident (RSPAP) screen. If the date on the End of the 104wks (if applicable) or the End of 208wks (if applicable) fields are:

  • in the future, but within 2 or 4 years, as applicable of the SEE referral date, then the customer is eligible for referral
  • in the past, then the NARWP has ended and they are not eligible for a SEE referral

The NARWP must have been recorded before the SEE referral can be made. The RSPAP screen automatically populates when a customer's Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) and Country of Residence (CRES) are coded.

Customers do not need to lodge a claim for income support payment for the NARWP to be assessed and coded.
