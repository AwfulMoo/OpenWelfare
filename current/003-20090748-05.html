Verbal claim

For every phone claim taken:

  • Right click on the Verbal claim template link below
  • Select 'Save link as'
  • Staff must save the template to their desktop
  • Open the template
  • Complete a new template for each period of self-isolation, quarantine or caring a customer is applying for
  • Do not delete any questions in this template
  • Always access the version in Resources for future claims
    • Content is updated regularly. Using the current version prevents accidental use of customer data from a previous claim
  • Complete all questions as per your discussion with the customer by:
    • Answering 'Yes' or 'No' as needed, and
    • Documenting relevant information
  • Any information in blue that is not relevant to the customer's claim should be deleted once actioned otherwise the template may exceed the character limit for Fast Notes
  • Use the hyperlinks to progress through the template
  • Copy and paste the completed template into the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Verbal Claim Fast Note
  • When completing the template, follow Operational Blueprint
  • Use this template and Operational Blueprint together

- copy and paste content into the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Verbal Claim Fast Note in Customer First
- delete the saved template
- start a new template from the below link for any new claim

Scenarios - policy to apply

Table 1: This table helps staff determine which policy to apply where a customer has multiple claims.

Scenario

Reason

Scenario 1

14 day policy applies to both claims

A customer calls on 10 December 2021. They have been self-isolating since 22 November 2021 due to

  • testing positive with COVID-19
  • being advised by a required authority to self-isolate

They have not recovered from the effects of COVID-19, and received a further instruction from a required authority to self-isolate.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Policy to apply for both periods of self-isolation:

  • Before 9 December 2021 (up to and including 8 December 2021)

This is because both periods of self-isolation started before 9 December 2021.

In the ‘date affected by event’ field in the workflow enter:

  • 22 November 2021 for the first 14 day payment - as this is the start of the customer’s self-isolation period
  • 6 December 2021 for the second 14 day payment - as this is the first claim period +1

Scenario 2

14 day policy applies to first claim

7 day on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 9 January 2022) policy applies to second claim

A customer calls on 10 December 2021. They have been self-isolating since 29 November 2021 due to

  • testing positive with COVID-19
  • being advised by a required authority to self-isolate

They have not recovered from the effects of COVID-19, and received a further instruction from a required authority to self-isolate.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Policy to apply for the

  • first period of self-isolation - before 9 December 2021 (up to and including 8 December 2021)
    • as the start date of this period was 29 November 2021
  • second period of self-isolation - on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 9 January 2022
    • as the start date of this period was 13 December 2021

Different policy applies for the second period of self-isolation, as this starts on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 9 January 2022.

In the ‘date affected by event’ field in the workflow enter:

  • 29 November 2021 for the first (14 day) payment – as this is the start of the customer’s self-isolation period
  • 13 December 2021 for the second (7 day) payment – as this is the first claim period +1

Scenario 3

7 day on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 9 January 2022) policy applies to both claims

A customer calls on 17 December 2021. They have been self-isolating since 9 December 2021 due to

  • testing positive with COVID-19
  • being advised by a required authority to self-isolate

They have not recovered from the effects of COVID-19, and received a further instruction from a required authority to self-isolate.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Policy to apply for both periods of self-isolation:

  • on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 9 January 2022

This is because both periods of self-isolation started on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 9 January 2022.

In the ‘date affected by event’ field in the workflow enter:

  • 9 December 2021 for the first 7 day payment – as this is the start of the customer’s self-isolation period
  • 16 December 2021 for the second 7 day payment – as this is the first claim period +1

Scenario 4

7 day:

  • on or after 9 December to up to and including 9 January 2022 policy applies to first claim
  • From 10 January 2022 policy applies to the second claim

A customer lodges 2 x EM252 claim forms.

Note: the EM252 Claim for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment form is no longer available. Customers must lodge a phone claim for any self-isolation, quarantine or caring periods that start on or before 17 January 2022.

The:

  • first claim form states the customer’s isolation period is for 7 days from 6 January 2022
  • second claim form states the customer’s isolation period is from 13 January 2022

In reviewing the claim forms, the customer meets all eligibility criteria for both claim periods.

Policy to apply for the

  • first period of self-isolation – on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 9 January 2022
    • as the start date of this period was 6 January 2022
  • second period of self-isolation - on or after 10 January 2022
    • as the start date of this period was 13 January 2022

Different policy applies for the second period of self-isolation, as this starts on or after 10 January 2022.

In the ‘date affected by event’ field in the workflow enter:

  • 6 January 2022 for the first 7 day payment - as this is the start of the customer’s self-isolation period
  • 13 January 2022 for the second 7 day payment - as this is the first claim period +1

Scenario 5

A customer lodges an online claim on 25 January 2022. They have been self-isolating since 23 January 2022 due to testing positive with COVID-19 using a Rapid Antigen Test (RAT) on 23 January 2022

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

7 day (on or after 18 January 2022) policy applies

This is because the period of self-isolation started on or after 18 January 2022.

The start date of the customer’s claim period will be 23 January 2022, the date they started self-isolating.

See Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Claims in Process Direct.

Scenario 6

A customer calls on 22 January 2022. They had a Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) test and started self-isolating on 17 January 2022. The test result came back positive on 19 January along with a directive to self-isolate from 17 January 2022.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

7 day (on or after 10 January 2022 and up to and including 17 January 2022) policy applies

This is because the period of self-isolation started on or before 17 January 2022.

Scenarios – generic – from 10 January 2022

Table 2: This table has generic scenarios that apply to all events for periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring that start on or after 10 January 2022 and up to and including 17 January 2022.

Scenario

Eligibility

Reason

Scenario 1

Start date of claim and multiple payments -example 1

A customer contacts the agency on 30 January 2022 to claim the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

They visited an exposure site on 7 January 2022. They were not aware of this until they received direct instruction from a required authority on 10 January 2022, advising them to self-isolate due to being a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19. The customer returned to work after 7 days.

On 4 February 2022, the customer tested positive with COVID-19 and started self-isolating for a second time.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria

Eligible

The

  • customer is eligible for 2 payments of Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • start date of the first payment is 10 January 2022 - date the customer started self-isolating and not the date the customer was exposed to COVID-19. Enter this date in the 'date affected by event' field in the workflow
  • the first payment is subject to the eligibility criteria from 10 January 2022
  • end date of the first payment is 16 January 2022
  • start date of the second payment is 4 February 2022 – date the customer started self-isolating for a second time
    • as the start date is on or after 18 January 2022, this payment is subject to the eligibility criteria from 18 January 2022 and processed in Process Direct. See Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Claims in Process Direct and Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment – Assisted Customer Claims
  • end date of the second payment is 10 February 2022

Note: customers can only be granted 1 Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment on any day as 2 or more payments cannot be released on the same day for any customer. Staff must complete the assessment of the second payment, but cannot finalise the claim with a grant outcome until the next day.

If the customer did not recover from the effects of COVID-19 before 10 February 2022, they may have been eligible to claim another payment from 11 February 2022, which is 7 days +1 from the start date of the second payment. The customer would need to meet all eligibility criteria and the claim would be processed in Process Direct.

Scenario 2

Start date of claim and multiple payments - example 2

A customer contacts the agency on 5 February 2022 to claim the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

They visited an exposure site on 7 January 2022. They were not aware of this until they received direct instruction from a required authority on 10 January 2022, advising them to self-isolate due to being a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19.

On 14 January 2022, the customer tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The

  • customer is eligible for 2 payments of Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • start date of the first payment is 10 January 2022 - date the customer started self-isolating and not the date the customer was exposed to COVID-19. Enter this date in the 'date affected by event' field in the workflow
  • end date of the first payment is 16 January 2022
  • start date of the second payment is 17 January 2022 – which is 7 days +1 from the start date of the first payment. This is because the customer tested positive during the first 7 day period of self-isolation. Enter this date in the 'date affected by event' field in the workflow
  • end date of the second payment is 23 January 2022

Note: customers can only be granted 1 Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment on any day as 2 or more payments cannot be released on the same day for any customer. Staff must complete the assessment of the second payment, but cannot finalise the claim with a grant outcome until the next day.

If the customer did not recover from the effects of COVID-19 before 23 January 2022, they may have been eligible to claim another payment from 24 January 2022, which is 7 days +1 from the start date of the second payment. The customer would need to meet all eligibility criteria and the claim would be processed in Process Direct.

Scenario 3

Start date of self-isolation is different to start date advised by a required authority

A customer contacts the agency on 22 January 2022 to claim the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

They visited an exposure site on 14 January 2022.

On 15 January 2022, they found out the site was an exposure site and started self-isolating. They received a personal instruction from a required authority to start self-isolating on 17 January 2022 due to being a close contact of a person who tested positive with COVID-19.

The personal instruction advised the customer to start self-isolating for 7 days from 16 January 2022.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

In the ‘date affected by event’ field in the workflow enter 15 January 2022, as this is the date the customer started self-isolating.

Do not enter 14, 16, 17 or 22 January 2022, as these are not the dates the customer started self-isolating.

Scenario 4

Multiple payments example 1

A customer contacts the agency on 15 January 2022. They have been self-isolating since 10 January 2022, due to direct instruction from a required authority, which states they were a close contact of a person who tested positive with COVID-19. They believe they may need to self-isolate for 14 days.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible for 1 payment only

For the customer to receive a second payment, they must:

  • contact the agency on the first day of the next period (or later) of their self-isolation period, and
  • test their eligibility for a second payment

This is because their period of self-isolation has not ended. The agency cannot be sure if the customer is required to self-isolate for more than 7 days.

Scenario 5

Multiple payments example 2

A customer contacts the agency on 1 February 2022. They are claiming for a 14 day self-isolation period that started on 10 January 2022.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible for 2 payments

The customer is eligible for 2 x 7 day payment (2 x $750).

This is because the customer has already self-isolated for a full 14 day period.

In the ‘date affected by event’ field in the workflow enter:

  • 10 January 2022 for the first 7 day payment - as this is the start of the customer’s self-isolation period
  • 17 January 2022 for the second 7 day payment - as this is the first claim period +1

Scenario 6

Multiple payments example 3

A customer has tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer takes longer than 7 days to recover and tests positive with COVID-19 again.

Their 7 day period of self-isolation continues for 5 more days.

The customer calls after completing their second self-isolation period.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible for 2 payments

The customer is eligible for 2 x $750 payments of the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

Even though the customer’s second period of self-isolation was not for a full 7 days, the:

  • payment is not pro-rata
  • customer meets all eligibility criteria for at least 1 day of the 7 day period

If the customer had not completed their second period of self-isolation, only 1 x $750 could be granted. The customer would need to call back to claim for the second payment on the first day of the next period (or later) to claim to test their eligibility. The customer would need to meet all eligibility criteria.

Note: customers can only be granted 1 Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment on any day as 2 or more payments cannot be released on the same day for any customer. Staff must complete the assessment of the second payment, but cannot finalise the claim with a grant outcome until the next day.

Scenario 7

A customer is a 15 year old casual worker at a café. They tested positive with COVID-19 and have been quarantined.

Not eligible

The customer is not at least 17 years old.

Note: if the customer was in the care of an adult, and the adult meets the other eligibility criteria, the adult can make a claim.

Scenario 8

A customer is a casual worker in a café. They lost their job when the café closed under the Stage 4 restrictions for their state or territory.

Not eligible

The customer has not been directed to self-isolate by a required authority due to being a close contact and has not tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer may be eligible for other income support payments.

Scenario 9

A customer gets a phone call from a required authority informing them to self-isolate as they are a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer:

  • cannot go to their workplace
  • can access 3 days leave entitlement, but they need to take another 4 days off to cover the 7 day period, and
  • meets all other eligibility criteria

Eligible

The customer:

  • has been informed to self-isolate or quarantine by a required authority, and
  • does not have enough leave to cover the full 7 day period

They are paid $750. The payment is not pro-rata.

Scenario 10

A required authority sends a letter to a customer informing them to self-isolate for 7 days. The letter advises the customer they are a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer:

  • cannot go to their workplace
  • has leave entitlements, however the business cannot pay them because of current financial pressures, and
  • meets all other eligibility criteria

Eligible

The customer cannot access leave entitlements as the business cannot pay these.

Even though the customer is a contact of a close contact, the letter from a required authority has advised the customer to self-isolate for 7 days.

Scenario 11

A customer’s employer has told all staff to self-isolate for 7 days. This is because another employee was diagnosed with COVID-19.

The customer’s employer was informed by a letter from the required authority that all staff were considered to be close contacts of a person who tested positive with COVID-19 and should self-isolate for 7 days. The required authority advised they will follow up by directly informing each close contact to self-isolate.

The customer then receives contact directly from the required authority informing them to self-isolate as they are close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria

Eligible

Although the required authority informed the employer that employees should self-isolate or quarantine, the customer is required to be directly informed by:

  • phone
  • email
  • SMS or
  • sending a personal letter

Once the customer receives contact directly from the required authority they are eligible.

The customer can contact the required authority to seek confirmation of the requirement to isolate.

The customer should retain evidence of contact with the required authority

Scenario 12

Household instruction example 1

Parents of a child have received a letter from a required authority informing them that their entire household needs to self-isolate as the child has been identified as a close contact of a confirmed case of COVID-19. The letter is titled ‘Dear Parents’. However, it includes the parents’ residential address. Both parents meet all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The letter from the required authority:

  • includes the parents’ address
  • means that all members of the household are a close contact of a confirmed case of COVID-19 and must self-isolate

Both parents are eligible as they meet all other eligibility criteria.

If the letter to the parents did not include their residential address, the parents would need to show they have been directly informed/ personally instructed to self-isolate or quarantine.

Scenario 13

Household instruction example 2

A customer’s partner receives a text message from a required authority informing them that their entire household needs to self-isolate, as they are a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19. The text message includes the name of the customer’s partner, but not the customer. The customer did not receive a text message.

The customer

  • lives in the same household as their partner who received the text message, and
  • meets all other eligibility criteria

Eligible

Although the customer did not receive a text message, they live in the same household as the person who received a text message. The text message advised the household to self-isolate due to being a close contact of someone who has tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer would not be eligible if the:

  • text message was generic, that is, it did not include the name of the customer’s partner, or a household address
  • customer did not live in the same household as the customer

Scenario 14

A customer calls to claim a payment as they have seen on social media their residential address is in a COVID-19 ‘hot spot’. The customer is unable to get to work during that time.

Not eligible

The customer has not been directly informed/personally instructed to self-isolate or quarantine by a required authority and have not tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer may be eligible for other income support payments.

Scenario 15

A customer hears on the radio that a restaurant she visited with her sister over the weekend is in a ‘hot spot’ and she must self-isolate or quarantine for 7 days.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Not eligible

The customer has not been informed to self-isolate or quarantine by a required authority and have not tested positive with COVID-19.

Scenario 16

The customer in the above scenario contacts the agency a week later. Her sister, who she visited the restaurant with, has tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer has since received an SMS from a required authority that she must self-isolate for 7 days due to being a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer has been informed to self-isolate or quarantine:

  • by a required authority, and
  • due to being a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19

The customer would not be eligible if:

  • they were informed to self-isolate or quarantine by a required authority, and
  • the reason the customer was informed to self-isolate or quarantine is due to:
    • complying with stage 3 or 4 restrictions in a state or territory,
    • living in an area that is deemed a ‘hot spot’,
    • returning from an area that is deemed a ‘hot spot’, and/or
    • visiting a venue, such as a restaurant or shopping centre, that is deemed a ‘hot spot’

Scenario 17

A customer has chosen to self-isolate as a result of a medical condition. The customer has not attended the workplace and is unable to work from home.

Not eligible

The customer has not been directed to self-isolate by a required authority due to being a close contact and has not tested positive with COVID-19.

Scenario 18

A sole trader receives an SMS from a required authority telling them to self-isolate for 7 days as a close contact of a person who tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer would have worked Monday to Friday each week had they not been told to self-isolate. As they are a sole trader they have no appropriate leave entitlements.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The sole trader has received an SMS informing them to self-isolate for 7 days.

The customer is eligible for 1 payment per 7 days or part thereof that they meet the eligibility criteria. The payment is not paid on a pro-rata basis.

Scenario 19

A customer receives a personal letter from a required authority to self-isolate for 7 days as a close contact of a person who tested positive to COVID-19.

The customer is paid pandemic leave from their employer. They do not usually work on the weekends.

The customer makes a claim for the weekend.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria

Not eligible

The customer was not likely to have worked during the period of isolation they are claiming.

Scenario 20

A customer calls as the child care centre their child goes to has been closed since 12 December 2021.

When asked if the child care centre was instructed to close due to instruction by a required authority, the customer advises yes, the Director of the child care centre said the centre had to be closed.

Not eligible

A required authority did not directly instruct the staff or students at the child care centre to self-isolate or quarantine.

Scenario 21

Liquid assets

A customer lodges a phone claim on 20 January 2022. They have been self-isolating since 17 January 2022 due to testing positive with COVID-19 using a Rapid Antigen Test (RAT).

The customer advises they have:

  • evidence of registering this test with their state health authority, and
  • $12,363.00 in a savings accounts as at 17 January 2022

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The liquid assets test only comes into effect for self-isolation, quarantine or caring periods that start on or after 18 January 2022.

Scenario 22

A customer has made a claim using the Claim for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment form (EM252) for a 7 day period from 10 to 16 January 2022.

Note: the EM252 Claim for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment form is no longer available. Customers must lodge a phone claim for any self-isolation, quarantine or caring periods that start on or before 17 January 2022.

The customers has appropriate leave entitlements to cover 6 days (10 to 15 January 2022) of the claim period.

The form asks ‘Have you exhausted all appropriate leave entitlements from this employer?’

The customer has recorded ‘From 15 January 2022 - To 16 January 2022’ as being the 1 day of the claiming period they ‘exhausted’ their appropriate leave entitlement with their employer.

Eligible

For the 7 day period starting on or after 10 January 2022, the customer is eligible for a payment if they have no appropriate leave for at least 1 day in the period claimed.
The customer had paid leave for 6 days (10 January 2022 to 15 January 2022) of the claiming period (10 January 2022 to 16 January 2022).

They exhausted their appropriate leave entitlement and had not appropriate leave from their employer to cover 16 January 2022, that is they did not have appropriate leave to cover their full 7 day claim period.

Scenarios – generic – from 9 December 2021 and up to and including 9 January 2022

Table 3: This table has generic scenarios that apply to all events for periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring that start on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 9 January 2022.

Scenario

Eligibility

Reason

Scenario 1 – COVID-19 Disaster Payment

The following will only apply if a COVID-19 Disaster Payment is activated for a relevant period from 9 December.

A customer has been working as a hairdresser and unable to work due to a restricted movement order preventing hairdressers from operating.

The customer worked 15 hours per week and has been paid the ‘less than 20 hours rate of the COVID-19 Disaster Payment.

The customer is identified as a close contact of person who has tested positive with COVID-19 on 12 December 2021and must self-isolate for 7 days.

The customer contacts the agency and is advised they may be eligible to claim $750 for the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment due to the direction to self-isolate. They feel this is more beneficial than the $450 COVID-19 Disaster Payment they have been claiming.

Not eligible

The reason the customer is unable to attend work is because of the restricted movement. The customer cannot answer that, if not for the direction to self-isolate, they would have worked as their workplace is not operating until the restricted movement order is lifted.

Scenario 2 – COVID-19 Disaster Payment

The following will only apply if a COVID-19 Disaster Payment is activated for a relevant period from 9 December.

A customer has 2 jobs, 1 as a café worker where they work 15 hours per week assisting with take away orders and the other working 15 hours per week in a retail store.

Due to a restricted movement order the customer is unable to continue working at the retail store and claims the COVID-19 Disaster Payment as they are losing 15 hours per week of work.

The customer:

  • can continue working in the café as they provide a take-away service
  • is paid the ‘ less than 20 hours rate’ of the COVID-19 Disaster Payment

A sole trader receives an SMS from a required authority telling them to self-isolate for 14 days as a close contact of a person who tested positive with COVID-19.

The customer would have worked Monday to Friday each week had they not been told to self-isolate. As they are a sole trader they have no appropriate leave entitlements.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The sole trader has received an SMS informing them to self-isolate for 7 days.

The customer is eligible for 1 payment per 7 days or part thereof that they meet the eligibility criteria. The payment is not paid on a pro-rata basis.

Scenario 20

A customer receives a personal letter from a required authority to self-isolate for 14 days as a close contact of a person who tested positive to COVID-19.

The customer is paid pandemic leave from their employer. They do not usually work on the weekends.

The customer makes a claim for the weekend.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria

Not eligible

The customer was not likely to have worked during the period of isolation they are claiming.

Scenario 21

A school was closed on 30 July 2020 as a case of COVID-19 was identified in a student.

At the direction of a required authority, the school principal instructed all staff and students not to attend the school. This was to allow deep cleaning of the school and contact tracing.

All schools in the area returned to remote learning from 5 August 2020.

A customer is a teacher at the school and calls to claim the payment.

Another customer is a parent of a child who attends the school, and calls to claim the payment.

Both customers are not eligible

Staff and students were instructed not to attend the school because of deep cleaning and contact tracing.

These customers are not eligible, as they were not instructed to self-isolate or quarantine by a required authority.

The customers will be eligible if, after contact tracing, a required authority:

  • identified close contacts, and
  • instructed the customers to self-isolate via letter, SMS, email or phone call

The customers should retain evidence of contact with a required authority.

Scenario 22

A customer calls as the school their child goes to has been closed since 5 August 2020.

When asked if the school was instructed to close due to instruction by a required authority, the customer advises yes, the Premier issued a direction of Stage 4 restrictions and all schools closed.

Not eligible

The school is closed as all year levels are under Stage 4 restrictions.

A required authority did not directly instruct the staff or students at the school to self-isolate or quarantine.
