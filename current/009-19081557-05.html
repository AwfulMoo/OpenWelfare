Manual Follow-Ups (MFU)

The following scenarios will produce Manual Follow Up (MFU) messages to support CP (XWP) processing. Most scenarios deal with gaining or losing entitlement to payment level CA and the effect on CP (XWP) grandfathering eligibility.

Only staff at the APS5 level or above can make changes to the grandfathering status.

MFUs where payment level CA is granted or reinstated

Table 1

Scenario

Message and Keywords

Details

Retrospective payment level CA entitlement outside 13 weeks – CP (XWP) remains cancelled

Check if customer is eligible for CAR (XWP) restoration

Keywords XWP, XWCGRF, I094PN

Work type ID - ZIAM_CDA_RES_MFU

If CA is granted or reinstated without any gaps, more than 13 weeks after the date of effect of suspension, cancellation or change of CA HCC only, CP (XWP) will not be automatically restored.

CP (XWP) grandfathering status ‘NOT’ will automatically be deleted and the previous status ‘GRF’ will be reinstated. However the CAR (XWP) benefit status will remain as CAN-CAC. No CAR paragraph will be generated.

An MFU is raised to review the carer’s income, assets and accommodation and manually restore CP (XWP) if appropriate.

Retrospective entitlement on or before 19 March 2020 –AGE/JSP

Check if customer is eligible for CAR (XWP)

Keywords XWP, XWCGRF, I096PN

Work type ID - ZIAM_CDA_RESGRA_MFU

WP customer has already transferred to AGE/JSP from 20 March 2020 or at the end of the bereavement period.

Subsequently, payment level CA is granted or reinstated with a Date of Effect (DOE) prior to 20 March 2020.

Customer gets payment level CA on both 19/03/20 and 20/03/20 and has no break in entitlement since then.

Grandfathered ‘GRF’ status code will automatically be applied to the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) and Grandfathering Override (GFD) screens from 20 March 2020. However AGE/JSP grandfathering status won’t be changed.

An MFU is raised to reject AGE/JSP and grant CP (XWP) manually from 20 March 2020 or the end of the bereavement period if applicable. If CAR (XWP) is granted, AGE/JSP will be automatically rejected or cancelled from the grant date. Overpaid AGE/JSP will be offset from CP (XWP) arrears.

Note:

  • manually delete the WP Residence Test Exemption on the ARD when indexing the CP claim.
  • the grandfathering type XWC 2020 Former WP-CARER will automatically be coded on the GFS screen.
  • the grandfathering type XWP 2020 Former WP/WID will automatically be deleted on the GFS when AGE/JSP is rejected

Retrospective entitlement before 20 March 2020 – AGE/JSP to CP (Standard)

Check if customer should be granted CAR (XWP)

Keywords XWP, XWCGRF, I098PN

Non Grandfathered CP Standard

Work Item ID - ZIAM_CDA_RESGRA_MFU

WP customer was transferred to AGE/JSP from 20 March 2020 or at the end of the bereavement period. Subsequently, customer has been granted CP (Standard).

Subsequently, payment level CA is granted, restored, or changed to payment level from CA HCC only or CA zero rate due to ETT, with a Date of Effect (DOE) prior to 20 March 2020.

Customer now gets payment level CA on both 19/03/20 and 20/03/20 and has no break in entitlement since then.

Grandfathered ‘GRO’ status code will be applied to the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) and Grandfathering Override (GFD) screens from the CP (Standard) grant date.

Note: ‘GRO’ status code allows carers who are eligible for CP (XWP) to be paid CP (standard).

An MFU is raised to reject AGE/JSP and grant CP (XWP) manually from 20 March 2020 or the end of the bereavement period. Then to re-grant CP (Standard)

Note:

  • manually delete the WP Residence Test Exemption on the ARD when indexing the CP claim
  • the grandfathering type XWC 2020 Former WP-CARER will automatically change to ‘NOT’ when CP (XWP) is manually granted from 20 March 2020
  • the grandfathering type XWC 2020 Former WP-CARER will automatically be coded on the GFS screen

Payment level CA entitlement stopped retrospectively on/before 20 March 2020.

CP (Standard) customer

Retrospective CDA Suspension/Cancellation – Manual debt action

Keywords XWP, XWCGRF, I119PN

Work type ID - ZIAM_CAR_SUSCAN_MFU

WP customer has already transferred to CP (XWP) and then was granted CP (Standard). Subsequently, customer’s last or the only payment level CA payment stops retrospectively with a DOE on or before 20 March 2020.

The CP grandfathering status code ‘GRF’ will automatically be deleted from the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) and Grandfathering Override (GFD) screens from 20 March 2020. CP grandfathering status code ‘GRO’ will be deleted from grant date of CP (Standard).

CP (Standard) will stay current due to the limiting date for the CP (Standard) grant date. A CAR/BLD line will be displayed on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Customer has received CP (XWP) when not eligible, but was eligible to be grandfathered to ‘AGE’ if they didn’t get payment level CA on 19 March 2020, or eligible to be grandfathered to JSP if under APA.

An MFU is raised to check if there is any debt and notify the customer. Check if a debt was raised via the Daily Rate Components (RAC) screen before finalising the CA suspension/cancellation activity. Consider alternative eligibility for AGE/JSP before raising a debt.

Note: Customer is not eligible for ‘AGE’ grandfathering if CA was payable since 19 March 2020.

The loss of payment level CA and CP (XWP) grandfathering provisions doesn’t necessarily mean the carer is not eligible to continue receiving CP (Standard).

Note:

  • manually insert the WP Residence Test Exemption on the ARD when indexing the AGE/JSP claim.
  • the grandfathering status of GRO and GRF for XWC 2020 Former WP-CARER will automatically be deleted
  • check that the grandfathering type XWP 2020 Former WP/WID is inserted when indexing AGE/JSP from 20 March 2020

Payment level CA entitlement stopped retrospectively after 20 March 2020

CP (Standard) customer

Retrospective CDA Suspension/Cancellation – Manual debt action

Keywords XWP, XWCGRF, I119PN.

Work type ID - ZIAM_CAR_SUSCAN_MFU

WP customer transferred to CP (XWP) and then was granted CP (Standard). Subsequently, customer’s last or the only payment level CA payment stops retrospectively with a DOE after 20 March 2020 while customer was paid CP (XWP).

The CP (XWP) debt in between the CA cessation and CP (Standard) grant date can be manually raised through the Daily Rate Components (RAC) screen by using reason Before Limiting Date (LTD) when taking the CDA/CAN activity to the AR screen.

An MFU is raised to check if there is any debt between when CP (XWP) eligibility was lost and CP (Standard) was granted, and to notify the customer of loss of CP eligibility for the period. Customer was not eligible for AGE/JSP grandfathering as they got payment level CA on 19 March 2020.

Note:

  • manually cancel CP (XWP) from the CA cancellation date.
  • the grandfathering status for XWC 2020 Former WP-CARER will automatically change to NOT from the CA cancellation date

Payment level CA entitlement stopped (or reinstated) – manually grandfathered

Check if customer is still eligible for CAR (XWP)

Keywords XWP, XWCGRF

Work type ID - ZIAM_CDA_CARGRF_MFU

The system will not automatically change the CP (XWP) grandfathered status or cancel CP (XWP) when payment level CA is stopped for a manually grandfathered CP (XWP) customer (i.e. a grandfathered status ‘GRF’ was manually coded). CP (XWP) will remain current.

An MFU is raised to check if customer is still eligible for grandfathering and CP (XWP).

If no longer eligible, update the grandfathering status to ‘NOT’ from the date of effect (DOE) of the loss of eligibility.

If payment level CA payment stopped on or before 20 March 2020, reject CP (XWP) and consider eligibility to grandfather for AGE/JSP.

Note: customer is not eligible for ‘AGE’ grandfathering if CA was payable for 19 March.

If payment level CA payment stopped after 20 March 2020, add a grandfathering status of ‘NOT’ from the DOE of break in payment level CA entitlement, and cancel CP (XWP) manually from the same date.

Note: CP (XWP) is not automatically restored for manually grandfathered customers when payment level CA is restored, re-granted, or changed from CA HCC only or CA CUR ETT to payment level. An MFU will not be produced again. In this case confirm the customer does not have a break in payment level CA entitlement before manually re-grandfathering. If eligible.

  • select the 2020 Former WP–CARER line from the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) screen
  • on the Grandfathering Override (GFD) screen, delete the grandfathering status ‘NOT’
  • manually restore CP (XWP)

Where CP (Standard) Episodic eligibility is lost (e.g. due to the child leaving care), and the DOE is less than 3 months from the grant date, and the customer is still eligible for CP (XWP) grandfathering, CP Standard will not automatically switch to CP (XWP).

CP Standard will automatically cancel. The grandfathering status will remain as ‘GRO’.

An MFU is raised to manually grant CP (XWP) from the date of cancellation of CP (Standard). Delete the grandfathering status of GRO and ensure CP is still payable (e.g. customer is still payable under the pensions means test).

Updating the 2020 Former WP - CARER grandfathering type

The carer’s grandfathering provisions and grandfathering status will appear on the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) screen. Changes to a carer’s grandfathering status are made on the Grandfathering Override (GFD) screen.

The initial grandfathering status will be set as GRF on 20 March 2020; this includes Wife Pension customers who are in a bereavement period on 20 March 2020 and transfer to Carer Payment (XWP) on their bereavement period end date (BPED) + 1.

Rules when manually updating the 2020 Former WP - CARER grandfathering type

When inserting a new entry (as opposed to changing and existing entry), the date of effect of any new 2020 Former WP–CARER grandfathering status entry cannot be the same as an existing grandfathering entry.

The date of effect of any entry must be equal or greater than 20 March 2020.

Any updates, including insertion and changes, will be applied as the latest status. If there are any subsequent statuses with a DOE that is later than the new/changing entry, the system will automatically delete them.

Any deletion of an existing status will make the previous status the latest one.

Customer must get payment level Carer Allowance when the most recent status is changed to GRF/GRO.

Where a grandfathered status has been applied manually and payment level CA ceases, a MFU activity will prompt to manually amend the grandfathered status.
