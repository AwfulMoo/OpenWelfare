Descriptions of location types for s19AB exemptions

Table 5: this table describes the s19AB location types available for location specific and non-locations specific exemptions and their descriptions for use.

Location type

For exemptions that are…

Description for use

Specialist/specialist trainee

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption when they are specialists or consultant physicians in specific specialties or are trainees undertaking a specialist program with specific Educational Institutions. See Specialist and specialist trainee class exemption.

NT

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption when they are physically located in the Northern Territory at the time they render a professional service.

ATSI HCS Class

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption, who are a general practitioner or consultant physician or a non-specialist physically located at an Aboriginal Community Controlled Health Service listed in the 19(2) or 19(5) Direction for Aboriginal Community Controlled Health Services.

DPA

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption, who are not on the register of Approved Placements under Section 3GA of the Act, are either a non-specialist or general practitioner and are physically located within a service location within a Distribution Priority Area (DPA) when they render a professional service or a professional service is rendered on their behalf.

Note: this does not include temporary residents for assisting at operations. These have to be sent to Health for consideration.

DWS

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption, who are not on the register of Approved Placements under Section 3GA of the Act, are a specialist or consultant physician in a specialty listed under Distribution Priority Area (DPA) & District of Workforce Shortage (DWS) class exemption and are physically located within a service location within a District of Workforce Shortage (DWS) when they render a professional service or a professional service is rendered on their behalf. Note: this does not include temporary residents for assisting at operations.

AGPT

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption undertaking an approved Australian General Practice Training Program (AGPT) under the general, rural or extended skills pathway.

AMDS

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption undertaking an approved placement on the Approved Medical Deputising Service (AMDS).

RLRP

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption undertaking an approved placement on the Rural Locum Relief Program (RLRP).

QCRDP

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption undertaking an approved placement on the Queensland Country Relieving Doctors Program (QCRDP).

SAPP

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption undertaking an approved placement on the Special Approved Placement Program (SAPP).

APED

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption undertaking an approved placement on the Approved Private Emergency Department Program (APED).

RVTS

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption undertaking an approved placement on the Remote Vocational Training Scheme (RVTS).

3GA DPA

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption, who are named on the Register of Approved Placements under Section 3GA of the Act are a non-specialist working towards specialty listed under 3GA Distribution Priority Area (DPA) & District of Workforce Shortage (DWS) class exemption and are at a service location within a DPA when they render a professional service or a professional service is rendered on their behalf.

Note: this does not include where the specialty is for general practice and the practice location is not in a DPA. These have to be sent to Health for consideration.

3GA DWS

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption, who are named on the Register of Approved Placements under Section 3GA of the Act are a non-specialist working towards specialty listed under 3GA Distribution Priority Area (DPA) & District of Workforce Shortage (DWS) class exemption and are at a service location within a DWS when they render a professional service or a professional service is rendered on their behalf.

Note: this does not include where the medical practitioner is a specialist trainee and the practice location is not in a DWS. These have to be sent to Health for consideration.

PR assist

non-location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption to assist at operations when they become permanent residents.

Specialist A/H

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption rendering professional services in the after-hours period and who are recognised for Medicare purposes as a general practitioner (specific recognition), or specialist or consultant physician in a specified specialty). See After-hours specialist class exemption for specifics.

Temp resident A/H

location specific

For medical practitioners who are covered by the class exemption rendering professional services in the after-hours period, who were first registered on or after 18 October 2001, are a temporary resident only and are not a general practitioner, specialist or consultant physician.

Academic

location specific

For medical practitioners covered by the s19AB class exemption for academics who are appointed to an academic position at one of the medical schools listed in Academic class exemption, rendering professional services to teach medical students at the location.

WHO

non-location specific

For medical practitioners covered by the class exemption who were Australian citizens or Australian permanent residents on 1 January 1997; and had commenced study outside Australia prior to 1 January 1997 at a medical school listed in the World Health Organisation's (WHO's) World Directory of Medical Schools (6th edition), including updates contained in the WHO's World Forum (up to and including Volume 17 Number 3, 1996).

5 Yr All

non-location specific

For medical practitioners on the 5 year Overseas Trained Doctors (OTD) program who have completed their initial 5 year obligations and Health has approved a non-location specific s19AB exemption for the remaining period of their s19AB restrictions.

Note: specialty code 515 is used at stem to flag medical practitioners on the 5 year OTD program - specialty code 515 should not be end dated.

Scaling

non-location specific

Automatically applied when a medical practitioner meets their obligation due to scaling.

5 Year (ind)

location specific

For medical practitioners on the 5 year OTD program who have a location specific exemption during the first 5 years of the program, Health to advise Medicare Australia of medical practitioners on the 5 Year OTD programs.

Note: specialty code 515 is used at stem to flag medical practitioners on the 5 year OTD program - specialty code 515 should not be end dated.
