Identifying if Away from Base travel is available

Step

Action

1

Request received for Fares Allowance (FAA) to attend Away from Base activity + Read more ...

Note: an Australian Apprentice is not entitled to FAA for the Australian Apprenticeship.

Student or education provider lodges request for FAA for student to attend an Away from Base activity.

Other assistance for the Away from Base activity may also be approved. For details see: Approval of ABSTUDY Away from Base assistance.

Is the student eligible for Away from Base assistance?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 9

2

Away from Base activity approved + Read more ...

See Approval of ABSTUDY Away from Base activities for details.

Is the Away from Base activity approved?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 9

3

Is the student being paid under the Masters and Doctorate award? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Masters and Doctorate Award Activity + Read more ...

Students can receive a maximum amount per year (including fares and other costs related to travel).

  • access the Travel Management Application (TMA) within Process Direct
  • select the bookings icon to view previously processed fares payments and other costs (for example, meals and accommodation)

Has the maximum limit already been reached?

  • Yes, further FAA is not payable. Go to Step 9
  • No, FAA (up to the maximum) for the journey is payable. Go to Step 10

5

Testing and Assessment programme + Read more ...

Is the student undertaking a Testing and Assessment programme?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Students can receive a maximum of 2 return journeys per year + Read more ...

Note: if a number of students from the same community are required to undertake the interviews or selection procedures, it may be more cost-effective to have a representative from the education provider travel to the community to conduct the interviews. See Non-student travel for further details.

  • access the TMA within Process Direct
  • select the bookings icon to view details of previously undertaken journeys

Has the student already undertaken 2 return journeys to attend a Testing and Assessment programme?

  • Yes, further FAA is not payable. Go to Step 9
  • No, FAA for the journey is payable. Go to Step 10

7

Maximum limits for FAA to travel to an Away from Base activity (apart from Masters and Doctorate or Testing and Assessment students) + Read more ...

Access the TMA within Process Direct and select the bookings icon to view details of previously undertaken journeys.

See Resources for an example of Limits of Assistance for Away from Base.

Has the student exceeded their Limits of Away from Base assistance for allowable journeys for FAA been reached?

  • Yes, further FAA is not payable. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Interstate travel to participate in the Away from Base activity + Read more ...

Is the student wishing to travel interstate to participate in the Away from Base activity?

Certain conditions apply for approval to travel interstate.

  • Yes, refer to Interstate travel for details. Procedure ends here
  • No, FAA is payable. Go to Step 10

9

FAA to travel to the Away from Base activity is not payable + Read more ...

Advise the student they are not eligible for ABSTUDY Away from Base travel.

If the travel request has been received by the:

  • ABSTUDY Smart Centre telephony, record details of discussion on a DOC. Explain their review and appeal rights. See Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process
  • ABSTUDY Travel team, finalise the travel request in the TMA with Booking status of 'Rejected' and record details of the decision in the Notes tab. See Coding pre-booked Fares Allowance (FAA) entitlements for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)

Procedure ends here.

10

FAA to travel to the Away from Base activity is payable + Read more ...

Determine if the FAA:

  • is to be authorised and booked in advance of travel
  • will be reimbursed after the travel has been undertaken

Students claiming reimbursement will be required to complete an ABSTUDY Fares Allowance Claim (SY032). The Resources page contains a link to the SY032.
