Customer has a payment nominee + Read more ...

The payment nominee must not be an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes.

Is the nominee an excluded payment nominee?

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for VIM, see Step 2 in the Finalising decision for Voluntary Income Management using workflow table
    • Note: unless the nominee arrangement has been made by court appointment, the individual may choose to cancel these arrangements in order to enter into a Voluntary Income Management Agreement. For more information, see Cancelling a nominee arrangement
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Customer has a payment nominee who is not an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes + Read more ...

Where Services Australia determines that the nominee arrangements are not working in the best interests of the individual and their children, care will need to be taken in cancelling the nominee arrangements and moving the individual onto Income Management.

Prior to the cancelation of any nominee arrangement, the agency should weigh up the consequences of making a direct payment to the customer of a percentage of their payments. A Services Australia social worker may need to assist in determining the best approach when considering cancelling some nominee arrangements.

7

Decision making + Read more ...

Before entering into a Voluntary Income Management Agreement with a customer, the agency must consider the extent to which it would be feasible to meet the priority needs of the individual. The agency must also have regard to any other matters (if any) considered relevant. This discretion allows the agency to refuse to enter into a Voluntary Income Management Agreement if, amongst other things, there is:

  • insufficient availability of approved merchants within the customer's geographic location, that is, the agency's ability to disburse income managed funds
  • insufficient access to purchasing mechanisms such as vouchers
  • an insufficient rate of payment being received by the customer, that is, may not be feasible to income manage small amounts
  • a likelihood of the customer being ineligible for Voluntary Income Management within the next 28 days
  • a likelihood of the customer becoming subject to a compulsory measure of Income Management measure within the next 28 days

Is it feasible to apply Income Management in relation to the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, see Step 2 in the Finalising decision for Voluntary Income Management using workflow table

8

Has the customer previously had a Voluntary Income Management Agreement? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, see Step 1 in the Finalising decision for Voluntary Income Management using workflow table

9

Previous Voluntary Income Management Agreement + Read more ...

A new Voluntary Income Management Agreement cannot be made if any of the following applies:

  • a Voluntary Income Management Agreement has been terminated by the customer within the last 21 days. The agency must not enter into another agreement with the customer until 21 days has elapsed from the date of termination. See the Resources page
  • an agreement has been cancelled on four or more occasions in the 12 month period preceding the date on which the new agreement is intended to commence

Does this apply to the customer?

  • Yes, see Step 2 in the Finalising decision for Voluntary Income Management using workflow table
  • No, see Step 1 in the Finalising decision for Voluntary Income Management using workflow table

Customer is not eligible for Income Management + Read more ...

Navigate to the Voluntary IM Assessment workflow under the Income Management menu and code the following:

  • Status: field - Ineligible
  • Select all applicable ineligible reasons. Note: more than one reason can be selected
  • Finalise the activity
  • Advise the customer of their right to request reviews of Income Management decisions

An auto DOC will be created. Annotate with any additional relevant information.

A system generated letter will be issued to the customer.

Note: customers who are exempt from Income Management under a higher measure may be eligible for Voluntary Income Management.

If the customer accepts Voluntary Income Management, and during a period of the Voluntary Income Management Agreement the customer's:

  • exemption from Disengaged Youth (DEY) or Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR) ends, (for example, they cease to be a full-time student), or
  • exclusion from Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) ends

They will revert to the relevant compulsory measure. See, Overview and hierarchy of Income Management measures.
