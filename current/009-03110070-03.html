A change in circumstances which may result in cancellation of CA + Read more ...

A person (care receiver, nominee or third party) advises Services Australia of a change in circumstances which may result in cancellation of CA.

During the conversation, the Service Officer must be mindful of permission to enquire rules and not to release information if the person has no permission to enquire. For more information see Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) authority (Centrelink). Where the Service Officer is satisfied there is enough information and/or evidence that would result in a cancellation of the carer's Carer Payment (CP), code the change in circumstances correctly. Note: if a customer, nominee or third party advises someone other than themselves is affected by FDV, staff can provide them with referral options for the person affected by FDV, from the Family and domestic violence – Support services intranet page or Payment and Service Finder.

2

Carer Processing staff (CD1) to review record + Read more ...

Upon allocation of the ACTDOC, Carer Processing staff are to undertake a thorough review of the care situation between the carer and care receiver. This may include, but is not limited to:

  • checking both the carer and care receiver records for any recent updates to their circumstances (for example: change of address, notes regarding contact)
  • attempting contact with the Carer
  • attempting contact with the Care Receiver or Nominee
  • issuing relevant review documents - including the Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance - non-medical review (SC236) form. See a link to the form on the Resources page

Note: If it is reasonable for the carer to have supporting evidence, ask them to provide this evidence. While care schedules are not to be requested, the carer may be able to provide other supporting documentation, for example, a letter from a hospital stating they attend to the care receiver on a regular basis. Requests for information should be made via the Request for Information (RFI) workflow where possible. Any forms issued manually will require a review to be set up via the RVR screen.

3

Cancellations of CA can be automatic or manual + Read more ...

For cancellations that are:

  • automatic, the carer's (or care receiver's) circumstances have changed and as a result of that change the carer is no longer qualified for payment.
  • manual, a Service Officer makes a decision to cancel a carer's payment and runs the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow to cancel CA. For example, the carer's record has been suspended for 3 months and now needs to be cancelled:
  • a manual cancellation may cancel all payments of CA to the carer for all care receivers
  • if cancellation is due to a carer completing the online task within 14 days following a change of address, manual cancellation must occur using Process Direct. See Step 3 in Table 2

Key all carer circumstances to allow the payment to auto cancel before considering a manual cancellation using the workflow. An auto cancellation will ensure the correct letter is sent to the carer. It will also ensure Services Australia has the most up-to-date record of the carer and carer receiver's circumstances. This may be important if their CA payment is subsequently re-granted.

The most common reasons for an auto-cancellation include:

  • permanent cessation of care:
    • see Care receiver permanently enters or temporarily/permanently leaves an institution
    • care receiver leaves care of the carer. See Step 8 in Table 2
    • carer ceases to provide care for care receiver(s). See Step 8 in Table 2
  • temporary cessation of care, in most cases, payment will only cancel once the 63 days of respite care have been used up. If the carer is in gaol or psychiatric confinement for:
    • more than 13 weeks because they have been charged with an offence, respite days cannot be used, and the payment must be cancelled reason IMP
    • 13 weeks or less, the payment is suspended reason IMP. If the carer is still confined after 13 weeks, the suspended payment will auto-cancel from the date of confinement. See:
    • Carer is absent from the caring situation
    • Care receiver is absent from the caring situation
    • Continuation of respite after the expiry of hospitalisation
    • Overseas absences
  • death of a carer
  • death of a child care receiver and the effect on CA
  • death of adult care receiver and the effect on CA
  • CA income review processing. Cancellation can occur due to 'Failed to reply to correspondence', excess income and estimate not accepted

For:

  • other automatic cancellations, go to Step 4
  • manual cancellations, go to Step 5

4

Automatic cancellation + Read more ...

Key the carer and/or care receiver's change in circumstances:

  • if the carer is not eligible to continue to receive CA, they will be auto-cancelled as a result of the system action when the activity is finalised
  • when a carer's CP (child) payment is cancelled, the associated CA (auto) payment will also cancel. See Eligibility for CA (auto) when a carer is claiming or receiving CP (child)
  • check if the carer is still eligible for CA (child). For example, if the CP (child) is cancelled due to income but the carer is still caring, or the carer is working more than 25 hours permanently and is still providing daily care to the child care receiver
  • if the carer wishes to test their eligibility for CA once their CP (child) and CA (auto) has cancelled, the carer will need to re-claim CA. Tell the carer that if they apply for CA within 28 days of cancellation, payment may be backdated to the date CP was cancelled

Procedure ends here.

5

Manual cancellation for CA using Carer cancellation and suspension workflow + Read more ...

Run the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow. For more information, see Carer cancellation and suspension workflow for CP and CA.

If the workflow is not available, see Step 3 in Table 2

6

Finalise activity + Read more ...

Whenever a decision is made to cancel CA, consider whether to cancel Carer Payment (CP). If the carer was also receiving CP, check continuing eligibility for CP. If necessary, cancel the CP:

  • finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • record details on a DOC in the carer's record
  • if the care receiver is a child and the carer is receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB), check whether FTB is still payable. See Child leaves customer's care/custody
  • if the carer and care receiver are partners, they may be entitled to be assessed as being separated from their partner due to ill health
  • after cancelling the carer's payment/s, check the care receiver's record and change their address if required. Issue an Accommodation Details Module (MOD AC) if the carer is unsure of the care receiver's new address. Update the Accommodation (AC) screen and Telephone Details (TD) screen

7

Check if a manual advice required + Read more ...

A manual letter will be required if a Manual Follow Up (MFU) activity has been created on the Activity List (AL) screen after the cancellation has been finalised.

If there is a MFU requiring a manual letter to be sent to carer, go to Step 8.

If there is no MFU, the system auto-sends a cancellation letter to the carer. Procedure ends here.

8

Send letter + Read more ...

To send the letter:

  • 'S'elect the MFU (ADV (Advice)) activity on the AL screen and complete either a Confirmation of rejection, cancellation, suspension or deferment of the carer's pension, allowance or benefit (Q134) or a free text letter (that is a Request for Information (RFI) letter)

To issue the Q134:

  • key 'LD' in the Nxt field and press [Enter]
  • key 'Q134' in the Letter Code field

Complete relevant details, giving the reason(s) for cancellation, and advising their review and appeal rights.

3

Manual cancellation for CA using Process Direct + Read more ...

An 'Update your caring details' online task generates when a carer has:

  • changed their address using their Centrelink online account or via the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app, and
  • advised they are no longer residing with the care receiver aged 16 years or over (adult)

The task will auto-display on the carers’ Centrelink online account or Express Plus Centrelink mobile app after completing their change of address online. It replaces the need to issue the carer with the Carer Allowance Questionnaire (SA381).

If the carer has not completed the ‘Update your caring details’ task within 14 days, a work item is allocated to staff via Process Direct to manually cancel CA 'failed to reply to correspondence' (FRC).

Locate Work Item in Process Direct.

Select the work item Caring Detail Review for Carer Allowance. The Customer and Claim information details screen will display.

Select then select Update DOR to display the Update Date of Receipt screen:

  • change the Receipt Date to:
    • Day 15, if this is within the current entitlement period
    • Date paid to +1, if customer has been paid since day 15
  • select Update > Process
  • from the Task Selector screen, select CJBA – Carers Benefit Action and select Next to display the Care Receiver Benefit Action screen
  • select the to display Create Care Receiver Benefit Action popup and update:
    • Care Receiver Name: If there is more than 1 care receiver, select the correct care receiver’s name
    • Action: CAN – CANCEL
    • Reason: FRC – FAILED TO SUPPLY REQUESTED INFORMATION
    • Date of Effect: Receipt Date
  • select Save
  • a provisional cancellation activity will appear on CJBA screen. Select Next
  • select Assess
  • the carer’s entitlement to CA is displayed on the ELD screen. Check the expected outcome has occurred. Select Finish
  • a pop up window will display with prepopulated text, including the Social Security Act reference. Add additional comments regarding the decision in Notes. The comments will appear in Notes tab
  • select Finish. The work item Carer Allowance Questionnaire Review status will change to completed

Note: if CA has been cancelled failed to reply to correspondence (FRC) due to non-completion of their Update your caring details online task, the carer can still complete the update within 13 weeks of the cancellation. See Change of address for CP and CA.

Procedure ends here.

4

Manual cancellation for CA using Customer First + Read more ...

In Customer First code via:

  • Care Receiver Benefit Action (CJBA) screens for cancellations relating to the care receiver, or
  • Benefit Action (BA) screen for cancellations relating to the carer

Codes which are specific to CA are used on the CJBA screen, while those of a more general nature are used on the BA screen. Some codes can be used on both screens.

For a list of codes for cancellation and whether they are used via the BA screen or the CJBA screen, and which screen the code can be used, see Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Carer Allowance (CA).

Note: carers should not be cancelled reason 'OTH'er unless there are system problems preventing correct cancellations or there is no other cancellation reason appropriate. For all cancellations reason OTH a manual letter must be sent to the carer advising the cancellation reason and their review and appeal rights.

5

Cancel via BA or CJBA screen + Read more ...

If the cancellation is for the carer, use the BA screen:

  • 'CDA' (Carer Allowance) in the Svc Rsn field
  • 'CAN' in the Action field
  • the cancellation reason in the Reason field. To obtain the relevant code, use field help ('?') or see Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for CA
  • Key the Effect Date. This is the date the payment will be cancelled from. Service Officers need to consider and apply the General 14 day notification provisions for customers
  • footer details. Key:
    • the source of the cancellation in the Source field
    • the date of receipt in the DOR field
    • 'I' to insert

See Step 2 in Table 3.

If the cancellation is for the care receiver, go to the Care Receiver Summary (CRS) screen and 'S'elect the correct care receiver for cancellation:

  • go to the CJBA screen
  • in the Action field, key 'CAN' as the 3 letter code for cancellation
  • in the Reason field, key the appropriate code. To obtain the relevant code either:
    • key '?' in the field to find the relevant code, or
    • see Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for CA
    • key the date in the Assessment Date field
    • 'I' to insert

Note: the assessment date cannot be after the Date of Receipt of the activity, as per warning edit shown on the CJBA screen.

See Step 1 in Table 3.

 

6

Carer claiming CP also receives CA for the same adult care receiver + Read more ...

If a new medical report for a CP claim was requested and results in the auto cancellation of CA for the current carer, or auto cancellation for another carer on CA, cancel the payment from date of determination.

In a separate activity, outside of the CP claim, cancel CA first:

  • Go to the (CRS) screen and 'S'elect the correct care receiver for cancellation
  • Code a new ADAT from date of determination in Customer Record (CR), this will cancel CA.
  • DOC the record advising of the reason for the cancellation
  • Delete the ADAT coding in CR.
  • Process the CP claim using PD. This will create the same ADAT line in the care receiver using the data stored in PD. The DOE will be the date of claim. This will not create a debt shell on the former current carer.

If there is more than 1 care receiver affected by the particular event, code details on the care receiver affected by the change.

7

Carer claiming CP and another carer received CA for the same adult care receiver + Read more ...

If a new medical report for a CP claim was requested and results in the auto- cancellation of CA for the current carer, or auto-cancellation for another carer on CA, cancel CA for the other carer from the date of determination.

If there is more than 1 care receiver affected by the particular event, code details on the care receiver affected by the change.

If the care receiver is a child, and

  • another carer receives CA for the child care receiver with a recognised disability, CA (child) remains payable, or
  • the information to determine the carer is still eligible for CA:
    • is sufficient, cancel CA for the other carer from the date of determination
    • not sufficient, procedure ends here

8

Care receiver leaves care of the carer, or carer stops providing care + Read more ...

Cancel the payment from date of event, from the first date the carer did not provide care and attention. In most cases, this will be the day after the person ceased to provide care to the care receiver. For example, the carer may have provided care to the care receiver in the morning before they left care, in which case the CA should be cancelled from the day after.

If there is more than 1 care receiver affected by the particular event, key details on the care receiver affected by the change.

Is the care receiver a child?

  • Yes, child care receiver only has a child record and does not have their own ‘Adult’ Centrelink record. If the cessation of care results in the cancellation of the carer's payment, provisional cancellation codes will appear on the CJAR screen. The code is 'Not in care' (LCC) for child care receivers. If the carer receives CA because of the care they provide for more than 1 care receiver who each qualify the carer in their own right, CA remains current:
    • in Customer First in the carer's record, go to the CRS screen and 'S'elect the appropriate care receiver to display the Care Receiver Task Selector (CETS)
    • go to the Care Details (CDCR) screen
    • key the Effect Date. This is the date the payment will be cancelled from. Service officers need to consider and apply the General 14 day notification provisions for customers
    • select the carer's CRN from the drop down list
    • select the Relationship to Care Receiver from the drop down list
    • key '0' in either the Care Days/Fortnight or Care % fields
    • key Source and Date of Receipt
    • press Add
    • key AR in the Next field and press [Enter] or continue
    • finalise the activity at the Assessment Results (AR) screen
    • key DDR in the Next field and press [Enter]
    • record details on a DOC in the carer's record (include name of care receiver)
  • No, go to Step 9

Note: if the child has their own Centrelink record go to Step 9, it may take some time for the system to reflect the Carer Allowance cancellation on the carer’s record. Exit the carer’s record and allow any ripple activities to complete

9

Did the carer live separately to the care receiver while care was being provided? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No:
    • in the carer's record, go to the Link Summary (LS) screen
    • key 'L' against the CDA link for the appropriate care receiver. The system will swap to the care receiver's record
    • go to the care receiver's Care Receiver Summary (CRS) screen and select 'SELF' and continue
    • in the Care Receiver Task Selector (CETS) screen, select 'Care Details' and continue to display the Care Details (CDCR) screen
    • key the Effect Date. This is the date the payment will be cancelled from. Service officers need to consider and apply the General 14 day notification provisions for customers
    • select the carer's CRN from the drop down list
    • key the Relationship to Care Rcvr field with the appropriate code
    • select the Relationship to Carer Receiver from the drop down list
    • select the 'No' radio button for the question: Does the Carer Live Separately?
    • key '0' in either the Care Days/Fortnight: or Care % fields
    • key Source: and Date of Receipt
    • press Add
    • key AR in the Next field and press [Enter] or continue
    • finalise the activity at the Assessment Results (AR) screen
    • a message will display stating that updates were applied successfully
    • key DDR in the Next field and press [Enter]
    • record details on a DOC in the carer's record and allow any ripple activities to complete.

Note: it may take some time for the system to reflect the Carer Allowance cancellation on the carer's record. Exit the carer's record and allow any ripple activities to complete.

See Step 1 in Table 3.

10

Carer living separately to care receiver while care was being provided + Read more ...

In the carer's record, go to the Link Summary (LS) screen and key 'L' against the CDA link for the appropriate care receiver. The system will swap to the care receiver's record:

  • go to the care receiver's Care Receiver Summary (CRS) screen and select 'SELF' and continue
  • the Care Receiver Task Selector (CETS) screen will display. Select 'Care Details' and continue to display the Care Details (CDCR) screen
  • key the Effect Date. This is the date the payment will be cancelled from. Service officers need to consider and apply the General 14 day notification provisions for customers
  • select the carer's CRN from the drop down list
  • select the Relationship to Care Receiver from the drop down list
  • select Yes to the question: Does the Carer Live Separately?
  • update the Living Separately section
  • Is the carer paid Relevant Minimum Wage? Key 'Y' or 'N' depending on the carer’s circumstances
  • select the No radio button at the question: Provides Daily Care?
  • key:
    • Hours of Care per Week as verified by Service Officer: '0'
    • Hours of Care per Week as advised by Customer: '0'
    • the Shared Care and Shared Care % fields if required. See Changes to shared care for Carer Allowance (CA)
    • Source and Date of Receipt
  • press Add
  • key AR in the Next field and press [Enter] or continue

See Step 1 in Table 3.
