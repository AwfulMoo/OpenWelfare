This page contains information about requesting an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt).

Requesting an ESAt

This table describes how to request a referral for an ESAt.

Step

Action

1

Determine whether a request for an ESAt is required + Read more ...

Request an ESAt where:

  • the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) flags that an ESAt may be required. See Actioning a pending Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) initiated Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) activity before requesting an ESAt
  • a job seeker has medical evidence and requires an assessment of their work capacity. Job seekers do not need an ESAt referral when lodging a medical certificate for temporary conditions. Consideration should be given to granting an exemption from mutual obligation requirements. See Assessment of a medical certificate to determine exemption from mutual obligation requirements for a temporary incapacity
  • an assessment for Youth Disability Supplement (YDS) is required
  • a job seeker volunteers for employment services, for example, a Disability Support Pension (DSP) customer wishes to volunteer for employment services (use referral code DSPV) or a job seeker is not claiming or receiving an income support payment and is seeking access to employment services
  • a DSP customer under 35 years old has participation requirements. DSP customers who after a Disability Medical Assessment (DMA) need an ESAt to confirm their work capacity (use referral code DSPU)
  • a DSP claimant has had their claim rejected and the Medical Assessment Team (MAT) recommend a review of work capacity and/or employment services referrals. This could be if:
    • the customer is assessed as manifestly ineligible for DSP by the MAT and the Assessor has recommended the customer requires an ESAt to review work capacity and/or employment services referrals, or
    • the customer was not in receipt of or claiming income support payment at the time of the MAT assessment and although MAT have not indicated an ESAt is required, a check of the customer's record indicates an ESAt request should be made, or
    • a DMA report has been submitted indicating medical ineligibility requiring a reassessment of work capacity and/or employment services referrals
  • a customer is temporarily not able to work or study and does not have a job or study to return to
  • exceptional circumstances exist

If a Service Officer determines that a customer is vulnerable and would benefit from an ESAt, contact the local Assessment Services team to make suitable arrangements for the customer. A 'vulnerable' customer may be someone who:

  • has a suspected mental health condition, and
  • is unable to be effectively assessed through usual procedures because they are disengaged from the health system, or
  • is unable to obtain suitable medical evidence

2

Before requesting an ESAt + Read more ...

Consider the following:

  • Ensure the job seeker has an active Job Seeker Identification Number (JSID). To request an ESAt referral, the customer must have a JSID and an active job seeker registration status
  • Ensure a Disability Support Pension (DSP) new claim/review of decision is not in progress. An ESAt should not be requested until the new claim/review of decision process has been finalised
  • Ensure there are no active ESAt referral activities (JSR/UPD, RFL/UPD or ESAt appointment required DOC) on the customer record
  • Ensure there are no unfinalised ESAt or JCA reports on the customer record. See Checking and actioning Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) reports
  • Ensure all relevant documentation, including historical Medical Information File Envelope (MIFE) information, has been scanned and attached to the customer's record before making the request
  • Confirm the customer's contact details are current
  • Ensure the customer's language requirements are correctly coded on the customer record

3

Making a request for an ESAt + Read more ...

If there are no open ESAt referral activities (JSR/UPD, RFL/UPD or ESAt appointment required DOC) on the customer record:

  • Use Fast Note
  • Enter 'ESAt' or 'ESAt referral' into the Search for Fast Note template box and select the Search button
  • Select template ESAt Appointment Required
  • Once the text has been confirmed update all the fields in the text to reflect circumstances, including any special needs information, deleting information that is not appropriate
    If there are exceptional circumstances or personal factors not recorded in the JSCI, include these details in the request
  • Leave the Fast Note open, it does not need to be transferred out or re-categorised as this will occur automatically

Assessment Services will make the ESAt booking.

4

Cancel or reschedule existing ESAT appointment + Read more ...

Service Officers cannot cancel or reschedule ESAt appointments.

Assessment Services are responsible for cancelling and rescheduling all ESAt appointments, for example, where the customer contacts to say that they will be unable to attend a future booked appointment, and wishes to arrange a new appointment time.

There may be situations where an ESAt request is made but is no longer required. In these cases, the customer will need to be transferred to Assessment Services.

Reasons for this may include the following:

  • the customer withdraws their claim
  • the customer has changed address and moved out of the area, so the assessment appointment needs to be rebooked in the new area
  • request made in error
  • the Service Officer determines that an ESAt initiated by the JSCI is no longer required
  • the job seeker is not receiving a payment with mutual obligation requirements and does not wish to proceed with an ESAt appointment. In these cases, the job seeker registration should be inactivated

If the ESAt is no longer required or an existing appointment requires rescheduling, contact Assessment Services with an explanation. After making the request, record details on a DOC.

Assessment Services will decide whether the ESAt request can be finalised as 'unable to complete'.
