Cancellation of payments and raising debts following cessation of study

Step

Action

1

Failure to notify + Read more ...

A student fails to notify Services Australia of ceasing study. They continue to receive payments to which they may not be entitled.

For further information, see Youth Allowance (YA) students fail to notify of ceasing full-time study.

Was the student advised of their obligation to notify the agency of this change of circumstance at any time since the grant?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Advise of notification obligations + Read more ...

Advise the student of their notification obligations:

  • Issue the customer with their notification obligations
  • Any negative adjustment arising from reassessment of ceasing study will not be recoverable. Finalised, no debt any negative adjustment
  • See:
    • Payment of ABSTUDY when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course
    • Payment of Youth Allowance (YA) and Austudy when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course
    • Deferring or discontinuing study before end of course for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme

Procedure ends here.

3

Cancelling the payment + Read more ...

Cancel the payment:

  • For YA, see Coding a cancellation
  • For ABSTUDY, see Cancellation of ABSTUDY payments
  • For Department of Social Services PES, see Cancellation of Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), ABSTUDY PES and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) PES payments
  • For AIC, see Cancellation of Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme payments

For Austudy and YA, determine whether a Participation Failure is to be applied.

Identify if the customer is eligible for income support and other products and services.

Did the debt result from a Centrelink Academic Reassessment Transformation (CART) review?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

CART reviews + Read more ...

For CART processing staff only.

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer and ask the date the study details changed.

Customer contact successful

If the date provided by the customer is:

  • equal to or earlier than that provided by the Educational Institution, accept this date
  • after the date provided by the Educational Institution, then this can only be used if verification is provided

Customer is unable to be contacted

If contact with the customer is unsuccessful, the date provided by the Educational Institution is accepted.

Educational Institution has provided a data match

If an educational institution has provided:

  • a date of effect in the match data, and
  • it is within 7 days of the load date, use this date as the date of event

Otherwise, the load date is to be used.

Staff are not to advise the customer of the load date or date of effect given by the educational institution.

See Processing Centrelink Academic Reassessment Transformation (CART) reviews. This provides an example of the date to use when raising a debt resulting from a CART review.

Did the update result in a study-related debt for the customer?

  • Yes, and the activity is being processed by the CART team or a debt raising team, go to Step 5
  • No, refer the debt activity to a debt raising team by:
    • adding the keyword DMB101 to the debt activity
    • transferring to region code XWA
    • documenting the reason for referral
    • procedure ends here

5

Raise the debt + Read more ...

  • Calculate the amount of the overpayment
  • Raise the debt by actioning an undetermined debt on the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS). Complete the Modify/Assess (OPMA) screen. See the Resources page for the codes:
    • Reason: field
    • Benefit Type Section of Act: field
    • To complete other fields, use field help ('?') for assistance
    • Press [Enter]
  • Send the Account Payable letter to the customer
  • Finalise the activity and note the Debt ID

6

Document record + Read more ...

Record details of the decision regarding a debt on a DOC. Include:

  • the Debt ID number in the summary line, and
  • all relevant Legislation and Policy Guideline references in the text

7

Determine if tax adjustment is required + Read more ...

Did the excess payment (negative adjustment) match the overpayment amount raised?

  • Yes, adjustment of the Payment Summary is not required
  • No, adjust the tax details
