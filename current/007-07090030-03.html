Restoration eligibility + Read more ...

If FTB cancelled before 1 July of the previous financial year, payment cannot be restored. The customer must complete a new claim. See the Resources page for an example.

Has the customer remained eligible for FTB since cancellation occurred in the current or previous financial year?

  • Yes, the customer has remained eligible for FTB since the date of cancellation, go to Step 2
  • No, the customer has not remained eligible for FTB since the date of cancellation, or FTB is CAN-INC and cannot be restored (see Restoration criteria for FTB CAN-INC table on the Resources page) they must lodge a claim, preferably online. See:
    • Claiming Family Tax Benefit (FTB) instalments for children in care other than a newborn or adopted child, or
    • Initial contact by customers claiming Family Tax Benefit (FTB) as a lump sum for a previous financial year only

2

FTB is cancelled and customer has requested restoration + Read more ...

Check FTB benefit status on the Assessment Explanation (AX), Benefit Status (XBS) and Document List (DL) screens.

For a list of cancellation codes that can be restored and protocols for who can code the restoration, see Cancellation, rejection and Current Zero Rate codes for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

If FTB cancelled in the current financial year due to:

  • CAN-OIS or CAN-ENR, go to Step 3
  • CAN-ENA, go to Step 4
  • income not provided (INP) or income over limit (INC), go to Step 5
  • whereabouts unknown (WUK), go to Step 6
  • child's income support rejected or granted from a date after FTB was cancelled, go to Step 9

  • overseas 6 weeks (6WK), see Step 4 in Table 2
  • overseas 56 weeks (O56), see Step 5 in Table 2
  • any other reason, see Step 1 in Table 2

FTB cancelled in the previous financial year

If FTB cancelled in the previous financial year due to non-income related reasons, CAN-OIS, CAN-ENR or CAN-ENA, see Step 8 in Table 2.

If FTB cancelled due to income (INC) in the previous financial year and/or customer is requesting to be assessed for a non-payment period in the previous financial year, see Step 7 in Table 2.

3

FTB cancelled in current financial year - Off Income Support (OIS) or Estimate Not Reasonable (ENR) + Read more ...

  • If Income Support has stopped and both ISP and FTB need to be restored, FTB should be restored first, particularly where the customer is eligible for Rent Assistance (RA)
  • If the customer is PPS/SUS-VMR or has a pending Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) new claim, when restoring FTB consider updating the customer's payment choice to avoid the possibility of overpayment
  • A different process applies to restore FTB in the current financial year if the customer has lodged a new claim

To restore FTB cancelled in the current financial year and a new claim has not been lodged

FTB CAN-OIS:

  • Use the Family Income and Choices workflow to restore and code a new estimate
  • The date of event (DOV) of the estimate is the date paid to plus 1 day (DPT + 1)
  • Restoration to occur from the date paid to plus 1 day (DPT + 1)
  • The workflow should automatically apply the correct DOV to the estimate
  • If automatic restoration has not occurred or the workflow is not available, code the restoration and estimate manually. Go to Step 4

FTB CAN-ENR:

  • The DOV of the estimate is the date paid to plus 1 (DPT+1)
  • Restoration to occur from the date paid to plus 1 day (DPT + 1)
  • Code the estimate and restoration manually. Go to Step 4

Payment choices:

  • cannot be updated within the Family Income and Choices workflow if the FTB status is CAN-OIS or CAN-ENR
  • must be updated manually on the Family Payment Choices (FPC) screen within the activity before completion

To restore FTB cancelled in the current financial year and a new claim has been lodged

If the customer has lodged:

  • an online claim, process the claim activity, ensuring the date of event of the income estimate is the date paid to plus one day (DPT+1) for CAN-OIS or CAN-ENR. See Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments. Procedure ends here
  • a combined paper claim for FTB and Parental Leave Pay (PPL) or a paper claim for FTB only, process the claim using Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), ensuring the date of event of the income estimate is the DPT+1 for CAN-OIS or CAN-ENR. See Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments. Procedure ends here.

4

Manual coding to restore + Read more ...

Go to the BA screen and code:

  • 'FTB' in the Svc Rsn: field
  • 'RES' in the Action: field
  • Complete the Source: and DOR: fields and press [Enter]
  • Event Date: field defaults to date paid to plus 1 day (DPT + 1) or 1 July of the current financial year. This date cannot be changed and an edit will appear if FTB cannot be restored from this date
  • Manually update the income estimate using the appropriate Date of event (DOV) for family assistance income estimates
    • ‘S’elect all families income estimate component screen(s) required from the FAO Income Task Selector (FITS) and press [Enter]
    • If an amount is currently recorded for a component of Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI), it continues to be assessed unless updated (code $0 if the customer advises $0)
    • If a screen does not exist for the current financial year, code ‘I’nsert in the Action: field and press [Enter]. Code the relevant financial year in the Financial Year: field
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and check the outcome is correct before finalising the activity
  • Run the Fast Note - FTB Restore > Income Estimate provided DOV: to record a DOC for the restoration activity

Procedure ends here

5

FTB cancelled in current financial year - INP or INC + Read more ...

New or revised income estimate required

The customer can provide an estimate verbally.

If the estimate is considered reasonable and the customer has remained eligible, FTB can be restored from date the new estimate is provided.

Note: if the customer has lodged a claim, this is to be treated as a request for restoration of payment. If the customer has lodged:

  • an online claim, process the claim activity, ensuring the date of event of the estimate is the date the estimate was provided
  • a combined paper claim for FTB and Parental Leave Pay (PPL), process the claim using ACC, ensuring the date of event of the FTB estimate is the date the estimate was provided
  • a paper claim for FTB only, cancel the claim activity on the AL screen and make any updates advised in the claim form within the BA restoration activity using the DOR as the date the claim was lodged and the DOV for the estimate is the date the estimate was provided

To restore FTB in the current financial year

Manually record the income estimate from the correct date of event:

  • ‘S’elect all FAO income estimate component screen(s) required from the FAO Income Task Selector (FITS) and press [Enter]
  • The estimate applies no earlier than the date the new estimate is provided, unless there has been another related change in circumstances. For more information see Date of event (DOV) for family assistance income estimates.
  • Amounts currently recorded for a component of Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) continues to be assessed, unless updated (code $0 if the customer advises $0)
  • If a screen does not exist for the current financial year, code ‘I’nsert in the Action: field and press [Enter]. Code the relevant financial year in the Financial Year: field

Go to the BA screen and code 'FTB' in Svc Rsn: field, 'RES' in Action: field, Source: and DOR: fields. Press [Enter].

Event Date field defaults to DPT +1 or 1 July of the current financial year. This date cannot be changed and an edit will appear if FTB cannot be restored from this date.

Discuss with the customer if there are any changes to the following:

  • child details (care arrangements, and education)
  • overseas absences (dates of travel, country)
  • income estimate for new financial year (April to June only)
  • FTB payment choice

Check if the customer is entitled to any arrears and that they are correct on the AR screen.

  • If restoring CAN-INP or CAN-FRC (after failing to reply to request for income) the estimate applies no earlier than the date the new estimate is provided, unless there has been another related change in circumstances. This could result in arrears not being paid for the whole period, however, the restoration period will be included in the reconciliation calculation
  • If restoring CAN-INC or CNP-INC, the estimate applies no earlier than the date the new estimate is provided, unless there has been another related change in circumstances. The customer's entitlement for the CAN-INC non-payment period can only be assessed after the end of the financial year when reconciliation occurs. The customer's FTB reconciliation letter will advise them:
    • they have not been assessed for the full financial year, and
    • to lodge a lump sum claim so their entitlement for that period can be checked. See Assessment of income cancellation periods in Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation
  • If the system is attempting to pay FTB arrears for the CAN-INC non-payment periods when being restored based on a previous Child Care Subsidy (CCS) estimate that is:
    • lower than the most recent estimate provided, code the FYSO screen for the CAN-INC non-payment period, using Status code CAN and Reason code OTH. This will stop incorrect FTB arrears from paying for this period. The arrears will be assessed in reconciliation when actual income is confirmed and the customer has lodged a lump sum claim or requested a reassessment within the relevant time frame
    • equal to or higher than the most recent estimate provided, pay the FTB arrears provided they are correct. For help in determining why arrears are paying see What to check when Family Tax Benefit (FTB) rate has changed

If the restoration result is correct, finalise the activity.

Use the Fast Note - Auto Text > Families > SUS/CAN/RES > FTB Restore to DOC the new claim or restoration activity.

Procedure ends here.

FTB CAN-WUK (whereabouts unknown) + Read more ...

New address details can be advised verbally, in person or by mail. Rent verification will be needed for RA to be paid for new address.

Have the address details been coded incorrectly (for example, wrong house number or incorrect spelling of street name)?

  • Yes:
    • Run Change in Contact Details workflow, or access the Address Summary (ADS) screen
    • Select the appropriate line, and select 'Continue'
    • Select 'No' as the response to the question 'Is RA being claimed on this Address?'

    • Complete Source: and DOR: fields
    • Select the Continue button
    • To ensure RA is reinstated, go to Accommodation History (ACS) screen and check if a Change of Address 'COA' line is recorded, delete so RA is payable according to the previous verified information. If no COA line, continue to restore
    • If rent not verified previously, advise customer rent verification with correct address must be provided for RA to be paid
    • Go to the BA screen
    • Code 'FTB' in Svc Rsn: field, 'RES' in Action: field, Source: and DOR: fields. Press [Enter]
    • The Event Date field defaults to DPT +1 or 1 July of the current financial year. This date cannot be changed and an edit will appear if FTB cannot be restored from this date
    • Check if the customer is entitled to any arrears and that they appear correctly on the AR screen
    • Use the Fast Note - Auto Text > Families > SUS/CAN/RES > FTB Restore to DOC the restoration activity
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

9

FTB CAN-DPP (dependent receiving precluding payment) + Read more ...

FTB may be restored if the child's income support payment claim is rejected or granted from a date after FTB was cancelled and there is a non-payment period.

Access the customer's DL screen and read the DOC regarding the cancellation.

Is there any other information that prevents customer's record being restored and actioned at first contact?

  • Yes:
    • Advise customer of action required and record details on a DOC
    • Make an 'enquiry' appointment, if appropriate, for customer to supply information
    • Advise customer that payment cannot be restored until necessary action taken. Procedure ends here
  • No, restore FTB if all required information is available and the customer has remained eligible since the date of cancellation
    Note: the child will have their own record
    • On the child's record, 's'elect the self line from the Child Selection (CHS) screen, and go to the Child General Circumstances (CHG) screen
    • If the income support payment is rejected, delete the DPP line or code CEA (ceased to receive other payments) from date FTB is restored
    • If the income support payment is granted from a date later than the date FTB was cancelled (for example, gap period from date FTB cancelled to date income support granted), delete the DPP line. FTB will auto cancel again from the income support start date
    • Update Child Deferment, Student/Income circumstances, and 16-20 educational details for the child if necessary
    • Complete activity
    • Code a current year FTB restoration activity on the customer's record, see Step 6 in Table 2
    • For a previous year FTB restoration, see Step 8 in Table 2

If the customer was cancelled due to the child claiming a precluding payment, FTB may remain current until the child's 18th birthday (if that is the date of grant) if:

  • the child is within 13 weeks of their 18th birthday and
  • the precluding payment status is YAL ASS

10

Restore FTB after ABSTUDY Incidentals Allowance cancellation + Read more ...

This process should avoid spanning financial years, if possible. If ABSTUDY Incidentals Allowance is granted near the end of the financial year, the ABSTUDY cancellation and the FTB restoration should be finalised before the end of June.

An ACTDOC with the keyword ACTFAO will allocate to appropriately trained staff. The FTB restoration is not to occur in the ABSTUDY Processing Service.

If FTB:

  • has cancelled (CAN-DPP) as a result of the ABSTUDY Incidentals Allowance grant, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • is current, to restore FTB for the particular child code a 'RCL' line from date FTB was cancelled +1 day for that child on the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen. Check if the customer is entitled to any arrears and that they appear correctly on the AR screen

11

Child has lodged a claim for Disability Support Pension + Read more ...

When a claim for DSP is received, FTB is cancelled for the child from the later of either the:

  • child’s 16th birthday, or
  • date of the DSP claim lodgement

See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for children aged 16-19 years for further information on cancellation.

If the parent has contacted to query the cancellation of FTB, FTB cannot be restored if the disqualifying payment claim is awaiting processing.

If the customer is in crisis or financial hardship, the DSP claim should be escalated for urgent processing, where:

  • an SA479 has not been completed on the DSP claimant’s record, or
  • the MAT recommendation (SA479) indicates manifest medically eligible

3

Supplying TFN + Read more ...

Was the customer given 28 days from the date of request to supply their partner's TFN and the customer has not provided their partner's TFN?

  • Yes, FTB cannot be restored until the TFN is provided. Advise the customer. Procedure ends here
  • No, in some cases the customer is not being issued a letter to advise the partner needs to supply a TFN. If information on the record suggests the customer was not given 28 days to provide the TFN, the cancellation letter can be considered a request to supply the TFN
    If 28 days has not been given to supply the TFN, the following interim process applies:
    • Restore FTB
    • Code an exemption for the partner's TFN; go to the Tax File Number Authority Summary screen and select 'FTB' service reason. Code 'PUC' (Partner uncontactable) as the exemption
    • Check the AR screen to ensure FTB is not cancelling with reason 'NTP'
    • Check if any arrears should be paid and that they appear correctly on the AR screen before finalising the activity
    • Give the partner 28 days from the date that the cancellation letter was sent to provide their partner's TFN. Code a manual review (RVR) to follow up if a TFN has been supplied 28 days from the date of the letter being sent. This review must be coded as a 'PUC' exemption, which will automatically provide a 90 day review period. Where the review matures and the customer has not responded, cancel FTB with reason 'FRC' (Failing to reply to correspondence) from DPT + 1
    • Record details on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

Did the customer or child/ren return to Australia within 13 weeks of the cancellation date?

  • Yes, FTB payments should be automatically restored from their date of return. No, the customer must reclaim FTB. Procedure ends here
Note: if the customer wants to dispute the information recorded on RSIM/CHRSIM or a Service Officer feels the data is incomplete or incorrect, see Referring inconsistencies in overseas departure and arrival information to Centrelink International Services (CIS)

FTB cancelled overseas more than 56 weeks (O56) + Read more ...

Note: if the customer wants to dispute the information recorded on RSIM/CHRSIM or a Service Officer feels the data is incomplete or incorrect, see Referring inconsistencies in overseas departure or arrival information to Centrelink International Services (CIS).

Did the customer and/or child/ren return to Australia on or before the cancellation date?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, the customer must reclaim FTB. Procedure ends here

6

Current financial year restoration + Read more ...

Restore FTB if the customer has remained eligible since the date of cancellation.

Note: review the customers FTB payment status for the full financial year and ensure all appropriate cancellations are restored. There may be instances where more than one FTB restoration activity is required. See the Resources page for examples related to overseas travel.

On the customer's record:

  • on the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen code 'RCL' line from date FTB was last paid to +1 after selecting the child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen (only where cancellation is due to child circumstances, for example, Youth Allowance rejected)
  • record any changes that have occurred since the date of restoration and check that the customer's current income estimate is reasonable
    • if recording an estimate for a new partner, the partner estimate is to be recorded from date partnered
    • update estimate using correct Date of Event (DOV)
  • in the same activity, restore FTB via the BA screen if necessary by coding:
    • 'FTB' in the Svc Rsn: field
    • 'RES' in the Action: field
    • the system will default the restoration date
  • if the customer is under income management (INM benefit status) and they have an arrears or lump sum payment entitlement, do not make any changes to the customer's record to allow this payment to be made. Contact the Income Management Team to allow income management to be turned on for the arrears payment before it is made
  • if the customer is income managed under the Voluntary Income Management measure, and the agreement was in place for more than 13 weeks, the Voluntary Income Management Continuation Assessment page is presented. See Effect on Income Management when payment is restored or re-granted

Before finalising activity on the AR screen, ensure the result is correct, including any arrears.

Note: FTB instalment arrears, reconciliation top-ups or lump sum payments may be used to recover debts.

Use the Fast Note - Auto Text > Families > SUS/CAN/RES > FTB Restore to DOC restoration activity.

Procedure ends here.

7

Reassessment of FTB cancelled income (INC) within the previous financial year + Read more ...

If CAN-INC or CAN-NIE (if the status was CAN-INC in the previous financial year and actual income details have since been received, the status will change to CAN-NIE. The Resources page contains an example), the customer's entitlement for the non-payment periods can only be assessed after the end of the financial year when reconciliation occurs, once actual income details are available.

FTB reconciliation letters will advise the customer if FTB has not been assessed for the full financial year to lodge a lump sum claim so entitlement for that period can be checked.

If a customer contacts requesting their non-payment period be assessed, and

  • their FTB was cancelled due to an uplifted estimate or change in circumstances, request they lodge a lump sum claim online
  • their FTB was cancelled due to a customer provided estimate (before 1 July 2020) and they are currently receiving FTB and requesting assessment of the income cancellation period within the previous financial year only, see Assessment of income cancellation periods in Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation
  • FTB is cancelled due to income in the previous financial year and is still cancelled in the current financial year, go to Step 9

Note: if the customer has remained eligible and the CAN-INC resulted from a customer provided estimate (before 1 July 2020), a lump sum claim for the CAN-INC or CAN-NIE non-payment period is not required. However, if a lump sum claim is received this will be considered a request for a review of the CAN-INC or CAN-NIE non-payment period.

If there is no entitlement for the previous financial year based on actual income, a new claim must be lodged to start receiving FTB instalments in the current financial year.

8

Restoration of FTB cancelled within the previous financial year + Read more ...

For a list of cancellation codes that can be restored and protocols for who can code the restoration, see Cancellation, rejection and Current Zero Rate codes for Family Tax Benefit (FTB). If the customer has not remained eligible for FTB since the date of cancellation, they must lodge a new claim.

If the customer has remained eligible, they do not need to lodge a claim. If a claim has been lodged, treat this as a request for restoration of payment.

To restore payment, a new claim activity must be coded. Go to Step 1 in Table 3.

9

FTB CAN-INC within the previous financial year and is still cancelled + Read more ...

To assess the CAN-INC non-payment period in the previous financial year and to restore fortnightly FTB entitlement in the current financial year, Service Officers must code a new claim activity providing:

  • the customer has remained eligible for FTB since cancellation
  • there has not been a change in circumstances, and
  • the cancellation scenario is eligible for restoration. See Restoration criteria for FTB CAN-INC table on the Resources page

Is the customer eligible for restoration?

  • Yes, a new claim activity must be processed, go to Step 1 in Table 3.
  • No, if the customer has not already, advise them to lodge a combined instalment claim and lump sum claim for FTB

FTB status CAN-OIS + Read more ...

Ensure the new income estimate is coded using a DOV = 1 July of the current financial year (earliest date).

FTB will restore from 1 July of the current financial year.

If CAN-OIS occurred within the final 6 weeks of the previous financial year (on or after 20 May), the period from the date of cancellation to 30 June of the previous financial year will be automatically assessed as an instalment period, and:

  • arrears are paid immediately if reconciliation is pending and FTB is not subject to an income test for any duration of the period. For example, the customer is single and ISP current above a nil rate for the entire period in the previous financial year, or
  • arrears are paid at reconciliation if FTB is subject to an income test for any duration of the period. For example. the customer has had a gap in their ISP, including ISP paid at a nil rate

The FTB Income Support Payment Explanation (FTBISPX) screen provides information relating to:

  • if a customer was on income support for the purposes of FTB, and
  • if they were exempt from the income test during the period of restoration

If CAN-OIS occurred prior to the final 6 weeks in the previous financial year (before 20 May), the period from date of cancellation to 30 June of the previous financial year will be automatically assessed as a lump sum claim period.

CAN-ENA, CAN-ENR or non-income circumstance + Read more ...

Ensure the new income estimate is coded using a DOV = 1 July of the current financial year (earliest date).

FTB will restore from 1 July of the current financial year.

The period from date of cancellation to 30 June of the previous financial year will be automatically assessed as a lump sum claim period.

If adequate income is available (such as reconciliation has completed or can complete in the current activity), payment will be made for this period immediately.

If adequate income is not available (such as reconciliation is pending), the lump sum claim will display as ASS/AIU and Payment will be made if eligible once reconciliation occurs.

Run the Fast Note - Auto Text > Families > SUS/CAN/RES > FTB Restore - Income Estimate provided DOV (or other appropriate option) to ensure the correct Act reference is recorded.

If CAN-ENR or CAN-ENA, include in the restoration DOC:

  • DOV = date of original FTB CAN-ENR or CAN-ENA in previous financial year
  • DOE = date of original FTB CAN-ENR or CAN-ENA in previous financial year (if reconciliation has occurred), or
  • DOE = 1 July of the current financial year (if reconciliation has not yet occurred)

FTB status CAN-INC + Read more ...

Ensure the new estimate is updated using the claim lodgement date as the date of event (DOV).

The customer's entitlement for the CAN-INC non-payment period can only be assessed after the end of the financial year when reconciliation occurs. The customer's FTB reconciliation letter will advise them:

  • they have not been assessed for the full financial year, and
  • to lodge a lump sum claim so their entitlement for that period can be checked. See Assessment of income cancellation periods in Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation

If the system is attempting to pay FTB arrears for the CAN-INC non-payment periods when being restored based on a previous Child Care Subsidy (CCS) estimate that is:

  • lower than the most recent estimate provided code the FYSO for the CAN-INC non-payment period, using Status code CAN and Reason code OTH. This will stop incorrect FTB arrears from paying for this period. The arrears will be assessed in reconciliation when actual income is confirmed, and the customer has lodged a lump sum claim or requested a reassessment within the relevant time frame
  • equal to or higher than the most recent estimate provided, pay the FTB arrears provided they are correct. For help in determining why arrears are paying. See What to check when Family Tax Benefit (FTB) rate has changed

The period from date of cancellation to 30 June of the previous financial year will be assessed as a lump sum claim period once income details for reconciliation have been received.

If income details are available (for example, reconciliation has completed or can complete in the current activity), payment will be made for this period immediately, if eligible.

If adequate income is not available (for example, reconciliation is pending), the lump sum claim will appear as ASS/AIU and payment will be made, if eligible, once reconciliation occurs.

Run the Fast Note - Auto Text > Families > SUS/CAN/RES > FTB Restore to ensure the correct Act reference is recorded. Include in the restoration DOC:

  • DOV = date of receipt (DOR) of new revised estimate
  • DOE = date of original FTB CAN-INC in previous financial year

Explain the reason for this result in the DOC.

Use the Benefit Status (XBS) screen as a guide to help DOC the outcome.

Go to Step 7.
