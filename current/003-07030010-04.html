Definition of home

Can Crisis Payment be paid for customers who are homeless or living in a car? + Read more ...

No.

Advice provided has clarified that home is a ‘fixed residence’ and CrP is not payable for homeless customers.

The Department of Social Services (DSS) has confirmed that a car is not a home for Crisis Payment.

An Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) precedent states that definition of home does not include a car. The precedent was in the context of Carer Allowance.

Changing homes

Can a claimant moving into a different unit or site within the same building or caravan park be eligible for Crisis Payment? + Read more ...

Yes.

There is no prohibition in legislation or policy that stops a claimant from moving into a different unit or site, within the same building or caravan park.

Anticipation of violence

Can a customer qualify for Crisis Payment due to an expectation of violence? + Read more ...

For example, a customer believes there is a reasonable threat from a partner being released from prison, tracking down and harming the claimant. No actual threat has yet been made: no emails, no SMS, just an expectation of the customer/warning from the police that the customer is likely to be harmed.

Social workers believe that if the authorities believe there is a valid future threat, the customer ought to qualify.

No.

CrP cannot be paid pre-emptively and the customer must have left the home as outlined in Operational Blueprint.

There is some misunderstanding that ‘intention to leave home’ is somehow a qualifying condition. It is an incorrect merging of the ‘left home’ and ‘established or intends to establish a new home’ criteria.

Part 2.23A, 1061JH (1) of the Social Security Act 1991:

  • a) the person has left, or cannot return, to his or her home because of an extreme circumstance, and
  • e) the person makes a claim for CrP within 7 days after the extreme circumstance occurred
    Note: examples of extreme circumstances that would qualify are….. a person being subjected to domestic or family violence

A customer can test their eligibility for any payment by lodging a claim and being assessed for that payment.

Financial hardship

Can the customer’s joint bank account balances be excluded from financial hardship assessment for Crisis Payment? + Read more ...

Generally No. However, if considered 'unrealisable' these funds may be excluded.

Social Security Guide, 3.7.4.10, Qualification for CrP - general provisions has details on the severe financial hardship test. The policy states:
'When assessing hardship all cash and readily realisable assets should be taken into account, including money in the bank and earnings due.'

Eligibility for Crisis Payment (CrP) explains that a 'readily realisable asset’ is any asset that is not deemed an unrealisable asset. In the case of Trust or Company income, this must be determined by a Complex Assessment Officer as per Unrealisable assets under the Assets Test hardship provisions.

For example, the joint account has been frozen as part of separation or divorce proceedings by either party, and the customer provides proof of this. Social Security Guide, 4.6.7.60, Determining severe financial hardship - readily available funds.

Long grass resident

Are long grass residents eligible for Crisis Payment? + Read more ...

No,

New claim for Crisis Payment Other Extreme Circumstances states that the treatment of ‘long grass’ people should be consistent with other homeless people.

A person who is already homeless would not qualify for a CrP on the basis of an extreme circumstance rendering them still homeless.

Returning home

Does a customer qualify for EXV if they left the home due to family and domestic violence but advise at the time of the social worker assessment for the EXV claim that they have now returned home? + Read more ...

No.

The person who at the time of assessment has subsequently returned to their home after submitting a Crisis Payment claim no longer qualifies under the extreme circumstances forcing departure from home category.

A person who has returned home would generally not be able to satisfy sections (1)(b) and (c) of section 1061JH of the Social Security Act 1991.

In cases of family and domestic violence, it is up to the social worker to be satisfied that it is unreasonable to expect the person to return home and that the person has established or intends to establish a new home. If a person chooses to return home before their Crisis Payment claim is assessed, the social worker would not be satisfied that these provisions have been met.

People returning to a violent relationship, particularly women, must be made aware of the support services available to them and offered appropriate referrals.

Arrears payments

Does a customer with an arrears payment of an ISP or FTB have this money taken into account when assessing financial hardship? + Read more ...

The financial hardship assessment is taken on the date of claim, based on the information provided in the claim by the customer: cash on hand, savings and investment balances including shares.

The system in Process Direct when processing EXV does not look at the Payment Summary (PS) screen for upcoming payments/recent arrears to determine financial hardship.

Deemed claims

What are the rules re contacting to claim/deemed claims for Crisis Payment? + Read more ...

See Contact requirements for Crisis Payment (CrP).

Customers claiming Crisis Payment should lodge their claim within 7 days of the extreme circumstance occurring (except for NHE, which is within 14 days).

If the customer contacts by the 7th day but is unable to lodge a claim at that time, the intent to claim provisions for vulnerable customers can apply.

Customers would then have an additional 14 days from this date to lodge their claim.

If there are special circumstances that prevent lodgement of the claim within 14 days, then this period to lodge can be extended for up to 13 weeks.

 

7 days to contact

How are the 7 days calculated? + Read more ...

Date of event is day 0. For example, date decided cannot return home.

Then the 7 day count starts the following day.

Take into account weekends and public holidays.

See examples in Contact requirements for Crisis Payment (CrP).

Claim contact

What is contact for a CrP claim? + Read more ...

Operational Blueprint very clearly defines what is and is not considered to be contact in regards to claiming CrP.

See what is considered contact in Contact requirements for Crisis Payment (CrP).

Name of
Crisis Payment

Can we change the name of Crisis Payment? + Read more ...

No.

This suggestion has been raised previously via recent Innovation challenges and social worker feedback. This issue has been raised with the Department of Social Services (DSS).

DSS advises there are no plans to change the name of the payment.

The legislation uses the term ‘Crisis Payment’, so legislation would need to be changed.

Age limits

Can a child claim Crisis Payment if they are getting Special Benefit with a non resident parent as NOM? + Read more ...

Special Benefit is a qualifying payment for Crisis Payment purposes. The child is to be assessed as per any other customer, taking information from parent as NOM. The child is considered a customer and must be considered the ‘victim’ of family and domestic violence and meet the other eligibility criteria.
