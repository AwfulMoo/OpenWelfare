Auto/Manual

1

Caring Responsibilities

A customer is considered to be experiencing a vulnerable circumstance due to caring responsibilities if they were caring for or the partner of a person who was suffering from a medical condition or was hospitalised within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card.

That person's medical condition, or circumstances relating to it, must have had a significant adverse effect on the customer's ability to submit their claim on the contact date.

Vulnerable circumstances due to caring responsibilities include the following circumstances in the 8 weeks before initial contact:

  • a Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA) customer was/is under 18 years of age
  • a CP and/or CA customer is caring for someone with a terminal illness who is a child not expected to live for more than 24 months or a person 18 or over not expected to live more than 3 months
  • a CP and/or CA customer is caring for someone who was hospitalised during the 8 week period
  • a CP and/or CA customer's caring responsibilities started during the 8 week period, that is. they have recently taken on the caring role

Where a medical report is already provided for the claim, an additional medical certificate is generally not required.

The Service Officer must document the reason for the decision.

Manual only

2

Death of a family member

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer was affected by the death of a partner or immediate family member.

Note: the term 'family member' is defined by the Social Security Act 1191 subsections 23(14) and 23(15) in relation to the relevant person as being:

  • their partner or parent/guardian
  • their sister, brother or child
  • any other person who, in the opinion of the Secretary should be treated as one of the relevant person's relations described above, for example an aunt may have raised the person and should be treated as the mother

When the person is affected by the death of their partner and may be seeking help, see Initial contact after the death of a partner of a non-customer couple.

Automatic or Manual

3

Domestic family violence

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer has been subject to family or domestic violence.

Consider a referral to a Social Worker. See Family and domestic violence to conduct the risk identification and referral process.

Note: where a Child Care Additional Subsidy (CCAS) flag of ACCS (Child Wellbeing) is present on the customer's record (or has been within the previous eight weeks), a pre claim vulnerable circumstance of Domestic Violence is automatically applied. No other CCAS flag type will be recognised as a pre-claim vulnerable circumstance.

Automatic or Manual

4

Family unit break down

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer was experiencing extreme family breakdown.

Manual only

5

Former SRSS recipient

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer was a recipient of Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS) payment (while holding a bridging visa and claimed Special Benefit on being granted a temporary protection visa).

Auto only

6

Holder of 866 visa

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer was:

  • granted an 866 visa while detained in an Australian immigration detention facility and released from detention on or after visa grant date, or
  • a holder of an 866 visa and there was a delay in the notification of visa grant

Manual only

7

Homelessness

The customer has a Homelessness Indicator applied on their record with a date of effect within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card.

A Service Officer must make sure that they have considered recording a Homelessness Indicator.

Auto only

8

Hospitalised

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card the customer was hospitalised and unable to make a claim.

Manual only

9

Humanitarian entrant

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer arrived in Australia and the arrival is the first time the person has arrived as the holder of a humanitarian visa.

See Initial contact from refugees, humanitarian entrants and protection visa holders for the current list of the qualifying 'humanitarian' visas.

Note: an 866 visa holder is not considered a 'humanitarian entrant to Australia'. For these customers, please consider 'Holder of 866 visa', see Item 6.

Automatic or Manual

10

Incapacitated

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer had a medical condition and the medical condition, or circumstances relating to it, had a significant adverse effect on them and stopped them from lodging a claim.

Manual only

11

International

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer was outside Australia when claiming.

Automatic or Manual

12

Interpreter required

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer was in need of an interpreter at their date of contact and there is a delay in claim completion due to Interpreter on Demand service availability.

Manual only

13

Involuntary nominee

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer has been appointed an involuntary nominee as a result of a Civil Administration Tribunal decision. This pre-claim circumstance should be manually recorded as 'Service Officer Discretion' and this reason should only be used where none of the other reasons listed apply. The Service Officer must document the reason for the decision.

See Appointing nominees examples of an involuntary nominee.

Manual only

14

New parent

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer became the parent of a dependent child born to them within that 8 week period.

See Calculating the start date soon after child birth.

Automatic or Manual

15

Prison or psychiatric confinement

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer was released from prison or psychiatric confinement.

Example 1

Rhianna was released from prison 6 weeks ago. Rhianna contacts to make a claim for JobSeeker Payment. As Rhianna is a vulnerable customer, record an intent to claim and advise Rhianna to lodge a claim within 14 days. If eligibility criteria is met and Rhianna lodges a claim within 14 days Rhianna will be payable from today.

Example 2

Michael was released from prison without notice in Far North Queensland and is heading back to country in the Northern Territory.

The nominee or a third party contacts Services Australia the day Michael is released. Tell the third party to encourage Michael to contact or lodge a claim as soon as possible (today if possible), with all supporting documents, to ensure the claim is assessed from the earliest date.

Example 3

A Corrections Officer advises Sammy will be released from prison in the near future. PRECLM cannot be coded as the customer is not qualified at time of contact. Record a DOC.

  • Sammy has an expected date of release of 20 August 2022
  • Corrections Officer contacts on behalf of Sammy on the 1 August advising Sammy is likely to be released on 20 August
  • Service Officer records a DOC with this information
  • Sammy submits claim 25 August 2022.

If eligible, Sammy is payable from date of release under Backdating provisions for claim lodgement

Manual only

16

Relationship breakdown

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer had a break down or separation in a relationship and is experiencing high stress.

If the customer indicates they have safety concerns for themselves or their family due to family and domestic violence, see Family and domestic violence to conduct the risk identification and referral process.

Automatic or Manual

17

Remote area

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer's usual place of residence was in a 'remote area' and the person was physically present in a remote area.

A customer may remain regarded as 'physically present in the remote area' during temporary absences of up to 8 weeks.

See Remote Area Allowance (RAA) eligibility and payment for determination of the locality and definition of 'physically present in the remote area'.

Automatic or Manual

18

Residence lost or damaged

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer's usual place of residence has been destroyed or sustained major damage because of an extreme circumstance.

An extreme circumstance may include, but is not limited to, flooding, fire, earthquake, cyclone and storms. See Eligibility for Crisis Payment (CrP) for the definition of an extreme circumstance.

Automatic or Manual

19

Service Officer discretion

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer was subject to other special circumstances beyond their control.

Note: this reason should only be used where none of the other reasons listed apply. The Service Officer must clearly document the reason(s) for the decision.

Manual only

20

System issues

Within the 8 weeks before the customer's initial contact date for a payment or concession card, known system issues stopped submission of an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) or online claim at the time of contact.

Manual only

21

Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH)

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer was experiencing risk to their wellbeing due to it being unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH).

Automatic or Manual

22

Victim of Crime

Within the 8 weeks before their initial contact date for a payment or concession card, the customer was a victim of crime, for example, possessions stolen. Customers should supply sufficient evidence to support their claim, i.e. Incident number.

Manual only

Determining the 14 day period to submit a claim

Customers have a 14 day period in which to submit their claim after contacting Services Australia, (the 14 day period can be extended in some circumstances but no more than 13 weeks).

The 14 day period generally begins the day after the customer contacts Services Australia. For example, if a customer contacts on Tuesday 4 January, the 14 day period starts from Wednesday 5 January and includes Tuesday 18 January.

The last day in which a customer has to submit a claim (within the 14 days) is usually the same day of the week as the day they registered their intention to claim (or the day the 14 day period was re-started).

However, if the customer's 14 day period ends on a weekend or a public holiday, they can lodge their claim on the next working day. For example, if a customer contacts on Saturday 7 May, they will be required to lodge the claim by Monday 23 May. Similarly if a customer contacts on Tuesday 12 January, they will be required to lodge the claim by Wednesday 27 January (26 January being Australia Day).

Examples of applying a deemed date of claim (DDOC) for a vulnerable customer

Item

Description

1

On 2 January 2021, Elijah contacts by phone to discuss a Carer Payment (CP) claim as Elijah has recently taken on the full-time care of their aging mother

Elijah started caring within the 8 weeks before contact and is therefore considered to have experienced a vulnerable circumstance. Elijah is advised to submit a claim within 14 days of the contact. A pre-claim vulnerable circumstance is recorded on 2 January 2021.

On 11 January 2021, Elijah starts an online claim for CP. Elijah contacts Services Australia on 23 January 2021. Elijah is aware the 14 day deadline to provide the remaining details needed to complete the claim has been missed.

Elijah advises the deadline was unable to be met due to caring for their mother continuously since the contact date on 2 January 2021. Elijah requests extra time to gather the additional details needed to submit the claim.

On 24 January 2021, Elijah submits a completed claim and evidence of their mother's continuing medical condition. The claim is assessed and determined on 29 January 2021.

  • Date of vulnerable circumstance = 2 November 2020 (started caring in the 8 week period before contact)
  • Date of first contact = 2 January 2021 (claim not submitted within 14 days of this contact date)
  • Date of subsequent contact = 11 January 2021 (within 14 days of claim submission)
  • Date of subsequent contact = 23 January 2021 (within 14 days of claim submission)
  • Date claim submitted = 24 January 2021
  • Date claim determined = 29 January 2021
  • Was customer qualified on the first contact date = Y
  • Was an extension to submit up to 13 weeks later approved = Y (no need to use subsequent contact dates)
  • Deemed date of claim = 2 January 2021

Note: if an extension of up to 13 weeks was not approved, the earliest contact date within 14 days of claim submission would be used, that is 11 January 2021.

These dates are derived under intent to claim provisions. This customer may be eligible for additional backdating provisions for claim lodgement.

2

Chloe - Family or domestic violence

Chloe contacts Services Australia on 28 March 2021. Chloe is calling from a women's shelter as has just left an abusive husband on 20 March 2021 and needs help. Chloe does not wish to speak with a social worker or make a claim for Crisis Payment and does not have time to undertake an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) claim today but will submit an online claim. Chloe will call again once new living arrangements are settled.

A vulnerable customer DOC was recorded on Chloe's record on 28 March 2021 as the vulnerable circumstance occurred within the 8 weeks before Chloe's first contact with the agency.

On 29 March 2021, Chloe contacts Services Australia and requests to speak with a Social Worker for help.

Chloe submits a JSP online claim on 31 March 2021.

  • Date of vulnerable circumstance = 20 March 2021
  • Date of first contact = 28 March 2021
  • Date of subsequent contact = 29 March 2021
  • Date claim submitted = 31 March 2021
  • Was customer qualified on first contact and claim was submitted within 14 days? = Yes
  • Deemed date of claim = 28 March 2021

The start date for Chloe's JobSeeker Payment (JSP) will be 28 March 2021 as that is the date that Chloe first contacted Services Australia, was qualified on that date, and submitted a claim within 14 days.

Note: the start date may be later when an exclusion period applies.

In order to be eligible for a Crisis Payment, Chloe was required to make contact with Services Australia within seven days of deciding to leave home after the crisis event and also needed to be in receipt of, or qualified for a primary payment.

As Chloe's first contact was on 28 March 2021 and this was not within 7 days of the extreme circumstance on 20 March 2021, Chloe will not be eligible for a Crisis Payment.

3

Daisy - Other Special Circumstances

Daisy attends a service centre on 4 July 2020 advising an intention to claim Parenting Payment (PP). After numerous attempts in office to submit an online claim or a staff Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), this was unable to be completed as system issues were intermittent.

After 2 hours, Daisy had to leave to attend another appointment and collect her children from school and was unable to attend the office again that day. As Daisy was unable to submit the claim that day due to system issues that were outside of her control, an 'other special circumstance' vulnerable customer DOC was recorded on Daisy's record on 4 July 2020.

Daisy contacted by phone on 6 July 2020 to complete the ACC and was advised system issues were continuing and it could not be completed. A paper claim was issued.

Daisy attended the office to submit her completed PP paper claim on 17 July 2020. The claim was assessed and determined on 26 July 2020.

  • Date of vulnerable circumstance = 4 July 2020
  • Date of first contact = 4 July 2020
  • Date of subsequent contact = 6 July 2020
  • Date claim submitted = 17 July 2020
  • Deemed date of claim = 4 July 2020

4

Char - Other Special Circumstances

Char attends a service centre on 20 March 2021 and advises that Char would like to claim JobSeeker Payment (JSP).

Char advises that they are the holder of an 866 visa, which was granted on 5 March 2021. However, Char was unable to submit a claim before as Char has been detained in an Australian immigration detention facility. Char started the claim for JSP with the Refugee and Asylum Seeker servicing team. Char was advised that more paper work would be required to support the claim.

Char returned to the service centre on 23 March 2021 with the required documents and the claim was submitted. The claim was assessed and determined on 25 March 2021.

As Char was detained in an Australian immigration detention centre and unable to submit the claim, Char has been considered as a customer experiencing a vulnerable circumstance.

  • Date of vulnerable circumstance = 5 March 2021
  • Date of first contact = 20 March 2021
  • Date claim submitted = 23 March 2021
  • Deemed date of claim = 20 March 2021

5

Bron - Other Special Circumstances

Corrections advise Services Australia on 1 August that Bron is due to be released from prison on 20 August. Bron is currently not allowed any contact and therefore an early claim cannot be lodged. PRECLM cannot be coded as Bron is not eligible for a payment on this date.

The Incarcerated Customer team DOC the contact.

Bron lodges a claim for JobSeeker Payment on 25 August, there is nothing recorded on the PRECLM screen but the Service Officer refers to Backdating provisions for claim lodgement and can manually adjust the start date of Bron’s claim to 20 August, as per the contact DOC.

6

Jack - Service Officer discretion

Jack cannot read or write, has limited support and doesn’t use technology. Jack continues to claim a payment and needs an Assisted Customer Claim and the only available appointment is in 3 days time. You book Jack into this appointment and code the vulnerable circumstance from date of contact.

However, if there is a same day appointment available and Jack chooses to wait 3 days, Jack would not be considered to be vulnerable.

7

Mary - Death of a family member with backdated start date

Mary's daughter contacts Services Australia on 20 May 2020 on behalf of Mary, to advise Mary's husband Bill died on 1 May 2020 and Mary will be claiming JobSeeker Payment (JSP).

Mary is considered a vulnerable customer due to the death of a family member. Backdated start date rules for claims made soon after the death of a partner should be considered after determining the date the claim was made.

  • Date of vulnerable circumstance (death of family member) = 1 May 2020
  • Date became qualified for JSP = 13 May 2020
  • Date of first contact = 20 May 2020
  • Qualified on first contact = Yes
  • Date claim submitted = 1 June 2020 (within 14 days of contact)
  • Deemed date of claim (date the claim was made) = 20 May 2020
  • DDOC within 4 weeks of death of partner = Yes
  • Qualified on date of partner's death = No
  • Start date = 13 May 2020 (date became qualified)
