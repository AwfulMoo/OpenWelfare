Using Payment and Service Finder

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Access Payment and Service Finder + Read more ...

Access Payment and Service Finder from:

  • Payment and Service Finder webpage. The Resources page contains a link to the service. Note: The menu tile has temporarily been removed from the Services Australia website homepage. This is due to space needed for high priority messages for COVID-19 and disaster assistance. Do not report this as a system issue
  • Centrelink online accounts - from the left menu, select Payments and Claims, then Claims, and Payment and Service Finder
  • Customer First - select Customer Online Account > Payment and Service Finder

Select the options that best describe the user's situation.

Some fields will have a question mark in the corner. The user can select this for more information about the question.

Payment and Service Finder is available to use in Simplified Chinese.

Users can:

  • select their preferred language from the menu in the top right corner
  • go back to English at any time after selecting Simplified Chinese

A disclaimer will advise the user the results are:

  • indicative only and
  • does not take into account all of their circumstances

The results are a guide only.

2

Select Payments or Services + Read more ...

Users can look for payments or services.

For:

  • payments, go to Step 3
  • services, go to Step 5

3

Questions - Payment Finder + Read more ...

The Payment and Service Finder takes about five minutes to complete.

If users access the Payment and Service Finder in their Centrelink online account, they can choose at the beginning of the service to prefill certain details from their record.

The service will ask the users questions about:

  • Residence
  • Relationship status
  • Age
  • Accommodation
  • Work circumstances study circumstances
  • Family circumstances
  • Health circumstances
  • Crisis situations
  • Farmer, Rural, Indigenous identity, Australian residency and Refugee status
  • Income and Assets

The results Payment and Service Finder provide are a guide only. Tell the user the service:

  • does not take into account all circumstances, and
  • is not a guarantee of payment eligibility

The user selects Submit after all answers have been entered.

4

Payment results + Read more ...

Based on the answers given, the service will show payments and concession cards the user may want to explore.

Each payment or concession card option shown includes a summary and a link to Find out more option to select.

Payments that can be estimated will also provide an Estimate rate now option to select.

Users can:

  • start again by selecting Restart. This will clear any information they have already entered
  • change their answers by selecting Change. This will clear all previous answers

This page will display a list of payments that may be available based on the user's answers.

This may include payments or benefit types, for example:

  • Youth Allowance - Student
  • ABSTUDY - Student
  • Low Income Health Care Card

The payments will include basic information, including:

  • a description of the payment
  • eligibility basics
  • a link to more information about the payment
  • a link to estimate their rate of payment (if applicable)

5

Questions - Service Finder + Read more ...

Users can find services available in their community that may be of assistance.

The service will ask the user to select their state and any of the following categories:

  • Disability and carers
  • Education
  • Employment
  • Family and domestic violence
  • Health circumstances
  • Housing
  • Legal services
  • Mental health
  • Money
  • Older Australians
  • Raising Children
  • Relationships
  • Separated parents
  • Travelling

Users can start again by selecting Restart. This will clear all previous answers.

The user selects Done after answering the above.

6

Service results + Read more ...

Based on their answers, the service will show services the user may wish to explore.

The services will show in categories with links the users can access.

A disclaimer will show to tell the user the results supplied may not list all the services available. Community support groups may be able to offer more help.

The results Payment and Service Finder provide are a guide only. Tell the user the service:

  • does not take into account all circumstances, and
  • is not a guarantee of payment eligibility

Estimating rates of payment

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Payments + Read more ...

Payments may include:

  • ABSTUDY
  • Age Pension
  • Austudy
  • Carer Payment
  • Disability Support Pension
  • JobSeeker Payment
  • Parenting Payment
  • Special Benefit
  • Youth Allowance
  • Child Care Subsidy
  • Family Tax Benefit*
  • Family Assistance - Youth Allowance/ABSTUDY comparison
  • Tertiary Access Payment

The service will provide the user with options including:

  • estimating their child support rate as part of this calculation
  • comparing Family Tax Benefit with Youth Allowance/ABSTUDY payments

2

Estimate rate of payment + Read more ...

Users can select Estimate rate now. The service will estimate the amount of payment.

This will prompt them to answer some additional questions. These questions will vary depending on the payment type.

The questions may include information about:

  • income (for example, employment income, income stream income, other Government payment income)
  • assets, (for example, savings/bank account balance, managed investments, real estate assets)
  • independence (for example, if estimating for Youth Allowance - Student payment)
  • parent/guardian details (for example, if estimating for Youth Allowance - Student payment)
  • family circumstances
  • family member details. For example, when estimating for Youth Allowance - Student payment. The service will ask about other people who are dependent on the same parent/guardian
  • other circumstances (for example, I have a disability, I am living overseas, I am in prison)

Based on the answers provided an estimated payment rate per fortnight will display.

Users will also be able to:

  • see a breakdown of the estimate
  • read more information
  • estimate another payment using the details already provided

The rate estimators are not a standalone service. Users can only access by answering the Payment Finder questions first.

Once the user completes the questions, the results page will show. This includes possible payments and a net estimate of the rate.

Learn more provides a link to further information, including how to claim online.

Back to payment results allows the user to select another payment to estimate. The responses they gave will prefill into the estimator where possible. This means the user will not have to re-enter data.

If the user wishes to start again they can select Restart. Alternatively the user can select the back option to change specific data already entered. Selecting Restart will clear all previous answers.

3

Commence online claim (only for users logged into their Centrelink online account) + Read more ...

Users who access Payment and Service Finder through the Centrelink online account will get a link to start an online claim.
