Update the H and R screens

Table 2: this table provides information on the H and R screens for the assessment of hospital claims.

Screen

Description

The H screen

The H screen is used for the assessment of hospital claims

The H screen can be accessed by entering a claim type of H on the VRDI screen.

The H screen must be used to assess all general private hospital claims. This will allow the system to calculate the appropriate patient stepdown.

Using the H screen the benefit is paid according to the hospital contracted rates held on Hospital Contracts Maintenance System (HCVS/VAMM).

The R screen

The R screen is used to process non-contracted hospital claims where approval is present, or where restrictions prevent payment of a private hospital claim according to the General Private Hospital Processing Rules.

This R screen can be accessed by entering a claim type of R on the VRDI screen.

The R screen is not to be used for general private hospital claims processing to force the system to pay the amounts claimed by hospitals.

If there are difficulties processing hospital claims please email the VAP Helpdesk.

Using the R screen the benefit is paid according to the rates held on the item fee file (QITI). The system will ignore hospital contract details when the claim is keyed on the R screen. VAP State Offices should only use the R screen for processes approved by the VAP Operations section.

Prior Approval (PARS) Records

Table 10

Step

Action

1

Prior Approval (PARS) Records

During hospital claims processing, the system automatically creates a pseudo Prior Approval Record, the approval information held on the patient’s prior approval (VEPI) record.

The system will automatically create the appropriate prior approval record during background assessing. If a Prior Approval Record is not created for the item claimed, does not exist or does not cover the service/s being claimed, an appropriate error message displays and the situation will need to be resolved during pend processing. It is not possible to pay hospital services if a prior approval record does not exist for the services involved.

There are a number of different types of prior approval record used/created during hospital claims processing.

When the Referral Type for a Hospital item is set to ‘5 DVA PRIOR APPROVAL’ and the item is claimed for an eligible card holder by a contracted Private Hospital, the system will generate a pseudo prior approval record.

2

Operations and Procedures (OP) Record

The operation and procedures (OP) record is used to record details of approvals granted for operations/procedures (medical services) and hospital services. It is also used to count the number of services and/or hospital bed days paid against that record.

Hospital services can be manually matched to an OP record.

Keeping a count of hospital bed days on the prior approval record ensures that the system counts consecutive days of admission to ensure a 3B Certificate is present when the length of the stay exceeds 35 days.

When the Prior Approval Treatment Type for an MBS operation/procedure item is set to OP, the item will be matched to the prior approval record and displayed in the NO field on the OP line.

Note: when the Prior Approval Treatment Type for an MBS operation/procedure item is set to OP, the item will be matched to the prior approval record and displayed in the NO field on the OP line.

3

HM Prior Approval Record

The HM record is a prior approval record (I in the AUT TYP field) that has been created by the system during background assessing.

The information can be viewed in Mainframe

Note: keeping a count of hospital bed days on the prior approval record ensures that the 35 day rule is correctly administered during hospital claims processing.

4

HM Pseudo (Integration) Approval Record

The HM record is a pseudo (integration) approval record (I in the AUT TYP field) that is created by the system during claims processing.

Note: it is not possible to manually match services to a pseudo (integration) approval record if there is continuity of a hospital stay e.g. the dates in a hospital episode continue on from those in an existing pseudo PARS record. The system will not create another record but will extend the END DATE of the existing record and increment the number of occupied bed days shown in the NO field.

5

Hospital Maintenance (HM) Record

Hospital Maintenance (HM) record is used to record details of approvals granted for hospital services and to count the number of hospital bed days paid against the prior approval record.

The record can be created by DVA as a prior approval record or it can be generated by the system during background assessing as a pseudo (integration) record.

  • If the Service Officer is unsure of the intention of the claim, they may contact the facility

Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP)

FAQ

PCI – Private Hospital Cardiac Package for items HX523-HX530. For further advice on assessing hospital claims, please contact a VAP PSO.

Item

Description

1

Question: What is PCI?

Answer: PCI stands for 'Percutaneous Coronary Intervention'. This was known as angioplasty with stent.

This procedure is a non-surgical procedure that uses a catheter (a thin flexible tube) to place a small structure called a stent to open up blood vessels in the heart that have been narrowed by plaque build-up. This is a condition known as atherosclerosis.

What is done? A catheter is inserted into the blood vessels either in the groin or in the arm using a special type of X-ray called fluoroscopy.

The catheter is threaded through the blood vessels into the heart where the coronary artery is narrowed.

When the tip is in place, a balloon tip covered with a stent is inflated. The balloon tip compresses the plaque and expands the stent.

Once the plaque is compressed and the stent is in place, the balloon is deflated and withdrawn.

The stent stays in the artery, holding it open.

2

Question: How is return to theatre treated?

Answer: Hospitals can claim for the second procedure by claiming a second interim PCI package.

In these cases, Hospitals must submit text in online claims or write on the manual claim why the second procedure was required. Second procedures must only be performed where:

  • the patient may be admitted due to an emergency, or
  • the patient becomes hemodynamically unstable during the first PCI procedure resulting in the need to return to theatre to complete the PCI procedure

DVA intends to monitor second package claims, due to a return to theatre.

3

Question: When an unrelated procedure is performed within the PCI package boundary days, can only theatre be claimed for the unrelated procedure?

Answer: Yes

Only claim theatre for the unrelated procedures. This is, because the PCI Package item includes accommodation for all procedures within the boundary days.

4

Question: How many days are in a package before outlier days can be paid?

Answer: The number of days varies per package based on what is undertaken during the procedure (there are 24 new items based on whether the procedure was performed as an emergency or on a planned basis).

The Boundary days of each Category (emergency versus planned) and the number of territories treated are outlined in table: Boundary Days for each Category and Number of Territories.

For episodes where the patient stays longer than the upper boundary day (outlier days), the hospital can claim a per diem rate for each additional admitted day. The per diem rate is set at the bottom level of the Advanced Surgery Group 2 and is claimed using the designated DVA Hospital Item for Outlier Days (H339).

5

Question: Unrelated procedure performed after PCI

Can the accommodation for the unrelated procedure be claimed after the PCI boundary days end (unrelated procedures performed after the PCI)?

Answer: Yes. Claim the unrelated procedure and accommodation after the PCI boundary days end.

If this is the case, how many outlier days go with the package before they end?

Answer: The accommodation claimed after the PCI boundary days end will correspond with the unrelated procedure performed. There is no limit on the outlier days.

Note: outlier days only relate to the PCI package items where the length of stay exceeds the boundary days for the package.

6

Question: Unrelated procedure performed before the PCI:

  • Where an unrelated procedure is performed prior to the PCI procedure, then accommodation for the unrelated procedure must be claimed on a per diem basis until the PCI procedure is performed and then accommodation is included in the PCI package
  • Are the two type of accommodation going to restrict, or will the system be paying a package and accommodation?

Answer: Hospitals can claim theatre and accommodation for the unrelated procedure prior to the PCI being done.

Claim accommodation at the per diem rate up to the day the PCI procedure is performed.

The hospital can claim further accommodation using the outlier day item code (H339), if the patient stays longer than the boundary days included in the interim PCI Funding Model.

No business rule restricts the claiming of per diem accommodation with the package.

7

Question: If the private hospital does not have a package item in their contract, is the hospital able to claim the ‘day only accommodation’ band D1-D4 (H277-H280)?

If so, who is eligible to claim for the theatre item?

Answer: No. If the hospital does not have the package in their contract, they will not be able to claim for this service and must not be providing the service.

The package is currently unable to be unbundled with accommodation and theatre paid separately.

The hospital must contact their DVA contract manager if they are interested in delivering this service.

8

Question: For hospitals that only perform the procedure or only provide accommodation, what should these hospitals be claiming?

For example, if a patient is in Hospital A for the procedure and then is transferred to Hospital B for an overnight stay?

Answer: DVA’s interim PCI Funding Model includes both theatre and accommodation.

It is not currently possible to unbundle these items.

DVA is working with affected hospitals to identify a solution.

9

Question: Does Hospital A claim package item (HX507-HX530) and Hospital B claim overnight stay (H339)?

Answer: DVA’s interim PCI Funding Model includes both theatre and accommodation.

It is not currently possible to unbundle these items.

DVA is working with affected hospitals to identify a solution.

10

Question: Confirmation that if the hospital has boundary set as 1-3 and anything paid after that is an outlier.

Indication/notation in the comments – previous package items, (for example HX506) usually are left with a comment so staff are aware of how many days are paid as boundary days and the rest as outlier)

Answer: There are different boundary days for different packages.

If the patient stays longer than the upper boundary day, the hospital can claim a per diem rate using DVA Hospital Item Number H339: Outlier Days in addition to the episode based payment for each additional admitted day.

11

Question: Can two PCI procedures be claimed on the same day?

Answer: A second procedure can be claimed if the patient:

  • was admitted due to an emergency, or
  • becomes hemodynamically unstable during the first PCI procedure, resulting in the need to return to theatre to complete the PCI procedure

12

Question: What date of service should the PCI item be claimed if the patient is admitted before the date of the procedure?

Answer: The date of service of the PCI package item is the date the PCI procedure was performed. The case payment commences on the day that the procedure is performed, as a patient may have been admitted prior to the day of the procedure. The day(s) prior to the procedure are to be claimed at the per diem rate under the relevant classification (medical, surgical, CCU, etc), with the day of the PCI procedure constituting a change in classification.

13

Question: Is the hospital required to indicate ALL the items that are relating to the package item if there are multiple items bundled/contained that package?

Answer: The claiming for the PCI packages is the same as those for the other packages in the contract: where there are multiple procedures that may be performed under the package item number, the hospital should be listing all the procedures performed on the invoice.

There are a number of PCI procedures which are standalone or performed in conjunction with another procedure. In the example where there are 2 packages that may apply with use of procedure item 38307. By adding the other relevant procedure items, it can be determined that the hospital is claiming the correct package item number.
