Quality On Line (QOL)

Considerations for QOL checkers checking Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment (AVTOP) claims

Item

Description

Minimum standards

Is there a minimum standards error + Read more ...

The checker needs to consider all Getting it Right (GIR) Minimum Standards and that the following has been recorded and is consistent across both Customer First and the AVTOP processing tool:

  • Proof of Identity (POI)
  • Residency
  • Person or the person’s close family member were in the place where the terrorist act occurred
  • Applicable travel warnings
  • Any monies received from foreign governments
  • Any involvement of the person or the person’s close family member in the commission of the terrorist act
  • Injury and life impact assessment correctly recorded

Rates

Is there a rate error + Read more ...

The checker needs to check that the calculation of the final amount is correct and recorded correctly in both Customer First and the AVTOP processing tool taking into consideration:

  • The injury assessment category matches injury assessment amount according to Schedule 1 – AVTOP Schedule of Injuries. The References page contains a link to Schedule 1
  • That life impact ratings match life impact amount
  • Reductions, deductions and exemptions (where appropriate)
  • For secondary victims, where the deceased received an AVTOP as a primary victim
  • The final figure apportioned accordingly to hierarchy of close family members (secondary victims) ensuring the correct close family members were paid

For information, see:

  • Primary victim eligibility and rate calculation for the Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment (AVTOP)
  • Secondary victim eligibility and rate calculation for the Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment (AVTOP)

Dates

Is there a date error + Read more ...

The checker needs to check that the claim was submitted within the claiming period or that special circumstances apply.

Event

Is there a program error + Read more ...

The checker needs to check that:

  • The terrorist act was declared
  • ‘In the place where the terrorist act occurred’ has been assessed appropriately
  • invitations to identified appropriate close family members were sent (secondary victims)
