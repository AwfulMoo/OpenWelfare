Initial contact - Making an initial BasicsCard offer

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer contacts to request a BasicsCard + Read more ...

Customers contacting via Smart Centre Call requesting the issue of a BasicsCard are to be referred to their nearest Centrelink Service Centre, Remote Service Centre or Remote Servicing Team (RST) as BasicsCards can only be issued via face-to-face interviews. Procedure ends here.

At a face-to-face interview, an income managed customer requests a BasicsCard, or an offer of a card is made to the customer as a method of meeting their priority needs.

A BasicsCard can be issued by Service Officers at:

  • Centrelink Service Centres
  • Remote Service Centres
  • Remote Servicing Teams (RSTs) with Centrelink system connectivity and BasicsCard stocks in a face-to-face interview with the customer

Confirm Customer Identity

If customer not already authenticated, confirm the customer's identification before proceeding with interview. As a result of the risk of issuing funds to third parties in error, it is essential that this step is carried out thoroughly and carefully.

Issuing the BasicsCard to a third party in error can result in hardship for the customer and financial loss to Services Australia.

If in any doubt, do not issue the BasicsCard and consult a Centrelink social worker, or the Income Management helpdesk for further assistance.

Payment nominees

If an income managed customer has a payment nominee the payment nominee will be issued the BasicsCard on behalf of the customer. See Nominee arrangements under Income Management

If the customer does not wish the payment nominee to be issued the BasicsCard, the customer must to cancel the nominee arrangement prior to being issued a BasicsCard.

2

Determine if request is for initial or replacement BasicsCard + Read more ...

Is this an initial or replacement BasicsCard, or a request to allocate funds to an existing BasicsCard?

  • To issue for the first time, go to Step 3
  • To issue a customer who has an existing BasicsCard with a replacement BasicsCard with the same expiry date, see BasicsCard replacement of card. Procedure ends here
  • To allocate funds to an existing BasicsCard, see Changes to Income Management priority needs. Procedure ends here

3

Determine if customer is eligible for a BasicsCard + Read more ...

All BasicsCards are to be issued in Centrelink system. Manual coding updates are to be done only when workflow is unavailable. For manual instruction see Step 3 in the Manual coding for issuing an initial BasicsCard table.

Workflow updates

To check if the customer is eligible for a BasicsCard, see:

  • The customer's Income Management Summary screen for their Income Management status. Customers are only eligible to be issued a BasicsCard if they are currently on Income Management, or if it is less than or equal to 60 days since the customers Income Management ended and they have an Income Management residual balance. Note: a customer is not to be given a BasicsCard if they are currently on 'Auto' Income Management
  • Under General Issues if the customer is eligible, the option 'Customer may be eligible for BasicsCard, BasicsCard Assessment is required' will display

Is the customer eligible for a BasicsCard?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Customer is not eligible for a BasicsCard + Read more ...

Customer is 'Auto' Income Management

If the customer is 'Auto' income managed a BasicsCard cannot be issued until the Initial Assessment interview has been conducted. This interview should be conducted as soon as possible, preferably at this contact, See Initial assessment of Income Management.

If an Initial Assessment interview cannot be completed at this time, advise the customer they are not eligible for a BasicsCard. Determine if there is any other assistance available to the customer. Record details on a DOC as to why the customer is not entitled. Procedure ends here.

5

Customer is eligible for a BasicsCard + Read more ...

For information on where the customer can use the BasicsCard in their community, search for TPO.

Customers can access information on organisations where they can use their BasicsCard using the Business and Organisation search tool located on the Services Australia Website (servicesaustralia.gov.au/basicscard). See the Resources page for a link to the Organisation Finder service.

Provide the customer with information on where the BasicsCard can be used, the general terms and conditions of use and benefits of the card for example, automatic transfer of funds to card. Note: the Customer Information Pack is not given to the customer at this stage. Only after the card is issued to the customer should this be provided.

Does the customer accept the offer of a BasicsCard?

  • Yes, run the Issue card workflow. See Step1 in the Issue of initial BasicsCard workflow table
  • No, determine if the customer is eligible for any other assistance, for example, a one-off or regular payment to a registered Third Party Organisation (TPO). Record the reason why the customer refused the BasicsCard. The two options are 'customer vulnerability' or 'special circumstances'. Record details on a DOC as to why the BasicsCard was refused. Procedure ends here

Issue of initial BasicsCard

Workflow for issuing initial BasicsCard

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Issue card workflow + Read more ...

The Service Officer is presented with an authentication screen. If the customer has Display on Access (DOA) DOCs the Service Officer must confirm that they have read the DOC.

The Service Officer must also confirm if the customer's record is password protected before they can continue.

Once confirmed, the Service Officer must indicate how they authenticated the customer. The Service Officer will not be able to continue through the workflow until the Customer has been fully authenticated.

If a customer has a nominee, the Service Officer will be presented with a variation of the authentication screen.

All sections will be greyed out except for the section allowing access to DOA DOCs and the customer's password. Once this is completed the Service Officer must then confirm the nominees name and password before the workflow will allow the Service Officer to continue.

If a nominee cannot recall their password refer to Appointing Nominees.

Once authentication is complete, if the customer has a payment nominee they will be presented with an 'assign card' screen.

An information message will display stating:

'BasicsCard can only be assigned to the customer where the payment nominee has given their permission. Where the customer is assigned the BasicsCard both the payment nominee and the customer must be authenticated'.

The 'assign card' screen will inform the Service Officer who the BasicsCard is currently assigned to, and allows the payment nominee to assign the BasicsCard to the principal (customer) if they wish to.

The Service Officer will be presented with the question 'assign BasicsCard to'. The options presented are 'nominee' or 'customer'.

If the nominee selects to have the BasicsCard assigned to the customer, they will be presented with the question 'does the nominee consent to assigning the BasicsCard to the customer'.

  • If 'no' is selected then the system will not allow the Service Officer to go any further and the Service Officer must navigate back and select 'nominee'
  • If 'yes' is selected then the 'continue' button will become available and allow the Service Officer to proceed

Note: if the customer does not have a payment nominee the assign card section will not appear.

Note: a customer who has exited Income Management, may request a BasicsCard within 60 days of Income Management ending to access residual funds in their IM account.

2

Thermally printing the card holder's name on the BasicsCard + Read more ...

  • When thermally printing the cardholder's name on the BasicsCard, the system will default to the 'Legal name'. This only needs to be changed if the customer/payment nominee goes by another listed name. Note: the name on the card can be a maximum 31 characters in length
  • The 'Print customer/nominee name on BasicsCard'? question allows the Service Officer to select if the customer/payment nominee name will be thermally printed on the BasicsCard. 'No' should only be selected where thermal printing is unavailable

Can thermal printing be used?

  • Yes, ensure the correct Office or Remote Printer is entered:
    • Remote Servicing Teams must select 'Remote' (USB) Printer, then enter the IP address of the thermal printer which will have been provided by the trip leader. Go to Step 3
    • If not using a remote printer, select 'Office Printer' and Card Printer id. Thermal Card Printers in offices are linked to the office code recorded on the User Profile. If unable to see printer, go to the User Profile screen to update the office code. Click Continue. The message 'card print request successfully sent' is displayed. Go to Step 4
  • No, select 'No' and enter the reason for not thermally printing the card. The two options available are 'BasicsCard previously printed - workflow re-run' and 'Printer not available'. Select Continue. Collect the BasicsCard from the stock or use previously printed card if re-running the workflow. Go to Step 3

3

Thermal printing not available + Read more ...

When thermal card printing is unavailable manually write the cardholder's name on the back of the BasicsCard with a fine point permanent marker:

  • When the card is issued to the customer write the customer's first name. For example, if issuing a BasicsCard to Michelle, write 'Michelle' on the back of the BasicsCard
  • When the card is issued to a payment nominee, write the nominees first name followed by '(NOM)'. This will indicate to merchants that the cardholder is a payment nominee for an income managed customer(s) and is permitted to hold more than one BasicsCard. For example: If John is a nominee for Kate, the card will have John written on the back of the card followed by '(NOM)' that is, 'John (NOM)'
  • If a payment nominee is a nominee for more than one customer, the card will have the nominee's first name and then the customer's first name on the back of each card that is, 'John (NOM Kate)'

A Work Item will appear on the customer's record when a hand written card has been issued. This Work Item will auto complete once a thermally printed card is issued to the customer/nominee at a later contact when a thermal printer is available.

4

Issuing the BasicsCard + Read more ...

While the BasicsCard has been printed with the cardholder's details, it has not been activated on the customer's record. This can only occur when the BasicsCard is swiped on the Issue Card screen, or the card numbers entered manually if swipe facilities are unavailable. The card status will also not change in the Inventory Tracking System for the BasicsCard until it has been entered on the customer's record.

If BasicsCard is thermally printed

  • Obtain BasicsCard from Thermal Printer
  • Check the name on the card matches the system

Activate BasicsCard

Swipe card to activate, or enter card numbers manually if swipe facilities unavailable. Note: once card has been activated, it cannot be used for another customer.

5

Did the card swipe and print successfully? + Read more ...

  • Yes,
    • Select 'Yes' to 'Did the card print successfully and/or swipe successfully'?
    • The Inventory Tracking System will be updated to show card has been issued
    • Note: once card has been activated, it cannot be used for another customer
    • There is no longer a requirement to complete a BasicsCard Receipt when issuing a new or replacement BasicsCard
    • Go to Step 7
  • No,
    • If card was damaged or misprinted, select 'No' under 'Did the card print successfully and swipe successfully' as this will not activate the BasicsCard.
    • Select option for why BasicsCard did not print/swipe successfully from the drop down box. This will update the card status in the Inventory Tracking System and remove this card number from available stock. This is critical to ensure stock levels are accurate. Go to Step 6.

6

Reprinting the BasicsCard + Read more ...

Select 'Reprint'

  • Obtain BasicsCard from Thermal Printer
  • Check the name on the card matches the system
  • Swipe card to activate. Note: once card has been activated, it cannot be used for another customer. BasicsCard number can be entered manually when swipe facility is not available
  • Select 'Yes' to 'Did the card print successfully and swipe successfully?'
  • The Inventory Tracking System will be updated to show card has been issued
  • Select Continue

Note: there is no longer a requirement to complete a BasicsCard Receipt when issuing a new or replacement BasicsCard.

7

Balance on Receipt and EFTPOS terminal + Read more ...

Once the BasicsCard has been activated, the Service Officer will be prompted to offer the option of Balance on Receipt and EFTPOS terminal to the customer/payment nominee. This service is only available at BasicsCard Balance on Receipt merchants. If the customer/payment nominee consents to the offer and 'yes' is selected, then a 'consent statement' will appear.

The customer/payment nominee must read and accept the consent statement in order to 'opt in' to Balance on Receipt and terminal. Once the consent statement is accepted the workflow will continue.

If the customer/payment nominee chooses not to accept the offer then 'no' is selected and the workflow will continue.

Note: the offer of Balance on Receipt should be made to the individual (customer/payment nominee) who has the BasicsCard issued in their name.

8

Setting the BasicsCard four digit PIN + Read more ...

  • Advise customer/payment nominee to select a secure four digit Personal Identification Number (PIN) that they will remember. Advise them of the importance of selecting a PIN that others will not guess.
  • Provide keypad to customer. (If keypad is unavailable, PIN can be changed by customer using the keyboard.)
  • Customer enters PIN on keypad (out of view of Service Officer)
  • Customer reconfirms PIN
  • Select Continue and Finalise Activity

A DOC will be created automatically.

Note: if the payment nominee wishes to have the BasicsCard issued to the principal (customer), this will be included in the text.

See Step 1 in the Finalising the BasicsCard workflow table.

Note: when issuing a customer with their initial BasicsCard the customer’s allocation method will default to residual. To change this see, BasicsCard changing daily spend, maximum card balance limits and allocation method.

Finalising the BasicsCard

Finalising the BasicsCard offer workflow

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Review current deductions and expenses + Read more ...

Review the Expense Management Summary screen to review expenses.

Payment nominees

When a BasicsCard is issued to a payment nominee on behalf of a customer the payment nominee is responsible for managing the customer's income managed funds, including allocations to the BasicsCard. See Nominee arrangements under Income Management.

Third parties

Third Parties who are not payment nominees cannot be issued the BasicsCard on behalf of the customer.

All customers

The BasicsCard is another allocation method for income managed funds. Service Officers must review current expenses to ensure the customer's priority needs are met via appropriate allocation methods.

Customers are able to contact the Income Management Line seven days a week between 8am and 5pm local time to transfer funds to their BasicsCard.

Note: if the customer/payment nominee is requesting an immediate transfer or changes to their BasicsCard expenses, review current expenses to ensure the customer's priority needs are met via appropriate allocation methods.

Is an immediate or regular allocation required or need to be updated?

  • Yes, these must be set up via the Manage Expenses workflow. Historical BasicsCard regular expenses will be displayed to assist if required. For coding any BasicsCard expenses see Changes to Income Management priority needs. The interview needs to be completed first. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Review BasicsCard daily spend and maximum card balance limits + Read more ...

By navigating to the BasicsCard summary screen, a Service Officer can review a customer's current daily spent limit as well as their current maximum card balance.

Changing daily spend and maximum card balance limits on BasicsCard

Daily Spend Limit: A customer can request to set this limit between $20 and $1,500. If the BasicsCard daily spend limit is reached for a 24 hour period (midnight to midnight), the customer will be required to wait until midnight before the daily spend limit resets and additional purchases using their BasicsCard can be made.

Maximum Card Balance: A customer can request to set this limit between $100 and $3,000. A customer may request to lower the daily spend or maximum card balance limits of their BasicsCard, however, if that customer is deemed as 'vulnerable', appropriate referrals should be made. While there is no longer a requirement for a Service Officer to make contact with a Service Officer from the customer's local office or a senior officer involved in the initial decrease of the limits, Service Officer should ensure a full assessment of the customer's circumstances is undertaken and any appropriate referrals made.

Has the customer requested to change their BasicsCard limit or daily spend limit?

  • Yes, see BasicsCard changing daily spend and maximum card balance limits. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Review BasicsCard payment allocation method + Read more ...

By navigating to the BasicsCard summary screen, a Service Officer can review a customer's current allocation method. When issuing the initial BasicsCard to the customer the payment allocation method will default to the residual method.

Residual Method: Customers on the residual method will have all residual Income Managed funds from their IM account transferred directly to the BasicsCard up to the maximum card limit. Customers can opt-out of this method.

Partial Method: Customers will be able to have regular allocations set up to their BasicsCard from their IM account. If a customer receives a reduced ISP and has insufficient funds to cover all their expenses the system will calculate and pay the reduced amount to the BasicsCard once all the other expenses have been paid.

Has the customer requested to change the way funds are allocated to their BasicsCard?

  • Yes, see BasicsCard changing daily spend, maximum card balance limits and allocation method. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Provide further information to the customer/payment nominee about the BasicsCard + Read more ...

Customers being issued their first BasicsCard or being issued a replacement BasicsCard will benefit from the key messages around keeping the BasicsCard safe, not throwing the BasicsCard away and not giving the BasicsCard to anyone else, watch the BasicsCard Video On Demand (VOD) at the discretion of the Service Officer. This video contains important BasicsCard safety messages and is available in a range of translations. If a Service Officer feels that it is culturally insensitive to play this VOD to a particular customer they should refrain from playing it and provide the messages verbally.

If it is appropriate to play the VOD set Lync connection to 'Do Not Disturb' while running through the VOD with the customer, as messages received through instant messaging can interrupt play and may display on screen.

5

BasicsCard Customer Information Pack + Read more ...

Issue customer with BasicsCard Customer Information Pack and use this to cover the following topics:

  • How to obtain BasicsCard balances via:
    • the BasicsCard Balance Enquiries Line or the Income Management Line 24 hours a day 7 days a week. Service Officers should demonstrate the BasicsCard Enquiries Line and the customer should also use the phone and successfully hear their BasicsCard balance by entering their BasicsCard number and PIN when prompted before leaving the Centrelink Service Centre. Service Officers need to ensure customers understand how this service works and that they should obtain their card balance before they shop
    • see the Resources page for a link to the customer 13 and 1800 numbers, which includes the contact details for the BasicsCard Balance Enquiries Line and the Income Management Line
    • the online BasicsCard balance enquiry service, including a mobile version, via the Services Australia Website (servicesaustralia.gov.au/basicscard)
    • registered online accounts (Web and IVR)
    • BasicsCard kiosk (where available)
  • Customers can transfer income managed funds to their BasicsCard:
    • between 8:00 am to 5:00 pm local time, 7 days a week by contacting the Income Management Line
    • using online and telephone self service, with an active self service registration. For more information see Transfer income managed funds to BasicsCard online
  • Discuss with customers how to report lost/stolen/damaged BasicsCards and their liability for funds lost prior to reporting. Reinforce with customer 24 hour reporting of lost/stolen BasicsCards via the Income Management Line and the importance of protecting their PIN and how to change their PIN
  • How to identify a BasicsCard merchant and where the BasicsCard can be used including National BasicsCard merchants
  • If required print BasicsCard listing of the available merchants in the customer's area from Office Locator and give to the customer. Customers can access information on organisations where they can use their BasicsCard using the Business and Organisation search tool located on the Services Australia Website (servicesaustralia.gov.au/basicscard). See the Resources page for a link to the Organisation Finder service

Steps to take when a dispute occurs. See BasicsCard dispute resolution.

  • Discuss priority needs and how they are met through the various allocation methods. See Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management
  • Complete checklist to ensure all aspects of the card issue has been addressed. Service Officers should reinforce the key messages from the BasicsCard VOD

Note: if the BasicsCard Customer Information Pack is not available, the BasicsCard customer terms and conditions must be downloaded from Services Australia Website and provided to the customer, See the Resources page for a link to the BasicsCard terms and conditions.

Does customer have a nominee + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, procedure ends here

7

Customer has nominee + Read more ...

If a person is a payment nominee:

  • They can make multiple purchase transactions and pay for each one individually using a different BasicsCard
  • Where a BasicsCard has been issued to a payment nominee, the payment nominee should advise the customer of the changes and destroy any existing card if the customer is not present
  • Remind the nominee that they must act in the best interest of the customer when using the BasicsCard and income managed funds must only be used for the benefit of the customer and their dependents
  • An auto DOC will be completed that will include details regarding who the BasicsCard has been assigned to. This may be the payment nominee or the principal if the payment nominee has chosen to have the BasicsCard issued in the principal's name

Updates coded manually + Read more ...

All BasicsCard issues are to be updated in workflow. Manual updates are to be done only when workflow is unavailable. If the workflow is available see Step 3 in the Initial contact - Making an initial BasicsCard offer table.

Manual updates

The Centrelink system will not allow cardholder's name to be thermally printed and will not automatically update the Inventory Tracking System (ITS). Updating the ITS will be completed via a monthly reconciliation process where the system will compare any active or assigned ITS cards within Centrelink system and will update the status in the ITS to 'issued' automatically.
