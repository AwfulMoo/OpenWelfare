4

Keywords + Read more ...

Check the Keywords that are associated with the claim by selecting Keyword

Keywords that may be applicable to Parenting Payment include:

  • SHARED, meaning the customer shares the care of the children and therefore, a care assessment may be required, and
  • URGVULN. If the claim displays the URGVULN keyword, the customer has indicated in the claim that they are experiencing one of the following:
    • Affected by Family and domestic violence
    • Have been in prison or psychiatric confinement for 14 days or more
    • Are a humanitarian entrant in Australia for the first time
    • Had to leave their usual home because of an extreme circumstance

Service Officers should attempt to contact the customer to discuss their crisis circumstance. Customers with the URGVULN keyword can submit their Parenting Payment claim without submitting any of the requested documents. They have 14 days to do this once the claim is submitted.

Relationships details + Read more ...

These details must be reviewed/updated before selecting Process.

Relationship details are viewed on the Transaction Summary (TS). The:

  • Link Summary (LS) table displays all known relationships to the customer including relationships with children
  • Marital Status (MS) table will display the martial status of the customer

Although the linking/unlinking are done via different screens, that is, MS or LS the process is the same for the partner and child.

Single

  • Verification of separation for new PP claimants is generally not required, unless the customer has advised they are:
    • separated under one roof (SUOR), see Member of a Couple (MoC) and Separated under one roof (SUOR) assessments, or
    • sharing accommodation, see Member of a Couple (MoC) and Separated under one roof (SUOR) assessments
  • If required, the MoC/SUOR assessment needs to be undertaken as per Member of a Couple (MoC) and Separated under one roof (SUOR) assessments before the claim is finalised

Note: do not issue Relationship Details Separated Under One Roof (SS293) to an ex-partner if the customer indicates in the claim there are safety concerns if forms are issued. Ensure if the customer has a separate postal address, that forms to the customer are only sent to the postal address.

Partnered

Partner details must be verified before finalising the online claim or ACC.

This is not required if the customer has lodged a Parenting Payment paper claim (SC277) or a MOD P which has been signed by both the customer and their partner.

For information, see Change in relationship status from single to partnered and Confirming a partnered relationship.

Relationship details may only need updating if the tables contain different information to the information provided in the claim. Note: relationship data should only be deleted or edited if recorded in error. If the relationship data is no longer applicable (for example, the customer has become married), add a new line.

For help, see the Resources page of Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions. It links to the Process Direct Entry hub/support page where Relationship updates are located.

Any children not already linked to the customer are to be linked via the LS screen.

If the relationships details are updated after processing has been started, the claim will need to be regenerated by selecting > Regenerate Claim.

Check child details + Read more ...

The following details within the claim must be checked against information held on the customer’s record for each child listed within the claim before proceeding, as the care assessment will determine the customer’s qualification for Parenting Payment in some cases:

  • Child in care
  • Child no longer in care
  • Percentage of care
  • Principal care status

Where there is a change notified for any of the above for any child listed within the claim:

  • a care assessment and/or principal carer determination is required where:
    • there is a change in the percentage of shared care, or
    • the care is disputed or disagreed, or
    • a child other than a newborn has come in to the customer’s care
  • updates will be required when processing the claim via the Child task selector where:
    • a child has left care, or
    • a reduction in the percentage of care for a child. If the reduction of care is not confirmed, code the reduction as a preliminary decision

If unable to make a change of care or shared care determination as not suitably trained or care is disputed, use the referral under more options for Assessment of Care.

Streamline rejection + Read more ...

Full claim coding is not required if the claim is to be rejected because of one of the following reasons:

  • FSD - Failed to Supply Documents. If the customer has intentionally provided incorrect information, (for example, completely unrelated documents or images) the claim must be rejected. Note: before rejecting FSD check to see if a vulnerable keyword is associated with the claim. Customers have 14 days from the date of submission to submit required documents.
    If the required documents were not requested within the claim, verbally or via RFI the claim must not be streamline rejected
  • NDE - No Dependents eligible. If the customer has no dependent children named in the claim as being in their care. Ensure a care assessment has been made where relevant
  • CO6 - Youngest child over 6. If the customers youngest child named in the claim as being in their care is over the age of 6 years old. Ensure a care assessment has been made where relevant

Note: Service Officers may use discretion when to apply a streamline, manual or automatic rejection. An automatic rejection is the preferred option. See Rejecting a Parenting Payment (PP) claim.

Action previous income support payment + Read more ...

Check start day/arrears + Read more ...

If the customer is claiming within 4 weeks of becoming qualified due to the birth of a child or a child entering their care as a dependent, PPP can be backdated. The PGA system should automatically backdate the claim start date to the child's date of birth/date child came into care. The start day is displayed on the PPP Assessment (PGAS) screen which can been pre-selected from the PPP Task Selection on the SWE screen or using the Super Key.

For more information about the start date for PP, see Start day for PP.

If the customer's partner also claimed an income support payment, see Calculating the start day for a claim made after partner's claim.

If the customer was getting another income support payment before claiming PP:

  • If they contact within 28 days of the child's date of birth, the start date is the date of birth. If notification is outside 28 days, the start date is:
    • the notification date (the date the customer claims PP), or
    • the date they advised an intention to claim, if they are considered a vulnerable customer and they lodged the claim within 14 days of the intent to claim being recorded
  • If they advise a change in care within 28 days of a PP child entering care and principal carer status has been verified, the start date is the date the child entered their care. If notification is outside 28 days, the start date is the notification date
  • If paid their previous income support payment since the PPP start day, the system should adjust PPP arrears to deduct the amount already paid. By coding the previous payment type in the BTR screen (on the ELD screen), the system will load the default details onto the screen. For coding information see Debts resulting from internal transfers and clearances
  • If their previous participation payment has been cancelled or suspended as a result of non-compliance, PPP is not payable before serving the remaining financial penalty. For example, they had an Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP), serious failure or a non-compliance cancellation. For more information, see Deferment, non-payment period or rate reduction period for PPP
  • If the start date is to be later than the contact date (CCD) or deemed contact date (DDC), code the later start date with reason Payability and remove any previous start date.
    The only circumstances where this should apply is where a customer claims PPP after they or their partner ceased work and expect the final employment income to be paid after the date they submitted their claim. Where the expected income would preclude PPP from being paid, the start date for PPP is to be advanced to the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) of the first period that PPP becomes payable, provided this is within 13 weeks after the date of claim.
    Note: if the start date is in the future of the system date, hold the claim until the start date for reason 'Future Transfer' and noting the information in the Progress of Claim (PoC) DOC
