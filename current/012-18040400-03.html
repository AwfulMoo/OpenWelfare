This page contains information for Registering Officers (RO) on completing the MI registration worksheets for claims made under the High Cost Claim Indemnity Scheme (HCCS), Run-Off Cover Indemnity Scheme (ROCS) and Incurred But Not Reported Indemnity Scheme (IBNR).

On this Page:

HCCS registration worksheet

ROCS and ROCS/HCCS registration worksheets

IBNR and IBNR/HCCS registration worksheets

HCCS registration worksheet

Registration worksheet for HCCS applications and handover

Table 1: This table explains how Registering Officers (RO) are to complete the HCCS registration worksheet for initial and subsequent applications.

Step

Action

1

Open the HCCS registration worksheet + Read more ...

The HCCS registration worksheet is a summary document completed by the RO which provides a breakdown of the financial details of the application.

Open the PDF template HCCS_Registration Worksheet located in the share drive folder at Medical Indemnity > HCCS :

  • select ‘save as’ and title the document 'HCCS_Registration Worksheet_[ARN]’ in the relevant ARN folder. For example 'HCCS_Registration Worksheet _ARN1500-1A-H '
  • refer to the HCCS application to complete most of the following fields on page 1:
    • Linked ARNs. Note: if 2 claims or more MI relate to the same incident, they are classified as linked and need to be referenced in the appropriate Linked Files field
    • ARN
    • Claim Ref number
    • Date received
    • Dr Name – [Dr] [Initial] [Surname]
    • Member ID
  • from the dropdown menu, select Specialist field. See the Resources page for Specialist codes for HCCS and IBNR
  • key the date of incident (DOI) and date of notification (DON). Note: check the application form. If the DOI is a date range add it into the Comments field and leave the DOI start date in the DOI field. See the Resources page for more information on Date of Incident (DOI) and the Date of Notification (DON)
  • in the Comments field, add any comments that may assist the Assessing Officer (AO) in their assessment of determining if the MI application is payable

Note: once the HCCS registration worksheet is completed, there may be further comments that need to be added.

2

Complete financial details + Read more ...

Complete the following:

  • in the Financial Summary table, key the Total Claim Cost in the Prelim total field. Note: this information is located on the HCCS application
  • in the Less HCC threshold field, based on the DON, select the relevant HCCS threshold from the dropdown menu:
    • $2 million for claims notified between 1 January 2003 and 21 October 2003
    • $500,000 for claims notified between 22 October 2003 and 31 December 2003
    • $300,000 for claims notified between 1 January 2004 and 30 June 2018
    • $500,000 for claims notified on or after 1 July 2018
    • Note: for a subsequent application select N/A
  • the Subtotal is equal to the Prelim total minus the HCCS Threshold. Note: once this amount is keyed, the HCCS Percentage Calculation amount will auto populate. For a subsequent application, the Prelim Total and Subtotal will equal the same
  • key the Total HCCS amount being claimed in this application. This is the amount claimable under HCCS as listed on the application

3

Complete costs + Read more ...

Complete each of the cost fields. If the relevant amount has not been provided, leave this field blank on the worksheet.

Complete the following:

  • in the Settlement/judgment costs field, key the total amount listed on the HCCS application. Use the HCCS Calculation spreadsheet to key the HCCS Percentage (C) (%) amount, see amount in the HCCS settlement field
  • in the Plaintiff/Claimant Legal costs, key the total amount listed on the HCCS application. Use the HCCS Calculation spreadsheet to key the HCCS Percentage(C) (%) amount, see amount in the HCCS Plaintiff field
  • in the Defence legal costs, key the total amount on the HCCS application. Use the HCCS Calculation Spreadsheet to key the HCCS Percentage (C) (%) amount, see the HCCS Defence field

Once these fields have been completed, the worksheet will automatically populate the Total Amount Sought, Sum of all HCCS amounts and will equal the Amount claimable under HCCS on the application.

4

Complete registration worksheet questions + Read more ...

On page 2, answer all Questions 1-5 by ticking the Yes or No checkbox on the HCCS worksheet.

Note: key any relevant comments in the spaces provided. This information may come from various areas such as payment application, HP Record Manager, generate reports and the Expense Reconciliation, see Medical Indemnity (MI) Schemes registration.

For Question 1, check the apportionment/cost-sharing section of the HCCS application to confirm if there is an apportionment in relation to this claim. If apportionment:

  • is not indicated, select the No checkbox
  • is indicated, select the Yes checkbox and key the details in the comments including the doctor’s name and apportionment percentage breakdown

For Question 2, confirm if the applicant received or may be entitled to receive a payment from another source (including third party contribution amount(s)) in relation to the claim arising from the incident(s). If the applicant has received monies from another avenue (for example a hospital or another insurer) and this amount has not been deducted from the amount claimed, note this amount here. If the answer is:

  • Yes, select the checkbox and key the amount to be deducted from this application
  • No, select the checkbox

For Question 3, check whether any part of the judgment or settlement amount is not payable under HCCS. Check if any third party contributions have been made toward the application (for example a clinic or hospital):

  • if so, select the Yes checkbox and note the details in the Comments field
  • if not, select the No checkbox

For Question 4, refer to the completed Expense Reconciliation Calculator confirm if the HCCS amount sought reconciles with the supporting documentation and all the necessary evidence been provided to support the payment amount. If the amount:

  • does reconcile, tick the Yes check box
  • does not reconcile, provide a comment for the reason and tick the No checkbox

For Question 5, refer to the completed reports for this application to confirm that the member is ROCS eligible (see the Undertake system checks table in Medical Indemnity (MI) Schemes registration). If the ROCS Report indicates that the member:

  • is not eligible for the ROCS scheme tick the No checkbox and key the DON related to this claim
  • is eligible, tick the Yes check box and key the Date of the Notification

RO to complete details:

  • Name:
  • Date:
  • Staff ID:
  • Position:
  • Contact Number:
  • Signature: (once printed)

5

Complete check list before handover to an Assessing Officer (AO) + Read more ...

ROs are to use the final page of the registration worksheet checklist to confirm that all areas of the registration worksheet have been completed. This will ensure the RO has checked their own work and all information has been entered correctly. Select the relevant checkbox as necessary:

  • key the RO logon number
  • complete the relevant checkboxes to highlight any issues that occurred during registration of the application
  • note any comments/Issues that occurred during registration in the space provided
  • Save and print the worksheet
  • hole punch the printed registration worksheet document

Note: ROs will be required to correct any registration errors submitted to AOs.

6

Prepare claim for handover to AO + Read more ...

To prepare the claim for handover to an AO:

  • open the Claims on Hand spreadsheet for the relevant year, for example 'Claims on Hand 2016-2017' and select the tab for the appropriate month
  • update the ARN field to correspond with the application just registered. Note: check all other details are correct on the worksheet
  • update the Status field of the application to Registered
  • add the following printed documents to the paper file in the order below:
    • registration checklist
    • registration worksheet
    • HCCS calculation spreadsheet
    • Expense reconciliation calculator
    • SAS report
    • ROCS report
    • UMPSP report (for IBNR claims)
  • sign the registration worksheet and Expense Reconciliation Calculator
  • folio each page, start from the last number in the application
  • place a blue tab on the registration worksheet near the Financial Summary heading
  • update the Claims on Hand spreadsheet in the shared drive (see the Resources page)

Handover to an AO by placing the paper file in the Assessment drawer.

ROs must ensure all prior registration steps have been completed and any HCCS application discrepancies/issues noted for the AO to consider. Once handed over, an AO will assess the HCCS application for eligibility for payment using the High Cost Claim Indemnity Scheme (HCCS) assessment worksheets.

Registration process ends here.

ROCS/HCCS registration worksheet responses Questions 1-4 + Read more ...

On page 2 of the registration worksheet, answer Questions 1-4:

  • key the Total Amount Claimed as listed on the ROCS application
  • select the relevant HCCS threshold by ticking the appropriate checkbox
  • in the Breakdown of the HCCS Indemnity Scheme Payment amount sought, key the financial details relevant to the HCC scheme only. Note: use the Calculation Spreadsheet to assist with the figures required
  • the Prelim total is the total amount paid by the insurer, refer to field L of the ROCS application
  • select the relevant HCCS threshold from the drop down menu. Note: for a subsequent application select N/A
  • key the subtotal. This amount is the Prelim total (field L) minus the HCCS threshold amount selected. Note: use the Calculation Spreadsheet to assist with the figures required
  • the Total HCCS amount being claimed in this application is equal to the subtotal divided by 50%
  • the HCCS Percentage will automatically populate once the totals have been keyed
  • HCCS Settlement or judgment costs. Use the ROCS/HCCS Calculation Spreadsheet to determine the HCCS percentage amount
  • in field 'D', key the corresponding amount as per the Calculation Spreadsheet
  • HCCS Plaintiff/Claimant legal. Use the ROCS/HCCS Calculation Spreadsheet to determine the HCCS percentage amount
  • in field 'E' key the corresponding amount as per the Calculation Spreadsheet
  • HCCS Defence legal costs. Use the ROCS/HCCS Calculation Spreadsheet to determine the HCCS percentage amount
  • in field 'F' key the corresponding amount as per the Calculation Spreadsheet

The Sum of all HCCS amount will auto-populate and the amount cannot be altered.

  • key the ROCS Settlement or judgment costs. Note: the total Settlement or judgment figure will auto populate when the settlement figure is keyed in the HCCS section
  • key the ROCS Plaintiff/claimant legal costs. Note: the total Plaintiff/Claimant legal costs figure will auto populate when the plaintiff costs are keyed in the HCCS section
  • key the ROCS Defence costs. Note: the total Defence legal costs figure will auto populate when the defence costs are keyed in the HCCS section
  • key the combined total ROCS/HCCS (total application cost) in ROCS CHF and then calculate 5% of the total in field 'J'

For Question 7, worksheet will be auto-populated.

For Question 8, check if there an apportionment in relation to this claim. Refer to the Apportionment/Cost-sharing section of the application to locate the answer:

Complete first Quality Assurance check for handover to an Assessing Officer (AO) + Read more ...

To prepare the claim for handover to an AO:

  • open the Claims on Hand spreadsheet for the relevant year, for example 'Claims on Hand 2016-2017' and select the tab for the appropriate month
  • update the ARN field to correspond with the application just registered. Note: check all other details are correct on the spreadsheet
  • update the Status field of the application to Registered
  • add the following printed documents to the paper file in the order below:
    • registration checklist
    • registration worksheet
    • HCCS calculation spreadsheet
    • Expense Reconciliation Calculator
    • SAS Report
    • IBNR Report
    • UMPSP Report (for IBNR claims)
  • sign the registration worksheet and Expense Reconciliation Calculator
  • folio each page, start from the last number in the application
  • place a blue tab on the registration worksheet near the Financial Summary heading
  • update the Claims on Hand spreadsheet in the shared drive (see the Resources page)

Handover to an AO by placing the paper file in the Assessment drawer.

Note: ROs will be required to correct any registration errors submitted to AOs.

ROs must ensure all prior registration steps have been completed and any discrepancies/issues noted for the AO to consider. Once handed over, an AO will assess the claim for eligibility for payment using the Incurred But Not Reported Indemnity Scheme (IBNR) assessment worksheets.
