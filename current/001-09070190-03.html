This page contains information on managing compulsory Quarterly Participation Interviews (QTIs) for Partial Capacity to Work (PCW) and Temporary Reduced Work Capacity (TRWC) (0-7 or 8-14 hours per week) job seekers.

On this page:

Triggering and recording a QTI requirement for a job seeker with PCW or TRWC

Completing a QTI for job seekers with a PCW or TRWC

Triggering and recording a QTI requirement for a job seeker with PCW or TRWC

Table 1

Step

Action

1

QTI + Read more ...

A QTI is a compulsory activity for job seekers with an assessed:

  • PCW of 0-7 or 8-14 hours per week, or
  • TRWC of 0-7 or 8-14 hours per week that extends beyond 12 weeks

A job seeker will be identified as requiring QTIs when an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) report has been accepted and recorded by the agency and the job seeker is assessed as having either a PCW or TRWC of 0-14 hours per week, including TRWC for less than 12 weeks in duration.

2

Triggering the QTI requirement + Read more ...

  • The ESAt report is accepted, triggering the 12 week count for the QTI. Note: the ESAt report will have a 'submitted' status for 28 days but this does not affect the commencement of QTI requirements. The job seeker's work capacity and QTI requirements are effective from the date the ESAt is accepted
  • If the job seeker has already lodged a medical certificate, follow the process to assess the medical certificate and update the Medical Certificate (MC) screen to either grant or not grant a Temporary Incapacity (INP) exemption

An automatic letter is generated for the job seeker advising of their mutual obligation requirements, and the requirement to negotiate a Job Plan with the agency.

3

An appointment is automatically booked + Read more ...

If the job seeker does not have an exemption from their mutual obligation requirements (for example, INP) with an end date more than 2 weeks in the future, an appointment will be automatically booked using the 'Quarterly Interview' appointment type.

This appointment is to ensure that the QTI is coded on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen and the Job Plan updated.

If job seeker has an exemption for at least 2 weeks, their QTI will be included in their Job Plan when the exemption ends.

4

Conducting the QTI requirement appointment + Read more ...

Phone interview pre-call notification

Consideration should be given to sending a pre-call notification using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) for job seekers with a phone QTI appointment. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging.

Does the job seeker attend the appointment?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 8

5

A Job Plan needs to be negotiated with the job seeker and the AEX screen coded + Read more ...

  • Launch the Service Profiling Update Workflow. The workflow can be launched directly from the booked appointment by selecting the 'execute' button, or via the Workspace page in Customer First
  • Discuss the new mutual obligation requirements as a result of the ESAt assessment with the job seeker
  • Check the customer's reporting requirement and process if required

Is the job seeker fully meeting their requirements through paid work or self-employment within their assessed bandwidth hours?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Job seeker is fully meeting requirements through paid work + Read more ...

Are details of the job seeker's paid work or self-employment activities included in their Job Plan and correctly recorded on the AEX screen?

  • Yes,
    • The job seeker does not have QTI requirements while they are fully meeting their requirements through work or self-employment
    • Advise the job seeker they must notify the agency of any reduction in their working hours or if they cease work. If this happens, they will be required to attend QTIs with the agency for the duration of their PCW or TRWC
  • No,
    • Update the customer's Job Plan and code the AEX screen with the appropriate activity details for paid work (PTW) or self-employment (SEL)
    • Advise the job seeker they must notify the agency of any reduction in their working hours or if they cease work. At that time, they will be required to attend QTIs with Services Australia for the duration of their PCW or TRWC
    • See Negotiating Job Plans for coding processes

Procedure ends here.

7

Job seeker is not meeting requirements through paid work + Read more ...

The job seeker will have QTI requirements for the duration of their PCW or TRWC.

If a job seeker continues to participate in DES, the Job Plan should not be updated by the agency. The DES provider will act on the coding of the activity on the AEX screen and will contact the job seeker to discuss whether the job seeker will keep participating in DES or not. If, after talking with their DES provider, a job seeker chooses to exit DES (and is eligible to exit), the Job Plan must be updated by Services Australia at that time.

If the job seeker has volunteered with a service provider and the provider has incorrectly removed the compulsory QTI activity from the Job Plan, the provider must be contacted to discuss adding the QTI back into the plan.

Note: incorrectly updating a job plan for a DES participant who has decided to continue with a DES provider will inadvertently suspend the customer from the provider.

Update the job seeker's Job Plan with the appropriate compulsory activity.

  • For PCW or TRWC longer than 12 weeks, add the 'Attend Quarterly Appointment' activity
  • For TRWC less than 12 weeks, add the 'Reduced Capacity Requirements' activity
  • For all PCW and TRWC (including TRWC of less than 12 weeks in duration) update the AEX screen with the Quarterly Participation Interview (QTI) code:
    • Use a start date equal to the date the Job Plan was signed
    • Use an end date equal to the latest end date recorded on the Work Capacity (WC) screen. This will be the TRWC end date or ESAt acceptance date plus 2 years for PCW job seekers

Procedure ends here.

8

Manual suspension of payments + Read more ...

As this is the job seeker's only compulsory activity, they must have any outstanding QTIs completed. If a job seeker fails to attend their QTI, their payment must be suspended to prompt contact with the agency.

Face to face appointments - Service Officers must:

  • Ensure the appointment is finalised as 'Did Not Attend' in the Centrelink Appointment System
  • Make one attempt to contact via their nominated phone number
  • If the customer cannot be contacted, their payment should be suspended with the reason 'Failed to Attend Interview' (FAI). See the Suspension process for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance (YA) job seeker table of Suspension of JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance (YA), for suspension coding information
  • Do not finalise the Service Profiling Update Workflow. The Service Component is to remain 'STA'rted so that the workflow can be run, and Service Component completed, when the job seeker eventually attends

Phone appointments - Service Officers must:

  • Try to contact the job seeker at the scheduled appointment time
  • If unable to contact the job seeker on the first attempt, try again approximately 15 minutes later
  • If unable to contact the job seeker after these attempts, ensure the appointment is finalised as 'Did Not Attend' in the Centrelink Appointment System
  • Include on a DOC sufficient detail about the outbound contact attempts
  • Payment should be suspended with the reason FAI (failed to attend interview. For help with coding, see the Suspension process for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance (YA) job seeker table
  • Do not finalise the Service Profiling Update Workflow. The Service Component is to remain 'STA'rted so that the workflow can be run, and Service Component completed, once the job seeker attends

If the job seeker contacts and states their QTI has not been conducted at the scheduled time, review the customer's record for DOCs about previous contact attempts. A QTI may need to be conducted while the job seeker is on the phone, or it may be necessary to reschedule. If uncertain, refer to a Service Support Officer (SSO).

Procedure ends here.

Completing a QTI for job seekers with a PCW or TRWC

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Job seeker attends QTI + Read more ...

Conduct the QTI by launching the Service Profiling Update Workflow. The workflow can be launched directly from the booked appointment by selecting the 'Execute' button, or via the Workspace page in Customer First.

2

Conduct QTI + Read more ...

During the interview discuss:

  • Whether the ESAt assessment is still current and valid, or if the customer's circumstances have changed
  • If the job seeker advises a change in circumstances, re-run the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) and refer for a new ESAt if appropriate, including where:
    • there has been a change in circumstances
    • the QTI activity has expired for a job seeker with a TRWC and the job seeker is likely to be eligible for QTI, as it appears they still have a work capacity of less than 15 hours per week
  • Discuss assistance recommendations from their last ESAt/Job Capacity Assessment (JCA)
  • Look at social participation options where the job seeker has been assessed as having a TRWC
  • Encourage voluntary job search, including voluntary participation with a jobactive, Disability Employment Services (DES) or Community Development Program (CDP) provider. Note: voluntary participation with a provider does not end the QTI requirement
  • Discuss and assess any employment the job seeker may be doing as paid employment may meet the job seeker's mutual obligation requirements and they may no longer be required to attend a QTI
  • Discuss voluntary work and other community assistance options
  • Provide information on the benefits of work (such as Working Credit and Mobility Allowance)
  • Outline the benefits of planning for the future and explain assistance services like Financial Information Services
  • Outline the disadvantages of long term income support dependence, and
  • Explore eligibility for other income support payments
  • If the job seeker has a TRWC, check when this period ends as the job seeker may no longer be required to attend QTIs and will need to re-engage with a jobactive, DES or CDP provider. A referral will be required when the TRWC period ends
  • Check the job seeker knows the date of their next quarterly participation interview and when other reviews or appointments will be

Select the appropriate radio button once a discussion topic is addressed.

3

Complete QTI + Read more ...

After the appointment is complete, finalise the Service Profiling Update Workflow. This will automatically update the status of the Service Component to 'COM' (completed).
