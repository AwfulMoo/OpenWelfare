Northern Territory (NT) participants + Read more ...

From 17 March 2021, Income Management (IM) customers in the NT can consent to transition from IM to the CDC.

Participants can only apply to exit the CDC using a CDC exit application, once they have commenced on the CDC program.

When an NT customer is on the CDC program and no longer on IM

When an NT customer provides consent to transition to CDC, they will be issued a notice. It will then generally take 28 days for them to be a CDC participant, however they can also transition on the day they request to be a CDC participant. The notice will specify a start date that the customer will become a CDC participant. This notice can be viewed on the History Summary (HS) screen.

The date a participant is on the CDC program can also be confirmed on the Q2AS screen by the Percentage DOE field Check the CDCS screen in Process direct to determine the restricted percentage.

Follow the exit process if the participant has commenced on the CDC program.

Consideration of Disability and Caring Responsibilities + Read more ...

The Delegate must consider the impact of a CDC participant’s disability or non-parental caring responsibilities on their ability to demonstrate reasonable and responsible management of their affairs, as it relates to the assessment criteria.

If a participant is unable to demonstrate this, the Delegate can still make a determination that the participant is to be exited from the CDC program, if the Delegate is satisfied the participant has reasonable and responsible management of their affairs.

Service Officers will need to clearly document how this provision applies in the Delegate Report.

This provision will be assessed on a case-by-case basis, and will need to be supported by evidence. It is not enough for the participant to be receiving a carer or disability support payment, they will be required to demonstrate how their disability or caring responsibilities impact their ability to show they can manage their affairs against each assessment criteria.

This may be through the inability to provide the supporting information required by the form, or where the participant transfers funds to another person to meet their primary needs.

Evidence that could be considered under this provision may include information recorded on the participant’s record including, but not limited to:

  • assessment and / or receipt of Disability Support Payment, Carers payments, Mobility Allowance, exemption from mutual obligation due to temporary incapacity (including when they have a job to return to)
  • medical documentation confirming the participant or the person the participant has caring responsibility for has a disability or is elderly
  • NDIA assessment and funded supports, including if the participant has a self-managed or agency managed NDIA plan
  • any other evidence that would satisfy the Delegate that the participant’s disability or caring responsibilities has a sufficient impact against the assessment criteria

The Service Officer should:

  • check the participant’s record for any disability/carer evidence, including customer summary, DL and documents
  • speak to the participant during the interview about how their disability or carer responsibilities impact their day-to-day life and how they use their card to manage their responsibilities
  • when completing the delegate report, note the disability or carer responsibilities, and how their circumstances support or impact their ability to meet each criteria.
    • For example:
      if a participant cannot show they can manage their financial affairs due to numerous account overdraws, or high percentiles for declined transactions, but this is because they are needing to purchase medication, this should not weigh negatively
      if a participant (Carer) cannot show they can manage their financial affairs (in section 5), however are maintaining NDIS allocated funding for their care receiver well, the criteria should not weigh negatively. The reasons for why the criteria has been met, and the participant’s carer situation should be explained in each question

Assessment Criteria 1-3 considerations

The following criteria should be considered when assessing an exit application:

Assessment Criteria 1: The interests of any children + Read more ...

When considering the interests of children, the participant has responsibility of children if:

  • they are receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB),
  • their partner is receiving FTB for the family unit,
  • the participant’s care arrangements are more than 35%, if it is less than 35% of care, the level of responsibility will be assessed.

If the care is less than 35%, they will be assessed accordingly against their care arrangements and responsibilities for the children. If the participant’s partner is the receiver of FTB, the interest of any children still needs to be assessed even if the partner pays for all expenses relating to the children. The arrangements should clearly be described in the delegate report.

When considering the interests of children, assess five main components:

  • Engagement in age appropriate socialisation activities
  • Evidence of Child Protection authority involvement
  • Evidence that finances are directed towards needs of children
  • If in principal care, that dependents meet immunisation requirements
  • Evidence that participant is meeting their Child Support obligations.

The family dynamics and who pays for what need to be considered with regard to the needs of the children. This is particularly important when assessing a partner who does not receive FTB or in shared care arrangements. Refer to the Cashless Debit Card Exits Guiding Principles in Resources for more information.

Assessment Criteria 2: Convicted of an offence or serving imprisonment + Read more ...

The ‘last 12 months’ period should be taken from the Date of the Decision, offence or imprisonment, not the date of Application or Assessment.

Process Direct will identify when a referral must be made for a National police check. This will be completed by the WQPM team.

It is important when checking for Crisis Payments to identify that the payment was made for PRI (Prison Release) and not for other circumstances.

Note: for serious offences, it is important to note any convictions and/or imprisonment within the past 5 years. Refer to the Cashless Debit Card Exits Guiding Principles in Resources for more information.

Assessment Criteria 3: Risk of Homelessness + Read more ...

When considering the participant’s circumstances, look at the previous 12 months and their experience of homelessness or when they may have been at risk of homelessness.

Considerations:

  • If the participant lives with a family member in a stable situation and contributes to housing by paying bills/and or food, then the participant needs to provide evidence of payments being made towards this
  • If the participant transfers funds to another person to meet housing costs, then the participant needs to provide evidence that these costs are being met. For example, rental receipts or rental agreements
  • If the participant is staying with a family member/friend but is sleeping on the couch ('couch surfing') then this would not be considered stable and would weigh negatively on the outcome
  • 12 weeks or less, or no agreed time frame would indicate temporary/short term housing, which would weigh negatively on the assessment
  • If the participant says they pay rent in a private arrangement but there is no evidence of regular and consistent payments, this will weigh negatively on their assessment

Go to Step 6

6

Assessment criteria 4-6 considerations

Assessment Criteria 4: Health and Safety + Read more ...

When assessing the Health and Safety of the individual and the community, consider the following:

  • History of alcohol or drug misuse, or problematic gambling
  • Negative impact on community health and safety (anti-social behaviour, serious offences etc) in the last 12 months
  • Respondent or Defendant to a Family Violence Order or other Protection Order in last 12 months.

Note: for serious offences, it is important to note any convictions and/or imprisonment within the past 5 years. Refer to the Cashless Debit Card Exits Guiding Principles in Resources for more information.

Assessment Criteria 5: Responsibilities and Circumstances + Read more ...

When assessing the participant’s financial capability, determine if the participant is able to manage and be safe from exploitation in the absence of a CDC.

Process Direct provides guidance via a set of interview questions and evidence based indicators to assess from the participant’s record:

  • The participant’s money management or budgeting practices
  • Absence of money mismanagement
  • Absence of financial exploitation.

Money Management

When assessing the participant’s money management strategies and budgeting practices, apply a holistic assessment to their circumstances and ensure they are meeting their and their children’s needs on a regular basis.

The participant should demonstrate they are applying appropriate monetary resources to meet their and their family’s needs as well as having stable or regular patterns of payment.

Financial Capability

To support the delegate in making an informed decision on a participant’s money management or financial capability, an understanding of the discretionary nature of these assessments is required to be made using a broad range of information and evidence available.

The following types of data and supporting documents/evidence are available to analyse and assess:

  • Bank statements
  • Credit or store statements
  • Card Provider Data
  • Transactions/transfers
  • Declined transactions
  • Cohort percentiles

Debts

When considering a participant’s debt/s, it is important to consider the affordability of their debts and ability to meet repayments consistently.

If there is a significant pattern of regular access to loans for the purpose of everyday purchases (e.g. groceries), this may indicate financial vulnerability and should be explored during the interview.

Financial Exploitation

Financial exploitation is defined as a person being subject to undue pressure, harassment, violence, abuse, deception or exploitation for resources by another person, or people, including family members and community members.

If there is evidence of another person frequently taking or requesting money from the participant, this may indicate financial vulnerability and should be explored during the interview.

Refer to the Cashless Debit Card Guiding Principles in Resources for more information.

Assessment Criteria 6: Engagement in the community + Read more ...

If the participant is in receipt of a working age payment and has mutual obligations, confirmation that they are meeting their mutual obligations as set out in their Job Plan or Activity Agreement is required. See Mutual obligation requirements.

Consider the past 12 months of their mutual obligations and assess if there is evidence of reckless non-compliance.

If the participant does not have mutual obligations, determine if they are engaged in a suitable activity, which may include volunteering.

Refer to the Cashless Debit Card Guiding Principles in Resources for more information.

Evidence Review Phase + Read more ...

Once a CDC Exit application is complete, assess the participant’s application against the following criteria, using the participant’s form responses, record and supporting documents:

  • The interest of any children for whom the person is responsible
  • Whether the person was convicted of an offence against a law of the Commonwealth, a State or Territory, or was serving a sentence of imprisonment for such an offence, at any time in the last 12 months
  • Risks of homelessness
  • The health and safety of the person and community
  • The responsibilities and circumstances of the person
  • The person’s engagement in the community, including the person’s employment or efforts to obtain work

Note: for more information, see the Cashless Debit Card Exits Guiding Principles document in Resources.

A comprehensive analysis of the financial evidence supplied by the participant will need to be completed.

Interview phase + Read more ...

Contacting the participant and booking interviews

Make 2 genuine attempts to call the participant to conduct an interview. The interview must be scheduled within 7 days of completing the evidence review.

Note: the Service Officer is not required to contact the customer before an appointment is made.

If the participant does not answer on the first attempt, they must be contacted again. A DOC should be added to the participant’s record, advising contact attempts have been made to complete an exit interview for their CDC exit application.

The next interview should be scheduled at a different time of the day and should be scheduled no more than 7 days after the first attempt.

If the participant does not answer the second attempt, DOC the record and follow the instructions in the ‘Delegate report’, responding with ‘Did not answer’ when directed. Go to Step 6

Conducting the interview

Service Officers must read the Interview Privacy Statement to the participant before commencing the interview.

Complete the interview questions in the question set within the Exit Tool.

Complete a Fast Note in Customer First. CDC Exit App - Interview Completed should be selected under Extra Details.

Note: if the participant requires a wellbeing assessment due to answers or information provided during the interview, refer to Wellbeing Exemptions from the Cashless Debit Card (CDC) for the process. DOC the participant’s record explaining they have been referred for a wellbeing assessment.

During the interview, if it is identified the participant has not supplied all of the documents required (i.e. there are additional bank accounts), complete the following:

  • DOC the participant record with notes on what documents are requested
  • Send the Request For Information letter
    • Go to Status tab, and place application ‘On Hold’.
    • Select On hold reason > On Hold Expiry Date
      Note: the On Hold Expiry Date is 22 days from the date the application was placed on hold or 29 days for remote areas and overseas. Refer to Allow extra time for mail delivery
  • To check if a customer’s location or correspondence nominee (where applicable) is in a remote area:
    • Go to Office Locator
    • Enter the postcode or town into the Office/Town field
    • Select Search Office/Town
    • Open the location from the Town Result List
    • Check the Remoteness field

If the participant is not required to provide more documents and does not require a wellbeing assessment, go to Step 9 to prepare the Delegate Report.

Pausing and rescheduling the interview

An interview can be paused for a number of reasons. If the participant requests the interview be rescheduled or the phone call is disconnected, reschedule the interview for another date and time. Record the new time and date in the Exit Tool.

Note: the interview must be rescheduled within 7 days. If they are unable to attend within the next 7 days, the application will proceed without an interview and begin the delegate report.

Preparing the Delegate report + Read more ...

After the interview is completed, the Service Officer must draft the Delegate report.

The Exit Tool will guide the Service Officer through each criteria for consideration under the legislation, when assessing if a participant can demonstrate reasonable and responsible management of their personal affairs (including financial affairs).

Collate the information provided in the participant’s application form, supporting evidence, Services Australia records and interview responses on the financial responsibilities of the participant to assist the Delegate with a broad and holistic understanding of the participant’s ability to demonstrate reasonable and responsible management of their personal affairs (including financial affairs) against the six criteria.

Refer to the participant’s information and evidence and prepare the Delegate report for each of the six criteria.

Note: if there is an outstanding police check, do not submit the final Delegate report until information from the police report has been provided, as this needs to be included in the report. This will not be progressed to Quality Assurance if there is still an outstanding police check.

Finalising the Delegate report

Once the Delegate report is completed, review the report for accuracy and ensure an evidence-based decision making approach was used whilst making the recommendations.

Wellbeing Assessment referral

For Cashless Debit Card (CDC) Exit Team only

Table 10: This table details referral action needed for a Wellbeing Assessment during the CDC exit application process.

Step

Action

1

Wellbeing Assessment + Read more ...

If it is identified during the CDC Exit assessment interview that the applicant presents with risks and concerns regarding their mental, physical or emotional wellbeing, the service officer can refer the participant for a Wellbeing Exemption from the Cashless Debit Card (CDC) if the participant meets the eligibility requirements.

Once satisfied that the participant meets the requirements, and they wish to proceed with the referral:

  • Create an open work item using Fast Note. Select Auto Text, use Generic > Cashless Debit Card > Wellbeing Exemption Request. This will present to the CDC Processing Team by Workload Manager
  • Do not complete the Fast Note
  • Advise the participant:
  • a Service Officer will contact them to discuss their request, and
  • they will remain on the CDC until a decision has been made

Staff must not refer a Wellbeing Assessment request before the participant has had a discussion about being transferred for a referral.

The CDC Exit assessment will continue while the Wellbeing Exemption is being assessed, due to the 60 day legislative timeframe. The applicant can request to withdraw their CDC exit application while a Wellbeing Exemption is being assessed. The CDC Exit application, and any Wellbeing Exemption referral can both assessed during the same time period.
