Role of the DVA Clearance Team + Read more ...

The DVA Clearance Team:

  • performs the clearance role with the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) for Centrelink
  • has access to the DVA payment system (DVA VIEW) for checking and verifying the payment status of mutual customers, and
  • verifies and codes all DVA income and benefits declared by the customer on the Department of Veterans' Affairs Summary (DVAS) screen

Information on specific DVA payment types is available on the Resources page.

2

Notification of income received from DVA + Read more ...

For new claims, also see Department of Veterans' Affairs Clearances.

Notification of income can be received either from the customer or from DVA.

When DVA grant an income support payment or Disability Compensation Payment (including variations in category) to a mutual customer:

  • Centrelink will be advised through the automatic clearance process
  • a Department of Veteran's Affairs Unique Identification Number (UIN) will appear on the Reference Numbers (NU) screen for matched customers

The DVA payment information will flow onto the customer's record and can be identified by the channel type DVA on the DVAS screen:

  • records with the channel type DVA, can only be modified by the DVA Clearance Team (SCS)
  • information dated before the link with DVA is established will need to be provided by the customer or confirmed with the DVA Clearance Team (SCS), if needed
  • if DVA payment data fails to load automatically, a manual follow-up (MFU) will be created. This is only for completion by the DVA Clearance Team (SCS)

The correct DVA payment type and date of event must be recorded or an overpayment may occur.

When a mutual Centrelink/DVA customer applies for a new income support payment, and DVA income is detected, a To Do Indicator (TDI) edit prevents the claim from completing until a DVA clearance is conducted. Claims with DVA income must be referred to the DVA Clearance Team (SCS).

For the clearance process, see Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) clearances.

DVA income coding needed + Read more ...

If the customer advises they are receiving income from DVA in a new claim or during an update, Service Officers are to request help from the DVA Clearance Team (SCS) to confirm and code income.

4

Family Tax Benefit (FTB) + Read more ...

Family Tax Benefit Part A is not income tested if the customer/partner receives:

  • DVA Service Pension
  • Veteran Payment
  • Income Support Supplement (ISS), or
  • Age Pension paid by DVA

When the DVA income is updated on the DVAS screen it will automatically apply the income test exemption to the customer's rate of FTB Part A.

Date for the coding:

  • the Veterans Affairs Pension (DVA) screen must be coded from date of grant of the DVA payment. This will make sure the FTB Part A income test exemption is applied correctly
  • when a DVA income support payment stops, it must be end-dated on the DVAS screen. Otherwise, an income test exemption will continue to apply, regardless of the estimate the customer provides
  • if appropriate, DVA income details may be coded to reassess FTB from the beginning of the previous financial year

The family income estimate must include the DVA income for the current financial year. This is separate to the DVA/DVAS coding.

Incorrect coding of DVA payments may result in the customer receiving the incorrect rate of FTB.

Help must be sought from the DVA Clearance Team for coding of DVA income on the DVAS screen.

Customers should be advised to include the amount of taxable DVA pensions and of non-taxable DVA payments in the family income estimate for FTB and CCS. See Updating income estimates for the current financial year.

Note: all customers receiving Child Care Subsidy (CCS) need to provide a family income estimate, irrespective of whether they receive an income support payment.

Income treatment of Wholly Dependent Partner (WDP) payment arrears for family assistance purposes.

When a customer is granted the WDP payment, they are usually eligible from the date of death of their veteran partner:

  • claims can take many years to assess and when backdated, results in significant arrears across multiple past financial years
  • any arrears owing from the date of death until the claim is finalised is paid in a lump sum
  • the WDP arrears must be included as part of the customer's family income estimate in the year that the arrears are paid to the customer
  • if a customer elects to convert their fortnightly ongoing payments of WDP into a lump sum, this must be included in the family income estimate in the financial year in which it is paid

The customer may also receive a lump sum payment from DVA of Additional Death Benefit with their grant of WDP. This is not assessable as income and does not need to be included in the family income estimate.

5

Request DVA Coding + Read more ...

To make sure the correct DVA income is recorded on the customer's record, Service Officers are to run the DVA Clearance/Advice Request Fast Note in Customer First Workspace.

Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Enquiry > DVA Clearance/Advice Request.

For urgent requests only, contact the DVA Clearance Team by phone.

Do not refer customers to this number.

6

Managing Activities + Read more ...

Place claim or activity on hold to user for 5 business days awaiting response.

DVA Clearance Team will close the Fast Note, code DVA income inside activity as appropriate, and DOC outcome.

The claim or activity will be taken off Hold in Customer First or changed to In Process for Process Direct.

When the work item is returned, complete the activity.

2

Disability Compensation Payment, Dependant Allowance and Specific Disability Item + Read more ...

Adjusted Disability Pensions are a group of payments include Disability Compensation Payments under the VEA and Permanent Impairment payments under the MRCA. From 1 January 2022, Adjusted Disability Pensions are exempt income for income support payments.

Adjusted Disability Pensions continue to be assessable income for the Low Income Health Care Card income test. A Limiting Amount (LMT) recorded on the DVAS screen will reduce the amount of Disability Pension being assessed by the system.

For periods of entitlement before 1 January 2022, these payments are treated as ordinary income for social security purposes. See Resources for more information.

For new grants of Compensation Payment, or if there has been a variation (Adjusted Disability Pension amount) in the percentage rate, a new entry at the new percentage is needed. Information about the rate will automatically be updated by the system based on the category selected. See Resources for further information.

A Limiting Amount (LMT) recorded on the DVAS screen will reduce the amount of Disability Compensation Payment being assessed by the system.

Note: Adjusted Disability Pensions, including Disability Compensation Payment, ceased to be included in the income test for income support payments from 1 January 2022.

Dependant Allowance was payable for periods before 20 September 2009. If the payment is commuted into a lump sum, it is an exempt lump sum for income and assets test purposes.

Go to Step 11.

3

Service Pension, Age Pension, Veteran Payment and Income Support Supplement (ISS) + Read more ...

If the customer receives one of these payments, they cannot receive an income support payment from Centrelink.

Note: the exception is where both payments were granted before 1974. Ordinary income is assessed in this circumstance. See the Resources page for more information.

Veteran Payment is a short-term income support payment paid by DVA. Recipients may claim with Services Australia while still in receipt of Veteran Payment. Contact is to be made with the DVA Clearance Team to negotiate an end-date for the Veteran Payment. Claims are not to be rejected without consultation with DVA.

When recording income the amount field is optional.

If needed, for:

  • Service Pension, system code is DVA
  • Veteran Payment, system code is VEP
  • Income Support Supplement, system code is ISS
  • Age Pension, system code is AGC

DVA clearance

Clearances are used to prevent dual payments. If a clearance for an income support payment is needed, a prompt will appear at the time of finalisation.

The clearance process involves:

  • checking what payments a customer may be in receipt of from DVA
  • negotiating an end date to that payment if appropriate

Parental Income Test (PIT): A PIT exemption applies for Youth Allowance (YA), ABSTUDY and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC). A PIT exemption may apply to all dependents linked to the record.

An income amount of at least 1 cent is to be recorded on the DVAS screen to prompt the PIT exemption.

Family assistance: If the customer/partner receives a DVA Service Pension, Veteran Payment or Income Support Supplement (ISS), or DSS Age Pension paid by DVA, Family Tax Benefit Part A is not income tested. When the DVA income is updated, it will tag over to reassess FTB. If appropriate, DVA income details may be coded to reassess FTB from the beginning of the previous financial year.

Go to Step 11.

4

Veteran Supplement + Read more ...

Veteran Supplement is a fortnightly amount paid to some veterans, war widow(er)'s and orphans.

To be eligible for Veteran Supplement, a customer must not receive:

  • an income support payment from DVA or Centrelink, or
  • the Military Rehabilitation & Compensation Act 2004 (MRCA) supplement

Note: this payment is not to be confused with Veteran Payment, which is an income support payment.

Three types of Veteran Supplement are payable:

  • Telephone Allowance rate (VST)
  • Pharmaceutical Allowance rate (VSP)
  • high rate (VSH) which includes both TAL and PhA

Go to Step 11.

5

War Widow(er)'s Pension + Read more ...

Do not update the DVA screen on a current War Widow(er)'s record if payment was granted before 20 March 1995, as this may cause them to lose their 'saved' pre March 1995 status which cannot be restored.

War Widow(er)'s Pension is a compensation like payment.

From 20 March 1995, War Widow(er)'s Pension became a precluding payment for social security purposes. This means, a customer cannot be paid an income support payment from Centrelink while receiving a DVA War Widow(er)'s Pension, if it was granted after 20 March 1995, unless the customer is not eligible for Income Support Supplement from DVA.

  • When recording income use the code WID
  • If a Limiting Amount has been recorded this will reduce the amount of War Widow(er)'s Pension being assessed by the system
  • War Widow(er)'s Pension is assessable income for the partner of a recipient
  • Rate information will automatically be attributed by the system

Parental Income Test (PIT): A PIT exemption applies to a War Widow(er)'s Pension for Youth Allowance (YA), ABSTUDY and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Parental Income Test purposes (PIT). A PIT exemption may apply to all dependents linked to the record.

Go to Step 11.

6

Wholly Dependent Partner (WDP) including compensation for eligible widows + Read more ...

WDP payments are a compensation like payment made under MRCA, similar in nature to the War Widow(er)'s Pension. These payments can be made as a periodic payment, as a lump sum, or a combination of both.

A multiple entitlement exclusion generally prevents a claimant from receiving an income support payment under social security law unless ineligible for Income Support Supplement from DVA. The multiple entitlement exclusion for WDP recipients lasts for the life of the claimant, whether taken as a lump sum or in periodic payments.

WDP is assessable income for any partner of a recipient regardless of its nature under social security law. The following coding may be recorded for WDP, depending on rate of fortnightly payment:

  • Where 100% of the fortnightly rate is paid, use code WDP
  • Where 75% of the fortnightly rate is paid, use code WDT
  • Where 50% of the fortnightly rate is paid, use code WDH
  • Where 25% of the fortnightly rate is paid, use code WDQ
  • Where the customer elects to convert 100% of their fortnightly rate and receive zero ongoing payments, use code WDZ

Recipients are granted at the 100% fortnightly rate of WDP but can later elect to convert 25%, 50%, 75% or 100% to a lump sum. The coding of WDP should be updated with the relevant ongoing fortnightly amount following the election of the lump sum.

For example, if a customer elects 75% lump sum, WDP coding should be updated to WDQ to represent the 25% ongoing fortnightly rate.

Lump sums are exempt income for income support payment assessment purposes, see Exempt lump sums.

Parental Income Test (PIT): A PIT exemption applies to WDP for Youth Allowance (YA), ABSTUDY and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) purposes). A PIT exemption may apply to all dependent children linked to the record.

Family assistance: While generally tax free, WDP payments are to be included in an FTB recipient's adjusted taxable income (ATI) as a tax-free pension or benefit. Lump sum payments are to be included as income in the financial year in which they are received.

Refer to Taxable income for family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payments for further information.

Go to Step 11.

7

Permanent Impairment + Read more ...

Permanent Impairment payments including Additional Compensation and Interim Compensation are compensation like payments. They are paid by DVA under the Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2004 (MRCA) or the Safety, Rehabilitation and Compensation (Defence Related Claims) Act 1988 (DRCA).

Grants under DRCA will be lump sums. MRCA payments start as a periodic payment and can be commuted to a lump sum.

Periodic payments made under MRCA were treated as ordinary income before 1 January 2022 and were Defence Force Income Support Allowance (DFISA) attracting.

  • When recording periodic payments use the code MPN
  • Lump sums are exempt income for income support payment assessment purposes, see Exempt lump sums

Permanent Impairment payments continue to be assessable income for the Low Income Health Care Card income test.

Family assistance: While generally, tax free Permanent Impairment payments made under DRCA and MRCA are included in the recipient's adjusted taxable income (ATI) as a tax-free pension or benefit for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) purposes. Lump sum payments are to be included as income in the financial year in which they are received.

Refer to Taxable income for family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payments for further information.

Go to Step 11.

8

Special Rate Disability Pension (SRDP) + Read more ...

These payments, paid under Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2004 (MRCA), are treated as ordinary income for Centrelink purposes before 1 January 2022 and were Defence Force Income Support Allowance (DFISA) attracting.

SRDP continues to be assessable income for the Low Income Health Care Card income test.

Family assistance: The payments are included in the recipient's adjusted taxable income for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and Child Care Subsidy (CCS) purposes.

When recording payments use the code MPT

Go to Step 11.

9

VCES/MRCAETS + Read more ...

Prescribed educational scheme benefits VCES and MRCAETS, if paid to parent/guardian on behalf of the child, are exempt income for social security purposes. A multiple entitlement exclusion exists for Youth Allowance (student), Austudy and ABSTUDY. See Resources for more information.

When recording payments on a:

  • student's adult record, use the code VCS on the DVA screen
  • child record, code DVA on the Child General Circumstances (CHG) screen

Go to Step 11.

10

Prisoner of War Recognition (POWR) Supplement + Read more ...

POWR Supplement is exempt income for the purposes of payments administered by Centrelink and for family assistance.

The exempt POWR Supplement is recorded on the Veterans Affairs Pension (DVA) screen.

When recording payments use the code PRS.

Go to Step 11.

11

Coding DVA screen + Read more ...

Coding of the DVA Screen should only occur if all information is available and a check of DVA VIEW system has occurred.

This is the responsibility of the DVA Clearance Team.

Inaccurate coding of DVA payments may result in the incorrect rate of FTB, and wrongful cancellation or rejection of income support payments.

Code Veterans Affairs Pension (DVA) screen.

In Customer First, select Add DVA Payment and code the following fields:

  • Type, make a selection from the pull down menu
  • DVA Reference, key the DVA Reference number if not already prefilled. This is an optional field
  • Event date, key date of grant or change in rate and select Continue
  • Fortnightly Amount '$', keyr the amount of the payment. Depending on the payment type, this field may pre-populate with an amount or a blank line
  • Source, key source of information
  • DOR, key date of receipt

When the fields are complete, select Update.

Record information in a DOC including:

  • DVA payment type and date granted/varied
  • clearance amount (if applicable)
  • date completed

If a DVA clearance is needed for an income support payment claim, a To Do Indicator (TDI) will typically generate when attempting to finalise the activity. This produces an error code that must be actioned by the DVA Clearance Team.

See Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) clearances for further detail on new claim clearances and coding in Process Direct.
