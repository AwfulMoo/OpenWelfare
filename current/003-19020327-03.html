This page contains information on assessing claims for Emergency payments from customers affected by the Far North Queensland Floods, February 2019. Emergency payments include Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP), Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) and their associated ex-gratia payments.

To lodge a claim, customers can:

  • call the Australian Government Emergency Information Line on 180  22  66 to claim over the phone, or
  • post their form and supporting documents to:
    Emergency Processing Centre
    Reply Paid 7815
    Canberra BC, ACT 2610
  • lodge their form and supporting documents at their nearest service centre, agent, access point or recovery centre (if available), or
  • fax their completed form and supporting documents to 1300  727  760

For further information on how a customer can claim, how to manage phone and paper claims, and how to action Callback DOCs and ACTDOCs, see Claiming emergency payments

Basic eligibility criteria for AGDRP and ex-gratia DRP claims

Table 3: This table describes the steps to assess basic eligibility for AGDRP/ex-gratia DRP for those affected by Far North Queensland Floods, February 2019. Service Officers should not process a claim unless they have completed formal training. Note: if a customer is not eligible for AGDRP/ex-gratia DRP claims are still to be recorded and the outcome noted as rejected. Claims cannot be rejected until formal approval has been provided. Staff will be notified via an Operational Message once they are able to reject payments.

Step

Action

1

Disaster determined and basic eligibility + Read more ...

A determination has been made that a major disaster has occurred for the purposes of AGDRP/ex-gratia DRP for people affected by Far North Queensland Floods, February 2019.

To confirm that AGDRP/ex-gratia DRP has been activated for this event, check that Operational Instructions exist.

Are there Operational Instructions?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, another social security payment may be payable to meet the needs of the customer. The claim should be rejected, see Processing AGDRP and NZ DRP claims in Customer First for the Rejecting AGDRP/NZ DRP claim table. Procedure ends here

2

Assess eligibility for AGDRP/ex-gratia DRP + Read more ...

See Eligibility for AGDRP and NZ DRP.

Note: the definition for ‘major damage’ has changed for Far North Queensland Floods, February 2019. Please refer to the Operational Instructions for the appropriate definition. See the Resources page for the Far North Queensland, February 2019 Operational Instructions. For the purposes of this event, this means that flood water that covers the interior of the principal place of residence would equal major damage.

For ex-gratia DRP a customer must have participated in the Australian taxation system for at least 1 year in the following past 3 financial years 2015-16, 2016-17, 2017-18 or will do so before 1 March 2020.
