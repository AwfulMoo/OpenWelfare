Scenarios

Item

Scenario

1

Student turning 16

Sarah turns 16 years old on 5 January of the year Sarah's ABSTUDY School Term Allowance (STA) entitlement automatically cancels CAN-S16. Even though Sarah will not be a recipient of STA when Term 1 commences for the year, because:

  • Sarah did commence for the term, and
  • has met the qualification requirements within the term entitlement period
  • Sarah retains eligibility for 4 days of pro rata rate of Term 1 STA payment

2

Parent no longer receiving qualifying payment

Millie's parent loses receipt of the Parenting Payment Single allowance on 17 January 2017. Millie's School Term Allowance record automatically cancels CAN-ANQ on that date. Even though Millie's parent is not in receipt of a qualifying payment for STA on the first day of school, because Millie did attend school in Term 1, and the parent was in receipt of a qualifying payment within the term entitlement period, Millie remains eligible for STA for Term 1.

3

Change in care arrangement of the student

Isaac is the child of separated parents Mary and Frank. Isaac was living with Mary up until 10 January when the care arrangement changed and Isaac went to live with Frank.

On 12 January, Frank submitted a Schooling A claim for Isaac. As Isaac will be in Frank's care during the school term, Frank will have the entitlement to receive the STA for Term 1. However, because Mary had some care of Isaac during the term entitlement period for Term 1, the system determines Mary will receive the full Term 1 STA payment. This is incorrect. To ensure Frank receives the STA payment for the term, Mary's ABT claim must be cancelled or rejected.

The cancellation or rejection will be from the first day of the relevant term entitlement period (in this case from 1 January). Frank's claim will then be granted from 1 January. Record a DOC detailing the actual change in parent/guardian dates.

4

Student had satisfactory attendance in Term 1 2020

In Term 1 2020, Ari's school advised attendance above 85%. Ari is entitled to the Term 2 STA payment due to satisfactory attendance in Term 1.

Payment of STA in Term 3 and 4 requires a student to have attendance of 85% or more in term 1 of 2020. As Ari had satisfactory attendance in Term 1, Ari is entitled to the Term 3 and 4 STA payment regardless of actual attendance in Term 2 and 3, as long as Ari remains enrolled and meets the Schooling A Award eligibility criteria.

5

Student had unsatisfactory attendance in Term 1 2020

In Term 1 2020, Billie's school advised attendance under 85%. Billie was not entitled to the Term 2 STA payment due to unsatisfactory attendance in Term 1.

Payment of STA in Term 3 and 4 also requires a student to have attendance of 85% or more in term 1 of 2020. As Billie had unsatisfactory attendance in Term 1, Billie is not entitled to the Term 3 or 4 STA payment.

Billie's school provides a letter advising that Billie's attendance in Term 2 and 3 was above 85% and considered satisfactory. However, as Billie had unsatisfactory attendance in Term 1, Billie is not entitled to the Term 3 or 4 STA payment and this remains unchanged.

6

Student enrolled in Home Schooling

Rose is enrolled with Home Schooling NSW and has been since the commencement of the current school year. Rose's parent lodges a claim for ABSTUDY Schooling A in Term 3 for the current calendar year. Rose is granted ABSTUDY.

With the new claim Rose provided a recently date letter from Home Schooling NSW, confirming Rose is registered for home schooling for the calendar year. As the letter from Home School NSW, is recently dated and confirms a current registration with Home Schooling NSW, Rose is considered to have meet the 85% attendance requirement in the relevant past terms and a manual attendance check is not required.
