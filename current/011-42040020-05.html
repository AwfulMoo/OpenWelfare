This page contains definitions of terms referred to throughout this document and details of the claimable practice nurse or registered Aboriginal Health worker items. It also contains a link to the Workforce Incentive Program (WIP) - Practice Stream on the Services Australia website.

On this Page:

Definitions

Definitions of terms

This table lists definitions of terms used within his document.

Term

Definition

Registered Aboriginal Health Worker

A person in the Northern Territory who is registered as an Aboriginal Health Worker under the Health Practitioners Act 2004 (NT), who is employed or retained (defined below) by a general practice, or by a health service that has an exemption to claim Medicare benefits under sub-section 19(2) of the Health Insurance Act 1973.

Practice nurse

A practice nurse is either a registered or an enrolled nurse and employed by, or their services retained by, a general practice.

Retained

Retained refers to a contract or agreement that has been put in place between a practice and a practice nurse or other health professional to perform services on behalf of practitioners in that practice.

Immunisation

Immunisation means administration of a registered vaccine included in the National Immunisation Program Schedule and vaccines covered in the Australian Immunisation Handbook 10th edition.

Claimable items

Items available for services performed by practice nurses and registered Aboriginal health workers

This table provides the details of the services practice nurse or registered Aboriginal health worker can provide on behalf of a medical practitioner.

Items

Description

10986 and 10987 - health assessment services

Item 10986 is a service provided by a practice nurse or registered Aboriginal health worker being the provision of a health assessment for a patient who is receiving or has received their four year old immunisation.

Item 10987 is a follow up service provided by a practice nurse or registered Aboriginal health worker, on behalf of a medical practitioner, for an Indigenous person who has received a health assessment.

10988 - immunisation service

Item 10988 is an Immunisation service provided to a person by a registered Aboriginal health worker.

The types of immunisations claimable under item 10988 administered by a registered Aboriginal health worker to an adult or child are a registered vaccine that is included on the Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods. This includes all vaccines on the National Immunisation Program Schedule and vaccines covered in the current edition of the Australian Immunisation Handbook.

Note: the following substances cannot be claimed under this item:

  • vaccines used experimentally
  • homeopathic substance
  • immunotherapy for allergies (for example desensitisation preparations)
  • other substances that are not vaccines

There may also be state or territory limitations on the administration of some vaccines such as those for tuberculosis, yellow fever and Q-fever.

Immunisation item 10988 is not payable for individual vaccinations. The General practitioner (GP) is only entitled to claim a single item 10988 for each patient visit where immunisations are provided.

10989 - wound management services

Item 10989 is a service for the treatment of a person's wound provided by a registered Aboriginal health worker.

Note:

  • The wound management item is payable only once per visit, even if more than one wound is treated during the same patient visit.
  • The medical practitioner does not need to be present during the treatment of the wound provided they conducted an initial assessment of the patient in order to give instruction in relation to the treatment of the wound.
  • Where a registered Aboriginal health worker provides ongoing wound management, the medical practitioner is not required to see the patient during each subsequent visit. The medical practitioner under whose supervision the treatment is provided retains responsibility for the health safety and clinical outcomes of the patient.

10997 - chronic disease services

Service provided to a person with chronic disease by a practice nurse or registered Aboriginal health worker.
