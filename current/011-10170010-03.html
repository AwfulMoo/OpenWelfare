Establish a Family Tax Benefit (FTB) link for a child between Centrelink and the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) 011-10170010

This page contains processes related to setting up links on child records between Centrelink and the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR). This includes how to respond to enquiries and how Families Override Officers sever a link set up in error.

On this page:

Respond to child AIR link enquiries for Family Tax Benefit

Families Override Officer action - AIR link established in error

Respond to child AIR link enquiries for Family Tax Benefit

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Investigate customer record + Read more ...

Navigate to the Medicare Information (MEI) screen for the relevant child, and check whether a link has previously been attempted.

If the Child is linked with AIR field displays:

  • a ‘Y’, then a successful link has been established. Procedure ends here.
  • ‘_’ ( the underscore indicates a link has not been attempted) a link request has been attempted but has been unsuccessful, go to Step 2
  • a 3 digit numeric AIR mismatch code (i.e. 400) a link request has been attempted but has been unsuccessful, go to Step 8. Note: the Resources page contains AIR Mismatch codes
  • ‘AWA’ a link has been attempted but no response has been received. This may take up to 14 days for AIR to return a response. Procedure ends here

Further details about the child's FTB link status and immunisation statuses, including details transferred from the AIR, can be viewed on the Child Immunisation Summary guided procedure.

2

Determine action required + Read more ...

If a link with the AIR has not been attempted previously, confirm with the customer if the child is:

  • enrolled in Medicare, go to Step 3
  • not yet enrolled in Medicare, go to Step 4
  • not eligible for Medicare enrolment, go to Step 4

If the customer has attempted to update an unlinked child's Medicare details using their Centrelink online account or Express Plus Centrelink mobile app. See Resources for examples of messages, issues and possible Service Officer action.

If the child has multiple Customer Reference Numbers (CRN) refer the record to the Data Quality Unit DQU (if not previously referred for multiple CRN assessment) using Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Data Quality > Select one of the following Templates:

  • Multiple CRN referral – Customer
  • Multiple CRN referral – Child
  • Urgent Multiple CRN Referral - Customer
  • Urgent Multiple CRN Referral - Child

3

Request Medicare number to establish link between Centrelink and the AIR + Read more ...

Can the customer provide the child's Medicare number and Individual Reference Number (IRN)?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, tell the customer to obtain the child’s Medicare number and IRN. Once the customer has the child’s Medicare number and IRN they can update the Medicare details using their myGov online account or Centrelink Express Plus mobile app

When a customer does not have legal authority to obtain Medicare details, escalate to a Service Support Officer (SSO). If appropriate, the SSO will obtain the number from Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) and refer the enquiry to the Centrelink AIR Liaison Officer. In the Centrelink AIR Liaison Officer referral DOC include the information provided by the customer about why they do not have the authority to obtain the details. Once the child is linked, if the child does not meet the immunisation requirements, it may be appropriate to discuss whether the customer is eligible for a Secretary Determination exemption from immunisation. See View and update a child's immunisation status and assess or update immunisation exemptions for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for assistance.

Procedure ends here.

4

Child not enrolled in Medicare + Read more ...

If the child is:

  • not yet enrolled, but is eligible for Medicare enrolment, tell the customer to enrol the child in Medicare. Once the child has been enrolled, the customer can provide the child’s Medicare details using their myGov Centrelink online service or the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app.
  • not eligible for Medicare enrolment
    • in Customer First, go to the Child Selection (CHS) screen
    • key 'S' in the box next to the child's name then either:
      press [Enter], key 'S' in the Medicare Info field on the Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen and press [Enter], or
      key 'MEI' in the Nxt field and press [Enter]
    • key 'Y' in the Establish new link with AIR (child not Medicare eligible): field
    • key a valid reason code in the Reason Medicare details not provided field, either:
      NER – Child is not enrolled in Medicare
      NEL – Child is not eligible for Medicare
    • press [Enter]
    • key in the Source and DOR fields and complete the activity
    • key AR in the Nxt field and press [Continue]
    • in Activity Notes key “Link requested for child not enrolled in Medicare / or not Medicare eligible (Service Officer login)”
    • press [Finalise and Continue]
    • DOC the record using the Re Immunisation Details Fast Note

How the link between Centrelink and the AIR is established

Data is transmitted to the AIR once the activity is completed – this can take up to 14 days to establish a link with the AIR. A child displays as in an awaiting “AWA” period on the Immunisation Details (IMD) and Medicare Information (MEI) screens until the link is established. If the 14 days ends and no match has been established the child will commence a 63 day grace period.

The AIR link status for FTB will not update in real time. If an exact, unique match exists, a link is established and the immunisation status is received overnight. If a match is not successful, a mismatch reason code is received overnight.

If a possible match or multiple matches exist, the AIR staff will attempt to match the child manually. This process can also take up to 14 days to complete.

Further detail about the child's link status and immunisation statuses, including details transferred from AIR, can be viewed on the Child Immunisation Summary Guided Procedure.

Procedure ends here.

5

Check if child is listed on customer record + Read more ...

Is the child on the Child Selection (CHS) screen?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, because the customer's claim has not yet been assessed. Record child's Medicare details in the Claim Progress DOC advising they will need to be updated when the claim is processed. Procedure ends here

6

Establish link with AIR for a child who is Medicare eligible and has been enrolled + Read more ...

Code the child's Medicare details:

  • in Customer First, go to the CHS screen
  • key 'S' in the box next to the child's name then either:
    • press [Enter], key 'S' in the Medicare Info field on the Child Task Selector (CHTS) and press [Enter], or
    • key 'MEI' in the Nxt field and press [Enter]
  • code the Medicare Number field with the Medicare number
  • code the Medicare Reference Number field with the individual reference number (IRN), (the IRN is the identifying number beside the child's name on the Medicare card)
  • key 'Y' in the Establish new link with AIR: field
  • key in the Source: and DOR: fields and complete the activity
  • press [Enter]

Data is transmitted to the AIR once the activity is completed – this can take up to 14 days to establish a link with the AIR. A child will be displayed as in an awaiting “AWA” period on the Immunisation Details (IMD) screen until the link is established. If the 14 days ends and no match has been established the child will commence a 63 day grace period.

The AIR link status for FTB will not update in real time. If an exact, unique match exists, a link will be established and the immunisation status will be received overnight. If a match is not successful, a mismatch reason code will be received overnight.

If a possible match or multiple matches exist, the AIR staff will attempt to match the child manually. This process can also take up to 14 days to complete.

Further detail about the child's link status and immunisation statuses, including details transferred from AIR, can be viewed on the Child Immunisation Summary Guided Procedure.

7

Record details on a Fast Note + Read more ...

Record details using the Advising Medicare no. for [enter child's name] Fast Note.

If the Fast Note is not available, include the following information in a DOC:

  • child's name (first name, second name and family name)
  • child's Medicare details, including individual reference number (IRN)
  • Medicare Number updated: Yes/No

Note: no DOC will be recorded if the update has been made by the AIR. Instead, notes will be attached to the activity to indicate that the Medicare number was input by AIR, a link requested for child not eligible for Medicare, or link ended and Medicare number input by AIR.

Tell the customer:

  • the Medicare number is used to match with the AIR to check the child's immunisation status for the assessment of family assistance payments
  • links generally occur overnight however can take up to 14 days. If the child cannot be matched using the information provided, the customer will be advised in writing
  • once the link has been established this information is updated each time the child's immunisation status changes on the AIR

Procedure ends here.

8

Confirm mismatch details + Read more ...

To confirm a mismatch exists, check that the:

  • Child Immunisation Summary guided procedure displays a warning which includes information about the mismatch, or
  • Medicare Information (MEI) screen displays a numeric code in the Child is linked with AIR field

The Resources page contains AIR Mismatch codes, guided procedure warning text and suggested action.

9

Identify and correct the cause of the mismatch + Read more ...

For example:

  • confirm the child's Medicare number has been recorded correctly. This can be done by:
    • checking existing documents scanned to the customer's record
    • consulting Medicare staff at collocated sites, or
    • making outbound contact to the customer
  • check the child's first name, last name and middle initial (including name spelling, type and order) is correct for both Centrelink and Medicare
    • If it is not possible to confirm the Medicare details are correct (e.g. consulting Medicare staff at collocated sites) for the customer, tell the customer to confirm their details with Medicare
  • check the child's date of birth (DOB) is recorded correctly for both Centrelink and Medicare. If the child’s DOB does not match, action the Issue on the Customer Issues page, go to Step 10
  • if siblings exist, ensure the Medicare Reference Number: field is coded with the correct individual reference number (IRN). The IRN is the identifying number beside the child's name on the Medicare card

Child has previously mismatched and a further mismatch has occurred

When the mismatch has previously been actioned and all details are correct (as per above checking process) but the subsequent AIR link attempt has resulted in a further mismatch which cannot be resolved, escalate to a Service Support Officer (SSO). The SSO will refer to the Centrelink AIR Liaison team who will seek assistance from the AIR to obtain a successful link.

The following details must be included in the escalation to SSO:

  • full name of affected child, including the spelling and order of names are confirmed as correct
  • confirmation the DOB is recorded correctly
  • Medicare number and IRN recorded on the MEI screen (or documented elsewhere on the record) are correct
  • previous action taken to resolve the discrepancy (for example corrected name/DOB etc. in Centrelink or Medicare record)

Procedure ends here.

10

Customer Issue on Customer Issues page + Read more ...

If an issue presents on the Customer Issues page ask the customer:

  • 'can I confirm <child name>’s details to ensure we are receiving up to date immunisation data for them?’ (Confirm correct spelling of child’s given and last names)
  • ‘could you please confirm your child’s date of birth and Medicare number so I can ensure we have the correct details recorded?’
  • if the child is 15 years or over, ask:
    • ‘If they have their own Medicare number, are they available to provide that to me? Otherwise do you have a previous Medicare number that <child name> has ever been listed on?’
  • if the customer wants to know why you are asking these questions, say:
    • ‘We need to ensure we have the correct information for <child name> so your payments are not affected’

Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Families > Enquiry > Child DOB Discrepancy to record the information provided by the customer. The Fast Note will automatically add the correct keyword and the open DOC will be allocated to the Centrelink AIR Liaison via WLM. Staff must not add/change keywords.

Families Override Officer action - AIR link established in error

For Families Override Officers only.

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Correcting a link where established in error + Read more ...

To update the Medicare Information (MEI) screen:

  • in Customer First, go to the CHS screen
  • key 'S' in the box next to the child's name then either:
    • press [Enter], key 'S' in the Medicare Info field on the Child Task Selector (CHTS) and press [Enter], or
    • key 'MEI' in the Nxt field and press [Enter]
  • key 'Y' in the End existing link with AIR: field code the Medicare Number field and Medicare Reference Number field with the correct Medicare number and individual reference number (IRN) (the IRN is the identifying number beside the child's name on the Medicare card) or leave blank if a name only match is required (see Step 3 in Respond to enquiries relating to a child AIR link table)
  • key 'Y' in the Establish new link with AIR: field
  • key the Source: and DOR: fields and complete the activity
  • press [Enter]

The immunisation statuses will not update in real time. A response from AIR will be received overnight.
