Finalising and attributing actions to Service Updates and Reviews in the IRS

Step

Action

1

Select customer record for whom the Review Result is to be calculated + Read more ...

Select the customer record in the Centrelink system, then go to the Review Summary (IRRS) screen.

Claiming Review Results

To claim a Review Result and attribute any action or reassessment on the customer's record to the review, the review must have been started in IRS at the time the action was processed.

Any actions/reassessments made on a customer record before the review is started (that is, has a Review Status of 'STA') will not be able to be attributed to the review on finalisation. This includes Partner Reviews.

Is there a review already initiated on the IRS?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, this procedure does not apply. See Manually loading a Service Update or Review in the Integrated Review System (IRS)

2

Actioning reassessments + Read more ...

All updates and coding should be actioned within the review image for direct Attribution to occur (for example, action the activity by first accessing the review in IRS and then creating the activity). This will set up the activity to be automatically selected for attribution.

The use of scripts for suspensions and cancellations will ensure that the result is auto selected and presented for attribution at the finalisation of the review.

Any employment income or updates actioned outside the review image will have to be manually selected on the Attribute Results (IRAR) screen. It is important to note the correct process to ensure that the activity is created from within the review image. It should be a rare occurrence that an activity needs to be manually attributed to a review. Activities which are not processed from within the review image are available to be attributed to any other review, so the activity may not be available to be manually attributed at review finalisation because it has been selected for attribution to another review.

3

Attribute the correct activities to the review + Read more ...

To access and finalise the review use the appropriate script. If the script is not available, select the review from the Review Summary (IRRS) screen and finalise by placing an 'F' next to the review. Press [Enter].

The system will navigate to the Attribute Results (IRAR) screen. This screen is used to attribute activities to the review prior to calculating a Review Result. A process known as Attribution is used to ensure that activities performed on the customer's record are credited to that review. The IRAR screen will display all non-excluded activities on a customer's record (not just review related activities) that have occurred over the life of the review.

Check the details of each activity.

Decisions will need to be made to:

  • Select only the activities which are directly or indirect triggered by the review, and
  • Ensure that a correct Review Result is calculated in accordance with the Compliance and Fraud attribution rules located on the Resources page
  • Care should be taken when manually attributing or unattributing actions to and from a review. The Review Result should be an accurate reflection of the rate variation the review has triggered

Some activities are not able to be attributed to a review, such as a CPI or a deeming activity. These are known as 'excluded' activities which are excluded from being attributed to a review. See the Resources page for more information on excluded activities and debts.

4

Identify and record any variation to customers payment + Read more ...

Any attribution of a suspension or cancellation action should be clearly documented with reasons why the suspension or cancellation was claimed.

If a suspension or cancellation is manually attributed to a review, then any restoration, re-grant of that payment, or grant of another primary payment must also be manually attributed to the review if that action occurred within 42 days after the customer's payment was suspended or cancelled.

IRS attributes restorations to a review by IRS if the review has a preceding suspension or cancellation action attributed. This occurs because savings claimed by a review cannot be reported as savings unless the customer's payment component has remained suspended or cancelled for 42 days from the date that action was processed. Or if a future date of effect if the action which suspended or cancelled the customer's payment was subject to a future Date of Effect.

Restorations, re-grant and grant actions should never be unattributed from a review unless approval to unattribute the action has been provided by a Quality Development Officer (QDO) or Senior Practitioner.

Note: any overpayment which has resulted from the review must be raised before it can be selected as part of this finalisation process.

When making an unfavourable decision, speak to the customer:

  • explain the decision
  • give them a chance to provide more information, and
  • advise their review and appeal rights

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact a customer before finalising the decision.

Is there a variation to the customer's payment to be recorded?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No,
    • If there is any other activity or DOC to be recorded, go to Step 6
    • If there no other activity or DOC to be recorded go to Step 7'

5

Variation to be attributed + Read more ...

'S'elect the reassessment activity from the IRAR screen. Check for more activities that may be attributable to the review by scrolling through the IRAR screen using [F8] and [F7].

A variation to a customer's payment should be processed immediately the customer advised or the information was obtained/verified. If it appears that a variation to a customer's payment has occurred as a result of the review and this has not been actioned previously in the Centrelink system then it must be done immediately and before the review is finalised.

Any attributable variation on a customer record over the life of the review will be displayed as an activity on the Attribute Results (IRAR) screen.

Note: If activities are selected that will give a Review Result over the maximum rate for the benefit the customer is receiving then an error message will be displayed and the user will have to de-select any activities incorrectly attributed.

Is there any other activity or DOC to be recorded?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Other activities to be attributed + Read more ...

Select the activity from the IRAR screen by placing an 'S' next to the activity.

Check for more activities which were a result of the review by scrolling through the IRAR screen using [F8] and [F7].

Activities attributable to the review can be directly or indirectly triggered by the review.

All activities that are attributable to the review and were triggered by the review should be attributed on finalisation. This will give a record of all of the activities related to the review, for example, suspension, cancellation or a debt which results from updating the customer's record. The activity numbers (AMR) can be confirmed via the Event Summary (ES) screen. This will enable the correct Review Result to be calculated by the IRS. Press [Enter].

7

Check the results + Read more ...

The IRS will navigate to the Finalise Review (IRFR) screen. The IRFR screen will display the final Review Result that has been calculated using the activities that have been attributed to the review on the IRAR screen. Where payment related activities are not selected a review result of PRT (Present Rate to Continue) will default.

Check the Result Component (IRRC) screen to view the payment component results for the review to ensure that the right variations have been captured. The DE List (XDE) screen may help to identify activities which suspended, cancelled or restored the customer's payment as this screen displays the daily rate of each activity.

Check if an activity has been attributed in error where it is not claimable under the Compliance and Fraud attribution rules, by scrolling through the IRAR screen using [F8] and [F7]. An activity can be unattributed from the review by de-selecting 'S' from the activity line. Care must be taken to not unattribute a restoration, re-grant or grant action which has been attributed to the review to offset suspension or cancellation savings.

Check that the Review Result appears correct based on the result of the investigations into the customer's circumstances.

Ensure that the calculated savings are an accurate reflection of the review outcome. Attributing and unattributing actions in order to obtain a 'close to expected' savings figure is not acceptable. Where there is a system fault preventing the correct amount of savings from being calculated it should be reported to a Senior Practitioner or a Quality Development Officer (QDO). Ensure that the correct activities are attributed to the review before reporting the problem.

Note: it is also possible to navigate to the IRRC, Debt Details (IRDD) and Manage Review (IRMR) screens from the IRFR screen by placing an 'S' in the 'go to' link and pressing [Enter]. After viewing, press [Enter] again and IRS will navigate back to the IRFR screen.

Do the Review Result and Reason appear correct and as per Compliance and Fraud attribution rules?

The Resources page contains a link to the rules.

  • Yes, ensure source and date of receipt (DOR) are coded, key 'Y' in the Finalise Review: field, and press [Enter]. Go to Step 8
  • No, press [Enter]. The IRS will navigate back to the Review Summary (IRRS) screen. The process of checking and selecting activities will need to be repeated, to ensure correctness. Then go to Step 8

8

Identify customer partner and related IRS reviews + Read more ...

Does the customer have a partner and does the partner have an IRS review that also must be finalised?

  • Yes, this procedure should be repeated for the partner's record. Then go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Review Result + Read more ...

The customer's review is now finalised and the IRS will navigate to the Review Summary (IRRS) screen. Review results are displayed on both the Review Results (IRRR) and on the Results Component (IRRC) screens.

The review may still be subject to Restoration Processing, called the Applicable Restoration Period (ARP). See Calculating a Review Result and Restoration Processing in the Integrated Review System (IRS) for more information.
