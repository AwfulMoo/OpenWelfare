Cancellation of payment after a compliance related suspension 001-10120100

  • Process Summary
  • Process
  • References
Expand all


This document outlines the policy and processes that apply where a payment is cancelled because a Community Development Program (CDP) job seeker has failed to re-engage or contact their provider following compliance action.

Customer failed to re-engage (CDR) cancellations

A CDP job seeker's participation payment will be cancelled CDR if they do not comply with a reconnection or further reconnection requirement notified by Services Australia, or a CDP provider as a result of a Non-Attendance Report (NAR), within 29 days of the requirement first being issued

Generally, the date of effect of the cancellation is the day after the date paid to (DPT+1). However, where the CDP job seeker is required to attend a re-engagement appointment for a reconnection or further reconnection requirement, the cancellation may be deferred until the next entitlement period end date after the 29 day period elapses where the:

  • Online Diary displays an appointment outcome of No Result Entered (NRE). In these cases the cancellation is deferred to provide additional time for the provider to record attendance for any re-engagement appointments the CDP job seeker was expected to attend during the entitlement period, or
  • CDP job seeker is due to attend a re-engagement appointment after the 29 day period elapses. If the CDP job seeker does not attend this appointment, cancellation will be deferred to close of business on the day after the appointment

If both of the above apply, cancellation will occur on the latter of the 2 dates (that is, the latter of the next Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) or the day after the appointment date).

Did not contact provider to re-engage (DCP) cancellations

A CDP job seeker's participation payment will be cancelled DCP where:

  • a Non-Attendance Report (NAR) has been submitted, and
  • the CDP job seeker fails to make contact with their CDP provider and agree to a reconnection appointment within 29 days of the start of the instalment period in which the NAR is received

The date of effect of the cancellation will be the latter of:

  • the NAR incident date, or
  • the day after the date paid to (DLP+1)

CDP job seeker does not contact following suspension

Where a 'DNA participation appointment' (APA) suspension has been applied and the CDP job seeker does not contact Services Australia to accept the reconnection requirement the suspension will be maintained. Payment will generally cancel DNL or FRP, but will be cancelled automatically after 13 weeks if not cancelled earlier for another reason.

Where a 'Non-Attendance Report Received' (ANR) suspension has been applied and the CDP job seeker does not contact their provider to discuss the suspension and accept a reconnection requirement, the suspension will be maintained until their payment is automatically cancelled DCP after 29 days.

Manual cancellation

While CDR and DCP cancellations should occur automatically in most cases, these cancellation reasons can be manually recorded if necessary.

Manual cancellation may be required if:

  • the CDP job seeker has not attended a re-engagement appointment within 29 days, and the system did not cancel payment on the next EPED after the 29 day period elapsed
  • an issue has occurred with the automatic cancellation, and manual processing is required. For example, manual NAR restoration or CDR cancellation reviews. For further information, see the Participation Compliance Manual Follow Up Review guide

CDP job seeker contact following CDR or DCP cancellation

Where payment has been cancelled CDR or DCP the CDP job seeker is deemed to have lost qualification for their participation payment due to an ongoing failure to meet their mutual obligation requirements.

  • Payment restoration is not appropriate where the cancellation has been correctly applied
  • To receive the payment again, the CDP job seeker will need to reclaim and re-test their eligibility

If a reclaim is lodged within 14 days of the cancellation date the claim start date must not be backdated to the date of cancellation, the claim start date will need to be amended to the date of claim. In this situation the start date of the claim will be the date the claim is lodged. For further details, see Calculating the start day general rule.

Review of cancellation decisions

Where the CDP job seeker has requested a review of a CDR or DCP cancellation, or it is apparent that the cancellation should not have occurred, the record must be referred to the Participation Solutions Team (PST). Service Officers outside of the PST are not to restore a record that has been cancelled CDR or DCP.

PST skilled service officers may restore a record as a result of a review of decision following a CDR or DCP cancellation where there is evidence to indicate the CDP job seeker:

  • contacted and/or attended a re-engagement appointment however this was not identified prior to the CDR or DCP cancellation due to:
    • incorrect or incomplete coding by Services Australia and/or CDP provider, or
    • a system issue or error. For example, delays in the transfer of information between the Department of Education, Skills and Employment and Services Australia's IT systems
  • was experiencing a major personal crisis and had no capacity to comply with any of their mutual obligation requirements in the 29 day period. Note: for a DCP cancellation, to warrant restoration, there would have to be significant factors that made it unreasonable for the CDP job seeker to contact their provider for the duration of the period. If the CDP job seeker was able to contact Services Australia, or tried to report during the period it is unlikely that restoration should occur

Note: this evidence must also ensure that despite an update made by a provider to an Online Diary result, the original requirement has been met or evidence exists confirming the CDP job seeker is no longer required to meet the original requirement.
