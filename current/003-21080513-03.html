Assessing urgent payment requests

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Basic eligibility + Read more ... There are modal links to rows in this table from the urgent payment files

Customers may be eligible for an urgent payment if they:

  • are in severe financial hardship:
    • as a single person, their liquid assets are less than the maximum fortnightly payment rate (including add-ons) for the payment they are receiving
    • as a member of a couple, their liquid assets are less than twice the maximum fortnightly payment rate (including add-ons) for the payment they are receiving
  • will not be placed in further financial hardship by the urgent payment
  • are receiving an eligible Centrelink payment. If they are receiving an ineligible payment, they cannot get an urgent payment and their request should be denied
  • are not receiving weekly payments
  • have not received an urgent payment on the same day
  • have not requested one urgent payment and one reconsideration of the decision on the same day
  • have not received 2 urgent payments in the last 12 months. Note: this only applies to urgent payment requests for exceptional and unforeseen circumstances. Customers may be eligible for an extra payment in extraordinary circumstances. Do not count urgent payments due to funerals, Centrelink delay or holiday processing
  • do not have a record in Environment 'I' (overseas)

See the relevant procedure for other eligibility criteria:

  • urgent payments due to exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances
  • urgent payments due to Centrelink delay
  • urgent payments due to expenses associated with a funeral
  • urgent payments due to holiday processing

2

Delegation for decision making + Read more ...

The decision to approve or deny an urgent payment is made by a Service Officer at the APS4 level or above, Service Delivery Partner Service Officers or specific Smart Centre staff. These are staff fully trained in urgent payment processing. This applies to Smart Centre telephony, service centre and processing team staff.

During periods of holiday processing only, when a request is made for an urgent payment due to departmental delay, this decision can be made at the APS3 level.

Staff assessing urgent payments must first complete all urgent payment training.

Staff not trained in assessing urgent payment requests (or for FHA customers):

  • cold transfer the customer via Services Australia Workspace to the urgent payment queue for the payment type (or the Farmer Assistance hotline)
  • document the customer record with any details obtained during the contact

If the customer has a Managed Service Plan (MSP), the details of the MSP may influence who the decision maker will be. For example, customers with a full or partial phone restriction in place should be transferred to their One Main Contact (OMC) or referred to the Personalised Services Officer (PSSO) if available, or advised to contact as per the MSP arrangements.

It may be appropriate for service centre or processing team staff to assess an urgent payment request. Examples include when a customer:

  • is being assisted by a staff member on another matter (such as restoration of payment) and the customer requests an urgent payment
  • presents as distressed or vulnerable and it would not be appropriate to put them on the phone
  • attends a service centre to request an urgent payment and they have a Managed Service Plan (MSP) in place on their record prohibiting them from attending face-to-face and a one-off variation has not been approved
    • An authorised delegate should direct the customer to leave the premises and to contact their OMC/PSSO as per the MSP arrangements. Tell the customer's OMC/PSSO of the contact so they can follow up with the customer. For more information, see Customer not complying with am MSP
    • If the MSP is managed by Personalised Services, the Service Officer must contact the Personalised Services phone queue

3

Customers on weekly payments + Read more ...

Customers cannot receive an urgent payment of their income support payment while they are on a weekly payment cycle. They also cannot receive an urgent payment from any other eligible payment they receive.

Any request for an urgent payment should be rejected. Customers cannot be removed from a weekly payment cycle to allow the urgent payment to be made and then placed back on a weekly payment cycle in the same pay period. A Service Officer trained in weekly payments should talk to the customer before removing them from weekly payments. See Assessing, coding and reviewing weekly payments.

Customers issued a hardship advance or urgent payment in the current entitlement period cannot start on the weekly payment cycle until the current period has ended.

4

Customers on a Managed Service Plan (MSP) + Read more ...

Review the MSP service channel restrictions to identify the customer’s contact arrangement.

If the customer is subject to service restrictions and is contacting via a channel that is restricted, follow the guidelines in Customer not complying with MSP.

If the customer is contacting via an appropriate service channel, contact the customer's OMC/PSSO and seek their advice about the request for urgent payment.

For more information, see:

  • Customer service delivered through a One Main Contact (OMC) as part of an MSP
  • Referring customers to and handling customer enquiries and correspondence for Personalised Services

5

Customers on Cashless Debit Card (benefit type IQN) + Read more ...

CDC participants may be eligible for an urgent payment.

If they have CDC funds to cover the expense, encourage them to use this money instead of an urgent payment:

  • CDC account balances are on the Transaction History screen via Workspace > Cashless Debit Card. This information is for staff only. Do not discuss it with the participant
  • If they choose to use CDC funds, they can make payments via the CDC or the linked online account. If regular payment options cannot be used, consider a one-off or urgent transfer from the CDC account

If they do not have CDC funds to cover the expense, or they do not want to use them, assess the urgent payment request as normal. See Urgent payment requests from CDC participants.

Urgent payments go into the customer’s unrestricted bank account unless the customer asks for it to be paid to their CDC account.

6

Payment methods + Read more ...

Urgent payments can be delivered by:

  • priority direct credit via the New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS), or
  • direct credit (standard processing)
  • Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) card only if all other payment options are not suitable or will place the customer in further hardship

Use NPP and RTGS only when the customer has had a regular payment issued to the payment destination (except for Crisis Payment and AGDRP). Customers will not be able to use these services if they changed their payment destination before requesting the urgent payment and have not yet received a regular payment into this account.

NPP is the preferred method of delivery for urgent payments. Payment by RTGS or direct credit will delay the customer's access to the payment by 1 to 2 business days.

When offering RTGS, make sure the customer understands that the bank may charge a fee for processing the payment and timeframes vary depending on the bank. Services Australia does not have details of timeframes or fees charged.

Rural Agency/Remote Service Centres do not have access to EBT cards. Do not refer customer or their representative to these sites to collect a payment. Due to the remote location of these sites, the method must be NPP or direct credit.

Farm Household Allowance (FHA) and Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS) customers can only be paid an urgent payment via RTGS or direct Credit.

If a customer has a Managed Service Plan (MSP) in place on their record, consider this when choosing how to pay an urgent payment. If the customer is prohibited from attending a service centre, other methods of delivery such as direct credit, RTGS or payment nominee may be more appropriate.

7

Customers with a history of urgent payment requests + Read more ...

This may indicate that a customer is having trouble managing their finances, or there are other ongoing issues. A holistic approach to servicing will support customers to manage their finances better.

When a customer has a history of requesting urgent payments, make sure they are not being placed in a cycle of ongoing hardship, unable to meet their ongoing expenses. If an urgent payment would mean the customer cannot meet their regular expenses and would increase their financial hardship, the request can be refused. The history could include multiple requests for each type of urgent payment.

Customers are limited to one urgent payment request (self-service or staff assisted), and one reconsideration of the decision on the same day. After this, all requests are to be rejected.

Explore other assistance options with the customer. Customers should be highly encouraged to access these services to manage finances better. The Resources page has a link for examples of phrasing and questioning to support unfavourable discussions with customers.

If the customer does not accept this offer, discuss with Team Leader to explore other options to provide holistic service.

Customers who have a history of repeated urgent payment requests should be considered for a Managed Service Plan (MSP). The history can include urgent payments because of expenses associated with a funeral. These provide extra support when there are vulnerabilities or barriers.

8

Customer aggression/ counterproductive behaviour + Read more ...

Services Australia staff must report and record all incidents of customer aggression. This is important:

  • it informs the agency’s response to these incidents
  • it enables effective support for both staff and customers

For how to report, record and escalate incidents, see Customer aggression - reporting and recording incidents.

Customer has a nominee or third party arrangement

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Correspondence nominee + Read more ...

A correspondence nominee may request an urgent payment on the customer's behalf, but the payment must be made to the customer.

See also: Obligations of nominees.

2

Voluntary payment nominee + Read more ...

A payment nominee cannot request an urgent payment on the customer's behalf. This request must be made by the customer.

If approved, the urgent payment must be issued to the nominee's bank account, unless the nominee agrees for payment to be issued to the customer or a third party. A nominee is not entitled to be issued or collect an Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) card.

Contact the nominee before making a decision. Tell the nominee that the customer has made an urgent payment request. This is to make sure all care and consideration is given to the welfare of the customer.

See also: Obligations of nominees.

3

Involuntary or court appointed payment nominee + Read more ...

If a guardianship order is in place with the Protective Office/Guardianship Authority (or other state based authority such as Public Trustee), the guardianship authority must be contacted about the request for an urgent payment.

This is because the customer is legally unable to handle their own affairs. In most cases, the customer's funds are arranged in such a manner by the nominee there is no capacity for funds to be redirected.

The existence of this type of nominee arrangement can be checked on the Nominee Relationship Summary (NORS) screen on the nominee's record. The NORS screen will show INV - involuntary (CRT or PAI).

Contact with the nominee must be detailed in the DOC.

See also: Appointing nominees.

4

Payment via a group payment schedule + Read more ...

Contact the organisation's administrator before making a decision. Contacting the organisation's administrator is considered appropriate to make sure that all care and consideration is given to the welfare of the customer.

The payment must be made via the organisation, unless the organisation's administrator directs otherwise.

The urgent payment issued via the schedule can only be issued to the nominated institution on Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) B, as a payment issued on any other EPED will fail to deliver. See Group payment schedules and payments to organisations.

An administrator cannot request an urgent payment on behalf of the customer, unless the administrator is the customer's correspondence nominee.

See also: Group payment schedules and payments to organisations.

5

Third party arrangements - partner or third party with permission to enquire (PPE) + Read more ...

A third party who only has PPE cannot apply for an urgent payment on the customer's behalf. The request must be rejected.

6

Dependent Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY under 18 years of age + Read more ...

These customers who have their regular payments directed to their parents may qualify for an urgent payment. However, the payment must be directed to the parents unless the parent directs otherwise.

A parent cannot claim an urgent payment on behalf of their dependent child, unless the parent is the customer's correspondence nominee.
