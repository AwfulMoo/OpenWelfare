This page contains examples of students claiming ABSTUDY to live away from home to do primary level study and a link to the National Course Approvals sub-site.

ABSTUDY and the away from home rate for primary level study

This table describes situations where students are claiming ABSTUDY to live away from home to do primary level study.

Item

Description

1

Approval of away from home rate where a student must repeat the final year of primary school due to lack of necessary skills

Kyeema comes from a remote community in South Australia. Kyeema completes primary school and is approved for the away from home rate to board at St Brigid's College to do first year of secondary school as there is no secondary school within reasonable travelling time of Kyeema's community.

St Brigid's College interviews students before starting and decide Kyeema lacks the skills to do secondary level study. Kyeema must repeat the last year of primary school to progress to secondary school in the subsequent year.

Kyeema is approved for the away from home rate to do primary level study because of the approval for away from home rate to do secondary school. St Brigid's College assessed Kyeema as needing to repeat the last year of primary school to successfully progress to secondary school in the next year, and St Brigid's College delivers primary level study.

2

Rejection of away from home rate for repeating the final year of primary level study at another school due to home conditions

Alkira lives in Wanaaring, New South Wales (NSW) and finished Year 6, which is the last year of primary school in NSW. Alkira is approved for away from home to study secondary level as there is no school within reasonable travelling time/distance of Wanaaring. Alkira has relatives in South Australia (SA) and the family's preference is for Alkira to stay with them. Alkira enrols in Year 7 at a South Australian school, which is the last year of primary school in SA.

Alkira is repeating the last year of primary school, but is not approved for away from home rate because secondary school is available in NSW, but the choice was to repeat a primary level again to access family support. Alkira was not required to repeat primary level by the secondary school for which we would pay away from home rate to attend.

3

Eligibility for away from home rate, prior to 1 January 2022, when repeating the final year of primary level study at another school due to a scholarship

Balun lives in Coffs Harbour, New South Wales (NSW) and completed Year 6, which is the last year of primary school in NSW. Balun won a scholarship to a Scotch College in South Australia (SA) that provides for away from home rate under ABSTUDY Policy Chapter 35. Balun will do Year 7 at Scotch College in SA.

The Department of Social Services has advised that for ABSTUDY purposes, Year 7 is considered secondary level study at Scotch College and St Peter's College in SA. A student who has completed the final year of primary school in another state and has a scholarship at either Scotch College or St Peter's College for Year 7 is eligible for ABSTUDY away from home rate.

However, Year 7 at all other schools in SA is primary level study and away from home rate is not approved. Even if a student has a qualifying scholarship and is doing the last year of primary school again, the requirements for away from home rate are not met. This is because there has been no approval for away from home rate to do secondary study at the South Australian school, and the secondary school has not required the student to repeat the last year of primary school to access secondary school. The student is simply doing the age-appropriate school year in SA.
