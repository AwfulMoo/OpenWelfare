Non-Medicare claiming documents submitted using digital self service channels

Occasionally customers will submit non–claims-related documents via the Medicare online account (MOA) for processing. Note: do not process documents for anyone other than the MOA account holder. If such documents are received, contact the MOA account holder and tell them the documents have to be lodged by the person themselves.

Table 1: this table explains what to do if a non-claimable or random document is received.

Document

Action

Medicare Two-way Claim form
  • If the Two-way form is clearly legible, all required sections have been completed and the form has been signed, process the form. See Process Medicare Two-way claims
  • If the form is not legible or signed, call the claimant and request they sign the form and resubmit
  • If the customer is unable to be contacted via phone, write to the customer using the Z1485 'Medicare two way claim rejected/incomplete', located in Medicare public letters

FA081 - Newborn Child Declaration

Follow these steps:

  • confirm customer’s service request as indicated on the FA081 form
  • confirm child is already enrolled in CDMS, including Safety net registration and My health record (if applicable):
    • Yes - NFA process submitted Medicare claim
    • No - enrol as per FA081 request and process submitted Medicare claim
  • confirm if FA081 has been lodged via the Customer First Workload Management system:
    • Yes - notate that relevant transaction/s are complete. Key 'DL' in NEXT field to display the Document List (DL) screen. Check for all MAENROL DOCs (Medicare Enrolment OLC, Medicare Enrolment FA081). All DOCs that relate to the MAENROL on the record will need to be annotated and completed
    • No - if the customer has requested Centrelink services, see Other images below for additional instructions

Family Safety Net Confirmation form

Process the form.

See Family registration and confirmation for Medicare Safety Net

Note: as customers are required to sign into the MOA or mobile app in order to submit this form, identity does not have to be verified as per step 1 in the instructions.

Medicare enrolment form (for partner or dependant)

Medicare enrolment forms cannot be submitted via Medicare online accounts or the Express Plus Medicare mobile app. Applicants should send the MS004 enrolment form and supporting documents by email or post.

Post to Medicare Enrolment Services

Email to Medicare Enrolment Services

If the form is incomplete or missing appropriate supporting documentation, contact customer by phone or send letter z0137, located in Medicare public letters.

Medicare Claim forms

Process as per usual for manual claim forms submitted in person or via post. Ensure the invoice/receipt is also attached and all claim details are clearly legible.

Other images – for example, proof of identity documents or non-Medicare - related forms (for example, Centrelink, Child Support, AODR, PBS, CAPS and DVA)

Save a copy of the form(s)/document(s) to the desktop.

If there is not a program reference number, for example a CRN for Centrelink, or it is not clear what the item is in relation to, for example, identity documents, call the customer to obtain details.

Forward documents via email to the appropriate business area. See Contact details > For non-Medicare related forms. The work item will be placed into the relevant area’s processing queue.

Ensure the form(s)/document(s) are deleted from the desktop.

Contact details

For non-Medicare related forms - when other images are submitted such as proof of identity documents or non-Medicare related forms, the documents should be forwarded to the appropriate business area by email

Processing issues for claims submitted using digital self service channels

Table 2: this table describes common processing issues and actions for claims submitted using digital self service channels.

Issue

Action

No image in FileNet / claim details not present or legible in FileNet

The claim must be rejected with reason code 453-455, if:

  • there is no image
  • the image available in Filenet is not legible, or
  • some of the claim details are not visible, for example patient name, item number

See step 7 in the Process Claims table on the Process page

Image in FileNet is of a photo of a screenshot or paper copy of the invoice/receipt

Accept photographs, provided:

  • all required claim details are clearly visible
  • match what is shown on the claim, and
  • there is nothing to suggest the statement has been altered or falsified

Image in File Net is of a Lodgement Advice or Statement of Claim and Benefit

If the claim is not present on the patient's history, process the claim using the details on the lodgement advice and add note to CDMS stating lodgement advice used.

If the service is present on patient's history, process the claim and reject with RSN code 162 as a duplicate claim and add note stating duplicate document was uploaded on CDMS.

Image in FileNet is of a random object (for example, family pet) and not receipt/invoice

If image is not part of the fraud reporting process, reject the claim with reason code 452. MOA claimants must attach evidence of service in order for a claim to be paid.

Claim lodged contains date of service greater than 2 years

If the invoice lodged has a date of service older than 2 years, reject the claim with 621.

If the customer has keyed a different date than listed on the invoice and it is still greater than 2 years, modify the date of service and reject with 621.

Claim has a 1-APA # message

Check the invoice uploaded in Filenet. This issue occurs when the customer has keyed a payee provider in a pathology claim.

If the PAYP line is not required, delete the PAYP line and continue assessing.

Claim is suspected to be fraudulent

See Medicare suspected fraud and Business Integrity (BI) flags.

If a claim is confirmed as fraudulent, do not pay the claim; reject it with reason code 621.

Claim has already been paid

Reject the claim with reason code 162.

Claim has an ‘8 – CONFIRM’ or ‘8 - MPI WARN’ message

When the Service Officer places the cursor under this message and presses [F1], the message 'When processing a claim via Online Claiming no further action needs to be taken PF9 to acknowledge warning, continue processing' appears.

If the service claimed is item range 13200-13260, make 2 attempts by telephone to confirm the Medicare Safety Net family composition.

For information on common ART return messages see: the resources section in In-vitro Fertilisation (IVF) and other Assisted Reproductive Technology (ART) MBS services.

Other processing issues

If there are other questions or issues specifically related to the assessment of the claim, discuss with a Programme Support Officer (PSO) or Team Leader.

System issues when processing claims submitted using digital self service channels

Table 3: this table describes system issues that require a work around or additional processes and referral.

Issue

Action

Item or notional charge on invoice or receipt is $10,000.00 or greater

Medicare online accounts and the Express Plus Medicare mobile app will not allow a customer to enter an item with a charge greater than $9,999.99 due to limitations on how many numbers can be keyed in Mainframe.

Where a customer has keyed an amount different to their invoice to enable them to lodge their claim online it can be considered to be a minor discrepancy and the claim edited to allow processing.

See Process claim submitted using digital self service channels if there is a minor discrepancy above for how to modify a claim.

See Charges $10,000 or more (greater than $9,999.99) for Medicare patient claims for which fields to amend and the values to use.

Return message “the EFT details are invalid”

When processing MOA claims, a return message displays: “the EFT details are invalid”. Staff at this point are unable to finalise the claim.

Ordinarily, staff will follow the process in Operational Blueprint 011-43050000 to address this, however, the MOA screen is missing the "EFT field" required to perform the function.

The workaround in the MOA screen is to clear the account name, BSB & account details from the fields to enable the staff to finalise the claim. This will then allow the system to record the claim as "HELD" in the claiming history (takes overnight) screen, until correct banking details are supplied.

Claims giving an Abend error

Service officers experiencing this error are asked to process the claim manually and refer to a PSO or team leader, who will review why the claim abended and take appropriate action.

A system fix is being developed to prevent known instances of the issue from occurring.

Examples of amendments when processing claims with a minor discrepancy

Acceptable

The following are examples of minor discrepancies between the claim details in Mainframe and the evidence in FileNet where Service Officers can correct the error and pay the claim:

  • Charge is displayed as $3.75 instead of $37.50
  • The date of service is 21/01/17 instead of 2/11/17
  • The provider number is 12354AB instead of 12345AB
  • Payee and servicing provider details or servicing and referring provider details have been transposed
  • Referring information not submitted
  • PayP line not entered
  • Additional lines can only be keyed when the service/s claimed are for the same provider
  • Additional items on invoice/receipt need to be added to the claim
  • Realignment of items numbers to allow for payment, that is Anaesthetic accounts
  • Assistant Surgeon items submitted on Surgeons invoice
  • Notional Charge invoices, where grouping of items for assessment is required
  • Amendment of out-patient invoices to in-patient
  • Invoices where the charge for an item or notional charge is $10,000.00 or greater and the claimant has entered a different amount (see Processing issues for claims submitted using digital self service channels table)
  • The in hospital indicator on the invoice is different to the claim
  • The date of service can be changed if the customer has picked an incorrect date that is displayed in the supporting information

Not acceptable

The below are examples of discrepancies between the claim details in Mainframe and the evidence in FileNet where Service Officers should not correct the error and should reject the claim with the relevant reason code:

  • An entirely different charge is keyed, for example $100 instead of $64.75, unless the item or notional charge on the invoice or receipt is $10,000.00 or greater, see Processing issues for claims submitted using digital self service channels table
  • An entirely different date of service has been keyed, for example 21/12/17 instead of 30/04/18
  • An entirely different provider number to any listed on the invoice has been keyed, for example 987654ZY instead of 12345AB
  • An entirely different patient, for example a newborn that is not yet enrolled on the Medicare card
  • If customer’s claim states that the service has been paid but the uploaded image indicates that claim has not been paid or fails to confirm payment has been made (or vice versa), the Service Officer is to reject every service in claim with reason code 455
