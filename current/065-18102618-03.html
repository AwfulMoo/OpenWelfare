This page contains information about preliminary checks needed before undertaking a home care or residential care means assessment.

On this page:

Home care means assessment - preliminary checks

Residential care means assessment - preliminary checks

Home care means assessment - preliminary checks

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Check Customer First for RCA/CUR status + Read more ...

In Customer First:

  • key the Customer Reference Number (CRN) in the CRN/BP field
  • select the CRN/BP hyperlink

An RCA/CUR status may exist as a result of:

  • a current or a previous home care or residential care means assessment
  • an entry to care that has been finalised automatically, or
  • the care recipient having a Means Not Disclosed (MND) indicator recorded

If the care recipient has an RCA/CUR status, go to:

  • the RCIRC screen and confirm the type of care assessed
  • the RMND screen and check the Means Not Disclosed indicator

Does the care recipient have a status of RCA/CUR for a current home care entry?

  • Yes, if the MND indicator is:
    • Y, go to Step 5
    • N, the care recipient has disclosed their means or an automatic assessment completed. A reassessment is needed for any updates. See Aged care reviews - reassessments and updates
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check for entry details and care type + Read more ...

Entry details and care type must be checked in order to determine what sort of assessment is needed:

  • Pre entry -care recipient has not started care
  • Post entry -care recipient has started care

Entry details are located in:

  • Customer First
    • Check the RIS and RCIRC screens
  • Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP)
    • Check the Care recipient summary screen
  • Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS)
    • Check the Assessments & Events tab > Entry Details

If multiple entry dates exist, confirm the original entry date and any breaks in care in order to determine the correct action.

Has the care recipient started home care?

  • Yes, and the first date of entry was:
    • on or after 1 July 2014, go to Step 4
    • before 1 July 2014 and no breaks in care of more than 28 days (excluding approved leave), go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Home care entry date before 1 July 2014 and no break in care + Read more ...

In ACMPS > Assessment & Events > Entry Details

  • select hyperlink in the Event ID field
  • check the Opt In by Care Recipient field

Has the care recipient opted in to post 1 July 2014 means testing arrangements?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the care recipient remains grandparented
    • A means assessment is not needed
    • Cancel the RCA/HOM activity in Customer First
    • Record the action taken via Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care MA Rejected-OTH. Ensure Fast Note is completed
    • Procedure ends here

4

Determine if DVA is responsible for the means assessment + Read more ...

The care recipient’s income source determines whether Services Australia or Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) completes the means assessment:

  • Centrelink or DVA income support payment, or
  • self-funded retiree

Note: where Services Australia is responsible for the means assessment of a care recipient receiving a DVA pension, the care recipient is considered a self-funded retiree.

If an Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care is lodged, check for a:

  • DVA reference number, and
  • payment type

In Customer First:

  • Check the Customer Summary screen for a DVA Unique Identification Number (UIN) recorded
  • Check Reference Number (NU) screen for DVA reference number

In ACMPS, check Identification Numbers tab for a DVA reference number or UIN.

Is there an indication DVA is responsible for the means assessment?

  • Yes, go to Responsible Departments task card in Resources
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Home care recipient changes to a new home care service + Read more ...

Has the care recipient moved to a new home care service within 28 days of leaving the previous home care service?

  • Yes,
    • A new means assessment is not needed
    • Complete the RCA/HOM activity with the details that are currently on record
    • If the care recipient has lodged an online or paper Aged Care - Calculation of your cost of care form, where applicable, update income and asset changes
    • If the care recipient has since been discharged from most recent service, update the Customer First RCIRC screen with discharge details
    • Record action taken via Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Assessment > HOM Care Assessment - FIN. Include change of service details
    • Attach the Fast Note to RCA/HOM and finalise
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Check if the care recipient is discharged or deceased + Read more ...

To determine if the care recipient is:

  • discharged,
    • Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP) – Care recipient summary screen for entry and departure dates, Care recipient – Event Summary for departure reasons
    • check ACMPS > Assessment & Events > Departure Details for a departure event
  • deceased,
    • check the DL screen in Customer First for notification of death coding
    • DEA screen in Customer First for notification of death coding

Is the care recipient discharged, deceased or lodging a means assessment for a previous period of care?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, and the care recipient:
    • is not in care, see Aged care means assessment - home care pre-entry
    • has entered care, including when a care recipient is currently Means Not Disclosed (MND), see Aged care means assessment - home care - post-entry
    • Procedure ends here

7

The care recipient is discharged or deceased + Read more ...

If a care recipient is discharged or deceased:

  • a means assessment may not be needed
  • ACMPS automatically applies care subsidy reduction (CSR) to zero
  • do not send 'Request for further information' letters

If the care recipient has:

  • died:
    • before entering care, go to Step 8
    • after entering care, go to Step 9
  • discharged (not deceased), go to Step 9

8

Care recipient has died before entering care + Read more ...

If the care recipient is coded as deceased in Customer First the RCA/HOM activity cannot be completed:

  • cancel the RCA/HOM activity
  • record the action taken in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care MA Rejected-OTH stating that the care recipient died prior to starting care
  • attach the Fast Note to RCA/HOM and finalise
  • Procedure ends here

If the care recipient is not coded as deceased in Customer First, reject the means assessment:

  • Select (S) the RCA/HOM activity
  • Go to the BA screen and reject the RCA/HOM:
    • SVC RSN: RCA
    • Action: REJ
    • Reason: OTH
    • Effect Date: Date of Event (DOV)
    • Notes: REJ-OTH Customer deceased before entry to home care
  • Record the action taken in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care MTA REJECTED-OTHER stating the care recipient died before starting care
  • Attach the Fast Note to RCA/HOM and finalise
  • Complete a death action if needed. See Death of an income support customer or their partner or see the DOC on the record if notification of the death is awaiting processing
  • Procedure ends here

9

Care recipient has died after entering care or discharged (not deceased) + Read more ...

If the care recipient is:

  • deceased, and the death is coded in Customer First:
    • the RCA/HOM activity cannot be completed
    • cancel the claim
    • record the action taken via Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care MA Rejected-OTH
    • If a manual letter is requested by the estate, go to Step 11, otherwise procedure ends here
  • discharged from care and is not deceased or is deceased and the death is not coded in Customer First:
    • go to Step 10

10

Care recipient is discharged from care or deceased and death is not coded in Customer First + Read more ...

If a care recipient is discharged from care or deceased (death not coded in Customer First), complete a means assessment if all supporting documentation is provided.

If a means assessment can be completed based on the information available:

  • complete the means assessment, see Aged care means assessment - home care post-entry
  • If a manual letter has been requested go to Step 11, otherwise procedure ends here

If a means assessment cannot be completed based on the information available:

  • do not issue 'Request for Further Information' letters
  • select (S) the RCA/HOM activity
  • go to BA screen and reject the RCA/HOM:
    • SVC RSN: RCA
    • Action: REJ
    • Reason: OTH
    • Effect Date: Date of Event (DOV)
    • Notes: REJ-OTH Customer discharged from home care before means assessment completed
  • record action taken in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care MA Rejected-OTH. Attach Fast Note to RCA/HOM and finalise
  • copy the Fast Note text into ACSP Care recipient in notes, see the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table
  • update the RCIRC screen with the discharge details
  • If a manual letter has been requested go to Step 11, otherwise procedure ends here

11

Fee letter requested + Read more ...

A manual fee letter confirming CSR to zero is only sent when it has been requested.

When a care recipient is deceased only the executor of the estate is able to request a manual letter. Evidence is needed to verify the person requesting the letter is the executor or administrator of the estate.

Create a manual letter:

  • Care recipient discharged prior to fees being set and is not deceased:
    • issue manual HC-9D series
  • Care recipient deceased prior to fees being set:
    • issue manual HC-9E series
  • Upload the manual letters to Customer First
  • Add ACSP Care recipient notes stating a manual letter has been sent
  • Procedure ends here

Residential care means assessment - preliminary checks

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check for entry details and care type + Read more ...

Entry details are located in:

  • Customer First - the RIS and RCIRC screens
  • Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP) – Care recipient summary screen
    • see the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to search for a care recipient – (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table
  • Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS) - check Assessments & Events tab
    • Entry and Departure Details confirm dates
    • If home care departure overlaps with residential care entry, send email for correction, template in Resources tab, hold RCA/NCL for 7 days for correction to be completed

If multiple entry dates exist, confirm the original entry date and any breaks in care in order to determine the correct action.

If a care recipient's responsible department changed from Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) to Services Australia, the date DVA lost responsibility for the means assessment displays as the date of entry on the RCIRC screen.

Has the care recipient entered residential care?

  • Yes, and the first date of entry to residential care was:
    • on or after 1 July 2014, go to Step 3
    • before 1 July 2014 and no breaks in care of more than 28 days (excluding approved leave), go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Residential care recipient date of entry before 1 July 2014 with no break in care + Read more ...

A means assessment is completed when a care recipient:

  • has opted in to post 1 July 2014 assessment rules. To confirm:
    • Locate care recipient in ACSP
    • From Care recipient tab, select Event summary
    • Locate the care recipient’s current entry into permanent residential care and select the status of Accepted to view entry details
    • Select Next twice to get to Step 3 which will identify if a care recipient was in care prior to 1 July 2014, and if so, whether they have opted in to post 1 July 2014 scheme
  • is seeking a means assessment to obtain a fee estimate as they are considering opting in to the post 1 July 2014 assessment rules

Has the care recipient met one of the above criteria?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, care recipient remains grandparented. An Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care may have been lodged to advise of changes to their income and/or assets or to request an assessment:
    • See Aged care means assessment - residential care pre 1 July 2014 - income assessment, and
    • Aged care means assessment - residential care pre 1 July 2014 - assets assessment for more information
    • Procedure ends here

3

Determine if DVA is the responsible department + Read more ...

The care recipient's income source determines whether Services Australia or DVA complete the means assessment:

  • Centrelink or DVA income support payment, or
  • self-funded retiree

Note: where Services Australia is responsible for the means assessment of a care recipient in receipt of DVA pension, the care recipient is considered a self-funded retiree.

If an Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care is lodged check for a DVA Unique Identification Number (UIN) or DVA reference number and payment type in all systems:

  • Customer First:
    • Check the Customer Summary screen
    • Check Reference Number (NU) screen
  • ACMPS:
    • Check Identification Numbers tab
  • In ACSP
    • Check Care recipient details screen

Is there an indication DVA could be responsible for the means assessment?

  • Yes, go to Responsible Departments task card in Resources
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check for RCA/CUR status + Read more ...

In Customer First:

  • key the Customer Reference Number (CRN) in the CRN/BP field
  • select the CRN/BP hyperlink on the customer record

An RCA/CUR status may exist as a result of:

  • a current or previous home care or residential care means assessment
  • an entry to care, or
  • the care recipient having a Means Not Disclosed (MND) indicator recorded

If the care recipient has an RCA/CUR status go to:

  • the RCIRC screen and confirm the type of care assessed
  • the RMND screen and check the Means Not Disclosed indicator

Does the care recipient have a Customer First status of RCA/CUR for residential care?

  • Yes, RCA/CUR and the MND indicator is Y:
    • go to Aged care means assessment - residential care post 1 July 2014 - post entry
  • Yes, RCA/CUR and MND indicator is N or blank:
    • If an Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care has been lodged as the care recipient is considering moving to a new facility, see Aged care means assessment - residential care post 1 July 2014 - pre-entry
    • Care recipient is currently RCA/CUR-MTA and has now entered care, see Aged care means assessment - residential care post 1 July 2014 - post entry
    • If an Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care has been lodged for the purpose of updating or correcting existing income and assets, see Aged care reviews - reassessments and updates
  • No, not RCA/CUR for residential care, go to Step 5

5

Check if care recipient is discharged or is deceased + Read more ...

A means assessment may be lodged for a previous period of care.

To determine if the care recipient is:

  • discharged:
    • check Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP) – Care recipient summary screen for entry and departure dates, Care recipient – Event Summary for departure reasons
    • see also, the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to search for a care recipient – (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table
  • deceased:
    • Check the DL screen in Customer First for any notification of death, and
    • DEA screen in Customer First for notification of death coding

Is the care recipient discharged, deceased or lodging a means assessment for a previous period of care?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, if the care recipient:
    • has not entered care, see Aged care means assessment - residential care post 1 July 2014 - pre-entry
    • has entered care, including when a care recipient is currently Means Not Disclosed (MND), see Aged care means assessment - residential care post 1 July 2014 - post entry
    • Procedure ends here

6

The care recipient is discharged or deceased + Read more ...

If a care recipient is discharged or deceased prior to completion of a means assessment, the aged care system automatically applies CSR to zero for the means tested care fees (MTCF). A fee is not set and a fee letter is not issued. A care recipient remains liable for their accommodation costs and must complete a means assessment to be assessed for assistance with accommodation costs (low means) as the provider will not receive any accommodation supplement payments.

No further information is requested if there is insufficient information to complete an assessment.

A means assessment will be referred for a manual assessment when it is signed by:

  • the care recipient after discharge from care
  • executors/administrators of the deceased care recipient's estate and written authority has been provided. See Requests for information after someone has died, or
  • the care recipient or authorised third party prior to discharge where a care recipient was previously assessed as Means Not Disclosed (MND) or is correcting a previous means assessment

Does this means assessment need to be referred for a manual assessment?

  • Yes,
    • Refer to Aged Care New Claim - Experienced staff for a manual assessment
    • Unassign the work item from the Customer First inbox and recategorise as ZIAM_RCA_DEA
    • Aged Care staff with New Claim - Experienced skill tag, see Aged care means assessment - manual assessment
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, if the care recipient:
    • died before entering care, go to Step 7
    • died after entering care, or discharged (not deceased), go to Step 8

7

Care recipient has died before entering care + Read more ...

If the care recipient is coded as deceased in Customer First the RCA/NCL activity cannot be completed:

  • Cancel the RCA/NCL activity
  • Record action taken in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care>Assessment>Aged Care MA Rejected-OTH stating that the care recipient died before entry into residential care
  • Procedure ends here

If the care recipient is not coded as deceased in Customer First, reject the means assessment:

  • Select (S) the RCA/NCL activity:
  • Go to the BA screen and reject the RCA/NCL
    • SVC RSN: RCA
    • Action: REJ
    • Reason: OTH
    • Effect Date: Date of Event (DOV)
    • Notes: REJ-OTH Customer deceased before entry to residential care
  • Record action taken in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care>Assessment>Aged Care MA Rejected-OTH
  • Attach the Fast Note to RCA/NCL and finalise
  • Complete a death action if needed. See Death of an income support customer or their partner or the DOC on the record if notification of the death is awaiting processing
  • Procedure ends here

8

Care recipient has died after entering care or discharged (not deceased) + Read more ...

If a care recipient is coded as deceased in Customer First, the means assessment cannot be completed in Customer First.

If the care recipient is:

  • deceased and the death has been coded in Customer First:
  • cancel the RCA/NCL activity
  • In ACSP, from Care recipient tab, select Care recipient notes, Subject is Other
  • In Notes field add: Means assessment could not be completed prior to discharge
  • Select Submit to save Notes added
  • If there is enough information to show that the care recipient’s resident status would have been low means, create a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Enquiry > Aged Care Discharged CR - Man Let Request to request a manual letter
  • If there is enough information to show that the care recipient’s resident status would have been not low means, record action taken via Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care MA Rejected-OTH including details of the means information used to determine the resident status
    • Procedure ends here
  • deceased and the death is not coded in Customer First, go to Step 9
  • discharged from care and is not deceased, go to Step 9

9

Care recipient is discharged from care or deceased and death is not coded in Customer First + Read more ...

A means assessment can be completed when:

  • all required documentation has been provided, or
  • care recipient was eligible for an automatic means assessment

Refer to Aged care means assessment for information on when a form is required.

If the care recipient receives an income support payment, has vacated the former principal home and is (or was) a member of a couple, coding may be required for a couple separated due to illness. The date of receipt for this update will be the date of the auto entry event unless there is evidence of an earlier notification date. If there was earlier notification of the care recipient entering care:

  • cancel the RCA/NCL activity
  • complete the illness separated coding in a separate activity
  • reindex the cancelled assessment activity
  • see Assessment of circumstances for a couple separated due to illness for more information.

Can the means assessment be completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 11

10

Care recipient discharged from care and means assessment is able to be completed + Read more ...

Complete the following:

  • Complete means assessment as per process in Aged care means assessment - residential care post 1 July 2014 - post entry
  • Record action taken in a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Assessment > RES Care Assessment – FIN including note that means assessment was completed after discharge
  • Attach the Fast Note to RCA/NCL and finalise activity
  • See the Resources page for the Aged Care Fee Calculator using income and assets from date of entry to calculate the resident status of care recipient
  • Update the RCIRC screen with discharge details and finalise via AR screen
  • Complete death action if needed. Refer to DOC on record if notification of death is awaiting processing, see Death of an income support customer or their partner
  • If the result of the assessment is that the care recipient is:
    • low means, create a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Enquiry > Aged Care Discharged CR - Man Let Request to request a manual letter
    • not low means, CSR to zero has been applied and no further action is required
  • Procedure ends here

11

Care recipient discharged from care and means assessment is not able to be completed + Read more ...

Note: if RCA/NCL is being rejected, to correct error E116AT - AC Home Ownership GFN or GFH is only for Residential Care Recipient: go to ACS screen and copy the previous details coded on ACS.

Complete the following:

  • Select (S) the RCA/NCL activity
  • Confirm entry details on RCIRC screen
  • Go to the BA screen and reject the RCA/NCL:
    • SVC RSN: RCA
    • Action: REJ
    • Reason: OTH
    • Effect Date: Date of Event (DOV)
    • Notes: REJ-OTH Customer discharged from residential care before means assessment completed
  • Record action taken via Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care MA Rejected-OTH include advice that there was further information required for means assessment to be completed
  • Attach Fast Note to RCA/NCL and finalise
  • Update the RCIRC screen with the discharge details
  • In ACSP:
    • Go to Care recipient tab, select Care recipient notes
    • Create care recipient note
    • Subject is Other
    • In Notes field add: Means assessment could not be completed prior to discharge
    • Select Submit

Complete a death action if needed, see Death of an income support customer or their partner or refer to DOC on record if notification of death is awaiting processing.
