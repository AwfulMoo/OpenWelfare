For Centrelink International Services (CIS) staff only

This page contains the process to finalise a medical assessment in relation to a foreign pension.

On this Page:

Medical information and referrals

Reviews and finalising assessments

Medical information and referrals

Table 1: This table describes the process required to obtain medical information and make referrals for assessments.

Step

Action

1

Foreign medical assessment reviews + Read more ...

Reviews are set to follow up return of international medical forms requested from customers and foreign medical reports by Health Professional Advisory Unit (HPAU).

Has a review in relation to a foreign medical assessment become due?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Return of foreign medical correspondence + Read more ...

Returned correspondence may be:

  • international medical forms been returned by the customer, or
  • an agreed foreign medical reports been returned by HPAU, or
  • a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) reports for New Zealand or Norway, or
  • a severely disabled assessment completed by a Disability Officer in International Services (CIS)

Has foreign medical correspondence been returned?

  • Yes:
    • for forms returned by a customer, go to Step 5
    • for JCA reports or foreign medical reports returned by HPAU, see Step 6 in Table 2
    • a severely disabled assessment, see Step 8 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Receipt of foreign invalidity claim or liaison request for medical review + Read more ...

No specific forms are required for Canadian, Indian or Maltese invalidity claims.

Is the medical assessment for an invalidity pension from Canada, India or Malta?

  • Yes, see Step 8 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check for recent medical information + Read more ...

The Resources page contains a link to the International Programme homepage for access to international medical forms and approved text for letters.

CIS are responsible for ensuring current and valid assessment of the customer's work capacity is available on the customer record.

Check the medical information on the customer's record.

Is recent medical information already on the customer record?

  • Yes, for:
    • New Zealand or Norway, see Step 8 in Table 2
    • other countries, go to Step 5
  • No, for:
    • New Zealand or Norway, arrange for a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA)
    • other countries, send a XOB999 using the approved text with the international medical forms to the customer
    • Set a manual review for 28 days to follow up progress. Procedure ends here

5

Referral to Health Professionals Advisory Unit (HPAU) + Read more ...

The Resources page contains a table of agreed medical forms for each country and a link to the International Programme homepage for information on referrals to HPAU.

For all other agreement countries except New Zealand and Norway, specifically agreed forms are required. HPAU are responsible for completing all specifically agreed forms.

Complete the webform to send referral to HPAU requesting completion of the relevant forms or specialist reports and set a manual review for 8 weeks.

Procedure ends here.

Reviews and finalising assessments

Table 2: This table describes the process required to review progress of assessments and send reports overseas.

Step

Action

1

Foreign medical assessment reviews + Read more ...

Reviews may be set to follow up return of:

  • medical forms by the customer
  • Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) reports or agreed medical reports by HPAU
  • severely disabled assessments by Disability Officer in International Services (CIS)

Has the customer failed to return requested medical forms?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, for reviews to follow up:
    • severely disabled assessments, go to Step 2
    • HPAU referrals, go to Step 3
    • JCA referrals, go to Step 4

2

Contact Disability Officer in CIS + Read more ...

The Resources page contains a link to the CIS homepage for contact details for CIS.

For severely disabled assessments for the Netherlands, the Disability Officers always complete the proforma. For New Zealand, the proforma is only completed where the Disability Officer disagrees with the JCA recommendation.

Contact the relevant CIS team.

Has the severely disabled assessment been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, set a further manual review to follow up and record details in a DOC. Procedure ends here

3

Contact Health Professionals Advisory Unit (HPAU) + Read more ...

The Resources page contains a link to the International Programme homepage for contact details for HPAU.

Have HPAU indicated that the customer has not complied with requests to provide information or attend appointments?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, set a further manual review to follow up completion of the agreed report. Record details in a DOC. Procedure ends here

4

Contact Assessment Services + Read more ...

For information on roles in managing JCA attendance and contacting Assessment Services, see Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) and Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) appointments.

Have Assessment Services indicated that the customer has not complied with requests to attend appointments?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, set a manual review to follow up completion of the JCA report. Record details in a DOC. Procedure ends here

5

Requirement to claim or maintain a foreign pension + Read more ...

In some cases, customers may be required to claim or maintain an existing comparable foreign payment, including foreign invalidity pensions.

Affected customers who fail to do not do so may have Australian payments suspended or be subject to deprivation rules.

Note: customers may be exempt from these requirements due to a medical condition.

See Foreign pension claims.

Contact the customer to discuss the requirement to provide information or undergo a medical assessment.

Does the customer agree to provide the information or undergo the assessment?

  • Yes, if necessary, contact Assessment Services to rebook JCA or resend customer forms or refer to HPAU again. Set a further manual review to follow up and record details in a DOC. Procedure ends here
  • No, if the customer is:
    • required to claim or maintain the foreign invalidity pension, follow the steps in Assisting customers to claim a foreign pension or Assisting customers to maintain an existing foreign pension. Procedure ends here
    • not required to claim or maintain the foreign invalidity pension, go to Step 8

6

New Zealand and the Netherlands + Read more ...

In addition to agreed medical assessments, for invalidity pensions from New Zealand or the Netherlands, a severely disabled assessment is also required.

Is the foreign medical assessment for New Zealand or the Netherlands?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Severely disabled assessments + Read more ...

For severely disabled assessments for the Netherlands, the Disability Officers always complete the international medical proforma. For New Zealand, the proforma is only completed where the Disability Officer disagrees with the JCA recommendation.

Has a severely disabled assessment been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, see Disability Support Pension (DSP) severely disabled assessments for International Agreements. Procedure ends here

8

Sending medical assessments to agreement countries + Read more ...

Once all necessary documents are available, copies of all relevant external medical information and agreed reports must be sent to the agreement partner with a liaison form.

For:

  • invalidity pension claims, see Processing a claim for a foreign pension
  • requested medical reviews, see Agreement liaisons, NZ CICs and exchange of information

Note: where the customer has not complied with requests for medical information or examinations, this should be noted on the liaison form.
