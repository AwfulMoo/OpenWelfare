The above percentages are Standard Rates. From 7 March 2022, families with multiple children in care aged 5 or under may receive an increased CCS rate up to 30% higher for eligible children, capped at 95%. This is known as Multiple Child Subsidy.

Customers can still claim CCS even if their family income estimate is equal to or above the higher income limit, only if they actually intend to use child care. If their ATI ends up being under the higher income limit, the subsidy they are entitled to receive is paid at reconciliation.

Information for previous years is available on the Child Care Subsidy - income thresholds, annual subsidy cap and hour rate page.

Higher rate of CCS - Multiple Child Subsidy

Table 2: this table shows examples of how the higher rate of CCS (Multiple Child Subsidy) applies.

Item

Description

1

Carmila’s income estimate entitles Camilla to a CCS rate of 50%.

Camilla has 3 children in child care aged 5, 3 and 2.

From 7 March 2022, the:

  • 5 year old's rate will remain at 50% because they will be the ‘Standard Rate’ child
  • CCS rate for the 3 and 2 year old children, who are the ‘Higher rate’ children, will increase by the full 30%. This is because their income tested CCS rate is more than 30% lower than the CCS cap of 95% for higher rate children (50% + 30% = 80%)

2

Danielle’s income estimate entitles Danielle to a CCS rate of 75.85%.

Danielle has 2 children in child care aged 4 and 1.

From 7 March 2022, the:

  • 4 year old's rate will remain at 75.85% because they will be the ‘Standard Rate’ child
  • CCS rate for the 1 year old child, who is the ‘Higher rate’ child, will increase but not by the full 30%. This is because the CCS is capped at 95% for higher rate children 95% (75.85% + 19.15% = 95%)

Combined families - MCS

Table 4: This table contains examples of Higher CCS (Multiple Child Subsidy) assessments for combined families.

Item

Description

1

Combined family - one child in each parent record

Mick and Carla are partnered and are both individually eligible for CCS for their children who are from previous relationships:

  • Mick’s child Robby, who is five, and
  • Carla’s child Mindy, who is four

With only one child each in the group aged 5 and under, neither of them would be eligible for higher CCS (multiple child subsidy) from the 7 March 2022 implementation date.

However, from July 2022 they will be recognised by the system as a combined family. Robby the five year old would be identified as the Standard Rate Child so Robby's CCS% would stay at the same income-tested rate.

Mindy would become a Higher Rate Child and would become entitled to the Standard Rate + 30% (capped at 95%). The date of effect for this increase would be 7 March 2022, so any arrears owing from this date will be paid to Carla in July.

If Mick and Carla do not want to wait for the arrears in July, they may access the increase earlier by having one parent claim CCS for both children.

2

Combined family - multiple children in each parent record

Darren and Sharon are partnered and they each have children from previous relationships who attend child care.

Darren is CCS current for children:

  • Barry, aged 5
  • Terrence, 4
  • Gwyneth - Anastacia, 3

Sharon is CCS current for children

  • Jane, 4
  • Abigail, 3
  • Boris, 2

From 7 March 2022, as long as they both are entitled to a CCS rate greater than 0%:

  • Sharon and Darren will each have a Standard Rate child in their own record (Barry and Jane), and
  • any eligible younger children will attract the Higher (Multiple Child) Rate of CCS

From July, the system will have the ability to assess children from both records as one unit. This means:

  • Barry will become the Standard Rate child for the whole family (as Barry is the oldest child aged 5 and under), and
  • Jane will change to a Higher Rate child

CCS arrears for Jane for the period 7 March to July will pay automatically to Sharon in July.

If Darren and Sharon wish to gain access to their higher rate of CCS entitlement immediately rather than waiting for the arrears in July, they may choose for one member of the couple to claim CCS for all the children earlier.

Otherwise they will be reassessed automatically in July 2022 and paid arrears for any CCS owing to them from the 7 March 2022.
