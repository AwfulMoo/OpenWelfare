For Families and Child Care trained staff only.

This page contains information for Service Officers when determining whether a trust income will affect a Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A customer's rate of payment.

Income from trusts assessed as maintenance income

This table describes how to assess income from a trust which can affect the rate of the Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A.

Step

Action

1

Initial contact + Read more ...

A Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customer or their partner receives income from a trust as child support or spousal maintenance.

For action at initial contact, or to determine if a manual assessment is required, see Non-cash maintenance income assessment for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

2

Is child or person a beneficiary of the trust? + Read more ...

They must be a beneficiary to receive income from the trust.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, there is no income to assess. Procedure ends here
  • Unsure, request trust documents. Go to Step 6

3

Has the child or the person actually received a payment from the trust? + Read more ...

If the trust is a discretionary trust, has the discretion to release the payments been exercised?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, there is no income to assess. Procedure ends here
  • Unsure, request trust documents. Go to Step 7

4

Was the trust set up by or for a deceased parent of the child? + Read more ...

  • Yes, the payment is not considered child support. Assess as ordinary income
  • No, assess as maintenance income

5

Do you need to request trust documents? + Read more ...

See need to request trust documents

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No:
    • to assess as ordinary income, go to Step 7
    • to assess as maintenance income, go to Step 8

6

Request trust documents + Read more ...

The documents are needed so that Centrelink can make a correct decision. These assessments are often complex and we need to know the complete picture.

  • Check if trust documents already provided (check Document List (DL) screen for relevant DOCs)
  • Issue a Module PT - Private Trust for income support customers only
  • Ask customer to provide, if available:
    • Trust Deed (contains information about the set up of the trust)
    • most recent income tax return of the trust including balance sheets and profit and loss report
    • most recent income tax return of the person (and partner)
  • Send written request (Q777) via the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure
  • Record details on a DOC
  • Input review on Review Registration (RVR) screen to ensure the customer is given 14 days to provide information if the request is sent on Day 1 (allow 21 days for remote area or overseas customers). See Allow extra time for mail delivery

If information has not been provided when the review is due:

  • call customer to find out when documents will be available and input review on RVR screen for that date
  • send second request, or
  • (as a last resort) cancel customer's FTB

When information is provided:

  • to assess maintenance income, go to Step 8
  • to assess ordinary income, go to Step 7

7

Assess ordinary income + Read more ...

For information about assessing income from trusts and to refer case to a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO), see Income and assets from business structures.

If income received by customer or partner:

  • invite customer to revise estimate for family assistance payments including taxable income from trust
  • for income support customers, assess and record income and assets from trust

If income received by FTB child:

Check if the FTB child income limit applies to the child and whether their income for the financial year is under the limit. See FTB child income.

Procedure ends here.

8

Assess as maintenance income + Read more ...

To record the amount received as private child support, see Recording private maintenance income on Centrelink systems.

If case is complex, see Identifying and making suitable referrals to the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO).
