CSHC Income + Read more ...

The income test for the CSHC uses adjusted taxable income (ATI) for:

  • the customer and their partner (if relevant) and
  • the applicable financial year

This includes deemed income from account-based income streams

See Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) income test and reference tax year, and Lodgement of Notice of Assessment (NOA) and other evidence of income for Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC).

ATI is the sum of:

  • taxable income, and
  • total net investment losses, and
  • target foreign income, and
  • reportable superannuation contributions, and
  • employer provided fringe benefits in excess of $1,000

Deemed income is calculated from the current balance of income streams. The deemed income is added to the ATI amount, with the combined total used in the CSHC income test.

Deeming will only apply to account-based-income steam(s) and not any other financial assets they own.

Review and submit claim + Read more ...

Customers can review and submit the online claim.

If all of the information is correct, the customer must tick I have read, understood, and accept my obligations before they can select Submit.

7

Finalising the claim + Read more ...

Customers cannot update the online claim details after they submit it. They will need to contact the Services Australia to provide information in relation to their claim. They can withdraw their claim online.

When the customer submits their claim, the system will:

  • index a new claim (NCL) activity or create a Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity on the customer's record. Day one processing includes indexing the claim if necessary
  • create a DOC
  • upload the customer contact details

The customer can view information entered from the Review Claim section of their claim.

Customers may receive online messages and notifications.

8

Next steps and printing the online claim + Read more ...

Customers can access their submitted online claim to return to the Next steps page at any time. They can also print their online claim.

2

Running Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) + Read more ...

Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) replicates the questions in the online claim.

Service Officers can run ACC when:

  • the customer is available and able to answer the questions in the claim, and
  • a customer is unable or unwilling to claim online, or helping a customer with starting the online claim

Note: If both members of a couple wish to claim and require an ACC, ACC will need to be completed for both members of the couple.

Claims started via ACC can submitted by the customer, in their online account (an active Centrelink online account is required).

When running ACC, tell the customer of any documents they need to provide or action they need to take to submit their claim. The customer will need to:

  • answer all mandatory questions
  • provide all required documents, and
  • verify their identity if a Confirmed Identify status is not recorded

Some customers may not be able to complete all of their 'Required' tasks before submitting their claim. Exceptions apply for some vulnerable customers.

3

Submitting the claim + Read more ...

Claims commenced online or via ACC cannot be submitted until all required tasks are completed and documents are provided, unless a claim submission exception applies.

Advise the customer a claim is not deemed to have been made until it is submitted. Check the customer record for any documents already provided that are relevant to a claim for CSHC.

A claim started using ACC will expire after 13 weeks if not submitted. The customer must then start a new claim. Services Australia can assess the claim in some circumstances from the original start date. See Circumstance Change Monitor for more information.

To submit their claim, the customer will need to:

  • access the draft claim to upload their documents and submit the claim. Service Officers or the customer can upload the required documents. See Step 10 in the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) Table of Claiming Age Pension
  • accept the verbal declaration

4

Completing Verbal Declaration + Read more ...

Read the customer the verbal declaration script ensuring they accept and tick the statement declaration box.

Service Officers should tick the Declaration box on the Review your claim page to confirm they have read the declaration script and the customer accepts it.

Once a verbal declaration is completed and the ACC submitted, a DOC will confirm that the verbal declaration was accepted. This DOC will display in:

  • the Notes section of Process Direct
  • Super Case (notes) of Customer First
  • the Customer Claim Summary

If the customer is identified as vulnerable and they submit the claim with outstanding documents:

  • Check the customer knows what they need to provide
  • Record on a DOC

If a customer is unable or declines to make a verbal declaration, the Service Officer must:

  • invite the customer to log on to their online services, accept the declaration and submit their claim, or
  • provide the customer with a Customer Declaration Form (CDF). Do not submit the claim without the CDF being completed and signed.

Where the customer cannot sign the CDF:

  • Send a Q004 to customer with a Customer Declaration Form (CDF) using the template in the Resources page.

As this cannot be added as a required document on Next steps, record a detailed note on the customer’s record stating the Q004 claim and signature block was issued with the expected due date.

Reminder: if a partner is eligible, available and also wishes to claim CSHC, run ACC with the partner in their own record.

4

Check documentation and scan + Read more ...

Check for the following documents for both the customer and partner (if applicable):

  • CSHC claimants need a confirmed identity status. They will need to provide documents to prove their identity if there is no ICI recorded. Original documents must be sighted, recorded and returned
  • Proof of age (must be age pension age at the time of claim lodgement)
  • Tax File Number (TFN) for Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC)
  • Other supporting documents. For example:
    • ATO Notice of Assessment
    • Tax Return
    • Payment Summary
    • Details of income stream product (SA330) or a similar schedule

For additional details see: Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) income test and reference tax year, and Lodgement of the Notice of Assessment (NOA) and other evidence of income for Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC)

Ensure the customer provides Identity and proof of age for their partner, if they are claiming CSHC on the same claim form.

Code the customer's identity documents. For more information, see Coding identity documents.

Check that all forms issued via FCCCS have been lodged. Customers will get a letter and list the documents they will need to support their claim.

Record details on a DOC:

  • if the system does not allow the coding of the proof of age document. For example, due to system limitations or
  • the customer is unable to provide one

5

Scanning + Read more ...

Scan documents if there is a need for verification.

See:

  • Coding identity documents
  • Sighting, recording and returning original documents

When receiving the CSHC paper claim, scan the paper claim and any supporting documents to the customer's record. Batch store on-site.

Scanning the claim form will create a SOA shell Work Item on the customer's record.

Service Officers will need to update the claim details into the Work Item. Use the details from the scanned claim and documents.

6

Extra details for grandfathered Energy Supplement customers + Read more ...

For customers grandfathered for Energy Supplement, bank account details (SHC) must be recorded.

If the customer is claiming within 42 days of an income support payment cancellation:

  • Update the Payment Destination Details (PAD) screen (SHC) with bank account details coded for the cancelled income support payment on the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen
  • The 'E008SH - Code SHC payment destination or ES NOT grandfathered status' warning will appear on the Assessment Warning and Error (AWE) screen when there are no bank account details recorded

For customers who have a payment nominee, record the bank account of the Payment Nominee on the PAD screen before linking the Nominee. Note: CSHC customers cannot get the Energy Supplement if their bank account details are set as cheque.

Check to ensure the correct relationship status is coded on the Marital Status (MS) screen. This ensures that the customer is paid the correct entitlement of Energy Supplement (partnered or single rate) if they want to get this payment.

Press [Enter], and complete all personal details.
