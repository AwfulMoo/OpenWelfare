Review case + Read more ...

Customer contact + Read more ...

Contact the customer to obtain the necessary information. If there is a CRN or PCC refresh or error message confirm the CRN is correct and they are eligible for PCC. The CRN/PCC can also be checked by using the Centrelink Customer First System. Staff must ensure that correct PCC is recorded on CDMS. If contact is unsuccessful, go to Step 5.

Note: if a nominee needs to be contacted and contact is unsuccessful, attempt to make contact with the customer, as above, and have them ask the nominee to call.

5

Contact unsuccessful + Read more ...

If contact was unsuccessful:

  • send a request for information letter using the standard letter template. See Letters table in Continence Aids Payment Scheme – Other requirements
  • if the error is due to an address error or a return to sender flag exists place, see Step 4 in Mail table in Continence Aids Payment Scheme – Other requirements
  • document outcome on the customer record (e.g. unable to contact, bank details letter sent, RTS etc.) and update the Comments column in the yyyymmdd Open Items/IDOCs or yyyymmdd CAPS Unpaid spreadsheet as appropriate

Go to Step 6 if customer contact was regarding a CRN or PCC refresh or error message, otherwise go to Step 8.

6

Scenarios - examples + Read more ...

The following details the possible scenarios and action where a PCC payment error has occurred:

  • Customer has passed away - CAPS entitlement is to cease. Note the payment error requires a file fix/cancellation in the Comments column in the CAPS unpaid spreadsheet
  • Customer has had a continuous PCC entitlement and is eligible to receive payment – a EXPCC error has occurred, see Step 7. Make note in the Comments column in the CAPS unpaid spreadsheet
  • Customer was not eligible to receive PCC entitlement or their eligibility to PCC entitlement has ceased - CAPS entitlement is to cease and note the payment error requires a file fix/cancellation in the Comments column in the CAPS unpaid spreadsheet
  • Customer has had a break in PCC entitlement - note in the comments column in the CAPS unpaid spreadsheet that review by the programme management team is required

7

CRN or PCC refresh or error message + Read more ...

Check details in Centrelink Customer First record to confirm CRN or valid PCC details.

Update the customer record with the correct details on CDMS.

If the customer has provided the correct CRN and their PCC entitlement is current then a refresh of the PCC details is required. Note: if a customer no longer meets eligibility due to expired PCC entitlement, their CAPS entitlement will cease and a PCC refresh is not required. If the customer's circumstances change the customer may re-apply.

Request a PCC refresh by emailing the PBS Helpdesk with the following details:

  • customer’s full name
  • Medicare card number
  • date of birth
  • CRN

Note: if a PCC error which is not related to a payment error displays while processing a CAPS application the application will need to be resubmitted once the PCC refresh has occurred.

If the PCC is incorrect or invalid and the customer has a non-neurological condition:

  • for a CAPS application reject the application and send an ended/rejected letter using the standard letter template, see Letters table in Continence Aids Payment Scheme – Other requirements
  • where the customer already had a valid CAPS entitlement, cease the CAPS entitlement. Go to Ceasing entitlement table in Continence Aids Payment Scheme – Customer contact, application, ceasing entitlement and review

Record a comment on the customer record, see Accessing system table in Continence Aids Payment Scheme – Other requirements.

8

Review + Read more ...

Where:

  • customer details have been updated, or
  • payments required a letter to be sent,

review the customer record after the pay run to check that payments have been released. Service Officers are to update the spreadsheet once that process is complete.

When all payment errors have been actioned and payments released or identified for file fix or write back update Open Items/IDOCs or CAPS Unpaid spreadsheet and forward to the Programme Management team. The Programme Management team will process file fixes or write backs to remove payments.

9

B locks or Z locks + Read more ...

Bank locks are applied to a bank account where a payment has been made to that account but errors. Bank locks are recorded in SAP/CPS. When the bank details are updated the system will attempt payment to the new bank account which does not have a lock and the bank lock will no longer apply. Occasionally bank locks do not automatically lift when bank details are updated. To determine if there is a B lock or Z lock, see step 5 in the CPS table in Continence Aids Payment Scheme – Other requirements.

To have a payment lock manually removed escalate matter to Programme Management, see Escalations table in Continence Aids Payment Scheme – Other requirements.

Record a note on the customer record, see Step 2 in Accessing system table in Continence Aids Payment Scheme – Other requirements.

Update the Comments column in the yyyymmdd Open Items/IDOCs spreadsheet.

Step

1

Fact of Death Data (FoDD) and Death Reports + Read more ...

Notification of a deceased CAPS customer can be received via a data match from the Department of Births, Deaths and Marriages (FoDD report) or notification by a member of the public (Death report).

Programme Management receive the following reports every Friday:

  • CAPS death report dd month yyyy
  • CAPS records with a FoDD update dd month yyyy
  • CAPS death report dd month yyyy processed last week

CAPS entitlements for all deceased customers are required to be ceased in the CAPS system. The entitlements need to be ceased by COB the following Wednesday so the customers do not appear on the next week's report.

The report is saved on the CAPS-administration drive by the Programme Management team and emailed to the CAPS processing team for action.

How the data is entered into CDMS (i.e. manually or automatically) determines how the customer record is updated:

  • an automatically processed date of death will result in a date of death for the customer and an entitlement end date and reason recorded in CDMS
  • manually entered data will result in only a date of death being recorded for the customer
  • the CAPS Registration screen ‘Deceased date’ field will be populated with the date of death recorded in CDMS for both automatically and manually entered data

Go to Step 4.

2

High Care report + Read more ...

A customer is not eligible to receive CAPS if they are a care recipient and the level of care required is:

  • a high ADL domain category, or
  • a high CHC domain category, or
  • a high behaviour domain category, or
  • a medium domain category in at least two domains, or

the level of care is assessed using the Aged Care Funding Instrument (ACFI) and is divided into three domains:

  • Activities of Daily Living (ADL)
  • Behaviour (BEH), or
  • Complex health care (CHC)

Customers listed in the High Care report need to have their entitlement reviewed and ceased. The report is saved on the CAPS-administration drive by the Programme Management team and actioned by the CAPS processing team. The report is password protected and the password is forwarded in a separate email.

Review the Customer record to determine if payments should cease and comments to ensure no relevant history applies.

Go to Step 4.

3

National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) Report + Read more ...

A customer is not eligible to CAPS if they are receiving a support for continence aids as part of their National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) plan through their consumable budget.

Customers listed in the NDIS report need to have their entitlement reviewed and ceased. The report is saved on the CAPS-administration drive by the Programme Management team and actioned by the CAPS processing team. The report is password protected and the password is forwarded in a separate email.

Review the Customer record to determine if payments should cease and comments to ensure no relevant history applies. For example where a customer has had payments ceased previously and have been reinstated determine if the latest NDIS data is correct.

Go to Step 4.

4

Process report + Read more ...

Open the customer's record, see Step 1 in Accessing system in Continence Aids Payment Scheme – Other requirements.

If a payment error is identified, see Error messages table.

If payments are to cease, see Ceasing entitlement table in Continence Aids Payment Scheme – Customer contact, application, ceasing entitlement and review.

Update the relevant spreadsheet (e.g High care, Fact of Death Data or Death report or National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) data ) including:

  • day actioned and payment ceased
  • any comments required for follow up, for example debt recovery, write backs or file fixes required, payments owed to the customer

Save updated reports in the CAPS-Administration drive in the relevant completed folder. For example, if FODD report has been actioned, then save it into CAPs Administration drive/FODD/Completed/relevant FY folder for review and audit purposes. The actioned spreadsheet is sent to the Programme Management Team via email.
