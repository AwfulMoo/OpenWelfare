Process for customer reporting lost, stolen or damaged BasicsCard

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Customer/payment nominee (authorised card holder) contacts to advise their BasicsCard was lost, stolen or damaged + Read more ...

  • If a third party is advising they have found a BasicsCard, see BasicsCard a third party reports card was found. Procedure ends here
  • If customer is advising they have found their previously lost or stolen Basics card, see BasicsCard reactivate posted or previously suspended card. Procedure ends here

Confirm the customer's identification before proceeding with interview. For assistance, see Authenticating a Centrelink customer. Note: there is no requirement to sight or photocopy customer identity documentation.

If it is the payment nominee contacting, ensure that the BasicsCard was issued to the payment nominee. Check that the payment nominee arrangement exists and the payment nominee's identity. If an income managed customer has a payment nominee, the BasicsCard will be issued to the payment nominee on behalf of the customer. This can be viewed on the BasicsCard Summary screen.

2

Processing + Read more ...

Updates are made using the Change BasicsCard status workflow. BasicsCard Status can be viewed on the BasicsCard summary screen.

Is Customer First Workspace available?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, see Step 1 in the Manual process for suspending a BasicsCard table

3

Blocked BasicsCard + Read more ...

The Card Transaction Provider (CTP) will automatically block the customer's BasicsCard, if the customer makes six incorrect PIN attempts in a row. The PIN will remain blocked until the customer makes contact to change or reset their PIN.

The Card Status: field on the BasicsCard Summary screen will show the Status of Blocked, Suspended, Closed or Active.

Has the card been 'blocked'?

  • Yes, the BasicsCard will remain blocked until the customer/payment nominee contacts Services Australia. See BasicsCard request to change Personal Identification Number (PIN). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Is the customer/payment nominee contacting via a Smart Centre or face-to-face? + Read more ...

  • Smart Centre, go to Step 6
  • Face-to-face, go to Step 5

5

Face-to-face contact + Read more ...

Replacement BasicsCard can be provided by Service Officer

When a replacement BasicsCard is issued, the old card will automatically cancel. Reactivation is not possible.

Issue a replacement BasicsCard. For assistance, see Initial offer or replacement of BasicsCard.

Procedure ends here.

6

Contact is via Smart Centre + Read more ...

Advise the customer/payment nominee:

  • the BasicsCard will be suspended
  • another card cannot be issued unless the customer is able to attend face-to-face at a Service Centre or with the Remote Servicing Team
  • the options if the card is found before another card is reissued. That is, the customer is to contact Services Australia via a Smart Centre and have the card reactivated
  • of the receipt number. Advise the customer/payment nominee the receipt number is to be retained as evidence of the date and time of notification. Note: this is the Centrelink system receipt number created when the customer's record is accessed. It is located in the header field of the customer's record
  • if they make contact outside the normal business hours, it may be necessary for them to ring back during business hours to reallocate funds
  • of the allocation options, if they are unable to have face-to-face contacts. If the customer cannot attend the interview for a replacement BasicsCard they may require access to their IM funds for priority needs. This may include recalling funds from the BasicsCard, stopping regular allocations to the BasicsCard, or setting up an alternative allocation method. For example, payment to a Third Party Organisation (TPO). For assistance, see Changes to income management priority needs

7

Balance or transaction summary requested + Read more ...

Has the customer/payment nominee queried whether the BasicsCard was used during the period from when it was lost, to when it was reported lost?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Balance or transaction summary + Read more ...

Access the customer's Transaction Summary screen to provide information to the customer. To access this screen go to Customer First > Workspace > Income Management > BasicsCard > Transaction Summary.

The time displayed on the Transaction Summary page will be the Eastern Standard Time. This may not necessarily be the customer's time zone.

The transaction summary can be printed. Note: the transaction summary can only print details of transactions from the date the last transaction summary was printed. For assistance, see BasicsCard balance or transaction summary enquiry.

9

Change BasicsCard status + Read more ...

In Customer First > Workspace, use the left hand side menu and select the following options, Income Management > BasicsCard > Change BasicsCard status.

From the Change BasicsCard Status page:

  • Authenticate the customer
  • Select Change required from drop down box
  • Select the Continue button
  • Check BasicsCard status action to correct
  • Reason for suspension - select reason from drop down box
  • Workflow will navigate to Auto DOC which needs to be updated and completed for the activity to finalise. Include full details on the DOC:
    • That the card was suspended
    • Reason it was suspended, and
    • That the customer was advised of the need to reallocate funds and to attend face-to-face for a replacement BasicsCard
    • If the customer/payment nominee suspects that the BasicsCard has been used by another party, the Service Officer should also include this information on the DOC
    • Ensure to include the reasons the BasicsCard was suspended, who contacted, time/date of suspension, receipt number and any other valid information
  • Review current expenses and allocations to the BasicsCard. For assistance, see Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management
  • Funds will continue to automatically transfer to the BascisCard. Therefore, the allocation method may need updating from Residual Payment method to Partial Payment method. For assistance, see BasicsCard changing daily spend, maximum card balance and allocation method
  • Funds may also need to be recalled from the BasicsCard back to the customer's Income Management account. For assistance, see Recall of funds from a BasicsCard to a customer's Income Management account

Manual process for suspending a BasicsCard

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

Systems are down + Read more ...

All BasicsCard suspensions are to be updated in Customer First. Alternative system updates are to be done only when Customer First Workspace is unavailable.

  • If workflow is unavailable, updates are to be manually coded, go to Step 2
  • If Centrelink systems are unavailable Service Officers with access to Citrix can use the National Index, go to Step 4
  • If Centrelink system and the National Index are unavailable use the BasicsCard Alternative Interface, see Step 1 in the Using the BasicsCard Alternative Interface and Contingency Support System table

2

BasicsCard Status - Manually coding + Read more ...

BasicsCards will be automatically 'blocked' by the Card Transaction Provider (CTP) if the customer attempts six incorrect PIN attempts in a row. The PIN will remain blocked until the customer makes contact to change or reset their PIN.

Ensure the BasicsCard Status is active. This information can be found on the BasicsCard Balance Enquiry (BCBE) screen.

Has the card been 'blocked'?

  • Yes, the BasicsCard will remain blocked until the customer/payment nominee contacts Services Australia. See BasicsCard request to change Personal Identification Number (PIN). Procedure ends here
  • No, and
    • contact is via Smart Centre, go to Step 3
    • contact is face-to-face, see Step 5 in the Lost, stolen or damaged BasicsCard table

3

Manual coding is available - Suspend the BasicsCard + Read more ...

Access the Centrelink system and locate the customer's record via the Customer Reference Number (CRN) or the Name Search (IN) screen.

  • Suspend the BasicsCard by going to the BasicsCard task selector (BCTS) screen and 's'elect Suspend/Reactivate Card or go direct to the Suspend/Reactive Card (BCSR) screen
  • Code the following fields on the BCSR screen:
    • Action: SUS (Suspend)
    • Suspend Reason: use the appropriate code LST (Lost), STL (Stolen) or Oth (Other)

Go to Step 7.

4

National Index updates - Centrelink system is unavailable + Read more ...

When the Centrelink system is unavailable, limited activities can be completed in National Index. These include:

  • obtaining BasicsCard Balances
  • suspending and reactivating BasicsCards, and
  • changing PINs for BasicsCards

National Index can only be accessed via Customer Record. To log into the National Index system:

  • Open Customer Record and enter 'NNS' in the system field and 'N' in the environment field

5

Search for Customer using the National Index + Read more ...

To search for a customer using the National Index:

  • Go to the IN screen with the system code set to 'NNS'
  • Search for the customer (same as if in Centrelink system). Press [Enter]
  • The Client List (CL) screen
  • Select the required customer, the National Index Person Details (PED) screen will display. This screen should provide enough information to authenticate the customer

6

National Index - Access BasicsCard - Suspend (BCSC) screen + Read more ...

  • Go to the BCSC screen. The Card number: field will be pre-populated from the customer's record
  • Code the following fields:
    • Suspend reason: use the appropriate code: LST (Lost), STL (Stolen) or Oth (Other)
    • Press [Enter]
    • The Card Balance will be displayed only after a successful response from the Card Transaction Provider (CTP) that the card has been suspended

Note: the Service Officer will be unable to document the customer's record. When the Centrelink system is available record details on a DOC in the customer's record.

Go to Step 7.

7

Advise the customer: + Read more ...

  • that the card has been suspended
  • that another card cannot be issued unless the customer is able to attend face-to-face. Advise of the options available if the card is found before another card is reissued
  • they will need to contact Services Australia via Smart Centre to have the card reactivated. Note: suspended BasicsCards can also be restored (reactivated) in National Index and the BasicsCard Alternative Interface if the Centrelink system is not available

Discuss allocation options, if the customer cannot attend an office and requires funds for priority needs. Regular expenses will continue to be allocated to the BasicsCard even if the BasicsCard has been suspended. These may need to be stopped.

If a customer is contacting outside of normal business hours or while the system is unavailable, it may be necessary for the customer to call back during business hours.

For assistance, see:

  • Recall of funds from a BasicsCard to a customer's Income Management account
  • Changes to income management priority needs
  • Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management

8

Record details on a DOC + Read more ...

If the Centrelink system is unavailable, the Service Officer will not be able to DOC the customer's record in the National Index or the BasicsCard Alternative Interface. Manually record details of changes, and update the customer's record when the Centrelink system is available.

Include the following details on the DOC:

  • The card was suspended
  • Reason it was suspended
  • The customer was advised of the need to reallocate funds and to attend face-to-face for a replacement BasicsCard
  • If the customer/payment nominee suspects that the BasicsCard has been used by another party, include this information on the DOC
  • Who contacted, time/date of suspension, receipt number and any other valid information

Using BasicsCard Alternative Interface and Contingency Support System

Table 3:

Step

Action

1

Alternative Interface + Read more ...

Is the BasicsCard Alternative Interface available?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, use the BasicsCard Contingency Support System (BCSS), go to Step 6

2

The BasicsCard Alternative Interface is an external website maintained by the BasicsCard Transaction Provider + Read more ...

The Alternative Interface provides BasicsCard:

  • balance
  • status
  • transaction history
  • status updates, and
  • can be accessed to suspend, activate (reactivate previously suspended cards) and unblock BasicsCards, when the BasicsCard number is known

The BasicsCard number must be known to use this interface

Generally, this would only be used when the Centrelink system and the National Index are not available, as the BasicsCard Alternative Interface does not contain any customer information. It cannot be used to locate a customer's record or to verify a customer's identity. Access and use of this facility is intended for Service Officers in the 24/7 Income Management Line or Level 1 and 2 Helpdesks.

Note: this must be used in conjunction with Income Management Business Continuity Procedures to authenticate customers.

3

To access the BasicsCard Alternative Interface web page + Read more ...

  • Open an Internet Browser session
  • Access the interface's web entry page. See the Resources page for a link to the login page for the BasicsCard Alternative Interface. Note: this can be added to 'Favourites' for future use
  • To access this system, enter your assigned: 'username' and 'password', then select Log in
  • Usernames are the user's full Services Australia email address, (for example, john.citizen@servicesaustralia.gov.au) in lower case
  • If the password is forgotten, select the forgotten password? link on the User Login screen. The email address will be the user's full Services Australia email address, (for example, john.citizen@servicesaustralia.gov.au) in lower case
  • If there are any issues accessing this facility, email the BasicsCard and TPO Services Team. See the Resources page for a link to contact details

4

Account or Card Search page + Read more ...

  • From the Account or Card Search page:
  • Select the Search by Card No and enter the 16 Digit BasicsCard Number, press Search
  • The Transaction History page will display the card balance, transactions and status
  • To update card status select the Card Status button. This will navigate to the Card Status page

5

Card Status page + Read more ...

  • Enter the Office Code
  • In the New Card Status box select the action required Suspend
  • Enter the Reason Code from the drop down box. Select Save which will update the card status
  • Navigate back to the Transaction History page. This will now show the card status as 'Restricted (No Withdrawals)'

The Service Officer will not be able to DOC the customer's record in the BasicsCard Alternative Interface. Manually record the details to be updated on the customer's record when the Centrelink system is available.

6

BasicsCard Contingency Support System (BCSS) + Read more ...

The BasicsCard Contingency Support system allows access to limited functions for BasicsCard when the Centrelink system, National Index and BasicsCard Alternative Interfaces are not operational.

The BasicsCard Contingency Support System will allow the following functions:

  • display current BasicsCard status
  • obtain BasicsCard balance
  • obtain Income Management account balance
  • suspend, reactivate or unblock a BasicsCard
  • change/reset a BasicsCard Personal Identification Number (PIN)

The BasicsCard Contingency Support System will automatically update details of any transactions completed when systems are restored.

To access the contingency system, use the link in Resources to the login page for the BasicsCard Contingency Support System. Note: add this address to 'Favourites' for future use.

See Resources for links to the training presentation available on the BasicsCard and TPO Services team homepage.

Note: the BCSS will not open with the Firefox internet browser. Ensure that the BCSS is opened using Internet Explorer (IE).
