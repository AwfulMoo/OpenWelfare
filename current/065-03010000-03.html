This page contains information on how to help a person claim Age Pension.

Select the relevant tab:

  • Self service tab explains how to help a person make an online claim
  • Assisted tab explains how to check a person's circumstances to ensure a new claim is required, complete an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) with them, and lodgement of claims and documentation

Self service

Making an Age Pension online claim

This table contains information for Service Officers to help customers or their correspondence nominees access their Centrelink online account to start, continue, and complete an online claim for Age Pension.

Step

Action

1

Check access to online account + Read more ...

An online claim will expire after 13 weeks if it has not been submitted. The customer must then submit a new claim. In some cases the claim can be assessed from the original start date, see Circumstance Change Monitor. If further assistance is required, for example, there has been a system error, refer to the Retirements Helpdesk.

Check the customer or nominee is registered with an active Centrelink online account. Organisation nominees must be registered for nominee organisation online services.

If they do not have an active account they must confirm their identity at their local service centre to register for a Centrelink online account.

Advise the customer how they can create a myGov account and link it to Centrelink. This will also enable them to receive Centrelink mail online through the myGov Inbox.

If the customer is partnered, consider whether their partner may also be eligible for Age Pension or another service offer. If the customer has permission to enquire on their partner’s behalf or their partner is present, discuss whether they would also like to lodge a claim.

Service centre staff can help customers with their online claim through self service terminals. Note: service centre staff must remind customers to submit their claim once all required tasks are completed. Alternatively, if the customer is unable to access their online account, the Service Officer can scan and upload the documents for them.

Customers can access the Payment and Service Finder on the website to check if Age Pension is an appropriate payment for them, and to start their claim.

Record the claim contact in a DOC and advise the customer to start and submit a claim as soon as possible to be paid from the earliest possible date. Advise the customer if they are having difficulty submitting the claim to contact Services Australia for assistance.

Note: different processes apply for customers in vulnerable circumstances, see Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.

2

Update My profile + Read more ...

On the My profile page, information from the customer’s record is pre-populated. Customers need to select Update Section for any sections such as Address or Relationship details that require an update. Note: accommodation details are covered within the claim questions.

For each part of My profile updated, they need to select Save.

Electronic messaging

Customers who provide a mobile phone number or email address will be advised that they will automatically be subscribed to Electronic Messaging (EM) services unless they have previously declined EM. They will be asked to nominate a preferred contact method (SMS or email) for electronic messages. They will receive messages via their preferred contact method to complete all Required tasks, see Electronic messaging (EM) - Targeted notifications.

If customers have linked their active Centrelink online account to their active myGov account they are automatically subscribed to receive Centrelink mail online. They can opt out of these services at any time.

3

Starting an online claim + Read more ...

Once registered with an 'active' online account, customers need to:

  • Sign into myGov and select Centrelink services
  • Select Payments and Claims > Claims > Make a claim from the menu, or select Start a new claim on the My online claims page
  • On the Make a claim page select Get started under the Older Australians category
  • Answer the pre-claim questions on the Check your eligibility for payment page to check they are claiming the most suitable income support payment for their circumstances. If they would not be eligible based on their answers, the online claim will advise them why and refer them to the Payment and Service Finder or to contact the agency to discuss a more suitable payment. They can select Back to return to Make a claim page to begin another claim type
  • After selecting Claim now, a message will remind them to check My profile to review and update their details before starting their claim. The customer can select:
    • Continue to go to the Claim navigation page to start their claim
    • My Profile to review and update their details. They can then select Return to your claim to go back to the Claim navigation page to start their claim

4

Claim progress + Read more ...

The Claim navigation page will display:

  • Status of the claim
  • Claim number
  • Advice for the customer to submit their claim as soon as possible to be paid from the earliest possible date
  • Sections of the claim that need to be completed

The sections of the claim must be completed in order and the next section cannot be started until the previous section is complete.

The sections are accessed by selecting the Start button.

Each section contains modules with relevant questions. The modules are displayed as tabs at the top of each page. On each question page, there are options to select:

  • Back to go back to the previous question or module
  • Save to save the claim at the current stage
  • Next to progress forward with the module questions

Select Help to access the Oliver Digital Assistant text for help with the questions on the current page. Help text will open in a separate window and can be printed.

When each module is completed and the end of the section reached, the Claim navigation page will display to select the next section.

As a section is completed, there is an option to select Edit to go back to a previously completed section to change the answers. To go directly to a module, select the relevant tab.

To return to the Claim navigation page after making changes, select the last module tab and select Next.

The claim does not need to be completed in one session. Select Save and Exit on the Claim navigation page at any time and the responses will be automatically saved, to continue with the claim later.

All compulsory questions must be answered before the claim can be submitted.

Expired claims

The online claim will expire after 13 weeks if not submitted. See Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM).

5

View or update a started online claim + Read more ...

To access their online claim, the customer needs to:

  • Sign into myGov and select Centrelink services
  • Select Payments and Claims > Claims > My online claims

A list of all started online claims will display.

If the status of the claim is incomplete, they can choose to either:

  • Continue claim to display the next question set to be completed
  • Cancel claim - and confirm they want to do this. Once cancelled, they will not be able to view or continue the online claim, and no claim information will be recorded. They can start a new online claim at any time

Review and confirm claim + Read more ...

When reviewing and confirming, customers are presented with a summary of all of the information they have provided so that they can check what they have entered is correct. The information displays in sections, for example, About your situation.

If the customer has not completed all required fields or needs to make any changes, they can select the Update button relevant to the answers that require changing. This will take them back to the relevant section of the claim to review/update the particular claim questions and responses.

Declaration

Once all information is correct, the customer must accept the declaration by ticking the I understand and agree to the above terms check box. Completing the declaration means that a signed claim form is not required.

Selecting Confirm Information will save all information provided. Customers won’t be able to make any changes to answers beyond that point. They will need to contact Services Australia if they want to provide further information in relation to their claim.

10

Next steps + Read more ...

This page advises the customer of any further information or tasks required for their claim to be submitted.

To submit their claim, the customer must answer all mandatory questions and provide all their required documents, including identity documents if there is no Identity Confirmation Indicator (ICI) recorded. Exceptions apply for some vulnerable customers.

To be assessed from the earliest possible date, customers need to provide all required information and supporting documents and submit the claim as soon as possible by the date provided. The date the claim has been submitted will generally determine the claim start date. Customers can access their saved claim for 13 weeks. After 13 weeks the claim will expire.

Documents and forms section includes:

  • A list of supplementary information and supporting documentation required from the customer
  • How many days they have to submit their claim
  • A link to download or print required forms
  • An Upload documents button

Additional information section includes:

  • Links to further information and brochures such as 'Confirming your identity'
  • Information about subscription to online letters and electronic messaging

If the 'required' tasks are not complete, e.g required documents are not uploaded, the claim cannot be submitted (some exceptions apply). Once the relevant tasks are completed, the status changes from required or supplementary to Done. If the task needs changing the customer can Edit it. Once all required tasks are completed the button will change to Submit. The customer can then submit the claim and will get a notification if they have successfully submitted it.

Customers will get an estimated completion date that the claim will be processed.

Customers can select:

  • Home to go back to their online account homepage
  • View claim history to see the history of the claim, including any documents or tasks they’ve completed
  • View claim answers to see the information they’ve provided

Customers can print their claim information now or any time later.

Customers can access their submitted online claim to return to the Next steps page at any time.

Once a claim has been submitted, the customer cannot update the online claim details. They will need to contact Services Australia if they want to provide information in relation to their claim.

Customers can withdraw their online claim once submitted.

11

Uploading documents for online claims + Read more ...

Customers can access their online claim using their Centrelink online account or the Centrelink Express Plus mobile app to upload documents required to be able to submit their claim, or upload supplementary documents.

On the Next steps page in Complete extra tasks there is an Upload button next to each outstanding task to use Upload documents.

Help the customer upload the documents and submit their claim online.

If necessary, Service Officers can scan the documents and submit the claim for the customer. When submitting a claim for a customer, Service Officers must ensure a verbal declaration is read to and accepted by the customer. See Step 8 in the Assisted Customer Claims (ACC) table. This will ensure the claim is submitted as soon as possible.

  • Scan the documents
  • Run the Request & Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure to mark the corresponding task as Provided and submit the claim

If the customer uploaded or provided documents before starting the online claim, the document displays as Required on the Next Steps page. The customer can either:

  • Re-upload the document on the Next Steps page from the Documents and Forms section
  • Contact by phone or in person to have the Request & Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure run to change the document status from Required to Provided

Note: if the customer contacts, remind them to submit their claim.

Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) + Read more ...

ACC may be used by Service Officers when a customer is unable or unwilling to claim online. ACC replicates the questions in the online claim. ACC must only be used when the customer is available to answer the questions in ACC. If a customer is unable or unwilling to claim online or via ACC, running the First Contact Concessions, Carers and Seniors (FCCCS) workflow will send out the correct forms. If both members of a couple want to claim payment, FCCCS should be run for both. If using ACC to claim Age Pension and Pension Bonus, Service Officers must recommend to the customer they contact a Financial Information Service (FIS) Officer before submitting their claim.

Correspondence nominees may be offered ACC when they want to apply for Age Pension on behalf of their principal/s. Where conditions are met they may apply on behalf of their principals using the Age Pension combined partner online claim. If they decline or are ineligible for ACC, running FCCCS will send out the correct forms.

ACC will identify any documents required for the claim. The documents can be uploaded by a Service Officer or the customer.

If the required documents include a form, for example, Business Details form (MOD F), this can be printed by the customer from the Services Australia website, or issued by a Service Officer.

Advise the customer the documents, including supporting documents required in a form, must be provided before the claim can be submitted.

For information about completing ACC with the customer, see Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) table.

Blind customers + Read more ...

Income and assets details are not required if only claiming the basic rate of pension. However, if claiming Rent Assistance or if their partner is claiming or receiving a means tested income support payment, all income and assets details for blind customers must be supplied.

Full details are required if the customer or their partner is receiving or claiming any of the following:

  • Compensation
  • Payments under the New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS)
  • Payments under an International Social Security Agreement
  • A New Zealand Government payment
  • A payment from the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)

If their partner is receiving a means tested income support payment, the customer's income and assets will affect the partner's entitlement.

  • Issue a Request for Ophthalmologist/Optometrist Report (SA013)
  • Check with the customer if they would like to appoint a nominee

For more information, see Contact in relation to an intended claim (CLK).

No supporting documents are required unless the customer wishes to supply extra medical reports to support their case.

Age Pension (Blind) customers can choose to receive their letters in an alternative format.

Payment destination page in ACC + Read more ...

Current payment destination details will display if the customer is currently in receipt of a payment. Either select this account or add a new account.

The customer must provide the following details for the relevant payment destination:

  • name of account
  • branch (BSB) number
  • account number

Customer does not have their payment destination details available

If the customer is unable to provide a payment destination within 14 days due to having barriers in opening a bank account, a temporary exemption can be granted for 28 days.

Note: the customer will not receive any payments until a payment destination is provided.

The Payment Destination page can be bypassed by selecting the I do not have bank account details checkbox. No account information will save, and the Service Officer can continue and submit the claim.

Before selecting the checkbox, consider all of the following:

  • This option must not be selected if the customer has a bank account but is unable to provide the details when ACC is being completed
  • This checkbox must only be selected if the customer does not own a bank account and is unable to provide a payment destination within 14 days due to having barriers in opening a bank account
  • If this option is selected, the customer must be advised that they will not receive any payments until a payment destination is provided

The customer must be advised to provide their bank details as early as possible.

Customer declaration

Where ACC is used to support the claim process, encourage the customer to submit the claim using their Centrelink online account or the Centrelink Express Plus mobile app so that the customer can monitor the progress of their claim through the Claim Tracker.

  • Read the customer the verbal declaration script
  • Tick the box on the Review your claim page if they accept
  • This will confirm that the declaration script has been read, and that the customer accepts it

Once the verbal declaration is accepted and ACC submitted a DOC containing 'Staff assisted verbal declaration accepted - Yes' will display in:

  • Process Direct notes
  • Customer First Super Case
  • the Customer Claim Summary

Note: This only applies where the claim declaration in the claim summary shows 'Staff assisted verbal declaration accepted'.

If the customer is unable to provide a verbal declaration, have the customer complete a CDF:

  • Print a Customer Declaration Form (CDF) using the template in the Resources page
  • The claim must not be submitted without the CDF being completed and signed
  • If present, ask customer to sign the CDF
  • Where the customer cannot sign the CDF:
    • Send a Q004 to customer with a Customer Declaration Form (CDF) using the template in the Resources page
    • As this cannot be added as a required document on Next steps, record a detailed note on the customer’s record stating the Q004 and CDF was issued. Include the expected due date
  • Where the customer has declined to accept the verbal declaration and a signed CDF has been provided, tick the Declaration box on the Review your claim page and annotate the DOC that the verbal declaration was declined, yet as a CDF has been signed and scanned to record the claim can be submitted by the Service Officer

If the customer requests a copy of their claim summary, this can be printed once the claim has been submitted in Process Direct via the print icon in the claim overview within ACC.

Partnered customers

If an SA002 or SA412 has been lodged with the details for the customer and their partner (who is also Age Pension age within the early claim period), check the following details.

In the SA002:

  • Question 1 - Do you have a partner
  • Question 2 - Is your partner also claiming Age Pension?
  • Question 3 - Indicate if your partner is using this form or a separate form for a claim or to provide their details

In the SA412:

  • Questions 2 and 3 from the SA002 will appear in the first section of the SA412 if the partner is within 13 weeks or over Age Pension age and not paid an income support payment, or if a new partner has been advised via FCCCS

If the customer answered Yes to questions 1 and 2 on the SA002 (question 2 on SA412), or if both have lodged separate claims, see Item 5.

4

Contact to clarify partner's intent + Read more ...

Ask the customer to clarify the partner's intent as soon as possible:

  • If the SA002 or SA412 has been lodged for both customer and partner and both wish to claim Age Pension, record a DOC on the partner's record.
    Note: for customers in vulnerable circumstances, see Intent to claim and vulnerable customers
  • If the partner wishes to claim but has not signed the form, follow the process as outlined in Claims received that are incomplete or incorrect
  • If claim is lodged either online or via paper by one member of the couple and
    • it is obvious that their partner would also be eligible, or
    • their partner is also claiming Age Pension, but they wish to provide their details separately, attempt to contact the partner by phone and ask them to complete an online claim or ACC. If the partner is unavailable issue a Q164 and DOC their record that if they contact, they should be invited to complete an online claim or have ACC run with them
  • If only the customer is claiming, record this information on their record in a closed DOC

Note: it is also acceptable to ask the Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) question when talking to the partner and to record their response on the Marital Status (MS) screen (if linked), or send an Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf (SS313) form for them to complete and return.

5

Check documentation and scan + Read more ...

Check for the following documentation for both customer and partner (if applicable):

  • Confirmation of identity is required for Age Pension. Documents to verify their identity are required if there is no ICI recorded. Original documents must be sighted, recorded and returned
  • Proof of age
  • Tax File Number (TFN)
  • Other supporting documentation, for example, Rent Certificate (SU523HD), rent verification by way of lease agreement or investment documents

Ensure Identity and proof of age is also provided for the partner if they are claiming Age Pension on the same claim form.

If required, code the customer's and partner's identity details using the Identity Confirmation Dashboard in Process Direct. For more information, see Coding identity documents.

Check that all forms issued via FCCCS and relevant supporting documents have been lodged. A letter is sent with the forms advising the customer of documents required to support their claim.

If there are discrepancies between the documentation supplied and the record, these must be addressed, resolved and documented prior to coding.

If the proof of age document cannot be coded (for example, due to system limitations), or the customer advises they are unable to provide one, include all necessary details on the DOC.

Scanning

Identity documents should only be scanned if they are required for verification purposes other than identity assurance.

  • Upon receipt of an Age Pension paper claim, scan the paper claim together with any supporting documents to the customer’s record
  • If documentation is provided separately, for example, after an online claim has been submitted:
    • Run the Request and Manage Customer Task guided procedure in Customer First. For an online claim or ACC, the Summary page will display Tasks (documents) requested at Next steps. Mark the corresponding Task as 'Provided'
    • Scan documents to the customer’s record
    • Select appropriate unstructured (UNS) form type if required

Note: if the customer provided the information in person, the Service Officer should submit the claim for them.
