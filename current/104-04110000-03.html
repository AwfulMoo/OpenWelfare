Authenticating a customer

This table describes the process required to authenticate a customer, or their authorised nominee, prior to personal information being disclosed.

Step

Action

1

Authentication required before any personal information released + Read more ...

A Service Officer must be satisfied the record belongs to the correct person before assisting with the enquiry.

Service Officers assess the authentication of paper claims by using information contained within the claim, against the information held on the record. Only once the Service Officer is satisfied that the person claiming is the owner of the record being accessed, can they proceed with processing the claim. See Background page for more information.

Warm transfers from Child Support do not need full authentication. Child Support will provide necessary customer details to ensure the correct customer record is located when transferring the call.

Note: if the customer has a password on their record, the Service Officer must confirm this at every contact before proceeding with the enquiry. See Customer password on a Display on Access DOC.

Is the person the nominee?

  • Yes, see Disclosing information to nominee. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Is the contact in person? + Read more ...

  • Yes, in person at a service centre or Services Australia agent (in remote areas), go to Step 3
  • No, via telephone, go to Step 4

Note: Use this same process to authenticate a person with Permission to Enquire (PPE) or permission to update (PPU) before granting access to the record.

3

Authentication at a service centre or Services Australia agent + Read more ...

A Service Officer must be satisfied the record belongs to the correct person before assisting with the enquiry.

If there is a password on a record:

  • this must be confirmed at every contact and
  • asked in addition to authenticating the customer and confirming PoRO

See Customer password on a Display on Access DOC.

Ask the person:

  • for an approved photographic identity document to complete facial comparison
  • to provide personal details that can be checked against the customer record
  • sufficient authentication questions to confirm proof of record ownership - remembering to vary the questions. For questions to assist in authentication, go to Step 6

Note: asking for a full name, date of birth and Customer Access Number (CAN) or Customer Reference Number (CRN) are not sufficient to establish record ownership as these details are easily known by a third party.

Has the customer been authenticated?

  • Yes, proceed with the enquiry. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

Note: if staff inadvertently access information of a person in whom they have a personal interest, they must record the incident in the Inadvertent Access Register.

4

Authentication via telephone + Read more ...

If there is a password on a record:

  • this must be confirmed at every contact and
  • asked in addition to authenticating the customer and confirming PoRO

See Customer password on a Display on Access DOC

A call from a customer can be received as authenticated or unauthenticated.

When answering a call that is not authenticated, Service Officers are encouraged to use any of the best practice greeting phrases. This will assist with identifying the caller before authenticating the customer and establishing PoRO.

An authenticated call can be received via:

  • a voice match. If the customer is enrolled for voice biometrics technology, and has entered their CAN/CRN and repeated their Common Pass Phrase (CPP)
  • the Interactive Voice Response (IVR) and the IVR has prompted the customer to enter their CAN/ CRN and enter their Personal Identification Number (PIN)
  • the IVR where the customer has logged into their Express Plus App and elected to speak with a Service Officer through the Digital Assistant 'Yes - Call now' option

A call can be received unauthenticated when a customer has:

  • not provided a CRN
  • provided their CRN and
  • entered their CAN/CRN or PIN into the IVR incorrectly
  • failed to match their Common Pass Phrase to their voiceprint

The colour of the Case Information window and the icon in the Authentication tab indicates the authentication status achieved and if further authentication is required.

If the Case Information window displays as green, the caller has been authenticated and no further authentication is needed.

The Authentication tab will show the authentication method as one of the following images:

Go to Step 5.

If the Case Information window displayed as amber, the caller has not been authenticated and further authentication is needed.

The Authentication tab will show the failed authentication method with one of the following images:

Go to Step 6.

If the Case Information window displays as red, the caller has not been authenticated and has entered an incorrect PIN or their Voiceprint has failed. Further authentication with caution is needed.

The Authentication tab will show the failed authentication method with one of the following images:

Note: For authentication processes for outbound customer contact via telephone, go to Step 6.

To enrol a customer in voice biometrics, see Voiceprints for phone self service.

For protocols and responsibilities when calling a customer, see Calling a customer or returning a customer's call.

5

Customer call is authenticated + Read more ...

When answering a call, the Service Officer must use the standard greeting:

'Hello, this is [Service Officer], can I please start with your full name?'

This is to ensure the correct record is being displayed.

It may also be that the partner or the nominee has called, or someone with no authority to enquire. The customer may have given their CAN/CRN and PIN to someone to enquire on their behalf.

If there is a password on a record:

  • this must be confirmed at every contact and
  • asked in addition to authenticating the customer and confirming PoRO

See Customer password on a Display on Access DOC

If the Service Officer is:

  • satisfied they are speaking with the customer proceed with the enquiry. Procedure ends here
  • satisfied they are speaking with an authorised representative, go to Step 7
  • not satisfied they are speaking with the customer, proceed with the call as if the customer is not authenticated, go to Step 6

6

Customer call is received unauthenticated + Read more ...

A Service Officer must be sure the customer record being accessed belongs to the person they are conducting business with, before making updates or releasing personal information.

For outbound calls, the Service Officer may disclose they are calling from Centrelink after the customer has provided the full name and date of birth shown on the record being accessed. The Service Officer should then proceed with authenticating the customer and establishing PoRO.

Note: If the Service Officer feels there may be a risk of family and domestic violence or there are privacy concerns, for example in Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH) or Member of a Couple (MoC) assessments, there is no requirement to disclose the program.

See Calling a customer or returning a customer's call 111-05050050

To authenticate the customer and establish PoRO, ask them to provide personal details that can be cross-checked with the customer’s record. Information stored on the customer record can assist with deriving questions to ask when authenticating a person.

Suggested screens to check include:

  • customer's current and/or previous address - Address Details (AD) screen
  • customer's CAN/CRN - Benefit Header
  • date of birth - Customer Person Details Summary (CPDS) screen
  • bank account details - Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen
  • partner's name - Person Details (PED) screen
  • children's names - Person Details (PED) screen
  • type of payment received - Person Details (PED) screen
  • country of birth - Legal Residence Information (RSLEG) screen
  • accommodation - Accommodation History (ACS)
  • telephone number - Telephone Summary (TDS) screen
  • due to report date - NSS General Information (NGI) screen

If, after attempting to authenticate the customer, the Service Officer is:

  • satisfied they are speaking with the customer, proceed with the enquiry. Procedure ends here
  • not satisfied they are speaking with the customer, proceed with the call as if the customer is not authenticated, go to Step 8

7

Caller is an authorised representative + Read more ...

If the Service Officer is satisfied they are speaking with an authorised representative, this is unauthorised access. Nominees and PPEs are not permitted to use a customer's CAN/CRN/PIN/WEB APP to access services.

Advised the authorised representative about:

  • PIN issue usage terms and conditions
  • online services for customers
  • requirement for customers to keep their PIN confidential
  • advise the authorised representative the service will be immediately locked and/or how to request access to online services again. Customers can have their PIN reset or re-register if they can demonstrate they will abide by conditions of use
  • advise authorise representative to recontact using the correct process
  • DOC the discussion about online services and lock customer's access. Procedure ends here

8

Do not release information over the telephone + Read more ...

Offer to call the customer back:

Service Officers must never provide customer information for the individual to confirm.

  • When using the call-back option check the telephone number provided is the one listed on the customer's record and ensure a full PoRO check is performed as part of the outbound call
  • If a callback is not appropriate, refer the customer to a service centre or Services Australia agent to complete authentication by providing an approved photo identity document
  • Record all details on a DOC

Note: if the Service officer suspects the person of fraudulently attempting to access another person’s record, the Service Officer must report the suspected fraud.

Procedure ends here.

9

Customer not able to be authenticated + Read more ...

Service Officers must ask the customer to provide an approved photo identity document.

If the customer is unable to provide an approved photo identity document, ask the customer if they are able to provide other identity documents that may support authenticating them as owner of the record.

Can the customer provide additional identity documents?

  • Yes, proceed with asking the customer to provide personal details that can be checked against the record. Ask sufficient authentication questions to confirm PoRO, remembering to vary the questions. For questions to assist in authentication, see Step 6
  • No, ask the customer to provide personal details that can be checked against the customer record. Ask sufficient authentication questions to confirm PoRO remembering to vary the questions. For questions to assist in authentication, see Step 6

10

Check authentication + Read more ...

Has the customer been authenticated and PoRO been established?

  • Yes, proceed with the enquiry. Procedure ends here
  • No, if the customer cannot confirm details and the Service Officer is still not satisfied they are speaking with the customer, do not release any information until they are authenticated.
    Advised the customer to provide identity documents including an approved photo identity document to confirm they are the owner of the record being access when they next attend the service centre

Record all details on a DOC.

Note: if the Service Officer suspects the person of fraudulently attempting to access another person's record, the Service Officer must report the suspected fraud.
