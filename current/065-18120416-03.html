Real estate estimate check – principal home

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Assessable real estate is the principal home + Read more ...

All real estate owned by a care recipient, including the principal home, will be:

  • assessed as an asset, and
  • checked to verify if the care recipient's estimated value is reasonable

Eligible care recipients can have an exemption for the principal home applied.

Does the principal home require a real estate check?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No,
    • See Table 2
    • If a valuation has been returned or the care recipient does not agree with a previous valuation, see Step 1 in Table 4

2

Determining the ownership of the principal home + Read more ...

Does the care recipient and/or their partner have legal ownership of their principal home?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, and the care recipient
    • has created a granny flat interest, see Granny flat provisions
    • has a life interest in the principal home, see Assessing a life interest in a home
    • lives in a retirement village, see Retirement villages
    • lives in a caravan or mobile home, see Assessing caravans, boats and park homes
    • is a non-homeowner, no further investigation required
    • For other real estate, see Step 2 in Table 2

3

Current review in place - REBS + Read more ...

See Valuation of real estate and other assets and Maintaining the value of real estate assets on customer records for more information.

Check if the principal home:

  • is currently recorded on REBS screen
  • has a valuation source code of VAL, ONS, EST, NVE, IDX or CLI
  • has a value coded within the last 12 months, and
  • the estimate provided is within 10% of the current assessed value

Does the principal home meet all of the above criteria?

  • Yes,
    • an estimate check is not required and the value of the principal home is accepted as reasonable
    • go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Principal home more than 2 hectares + Read more ...

The principal home includes any adjacent land up to 2 hectares provided it is used for private or domestic purposes and is on one title. Any land over the first 2 hectares is assessed separately unless an exemption applies under the extended land use test.

Is the principal home and adjacent land more than 2 hectares?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Principal home - extended land use test + Read more ...

If the principal home and adjacent land is on one title and is over 2 hectares, the extended land use test treats the whole property as the principal home. See Assessing house and curtilage for more information. This only applies when care recipients (or their partner) currently receive an Age Pension or Carer Payment. Check REBS for any previous assessment of extended land use.

Does the principal home meet the extended land use test?

  • Yes, all land will be considered as the principal home, go to Step 6
  • No, house and curtilage is assessed as the principal home, adjacent land over 2 hectares will be assessed as real estate, go to Step 7

6

Principal home - extended land use test assessment is valid + Read more ...

No valuation for a principal home assessed under the extended land use rule is required when:

  • REBS shows that the principal home currently meets the extended land use test
  • the property is still on a single title, and
  • the value of the home is more than the value of the first asset threshold
    • See the Department of Health and Aged Care - Schedule of fees and charges on the Resources page for asset thresholds.

Does the principal home meet these criteria?

  • Yes, no valuation is required, go to Step 8
  • No, ask for the valuation of the principal home if:
    • the care recipient owns other real estate, see Table 2
    • no other real estate owned, see Table 3

7

Assessing house and curtilage + Read more ...

See Assessing house and curtilage to determine how house, curtilage and adjacent land is determined and assessed. All details of real estate are collected in the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care form and a MOD R – Real Estate form is not required.

For land that does not meet the extended land use exemption criteria:

  • record on REBS using 'Real Estate/Business Type' - HMB
  • do not record the 'RCA Former Prncpl Home' indicator for self-funded retirees
  • See Assessing and coding the Real estate details Mod R for assistance in updating REBS

To continue with determining an estimate for the principal home and surrounding 2 hectares, go to Step 8.

8

Protected person in principal home + Read more ...

A principal home is exempt from a means assessment if a protected person is living in it on the relevant date. See Aged care means assessment - protected person for more information.

Is there a protected person in the principal home at the relevant date?

  • Yes,
    • No estimated value is required for the principal home
    • If the care recipient owns other real estate, including land adjacent to the principal home that is more than 2 hectares, see Table 2
    • If no other real estate owned, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Estimate provided - is the care recipient's net share more than the first asset threshold + Read more ...

The value of the principal home is capped at the value of the first asset threshold contained in the Department of Health and Aged Care - Schedule of Fees and Charges post 1 July 2014. Due to this capped value, if a care recipient’s estimate of their net share of the principal home is over this amount, no estimate check is required.

Note: the net share applies after any mortgages or loans are deducted and takes into account the percentage of the property owned by the care recipient.

Is the care recipient’s net share of the principal home more than the first asset threshold as stated in the Department of Health and Aged Care - Schedule of Fees and Charges post 1 July 2014?

  • Yes,
    • No estimate check is required
    • If the care recipient owns other real estate including land adjacent to the principal home that is more than 2 hectares, see Table 2
    • If no other real estate owned, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Estimate check for principal home + Read more ...

Using the estimated market value provided by the care recipient, complete real estate estimate check using:

  • An approved property website - see the Resources page for a list of websites
    • these may provide information including size of land, number of bedrooms, and condition of property. Note: paid reports must not be requested from property information websites
  • Google search for property including if currently for sale
  • A search of other similar properties recently bought and sold in the surrounding area
  • If the property is currently for sale, check that the advertised price is reasonable when compared to other properties in the area

Consider if the estimate provided can be determined to be reasonable:

  • Allow some discretion when determining a reasonable estimate
  • Look for similar properties in the same street, suburb or town currently advertised for sale and compare size, condition and age of properties
    • If the estimate provided is within 10% of similar properties currently advertised in the area accept the estimate
  • Be flexible by considering that people may pay less for property than an advertised price
    • There may be issues affecting the property value such as termites, flood/fire damage, asbestos, etc. This may allow a lower estimate to be accepted
  • Where possible, the care recipient’s estimate is accepted to ensure their means assessment is completed promptly

Can the care recipient’s estimate of their net share of the principal home be assessed as reasonable?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, ask for a valuation
    • If the care recipient owns other real estate, including land adjacent to the principal home that is more than 2 hectares, see Table 2
    • If no other real estate owned, see Table 3

11

Estimate considered reasonable + Read more ...

If the estimate of principal home is assessed as reasonable, document the following in any progress of claim Fast Notes and the final assessment Fast Note:

  • Real estate estimate check has been completed for: (insert address)
  • The care recipient estimate of $ XXXXXX is considered reasonable as per the results of approved website check on DD/MM/YY

Complete updates on RCAA/REBS screens in the RCA/NCL.

Does the care recipient have a principal home on a block larger than 2 hectares (not extended land use) or own other real estate?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, procedure ends here

Other real estate estimate check + Read more ...

2

Current real estate review in place + Read more ...

Is the estimate provided within 10% of the current assessed value?

  • Yes,
    • An estimate check is not required and the estimate can be accepted as reasonable
    • go to Step 4
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3

3

Estimate check for real estate + Read more ...

Using the estimated market value provided by the care recipient, complete real estate estimate check using:

  • An approved property website – see the Resources page for links
    • These provide information including size of land, number of bedrooms, and condition of property. Note: do not request paid reports from property information websites
  • Google search for property including if currently for sale
  • Search of similar properties recently bought and sold in the surrounding area
  • If the property is currently for sale, check that the advertised price is reasonable in comparison to other properties in the area

Consider if the estimate provided is reasonable:

  • Allow some discretion when determining a reasonable estimate
  • Look for similar properties in the same street, suburb or town that are currently being advertised for sale and compare size, condition and age of properties
    • Accept the estimate if it is within 10% of similar properties currently advertised in the area
  • Be flexible and take into account that people may pay less for property than an advertised price
    • There may be issues affecting the value of the property such as termites, flood/fire damage, asbestos, etc. which allows a lower estimate to be accepted
  • Where possible, the care recipient’s estimate will be accepted in order to ensure their means assessment is completed promptly

See Valuation of real estate and other assets for more information including overseas real estate and types of real estate not included in the risk based model.

Can the care recipient’s estimate of their other real estate be assessed as reasonable?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, ask for a valuation, see Table 3

Real estate estimate is reasonable + Read more ...

  • The care recipient estimate of $ XXXXXX is considered reasonable as per the results of approved website check on DD/MM/YY
  • Updates completed in RCA/NCL on RCAA screen
  • Repeat estimate check process for each property owned by care recipient
  • Procedure ends here for estimate check

Valuation requests

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Care recipient’s estimated value of real estate is unreasonable + Read more ...

If the estimate for a property, including the principal home, is assessed as unreasonable create a Progress of claim Fast Note which states:

  • Real estate estimate check complete for address (insert address)
  • The care recipient estimate of $XXX,XXX is considered 'unreasonable' as per the results of the approved website check on date DD/MM/YY – has now been referred for formal valuation
  • If valuation is required as part of an initial means assessment (RCA/NCL), place claim on hold for 21 days adding keyword ^^AVO
  • Proceed with valuation request

2

Real estate valuation - complete SA470 + Read more ...

Complete an email requesting a valuation and complete the Services Australia Valuation Request (SA470) form - see the Resources page for a link to this form.

Save a copy of SA470 and complete all details for property requiring valuation. Including:

  • legal description of property
  • correct address details (refer to rates notice or other documentation for rural properties)
  • all details of the property that have been obtained from the care recipient
  • in comments: any details of estimate checks that have been completed
  • the care recipient’s estimated value of property
  • any details that need to be taken into account when assessing value

3

Refer SA470 to Valuation Helpdesk + Read more ...

  • Attach the completed SA470 to an email and send to the Valuation Helpdesk
    • See the Resources page for contact details
  • Email contents to include:
    • Subject: Aged care valuation/Full valuation or estimate check/CRN
    • Details of estimate checks that were completed
    • Estimate not accepted as reasonable due to
    • Care recipient estimate of property = $ (insert value)
    • CRN 111222333S, aged care estimate pre/post 1 July 2014
    • Current RCA status is: NCL or CUR – delete not applicable
  • Upload the SA470 to Customer First record
    • See Attaching electronic documents to a Centrelink customer's record using Document Tools in Customer First
  • Delete the original saved SA470 once the email is sent
  • For RCA/NCL – ensure keyword ^^AVO is added and claim held for 21 days

Procedure ends here.

Return and review of valuations

Table 4

Item

Description

1

Return and review of valuations + Read more ...

  • Valuation Helpdesk has completed real estate valuation, go to Item 2
  • Valuation Helpdesk has been unable to complete valuation, go to Item 3
  • Care recipient wishes to appeal real estate valuation:
    • Care recipient is in receipt of an income support payment (ISP), go to Item 4
    • Care recipient not in receipt of an ISP, go to Item 5

2

Real estate valuation returned by Valuation Helpdesk - SA470 + Read more ...

When the submitted SA470 valuation request is complete the Valuation team will create a DOC on the care recipient’s record with a detailed outcome of the valuation:

  • If there is a current started RCA/NCL, the DOC is completed and the claim is taken off hold ready for allocation
  • If there is no started RCA/NCL, the DOC will be open with an ACTRCA keyword added and transferred to region RCA
  • Once the valuation is completed and ready to be assessed, update the value of principal home or other real estate in RCA/NCL or other activity
  • For a principal home update RCAA screen
  • For other real estate, see Assessing and coding the Real estate details Mod R for assistance in updating REBS

3

Real estate valuation not able to be completed + Read more ...

When the Valuation Helpdesk has not been able to complete a valuation or there has been a change in financial position:

  • a Manual Follow Up (MFU) activity is created
    • See Step 9 on the Process page of Valuation of real estate and other assets for more information

4

ISP received - care recipient does not agree with the valuation of other real estate + Read more ...

When a care recipient in receipt of an income support payment (ISP) disputes the valuation of a property:

  • the review and new valuation request must be actioned by an APS 5 or higher, and
  • authority to inspect the property must be obtained

If   the care recipient is receiving an Income Support Payment (ISP) see the Customer contacts about the current assessed value of real estate table in Maintaining the value of real estate assets on customer records for more information.

5

Care recipient with no ISP does not agree with a valuation a formal review is requested for valuation of the principal home + Read more ...

  • Care recipient is not receiving an ISP. Complete an SA470, including all updated/other real estate information provided and upload to the customer record
  • Email the SA470 to the Valuation Helpdesk
  • Include advice that the care recipient does not agree with a previous manual valuation
  • Record the application for a formal review of the decision. See Request for an explanation or application for a formal review
