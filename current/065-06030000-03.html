Checking customer details and the claim

Step

Action

1

Lodgement of claim or documentation + Read more ...

Service Officers must stream the claim on the day it is received and check that a claim assessment can be completed.

See Claiming Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) for what to do upon receipt of a paper claim or documentation to support a claim, and how to help customers upload documents for an online or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC).

An online or ACC for CSHC cannot be submitted until all required tasks have been completed (exceptions apply).

Verbal claims completed using ACC must not be submitted or finalised until a verbal declaration has been accepted by the customer or a signed Customer Declaration Form (CDF) has been scanned to their record. See the Using Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) for CSHC table for more information.

If a rejected claim needs to be opened and reassessed, create a SOA shell in Process Direct. For more information see Indexing, re-indexing and cancelling claim activities.

2

Locate and review claim + Read more ...

Request or search for the claim work item via Inbox:

On the Transaction Summary Page (TS) select to review the customer’s claim responses and scanned documents.

Online claim/ACC submitted

When an online or ACC is submitted, a CSHC claim work item is created in Process Direct containing the claim information. Go to Step 4

Paper claim lodged

Where a paper CSHC claim (SA296, SA296A, SA443) has been uploaded view the scanned claim form. See Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images.

For partnered customers, take note if lodged as a joint claim. Where a joint claim has been lodged, the assessment process has to be followed for both customer and partner.

3

SOA shell created + Read more ...

When a paper claim is scanned to the customer's record, a SOA shell work item is generated on the customer's record. This contains no claim information apart from the:

  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Claim ID
  • Claim dates and
  • personal details.

The claim information is added into the work item using the scanned claim and documentation.

If the customer and partner have lodged a joint claim, when the claim is ready to be processed a manual SOA shell for the partner will be required.

Common screens that may need updating via SWE:

  • personal information (name, address, phone number)
  • interpreter information and language details (OCD)
  • Office Code (OC)
  • Payment Destination Details (PAS/PAD/PASB/PA/PAN) screen. If provided, select to add or update a customer's payment destination. Update customer's BSB, account number, account title for Benefit System: SHC – Seniors Health Card
  • Alternative identity (POI) if required

4

Check Customer Summary + Read more ...

Before contacting the customer or processing the claim, staff must check the Customer Summary in Process Direct and/or Customer First for customer specific warnings.

This includes checking:

  • the Customer Incident Management System (CIMS), and
  • the Managed Service Plan (MSP). See Customer aggression - Managed Service Plan (MSP) for more information

This alerts staff to the actions they can complete for the customer, and what action they advise customers to take.

5

Qualification on date of claim + Read more ...

On the day the claim was lodged was the customer Age Pension age?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, the customer is not qualified and claim must be rejected. Go to Processing Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) claims. Procedure ends here

See the Resources page for a link to the Age Pension age rules.

Note: early claim provisions do not apply to the CSHC. If the person is not qualified for CSHC on the date of claim lodgement the claim must be rejected. This includes the following situations:

  • A customer has claimed CSHC prior to becoming Age Pension age
  • Before the Age Pension cancellation date
  • Before the end of a NARWP

6

Residence requirements + Read more ...

On the day the claim was lodged, does the customer satisfy the residence requirements? See Residence assessment for customers claiming Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC).

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, the customer is not qualified and claim must be rejected. See Processing Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) claims. Procedure ends here

7

Customer located in Australia + Read more ...

Check if the customer was physically present in Australia on the date they lodged their claim, by checking the customer’s travel movements (if available) using the Travel Outside Australia Summary (TOAS) and Immigration Movements (RSIM) screens.

On the day the claim was lodged, is there evidence that the customer was not in Australia?

  • Yes, the customer is not qualified and claim must be rejected. See Processing Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) claims. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Customer receives a pension or allowance + Read more ...

On the day the claim was lodged, was the customer in receipt either of a:

  • Social Security pension, or
  • Social Security benefit
    • Yes, the customer is not qualified and claim must be rejected Go to Processing Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) claims. Procedure ends here
    • No, go to Step 9

9

Customer receives a DVA pension or allowance + Read more ...

On the day the claim was lodged, was the customer in receipt any of:

  • Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) Service pension,
  • DVA Age Pension,
  • Veteran Payment
  • Income Support Supplement (ISS)
  • DVA CSHC

See Income from Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) for more information.

  • Yes, the customer is not qualified and claim must be rejected. See Processing Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) claims. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Identity Confirmation + Read more ...

The claimant must meet the Identity Confirmation requirements. Identity Confirmation for a partner is only needed if the partner is also applying for CSHC.

On the TS screen, check the customer's Identity Status.

Has the customer met the Identity Confirmation requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, consider the following options:
    • provide identity documents, see Alternative Identity
    • provide proof of age, see Verifying the date of birth or age of a customer or child
    • attend in person to confirm identity, see Supporting Identity Confirmation for customers unable to attend in person

Where the identification requirements cannot be met, the claim will be rejected. See Processing Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) claims. Procedure ends here

11

Tax File Number (TFN) + Read more ...

Has the customer provided their (and their partner's) Tax File Number (TFN) (unless an exemption is granted)?

See Tax File Number (TFN) for Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) for more information.

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, a request for information is required, take note of the required information, go to Step 12

12

CSHC income test + Read more ...

Has the customer declared Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) for the reference year less than the Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) - Taxable Income Limit?

Note: this should include any deemed income from account based income streams.

For details of Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) for CSHC, see Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) income test and reference tax year.

See Rates and Thresholds for Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) - Taxable Income Limits

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No, the claim will be rejected due to income. See Processing Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) claims. Procedure ends here

13

Evidence of income + Read more ...

Has the customer provided the required evidence of income?

See Lodgement of the Notice of Assessment (NOA) and other evidence of income for Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) for more information.

  • Yes, go to Step 14
  • No, take note of the required information, go to Step 14

14

Partner’s intent to claim + Read more ...

Is the CSHC claimant single or has a partner who either:

  • has also claimed CSHC (including as part of a joint paper claim ), or
  • is in receipt of an income support payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 15
  • No, and their partner may also be eligible for CSHC or another service. Attempt to contact the partner by phone to clarify their intentions
    • If the partner is able to be contacted, invite the partner to complete a claim for CSHC and DOC the outcome
    • If the partner is unable to be contacted, in the partner record issue a Q164 and DOC the record with the contact attempts. Add also that they should be invited to complete a claim for CSHC

15

Required information + Read more ...

Has the customer provided a completed claim with all the required information?

  • Yes, go to Step 16
  • No, ensure the customer contact details are recorded correctly before making a request for information.
    • For online claims submitted under a claim submission exception and/or where there is evidence the customer may be in a crisis or need special help, make 2 genuine attempts to contact to explain the claim requirements. Check if the customer needs more time to meet the requirements due to their circumstances. Request the information and allow a reasonable timeframe to respond
    • For online claims where the customer has intentionally provided non-claim related documentation to allow their claim to be submitted, the claim must be rejected
    • For online claims, where more information is needed to assess the claim, contact by phone to explain the claim requirements and document the outcome. Request the information verbally or in writing and allow a reasonable timeframe to respond
    • For incomplete paper claims, contact by phone to explain the requirements and document the outcome. Request the information verbally or in writing and allow a reasonable timeframe to respond. See Claims received that are incomplete or incorrect

Update the Progress of claim Note/DOC or where a progress of claim DOC is required, create a Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Older Australians > Claims > Progress of AGE/SHC/PDB claim.

Where there is a new claim activity, HOLD the claim activity to due date that allows for a reasonable timeframe for a request for information.

Note: where a joint paper claim has been lodged, a request for information may also be required in the partner’s record.

Procedure ends here until customer responds or the allowed time has lapsed

16

Referrals + Read more ...

Is a specialist referral required?

  • Yes, select More Options > Referral. From the Select Referral Type drop down list select:
    • International Services (CIS) - Residence Assessment, if the data transfer from the Department of Home Affairs was not successful
    • Assessment of Care Arrangements (FAO) - Dependent child/ren, if there is a change in care of a child. This could be a change in shared care arrangements or a dependant entering or leaving care.
    • Hold claim pending outcome of specialist assessment
      In the text, note the care assessment is required for a CSHC claim. FAO processing see Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and income support payment new claim processing interactions
  • No, see Processing Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) claims.
