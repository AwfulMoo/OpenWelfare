This page contains the process for completing an aged care income assessment for care recipients who first entered permanent residential care prior to 1 July 2014 and are grandfathered.

On this page:

Entry event or income assessment is received

Actioning an income assessment

Actioning an income reassessment where a current income assessment exists

Entry event or income assessment is received

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Check for entry details and care type + Read more ...

Entry details must be checked to confirm that care recipient is grandfathered.

The entry details are located in:

  • Customer First - the RCA Institution Summary (RIS) and RCA Circumstance (RCIRC) screens
  • Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP):
    • Go to the Care recipient summary screen
    • To confirm Opt In status, select relevant entry into care and click on Entry status hyperlink
    • Click on Next until step 3 is showing, this will display Opt in status for the care recipient

Where multiple entry dates exist the original entry date and any breaks in care will need to be confirmed. This is in order to determine the correct assessment scheme and action.

Was the care recipient's first date of entry into care prior to 1 July 2014 with no break in care more than 28 days (excluding approved leave)?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No,
    • Care recipient is not grandfathered and need to be assessed under current assessment scheme
    • See Aged care means assessment - preliminary checks
    • Procedure ends here

2

Entry event or income assessment is received + Read more ...

What is the source of this activity?

  • Auto entry event (RCA/NCL) has been received, no form lodged, go to Step 3
  • Income assessment form has been received, see Income assessment received (table 2)
  • Care recipient or authorised third party is contacting regarding a current income assessment, see Care recipient with current income assessment (table 3)

3

RCA/NCL has been received for grandfathered care recipient + Read more ...

Entry event has been received indicating a grandfathered care recipient has changed providers. There has not been a break of more than 28 days and care recipient has not opted in to new scheme. Check the Benefit Status (XBS) screen in RCA system to see a history of RCA benefit status.

An income assessment can be completed automatically via data matching for a care recipient who:

  • had a current income assessment (RCA/CUR), and has not had a break in care of more than 28 days
  • currently receives a means tested income support payment
  • is a Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) recipient and is eligible for an automatic assessment. See the Resources page to determine the responsible department for aged care assessments

Does the care recipient meet one of these criteria?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Automatic assessment failed - checking and finalising the RCA/NCL + Read more ...

The RCA/NCL activities are able to be finalised using information held on record.

Prior to finalising the RCA/NCL the Service Officer should:

  • confirm date of care commencement in ACSP
  • update the RCIRC screen if required and confirm Assessment Scheme field states that care recipient is PRE2008 or POST2008
  • update RCA dependents (RDEP) screen if care recipient has dependent child/ren
  • document the activity using a Fast Note. Select Auto Text, use Aged Care > Assessment > Res Care Assessment - FIN. Include in the free text section that the care recipient was eligible for automatic assessment and any actions taken prior to finalisation

Procedure ends here.

5

Confirming care recipient's previous circumstances + Read more ...

A Means Not Disclosed (MND) status will continue with a new service when a care recipient is grandfathered and was previously:

  • RCA/REJ-MND, or
  • RCA/CAN-MND

Does RCIRC have a discharge date for the previous entry into care?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Coding departure for previous entry into care + Read more ...

When a care recipient does not have a current aged care (RCA) status, there will be no current link with ACMPS and any departure from care can not be updated. Departure details will need to be coded:

  • Check ACSP to confirm dates:
  • Care recipient > Care recipient summary for entry and departure dates
    • Care recipient > Event summary for departure reasons
  • Access the AL screen, take note of the date of receipt and (C) cancel RCA/NCL
  • Access the RCIRC screen and code discharge field
  • Proceed to the AR screen to finalise

Once discharge has been coded, re-index RCA/NCL. On IN screen:

  • code Surname of Person and DOB fields
  • compete the following Claim Details:
    • ServRsn: RCA
    • Act: NCL
    • Srce: LIA
    • DOR: original date of receipt of auto (*RC) activity
    • select Continue
    • select the customer from the list and select Continue
    • on Person Details (PED) screen key 'Y' to confirm and then Continue

Go to Step 7.

7

Income assessment to be rejected - MND + Read more ...

MND care recipients will continue to pay the maximum income tested fee (ITF) while in care. ACSP will show that a letter has been sent advising the care recipient and service provider of the ITF they are liable to pay.

To confirm a letter has been issued:

  • In ACSP:
    • locate care recipient record
    • view letters in Correspondence > Letter summary
  • In ACMSP:
    • locate care recipient record
    • view letters under Communication history tab
  • In SPARC:
    • locate care recipient record
    • view letters on Income Testing Letter Reprint Facility (2.13)

When care recipient meets these criteria:

  • select (S) the RCA/NCL activity from the AL screen
  • access the RCIRC screen and code new admission details
  • Go to RMND and (I) Insert the new date of admission to Means Not Disclosed Date of Event field
  • go to BA screen to reject the RCA/NCL:
    • SVC RSN = RCA
    • Action = REJ
    • Reason = MND
    • Effect Date = Date of event/entry (DOV)
    • Notes: PRE2008/POST2008 CR MTA Rejected MND
  • access the AR screen, do not finalise activity
  • return to RCIRC and confirm it contains correct Assessment Scheme and RIS screen confirms that Grandfathered: Y
  • use a Fast Note. Select Auto Text, use Aged Care > Assessment > Aged Care MTA REJECTED-OTHER to record the action taken
  • attach Fast Note to RCA/NCL and finalise via AR screen
  • procedure ends here

What is the current RCA status for this care recipient?

  • RCA/CUR, go to Step 2
  • RCA/REJ-MND or RCA/CAN-MND, go to Step 3

3

MND care recipient now providing income details + Read more ...

Has the care recipient subsequently been granted an income support payment since the rejection of means assessment?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Means assessment to be granted - 'income support recipient' (Services Australia) + Read more ...

If care recipient has been granted an income support payment, the income details provided for their payment can now be used to complete an income assessment.

If a care recipient was granted an income support payment within 3 months of their means assessment being rejected/cancelled, it can be backdated to their date of entry.

If more than 3 months has elapsed, the care recipient will retain their 'MND' status from the date of entry and the status of 'DSS' will apply from the date of grant of the income support payment only.

  • Manually index an RCA new claim on the IN screen and record the following fields with:
    • ServRsn: RCA
    • Act: NCL
    • Srce: LIA
    • DOR: code the date of grant of the income support payment
  • Within the activity, go to the RMND screen and 'I'nsert the date of grant of the income support payment in the Means Not Disclosed Date of Event: field and update the Means Not Disclosed Indicator: field to 'N'o
  • Access the AR screen and place an 'N' in the Finished: field. Do not finalise the activity
  • Check the RCA event on the EV List (XEV) screen. The date of event of the RCINC and the RCSTAT entries is the date of grant of the income support payment and the RCSTAT status should be 'DSS'
  • Finalise the activity on the AR screen and record the details on a DOCProcedure ends here

5

Care recipient previously failed to provide information to complete income assessment + Read more ...

When an income assessment is being requested, contact the care recipient or authorised party who is making the request to confirm date of effect for this assessment.

This may be:

  • the original date of entry into care
  • a subsequent date of entry into care
  • the date that the care recipient had a change in circumstances

Any verification documents that are required will need to contain information for the relevant date of effect.

Income and asset details supplied as part of an income assessment may require verification to ensure that the information provided is true and accurate.

Compare the information provided with the Home Care means assessment form Checklist ensuring all necessary supporting documentation has been provided with the claim.

Is there more information required before this assessment can be finalised?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No,
    • if care recipient has departed care, go to Step 7
    • if care recipient still in care, go to Step 8

6

Requesting further information + Read more ...

Attempt to contact the care recipient or authorised third party. There is no obligation to provide information verbally but Service Officers are to advise the benefits and the potential consequences of failing to provide the information within the required timeframe.

If a written request is required, see Aged care letters - creating manual letters.

The Resources page has a link to the RFI letter templates.

The contents of an RFI should be outlined in an Aged Care Progress of Claim Fast Note.

Place on hold for 21 days plus an allowance for the letter to be received.

Include any updates to income and assets that have been made from verbal advice.

Procedure ends here.

7

Care recipient has departed care + Read more ...

When a care recipient has been coded as deceased in Customer First, a means assessment will not be able to be coded and a manual income assessment is required.

Has the care recipient been coded as deceased in Customer First?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Completing income assessment + Read more ...

Complete income assessment:

  • Manually index an RCA new claim on the IN screen and record the following fields with:
    • ServRsn: RCA
    • Act: NCL
    • Srce: LIA
    • DOR: date of lodgement of form or verbal application
  • Go to the RCA Task Selector (RCTS) screen
  • (S) select the income screens that need to be updated and complete updates required. Note: if data already exists on a screen, a Y will appear on the RCTS screen. Check this data for accuracy
  • The coding of income for aged care purposes aligns with the Social Security Act 1991 and the following links provide further information:
    • Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services
    • Managed investments - adding a new investment
    • Adding shares and securities
    • Superannuation
    • Assessing deprivation/gifting
    • Income streams
    • Exempt Funeral Investments (Funeral Bonds)
    • Income from Department of Veterans' Affairs
    • Exempt lump sums (for example, National Redress payments)
  • If the care recipient has dependent children in their care, in Customer First record on the RCA dependants (RDEP) screen:
    • Number of Dependants Date of Event: DOV of means assessment
    • Total Number of Dependants: enter number of dependent children
  • Within the activity, go to the RMND screen and 'I'nsert the relevant date of effect in the Means Not Disclosed Date of Event: field and update the Means Not Disclosed Indicator: field to 'N'o
  • Go to the AR screen and place an 'N' in the Finished: field. Do not finalise the activity
  • Return to RCIRC and confirm it contains correct Assessment Scheme and RIS screen confirms that Grandfathered: 'Y'
  • Check the RCA event on the EV List (XEV) screen. The date of event of the RCINC and the RCSTAT entries is the date of grant of the income support payment and the RCSTAT status should be 'DSS'
  • Record the details in a Fast Note and attached to RCA/NCL, finalise via AR screen
  • Procedure ends here

9

Care recipient deceased, income assessment lodged + Read more ...

Manual calculation of income is required to determine liability for income tested fees (ITF).

Customer First will not allow any further coding/updates to be made where the record has been coded as deceased. Assessable income, including deemed income and income stream assessments, can be obtained by coding the means assessment in the Customer First training environment. All financial investments can be totalled and coded as a lump sum on Savings Summary (SVS) screen and deemed income amounts can then be obtained from Financial Investment Summary (IVIS) screen.

Once all income amounts have been obtained, see Aged Care Reviews - Manual adjustments for Pre 1 July 2014 Residential Care.

Procedure ends here.

Actioning an income reassessment where a current income assessment exists

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Care recipient or authorised third party contacts regarding current income assessment + Read more ...

If care recipient/third party:

  • Advises income details have changed and customer wants income to be reassessed, go to Step 2
  • Advises current income assessment and costs have placed customer in hardship, refer to Aged care financial hardship assistance - overview
  • Procedure ends here

2

Care recipient advises income details have changed + Read more ...

When a reassessment has occurred, any income tested fee (ITF) paid by the resident will be adjusted (where applicable). When a care recipient's income has increased or decreased, the ITF will be adjusted from the date of effect coded in the reassessment. Any refunds will be calculated at the following quarterly review.

Update changes that have been advised:

  • access the RCA Task Selector (RCTS) screen in the RCA system and press [Enter]
  • 'S'electing the item(s) of income to be updated. Note: if data already exists on a screen, a Y will appear on the RCTS screen. Check this data for accuracy
  • update the income amounts on the appropriate screens
  • code individual financial assets if information is available
  • investments that were assessed under the deeming provisions were totalled and recorded on the Direct Investment (SVDI) screen as Type = 'OTH' or 'from SA316'
  • the date of effect to be used is the latter of:
    • the date of change, or
    • the date of entry to the facility
  • record all details on a DOC
  • finalise the assessment on the Assessment Result (AR) screen
  • see Aged care reviews - reassessments and updates for information on fee changes and refunds
  • Procedure ends here
