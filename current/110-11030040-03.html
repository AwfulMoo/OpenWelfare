Coercive information notice + Read more ...

Before issuing a coercive notice, consider:

  • Alternatives. Whether the information is available by other means, for example a voluntary request. The use of a notice might be the preferred option, both for the department and for notice recipients. Alternative ways of obtaining information are not always practical, for example, if time is a crucial factor or the person is unlikely to provide the information voluntarily
  • Compliance costs. Weigh up the importance of the information sought against the probable cost to the notice recipient of providing it
  • Scope. The use of a notice must relate to administrative questions of establishing a person’s qualification, payability and rate of payment. The notice should seek only the information that is necessary for current information requirements
  • Reasonable belief. There must be a reasonable belief established that the notice recipient is able to provide the information or requested documents

Recording third party request + Read more ...

Create a Req. Info 3rd Party activity and code relevant details.

Coercive information notice

Select the relevant information notice from the Investigation Toolkit, complete fields and issue the notice to the third party. To select the correct notice, investigators must consider the payment type that is the subject of the administrative investigation.

FI003C is to be used to obtain general information from third parties, such as financial institutions, real estate agents and educational institutions.

FI004C is to be used to obtain first and follow up employment verification information from third parties.

Note: a Third Party Contact Database in iQ holds contact details for numerous third parties to help Intelligence and Investigations Branch staff send requests to the appropriate person and address.

Voluntary request letter + Read more ...

The Voluntary Request Letter template (IMS002) must be used if there is no legislative basis to exercise the coercive information-gathering power to obtain information or documents from third parties as detailed in Part 5 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999.

It is also to be used to request additional information and/or documents to help the Commonwealth Director of Public Prosecutions (CDPP) when coercive information gathering powers cannot be exercised.

Note: when requesting information from Commonwealth, State or Territory Governments, it is preferable that any existing disclosure mechanism be used to gather information first, as a matter of courtesy.

The Voluntary Request Letter (IMS002) is available from the Investigation Toolkit.

Note: a Third Party Contact Database in iQ holds contact details for numerous third parties to help Intelligence and Investigations Branch staff send requests to the appropriate person and address.

Recording response to third party request + Read more ...

Select the appropriate activity and record relevant details.

Upload documents sent and received as an attachment to the activity.

Update status of activity to Completed (Closed).

5

No response received + Read more ...

If a notice recipient does not respond within 21 days, phone the recipient and allow a further 5 working days for them to comply.

If possible, contact the third party by phone to request the return of information request. Allow extra time if appropriate. Update the investigation record to reflect the extension of time.

When no reply is expected or likely to be received, review the progress of the case and determine the action to be taken.

If no response is received, update investigation record in the appropriate case management system, add notes and update status of the activity to 'No response further Req' (Closed)

6

Use of notice responses + Read more ...

Notice recipients have a privilege against self-incrimination when responding to coercive information gathering notices.

A person cannot refuse to provide the requested information or document(s) because it may incriminate them.

However, there are protections for individuals when compelled to provide information in response to a notice. These protections prevent the use of information or documents against the person that provided them (with a limited exception). In addition, any information or evidence given that would tend to incriminate the person may not be used to gather other evidence against that person.

Limited exception - information or documents given by an individual are only admissible in criminal proceedings against that individual where that person has provided false information or documents.

FI003C and FI004C notice templates include text advising recipients of their rights and obligations in relation to responding to the notice and the operation of self-incrimination provisions.

Birth, Deaths and Marriages + Read more ...

A customer's official date of birth (DOB) for Services Australia purposes is the date indicated by:

  • State or Territory Registrar of Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) if born in Australia, or
  • Department of Home Affairs if born outside of Australia

Only the dates identified on documents issued by these agencies can be accepted as the official DOB.

To verify a DOB, death or marriage, issue the appropriate request for the required state. This is available in the Investigation Toolkit. For help, see:

  • Verifying the date of birth or age of a customer or child
  • Confirmation of the death of an adult or child

Child care centres, schools and sporting organisations + Read more ...

Before requesting information, a Critical Decision Record must be completed and approved by Assistant Director of Investigations. See Critical decision recording.

If approved, complete the appropriate letter from the Investigation Toolkit and issue request:

  • notice requiring information - general questions (FI003C)

eBay + Read more ...

eBay developed the Law Enforcement eRequest System to enable law enforcement officers seeking records for investigations that relate to either the eBay marketplace or the PayPal global payment service to quickly and securely request this data. It replaces fax-based data requests.

All requests must be in accordance with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) outlined in schedule 1 of the Privacy Act 1988.

Accessing eBay data

See the Resources page or the Investigation Toolkit for the link to the eBay Law Enforcement eRequest System.

At the Law Enforcement eRequest System homepage, Investigators are to enter their 'human.services.gov.au' email address into eBay's secure system, and then enter the security code as shown in the image (click Refresh Image if hard to read).

An email will be sent with a link to proceed to ask relevant questions.

For help, contact the Identity fraud .

4

Employers + Read more ...

When verification of earnings is required from an employer, issue a Request for employment details (FI004C - first request) and allow 21 days for reply.

If further information is subsequently required, issue a Request for further employment questions (FI004C - second request).

These letters are available from within the Investigation Toolkit.

No response

If the employer does not respond within 21 days, phone the employer and allow a further five working days for them to comply.

5

Financial institutions + Read more ...

See Electronic Data Exchange with Financial Institutions tool.

Information which can be requested from Commonwealth Bank of Australia includes:

  • account details (account number, name, BSB and current balance as at the time of request)
  • other persons linked to the account (joint account holder)
  • a summary of transactions in the requested period (starting and ending balances for the period, and total deposits and withdrawals)
  • additional details provided on opening of account (customer's date of birth and address)
  • other accounts held by the customer (transaction accounts, savings accounts and term deposits)

A written request for information will need to be sent to financial institutions not covered by the electronic data exchange. Issue a Notice requiring information - general questions (Fi003C). This is available from within the Investigation Toolkit.

An Affidavit Request Template is also available.

6

Neighbourhood enquiries + Read more ...

Before making any enquiries, a critical decision record must be completed and approved by Assistant Director of Investigations. See Critical decision recording.

A neighbourhood enquiry can only be conducted with the consent of the third party involved and is a voluntary arrangement that may stop at any time. These enquiries are not to be conducted by mail.

7

Equifax + Read more ...

Equifax is the largest single source of data-based business and credit information in Australia and New Zealand. Available information includes:

  • credit reports
  • identity verification
  • financial, asset, and income information searches

Consumer Credit File information includes:

  • name
  • address
  • previous address
  • date of birth
  • sex
  • drivers licence number
  • last employer
  • details of applications for credit
  • any public record data

The Services Australia has an agreement with Equifax to utilise an online automated platform to obtain certain reports.

Reports available through the portal include:

  • Land Title searches
  • Corporate and Company searches
  • Bankruptcy Extracts

See the Resources page for a link to the Equifax site.

For help, contact the Tactical Identify fraud .

Caution

Information should only be sought from Equifax where no other avenue of enquiry is available. Access to a person's record will appear on their Equifax record, so the access must be able to be justified.

Non-customer records should only be accessed if there is other evidence to suggest a customer has, or may have, misrepresented their circumstances and this cannot be substantiated by standard enquiries or by debt recovery staff. The checks should not be conducted as a matter of routine during a compliance tip-off review.

Criminal investigation + Read more ...

Coercive information gathering powers must not be used in an Intelligence and Investigations Branch criminal investigation. Coercive information gathering powers cannot be lawfully used to answer administrative questions of qualification, payability and rate of payment at the same time as conducting a criminal investigation.

Investigators should utilise techniques generally open to criminal investigations to obtain information from third parties. This may include:

  • requesting documents or information voluntarily
  • requesting a voluntary witness statement, or
  • use of search warrants
