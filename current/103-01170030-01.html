From 17 March 2021, Income Management (IM) customers or customers in profile for IM (Disengaged Youth (DEY) or Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR) based in the Northern Territory (NT) can permanently transition to the Cashless Debit Card (CDC) program. Customers on an income support payment who are living in the NT and are not currently on IM, will be able to volunteer for the Cashless Debit Card (CDC) from this date.
For more information on this process or to initiate an IM to CDC transition, see Income Management (IM) to Cashless Debit Card (CDC) Transition in the Northern Territory, which also contains staff resources and links to the transition overview Intranet page for additional information.

This document explains how to conduct an Initial Assessment interview to place a customer onto Income Management, under the Disengaged Youth or Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient measures.

Initial Assessment interview

An Initial Assessment interview is needed to place a customer on Income Management, under the Disengaged Youth and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient measures. The interview:

  • explains how Income Management works
  • determines how their income managed funds can be best used to meet the priority needs of the customer and their family, and
  • give information about other services which may help them, including Centrepay

The interview can be held face to face or over the phone. This is conducted with the customer and if applicable, their partner or payment nominee.

The interview provides an opportunity to advise customers in profile for IM (Disengaged Youth (DEY) or Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR) who live in the Northern Territory they do not have to start IM, but instead permanently transition to the Cashless Debit Card.

Failure to attend interview

If a customer does not attend an Initial Assessment interview within their Income Management engagement period, Income Management will be automatically applied to their payments.

If a customer still does not attend an Initial Assessment interview 28 days after Auto Income Management starts, their payments may be suspended.

Preparing for an Initial Assessment interview

To prepare for an Initial Assessment interview, Service Officers must:

  • Check whether the customer needs an interpreter, and book interpreter if needed
  • Check whether the customer has an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes:
    • If the customer does not have an excluded payment nominee, the customer is eligible for Income Management. However, the payment nominee must attend the Initial Assessment interview as they are responsible for managing the customer's payments
    • If the customer is a payment nominee for another person, that person's payments will also be income managed
  • Determine which exemption category applies to the customer:
    • For customers without dependent children, check for current education and rate of payment details should the customer want to make an exemption application
    • For customers with dependent children, check age of children to determine what evidence will be needed by the customer if they want to make an exemption application
  • Check the record for any vulnerability indicators
  • Check the customer's current rate of payment and any regular income
  • Check whether the customer has any current Centrepay deductions or Rent Deduction Scheme payments
  • Have relevant Income Management and self-service communication products available for the customer
