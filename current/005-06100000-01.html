Deductions from Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS) payments to repay money owing to Department of Home Affairs 005-06100000

  • Background

  • Resources
Expand all


This document outlines the process when the Department of Home Affairs requires Services Australia to reduce the customer’s Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS) payment to recover monies owed to them.

Services Australia debt vs the Department of Home Affairs’ debt

A debt to Services Australia occurs if the customer has received an amount of SRSS payment that they were not entitled to, for example:

  • a change in circumstances occurs for a period already paid to the customer and the SRSS payment is reassessed
  • loss of entitlement to SRSS payment, such as:
    • change of visa status
    • relationship status
    • care of dependent children
    • accommodation
    • rent
    • left the country
    • in prison
    • liquid assets over the allowable limit
  • income received, such as:
    • income from employment
    • self-employment
    • compensation
    • investments has not been taken into account when calculating the SRSS payment paid
  • Administrative or system error

A Department of Home Affairs debt occurs when a SRSS customer is required to repay amounts provided to them by a Department of Home Affairs contracted service provider.

These may include:

  • financial loans
  • pay for damages to a property
  • unpaid utilities bill
  • other services

How the Department of Home Affairs advises Services Australia of the debt

Money owing to the Department of Home Affairs will not be ‘raised as a debt’ on the Services Australia's system. Department of Home Affairs will advise Services Australia:

  • the debt via the data link, and
  • the deduction will be automatically applied to the customer's payment

Five per cent (5%) of a customer's total SRSS payment (after the income test is applied) will be deducted each fortnight until:

  • the Department of Home Affairs' debt is fully repaid, or
  • the customer's SRSS payment is cancelled

Services Australia advises the Department of Home Affairs when the total sum of money has been recovered. This advice will be provided automatically to the Department of Home Affairs via the data link.

Notifying the customer of a Department of Home Affairs debt

The Department of Home Affairs are responsible for managing the details of the money owing including:

  • notifying the customer they have to repay money, and
  • advising the customer when the money owing has been repaid in full

All enquiries regarding debts as a result of monies owed to the Department of Home Affairs should be referred to the customer's Department of Home Affairs contracted Service Providers .

Services Australia and the Department of Home Affairs debt recovery hierarchy

In the debt recovery hierarchy, the Department of Home Affairs debts are recovered first. For example, a SRSS customer has a Services Australia debt being recovered by withholdings from their SRSS payment and Services Australia are then advised of a Department of Home Affairs debt. The Services Australia debt withholdings are to cease and the Department of Home Affairs deduction recovery is to commence. All outstanding Services Australia debts are transferred to the Department of Home Affairs.
