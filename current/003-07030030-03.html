New claim for Crisis Payment (CrP) after release from prison or psychiatric confinement

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer contact + Read more ...

A customer can apply for CrP if they have:

  • been released from prison, psychiatric confinement or juvenile detention and
  • spent 14 or more days there as a result of being charged with committing an offence

To be eligible for CrP, a customer must be in receipt of, or have claimed and be qualified (and payable) for a:

  • social security benefit
  • social security pension, or
  • ABSTUDY Living Allowance

Note: on the benefit status line in Customer First (CF) or Customer Record (CR) in the customer's record, the primary payment must show as CUR (current) or NCL (new claim).

  • See how to view receipt of ABSTUDY Living Allowance on the system

Check Search online claim link in Workspace (Customer First) if an online claim exits.

Is the customer getting or have they made a new claim for a social security pension, a social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Customer not in receipt of social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance + Read more ...

Customers released from prison may be eligible to have their income support payment restored.

Is the customer eligible to have their income support payment restored?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the customer needs to make a new claim for social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance. Go to Step 4

3

Restoration of social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance + Read more ...

If a payment can be restored and the customer is partnered:

  • Check the payment rate and Marital Status (MS) screen
  • If qualifier code: 'GAC' (In Gaol) is there, see Assessment of circumstances for customers with partner in prison. This must be updated before the CrP claim being assessed

Disability Support Pension (DSP)

Customers may have their DSP suspended due to imprisonment (SUS-IMP) for up to 2 years. There is no need for a new claim in these cases. Instead, the DSP may be restored from the date of release.

  • See the Resources page for contact details for release of medical information
  • Medical records can help with DSP claims, and help the customer's doctor to assess them for ongoing medication. Usually prisoners are released with up to 3 days medication. It may be hard for a new doctor to assess some illnesses or medical conditions, for example, schizophrenia, in such a short period

Parenting Payment (PP)

  • PP should not be granted with a future start date for parents about to be released from prison, because the customer will not usually have a dependent child in their care while in custody
  • If they do have a child with them in custody, and the child remains with them when released, PP can be restored or granted upon the customer's release from prison
  • In most cases, JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) should be assessed for these customers before their release from prison
  • PP backdating rules allow a claim to be backdated to the date the child came into the customer's care, if the claim for PP is made within 28 days of the child entering care
  • If the child enters the customer's care and they qualify for PP on the day of release, they should receive a 'top up' of the difference between PP and the payment they were granted (JSP or YA) before their release. The difference in rates of CrP should also be topped up

For more about restoring other payments, see Restoration of payments (CLK).

  • No,

  • Tell the customer to claim income support and CrP online if they have access to online services

Customer does not want to pursue claim + Read more ...

A claim for CrP - PRI is not pursued if the customer identified they have incorrectly enquired about CrP:

In all cases where the CrP is not pursued:

7

Confirm eligibility criteria - customer contact within 7 days (Deemed claim provisions) + Read more ...

A customer must contact Centrelink within 7 days of the date of release from Prison or Psychiatric Confinement.

After the initial contact, the customer must lodge the claim within 14 days. See Within 7 days after the extreme circumstance occurred.

The system will assess the event date (the date the customer was released from prison or psychiatric confinement) against the date the claim was lodged. If the claim was lodged more than 21 days after the event date, the system will display a rejection of LOD - did not lodge in time on the entitlements (ELD) screen.

If the claim was lodged between 8 and 21 days after the event date, the following warning will display:

'SR623 - Prison Actual Release date is between 8-21 days, check contact date'

Note: the event date is considered to be day 0.

If the customer is released from prison or psychiatric confinement located overseas, they can contact from overseas, but the claim must be made from within Australia in the allowable timeframe. See the Resources page for a link to Confirming admission and release.

When did the customer lodge the claim?

  • Within 7 days after event date, go to Step 9
  • Within 8-21 days after the event date (SR612 warning message displaying), go to Step 8
  • More than 21 days after the event date, go to Step 11

8

Customer lodged claim between 8-21 days after event date + Read more ...

If there is a record of contact, the customer satisfies the contact requirements for the CrP claim, and deemed provisions apply.

Did the customer contact within 7 days of the extreme circumstances (event) date?

9

Confirm eligibility criteria - customer in Australia + Read more ...

The customer must be in Australia on the date of claim to be granted a CrP. The system will check if the customer is recorded as being outside Australia or living in another country and present the following warning:

‘SR598 - Customer may not be in Australia’

If this warning presents:

  • Check Travel Outside Australia (TOAS) screen to see if the customer is outside Australia. Refresh the immigration datalink if the record shows they are outside Australia, but the Service Officer believes they are in Australia.
  • Check Country of Residence (CRES) screen to see if the customer is living in Australia. If the information needs to be updated, see Coding the CRES screen

Was the customer in Australia when the claim was lodged?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, customer is not eligible, reject the claim:
    • Update the Manual rejection field to NIA - Not in Australia
    • Select Assess and then Next
    • Go to Step 11

10

Confirm eligibility criteria - customer in Prison for 14 days or more + Read more ...

The customer must have spent 14 or more day in prison or psychiatric confinement. The customer must have been charged with committing an offence.

Note: customers who have been in psychiatric confinement but have not been charged with committing an offence are not eligible for CrP - PRI.

There are a number of ways to identify the customer’s admission and release date. See the Resources page for a link to Confirming admission and release.

  • Check the ABH or PNSH screen and check the imprisonment code and date of event
  • Check DOCs on the customer’s record. The prison admission script generates a DOC as part of the prison admission review while will included an admission date
  • Check the customer's release documents for an admission and release date
  • If the prison admission date cannot be identified on the customers record the period in custody may be able to be confirmed by contacting the Prison Liaison Officer for that prison, or the prison/institution directly
  • Check the customer has not already received a Crisis Payment in the 14 days preceding the event date on the CRISIS_PS screen

Was the customer in prison or psychiatric confinement for more than 14 days because they were charged with committing an offence?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, customer is not eligible, reject the claim:
    • Update the Manual rejection field to L14 - Period of imprisonment is less than fourteen (14) days
    • Select Assess and then Next
    • Go to Step 11

11

Determine grant or reject outcome + Read more ...

The claim may present an error or warning message. See the Resources page for more information.

The ELD screen will display the outcome of the claim. Confirm that the expected outcome is reflected.

If the claim is incorrectly indicating a grant or rejection, go back to the CRISIS PRI questionnaire using the Super Key field to check all data is entered correctly. If a manual grant or rejection has been identified this can be coded using the Manual rejection reason or Manual Grant indicator fields. See the Resources page for a list of rejection codes and reasons.

  • Select Finish
  • Review the prepopulated Claim Finalisation DOC and add any further information if required
  • Select Finalise
  • A letter will be sent to the customer notifying them of the outcome
  • If the claim is selected for QMA, add 'Crisis Payment should be paid via NPP - QMO to action after grant' to the DOC

If the ELD screen is displaying:

  • Grant, and the customer
    • has a payment nominee, go to Step 13
    • does not have a payment nominee, go to Step 14
  • REJ-PEC, go to Step 12
  • Reject for any other reason, procedure ends here

12

ELD Screen is displaying REJ-PEC + Read more ...

Crisis Payment can only be paid once for an extreme circumstance reason.

If a customer has already been granted a CrP-PRI with the same release date:

  • check the previous claim and related DOCs
  • determine if the customer lodged this CrP-PRI for the same extreme circumstance

Has the customer already been paid a CrP-PRI for the same extreme circumstance?

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible
    • The system will display rejection reason PEC on the ELD screen
    • Procedure ends here
  • No,
    • Override the rejection using the Manual grant indicator on the CRISIS_PRI screen
    • Go to Step 13

13

Payment nominee + Read more ...

If the customer's payments are directed to a payment nominee and the customer is unable to access funds from the Public Trustee on the day, contact the State/Public Trustee.

  • Ask the State/Public Trustee if they can advance the customer funds:
    • If the State/Public Trustee can to advance the customer funds, tell the customer
    • If the State/Public Trustee cannot advance the customer funds but grants approval to deposit CrP directly into the customer's account, take note of the approving officer's name
    • If payment is to be made to the State/Public Trustee, the Org Service Contact Details (OSCD) screen provides contact details for the organisation, or see Public Trustee offices
  • If the customer has been released from prison and needs payment that day, nominee details do not need to be changed. Payment can be made by the New Payments Platform (NPP), Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) or Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT).
  • If the customer has been released on another day, the payment nominee details have to be adjusted
    • Note details of the organisation from the customer's record
    • Run the Nominee Action script to end the payment nominee details
    • Deliver an immediate payment by their preferred method of payment

Run the Nominee Action script to reinstate the payment nominee details.

Some manual intervention will be needed when starting a nominee arrangement with the same nominee on the same day. As the system will recognise the same payment destination, press [Enter] through the payment destination linking process, and then press OK.

If the nominee arrangement cannot be re-recorded the same day, create a review to record the next business day.

14

Payment of Crisis Payment + Read more ...

Payment should be made using the New Payments Platform (NPP).

If the claim is selected to go to QMA, add 'Crisis Payment should be paid via NPP - QMO to action after grant' to the DOC.

If NPP is unavailable, payment should be made by Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS).

Direct credit can also be used if neither NPP or RTGS is available.

Only if these options are unavailable should an EBT card be considered.

For Farm Household Assistance (FHA) customers:

Approval of the customer's Social Service Plan (SSP) must occur before paying the Crisis Payment. The system will automatically approve this within 15 minutes. To check if the SSP has been approved, see Navigating to SSP. Once approved, pay the Crisis Payment using RTGS. Otherwise, the payment will deliver using direct credit the following business day.

13

Payment nominee + Read more ...

If the customer's payments are directed to a payment nominee and the customer is unable to access funds from the Public Trustee on the day, contact the State/Public Trustee.

  • Ask the State/Public Trustee if they are able to advance the customer funds:
    • If the State/Public Trustee can advance the customer funds, tell the customer
    • If the State/Public Trustee cannot advance the customer funds but grants approval to deposit CrP directly into the customer's account, take note of the approving officer's name
    • If payment is to be made to the State/Public Trustee, the Org Service Contact Details (OSCD) screen provides contact details for the organisation, or see Public Trustee offices
  • If the customer has been released from prison and needs payment that day, nominee details do not need to change. Payment can be made by the New Payments Platform (NPP), Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) or Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT).
  • If the customer has been released on another day, the payment nominee details have to be adjusted
    • Note details of the organisation from the customer's record
    • Run the Nominee Action script to end the payment nominee details
    • Deliver an immediate payment by their preferred method of payment

Run the Nominee Action script to reinstate the payment nominee details.

Some manual intervention will be needed when starting a nominee arrangement with the same nominee on the same day. As the system will recognise the same payment destination, press [Enter] through the payment destination linking process, and then press OK.

If the nominee arrangement cannot be re-recorded the same day, create a review to record the next business day.

14

Payment of Crisis Payment + Read more ...

Payment should be made using the New Payments Platform (NPP).

If the claim is selected to go to QMA, add 'Crisis Payment should be paid via NPP - QMO to action after grant' to the DOC.

If NPP is unavailable, payment should be made by Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS).

Direct credit can also be used if neither NPP or RTGS is available.

Only if these options are unavailable should an EBT card be considered.

For Farm Household Assistance (FHA) customers:

Approval of the customer's Social Service Plan (SSP) must occur before paying the Crisis Payment. The system will automatically approve this within 15 minutes. To check if the SSP has been approved, see Navigate to SSP. Once approved, pay the Crisis Payment using RTGS. Otherwise, the payment will deliver using direct credit the following business day.

Other considerations for prison release customers

Description

1

Hardship advance or anticipated payment + Read more ...

If a customer has been released from prison or psychiatric confinement and is in severe financial hardship, it may be appropriate to offer the customer a hardship advance of the first instalment of their regular payment. See Making a decision about a hardship advance or anticipated payment.

2

Defer debt recovery for 28 days + Read more ...

Customers who are in prison should have the recovery of their debts deferred until release. Upon release, customers typically need further deferment until they can negotiate a debt repayment arrangement.

If a customer who has been released from prison has an outstanding debt, Prison Liaison Officers (PLO) with access to the Debt Recovery script can temporarily write-off the debt for 28 days. This allows the customer to receive full entitlement for 2 pay periods and minimises hardship. See Prison Release under Debt Recovery general information.

PLOs can tell the customer that there are a number of repayment options to repay their debt. The PLO can negotiate for a preferred payment option on the customer's behalf. If a PLO is not available before release, the customer can use their Centrelink online account or contact the Debt Management Branch to organise repayment.

See:

  • Identifying and managing debt activities on the system
  • Temporary write-off of debts

3

Reduction or suspension of advance payments + Read more ...

If the customer has been released from prison and meets the financial hardship criteria for Crisis Payment, they are eligible for a reduction or suspension in recovery of an existing advance payment for 4 weeks. See Changes to the repayment rate of an advance payment.

4

Give a temporary exemption from mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

Prison release customers generally find it difficult to look for work immediately after their release. Many need to sort out basic personal needs such as travelling home, permanent accommodation, food, clothing and medical assessments. See Exemptions from mutual obligation requirements for prison release customers.

5

Centrepay offer + Read more ...

Offer Centrepay.

Customers released from prison usually have a higher than average debt and Centrepay may help with budgeting.

Tell the customer that Centrepay is a free service that allows for voluntary deductions from customer's Centrelink payments to cover bills such as electricity, gas, telephone, water, court fines, some chemist methadone programs, whitegoods and education fees. See Centrepay.

6

Update circumstances on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen + Read more ...

Information recorded on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen alerts Service Officers and Employment Service Providers to job seekers who may have a higher risk of non-compliance, such as released prisoners.

See Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirement.

If the customer was not in prison for at least 14 days, check if other categories should be recorded, for example drug and alcohol dependency, homelessness; lack of literacy and numeracy skills; psychiatric problems or mental illness. See Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirement.
