This page contains information on former resident provisions that apply to Australian residents who left Australia to live in another country and later return to live in Australia and then are transferred to or claim Age Pension or Disability Support Pension (DSP).

Transfer to or claim Age Pension or DSP

Step

Action

1

The customer advises of overseas departure + Read more ...

The Portability Script - Departures and Returns will generally correctly assess if the former resident provisions apply to a customer. This procedure should only be used if the portability script is not working.

Is the script working?

  • Yes, go to Coding departures and returns for customers leaving Australia
  • No, and the interview cannot be delayed, go to Step 2
  • No, and the interview can be delayed,
    • Book an appointment to be completed by the customer's local Service Centre
    • Ensure customer contact details are correct
    • Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

2

Check if the customer is paid Age Pension or DSP + Read more ...

Is the customer currently in receipt of Age Pension or DSP?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the customer's payment is not affected by former resident provisions. Continue portability processing. Go to Step 9

3

Financial assistance under the Medical Treatment Overseas Program (MTOP) + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving financial assistance under the MTOP, either because they are receiving treatment or are accompanying such a person?

  • Yes, the customer is not affected by former resident provisions while receiving financial assistance under the MTOP. Code a review if MTOP payment ceases within 2 years. Continue portability processing. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Determine date of grant or transfer + Read more ...

Check Pensions General Information (PNGI) screen and the Additional Residence Details (ARD) screen.

Was the original date of grant or the transfer from an international agreement assessment to autonomous assessment less than 2 years ago?

  • Yes, check the Country of Residence (CRES) screen to verify if the customer lived in a country other than Australia. If yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the customer's payment is not affected by former resident provisions. Go to Step 9

5

Check if the customer's most recent arrival for residence in Australia was less than 2 years ago + Read more ...

Was the customer's most recent arrival for residence less than 2 years ago?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, the customer's payment is not affected by former resident provisions. Go to Step 9

6

Previous residence in Australia and return to Australia + Read more ...

Did the customer:

  • previously reside in Australia, and
  • leave Australia and at some point reside in a country other than Australia, and
  • return to reside in Australia?
    • Yes, customer's payment may be affected by former resident provisions, go to Step 7
    • No, customer's payment is not affected by former resident provisions, go to Step 9

7

Check if the customer granted or transferred to the pension since their most recent arrival for residence + Read more ...

It is very important to understand that a customer who has been receiving DSP outside Australia on an indefinite basis, who returns to Australia for residence, is transferred to an Age Pension while in Australia and then leaves Australia within 2 years will not usually be subject to former resident rules. So long as the customer has remained qualified for payment continuously (without use of an international agreement), the former resident rules do not apply to this customer as the customer did not return to Australia purely to gain an indefinitely portable payment.

If the customer was previously paid under an agreement and transferred to the same payment under autonomous assessment rules, then this is to be assessed as a transfer for 'former resident' purposes. This customer may be able to transfer back to agreement assessment, if travelling to an agreement country and qualifies under the agreement specific rules.

If a customer has been in Australia for less than 2 years since their latest return, then it is possible that they did not actually return to Australia as a resident. In this case, check if the recent period in Australia has been incorrectly recorded on the CRES screen and remove if necessary. The former resident rules do not apply to this customer if they have not returned to Australia for residence.

  • Yes, customer's payment is affected by former resident provisions. Go to Step 8
  • No, customer's payment is not affected by former resident provisions. Go to Step 9

8

Customer's payment is affected by the former resident provisions + Read more ...

The Assessed former resident: field on the Residence Savings (RSS) screen will generally display a 'Y' and an expiry date 2 years after the date of commencement of the customer's current period of Australian residence.

If this isn't assessed automatically, authorised staff (APS5 or 6 in CIS) are able to apply an override to the system generated result by coding the Former resident override: field on the RSS screen. This will be required when the customer was previously paid under an agreement and transferred to autonomous assessment rules.

System will automatically suspend payment upon departure from Australia, reason 'FAR' (former Australian resident) if residence has been recorded on CRES screen correctly.

For how to code the CRES screen, see Coding the Country of Residence (CRES) screen.

If the customer returns to Australia within 13 weeks, payment can be restored from the date of return to Australia. Note: arrears cannot be paid for any part of the customer's absence from Australia unless they are payable under an agreement.

An auto review will cancel the record when the customer has been outside Australia for 13 weeks.

Note: all cases where a customer affected by the former resident provisions may be eligible for payment under an international agreement must be referred to CIS. See Centrelink International Services (CIS) contact details for staff.

Record the decision in a DOC.

Advise the customer of the decision and issue a Pre Departure Interview - Portability Decision (XOB101) letter.

Procedure ends here.

9

Customer's payment is not affected by former resident provisions + Read more ...

The Assessed former resident: field on the Residence Savings (RSS) screen should not display a 'Y'.

If this isn't assessed correctly, authorised staff (APS 5 or 6 in CIS) are able to apply an override to the system generated result by coding the Former resident override: field on the RSS screen.

Continue normal processing of portability absence. See Age Pension customer going overseas or Disability Support Pension (DSP) customer going overseas.
