Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) + Read more ...

Only AIC Smart Centre staff can change the payment destination of AIC records.

3

YA or ABSTUDY + Read more ...

Changes to a Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY customer's payment destination.

Customers who are under 18 and who are dependent on their parents will have their payments sent directly to the parent.

Once the customer has turned 18, the payment must either be paid to an account held by the customer (or parents, if the customer has a joint bank account with them), or a payment nominee arrangement must be made.

A parent who has been receiving their under 18 YA child's payments may have authority to provide new bank account details in the child's own name when the child turns 18.

If the customer is under 18 and dependent and their payment goes to their parent, see Under 18 year old Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY customer wants to stop payment going to their parents. Procedure ends here.

For all other changes to a YA or ABSTUDY customer's payment destination, go to Step 5.

4

Customer separating from their partner + Read more ...

When a customer separates from their partner, ensure that the customer's bank account details are correct and whether they wish to have payments made to the same account.

If a customer has payments made to a joint account, advise customer if money is paid into the joint account, the former partner will have access to their payment.

Note: under no circumstances should the payment destination be changed to an account held jointly with the separated partner. Go to Step 5.

6

Third party request for change of payment destination + Read more ...

Generally, payment destination details can only be changed by the customer or a person with payment authority for that customer. This can include a faxed request from a bank, on their letterhead and signed by the customer.

A parent who has been receiving their under 18 YA child's payments may have authority to provide new bank account details in the child's own name when the child turns 18.

There may, however, be the situation when a partner contacts the agency to advise a change to their joint bank account details. In these cases extra care must be taken before a change is made:

  • The Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) indicator must be Y
  • The identity of the partner must be verified. Name, address, date of birth and Customer Reference Number (CRN) are not sufficient, as an ex-family member may know these details
  • A check of the customer record must be made to ensure marital circumstances are unchanged
  • The partner must be able to provide the details of the previous account, and:
    • the previous account must be a joint account, and
    • the new account must also be a joint account that the customer has legal access to, that is, their name must appear on the joint account
  • The Service Officer must not have any doubts about the 'bona fides' of the third party requesting that the bank account be changed

There are serious consequences when money is paid to the wrong person. If any doubt exists as to the accuracy of the information, the Service Officer must not make the change to the customer record. It is the responsibility of the Service Officer and the agency to check the accuracy of the information prior to its use.

If the employee has any doubts as to the validity of the advice or the 'bona fides' of the third party, the customer must be contacted by telephone or in writing before the change is actioned.

A one-off or temporary third party may request to cease receiving payments on the customer's behalf. Use the Change Payment Destination workflow to action this request.

A correspondence nominee can request a change in the customer's payment destination when:

  • there is no payment nominee arrangement in place, and
  • the new account is in the customer's name or the payment is to be directed to a group institution

The Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf (SS313) form, must be completed if the correspondence nominee is to be appointed as a payment‚ nominee.

If a payment nominee requests a change in the customer's payment destination, see Step 7 in Table 2.

7

Check customer's identity + Read more ...

Due to the fraud risks associated with changing payment destination, additional authentication checks must be conducted, even if the customer has authenticated using their Voice Print.

All customers must be asked to confirm their current payment destination details (prior to requested change). This can be either BSB code and account number or just simply branch name and account number.

If the customer is not voice authenticated, or the voice authenticated customer cannot confirm their current payment destination, ensure extra precaution is taken, and the customer can answer questions that are not common knowledge.

Examples of additional questions:

  • the approximate amount of their last payment to that account (other additional identity documentation can be checked when the customer turns 18 and wants to change payment into their own bank account)
  • if they are a JobSeeker Payment customer, whether they had casual earnings on their last report:
    • ask questions regarding the employer's name, and
    • the amount of wages declared (approximate figures are fine)
  • the middle name of their child (if applicable) and the date of birth

Note: if the current payment destination is a rehabilitation centre, correctional centre trust account or respite nursing home, the customer may not know the account details of the organisation. In these cases have the customer state their previous payment destination details and the amount of their last payment delivered to that account (this does not include by group schedule).

If the customer cannot provide these details or the details are incorrect, other identification or authentication questions are required before updating the account destination. See, Authenticating a customer.

If the identity of the customer is proven beyond doubt, proceed to update payment destination. See Step 1 in Table 2.

If there are any concerns about the identity of the customer:

Offer to call the customer back on the phone number on the system, or

In cases where it may be difficult to offer a callback option, refer the customer to their service centre to further establish their identity. Advise the customer to bring identifying documents and verification of new account details to the service centre.

Record all details on a DOC. Procedure ends here.

Ask to sight additional Identity documentation that they may have with them. Ask to see verification of the new account details, for example, a passbook or letter from the bank.

8

Third party + Read more ...

Is the change of payment destination to be redirected to a third party?

  • Yes, there are 2 situations in which a third party can receive the customer's payment. These are an 'appointed payment nominee' and a 'group schedule to a nursing home'.
    • If the customer has provided a completed Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf (SS313) form, see Appointing a nominee. Procedure ends here
    • If the customer has provided a completed Please send my payment to an institution - Group Payment (SS270) form, see Group payment schedules and payments to organisations. Procedure ends here
    • If the customer has not completed either of the above forms, go to Step 9
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

9

Payment details provided for third party but no nominee arrangement + Read more ...

If there is not a payment nominee arrangement in place, the payment destination should only be changed to an account the customer has legal access to.

If the customer cannot provide account details or nominee/group payment form, payment should be suspended.

If the payment type cannot be suspended, it is to be cancelled.

Send a manual letter to the customer to advise of suspension/cancellation and the need to provide account details/nominee form.

See Suspension of payments (CLK) or Cancellation of payments (CLK).

Procedure ends here.

3

Direct credit + Read more ...

  • Check that the account is one to which the customer has legal access, that is, in their own name, or their name appears on a joint account
  • Customers who bank with credit unions must provide their account number, not their member number, or their payments will reject. This applies even where the customer has historically provided their member number (to Centrelink or any other third party)
  • If the customer has more than one payment, check if other accounts need changing (including Income Management General Payment File (GPY) payment)
  • Both the account number and the financial institution (FI) code / BSB number are required. If FI code is unknown, access the CODES facility in the computer system
    Note: Westpac account numbers require a hyphen between the second and third digits (i.e 12-3456) or the system will not accept it as a valid account
  • Account numbers with leading zeroes (i.e. 00012345) must be recorded exactly as described. Failure to include all leading zeroes can result in funds directed to incorrect recipients.
  • Centrelink payments should not be made into an account where the customer is having NDIS payments made. NDIS deposits made into this account are exempt from the income and assets test, including deeming.

To change the payment destination in Customer First, use the Change in Payment Destination workflow, (Customer First>workspace>Payments and services> Change Payment Destination). If the workflow is unavailable, go to Step 4 for manual processing instructions.

Run the Change Payment Destination workflow:

  • Caller Contact screen - update contact details
  • Additional Checks screen - the customer will be prompted to confirm previous payment destination details
  • Update Payment Destination screen - select payment types that require updating and enter new details. Note: for CCS payments select the FAO Service Reason
  • Finalise the activity
  • If the customer is advising a change to their nominated payment destination for a started family assistance claim, ignore the warning I031ZZ - Task already started by another activity for the client. This will not inhibit the update. Once the update is completed, a DOC will be recorded. Processing Services staff will refer to this DOC when processing the new claim
  • For income support customers, check if the existing account details need updating or add any new account details. Access the Savings Summary (SVS) screen to see if the account numbers or balances need updating. See Coding Income and Assets for Centrelink payments and services
  • For Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) customers, only those who are grandfathered for Energy Supplement will receive the quarterly supplement from 20 March 2017 onwards
  • Finalise any further updates via the Assessment Results (AR) screen and record the details on a DOC

Note: at times the Payment Summary (PS) screen will not update after the payment destination on the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen has been changed.

  • If this occurs:
    • code a refresh activity (RSH) on the Benefit Action (BA) screen
    • finalise and complete the activity
  • If that is not successful:
    • code a full stop after the customer's name in the title of the Payment Destination (PA) screen
    • finalise the activity
    • go back into the PA screen to remove the full stop after the customer's name in the title field
    • finalise and complete the activity
  • If the PS screen has still not updated, contact a Service Support Officer (SSO), Technical Peer Support (TPS) or Access Support Centre (ASC) for further investigation

Is the customer receiving CCS?

  • Yes, access the customer's record in Process Direct. Go to the Payment Destination Details table by entering PASB in the Super Key field and review payment details for Service Reason FAO
    • If FAO destination details align with Customer First, procedure ends here
    • If FAO destination details do not exist or differ from those recorded in Customer First, complete a SAP Refresh []. If details are still not correct, manually update payment details in Process Direct. Go to Step 8
  • No, procedure ends here.

5

Direct credit exemption reasons + Read more ...

If a direct credit exemption has been coded, no payment will be issued to the customer until the payment destination details have been supplied. Services Australia no longer issues regular payments by cheque. Cheque will only display while the direct credit exemption is in place.

The payment will show on the Payment Summary (PS) screen but will not be issued until:

  • direct credit details are coded and then all outstanding payments will need to be issued to the customer, or
  • if the customer is in severe financial hardship, other payment methods will need to be considered

Payment by EBT card may be considered but EBT cards can only be issued in denominations of either $20 or $50. The maximum amount that should be issued is $200.

Note: in Process Direct, the direct credit exemptions can be viewed on the Payment Destination Details (PAS) screen. Changes can only be made in Customer First using either the Change Payment Destination workflow or the manual update process.

Procedure ends here.

6

Group Payment Schedule + Read more ...

Customer currently being paid by a Group Payment Schedule:

  • Where the customer's payments are currently being paid by a Group Payment Schedule, there is no need to complete a Please send my payment to an institution - Group Payment (SS270) form to cease the arrangement. The existing arrangement may be ceased by a Group Payment Administrator or financial institution representative
  • Use the Change Payment Destination workflow to update details
  • On PAPN screen:
    • code new payment destination
    • delete Group payment code
  • Check PS screen to ensure payment destination has occurred
  • If change of payment destination is from a date in the past and payments have been issued to group org that the organisation is not entitled to:
    • check that the organisation has not issued the payments to the customer
    • advise org payments will be recovered from their next schedule
    • code Direct Debit Adjustment (DNA) screen to recover any overpaid funds to the org, see Reissuing a group payment paid to an organisation in error
    • reissue payments to customers new account
  • The customer may cease the voluntary arrangement at any time and recommence receiving the payment in their own right. If the customer requests that another approved institution be paid, an SS270 form will need to be completed by the customer and the new institution. If the customer does not already have one, the SS270 form should be issued and the customer advised that the change will not take place until after the form has been returned. For more information see Group payment schedules and payments to organisations
  • If the customer requests another person or organisation to be paid as a Payment Nominee, see Appointing Nominees

Procedure ends here.

7

Payment nominee currently receives payment + Read more ...

Payment nominees can request to change the customer's payment destination to an account that is nominated and maintained by the nominee. If the nominee no longer wishes, or is unable to meet their obligations, the nominee arrangement must be cancelled

If the customer requests to have their payment destination changed to an account in their name and their nominee arrangement is:

  • Voluntary, the nominee arrangement must be cancelled before any updates are made to the payment destination. Note: customers can request cancellation of a voluntary nominee arrangement verbally (the Change Payment Destination workflow incorrectly states that the request must be in writing. Generally cancellation requests from the nominee must be in writing)
  • Involuntary, for example, Court Appointed, Guardianship Board, Public Trustee, no update is to be made and the payment nominee must be contacted

When ending a Payment Nominee relationship and no new payment destination details have been supplied, contact the customer by phone to obtain new details.

  • Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer
  • If the customer cannot be contacted, manually suspend the payment to prevent the payment from being sent to the former nominee's bank account
  • Refer to Cancelling a nominee arrangement for details on the suspension action required

On receipt of the new direct credit payment details, use the Change Payment Destination workflow to update details.

If the customer's payment has been suspended:

  • Restore payment with correct arrears. Create a DOC and ensure that it meets Online Document Recording (ODR) standards for significant decisions
  • If required, reissue to the customer any payments that were made to former nominee after nominee arrangement ended. See Requesting the return of a direct credit, New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) payment made to an incorrect recipient for more information

If a new payment nominee is to be appointed, see Appointing Nominees.

To change the account details - Organisation Payment Nominees

If the payment nominee is an organisation, updates to payment destinations are limited to specific staff via the ICT Security Portal. Cancelling organisation nominee arrangements is not limited to specific staff. If unable to make the required updates, re-categorise SS313/SS270 work item in Workload Management to Group payment work item. See Changing personal Details of nominees.

Note: some payment nominee organisations operate and maintain trust accounts that are in the customer's name.

To change the account details - Individual Nominees

Using the workflow:

Run the Change Payment Destination workflow to update the payment destination:

  • Caller Contact screen - update contact details
  • Additional Checks screen - the nominee will be prompted to confirm previous payment destination details
  • Update Payment Destination screen - select payment types that require updating and enter new details
  • Finalise the activity. Record the details on a DOC

Manual coding (where the workflow is not available):

  • In the customer's record, go to the Nominee Link Summary (NOLS) screen and select the Nominee relationship to be viewed. The Nominee Task Selector (NOTS) screen will be displayed
  • Select the Nominee Destination Summary (NODS) screen and 's'elect the appropriate payment destination to update (a nominee may have multiple payment destinations if they are nominee for multiple customers)
  • On the Nominee Destination Details (NODD) screen over type the relevant fields to be updated. In the Action: field code a 'C' for Change

For more information, see Changing personal details of nominees.

  • No, procedure ends here

9

Farm Household Allowance, Status Resolution Support Service and Child Care Subsidy + Read more ...

Current payment destination details can be viewed in Customer First by navigating to Customer Details screen (CRN/BP)>Bank Details Edit tab.

Updating Payment destination

Check that the account is one to which the customer has legal access, that is, in their own name, or their name appears on a joint account.

Payment destination updates for the SAP system, are completed via Process Direct:

  • Open Process Direct
  • Select Customer Summary tile
  • Enter the customer CRN in CRN field and press [Enter]
  • Select the record from Search Results
  • Enter 'PASB' (Payment Destination Details) in the Super Key field
  • To update an existing Service Reason, select the pencil icon to change the existing bank details
  • To create a payment destination for a new Service Reason, select the Add New Row icon
  • Enter the new bank details on the 'Change Payment Destination Details' or 'Create Payment Destination Details' as applicable
    Note: Westpac account numbers require a hyphen between the second and third digits (i.e 12-3456) or the system will not accept it as a valid account
  • Select Save
  • Select Assess to confirm changes
  • Select Finish to finalise
  • Add any notes if required
  • Select Finalise

You will be presented with a confirmation box of any changes, select OK.

Note: the payment destination will be applied from the date the change is completed. Where a payment destination has been updated as part of a new claim, the date of event will need to be changed to the grant date on the Bank Details Edit tab.

Alternatively, where Process Direct is unavailable, the payment destination can be updated manually via the Customer Details screen in Customer First:

  • Note the details of the account to be changes as Bank Details Edit does not advise which bank account is for which payment type
  • Navigate to Customer Details screen
  • Select Bank Details Edit tab
  • Select Edit List and update the required fields:
    • Bank Key (BSB)
    • Bank Account (number)
    • Account Name (name the account is held)
    • Valid from (date the change is made or date of grant if updated for a new claim)
    • Save

  • No, procedure ends here
