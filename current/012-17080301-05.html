ROCS overview

Category

Description

Eligibility

(members)

Must meet one of the eligibility criteria in 34ZB (2) of the Medical Indemnity Act 2002

Eligibility

(Applicant)

All participating MIIs and MDOs are eligible

Notification Date

Incident/Claim must be notified on or after 1 July 2004

Criteria
  • Member must be eligible on the date the claim is made or notified
  • the incident, or series of incidents, must have occurred in the course of, or in connection with, the person’s practice of their medical profession
  • the member must have a ROCS contract in place or indemnity from an MDO which covers the claim in question
  • the applicant (insurer) must have made a payment, or be liable to pay a payment in relation to the claim
  • the payment must be made in line with the insurer’s indemnity policy issued and within the ordinary course of business
  • the applicant must not have been notified of the occurrence of the incident, nor claim against or by the person in relation to the incident before 1 July 2004.

What is not payable?

The applicant cannot claim an amount if:

  • another party has responsibility to pay it
  • it is an insurer-to-insurer payment
  • the cost wasn’t incurred in relation to the incident
  • the judgment or settlement amounts are still within the appeal period or have been appealed or stayed
  • it’s a GST component of a defence cost that is not exempt – see ‘Medical Indemnity and GST’

Clarification

The ROCS contract or MDO indemnity is based upon the last arrangement in place with the MII/MDO. This means, if the member had obtained cover for incidents that occur within the course of treating a public patient in a public hospital, and the employer does NOT indemnify the member for this, incidents that occur within the course of treating public patients in public hospitals are allowable claims under ROCS.

Similarly, if the last cover in place covers incidents that occur outside of Australia and her external territories, this is also an allowable claim.
