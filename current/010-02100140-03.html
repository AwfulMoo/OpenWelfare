Identifying if compassionate travel is applicable

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Variations to school vacation travel + Read more ...

This process is for compassionate travel, for information on ABSTUDY FAA eligibility, Service Officers must refer to:

  • Fares Allowance (FAA) for ABSTUDY
  • Assessing eligibility for Fares Allowance (FAA) for ABSTUDY

Does the student have school vacation travel booked within 2 weeks of the requested compassionate travel date?

  • Yes, the request should be treated as a variation to the original school vacation travel. See Fares Allowance (FAA) authorised in advance of travel for ABSTUDY
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Eligibility + Read more ...

To be eligible for FAA for compassionate travel the student must:

  • be full-time or qualify for a study load concession,
  • have approval for FAA and not travelling for an Away From Base activity
  • be an excluded or expelled secondary school student
  • meet one of the circumstances justifying travel for compassionate reasons

Note: if a student has received FAA for a dependent (or dependents) to travel with them to the study location, the dependent is also eligible for compassionate travel where it is necessary for them to return home with the student.

Does the student meet the eligibility criteria for FAA for compassionate travel?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, see Step 9 in Table 2

3

Excluded or expelled students + Read more ...

Is the student applying for compassionate travel due to being an expelled or excluded secondary school student?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Does the reason given by the student meet one of the circumstances justifying compassionate travel? (not including expelled or excluded students) + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, see Step 9 in Table 2

5

Compassionate travel is limited to 2 return trips per student per year + Read more ...

Trips for excluded students are not included in the limit of return trips for compassionate travel.

Note: under exceptional circumstances, compassionate travel beyond these limits may be approved. Escalate to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk via Local Peer Support (LPS). LPS will escalate to ABSTUDY Away from Base and Travel Service Support Officer (SSO) who will register with Level 2 Helpdesk.

Have the 2 trips already been claimed?

  • Yes,
    • if response received from Level 2 Policy Helpdesk provides approval to pay ABSTUDY Additional Assistance, go to Step1 in Table 2
    • all other circumstances go to Step 9 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

6

Education institutions applying for travel for an excluded student + Read more ...

Education institutions applying for travel for an excluded student must complete the ABSTUDY expulsion checklist. See Resources for the checklist.

Compassionate travel for an excluded ABSTUDY student to return to their permanent home can be granted if the school confirms all the following:

  • The ABSTUDY student is considered to be at harm to themselves or others as defined in the respective state/territory requirements
  • The school has followed necessary steps outlined in the state/territory legislative requirements, including contacting parent(s)/guardian(s) and the respective authorities, like the Police, Department of Child Safety/Children Services
  • The school has discussed alternative local solutions with the parent(s)/guardian(s), like relocating the student to family in the local area who can accommodate and enrol the student at another school
  • No other source of funding is available, including parent(s)/guardian(s) or the school, and
  • The school confirms these steps have been taken

Note: excluded students required to travel home under supervision, may have a supervisor travel with them. This can be a parent/guardian or a representative from the educational institution. People taking this type of travel will be approved as supervisor travellers under non-student travellers.

Does the completed ABSTUDY expulsion checklist confirm all the above criteria have been met?

  • Yes, see Step 8 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 9 in Table 2

Evidence needed to support a compassionate travel claim

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Reason for compassionate travel + Read more ...

Is the compassionate travel to attend a funeral?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Compassionate travel to attend a funeral + Read more ...

In some Indigenous communities, mentioning the name of a deceased person may cause distress and/or offence.

Attempt to verify the funeral date using the Indigenous Funeral Notices homepage. Resources has a link to the page. If unavailable, try to contact the nearest service centre and liaise with the local community or Indigenous Service Officer (ISO) to confirm the funeral details.

Note: Students travelling for this reason are still subject to mandatory supervision requirements. A family member may travel as a supervisor if they are escorting the student to the travel location.

Go to Step 4.

3

Student is ill or injured + Read more ...

If compassionate travel is for a student, go to step 4

FAA travel may be approved for someone other than the student when the student is ill or injured. This can be to visit the student or to accompany them to specialist appointments when it is compulsory for the parent/guardian to attend the appointment.

To approve this travel the student must:

  • be ill or injured and it has been deemed appropriate for a guardian, family or community member to visit at the term address
  • provide a medical certificate advising the nature of the illness and if the student is able to travel. Further medical certificates will not be required for travel within the period covered by the medical certificate

Where the student is suffering from a terminal illness, both parents/guardians may be approved for FAA.

Once the medical certificate for the student has been provided, got Step 8

4

Inform student they must provide evidence + Read more ...

Inform the student they must provide documentary evidence to support their compassionate travel claim.

Evidence may be a statement from:

  • a school
  • a doctor
  • the hospital
  • a community authority, or
  • a community person responsible for cultural matters (if the travel request is for the student being required to return home for a cultural activity)

These documents are required to grant the FAA. If the evidence:

  • cannot be provided prior to travel, go to step 5
  • has been provided or verified, go to step 6

5

In some circumstances evidence will not be available until after the student's travel + Read more ...

If the Service Officer thinks the basis for the compassionate travel request is genuine and providing evidence would delay the student returning home, documentary evidence can be provided after the travel has occurred.

Note: ensure cultural sensitivity is taken into account with the decision. Refer to an Indigenous Specialist Officer (ISO) for assistance.

Can documentary evidence be provided after the journey?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 9

6

FAA approved + Read more ...

If FAA is to be approved, inform the student that if evidence is not provided (or is not sufficient), an overpayment will be raised to recover the FAA.

If required:

  • issue a Q999 requesting evidence to support their claim. See the Resources page for template. Allow extra time for mail delivery
  • create a DOC with advised details, use Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > Fares Allowance > Evidence for Compassionate Travel > Confirm
  • place the open Fast Note on hold for 21 days

Is the student travelling between their usual study location and permanent home address?

Yes, go to Step 8

No, go to Step 7

7

Check for approved alternate destination + Read more ...

Advise the student compassionate travel is usually approved only between the permanent home and the study location.

Does the student have exceptional circumstances where travel can be approved to a location other than the permanent home address or usual study address? See Determining the appropriate travel destination for ABSTUDY Fares Allowance (FAA).

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

FAA payable + Read more ...

FAA for ABSTUDY can be approved for a student or a non-student traveller that is travelling to the student because of illness or injury.

Determine if the FAA is to be:

  • approved for someone other than the student, see Non-student travellers for ABSTUDY. Procedure ends here
  • authorised and booked before travel, or
  • costs reimbursed after travel

If FAA is approved for an excluded secondary school boarding student, send an ABSTUDY action request Fast Note to the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Team to reassess the student's ABSTUDY rate and include the date the student discontinued. Select Auto text > Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > Update > ABSTUDY Action request > Confirm.

Note: Travel Service Officers are required to cancel any future travel that is booked or requested for an excluded secondary boarding student that is not required.

See Coding pre-booked Fares Allowance (FAA) entitlements for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES).

Procedure ends here.

9

FAA is not payable + Read more ...

Advise the education institution, parent or student they are not eligible for FAA for compassionate travel.

If the travel request was received by ABSTUDY Smart Centre telephony, record details of the discussion on a DOC. Explain their review and appeal rights. See Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process.

If the travel request was received by the ABSTUDY Travel Team, finalise the travel request in the Travel Management Application (TMA) with a Booking status of 'Rejected' and record details of the decision in the Notes tab. See Coding pre-booked Fares Allowance (FAA) entitlements for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES).

If the student is an excluded secondary school boarding student, send an ABSTUDY action request Fast Note to the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Team to reassess the student's ABSTUDY rate and include the date the student discontinued. Select Auto text > Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > Update > ABSTUDY Action request > Confirm.

Check if the student's circumstances entitle them to FAA under other criteria.

See Fares Allowance (FAA) for ABSTUDY.
