Assess COVID-19 General Practice Incentive payment eligibility

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Log into system + Read more ...

Log on to PIP Online

2

Check COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive payment amount + Read more ...

Select the COVID-19 Vaccine General Practice Incentive from the main menu.

The COVID-19 Vaccine General Practice Incentive screen displays.

Is the practice asking if a payment was generated for an individual patient?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 7

3

Download COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive patient list + Read more ...

  • Under Patient List, select View for the relevant calculation quarter:
    • The Downloads pop up displays
  • Select the downloaded file matching the Practice ID

In this download:

  • practices can only see:
    • paid COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive payments for individual patients
  • Service Officers will see:
    • paid, held and cancelled COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive payments. Payment Date populates with a hyphen ‘-‘

Note:

  • do not look at the patient list of another practice to confirm the payment recipient
  • Program Management cannot investigate services provided to individuals to determine eligibility or timing of payments

4

Check for patient in current payment period + Read more ...

Ask the practice which patient/s they want to confirm payment for.

Is the patient on the list?

  • Yes, confirm with the practice that they received a payment for the patient/s. Procedure ends here
  • No, check which payment quarter the second assessment service was claimed. If the date is:
    • within another payment period, go to Step 5
    • not within another payment period, check that both assessment services were billed correctly. Go to Step 6

Note:

  • do not look at the patient list of another practice to confirm the payment recipient
  • Program Management cannot investigate services provided to individuals to determine eligibility or timing of payments

5

Check for patient in different payment period + Read more ...

Download the patient list for the relevant payment quarter and check for the patient.

Is the patient on the list?

  • Yes, confirm with the practice that they received a payment for the patient/s. Procedure ends here
  • No, check that both assessment services were billed correctly. Go to Step 6

Note:

  • do not look at the patient list of another practice to confirm the payment recipient
  • Program Management cannot investigate services provided to individuals to determine eligibility or timing of payments

6

Check first and second-dose assessment services were billed correctly + Read more ...

See the Scenarios where a practice may get a lower/later COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive payment table on the Resources page for the below scenarios.

Ask the practice what MBS item numbers they have billed.

Cross check these with the top 4 rows of MBS item numbers listed (these trigger the payment) in Table 3 on the Resources page of Temporary Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) items in response to Coronavirus (COVID-19).

Did the practice correctly bill at least one first-dose and one second-dose assessment service for the patient?

Yes, and the practice:

  • billed both assessment services correctly before the relevant point-in-time, check the practice details, go to Step 7
  • gave multiple first-dose and second-dose assessment services, see Scenario E on the Resources page
  • gave first-dose and second-dose assessment services but had an error billing one/both items, see Scenario F on the Resources page
  • gave first-dose and second-dose assessment services but delayed billing, see Scenario G on the Resources page
  • gave first-dose and second-dose assessment services but another practice was first to bill the first and second-dose assessment services for the same patient, see Scenario H on the Resources page

No, and the practice:

  • only gave one first-dose assessment service or only one second-dose assessment service, see Scenario B on the Resources page
  • gave multiple first-dose assessment services, and no second-dose assessment services, see Scenario C on the Resources page
  • gave multiple second-dose assessment services, and no first-dose assessment services, see Scenario D on the Resources page

Note: do not look at the patient list of another practice to confirm the payment recipient.

7

Check the PIP approval date of the practice + Read more ...

Ask the practice to confirm the date they started billing COVID-19 assessment services.

Was this before the date they were approved for PIP?

  • Yes, tell the practice they will only be paid incentive payments for assessment services provided from the date they were approved for PIP. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Check the GPs provider numbers + Read more ...

Ask the practice to confirm the provider numbers for the GPs who billed the services.

Are all of the GPs registered in PIP with the correct provider number?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, tell the practice:
    • they will not receive a payment for services provided by a GP that was not registered with the correct provider number in PIP at the date of the first and/or second-dose assessment service. See the Scenarios where a practice may get a lower/later COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive payment table on the Resources page for payment scenarios
    • the GP must submit a 'Practice Incentives individual general practitioner, nurse practitioner or health professional details form (IP003)' with a GP start date before the items were billed at least 7 days before the point-in-time date
    • as long as another practice has not billed the first and second-dose assessment service first, the services billed in the historical reference period will be paid with the next quarterly COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive payment

9

Check associated GPs were current at date of service + Read more ...

Select Associated GPs from the main menu.

Ask the practice to confirm all current practitioners working at the practice.

Are any of the GPs showing as 'non-current', or not listed as a 'current GP' on the Associated GPs screen?

  • Yes, tell the practice:
    • they will not receive a payment for services provided by a GP that was not registered in PIP at the date of the first and/or second-dose assessment service. See the Scenarios where a practice may get a lower/later COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive payment table on the Resources page for payment scenarios
    • the GP must submit a 'Practice Incentives individual general practitioner, nurse practitioner or health professional details form (IP003)' at least 7 days before the point-in-time date
    • as long as another practice has not billed the first and second-dose assessment service first, the services billed in the historical reference period will be paid with the next quarterly COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive payment
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Check amalgamation/change of ownership + Read more ...

Check if an amalgamation or change of ownership has commenced or been finalised in PIP Online since the assessment services started.

Is an amalgamation or change of ownership in progress or finalised?

  • Yes, tell the practice that incentive payments will be paid to the registered practice owner in PIP at the point-in-time date for the quarter that the second-dose assessment service was provided
  • No, tell the practice that a payment was not made for the patient/s as the payment requirements were not met. See the Scenarios where a practice may get a lower/later COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive payment table on the Resources page for scenarios where payment is not made

Note: Program Management cannot investigate services provided to individuals to determine the eligibility or timing of payments.

See also:

  • Change of practice ownership for PIP and WIP – Practice Stream
  • Practice amalgamation for PIP and WIP - Practice Stream
  • Practice location amendments for PIP and WIP - Practice Stream

Send a Paid Patient List to a practice

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Log into system + Read more ...

The PIP Online system will not allow practices to view their Paid Patient List where a payment has been cancelled. Service Officers are able to provide this list to practices via the HPOS Mail Centre.

Note: the Paid Patient List can only be sent via the HPOS mail centre. At least one owner or authorised contact must have an RA number registered in PIP. Do not post or email the Paid Patient List to the practice.

  • Log on to PIP Online

2

Download COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive patient list + Read more ...

  • Select the COVID-19 Vaccine General Practice Incentive from the main menu
  • Under Paid Patient List, select View for the relevant calculation payment quarter:
    • The Paid Patient List will download to the Service Officers ‘Downloads’ folder

Note: the downloaded Paid Patient List includes the Practice ID in the document title. Ensure the document has the correct Practice ID in the title when attaching to the message.

3

Confirm authorised contact has a Registration Authority (RA) number + Read more ...

  • Select Practice Staff System Access from the main menu

Does the authorised contact have an RA number registered in PIP?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, advise the authorised contact that the Paid Patient List will be sent via Health Professional Online Services (HPOS) to a staff member with registered system access.

Note: the Paid Patient List can only be sent via the HPOS mail centre. At least one owner or authorised contact must have an RA number registered in PIP.

4

Send the Paid Patient List to the authorised contact + Read more ...

  • Open the HPOS Mail Centre
  • Select PIP from the menu options
  • Select compose new mail
  • Select the Provider Stem/RA radio button
  • Enter the RA number for the authorised contact from the Practice Staff System Access section of the PIP Online system
    • The authorised contact’s name will populate
  • Enter subject: ‘PIP COVID-19 Vaccine General Practice Incentive Patient list for <Practice name> for the <month> payment quarter’
  • Select choose file and upload the Paid Patient List from the Downloads folder
    • Ensure the Practice ID in the document title is correct
  • Send the mail to the authorised contact

5

Delete the Paid Patient List + Read more ...

In accordance with Services Australia’s privacy policy, Service Officers must delete any copies of the Paid Patient List from their computer.

To delete, open the Downloads folder and delete the item.
