Decision to suspend or cancel a customer's SRSS payment made by the Department of Home Affairs

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Department of Home Affairs determines entitlement to SRSS payment + Read more ...

The Department of Home Affairs:

  • may determine the customer is no longer eligible for the SRSS payment, or
  • their payments need to be suspended while their eligibility is determined
  • sends Services Australia suspension/cancellation via the datalink which is automatically applied to the customers record
  • provides a Stop/Suspension Modification listing

Staff must check the suspension/cancellation has been applied correctly and complete a DOC on the customer's record.

To check if the suspension/cancellation has been applied correctly:

  • for suspension, go to Step 2
  • for cancellation, go to Step 3

2

Department of Home Affairs has requested suspension of the customer's SRSS payment + Read more ...

The Department of Home Affairs has advised via the datalink and the Stop/Suspension Modification listing:

  • the reason why the customer's SRSS payment has been suspended
  • the date the suspension has been applied

In the SRSS customer's record in Customer First:

  • navigate to the customers most recent approved Social Service Plan
    • check the date of effect
  • check the suspension reason is displaying as notified by the Department of Home Affairs
  • record details on a DOC using the SRSS SUS Fast Note

Note: if the suspension has not been applied, or the wrong date and/or reason has been applied, escalate to the SRSS Helpdesk.

Procedure ends here.

3

Department of Home Affairs has requested cancellation of the customer's SRSS payment + Read more ...

The Department of Home Affairs has advised via the datalink and the Stop/Suspension Modification listing:

  • the reason why the customer's SRSS payment has been cancelled
  • the date the cancellation has been applied

In the SRSS customer's record in Customer First:

  • navigate to the customers most recent approved Social Service Plan
    • check the benefit status is displaying the correct cancellation reason
    • check the date of effect
  • end the DOA DOC 'Customer is an SRSS'
  • check the primary indicator on the partner who remains on SRSS and update the indicator to Yes if required
  • record the details on a new DOC using the SRSS CAN Fast Note

Procedure ends here.

Decision to suspend, cancel or restore customer's SRSS payment made by Services Australia

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Is the payment to be suspended, cancelled or restored + Read more ...

Decision made by Services Australia, to:

  • suspend, go to Step 2
  • restore, go to Step 3
  • cancel, go to Step 5

2

Customer's SRSS payment to be suspended + Read more ...

In the SRSS customer's record in Customer First:

  • navigate to the customers most recent approved Social Service Plan
    • run the Change Processes guided procedure, selecting 'SSP Suspension' with the correct date of effect
    • approve the follow-up Payment Determined Social Service Plan
  • suspension letter will be issued to the customer
  • record details on a DOC using the SRSS SUS Fast Note

Procedure ends here.

3

Determine SRSS Restoration + Read more ...

Service officers must determine if the payment can be restored.

Is it appropriate to restore SRSS payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Restoring SRSS payment + Read more ...

If the customer was suspended due to failure to report, all reporting requirements must be actioned first before the Change Processes guided procedure can be run.

Note: Service officers must update the customer's record before restoring the payment.

In the SRSS customer's record in Customer First:

  • navigate to the customers most recent approved Social Service Plan
  • run the Change Processes guided procedure, select 'SSP Restoration' with the correct date of effect

Is the customer entitled to any arrears generated?

  • Yes:
    • approve the follow-up Payment Determined Social Service Plan
    • a restoration letter will be sent to the customer
    • record details on a DOC using the SRSS Restored Fast Note
  • No:
    • escalate to ICT
    • do not approve the Social Service Plan
    • DOC the record using the SRSS ICT escalation Fast Note
    • once resolved, approve the Social Service Plan

Procedure ends here.

5

Customer is to be manually cancelled off SRSS payment + Read more ...

Services Australia made the decision to cancel the customer's SRSS payment, or the SRSS customer has requested cancellation of SRSS payment.

SRSS payments will usually cancel automatically after 13 weeks of suspension, or a follow up Payment Determined Social Service Plan is generated. Services Officers must check and approve the Social Service Plan.

Where the customer's payment is current zero rate, the Service Officer will need to manually cancel the payment:

  • after 6 fortnights with a date of effect 6 fortnights +1
  • with cancellation reason code E6F (6 consecutive nil income), or
  • E6P (partners earning)

To cancel the payment:

  • run the Change Processes guided procedure, selecting Cancellation with the correct date of effect
  • approve the follow-up payment Determined Social Services Plan
  • record details on a DOC using the SRSS CAN Fast Note
  • Email SRSS Helpdesk who will advise the Department of Home Affairs

Note: if a customer is cancelled part through their Entitlement period, any money they are entitled to will not be paid immediately, but will be paid on their usual Entitlement Period End Date.

Procedure ends here.

Customer contacts about decision made by Services Australia

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Customer contact + Read more ...

If the customer contacts about a:

  • cancellation, go to Step 2
  • suspension, go to Step 3

2

Cancellation actioned by Services Australia + Read more ...

Services Officers are to:

  • view the cancellation code on the Services Reason Summary (SRS) screen or the Allowance/Benefit (XBS) screen by selecting ALL in System. See Resources for a list of cancellation codes
  • check DOC's on the customers record for the cancellation reason
  • tell the customer the cancellation reason
  • DOC the record
  • email the SRSS Helpdesk if there has been multiple contacts, or for vulnerable customers. The SRSS Helpdesk will liaise with the Department of Home Affairs for advice

If the SRSS payment can be restored, go to Step 4.

Otherwise, procedure ends here.

3

Suspension actioned by Services Australia + Read more ...

View the suspension code on the Service Reason Summary (SRS) screen or the Allowance/Benefit history (XBS) screen by selecting ALL in System. See Resources for a list of suspension codes.

If the customer:

  • has any outstanding documents, advise of required information/documentation before payments can be restored
  • not completed their 6 monthly review - complete the review with the customer, or handover to an appropriate SRSS trained officer
  • suspended due to liquid assets and now in hardship. Request evidence of any significant changes and refer to the SRSS Helpdesk
  • suspended for any other reason, check to see if any more information is needed from the customer before payment can be restored

4

SRSS payment can be restored + Read more ...

Customer has supplied required information or taken necessary action.

Service Officers must update the record before restoring and DOC the change(s).

Customer has returned:

  • all outstanding documents/information, and/or
  • the 6 monthly review
    • complete the review activity and any outstanding failures. See Actioning Status Resolution Support Services payment reviews
  • run the Changes Processes guided procedure, select 'SSP Restoration'
  • approve the follow-up Payment Determined Social Service Plan
    • check correct arrears are paid
  • tell the customer of the outcome and their payment details
  • record details on a DOC using the SRSS Restored Fast Note
