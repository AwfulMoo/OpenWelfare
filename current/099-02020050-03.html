Advice of death of a customer received + Read more ...

When a person contacts to advise of the death of a customer, staff must offer:

  • the services of a social worker in the first instance or at any time the notifier becomes or show signs of distress during the contact
  • to issue the pamphlet Needing help after someone has died? (HD001) and if accepted, issue to the person if at a service centre, otherwise refer them to ‘Death of a loved one’ content on the Services Australia website

The following cultural issues need to be respected and considered by Service Officers when being advised of the death of an Indigenous customer:

  • the name of the deceased person cannot be mentioned by immediate family members. This means immediate family members may have to ask other relatives/friends to call on their behalf or attend the interview with the notifier, because they cannot say the name of the deceased
  • in this situation, it may also be offensive to the person for the Service Officer to say the name of the deceased person. Service Officers should sensitively ask the person if it is all right for them to use the deceased customer's name. If the person is not comfortable with this, avoid using the deceased customer's name wherever possible. See Cultural considerations when someone dies

3

Confirm the death of the deceased customer + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure in the deceased customer's record to determine if death can be confirmed.

Confirmation of death can be accepted where the customer's full name is provided together with at least 2 of the following:

  • deceased customer's last address in full
  • date of birth
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • partner's name

Confirmation of death also needs the following details about the informant:

  • name
  • address
  • phone number

See Confirmation of the death of an adult or child.

Confirm if the person notifying of the death is the executor of the estate. If the informant is not the executor, but knows the executor details, obtain:

  • the name and address of the executor, and
  • the name of the funeral director or hospital

Nominee arrangements and Power of Attorney (POA) stop at the time of death. Any correspondence, including the payment summary and possible debt recovery action from a deceased estate, will be sent to the:

  • executor, or
  • administrator of an estate

Where executor details are not known at the time of death notification, the executor details fields in the Notification of death guided procedure must be left blank.

Note: executor details can be recorded manually on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen if details become known later.'

Multiple executors

If the deceased customer has multiple executors:

  • record the details of the executor who first notifies of their executor status on the deceased customer's DEA screen
  • if another executor notifies, create a DOA DOC on the deceased customer's record, stating the person has multiple executors. Enter the details of all of the executors in the DOC, including the executor who notified first
  • proof of authority must be obtained before releasing details to an executor

Note: multiple executors are not the same as disputed executors. If a person has multiple wills, or changed them, there could be disputes about who the executors are.

Can informant supply enough details to confirm death?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No:
    • obtain all relevant details from the informant, including their own name, address, contact number and relationship to the deceased
    • obtain executor details, if known
    • obtain the name of the funeral director or hospital where the deceased passed away, if known
    • in the deceased customer's record, run the Notification of death guided procedure. The guided procedure will not code the death, but will record details on a DOC. This becomes an open work item and will be allocated to a Service Officer with the required skill to complete. If the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item. Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED
    • If multiple notifications are received about the death of the same person do not re-run the guided procedure. Instead, check the record and annotate the original DOC to state details are correct and who advised. Go to Step 4
    • if there is any doubt as to the deceased's identity, contact the funeral director or the hospital where the deceased passed away. If this is not an option and contact with relatives is proposed, social worker involvement may be necessary. Where appropriate, send Request for Verification (SS257) form. Go to Step 4

4

Death not confirmed + Read more ...

Suspend payment until the death is confirmed. The suspension date of event should be the unconfirmed date of death or date paid to plus 1 (DPT + 1), whichever is later:

  • suspend the payment by:
    • selecting the suspension option in the Notification of death guided procedure, or
    • manually coding the suspension for reason 'other' (OTH)
  • create a DOC on the customer's record, making sure it meets Online Document Recording (ODR) minimum standards for significant decisions and including what action is needed to confirm the death
  • do not issue a suspension letter, cancel the advice activity on the Activity List (AL) screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension
  • it is not necessary to code a manual review as there is already an open DOC/work item

Note: it is imperative the death is confirmed and actioned as a matter of urgency. For Service Officers confirming the death, contact the funeral director or the hospital where the deceased passed away. If this is not an option and contact with relatives is proposed, social worker involvement may be necessary.

When the death is confirmed, go to Step 5.

5

Check for started activities + Read more ...

A death action cannot be processed with activities on the AL screen. The Notification of death guided procedure will provide a prompt if there is an activity on the AL screen.

Note: if a deceased customer's record was suspended while the death was being confirmed and their death has now been confirmed, check for started activities on the AL screen manually.

Where possible make sure any activities are completed and run the daily driver (DDR).

If there:

  • is a STA debt activity on the AL screen, create an open work item advising of the death and requesting the debt activity be completed. The work item will automatically map to the relevant area. Use Fast Note – select Auto text, use Debts > DMB Raising > Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request. Note: held debt activities on the partner’s record will not prevent the death action being processed
  • is RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM activity on the AL screen, add the keyword RCAPRI. The RCA team will action the activity and complete the death action
  • are any other outstanding activities on the customer’s or partner’s record, (for example, claims, reassessments or open DOCs) process them. Service Officers who do not have the skills to process the outstanding activities should immediately contact the team or Service Officer who needs to complete the outstanding activity. If unsure who to contact to get this activity completed, refer to existing escalation channels

Have all started activities been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

When it is not possible to complete an activity + Read more ...

If there is an outstanding activity that the Service Officer is unable to complete, suspend the deceased customer's payment if not already suspended.

The suspension option is not available in the Notification of death guided procedure if there is an activity preventing the death action:

  • manually suspend using the reason 'OTH' (other) pending completion of the outstanding activity
  • the suspension date of event should be the date of death or date paid to plus 1 (DPT + 1), whichever is later
  • record a DOC on the customer's record, ensuring that it meets Online Document Recording (ODR) minimum standards for significant decisions and including what action must occur so the death can be actioned
  • as the customer's death has been confirmed, do not send a letter. It is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension

If an open DOC/work item about the death notification has not already been created, either:

  • run the Notification of death guided procedure to generate an open work item for the relevant staff to complete the death action once the outstanding activities have been completed, or
  • if the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item. Use Fast Note – select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED
  • when activity is completed, go to Step 7

7

Check if restoration is needed + Read more ...

Is payment suspended?

  • Yes, restore payment if suspended. Ensure no arrears are issued. Inhibit the restoration advice, as it is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 8

Deceased customer was due to report + Read more ...

Are the deceased customer's employment income details for the last reporting period known or can the person notifying of the death provide the customer's employment income details for the last reporting period?

  • Yes:
    • use the Earnings and reporting workflow tool to process the reporting statement. Note: the date of lodgement on the Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen may need to be changed first to the day before the date of death. For more information, see Changing Entitlement Period End Dates (EPED)
      If the deceased person was an allowee, run the Earnings and reporting workflow tool after the death action is finalised. The system will display the reporting period as the Entitlement Period Start Day to the day before the customer died
    • Go to Step 10

Stimulate the payment + Read more ...

Stimulate the payment using the Earnings and reporting workflow tool.

Take the deceased customer off reporting by updating the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen.

11

Process the death action + Read more ...

If the customer was receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) or Child Care Subsidy (CCS), do not unlink children before coding death action. The Notification of death guided procedure will cancel FTB/CCS and allow children to be linked to another person once daily driver has run. Invite new carer to claim FTB for the child/ren. See Death of a family assistance payment or Paid Parental Leave scheme customer.

Death action will cancel the deceased person's payment.

If the guided procedure is not available, manually code the death action.

When recording a death the Notification Handler (NOHL) is incorrectly using the date of notification as the date to increase the survivor's rate to the single rate. The workaround for this is to record the date of receipt (DOR) as the later of either the date of death (DOD) or date paid to + 1 (DPT + 1).

Note: the workaround is auto-applied if the Notification of death guided procedure is used.

Once the death action has been recorded, go to Step 1 in Table 2.

Surviving partner's bereavement payments

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Switching to the partner's record + Read more ...

If the surviving partner was a joint owner of any assets with their late partner, full ownership of the assets legally transfers to the surviving partner following the death of their partner. However, for social security purposes, if assets are owned in joint names with the deceased partner, the surviving partner is only assessed as owning 50%. The system will continue to asses that the surviving partner owns 50% of the asset until the surviving partner advises of the transfer of assets. Where the transfer of the deceased's assets and/or income to the survivor is likely to affect the survivor's rate, code a manual review for 16 weeks following the date of death.

Check whether assets owned by the deceased or superannuation, compensation or other payments made to the deceased will be transferred to the surviving partner. They should not be assessed until they become available to the surviving partner.

If the surviving partner is granted a reversionary income stream, it is to be assessed from the date that it is first entitled to be received. A lump sum arrears payment received by the reversionary beneficiary, accrued between the death of the primary beneficiary and when the reversionary beneficiary commences to get regular payments, is assessed as income from the income stream.
This means the income is assessed over the period to which it relates and may result in a debt for that period.
Effective from 9 May 2018, where bereavement provisions apply and the assessment of lump sum arrears result in a debt during the bereavement period for the surviving spouse, it can be waived under the legislative instrument 'Social Security (Waiver of Debts - Bereavement Period) Specification 2017' made under subsection 1237AB(1) of the Social Security Act 1991.
Note: any debt that is incurred after the Act's 14 week bereavement period has ended would not be waived under the provisions of this legislative instrument. The legislative instrument is not retrospective. This means that any of these debts that occur before 9 May 2018 cannot be waived under class of debt waiver provisions.

If the deceased had any Working Credits, these credits cannot be transferred to the surviving partner.

If the surviving customer may be entitled to a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP), which is not auto-issued, a BRV activity will appear on the AL screen for a manual calculation to be made.
Note: in some cases, the calculation may result in a zero entitlement.

2

Surviving partner is a homeowner and either an aged care resident or in a care situation + Read more ...

If the surviving partner has a status of RCA/CUR-DSS, they have started aged care. See the RCA Circumstance (RCIRC) or RCA Institution Summary (RIS) screens to confirm that surviving partner is in Care Type: Residential and not Care Type: Home.

Their former home is an exempt asset, if the:

  • surviving partner is either an aged care resident or in a care situation, and
  • customer was residing in the principal home

When the customer at home dies, a 2 year exemption period starts for the partner. Other exemptions may also be applicable for aged care residents. See Vacation of principal home due to illness.

If a member of a couple dies after their partner vacated their home to enter a care situation, the start date of the 2 year exemption period is set from the later of the date:

  • the partner entered a care situation
  • the deceased customer entered a care situation (if applicable)
  • of the customer’s death, if they resided in the principal home

Check the Homeowner Ownership field is a homeowner (for example, codes GFH, NHH or HOM) on the surviving partner's Accommodation (AC) screen.

Is the surviving partner a homeowner and an aged care resident or in a care situation?

  • Yes. To make sure the former home is assessed, create an open work item in the surviving partner’s record, if:
    • partner is RCA/CUR: create a Fast Note, Aged Care > Enquiry > Aged Care Prin Home Rev, for Aged Care (RCA), to review the value of the home and include it in any current means assessment. Go to Step 3
    • partner is in other care situation: create a Fast Note, Assessments and Reviews > Income and assets > Action required – Home vacation, to refer the decision to a Home Vacation Review processing Service Officer. They will determine and update the required vacation of home date, as the partner of a customer in a care situation has passed away. Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

4

Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP) for DVA customers + Read more ...

DVA may be responsible for paying the lump sum bereavement payment.

Transfer the BRV activity to the DVA Clearance Team (SCS) if the deceased partner was in receipt of any of the following payments from DVA:

  • Social Security Age Pension (paid by DVA)
  • Age Service Pension
  • Partner Service Pension
  • Invalidity Service Pension
  • Carer Service Pension
  • Veteran Payment
  • Income Support Supplement (ISS)

Transfer the BRV activity to the DVA Clearance Team (SCS) if both the surviving partner and deceased partner were receiving an income support payment paid by Services Australia and either of them was receiving a Defence Force Income Support Allowance (DFISA).

If the deceased partner was in receipt of a DVA Disability Pension, DVA automatically pays the LBP to the surviving partner.
Note: where the surviving partner is a social security pensioner, they can get a LBP from DVA as well as from Services Australia.

SCS will assess entitlement for the LBP, and calculate the amount.

If SCS is not responsible for finalising the bereavement activity, and the survivor is in receipt of a:

  • pension, go to Step 5
  • allowance, go to Step 6

Where the bereavement activity has been finalised by SCS, see Step 1 in Table 3.

5

Surviving partner gets a pension and is entitled to LBP + Read more ...

Check eligibility to LBP. If eligible, calculate the LBP. Manually code the payment as follows:

  • select the PEN/OTP BLS activity. Only cancel the BLS activity or Manual Follow-up (MFU) if the customer is not entitled to a LBP. In that instance, record details of decision on a DOC
  • on the One Off Payment (OOP) screen, in the:
    • Date of Effect field, key the date
    • Payment Type field, key 'BLS'
    • press [Enter] to view the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • select Optional Actions > Partner/Ex-Partner > Pensions/Allowances > One Off Payments (OOP) from the left hand menu
  • key the LBP amount in the Amount column on the Bereavement Payment line
  • press [Enter] twice to return to the AR screen
  • finalise the activity
  • record LBP calculation on a DOC or annotate Notification of Death DOC
  • issue Q150 to advise of LBP

See Step 1 in Table 3.

6

Surviving partner gets allowance and is entitled to LBP + Read more ...

If the surviving partner gets an allowance and is a long-term social security recipient, they are entitled to LBP:

  • calculate the LBP
  • cancel the PEN/OTP BLS activity because LBP must be paid in the NSS system
  • manually code the payment as follows:
    • in the Nxt field, in the NSS system, key 'OOP'
    • in the Date of Effect field, key the date
    • in the Payment Type field, key 'BLS' and press [Enter] to view the Assessment Results (AR) screen
    • select Optional Actions > Partner/Ex-Partner > Pensions/Allowances > One Off Payments (OOP) from the left hand menu
    • key the amount of LBP amount in the Amount column on the Bereavement Payment line
    • press [Enter] twice to return to the AR screen
    • finalise the activity
    • record LBP calculation on a DOC or annotate the Notification of Death DOC

Is the surviving partner in receipt of JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, issue Q150 to advise of LBP. Go to Step 1 in Table 3

7

Surviving partner gets JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance + Read more ...

An MFU will be generated to check eligibility to Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV).

PBV is paid to a JSP or YA customer if the customer:

  • is receiving JSP or YA when they advise of their partner's death
  • and their deceased partner were Australian residents when the partner died
  • and the deceased person were a couple when their partner died
  • or their correspondence nominee has notified of the partner's death
  • had not re-partnered when they notified of the death
  • notified of the death within the 14 week bereavement period or within the duration of the pregnancy if the pregnancy is more than 14 weeks from partner's death

If the customer is also eligible for a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment, they must relinquish the LBP writing. This includes customers who have already been paid LBP and wish to get PBV instead.

Is the customer entitled to PBV?

  • Yes:
    • in Process Direct, use bereavement calculator to estimate PBV entitlement
    • if PBV estimate is more than LBP, enclose Application for Partner Bereavement Payment (SA490) form with Q150 letter
    • see Assessing entitlement to PBV and Calculating Partner Bereavement Payment
    • issue Q150 to advise of LBP and invite PBV application
  • No:
    • issue Q150 to advise of LBP
    • go to Table 3

Result on surviving partner's payment + Read more ...

If receiving a pension:

  • the rate will be reassessed to the single rate
  • an Income and Asset Update (SA220) form will be issued 16 weeks after the date of death to the surviving partner. This acts as a bereavement review which lets the customer update their income and asset details either online or by returning the completed SA220
  • if the surviving partner was in receipt of Carer Payment (CP) to care for their deceased partner they can continue to get CP until the end of the 14 week bereavement period, at which time payment will cancel. Note: CP (XWP) continues during the bereavement period if the care receiver who died was the only or last CA care receiver. A bereavement period does not apply if CP (XWP) continues, because the customer gets payment level CA for a different care receiver
  • tell the customer they will need to claim another payment before the end of the bereavement period

If receiving JobSeeker Payment, Youth Allowance, Special Benefit (under JSP or YA conditions) or Parenting Payment:

  • the rate will be reassessed to the single rate
  • payments are subject to the lodgement of reporting statements
  • the customer can be given an exemption from mutual obligation or activity test requirements for the 14 week bereavement period. If the customer is pregnant when their partner died, and the expected date of confinement is more than 14 weeks in the future, the participation exemption is extended for the duration of the pregnancy

If receiving Carer Allowance (CA) and an income support payment (other than CP) and their partner was the deceased care receiver, a CA Bereavement Payment may be payable.

If receiving Partner Allowance (PA):

  • the customer is entitled to stay on PA for the remainder of the bereavement period
  • tell the customer they will need to claim another payment before the end of the bereavement period. Note: an auto-advice is issued toward the end of the bereavement period to remind the customer to claim an income support payment

Action to take after death action is completed + Read more ...

After completing the death action:

  • if the customer is on 2 weekly reporting because their partner had employment income Service Profiling will need to be refreshed. Go to the Profile Summary (PQS) screen and code 'Y' in the Reprofile field. Complete activity
  • if the surviving partner is granted a reversionary income stream, assess from the date it is first received
  • if the surviving partner's payment cancels immediately afterwards, as a result of the customer's death, a Q134 letter may need to be sent to the surviving partner advising of the cancellation reason, if no auto-advice is issued
  • record the details of the bereavement on a DOC. Note: the Notification of Death guided procedure should auto-record all of these details
  • offer referral to social worker if appropriate
  • update executor details after the death has been recorded if not coded during the death action process
  • identify any service providers to be advised of the death
  • check AL screen on the deceased customer's record, cancel any RCA Manual Follow-up (MFU) activities for income support payment to be cancelled
