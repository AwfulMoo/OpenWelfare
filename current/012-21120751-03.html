Basic eligibility + Read more ...

When answering enquiries it is important customers know the eligibility requirements. The main requirements are:

  • the claim is submitted in an approved form. An approved form is:
    • an application through the claimant's Medicare online account through myGov
    • the Express Plus Medicare mobile app, or
    • Medicare online claiming or using the COVID-19 Vaccine Claims Scheme (CVCS) Application form (MO062). This is available on the Services Australia website
      Note: the first 2 options are only available where the claimant is the patient or for someone under 14 years of age who is on the claimant’s Medicare card
  • the patient developed a clinical condition or an administrative related harm that has been diagnosed by a relevant medical practitioner. The CVCS medical report form (MO063) must be completed by a medical practitioner who either:
    • made the diagnosis and is qualified to make that diagnosis, or
    • has all the reports from the treating practitioner
  • the patient received a COVID-19 vaccine under the COVID-19 vaccine rollout. Three COVID-19 vaccines were included in the rollout:
    • AstraZeneca Vaxzevria (COVAST)
    • Moderna Spikevax (MODERN)
    • Pfizer Comirnaty (COMIRN)
      Note: if the patient receive a COVID-19 vaccine that is not one of the 3 above the claim will not be eligible
  • the patient incurred out of pocket costs and/or lost earnings and carer costs (both past and future) of at least $1,000

Frequently asked questions and details about conditions covered by the scheme are on the Resources page.

2

Where to find more information + Read more ...

If the person is making general enquiries and wants to find out more about the Scheme, they can:

  • visit the Services Australia website
  • call the Services Australia support line:
    • 1800 653 809 Option1 then option 5
  • visit the Department of Health and Aged Care website

Frequently asked questions and details about conditions covered by the Scheme are on the Resources page.

Staff can offer to cold transfer customers:

  • For general enquiries – Services Australia Workspace option COVID Vaccine Claim
  • For complex or existing claim enquiries – Services Australia Workspace option Medicare Medical Indemnity

Customer wants to make an application + Read more ...

To apply, customers need to submit an approved form. An approved form is:

  • an application through the claimant's Medicare online account through myGov
  • the Express Plus Medicare mobile app, or
  • Medicare online claiming or using the COVID-19 Vaccine Claims Scheme (CVCS) Application form (MO062). This is available on the Services Australia website
    Note: the first 2 options are only available where the claimant is the patient or for someone under 14 years of age who is on the claimant’s Medicare card

Service centre staff can print the forms MO062, MO063 and MO063 (authorisation form) for the customer if requested. The Resources page has links to the forms.

2

Scan and return documents + Read more ...

If a customer attends a Service Centre to lodge their application or support documents:

  • Scan all documents immediately and email the documents internally to the COVID19.VACCINE.CLAIMS.SCHEME mailbox. Make sure the email is sent with an email classification of ‘Official:Sensitive’
  • Return all documents to the applicant or dispose of the documents securely
  • Do not keep or store documents at the service centre

3

Explain next steps + Read more ...

Do not give advice on whether the documents provided are sufficient for the purpose of the scheme. Let the person know that once the assessor receives these documents they will be in contact. This contact is usually in writing.

If the customer has general enquiries about the Scheme, see Table 1.

Customer at risk of suicide or self-harm – refer to Social Worker

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Record information + Read more ...

If the customer threatens self-harm and there are serious concerns for their wellbeing and safety, record the following details:

  • Name (full name if possible)
  • Phone number
  • If appropriate, ask for home address

Advise the following:

  • 'With your consent, I will refer you to a social worker who may be able to assist you'

2

Contact Social Worker + Read more ...

Make contact with a social worker through normal referral protocols.

To transfer a call:

  • select the Services Australia Workspace social work transfer option and choose ‘option 1’ from the IVR list for priority access to a social worker
  • advise the social worker that Services Australia is taking calls/managing enquiries on behalf of the COVID-19 Vaccine Claims Scheme (CVCS). Provide the customer’s:
    • name
    • address
    • phone number and CRN, if they have one
  • provide a summary of the concerns to the social worker and the caller’s contact details
  • transfer the call

If an escalation to police:

  • is required, including when the call is disconnected before transfer to a social worker, go to Step 3
  • was not required, advise a Team Leader or supervisor and go to Step 3

3

Further escalation + Read more ...

If an escalation to police is required, follow usual Service Australia procedures.

Escalate the incident using Services Australia templates and protocols. This includes escalation to the police when required via Security Incident Reporting > Police contact.

For more information on security incident reporting see Customer aggression – Reporting and recording incidents.

Advise a Team Leader or supervisor of the action taken.
