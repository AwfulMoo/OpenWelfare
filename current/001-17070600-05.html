Start date of payments - FAQ

Table 2

Item

Description

1

When do I get paid?

A: There are a number of factors that may affect the start date of payments such as waiting and exclusion periods. The start date of payments are assessed on a case by case basis.

As a general rule job seekers must supply all supporting documentation and meet the qualification and eligibility criteria for payment.

Job seekers subject to RapidConnect requirements

Generally job seekers are payable once they have met their RapidConnect requirement by either:

  • acknowledge your Job Plan online (if referred to the Workforce Australia Online),
  • or attend an appointment (if referred to an Employment Service Provider)

Failure to meet RapidConnect requirements within 28 days of the Participation Interview, the income support claim is rejected or cancelled, (reason code FCJ 'Failed to connect with Employment Providers').

Job seekers not subject to RapidConnect requirements

Generally, when a job seeker is exempt from RapidConnect requirements the start day is the date the claim was submitted with all supporting documentation if they meet the qualification and eligibility for payment.

For more information, see:

  • Calculating the start day general rule
  • RapidConnect

Providers - FAQ

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Can I change my provider?

Job seekers will select or be automatically allocated a provider including Online Employment Services during their initial contact (Participation Interview) with the agency.

When a job seeker registration is completed, eligible job seekers will be referred to Workforce Australia Online (unless already connected to a Workforce Australia Employment Services Provider) to access the assistance available through Workforce Australia. The Department of Employment and Workplace Relations may determine a Workforce Australia provider is more suitable for the job seeker and refer them onto the provider at commencement.

Job seekers who approach Services Australia for any transfer reason should be advised to discuss their situation with their Employment Services Provider. Services Australia has no role in transferring job seekers between providers.

For more information, see Selection or allocation of a Workforce Australia employment service.

2

What happens when I'm referred to Workforce Australia Online for Individuals?

The role of Services Australia in online employment servicing is to explain to the job seeker that they:

  • have been selected for the OES or ESM and are required to register with the Workforce Australia website via myGov
  • must agree to a Job Plan via their Workforce Australia account
  • will not be required to attend face to face appointments with a Workforce Australia provider
  • can contact the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations to discuss:
    • their participation
    • issues accessing the Workforce Australia website
    • agreeing to the Job Plan, or
    • exiting from the OES

For more information, see Department of Employment and Workplace Relations.

3

Why have I been referred to a Community Development Program (CDP) provider?

A: Job seekers who live in remote arears are automatically selected and referred to a Community Development Programme (CDP) provider.

CDP providers deliver a range of services based on the needs of both the community and the individuals in that community.

For eligible job seekers living in one of 60 remote servicing regions, CDP is the only Employment Services Provider type available. These job seekers cannot be referred to:

  • Workforce Australia
  • Workforce Australia - Transition to Work (TTW), or
  • Disability Employment Services (DES)

For more information, see Community Development Program (CDP).

Types of mutual obligation requirements - FAQ

Table 5

Item

Description

1

What are mutual obligation requirements?

Mutual obligation requirements are designed to ensure that unemployed people receiving activity tested income support payments are actively looking for work and are participating in activities that will help them into employment.

When claiming a payment, job seekers are generally required to engage with employment services before receiving payment. Job seekers will then be required to enter into a Job Plan which outlines all of a job seeker's mutual obligation requirements. These requirements will generally include looking for and accepting suitable work, attending appointments with their employment services provider, attending all job interviews and undertaking other suitable activities.

Job seekers' mutual obligation requirements are generally determined by the following factors:

  • age
  • assessed work capacity
  • the primary responsibility for the care of a child.

For more information, see Mutual obligation requirements.

2

How many hours am I required to do each week?

Job seekers with full-time (30+ hours a week) mutual obligation requirements should be:

  • looking for work on a full-time basis, and
  • actively addressing any circumstances that may affect their capacity to undertake paid work

The following job seekers have part-time (15 hours a week) mutual obligation requirements:

  • Principal carer parents, once the youngest child in their care turns 6 years of age
  • Job seekers with a partial capacity to work (PCW) of 15 to 29 hours per week
  • Mature age job seekers, 55 years and over

For more information, see Applying mutual obligation requirements.

Principal carers - FAQ

Table 6

Item

Description

1

What sort of activities can I do, I have school aged children and my time is limited?

Principal carers have Part-time requirements mutual obligation requirements.

'Part-time' refers to the ways in which their requirements vary from other job seekers, and the extra flexibilities they are allowed, in recognition of their family care responsibilities.

This means mutual obligations, appointments and other requirements should be scheduled to occur during school hours only (generally between 9 am and 3 pm during school terms), unless otherwise requested or agreed to by the principal carer. This includes participation in employment services and requirements negotiated by providers.

For more information, see Mutual obligation requirements for principal carers.

2

What do I do in school holidays?

Principal carer parents are not required to meet any requirements for the fortnight during which the Christmas Day public holiday falls. However, they are still required to lodge their report to Services Australia for this period in order to report any earnings and stimulate their payment.

During school term holidays, job seekers are to discuss their individual circumstances with their provider.

For more information, see Mutual obligation requirements for principal carers.

Partial capacity to work - FAQ

Table 7

Item

Description

1

Partial capacity to work (PCW)

Job seekers with a partial capacity to work (PCW) have had an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) completed to determine the number of hours they are able to participate.

The PCW bandwidths and mutual obligation requirements are:

  • 0-14 hours per week
    • These job seekers are not required to look for or undertake work or study. They will satisfy their mutual obligation requirements by attending a compulsory quarterly interview appointment with Services Australia
  • 15-22 hours per week
    • Job seekers will be required to look for work, or undertake work or study of 15-22 hours per week
  • 23-29 hours per week
    • Job seekers will be required to look for work, or undertake work or study of 23-29 hours per week and will be required to accept suitable paid work within this range

For more information, see Identifying people with a partial capacity to work.

1

What do I need to do if I have caring responsibilities?

In some instances Services Australia can grant a short term exemption.

To determine if job seekers are eligible for a caring responsibilities exemption, we require evidence of the short term caring responsibilities and we will ask questions such as:

  • who will you be caring for?
  • what level of care/assistance do you provide?
  • how long is the care arrangement expected to last?

This information helps us to determine if a caring exemption can be granted.

For more information, see Exempting job seekers from their mutual obligation requirements due to caring responsibilities.

Mutual obligation requirement - FAQ

Table 9

Item

Description

1

What are mutual obligation requirements?

Mutual obligation requirements are designed to ensure unemployed people receiving these payments are actively looking for work and are participating in activities that helps them into employment.

For more information, see Applying mutual obligation requirements.

2

Can I do volunteer work?

Generally approved volunteer work is undertaken by mature age job seekers aged 55 and over.

Voluntary work is undertaken in an approved not-for-profit community organisation in a verified voluntary work position.

If an Employment Services Provider or the digital services contact centre determines voluntary work would be beneficial to the job seeker's employment prospects, job seekers can undertake voluntary work to satisfy their annual activity requirement or to contribute towards satisfying their mutual obligation requirements.

Job seekers can find further information about on our Services Australia website.

See Voluntary work as an approved requirement.

3

What do I do if I can't meet one of the requirements in my Job Plan?

Job seekers have a jobseeker account they can link to their MyGov account. Once linked job seekers access their Dashboard and can manage their requirements via their calendar.

Job seekers can add personal events such as attending job interviews, caring responsibilities, medical appointments, school requirements, legal appointment, community service, cultural and religious requirements/obligations.

If job seekers need to speak to their Employment Service Provider they can contact Department of Employment and Workplace Relations (see Employment Services).

More information is available for job seekers on the:

  • How to use the Workforce Australia website (workforceaustralia.gov.au) and
  • Department of Employment and Workplace Relations

4

I'm going to a job interview, what do I need to do?

Job seekers can add a personal event such as at attending job interviews via their Calendar. Job seekers record the details.

More information is available for job seekers on the How to use the Workforce Australia website (workforceaustralia.gov.au).

5

What if I'm sick and can't meet one of my requirements?

Job seekers can add a personal event to their Workforce Australia calendar however they may be instances where job seekers maybe required to provide medical evidence.

Job seekers may be eligible for a temporary medical exemption, to assess a temporary medical exemption, we require medical evidence completed by a treating health care professional.

For more information, see Assessment of a medical certificate to determine exemption from mutual obligation requirements for a temporary incapacity.

6

I'm working why do I have to look for work?

To qualify for a job seeking payment (JSP/YAL JSK) a person must the following eligibility criteria:

  • be of qualifying age
  • meet Australian residence requirements
  • not be in receipt of any other social security benefit, social security pension or a Service Pension, Income Support Payment (ISP) or Veteran Payment
  • not be receiving ABSTUDY Living Allowance
  • be unemployed, or
    • unable to look for work due to a medical condition, illness or injury, or
    • employed or studying full time and are unable to undertake these due to a medical condition, illness or injury and have a job or study to return to
  • be prepared to negotiate and enter into a Job Plan if required, or comply with or renegotiate an existing Job Plan
  • satisfy the requirements of a Job Plan

For more information, see:

  • Eligibility for JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
  • Eligibility for Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker)

1

I'm self employed what are my mutual obligation requirements?

All job seekers can choose to participate in self-employment as an approved requirement. This may be added to a job seeker's Job Plan as the only compulsory requirement, if the job seeker is able to fully meet their mutual obligation requirements by doing enough hours, and declaring enough business income to satisfy the sufficient work test.

Job Plans - FAQ

Table 11

Item

Description

1

What's a Job Plan?

A Job Plan is an agreement you enter into, it includes all the mutual obligation requirements you agree to do in order to receive payments. A Job Plan records the requirements negotiated between a job seeker and their Employment Services Provider or Services Australia that will satisfy their obligation requirements.

For more information, see Job Plans.

2

How do I complete a Job plan?

Generally job seekers can complete their Job Plan online.

Provider Managed job seekers

Job seekers will negotiate their Job Plan with their Employment Services Provider if they are provider managed. For more information, see How to use the Workforce Australia website.

Centrelink managed job seekers

Job Plans may be negotiated by suitably skilled Service Officers either in person or over the phone where all necessary documentation and/or information is available. Job seekers must formally agree with the Job Plan either by signing a printed copy, or by using their online account (not including the Centrelink app) to provide agreement. Job Plans cannot be finalised by verbal agreement.

For more information, see Negotiating Job Plans.

3

What sort of activities are in a Job Plan?

To meet mutual obligation requirements, job seekers must participate in a range of activities aimed at improving their employment prospects and targeted towards a job seeker's individual needs. Compulsory and voluntary activities, or a combination of both, can be included in the Job Plan.

Some examples of types of activities in a Job Plan include:

  • Attend Appointments
  • Undertake Paid Work
  • Accept referrals for jobs
  • Self Employment
  • Adult Migrant English Programme
  • Undertake studies
  • Provide a copy of jobs applied for each month

4

If I have questions about my Job Plan, who do I speak to?

If a provider managed/Workforce Australia Online job seeker enquires about the mutual obligation requirements contained in their Job Plan or they require a new Job Plan, refer them to their Employment Services Provider or the Digital Services Contact Centre for Workforce Australia Online job seekers.

5

If I don't agree with the requirements in my Job Plan what do I need to do?

If a provider managed job seeker wishes to lodge a complaint regarding their provider or their mutual obligation requirements, refer the job seeker to their Employment Services Provider.

Reporting - FAQ

Table 12

Item

Description

1

Why do I have to report?

Job seekers are required to report so the correct entitlement can be determined based on their and/or their partner's employment income. Reporting captures any changes to a customer's circumstances (such as change of address, phone number etc.).

Regular reporting also confirms job seekers are continuing to meet their mutual obligation requirements and remain eligible for payments.

2

When do I have to report?

Most job seekers claiming for or receiving JSP or YA (job seeker) with mutual obligation requirements are placed on two weekly reporting frequency.

Job seekers getting payments and have provided their mobile number or email address, are sent a reminder via email or Short Message Service (SMS) the day before they are due to report.

3

How do I report?

Job seekers can report through self service (online accounts, Express Plus Centrelink mobile app and phone). Job seekers can:

  • view a summary of their due to report dates and reporting periods in their online account. This displays all reporting periods within 12 weeks of the date viewed
  • access this summary by selecting Income and Assets > Employment Income > Next reporting periods from the menu

4

What if I start working what do I report?

Job seekers need to report the amount, hours and the period for which they were paid. They can do this via their self service (online accounts, Express Plus Centrelink mobile app and phone) on their reporting day.

For more information, see Reporting requirements for job seekers.
