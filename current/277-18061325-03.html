This procedure contains instruction for WA Ex-nuptial child support cases only.

This page contains information about the process of checking and collecting Child Support overpayments. It also contains information about other payee debts and the associated collection actions.

Check overpayment is correct + Read more ...

Determine the event that caused the overpayment and check the overpayment has generated correctly by referring to the relevant document, using the hyperlinks below:

  • errors of fact (including keying errors)
  • invalid decisions
  • errors made by delegates (e.g. Change of Assessment and Departure Prohibition Order decisions)

3

Determine if overpayment is recoverable + Read more ...

Not all overpayments are recoverable by Services Australia. In this case, the payer may choose to apply to a court for an order that the payee has to repay some or all of the overpayment.

The action taken to address an overpayment depends on how it was created. Determine whether the overpayment is collectable before discussing with the payer and/or payee. Do not recover overpayments from retrospective variations caused when:

  • the payer failed to comply with their legal obligation to lodge their tax return on time, go to Step 4
  • the payer failed to advise us of a change of address which resulted in a terminating event, go to Step 5
  • the assessment ended as a result of an objection, AAT decision or court order which determined that the application for assessment should not have been accepted, go to Step 7
  • a court made a declaration under section 107 of the Child Support (Assessment) Act 1989, go to Step 6

This policy applies to both new and existing overpayments.

If there is an existing overpayment that has not been fully recovered check the notepads associated with the overpayment to determine if this policy was applied. If not, immediately consider if the policy should be applied to the remaining overpayment.

If it is determined that an overpayment is not or may not be recoverable, action must be taken to immediately prevent Cuba automatically recovering the overpayment. This applies even if Service Support Officer (SSO) confirmation is expected the following day:

  • record an overpayment waiver (gift) - see Overpayments Cuba Process Help
  • document in the Manage Gift and Overpayment window
    • use the subject line 'Overpayment Policy'
    • document that the payment was removed as per the overpayment policy and not because the payer decided to waive the debt
  • delete any system generated letters

If the SSO advises the overpayment is not recoverable, go to Step 3 in the Customer contact table.

If the SSO advises the overpayment is recoverable:

  • reverse an overpayment waiver (gift)
  • document in the Manage Gift and Overpayment window that the waiver was reversed as the overpayment is recoverable
  • delete any system generated letters

Go to Step 1 in the Customer contact table.

4

Overpayment caused by late lodgement of tax return + Read more ...

If the payer lodges a tax return later than the timeframes set by the Australian Taxation Office (ATO), the new income may replace a higher income being used in the assessment, this may create an overpayment. Because the payer has failed to comply with their legal obligation and this has resulted in an overpayment, Child Support will not recover the overpayment. Note: this usually applies to periods prior to July 2008 as new ATO income rules generally prevent overpayments occurring due to late lodgement.

If the customer lodged a late tax return after June 2008 and it is a reassessment based on Section 11 of the Child Support (Assessment) Regulations 2018, refer the case to Program Advice via a SSO.

See:

  • Income processing for Child Support customers
  • Technical and policy escalation process (CS)

Prevent automatic recovery of the overpayment while technical advice is being sought. Where the payer has asked the ATO to retrospectively record that they are ‘not required to lodge’ for a past year and this results in an overpayment, Child Support will not recover the overpayment.

Go to Step 1 in the Customer contact table.

5

Overpayment caused by residency decision + Read more ...

When a payer changes their address, they are required to notify Child Support within fourteen days.

If a payer changes their address to a non-reciprocating jurisdiction and they have ceased to be an Australian resident a terminating event will occur. The terminating event date will most likely have taken place from a date in the past, this will cause an overpayment.

Child Support will not recover the overpayment if the payer did not advise of the address change (within fourteen days) resulting in a delayed residency decision and terminating event. Remove the overpayment at the same time residency decision is made. See Residency decisions for Child Support customers - Specialist Service Officer.

Note: extra time can be allowed to allow for normal passage of the post or any special circumstances. The customer must advise Child Support as soon as is reasonably practical.

Go to Step 1 in the Customer contact table.

6

Section 107 court orders + Read more ...

If an overpayment occurred due to a declaration under section 107 of the Child Support (Assessment) Act 1989 there is no longer a registered maintenance liability.

The overpayment is not a debt due to the Commonwealth and therefore not recoverable.

For more information, see Section 107 order received by Child Support and follow this process.

Go to Step 1 in the Customer contact table.

7

Assessment ends due to objection, AAT or court order + Read more ...

When the outcome of an objection, AAT decision or court order (other than an order made under section 107) means that an application for a child support assessment should not have been accepted:

  • the assessment ends from the start date
  • there is no longer a registered maintenance liability

See Ending case or liability (CS).

The overpayment is not a debt due to the Commonwealth. Therefore, Child Support cannot recover the overpayment from the payee. The overpayment must be removed at the same time that the review/appeal decision is implemented to prevent any automated debt recovery.

Go to Step 1 in the Customer contact table.

Contact the customers to discuss an overpayment or payee debt

Table 2: This table describes the process Service Officers follow when contacting a customer to discuss an overpayment or payee debt.

Step

Action

1

Contact the customer(s) + Read more ...

If the overpayment is recoverable, go to Step 2.

If the overpayment is not recoverable, go to Step 3.

2

Contact payer (overpayment recoverable) + Read more ...

Contact the payer and discuss the following:

  • how the overpayment occurred, apologise if the overpayment was created due to our error
  • the amount of the overpayment
  • options available to address the overpayment
    • waive the overpayment, see Step 1 in the Election to waive table
    • partially waive the overpayment, see Step 6 in the Election to waive table
    • leave the overpayment on the child support account to apply against future liabilities when there is an ongoing liability, ask if they are willing to continue to pay an amount for child support if the payee requests recovery at less than 100% of the ongoing liability, see WA Ex-nuptial child support cases - Negotiating a Child Support debt repayment and payee payment arrangement defaults
    • attempt collection from the payee when there is no ongoing liability, see WA Ex-nuptial child support cases - Negotiating a Child Support debt repayment and payee payment arrangement defaults
  • Child Support is not obliged to refund the overpaid amount to them until the amount is recovered from the payee

Refunding the overpayment with monies from consolidated revenue before recovery from the payee may be considered certain circumstances. See Creating consolidated revenue debts. Creating a consolidated revenue debt is not the preferred method of managing an overpayment and careful consideration must be given before offering this as an option.

If the payer elects to waive the overpayment, go to Step 1 in the Election to waive table.

If the payer declines to waive the overpayment, go to Step 6 in the Election to waive table.

3

Contact the payer and payee (overpayment not recoverable) + Read more ...

Contact the payer and payee to advise:

  • how the overpayment occurred
  • the amount of the overpayment
  • Child Support will not recover the overpayment, provide the appropriate reason, as:
    • it resulted from the payer's failure to comply with the legal obligation:
      • the payer lodged a tax return late (including where the customer has requested a status change to 'not required to lodge' outside the normal lodgement timeframe) and this created the overpayment
      • failure to notify Child Support of address change resulting in terminating event
    • the overpayment is not a debt due to the Commonwealth, Therefore, will not be recovered by Services Australia:
      • overpayment caused by section 107 court order, or
      • assessment ended from the start date due to an objection, AAT decision or court order
  • the payer may choose to apply to the court for an order under section 143 requiring the payee to repay all or part of the overpayment. If the payer seeks such an order:
    • they should seek an order which states the amount to be repaid
    • section 143 orders cannot be registered with Services Australia for collection except when a court makes a declaration under section 107 along with an order under section 143 for recovery of the overpayment (a parentage overpayment order)
  • there are no objection rights to the decision

Note: if the overpayment policy in this document has been applied, however some of the overpayment has already been recovered, this amount will not be refunded to the payee.

Go to Step 7 in the Election to waive table.

Customer elects/declines to waive an overpayment

Table 3: This table describes the process Service Officers follow when speaking with a payer about the option of waiving an overpayment.

Step

Action

1

Payer elects to waive overpayment + Read more ...

If the payer elects to partially or fully waive the overpayment advise that:

  • they will receive an Overpayment waiver cooling off - Payer letter providing seven days from the date the letter is received to withdraw their election to waive the overpayment
  • after the cooling off period they will no longer be able to ask Child Support to recover the overpayment or have the overpayment credited against their child support liabilities
  • the waived amount cannot be reclaimed or used to offset a future debt, and
  • in the case of a partial waiver Child Support will:
  • contact the payee to make a payment arrangement for the remaining amount, and
  • contact the payer again to advise of their ongoing child support obligations, go to Step 6

Document the payer’s decision to waive the overpayment in the Manage Gift and Overpayment window. See Documenting Child Support information.

2

Process overpayment waiver + Read more ...

Process the overpayment waiver immediately to prevent the overpayment automatically being recovered from the payee's next entitlement. See Overpayments Cuba Process Help.

3

Issue overpayment waiver cooling off letter + Read more ...

Complete the Overpayment waiver cooling off - Payer letter template and paste it into a unique letter in Cuba (MX0-1 letter). See Letters for Child Support customers.

The letter asks the payer to contact Child Support within seven days of receipt if they elect to reverse their decision. In addition to these seven days, allow an extra five calendar days to ensure sufficient time for the payer to receive the letter. Allow international customers 21 calendar days to respond.

4

Payee contact + Read more ...

If the payer waives the overpayment, the payee is not required to repay the overpaid amount. Contact the payee and discuss the following:

  • how the overpayment occurred
  • the amount of the overpayment
  • payer election to waive the overpayment
  • the payer has a seven day cooling off period to reconsider their decision to waive, after which Child Support will contact the payee to either:
    • confirm the waiver
    • negotiate a payment arrangement (if the payer reverses the decision to waive the overpayment)

Note: Child Support will allow five days for postage.

Document all contact attempts and the details of the discussion with the payee in the manage Gift and Overpayment window. See Contact with Child Support customers.

5

Expiry of cooling off period + Read more ...

If the payer does not withdraw their election to waive the overpayment within the cooling off period:

  • contact the payee to confirm the waiver
  • delete the Payee Overpayment intray
  • auto-unlock the customers, see Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support

If the payer withdraws their election to waive the overpayment within the cooling off period reverse the overpayment waiver. See Overpayments Cuba Process Help

Contact the payer and payee and explain the effects of the election being withdrawn.

6

Payer declines to waive recoverable overpayment or partially waives recoverable overpayment + Read more ...

If the payer elects not to waive the overpayment or partially waives the overpayment advise them:

  • Child Support will attempt to negotiate a payment arrangement with the payee to recover the overpayment
  • if there is no ongoing child support liability Child Support will refund the payer when payments are received from the payee (Note: this is not an automatic Cuba function)
  • when there is an ongoing child support liability:
    • the overpayment will remain as a credit on their account and may apply against future child support liabilities
    • they may be required to continue to pay child support to ensure the payee receives ongoing support for the child/ren
    • Child Support will contact them to advise of their ongoing child support obligations after negotiating an arrangement with the payee

Note: the credit created by the overpayment cannot be applied against other cases. It can only be applied against child support liabilities for the payee who has been overpaid.

Go to WA Ex-nuptial child support cases - Negotiating a Child Support debt repayment and payee payment arrangement defaults.

7

Remove overpayment + Read more ...

An overpayment must be removed from Cuba if it is not recoverable. If part of the overpayment has already been recovered (because Child Support was unable to take preventative action), remove the remaining amount of overpayment:

  • use the Gift functionality in Cuba to remove the overpayment if not already actioned, see Overpayments Cuba Process Help
  • document the discussion with the payer and/or payee in the Manage Gift and Overpayment window:
    • use the subject line ‘Overpayment Policy’
    • document that the payment was removed as per the overpayment policy and not as a gift
  • delete the Payee Overpayment intray
  • create the appropriate unique letter to both customers. If the overpayment was caused by:
    • the late lodgement of a tax return, send both the:
      • Removal of overpayment - Income change - payee
      • Removal of overpayment - Income change - payer
    • a delayed residency decision, send both the:
      • Removal of overpayment - Residency - payee
      • Removal of overpayment - Residency - payer
  • unlock the customers in-line with the Finalisation guidelines for Child Support staff

See Letters Cuba Process Help.

Other types of payee debt and required action

Table 4: This table describes the process Service Officers follow when dealing with other types of payee debt, and finalising such debts.

Step

Action

1

Non agency payments (NAP) + Read more ...

When Child Support credit a NAP and later vary the assessment, an excess NAP credit may be created. The excess NAP credit:

  • cannot be refunded or waived by the payer
  • cannot be recovered from the payee
  • remains as an account credit that can be applied against future liabilities

The excess NAP credit cannot be collected as the money has not been collected or passed on.

Contact customers

Contact the customers and explain:

  • an excess NAP credit has been created due to a retrospective variation
  • the amount of the credit
  • that the NAP credit will remain on the payer’s child support account and be credited against future liabilities (if the case has an ongoing liability)
  • the payee will not receive child support until the credit amount has been used in full
  • if the case has ended and there is no ongoing liability the payer can seek to recover the overpayment directly from the payee

See Contact with Child Support customers.

Document the details of the discussion with the customers in the Communications window. See Documenting Child Support information.

Delete the Payee Overpayment intray and auto-unlock the customers, see Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support.

This is the end of the process if there is an excess NAP credit only.

2

Money incorrectly received from third party + Read more ...

A payee will have a debt when Child Support disburse monies received from a third party in error, believing them to be funds of the payer. Child Support must refund the third party.

Contact the payee immediately and advise them that they may have received a payment in error, and if so it must be repaid. Advise the payee not to spend the money, if they have not already done so. Advise the payee that details will be confirmed and they will be advised. See Creating consolidated revenue debts.

Once the consolidated revenue debt has been created, the payee must be contacted to discuss:

  • how the overpayment occurred
  • the amount of the overpayment
  • they are required to repay the amount

See WA Ex-nuptial child support cases – Negotiating a child support debt repayment and payee payment arrangement defaults.

Note: there is no need to contact the payer to discuss the payment arrangement negotiated with the payee.

3

TRIP refund to ATO + Read more ...

A payee will have a consolidated revenue debt when Child Support refund the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) because:

  • Child Support used an incorrect tax file number (TFN) and received a TRIP belonging to a taxpayer that is not the customer
  • ATO seeks repayment of the TRIP because of fraudulent activity of someone other than the customer

For more information, see Tax Refund Intercept Process (TRIP) exceptions (CS).

When this occurs, negotiate a payment arrangement with the payee. See WA Ex-nuptial child support cases – Negotiating a child support debt repayment and payee payment arrangement defaults

4

Section 143 order + Read more ...

Where a customer owes a debt that arises under a section 143 order follow the process in WA Ex-nuptial child support cases - Section 107 and section 143 orders for Child Support customers.
