This page contains frequently asked questions for Provider Digital Access (PRODA) and Adaptors to Web Services, PRODA user guides and screenshots for staff to help users manage their PRODA account, and links to the Services Australia and Australian Government Proof of Age card websites.

On this page:

General information about PRODA accounts

Table 1

Item

FAQ

1

Question: What is PRODA?

‏Answer: Provider Digital Access (PRODA) is an online authentication system based on a username, password and verification code to log on. The system is designed to provide secure access to specific Government services using one log on method. PRODA is digital, portable, and does not need additional hardware or software installed. Users can change and manage their account details online in PRODA including changing name or contact details.

Specific government services will gradually be added to PRODA, so access to these services will only require one authentication method.

2

Question: Is PRODA secure?

Answer: Yes. It is a unique username and password that uses a verification code. The PRODA account allows access to services that involve personal and financial information.

It is important the agency correctly verifies a user's identity and that they do not share their log on details with anyone else because their PRODA account may allow access to services that involve personal and financial information. It is important that users do not share their username and password with anybody.

3

Question: Who can create a PRODA account?

Answer: Anyone can register for a PRODA account. It is important that each person has their own account because their PRODA account may allow access to services that involve personal and financial information.

4

Question: Can a user have more than one PRODA account?

Answer: No. Once their identity has been verified the system will not allow their identity documents to be used again.

Note: email addresses and mobile phone numbers are also unique to an account. Once a user has verified an email address or mobile phone number, they will not be able to use them for another account.

5

Question: Can I share my PRODA account logon details?

‏Answer: No. It is important that each person must have their own account and not share their logon details with anyone.

6

Question: Why do I need to verify my identity again?

‏Answer: The new solution requires a greater level of security. The agency needs to ensure a high level of certainty that the person performing the transaction is correct and the agency knows who they are.

7

Question: Can users log on to their PRODA account using a smartphone or tablet?

Answer: Yes. Although some services will not display correctly on a mobile device as they may not be optimised for use on a smartphone and may be difficult to navigate.

8

Question: Why does PRODA require verification codes?

Answer: In addition to a username and password, verification codes provide an additional layer of security to the account.

9

Question: What do I do if I forget my logon details?

Answer: If the user has forgotten their logon details they can recover them using the links on the PRODA log on/create a new account page. The user can request a forgotten username and reset a forgotten password by selecting the links available on the log on/create a new account page.

See Helping users self-manage their PRODA account.

10

Question: Will PRODA accounts expire?

‏Answer: No. PRODA accounts will not expire.

Note: a cancelled account will expire after 90 days and the user will be required to create a new account to access their services.

11

Question: What is My communities?

Answer: My communities will allow users to link to specific government online services.

My communities contains groupings of one or more shared services that have a shared linking process. For example: Healthcare provider and administrators is a group known by Services Australia, currently only allows access to Health Professional Online Services (HPOS). Other online services for the agency will display in this grouping in the future.

12

Question: What is Healthcare provider and administrators?

Answer: Healthcare provider and administrators currently only allows healthcare provider and administrator access to Health Professional Online Services (HPOS). Oher online services for the agency will display in this community in the future.

13

Question: What is NDIS for providers?

Answer: NDIS for providers will allow providers registered in the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) to access the new NDIS online provider portal when it becomes available.

NDIS providers will need to check the National Disability Insurance Agency (NDIA) website (www.ndis.gov.au) for details of when the provider portal will be available to access through their PRODA account and how to set up access to the new provider portal.

Scheme information: The NDIS is a new way of providing support for people living with disability. It is a whole-of-life approach - a major reform of disability services in Australia, designed to provide the right support according to each person’s needs and goals.

14

Question: How do users set up the mobile app?

Answer: If they would like to use the PRODA app to generate codes, they need to download it from the app store or google play and go through the set-up process within the app itself. They can find the PRODA code generator in the app store for their Apple or Android device. Other types of devices are not supported at this time.

They do need a smartphone or tablet in order to use the app. The app is the agency’s recommended method, it will avoid filling their email account and text messages with codes.

Once the app is installed on their device, they open the app and enter their username and password for their PRODA account. Then they enter the verification code that will be sent to their preferred channel (email address or mobile phone). The app will then perform a sync process with their PRODA account and start to generate codes.

They should then go back into their PRODA account and select Mobile App as the verification code preferred channel.

They can then start using the app to generate codes.

15

Question: Can I access My Health Record System using PRODA?

Answer: Yes. Individual healthcare providers can access the read-only My Health Record National Provider Portal using PRODA. The organisation they work at needs to have created an authorisation link in HPOS first.

Healthcare providers and participating supporting organisations cannot use the My Health Record National Provider Portal. They must have a National Authentication Service for Health (NASH) PKI certificate to access the My Health Record system.

16

Question: What are the supported browsers for PRODA?

Answer: Using the latest internet browser helps maintain and improve online security.

Minimum browser versions needed to use the agency's online accounts:

  • Internet Explorer 9
  • Mozilla Firefox 30
  • Google Chrome 39

Other browsers may work, but they have not been tested and are not supported.

If the user is unable to access their account, or if it is not displaying correctly, see: Escalating PRODA incidents, complaints and feedback.

17

Question: Can PRODA be used for secure messaging?

Answer: No.

Note: the agency does not support the use of PKI for purposes outside of their intended use (e.g. secure messaging).

How users create and access a PRODA account

Table 2

Item

FAQ

1

Question: How do I create a PRODA account?

‏Answer: To create a PRODA account, you will need to:

  • Create your account - you will need to provide basic personal information, create a username and password, and provide contact information for the secure management of your account
  • Verify your identity – we need to verify your identity online using Australian issued identity documents which requires you to provide key information from your documents. Your identity will be verified online and in real-time using the Government’s Document Verification Service (DVS)
  • Select your community – you need to select either the Healthcare providers and administrators community tile or NDIS for providers community tile to access an online service through PRODA

See Helping users create a PRODA account.

2

Question: The system says it has sent the user a verification code, but they have not received it, what can they do?

Answer: For security reasons the Get code button will be disabled for 10 minutes after they have selected it. If they have not received their code, they should:

  • Check the contact details they have entered for errors if they are creating or updating their contact details. If they find a mistake, select the Back button on screen, edit their information and select Get code again
  • Check that their device has service
  • Wait 10 minutes until the Get code button is re-enabled and select it again. Occasionally the code can take some time to come through

If they do not receive their verification code after checking the above, they will need to choose a different channel from the Backup options screen.

3

Question: What is a username?

‏Answer: The username is created by the user. It can contain uppercase and lowercase letters as well as numerals, but it cannot contain special characters or spaces. It must be unique within the system.

4

Question: How many characters does a password need to be?

Answer: A password must contain at least 10 characters with a maximum of 20, and must contain at least 1 uppercase, 1 lowercase and 1 numeric or special character (such as @ * $ %).

5

Question: How can users get verification codes?

Answer: Verification codes can be sent to users through their choice of 3 methods. They use the drop down box in their PRODA account to select their preferred option:

  • mobile app - a code generated by the PRODA app on their smartphone or tablet device
  • email - a code will be sent to their email each time they attempt to log on
  • SMS - a code will be sent to them via text message each time they attempt to log on.

To save their preferred option the user will need to select the adjacent edit once it becomes selectable.

Should they be unable to access their preferred option when logging on, the agency will give them the opportunity to request a code using a different option if they have registered the PRODA app or a mobile phone number.

Once they have generated or received a code they will need to enter it into the system as part of the log on process.

Verification codes only last for 10 minutes from when they appear in their preferred delivery method.

Note: if a user changes their preferred method, they will not need to enter any codes until they log on next time.

6

Question: What if users want to link to their online services later?

Answer: They can link to their online services within PRODA at a later date. They simply log on to PRODA, go to the My communities screen, select the relevant tile and search for their registration with the relevant online service.

The user can delay linking until later, however this will restrict the services the user can access.

Verifying identity for a PRODA account

Table 3

Item

FAQ

1

Question: How do users confirm their identity?

Answer: The new solution requires a greater level of security. The agency needs to ensure a high level of certainty that the person performing the transaction is correct and the agency knows who they are. As this is a new system, users are still required to complete the Your Identity process, even if they are known to the agency.

When setting up an account there is a requirement that the agency verifies their identity through the Document Verification Service (DVS).

  • They will need 2 documents from the following category:
    • Medicare card
    • Australian driver licence
    • ImmiCard
    • Australian passport
  • They will need 1 document from the following list:
    • Australian passport (if not used above)
    • Australian birth certificate
    • Citizenship certificate
    • Foreign passport (with visa)
    • Certificate of registration by descent

These documents will be verified one at a time. Once a document is selected, the information needs to be entered exactly as it is found on the document.

It is recommended using Medicare card, Australian drivers licence and Australian passport.

If their name is different on any of these documents, evidence of the name change will be required by providing a Marriage Certificate or Change of Name Certificate.

If they do not have these documents, a wider range of documents can be accepted via a manual process.

2

Question: The user's name is different on the document they want to use, can they still use it to verify their identity?

Answer: Yes. They must still enter the name exactly as it is displayed on the document. They may then be required to provide documents that demonstrate that their name has changed. When prompted, they select one of the following and upload the information on to the Document Verification Service:

  • Marriage Certificate
  • Change of Name Certificate

If they do not have these documents, a wider range of documents can be acceptedvia a manual process.

3

Question: The system says it cannot verify the user's name change, what can they do?

Answer: Unfortunately not every person will be able to verify their change of name online due to a lack of information held by the Document Verification Service, or issues with the information recorded on their identity document.

As a result, the user needs to finish verifying their name change using a paper form. They should download the Manual identity verification for Provider Digital Access form provided, then submit the form to the agency. A Service Officer will finalise their change of name for them.

4

Question: The user wants to change their name on the PRODA system, how can they do that?

Answer: The user accesses their account, selects the My details link and then Edit. This will give them 2 options:

  • Add a new name - This option is useful if they want to use a name that has not been previously verified by the system. The new name must be as a result of marriage or legal name change, and evidence must be provided (Marriage Certificate or Change of Name Certificate). Advise them to use information on their document to go through the online verification process and add their new name
  • Change current name - this option is useful if they have multiple names previously verified by the system. This option allows them to change their name as it is displayed and used in the system.

See Helping users self-manage their PRODA account.

5

Question: The user does not have the documents with them, what can they do?

Answer: The verification process allows the user to save their progress and come back to it later.

The user should select Save and continue later to save their progress and log out.

The process can be completed later from any computer/tablet. If they have some, but not all, of the documents they need, they should select 'I don't have any of these documents' to advance to the other types of documents that they have with them.

6

Question: The system says the user's document could not be found or document failed, what can they do?

Answer: If the system cannot match their document details, it will provide some information about the reason why. The user will receive one of 3 possible reasons:

  • Unable to be confirmed - the details they have entered do not match those of any documents held by the issuing agency, or the document is marked as being invalid (a death is recorded against it, or it is involved in fraudulent activity). The user should check the details they have entered are correct and try again, or select a different document type to enter
  • Document verification is unavailable - some issuing agencies have yet to make all their records available for online verification. If they receive this error it is because documents issued in the year they have entered cannot verified electronically. The user should choose a different document type
  • System unavailable - the Document Verification Service (DVS) is a separate government service managed by the Attorney-General's Department. Occasionally it may experience technical difficulties and become unavailable. If this is the case, the user should save their application and try again later

Note: the agency is not responsible for the operation of the DVS.

7

Question: The user has tried to register but does not have all of the documents required?

Answer: The user will select I don't have these documents and progress to download the Manual identity verification for Provider Digital Access form.

8

Question: How can users register if they are an overseas trained doctor?

Answer: The Document Verification Service (DVS) will accept an international passport with a 'permitted to work' visa. The other documents may be supplied by the Manual identity verification for Provider Digital Access form.

9

Question: The user is from overseas, will their documents work as they have not been issued by an Australian authority?

Answer: International passport with a 'permitted to work' visa will work through the Document Verification Service. Other documentation will need to be assessed manually.

10

Question: None of the user's identity documents work, how can they apply manually?

Answer: The user will select the 'I don't have any of these documents' option throughout the Document Verification Service process and be automatically navigated to the manual process.

11

Question: How can I obtain a Proof of Age card?

Answer: It needs to be obtained in your state or territory.

For more information, see link for the relevant state/territory on the Australian Government website (australia.gov.au) Proof of Age card page. See Proof of Age card.

12

Question: How can it be identified that a customer has had their name changed at the Births, Deaths, and Marriages (BDM) registry?

Answer: Procedures differ for each State and Territory. See below for details:

Australian Capital Territory (ACT)

A notation is made on the back of any birth certificate issued advising the new name and the date of change.

New South Wales (NSW)

When a change of name occurs, the letter N is placed after the birth certificate's registration number. This was implemented around the mid 1990's. In the section titled 'Endorsements' the details of the previous name are recorded. For certificates issued prior to this time the previous name is recorded at the top of the certificate with the new name recorded on either the side or bottom of the certificate.

Northern Territory (NT)

A notation on a birth certificate indicating a change of name is optional. An asterisk at the bottom of the certificate indicates a change of name has occurred but it is still not required to have the name recorded.

Queensland (QLD)

If a person requires the change of name to be recorded on their birth certificate, the change of name is recorded on the front of the certificate and previous names on the back. If they choose not to have the new name appear, the birth certificate will state that a change of name has been registered but will not state the new name.

South Australia (SA)

The recording of a change of name on a SA birth certificate is optional. If the person does not want it recorded on their birth certificate a Change of Name certificate can be issued which includes the previous name(s). If the person requests the details to be included on their birth certificate the new name details are recorded on the reverse side of the certificate.

Tasmania (TAS)

The noting on a birth certificate of a change of name is optional. If the person chooses YES to having it recorded there is an N placed after the registration number on the certificate. This came about in the year 2000.

Victoria (VIC)

BDM advises that the letter N is recorded after the registration number on the birth certificate indicating a name change. A letter N is recorded for each name change so there can be more than one letter N on any certificate. This has been in place since 1986. When the letter C appears on a certificate after the registration number it indicates that a correction has been made to the certificate. This includes the correction of any spelling errors.

Western Australia (WA)

If a change of name was completed before 1974, a stamp was placed on the birth certificate noting the amendment.

If a change of name was completed from 1974 onwards, the person is asked whether they want the details of the change to appear on their birth certificate. It is not mandatory.

Question: Will PRODA affect the National Authentication Service for Health (NASH) PKI certificates?

‏Answer: Yes for individual healthcare providers only. They can access the read-only My Health Record National Provider Portal using PRODA. NASH PKI individual certificates are being phased out.

5

Question: What will happen to an existing Medicare PKI individual certificate if a PRODA account is created now?

Answer: Nothing. The user can still use their Medicare PKI individual certificate at the same time as their PRODA account.

Note: users can revoke their PKI certificate at any time by submitting the Request to Revoke or Reissue a Public Key Infrastructure Certificate (HW003) form.

6

Question: When will a Medicare PKI individual certificate expire?

‏Answer: The user can check the expiry date of their Medicare PKI individual certificate on the Verizon website.

7

Question: Will PRODA allow access to the same services as a user's Medicare PKI Individual certificate?

Answer: PRODA will allow access to services within HPOS, but not services outside HPOS.

National Authentication Service for Health (NASH) PKI certificates will be unaffected by the introduction of PRODA. Users will still be required to login using their NASH PKI certificate to access the My Health Record system.

PRODA does not replace any Site PKI Certificates, they are still required.

8

Question: Will users still receive a Medicare PKI individual certificate renewal letter?

Answer: Yes. Approximately 3 months before its expiry date.

Linking records for healthcare providers and administrators (only)

Table 7

Item

FAQ

1

Question: Do users need to link their current records after they register for PRODA?

Answer: Users will need to link all current identifiers they have with the agency to access the full range of services they have access to within their community.

For providers the account details created will need to match the account details held in both the Healthcare Identifiers (HI) Service and provider directory system (PDS).

Other users would need to match the identifiers via the relevant system linked to their PRODA community.

If the name details are different the linking cannot occur.

Question: Can Digital Health admin staff link existing records?

Answer: Yes. They need to enter the Organisation Maintenance Officer (OMO) or Responsible Officer (RO) number if known or a Healthcare Provider Identifier - Organisation (HPI-O) number they are linked to.

Note: providers can link their Healthcare Provider Identifier - Individual (HPI-I) number.

3

Question: How do users link their existing records in PRODA?

Answer: Once the user has selected Healthcare providers and administrators in the My communities page, they can search for their existing records currently held by Services Australia, including provider numbers and Healthcare Provider Identifier - Individual (HPI-I) number.

Note: if adding a Medicare Provider Number, users need to add '0' at the beginning if the digits are 8 or less.

4

Question: What numbers can users add?

Answer: Users only need to add numbers that affect the services they can access within the agency's systems.

For example, if they are a medical provider it is important to add their Medicare provider number so that they are given access in the system to clinical functions and patient data. Users should refer to the drop down list on the Search for your existing records screen to see what type of numbers the agency is referring to. If they have a number that is not on this list, it means they do not need it.

5

Question: The system says the user's number/identifier does not match, what do they do?

Answer: It is possible that there are some discrepancies in the data the agency holds about them in different areas of the agency. For example, the personal information they have entered for this account may be different to the data saved against their Medicare provider number registration due to a change in circumstances or timing. Therefore, they may not be able to link all numbers in every case.

If a match cannot be found for the number but they are sure they entered the number correctly and it has been issued to them, they should select the checkbox to request help from a Service Officer. They need to add a short explanation in the comments box advising what kind of number it is or which area of the agency issued it (e.g. Medicare provider number).

6

Question: Why does the agency need to link up the user's numbers/identifiers?

Answer: Access to online services depends on the user's registration(s) with the programme areas within the agency. This includes Medicare provider numbers, Healthcare Identifier (HI) Service identifier numbers, pharmacist registrations and Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) provider numbers etc.

Most of these numbers relate to clinical services they are authorised to provide, so if they are working in an administrative role it is possible they do not have any numbers recorded in their name with the agency. HI Service administration officers can add the Healthcare Provider Identifier - Organisation (HPI-O) number they are linked to, to gain access to Healthcare Identifiers in HPOS.

To ensure they can access all the services they are entitled to, a link needs to be established between their existing registration(s) and their PRODA account. For providers, the easiest way to find all numbers relating to them in the agency's databases is via their Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (Ahpra) number.

If their Ahpra number does not return all identifiers linked to the agency, they can also use other identifiers from the drop down list on the Search for your existing records screen.

  • Ahpra Medical Registration number. This is a common element held across a range of the agency's databases, and is more likely to provide the desired result of linking up all numbers the agency has stored for them. If they are registered with Ahpra, the agency can use this number to find other numbers they have across the agency.
  • Add any number the user has. Alternatively, they can add numbers one by one until they have added all the different numbers issued to them. If they add their Medicare provider number the system will automatically find and add any other provider numbers issued to them.

They can add more numbers later within the PRODA management function. They simply log on to PRODA and go to the My communities screen and select the relevant tile from their selected communities.

7

Question: What if users do not have any numbers/identifiers?

Answer: Users don't necessarily have to have any numbers issued to them to be able to register and use online accounts. If they don't have any, it is likely they're setting up this account in order to perform delegated functions on behalf of another user, or administrative functions for their organisation.

If they don't have any numbers issued in their name, they should answer the guided questions and they will automatically skip past this section to finalise their PRODA registration.

Delegations for Health Professional Online Services (HPOS)

Table 8

Item

FAQ

1

Question: Will a user's existing delegations set up using a PKI certificate automatically move for HPOS when they set up a PRODA account?

Answer: No. Settings related to a user's Medicare Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) individual certificate will not carry over to their PRODA account.

Settings established in HPOS when using a Medicare PKI individual certificate, such as delegates and favourites, will not be carried over when a user logs on to HPOS using a PRODA account. They will need to set them up again when they log on to HPOS for the first time using their PRODA account. It also provides an opportunity to ensure their delegations are up-to-date.

When setting up delegates the person being given delegation must have either a Medicare PKI individual certificate or a PRODA account before the provider can delegate to them.

2

Question: Will the user's PIP and WIP – Practice Stream access for practice owners or authorised contact person/s automatically move for HPOS when they set up a PRODA account?

Answer: No. PIP and WIP – Practice Stream need to be contacted and advised of the new RA number

3

Question: Can a user give their delegate their username and password?

Answer: No. A username and password must be unique to the person who has the PRODA account and must not be shared. Their delegate must create their own account and provide the RA number to the provider to add them as a delegate in HPOS.

Their PRODA account allows access to services that involve personal and financial information.

Each person accessing HPOS needs to have their own account, including health professionals and administrators such as practice managers.

4

Question: Can a provider nominate a delegate from their Medicare PKI individual certificate and from their PRODA account?

Answer: Yes, If the provider has both a Medicare PKI individual certificate and a PRODA account, they can nominate the delegate via both authentication methods. The provider will display twice in the Select provider screen for the delegate with the 2 separate RA numbers.

The provider will not see transaction performed by the delegate in My access history if the provider is logging on with their PKI and the delegate's transactions were performed when acting, using the provider's PRODA RA number and vice versa.

If the delegate has both a PKI and a PRODA account and the delegate would like to be nominated for both authentication methods, the delegate will need to provide both RA numbers to the provider. The delegate will display twice in the providers nominated list with the 2 separate RA numbers.

Question: How do I get access to NDIS for providers?

Answer: myplace will be available in July.

Question: I have my PRODA account, what now?

Answer: Further detail on how to access myplace will be provided in advance of 1 July 2016. Keep checking the NDIS website, www.ndis.gov.au, to stay up to date.

Question: Can I use my AUSkey to access the new NDIS portal myplace?

Answer: No. You can only access myplace through establishing a PRODA account. If an individual does not establish a PRODA account they will be unable to make a claim for payment or view participant and planning information on behalf of providers, as the current portal will be closed.

6

Question: I have heard that some fellow NDIS providers have been contacted to register for a PRODA account to access a new portal called myplace. I am an NDIS provider but I have not received anything.

Answer: In July, NDIS providers will use a new provider portal called myplace accessible only through the establishment of a PRODA account. Due to the number of individuals who will need to create a PRODA account, the National Disability Insurance Agency (NDIA) is requesting that people not apply for a PRODA account until the registered provider they act on behalf is contacted by the NDIA. The NDIA will contact providers over the coming weeks.

The NDIA is processing applications for registration as quickly as possible. Once registered, you will need a PRODA account, but you will need to wait until after you have received your certificate of registration from the NDIA to start this process.

7

Question: I am a large provider and needs to establish a PRODA account for several people. What can I do?

Answer: Due to the large number of providers who will need to access myplace from 1 July, the Agency is requesting that only the nominated Account Manager apply for a PRODA account ahead of 1 July.

This will ensure that every provider can access myplace when it comes online in July.

Once the nominated Account Manager has established their account with PRODA and has access to myplace from 1 July 2016, additional users can apply for a PRODA account. Their access will need to be approved by the Account Manager.

8

Question: I am an allied health professional and already have a PRODA account, do I need to create another account?

Answer: No. If you already have a PRODA account, you do not need to create a new one. Your existing PRODA account will allow the access to myplace from 1 July.

9

Question: I have applied to be registered as an NDIS provider, but have not heard from the NDIA, should I create a PRODA account?

Answer: The NDIA is processing applications for registration as quickly as possible. However, you should not apply for a PRODA account until you have received your certificate of registration from the National Disability Insurance Agency (NDIA).

Question: Can I access information in the old Provider Portal once myplace comes online?

Answer: Yes. The NDIA will provide further information in early June. Keep checking the NDIS website, www.ndis.gov.au, to stay up to date.

11

Question: Will details of my existing participants be in the new Provider Portal?

Answer: Yes. The NDIA will provide further information in early June. Keep checking the NDIS website, www.ndis.gov.au, to stay up to date.

12

Question: Do I still need my AUSkey for accessing NDIA?

Answer: Current providers will need to retain their AUSkey to ensure they have access to the existing Provider Portal. While providers will not be able to use the existing Provider Portal to claim or amend records, they will have historical read only access for a period of time.
