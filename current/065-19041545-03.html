This page contains the process to follow when a manual means assessment is required.

On this page:

Determine when to complete a manual assessment

Residential care manual means assessment - post 1 July 2014

Determine when to complete a manual assessment

Table 1

Item

Action

1

Requests for a home care manual assessment + Read more ...

A manual assessment is not needed where a home care recipient has departed care before the completion of a means assessment. It should still be completed if it has been signed by the care recipient or authorised third party after departure from care.

See Aged care means assessment - preliminary checks where a request is received for a manual home care means assessment.

2

Requests for a residential care manual assessment - post 1 July 2014 assessment schemes + Read more ...

Only complete a manual assessment for care recipients who entered care on or after 1 July 2014, and they were assessed as a low means care recipient.

See Table 2 for Residential care manual means assessment - post 1 July 2014.

3

Requests for residential care manual assessment - pre 1 July 2014 assessment schemes + Read more ...

Pre 1 July 2014 income assessment:

  • Do not complete a manual assessment
  • See Aged care means assessment - residential care pre 1 July 2014 - income assessment for further information
  • Procedure ends here

Pre 1 July 2014 assets assessment:

  • Complete a manual assessment
  • See Aged care means assessment - residential care pre 1 July 2014 - assets assessment for the manual pre 1 July 2014 assets assessment process
  • Procedure ends here

Residential care manual means assessment - post 1 July 2014

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Request for a manual means assessment received after departure from care + Read more ...

Does a correct and valid means assessment already exist for the date of entry to care? Note: this includes means not disclosed (MND).

  • Yes
    • Complete a manual reassessment
    • See Aged care reviews - discharge and departure reviews to complete this process
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check if the care recipient has departed care and the reason for departure + Read more ...

  • Access the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP)
  • Locate the care recipient record
  • Go to Care recipient > Care recipient summary screen to check if a departure date is coded
  • Check Customer First for any notification or coding showing the care recipient is deceased. This may include:
    • a person (including third parties) contacting Services Australia directly to advise of the death of the recipient, or
    • notification by a service provider

Is there a departure from care in ACSP or deceased coding (or notification) in Customer First?

  • Yes,
    • Refer the manual assessment request for Aged Care New Claim - Experienced skill tagged staff to complete
    • Unassign the work item from the Customer First inbox
    • Recategorise the work item as ZIAM_RCA_DEA
    • Aged care staff without New Claim - Experienced skill tag, procedure ends here
    • Aged care staff with New Claim - Experienced skill tag, go to Step 3
  • No,
    • Complete a means assessment, see Aged care means assessment - preliminary checks
    • Procedure ends here

3

Check if there is enough information to complete the means assessment + Read more ...

Review the calculation of your cost of care answers against the Checklist to confirm:

  • all supporting documentation needed has been given
  • the care recipient or authorised third party has signed all forms lodged - see Aged care - consent and disclosure for more information

Note: if the care recipient is deceased, confirm that evidence has been provided that the person requesting the means assessment is the executor or administrator. If not, this must be requested before any assessment is completed.

Care recipients in receipt of a means tested income support payment may be eligible for a verbal claim. See Verifying income and assets to determine when supporting documentation is needed.

Is more information needed to complete this means assessment?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

More information is needed to complete a means assessment + Read more ...

Attempt to get the information verbally (from the care recipient or authorised third party).

If this is not possible:

  • Request the outstanding information using the 'Aged Care Request for Further Information' letter. See Aged care letters - Request for information (RFI)
  • Upload the completed letter to Customer First in PDF format
  • Record a progress of claim (POC) document
  • Hold for 21 days plus allow extra time for mail delivery

Did the care recipient or authorised third party give the information needed?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the care recipient is deemed not low means
    • Update the Progress of Claim document with the outcome, then go to Step 8

5

Complete the means assessment + Read more ...

Calculate the assessable income and assets.

If the care recipient is:

  • Deceased (in Customer First)
    • Complete the RCA/NCL activity in the Customer First Training Environment
    • See Customer First training environment for assistance if required
  • Not deceased (in Customer First)
    • Complete the RCA/NCL activity in Customer First

In Customer First (or Training Environment):

  • Use the RCA Task Selector (RCTS) screen to code income and asset information
    • (Deceased care recipients only) Code bank balances together as a lump sum on the Savings screen
  • Confirm the results on screens
    • RCA Assessment Determination (RCAD), and
    • RCA Income Assessment Summary (RIAS)
  • (Deceased care recipients only) Copy a screenshot of RCAD and RIAS into a Word document

In the Aged Care Fee Calculator (available on the Resources page):

  • Use the Aged Care Fee Calculator tab to calculate the correct fees for each period, based on the assessable income and assets from RCAD and RIAS
  • A new calculation is needed for each change in income and assets, including Consumer Price Index (CPI) and threshold changes (occurring on 20 March, 1 July and 20 September)
  • Use the Manual Adjustment Calculator tab
    • Key the correct fees (result from the Aged Care Fee Calculator tab)
    • Complete all Care Recipient Profile details

In Customer First:

  • Upload a copy of the calculations (if deceased, include the RCAD and RIAS screenshots) in PDF format
    • Using Document Tools and Form Type of UNS005-Financial
    • See Attaching electronic documents to a Centrelink customer's record using Document Tools in Customer First for more information

On the date of entry into permanent care, was the care recipient assessed as having a resident status of low means?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Care recipient was assessed as a low means resident on date of entry to care + Read more ...

  • Complete a Fast Note with the text 'Aged Care means assessment manually completed'. Care recipient assessed as a low means resident
  • View uploaded documents in Document Tools tab
  • Add the same text in Care recipient > Care recipient notes in ACSP
  • Complete and send the 'Aged Care Manual Assessment Post 1 July 2014 - MPB email template'. See the Resources page for the email template
  • Go to Step 8

7

Care recipient was assessed as a not low means resident on date of entry to care + Read more ...

Means tested care fee (MTCF)

When a means assessment is not completed before departure from care:

  • fee letters are not automatically sent
  • care subsidy reduction (CSR) to zero applies for any MTCF
  • the service provider receives the full care subsidy

Code departure

  • Update the RCIRC screen in Customer First with the discharge date and reason
  • Add note in Care recipient > Care recipient notes in ACSP stating 'Means assessment not able to be completed before departure'

Accommodation costs

  • The care recipient is
    • liable to pay an accommodation payment to cover their accommodation costs
    • not eligible for any Government assistance with their accommodation costs
  • Accommodation payments are negotiated directly with the service provider

Create a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Aged Care > Enquiry > Aged Care CSR to Zero - Granted.

Is there a request for a fee letter?

  • Yes, to send manual letters, go to Step 8
  • No, procedure ends here

8

Manual letter needed + Read more ...

Send the applicable manual letters from the 'Initial Fee Letter - Manual means assessment (including CSR to Zero) series'. See the Resources page for the letter templates.

For low means care recipients:

  • Accommodation Contribution - Discharged - not deceased (MT/MU/MV), or
  • Accommodation Contribution - Discharged - deceased (MK/ML)

For not low means care recipients:

  • Accommodation Payment - Discharged - not deceased (NT/NU/NV)
  • Accommodation Payment - Discharged - deceased (NK/NL)

In Customer First:

  • Upload a copy of the letter
    • in PDF format
    • using Document Tools and Form Type of UNS005-Financial:
    • See also Attaching electronic documents to a Centrelink customer's record using Document Tools in Customer First
  • Update Fast Note with details of letters issued

In ACSP:

  • Add note in Care recipient > Care recipient notes stating manual letters sent. Include the letter type
