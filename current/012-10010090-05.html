Scenarios that may result in SWPE or payment decrease

Table 6

Situation

Responsibility

The Practice's rural classification for WIP - Practice Stream decreased when transitioned to the Modified Monash Model (MMM)

The Australian Statistical Geographical Classification - Remoteness Area (ASGC-RA) classification has been replaced by the MMM to determine a practice's WIP - Practice Stream Rural Loading. A practice's rural classification may have changed. This could result in a payment decrease.

Under the ASGC-RA, a practice classified as RA2 receives a 20% rural loading.

Under the MMM the practice is:

  • reclassified to a MM2 and receives a 0% rural loading
  • no longer eligible to receive a rural loading under the MMM

A practice's MMM classification can also result in an increase in payments. Under the ASGC-RA, a practice classified as RA4 receives a 40% rural loading.

Under the MMM, the practice:

  • is reclassified to a MM6 and receives a 50% rural loading
  • the practice will receive an additional 10% rural loading under the MMM

Practice splits an additional location from the main location

When a practice requests that an additional location be split from the main location:

  • the additional location will be closed under the original practice profile, and
  • registered for the relevant program in its own right.

The additional location’s associated GPs are end-dated and the location is closed from the day before the approval date of the new PIP Practice ID.

The Standardised Whole Patient Equivalent (SWPE) value of the additional location will stay with the original PIP Practice ID.

The SWPE value of the new Practice:

  • will be calculated from its date of approval in PIP, and
  • will take approximately 6 payment quarters for the practice to establish a full SWPE value

See also: Practice location amendments for Practice Incentives Program (PIP) and Workforce Incentive Program (WIP) - Practice Stream.

Practice submits a new application instead of a change of ownership

When a practice undergoes a full change of ownership, the new owner must submit:

  • the necessary completed forms, and
  • supporting documents

If the practice’s current accreditation is included in the sale, the practice will keep its historical SWPE.

If a new application is submitted by the practice’s new owners instead of a change of ownership, then:

  • the SWPE value will begin from the date of approval, and
  • the historical SWPE value of the practice will not be transferred to the new owner

Escalate to the Programme Support Officer via the Incentives Escalation Database for further investigation.

See also: Change of practice ownership for Practice Incentives Program (PIP) and Workforce Incentive Program (WIP) - Practice Stream.

Practice advises practice closure instead of amalgamation

When 2 or more practices amalgamate, the amalgamated location must submit the necessary completed forms and supporting documents. The SWPE value of the closing practice will then be transferred to the current location.

If a practice closure form is submitted for the closing location, the Practice ID will be closed. The SWPE of the current practice will not include the services of the amalgamated practice.

  • Escalate to the Programme Support Officer via the Incentives Escalation Database for further investigation.

See also: Practice amalgamation for Practice Incentives Program (PIP) and WIP - Practice Stream.

Practice billing services under payee provider number of ancillary provider

If the practice is billing services under a payee provider arrangement that is using the provider number of an ancillary provider, then the services billed under the payee provider number will not contribute to the practices SWPE value. This is because only the services provided by GPs and nurse practitioners are included in the SWPE value of a practice.

The practice must use the provider number of a GP or nurse practitioner when billing services under a payee provider number, and update their Associated GPs:

  • online through HPOS, or
  • by submitting a complete Practice Incentives individual general practitioner, nurse practitioner or health professional details form (IP003)

Practice billing services under payee provider number which has been removed from PIP

If the GP associated with the practice’s payee provider number is removed from the practice profile, the services billed under the payee provider number will no longer count towards the practice’s SWPE value.

Practice billing services under a payee provider number linked to a GP that has left the practice

If a payee provider arrangement is not updated after the GP associated with the provider number leaves the practice, the services billed under the payee provider number will no longer count towards the practice’s SWPE value.

Accreditation was not included in the sale of the practice

The new practice owner is only entitled to the historical SWPE value if the practice’s current accreditation was included in the sale.

If the practice’s current accreditation is not included in the sale, the practice must re-apply for the relevant program/s. The new SWPE reference period will begin from the date of sale.

See also: Change of practice ownership for Practice Incentives Program (PIP) and Workforce Incentive Program (WIP) - Practice Stream.

Changes to incentive eligibility

Payments may decrease when the practices eligibility for individual incentives changes, such as when a practice:

  • no longer meets an individual incentive eligibility criteria, the practice is no longer eligible for the payment.
  • has changes that impact the practice’s participation in a certain payment Tier or Level
  • is subject to compliance activity which affects the practices eligibility for an individual incentive program or an individual incentive quarterly payment

See also: Individual Incentive Programs.

The address linked to the provider number does not match the address listed in PIP and WIP online

The address linked to a GPs provider number must match the location where a GP is practising.

If a practice relocates or new provider numbers are issued, practices are required to inform us of the change.

The practice has not registered all current GPs or nurse practitioners working at the practice

Services provided by the practice’s current GPs and nurse practitioners who are not registered on the practice profile will not contribute to the practice’s SWPE value.

Practices have an obligation to ensure that the details of all GPs and nurse practitioners working at the practice are up-to-date.

The practice must either add the GP or Nurse Practitioner via:

  • online through HPOS, or
  • by submitting a complete Practice Incentives Individual general practitioner, nurse practitioner or health professional details form (IP003)

Payment calculation switches from default SWPE to the true SWPE (WIP)

Practices that do not have a historical SWPE value will be allocated a default SWPE value of 1000.

It takes approximately 6 payment quarters to establish a full SWPE value. After this time the true SWPE value will be used to calculate incentive payments.

If the true SWPE is less than the default SWPE of 1000, the practice will experience a decrease in incentive payments.

Practices that do not have a historical SWPE value:

  • Newly established practices
  • Practices that are registered for the WIP - Practice Stream but are not registered for PIP
  • Practices that are registered for PIP and WIP - Practice Stream that are not PIP consenting

Health Professional or Allied Health Professional hours changed (WIP - Practice Stream)

The total incentive amount payable per annum depends on the type/s of health professionals engaged by the practice.

For example, if a practice reports hours for a registered nurse the practice’s payment will be calculated based on the higher incentive payment of $25,000 per annum.

If the practice employs an enrolled nurse the following quarter for the same hours, the practice’s payment will be calculated based on the lower incentive amount of $12,500 per annum.

As a result the practice’s WIP - Practice Stream payment will decrease.

Practice changes ownership part way through the payment quarter

When a practice changes ownership the next quarterly payment will be paid to the registered owner at the point-in-time date.

Pro rata payments are not calculated for changes of ownership that occur part way through the payment quarter.

If the previous owners have incorrectly received monies that belong to the new owners, it is the responsibility of the new owners to seek recovery from the old owners independently if they choose to do so. If an administrative error has been made, the Incentives Processing team will action a recovery process.

See also: Change of practice ownership for PIP and WIP - Practice Stream.

Practice changes ownership part way through the payment quarter

When a practice closes the owner/s are not eligible to receive any withheld payments for the payment quarter that the practice closed.

Pro rata payments are not calculated when a practice closes part way through the payment quarter.

See also: Practice closure or withdrawal in PIP and WIP - Practice Stream.

The practice is non-complaint

The Program Management (Payments Team) will manage this.

The practice provided 2 doses of the COVID-19 vaccine to a patient, but were not paid the COVID-19 Vaccine General Practice Incentive payment

When a PIP practice provides a patient with both a first-dose and a second-dose of the vaccine using a COVID-19 vaccine suitability assessment service, a COVID-19 Vaccine General Practice Incentive payment will be calculated for the practice.

The practice must:

  • meet the eligibility criteria, and
  • be the first eligible practice to bill and process both COVID-19 vaccine suitability assessment services successfully

See the Resources page of COVID-19 Vaccine General Practice Incentive for the PIP for payment scenarios.

Aged Care Access Incentive (ACAI) bank statement reference number

The bank statement related to an ACAI payment has a reference number. This includes the:

  • practitioner’s provider number stem (first 6 digits), and
  • payment run number

For example, if the provider number is 123456AB and the payment run number is 043, the reference number is 123456043.
