Determining an exclusion from VWPR Youth Income Management

Customers who do not meet the automatic exclusion criteria can still request an exclusion. If the customer requests an exclusion, they must be referred to an Income Management trained social worker within the declared Income Management area where the Income management commenced who will assess the exclusion request.

Step

Action

1

Customer is suicidal or at immediate risk + Read more ...

Is the customer at immediate risk or has expressed suicidal intentions?

  • Yes, refer customer to a social worker. For more information see:
    • Customers talking about suicide or self-harm, or
    • Referring customers to a social worker

Procedure ends here.

  • No, continue with discussions about exclusions or exiting VWPR Youth Income Management:
    • Go to Step 2
    • Go to Step 3
    • Cold transfer the call to the Income Management line, Indigenous Services Line (if appropriate) or Multicultural Call (if appropriate). The Resources page contains a link to Office Locator for National Transfer Numbers. Procedure ends here

2

Service Officers not trained in Income Management + Read more ...

Discussions regarding exclusions or exiting Income Management should only be conducted by Service Officers trained in Income Management.

Is the Service Officer Income Management trained?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No:
    • Seek assistance from a Specialised Service Officer (if available) or Service Support Officer, or
    • Contact the Access Support Centre (select Income Management option) for assistance. See the Resources page for a link to the Access Support Centre in Office Locator
    • Go to Step 3

3

Customer requests exclusion from Income Management + Read more ...

Determine the customer's Income Management measure. Access the Income Management Summary screen sections:

  • Qualification Details block for current Income Management customers, or
  • Customer is in Service Profiles block for customers who have been identified for a measure of Income Management

Under which measure is the customer being income managed or identified for Income Management?

  • VWPR due to an automatic trigger (this will display as 'Vulnerable - Special Benefit', 'Vulnerable - UTLAH' or 'Vulnerable - Crisis' on the Income Management Summary screen), go to Step 4
  • VWPR due to a VWPR Notice as determined by a Services Australia social worker:
    • Advise the customer they are not eligible for an exclusion from Income Management
    • Advise the customer they can request a reconsideration of their VWPR Notice
    • Record details on a DOC
    • Procedure ends here
  • Disengaged Youth (DEY) or Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients (LTWPR) measures, the customer is not eligible for an exclusion from Income Management. Record details on a DOC. Advise the customer they may be eligible for an exemption from Income Management. Procedure ends here
  • All other measures: advise the customer an exclusion from Income Management is not available for the measure under which their payments are income managed. Record details on a DOC. Remind the customer they can apply for a formal review of an Income Management decision. Procedure ends here

4

Customer no longer has VWPR Youth automatic trigger + Read more ...

Customers income managed under VWPR Youth can ask to be exited from Income Management if they:

  • No longer meet the Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH) criteria of independence
  • Do not receive Under 16 years Special Benefit (SpB-USY), or
  • Are no longer under 25 years and release from prison or psychiatric confinement (Crisis)

For example:

  • Customer turns 22 and transfers to Jobseeker Payment (JSP)
  • Customer no longer meets UTLAH criteria
  • Prison/psychiatric confinement release customer turns 25

Does customer meet criteria to exit VWPR Youth Income Management?

  • Yes, launch the Vulnerable IM Assessment workflow
    Note: system limitations prevent VWPR-Crisis customers who have turned 25 from being exited via this workflow. Record details on a DOC, including disbursement interview details, and email a request to end Income Management to Income Management Helpdesk who will code the customer as ineligible and disburse any residual funds as per information recorded in the DOC
    • Answer 'yes' to the question 'Does the customer wish to be exited from Vulnerable Income Management?'
    • Although not compulsory, customers exiting Income Management can choose to have a Disbursement interview to discuss, among other things, how any residual Income Management funds will be disbursed and ongoing priority needs will be met
    • Record details on a DOC
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Customer requests to exit Income Management due to their rate of payment + Read more ...

Customers will automatically be granted an exclusion from VWPR Youth Income Management if they have received less than 25% of their maximum basic rate of payment for at least 4 out of the last 6 fortnights. This does not include non-payment periods or reduced rates of payment due to non-compliance.

Is the customer requesting an exclusion from VWPR Youth Income Management because they believe they meet this criteria, but have not been granted an automatic exclusion?

  • Yes:
    • Advise the customer a specialist team, the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT), is responsible for determining an exclusion from VWPR Youth Income Management due to rate of payment
    • Create a Fast Note detailing the customer's request. Select Auto Text, use Income Management > Update > IM Exemption Request. Include any relevant information not available on the customer's record. Ensure the customer's phone number is correct in case further information is required
    • Advise the customer they can contact the agency after 2 working days to enquire about the progress of their exclusion request
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

VWPR Youth Exclusion categories + Read more ...

Social workers determine whether a VWPR Youth Income Management customer is eligible for an exclusion for the following reasons:

  • Income Management would place the customer's mental, physical or emotional wellbeing at risk including any of the following situations:
    • Not being able to meaningfully engage in the Income Management process due to mental health issues
    • Not having the capacity to comprehend the operation of Income Management, or
    • Experiencing serious instability in their housing or living situation and Income Management would affect their ability to direct funds to housing

7

Concerns about the impact of Income Management + Read more ...

For example, the customer:

  • Has significant mental health issues impacting on their understanding of Income Management
  • Cannot understand the Income Management process or their record indicates an intellectual impairment or disability, or
  • Presents with an advocate or community agency representative who is concerned about the customer's wellbeing or the impact of Income Management, or
  • May be at risk of homelessness as there are difficulties in allocating Income Management funds due to their accommodation circumstances

Is there concern about the impact of Income Management on the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Customer has vulnerabilities + Read more ...

For example, the customer:

  • Is under 18 and not connected to any support services
  • Is experiencing family or domestic violence
  • Is extremely distressed during an interview, or in extreme hardship
  • Is struggling to understand IM and how to spend or allocate their money
  • Has a support worker who is concerned about the impact of Income Management, or
  • May be at risk of homelessness due to Income Management

Is the customer considered vulnerable?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

9

Social worker referral + Read more ...

Does the customer meet one of the exclusion criteria, or they are requesting an exclusion from VWPR Youth Income Management?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 11

10

Refer customer to a social worker + Read more ...

  • Contact the local Income Management social worker and create a referral using the Guided Procedure
    • To contact the local Income Management social worker, find the Income Management location the customer belongs to on the Income Management Summary screen, the IM location is listed under Qualification Details. Select the LGA to be directed to Office Locator
    • From Office Locator, search for the LGA, select the Income Management Centre from the options returned
    • Access the information in 'Staff on Site' to contact the local social worker and explain the customer's situation.
      The social worker may arrange to see the customer immediately, or book them an appointment for a later date (to give them time to gather evidence or if they are too distressed for an interview straight away)
  • If a local Income Management social worker is not available and immediate assistance is required:
    • Contact the Social Work Line and warm transfer the customer using Services Australia Workspace menu option Social Work
    • If a customer cannot be warm transferred to a social worker, create a referral using the Guided Procedure. Request the social worker to contact the customer to discuss an exclusion from Income Management. Ensure customer contact details are up to date. For more information, see Social work services

If a local Income Management social worker is not available and immediate assistance is not required:

  • create a referral using the Guided Procedure, requesting the social worker to contact the customer to discuss an exclusion from Income Management. When making the referral, select the Income management area relevant to the customer and one of the following:
    • Investigate VWPR Eligibility
    • Reconsideration of IM VWPR
    • Vulnerable customer with Complex Concern
    • VWPR Youth Exclusion
  • ensure customer contact details are up to date. For more information, see Social work services
  • procedure ends here

Note: although a customer may seem distressed, please ensure they are in one of the exclusion categories before referring them for exclusion from Income Management.

11

Explain Income Management and review process + Read more ...

If the customer does not wish to apply for an exclusion from Income Management, or does not agree with the decision to apply Income Management, advise the customer:

  • why they have been identified for Income Management and the benefits
  • how they can access their income managed money, see Coding Income management expenses for the flexible allocation options available. If appropriate, this may include one-off allocations, Restricted Direct Payments or Unrestricted Cash Payments. Note: Unrestricted Cash Payments are only to be made if the customer's current and reasonably foreseeable priority needs have been made
  • about the review and appeal process. See:
    • Reviews of Income Management decisions
    • Request for an explanation or application for a formal review

Record details of discussions on a DOC.
