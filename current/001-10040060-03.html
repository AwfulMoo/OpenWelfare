Proper notification and reasonable notice

Table 1: This table describes how to assess if a job seeker or ParentsNext participant has received appropriate and reasonable notice.

Item

Description

1

Proper notification of the requirement to attend an appointment + Read more ...

Services Australia and Employment Services Providers, including ParentsNext providers, are delegated to issue formal notification in relation to compulsory requirements.

A notification must include the:

  • date, time and location of the appointment, and
  • consequences for failing to attend or failing to advise of an inability to attend

Note: if possible, appointment notifications should also include the contact person, purpose and/or reason for the appointment, however not doing so does not invalidate the requirement. This is because all Job Plans and Participation Plans include a requirement to contact the provider with an acceptable reason if unable to meet a requirement.

The requirement to enter into a Job Plan or Participation Plan can be included within the original notice or delivered verbally at or before the appointment.

2

Proper notification of the requirement to attend, undertake or provide evidence of a compulsory activity + Read more ...

For job seekers in the Community Development Program (CDP), Disability Employment Services (DES) or ParentsNext participants, the requirement must be recorded in the Job Plan or Participation Plan and must include the following:

  • a description of the requirement
  • start and end date the job seeker is required to participate, and
  • the number of hours per fortnight of required participation

CDP and DES job seekers receive formal notification of their Job Search Requirement through their Job Plan. This includes the number of Job Search efforts required, the date the efforts are due to be reported, and how they can meet this requirement and report their Job Search efforts.

For job seekers in Workforce Australia and Workforce Australia Online, the Job Plan is simplified and must only include mandatory requirements like:

  • achieving a monthly points target which includes a minimum job search requirement
  • attending compulsory appointments, and
  • participation in Mandatory Activation Point tasks as required

Workforce Australia job seekers receive formal notification of their Points Requirement (including minimum job search) via their Workforce Australia homepage.

All job seekers are required to make prior contact with the provider and provide an acceptable reason if unable to comply with a requirement.

Under the Targeted Compliance Framework, job seekers are responsible for recording their own attendance at activities using the Workforce Australia App, Homepage, or by contacting their provider by close of business on the day of the requirement.

Providers are required to issue formal notification any time they set or change a compulsory requirement. This includes when they:

  • set the requirement for the first time in a job seeker's Calendar/Activity Diary
  • reschedule a requirement because the job seeker has an acceptable reason for not being able to meet a requirement
  • adjust requirements to reflect they no longer need to be met (in part or in full) due to another requirement being set, or
  • set a re-engagement requirement

When a separate notification is issued requesting evidence of an activity, it must include:

  • the evidence the job seeker needs to provide
  • when the evidence needs to be provided
  • the organisation the information should be provided to

3

Methods of notification + Read more ...

Formal (or primary) notification

Formal notification is a notification that meets the requirements under Social Security Law. It must include the following details:

  • What the requirement is, for example, an appointment or an activity
  • The purpose or reason for the requirement (if relevant)
  • The date and time of the requirement, or the date and/or time the requirement needs to be completed by
  • The location or address of the requirement (if relevant)
  • That the job seeker must contact their Provider before the requirement if they have an acceptable reason for not being able to meet it
  • The consequences of not meeting the requirement, and
  • A statement that the Notification is being issued under Social Security Law, explaining the job seeker must meet their requirements in order to receive income support payments

Formal (primary) notification methods used by providers can include:

  • Letter-mailed - a letter is mailed to the job seeker's residential address on the day the requirement is scheduled
  • Letter-handed - a letter is handed to the job seeker in person as they are currently with the Provider
  • Phone (script) - a script is read to the job seeker by their provider as the requirement is being scheduled. Note: leaving a message on an answering machine or with another person in the job seeker's household does not count as formal notification
  • Face to face (script) - a script is read to the job seeker as they are in attendance at the provider's office as the requirement is being scheduled
  • Email -sent to the job seeker with a read receipt sent back to the Provider. This is only available if it is recorded as the job seeker's preferred method of Notification

Secondary notification (reminders)

Depending on the requirement, job seekers may also receive reminders.

Job seekers may receive reminders through:

  • SMS
  • Email
  • A Push Notification on their Job Seeker App
  • An Inbox message through their homepage on the Workforce Australia website

4

Keeping notification records + Read more ...

Both Services Australia and providers are required to keep accurate records of all notification and contact attempts, whether successful or not.

These records are used when determining if a financial penalty is appropriate when non-compliance occurs.

Records should include:

  • the date and method of any notification or reminder
  • the nature or purpose of the requirement
  • that a compliance warning was issued
  • any explanations given by a job seeker for requesting to reschedule a notified appointment

When notification of a requirement was provided in writing, a copy should be kept as evidence of notification.

5

Services Australia notification records + Read more ...

Evidence that proper notification has occurred is shown by one or more of the following:

  • written notification of the appointment or the requirement on the History Summary (HS) screen
  • the job seeker's Job Plan or Participation Plan
  • details recorded in a DOC when the job seeker was verbally notified of the appointment and no workflow was used to facilitate the appointment
  • information recorded in the Online Diary
  • any other appointment, notification and reminder details recorded

6

Guidelines for reasonable notice timeframes + Read more ...

The job seeker must be given enough time between formal notification being issued and a requirement occurring to receive the notification and prepare for the requirement.

Reasonable notice will vary according to the circumstances of the notification and whether or not the job seeker was directly consulted prior to notice being given.

Direct consultation:

When an appointment has been made in direct consultation with a job seeker and the job seeker has agreed to attend, reasonable notice is considered to have been given, even if the appointment is on the same day as the notification.

Reasonable notice time frames for other formal notification methods:

The Employment Services System (ESS) ensures that reasonable notice timeframes are met when the provider is setting requirements and creating formal notification for job seekers, preventing a requirement on a specific day or use of a particular type of notification if these will not meet the following policy guidelines:

  • Letter mailed to a job seeker - 6 business days, Community Development Program (CDP) providers are required to post their letters 8 business days ahead of the scheduled appointment
  • Letter handed to a job seeker - 3 calendar days
  • Verbal script read face to face or over the phone - 3 calendar days
  • Email (can only be used if this is the job seeker's preferred notification method) - 2 business days with a read receipt sent back to confirm the job seeker has received, read and acknowledged the notification

When direct consultation has not occurred and the job seeker disputes notification of the requirement, Service Officers should consider the following:

  • the address where the letter was sent
  • if the notification was made by email, whether the requirement for a read receipt was met
  • if there was sufficient time for the job seeker to receive the notification, and
  • if there was sufficient time for the job seeker to make the necessary arrangements required to attend. Examples include arranging transport to the appointment or making child care arrangements

Note: in rural and remote areas, local mail delivery conditions and the job seeker's arrangements for collecting mail should also be taken into consideration.

Provider notification records + Read more ...

The Employment Services System (ESS) retains a history of any formal notification issued on the Notification List.

The notification record must be provided if a non-compliance event is reported to the agency. The Services Australia IT system automatically picks up the notifications for any requirements scheduled in the job seeker's Calendar.

Linked notification record/s can be viewed in the Participation Compliance workflow when conducting a compliance investigation. Linked notifications are available within the relevant provider report on the Participation Compliance Workflow Summary screen.

Job Seeker Compliance Framework:

  • The notification linked to the Non-Attendance Report (NAR) can be found immediately above the 'Evidence' heading within the report
  • Notifications linked to Participation Reports and Provider Appointment Reports (PAR) appear under the 'Notification' heading

Targeted Compliance Framework:

  • The type of notification used by the provider is recorded on the other related information page under the recent non-compliance link available within the Compliance Investigation workflow, or within the NCE on the Provider Report Summary screen

Details in the linked notification records should be consistent with other details recorded by the provider.

Details of the notification given may not be displayed in these links, for example when:

  • a verbal notification has been issued
  • the notification was generated via the Activity Diary/Calendar, or
  • if the notification has been archived

The information provided when reporting the non-compliance event will include evidence to satisfy appropriate notification. Further information from the provider is only required when the job seeker disputes notification and/or reasonable notice.

When notification was made using the Activity Diary/Calendar, the record will show the print date and indicate notification was generated from the Activity Diary/Calendar. This should be accepted as appropriate notification unless disputed.

When a requirement was scheduled in the Calendar, job seekers will also be able to see the notice issued online via their homepage on the Workforce Australia website or in the Workforce Australia App.

Providers may include additional relevant information regarding notices issued when reporting a non-compliance event, for example:

  • the date a locally printed letter was handed or posted to the job seeker, or
  • any follow up contacts to confirm appointment

If a job seeker challenges some aspect of the notification, and that aspect is not clearly documented, confirmation may need to be sought from the provider.

2

Archiving of provider notifications + Read more ...

The Department of Employment and Workplace Relations archiving rules for notifications are:

  • primary notifications, for example letter or written notifications, are archived after 9 months from the date notification was created
  • secondary notifications (for example SMS) can be archived 14 days after they are created

Participation Solutions Team (PST) skilled Service Officers can request archived notifications from the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations via the Participation Obligations Helpdesk see Resources for contact details.

3

Provider notification scripts + Read more ...

The Department of Employment and Workplace Relations provides endorsed scripts to assist providers to meet the notification requirements under the legislation.

When a provider has recorded a notification method of either phone or face-to-face, the job seeker has been verbally notified using an endorsed script.

When notification includes the use of an endorsed script, appropriate notification has been given. Further information should only be sought if a job seeker challenges some aspect of the notification, and that aspect is not documented.

4

Provider notification letters + Read more ...

The Department of Employment and Workplace Relations issues providers with endorsed letters and letter templates to assist providers meet the notification requirements under legislation.

There are 2 different types of notification letters providers may use to provide written notification of an appointment or compulsory requirement.

Provider produced compliance letters

These letters are developed by the provider based on the endorsed letter template, it contains all the required notification elements.

The provider is required to keep a hard copy of the signed letter on the job seeker's file. This may be requested required.

Locally printed systems generated

These are standard letters that contain all the required notification elements. They are printed, signed and posted locally by the individual provider.

A hard copy of the letter should be requested if required for the Authorised Review Officer (ARO) or Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT).

5

CDP provider issued Job Seeker Activity Diary + Read more ...

Information contained in the Activity Diary supplement other forms of notification (including the Job Plan) to notify a CDP participant of their requirements to attend and participate in scheduled activities or training. The Activity Diary details the required activity, attendance dates, location, start and end times that the job seeker must comply with that may not be included in their Job Plan.

The Activity Diary contains appropriate warning messages about consequences for non-attendance and not advising beforehand of an inability to attend.

6

Provider email notification + Read more ...

The Department of Employment and Workplace Relations issues providers with endorsed email forms and templates to assist providers to meet the notification requirements under legislation.

The email option will only be available when the job seeker has nominated this as their primary form of preferred notification. The email must be sent by the provider at least 2 days prior to the date of the requirement and include a read receipt, which will send an acknowledgement back to the Provider.

If acknowledgement does not occur at least one day prior to the requirement, the job seeker must be given formal notification using another method.
