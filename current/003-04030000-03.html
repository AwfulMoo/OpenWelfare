Reductions

Table 1: steps Service Officers must take to consider if any reductions will apply to an AVTOP claim for both primary victims and those who have died as a direct result of a declared terrorist act.

Step

Action

1

Is the victim or the deceased person: + Read more ...

  • 16 years or older, go to Step 2
  • Under 16 years, reductions do not apply. Procedure ends here

2

When did the claimant or deceased person leave the country? + Read more ...

Determine the date the claimant or the deceased left Australia.

3

Check the current travel advice + Read more ...

Check the Smartraveller website for the current travel advice and find the date the current advice was issued. See the Resources page for a link to the Smartraveller website.

Did the claimant leave the country after this date?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check historical travel advices + Read more ...

Services Australia will work with the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade to source all the travel advices that have been issued in the period up to 6 months before the declared terrorist act. See the Resources page of the Operational Blueprint file for the specific terrorist act for all travel advices that have been received.

Was a travel advice issued before the claimant's departure from Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Request relevant travel advice + Read more ...

Where there is no travel advice available, either on the Smartraveller website or the Resources page of the Operational Blueprint for the specific terrorist act, escalate a request for travel advice to the Emergency Management Level 2 Helpdesk using the Payment Query Template located on the Resources page. Note the date the claimant left the country so the appropriate travel advice can be found.

When the travel advice is received, go to Step 6.

6

Did the victim or the deceased person fail to take reasonable steps to avoid harm or act recklessly when the terrorist act occurred? + Read more ...

  • Yes, reduce AVTOP by 10%. Go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Did the victim or the deceased person, despite travel advice on an Australian government website advising people to reconsider their need to travel to that place because of a high risk of a terrorist act (level 3 travel advice), travel to that place? + Read more ...

  • Yes, reduce AVTOP by 15%. Go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Did the victim or the deceased person, despite travel advice on an Australian government website advising people against travelling to that place because of a high risk of a terrorist act (level 4 travel advice), travel to that place? + Read more ...

  • Yes, reduce AVTOP by 20%. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Did the victim or the deceased person, despite being directed by an official of Australia or a foreign country to leave or not go to the place where the terrorist act occurred because of a high risk of a terrorist act, travel to that place? + Read more ...

  • Yes, reduce AVTOP by 50%. Go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Does the victim or the deceased person fit into more than one category for a reduction? + Read more ...

  • Yes, where more than 1 category for a reduction applies, for example, a victim/deceased person acted without regard to the consequences and against the relevant travel advice, only the highest reduction percentage applies. Go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 11

11

An AVTOP must not be reduced if the victim or the deceased person was in the place where a terrorist act occurred if: + Read more ...

  • they were there for a humanitarian purpose, or
  • they were there on official business for the Government, a State or Territory, or
  • for any reason the Secretary considers appropriate not to reduce the amount of AVTOP

Any consideration for an exemption to the application of a reduction must be based on the circumstances of the case. Considerations may include:

  • the date terrorist act occurred
  • the nature of the travel advice
  • the detail provided in the travel advice
  • the reason they travelled

When considering exempting a claim from a reduction, the claim and the reason the reduction should not be applied must be escalated as a payment query via email to the Emergency Management Level 2 Helpdesk. The Payment Query Template is located on the Resources page. The Helpdesk will examine the claim and either provide advice on proceeding or escalate further for guidance.

Does the victim/deceased person qualify for an exemption to the reduction?

  • Yes, reduction does not apply. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 12

12

Calculate the amount the AVTOP will be reduced by + Read more ...

AVTOP assessed amount x reduction % = reduction amount.

Deductions

Table 2: steps Service Officers must take to consider if any deductions will apply to an AVTOP claim for both primary victims and those who have died as a direct result of a declared terrorist act.

Step

Action

1

Is the claimant a primary victim or a secondary victim? + Read more ...

  • Primary victim, go to Step 2
  • Secondary victim, go to Step 4

2

Has the primary victim received financial assistance from a foreign country for an injury that is the direct result of the terrorist act? + Read more ...

The claimant is required to declare any monies received on their claim form.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, no deduction is to apply. Procedure ends here

3

Were the monies received for an economic loss resulting from the injury? + Read more ...

  • Yes, no deduction is to apply. Procedure ends here
  • No, reduce the AVTOP by the amount of money received from a foreign country. Procedure ends here

4

Did the deceased person receive any monies from a foreign country prior to their death? + Read more ...

The claimant is required to declare any monies received on their claim form.

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Did the secondary victim or any other close family members receive any monies from a foreign country for the death of the deceased person? + Read more ...

The claimant is required to declare any monies received on their claim form.

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, no deduction is to apply. Procedure ends here

6

Was the monies received for an economic loss resulting from the injury? + Read more ...

  • Yes, no deduction is to apply
  • No, reduce the AVTOP by the amount of money received from a foreign country
