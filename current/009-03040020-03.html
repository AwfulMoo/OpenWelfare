Assessing a recognised disability as part of the Carer Allowance (CA) (child) new claim procedure

Step

Action

1

Assessment of a recognised disability + Read more ...

The term 'recognised disability' refers to a list of specific disabilities. If the child has a recognised disability:

  • this is coded on the Child Medical Details (CDMD) screen
  • the Disability Care Load Assessment (DCLAD) (2010) is not needed

The Medical Report - Carer Allowance for a child under 16 years (SA333TDR/SA426) provides information needed to determine a recognised disability (see Questions 7 and 8). Check the form is the current version so that it contains the most recent list of recognised disabilities

2

Examine the SA333TDR/SA426/SA422/SA431 + Read more ...

If the THP has not indicated any condition(s) in Question 7 and/or 8, but has ticked 'Yes' at Question 9, contact the THP to confirm the child care receiver has a recognised disability.

Note: only issue the carer another medical report in limited circumstances. For example, cannot contact the THP or the THP has failed to answer numerous questions, such as, questions 10 to 20

Has the Treating Health Professional (THP) indicated in Question 7 and/or Question 8 that the child has a disability and/or Medical Condition?

  • Yes, if the THP has indicated in questions 7 and/or 8 the child care receiver has a disability and/or medical condition, then question 9 should be recorded as ‘Yes’. If the THP has missed question 9 or answered 'No', there is no requirement to contact the THP or issue another Medical Report. The child care receiver is not to be tested under the DCLAD. Go to Step 4
  • No, if the THP has not indicated at either Questions 7 and/or 8 the child care receiver has a recognised disability and ticked 'No' at Question 9, go to Step 3

3

The care receiver does not have a recognised disability + Read more ...

Assess the child care receiver under the DCLAD. The claim cannot be 'fast-tracked' through the medical assessment process.

See Coding a claim for Carer Allowance (CA) where the care receiver is under 16.

Procedure ends here.

4

Assessing a child with a recognised disability + Read more ...

The carer does not need to provide a Care Needs Assessment (CNA) for a child under 16 years (SA408 or SA394) form. This is because we have sufficient information is available to continue with the new claim process.

Recognised Disabilities only refer to medical eligibility. A carer still has to meet the other qualifications in order to be granted.

5

Child Medical Details (CDMD) screen + Read more ...

  • During coding a new claim, the medical details will need to be recorded on the CDMD screen. For help with coding, see Coding a claim for Carer Allowance (CA) where the care receiver is under 16
  • Processing staff must manually assess the Period of Condition (POC) as part of the claim process. See Coding a claim for Carer Allowance (CA) where the care receiver is under 16
  • Coding should be done in the care receiver's record, unless the care receiver does not have their own record (this is the case with many child care receivers). If the child does not have their own record, coding is done in the carer's record
  • Using details from the Medical Report, code the fields on the CDMD screen, using field help (?) for coding values

6

The CDMD screen + Read more ...

Some codes are for medical conditions:

  • which will always be a recognised disability
  • that may or may not be a recognised disability depending on factors such as the:
    • age of the care receiver, and
    • severity of the condition

For example, Epilepsy can only be considered a recognised disability if it is 'uncontrolled while on medication'. This means medication does not substantially reduce the frequency of seizures and the child may require immediate or emergency attention to prevent harm resulting from a seizure.

Further investigation is needed if the diagnosis is Coeliac Disease and the THP has ticked 'other inborn errors of metabolism treated by medically prescribed diet to prevent neurological disability and/or severe organ damage' (MET). This is because Coeliac Disease on its own does not meet this recognised disability.

  • If the disability code used is always a recognised disability, a 'Y' will default into the appropriate field and a warning is produced stating 'The Recognised Disability field for Med Cond ### has been changed' (where ### is the medical condition coded)
  • If the disability code used is only a recognised disability in certain circumstances (for example, Severe Multiple Disabilities - Seizures (SMS) is only a recognised disability if the child is under 6 months of age.), a warning is presented if the field is not code. The warning will state '### may be a recognised disability' (where ### is the medical condition coded). If unsure whether it meets the criteria, investigate the warning by checking the References page
  • If the disability is a recognised disability, code a 'Y' in the Recognised Disability: field. Press [Enter]
  • Coding the 14 or more hrs extra care weekly? field with ‘Y’ or ‘N’ is mandatory. The carer will be granted Health Care Card (HCC) only CA if this field is coded 'Y' and the carer does not qualify for a fortnightly payment of CA at the end of the grant process. If the child has a recognised disability, they are considered to automatically meet the conditions of requiring extra care and attention for 14 hours or more per week
  • The Period of Condition (POC) eligibility requirements apply to recognised disabilities and also to HCC only CA. Processing staff are expected to manually assess the POC as part of the claim process. If the Period of Condition is less than 12 months, go to Step 7

7

Period of Condition (POC) less than 12 months + Read more ...

Check all medical evidence on record including previous medical reports.

Contact the THP to clarify why they consider the condition will be present for less than 12 months.

  • If confirmed with the THP that the condition is:
    • permanent or temporary for at least 12 months, record a DOC with the confirmation information, go to Step 8
    • less than 12 months, refer the case to the Carer Helpdesk for advice
  • If returned from the Carer Helpdesk and the advice received is the condition is:
    • permanent or temporary for at least 12 months, record a DOC with confirmation information. The Carer Helpdesk will also advise if CA will need to be manually reviewed in the future. See How to code and action a manual review
    • less than 12 months, record a DOC with the information, go to Step 8

Note: the List of Recognised Disabilities contained within the Guide to Social Security Law is broken into 2 categories - Recognised Disabilities and Recognised Medical Conditions. The References page contains links to both categories.

8

Recognised disability assessment complete + Read more ...

See Coding a claim for Carer Allowance (CA) where the care receiver is under 16 to finalise the claim.
