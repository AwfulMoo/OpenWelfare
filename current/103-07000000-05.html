Tax Time 2021-22 Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Table 2

Question

Answer

How do I know if I need to lodge a tax return?

Customers can check with the ATO if they need to lodge a tax return by searching ‘do I need to lodge a tax return’ on the ATO website.

What information is on my Centrelink payment summary?

Centrelink payment summaries show:

  • Centrelink payments
  • payment dates
  • most taxable and reportable amounts
  • tax withheld amounts – tax deductions
  • Remote area allowance amounts, if you get it.

How do I get my Centrelink payment summary?

Your 2021-22 Centrelink payment summary will be available after 1 July.

Customers can get a copy of their payment summary using:

  • the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app
  • Centrelink online account through myGov
  • Once customers have logged in, they need to go to ‘request a document’ to request a copy of their payment summary. There’s instructions on how to do this on the Services Australia website
  • Payment summary information should also be available in the ATO’s myTax system or through a registered tax agent by mid-July each year
  • Customers can check when pre-fill information is available by searching ‘pre-fill availability’ on the ATO website

I don’t use online services – how else can I get my Centrelink payment summary?

If customers don’t use online services, they can also get a copy of their payment summary by using Centrelink phone self-service. All they need to do is use the ‘request a document’ service.

I haven’t had to lodge a tax return before where can I get more information?

Some customers may need to lodge a tax return this year where they haven’t before. They can search for ‘Tax Time Toolkit’ on the ATO website which will provide information on lodging tailored to their situation.

Why isn’t my payment on my payment summary?

During 2021-22 tax year, Services Australia delivered a number of support measures to customers. The following support payments will not appear on the payment summary:

  • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • New Zealand Disaster Recovery Allowance (NZ DRA), and
  • Disaster Recovery Top Up

Customers can determine the total amount of these payments received by accessing any of the following channels;

  • View payment history (online account)
  • View letters (online account or post)
  • Contacting us

Customers should be aware that these payments are taxable and must be manually included in their tax returns.

Consumer Travel Support Program is a taxable payment. A business will not be issued a Centrelink Payment Summary however the payment may need to be included in an individual’s tax return (for example, they may be a sole trader) or the business’s tax return.

Some emergency and pandemic payments delivered aren’t taxable and won't show on payment summaries. These include:

  • The cost of living payment
  • Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment
  • COVID-19 Disaster Payment

For a full list of payments that are included on the Centrelink Payment Summary, customers can search for Centrelink payment summary on the services Australia website.

It’s 1 July, why can’t I get a copy of my Centrelink Payment Summary?

If a customer is unable to get a copy of their Centrelink Payment Summary for the 2021-2022 financial year, Service Officers should first complete the following checks;

Did the customer receive a taxable payment that appears on the Centrelink Payment Summary for 2021-22 financial year?

  • If yes, continue to next question
    (For a full list of payments that are included on the Centrelink Payment Summary, customers can search for Centrelink payment summary on the services Australia website).
  • If no then there may have been a delay in sending the customer’s information to the ATO. Encourage them to wait until 14 days after 1 July and check their Centrelink online account.

Did the customer only receive a pandemic or emergency support payment during the2021-22 tax year delivered by Services Australia? If yes, the customer will not receive a Centrelink Payment Summary but must include the payment in their tax return. See;

  • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • New Zealand Disaster Recovery Allowance and
  • Disaster Recovery Allowance Top-up.

Customers can determine the total amount of any of these payments received by:

  • View payment history (online account)
  • View letters (online account or post)
  • Contacting us

Is there a DOC on the DL screen showing that a tax MFU has been actioned on the customer's record in the last 14days?

  • If yes, the MFU will have delayed pre-fill from occurring in early July. The customer will not be able to get a copy of the payment summary until the first Payment Summary amendment files have been run. This occurs 14days from 1 July and then weekly each Wednesday during the tax year
  • If no, and customer’s circumstances do not meet any of the other FAQ checks, consider raising a mySupport incident

When should I do my tax?

Centrelink payment summary information is usually pre-filled in the ATO myTax lodgement service by mid-July. Customers should wait for all pre-fill before lodging their tax return to reduce any errors. ATO advise that pre-fill of data from any employers, banks, private health insurance, shares, dividends etc. is usually available by late July. If you log into the myTax system via myGov and these details are not pre-filled you can add this information manually from your Centrelink payment summary.

The Centrelink Payment Summary doesn’t show the total amount of what the customer received this financial year?

This is because Centrelink payment summaries will only show all taxable and reportable payments required to be reported to the ATO.

Some payments are tax exempt so will not show on the payment summary. Refer to Taxable and non-taxable (tax exempt) payments for Centrelink Payment Summary

If the customer requires all payments received from Centrelink including tax exempt payments staff can view TXYTD to show for a given period. Refer to Using the Tax Year to Date (TXYTD) screen.

Why does the period end date on my Centrelink Payment Summary end before June 30?

This is because the customer's fortnight spans the tax years with the EPED falling in the next financial year (after June 30). In these cases this payment will be reported in the next EOFY period. This often affects Pension systems customers whose payment delivery timeframe is 2 days.

For example, customer's payment summary states customer was paid for the period 01/07/2021-20/06/2022 and not 30/06/2022. This is because the customer's last pay period to fall within the financial year period ended on 20/06/2022. Customer's next pay period 21/06/2022-05/07/2022 with payment to be received 07/07/2022 will fall due in the next financial year and will not be included on the customer's 2021/2022 payment summary.

Example - lump sum arrears payments

An example where arrears payment is spread over 2 financial years.

Item

Description

1

Jethro has applied for Age Pension (AGE) on 1 April 2021.

His claim is initially rejected and following multiple reviews and appeals, the decision to reject his claim is overturned. Due to the time lapse between the initial rejection and the decision to grant AGE several months have passed before the claim is processed and granted on 1 January 2022.

Jethro is entitled to AGE from 1 April 2021. He receives payment for the entitlement (1/04/2021 to 1/01/2022) on 3 January 2022. Jethro continues on payment and on 1 July 2022 is issued a Centrelink payment summary for the 2021-22 financial year.

Although some of his payment is for entitlement in the 2020-21 financial year, because it is paid to Jethro on 3 January 2022 (in the 2021-22 financial year) it will present on the 2021-22 payment summary and not the 2020-21.
