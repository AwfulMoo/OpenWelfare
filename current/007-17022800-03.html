Description

1

Action required by staff skilled in Parental Leave Pay (PPL), Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) or Family Tax Benefit (FTB) new claims only + Read more ...

A claim or activity must be checked to determine if it can be processed or if further information is needed. This is known as 'streaming'. Streaming includes checking if scanned documents have been provided to support the claim or activity and forms have been signed appropriately. If all required information and/or documents are available then processing can be completed, see Table 2.

If extra information is required, Service Officers must attempt to contact customers initially by phone, and create/annotate progress DOC/Note with information required and contact attempts made.

The following instructions are for staff proficient in processing PPL, DAP or FTB new claims only, who are allocated a new claim type they are not trained in.

PPL or DAP claims also assessed for FTB

  • If a claim for PPL or DAP is lodged only for a newborn or adopted child, and the customer or their partner is FTB current (CUR or CZR) for other children, FTB entitlement must also be assessed for the newborn/adopted child
  • Staff proficient in PPL new claims but not trained in FTB new claims must not process these claims
  • Staff must add the keyword INDOCFTB to the claim

FTB claims also assessed for PPL

  • Paper claims may be incorrectly classified as FTB only where they should be indexed as a combined claim for FTB and PPL
  • Staff proficient in FTB new claims but not trained in PPL new claims must not process these claims
  • Staff must add the keyword INDOCPPL to the claim

Once the appropriate keyword is added:

  • Do not process the claim
  • Unassign the claim using Second Contact Attempt Required
  • Return the claim to the allocation pool

The claim will be reallocated to an appropriately trained staff member.

2

Action required before contacting customers by phone + Read more ...

Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) can be used to send an SMS to advise a customer, subscribed to Centrelink Electronic messaging, that they will be receiving a phone call.

The 'pre-call' notification will be sent in 'real time' as long as it is created between 8.00am and 8.00pm (using the customers local time). Do not use the 'pre-call' notification outside these times.

If permission to enquire has been given, these may also be sent to the customer's partner or their parent. If so, the message must be generated on the Centrelink record of the partner or parent.

See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging.

3

Customer contact is successful and information can be provided verbally + Read more ...

Advise the customer of the progress of their claim/activity and the next steps required.

Record any information collected over the phone in a progress DOC on the customer's record, including if still waiting for further information or evidence.

If a customer is required to provide documentation, encourage and help them to upload the documents online.

4

Unable to contact customer or customer unable to provide information verbally + Read more ...

  • Update and finalise the customer's contact details outside the claim activity before sending out a request for evidence
  • If the required evidence relates to a shared care/change of care decision, see Item 9
  • Otherwise, issue a formal request:
    • In Process Direct, using Request Documents for DAP and PPL online claims
    • In Customer First, using a Q888 for CCS and ACCS claims
    • In Customer First, using the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure Note: when printing locally, always send a reply paid envelope with requests for additional information
    • For more information see:
      Information requests for FTB, PPL and DAP claims and Add Newborn activities
      Processing Child Care Subsidy (CCS) claims
      Processing Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Temporary Financial Hardship) applications
      Processing Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work) applications
      Processing Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Grandparent) applications
  • Progress DOC/Note must be created/updated with information required and contact attempts made. For more information see Item 12
  • Manually place the claim on hold on the AL screen in Customer First. For more information see Item 7
  • In Customer First - do not complete the Staff Workflows (guided procedures) within an online claim until the claim is ready to be processed. This is to prevent data upload errors and the need for manual coding

5

Streaming early claims + Read more ...

As with post-birth claims, early claims must be checked to ensure the customer has provided all required information and initial eligibility criteria are met, e.g. work test for the Paid Parental Leave scheme.

Tax File Numbers and bank account details are to be provided along with supporting documents to verify payment eligibility before claim finalisation.

See Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments for details.

6

Refugee humanitarian and protection visa holder claimants + Read more ...

Refugee, humanitarian and protection visa holders can make a claim for FTB by lodging a 'Claim form for persons granted a Humanitarian/Refugee visa' (SS415). See Refugee and humanitarian entrant service offer for further details. Note: PPL and DAP cannot be claimed using this form.

  • FTB claims lodged via SS415 are one of the highest priorities. WLM allocates them to the next available Service Officer for priority processing
  • Refer to the FTB Refugee claim DOC created by the interviewing Service Officer for details not included on the SS415 claim form
  • If more information is needed to assess the claim, the Service Officer will contact the author of the FTB Refugee DOC where the SS415 form was received
  • Requests Documents Q777 guided procedure should only be run as a last resort to obtain information. Note: if a Tax File Number (TFN) is required for the FTB claim, contact the author of the FTB Refugee DOC. In most cases, a TFN has been urgently requested from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) and no follow up is required with the customer
  • If it is identified that the claimant may be eligible for other payments (PPL/DAP/Stillborn Baby Payment (SBP)), run ACC and obtain a verbal signature declaration or issue a Families Declaration Form (FDF). See Online or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) for family assistance, Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)

7

Claims/updates requiring further information must be held + Read more ...

Hold claims for the following number of days in these circumstances:

  • For requests for information sent using Request Documents in Process Direct or the Q777 in Customer First (or another prescribed letter), refer to timeframes for resubmit date
  • 7 days for referrals to Policy, Service Support Officers (SSO), Manual Intervention Team (MIV) or Member of a Couple (MoC) assessment
  • 7 days when a Customer First system issue is referred to National ICT Referral Officer (NIRO). The NIRO will annotate the progress DOC with the resolution and email to the referring Service Officer
  • In Process Direct, claims escalated by Roxy Digital Assistant (DA) do not need to be manually held. Roxy DA will automatically hold these claims. If the claim is not automatically placed on hold, hold for 7 days. Service Officers can check the progress of an escalation through the ICT Interaction Viewer (ICTIV) screen in Process Direct
  • 1 day when awaiting an Interstate Transfer (ITST)
  • 14 days when awaiting another claim to be processed (re-hold for another 14 days if necessary)
  • No more than 7 days when waiting for a document to appear as a scan (including rescan requests), or when awaiting other coding to be performed
  • Note: when claims are placed on hold in Process Direct, an ‘On Hold’ reason is required. Where a claim is not automatically placed on hold and/or no other on hold reasons are appropriate, select reason Referral - Families.

8

When retaining a held item in inbox (for Service Officers allocated work from WLM only) + Read more ...

Service Officers receiving work from Workload Management (WLM) should retain held work items in their inbox by setting themselves as the Employee Responsible if the:

  • case is likely to result in political or media attention
  • claim is of such a complex nature that multiple touch points will be necessary
  • case is referred to Service Support Officers (SSO), National ICT Referral Officers (NIRO), policy or ICT
  • case is related to a Customer Relations Unit (CRU) complaint
  • claim is pending an interstate transfer (ITST) or rescan
  • customer is located in another time zone and it is too early or late to contact
  • various claims can be processed by the one staff member (for example Service Officer has been allocated PPL on the customer, partner also has a DAP claim that the Service Officer can process)

If a call relating to a held activity is received and the notes in the progress DOC/Note indicate it has been referred, for example, to ICT, a NIRO or SSO, the activity must not be completed unless the resolution has been received. Service Officers can escalate the activity for actioning as per existing processes if necessary. All work must be finalised as soon as supporting information, documentation or resolution/response is received. Service Officers must action any 'due' claims or activities held in the inbox daily. See Workload Management.

Note: any claims or activities that require further information are to be placed on hold with sufficient information in the progress DOC/Note. When they are 'due' they will be allocated to a Service Officer with the relevant skill tag to be actioned.

9

Shared care/ change of care + Read more ...

If more information is required to make a care decision, all affected parties must be contacted and information obtained/confirmed verbally if possible.

  • If a customer is verbally advising a change in shared care (by phone or at a service centre) or if a work item is allocated for processing, make one genuine attempt to contact the other carer unless an exception applies and it is deemed inappropriate or unnecessary to contact
  • When the customer and/or the other carer cannot be contacted verbally, or disagrees with the advised care arrangement
    • Use the Child - Change in Care Arrangements script to issue requests for evidence of care to each party. This script produces a DOC on the customer's record
    • Complete the DOC recorded by the script
    • Use Fast Note select Auto Text use Families > Claims> Families claim progress DOC
  • Keywords 'SHARED' or 'COCARE' should be added to all shared care and change of care claims and activities to ensure they are allocated correctly in WLM. Online claims where the customer indicates there is a shared care arrangement and reassessments with the source code FA012 will automatically have the 'SHARED' keyword attached to the activity
  • Appropriately skilled staff in Child Support Smart Centres manage all disputed and disagreed care situations
  • Where a new shared care arrangement or change of care is advised in a CCS claim, the shared care coding cannot be completed within the CCS Claim in Process Direct

For more information, see:

  • Customer advises care arrangements for FTB
  • Change of care for FTB
  • Notification and assessment of shared care arrangements for FTB
  • Processing Child Care Subsidy (CCS) claims

10

Tips for using the Child Change in Care Arrangements script: + Read more ...

Using the Child Change in Care Arrangements script

  • Select the correct letter code (Q810). If the customer also receives an income support payment (ISP), ensure the FTB and ISP radio box is selected within the Q810
  • Ensure the correct child names are listed
  • Select the correct contact made by option - Customer or Other party depending on who the letter is being sent to. This ensures the content is correct
  • Select forms or documents to be enclosed (FA012, Calendar etc.) Note: there is an issue with the script when the None option is chosen for the Enclosures section. This option documents that no information is required from the customer and is creating confusion. Pause the script or annotate the DOC to tell other Service Officers what has been requested from the customer. For example, 'Customer has been requested to provide evidence of care'
  • The Details of your child's care arrangements (FA012) form should generally only be sent if shared care information has not been provided within the claim, verbally or in writing. See Change of care for FTB
  • If forms are to be enclosed the letter print job must be cancelled on the HS screen and changed to local print
  • Once the letter is printed locally, all listed forms/documents must be enclosed along with a reply paid envelope
  • If sending multiple letters, for example Request Documents is also being issued requesting other information for the claim, letters should be printed locally and sent in the same envelope so the customer receives all requests for information together

11

Progress DOC/Note + Read more ...

All progress DOCs/Note must have enough information to tell other Service Officers what information and/or documents have been requested and are outstanding.

Online claims - FTB/PPL/DAP/Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS)

An automated progress DOC is generated containing information about what the customer was requested to provide at Next Steps in their online claim. The progress DOC must be annotated with any contact attempts made, extra information required and/or collected verbally, requested by letter or that may impact the claim.

Assisted Customer Claims (ACC) - FTB/PPL/DAP/Stillborn Baby Payment

An automated progress DOC is generated containing information about what the customer was requested to provide at Next Steps in the ACC. The progress DOC must be annotated with how the customer has met the signature requirements and include any extra information collected verbally, requested by letter or that may impact the claim.

Paper claims in Customer First

When the claim is held pending further information, create a Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Families > Claims > Families claim progress DOC. The date of receipt of the progress DOC must be the date the claim was streamed. This will ensure the DOC is chronologically presented for the reader.

Process Direct – CCS/ACCS

When the claim is held pending further information or error resolution, create a progress Note in Process Direct:

  • select and code:
    • Category field, select Claim from the dropdown list
    • Summary field: CCS/ACCS Claim Progress
    • Content:
    • Transaction ID, key current claim ID number
    • CCS claim has been placed on hold [key hold reason]
  • select Save

Paper claims in Process Direct - (Social Online Application (SOA) shell)

When the claim is held pending further information or error resolution, create a progress Note in Process Direct:

  • select and code:
    • Category field, select Claim from the dropdown list
    • Summary field: PPL/DAP Claim Progress
    • Content:
    • Transaction ID, key current claim ID number
    • CCS claim has been placed on hold [key hold reason]
  • select Save

Vulnerable customers

For vulnerable customers, for example customers in crisis, in remote areas, see Intent to claim and vulnerable customers. It is appropriate for the progress DOC/Note to be recorded as a Display on Access (DOA) DOC.

12

Information that must be included in Progress DOCs/Notes + Read more ...

The summary line of the progress DOC/Note must state [Benefit reason (FTB/PPL/etc)] Claim Progress'.

The progress DOC/Note must include the following information:

  • What payment/s the customer is claiming, and the date the claim was lodged
  • Details of all information/documents the customer has been requested to provide, including information already provided verbally
  • How information/documents were requested, for example online claim, ACC, Request Documents in Process Direct, Q777 or Q888
  • The date the information/documents are due
  • Details of any referrals, for example NIRO, policy including reason for referral and referral identification numbers
  • Logons of any additional contacts recorded (Team Leader for direct referrals, or SSO for SSO escalated referrals). See National ICT Referral Officer (NIRO) Process
  • Any customer contact attempts made
  • Any discussions with the customer relevant to the claim
  • Any discussions with the customer about potential entitlement to other payments
  • For ACC - how the claim signature requirements have/will be met - verbal signature declaration or FDF:
    • Once a verbal declaration is completed and the ACC has been submitted, the progress doc no longer needs to be annotated because a doc confirming verbal acceptance of the declaration will automatically display in Process Direct notes, Customer First Super Case and Customer Claim Summary.

Progress DOCs for shared care or change of care claims or reassessments must also include:

  • Details of all evidence/forms/documents requested from both carers
  • The other carer's customer reference number
  • Details of the child and care arrangements being claimed for, for example other carer is claiming 65% care of child xxxxx from xx/xx/xxxx (date)
  • Details of any discussions or any other information provided by the customer relevant to the care assessment

Note: for shared care or change of care assessments, a progress DOC must be recorded on all carers' records.

Processing streamed new claim or activity + Read more ...

When a streamed claim or activity becomes 'due' or claim allocated has all the required information and documentation available to be assessed, processing can be undertaken. 2

Processing online Families claims + Read more ...

Use the following tips to minimise errors and rework:

In Customer First

  • Complete in order all guided procedures within the claim activity. There are very limited reasons to set any of these guided procedures to Not required. Examples are outlined in Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments
  • Do not set the Finalise claim guided procedure to Not Required. Only once this has been run will provisional data be loaded into the new claim (NCL) activity
  • Do not exit the claim activity to manually code data into the new claim activity. If there is a valid reason for additional manual coding in the new claim activity, do this after completing the Finalise Claim guided procedure but before finalising the activity via the Activity Results (AR) screen

In Process Direct

  • For PPL and DAP standalone claims - if the customer provides additional information when processing a claim, it can be updated manually on the relevant screen with the claim activity
  • Do not exit the claim activity to manually code data outside the new claim activity in Process Direct. If there is a valid reason for additional coding outside the new claim activity, this is to be completed in Customer First following the relevant process outlined in Operational Blueprint
  • For CCS/ACCS, claims must be selected from the Process Direct Inbox. Do not select the claim from the Transactions page in Customer Summary of Process Direct. Failure to select claims from the Inbox can result in errors and incorrect assessment of CCS

See:

  • Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments
  • Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims
  • Processing Child Care Subsidy (CCS) claims
  • Processing Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Temporary Financial Hardship) applications
  • Processing Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work) applications
  • Processing Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Grandparent) applications

3

Processing Families paper claims + Read more ...

To ensure information is correctly assessed and recorded on the customer's record, use Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) to record the details from the following paper claims:

  • Claim for Paid Parental Leave and Family Assistance (FA100) (for FTB and combined FTB/PPL claims)
  • Claim for an annual lump sum payment of Family Tax Benefit (FA048)
  • Claim for Bereavement Payment (FA008)

Note: ACC cannot be used to process claims for persons granted a humanitarian or refugee visa (SS415). These are coded manually.

Before starting ACC, update the customer's personal details via the Change Contact Details guided procedure as these questions are no longer asked in ACC.

See Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments.

4

Verbal claims using Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) + Read more ...

A verbal ACC is only to be offered to customers who are unable or unsuitable to complete an online claim, remote and Indigenous customers and customers in crisis if they are experiencing difficulty in understanding or complying with claim requirements, or if they require a speedy outcome to meet immediate financial needs.

The customer must be offered the option to complete a Verbal Signature Declaration. If the Verbal Signature Declaration is not agreed to, the customer will need to sign and return a Families Declaration Form (FDF) before the Service Officer can submit the ACC.

See:

  • Online or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) for family assistance, Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • Helping customers in crisis or financial hardship claim family assistance
  • Helping Indigenous customers claim family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments

5

If the claim outcome is Rejected (REJ) or Not Effective (NEF) + Read more ...

If the provision of additional supporting documents would enable the claim to be reassessed include details of all documents/information outstanding in the REJ/NEF DOC/Note.

6

Office codes + Read more ...

Check the customer's office code is showing the customer's (and partner's) preferred Service Centre.

If the customer has provided a service centre preference within a new claim or activity it must be updated.

This will ensure any future activities are allocated correctly according to National mapping rules.

7

Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) + Read more ...

PPE must be checked and updated if it differs from that advised in the customer's claim.

This will ensure that the partner can make enquiries if authorised or the customer's privacy protected if an existing PPE authority is to be removed.

See Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) authority.

8

Contacting customers + Read more ...

The customer should be contacted by phone in these specific circumstances:

  • If a customer is claiming Family Tax Benefit (FTB) as a lump sum and their income indicates potential entitlement to FTB for the current financial year, discuss with the customer the advantages of claiming as a current zero rate (CZR) customer
  • If the claim outcome is an unfavourable decision or is not a result the customer is expecting, make genuine attempts to contact the customer to explain the decision. See Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK). DOC these attempts on the customer's record. The customer can provide further information that may result in the decision being changed. This can reduce additional contacts and possible escalation through the formal review process. If the decision is unchanged, advise the customer of their appeal and review rights
  • If it is identified the customer may be eligible for another payment or that another payment would be more appropriate for the customer due to a change in circumstances. For example, benefit transfer from JobSeeker Payment (JSP) to Parenting Payment (PP). For more information, see Table 4

When successful contact has occurred, advise the customer:

  • rate of payment (including arrears &/or overpayment)
  • date of effect &/or start date of payment
  • child support implications
  • any complex/unusual issues
  • review and appeal rights
  • self-managed services (registration and promotion)
  • impacts on, or an invitation to claim an income support payment if appropriate
  • notification and reporting requirements if appropriate

DOC any discussions and any attempts to contact the customer.

9

Potential entitlement to another payment + Read more ...

If potential entitlement to another payment, for example Parenting Payment, is determined due to the birth of a new baby or a child entering care, issue a Q999 to the customer.

See Assessing family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme claims for the process to follow and suggested Q999 text.

Finalisation Document + Read more ...

A finalisation DOC:

  • is always required when a claim/activity has been finalised and the claim outcome DOC is not the most recent DOC on the customer's record
  • allows readers to quickly identify the outcome of the claim and refers the Service Officer to the full grant/REJ/NEF DOC
  • is to be clear and succinct, outlining the outcome of the claim and quote the date of the Grant/REJ/NEF DOC

A Note is to be added in Process Direct for CCS/ACCS claims completed in Process Direct.

Summary Line: FTB/PPL/DAP/CCS/ACCS (select appropriate payment type) claim finalised

Detail/Content: FTB/PPL/DAP (select appropriate payment type) claim finalised see separate grant/reject doc dated XX/XX/XX for full details.

Note: the date of receipt of the finalisation DOC is the date the claim was finalised.

4

Non-New Claim work items + Read more ...

If the Service Officer has the appropriate skills, they are to action all other activities on the customer's record. This may include, but is not limited to; open DOCs, Manual Follow-ups (MFUs), advices, reviews, ripple manager activities and reassessments.

It is important to check for both Income Security Integrated System (ISIS) activities (viewable on the Activity List (AL) screen) and SAP native processes, such as Interaction Records.

To action SAP native processes, see Customer First SAP native processes, Customer details and cases and Interaction Records.

To action MFUs, see Processing Manual Follow-ups (MFU) for families.

To action ripple manager activities, see Ripple manager consequences.

Note: some activities such as ripples may not show immediately on a customer record. It is still a requirement, however for these to be actioned as part of the final records check. Further, linked records (particularly for shared care claims) need to be checked to ensure relevant ripples are completed.

To force triggered ripples to display:

  • Go to the Trans/Trigger List (TTL) screen via Nxt: field
  • Key 'R' beside the outstanding RIP activity - then exit the record for one minute to wait for the ripple to display
  • Re-enter the record and complete the rippled activity via the Activity Results (AR)

Inhibited advices:

  • If an advice has been inhibited when processing (for example, rejecting other) a started advice (ADV) or MFU will be created. Send the manual advice where appropriate and complete the activity. If a manual advice is not required, complete the activity with no further action. See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) manual letter activities

3

National ICT Referral Officer (NIRO) Process (claims process in Customer First) + Read more ...

All Customer First FCC claim-related system issues, including tools or guided procedures that are not functioning as intended must be escalated. This is so business owners are made aware of the problem, and a fix can be developed and implemented. Unapproved manual workarounds are not to be attempted.

Service Officers can directly refer to a NIRO in the following circumstances:

  • Record is impacted by a known system issue and an approved resource (NNU, Operational Blueprint etc.) advises that ICT referral is required. These referrals should be made using the 'FCC Direct Known Issue' ICT Referral Request webform
  • Where it has been identified that a mutual Centrelink and Child Support customer may be impacted by the P24845 step-parent claimant issue, these referrals should be made using the 'P24845 Workaround Request' webform

After checking for resolutions or approved workarounds using relevant resources (NNU, Operational Blueprint, mySupport, NIRO Current Issues page) the Service Officer must refer the case to a Service Support Officer (SSO) (unless a known system issue is impacting) in accordance with existing escalation protocols. Where required, the SSO will then escalate to a NIRO.

Note: the NIRO process does not apply to claims processed in Process Direct, as well as Families non-new claim (NNCL) activities, unless otherwise specified.

Known system issues (not including the P24845 step parent claimant issue) can be referred by a Service Officer directly to the NIRO via the 'FCC Direct (Known Issue) ICT Referral Request webform.

Records of a mutual Centrelink and Child Support customer that may be impacted by the P24845 step-parent claimant issue should be made by a Service Officer directly to the NIRO using the 'P24845 Workaround Request' webform.

For any unknown system issue impacting customer outcomes, the SSO will complete the ICT Referral Request form on the Service Officer's behalf and send an email confirming that the ICT referral has been made.

Notes on the Activity List (AL) screen are to include 'referred to FCC NIRO (date), see Progress/ICT Referral doc.'

The progress DOC should be updated with the following information:

  • The date the referral was made
  • A brief description of the issue/problem

Note: if reporting a known issue, include the error code PXXXXX or NNU reference number. If a BUG Error is being reported, include the BUG error code.

The NIRO will then annotate the progress DOC with the ICT referral date and SD number if escalated to ICT.

Once an ICT resolution email is received from the NIRO, any action must be completed before the close of the next business day.

See the National ICT Referral Process Featured content link on the Business Process Branch intranet page. The Resources page has a link.

Roxy Digital Assistant (DA) Process (Process Direct) + Read more ...

All Process Direct Families claim-related, CCS and ACCS NNCL activity system issues must be escalated. This is so business owners are made aware of the problem and a fix can be developed and implemented. Unapproved manual workarounds are not to be attempted.

Service Officers must adopt the Recognise, Research, Support process before escalating to ICT via Roxy DA.

After checking for resolutions or approved workarounds using relevant resources (NNU, Operational Blueprint, mySupport, Process Direct - Current Issues page) and seeking technical support (unless a known system issue is impacting), Service Officers can raise an incident via Roxy DA. See Reporting ICT issues in Process Direct

Roxy DA will record a Note with the details of the incident raised. If there is a started new claim activity in Customer First, Notes on the Activity List (AL) screen are to include 'referred to ICT via Roxy DA, see Note in Process Direct.'

Note: if there is a corresponding NCL activity on the AL screen in Customer First, this must also be placed on hold with the same resubmit date as the Process Direct work item.

The progress DOC should be annotated to advise escalation to ICT has occurred via Roxy DA in Process Direct.

When Roxy DA refers an issue, the Process Direct Business Triage team will review the referral and forward it on to the relevant ICT team. ICT referrals that do not contain enough information will be returned un-actioned.

When the Process Direct Business Triage team or ICT have resolved the referral, an email will be sent to the referring Service Officer to take the appropriate action to complete the claim/activity. Other Service Officers can refer to the ICT Interaction Viewer (ICTIV) screen in Process Direct to check the progress of an escalation.

Level 2 Policy Help Desk referrals + Read more ...

The Help Desk gives policy and procedural advice to support Service Officers.

Current Operational Blueprint procedures may tell staff to escalate an enquiry to the Help Desk. If Operational Blueprint requires a policy escalation, staff may use discretion to either refer these mandatory escalations directly to the Level 2 Policy Help Desk, or seek the support of a Service Support Officer (SSO) who will escalate the enquiry on their behalf.

Service Officers must seek the support of an SSO before escalating enquiries to policy if Operational Blueprint does not advise Level 2 Policy Help Desk referral is required.

Mandatory escalations may include, but are not limited to:

  • Residence exception assessment required for Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • Urgent Tax File Number (TFN) process
  • When FA081 was not issued to the customer at the time of birth
  • Rate of FTB bereavement payment when a child has died overseas
  • Child was not in full-time study at the time of death and the child would have turned 16 during the FTB bereavement period
  • PPL secondary claim rejections
  • Mandatory continuous adjustment (MCA) applied incorrectly
  • Requests for temporary exclusions from debt recovery due to exceptional or unforeseen circumstances, prior to money being withheld from FTB arrears, lump sum or top up payments

Service Officers referring mandatory escalation to Level 2 Policy Help Desk:

  • Referrals must be made via the Level 2 Policy Help Desk online enquiry form, with the Service Officer's Team Leader listed in the Additional Contact Name field
  • Place the claim on hold for 7 days and allocate back to the general pool
  • Record progress DOC with details of the escalation and additional contact details
  • If a response from Level 2 Policy has been received, the Service Officer is to locate the claim and assign it to themselves. All work must be finalised as soon as a resolution/response is received
  • The Service Officer is required to make any required updates based on the Level 2 Policy Help Desk response
  • If the Service Officer cannot action the required updates, it is the responsibility of the Team Leader listed as the additional contact to allocate to another appropriately skilled Service Officer to make the relevant update

SSOs escalating a Level 2 Policy Help Desk enquiry on a Service Officer's behalf:

  • Referrals must be made via the Level 2 Policy Help Desk online enquiry form, with the referring Service Officer listed in the Additional Contact Name field
  • The Positional Mailbox Address field is to include the SSO's relevant positional mailbox
  • When a response from the Level 2 Policy Help Desk has been received, the SSO will update and finalise the activity. The only exception to this would be new claim escalations from processing staff, when the SSO will complete the update and then refer the claim back to the Service Officer to finalise, if available. In the Service Officer's absence, the SSO will finalise the claim
