Resolving Return messages

Table 1

Step

Action

1

1-ACC Item + Read more ...

When the accommodation item keyed is not the appropriate one for the patient’s classification on the date of service involved, the error message 1-ACC ITEM is displayed, and the system highlights the item number and dates of service involved.

The system is unable to calculate the amount payable if the accommodation item claimed is not appropriate in relation to the service details that have been keyed.

Service Officers must examine the account documents to verify the type of accommodation involved and to ensure that all service details have been keyed correctly.

Check keying or associated information:

  • Correct any errors
  • Select [Enter]
  • Change the item number of the date specified by the system to the item number equivalent to the classification
  • Key the appropriate RSN code

For online claims

Rejecting an ICD Line

Private hospitals transmit a ICD line with each transmission, even if the accommodation claimed does not require an ICD e.g. Rehab, NHTP, Psych or Surgical/Advances Surgical admission.

Service Officers must reject the ICD line by typing ‘S’ in the P field.

2

1-ACC NOT + Read more ...

When the hospital accommodation item is being processed and the service it represents is not in the contract that applies on the date of service, the error message 1-ACC NOT is displayed and the system highlights the item number and dates of service involved.

Not all hospitals provide the full range of hospital accommodation services that are available e.g. medical care, surgical care, palliative care, sleep disorder, intensive care, cardiac care, psychiatric care, dialysis care. Hospitals are contracted to provide the types of care for which they have the facilities and expertise.

Service Officers must examine the account documents and ensure that the provider number/service details have been keyed correctly.

If all of the service details have been keyed correctly:

  • If additional information is available from the account documents, use the DVA Hospital Contracts Enquiry HCVS/VAMM System and the DVA hospital item lists to determine the correct accommodation item
  • If additional information is not available from the account documents, seek clarification from the hospital or reject the services with reason code 201 (service claimed not covered under current contract)

After the Enter key is pressed, the system calculates any amount payable and the payment process continues in the normal manner.

3

1-ADM DATE + Read more ...

Where the system cannot uniquely match the services being processed to a prior approval record and the date of service involved is prior to an existing prior approval record that has no admission date recorded, the message 1-ADM DATE is displayed and the first item number involved is highlighted.

Service Officers must check the information held on the patient’s prior approval record (VEPI) screen to identify any relevant prior approval records.

When identifying relevant PARS records on the VEPI screen, use the following guidelines:

  • If the claim being processed is a new admission, look for a PARS record that has an effective date that is close to the admission date. Old PARS records that have not had discharge dates inserted do not need to be considered for new admissions.
  • If the claim being processed is a continuation claim, look for a PARS record that has an effective date close to the admission date shown on the claim form rather than considering the accommodation From date involved
  • If the patient has been transferred to another hospital, look for the latest PARS record into which the dates of service shown on the claim form can fall

Note: It is possible for a prior approval record with a later effective date to be the relevant PARS record. This can occur when the patient is actually admitted to hospital a few days before the effective date of the PARS record.

If there is no prior approval, record available on the system that relates to the admission claimed:

  • If the dates of service are older than 2 weeks from the date of lodgement, reject the services involved with reason code 279 (prior approval not sought item requires prior approval)
  • If the dates of service are within 2 weeks of the date of lodgement, contact Veterans' Affairs Processing Helpdesk to arrange for a prior approval record to be raised

If an approval record is present, but has been rejected e.g. client not eligible, reject the service/s with reason code 289 - prior approval sought but not approved for this item.

If an approval record is not present, email the claim to the VAP Policy Section - Tier 3 for appropriate PARS action. If after 4 weeks DVA have not made a decision regarding the payment of the claim, the claim should be rejected with reason code 279 - DVA Prior Approval not present - contact DVA. See DVA - Provider enquiries for contact details.

Note: if dates are missing on PARS, Service Officers who have the appropriate access can add the admission date via the VAMM transaction.

4

1-DATE ERR + Read more ...

When the date of service keyed for an accommodation item overlaps with another accommodation service line on the payment screen or the patient’s claims history, the error message 1-DATE ERR is displayed and the system highlights the item number and dates of service involved.

The system is unable to calculate the number of bed days for which payment is to be made when the dates of service in accommodation lines overlap. The Service Officer must examine the account documents to verify the dates and number of bed days involved.

The action to be taken depends on the particular situation involved.

5

1-EXP CONT + Read more ...

When a new admission or readmission is being processed and the dates of service immediately follow those in a previous period of hospitalisation which has a continuation disposal code, the error message 1-EXP CONT is displayed and the system highlights the item number and dates of service involved.

Service Officers must examine the account documents to:

  • Determine the correct admission indicators and disposal codes for the accommodation lines involved
  • Change the admission indicators and/or disposal codes to reflect the situation involved

After the [Enter] key is pressed, the system calculates the amount payable and the payment process continues in the normal manner.

6

1-EXP NEW + Read more ...

When a continuation accommodation line is being processed and the dates of service immediately follow those in a previous period of hospitalisation, which has a discharge disposal code, the error message 1-EXP NEW is displayed and the system highlights the item number and dates of service involved.

Service Officers must examine the account documents and determine the correct admission indicator that applies. Take the following actions:

  • If the account documents indicate a new admission is involved, key admission indicator N (new admission) in the AD field and continue processing
  • If the account indicates a readmission is involved, key admission indicator R readmission) in the AD field and continue processing
  • If the account indicates that the claim is a continuation claim, key admission indicator R (readmission) in the AD field and continue processing

Note: when the account documents indicate that the claim is a continuation of a previous claim, it means that the disposal code keyed for the previous period of hospitalisation was incorrect. Keying R (readmission) in the AD field in this situation ensures that the bed days involved are included as part of the original stepdown counts.

1-INV BULK + Read more ...

When the number of bed days entered in the NO field does not correspond with the number of days in the accommodation period being processed, the error message 1- INV BULK is displayed and the system highlights the item number and number of beds involved.

Service Officers must examine the account documents to identify any leave days/billing errors and to ensure that the service details have been keyed correctly. The action that needs to be taken depends on the particular situation involved.

General

Hospitals may transmit Online claims with missing or incorrect data such as ‘TO’ date, NO of days and Admission/Discharge indicators.

Service Officers are required to tidy up transmitted data by inserting the missing information.

Correcting Accommodation Dates

When a Hospital transmits claims online, the accommodation may be transmitted with overlapping days. Services Officers must change the information according to the number of days claimed.

1-INV DISP + Read more ...

When an invalid/inconsistent discharge/separation (disposal) code is applied to the ‘R’ field, the 1-INV DISP message will display. When the ‘Day Only’ flag on the HCVS is ‘Y’, only a single period of accommodation can logically apply, so the discharge code ‘S’ (still inpatient) is invalid.

12

1-INV AD + Read more ...

When an invalid/inconsistent admission code is applied to the ‘AD’ field, the 1-INV AD message will display. When the Day Only flag on the Item Fee File is ‘Y’, the admission code for the service must be set to ‘N’ for new.

1-ITEM NOT + Read more ...

When a package deal item or a fixed price (miscellaneous) item is being processed and the item is not in the contract that applies on the date of service, the error message 1-ITEM NOT is displayed and the ITEM field involved is highlighted.

Note: not all hospital contracts contain packages and/or fixed price (miscellaneous) items. Whether a contract contains package and/or fixed price items depends on the contract negotiated with the individual hospital.

Service Officers must examine the account documents and ensure that the provider number/service details have been keyed correctly. If all of the service details have been keyed correctly:

  • If additional information is available from account documents, use the DVA Hospital Contracts Enquiry HCVS/VAMM System to determine the correct item number
  • If additional information is not available from the account documents, seek clarification from the hospital or reject the services with reason code 201 (service not covered under current contract - contact DVA)

 

14

1-LST DT + Read more ...

When a hospital accommodation or same day accommodation line is being processed and a date has not been keyed in the TO field or the date keyed in the TO field is invalid, the error message 1-LST DT is displayed and the incorrect details are highlighted.

The message 1-LST DT can be displayed in a number of different situations and the actions to be taken varies depending on the particular situation involved.

They can be grouped as follows:

  • A date has not been keyed in the TO field when it is required or a ‘?’ is present
  • The date keyed in the TO field is invalid e.g. not valid date, prior to the date keyed in the DOS field, the incorrect year, date keyed when not required

15

1-OTH CLM + Read more ...

When the system detects that:

  • The date of service for a procedure item being processed is the same as the date of service for a procedure item in another claim, or
  • The date of service for the procedure item being processed falls into an accommodation period in another claim

The error message 1-OTH CLM displays and the accommodation procedure items and date of service involved are highlighted.

Note: if the conflict is between the procedure item claimed and an accommodation period in another claim, the information displayed at the bottom of the screen will provide details of the accommodation period/patient classification involved.

Service Officers must locate the other claim before a decision can be made regarding the processing action to be taken. The other claim may have already been paid or may still be in pend awaiting finalisation.

When attempting to locate the other claim, check the patient’s claims history to determine whether the other claim involved has been paid:

  • If the other claim has been paid, review the claim documents involved and determine the reason why the second claim was lodged e.g. it could be an amended account or a request for an adjustment
  • If the other claim has not been paid and it is still awaiting pend resolution, check the paperwork for the other claim to determine if this claim can be paid

PSOs may use the VHSA transaction to take the appropriate action needed to correct the VHSA screen.

16

1-RE GT 7 + Read more ...

When a readmission is being processed and the admission date keyed is more than 7 days after the previous discharge date, the error message 1-RE GT 7 displays and the system highlights the item number and dates of service involved.

Because the conflict is between the admission indicator keyed and the disposal code on a previous hospital segment, the PF11-CALC key is pressed to swap to the Pend Resolution Calculation Screen where the admission indicator will be displayed.

Services Officers must examine the account documents and then take the following actions:

  • Check keying of service details (especially the admission date) and ensure that the admission indicator keyed is correct for the situation involved
  • If keying is correct, change the admission indicator in the AD field from R (readmission) to N (new admission). Before changing the admission code:
    • check with the hospital that the veteran was not transferred from another hospital, or
    • if there was a previous accommodation that has not been submitted
  • After the [Enter] key is pressed, the system calculates the amount payable and the payment process continues in the normal manner

17

1-SVC MISS + Read more ...

When a hospital accommodation line/s is being processed and the system detects that a hospital accommodation period is missing, the error message 1-SVC MISS is displayed and the system highlights the subsequent accommodation period involved.

The message 1-SVC MISS displays in a number of different situations and the actions to be taken may vary depending on the particular situation involved:

  • A continuation accommodation period is being processed and the dates of service do not immediately follow on from the previous accommodation period
  • A continuation or readmission is being processed and there is no previous accommodation period on the screen or the patient’s claims history
  • A readmission is being processed and the previous accommodation period did not have a discharge disposal code

Check keying, correct any errors and select [Enter]. If keying is correct and the patient has been on leave:

  • key the necessary leave days and select [Enter] (manual), or
  • contact the provider and advise them to resubmit the claim (online)

If keying is correct and an interim accommodation claim is missing:

  • attempt to obtain the missing claim prior to processing this claim

1-THTR NOT + Read more ...

When theatre fee item M002 is being processed and the theatre band associated with the MBS procedure item entered is not in the contract that applies on the date of service, the error message 1-THTR NOT displays and the system highlights the theatre fee item number (M002) and date of service involved.

Because the error relates to the theatre band involved, the PF11-CALC key is pressed to swap to the Pend Resolution Calculation screen where the theatre band will be highlighted.

Note: not all hospital contracts contain all of the theatre bands that are available. The range of theatre bands included in a particular contract depends on the types of procedures the hospital is contracted to perform. 

If the item has a theatre band, refer the MBS items to the VAP Helpdesk with:

  • a request to ensure the effective date of the theatre band is the same as the effective date of the HCVS Service level on QITI
  • use Shift [F9] or [F11], then [F7]

2-3B DAYS? + Read more ...

When a CERT (Acute Care (3B) Certificate) line has been keyed and the number of days calculated by the system exceeds 30 days or the patient has been discharged prior to the To date on the CERT line and that discharge date has been recorded on the patient’s prior approval (VEPI) record, the error message 2-3B DAYS? displays and the system highlights details on the CERT line.

The Service Officer must examine the account documents and ensure that the service details (including Acute Care (3B) Certificate details and disposal code) have been keyed correctly. 

20

4-PAR NFND + Read more ...

Where there is no prior approval record present for the services being processed or the prior approval record for the services being processed has been rejected, the error message 4-PAR NFND displays and the first item number involved is highlighted.

Service Officers must display the prior approval record (VEPI) screen for the patient involved to determine whether a record exists that has been rejected:

If a rejected prior approval record exists for the service involved:

  • and the record has been rejected on eligibility grounds e.g. the Comments field states 'patient not eligible', reject the service claimed with reason code 211 (patient not eligible at date of service)
  • If the record has been rejected for any other reason, reject the service claimed with reason code 289 (prior approval sought but not granted for this item)
  • If a prior approval record covering the services involved does not exist, reject the service involved with reason code 279 (prior approval not sought - item requires prior approval)

Refer these claims to the Veterans' Affairs Processing Helpdesk for PARS to be placed onto the system. If after 4 weeks there has been no response from DVA, the claim is then rejected.

Note: the message 4-PAR NFND can also be displayed when the patient has moved interstate and the prior approval record is on the original file number. In these situations, use the Name Search (VNSI) transaction to identify the other file number involved.

 

21

4-PAR NFND/2-CHK 3B? + Read more ...

For Online claims

If the error message 4-PAR NFND or 2-CHK 3B? are received, and a certificate has been transmitted, the certificate may have attached to the incorrect period of accommodation or the PARS record may not have been created. A PARS record is present, but associated with the incorrect episode.

Enter ,p at the end of the command line to reselect the patient.

This allows the system to reassess the claim and create PARS or allow 3B Cert to match to correct PARS. When the message ‘UPDATE OK’ shows on the screen, this indicates the patient has cleared and the claim is now available for assessing.

4-PAR NMCH + Read more ...

Service Officers must check the information held on the patient’s prior approval record (VEPI) screen before a decision is made to either accept or reject the services involved.

When identifying the PARS records on the VEPI screen that need to be considered for the claim being processed, use the following guidelines:

  • If the claim being processed is a new admission, look for a PARS record that has an effective date which is close to the admission date. Old PARS records that have not had discharge dates inserted do not need to be considered for new admissions
  • If the claim being processed is a continuation claim, look for a PARS record that has effective date close to the admission date shown on the claim form rather than considering the accommodation from date involved
  • If the patient has been transferred to another hospital, look for the latest PARS record into which the dates of service shown on the claim form can fall

If there is no prior approval record available on the system which relates to the admission claimed, reject the service involved with reason code 279 (prior approval not sought - item requires prior approval).

If a relevant PARS record has been identified, it should be accessed and the Comments field checked. Pressing the PF5-ACCEPT key will display further messages identifying why the services do not uniquely match the PARS record involved. 

PAR NMCH messages will also appear if there is an open ended prior approval record. Service Officers, who have the VAMM access, must go into the VAMM transaction to end date any open ended prior approval records. Services Officers that do not have access to VAMM must ask a VAP PSO to update VAMM for them. The system will generate pseudo PARS for gold card holders in contracted Hospitals.

7-LONG STY + Read more ...

When the system detects that a patient has been in hospital for more than 35 continuous days, the error message 7-LONG STY is displayed (payment cannot be made at the normal bed day rate for days in excess of 35 unless an Acute Care (3B) Certificate is supplied). The date of the first accommodation day that needs to be covered by the 3B Certificate is shown as part of the message e.g. 7-LONG STY 050805.

Payment cannot be made at the normal bed rate for days in excess of 35 unless an Acute Care (3B) Certificate is supplied. If an Acute Care (3B) Certificate has not been supplied, the days in excess of 35 are rejected S294 or a lower level (nursing home type) payment is made H271

Note: when the message 7-LONG STY is displayed, the system will have taken into account any relevant Acute Care (3B) Certificate periods that are already recorded on patient’s prior approval (VEPI) record.

For Online claims

Acute Care 3B Certificate

Hospitals are only required to submit Acute Care (3B) certificates for stays longer than 35 days of hospitalization or with a break of no more than 7 days.

If hospitals submit claims for non-acute care e.g. rehab, NHTP, Psych, Cert days are not required and can be deleted.

Key a Manual 3B Cert

If required, a 3B Certificate can be keyed manually using the same claim ID number with a letter ‘C’ at the end.

For example, if the claim ID number is A1234@, use claim ID number A1234C to key in the 3B certificate.

8-CHK CONT + Read more ...

When the number of bed days entered in the NO field equals the actual number of days in the hospital accommodation period being processed, the warning message 8-CHK CONT displays and the number of bed days involved is highlighted.

Service Officers must examine the account documents to confirm whether the patient is still an inpatient of the hospital on the date involved. Take the following actions:

If information on the account documents confirm that the patient is still in hospital, press the PF9-NO WARN key to acknowledge the warning message. The explanation for reason code 296 will be printed on the Statement of Benefit after the claim has been paid.

If information on the account documents indicate that the patient has been discharged, seek clarification from the hospital regarding the number of days claimed. Where the patient is discharged, the date of admission and date of discharge are considered to be one day for payment purposes. 

25

8-CHK HIST + Read more ...

When the system detects that an item that has limitations on the number of services payable in a specified period is claimed, the warning message 8-CHK HIST is displayed and the item number/s involved is highlighted.

In these situations, it is necessary to check QITI to establish if there are any limitation/restrictions involved. If there are no restrictions, press the [PF9-NO WARN] key to pay the services involved.

26

8-NON CON + Read more ...

When the system detects that the services being claimed have been performed in a non-contracted private hospital, the warning message 8-NON CON displays and the hospital provider number and item numbers involved are highlighted.

Payment can only be made if the hospital treatment provided is related to the medical condition/s specified in the Comments field on the prior approval record. Payment can only be made at the rate (if any) specified in the prior approval record as this is the rate agreed to by DVA for the admission.

Online Claims

The ECLIPSE Online Claiming facility allows providers of hospital services to send messages that contain claims for payment to private health Funds.

It passes signed encrypted messages in XML format via the Internet between the providers, the ECLIPSE workflow engine (the Hub) and the Private Health Funds.

The IHC DVA Online Hospital claiming system utilizes the ECLIPSE framework. Claims can be submitted 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

The Claim ID is generated by the ECLIPSE Hub and transmitted back to the provider once the claim has been successfully received by the mainframe. In order to retrieve the claim ID, the client must retrieve the status report.

The claims submitted by a Hospital provider to DVA through the ECLIPSE system are transmitted in segments. There are 19 types of segments permitted in the IHC. Each segment is distinguished by a three letter abbreviated segment title.

Online Hospital claims are to be accessed using the State Online Source Office Code.

The state online source office codes to be used to request claims from the other States e.g. CVZY, EVZY, KVZY, IVZY, GVZY.

The only field that cannot be edited for online hospital claims processing is the charge. All other fields may be altered to effect processing of the claim.

Additional lines cannot be inserted into online claims. All lines (excluding Cert lines) cannot be deleted.

No claims submitted online should be paid manually.
