Customer withdraws claim, lodges duplicate claim or claims too early

Table 1: This table describes the actions to take when a customer wants to withdraw their PPL claim, lodges a duplicate claim, or lodges a claim outside the allowable early claim period.

Portions of this process are for Families and Child Care Smart Centre Processing staff only.

Item

Description

1

Claim level withdrawal + Read more ...

If the PPL claim has been assessed (e.g. ASS-PCB or ASS-ASD), see Item 2

Customers can only withdraw an effective claim for PPL before their payment status is CURrent for that child. See Withdrawal of Claims

Discuss the reasons for the withdrawal request with the customer and, where appropriate, offer any further referrals or assistance.

Advise the customer that once their claim has been withdrawn:

  • it will include all components of PPL (that is, both the PPL period and Flexible PPL days), and
  • it cannot be reinstated. If the customer later changes their mind they must complete a new claim

Note: if a customer is requesting to withdraw a claimed Flexible PPL day:

  • it will be returned to their unclaimed Flexible PPL days balance, and
  • they can claim it for another date that is within 2 years of the child’s birth or adoption

To action a request to withdraw or update a claimed Flexible PPL day, see Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days.

Is the customer requesting to withdraw their PPL at a claim level?

  • Yes, and the claim is:
    • PPL-ASS Claims, see Item 2
    • PPL-CUR, the customer’s claim must be cancelled. See Cancellation of Parental Leave Pay (PPL). Procedure ends here.
  • No, see Item 3

2

Withdrawal of ASSessed claim + Read more ...

Claims that are assessed and not current, such as ASS-PCB or ASS-ASD, can be withdrawn

PPL claims can be withdrawn at either a:

  • Claim level - the full 18 weeks of PPL including any Flexible PPL days, or
  • Day Level - Flexible PPL days. Note: these days will need to be updated using the PPL change in circumstances

Customers can request to withdraw their claim for PPL or Flexible PPL days.

Manually update the P1CA screen:

  • Action: REJ (rejected)
  • Reason: CLR (Withdraw/Voluntary surrender)
  • Effect Date: the date of receipt (DOR) for the pre-claim/post claim
  • LVL: Claim level (for Flexible PPL Days. See Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days
  • Notes: record the relevant details

DOC the customer's record confirming:

  • the request to withdraw the claim and
  • they were advised to submit a new claim for PPL if they change their mind

3

Customer lodges duplicate PPL claim + Read more ...

Check if this is a duplicate claim see if the information in the subsequent claim is the same as the original claim.

If the customer has provided new information about their PPL, this information will not be updated within the duplicate claim. This is to actioned as a change of circumstances.

If the customer:

  • has provided new information about their PPL, e.g. advising a return to work, see Customer notifies a change of circumstances for Paid Parental Leave Pay (PPL) once the duplicate claim has been finalise
  • is making changes to their Flexible PPL days, including changing/claiming additional Flexible PPL days, see Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days once the duplicate claim has been fialised

If a customer:

  • has lodged a previous effective claim, and
  • subsequently lodges a duplicate claim for the same child with the same information,
  • see Table 2

4

Claim lodged outside allowable early claim period + Read more ...

If the pre-birth claim was lodged outside the allowable early claim period, the claim must be made NEF.

The online claim prevents claims from being submitted if the customer is not expecting a child to come into their care within 97 days.

However, customers are not prevented from lodging a Claim for Parental Leave Pay and Family Tax Benefit (FA100) form outside the allowable timeframe.

Processing paper claims where PPL is NEF

  • Cancel claim activity
  • Update any new details provided by the customer in the claim (phone, address, income estimates)
  • Use Fast Note to record details of the claim outcome
    e.g. claim outcome is NEF, any other changes made to entitlements
  • Send a manual Q999 letter to the customer advising the outcome. See Resources page for suitable Q999 text

The customer will be required to complete a new claim within the allowable early claim period.

NEF assessments, coding NEF and following customer contact

Table 2: This table contains information about automatic NEF assessments, manual NEF coding, and what to do when a customer contacts after their PPL claim has been made NEF.

Portions of this process are for Families and Child Care Smart Centre Processing staff only.

Step

Action

1

NEF assessments + Read more ...

Pre-birth and post-birth PPL claims can be made NEF.

In most cases, the NEF will occur automatically when processing the claim. However, manual coding can be completed in certain circumstances.

Automatic NEF assessments

For PPL pre-birth claims that have been assessed and are awaiting proof of the child's birth or entry to care, a reminder letter will be sent 14 days after the nominated start date to provide the proof.

If the proof is not provided within 56 days of the nominated start date, the claim will be automatically assessed as NEF.

(PPL already claimed by customer

For duplicate PPL claims lodged for children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020 where the full 18 weeks was not paid,any subsequent claims will automatically assess as Not Effective (NEF) when processed.

Claims will be assessed as NEF-PAC (PPL already claimed by customer), if the customer’s full PPL entitlement has not been paid.

For duplicate PPL claims lodged for children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020 where the maximum PPL entitlement has been paid, any subsequent claims will automatically assess as rejected REJ-PAS (PPL already paid to customer) when processed.

This includes claims cancelled at the customer’s request (CAN-CRQ). Note: if a customer’s original claim was CAN-CRQ, the duplicate claim will automatically NEF.

Note: if the customer has previously requested their initial PPL claim for the child be cancelled CRQ, this decision cannot be appealed. See Cancellation of Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

If the customers advises the CRQ cancellation is incorrect, consider if the payment should be restored. For more information, see Restoration of Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

For duplicate claims lodged for children born or adopted before 1 July 2020, complete a manual NEF assessment. Go to Step 4.

Customer contact

If the customer contacts after their claim was made NEF, go to Step 5.

2

Manual NEF assessment + Read more ...

Check the customer has met all general requirements for an effective claim.

Is further information required to make the claim effective?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, see Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave payments. Procedure ends here

3

Request information/documentation + Read more ...

Contact the customer as soon as possible and advise what information is required to determine their claim.

If further information was required for the online claim, the customer may have already been advised to provide this information on the Next steps page of their claim.

Check if the information has already been requested from the customer, e.g. on the Next steps page of the online claim.

Consider contacting the customer by phone if the information required to finalise the claim can be provided by phone immediately.

Prior to calling, Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) may be used to advise the customer they will be receiving a call. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging.

If details cannot be provided by phone and/or have not already been requested, send a Q777 via the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure for the customer to provide the requested information/documents within 14 days. Allow extra time for mail delivery.

Requests for bank account details and Tax File Number (TFN) must be held for 28 days.

Allow extra time for mail delivery for resubmit dates

Has the required information/documentation been received by the resubmit date?

  • Yes, see Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave payments. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Manual NEF coding + Read more ...

Do not manually NEF a duplicate claim while there is an existing PPL claim for the same child with a status of ASSessed. If the PPL claim status is ASSessed, the current/original PPL claim will need to be processed/finalised before the NEF claim can be actioned.

To manually NEF a PPL claim with status other than ASS:

  • Select the PPL claim
  • While processing the claim, go to the PPL Claim Summary (P1CS) screen. Current PPL claim information will display
  • Key 'A' to go to the PPL Claim Action (P1CA) screen
  • Manually update the P1CA screen:
    • Action: select NEF (not effective)
    • Reason: select the appropriate manual NEF code
    • Effect Date: the date of receipt (DOR) of the pre-claim/post-claim
    • Notes: outline the relevant details

An error message will display if the information in each field does not correspond correctly with the NEF reason.

Note: if the customer has been granted PPL for the child in another claim, ensure the payment is not incorrectly cancelled/NEF as a result of the NEF action within the duplicate claim.

Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen and use Fast Note to record the outcome.

An automatic letter will be sent to the customer to advise the outcome, except where the reason CAM (Claim already made) is selected. Where CAM is selected a Manual Follow Up (MFU) will be created advising the Service Officer that a manual Q999 will need to be sent to the customer. See the Resources page for Q999 text.

If an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) cannot be used for the paper claim

If:

  • the customer has not provided the requested information (such as nominated start date for their PPL period) within the allowable timeframe, and
  • there is not enough information contained in the paper claim to use the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC):
    • make any required updates of new details that have been provided by the customer in the new claim (phone, address, estimates)
    • use Fast Note to record the claim outcome, e.g. claim not effective (NEF), any other changes made to entitlement
    • send a manual Q999 letter to the customer advising the outcome. See Resources page for suitable Q999 text

Procedure ends here.

5

Customer contacts about their NEF claim + Read more ...

Has the customer provided all the requested information required to make the claim effective?

Check the customer’s record to determine why their claim was not effective (NEF).

Refer to the NEF DOC and/or letters for the explanation.

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, advise the customer:
    • What information is required for their claim to be effective (refer to the progress DOC/letter sent to the customer to see the requested documents required to complete the claim).
    • Their claim can only be assessed from the date all the required information is received
    • Procedure ends here

6

Index a new claim + Read more ...

The date of receipt (DOR) used when re-indexing the claim is the date the requested information was lodged.

Non-FCC processing staff:

  • Using Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), index the new claim using the new information and the information from the initial claim
  • When submitted, this will be allocated by WLM to an appropriately trained staff member to process
  • Include details in the Progress DOC

FCC processing staff:

  • Using Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), index the new claim using the new information the information from the initial claim

If all requested information has been provided for a:

  • NEF pre-birth claim, go to Step 7
  • NEF pre-birth claim and the child has now been born/entered into care, and all relevant information has been provided to make an effective post-birth claim, go to Step 8
  • NEF post-birth claim, go to Step 8

7

Indexing NEF pre-birth claims + Read more ...

If all requested information has been provided:

  • Within 45 days of the date the pre-birth claim was made NEF and the financial year has not changed since then, the claim can be assessed using the new information and the information from the initial claim
    • DOR = the date the new information was provided
    • Contact the customer if there are any concerns that their circumstances have changed since the initial claim was submitted
  • Within 45 days of the date the pre-birth claim was made NEF, and the financial year has changed, obtain the relevant financial year income for the new PPL assessment
  • After day 45 and in the same financial year in which the initial pre-claim was made NEF
    • DOR = the date the new information was provided
    • Contact the customer before processing the pre-claim. Prior to calling, Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) may be used to advise the customer they will be receiving a phone call. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging
    • If the child has not yet been born or entered care, contact the customer before processing the claim to confirm the remaining information from the initial claim
    • If the child has now been born/entered care, the customer will need to provide all information relevant to make an effective post-birth claim including the child details, birth verification and return to work details
  • After day 45 and the financial year has changed, advise the customer they must make a new claim. Procedure ends here

If the pre-birth claim has been NEF-POB, a new claim must be generated using Assisted Customer Claim.

  • DOR = the date the post birth information has been provided. This may result in the customer’s PPL nominated start date being changed to a later date
  • Use information from the initial claim to recreate and finalise the claim

Claim finalisation

See Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave payments.

Procedure ends here.

8

Indexing NEF post birth claims + Read more ...

If all requested information has been provided:

  • Within 45 days of the date the claim was made NEF, the claim can be reassessed using the new information and the information from the initial claim
    • DOR = the date the new information was provided
    • Contact the customer if there are any concerns that circumstances have changed since the initial claim was submitted
  • More than 45 days since the claim was made NEF:
    • DOR = the date the new information was provided
    • Contact the customer before processing the claim. Check if any details have changed. Prior to calling, Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) may be used to advise the customer they will be receiving a phone call. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging
    • If no details have changed and the customer still meets all eligibility including primary care, return to work, etc. use the information from the initial claim
    • If the customer’s circumstances have changed, request they lodge a new claim to be assessed for PPL. Procedure ends here

If the customer has previously provided proof of birth and it is scanned on the record, it can be used for the claim. View the scanned image via Document Tools in Customer First.

Verification of birth can be used to process the new claim once confirmation has been received that the customer has not returned to work.

Claim finalisation

See Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave payments.

Procedure ends here

Manual checks and recording details

Table 3: This table describes the steps to follow before recording the details of a not effective, withdrawn or rejected PPL claim.

Portions of this process are for Families and Child Care Smart Centre Processing staff only.

Item

Action

1

Manual rejection coding + Read more ...

Pre-birth and post-birth PPL claims can be rejected. Generally this will happen automatically based on the customer’s eligibility for payment. However, in some cases a manual rejection may be required.

Ensure the customer is not eligible for PPL and the reason to reject is correct.

If a manual rejection needs to be recorded:

  • Select the PPL claim
  • While processing the claim, go to the PPL Claim Summary (P1CS) screen. Current PPL claim information will display
  • Key 'A' to go to the PPL Claim Action (P1CA) screen
  • On the P1CA screen:
    • Action: select REJ (reject)
    • Reason: select the appropriate manual rejection code
    • Effect Date: key the date the rejection is to commence
    • Notes: outline the relevant details for the cancellation

An error message will display if the information in each field is not consistent with the rejection reason.

Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen

If the manual rejection code is OTH, send the customer a manual free text (Q999) letter advising their claim is rejected and the reason. When creating the Q999:

  • Select PPL as the Service Reason
  • Select option 1 to include the PPL Act reference. This will include the correct 28 day Appeal paragraph

Procedure ends here.

2

Customer contacts to discuss PPL rejection + Read more ...

Review the claim details to see why the claim was rejected. Refer to the REJ DOC and/or letters for the explanation.

Discuss PPL eligibility requirements with the customer. There may be other eligibility requirements that the customer has not met, in addition to the specified rejection reason. Discuss general eligibility in addition to the specified reason. Generally, to be eligible for PPL, the customer must meet the work test, income test, primary care, etc.

If the customer:

  • has supporting evidence to allow the claim to be reassessed, advise the customer about PPL review and appeal timeframes and what evidence is required to reassess the REJ assessment
  • does not have supporting evidence to allow the claim to be reassessed, advise them about PPL review and appeal rights
  • believes the decision to reject their PPL claim is incorrect and no additional information is required for their new claim to be reassessed, see Item 5

3

Customer contacts regarding REJ-PAS assessment + Read more ...

REJ-PAS (PPL already paid to customer)

For children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020 where the full PPL entitlement was paid, any subsequent claims will automatically assess as Reject (REJ) when processed.

If a customer contacts to discuss the REJ-PAS outcome, check the customer's record to confirm all PPL payments that the customer was eligible to receive have been paid.

4

Customer contacts to have PPL reassessed: PPL rejected BNR - birth not registered + Read more ...

Is the claimant advising they have registered or applied to register their child’s birth?

  • Yes, index a new claim:
    • Post birth claims can be re-indexed once the customer advises they have registered or applied to register the birth of their child. This can be provided verbally
    • If the customer submitted a pre-birth claim and used the Newborn Child Declaration (FA081) form or Add Newborn child online service or mobile application, a new claim activity will need to be created using the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC)
    • The date of receipt of the claim is the date the customer confirmed the birth registration. If this is within 28 days of the child's date of birth (DOB), the nominated start date may be the child’s DOB or a later date as elected by the customer. If the customer advises outside 28 days of the child's DOB, date of receipt is the date they advise
    • The claim will grant after it has been allocated and fully reassessed with the birth registration status updated from No to Yes. In some circumstances the claim may reject for another reason, e.g. returned to work since becoming the primary carer (RWP). See Item 5 for Rejection reasons other than BNR
  • No, the claimant must register or apply to register the birth of their child before PPL can be granted for the child. If the customer does not understand or does not agree with the decision, and they want to request an explanation or apply for a formal review, see PPL employee initiated reviews and appeals. Procedure ends here

5

Customer contacts to have PPL reassessed (PPL rejected for reason other than birth registration) + Read more ...

Advise the customer what information is required for their claim to be reassessed. Refer to the progress/rejection DOC for additional information. Alternatively, the customer may advise they have additional information to provide which may change the outcome of the claim.

If additional information is not available to allow their claim to be reassessed, and they want to request an explanation or apply for a formal review, see PPL employee initiated reviews and appeals. Procedure ends here. If additional information is available to allow their claim to be reassessed, and all of the information:

  • Has not been provided, advise the customer what information needs to be provided for their claim to be reassessed.
    Procedure ends here
  • Has been provided, generally for the initial PPL claim to be re-indexed the customer needs to provide all requested information within 28 days of the decision being made. In some circumstances the customer may require more than 28 days to provide all of the required information, e.g. evidence from a specialist.

Did the customer provide all of the requested documents/ information within 28 days, or is the Service Officer satisfied the customer needed more time?

  • Yes, a reassessment of the customer’s PPL entitlement can be completed. Re-index the original claim, using the original date of receipt (DOR) of the claim. Contact the customer if there are concerns or circumstances that have changed since the initial claim was submitted. See Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave payments
  • No, advise the customer they can request an explanation or apply for a formal review. See PPL employee initiated reviews and appeals. Procedure ends here
