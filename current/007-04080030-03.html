Conducting compulsory participation interview with PPS customer

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Compulsory Participation Interview + Read more ...

  • Attempt to contact the customer at the scheduled appointment time. Service Officers should create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). After creating the SMS, wait 1 to 2 minutes before making the outbound attempt
  • If unable to contact the customer on the first attempt, try again approximately 15 minutes later. (Two genuine contact attempts must be made to the customer)
  • Sufficient detail about the outbound contact attempts must be included in a DOC

Has the customer attended the phone appointment?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 2

Where no appointment has been booked due to customer not having current telephone contact details:

  • An email will be sent to the relevant Service Zone for action
  • The email must be actioned within 48 hours to ensure an appointment is made for the customer in a timely manner

2

Did Not Attend (DNA) process + Read more ...

If the customer fails to attend the appointment their payment should be suspended FAI - Failed to attend interview.

  • When considering whether to suspend payment, take into account:
    • the existence and type of any vulnerability and its impact on the customer's capacity to comply
    • any recent events (as documented) that may impact on the customers capacity to comply, or
    • any other known factors that may indicate customer was unable to comply, or had a reasonable excuse for not doing so

Reasons for not suspending must be clearly documented on the customer's record

3

Suspension of payment following DNA + Read more ...

If the Service Officer decides that a suspension is appropriate, code the suspension manually via the Benefit Action (BA) screen with the date of effect being the date of the missed appointment.

Document the suspension by recording all relevant details using the Suspend Cancel Reason Fast Note.

Record a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Generic > SUS/CAN/RES > Suspend Cancel Reason.

Complete the Fast Note.

4

Restoration of suspended payment following a DNA + Read more ...

If a customer makes contact following suspension of payment for non-attendance at a compulsory participation interview, advise them:

  • they have been suspended due to missing their appointment
  • if they are unable to attend any appointment requested of them, they must make contact before the appointment to reschedule and avoid suspension

Restore the payment with arrears, if entitled, and manually rebook the compulsory participation interview appointment for the next available time. If the rescheduled appointment is within the next 5 business days, advise the customer of the time and date as no appointment advice will be issued.

Document the restoration by recording all relevant details using the Restore (Payment Type) DOV: Fast Note.

Record a Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Generic > SUS/CAN/RES > RESTORE (Payment type) DOV.

Complete the Fast Note.

5

Before the interview check the customer's record + Read more ...

  • Check the customer's payment is current before conducting the interview. If the payment has been suspended see Restoration of Parenting Payment (PP), before the interview is completed
  • Check the customer's contact details and any shared care information is correct and update as required
  • Check the FTB Shared Care Summary (FSS) & Shared SSA Care (SSC) screens to ensure they reflect correct care details
  • If contacting the customer by phone before the interview (for example outbound pre-appointment contact), explain the purpose of the interview and what evidence they should provide before the phone appointment for example, medical evidence, proof of home schooling or foster care, to support exemptions
  • Document the discussion including any requests for information or verification

6

Conduct the participation Service Update + Read more ...

Launch the Service Profiling Update Workflow via Customer First directly from the relevant booked appointment.

Complete the questions and finalise the Workflow. If this is not 'COM'pleted, errors will be experienced in processing the rest of the Service Component.

This will automatically update the status of the Service Component to 'COM' (completed). Note: when the Service Component is completed it cannot be restarted.

7

PP compulsory participation + Read more ...

It is the Service Officer's responsibility to provide all relevant information throughout the interview to the customer.

Confirm if the customer is participating in any of the following activities at this time:

  • Self-employment suitable paid work, for at least 30 hours per fortnight (the income declared must meet the national minimum wage). For self-employed customers the Net Business Income needs to be the equivalent or more of 30 hours per fortnight at the national minimum wage
  • Studying for at least 30 hours per fortnight in an approved course
  • Combination of paid work (including self-employment) and study of at least 30 hours per fortnight

If the customer is self-employed check how recent the last business income assessment is and determine if a new assessment is required.

It is also necessary to determine if the customer may be eligible for a long term exemption from their mutual obligation requirements based on their current circumstances. This includes exemptions due to:

  • caring for a large family (where the customer is the principal carer of 4 or more dependent children under 16 years of age, or a secondary pupil child from 16-19 years of age)
  • having a dependent child home-schooled
  • supervising distance education of a dependent child
  • being a registered and active Foster Carer
  • caring for a sick or disabled child but not qualify for Carer Payment (CP)
  • being a relative carer (other than a parent) with a parenting or protection order under the Family Law Act
  • being a family or community member (not the parent) caring for a child under a care plan supported by the state/territory child protection authority

Evidence may be required from the customer to support these exemptions.

8

Information to discuss with the customer + Read more ...

  • Discuss options for satisfying their mutual obligation requirements. If the customer is not already satisfying the requirements, offer some examples of how to do so.
  • Explain the customer's obligations in relation to finding and accepting suitable child care, being clear that approved child care is considered to be suitable, and must be accepted if it is available. If approved child care is not available, it is up to the customer to determine whether other child care options are suitable. Consider using the Child Care Availability System to help the customer locate child care services. Discuss Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS)
  • Discuss additional allowances, Pension Education Supplement and Education Entry Payment (EdEP) for full-time study
  • Discuss available programs and services, Adult Migrant English Program (AMEP), Skills for Education and Employment (SEE), Financial Counsellors, 'We Speak Your Language' brochures, etc.
  • Mention that the customer may be required to report personally, on a regular basis, even if an exemption is granted. Clarify that this type of reporting is different from how they may be currently reporting their income ( online, phone, in person or in writing by fax/post/third party). If personal lodgement of SU19 forms will be required (customer is not eligible for self service reporting), discuss whether a change in payday code may make it easier for them to attend on a particular day, and give them the option to change either now or in the future. Explain that customers may discuss their needs with the agency at any time
  • Explain that the customer's reporting options will be confirmed at the end of the interview, based on the assessment that is made during the interview
  • Explain the compliance framework, and the possible penalties which can be applied once compulsory participation requirements start

See the Resources page for a link to mutual obligation requirements and exemptions for principal carers information on the Services Australia website.

Conducting compulsory participation interview with PPS customer - further actions

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

What are the customer's current participation circumstances? + Read more ...

If the customer is:

  • eligible for a long term, or automatic exemption from mutual obligation requirements, go to Step 2
  • currently fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements through work or study, go to Step 3
  • currently not fully meeting their requirements or is eligible for a short term exemption, go to Step 4

2

Exemption from mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

Exemptions from mutual obligation requirements for job seekers who are the principal carer of a dependent child vary according to the type of payment they receive and the age of the youngest child.

If it is deemed appropriate, code an exemption from Mutual obligation requirements for principal carers.

A principal carer granted an exemption (16 weeks +) is required to register as a job seeker, however, connection to an Employment Services Provider is not compulsory nor do they need to have any further explanation of their mutual obligation requirements, unless they volunteer for employment services.

Go to Step 5 for the process to determine the type of job seeker registration required.

3

Fully satisfying mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

A principal carer (regardless of payment type) who is fully meeting mutual obligation requirements will be Centrelink managed and required to negotiate a compulsory Job Plan with Services Australia.

Update the appropriate Activity Type on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen to reflect the customer's current activity with a maximum of 52 weeks. Code:

  • PTW for customers fully satisfying using paid work
  • SEL for customers fully satisfying using self-employment
  • PFT for customers fully satisfying using approved study
  • DFR for customers fully satisfying using Defence Force Reserves Service
  • SPW for customers fully satisfying using a combination of study and paid work
  • SSE for customers fully satisfying using a combination of study and self-employment
  • PWS for customers fully satisfying using a combination of Paid Work and Self-Employment
  • The combination of PTW and SEL can be coded on the AEX screen

Customers 55 years of age or over can use voluntary work or a combination of voluntary work and other paid employment to satisfy requirements.

Any voluntary work activity, or combination involving voluntary work for customers aged 55 years or under, needs to be approved by the Employment Services Provider.

To negotiate a Job Plan with the customer, run the Job Plan Workflow

The plan will be based on the customer's individual circumstances and will include the activities the customer has agreed to undertake to meet their mutual obligation requirements as determined by their individual circumstances.

All principal carers meeting their mutual obligation requirements are required to register as a job seeker, however, a connection to an Employment Service is not compulsory.

Go to Step 5 for process and to determine the type of job seeker registration required.

4

Not satisfying mutual obligation requirements or not eligible for an exemption + Read more ...

The customer is required to be registered as a job-seeker. This may have been actioned as part of the 5 year 9 month briefing interview. Check before proceeding with the next steps.

  • To register as a job seeker run the Looking for Work workflow. This incorporates the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) and referral option to the Assessment Services Team for an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) if required
  • For those not requiring a referral to an Employment Services Assessment, the customer will be connected to the Online Employment Service where eligible
  • Code other appropriate referrals and/or programs as necessary
  • Ensure the customer's AEX screen displays the current activity of Job Search (JSE) and remove any other code as necessary. The provider will negotiate the Job Plan and appropriate activities for the customer
  • If the customer has a current 'Full-time student' (FTS) code on their AEX screen from a previous student payment assessment and coding, this must be removed via the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen
  • To remove the FTS code from the AEX screen, code the customer as 'Not a Student' (NST) in the Student Status: field screen (on the NES screen), from the date the customer's mutual obligation requirements commenced (their youngest child's sixth birthday). This will automatically end date the FTS code on the AEX screen

5

To register as a job seeker + Read more ...

This step may have been completed as part of the 5 year 9 month briefing interview. Check before proceeding with the next steps.

Does the customer wish to volunteer to participate in an Employment Services?

  • Yes,
    • Run the Looking for Work workflow to register the customer as a No Stream Service Referral Required (NSSRR)/No Remote Referral Required (NRRR) with a Meeting Requirements 'PCP' reason and select 'Y' for a Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) referral
    • The JSCI will determine whether the customer can be referred directly to employment service or whether an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) is required
    • If the customer is referred for an ESAt, the ESAt assessor will determine the most suitable employment service for the customer
  • No, run the Looking for Work workflow to register the customer as NSSRR/NRRR with a Meeting Requirements 'PCP' reason and select 'N' for a JSCI referral. Note: if the customer is currently registered as a job seeker do not run the workflow

6

Confirm, update reporting requirements + Read more ...

Based on the information collected in the interview, review the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen and confirm or manually update the reporting frequency code.

For customers who have been recognised for an ESAt appointment and have not yet gained medical evidence, 2 weekly reporting frequency applies.

Advise the customer of the reporting obligations and options including any flexible options they have.

Provide customers with Self Service reporting information and register them to these services if possible. See Self Service Access Facility (SSAF) for reporting employment income, participation and change of circumstance details.

Principal carers will generally not have access to self service reporting during the first 12 weeks of compulsory mutual obligation requirements, and must lodge Reporting Statements (SU19 forms) in person according to their frequency. However, PP customers who have an approved activity of PTW, or who are exempt and have earnings to declare can still use self service reporting. Record details in a DOC.

7

Record interview information + Read more ...

A DOC is created at the completion of an Interview, include the following:

The interview record - individual circumstances:

  • Meeting mutual obligation requirements through suitable paid work, self-employment or study
  • Looking for Work - No Action Taken or Customer volunteered to be registered
  • Job Plan negotiated
  • Customer has exemption (specify exemption reason)
  • Customer is required to meet part-time work mutual obligation requirements
  • Customer registered as a job seeker and connected to a provider or the Online Employment Service

Reporting requirements:

  • Customer is required to report X weekly
  • Customer has been advised of Self Service reporting options and has/has not been registered

Referrals - include details of any referrals to programs:

  • Customer has been referred to XXX program
  • If a referral to Assessment Services is made/required for an ESAt. Details of this should be included here

Compliance Framework:

  • Compliance Framework and possible penalties have been explained to customer

Other:

  • Any other information that was discussed during the interview relevant to the customers circumstances
