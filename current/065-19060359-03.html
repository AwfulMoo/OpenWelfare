This page contains information on how to help a person apply for a loan under the Home Equity Access Scheme (the Scheme).

  • The self-managed tab explains how to help a person apply for the Scheme online
  • The assisted tab explains how to check a person's circumstances to ensure a new claim is required, complete an Assisted customer claim (ACC) with them, and lodgement of claims and documentation
  • The SA310/SA496 tab describes the information in the SA310/SA496 paper application form including Parts A - F

Self-managed

Applying for the Home Equity Access Scheme online

Step

Action

1

Check access to online account + Read more ...

Check the customer and any applying partner is registered with an active Centrelink online account.

If they do not have an active account they must confirm their identity at their local service centre to register for a Centrelink online account.

Advise the customer how they can create a myGov account and link it to Centrelink services. This will also enable them to receive Centrelink mail online through the myGov Inbox.

Service centre staff can help customers with their online claim through self-service terminals.

Record the claim contact in a DOC and advise the customer they can proceed to start and submit an application online.

Customers can also access the Payment and Service Finder to check if there are other payments appropriate for their circumstances.

2

Update My profile + Read more ...

On the My profile page, information from the customer’s record is pre-populated.

Customers can access their profile by selecting My Details > Personal and Contact Details > My profile via the home page links or the dropdown Menu.

Customers should review the information in their profile and if updates are required, select the Update link for the applicable sections.

For each part of My profile updated, they need to select Save.

Electronic messaging

Customers who provide a mobile phone number or email address will be advised that they will automatically be subscribed to Electronic Messaging (EM) services unless they have previously declined EM. They will be asked to nominate a preferred contact method (SMS or email) for electronic messages.

If customers have linked their active Centrelink online account to their active myGov account they are automatically subscribed to receive Centrelink mail online. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging for more information. They can opt out of these services at any time.

3

Starting a Home Equity Access Scheme online claim + Read more ...

Once registered with an 'active' online account, to start a claim application customers need to:

  • sign into myGov and access their linked Centrelink online account
  • select the Payments and Claims link on the Home page the select Make a claim, or open the Menu and select Payments and Claims > Claims > Make a claim
  • select Get started under the Older Australians category on the Make a Claim page
  • read the information about the Scheme
  • select Apply for a loan under the Home Equity Access Scheme
  • complete the questions on the Eligibility Check page then press Continue

Once the customer completes the Eligibility Check and presses Continue, the online claim is registered as a started claim. The customer has 13 weeks from this date to complete and submit the claim. The online claim will expire if not submitted within 13 weeks. See Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM) for more information.

If the customer would not be eligible for a Home Equity Access Scheme loan based on their answers, they will be advised why and referred to the Payment and Service Finder to see what other payments they may be eligible for. They can select Back to return to Make a Claim page to begin another claim type.

4

Claim progress + Read more ...

Once the claim is started, an Introduction page is displayed and provides information to the customer about what to expect with instruction to:

  • complete the claim and upload any supporting documents, submit the claim for processing, and
  • tell us if anything changes

The customer is to read the information on the Introduction page and when ready press Continue to start progressing through the claim.

The customer is guided through the process of completing their claim, which is broken down into sections. The sections of the claim must be completed in order and the next section cannot be started until the previous section is complete.

The first section is accessed by selecting the Start button.

Each section contains pages of relevant questions. On each question page, there are options to select:

  • Back to go back to the previous question or page
  • Next to progress forward to the next question
  • Continue to save the claim answers and continue to the next page of questions

When each module is completed and the end of the section reached, the Start button becomes enabled for the next section. Answers in previously completed sections can be updated by selecting Edit.

The claim does not need to be completed in one session. Select Continue at any time and the responses will be automatically saved. The customer can exit the claim by pressing Home and then continue with the claim later.

5

View or update a started online claim + Read more ...

If the customer exits their online claim, to access it again the customer is to:

  • sign into myGov and access their linked Centrelink online account
  • select the Payment and Claims link on the Home page then select My online claims, or open the Menu and select then Payments and Claims > Claims > My online claims

A list of all historical and current online claims will display.

If the status of the Home Equity Access Scheme claim is Incomplete, they can choose to either:

  • Continue claim to display the next question set to be completed
  • Cancel claim and confirm they want to do this. Once cancelled, they will not be able to view or continue the online claim, and the cancellation cannot be undone. No claim information will be saved or recorded. They can start a new online claim at any time

6

Review and confirm + Read more ...

Based on information the customer has provided, if they met the combined claim criteria, this is where they will be asked if they would like to submit an application for both themselves and their partner.

When all questions have been completed the Review and Confirm section provides a summary of all of the information provided by the customer so that they can check what they have entered is correct.

If the customer wants to review particular claim questions or update their answers, they can make updates by selecting the Update button for the applicable section. This will take them back to My profile or the relevant section of the claim to review/update the particular claim questions and responses.

Declaration and Acknowledgement

Once all information is correct, the customer must read the Declaration and Acknowledgement and accept them by ticking the boxes.

The customer must then select Confirm Information to save the claim information. Once selected no further changes can be made to the information entered into the claim. The customer can view the details but not make further edits.

7

Next steps + Read more ...

The last section of the claim is the Next Steps page which advises the customer of any documents they need to provide and tasks they or any applying partner, need to complete before submitting their claim.

Combined claims require the customers partner to complete a few tasks including reading and agreeing to the answers the customer provided in the application. The partner is also asked to provide consent to applying for a loan under the Scheme for themselves, and the customer.

The partner must complete their tasks in their own Centrelink online account within 21 days.

Tasks may be supplementary or required. The claim cannot be submitted until all required tasks are completed.

If a task is supplementary encourage the customer to still complete it if they can. It will help Services Australia to assess the claim and identify if the customer can get additional payments and services.

8

Uploading documents for online claims + Read more ...

Where a task requires the customer to provide a document, there is an Upload documents button next to the applicable task. The customer can use this to upload documents directly into the online claim.

Encourage the customer to access their Centrelink online account via myGov to upload documents and submit their claim. This is the quickest and easiest way for customers to complete their tasks and enable claim submission.

If needed, help the customer upload the documents and submit their claim online. Once the customer has selected a document to upload, they submit it as follows:

  • Press Ready to Submit
  • Complete a Declaration about the document(s) by ticking the box, and
  • Press Submit Documents

Once the uploaded document(s) have been submitted the associated tasks will display a status of Done.

  • If a task requires a customer to attend a Service centre to provide documents, Service Officers should: Scan the documents
  • Code identity documents in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard in Process Direct. If the Identity Confirmation Status is updated to Confirmed, complete the associated identity task
  • For all other documents, run the Request & Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure to complete the associated task

If the customer uploaded or provided documents before starting the online claim, a task may still display on the Next Steps page for the document to be provided. The customer can either:

  • upload the document again on the Next Steps page, or
  • contact Services Australia to have the Request & Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure run to complete the task. Before completing the task for the customer, Service Officers must check that the document has been provided

9

Claim submission and expiry + Read more ...

Once all required tasks display a status of Done, the Submit button will become enabled and the customer or if it is a combined claim, the partner can submit the claim. To be assessed from the earliest possible date, customers should provide all the requested information and submit the claim as soon as possible.

Once submitted, a message will display to the customer to confirm their claim for the Scheme has been successfully submitted and the following information is displayed to the customer:

  • Claim submission date
  • Estimated completion date
  • Claim ID

The page also provides options for the customer to view the claim history as well as view and print a copy of their claim answers.

The customer can press Home to return to their Centrelink online account homepage from where they can track the progress of their claim.

The partner can also track the progress of the combined claim in their own Centrelink online account.

Once a claim has been submitted, the customer cannot update the online claim details. They will need to contact Services Australia if they want to provide information in relation to their claim.

Customers can withdraw their online claim at any time after it has been submitted via My online claims. Once a claim has been withdrawn it cannot be reinstated. The customer can apply again by starting a new online claim at any time.

Upon lodgement of the claim, a Work Item will be automatically created and allocated to the Home Equity Access Scheme National team for assessment and processing. Where the claim is a combined partner claim, there will only be 1 Work Item created for processing both loan applications.

For information about the assessment process, see Assessing Home Equity Access Scheme applications.

10

Combined claim partner task + Read more ...

Partners have 21 days to respond to the notification sent to their phone and myGov Inbox to complete a 'Task for Home Equity Access Scheme combined partner claim' by:

  • Signing into myGov and accessing their own linked Centrelink online account
  • Selecting the outstanding 'Task for Home Equity Access Scheme combined partner claim'
  • Giving permission for their partner to claim on their behalf
  • Answering questions about their payment destination
  • Reviewing the answers supplied by their partner in the claim
  • Accepting and declaring that the information provided is correct
  • Lodging supporting documents if applicable using the upload document service
  • Providing their consent to participate in the Scheme

Once both the customer and partner have completed all steps, the customer is required to:

  • Sign into myGov and access their linked Centrelink online account
  • Select the open claim
  • Ensure all required tasks are completed and required documents have been uploaded
  • Submit claim

Note: if the partner does not complete the task within 21 days of being notified, the partners task will expire, and the claim reverts back to an individual Scheme claim for the customer only.

11

Further assistance + Read more ...

'Channel hopping' allows:

  • the customer to start a claim online and have it continued by a Service Officer, via Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) or
  • a Service Officer to help a customer with starting a claim via ACC, which would then be completed by the customer online (an active Centrelink online account is required)

For more information, see Assisted customer claim (ACC) table on the Assisted tab.

Digital Assistant (DA)

The Oliver Digital Assistant (DA) provides help text on each question. This include static help text which displays in a blue box underneath the question, and a dynamic DA which provides pre-programmed responses to questions the customer enters. Customers can access the dynamic DA by selecting Click here to launch the digital assistant which appears under each question.

System issues

If the customer reports a system issue or fault with their online claim, report it via mySupport using the Pension Loans Scheme (PLS) issues form.

Step

Action

1

Initial contact + Read more ...

Where a customer contacts to enquire about applying for a Home Equity Access Scheme loan, see Initial contact by customers applying for the Home Equity Access Scheme. Discuss the Scheme eligibility criteria with the customer to determine if they are likely to meet the requirements.

If the customer is partnered and their partner also wants to apply for a loan under the Scheme, staff can complete this as a combined claim if they meet the criteria. Staff must complete eligibility checks for the applying partner.

Does the customer meet the basic eligibility requirements for the Scheme?

2

Online claim offer + Read more ...

If the customer wants to proceed to apply for a Home Equity Access Scheme loan, an online claim must be offered first. Explain the benefits of applying for the Scheme online, such as:

  • Help to apply for the right payment through streaming questions and early warnings if they may not be eligible
  • Less information for them to provide, as information from their Centrelink record will be pre-populated into the online claim
  • They can monitor the progress of their claim through their Centrelink online account
  • They can complete a combined partner claim if their partner also wants to apply

Is the customer willing and able to complete the claim online?

  • Yes, ensure they are registered with an active Centrelink online account and help them to start their claim online. For help see the Self-managed tab
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Assisted customer claim (ACC) offer + Read more ...

ACC may be used by Service Officers when a customer is unable or unwilling to claim online. ACC replicates the questions in the online claim and must only be used when the customer is available to answer the questions in ACC.

Does the customer wish to proceed with an ACC?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, if the customer wants to apply using the paper application form (SA310/SA496) see the SA310/SA496 tab. If they do not wish to proceed with an application at this point in time:
    • encourage the customer to seek independent legal or financial advice before making a decision to apply for a Home Equity Access Scheme loan
    • advise the customer of the Payment and Service Finder which they can use to see what other payments they may be eligible for
    • record a DOC via the Document List (DL) screen to document the discussion
    • procedure ends here

4

Before starting ACC + Read more ...

Before starting ACC:

  • check the Document List (DL) screen for any DOCs relevant to a claim for the Scheme
  • update the customer's personal details via Customer Summary, using the Task Selector in Process Direct and, if required, update their contact and interpreter details via the Other Contact Details (OCD) screen in Customer First, as these questions are no longer asked in ACC.

If ACC was started before the updates were made, the claim can be exited and the updates made outside the claim before submitting it. The updates will appear on the Review page in ACC.

5

Starting a claim via ACC + Read more ...

Access ACC via the Assisted Claim desktop icon. Enter the customer’s CRN and press Go.

A list of customer’s current and historical claims will display. If a claim has already been started and has a status of Incomplete, select Continue claim. If not, start a new claim:

  • Select Make a Claim
  • Select the Get started link under the Older Australians heading
  • Read the information about the Scheme to the customer
  • Select Apply for a loan under the Home Equity Access Scheme
  • On the Eligibility Check page:
    • read the privacy message in full to the customer and select the customer's response
    • complete the streaming questions based on the customer’s answers then press Continue

Once the Eligibility Check has been completed and Continue pressed, a started claim is registered. The customer has 13 weeks from this date to complete and submit the claim. They can do this via their online account or continue via ACC. The online claim will expire if not submitted within 13 weeks. See Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM) for more information.

If the customer would not be eligible for a loan based on their answers, advise them of the reason why and refer them to the Payment and Service Finder to see what other payments they may be eligible for. Select Back to return to Make a Claim page to begin another claim type.

  • complete the claim and upload any supporting documents

The first sections are is accessed by selecting the Start button.

Review and confirm + Read more ...

When all questions have been completed, the Review and Confirm section provides a summary of all of the information provided by the customer. Confirm the information with the customer to ensure it is correct.

If updates are required, select the Update button for the applicable section. This will navigate to My profile or the relevant section of the claim to review and make updates.

Declaration and Acknowledgement

Once all information is correct, the customer must complete the Declaration and Acknowledgement

Encourage the customer to access the claim via their online account to read and accept the declaration and acknowledgement. They can then proceed to submit the claim which will enable them to monitor the progress of their claim through the Claim Tracker and receive notifications about their claim.

If a customer is willing to complete the claim online, remind them to submit the claim as soon as possible or it will expire after 13 weeks.

If the customer declines the offer to complete the claim declaration online:

  • Read the verbal declaration and acknowledgements script to the customer, ensuring they accept the statement. Ask the customer to acknowledge the declaration and agree that they understand and accept their obligations. Once this has been read to the customer and they have accepted their obligations, identify this by ticking the boxes
  • Record a DOC on the Document List (DL) screen with 'Verbal declaration script read and accepted by customer for the Home Equity Access Scheme claim via ACC'

Note: applicants who complete a verbal declaration are required to provide a physical signature via the SA310 or SA496 Part A. This will either be requested on the Next Steps page of the claim, or the Home Equity Access Scheme National team will send a written request when the claim is assessed.

  • do not proceed further with the claim
  • advise the customer the claim will be withdrawn
  • exit the claim and cancel the claim via the My Online Claims page

Encourage online channel + Read more ...

Applications for the Scheme can be made by completing a paper application form (SA310 partnered or SA496 single) and submitting it by post or to the nearest Service Centre. However, customers are encouraged to apply for the Scheme online.

If the customer wants to proceed to apply for a loan under the Scheme, offer an online claim first and explain the benefits of applying online, such as:

  • Help to apply for the right payment through streaming questions and early warnings if they may not be eligible
  • Less information for them to provide, as information from their Centrelink record will be pre-populated into the online claim

2

Home Equity Access Scheme application (SA310/SA496) + Read more ...

Where the customer declines the offer of an online claim or ACC, they may apply using the Home Equity Access Scheme application form (SA310 partnered or SA496 single). Advise that the form can be downloaded from the Services Australia website.

Applying for a loan using the paper form involves:

  • completing the form and relevant parts of the SA310/SA496
  • collecting all documents needed as outlined in the application forms
  • lodging the claim and supporting documents

Encourage the customer to read the Scheme information on the website and to seek independent legal or financial advice before making a decision to apply for a loan under the Scheme.

Record a DOC on the Document List (DL) screen to verify customer declined the offer of an online claim and ACC.

For more information about the paper application form, go to Step 3. Else procedure ends here.

3

Notes pages + Read more ...

The SA310/SA496 commences with a series of Notes pages which contains the terms and conditions information about how to apply, eligibility and what to consider prior to applying. Customers must read the information prior to completing the form. The Notes pages do not need to be submitted with the application. Customers should retain the Notes pages for their reference.

4

Claim progress + Read more ...

Once the customer has read the Notes pages they can proceed to answer the questions in the application form. The first questions aim to help the customer identify if they may be ineligible for a loan, by advising them to stop if their answer means they cannot be paid under the Scheme.

If the customer is advised to stop, they should not proceed with the application. Encourage customers who are ineligible to refer to the Payment and Service Finder to see what other payments they may be eligible for.

If the customer is not advised to stop, this does not mean they are eligible (a determination of eligibility can only be made once an application has been lodged) but means they can proceed to progress through the form. They must answer all mandatory questions. The form will advise if the customer can skip any questions based on the answers they provide.

5

Next steps + Read more ...

The Next Steps page helps the customer identify which supporting documents they need to provide with their application. This is determined by their answers at certain questions.

The customer must ensure they have all the document(s) applicable to their circumstances before they lodge the claim. If the applicable documents are not provided with the application form at lodgement, the application will not be accepted.

6

Declarations and Authorities + Read more ...

The final section of the SA310/SA496 is the gathering of signed declarations and authorities from the customer and other parties who have a registered interest in the real estate offered by the customer as security for the Scheme loan. This may include:

  • The customer’s partner, if they are a member of a couple
  • Co-owners of the property
  • Individual or corporate trustees where the real estate is the asset of a trust
  • Company directors or secretaries for real estate owned by a company

The section is broken down into six parts (Part A to Part F). The customer will have determined at the Next Steps page which parts they need to complete.

Note: customers who apply for a loan using the SA310/SA496 are always required to complete at least Part A and Part B of the form. Some online claimants are also required to provide certain parts of the SA310/SA496 which they can upload directly into their claim.

For more information about Parts A-F see Completing parts A–F of the SA310/SA496.

7

Lodgement + Read more ...

When the customer has completed the form, including obtaining relevant declarations and authorities via Parts A-F, and have their supporting documentation ready, they can lodge the application in person at a Service Centre or by posting the form to Services Australia. Details about how to lodge the form are provided in the Notes pages.

Where a paper form has been lodged, Service Officers should scan the form and ensure all mandatory questions have been answered and the supporting documentation provided. Scan the form and documents to Document Tools on the customer’s record on Day 1 for action by processing staff.

Once the SA310/SA496 has been scanned, a Work Item for a claim will be automatically created and allocated to the Home Equity Access Scheme National team for assessment and processing.

For information about the assessment process see Assessing Home Equity Access Scheme applications.

Completing parts A-F of the SA310/SA496

Table 2:

Item

Description

1

Signed authorities and declarations + Read more ...

The purpose of Part A to Part F of the Home Equity Access Scheme application form (SA310 partnered or SA496 single) is to obtain signed authorities and declarations for the loan from the applicant and any other party who has a registered interest in the real estate offered as security for the loan. This may include:

  • the customer’s partner, if they are a member of a couple
  • co-owners of the property
  • individual or corporate trustees where the real estate is the asset of a trust
  • company directors or secretaries for real estate owned by a company

An applicant may be required to provide one or more parts with their application. Some applicants may not be required to provide any parts at all, for example single customers who apply online and offer real estate that they have 100% ownership of.

Parts A-F may be required regardless of which lodgement channel an applicant uses. Whether they apply using an online claim, ACC or the SA310/SA496, the applicant will be advised at the Next Steps section which parts, if any, they are required to provide.

Where an applicant is required to provide Part A – Part F, they should keep a copy of the signed pages for their own records.

It is the applicant’s responsibility to arrange and obtain the signed declarations and authorities to provide with their application. Services Australia do not issue written requests to third parties to seek their consent to the Scheme loan, however upon receiving all supporting documentation may make verbal contact with the impacted parties prior to granting the Scheme loan to ensure they understand the Scheme and possible impacts for the property.

For more details about each part, go to Step 2.

2

Part A – Applicant and partner declarations and authorities + Read more ...

Part A of the SA310/SA496 is used to obtain the applicant and (if any) their partner’s signed declaration and acknowledgements for the Scheme loan, and authority to inspect the real estate property offered as security for the loan.

All applicants must complete Part A except single customers who apply online (including ACC, unless a verbal declaration is completed in which case the customer needs to provide Part A).

For customer who are a member of a couple (MoC), their partner must also read and sign Part A, even if they are not applying for a loan themselves. It is a legislative requirement per Section 1136(1B)(2) of the Social Security Act 1991 that both MoC sign the request for either member to participate in the Scheme. It is for this reason that all partnered applicants, even those who apply online or via ACC, must complete the SA310 Part A to verify their signed joint consent to the loan application.

3

Part B – Real estate details + Read more ...

The purpose of Part B is to obtain details for the real estate property being offered as loan security. It is similar to the Real Estate Details (MOD R) form but contains information specific to the Scheme.

Only applicants who apply using the SA310/SA496 paper form are required to provide Part B with their application form. If more than one property is offered as security, a separate Part B must be completed for each property.

Online applicants are not required to provide Part B, as the online claim (including claims actioned via ACC) obtain the relevant real estate details within the claim. However, if the Home Equity Access Scheme National team require further information about the real estate property, they may ask the applicant to provide a Part B post-lodgement.

Part B must be signed by the applicant.

4

Part C – Co-owner declaration + Read more ...

Part C of the SA310/SA496 is required if the real estate offered as security for the loan is co-owned with another person(s) (not including the applicant’s partner). The co-owners must read and sign Part C before a witness. If there is more than one co-owner, a separate Part C must be provided for each individual.

If the applicant is a member of a couple and jointly owns the property with their partner, they are not required to provide Part C. Partner declarations and authorities are obtained via Part A.

5

Part D – Individual trustee guarantee declaration + Read more ...

Part D of the SA310/SA496 is required if the real estate offered as security for the loan is the asset of an individual trust. The trustee must read and sign Part D before a witness. If there is more than one trustee, a separate Part D must be provided for each trustee.

6

Part E – Corporate trustee guarantee declaration + Read more ...

Part E of the SA310/SA496 is required if the real estate offered as security for the loan is the asset of a corporate trust. The trustee(s) must read and sign Part E before a witness.

Up to three trustee(s) can sign on one Part E. If there are more than three trustees, a separate Part E must be provided for the additional trustee(s). Each Part E must be company stamped (if applicable) by the trustee(s).

7

Part F – Company guarantee declaration + Read more ...

Part F of the SA310/SA496 is required if the real estate offered as security for the loan is the asset of a company. The company director(s) and/or secretaries must read and sign Part F before a witness.

Up to three company director(s) or secretaries can sign on one Part F. If there are more than three directors or secretaries, a separate Part F must be provided for the additional party. Each Part F must be company stamped (if applicable) by the company director(s).
