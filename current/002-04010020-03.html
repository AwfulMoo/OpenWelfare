Determining eligibility for Remote Area Allowance (RAA)

Table 1: This table describes the process for determining if a student is entitled to RAA when their home and term address are different.

Step

Action

1

Determine the student's usual place of residence + Read more ...

For more information about determining a usual place of residence, see the Resources page of Remote Area Allowance (RAA) payment and eligibility.

Is the student an ABSTUDY secondary student who boards away from home during the term to study:

  • Yes, these students have their permanent home recognised as their usual place of residence for RAA, go to Step 6
  • No, is a Youth Allowance, ABSTUDY or Austudy student whose usual place of residence is their:
  • term address, go to Step 2
  • home address, go to Step 5

2

Student's term address + Read more ...

Is the student's term address in an RAA Specified Area?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Youth Allowance (YA), ABSTUDY and Austudy customers + Read more ...

For YA, ABSTUDY and Austudy customers, the student is not entitled to RAA while their usual place of residence is not in an RAA Specified Area.

To avoid incorrectly paying RAA to customers whose usual place of residence is not in the RAA qualifying zones, a student's term (TER) address must be recorded.

In Customer First use the Change in contact details guided procedure and:

  • In the Address Type: field code (TER) and on the Address Details (AD) screen, code the home address (HOM) as not in an RAA Specified Area, with the RAA: field: coded as 'N',
    or
  • In the Address Type: field code (TER) and on the AD screen end date the HOM address if it is within the RAA Specified Area, leaving only the (TER) address
  • If the customer no longer has a home address (for example, sold home or not returning) end date the HOM address, leaving only the (TER) address

See Step 1 in the Recording decision and adding a manual review table.

4

Student is entitled to receive RAA + Read more ...

To receive RAA, the YA, ABSTUDY or Austudy student's term (TER) address must be coded as their home (HOM) address on the system. This is because the RAA indicator (which determines the payment of RAA) cannot be coded on a term (TER) address.

  • In the customer's record, go to the Address Summary (ADS) screen and 'S'elect the home address (HOM) to go to the Address Details (AD) screen
  • Code the term address details in the relevant fields. For more information see Updating address details
  • The Remote Area for RAA: field will automatically change to 'Y' if the address is in an RAA Specified Area
    Note: if an address is in a possible RAA Specified Area but the indicator does not automatically change to 'Y' email the Payment Summaries, Allowances and Supplements team. The team will determine if the location qualifies for RAA
  • Complete the Date of Receipt: and Source: fields

See Step 1 in the Recording decision and adding a manual review table.

5

Student's home address + Read more ...

Is the student's home address in an RAA Specified Area?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, the student is not entitled to RAA while their usual place of residence is not in an RAA Specified Area. See Step 1 in the Recording decision and adding a manual review table.

6

Is the customer in receipt of ABSTUDY as secondary boarding student? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

The student is entitled to RAA for the first eight weeks of their absence from their home address + Read more ...

This includes ABSTUDY tertiary students or Australia Apprentices living away from home.

  • In the customer's record, go to the (ADS) screen and select the home address by coding an 'S' next to the (HOM) type
  • This will take you to the Address Details (AD) screen
  • Ensure the home address details are recorded in the relevant fields. For more information, see Updating address details
  • The Remote Area for RAA: field will automatically change to 'Y' if the address is in an RAA Specified Area
    Note: if an address is in a possible RAA Specified Area but the indicator does not automatically change to 'Y' email the Payment Summaries, Allowances and Supplements team. The team will determine if the location qualifies for RAA
  • Complete the Date of Receipt: and Source: fields
  • Record the customer's term address by coding TER (term) in the Address Type: field and then completing the address details Do not code the Remote Area for RAA: indicator

See Step 2 in the Recording decision and adding a manual review table.

8

ABSTUDY customer living away from home as a secondary boarding student + Read more ...

The student is entitled to RAA for the full period in which their usual place of residence remains located in an RAA Specified Area. There is no eight week limit.

To ensure the system does not cancel the RAA, the term address must be coded as the postal address using the following workaround when coding the RAA:

  • In the customer's record, go to the ADS screen and 'S'elect the home address (HOM) to go to the AD screen
  • Ensure the home address details are recorded in the relevant fields. For more information, see Updating address details
  • The Remote Area for RAA: field will automatically change to 'Y' if the address is in an RAA Specified Area
    Note: if an address is in a possible RAA Specified Area but the indicator does not automatically change to 'Y' email the Payment Summaries, Allowances and Supplements team. The team will determine if the location qualifies for RAA
  • Complete the Date of Receipt: and Source: fields
  • Record the customer's term address as a postal address by coding 'POS' in the Address Type: field and then completing the address details. Do not code the Remote Area for RAA: indicator for the postal address. This coding ensures the RAA continues to be paid for their duration of the term address occupancy

This will result in the customer's mail being sent to the term address (recorded as the postal). Therefore, if the customer does not want the mail to go to that address, (for example U16 ABSTUDY customers who wish or need their parent/guardian to receive letters and notifications), the term address must then not be coded on the AD screen. Instead, the customer's term address must be coded in a Display on Access DOC.

See Step 1 in the Recording decision and adding a manual review table.

Recording decision and adding a manual review

Table 2: This table describes the process for recording a decision about a student's entitlement to RAA and adding a manual review to their file.

Step

Action

1

Record details of decision + Read more ...

  • Advise the customer of their entitlement or non-entitlement to RAA and that they must advise the Services Australia within 14 days if their circumstances change including if their address, rent or usual place of residence changes
  • Record details of decision, including the need to record the term address as the home address on a DOC, if appropriate

Procedure ends here.

2

Record details of decision - for customers eligible for RAA for eight weeks only + Read more ...

  • Advise the customer of their entitlement to RAA for eight weeks and that they must advise Services Australia within 14 days if their circumstances change including if their address, rent or usual place of residence changes
  • Code manual review with due date of eight weeks from the date the customer moved address
  • Record details of decision on a DOC.

3

At review - eight weeks after the customer moved address + Read more ...

To cease paying RAA to customers who cease to be eligible after eight weeks of RAA payments, a student's term (TER) address must be recorded as follows:

  • In the Address Type: field code (HOM) and end date the home address (HOM) if it is within the RAA area, leaving only the (TER) address
  • Record details of action on a DOC

Note: if the customer returns home or moves to another address, they need to advise Services Australia of the change, so the process starts over again from Step 1 in the Determining Eligibility for RAA table, with the determination of what is the customer's usual place of residence.
