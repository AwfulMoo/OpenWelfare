Gather employee information and hand off process

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer contacts advising of potential dispute with Parental Leave Pay (PPL) employer due to payment delivery or record keeping + Read more ...

Employer obligation disputes include matters where:

  • no PPL payments have been received by the employee or payments have ceased prior to the full amount being paid
  • employer is, or undergoing insolvent/liquidation proceedings
  • incorrect amount of PPL is, or has been paid (Note: employee must check deductions with employer prior to commencing dispute action)
  • the employer is making unauthorised deductions
  • PPL payments have not been paid in the employee’s regular payroll cycle
  • pay records/payslips/Payment Summary are not provided or the employee disagrees with the amount

Discuss and gather information from the employee relating to the issue.

Check and confirm:

  • employee's PPL is current as per the benefit status line
  • employer details such as name, phone and ABN are correct, as outlined within the online claim
  • payments sent to the employer on the Payment Summary (PS) screen. If applicable see Delivery of Parental Leave Pay (PPL)

Note: if employee's claim status is PPL/ASS-EIA or PPL/ASS-EIR this means the claim is awaiting employer registration or acceptance of the employer determination. For these status codes do not send a Handoff to PPLEPT - CADR Fast Note. The PPLEPT will take appropriate action after the required timeframe of 14 days.

If PPL instalments have been withheld by the employer, request evidence verbally from the employee. This could include payslips or bank statements and evidence the employee can upload via document lodgement service.

Does the employee believe that their employer’s business has or will cease trading due to insolvency or liquidation?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Immediate referral + Read more ...

Immediate referral to the PPLEPT is required if any of the following applies:

  • The matter involves bullying or harassment
  • The employee is currently involved in an existing industrial dispute with their employer
  • The employer has withheld PPL instalments. Note: evidence of this must be provided by the employee

Is immediate referral to the PPLEPT appropriate?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Unresolved dispute + Read more ...

Has the employee attempted to resolve the matter with their employer or been unable to contact their employer so a resolution can be sought?

  • Yes, and the employee:
    • has previously contacted regarding their employer not providing their PPL, go to Step 4
    • has not previously contacted regarding their employer not providing their PPL go to Step 5
  • No, tell the employee that they will need to contact their employer to discuss and attempt to resolve the matter:
    • Record details of the dispute on a DOC on the employee’s record
    • Note: the Customer enquiry about employer obligations Fast Note can be used to record details of initial customer enquiries about PPL employer delivery obligations. These DOCs are information only and should be closed. There is no requirement for this information to be sent to the PPLEPT
    • Procedure ends here

4

Subsequent contact by the employee about their employer not providing PPL + Read more ...

Check the Document List (DL) screen on the employee’s record for an ADR (Dispute ID) DOC. If the ADR (Dispute ID) DOC has been finalised, tell the employee the details/outcome explained in the ADR (Dispute ID) DOC.

If the ADR (Dispute ID) DOC has not been finalised, check the Activity List (AL) screen. The ADR (Dispute ID) DOC maybe held or started.

Is there a started or held ADR (Dispute ID) DOC?

  • Yes, tell the employee that a specialist team is currently reviewing the record and will be in contact with them once an outcome has been reached
    • Hand off to PPLEPT is not required
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Hand off to the PPLEPT + Read more ...

Tell the employee that the matter will be referred on for further investigation. Ask them to upload evidence (such as payslips and/or bank statements), to avoid delay in PPLEPT actioning this request.

The employee will be contacted by a staff member within 2 business days to gather further information and discuss the issue.

Use the Handoff to PPLEPT - CADR Fast Note to escalate the case to PPLEPT. The following information should be obtained from the customer and recorded in the Fast Note:

  • Extra Details: Handoff to the PPLEPT - CADR
  • Cus enq about PPL provided by: Employer CRN
  • Confirmed PPL current and payment issued to employer: Yes/No
  • Enquiry related to: Delete below what is not relevant
  • Payment/s not received: List details of dates and how many PPL instalments have not been received
  • Is the employer insolvent/liquidated or going through an insolvency/liquidation process?: Yes/No/Unknown
  • Is the employer still operating a registered business? Yes/No/Unknown
  • Have payslips been issued: Yes/No
  • Have any issues occurred with tax deduction amounts: Yes/No
  • Has a Payment summary been issued to the employee: Yes/No/N/A
  • Other enquiry: Include details
  • Has the employee discussed the issue with the employer?:
  • Yes, details of discussion outcome: [Free text here]
  • No, attempt to identify the reason, e.g. employer uncontactable, insolvency/liquidation processes, etc. [Free text here]
  • Advice provided: [Free text here]
    • “Advise the customer the matter will be referred on for investigation and a staff member will contact them within 2 business days to gather further information and request any evidence to support non-payment/part payment issue, e.g. payslips, bank statements, etc.”
  • Further information: [Free text here].

Note: the Customer enquiry about employer obligations Fast Note is still available for Service Officers to record details of initial enquiries from customers about employer obligations in relation to the delivery of their PPL. These DOCs are for information only and should be closed. There is no requirement for this information to be sent to the PPLEPT.

Commencing dispute resolution

For Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) staff only.

Table 2

Step

Action

1

A 'Handoff to PPLEPT - CADR' Fast Note is received advising of a potential dispute + Read more ...

Determine if there is sufficient information supplied about the dispute. If all information has been supplied, go to Step 6.

If further information is required, make genuine attempts to contact the employee.

Has contact with the employee been successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 6

2

Is the employer's business insolvent or in liquidation? + Read more ...

The employee may be aware that their employer's business has or will cease trading due to insolvency or liquidation. If the employee is able to provide information, gather further details of the insolvency or liquidation such as the date the employer became insolvent, etc.

If PPL instalments have been withheld from the employee, request evidence from the employee.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Employer's business insolvent or in liquidation + Read more ...

Conduct an employer insolvency check through the Australian Securities and Investment Commission (ASIC) Website. See the Resources page for a link. Note: when searching the ASIC website, remove the first 2 numbers of the Australian Business Number (ABN) to get the Australian Company Number (ACN).

Is the employer listed on the ASIC website as being insolvent/liquidated?

  • Yes, revoke employer payability determination from the date the employer became insolvent or the employee's last date paid to plus one. This will result in an overpayment to the employer that will require further investigation. See Delivery of Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Discuss the dispute with the employee + Read more ...

Attempt to identify if Services Australia should become the paymaster.

Has the matter been resolved between the employer and the employee?

  • Yes, no further action is required. The employer record does not require a DOC. Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • tell the employee that appropriate documentation will be required to support the period of the dispute
      Note: supporting appropriate documentation may be different for each employee, based on their individual circumstances. Generally, payslips and bank statements are requested as these documents should reflect any amounts of PPL paid by the employer to the employee
    • go to Step 5

5

Unresolved dispute + Read more ...

Consider whether immediate action is required by the agency. This may be appropriate in the following circumstances:

  • Employee has attempted to resolve the dispute directly with the employer
  • Employee has been unsuccessful with attempts made to contact the employer
  • An existing industrial dispute exist
  • Bullying/harassment issue or similar
  • Insolvency
  • Employer withholding PPL instalments

Has the employee confirmed one or more of the above circumstances?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No:
    • Request employee to contact the employer to discuss and endeavour to resolve the matter
    • Record details in a DOC on both the employee and employer records with the dispute details
    • Procedure ends here

6

Attempt first phone contact with the employer + Read more ...

Make a genuine attempt to contact the employer to discuss how the issue can be resolved. Attempt all contact numbers recorded on the system and through other avenues e.g. White Pages, Google etc.

Has the first attempt to contact the employer been successful?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 7

7

First phone contact attempt with the employer unsuccessful + Read more ...

On the employee’s record, issue an Acknowledgement letter (Q835):

  • Go to the Letter Display (LD) screen
  • Select the relevant options, then Next to scroll to the next page. Select continue to confirm each option

Document the employee’s record:

  • Use Fast Note >Auto Text > Families > Updates > ADR - Commenced PPL Obligations
  • Confirm all fields in the text to reflect the details
  • Close the DOC

Document the employer's record:

  • Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Families > Updates > ADR - Unsuccessful contact and confirm all fields in the text to:
    • reflect the details of the first unsuccessful contact attempt, and
    • remove references of the Q836 being issued
  • Hold the DOC until the following business day. Once the DOC is due, go to Step 8

8

Attempt second phone contact with the employer + Read more ...

Attempt to phone the employer. Use all existing contact numbers on the employer’s record, ABN lookup, google search and white pages to make a genuine attempt to contact the employer.

Has phone contact with the employer been successful?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Send a request for employer to contact + Read more ...

Document the employer’s record:

  • Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Families > Updates > ADR - Q834 – request for contact issued
  • Confirm all fields in the text to reflect the details of the first unsuccessful contact attempt
  • HOLD the DOC for 14 days from the date of issue of the Request to Contact letter

Issue a Request to Contact (Q834) to the employer:

  • Go to the Letter Display (LD) screen and enter Q834 in the letter code
  • Select the relevant options, then Next to scroll to the next page. Select continue to confirm each option
  • The Q834 asks the employer to contact the PPLEPT within 14 days to discuss the matter. It also must include:
    • employee details
    • the role of the employer
    • Fair Work Ombudsman (FWO) warning statement

Has the employer responded within the allowable time frame?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, see Step 7 in Table 3

8

Fair Work Ombudsman (FWO) referral + Read more ...

When the employer fails to respond or to comply with the Q836 Notice of Resolution or Q834 Request to contact letters, the matter will be referred to the FWO for resolution.

  • Collate all relevant dispute information (DOC, letters and any evidence) and send to the delegate officer (Assistant Director, Executive Level 1) for approval
  • Once the delegate has approved the FWO referral, send to the Fair Work Ombudsman’s PPL email address and cc the New Parent Programmes Team (email classification: FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY). The FWO referral template and the FWO checklist are located in the Resources tab
  • Transfer the payment responsibilities to the agency from the last date paid to plus 1. See Step 1 in Finalising the process table in Delivery of PPL
  • Tell the customer that no arrears payments will be issued until the FWO outcome is received
  • Issue to employer and employee respectively:
    • PPL - Fair Work Ombudsman Referral – Employer (Q843)
    • PPL - Fair Work Ombudsman Referral – Employee (Q842)
  • Record details on a DOC in the employer's record:
    • Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Families > ADR <Claim ID> FWO referral and select Continue
    • Confirm all fields in the text to reflect the details of the FWO referral
    • HLD (hold) the doc for 28 days from the date of the FWO referral
  • DOC the progress in employee's record:
    • Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Families > Updated > FWO Referral - employee and select Continue
    • Close the DOC

The employer determination will not be revoked at this stage, as the FWO will make the final decision on the outcome regarding the employer's responsibilities to the employee.

Note: Restricted Access customers must be phoned prior to FWO referral to notify of release of personal information to a third party.

9

FWO outcome + Read more ...

The FWO will determine:

  • Penalties/ infringements for the employer. Note: at no stage in this assisted dispute resolution process will the agency determine any penalties or infringements
  • Arrears amounts payable to the employee and who is responsible for payment i.e. the employer or the agency. If the agency provides the arrears, the agency will also be responsible for employer debt raising and recovery action

10

FWO notifies Services Australia of outcome of their investigations + Read more ...

The FWO may decide, as a result of the investigation that no action is required or alternatively, the FWO may prepare the matter for litigation and notify the agency in writing. The FWO will forward the relevant documents, which may include outcome of investigations, FWO Minutes, handwritten discussions between FWO and employer, FWO recommendations and potential action for the agency.

FWO may seek comment from Services Australia's Secretary or the PPL Delegate.

Department of Social Services (DSS)

The PPL Delegate will discuss FWO findings and recommendation with the PPL programme officer and then forward the FWO documents to DSS PPL Delegates. The PPL Delegate will discuss with DSS Delegates an agreed outcome resulting from the FWO investigations, for example, FWO recommendations, raise a debt with the employer and subsequent recovery action and pay the PPL funds to the employee.

Services Australia

The PPL delegate will consider whether any additional penalty under the PPL Act should be pursued that is, whether a criminal prosecution should be sought. The PPL Delegate will notify FWO in writing (via email) whether the agency/DSS agree with the FWO recommended litigation being sought, either Infringement or Civil Court Penalty and will advise of any additional action being taken by the agency including criminal prosecution and employer debt amounts.

11

PPLEPT Action + Read more ...

The PPLEPT will take any action in relation to debt raising, recovery and customer contact as a result of the outcome.

Where FWO has determined that no infringement will be incurred, PPLEPT will issue a Q852 Infringement Warning letter to the employer.

The PPLEPT will:

  • record the outcome on the employer's record by annotating the ADR<Claim ID> FWO referral Fast Note with:
    • the date of receipt (DOR)
    • details of the FWO outcome
    • details of action required by PPLEPT
    • indicate if a Q852 letter was issued
  • record the outcome on the employee's record by annotating the ADR FWO referral - employee Fast Note with:
    • the DOR
    • details of the FWO outcome
    • details if contact was successful with employee
  • Close all DOCs

Procedure ends here.
