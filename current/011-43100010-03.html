Lodging Medicare Two-way claims

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Claimant visits a service centre to claim in person + Read more ...

The claimant must provide service centre staff with:

  • a completed and signed Medicare Two-way claim form (MS001), and
  • the original accounts and receipts when paid
  • a Medicare Claim form (MS014) is not required if the account is paid

If information is missing on the form, ask for the missing information or hand the forms back to the claimant and ask them to resubmit when they have all the required information.

Note: when the claimant provides a Medicare Two-way claim form at a service centre, it must be processed as a Two-way claim. See Table 2.

2

Claimant lodges Medicare Two-way claim at a service centre, or by post for processing later + Read more ...

The claimant must provide service centre staff with:

  • a completed and signed Medicare Claim form (MS014)
  • a completed and signed Medicare Two-way claim form (MS001) or participating private health insurers claim form (available from their website), and
  • the original accounts and receipts when paid

Note: documentation must be handled according to Manage Medicare Scanning procedures.

3

Claimant lodges their Medicare Two-way claim by Medicare online account + Read more ...

The claimant must have uploaded:

  • completed and signed Medicare Two-way claim form (MS001) or private health insurers claim form (available from their website), and
  • original accounts and receipts when paid

Note: where an account is over $9,999.99 a completed and signed Medicare Claim form (MS014) must also be uploaded.

4

Claimant lodges their Medicare Two-way claim by Express Plus Medicare + Read more ...

The claimant must have uploaded:

  • a photo of a completed and signed Medicare Two-way claim form (MS001) or private health insurers claim form (available from their website), and
  • photos of original accounts and receipts when paid

See also: Process digital self- service claims

Note: where an account is over $9,999.99 a completed and signed Medicare Claim form (MS014) must also be uploaded.

5

Claimant lodges their Medicare Two-way claim with their private health insurer where the account is paid or unpaid + Read more ...

The claimant must lodge the following with their private health insurer:

  • a completed and signed private health insurer claim form (available from their website) or the Medicare Two-way claim form (MS001)
  • a completed and signed Medicare claim form (MS014), and
  • the original accounts, and receipts when paid

Note: the private health insurer forwards the claim to Medicare who processes the Medicare claim. Medicare then forwards the claim to the private health insurer electronically (or manually if unable to complete electronically) for processing.

Process a Medicare Two-way claim

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Process the Medicare claim as required + Read more ...

Reprocess a claim

If a Medicare Two-way claim is to be reprocessed, select the services for transmission via the PHEB screen after reprocessing the Medicare component.

Where more than one item line is included in the Medicare component of a Medicare Two-way claim, delete and reprocess the entire claim. This avoids problems when the benefit is subject to multiple service rules.

For information on how to process a claim, see:

  • Claims processing in Medicare
  • Process digital self service claims

2

Check if the private health insurer participates in Medicare Two-way + Read more ...

Check the list of private health insurers in Medicare. Participants in Medicare Two-way have a Two-way Fund Brand ID listed in this contact list.

Do they participate?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the claim cannot be submitted electronically. See Step 3 in Table 5

3

Access PHEB through Mainframe + Read more ...

  • Key control line PHEB
  • Press [Enter]

4

Enter claim form fields + Read more ...

Key information from the Medicare Two-way claim form (MS001) or private health insurer’s claim form into the appropriate fields:

  • FUND BRAND ID – see the List of private health insurers in Medicare on the Resources page
  • MEMBERSHIP NUMBER – if membership number is incorrect or not provided, see Table 3
  • CARD NUMBER and PATIENT (first name)
  • COMPENSATION CLAIM – key 'Y' (yes), 'N' (no), or ‘?’ (if unknown)
  • HOSP FROM DATE – (date of admission) and TO – (date of discharge)
  • TODAY CLAIM. If the claims were:
    • paid today, leave 'Y'
    • not paid today, key 'N'
  • HOSPITAL NAME
  • Press [Enter]

5

Populate the PHEB screen + Read more ...

PHEB screen is populated from the patient’s claims history with:

  • TRANS TYPE (transmission type) – EDI or ECL (ECLIPSE))
  • DOL (date of lodgement – populates with date of processing)
  • TRADING NAME (private health insurer name)
  • DOB (date of birth)
  • CLAIMANT'S FAMILY NAME
  • FIRST NAME (claimant's fist name)
  • AD (address)

Note: the PAYEE TYPE field displays either P (Account has been paid in favour of claimant) or U (unpaid account. The payment is made in favour of the provider).

6

Permanent address field + Read more ...

The PERMANENT ADDRESS field is now unprotected.

Key either:

  • 'P' (permanent) enter new permanent address if required
  • 'T' (temporary) enter new temporary address if required

7

Selection field + Read more ...

  • Key 'S' in the selection field to select the items lines to be transmitted.
  • Press [F3] to update

The screen displays showing the message I-2 WAY OK

The item lines and other patient and claimant identification details are automatically transmitted to the private health insurer after the close of business.

Note: miscellaneous items (e.g. facility fees - non MBS items) cannot be transmitted.

8

Record successful Two-way transmission + Read more ...

Service centre – Service Officers must notate on the Medicare Two-way claim form (MS001) 'successfully transmitted'.

PaNDA claims – Service Officers must add processing notes 'Two way information successfully transmitted'.

Digital claims (Medicare online account/App claims) – Service Officers must leave comments in CDMS, stating 'successful Two way transmitted electronically for MOA/App claim DOL DDMMYY'.

9

Clear screen + Read more ...

Press [Pause–Break] to clear the screen.

Process an incomplete Medicare Two-way claim form

Table 3

Item

Missing information and required action

1

Claimant’s signature missing on Medicare Two-way claim form (MS001) + Read more ...

Attempt to call the claimant at least once:

  • Advise the claimant they are being contacted because the Medicare Two-way claim form is not signed
  • Seek the claimants consent to process the Medicare Two-way form
  • Notate the contact 'Customer contacted to complete Medicare Two-way form'
  • Record the outcome in PaNDA for PaNDA claims or in CDMS for digital claims (Medicare online account/App claims)
  • Complete claim processing if the claim is otherwise complete

If unable to reach the claimant by phone or they do not consent to processing the Two-way claim:

  • Return the Medicare Two-way form and other associated documents with a Medicare Two way claim rejected or incomplete letter (Z1485)
  • For PaNDA claims - upload a copy of the letter into PaNDA and mark the claim as closed
  • For digital claims (Medicare online account/App claims) – Add notes in CDMS 'Details missing signature on Two Way claim form, Z1485 letter posted'

See Resources for a link to the Medicare letter templates and to forms.

2

Private health insurer name + Read more ...

Contact claimant to obtain name of the private health insurer.

If the private health insurer cannot be identified:

  • cancel the Two-way transaction
  • return the Medicare Two-way claim form, other associated documents and a letter of explanation (Z1485) to the claimant:
    • For PaNDA claims - upload a copy of the letter into PaNDA and mark the claim as closed
    • For digital claims (Medicare online account/App claims) – add notes in CDMS 'Details missing health fund details on Two-way claim form, Z1485 letter posted'

The Resources page contains a link to the Medicare letter template.

3

Private health insurer membership number + Read more ...

If the private health insurer membership number is not on the claim form, contact claimant to obtain the membership number.

If the membership number cannot be identified and the fund is known:

  • Complete all sections of the Unable to transmit Medicare Two-way claim form (HW038). Section 2 reference number refers to the service operator mainframe log on this identification number, and must be filled out
  • Attach the:
    • Medicare Two-way claim form (MS001)
    • Medicare Gap Statement of Benefits
  • Forward to the private health insurer by external post

The Resources page contains a link to the forms and contact details in the List of private health insurers.

4

Permanent address indication + Read more ...

If the claimant has not indicated whether the address on the claim form is permanent or temporary, key '?' in the PERMANENT ADDRESS field to move to the next tab.

5

Compensation indication + Read more ...

In the COMPENSATION CLAIM field, key 'Y' or 'N' or (?) if unknown to move to the next tab.

6

Hospital name + Read more ...

If the claimant has not written the hospital name on the claim form, key '?' in the HOSP NAME field to move to the next tab.

7

Hospital admission date + Read more ...

If claimant has not written the hospital admission date on the claim form:

  • Cross-reference the account or receipt with the claim form to determine the hospital admission date
  • Key a HOSP FROM date equal to the date of service of the first in-hospital service

8

Hospital discharge date + Read more ...

If the hospital discharge date is not on the claim form, key in the last date of service in the claim, or today's date if patient is still in hospital.

Note: where a pathology service retrieved outside the selection of the HOSP FROM and HOSP TO period, a warning message W-PATH SELN displays. The service can be selected for transmission via PHEB as the services relate to the period of hospitalisation.

Transmitting a duplicate service

Table 4

Item

Description

1

Duplicate services can be transmitted + Read more ...

  • if the same service lines exist in the Medicare Two-way database:
    • and the same service lines with a different Fund Brand ID exists on the PHEB screen, or
    • with the same Fund Brand ID and the original service lines have a rejected or transmission failed status
  • if this line and the associated SELECTION field is displayed yellow with the message W-DUP SELN

2

Duplicate services cannot be transmitted + Read more ...

  • if the same service lines exists in the Medicare Two-way database, and:
    • the same service lines with the same Fund Brand ID exists on the PHEB screen, and
    • the lines display as protected blue with no associated SELECTION field

Ancillary claims and Two-way claims that cannot be submitted electronically

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Does the private health insurer participate in Medicare Two-way? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

The Resources page contains a list of private health insurers in Medicare. Participants in Medicare Two-way have a Two-way Fund Brand ID on this list.

2

Claim cannot be submitted electronically + Read more ...

If the claim is an ancillary claim or claim that cannot be transmitted electronically via the PHEB screen, complete and print the Unable to transmit Medicare Two-way claim (HW038) form.

  • Complete all sections of the form. Section 2 reference number refers to the service operator mainframe log on this identification number, and must be filled out
  • Ensure the appropriate reason is ticked. If the box 'Other' is ticked, give a short explanation of the reason. E.g., change of address with the private health insurer. Do not use abbreviations such as 'E-NO SVC FND'
  • Attach the Medicare Two-way claim form (MS001) or participating health fund claim form with Medicare Gap Benefit Statement, or if it is an ancillary claim, attach the documents to the completed HW038 form
  • Forward to the participating health fund by external post
  • Go to Step 4

The Resources page contains a link to the Unable to transmit Medicare Two-way claim form (HW038).

3

Complete Medicare Two-way claim rejected or incomplete (Z1485) letter + Read more ...

Advise the claimant their claim cannot be forwarded to the private health insurer:

  • Use the Medicare Two way claim rejected or incomplete (Z1485) letter
  • In Letter Content, select 'No two-way agreement with health insurance provider' to populate the text 'We cannot forward your claim information because we do not have a Two-way agreement with your private health insurer'
  • Print the letter
  • Attach the:
    • Medicare Two-way form or Private Health fund form
    • Medicare Gap Statement of Benefit / Ancillary claim and return to the customer
  • Go to step 4

Note: the Unable to transmit Two-way form (HW038) is not required. This form is an internal form used to send claim details to a participating health fund only. This form is not sent to the customer.

4

Record Service Officer actions + Read more ...

Service centre – no notation required by Service Officers. The Service Officer must forward the claim to the private health fund by external post.

PaNDA claims – Service Officers must add notes in PaNDA:

  • The claim has been forwarded to the private health fund by external post, or
  • The claim has been returned to the customer because their private health insurer does not participate in the Medicare Two Way program

Digital claims (Medicare online account/App claims) – Services Officers must comment in CDMS:

  • Unable to transmit electronically to fund:
    • Form HW038 posted to fund, or
    • Letter Z1485 posted to customer
