FAQ - Dementia and Cognition Supplement in Home Care packages

Item

Question and answers

1

Question: When did the Dementia and Cognition Supplement come into effect?

From 1 August 2013. Home Care services have been able to apply to receive the supplement.

2

Question: Who is eligible for the Dementia and Cognition Supplement?

Care recipients may be eligible for the supplement if they meet the following criteria:

  • the care recipient scores 10 or more on the Psychogeriatric Assessment Scales (PAS) using either the:
    • Cognitive Impairment Scale or
    • Cognitive Decline Scale – suitable if the care recipient is non-verbal, has reduced fine motor skills or is visually impaired
  • for care recipients from culturally and linguistically diverse backgrounds the care recipient scores 22 or less using the Rowland Universal Dementia Assessment Scale (RUDAS)
  • for care recipients who are Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander people living in a rural or remote area the care recipient scores 33 or less using the Kimberley Indigenous Cognitive Assessment (KICA-Cog)

3

Question: Is a score of 0 an acceptable score?

Yes. For both RUDAS and KICA-Cog purposes, care recipients who have a score of 0 are eligible for the Dementia and Cognition Supplement.

4

Question: Is there an application form?

Yes. Home care providers must download and complete AC014 form.

5

Question: How much is the supplement?

From 20 March 2019, the Dementia and Cognition Supplement rate is 11.5% per cent of the care recipient’s basic subsidy.

Prior to 20 March 2019, the supplement rate was 10 per cent of the care recipient’s basic subsidy.

The basic daily subsidy is indexed on an annual basis. Current and historical rates of the Dementia and Cognition Supplement are published on the Department of Health and Aged Care’s website.

6

Question: What about care recipients who were in receipt of Extended Aged Care at Home Dementia (EACH-D) packages on 31 July 2013?

Care recipients in Extended Aged Care at Home Dementia (EACH-D) packages on 31 July 2013 automatically converted to a Home Care Level 4 package. EACH-D care recipients did not need to be re-assessed in order to be eligible for the Dementia and Cognition Supplement.

7

Question: How is the Level 4 supplement rate calculated for existing EACH-D care recipients?

The higher rate is calculated using a formula and includes the Level 4 basic subsidy, the Level 4 Dementia and Cognition Supplement amount and the top-up supplement amount.

Do not use the formula when responding to home care service queries. The Aged Care Program Branch use it for updating periodic rates changes purposes.

  • Higher Level 4 subsidy formula is ((BS + DEM + Top-up)/1.115))
  • Higher Level 4 supplement formula is ((BS + DEM + Top up)/1.115*0.115

For queries specifically about the formula, follow the escalation process prior to forwarding to the Aged Care Support Mailbox positional mailbox. See a Team Leader for the escalation process.

8

Question: How long is the diagnosis valid for?

Once a care recipient has become eligible for the dementia and cognition Supplement, they do not need to be reassessed if their package level changes, or they change service providers.

If a revised assessment revokes their eligibility and makes them ineligible ,consultation with the Aged Care Programmes Branch is required. The Aged Care Programmes Branch will then be required to undertake further action regarding the treatment of the revised assessment.

Follow the escalation process prior to forwarding to the Aged Care Support Mailbox positional mailbox. See a Team Leader for the escalation process.

9

Question: Will a care recipient who meets the eligibility criteria for both Veteran Supplement and the Dementia and Cognition Supplement receive funding for both?

No. In cases where the care recipients are eligible for both the Veterans’ Supplement and the Dementia and Cognition Supplement in home care, the service provider will only receive the Veterans’ Supplement.
