Collecting evidence for job seeker compliance decisions

Table 1

Item

Description

1

General principles for collecting evidence + Read more ...

Job seeker compliance decisions are evidence-based decisions. This means the decision must be based on relevant facts.

All compliance investigations include evidence from both the person who reported the failure or generated the investigation, and the job seeker. Whether any further evidence is required to reach a decision, will depend on the clarity and consistency of these two forms of primary evidence.

The decision maker must be confident they have sufficient evidence to establish the facts relevant to the reported failure to make an informed decision.

Any evidence collected as part of a compliance investigation should:

  • be specific to the reported non-compliance incident
  • focus on the issues that must be considered under the applicable legislation and policy. For example, the nature of the non-compliance event, whether the requirement was reasonable based on the job seeker's individual circumstances, the job seeker's capacity to comply and to make prior contact (when relevant to the decision)

The Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer or Service Officers with UNPP security resource should attempt to gather sufficient evidence, complete the investigation and make a decision during the initial contact with the job seeker.

2

Collecting additional evidence + Read more ...

Additional evidence is only required when there is insufficient evidence to reach a decision without it and where the additional information is likely to influence the outcome of the decision.

When additional evidence is required, the decision maker should explore whether this information already exists on the job seeker's record (for example, on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen) or if the job seeker can upload it during the conversation using Upload documents.

Additional verbal statements should also be gathered as a part of the initial job seeker contact when it will impact the outcome of the decision. For example:

  • additional supporting documentation does not exist on the job seeker’s record, or it would be unreasonable for the job seeker to supply it as a part of the conversation, or
  • the job seeker’s version of events is significantly different to the information supplied by the person reporting the non-compliance

3

Mutual obligation failure financial penalty decisions + Read more ...

When a mutual obligation failure is being considered, if additional information cannot be obtained during the job seeker's initial contact, a decision must be made based on the available evidence.

This is because the determination of a mutual obligation failure may result in a financial penalty which impacts the job seeker's entitlement to payments for their reporting period.

The Participation Compliance workflow does not permit a mutual obligation failure penalty investigation to be held pending the return of evidence, nor can the job seeker's payment be released for the relevant entitlement period until the compliance determination has been finalised.

PST-skilled Service Officers must consider what is likely, or more likely, to have occurred for the reported instance of non-compliance by considering factors like:

  • exacerbation of previously declared and evidenced circumstances
  • declared natural disasters
  • if the circumstances disclosed as having impacted compliance in this instance were unforeseen or exceptional in nature

4

Additional evidence for unemployment failures, work refusal failures and Job Seeker Compliance Framework decisions + Read more ...

When the job seeker requires time to source and supply additional evidence, for an unemployment or work refusal failure, or for a failure under the Job Seeker Compliance Framework, they must be given reasonable opportunity to provide this before a decision is made. The PST-skilled Service Officer or Service Officer with UNPP security resource negotiates this timeframe with the job seeker. There is no mandatory timeframe for evidence to be supplied, however the timeframe should be appropriate for the job seeker's needs. Two working days is generally considered appropriate if the job seeker is unable to offer a reasonable timeframe to supply the evidence.

The Service Officer must:

  • explain the benefits of providing the evidence in the agreed timeframe and the impact that failing to provide the evidence may have on the decision
  • document in the Further Evidence section:
    • what evidence was requested
    • why and how this is likely to influence the outcome of the decision
    • when it is due, and
    • the job seeker is aware a decision about the failure will be made based on the available evidence if nothing additional is provided by the due date

The workflow will go to the Save and Exit page and automatically select the appropriate save and exit option to temporarily remove the contact trigger pending the return of evidence.

For Job Seeker Compliance Framework investigations, the system will auto restore payment if it is currently suspended 'DNA participation appointment' (APA).

On the Activity List (AL) screen, ensure the activity has been held with an appropriate due date, generally this will be the 'Due date given + 1'.

If the job seeker does not provide the requested evidence by the required date the contact trigger will be reinstated and the job seeker will not be able to complete their report until they have spoken to the PST. A decision should be made based on the available information. Facts that cannot be substantiated with evidence may either be given lesser weight, or be disregarded.

Types of evidence

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Types of evidence + Read more ...

Evidence collected during a compliance investigation can take different forms and may need to be collected from a variety of sources.

Evidence may include:

  • reported non-compliance event, see Item 2
  • job seeker's statement, see Item 3
  • job seeker circumstances impacting capacity to comply, see Item 4
  • phone contact with provider, see Item 5
  • contact with or evidence from external third parties, see Item 6
  • job seeker's Services Australia record, see Item 7
  • Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Support Officer (SSO), social worker or specialists, see Item 8
  • job seeker behaviour and presentation, see Item 9
  • Employment Services Assessment (ESAt), see Item 10

2

Reported non-compliance event + Read more ...

The reported non-compliance event is the starting point for all compliance investigations.

The report submitted by the provider, or non-compliance event generated by Services Australia is a primary source of evidence and must always be considered. The report provides information about the non-compliance event, the incident date, notification and any other relevant information available at the time of the report.

When a non-compliance event was reported by the provider, the full report is accessed via a link within the Compliance Investigation workflow. In addition, relevant information is extracted from the report to display at appropriate pages in the workflow.

If necessary, additional information may be sourced from the Employment Services System (ESS) using the links within the Compliance Investigation workflow:

  • Job/Participation Plan
  • Provider Recorded Information
  • Online Diary
  • Job Seeker Calendar. Note: this will display for Targeted Compliance Framework compliance action only

3

Job seeker's version of events + Read more ...

A job seeker's statement is considered primary evidence in the investigation and must always be considered when investigating a reported non-compliance event. Compliance action is not applied without first giving the job seeker an opportunity to explain the circumstances that led to the reported incident of non-compliance.

Under the principles of natural justice, all job seekers have a right to give their version of events when an incident of non-compliance is reported. This includes providing an opportunity to:

  • explain the circumstances that led to the alleged failure to comply, and
  • explain how their circumstances impacted their capacity to comply or ability to make prior contact (if relevant)

Generally this evidence can be collected verbally. A factual summary of the conversation must be recorded by the PST-skilled Service Officer or Service Officer with UNPP security resource.

When collecting a job seeker's statement the Service Officer is responsible for leading the discussion using:

  • open questions to encourage the job seeker to give their version of events and explain any factors they believed impacted compliance
  • specific questions if further clarification is required or to resolve inconsistencies
  • strong call-handling skills to make sure the conversation remains focused on the specific non-compliance event as reported

The Service Officer records a concise and factual summary of the job seeker's statement in the Compliance Investigation workflow. Any additional evidence the job seeker would be willing to provide to support their statements should be recorded as part of this statement. The Service Officer should always encourage the job seeker to supply any additional, supporting documentation, even for Mutual Obligation Failure investigations. Whilst the Mutual Obligation Failure cannot be held pending this evidence being supplied, the documentation may be relevant if they wish to appeal the original decision once their evidence is lodged.

4

Circumstances impacting compliance + Read more ...

Ongoing job seeker circumstances may have a direct and significant impact on their ability to comply with their mutual obligation requirements and can play an important part in making a compliance decision.

When a job seeker is claiming an ongoing/intermittent circumstance directly contributed to non-compliance, check if the circumstance, impact and sufficient evidence are currently documented on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen.

Where a circumstance impacting compliance exists and has been confirmed previously, this can be used as evidence for the compliance investigation, particularly where the job seeker is stating a known circumstance has worsened.

If a job seeker's circumstances impacting compliance are not recorded, or there is insufficient information/evidence for the purposes of a compliance investigation, it must be gathered and documented on this screen during the investigation. Information recorded or updated on this screen during the investigation forms part of the evidence base for the investigation and can be viewed later as part of the completed summary for the compliance investigation.

5

Phone contact with provider + Read more ...

When there are discrepancies between a provider's statement and the job seeker's statement which would influence the decision, additional information/evidence may need to be considered.

The provider should always be offered the opportunity to respond to the job seeker's statement when it conflicts with theirs. A telephone conversation at the job seeker's initial point of contact is generally the most effective method to help substantiate one singular version of events and make sure relevant information has not been missed.

When recording verbal statements as evidence, care needs to be taken to note all factual information relevant to the decision along with the source of the evidence.

When attempts to contact the provider by phone have been unsuccessful:

  • Targeted Compliance Framework decisions are made based on the available information
  • Job Seeker Compliance Framework reports should be returned to the provider. Use an appropriate 'returned report' rejection reason on the Decision and Penalty page in the Compliance Investigation workflow and include specific information in the free text to the provider to clearly explain the discrepancy/disputed information that requires further clarification. Providers have 5 days to resubmit the report with the required information if they still wish to pursue compliance action under the Job Seeker Compliance Framework

6

External third party information + Read more ...

Contact with third parties can be an effective way to clarify discrepancies, confirm statements and help to substantiate one single version of events.

A third party contact:

  • is only required if it is likely to influence the decision,
  • should be attempted as a part of the initial job seeker contact, and
  • must be considered and weighed against all other evidence

In general, third party contacts are considered additional evidence. They are helpful in situations where conflicting information has been provided by the job seeker's statement and the person who reported the incident of non-compliance.

Third party contacts may include:

  • previous employer to gather further detail on events leading to unemployment due to misconduct or voluntarily ceasing suitable employment
  • youth workers
  • medical professionals

7

Information contained on the job seeker's Services Australia record + Read more ...

The job seeker's Services Australia record should be used to gain insight into their circumstances and can also be used as evidence, or when weighing evidence.

The Compliance Investigation workflow contains links to a wide range of information about the job seeker's circumstances and links to other relevant information:

  • Recent non-compliance action, including a Capability Assessment or Comprehensive Compliance Assessment (CCA)
  • Income declared and hours worked in the two previous reporting periods
  • Services Australia Rates Estimator
  • Address History
  • View ESAt for Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) referrals
  • Recent activities and exemptions
  • Circumstances impacting compliance
  • Medical certificates

Other information available to Services Australia may also be available outside the Participation Compliance workflow, for example on the following screens:

  • Document List
  • Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen also showing hours worked in one period
  • Document Tools

8

PST-skilled SSO, PST-skilled social worker or specialists + Read more ...

Consultations with a PST-skilled SSO, PST-skilled social worker or specialists can assist in establishing the facts and/or in weighing the evidence.

Examples of when this contact may be appropriate include contact with a:

  • Services Australia specialists including PST-skilled SSO, PST-skilled social workers, Indigenous Service Officers (ISO), Indigenous Cultural Consultants or Multicultural Services Officers (MSO) consultation to assist with identifying how job seeker circumstances impacted their capacity to comply with a specific requirement or to make prior contact
  • Services Australia assessors or the job seeker's treating health care professionals to gather information about how their medical condition(s) impact(s) on capacity

It is preferable that evidence of this nature is gathered verbally while the job seeker is on the phone. This allows follow up questions, quicker decision making and if required, appropriate interventions to support the job seeker. However, evidence from third parties may also include written statements or other supporting documents.

Contact with specialists in regards to understanding the impact of job seeker's circumstances on capacity to comply are recorded on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen.

PST-skilled Service Officers are not required to source additional written evidence to verify the professional advice given by a Services Australia social worker in relation to making a compliance decision. For example, when a PST-skilled social worker provides guidance about the impact of a mental health condition on the job seeker's capacity to comply, a PST-skilled Service Officer should not seek to source a medical certificate verifying the same information.

See Accessing a PST-skilled social worker for information on when consultation with a PST-skilled social worker may be required or appropriate.

9

Job seeker behaviour and presentation + Read more ...

Non-verbal factors established from contact with the job seeker can be considered as evidence when it is relevant to the investigation.

For example, advice about the job seeker's presentation, engagement in discussion, tone, or perceived interest in complying with their compulsory requirements may help inform the compliance investigation outcome.

This type of evidence is only recorded when it is specifically relevant to the decision under consideration. Sound personal judgement is needed to determine when it may be appropriate to consider and record this type of information. The information must be written in an objective manner, based on fact, and appropriately linked to the decision under investigation. Any information recorded in the compliance investigation may be requested by job seekers under Freedom of Information (FOI).

10

Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) Reports + Read more ...

The Report Assessment Summary of certain ESAts incorporates a 'risk of non-compliance' rating and details of specific vulnerabilities the job seeker has or barriers that may affect their capacity to comply. This rating may be used to inform a compliance decision.

When investigating an unemployment failure, work refusal failure or Job Seeker Compliance Framework compliance action, an ESAt may be initiated if the Service Officer determines assessment of a possible medical condition would benefit the job seeker. This may include circumstances where the job seeker is not certain how their condition may impact their compliance more broadly, or when their responses to the Job Seeker Snapshot do not trigger an ESAt.

When a Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer determines an ESAt is required and the ESAt is not triggered by the Job Seeker Snapshot (or a Job Seeker Snapshot cannot be run), a referral using the referral reason of Participation Failure Pending (PFP) should be made. This referral reason ensures:

  • the Assessor gives priority to returning the report, and
  • the report contains advice about the risk of non-compliance rating

For further information see Understanding Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) reports.

Methods for returning evidence required for an outstanding compliance action + Read more ...

When additional evidence has been requested, the Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer recommends the job seeker lodges this using Upload documents.

Job seekers may occasionally attend a service centre or Agent or contact a Smart Centre.

The job seeker should be encouraged to use Upload documents during their initial contact. Support the job seeker if they request assistance to use the Upload documents.

2

Scanning evidence + Read more ...

When a job seeker attends a service centre to supply the requested evidence and Upload documents is not available, staff are required to scan the documents to the job seeker's record.

Generally, the 'Supporting Documentation (UNS11)' classification page should be used unless a more appropriate option is available. For example, medical evidence should be classified using the 'Medical (UNS008)' image instead.

When supporting evidence is lodged through an Agent, the Agent will fax it to the National Business Gateway (NBG). The NBG will endeavour to scan this evidence within approximately 3 hours.

3

Complete any updates not directly relating to the compliance investigation and refer job seeker to the Participation Solutions Team (PST) + Read more ...

Once the image has been scanned, the job seeker is required to contact the PST so the evidence can be assessed and outstanding compliance action finalised.

Any specific updates that are not directly relating to the compliance investigation should be completed before referring the job seeker to the PST.

For example, a temporary incapacity exemption should be assessed when the job seeker requires and a Centrelink Medical Certificate is supplied.

Assessing evidence for job seeker compliance investigation decisions

For Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officers and Service Officers with UNPP security resource only

Table 4

Item

Description

1

Assessment of evidence + Read more ...

Evidence gathered as part of a compliance investigation should be assessed by considering the:

  • relevance of the evidence to the incident
  • nature of the evidence
  • source of the evidence and/or deemed reliability of the witness, and
  • credibility of the evidence, that is, how convincing it is, is it corroborated or contradicted by other evidence

The assessment of evidence is known as 'weighing' the evidence. This is an important part of the compliance investigation.

Assessing and weighing each piece of evidence allows the decision maker to determine how much impact the evidence has on the investigation.

2

Weighting of evidence + Read more ...

Evidence is weighted, or considered, to form an opinion about the most likely version of events.

Information that cannot be substantiated with evidence should either be given lesser weight or may need to be disregarded to form a decision.

When a claim is made that may affect the outcome of an investigation, several lesser weighted pieces of evidence may together result in an assessment this was the more probable version of events.

Evidence about a job seeker's circumstances around the time of the non-compliance event where the job seeker's circumstances do not directly relate to the incident under investigation may help establish the context in which the non-compliance event occurred, however, would not have the same weight as evidence that specifically relates to the non-compliance event.

If conflicting evidence cannot be resolved, or there is insufficient evidence available, weighing the evidence can assist in reaching a decision based on the 'balance of probability', such as, what is more likely to have occurred.

If this is required, the way in which evidence has been weighed must be detailed on the Decision and Penalty page of the Compliance Investigation workflow.

For further information see the Recording evidence for job seeker compliance decisions table.

The Resources page contains some examples of how evidence is weighted.

Recording evidence for job seeker compliance decisions in the Participation Compliance workflow

Table 5

Item

Description

1

General requirements for recording of evidence + Read more ...

All evidence relevant to the compliance investigation and decision must be documented within the Compliance Investigation workflow.

Effective documentation of evidence:

  • details the source that has provided the evidence
  • explains the content of the evidence as it relates to the decision
  • includes only evidence that is relevant to the decision
  • avoids including information that does not 'add value'
  • refers to the location of the information, if found external to the workflow, on the job seeker’s record
  • uses simple language which others can easily understand
  • can be substantiated on review

2

Recording the job seeker statement + Read more ...

The job seeker's statements regarding their reasons for the non-compliance event and their reasons for not making prior contact are recorded on the Investigation page of the Compliance Investigation workflow, in response to the following questions:

  • Is there anything else they want to add to explain the failure / What reasons have they given for the failure?
  • Why didn't you contact your provider before you were due to attend?

The job seeker statements should be written as a true reflection of all relevant information the job seeker wanted considered as part of the decision. It should also reflect the details of any evidence they have available or they believe they could provide to support the validity of their statements.

It is not appropriate for the Service Officer conducting the compliance investigation to record their own commentary or opinions about the job seeker's explanation as part of the job seeker statement.

Before finalising, check enough detail has been included to ensure another person reviewing the case at a later date will clearly understand the job seeker's explanation of what occurred and any factors the job seeker believes impacted their capacity to comply.

3

Recording sufficient evidence or further evidence obtained + Read more ...

The Compliance Investigation workflow is intuitive. The Further Evidence section on the Investigation page is only displayed when the job seeker's reasons for non-compliance and prior contact indicate substantiating evidence has been provided or if further evidence is required to make an assessment.

The further evidence section is used to record details of the evidence as it relates to the job seeker's reason for the non-compliance event and prior contact reasons.

In this section, the Service Officer conducting the compliance investigation must record:

  • whether sufficient evidence already exists to confirm the excuse for the failure
  • whether sufficient evidence already exists to confirm the reason for not contacting or that the provider was contacted with a good reason beforehand
  • the method that will be used to collect further evidence, where possible this should be via phone contact
  • if additional evidence has been requested, the date this is due (work refusal failure, unemployment failure and Job Seeker Compliance Framework compliance action only) and the impact the evidence is likely to have on the decision outcome (or the likely outcome if the requested evidence is not supplied)
  • who needs to be contacted for additional evidence
  • a summary of the additional evidence that has been provided

Where more than 1 phone contact has occurred to gather further evidence, record the details of the primary contact as only 1 type of contact (for example, provider, employer or other). The details of additional contacts, such as organisation/business name, contact person, contact number and a summary of the evidence provided is then captured in the Summary of evidence requested/provided field.

If additional evidence has been requested and a due date recorded for a work refusal failure, unemployment failure or Job Seeker Compliance Framework compliance action, the Save and Exit page will need to be completed before exiting the Compliance Investigation workflow.

4

Recording evidence from providers + Read more ...

When a provider reports a non-compliance event, this report is a key part of the evidence base for the decision. Key information from this report, and a link to the full report, are automatically maintained as part of the decision history.

When additional contact with the provider occurred as part of the investigation, this must be recorded on the Further Evidence screen in the Compliance Investigation workflow. Service Officers conducting the investigation should record:

  • details of who provided the additional evidence
  • a summary of all factual information relevant to the decision obtained during this contact

Comments which represent an opinion should be noted as such, and only included if relevant to the decision making process.

Note: all information supplied by a provider may be requested by the job seeker under Freedom of Information unless the provider has requested this information not be released and provides valid reasons why the release should not occur.

While information from the provider forms part of the failure evidence base and elements of this may need to be referenced when summarising the decision, there is no requirement to include provider comments verbatim within the decision summary as this will already be recorded within the Compliance Investigation workflow.

5

Recording specialist and other third party contact + Read more ...

Contact with specialists and other third parties must be recorded on the Further Evidence page in the Participation Compliance workflow, with the source clearly identified.

A summary of all factual information relevant to the decision obtained during this contact must be included. Comments which represent an opinion should be avoided.

In every instance where a job seeker's circumstance is identified as having an ongoing impact on the job seeker's capacity to comply, the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen should be accessed from the Compliance Investigation workflow. Record evidence on this screen to support the new or changed circumstances.

All information supplied by a third party may be requested by the job seeker under Freedom of Information unless the third party has requested this information not be released and provides valid reasons why the release should not occur.

6

Recording evidence from the job seeker's record + Read more ...

Evidence sourced from the job seeker's record is recorded on the Decision and Penalty page in the Compliance Investigation workflow. Details must include the relevant location and nature of the evidence.

For example, 'On the EAPP screen, the income and hours worked in the two previous reporting periods is shown confirming that the job seeker worked on the failure incident date.'

7

Decision rationale + Read more ...

The decision rationale is recorded on the Decision and Penalty page in the Participation Compliance workflow. The rationale must include:

  • the matters that were taken into account when forming the decisions
  • how the relevant facts were substantiated (that is, the evidence that was used to support these)
  • the weight that was given to evidence considered in the compliance investigation process
