Victoria (4-10 June 2021) - locating details in an online claim

The claiming period has ended for Victoria. Staff will only assess online claims for this event that were lodged before the claiming period ended on 2 July 2021.

Note: the extension of eligibility to include customers who were present in a relevant area does not apply to this event.

Table 1: for claims lodged using a Victoria (4-10 June 2021) online claim only. For help to progress any steps, contact Technical Peer Support.

Step

Action

1

Has the customer's first claim for this event been rejected? + Read more ...

A customer may have submitted for this event:

  • a claim that has already been rejected, and
  • a new claim with new information

A new claim decision cannot be made when a customer is trying to claim for the same event.

The customer will receive a decision letter. It will tell them they can request an explanation (review) of the decision. New information can be discussed as part of that process and a new decision made.

To check if a customer's first claim has been rejected, in Customer First in the customer's record go to the:

  • Document List (DL) screen to check for a COVID-19 Disaster Pymt Rejected Fast Note
  • Disaster Recovery Payment Claims Workflow to check for a completed claim and outcome in the workflow

Has the customer's first claim already been rejected?

  • Yes,
    • do not run the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow
    • go back to the SOA to finalise it so the claim is not reissued

- select User Status

- select the Set New Status option

- a window will open in this page and provide a list of status options

- select Not Required

  • add a DOC to the customer's record by running the Fast Note workflow

- Location: Customer First > Workspace > Documents and Correspondence > Fast Note

- Service Reason: select EXG - Emergency Exgratia

- Document Completion: select Yes, otherwise the Fast Note will not close and will create a new work item

- Extra Details to enter a title for the DOC. Type in this field COVID-19 DP Subsequent

- clearly document: claim ID (copy/paste the claim ID from the SOA), Victoria claim and that a claim decision has already been made

  • select Continue
  • procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Assess eligibility + Read more ...

Eligibility Details section will always show a customer is eligible.

Check the number of hours worked. This information is needed to launch the correct workflow. That is:

  • COVID-19 Disaster Payment (20 hours or more) - pays $500
  • COVID-19 Disaster Payment (less than 20 hours) - pays $325

Staff must not select any Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment workflow.

3

Is the customer trying to claim for another event? + Read more ...

After reviewing the relevant period and an employer / residential address, does it look like a customer is trying to claim for another event?

  • Yes,
    • a decision must be made under the event the customer has claimed
    • the claim must be rejected using the workflow for Victoria (4-10 June 2021)
    • no further assessment is needed
    • if the customer is trying to claim for another current event, they will need to claim under that event for their claim to be assessed. Customers can only claim for a relevant period and event if a claiming period has started
    • if the customer is trying to claim for another state or territory that has not been announced they can only claim when a payment is made available. The Services Australia website will be updated accordingly
    • do not transpose information from a Victoria (4-10 June 2021) claim into another event workflow
    • see Running the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow to reject the claim
  • No, go to Step 4

4

COVID-19 Disaster Payment or the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment for the same 7 day relevant period (4-10 June 2021) + Read more ...

A customer cannot receive a payment if they have already received a:

  • COVID-19 Disaster Payment for the same 7 day relevant period
  • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment that covers the full 7 day relevant period

Return to Customer First.

Go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen.

In the Display Payments For: field, select FSP.

In the Number of months in past: field, key 4 months since the start date of the activation.

If an FSP payment shows, check the customer's Document List (DL) screen.

Review any COVID-19 Disaster Pymt Granted DOC, CUS CLM EXG FSP DOC, Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Granted DOC or any other DOC relating to these payments to find out if the 7 day period was 4-10 June 2021 inclusive.

Has a COVID-19 Disaster Payment or Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment already been made for the same 7 day relevant period?

  • Yes,
    • the customer is not eligible to receive a payment
    • when running the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow, reject the claim. Approval has been given to reject claims for this event
    • go to Step 14
  • No,
    • the customer meets this eligibility requirement
    • go to Step 5

5

Residency details sub-heading + Read more ...

Return to the SOA to check the customer's information under Residency details.

Return to Customer First.

The system does not automatically assess a customer's residence status.

To avoid granting payments incorrectly, staff must make sure:

  • all residence data is coded
  • for non-Australian residents (who could claim online as they could link their myGov and Centrelink online accounts), manually check they meet the eligible visa requirements

To do this, staff must complete the following before running the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow.

  • check the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen for citizenship, visa and/or New Zealand residence status details
    • for NZ citizens, check the Visa (Sub) Class code for a 444 visa
    • check the New Zealand residence status

- Grandfathered (GRF) is a protected Special Category Visa (SCV) holder and an Australian resident

- Transitional (TRA) is a protected SCV holder and an Australian resident

- NOT is a non-protected SCV holder and a non-Australian resident

  • check the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen for country of birth or travel documents
  • check the Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen for travel details
  • details on the RSLEG, RSIMME and RSIM screens may be refreshed by RSIMME update
  • make sure the Country of Residence (CRES) screen is coded with the country the customer normally lives in. For example, if the customer normally lives in New Zealand but is temporarily working and studying in Australia, code CRES as New Zealand not Australia
  • if there are no residence details on the RSLEG screen, see Recording legal residence status to complete residence coding on the record

To check if a visa is a permanent visa, see the References page for a link to the Social Security Guide, Chapter 9.2, Visa subclasses & payment eligibility for visas issued after 1/9/94.

Is the customer an Australian Resident or the holder of an eligible visa?

  • Yes,
    • the customer meets this eligibility requirement
    • go to Step 6
  • No,
    • the customer is not eligible to receive a payment
    • when running the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow, reject the claim. Approval has been given to reject claims for this event
    • keep assessing the claim. All relevant eligibility criteria will be explained to a customer if they request a review (explanation of the decision) go to Step 6

My circumstances sub-heading - unable to earn income + Read more ...

Check the customer's response next to Unable to earn an income due to COVID-19 lockdown.

This is based on the question in the online claim 'Were you unable to earn an income at any time between 4-10 June 2021 due to a COVID-19 lockdown that has been declared as a COVID-19 hotspot'.

At any time means, the customer was unable to earn an income for at least 1 day between 4-10 June 2021 inclusive.

If the customer has answered:

  • Yes,

    • go to Step 7
  • No,
    • the customer is not eligible to receive a payment
    • when running the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow, reject the claim. Approval has been given to reject claims for this event
    • keep assessing the claim. All relevant eligibility criteria will be explained to a customer if they request a review (explanation of the decision) go to Step 7

8

My circumstances sub-heading - start date of 7 day period being claimed for + Read more ...

Check the date next to Start date of 7 day period being claimed for.

A customer's online claim may state a start date of impact before 4 June 2021. Staff must count 7 days from that date, to make sure the customer is impacted for at least one day between 4 and 10 June 2021.

If a customer is not impacted for at least one day between 4-10 June 2021, they are not eligible to receive a payment.

For example, a start date of impact is:

  • 29 May 2021 or after, they meet this eligibility requirement

Counting 7 days from 29 May 2021 is 4 June 2021. They are impacted on at least one day between 4-10 June 2021

  • 28 May 2021 or before, they are not eligible

Counting 7 days from 28 May 2021 is 3 June 2021. They are not impacted on at least one day between 4-10 June 2021

See Start Date Scenarios - Victoria for examples and calculations

Has the customer been impacted for at least one day between 4 and 10 June 2021?

  • Yes,
    • the customer meets this eligibility requirement
    • go to Step 9
  • No, the customer is claiming for a period outside of the 'relevant period'
    • the customer is not eligible to receive a payment
    • when running the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow, reject the claim. Approval has been given to reject claims for this event
    • keep assessing the claim. All relevant eligibility criteria will be explained to a customer if they request a review (explanation of the decision) go to Step 9

    • the customer meets this requirement

11

Customer answered Yes to the employer funded appropriate leave question + Read more ...

Check the date/s next to the question Dates you have or will claim this leave from this employer.

If appropriate leave entitlements do not cover the entire 'relevant period' from 4-10 June 2021 inclusive, the customer meets this eligibility requirement. For example, if a customer has 'appropriate leave entitlements' that cover 3 days of the 'relevant period', they meet this eligibility requirement.

Note: the customer must meet all eligibility requirements for at least 1 day within the remaining 4 day period.

A customer will not meet this eligibility requirement if their appropriate leave entitlements cover the full 'relevant period' from 4 June to 10 June 2021 inclusive.

Do not contact a customer to decide what type of leave they have taken. If the customer has recorded dates in the 'exhausted all leave/exhausted all appropriate' question, it is deemed 'appropriate leave'.

Has or will the customer access appropriate leave entitlements that cover the entire 'relevant period' from 4-10 June 2021 inclusive?
