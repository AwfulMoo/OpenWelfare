Cancellation of Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), ABSTUDY PES and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) PES payments 102-10020240

This document outlines the circumstances for cancellation of Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), ABSTUDY PES and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) PES payments.

Cancellation circumstances

A student may have their PES, ABSTUDY PES or AIC PES payments cancelled if:

  • they have had a change in their circumstances
  • they fail to comply with the requirements for payment
  • another type of payment is more beneficial, or
  • they voluntarily cease payment

Payments can be cancelled either automatically or manually.

Automatic cancellation

Automatic cancellation of PES, ABSTUDY PES or AIC PES occurs following reassessments that result in no continuing qualification to a PES, ABSTUDY PES, AIC PES or their qualifying benefit, or if the student is no longer undertaking approved study.

ABSTUDY PES will be cancelled automatically if the customer's qualifying payment of Parenting Payment Single (PPS) has cancelled due to:

  • the customer's youngest child turning 8 years of age

In these cases, the customer will normally be automatically transferred to

  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP), or
  • Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker)

On transfer to 1 of these qualifying payments, the customer's entitlement to ABSTUDY PES will be reassessed and the payment restored if they are eligible. ABSTUDY PES will continue to be paid until the customer has completed the course they commenced while in receipt of their previous qualifying payment, under existing ABSTUDY progress rules (reasonable time and limits of assistance).

Manual Cancellation

Manual cancellations are needed where the loss of qualification is not the end result of a reassessment but the result of a decision to cancel PES, ABSTUDY PES, or AIC PES payments. For example, a student or AIC applicant has asked Services Australia to cancel PES, ABSTUDY PES or AIC PES payments (despite still being eligible) or a Service Officer has decided payments should be cancelled due to the student or AIC applicant's failure to reply to correspondence.

PES, ABSTUDY PES and AIC PES payments are cancelled temporarily for customers transferring from a qualifying payment to Jobseeker Payment. A manual cancellation is needed due to a system limitation and is followed by a restoration, once the YA or JSP claim has been finalised. See, Cancellation of, or changes to qualifying payment for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), ABSTUDY PES or Assistance for Isolated Children PES.

Language, Literacy and Numeracy Supplement (LLNS) and Skills for Education and Employment (SEE)

Customers who wish to participate in Skills for Education and Employment (SEE) and do not qualify for PES, may be eligible for the Language, Literacy and Numeracy Supplement (LLNS) of $20.80 per fortnight.

The supplement provides financial assistance for job seekers who want to improve their English language, literacy or numeracy skills as part of their plan to return to work. This assistance is to enable job seekers undertaking SEE to meet the additional costs associated with attendance and provides added incentives for them to participate in the program.

Date of Effect Rules

The General Date of Effect Rules apply to PES and are covered under the Social Security Guide 3.10.5.10. They do not apply to ABSTUDY PES and AIC PES.

Cancellations of PES, ABSTUDY PES or AIC PES

Cancellations of PES are handled by various Service Officers depending on the type of PES payment:

ABSTUDY PES - Service Officers in ABSTUDY Processing Services or Service Centres that deal with ABSTUDY. See Processing arrangements for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY PES.

Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme - Service Officers in AIC Smart Centre Processing only.

PES - Service Officers in Service Centres and Smart Centres should only cancel PES in the following situations:

  • voluntary surrender - where the customer has requested for PES to be cancelled
  • automatic cancellation as a result of the cancellation of the customer's qualifying payment
  • where a customer has advised that they have ceased study
