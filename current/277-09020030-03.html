This page details the process for dealing with a Level 2 complaint.

Throughout this procedure the role:

  • Service Officer (SO6) refers to either a Complaints Officer or Personalised Services Service Officer (PSSO)
  • Complaints Team Leader refers to a Complaints Service Support Manager (SSM)

Acknowledge complaint (Complaints Officer) + Read more ...

Customer has used multiple avenues to lodge a complaint + Read more ...

If the customer:

  • lodged a number of complaints about the same issues, for example they have also lodged a complaint with the Commonwealth Ombudsman or an MP, or
  • complaint issues are already being addressed by another Complaints Officer

Determine if there are any additional complaint issues. Liaise with the other officer to determine how the customer is to be managed to minimise double handling and maximising customer satisfaction.

5

Management of multiple complaints + Read more ...

If a customer lodges a new complaint while an existing complaint is being investigated (by a different Complaints Officer), transfer the call to the owning Complaints Officer. If they are unavailable and the call is regarding:

  • Existing complaint - provide an update to the customer using the documentation recorded in the Feedback and Review window, If the customer still requests to speak with the owning Complaints Officer, contact them through all available channels to see if they are available and transfer the call if appropriate. If the Complaints Officer is not available to take the call, commit to a call back to be completed within 2 working days, create a client call back intray and route to the owning Complaints Officer. Send an email to the Complaints officer confirming this action and copy in their Team leader.

Do not transfer the call to the Complaints Officer's voicemail

  • A new issue - advise the customer the new complaint issues will be referred to the Complaints Officer already investigating their initial complaint. Advise the complaint timeframes to investigate the new issues and provide an outcome. Advise the owning Complaints Officer of the new complaint and determine if an additional complaint should be recorded or if the new issue/s can be added to the existing complaint
  • Recently finalised complaint - if the complaint is about the outcome of a previously finalised complaint, review the outcome and discuss with the customer. If the customer remains dissatisfied with the outcome of the complaint and the original outcome was correct, provide further escalation options for example escalation to Commonwealth Ombudsman. Note: it may also be appropriate to initiate a Personalised Services (PS) referral. PS accepts referrals from customers where a complaint has occurred and there is a real and immediate threat of media or external escalation or the need for a Personalised Services Service Officer (PSSO) due to multiple and/or complex customer issues

If the customer wants further explanation about a written response that has not been discussed, attempt to transfer the call to the owning Complaints Officer. If they are unavailable, attempt to discuss the outcome with the customer using the complaint finalisation summary. If the customer requires further information or still wishes to speak with the owning Complaints Officer, commit to a 2 day callback, create a Client callback intray and route to the owning Complaints Officer.

Do not transfer the call to the Complaints Officer's voicemail

1

Restricted service customers + Read more ...

Complaints Officers may receive correspondence or phone calls from customers who have been placed on restricted service by Personalised Service (PS). See Personalised Services. Check the Profile window for the restriction detail for advice on managing these cases.

All communication with the customer must adhere to the restricted service arrangements. If the customer has not made contact in accordance with the restriction, remind the customer of the decision as outlined in correspondence sent to them. For example, if a customer who is restricted to 'write only’ is transferred to the Specialised Complaints queue, advise them they need to raise any issues in writing. Tell the customer the call will be ended, no further discussion should be entered into. End the call.

Document the call in the PS stacking document in the Communication window. Use after call work so this is done immediately and advise the Personalised Services Service Officer (PSSO) of the customer contact.

PS managed case - If a customer lodges a complaint within their service restriction requirements, check the Restricted Services Agreement as there may be certain issues the customer has been instructed that they cannot re-raise.

If the customer is raising issues that have previously been addressed, remind them of the restriction on that particular issue. Any new issues raised should be managed by the PSSO to as a Level 1 complaint.

If a customer lodges a complaint regarding the decision to restrict their service:

  • The customer needs to adhere to their restriction in lodging the complaint for example if the restriction is 'write only’, the complaint must be in writing
  • If a complaint has already been lodged regarding the restriction before loading the complaint. Do not load another level 2 complaint if there is an open complaint about the same issue
  • If this is the first complaint regarding the restriction, liaise with the PS SSM regarding the reasons for the restriction and the agreed complaint outcome

Note: a customer with a restricted servicing arrangement can make a request for the restriction to be reviewed at any time. The request for a review of the decision must be in writing and addressed to the National Manager, Customer Service.

2

Complaint from a Personalised Services (PS) customer + Read more ...

Contact the Personalised Services Service Officer (PSSO) before recording the complaint to determine how the customer’s issues will be managed. It is important to consider the PS approach when managing complaints from a PS customer.

If the PSSO is to manage the customer’s issues, then record the customer’s call in the PS Stacking document or record a Level 1 complaint for the PSSO. Include in the document that the matter has been referred to PS for management.

If the complaint is to be managed as a Level 2 complaint by a Complaints Officer:

  • Discuss any proposed actions with the PSSO to ensure they are consistent with the existing Managed Service Plan (MSP)/Personalised Services Plan (PSP)
  • Any written responses to the customer must be reviewed by the PSSO prior to sending. Email a copy of the letter to the PSSO and copy in the relevant PS SSM requesting approval of the letter within 2 working days. If approval is not received within 2 working days, follow up with the PS SSM

Repeat calls from PS Customers

For repeat callers, Complaints and PS leadership staff will liaise to develop a customer management strategy. A PS SSM will send an email to the Complaints teams advising of the agreed strategy for the customer and any specific message to be relayed to the customer. For example 'their PSSO is investigating the issues raised and will provide an outcome by a determined day/time'. Specific details will also be recorded in the Profile window.

Follow the agreed strategy as outlined in the email and profile window. If the instructions state the customer is to be immediately transferred to the PSSO or PS SSM do not engage in lengthy discussions with the customer – refer them straight to their PSSO.

Discuss the call with the Complaints SSM to enable PS and Complaints to work collaboratively and determine if a new Level 2 complaint will be taken.

Document the call in the PS stacking document. Note: use after call work mode to ensure details are documented on the case as soon as possible.

3

Complaints about staff behaviour + Read more ...

Staff behaviour complaints specifically relate to the way a staff member interacted with the customer, for example a staff member was rude, aggressive, or made inappropriate comments. Ask clarifying questions and confirm the complaint is about the Service Officer’s behaviour as opposed to their actions or process.

There are instances where a Level 2 Complaints Officer investigates a behavioural complaint. This may occur in where sensitivity or complexity apply. In these circumstances the Complaints Officer will listen to the call recording. All other behaviour complaints should be referred to the Service Officer’s Team Leader.

Assertive behaviour may be perceived by the customer as threatening. For example, a customer may state a Service Officer was threatening in their behaviour because they said they were going to take enforcement action. An explanation of Child Support collection and enforcement policy and the reason enforcement action would be taken, may be an appropriate way to resolve the issue.

Level 2 behavioural complaints are recorded using either of the following category and complaint issues.

Quality of Service - Bias

Lack of courtesy - Perception of bias

When a customer makes a behavioural complaint:

  • consider a well-placed and appropriate apology. An apology should be timed appropriately - not delayed where possible and appropriate, sympathy and an explanation may be more appropriate. The Resources page contains a link to the NSW Ombudsman guide to an apology
  • document sufficient specific information to enable the behavioural complaint to be investigated, but minimal information about the Service Officer (first name, team and site only)
  • explain to the customer that the complaint about behaviour will be referred to the relevant staff member’s Team Leader. The complaint will be managed internally and no further feedback will be provided to the customer. If the customer does not accept this remedy the Level 2 Officer is then responsible for updating the customer about the behavioural complaint outcome

Contact the Services Officer’s Team Leader to advise of the complaint, discuss the complaint issues and timeframes for resolution. Create a complaint follow up intray and route to the relevant staff member’s Team Leader for management of the behavioural aspect of the customer complaint. Record in the complaint notes the actions taken. The Level 2 Officer will not finalise the Level 2 complaint until the Team Leader has actioned the behavioural aspect of the customer’s complaint.

The Team Leader review must include listening to the relevant call recordings, providing feedback to their staff as appropriate and documenting action taken into the complaint notepad.

The Team Leader will record the following information in the Level 2 complaint notepad:

  • Date call recording reviewed – date call recording listened to by the Team Leader
  • Officer completing – Team Leader’s first name/team/site
  • Outcome summary – upheld/not upheld, any comments

When finalising the complaint, record the remedy of the behavioural complaint as 'REFER TO OWNING TEAM TO ACTION' and the outcome is to be recorded by the Level 2 Service Officer after the Team Leader has documented their findings. Continue to manage all other complaint issues raised by the customer to resolution.

Feedback for behavioural complaints

Team leaders who manage behavioural complaints about their staff provide feedback as appropriate. Documentation in Cuba should only reflect that the customer’s behavioural complaint has been actioned. The Team Leader processes the behavioural complaint outcome in Cuba without finalising the complaint. The Level 2 officer will finalise the complaint in Cuba.

If a customer or staff member’s privacy may have been compromised by unauthorised access to information or other fraudulent activity see Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption.

Note: if the complaint issues relate to an allegation of staff fraud, the Complaints Officer must advise the Team Leader of the complaint details. The Team Leader will evaluate if further escalation is needed.

4

Privacy incidents - Complaints with an alleged privacy incident + Read more ...

Failure to report a privacy incident in a timely manner can result in serious consequences. Timely action can reduce the risk of physical harm to customers or other negative consequences.

On day 1 of receiving the privacy complaint:

  • complete the Privacy Incident Report form on the Security Incident Reporting page
  • forward the Incident Report form to the Privacy Contact Officers from the Complaints team email address
  • Copy in the Complaints Officer's Complaints Service Support Manager

The Resources page contains links to the Security Incident Reporting page and the contact details for the Privacy Contact Officers.

All alleged privacy incidents must be referred to the Privacy team for review.

Privacy complaints which can be resolved within a reasonable timeframe

The Privacy team will undertake the following actions:

  • Liaise with the originating business area. If an business area cannot be identified the Privacy team will liaise with Complaints
  • Determine how the customer will be managed

Privacy complaints which are complex and the Privacy team has advised the review process will be lengthy.

Advise the customer:

  • The agency treats any allegation of a privacy incident seriously
  • The privacy incident has been referred to the appropriate area so enquiries can be conducted
  • Complaints Officer will close the complaint as escalated, however the business area will be advised of the outcome of the privacy incident complaint
  • the business area will contact the customer

Confirm how the customer would like to be contacted by the business to be advised of the outcome of their complaint. Record details such as telephone number or email address. Notify the business area of the potential privacy breach. Finalise the complaint.

Complaints which present with both a privacy and another complaint issue

  • If there are additional issues to be investigated, manage as a Level 2 complaint
  • If a written response to the customer is required regarding the privacy complaint, the Privacy team will draft or clear any privacy related content in the Complaint outcome letter

Seek clearance of privacy related content from the Privacy team:

  • from the Complaints team email address
  • forward all draft correspondence containing privacy matters
  • copy in Complaints Officer's Complaints Service Support Manager

The Resources page contains a link to Complaints Contact details for Complaints and Privacy Contact Officers email address.

Complaints from children + Read more ...

Services Australia considers complaints from children to be sensitive in nature, and must be managed accordingly. A list of Escalated Complaints Officers trained to manage complaints from children is available on the External and Escalated Complaints SharePoint page.

The Parent Support Team are responsible for managing all other aspects of cases involving parents and carers under the age of 18, from the point of case registration until they turn 18.

When assisting individuals under 18, be aware of the agency's Child Safe Policy.' We are all responsible to help children feel safe when interacting with us, and take their concerns seriously'.

If a child is in immediate danger or risk of harm:

  • A delegate must call 000
  • All other staff must contact a member of the leadership team immediately

Urgent or high profile Escalation

Urgent, high profile issues or incidents are referred to the Escalation Coordination team. The escalation process is mandatory, and must be followed correctly. A National Manager must clear all escalations before being sent to the team. See Risk identification and management of threats to the safety or welfare of a child.

Support to assist in identifying and responding to child safety concerns are available on the Child Safe Framework page.

Go to the Research in Progress intranet page, the Resources page contains a link.

Complaints about staff personal characteristics + Read more ...

Complaints about Service Officers which are based on their age, sex, religion, race, sexual orientation, gender identity, intersex status, marital status or any other personal characteristic (perceived or known) must be treated with sensitivity and regard to anti-discrimination principles. Any decision about a complaint must be based solely on the service provided and the actions taken by the Service Officer in resolving the customer enquiry or making a decision.

If locking the customer to a different Service Officer who does not have this particular characteristic is recommended (for example locking from a female Service Officer to a male Service Officer) advise the Service Officer’s Team Leader or manager that this decision must be made in consultation with the Service Officer against who the complaint was made. The decision must equally balance the needs of the Service Officer with maintaining an ongoing working relationship with the customer.

Inform the customer that:

  • continued unreasonable conduct or inappropriate behaviour towards staff members will not tolerated, and
  • similar behaviour in the future may result in limiting their interactions with Child Support to written correspondence only

See Personalised Services for more information.

A complaint about staff’s personal characteristics should also prompt consideration of a referral to PS.

Complaints from Indigenous customers requiring specialised support + Read more ...

The Child Support Indigenous Team provides access to Child Support services for Indigenous customers needing extra support. The team assists Indigenous customers resolve complex Child Support enquiries in real time or by accessing digital services. The team focuses on:

  • supporting customers in applying for Child Support who may or have had difficulty doing so, that is failing the Maintenance Action Test
  • negotiating payment arrangements and collection activities
  • achieving accurate assessments by updating care or income details

Generally, customers are referred to the Child Support Indigenous Team when there are language, accessibility barriers or complex matters requiring an assisted level of service. Multiple refused applications for Child Support may be an indicator for a referral.

How can customers be referred to this team?

Service Officers, Social Workers and Remote Service Teams are able to refer an Indigenous customer, see Complaints - Level 2 Complaints for contact details.

12

Transgender, intersex and non-binary complaints + Read more ...

The Australian Government guidelines on the recognition of Sex and Gender apply to all Australian Government departments and agencies that maintain personal records (including employee records) and collect sex or gender information.

The guidelines outline a consistent standard of evidence when gender is being verified or changed. The guidelines also define the gender classifications of male (m), female (f) and non-binary (x). A person of non-binary gender does not exclusively identify as either male or female.

Assisting transgender, intersex and non-binary customers

People who identify with a gender other than the sex they were assigned at birth or who do not identify exclusively with 1 sex, can request to have this reflected on their records. The Australian Government Guidelines on the Recognition of Sex and Gender have standardised the evidence required for a person to change their sex/gender in personal records held by Australian Government departments and agencies.

When assisting customers to update their gender details, be aware of issues that may include:

  • identification difficulties
  • the need to address the customer by their preferred name and title in line with Our Service Commitments

Level 2 Complaints Officers:

  • Receive complaints from customers who raise concerns about gender issues via warm transfer from Level 1 staff if they have been unable to resolve the matter at first contact
  • May be required to consider gender in relation to complaints, see the Intranet page Gender Guidelines. The Resources page has a link to Gender Guidelines
  • Offer to have the customer's details updated in all service brands (Centrelink, Medicare and Child Support). If accepted by the customer, the Complaints Officer notifies their Manager who will liaise with their equivalent in the other service brands to ensure the update is completed
  • Offer a referral to a social worker if appropriate

For more information, see Changing sex and/or gender details on customer records.

Complaints relating to ATO issues + Read more ...

Complaints are escalated to Level 2 when they are about Australian Taxation Office (ATO) issues or there is a need to collaborate with the ATO about a complaint for the benefit of mutual customers. When the ATO issue cannot be resolved administratively the complaint is escalated by:

  • Level 1 staff, under escalation reason 5 – Complex, cross programme or multiple issues
  • Personalised Services Service Officers or External Complaints – Child Support (ExCCS) Service Officers, via the Agency/ATO Complaint Support Template, the Resources page includes a link to the template

Liaison with the ATO is managed by the escalated complaints team. This ensures customers are given correct and consistent advice, administrative options are considered and customers are only required to liaise with 1 department to have their issues resolved.

Explore administrative options

Complaint Officers must attempt to resolve an ATO complaint using administrative options, current complaint handling procedures and protocols and by seeking technical advice where appropriate.

This includes reviewing the status of the 'Client of Interest' indicator on the customer’s income screen, to ensure the indicator is set correctly. Client of Interest indicator options are:

  • 0 – No information is required from the ATO
  • 1 – Refund and income information is required from the ATO
  • 2 – Income information only is required from the ATO

Use the ATO system, ICP, to gather all relevant information prior to engaging with the ATO for additional support. For information about ICP searches see:

  • ICP Manual link on the Business Systems Interface (BSI) Section HSI page
  • External searches guide - General searches Level 1 - ICP search

Check for Systemic Issues

Check Network News Update (NNU) for issues associated with ATO income transfers and whether any bulk fixes or workarounds will be implemented. The Technical Support Team can also provide information and assistance on systemic issues. Systemic issues have the potential to impact large groups of customers and may create financial hardship.

Implement resolution

An administrative resolution to a complaint will be possible in some circumstances, such as when an amended adjusted taxable income (ATI) is due in whole or in part to a change to at least 1 of the 'other income components' of a parent’s ATI. For child support purposes these include:

  • reportable fringe benefits
  • target foreign income
  • total net investment loss
  • specified tax free pensions or benefits
  • reportable superannuation contributions

Services Australia can amend a child support assessment to use the amended other income components of the parent’s ATI, such as where the ATO amends the parent’s reportable fringe benefits amount. See Australian Tax Office (ATO) income for Child Support customers for information about ATO amended incomes.

If an amendment of a parent’s income is due in whole or part to a change to at least 1 of the other income components a correcting errors submission and Cuba Incident Form (CIF) is required, see Correcting a complex Child Support error.

To update a customer's ATI, including another income component, see:

  • Australian Tax Office (ATO) income for Child Support customers for information about the date of effect of an amended income
  • Income Cuba Process Help for information about processing in Cuba

Referral to Personalised Services

While the ATO complaint issue is investigated and an outcome provided, the correction to the customer’s assessment may take some time. In the interim refer the customer to Personalised Services for ongoing management and support.

Personalised Services and ExCCS referral for ATO liaison

Personalised Services Service Officers and ExCCS Service Officers request contact with the ATO by emailing the Agency/ATO Complaint Support Template to ATO complaints, see Complaints - Level 2 Complaints. The Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between Services Australia and the ATO makes provision for urgent enquiries, including sensitive complaints from vulnerable customers. If a request is urgent the Personalised Services Service Officer or ExCCS Service Officer will forward the template and phone the Complaints Program Manager to request a more immediate response. The Complaints Program Manager will liaise directly with the ATO liaison officer if required.

The Personalised Services Service Officer or ExCCS Service Officer will obtain permission from the customer for Services Australia to contact the ATO, see Personalised Services - Customer contact for Personalised services table - Step titled Discussion points.

Approval required before ATO contact

If unable to resolve the complaint administratively the Complaints Officer must obtain written approval from the Complaints Service Support Manager (SSM) and approval from the customer before requesting information from the ATO.

To seek approval from the customer for Services Australia to contact the ATO:

  • advise the customer Services Australia must contact the ATO to try and resolve the complaint
  • ask the customer for their permission for Services Australia to liaise with the ATO about the customer's complaint
  • if the customer does not give permission for Services Australia to liaise with the ATO, advise the customer will need to contact the ATO themselves
  • if the customer gives permission for Services Australia to liaise with the ATO:
    • advise the customer that liaison with the ATO does not guarantee their complaint will be resolved
    • advise the customer they do not need to contact the ATO and that the ATO will respond to the Services Australia request for information within 5 business days of receiving the request
    • advise the customer that Services Australia intends to resolve complaints in 10 working days and will keep them informed of any delays
    • document in Cuba that the customer has agreed for Services Australia to contact the ATO

Comply with the Services Australia obligations under the Privacy Act when managing a complaint requiring collaboration and liaison with the ATO.

To seek written approval from the SSM to contact the ATO:

  • Complete the Agency/ATO Complaint Support Template, a link is included on the Resources page. The template must:
    • Only contain information about income and or impacts on the ATO. No other child support issues, such as care, access or payment should be recorded on the template
    • Confirm the customer has given their permission for Services Australia to liaise with the ATO about the customer's amended income complaint
  • Forward the completed template to ATO complaints and copy in the Child Support Complaints Program Manager and their Team Leader, see Complaints - Level 2 Complaints
  • Record this information in the complaint notepad in the Complaints window

Child Support Complaints Program Manager actions request

The Child Support Complaints Program Manager:

  • Emails the Agency/ATO Complaint Support Template to the ATO for action. The referring email includes:
    • Subject: ATO/Agency Complaint Support
    • a reference number, which is the date the complaint was recorded in Cuba and the customers tax file number (TFN), that is, 'date – TFN'
  • Records action taken in the complaint notepad in the Complaints window
  • Records the ATO response in the complaint notepad in the Complaints window and advises the Complaints Officer, Personalised Services Service Officer or ExCCS Service Officer (the ATO will respond within 5 business days as agreed in the MOU)
  • Follows up with the ATO if the ATO has not responded within 5 business days

Note: the MOU makes provision for urgent enquiries, including sensitive complaints from vulnerable customers. If a request is urgent liaise directly with the ATO liaison officer to request a more immediate response.

ATO requests further information

Where the ATO requests information/clarification from Services Australia:

  • the Complaints Program Manager:
    • sends an acknowledgment email to the ATO within 1 day providing the reference number, which is the date the complaint was received from the ATO and the customer's TFN, that is 'date – TFN'
    • records the ATO request in the complaint notepad in the Complaints window
    • creates a Complaint Follow Up intray and changes the due date to the following day
    • routes the Complaint Follow Up intray for action to the Complaints Team Leader with portfolio responsibility for managing ATO requests
    • emails the Team Leader advising of the request for allocation
  • the Complaints Officer:

Accessing call recordings + Read more ...

Call recordings must be accessed as part of a Level 2 complaint investigation in the following circumstances:

  • the complaint is about a Team Leader

First Contact Resolution is not accepted after a Team Leader has listened to a call recording when seeking to resolve a Level 1 complaint and the complaint has been escalated to Level 2

the Level 2 officer considers listening to the call recording will inform their investigation

If a Complaints Officer determines that it is appropriate to provide feedback after they have reviewed a call recording, use the Staff Feedback tool.

Complaints Officers who receive complaints about staff behaviour in most instances, will contact the Team Leader of that staff member the complaint is about and ask that they review the complaint. Do not include the full name of the Service Officer.

Note: if the Complaints Officer is unable to locate a call recording as part of the investigation they must make a decision to uphold or not uphold the complaint based on all the other available information. Not being able to locate a call recording does not automatically result in the complaint being upheld.

Locking + Read more ...

If a customer’s issues are sensitive and urgent and need to be addressed as a high priority, the Complaints Officer will lock a customer until the complaint issue is either resolved or a Personalised Services referral is allocated.   • Customer locked to a Service Officer - liaise with their Team Leader/Service Officer to discuss the actions required and the timeframe for completion to resolve the issue

Overriding decisions + Read more ...

A Service Officer does not have authorisation or delegation to override the decision of the original decision maker. However, it may be appropriate to make a new decision, for example negotiate a new payment arrangement or correct an error to resolve the complaint. See Correcting errors on Child Support cases.

Where a decision has been made to uphold a complaint issue on the basis that an inappropriate decision has been made, for example inappropriate payment arrangement, EW not implemented, s72A notice issued prematurely, discuss the original decision with the relevant Team Leader and negotiate a new arrangement where appropriate. If unable to reach agreement with the decision maker’s Team Leader, refer the matter to the Team Leader who will manage the issue.

Note: Team Leader consultation is important when managing complaints from specialist business areas such as Child Support Intensive Collection Services or PS. If the matter is urgent for example the customer advises garnishee notice issued is causing extreme financial hardship and the Team Leader is unavailable, make a decision based on the available information and advise the Team Leader as soon as practicable.

  • Where a decision has been made to uphold a complaint issue on the basis that an incorrect decision has been made for example care levels incorrectly calculated) consider if it is appropriate to correct the error through an administrative process, see Correcting errors on Child Support cases for more information. If the error cannot be corrected administratively, advise the customer of their objection or appeal rights and the objection or appeal process

Action

Resolution + Read more ...

The outcome of a complaint may not always meet the customer’s expectation but the complaint is considered to be finalised when the following circumstances exist:

  • An investigation has been completed and the complaint has been deemed as upheld or not upheld
  • Customer has been notified of the outcome by phone or in writing. Note: do not wait for the outcome of a CIF or error correction to finalise a complaint.   • Child Support has taken all reasonable actions to resolve the issue

  • not been upheld, explain why Child Support performed the actions that led to the complaint. Refer to the relevant legislation, policy and procedures where appropriate

Note: the terminology ‘upheld' and 'not upheld' are internal processes and these terms should not be discussed with the customer, a Level 2 Complaint would never have an outcome of ‘escalated’.

  • Arrange for the letter to be peer reviewed to check for technical accuracy and it meets the Plain English guidelines
  • If the customer is managed in Personalised Services email the letter to the owning Personalised Services Service Officer/SSM for approval

  • copy the body of the letter into the Complaints window notepad
