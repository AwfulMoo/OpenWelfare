For Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Smart Centre staff only.

This page contains information on assessing qualification for the AIC Scheme.

Eligibility assessment

Testing eligibility requirements for AIC

This table describes the steps to correctly assess qualification for the AIC Scheme.

Step

Action

1

Australian citizens or permanent residents or New Zealand citizens permanently settled in Australia + Read more ...

Are the student and the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) applicant Australian citizens or permanent residents or New Zealand citizens permanently settled in Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the student is not qualified for AIC. Go to Step 9

2

Age requirements + Read more ...

Does the student meet the AIC age requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the student is not qualified for AIC. Go to Step 9

3

Isolation conditions + Read more ...

Are isolation conditions for AIC met?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the student is not qualified for AIC. Go to Step 9

4

Enrolment in approved study + Read more ...

Is the student enrolled in approved study for AIC?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the student is not qualified for AIC. Go to Step 9

5

Other government assistance + Read more ...

Is the student or applicant receiving other Australian Government assistance?

Note: families of students receiving AIC may also receive Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for the same student.

  • Yes, the student's eligibility or rate of AIC payment may be affected. For more information, see Australian Government Assistance other than AIC received by applicant or student. If the student is receiving:
    • Disability Support Pension (DSP) or Parenting Payment Single (PPS) and is studying at primary or equivalent ungraded level, they may quality for AIC Pensioner Education Supplement. Go to Step 6
    • (or a parent is receiving on their behalf) another Australian Government income support payment, education or training assistance or similar payments, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Custodial or state care + Read more ...

Is the student in a custodial institution or wholly or substantially supported while in State authorised care?

  • Yes, the student is not qualified for AIC. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Approved living arrangement and allowance criteria + Read more ...

Check if the student is in one of the following living arrangements:

  • Boarding away from home in order to have daily access to appropriate education
  • Living at home while enrolled in distance education study, undertaking home schooling or other approved study
  • Living in the family's second home so they have daily access to a local education institution
  • In one of the above living arrangements but receives Disability Support Pension or Parenting Payment Single and is undertaking primary or equivalent ungraded study

Is the student in one of the above living arrangements?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, the student is not qualified for AIC, go to Step 9

8

Student may be qualified for AIC + Read more ...

Advise the applicant to lodge a claim for AIC.

If claim for AIC already lodged, see Assessing claims and forms for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC).

9

Student not qualified for AIC + Read more ...

Check qualification to other Centrelink administered payments for students.

See Rejecting an Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme claim.
