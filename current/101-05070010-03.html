Complex Assessment Officer referrals

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Program reviews of private trust and private companies + Read more ...

Is the customer responding to a request for information about a program review of private trust and/or private company?

  • Yes, scan the documents, including the program review letter if provided.
    • These documents will be allocated to CAOs by the system.
    • CAO referral is not required for these reviews
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Pre-referral actions + Read more ...

Before making a CAO Referral, assess customer’s circumstances:

  • If the customer indicates they have not been involved in a private trust or company in the last 5 years, but their record is linked to a private trust or company record, a CAO referral is required to update the information
  • For new claims:
    • If it is clear the claim is to be rejected, for example, basic eligibility reasons are not met, do not refer the case to the CAO
    • CAO referrals are not to be made for claims that will be rejected for a qualification reason
    • If a CAO assessment is required to determine payability, the CAO report must be completed and the recommendations applied before assessing the claim
  • Update the relationship status of the customer before the referral. For example, link or unlink as required.
    Note: if the referral is part of a new claim, do this outside of the claim activity
  • Make sure Private Trust (MOD PT) and/or Private Company (MOD PC), provided in person or as part of an online
  • Tell the customer about any relevant modules and information required
  • Scan any documents provided. Do not create a CAO referral where it is evident that required supporting documentation has not been provided. Make a request for information
  • Check for any existing CAO Referrals:
    • In Customer First check the Activity List (AL) and the Interaction History screens and Process Direct
    • Only one referral is required including for partnered customers
    • If there are duplicate or multiple referrals, cancel all but the first
    • Where an existing CAO referral for a 'reassessment' is required for a 'claim', add the keyword CAONCL to ensure the correct work allocation priority is set
  • If an additional CAO referral reason or additional details have been provided, annotate the first referral with the details. No new referral is required
  • If AUSTRAC reports were obtained as part of the initial review, refer to the AUSTRAC Access and Disclosure documents to make sure that they were not used as evidence and are not forwarded to the CAO as part of the referral

Is there an existing CAO referral and there is a need for immediate or urgent completion of the CAO assessment?

  • Yes, see Table 2
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Assets hardship + Read more ...

Is a CAO referral being considered for an assessment of assets hardship?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

Note: When a customer is being paid under assets hardship provisions and notifies of a change in circumstance, income or assets, a CAO reassessment request is to be sent via a Fast Note, see Assets hardship provisions.

4

Assets hardship CAO + Read more ...

Before inviting a claim under assets hardship provisions, the case must be discussed with a CAO. CAOs can advise of the issues to be considered and concessions that may be available to the customer.

To find a CAO in the Asset Hardship Team, in Office Locator select the People tab and search for 'Assets hardship'.

Service Officers are to take no action to approve or reject payment under the assets hardship rules. Refer all cases to the CAO for determination.

Has the case been discussed with a CAO from the Asset Hardship Team?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, create an open work item using Fast Note. Select Auto Text, use Complex Assessment > Request for CAO Action > CAO Asset hardship. Include an overview of the special circumstances that have led to the customer seeking consideration under assets hardship. The CAO will contact the customer to discuss their circumstances and initiate a claim if appropriate. Procedure ends here

5

CAO assessment reason + Read more ...

Determine the reason the CAO assessment is needed:

  • Home Equity Access Scheme, formerly Pension Loans Scheme (PLS)) see Table 2
  • Special Disability Trust (SDT), see Table 2

Check if the customer and/or partner are involved in or claiming one or more of the following:

  • Private Trust in the last 5 years (including bank accounts held in trust)
  • Private Company in the last 5 years (unless the private company acts as trustee only for Self Managed Superannuation Fund (SMSF), and does not trade, nor hold any assets nor liabilities in its own right. An assessment of these private companies is not required)
    Note: where the customer's rate is reduced due to a trust distribution or company dividend and they may be experiencing family and domestic violence (FDV), include this in the CAO referral notes
  • Home Equity Access Scheme (formerly Pension Loans Scheme (PLS))
  • Special Disability Trust (SDT)
  • Farm Household Allowance (FHA) reviews
  • Assets hardship
  • Partnerships and sole traders, including rural, where there are multiple business activities that are unrelated. This excludes investment income and rental income, see Offsetting of profit and losses between businesses
  • Trading partnerships of more than 2 members and trading partnership/sole traders that hold multiple encumbrances and secured loans
  • Irrecoverable loans/failed loans/scams
  • Life interest or surrender of a life interest
  • Beneficial ownership and constructive trusts
  • Actuarial valuations
  • Granny flat arrangement where deprivation may have occurred
  • Transfer of farm for forgone wages
  • Sale leaseback and sale on terms or term contracts
  • Contrived arrangements (cases involving situations where a customer manipulates their circumstances to take advantage of the income/assets test)
  • A contingent, remainder or reversionary interest created by a customer or acquired for valuable consideration
  • Asset-test exempt lifetime or life expectancy income streams from Self-Managed Superannuation Funds (SMSF) or Small APRA Superannuation Funds (SAF)
  • Annuity - Deferred annuity, a purchased annuity or annuity received on transfer of property for disposal purposes (not an annuity purchased from a financial institution)

Are the customer and/or partner involved in or claiming one or more of the above?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, if not specified as a CAO assessment reason, for all other types, follow normal escalation processes. Technical Peer Support (TPS) and /or Service Support Officer (SSO) are to investigate the individual circumstances before any referral to a CAO is made. See Service Officer role when seeking Technical Peer Support (TPS) in Smart Centres and Access Support Centres (ASC)

Note: if the customer makes a Request for urgent processing of a new claim when experiencing financial hardship and meets the criteria, the CAO Referral must be made before any escalation action is undertaken. Make sure all required documents have been provided before making the CAO referral, including where urgent processing is required,

If urgent processing is required and the CAO referral has been made and remains pending assessment, see Table 2.

6

Deregistered Private Companies + Read more ...

Is a CAO referral being considered for a deregistered Private Company?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Deregistered Private Companies + Read more ...

Is the entity a deregistered Private Company and either of the following has occurred:

  • Customer/partner are already linked to the Company's CRN and the Company CRN has not already been coded as 'Wound Up'. Check the Trust and Company Miscellaneous (TRMD) screen (Environment A) via the Company CRN (Wound Up indicator Y)
  • Customer/partner have declared gifting/deprivation in relation to the deregistered Private Company in the last 5 years before claiming
  • Customer/partner have declared an involvement in relation to the deregistered Private Company while receiving an income support payment
  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, a CAO referral is not required. Procedure ends here

8

CAO referral for new claims + Read more ...

Is a CAO referral required for a new claim or current customers with new trust/company involvement, or LIC renewal?

  • Yes:
    • If using Process Direct, go to Step 9
    • If using Customer First, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 10

9

CAO referral in Process Direct + Read more ...

  • Select > Referral
  • Select Referral Type: Complex Assessment Officer
  • Update the KPI Date. For help with Key Performance Indicators, see Timeliness standards - Claims processing
  • Update the Expected Start Date. For LIC claims, record the date 8 weeks before the date of grant
  • Include the Referral Reason/s for the CAO to assess, for example, 'Trusts', 'Asset Hardship Claim', 'Private Companies'
  • In the Document List check the box against all documents provided, noting that the list will update when the referral reason/s are updated
  • In the Notes field, include a reason for requiring CAO assessment. The reasons are required regardless of having nominated the Referral Reason

Go to Step 11.

10

CAO referral in Customer First + Read more ...

Run the CAO Referral guided procedure.

For a new claim or current customers with new trust/company involvement, or LIC renewal:

  • Select Assessment Type: New Claim
  • Update the KPI Due Date. For help with Key Performance Indicators, see Timeliness standards - Claims processing
  • Update the Date payment is due to commence. For LIC claims, record the date 8 weeks before the date of grant

CAO referral required for non-new claim assessment:

  • Select Assessment Type: Reassessment
  • Where the customer is suspended FRC or FRT, as failed to reply to a CAO request for information, select Customer Suspended: Yes

For new claims and reassessments:

  • Select the Case Type to populate the document list (noting not all Case Types require documents)
  • More than one Case Type can be identified to add documents. Ensure to select all documents provided and scanned for each identified Case Type
  • Other Referral can be used to identify additional Case Types and free text is also available to list other supporting documents supplied
  • Update Reason For Referral and DOC Details with reason for requiring CAO assessment. The reasons are required regardless of having nominated the referral Case Type

For a CAO reassessment referral required to support a Business Integrity assessment select BI Referral and select the assessment reason:

  • Authorised Review Officer, for ARO referrals (includes keyword AROCAO)
  • Random Sample, for Random Survey referrals (includes keyword RSSCAO)
  • Yes -Other, for any other Business Integrity referral, including those from Debt team (included keyword BICAO)

Note: for Business Integrity referrals as part of Taskforce Integrity, after the CAO referral is created, manually add the keyword TKFCAO

For FHA review referrals:

  • select the Payment Type Exceptional Circumstances Relief Payment
  • Add Keyword CAOFHA
  • To make sure delivery of FHA referrals to FHA CAOs, the referral doc must remain open and must be in region code FHH

Note: within the CAO Referral tools, the following Referral Reason/ Case Type may be available, however they are not appropriate referral reasons:

  • Special Disability Trust (SDT)
  • Annuities
  • Multiple realisation of Superannuation Investments
  • Unlisted/ overseas shares

11

Hold activity pending referral outcome + Read more ...

If a CAO referral is made within Process Direct, the new claim status should automatically change to On Hold with reason Referral to CAO and a DOC will be recorded on the Document List (DL) screen with Customer Referral work item ID. The claim will remain On Hold until the CAO referral is completed.

If the activity (including new claims) status has not updated automatically, place the activity On Hold for 42 days. The activity status will update when the CAO referral is completed.

Note: on hold for 42 days does not reflect expected processing time.

If the activity stops to be on hold and the CAO referral remains open:

  • Confirm the CAO referral is required and the CAO assessment is not completed
  • Refer to progress notes and determine if the assessment is pending a CAO request for information
  • Return the status to On Hold for a minimum of 14 days, for the assessment to be completed
  • DOC on hold reason. Where an open DOC is necessary, hold for the same duration
  • If there is evidence an urgent escalation is required, see Table 2.

If the CAO was unable to make a determination because the customer failed to provide requested information, the CAO will record a DOC including details of the outstanding information. See Rejecting a Centrelink claim.

12

CAO assessment completion + Read more ...

When the CAO assessment is completed, the Service Officer must check the CAO assessment DOC for any actions that they must complete.

Information received post CAO assessment

If the customer provides further information related to the CAO assessment and before the claim finalisation, the Service Officer is required to make a new CAO referral.

This includes where the CAO is unable to complete the assessment, as the customer failed to respond to a request for information. Before rejecting the claim, check the customer record to determine if the requested information has been provided since the CAO referral was set to Not Required.

In these circumstances, it is appropriate for a Team Leader or Service Support Officer to escalate for immediate processing. See Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing.

CAO escalation process + Read more ...

An escalation to request CAO action may be required for:

  • Home Equity Access Scheme (formerly Pension Loans Scheme) - Loan repayments see Repayment of Pension Loans Scheme (PLS) loans
  • Home Equity Access Scheme (formerly Pension Loans Scheme) - Claim withdrawal see Processing claims for the Pension Loans Scheme (PLS)

  • Exemption for income trust distribution and company dividend for customers affected by Family Domestic Violence

Is there a specific advice provided within Operational Blueprint to escalate a request for CAO action?

  • Yes, create an open work item using Fast Note. Select Complex Assessment > Request for CAO action. Select the Fast Note related to the CAO referral reason
    • CAO General
    • CAO Farmhouse Allowance
    • CAO Asset hardship
    • CAO Forgone Wage
    • CAO Special Disability Trust
    • CAO Home Equity Access Scheme
    • CAO Business Integrity
    • Social Worker FDV assessment for CAO

Note: use the CAO General Fast Note, when the reason for advice or CAO escalation does not match a listed referral reason. For example, Trusts, Companies, Residential Aged Care

  • No, go to Step 2

2

Immediate processing of CAO referral due to priority processing + Read more ...

Where immediate processing criteria are met, complete the referral. See Table 1.

Where a CAO referral has been made and remains pending assessment, an escalation may be required.

Is the customer considered vulnerable and/or have complex needs that requires priority processing by a CAO?

  • Yes, go to Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Urgent processing of CAO referral due to financial hardship + Read more ...

For urgent cases where financial hardship criteria are met, first complete the referral as instructed CAO assessment reason.

If a customer's circumstances require urgent processing of a new claim due to financial hardship, an escalation may be required.

Do all of the following apply?

  • Customer claiming or receiving a payment covered by financial hardship provisions
  • Customer meets the financial hardship criteria
  • CAO referral has been made
  • All required supporting documents provided

If:

  • Yes, make a request for urgent action on the CAO assessment. Create an open work item using a Fast Note. Select Auto Text, use Complex Assessment > CAO Financial Hardship. Select either:
    • CAO FHA financial hardship (for Farm Household Allowance)
    • CAO General financial hardship (for all other payments and CAO referral reasons)
  • No, do not escalate. Procedure ends here
