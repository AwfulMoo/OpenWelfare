Conduct SOI in Process Direct

Table 1: This table outlines how to conduct an SOI in Process Direct.

Step

Action

1

Medical Assessment Team (MAT) Assessors attempt SOI where outcome is to reject due to Insufficient Medical Evidence (IME) + Read more ...

Where a MAT Assessor determines there is insufficient medical evidence to assess a customer’s medical eligibility for DSP, they:

  • attempt to contact the customer to deliver the SOI (as outlined in the Customer First tab)
  • record the SOI outcome using one of the following relevant SOI Fast Notes in Customer First:
    • DSP NCL SOI Successful - Assessor successfully completed SOI
    • DSP NCL SOI Unsuccessful - Assessor SOI attempt was unsuccessful
    • DSP NCL SOI Not Required - SOI not required (for example, the customer is deceased or no contact details)

In certain circumstances, the Assessor does not record an SOI Fast Note. If appropriate, the Assessor can give the customer an extra 5 days to provide further medical evidence to support their DSP claim. If 5 days is given, the Assessor:

  • records the contact via AssessServ Contact Fast Note, and
  • holds the MAT Referral

After 5 days, if medical evidence from the customer:

  • is not received, the Assessor:
    • submits an SA479 recommending to reject the DSP with reason IME, and
    • records the SOI Successful Fast Note
  • is received and there is no change from the MAT recommendation to reject with reason IME:
    • the Assessor submits an SA479 as reject IME
    • attempts an SOI, and
    • records the outcome via relevant SOI Fast Note
  • is received and the MAT recommendation changes to another rejection reason (for example, NDT), manifest grant or JCA referral required:
    • the Assessor does not record an SOI outcome
    • Disability Processing Service Officers must follow usual process to action claim and conduct SOI

Does MAT/SA479 recommend to reject the DSP claim with reason IME?

  • No, go to Step 2
  • Yes, no further action is required:
    • do not run SOI Workflow in Process Direct
    • procedure ends here

2

SOI for claims selected for QMA + Read more ...

If the claim has been selected for Quality Management Application (QMA) the Quality Management Officer (QMO) must complete the SOI. Exceptions to this include where the:

  • Service Officer who submitted the claim is undertaking consolidation of their training
  • Smart Centre Critical Response Team (SCCRT) is managing the claim. In this instance, the Service Officer who submitted the claim will attempt the SOI (see below), or
  • the claim is recommended for rejection with reason Insufficient Medical Evidence (IME). In this instance, the MAT Assessor attempts the SOI

Note: Service Officers undertaking consolidation must add 'As part of DSP consolidation, the SOI will be actioned by the Service Officer that submitted the claim.' to the claim finalisation notes. This will allow the QMO to easily identify these claims.

QMO responsibilities:

  • complete the QMA check
  • find and fix any errors
  • complete the work item
  • give feedback to the submitting Service Officer
  • carefully check the claim finalisation notes to determine if the Service Officer who submitted the claim will conduct the SOI themselves. Check there is an indication the Service Officer is in consolidation of their DSP learning, or the claim is managed by the SCCRT
  • action the SOI through the Service Offer Interview workflow in Process Direct, as required in all other instances
  • complete any post SOI actions. For example:
    • Release of Medical Assessments
    • Request for SME Quality Check

Smart Centre Critical Response Team (SCCRT):

  • SOIs for DSP claims finalised by SCCRT are not to be actioned by the QMO when the claim is selected for QMA
  • SCCRT staff will action the SOI before finalising the claim and record SOI details in the Grant/Rejection DOC
  • QMO must carefully check the customers record to determine if the claim has been finalised by SCCRT
  • claims are identified by a SCCRT DOA (Display on Access) DOC. The outcome of SOI contact will be documented in the Grant/Rejection DOC
  • QMO must complete QMA per normal process
  • all follow-up actions, including the SOI Workflow, will be completed by SCCRT
  • SCCRT will monitor the claim and action the SOI Workflow when claim is finalised. QMO does not need to advise SCCRT when the QMA check has been completed

Service Officers must regularly check the QMA Feedback tile in Process Direct for their QMA results and feedback.

3

SOI on overtime + Read more ...

Claim finalisation SOIs during overtime:

  • during overtime, SOI attempts are still to be actioned (being mindful of the customer's local time zone)
  • record all successful and unsuccessful SOI attempts using the Service Offer Interview workflow
  • when contacting a customer:
    • introduce yourself
    • inform the customer that the call may be recorded for quality assurance and training purposes. See Calling a customer or returning a customer's call
    • authenticate the customer, or their authorised nominee, before personal information being disclosed. See Authenticating a Centrelink customer
    • advise work is being conducted outside normal business hours to action their claim
    • ask if it is convenient for them to discuss the outcome at that time

If the customer advises it is not a convenient time, explain:

  • the claim has been actioned and written advice will be sent
  • if they require a detailed explanation they can contact and request this after they receive their letter

Update using Service Offer Interview workflow recording contact as successful and add text, ‘contact successful however no claim SOI conducted on OT at customer request, claim finalised and customer advised decision letter will be issued'.

If the customer:

  • agrees to proceed, conduct the SOI
  • reacts adversely to the SOI discussion e.g. makes threats of self-harm:
    • see the Process page of Customers talking about suicide or self-harm
    • follow after hours procedures for escalations
    • if a Service Officer requires personal support after a difficult customer, speak to a Team Leader or Manager. They can provide critical incident reporting and staff support. Where required, Service Officers can contact the Employee assistance program (EAP)

SOI exceptions during overtime

For all claims actioned during overtime (weeknights and weekends) where Social Work assistance is not indicated to be required, Service Officers must review the customer record and determine if an SOI should be attempted outside of normal business hours before finalising claim.

Examples of exceptions where an SOI may not be appropriate:

  • manifest eligibility (e.g. terminal, nursing home level care etc.)
  • it would adversely impact the customers wellbeing
  • bereavement cases
  • clearly documented CRU complaints
  • likely to result in an escalation such as media interest
  • if it is clear from the customer record they require an interpreter for Services Australia interactions
  • conflicting time zones - non-appropriate local time zone for customer contact to be attempted

If it is not appropriate to conduct SOI on overtime, DSP claim should not be finalised, refer to Rejecting a new claim Disability Support Pension (DSP) including manifest rejections:

  • annotate the Progress of Claim DL/Notes indicating that claim has not been finalised because SOI attempt on overtime was not considered appropriate
  • apply any updates via Selective Application of Data (SAD)
  • select Status icon and change status to On Hold until next working day for reason Pending Customer Contact
  • Hold to User

4

SOI during normal business hours + Read more ...

Check circumstances

An SOI is required if the:

  • DSP claim:
    • has been rejected
    • was granted and the customer or their partner has earnings, unless the customer and/or partner meet either the Pension Monthly Exception Rule or the 1WE or 2WE exception. See Recording and correcting employment income details
    • will be granted and an update to their Family Tax Benefit is required, or
  • customer, or their nominee, has requested contact about the claim outcome

If the DSP claim has gone to QMA, the SOI should be attempted by the Quality Management Officer (QMO) once the check has been completed.

If DSP claim is finalised after the QMA check, QMO must action the Service Offer Interview workflow. QMO is responsible for completing any follow-up actions as a result of SOI. For example, release of Medical Assessments and request for SME quality checks.

An attempt to contact the customer by phone must be made on the same day the claim is finalised wherever possible, to deliver the SOI and advise the outcome of their claim.

Check if SOI is required + Read more ...

In limited circumstances, an SOI is not required. This includes where the customer:

  • is deceased
  • is in prison
  • is in psychiatric confinement
  • has no contact number on record
  • identified as not appropriate. For example, customer is overseas
  • has service channel restrictions preventing telephone contact under a current Managed Service Plan

Note: if a customer has a Managed Service Plan, contact is first made with the One Main Contact (OMC), Back up Contact or Personalised Services Service Officer (PSSO) if assigned, to discuss how to best approach the SOI.
See Customer service delivered through a One Main Contact (OMC) as part of a Managed Service Plan (MSP).

In the above circumstances, where a correspondence nominee or Executor arrangement exists, an SOI attempt should be made.

Prepare for delivering a quality SOI + Read more ...

Before contacting the customer, including during the lead in time of 5 minutes after issuing a pre-call SMS, check all aspects of the customer's claim.

Navigate to Documents icon to review documents (MAT, JCA, DMA) to assist with explanation of decision.

Claim rejection

If the claim is a rejection because of:

  • medical ineligibility, review MAT/JCA/DMA reports, consider what questions the customer may have, and possible responses
  • non-medical ineligibility including rejection reasons OTH, IME, FRC, FME, GCD & DIA, consider what questions the customer may have, possible responses and what further documents are required

Review the customer’s circumstances such as marital status, children in care.

Consider possible alternative income support payments and family assistance.

Consider if any internal or external referrals may be required. For example, Social Worker, employment assistance. Note whether an ESAt is required, either because the claim has been rejected on the basis of the DMA outcome, or where MAT has recommended this in the SA479.

Claim grant

If the claim is a grant, consider:

  • what information is required and how to explain this to the customer
  • what questions they may have and possible responses
  • reporting requirements if customer/partner have employment income. For details see Recording and correcting employment income details

Check Snapshot Icon for any outstanding activities. After navigating and reviewing information, select Previous button to step back to the Service Offer Interview Summary page.

Previous button may need to be selected multiple times if multiple pages have been visited. If unable to navigate back to Service Offer Interview Summary page, key START in the Super Key field > select Service Offer Interview workflow > Next > view Service Offer Interview Summary page

Contact successful + Read more ...

If contact is successful:

  • complete the privacy statement
  • confirm the customer's identity
  • deliver the SOI using the Service Offer Interview Summary Report:
    • Customer Details. This information is available to assist in verification of customer identity
    • Claim Summary. Displays claim ID, date of claim submission, claim outcome (granted or rejected) and the reason for the claim outcome where the claim has been rejected
    • Explain claim outcome. Displays appropriate talking points. The content is dependent on whether the claim was rejected for medical or non-medical reasons

      Granting DSP + Read more ...

      The discussion should include:

      • basic details confirming:
        • preferred language
        • child/family details and other information is correct
      • customer support
        • advise details about payments and concessions being made available to the customer
        • information about where and when the payments will be made and how much
      • if the customer is currently on an income support payment ensure they are made aware of the possible change in payment delivery for a pension
        • notification requirements
        • rate of payment
        • reporting requirements
      • updating Family Tax Benefit income estimates
        • if the customer has a dependent child, (under the age of 19) in their care and they or their partner are not in receipt of Family Tax Benefit, tell the customer to use the Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website to see if they may be eligible for Family Tax Benefit. See Updating Family Tax Benefit income estimates
      • self-service options:
        • Online account. Access via myGov
        • Express Plus mobile apps. Customers can use the app to do most of their business
        • Centrelink phone self service. Range of options including reporting employment income
        • Service Australia website. Customers can access a full range of online guides
      • customer service, advise details about:
        • internal referrals. For example, social worker or Financial Information Service (FIS) Officer
        • other services being accessed by the customer, service centre and Smart Centre hours and contact details
        • other internal services such as the JobSearch facilities

          Rejecting DSP + Read more ...

          The discussion should include:

          • due to failure to reply to correspondence, or due to insufficient medical evidence, tell the customer that once all required documents are provided the claim may be reassessed
          • on non-medical grounds, explain the reason. For example, income exceeded cut-off limit, residency criteria not met
          • on medical grounds, explain the reason for rejection including:
            • the relevant DSP medical eligibility requirements. For example, requirement for conditions to be fully diagnosed, treated and stabilised
            • why the customer did not meet these requirements based on available medical evidence
            • the assessment(s) undertaken to support the decision (such as JCA and DMA)
            • these assessments carefully considered all available medical evidence
            • DSP residence rules to the customer

              Discuss possible income support payment + Read more ...

              This section displays if the customer is not current on an income support payment.

              Advise possible alternative income support payments the customer may be eligible for (such as JobSeeker Payment) and explain:

              • how the customer can claim the relevant payment
              • incorrect claim provisions may apply
              • customers aged under 22 assessed as having a partial capacity to work will be independent for Youth Allowance job seeker purposes so the parental income test will not apply
              • how mutual obligation requirements for alternative payments are modified based on JCA recommendations
              • eligibility for Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) and Pharmaceutical Allowance (PA) if the customer has a partial capacity to work
              • the 'claiming DSP' exemption has been end-dated and what this means in regard to meeting participation requirements, and
              • if the customer has a dependent child (under the age of 19) in their care and they or their partner are not in receipt of Family Tax Benefit, the customer can use the Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website to check their eligibility for Family Tax Benefit

                Discuss referral to Employment Services + Read more ...

                If the customer is on a payment which requires participation and has a permanent work capacity which is more than 15 hours per week, discuss Employment Services referrals and requirements for their current income support payment.

                Referral
                Refer (or offer referral) to suitable employment services for support in retraining and building their work capacity. This is particularly important if the claim is rejected because the customer has not actively participated in a Program of Support (POS):

                • focus on connection to the service recommended by the Assessor, such as Disability Employment Services (DES). Take into account the customer's participation requirements. For example, if the customer has a Partial Capacity to Work (PCW) or Temporary Reduced Work Capacity (TRWC) of less than 15 hours per week, they are not required to participate in employment services but should still be strongly encouraged to participate on a voluntary basis
                • tell the customer their participation and periods of exemption or non-participation in recommended employment services may affect the outcome of any future claims for DSP (this advice must be recorded as an annotation of the Rejection DOC). The customer must not be advised a future DSP claim will be automatically successful if they participate in a program. Note: provision of this information is especially important if the claim is being rejected because the customer has not actively participated in a POS or due to an insufficient impairment rating that may change on appeal or in the future
                • if an ESAt is required as per recommendation by the Medical Assessment Team (MAT) or DSP rejection after a Disability Medical Assessment (DMA)

                  Discuss referral to a Social Worker + Read more ...

                  To commence discussion of Social Worker intervention, if required, and action referral as appropriate.

                  Reviews and appeals + Read more ...

                  Before discussing review and appeal options, check the customer understands the decision was made taking into account all the available information. If the customer:

                  • is not satisfied with the SOI explanation of the claim decision, advise them of their review and appeal rights:
                    • they can apply for a formal review at any time
                    • it is important to apply for a formal review within 13 weeks of being notified about the decision. If their application is made more than 13 weeks after being notified of the decision and the decision can be changed, they may only receive their entitlement from the date they applied for the formal review
                  • wants a:
                    • further explanation of the decision from an officer not involved in their claim, record their request
                    • formal review of the decision, record their application

                  Check if the customer will be providing more evidence (including medical evidence) not previously provided. If they are, encourage them to lodge it as soon as possible (preferably within 7 days). Advise that further assessments may be required depending on the new evidence.

                  Note: the customer can apply for a formal review whether or not more evidence will be provided.

                  SOI completed with customer + Read more ...

                  Return to Service Offer Interview Summary page.

                  • Outcome, select Successful
                  • Outcome Reason defaults to Service Offer Interview Finalised
                  • Select topics discussed with the customer, select appropriate checkboxes to reflect information discussed
                  • Free text is available under Further Details to add more information

                  Complete the Service Offer Interview Summary screen and select Next.

                  Go to Step 14.

                  Contact unsuccessful + Read more ...

                  Check if ESAt required + Read more ...

                  The Service Offer Interview workflow goes to the Referral page to complete an ESAt referral.

                  • a DMA report has been submitted indicating medical ineligibility requiring a reassessment of work capacity and/or employment services referrals

                  Note: the Create an ESAt referral drop-down defaults to No when the SOI outcome is indicated as unsuccessful or not required.

                    • post MAT, go to Step 15

                  • annotate the Progress of Claim DOC indicating that claim has not been finalised because SOI attempt on overtime was not considered appropriate
                  • apply any updates via Selective Application of Data (SAD)
                  • run the Streaming & Progress DOC script selecting the SOI on hold reason (^^SOI keyword is applied to the claim activity).
                  • Hold to User

                  4

                  SOI during normal business hours + Read more ...

                  Check circumstances

                  An SOI is required if the:

                  • DSP claim:
                    • has been rejected
                    • was granted and the customer or their partner has earnings, unless they meet the Pension Monthly Exception Rule or the 1WE or 2WE exception. See Recording and correcting employment income details
                    • will be granted and an update to their Family Tax Benefit is required, or
                  • customer (or their nominee) has requested contact about the claim outcome

                  If the DSP claim has gone to QOL, the SOI should be attempted by the Service Officer who submitted the claim once it has successfully passed.

                  The Service Officer who finalised the DSP claim is responsible for completing any follow-up actions as a result of SOI such as, release of Medical Assessments and request for SME quality checks.

                  An attempt to contact the customer by phone must be made on the same day the claim is finalised wherever possible, to deliver the SOI and advise the outcome of their claim.

                  5

                  Check if SOI is required + Read more ...

                  In limited circumstances, an SOI is not required. This includes where the customer:

                  • is deceased
                  • is in prison
                  • is in psychiatric confinement
                  • has no contact number on record
                  • SOI is not appropriate. For example, customer is overseas
                  • has service channel restrictions under a current Managed Service Plan - preventing telephone contact

                  Note: if a customer has a Managed Service Plan, contact the One Main Contact (OMC), Back up Contact or Personalised Services Service Officer (PSSO) if assigned, to discuss how to best approach the SOI. See Customer service delivered through a One Main Contact (OMC) as part of a Managed Service Plan (MSP).

                  In the above circumstances, where a correspondence nominee or executor arrangement exists, an SOI attempt should be made.

                  • Yes, go to Step 6
                  • No, run and complete the Fast Note. Select Disabilities, use Claims > DSP NCL SOI Not Required

                  • Yes, go to Step 11
                  • No, procedure ends here

                          Rejecting DSP + Read more ...

                          The discussion should include:

                          • due to failure to reply to correspondence, or insufficient medical evidence, tell the customer that once all required documents are provided the claim may be reassessed
                          • on non-medical grounds, explain the reason. For example, income exceeded cut-off limit, residency criteria not met
                          • on medical grounds, explain the reason for rejection including:
                            • the relevant DSP medical eligibility requirements. For example, requirement for conditions to be fully diagnosed, treated and stabilised
                            • why the customer did not meet these requirements based on available medical evidence
                            • the assessment(s) undertaken to support the decision. For example, JCA and DMA, and
                            • these assessments carefully considered all available medical evidence
                            • DSP residence rules to the customer

                          Discuss possible income support payment

                          This section displays if the customer is not current on an income support payment.

                          Advise possible alternative income support payments the customer may be eligible for (such as JobSeeker Payment) and explain:

                          • how the customer can claim the relevant payment
                          • incorrect claim provisions may apply
                          • customers aged under 22 assessed as having a partial capacity to work will be independent for Youth Allowance job seeker purposes so the parental income test will not apply
                          • how mutual obligation requirements for alternative payments are modified based on JCA recommendations
                          • eligibility for Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) and Pharmaceutical Allowance (PA) if the customer has a partial capacity to work
                          • the 'claiming DSP' exemption has been end-dated and what this means in regard to meeting participation requirements
                          • if the customer has a dependent child (under the age of 19) in their care and they or their partner are not in receipt of Family Tax Benefit, the customer can use the Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website to check their eligibility for Family Tax Benefit

                          Discuss referral to Employment Services

                          If the customer is on a payment which requires participation and has a permanent work capacity which is more than 15 hours per week, discuss Employment Services referrals and requirements for their current income support payment.

                          Referral

                          Offer referral to suitable employment services for support in retraining and building their work capacity. This is particularly important if the claim is rejected because the customer has not actively participated in a Program of Support (POS):

                          • focus on connection to the service recommended by the Assessor, such as Disability Employment Services (DES). Take into account the customer's participation requirements.
                          • for example, if the customer has a Partial Capacity to Work (PCW) or Temporary Reduced Work Capacity (TRWC) of less than 15 hours per week, they are not required to participate in employment services but should still be strongly encouraged to participate on a voluntary basis
                          • tell the customer their participation and periods of exemption or non participation in recommended employment services may affect the outcome of any future claims for DSP (this advice must be recorded as an annotation of the Rejection DOC). Do not tell the customer a future DSP claim will be automatically successful if they participate in a program. Note: provision of this information is especially important if the claim is being rejected because the customer has not actively participated in a POS or due to an insufficient impairment rating that may change on appeal or in the future
                          • if an ESAt is required as per recommendation by the Medical Assessment Team (MAT) or DSP Rejection after a Disability Medical Assessment (DMA)

                          Discuss referral to a Social Worker

                          Commence discussion of Social Worker intervention, if required, and action referral as appropriate.

                          Reviews and appeal

                          Before discussing review and appeal options, check the customer understands that the decision was made taking into account all the available information:

                          • If the customer is not satisfied with the SOI explanation of the claim decision, advise them of the review and appeal rights, including:
                            • they can apply for a formal review at any time
                            • it is important to apply for a formal review within 13 weeks of being notified about the decision. If their application is made more than 13 weeks after being notified of the decision and the decision can be changed, they may only receive their entitlement from the date they applied for the formal review
                          • If the customer wants a:
                            • further explanation of the decision from an officer not involved in their claim, record their request
                            • formal review of the decision, record their application
                          • Check if the customer will be providing more evidence (including medical evidence) not previously provided. If they are, encourage them to lodge it as soon as possible (preferably within 7 days). Advise that further assessments may be required depending on the new evidence

                          Note: the customer can apply for a formal review whether or not more evidence will be provided.
