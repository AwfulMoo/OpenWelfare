Contact the customer

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer contact + Read more ...

Make genuine attempts to contact the customer to negotiate repayment of the debt. See Telephone standards.

The Resources page for Quality Call Framework Quality Call Standards at a Glance.

Was contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Successful customer contact + Read more ...

Authenticate the customer before speaking with them. Suggested authentication questions for:

  • providers and practices, ask them to provide:
    • full name of provider,
    • practice location/address as listed on SAP, and
    • practice manager's details and contact number
  • organisations (Simplified Billing customers and organisations with debts), ask them to provide:
    • name of organisation
    • current address as listed on SAP, and
    • details, contact number and/or email of the contact person

There is no requirement for a Service Officer to disclose their full name to customers, but they must:

  • identify themselves, and
  • explain they are calling from Services Australia - Medicare

Use the following greetings as a guide:

  • Inbound calls: 'Thank you for calling Services Australia, you are speaking with <your name>'
  • Outbound calls: 'Hello this is <your name> from Services Australia - Medicare. May I speak with <customer name>?'

See Privacy concerns when calling customers, returning calls or leaving messages on answering machines.

For outbound calls, check that it is a convenient time to speak with the customer. If it is not a convenient time, organise another time to call back.

Consider the following:

  • Negotiate the debt as soon as possible after it is raised
  • Negotiate to achieve the best repayment outcome
  • The preference is payment in full by the due date
  • Send a letter to confirm negotiations

Is customer being transferred to another team?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Unsuccessful customer contact + Read more ...

If possible, leave a message:

  • “Hi this is <name> from Services Australia - Medicare. Please call me back on 1800 037 635 and quote the reference number <Invoice number>. Thank you.”

If contact is unsuccessful send a contact letter to the customer.

Go to Step 5.

4

Transferring calls + Read more ...

To introduce the customer to a Service Officer in another section, make a warm transfer.

When transferring a call:

  • explain to the customer the reason for transferring their call
  • tell the customer:
    "Hi <Customer>. I have <name> from <section (for example, Medicare processing)> on the line. They will be able to assist with your enquiry."

The Resources page has a link on how to use the Cisco phone.

5

Document + Read more ...

Document attempts to contact the customer on their record in SAP, including the:

  • phone number
  • reason for the call
  • if call was transferred, and
  • outcome of the call

For example, 'Called customer on 02 12 345345 and they agreed to repay debt in full by xx/xx/xxxx'.

The Resources page has details on customer contact standards.

Instalment plans

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Paying by instalments or deferring the date of payment + Read more ...

A customer may request to:

  • pay the debt in instalments, or
  • defer the date of payment

To determine an acceptable repayment amount, consider the:

  • customer's capacity to pay, and
  • the value of the debt.

Evidence may be required to support the debtor's claim that they cannot repay in full. This can include:

  • latest tax returns (ATO - Notice of Assessment)
  • financial statements or Evidence of Assets and Liabilities
  • reasons for their claim
  • a statement of financial position, showing their current and expected future financial positions
  • their genuine commitment to repay the debt in the prescribed timeframe
  • any independent assessment and/or advice received
  • proof showing that enforcing the debtor to discharge their debt in 1 year or less will cause financial hardship

Debts should be repaid within 12 months. The delegate may approve a longer period after considering the debtor's circumstances.

Note: only Medicare Debt Recovery staff have delegation to accept instalments for Medicare debts.

When necessary, email the delegate (APS5 or 6 in Medicare Recovery) for advice.

2

Delegate decision + Read more ...

A delegate can accept the instalment plan if:

  • the debt amount is within their delegation
  • they consider the amount of the instalment appropriate
  • the debtor agrees to the repayment schedule, and
  • the debtor understands the consequences if they default on their payments before the debt (including interest) is fully repaid. This may include:
    • legal action,
    • applying interest and
    • referring the debt to an external collection agency

3

Instalment plan approval + Read more ...

Open the Instalment Plan spreadsheet template from the shared drive > Finance > Instalment Plan. See Resources for a link to the Instalment Schedule template.

To set instalment plan up:

  • In the Data Entry tab complete details for the payment plan:
    • Contact Date: key the date the customer contacted (dd/mm/yyyy)
    • Debt Amount: Due amount as on SAP
    • Due Date: As per SAP
    • Repayment: Agreed payment amount per frequency
    • 1st Repayment Date: Agreed date
    • No. of Payments: Auto filled
    • Interest Type: Seek advice from Operational Team Leader (OTL)
    • Interest Rate: Seek advice from OTL
  • A letter auto generates from details entered in the Data Entry tab. Letter can be printed from the Letter tab in the spreadsheet
  • Save the instalment plan to the shared drive > Finance > Instalment Plan using the customer name and customer number. For example, Jones_BB000000XX
  • Print the Letter and Instalment Schedule from tabs in the spreadsheet
  • Email or mail the Letter and Instalment Schedule to the customer

In SAP, run Transaction FBL5N - Customer Line Item Display:

  • Attach the documents to SAP
  • Update the Assignment field. For example, IPF/IPM/IPW. See Resources for a list of the Assignment field values
  • Go to the Dunning Block field and key 'A'

Recovery of Medicare debt - deceased customer

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Recovery of debts for deceased customers + Read more ...

Exercise sensitivity when discussing a debt with:

  • the nominated executor
  • Public Trustee
  • Court, or
  • administrator with authority to deal with the proceeds of the estate

If notification is received via telephone or email advising the customer is deceased, obtain the following:

  • name of caller
  • relationship to the deceased
  • contact telephone number
  • details of the executor (if known)

Send an advice to the deceased customer's estate for all legally recoverable outstanding debts.

2

Contact + Read more ...

Do not pursue any debt until 4 weeks after the customer’s death.

If pursuing recovery of a debt:

  • the debt is only recoverable from the debtors' estate
  • do not accept payment of a debt from any source other than the deceased customer's estate
  • do not garnishee any financial institution account/s belonging to a deceased customer's estate

3

Write off a debt due to no estate or insufficient funds + Read more ...

Write off the debt if:

  • an executor or third party notifies:
    • there is no estate, or
    • the estate has been distributed
  • the estate has:
    • partially repaid the debt, and
    • insufficient funds remaining to pay the balance
  • the executor or any assets/property cannot be located

Obtain written advice from the executor, a relative or carer advising there is no estate. Add these details to SAP notes and update the Assignment field.

4

Not cost effective + Read more ...

Make reasonable effort to recover the amount from the estate.

Write off a debt where it is not cost effective to recover (where the outstanding debt balance is less than $100).

5

Action a write off + Read more ...

See Debt for write off table in Financial Adjustments for Medicare debt for the write off process.

  • Update the Assignment field and create a note on the customer record. For example:
    • After further investigation, customer passed away on <date>
    • Advice received that there is no estate
    • Estate has insufficient funds available, or
    • No details in relation to the executor of the will
  • Include in the SAP note:
    • “Debt of <amount> written off - (not legally recoverable or not cost effective to recover) under the Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Rule 2014, rule 11, Recovery of debts”.

  • If the debt:

Record the details of the dispute outcome in SAP.

Bankruptcy

Table 5

Step

Action

1

General bankruptcy information + Read more ...

Where a customer advises they have applied for bankruptcy or been declared bankrupt, check with Australian Financial Security Authority (ASFA) for the bankruptcy details.

When a customer is declared bankrupt, some Medicare debts cease to be payable during the bankruptcy. The Bankruptcy Act does not release a bankrupt from a debt incurred by means of fraud.

Where required, the submit a proof of debt to the Administrator to be registered as a creditor. This allows any potential dividends from the trustee to go towards the debt.

If a customer is bankrupted seek advice from a Team leader. If necessary, seek advice by email from the Legal Services Branch before taking legal action.

2

Customer contact + Read more ...

Advise customers considering bankruptcy or who are bankrupted:

  • recovery of debts covered by bankruptcy will stop for the bankruptcy period
  • they should include all Medicare debts when informing their bankruptcy trustee
  • do not give advice about debts which may be due to fraud

3

Debts not due to fraud + Read more ...

For debts that are not due to fraud, permanently write off the debt/s. See Financial adjustments for Medicare debt.

Advise the customer in writing that recovery does not continue after they are discharged from bankruptcy.

4

Debt raised due to fraud + Read more ...

For debts raised due to fraud:

  • Apply a temporary write off up to the date the customer is scheduled to be discharged. See Financial adjustments for Medicare debt
  • Send a letter to the customer 1 month before the end of bankruptcy to advise the debt is recoverable
  • After the customer is discharged:
    • the temporary write off ends, and
    • recovery of the debt recommences

Requests for Centrelink Information

Table 6

Step

Action

1

DMO Request for release of information + Read more ...

Complete the Request for Centrelink information release form in the shared drive - FINANCE > Release of Information and:

  • email to the delegate

If the delegate provides:

  • Customer information:
    • Update the information in SAP
    • Attach the information form to the customer's record in SAP
    • Add case notes
  • No customer information:
    • Attach the information form to the customer's record in SAP
    • Add case notes

2

Delegate action required + Read more ...

When a completed Request for Centrelink information release form is received, investigate and collect information for the purpose of disclosure.

If the customer has a Centrelink record update the information on the release form and return to the DMO by email. Otherwise return the form to the DMO advising no new information was found.

Note: if the Centrelink information is required for Fines and Cost processing, this process does not apply. Data required for Centrelink purposes may be accessed directly.
