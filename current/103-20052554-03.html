Step

1

Participant displays low IT literacy skills and may be Vulnerable for the purpose of the CDC + Read more ...

If a service officer identifies a participant who may be vulnerable for the purpose of the CDC, due to displaying very low IT literacy skills and unable to use a computer or facilitate online banking (not because they don’t have access to the internet or online banking).

Service Officer to contact the card provider and obtain the following information:

  • Does the participant have an online account
  • If they do have an online account, how often and when it was last used

If required, discuss with the participant:

  • how to set up the online account,
  • how to effectively use the online account,
  • that there are tutorials on the card provider's website if required

If the participant requires a vulnerable CDC assessment, send an email to the Cashless Debit Card Processing Team using the above details obtained from the card provider. See Resources for email template.

2

CDC Vulnerable participant request + Read more ...

For Cashless Debit Card (CDC) Processing staff only

Email received via CDC Processing Mailbox:

  • Contact the card provider to establish if the participant has an online account and if/when it was last used
  • Contact the participant to:
    • confirm they are unable to use online services, including online banking due to low IT literacy.
    • outline the process of accessing funds due to deactivation of their online account and contact the CDC Hotline will be required for all transfer requests.

Does the participant consent to be having their online account deactivated as a vulnerable participant?

  • Yes, place vulnerable DOA DOC on record and email the card provider. See Resources. Procedure ends here.
  • No, DOC record of discussion. Procedure ends here

Participant contacts requesting a transfer of their $200 Other Expenses Limit + Read more ...

  • Indue available balance

Contact Indue to confirm the participant's online account is still deactivated.

Participant contacts requesting an Indue to Indue transfer + Read more ...

Indue to Indue transfers have an increased risk of fraudulent activity. Complete further Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO) if required. See Authenticating a Centrelink customer.

Once PoRO is complete, contact Indue and confirm the participant’s account balance.

If the participant has sufficient funds available, obtain and document the following information.

  • Why the participant cannot facilitate this transfer themselves online [CDC Vulnerable participant]
  • Amount required:
  • Indue account number:
  • Transfer to Account details:
    • Participant name:
    • CRN:
    • Indue account number:

If the participant is entitled to an Indue to Indue transfer, go to Transferring CDC Funds, Table 1, Step 4 to action this request. Procedure ends here.

Note: for Vulnerable participants, evidence is not required for their available $200.00 other expense transfer. For requests above the available $200.00 standard evidence requirements apply.

4

Participant contacts to advise they need to pay rent, bills or other expenses + Read more ...

Supporting documents must be provided to support requests for Housing, Other Expenses increases and one-off expense/transfers.

Supporting documentation for Housing:

Existing information on the Accommodation Summary Screen (ACS) or Document Tools can be used as supporting documentation for a 12 month limit increase from ACS date of verification, if the verification event is within 12 months and the Rent Verification field is coded:

  • Verified Formal (VEF)
  • Verified Rent Certificate (VEC))
  • Verified Declaration (VED)
  • Verified Electronically (VEE)

If the ACS has not yet been verified, the following existing documentation on Document Tools can be used:

  • Rent Certificate (SU523HD) - 12 months from the date the form was received by Services Australia

Insufficient Supporting Documentation on ACS

Is a change of circumstances update required?

  • Yes, see Update address details
  • No, advise the participant they are required to provide the following documentation to be assessed for a 12 month limit increase:
    • Lease agreement (12 months from signature date or contract end date, whichever is earlier)
    • Tenancy agreement (12 months from signature date or contract end date, whichever is earlier)
    • Mortgage contract (12 months from signature date or contract end date, whichever is earlier)

This includes where participants are not eligible for, or not claiming Rent Assistance, exempt from rent verification requirements, or ACS is not verified.

Other supporting documentation

If the participant is unable to provide the required documentation, they can provide the below documents for an approval period of 6 months from date of request:

  • a document from the accommodation provider signed or with a letter head stating address, dates and amount for ongoing accommodation, or
  • a Statutory Declaration from the participant confirming their address and accommodation details, and either:
    • 3 or more rent receipts in the participant's name and with the address (less than 6 weeks old)  or
    • a bond or deposit receipt with address

Appropriate evidence for Other Expenses and one-off expense/transfer requests:

  • Statements
  • Invoices
  • Receipts
  • Bills
  • Email from a business/seller

Note: Statutory Declaration forms should not be used in isolation as a form of evidence, however may be used to support other evidence.

Check previous docs on the participant’s record for an already approved ongoing Housing or Other Expenses request.

  • If an ongoing Housing or Other Expenses Limit transfer has been approved, go to Step 5
  • If this is a new request and there are no previously approved ongoing Housing or Other Expenses Limit requests, go to Step 6

Note: the term 'ongoing' is not indefinite, it is approval for a specified period of time.

5

Approved ongoing Housing or Other Expenses Limit transfers + Read more ...

  • Review the approval doc on the participant’s record to ensure the approval of the request is still within date
  • Select the Transaction History screen from the Customer first workspace to confirm:
  • the date of the last transfer to ensure it aligns with the 28 day transfer schedule (approval amount is the total amount over a 28 day period in line with other Limit requests)
  • the participant’s account balance

If the request is still within the approval date, aligns with the transfer scheduled and there are sufficient funds in the participant’s account, go to Transferring CDC Funds, Table 1, Step 4 to action this request.

6

New request for ongoing Housing or Other Expenses Limit transfer + Read more ...

If there is no previously approved ongoing Housing or Other Expenses Limit transfer recorded, discuss and obtain the following information:

  • What the request is for (Housing, Other Expenses)
  • Amount required
  • End date: [approval end date i.e. End of lease)
  • Suitable evidence has been provided
  • Bank account details of where the transfer is to be directed
  • Participant's Indue account number
  • Confirmation of participant contact details

If satisfied the request meets the required criteria, create a new closed Fast Note using the CDC Vulnerable Transfer Approved Fast Note and go to Transferring CDC Funds, Table 1, Step 4 to action this request.

7

Request for a one-off expense/transfer + Read more ...

Requests for a one-off transfer for a specific purchase, will be considered with appropriate evidence.

Discuss and confirm the following:

  • The amount required
  • The reason for payment
  • Whether suitable supporting documentation has been provided (supporting documentation is mandatory for all one-off expenses)
    • If supporting documentation is available, copy the Document ID from Document Tools, or save a copy to upload within the workflow.
  • The payment destination for the transfer (bank details)

If satisfied the request meets the required criteria, go to Transferring CDC Funds, Table 1, Step 4 to action this request.

8

Actioning WLM Lodged requests + Read more ...

Lodged requests will be allocated via Workload Manager to staff with appropriate access.

Housing/Other Expenses Limit: at the 7 day review date check for evidence:

  • If no evidence has been supplied, hold again for 7 days
  • If evidence has been supplied but is insufficient, call the participant and advise what is required. Hold again for another 7 days

Housing/Other Expenses Limit: at the 14 day review date check for evidence:

  • If no evidence has been supplied, select the 'Reject' radio button and finalise

One-off direct Entry Transfer: at the 7 day review date check for evidence

Is there is sufficient supporting documentation?

  • Yes, select the ‘Approve’ radio button and finalise
  • No, select the ‘Reject’ radio button and finalise. Advise the participant of the outcome. If the request has been rejected, discuss alternate transfer options

1

Participant requesting to be removed from the Vulnerable Customer Register + Read more ...

Participants can contact and to have their online account reactivated at any time.

CDC Hotline Service Officer to send an email to the Cashless Debit Card Processing Team requesting an assessment for the Vulnerable CDC status to be removed.

CDC Processing Staff are to:

  • contact the participant and discuss why their circumstances have changed to warrant reactivation of their online account
  • expire the Vulnerable Customer Display on Access Document on the participant’s record
  • email the card provider requesting the re activation of the participants online account
  • advise the participant they are now responsible for facilitating their online transfers
