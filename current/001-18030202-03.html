This page contains information on the key features of the Targeted Compliance Framework.

Key features of the Targeted Compliance Framework

This table outlines the role of the key features that form the Targeted Compliance Framework.

Item

Description

1

Personal responsibility and engagement + Read more ...

Job seekers have a personal responsibility:

  • for remaining engaged with their provider and in activities that will potentially lead to work
  • to keep their provider informed and to negotiate compulsory requirements that take into account their individual capability and assessed capacity
  • for recording their own attendance at requirements
  • to know where they stand - keeping an eye on their status and knowing what will happen if they miss a requirement

2

Mutual obligation failures and payment suspension + Read more ...

Job seekers who fail to meet a compulsory requirement commit a mutual obligation failure.

When a mutual obligation failure has been committed payments may suspend.

Payment will suspend immediately if:

  • the job seeker is a Community Development Program (CDP) job seeker - these job seekers are subject to the Job Seeker Compliance Model (JSCM), or
  • the job seeker is in the penalty zone under the Targeted Compliance Framework (TCF), or
  • the failure is a work refusal or unemployment failure under the TCF

If the job seeker is in the green or warning zone payment will not be suspended immediately.

Job seekers will receive a message from DESE/ ESSWEB telling them they must within 2 business days:

  • contact their provider to re-engage and/or
  • satisfy their provider they have a valid reason for missing their requirement

If a job seeker contacts Services Australia about this message, they must be told to contact their provider as soon as possible to avoid payment suspension.

If the job seeker does not contact their provider, their payment will be suspended after 2 business days. Their payment will remain suspended until they contact their provider and meet any reengagement requirements the provider sets.

3

The warning zone and demerits + Read more ...

Job seekers who commit a mutual obligation failure in the green or warning zone may also incur a demerit if their provider does not accept they had a valid reason for the mutual obligation failure.

A first demerit will move the job seeker from the green zone to the warning zone. Three demerit points in 6 months triggers a Capability Interview, 5 demerit points in 6 months triggers a Capability Assessment. Failures relating to job interviews or job referrals will fast track the job seeker to the next assessment point.

Demerits provide a warning system, making it simple and easy to identify and address non-compliant behaviour.

4

Capability Interview + Read more ...

The Employment Services System triggers a Capability Interview when a job seeker accrues 3 demerit points in a 6 month period or is fast tracked after accruing a demerit for a job interview or job referral failure and the Capability Interview is the next available assessment.

Providers are responsible for notifying the requirement to attend the Capability Interview. The Capability Interview requirement will include attendance at a face to face interview within 2 business days from the date of contact with the job seeker, unless allowable circumstances prevent this from occurring.

Note: job seekers are not required to participate in another Capability Interview if:

  • the job seeker has already had a Capability Interview conducted within the last 60 days, and
  • the last Capability Interview was conducted by the same Provider organisation and employment services contract, and
  • the job seeker was found to be capable of meeting their compulsory requirements and continued in the Warning Zone, and
  • the job seeker was not subsequently returned to the Green Zone following a Capability Assessment

The Capability Interview provides a further opportunity for the job seeker to discuss any circumstances impacting their compliance with their provider.

During the Capability Interview, the provider reviews their circumstances and their Job/Participation Plan to ensure they are in the right stream service and that their capacity has been appropriately assessed. The provider will re-negotiate the Job/Participation Plan with the job seeker if they consider any of the terms inappropriate.

This safety net encourages job seekers to take personal responsibility for their requirements and participate to the full extent of their capacity while also serving as a warning of the consequence of persistent non-compliance.

5

Capability Assessment + Read more ...

A Capability Assessment conducted by Services Australia when a job seeker accrues 5 demerit points in 6 active months, or is fast tracked after accruing a demerit for a job interview or job referral failure and the Capability Assessment is the next available assessment.

The provider is responsible for notifying the Capability Assessment requirement. The job seeker is responsible for contacting the Participation Solutions Team to arrange a Capability Assessment. PST-skilled Service Officers conduct the Capability Assessment.

The Capability Assessment is an evidence based review that acts as a further check to ensure that all circumstances impacting compliance have been identified and that the job seeker is in the right stream, their capacity has been appropriately assessed and their Job/Participation Plan requirements are appropriate to their circumstances.

This safety net ensures that job seekers are not potentially subject to penalties unless their non-compliance is persistent and requirements have been assessed by both their provider and Services Australia as appropriate to their circumstances.

6

Financial penalty zone and tiered financial penalties + Read more ...

Job seekers who are deliberately and persistently non-compliant move into the financial penalty zone if both a Capability Interview and Capability Assessment determine requirements in their current Job Plan/Participation Plan to be appropriate to their circumstances.

In this zone, job seekers who demonstrate full compliance for 3 active months are return to the green zone; however, those who continue to be non-compliant will face financial penalties or payment cancellation.

The tiered system of payment reduction and cancellation for all failures in the financial penalty zone is also easy and simple for job seekers to understand.

The financial penalties provide a strong deterrent for the minority of job seekers who are not meeting agreed and suitable compulsory requirements.

The intent of the financial penalties is to reduce non-compliant behaviour and keep all job seekers engaged with their provider and activities.

7

Unemployment failures and work refusal failures + Read more ...

Job seekers who leave suitable work voluntarily without reasonable excuse or are dismissed due to misconduct commit an unemployment failure. Job seekers who refuse suitable employment without reasonable excuse commit a work refusal failure.

Note: unemployment failures and work refusal failures do not apply to ParentsNext participants.

Customers who commit an unemployment failure incur an unemployment preclusion period of 4 weeks, 6 weeks for a relocation job.

Payment is cancelled and a post-cancellation preclusion period applies for job seekers who commit an unemployment failure or work refusal failure while in receipt of payment.

Unemployment and work refusal failures reinforce the principle that income support payments for the unemployed should only be available to those who are genuine in their efforts to move into paid work.

8

Post-cancellation preclusion periods + Read more ...

When payment is cancelled due to persistent mutual obligation failures, unemployment failure or work refusal failures, a post-cancellation preclusion period prevents job seekers who reclaim from immediately returning to payment.

During this period, access to the concession card and family payments is retained but no other waiver or hardship provisions apply, reinforcing the expectation that job seekers do all they can to accept and remain in suitable work.
