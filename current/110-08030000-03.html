Regular, notification or variable reporter requirements

Table 1: This table describes the different reporting requirements based on whether a customer is a regular, notification or variable reporter.

Step

Action

1

Customer lodges fortnightly report, or a variable/notification reporter contacts Services Australia + Read more ...

The work item ZIAM_PARTI_EXE - Participation Exemption can be created when:

  • a customer has declared income however on a temporary exemption, or
  • their Employment Services Provider have advised customer has been placed into part time/casual work and income not declared

If no income or hours have been declared for the customer but notification has been received advising of a recent job placement:

  • For regular reporters with a report due, go to Step 2
  • For notification reporters or variable reporters who do not have a report due, see Step 1 in Table 2

2

Regular reporters with a report due + Read more ...

Open the 'Earnings and Reporting Workflow Tool'.

If no income or hours have been declared for the customer, but a 'transaction' has been submitted by one of the Employment Services Providers advising of a recent job placement, the following alert will appear.

‘Notification has been received from an Employment Services Provider that the customer was placed in casual or part time work with <employer name> on <date>. Earnings from <employer name> have not been declared at this contact – discuss with customer. No employment income reported for the <customer/partner> for <employer name>. Select and action the relevant issue.’

Does the above alert appear within the workflow?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, complete the workflow. Procedure ends here

3

Alert appears in workflow that customer has a job placement but no earnings received + Read more ...

If the customer has lodged their Reporting Statement by fax or mail, contact the customer and/or Employment Services Provider by phone to discuss the job placement.

Is the customer or provider able to be contacted?

  • Yes, see Step 2 in Table 2
  • No, select the option 'Further discussion required with Employment Services Provider or the employer'. See Step 7 in Table 2

Updates regarding ESP notification of commencing employment

Table 2: This table describes how to record the outcome of the discussion with the customer about the Employment Services Provider notifying commencement of employment.

Step

Action

1

Notification reporters or variable reporters who do not have a report due + Read more ...

An Urgent Issue will appear on the Customer Issues screen in Customer First.

Click on the Issue. The Outcome Recording page will come into view. The Discussion Dialogue box states:

‘Notification has been received from an Employment Services Provider that the customer has been placed in casual or part time work with <employer name> on <date>. Earnings from <employer name> have not been declared at this contact – discuss with customer. No employment income reported for <customer/partner> for <employer name>. Select and action the relevant issue.’

Select the appropriate outcome from the Service Outcomes box. If the outcome is:

  • Customer will now declare income from employment, go to Step 3
  • Earnings previously declared, go to Step 4
  • Job commencement date is in the future, go to Step 5
  • Information is incorrect or the job did not eventuate, go to Step 6
  • Further discussion required with employment service provider or employer, go to Step 7

2

Discuss the job placement with the customer and determine the reasons for non-declaration of income + Read more ...

There are 5 dropdown options for actioning the alert within the Earnings and Reporting Workflow. Select 1 of the following options in order to proceed with the workflow:

  • Customer will now declare income from employment, go to Step 3
  • Earnings previously declared, go to Step 4
  • Job commencement date is in the future, go to Step 5
  • Information is incorrect or the job did not eventuate, go to Step 6
  • Further discussion required with employment service provider or employer, go to Step 7

3

Customer will now declare income from employment + Read more ...

Select this option if the customer now wants to declare employment income paid to them and/or hours they have worked. Staff must record this outcome before recording any new details.

Continue with the workflow.

Procedure ends here.

4

Earnings previously declared + Read more ...

Select this option where a customer indicates that they have declared income from the employer in a previous fortnight and this has been confirmed.

Continue with the workflow.

Procedure ends here.

5

Job commencement date is in the future + Read more ...

Select this option where a customer indicates that the start date of a job placement is in the future. Alerts will appear over the next three reporting periods if no employment income or hours are declared.

Continue with the workflow.

Procedure ends here.

6

Information is incorrect or the job did not eventuate + Read more ...

Select this option where a customer indicates that the job did not eventuate or the information is incorrect.

Check the Provider recorded information workflow. The Job referrals and placements section displays job referral and placements recorded by the provider in the Department of Education, Skills and Employment system within the last two weeks. Click the Search button to view more job referrals recorded prior to the last two weeks. For further information, see Participation with jobactive.

If information is not available on the Provider Recorded Information workflow, contact the customer's local Employment Services Provider and confirm this information. If the provider is unavailable and the customer can provide the employer details, confirm directly with the employer if permission by the customer has been given. Document any contacts made to resolve the discrepancy in the employment income declaration. Continue with the workflow.

Procedure ends here.

Do not select this option. When the customer indicates the information is incorrect, go to Step 7.

7

Further discussion required with Employment Services Provider or employer + Read more ...

This option is only to be used in the instances where:

  • neither the provider nor the employer can be contacted to confirm the information as provided by the customer or
  • the customer has lodged their Reporting Statement by fax or mail and cannot be contacted

The outcome 'Pending Further Investigation' (PFI) will be recorded and a Manual Follow-up (MFU) will be created for further discussion with the customer, provider or the employer. Payment can be made. Continue with the workflow.

Record all relevant information the customer has provided on a DOC. If processing via the workflow, use may also be made of the Notepad Facility.

Record all relevant details the customer has given and/or any contacts made to resolve the discrepancy in the employment income declaration on a DOC. If processing via the workflow, use may also be made of the Notepad Facility.
