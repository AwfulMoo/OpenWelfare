Scenarios - general

For periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring that start:

  • from (on or after) 9 December 2021 (ending on 17 January 2022), see Scenarios - from 9 December 2021
  • before 9 December 2021 (that is, up to and including 8 December 2021), see Scenarios - before 9 December 2021

If a New South Wales resident tries to claim for the Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment, see Scenarios - eligibility for either New South Wales or Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

Scenarios - eligibility for either New South Wales or Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment

Table 6: Scenarios where, from 5 July to 16 September 2020, customers living in New South Wales could claim the Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

Scenario

Eligibility

Reason

Scenario 1

A required authority informs a customer who resides in New South Wales that they:

  • tested positive with COVID-19, and
  • must self-isolate for 14 days from 20 September 2020

The customer works in Victoria and meets all other eligibility criteria for the payment.

Eligible for the New South Wales Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment

The customer:

  • was informed to self-isolate by a required authority, and
  • resides in New South Wales

The customer's period of self-isolation falls after 16 September 2020. This is after the New South Wales Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment started.

Scenario 2

A required authority informs a customer who resides in New South Wales that they:

  • tested positive with COVID-19, and
  • must self-isolate for 14 days from 23 August 2020

The customer works in Victoria and meets all other eligibility criteria for the payment.

Not eligible for the New South Wales Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

Eligible for the Victorian Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

The customer:

  • was informed to self-isolate by a required authority, and
  • resides in New South Wales

The customer's period of self-isolation ended before 16 September 2020. This is before the New South Wales Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment started.

Scenario 3

A required authority informs a customer who resides in New South Wales that they:

  • tested positive with COVID-19, and
  • must self-isolate for 14 days from 12 September 2020

The customer works in Victoria.

Service Officers should assess the claim under the New South Wales Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

If the customer is:

  • eligible, process the claim
  • not eligible, assess and process the customer’s claim under the Victorian Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment

As the claim started before 17 September 2020 and ends after 17 September 2020, assess the claim under the New South Wales Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment first.

If the customer is not eligible, assess and process the claim under the Victorian Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.
