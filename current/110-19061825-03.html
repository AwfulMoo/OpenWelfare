Customer contacts about a compliance intervention decision

Employment income debts raised pre 2015, pre 7 December 2020 CAM changes and debts raised with bank statements

This page has the process for Compliance Officers (CO) when a customer contacts Services Australia about their compliance intervention decision.

Customer contacts about a compliance intervention decision

Table 1.

Step

Action

1

Determine reason for call + Read more ...

Is the customer requesting details about a decision?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, if the customer is contacting with new details or to advise documents have been lodged:
    • Make sure the customer has all the new details needed for a reassessment to be undertaken
    • Find the scanned images using Document Tools in Customer First. Note: scanned image work items must be completed once the document has been viewed. See Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images
    • Progress the reassessment with the customer, go to Step 5

2

Employment income debts raised pre 2015, pre 7 December 2020 CAM changes and debts raised with bank statements information + Read more ...

The following debt scenarios need more action from the SME and must be escalated to the Policy Engagement Team for more advice/action required:

  • pre 2015 debt calculations where averaged ATO income information was used, go to Step 1 in Table 2
  • employment income paid before 7 December 2020, (December 2020 - Change Assessment Model (CAM), go to Step 2 in Table 2
  • using bank statements to verify employment income, go to Step 3 in Table 2

For all other scenarios go to Step 3.

3

Customer requests details about a decision + Read more ...

Discuss the decision with the customer using information on the customer’s record. In line with Services Australia Service Commitments, always:

  • discuss and clarify any misunderstandings
  • provide an opportunity to supply new details or evidence
  • correct any errors

The Resources page has a link to the Service Commitments on the Services Australia website.

If errors are identified during this discussion, they must be corrected with the customer over the phone. This is not a reassessment. Recovery of the debt is not to be paused. If the customer is satisfied following the updates, go to Step 10.

If the customer has concerns with repaying a debt, remind the customer of the payment options available. For more details, see Procedures for arranging withholdings to recover debts.

If the customer requests details about a debt decision, clarify with the customer exactly which information or documents they need, see:

  • Information and documentation available for administrative release to online compliance intervention customers.
  • Administrative Release
  • Customer requests access to their personal information

If the customer requests documents under administrative release arrangements, issue a cover letter with the requested documents. Note: check with Quality Development Officer (QDO) or Team Leader before sending any details to a customer.

Use the Administrative Release letter template found under the sub-site on Operational Blueprint > Letters > Centrelink templates > Compliance. The Resources page has a link to the Letters sub-site.

Before sending the letter, check User Profile and manually change printer settings.

Is the customer satisfied with the decision?

  • Yes, go to Step 16
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Determine if customer has new details or evidence for a reassessment + Read more ...

If the decision has been discussed and the customer is still not satisfied with the decision, ask the customer if they have new details or evidence to provide.

A reassessment is only to be undertaken if the customer provides new details.

Does the customer have new details they wish to provide?

  • Yes, if the customer has new details:
    • available over the phone a reassessment is needed. Debt recovery can be paused before starting the reassessment, go to Step 6
    • but cannot provide the details during the phone call, they will need to provide this before a reassessment can start. Go to Step 5
  • No, if the customer has no new details or evidence to provide, or they are still not satisfied with the decision after a reassessment, tell them of their review and appeal rights. If the customer:
    • requests an explanation of the decision, go to Step 15
    • wants to apply for a formal review of the decision, record the application. Procedure ends here

5

Customer can give more details + Read more ...

If the customer can give more details but they cannot provide it during the initial call, encourage the customer to call back with the details once it is available.

Only request the customer lodge documented evidence if it is not practical to accept the details verbally, or evidence is needed to confirm the verbal update

Customers can upload documents online or using Express Plus Centrelink mobile app if they have a Centrelink online account.

If the customer cannot access this service, discuss other options:

  • visit their local service centre to have the documents scanned
  • mail the documents to the Income confirmation mailing address

Tell the customer:

'You will need to provide this details before a reassessment can be undertaken. Once the details are received, the decision will be reassessed. If you fail to provide these details, we will be unable to reassess the decision.'

The customer must phone back to notify once documents have been lodged. When the customer phones back, find the scanned images using Document Tools in Customer First, confirm receipt and progress the reassessment with the customer.

6

Pause debt recovery + Read more ...

If the customer requests an explanation or applies for a formal review of the decision, recovery of the debt can be paused for the duration of the process.

This applies when the customer:

  • requests a reassessment and has provided all of the relevant details or evidence
  • requests an explanation by a Subject Matter Expert (SME)
  • applies for a formal review by an Authorised Review Officer (ARO)
  • requests an Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) first review

Before proceeding, pause recovery of the debt under review.

Note: when updating the OPWOA screen apply a temporary write-off for 6 months rather than the 3 months stated. Make sure the 6 months is documented on the customer record. This rule is specific to Compliance staff only.

If the customer has:

  • provided all relevant details or evidence for a reassessment to be undertaken, go to Step 7
  • requested an explanation of the decision, go to Step 15
  • applied for a formal review of the decision, record the application
    • procedure ends here

7

Customer has provided new details or evidence for a reassessment + Read more ...

Once the customer has provided new details or evidence, a reassessment can be undertaken.

Note: if the customer had the recovery fee applied in the original debt, the recovery fee must be removed in the reassessment, as the customer is meeting their obligations by providing details about their employment income confirmation.

Go to Step 8.

8

Calculation sheets + Read more ...

Calculation sheets are only relevant for the following interventions:

  • Employment Income Confirmation (EIC)
  • Check and update past income (CUPI)
  • Employment Income Interventions (Phone based) (EIIP)
  • Employment Income Interventions (Mail based) (EIIM)
  • Unearned Income Interventions (UII)
  • JobKeeper Interventions

If a calculation sheet is not needed, go to Step 9.

If a new calculation sheet needs to be created for a reassessment, Copy and Paste the previous calculation from the .XLSX file uploaded on the customer record into the new calculation sheet. Note: the original calculation sheet does not need to be deleted, as this is a new decision from the reassessment.

See:

  • Earnings Apportionment Tool (EAT)
  • the Resources page for the Net income to gross income calculator tool

All EAT and Net to Gross calculation sheets must be saved as an .XLSX file for them to be uploaded to the customer record.

To save the EAT or Net to Gross sheet as an .XLSX file:

  • The CO must save the file to their H: Drive as an ‘Excel workbook’ rather than an ‘Excel Macro-Enabled Workbook’. To do this, select:
    • File
    • Save As
    • Browse to find the H: Drive
    • Change the Save as type to Excel workbook to save the file as an .XLSX file

The sheets must then be uploaded to the customer’s record using the Upload Document function on the Customer First Tools screen. See Attaching electronic documents to a Centrelink customer's record using Document Tools in Customer First.

All calculation sheets must be uploaded using the reason code DBTCAL.

For multiple match employers, save each calculation with the Customer Reference Number (CRN) and employer number as outlined in the examples below:

  • EAT sheet for:
    • 1st employer save as - 123456789A01
    • 2nd employer save as - 123456789A02
  • Net to Gross sheet for:
    • 1st employer save as - 123456789A01_N2G
    • 2nd employer save as - 123456789A02_N2G

If there are multiple Net to Gross sheets needed for one employer, separate calculation sheets will need to be created for each financial year. Save each Net to Gross sheet for the same employer as shown below:

  • 1st sheet save as - 123456789A01_N2G_FY2016-17
  • 2nd sheet save as - 123456789A01_N2G_FY2017-18

After all calculation sheets have been uploaded, delete the saved files from their H:drive.

If a decision is returned in Quality On Line (QOL), a request must be made to remove the calculation sheet from the customer record. The Resources page has a link to the ECRM Image Deletion Request form. This will make sure, only the correct calculation sheet is attached to the customer record.

For more details, see Earnings Apportionment Tool (EAT).

9

Use ADEX to calculate debt + Read more ...

The Debt Explanation (ADEX) debt calculation tool must be used in all cases if it is possible and practical to recalculate the debt.

Complete the following to determine if a recalculation can be done using ADEX:

  • Make sure the Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen is coded in the correct system
  • Check the Assessment Results (AR) screen to make sure the system has correctly calculated any potential overpayment, for example:
    • Debt schedule - do the debt dates/date of event (DOV) match the period?
    • Check Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS)/ Income Bank (NIB)/ Employment Income Assessment (EIA) to make sure the updates appear as expected
    • Can a reassessment be made using the Benefit Action (BA) screen? Use REA in relevant host system or RCR in PEN/PGA (for cancelled PEN and PGA records) and leave Reason blank

Note:

  • Check if Review of Assessment (ROA) coding is needed to generate allowable arrears, if corrected income is less than declared however system did not generate arrears for those periods/fortnights
  • If 2 ADEX debt calculations have been amalgamated and raised as 1 debt, print and scan the amalgamated ADEX debt scheduler to the customer record and document it, see Scanning Centrelink documents using an MFD

Can an ADEX debt calculation be completed?

  • Yes, calculate the ADEX debt calculation with the customer over the phone then go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Use MultiCal to calculate the debt + Read more ...

If an ADEX debt calculation is not possible, a MultiCal debt calculation may be needed.

Check the following to determine if a MultiCal is needed:

  • Limiting Date (LMTD) screen (in each system) or NSS General Information (NGI), Pensions General Information (PNGI) or PP General Information (PGGI) screens has limiting dates relevant to the period
  • Customer is cancelled or suspended
  • Assessment Results (AR) screen has a before the limiting date (BLD) line
  • Customer cancels or suspends within the activity
  • No debt on AR screen where one is expected
  • Larger debt result than expected - that is, historical rejection/cancellation of payment
  • Debt start/end dates are outside of the employment income update

If the outcome is not correct, these debts may need to be calculated using MultiCal.

If a MultiCal is needed:

  • Get all the details needed from the customer and document on the customer's record. The Resources page has a link to the documentation requirements
  • If the customer is on the phone, use this suggested dialogue:
    'Thank you for your time today. The details you have provided will be assessed to determine if an overpayment has occurred. Once the assessment has been completed, we will contact you to advise the outcome.'
  • The MultiCal activity remains the responsibility of the CO to progress. If unable to action the MultiCal activity, the activity remains the responsibility of the site to progress. The responsible Team Leader must reassign the activity to another CO with the required skill set

CUPI Service and EIC online compliance interventions only: Debt Management has endorsed the offsetting of credits to occur outside the debt period as long as they fall within the CUPI service match period (either a single match year or consecutive match years). This is strictly limited to employment income assessments for Employment Income Confirmation (EIC) and the CUPI service (Pay-As-You-Go). To enable this within the MultiCal tool:

  • make sure the scope of assessment correctly reflects the Service Match Period
  • select CRTL+SHIFT+L keys
  • select Yes to Arrears exclusion override
  • select Calculate Debt from the Calculate menu
  • review the Amount Overpaid to make sure the Credits have offset the debt correctly

All MultiCal debt calculations must be printed, scanned and uploaded to the customer’s record. See Using the MultiCal debt calculator.

11

Issue letter to customer + Read more ...

Issue a manual Q053 debt outcome letter to the customer to notify them of the outcome.

Note: for EIC and CUPI debts that have been reduced to zero, check how the original debt was raised. If the original debt was manually raised (ONM), a manual Q053 must be issued. If the original debt was raised by the online platform (ONL), an outcome letter will be auto-generated once the debt activity has been completed.

See the Resources page for the manual Q053 advice approved text. Make sure the relevant Income Confirmation phone number is included in the letter.

If a change to the customer's record results in a modification to the original debt, follow instructions in Changing account details on debts raised in DMIS Do not issue a Q417 letter.

Is the customer on the phone?

  • Yes
    • if only corrections were made, go to Step 16
    • if a reassessment was undertaken, go to Step 13
  • No, contact the customer to explain the outcome, go to Step 12

12

Contact customer + Read more ...

If the customer is not on the phone, contact the customer to explain the outcome.

If the recalculated outcome resulted in zero debt, a decreased debt or no change, make 1 genuine attempt to contact the customer on all available numbers.

If the original debt increases:

  • Make genuine attempts to contact by phone
  • Follow the phone contact requirements
  • Record all attempts to contact the customer in a DOC (including successful contact attempts. The Resources page includes the requirements for documenting

Was contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No, go to Step 14

13

Customer conversation + Read more ...

Provide the customer with an explanation of the reassessment outcome.

Is the customer satisfied with the outcome?

  • Yes, go to Step 14
  • No, tell the customer of their review and appeal rights. If the customer wants to:
    • Request an explanation of the decision, go to Step 15
    • Apply for a formal review of the decision, record the application. Procedure ends here

14

Restart debt recovery + Read more ...

If the debt remains and the customer is satisfied with the outcome or customer contact was unsuccessful, restart debt recovery:

  • Go to the Debt List (OPDL) screen
  • 'S'elect the debt to be restarted
  • Go to the Write Off (OPWO) screen
  • Code 'Y' in the Restart Recovery field and press [Enter]

Go to Step 16.

15

SME explanation of the decision + Read more ...

Important: if the initial contact Compliance Officer (CO) is a SME (independent of the original decision and any reassessed decision) who is able to provide a SME explanation:

  • record the request, and
  • provide the explanation

If the customer needs to be recontacted to finalise the explanation, the CO is to hold the explanation work item.

Before recording a request for an explanation of the decision, check the date of the pause applied. If needed, apply a further temporary write-off. See Step 4 of Process for inbound contact through service centres or Call operations able in Recovering debts pending review of decision.

Note: when updating the OPWOA screen apply a further temporary write-off for 6 months rather than the 3 months stated. Make sure the 6 months is documented on the customer record. This rule is specific to Compliance staff only.

Is a SME referral required?

  • Yes, record a request for an explanation of the decision, see Request for an explanation or application for a formal review (CLK)
    • A CO SME will provide an explanation. Vulnerable customers will have access to a priority explanation
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, and the customer is:
    • Satisfied with the outcome, the SME will document the record and restart debt recovery where there is still an amount owing. Procedure ends here
    • Not satisfied with the outcome and wants to apply for a formal review, record the application. Procedure ends here

16

Document customer record + Read more ...

Document the details covered in the conversation with the customer in a DOC on the customer's record.

The Resources page includes the requirements of documents.

Employment income debts raised pre 2015, pre 7 December 2020 CAM changes and debts raised with bank statements

Table 2.

Step

Action

1

Pre 2015 debt calculations where averaged ATO income information was used + Read more ...

If ATO income information was averaged and used to calculate the debt, the Subject Matter Expert (SME) should investigate the customer’s record and get the following information:

  • is there any extra evidence the customer can supply to support the application of the ATO information, or
  • have new details been provided

Ask the customer if they have further earnings information they can supply. Whenever possible get the information verbally from the customer. When taking verbal information make sure the correct information is obtained as per CAM changes:

  • from 7 December 2020, customers report the gross employment income they or their partner were paid in their reporting period, as shown on the payslip
  • prior to this customers have to calculate and report their earnings based on the hours they worked and amounts received in their Centrelink reporting period, regardless of whether they had been paid or not

Once you have discussed the debt and/or obtained new information, tell the customer you will call them back with more information about the information given and the next steps.

An escalation to the Policy Engagement Team is required, go to Step 4.

2

Pre 7th December 2020 - CAM + Read more ...

Where a customer contacts about an explanation of decision, or a SME regarding a debt that was raised prior to the Change Assessment Model (CAM) changes, the CO is to explain the decision to the customer. This includes establishing and clarifying what the customer does not understand or does not agree with.

Employment income paid in an entitlement period before 7 December 2020 must be assessed using the pre ‘Changing the Social Security Income Assessment Model’ (CAM) rules:

  • from 7 December 2020, customers report the gross employment income they or their partner were paid in their reporting period, as shown on the payslip
  • prior to this customers have to calculate and report their earnings based on the hours they worked and amounts received in their Centrelink reporting period, regardless of whether they have been paid yet

Ask the customer if they have more earnings information they can supply. Whenever possible take the information verbally from the customer. When taking verbal information make sure the correct information is obtained as per CAM changes above.

Once you have discussed the debt and/or obtained new information, tell the customer you will call them back with more information about the information given and the next steps.

An escalation to the Policy Engagement Team is required, go to Step 4.

3

Using bank statements to verify employment income + Read more ...

Where it has been identified that bank statements were used in a debt calculation in the first instance, seek payslips or payroll information from the customer.

Ask the customer if they have more earnings information they can supply. Whenever possible take the information verbally from the customer. When taking verbal information make sure the correct information is obtained as per CAM changes above:

  • from 7 December 2020, customers report the gross employment income they or their partner were paid in their reporting period, as shown on the payslip
  • prior to this customers have to calculate and report their earnings based on the hours/days they worked and amounts received in their Centrelink reporting period, regardless of whether they had been paid or not

Where payroll information cannot be given by the customer either verbally or in writing, more information is required from the customer to support previously supplied bank statements.

Bank statements may be used as verification of income if the customer can give more information to support the bank statements. An accurate assessment can be made on each net deposit.

The SME needs to be satisfied that the information given about each net deposit, has enough details to establish the customer’s earnings for the relevant period. The customer needs to agree and understand that these amounts accurately represent their earnings for the relevant period.

To do this, the SME must ask the customer to give more information around each net deposit.

See the Resources page for Questions Set for the use Bank Statement.

Once you have discussed the debt and/or obtained new information, tell the customer you will call them back with more information about the information given and the next steps.

An escalation to the Policy Engagement Team is required, go to Step 4.

4

Escalation to the Policy Engagement team + Read more ...

Once you have discussed the debt and/or obtained the necessary information, tell the customer you will call them back with more information about the information given and the next steps.

In the escalation, give a summary of what was discussed and any new information given by the customer. The escalation needs to include information about the debt including what evidence was used to calculate the debt

Give a summary of what was discussed including:

  • period and amount of initial debt
  • what information was used to raise the initial debt
  • what period was verified and who was the employer
  • what was used to calculate the debt e.g.:
    • was the debt calculated using earnings based on the exact hours the customer worked and the amounts received in their Centrelink reporting period
    • were weekly or fortnightly payslips used
    • was information received verbally or payslip/reports provided
  • for bank statements - what period does the bank statements relate to
  • for pre 2015 averaged ATO information - what period does averaging relate to and who was the employer/s
  • what new information has the customer supplied, e.g. days worked, amounts paid, when was payment made

Make sure the reasons for the referral are fully documented in the referral to the CADO Service Desk Online Form.
