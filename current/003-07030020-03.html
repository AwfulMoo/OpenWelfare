Check qualification and payability for Social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance + Read more ...

To be eligible for CrP-EXO, a customer must be receiving, or have claimed and be qualified (and payable) for:

  • a social security benefit
  • a social security pension
  • ABSTUDY Living Allowance

On the benefit status line in the customer's record, the primary payment must show as CUR (current).

  • For Farm Household Allowance customers, this will appear as (FHA-CUR)
  • For ABSTUDY customers, view receipt of ABSTUDY Living Allowance on the system

Note: if the customer does not have a status of CUR for a social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance, check the record for a submitted income support payment claim.

6

Customer does not want to pursue claim + Read more ...

A claim for CrP-EXO is not pursued where the customer identified they have incorrectly enquired about CrP.

In all cases when the CrP is not pursued:

  • Explain to the customer there are no review or appeal rights when a claim is not pursued as it has not been formally assessed and determined
  • Clearly record the discussion on the customer’s record using Notes
  • Discuss alternative help and offer the customer the chance to test eligibility for an Urgent Payment or Advance Payment
  • It is only appropriate to withdraw claims after the customer advised/agreed they no longer want to pursue the claim

Procedure ends here.

6

Confirm eligibility criteria - forced to leave home and establish a new one + Read more ...

A customer cannot get a CrP-EXO if they:

  • intentionally brought about the extreme circumstance with a view to getting a payment of CrP
  • were evicted from their home due to not paying their mortgage or rent
  • had to move out because their landlord is selling the property or moving back in
  • decided to move to another home
  • are living in government housing and have applied for a transfer to a different location, but are still living at the current location or intend to return while they await the transfer (they have not established a new home)
  • received a disaster relief payment administered by the local, State or Commonwealth Government for the same extreme circumstance

Note: if the AGDRP or DRP (or any other disaster relief payment) is announced as being available:

  • CrP is only payable to people who lodged a claim for CrP before the day of the announcement of the disaster relief payment, provided they meet all other eligibility conditions
  • customers who have been granted CrP before the announcement could also test their eligibility for a disaster relief payment

Some common reasons a customer may claim and be eligible for a CrP-EXO include (this list is not exhaustive):

  • a house fire
  • flooding (for example, because of a burst water main or random flooding event not covered by a disaster relief payment)
  • severe structural damage
  • if the customer feels unsafe (for example, because of community violence or repeated break-ins/burglaries)

Has the customer been forced to leave their home because of an extreme circumstance and establish a new home?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, customer is not eligible, go to Table 3

7

Review information and evidence + Read more ...

Evidence of the extreme circumstance must be verified through a professional third party, such as emergency services, government authority or relevant business/trade.

Contacting third parties is needed to confirm information/evidence where:

  • customers are having difficulty obtaining evidence
  • clarification of information/evidence is needed, such as police to verify a police report number

Personal references and photos of damaged property are not considered acceptable evidence.

Is follow up of information/evidence needed?

  • Yes,
    • If evidence is unclear or unreadable, make 2 genuine attempts to contact to clarify information/evidence or request legible evidence
    • If evidence is not submitted with a paper claim, make 2 genuine attempts to contact to submit evidence
    • If contact with a third party is required, make 2 genuine attempts to contact
    • Place claim on Hold for one day if contact is unsuccessful and record using Notes and include evidence requirements
    • After 2 attempts, if customer or third party cannot be contacted, reject the claim, go to Table 3
  • No, go to Step 8

Note: contact is not needed where information or evidence is clearly a deliberate attempt of deception to obtain a CrP-EXO.

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, customer is not eligible, reject the claim, go to Table 3

9

Confirm eligibility criteria - establishing a new home + Read more ...

A customer must have established, or be intending to establish, a new home as because of the extreme circumstance.

CrP-EXO is payable where the customer intends to return home after it becomes liveable, but have established a new home in the interim. See Resources.

Verification of the customer establishing, or intention to establish, a new home is required through a third party. This could include contacting either a private landlord or a state housing authority to confirm that the customer is vacating, or has the intention to vacate a property.

Has the customer met the eligibility criteria of establishing, or intending to establish, a new home?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, the customer is not eligible, reject the claim, go to Table 3

10

Confirm eligibility criteria - financial hardship + Read more ...

Acceptable documents to verify account balances can include bank statements or a digital copy of account information and transactions, if they show the:

  • account holder name(s)
  • BSB code and account number
  • current account balance

The delegate must be satisfied that the supporting documents provided reflects the customer's financial position at time of claim.

The customer can use a combination of supporting documents to provide the information needed. For example, a copy of their account information and a copy of their account transactions.

Has the customer met the eligibility criteria of financial hardship?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, customer is not eligible, reject the claim, go to Table 3

11

Review and update customer’s Income Support Payment (ISP) to reflect current circumstances + Read more ...

Review/update ISP of customer to make sure it is the correct payment for the customer’s circumstances, for example:

  • change in marital status
  • loss of eligibility for Carer Payment
  • dependent child/children leaving care
  • any other situation which may change the ISP payment type and/or rate

Staff may need to refer to Payment Finder to check the most appropriate payment for the customer.

Service Officers with the relevant ISP skills/training should apply the updates (payment transfer/ACC/assessing the new claim).

Note: the customer's ISP rate must be correct for their current circumstances before going to the next step. This will affect the rate of CrP payable.

Is handoff needed to have ISP updates completed?

2

Confirm eligibility criteria - customer contact within 7 days of event date (deemed claim provisions) + Read more ...

A customer must contact Centrelink within 7 days of the date the customer decided that they cannot return to their home because of the extreme circumstance having occurred.

After the initial contact, the customer must lodge the claim within 14 days. See Contact for the purposes of intent to claim and deemed claim provisions.

If the claim was lodged between 8 and 21 days after the event date, the following warning will show:

  • SR612 - Extreme circumstance is between 8-21 days, check contact date

Note: the event date is considered to be day zero.

When did the customer lodge the claim?

  • Within 7 days, go to Step 4
  • Within 8-21 days (SR612 warning message showing), go to Step 3
  • More than 21 days, go to Step 6

If a record of the contact exists, the customer satisfies the contact requirements for the CrP-EXO claim, and deemed provisions apply.

Confirm eligibility criteria - customer in Australia + Read more ...

The customer must be inside and living in Australia on the date of claim to be granted a CrP-EXO.

If the customer is recorded as being outside Australia or living in another country the system will show the following warning:

  • SR598 – Customer may not be in Australia

If this warning shows:

  • Check the Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen to see if the customer is outside Australia
  • Refresh the Immigration datalink if the record shows they are outside Australia, but believe they have been in Australia. For more details, see Activating the Department of Home Affairs datalink and contingency procedures if datalink is unavailable
  • Check the Country of Residence (CRES) screen to see if the customer is living in Australia
  • Update the CRES screen if necessary. For more details, see Coding the Country of Residence (CRES) screen

Was the customer in Australia when the claim was lodged?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, customer is not eligible
    • the system will show the rejection reason NIA - Not in Australia on the ELD screen

11

Payment of CrP + Read more ...

Payment must be made via the New Payments Platform (NPP).

Only when NPP is unavailable, payment should be made by Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS).

Direct credit can also be used if neither NPP or RTGS is available.

For Farm Household Assistance (FHA) customers:

  • Approval of the customer's Social Service Plan (SSP) must occur before paying the Crisis Payment
  • The system will approve this within 15 minutes
  • To check if the SSP has been approved, see Navigating to SSP
  • Once approved, pay the Crisis Payment via RTGS. Otherwise, the payment will deliver via direct credit the following business day

12

Identify if referral to social worker is needed + Read more ...

After processing the CrP-EXO claim (or awaiting QMO), if the customer needs extra support and help because of multiple and complex issues, complete and/or consult a Social work service referral.

Offer the referral in addition to claiming CrP-EXO, not as part of a claim for CrP-EXO. Do not create a referral from within the CrP-EXO claim, as this will place the claim on hold.
