Assessment and recording of trust and company distribution income

Item

Description

1

Determining Trust/Company distribution amounts + Read more ...

Distribution amounts can be determined by examining the private trust's distribution or private company's dividend statement.

Distribution amounts paid to an attributable stakeholder can be disregarded include:

  • amounts already been included as attribution income
  • amounts from prior year profits

The gifting provisions in relation to distributions paid to a non-attributable stakeholder will be disregarded if:

  • the customer can show they were not in receipt of an income support payment and could not have reasonably known they would require income support at the time the distribution or dividend was paid, or
  • the non-attributable stakeholder has been assessed as a Genuine Investor and the distribution paid to the Genuine Investor is commensurate with their investment in the entity

For the non-controller the distribution will be assessed as income for 52 weeks. Set up a review to update the Trust/Company Distribution (TRD) screen 52 weeks after the distribution. See the Resources page for an example.

Where a controller ceases involvement with the entity, any distribution (excluding final distribution) received prior to severing ties is assessed for 52 weeks. Set up a review to update the TRD screen 52 weeks after the distribution. See the Resources page for an example.

2

Recording Trust/Company distribution amounts + Read more ...

Go to Trust/Company Distribution (TRD) screen and code the following fields:

  • Event Date: record the date of the resolution to distribute
    • if the resolution to distribute states a percentage and not a specific amount, the beneficiary's legal entitlement to a distribution only commences from the date the amount is known. This is usually when the tax return is completed. The date the amount is known is the date of event
    • if the resolution declares to distribute a specific amount for a beneficiary but the trust tax return has not been lodged at time of the resolution, the date of event is from the date the resolution was made, not from the date the tax return was lodged
  • Amount: record amount of the distribution in whole dollars
    Note: if distributions have not ceased, but no distribution has been paid for a particular financial year, $0 should be recorded in order to ensure a review is triggered correctly for the following financial year's returns and financial statements
  • CRN: Code the Customer Reference Number (CRN). The distribution amount will pass through to that customer's record and display on the corresponding Trust/Company Income and Assets Distribution (TRIAD) screen. If the customer is also a controller of the trust/company only the distribution amount in excess of their attributed income will be treated as distribution income
    Note: where the distribution is made to a child and that child has a CRN because their parent may be receiving family assistance, do not record the child's CRN
  • Gen INV: or Deprivation:
    • If the distribution was made to a genuine investor, code 'GNI'
    • If the distribution was made during the 1999/2000 year, code 'CON'
    • If either 'GNI' or 'CON' have been recorded in the Gen INV: or Deprivation: field this amount will not be treated as being deprivation/gift of the controllers
  • Distrb Ceased: If the distribution amount is set to zero and the customer has received the final distribution, enter 'Y'. This will help when deciding which trust/company records can be archived and which should be selected for a program review. It is essential to leave this field blank if the customer may receive a distribution in the future
  • Multiple Distributions: If a Trustee resolves to make multiple distributions to a customer within a financial year, each distribution needs to be assessed as income for a period of 52 weeks from the date of the resolution to distribute. For example, if the Trustee resolves to make a distribution of $5000 on 1 August 2015, a TRD screen will be recorded with a date of event of 1 August 2015 and a distribution amount of $5000. If the Trustee resolves to make a further distribution of $2000 on 1 March 2016, a new TRD screen will need to be recorded with a date of event of 1 March 2016 and a distribution amount of $7000 ($5000 plus $2000). If no further distributions are made to the customer, the TRD screen needs to be recorded with a distribution of $2000 ($7000 - $5000) from 30 July 2016 (for example 52 weeks after the resolution to distribute) and a TRD screen with a nil distribution and a date of event of 28 February 2017. A review should be set to follow up any further distributions and update the TRD screen as required, 52 weeks after each distribution

This process will normally form part of coding all an entity's income, assets and attribution details. As such, ensure distribution details are included when completing a DOC outlining the actions taken.

The Trust/Company Distribution Summary Screen (TRDS) will display:

  • the event date
  • the total distributions to non-genuine investors or non-concession amounts
  • the total distributions to genuine investors or concession amounts

3

Work Bonus + Read more ...

Is any linked customer of Age Pension age and in receipt of an Income Support Payment (other than PPS):

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Work Bonus income percentage to be updated + Read more ...

An update to the Work Bonus income percentage is needed when:

  • the trust or company is assessed for the first time, or
  • a change in income earned from the customer's personal exertion been identified. For example, the trust or company now has an employee who is undertaking work previously done by the customer?

Is an update required?

  • Yes:
    • navigate to the Trust and Companies Income and Assets Summary (TRCIAS) screen
    • select the entity by entering s in the field next to the entity name and press enter
    • enter the customer and partner’s (if applicable) Work Bonus Income % as advised by the customer
    • enter the Work Bonus Event Date – this is the date the Work Bonus income % applies from. The date cannot be before the first instance of income from the trust or company for the customer
    • Enter the source and date of receipt
    • Note: The TRCIAS screen is able to be updated outside of any other outstanding activities on the customer’s record (for example new claims)
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Trust has been vested and a beneficiary only received a distribution within the last twelve months + Read more ...

Any income distributions to customers, who are only beneficiaries of the trust, will need to be maintained as income for a period of twelve months from the date the trustee resolved to make the distribution. This may mean the trust cannot be coded as being vested until the distributions cease to be assessable.

The trust income, assets, liabilities and attribution should all be set to nil but any distributions to beneficiaries only will be coded/remain on the TRD screen until they cease to be assessable.

The CAO should code a review on the RVR in the trust record as follows:

  • ServRsn: TRF
  • RevRsn: TDI
  • Due Date: This will be 52 weeks from the date of the resolution to distribute
  • Text: The CAO should include details of updates that need to be made to the trust record such as reducing distributions to nil and coding the ‘Wound Up’ indicator on TRMD
