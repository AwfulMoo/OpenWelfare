3

Sale of home and purchase of another home - exemptions + Read more ...

If the customer sold their home and is planning to purchase another home, but they have not yet purchased another home, they are still a homeowner during the initial 24 months exemption period if the date of sale is 1 January 2023 or later, until they move into their new home. For sales finalised before 1 January 2023, the exemption period is 12 months.

An additional 12 months exemption may be available only if the customer has a continuing intention to apply the proceeds of the sale to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate a new principal home and they:

  • have made reasonable attempts to obtain a new principal home, and
  • have been making those attempts within a reasonable period after selling the principal home (evidence may be required), and
  • have experienced delays beyond their control in obtaining a new principal home

During the initial or extended exemption period:

  • The value of principal home sale proceeds that are intended to be used to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate a new principal home is exempt under the assets test
  • The full amount is still deemed. For sales on or after 1 January 2023, funds are deemed at the lower deeming rate only
  • The customer is considered a homeowner

4

Valuation + Read more ...

The payments paid and/or the payments expected to be paid under the sale agreement will be multiplied by a discount factor to determine if a valuation is required.

The result is compared to the current market value of the property to determine if deprivation has occurred.

The discount factor will depend on:

  • the upper deeming rate at the date of the agreement
  • the term, in years, for the repayment
  • whether the purchase price is paid in regular instalments or as a single payment

Is the outstanding balance to be paid in a single payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, in equal instalments, go to Step 6

5

Single payment + Read more ...

Calculate discount factor

1 - (N x R) + {N x R x (N - 1) x R/2}

N = term for repayment in years

R = upper deeming rate at the date of agreement, for example 6% is equal to 0.06 at the date of the agreement.

Multiply the amount to be received by the result of the above calculation that is the discount factor. This gives the estimated current value.

See Step 1 in the Home ownership and deprivation assessment table.

6

Equal instalments + Read more ...

Calculate discount factor

1 - (N x R/2) + {N x R x (N - 1) x R/4}

N = term for repayment in years

R = upper deeming rate at the date of agreement, for example 6% is equal to 0.06 at the date of the agreement.

Multiply the total amount to be received by the result of the above calculation; that is, the discount factor. This gives the estimated current value.

Determine deprivation + Read more ...

If the property appears to have been sold below its true market value a valuation is required (if not already received) as deprivation may apply.

Deprivation is determined by obtaining an actuarial valuation and an approved valuation and calculating any difference:

  • Request an actuarial valuation to obtain the true value of the purchase price
  • To establish the actual value of the home, request a valuation of the property from an authorised valuer unless a valuation has already been completed. See Valuation of real estate and other assets at new claim
  • When the valuations are returned, assess if deprivation has occurred. The deprivation amount is the difference between the approved valuation from an authorised valuer and the actuarial valuation

Treatment of sale proceeds where customer or partner is in receipt of RCA and intends to use all the proceeds to purchase or build another home within 24 months, sales from 1 January 2023 onwards + Read more ...

Note: until a system release is available, the following workaround coding must be followed to ensure the proceeds of sale are assessed correctly for residential care assessments.

The amount of the primary sale proceeds that the customer intends to use to purchase, build, rebuild, renovate or repair a new primary residence must not be recorded as an asset. The deemed income from this amount needs to be assessed at the lower deeming rate only. To do this, the deemed income must be calculated and recorded manually.

Manual calculation for the deemed income for pensioners, allowees with a pensioner partner or single allowee customers:

  • (Intended amount for the new primary residence invested in financial assets) x 0.0025
  • If the customer is partnered, the result must be divided by 2 and the result coded individually on the customer and partner’s records.

Manual calculation for the deemed income for allowee customers without a pension partner:

Income will need to be split according to the ownership percentage of the financial asset that the funds are invested in.

  • (Intended amount for the new primary residence invested in financial assets) x 0.0025
  • Divide the result by the customer and partner’s advised ownership of the funds. EG, if the sale amount intended for the new home is $400,000, the customer advises that they own $300,000 and the partner owns $100,000, their deemed income must be calculated on their share.
  • If the asset is owned 100% by one member of a couple, then all income will be attributed to the owner of the asset.

Refer to the Resources page for a deeming calculator.

The annual deemed income amount must be recorded on the OINS screen for the customer, and partner if applicable.

Go to the OIN screen, complete the fields as follows

  • Type: OTH - other
  • Description: HOME SALE PROCEEDS
  • Reference:
  • Event Date: Date of sale
  • Amount $: calculated deemed income amount
  • Freq: The amount will be assessed over a 52 week period. Key the frequency as 'ANN'

Note: ‘HOME SALE PROCEEDS’ is a descriptor only to refer to the deeming income being applied to the portion of sale proceeds, that are being used to purchase, build, rebuild, repair or renovate a new principal home. Only use the above description to allow identification of these records when an ICT solution is available.

Unable to access sale funds due to temporary legal restriction + Read more ...

When a customer’s principal home is sold they may be unable to access the funds for legal reasons. This can be the result of situations such as a pending property settlement following the breakdown of a relationship or the settlement of an estate.

From the date of sale of the home until the date a legal settlement is reached, if the customer does not have a legal entitlement to the proceeds of the sale, a nil income or asset value is assessed for the duration of this period. For this period the customer is assessed as a non-homeowner.

    • Procedure ends here
