Table 2

Step

Action

1

Confirm contact and payment destination + Read more ...

Tell the customer the purpose of the call:

Suggested dialogue:

‘Services Australia has raised your Income Compliance debt using averaging of Australian Taxation Office (ATO) income information. We no longer use the Averaging of ATO information to calculate debts. Because of this we a refunding any money you have paid towards the debt. Your refund will be paid into your bank account.’

Confirm the customer’s contact details and payment destination details.

Obtain the new details if they need updating. If the customer advises new details, they need to be updated on all CRNs.

Are updates required?

  • No, go to Step 4

2

Update the customer’s details + Read more ...

Use Customer Summary to update details:

  • select Process to go to the Task Selector (TS) screen
  • from Task Selector select:
    • the relevant screens to be updated, or
    • for all tasks, Select all tasks
  • select Next to go to the relevant screens

Once on the relevant screen, to add new details:

  • select
  • key the required information
  • Save

To edit previously keyed details:

  • select Edit
  • edit the required information
  • Save

Once all updates have been made:

  • select Assess to go to the Entitlement (ELD) screen
  • from the ELD screen, select Finish
  • record the details in the finalisation DOC
  • select Finalise to return to the Income Compliance refund details screen

Are updates required to the payment destination?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

Update payment destination + Read more ...

Check the type of update needed:

  • To add new details:
    • select
    • key the new Payment Destination details
    • Save
  • To edit the new payment destination details:
    • select Edit
    • edit the Payment Destination details
  • Select Delete to remove any lines
  • Save to complete update

4

Finalise Income Compliance Refund + Read more ...

This will:

  • zero the customer’s debt, and
  • refund any repayments the customer has made

To finalise:

  • Select Finalise to go to the Finalise Income Compliance Refund screen
  • ‘Have you confirmed all the customer's contact details are up to date?’ Y/N
  • ‘Have you confirmed the customer's payment details are up to date?’ Y/N

If the customer has a payment nominee, extra questions will display:

  • For voluntary payment nominees
    ‘Does the customer want to receive the refund in their own bank account?’ Y/N
  • For involuntary payment nominees
    ‘Have you informed the customer that the Income Compliance refund will go to their payment nominee?’ Y/N

Select Finalise:
‘Are you sure you want to finalise this refund? Once you finalise, the refund cannot be undone.’ Yes/No

Once finalised, record details in a Note. See the Resources page for the template.

Once finalised, the Income Compliance Refund Activity will display a Status of On Hold with the Reason Awaiting Further Action. No further action is required.

5

Create follow up action + Read more ...

Arrange for the CRNs to be relinked by DQU following payment of the refund.

To do this, create a manual review on the customer’s record for future action. Code the review on the Review Registration (RVR) screen.

Code the following:

  • Service Reason: customer’s payment type
  • Review Reason: Return of Form/ Correspondence
  • Due Date: 5 business days from today
  • Keyword: RRPDQU
  • Workgroup: EII
  • Position: EIIWORK
  • Text: Confirm customer’s income compliance refund letter has been issued. If refund letter issued, contact DQU to relink multiple records
  • Source: INT
  • DOR: T

When the review is due, go to Step 6.

6

Referral to DQU to relink + Read more ...

When the due date has been reached:

  • Action the manual review from the FAL screen.
  • Check the Payment Summary (PS) screen in Customer Record to confirm if the payment was delivered.
  • Use the History Summary (HS) screen in Process Direct or search Outbound Correspondence tool to confirm the letter status is PR (printed). For help, see Viewing or reissuing a letter or electronic message

Procedure ends here.

9

AUS178 form required + Read more ...

Ask the customer to complete and return an International bank account form (AUS178) for the country where the account is.

The form is available on the Services Australia website. See the Resources page for a link.

Advise the customer they have 56 days to return the completed form. (28 days plus an extra 28 days to allow for delivery by post due to COVID-19.)

Encourage the customer to:

  • Use the Upload Documents service if they have a Centrelink online account. Suggested guidance:
    • 'When prompted to select the document type you want to upload, select Centrelink Form
    • 'Enter the form title or code, then select Continue
    • 'You’ll find your form title at the top of your form. The form code is at the bottom of your form
  • Fax the completed form to Centrelink International Services. See the Resources page for a link to contact details

If they cannot upload documents or fax, they can mail the completed form.

Create a manual review on the customer’s record for future action. Code the review on the Review Registration (RVR) screen.

Code the following:

  • Service Reason: customer’s payment type
  • Review Reason: Return of Form
  • Due Date: 14 business days from today
  • Keyword: RRPIBAR
  • Workgroup: EII
  • Position: EIIWORK
  • Text: Confirm customer has returned completed AUS178. If received progress customer’s Income Compliance Refund.
  • Source: INT
  • DOR: T

Annotate the DOC on the customer's record. See the Resources page for the template.

When the review falls due, go to Step 10.

Notice to prepare a Refund Account (SF030) + Read more ...

Issue the third party refund via the Notice to prepare a Refund Account (SF030).

Key the relevant details into the form:

  • Name of customer: key the third party details
  • Address of customer: key the care of (c/o) address provided by the third party
  • Direct Credit Details: key the bank/financial institution, account number and BSB number as confirmed with the third party

Send the following via email with the SF030 to:
FINANCIAL.SHARED.SERVICES@servicesaustralia.gov.au

  • Screenshot of the Repayment History (OPRH) screen of the customer’s record. Make sure it shows the third party CRN
  • Payment Summary (PS) screen of the third party record
  • If the third party’s payment destination has changed, a screenshot of the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen

8

Advise solicitor purpose of call + Read more ...

‘As the solicitor of the estate of the late <customer name> we are calling to let you know that a refund is payable to the estate.

'<Customer name> previously had a debt/s raised using averaging of Australian Taxation Office (ATO) income information. We no longer do this and will refund the repayments made to these debt/s.

'So that we can pay the refund, we need to confirm the preferred payment option with you on behalf of the executor or administrator.’

Explain the options for the refund:

Cheque: ‘If you opt for the refund payment by cheque, you will receive a cheque in the mail. When banking the cheque, you will be required to provide proof of identity and proof of legal authority to the estate to the bank.

Direct Credit: ‘If you opt for the refund payment by direct credit, the agency will request proof of legal authority to the estate before processing the refund payment. Proof will vary depending on the refund amount.’

Tell the solicitor:

‘The agency will not make individual payments to beneficiaries. Only one payment will be made to either the executor, administrator or solicitor which can then be distributed to the beneficiaries.’

If the solicitor is still acting or representing the executor or administrator

Record their details on the DEA screen. For more information, see Confirmation of the death of an adult or child.

If the solicitor is busy and unable to make time to discuss any details

The solicitor can give permission to take information from the paralegal and/or assistant.

If this occurs, staff must document the following details on the customer’s record:

  • solicitor name
  • date/time of contact
  • confirmation that the solicitor has given permission for the paralegal and/or assistant to provide the executor information

If the solicitor is no longer acting or representing the executor or administrator

  • Obtain the executor or administrator details. This includes the name, address and contact details
  • Record the details on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen. For more information, see Confirmation of the death of an adult or child
  • Then go to Step 10

Discuss refund with executor or administrator + Read more ...

First confirm that the person on the phone is the executor or administrator of the estate of the deceased customer.

If the person on the phone advises they are not the executor or administrator for the deceased customer and their details are on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen, these must be deleted. This includes if the person is the beneficiary.
