What is considered contact

'Contact' for claiming a Crisis Payment is considered to mean contact by any available channel by a person seeking help in the form of a social security payment or concession card.

For example, through online channels, at a service centre or by the phone.

Note: the person does not need to mention a specific payment/concession card or know what they are eligible for when they make contact, only that they need help.

For example, a customer calls their regular payment line and explains that they came into contact with a confirmed case of COVID-19. The person asks if there is any help that they might be eligible for.

See the National Health Emergency section in Contact requirements for Crisis Payment (CrP) for more details on how to calculate the contact and claiming period.

What is not considered contact

Contact by a person by any available channel that deals exclusively with a payment/concession card the person already receives, is not considered to be ‘contact’ for claiming a Crisis Payment.

For example, a customer’s income reporting period happens to fall within 14 days of them going into self-isolation. The customer reports their income online. After 16 days, the customer calls their regular payment line to say they need to self-isolate and if there is any help available to them. Reporting income is not considered ‘contact’ for the purposes of claiming a Crisis Payment. The customer has missed the 14 day contact timeframe, and is not eligible for a Crisis Payment for the National Health Emergency.

Case examples

Type of claim

Example

Need to be in quarantine or self-isolate

CrP-NHE is only payable to customers who must be in quarantine or self-isolation, or caring for someone, as per Commonwealth, state or territory or health professional authority directions after:

  • having a COVID-19 test
  • being instructed by an appropriate authority or professional to self-isolate

Periods of quarantine differ by state and territory and if returning from overseas.

Periods of self-isolation do not have a specific required length of time. This is because there are different factors for each case. For example, needing to self-isolate until test results are received versus needing to self-isolate after being diagnosed with COVID-19.

The requirements to quarantine or self-isolate must be due to advice from, or a requirement made by:

  • the Commonwealth
  • a state or territory government, or
  • a health professional
  • after being tested for COVID-19 and:
  • being diagnosed with COVID-19
  • awaiting results of a COVID-19 PCR test

Place of quarantine/isolation: a person can be in quarantine or self-isolate at home, in a motel, hospital or any other appropriate place. However, a person cannot qualify for CrP-NHE to care for a person who is in quarantine/self-isolation who is in a hospital as care is being provided by hospital staff.

The following are not eligible for a CrP-NHE:

  • People who are staying home to avoid getting COVID-19
  • People who are staying home because they have an existing health condition, even if told to do so by a health professional. They are not considered to meet the definition of 'required' to isolate
  • People who are staying home but are still able to go out to complete essential activities such as shopping or work
  • A person who is caring for one of the above people
  • A person who states they are caring for a person who is in quarantine/self-isolation who is in a hospital as care is being provided by hospital staff
  • A person who has returned a negative RAT result, even where they may be a close contact
  • A person who is isolating as a close contact but has not been directed to do so by a health professional and has not undertaken a PCR test or tested positive to a RAT

People who have had a COVID-19 PCR test need to quarantine/self-isolate while they wait for their results, even if they live in an area without any active cases.

Some people may need to quarantine/self-isolate on more than one occasion, even if they were in quarantine or self-isolation only recently.

A customer must give evidence of a COVID-19 PCR test or positive RAT result to meet the eligibility requirements.

Caring for an immediate family member or member of the person’s household

To qualify for a CrP-NHE, the claimant’s immediate family member or member of their household must need the claimant to provide care during their period of quarantine or isolation at the place that the care receiver is staying. Evidence of a COVID-19 PCR test or result, a positive RAT result, or confirmation from state or territory health authority that the positive RAT has been registered must be provided.

The following are examples of when an immediate family member or member of the person’s household would be regarded as requiring care:

  • The care receiver is a child or grandchild under 16 years
  • The care receiver is an elderly frail parent or grandparent
  • The care receiver needs daily care or support that may include administering medication, help with bathing and preparation of meals

Note: a person cannot qualify for a CrP-NHE to care for a person who is in quarantine or isolation in a hospital, as care is being provided by hospital staff.

A customer must give evidence of a COVID-19 test or result for the person they are caring for to meet the eligibility requirements.

Can only receive 2 payments in the 6 months immediately before the date of receipt of their current claim

Customers paid CrP-NHE twice in the 6 months before their current claim are ineligible.

Customers are stopped from lodging an online CrP-NHE claim if they have received 2 CrP-NHE payments in the last 6 months. They will be able to lodge a future online CrP-NHE claim when they have had less than 2 CrP-NHE paid in the previous 6 months. Customers can lodge a paper claim (SU717), or through Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) if they cannot lodge online. The claim should auto reject with the reason CRH (already received 2 CrP-NHE in preceding 6 months).

Example:

  • Customer CrP-NHE claim:
    • Lodged 4 June 2020
    • Self-isolation date 2 June 2020
    • Claim granted 4 June 2020
  • Next claim:
    • Lodged 11 July 2020
    • Self-isolation date 6 July 2020
    • Claim granted 13 July 2020
  • Next claim:
    • Lodged 30 December 2020
    • Self-isolation date 26 December 2020
    • 6 months immediately preceding the date of the current claim = 30 June 2020
    • Other claims granted in the period 30 June 2020 to 30 December 2020 are 11 July 2020.
    • The last claim 11 July 2020, granted 13 July 2020, was the only other claim within the last 6 months (2 payments within 6 months immediately before the date of the current claim, counting back from the claim submission date)
    • Customer is eligible and the claim is granted on 4 January 2021.
  • Next claim:
    • Lodged 7 January 2021
    • Self-isolation date 1 January 2021
    • 6 months immediately before the date of the current claim is 7 July 2020
    • Claim auto rejected reason CRH on 8 January 2021, as 2 CrP-NHE payments granted in the last 6 months. That is, counting back from the lodgement date 7 January 2021, two payments were made on 13 July 2020 and 4 January 2021
    • The customer can lodge a further CrP-NHE claim on 14 January 2021.

Started quarantine/isolation on or between 1 July 2022 and 19 July 2022

Fran is receiving a Carers Payment and is caring for her son Patrick who is 11 years old. Patrick tested positive to COVID-19 on 4 July 2022. Fran contacts the agency on 1 August 2022 to enquire about claiming a CrP-NHE.

The Service Officer records an intention to claim and refers Fran to claim for CrP-NHE via online services. Fran has until 15 August 2022 to submit her claim and provide acceptable evidence.

Example cases to determine eligibility for CrP-NHE

Description

Caring for someone in self-isolation while waiting for COVID-19 test results

Fred receives Parenting Payment for caring for his 5 year old son Blake. Blake has woken up with a sore throat and cough. Fred has contacted his doctor and has taken Blake for a COVID-19 test. Fred has $37 in liquid assets and applies for CrP-NHE.

The claim is granted because he is providing care to his son who needs to self-isolate while waiting for the outcome of the test results.

2

State government requirement to enter quarantine or self-isolation

Jamila lives in a high density tower, which the state government has locked down for 5 days to manage the spread of COVID-19. Jamila does not have any symptoms of COVID-19 and has not yet had a test. She receives Age Pension and has $200 in her bank account.

Although Jamila has been directed to self-isolate by a state authority and claims CrP-NHE, she is not eligible, as she has not been tested for COVID-19.

3

Returning from overseas - severe financial hardship

Miranda gets the single rate of Age Pension. She called to say she has recently returned from an overseas holiday, and has been in quarantine at a hotel for 3 days of the 14 required days.

While in quarantine, Miranda has taken a COVID-19 test as directed by the appropriate authority. Miranda’s liquid assets total $455, which is less than the fortnightly amount of the maximum payment rate of her pension.

Miranda lodges a claim for CrP-NHE, which is granted based on what Miranda has advised about her circumstances.

4

Requirement to quarantine or self-isolate

Alex is a student and gets Youth Allowance. He was also employed casually as a waiter at his local café, but recently lost his job. While looking at the website, Alex notices some information about Crisis Payment and wonders if he is eligible and calls us to find out.

The Service Officer determines that Alex meets the financial hardship criteria for CrP-NHE, but discovered that Alex does not need to self-isolate. Because of this, he is not eligible for CrP-NHE.

5

Caring for someone in quarantine or self-isolation

Muhammed was a full time flight attendant and has never received an Income Support Payment before. With many flights cancelled, Muhammed has been stood down from his job and is not receiving any income. He calls to seek help and lodges a claim for JobSeeker Payment, noting that he has less than $500 in his bank account.

The Service Officer asks Muhammed a few questions to see if he may also be eligible for CrP-NHE once his JobSeeker Payment claim is granted. Muhammed mentions that his partner is sick and is required to self-isolate while awaiting results of her COVID-19 test due to coming into close contact with a person with COVID-19. Muhammed is going to cook for her and make sure she takes her medication while she is in isolation, so he is granted CrP-NHE.

6

Caring for children who cannot attend school

The school that John’s children attend has been closed temporarily due to COVID-19. John gets Disability Support Pension and usually does a few hours a week at his casual job. However, John now needs to stay home to look after his children and asks if there is any help available, as he can no longer work.

John meets the financial hardship criteria but is not eligible for CrP-NHE. This is because John’s children do not need to self-isolate, even though they have been sent home from school.

7

Self-isolation due to Covid-19 diagnosis

Hyejin is receiving JobSeeker Payment and says she only has $13 in her bank account and $7 cash in her wallet. She was diagnosed with COVID-19 3 days ago and has been in self-isolation since.

Hyejin meets all other eligibility criteria, so she is granted a CrP-NHE.

8

Contacting or claiming within 14 days of caring for someone in quarantine or self-isolation

Maria has two children aged 5 and 8, who returned from an overseas trip on 1 April 2020 and needed to self-isolate for 14 days. Maria had to leave her job to care for them while they were in self-isolation. After the 14 day quarantine period finished and the children had returned to school, Maria contacted Services Australia about getting a payment as she was no longer employed, and was granted JobSeeker Payment.

The Service Officer made some further queries about Maria’s situation to see if she was also eligible for CrP-NHE. However, as it had been more than 14 days since the start of the self-isolation period, Maria was not eligible.

9

Self-isolation due to medical condition

Julia gets the single rate of JobSeeker Payment and works casually as an office manager for a small business. Julia suffers from severe asthma and on her recent visit to the doctor, was told that she should work from home as she is in a high risk category for COVID-19. Julia’s boss has been allowing her to work from home since then.

As Julia has not been diagnosed or in close contact with someone with COVID-19 and is not unwell with COVID-19 symptoms, she does not meet the eligibility criteria for CrP-NHE.

10

ABSTUDY interstate travel with parent/guardian

Talla is a 14-year old student who gets ABSTUDY Living Allowance. She lives at home in Kalgoorlie, Western Australia with her father. She needs to travel from Kalgoorlie to Adelaide to continue her schooling. Because of Talla's age, her aunty travels with her to and from her school at the beginning and end of the school term.

Upon arrival in Adelaide, Talla and her aunty are told that the South Australian government needs them to self-isolate for 14 days and take a COVID-19 test. Talla's aunty calls Services Australia to see if she can get a CrP-NHE due to caring for Talla.

Talla's aunty does not meet the definition of a family member or member of a household who is providing care, but the Service Officer tells the aunty that she can get a CrP-NHE for herself. This is because she also needs to self-isolate and is getting Age Pension. The Service Officer also tells the aunty that Talla can get a CrP-NHE too, because she gets ABSTUDY Living Allowance and is self-isolating after having a COVID-19 test.

11

ABSTUDY interstate travel with parent/guardian

Michael is a 15 year old ABSTUDY Living Allowance student travelling home to Brisbane from his boarding school in Melbourne at the end of term. His legal guardian, Jonathan, is travelling with him. Once they return to their home in Brisbane, they both need to self-isolate for 14 days and take a COVID-19 test due to a requirement made by the Queensland government.

Jonathan thinks he might be able to get 2 CrP-NHE payments because he gets JobSeeker Payment and is caring for Michael while he is in self-isolation, but is also in self-isolation himself.

Jonathan calls Services Australia and is told he is only eligible for one CrP-NHE, for himself. This is considered to be one event, not separate events.

A person who is in quarantine or isolation and caring for another person who is also in quarantine or isolation can only be paid one CrP-NHE for themselves. Michael can also get his own CrP-NHE payment, as he is also self-isolating after being tested for COVID-19.

12

Returning from overseas

Terence and Monir have been travelling interstate and return home after applying for a permit to cross the border into their home state. They are required by the state government to self-isolate at their own home for 14 days and take a COVID-19 test.

Terence and Monir have $320 in bank accounts. They both claim CrP-NHE payment and are both eligible to be paid.

13

2 quarantine or self-isolation periods

Astrid was feeling unwell with a sore throat and cough. After calling her doctor, she was told to get a test for COVID-19 and self-isolate until she received the results, which she received 2 days later. As the result was negative, she went to work the following day. 3 days later, Astrid’s son Marcus was displaying symptoms of COVID-19. Astrid took Marcus to get tested and provided care to her son, who needed to self-isolate while they waited for the test results.

Astrid meets all of the criteria for CrP-NHE and lodges two claims within the required timeframe. As the claims are for 2 separate self-isolation events, both claims are granted.

Astrid will not be able to receive further payments of CrP-NHE until 6 months elapses from the last payment of CrP-NHE.

14

Multiple claims for same quarantine or self-isolation period

Biswesh was contacted by the Department of Health and Aged Care, who informed him that he had been identified as coming in close contact with a confirmed case of COVID-19. He was instructed to take a COVID-19 test, self-isolate for 14 days, and monitor for any symptoms. The day after entering self-isolation, Biswesh claimed CrP-NHE and was granted soon after, as he was getting JobSeeker Payment and met all other criteria. Wondering if he could get more than 1 payment, Biswesh lodged another claim for CrP-NHE on the same day.

While processing the second claim, the Service Officer checked the CRISIS_PS screen and saw that a CrP-NHE had already been granted for the same quarantine or self-isolation event date. Because Biswesh claimed again for the same quarantine or self-isolation period, his second claim was rejected.

15

Self-isolation where customer has no fixed address or is homeless

Jo gets Age Pension. In her claim for CrP-NHE, she said that she has no fixed address but has been in self-isolation.

As a person can quarantine or self-isolate in any appropriate place, a Service Officer calls Jo to ask her where she is self-isolating. Jo tells the Service Officer she has been staying at a friend’s place while waiting for her COVID-19 test results. As Jo is self-isolating in an appropriate place and she meets all other criteria, her claim is granted.

16

Self-isolation where customer has no fixed address or is homeless

Cal gets JobSeeker Payment and says in his claim for CrP-NHE that he has been told to self-isolate.

As Cal’s record indicates that he may be homeless, a Service Officer calls him to check where he is self-isolating to confirm it is in an appropriate place. Cal tells the Service Officer that he is living on the streets. The Service Officer rejects his claim because he is not considered to be self-isolating in an appropriate place.

17

Previous claim rejected NEG – Not eligible payment ended

Monica contacts Services Australia on 29 July 2022 to discuss the outcome of a claim she made for CrP-NHE on 8 July 2022.

Whilst reviewing the claim details the Service Officer identifies that Monica declared in her online claim the date she started isolating was 30 June 2022. However, the evidence provided showed the PCR test dates was 4 July 2022. As an incorrect declaration was made in the online claim, the claim for CrP-NHE was rejected NEG.

As Monica is contacting the agency before 2 August 2022, the Service Officer records a Fast Note with the customer’s intent to claim and tells her to lodge a new claim for CrP-NHE via their online services within 14 days.

Following her contact on 29 July 2022, Monica submits a claim for CrP-NHE online on 10 August 2022.

Monica is eligible for a Crisis Payment for the National Health Emergency because she:

  • made an intent to claim before 2 August 2022
  • submitted a claim within 14 days from her contact for an isolation period that started on or between 1 July 2022 and 19 July 2022
