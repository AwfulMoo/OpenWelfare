4

Multiple work items + Read more ...

Multiple ACCS (child wellbeing) determinations for the same child care provider may be allocated as a bundle, including sibling assessments.

  • Where a large number of CWB determinations have been bundled, and staff are not able to fully complete, the parent work item and any other provisional sibling determinations must be finalised as a minimum
    • to identify siblings, look for matching start and end dates to the parent work item, or where this can be identified via evidence lodged
    • where required, the document ID on the ACCS child wellbeing table can be cross referenced to the corresponding transaction/work item ID by expanding a provisional determination, and viewing the 'related document' number under the Documents header
  • Where other non-sibling determinations are not able to be finalised by the Service Officer for a valid reason approved by their line manager, the remaining work items can be unassigned and returned to the national allocation pool. Valid reasons may include:
    • Service Officer unavailable
    • health and wellbeing concerns
    • other reasons appropriate for individual staff circumstances

For more information on workload allocation and managing work items, see Work Optimiser for staff.

Confirm supporting evidence is current + Read more ...

Any evidence provided must not be more than 6 months old from the commencement date of a determination.

The six month period should be looked at in terms of months rather than specific dates. For example, if a determination application period commences anytime in November 2021, it is reasonable to accept any evidence dated May 2021 and onwards.

Evidence could be dated 6 months and 1 day up to 6 months and 31 days.

If the document is a Word document incorrectly displaying today’s date, check the currency of the evidence by selecting File and confirming the 'last modified' date.

The Resources page has further information and scenarios on currency of evidence.

  • Yes, go to Step 9

11

Confirm supporting evidence child is or was at risk + Read more ...

Confirm supporting evidence contains sufficient information the child is or was taken to be at risk of serious abuse or neglect.

A child is taken to be at risk of serious abuse or neglect if the child is at risk of suffering harm as a result of being subject to, or exposed to one or more of the following events:

  • serious physically, emotional or psychological abuse
  • sexual abuse
  • domestic or family violence
  • neglect
  • Harm, means any detriment to the child wellbeing
  • Neglect, means a failure to be provided with the basic needs essential for the child’s physical and emotional wellbeing

The evidence provided should:

  • focus on the presence of risk of serious abuse or neglect (or evidence of actual abuse or neglect). For example by referencing indicators of abuse or neglect or risk factors
  • describe the child's circumstances in a way they can be clearly linked to the definition of 'at risk'
  • explain how the circumstances of the family are linked to the risk to the child (where applicable)
  • highlight how the family's issues are a barrier to the family caring appropriately for the child thereby leaving the child 'at risk'

There should be enough information to determine the child is currently suffering harm from any of these events, regardless of if:

  • they occurred in the past
  • are currently happening, or
  • they are likely to occur in the future?

There are certain circumstances that, on their own, do not mean a child should be taken to be at serious risk of abuse or neglect. These include:

  • the income of the individual or individuals who have care of the child
  • the ethnic, cultural, religious or racial background of the child or the child's immediate family
  • the geographical location in which the child and the child's immediate family resides
  • the child's place of residence is statistically an area of socio-economic disadvantage
  • the child is likely to benefit from early childhood education and care programs
  • the child has a disability, or
  • the child is in a (informal) foster care or kinship care arrangement

The Resources page has detailed information and scenarios about ACCS (child wellbeing) evidence requirements.

Note: historical evidence, which is dated within the last 6 months can be viewed/used where:

  • the determination being assessed is going to be rejected due to evidence not meeting the ‘at risk’ definition, or
  • the service has submitted an appeal for a rejected ACCS (child wellbeing) determination

Does the evidence contain enough information to conclude the child meets the ACCS (child wellbeing) definition of ‘at risk’?

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, go to Step 15

12

Determine the correct number of risk weeks + Read more ...

A determination must be given for full weeks, and can range from one week to a maximum 13 weeks or periods of up to 52 weeks, if the child:

  • is on a long term protection order
  • is in formal foster / kinship care, or
  • Is enrolled at one of the services participating in the Early Years Education Program

For a child to meet the extended determination period, one of the following must be supplied:

  • the current court order (which has not expired), or
  • a letter from a State or Territory Protection Agency, confirming a long term protection order is in place

Have either of the above documents been supplied?

  • Yes, the child meets requirements to have the determination for an extended period, go to Step 13
  • No, the child should be assessed for a reduced period of 13 weeks or less, go to Step 13

13

Determine if evidence supports period of determination + Read more ...

Determine whether the evidence supports the child is considered at risk for the period of time which the service applied for. Check:

  • evidence supports the number of risk weeks the service has claimed in the determination, or
  • duration of the early intervention program matches the number of risk weeks

If the evidence supports the child being at risk for the period of the determination the risk weeks will not need to be amended.

If evidence does not support the child being at risk for the entire period (for example, if the evidence only supports the child being at risk for a period of 10 weeks but service claims 13 weeks), update the end date of the determination to the Sunday of the week the evidence supports the child being at risk.

If the evidence contains a statement of support for a period of time, (for example, ‘support application of Child Wellbeing for a period of 13 weeks’, calculate the period from the date the evidence is issued. Update the weeks to start from the first Monday after date the evidence was issued.

Go to Step 14

Determination must be equal/within Enrolment validity for Arrangement type CWA/ACCS + Read more ...

This error will occur in the event the child's enrolment with service has ceased or the application contains dates where there is no active enrolment.

If the above error presents:

  • Go to the Enrolments icon and note the Arrangement End date for the child's enrolment
  • Go to the provisional CWB line and edit the End Date and assess ACCS (child wellbeing) entitlement. As CWB can only be assessed for full weeks it must be ended on a Sunday. If the enrolment ended mid-week the end date can be the Sunday following the date the enrolment ceased
  • If the enrolment has since been disputed by the individual, the determination cannot be approved. If the enrolment is showing as disputed, contact the child care service to inform them:
    • of the enrolment status, and
    • the determination will be placed on hold for two weeks to allow time for the child care service and individual to resolve the enrolment dispute
  • Place the activity on hold and create case notes on the record. If the enrolment remains disputed after 2 weeks, reject the determination reason, Evidence does not meet minimum requirements. Contact the child care service to advise of the rejection
  • ensure details of any updates are recorded in Case Notes when finalising the activity

22

Expedite change of care and CCS processing + Read more ...

Where a change of care has occurred, it is mandatory to attempt to contact the losing carer by phone initially, in most cases. There are some exceptions to this.

Where the child is in care of the Minister and is identified as a child at risk it may not be appropriate to contact the losing care provider. If the Service Officer is satisfied based on the evidence provided by the gaining care provider a change of care has occurred, the claim can be escalated for priority processing.

Where the Service Officer has the adequate skill set to action the FAO legacy and CCS claim, end to end processing may occur, that is, the families and CCS claims may be processed by the ACCSPT Service Officer to allow payment of ACCS (child wellbeing) to commence.

Revoke a determination + Read more ...

If determination has been granted incorrectly and the service has lodged sessions reports within the determination period, provide feedback to the Service Officer who incorrectly assessed the determination application, noting the determination is not to be revoked.

An approved child wellbeing determination can be revoked if the:

  • service has not lodged session reports for any of the determination period, that is the determination:
    • has a future start date, or
    • commences on the Monday of the current week

Do not delete a child wellbeing determination.

    • ACCS Cap reached
    • Evidence does not demonstrate child at risk
    • Evidence does not meet minimum requirements
    • No certificate lodged
    • No evidence provided for determination
    • Child no longer at risk
    • Evidence more than 6 months old

Where the circumstances are not represented in the above list:

  • select Evidence does not demonstrate child at risk, and
  • record the reason in the free text box
