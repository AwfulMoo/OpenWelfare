2

JETCCFA current customers + Read more ...

Current JETCCFA customers were included in the CCF/CCR call to action mail out and are therefore eligible for streamlined transition.

  • Pre (Transition to Work) release: Advise customers they may be eligible for ACCS (Transition to Work). Explain they will be sent a notice on 4 June inviting them to lodge an application for ACCS (Transition to Work). See below for information about applying for ACCS
  • Post (Transition to Work) release: Advise customers they must provide their CTA information before they can apply for ACCS (Transition to Work). The application should be lodged before 2 July if they wish to be assessed for the higher rate of assistance as there are no backdating provisions for ACCS (Transition to Work)
  • Applying for ACCS (Transition to Work) – from 4 June 2018

Advise customers they should lodge an application as soon as possible because ACCS (Transition to Work) cannot be backdated. Customers can apply:

  • Digitally
  • Via online services. Once CCS has been assessed, the ‘Transition to Work’ option will be available from the Child Care Subsidy submenu if they receiving an eligible income support payment and have at least one (Transition to Work) activity recorded for CCS
  • By completing the FA085 Application for Additional Child Care Subsidy (Transition to Work). See the Resources page for a link to CCS forms

Advise customers if they provide their CTA information, but do not apply for ACCS (Transition to Work) before 2 July 2018, they will be assessed for CCS but not ACCS.

For the CCS streamlined process see Step 1 in the Recording Call to Action information table.

3

GCCB Current customers + Read more ...

Current GCCB customers were included in the CCF/CCR call to action mail out and therefore eligible for streamlined transition.

When providing CTA information, customers will be asked to provide further information to transition to ACCS (Grandparent), including:

  • evidence of their grandparent relationship to the child, and
  • evidence they have 65% or more care of the child (parenting orders etc.), including autonomy of decision making for the child (responsible for day-to-day decisions about the care, welfare and development of their grandchild)

ACCS (Grandparent) evidence

To support GCCB customers transition to ACCS (Grandparent), evidence requirements have been streamlined:

  • In all cases, customers must verify their relationship to the child if this has not already been established, noting the customer must meet the definition of a grandparent for the purpose of ACCS (Grandparent)
  • Customer has 100% care of the child: If Services Australia has 100% care recorded, and evidence exists on record, no further evidence is required. Current evidence is sufficient to verify care arrangements and substantial autonomy of decision making
  • Customer has less than 100% care:
    • Evidence of percentage of care is required
    • Evidence of autonomy of decision making is required if not previously provided, or not detailed in other evidence on hand (e.g. FTB shared care assessment)

Where a customer is required to provide evidence to support their ACCS (Grandparent) application, and this is not provided within 28 days, the ‘deemed’ ACCS (Grandparent) claim will be not effective, however they will be assessed for CCS. The customer will need to lodge a new ACCS (Grandparent) application and the maximum 28 day backdating provision will apply. In these cases, customers should be encouraged to lodge a new application as soon as possible.

If a customer contacts because they are unable to submit their CTA online due to evidence requirements, and they advise this information has been provided previously, record the customer’s CTA information via the Roxy call to action script so the CTA can be submitted. Advise the customer they may be required to provide evidence if it is not accessible from their record when assessing their ACCS (Grandparent) application. For the CCS streamlined process see Step 1 in the Recording Call to Action information table.

4

Special Child Care Benefit (SCCB) customers + Read more ...

Current SCCB customers were included in the CCF/CCR call to action mail out and therefore eligible for streamlined transition to CCS.

  • Temporary financial hardship: Advise the customers they should provide their CTA information as soon as possible. Explain that SCCB will end on 1 July 2018. If they are still in financial hardship they will need to apply for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship):
    • digitally via online services. Once CCS has been assessed, the ‘Temporary Financial Hardship’ option will be available from the Child Care Subsidy submenu
    • by completing the FA086 Claim for Additional Child Care Subsidy (Temporary Financial Hardship). See the Resources page for a link to CCS forms
  • Child at risk: Advise the customer they should provide their CTA information as soon as possible. Explain that their current higher level of assistance will end on 1 July 2018. They should speak to their child care service who are responsible for helping them access the higher rate of assistance from 2 July 2018

For the streamlined transition process see Step 1 in the Recording Call to Action information table.

5

CCF Lump Sum Claim customers + Read more ...

Lump Sum Claim (LSC) customers were not selected for streamlined transition as they are not considered to be CCF current. Customers who lodged a lump sum claim for the 2015/16 or 2016/17 years were sent a notice in April inviting them to lodge a claim for CCS.

Advise customers they have three options to be able to receive child care fee assistance from 2 July 2018:

  • CCS Pre-claim:
    • Complete a CCS new claim (available from April 2018), preferably via online services
    • Lodge a LSC to access CCF/CCR entitlement for 2017-18
  • Combined CCF/CCS claim:
    • Complete a CCF claim for ongoing fee reduction from April 2018, which will include the expanded CCS questions, so no additional CCS claim will be required
    • Lodge a LSC to access the balance of CCF/CCR entitlement for 2017-18
  • CCS new claim:
    • Complete a CCS new claim between 2 and 29 July 2018 to ensure the claim can be backdated to 2 July, preferably via online services
    • Lodge a LSC to access CCF/CCR entitlement for 2017-18

Customers should be encouraged to lodge a pre-claim as soon as possible due to the 28 day backdating provisions.

See Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) claims.

6

Non-current customers using child care before 2 July 2018 + Read more ...

Customers using an approved child care service should be encouraged to claim CCB as reduced fees before 2 July 2018. By doing so, they can also be assessed for CCS from 2 July 2018. Customers should be encouraged to claim CCB/CCS via online services, but can also claim verbally via staff assisted channels. Claims will include an additional set of questions for CCS.

Paper claims

If the customer lodges a FA002 between early April and 2 July 2018 and are granted CCF, a CTA notice will be issued requesting they provide additional information to transition to CCS. Service Officers who grant CCF on or after 15 June 2018 should attempt to telephone the customer to obtain the additional information for CCS. This is to avoid any delays in customers receiving this request due to mail delivery.

Note: after 23 September 2018 obsolete versions of FA002 will not be accepted.

7

Non-current customers using child care from 2 July 2018 + Read more ...

Customers not included in the transition process, and who will only be using an approved child care from 2 July 2018, can lodge an early claim for CCS. Encourage customers to claim CCS via their online account. Service Officers can complete a verbal claim via the Assisted Customer Claim. See Claiming Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS).

Advise the customer if their child care service lodges the initial enrolment:

  • before they lodge their CCS claim, it will present for confirmation as part of the online claim (or Assisted Customer Claim for staff). Note: customers who lodge an offline (paper) claim will need to confirm enrolment outside the claim process
  • after they lodge their CCS claim, they will need to confirm enrolment separately. This must be done before their entitlement can be determined

Child Care Subsidy payments cannot be made until enrolment has been confirmed.

8

Registered Care (CCI) customers and Registered Care providers + Read more ...

CCS is only payable for child care provided by approved child care services. There are no provisions for child care fee assistance for children attending registered care.

Registered Care customers (CCI)

  • Advise customers they can claim for periods of care provided by a registered care provider before 2 July 2018. CCI claims can only be lodged up to 12 months after the care was provided. Claims lodged for registered care provided on or after 2 July 2018 will be rejected.
  • If the customer will be using an approved child care service from 2 July 2018, advise them they will need to lodge a new claim for CCS. See Claiming Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS).

Registered Care providers (RCP)

  • Advise the care provider they can still lodge an application to be a registered carer up until 1 July 2019 in relation to care provided prior to 2 July 2018. Applications lodged on or after 2 July 2019 will be rejected.

Information on CCS activity types + Read more ...

If a CCS activity has been allocated for processing, the activity type needs to be identified to determine the correct process to follow.

The types of CCS activities during Transition are:

  • Draft CTA activity
  • Customer Submitted CTA activity
  • Staff changed CTA activity from Draft to In Process
  • CCS pre claim

A CTA activity is a claim shell which has been created to record customer’s CTA information. Where the claim is in:

  • Draft mode:
    • the claim activity contains no recorded data
    • the customer has not responded to their CTA
    • the customer can view and complete the CTA activity via their Online Services
  • In Process:
    • the Claim activity will contain information that has been submitted by the customer, or
    • may contain provisional data previously entered by a Service Officer
    • where a Service Officer has changed the activity status from Draft to In Process, the activity can no longer be viewed or actioned by the customer via their Online Services

Child Care Benefit (CCB) claims lodged after 14 April 2018 will contain CCS information to enable transition to the new payment. Where a CCB/CCS claim is lodged, the CCB and CCS components will be separated, with the CCB claim created in Customer First, and the CCS claim in Process Direct, that is, the claim will be split. These ‘separate’ claims will not be linked and are processed independently. This means they will be allocated as separate activities within WLM where auto assessment is not met.

2

Identifying CCS activity types + Read more ...

Identification of the CCS activity type will determine how the activity is processed. To establish the type of CCS activity:

  • Navigate to the Inbox and insert the Claim ID
  • Select the Child Care Subsidy claim
  • Select Claim to display the Claim Application page

If the Claim Status is Draft, the activity is a Draft CTA activity. Allocation for Draft activities should not occur. If the customer has contacted to provide their CTA information, see Recording Call to Action Information. Procedure ends here.

All other CCS activities will reflect a status of ‘In Process’ and can be further be identified by a combination of the ‘Submitted By’ field on the Claim Application page, and the information within View Claim. To View the Claim information, select the Show icon located in the top left corner:

  • Customer Submitted CTA activity: the ‘Submitted By’ field displays the customer’s CRN, and the View Claim information contains no customer name, additional contact details, or residency details
  • Staff changed CTA activity from Draft to In Process: the ‘Submitted By’ field displays a Logon ID, and the View Claim information contains no other information than Child details
  • CCS pre Claim: the ‘Submitted By’ field displays the customer’s CRN (if submitted online) or Logon ID (if submitted via an ACC), and the View Claim information contains the customer’s full name, additional contact details, claim type and Child details

3

CCS processing hierarchy + Read more ...

CCS and Families legacy claims are to be processed in order of submission to ensure the most recent information is applied to the record.

To check for other CCS and Families claims:

  • Locate the customer’s CRN
  • Select Home
  • Select the Customer Summary tile and insert the customer’s CRN into the relevant field
  • Select the customer from the Search Results
  • Select Transactions to display all submitted claims

CCS claims and Families legacy claims are to be processed in the order of submission by the customer, except where there is a:

  • CCS claim and a Families legacy claim (e.g. FTB) on the record with the same submission dates - process the Families legacy claim first
  • Draft CTA activity and a CCS Claim on the record - process the CCS claim first and ensure children are added to the assessment. Place the activity on hold to 24 September 2018. No further action is to be taken with the Draft CTA activity
  • Staff changed CTA activity from Draft to In Process and a CCS Claim on the record; process the CCS claim first and ensure children are added to the assessment. The Staff changed CTA activity In Process should be held until 24/09/18
  • Full CCS claim has been processed prior to the CTA being actioned, the CTA should be held until 24/09/18 as it is no longer required. If the customer has ‘submitted’ the CTA online, update any change of circumstances separately (i.e. income estimate or activity test)

Examples:

  • CCB current customer lodges a CCS claim, however does not action their CTA activity (the CTA activity is a Draft activity). The CCS claim is processed, no further action is required with the Draft CTA activity
  • CCB current customer lodges a CCS claim and later submits their call to action with updated activity test details. The CCS claim is processed and the CTA is placed on hold until 24/09/18. The updated activity test details are coded via the relevant ATD table(s) as this information was received after the initial full CCS claim
  • CCB current customer contacts and requests a child be added to their CCB assessment. The Assisted Customer Claim is run to obtain the relevant information and submitted. As the customer has not responded to their CTA activity, there will be two CCS activities in Process Direct – the Draft CTA activity and the CCS claim. The Families legacy claim is processed first, followed by the CCS claim. No further action is to be taken with the Draft CTA activity
