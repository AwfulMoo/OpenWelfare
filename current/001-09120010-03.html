Preparing a medical certificate for scanning + Read more ...

A valid medical certificate does not have to be a Centrelink Medical Certificate form (SU415 or SU683) but it must provide the required information.

If scanning a Centrelink Medical Certificate form (SU415) with barcode:

  • Scan as SU415, following usual process for documents with a barcode

If scanning a Non-Centrelink medical certificate with no barcode, or if the barcode on the SU415 appears to be of poor quality and may not be readable:

  • Place the UNSMCER reclassification page in front of the medical certificate. The Resources page contains a link to the UNSMCER reclassification page
  • For additional medical certificates, use a separator page between each document

Do not scan or reclassify medical certificates using

  • UNS008 medical/sensitive (MIFE) documents classification (the Resources page contains a link to the UNS008 medical/sensitive (MIFE) documents classification page), or
  • other unstructured medical evidence document codes

Use the Store Only scanning option on the MFD only where:

  • staff have already assessed the medical certificate, and
  • creation of a new work item is not required

If a medical certificate is received by mail or fax at a service centre:

  • Assess the medical certificate no later than close of business on the day after lodgement
  • Scan the medical certificate to the customer record

See Scanning Centrelink medical/sensitive documents using an MFD for more information regarding this process.

3

Assess the medical certificate for temporary incapacity exemption + Read more ...

A temporary incapacity exemption can be granted if evidence shows that a job seeker with mutual obligation or participation requirements is temporarily unable to work at least 8 hours per week due to a medical condition.

Points to consider when determining if a job seeker can be granted a temporary incapacity exemption:

  • exemption is to be based on the current medical certificate. Consideration can also be given to additional information provided by:
    • the Medical Condition Summary (MCSS) screen
    • Document Tools to view previously lodged medical evidence
    • current and valid assessment of the job seeker's work capacity (ESAt or JCA reports)
    • reported earnings
  • all medical conditions listed on the medical certificate must be considered when determining if a temporary incapacity exemption can be granted, except where:
    • the primary condition listed on the medical certificate is primarily attributable to drug and/or alcohol dependency or misuse and the job seeker is not a Community Development Program (CDP) participant. A temporary incapacity exemption cannot be granted for this situation. This is regardless of whether there is a secondary temporary condition not related to drug or alcohol, which indicates an exemption may be applied. If the medical certificate does not differentiate between primary and secondary, the Service Officer must determine if the customer's incapacity is wholly or mainly attributable to drug or alcohol dependency/misuse. This can be based on previous medical information provided by the customer and/or a current and valid ESAt or JCA report. If unsure, discuss with appropriate person for assistance (for example, customer's treating doctor, Service Support Officer, Technical Peer Support)
  • if all medical conditions listed are permanent with no exacerbations with a prognosis of 24 months or more, a temporary incapacity exemption cannot be granted
  • a temporary incapacity exemption may be granted where a condition is considered 'permanent', but the prognosis states the resulting incapacity for all work is expected to be temporary (less than 2 years)
  • a prognosis of 'Uncertain' is acceptable and applies to TMP/ PRM/ REC
  • if multiple medical conditions are listed, including both temporary and permanent, a temporary incapacity exemption can be granted if the job seeker is unable to work at least 8 hours per week due to at least 1 of the temporary or exacerbated/recurring conditions
  • if the customer has received repeated, continuous exemptions for the same primary conditions, this may indicate their incapacity for work is no longer temporary (expected to persist for less than 2 years). A temporary incapacity exemption can still be granted based on the current medical certificate if it lists new primary conditions, or if the conditions causing incapacity for work have recently changed
  • check if all medical conditions are included in a current and valid ESAt or JCA report. Information from the report can be used unless the new medical evidence indicates the previously assessed medical condition(s) has changed therefore the report is no longer valid (for example, existing spinal condition has worsened or treatment has changed). New conditions are to be assessed based on the latest medical evidence supplied. Note:
    • if there is conflicting information between the medical certificate and a current and valid ESAt/JCA report, and unable to clarify with the health professional, consider escalating to appropriate level for advice if unsure which information to base decision on (for example, Service Support Officer, Technical Peer Support or Level 2 Helpdesk)
    • do not place medical certificate on hold. Medical certificate must be actioned
  • Even if the customer is temporarily incapacitated for all work, an exemption may not be granted if the treating medical practitioner has provided information on the medical certificate which clearly shows the customer can meet their requirements in other ways (for example, by undertaking job searches). A decision not to grant an exemption for this reason must be supported by current, clear medical evidence in all cases

If the medical certificate is lodged:

  • for a non-new claim and the customer is on a current payment, go to Step 4
  • as part of a new claim for an income support payment with mutual obligation requirements, go to Step 5

4

Process medical certificate for a non-new claim activity + Read more ...

Suitably skilled Service Officers are to key the medical certificate using the task selector in Process Direct (if relevant training has been undertaken), or Medical Certificate guided procedure in Customer First.

Note: the fields to be completed and the order in which they appear, will vary depending on whether the guided procedure or task selector is used.

Complete all relevant fields within the workflow including the following if required:

  • Event Date is the same as the Unfit to work/study/participate from date unless it is a future date. If it is a future date, record the Event Date as today's date (the date the medical certificate is being recorded). Note: this is not the Date of Receipt unless the Date of Receipt is today
  • Treating Health Professional's Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (Ahpra) provider number (leave blank if not provided)
  • Unfit to work/study/participate from field, use the from date on the medical certificate
  • Unfit to work/study/participate to field, use the to date on the medical certificate. Note: this cannot exceed 13 weeks, which is 91 days (that is, the from date plus 90 days)
  • determine if a serious illness exemption can be granted by completing the questions. See Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements for serious illness
  • determine if an Incapacity Exemption is to be granted or not:
    • if Not Exempt, choose an appropriate Non Exemption reason. See the Resources page for list and explanation of available reasons
  • Date of Receipt (DOR): the date the medical certificate was initially received/scanned. Note: this field is auto populated if the task selector in Process Direct is used. As it cannot be changed, it is to be disregarded when QOL is undertaken as per the Background of Quality On Line (QOL). Process Direct may be identified in the Activity heading NSS/REA in the MC screen, while Customer First will display as Act: NSS/DLY in the MC screen

Note: if a customer has a medical certificate previously keyed where a temporary incapacity exemption was not granted and subsequently submits a new medical certificate where an incapacity exemption is to be granted, key the incapacity exemption as per the details on the new medical certificate. Do not amend the details of the non-incapacity exemption already keyed on the MC screen. Overlapping the dates is not an issue if an exemption is to be applied and the decision for the previous medical certificate was to not exempt. However if the from and to dates on the new medical certificate are the same as the previous medical certificate, the Event Date is the date the information is being keyed.

5

Process medical certificate for a new claim activity + Read more ...

Note: only suitably skilled Service Officers are to process a medical certificate as part of a new claim. Do not use the task selector in Process Direct or the guided procedure in Customer First to process a medical certificate within a new claim activity. This must be actioned manually within the new claim activity.

Go to the Medical Conditions (MC) screen. In Process Direct, in the Super Key field, key 'MC' to go to the Medical Assessment screen. Select '+' to create a new medical assessment and complete the following fields:

  • key the Event Date as the Unfit to work/study/participate from date unless it is a future date. If it is a future date, record the Event Date as today's date (the date the medical certificate is being recorded)
  • select the relevant Assigning Professional
  • key the Assigning Professional's name
  • key the Treating health professional's phone number
  • key the Treating health professional's provider number (leave blank if not provided)
  • select the relevant Hours per week from the drop down box
  • select the Incapacity Exempt Indicator from the drop down box (i.e. outcome of decision - Y if exempt, N if not exempt and S if serious illness criteria applies)
  • select the Incapacity Exempt Reason Code from the drop down box
  • key the Incapacity Start Date using the from date on the medical certificate
  • key the Incapacity End Date using the to date on the medical certificate (13 weeks (91 days) is the maximum period a medical certificate can be keyed for unless it is a serious illness exemption and the medical certificate covers a longer period)
  • select Save. The Create Medical Condition field will display. Complete the following:
    • select the Medical Condition Code from the drop down box
    • select the Incapacity type by code from the drop down box. Options are permanent, temporary or recurring (exacerbation of a permanent condition)
    • select Save. Record all permanent, temporary or recurring conditions listed on the medical certificate. To record multiple conditions, select Save and Add Another to create additional medical conditions

Note: if a customer has a medical certificate previously keyed where a temporary incapacity exemption was not granted and subsequently submits a new medical certificate where an incapacity exemption is to be granted, key the incapacity exemption as per the details on the new certificate. Do not amend the details of the non-incapacity exemption already keyed on the MC screen. Overlapping the dates is not an issue if an exemption is to be applied and the decision for the previous medical certificate was to not exempt. However if the from and to dates on the new medical certificate are the same as the previous medical certificate, the Event Date is the date the information is being keyed.

See the Resources page for examples.

2

Determine if an ESAt request is required + Read more ...

Note: ESAt referral requests are not applicable for ParentsNext participants

Once a decision is made to grant or not grant a temporary incapacity exemption, consider if an ESAt referral request is appropriate to reassess the job seeker's work capacity. An ESAt may be considered if the medical certificate and/or other available information indicates the job seeker:

  • has permanent medical condition(s) that impact their ability to work
  • has medical condition/s that is medium or long term (24 months) in nature, including episodic or chronic conditions (for example. Schizophrenia, psychosis, depression and anxiety)
  • cannot be granted a temporary incapacity exemption and may have a Temporary Reduced Work Capacity of less than 30 hours per week
  • has medical condition(s) that appear to be no longer of a temporary incapacity nature based on multiple medical certificates being lodged for the same condition

Do not request an ESAt where:

  • the job seeker is a compulsory ParentsNext participant, or
  • the job seeker had medical conditions assessed through a previous ESAt that is still valid (for instance, no significant change in medical conditions), or
  • the conditions are temporary (less than 13 weeks)

See Request an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt).

3

Consider recording a new job seeker circumstance + Read more ...

A medical certificate may indicate there are personal circumstances that can directly impact on the job seeker's ongoing capacity to comply with their mutual obligation requirements. These job seekers may require additional support.

Update the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen where there has been a change to the job seeker's circumstances (even if a temporary incapacity exemption is not granted) that:

  • potentially impacts the job seeker's ability to participate on an ongoing basis, and
  • puts the job seeker at a higher risk of non-compliance (for example, a drug or alcohol dependency)

See Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirements.

Note: only update the Job Seeker Snapshot if the customer is in the service centre or on the phone.

5

Inviting a claim for Disability Support Pension (DSP) + Read more ...

Only invite job seekers to claim DSP if there is clear evidence they are likely to meet all eligibility criteria. It is not appropriate to invite a claim for DSP simply because a job seeker has a permanent condition.

Has the decision been made to invite a claim for DSP?

  • Yes, see Claiming Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • No, or if eligibility is unclear based on the medical evidence, go to Step 6

6

Connection with Employment Services or ParentsNext Provider + Read more ...

If an incapacity exemption has not been granted and the job seeker or participant does not have a Temporary Reduced Work Capacity or a Partial Capacity to Work of 0-14 hours per week, ensure the job seeker or participant is connected to an appropriate Employment Services or ParentsNext Provider (if not already referred).

Determine if the job seeker is subject to modified mutual obligation requirements. This is normally assessed from the job seeker's most recent ESAt.

See:

  • Approved activities for job seekers assessed as having a partial capacity to work (PCW), and
  • Approved activities for job seekers assessed as having a temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC)

For ParentsNext participants

If an incapacity exemption has not been granted and the parent:

  • has not yet been referred to a ParentsNext provider, ensure they have a future ParentsNext appointment booked
  • has already been referred to a ParentsNext provider, it will be the responsibility of the ParentsNext provider to re-engage the parent. The agency is not required to undertake any follow up at the end of the exemption

7

Job seeker contact and key messages after assessment of medical certificate + Read more ...

When a temporary incapacity exemption is:

  • not granted, an automatic notification is sent to the job seeker (check the letter has been issued)
  • granted, an automatic notification is not sent to the job seeker

If the assessment is completed without the job seeker present, the Service Officer must make 2 genuine attempts to contact the job seeker and advise them of the outcome regardless of whether an exemption has been granted or not. If unable to contact the job seeker, record details of contact attempts. See How to code and action a manual review if required.

Discuss the following points with the job seeker, if appropriate:

  • requirement to notify if there is a change in their circumstances
  • if a temporary incapacity exemption is not granted, the job seeker will receive notification they must continue to meet their mutual obligation or participation requirements. If they are unable to attend an activity (for example, due to their illness or injury) they must contact their provider as soon as possible to explain why they cannot attend
  • if a temporary incapacity exemption is granted and they are still unable to meet their requirements when their current medical certificate ends, they need to lodge a new one. An assessment will then be done to determine if a further temporary incapacity exemption can be granted

If applicable, the Service Officer is to:

  • ensure the Job Plan for Centrelink managed job seeker's reflects their current circumstances, taking into account any Partial Capacity to Work, or Temporary Reduced Work Capacity or request an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) if appropriate
  • negotiate a new Job Plan if required

8

Medical certificate assessments available in Process Direct + Read more ...

Service Officers can assess medical certificates via a guided Task Selector in Process Direct.

From 28 September 2019, scanned medical certificates (SU415 and SU683):

  • automatically create a Change of Circumstance (COC) medical certificate activity in Process Direct
  • allocate via Workload Manager (WLM)

Status of work items in WLM:

  • When a scanned medical certificate is assessed and completed in Process Direct, the WLM work item will automatically be closed
  • If the task selector is started, but not completed, in Process Direct, the work item will remain open. In these cases the status of the WLM work item should be checked and updated as required as per Workload Management

To identify the correct medical condition, Service Officers can select the 'Medline' hyperlink on the Medical Condition Information screen, which goes to the MedlinePlus website. Service Officers can use this website to search for relevant medical conditions.

Note: the Process Direct Task Selector, does not allow for the DOR (Date of Receipt) to be changed. Quality On Line (QOL) checkers must disregard the DOR question within the QOL tool.

Ending an exemption

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Notification to end exemption + Read more ...

Job seekers who notify Services Australia they no longer require an exemption from their mutual obligation or participation requirements must provide evidence from their medical practitioner stating they now have capacity to work for at least 8 hours or more per week.

Has new medical evidence been provided?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here as no change can be actioned without updated medical evidence

2

End exemption + Read more ...

Once new medical evidence is received:

  • manually update the Unfit to Work/Study/Participate To date on the Medical Conditions (MC) screen based on the new medical certificate. This will then auto-update the exemption end date on the Activity and Exemptions (AEX) screen
  • For job seekers:
    • connect or reconnect the job seeker with their provider, unless the job seeker indicates they will be undertaking an approved activity that will make them Centrelink managed
    • if the job seeker will be Centrelink managed, negotiate a new Job Plan with the job seeker
  • For ParentsNext participants, if the parent:
    • has not been referred to a ParentsNext provider, the system will automatically book a new ParentsNext appointment at the end of the exemption so the parent can be referred to a ParentsNext provider
    • has already been referred to a ParentsNext provider, it will be the responsibility of the ParentsNext provider to re-engage the parent at the end of the exemption period. The agency is not required to undertake any follow up at the end of the exemption
  • record a DOC confirming the advice given and any other relevant information disclosed by the job seeker
