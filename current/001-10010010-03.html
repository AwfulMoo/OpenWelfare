Targeted Compliance Framework

Provider roles and responsibilities under the Targeted Compliance Framework

Table 1: This table outlines the jobactive, Disability Employment Services (DES) and ParentsNext provider's (provider's) responsibilities in managing job seeker non-compliance with their compulsory requirements using the Targeted Compliance Framework.

Item

Description

1

Providers responsibilities to support ongoing job seeker participation and compliance + Read more ...

Providers responsibilities include:

  • explaining compulsory requirements, ensuring job seekers understand what they need to do in return for receiving a participation payment and what can happen to their payment if they don’t meet compulsory requirements
  • negotiating compulsory requirements in Job/Participation Plans that comply with legislation and applicable policy
  • ensuring a job seekers compulsory requirements are reasonable according to their individual circumstances and assessed capacity
  • ensuring job seekers are aware of tools that are available to assist them record attendance at requirements and expectations in relation to managing their own compliance
  • recording attendance at appointments and with other requirements where requested or a job seeker is unable to do so
  • rescheduling or removing appointments/requirements where a job seeker makes prior contact and provides an acceptable reason why they are unable to attend
  • when aware of non-compliance, making an outbound contact attempt to the job seeker on the day of a missed requirement

2

Provider’s delegated roles and responsibilities when managing non-compliance using the Targeted Compliance Framework + Read more ...

Providers have responsibility for monitoring compliance with compulsory requirements and have delegation for some decisions under Social Security Law.

Their responsibility includes:

  • identifying and recording that an incident has occurred that constitutes a mutual obligation failure, work refusal failure or unemployment failure
  • identifying and electronically advising Services Australia using the Employment Services System (ESS) of a mutual obligation failure or work refusal failure so that payment can be suspended
  • identifying and recording a valid reason does or does not exist for a mutual obligation failure for the purpose of confirming or removing a demerit
  • setting and notifying reconnection requirements for mutual obligation failures and work refusal failures, notifying Services Australia the deemed date of notification and when conditions have been met for suspension to be ended
  • undertaking a Capability Interview
  • notifying a reconnection requirement when a further demerit is confirmed after a Capability Interview triggering a Capability Assessment
  • using the Department of Education, Skills and Employment's Employment Services System (ESS) to notify Services Australia that a mutual obligation failure, work refusal failure or unemployment failure requires assessment of whether a reasonable excuse exists for the purpose of determining whether a penalty should be applied

3

When does a job seeker need to contact their provider? + Read more ...

A job seeker needs to contact their provider:

  • if they are unable to attend an appointment or comply with the requirement, contact with the provider must occur prior to the scheduled start time
  • if they are having trouble recording their own attendance at a requirement or they have attended a requirement and the provider is recording attendance on their behalf because they are unable to record it for themselves
  • if there are circumstances that are affecting their ongoing capacity to comply with a requirement
  • following payment suspension triggered by a mutual obligation failure or work refusal failure
  • where a Capability Interview is outstanding
  • on advice from Services Australia following a Capability Assessment where the outcome is to return a job seeker to the green zone and their Job Plan/Participation Plan needs to be renegotiated
  • job seekers who disagree with the provider's decision to retain a demerit or record a mutual obligation failure, must also contact their provider to discuss this in the first instance. If after talking with their provider they are not satisfied with the outcome the job seeker may then contact the Department of Education, Skills and Employment to discuss

4

Notification + Read more ...

Providers are responsible for notifying the job seeker of:

  • the decision to record a demerit (through ESS or the job seeker Dashboard in Job Seeker App/jobactive website)
  • the action required to have a suspension lifted (reconnection requirement), including the requirement to contact Services Australia for a Capability Assessment

Job Seeker Compliance Model

Provider roles and responsibilities under the Job Seeker Compliance Model

Table 2: This table outlines the responsibilities of Community Development Program (CDP) providers when managing CDP job seeker non-compliance with compulsory requirements using the Job Seeker Compliance Model.

Item

Description

1

CDP Providers responsibilities to support ongoing participation and compliance + Read more ...

CDP providers responsibilities include:

  • explaining compulsory requirements, ensuring CDP job seeker's understand what they need to do in return for receiving a participation payment and what can happen to their payment where they don’t meet compulsory requirements
  • negotiating compulsory requirements in Job Plans that comply with legislation and applicable policy
  • ensuring a CDP job seeker's compulsory requirements are reasonable based on their individual circumstances and assessed capacity
  • recording attendance at appointments and with other requirements
  • rescheduling appointments/requirements where a CDP job seeker makes prior contact and provides an acceptable reason why they are unable to attend

2

CDP provider's delegated roles and responsibilities when managing non-compliance using the Job Seeker Compliance Model + Read more ...

CDP providers have responsibility for monitoring compliance with compulsory requirements and have delegation for some decisions under Social Security Law.

Their overall responsibility includes:

  • electronically advising Services Australia through the Employment Services System (ESS) that a participation payment is not payable as a result of non-attendance at a compulsory appointment
  • setting and notifying reconnection and further reconnection requirements after a CDP job seeker has failed to attend a compulsory appointment
  • using the Department of Education, Skills and Employment's Employment Services System (ESS) to notify Services Australia that a Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP) or connection, non-attendance, reconnection, No Show, No Pay, or serious failures requires investigation

3

When does a CDP job seeker need to contact their provider + Read more ...

A CDP job seeker will need to contact their provider:

  • if they are unable to attend an appointment or comply with a requirement, contact with their provider must occur prior to the scheduled start time, where possible
  • if there are circumstances that are affecting their ongoing capacity to comply with a requirement
  • following payment suspension triggered by a Non-Attendance Report (NAR)

4

Notification + Read more ...

CDP providers are responsible for notifying the CDP job seeker:

  • that their participation payment has stopped due to a missed appointment
  • of the action required to restart their payment (reconnection requirement)

CDP providers are responsible for notifying Services Australia:

  • where they wish to withdraw compliance action
  • where a job seeker fails to attend a Compliance Activity re-engagement activity, or where the job seeker does not sign their amended Job Plan which includes the Compliance Activity
