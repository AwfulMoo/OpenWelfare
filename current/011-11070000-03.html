Responding to calls on the AODR AMP enquiry line

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Answers a call to the AODR AMP line + Read more ...

Welcome the caller by stating 'Welcome to the Australian Organ Donor Register, this is (state your first name)' or use Services Australia standard greeting.

Caller requests a search of the AODR to be undertaken

Advise the caller that a security check must be conducted first. See Table 2.

Ask the caller for their:

  • name
  • business name
  • pass phrase

Has the AMP has forgotten their pass phrase?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, all AMP details are correct, go to Step 3

2

AMP has forgotten their pass phrase + Read more ...

Ask the

  • address of the agency they are employed with, and/or
  • their contact phone number

Where these details match the details recorded for the AMP Service Officers may assist the AMP with the pass phrase by providing them with a prompt or clue. For example:

  • it might be the name of your child or pet
  • it's a colour

Does the AMP correctly remember their pass phrase?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, a search is unable to be conducted, advise them to email aodr@servicesaustralia.gov.au. This email is available 24 hours a day

Note: If another AMP is available they may conduct a search or access online services via PRODA. Where an AMP does not wish to email direct, escalate to a PSO.

3

Check for AMP registration end date + Read more ...

Is the AMP registration end dated?

  • Yes, a search is unable to be conducted, advise the AMP to contact the AODR team
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Requests details for search + Read more ...

Ask the AMP to provide the following details for the person they want to search for:

  • Family name
  • Gender
  • Date of birth

5

Undertake search + Read more ...

Conduct the search, see Search and identify an ODR on behalf of Authorised Medical Personnel table.

Ask the AMP to advise the first name and any other names they have for the person:

  • do not key the person's first name when undertaking the initial search as this will not identify individuals who may be known by one name but recorded a little differently on the system, that is Sam/Samantha, Maree/Marie
  • when searching, consider if the person may be known by their middle name

6

Review records identified from the initial search to identify possible match(s) and request additional information if required + Read more ...

If unable to identify the organ donor, ask the AMP if they have any more information for this person, such as:

  • Medicare card number
  • Organ Donor registration number (Donor ID)
  • address (full address and/or postcode and/or state)

The Medicare card number can be used to assist with a search but should not be used in place of a 'detailed search' as many AODR records are not linked to a Medicare record.

Determine if match(s) found:

  • Yes, possible match is found, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Check the Program details tab to check if there is an entitlement end date recorded + Read more ...

Where a possible match has a recent end date and reason of 'deceased' the record could belong to the person being searched for. In this instance the Service Officer can advise the AMP of a possible match with a recently deceased person, go to Step 9.

Is there an end date recorded?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Advise no records match the details provided + Read more ...

If there are no matching records, advise the AMP no records match those details and go to Step 10

Note: where Service Officers think it would be useful, they can advise the AMP to seek additional information, for example other names, previous addresses and call back for another search.

9

Advise possible match(s) found + Read more ...

Advise the AMP that there is a possible match/s for those details and provide the required donation decision details:

  • Donor Status key (Yes or No)
  • Donor Decision Type (intent or consent)
  • the organs and/or tissues for donation (all or list the organs/tissues nominated - not applicable for No decisions/Objections to donate)
  • most recent decision date (advise date)
  • if there is a comment recorded (advise the comment where it is relevant)

Note:

  • where Service Officers think it would be useful, they can advise the AMP to seek additional information, for example other names, previous addresses and call back for another search
  • Service Officers must not provide any processing information that has been recorded as a comment

Note: where only one potential match is found which matches on name, DOB and gender, operators can also advise the AMP of the full name and address recorded for the registration.

An AMP may request donation decision details in writing. Advise the AMP to apply for online access to the AODR using PRODA. See Table 2 for more information on responding to calls about the AODR AMP system.

10

Finalise call + Read more ...

Ask the AMP if they have any further search requests:

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, thank the AMP for calling

Where the family of a donor wishes to obtain a copy of the AODR registration form or documentary evidence confirming their loved one's AODR registration, Services Australia is happy to consider requests submitted in writing by the executor of the deceased person's estate. The request should be addressed and emailed to the Director, Registers and Customer Support. The Resources page contains a link to the AODR email address.

Services Australia does not typically provide personal registration details to third parties without legal authority, such an Executor of a deceased estate. Documentary evidence will be requested to confirm the person submitting the request is the Executor of the estate or demonstrates their legal authority.

Responding to calls about the AODR AMP online system

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Registration for the AMP online system + Read more ...

Callers requesting access to the AMP online system should be advised that a Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account is required to securely access the AMP online system.

Previously AMPs used a PKI certificate to access the AODR AMP online system. This is no longer available. New AMPs must obtain a PRODA account.

Instructions on how to apply for a PRODA account are available on the Services Australia website. See the Resources page for a link to the instructions.

There are three steps to create a new PRODA account:

  • create the account
  • verify the documents, and
  • match existing services

2

Registration approvals + Read more ...

Once the AMP receives their Registration Authority (RA) number as part of their PRODA account, they must notify the National Office AODR Team. National office will add this RA number to the AMP system which enables the AMP to access the online system.

The AMP can then proceed to the system through Health Professional Online Services (HPOS).

3

Online Access Issues calls + Read more ...

If an AMP is experiencing issues with PRODA or HPOS while attempting to access the AMP site, do not transfer to eBusiness.

When an AMP calls in relation to any PRODA or HPOS issues:

  • get all details of the issue they are experiencing
  • request the AMP's contact details
  • email the National Office AODR Team with the AMP's details and outline the PRODA or HPOS issues they are having

How to identify an AMP in the AODR AMP Enquiry System

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Open browser + Read more ...

From the intranet home page Medicare Portal via the Customer Service tab.

2

Source office code + Read more ...

Select KMAO or your existing Source office code from the Source Office drop down menu.

Select AODR AMP Enquiry System drop down as the eBusiness System and select Submit.

3

AMP screen + Read more ...

From the Main Menu of the AMP screen.

Select Identify Authorised Medical Personnel (AMP) and AMP list from database is displayed in alphabetical order.

4

Verify details + Read more ...

Verify AMP details, see Table 1.

Note: if an end date is recorded against the AMP's registration, Service Officers must not provide any information about the ODR.

Once the AMP details are verified, perform search and identify an ODR on behalf of an AMP.

Search and identify an ODR on behalf of an AMP

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Select search + Read more ...

Select CDMS Consumer Search from the Australian Organ Donor Register (AODR) screen in the AODR AMP Enquiry System and the AMP Inquiry Search screen is displayed.

All searches on behalf of AMPs are to be conducted on this screen.

2

Search option + Read more ...

On the detailed search tab, select Australian Organ Donor Register at the program drop down menu:

  • ensure that the Search on behalf of an AMP option is checked
  • select corresponding AMP Name from drop down menu list

Key the following details:

  • Family name
  • Gender
  • Date of birth (or age range)

3

Entitlement + Read more ...

Select Entitlement button.

The system searches the CDMS for existing registration based on the search criteria. The results are displayed as either:

  • matched consumers with an AODR entitlement
  • matched Donor Only entitlements
  • both of the above

See Table 1.

Note: information message No donor records found (00481) is returned if no matching data is found.

  • If more than 50 records matching the search details have been found, the More records found message is returned. Refine the name search details to display a shorter list

4

Search results + Read more ...

From the search results displayed select:

  • Entitlement for the AODR record and the Inquire screen is displayed
  • View AODR details for a Donor only record and the Donor Only entitlement screen is displayed

5

View possible match(s) + Read more ...

Select tabs at the top of the screen to view these registration details:

  • Personal Details
  • Donor Decision Details
  • Program details (to view Entitlement end date details)
  • Donor Card Replacement (not required or disclosed for AMP ODR searches)
  • Comments

Navigate to the Personal button at the bottom of the screen for the registrant's address. Confirm the address by selecting the Address tab.

6

Review Possible matches to AMP + Read more ...

Determine if any of the records identified are a possible match, see the Responding to calls on the AODR AMP telephone enquiry line table.

When completed, return to the Inquiry Search screen by selecting Search Page.

SAS Portal access after-hours or in the event of an emergency

Table 5

Step

Action

1

After hours or in the event of an emergency access + Read more ...

If Service Officers need access to the AODR AMP Enquiry System in an emergency, e.g. if CMDS cannot be accessed, the AODR potential donor search system must be accessed via the SAS Portal and the AMP Emergency list via SharePoint or TAS AODR drive.

The Resources page contains a task card on how to access the AODR contingency search facility once confirmation of the AMP details via the Emergency AMP list has been completed.

Note: to gain access to the SAS Portal, Service Officers must contact their Team Leader to request access via the Program Management/Policy AODR Team.

2

Caller details not found + Read more ...

If the caller details are not on the list:

  • advise the caller that the system is currently down, and
  • the caller's name is not on the manual list and the issue will be escalated

Advise your Team Leader who will escalate to the Program Team to confirm if the caller should be on the list.
