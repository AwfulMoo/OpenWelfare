Recognised activities + Read more ...

To meet the Activity Test, the customer (and their partner) must be participating in one or more recognised activities, or have an exemption.

Customers (or partner) can include a reasonable amount of travel from the child care service to the place of activity (e.g. work/study) and from the place of activity to the child care service. The Resources page has examples of how to include travel in activity hours.

While evidence is not required when reporting activity details, customers may be required to provide it at a later date if subject to a compliance check.

To provide more information to customers about the following recognised activities:

  • Paid work (including paid and unpaid leave), see Item 2
  • Training, see Item 3
  • Approved course of education or study, see Item 4
  • Voluntary work, see Item 5
  • Unpaid work experience or unpaid internship, see Item 6
  • Unpaid work for a family business, see Item 7
  • Actively looking for work, see Item 8
  • Actively setting up a business, see Item 9
  • Other activity, see Item 10

To explain Activity Test results, see Item 11

2

Paid work (including paid and unpaid leave) + Read more ...

This activity includes paid work as a self-employed person.

The number of hours declared can include reasonable travel from the child care service to the place of work and return.

Casual and irregular work:

  • Customers (or partner) who work casual or irregular hours can estimate their hours of activity over a three month (6 CCS fortnights) period. The largest number of estimated hours worked in a fortnight should be used when reporting their activity details. Customers should update their hours of work as appropriate if their largest number of hours on a fortnight in the three month period changes. The Resources page has an example of estimating irregular work
  • Estimating hours does not apply to fly-in fly-out, seasonal, contract and shift workers with a consistent number of fortnightly hours as these are covered by other recognised activities

Paid and unpaid parental leave:

  • The period of leave will be recognised at the same level of activity as declared for paid work (or self-employment)
  • The work hours before taking leave must be at least 8 hours per fortnight
  • There is no limit to the period of unpaid parental leave, however:
    • the period of leave will be within the terms and conditions of their employment contract for individuals who are permanently employed
    • leave is only recognised for individuals who are self-employed if their business continues to operate and they intend resuming work in their business in the foreseeable future
    • leave is only recognised if the individual who works from contract to contract or casually for a regular employer (or an employment agency) intends to resume work after the period of leave
  • Leave will not be recognised as unpaid parental leave if the person:
    • has resigned or been made redundant from their employment
    • was self-employed and their business ceases to operate
    • has completed their employment contract with no understanding or arrangement with an employer they will return to work at a future date, or who have no intention to return to work (either for the same or a different employer) in the foreseeable future
    • has moved from employer to employer with no formal employment arrangement or contract (such that they could not be considered to be taking leave) and have withdrawn from the workforce for the birth of a child

Paid and unpaid annual leave, sick leave, long service leave, carers leave and other paid leave:

  • This refers to leave granted under the terms and conditions of a customer's (or partner's) employment
  • The period of leave will be recognised at the same level of activity as declared for paid work (or self-employment)
  • If the work hours prior to taking leave were less than 8 hours per fortnight, and the customer (or partner) is not combining it with any other activity/ies during the period of leave, then the period of annual, long service or sick leave does not meet the Activity Test. For example, if the customer was working 7.5 hours per fortnight, this would not meet the Activity Test

Unpaid leave (except for unpaid parental leave) can only be used to meet the Activity Test for a continuous period of 6 months

  • Leave taken by a self-employed customer (or partner)
  • Leave for self-employed customers (or partner) will be taken as paid work where the leave would be considered annual leave, long service leave, leave for illness or injury, carer's leave or parental leave if they were an employee
  • The period of leave will be recognised at the same level of activity as declared for their self-employment
  • A medical certificate of commonwealth statutory declaration may be required if subject to a compliance check

3

Training + Read more ...

The customer (or partner) must be enrolled in, and actively working towards completing, a training course for the purpose of improving their work skills, employment prospects or both.

  • Includes customers receiving Austudy or ABSTUDY and most certified courses, including apprenticeships and traineeships, included under the Student Assistance (Education Institutions and Courses) Determination 2009 (No. 2)
  • Includes practicums or vocational placements
  • Hours declared should include self-directed hours of study outside scheduled hours of training
  • Semester and vacation breaks during the course are included, but not vacation breaks at the start or after the course has finished
  • Courses should have a direct impact on the customer's (or partner's) professional development, including, for example:
    • Adult literacy
    • English language (such as, Skills for Education and Employment or the Adult Migrant English Programme)
    • Improving computer skills
    • Guidance on starting a small business
    • Community based, work skill focused training as listed on myskills.gov.au. The Resources page has a link to the website
    • Does not include courses catering primarily to hobbies or personal interests, such as cooking courses, art classes, foreign languages or alternative therapies

4

Approved course of education or study + Read more ...

The customer (or partner) must be enrolled in, and actively working towards completing, a secondary or tertiary course of education or study.

  • Includes customers receiving Youth Allowance (Student), Austudy or ABSTUDY
  • Includes courses of education defined in s541B Social Security Act 1991, including Years 10-12 and Level 1 to 10 (Certificate I to Doctoral Degree) of the Australian Qualifications Framework (AQF)
  • Includes practicums or vocational placements
  • Hours declared should include self-directed hours of study outside scheduled hours of training
  • Semester and vacation breaks during the course are included, but not vacation breaks at the start or after the course has finished

5

Voluntary work + Read more ...

This activity can be used if the voluntary work:

  • could reasonably be expected to improve work skills or employment prospects, or
  • be for a charitable, welfare or community organisation, or
  • be for a school, preschool or centred based service if the work directly support the learning and development of children at that school, preschool or service

If the voluntary work is the only activity being undertaken, only the first 16 hours will count towards the Activity Test.

Note: Prior to the 13 July 2020 some customers would have received more subsidised hours then they were entitled to. This occurred when the customer had 'Other' activities recorded with Voluntary work. Their total voluntary work hours were included when calculating their total hours of subsidised care. There will be no recovery action of the extra hours the customer received.

Charitable, welfare or community organisation includes:

  • A charity registered with the Australian Charities and Not-for profit Commission
  • Other registered voluntary organisation
  • Community organisations with a charitable purpose
  • Welfare or other community organisations such as churches, sporting clubs, schools or an emergency management body (such as the State Emergency Service)
  • Volunteering at a school, preschool or centre based service includes reading to children or supporting children in other learning activities. It does not include activities considered to be parental duties such as being on a school's parents and citizens committee

Note: foster care and home schooling are not considered voluntary work for the purposes of the CCS Activity Test.

The Resources page has an example of volunteering as a recognised activity, and examples of activities which may or may not meet the Activity Test.

6

Unpaid work experience or unpaid internship + Read more ...

This activity recognises unpaid work experience or internship which will build experience in a particular occupation or industry, enabling a customer (or partner) to maintain or improve work skills or employment prospects, or both.

  • There must be no employment relationship and it must not be undertaken as a requirement of a course of education or training
  • The customer (or partner) must not be doing 'productive' work - that is, it must not be work that would otherwise be done by a paid employee. This is to lessen the risk of employers exploiting unpaid work experience participants or interns to avoid paying someone to do the particular job. The main benefit should be a meaningful learning experience, training or skill development
  • There is no time limit on this activity as there are customers (or partners) who genuinely do work experience as part of a Commonwealth or State employment program

7

Unpaid work for a family business + Read more ...

To be working unpaid in a family business which may be a sole proprietor, partnership, corporation or limited company.

To be considered a family business, the business must be owned by a member of their immediate family, that is:

  • their parent
  • the partner of their parent
  • their partner
  • a sibling (a child of the customer's parent)
  • their child
  • a partner of their child

8

Actively looking for work + Read more ...

A customer (or partner) is actively looking for work if they engage in one or more of the following activities during the fortnight:

  • looking for job vacancies
  • preparing resumés and job applications
  • contacting potential employers
  • preparing for, and attending, job interviews

The customer (or partner) does not need to be unemployed to have this as their activity.

If looking for work is the only activity being undertaken, only the first 16 hours will count towards the Activity Test.

If actively looking for work activity is part of JSP, YAL-JSK, PPP/PPS and SpB with compulsory participation requirements customers see Table 2, Step 3 for additional information.

Note: Prior to the 13 July 2020 some customers would have received more subsidised hours then they were entitled to. This occurred when the customer had 'Other' activities recorded with Actively looking for work. Their total Actively looking for work hours were included when calculating their total hours of subsidised care. There will be no recovery action of the extra hours the customer received.

9

Actively setting up a business + Read more ...

This applies to a business that has not yet started to operate and the customer (or partner) must be engaged in one or more of the following activities in relation to the proposed business:

  • obtaining finance, advice and support
  • attending and organising events, including meetings, networks and seminars
  • developing business, marketing or other plans

This activity is limited to a maximum of 26 weeks in any 12 month period. Ensure the activity period does not exceed 26 weeks, when recording 'Setting up a Business'. If the customer has accessed less than 26 weeks for this activity within a 12 month period and uses this activity again, they will only be able to access the balance up to 26 weeks.

10

Other activity + Read more ...

Before deciding whether an 'other' activity is an appropriate activity, check if should be recorded as one of the existing recognised activities available. For example, a person exercising or caring for animals at an animal shelter. If the animal shelter is a recognised charity, the activity may be considered voluntary work, instead of 'Other'.

To be considered a recognised activity, it must maintain or improve work skills or employment prospects or both.

When submitting a request for an activity to be considered, customers are to provide:

  • details of the activity being undertaken (this may include useful details/context to support their request)
  • start date (and end date if known)
  • hours per fortnight
  • a statement addressing how the activity would improve their work skills or employment prospects (or both). Note: the free text information (if submitted online) or the verbal statement received from the customer can be used
  • evidence to support their application (if required). This can include documentation from a third party that shows the individual's participation in the activity and the dates the activity occurred. Acceptable evidence may include:
    • documents relating to the organisers of the activity including official letterheads of organisations
    • rosters / schedules
    • other documentation that demonstrates a link between the activity and the customer or partner doing the activity
    • a commonwealth statutory declaration may be accepted if no other document can be provided or if a third party is not involved

When assessing a request for an activity to be recognised, Service Officers may also determine whether, depending on the nature of the activity, it should only be recognised for a limited period of time, or for a limited number or hours per fortnight.

Note: if unable to determine whether the activity is an appropriate activity to meet the CCS Activity Test, refer the details to the Level 2 Policy Help Desk. If appropriate, the Department of Education, Skills and Employment will be consulted to make the assessment.

Activity Test result + Read more ...

The hours of subsidised care a customer is entitled to is based on their hours of recognised activity. This is referred to as the Activity Test result.

For partnered customers, the subsidised hours will be based on the member of the couple with the lowest Activity Test result, even when an exemption applies.

If both members of a couple have an exemption, the hours of subsidised care are determined by the partner with lowest hours of entitlement.

Note: this does not apply to grandparent principle carers not receiving income support or individuals temporarily outside of Australia. These customers remain eligible for 100 hours regardless of the customer and partner Activity Test result.

If the hours of recognised activity per fortnight are:

  • less than 8 hours and the ATI is more than the lower income threshold, the maximum hours of subsidised care per CCS fortnight is NIL
  • less than 8 hours and the ATI is equal to or below the lower income threshold, the maximum hours of subsidised care per CCS fortnight is 24 hours
  • 8 hours to 16 hours, the maximum hours of subsidised care per CCS fortnight is 36 hours
  • more than 16 hours to 48 hours, the maximum hours of subsidised care per CCS fortnight is 72 hours
  • more than 48 hours, the maximum hours of subsidised care per CCS fortnight is 100 hours

An automatic Activity Test result may apply based on an individual's or family's circumstances. See Table 2.

Low income Activity Test result + Read more ...

3

JSP, YAL-JSK, PPP/PPS and SpB with compulsory participation requirements + Read more ...

If a customer or their partner is receiving one of these payments and has compulsory participation requirements, they will have an automatic Activity Test result of 36 hours.

Customers should still report the activities they are engaged in. If the activity hours reported:

  • are less than the automatic Activity Test result, the higher automatic Activity Test of 36 hours will apply
  • exceed the automatic Activity Test result, they may be able to access more hours of subsidised care (noting the lower Activity Test result is used where a customer is partnered)
  • If actively looking for work is the only activity recorded and their activity hours exceed 16, customers with compulsory participation requirements will receive a higher activity test result than the automatic 36 hours

If a customer or their partner is exempt from compulsory participation requirements due to exceptional circumstances, they will have an automatic Activity Test result of 100 hours. This excludes large family, home schooling, or distance educating children exemptions.

4

Carer Allowance (CA) + Read more ...

Care for the Activity Test is care that would prevent the person from engaging in other recognised activities due to their caring responsibilities. The care may be active, supervisory or monitoring. Periods of caring should not overlap with other activities the customer advises they are engaged in, such as study or work. See the Resources page for examples.

Customers must be receiving Carer Allowance (CA) to have the automatic Activity Test result of 72 hours.

Customers in receipt of CA can also report a combination of caring and other activities which will increase their hours of entitlement to 100 hours per fortnight. The total hours must exceed 48 hours per fortnight to be entitled to 100 hours per fortnight.

The Resources page has an example of combining caring hours with additional activities.

2

Pre-school exemption + Read more ...

Families will have an Activity Test exemption giving them access to 36 hours of subsidised care if they:

  • are entitled to less than 36 hours of subsidised child care per fortnight, and
  • have a child attending an early educational program at a centre-based day care service (e.g. preschool or kindergarten) in the year that is 2 years before Grade 1 of school

For example, a child due to start primary school in February 2020 is considered a preschool aged child for 2019. The child will attend Grade 1 in 2021. The Resources page details the primary school levels for child care fee assistance.

To receive this exemption:

  • the customer must advise the date their child expects to start primary school (how to record child education details), and
  • the session report lodged by the child care service must confirm the child's attendance at an approved program

If the customer advises their child's primary school start date and may be eligible for this exemption, to view details:

  • Select Child Care
  • Under Entitlement, the hours display in the Pre-School Additional Hours column. The number of hours depends on the customer's current Activity Test result. For example if the result is 0 hours, 36 displays. If the result is 24 hours, 12 displays

The additional pre-school hours will take effect in the calendar year before the date the child is due to start primary school. It starts on the first Monday of a CCS fortnight. It ends on the last CCS Sunday of the calendar year the exemption applies to.

For example, if the child is due to start primary school on 4 February 2020, the pre-school exemption will take effect from 14 January 2019 and end on 12 January 2020.

  • The exemption only applies for the child attending the preschool or kindergarten program, not other children in the customer's care
  • Only pre-school exemptions from 2020 apply to the calendar year before the date the child starts primary school. Pre-school exemptions before 2020 apply to the 12 months and one day period before the school start date

If pre-school additional hours do not display correctly on the Child Care Summary for children attending an early education program, code a 'Has not started studies' line from the child's date of birth.

Procedure ends here.

3

Customer or partner outside Australia + Read more ...

If temporary travel outside Australia is recorded for the customer (via a data link with the Department of Home Affairs), the customer will have an Activity Test result of 100 hours for up to 6 weeks from the date of departure.

If the 6 week period is exceeded, it will affect eligibility and entitlement unless an extension is assessed. Requests for portability extensions are assessed on a case by case basis and should be referred to the International Services Centre (CIS) for assessment. The Resources page contains a link to the CIS.

If a customer's partner travels or lives outside Australia, they will have an Activity Test result of 100 hours that is not time limited. This exemption is automatically applied using the Immigration link with Home Affairs. If the Immigration link is not successful:

  • Select the partner from menu
  • Go to the Immigration Movements (RSIM) screen
  • If there is no Immigration link for the partner, go to the partner's record and update the Travel Outside Australia Details (TOAD) screen with relevant information
  • Finalise the activity resulting in the exemption being applied
  • If no Home Affairs link exists and information was manually recorded, customers must advise the agency when their partner returns to Australia to avoid a potential overpayment

Procedure ends here.

4

Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) exemptions + Read more ...

Customers will automatically have access to 100 hours of subsidised child care per fortnight if granted:

  • ACCS (Child Wellbeing). Note: ACCS (Child Wellbeing) is granted at a child level. This means each child would need to have ACCS (Child Wellbeing) approved to receive 100 hours of subsidised care)
  • ACCS (Grandparent)
  • ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship)

Is the customer receiving an eligible ACCS payment?

  • Yes, the family will have access to 100 hours of subsidised child care per fortnight. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Exemptions for grandparent principle carers not receiving income support + Read more ...

Customers can be exempt from the Activity Test if they (or their partner) are the grandparent or great-grandparent with principal care of a grandchild and they do not receive income support.

To be eligible customers may be required to provide evidence:

  • of their grandparent relationship to the child (if not already established for Family Tax Benefit purposes)
  • they provide 65% or more care for the child
  • they have substantial autonomy for day-to-day decisions about the child's care, welfare and development

See Processing ACCS (Grandparent) for acceptable evidence and how to assess the evidence.

Is the customer or their partner a grandparent principal carer who is not receiving income support?

  • Yes, record details as required. See Table 3, Step 6 in Coding Activity Test details for Child Care Subsidy (CCS). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Exemption for a person living with a disability or impairment + Read more ...

A disabled person can be exempt from the Activity Test if, as a result of their disability or impairment, they:

  • are unable to significantly engage in a recognised activity, or
  • would be unable to adequately care for their child/ren if the child/ren did not attend child care

These exemptions are self-assessed, however customers may be required to provide evidence if requested, such as a random sample survey or compliance check.

Is the customer requesting an exemption from the Activity Test as they are disabled or have an impairment?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Explain eligibility for exemption due to disability or impairment + Read more ...

Advise the customer to be eligible for this exemption they must be either:

  • receiving a DVA invalidity service pension
  • participating in an independent living program provided by CRS Australia or other body determined by the Minister
  • diagnosed by a medical practitioner or clinical psychologist as a person who is impaired to a degree that significantly incapacitates them, or
  • included in a class of persons determined by the Minister, by legislative instrument, to be a disabled person for the purposes of this exemption

If Service Officers have concerns about the customer's meeting the above criteria, they should request the customer lodge evidence to support their request for an exemption.

Does the customer meet the requirements for an exemption due to a disability or impairment?

  • Yes, update the customers disability or impairment information. See Table 3, Step 3 in Coding Activity Test details for Child Care Subsidy (CCS). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Exemption for a person who provides constant care for an adult or child with a disability + Read more ...

An exemption is automatically granted if the person is in receipt of Carer Payment (CP) or has had CP rejected or cancelled due to the income or asset test in the last 5 years.

This exemption also applies to customers who do not qualify for CP because they, or the person they care for, do not meet the income or asset test. These customers have not had CP rejected or cancelled in the last 5 years but would otherwise be eligible for CP because they have met the constant care requirement.

For example, a customer getting an income support payment (other than CP) would not be eligible for this exemption. These customers would be expected to meet the income and asset test if assessed as eligible for CP.

Customers are considered to be providing constant care if they personally provide care on a daily basis for a significant period during each day. The care may be active, supervisory or monitoring. They need to be providing at least the equivalent of a normal working day in personal care and be unable to engage in other recognised activities due to the full-time caring responsibility.

If unable to find details of CP rejection due to the income or assets test, and if concerned about whether the customer meets the criteria for this exemption, ask the customer to lodge evidence to support their request for an exemption.

Is the customer unable to engage in a recognised activity because they are providing constant care for an adult or child with a disability and not eligible for CP due to not meeting the income or asset test?

  • Yes, add caring details via the Activity Test Details (ATD) screen in Process Direct via Super Key field. See Table 3, Step 4 in Coding Activity Test details for Child Care Subsidy (CCS). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Exemptions for imprisonment or psychiatric confinement + Read more ...

This automatic exemption applies when the person is being detained while under sentence for a conviction and not on release or parole or the person is in custody pending trial or sentencing.

There are no evidence requirements for confirmation of imprisonment or psychiatric confinement. Details can be updated by the customer or partner online or verbally.

Is the customer/partner reporting imprisonment or incarceration for either themselves or their partner?

  • Yes, and the exemption has:
    • automatically applied. The customer will have an Activity Test result of 100 hours. Procedure ends here
    • not automatically applied, add exemption for imprisonment or psychiatric confinement. See Table 3, Step 5 in Coding Activity Test details for Child Care Subsidy (CCS). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 10

12

Exemptions from the Activity test due to Coronavirus (COVID-19) + Read more ...

Child Care Subsidy (CCS) customers did not need to pay child care fees from 6 April 2020 until 12 July 2020.

The Government announced no session fees will be charged to families between 6 April 2020 and 12 July 2020. Updates made to Activity Test Details (ATD) screen will have no effect on a customer’s Child Care Subsidy entitlement. This was because customers could access child care free of the activity test or means test during the Business Continuity Payment period.

Did customers still need to update work test/activity hours?

  • Customers did not need to update activity hours if they’re on leave or need to self-isolate
  • They did not need to update if their employer closed due to a public health advice or as a precautionary measure, and would return to that job when their employer reopens
  • If a customer advised additional activities, update the activity test accordingly
  • Remind customers to check their activity hours before CCS started again on 12 July 2020

If CCS customers contact regarding a change to activity hours for any reason not related to Coronavirus (COVID-19), update the activity test details as per normal procedures.

Where activity has reduced or paused due to Coronavirus (COVID-19):

  • If CCS customers contact to advise their activity hours have reduced, there was no need for them to change their hours right then
  • If a CCS customer has irregular hours, they would normally estimate hours across 3 months. If activity hours have reduced, there is no need to change their activity hours

Where work ceased and they advised they do not have a job to return to:

Update the ATD screen to reflect the activities the customer may now be undertaking

Eased Activity test requirements from 13 July 2020 until 4 April 2021

From 13 July 2020 until 4 April 2021 the CCS activity test requirements have been eased. This applies for families whose activity test hours have reduced due to COVID-19. Impacted families can get up to 100 hours per fortnight of subsidised care, where they:

  • had an activity test result of more than 0 before COVID-19 (around 1 March 2020 or before)
  • can no longer engage in the same number of hours of recognised activities, and
  • are currently doing more than 8 hours of recognised activity

Exceptional circumstances + Read more ...

    • the customer is seeking an increase in their assessed subsidised hours or wants to access more than 100 hours of subsidised care per fortnight
  • If the customer or their partner does not have any exceptional circumstances which would enable them to engage in recognised activities, they are not eligible for an exemption from the Activity Test.
