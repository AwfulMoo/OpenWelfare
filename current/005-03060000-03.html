Initial assessment for raising AoS debts

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Initial Interview + Read more ...

An AoS debt will only be raised when:

  • there is a current AoS arrangement in force
  • the assuree is not receiving sufficient support from their assurer, and
  • the assuree is in receipt of an AoS recoverable payment

During the new claim process for an AoS recoverable payment, the Service Officer must code the AoS Checklist (AOCL). This will make sure the AoS debt begins accruing on the Debt List (OPDL) screen of the assurer's record.

Is the accrued AoS debt to be recovered from the assurer?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

The assuree is liable for the debt, not the assurer + Read more ...

If a debt is accrued by the assuree during the AoS period, rising from a contravention of the Act, then the debt is recoverable from the assuree only. For example, when an assuree fails to accurately declare their earnings.

  • Clearly DOC both the assuree and assurer record, outlining the calculated amount
  • Raise the debt on the assuree's customer record under the appropriate payment and ensure the debt activity is forwarded to the appropriate debt recovery team

Consider this when calculating the AoS debt.

Procedure ends here.

3

Check for a DOC on the assurer's record from a social worker + Read more ...

As assurees and assurers are all required to complete quarterly reviews, it is important to determine whether contacting the assurer will place the assuree at risk. Check whether there is a social worker DOC on the record before discussing anything with the assurer, and whether there is any debt advices suppressed due to family and domestic violence reasons on the Inhibiting Letters (AOIL) screen.

For more on what is disclosed during the AoS interview, see Assuree makes a claim for an AoS recoverable payment.

Is there a recommendation from a social worker for contact to be delayed, or coding on the AOIL screen?

  • Yes, decide if contacting the assurer will put the assuree at risk. If unsure, lodge a request with the National AoS team using the Helpdesk webform. The Resources page contains a link to the form. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check that letters have been issued to the assuree and the assurer + Read more ...

When they are first granted an AoS recoverable payment, an automatic letter issues to the assure telling them Services Australia will begin raising an AoS debt for their assurer to repay.

A similar automatic letter issues to the assurer/s, telling them an AoS debt has started accruing for which they are liable to repay. The only time this letter would be delayed is if a social worker advises in a recommendation that this will put the assuree at risk. Additionally, each quarter the assurer/s and assuree are issued a review form for completion. Once completed the assurer's AoS debt is updated and a reminder letter is issued advising the assurer the AoS debt is accruing.

Check the History Summary (HS) screen for all letters issued.

Has the assurer been advised there is an AoS debt accruing?

  • Yes, the case needs to be referred to the National AoS team for further action. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

The assurer needs to be advised about the AoS debt accruing + Read more ...

During new claim processing of the assuree's claim, the assurer should have been contacted and advised of their liability. See Assuree makes a claim for an AoS recoverable payment for more information.

If the assurer was not contacted prior to the assuree's new claim being processed, and a MOD AR is not coded on the assuree's record, then a DOC should be on the record outlining why a decision was made to grant the payment.

If there is no evidence of a MOD AR being issued to the assurer, or of reasonable attempts made to contact the assurer prior to granting the claim, then refer the case to the relevant processing area for follow up. For example, if the assuree was granted Age Pension without any attempts being made to contact the assurer, refer the case back to the Age Pension processing area so they can follow up.

It is the responsibility of the relevant processing area to contact the assurer and advise them of the claim grant, and the subsequent accruing AoS debt that they are liable to repay. If the assuree was granted a jobseeker payment without the assurer being notified, refer the case to the relevant Service Officer who granted the claim.

Use the Locator to find the appropriate processing site. The Resources page contains a link.

Only contact the National AoS team if experiencing difficulties with contacting the assurer.

Has the assurer been advised about the AoS debt that has been accruing?

  • Yes, this case needs to be referred to the National AoS team for further action. Send an ACTDOC activity to region code 'AOS' for follow up. Procedure ends here.
  • No:
    • ensure that 2 genuine attempts to contact the assurer have been made and issue a MOD AR before escalating the matter to the National AoS team
    • clearly DOC the record with the actions taken
    • go to Step 6

6

Contact details when referring AoS cases to the National AoS team + Read more ...

Service Officers must now refer this case to the National AoS team so the AoS debt can be raised. Check which of the following webforms is best suited to the circumstance:

  • To confirm if there is an existing AoS case, lodge the Is there an AoS? webform
  • If there is an active AoS case but no coding on the Assuree Details (AOAE) screen, lodge the Request Entry of AoS Start Date webform
  • Policy enquiries should be lodged via the Assurance of Support Policy Helpdesk webform
  • To refer cases directly to the National AoS team:
    • search for 'AOS' in the Locator to obtain details about the team
    • alternatively, send an ACTDOC directly to the AOS region code

The Resources page contains links to the webforms.

AoS Processing staff, see Step 1 in Table 2.

Completing reviews and raising AoS debts

For Assurance of Support (AoS) Processing team only.

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Auto-generated system activities + Read more ...

Once an assuree is granted a recoverable income support payment, and if they still have more than 4 months remaining on their AoS period, an AOS/RR1 quarterly review activity is set up on an AoS case record Future Activity List (FAL) screen to mature in 3 months.

This activity counts the time an assuree has been in receipt of a payment. It will have one of the following keywords and activity notes included:

  • AOSQTR1: AoS Review 1st Quarter
  • AOSQTR2: AoS Review 2nd Quarter
  • AOSQTR3: AoS Review 3rd Quarter
  • AOSQTR4: AoS Review 4th Quarter

The cycle will continue again from AOSQTR1 if the AoS arrangement has not expired by the time AOSQTR4 has matured.

For every RR1 activity that is triggered on the AoS case record, a corresponding activity matures on the assuree's record:

  • during the first, second and third reviews, an AOS/RR2 activity displays on the assuree's record
  • during the fourth review when the AOSQTR4 RR1 activity matures, this will trigger an AOS/RR4 activity instead on the assuree's record

Note: only staff from the AoS Processing team and the Debt Recovery team should complete any AoS debt processing activities.

Is there an AOS/RR1 activity on the AoS case record?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, it is not yet time to raise a debt. Finalise any AoS review activity on the record which will move the case forward. See AoS reviews for more information. Procedure ends here.

2

Check the case + Read more ...

Check the details of the case to ensure that:

  • The AoS is still currently in force
  • That the dates the assuree received a recoverable payment is verified
  • All assurer/s are listed
  • This is not a family and domestic violence case (check the AOIL screen), and
  • Whether there is any debt raised against the assuree themselves. If so, take this off the calculation for the AoS debt that belongs to the assurer

If there is an AOS/RR1 activity on the AoS case record FAL screen, check the assuree record for the corresponding activity. Use the Case Summary (AOCS) screen to find the assurer/s and assuree/s records.

Is there an AOS/RR2 activity on the assuree record?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 6

3

Actioning an AOS/RR2 activity to calculate the debt + Read more ...

The AOS/RR2 activity enables staff to check the AoS debt calculation, update the corresponding debt shell on the Debt List (OPDL) screen of the assurer/s record and ensure that the status of the AoS debt is 'Protected'.

  • Select the AOS/RR2 activity from the assuree record which will display the Review Details (AORD) screen. This screen summarises the payment amounts periods of payment over the last few months
  • Once the RR2 activity is selected, the debt calculation must be checked using the Payment Summary Tool. The tool can be accessed via the Calculators and tools link on the intranet under 'C'. The Resources page also contains a link
  • Run the Tool in the assuree record and check the Start and End dates of the payment. Write down the calculation on a spreadsheet so it can be double checked, and in case an ARO needs to verify the calculation
  • Check that the total amount (for each payment) is correct, and that the dates align. If part of the debt is an assuree debt, make sure this is deducted from the AoS debt and raise it on the assuree's record

Did the system automatically calculate the right amount?

  • Yes, put a 'Y' next to the Finished field to complete the activity. Go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Manual calculation + Read more ...

If the system did not calculate the right amount, manually adjust the calculation.

  • On the Manual Adjustment (AOMA) screen, put a 'C' in the field at the left of the applicable line, and add the new amount under the Man Calc field
  • If there is more than one adjustment, amend each line accordingly

Note: the same thing can be done if the dates are incorrect and need to be amended.

Have all details been corrected?

  • Yes, complete the activity:
    • if completing an RR4 activity, go to Step 9
    • otherwise, go to Step 5
  • No, review the calculation and seek specialist advice if necessary

5

Completion of the AOS/RR2 activity + Read more ...

Once the AOS/RR2 activity has been completed, the system will automatically generate a letter to the assurer/s the following day.

From Quarter 1 to 3, the assurer/s will receive a reminder letter advising them that an AoS debt is accruing. However, once the fourth quarter has passed an AOS/RR4 activity will trigger on the assurer's Activity List (AL) screen requiring further action. This activity will also display when the AoS case has expired, and will allow staff to change the status of the AoS debt to 'Determined'.

Place a DOC on the record outlining the action that has been completed.

Procedure ends here.

6

Determine required processing action + Read more ...

If there is no AOS/RR2 activity, there can only be 2 types of activity on the assuree records:

  • AOS/RR4 activity which needs to be completed every fourth quarter of active AoS cases, or
  • AOS/EXE activity which is triggered when an AoS case is due to expire

Is there an AOS/RR4 activity on the AL screen of the assuree record?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Completion of the AOS/RR4 activity + Read more ...

If there is no RR2 activity on the assuree record, there should be an RR4 debt activity. The RR4 activity triggers every fourth quarter on the assuree record. It allows staff to check the AoS debt calculation (manually adjusting if required) before changing the status of the AoS debt from 'Protected' to 'Undetermined'.

Once the activity is selected, the Manual Adjustment (AOMA) screen is displayed. As with RR2 activities, the Payment Summary Tool must be run and the debt calculation checked. This assists in determining whether the calculation requires adjustment through the AOMA screen.

Completion of this activity will create an undetermined AoS debt shell on the primary assurer record.

Note: any 'Protected' AoS debts for the assuree which are already accrued on the assurer's record will be included in the undetermined debt amount. Some older cases will have multiple protected AoS debts sitting on the assurer's record.

Is a manual adjustment required?

  • Yes, complete a manual calculation as per Step 4
  • No, finalise the activity:
    • if the case has expired, go to Step 8
    • if the case has not expired, go to Step 9

8

Actioning the AOS/EXE activity + Read more ...

The 'EXE' debt activity triggers once the AoS case is due to expire. This activity allows staff to check the AoS debt calculation to ensure it is correct, before it changes the status of the debt from 'Protected' to 'Undetermined'.

Note: for cases with multiple assurers, it is the responsibility of the assessing officer to review the support each assurer has provided, and make the correct assessment.

  • Select the 'EXE' activity, which opens the Review Details (AORD) screen. Check that the details are correct
  • As with the RR2 and RR4 activities, the Payment Summary Tool must be run to check the final calculated debt amount

Is the final calculated AoS debt amount correct?

  • Yes, finalise the activity. Go to Step 9
  • No, complete a manual calculation as per Step 4

9

Undetermined debt activity + Read more ...

Once the AOS/EXE or the AOS/RR4 activity has been completed on the assuree record, the system will automatically trigger an undetermined debt activity on the assurer/s record.

From Quarter 1 to 3 the assurer/s will receive a reminder letter advising them that an AoS debt is accruing. However, once the fourth quarter has passed, an AOS/RR4 activity will trigger on the assurer's Activity List (AL) screen requiring further action. This activity will also display when the AoS case has expired, and will allow staff to change the status of the AoS debt to 'Determined'.

  • Place a DOC on the record outlining the action that has been completed
  • Using AOCS screen go to the assurer/s record
  • On the AL screen, complete the undetermined debt shell activity. Once this activity has been completed, this will issue an automatic Debt Statement to the assurer outlining the total AoS debt amount accumulated over the previous 12 months
  • If there are multiple co-assurers, check each record to ensure that the activities have been finalised and the debt is showing as 'multi-liability'
  • If a bank guarantee exists, a letter to the Commonwealth Bank (CBA) will also be generated. This will only occur when funds remain in the bank guarantee
  • If no bank guarantee exists, or it has been depleted, the assurer will be issued a statement outlining how much they must repay and when. A contact number for the debt recovery team will also be included in the Debt Statement should the assurer wish to set up alternative payment arrangements
  • The bank guarantee will continue to be held by the CBA until the end of the AoS period, or until it is depleted, whichever occurs first. Ensure that there are DOCs on both the assuree and assurer/s records
  • Check that there is another AOS/RR1 activity on the AoS case record Future Activity List (FAL) screen scheduled to mature in three months

Procedure ends here.

Process for reviewing AoS debts

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Ongoing reviews and statements + Read more ...

This cycle of automatic reviews and statements continues until the end of the AoS period.

2

AoS period expires + Read more ...

For Assurance of Support (AoS) Processing team only.

The National AoS Processing team is responsible for actioning any quarterly review activities (AOS/RR1, AOS/RR2, AOS/RR3 and AOS/RR2) or the final debt activity (AOS/EXE) on all AoS records.

  • AOS/RR1 activity: Quarterly review which occurs on the AoS case record
  • AOS/RR2 activity: Quarterly activity which occurs on the assuree records
  • AOS/RR3 activity: Quarterly activity which occurs on the assurer/s records and issues the letters
  • AOS/RR4 activity: Annual activity which occurs on the assuree records
  • AOS/EXE activity: AoS expiry review activity which occurs on the assuree records

These reviews:

  • allow staff to check the calculated AoS debt amount manually, and update if necessary
  • change the debt from 'Undetermined' to 'Determined'
  • issue a Statement of Account (also known as a Debt Statement) to the assurer/s with the calculated AoS debt amount accrued over the previous 12 months
  • where a bank guarantee exists, issue a letter to the CBA outlining the amount of funds that must be transferred to Services Australia to repay the final debt, and that any remaining funds can now be released

Note: staff must always check that the calculated debt amounts listed are correct before finalising either activity. This includes reviewing whether the assuree accrued a debt, and if this affects the AoS debt total.

3

Waiving an AoS debt + Read more ...

For the Assurance of Support (AoS) Processing team and Debt Recovery team only.

An AoS debt cannot be waived on the following grounds:

  • That the assurer did not understand their responsibilities in relation to an assuree receiving an AoS recoverable payment
  • Due to administrative error as s1237A of the Social Security Act 1991 only applies where the payment is received by the debtor (assurer). An assurer does not receive the payment so s1237A does not apply. For instance, where an assuree had received an incorrect rate of recoverable payment, due to an administrative error. Although the error was administrative, the payment was made to the assuree rather than the debtor. As such, the debt cannot be waived due to administrative error as the assuree is not the debtor in the AoS case

However, consider waiver of an AoS debt due to special circumstances. The special circumstances may apply to the assurer and/or the assuree

Note: if the debt is finalised and determined, as the AoS has expired, an AoS debt is waived in the same way as other debts. However:

  • If the debt is still protected (and the case is still current), then the AoS Processing team can only reduce the debt amount by the waived amount before the recovery occurs
  • If it is determined an amount is to be waived and there has already been recovery action, the amount is to be returned to the assurer. This means that if the bank guarantee has been used to recover all or part of the debt, that amount will be returned

4

Distinction between an AoS debt and a debt arising from a contravention of the Act + Read more ...

For example, the assuree claims fraudulently, the assuree incorrectly declares earnings.

  • Where a recoverable payment is made to an assuree in accordance with social security law during the AoS period, an AoS debt is raised by the AoS Processing team under section 1227 of the Social Security Act 1991. Liability for repayment of an AoS debt rests with the assurer/s
  • If the assuree is paid an amount to which they are not entitled and it is determined this amount is a legally recoverable debt, the debt is raised against the assuree under the relevant legislation (for example, S1223 Social Security Act 1991). The assuree, not the assurer, is liable to repay these amounts. Normal debt raising procedures are to be followed, including consideration of non-recovery (for instance, waiver)

5

Request for an explanation or application for a formal review of an AoS debt decision + Read more ...

An explanation of an AoS debt raised from 15 January 2007 should be provided by the National AoS Team.

Check the date the debt was raised via the General Information (OPGI) screen. This is to decide who should provide an explanation, the National AoS Team or the Decision Maker (DM).

For debts raised before 15 January 2007, the DM is the staff member who granted the recoverable payment for the assuree. This is because this staff member held the initial responsibility for:

  • establishing that the assurer/s was/were unwilling and/or unable to provide support to the assuree, and
  • ensuring that the assurer/s understood the consequences of the assuree being paid a recoverable payment, that is, that the assurer/s will accrue a debt equal to the amount of the recoverable payments made to an assuree during the AoS period

If a customer applies for a formal review of an AoS debt decision, a review will be undertaken by an Authorised Review Officer (ARO).
