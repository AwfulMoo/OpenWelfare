3

Assessment of leave payments for a temporary absence from employment (where the employer/employee relationship still exists) + Read more ...

Temporary absence from employment can be indicated by the fact no separation certificate has been issued, leave continues to be paid periodically, or the employee intends to return to same employer immediately afterwards.

Leave payments (including any leave loading representative of the leave period) received for a temporary absence from employment are employment income.

The customer must advise the amount of income paid for time off in each entitlement period as well as for work done. There is no need to separate these amounts. Record both leave and work on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) using 1 entry per employer, per entitlement period.

If required, code the number of hours ‘worked’ equivalent to the number of hours for which leave was paid, for the relevant entitlement period. Hours to be coded as ‘HOP’ with DOV = the EPED of the relevant period.

Income Bank and Working Credits allow certain income support recipients of workforce age to offset income from employment against credits accrued during periods of low or no income.

The Work Bonus is a discount applicable to the assessment of eligible income earned by Age Pension age pensioners. Work Bonus does not apply to Parenting Payment Single customers who are Age Pension age.

If the customer’s assets change as a result of receiving a leave or termination payment, assets should be updated. See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services.

4

Assessment of leave payments where the employee does not take a leave of absence but is paid out some accrued leave entitlements + Read more ...

Leave payments where the employee does not go on the leave and is still in employment are remunerative lump sums.

No Employment Separation Certificate (SU1) is issued, and any leave paid occurs at the same time as normal wages can indicate this.

In this case, record the employee’s actual wages on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) as employment income as usual. The paid out accrued leave entitlements are assessed as a remunerative lump sum for up to 52 weeks from the date the leave payments are paid.

Record the amount of the leave payment on the EAPP screen as a new employer (for example, Employer name - paid out leave). Record the Type of Employment income as EAR with the event date being the date the leave payment was paid and the frequency as LOP. Code the start and end date for the same period of time for which the income was paid, up to 52 weeks.

Working Credit allows certain income support recipients of workforce age to offset income from employment against credits accrued during periods of low or no income.

The Work Bonus is a discount applicable to the assessment of eligible income earned by Age Pension age pensioners. Work Bonus does not apply to Parenting Payment Single (PPS) customers who are Age Pension age.

If the customer’s assets change as a result of receiving a leave or termination payment, assets should be updated. See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services.

5

Assessment of leave payments received on termination of employment + Read more ...

Termination of employment can be indicated by:

  • an Employment Separation Certificate (SU1) being issued, or
  • leave that should be paid periodically is paid in a lump sum

This is only assessable income for customers or partners claiming or receiving:

  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
  • Partner Allowance (PA)
  • Widow Allowance (WA)
  • Parenting Payment Single (PPS) and Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP)
  • Youth Allowance (YA)
  • Austudy
  • Disability Support Pension (DSP) (except customers who are permanently blind)
  • Interim Farm Household Allowance
  • Farm Household Allowance (FHA)

If the customer or their partner is getting one of these payments, record this leave as an Income Maintenance Period (IMP). Note: only redundancy payments received on or after 20 September 2006 are to be included in the IMP.

Do not record leave payments paid in a lump sum on termination of employment as income for customers not subject to an Income Maintenance Period (IMP). However, assess the asset value and any deemed income generated by investing the payment.

Not receiving an IMP affected payment:

  • Single customer, see Item 2 in Table 2
  • Members of a couple, neither receiving an IMP affected payment, see Item 4 in Table 2
  • Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) only customers, see Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) income test

Note: different coding may apply if the customer or partner is getting ABSTUDY.

For ABSTUDY customers who receive lump sum termination payments due to a cessation of employment on or after the date of grant. These amounts are coded on Other Income (OIN) as follows:

  • Type: OTH
  • Description: Employers name
  • Reference: Employer reference (if applicable)
  • Start Date/Event date: Date the lump sum termination payment was paid
  • Amount: Amount of the payment. This does need to be broken down into individual components
  • Freq: field coded as ANN

The system will generate a review to zero the income off 12 months after the event date.

See the Resources page for examples of coding requirements for ABSTUDY customers with a partner on an IMP affected payment.

If the customer’s assets change as a result of receiving a leave or termination payment, assets should be updated. See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services.

6

Stand down payments for non-teaching support staff employed by the NSW Department of Education + Read more ...

In New South Wales (NSW), non-teaching support staff (teacher’s aides, library assistants or other non-teaching staff), are employees of the NSW Department of Education. As they are unable to work during term breaks, they receive a stand down payment, based on them meeting certain criteria.

Stand down payments have never been considered leave payment for Social Security purposes:

  • Before 20 April 2010, stand down payments were assessed as a remunerative lump sum and held for a 52-week period from the date the customer received it
  • From 20 April 2010, these payments are assessed as ordinary employment income earned in the customers entitlement period

If the customer’s assets change as a result of receiving a leave or termination payment, assets should be updated. See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services.

Coding of termination and leave payments in Process Direct

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Single customer on IMP affected payment + Read more ...

If the customer is single and is getting a payment subject to the Income Maintenance Period (IMP):

  • Employment is engaged on a continuing basis (temporary leave)
    • Record leave payment as EAR - Earnings from Employment on Earnings (EAPP)
    • If required, code the number of hours ‘worked’ equivalent to the number of hours for which leave was paid, for the relevant entitlement period. Hours to be coded as ‘HOP’ with DOV = the EPED of the relevant period
  • Employment has ceased (termination)
    • Record termination payment on Employment Separation (IMP, IMPS)
  • Accrued employment income paid out (lump sum) - no actual absence from employment taken
    • Record leave payment as EAR - Earnings from Employment on Earnings (EAPP) for up to 12 months from the date the leave payments are paid using LOP frequency
    • Code the start and end date for the same period of time for which the income was paid, up to 52 weeks

If the customer’s assets change as a result of receiving a leave or termination payment, assets should be updated. See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services.
