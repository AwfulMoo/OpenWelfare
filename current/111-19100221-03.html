Step

Action

Staff research + Read more ...

To dispute a QOL or QMA result, the Service Officer must:

  • Research the business rules to support the dispute, for example, in Operational Blueprint or a task card that was current at the time of processing
  • Contact Technical Peer Support (TPS) to determine if lodging a formal dispute is appropriate. Note: disputes about Employment Services, JobSeeker Payment or Emergency work do not need a TPS contact, go to Step 2

Has TPS contact been made?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, contact TPS. Procedure ends here

2

Enter escalation form + Read more ...

Service Officers complete the Quality Checking Dispute Escalation Form (dispute form) and include the research supporting the dispute. Note: do not include any customer identifying data, such as Customer Reference Number (CRN) or names.

  • Open the Quality Checking Dispute Escalation form
  • Select + new item and complete all fields. Mandatory fields are marked with an asterisk (*). QOL AMR or QMA Work Item ID and the date activity was submitted must be entered exactly as it appears in QOL or Process Direct
  • Select Submit for review

A notification email will be sent when the dispute is finalised.

Procedure for service officers end here.

The Resources page has a link and instructions on how to access the Quality Checking Dispute Escalation form.

3

SSO review and decision + Read more ...

The Service Support Officer (SSO) finds the Quality Checking Dispute Escalation Form in SharePoint and makes an assessment.

Open the Quality Checking Dispute Escalation form:

  • In the header row, use the filters to find disputes for the Smart Centre or work from the National Branch
  • Select the Edit icon to open the dispute form
  • Review the information entered by the Service Officer

Is the dispute out of scope for the SSO?

  • Yes, transfer the dispute by selecting the National Pool button. This will revert the escalation to New and place it into the National Pool for re-allocation. Procedure ends here
  • No, add SSO log on ID, comments supporting the decision and action needed
  • In the SSO determination field, select either:
    • QC dispute decision overturned (agree with dispute)
    • QC dispute decision upheld (agree with the original check result)
    • QC dispute decision upheld (upheld for a different reason)
  • Add the Date of decision
  • The Status will update automatically based on the SSO determination
  • Once complete, select Submit

If the SSO determination is:

  • QC dispute decision overturned:
    • an automatic email is sent to the original checker with right of reply information
    • SSO will need to set a reminder for 7 days to finalise the review
  • QC dispute decision upheld or QC dispute decision upheld (upheld for a different reason):
    • an automatic email is sent to the Service Officer explaining the outcome

Note: if the SSO identifies errors, they must update the customer record according to the SSO's decision, as soon as possible. Do not wait for the outcome of the right of reply process. Go to Service Support Officer (SSO) role in Technical Peer Support (TPS) in Smart Centres to update the customer record (Customer First and Process Direct) and complete QOL (if needed).

Action

Staff research and submit dispute + Read more ...

To dispute a QOL or QMA result, the Service Officer must research the business rules to support the dispute, for example the Operational Blueprint file or task card that is current at the time of processing.

Quality Team review + Read more ...

The Quality Team reviews the request. If information is missing, contact the Service Officer. Otherwise, enter the dispute information into the Quality Checking Dispute Escalation Form (dispute form).

  • Open the Quality Checking Dispute Escalation Form
  • Select + new item and complete all fields. Mandatory fields are marked with an asterisk (*)

  • Enter Reviewer log on ID: and add comments to support the reviewer recommendation
  • Select a reviewer determination of either:
    • Recommend overturned (agree with dispute)
    • Finalised - QC decision upheld (agree with original QOL/QMA decision)
    • Recommend upheld for a different reason (checking outcome is correct but for a different reason to the original)
  • The Status: will update automatically, based on the reviewer determination
  • Once complete, select Submit

If the reviewer determination is:

  • Finalised - QC decision upheld:
    • immediately update the customer record according to the original QOL/QMA decision. Go to Service Support Officer (SSO) role in Technical Peer Support (TPS) in Smart Centres to update the customer record (Customer First and Process Direct) and complete QOL (if needed)
      An automatic email will be sent to the Service Officer and Team Leader
  • Recommend overturned or Recommend upheld for a different reason:
    • seek further review of the decision by a Service Support Officer

Right of reply process

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Right of reply process - original checker + Read more ...

The original QOL/QMA checker reviews the QC decision overturned - right of reply email. If they:

  • Agree with the overturned decision (dispute is upheld) request the local Service Support Officer (SSO) or Team Leader enter supporting comments within the dispute form and update the Status Field with 'Finalised QC decision overturned'. Procedure ends here
  • Disagree with the overturned decision, discuss with the local SSO or Team Leader. Give the SSO/TL a detailed explanation, including operational resources, of why the outcome is not accepted. Request they enter these supporting comments within the dispute form. Once these comments have been added to the form select Submit

The original QOL/QMA checker must add their response to the dispute form within 5 working days of the Date of Decision. If no response is provided within 7 working days, the SSO/CAO who completed the dispute may 'Finalise' the outcome.

2

Right of reply - Service Support Officer + Read more ...

If the original checker disagrees with the overturned decision the dispute status will update to 'Escalated' and an addition email sends automatically to the relevant staff.

The SSO must review the comments provided by the original checker. If the SSO:

  • Agrees with the additional comments the original result should be upheld. The SSO or Team Leader should update the comments within the dispute form and update the Status Field with 'Finalised - QC decision upheld.' Procedure ends here
  • Disagrees with the additional comments the dispute may require escalation to an independent third party review

Before seeking a third party review, SSOs should discuss the outcome. If no agreement can be reached, the overturning SSO must seek a third party review via the relevant program or policy team to obtain a resolution. If unsure of the relevant stakeholder required to complete this review, contact Quality and Technical Support with the details. Requests for third party reviews will only be actioned when they contain all required supporting information.

The current status of the dispute stands until any agreement is reached or a third party review is finalised.

SSOs should not alter the decision coded by another SSO in the SharePoint escalation form without completed and required consultation.
