This page contains identity document coding tips, Frequently Ask Questions (FAQ) about identity documents, a link to identity information on the Services Australia Website, links to external websites and attachments for Document Verification Service (DVS) documents. Links on this page may be unavailable to external audiences.

Identity document coding tips

Table 1:

Note: for further assistance with coding an identity document and escalation process, refer to Coding Identity Documents.

Scenario

Coding tip

Customers with different legal names

Where the name on the identity document is different to the legal name on CPDS, ask the customer how this occurred. This can indicate changes in the customer’s circumstances, e.g. they have partnered or separated.

Customers must provide a linking document, refer to Changing the Legal name of customers or their children.

Common BP Match Error resolutions

1Check the Name and DOB on the identity document and the legal name in CPDS screen match the information coded on the identity confirmation dashboard. If the information matches, you can override the BP Match error by ticking the override box after attempting to verify.

If the document does not match and the identity document is issued by Births, Deaths and Marriages or Department of Home Affairs, code the middle names with the first names. For Example: Customer Name: John, William, Smith, “John William” can be coded as the First Name and “Smith” as the last name.

Single Name Customers

As a general rule, place a hyphen “-“ in the first name field. If the document still does not verify, try switching with the surname field. Otherwise, refer to the operational blueprint Coding Identity Documents for coding assistance and escalation process.

Date Of Birth (DOB) discrepancies

Where there is a DOB difference between the identity documents and the customer’s record, refer to Verifying the date of birth or age of a customer or child to correctly assess the official DOB of a customer.

DOB cannot be different for any identity document used to verify a customer’s identity.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ) about identity documents

Table 2:

Item

Description

1

Why has legal name failed to match in the Document Verification Service (DVS)?

DVS matches information from the issuing authority. Details must be entered as they appear on the document in order to correctly match the identifying information contained on an identity document. Service Officers must also check the Customer Personal Details Screen (CPDS) to confirm the customer’s legal name(s) as known to Services Australia.

2

What are the advantages of a current address on a document?

Documents with current address details show a person's 'locational identity'. That is, the person is at a particular address and is contactable at that address.

In addition, people will often throw out documents when a replacement arrives. The 'old' document could be found and used by someone else.

3

Question: What can be done if there is doubt about the validity of the rates notice, utilities account or vehicle registrations document?

If there is doubt about the validity of the account the Service Officer must either:

  • request proof of payment
  • verify the details with the issuing agency, or
  • request an alternative SUIC document

Proof of payment can include direct debit, a receipt, Centrepay deductions on the Payment Instruction Summary (PINS) screen or, cash register imprint.

4

The document has expired, can it be used for identification purposes?

Some documents that have an expiry or end date can be accepted. For example, Foreign Passports are acceptable for up to two years after expiry date. Refer to each document's criteria for full details.

5

Is a formal death certificate from Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) acceptable as identity?

No, death certificates are not included as acceptable identity documents. Although the BDMs verify the death of the person, they do not verify the details of any survivors that may be mentioned on the certificate. The death certificate therefore provides no further evidence of the person's identity.

6

Are ceremonial, commemorative or decorative birth certificates acceptable as identity?

Ceremonial, commemorative or decorative birth certificates are not acceptable Commencement of Identity (CoI) documents. These certificates do not include the required information to verify through the Document Verification Service

7

Is a document not issued by Australian authorities able to be used as a Commencement of Identity document? (for example, Document issued by the United Nations High Commissioner)

No, only documents issued by Australian authorities are able to be used for confirming the Commencement of an Identity in Australia. This is because the person must establish what name they are known by to Australian authorities.

8

What if a person was born in Papua New Guinea when it was still an Australian Territory?

Papua New Guinea was an Australian territory until 1 September 1975. A person born or married in a former Australian Territory would be issued an Australian birth/marriage certificate. These certificates are acceptable identity documents and must be coded in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard. For Papua New Guinea birth/marriage certificates (prior to September 1975), when recording, the 'State' field is recorded as QLD.

9

What Australian External Territory identity documents are acceptable as confirmation of identity?

Note: Australian External Territories include Norfolk Island, Christmas Island, Cocos (Keeling) Islands and Papua New Guinea (pre-September 1975 only)

The following Birth Certificates can be verified using the Identity Confirmation Dashboard:

  • Christmas Island from 1993
  • Cocos Island from 1977
  • Papua New Guinea pre September 1975

Norfolk Island, Papua New Guinea (post September 1975), Christmas Island (pre 1993) and Cocos Island (pre 1977) issued birth certificates cannot be verified through the Identity Confirmation Dashboard and should only be used when considering an alternative identity assessment. See Alternative Identity.

Note: many Norfolk Island residents are born in NSW and are issued an Australian birth certificate from NSW Births, Deaths and Marriages or other Australian identity documents, such as a valid Australian passport or driver licence. These documents would be recorded as per current processes.

Australian External Territory Drivers Licences cannot be coded into the Identity Confirmation Dashboard and must only be used as part of an alternative identity assessment if the customer has no other substitute identity document. See Alternative Identity.

Identifying name, sex and/or gender on a customer’s BDM birth certificate

Table 3:

State/Territory

Description

Australian Capital Territory (ACT)
  • A notation is made on the back of the birth certificate issued advising the new name and the date of change
  • Individuals have the option to exclude the name/gender change history from the birth certificate
  • Where the change of name/gender is excluded from, the birth certificate, an asterix (*) will be included next to the registration number. There will be no other notation

New South Wales (NSW)
  • Where a change of name is recorded on a birth certificate, the letter N will be included next to the registration number
  • For birth certificates issued prior to the 1990s, the previous name is recorded at the top of the certificate with the new name recorded on either the side or bottom of the certificate
  • A new birth certificate will not include any notation to identify a change of gender/sex, including no reference to previous names the person has had

Northern Territory (NT)
  • A notation on a birth certificate indicating a change of name or sex is optional
  • An asterisk at the bottom of the certificate indicates a change of name has occurred but it is optional to record previous names on the birth certificate
  • Where the change of name/gender has not been included on the birth certificate, the certificate will have an asterix (*) next to the registration number

Queensland (QLD)
  • The recording of a change of name on a birth certificate is optional. If a person requires a change of name to be recorded on their birth certificate ,it will be recorded on the front of the certificate and previous names on the back or a notation will be made on the back noting the name change
  • A new birth certificate will not show the gender has changed, it will only show the birth has been re-registered

South Australia (SA)
  • The recording of a change of name on a birth certificate is optional. If the person does not want it recorded on their birth certificate a Change of Name certificate can be issued which includes the previous name(s).
  • If the person requests the details to be included on their birth certificate the new name details are recorded on the reverse side of the certificate
  • Where the change of name/gender has not been included on the birth certificate, the certificate will have an asterix (*) next to the registration number
  • There is no notation made on the new birth certificate for a change of sex

Tasmania (TAS)
  • The recording of a change of name on a birth certificate is optional. If the person chooses to have it recorded there is an N placed after the registration number on the certificate
  • There is no notation made on the new birth certificate regarding change of name/gender, including no reference to previous names unless requested by the individual

Victoria (VIC)
  • BDM advises that the letter N is recorded after the registration number on the birth certificate indicating a name change. A letter N is recorded for each name change so there can be more than one letter N on any certificate. This has been in place since 1986
  • When the letter C appears on a certificate after the registration number it indicates that a correction has been made to the certificate. This includes the correction of any spelling errors
  • Gender/name details will be excluded from birth certificates where gender details have changed
  • The birth certificate will have either an N or C in the top right corner to indicate there has been a change of name or correction

Western Australia (WA)
  • If a change of name was completed prior to 1974 a stamp will be placed on the birth certificate noting the amendment
  • If a change of name is completed after 1974, the person is asked whether they want the details of the change to appear on their birth certificate. It is not mandatory. No codes are used, the details are written in normal text
  • Recording a change of name/gender on a birth certificate is optional. There is no notation on the new birth certificate about previous names or gender details, unless the individual requests their inclusion
