This page contains process summary to identify a customer for Income Management (IM) under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR) measure.

Eligibility assessment

Identifying customers for IM under the LTWPR measure

This table outlines the criteria to identify a customer for Income Management under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients (LTWPR) measure.

Step

Action

1

Customer identified for IM under the LTWPR measure + Read more ...

A customer can be identified by the system for Income Management under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR) measure when a trigger event occurs. This can be:

  • change of address to the Northern Territory
  • grant of a qualifying payment
  • required time on payment has been reached
  • an exemption has ended
  • an excluded payment nominee arrangement has ended and the customer’s ineligibility coding has been removed

2

Is the customer already income managed? + Read more...

Is the customer already being income managed under the Cape York Initiative, or the Child Protection, Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) or Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients (VWPR) measures?

  • If yes, the customer will remain on Income Management under these measures. There is a hierarchy of Income Management measures, see Overview and hierarchy of Income Management which puts these measures above the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients (LTWPR) measure. Procedure ends here
  • If no, go to Step 3

3

Excluded payment nominee for IM purposes + Read more...

Does the customer have an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes?

See the customer's Nominee Link Summary (NOLS) screen for nominee information.

  • If yes, the customer is not eligible for Income Management
    • A review of the nominee arrangement should take place to determine whether it is working in the best interests of the customer and that they are not used in a way to avoid Income Management
    • If the arrangement is to continue, record the customer as ineligible. For details see Initial Assessment interview for Disengaged Youth and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients
    • Procedure ends here
  • If no, go to Step 4

4

Customer aged 25 years or older and under pension age + Read more...

Is the customer aged 25 years or older and under Age Pension age?

  • If yes, go to Step 5
  • If no, customers aged under 25 years will be considered under the Disadvantaged Youth category. Customers over Age Pension age can volunteer for Income Management. Procedure ends here

5

Customer in receipt of a category E welfare payment + Read more...

To be eligible for Income Management under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient measure, a customer must be in receipt of a category E welfare payment.

Category E welfare payment types are:

  • Youth Allowance (YA)
  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
  • Special Benefit (SpB)
  • Parenting Payment Single (PPS)
  • Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP)

Is the customer in receipt of a category E welfare payment?

  • If yes, go to Step 6
  • If no, the customer is not eligible for Income Management under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient measure. Procedure ends here

6

Timeframe customer has been receiving category E welfare payments + Read more...

Has the customer been in receipt of one or more category E welfare payments for at least 364 days (52 weeks) of the previous 728 days (104 weeks) at the test time?

Current zero rate (CZR) and periods where payment is suspended count towards the 52 weeks. Periods where the payment is assessed (such as serving a liquid assets waiting period) do not count towards the total.

  • If yes, go to Step 7
  • If no, the customer is not eligible for Income Management under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients measure. Procedure ends here

7

Customer's usual place of residence is in the Northern Territory + Read more...

The usual place of residence is determined by the system based on the customer's home (HOM) or term (TER) address.

A customer meeting all other eligibility conditions can be identified for Income Management when the address is updated to indicate that the customer now lives in the Northern Territory.

Is the customer's usual place of residence in the Northern Territory?

  • If yes, the customer is eligible for Income Management under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients measure. The system flags the customer as eligible for Income Management and an Initial Assessment interview is required, see Initial Assessment interview for Disengaged Youth and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients. The process for engaging the customer commences. Procedure ends here
  • If no, go to Step 8

8

Usual place of residence not within the Northern Territory + Read more ...

The customer will not be considered for Income Management under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient measure.

Important:

  • Customers currently being income managed under the LTWPR measure who have their HOM or TER address updated to reflect that they now live outside of the Northern Territory, will continue to have their payments income managed for 13 weeks from the date they moved
  • Customers who move out of the Northern Territory during their engagement period may continue to have their payments income managed

For more information see Customer moves out of Income Management area.
