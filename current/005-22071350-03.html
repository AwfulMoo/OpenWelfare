Investigating potential SRSS debts

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Keep a complete and accurate record of all debt information + Read more ...

Record in a DOC all information reviewed as part of the investigation and assessment of an overpayment. This includes scans, DOCs and other supporting evidence.

Use the debt worksheet for any debt that is raised, scan to the customer's record and upload to mySupport if an escalation is required.

If the debt outcome after a reassessment is Finalised No Debt (FND):

  • the debt worksheet is not essential. Do not scan it to a customer's record
  • document the investigation and reason for escalation on the customer's record

Service Officers must:

  • establish what the customer was actually paid, and
  • calculate what the customer was entitled to be paid using information which has been verified

The difference between the 2 is the debt.

Record all debt calculations and supporting documentation on the customer's record.

The Resources page has a copy of the debt worksheet and the link to mySupport.

2

Locked payments + Read more ...

Check the customer record to work out if there are any payments with an active payment lock.

Go to Open Item List from Customer First Workspace.

If the payment component has a padlock icon, this payment has an active lock. It is a locked payment.

An active payment lock will only prevent assessment of an undetermined debt shell if the components, amounts and dates of the locked payment match the debt shell.

If an active payment lock is identified, it needs to be unlocked to allow any offsetting to occur and so the debt shell can be correctly assessed and finalised. Refer the record to ICT.

See the Resources page for the correct webform and a link to mySupport.

Go to Table 2.

Raising potential SRSS debts

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check the amount of the debt shell is correct + Read more ...

In Customer First, go to Workspace > Financial Customer Care > Customer Documents.

In the Debt Documents block:

  • Locate the undetermined Debt ID, in the Debt ID column
  • Note the Billdoc No. associated with the relevant Debt ID
  • Select the SSP hyperlink next to the relevant Debt ID to go into the SSP

In the Payments Table block:

  • Check for lines with the same Billdoc No. This shows the posting date, amount the customer has been paid, and the start and end dates for the period paid
  • Selecting the Billdoc No. displays the daily rate for each day
  • Select the SSP hyperlink to go to the SSP

2

Reviewing the data in the SSP + Read more ...

The screen now displays the SSP that created the debt shell.

  • Note the Approval Date of the SSP
  • Expand the Compare Circumstance assignment block to view the Reassessment Reason Codes
  • Expand the Gross Entitlement Amounts assignment block to display the daily rate of payment and the components for each period
    Note the Gross Entitlement Amounts and check the daily rate for each item. This will allow comparison with the daily rate from the previous SSP
  • Select the date to expand the field to show further details

3

Compare the debt creating SSP with the previous SSP + Read more ...

To identify the amount of the daily rate change, compare the SSP with previous SSPs. To go to the previous SSP, select the hyperlink on the General Details information titled Previous Decision.

Compare the 2 SSPs

  • When comparing the SSPs, look for changes in the:
    • Gross Entitlement Amounts
    • Start and end dates for the item
    • Retrospective adjustment in planned payments - arrears or overpayment
    • Entitlement Items
  • Calculate the difference between the 2 rates
  • Multiply the difference by the number of days in the debt period

The outcome should equal the amount in the debt shell.

Other checks

  • Check Payment List to determine if the debt may be as a result of a duplicate payment
  • Check if a debt shell has been manually created and the debt raised or written off for the same period of the automatic debt shell
  • Check if the updates that generated the debt were made correctly

Is the outcome amount equal to the debt shell?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Debt offsetting + Read more ...

Check the updates and correct the record if required, then run the Change Process.

Where a reassessment of the customer's record is completed, any arrears generated as a result may automatically finalise or change the debt amount. Allow time for the system to apply the update, offsetting may take 24 hours to adjust. Hold the debt activity to 'User' in WLM to check the next day.

Is the adjusted amount correct?

  • Yes:
    • If the debt shell been finalised no debt, procedure ends here
    • If the debt shell has not been finalised, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Incorrect adjusted amount + Read more ...

If an adjustment has not been applied to the debt:

  • escalate the case to the SRSS Helpdesk
  • record details on a DOC
  • once the debt is adjusted, go to Step 6

If the corrective action did not automatically remove the debt (FND):

  • refer the record to ICT via mySupport. See the Resources page for the correct webform and a link to mySupport
  • do not finalise the debt as FND
  • procedure ends here

6

Determine the reason for the debt + Read more ...

Check for information that shows why the payment rate has changed for the period of the debt. Review the:

  • Document List (DL)
  • Event Summary (ES)
  • The Compare Circumstances tab - indicates the reason for changes applied in the SSP
  • SSP that created the debt:
    • Look at the date the SSP was created and approved
    • The Decision Basis of the SSP provides the information on the customer record that contributed to the SSP assessment

Note: if there is a time lag between these dates, this indicates a change of circumstances may not have been applied to recent payments.

Is the reason for the debt known?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Change in payment rate is not clear + Read more ...

If the reason for the change in payment rate is not clear, review information on the customer's record to identify what changed for the period of the debt. Review the information on the record, including but not limited to the screens listed below.

Income details

  • Earnings (EANS)
  • Other Income (OINS)
  • Foreign Pension/Annuities Summary (FIPS)
  • Real Estate & Business Income (REBS)
  • Other Government Payment (OGP)

Other details

  • Reporting Regime (RPRS)
  • Reporting History (RPRH)
  • Accommodation circumstances - determine Rent Assistance eligibility on Accommodation Summary (ACS)
  • Marital Status (MS)
  • Relationship tab for changes in dependent children

SRSS specific information

In Customer First, select the CRN hyperlink to go to customer details. Select the Circumstance Data tab and scroll down to display the tab titled SRSS Information. This will show details about:

  • Housing type
  • Band type
  • Liquid Asset details
  • Eligibility periods
  • Family Group details/Primary carer indicator

Is the change of payment clear?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, get help from the SRSS Helpdesk as per standard processes. Record details in a DOC

8

Sensitive debts + Read more ...

SRSS debts are classified as sensitive if they meet one of the following:

  • customers are under 18 years of age at the date of the debt
  • deceased customers
  • customers who are sensitive in some other manner, for example Family and Domestic Violence (FDV)

Is the debt a sensitive debt?

Note: if unable to determine, get help from the SRSS Helpdesk as per standard processes. Record details in a DOC.

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

9

Finalising a sensitive debt + Read more ...

  • Raise the debt
  • Inhibit the letter on the Account Payable (OPOAD) screen by selecting 'Y' to suppress advice
  • Record the date sent, log on ID and the comments (for example, sensitive debt) on the Key Dates (OPKD) screen
  • Go to the Debt Comments (OPDC) screen. Use the text advice template to complete the Comments field
  • Press [Enter] to go to the Assessment Finalisation (OPAF) screen to finalise the debt
  • Do not contact the customer for recovery
  • DOC the record
  • Transfer the debt to the Department of Home Affairs, see Table 5

10

Finalising the debt + Read more ...

Review the available information.

If the debt is to be Raised:

  • Select the debt activity on the Activity List (AL) screen
  • Go to the Modify/Assess Debt (OPMA) screen to record:
    • the calculated amount,
    • PGPA legislation and
    • reason for the debt
  • Go to the Account Payable (OPAOD) screen to complete:
    • the debt advice using the Debt Advice text template
    • contact name and number 1800316556
  • Go to the Assessment Finalisation (OPAF) screen and select Y to finalise the debt
  • Once the debt has been raised, go to Step 11

If the debt is unable to be determined:

  • Escalate the case to the SRSS Helpdesk
  • Record details on a DOC

11

Follow up actions required + Read more ...

After investigating and finalising the debt shell, record the outcome of the debt investigation using the Fast Note templates.

Where a debt has been raised, make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer to advise of the decision to raise the debt.

Has the customer accepted the debt?

  • Yes, code repayment arrangement. Refer to Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS) payment debt recovery. Process ends here.
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3

Reviewing a decision to raise an SRSS Debt

Table 3: This table outlines the process if a customer is requesting a review of their SRSS debt.

Step

Action

1

Customer requests a review of the debt + Read more ...

On the Debt Amount (OPDA) screen confirm if the debt is with Services Australia or if previously transferred to the Department of Home Affairs.

Has the debt been transferred to the Department of Home Affairs?

  • Yes, tell the customer to lodge the review request with the Department of Home Affairs. If the Department of Home Affairs deems the review request appropriate, they will lodge the review request with Service Australia. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Customer requests a further explanation of the decision to raise a debt + Read more ...

Investigate the customer's record. Check DOCs to find the reason why the debt was raised.

If the customer is not satisfied with the reason for the debt:

  • Ask if they can send any information relevant to the decision to assist with the review
  • if further information can be provided issue an SRSS RFI using the Request For Information guided procedure
  • record the information using the Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Multicultural SRSS Debts > SRSS Debt Review Request
  • the DOC will be allocated via WLM to SRSS Debt processing staff

Note: do not run the Internal Review/Appeal script used to record an appeal. This does not apply to SRSS debts.

3

SRSS Debt processing staff + Read more ...

Debt processing staff pick up the activity in WLM:

  • If no further evidence is required, review the debt based on available evidence
  • If an RFI was issued, hold the debt to user until day 22
  • If a debt is reviewed following an explanation of decision request, and additional calculations are completed, complete and scan the debt worksheet to the customer's record

After reviewing the record, is the decision correct?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Decision to be changed + Read more ...

If the customer has provided information that could affect the outcome of the SRSS debt:

  • record the information in a DOC
  • escalate the case to the SRSS Helpdesk

Procedure ends here.

5

Decision unchanged + Read more ...

Explain the reasons for the decision to the customer using information available on their record.

Has the customer requested further consideration, or asked about further avenues of review?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, finalise the review of decision
    • DOC details using the SRSS Debt Review Outcome Fast Note including why the decision is affirmed and any contact/discussions with the customer
    • Issue a Q999 letter to advise the customer of the decision, using the Debt Review Outcome template
    • Procedure ends here

6

Customer has requested a further review of decision + Read more ...

If the customer requested a further review, escalate the case to the SRSS Helpdesk via email and DOC the record.

SSRS Helpdesk review

Step

Action

1

SRSS Helpdesk considers the customer's circumstances + Read more ...

Does the SRSS Helpdesk confirm the decision is correct?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

SRSS Helpdesk will:

  • notify the Department of Home Affairs that the decision is correct

Transferring SRSS debts to the Department of Home Affairs

Table 5:

Step

Action

1

Transfer SRSS debt to the Department of Home Affairs + Read more ...

  • Go to the Debt List (OPDL) screen and select the debt
  • Go to the Debt Amount (OPDA) screen and key XBP in the Change reason field, then press [Enter]
  • The system will confirm the amount to be transferred, press [Enter]
