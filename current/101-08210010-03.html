Assisting customers to update their study details

Online Study Update – Manual Processing

Manage Study Details option online

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Confirm end date and indicate if further assistance is required + Read more ...

When a customer uses this service to report the end date of their studies, they can indicate if they will require further assistance from Services Australia, once they have finished studying.

The customer will see a receipt page:

  • confirming their study end date, and
  • outlining any further action they must take to remain on payment or receive further help

Where an update to the customer's record is not possible, or the update activity fails, an appropriate handoff is created for Manual Follow-up (MFU), depending on the exact circumstances.

Note: JobSeeker Payment (JSP) streamlined claims are offered as further financial assistance for Austudy (AUS) and Youth Allowance (YA) students 22 years of age or over at their study end date, when their study end date is within 28 days.

This option presents to the customer as a ‘Confirm course end date’ task on their Centrelink online account home screen. This option will not present if the customer has:

  • an outstanding study details manual follow up (MFU) activity
  • an existing JSP claim submitted, or
  • reached their student end date

2

Editing course details + Read more ...

A customer uses this service to report any changes to their current course details.

The customer is able to edit their:

  • student ID
  • study load
  • contact hours per week, and
  • course end date

The following fields cannot be changed:

  • education institution's name
  • course title
  • study level, and
  • course start date

The customer will see a receipt page confirming their changes.

Where a customer changes:

  • only the student ID, the system attempts to auto complete the activity
  • only the course title, there will be a started interaction on Notes
  • other information, a manual update of the record may be needed. A transaction For Manual Action will show on Interaction History

3

Add new course + Read more ...

A customer uses this service to report they will study a new course in the next study period.

  • The Add new course option can be selected if the customer:
    • has no future full time, or concessional study load course recorded
    • has an intention to study recorded, or
    • is receiving Youth Allowance (job seeker) (YAL-JSK)
  • If there is a future full time course on their record, the customer:
    • can only view and remove this course
    • cannot edit these details. If wanting to change, they must use the add new course workflow (My profile only)
  • If the service identifies that the customer may qualify for Relocation Scholarship, they will be asked to provide their family home address
  • Youth Allowance and Austudy customers can also search for a course using predictive text. They can:
    • select other and unknown fields if they do not know their course code
    • manually enter information if the field is blank
    • adjust course start and end dates if existing data prepopulates and is incorrect
  • Youth Allowance (job seeker) customers, in some cases, may be transferred to Youth Allowance (student) automatically if:
    • the customer added a Tertiary Group B course, and
    • the update can be applied by the system

4

Intending to continue study in the next semester but does not know the full details of the course + Read more ...

A customer uses this service when they:

  • think they will be continuing study in the next semester, but
  • do not know the full details of the course

A customer records the expected start date of their next course. The following dates are coded onto the record:

  • The last date of the intention to study (next course start date - 1), and
  • The start date of the intention to study (most recent or current course student end date + 1)

Where the online update fails, an activity For Manual Action is created on Interaction History.

5

Youth Allowance - turning 25 processing - full implementation + Read more ...

Youth Allowance (YA) is only paid for courses started before the customer turns 25 years of age.

The service performs slightly differently for customers who either turn 25:

  • before their current course 'Student end date' (= over 25 customers) or
  • within 20 weeks of their current 'Student end date' (= turning 25 customers)

See Determining minimum and maximum age for Youth Allowance (YA)

Assisting customers to update their study details

Table 2

Item

Description

1

No study details are recorded + Read more ...

If no study details are recorded on the customer's record online, check the customer's Centrelink record.

Possible cause: No study details have been recorded on customer's record in the last 4 weeks.

Check customer's circumstances and update study details as needed. See the following procedures for more information:

  • Assessing study load requirements
  • Satisfying the activity test for Youth Allowance (YA) by undertaking full-time study
  • Satisfying the activity test for Austudy
  • Study requirements for ABSTUDY
  • Study requirements for ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
  • Assessing qualifying study for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)

2

Unable to extend the end date for studies by more than 4 weeks + Read more ...

A customer cannot extend the end date of their full-time studies by more than 4 weeks using their Centrelink online account.

  • Advise the customer this action cannot be taken using online accounts
  • Update customer's record as needed. See:
    • Student to job seeker transfers
    • Effect of end of course on payments for students

3

Unable to extend the end date for studies at all + Read more ...

The end date of the customer's full-time studies can only be updated once using their Centrelink online account.

Possible causes:

  • The customer has previously extended the end date using online accounts
  • A Service Officer has previously extended the customer's end date

Update customer's record as needed. See:

  • Student to job seeker transfers
  • Effect of end of course on payments for students

4

Updates do not appear on the Manage Study Details page + Read more ...

Some updates made by the customer may have to be manually processed. This can take a few days to action before the details are on the customer's Manage Study Details page in their Centrelink online account.

Action to be taken:

  • Check for any Notes on the customer's interaction record to confirm manual processing is required. See Step 2 in Table 3
  • Advise customer it may take a few days before they are able to view the updates online

5

The option to record a study intention is not available + Read more ...

Possible causes:

  • Customer already has a study intention on their record
  • Customer's current course end date is more than 13 weeks away

Action to be taken:

  • Check the customer's Education Course History (EDCH) screen for study details on their record
  • Update if required

See the following procedures for more information:

  • Assessing study load requirements
  • Satisfying the activity test for Youth Allowance (YA) by undertaking full-time study
  • Satisfying the activity test for customers who are intending to study for Austudy
  • Study requirements for ABSTUDY
  • Study requirements for ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
  • Changing study or training details

6

The educational institution's name is incomplete or the spelling is incorrect + Read more ...

The agency’s database has limited space for the educational institution name, so abbreviations are common.

Corrections cannot be made to the educational institution name.

7

Service is temporarily unavailable + Read more ...

If a Service Officer is looking at the customer's record and that customer tries to update online, they will get the message 'This service is not currently available'.

Possible causes:

  • Centrelink system is not available
  • Customer's record is locked, either by a Service Officer or an activity being actioned
  • An activity has been started by the customer on their record

Action to be taken:

  • Check the Centrelink system to see if it is available
  • Check the customer's Lock Override (LOVER) screen to see if someone is in the customer's record
  • Check the customer's Activity List (AL) screen for any started activities

Also see Item 8 Record is locked and unable to access.

8

Record is locked and unable to access + Read more ...

Possible causes:

  • If a Service Officer is looking at the customer's record and that customer tries to update online, they will get the message 'This service is not currently available'
  • A customer accessing their Centrelink online account will lock their record in the Centrelink system
  • The customer may have used the exit button at the top of the screen to close their browser
  • The computer used by the customer may have shut down abnormally while they were accessing their Centrelink online account

Action to be taken:

  • Advise the customer to wait 30 minutes and try again
  • Advise customer to log out first by using the 'log out' button provided on the page and then close the browser

Also see Item 7 Service is temporarily unavailable.

9

I can't see the course I completed last year + Read more ...

This service will only show courses that have finished within the last 4 weeks.

Advise the customer that the service will only show courses that finished within the last 4 weeks.

10

I sent in or uploaded a form advising of my new course details and the new details are not displayed + Read more ...

Form has not yet been processed and information not entered into the customer's Centrelink record.

Action to be taken:

  • Try to find the document. Check with the customer how long ago the form was uploaded. Documents are sometimes uploaded on partner's or parents' records
  • Service Officers are to use their discretion to decide what action to take based on the information given

11

Course details displayed are incorrect + Read more ...

Customer/agency error.

In Customer First, enter the correct details onto the Education Course Details (EDC) screen and other screens as applicable. See Coding new study details.

12

Why does the course code display 'Not recorded'? + Read more ...

Customer has not advised the department of the course code.

If the customer is present (phone or in person), and they:

  • have the course code, update the Education Course Details (EDC) screen in Customer First
  • question why their record doesn't have the course code, advise it is not essential for the agency to have the information

Online Study Update – Manual Processing

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Online Study Update allocated for Manual Processing + Read more ...

Workload Manger (WLM) will allocate a Change of Circumstance (CoC) transaction when a YA, PES, ABSTUDY or Austudy customer:

  • updates their study details using:
    • their Centrelink online account, or
    • the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app, and
  • the system does not complete the study update

The online study update shows as the following:

  • Youth Allowance (YA) and Austudy: ZCOC_STUDY_DETAILS
  • ABSTUDY: ZALL_ABY_STUDY_UPD
  • PES: ZCOC_ZEPF_STUDY
  • ABSTUDY PES: ZCOC_ZEPA_STUDY

Is there an Online Study Update ZCOC or ZALL allocated in Process Direct?

  • Yes, go to step 2
  • No, continue with course updates in Customer First. Go to Step 3

2

Change of Study Details ZCOC or ZALL allocated in Process Direct + Read more ...

Process Direct is not used when a Change of Circumstances (ZCOC or ZALL) activity is allocated with a status of In Progress or For Manual Action.

When a Change of Circumstance (CoC) activity is allocated in Process Direct:

  • Review the transaction overview. Select
    View details of the customer updates in the overview. Select to open the information in a separate window. The information remains open while processing updates in Customer First
  • View the Notes screen to review any Customer Update Notes
  • Copy the CRN
  • Open Customer First
  • CRN/BP: field, enter the CRN
  • Select [Enter]

Is there a started REA activity on the Activity List (AL) in Customer First?

  • Yes, go to step 3
  • No, go to step 4

3

Started REA activity in Customer First + Read more ...

If an Online Study Details update contains an error, a started REA activity will show on the Activity List (AL) screen in Customer First.

Note: for PES customers only, if using Process Direct, the message 'This work item cannot be processed using Process Direct’ shows.

For customers advising a change in study load, go to Step 5.

For other updates, select the started REA activity from the AL screen:

  • go to the Education Course History (EDCH) screen
  • select the line to update the Education Course Details (EDC) screen. See Coding new study details
  • go to Activity Results (AR), check the outcome and finalise the activity
  • record details on a DOC

After completing the manual action, update the CoC status from For Manual Action to Completed on Interaction History in Customer First.

If more information is needed, make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer. If contact is unsuccessful:

  • Pick up the REA activity in Customer First from AL
  • Hold the activity for 14 days
  • Do not action the work item in Interaction History, go to Step 6

Procedure ends here.

4

No started REA activity in Customer First + Read more ...

For customers advising a change in study load, go to Step 5

For customers updating their study details:

  • go to the Education Course History (EDCH) screen
  • select the line to update the Education Course Details (EDC) screen, see Coding new study details
  • go to Activity Results (AR), check the outcome and finalise the activity
  • record details on a DOC

After completing the manual action, update the CoC status from For Manual Action to Completed on Interaction History in Customer First.

If more information is needed, make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer. If contact is unsuccessful, go to Step 6.

Procedure ends here.

5

Change of Circumstances (ZCOC) Full Time Study (FTS) to Part Time Study (PTS) + Read more ...

If a customer advises a change in study load, a ZCOC_STUDY_DETAILS activity will create an REA activity on the Activity List (AL) screen in Customer First.

If additional documents are needed to process the updated, go to Step 6.

Within the REA activity, the Education Course Details (EDC) will have a provisional update with the Participation Status coded as Part Time Study (PTS).

Because Part Time Study (PTS) must not be coded, select the REA activity from the Activity List (AL) screen and update as follows:

  • go to the Education Course History (EDCH) screen and select the line to update. On EDC:
    • Participation Status: field, Full Time Student ('FTS')
    • Student End Date : field, key the last day studied
  • go to the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen
    • Event Date: field, key the Student End Date + 1
    • Student Status: field, Not a Student ('NST')
  • go to the Activity Results (AR) screen and finalise
  • record details on a DOC

After completing the manual action, update the CoC status from For Manual Action to Completed on Interaction History in Customer First

For ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY PES, see Changing study load from full-time to part-time or concessional study load for ABSTUDY or ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)

For YA, Austudy and PES, see Changing study load from full-time to part-time or concessional study load for Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy or PES

Procedure ends here.

6

Request documents + Read more ...

If more information is required and contact was unsuccessful:

  • Navigate to Process Direct
  • Go to inbox
  • Enter the customer's CRN
  • Select Go
  • Select the study details work item
  • Select >Request Documents
  • Enter the customer's benefit type e.g YAL, AUS
  • Benefit status: Continuous
  • Select the required document/s then >>
  • Select Finish

This will place the work item on hold for 14 days pending documentation.

Return to Customer First and DOC the record advising what documentation has been requested and that the activity has been placed on hold.
