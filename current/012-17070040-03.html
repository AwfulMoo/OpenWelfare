Is the date of service of the claim more than 2 years before today’s date?

Check reason for requesting a DVA late lodgement + Read more ...

Check with the caller the reason for requesting a DVA late lodgement for a claim.

Administrative errors are not acceptable reasons for a DVA late lodgement. Some examples of administrative errors are:

  • poor book keeping
  • inadequate staffing/resources/training
  • an issue with software, system or computer malfunctions

Is the reason due to an administrative error?

  • Yes, advise the practice that the agecny will not approve applications for these reasons
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Verify reason is acceptable + Read more ...

Verify with practice that there are extenuating circumstances for requesting a DVA late lodgement for a claim.

Some examples of acceptable reasons are:

  • Hospitalisation, or serious long term medical condition, and extensive period of recovery (however it would not be acceptable if the provider was still practising during this period)
  • Evidence to demonstrate that if a longer period is not allowed, there will be impact on the ongoing viability of the practice, such as certified copies of bank statements or tax returns
  • A natural disaster, such as flood or fire
  • A compliance audit on the provider carried out by DVA that has resulted in the provider needing to resubmit claims

These examples are a guide only and do not cover all possible acceptable reasons.

Does the Provider’s explanation meet extenuating circumstances criteria?

Note: a final decision is not to be made at this stage. Only give advice on possible eligibility. If a provider insists on submitting an application, go to Step 5

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, advise the practice ‘The agency will not approve applications for these reasons’

5

Mandatory documents + Read more ...

Advise practice of the following mandatory documents required for an application for late lodgement:

  • An application for late lodgement of a DVA claim for treatment services form signed by the Provider. See the Resources page for a link to the form
  • Correctly completed original treatment services voucher, certified by the patient. See the Resources page for a link to DVA forms
  • Evidence to demonstrate the circumstances preventing lodgement within the 2 years, or
  • Evidence to demonstrate that if a longer period is not allowed, there will be impact on the ongoing viability of the practice, such as certified copies of bank statements or tax returns

Applications for late lodgement will not be accepted electronically. They are only required for claims submitted for the first time outside the 2 year period, not adjustments.

6

Proceeding with DVA Late Lodgement application + Read more ...

If the practice wishes to proceed with the DVA late lodgement claim, send the Application for Late Lodgement of DVA Claims (D9207) form to the Provider via post or fax.

See the Resources page for a link to the form.

Claim is received with a Late Lodgement Application form + Read more ...

Check the Veteran's history.

When a claim and an Application for Late Lodgement of DVA Claims (D9207) is received by Veterans’ Affairs Processing (VAP) operations, the claim will be allocated the VAP Late Lodgement work type.

For help, see:

  • Search work items and Assign to Me table
  • Managing work items in the Worklist table

Service Officers briefly assess the claim prior to escalating for delegation:

  • Check the Veteran’s history to determine if the claim has previously been paid
  • Veteran’s history is stored on the system mainframe for a period of 2 years from the Date of Processing

To check Veteran’s history:

  • Key 'VHOI, Veteran’s file number' and press [Enter]
  • Press [F8] to scroll through history
  • Search through the entire Veteran’s history for evidence that the claim has previously been submitted and paid

Forward claim details to VAP Policy Section Tier 3 if the provider has:

  • made any reference in the Application for Late Lodgement or accompanying documentation that indicates the provider has transferred or has the intention to transfer the burden of payment onto the DVA client. (This includesg advising the client to seek reimbursement from DVA, another Commonwealth or state department/agency, or instructing them to perform any other private payment arrangement for the relevant services), and/or
  • referred to the DVA client’s mental health state or mental health condition in the Application for Late Lodgement or accompanying documentation

If the claim has:

  • previously been paid
    • Service Officers enter the details as per normal manual data entry processing instructions. See Claims for Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP) for more information
    • Once claim is in Pend status, reject services with RSN 162. Finalise claim as per normal processing instructions
  • not previously been paid, go to Step 3

3

Escalate to delegate + Read more ...

Send claim for delegation to Veterans’ Affairs Processing Team leader (TL5) for assessment.

In accordance with DVA’s Late Lodgement policy (see Background page), TL5 forwards the claim along with recommendation to Service Delivery TL6 (the Delegate) who may act as a delegate on behalf of DVA.

The Delegate (TL6) considers the recommendation of TL5 to extend the 2 year lodgement period where the servicing provider can provide evidence that:

  • demonstrates a serious personal issue or natural disaster which prevented the claim from being lodged within the 2 year period from the date of service
  • if a longer period is not allowed it will impact the ongoing viability of the practice

If approval for a late lodgement is agreed/undetermined, the delegate (TL6) may forward all documentation to the VAP Policy Section Tier 3, to seek input/advice, however the final decision sits with the TL6. The Tier 3 helpdesk may also liaise with DVA for advice regarding a decision.

Upon advice from DVA, allowances for further investigation must be made where the Veteran/s associated with the claim has evidence of known mental health conditions.

The claim and application may be escalated to the DVA delegation if:

  • There is any reference in the Application for Late Lodgement or accompanying documentation which indicates that the provider has transferred or has the intention to transfer the burden of payment onto the DVA client. This includes advising the client to seek reimbursement from DVA, another Commonwealth or State department/agency, or instructing them to perform any other private payment arrangement for the relevant services, and/or
  • There is reference to the DVA client’s mental health state or mental health condition in the Application for Late Lodgement or accompanying documentation

The claim and application must be forwarded to the VAP helpdesk for further consideration.

4

Assessing late lodgement claims + Read more ...

Once a late lodgement application has been assessed, the delegate will reassign the claim and supporting documentation to the Service Officer for processing.

If the Delegate has:

  • Approved payment of the service
    • Key the claim details as per normal manual data entry processing instructions. See Claims for Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP) for further information
    • Once claim is in Pend status, use PI 17 to pay service (Over key Control line with VASI)
  • Declined payment of service
    • Send the PROVIDER Late Lodgement not approved (Z1664) letter along with certified copies of the claim and Late Lodgement Application form. The Resources page contains links to the form and the Medicare Provider letters
    • The claim does not need to be processed

The decision of the DVA or their Delegate is final.

5

Request culled history + Read more ...

Data is periodically culled from the system and will no longer appear on the Veteran’s History screen.

The data is still available upon request via the VCHI screen and an online reporting tool, the Fuji Xerox Document Management System.

If the date of service (DOS) is greater than 5 years old, a Treatment Data Report (TDR) is required to be requested from Veterans’ Affairs. Forward claims details to VAP Policy Section Tier 3 for referral to Veterans’ Affairs. Only refer claims to Veterans’ Affairs for a TDR when the application for late lodgement has been approved.

Key VHOI, Veteran’s file number and press [Enter]. The VAP - VETERAN HISTORY will display.

From this screen, to view the cull date:

  • Under ITEM column the first line CULL displays
  • Find the PRC DT column
    • First line displays a date DDMMYY (for example 010316)
    • This is the cull date

Request history from the month of the service being processed (for example, the date of service in the Late Lodgement claim) through to the culled history/treatment data request date obtained from VHOI screen.

  • Key VCHI, File No. and press [Enter]
  • In the PERIOD field, key the dates required (MMYY) in 6 month intervals and press [Enter]
  • U to Update

The Veteran’s culled history has been requested for the period entered and is actioned each Monday morning.

Place the claim in Pend in PaNDA, with the correct Pend Reason type until culled history can be accessed the following Monday.

Culled history is returned in Date of Processing order.

6

Access Culled History or Treatment Data Request + Read more ...

Each Monday, an online report is generated via Fuji Xerox Document Management System (formerly Hermes Precisa Pty Ltd (HPA) Online reporting tool).

To access the report access:

  • HSI homepage
  • Select the Intranet in the Search options
  • Select F from the global A-Z bar
  • Scroll down and select Fuji Xerox Document Management System from the list
  • Expand the Medicare folder
  • Expand the CSD folder
  • Select Business Analysis and Support from the list
  • Key 'VAMHCARC' in the Print Job Name field and select Search
  • Select the required report

This report generates and displays culled history/treatment data in the same format as the VHOI screen.

  • Scroll through Culled History Report until the Veterans details are listed at the top of the page
  • Search through Veteran’s culled history to verify if claim has previously been paid, and is no longer listed on the VHOI screen.

If claim has:

  • previously been paid:
    • reject services with RSN 162 and attach relevant page of culled history report to claim
    • finalise claim as per normal processing instructions
  • not previously been paid, once claim is in Pend status, use P1 17 to pay service (over-key control line with VASI) unpaid go to Step 5

7

Escalation to Veterans’ Affairs + Read more ...

The decision from Veterans’ Affairs or their delegate is final.

However, if a provider wants to seek further clarification regarding an unsuccessful application or would like to appeal the decision, advise the provider to contact Veterans' Affairs directly to discuss their options.
