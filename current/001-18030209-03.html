This page contains information about the financial penalties that are applied as a result of a mutual obligation failure, work refusal failure or unemployment failure.

Impact on job seeker as a result of a failure

Item

Description

1

Accepting a financial penalty as part of reporting + Read more ...

Job seekers can choose to accept the financial penalty for certain mutual obligation failures without a compliance investigation.

These failures include a first or second failure in the financial penalty zone for non-attendance at:

  • provider appointments
  • third party appointments
  • job interviews
  • activities, or
  • job search requirements

Where the job seeker is reporting online, they are advised of the requirement that they missed and if they have a good reason for not meeting the requirement to contact the agency. If the job seeker indicates they:

  • do not want to talk to the agency, completing the report will automatically apply the financial penalty
  • do want to talk to the agency, a failure becomes an active failure and prevents finalisation of the report until the compliance investigation is completed. The job seeker must contact the PST

Where a Service Officer is using the Earnings and Reporting tool to assist the job seeker to report, the option to accept a financial penalty without discussing with the PST is presented. The Earnings Outcome page displays information about the mutual obligation failure to discuss with the job seeker. Service Officer must record the job seeker's response to the question 'Does the customer want to talk to the Participation Solutions Team before they finish their report?'.

If the job seeker:

  • does want to discuss with the PST, select 'Yes', and continue with the workflow to submit all earning and circumstance updates. Transfer the job seeker to the PST. The PST will conduct the investigation and complete the report
  • does not want to discuss the failure with the PST, advise the job seeker their payment will be reduced as they have accepted the financial penalty. Select 'No' and continue the workflow to submit all earning and circumstance updates. The Assessment Results (AR) screen will display the reduced rate for the current instalment period. Finalise the activity to complete the report and automatically apply the financial penalty

Job seekers can choose to talk with Services Australia about any failure if they have evidence they would like considered before a financial penalty is applied.

In certain situations, job seekers cannot accept a financial penalty when reporting without talking with Services Australia, including where the mutual obligation failure relates to misconduct or where the penalty would result in payment cancellation.

2

Payment reduction for first and second failure penalties + Read more ...

The financial penalty for a first or second mutual obligation failure in the penalty count is calculated based on the total value of JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA), Parenting Payment (Single) or Special Benefits (SpB) payable, including any supplements paid with the fortnightly instalment, after the income test has been applied.

Supplements affected include:

  • Energy Supplement
  • Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA)
  • Rent Assistance (RA) (when not paid through Family Tax system)
  • Pension Supplement
  • Youth Disability Supplement (YDS)
  • Approved Program of Work Supplement (APWS)
  • Training Supplement
  • National Green Jobs Corps Supplement

The amount of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) payable to the job seeker or their partner is unaffected by a penalty, as is any Rent Assistance (RA) paid with FTB.

3

Date of effect for mutual obligation failure penalties + Read more ...

Generally, a payment reduction for a first or second mutual obligation failure will affect the participation payment for the period in which the mutual obligation failure occurred.

Where a financial penalty for a mutual obligation failure is accepted by the job seeker as part of finalising their reporting, the associated payment reduction will immediately affect the payment due.

Where a Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer conducts a compliance investigation, the financial penalty will immediately affect the payment due.

4

Multiple mutual obligation failures in the same entitlement period + Read more ...

A first and a second financial penalty in the penalty count may be applied in the same entitlement period.

Where a second financial penalty has been applied, the job seeker will not receive a participation payment for this entitlement period. As the job seeker no longer has compulsory requirements for this entitlement period, any subsequent mutual obligation failures cannot be applied for that entitlement period.

Once the record is cleared, background processing automatically removes subsequent mutual obligation failures with an event date in an entitlement period to which a second penalty has been applied.

Before applying the financial penalty for a third mutual obligation failure, Service Officers should check that a second financial penalty has not already been applied for the same entitlement period.

5

Non-compliance cancellations and dates of effect + Read more ...

The date of effect of the payment cancellation resulting from:

  • failure to re-engage within 4 weeks of a mutual obligation failure or work refusal failure is the date the reconnection requirement was notified
  • a third mutual obligation failure or work refusal failure is generally the start of the instalment period in which the failure occurred. Where reporting for this instalment period has already been completed, this cancellation will apply from the start of the next available instalment period
  • an unemployment failure is the next payment period after the decision is made

The non-compliance cancellation codes and reasons are:

  • CDR - a participation suspension has been applied and re-engagement has not occurred within 4 weeks of the reconnection requirement deemed date of notification
  • TCP - Mutual obligation or work refusal penalty
  • TCU - Unemployment failure penalty
  • TCF - record has been suspended for more than 13 weeks following failure to meet a provider requirement and a CDR cancellation has not occurred
  • TCW - record has been suspended for more than 13 weeks following a provider reported work refusal failure and a CDR cancellation has not occurred

Note: non-compliance cancellations are non-restorable. The job seeker will need to reclaim to restart payment.

6

Duration of post-cancellation non-payment period + Read more ...

The post-cancellation non-payment period commences from the date of effect of the non-compliance cancellation.

The duration of the post-cancellation non-payment period for:

  • a third mutual obligation financial penalty is 4 weeks
  • a work refusal failure is 4 weeks
  • an unemployment failure while in receipt of payment is 4 weeks, or 6 weeks where the failure related to a relocation job

7

Unemployment preclusion period + Read more ...

The unemployment preclusion period commences from the date the unemployment failure was committed, which is generally the date of unemployment.

The duration of the unemployment preclusion period is 4 weeks or 6 weeks if the job seeker left a relocation job within the 6 months immediately prior to the unemployment failure.

Job seekers may have self-serve part or all of the unemployment preclusion period prior to claiming payment.

The unemployment preclusion period affects the total amount of participation payment that would otherwise have been payable, including supplementary payments paid with the participation payment. The amount of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) payable to the job seeker or their partner, including Rent Assistance (RA) paid with FTB, is not affected by the unemployment preclusion period.
