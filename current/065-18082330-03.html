Home care means assessment process – pre-entry

Table 1: This table contains the steps to complete a pre-entry home care means assessment in Customer First and the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS).

Step

Action

1

Preliminary check and dates for pre –entry assessment + Read more ...

See Aged care means assessment – preliminary checks to confirm the claim is ready to assess.

Determine the relevant dates for a home care pre-entry means assessment.

  • Date of event (DOV)
  • Date of receipt (DOR)
    • date the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care is received, or
    • date contact is made for a verbal application
  • Date of effect (DOE)
    • for care recipients receiving an income support payment (ISP)

Does this care recipient have a status of RCA/CUR in Customer First?

  • Yes, and the care recipient is:
    • RCA/CUR-MTA, go to Step 2
    • RCA/CUR-DSS or RCA/CUR-SFR, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

2

Care recipient is RCA/CUR-MTA and requests a pre assessment + Read more ...

If the status is RCA/CUR-MTA, the care recipient has previously completed a pre-entry means assessment:

  • Go to RCA Means Test Details (RMND) screen
  • Confirm the means assessment care type

Current pre-entry care type is home care:

  • A new means assessment is not needed
  • Update the existing means assessment with the new income details the care recipient has given
  • The updated information is used when they enter care
  • See Aged care reviews - reassessments and updates for further information
  • Procedure ends here

Current pre-entry care type is residential care:

  • Cancel the current assessment from the Benefit Action (BA) screen:
    • Key ‘RCA’ in the Service Reason field
    • Key ‘CAN’ in the Activity field
    • Key ‘OTH’ in the Reason field
    • Enter 1 day prior to the DOV for the new assessment in the Date of Effect field
  • Go to Step 4

3

RCA/CUR-DSS or RCA/CUR-SFR and pre-entry assessment requested + Read more ...

If there is no discharge coded in ACMPS or System for the Payment of Aged Residential Care (SPARC):

  • An RCA/HOM activity cannot be finalised
  • Cancel any RCA/HOM activities on the AL screen
  • Code a new assessment in an RCA/REA activity

Is the care recipient currently in home care or residential care?

  • Yes:
    • Go to the RMND screen
    • Delete any existing entries
    • Update Reform Enquiry Means Test DOE to date assessment finalised
    • Update Care Type to Home
    • Go to Step 4
  • No:
    • Update RCA Circumstance (RCIRC) screen with discharge details
    • Finalise via the Assessment Results (AR) screen
    • Go to Step 4

4

Check the source of the means assessment + Read more ...

Index an RCA/HOM activity (if required). See the manual processing table in Indexing a customer’s claim on the system for help.

What is the source of the means assessment?

  • Verbal assessment, go to Step 5
  • Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care, see Table 2

5

Verbal applications + Read more ...

When undertaking a verbal application:

  • Ask the care recipient if they want to give their income details to receive a home care means assessment
    • Advise the care recipient maximum aged care fees may be payable if income details are not given
  • Discuss when the care recipient’s Customer First record was last updated
    • Update new information as needed
    • If needed, use the questions on the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care as a guide while on the phone with the care recipient
  • Read the ‘Declaration for the person the assessment is for' to the care recipient and record the response

Can the care recipient complete the home care means assessment verbally? See Aged care means assessment for help.

  • Yes, see Table 3
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Unable to complete an application verbally + Read more ...

Do not complete a verbal application:

  • if further information is needed, or
  • the care recipient does not want to disclose their means

Does the care recipient want to continue with the home care means assessment?

  • Yes, and further information is needed, see Table 2
  • No, and the care recipient:
    • does not want to disclose their means, see the Table 5
    • withdraws their claim, see Step 6 in Table 5

Outstanding information

Action

1

Check Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care application for an authorised person or organisation + Read more ...

If the Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf section on the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care has been completed, and all signatures/documentation are provided, see Appointing nominees to code a nominee. This nominee will be added to all aged care systems through the means assessment.

Check if additional information or documentation is required with the claim + Read more ...

  • Confirm all details in the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care are complete

  • Check all supporting documentation is provided with the application or can be accepted verbally
  • Check that the care recipient or an authorised third party has signed the form. See Aged care – consent and disclosure for more information

Income details supplied as part of a home care means assessment may need verification to confirm the information provided by the care recipient is true and accurate.

For care recipients in receipt of an income support payment (ISP), see Verifying income and assets for supporting documentation needed.

Is more information needed before the assessment can be finalised?

  • Yes, and request for information has:
    • not previously been issued, go to Step 3
    • previously been issued, see Table 5
  • No, see Table 3

3

Collect outstanding information + Read more ...

Additional information can be taken over the phone when no supporting verification documentation is needed to complete the assessment.

Is supporting documentation needed?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Collect information verbally + Read more ...

Call the care recipient, nominee or POA.

Although there is no obligation to provide information verbally, Service Officers must advise the benefits and potential consequences of failing to provide the information.

Note: if the means assessment form contains nominee details, they can be contacted for information.

Can the information be collected verbally?

  • Yes, collect the required information and see Table 3
  • No, and
    • contact was unsuccessful, go to Step 5
    • the care recipient wants a written request for information, go to Step 5
    • verification is needed, go to Step 5
    • the care recipient does not want to disclose their means, see Table 5

5

Issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter + Read more ...

  • Issue an aged care RFI letter, see Aged care letters - creating manual letters
  • Complete a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) referral (if needed)
  • Place claim on hold allowing 21 days plus an allowance for the letter to be received for the care recipient to respond to the request
  • Procedure ends here

Contact and address details

Table 3: This table contains the steps to update contact details for a care recipient and aged care nominee.

Step

Action

1

Dependent children + Read more ...

A care recipient is exempt from paying the home care income tested care fee (ITCF) if they have a dependent child in their care. However, income details must be maintained on the record.

If a care recipient has a dependent child in their care, on the RCA dependants (RDEP) screen record:

  • Number of Dependants Date of Event: DOV of means assessment
  • Total Number of Dependants: enter number of dependent children

See the References page for a link to the Aged Care Act 1997 definition of a dependent child.

2

Update care recipient name and home address details + Read more ...

If the care recipient provides a new home address, telephone details or alternate names:

  • Select the RCA/HOM activity
  • Go to the Contact Detail Task Selector (CDTS) screen
    • Select Customer Details to update alternate names on Customer Person Detail (CPD) screen
    • Select Address to update the care recipient’s home address on the Address Details (AD) screen
  • Update the contact number on the Telephone Details (TD) screen
  • Update the email address on the Email Address (EMA) screen
  • Update the office code back to the Home Office

3

Update care recipient postal address + Read more ...

Update a postal address in Customer First when requested by:

  • a care recipient
  • a court appointed guardian or administrator
  • a power of attorney (POA), or
  • an existing Centrelink correspondence nominee, when the postal address is not the same as their own postal address

A postal address is not updated in Customer First when requested by:

  • an existing Centrelink correspondence nominee, when the postal address is the same as their own postal address

For additional information on updating addresses (including postal) see:

  • Updating address details
  • Appointing nominees
  • Aged care request for a nominee
  • Accepting information from and disclosing information to Power of Attorney

Income and assets details

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Assets and income information + Read more ...

An Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care requests a care recipient to provide details of their income. Supporting documentation may include a history of income and assets.

A care recipient in receipt of an income support payment (ISP):

  • May choose to use the income information currently recorded in Customer First
  • If it is more than 2 years since the existing details were updated, attempt to confirm (by phone) if income or assets need updating
  • If contact is unsuccessful, accept the care recipient’s declaration of income

If the partner has recently died, do not contact the care recipient or nominee. See Bereavement reviews for further information on reviews following the death of a member of a couple.

Has the care recipient advised of any changes to income and/or assets?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Select screens for updating income and assets + Read more ...

Care recipients receiving an ISP may provide details of changes that have occurred before the date of event (DOV) of the assessment. Code these changes as part of the home care means assessment.

The DOV for multiple ISP updates must include:

  • the date of the change – as stated in the supporting documentation
  • the date of lodgement – if further changes have occurred

To update:

  • Select the screens from Residential Care Task Selector (RCTS) screen
    • If data already exists on a screen, a Y appears on the RCTS screen
    • Check the data for accuracy

The coding of income and assets for aged care purposes aligns with the Social Security Act 1991. See the below links for further information:

  • Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services
  • Financial Investments
  • Exempt income and assets for Centrelink payments
  • Assessing deprivation/gifting
  • Foreign income and assets
  • Aged Care means assessment with total assessable assets equal to or greater than $10,000,000 (and applicable assessable income)

3

Check if employment income is received + Read more ...

Does the care recipient (or partner) have employment income?

  • Yes, and care recipient (or partner):
    • receives an ISP, go to Step 4
    • does not receive an ISP, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

4

Code employment income – ISP care recipients + Read more ...

Employment income reporting requirements for a care recipient (or partner) in receipt of an ISP:

  • Statement reporter
    • Must report any income each fortnight before ISP is paid
  • Notification reporter
    • ISP paid automatically and must report any income or other changes within 14 days of the change occurring

ISP care recipients (or partners) should already have employment income coded. If this is the first advice that the care recipient (or partner) receives employment income, see Recording and correcting employment income details for help.

Go to Step 6.

5

Code employment income – self funded retirees + Read more ...

Care recipients not in receipt of an ISP:

  • Do not need to update their income each fortnight
  • Have their income assessed as a notification reporter i.e. must report income or other changes within 14 days
  • If income is variable, average this over a suitable period

See Determining the Date of Event for employment income for help with coding income for notification reporters.

6

Other income sources + Read more ...

A care recipient (or partner) may have income from other sources. For example:

  • rental income for their own home or other real estate
  • income from a business – sole trader or partnership
  • an involvement in a Private Company or Private Trust (including Special Disability Trust)

Does the care recipient have income from real estate or business (sole trader or partnership)?

  • Yes to any of the above, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 10

7

Real estate and business income + Read more ...

Update the Real Estate and Business (REBS) screen if the care recipient (or partner) indicates they have:

  • rental income from real estate, including their principal home
  • income from a business partnership, a farm or from operating as a sole trader

Does the care recipient (or partner) receive rental income from real estate, including their principal home?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Code rental income + Read more ...

For help with coding rental income, see Assessing and coding the Real estate details Mod R and Income from boarders and lodgers

  • Add new property details:
    • Update rental income
    • Code asset value as $0
    • For care recipients in receipt of a means tested ISP, issue a Real Estate details Mod R form (see the Resources page for a link to the form). See Requesting information (CLK)
  • Update existing property details:
    • Update rental income
    • Do not change the previously coded asset value

9

Code business income + Read more ...

When a care recipient (or partner) receives income from a business, include the business income in the home care means assessment.

If previously recorded business income has changed, see Changes to income and assets from a business structure.

Does the care recipient (or partner) receive income from a sole trader business or a business partnership?

  • Yes, code income only then, go to Step 10
    • For sole trader business coding, see Sole traders
    • For business partnership coding, see Partnerships
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Interest in a private trust or company + Read more ...

Does the care recipient have an interest in a private trust or private company?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, see Table 5

11

CAO referral required for private trust or private company + Read more ...

If the care recipient (or partner) has an interest in a private trust or private company:

  • See Trusts or Companies for details on documents needed and Complex Case Officer (CAO) referrals
  • See Identifying and making suitable referrals to the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) for information on referring to a CAO for assessment

In Customer First:

  • Use the CAO Referral guided procedure to complete the CAO referral in the RCA system
  • Place the claim on hold for 28 days pending return from the CAO
  • The CAO will document their assessment on the care recipient record
    • The assessment may include coding instructions for the actioning Service Officer
  • When the CAO assessment is complete, see Table 5

Finalise the RCA/HOM activity

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Reject or withdraw claim + Read more ...

Is this claim being rejected or withdrawn?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check RCA circumstance (RCIRC) screen + Read more ...

Check the RCIRC screen fields are populated as below:

  • DSS Require Income Details displays Yes
  • Date of Effect displays the date of receipt (DOR) of the assessment
  • Update the DOE if required

3

Update the RCA means test details (RMND) screen + Read more ...

Aged Care pre-entry assessments are valid for a maximum of 120 days from the date the assessment is completed.

When the activity is completed, the DOE defaults to the date the activity is completed.

If the assessment is completed:

  • the same day as the DOR, the data on this screen is correct
  • after the DOR, the screen needs correcting

To add the validity period start date, go to the RMND screen and update:

  • Enter the date assessment was completed in the Reform Enquiry Means Test DOE field
  • Key ‘Y’ in the Reform Enquiry Means Test Ind field
  • Key ‘HOM’ in the Reform Enquiry Care Type field

Existing Reform Enquiry Means Test data prevents the above coding. In these cases:

  • Key ‘D’ to delete any previous and existing data
  • Press [ENTER]
  • Key ‘Y‘ to confirm deletion and
  • Prior to pressing [ENTER] key ‘RMND’ in the Nxt field
  • Press [ENTER]
  • Update the blank Reform Enquiry Means Test DOE entry

Is this assessment being finalised as means not disclosed (MND)?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 6

4

Finalise the RCA/HOM as means not disclosed (MND) + Read more ...

Care recipients pay the full amount for the cost of their care if they choose not to disclose their income.

Finalise the means assessment as MND:

  • Select the RCA/HOM activity
  • Go to the RMND screen and update:
    • Enter the DOR of the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care in the Means Not Disclosed Date of Event field
    • Key ‘Y’ in the Means Not Disclosed Indicator field
    • Enter the date assessment was completed in the Reform Enquiry Means Test DOE field
    • Key ‘Y’ in the Reform Enquiry Means Test Ind field
    • Key ‘HOM’ in the Reform Enquiry Care Type field
  • Existing Reform Enquiry Means Test data prevents the above coding. To display a blank Reform Enquiry Means Test entry to update:
    • Key ‘D’ to delete data (current and historical)
    • Press [ENTER]
    • Key ‘Y’ to confirm deletion
    • Before pressing [ENTER], key ‘RMND’ in the Nxt field
    • Press [ENTER]
  • Access the Fast Note workflow. Use Fast Note - Auto Text > Aged Care > Assessment > HOM Care Assessment – FIN to document MND action taken
  • Go to Step 7

5

Reject or withdraw a home care pre-entry means assessment + Read more ...

A pre-entry means assessment is rejected if:

  • an aged care RFI was issued and the care recipient has not provided documentation within 21 days (plus an allowance for the letter to be received), or
  • a care recipient withdraws their means assessment request

To reject the means assessment:

  • Key ‘S’ in the RCA/HOM activity
  • Go to the BA screen
  • Code the fields:
    • Key ‘RCA’ in the SVC RSN field
    • Key ‘REJ’ in the Action field
    • Key ‘OTH’ in the Reason field
    • Enter the Date of Event (DOV) in the Effect Date field
    • Key ‘REJ-OTH RCA/HOM REJ – failed to provide information’ or ‘REJ-OTH Home Care pre-entry assessment withdrawn’ in the Notes field

6

Attach Auto-text Fast Note to claim activity + Read more ...

When the claim activity coding is complete, attach a Fast Note to record the assessment outcome.

Before finalising the activity:

  • Access the Fast Note workflow. Use Fast Note:
    • Finalised claims – Auto Text > Aged Care > Assessment > HOM Care Assessment – FIN to document the assessment outcome
    • Rejected claims – Auto text>Aged Care>Assessment>Aged Care MA Rejected-OTHER to record the action taken
    • Withdrawn claims - Auto text Aged Care>Assessment>Aged Care means assessment Withdrawn
  • Link the Fast Note to the open RCA/HOM activity
  • Complete the applicable Fast Note fields
  • Clearly document other relevant information, for example:
    • If the assessment was completed as a verbal declaration
    • If an update was made that differs from the information lodged (for example a care recipient failed to advise of a bank account balance on the Aged Care – Calculation of your cost of care form but later provides a verbal balance)

7

Assessment result (AR) + Read more ...

When the Fast Note is complete:

  • Key ‘S’ in the RCA/HOM activity from Activity List (AL) screen and navigate to the AR screen
  • Check the assessment results on AR:
    • the first ATH line and RCA Benefit line have a ‘Period End’ date equal to the date of event (DOV) of the assessment
    • any arrears or overpayments calculated are correct (based on DOV and date of receipt (DOR))

Is the means assessment MND or rejected?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Additional action for MND and rejected means assessments + Read more ...

MND

  • A pre-entry assessment letter is not issued when the MND indicator is Yes
  • If the care recipient asks for a letter, advise them:
    • ‘As you have elected not to disclose your means we are unable to issue you a letter until you commence receiving care. You may need to negotiate the cost of your care directly with the service provider.’

Rejected

  • Issue a letter to the care recipient and nominee using the AC - Request Further Information - Final notification - CR-NM template
  • Note: a letter is not issued for withdrawn claims.

9

Follow up action + Read more ...

Follow up action may include:

  • Update financial disclosure permission where required – see Aged care – consent and disclosure
  • Complete any progress of claim DOCs or scans
  • Contact the care recipient if the assessment results in a significant reduction to any Income Support Payments (ISP), see Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK)
  • If the assessment generates a social security debt needing investigation, clearly document the details relating to the potential debt for the investigating Service Officer
  • Check if further action is needed on the partner’s record, including an outstanding RCA/HOM activity
  • If the means assessment is completed verbally, issue an Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf form (SS313)
  • See Appointing nominees or Aged care request for a nominee
