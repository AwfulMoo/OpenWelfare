3

Criteria to be reviewed + Read more ...

Do any of the following criteria apply to the applicant and/or student's circumstances?

  • the applicant or the applicant's partner qualifies for one of the prescribed forms of Commonwealth Government assistance
  • the applicant is taken to be receiving their Income support payment during an employment nil rate period
  • the student would, but for age (that is, under the minimum school leaving age), qualify for independent status as an orphan, a student whose parent(s) cannot exercise parental responsibilities, or as a homeless student
  • the student is in State care, under the minimum school leaving age and not eligible for away from home entitlements
  • the applicant or the applicant's partner is the holder of a current Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) or an automatic issue Health Care Card (HCC), including an HCC issued on the basis of receipt of maximum rate Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A or an extension HCC

When exemptions do not occur

An exemption does not occur for ABSTUDY:

  • for a period after the expiry date on the card
  • if an applicant holds a HCC because they receive a social security Mobility Allowance (MOB) or Carer Allowance (CA) (in respect of a disabled child)
  • if a Foster Child HCC (FST) has been issued to an applicant in respect of another child in their care, that is, not the ABSTUDY student (if an applicant is a holder of a Foster Child HCC for an ABSTUDY student, the student/applicant may be eligible under the State care criteria), or
  • if the applicant or the applicant's partner is not in receipt of an Income support payment, and holds only a Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) or a Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC)

4

Attendance requirement for Term 1 + Read more ...

If the student is/was enrolled in and attending a secondary school in Term 1 of the school year, School Term Allowance is payable for Term 1 automatically.

Is/was the student enrolled in, and attending in Term 1 of the school year?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

5

Customer is eligible for School Term Allowance in Term 1 + Read more ...

The first term School Term Allowance is automatically paid if the student is recorded as enrolled and attending a secondary school in Term 1 of the year. To pay School Term Allowance for Term 1, ensure the date used at the Student Start Date and Course Start Date fields on the Customer Study Details (EDC) screen in Process Direct or Education Course Details (EDC) screen in Customer First is a date valid for Term 1 of the school year.

Note: the term entitlement period is used to assess for qualification for School Term Allowance in a particular term. However, the Student Start Date coded should still be the actual date the student commenced study for the school year.

Go to Step 6.

6

Attendance requirements for Terms 2, 3 or 4 + Read more ...

A student must attend at least 85% of the required days in the term prior to that for which payment is assessed. Evidence of attendance of 85 per cent or more in the previous term(s) must be received by Services Australia before payments will be made.

Students who do not have an attendance requirement for their study (home schooling, distance education or non-school institutions such as TAFE) will be required to establish they have completed the workload requirements of the course. Students undertaking Home Schooling in NSW will be required to confirm a current registration in order to meet the 85% attendance requirement for payment of ABSTUDY School Term Allowance in the relevant term.

Note: due to coronavirus (COVID-19) disrupting normal study arrangements for many students during 2020, payment of STA in Term 3 and 4 2020 requires a student to have attendance of 85% or more in Term 1 of 2020. An attendance check for attendance in Term 2 and 3 2020 was not undertaken. If the student has not attended 85% in Term 1 2020, the student will not be eligible for payment of STA in Terms 2, 3 and 4 in 2020.

See Processing ABSTUDY School Term Allowance payments for guidance.

Has the school advised the student attended 85% of the required school days in the term previous to the one for which payment is being assessed?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, see Step 2 in Table 2

7

Customer is eligible for School Term Allowance for Terms 2, 3 or 4 + Read more ...

A payment for Term 2, 3 or 4 is payable by recording the previous term's satisfactory attendance result, see Processing ABSTUDY School Term Allowance payments

Record the attendance result in either:

  • Process Direct:
    • Go to the Education Payments School Attendance (EYSA) screen
    • In the Action column select the correct year
    • Edit the relevant Term
    • In the Attendance field select Satisfactory
    • Select Save
    • Go to the Entitlements (ELD) screen
    • Key the Receipt Date and Channel. Select Save
    • Select Assess and address any errors/warnings
    • Select Assess
    • Select Finish. Record details of the update
    • Select Finalise
  • Customer First:
    • Go to the Education Payments School Attendance (EYSA) screen
    • Select the appropriate year and the terms will display
    • In the Attendance menu, select the Select AN Option for the relevant term
    • Select SAT or UNSAT depending on attendance result provided by the school

The system has inbuilt edits to prevent coding of a future term or year for which attendance results could not have yet been completed. See the Resources page for more information.

If the student has not attended 85% in Term 1 2020, code Unsatisfactory/UNSAT on the EYSA screen for Term 2, Term 3 and Term 4 in 2020.

Note: for payment of Term 3 and 4 2020 STA payment, record Satisfactory/SAT if the student had 85% attendance or more in Term 1 2020.

Record details of the decision on a Note/DOC:

  • STA for term 2/3/4, 20XX (delete terms that are not applicable)
  • Attendance: Satisfactory
  • School name:
  • Status: ABT/CUR (Current)
  • EYSA screen coded SAT: Y/N
  • STA payment released: record details of term/s, year and amounts
  • APM 84.2 & APM 84.3 refer

Procedure ends here.

Processing when a customer is not eligible for School Term Allowance

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Customer is not eligible for School Term Allowance in Term 1 + Read more ...

If the student is not enrolled and attending school in Term 1, School Term Allowance is not payable for that term. To prevent School Term Allowance paying for Term 1, ensure the Student Start Date on the Customer Study Details (EDC) screen in Process Direct or Education Course Details (EDC) screen in Customer First is a date after the end of Term 1 in the school year.

Note: the Student Start Date should still be the actual date the student commenced study for the school year.

Procedure ends here.

2

Customer is not eligible for School Term Allowance + Read more ...

If the student has not attended 85% in either Term 2, 3 or 4 of the school year, code attendance as unsatisfactory for the relevant terms.

Code the attendance in either:

  • Process Direct:
    • Go to the Education Payments School Attendance (EYSA) screen
    • In the Action column select the correct year
    • Edit the relevant Term
    • In the Attendance field select Unsatisfactory
    • Select Save
    • Key the Receipt Date and Channel. Select Save
    • Select Assess and address any errors/warnings
    • Select Assess
    • Select Finish. Record details of the update
    • Select Finalise
  • Customer First:
    • Go to the Education Payments School Attendance (EYSA) screen
    • Select the appropriate year and the terms will display
    • In the Attendance menu, select Unsatisfactory for the relevant term
    • Finalise the activity on the Assessment Result (AR) screen

Note: due to COVID-19 disrupting normal study arrangements for many students during 2020, qualification for payment of STA in Term 3 and 4 2020 requires a student to have attendance of 85% or more in Term 1 of 2020. If the student has not attended 85% in Term 1 2020, code Unsatisfactory/UNSAT for Terms 2, 3 and 4 in 2020.

Record details of the decision on a Note/DOC:

  • STA for term 2/3/4, 20XX (delete terms that are not applicable)
  • Attendance: Unsatisfactory
  • School name:
  • Status: ABT/CUR (Current)
  • EYSA screen coded UNS: Y/N
  • STA payment not released
  • APM 84.2 & APM 84.3 refer

Procedure ends here.

3

Student is not eligible for School Term Allowance + Read more ...

Tell the customer that if there are any changes in their circumstances, they should inform the Services Australia within 14 days, and their situation will be reviewed.

Note: while staff can give general advice based on information provided by the customer, encourage them to test their eligibility by lodging a claim. Do this via the ABSTUDY Claiming Tool or by Completing the Claim for ABSTUDY Schooling A (SY018).

Record details of the decision on a Note/DOC.
