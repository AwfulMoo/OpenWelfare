Verification of 10 years qualifying residence + Read more ...

Qualification for some income support payments, for example Age Pension and Disability Support Pension, require 10 years residency or a qualifying residence exemption (QRE).

Proof of arrival should be requested from Centrelink International Services (CIS) if the customer is reliant on a period prior to 1 September 1994 being counted towards the 10 years and does not have a QRE.

3

Confirmation of Proof of Arrival + Read more ...

Proof of arrival (POA) is used for the customer's commencement of identity when the customer is born outside Australia.

POA may also be used to determine that a claimant meets the qualifying residence requirement.

In order for a customer to obtain a Confirmed Identity they must provide original POA documents face to face to Services Australia, see Coding identity documents.

Only where an assessment of Alternative Identity Confirmation is required can the information from the Department of Home Affairs datalink be used to support this assessment.

Note: CIS only action requests where POA is required for a customer born outside Australia.

Customers should not be referred to the Department of Home Affairs to obtain arrival details in Australia. This information can be obtained by CIS.

Is the customer able to provide a valid document used to establish Commencement of Identity?

  • Yes,
    • and customer has all required identity documents, code documents using the Identity Confirmation Guided Procedure, see Coding identity documents. Procedure ends here
    • and customer has some identity documents and will return at a later time with further documentation, code documents available using the Identity Confirmation Guided Procedure, see Coding identity documents. Procedure ends here
    • to assess qualifying residence requirement run the Residence Verification script. The script must be used when available. Procedure ends here
  • No, and the customer requires assessment for Alternative Identity Confirmation, run the Residence Verification script. Go to Step 4

4

Residence Verification script + Read more ...

For processing within Customer First, Customer Record or mainframe/ISIS the Residence Verification script will automatically request information from the Department of Home Affairs through the Immigration Datalink. Ensure that the legacy Customer Person Detail (CPD) screen is updated with all relevant information as the script uses these details to search for the customer.

If information is available through the datalink, the information will be provided immediately through the script. In this situation, POA can be recorded immediately for the customer.

For claims within Process Direct, the script is not available.

Is the script available?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 5

Record Proof of Arrival (POA + Read more ...

Where an assessment of Alternative Identity Confirmation is required the information from the Immigration Datalink OR details on the customer's POA document can be used to support this assessment. Refer to the following manual coding:

Go to the legacy Proof of Identity (POI) screen:

  • Code 'VI' in the Code field

  • Code 'AU' in the Ctry field

  • Press [Enter]

2

Record a Proof of Arrival (POA) referral to CIS + Read more ...

In Process Direct create a new referral for Centrelink International Services.

  • Record as many customer details as possible in the referral, in the following format:
    • PPT: passport number/country of issue if available
    • Name: correct spelling of name (if married, maiden name as well)
    • Alias: all known aliases
    • DOA: date of arrival if known, otherwise approximate
    • POA: place/port of arrival
    • MOT: mode of transport, i.e., name of ship or airline and flight number (if possible)
    • PAR: if customer arrived as a child with parents, full names and dates of birth of parents
    • SIB: if there were siblings record their names and dates of birth
  • Select Finish to send the referral to Centrelink International Services (CIS)

In Customer First, Customer Record or mainframe/ISIS create a DOC with the summary line POA to be verified by CIS using the keyword POA. Note: do not complete the DOC.

  • Record as many customer details as possible on the DOC, in the following format:
    • PPT: passport number/country of issue if available
    • Name: correct spelling of name (if married, maiden name as well)
    • Alias: all known aliases
    • DOA: date of arrival if known, otherwise approximate
    • POA: place/port of arrival
    • MOT: mode of transport, i.e., name of ship or airline and flight number (if possible)
    • PAR: if customer arrived as a child with parents, full names and dates of birth of parents
    • SIB: if there were siblings record their names and dates of birth
  • Transfer the DOC to region WSI (this is a Centrelink International Services (CIS) office code)
  • Arrivals after 1965 need the customer's name, variations (if any) and date of arrival. Arrivals before 1965 also require the customer's ship or flight details and the port at which they arrived
  • The agreed turnaround time for a response from CIS is ten days. While awaiting confirmation of POA, consider granting the payment (when eligibility has been confirmed) using Alternative Identity. Ensure the customer's identity before using Alternative Identity Confirmation
  • If the customer is in hardship contact CIS to advise that an urgent response is required. For contact details, see Centrelink International Services (CIS) - contact details for staff

Is residence information prior to 1 September 1994 also required?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, when the response is received, go to Step 4

3

Residence information prior to 1 September 1994 + Read more ...

Where the Service Officer determines that the Department of Home Affairs information prior to 1 September 1994 is required, record a DOC on the customer's record.

On the DOC record the appropriate reason for requesting the POA information:

  • to verify 10 years Australian residence for Age Pension or Disability Support Pension (DSP), or
  • to verify periods of qualifying Australian residence prior to 1 September 1994 to correct the Country of Residence (CRES) screen, or
  • to verify Working Life Residence (WLR)

Include as much detail as possible as to the reason for referral, including:

  • any alternative names, including alternative spelling, used by the customer
  • details of travel documents, including passports, held by the customer

When the response is received, go to Step 4.

4

Actioning referral when transferred back to the service centre + Read more ...

The following procedures will apply when the DOC or referral is transferred back to the originating service centre.

Can CIS confirm the date of arrival?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Arrival has been verified + Read more ...

Check the DOC or referral from CIS to determine whether the verification was obtained from the Department of Home Affairs visa records or through a citizenship request to the Department of Home Affairs.

Has verification been obtained from the Department of Home Affairs database or microfiche records or through a citizenship request from the Department of Home Affairs?

  • For verification obtained from the Department of Home Affairs database or microfiche records, go to Step 6
  • For verification obtained through a citizenship request from the Department of Home Affairs, go to Step 7

6

CIS confirms date of arrival from the Department of Home Affairs database or microfiche records + Read more ...

The Service Officer must enter the information from the referral onto the legacy Proof of Identity (POI) screen.

Go to the legacy POI Document Selection (PDS) screen and 'S'elect the 'VI' Australian Visa line. The legacy POI screen will display. Code the fields with following details:

  • In the Reg/Serial No field code details as provided by CIS or if no visa number available, code 'POA confirmed by CIS'
  • The date as advised by CIS in the Iss/Exp date field
  • 'NAT' in the State field
  • 'AU' in the Ctry field
  • The date as advised by CIS in the Date Arrived field
  • Procedure ends here

7

CIS confirms arrival from Department of Home Affairs citizenship records + Read more ...

When the response has been received from CIS, record the relevant details:

  • Go to the legacy PDS screen and 'S'elect the 'AC' Australian Citizenship line
  • The legacy POI screen will display. Code the following fields on the 'AC' line:
    • Reg/serial No code 'DIAC confirmed'
    • The date as advised by the Department of Home Affairs in the Iss/Exp Date field
    • 'NAT' in the State field
    • 'AU' in the Ctry field

Procedure ends here.

8

Proof of arrival not confirmed by CIS + Read more ...

If no record can be found regarding the customer's arrival in Australia, the Service Officer must determine if Alternative Identity applies.

Can Alternative Identity Confirmation be used?

  • Yes, Grant the claim using Alternative Identity Confirmation
  • No, POA cannot be coded. Advise the customer to provide additional information within 14 days to confirm their arrival in Australia. If the requested information is not provided after 14 days, reject the claim insufficient identity documentation. Procedure ends here

Customer is an Australian Citizen + Read more ...

Confirmation of Citizenship can be obtained from the Department of Home Affairs Citizenship branch.

  • the Department of Home Affairs will only confirm one customer per form
  • Completing this form will send an automatic email request to the Department of Home Affairs to confirm the customer's citizenship. The Resources page has a link to the form
  • A copy of the request can be printed by clicking on the print button at the bottom of the form

On return of information from the Department of Home Affairs Citizenship Branch, has citizenship been confirmed?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, advise the originating service centre, citizenship cannot be confirmed. Procedure ends here

9

Confirmation of POA has been found + Read more ...

  • If the claim is within Process Direct, update the claim notes in Process Direct, advising whether POA has been confirmed, what information was located and where. Change the claim status to In Progress so that the claim activity will be reallocated
  • If the claim is in Customer First, Customer Record or mainframe/ISIS record a DOC on the customer's record advising whether POA has been confirmed, what information was located and where. This DOC does not need to be transferred to the processing team and can be closed
  • If the customer is a New Zealand citizen, ensure that the New Zealand residence status: field on the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen has been completed

If the customer is verified as an Australian resident, ensure that the Aust citizenship verification ind: field on the Residence Savings (RSS) screen is coded. This will ensure that the service centre doesn't have to request this to be coded for residence purposes as only CIS are able to update this field.
