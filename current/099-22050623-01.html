Processing Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV) claims 099-22050623

This document explains how to process Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV) in Process Direct. This includes online claims, Assisted Customer Claims (ACC) and existing customers. PBV claims are processed by a specialist team.

Processing PBV in Process Direct

Use Process Direct to review the PBV claim information and to process the claim once all required information is available.

A PBV work item is created when:

  • a JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) claim has been submitted online or via Assisted Customer Claims (ACC) and the person’s partner has died within the last 4 weeks, or
  • the notification of death has been actioned for a member of a couple. This will generate a Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity on the surviving partner’s record

Process Direct:

  • contains information provided by the customer or their correspondence nominee online or recorded using ACC
  • loads provisional information into the PBV claim activity
  • lists any documents the customer has been asked to provide
  • has functions to view scanned documents, request information and make referrals

RapidConnect and mutual obligation requirements

Customers who apply for PBV are exempt from RapidConnect and mutual obligations for 14 weeks from the date of the partner’s death.

Automatic claim processing

PBV claims are not eligible for automatic claim processing and will be allocated to a specialist team.

Customer contact

If a customer asks for an explanation or applies for a formal review of the decision, the Service Officer must enter their own logon as the Decision Maker when running the script.

If the claim was incorrectly granted, give feedback to the Business Enablement and Quality Branch.

Reassessment of PBV claims

Customers can request a reassessment of their claim within 13 weeks of rejection. If the customer contacts more than 13 weeks after the rejection letter was sent, they must reclaim JobSeeker Payment (JSP).

Make sure all required information is available before re-indexing the original claim.

Separate procedures apply if PBV was rejected due to:

  • not supplying documents (FSD)
  • not lodging or reporting on time (DNL, FRP)

Customers can request a reassessment of rate of PBV if they were paid at the non-pregnant rate and later notifies they are pregnant. They must provide medical evidence before the reassessment of rate can occur.

Intent to claim for vulnerable customers

Customers are considered vulnerable if they experienced a vulnerable circumstance at any time in the 8 weeks before contact and this was the reason they were unable to submit their claim on the contact date. See Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.
