Do not record or scan any information about the suspected fraud and corruption on the record of the person the report is being made against or the informant.

A member of the public attends a service centre with documents supporting a report of suspected fraud and corruption they have submitted

Take photocopies of the documents. Add a cover note with the details of the person suspected of fraud and corruption including:

  • name
  • date of birth or approximate age
  • address
  • gender
  • date the report was submitted

A member of the public attends a service centre with documents wanting to report suspected fraud and corruption

Send documents to the Tip-off Operations team. Add a cover note with the details of the person suspected of fraud and corruption including:

  • name
  • date of birth or approximate age
  • address
  • gender
  • state that no report has been submitted

Confirm the person is happy to be contacted and include the details on the cover note.

Send the documents by internal mail to the Tip-off Operations team.

Health suspected fraud and corruption

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Determine who is reporting suspected fraud and corruption + Read more ...

  • To report suspected fraud and corruption identified as part of your normal duties go to Step 2
  • To help a member of the public to report suspected fraud and corruption go to Step 3

2

Report suspected fraud and corruption + Read more ...

Do not:

  • search records of the person the report is being made against to complete the report
  • give out any information located on the customer's record, or
  • advise that a report is being submitted

For allegations of health provider fraud and corruption complete a report using the Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption system. Include the following information, if known:

  • Provider number
  • Approval number
  • Name of provider / practice staff member
  • Name of practice
  • Address of practice
  • Customer name
  • Medicare number
  • Nature of offending

Attach all relevant detail, documents or supporting information about the suspected fraud and corruption to the report, for example:

  • Forms/Documents/Photo
  • CCTV Details
  • Call Recording Details

For suspected customer fraud and/or corruption, contact a Programme Support Officer (PSO). If recommended by the PSO, complete a report using the Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption system. Include the following information, if known:

  • Customer name
  • Medicare number
  • Reason for suspicion/nature of contact
  • phone number they are calling from (for Service Delivery calls)

Attach all relevant detail, documents or supporting information about the suspected fraud and corruption to the report, for example:

  • Forms/Documents/Photo
  • CCTV Details
  • Call Recording Details

For further information, see the Resources page for a list of Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs).

3

Helping a member of the public to report suspected fraud and corruption + Read more ...

Health provider fraud and corruption is fraud and corruption committed by health providers or practice staff. The customer must provide any supporting documents directly to the Department of Health and Aged Care. Provide the contact details to the customer.

Child Support suspected fraud and corruption

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Determine who is reporting suspected fraud and corruption + Read more ...

  • To report suspected fraud and corruption identified as part of your normal duties go to Step 2
  • To help a member of the public to report suspected fraud and corruption go to Step 3

2

Report Suspected Fraud and corruption + Read more ...

Complete a report using the Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption system for fraud including:

  • making false or misleading statements
  • use of false names or documents
  • employee/employer collusion
  • failure to meet employer obligations
  • impersonating an officer
  • offences relating to Departure Prohibition Orders
  • Organisation has failed to remit in line with s72A notice or Employer withholding

Attach all relevant detail, documents or supporting information about the suspected fraud and corruption to the report, for example:

  • Forms/Documents/Photo
  • CCTV Details
  • Call Recording Details

See Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Note: EF1, s72A (failure to deduct), s72B, s120 or s161 must be referred by Employer Services only. Refer to Child Support information gathering powers for more information.

The referring officer will receive an email to advise if the report has been accepted or rejected.

The notepad on the Client Profile/Communication window will be updated to reflect that the report was either accepted or rejected, see Documentation Cuba Process Help, These reports are highly valued by Services Australia as an important method for fraud and corruption detection.

3

Helping a member of the public to report suspect fraud and corruption + Read more ...

Members of the public can report by:

  • Services Australia website
  • telephone
  • mail

See the Resources page for website and contact details.

The Service Officer may help the member of the public by completing the Child Support report fraud form on their behalf. See the Resources page for a link.

If there are supporting documents go to Step 4.

4

Documents + Read more ...

Do not record any information about the suspected fraud and corruption on the record of the person the report is being made against.

Any information documented in Cuba may be released to the customer under a freedom of information (FOI) request, including the Service Officer's name.

A member of the public attends a service centre with documents supporting a report of suspected fraud and corruption they have submitted

Take photocopies of the documents. Add a cover note with a blank piece of paper that states the details of the person suspected of fraud and corruption if known including:

  • name
  • date of birth or approximate age
  • address
  • gender and
  • the date the report was submitted

A member of the public attends a service centre with documents wanting to submit a tip-off

Send documents to the Tip-off Operations team. Add a cover note with a blank piece of paper that states the details of the person suspected of fraud and corruption if known including:

  • name
  • date of birth or approximate age
  • address
  • gender
  • state that no report has been submitted

If the person has given consent to be contacted further, make sure this information is included on the cover note.
