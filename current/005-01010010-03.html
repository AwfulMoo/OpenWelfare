Initial contact about claiming a payment

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Contact in relation to an intended claim and date of qualification + Read more ...

There are several ways Services Australia may be contacted in relation to a refugee, humanitarian entrant or protection visa holder's intent to claim.
The Humanitarian Settlement Program (HSP) service provider, the customer or a third party may contact to claim a payment on the date of qualification. This contact may be by phone, in person, mail or fax.

See Contact in relation to an intended claim for more information.

For a person to make a legal claim for Australian social security payments they must be in Australia and an Australian resident at the time of lodging the claim. There are a few exceptions to this rule. See Assessing if a customer is an Australian resident.

Is the customer under the age of 18 and not have a parent or relative over the age of 21 to look after them in Australia?

  • Yes, the customer is considered an unaccompanied humanitarian minor (UHM). See determining financial assistance for unaccompanied humanitarian minor. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Search for the customer's record + Read more ...

Access the Search (IN) screen and enter the customer's surname, first name and date of birth. Press [Enter]. A search should include any other names the customer may have been known as (for example maiden name).

For more information, see Searching for a customer on the system.

Does a record exist?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, see Adding a customer to the system, to create a customer record. Once customer is added go to Step 3

3

Customer holds a temporary protection visa subclass + Read more ...

Is the customer holding a temporary protection visa subclass (449, 785, 786 or 790)?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Visa subclass 449, 785, 786 or 790 + Read more ...

The customer may be eligible for Special Benefit and Family Tax Benefit.

To claim Special Benefit, a customer can lodge a claim online, Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) or paper claim.

Offer ACC to customer. The SpB online and ACC are located under:
Make a Claim > Other payments > Special Benefit > Apply for Special Benefit

If the customer selects the paper claim option, issue the Special Benefit (SU004) paper claim. Resources has a link to the SU004 form.

Advise customer the following must be provided for the claim:

  • Confirm identity
  • Tax File Numbers for customer (and partner if applicable)
  • Bank statements for all accounts from the last 3 months for customer (and partner if applicable)
  • Lease Agreement or Rent Certificate (if applicable)

Go to Step 6.

5

Arrival under the Humanitarian Program + Read more ...

Establish the date the customer arrived on, or was granted a refugee, humanitarian or protection visa (including those arriving on a visa subclass 202 under the Community Support Program):

If the customer has been in Australia for:

  • less than 13 weeks, go to Step 7
  • more than 13 weeks, or if customer is claiming one of the following payments, offer Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) and refer to mainstream channels:
    • Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance
    • JobSeeker Payment
    • Disability Support Pension
    • Youth Allowance (full time student)
  • procedure ends here

6

Vulnerable customer + Read more ...

Is the customer considered to be in vulnerable circumstances?

For further information, refer to Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.

  • Yes, record the contact with the customer in a DOC, including the details in relation to why the customer is in a vulnerable circumstance. If there is an existing DOC outlining the customers vulnerability, record details of subsequent contact and see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Customer is not considered to be in vulnerable circumstances + Read more ...

If the customer arrived under the humanitarian entrant under the CSP and:

  • is not applying for a payment, See Refugee and humanitarian entrant service offer and offer a:
    • referral to Workforce Australia Online as a volunteer
    • claim for Low Income Health Care Card (LIC)
  • is applying for a payment, go to Step 8

All other entrants, to receive an income support payment or service from the agency, a customer must lodge a completed claim using an online claim, an ACC or a paper form. See Claim lodgement of Centrelink claims.

Alternatively, the customer may wish to attend a new claim appointment with the Zone Refugee Servicing team. Note the claim is assessed from the date the completed claim with all supporting documentation is lodged and accepted.

Does customer want to attend appointment with Zone Refugee Servicing team?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, direct customer to lodge claim through mainstream channels

8

CSP entrant applies for payment + Read more ...

Is the customer (assuree) subject to an AoS when applying for an AoS recoverable payment?

  • Yes, go to Assuree makes a claim for an Assurance of Support (AoS) recoverable payment. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

9

CSP entrant applies for non-recoverable payment + Read more ...

If a customer applies for a payment that is not an AoS recoverable payment, no debt will be incurred for the AoS assurer.

Customers can apply for the following non-recoverable payments:

  • Family Tax Benefit:
    • run the Assisted Customer Claim workflow
    • complete a Letter of Authority - for persons granted a humanitarian/refugee visa (SS435), and
    • see Table 1, Step 9 and Step 10 in Refugee and humanitarian entrant service offer
  • Disability Support Pension DSP
  • Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA)
  • See Refugee and humanitarian entrant service offer and determine if the customer is eligible for another payment

Procedure ends here

Booking a new claim appointment

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Booking a new claim appointment and interpreter + Read more ...

Contact the Zone or National Refugee Servicing team.

  • Arrange a new claim appointment (which is generally made within 3 days of contact) at the relevant Refugee Servicing team site
  • If contact is unsuccessful, create an ACTDOC and add keywords 'REFCLAIM' and 'ACTREF' and advise the customer they will be contacted within 48 hours to arrange a new claim appointment
  • The Refugee Servicing team will organise an interpreter in the preferred language (dialect) of the customer if needed. See Booking an interpreter for an appointment
  • Record details of the appointment in a DOC on the customer's record

2

Advice to Humanitarian Settlement Program provider or third party and customer + Read more ...

Advise that if the customer meets the qualification today, they have 14 days after today to lodge their claim. If they lodge within the 14 days this contact may be considered the date of claim, see Intent to claim and vulnerable customers. The start date is calculated on the day a claim is made. In some circumstances, this may be earlier or later depending on factors such as backdating provisions, waiting periods or special circumstances.

If the customer experiences difficulty completing claim forms, the Service Officer should assist. See Standards for helping customers complete claims and forms.

At the new claim interview, the customer must provide relevant identity documents and other documents. For example, ImmiCard, travel document, bank account details, if available.

Customers with a qualifying humanitarian visa for Crisis Payment will have their entitlement for Crisis Payment assessed at their new claim interview.

Visa subclass 866 Protection holders are not entitled to a Crisis Payment. See:

  • Eligibility for Crisis Payment (CrP)
  • New claim for Crisis Payment for humanitarian entrants newly arrived in Australia

3

Other available payments + Read more ...

Newly arrived refugees with children may wish to test their eligibility for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

Children aged 16 or over must satisfy the education/training requirements or have an exemption.

Children aged 16 or over may wish to test their eligibility for Youth Allowance (YA).

Customers may be eligible for Double Orphan Pension if they are caring for a child who is a refugee and both parents are living outside of Australia or their whereabouts are unknown.
