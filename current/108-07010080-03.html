Recording FEG and GEERS payments

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer receiving FEG or GEERS payments + Read more ...

Services Australia is advised of recipients of the FEG or GEERS funding on a regular basis through liaison between the Department of Education, Skills and Employment and the National Business Gateway.

The National Business Gateway runs a script to identify customers receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and/or a payment affected by an Income Maintenance Period (IMP) who have received a payment through FEG or GEERS.

  • If the customer or partner is in receipt of JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA), Parenting Payment (Partnered or Single), Disability Support Pension (DSP) (except customers who are permanently blind), Austudy and Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and/or Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and benefit status is current (CUR), suspended (SUS) or assessed (ASS), a Q053 is sent by National Business Gateway. An ACTDOC is created and a ZIAM_FEG_LPG work item will generate to be allocated to staff possessing the NV_DHS_General Layer phase 10 Workload Management skill tag for action. Go to Step 2
  • If the customer or partner is receiving FTB and/or CCS only. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • If there is a DOC advising of a Department of Education, Skills and Employment overpayment, see Step 4 in Table 2

2

The ACTDOC is actioned at the service centre + Read more ...

Has the customer already advised of amounts received?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Contact customer (via phone or letter) requesting further information + Read more ...

Are details available immediately?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, set up a review to check customer has returned required information. When details are provided, go to Step 4

4

Receiving payment affected by IMP? + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving a payment affected by an IMP?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Calculate the IMP based on the FEG or GEERS Annual Leave, Pay in lieu of notice, Redundancy and Long Service Leave components received + Read more ...

This information appears in the ACTDOC, however the payment amounts must be confirmed with the customer.

Determine if an IMP will be imposed for customer due to the receipt of the FEG or GEERS payment. Update customer's record as required.

6

Customer affected by LAWP? + Read more ...

Is customer affected by the Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP)?

Note: this only applies to JSP, YA and Austudy customers.

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Determine if a LAWP will be imposed for customer due to the receipt of the FEG or GEERS payment + Read more ...

Update customer's record as required.

8

Record details of decision + Read more ...

Record details of decision on a DOC, including details of any IMP or LAWP that may be incurred due to payments received.

Is the customer also receiving FTB and/or CCS?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, procedure ends here

Updating or reviewing the FTB/CCS income estimate

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check the date the FEG or GEERS payment was received and the date the FTB/CCS income estimate was last revised + Read more ...

Does the customer need to provide a new estimate?

  • Yes, contact the customer again (via phone or letter) requesting further information then go to Step 2
  • No, annotate the existing DOC to record the current FTB/CCS estimate to include the FEG or GEERS payments. Procedure ends here

2

Set up a review if required + Read more ...

If the information is not available immediately, set up a review to check the customer has returned the required information.

When details are provided, go to Step 3.

3

New FTB/CCS estimate provided + Read more ...

When the customer provides a new FTB/CCS estimate, run the Family Income and Choices workflow to update the customer's estimate.

Note: FEG or GEERS payments are usually taxable income for the financial year in which the payments were received, however the taxable amount must be confirmed with the customer.

4

Department of Education, Skills and Employment Overpayment + Read more ...

If GEERS income has previously been coded on the customer's record, reverse the income and if appropriate pay the customer any arrears owed.
