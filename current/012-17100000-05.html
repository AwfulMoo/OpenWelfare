General FAQs

DVA Webclaims - general FAQs

Table 1: This table describes frequently asked general questions about DVA Webclaims.

Item

Description

1

Question: Who can use DVA Webclaim? + Read more ...

Answer: Allied Health and Medical professionals, or their delegates, can lodge claims via DVA Webclaim by using an individual Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) certificate or, for provider stems registered by DVA, through Provider Digital Access (PRODA)

2

Question: Who is excluded from using DVA Webclaim? + Read more ...

Answer:

  • Community Nursing
  • Private hospitals
  • Nursing Home customers

3

Question: How does DVA Webclaim work? + Read more ...

Answer:

  • DVA Webclaim is a paperless, internet-based claim channel that is accessed via the Health Professional Online Services (HPOS) portal on the Services Australia website
  • It provides real-time assessment responses
  • Service providers, or their delegates, enter information into the DVA Webclaim form and submit for immediate assessment
  • Claims will return a status of either 'Paid' (complete) or 'Requires Further Assessment
  • Claims that require further assessment are pended for operator processing within 2 business days

4

Question: Is DVA Webclaim secure? + Read more ...

Answer: Yes. DVA Webclaim is accessed via the HPOS portal on the Services Australia website. The use of PKI encryption or the Provider Digital Access (PRODA) login ensures that submitted data is secure.

5

Question: Is there a cost to using DVA Webclaim? + Read more ...

Answer: No. DVA Webclaim is available to customers at no cost. It is an internet-based claiming system that requires the customer to have a computer, an internet connection and an individual PKI certificate. A site PKI certificate cannot be used.

6

Question: Can a patient eligibility check be done through DVA Webclaim? + Read more ...

Answer: Online Veteran Verification is not enabled through DVA Webclaim however customers will be notified of the patient's eligibility after submitting the claim.

7

Question: Do customers need to submit paperwork? + Read more ...

Answer: No, the system is entirely paperless.

Accessing DVA Webclaim FAQs

FAQs regarding accessing the DVA Webclaim

Table 2: This table describes frequently asked questions about accessing DVA Webclaims.

Item

Description

1

Question: Does DVA Webclaim integrate with a customer's practice software? + Read more ...

Answer: No, DVA Webclaim is an internet, not software, based claim channel. It has been designed for smaller practices whose clientele is not big enough to warrant the purchasing of online claiming software.

2

Question: Can DVA Webclaim be accessed on a tablet or smartphone? + Read more ...

Answer: DVA Webclaim uses the Health Professional Online Services (HPOS) portal which has been designed for PC laptop usage, however, customers can log on to DVA Webclaim on a tablet if the device has a USB port or chip reader. DVA Webclaim cannot be accessed on a smartphone.

3

Question: Where is the HPOS logon page? + Read more ...

Answer: From the Services Australia homepage:

  • select Health Professionals
  • select logon in the top right-hand corner

For help with HPOS Logon customers can call eBusiness.

The Resources page contains a link to Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

Item

Description

Question: How does a customer get a PKI certificate? + Read more ...

Claims FAQs

FAQs regarding DVA Webclaims

Table 4: This table describes frequently asked questions about DVA Webclaims.

Item

Description

1

Question: What will the claim ID be? + Read more ...

Answer: Claim IDs will be automatically generated each time the customer starts a new claim. The format is one alpha followed by 4 numeric values. The claim ID cannot be changed.

2

Question: Can a customer save a claim and go back to it later? + Read more ...

Answer: Yes, a claim can be saved for up to 2 years, and edited at any time. The claim ID will be the same.

3

Question: What happens if invalid data is entered into DVA Webclaim? + Read more ...

Answer: Claims are assessed in real-time. When invalid data is entered, DVA Webclaim displays an error message at the top of the page which includes instructions on how to resolve the error.

4

Question: How many patients, services and vouchers can be submitted in a claim? + Read more ...

Answer:

  • One patient per claim is permitted
  • A maximum of 3 vouchers can be submitted per claim
  • Each voucher can have a maximum of 6 items
  • All items on a voucher must have the same date-of-service and treatment location

5

Question: What items can be claimed via DVA Webclaim? + Read more ...

Answer: All DVA allied health and medical schedule items can be claimed by via DVA Webclaim. Only private hospital admissions community nursing and nursing home contributions are excluded.

6

Question: Can customers submit both DVA and MBS items in the same claim? + Read more ...

Answer: Yes.

7

Question: Are signatures required to lodge a DVA Webclaim? + Read more ...

Answer: No. The system is paperless.

Declarations such as those provided by veterans when claiming additional dentures or glasses within the allowable timeframe are still required. However, these documents are not required to be submitted to Services Australia. Health professionals must keep this paperwork for their own records in accordance with their state or territory legislation for the retention of documents.

8

Question: What is the Account Reference Number used for in DVA Webclaim? + Read more ...

Answer: This is an optional field that allows customers to enter their own unique patient reference IDs to help with account reconciliation.

9

Question: Do customers have to enter a condition treated for white card holders? + Read more ...

Answer: Yes. When a claim is lodged for a white card holder, DVA Webclaim will display the White Card Condition Treated or Additional In field. This is a mandatory field that the customer must enter text into.

10

Question: Can a customer do a 10900 eligibility check in DVA Webclaim? + Read more ...

Answer: No, customers will still be required to contact the VAP Hotline to determine if a veteran is entitled to have a 10900 consultation.

The Resources page contains a link to Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

11

Question: Can customers delete claims? + Read more ...

Answer: In most cases a claim lodged the same day with the status 'Complete' or 'draft' can be deleted if the claim was lodged that day. If the claim cannot be deleted, providers should contact the VAP Hotline.

The Resources page contains a link to Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

12

Question: Can customers delete a claim that has gone into pend? + Read more ...

Answer: Yes, however the health professional must contact the VAP Hotline to request this.

The Resources page contains a link to Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

13

Question: Can claims that are greater than 2 years be lodged via DVA Webclaim? + Read more ...

Answer: No, these claims must be submitted for manual processing.

Payments, statements and support FAQs

FAQs about DVA Webclaim payments, statements and support

Table 5: This table describes frequently asked questions about DVA Webclaim payments, statements and supports.

Item

Description

1

Question: How soon does a health professional receive payment? + Read more ...

Answer: Usually within 2 business days of the claim being finalised.

2

Question: How do customers access their statements? + Read more ...

Answer: Customers can access their statements the day after a claim is finalised, through the Health Professional Online Services (HPOS) portal.

3

Question: How long are statements available for? + Read more ...

Answer: Statements will be available through the HPOS portal for 2 years.

4

Question: Can customers access complete claims history reports through the HPOS portal? + Read more ...

Answer: Yes, customers can download the last 2 years of their entire DVA claims history in Comma-Separated Values (CSV) format, regardless of claim channel, to help with reconciling accounts.

5

Question: Do customers need to subscribe in HPOS to receive their statements? + Read more ...

Answer: No, DVA Webclaim Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT) statements are available through automatic subscription and will appear in the Mail Centre on the health professional's HPOS account.

6

Question: What format are provider statements available in? + Read more ...

Answer: In PDF and RTF format.

7

Question: What support is available to customers when lodging a DVA Webclaim? + Read more ...

Answer: For support with:

  • the HPOS portal, or other technical issues with lodging a claim, customers can call, email or write to eBusiness
  • DVA business rules, or VAP processing requirements and claim enquiries, customers can call the VAP Hotline or email VAP

The Resources page contains a link to Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

8

Question: Can a customer still request a duplicate provider statement? + Read more ...

Answer: DVA Webclaim is designed as a self-service option, however, VAP staff can issue a duplicate provider statement if requested.

9

Question: How can a claim be identified on the Treatment Account System as being submitted on the DVA Webclaim channel? + Read more ...

Answer:

  • A claim submitted on the DVA Webclaim channel can be identifiable by a 'W' in the C field on the VQ screens
  • DVA Webclaims can be accessed directly by using the DVW claim type modifier
  • On patient history (VHOI), manual claims and those sent using DVA Webclaim, are both identified by a space after the claim ID and will not be distinguishable from each other on this screen

Allied health FAQs

FAQs for allied health professionals

Table 6: This table describes answers to allied health professional queries.

Item

Description

1

Question: Do customers have to submit a separate item for loadings? + Read more ...

Answer: No, any item that attracts a loading will automatically add the additional amount to the benefit.

2

Question: How do customers claim dental step-down or jaw indicator items? + Read more ...

Answer:

  • DVA Webclaim will identify any item that requires the customer to specify if the service was performed on the upper or lower jaw. A drop-down list will appear against these items and the customer must select either UPR (upper) or LWR (lower).
  • Items that have a two-tiered fee structure require the customer to enter the treated tooth number.

3

Question: How do customers claim Remote Area Allowance item OT80? + Read more ...

Answer: The customer needs to key OT80 in the line immediately after the associated consultation item. The Distance Travelled field will open and the customer must enter the distance travelled on the same voucher.

4

Question: How do customers claim optical lens items when there is a valid reason for exceeding items provided within the past 2 years? + Read more ...

Answer: When an optical lens or frame item is claimed, DVA Webclaim will automatically display the Replacement Reason field if the veteran has received glasses in the past 2 years. Customers must select from:

  • LS - Lost
  • BK - Broken
  • RC - Significant Refraction Change

The customer does not have to submit the veteran's signed declaration but the health professional is required to keep it for their own records.

5

Question: How should a customer claim travel (kilometres)? + Read more ...

Answers: Kilometres are claimed by entering KM in the Item field below the associated consultation item.

  • Enter the number of kilometres travelled in the Distance Travelled field
  • A minimum of 10 and a maximum of 999 KM can be entered

Table 7: This table describes information about the various fields that appear when more information is required when making a claim.

Selects the applicable time duration from a drop-down list.

from a drop-down list.

Enters the condition treated.
