Frequently Asked Questions - CDC Exits

See also Attachments, below, for more Q&As

Item

Details

1

Applying to Exit CDC

  • An exit application form is available on Services Australia website for you to fill out if you would like to exit the Cashless Debit Card program
  • You can download a copy of the application form at any time and return it by submitting your documents by Post or by uploading direct to your Centrelink online account
  • If you do not have access to a scanner or computer, visit your nearest Centrelink Service Centre, local partner, or you can post your application to: Services Australia, Reply Paid 7800, Canberra BC ACT 2610

2

Progress of Applications

  • Processing times will vary for each application. The length of time a particular application takes to process will depend on a range of factors, including the amount of time it takes to gather the needed evidence and properly assess the application. Some applications will be complex and may take time to assess all the details
  • If you would like to provide more details or documents that supports your application you can upload the evidence to your Centrelink online account.'

Complete applications lodged on or after 17 March 2021 must be assessed within 60 days, or the applicant will be automatically exited. If the applications are not complete, or supporting documentation is not provided, the 60 day assessment timeframe will not start until this information is provided.

3

What will the phone interview be about?

During the phone interview, you will talk about your personal circumstances, such as your housing situation and financial affairs.

4

Will I know when I will get the phone call for the interview?

We will contact you to discuss your application as soon as we can. We conduct interviews in the order of applications received. We will make two separate attempts to contact you and our calls are from a private number. We will also send you a text message or email before the call depending on your notification preferences.

We cannot provide you with an exact time for your interview but if you missed it let us know and we will arrange another time to contact you.

5

What if I am not able to complete a phone interview

You will need to advise us if you cannot attend a phone interview within the next 7 days. If you are not able to complete your interview within this time, we will continue to assess your application without an interview. This means you may not be able to tell us everything we need for your application to be successful.

Alternatively, you can withdraw your application and resubmit a new one at a more suitable time.

6

Why do I need to provide evidence?

We request documents to support your application when you submit your application form. This helps us to make a fair decision by looking at your individual circumstances. If you do not provide this evidence with your form, we will call you and send a letter to ask you to provide this. You will have 14 days to provide the documents from when we asked you. If you need extra time to provide these documents please let us know.

7

What additional evidence do I need to provide?

To support your application, you may need to provide the following (where relevant) so that your application can be assessed against the relevant legislation:

  • Copies of school attendance reports for each child for the last 4 terms
  • Copies of evidence to support claims of having a disability or care arrangements, such as medical certificate or NDIA agreements
  • Copies of evidence that your primary needs have been met, such as rental statements, utility statements, or bank statements (including any joint accounts)
  • Copies of any credit card or store card statements, including Gem Visa Cards, or Go MasterCard, for the last 3 months
  • Copies of any buy now pay later services, such as from Afterpay, or Zip, statements for the last 3 months

8

How can I provide the requested documents?

You can provide the documents by uploading direct to your Centrelink record, by post or by attending a Service Centre or Centrelink Agent.

If you upload your documents direct to your Centrelink record, please use form code SS526.

9

What happens if I do not provide requested documents?

This information is needed to support your application to exit the program. If you do not provide this information within 14 days of our request, your application will not progress. This means that you will stay on the Cashless Debit Card.

You will need to complete and submit a new application form with all supporting documents.

10

How will I know if I have been successful in exiting the Cashless Debit Card?

We will notify you in writing if you are successful or unsuccessful in exiting the Cashless Debit Card.

11

If I am approved to exit the program, when will this be actioned?

After you get an outcome notification, you are paid in full into your personal bank account. If you received a letter saying you will be exited, there may be a short delay between when you get this letter and when we action your exit. We will get to this as soon as possible before your next payment.

12

Can I apply for a formal review if I am unsuccessful in exiting the program?

Yes. The letter sent to you tells you that you were unsuccessful in exiting the Programme and would have provided details about the reasons for the decision.

If you do not agree with the decision made to remain on the Cashless Debit Card, you can apply for a formal review of the decision by contacting the Cashless Debit Card Hotline on 1800 252 604.

13

Can I reopen the exit application if I provide the additional documents needed?

No, an exit application cannot be reopened. You will need to fill out a new Cashless Debit Card exit application form and provide the supporting documents requested on this form. The exit application form is available on the Services Australia website for you to download or we can send you one.

You can return this to us by uploading direct to your Centrelink Online account, by visiting your nearest Centrelink Services Centre, or by post to Services Australia reply paid 7800, Canberra BC ACT 2610.

Scenarios of documents received to support an application

  • Example 1 - SS526 signed on 01 September 2020
    • 3 months of bank statements provided up to 17 August 2020
    • This is acceptable, as it is within the allowable one month time period
  • Example 2 - SS526 signed on 01 September 2020
    • 3 months of bank statements are provided ending on 30 July 2020
    • This will be returned for insufficient evidence, as the statements are not within one month of the SS526 being signed
  • Example 3 - SS526 signed on 01 September 2020
    • Participant has provided supporting documents after the exit application form has been submitted. Bank statements provided show transactions up to 15 September 2020
    • This would mean bank statements are needed for before the 3 month period 16 June 2020 to 15 September 2020

Buy Now, Pay Later Fact Sheet

Acceptable documents

Participants should provide supporting documents at the time of submitting their application form. If the participant does not provide the documents at the time of submitting their application form, they must send them within 14 days, plus any extra time for mail delivery as outlined in Allow extra time for mail delivery. See Process Table 2 for steps to undertake when this occurs.

Participants must provide copies of documents with details of bank, building society or credit union account balances and transactions for the last 3 months. If they are within 1 month of the exit application form being signed, these can be accepted. See Resources page for scenarios of document date ranges.

Participants will also need to provide the following documents (if applicable):

  • School attendance reports for each school aged child they are responsible for, provided by their school. The report should list the number of explained and unexplained absences for the last 4 school terms. School attendance records can be provided over consecutive years. If the child has not yet completed a full year of school, attendance report for the school terms the child has attended are required
  • Evidence to support their claims of having a disability or care arrangements, such as a medical certificate or National Disability Insurance Agency (NDIA) agreements
  • If the participant transfers funds to another person’s account to meet their primary needs, the participant will need to provide evidence these needs are being met. This could include proof of housing payments, rental statements or details of their housing situation loan payments direct debit arrangements, utility statements
  • Credit or store card statements for the last 3 months
  • Transaction details for any Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL) services, such as Afterpay, or Zip, for the last 3 months. See the Buy Now, Pay Later Fact Sheet for a comprehensive explanation of what is required.

Documentation can include:

  • Proof of housing payments
  • Rental statements
  • Details of the participants housing situation
  • Direct debit arrangements
  • Evidence of bill payments
  • Details of any state or territory fines.

Bank/credit or store card statements as acceptable evidence

For supporting bank/credit or store card statements for all accounts owned (including joint accounts) to be deemed as acceptable evidence, they must have:

  • Full name, BSB and account number on the first page
  • Dates of transactions and running balance
  • Details of transactions, and
  • Page and/or date sequencing

For joint accounts, the participant must get consent from the other account holder(s) to provide the agency with the joint account information.

When documents (for example, bank statements) do not have detailed transactions listed, or a running balance staff must request more evidence, such as screenshots or interim statements.

The following formats of bank/credit/store card statements can be accepted as evidence:

  • Bank/credit/store card statements (printed)
  • Transaction/Online Statements
  • Interim statements (printouts over the bank counter)
    • This can only be accepted in conjunction with a bank or transaction statement to be classed as acceptable evidence
  • Screenshots can be accepted if they meet the standards outlined above, i.e. full name, account number and running balance etc.

Customers are not required to provide statements for their loan accounts. This includes home loans, personal loans, debt consolidation loans or Payday loans.

Not all BNPL merchants provide their customers with a statement. In the absence of a participant being able to provide a transaction statement, the agency will accept the following types of evidence which includes a screenshot of their account.

  • Account with order amount, amount owing and upcoming payments;
  • Payment schedule with details of the latest transactions;
  • Completed order list

Note: all screenshots should include the participant’s name.

See the Buy Now, Pay Later Fact Sheet.

If the participant has had a significant change in financial responsibilities/circumstances in between the time they provided the bank statements with the signed exit application form and their interview, the participant may need to provide updated bank statements (and other documents if required) as evidence of this.
