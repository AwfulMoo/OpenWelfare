CSHC claims + Read more ...

Is the customer claiming Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC)?

  • Yes, these claims are not eligible for urgent claim processing:
    • Customers would generally not meet financial hardship criteria as they are mostly self-funded retirees and have access to other income
    • Customers are not residentially qualified to claim Age Pension who cannot support themselves should test their eligibility for Special Benefit (SpB)
    • Customers who are vulnerable or have complex needs may be eligible for immediate claim processing

Urgent processing of a new claim due to financial hardship + Read more ...

Note: when a customer has been assessed as in financial hardship and a previous Urgent Claim Processing Fast Note has not been actioned, they may be eligible for immediate claim processing.

If the customer is claiming:

  • Special Benefit (SpB) see Step 15 in Table 2

4

All information and verification provided + Read more ...

Check the customer has provided all information and verification needed for the claim to be finalised and documents have been correctly scanned.
Note: urgent processing is unable to be done if information is still outstanding.

  • Check the Document List (DL) screen for a Progress of claim DOC. This DOC provides information on where the claim is in the assessment process, and if any additional information has been requested from the customer
  • Check Notes in Process Direct
  • Compare the documents provided against the Required Customer Tasks via Interaction History to ensure all appropriate documents have been provided for their claim to be finalised, see Viewing Centrelink customers digital images
  • Check the Document Tool in Customer First for the claim documents to determine if they have been scanned incorrectly. To follow up any documents incorrectly and / or not scanned onto the customers' record, see:
    • Digital image not located on Centrelink customer record
    • Requesting a document rescan/retrieval or location

For a guide on what documentation may be required, see:

  • Claiming ABSTUDY
  • Youth Allowance and Austudy students and Australian Apprentices online claims and Assisted Customer Claims
  • Claiming Age Pension
  • Claiming Low Income Card (LIC) or LIC renewals
  • Claiming Carer Payment CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA)
  • Claiming JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
  • Claiming Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker)
  • Claiming Parenting Payment (PP)
  • Documents required for Centrelink new claims for supporting documents based on customer circumstances

Has all required documentation been provided?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, tell the customer to provide the outstanding documents and DOC the record. Procedure ends here

5

Assess financial hardship + Read more ...

Customers must meet the criteria for urgent processing of a new claim due to financial hardship.

For LIC customers, their individual circumstances must support that a delay in processing the claim/renewal will place the customer in financial hardship.

Note: financial hardship provisions do not apply to:

  • customers transferring from one income support payment to another unless
    • their previous income support payment was cancelled more than 14 days ago, or
    • an urgent transfer is required for Crisis Payment claimants
  • dependent Youth Allowance (YA) Students, Jobseekers and Australian Apprentices and ABSTUDY Apprentices and Living Allowance customers, living with parent(s)/(guardian(s)

Is the customer deemed to be experiencing financial hardship or claiming Crisis Payment?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Customer is not assessed as experiencing financial hardship + Read more ...

Advise the customer:
'We are sorry for the delay in assessing your claim. The agency is experiencing a high demand at the moment. Our priority is to finalise claims for people who are experiencing difficulties as quickly as possible and the agency has committed additional resources to claims processing to resolve the issue, however this may still be some weeks away.' They will be contacted as soon as possible.

If the customer is not satisfied with the outcome:

  • follow the normal complaint escalation process Managing customer complaints and feedback, and
  • advise them of other options they can consider while waiting for their claim to be assessed, e.g. referral to external support services

Discuss with customer and consider other referrals.

Record the conversation:

  • in Customer First or Customer Record, annotate the Progress of Claim (POC) DOC if available, otherwise create a closed DOC, or
  • annotate the Progress of Claim Note within Process Direct on the customer's record

The DOCs must include the approved text for 'Determination has been made that customer is not in financial hardship' found on the Resources page.

Procedure ends here.

  • Disability Pension (DSP)

15

Customer is claiming Special Benefit (SpB) + Read more ...

Determine if the customer requires urgent processing of a SpB new claim due to being an identified vulnerable customer.

SpB is a payment of hardship and last resort. As such, the only SpB new claims that are considered urgent are where the customer:

  • has lodged a claim for Crisis Payment (CrP) at the same time as the claim for SpB
  • has experienced family and domestic violence
  • is under the age of 16 and Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH) has been granted by social worker
  • has BVF visa subclass 060 and/or Red Cross letter confirming part of the Support for Trafficked People Program
  • has been released from 14 or more nights in prison or psychiatric care as a result of being charged with committing an offence and lodged their claim more than 14 days ago
  • Australian Citizen Children (ACC) SpB claims where custodial parent is not in receipt of FTB for the child
  • was a SRSS recipient within the 8 weeks prior to initial contact date and has lodged the claim for SpB on being granted a temporary protection visa
  • is experiencing homelessness and a Homelessness Indicator has been applied to their record

Does the customer meet one of the above criteria?

  • Yes,
    • Create an open work item using Fast Note, with the following information:
      Select Auto Text option > Generic > Urgent Claim Processing > SpB Vulnerability criteria met
      Select Confirm
    • Advise the customer the claim has been escalated for urgent processing.
    • Do not advise the customer to call back to check on their claim process. Promote and encourage the use of self service applications to allow customers to monitor their claim outcomes and progress.
  • No, check the customer's record for a progress DOC to see if anything is delaying the claim and advise the customer accordingly. Record a DOC with action taken
