Geospatial tool

To assess if a customer lives, works or was present in a relevant area:

  • Open the TAS Live Feed App
  • Place the address given by the customer in the search box. Residential and employer addresses may need to be checked
  • Select Search (magnifying glass icon)
  • Select COVID-19 Disaster Payment in the Layers menu on the right
  • The address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible to receive a payment
  • Continue to assess the claim

Scenarios - eligibility

Table 1

Scenario

Outcome

Unable to attend work due to exemption from mask wearing

The customer works in a café as a casual for 25 hours per week and is currently receiving an income support payment.

The customer has an exemption from wearing a mask, however, a state public health order mandates that people working in food and beverage outlets must wear a mask.

Their employer tells the customer that they cannot attend work.

Eligible

The customer is eligible.

The customer’s rate of payment will be $200.

Note: if there was no public health order to mandate mask wearing and this was a condition of employment imposed by the employer, the customer would not be eligible for payment.

Customer has been receiving JobSeeker Payment since 19 June 2021.

They normally work 16 hours per week.

Due to the restricted movement, the customer has lost all 16 hours per week.

The customer has reported regular earned income since their first reporting period after 22 August 2021 and meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

Customer is eligible to receive $200 for the relevant period.

The customer will make a claim for the payment and confirm that they meet the criteria for the relevant period.

Customer is receiving a zero rate of an income support payment and up until the movement restrictions was able to earn income working at a local café.

The customer then started working delivering take-out food when the movement restrictions started.

Their income was reduced from $1000 per week to $800 per week as they lost 8 hours of work overall.

Their income support has stayed current during this time at a zero rate, as the income has been over the threshold.

Customer resides in an eligible Local Government Area (LGA).

Eligible

Customer is eligible to receive $200 for the relevant period.

The customer was receiving a zero rate of an income support payment at the time they are claiming.

They are eligible for payments for each relevant period they meet they have lost 8 hours or at least one full day's work.

Customer is receiving an income support payment and has recorded self-employment income as they run a hobby farm.

This income has been reduced because of the restricted movement.

Before the movement restrictions, the customer was receiving $100 per fortnight for their income support payment.

After the movement restrictions, the customer is making no employment income and has updated their income reporting.

The customer is receiving full rate of their income support payment.

Customer resides in an eligible LGA and meets all other criteria.

Eligible

Customer is eligible to receive $200 for the relevant period.

The customer will make one claim for the payment and confirm that they meet the criteria for the relevant period.

Customer is receiving a Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) payment since 5 January 2021.

They have not been working or declaring earnings from employment.

The customer was due to start work on 18 August 2021. Due to movement restrictions, they were unable to.

Ineligible

As no employment earnings were declared after 22 August 2021, they are not eligible to receive a payment.

Customer is receiving an eligible DVA payment. The customer declared employment income for five hours in one fortnight in September 2021.

The customer declares that due to the movement restrictions they have lost more than 8 hours in the first relevant period.

The customer meets all other eligibility requirements.

Eligible

Customer is eligible to receive $200 for the relevant period.

The customer will make a claim for the payment and confirm that they meet the criteria for the relevant period.

Customer is receiving an income support payment while also working part time as an essential worker. Customer lives and works in a declared COVID-19 hotspot.

Customer has 2 children, aged 4 and 7, who ordinarily attend child care while the customer is at work. Due to the movement restrictions, the customer’s child care centre has closed. The customer is unable to perform their work from home and unable to earn income for the period of the movement restrictions.

The customer would normally work 7 hour shifts 2 times during the period of restricted movement.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

Customer is eligible to receive $200 for the relevant period.

The customer will make a claim for the payment and confirm that they meet the criteria for the relevant period.

Customer is not vaccinated against COVID-19 and their employer does not allow them to attend work

The customer is receiving an income support payment and was working 10 hours per week in a declared COVID-19 hotspot. The customer also lives in the same relevant area.

The customer has been told by their employer they cannot attend work, as they are not vaccinated against COVID-19.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer is eligible to receive $200 for the relevant period.

The customer will make a claim for the payment and confirm they meet the criteria for the relevant period.
