Managing enquiries from DVA Clients

This table describes processes in Services Australia for managing enquiries from Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) clients.

Item

Action

1

DVA Online Service + Read more ...

My Service is the DVA Online Service. Clients access their MyService account via the myGov portal.

If the client has not previously registered for a MyService account advise them to contact DVA directly to obtain an ‘Activation Code’. This lets them link their MyService to their myGov.

2

SSO - Check, verify and forward DVA forms and client correspondence + Read more ...

Proof of Identity (POI) documentation is required when DVA clients lodge new claims.

The client may need different categories of POI, depending on the type of claim. Requirements are usually identified on the claim form.

See the Resources page for a link to the DVA website forms page.

Certified copies of documents provided by client

A DVA client may provide certified copies of documents. For example, documents signed by a Justice of the Peace. See page 2 of the 'Proving your Identity to DVA form' for information about who can certify copies of documents. The Resources page includes a link to this form on the DVA website.

Verifying documents

When verifying documents

  • Sight the original document and take a photocopy.
  • Stamp the first page of each document with a Services Australia ‘Original sighted and returned’ stamp. If this is not available complete the following on the documents:
    • logon of Services Australia officer verifying the documents
    • signature of Services Australia officer verifying the documents
    • address of service centre
    • a 'date received' stamp

Note: return any original documents mailed to DVA to the client by registered post.

Check forms and correspondence

Check all claim/review forms lodged by Veterans to ensure the following has been completed correctly:

  • Full Name
  • Date of Birth
  • Signature
  • Client file number (if known)

Send documents to DVA

Place all correspondence, forms and supporting documents in an DVA reply paid envelope (GPO Box 9998, Brisbane, QLD, 4001) ensuring:

  • documents are in correct page order and same orientation
  • individual client's paperwork is packaged together. For example:
    • use a paperclip to keep the documentation together, or
    • place each of the client's documentation in an individual envelope.
  • advise client that the documents will be posted to DVA by mail and to follow up with DVA directly in 1-2 weeks to ensure they have been received.

Clients in Hardship

If a client identifies as being in hardship and DVA needs the documents urgently, email the documents directly to DVA at VIS@dva.gov.au. This should be used in extenuating circumstances only.

The email subject line should include ‘URGENT’ followed by the client reference number and/or clients full name.

Note: The documents should also be sent by mail to DVA.

See the Resources page for a link to the DVA website forms page.

3

VIS Officers Only - DVA Veterans' Information and Enquiry Window (VIEW) + Read more ...

VIS Officers are given read-only access to the DVA client management database Veteran's Information and Enquiry Window (VIEW) via a DVA User ID and password.

Access to VIEW is using Services Australia's Citrix Gateway (VIEW will only open as required from Citrix Receiver 10).

VIS Officers use VIEW to:

  • verify DVA client identification, see Item 2, Proof of identity requirements
  • refer to details on the DVA client's case record

VIS Officers must log into VIEW each fortnight to avoid access being suspended. Save a link to VIEW under intranet 'Favourites' to save time.

Contact the Face-to-Face Partnerships Team if access has been suspended or for any other issues accessing VIEW.

The Resources page includes a link to the VIEW access task card for VIS Officers.

4

VIS Officers Only - Proof of identity requirements + Read more ...

Acceptable proof of identity from DVA clients presenting in person at a VIS service centre are:

  • the DVA client's treatment card, or
  • current, valid purple or orange Australian Defence Force (ADF) Identity Card

If further checks are required, ask the DVA client at least 3 questions that can be verified from the DVA Veterans' Information and Enquiry Window (VIEW). For example:

  • DVA File Number
  • date of birth
  • partner's full name
  • address
  • rate of disability pension
  • accepted disabilities

For information about proof of identity documentation for new claims see Check, verify and forward DVA claim/review forms and client correspondence

5

VIS Officers Only - Check, verify and forward DVA claim/review forms and client correspondence + Read more ...

Proof of Identity (POI) documentation is required when DVA clients lodge new claims.

The client may need different categories of POI, depending on the type of claim. Requirements are usually identified on the claim form.

See the Resources page for a link to the DVA website forms page.

Certified copies of documents provided by client

A DVA client may provide certified copies of documents. For example, documents signed by a Justice of the Peace. See page 2 of the 'Proving your Identity to DVA form' for information about who can certify copies of documents. The Resources page includes a link to this form on the DVA website.

Verifying documents

VIS Officers may certify photocopies of original documents if the VIS Officer meets the criteria for 'permanent employee (with 5 or more years of continuous service) of The Commonwealth', as outlined in Factsheet DVA06 - Proof of Identity Requirements. The Resources page includes a link to this factsheet on the DVA website.

When verifying documents:

  • sight the original document and take a photocopy
  • on the document copy, record:
    • name of Services Australia officer verifying the documents
    • signature of Services Australia officer verifying the documents
    • date of verification
    • address of VIS service centre
    • official VIS stamp if available, otherwise a 'date received' stamp
    • for single page documents, verify the document by writing the words 'Certified true copy', or 'This is a certified copy of the original as sighted by me' on the copy
    • for multiple page documents:
    • verify the document by writing on the front page, 'I certify this and following [insert number of pages] pages to be a true copy of the original sighted by me', and
    • initial all subsequent pages of the document

Note: any original documents mailed to DVA will be returned to the client by registered post.

Check forms and correspondence

Check all claim/review forms lodged by Veterans to ensure the form has been completed correctly.

DVA client correspondence must:

  • state the client's name and DVA file number
  • be date stamped by Services Australia
  • be stamped with the 'Original sighted' stamp where copies of original documents are taken, and
  • include the section destination

Send documents to DVA

Place all correspondence, forms and supporting documents in a large Express Post envelope ensuring:

  • documents are in correct page order and same orientation
  • individual client's paperwork is packaged together, for example use a paperclip to keep the documentation together or place each of the client's documentation in an individual envelope

Post to Department of Veterans' Affairs.

See the Resources page for a link to the DVA website forms page.

VIS Officers Only - Updating financial information for DVA clients + Read more ...

Use the DVA Veterans' Information and Enquiry Window (VIEW) to verify the client's identity.

Ask the client to provide details for 2 of the items on the following identification checklist:

  • current pension payment account details (including BSB and account number)
  • partner's full name
  • details of investments
  • accepted disabilities

To change payment destination details clients must comply with the above security check as well as provide their:

  • DVA file number
  • date of birth
  • previous bank account number

Note: if the client has already provided their previous bank account details as part of the initial identity verification, ask the client a further question from the identification checklist above.

7

DVA Bereavement Notification + Read more ...

If a client attends a Service Centre for a Death Notification advise them to contact DVA directly. If the client needs assistance, offer a warm transfer.

8

Escalation of operational issues and complaints + Read more ...

Contact the Face-to-Face Partnerships Team to escalate operational issues or DVA client complaints about the delivery of services to Veterans.

See also Managing customer complaints and feedback.

9

VIS Officer training + Read more ...

For VIS Officers and Face-to-Face Partnerships Team only

The Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) provides new VIS Officers with face-to-face training over 2 days.

The Face-to-Face Partnerships Team coordinates and schedules VIS training with DVA.

VIS Officers must complete the External Training Attendance online form to ensure their training is reflected in the Learning Management System (LMS) on ESSentials.

See the Training & Support page for:

  • how to request training for new VIS Officers
  • information about refresher training (to be completed by VIS Officers every 2 years)
  • a link to the External Training Attendance online form

See the Resources page for the VIS factsheet which contains information to assist VIS Officers.
