This page contains the process for assessing a customer's request for urgent payment. The decision to approve or deny an urgent payment is made by a Service Officer at the APS4 level or above, Service Delivery Partner Service Officers or specific Smart Centre staff. These are staff fully trained in urgent payment processing. This applies to Smart Centre telephony, service centre and processing team staff.

This self service option is not available to some customers:

5

Authenticate customer/nominee + Read more ...

Before assessing or issuing an urgent payment, make sure the customer record is the correct one. Asking authentication questions to verify Proof of Record Ownership (PORO). See Authenticating a Centrelink customer.

For face-to-face contact:

  • It is preferable the customer provide identity documents, including at least one photo identity document for a visual comparison
  • If this is not available and the Service Officer is in doubt they are speaking with the customer, ask further questions. Do not limit these to details a customer's friends and relatives would know such as the customer's date of birth or how many children are in the family. The questions must be searching, but not so personal as to be intrusive. If appropriate, explain to the customer why they are being asked these questions

If the customer or nominee cannot confirm details and the Service Officer is not satisfied they are speaking with the customer, do not release any information. Do not run the Immediate Payment requests script. See Intertwined Centrelink records.

Note: urgent payments for FHA customers cannot be processed using the Immediate Payment Request script.

    • If a nominee or third party is applying on behalf of the customer, go to Step 6
    • If the customer is applying, go to Step 7
  • No, deny the urgent payment request, see Processing urgent payment requests

6

Nominee or third party requesting urgent payment on behalf of customer + Read more ...

A correspondence nominee is the only type of nominee or third party that can apply for an urgent payment on the customer's behalf. The payment can only be made to the customer when a request is received from a correspondence nominee.

Is the nominee a correspondence nominee?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, an application cannot be made. Procedure ends here

7

Investigate other assistance + Read more ...

Discuss other assistance options with the customer/nominee before proceeding with an urgent payment request.

This may be:

  • referrals to community organisations
  • referrals to accommodation assistance
  • financial counselling
  • Centrepay deductions
  • weekly payments
  • Crisis Payment due to family and domestic violence or other exceptional circumstances. Note: social workers decide if a customer is eligible for a Crisis Payment due to family and domestic violence

Staff must:

  • explore other assistance before assessing eligibility for an urgent payment
  • include in the urgent payment DOC details of what was discussed

Has the customer accepted the offer of other assistance?

  • Yes, urgent payment is not required. Run the Immediate Payment requests script and select Alternative Assistance option. Procedure ends here
  • No, include in the urgent payment DOC that other assistance was offered but refused. Go to Step 8

8

Check the customer has available funds + Read more ...

An urgent payment is paid in arrears. It is limited to the number of days between the 'date paid to' and the date on which the payment is being processed.

The available amount is less deductions, for example, for debt repayments, tax, Centrepay, immediate payment deduction. This leaves enough funds to meet these commitments.

To check a customer’s accrued entitlement:

  • In Customer First, select the correct system for the payment type
  • On the Payment Summary (PS) screen, key 'R' in the box next to the future payment, select Continue twice (in some cases a third ‘click’ may be required)
  • If there are available funds, this will display on the Issue Future Payment (IFP) screen

Do not process the urgent payment on this screen.

Has the customer’s entitlement accrued to an amount that will meet their immediate need?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the urgent payment request. See Processing urgent payment requests

9

Is this the first request today? + Read more ...

A customer is entitled to one urgent payment request (including any requests using self service), and one reconsideration of an urgent payment request per day.

Check:

  • Document List (DL) screen for any staff assisted or IVR requests
  • Super Case Notes in Customer First for details (this will show details of any requests via online services)
  • Notes in Process Direct for details of any self service rejections
  • If the customer used self service and selected funeral or late payment, this will be recorded as a rejection

Is this the first request for urgent payment today?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No,
    • If they made a request today and they disagree with the decision, they can ask for the decision to be reconsidered. See Customer disagrees with an urgent payment decision. Procedure ends here
    • If a request and reconsideration of that decision has been made today, the request is denied See Processing urgent payment requests. If the customer is only requesting an explanation of the decision, this does not need to be recorded

Is the customer receiving an eligible payment? + Read more ...

The customer must be receiving an eligible Centrelink payment.

An urgent payment cannot be granted for an ineligible payment type.

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the urgent payment request. See Processing urgent payment requests

3

Do exceptional and unforeseen circumstances apply? + Read more ...

An urgent payment is not to be made for regular expenses if:

  • the circumstances are not exceptional and/or could be foreseen (for example, the customer has been evicted due to not paying their rent), or
  • the customer has solely contributed to the delay in payment

Assess each request on its merit. While the customer may be requesting the funds for a regular expense, they may need the funds now due to an exceptional and unforeseen circumstance. For example, a customer is requesting money for food. On investigation, the reason for their request is that their fridge broke down, all food is spoilt and they have used their available funds to repair/replace the fridge.

Use effective questioning techniques to help decide. See the Resources page for a link.

Is the customer's financial hardship due to exceptional and unforeseen circumstances?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the urgent payment request. See Processing urgent payment requests

4

Accrued entitlement meets immediate need + Read more ...

The Immediate Payment requests script will calculate the available funds (the accrued entitlement). This amount cannot be overridden.

  • an urgent payment is paid in arrears and is limited to the number of days between the 'date paid to' and the date on which the payment is being processed
  • less deductions, for example, for debt repayments, tax, Centrepay, immediate payment deduction. This leaves enough funds to meet these commitments

To check a customer’s accrued entitlement:

  • In Customer First, select the correct system for the payment type
  • On the Payment Summary (PS) screen, key 'R' in the box next to the future payment, press continue, then press continue again
  • If there are available funds, this will display on the Issue Future Payment (IFP) screen

Do not process the urgent payment on this screen.

Has the customer’s entitlement accrued to an amount that will meet their immediate need?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the urgent payment request. See Processing urgent payment requests

5

Negotiate the amount to be paid + Read more ...

The amount payable is the smallest of:

  • available funds calculated by the system
  • the amount the customer requested
  • the minimum amount it will cost to meet the customer's immediate hardship need, or
  • the maximum amount of $200

Before offering an urgent payment, consider:

  • Is the amount being offered the minimum required to meet the customer's immediate need?
  • If paid, how will the customer meet their regular ongoing expenses such as rent, food, utilities on their next payday? Reduce the amount offered or deny the request if the customer cannot meet their regular ongoing expenses
  • Does the customer history indicate they are struggling to meet regular ongoing expenses or they appear to be in a crisis cycle? Consider a referral to help customers build their financial capability or completing a weekly payment assessment with an Authorised Weekly Payment Officer (AWPO)

Discuss with the customer and confirm they understand:

  • any money they receive for urgent payment will reduce their next fortnightly payment, and
  • how they intend to meet their regular expenses with the reduced funds received on their regular payday

While the system will allow as little as $1 to be paid, when determining the urgent payment amount consider:

  • most stores require a minimum purchase of between $10 to $15 to use EFTPOS
  • ATMs issue a minimum of $20
  • Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) cards can only be issued in denominations of $20 or $50 as these are the amounts available at ATMs

Will receiving the urgent payment extend or make the customer's financial hardship worse?

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for an urgent payment. Deny the payment. See Processing urgent payment requests
  • No, see Processing urgent payment requests

Make decision when the customer is applying for urgent payment due to extraordinary circumstances

Table 3: the process to follow when a customer has received 2 urgent payments for exceptional or unforeseen circumstances in the last 12 months. Note: only a Service Officer at the APS4 level or above can make this decision. A social worker can make a recommendation to grant a payment but this still needs to be approved by a TPS, Team Leader or other staff member above team leader delegation.

Step

Action

1

Check if the customer wants to proceed + Read more ...

When the customer has already received 2 or more urgent payments in the last 12 months, only consider another urgent payment if:

  • the customer is experiencing severe financial hardship due to extraordinary circumstances, and
  • all other options have been exhausted

Generally, 'extraordinary circumstances' means circumstances that significantly exceed the threshold for the normal grant of an urgent payment.

The Service Officer must establish that the customer is experiencing:

  • severe financial hardship, and
  • extraordinary circumstances

If the customer is on a Managed Service Plan (MSP), the Service Officer must consult with the OMC.PSSO.

Does the customer want to continue with the urgent payment request?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, deny the urgent payment request. See Processing urgent payment requests

2

Evidence of extraordinary circumstances + Read more ...

The decision that extraordinary circumstances apply is discretionary.

Tell the customer evidence is required to support their claim of extraordinary circumstances (if possible).

In some situations, customers may be unable to provide evidence due to the nature of the extraordinary circumstances.

Verify the customer's circumstances when considering more than 2 urgent payments in 12 months. The method of doing so should be responsive to the extraordinary nature of the customer's circumstances. Verify circumstances if possible, but this may not be possible in every case.

Speak to Technical Peer Support (TPS) if unsure whether the urgent payment request should proceed, where evidence is unable to be provided.

Can the customer provide evidence to verify the extraordinary circumstances?

  • Yes,
    • If the customer has documentary evidence, go to Step 3
    • if the Service Officer needs to contact a third party for verification, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • If the customer cannot provide verification but SO considers payment should be approved, go to Step 8
    • Urgent payment to be denied
    • Explore other assistance with the customer
    • Then see Processing urgent payment requests

3

Providing verification + Read more ...

Customer is on the phone:

If the customer can provide the verification via Upload documents, go to Step 6 once verification is received.

If customer cannot provide verification via Upload documents, can they provide verification at a service centre.

  • Yes, go to Step 6 to have conditional approval approved.
  • No, deny request and tell the customer they will need to provide verification on the same date for the request to be reconsidered. See Processing urgent payment requests

Customer is in the service centre:

Do they have the verification with them?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, deny request and tell the customer they will need to provide verification on the same date for the request to be reconsidered. See Processing urgent payment requests

4

Ask customer for permission to contact third party and disclose identifying information + Read more ...

The Service Officer can make up to 2 outbound calls to a third party to verify the customer's circumstances.

Generally, the third party is a person independent of the customer (not a family member or friend) and who is a party in the situation.

For example:

  • for a medical condition/medication this may be a doctor, hospital or pharmacist, or
  • for housing issues a landlord, real estate agent or housing authority

If there is no person independent of the customer who can verify the circumstance, this may be an indicator that the customer has broader issues.

For example, if the customer has had to leave the house due to a family breakdown/separation, are they affected by family and domestic violence? In these cases, it would be appropriate to make referral to a social worker via a warm handoff to the Social Work Line.

If unable to contact a social worker, complete a Fast Note. Select Auto Text. Use Emergency > Urgent Payment > Referral to SWO due to UP assessment.

The urgent payment request will be denied if the third party cannot be contacted by phone, or the third party chooses not to disclose information to the Service Officer. The customer still has the option to provide documentary evidence supporting their circumstances.

Ask the customer if they give permission for the Service Officer to:

  • contact the third party by phone to verify the customer's circumstances
  • provide enough information to the third party to identify the customer, for example, the customer's name and date of birth

Does the customer give permission to contact the third party and disclose identification information?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, deny the request for urgent payment. See Processing urgent payment requests

5

Contact third party by phone + Read more ...

Make up to 2 outbound calls to the third party.

Protect the customer's privacy:

  • be aware that customer records are confidential
  • only disclose the limited information the customer has consented to
  • do not conduct 3-way conversations with the customer and the third party unless intending to transfer the customer to the third party

Before disclosing any information, confirm the third party details (for example, name, company, ABN, phone number). If this information cannot be verified, check the:

  • ABN via ABN Lookup using the link on the Resources page
  • name of the person/company and phone number via the White Pages using the link on the Resources page

If the details of the third party:

  • cannot be confirmed:
    • end the call with the third party
    • tell the customer the request for urgent payment is denied
    • see Processing urgent payment requests
  • are confirmed:
    • tell the third party the Service Officer's name and advise the call is from Centrelink to verify the customer's circumstances
    • ask appropriate questions for the situation
    • ask if there is an alternative way to resolve this situation
    • do not push the third party if they do not want to disclose information due to confidentiality/privacy reasons. Tell the third party the type of evidence the customer will require in writing from them, and that the customer will need to give this evidence to Centrelink
    • thank the third party for their time

Did the third party provide verbal evidence to support the customer's claim of extraordinary circumstances?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, and the customer cannot provide documentary evidence, advise the request for urgent payment is denied. See Processing urgent payment requests

6

Seek approval for decision to grant urgent payment + Read more ...

If satisfied the customer is experiencing severe financial hardship and can provide evidence to support the claim of extraordinary circumstances, seek approval to grant the urgent payment from:

  • Technical Peer Support (TPS)
  • a Team Leader, or
  • a staff member above Team Leader delegation (this does not include social workers)

While social workers may make a recommendation for an urgent payment, full assessment, evidence and approval processes are still required.

If the Service Officer is a TPS staff member, they must also refer to another TPS or a Team Leader for secondary approval.

Service Officers at the APS4 level making a decision to deny an urgent payment are not required to seek further approval.

The amount payable is the smallest of:

  • available funds calculated by the system
  • the amount the customer requested
  • the minimum amount it will cost to meet the customer's immediate hardship need
  • the maximum amount of $200

Does the TPS, Team Leader or staff member above this delegation approve the decision to grant urgent payment?

  • Yes,
    • verification has been received ,
      - complete any Digital Image work item/s for evidence provided to support the request, then
      - see Processing urgent payment requests
    • Conditional approval has been given, go to Step 7
  • No, urgent payment is denied:
    • complete any Digital Image work item/s for evidence provided to support the request,
    • Revisit other assistance with the customer
    • The decision to deny extraordinary circumstance is final and cannot be reconsidered
    • See Processing urgent payment requests to record decision to deny request.

7

Provide conditional approval + Read more

Run the Immediate Payment requests script to record the decision and transfer the DOC to the customer's nearest service centre. Ensure all details of the verification required are included in the DOC.

Tell the customer conditional approval for the urgent payment is granted. They need to:

  • Attend their nearest service centre by close of business (COB) on the day of conditional approval (unless an earlier time is stated on Office Locator)
  • Customer to advise CLO they are there to provide verification for an urgent payment
  • Provide identity documents, including 1 photo identity document for a manual-visual comparison, and/or they will be asked questions from information on their customer record to verify Proof of Record Ownership. See Authenticating a Centrelink customer for a detailed process. If they cannot confirm their identity or establish Proof of Record Ownership the EBT card will not be issued
  • Provide the documents required to verify the extraordinary circumstances for the conditional approval

Procedure ends here for the Service Officer providing conditional approval.

8

Seek approval for decision to grant urgent payment where evidence cannot be provided of customer circumstances + Read more ...

If satisfied the customer is experiencing severe financial hardship and evidence cannot be provided to support the customer's claim of extraordinary circumstances, seek approval to grant the urgent payment from:

  • Technical Peer Support (TPS)
  • a Team Leader, or
  • a staff member above Team Leader delegation (this does not include social workers)

Include details of why you consider the urgent payment should be granted.

Does TPS, Team Leader or staff member above this delegation approve the decision to grant the urgent payment?

  • Yes, see Processing urgent payment requests
  • No, urgent payment is denied:
    • explore other assistance with the customer, then
    • see Processing urgent payment requests to record the decision to deny payment

Check if the customer meets the criteria + Read more ...

If the customer has previously been denied an urgent payment for failing to meet the exceptional and unforeseen or extraordinary circumstances the Advance Payment cannot be issued as an urgent payment.

  • severe financial hardship, and
