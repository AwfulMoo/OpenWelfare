Check if the customer is a former resident + Read more ...

Ask the customer:

  • the date of departure
  • the date they expect to return to Australia and
  • the country to which they are travelling

Once this information is obtained:

  • Check the original date of grant of the pension on the Pensions General Information (PNGI) screen. If within the last 2 years, check the date of last arrival into Australia for residence on the Country of Residence (CRES) screen

  • Check absences on the Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen. If the customer has had lengthy and/or frequent absences, the CRES screen may have to be amended

Did the customer:

  • previously reside in Australia, and
  • leave Australia and at some point reside in a country other than Australia that was not Norfolk Island, and
  • return to reside in Australia within 2 years before the next planned departure, and
  • were they granted or transferred to their current pension within 2 years before the planned departure?

This rule does not apply to some customers who transferred to Age Pension from another indefinitely portable payment type (Disability Support Pension, Wife Pension, Widow B Pension or Bereavement Allowance). It also does not apply if the customer is travelling for treatment under the Medical Treatment Overseas Program.

If the customer lived on Norfolk Island immediately before their most recent arrival in Australia and never lived in Australia before living on Norfolk Island, former resident provisions do not affect their payment and it may be necessary to code a former resident override.

For more information, see Former resident provisions or Former resident overseas absences when a payment is based on past/prior periods of residence.

Is the customer saved from proportional portability under the pre 20 September portability rules? + Read more ...

    • Resident in Australia on 8 May 1985 and going to a non-agreement country

For assistance calculating WLR, see Working Life Residence (WLR)

5

Age Pension is proportional - customers paid under current rules + Read more ...

Rate of pension will be proportional after the customer has been absent from Australia for 26 weeks. The rate of pension payable will be based on the customer's own WLR. The maximum rate of pension will be payable if the customer has more than the maximum working life residence (WLR).

Code the customer's current and historical residence on the Country of Residence (CRES) screen. The WLR will be automatically calculated and default to the Australian Working Life Residence (AWLR) screen.

Ask the customer details of their residence in Australia and record them on a DOC. These details may have to be verified before a making a final portability decision.

Completing the portability interview and coding overseas absence

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Consider whether additional add-ons are payable + Read more ...

Additional add-on payments may be payable, but each of these has their own eligibility and payability requirements that must be assessed before payment.

New rate including Pension Supplement

Add-ons and income supplements are not payable if:

  • the absence is leaving Australia to live in another country, or
  • the customer is saved under the pre 20 September 2000 portability rules

Add-ons and supplements will only continue to be paid if the customer continues to retain qualification. For example, a person must continue to pay rent to receive Rent Assistance (RA). For detailed information, see Portability of add-ons.

Once a temporary absence exceeds 6 weeks, only the Pension Supplement Basic Amount is payable, if the customer retains qualification for their primary income support payment.

Transitional rate

The transitional pension rate inside Australia will continue for 6 weeks from departure if the customer is leaving temporarily. After 6 weeks, or if the customer has left Australia to live in another country, the rate will decrease to the transitional pension rate outside Australia and any other add-ons or income supplements will cease. The transitional pension rate outside Australia will also be paid to customers saved under the pre 20 September 2000 portability rules

Coding the departure correctly will cause these payments to cease if portability rules stop payment. If entitlement ceases because of loss of qualification (for example, ceases to pay rent), code the loss of qualification to stop payment.

Add-on payments may be portable for a customer paid under an international agreement depending on the terms of the relevant agreement.

2

Changes to income, assets or other circumstances + Read more ...

Has the customer had any changes in income, assets or other types of circumstances (for example, separation from partner)?

Changes to income or assets are common, as a customer may have withdrawn money from accounts or investments to finance their trip. Any reduction in investments may entitle the customer to an increase in rate. Note: while asking if a customer has had any income or asset changes is acceptable, do not ask how they have financed their trip, as this is a breach of privacy.

If the customer is going overseas long term and owns their own home, it is very important to follow up the asset value of the home and any income (for example, rental income) which may be received.

The home property in Australia or money from the sale of the home is considered an asset immediately if the customer:

  • has permanently left the property and has not sold the home
  • has sold the home but has no intention to purchase another, or
  • owns a home overseas which will become their principal residence

If the circumstances above are not applicable, either:

  • the property will become an asset 12 months after vacation, or
  • the money from the sale of the home will become an asset 12 months after the property was sold

In addition, if the property is sold, the asset value may be exempt for 12 months but the income is deemed from the date it is received. If the asset value of the home will affect payment, particularly if it will stop payment, ensure the customer is aware this will occur after 12 months absence from their home. See Permanent vacation of principal home.

Check if the customer is renting the property and if so, obtain details of the rental amount, mortgage owed, etc. Code a Vacation of Home Property (VHP) review and issue a Questionnaire regarding property overseas or inside Australia (XOB140). This will obtain all relevant information and the authority to inspect for a valuation if required.

  • Yes,
    • Update the income and/or asset details
    • Record the information on a DOC
    • Advise the customer of any effects on their rate of payment or any possible future impact
    • Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

Complete the portability interview + Read more ...

  • Explain the decision to the customer. If applicable, ensure the customer is aware their income support payment may stop or reduce after 26 weeks absence from Australia (12 months absence if the customer is saved under the pre 20 September 2000 portability rules)
  • Any Pension Supplement received will reduce to the basic amount after 6 weeks. For more information, see Pension Supplement overseas absences
  • A transitional customer's rate will reduce to the transitional pension rate outside Australia after 6 weeks. Note: if a person remains outside Australia after their payment has ceased to be portable, their payment can be restored if they return to Australia within 13 weeks of their payment ceasing. However, if the person is receiving a transitional rate of pension they will lose entitlement to this rate and will be paid under the new rules
  • If the customer is being paid Rent Assistance (RA) check if they will stop paying rent on their departure
  • Ensure all customers understand before their departure if they have to contact when they have returned to Australia
  • Advise them of the requirements for continuation of payment on their return to Australia
  • Ask the customer if they would like to nominate an agent to act on their behalf while they are overseas and, if so, record the details on a DOC

Check if the customer has employment income or is a stimulus customer

  • Ask if the customer has employment income or is a 2WE/variable lodgement reporter. If they are, see the processing instructions in Recording and correcting employment income details

Finalise interview

  • Record details of the decision on a DOC including the dates of departure, contact details, WLR details and the decision made regarding payment
  • Give the customer the contact details for CIS so they can contact Services Australia while overseas, see Centrelink International Services (CIS) – contact details for customers
  • Ask the customer if they have any further questions
  • Record a review to issue the customer with a Pre Departure Interview - Portability Decision (XOB101) letter when the Portability Script - Departures and Returns becomes available, as it cannot generate manually. If it is an agreement portability case, the CIS Service Officer should issue an XOB999 letter with appropriate paragraphs

4

Change of address/overseas contact number + Read more ...

  • Record the change of address on Address Details (AD) screen if the customer has provided new address details. Note: it is not mandatory for the customer to provide an address outside Australia
  • Code the overseas telephone number (if available)
  • Code the new Centrelink Service Centre on the Office Code (OC) screen, especially if CIS continues payment

5

Recording the departure + Read more ...

  • On the Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen:
    • Code the date of departure in the Event date field. This must be the date the customer will leave Australia, not the date of arrival at their destination
    • Code the destination country in the Country field. Code 'XT' if the customer is travelling to multiple destinations, the destination country is unknown, or they are travelling outside Australia on a cruise
    • Leave the Reason, End date and Ext Rsn fields blank
    • Code Source and DOR fields if required
    • Press [Enter]
  • If the customer knows their exact return date, code it on the RSCD screen. Record a second line with the date of return in the Event date field and Australia in the Country field. Leave the Reason, End date and Ext Rsn fields blank

6

Travel for less than 26 weeks + Read more ...

Has the customer provided an expected date of return and is that date within 26 weeks of the departure?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Working Life/Age Residence (WLR/ WAR) + Read more ...

For CIS staff only

If the customer's rate of pension is or will be affected by their or their partner's WLR or WAR, residence details must be recorded.

Details must include at least the customer's arrival dates for permanent residence and the status on arrival, departure dates where the customer stopped residing in Australia, any changes in the customer's residence status while in Australia and, for arrivals before 1965, the name of the ship on arrival. Obtain this information by phone or by sending the customer a form with the portability letter.

Verifying the customer's residence in Australia is a discretionary decision. If verification is appropriate, there are several ways to obtain the necessary proof.

  • Activating the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen (if not already done) will provide a very quick and accurate verification of the customer's recent arrivals and legal status on arrival on the Immigration Advised Details (RSIM) and Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screens
  • Copies of documents provided by the customer
  • Microfiche and microfilm held by CIS
  • For more detail on how to work out the working life/age residence and proportional rates, see Working Life Residence (WLR) and Australian pensions paid outside Australia, the proportional rate

8

Recording residence and proportional portability + Read more ...

For CIS staff only

From the Residence Cluster Task Selector (RETS) screen, 'S'elect the Country of Residence (CRES) screen and the Additional Residence Details (ARD) screen.

  • Enter the details of countries where the customer has lived permanently (not just on a holiday or visit) on the CRES screen. See Coding the Country of Residence (CRES) screen for more information. The system will calculate the working life residence (WLR) automatically on the Australian Working Life Residence (AWLR) screen. For more detail on how to work out the working life, residence and proportional rates, see Working Life Residence (WLR). Note: if the customer is leaving Australia to live in another country, record the change on the CRES screen
  • On the ARD screen, code the date of departure in the Date of Event field
  • Code the Proportional Portability Savings field with the result determined in the earlier step. If a result is not required or the customer's payment will be proportional code 'N'. If the result of the decision is that the customer's payment will not be proportional due to portability savings, code 'Y'
  • Code the date of departure in the Date of Event field in the second block of information on the ARD screen
  • Code the WLR from the AWLR screen (or the partner's WLR if applicable) in the Aust Working Life Residence field

9

Long-term travel to New Zealand + Read more ...

If CRES and RSCD details align, the Long Term Country Code on the Residence Savings (RSS) screen will be auto populated by the system to force the correct New Zealand rate calculation method to apply.

If CRES and RSCD do not align, the system will leave the Long Term Country Code field unchanged, generating a PGR review, which will update it at maturation.

Where a customer’s departure to New Zealand is temporary in nature but exceeds 12 months in duration regard the customer as being present long-term in New Zealand but the CRES and RSCD will not align. To ensure the correct rate is paid update the Long Term Country Code field manually.

Is the customer departing temporarily for New Zealand and the intended length of absence exceeds 12 months?

  • Yes, update the Long Term Country Code field on RSS to ‘NZ’
    • Go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 10

Submit activity + Read more ...

  • Do not suppress the auto advice
  • Check the results and ensure all the correct action has occurred. Finalise the activity
