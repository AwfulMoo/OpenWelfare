Claiming periods

For claiming periods that start on or before 17 January 2022

The claiming period has closed. Late claims will not be accepted.

Note: customers could call the agency on 180 22 66 to lodge a verbal claim up to and including 28 July 2022. See Lodging claims.

For claiming periods that start on or after 18 January 2022

As the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment was re-instated and can be claimed again from 20 July 2022, there are different claiming periods, depending on the 7 day self-isolation, quarantine or caring period claimed.

The payment re-instatement has been backdated to 1 July 2022.

This means customers can claim for a self-isolation, quarantine or caring period that started:

  • between 1 and 20 July 2022 (inclusive) - claims must be lodged by 11:59 pm Eastern Standard Time on 2 August 2022
  • on or after 21 July 2022 by lodging their claim within 14 days from the start date of the 7 day period they are claiming
    • A customer’s period of self-isolation, quarantine or caring period must start on or before 30 September 2022
    • For a 7 day claiming period that starts on 30 September 2022, claims must be lodged by 11:59 pm Eastern Standard Time on 13 October 2022
    • Customers cannot claim for a self-isolation, quarantine or caring period that starts on or after 1 October 2022

The online claim will not allow late claims to be lodged.

Late claims:

  • can only be lodged over the phone, and
  • will only be considered under 6 special reasons

Staff must not tell customers what these reasons are.

For more details about late claims, see the Process page in Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment - Assisted Customer Claims.

Key policy differences

Staff must check the period of self-isolation, quarantine or caring a customer is claiming.

Different eligibility criteria apply depending on the period the customer is claiming.

Table 1:

Policy

What is the change?

Customer is claiming as they, or a person in their care, is a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19

For periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring starting:

  • on or after 18 January 2022 a definition of close contact has been included
    • it is not a requirement for customers to be informed by a required authority to self-isolate or quarantine
    • customer’s can self-declare they meet the Australian government’s definition of close contact
    • for claims lodged on or after 27 April 2022, customers can self-declare they meet their state or territory’s definition of close contact (not just the Australian government’s definition)
    • for claims lodged on or after 8 May 2022, for all close contact impact reasons, customers can self-declare their employer has directed them not to attend their workplace even though their state or territory government does not require them to self-isolate
  • on or before 17 January 2022 no definition existed for close contact
    • customers must be informed by a required authority to self-isolate or quarantine

Customer is claiming as they, or a child 16 years or under in their care has tested positive with COVID-19

For periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring starting:

  • on or after 10 January 2022
    • customers do not need to be informed by a required authority to self-isolate or quarantine
    • customers must have evidence, if requested, of:
      - a positive test result for COVID-19 from a testing centre (this includes pathology centres) or health professional (that is, evidence of a positive Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) test), or
      - evidence they have registered for a positive test result for COVID-19 with a health official, for example Services NSW (that is, evidence they have registered a positive result from a home-administered Rapid Antigen Test (RAT) with their state or territory health authority)
  • on or before 9 January 2022
    • customers must be informed by a required authority to self-isolate or quarantine

Customer is claiming as a person in their care (other than their child) has tested positive with COVID-19

For periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring starting:

  • on or after 10 January 2022
    • customers must have evidence, if requested, of:
      - a positive test result for COVID-19 from a testing centre (this includes pathology centres) or health professional (that is, evidence of a positive Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) test), or
      - evidence they have registered for a positive test result for COVID-19 with a health official, for example Services NSW (that is, evidence they have registered a positive result from a home-administered Rapid Antigen Test (RAT) with their state or territory health authority)
  • on or before 9 January 2022
    • customers did not need to be informed by a required authority to self-isolate or quarantine

Claiming period

For periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring starting:

  • on or after 18 January 2022
    • customers must lodge their claim within 14 days from and including the first day of their 7 day claiming period. See the Late claim scenarios
    • the claim closure date for the payment is 11:59pm Eastern Standard Time on 13 July 2022
  • on or before 17 January 2022
    • a customer’s period of self-isolation, quarantine or caring must start on or before 30 June 2022
    • they have until 28 July 2022 to claim

Claiming channels

For periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring starting:

  • on or after 18 January 2022
    • customers can claim online
    • phone claims are completed using Assisted Customer Claim (ACC)
    • claims are processed in Process Direct
  • on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 17 January 2022
    • customers can call Services Australia on 180 22 66 to lodge a verbal claim
    • Note: the Claim for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment (EM252) form is no longer available
    • claims are processed in Customer First
  • on or before 8 December 2021 or earlier
    • customers can call the agency on 180 22 66 to lodge a verbal claim
    • claims are processed in Customer First

See Lodging claims

Payment period and amount

For periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring starting:

  • on or after 18 January 2022
    • customers can claim a 7 day payment period
    • the rate of payment depends on the number of hours lost
      - 20 hours or more, the rate of payment is $750
      - between 8 and less than 20 hours or at least one full day of work, the rate of payment is $450
      - less than 8 hours, no Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment is payable
  • on or after 9 December 2021 and up to and including 17 January 2022
    • customers can claim a 7 day payment period of $750
  • before 9 December 2021 (up to and including 8 December 2021
    • customers can claim a 14 day payment period of $1,500

See Payment rates

Second or subsequent claims under a close contact reason

For periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring starting:

  • on or after 18 January 2022
    • customers can make 1 x 7 day claim even if the self-isolation, quarantine or caring period is greater than 7 days
    • if the customer or person in their care then tests positive with COVID-19, or the customer starts caring for a different person (because of that person being a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID, or testing positive with COVID-19), they can lodge a new claim for another period of 7 days
  • on or before 17 January 2022
    • customers can be eligible for additional periods of self-isolation or quarantine (they must be informed by a required authority to self-isolate or quarantine again)

Customer is caring for a person who has to self-isolate or quarantine as they are a close contact of a person who has test positive with COVID-19, and the person has a disability or severe medical condition

This new clause was added and applies to all policies

Income support payment (ISP) requirements

For periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring starting:

  • On or after 9 December 2021
    • customers cannot receive the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment if they receive an ISP on any day of the 7 day period
  • Before 9 December 2021 (up to and including 8 December 2021)
    • customers can receive the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment if they received an ISP for up to 13 days of the 14 day period
