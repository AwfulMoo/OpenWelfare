Checking immigration/residence and identity screens

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer advises that they and/or their partner (if applicable) are Australian citizens + Read more

This procedure can be followed when:

  • processing a new claim
  • an activity lifts a residence quarantine, and the customer's Australian visa or citizenship information is updated by the Immigration Datalink
  • error E520RS or S520RS ‘Country of citizenship as Australia has not been verified’ is presented in a claim

The new claim process will attempt a match or refresh of the customer's Immigration information using the Immigration Datalink. If successful and the information is available, the customer's record will automatically be updated with Australian citizenship grant date and passport details.

Note: the Immigration Datalink provides Australian citizenship granted since 2000.

2

Check RSLEG, RSIMME and RSS residence screens for proof of citizenship + Read more

Channel code: next to citizenship information if:

  • IMM - information came from the Immigration Datalink
  • IMC - staff in Centrelink International Services (CIS) verified the information

To check if:

  • Australian citizenship details have been recorded,
    • go to the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen
    • check the Country of citizenship
      Note:       if a customer and/or their partner do not have a record of Australian citizenship on RSLEG but has a permanent visa or is a protected SCV holder, this can be used for qualification purposes instead of Australian citizenship
  • Australian passport details have been recorded,
    • go to the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen
  • Australian citizenship has previously been manually verified by staff in CIS,
    • the Residence Savings (RSS) screen
    • check the Aust Citizenship Verification Ind: field

Note: if the data provided by the Immigration Datalink is inconsistent with what the customer has advised, see Referring inconsistencies in overseas departure and arrival information to CIS

Is there:

  • an immigration record of the person as an Australian citizen on the RSLEG screen with a channel code of IMM?, or
  • an Australian passport on the RSIMME screen with a channel code of IMM or IMC? (if yes, check that the date of event for Australian citizenship on RSLEG is correct, and update if applicable), or
  • confirmation Australian citizenship has been manually verified on the RSS screen?

Yes, and

  • if error E520RS or S520RS are presenting, see Step 11 in Table 2
  • If no errors procedure ends here

No, go to Step 3

3

Check identity documents supplied by customer for proof of citizenship + Read more ...

The customer/partner may have given evidence of Australian citizenship as part of the Identity Confirmation process.

Check verification history for Australian citizenship, passport or birth certificate documents. In:

  • Process Direct
    • go to the Identity Summary screen (via Identity Confirmation Dashboard)
  • Customer First
    • go to Identity Summary (for older records may need to check the POI Historical Details (POIH) screen)

Has the customer/partner provided an Australian citizenship or passport document?

  • Yes, check that the date of event for Australian citizenship on the RSLEG screen is correct, and update if applicable, procedure ends here
  • No, if the customer has:
    • provided an Australian or Norfolk Island birth certificate, and/or they commenced to reside in Australia before 26 January 1949, go to Step 6
    • provided a Papua New Guinea birth certificate dated prior to 16 September 1975, go to Step 7
    • no identity documents proving Australian citizenship, go to Step 4

4

Check for permanent visa or protected Special Category visa (SCV) + Read more ...

Check the customer’s visas on the RSLEG screen.

If the visa in the Subclass Code: field is:

  • 444 check the New Zealand residence status: field. This will show if the customer is a protected (GRF or TRA) or not protected (NOT) SCV holder
  • not a 444, check the visa subclass number or the Residence Results Display (RSRD) screen to determine if it is a permanent or temporary visa

Does the customer hold a protected SCV or permanent visa?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, and the customer cannot provide verification of citizenship or permanent residence, see Step 6 in Table 2

5

No record of citizenship but permanent visa or protected SCV status is confirmed + Read more ...

If a person is not an Australian citizen, but holds a protected SCV or permanent visa, they are entitled to payments and concession cards, but they may be subject to a Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP). (A person can receive an exemption from the NARWP if they are an Australian citizen.)

Check the table of payments subject to NARWP and if possible identify if the customer has already served the NARWP.

Would removal of Australian citizenship coding affect the customer’s qualification to an entitlement?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, the customer does not need to serve a NARWP or they have already served the NARWP
    • As citizenship has not been proven but a permanent visa can be confirmed, this should be used for qualification purposes
    • If Australian citizenship has been recorded on the RSLEG screen, delete the entry and code their Country of Citizenship
    • In the absence of anything else, code the customer/partners citizenship as their country of birth and the relevant visa subclass. For assistance coding the information, see Recording legal residence status
    • Continue with the relevant activity
    • Procedure ends here

6

Residing in Australia prior to 26 January 1949 + Read more ...

Was the person born before 26 January 1949 and were they residing in Australia or Norfolk Island on 26 January 1949?

  • Yes, the person is an Australian citizen
    • Ensure the Country of Residence (CRES) screen reflects the correct date the customer commenced residing in Australia. Further verification of residence is not required. See Step 10 in Table 2
    • If the person was residing on Norfolk Island on that date, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Born in Papua New Guinea (PNG) prior to 16 September 1975 + Read more ...

Was the person born in PNG before 16 September 1975?

  • Yes, the customer is likely an Australian citizenship see Step 7 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Born in Australia prior to 20 August 1986 + Read more ...

Was the person born in Australia before 20 August 1986?

  • Yes, the person is likely an Australian citizen. see Step 7 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Born in Australia or Norfolk Island after 20 August 1986 + Read more ...

Was the person born in Australia, either:

  • after 20 August 1986 and at least one of their parents (at time of the person's birth) were:
    • an Australian citizen,
    • a permanent visa holder or protected SCV holder?
  • between 1 September 1994 and 26 February 2001 and at least one of their parents
    • has a New Zealand SCV holder (protected or non protected)?

Yes, go to Step 10

No, go to Step 11

10

Born in Australia or Norfolk Island after 20 August 1986 to permanent residents + Read more ...

To confirm Australian citizenship for a person born in Australia or Norfolk Island after 20 August 1986 verification that one of their parents is an Australian citizen, permanent visa holder or SCV holder is required.

Note: if the person was born on Norfolk Island, they should provide verification that one of their parents was an Australian citizen or permanent visa holder or official Norfolk Island resident at that time.

Verification may be:

  • information found on parent’s Centrelink record such as their Australian:
    • birth certificate
    • passport (current or expired) or
    • Citizenship Certificate
  • evidence of parent’s permanent visa

Has Australian legal permanent legal residence been verified for one of the customer’s parents?

  • Yes, the person is likely to be an Australia citizen, see Step 7 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 11

11

Born in Australia after 20 August 1986 and residing in Australia up to age 10 + Read more ...

A person may acquire Australian citizenship if they were born in Australia and have lived here for 10 years regardless of what type of visa their parent(s) had. Ask for verification of citizenship if not already provided.

Was the person born in Australia after 20 August 1986 and did they reside in Australia up until their 10th birthday?

  • Yes. Make sure their period residing in Australia has been recorded on the Country of Residence (CRES) screen:
    • If the customer is subsequently providing an Australian passport or Australian citizenship certificate as proof, see Step 4 in Table 2
    • If not already recorded and the customer cannot provide verification, see Step 7 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 12

12

Born in Australia after 20 August 1986 and not residing in Australia up to age 10 + Read more ...

The customer must provide proof, either an:

  • Australian passport
  • Australian Citizenship Certificate
  • Australian Citizenship extract

Note: an Australian or Norfolk Island birth certificate is not sufficient evidence and cannot be accepted as proof of citizenship (in this instance).

Check the customer's record:

  • If already recorded on the POIH, RSIMME or Identity Summary screens. see Step 12 in Table 2
  • If not already recorded and the customer:
    • subsequently provides one of these as proof, see Step 8 in Table 2
    • cannot provide verification, see Step 7 in Table 2

Proof of citizenship + Read more ...

4

Verification of citizenship is not immediately available + Read more ...

Check the reason for updating citizenship is it:

  • as part of the First Contact Services Offer (FCSO) workflow
  • as part of a verbal claim for a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC), or
  • to prevent cancellation for a residence related reason due to an update (for example. Portability interview, Health Care Card reissue)?

Is the reason for updating citizenship listed above?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, Australian Citizenship must be verified before the claim or update can be finalised
    • If a customer has the documents at home, ask the customer to upload their supporting documents using their Centrelink online account. Record contact and any relevant information in a Note/DOC
    • If the customer does not have the documents at home, record contact and any relevant information in a Note/DOC
    • Go to Step 6

5

Verification of citizenship is not immediately required + Read more ...

The Service Officer is:

  • indexing a claim using the First Contact Service Offer (FCSO) workflow
  • processing a LIC verbal claim, or
  • correcting a cancellation for a residence related reason

Generally, residence information may be accepted pending return of the information, unless the Service Officer has justifiable doubts as to the validity of the information provided.

If necessary, ask the customer to provide verification within 14 days. Staff should record the information provided by the customer on the system, they should not finalise the claim without this verification being provided.

Indexing a claim using FCSO

The Service Officer should code the minimum residence information provided by the customer on the system to allow FCSO to finalise.

This will generally require the Country of Birth: field and Lived or travelled outside Australia since 1 Sept 1994: field on the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen as well as the Date of Event: and Country of Citizenship: fields to be updated on the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen.

Tell the customer they should provide documents to verify their details when they lodge their claim. The residence information should be verified before finalising the claim.

Processing a LIC verbal claim

Residence information should be entered on the system to allow the workflow to be completed. If the customer is already receiving payment, verification would generally not be necessary.

Cancellation for a residence related reason during a non-residence related update

Customers who have a residence quarantine on their record may have their payment cancelled for a residence related reason when their residence information is updated.

The customer’s residence information should be entered on the system to allow the update to be finalised without cancellation occurring. If their Country of Residence (CRES) screen shows that they have been residing in Australia on and before 1 September 1994, the Service Officer can update the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen with the visa subclass '111' and visa grant date as date they started residing in Australia in order to restore the payment.

Continue with the activity and advise the customer to provide documents as follow up if necessary.

Procedure ends here

6

Confirm whether verification of citizenship is required + Read more ...

It is not necessary for a customer and/or their partner to be Australian citizens for the customer to be residentially qualified for a Centrelink administered payment, if they meet other residence criteria.

If any of the following apply, it is generally not necessary to record a person's Australian citizenship before granting their claim:

  • the person is a permanent visa or protected SCV holder, or
  • the person qualifies for the payment or card as a non-protected SCV holder or has a certain temporary visas that qualify customers for some payments
  • the person qualifies for parental leave payments, family assistance and concession cards (excluding Ex Carer Allowance Health Care Card (EHC)) if they are a non-protected SCV holder.
  • if the person is claiming a NARWP affected payment, it has already been served or the customer is exempt from the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) without reliance on their Australian citizenship

Do any of the above apply (and are verified)?

  • Yes, as Australian citizenship has not been proven but a permanent visa can be confirmed, only the permanent visa should be coded.
    • If Australian citizenship has been recorded on RSLEG, delete the entry
    • If their country of citizenship is not known, code their country of birth as their country of citizenship. For assistance coding the information, see Recording legal residence status
    • Continue with the relevant activity
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Request verification from CIS + Read more ...

If the customer does not have any verification, Services Australia can help in verifying both citizenship and/or permanent visa status for the customer and/or their partner.

If the customer states they or their partner (as appropriate) are an Australian citizen, coding the Country of Citizenship as 'AU' will generally create an error in the activity unless:

  • an identity confirmation exemption has been recorded or
  • a suitable confirmation document is recorded on the POIH or Identity Summary screens or
  • the datalink has verified Australian citizenship

If the edits occurs after procedures have been followed to this point, refer to CIS. To send a request for verification of citizenship to CIS:

In Process Direct: + Read more ...

  • select > Referral
  • Referral type: International Services (CIS)
  • Referral reason: Residence Verification Service Request
  • On the Referral details screen, complete the Additional information template

If the customer was born on or after 20 August 1986, and verification of one or both parents' details are also required, note this in the Referral and detail if the parent is:

  • an Australian citizen
  • permanent visa holder parent
  • an SCV holder (for customers born before 26 February 2001)
  • a protected SCV holder (for customers born on or after 26 February 2001)

In Customer First: + Read more ...

  • use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Centrelink International Services > Residence > Intl - verify citizenship request

Include the following information in the Fast Note:

  • PPT: overseas passport number/country of issue if available
  • Name: correct spelling of name and if married include maiden name
  • Alias: all known aliases
  • PAR: if the customer arrived as a child with parents, full names and dates of births of parents

If the customer was born on or after 20 August 1986, and verification of one or both parents' details are also required, note this in the Fast Note and detail if the parent is:

  • an Australian citizen
  • permanent visa holder parent
  • an SCV holder (before 26 February 2001)
  • a protected SCV holder (on or after 26 February 2001)

Place the new claim or activity on hold awaiting a response from CIS + Read more ...

When CIS has completed their investigation, continue with this process.

CIS will investigate, document their findings and take the new claim or activity off hold for reallocation via WLM. If citizenship has been verified, the Aust Citizenship Verification Ind: field on the Residence Savings (RSS) screen may also have been updated by CIS staff.

Has citizenship been verified?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Customer can contact Home Affairs for verification of citizenship + Read more ...

If staff have not been able to verify citizenship by any other means, tell the customer they:

  • can apply for evidence of Australian citizenship from Home Affairs through their website
  • or they can contact Home Affairs to request evidence
  • it is the customer's choice and there is a cost

Note: Services Australia will not make the customer take this option and are just letting the customer know that it is available.

Has the customer stated they will request evidence of citizenship from Home Affairs?

  • Yes, put the claim or activity on hold awaiting return of the evidence. Procedure ends here.
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Person is not an Australian citizen or customer cannot provide evidence + Read more ...

  • The relevant person is not to be recorded as an Australian citizen
  • If the person was born outside Australia, record their country of birth from the RSIMME screen as their country of citizenship on RSLEG screen. If the customer has proof of a permanent residence visa, or RSLEG has a record of a permanent residence visa received from the Immigration Datalink, use this rather than the Australian citizenship
  • If the person was born on Norfolk Island before 20 August 1986, record their country of citizenship as Australia. If after this date, record the country of citizenship of the customer's parent(s). Do not record the country of citizenship as Norfolk Island
  • If the person's first arrival was before 1 September 1994 and they were residing in Australia at the time, accept permanent residence, unless there is reason to believe otherwise. Go to the RSLEG screen and record the country of citizenship as the country of birth and manually add a visa, code '111' as the Subclass with 1 September 1994 as the Grant Date
  • If the person's first arrival was after 1 September 1994 and no records can be located, the claim should not be granted until the customer provides some proof of legal residence status
  • Record information and reasons for decision on a Note/DOC
  • Continue with the new claim or activity

Procedure ends here.

10

Check if proof of citizenship is recorded + Read more

  • To be accepted, an Australian passport, citizenship certificate of birth certificate must be recorded either:
    • on the RSIMME screen or
    • as part of Identity Confirmation process, or
    • previously on the POIH screen
  • If the Service Officer is adding Australian passport details to the RSIMME screen it is necessary to refresh the Immigration Datalink to verify the passport via the datalink

Note: if any of these documents cannot be recorded as above (for example Identity Confirmation has been finalised and a different commencement or identity document has been used), CIS staff will need to record the verification.

Have any of these documents been recorded?

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, go to Step 11

11

Australian citizenship needs to be recorded on RSS screen + Read more

Make sure all verification document(s) have been scanned to the customer's record.

Refer the case to CIS for coding.

  • In Process Direct:
  • select > Referral
  • Referral type: International Services (CIS)
  • Referral reason: Residence Verification Service Request
  • Add relevant information, for example 'Australian cit. verification request' and list the documents verifying Australian citizenship that have been scanned to the customer’s record
  • Select Finish
  • In Customer First:
    • use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Centrelink International Services > Residence > RSS coding required for AU citizen

Hold the claim / activity

Hold the new claim or activity until CIS replies.

CIS staff will record the Aust Citizenship Verification Ind on the Residence Savings (RSS) screen. This screen can only be coded by CIS staff.

Has citizenship been verified on RSS for the relevant person?

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, clarify any further requirements with CIS. Procedure ends here

12

Customer/partner is an Australian citizen and evidence has been provided and recorded + Read more ...

  • Make sure Country of Citizenship recorded as Australia on the RSLEG screen
  • and that the date of event for Australian citizenship is correct
  • Request the Immigration Datalink on the RSIMME screen if Australian passport details are not recorded with an IMM channel
  • Finalise activity or continue with new claim (if relevant)
  • Record details on a Note/DOC
