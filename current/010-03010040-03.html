All Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme assessments are completed by the AIC Smart Centres.

This page contains information on how to assist customers to complete the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Current Tax Year Assessment (SY042).

AIC form SY042

Completing the Assistance for Isolated Children form (SY042)

This table describes information on how to complete the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Current Tax Year Assessment (SY042).

Item

Description

1

Income has reduced + Read more ...

The Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Current Tax Year Assessment (SY042) is completed by the AIC applicant to advise a drop in income if:

  • They are claiming Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA), and
  • there has been a substantial reduction in income in the current financial year and the drop in income will be for at least two years from the later of the date of the drop or 1 January in the year of study. The current year income may be used in the Parental Income Test instead of their income for the base tax year. See References for more information.

For a current income assessment, the customer will need to state:

  • The date the drop in income occurred.
  • How long they think the reduction will last.
  • An estimate of the duration of the drop in income.
  • Reason for the drop in income (e.g. retirement, permanent invalidity, retrenchment, long term unemployment, drought, bush fires or other circumstances causing hardship). The reason should be one that could reasonably be expected to last for at least two years. For more information, see References.
  • Date last worked.
  • If they have ceased employment, information on lump sum payments received.
  • An estimate of their expected income for the current financial year.

2

Income has increased + Read more ...

The SY042 is completed by the AIC applicant to advise an increase in income if.

  • the applicant is claiming Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA), and
  • parental income in the current tax year has increased by more than 25% from their income for the base tax year, and
  • parental income in the current tax year is more than 25% higher than the parental income free area, see A-B payment rates and thresholds

The applicant should complete the SY042 as soon as they are aware of the increase in income.

Customers need to provide an estimate of their (and their partner's) income for the current tax year.

If a reverse current income assessment is necessary after an increase in income, entitlement to ABA is reassessed from 1 October of the year of study.

3

Estimate income for current financial year + Read more ...

When the AIC applicant (and partner) are estimating their income for the current tax year (1 July to 30 June) they should include the following:

  • estimated taxable income from all sources in Australia including any taxable lump sum payments received or expected to be received upon termination of employment (for example, severance, superannuation and payments in lieu of long service leave)
  • an estimate of all income earned overseas
  • reportable superannuation contributions
  • expected total net investment losses (negative gearing)
  • child support or partner maintenance paid (this is deducted from current tax year income)
  • fringe benefits - if the applicant/partner expects to receive fringe benefits from an employer, as shown on any payment summary from their employer

Warning on estimating income

Advise the applicant that underestimating income may result in an AIC ABA debt which will have to be repaid. Where doubt exists, it is better to over-estimate. An extra payment can be made if actual income turns out to be lower.

Income is checked with the Australian Taxation Office and AIC entitlement is reassessed if actual income is different to estimated income.

4

Declaration + Read more ...

The information provided on SY042 can only be processed if the applicant (and partner) has signed the declaration.

5

Approval of estimate for a drop in income + Read more ...

Before an estimate is approved for the purpose of the concession, the following will be taken into consideration:

  • Is the estimate reasonable?
  • Has there been a substantial reduction in parental income when the current year income is compared with the base year?
  • Have the applicant and partner included their actual income for the current year including any Superannuation or lump sum payments?

Note: where the income drops during the financial year, applicants sometimes provide their projected reduced income as their estimate.

6

Date of Effect for a current income assessment + Read more ...

  • If the drop in income occurred before 1 January in the year of study, the date of effect for the concession will be 1 January
  • If the income dropped after 1 January in the year of study, the date of effect will generally be the date that the income dropped
  • If the drop in income is the termination of a parent's employment and annual leave and or long service leave was paid out, then the number of weeks of paid leave is added to the date that the employment ceased. (e.g. If a parent ceased employment on 1 March and had 10 weeks of leave and long service paid out in their termination payment, the date of effect would be 10 May)
