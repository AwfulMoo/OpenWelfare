Death of both members of a couple

Step

Action

1

Customer outside Australia and/or paid under an international agreement? + Read more ...

Is the customer outside Australia/ and or paid under an international agreement?

  • Yes, refer to International Services. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Initial actions + Read more ...

When a person contacts to advise about the death of a customer, staff must offer:

  • A social worker referral in the first instance or at any time the notifier becomes or shows signs of distress during the contact. See Referring customers to a social worker for more information
  • The ‘Needing help after someone has died? (HD001)’ pamphlet. Issue if accepted

The following cultural issues need to be respected and considered by Service Officers when being advised of the death of an Indigenous customer:

  • the name of the deceased person cannot be mentioned by immediate family members. This means immediate family members may have to ask other relatives/friends to call on their behalf or attend the interview with the notifier, because they cannot say the name of the deceased
  • in this situation, it may also be offensive to the person for the Service Officer to say the name of the deceased customer, if unsure, Service Officers should sensitively ask the person if it is all right for them to use the deceased customer's name. If the person is not comfortable with this, avoid using the deceased customer's name wherever possible. See Cultural Considerations when someone dies

Does either of the deceased customers' records indicate that they were income managed, as shown by INM on the benefit status line?

  • Yes, follow process to update record for death of an income managed customer. Return to this procedure and go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Confirm the death of the deceased customer + Read more ...

If both members of a couple die at the same time, the older partner is deemed to have died first and the younger partner is regarded as the 'survivor' when assessing entitlement to a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment or Partner Bereavement Payment. Select the older partner first.

If advice of the death of both members of a couple is received at the same time but they did not die at the same time, action the deaths in the order of the customer who died first.

Run the Notification of death guided procedure in the deceased customer’s record that will be processed first, to determine if death can be confirmed.

Request confirmation of death from the informant. This can be accepted where the customer’s full name is provided together with 2 of the following items:

  • last address in full
  • date of birth and age at death
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • partner's name

See Confirmation of the death of an adult or child.

Confirm if the person notifying of the death is the executor of the estate. If the informant is not the executor, but knows the executor details, obtain:

  • the name and address of the executor, and
  • the name of the funeral director or hospital

Nominee arrangements and Power of Attorney (POA) stop at the time of death. Any correspondence, including the payment summary and possible debt recovery action from a deceased estate, will be sent to the:

  • executor, or
  • administrator of an estate

Services Australia's ability to finalise any outstanding matters will be impacted if executor or administrator details are:

  • incorrectly recorded, or
  • not recorded when details have been provided

Where executor details are not known at the time of death notification, the executor details fields in the Notification of Death guided procedure must be left blank.

Note: executor details can be recorded manually on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen if details become known later.

Can informant supply enough information to confirm the death of the selected customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Death cannot be confirmed at initial contact + Read more ...

Using the Notification of death guided procedure, obtain and record all relevant information from the informant including:

  • their own name, address, contact number and relationship to the deceased
  • executor details, if known
  • name of the funeral director or hospital where the deceased passed away, if known

The guided procedure will not code the death, but will record details on a DOC. This becomes an open work item and will be allocated to a Service Officer with the required skill set to confirm the death.

If the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item. Use Fast Note – select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED.

Repeat the above steps for the partner.

When multiple notifications are received in relation to the death of the same person do not re-run the guided procedure. Check the record and annotate the original DOC to state details are correct and who advised.

If in doubt as to the deceased's identity contact the funeral director or hospital where the deceased passed away. If this is not an option and contact with relatives is proposed, social worker involvement may be necessary.

Where death cannot be confirmed and the customer's whereabouts cannot be established, payment is to be suspended until the death can be confirmed. The suspension date of event should be the unconfirmed date of death or the date paid to plus 1 (DPT + 1), whichever is the later:

  • select the suspension option in the Notification of Death guided procedure, or
  • manually code the suspension for reason 'OTH' (Other)

Record a DOC on the customer's record, ensuring that it meets GIR DOC Minimum Standards for significant decisions and including what action is required to confirm the death.

Do not issue a suspension letter. Cancel the advice activity on the Activity List (AL) screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension.

For more information, see:

  • Confirmation of the death of an adult or child, and
  • Suspension of payments

Note: the death must be confirmed and actioned as a matter of urgency.

Procedure ends here until death can be confirmed.

5

Check payments + Read more ...

Check if the deceased customer receiving any of the following payments:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) instalments
  • Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • Ancillary benefits for a Regular Care Child
  • Paid Parental Leave (PPL)
  • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)

If yes,

  • Code the child out of care
  • See Death of a Family Assistance or Paid Parental Leave Scheme Customer Process page, Table 1 Steps 5 and 6

If this activity submits to QOL, request an urgent QOL check so the death action can be finalised.

6

Check for started activities + Read more ...

Restore payment if suspended and determine if any arrears are payable, taking into consideration the confirmed date of death. The restoration advice must be inhibited, as it is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer.

If both partners died at the same time access the elder customer's record first. Otherwise access the record of the person who died first (this is important to ensure correct Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP)).

A death action cannot be processed with activities on the Activity List (AL) screen. The Notification of death guided procedure will provide a prompt if there is an activity on the AL screen.

Note: if a deceased customer’s record was suspended while the death was being confirmed and their death has now been confirmed, check for started activities on the AL screen manually.

Where possible ensure any activities are completed and run the daily driver (DDR):

  • if there is a 'STA' debt activity on the AL screen, create an open work item for region XWA advising them of the death and requesting the debt activity be completed.
    Use Fast note - select Auto text, use Debts > DMB Raising > Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request. See Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer
  • if there is an RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM activity on the AL screen, add the keyword RCAPRI. The RCA team will action the activity and complete the death action
  • for other activities, immediately contact the team or Service Officer who needs to complete the outstanding activity. If unsure who to contact, refer to existing escalation channels

Have started activities been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

When it is not possible to complete an activity + Read more ...

If there is an outstanding activity that the Service Officer is unable to complete, the customer's payment should be suspended:

  • manually suspend with reason 'OTH' (Other)
  • the suspension date of event is the date of death or date paid to plus 1 (DPT + 1), whichever is the later
  • record a DOC on the customer's record, ensuring that it meets GIR DOC Minimum Standards for significant decisions and including what action must occur so the death can be actioned
  • as the customer's death has been confirmed, no letter must be sent. It is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension
  • if an open doc/work item regarding the death notification has not already been created:
    • run the Notification of death guided procedure to generate an open work item for the relevant staff to complete the death action once the outstanding activities have been completed, or
    • if the guided procedure is not available create an open work item.
      Use Fast note - select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED

When activity is completed, go to Step 8.

8

Check if restoration is needed + Read more ...

Is payment suspended?

  • Yes, restore payment if suspended. Ensure that no arrears are issued. Inhibit the restoration advice, as it is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 11

9

Check reporting date + Read more ...

Was either of the deceased customers due to report, or lodge a reporting statement?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 12

10

Deceased customer was due to report + Read more ...

Are the deceased customer's employment income details for the reporting period in which they passed away known or can the person notifying of the death provide the customer's employment income details for the last reporting period?

  • Yes:
    • use the Earnings and Reporting tool to process the reporting statement. Note: the date of lodgement on the Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen may need to be changed first to the day before the date of death. For more information see Changing Entitlement Period End Dates (EPED)
    • go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 11

11

Stimulate the payment + Read more ...

Stimulate the payment using the Earnings and reporting workflow tool.

Take the deceased customer off reporting by updating the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen.

12

Process the death actions + Read more ...

If both partners died at the same time, access the elder customer's record first. Otherwise, access the record of the person who died first.

Run the Notification of death guided procedure to code the death action.

If the guided procedure is not available

  • Manually code the death action
  • When recording a death, the Notification handler (NOHL) is incorrectly using the date of notification as the date to increase the survivor's rate to the single rate. If coding the death action manually, the workaround for this is to record the date of receipt (DOR) as the later of date of death or DPT + 1

Record a DOC with information about the death notification.

Inhibit letters to both members of the deceased couple. Ensure that any bereavement letter are not sent.

13

Check entitlement to bereavement payments + Read more ...

Both partners' deaths advised at the same time.

The LBP is calculated using the rates applicable on the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) after the survivor's death if:

  • both members of a couple die within the initial bereavement period (i.e. within 14 weeks of each other) and
  • the deaths are advised at the same time

See Lump Sum Bereavement Payment and Calculation of a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment:

  • ensure that letters to both members of the couple are inhibited before completing the partner's death action. This ensures that any bereavement letter is not sent to the partner who died later
  • the LBP is payable to the surviving partner's estate or the most appropriate person
  • if both members of the couple were pensioners the system will calculate and pay the LBP. However, the LBP amount can be checked before it is issued:
    • select the BLSP line, in the Nxt field and key 'OOP'
    • update the LBP field if the amount is incorrect
  • if the couple were both allowees or a pensioner/allowee couple the LBP must be manually calculated and paid.
  • if the survivor received JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance, they may be eligible for LBP or Partner Bereavement Payment. See Assessing entitlement to a Partner Bereavement payment (PBV) and Assessing entitlement to a Lump Sum Bereavement payment (LBP)
  • record the details of the bereavement on a DOC. Note: the Notification of Death guided procedure should auto-record all of these details

14

In the partner's record + Read more ...

If both members of a couple die on the same day, an adjustment may need to be made to the Marital Status (MS) screen of the 'survivor' to enable their death action to be completed. This is required when the error 'E167CU - Date of death must be on or after last relationship start date' appears on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen:

  • go to MS screen
  • 'C'orrect the event date the customer became widowed to the day before
  • finalise the activity by navigating to Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • zero any Before Limiting Date (BLD) adjustments. The BLD period should only be for the adjusted and original widowed dates, that is ,one day

Run the Notification of death guided procedure (include executor details, if known).

If the guided procedure is unavailable, manually code the death action.

Identify any service provided to be advised of the death.

Executor details can be updated after the death has been recorded, if details were not available at the time of death notification, but became later.
