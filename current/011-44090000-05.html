Immigration data link search results

Table 1: what Service Officers must consider when a client status is refused/withdrawn or the search returns multiple permanent residency (PR) applications, multiple client matches or conflicting information.

Client status

Details

Refused

Considerations:

  • Has the applicant lodged an appeal against the refused decision? Note: this information can be obtained from the MES application form

What it means:

  • Evidence of all permanent residency application dates is required
  • May impact the period in which the applicant is eligible for a MES – MES eligibility dates need to be determined, search the MES database and then process application in the MES database, see Table 4 and Table 5
  • If an appeal has been lodged, the applicant continues to be eligible for Medicare, and therefore not be eligible for a MES, search the MES database and then process application in the MES database as either reject or pend, see Table 4 and Table 7

Withdrawn

Withdrawal of a permanent residency application occurs at the applicant's request. Evidence of an appeal is not required in this circumstance.

Considerations:

  • Does the applicant have another valid permanent residency application/s that covers the period they are claiming?

What it means:

  • Evidence of all permanent residency application dates is required
  • May impact the period the applicant is eligible for a MES - MES eligibility dates will need to be determined, search the MES database and then process application in the MES database, see Table 4 and Table 5
  • Although 1 permanent residency application may be withdrawn, the applicant may have lodged another valid permanent residency application that is still ongoing. The applicant may be eligible for Medicare, and not eligible for a MES, search the MES database and then process application in the MES database as either reject or pend, see Table 4 and Table 5

Multiple permanent residency applications

A person can lodge more than 1 application with the Department of Home Affairs.

Considerations:

  • Evidence of all permanent residency application dates is required
  • Do the permanent residency application dates fall within or before the period in which the applicant is claiming a MES?
  • Are all permanent residency applications valid – does 1 permanent residency application have a client status of ‘Lodged’?
  • Do the permanent residency (PR) applications have a Refused or Withdrawn status? Refer to information above

What it means:

  • May impact the period the applicant is eligible for a MES - MES eligibility dates will need to be determined, search the MES database and then process application in the MES database, see Table 4 and Table 5
  • If there is a valid application and it covers the MES claiming period, the applicant may be eligible for Medicare, and therefore not eligible for a MES, search the MES database and then process application in the MES database as either reject or pend, see Table 4 and Table 5

Immigration data link returns multiple matches for the applicant

Check:

  • Confirm that all data links are for the same person, for instance check the date of birth in the search results matches the date of birth included on the application or supporting documentation is correct
  • Search results could include persons with similar personal details

Conflicting information

If information is conflicting between one or more of the following:

  • MES application form
  • supporting documentation, Immigration data links
  • CDMS – entitlement information/comments

and MES eligibility cannot be determined, escalate the application to a more experienced Service Officer by recategorising the work item

Medicare entitlement types and MES eligibility

Table 5: MES eligibility based on a person's Medicare entitlement type, including under a Reciprocal Health Care Agreement (RHCA).

Item

Description

1

Australian citizens

Australian citizens are eligible for a MES if they:

  • have been absent from Australia for 5 years or more (from initial departure), and
  • have not returned to reside

2

Permanent residents (migrants)

Permanent residents of Australia are eligible for a MES if they:

  • have been absent from Australia for 12 months or more, or
  • do not live in Australia (have never resided in Australia), and/or
  • have not returned to Australia to reside

Australian permanent resident holders or Australian citizens who reside in Cocos (Keeling), Christmas or Lord Howe Islands are eligible to enrol in Medicare.

3

New Zealand citizens

New Zealand citizens are eligible for a MES if they travel frequently in and out of Australia and spend less than 6 months in a 12 month period in Australia or does not intend to reside in Australia.

From 1 July 2014, if an applicant is a New Zealand citizen travelling frequently in and out of Australia and it has been assessed that they spend 6 or more months in a 12 month period in Australia, they are considered to be residing in Australia and are eligible to enrol in Medicare. Any previously approved applications for a MES are not relevant to the current application.

4

Applicants for permanent residency

Applicants for permanent residency, including applicants for Permanent Protection, are eligible for a MES if any of the following apply:

  • they do not have permission to work, and they do not have a spouse, de facto, parent or child who is an Australian citizen, New Zealand citizen or permanent resident holder
  • the application for permanent residency has been refused and they have not lodged an appeal against the refused decision relating to a permanent resident visa application
  • they have applied under an Aged Parent visa (subclasses 804 or 103) and are not eligible to be enrolled in Medicare under a Reciprocal Health Care Agreement (RHCA), or
  • they have applied for a Contributory Parent visa (subclass 143 or 864), but do not hold a Temporary Contributory Parent visa (subclass 173 or 884), and are not eligible to be enrolled in Medicare under an RHCA

5

Retirees (visa subclasses 405 and 410)

Retirees are eligible for a MES if they:

  • hold a visa subclass 405 and are not enrolled in Medicare, or
  • hold a visa subclass 410 issued after 1 December 1998 and are not enrolled in Medicare

Visa subclass 410 applied or issued before 1 December 1998

People who were issued a visa subclass 410 before 1 December 1998 and are from a Reciprocal Health Care Agreement (RHCA) country had the option to:

  • participate in the Medicare program, or
  • apply to receive a Medicare levy exemption annually, see Resources for a link to ATO – Medicare levy exemption information

This excludes visitors from Belgium, Norway and Slovenia as these countries did not have an RHCA with Australia before 1 December 1998.

See RHCA eligibility for Medicare and Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS).

The holder of a visa subclass 410 applied or issued before 1 December 1998 is eligible for a MES if:

  • they have no record in CDMS or their Medicare record states Medicare levy exemption in the Comments tab, and
  • they are not from an RHCA country

6

RHCA - Belgium

A Belgian citizen or a non-Belgian citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • lived in Belgium for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia or
  • lived in Belgium for 6 month or more prior to arriving in Australia but cannot provide one of the following:
    • evidence of being insured in the Belgian Health system, or
    • a valid European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) bearing the initials BE

7

RHCA - Finland

A Finnish citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • lived in Finland for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia
  • are a Finnish student

A non-Finnish citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • are a Finnish student
  • lived in Finland for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia
  • lived in Finland for more than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia, but cannot provide one of the following:
    • a valid European Health Insurance card (EHIC) bearing the initials FI, or
    • valid sickness Insurance Card issued by the Finnish Social Insurance Institution, or
    • 2 residency documents

8

RHCA - Republic of Ireland

A Republic of Ireland citizen or non-Republic of Ireland citizen is eligible for a MES if they lived in the Republic of Ireland for 6 months or more before arriving in Australia.

Note: if they lived in the Republic of Ireland for less than 6 months, their Medicare eligibility would depend on their last place of residence. The Reciprocal Health Care Agreement (RHCA) provides access to limited health care while in Australia and medical treatment in a public hospital as a public patient only. They are not issued with a Medicare card.

9

RHCA - Italy

An Italian citizen is eligible for a MES if:

  • They do not hold current Italian health insurance (EHIC or Tessera Sanitaria card)
  • They can provide evidence from Registry of Italians Residing Abroad (AIRE) that proves their Italian healthcare is no longer valid
  • They have already been in Australia for a period beyond 6 months from their date of arrival (and have not departed) Note: applicants may be eligible for MES for part of a financial year that is a period beyond 6 months from their arrival until the remainder of their authorised stay. Applicants may hold a Medicare card for 6 months from their arrival date

A non-Italian citizen is eligible for a MES if they lived in Italy prior to arriving in Australia (only Italian Citizens are eligible for Medicare under the Reciprocal Health Care Agreement (RHCA)).

10

RHCA - Malta

A Maltese citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • lived in Malta for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia
  • have already been in Australia for a period beyond 6 months from their date of arrival (and have not departed)
  • are a Maltese student

A Maltese citizen is eligible for Medicare for the first 6 months of their arrival in Australia. If their stay in Australia exceeds 6 months and they do not depart, they may be entitled to a MES for the remainder of their authorised stay.

A non-Maltese citizen (who resided in Malta prior to arriving in Australia) is eligible for a MES.

11

RHCA - Netherlands

A Dutch citizen or a non-Dutch citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • lived in the Netherlands for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia (including students), or
  • lived in the Netherlands for 6 months prior to arriving in Australia, but cannot provide one of the following:
    • evidence of valid basic health insurance with a Netherlands health insurer, or
    • a valid European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) bearing the initials NL
    • evidence that their EHIC or basic insurance in the Netherlands has been cancelled

12

RHCA - New Zealand

A New Zealand citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • lived in New Zealand before entering Australia and are
  • visiting Australia for less than 6 months in a 12 month period
  • are a New Zealand Diplomat
  • do not intend to reside in Australia

A non-New Zealand citizen is eligible for a MES if they lived in in New Zealand before prior to arriving in Australia.

If they lived in New Zealand for less than 6 months, their Medicare eligibility would depend on their last place of residence (if they do not meet other Medicare eligibility criteria).

13

RHCA - Norway

A Norwegian citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • are a Norwegian student
  • are a Norwegian Diplomat
  • lived in Norway for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia
  • lived in Norway for 6 months or more prior to arriving in Australia, but cannot provide one of the following:
    • evidence of a valid membership with the Norwegian National Insurance Scheme
    • a valid European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) bearing the initials NO

A non-Norwegian citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • are a student in Norway
  • lived in Norway for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia
  • lived in Norway for 6 months or more prior to arriving in Australia, but cannot provide one of the following
    • evidence of a valid membership with the Norwegian National Insurance Scheme
    • a valid European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) bearing the initials NO

14

RHCA - Slovenia

A Slovenian citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • lived in Slovenia for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia, or
  • lived in Slovenia for 6 months or more prior to arriving in Australia, but cannot provide one of the following:
    • evidence of valid insurance in the Republic of Slovenia
    • a valid European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) bearing the initials SI

A non-Slovenian citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • lived in Slovenia for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia
  • lived in Slovenia for 6 months or more prior to arriving in Australia, but cannot provide one of the following:
    • evidence of valid insurance in the Republic of Slovenia
    • a valid European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) bearing the initials SI

15

RHCA - Sweden

A Swedish citizen is eligible for a MES if they did not live in in Sweden for 6 months or more immediately prior to arriving in Australia.

A non-Swedish citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • lived in Sweden for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia, or
  • lived in Sweden 6 months or more prior to arriving in Australia, but cannot provide one of the following:
    • a valid Health Insurance Certificate issued by the Swedish Social Insurance Office
    • a valid European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) bearing the initials SE
    • 2 residency documents

16

RHCA - United Kingdom (UK)

A British citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • lived in the UK for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia (including students)

A non-British citizen is eligible for a MES if they:

  • lived in the UK for less than 6 months prior to arriving in Australia (including students)
  • lived in the UK immediately prior to arriving in Australia, but cannot provide one of the following:
    • a valid European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) bearing the initials UK
    • a valid National Health Service Medical card (Great Britain and the Isle of Man)
    • a valid Health Service Medical card (Northern Ireland)
    • Proof of valid insurance issued by the Guernsey States Insurance Authority or other proof of residence in the Bailiwick of Guernsey, or
    • 2 residency documents
