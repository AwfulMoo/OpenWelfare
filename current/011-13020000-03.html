This page contains information and procedures for staff handling enquiries relating to the External Breast Prostheses Reimbursement Program (EBPRP).

On this page:

Staff responsible for handling EBPRP enquiries

Refer an enquiry to EBPRP Processing team

Manage claim lodgement and payment delay enquiries

Electronic funds transfer enquiries

Financial hardship or requests for urgent payments

Enquiries about rejected EBPRP claims

Complaints about EBPRP

Publication material enquiries from organisations

Staff responsible for handling EBPRP enquiries

Table 1: This table describes types of enquiries for EBPRP and who is responsible for handling the enquiries.

Item

Enquiry type

1

Service Officers + Read more ...

Service Officers located in service centres or public enquiry line staff are restricted to:

  • provision of claim forms, if requested
  • directing the caller to Services Australia website to download the claim form
  • obtaining missing information on the claim form

Service Officers who are not suitably skilled in the EBPRP program should not provide advice on EBPRP enquiries.

Go to Table 2.

2

EBPRP Processing team + Read more ...

Respond and action anything more complex than a Service Officer enquiry including:

  • payment or rejection determinations
  • questions around eligibility or policy
  • financial hardship
  • requests for reimbursement for prostheses from customers who have received previous reimbursements

3

EBPRP Program Management + Read more ...

EBPRB Program Management (includes Policy Officers and Program Managers), respond and action anything more complex than a general EBPRP processing enquiry including:

  • complaints/feedback items
  • queries regarding the purchase of prostheses overseas
  • liaison with Department of Health and Aged Care regarding:
    • policy clarification
    • financial hardship
    • duplicate statement requests
    • EFT traces
    • review of program documentation to ensure current standard letter templates
    • discretionary claims within 2 years

Refer an enquiry to EBPRP Processing team

Table 2: This table describes how Service Officers escalate an EBPRP enquiry to the EBPRP Processing team.

Customer query

Service Officer response

If I purchase a prosthesis can I be reimbursed?

or

I purchased a prosthesis 2 years ago. Can I get another one?

In person:

  • contact the EBPRP Processing team for further assistance

Over the phone:

  • 'You may be eligible for reimbursement. I can transfer you directly to the EBPR Processing team to assist you further'
  • warm transfer the call immediately to the EBPRP Processing team

Manage claim lodgement and payment delay enquiries

Table 3: This table describes how to respond to payment delays or confirmation that the EBPRP claim has been received.
Note: Smart Centre and service centre staff do not have access to the EBPRP system and must complete steps 1-4 below before transferring the call to the EBPRP Processing team.

Step

Action

1

Obtain Medicare card number + Read more ...

Obtain the customer’s Medicare card number, and:

  • Proof of Identity
  • Amend/update customer details in CDMS

2

Check Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) + Read more ...

Check:

  • the Personal Comments field in CDMS and locate the EBPRP receiving/processing comment
  • customer restrictions in CDMS when verifying the Medicare card

Follow the appropriate procedures with the message received.

3

Check receipt of claim details + Read more ...

If receipt of claim details are:

  • Present, advise customer if the claim has been processed
  • If no comment or any other comments have been left for a rejection or discretionary request, warm transfer the call to the EBPRP Processing team. Note: do not discuss rejections or discretionary request with customer
  • Not present, go to Step 4

4

Request further information + Read more ...

If the claim has not been receipted in the Personal Comments field in CDMS, ask the customer:

  • the approximate date of lodgement
  • whether the claim was lodged at a service centre (and if so, which office), by mail or via email
  • the preferred contact number

Note: the preferred telephone contact number might differ from the details recorded in CDMS.

5

Contact EBPRP Processing team + Read more ...

Contact EBPRP Processing team and provide the above details.

6

Follow instructions from EBPRP Processing team + Read more ...

Follow instructions given by the EBPRP Processing team Service Officer.

Electronic funds transfer enquiries

Table 4: This table describes how a Service Officer requests urgent processing of an EBPRP claim.

Step

Action

1

Refer request to EBPRP Processing team + Read more ...

For urgent payment requests or claims of financial hardship, Service Officers can request urgent processing of the claim form.

Note: all reimbursement payments are made via electronic funds transfer (EFT) into the customer’s nominated bank account.

Refer all requests to the EBPRP Processing team

2

Follow instructions from EBPRP Processing team + Read more ...

Follow instructions provided by EBPRP Processing team support.

Note: delays in EFT processing can be experienced with some financial institutions.

Financial hardship or requests for urgent payments

Table 5: This table describes how Service Officers manage financial hardship or urgent payment requests.

Step

Action

1

Handling the enquiry + Read more ...

Services Australia may arrange for urgent processing of a claim form to expedite the reimbursement in exceptional circumstances. Warm transfer the customer to the EBPRP Processing team so they can evaluate the claim.

For:

  • EBPRP Processing team, go to Step 5
  • other Service Officers accepting the claim:
    • by phone, go to Step 2
    • in person, go to Step 3

2

Phone enquiries + Read more ...

Advise the customer their claim may be processed urgently and paid via EFT into their bank account.

Procedure ends here for phone enquiries.

3

Enquiries in person + Read more ...

Tell the customer that:

  • their claim may be processed urgently
  • will be paid via EFT into their bank account

Go to Step 4.

4

Check the claim form is complete + Read more ...

Check the External Breast claim form (NH005) is complete, see Validate claim form for External Breast Prostheses Reimbursement Program (EBPRP):

  • phone the EBPRP Processing team and advise that a customer who is requesting urgent processing of their claim is present
  • scan the claim and receipt into PaNDA using the following categories:
    • Program – Miscellaneous
    • Work Type – External Breast Prosthesis
    • Medicare card number
  • call the EBPRP Processing team explaining that, as discussed, this is a claim form and receipt for urgent processing
  • hand the originals back to the customer
  • procedure ends here for service centre staff

5

EBPRP Processing team process the claim + Read more ...

This step is for EBPRP Processing team only.

Process the claim on the same day of receipt of the emailed documents from the service centre.

Enquiries about rejected EBPRP claims

Table 6: This table describes how the EBPRP Processing team respond when a customer contacts a service centre to enquire about a rejected EBPRP claim.

Step

Action

1

Service Officer action + Read more ...

Obtain:

  • customer’s Medicare card number
  • complete POI and housekeeping
  • reference number at the top of the letter (right side) or EBPRP Claim ID or other reference if noted on the letter

Warm transfer call immediately to EBPRP Processing team.

Note: service centre staff are not to discuss rejected claims with customer. These calls must be warm transferred to the dedicated EBPRP Processing team in accordance with EBPRP Processing team enquiries.

2

EBPRP Processing team action + Read more ...

Discuss the circumstances of the rejected claim with the customer.

Use the EBPRP script for rejected claims.

Complaints about EBPRP

Table 7: This table describes how the EBPRP Processing team respond to complaints received about EBPRP.

Step

Action

1

Service Officer receives a complaint via phone + Read more ...

Service Officers are to warm transfer all complaints to the EBPRP Processing team with the following information:

  • ensure Service Officer has identified the customer and completed house keeping
  • customer's contact details (daytime phone and email)
  • Medicare card number
  • time and date of call
  • full details of the complaint

2

EBPRP Processing team action + Read more ...

If a customer wants to lodge a formal complaint:

  • the EBPRP Processing team are to register the complaint in the Customer First Tool, as per the instructions in Managing customer complaints and feedback, or
  • Service Officers are to direct them to the Services Australia website where they can record the complaint

3

EBPRP Processing team + Read more ...

If the complaint:

  • has been recorded through complaints and feedback, it will be allocated by the Feedback and Complaints team to the appropriate area
  • relates to a policy decision, escalate to the Program Management team
  • relates to an operational matters, direct it to the EBPRP team leader

Apply internal procedures to resolve the complaint.
