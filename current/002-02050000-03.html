2

Farm assets assessments changes by date + Read more ...

Claims submitted from 11 June 2020:

  • assess these claims under the new single FHA asset test threshold of $5.5 million. Customers will no longer need to meet separate Tier 1 and Tier 2 asset test but a single FHA asset test. Whilst the asset test is the total of all their farm and non-farm assets, farm assets need to be assessed separately for aggregation purposes before being added to the non-farm assets
  • the single FHA assets test threshold is $5.5 million:
    • per single customer
    • per couple (i.e. not $5.5 million each)
  • the asset test is not subject to indexation. It will remain at $5.5m unless a legislative amendment is made
  • Go to Step 7

Claims submitted before 11 June 2020 but finalised on or after 11 June 2020:

  • these claims must be assessed under the:
    • Tier 1/Tier 2 assets test rules for the period prior to 11 June 2020; and
    • new single FHA assets test on or after 11 June 2020
  • if the customer is ineligible for payment under the Tier 1/Tier 2 assets test but would be eligible for payment under the single FHA assets test then payment must be granted from 11 June 2020
  • if the customer is ineligible for payment under both assets tests then they must be rejected using the applicable rejection reason under the Tier 1/Tier 2 assets test
  • Go to Step 6

For more information, see Definition of farm assets on the Background page.

3

Change to asset details + Read more ...

If a customer is notifying a change of circumstances related to their farm assets, continue to assess FHA under the legislation in force at the time of claim.

Customers are not obliged to notify small changes to asset values.

Generally, the customer's advice of the change is to be accepted, but in some cases, verification may be required. See Verifying and recording assets changes.

The value of an asset is assessed as for social security payments, however, there are some differences for certain types of assets:

  • Water rights
  • Superannuation

Farm Business Assets screen in Process Direct will no longer be used to record asset values for reassessment with a date of effect on or after 11 June 2020. Values coded on each screen e.g. REBS, OAS, and TRCIA; will be used to automatically calculate the customer's assets. Rural exemption fields on each screen are used to identify if assets are farm or non-farm assets. This allows farm assets to be aggregated prior to applying the assets test.

When a customer contacts with an asset update after 11 June 2020, review all assets.
If updates are required, use the FHA workflow Income and Asset Update.

If the customer is notifying:

  • a new asset or change to existing asset, see:
    • Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services
    • Assessing assets at their net market value
    • for asset changes with a date of effect on or after 11 June 2020, once the assets updates have been completed, go to Step 11
    • for asset changes with a date of effect prior to 11 June 2020, once the assets have been updated, go to Step 6
  • sale of an asset, go to Step 4
  • deprivation/gifting of assets, go to Step 5
  • forced disposal of livestock, see Assessing income for Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
  • changes to assets under a trust or company, see Identifying and making suitable referrals to the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO)
  • a farm asset exemption has been determined by a CAO, go to Step 8
  • changes to assets for a sole trader, see Assessing sole trader assets
  • changes to assets for a partnership, see Assessing partnership assets

4

Sale of an asset + Read more ...

The cash proceeds received by a customer due to the sale of an asset are not assessed as income. Including any capital gains achieved, for example, a garage sale, firearms buyback scheme.

The customer's assessable assets may need adjusting due to the sale. Any proceeds of cash or extra funds in financial accounts are subject to the usual deeming provisions.

If the customer is advising:

  • the forced disposal of livestock, see Assessing income for Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
  • the sale of the principal home or other real estate, see Assessment and sale of Real Estate.
  • the sale of the farming property, see Change of circumstance for Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
  • the sale of an asset, see Assessing assets at their net market value

Once completed the assets updates, go to Step 11.

5

Deprivation/gifting of assets + Read more ...

For FHA, the treatment of gifted assets is consistent with social security payments.

For coding assistance, see Assessing deprivation/gifting.

Once deprivation/gifting has been coded, go to Step 11.

6

Tier 1 and Tier 2 asset details + Read more ...

Code the FHA Farm Business Assets Task for updates before 11 June 2020. In Customer First assets coded will be assessed under the Tier 1, non-farm asset test.

If the asset is determined to be a farm asset, for example, farm land or business, the asset must have the FHA Rural exempt indicator applied in Customer First, and the value recorded in the FHA Farm Business Assets Task.

To update details, run the FHA Farm Business Assets Task in Process Direct:

In the customer record, key FBA in Super Key

  • to update the Farm Business Assets, run the Farm Assets Tier 1 and Tier 2 sub-task. Select Add:
  • Tier 1 Amount = the value of any non-farm assets of a farm-related trust or company. Note: Tier 1 assets not related to a farm trust or company are not coded in this workflow. Code using the relevant screens. The system will then calculate total Tier 1 assets using the values recorded in the workflow and Customer First
  • Tier 2 Amount = the total net value of farm assets, including business/farm assets, farming property and any primary production assets held in a trust and/or company. Manually calculate the total net value of the farm asset and enter the amount into the guided procedure
  • Percentage owned, key the percentage owned by the customer and their partner
  • Start Date:
    • for new claims, key the start date of the claim
    • for current customers, key the date of effect
  • End Date, key '31/12/9999'. This can be updated if the customer advises a change of circumstances
  • Select Save

Only one current line can be coded in the guided procedure. If Tier 1 and Tier 2 assets are owned by the customer at different percentages, assess and code an adjusted percentage.

For example, Tier 1 assets are 100% owned by the customer and Tier 2 assets are 100% owned by the partner. Code the guided procedure with the total Tier 1 and Tier 2 values and the ownership split 50/50.

Review the details and finalise the update:

  • select Assess
  • in the Update Header Data:
    • Receipt Date, key the date the customer notifies
    • Channel, key the channel the information was received
  • select Save

Record the details on a DOC.

Is the customer involved in a Trust and/or Company?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

Determining and coding exempt Non-farm Assets

Table 2: For FHA processing staff only.

Step

Action

1

Is the asset a non-farm asset under section 35 of the Farm Household Support Act 2014? + Read more ...

Is the asset being assessed:

  • a right or interest in land used wholly or mainly for the purposes of a farm enterprise, OR
  • a right or interest in livestock, crop, plant or equipment that is either used wholly or mainly for the purposes of the farm enterprise or is produce of a farm enterprise, OR
  • a loan that meets the beneficiary loan requirements?
    • Yes, the asset is a farm asset. Go to Step 8
    • No, the asset is a non-farm asset. Go to Step 2

2

Is the asset held for the purposes of carrying out a farm enterprise? + Read more ...

Consider:

  • what industry does the farm enterprise operate in? Is this the same industry the asset relates to?
  • is there a formal document that requires a person to hold the asset in order to conduct their business? (For example, a cooperative share standard may require a dairy farmer to hold a certain number of shares in order to supply their milk product to the cooperative)
  • if there is a formal requirement, what value of the asset must be held? (for example, suppliers of the previous Murray Goulburn Cooperative (MG Co-op) were required to hold a minimum of 500 shares plus a number of shares based on the amount of milk solids they supply
  • compare the customer's required holdings with their actual holdings. Holdings in excess of the required amount will not be exempt

Note: search company websites for corporate documents in order to gather this information - look for Constitution documents or Share Policy documents.

Service Officers are not to contact organisations directly. For assistance to source particular information, refer the query to the Technical Peer Support officer.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 8

3

Can the asset only be held for the purposes of carrying out a farm enterprise? + Read more ...

Consider:

For shares, what type of entity are the shares for (private or public owned)?

For example, there were 2 separate Murray Goulburn entities in which a person may have an interest.

The Murray Goulburn Cooperative was privately owned by suppliers. Only suppliers of Murray Goulburn (dairy farmers) were able to purchase shares.

The Murray Goulburn Unit Trust was listed on the ASX, and as per the Murray Goulburn website, members of the public were able to purchase units in order to benefit financially from the performance of Murray Goulburn.

Can the asset be held only by farmers?

For example, some companies offer employee share schemes. Such shares would not be exempt under the Rule as they can be held by a person who is not a farmer in the relevant industry.

Are there any other benefits to be gained from holding the asset?

If there is a benefit to be gained from holding the assets, other than the ability to carry out the farm enterprise, the asset may not meet the criteria.

Search company websites for corporate documents to gather this information. Look for Constitution documents or Share Policy documents.

Service Officers are not to contact organisations directly. For help sourcing particular information, refer the query to the Technical Peer Support officer.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 8

4

Is the asset cash or an amount deposited with or lent to a financial institution? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Does the non-farm asset meet all criteria for exemption under the Amendment Rule? + Read more ...

  • Yes, the asset is to be exempted from both the asset calculations
    • for shares, go to Step 6
    • for all other assets, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

6

Coding exempt shares + Read more ...

Although the shares are exempt from assessment for the purposes of FHA, where the customer is partnered with another income support recipient or age pension recipient, the shares will still be assessable for the partner and therefore must be coded.

Shares and managed investments listed on a sole trader or partnership balance sheet that are not part of the working capital of the business are considered to be personal assets. Deduct the value of these assets from the balance sheet. Do not record these assets on the business record.

Code shares according to Shares and securities.

If coding shares as part of a new claim, use the relevant task in Process Direct.

For shares up to the share standard:

Using the FHA workflow Income and Asset Update, on the SIUS screen enter the description '(Company Name) Set Share Standard' in Company and the share standard held in Product. Apply the FHA Rural Exempt Indicator. This will prevent the assets from assessment under the Farm Household Allowance asset test. The value of these shares will still be assessed for other payment types

  • using the FHA exemption indicator also exempts the assets from deeming
  • Do not code exempt assets in the Farm Asset Determination guided procedure, as they are exempt from both FHA asset tests

For shares held over the share standard:

Shares held above the required holdings are not exempt. Record these separately on the SIUS screen. Enter the description '(Company Name Additional Shares' in Company and the number of shares held above the standard in Product. Do not apply the FHA rural exempt indicator

Record details on a DOC.

7

Coding other exempt assets + Read more ...

Code the asset details using the FHA workflow Income and Asset Update.

If coding assets as part of a new claim, select the relevant task in Process Direct.

Apply the FHA Rural Exempt Indicator. This will prevent SAP from assessing the assets, but will allow them to be assessed for payments delivered in the legacy system.

For financial assets, applying the FHA exemption indicator will also exempt the assets from deeming.

Do not code exempt assets in the Farm Asset Determination guided procedure, as exempt assets are exempt from both FHA asset tests.

Record details on a DOC.

8

The asset cannot be exempt from the assets test + Read more ...

If the asset cannot be exempt under the Non-farm Asset Rule, consider:

  • if there is a loan or liability over the asset (the value of the asset may be reduced by the value of the liability)
  • if Asset Hardship Provisions apply

Identifying and assessing water assets for FHA

Table 3: For FHA processing staff and Complex Assessment Officers only.

Step

Action

1

Treatment of water assets + Read more ...

Water allocations/licences/entitlements held within the Murray Darling Basin only are to be assessed as assets for Farm Household Allowance (FHA) purposes. Water assets held in other water bodies, are not to be assessed as an asset.

The Resources page contains a table that explains the treatment of water assets based on claim submission date and when the claim is determined and a link to the Murray Darling Basin Authority for assistance in determining water catchment.

Only use the following assessment if the FHA customer is single or partnered with another FHA customer. If the FHA customer is partnered with another income support payment (ISP) customer, seek other assessment and coding advice from the Level 2 helpdesk.

Does the customer or partner have water assets held within the Murray Darling Basin?

  • Yes:
    • If the water assets are identified with the FHA claim or as a change of circumstance update, go to Step 2
    • If the customer is contacting for assistance in estimating the value of water, go to Step 4
  • No, the water is not assessed as an asset. Record the details of the water in a Fast Note. Procedure end here

2

Water assets are identified with the customer's FHA claim or as a change of circumstances update + Read more ...

The customer needs to provide details and evidence of all water assets held including:

  • type of water asset held
  • volume associated with each type of water asset
  • estimated market value of one unit of the water asset ($/ML)
  • proof of ownership documents for all water assets
  • apportionment of water use (that is, is the water used mainly (50% or more) for the farm enterprise

Has the customer provided all details and evidence?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No:
    • contact the customer to advise what information is missing
    • issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter
    • allow extra time for mail delivery of the request
    • hold the activity until the day after the allowable time has expired

3

Activity falls due + Read more ...

Has the customer returned the requested information?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No:
    • for current customers, suspend FHA. See Suspending, cancelling and restoring Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
    • for new customers, reject the claim using the most appropriate reason

4

Customer contacts asking how to value their water assets for an FHA claim + Read more ...

Ask the customer if they can provide a reasonable estimate of the water assets held within the Murray Darling Basin. Verbally accept the estimate.

To help the customer to determine a reasonable estimate, refer to the current Murray Darling Basin market prices. The Resources page contains a link to the Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry - Market price information for Murray Darling Basin water entitlements.

If they are unable to provide a reasonable estimate:

Advise them to speak with their financial adviser or accountant

  • review their Farm Financial Assessment if one has been completed, details of assets should have been included
  • advise them to speak with their Rural Financial Counsellor
  • refer them to their state or territory water authority for assistance. See the Resources page

Estimate of market value must be provided in the following format for each type of water asset owned:

  • Water Asset Type
  • Water Asset Volume: for example xx GL/KL/ML
  • estimated value per unit: for example $xx/ML
  • apportionment of water use, for example 50% farm use; 40% agistment use; 8% environmental regeneration and 2% domestic use

Advise the customer:

  • their estimate must be supported by evidence
  • they must submit their estimate with the MOD R or Income and Assets form and water licence/certificate/deed/statement/title documents requested in that form

Details of multiple water assets may be provided on one document. This depends on the documentation provided by the customer. For example, a Victoria Water Register Allocation Account Statement or separate documents for each type of asset.

5

Determine if the estimated value of each water asset is reasonable + Read more ...

The customer must supply evidence to support their estimate:

  • the customer may have obtained advice/documentation from their water authority to show the current market value of the asset
  • if the asset is held as security for loan, it is likely the asset would have undergone a formal valuation
  • if the customer is located within the Murray Darling Basin, the Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry publish water market price information on a quarterly and monthly basis. The average figures in this report can be used to assess the reasonableness of a customer's estimate
  • water licences/statements etc. usually show a water volume
  • some state and territory authorities provide useful tools to show current market trends
  • consider the type of system the water is taken from (regulated or unregulated) and what impact this may have on the potential value of the asset
  • if the customer has already completed their Farm Financial Assessment see the asset values included in the assessment

Any of the above types of evidence can help in deciding if the estimate is reasonable.

See Resources page for a link to the current water reports and examples for calculating the market value.

Is the customer's estimate reasonable?

  • Yes, code the water asset. Go to Step 6
  • No, contact the customer and advise their estimate has not been accepted. They must provide a new estimate either verbally or in writing or the claim will be rejected

6

Assessment and coding of water assets + Read more ...

Is the water entitlement right wholly or mainly used for running the farm enterprise?

  • Yes, the water entitlement right is a farm asset. If the water entitlement asset:
    • is attached to land, go to Step 7
    • is not attached to land and can be sold in its own right, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

7

Water asset is attached to land + Read more ...

From 11 June 2020, the value of the water entitlement right is not to be included with the value of the land the water asset is attached to. It is not recorded as an asset.

For assessments with a date of effect before 11 June 2020, code the value of the land plus water on the Real Estate 'RE' screen, see Assessing and coding the Real estate Details Mod R.

Apply the FHA Rural exemption indicator.

For claims prior to 11 June 2020, take into consideration the value of water assets when recording the net farm assets value in the Farm Asset Determination guided procedure for claims.

Procedure ends here.

8

Water asset is not attached to land and can be sold in its own right + Read more ...

The value of the water entitlement asset is to be included with the value of the Primary Production business assets.

Code the business assets on the Business (BUS) screen, See Assessing and coding the Business details for sole traders and partnerships Mod F.

Apply the FHA Rural exemption indicator.

For claims before 11 June 2020, take into consideration the value of water assets when recording the net farm assets value in the Farm Asset Determination guided procedure for claims.

Procedure ends here.

9

Water asset is not considered a farm asset + Read more ...

If the water entitlement right is not wholly or mainly for the farm enterprise, its value cannot be assessed as a component of the farming land or business.

Using the FHA workflow Income and Asset Update, code the details of the water asset on the Other Assets (OAS) screen:

  • Type: 'OTH' other assets
  • Description: record a description (water asset)
  • Event Date: date the update will take effect from
  • Total Asset $: code the water asset net market value
  • %Owned Client: if the assets are not entirely owned by the customer, code the percentage owned by the customer
  • %Owned Partner: code the percentage owned by the partner.
    If coding in Process Direct, go to the partner's record within the new claim to code the % owned

Procedure ends here.

Assessing superannuation for FHA

Table 4: For FHA processing staff only.

Step

Action

1

Determine whether the superannuation is assessable + Read more ...

To determine if the superannuation is an assessable asset, consider:

Is the customer over pension age?

Does the customer receive a benefit from the fund?

If the answer to:

  • Both of the above questions is 'No', the superannuation is not assessable and does not need to be recorded.
    A future action review will be initiated once the customer reaches pension age.
    Procedure ends here
  • either of the above questions is 'Yes', assess the superannuation for the purposes of determining eligibility for FHA.
  • Go to Step 2

2

Superannuation is subject to the assets test for FHA + Read more ...

If the customer has only non-farm assets in their superannuation fund, it will be assessed as under social security law. For help, see Assessing superannuation.

If the customer has farm and non-farm assets in their superannuation fund, (for example. farm land or livestock is owned by a self-managed superannuation fund,) the farm asset components should be coded separately from the non-farm asset components to ensure that farm assets can be aggregated prior to the application of the assets test.

To code:

  • non-farm asset components of a superannuation fund, go to Step 3
  • farm asset components of a superannuation fund, go to Step 4

3

Code the superannuation non-farm asset components + Read more ...

  • calculate the value of the non-farm asset components of the superannuation fund and code on the relevant superannuation screens in Customer First
  • the value of the non-farm components of the superannuation fund will be assessed as a non-farm asset and will be subject to deeming

4

Code the superannuation farm asset components + Read more ...

  • calculate the value of the farm asset components of the superannuation fund and code on the relevant superannuation screens in Customer First
  • Apply the FHA exemption indicator if available on the screen
    • this will prevent the value from being assessed under the Tier 1 assets test, and will also exempt the value from deeming
    • the value will still be accurately assessed for payments delivered in the legacy system
  • if the FHA indicator is NOT available, use BTH - (for example, on MIS/MIUS)
  • the value of the farm components held in the superannuation fund must be coded in the Farm Asset Determination GP for claims lodged before 11 June 2020
    • this will ensure the farm asset components of the superannuation fund are assessed as a farm asset
  • record coding actions on a Display on Access DOC

Note: the use of the BTH exemption indicator is a workaround, which is suitable for use where an FHA customer is not partnered, or where both members of a couple are claiming/receiving FHA.

If the FHA customer is partnered with another income support payment (ISP) recipient, seek alternative assessment and coding advice from the FHA Level 2 helpdesk.
