This page contains details to assist in determining the closing date for an ABSTUDY claim.

Determining closing date for ABSTUDY claims

Step

Action

1

Course, activity or training + Read more ...

What type of course, activity or training is the customer participating in?

  • For ABSTUDY PES students, go to Step 4
  • A full year or late starting course, go to Step 2
  • A course of less than one year, including short courses which overlap consecutive calendar years, go to Step 3
  • A testing and assessment activity, go to Step 5
  • A customer in lawful custody, go to Step 6
  • An Open Universities Australia course of study, go to Step 7
  • An Australian Apprenticeship, go to Step 11
  • If applying for reimbursement of Fares Allowance, go to Step 8
  • If applying for Away from Base assistance, go to Step 9

2

Claim lodged by end of calendar year + Read more ...

Has the student lodged their claim by 31 December of the year of study, which is also the year they wish to receive arrears?

  • Yes, they will receive full entitlement (which may include back payment) in accordance with their eligibility, see Calculating the start date for ABSTUDY. Procedure ends here
  • No, they will only be eligible to be backdated in the year they applied, unless they are a vulnerable customer or the claim was lodged late due to circumstances beyond their control. Go to Step 10

3

Claim lodged by end of course + Read more ...

Has the student lodged their claim by the end of the course?

  • Yes, they will receive full entitlement (which may include back payment) in accordance with their eligibility, see Calculating the start date for ABSTUDY. Procedure ends here
  • No, they may not be eligible for ABSTUDY assistance for the course, unless they are a vulnerable customer or the claim was lodged late due to circumstances beyond their control. Go to Step 10

4

ABSTUDY PES + Read more ...

Has the ABSTUDY PES student lodged their claim by the relevant closing dates for PES?

  • Yes, they will receive full entitlement (which may include back payment) in accordance with their eligibility, see Calculating the start date for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and ABSTUDY PES. Procedure ends here
  • No, where a claim is not received by the above closing dates, payment can only be granted from the date of claim unless they are a vulnerable customer, or the claim was lodged late due to circumstances beyond the applicant's control. Go to Step 10

Note: in all cases, ABSTUDY PES claims must be lodged prior to the student discontinuing study.

5

Testing and Assessment claim + Read more ...

Was the claim for Testing and Assessment lodged by the Testing and Assessment claim closing date?

  • Yes, the student will receive the full entitlement, see Assessing a claim for ABSTUDY Allowances and Part-time Award. Procedure ends here
  • No, the student may not be eligible for ABSTUDY assistance for the testing and assessment activity unless they are a vulnerable customer or the claim was lodged late due to circumstances beyond their control. Go to Step 10

6

Lawful custody claim + Read more ...

Claims for reimbursement of essential course costs under Lawful Custody Allowance must be lodged before 1 April in the year immediately following the year of study or apprenticeship activity.

Was the claim lodged before 1 April in the year immediately following the year of study or apprenticeship activity?

  • Yes, the customer will receive the full entitlement
  • No, the customer may not be eligible for ABSTUDY assistance for the essential course costs unless they are a vulnerable customer or the claim was lodged late due to circumstances beyond their control. Go to Step 10

7

Claim lodged by end of the Open Universities Australia study period + Read more ...

Was the claim lodged by the end of the Open Universities Australia study period for which assistance is sought?

  • Yes, the student will receive full entitlement (which may include back payment) in accordance with their eligibility, see Calculating the start date for ABSTUDY. Procedure ends here
  • No, the student may not be eligible unless they are a vulnerable customer or the claim was lodged late due to circumstances beyond their control. Go to Step 10

8

Fares Allowance + Read more ...

If applying for reimbursement of Fares Allowance, an ABSTUDY Fares Allowance Claim (SY032) must be lodged before 1 April in the year immediately following the year of study.

Graduation and examination travel reimbursement claims must be submitted within 3 months of travelling.

Has the claim for reimbursement of Fares Allowance been lodged prior to the closing date?

  • Yes, see Reimbursing fares and additional costs after travel has been undertaken for ABSTUDY. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 10

9

Away from Base + Read more ...

If applying for Away from Base assistance, a submission must be lodged by the institution or individual student by the relevant Away from Base submission closing dates.

Has the claim for Away from Base assistance been lodged prior to the closing date?

  • Yes, see Assessing the ABSTUDY Submission for Away From Base Activity Funding (SY034). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Claim lodged after closing dates + Read more ...

When a claim is lodged past the closing date due to circumstances beyond the applicant's control, students may still receive full benefits.

The applicant must provide the claim as soon as possible and a written statement explaining the reason for late lodgement. Evidence may be requested if the decision maker considers it necessary.

Claims lodged past the closing date with no circumstances beyond the applicant's control, or without vulnerability notes recorded, have no entitlement to ABSTUDY.

Was the claim lodged past the closing date due to circumstances beyond the applicant's control or has the customer claimed under vulnerability provisions?

  • Yes, see Calculating the start date for ABSTUDY or Calculating the start day for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and ABSTUDY PES. Procedure ends here
  • No, the claim must be manually rejected using the reason code 'OTH'. see Rejecting an ABSTUDY claim

11

Australian Apprentices + Read more ...

Australian Apprentices will receive payments (where eligible) from the date they submit a claim unless they are a vulnerable customer or unable to lodge due to circumstances beyond their control.

Note: they may be entitled to an extension of time to lodge their claim due to a medical condition or special circumstances.
