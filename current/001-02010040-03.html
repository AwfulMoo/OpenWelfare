Search by ID + Read more ...

LAD entries have a unique numeric ID. The ID does not relate to any other Services Australia identifier.

Search by name + Read more ...

LAD searches do not allow wildcard characters. No results will display unless there is an exact match with the keyed entry.

For organisation name searches, consider:

  • Have they had a different name before?
  • Are they listed under a parent or subsidiary entity name?
  • Are they known by another name?
  • Search for 1 word in their name (e.g. for 'ABC Parent's Association, try 'ABC' or 'Parent')

To search by organisation name:

  • Key the search term in the Organisation Name field, and
  • Select Search

To search for an organisation in a specific area:

Note: the 'Geographical' search options are currently not available.

Search results + Read more ...

Each organisation will display:

  • Status (Approved or Not Approved)

  • Physical Address

To refine the results, select Refine and change the selection criteria.

Voluntary work organisations with more than 1 location

More than 1 location can be approved on the Voluntary Work - Request for Organisational Approval (SU461) form. Include the physical address, best contact person and details for each location.

Each location will have separate entries in the LAD along with the physical address. The approved or not approved outcome is for that physical address. If job seekers want to do voluntary work at a location not in the LAD, complete a new assessment.

If there are duplicate records for the same organisation, email to the Voluntary Organisation Team asking them to investigate.

If in contact with:

  • a job seeker, go to Step 6
  • an organisation, see Step 1 in Table 2

6

Job seeker contact + Read more ...

If the organisation has been located and is approved, it may be possible to include voluntary work as an approved activity in the job seeker's Job Plan.

If the organisation cannot be located or is not approved, the job seeker must ensure they prioritise their mutual obligation requirements and appointments over any voluntary work they do with the organisation.

The job seeker can invite the organisation to apply or re-register with Services Australia. If asked, give the job seeker a Voluntary Work - Request for Organisation Approval (SU461) form for their organisation to complete. The Resources page has a link to the form.

Procedure ends here.

2

SU461 received + Read more ...

With an SU461, community based organisations must give proof of their:

  • not-for-profit status
  • current public liability insurance cover
  • current personal accident/voluntary workers insurance cover

Note: government sector agencies are not-for-profit and do not need to supply proof of their status They will also have appropriate insurance cover and do not need to supply proof of insurance.

Treat Australian public schools as government sector organisations for voluntary work organisation assessments.

However, independent Parent and Citizen Associations and private schools are not government organisations and must provide proof.

Note: The Voluntary Work - Request for Organisation Approval (SU461) form can only be submitted in person at a Centrelink office by a representative of the organisation. If it is lodged by the customer, it is to be actioned accordingly.

Has the community based organisation given all required proof with the SU461?

Proof not provided + Read more ...

Do not accept the SU461 unless the organisation provides all required proof.

Tell the organisation what proof to give and to submit the SU461 with the proof.

Procedure ends here.

4

Review and Appeal rights + Read more ...

Social security law review and appeal rights do not apply to requests from organisations seeking voluntary work organisation approval.

This means organisations cannot request a form review of decision by an Authorised Review Officer.

The Voluntary Organisation Team will accept any review or appeal request. To ask for a review, email the Voluntary Organisation Team advising who wants the review and why.

Has the organisation given more supporting proof?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, procedure ends here

5

Sending documents to the Voluntary Organisation Team + Read more ...

Service Officers must:

  • scan both sides of the SU461 and/or any supporting documents onto the local Multi-Function Device (MFD)
  • retrieve the scan from the MFD
  • email scanned documents to the Voluntary Organisation Team for assessment
  • store paper documents in the scan batch on site. The Voluntary Organisation Team may want another copy if they do not get all pages in the scan

Service Officers must not attach the SU461 or supporting proof to a job seeker's Centrelink record. When the documents are emailed to the Voluntary Organisation Team, procedure ends here.

Note: job seekers may provide a Voluntary Work - Verification of Voluntary Work (SU462) form. This form should be kept on-site and returned to the job seeker. The form will be considered when the organisation's request to be an approved voluntary work organisation is made.

  • name

3

Verifying not-for-profit status + Read more ...

Government sector agencies are not-for-profit and do not need to supply proof of their status.

Community based organisations must provide proof they are not-for-profit. Funds raised by the organisation must not be paid to any members, even if it stops operating.

Treat Australian public schools as government sector organisations for voluntary work organisation assessments. However, independent Parent and Citizen Associations and private schools are not government organisations and must provide proof.

Acceptable proof includes:

  • Proof of membership of a state, territory or regional volunteer centre
  • Articles of incorporation
  • Certificate of incorporation
  • Notification of a name change of an incorporated entity
  • A copy of the organisation's constitution showing how funds are managed, or
  • Proof the organisation is a charitable trust

Has the organisation proved they are not-for-profit?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, do not approved the organisation. Record this in the Local Activities Database (LAD), see Step 1 in Table 4

4

Verifying insurance cover + Read more ...

Government sector agencies will have appropriate insurance cover and do not need to supply proof of insurance.

Community based voluntary work organisations must provide proof they have both:

  • public liability insurance of at least $5 million, and
  • personal accident/voluntary workers insurance

Public liability insurance covers the cost of claims made against organisations by third parties, if their services cause injury or property damage.

Personal accident/voluntary workers insurance covers the cost of claims against an organisation by volunteers, due to a work accident.

The level of personal accident/voluntary workers insurance may differ for each organisation. Services Australia cannot advise what level of cover is appropriate. If organisations ask Services Australia about what is an appropriate level of cover, tell them to get professional advice.

Documents issued by the insurance provider are acceptable proof if they confirm the:

  • organisation name
  • type of insurance(s) purchased
  • coverage period, and
  • coverage amount

Acceptable proof includes:

  • Certificate of Currency
  • Certificate of Insurance
  • written or verbal statements from the insurance provider

The following do not prove the organisation has paid for or has active insurance policies:

  • Tax invoices
  • Renewal notices
  • Policy quotes
  • Policy schedules, and
  • Product Disclosure Statements (PDS)

They are not acceptable proof.

Has the organisation provided all required proof?

  • Yes, approve the organisation
  • No, do not approve the organisation

Record the outcome in the Local Activities Database (LAD), see Step 1 in Table 4.

Action

Searching the LAD + Read more ...

2

Updating the LAD + Read more ...

On the Organisation page, record the following:

  • Office - Select the service centre closest to the organisation's permanent address. If needed, use Office Locator to find the closest centre
  • Organisation Name - As listed on the Voluntary Work - Request for Organisation Approval (SU461) form. If the community knows the organisation by other names, enter the organisation's full name and any local names
  • Address/Town or Suburb/State/Postcode - Record the address of the location where the voluntary work opportunities are available. Where an organisation is based in more than 1 location, the LAD will need a separate entry for each location
  • Location/Location Postcode - Record the town/suburb and postcode where the voluntary work opportunities are
  • Phone Number/Fax Number/Contact Name - Record details the organisation gives on the SU461

Is the voluntary work organisation approved?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

Organisation approved + Read more ...

    --------------------------------------------------------------------
    Other Organisation Information:
    - Postal Address: [ ADDRESS, SUBURB, STATE, POSTCODE ]
    - Email Address: [ RECORD DETAILS or N/A ]
    - Alternate Contact Number: [ RECORD DETAILS or N/A ]
  • Select Save to finalise the updates and go to Step 5

4

Organisation not approved + Read more ...

On the Organisation page:

  • Make sure the Approved checkbox is not ticked
  • Make sure the Approved Date field is blank, and
  • Use the template below to update the Comments field and delete any options that do not apply:
    Date Last Assessed: [ TODAY'S DATE ]
    --------------------------------------------------------------------
    This organisation could not be approved as:
    - the SU461 was not complete or not signed
    - evidence of not-for-profit status required
    - evidence of current public liability insurance required
    - evidence of current personal accident/voluntary worker insurance required
    - organisation is neither a not-for-profit community based organisation or government sector agency
    --------------------------------------------------------------------
    - Postal Address: [ ADDRESS, SUBURB, STATE, POSTCODE ]
    - Email Address: [ RECORD DETAILS or N/A ]
    - Alternate Contact Number: [ RECORD DETAILS or N/A ]
  • Select Save to finalise the updates and go to Step 5
