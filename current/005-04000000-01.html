This document outlines the services available for customers who have limited English proficiency, are deaf, or hard of hearing.

Interpreting services during COVID-19

Services Australia introduced changes to interpreting services during the coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic. These services continue to be available for customers with limited English proficiency:

  • Interpreter Connect service (on-demand telephone interpreters): connects staff immediately to a telephone interpreter via an automated service
  • Booked telephone appointments: staff can book a telephone interpreter for future appointments
  • Booked on-site appointments: only available by exception, for example Auslan and sign language appointments
  • Rostered interpreter service (sessional):
    • provides staff with rostered on-site interpreters at service centres with high language needs. Restrictions to the service depend on the service centre’s COVID status level
    • Indigenous rostered interpreters are available at Broome, Kununurra and Halls Creek Service Centres

Interpreting and translating services

An interpreter accurately conveys the spoken message or signed language from one language to another. Interpreters provide services in person or over the telephone.

A translator is required to translate written communication from one language to another.

Language Services overview

Language Services provides free interpreting and translating services for customers with limited English proficiency. These services include:

  • Interpreter Connect (on-demand telephone interpreters)
    • Immediate access to an interpreter over the telephone
    • For all appointments across Centrelink, Medicare and Child Support, can be engaged in all Smart Centres and service centres
    • See Contacting an on-demand telephone interpreter (Interpreter Connect)
  • Booked interpreters
    • Requests for future on-site and telephone appointments
    • See Booking an interpreter for an appointment
  • Rostered interpreters
    • Rostered in service centres with high language assistance needs
    • For booked appointments and ad-hoc. Refer to local site protocols on how rostered interpreters are used
    • See Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System and Create and manage appointment profiles in the Centrelink Appointment System
  • Translations
    • For when documents in other languages need to assess eligibility for payments and services
    • See Translation of documents
  • Indigenous interpreters
    • Accessed by staff for on-site and telephone appointments to help Indigenous Australians with limited English language proficiency
    • Interprets words and concepts, as many English words have no equivalent word in most Indigenous languages
    • See Accessing an Indigenous interpreter
  • Interpreters for the deaf or hard of hearing
    • For customers who are deaf or hard of hearing
    • If the customer needs an interpreter for a future appointment, see Interpreter Services for customers who are deaf or hard of hearing
    • If the customer needs an interpreter now, explore the customer's nominee arrangement, or see Using the National Relay Service (NRS). Note: the National Relay Service is not a free service

Other language services

Customers with limited English language proficiency are also assisted through:

  • Multilingual Phone Service (MPS) operated by bilingual staff who assist customers in their preferred language
  • Bilingual staff in receipt of the Community Language Allowance (CLA) use their language skills during the course of their duties
  • printed multilingual products available through the Services Australia website and in service centres
  • Payment and Service Finder in Simplified Chinese
  • Indigenous Language Officers (ILO): a small number of ILOs are based in target locations where there is limited or no access to formal interpreting services
