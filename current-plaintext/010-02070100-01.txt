This document outlines the procedure to determine if a student is
eligible for ABSTUDY assistance to attend an approved Away from Base
activity.

Eligibility to attend an Away from Base activity

A student must test their eligibility for each Away from Base activity
they are attending.

To qualify for Away from Base assistance, the student must:

be attending 1 of the following approved activities:

-   testing and assessment
-   placement
-   field trip
-   residential school
    For more information, see Approval of ABSTUDY Away from Base
    activities.

be eligible for 1 of the following Awards:

-   Schooling A Award
-   Schooling B Award
-   Tertiary Award
-   Part-time Award
-   Masters and Doctorate Award
-   Lawful Custody Award
-   Testing and Assessment Award

lodge an ABSTUDY claim by the closing date

incur costs not approved for assistance under the Indigenous Advancement
Strategy (IAS)

not have exceeded the limits on Away from Base activities for the course

be isolated from the location of the approved activity or attending a
compulsory on-campus residence during a residential school or testing
and assessment programme

The Resources page contains examples of a student's eligibility for Away
from Base assistance.
