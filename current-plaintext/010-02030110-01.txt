This document outlines the eligibility criteria for ABSTUDY Masters and
Doctorate Award.

Eligibility criteria

To be eligible for the ABSTUDY Masters and Doctorate Award, the student
must meet the general eligibility criteria for ABSTUDY in addition to
the following:

-   be enrolled in an ABSTUDY approved Masters or Doctorate degree
    course as a full-time or concessional study-load student, and
-   not receiving or expect to receive any other form of government
    assistance (for example, a student cannot be in receipt of both the
    ABSTUDY Masters and Doctorate Award and the Australian Government
    Research Training Program Scholarship)

Part-time Masters and Doctorate students who do not qualify for a study
load concession are entitled to apply for the ABSTUDY Part-time Award.

Note: Australian Apprentices are not entitled to Masters and Doctorate
Award.

Allowances and benefits available

A student approved for a Masters and Doctorate Award may be entitled to
one or more of the following:

-   Living Allowance or Pensioner Education Supplement
-   Incidentals Allowance
-   Additional Incidentals Allowance
-   Thesis Allowance
-   Assistance to pay Commonwealth Supported Places commitment
    (previously known as HECS), or compulsory course fees
-   Relocation Allowance or Fares Allowance
-   Away from base assistance
-   Additional Assistance
-   Relocation Scholarship
-   Student Start-up Loan (SSL)

Claiming allowances and benefits under the ABSTUDY Masters and Doctorate Award

The claiming method differs depending on which allowance(s) and/or
benefit(s) the student is claiming. The Process page provides guidance
for Service Officers assisting students.

Lodging claims and supporting documents

Where a student is required to submit a paper claim and/or supporting
claim documents, these can be lodged using any of the following options:

-   [] Online - using the Upload documents service
-   Fax - see Processing Service details for: ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY PES
-   Post - via a reply paid envelope or by posting to Services
    Australia. See Processing Service details for: ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY
    PES
-   In person - at a service centre, or in remote areas, with a
    Centrelink field officer
