This document outlines information about common applicant enquiries
about the End of Year (EOY) circumstance review form and questionnaire.
This is sent in late October to applicants of continuing Assistance for
Isolated Children (AIC) students.

The review is done to confirm an AIC student's isolation or other
circumstances. This includes education details and living arrangements
for the following year and to decide their eligibility for continuing
benefits.

Applicant enquiries

Enquiries may include:

The applicant's payment has ceased:

-   AIC-trained staff are able to take an applicant's EOY review
    verbally

The applicant has lost or misplaced or did not receive their EOY review
form:

-   AIC-trained staff are able to take an applicant's EOY review
    verbally

The applicant is no longer the parent/guardian of the student and is no
longer claiming AIC

The applicant (and/or partner) does not have a Notice of Assessment
(NOA) from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) for the relevant
financial year

The applicant wants an explanation of the reassessment process

The applicant wants to know what date they have to lodge the form
