This document outlines how to assess the principal carers large family,
foster carer, home schooling, distance education or carer non-parent
exemption review.

Exemption review

Circumstances of a principal carer for dependent child/ren are looked at
the end of an exemption period for mutual obligation requirements, for
one of the following reasons:

-   large family - four or more dependent children, or
-   registered and active foster carer, or
-   providing home schooling - Note: if the eligibility criteria are
    met, parents/guardians providing home schooling to their children
    may be eligible to apply for the Assistance for Isolated Children
    (AIC) Scheme, or
-   children are undertaking distance education, or
-   relative (not a parent) caring for a child at the direction of a
    Parenting Order, or
-   family or community member (not a parent) caring for a child under a
    formal State/Territory protection case plan

Changes from 1 July 2021

From 1 July 2021, Principal carers large family, foster carer, home
schooling, distance education or carer non-parent exemption reviews were
replaced by online tasks or request to contact letters for those not
signed up for self-service 28 days from the expiry of the exemption on
the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen. These changes do not
affect Remote Customers who will continue to be serviced as per current
arrangements.

Note: during a transition period, appointments booked on or after the 1
July 2021 by the service profiler will still need to be conducted (this
includes rescheduling if required).
