For staff in the Tasmanian Transport Programs (TTP) only.

This document explains how to assess a Tasmanian Freight Equalisation
Scheme (TFES) claim for a horse/s.

TFES horse claims

A person or business may be eligible to claim assistance when they ship
a horse or horses between:

-   Tasmania and the Mainland of Australia, or
-   King Island and the Furneaux and the Main Island of Tasmania

There are different types of TFES claims for horses:

Northbound domestic horse claim – Tasmanian bred horses that are shipped
to the mainland of Australia for permanent retention

Northbound other markets horse claim – Tasmanian bred horses that are
shipped to the mainland of Australia for the purpose of transhipment

Intrastate horse claim:

-   Tasmanian bred horses that are shipped to King Island or the
    Furneaux Group for permanent retention, or
-   horses bred on King Island or the Furneaux Group that are shipped to
    the Mainland of Tasmania for permanent retention

Southbound domestic horse claim for Primary Producers - Australian
mainland bred horses that are shipped to Tasmania by eligible Primary
Producers. The horse is to be used for breeding or stock work by the
claimant in their primary production activities in Tasmania

Southbound domestic horse claim for Manufacturers and Miners -
Australian mainland bred horses that are shipped to Tasmania by eligible
Manufacturers or Miners. The horse is to be used by the claimant in
their manufacturing or mining processes in Tasmania

Southbound imported horse claim for Primary Producers - horses that are
imported from overseas and shipped to Tasmania via a mainland Australian
port by eligible Primary Producers. The horse must have no Australian
equivalent. The horse is to be used for breeding or stock work by the
claimant in their primary production activities in Tasmania

Southbound imported goods horse claim for Manufacturers and Miners -
horses that are imported from overseas and shipped to Tasmania via a
mainland Australian port by eligible Manufacturers or Miners. The horse
must have no Australian equivalent. The horse is to be used by the
claimant in their manufacturing or mining processes in Tasmania

Broodmare on a round trip – are Tasmanian based mares which are
transported to the Australian Mainland for servicing and returned to
Tasmania. They must be either bred in Tasmania or have had at least one
foal in Tasmania

Foals – born from an eligible brood mare may be eligible on the return
journey.

Claim receipt and allocation

TFES horse claims are lodged by:

Online (for registered claimants) using Centrelink Business Online
Services (CBOS) and choosing either:

-   Tasmanian Bulk Upload System (TBUS) - the claimant enters the
    shipment information into an excel spreadsheet template, which is
    then uploaded to the system. The template and instructions are given
    to the business when approved as a TFES claimant
-   Third Party Portal – the claimant enters shipment information
    directly into the CBOS system

Claim for assistance – domestic goods form (TAS002) or;

Claim for assistance – goods going to other markets form (TAS022) manual
claim form and sent by:

-   email
-   post, or
-   fax

Note: the claim must be lodged using one of the online methods if the
claim is:

-   lodged by a TFES claims agent on behalf of an eligible claimant
-   for southbound imported goods

Supporting documents

Supporting documents must be lodged with the claim. This includes, but
is not limited to:

-   TFES-Additional information – Horses form (TAS021)
-   freight forwarder or shipping company invoices
-   supplier invoices
-   consignment notes
-   additional letter, email, or contacts
-   bill of lading documents
-   waybill documents
-   Verification of Transport Details – Southbound form (TAS005)
-   Tariff concession order (TCO) numbers or TFES statutory declaration

Required documents vary, depending on the claim type.
