This document outlines how Services Officers answer telephone and
face-to-face enquiries about the COVID-19 Vaccine Claims Scheme (CVCS).
It also covers scanning and recording documents lodged at a service
centre.

Background

Eligible claimants can submit a claim to Services Australia for
compensation under the CVCS).

Due to the nature of the CVCS:

-   conversations about applications must be managed with sensitivity
-   ensure applicants are treated with respect and the applicant’s
    privacy is protected

A person may attend a services centre to:

-   lodge their application and/or supporting documents
-   enquire about the scheme

See the Process page for more information on managing customer
enquiries.

CVCS enquiry line

The Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) general enquiries line has an
option for enquiries about CVCS.

If a customer calls and selects this IVR option, a Service Officer who
has had the training will manage the call.

Interpreter and translation services

Individuals with limited English, or who are deaf or hearing impaired,
may need help when contacting Services Australia.

Service Officers can:

-   access on-demand telephone interpreter services on behalf of
    individuals
-   direct individuals to information and resources in their requested
    language
-   refer individuals to translation services
