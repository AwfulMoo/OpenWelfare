This document outlines payments available to suspected victims of human
trafficking, slavery and slavery-like practices in Australia.

Bridging visa F sub class 060 (BVF)

The Bridging visa F sub class 060 (BVF) is granted only to suspected
victims of human trafficking. Holders of this visa are eligible for
Special Benefit, family and ancillary payments.

Referred Stay visa sub class 852 (Permanent)

The Department of Home Affairs may offer the Referred Stay visa
(Permanent) sub class 852 to the Bridging visa F holder if the victim
meets their criteria. These are generally that the Bridging visa F
holder will be in danger if they return to their home country, due to
participating in the criminal justice process in Australia.

Password on a Display on Access (DOA) DOC

Due to the sensitivity with this initiative:

-   it is appropriate for the interviewing and processing of Special
    Benefit (SpB) claims received from victims of trafficking, to be
    processed by the Special Benefit national processing team
-   the customer must have a password on their record

Service Officers must:

-   check the SpB claim is complete (with the correct documents)
-   scan all supporting documents
-   submit a Special Benefit - Financial Hardship Fast Note to request
    urgent processing

Special Benefit mutual obligation exemption

Criminal Justice Stay Visa and Human Trafficking subclass 060 are not
subject to mutual obligation requirements.
