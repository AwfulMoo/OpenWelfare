This document outlines some of the common telephone standards to be
aware of and comply with when interacting with customers.

Purpose of standards

The purpose of these standards is:

-   to present a professional image to customers and the general public
-   to advise the correct procedure where necessary, for example, in
    maintaining privacy

Maintaining these standards will allow Centrelink to meet its objectives
about customer contact by telephone.

Examples of standards

Examples of the standards include:

making sure that materials such as pen and paper are close at hand

phrases to use when:

-   placing a customer on hold
-   checking in with them, and
-   taking the customer off hold

being professional in all dealings with customers, for example be:

-   courteous
-   respectful, and
-   use a pleasant and varied tone of voice

never behaving in a judgmental or patronising manner

observing the duty of care principles, privacy principles, and the
confidentiality provisions of the Social Security Act 1991 in all
dealings with customers and staff

check and comply to the Business Imperatives at every contact
