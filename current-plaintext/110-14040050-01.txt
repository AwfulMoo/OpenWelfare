This document outlines information relating to ABSTUDY customers granted
independent status who require their entitlement to this rate of payment
to be verified in the course of a Random Sample Surveys (RSS) review.

Customers do not qualify under the Independent criteria

Customers who do not qualify under any of the Independent criteria may
need a Parent(s)/Guardian(s) details - for the BASE tax year and CURRENT
tax year for dependent Youth Allowance or ABSTUDY customers form (MOD
JY) where parental income information is not already available on the
customer's record.

Verification of Independence categories

This procedure outlines the documentation required to verify the
customer's rate of payment. It includes information as to how the
following independent rate categories are to be verified:

-   22 years of age or older
-   has or previously had a dependent child
-   member of a couple or previously was a member of a couple
-   de facto relationship for at least 6 months
-   working full-time or registered as unemployed for at least 3 of the
    past 4 years
-   orphan and 15 years of age or older
-   parents cannot care for customer
-   caring for another person's dependent child
-   be of school leaving age in the particular state or territory or at
    least 16 years of age, and meet the unreasonable to live at home
    criteria
-   15 years of age or over and has adult status in a traditional
    community
-   18 years of age or over and previously been in lawful custody for a
    total of at least 6 months
-   16 years of age or over and was adopted or fostered by a
    non-indigenous family for more than 2 years and now lives in an
    aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander community
-   been out of secondary school for at least 14 months and have worked
    and earned in a 14 month period, 75% or more of Wage Level A of the
    National Training Wage schedule included in a modern award
-   worked for on average 30 hours a week for a total of 18 months
    within a 2 year period
-   worked continuously for at least 15 hours a week for at least 2
    years since they last left secondary school
-   earned at least $15,000 gross income in approved agricultural work
    anytime between 30 November 2020 and 31 December 2021
