This document outlines the role of Services Australia in Child
Protection Income Management (CPIM).

On this Page:

Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO)

Services Australia has nominated a position known as the Income
Management Contact Officer (IMCO). The IMCO is responsible for liaising
with the State or Territory Child Protection Authority in relation to
customers referred by them to the agency, who might benefit from Income
Management.

Services Australia’s role

Services Australia will:

-   Action any requests from a State or Territory Child Protection
    Authority received through the Unified Government Gateway (UGG)
-   Be the single point of contact to receive any referrals for Income
    Management (IM) sent by Child Protection Authorities
-   Once a customer has been identified, determine if the customer meets
    all eligibility criteria for CPIM
-   Contact eligible customers to book the Income Management Initial
    Assessment interview
-   Within the interview, explain the purpose of IM, including how to
    access income managed funds, and the process for disbursement of
    income managed funds
-   Manage the customer's IM funds to assist them in meeting their and
    their family's priority needs
-   Contact the Child Protection Authority case worker 35 days prior to
    the end of the IM period to discuss whether IM should be extended or
    ceased
-   Undertake further reviews/contact during the IM period as
    appropriate
-   Facilitate the flow of information between Services Australia and
    the Child Protection Authorities in relation to the referred
    customer, for example, changes in care or changes in payment
