This document outlines steps that Complex Assessment Officers must take
when a customer and their previous partner have agreed to transfer
responsibility for a Home Equity Access Scheme (the Scheme) debt and the
agreement is stated in a Court ruling or Order.

Conditions to be met

The following conditions must be met in full for the procedure here to
be applicable:

-   The agency is notified of the end of the marriage or marriage-like
    relationship and a completed Module S has been provided
-   The previous members of a couple named in the Module S both have
    outstanding debts under the Scheme
-   Property offered as security for the Scheme loan was co-owned prior
    to end of the relationship
-   A Court of Law has formalised the legal separation or divorce
    proceedings of persons named in the Module S
-   A Consent Order, Court-issued annulment document or some other
    judicial ruling that stipulates the transfer of full ownership of
    the Scheme security to one person (transferee) and sets out the
    repayment responsibility of transferee for the Scheme debts of both
    persons
-   A copy of court documents showing the mutually binding judicial
    outcome must be provided to the agency
-   Title on the property is registered solely in the transferee's name
-   Both persons must provide consent to the agency in order for the
    information to be applied to their records

For more information about the Scheme eligibility, see Home Equity
Access Scheme.
