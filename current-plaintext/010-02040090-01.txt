This document outlines how to assess and record a student or Australian
Apprentice's independence for ABSTUDY on the grounds of previous lawful
custody.

Independence criteria

Students and Australian Apprentices applying for ABSTUDY are considered
independent if they have spent a total of 6 months or more in lawful
custody.

Secondary students must be 18 years of age or older to qualify for
independence due to previous lawful custody.

This category of independence is permanent. Once granted, the student or
Australian Apprentice remains eligible for independent status for this
claim and any other claims for ABSTUDY.
