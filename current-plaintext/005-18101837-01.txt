This document outlines how a newly arrived refugee who will remain
Centrelink managed post the end of their ‘Refugee in first 13 Weeks’
(REF) mutual obligation exemption, may fully satisfy their mutual
obligation requirements by participating in Refugee Activities.

Refugee Activities (RAC) availability

The Refugee Activities approved activity is only available to newly
arrived refugees with mutual obligation requirements who are eligible to
remain Centrelink managed post the end of their Refugee exemption. See,
Refugee and humanitarian entrant service offer for more information.

Suitable activities to satisfy mutual obligation requirements as 'Refugee Activities'

A newly arrived refugee is able to fully meet their mutual obligation
requirements via Refugee Activities if they are participating in one of
the following:

-   the Humanitarian Settlement Program (HSP)
-   the Adult Migrant English Program (AMEP)
-   Workforce Australia as a volunteer

Recording the Refugee Activities approved activity

The Refugee Activities approved activity is automatically populated on
the Activities and Exemptions (AEX) page when it has been added to the
customers Job Plan.

The Refugee Activities approved activity cannot be added to the
Activities and Exemptions (AEX) page manually.

See Negotiating Job Plans for newly arrived refugees following the
‘Refugee in First 13 Weeks’ (REF) exemption for further details.
