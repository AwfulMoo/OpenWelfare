This document outlines details of emergency or disaster travel for
ABSTUDY.

ABSTUDY Fares Allowance (FAA)

Emergency and disaster travel is available to assist secondary or
tertiary ABSTUDY students where their safety or participation in
education is at risk due to experiencing an emergency or disaster.

Where eligible, travel assistance may be given to the student to ensure
they can return:

-   home,
-   to their study location, or
-   to an alternative safe location

Eligibility criteria for emergency or disaster travel

Students who are eligible for FAA may access emergency and disaster
travel if:

-   they are approved to live Away from Home, or they are approved for
    an Away from Base activity and
-   are experiencing an emergency or disaster

In some circumstances, other people may also be entitled to FAA,
including:

-   parents/guardians
-   a family member or community member with significant ties to the
    student
-   a students' dependant/s
-   an education institution representatives

Note: there are no limits to emergency and disaster travel.

Claiming emergency and disaster travel

How to claim emergency and disaster travel depends on the student’s
circumstances

Claims will be lodged by:

the student or applicant where they are:

-   participating in an Away from base activity
-   living away from home and boarding in a private board arrangement
-   living away from home as a tertiary student

a boarding school or hostel where:

-   the secondary student is living away from home and boarding at the
    school or hostel

Evidence

Evidence in the form of a statement will be required where the emergency
or disaster situation cannot be confirmed through a reputable source.
