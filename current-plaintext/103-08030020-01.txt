This document outlines how a customer is able to cease Australian
Taxation Office (ATO) tax deductions from their income support payments,
Parental Leave Pay (PPL) or Dad and Partner Pay (DAP), and how to action
such a request.

Income support payments

The request to cease a tax deduction can be made using [] self-service
online, over the phone or in writing, either in a letter or a statement.

ATO tax deductions are stopped by coding the Tax Instructions (PITX)
screen.

Details of the request including the source of the request and the date
it takes effect from are documented on the customer's record.

PPL

The default tax rate is 15%, unless the customer receives PPL from their
employer. Customers can nominate a different tax rate between 0-50% by
using [] Online Services or by contacting by phone or in person.

Customers receiving PPL from their employer will need to contact their
employer to discuss their tax rate.

DAP

The default tax rate for DAP is 15%, however customers can nominate
between 0-50%. Changes to the nominated tax rate can only be applied
prior to the payment being stripped. Customers can request a change to
their nominated tax rate by using [] Online Services or by contacting by
phone or in person.
