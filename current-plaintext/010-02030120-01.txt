This document outlines the eligibility criteria to qualify for the
Lawful Custody Award. To be eligible for the ABSTUDY Lawful Custody
Award, the student or Australian Apprentice (apprentice or trainee) must
meet the general eligibility criteria for ABSTUDY. They must also
satisfy specific criteria for lawful custody and the course of study.

Eligibility for Lawful Custody Award

Once meeting the general ABSTUDY eligibility criteria, a student or
Australian Apprentice is eligible for the ABSTUDY Lawful Custody Award
if they are:

-   studying a suitably approved course or undertaking an Australian
    Apprenticeship, and
-   in lawful custody for a period of more than two weeks, and
-   the correctional institution must agree to the customer receiving
    the assistance

Allowances and benefits available to students

A student approved for Lawful Custody Award may be entitled to:

-   Lawful Custody Allowance, and/or
-   Away from Base assistance (where permission has been given by
    correctional institution for the customer to attend

Allowances and benefits available to apprentices

An Australian Apprentice approved for the Lawful Custody Award may be
entitled to Lawful Custody Allowance only. Away From Base Assistance is
not available to Australian Apprentices.

Note: an Australian Apprentice's Commonwealth registration number must
remain current while in lawful custody. If the number is suspended there
is no entitlement to ABSTUDY as an Australian Apprentice.
