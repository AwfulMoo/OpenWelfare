This document outlines specific information about the Agreement
including the process of making a claim for Australian payment under the
agreement and coding of German payments.

Start date

The Agreement with Germany started on 1 January 2003. A Supplementary
Agreement to include double coverage provisions started on 1 October
2008.

Claiming and benefits covered

The Agreement allows customers to:

-   lodge a claim for payment from either country
-   add together periods of residence in Australia and periods of social
    security coverage in Germany, to meet the minimum requirements for
    payment

Australian payments covered:

-   Age Pension
-   Disability Support Pension (DSP)
-   Parenting Payment Single (PPS)
-   Double Orphan Pension (DOP)
-   Carer Payment (CP)

Note:

-   Only ‘severely disabled’ can be granted DSP outside Australia
-   Only 'widowed persons' can be granted PPS
-   The DOP and Bereavement Smart Centre Processing Team assess and code
    all DOP claims. Re-categorise DOP claims to ZIAM_DOP_NCL. For help,
    see Bereavement/Double Orphan Pension Helpdesk

Customers can claim Australian payments under the Agreement by
contacting Services Australia or the German authorities.

German payments covered:

-   Old Age Pension
-   Invalidity Pension
-   Survivor Pension

Customers can claim a foreign pension by contacting Services Australia
or the German authorities.
