Changing study load from full-time to part-time or concessional study load

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Study load to be reduced + Read more ...

The YA, Austudy or PES student advises they are reducing their study load.

If their study load will be:

  • 75% or more of the normal amount of full-time study, go to Step 3
  • less than 75% of the normal amount of full time study, go to Step 2

2

Aggregation of study load + Read more ...

Aggregation is 'adding' the study load of multiple study periods together to determine whether someone is undertaking full-time study. Therefore, if the student has dropped to a part-time study load in the current study period, they may still be considered a full-time student if their study for the academic year can be added together to reach an aggregated 75% study load.

For customer to take advantage of the aggregation rules, they must be enrolled in all units/subjects being included in the aggregation assessment at the start of the study period. For further information regarding aggregation of study load refer to Assessing study load requirements.

Could the student still be considered full-time if their study load is aggregated over the full academic year?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Full-time student + Read more ...

The customer is still considered to be a full-time student; there is no need to reassess payments.

Tell the customer to notify Services Australia within 14 days if their study load reduces to below 75%.

If the customer has been assessed as a full-time student due to aggregation of study load:

  • use the TCSI Review Exemption Fast Note in Customer First, and
  • advise the review exemption end date

This will be referred to the TCSI processing team to update.

Procedure ends here.

4

Percentage range of normal study load + Read more ...

When a PES customer’s study load is reduced, allowable time and satisfactory progress may need to be reassessed. See, Assessing satisfactory progress and allowable time for tertiary students for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)

Study load is between 66% and 74%:

For customers undertaking between 66% and 74% of the normal full-time study load, determine eligibility before returning to this process:

  • Assessing concessional study loads for YA
  • Assessing eligibility for the 66% concessional study load for Austudy
  • Assessing eligibility for the 66% concessional study load for PES

See Step 1 in Table 2.

Study load is between 25% and 65%:

Customers receiving YA (student) cease to be eligible if their study load is less than 66%, unless they have a temporary incapacity exemption. If the YA (student) has reduced their study load:

  • Due to a temporary incapacity, see YA Student or Australian Apprentice becomes incapacitated
  • Due to the death of their partner and has a current a Partner Bereavement (BPP or BPE) activity test exemption on the AEX screen. See Activity test exemptions for YA (student) customers when their partner dies. Procedure ends here
  • Voluntarily. This is the same as ceasing study. The study details must be updated to cancel the student payment. See Table 2 in Payment of YA and Austudy when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course

For Austudy or PES customers undertaking between 25% and 65% of the normal full-time study load, determine eligibility before returning to this process:

  • Assessing eligibility for 25% concessional study load for Austudy
  • Assessing eligibility for the 25% concessional study load for PES

See Step 3 in Table 2.

Study load is less than 25%:

Customers undertaking less than 25% of a normal full-time study load no longer qualify for payments as a student. The customer may test their eligibility for another income support payment, for:

  • YA and Austudy, see Cancellation of Austudy or YA students and Australian Apprentices
  • PES, see Cancellation of PES, ABSTUDY PES and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) PES payments

Procedure ends here.

Assessing and coding concessional study load

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Concessional study load - 66% to 74% + Read more ...

Is the student eligible for a 66% to 74% concessional study load?

  • Yes, Go to Step 2
  • No,
    • for YA and Austudy, see Payment of YA and Austudy when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course
    • for PES, see Cancellation of PES, ABSTUDY PES and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) PES payments
    • Procedure ends here

2

Student eligible for 66% to 74% concessional study load + Read more ...

  • Go to EDCH, if there is current full-time study recorded, select the current course line:
    • In the Student end date: field, code the customer's last date of full-time study
    • Code the Source and Receipt date: fields and 'C'orrect in the Action: field
  • 'I'nsert a new EDC line, with the following details:
    • Participation Status Event Date (PSED) field: Student End Date of previous course +1 day
    • Participation Status field: 66% Concessional Student (C66)
    • Inst Course Start Date field: the actual day the course starts
    • Student Start Date field: the date the student commenced the concessional study load
    • Inst Course End Date and Student End Date field: the end date of the approved concessional study load period. This study load reduction reason cannot exceed half an academic year
    • Record details in a DOC

Procedure ends here.

3

Concessional study load - 25% to 65% + Read more ...

Is the Austudy or PES student eligible for a 25% to 65% concessional study load?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No:
    • for YA and Austudy, see Changing study details to cancel payment when ceasing studies table in Payment of YA and Austudy when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course
    • for PES, see Cancellation of PES, ABSTUDY PES and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) PES payments
    • Procedure ends here

4

Austudy or PES Student eligible for a 25% to 65% concessional study load + Read more ...

  • Go to EDCH, if there is current full-time study recorded, select the current course line:
    • In the Student end date: field, code the customer's last date of full-time study
    • Code the Source and Receipt date: fields and 'C'orrect in the Action: field
  • 'I'nsert a new EDC line, with the following details:
    • Participation Status Event Date (PSED) field: Student End Date of previous course +1 day
    • Participation Status field: 25% Concessional Student (C25)
    • Inst Course Start Date field: the actual day the course starts
    • Student Start Date field: the date that the student commenced the concessional study load
    • Inst Course End Date and Student End Date field: the end date of the approved concessional study load period
    • Record details in a DOC
