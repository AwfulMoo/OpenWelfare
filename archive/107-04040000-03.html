This page has information to support International Services (CIS) staff to determine and raise debts due to lump sum foreign pension arrears including embargo under an international social security agreement.

4

Information to be recorded + Read more ...

Access to tools to help in converting Australian dollar amounts and coding arrears periods is available for staff in CIS through the International Services homepage. See the Resources page for a link.

Information needed:

If there is insufficient information to apply any of the following, clarify details with the customer. Information may be accepted over the phone but delegates must be satisfied the information being used is accurate and clearly documented.

Arrears period

In all cases, the arrears period must be obtained:

  • Arrears Period Start Date (APSD). Note: foreign survivor payments are only assessed from the Bereavement Period End Date (BPED). In these cases, the APSD is adjusted to the BPED + 1
  • Arrears Period End Date (APED). Note: if the APED is unknown it can be assumed to be the end of the calendar month before the date of issue of the notice of grant (NOG)

Arrears amount or foreign pension rates

If available, the arrears period and lump sum amount in the source currency may be coded. However, there are situations where arrears amounts may not be used, for example, where:

  • the country only provides rate information and not an arrears amount
  • the arrears amount provided is not the gross amount, for example, tax has been deducted or amount reduced for debt recovery
  • the arrears amount includes components that are exempt from the income test for that country
  • there are significant variations in the rates of the foreign pension, or
  • policy adjusts the arrears period being assessed, for example, foreign survivor payments

If the arrears amount cannot be used, the rates during the arrears period must be converted to Australian dollars and coded like an ongoing foreign pension for the arrears period.

Note: if necessary, any missing changes in the periodic rates of a foreign pension for the arrears periods, for example, for indexation, may be derived. Access to tools is available for staff in CIS through the International Services homepage. The Resources page includes a link to this homepage.

Exchange rate

To make sure the debt reflects the value of foreign pension arrears the customer receives, policy is to use the exchange rate that was applicable at the date the arrears amount was received for the period of the arrears.

When coding an arrears amount, system processing will automatically use the exchange rate applicable as at the Date of Event (DOV) coded. Otherwise, rates during the arrears period must be manually converted according to the applicable exchange rate and coded in Australian dollars.

Exchange rates are available on the Foreign Exchange Summary (RDFXS) screen.

If the date of receipt of the arrears is unknown, including indirect embargo, policy is that it can be deemed to be:

  • the month of issue of the notice of grant (NOG), or
  • the following month if the notice is issued within 5 days of the end of the month and it is in the customer's favour, or
  • the end of the arrears period +1 for future grants, for example, Germany

For direct embargo, the actual exchange rate used to convert the foreign currency is used. The rate is confirmed by the Finance Team in Zone Tasmania and sent to CIS with the receipted amounts.

Code rates during the arrears period + Read more ...

Note: coding of foreign pensions may be affected by the Agreement with New Zealand, see New Zealand Agreement and foreign pension information.

Navigate to the Foreign Income/Pensions Summary (FIPS) screen.

'S'elect the relevant foreign pension record to navigate to the Foreign Pension Detail (FPD) screen.

Code:

  • The Arrears Period Start Date (APSD) or Bereavement Period End Date (BPED) + 1 as appropriate in the Event Date field
  • 'AUD' in the Currency field. Note: where the determined exchange rate to be used is within 5% of the existing rate, use UK currency (GBP)
  • Determined exchange rate (from Step 4) in the Exchange Rate field
  • Rate of foreign pension in the relevant income fields (for example, Basic Amount, Social/Welfare Amt)
  • Code the Source and DOR fields as applicable
  • Press [Enter]. Note: ignore the warning about the incorrect currency/exchange rates
  • 'S'elect the relevant foreign pension record off the FIPS screen again and code all rate variations during the arrears period
  • If there is a gap between the end of the arrears period and the date of effect of the original NOG coding, code:
    • Arrears Period End Date (APED) + 1 in the Event Date field
    • '0' in the relevant income field relevant income fields (for example. Basic Rate, Soc/Welfare Amount)

Go to Step 9.

7

Coding an arrears amount + Read more ...

Note: coding of foreign pensions may be affected by the Agreement with New Zealand, see New Zealand Agreement and foreign pension information.

Navigate to the Foreign Pension Details (FPD) screen.

Code:

  • Country of Payment and Type fields as for the main pension
  • 'ARREARS LUMP SUM' in the Description 1 field
  • The source currency of the lump sum in the Currency field
  • Date of receipt of the arrears lump sum in the Date of Event field
  • Arrears Period Start Date (APSD) in the Arrears From field
  • Arrears Period End Date (APED) in the Arrears To field
  • Arrears lump sum amount in source currency in the first field (for example, Basic Rate, NZ Actual)
  • Code the Source and DOR fields as applicable
  • Press [Enter]

Go to Step 9

8

Coding arrears because of foreign pension rate increases + Read more ...

Note: coding of foreign pensions may be affected by the Agreement with New Zealand, see New Zealand Agreement and foreign pension information.

Due to system limitations, only the difference between the existing rate and the new rate is coded separately. Care must be taken to make sure any concessional treatment of foreign pensions is applied manually before coding, see International Social Security Agreements.

Determine:

  • rates of foreign pension for the arrears period, taking into account any concessional treatment
  • difference between the existing rate of foreign pension and the new rates for the period of the arrears

Note: if the period of arrears for the rate revision applies for a period where no rate was applicable due to previous date of effect and arrears debt calculations, policy is to assume that the rate of foreign pension continued to apply for that period.

Navigate to the Foreign Income Details (FID) screen.

Code:

  • Country of Payment: field
  • ‘OIN' in the Type: field
  • 'XX YYY Rate Revision Arrears' in the Reference/Description: field, where XX is the relevant 2 digit country code and YYY is the foreign payment type
  • Arrears period start date in the Event Date: field
  • 'AU' in the Currency: field. Note: if the determined exchange rate to be used is within 5% of the existing rate, use UK currency (GBP)
  • Determined exchange rate (from Step 4) in the Exchange Rate field
  • Difference between the existing rate of foreign pension and the new rates in the Income Amt: field
  • Code the Source and DOR fields as applicable
  • Press [Enter]

Note: ignore the warning about the incorrect currency/exchange rates.

'S'elect the relevant record of the FIPS screen again and code all rate variations during the arrears period.

'S'elect the relevant record again and code:

  • arrears period end date + 1 in the Event Date: field
  • '0' in the Income Amt: field

9

Overpayments and debts + Read more ...

Note: foreign pension arrears that are identified as being received before 1 July 2004 are treated differently. Arrears debts can only be raised under an international social security agreement if the foreign pension was covered by an agreement that was in force at the time and only for the customer.

Negative adjustments of less than $50.00 are automatically waived. However, adjustments before a limiting date must be manually calculated, see Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts.

Negative adjustments of more than $50.00 should be reviewed to make sure there are no unanticipated adjustments during or outside the debt period.

Information and tools to help staff in International Services in reviewing negative adjustments for CFP arrears debts is available to through the International Services homepage. The Resources page includes a link to this homepage.

Navigate to the AR screen and check any negative adjustments.

Is there a recoverable debt of more than $50.00?

  • Yes, make sure the Dbt Act field on the Assessment Consequences (ASC) screen is coded as 'INV' before finalising the activity. Go to Step 1 in the Action required to record and recover CFP arrears debts table
  • No, finalise the activity. If the CFP was from:
    • Direct embargo country, see Step 4 on the System coding and reconciliation of embargoes table in Finalising embargoes - action for International Services (CIS) staff. Procedure ends here
    • Indirect embargo country, record all details in a DOC. Send a liaison to other country indicating to release the arrears to the customer, see Agreement liaisons, NZ CICs and exchange of information. Procedure ends here
    • Any other country, record all details in a DOC. Procedure ends here

Indirect embargo requests + Read more ...

Information on the agreed process for embargo countries, including contact details for the foreign pension authorities and methods of information exchange, is available for staff in CIS through the International Services homepage. See the Resources page for a link.

Once the customer debt is calculated:

  • Record and send a liaison to the foreign pension authority by the agreed method requesting embargo of the customer's calculated debt amount
  • Navigate to the Foreign Claim Summary (FGS)
  • Enter 'E' against the relevant payment to navigate to the Foreign Indirect Embargo (FGIE) screen

    • Embargo Start Date: Note: this is the start date of the recoverable debt as derived above

    • Arrears Start Date:
    • Arrears End Date:
    • Amt to be recovered $A:
    • Date Foreign Ctry Advised:
    • Centrelink List Ref:
  • Finalise the activity
  • Run the Multilingual Letters Script and issue 'BLA008' to the customer
  • If the customer has a partner, run the Multilingual Letters Script and issue 'BLA012' to the partner. Place the partner's debt activity on hold for 3 months

No further action is needed until the embargo for the customer is ready to be finalised. See Finalising embargoes - action for International Services (CIS) staff.

4

Partner debts and direct embargo + Read more ...

For direct embargo, it is possible to recover partner debts from the customer's arrears payment in some cases.

Note: no partner arrears debt exists if the partner is deceased at the time the overpayment is calculated, even if the partner was alive during the arrears period.

Is there a partner debt?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, see Step 4 in the System coding and reconciliation of embargoes table in Finalising embargoes - action for International Services (CIS) staff. Procedure ends here

5

Permission to recover partner debt from direct embargo + Read more ...

Permission may be sought from the customer to allow recovery of their partner's debt from the customer's arrears. Permission may be obtained from the customer over the phone but must clearly recorded in a DOC.

Has permission to recover the partner's debt been obtained?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, the partner's debt must be raised per normal procedures ensuring the reason for the debt is 'CFP', see General debt raising information. Continue with the embargo for the customer. See Step 4 in the System coding and reconciliation of embargoes table in Finalising embargoes - action for International Services (CIS) staff. Procedure ends here

6

Coding the repayment for the partner + Read more ...

Finalise the partner debt activity per normal procedures ensuring the reason for the debt is 'CFP' and inhibit the advice.

On the Debt List (OPDL) screen, 'S'elect the debt to be adjusted to navigate to the Adjust Repayment (OPAR) screen.

  • Code:
    • 'POG' (Other Agency adjustment) in the Source field
    • the recovered amount in the Amount $ field
    • the date of action in the Date Paid field
    • 'Debt recovered from partner foreign pension arrears' (free text) in the Reason field
  • Finalise the activity
  • Record the details in a DOC
  • Run the Multilingual Letters Script and issue 'BLA012' to the partner
  • Note the amount for coding the embargo on the customer's record

Then continue with the embargo. See Step 4 in the System coding and reconciliation of embargoes table in Finalising embargoes - action for International Services (CIS) staff.
