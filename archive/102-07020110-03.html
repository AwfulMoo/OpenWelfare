Assessing a student or Australian Apprentice who becomes incapacitated

Table 1: This table describes the steps to be taken by a Service Officer when a current Austudy student or Australian Apprentice advises they are incapacitated.

Step

Action

1

A current Austudy student or Australian Apprentice advises they are incapacitated + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving Austudy as an Australian Apprentice?

  • Yes, go to Step 3 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Temporary incapacitated + Read more ...

Does the student have a temporary incapacity?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2 in Table 2

3

Still enrolled in a course of study + Read more ...

Is the student still enrolled in a course of study?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 7

4

Current percentage of full time study undertaken + Read more ...

Is the student currently undertaking at least 75% of the normal amount of full-time study?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Student currently undertaking at least 75% of the normal amount of full-time study + Read more ...

The student can still qualify for Austudy as long as they remain enrolled and continue to make satisfactory progress in their course and they will still finish the course within the allowable time for that course.

If there is any doubt as to whether the student is still enrolled or is making satisfactory progress, the student must provide proof of enrolment (POE) in their course of study and/or a letter from their educational institution. The letter must state the student's incapacity does not have any impact on their ability to complete the course within the allowable time (that is, they are able to make up any course work they have missed).

Note: a student can still remain enrolled and be making satisfactory progress whilst incapacitated. For example, the student may not be able to attend classes for a short period of time but can make up the course work they have missed upon their return.

Record details of decision and discussion with the student on a DOC. Procedure ends here.

6

Qualifies for 25% study load concession + Read more ...

Is the student undertaking at least 25% of the normal full-time study?

  • Yes, the student may continue to receive Austudy if they qualify for the 25% study load concession.
    Note: verification of incapacity is required, see Assessing eligibility for 25% concessional study load for Austudy for further details
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Student not enrolled in a course of study or undertaking less than 25% study load + Read more ...

As the student is no longer undertaking a sufficient study load they are not qualified for Austudy.

For a full list of cancellation codes, see Coding a cancellation.

Does the student intend to return to study?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 1 in Table 2

8

Test eligibility for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) + Read more ...

If the student intends to return to their study when their incapacity ends and the institution allows them to keep their place in the course (that is, the student has deferred their course) the student can test their eligibility for JSP for the period of their incapacity. For further information, see Eligibility for JobSeeker Payment (JSP).

This period of deferment due to illness will not affect the student's allowable time enabling them to finish the course within the required time. For further information, see Determining allowable time/reasonable time for a course of study.

Note: if the student's incapacity ceases before the student can resume studies (for example, the incapacity ceases in the middle of a semester and the student cannot resume studies until the following semester), the customer will cease to be eligible for JSP as they are currently not incapacitated, and the customer can claim Austudy as a continuing/intending student.

Assessing a student or Australian Apprentice for other payments

Table 2: This table describes information for Service Officers to assist students when they become incapacitated and they are no longer eligible for Austudy.

Step

Action

1

Test eligibility for Income Support payments + Read more ...

  • If the student wishes to receive an income support payment, they can test their eligibility for JobSeeker Payment (JSP)/Youth Allowance (YA) incapacitated
  • Record all decisions and discussions with the student on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

2

Eligibility for a Disability Support Pension (DSP) + Read more ...

  • Although the student may still qualify for Austudy (if they are still undertaking qualifying study), the student can test their eligibility for DSP. For further information, see Eligibility for Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • If they continue to study full-time, they can also test their eligibility for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) while in receipt of a qualifying payment. Next see Initial contact with students claiming Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and ABSTUDY PES
  • If they are undertaking at least 25% of the normal amount of full-time study, they may qualify for PES under the 25% concessional study load while in receipt of a qualifying payment
  • Record details of advice given to customer on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

3

Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

An Austudy Australian Apprentice customer who is temporarily incapacitated and unable to continue their Australian Apprenticeship will be eligible to receive Austudy as long as their Commonwealth registration number remains current.

If the Commonwealth registration number is suspended or cancelled, there is no entitlement to Austudy as an Australian Apprentice. The status of the registration number can be checked on the Apprenticeship Details (NAPD) screen.

Is there continuing entitlement?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

If there is continuing entitlement + Read more ...

Services Australia must be provided with an original medical certificate which:

  • must be copied then returned to the customer, as they may be required to provide a copy to their employer
  • states the nature of the condition leading to absence
  • confirms the condition
    • prevents the customer from undertaking their full-time Australian Apprenticeship
    • is a temporary incapacity
  • states the date on which
    • the period of incapacity commenced
    • the customer is expected to be able to resume their full-time Australian Apprenticeship.
      If the condition is a long term or permanent incapacity, investigate if the customer may be eligible for another income support payment, for example, JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Disability Support Pension (DSP), see Eligibility for Disability Support Payment (DSP)

Details of the customer's medical condition must be recorded on the Medical Conditions (MC) screen. This will allow the system to generate Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA) payments if appropriate.

Update the customer's employment income if required, for example, if no longer being paid wages for the period of incapacity

  • If the customer is still being paid income while incapacitated, then they will be required to continue to report their employment income via self-service
  • The customer's income is recorded as IOP generally so no updates should be required. A medical certificate generally does not preclude the customer from reporting
  • Advise the customer to notify the agency immediately if their Commonwealth registration number is suspended or cancelled to avoid an overpayment

Record details on a DOC

Procedure ends here

5

If there is no entitlement + Read more ...

  • Check the Apprenticeship End Date: field on the Apprenticeship Details (NAPD) screen. The Department of Industry database should automatically transfer information to indicate the date the customer is no longer an Australian Apprentice (date Commonwealth registration number was cancelled or suspended), see Customer no longer an Australian Apprentice
  • Investigate if the customer may be eligible for another income support payment

Also see:

  • For a temporary incapacity - JobSeeker Payment (JSP) - incapacitated or
  • For a long term incapacity - Eligibility for Disability Support Pension (DSP)
