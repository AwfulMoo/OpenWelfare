Assess if the incapacity is temporary or permanent + Read more ...

Does the student have a temporary incapacity?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the student may still qualify for Austudy if they are still undertaking qualifying study:

3

Still enrolled in a course of study + Read more ...

Is the student still enrolled in a course of study?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 8

4

Current percentage of full time study undertaken + Read more ...

Is the student currently undertaking at least 75% of the normal amount of full-time study?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, and the student has:
    • reduced their study load, go to Step 6
    • deferred study and/or taken a leave of absence, go to Step 8

5

Student currently undertaking at least 75% of the normal amount of full-time study + Read more ...

The student can still qualify for Austudy if:

  • they remain enrolled and
  • continue to make satisfactory progress in their course and
  • they will still finish the course in the allowable time for that course

The student must give proof of enrolment (POE) in their course of study and/or a letter from their educational institution if:

  • there is doubt the student is still enrolled or
  • there is doubt they are making satisfactory progress

The letter must state the student's incapacity does not have any impact on their ability to complete the course within the allowable time. i.e they are able to make up any course work they have missed.

Note: a student can still remain enrolled and be making satisfactory progress while incapacitated. For example, the student may not be able to attend classes for a short period of time but make up the course work when they return.

Record details of decision and discussion with the student on a DOC. Procedure ends here.

6

Enrolment in at least 25% of normal full time study load + Read more ...

A 25% study load concession may be granted if the customer has a disability that would prevent them undertaking a full time study load in the course.

Does the customer have a substantial physical, psychiatric or intellectual disability?

  • Yes, the student may quality for the 25% study load concession
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Student has reduced their study load + Read more ...

A student may still be considered eligible for Austudy if:

  • they have a temporary incapacity and
  • they remain enrolled in an approved course and
  • they intend to return to full-time study following the period of temporary incapacity

These customers are considered continuing/intending students. The Resources page has examples.

If the student intends to return to full-time study at the end of their incapacity period, do not update the Education Course Details (EDC) screen. If the customer has an Allowable Time End Date recorded, complete an Allowable Time assessment to consider the reduced study load. Treat these customers as full-time students for the purposes of qualifying for Austudy as a student.

The student must give evidence within 14 days for Services Australia to decide if they still quality for a student payment:

  • A Centrelink Medical Certificate
    • Issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter via the Request for Information guided procedure to the customer to request medical evidence. The guided procedure will create an activity to follow up the return and assessment of the information
  • Evidence they remain enrolled
  • Verbal advice they intent to return to full time study following the incapacity

The student must give more information from their educational institution if:

  • the period of incapacity ends during a current study period, and
  • due to institutional requirements, they cannot re-enrol/ recommence their course until the next study period

Has the customer given sufficient evidence within 14 days and is entitled to remain on Austudy?

  • Yes,
    • Record details on the Medical Conditions (MC) screen
    • Complete the Approved Reduced Study load Fastnote
    • Tell the customer they need to advise Services Australia within 14 days if they are unable to return to full-time study following the exemption period and their payment will be cancelled
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Student has deferred or taken a leave of absence + Read more ...

Students who are temporarily incapacitated and are not enrolled in a course of study are not qualified for Austudy.

Austudy needs to be cancelled.

The customer should be considered for a more appropriate payment as they are no longer considered a student.

  • they are temporarily incapacitated and

Is there continuing entitlement?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Continuing entitlement + Read more ...

The Australian Apprentice or Trainee must provide:

  • A Centrelink Medical Certificate
    • Issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter via the Request for Information guided procedure to the customer to request medical evidence. The guided procedure will create an activity to follow up the return and assessment of the Medical Certificate
  • Verbal advice they intent to return to full time Apprenticeship/ traineeship following the incapacity

Has the customer given sufficient evidence within 14 days and is entitled to remain on Austudy?

  • Yes,
    • Record details on the Medical Conditions (MC) screen
    • DOC the record, stating their incapacity period and that the medical certificate has been recorded
    • Tell the customer they need to advise Services Australia within 14 days if they are unable to return to full-time study following the exemption period and their payment will be cancelled. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

3

No entitlement + Read more ...

Check the Apprenticeship End Date: field on the NAPD screen. The Australian Apprenticeships Support Network (AASN) database should automatically transfer details to indicate the date the customer is no longer an Australian Apprentice (date Commonwealth registration number was cancelled or suspended)

Check if the customer may be eligible for another income support payment:

  • For temporary incapacity, see Customers claiming Jobseeker Payment (JSP)/ Youth Allowance (YA) (incapacitated)
  • For long term incapacity, see Eligibility for Disability Support Pension (DSP)

Record all decisions and discussions with the Austudy recipient on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

4

Requested evidence not provided within 14 days + Read more ...

If the customer fails to provide evidence to Services Australia of their change of circumstances within 14 days, their payment should be cancelled until evidence is provided.

See Customer no longer an Australian Apprentice.
