Reportable non-taxable payments and components

The non-taxable payments and components in the table below are included on the payment summary and are reportable to the ATO. The component amount will be included with the main payment in the 'Tax Exempt' amount column on the payment summary.

From the 2012/2013 financial year onwards, the ATO requested only these non-taxable payments subject to the ATO's Reform to Income Tests (RIT) be reported. While RITs payments do not affect a customer's taxable income, they do affect the income tests for a range of government benefits and obligations.

Bereavement payments are not included with the BASIC component where the primary payment is non-taxable.

Bereavement payments

The bereavement support options available to customers may be required to be displayed on the Centrelink payment summary and reported to the ATO depending on their taxability. See below for further information about each bereavement support option.

Bereavement payment (BRV) is a non-taxable non-reportable component, payable to the surviving partner or the caregiver for up to 14 weeks after the death of the partner or care receiver. It is not included on the Centrelink payment summary or reported to the ATO.

Bereavement allowance (BVA)

Bereavement Allowance is a taxable income support payment, payable in instalments for up to 14 weeks after the death of a person's partner. Bereavement allowance was closed to new entrants from 20 March 2020.

BVA will automatically display on a Centrelink payment summary and is reportable to the ATO.

Lump Sum Bereavement payment (BRVLS)

Lump sum bereavement payment is a component, payable to:

  • Surviving partner or the surviving caregiver - non-taxable up to the tax-exempt amount. Any amount above the tax-exempt amount is taxable
  • Non-partner (except the carer) - entire lump sum is non-taxable

BRVLS is a non-taxable non-reportable component with the exception of amounts over the tax free amount deemed to be taxable. These amounts are reported to the ATO where the primary payment is taxable.

Ordinary Payments

Concessional tax treatment to an ordinary payment is applied to a customer that is not entitled to a bereavement payment during the 14 week bereavement period.

A manual tax adjustment may need to be made to the survivor's tax record where the survivor's new single rate is higher and no Lump Sum payment is payable. The tax free amount is the difference between the previous combined rate and the new single rate during the bereavement period. This tax free amount then needs to be deducted from the customer's tax record.

Any bereavement amount to be deducted from a taxable ordinary payment will reduce the taxable information provided to the ATO.

Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV)

One-off Partner Bereavement Payment is a component, payable to eligible customers on JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance (YA). It is non-taxable up to the tax free amount. Any amount above the tax free amount is taxable.

PBV is a non-taxable non-reportable component with the exception of amounts over the tax free amount deemed to be taxable. These amounts are reportable to the ATO where the primary payment is taxable.

Tax Adjustments for payment summaries - Bereavement Payments

Service Officers manually calculate and determine tax adjustment for the bereavement amount using the Bereavement Payment Taxation Job Aid.

Adjustments on the Tax Details Adjustment (TXAJ) screen for bereavement payments are only made where the primary payment is taxable for the relevant period.

The bereavement amount to be adjusted is applied via the TXAJ screen under the survivor's current payment using component code of 'BASIC'. Adjustments made by the SO with TXAJ access, will be automatically included in the customers Centrelink payment summary for the relevant financial year. See Adjusting tax details for Bereavement Payments for more information.

Disaster payment taxability

Tax Free payments

Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) and ex-gratia DRA for bushfire events that commenced in the 2019/20 financial year are tax free and not reportable to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO). Customers who need to lodge a tax return or who are advising they are not required to lodge a tax return to Services Australia, should not include these non-taxable payments. See DRA and NZ DRA for further details.

Note: ex-gratia DRA was a term used for emergency events before October 2020. Ex-gratia DRA is now called Disaster Recovery Allowance for New Zealand Special Category (subclass 444) visa holders (NZ DRA)

Taxable payments

Except for the events noted above, DRA is taxable and reportable to the ATO for any other event (e.g. flood, storm, cyclone). DRA is also taxable and reportable to the ATO for bushfire events that commenced prior to or after the 2019-20 financial year. These payments will display on the Centrelink payment summary as taxable. Customers that need to lodge a tax return must include these taxable payments.

How disaster payments display on the Tax Payment Summary Details (TXGD) screen

For NZ DRA (and ex-gratia DRA for emergency events before 2020), the Benefit Type for the TXGD screen is FSP. Payments of NZ DRA made after this date will not display on the TXGD screen.

For DRA the Benefit Type for the TXGD screen is DRA.

From the 2014/15 financial year, the corresponding Benefit Qualifier / Event code also displays on the TXGD screen for reportable disaster payments. Event codes can be found in the Minimum Activity Standards for each event. See the Resources page of event specific operational blueprints for a copy of the relevant Minimum Activity Standards.
