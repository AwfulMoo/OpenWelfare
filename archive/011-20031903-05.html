MBS items and assessing - FAQs

Table 1

Question

Answer

Where can I find information about the telehealth and telephone items?

All of the items are available on MBS Online. See the telehealth information on the home page.

Can health professionals bill a normal attendance item to attend to a patient with suspected COVID-19?

Yes, normal attendance items can be billed if the services performed meet the requirements in the item descriptor.

Assignment of benefit requirements during COVID-19 - FAQs

Table 2

Enquirer

Question and answer

Where can health professionals find more information about the requirements of an email agreement for an assignment of benefit?

The Services Australia website provides information on what is required where a health professional enters into an email agreement.

See Bulk billing a telehealth video consultation

Can I process a manual bulk bill claim during COVID-19 when there is no patient signature?

Yes, the Department of Health and Aged Care has confirmed that verbal consent from the patient is sufficient where reasonable efforts have been made to obtain a signature.

Manual bulk bill claims received during COVID-19 can be accepted and processed with or without a patient signature.

Note: claims received with a date of lodgement from 13 March 2020 can be accepted, where the following has been provided:

  • A completed DB1 header, signed by the health professional and witness
  • An assignment of benefit form stating why the patient is unable to sign. For example, COVID-19, risk of exposure to COVID-19, verbal agreement, telehealth, phone consult

Health professionals should keep their own record that the patient agreed or acknowledged the service was provided, and that the Medicare benefit could be paid directly to them.

See Bulk bill claims in Medicare.

Can I process a bulk bill adjustment during COVID-19 when there is no patient signature?

Yes, the same temporary policy applies for manual bulk bill claims and latter day adjustments (see above). See Bulk bill latter day adjustment (LDA) claims in Medicare.

Co-claiming examples for COVID-19 vaccine suitability assessment services

Example 1 - no text is supplied with claim

A GP is co-claiming:

  • a general attendance item 23
  • monitoring and support CDM item 10997
  • 2 x bulk bill incentives item 10991
  • COVID-19 Vaccine Suitability Assessment item 93625

Actions:

  • Item 93625 and consultation item 23 restrict. Text is needed to support payment
  • QITI PF11 Item Restrictions shows pay High
    • Reject item 93625 because it has a lower benefit than item 23
  • Item 10991 restricts with item 93625. Items 23 and 10997 are eligible for a bulk bill incentive
    • Pay 2 x item 10991
  • Item 10997 does not restrict. No text is needed to allow payment
    • Pay item 10997
