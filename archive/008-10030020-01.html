This document outlines information relating to Mobility Allowance (MOB) service profiling reviews undertaken to ensure the customer has continuing eligibility. This procedure provides information on assessing a customer's continuing eligibility for Mobility Allowance (MOB) when they have lodged an employment and/or training review.

Lodgement of MOB reviews

MOB reviews are processed by the Mobility Allowance National Team (AMW). Review forms lodged at a non-processing service centre must be scanned on the day of receipt. Scanning the form automatically removes the review activity from the Future Activity List (FAL) screen and the work item is allocated to AMW.

Higher rate of MOB

Customers currently receiving MOB are automatically assessed by the system for eligibility for the higher rate. If eligible, the rate of MOB automatically increases to the higher rate.

Review mailed to customer

If selected for review, a customer is automatically issued a Review of Mobility Allowance (MA003) form. They have 21 days to return the review form. If it is not returned within this time, an automatic second request is sent allocating another 21 days. If the review is not returned within 21 days of the second request, payment is automatically suspended. The customer then has 21 days to contact Services Australia to restore their payment. If this does not happen, MOB is automatically cancelled.

Accessible (electronic) version of the Mobility Allowance Review

Customers selected for a Mobility Allowance (MOB) Employment and Training review may request an accessible/electronic version of the review form if they have a vision impairment. The customer will receive the review form with information advising them to contact Services Australia's Disability and Carers line if they would like an electronic version of the form. See the Resources page for contact details.

Customers can be directed to the Services Australia website where an accessible/electronic version is available. It is designed to be used with assistive technology. Once completed, the customer must print the form, sign and return it by the due date.

If the customer is unable to access the electronic version themselves, the Service Officer is to send an email to the Accessible Letters Team requesting a fillable Mobility Allowance review form be sent to the customer. See Electronic version of MOB review form table on the Process page.
