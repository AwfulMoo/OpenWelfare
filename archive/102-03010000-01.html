This document outlines the process to take when recipients notify of a change of address.

Notification of change of circumstances

When a recipient changes their address, either temporarily or permanently, they need to notify Centrelink of this change in circumstances within 14 days of moving. They will also be required to advise of any other changes in circumstances as a result of their move, for example if they owned their previous home, they will need to provide details of what has happened to it.

Every recipient's address (including postal address) and telephone contact details (including mobile phone numbers) must be checked at every contact, and updated if required.

Note: if the recipient has authenticated themselves via Interactive Voice Response (IVR), address details do not need to be confirmed.

Centrelink may ask recipients to provide specific information about particular events or changes in circumstances. These requests can be made in writing and recipients usually have 14 days to respond. Failure to respond may lead to suspension or cancellation of payments.

If a recipient requests mail to be sent to a different address this is recorded as a postal address.

Recipients experiencing homelessness

Recipients receiving income support payments are required to provide a current address, if the recipient is homeless or unable to provide a current residential address, the Service Officer should apply a homelessness indicator to the recipient's record and assist them to provide an alternative address at which Services Australia may contact them. The Service Officer should also implement procedures to assist the recipient in their needs, this may include referrals to Centrelink professionals or external agencies.

Note: recipients who are receiving or applying for Rent Assistance (RA) must have a current residential address.

Recipients with an outstanding debt

Recipients who have an outstanding debt have a legal obligation to notify Services Australia of any change of address within 14 days while a debt is still owing, even if they are no longer receiving a payment.

CP and/or CA carer or care receiver notifies of a change of address

When a carer or a care receiver notifies a change of address further information is required to assess whether the carer is still providing sufficient care to maintain qualification for payment. See Change of address for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) for more information.

Note: a recipient may be on a Centrelink payment in their own right as well as being a care receiver for someone else’s CP and/or CA payment.

Income managed recipients

When an income managed recipient notifies a change in circumstances, including a change of address or change in rent, a review of their priority needs and expense allocations is required. See Changes to Income Management priority needs for more information.

Income managed recipient who move out of a declared Income Management area

Recipients:

  • whose payments are currently income managed and who move indefinitely or permanently outside of a declared Income Management area will continue to be income managed
  • who have been identified for Income Management and move out of a declared Income Management area before Income Management commences, or provide a retrospective change of address, may still have their payments income managed

See Customer moves out of an Income Management area for more information.

Recipients who move into a declared Income Management area

When a recipient moves into a declared Income Management area, they may be profiled or referred for Income Management if they meet the criteria for an Income Management measure available in that location.

The location criteria for Income Management eligibility is based on a recipient's permanent residential address (that is, Home or Term address), therefore it is important to confirm the recipient's move is permanent and not temporary to avoid them being incorrectly identified for Income Management.

ABSTUDY students or nominees

If a recipient is the applicant for an ABSTUDY student, (for example, for Schooling A payments) or they act as a nominee for another person, their details will need to be updated on that customer’s record as well.

Notification of changes to other programs

Recipients should be advised that Services Australia does not routinely transfer information between services. The recipient may need to contact other programs and agencies, for example, Centrelink, Child Support and Medicare, to update relevant information.

Recipients with an outstanding Rent Assistance Profile Review

If a recipient has an outstanding Rent Assistance Profile Review, the address update must be done inside the review, unless the update is for a future date. If the recipient wishes to advise a future change of address, they will need to complete their review before they can make the update.
