Applicant for permanent residency

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Documents needed to enrol + Read more ...

Customers will need the following documents. They must provide these documents for each person on the enrolment form:

  • current passport or Immicard, and
  • evidence they have applied for permanent residency. This must be:
    • an email or letter from the Home Affairs or Migration Agent, and have details about the category of their visa subclass and date of lodgement. A digital copy on a phone, tablet or laptop is acceptable

Service Officers must:

  • complete a VEVO check, and
  • record all documents in CDMS when registering a customers Medicare entitlement

Note: a customer’s identity can be verified by information provided via VEVO, or the Immigration Data Link. If the link or VEVO is established, Service Officers are not required to use DVS. See Table 3 Step 5

If there is no data link or VEVO is not established, Service Officers must verify the identity documents in DVS before processing the application. Go to Table 3 Step 1

2

Register the customer and enter the entitlement details + Read more ...

Register the customer for Medicare. Enter their entitlement details as per the below.

To enrol an applicant for permanent residency (including subclass 191):

  • Entitlement Type - conditional migrant
  • Country of relevance - this is the previous country of residence before arriving in Australia
  • Start Date - use the date the customer:
    • lodged their application (onshore)
    • entered Australia to live (offshore)
  • End Date - 12 months from the start date
  • End reason - entitlement ceased

Note: the applicant does not have permission to work if the entitlement type shows a dash (–) or non alpha on the CDMS link. Go to Step 3.

3

Applicant does not have permission to work + Read more ...

If they do not have work rights they must show proof of 1 of the below:

  • a relationship with a parent, de facto, spouse, or child who is:
    • an Australian citizen, or
    • holds a permanent resident visa and resides in Australia

Service Officers must enter the relationship documents in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS).

Note: if only 1 person in the family has work rights, only that person will be able to enrol in Medicare.

Eligibility is not based on the customer's passport or citizenship. It is based on the country they were living in before arriving in Australia. See Document types for Medicare.

4

Change of name on an interim Medicare card + Read more ...

Customers must provide their change of name documents.

For an initial enrolment:

  • Search for the name recorded with the Department of Home Affairs (Home Affairs). This will help match the customer to Home Affairs
  • Link the customer to their Home Affairs record. See Table 8
  • When the link is complete, change the customer's name

Any changes from the Home Affairs will now update the Medicare system.

If there is no Home Affairs link, the applicant's name may be changed once the enrolment is affected.

For a previously enrolled customer:

  • Change the customer's name if they are currently enrolled under the name held by the Home Affairs

Appeals to a visa refusal

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Appeals to a visa refusal + Read more ...

When the Department of Home Affairs refuse an application for permanent residency, the customer will get a notice telling them of the decision.

Medicare will cease their entitlement. The system will send a letter telling them about the refusal.

Customers can appeal this decision and they have 28 days to do this. The customer is eligible for Medicare while appealing the decision.

Note: where a child is born in Australia to parent or parents who have lodged an appeal against a visa refusal, the child may be eligible to enrol. The child must hold a bridging visa, and this must be on the Department of Home Affairs letter and the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) letter.

See Child born in Australia enrolling in Medicare.

2

Documents required + Read more ...

To re-enrol, customers will need to provide:

  • proof of an appeal. They must show one of the below:
    • Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) document dated within the last 2 years of date of processing
    • Federal Court or High Court document
      Note: go to Step 3 to see what proof of an appeal is required
  • a valid visa with permission to work via one of the below:
    • in their passport
    • in the form of a travel document
    • an email or a letter from the Department of Home Affairs
  • an identification document via one of the below:
    • passport
    • travel document
    • Immicard
    • Current Australian driver’s licence (only if the passport, travel document or Immicard was previously recorded in CDMS as the initial enrolment identification)

Service Officers must do a VEVO check.

Note: a customer’s identity can be verified by information provided via VEVO, or the Immigration Data Link. If the link or VEVO is established, Service Officers are not required to use DVS. See Table 3 step 5

If there is no data link or VEVO is not established, Service Officers must verify the identity documents in DVS before processing the application. Go to Table 3 Step 1

Expired foreign passport

To extend a Medicare card when a visa is refused, Services Australia can accept an expired foreign passport up to a maximum of 2 years from the expiry date providing this passport number:

  • was recorded in CDMS at the initial enrolment, and
  • can be verified in VEVO when they present

Note: an expired Immi Card is still a valid identity document. Do not accept an expired Immi Card as evidence of identity for initial enrolments.

3

Proof of the appeal documents + Read more ...

Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) documents

The letter must be dated within the last 2 years of date of processing.

If it is over 2 years old, ask the customer to provide a current letter. It must show that their appeal is still ongoing.

Federal Court or High Court documents

Federal Court documents must have one of the below:

  • a court hearing date, or
  • advice that a court hearing date will provided in the future

This document must identify the customer by name. If it only has a pseudonym, ask the customer to provide an additional letter that has:

  • their name, and
  • that they are referred to by the pseudonym

Service Officers must not reject or refuse an application where a customer cannot provide evidence of an appeal.

Service Officers must escalate the application to Medicare Eligibility Helpdesk if:

  • they cannot determine that the appeal is ongoing
  • the customer cannot provide evidence of an appeal
  • the Service Officer cannot determine if there is a letter confirming the person’s pseudonyms

Customers can apply for a Ministerial Intervention. They can do this with Department of Home Affairs Minister’s office. Send these to the Enrolments and Eligibility team.

Extend a Medicare card

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Log in to Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) + Read more ...

On the CDMS home page:

  • Select Amend on consumer details
  • Key the Medicare card number in ID Number field
  • Select Group

Note: a Medicare Payment Integrity (MPI) restriction may be on the record. This will prevent any changes. If this occurs, see the Comments in Group or contact a HSD Eligibility PSO.

A customer’s identity can be verified by information provided via the VEVO, or the Immigration Data Link. If the link or VEVO is established, Service Officers are not required to use DVS. See Table 3 step 5

2

Extend the entitlement and issue a replacement + Read more ...

The allowable extension periods (see Resources page) for applicants are:

  • permanent residency (Flag P) - 12 months from the date the application is processed by Medicare
  • permanent protection visas (Flag Z) - 12 months from the date the application is processed by Medicare

To extend the entitlement details:

  • Select the Card Requests tab

In the Group Eligibility end date field:

  • key the extended date
  • tick the checkbox to replace the current card
  • Select the Replacement Reason from the drop down
  • Select the Card Request Source from the drop down
  • Select Comments and key the below if the visa is refused:
    • 'AAT letter dated dd/mm/yy, case number, extended for --mths'
  • Select Validate
  • Select Validate
  • Select Update. This will confirm the details

This date will now show in Entitlement. A new entitlement does not need to be added or amended.

Do not change the Group Eligibility Start Date.

All documents and any new identity documents must be entered in CDMS.

Note: when making updates in CDMS after the initial enrolment, try to locate the immigration data link and add it to the Medicare record. This will ensure that immigration updates will show in CDMS.

Any new identity documents must be verified through DVS unless the identity can be linked through the Immigration Data Link and VEVO.

3

Adding documents to CDMS + Read more ...

On the CDMS home page:

  • Select Amend Consumer Details
  • Key the Medicare card number in ID Number field
  • Select Entitlement
  • Select Documents tab

Where a person has more than 1 Entitlement type

  • Select the radio button against an entitlement
  • Select Amend
  • Select Documents tab

The Eligibility and Residency Documents screen will display.

Document Types

  • Select or key the details of each of the customers eligibility documents:
    • Eligibility Document (mandatory)
    • Residency Document (if applicable)
    • Reference Text (number or text of the document, if required)
    • Reference Date (if required)
  • Select Validate

These documents will now appear in document reference. Repeat if other documents are to be added.

For more information, see Documents required to register a customer for Medicare in CDMS.

Assess additional identity documents + Read more ...
