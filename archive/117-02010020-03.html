    • Do not sent an automatic release notice

Notification of a lump sum settlement + Read more ...

Note: some settlement documents only require one signature. For example, some do not require the defendant signature or settlements that have been approved or sanctioned by the court (stamp/seal).

WA workers compensation cases, may require a signed letter. For example, non-disapproval or Memorandum of Agreement (15B) form from the Registrar finalising the terms of the settlement.

Preclusion period and debt cannot be actioned + Read more ...

The CRT are to phone the compensation claims officer or customer's solicitor.

If successful contact:

  • Request a completed SS446 and settlement documents be faxed
  • Cancel the CMM/CHG activity or scanned document
  • Record all action taken in a DOC. For example, successful contact made with compensation payer to re-fax a completed SS446 with settlement documents. Original request disregarded

If unsuccessful contact:

  • Cancel the CMM/CHG activity or scanned document
  • Record contact attempt made and record details of information required in a DOC. For example, Unsuccessful contact. Request has been disregarded as further information is required for action. A completed SS446 with signed settlement documents are required to be faxed

6

Single lump sum paid to settle multiple claims and events + Read more ...

Claims are not linked in the Compensation Management System (CMS):

  • Treat as separate matters
  • Apportion to each event

If there is no specific apportioned amounts for each event:

  • the lump sums are divided equally between each claim, and
  • payments are not aggregated

Go to Step 9

7

Two or more compensation payers or defendants + Read more ...

Claims must be linked in CMS:

  • The CRT must determine the primary payer of the final settlement
  • Record the total lump sum against that payer

Go to Step 9

8

More than one lump sum made for the same event + Read more ...

When one of these payments are in respect of lost earnings, the lump sums are aggregated and treated as one lump sum.

1

Check coding + Read more ...

Before calculating the lump sum settlement, check CMS that all advance payments are recorded to allow for auto aggregation.

For WA Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) the date of settlement is the day the Registrar signed the MOA Form (15B). Legal costs are to be aggregated (see SS446 form).

Select:

  • Relevant Claim ID
  • Lump Sum Payments
  • Add Lump Sum

Code the following fields:

  • Settlement Date:
    • Code the date the formal decision or agreement of the compensation claim was made. For example, the date all parties reached an agreement. This may be determined from settlement documents, Deed of Release, Discharge or other documents where evidence of the claim supports the date determination being made
    • If the dates differ, use the first date unless the settlement covers the date the divisor changed, in which case apply a beneficial approach and use the later date
    • In some circumstances the settlement date may be represented by the date the Registrar signed the non-disapproval letter or Memorandum of Agreement (MOA)
    • Each compensation claim must be assessed against the individual circumstances of the case and time or events where an agreement has been reached
  • Settlement Type: select from drop down box

See the Resources page for list of codes and situations in which they are used

  • Gross Settlement Amount: code the amount including agreed 'costs'
  • Payback of Periodic Compensation:
  • Economic Loss amount: left blank, will default to 50%
  • Issue Advice Automatically: select from drop down box

Where a Preliminary Notice is not sent, CRT should check with the compensation payer if the funds have:

  • been released:
    • send the Q928 Nil recovery letter to the compensation payer
    • raise the debt to the customer (where applicable)
  • not released and the customer is in receipt of a CAP:
    • update the Issue Preliminary Advice field: to 'M'anual (the recovery notice will go to the compensation payer)

4

Transport Accident Commission (TAC) 18 month waiver + Read more ...

The amount of compensation affected payments (CAPS) paid during the Transport Accident Act (TAA) non-payment period should not be recovered under section 1178 of the Social Security Act 1991, if:

  • The injured Centrelink customer is not considered to be an earner under the TAA, and
  • Does not receive compensation for the first 18 months following their injury
  • Waive this portion of the debt under section 1237AAD of the Social Security Act 1991

Where the injured person is a non-earning minor, apply section 1237AAD to waive recovery of any CAP paid up until the injured minor's eighteenth birthday.

18 month waiver procedures:

Code the lump sum as usual, do not submit the activity and ensure that the advice is coded ‘M’, then:

  • Print off the Lump Sum Assessment Results (CMAR) screen
  • Run the PS tool to ensure the debt is correct and any debts on the Debt List (OPDL) screen within the compensation debt period is taken into account
  • Check all amounts are correct in the lump sum activity

When all amounts are correct, the original lump sum activity can be submitted and will appear on the OPDL screen:

  • For the original preclusion debt, print the:
    • Modify/Assess Debt (OPMA) screen
    • Debt Amount (OPDA) screen
    • Component Amount (OPCA) screen
  • Using the OPDL screen, create the waiver debt, this can include multi payment types
  • On the OPCA screen, code all payments made to the customer, then
  • On the OPDA screen, create the debts for the amended debt amount (s1184G insurer debt & s1184F customer debt), then
  • On the OPCA screen code all the payments, when debt has been created the original debt(s) can be zeroed off (no debt using reason code MUL)

This will ensure the system has correctly adjusted the customer’s group certificate

  • Record all action in a DOC
  • Complete the 18 month waiver submission form and submit to the team leader for approval
  • Once approved, scan the paperwork to the customer’s record

Go to Step 5

Manually adjust payment + Read more ...

  • Ensure the payment components are recorded in the correct financial year, see Processing manual Follow-up (MFU) tax activities for compensation debts
  • Delete, to delete the payment component
  • Back
  • Delete Payment Charge - to delete payment line

The $600 Carer Supplement is a recoverable CAP when the underlying payment is a CAP. Supplementary payments are recoverable when the underlying payment is a CAP.

  • Save and Exit to save Lump Sum Assessment details

6

Does the customer have an indicator for family and domestic violence? + Read more ...

A Display on Access (DOA) Fast Note ‘Special Contact Considerations (SCC) – Sensitive Issue - ‘FDV Concern Identified’ ‘will show for customer records with a family and domestic violence (FDV) concern.

Is there an indicator on the customer’s record?

  • Yes, do not garnishee any outstanding customer debts, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Is contact with the customer required? + Read more ...

Check if the customer debt/s meet the following criteria:

  • A single high value debt of more than $10,000
  • 3 debts with a value of more than $5,000
  • 4 or more debts, or
  • If the customer has identified vulnerabilities including, but not limited to:
    • customers under 16 years of age
    • customers over 80 years of age
    • homelessness
    • mental illness
    • family and domestic violence, or
    • low literacy or numeracy skills

Does the customer meet the above criteria?

  • Yes,
    • Consider garnishee action with the customer to recover debts
    • Before calling a customer, send a pre-call SMS message if the customer has a telephone number on record and has subscribed to received SMS messages via Electronic Messaging
    • Go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Does the customer have a future preclusion period and/or an outstanding debt? + Read more ...

  • Yes, before completing the lump sum coding:
    • Contact the compensation payer to ensure the funds are ready to be released
    • Make genuine attempts to contact the customer and let them know their payment will cancel
    • Record details of each contact attempt on a Fast Note
    • Discuss the future preclusion period and any debt (if raised)
    • See Resources for suggested staff scripts when explaining a lump sum preclusion period to the customer and frequently asked questions
    • If the customer has an outstanding customer debt, discuss garnishee action while customer is on the phone
    • Record details of discussion(s) including preclusion period and/or garnishee action with customer on a Fast Note
    • Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Complete the lump sum activity + Read more ...

On the Compensation Management Summary screen, select the started Lump Sum activity under Action Item

On the Lump Sum screen:

  • Complete Source and Receipt Date
  • Select Continue
  • The Assessment Results (AR) screen will show
  • Select Submit to finalise the lump sum

If no Preliminary Notice has been sent and the debt is nil, send the Q928 Nil recovery notice to the compensation payer.

If a manual letter is required, see Compensation Online Advice (OLA) letters.

Waive any debt less than $50 and create a Fast Note.

CRT should complete the tax adjustment. See Processing Manual Follow-up (MFU) tax activities for compensation debts

10

Urgent requests + Read more ...

Most letters will be delivered by the customer’s or nominated third party’s preferred delivery channel. The customer or third party must be given a reasonable amount of time to receive the letter by post.

As the delegate, determine if the request is 'urgent' and/or a letter is required to be given on the same day.

Is an urgent or same day letter required?

  • Yes, fax a copy of the letter on the same day, see Faxing letters using Outlook and Customer First
  • No, procedure ends here
