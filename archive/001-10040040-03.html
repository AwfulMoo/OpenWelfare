This page contains procedures for Prison Liaison Officers and Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officers when a job seeker has been imprisoned.

Finalising outstanding participation suspensions

Table 1: This table contains information about finalising an outstanding participation suspensions when a job seeker has been imprisoned.

Step

Action

1

Suspend or cancel due to imprisonment + Read more ...

When the imprisonment suspension is finalised, background processing will usually automatically finalise any outstanding participation suspensions.

This includes the following outstanding participation suspensions:

  • DNA participation appointment or activity (APA)
  • Non-attendance report received (ANR)
  • Did not meet provider requirement (TCF)
  • Provider work refusal failure (TCW)

Occasionally, an error message will present on the Assessment Error and Warnings (AWE) screen which will prevent an imprisonment suspension from being finalised. When this occurs, manual intervention will be required.

Is a participation suspension preventing finalisation of imprisonment suspension or the imprisonment suspension being applied from the correct date of effect?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 4

2

Check the participation suspension reason + Read more ...

Prison Liaison Officers finalise 'DNA participation appointment or activity' (APA) participation suspensions via Benefit Action (BA) screen. Once payment is restored, the imprisonment suspension can then be processed.

Manual intervention by a PST-skilled Service Officer with a WNPPD security resource is required for the following participation suspensions:

  • Non-attendance report received (ANR)
  • Did not meet provider requirement (TCF)
  • Provider work refusal failure (TCW)

Is manual intervention required by a PST-skilled Service Officer to lift a participation suspension?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Manual intervention required + Read more ...

The Service Officer processing the prison admission is responsible for creating an ACTDOC. The ACTDOC must advise the date of imprisonment and the reason manual intervention is required to lift the outstanding participation suspension.

Note: manual intervention is only appropriate where a participation suspension prevents finalisation of the imprisonment suspension or the imprisonment suspension being applied from the correct date of effect.

The PST-skilled Service Officer:

  • accesses the Participation Compliance workflow
  • selects the outstanding Suspension Report
  • selects the 'Re-engagement link' and then the appropriate reason for the imprisoned job seeker
  • finalises the activity
  • completes the ACTDOC
  • clears out of the job seeker's record to trigger the background processing which lifts the participation suspension

4

Outstanding Capability Assessments, compliance investigations and Comprehensive Compliance Assessment (CCAs) + Read more ...

When the record is cleared after an imprisonment suspension has been finalised this also triggers automatic background processing to finalise any other outstanding compliance action. This includes:

  • Capability Assessments
  • mutual obligation, unemployment and work refusal failures
  • No Show, No Pay, connection, non-attendance, reconnection and serious failures
  • Unemployment Non-Payment Periods
  • Comprehensive Compliance Assessments

Occasionally, manual intervention is required when background processing is unable to finalise the started compliance action automatically.

Is manual interventions required?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, procedure ends here

5

Manual intervention required to finalise outstanding compliance action + Read more ...

PST-skilled Services Officers with a WNPPD security resource are responsible for all manual intervention to finalise outstanding compliance action that the system was unable to automatically finalise.

Access the started compliance investigation in the Participation Compliance workflow.

The appropriate rejection reason for an imprisoned job seeker is: Legal requirement or restriction - Unable to comply (ERA).

Generally, the workflow will automatically identify this rejection reason, however, this should be checked before finalising the compliance action.

For further information, see Rejecting a compliance action.

Returning to payment after imprisonment - Targeted Compliance Framework

Table 2: This table contains information for job seekers, including ParentsNext participants, who return to a participation payment after imprisonment who will be managed under the Targeted Compliance Framework.

Item

Description

1

Key messages for job seekers returning to payment: + Read more ...

Job seekers are required to:

  • do all they can to find work (does not apply if a ParentsNext participants)
  • participate in employment services and other activities to the full extent of their capability and assessed capacity
  • talk with their provider about any circumstances that are likely to impact their capacity to comply with compulsory requirements
  • record any personal events that will affect scheduling of compulsory requirements (for example, parole requirements), in the JobSeeker Account Calendar
  • talk their provider before the requirement if there is good reason why they are unable to meet the requirement
  • check their participation status in their JobSeeker online dashboard and talk with their provider about what will happen if they don't meet a requirement; and
  • re-connect with their provider as quickly as possible after they miss a requirement

2

Payment start date + Read more ...

The payment start date may be affected by an unemployment preclusion period or post-cancellation non-payment period for a non-compliance event that applied prior to imprisonment. The Targeted Compliance Framework does not include waiver provisions for payment during these periods.

3

Targeted Compliance Framework zones + Read more ...

Job seekers who return to payment, will either be in the green zone, warning zone or financial penalty zone.

The Employment Services System automatically identifies which zone the job seeker is in when payment is restored or re-granted.

  • If participation payments have been cancelled for less than 6 months, the job seeker will generally return to the zone they were in prior to imprisonment
  • If participation payments have been cancelled for more than 6 months, the job seeker will start in the green zone

The job seeker will be able to check their current participation status when they log into their job seeker account and should be encouraged to discuss any questions they have about this status with their provider.

4

Demerit balance + Read more ...

Job seekers who return to the warning zone will have their demerit balance calculated based on the number of demerits that have been accrued in the last 6 active months.

Job seekers can keep track of their demerits via their job seeker account and should be encouraged to talk with their provider if they have questions about their current balance.

5

Capability Assessments: + Read more ...

When a participation payment is restored or regranted, the Employment Services System will automatically retrigger a Capability Assessment if the job seeker still has 5 demerit points when they return to payment and a Capability Assessment has not been completed.

Alert messaging will assist Service Officers identify when this has occurred.

When a Capability Assessment is retriggered the job seeker must be referred to the PST. A contact trigger will prevent the job seeker from finalising their reporting until the Capability Assessment is completed.

See Capability Assessments for more information.

6

Job seekers in the financial penalty zone: + Read more ...

Job seekers who return to the financial penalty zone will lose some or all of their payment if they don't meet their requirements and don't have a reasonable excuse.

Job seekers in this zone will return to the penalty count that had accrued prior to imprisonment.

It's important these job seekers receive very clear messages about the importance of keeping track of requirements, updating Personal Events in the job seeker account and talking with their provider if they are having difficulty meeting requirements so they can avoid financial penalties or payment cancellation.

Returning to payment after imprisonment - Job Seeker Compliance Model

Table 3: This table contains information for job seekers who return to a participation payment after imprisonment who will be managed under the Job Seeker Compliance Model.

Item

Description

1

Key messages for job seekers returning to payment: + Read more ...

Job seekers are required to:

  • do all they can to find work
  • participate in employment services and other activities to the full extent of their capability and assessed capacity
  • talk with their provider about any circumstances that are likely to impact their capacity to comply with compulsory requirements
  • talk their provider before the requirement if there is good reason why they are unable to meet the requirement
  • re-connect with their provider as quickly as possible after they miss a requirement

2

Payment start date + Read more ...

An Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP) or serious failure period that was applied prior to imprisonment may affect the payment start date for a participation payment following imprisonment.

Job seekers who would like to discuss waiver or hardship provisions are to be referred to the PST.

3

Penalty amount recovery + Read more ...

Outstanding penalty amounts for a No Show, No Pay, non-attendance or reconnection failure that was applied prior to imprisonment may impact the amount of participation payment that is payable.

See Calculating the effect of Job Seeker Compliance Model failure penalties on fortnightly instalments for more information.
