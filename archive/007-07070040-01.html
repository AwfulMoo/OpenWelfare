Calculating Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part B 007-07070040

Expand all


This document outlines how the rate of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part B is calculated. FTB Part B is automatically included in an individual's FTB entitlement if FTB Part B eligibility requirements are met.

On this Page:

FTB Part B

FTB Part B is not paid to Approved Care Organisations (ACO).

FTB Part B includes:

  • Standard Part B rate per family, based on the age of the youngest eligible FTB child and whether or not the customer is a grandparent carer. Shared care can affect the rate. The Part B supplement is payable after the end of the financial year
  • Energy Supplement Part B where the customer is grandfathered

Income test

Single customers

  • For single customers receiving an income support payment, FTB Part B is not income tested
  • For single customers not receiving an income support payment, FTB Part B is only payable if the customer's adjusted taxable income (ATI) does not exceed Part B primary earner income limit

Partnered customers

Except for periods when the customer or their partner receives an income support payment, FTB Part B is only payable if the ATI of the primary earner does not exceed the Part B primary earner income limit.

If the primary earner's income exceeds this amount, FTB Part B is not payable.

If the income of the primary earner does not exceed the limit, the maximum FTB Part B rate reduces if the lower earner's income is above the Part B secondary earner income free area.

Return to work assistance

Return to work assistance is available for eligible FTB Part B customers if they or their partner started or returned to work for the first time after caring for a child. FTB Part B may be paid free of the secondary earner income test for the quarantine period. Eligibility is assessed at FTB reconciliation based on return to work details recorded.

Effect of Paid Parental Leave scheme payments

FTB Part B is not payable during the 18 week Parental Leave Pay (PPL) period. If the customer claims PPL, FTB Part B ceases from the expected date of birth or entry into care, to reduce risk of overpayment. FTB Part B is adjusted once proof of birth is recorded for the PPL claim.

FTB Part B is payable during the period where a customer (or their partner) is receiving Dad and Partner Pay (DAP). If 1 member of a couple is receiving PPL at the same time the other is receiving DAP, FTB Part B will not be payable as the PPL precludes entitlement.

Customers should include PPL and DAP as part of their estimated taxable income used for calculating family assistance rates, including FTB Part B.
