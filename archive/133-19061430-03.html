Make a Medicare claim

Item

Description

1

Make a Medicare claim

Customers can claim all Medicare services except for bulk-billed or simplified billing services.

Customers need to have current Medicare eligibility and have bank details recorded. If the customer has an ‘Invalid bank details’ flag on their record, they will be presented with a task to confirm their bank details. Tasks will be displayed on the home page. The customer cannot submit any claims online until the task is completed.

They will need the invoice or receipt from their health professional and details of:

  • the service they are claiming (the service must have been provided for someone on their Medicare card)
  • referral details (if needed)
  • the amount they paid

See:

  • Claiming Medicare benefits online
  • Online guide Medicare online account help - Submit a claim
  • User help guide ‘Help customers to make a Medicare claim online’ on the Resources page
  • The Resources page for a link to the Services Australia website for proof of payment and advice on taking/scanning clear photos and documents

History and statements

Item

Description

1

Medicare claims history

Customers can view their Medicare claims history for previous and current Medicare cards for the past 3 years in a combined statement. They can view details for themselves and for dependents under 14 years of age listed on their Medicare card.

Note: claims history does not include rejected or adjusted claims. To view details for a previous card or for adjusted claims, the customer needs to request in writing through Release of Information.

Customers can:

  • access claim information for the last 3 years
  • view, save or print the statement

Children aged 14-17 can contact Release of Information to request a Medicare claim history statement.

If there is more than one person listed on the Medicare card, customers can select who the statement is for.

See Claims history and claims history statement online.

Medicare card

Item

Description

1

Order a replacement or duplicate card

Customers can order online up to 2 replacement cards and 2 duplicate cards each year.

To order a duplicate card, it must list more than 1 person.

To request a new card in the Medicare online account:

  • sign into myGov
  • select Medicare > My card
  • select Get a replacement card or extra card
  • select the reason for the new card request from the Why do you need a replacement Medicare card? list
  • select Next
  • select Edit to change postal or residential addresses or if addresses are correct, select Confirm addresses
  • Success – You have ordered a new card

Note: customers updating their address:

  • will see information about family and domestic violence support services. This message will only appear once per session
  • won’t be able to enter their new postal address if they advise the postal address isn’t for everyone on the card. They will be prompted to get a new Medicare card and number of their own

2

You have more than one Medicare card

Customers with more than one Medicare card must enter a card number when they access their Medicare online account. They will then see the following message You have more than one Medicare card.

Customers can save the number they entered, or choose to enter a card number every time they sign in.

To access the Medicare online account:

  • Sign into myGov
  • You have more than one Medicare card number - enter the card number you want to use – enter the Medicare card number and IRN
  • select Continue
  • You’re using card number xxxx xxxxx x - Do you want to access your Medicare online account with this card number next time you sign in? Yes or No

Once customers are in their online account, they can access their other Medicare cards from the My card page. Go to Step 3

Note: if error message Check the details displays, a new Medicare card may have been issued. The customer must update the Medicare card issue number (last digit) they entered.

3

Use my other cards

Customers with more than one Medicare card can access their other Medicare cards from the My card page.

Customers must know the Medicare number they want to access.

To protect the privacy of all persons listed on the card, customers do not see details about the other card initially. Customers must enter the Medicare card number, and be eligible to access that card online.

To access another card:

  • Sign into myGov
  • Select Medicare > My card
  • Select Use my other card
  • You have more than one Medicare card number – You’re using card number xxxx xxxxx x. Enter the card number you now want to use – enter Medicare card number and IRN select Next
  • You’re now using card number xxxx xxxxx x – Do you want to access your Medicare online account with this card number next time you sign in? Yes or No
  • You’re now using card number xxxx xxxxx x - select Close

See user help guide ‘Help customers manage more than one Medicare card number online' on the Resources page.

4

Get your own Medicare card and number

Some customers can copy or transfer to a Medicare card of their own.

For a list of customers this service is available to see Copy or transfer to an existing card online in Copy or transfer a person or child to another Medicare card.

Note: to get a new card with children see Item 5

To get a Medicare card of their own in the Medicare online account:

  • Sign into myGov
  • Select Medicare > My Card
  • Select Get a new card and number
  • Family and domestic violence message will display, select Close
  • Get a new Medicare card - Who is this new card for? select Myself
  • Your current Medicare card – What do you want to do with your current card? select option Leave the card or Stay on the card > Next
  • Confirm your address – select Edit to update or Next
  • Confirm your bank details – select Edit to update details or Next
  • Review and submit – select Edit to update details or Submit
  • Your new Medicare card – Success, select Check your details > Home

See user help guide ‘Help customer to get their own Medicare card and number online’ on the Resources page.

6

Add someone to my card

Some customers aged 15 years or older can copy or transfer to an existing Medicare card in the Medicare online account.

Copying or transferring to an existing card online is a two-step process:

  • A person on the existing card (the inviter) creates an invite code through the ‘Add someone to my card’ option, then gives it to the person they want to add to their card (the invitee). The inviter must give the invite code to the invitee, whether that be verbally, via SMS or email. The invite code will not be sent automatically to the invitee
  • The invitee enters the invite code through the ‘Manage my card invites’ option, then chooses whether they want to copy or transfer to the inviter’s card

For more information and a list of customers, who are eligible to use this service see Copy or transfer to an existing card online in Copy or transfer a person or child to another Medicare card.

To invite someone to an existing card:

  • Sign into myGov
  • Select Medicare > My Card
  • Select Add someone to my card
  • Family and domestic violence message will display, select Close
  • Before you start page will display, select Next
  • Tell us who you’re inviting – enter Medicare card number, IRN, full name as shown on card, date of birth select Next
  • Confirm your addresses – select Edit to update or Next
  • Confirm your bank details – select Edit to update details or Next
  • Review and submit – select Edit to update details or Submit
  • You’ve created an invite code
  • Select Manage my card invites or Home

To withdraw an invite:

  • Sign into myGov
  • Select Medicare > My Card
  • Select Manage my card invites
  • All pending invites created within the last 7 days will display under Invites you’ve created
  • Select the invite to be withdrawn
  • Select Withdraw invite
  • Are you sure you want to withdraw your invite? Select Yes or No
  • Select Manage my card invites or Home

To accept an invite:

  • Sign into myGov
  • Select Medicare > My Card
  • Select Manage my card invites
  • Enter invite code, select Next
  • Tell us who invited you – enter Medicare card number, IRN, full name as shown on card, date of birth select Next
  • Your current card – What do you want to do with your current card? Select Leave the card or Stay on the card, then Next
  • Confirm your bank details – select Edit to update details or Next
  • Review and submit – select Edit to update details or Submit
  • Your new Medicare card – Success, select Check your details or Home

See user help guide 'Help customer copy or transfer to existing card online' on the Resources page.

7

Leave my other card (end a card association)

  • Some customers listed on 2 Medicare cards, can end the association on 1 of their cards
  • These customers:
    • must access their Medicare online account with the card they want to keep. They can then request to leave their other card
    • don’t need to know their other card number to leave it
  • For more details and a list of customers who are eligible to use this service see: Ending a customer's association on a card > End association online

To leave a card:

  • Sign into myGov
  • Select Medicare > Access the card they want to keep
  • Select My card
  • Select Leave my other card
  • Family and domestic violence message displays, select Close
  • Before you start page displays, select Next
  • Leave your other card – You are using card number xxxx xxxxx x - Do you want to leave your other card? Yes, I only use one card number or No, I still use both card numbers select Next
    • if No is selected – You don’t need to do anything – You told us you want to keep both your Medicare numbers- select My card or Home
  • Review and submit – This will be your only Medicare card number xxxx xxxxx x, select Submit
  • You’ve left your other card – Success – select My card or Home

See user help guide 'Help customers leave a card (end association) online' on the Resources page.

Medicare Safety Net

Item

Description

1

View your Safety Net balance

Customers registered as an individual, or as the family contact (FC) or spouse/partner (SP) of a Medicare Safety Net family, can view their Medicare Safety Net balance.

The Medicare Safety Net balance shows out-of-pocket expenses between the Medicare rebate and the provider’s charge per calendar year. Customers can view details of their particular Safety Net. This can be:

  • original
  • extended concessional
  • extended general

A Medicare Safety Net balance for the previous and current calendar years shows:

  • the Medicare Safety Net balances:
    • the customer's Medicare Safety Net threshold
    • out of pocket costs and unverified payment amount
  • Confirm your family (in certain circumstances – see Item 2)

To view a Safety Net balance:

  • Sign into myGov
  • Select Medicare > Services menu > Safety Net
  • Select the year required from Medicare Safety Net period for drop down list

2

Confirm Medicare Safety Net family

Customers registered as the family contact (FC) or spouse/partner (SP) of a Medicare Safety Net family, can view and confirm their Medicare Safety Net family when their family reaches 70% of the relevant threshold for the previous and/or current calendar year. For a list of customer circumstances this service is available too, see Medicare Safety Net confirmation in Family registration and confirmation for Medicare Safety Net.

To confirm a Medicare Safety Net family:

  • sign into myGov
  • select Medicare > Services menu > Safety Net
  • select the year required from the Medicare Safety Net period for drop down list
  • select Confirm your family
  • select Begin
  • a list of current Safety Net Family members displays
  • Do you need to change your Medicare Safety Net family details? Select Yes or No

Customers will not be able to make a change to their Medicare Safety Net family online.

  • Review and submit – select Submit or Cancel
  • You’ve confirmed your Medicare Safety Net family – select View your Medicare Safety Net balance or Home

See user help guide ‘Help customers confirm their Medicare Safety Net family online’ on the Resources page.

3

Confirm your Medicare Safety Net family digital message

When a Medicare Safety Net (MSN) family reaches 70% of the MSN threshold, the family contact (FC) gets a letter (Letter F). The letter asks them to confirm the composition of the family.

If the family contact (FC) has asked for electronic messaging, they will get a digital message in their myGov Inbox. They can then confirm via their Medicare online account or Express Plus Medicare mobile app. The customer must be the family contact (FC) or spouse/partner (SP) to confirm and have no change to the family composition.

Immunisation history

Item

Description

1

Immunisation history

Customers can view, print or save an immunisation history statement or COVID-19 digital certificate (digital certificate) for themselves (or their children under 14 years). The person needs to:

  • be included on the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR)
  • be registered in Medicare with an active record
  • have received the required number of COVID-19 vaccine doses to be considered vaccinated (digital certificate only)

Note: If a child is under 14, they need to be on the parent’s Medicare card.

Customers aged 14 years or over can view their own immunisation history or digital certificate, if eligible.

The following details can be viewed using the AIR online services:

  • name of person
  • date of birth
  • National Immunisation Programme (NIP) immunisation status (individuals under 20 only)
  • COVID-19 status (eligible individuals only)
  • immunisation services recorded
  • next NIP immunisation(s) due including date due (individuals under 20 only)
  • notices (school entry caveat displays for children under 12 only)

To access an immunisation history statement in the Medicare online account:

  • sign into myGov
  • select Medicare > History and statements menu > Immunisation history
  • select a person’s name from the list of radio buttons
  • read the declaration (if selecting another person’s name)
  • select View immunisation history statement (PDF) or View COVID-19 digital certificate (PDF)

Depending on the customer’s device, they can either Open or Save the document.

See online guide Medicare online account help - Get an immunisation history statement.

Individual Healthcare Identifier (IHI)

Item

Description

1

IHI history and alternative names

Every eligible person across Australia has a 16-digit identification number.

An IHI does not contain health information. It is included in clinical information to give patients and healthcare providers confidence that the right health information is matched to the right person.

Note: the IHI is not their Medicare card number.

Customers can:

  • view their IHI number, select Medicare > My details > Personal details
  • add, view or change any alternative names held against their IHI information

See Individual Healthcare Identifier (IHI) in Healthcare Identifiers (HI) Service.

Medicare Safety Net

Customers registered as an individual, or as the family contact (FC) or spouse/partner (SP) of a Medicare Safety Net family can view their Medicare Safety Net balance.

  • extended concessional
  • extended general

3

Confirm your Medicare Safety Net family digital message

When a Medicare Safety Net (MSN) family reaches 70% of the MSN threshold, the family contact gets a letter (Letter F). The letter asks them to confirm the composition of the family.

If the family contact has asked for electronic correspondence, they will get a digital message in their myGov Inbox. They can then confirm via their Medicare online account or Express Plus Medicare mobile app. The customer must be the family contact (FC) or spouse/partner (SP) to confirm and have no change to the family composition.
