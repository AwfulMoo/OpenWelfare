2

Indications of family and domestic violence + Read more ...

The presence of family and domestic violence may indicate a person is not a member of a couple and must be considered when making a decision about a customer’s relationship status. To reach a favourable decision (the customer is not a MoC) assessors must be satisfied the customer is not in a MoC relationship with the ex-partner.

If any of the 5 factors are present, family and domestic violence may be affecting the customer's inability to change their circumstances and must be considered. For example, but not limited to:

  • joint accounts may exist due to financial abuse
  • domestic duties such as cleaning, washing etc. may not be split due to the fear of repercussions

If a customer in a family and domestic violence situation still considers themselves partnered and will not separate from their partner, this may result in an adverse decision. A social worker should be consulted when the decision maker is considering an adverse decision.

See Family and domestic violence section to see how the presence of family and domestic violence can be identified.

Information provided by the customer may be very sensitive. It must not be made available to the ex-partner or vice versa.

As contact must not be made with the ex-partner to discuss the other person’s review when there are family and domestic violence issues, complete the SUOR review using other means such as Centrelink records, social worker recommendations, and contact with the customer's nominated referee if required.

The customer must be advised that if the ex-partner is a current Centrelink customer, the agency is required to send them a letter notifying the outcome of the SUOR review. This will let the customer plan for the arrival of the letter in case they are concerned it may affect their safety.

If both parties are getting Centrelink payments and both have identified family and domestic violence concerns against each other:

  • Each customer must complete and return a SS293
  • Any required contact must only relate to their form/review. There should be no discussion or disclosure about the ex-partner’s circumstances/review
  • There may be a need to engage with a Social Worker for help with the best way to engage with either/both parties if required

1

Making a decision about an SUOR review - consider the 5 factors + Read more ...

When deciding whether a customer is a MoC, the Social Security Act 1991 has 5 factors to consider. See the Resources page for information on the 5 factors.

For more on recording key information, use the MoC decision support template on the Resources page. The details can be transferred into the SUOR review decision Fast Note.

Base the decision on all available information, such as:

  • SS293 form(s)
  • Centrelink records of the customer and the ex-partner (if they have a record), including the DL screen and scanned documents
  • Phone interview
  • External sources if required, such as contact with third parties, e.g. social worker or referee. Do not access social media accounts belonging to the customer or ex-partner unless it is part of a compliance investigation

Assess each case on its own merits. Consider sensitivities around sexual orientation, cultural issues, ethnicity, religious beliefs and other vulnerable groups.

After examining the 5 factors and verifying any details, consider all aspects of the case to decide whether the customer is a MoC or not.

Aspects to consider:

  • Is there any family and domestic violence identified? The presence of family and domestic violence may indicate a person is not a member of a couple and must be considered when making a decision about a customer’s relationship status. This includes retrospective assessments required due to claims for Crisis Payment (CrP) as a result of family and domestic violence
  • If additional information was requested which has not been provided (for example, the customer failed to respond to a Q164 request or referee has failed to respond to Q270 request), is there enough information to make a decision?
  • How compelling is the evidence?
  • Is it from an objective, reliable, qualified or credible source?
  • Does the information come from a source first hand or a third party who does not have knowledge first hand of the situation (hearsay)?
  • Does the person providing information have a vested interest in the matter, or is there any malicious intent evident?
  • Does one piece of information support or conflict with other information?
  • Do indicators that the customer is a MoC outweigh the indicators that the customer is not a MoC?
  • Is the customer living with the ex-partner for specific reasons such as work or health?
  • Has the customer admitted they are in a relationship with the ex-partner?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 5

6

Unfavourable decision (customer is no longer SUOR) - advise the customer of the outcome + Read more ...

The Decision Maker (DM) must explain the decision to the customer. Make 1 genuine attempt to contact the customer and the ex-partner (if they are also a customer) about the SUOR review outcome before issuing a letter.

If there are indications of family and domestic violence:

  • offer referral to social worker services for support if this has not already occurred
  • do not contact the ex-partner
  • if contact with the customer is successful, the customer must be advised that if the ex-partner is a current Centrelink customer, the agency is required to send them a letter notifying the outcome of the relationship assessment. This gives the customer a chance to plan for the arrival of the letter in case they are concerned it may affect their safety.

If both parties are getting Centrelink payments and both have identified family and domestic violence concerns against each other:

  • Each customer must complete and return a SS293
  • Any required contact must only relate to their form/review. There should be no discussion or disclosure about the ex-partner’s circumstances/review
  • There may be a need to engage with a Social Worker:
    • for help with the best way to engage with either/both parties if required, or
    • if any additional unusual circumstances need to be considered (e.g. only one person has returned their form so far and it is an unfavourable decision)

The customer can request an explanation or apply for a formal review when the unfavourable decision is finalised and there is an operative effect on them. See MoC assessments and reviews/appeals for when a MoC assessment is considered to have an operative effect.

If new information has been provided after the SUOR review has been finalised, the:

  • information should be processed, and
  • SUOR review reconsidered

The SUOR review does not need to have an operative effect on the customer and a formal review is not required in order to consider the new information. See Step 4 of Table 1 of Request for an explanation or application for a formal review

if the SUOR review has an operative effect which is adverse to the customer when finalising the SUOR review outcome, see Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision.

Record details of any attempts to contact in the SUOR Review decision DOC.

Do not replicate the DOC on the ex-partner's record if:

  • there are indications of family and domestic violence, or
  • they are not a current Centrelink customer

If there are indications of family and domestic violence

  • Instead, create a manual DOC using Fast Note. Select Auto text > Assessments and Reviews > Member of a Couple > XX Decision DOC: SUOR/MOC - F&DV DOC
  • If both parties are getting Centrelink payments and both claim family and domestic violence against each other:
    • Run on each person's record separately the Fast Note: Auto text > Assessments and Reviews > Separated Under One Roof > SUOR Review decision

    • While this would be rare, a decision to continue SUOR would need to be revisited with help from a Social Worker if:
      - the assessment was started due to the other person not replying on time,
      - the other person then returns their form and the decision is unfavourable (they are a MoC)

If there are no indications of family and domestic violence

Code a future SUOR review + Read more ...

Code a review for every current Centrelink customer.

  • Service Reason: the customer's payment type. For customers in receipt of:
    • FHA, select FHS (Farm Household Support)
    • CDA, select CAR (Carer Payment)
  • Review Reason:

  • Notes: comments relating to the future review

If the ex-partner is also a current Centrelink customer and there are:

  • indications of family and domestic violence:

    • If the customer the alleged perpetrator, do not code a review on the ex-partner’s record

Customer and ex-partner are no longer SUOR + Read more ...

The evidence provided indicated that the customer is no longer SUOR and the 2 parties will continue to reside together, the determination will be that the customer and ex-partner are in a member of a couple relationship. In this scenario if further evidence is subsequently provided, the customer can request a review of decision.

  • Record all details on a DOC using Fast Note. Select Auto text > Assessments and Reviews > Separated Under One Roof (SUOR) > SUOR Review decision. Include that the SUOR assessment has ceased and the date of effect.

  • Check for and cancel any future SUOR reviews via the FAL screen.
