Transfer of excess SFA (Group 2)

Where a secondary school student's total annual entitlement of combined Living Allowance, Rent Assistance and Remote Area Allowance is insufficient to meet the board charge of the school or hostel, any excess SFA (Group 2), up to the maximum available, is transferred across to top-up the shortfall.

Terminology and formula used

Table 1: This table describes the terminology and formula used by the system from the coding on the School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen to calculate a top-up from the SFA (Group 2) for payment to cover the shortfall in boarding expenses.

Acronym

Name

Description

SFC

School Fee Charge

Amount charged for tuition by school

TSFA

Total School Fee Allowance

Total SFA (Group 2) after application of the means test

BC

Boarding Charge

Amount charged for board by school or hostel

TBE

Total Boarding Entitlement

Total Living Allowance, Rent Assistance and Remote Area Allowance after application of means test
  • TSFA minus SFC = available excess of SFA (Group 2)
  • BC minus TBE = shortfall in boarding entitlements
  • Top-up = the lower of the available excess SFA (Group 2) and the shortfall in boarding entitlements.

Scenario

Description

1

Calculating top-up for 2 sons in study and top-up sufficient for shortfall

Joan's permanent home is in a remote community 119 kilometres from the nearest government school offering secondary level study. Joan is in receipt of an income support payment and has 2 sons who are of secondary school age. Rex is 14 years old and Ruben is 16 years old. Both are approved for away from home rate, and are attending Clontarf College as boarding students. Clontarf College charges are $11,500 per year board for students under 16 years and $16,000 for students 16 years and over, and $1,200 per year for tuition fees. As Clontarf College is a boarding school, payments must be directed to the school. Clontarf College's board and tuition charge must be coded on the School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen in order to direct the payment to the college, and to ensure the system can automatically calculate the transfer of any excess SFA (Group 2) to top-up any shortfall in boarding entitlements where necessary.

Rex's entitlement:

Rex is entitled to a total of $10,186.11 in Living Allowance, Rent Assistance (RA) and Remote Area Allowance (RAA) to assist with boarding costs, and a total of $11,011 in SFA (Group 2) to assist with tuition costs.

Rex has a shortfall of boarding entitlements of $1,313.89 ($11,500 board charge minus $10,186.11 board entitlement), but an excess of SFA (Group 2) of $9,811 ($11,011 SFA (Group 2) minus $1,200 tuition charge).

The system will automatically act to transfer only the necessary amount, up to the maximum available, of excess SFA (Group 2) to meet the shortfall of the board charge. An amount of $1,313.89 would be transferred to meet boarding costs. The remaining unused $7,903.11 of SFA (Group 2) is not available to the school or student for use.

Calculation for Rex (as per table found above)

SFC = $1,200

TSFA = $11,011

BC = $11,500

TBE = $10,186.15

TSFA ($11,011) minus SFC ($1,200) = available excess SFA (Group 2) $9,811

BC ($11,500) minus TBE ($10,186.11) = shortfall in boarding entitlements of $1,313.89

Top-up = the lower of the available excess SFA (Group 2) ($9,811) and the shortfall in boarding entitlements ($1,313.89). Top-up will be $1,313.89.

Ruben's entitlement:

Ruben is entitled to a total of $15,348.25 in Living Allowance, RA and RAA to assist with boarding costs, and a total of $11,011 in SFA (Group 2) to assist with tuition costs.

Ruben has a shortfall of boarding entitlements of $651.75 ($16,000 board charge minus $15,348.25 board entitlement), but an excess of SFA (Group 2) of $9,811 ($11,011 SFA (Group 2) minus $1,200 tuition charge).

The system will automatically act to transfer only the necessary amount, up to the maximum available, of excess SFA (Group 2) to meet the shortfall of the board charge. An amount of $651.75 would be transferred to meet boarding costs. The remaining $8,565.25 of SFA (Group 2) is not available to the school or student for use.

Calculation for Ruben

SFC = $1,200

TSFA = $11,011

BC = $16,000

TBE = $15,348.25

TSFA ($11,011) minus SFC ($1,200) = available excess SFA (Group 2) of $9,811

BC ($16,000) minus TBE ($15,348.25) = shortfall in boarding entitlements of $651.75

Top-up = the lower of the available excess SFA (Group 2) ($9,811) and the shortfall in boarding entitlements ($651.75). Top-up will be $651.75.

2

Calculating top-up-and insufficient for shortfall

Stanley is 14 years old and approved for away from home rate to undertake a specialist agricultural course at Moon Hill Agricultural College. Moon Hill College charges $15,000 per year in boarding charges and $6,000 per year in tuition charges. As Moon Hill College is a boarding school, payments must be directed to the College. Board and tuition charge must be coded on the School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen in order to direct the payment to the college, and to ensure the system can automatically calculate the transfer of any excess School Fees Allowance (SFA) (Group 2) to top-up any shortfall in boarding entitlements where necessary.

Stanley's entitlement:

Stanley is entitled to a total of $9,711.61 in Living Allowance and Rent Assistance to assist with boarding costs, and a total of $11,011 in SFA (Group 2) to assist with tuition costs.

Stanley has a shortfall of boarding entitlements of $5,288.39 ($15,000 board charge minus $9,711.61 board entitlement) but an excess of SFA (Group 2) of $5,011 ($11,011 SFA (Group 2) minus $6,000 tuition charge).

The system will automatically act to transfer only the necessary amount, up to the maximum available, of unused SFA (Group 2) to meet the shortfall of the board charge. In this case, Stanley has an excess of SFA (Group 2) of $5,011 which is transferred. However it is insufficient to cover the total board shortfall. The remaining shortfall is the responsibility of the parents.

Calculation for Stanley

SFC = $6,000

TSFA = $11,011

BC = $15,000

TBE = $9,711.61

TSFA ($11,011) minus SFC ($6,000) = available excess SFA (Group 2) of $5,011

BC ($15,000) minus TBE ($9,711.61) = shortfall in boarding entitlements of $5,288.39

Top-up = the lower of the available excess SFA (Group 2) ($5,011) and the shortfall in boarding entitlements ($5,288.39). Top-up will be $5,011.

1

Emily is 14 years old and lives on Palm Island. The local school is a recognised limited program school, therefore Emily meets the eligibility criteria for the ABSTUDY away from home payment.

Emily applies for ABSTUDY to attend Cairns State High School starting on 22 January 2018, and provides a Rent Certificate with the claim advising $400 a fortnight will be charged for meals and accommodation to board privately in Cairns starting on 20 January 2018.

As a secondary student, Emily is entitled to the away from home rate from 1 January 2018, but not eligible for Rent Assistance (RA) until 20 January 2018.

To ensure correct payment of RA and the away from home rate, 2 event dates will need to be coded on the AC screen within the ABSTUDY claim. For event date 01/01/2018 code:

  • Home Ownership: 'NHO'
  • Address Type: 'HOM'
  • Event Date: '01/01/2018'
  • Type of Rent: 'NRP'
  • Sharer Status: 'NAS' (Note: coding 'LWP' will result in the claim rejecting Doesn’t meet Age Requirements (AGD))

For event date 20/01/2018 (the date the private boarding arrangement commences) code:

  • Home Ownership: 'NHO'
  • Address Type: 'TER' (or 'HOM' if permanent home is in a RAA area)
  • Event Date: '20/01/2018'
  • Type of Rent: 'BOA'
  • Sharer Status: 'SHR' (or 'SHH' if the board provider owns the home)
  • Gross/Total Household Rent: '$400'
  • Net Rent Amount: '$400'
  • Rent Verification: 'VEC' - (as Rent Certificate SU523HD has been provided as part of the new claim)
