View Schoolkids Bonus details + Read more ...

Go to the Schoolkids Bonus Explanation screen via the Workspace > Family Assistance menu option.

This screen can be used to check the date the Schoolkids Bonus payment was issued, the education level, amount paid, and whether the rate was affected by shared care or a blended family assessment.

2

Eligibility for FTB Part A customers + Read more ...

A person was eligible for Schoolkids Bonus for a child in their care on a bonus test day if all the following were met:

  • they were entitled to FTB Part A for the child on the bonus test day via an FTB instalment claim, FTB lump sum claim or FTB bereavement claim
  • for bonus test days in 2015 and 2016, the family's adjusted taxable income was $100,000 or less
  • their FTB child met the study requirements

FTB instalment customers were paid the Schoolkids Bonus if they were eligible based on their legislative rate of FTB Part A.

During FTB reconciliation for the 2014-15 and 2015-16 financial years, families whose actual adjusted taxable income was:

  • over $100,000 incurred an overpayment for any Schoolkids Bonus amount paid for bonus test days in that financial year
  • $100,000 or less could receive a top-up payment of Schoolkids Bonus for bonus test days in that financial year

Retrospective grants and reassessments of FTB entitlement for a period including a bonus test day could continue to generate a Schoolkids Bonus payment or debt.

If FTB Part A payments were prohibited due to non-lodgement of tax returns as at the relevant bonus test day, Schoolkids Bonus was only payable (if eligible) when the prohibition is lifted or FTB is reconciled for the relevant financial year.

3

Study requirements for Schoolkids Bonus for FTB Part A customers + Read more ...

Study requirements were met for an FTB child:

  • aged under 16 if they:
    • participated in primary or secondary education or undertook an eligible activity for at least 1 day in the 6 months before the bonus test day, and started the study/activity for at least 1 day in the 6 months after the bonus test date), or
    • started primary education in the 6 months after the bonus test day (a start-up payment was paid for the bonus test day)
  • aged 16 to turning 19 in the relevant calendar year if they were in full-time secondary study (or had an FTB exemption category coded) for at least 1 day in the 6 months before the bonus test day, and they were studying and had not yet completed year 12 or equivalent (they can be repeating Year 12)

If a child completed secondary education in the 6 months before the bonus test day and had undertaken primary or secondary education on at least 1 day in 2012, a School Leaver Payment was paid for the bonus test day.

There are state and territory differences for primary and secondary schooling, for example secondary school starts in Year 7 for all states except South Australia where it starts in Year 8.

4

Eligible activities + Read more ...

Schoolkids Bonus study requirements could be met by undertaking an eligible activity.

For children under 16, eligible activities were:

  • home education at the primary or secondary level
    • they received education at their home or another person's home
    • the education wholly or substantially replaced the education they would otherwise receive by attending school
    • they were enrolled or registered in the State or Territory in which they resided as a home schooled student
  • studying the equivalent of primary or secondary education at a foreign education institution
  • special education from the calendar year after they turned:
    • 4 years and 5 months for primary Schoolkids Bonus amount
    • 11 years and 5 months for secondary Schoolkids Bonus amount

For children aged 16 or over, eligible activities were:

  • full time home education accredited towards a formal secondary qualification
  • studying overseas full-time that would help or allow them to complete the final year of secondary school or an equivalent level of education
  • special education

Special education

This is education that is not primary or secondary education and is primarily for young people with a disability, specialised to their needs and capacity and designed to help them function better in society.

Students attending Special Education were entitled to the secondary rate of Schoolkids Bonus from the calendar year following the year they turned 11 years and 5 months.

Unschooling

Children being un-schooled did not qualify for Schoolkids Bonus.

5

Blended families, changes in care and shared care + Read more ...

Only FTB Part A customers with an eligible child in their care on the bonus test day were eligible to receive Schoolkids Bonus.

If care of a child was shared by more than one carer, the percentage of shared care used for family assistance, the FTB % as displayed on the FYSH Grp Data Display (FYSHA) screen, was used to determine the amount of Schoolkids Bonus payable. Enter the child's customer reference number to display the relevant shared care percentage for the child.

Across states: FTB customers sharing the care of a child across state borders would receive Schoolkids Bonus based on their child’s age and the customer's state of residence. In some situations this would result in different rates of Schoolkids Bonus being paid.

Blended families: Schoolkids Bonus was paid to each member of the couple according to their elected blended family percentage on the bonus test day.

Adjustment to blended family

If a blended family receives Schoolkids Bonus and later adjusts their blended family percentage or transfers all payments into one customer's name during the financial year, this is not treated as a change of circumstances for a Schoolkids Bonus top-up: Schoolkids Bonus should not be redistributed between parents.

6

Bereavement + Read more ...

Child bereavement

If a bonus test day fell during the bereavement period for a deceased FTB Part A child (who would have met the eligibility rules for Schoolkids Bonus had they not died), Schoolkids Bonus would be paid to the FTB customer for that child.

For more information, see Bereavement payment of FTB.

Death of potential FTB claimant

If a potential FTB claimant died and would have been eligible for FTB Part A and Schoolkids Bonus in the period before their death but had not claimed, an appropriate person may submit a claim in substitution for the deceased person. Notification time limits applied.

For more information, see Initial contact after the death of a potential family assistance and Paid Parental Leave customer.

7

Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) mutual customers + Read more ...

If receiving DVA payments under the Veterans' Children Education Scheme (VCES) and the Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act Education and Training Scheme (MRCAETS), DVA would pay Schoolkids Bonus to mutual customers for mutual students unless the customer was subject to Income Management either through the Child Protection or Voluntary Income Management schemes.

If the mutual customer was Income Managed, Services Australia would pay Schoolkids Bonus and apply Income Management.

The Schoolkids Bonus Explanation screen details Schoolkids Bonus eligibility, including where Schoolkids Bonus is not paid due to DVA data matching.

Upon completion of claims for FTB, the Schoolkids Bonus Explanation screen displayed a status of DVA Matching. To prevent both Services Australia and Department of Veteran Affairs paying Schoolkids Bonus for the same child, daily data matching took place.

  • If the data match returns no link to DVA, the FTB customer is paid Schoolkids Bonus by Services Australia and the eligibility details update on the Schoolkids Bonus Explanation screen
  • If the data match returns a link to the DVA, the ineligibility reason of DVA child displays on the Schoolkids Bonus Explanation screen. Schoolkids Bonus is paid by DVA

If a duplicate payment has been made, e.g. payment made by both Services Australia and DVA, a debt should be raised by Services Australia due to delegations under the legislation.

8

Updating primary and/or secondary education details + Read more ...

The period to notify a change to a child’s education status and receive a top-up of Schoolkids Bonus for the 2015-16 financial year has now ended. To prevent payment, an override should be applied to payments being produced as a result of changes to education status. See Table 4 for override coding.

If a 2015-16 FTB lump sum claim is lodged after 30 June 2017 and special circumstances have been identified, child education details will need to be recorded using the Child Education Details workflow.

Note: due to the cessation of Schoolkids Bonus it is no longer necessary to update the child education details for children under the age of 15 years and 10 months for a date of event (DOV) after 31 December 2016.

Debts + Read more ...

A Schoolkids Bonus (SKB) debt could arise if the FTB customer was not entitled to the payment for any reason. This includes a retrospective assessment where it is determined that they were not entitled to the payment already received for any relevant bonus test day.

For example, the customer is paid for the child for bonus test day of 1 January 2014. They then advise the child left care on 29 December 2013. They are not entitled to FTB Part A or SKB payment for bonus test day 1 January 2014.

FTB customers who lose FTB eligibility as a result of FTB non-lodger processing will also lose their entitlement to SKB payments for the bonus test days in the relevant financial year. See Item 4.

If FTB Part A entitlement is lost:

  • on a date after the relevant bonus test day, a debt is not raised, e.g. SKB is paid for 1 January 2016 and the FTB customer then advises the child left care on 6 January 2016
  • retrospectively, on a date on or before an SKB bonus test day, the FTB customer is not entitled to any SKB paid and the SKB will be raised as an overpayment (this may not apply where FTB is cancelled retrospectively due to a Youth Allowance backdated claim)

If SKB is paid to an FTB customer for a child for a bonus test day and the child then claims and is granted a qualifying income support payment (ISP) for a period covering the bonus test day, see Item 10 in Table 3.

Debts are to be raised as standalone SKB debts as it is a separate payment, not a component of FTB. SKB debts should not be combined with family assistance debts, e.g. reconciliation or FTB qualification debts.

SKB debts can be recovered from an FTB top up or through withholding of ongoing FTB fortnightly payments.

The SKB may be used to offset family assistance debts.

2

FTB reconciliation process + Read more ...

Schoolkids Bonus (SKB) is impacted by FTB reconciliation outcomes.

For 2013 and 2014 bonus test days

To maintain SKB entitlement, a person must remain entitled to FTB Part A at reconciliation. If entitlement to FTB Part A is lost at reconciliation for any reason, an automatic SKB debt is raised. SKB debts may be recovered from an FTB top-up.

An FTB customer not eligible for SKB during the financial year because their estimated adjusted taxable income was greater than the FTB Part A income test is automatically paid SKB when their FTB is reconciled and they gain entitlement to FTB Part A and all other eligibility criteria are met. Note: this includes cases where income has been confirmed late, e.g. after the end of the lodgement year.

For 2015 and 2016 bonus test days

To maintain SKB entitlement, a person must remain entitled to FTB Part A at reconciliation and have an adjusted taxable income of $100,000 or less.

An FTB customer not eligible for SKB during the financial year because their estimated adjusted taxable income was greater than $100,000 is automatically paid SKB when their FTB is reconciled if their actual adjusted taxable income was $100,000 or less and all other eligibility criteria are met. Note: this includes cases where income has been confirmed late, e.g. after the end of the lodgement year.

SKB top-ups are paid immediately and do not depend on receipt of an ATO wash-up transaction. FTB top-ups and SKB top-ups may be paid at different times.

A letter is not sent advising the SKB payment.

Prohibition of FTB instalments

If SKB was not paid during the financial year due to Prohibition of FTB instalments (PIP), the SKB will be paid when FTB reconciles and Part A eligibility is confirmed and all other eligibility criteria are met, e.g. income test. Note: this includes cases where income has been confirmed late, e.g. after the end of the lodgement year.

Debts and debt recovery

If SKB was paid to an FTB customer during the financial year based on estimated income of $100,000 or less and their actual adjusted income for the financial year was more than $100,0000 or they were not entitled to FTB Part A on the bonus test day, an automatic SKB debt is raised. SKB debts may be recovered from an FTB top-up.

3

FTB reconciliation impacts for single income support payment (ISP) customers + Read more ...

Single FTB customers who received an ISP for the 2014-15 and/or 2015-16 financial year had their Schoolkids Bonus (SKB) entitlement reassessed when FTB reconciliation occurred.

The SKB income test did not initially apply to single FTB customers on an ISP on the bonus test day due to the SKB assessment income test exemption rules for FTB instalment customers. The SKB income test did apply when FTB reconciliation occurred.

For most of these FTB customers, this did not change their SKB entitlement. However, for a small group who had a most recent income estimate recorded of over $100,000 an SKB debt (debt reason SIN) was automatically raised for all SKB payments made in the reconciled year. These FTB customers had to provide a revised annual income estimate if applicable to have their entitlements reassessed. A revised annual income estimate of $100,000 or less resulted in the SKB debt (debt reason SIN) automatically finalising as no debt.

4

FTB non-lodger or late lodger assessment + Read more ...

If an FTB customer becomes an FTB non-lodger and a debt is raised for all FTB Part A received for the relevant financial year, the Schoolkids Bonus (SKB) payments paid for the bonus test days in the relevant financial year are also raised as a debt as a result of the loss of eligibility to FTB Part A.

SKB debts are raised separately to the FTB non-lodger debt as it is not a component of FTB.

  • SKB debts resulting from the FTB non-lodger processing are identified as a Schoolkids Bonus Non-Lodger debt (SNL)
  • If the customer is no longer a non-lodger for FTB Part A for the relevant financial year and does not have an entitlement to SKB, the debt is automatically determined as an SKB qualification debt (SNE)

SKB debts as a result of FTB non-lodger processing are automatically determined and Account Payable notices automatically issued. An Account Payable notice is issued for any SKB payments they received during the non-lodger year.

It may be necessary to explain the FTB reconciliation result (non-lodger) as SKB entitlement is lost when FTB is cancelled. See Enquiries about FTB reconciliation or lump sum claim.

If FTB Part A eligibility is regained, e.g. tax returns are lodged, SKB entitlement is also regained. The SKB non-lodger debt is zeroed and finalised no debt, any resulting SKB payment adjustment is automatic. A letter is sent advising that the debt is no longer recoverable and does not need to be repaid.

If eligibility is lost for a bonus test day as a result of being an FTB Potential Non-lodger or FTB Non-Lodger, that bonus test day will display on the Schoolkids Bonus Explanation screen with the ineligible reason Potential FTB Non Lodger or FTB Non Lodger.

If an undetermined SKB qualification debt is present for a bonus test day that is subject to FTB non-Lodger processing, this debt will not be picked up by the automated processing. The debt shell will remain on the Activity List (AL) screen and will need to be actioned under existing SKB qualification debt processing guidelines.

SKB not paid during the instalment period -SKB top ups to late lodgers

As SKB is a standalone payment, there are no time limitations applied to payment of SKB top-ups for customers where:

  • SKB was not paid during the instalment period as the income estimate was above the SKB income limit, and
  • the adjusted taxable income in reconciliation is equal to or below the income limit

All other eligibility criteria must be met, including the notification of child education details before the end of the lodgement year.

This means SKB top-ups resulting from an over-estimate of income (over $100,000) are payable for the 2014-15 and 2015-16 financial years where actual income has been confirmed late, e.g. after the end of the lodgement year, and is $100,000 or less.

5

Auto-adjustment and offsetting of Schoolkids Bonus debts + Read more ...

Schoolkids Bonus (SKB) debts created as a result of a person losing SKB eligibility as a result of FTB reconciliation or re-reconciliation were automatically determined with a debt reason code of SIN or SNE.

SKB qualification debts identified as a result of the FTB late lodger process were also automatically determined. In this case the SKB non-lodger (SNL) debt was zeroed and an automatically determined (SNE or SIN) debt was created.

The debt reason code can be viewed on the Debt List (OPDL) screen and depends on the reason for the loss of eligibility:

  • SNL = SKB debt following an FTB Non-Lodger determination
  • SIN = SKB debt as actual income was above $100,000
  • SNE = SKB debt due to a loss of eligibility for a reason other than income

Automatic SKB debts (SIN, SNE and SNL) cannot be adjusted manually:

  • If there is a subsequent reassessment and the FTB customer regained entitlement to SKB for the relevant child for the bonus test day, the corresponding debt automatically adjusted (finalised no debt) eliminating the need for an SKB override.
  • If the debt did not auto-adjust, the case had to be reviewed to establish why this was occurring. E.g. if due to an SKB override, the override needed to be removed (take the override entries for the affected child and bonus test day back to 'Select An Option'). See Table 4 for override coding.
  • SNE debts determined before 13 June 2015 did not automatically adjust. It was only on or after this date that SNE debts automatically adjusted to changes in Schoolkids Bonus entitlement if reconciliation occurred.

SKB debts with a debt reason other than SIN, SNL or SNE did not automatically adjust. In these instances, an SKB override was needed to prevent a duplicate payment of SKB and the corresponding SKB debt needed to be adjusted or finalised no debt (FND).

Any change to SKB entitlement was captured on the Schoolkids Bonus Explanation screen but any auto adjustments of debts did not display. Check the Payment Summary (PS) screen and Activity Results (AR) screen to determine if an auto-adjustment occurred before referring an SKB override request.

6

Interim pause of Schoolkids Bonus for 2013 bonus test days + Read more ...

An interim pause process was applied from 1 July 2014 for Schoolkids Bonus (SKB) reassessments for the 1 January 2013 and 30 June 2013 bonus test days.

The pause was lifted on 25 October 2014 and all 'paused' SKB reassessments were completed. The system automatically reassessed entitlement for 1 January 2013 and 30 June 2013 bonus test days for FTB customers who had a potential change in their SKB rate.

Changes which triggered a reassessment included:

  • 2012-13 FTB non-lodger determinations
  • child education details updated on or after 1 July 2014 that impacted on the rate of SKB for the 1 January 2013 and/or 30 June 2013 bonus test days
  • any reassessment of FTB Part A that covered the 1 January 2013 and/or 30 June 2013 bonus test days

7

Retrospective grant of FTB, including FTB lump sum claims + Read more ...

If FTB Part A is to be granted and Schoolkids Bonus (SKB) is payable for an FTB child who was previously in receipt of an income support payment (ISP) and was paid SKB for the same bonus test day:

  • Check the payment destination of the FTB claimant by selecting the FAO system type from the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen and determine who owns or has access to the bank account that the FTB payments and SKB will be paid into
  • Check the Links Summary (LS) screen for a child/parent link
  • Check the Payment Destination for the child/student who received the SKB payment

Does the FTB claimant have access to the ISP customer's account or is it a joint account with the income support customer?

  • Yes, the SKB payment is not paid to the FTB claimant. No SKB debt is raised and no update is needed on the ISP or FTB records
  • No, the SKB payment is paid to the FTB claimant and the amount that was paid to the ISP customer is raised as a debt
    • Complete the FTB new claim. SKB will be paid with the new claim
    • Investigate the ISP record to raise SKB debt

5

Payability + Read more ...

Schoolkids Bonus payments were not made to ISP customers if they:

  • received a nil rate for the instalment period that covered the bonus test day
  • were required to report for the entitlement period which covered the bonus test day and had not done so
  • were serving a waiting period or preclusion on the relevant bonus test day

If ISP was suspended and subsequently restored and the customer was eligible for payment on the bonus test day, Schoolkids Bonus was paid.

Bereavement + Read more ...

7

Income test and previous manual override + Read more ...

From 1 January 2015, an income test applied. Eligible families and young people with an adjusted taxable income of $100,000 or less for the year in which the bonus test day occurred were paid Schoolkids Bonus.

Exemptions

There were some exemptions from the income test, including students who received Schoolkids Bonus and the following payments in their own right:

  • Disability Support Pension (DSP) Blind
  • Special Benefit (SpB)
  • certain veterans’ education allowance payments

Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customers were not exempt from the income test when assessing their eligibility for Schoolkids Bonus for a child who also received SpB or DSP (Blind). For example, a DSP (Blind) parent receiving FTB Part A for a child in school is not exempt from the income test.

The income test did not apply to bonus test days in the 2013 or 2014 calendar years, including assessments made for these bonus test dates after 1 January 2015.

Actual income is more than $100,000

A manual override was coded via the Schoolkids Bonus School Leaver Details and Schoolkids Bonus Override (!EDSL) screen for ISP customers with an adjusted taxable income above $100,000 for the year in which the bonus test day occurs.

This resulted in an automatic debt being raised for a Schoolkids Bonus payment made for a bonus test day in the financial year.

The !EDSL screen has been decommissioned. The manual debt raising process now applies. See Debt Identification.

Actual income is no longer more than $100,000

If all other eligibility criteria are met, a manual payment may be made using either the One Off Payment (OOP) or Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen.

Single ISP customers

Single customers of an ISP for an entire year were not required to confirm income at the end of the financial year in the same manner as Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customers were. These cases were identified by Services Officers when, for example:

  • an ISP customer advised they became partnered before a bonus test day and their partner's adjusted taxable income, combined with their own, would preclude Schoolkids Bonus entitlement
  • an ISP customer who received Schoolkids Bonus has their ISP later cancelled due to excess income
  • a dependent YA student's parents' adjusted taxable income for the year in which the bonus test day falls is over the Schoolkids Bonus income threshold

8

Debts + Read more ...

A Schoolkids Bonus debt could arise if the customer was not entitled to the payment for any reason.

At the end of the 2014-15 or 2015-16 financial years, if the ISP customer's actual income for the relevant financial year was more than $100,000 their entitlement to Schoolkids Bonus was reassessed. A manual override was previously coded to preclude payment of Schoolkids Bonus.

Information about the customer's income may be received from them self-identifying their actual income, staff identifying the actual income or via existing compliance processes.

If an amount of Schoolkids Bonus was paid under Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for the child for a particular test day and the child then claims and is granted an ISP for the period covering the bonus test day, see below.

9

Retrospective grant of income support payment (ISP) + Read more ...

If an ISP was granted to a student and Schoolkids Bonus was payable but had already been paid for the student for the same bonus test day to their parent/guardian, the Service Officer would determine if Schoolkids Bonus should be paid to the student by checking the payment destination in the student's claim.

Did the parent/guardian have access to the account that the Schoolkids Bonus payment was being paid into?

  • Yes, the Schoolkids Bonus payment was not paid to the student. No Schoolkids Bonus debt was raised on the parent/guardian
  • No, the Schoolkids Bonus payment was paid to the student and the Schoolkids Bonus amount that was paid to the parent/guardian was raised as a debt

10

MFU processing - manual payment + Read more ...

If Schoolkids Bonus eligibility is identified by the system for an ISP customer, a Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity is created and can be identified by the keyword SKBISP.
