Reviews and reassessments of hardship assistance – home care

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Hardship review work item received in Customer First + Read more ...

Why is hardship being reviewed?

  • Scheduled review of income and assets, go to Step 2
  • Review of original decision to grant hardship, go to Step 3
  • Review of original decision to reject hardship, new assessment required, see Aged care financial hardship assistance - assessment
  • Change in circumstances, go to Step 4
  • Care recipient has changed provider during hardship period, go to Step 7

2

Review of income and assets + Read more ...

Review income and assets currently recorded and confirm they are still below thresholds for financial hardship (the threshold limits are available on the Department of Health website - Schedule of Fees and Charges for Residential and Home Care). If details require updating, see Aged care reviews – reassessments and updates.

Has this care recipient had a change in circumstances and a new hardship calculation is required?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • Record outcome using Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Aged Care > Hardship > Fin Hardship REVIEW
    • Set a new 12 months review if required
    • Procedure ends here

3

Review of decision for hardship assessment + Read more ...

To correct details of an existing hardship assessment use the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS):

  • Navigate to the Hardship & Income Suspension tab
  • Select Edit List in the relevant hardship record (Maintain Hardship BDF or Maintain Hardship ITF)
  • Select the Correction button and update the Start Date for each fee type if required
  • In the Hardship Amount field, if the hardship type is:
    • BDF/ITF as a partial supplement/reduction, key the hardship assistance amount, where applicable
    • BDF to $0, do not key an amount. The system sets the BDF to $0 automatically
  • If the hardship assistance grant reduces the ITCF to less than $1, ACMPS automatically sets the ITCF to $0
  • Add notes in the Hardship Comments field
  • The assessment creation date and time displays under Hardship Assessment
  • Key the End Date field with the hardship assistance end date
  • Add notes under Hardship Comments
  • Select Save

Once all coding is complete, go to Step 6

4

Change of circumstances – income and/or assets + Read more ...

Use the Hardship Calculator in Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS). Calculate using new income and assets and with existing essential expenses. For assistance, see Add income and expenses into ACMPS calculator.

Based on the new assessment, is the care recipient still eligible for hardship assistance?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, see Table 3 to cease or revoke hardship assistance

5

Reassessment of home care hardship + Read more ...

Reassessment of hardship assistance for home care may result in a new amount taking effect from a later date. If hardship is being reviewed based on a change of circumstances (verification provided), use the change of circumstances date.

In ACMPS, go to existing BDF and/or ITCF hardship details:

  • Select Edit List in the relevant hardship record (Maintain Hardship BDF or Maintain Hardship ITF)
    • If both fee types are recorded, end date each fee type separately
  • Select the Correction button and End date the record 1 day before the start date of the reassessment
  • Add notes in the Hardship Comments field
  • Select Save

To code reassessment in ACMPS:

  • Go to the Hardship & Income Suspension tab
  • Select Edit List in the Maintain Hardship BDF and/or Maintain Hardship ITF field
  • Select Insert to create a new record line
  • In the Hardship Type field, select from the dropdown menu the applicable fee reduction option:
    • BDF as a partial supplement or BDF to $0
    • ITF as a partial reduction or ITF to $0
  • In the Hardship Amount field, if the hardship type is:
    • BDF/ITF as a partial supplement/reduction, key the hardship assistance amount
    • BDF to $0, do not key an amount. The system sets the BDF to $0 automatically
    • If the hardship assistance grant reduces the ITCF to less than $1, ACMPS automatically sets the ITCF to $0
  • Add notes in the Hardship Comments field
    • The assessment creation date and time displays under Hardship Assessment
  • Key the Start Date field with the hardship assistance start date
  • Key the End Date field with the hardship assistance end date
  • Select Save to apply the hardship assistance

Once all coding completed, go to Step 6.

6

Completing reassessment of hardship + Read more ...

  • Create a manual letter using the Hardship Review templates located in Operational Blueprint Letters and Electronic Messaging
  • Upload letters to Customer First via Document Tools in PDF format
  • Create a Fast Note in Customer First with a summary of the decision and details of letters sent
  • In ACMPS, go to Notes and add Fast Note text
  • Complete any outstanding reviews, see SME explanations, ARO referrals and implementing ARO decisions
  • Procedure ends here

7

Care recipient changes provider during hardship period

+ Read more ...

When a care recipient changes provider during a period of hardship assistance being granted, they may request a letter is issued to their new provider with hardship assessment.

  • Create a manual letter using the aged care Hardship Grant templates located to the provider using in Operational Blueprint Letters and Electronic Messaging
  • Scan and upload the letter to Customer First as a Portable Document Format (PDF):
    • The letter shows in the Document Tools tab, see Attaching electronic documents to a Centrelink customer's record using Document Tools in Customer First
  • The letter advises:
    • the fee amounts payable. For example, the maximum BDF payable is $10.75 and the care recipient needs to pay $1.75
  • Complete details using start date of care with new provider as start date
  • Complete any outstanding work items and create a Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Aged Care > Hardship > Hardship Review to record action taken

Review of an asset that cannot be sold or borrowed against (unreasonable to sell) + Read more ...

A review of an asset that cannot be sold or borrowed against will check if a property has been sold and will not confirm details with third parties. To check if there is a record of a property being sold check an approved property website - see the Resources page for a list of websites. Paid reports must not be requested from property information websites.

Search for address of property to see if it is currently being actively marketed. If the property is currently on the market, create a review due in 6 months to review status of property again.

3

Review of income and assets + Read more ...

Review income and assets currently recorded and confirm they are still below thresholds for financial hardship. Take into account the new start date if required.

Has this care recipient had a change in circumstances and a new hardship calculation is required?

  • Yes,
    • go to Step 4
  • No,
    • Create a Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Aged Care > Hardship > Hardship Review
    • Set a new 6 or 12 month review if required
    • Procedure ends here

4

Change of circumstances – income and/or assets + Read more ...

A hardship assessment may be subject to a review of decision and this may lead to a change:

  • from the start date which leads to a correction of original coding
  • from a date after the start date after change in circumstances
  • in the hardship reduction rate that has been calculated for a period

Use the Hardship Calculator in Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS). Calculate using new income and assets and with existing essential expenses. For assistance, see Add income and expenses into ACMPS calculator.

Based on the new assessment, is the care recipient still eligible for hardship?

  • Yes,
    • To update the original assessment, go to Step 5
    • For a change in circumstances which changes hardship reduction AFTER the start date go to Step 6
  • No, see Table 3 to cease or revoke hardship assistance

5

Change to original residential or respite care hardship grant + Read more ...

Updating any field in a current hardship assessment will replace any existing coding. If hardship is being updated to apply to a new fee type, existing coding will need to be deleted and new hardship registered.

To update a hardship assessment, in ACSP:

  • Go to Hardship and income suspension > Hardship summary:
    • Existing hardship will appear in Current and future hardship
    • To update current hardship click on hyperlink under Status field
    • Current hardship assessment will be displayed
    • Hardship type: the fee type is not able to be corrected
    • Click on Update to make screen fields ready for update
    • Start date: change field to new start date if required
    • End date: correct to new date if required
    • Maximum hardship reduction: update if required, ‘Yes’ for BDF and accommodation costs only
    • Care recipient contribution: update this field for all fee types
    • Fee reduction amount: update for all fee types except BDF
    • Reason: Review of decision
    • Additional notes: details that have been changed and reason for update, include full daily accommodation payment (DAP) amount in notes if RAD/DAP hardship coded
    • Click Submit

Go to Step 7.

6

Change in circumstamces changes hardship after start date for respite and residential care + Read more ...

When a care recipient has had a change in circumstances and there is a new hardship reduction from a date after the start date:

  • the original coding will need to be end-dated, and
  • a new hardship registered

In ACSP:

  • locate the care recipient record
  • go to Fees and charges > Fees and charges details to confirm fees
  • go to Hardship and income suspension > Hardship summary:
    • Existing hardship will appear in Current and future hardship
    • Click on Update to make screen fields ready for update
    • End date: correct to day prior to review date
    • Reason: Review of decision
    • Additional notes: details that have been changed and reason for ending hardship assessment
    • Click Submit
  • To code the new assessment from the review date, from Hardship summary:
    • Click on Register hardship
    • Hardship type: add relevant fee
    • Start date: date that new assessment commences
    • End date: update end date
    • Maximum hardship reduction: ‘Yes’ or ‘No’ (MTCF and MTC will be No)
    • Care recipient contribution: update this field for all fee types
    • Fee reduction amount: to be completed for all fee types except BDF
    • Reason: select most relevant change in circumstances
    • Additional notes: reason for new start date and assessment stating what has changed
    • Click Submit
  • Hardship summary will display details under Current and future hardship

Go to Step 7.

7

Reviewing current hardship coding for Post 1 July 2014 RAD/DAP Hardship + Read more ...

For RAD/DAP hardship:

  • the care recipient contribution coded and will remain constant for the period of hardship
  • the fee reduction amount will not be displayed
  • the service will be paid a hardship accommodation supplement from 1 August 2022
  • this supplement will the maximum accommodation supplement payable for the service less any care recipient contribution
  • any RAD/DAP hardship granted for a period prior to 1 August 2022 will be paid as a manual adjustment by Aged Care Allied Health (ACAH)

In ACSP, go to Hardship and income suspension > Hardship summary and review current hardship coding.

Is there correction or change to RAD/DAP hardship required from a date prior to 1 August 2022?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Correcting a previous RAP/DAP hardship for period prior to 1 August 2022 - manual adjustment + Read more ...

Any RAD/DAP hardship granted for a period prior to 1 August 2022 will be paid as a manual adjustment by Aged Care Allied Health (ACAH). Any subsequent review or correction to RAD/DAP hardship for period prior to 1 August 2022 will also need to be completed as a manual adjustment. For any period from 1 August 2022, hardship supplements will be adjusted automatically.

Example: Care recipient granted RAD/DAP hardship from 1 March 2022

  • RAD/DAP hardship is coded in ACSP from 1 March 2022
  • Supplement is paid manually to the service for period 1 March 2022 to 31 July 2022
  • Supplements will be paid automatically from 1 August 2022
  • A review of hardship changes the RAD/DAP hardship reduction amount
  • Details from 1 March 2022 to 31 July 2022 will need to be referred to ACAH for a manual adjustment to be paid
  • ACSP updated coding will then adjust any RAD/DAP hardship from 1 August 2022 onwards

Open the AC RAD DAP manual review tracker and complete the fields:

  • Logon
  • CRN
  • Care recipient ID – ACMPS ID
  • SPARC ID
  • RACS
  • Care recipient full name
  • Start Date: this is the date from the earlier RAD/DAP hardship is being changed from. If RAD/DAP hardship is being revoked, this will be the original start date for hardship
  • End Date: this will be the end date up to which the earlier RAD/DAP hardship is being changed – latest date will be 31 July 2022
  • Old fee reduction amount: original fee reduction amount that was calculated/advised
  • New fee reduction amount: if hardship has been ceased or revoked, this will be $0.00
  • Comments: add comments stating why RAD/DAP hardship has been changed. For example, review of decision, cessation, revocation, early lodgement for continuation of hardship

9

Completing reassessment of hardship + Read more ...

  • Create a manual letter using the Hardship Review templates located in Operational Blueprint Letters and Electronic Messaging
  • Upload letters to Customer First via Document Tools in PDF format
  • Create a Fast Note using Aged Care>Hardship>Hardship Review in Customer First with a summary of the decision and details of letters sent

    • Click on Add note

    • In Note field, add a short summary of the decision outcome. Include all fees that have reduced, amounts, period of reduction (start and end dates), manual letters have been sent, Service Officer's user name and logon
    • Click Submit
  • Complete any outstanding reviews, see SME quality checks, ARO referrals and implementing ARO decisions
  • Procedure ends here

10

Care recipient changes provider during hardship period + Read more ...

When a care recipient changes provider during a period of hardship assistance being granted, they will need to provide a copy of their aged care accommodation agreement. This will confirm their accommodation costs for the new provider. Issue an RFI to request this if not already provided.

  • Apply new fees and charges to existing hardship calculations and complete new assessment. See the Recording a hardship assistance outcome - residential and respite care table in Aged care financial hardship assistance - assessment
  • Procedure ends here

Note: unless a new means assessment (RCA/NCL) has included updates, request this information in the review.

Cease or revoke hardship assistance

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Care recipient no longer eligible for hardship assistance + Read more ...

A reassessment of income and/or assets may find a care recipient ineligible for hardship from:

  • the initial start date for hardship (revoke), or
  • a later date (cease)

If the review backdates to the start date of a hardship claim, the hardship assistance is revoked. A notice is issued to the care recipient, nominee and service provider which advises that hardship is being revoked and gives them 28 days to make a submission regarding this decision.

What is the action required?

  • To cease hardship assistance after a change in circumstances, go to Step 2
  • To issue a notice that hardship may be revoked, go to Step 6
  • To revoke hardship assistance for home care, go to Step 7
  • To revoke hardship assistance for respite and residential care, go to Step 8

2

Cease Hardship assistance – date of event + Read more ...

Cease hardship assistance if a change in circumstances results in no entitlement to hardship assistance including:

  • Changes to income and/or assets result in care recipient being over hardship thresholds
  • Sale of a property that had been assessed as an asset that cannot be sold or borrowed against

Date of event

The date of the change in circumstances will be the date of event for ceasing hardship assistance. For example the settlement date for a property sale. If a care recipient has departed care, the date of event is the day prior to departure.

What care type is hardship assistance being ended for?

  • Home care, go to Step 3
  • Respite or residential care, go to Step 4

3

Cease hardship assistance coding – home care + Read more ...

To end current hardship assistance for home care:

  • In the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS):
    • Navigate to the Hardship & Income Suspension tab
    • Select Edit List in the relevant hardship record (Maintain Hardship BDF or Maintain Hardship ITF)
    • Select the Correction button and update the End Date to cease the hardship supplement for each fee type
    • Add notes under Hardship Comments
    • Select Save

Go to Step 5.

4

Cease Hardship assistance coding – respite and residential care + Read more ...

  • Go to Hardship and income suspension > Hardship summary:
    • Existing hardship will appear in Current and future hardship
    • To update current hardship click on hyperlink under Status field
    • Current hardship assessment will be displayed
    • Click on Update to make screen fields ready for update
    • End date: correct to new end date based on change in circumstances
    • Reason: select relevant reason code
    • Additional notes: brief summary of why hardship end date has changed
    • Click Submit
  • Hardship assessment will now be viewed in Historical hardship

Go to Step 5.

5

Cease hardship finalisation + Read more ...

To complete cessation of hardship assistance:

  • Create a manual letter using the Hardship Review templates located in Operational Blueprint Letters and Electronic Messaging
  • Upload letters to Customer First via Document Tools in PDF format
  • in Customer First add a Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Aged Care > Hardship > Aged Care Fin Hardship Cease/Revoke with a summary of the decision and details of letters sent
  • Home care – in ACMPS, go to Notes and add Fast Note text
  • Procedure ends here.

6

Issue notice of intention to revoke hardship assistance + Read more ...

The care recipient, nominee (if applicable) and service provider must be notified in writing that Services Australia is considering revoking the hardship assistance. All parties invited to make a written submission within 28 days. Hardship assistance cannot be revoked prior to day 29 from the date of issue of this letter.

  • Create manual letters using the Hardship Review templates located in Operational Blueprint Letters and Electronic Messaging
  • Create or annotate Fast Note and include:
    • The reason for the revocation
    • Held pending response/submission from care recipient
    • Details of letters sent
    • Act references
  • Hold Fast Note for 28 days
  • Upload the letters to Customer First via Document Tools in PDF format
  • Procedure ends here

7

Revoke hardship assistance for home care + Read more ...

Prior to actioning a revoke of hardship assistance, confirm that a notice of intention to revoke hardship was issued. Revoke of hardship assistance cannot be completed prior to day 29 from issue of this letter.

If a submission is received from the care recipient, nominee or service provider that changes the decision to revoke hardship assistance, see Table 1 for reassessment of hardship.

To revoke hardship assistance for home care:

  • Go to ACMPS:
    • Navigate to Hardship & Income Suspension under Maintain Hardship BDF and/or Maintain Hardship ITF
    • Select the Mark Delete option to delete the hardship supplement
    • Add notes under Hardship Comments
    • Select Save

Go to Step 9.

8

Revoke hardship assistance for respite and residential care + Read more ...

Prior to actioning a revoke of hardship assistance, confirm that a notice of intention to revoke hardship was issued. Revoke of hardship assistance cannot be completed prior to day 29 from issue of this letter.

If a submission is received from the care recipient, nominee or service provider that changes the decision to revoke hardship assistance, see Table 1 for reassessment of hardship.

Care recipients who still have a current RAD/DAP hardship reduction will transition into ACSP. This is so they no longer require manual payments to be made:

  • RAD/DAP hardship will be transferred into ACSP and automatic hardship payments will commence from 1 August 2022
  • If a RAD/DAP hardship is to revoked from a date prior to 1 August 2022 this will require manual action

To revoke hardship assistance for respite and residential care, in ACSP:

  • Go to Hardship and income suspension > Hardship summary:
    • Existing hardship will appear in Current and future hardship
    • To update current hardship click on hyperlink under Status field
    • Current hardship assessment will be displayed
    • Click on Delete
    • Update fields on Delete hardship screen
    • Reason: select relevant reason
    • Additional notes: brief summary of why hardship has been revoked
    • Click Submit
    • Hardship assessment will now be viewed in Historical hardship with a status of Deleted

Is there RAD/DAP hardship that is being revoked from a date prior to 1 August 2022?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

9

Correcting a previous RAD/DAP hardship that was paid manually + Read more ...

Any adjustments, including an early end date to RAD/DAP hardship that take effect prior to the transfer date need to be managed manually. This is as they were paid as manual payments.

Example: Care recipient granted RAD/DAP hardship from 1 March 2022

  • RAD/DAP hardship is coded in ACSP from 1 March 2022
  • Supplements paid manually to the service for period 1 March 2022 to 31 July 2022
  • Supplements paid automatically from 1 August 2022
  • A review of hardship changes the RAD/DAP hardship end date to 30 April 2022
  • Details from 30 April 2022 to 21 July 2022 will need to be referred to ACAH for a manual adjustment to be reviewed
  • Updating the new end date in ACSP will then adjust any RAD/DAP hardship from 1 August 2022 onwards

Open the AC RAD DAP manual review tracker spreadsheet and complete fields:

  • Logon
  • CRN
  • Care recipient ID – ACMPS ID
  • SPARC ID
  • RACS
  • Care recipient full name
  • Start Date: this is the date from the earlier RAD/DAP hardship is being changed from. If RAD/DAP hardship is being revoked. This will be the original start date for hardship
  • End Date: this will be the end date up to the earlier RAD/DAP hardship is being changed. If RAD/DAP hardship has transferred into ACSP from 1 August 2022, this will be up to latest date of 31 July 2022
  • Old fee reduction amount: original fee reduction amount that was calculated/advised
  • New fee reduction amount: if hardship has been ceased or revoked, this will be $0.00
  • Comments: Add comments stating why RAD/DAP hardship has been changed. For example, review of decision, cessation, revocation, early lodgement for continuation of hardship

Go to Step 10.

10

    • Click Submit
