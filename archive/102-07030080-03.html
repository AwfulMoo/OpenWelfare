Determining entitlement after returning to or commencing work

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Returning to or commencing work + Read more ...

If a customer contacts to advise they have commenced employment and it is not their reporting day, record a DOC with employment details. If the customer is a variable reporter, change their reporting frequency to 2 weekly. Tell them to report their employment details on their reporting day. Explain to them how to report correctly.

Obtain the following details from the customer and record in a DOC:

  • date employment commenced
  • type of employment: part-time, casual, full-time ongoing
  • employer's name and Australian Business Number (ABN)
  • employer's phone number
  • gross employment income per week/fortnight
  • period of employment, if applicable

Ensure the customers reporting frequency is 2 weekly. Tell them of any changes to their reporting requirements. For example, their next reporting date and requirement to report every 2 weeks.

Family assistance and income support reporting obligations are different:

  • Income support customers must report actual income paid, not just an estimate. Service Officers must discuss income reporting requirements at every opportunity and ensure actual income paid and family assistance income estimates are both updated when required
  • If either member of a couple is receiving family assistance, their estimate must include income support payments received, employment income and any other components of adjusted taxable income (received or expected) for the financial year for both members of the couple

2

Partner returning to or commencing work + Read more ...

If a customer contacts to advise their partner has commenced employment, see Partner commencing or returning to work.

3

Australian Apprenticeship + Read more ...

Has the customer commenced an Australian Apprenticeship?

  • Yes, consider whether the customer should apply for another payment:
    • Claiming ABSTUDY
    • Initial contact with students or Australian Apprentices claiming Youth Allowance (YA)
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Cancel or continue payments + Read more ...

If the customer:

  • does not wish to provide employment and income details, and requests immediate cancellation of payment or withdrawal of a new claim, go to Step 5
  • provides employment and income details and wants payment to continue if entitled, go to Step 6

Note: if the customer has a nominee, see Nominees.

5

Customer requests cancellation of payment + Read more ...

Explain to the customer they may still be entitled to receive income support payments.

If employment is non-ongoing, part-time or casual, customers can continue to report their income and retain entitlement to the employment income nil rate period.

If employment is full-time ongoing, customers can continue to report their income and deplete their Working Credit before payment is cancelled.

If the customer chooses to cancel their payment immediately and does not want to continue reporting or advise of their employment and income details, tell them they will lose access to their Working Credit balance and any remaining income support and concessions they may be entitled.

Does the customer wish to cancel payments immediately and declines to provide employment and income details?

  • Yes, for customers:
    • currently in receipt of payment, see Step 3 in Table 2
    • with a new claim in process, see Cancel or withdraw an online claim. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Assess continuing eligibility and entitlement to income support payments + Read more ...

For JSP or YA job seekers, determine if the customer has ongoing entitlement to payment. They must still satisfy the sufficient work test, mutual obligation requirements and continue to report on their normal reporting due date.

Most JSP and YA (job seeker) customers who have returned to full-time ongoing work will no longer be qualified for payment as they are not considered unemployed. They will be able to deplete their Working Credit balance before payment is cancelled.

However, certain job seekers in receipt of JSP or YA (job seeker) who commence full-time ongoing work may still be eligible for payment.

The most common examples of full-time workers who may qualify for JSP without further activity requirements are:

  • job seekers with a pensioner partner (the income test cut-off point for the JSP partner is higher than applies to individuals or allowance couples
  • long term single JSP customers aged 60 or over who receive Rent Assistance (RA)
  • JSP single principal carers
  • job seekers with a working credit balance in the entitlement period they are paid employment income, see Step 1 in Table 2

The Resources page contains a link to a matrix, which has more details to assist with determining the appropriate action in various situations.

See Unemployed eligibility for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance (job seeker and Paid work as an approved activity.

If the job seeker:

  • does not have continuing entitlement and payment has not been cancelled, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • does not have continuing entitlement and payment has been cancelled under reason 'FTE' (Full time employment, paid income not advised), go to Step 7
  • does have continuing entitlement, including where payment has been cancelled under reason 'FTE' (Full time employment, paid income not advised), go to Step 8

7

Non-ongoing entitlement to JSP and YA (job seeker), and cancelled under reason 'FTE' + Read more ...

When a provider advises a job seeker has been placed in full-time ongoing employment, an electronic transaction will be sent via the Department of Employment system and Workplace Relations to Centrelink, which updates the job seeker's record.

The transaction will automatically update the Employment Status with code 'FTO' code on the Customer Employment Status (EAES) screen.

When this occurs, employment income must be recorded as full-time ongoing so the customer can access their Working Credit balance. After the depletion of any working credits, payment will be auto cancelled under reason 'FWC' Full-time Employment - Working Credit balance is zero.

If there is no ongoing income recorded, the job seeker's payment will be auto cancelled under reason 'FTE' from the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) of the fortnight in which they commenced full time employment. The job seeker is not able to utilise their Working Credit balance unless employment income is reported and payment is restored.

If it is confirmed the job seeker has commenced full-time ongoing employment, to restore payment and ensure Working Credit provisions are applied correctly:

  • restore the payment (if appropriate) via the Benefit Action (BA) screen. Before finalising, code employment income from the date the job seeker is first paid and then finalise the activity
  • record details on a DOC

If the job seeker disputes they have not commenced work or the work is non-ongoing (i.e. casual or only part-time), see Workforce Australia/Community Development Program (CDP) registration inactivated and cancellation of income support in error.

Procedure ends here.

8

Ongoing entitlement for payment, including where payment cancelled under reason 'FTE' + Read more ...

If the customers payment has been cancelled, restore payment only after determining they meet the sufficient work test and mutual obligation requirements, and can still be considered unemployed.

For customers with casual or part-time employment:

  • Code a new entry of 'NFT' (Not full-time ongoing employment) with DOV the day after coding 'FTO'. Do not delete the 'FTO' details from the EAES screen. An activity will be sent to the provider advising of a change in employment status for the customer
  • Process outstanding reports. Assess all employment income paid during the entitlement period as IOP or LOP. See Recording and correcting employment income details
  • If payment is cancelled 'FTE', restore the payment via the Benefit Action (BA) screen and finalise the activity
  • Check the customer's Job Seeker Registration is reactivated
  • If the customer will be meeting their mutual obligation requirements, record the employment as an approved activity on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen and enter into a Job Plan if appropriate. See Paid work as an approved mutual obligation requirement. The customer should then be 'suspended' from participation in employment services and will be Centrelink managed
  • Tell the customer to continue reporting and include any employment income paid on their normal reporting day. If income is above the relevant income limit, the nil rate period process may apply. See Employment income nil rate period
  • Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

For customers with full-time ongoing employment who still qualify for payment (as determined at Step 6)

  • Code a new entry of 'NFT' (Not full-time ongoing employment) with DOV as the day after coding 'FTO'. Do not delete the 'FTO' details from the EAES screen. An activity will be sent to the provider advising of a change in employment status for the customer
  • Process outstanding reports. Assess all employment income paid during the entitlement period as IOP or LOP. See Recording and correcting employment income details
  • If payment is cancelled 'FTE', restore the payment via the Benefit Action (BA) screen and finalise the activity
  • Check the customer's Job Seeker Registration is reactivated
  • If appropriate, record the employment as an approved activity on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen and enter into a Job Plan. See Paid work as an approved mutual obligation requirement. The customer should then be 'suspended' from participation in employment services and will be Centrelink managed
  • Tell the customer to continue reporting and include any employment income paid on their normal reporting day. If income is above the relevant income test, the nil rate period process may apply. See Employment income nil rate period
  • Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

Note: this procedure does not affect a customer's right to cancel their entitlement to an Allowance voluntarily.

Unfavourable decision, change to reporting or cancellation

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

Customer does not have an ongoing entitlement or has Working Credit to deplete + Read more ...

For customers with full-time ongoing employment, code the employment income, via the Earnings and reporting workflow tool, with the following:

  • Date of Event - the date the customer was paid by their employer. If the customer has worked but not yet been paid, the Date of Event will be the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) to code hours only
  • Amount - the gross amount paid
  • Frequency field - code as the employer pay period frequency
    • Weekly (1WE) - where the customer is paid the same amount, on the same day every week
    • Fortnightly (2WE) - where the customer is paid the same amount, on the same day every fortnight
    • Income for one period (IOP) - where income can vary or first employment income amount is for less than the full entitlement period. If this occurs, code the first EPED as IOP and the following EPED as the relevant ongoing frequency
    • Long Period/Lump sum (LOP) - where income is for more than the entitlement period. Start and End dates will be required.
    • If coding hours only, select HOP to code hours worked in the entitlement period
  • Empl Sts (Employment Status) field - as 'FTO' (full-time ongoing). This can only be coded with a 1WE or 2WE frequency. Leave blank for IOP or LOP frequency
  • Hrs PF field - number of hours worked. This can be coded with 1WE or 2WE frequency. If coding income using IOP or LOP, leave blank and use frequency HOP to code hours. Ensure Empl Sts field is coded as 'FTO' (full-time ongoing). See Recording and correcting employment income details for more information
  • Income support payments will cancel automatically when the customer's Working Credit has depleted
    • If employment income precluded payment and JSP/YAL (job seeker) does not cancel automatically, go to Step 3

Note: Check eligibility for Special Employment Advance (SEA) and offer to customer before payment ceases. Applications for Special Employment Advance (SEA) can be:

  • taken at a service centre, or
  • uploaded through the Express Plus Centrelink mobile

When making an unfavourable decision, the Service Officer must:

  • explain the decision to the customer and give them an opportunity to provide further details or evidence relevant to the decision
  • tell them about their review and appeal rights, and
  • if needed, record a request for an explanation or review of the decision

This includes unfavourable decisions made by Service Officers within a Smart Centre.

If a Service Officer is not speaking with a customer at the time of making a decision, they must make 2 genuine attempts to contact a customer before finalising an unfavourable decision.

2

Update the customer's reporting frequency + Read more ...

  • If the customer is on variable reporting ensure their reporting frequency has been updated to 2 weekly. See Reporting requirements for job seekers
  • If the customer is not manually placed on reporting, the Service Profiler will automatically update the customer's reporting frequency. This will occur for customers who have had one or more instances of their and/or partner employment income occurrences greater than the customer's income free area in the last 12 weeks

For more information on updating reporting frequency, see Reporting overview.

3

Cancellation of payment + Read more ...

  • If the customer is a job seeker who declines to advise employment and/or income details and requests cancellation of payment immediately:
    • for customers on variable reporting, the customer will need to be placed on 2 weekly reporting first to ensure payment cancels from the correct Date of Event (DOV)
    • for customers commencing full-time ongoing employment, code the EAES screen with 'FTO' from the date they commenced work. This will cancel payment under reason 'FTE' from date paid to plus 1 (DPT +1)
    • for customers commencing full-time non-ongoing, part-time or casual work, see Cancellation of JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) at customer's request. If the customer requests cancellation of their payment and does not want to report with employment and/or income details, the customer
      will be unable to deplete any Working Credit and/or
      any entitlement to 6 fortnights at nil rate will be forfeited
  • If the customer does not report, the payment will cancel 'DNL' 14 days after the due to report date:
    • the customer will be unable to deplete any Working Credit
    • any entitlement to 6 fortnights at nil payment will be forfeited
  • If a customer is on variable reporting and does not intend to report:
    • payment cancels from date paid to plus 1 of the last Reporting Statement lodged
    • if the customer has received any payments after that date the system will treat these as overpayments and create a debt

Note: if automatic cancellation, i.e. FTO, but payment has not cancelled automatically as expected, follow normal escalation procedures.

Check eligibility for Special Employment Advance (SEA) and offer to customer before payment ceases. Applications for Special Employment Advance (SEA) can be:

  • taken at a service centre, or
  • uploaded through the Express Plus Centrelink mobile

Do not cancel the payment if the customer wishes to apply for SEA. Advise customer to complete SEA application (SU514) and upload through the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app.

4

Other considerations + Read more ...

  • If the customer's payment is being cancelled and their circumstances later change, they may have to lodge a new claim. JSP/YA job seeker customers can lodge the claim online or staff can complete the Assisted Customer Claim workflow
  • If the customer has a partner on income support, reassess the partner's entitlement and advise the effect of the customer's income on their partner's continuing entitlement
  • Advise customer of possible entitlement to a Health Care Card (HCC) or Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) once payments cease
  • Parenting Payment will suspend after cancellation of their partner's payment
  • If applicable, request and update the estimate of annual income for Family Tax Benefit. Customers can give a verbal estimate. Note: if either member of a couple is receiving family assistance, their estimate must include income support payments received, employment income and any other components of adjusted taxable income (received or expected) for the financial year for both members of the couple
  • Where the customer has a nominee arrangement in place (voluntary or involuntary), contact with the nominee may be required before taking action requested by the customer. For more information, see Nominees
