Receive and review an application for registration of an overseas maintenance liability

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Application received + Read more ...

This step is performed by the Scanning and Digitalisation Team.

Child Support may receive an application:

  • directly from a payee
  • directly from a payer
  • from a payee or payer’s representative
  • from an overseas authority on behalf of a payee or payer

If the application has been sent by a payee, a payer or a representative of a payee or payer, the applicant should be recorded in Cuba as the payee or the payer.

If the application has been sent by an overseas authority, the applicant should be recorded in Cuba as the overseas authority. If unsure that the application has been sent by an overseas authority, refer to the Central Authority Address Listing for Child Support International customers.

Check carefully if it appears a representative or an overseas authority has sent the application – some applications from overseas third parties acting as a representative may incorrectly appear to be from an overseas authority.

Review the application to determine if the applicant should be recorded in Cuba as the:

  • payee
  • payer, or
  • overseas authority acting on behalf of the payee

If the applicant is the payee or an overseas authority, check the address recorded for the sending of payee correspondence and/or cheques. If the correspondence and/or cheques should be sent to:

  • the Central Authority - the application is being made on behalf of the payee
  • the payee, the application is being made by:
    • the payee, or
    • the payee and has been forwarded by the Central Authority

If the application is written in a language other than English, check if the application has been translated.

If the application has not been translated, request a translation of the document. For more information, see Interpreter Services.

2

Create payee or payer + Read more ...

This step is performed by the Scanning and Digitalisation Team.

Before creating a record for the applicant, search Cuba for any existing records for the customer using:

  • any case number provided by the customer
  • any reference numbers (TFN, CRN, or CSRN) provided by the customer. Note: TFN can be located from a search of AIS or ICP using the customers details
  • the customer's current and previous names (including maiden names) but not middle names
  • the customer's first name and a surname of unknown (this may identify customers who were children in a previous case)
  • the customer’s date of birth

If the applicant is already recorded on Cuba, do not create a new record. Update any details that have changed. See Customer record Cuba Process Help.

If the applicant is not recorded on Cuba create a new customer record. See Customer record Cuba Process Help.

If the Overseas Authority is the applicant, record the Overseas Authority address as payee’s postal address. If the address cannot be located on the application refer to the Central Authorities address details for the address.

Record the details of the application in the Communication window. Create a Court Order Received/AGR Received intray and route to:

Region: INTERNATIONAL

Office: INTERNATIONAL HOBART

Stream/Team: NO S001 OVERSEAS TM

Position: A OVERSEAS COURT ORDERS

3

Review application + Read more ...

An application for registration of an overseas court order can be made by:

  • the payee or payer via the overseas authority of the reciprocating jurisdiction in which they reside
  • the payee residing in a reciprocating jurisdiction: if the order was made by, or is registered in, a court of the reciprocating jurisdiction in which they reside
  • the authorised agent for the payee: if the order was made by, or is registered in, a court of the reciprocating jurisdiction in which the payee resides
  • the overseas authority of a jurisdiction on behalf of the payee (agency reimbursement)
  • a payee who is a resident of Australia
  • the payer
  • the authorised agent for the payer

An application for registration of other types of overseas liabilities, for example administrative assessments or agreements registered with a court or administrative authority, can be made by:

  • the payee or the payer or the authorised agent of the payee or payer via the overseas authority of the reciprocating jurisdiction in which the payee or payer resides
  • the overseas authority of a jurisdiction on behalf of the payee (agency reimbursement)
  • a payee who is a resident of Australia
  • the payer
  • the authorised agent for the payer

If the application has not been sent by the payee or payer that is it has been sent by a third party, determine if the third party is:

  • an overseas authority
  • an authorised agent
  • a representative - with ordinary authority or unauthorised

Refer to the Central Authority Address Listing for Child Support International customers to confirm that an organisation is the designated overseas authority for the jurisdiction in which the payee or payer resides. Some overseas organisations such a legal or community organisations may appear to be an overseas authority, when in fact they are not.

If the representative is an authorised agent or has ordinary authority, record the representative details in Cuba. See Child Support authorised agent and Child Support representative with ordinary authority.

An authorised agent must provide a written document to Child Support conferring a general power of attorney to act on behalf of a payee or payer, or a power of attorney to act on behalf of a payee or payer for child support purposes.

Other representatives, whether or not authorised, cannot make an application for registration of an overseas court order on behalf of a payee or payer. However, a representative with ordinary authority can forward an application made by the payee or payer. If an application has been made by a representative, issue the payee or payer a unique letter:

  • advising that their representative cannot make an application to register an overseas maintenance liability on their behalf
  • if writing to a payer, advising they may make their own application which either they or their authorised representative may send to Child Support or via their overseas authority
  • if writing to a payee and the overseas liability is a court order, advising they may make their own application which either they or their authorised representative may send to Child Support or via their overseas authority
  • if writing to a payee and the overseas liability is an agreement or an assessment, advising they may make their own application which must be sent to Child Support via their overseas authority
  • include objection rights

Procedure ends here.

Identify the relevant type of application in the steps below.

References has a link to the Child Support Guide 3.6.2: Registering overseas maintenance liabilities for collection.

Note: if the application is written in a language other than English, check if the application has been translated. If the application has not been translated, request a translation of the document, for more information, see Interpreter Services.

4

Made by payee who is a resident of a reciprocating jurisdiction or by their authorised agent – liability not a court order + Read more ...

The application must be given to Child Support by an overseas authority, if:

  • the payee is a resident of a reciprocating jurisdiction, and
  • the overseas liability is not a court order, that is it is an agreement or an assessment

If an application for an overseas liability that is not a court order is sent directly from the payee or their authorised agent to Child Support:

  • contact the payee or the authorised agent to advise that the application cannot be accepted and they should contact the Overseas Authority in their jurisdiction. Check the Central Authority address listing, if the contact details for the Central Authority are not available, seek help from a Service Support Officer (SSO)
  • issue a unique letter to the payee or the authorised agent advising that their application has been refused because the application was not sent via or by the overseas maintenance authority in the jurisdiction in which they reside. Include objection rights

See Contact with Child Support customers.

Record any discussions with the payee in the Communication window. See Documentation Cuba Process Help.

Note: if the payee is a resident of a reciprocating jurisdiction and the payer is a resident of Australia the payee may make an application for an Australian Assessment. For more information see Applications for a child support assessment.

5

Made by payee who is a resident of a reciprocating jurisdiction or by their authorised agent - liability is a court order + Read more ...

A payee who is resident of a reciprocating jurisdiction or their authorised agent can make an application directly to Child Support if the overseas liability is a court order made by, or registered in, a court of the reciprocating jurisdiction in which they reside.

If the payee is not a resident of the reciprocating jurisdiction in which the overseas court order was made and it has not been registered in a court of the payee’s current reciprocating jurisdiction:

  • contact the payee or their authorised agent to advise why their application cannot be accepted and that they should contact their Overseas Authority. Check the Central Authority address listing, if the contact details for the Central Authority are not available, seek help from a Service Support Officer (SSO)
  • issue a unique letter to the payee or their authorised agent advising that their application has been refused because the order is not registered in a court in their jurisdiction and that unless it is, the application must be sent via or by the overseas maintenance authority in the jurisdiction in which they reside. Include objection rights

See Contact with Child Support customers.

Record any discussions with the payee in the Communication window. See Documentation Cuba Process Help.

If the application can be accepted, check the court order to ensure it is:

  • still valid and enforceable
  • the most recent order

Contact both parents as part of the pre-registration interview.

If 1 parent has not been contacted or is unable to confirm the information requested, write to the Overseas Authority. Issue the UQ40.00 - Requesting information about a Court Order - Central Authority letter.

See Contact with Child Support customers.

6

Made by overseas authority of a jurisdiction on behalf of the payee + Read more ...

Overseas authorities can make an application for an agency reimbursement liability. In general the application will be considered to be made on behalf of the payee if the payments are to be sent directly to the overseas authority.

Agency reimbursement – a small number of these cases, excluding New Zealand, are agency reimbursement, that is an amount that an overseas authority has paid to the payee for which it seeks reimbursement from the other parent.

7

Made by payee in Australia + Read more ...

The payee can make an application directly to Child Support if they are a resident of Australia.

If the application is for the registration of an order or agreement ensure it is:

  • still valid and enforceable, and
  • the most recent order or agreement

Contact both parents as part of the pre-registration interview. If 1 parent has not been contacted or is unable to confirm the information requested, write to the Overseas Authority. Issue the UQ40.00 - Requesting information about a Court Order - Central Authority letter.

See Contact with Child Support customers.

8

Made by payer or their authorised agent + Read more ...

A payer or their authorised agent can make an application directly to Child Support whether they are a resident of Australia or a reciprocating jurisdiction.

Where the payer is a resident of a reciprocating jurisdiction they can also make an application via the Overseas/Central Authority of the country in which they live.

9

Made by payee who is resident in a non-reciprocating jurisdiction or by their authorised agent + Read more ...

If an application to register an overseas maintenance liability is made by a payee who is a resident of a non-reciprocating jurisdiction or by their authorised agent:

  • reject the application and
  • issue a unique letter to the payee

Note: if the payee is a resident of a non-reciprocating jurisdiction they can make an application for an administrative assessment directly to Child Support if all eligibility requirements are met. The application does not need to come through an overseas authority. If the applicant payee wants to apply for an administrative assessment, see Applications for a child support assessment.

Assessing an application for registration of an overseas maintenance liability (excluding NZ)

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Create customer record on Cuba + Read more ...

Before creating a record for the other party, search Cuba for any existing records using:

  • any case number provided by the customer
  • any reference numbers (TFN, CRN, or CSRN) provided by the customer. TFN's can be located from a search of AIS or ICP using the customer's details
  • the customer's current and previous names (including maiden names) but not middle names
  • the customer's first name and a surname of unknown (this may identify customers who were children in a previous case)
  • the customer’s date of birth

Note: if the customer does not have an existing record in Cuba to request data from the ATO, the initial search must be conducted through Cuba. See Legal notice for Child Support access to Australian Taxation Office (ATO) data and systems.

If the other party is recorded on Cuba do not create a new customer record. Update any details that have changed. See Customer record Cuba Process Help.

If the other party is not recorded on Cuba create a new customer record.

Lock the payer and payee. See Transfer or lock a customer Cuba Process Help.

2

Residency decision + Read more ...

A residency decision must be made for the payer and payee, see Residency decisions for Child Support Customers.

If neither the payee nor the payer are residents of Australia, refuse the application.

3

Create child record + Read more ...

If the children to the case are not already recorded in Cuba create a new record for each relevant child. See Customer record Cuba Process Help.

For spousal-only maintenance orders do not create records for children. Cuba will automatically add the payee to the case as a child.

Note: children’s records created pre-Cuba may have the payee’s or payer’s surname, or they could have ‘unknown’ as their surname. If an existing record is located for either payer or payee, use all options to search for the children.

4

Scrutiny + Read more ...

If the application is already recorded on Cuba, issue a unique letter to the customer advising a previous application has been registered.

Review the application to determine if the child or spouse maintenance liability is a registrable overseas maintenance liability. To be registrable, the liability must be:

  • a maintenance order for a periodic amount made by a judicial authority of a reciprocating jurisdiction
  • a maintenance agreement for a periodic amount registered by a judicial or administrative authority of a reciprocating jurisdiction
  • a maintenance assessment for a periodic amount issued by an administrative authority of a reciprocating jurisdiction
  • arrears of periodic amounts in relation to any of the above liabilities
  • an Agency Reimbursement Liability (an amount that has been paid to a payee of a child maintenance liability by an overseas authority for which the overseas authority seeks reimbursement from the payer)

For more information on the types of liabilities that can be registered with Child Support, References has a link to the Child Support Guide 3.6.1: Registrable overseas maintenance liabilities.

Enforcement orders are not registrable

Some jurisdictions, particularly the U.K., make enforcement orders after they have registered an Australian assessment. Enforcement orders enable the court to take action to collect the registered Australian liability if the paying parent refuses to pay. Enforcement orders are not maintenance orders and are therefore not registrable.

An enforcement order will usually clearly state that it is an order for enforcement (rather than a maintenance order). The enforcement order may be for a different amount than the Australian assessment as the overseas court may make its own assessment of the paying parent’s capacity to pay the registered Australian assessment.

If it is not clear whether the order is a maintenance order or an enforcement order, seek help from an SSO, see Technical support Cuba Process Help.

Overseas court order made in response to a transmitted Australian assessment

The order may have been made in response to a transmitted Australian assessment. The domestic laws of some reciprocating jurisdictions require the transformation of Australian assessments into judicial maintenance liabilities (court orders) to enable that jurisdiction to register and enforce it. These orders are different to overseas enforcement orders and can be registered if they are registrable overseas maintenance liabilities.

The court in the reciprocating jurisdiction may set a level of maintenance that differs from the Australian assessment.

If the overseas order is registered, the Australian assessment will end from the day before the court order is registered. Any arrears that have accrued under the assessment up to the date that the assessment ends, will remain payable.

Liability is registrable

If the order, agreement or assessment is a registrable overseas maintenance liability, check the case to determine if there is a registered maintenance liability in place.

The maintenance liability in the new application cannot be registered if there is a current liability registered:

  • that relates to the same payer, payee and child/ren, and
  • the maintenance liability in the new application was made prior to that Child Support has registered

This is because Child Support only became aware of it after the current liability was registered. However, the arrears that arose under the earlier liability can be registered.

Liability is not registrable

It the order, agreement or assessment is not a registrable overseas maintenance liability, go to Step 8.

5

Court orders + Read more ...

A court order may be:

  • a final court order, including an order made in response to a transmitted Australian assessment to enable registration
  • a provisional order

Final court order

Ensure the document is a final court order. A final order will generally have:

  • a heading of 'Order' or 'Court Order', and
  • a court seal, and may include phrases such as
    • it is ordered, or
    • it is ordered by consent

If an incomplete or unsealed court order has been received, contact the Overseas Authority to advise the application cannot be actioned until a complete and sealed copy of the order is received.

Issue a unique letter to the Overseas Authority. Create an Instakeholder Follow Up intray to monitor whether a response is received. See Intray management Cuba Process Help.

Ensure the court order is the most current order:

  • a court order sent from an overseas authority is assumed to be the most current order
  • if the Court Order is sent directly to Child Support by the payer or payee confirm with both parties during the pre-registration interview that it is the most current order
  • if unable to contact either or both parties issue:
    • UQ40.01 - Advising request sent to CA for information about court order - Applicant, and
    • UQ40.06 - Request for information - Application received to register and enforce court order in Australia - Non-Applicant letter, and
    • UQ40.00 - Requesting information about a Court Order - Central Authority letter.
      Note: only issue the letter to the overseas authority if not already done, see Step 6 in the Application received table - Made by payee in Australia

Provisional orders

Ensure the document is a provisional order. A provisional order will generally have:

  • a heading of 'Provisional Order' or 'Provisional Court Order', and
  • a court seal, and may include phrases such as
    • it is ordered, or
    • it is ordered by consent

A provisional order made in a court of a reciprocating jurisdiction cannot be registered until the order is registered or confirmed in an Australian court. If the Australian court has specified a date from which the confirmed provisional order applies, then the order applies from that date.

If the Australian court has not specified a date then generally Child Support will give effect to the order from the date it is confirmed by the Australian court, although it may depend on the terms of the order. If unsure which date to use, escalate by email to Child Support Program Branch.

If an administrative assessment for child support can be made, a provisional order cannot be confirmed in an Australian court unless:

  • Child Support has a registered case with an existing overseas order, and
  • the new order is a provisional variation order

If an administrative assessment can be made, issue a unique letter advising the Overseas Authority of the option to apply for an administrative assessment. Advise the Overseas Authority that Child Support can accept:

  • a request to register a final order made in a reciprocating jurisdiction
  • a request to register a final maintenance assessment made in a reciprocating jurisdiction
  • a request to register an agreement which has been registered by a court or administrative authority of a reciprocating jurisdiction
  • an application to establish an Australian child support assessment. See Application for a child support assessment

If a provisional order that has been made in a reciprocating jurisdiction needs to be confirmed, see Variations to registered overseas maintenance liabilities (excluding New Zealand)

6

Determine if arrears are payable + Read more ...

If arrears are owed, be satisfied the arrears relate to unpaid periodic amounts.

If not satisfied, request evidence from the Overseas Authority or customer as part of the pre-registration interview.

Examples of acceptable evidence include:

  • a statement issued by a Central Authority (certified or not certified)
  • a statement issued by an overseas authority such as Court or Enforcement Authority (certified or not certified)
  • written statements from either customer detailing specific amounts paid or amounts owed, including dates
  • bank statements from either customer with supporting explanation
  • documentation from legal representatives

Consider the relevance and reliability of each piece of evidence, such as its source and date.

Example

A certified statement from a court would normally be relied on more than an unverified statement from a customer.

Take action if the amounts provided by the overseas authority or either parent are:

  • inconclusive
  • contradictory, or
  • disputed by the customers

Obtain more information from both parties and consider the merits of the evidence when making the decision.

Document the basis of the decision.

7

End date of liability + Read more ...

Check the application for the end date for the maintenance liability.

If there is no end date, confirm the end date as part of the pre-registration interview.

Contact the payer, payee and if required the Overseas Authority,

Canadian liabilities

Canadian orders for child support do not include end dates. This is because child support is payable in Canada until 1 of the following occurs:

  • a court of competent jurisdiction determines that support should be terminated
  • the parties agree to end support for a particular child, or
  • the order states that support ends on a specific date or when a certain event takes place and that event has taken place. For example the child finishing post-secondary education

If none of the above applies enter a default end date equivalent to the date the child turns 24 years of age. Document that the end date entered is a default end date.

For more information about Canadian liabilities, Resources has the Child and Spousal Support Caseworker Guide: Canada-Australia attachment.

See Table 3, Contact payer/payee or overseas authority.

8

The order, agreement or assessment is not registrable + Read more ...

Liability made in non-reciprocating jurisdiction

If the maintenance liability is not registrable because it was made in a non-reciprocating jurisdiction, advise the applicant that:

  • the liability cannot be registered and why
  • they can make an application for an administrative assessment directly to Child Support if all eligibility requirements are met

Document the contact and the decision not to register the liability in the Communication window. Procedure ends here.

If unable to contact the applicant then issue a unique letter explaining:

  • the decision and
  • that an application for assessment may be accepted if eligibility requirements are met

Liability has no periodic amount

If the application has been made by the payer and the overseas maintenance liability is not registrable because it does not require a person to pay a periodic amount or arrears of periodic amounts:

  • contact the payer to advise that the liability cannot be registered for collection by Child Support as it does not require payment of a periodic amount
  • document the conversation in the payer’s Communication window
  • issue the payer with a unique letter advising Child Support has refused their application for registration of an overseas maintenance liability because the amount ordered is not periodic. Include objection rights

If the application has been made by the payee and the overseas maintenance liability is not registrable because it does not require payment of a periodic amount or arrears of periodic amounts:

  • contact the payee to advise that the liability cannot be registered for collection by Child Support as it does not require payment of a periodic amount
  • explain that they may apply for non-periodic amounts such as a lump sum, insurance payments, school fees or medical or dental costs, to be entered into the Child Support Register to enable them to take their own enforcement action
  • explain that Child Support cannot collect non-periodic amounts entered into the Child Support Register, rather the debt is due to them
  • explain they can start their own enforcement proceedings in court or they can ask the Attorney-General’s Department, via Child Support, to start proceedings on their behalf
  • document the payee’s decision whether to apply to have the non-periodic amounts entered into the Register in the payee’s Communication window

If the payee wants non-periodic amounts entered into the Register:

  • record the details of the non-periodic amounts in the Case window notepad with a Subject title of ‘s25A entry – non-periodic amount’
  • contact the payer to advise that an overseas maintenance liability has been entered into the Child Support Register following an application from the payee. Explain that the liability is a debt to the payee, not Child Support. Explain that the payee has the option to start proceedings in court to enforce the liability
  • document the conversation in the payer’s Communication window
  • issue the payer with a unique letter advising Child Support has accepted an application from the payee for a non-periodic overseas maintenance liability to be entered into the Child Support Register. Explain the debt is payable to the payee, not Child Support. Include the details of the non-periodic amounts and objection rights
  • issue the payee with a unique letter advising Child Support has refused their application for registration of an overseas maintenance liability because the amount ordered is not periodic. Include objection rights. Confirm they have elected for the non-periodic amount to be entered into the Child Support Register and that the debt is due to them, not Child Support. Explain they may start their own court proceedings to enforce the debt or they can ask the Attorney-General's Department to initiate proceedings on their behalf. Include the details of the non-periodic amounts.

If the payee does not want non-periodic amounts entered in the Register, issue the payee with a unique letter advising Child Support has refused their application for registration of an overseas maintenance liability because the amount ordered is not periodic. Include objection rights. Confirm they have elected not to apply for the non-periodic amount to be entered into the Child Support Register and that this election does not prevent them from making an application in the future.

See Contact with Child Support customers.

Procedure ends here.

4

Contact Overseas Authority or payee + Read more ...

If required contact the Overseas Authority or payee to obtain evidence to make a decision on the application. This may include determining:

  • the end date of the liability
  • the arrears owing
  • the validity of the order if the order was provided to Child Support by the payer
  • whether any non-periodic amounts are to be entered into the Child Support Register

See Contact with Child Support customers.

If the overseas maintenance liability includes non-periodic amounts:

  • contact the payee to advise that the non-periodic amounts in the liability cannot be registered for collection by Child Support
  • explain they may apply to have these amounts entered into the Child Support Register to enable them to take their own enforcement action
  • explain that Child Support cannot collect non-periodic amounts entered into the Child Support Register, rather the debt is due to them
  • explain that they can start their own enforcement proceedings in court or they can ask the Attorney-General’s Department, via Child Support, to start proceedings on their behalf
  • ask the payee whether they wish to apply to have the non-periodic amounts entered into the Register and document the decision in the payee’s Communication window

If the payee cannot be contacted within 48 hours and the payee made the application, send a CAL letter. See Letters Cuba Process Help.

If the payee cannot be contacted with 48 hours and the application was made via or by an Overseas Authority, issue a

  • UQ40.01 - Advising request sent to CA for information about a court order - Applicant letter and
  • UQ40.00 - Requesting information about a court order - Central Authority letter.

Continue to try to contact payee by phone after the letter has been sent. Allow the payee: these timeframes to respond to the letter:

  • 14 days if living in Australia
  • 28 days if not living in Australia

Allow the Overseas Authority 6 weeks to provide a response.

Document contact with the Overseas Authority or payee in the Communication window. See Documenting Child Support information .

5

Contact payer + Read more ...

Contact the payer to conduct a pre-registration interview. If a contact number is not available, conduct searches as per Customer Location (Tracing). Check time zones to ensure the payer is contacted at an appropriate time.

See Contact with Child Support customers.

Discuss the following with the payer:

  • if they were not the applicant, confirm they are aware of the application
  • if the application is for registration of an order or agreement, confirm:
    • the order or agreement is the most current liability
    • children that the order or agreement relates to
    • if the order or agreement is for spousal or child support
    • the on-going liability
    • the end date of liability
    • if they require a copy of the order.
      Note: if the court order is provided to the payer ensure any of the payee's personal information is removed. For example their contact details
  • advise of any arrears owing as calculated by the Overseas Authority in the overseas currency and the amount in AUD based on the exchange rate for that day
  • advise of options for varying the application in Australia or the originating country
  • advise of payment options for child support including offering employer withholdings and confirm how payments are to be made. See Payment options for customers
  • if required obtain information to determine residency
  • if the payee has also applied for a non-periodic amount to be entered into the Register, explain:
    • this liability is payable to the payee, not Child Support, and
    • the payee has the option to start proceedings in court to enforce this liability

If the payer cannot be contacted within 48 hours, issue a UQ40.06 - Request for information - Application received to register and enforce court order in Australia - Non-applicant letter.

Continue to attempt to contact payer by phone after the letter has been sent. Allow the payer these timeframes to respond to the letter:

  • 14 days if living in Australia
  • 28 days if not living in Australia

Document contact with the payer in the Communication window.

Review application + Read more ...

Review the application to clarify the details. If necessary, make a decision on:

  • the validity of the order or overseas maintenance liability
  • arrears owing
  • end date of liability

8

Validity of order or overseas maintenance liability disputed + Read more ...

If either parent disputes the validity of the order or overseas maintenance liability ask the parent to provide evidence. For example a new order.

Examples of a valid dispute

  • A parent raises concerns regarding ongoing enforceability or validity of the order on the grounds of residency of the other parent
  • A parent believes that there are laws in the other country affecting ongoing enforceability
  • There are new orders affecting the order being registered

Contact the Overseas Authority to advise them of the valid dispute. Create an Instakeholder Follow Up intray to monitor whether a response is received.

If no response to a request for information is received within the 6 week intray time frame make a decision on the application using the information available:

  • registering
  • refusing
  • making ineligible

If needed seek help from an SSO.

9

Arrears + Read more ...

Consider the relevance and reliability of each piece of evidence provided, such as its source and date. For example a certified statement from a court would normally be relied on more than an unverified statement from a customer.

Take action if the amounts provided by the overseas authority or either parent are:

  • inconclusive
  • contradictory, or
  • disputed by the customers

Obtain more information from both parties and consider the merits of the evidence when making the decision.

Document the basis of the decision.

End date of liability

If both parties agree on the date, the end date can be applied to the case. If the parties disagree ask them to provide evidence so a determination can be made. Where appropriate, contact the Overseas Authority.

The end date can be updated if reliable information is received. If after repeated attempts to confirm an end date no answer has been received, apply the date the youngest child turns 18 years of age.

Note: if an end date cannot be confirmed for a spousal maintenance order the order will continue indefinitely.

If unsure of what date to apply seek help from an SSO. See Technical support Cuba Process Help.

Registration + Read more ...

The application for registration of an overseas maintenance liability must be accepted or refused within 90 days of receiving the application.

Any arrears from a previously registered liability that accrued prior to the application are still collectable.

If unsure of what the liability is under the court order, or of the arrears amount, seek help from an SSO.

2

Application affects an existing registration + Read more ...

If unsure whether the overseas liability can be registered, seek help from an SSO see Technical support in Child Support.

If there is an existing registered liability for the same parents and children, an overseas maintenance liability can be registered for ongoing amounts if:

  • the overseas maintenance liability was made after the existing registered liability. The existing registered liability will end from the day before the overseas liability is registered
  • there is an existing liability the overseas maintenance liability can be registered. For example an Australian assessment, that was made after the overseas maintenance liability but has never been registered, has always been private collect

Where the overseas liability was made before the existing registered liability and the later liability is registered, the first overseas liability cannot be registered for ongoing amounts. Periodic arrears that accrued for a period before the registration of the later liability can be registered.

Exchange rate + Read more ...

If registering a new application (unless it is a variation to an existing liability, where an existing maintenance liability is registered) ensure the exchange rate is updated for the new application.

If converting foreign currencies to Australian dollars, the exchange rate is:

  • the rate on the day that the child support application is received by the Services Australia

Note: for applications received before 20 March 2018, use the rate on the day the case is accepted on Cuba

If the exchange rate for a specific country is not available, email the International Disbursements Team. Document the rate and source in the Case window notepad. See Documenting Child Support information.

6

Clauses, including non-periodic amounts + Read more ...

The Court orders may include a range of clauses, including CPI variations, periodic amounts for times of unemployment or non-periodic amounts such as a lump sum, insurance payments, school fees or medical or dental costs.

Record any clauses including non-periodic amounts in the Case window notepad. Do not record any clauses in the Clauses window as this is only used for Stage 1 cases.

If the payee has applied for non-periodic amounts to be entered into the Register to enable them to enforce the liability, record the details of these amounts in the Case window notepad with a Subject title of ‘s25A entry - non-periodic amount’.

Note: Low Income Non-Enforcement (LINE) periods have been applicable since 19 July 2007 when they were introduced for International cases. For more information see Stage 1 cases - Low income non-enforcement periods.

7

Run eligibility + Read more ...

Cuba is unable to apply the correct formula to many non-parent carer cases and there is no Cuba warning to advise this. Check non-parent carer cases to determine if they are being assessed correctly. If the formula is not being applied correctly, or if in doubt, escalate the case to an SSO.

Before running eligibility check the case to ensure the liability has been registered correctly. See Eligibility Cuba Process Help.

Check the Pre-confirmation Results window before accepting or refusing the application including:

  • errors
  • warnings
  • indicators for the new case and any related cases

Check warnings and fix errors before saving eligibility.

If the application is refused or partially accepted and it is expected to be eligible, select 'Cancel'. Check the ineligible reason and all the information keyed in each window. Run eligibility again.

If errors have been fixed, warnings understood, the assessment correct and ready to make a decision on the application for child support save Eligibility.

If needing help to fix an error or warning, or to understand why a change is occurring, seek help from an SSO.

  • In the intray, record:

    • who the cheque should be made out to

To record these details, see Disbursement Cuba Process Help, recording disbursement options and Payee Disbursement Options window.

In the Payment Options window record how the payer will make payments to Child Support. This includes the frequency of payments and the payment method. For example monthly payments paid manually, via Australia post.

There is no need to adjust anything in the Payment options window if the payer is not in Australia, and:

  • has refused to pay
  • has requested the case be transmitted
  • there has been no contact

It will default to manual payments. The details will be updated when the case is transmitted.

For payers who prefer to have employer deductions, establish employer withholding, - see Employer Withholding (linking) for Child Support customers.
