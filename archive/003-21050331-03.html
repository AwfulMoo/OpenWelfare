Travel Agent 2 - Payment Destination Summary + Read more ...

Check the Payment Destination Summary section:

This is the bank account that the payment (if granted) will be paid into. Even if rejecting this claim, complete this process now in case there is a review and the decision is changed.

During the online claim, when the applicant reaches the bank details step, they will see either:

  • Round 1 bank account details (pre-filled)
    • For Round 1 claims with a grant outcome, the Round 2 online application will show the bank account the applicant provided in their Round 1 online application
    • The applicant can review the data at that point and change it
    • If changed, the Payment Destination Summary in Process Direct will show the difference between the Round 1 and Round 2 bank details
  • No bank account information
    • For Round 1 claims where a reject outcome was overturned and a manual payment made by the !FFOAM screen, the Round 2 online application will show no Round 1 details
    • The applicant will need to enter bank details for Round 2
    • The Payment Destination Summary in Process Direct will only show Round 2 bank details

Review the bank details:

  • Check the BSB provided (using Search BSB numbers) to make sure the branch is in Australia
  • Check the bank account name is the name of the business (use the ABN Look up tool to set up a link)

Select the hand shake icon on the right of the screen:

  • Select one of the following options which show in the drop-down menu under Classification:
    • Accepted
    • Rejected, or
    • Not required
  • Add Assessment notes to show why the classification option was selected
    For example, ‘BSB confirmed to be with Westpac at Torquay branch and account name same as business’
  • Only select Rejected if there are concerns details may be fraudulent

Are there concerns information may be fraudulent?

  • Yes,
    • add Assessment notes wherever Rejected was selected
    • document why the claim may be fraudulent
    • continue reviewing the claim
    • report the suspected fraud after assessing the whole claim
    • go to Step 5

Rejecting claims – business is not eligible

Table 5: What to do when rejecting a claim after following the above processes. For help to progress any steps, escalate to the Level 2 Helpdesk.

Step

Action

1

Who completed the claim assessment? + Read more ...

If the decision was made by:

  • Services Australia (turnover was less than $500,000), go to Step 2
  • Austrade (turnover was $500,000 or more) verbally advising the applicant, go to Step 4

2

Is the applicant on the phone? + Read more ...

  • Yes, tell the applicant why the business does not meet requirements for the payment. Go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Try to contact the applicant + Read more ...

Only staff in the ‘day’ team are to complete this action. Check the Round 2 Assessments spreadsheet before contacting an applicant.

Find the applicant’s contact details by selecting the Personnel icon in the business’ claim.

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the applicant.

Contact successful:

  • Ask the applicant to provide Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO). Do not give hints or prompts that help the applicant answer the questions
  • Tell the applicant why the business’ claim is not successful. If the applicant does not agree, the letter they receive will outline how they can lodge an appeal
  • Note the discussion as part of the Assessment Summary
  • Go to Step 4

If unable to contact:

  • Update claim workflow
    • Select the AusTrade icon
    • Select the Handshake icon
    • Record 'Pending Rejection - first contact unsuccessful' in the Add Assessment Notes
    • Save this update
  • Update ‘Round 2 Assessments’ spreadsheet
    • Record in the Notes column the unsuccessful contact
  • Next day
    • In the Round 2 Assessments spreadsheet, review all claims with the officer's name and ‘unsuccessful contact' in the notes
    • Make a second contact attempt
    • If unable to contact on the second attempt, go to Step 4

4

Record decision + Read more ...

  • Select the AusTrade icon
  • Select the Handshake icon (or green tick/red cross icon) on the right of the screen
  • Select one of the following options which show in the drop down menu under Classification:
    • Accepted
    • Rejected, or
    • Not required
  • Complete the Round 2 Assessment template
  • Check the number of characters in Microsoft Word by selecting Review tab > Word Count and checking Characters (with spaces). Remove characters as per instructions in the Round 2 Assessment template if there are more than 3000. Otherwise the claim cannot progress
  • Paste this into Add Assessment Notes
  • Select Submit

Note: attach the Round 2 Assessment template in Process Direct before rejecting the claim. The correct process is explained below.

5

Quality check? + Read more ...

Has the Service Officer met quality checking requirements and no longer needs their work checked by a quality checker?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, refer the claim for quality checking before finalising:
    • Refer to the quality checker assigned to their claim
    • If they need an urgent quality check, escalate to the level 2 Helpdesk
    • When the quality check result is received, go to Step 6

6

Implementing a reject decision + Read more ...

Before implementing a reject decision, make sure the Round 2 Assessment template is recorded into the AusTrade Assessment Box as per the latest details on the claim.

To set the claim status to reject:

  • Select the red action button (red button with an up arrow)
  • Select the Reject Application option
  • Record the rejection reason (drop down menu)
  • Add notes in the decision field, as per the Reject outcome on the Round 2 Assessment template
  • Select Save (this locks in the claim outcome as rejected)

The letter to the business will say the rejection reason is one of the following:

  • Qualification not met, or
  • Services Australia has not received proof of eligibility as requested

  • No, procedure ends here

5

Applicant requests an explanation of the decision + Read more ...

If the applicant does not understand the decision after a discussion about a claim outcome (other reasons), they can request an explanation which will be provided by a SME:

  • Open Customer Record and search for the record. Then go to Environment B
  • While in the business' CRN (second CRN), launch the Script Selector using the link at top left of screen
  • Key ‘Review’ in the search field, then run the Internal Review/Explanation script to record the request for an explanation
  • Select Add new explanation or review
  • Select Path ‘I am registering a new request for explanation’
  • Customer Circumstances: No (these do not apply to a business)
  • Administrative Details:
    • Source of Request: as per request
    • Date of Decision: date claim finalised
    • Payment Type the Decision has been made against: Ex-gratia One Off Payment
    • Disaster ID: C40 COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program
    • Process Type: Select the most appropriate option from drop down list
    • Decision Maker: logon of original claim decision maker
    • Which Office/Team made the Decision: TCH (regardless of who made original decision)
    • Date Decision Queried: as per request
    • Reason for Contact: Select the most appropriate option from drop down list
    • Special Case Indicator: leave blank
  • Original Decision: Rate or Rejection
  • Reason for Decision: Qualification not met
  • Reason for Explanation: Record the applicants reason for request in free text field
  • Transfer to relevant SME or continue Explanation:
    • Yes if the SME is recording the explanation and ready to finalise
    • No if the Service Officer is referring request for explanation to a SME
  • To which site do you want to transfer the request (if no selected above): TCH
  • Add Keyword: 'CTSPEXP2'. This must be added to make sure these work items allocate to a Service Officer with the skills to complete these appeals

If providing the explanation as a SME, go to Step 6.

Otherwise, procedure ends here.

6

Completing the request for explanation of a claim outcome + Read more ...

Open Customer Record, and search for record, then go to Environment B.

To understand and explain the decision, use:

  • Information in the completed Round 2 Assessment template
  • Claim content and uploaded documents in Process Direct (attached to claim)
  • Uploaded documents in Customer First

If the applicant is not on the phone, make 2 genuine attempts to contact them to explain the claim outcome decision.

Contact successful (or applicant is on the phone)

Is the applicant satisfied with the explanation?

  • Yes, run the Internal Review/Explanation script to finalise the explanation
  • No,
    • The business has no further review or appeal rights through Services Australia
    • If the applicant requests an ‘Exceptional Circumstances’ Review, this is completed by Austrade. Tell the applicant to contact Austrade by emailing Consumer Travel Support
    • Run the Internal Review/Explanation script to finalise the explanation

If unable to contact

  • Run the Internal Review/Explanation script to finalise the explanation. The script will issue a Q449 letter advising that contact was unsuccessful and if they would like to talk about the decision they can contact Services Australia
  • When preparing the Q449 letter, select Program, and enter ‘COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program - Round 2’ in Program Name

Procedure ends here.
