Payment of TAL

Table 1

Step

Description

1

Confirm eligibility + Read more ...

If advice has been received from the Payment Summaries, Allowances and Supplements Business Team following an enquiry about a customer's eligibility for TAL, go to Step 5.

When confirming a customer's eligibility to TAL the following checks should be performed:

  • Customer must be a telephone subscriber (check Telephone Summary (TDS) screen) on the relevant test day:
    • 1 January
    • 20 March
    • 1 July
    • 20 September
  • For Higher rate of TAL, internet connection must be coded (check Internet Connection details (ICD) screen

Note: TAL payments issued can also be viewed on the One Off Payment (OOP) screen in Customer First.

More checks are needed for the following eligible payment types:

  • Disability Support Pension (DSP) customers, see Item 1 in Table 2
  • Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customers, see Item 2 in Table 2
  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP) customers, see Item 3 in Table 2
  • Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) customers, see Item 4 in Table 2
  • Parenting Payment Single (PPS) customers, see Item 5 in Table 2
  • Special Benefit (SpB) customers, see Item 6 in Table 2
  • Youth Allowance (job seeker) customers, see Item 7 in Table 2

If customer is confirmed to be eligible for TAL but has not been paid, go to Step 2.

2

Unpaid TAL for eligible customers + Read more ...

If a customer contacts about an unpaid TAL payment:

  • Check TDS screen and make sure Telephone subscriber Y is coded
  • Check there has been a relevant TAL test day (1 January, 20 March, 1 July and 20 September) Note: TAL is usually issued on the customer's first Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) that falls on or after the TAL test day
  • Check the Payment Summary (PS) screen for a 'One time pay'
  • 'S'elect the 'One time pay'

Does the Payment Detail (PD) screen display 'THIS IS A FUTURE PAYMENT AND IS SUBJECT TO ALTERATION', and shows a Tal $ amount?

  • Yes, the record is affected by a known system issue:
    • Report the CRN to the ICT Service Desk to request a strip of the payment. This will allow payment to be issued to the customer. The Resources page contains a link to mySupport
    • DOC the customer's record
    • Upon reply from the ICT Service Desk, advising the payment has been made, check the customer's record to confirm the payment and the delivery date
    • Advise the customer the TAL payment has been made and when to expect the payment
    • DOC the customer's record
  • No, check the customer's eligibility according to the payment they are receiving in Table 2. If customer appears eligible for TAL but has not been paid, go to Step 3

3

Payment decision + Read more ...

Is the customer eligible for TAL?

  • Yes, and the system is:
    • paying the amount owing, procedure ends here
    • not making the payment, go to Step 4
  • No:
    • advise the customer
    • record the details on a DOC
    • procedure ends here

4

System is not paying TAL to an eligible customer + Read more ...

  • email the Payment Summaries, Allowances and Supplements Business Team:
    • see the Resources page for a link to the team contact details
    • use the Positional Mailbox
  • include the:
    • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • customer name
    • TAL payment date, and
    • eligibility status
  • advise the customer their entitlement needs to be checked
  • record the details on a DOC
  • the business team will reply to the email with their advice
  • procedure ends here until the reply email has been received

5

Advice received from Business Team + Read more ...

  • Follow the instructions in the email
  • Contact the customer to advise the outcome
  • Record the details on a DOC
  • Procedure ends here

Eligibility checks for TAL

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Disability Support Pension (DSP) customers + Read more ...

The customer is eligible for TAL if they meet all of the following:

  • a telephone subscriber (check the TDS screen)
  • under 21 years
  • has no dependent child/ren

Note: DSP customers that meet the above conditions are also eligible to receive the higher rate of TAL if they hold a home internet connection. Check the ICD screen.

2

Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customers + Read more ...

The customer is eligible for TAL if they are a telephone subscriber (check TDS screen) and any of the following applies:

  • they are over 60 and have been receiving income support payments continuously for at least 9 months
  • they are single and the principal carer of a dependent child, or
  • they are the principal carer a dependent child and their partner is over 60, receives FHA or JSP, and has been receiving income support payments continuously for at least 9 months

Note: FHA customers that meet the above conditions are also eligible to receive the higher rate of TAL if they hold a home internet connection. Check the ICD screen.

3

JobSeeker Payment (JSP) customers + Read more ...

The customer is eligible for TAL if they are a telephone subscriber (check TDS screen) and any of the following applies:

  • they are over 60 and have been receiving income support payments continuously for at least 9 months
  • they have a partial capacity to work as assessed by an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt)
  • they are single and the principal carer of a dependent child, or
  • they are the principal carer of a dependent child and their partner is over 60, receives FHA or JSP and has been receiving income support payments continuously for at least 9 months

Note: JSP customers who meet the eligibility for TAL but are receiving a JSP transitional rate are not entitled to TAL.

4

Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) customers + Read more ...

The customer is eligible for TAL if they are a telephone subscriber (check TDS screen) and any of the following applies:

  • they are over 60 but under Age Pension age and have been receiving income support payments continuously for at least 9 months
  • they have a partial capacity to work as assessed by an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt), or
  • their partner is over 60, receives FHA or JSP, and has received income support payments continuously for 9 months

5

Parenting Payment Single (PPS) customers + Read more ...

The customer is eligible for TAL if they are:

  • a telephone subscriber (check TDS screen), and
  • under Age Pension age

6

Special Benefit (SpB) customers + Read more ...

The customer is eligible for TAL if they meet all of the following:

  • they a telephone subscriber (check TDS screen)
  • they are over 60 but under Age Pension age, and
  • they have been receiving income support payments continuously for at least 9 months

7

Youth Allowance (job seeker) customers + Read more ...

The customer is eligible for TAL if they are a telephone subscriber (check TDS screen) and any of the following applies:

  • (not a full-time student or apprentice) they have a partial capacity to work as assessed by an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt)
  • (not a full-time student or apprentice) they are single and the principal carer of a dependent child, or
  • (not a full-time student or apprentice) they are the principal carer a dependent child and their partner is over 60, receives FHA or JSP, and has been receiving income support payments continuously for at least 9 months

Information about TAL

Item

Description

1

When is the higher rate of TAL payable? + Read more ...

A higher rate of TAL is payable to Disability Support pension (DSP) customers under 21 with no dependent children who:

  • are eligible for basic TAL, and
  • have a home internet connection in their name or their partner's name

Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customers will qualify for the higher rate of TAL, if they:

  • are eligible for basic TAL, and
  • have a home internet connection in their or their partner's name

Note: members of a couple may be eligible for different rates of TAL. For details of the rate and higher rate of TAL, see Payment rates and thresholds.

2

When TAL is not payable + Read more ...

TAL is not payable if:

  • the customer is not qualified for TAL on the TAL test day
    • for example, if the customer is not a telephone subscriber (and the customer's Telephone Details Screen are recorded accordingly) on the TAL test day
  • the customer leaves Australia permanently
  • the customer remains overseas as a temporary absence for more than 6 weeks
  • from 20 September 2009, even though a person may qualify for TAL on a test day, TAL is not payable if, on that day, the person:
    • is receiving a transitional rate pension
    • is receiving Pension Supplement, and their pension supplement amount is greater than the pension supplement basic amount
    • is receiving a seniors supplement under the Veterans' Entitlement Act
    • is receiving Commonwealth Seniors Health Card quarterly supplement under the Social Security Act
    • has an election in force to receive their pension supplement minimum amount on a quarterly basis
    • is receiving Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act supplement, or
    • is a Commonwealth Seniors Health Card holder who has elected not to receive Commonwealth Seniors Health Card quarterly supplement
  • from 20 March 2020, even though a person may qualify for TAL on a test day, TAL is not payable if, on the day the person:
    • is receiving JobSeeker Payment (JSP) at the transitional rate
    • is receiving Pension Supplement and their pension supplement amount is greater than the pension supplement basic amount
  • The Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) pays the Veteran Supplement at the TAL rate to the customer. TAL is not payable by Centrelink to customers receiving the following DVA payments:
    • War Widows Pension
    • Disability Compensation Payment at Special Rate (Totally and Permanently Incapacitated (TPI))
    • Extreme Disablement Adjustment
    • Service Pension
    • Veteran Payment
    • Income Support Supplement (ISS)
    • DVA Age Pension
    • DVA Veteran Supplement at the TAL rate
    • DVA Veteran Supplement at the high rate
    • an increased rate of Disability Compensation Payment because of being an amputee
  • The person's partner is a World War 1 veteran. These veterans will receive a full refund of their telephone rental from DVA. In these cases a low rate of TAL is payable to the partners of these customers. This will occur unless the partner fails to qualify in their own right or the couple are illness separated

3

Backdating payment of TAL + Read more ...

TAL can only be backdated if it can be proven:

  • the customer had previously advised Centrelink they were a telephone subscriber, and
  • Centrelink had not recorded this, (for example customer advised on a review form)

Otherwise, payment of TAL commences from the first TAL payday after notification. For backdating information, see How to pay Telephone Allowance (TAL) arrears.

4

Savings provision for TAL cancelled + Read more ...

The savings provision is cancelled if the customer is no longer partnered, or the partner is granted:

  • a Centrelink income support, or
  • a Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) payment that attracts Pension or Veteran Supplement

It is also cancelled if:

  • the customer loses entitlement to TAL, or
  • the current member of a couple rate of TAL is higher than the saved rate

5

Entitlement to TAL after cancellation + Read more ...

If an eligible customer's income support payment is cancelled due to the customer or partner commencing employment, TAL is automatically payable for 6 months after date of effect of cancellation.

The exceptions are:

  • DSP customers, under 21 with no dependent children, who automatically maintain payment for a 12 month period after cancellation due to work, and
  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (job seeker) customers who have a partial capacity to work as assessed by an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt). These customers remain qualified for TAL for 12 months if their payment is cancelled due to employment

The customer must continue to satisfy the TAL criteria at the date TAL is being paid (they must remain telephone subscribers). Note: for DSP customers eligible for higher rate TAL, their rate may change, even during their extension period, if they become or stop being a home internet subscriber. See Payment of Telephone Allowance (TAL) to customers suspended or cancelled due to earnings.

6

Entitlement to TAL after cancellation due to employment income + Read more ...

Parenting Payment (PP) customers, under Age Pension age, who claimed PP on or after 1 July 2006 and have an assessed partial capacity to work due to a disability, will have access to TAL for 52 weeks after their and/or their partner's employment income is sufficient to cancel them from payment, on or after 1 January 2008.

See the Resources page in Payment of Telephone Allowance (TAL) to customers suspended or cancelled due to employment income for a table summarising the payments entitled to an extension of TAL when cancelled.

7

Coding details when TAL or Veteran Supplement at the TAL rate is paid by the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) + Read more ...

DVA stopped paying TAL from 20 September 2009. From 20 September 2009 customers receive Veteran Supplement at one of three rates, that is Veteran Supplement at the TAL rate (VST), Veteran Supplement at the Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA) rate (VSP) or Veteran Supplement at the high rate (VSH). Veteran Supplement at the high rate includes both TAL and PhA.

On the Veterans Affairs Pension (DVA) screen code:

  • 'TAL' or 'VST/VSH' in Type: field
  • DVA reference number in DVA Reference: field
  • date of grant of new claim in Event Date: field
  • no amount in Fortnightly Amount $.c: field as it is not needed
  • 'Y' in Exempt Pymt Ind: field
  • status code of ON/OFF

The Veterans Affairs Pension Summary (DVAS) screen will display. 'Not income' displays in the Amount: field.

Note: the Client ID: field is a display only field that contains the DVA Unique Identifier which is a number allocated by DVA to customers who receive a DVA Disability Pension and also receive a Centrelink payment. This number, (unlike the DVA reference number), will not change if the customer moves to a new state.
