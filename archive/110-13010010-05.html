Questioning Techniques Guide
How to issue an indirect message for EIIP - educational message not delivered

Education message

Item

Description

1

Earnings reporting obligations (not for self-employed customers)

'You need to report the gross income your employer paid you in your 14 day Centrelink reporting period. Your gross income is the amount your employer pays you before tax and other deductions. You can find your gross pay amount on your payslip.

'If you don't get payslips, you should ask your employer to provide them or tell you the gross income. It's a requirement that all employers should provide a payslip to their employees.

'There are no changes to how you report the number of hours you worked, keep telling us how many hours you actually worked in your reporting period, which may not match your payslip.'

If they have a partner who is also working: 'It is important that you report any income you and your partner get from employers. This includes JobKeeper Payment.'

'If you are paid an allowance for out-of-pocket expenses (for example, laundry, uniform or travel allowance), you do not need to include this when reporting your gross income to us.

'If you are paid any type of employment lump sums such as commissions, bonuses, leave and/or termination payments, or your income includes salary sacrificing and/or Fringe Benefits, these may be assessed differently. It is best to contact Services Australia.

'If you get any compensation, WorkCover or income protection payments, you must advise Services Australia within 14 days.'

2

Where to report earnings

'Are you aware that you can report your employment income by going to my.gov.au, signing in and selecting Centrelink to access your online account or using the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app?

'The agency understands that personal factors or circumstances may sometimes prevent you from declaring the correct amount of your employment income for the days worked within your Centrelink fortnight period.

'If this happens, you can correct your previously reported income for up to 6 fortnights in the past through your Centrelink online account, If you are unable to do this, you can contact Services Australia and we will help you to correct the income.'

3

Recovery Fee

A Recovery Fee is not applied to customers in receipt of Age Pension (AGE), Carer Payment (CP) or ABSTUDY.

The Recovery Fee will not apply to partner income even if the partner's income is incorrectly declared or not declared.

Suggested dialogue:

'Are you aware if you incorrectly declare your income from employment, Services Australia may impose a 10% recovery fee to the debt?

'To avoid being incorrectly paid and having a 10% recovery fee imposed, it is important that you declare all your employment income correctly on your reporting day.'

4

Working Credit/ Income Bank/ Work Bonus scheme

'Are you aware of the Working Credit/Income Bank/Work Bonus scheme?

'The working credit/income bank/work bonus scheme allows you to build up credits in each fortnight.

When you have income from employment, you can draw on your working credits/ income bank/ work bonus to reduce the effect of that income on your income support payment.

'Only income from your employment can be used to affect these credits in this way'.

5

Employment Diary and Worksheet (SU505)

'To help your keep a track of your employment income, you can download an earnings worksheet (SU505) from Services Australian website (www.servicesaustralia.gov.au). You can fill it out on your device or print it and compete it by hand.

'This information will help you to record and declare your gross employment income paid to you within your Centrelink fortnightly period'

6

Data Matching

'Are you aware that we get information about your income from other government agencies, such as the Australian Taxation Office?

If we get information that your income is different to what you have told us when getting a payment, we will ask you to review and update your information.

Therefore, we recommend that you please remember to keep all records of your employment. This will reduce the need for you to contact your employer'.

7

Notification requirements

'Are you aware that you are obligated to notify the Agency, generally within 14 days, of any changes in your circumstances? For example, change of address, relationship status.'
