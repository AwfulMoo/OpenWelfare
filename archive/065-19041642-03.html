Determine eligibility

Step

Action

1

Age requirements + Read more ...

To be eligible for the Scheme a customer must be of Age Pension age, or partnered to a person who is of Age Pension age. Age rules for Age Pension are available on the Services Australia website, see Resources page for a link.

Has the customer reached Age Pension age?

  • Yes, customer meets the age requirements, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Relationship status + Read more ...

Is the customer partnered?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, customer is not eligible as they have not reached Age Pension age nor partnered to a person of Age Pension age. Go to Step 10

3

Age Pension age partner + Read more ...

Has the customer's partner reached Age Pension age?

  • Yes, customer meets the age requirements. Go to Step 4
  • No, customer is not eligible as neither they nor their partner is Age Pension age. Go to Step 10

4

Qualifying payment + Read more ...

To be eligible for the Scheme the customer must receive or be qualified for a Home Equity Access Scheme qualifying payment. They are not required to have a payable rate under the income and assets tests. See Assess the qualifying payment status table for the process to determine this criteria.

Does the customer qualify for one of the qualifying payments?

  • Yes, customer meets the qualifying payment criteria, go to Step 5
  • No, customer is not eligible for the Scheme. Go to Step 10

The Scheme (Loan Only) is payable to customers that do not get the Age Pension because they fail the income and/or assets test. That is, if they are current or qualify for the Farm Household Assistance (FHA).

Note: FHA is not payable to customers that are entitled to get or are qualified for an age pension, other than participating in the Scheme.

5

Real estate as loan security + Read more ...

See Security for the Home Equity Access Scheme for details about real estate that can be used as security.

Does the customer, or their partner, own real estate in Australia that they are willing to offer as security for the loan?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, customer is not eligible for the Scheme, go to Step 10

6

Adequate and appropriate insurance + Read more ...

The real estate used to secure the loan must be adequately and appropriately insured. Customers must provide supporting documentation at the time of claim lodgement.

Does the customer have adequate and appropriate insurance for each real estate property they have offered as loan security?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, customer is not eligible for the Scheme, go to Step 10

7

Bankruptcy or insolvency + Read more ...

The customer must declare if they are bankrupt or have a personal insolvency agreement when they submit their loan application. Before approving the application, a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) conducts a Bankruptcy Register Search (BRS) through Equifax.

Is the customer bankrupt or subject to a personal insolvency agreement?

  • Yes, customer is not eligible for the Scheme, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Customer meets the eligibility criteria + Read more ...

Has a loan application already been lodged?

  • Yes, the claim will be automatically allocated to a CAO for assessment and processing. See Assessing Home Equity Access Scheme claims for details about the assessment process. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Terms and conditions + Read more ...

Customers (and their partners, if they are a member of a couple) must agree to the Scheme terms and conditions to be eligible. Consent to the terms and conditions is provided through the loan application.

If the real estate offered by the customer as security for the loan is co-owned by another party other than the customer's partner, or is the asset of a trust or company, written consent is also required from the impacted parties. See Security for a Home Equity Access Scheme for details about third party consent.

If the customer has not already done so, it is recommended they:

  • read the terms and conditions on the Services Australia website and make sure they (and their partner, if applicable) have a full understanding of the Scheme before to lodging an application
  • seek independent legal or financial advice before applying for the loan

The customer can also access the Financial Information Service (FIS) to discuss the Scheme.

If the customer wishes to proceed with an application, they can lodge online via their Centrelink online account. See Claiming a Home Equity Access Scheme loan for more details.

Procedure ends here.

10

Customer not eligible for the Home Equity Access Scheme + Read more ...

Tell the customer of the reason(s) why they are not eligible for the Scheme and document the discussion.

If the customer has already lodged a loan application, it will be automatically allocated to a CAO for assessment and processing.

Consider other available Centrelink payments, benefits or services the customer may be eligible for.
