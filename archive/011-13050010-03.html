This page contains information on how to process overpayments and incorrect payments on subsequent claims for reimbursement for the External Breast Prostheses Reimbursement Program (EBPRP).

On this Page:

Claimant identifies overpayment

Incorrect payment on subsequent claim

Overpayment identified

Claimant identifies overpayment

Table 1: This table describes the process undertaken by the EBPRP Processing Team when a claimant identifies an overpayment to the agency.

Step

Action

1

Claimant notifies of overpayment + Read more ...

Claimant contacts Services Australia stating they have been overpaid.

2

Send details to Program Management + Read more ...

Email documentation to Program Management to start the debt recovery process.

Include in the documentation the claimant's:

  • Medicare number
  • EBPRP claim number
  • Brief outline of the conversation had with the claimant
  • How the overpayment was identified
  • Copy of the claim form and account, and
  • Screenshot of the claimant’s claiming history from Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS)

3

Record details in comment on EBPRP claims screen + Read more ...

Enter a comment on the claimant's personal section in CDMS and in PaNDA that the claimant contacted the agency. Include a brief outline of the conversation and that the details have been sent to Program Management to begin the debt recovery process.

4

Internal procedures to raise the debt + Read more ...

Program Management will begin internal procedures to raise the debt. Approval to raise the debt recovery is required by the Director of the Program.

Procedures for Program Management on how to raise the debt are filed with Program Management Team and cannot be used by service officers outside of Program Management.

Program Management will send an email to EBPRP Processing Team that the debt recovery process has commenced against the claimant.

5

Service Officers + Read more ...

No further action is required by the Service Officers.

Incorrect payment on subsequent claim

Table 2: This table describes the process undertaken by EBPRP Processing Team when an incorrect payment on a subsequent claim for reimbursement is identified.

Step

Action

1

Check if claimant's reimbursement was not a result of breast cancer + Read more ...

Has the claimant been reimbursed for a prostheses that was not a result of breast cancer?

Note: this may be identified during quality checking or at previous stage.

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here

2

Forward claimant details to Program Management + Read more ...

Send an email to Program Management with all claimant information, including:

  • Medicare number
  • Claim ID
  • Claimant name
  • Reason why overpayment occurred
  • Provide a full explanation as to why the debt recovery process is to commence. Whether or not the claimant has been advised of this, and that they are aware they will be required to repay the money to the agency

Note: this will be followed up with a phone call if there is any further information that needs to be discussed with Program Management. The conversation is to be backed up by an email outlining the details of the conversation and forwarded to Program Management to be filed against the claimant’s existing file in the program drive and program inbox.

3

Confirm with claimant + Read more ...

When the claimant has been contacted by phone, use identification processes to identify the claimant

Program Management will contact claimant by phone to confirm breast surgery was not as a result of breast cancer.

4

Inform claimant that the claim will not be processed + Read more ...

Program Support will inform claimant that as the breast surgery was not as a result of breast cancer, the claim will not be processed. If the claimant has previously claimed with the program, then the debt recovery process will commence for the previous external breast prosthesis payment.

5

Internal procedures to raise the debt + Read more ...

Program Management will begin internal procedures to raise the debt. Approval to raise the debt recovery is required by the Director of the Program.

Procedures for Program Management on how to raise the debt are filed with Program Management Team and cannot be used by service officers outside of Program Management.

Program Management will send an email to EBPRP Processing Team that the debt recovery process has commenced against the claimant.

6

Record details in a comment on the EBPRP claimants claim screen + Read more ...

EBPRP Processing Team to enter comment on the claimants EBPRP claims screen recording a brief transcript of the conversation with the claimant.

Overpayment identified

Table 3: This table describes the process undertaken by EBPRP Processing Team when an overpayment is identified.

Step

Action

1

Email Program Management + Read more ...

Forward an email to Program Management with claimant information including:

  • Medicare number
  • Claim ID
  • Claimant name
  • Reason why overpayment occurred
  • Copy of the claim form and account, and
  • Screenshot of the claim screen

Send claim to Program Management.

Note: Program Management will investigate and make the determination if the debt recovery process will commence.

2

Record details in comment on EBPRP claims screen + Read more ...

Enter a comment on the claimant's Personal tab in CDMS and PaNDA with a brief description of the issue and advise it has been sent to Program support.

3

Internal procedures to raise the debt + Read more ...

Program Management will begin internal procedures to raise the debt. Approval to raise the debt recovery is required by the Director of the Program.

Procedures for Program Management on how to raise the debt are filed with Program Management Team and cannot be used by service officers outside of Program Management.

Program Management will send an email to EBPRP Processing Team that the debt recovery process has commenced against the claimant.

4

Received result from Program Management + Read more ...

Program Management will send an email to EBPRP Processing Team that the debt recovery process has commenced against the claimant.

No further action is required by the EBPRP Processing Team.
