This page contains information relating to when a change of circumstances will trigger an Accelerated Claimant Matching (ACM) review.

ACM reviews

Triggers and matching process

This table describes examples of changes to a customer record that will trigger an ACM review.

Item

Description

1

Tax File Number (TFN) + Read more ...

TFN matching identifies 2 or more customers who have provided the same TFN in association with their Centrelink claims. For privacy reasons, once the TFN is entered into the Centrelink database it becomes encrypted. The ACM matching engine will identify any cases in which the encrypted TFN is the same.

2

Bank account + Read more ...

Bank account matching identifies 2 or more customers who have provided the same bank account details for the payment of benefits. The ACM matching engine will identify any cases in which the payment details (BSB Code and Account number) are identical, and no current relationship between the customers exists.

3

Debt Matching + Read more ...

Debt Matching identifies all outstanding debts with no arrangement for recovery in place. The ACM matching engine uses the criteria for identity matching to find customers who may have multiple Customer Reference Numbers (CRNs) where one of the CRNs has an outstanding debt.

4

Identity matching + Read more ...

Identity matching occurs where personal identity information on a new/changed record is matched with the same personal identity information on an existing Centrelink record.

5

Family assistance vs Income Support Child Matching + Read more ...

Family assistance vs Income Support Matching identifies customers who are receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for a child who may also be receiving an income support payment. The FTB child identity details are compared against income support customer identity details.

Before any action is taken on these reviews, a number of checks should be done. These include:

  • check the identity details of the FTB child and income support customer to ensure they are the same person
  • check the parent record to ensure that FTB is paid for that child
  • check that an income support payment is paid for the child
  • check whether any FTB and income support payments for the child have been paid for the same period

If the income support customer is receiving Youth Allowance (YA), and the rate of YA is lower than the rate of FTB paid to the parent, both the YA customer and the FTB customer should be contacted. If the income support customer is receiving a payment other than YA, only the FTB customer should be contacted.

6

Member of a Couple (MOC) Matching + Read more ...

MOC matching identifies customers:

  • Who are sharing accommodation with a person with whom they have had a previous relationship.
  • Where 2 customers have not previously been partnered and they have the same bank account details and same address.
  • Who are residing at the same address as an ex-partner with whom they have an identified Child Support relationship. These customers may not have been previously linked on Centrelink records as per the existing project, but have a common child as identified by Child Support.

7

YA/ABSTUDY Homeless vs. Parental Address + Read more ...

The YA Homeless versus Parent Address matching project identifies homeless rate Youth Allowance (YA) customers with address details identical to the address details on parental records.

This project will use existing ‘change’ data methods used for address matching.

The review process for these selections is relatively straight forward. Service Officers will be required to:

  • identify the current notified address details of the homeless YA customers’ and the parent/s’ address details
  • obtain verification of address details for the YA customers
  • compare the address information and make a decision about whether the address details are the same

Where a decision is made that the address details are the same for the YA homeless customer and the parent/s, the Service Officer may be required to:

  • Make primary benefit changes - such as cancel the homeless rate.
  • Change the accommodation circumstances to reflect that the customer is living with parent/s. The effect will be a cancellation of any Rent Assistance (RA) payment to the YA customer.
  • Raise any debts which may result due to excess payments.

8

Investment Property Matching + Read more ...

The ACM Investment Property Matching identifies customers who have an investment property notified and do not have income recorded from this property. These details are matched against current customers whose current address details are identical to the investment property.

This project will use existing ‘change’ data methods used for address matching.

The review process for these selections is relatively straight forward. Service Officer will be required to:

  • identify the investment property address and whether income is recorded from this source
  • verify that the other customer/s address details are the same as the investment property and whether they pay, or are liable to pay rent or rent type payments for the address
  • make a decision about whether any action needs to be taken on the match records

Where a decision is made that the address details are the same for the match record and the investment property owner, the Service Officer may be required to:

  • Code income on the customer with the investment property. This may affect the customer's entitlement. See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services for assistance.
  • Change the accommodation circumstances to reflect true address details if any changes are identified. See Updating address details for assistance, if required.
  • Raise any debts which may result due to excess payments.
