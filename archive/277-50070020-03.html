2

The customer is advising that they and/or their partner are unable to reside in their own home due to illness + Read more ...

If the separation is temporary but not permanent or indefinite, the customer and their partner are not illness separated according to the definition. However they may be a respite care couple and entitled to the higher single rate of payment. See Customer is separated from their partner due to respite care.

If the customer advises they are temporarily living away from their principal home and they are not illness separated or a respite care couple, they may be a temporarily separated couple and entitled to a higher single rate of Rent Assistance. 3

Assess if the customer is a member of an illness separated couple + Read more ...

A couple is considered to be an illness separated couple where:

  • they are unable to live together in their home, and
  • the inability to live together:
    • is due to illness or infirmity of either or both of them, and
    • results in their living expenses being greater or are likely to be greater than otherwise, and is likely to continue indefinitely

Aspects to consider when assessing if the customer is a member of an illness separated couple:

  • Inability to live at home due to illness or infirmity:
    • it is not the intention that medical evidence is required for the majority of assessments
    • for entry to an aged care home, the RCA Institution (RIS) screen can be used to verify entry into residential care by checking Admission Date and Care Type: Residential
    • for a Supported Residential Service (SRS), a copy of the Residential Statement should be requested to confirm the level of care provided to the customer
    • retirement villages do not usually provide care. If the customer advises they are receiving care from a retirement village, a copy of the care plan should be requested
  • Living expenses:
    • ask the customer if their living expenses have increased or are likely to increase due to illness separation, noting that it is not the intention that evidence of the customer's living expenses pre and post illness separation be obtained
  • Indefinite separation:
    • determine if the illness is indefinite, noting that 'likely to continue indefinitely' generally means it is highly probable, based on available evidence that the term of the separation is for a period of time that is unknown or unstated

Is there sufficient information to assess the illness separation?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 9

4

There is sufficient information to assess the illness separation + Read more ...

When assessing the customer's circumstances it may become apparent that the customer is not simply an illness separated couple but they may be either living separately and apart due to 1 member of a couple being institutionalised due to a severe and debilitating illness or they are genuinely living separately and apart on a permanent or indefinite basis.

  • Living separately and apart due to 1 member of a couple being institutionalised due to a severe and debilitating illness. An assessment of living separately and apart in this circumstance can only be made if:
    • the incapacity of the institutionalised partner is severe, debilitating and of a permanent nature, for example, Huntington's disease, advanced stages of Alzheimer's disease, and
    • the institutionalised partner is totally and permanently incapable of providing their partner with such things as companionship, comfort, physical, intellectual or emotional support
    • evidence of the permanent or indefinite separation must be provided from the nursing home or approved institution, together with medical evidence on the type and severity of the illness
    • separation can be a sensitive topic for some customers, particularly elderly couples who may not be able to emotionally disconnect from their partner, regardless of the financial impacts. If a customer still strongly believes and states that they are a member of a couple with their partner, the records are not to be unlinked. In these situations, the customer and partner will still be considered to be an illness separated couple
  • Genuinely living separately and apart on a permanent or indefinite basis as there has been an estrangement or breakdown in their relationship - this requirement must consider both a physical and emotional separation between the customer and their partner

Considerations when assessing a customer as living separately and apart due to 1 member of a couple being institutionalised due to a severe and debilitating illness

  • 'Living separately and apart on a permanent or indefinite basis' should not generally be accepted if the couple are living apart solely due to ill health. All other options should be explored before it has been determined that the couple are living separately and apart. References has links to the Social Security Guide
  • Careful consideration must be given to correctly assess the customer's circumstances as it directly impacts on their entitlements and rate of income support payment
  • Care must be taken to ensure the consequences of the decision when assessing a customer as living separately and apart due to 1 member of a couple being institutionalised due to a severe and debilitating illness are discussed with the customer prior to unlinking. This may include consultation with the FIS Officer

The decision to assess a couple as Living separately and apart - 1 member of a couple institutionalised due to a severe and debilitating illness, as outlined above, should only be undertaken if:

  • the couple's circumstances have been fully investigated, and
  • the couple do not meet the overall 5 factors that determine a member of a couple as per the Social Security Guide. References has a link to the Social Security Guide, 1.1.M.120, Member of a couple, and
  • all scenarios and effects of the income / assets tests have been explored and explained for now, for 2 years in the future and beyond that

Resources has examples of the effect on the income and asset tests for a customer assessed as living separately and apart.

5

Make determination + Read more ...

The customer's circumstances indicate that:

  • the customer is not a member of an illness separated couple, go to Step 6
  • the customer is a member of an illness separated couple, go to Step 7
  • the customer is living separately and apart due to 1 member of a couple being institutionalised due to a severe and debilitating illness,
    go to Step 8
  • the customer is genuinely living separately and apart. That is, the customer is living apart on a permanent or indefinite basis as there has been an estrangement or breakdown in their relationship. See Change in relationship from partnered to single

6

The customer is not a member of an illness separated couple + Read more ...

  • Advise the customer:
    • they do not meet the requirements of an illness separated couple and do not qualify for payment at the single rate
    • to contact the agency if their circumstances change
    • their review and appeal rights
  • Update customer/partner record if required, for example, address details
  • Record the decision on a DOC
  • Issue an Illness Separation decision - unfavourable (Q888) letter to advise the customer in writing of the decision. Note: there is no requirement to issue a decision letter to a customer where the assessment is about a new claim. The new claim grant/reject letter forms part of the illness separated decision

Procedure ends here.

7

The customer is a member of an illness separated couple + Read more ...

Where the customer is assessed to be a member of an illness separated couple, Service Officers must advise the customer to supply details of their or their partner's accommodation arrangements.
Note: for entry to an aged care home, the RCA Institution (RIS) screen can be used to verify entry into residential care by checking Admission Date and Care Type: Residential.

This includes:

  • proof of admission
  • date home was vacated
  • any verification of rent paid (if residing in private accommodation)

See Step 1 in Table 2.

8

Customer is living separately and apart due to 1 member of a couple being institutionalised due to a severe and debilitating illness + Read more ...

Under the provisions of living separately and apart (on the grounds of 1 member of a couple being institutionalised due to a severe and debilitating illness), the individuals are deemed to be single and paid a single rate of income support payment. The single income and assets test provisions will apply to the individuals assessed as living separately and apart.
Note: if the customer entering care is considered to be living separately and apart and, regardless of whether the partner remains in the former principal home, the incapacitated person's share (as either tenant-in-common or joint tenants) of the principal home continues to be an exempt asset for up to 2 years, or longer if further exemptions are met. See Vacation of principal home due to illness.

If it is determined the customer and the partner are no longer a member of a couple:

  • for Parenting Payment Partnered customers, they may be eligible to transfer to Parenting Payment Single (PPS). See Claiming Parenting Payment (PP)
  • for other income support customers, in the customer's record, update the Marital Status (MS) screen:
    • enter 'MS' in Next: field to access new screen for updating
    • Event Date: field, enter date the change to relationship status occurred
    • Marital Status: field, enter a single marital status code
    • PPE Cust and PPE Ptnr: fields, leave blank
    • Qualifier Code: field, leave blank
    • Source: field, enter the source of information
    • Receipt Date: field, enter the date of receipt
    • select Continue to navigate to the Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen
    • update any other information by selecting the appropriate screens from the CDTS screen, for example, change of address, change of payment destination

Is the customer or partner currently in Residential Aged Care (RCA/CUR)?

  • Yes, additional coding on the RCAA screen is required before finalising the activity, go to Step 14
  • No:
    • Navigate to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity
    • Record outcome and reasons for decision on a DOC
    • Issue an Illness Separation decision - favourable (Q888) letter to advise the customer in writing of the decision. Note: there is no requirement to issue a decision letter to a customer where the assessment is about a new claim. The new claim grant/reject letter forms part of the illness separated decision
    • Procedure ends here

Note: Partner Allowance (PA) will automatically cancel once the MS screen is updated with a singular marital status code. PA customers will need to test their eligibility for a different payment if they still require income support. For example JobSeeker Payment.

9

Inadequate information to assess illness separation + Read more ...

Make an outbound attempt to contact the customer by phone to request the customer to provide supporting evidence from a treating doctor or relevant health professional.

Was the outbound contact to the customer successful?

  • Yes, create a closed DOC detailing the following:
    • 'The customer has been requested to provide supporting evidence from a treating doctor or relevant health professional. The customer can provide this by uploading it to their online account or by visiting a service centre. The customer has been advised that if they do not provide these details within 14 days, they will continue to be paid at the partnered rate. This request has been made under Section 63 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999'
    • code a manual review on the RVR for 14 days in the future, adding keyword ILLSEP to ensure the return of evidence is followed up
  • No, issue a Request to contact (Q164) letter via Online Advice
    • create a closed DOC, detailing the issue of the Q164. Include instructions in the DOC to request evidence when the customer responds to the request to contact letter
    • code a manual review adding keyword ILLSEP to mature after an allowance for mail delivery has been added

Pending the return of evidence for the illness separation, it's possible the customer may be living in separate accommodation and eligible for rent assistance.

See also: Temporary accommodation and Rent Assistance to determine if the customer is eligible to receive a higher rate of RA.

If the customer moved and it has been determined they are eligible to receive a higher rate of RA, go to Updating address details.

Procedure ends here.

11

Unfavourable decision - customer is not a member of an illness separated couple + Read more ...

Where there isn't supporting evidence to grant the illness separated rate, the customer is to continue to be paid the partnered rate.

  • When an unfavourable decision is to be made, a Decision Maker (DM) must provide the customer with an explanation of the decision. The DM must also advise the customer of their review rights and if required, record a request for a review of the decision
  • If the customer is not present at the time of the updates, Service Officers must make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer by phone prior to issuing written advice of the decision outcome
  • Once an attempt to contact the customer has been successful and they have been advised of the decision or 2 genuine attempts have been made, record the decision on a DOC
  • Issue an Illness Separation decision - unfavourable (Q888) letter to advise the customer in writing of the decision. Note: there is no requirement to issue a decision letter to a customer where the assessment is about a new claim. The new claim grant/reject letter forms part of the illness separated decision
  • Close all relevant open activities/work items on the customer's record

Procedure ends here.

12

Customer advises they will be a member of an illness separated couple in the future + Read more ...

If a customer advises their relationship status will change at a future date:

  • do not code the change
  • advise the customer:
    • of general 14 day notification provisions and that they will to contact the agency as soon as the change of relationship status occurs
    • if the change results in an increase to payment rate, the increase is effective from the date Centrelink is advised. This includes where the customer notifies of the change within 14 days. For further information, see Notification Handler (NOHL)
  • document the record outlining the discussion

Procedure ends here.

13

Work item in relation to reviewing the customer's illness separation rate + Read more ...

Service Officers can use discretion to determine if there is the possibility a change of circumstances may occur and set a manual review if deemed appropriate. For example, where the customer enters a rehabilitation facility and the length of time is not known and therefore indefinite.

When the review activity matures, check the customer's record to determine the reason for review and confirm whether the customer is still eligible for the illness separated rate. Use Step 3 to help check if they meet the illness separated criteria.

Is the customer eligible to continue to be paid the illness separated rate?

  • Yes, document the record outlining reason for decision:
    • Issue an Illness Separation decision - favourable (Q888) letter to advise the customer in writing of the decision. Note: there is no requirement to issue a decision letter to a customer where the assessment is about a new claim. The new claim grant/reject letter forms part of the illness separated decision
    • Set a Manual Review, adding keyword ILLSEP, to review the customer's circumstances at a future date if required
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, see Step 8 in Table 2

14

Residential Care Assessment (RCA) customer/partner now deemed to be single + Read more ...

If a customer who is currently in residential Aged Care had a partner living in their former principal home, it would be exempt from being counted as an asset for Aged Care Means Test Assessment (MTA). If determination has been made that a customer in residential care is now single, this exemption will no longer apply. Further updates are critical to ensure Aged Care fees remain correct.

If customer or partner are currently in residential Aged Care:

  • Check the RCA Institution (RIS) screen and confirm the Admission Date and Care Type: Residential
  • Complete updates on RCAA screen including:
    • Date of Effect: enter date the change to relationship status occurred
    • Date of Entry into care: delete previous date from this field as coding a duplicate date of entry will delete previous data
    • Home Ownership Status: update to HOM, NHO or HRV
    • CMV of principal Home: current market value of principal home if home now assessed
    • % Owned C/P: update ownership percentage of principal home
    • Action: 'I' to insert a new page
  • For assistance with coding, contact Aged Care (RCA) team:
    • Select Office Locator
    • Select Notes
    • Contact Access/Tech Peer Support number
  • Navigate to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity
  • Record outcome and reasons for decision on a DOC
  • Issue an Illness Separation decision - favourable (Q888) letter to advise the customer in writing of the decision. Note: there is no requirement to issue a decision letter to a customer where the assessment is about a new claim. The new claim grant/reject letter forms part of the illness separated decision

Procedure ends here.

Step

Action

1

Customer assessed as illness separated - MS screen update + Read more ...

When it has been established the customer and their partner are an illness separated couple, Service Officers will need to update the MS screen with the appropriate relationship qualifier code. This is done to ensure customers entitlements are treated differently to their recorded relationship status.

To update the MS screen:

  • enter 'MS' in Next: field to access new screen for updating
  • Event Date: field, enter the date the customer became illness separated
  • Marital Status: field will remain as MAR, REG or DEF
  • PPE Cust and PPE Ptnr: fields, update as required
  • Qualifier Code: field, enter the appropriate illness separated relationship qualifier code:
    • Ill Sep / Both Left Home (ILB), where both the customer and partner have left home due to illness
    • Ill Sep / Left Home (ILC), where the customer only left home due to illness
    • Ill Sep / Ptnr Left Home (ILP) , where the partner only left home due to illness
  • Source: field, enter the source of the information
  • Receipt Date field, enter the date of receipt. Note: the date of receipt is the date that Centrelink is advised of the change. See Notification Handler (NOHL) for further information, if required
  • select Continue to navigate to the Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen
  • update any other information by selecting the appropriate screens from the CDTS screen, including the Address Summary (AD) and the Accommodation Details (AC) screens. The AD and AC screens will be presented for 1 'S' or both 'B' partners (dependant on whether only 1 or both members of a couple have moved)
  • navigate to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity
  • record outcome and reasons for decision on a DOC

Note: after entering the appropriate 'illness separated' code on the MS screen, the records of the customer and their partner will continue to be linked and correctly display as 'member of a couple'.

2

Establish if the customer or their partner are in aged care + Read more ...

Has the customer or their partner entered an Aged Care facility or are in hospital awaiting admission to an aged care home?

  • Yes, update the customer's or their partner's address details as required
    • For help see Step 8 in Table 1 in Change of address to an aged care home
    • Go to Step 6 to finalise the activity and issue letter
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Address Details (AD) screen + Read more ...

Select the address(es) from the AD screen that require updating

  • Address Type: field, select the appropriate address type. Select Continue
  • Start Date: field, enter the start date of the address (any date up to 14 days in the future for POS or HOM)
  • Make partner's address the same as customers?: field, update as required
  • Is RA being claimed on this Address: field, update as required
  • Is this address in a Remote Area for the purposes of Remote Area Allowance?: field, update if prompted. The value in this field will default to 'Yes' or 'No' based on the postcode entered. If the postcode spans both remote and non remote areas, the message 'Please indicate if address is remote or not' displays. Enter either 'Y' or 'N'
  • Send All Mail Here?: field, update if prompted. This field is only relevant for the TEM and TER address types
  • code the customer's new address, see Rapid Addressing
  • Source: field, enter the source of information
  • Receipt Date: field, enter the date of receipt
  • select Continue

For more information on changing a customer's address, see Updating address details.

4

Accommodation Details (AC) screen + Read more ...

Illness separated customers who own their principal home and enter a care situation are not ineligible homeowners for Rent Assistance (RA) purposes. They may be entitled to RA if paying rent at their new address. See Rent Assistance (RA) for care receivers.

If the customer was a home owner, assess the treatment of their principal home, vacated due to illness.

The Address Type: field should have defaulted from the AD screen. If not, enter the address type.

  • Event Date: field, enter the date of event for the accommodation details that are being recorded
  • Home ownership: field, update if required. If the customer is a homeowner, code as 'LHO' (lives elsewhere)
  • Type of Rent: field, enter the type of rent paid
  • Sharer status: field, code 'EXE' or 'XSH'
  • Rent Amount: field, update the amount of rent paid and frequency
  • Rent Verification: field, update the verification details. See Rent Assistance (RA) verification
  • Reason Home vacated: field, enter the reason home was vacated. Suitable codes are, 'CRR' - to provide care in a private home or 'ONH' - due to ill health (other than in nursing home)
  • Date Home vacated: field, enter the date the home was vacated
  • Source: field, enter the source of information
  • Receipt Date: field, enter the date of receipt
  • Select Continue
  • navigate to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity
  • Record details on a DOC

5

Does the customer have children? + Read more ...

  • Yes, check if they need to revise their current Families income estimate and advise choices available to help reduce the chance of an overpayment during the reconciliation process. See Updating income estimates for the current financial year. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Finalise the activity and issue a decision letter Q888 + Read more ...

Advise the customer of the following:

  • that their new rate of payment (including any RA) and that the partnered income/assets test still applies. The illness separated rate is equivalent to the single rate of payment. For:
    • rate information, see Legislation and Payment rates and thresholds
    • details about the income/assets test see
      Income Test for illness separated or partner in prison customers
      Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) Income and assets test
      Allowance income and assets test
  • that should their circumstances change and they are no longer illness separated, they must inform the agency within 14 days of their circumstances changing
  • that Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part B will still be assessed on the income of the lower earner. See Income test for family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payments

Set a Manual Review to review the customer's circumstances at a future date if required. Add keyword ILLSEP. As it is a requirement that the inability to live together is likely to continue indefinitely, there should generally be no need to review the customers circumstances at a future date. Service Officers can use discretion to determine if there is the possibility a change of circumstances may occur and set a manual review if deemed appropriate. For example, where the customer enters a rehabilitation and the length of time is not known and therefore indefinite.

Issue an Illness Separation decision - favourable (Q888) letter to advise the customer in writing of the decision. Note: there is no requirement to issue a decision letter to a customer where the assessment is about a new claim. The new claim grant/reject letter forms part of the illness separated decision.

7

Continuing entitlement + Read more ...

As a result of the customer and their partner being an illness separated couple, check that they are receiving appropriate income support payments, and are aware of outcomes and obligations.

For example:

  • Would the customer who is ill qualify for Disability Support Pension, JobSeeker Payment (incapacitated)?
  • If both members of a couple reside in the same accommodation and 1 partner receives Carer Payment (CP) or Carer Allowance (CA) because they are caring for the other partner. See Change of address for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA)
  • If the partner is on Carer Payment or Carer Allowance, it may be necessary to advise of the cessation of care rules for temporary or long term periods. See:
    • Respite provisions for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA)
    • Hospitalisation provisions for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA)

Procedure ends here.

8

Customer is no longer illness separated + Read more ...

Has the change of circumstances already occurred?

  • Yes, the record will need to be updated to remove the illness separated relationship qualifier code. Any other details as advised by the customer will also need to be updated. On the Marital Status (MS) screen, record the following details:
    • Enter 'MS' in Next: field to access new screen for updating
    • Event Date: field, enter the date the customer ceased being illness separated
    • Marital Status: field, use the same code as previously stated
    • PPE Cust and PPE Ptnr: fields, update as required
    • Qualifier Code: field, leave blank
    • Source: field, enter the source of information
    • Receipt Date: field, enter the date of receipt
    • Select Continue to navigate to the Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen
    • Update any other information by selecting the appropriate screens from the CDTS screen, for example, change of address. Ensure the customer's and partner's home address are the same
    • Navigate to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity
    • Record outcome and reasons for decision on a DOC
    • Issue an Illness Separation decision - unfavourable (Q888) letter to advise the customer in writing of the decision. Note: there is no requirement to issue a decision letter to a customer where the assessment is about a new claim. The new claim grant/reject letter forms part of the illness separated decision
  • No, as the customer is advising of the change of circumstances at a future date:
    • do not code the change. The system will not allow service officers to code a change for a future date and the E116CU – Event Date cannot be greater than today's date error will appear
    • advise the customer of general 14 day notification provisions and that they will need to contact the agency when the change of relationship status occurs
    • set a Manual Review, adding keyword ILLSEP, to review the customer's circumstances at a future date to follow up change in circumstances as advised by the customer
    • record a DOC outlining the discussion
