2

Refusal to pay + Read more ...

If a customer refuses to pay their debt, find out why and offer a solution. For example:

  • advise their review and appeal rights
  • reduced payments, or
  • no payments for a period

If the customer still refuses to pay, explain the recovery action that may take place under cost effectiveness guidelines.

Do not threaten the customer with recovery action. This can escalate the situation.

Is the customer still refusing to enter into a reasonable payment arrangement?

  • Yes:
    • Record the contact using the Unsuccessful Engagement refusal to pay Fast Note. Include details of the discussion and warnings given to the customer
    • Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • Record the contact using the Successful Engagement Fast Note
    • Go to Step 3

Note: do not close any Fast Note(s) automatically left open.

3

Instalment amount + Read more ...

Ask the customer how much they can afford to pay by regular instalments. The customer must make an offer based on their capacity to pay. The aim is to recover the debt in the shortest possible time, without placing the customer in financial hardship.

Do not tell the customer the minimum:

  • acceptable amount, or
  • acceptable period

Self-employed customers that do not get a regular income from a third party must complete a financial circumstances assessment. The financial circumstances assessment is for their personal income and expenses, not the business.

Is the instalment rate at or above the acceptable cash level?

  • Yes, accept the customer's offer. Go to Step 4
  • No, tell the customer a Financial Circumstances assessment is needed to accept their offer. Note: a customer may increase their offer rather than complete a financial circumstances assessment. Accept the increased offer if it meets the acceptable cash level.

4

Payment method + Read more ...

Ask the customer which payment method they would like.

For:

  • Direct debit - The maximum direct debt amount is $500,000 for any frequency. See Voluntary deduction (direct debit) arrangements to recover debts

For all other payment methods, give the customer the payment details:

  • Internet - The customer can make payments via the Money You Owe option online within a Centrelink online account or via Express Plus Centrelink mobile app. They can use a debit or credit card.
    • To help a customer set up their myGov account, see How customers create a myGov account and link services
  • BPAY® - The customer can make payments with BPAY® through their financial institution.
    • Use Biller Code 21915
    • If the customer's financial institution allows a BPAY® credit card payment there may be additional fees or interest charges. They should check this with their financial institution before they make the payment
  • Australia Post - The customer can make payments at any Australian post office or postal outlet
    • They can pay via EFTPOS or cash, using the bar-code on their letter or payment card. The letter can be re-printed
    • A payment card may take up to 2 weeks to issue
  • POSTbillpay - This is a phone or online payment made via Australia POSTbillpay
    • Use POSTbillpay code: 0802
  • Third party withholdings - only available where the customer asks for them. See Third party withholdings for debt recovery
  • Mail - Make cheques payable to the 'Collector of Public Money'. Post the cheque to the return address on the top left of their Account Payable. For contact details, see Collector of Public Money. Cheques are not a preferred payment option

5

Date to commence payments + Read more ...

Ask the customer when they can start to make the payments. Payments should align with the customers pay cycle (regular instalments) or when the funds are available (lump sum).

The start date must:

  • be within 1 month - Do not make an arrangement that starts more than 1 month in the future. The customer will continue to accrue Interest Charge (IC) until they make the first payment. Customers can also use this as a method to avoid other recovery action such as a tax garnishee or forget about the payment if it is too far into the future
  • start after the due date of a debt - If an arrangement start date is before the due date of a debt, and a payment that is due before the due date is insufficient or not paid:
    • a 'Payment Not Received' letter does not issue
    • a broken arrangement activity does not generate
    • Interest Charge (IC) applies from the due date for non-current customers

6

Discuss commitment made to pay debt + Read more ...

Repeat the details of the arrangement including the:

  • amount
  • date, and
  • method

Confirm the terms of their payment arrangement. For example:

'Today you have entered into a verbal agreement to repay this debt. It is important you keep to the terms of the agreement you have made today. If you are unable to make a payment at any stage, please contact us straight away so can we help you.'

Staff must tell the customer about the consequences of not keeping to their payment arrangement. This includes telling them about the recovery actions that may take place under the cost effectiveness guidelines:

Do not threaten the customer with recovery action. This can escalate the situation.

2

Set up an irregular cash payment arrangement + Read more ...

For any lump sum payments, including payment in full.

  • In the Create new arrangement type field, key 'IRR' and press [Enter]
  • Complete the below fields on the Irregular Cash Payments (OPIC) screen:
    • Agreed Date - the date the cash agreement is made. This must be today's date or a date not more than 7 days in the past
    • Amount $
    • Expected Date - the date the irregular cash payment is due. This must be today's date or later
    • Comments - (if necessary)
    • Press [Enter]
  • Go to the Assessment Finalisation (OPAF) screen:
    • Key 'Y' in the Finished? field
    • Press [Enter]
    • If the activity is to be Quality On Line (QOL) checked, key 'Q' in the Finished? field
  • The customer will get a cash arrangement confirmation letter. This will confirm the details of the arrangement
  • Record the details on a DOC and include:
    • the Section of the Act that covers the decision
    • the balance of the outstanding debt
    • the irregular cash payment amounts
    • the dates the irregular cash payments will be made
    • the payment method. For example, at a post office with a payment card
    • the customer's employment details (name and location). Record if the customer is unwilling to provide employment details
    • gross income, as detailed in acceptable cash levels
    • specific details of any warnings given
    • any other relevant details from the discussion

Is the customer going to make a regular instalment arrangement also?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, procedure ends here

3

Set up regular cash payments + Read more ...

  • In the Create new arrangement type field, key 'CSH' and press [Enter]
  • Complete the below fields on the Regular Cash Payments (OPRC) screen:
    • Agreed Date - the date the cash agreement is made. This must be today's date or a date not more than 7 days in the past
    • Pay Start Date - the date the first payment of the regular cash arrangement is due
    • Amount $
    • Frequency
    • Comments - (if necessary)
  • Press [Enter]
  • Go to the Assessment Finalisation (OPAF) screen:
    • Key 'Y' in the Finished? field
    • Press [Enter]
    • If the activity is to be Quality On Line (QOL) checked, key 'Q' in the Finished? field
  • The customer will get a cash arrangement confirmation letter. It will confirm the details of the arrangement
  • Record the details on a DOC and include:
    • the Section of the Act that covers the decision
    • the payment amount offered
    • the balance of the debt outstanding
    • the regular cash instalment amount agreed to
    • the date the instalments are to start
    • the payment method. For example, Centrelink online account or Express Plus Centrelink mobile app
    • the customer's employer details (name and location). Document if the customer is unwilling to provide employment details
    • gross income, as detailed in acceptable cash level
    • specific details of warnings of other recovery action
    • any other relevant details from the discussion

Go to Step 4.
