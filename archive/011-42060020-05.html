Surgeons not operating independently

Where 1 surgeon assists the other or the surgeons both assist each other, the surgeons cease to be operating independently and it ceases to be considered as conjoint surgery.

In this case, if there is:

  • an item number of '1 surgeon' (for example, item 30536), the principal surgeon claims the normal schedule item that covers the service and the co-surgeon claims as assistant fee
  • no item for '1 surgeon', for example, item 45564 and 45565, or conjoint surgery is covered by only 1 item, for example, item 41575 (removal by 2 surgeons), the entire claim is subject to the multiple operation rule and only 1 assistance fee can be paid. The surgeons involved would need to sort out payment arrangements between themselves

Assistance at caesarean section (items 51306 and 51309)

There are 2 specific items that provide benefits for assistance at a caesarean section delivery. The general rules applying to assistant surgeon benefits apply to these items.

  • 51306 - Assistance at a delivery involving a Caesarean section. The item has a set fee and benefit
  • 51309 - Assistance at a series or combination of operations which are identified by the word 'Assist' and assistance at a delivery involving Caesarean section

Where all the services that were performed in addition to the caesarean section are eligible for benefit for an assistant fee, the fees are calculated in the following manner:

Example A

  • Caesarean - Schedule fee $800
  • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $200
  • Operation B (Assist) - Schedule fee $100
  • Calculate the fee for the operations by applying the multiple operation rule as follows:
    • Caesarean - $8000 x 100% = $800
    • Operation A (Assist) - $200 x 100% - $200
    • Operation B (Assist) - $100 x 50% = $50
    • Total Schedule fee - $1050
  • Calculate the derived fee for item 51309, that is, 20% of the total Schedule fee
    • $1050 x 20% = $210

Note: these are example figures only - access MBS for the current schedule fees.

Where not all the services that were performed in addition to the Caesarean section are eligible for an assistant fee, the claim should be assessed in the following manner:

Example B

  • Caesarean - Schedule fee $800
  • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $500
  • Operation B - Schedule fee $300.00
  • Operation C (Assist) - Schedule fee $100

Note: the surgeon's fee is obtained by basing the caesarean section at 100% of the Schedule fee and then applying the multiple operation rule to the remaining items as follows:

  • Caesarean - $800 x 100%
  • Operation A - $500 x 100%
  • Operation B - $300.00 x 50%
  • Operation C - $100 x 25%
  • To obtain the assistant's fee, reapply the multiple operation rule but only to those services for which an assistant fee is payable:
    • Caesarean - $800 x 100% = $800
    • Operation A (Assist) - $500 x 100% = $500
    • Operation C (Assist) - $100 x 50% = $50
    • Total schedule fee = $1350
  • Calculate the derived fee for item 51309, that is, 20% of the total Schedule fee
    • $1350 x 20% = $270

Note: all figures are only examples - access MBS for the current schedule fees.

Assistance at interventional obstetric services (item 51312)

This item provides benefits for assistance at the following interventional obstetric services included under Group T4 of the MBS:

  • 16606 - fetal blood sampling
  • 16609 - fetal intravascular blood transfusion
  • 16612 - fetal intraperitoneal blood transfusion
  • 16615 - fetal intraperitoneal blood transfusion
  • 16627 - feto-amniotic shunt
  • 16633 - procedure on multiple pregnancies relating to items 16606 to 16615 and 16627

Benefits are limited to these interventional items, and are payable as a derived fee, that is, 20% of the fee for the procedure or combination of procedures at which assistance was rendered.

Assistance at oral and maxillofacial services

Assistance rendered by a medical practitioner (items 51300 and 51303)

Benefits are payable under these items for assistance at an operation listed in Category 4 of the MBS (oral and maxillofacial services by approved dental practitioners) as long as the assistance is rendered by a medical practitioner.

Assistance rendered by an approved dental practitioner (items 51800 and 51803)

Where the assistance is rendered by an approved dental practitioner (who is not also a medical practitioner), benefit is payable under these items in Group O2 - Assistance at Operation of the MBS. The general rules applying to assistant surgeon benefits apply to these items.

Levels of benefit (assistance at operations)

There are 2 levels of benefit payable for assistance at an operation or combination of operations under items 51300 and 51303.

  • 51300 - Assistance at any operation identified by the word 'Assist' for which the fee does not exceed $558.30 or at a series or combination of operations identified by the word 'Assist' where the fee for the series or combination of operations identified by the word 'Assist' does not exceed $558.30
  • 51303 - Assistance at any operation identified by the word 'Assist' for which the fee exceeds $558.30 or at a series of operations identified by the word 'Assist' for which the aggregate fee exceeds $558.30

Note: these are example figures only - access MBS for the current schedule fees.

A number of examples are included to show how to calculate assistant surgeon benefits.

Single operation

Example A

  • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $250
  • A benefit is payable, for assistance under item 51300, as the fee for the operation does not exceed $558.30

Example B

  • Operation B (No assist) - Schedule fee $300
  • No benefit is payable for assistance, as the item description does not include the word 'Assist'

Note: these are example figures only - access MBS for the current schedule fees.

Multiple operations

Where multiple operations are performed and all the services are eligible for an assistant fee, the multiple operation rule applies. As outlined below, the aggregated total Schedule fee determines if an assistant fee is payable under item 51300 or 51303.

Note: the total Schedule fee for assistance at operation in respect of multiple procedures must be calculated after the application of the multiple operations rule.

Example A

  • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $250
  • Operation B (Assist) - Schedule fee $80
  • Calculate the fee for the operations:
    • Operation A (Assist) - $250 x 100% = $250
    • Operation B (Assist) - $80 x 50% = $40
    • Total Schedule fee = $290
  • Check which assistant item applies, based on the total Schedule fee for the operations
  • Item 51300 applies as the total Schedule fee for the operations does not exceed $558.30

Example B

  • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $400
  • Operation B (Assist) - Schedule fee $250
  • Operation C (Assist) - Schedule fee $200
  • Calculate the fee for the operations:
    • Operation A (Assist) - $400 x 100% = $400
    • Operation B (Assist) - $250 x 50% - $125
    • Operation C (Assist) - $200 x 25% = $50
    • Total Schedule fee = $575
  • Check which assistant item applies, based on the total Schedule fee for the operations
  • Item 51303 applies as the total Schedule fee for the operations exceeds $558.30
  • Calculate the derived fee for item 51303, that is, 20% - of the total Schedule fee for operations A, B and C
  • $575 x 20% = $115

Note: these are example figures only - access MBS for the current schedule fees.

Multiple operations where not all services are eligible for an assistance fee

In some cases of multiple operations where assistance is given, some of the services are not eligible for an assistant fee because they do not have the word 'Assist' in their item description. In this situation, the multiple operation rule is applied to all the operations in the usual way, to determine the surgeon's fee. To determine the assistant's fee, the multiple operation formula is then re-applied but only to those services that are eligible for an assistant fee and at which assistance is rendered.

Example

  • Multiple operation rule - Surgeon
    • Item A - $300 x 100%
    • Item B - $250 x 50%
    • Item C - $200 x 25%
    • Item D - $150 x 25%
  • Multiple operation rule - Assistant
    • Item A (Assist) - $300 x 100%
    • Item B (No Assist)
    • Item C (Assist) - $200 x50%
    • Item D (Assist) - $150 x 25%
  • The resultant aggregate fee determines if item 51300 or item 51303 applies to the assistant fee

Note: these are example figures only - access MBS for the current schedule fees.

2 or more surgeons operating - surgeons operating independently

Where 2 or more surgeons operate on the same patient on the same occasion and they do not:

  • assist the other surgeons
  • administer, or assist in the administration of, the anaesthetic

they are deemed to be operating independently.

The procedures they perform are considered as separate operations, and if any of them employs an assistant, the assistant fees are calculated separately for the services of each surgeon.

See Surgical operations assessing rules in Medicare for more information.

The following provides examples of various scenarios of surgeons operating independently.

Example A

Dr Steve and Dr Michael operate independently on a patient on the same occasion. Dr Ted assists Dr Steve. All of the items at which assistance is given are eligible for an assistant fee.

  • Dr Steve
    • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $500
    • Operation B (Assist) - Schedule fee $450
    • Operation C (Assist) - Schedule fee $200
    • Assisted by Dr Ted
  • Dr Michael
    • Operation 1 - Schedule fee $300
    • Operation 2 - Schedule fee $150

Apply the multiple operation rule to the services that are eligible for an assistant fee and at which assistance is rendered.

  • Dr Steve assisted by Dr Ted
    • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $500 x 100% = $500
    • Operation B (Assist) - Schedule fee $450 x 50% = $225
    • Operation C (Assist) - Schedule fee $200 x 25% - $50
    • Total Schedule fee = $775

Compare the total Schedule fee to items 51300 and 51303 to determine which assistant item applies to Dr Ted's services.

  • Item 51303 applies because the total Schedule fee for the operations exceeds $558.30

Calculate the derived fee for item 51303, that is, 20% of the total schedule fee for operations A, B and C.

  • $775 x 20% = $155

Note: these are example figures only - access MBS for the current schedule fees.

Example B

Dr Steve and Dr Michael operate independently on a patient on the same occasion. Dr Ted assists Dr Steve. Not all of the items at which assistance is given are eligible for an assistant fee.

  • Dr Steve
    • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $600
    • Operation B - Schedule fee $540
    • Operation C (Assist) - Schedule fee $320
    • Operation D - Schedule fee $250
    • Assisted by Dr Ted
  • Dr Michael
    • Operation 1 - Schedule fee $340
    • Operation 2 - Schedule fee $100
  • To calculate the fee for the operations, the multiple operation rule is applied as follows:
    • Dr Steve assisted by Dr Ted
    • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $600 x 100% = $600
    • Operation B (No assist) - $0
    • Operation C (Assist) - Schedule fee $320 x 50% = $160.00
  • Operation D (No assist) - $0
  • Total Schedule fee = $760

Compare the total Schedule fee to items 51300 and 51303 to determine which assistant item applies to Dr Brown's services.

  • Item 51303 applies because the total Schedule fee for the operations exceeds $537.15

Calculate the derived fee for item 51303, that is, 20% of the total Schedule fee for operations A and C.

  • $760 x 20% = $152

Note: these are example figures only - access MBS for the current schedule fees.

Example C

Dr Steve and Dr Michael operate independently on a patient on the same occasion and, although each employs the same assistant, the 2 surgeons are still deemed to be operating independently. All of the items at which assistance is given are eligible for an assistant fee.

  • Dr Steve
    • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $450
    • Operation B (Assist) - Schedule fee $260
    • Operation C (Assist) - Schedule fee $200

  • Dr Michael

  • Dr Steve assisted by Dr Ted
    • Operation A - $450 x 100% = $450
    • Operation B - $260 x 50% = $130
    • Operation C - $200 x 25% = $50
    • Total Schedule fee = $630
  • Dr Michael assisted by Dr Ted
    • Operation 1 - $200 x 100% = $200
    • Operation 2 - $200 x 50% = $100

  • Dr Ted assisting Dr Michael

Multiple surgeons not operating independently

Where 2 or more surgeons operate and each performs 1 or more operations but 1 assists the other, or they both assist each other, the surgeons are not considered to be operating independently. The multiple operation rule is applied to all the operations combined. The assessment of the assistant's fee is based on the fee for the combined operations.

Example A

Dr Steve and Dr Michael each perform a series of operations on a patient on the same occasion. Dr Michael also assists Dr Steve. Each of Dr Steve’s services is eligible for an assistant fee.

  • Dr Steve
    • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $500
    • Operation B (Assist) - Schedule fee $400
    • Operation C (Assist) - Schedule fee $300
    • Operation D (Assist) - Schedule fee $200
    • Assisted by Dr Michael
  • Dr Michael
    • Operation 1 - Schedule fee $450
    • Operation 2 - Schedule fee $100
    • Dr Michael assisting Dr Steve
    • Assisted by Dr Steve

To calculate the fee for the operations, apply the multiple operation rule as follows:

  • Dr Steve and Dr Michael
    • Operation A - Schedule fee $500 x 100%
    • Operation 1 - Schedule fee $450 x 50%
    • Operation B - Schedule fee $400 x 25%
    • Operation C - Schedule fee $300 x 25%
    • Operation D - Schedule fee $200 x 25%
    • Operation 2 - Schedule fee $100 x 25%

To calculate the fee for the assistant, reapply the multiple operation rule only to those services that are eligible for an assistant fee, and at which assistance is rendered:

  • Dr Steve assisted by Dr Michael
    • Operation A (Assist) - Schedule fee $500 x 100% = $500
    • Operation B (Assist) - Schedule fee $400 x 50% = $200
    • Operation C (Assist) - Schedule fee $300 x 25% = $75
    • Operation D (Assist) - Schedule fee $200 x 25% = $50
    • Total Schedule fee = $825
  • Compare the total Schedule fee with items 51300 and 51303 to determine which assistant item applies
  • Dr Michael assisting Dr Steve
    • Item 51303 applies to the assistant services as the total Schedule fee for the operations exceeds $558.30
  • Calculate the derived fee for item 51303, that is, 20% of the total Schedule fee - $825 x 20% = $165

Note: these are example figures only - access MBS for the current schedule fees.

Dr Steve and Dr Michael each perform a series of operations on a patient on the same occasion. Both surgeons assist each other.

    • Operation C (Assist) - Schedule fee $200

Assistance at cataract and intraocular lens surgery (items 51315 and 51318)

Benefits for item 51315 are payable for assistance at cataract and intraocular lens surgery covered by items 42698, 42701, 42702, 42704 or 42707, when performed in association with services covered by items 42551 to 42569, 42653, 42656, 42725, 42746, 42749, 42752, 42776 or 42779.

Items 51300 or 51303 are payable with item 51315 for items 42551 to 42569, 42653, 42656, 42725, 42746, 42749, 42752, 42776 or 42779 and any additional item that includes the word ('assist').

Benefit for item 51318 can be paid with 51300 or 51303 if both of the following apply:

  • item 42698, 42701, 42702, 42704 or 42707 is claimed by the surgeon and one of the conditions listed in item 51318 applies
  • another operation item is claimed by the surgeon which includes the word '(assist)' in its description
