Status of claim or automatic payment - customer calls to enquire

Step

Action

1

Customer calls to check the status of claim or payment (including an automatic payment) or to ask about providing identity document + Read more ...

Complete Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO). Do not give hints or prompts that help the customer answer the questions.

Check the customer's record in Customer First to find out if their claim has been:

  • granted (but not paid) - not an automatic payment:
    • in the customer's record, key 'DL' in the Next field to go to the Document List (DL) screen
    • if a claim has been granted the DL screen will show CUS CLM EXG FSP (DATE CLAIM GRANTED)
    • the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow will also show a status of Completed
    • go to Step 2
  • granted but not successfully delivered to the customer's bank account - this includes automatic payments:
    • check the payment has been granted
      - in the customer's record, key 'DL' in the Next field to go to the Document List (DL) screen
      - if a claim has been granted, the DL screen will show CUS CLM EXG FSP (DATE CLAIM GRANTED)
    • check if the payment has been returned
      - key 'PS' in the Next field to go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen
      - a payment returned by a financial institution shows as RTN
      - if the payment has been returned, see Replacing direct credit, New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) payments returned from financial institution
      - if the payment has not been returned, check the customer’s bank details. If the payment has not gone to the correct account, see Requesting the return of a direct credit, New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) payment made to an incorrect recipient
    • procedure ends here
  • pending rejection:
    • in the customer's record, the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow will show a status of Started
    • go to Step 3
  • not yet assessed:
    • in the customer's record, go to Workspace
    • select the Search Online Claims (Claims Online) workflow
    • when selected, the Search Criteria window will open
    • make sure the Claim ID or CRN is showing. If not, record it in the field and select Search
    • claims show in the Result List
    • the claim will have a category of Emergency Payment
    • go to Step 10
  • assessed, but specialist staff have tried to make outbound contact to clarify information (for example, payment nominee bank details) before finalising the claim
    • in the customer's record, key 'DL' in the Next field to go to the Document List (DL) screen
    • review any DOC that shows specialist staff have tried to contact the customer to discuss their COVID-19 Disaster Payment claim, but were not successful
    • go to Step 12

If the customer has called to:

  • ask about the decision made on their claim, go to Step 8
  • ask why they have not received an automatic payment or why their payment has reduced, go to Step 13
  • ask about a myGov message and/or SMS advising them automatic payments will stop due to coming out of a lockdown area, go to Step 14
  • return a payment, including an automatic payment, they have already received, go to Step 16
  • update their bank account details, go to Step 17
  • advise:
    • they previously tried to claim via phone or online, and
    • the claiming period for the relevant period has closed, depending on the payment the customer is trying to claim, see If a claiming period has closed in:
      Assessing COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims, or
      Assessing income support payment customers COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims
    • ask about providing identity documents, go to Step 18

2

Granted and not paid - not an automatic payment + Read more ...

If the customer has called to ask why they have not received an automatic payment, go to Step 13.

Contact Technical Peer Support.

Technical Peer Support must:

  • complete the payment query template
  • include the following in the payment query template:
    • Customer's CRN and name
    • Customer has claims for relevant period: (enter relevant period)
    • LGA: (enter LGA)
    • Event Code: (enter event code)

Do not follow the Escalation of claims table (as the claim has already been finalised, it cannot be selected for 'Follow-up').

Go to Step 11.

Pending rejection + Read more ...

If the customer:

  • does not want to update online claim information, go to Step 4
  • wants to update online claim information, go to Step 7

  • Tell the customer:
    • their claim has been assessed

    • they will receive a letter when their claim has been finalised

7

Pending rejection - customer wants to update online claim information + Read more ...

Staff cannot take a verbal change of response – do not accept them.

Tell the customer:

  • to withdraw their online claim and to resubmit it as soon as possible before the claiming period for the relevant period ends
    Note: for claiming period end dates, see ‘Timeframes for claiming’ in the Operational Blueprint for the event
  • if their claim is finalised (rejected) before they do this they will receive a letter
    • advising of the decision, and
    • how to request an explanation of the decision if they do not agree with the claim outcome

Check the customer's Document List (DL) screen for a COVID-19 DP Not Finalised DOC.

Is there a COVID-19 DP Not Finalised DOC?

  • Yes,
    • update the COVID-19 DP Not Finalised DOC with what was discussed with the customer
    • procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 11

8

Claim decision has been made + Read more ...

Check the decision made on the customer’s claim

On the customer's Customer Summary screen check the Benefit Status line

  • if a claim has been rejected, it will show EXG/REJ
  • if a claim has been granted, it will show EXG/CUR

The Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow will show a status of Completed

The Payment Summary (PS) screen will show the amount paid

The Document List (DL) screen will contain a DOC that outlines the reason for the decision

  • if a claim has been rejected, a
    • COVID-19 DP REJECTED Fast Note
  • if a claim has been granted, a
    • COVID-19 DP Granted PHONE Fast Note, or
    • COVID-19 DP Granted ONLINE Fast Note

If the customer wants to correct information in their online claim that has already been rejected, staff cannot take a verbal change of response.

Tell the customer:

  • if their claim has been granted, how much they were paid
  • if their claim has been rejected they do not meet the eligibility requirements
  • they will receive a letter that outlines the decision about their claim
  • if they disagree, they can request an explanation of the claim outcome
    • Note: if a customer wants to complain about a decision, see Level 1 - Manage customer complaints and feedback. Customer's complaints should be referred to the Level 2 Escalated Complaints team if they meet the customer complaints escalation reasons

Has the customer requested an explanation of the decision?

  • Yes, see COVID-19 Disaster Payment - review process
  • No, go to Step 9

  • To withdraw their online claim and to resubmit it as soon as possible
  • If their claim is finalised before they do this they will receive a letter. This will:
    • advise of the decision, and
    • say how to request an explanation of the decision if they do not agree with the claim outcome

Procedure ends here.

    • select Set New Status
    • a window will open in this page and provide a list of status options: select Completed
    • select Save, or the update to Completed will not occur

Is this an abridged claim?

  • Yes. To stop the automatic abridged claim payment from being made, staff must annotate the CUS CPD UPD CLI COVID-19 Pay Clm Abridged DOC and update the SOA:
    • go to the Document (DL) List
    • find the CUS CPD UPD CLI COVID-19 Pay Clm Abridged DOC for the claim the customer wants to withdraw
    • annotate the document with claim withdrawn
    • procedure ends here
  • No, procedure ends here

13

Customer has called to ask why they have not received an automatic payment or why their payment rates have reduced + Read more ...

When a state or territory has reached 70% full vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older:

  • Automatic payments will stop
  • The Operational Blueprint for the event will include the:
    • date customers will need to apply for new relevant periods as they open
    • relevant period and date automatic payments will stop from
  • Customers claiming for relevant periods before these dates will continue to receive automatic payments up to and including the relevant period in which automatic payments stop, unless a change of circumstances occurs or the customer has a claim granted for a relevant period before the start of their automatic payments
  • Note: payment rates will reduce once a state or territory has reached 80% full vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older
    • see ‘Payment amounts’ and ‘Timeframes for claiming’ in the Operational Blueprint for the event for the relevant periods the final payments will apply to
    • to be eligible for the COVID-19 Disaster Payment once a state reaches 80% full vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, they must:
      - have received payment for the relevant period immediately before the state or territory reached 80% full vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older
      - see ‘General eligibility’ for the applicable relevant period in the Operational Blueprint for the event
  • Go to Step 15

Where a state or territory has not reached 70% full vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older:

The customer does not need to complete an online task in their Centrelink Online Account to continue getting an automatic payment.

Note: if the customer lodges a change in circumstances in their online account, or they have a claim granted for a relevant period before the start of their automatic payments, this may change the rate of payment or cancel future automatic payments.

If a customer has a claim granted for a relevant period before the start of their automatic payments:

  • the customer will receive automatic payments up to an including the last relevant period they received an automatic payment for,
  • however, their automatic payments will then stop

For example: a customer is granted a payment in relevant period 3 and then receives an automatic payment for relevant period 4. The customer is then granted a payment in relevant period 1. The customer will receive an automatic payment for relevant period 2. The system will then stop automatic payments for the customer as it will detect payment has already been made for relevant period 3 and 4. To receive payment in relevant period 5, the customer must lodge a new claim.

To check if a customer is set to receive automatic payments, in Customer First, check the Document List (DL) screen.

If a customer has already received an automatic payment, the DOC titled CUS CLM EXG FSP will show AUTO as the Source.

Check the DOC to find out the relevant period the customer has been paid for.

In the customer's record, key 'PS' in the Next field to go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen.

If NPP shows a Delivery Date in the last 2 days, tell the customer:

  • to check their bank account
  • an immediate payment has been issued

If NPP and a Delivery Date in the last 2 days does not show, it could take approximately 8 days from the start date of the next relevant period for the automatic payment to be issued.

An automatic payment will not issue when a customer:

  • Claimed for a relevant area no longer deemed a COVID-19 hotspot. Note: if this applies, the heading ‘Where automatic payments stop’ will show on the Background page of the Operational Blueprint for the event. Go to Step 14
  • Claimed they were 'present in' a COVID-19 hotspot
    • Note: automatic payments do not apply to customers who claim they were ‘present in’ a COVID-19 hotspot
      The customer will need to lodge a claim for each new relevant period where the reason they are claiming is due to being 'present in' a COVID-19 hotspot
  • Has completed a change in circumstances (over the phone or online), where the change In circumstances has cancelled or will cancel in future automatic payments
    • The DL screen will show a DOC with the keyword CoCFSP
  • In a state or territory that has not reached 70% full vaccination, has a claim granted for a relevant period (over the phone or online) that is before the start of their automatic payments
  • Has voluntarily opted out of automatic payments (over the phone or online)
    • The DL screen will show a DOC with the keyword CoCFSP
  • Was granted an Income support payment customers - COVID-19 Disaster Payment ($200 rate) and they are no longer receiving an income support payment (ISP)
    • Check the benefit status line on the Customer Summary screen - no longer receiving an ISP will show as CAN
  • Was granted the higher rate of the COVID-19 Disaster Payment and they are now receiving an ISP
    • Check the benefit status line on the Customer Summary screen - now receiving an ISP will show as CUR

If any of the above reasons apply to the customer, tell them this is why they have not received an automatic payment.

The customer will need to lodge a claim:

  • for the next new relevant period, once the claiming period starts,
  • based on their current circumstances

If:

  • eligible to receive a COVID-19 Disaster Payment, automatic payments will re-start
    • Note: this does not apply to customers who claim they were ‘present in a COVID-19 hotspot’ – these customers need to claim for each new relevant period they were ‘present in a COVID-19 hotspot’
  • not eligible to receive a COVID-19 Disaster Payment, automatic payments will not re-start until the customer meets all eligibility requirements for payment

If none of the above reasons apply to the customer, tell them it could take approximately 8 days from the start date of the next relevant period for the automatic payment to be issued. They do not need to complete an online task in their Centrelink online account to receive the automatic payment.

Go to Step 15.

14

Automatic payments stopping - Victoria (August 2021) + Read more ...

Automatic payments are stopping where:

  • customers were granted a COVID-19 Disaster Payment as they live or work in a relevant area that is deemed a COVID-19 hotspot,
  • that relevant area is no longer deemed a COVID-19 hotspot, and
  • the customer lives in that relevant area

COVID-19 hotspot areas are:

  • decided by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer, and
  • not the same as ‘stay at home orders’, which are decided by state/territory governments

Customers still unable to work and earn their usual income will need to reapply for the next available relevant period under the impact reason:

  • live or work in a state or territory that has an area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer
    • for online claims, this shows as ‘I am impacted by a COVID-19 restricted movement, but do not live, work and was not present in a COVID-19 hotspot area’
    • if granted, the customer will continue to receive automatic payments until they advise of a change in circumstances

For a definition of ‘unable to work and earn their usual income’, see the Background page of the Operational Blueprint for the event.

Victoria

  • COVID-19 Disaster Payment – Victoria (August 2021), and
  • Income support payment customers COVID-19 Disaster Payment Victoria (August 2021)

For customers living in Greater Shepparton, the last automatic payment was paid for relevant period 6 (10-16 September 2021 inclusive):

  • these customers were not advised by the agency automatic payments were stopping

Customers in other areas of Victoria may have received a message in their Centrelink online account through myGov and/or SMS that advised as the relevant area they claimed under is coming out of a lockdown area:

  • automatic payments are stopping
  • the last automatic payment date was 10 September 2021 for relevant period 3-9 September 2021
    Note: even if the relevant area they claimed under was not coming out of a lockdown area, as they received the message automatic payments stopped

If a customer calls to discuss their automatic payments stopping, tell them for any new relevant period:

  • they will be able to reapply for the COVID-19 Disaster Payment under the impact reason that best suits their circumstances
  • if eligible, automatic payments will restart
  • new information on relevant periods and claiming dates will be available on Services Australia’s COVID-19 Disaster Payment page

Go to Step 15.

16

Customer wants to return a payment they have already received + Read more ...

Do not follow this process if the customer was incorrectly paid or if there is an error with an automatic payment.

This process can be followed where a customer was eligible to receive a COVID-19 Disaster Payment but is asking to return it. This is because they now qualify for a state or territory small business grant.

No debt will be raised, as the customer was eligible to receive a COVID-19 Disaster Payment. If the customer was not eligible to receive a COVID-19 Disaster payment, see COVID-19 Disaster Payment – customer paid incorrectly.

Customers can:

  • attend a service centre after COVID-19 lockdown restrictions end to return a payment already received
    See Processing a returned payment for options available to the customer when they attend the service centre
  • send a cheque to the agency (Centrelink, Reply Paid 7800, Canberra BC ACT 2610)
  • if they do not wish to wait until after COVID-19 lockdown restrictions end

Tell the customer to:

  • make the cheque out to ‘Centrelink’
  • send the cheque with a letter that includes:
    • their name
    • their Customer Reference Number (CRN), and
    • the payment they are returning

Add a DOC to the customer's record by running the Fast Note workflow. This is so there is a DOC on the customer's record to show they have called to discuss wanting to return a payment they have already received:

  • Location: Customer First > Workspace > Documents and Correspondence > Fast Note
  • Service Reason: select EXG - Emergency Exgratia from the drop down
  • Document Completion: select Yes, otherwise the Fast Note will not close and will create a new work item
  • Extra Details to enter a title for the DOC. Key in this field 'COVID-19 return payment'
  • Clearly document:
    • event and relevant period the customer is claiming for
    • what was discussed with the customer
  • Select Continue

If the customer also wants to stop ongoing automatic payments, see COVID-19 Disaster Payment - automatic payment and change of circumstances.

Procedure ends here.

Providing identity documents + Read more ...

A customer may call to ask about providing identity documents as they were told during their phone claim to attend in person to provide identity documents within 112 days.

Tell the customer:

  • ‘Due to the impacts of COVID-19 and continued unforeseen lockdowns, customers are required to provide identity documents once lockdowns have ended, and not necessarily within a strict 112 day timeframe
  • You will not receive another extension to provide identity documents however please provide them at your nearest Service Centre as soon as the lockdown in your area has ended to ensure you meet the payment requirements around identity
  • If you are required to provide identity documents and fail to meet that requirement, an overpayment will be raised at a later date for any payments of COVID-19 Disaster Payment received’
