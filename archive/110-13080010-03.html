Locate relationship data/tip-off + Read more ...

  • Go to the Match Data (IRMD) screen while in the review

Consolidated RSI data have a combination of triggers, e.g. NC/IDM/ATO relationship data. Be aware of all details to help and influence the conversation and decisions.

Note: some Tip-offs (TIP/ORE) are loaded individually.

Consolidated risks

The consolidated profile incorporates the following:

  • New Child (NC)
    These reviews identify customers who have been getting a single rate of payment (PPS) for more than 9 months and have advised the birth of a new child
  • ATO/MOC
    Australian Taxation Office (ATO) reviews identify customers who are claiming a payment at the single rate and information from ATO indicates the customer may be partnered
  • IDM/MOC
    Internal Data Matching (IDM) reviews identify:
    • Customers sharing accommodation with a person from a previous relationship
    • Customers who have not been partnered but have the same bank account details and the same address
    • Customers living at the same address as an ex-partner with whom they have an identified child support relationship
  • DHA/MOC
    Department of Defence (DHA) reviews identify customers who are claiming a payment at the single rate and are recognised as partnered by the Defence agency that employs them
  • IMM/MOC
    Department of Home Affairs (IMM) reviews identify customers who have left Australia on the same day and same point of departure as a person with whom they have previously been linked as a partner on Centrelink records
  • TOE/MOC
  • TOI/MOC

Tip-offs (TIP/ORE)

To view the:

  • Tip-off Analysis, see the Tip-off Analysis folder on the shared drive
  • Tip-off Reports, check the IRMD screen to see if the Centrelink report is public reported fraud (TIP) or staff reported fraud (ORE):
    • TIP - View a suspected fraud (TORS) (Tip-Off External)
      The IRMD screen will display the tip-off ID
    • ORE- Reports of Suspected Fraud and Corruption (Tip-Off Internal)
      The IRMD screen will display the report ID as numbers 00001234567, without RSF in front of it

The Previous IRS Review ID field relates to any other reviews loaded, which are now consolidated into the RSI.

When viewing the Tip-off information, be aware of other details provided (third party information). This information may influence the customer conversation and any decisions about scope, e.g. if third party verification is required.

If a Tip-off advises documents were provided and they are not saved in the Tip-off Analysis folder, see Step 2 in Table 9 to request the documents.

Determine alleged partner intervention + Read more ...

Check if the customer and alleged partner are current customers in their own right. To identify linked interventions, go to the Link Summary (IRLS) screen in the IRS intervention.

Load alleged partner intervention + Read more ...

On the alleged partner's New Review (IRNR) screen, complete these fields to add the intervention:

  • Review Type: RSI, TIP or ORE
  • Review Reason: MOC
  • Cycle Number: to match the one on the customer's record
  • Service Reason: enter the service reason for the review to be created e.g. JSP, PPS, AGE
  • Review Method and Review Origin: same as the customer

After selecting Continue, a REG (registered) intervention will be on the alleged partner's record. The next action is to link it to the current customer's record in IRS.

On the Link Summary (IRLS) screen, complete these fields to link the records:

  • CRN column: code the other primary customer's Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Rev ID column: due to a known system issue
    Code review ID 001 if the customer's review to be linked has a Review ID of 000
    Otherwise, code the same as the customer’s review
  • Lnk Rsn column: key 'MOC'

For help loading and linking reviews, see Linking reviews in the IRS.

3

Determine scope period - 'Date of Scope Assessment' (DOSA)

When to do this + Read more ...

  • initial phone conversation, unless both customers are on payments - set the scope start date when the second customer contacts
  • actioning the intervention when the customer has failed to contact

What period needs to be updated? + Read more ...

Use the relationship data as a guide to start the conversation with the customer. Use questioning skills to gather further information from the customer about their circumstances to determine the period the customer was a member of a couple.

Determine the scope start date by:

  • Internal Data Matching (IDM) member of a couple (MOC)
    load date on the Match Data (IRMD) screen
  • TIP/ORE:
    date original tip-off was created in View a suspected fraud (TORS) or the Reports of Suspected Fraud and Corruption System, unless an earlier date of a significant event is identified in the tip-off or analysis (e.g. marriage or purchase of property)
  • Parenting Payment Single (PPS) New Child Service Update:
    date of birth of youngest child, or Department of Home Affairs member of a couple:  date customer and ex-partner departed country, or
  • Defence Housing Authority (DHA) member of a couple:
    start of tenancy as a spouse in DHA housing
  • Australian Taxation Office (ATO) member of couple:
    start date of 1 July of Match year
  • Combination of trigger(s):
    earliest scope start according to the trigger(s), e.g. for an IDM and New Child Service Update, the start date will be the date of birth of the youngest child
  • An earlier date of a significant event identified during the customer conversation, e.g. marriage, engagement

If the customer was not on payment on the date identified in the trigger(s), the start date will be customer’s payment start date.

Determine the scope end date by:

  • the customer's date paid to (DPT) at the DOSA, or following a manual suspension
  • if the non-compliance pre-dates the scope start date, information can be requested for up to 12 months before the scope start date. If there are multiple compliance triggers, use the earliest scope start date

What information is required? + Read more ...

  • details relevant to the compliance trigger(s)
  • previously undisclosed or any other related information

How is information provided? + Read more ...

Information can be provided from either:

  • Verbal updates
    The customer can provide all of the required information over the phone. Trusting the information they give over the phone is appropriate, unless discussions indicate that documented verification is required. Use various questioning techniques to obtain the required information from the customer
  • Documented verification
    There is a discrepancy between the relationship data/tip-off and what the customer is advising. Seek evidence from the customer which substantiates the verbal advice they have provided.

Requests may be refined when:

  • information has been previously verified
  • start/end dates of the change in marital status are known (and are within scope)
  • customer/partner/ex-partner were not in receipt of income support

Updates/verification to be requested for:

  • the scope period only
  • customer/alleged partner and/or relevant ex-partners
  • when a customer and partner both have interventions, they are to be assessed under the same scope

A combination of documented verification and verbal updates can be used to finalise an intervention.

If extra information is received outside the requested scope of intervention, this must be coded by the Compliance and Debt Operations Branch.

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Check for anomaly or mismatch + Read more ...

Use all information available to determine if an anomaly or mismatch exists between the customer and the relationship data/tip-off.

To see if there in an identification mismatch:

  • Check for alternative spelling or misspelling of a customer's name does not automatically mean a mismatch
  • Look for a link between the identity details, e.g. parent/child, or other family relation. This may include same names, different date of birth, where a person could have picked up another person's Tax File Number (TFN) and started using it unknowingly
  • Take extra care for twins or intertwined records

Check the following screens:

  • Customer Personal Details Summary (CPDS) for alternative names
  • Link Summary (LS) and Child Selection (CHS) for possible parents/children
  • Document List (DL) and Display on Access (DOA) for any DOCs which record the use of alternative names for employment or other purposes
  • Address History (ADH) and the Online Search (OLS) facility to see if the customer lives at that address or in that zone
  • Tax File Number Summary (TFN) to compare the TFN listed in the Match Data to the TFN on the customer's record

The CO can decide it is not a mismatch and should continue if there are at least 2 commonalities between the customer and the relationship data/tip-off. For example, name and date of birth matches, or name and address matches.

When to issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter + Read more ...

2

Request documented evidence + Read more ...

The customer must be given the chance to provide suitable verification documents before contacting third parties.

Tell the customer:

  • what information is required
  • a letter will be issued requesting the information (as per the phone conversation)
  • they may call back at any time to provide the information verbally
  • the expected response date (22 days)
  • if they cannot provide the documents within that time, they should call and request an extension (7 days)
  • they will be contacted and advised of the outcome and of any adverse decisions
  • the request is being issued under:
    • ISP - section 63 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999, or
    • FTB - Family Assistance (Administration) Act 1999
  • their payments may be suspended and/or cancelled if they do not return the documents by the Expected Response Date

Encourage the customer to call back with the information verbally wherever possible. The customer does not have to wait until they receive the Request for Information to call back or lodge the information, unless the CO needs documented evidence to make a determination. If the verbal updates are insufficient, documents are still required as outlined in the request for information letter.

Tell the customer how to lodge the required information:

  • Attach a copy of their letter with their documents
  • Encourage the customer to upload the documents online
  • If they cannot upload documents, tell them to mail documents to the Customer Compliance mailing address

Advise the customer of their notification obligations. Check their record is up to date before finalising the call.

To avoid delays, the customer should phone back to notify 6 days after lodging the documents. If the customer phones back, confirm receipt and progress the intervention with the customer over the phone.

Make sure all relevant information is included on the intervention DOC. Include if the customer is advised their payments may be suspended and/or cancelled if they do not return the documents by the expected response date. See the Resources page for DOC requirements.

3

Issue Request for Information (RFI) letter + Read more ...

Issue the RFI letter via Workspace > Guided Procedures. Select option a completed (from description) form and all supporting documents.

Forms: the RFI does not send forms to the customer. Advise the customer they can download the SS284 and SS293 on the Services Australia website.

Suggested verification documents for RSI:

  • Sufficient written documentation to verify a bona fide separation has occurred
  • Some review specific options:
    • travel documents, e.g. a copy of itinerary booking, for Department of Home Affairs triggers only
    • written verification from Department of Defence. (If special permission has been granted for the customer to continue residing at the Defence Housing Authority (DHA) residence, when there has been a marriage/relationship breakdown). DHA member of a couple triggered reviews only
    • payslips for the Defence Force member. DHA member of a couple triggered reviews only
    • copy of full tax return/s
