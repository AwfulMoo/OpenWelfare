Assessing satisfactory progress for PES secondary students

Step

Action

1

Customer contacts Smart Centre Telephony + Read more ...

Is the Service Officer skilled in PES processing?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, record the details in a Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > Pensioner Education Supplement > PES Study. Update Action DOC. Procedure ends here

2

Secondary studies + Read more ...

Students undertaking secondary studies other than a third or subsequent attempt at Year 12 are generally considered to be making satisfactory progress in their course and therefore qualify for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), providing they meet all the other qualification and payability provisions.

Note: where exceptional circumstances apply and unable to make a determination, the case can be referred to the relevant Level 2 Policy helpdesk. See the Resources page for a link to the Level 2 Policy Help Desk.

3

Disregarding previous attempts at Year 12 + Read more ...

Previous attempts by a PES student at Year 12 can be disregarded for any of the following reasons:

  • student failed because English was not their first language
  • student studied part-time
  • student completed or discontinued studies in that year within six months of the start of the academic year
  • student failed the year because of an illness that had not been diagnosed when the student started study in that calendar year, or for other circumstances beyond the student's control that were not evident before the student started study in that year
  • student undertook the previous course more than 10 years ago

Can any of the student’s previous attempts at Year 12 be disregarded?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Third or subsequent attempt + Read more ...

After disregarding previous attempts, is this now the student’s third or subsequent attempt at Year 12?

  • Yes, the student is not making satisfactory progress. Go to Step 5
  • No, the student is making satisfactory progress in their secondary course. Go to Step 6

5

The student has not made satisfactory progress and does not qualify for PES + Read more ...

Complete the following tasks:

  • Reject the claim, see Assessing Claim for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
  • Advise the student of their review and appeal rights
  • Record details of the decision and any discussion with the student on a DOC

6

Customer making satisfactory progress + Read more ...

If the customer satisfies the progress rules, complete the following tasks:

  • Advise the student of the allowable time for their course
  • Advise the student that if they withdraw from the course or cease to undertake a full-time or concessional study load, they will not qualify for PES
  • Advise the student to notify, within 14 days, any changes in their circumstances
  • Ensure correct education details are coded on the system
  • Record details of the decision and any discussion with the student on a DOC
