FTB rate

When a customer queries why their rate of FTB has changed a thorough investigation needs to be undertaken by the Service Officer before it is escalated to a Technical Peer Support Officer (TPS).

Service Officers should first check for any DOCs that would indicate a change of details which would reduce or increase the rate of FTB.

Date of rate change in:

Process Direct

  • Enter AX in Super Key which will default to the service reason
  • Ensure that the Family Tax Benefit service reason is showing in the Service Reason Field
  • Select the Expand details tab on the AX screen to display the Assessment Details (ASDT) screen for the child
  • To close the ASDT screen, select the Expand details tab

Customer First

  • Go to Assessment Explanation (AXFTB) screen
  • Select child/ren and view details on the Assessment Details (ASDT) screen
  • Use <Prev and Next> to scroll through pages and compare details for relevant effect dates

2

Child's age and study details + Read more ...

  • Code REXFTB in NXT: field
  • Press [Enter] to see the number of children assessed for each age group
  • View current education/training details in Customer First
  • Select Workspace> Children> Child education summary
  • Code AX in NXT: to see individual child eligibility details/ summary

Child age and study circumstances that could affect the rate of FTBA include:

  • child turning 13 years of age
  • child is aged 16 or over and they do not meet the FTB study requirements
  • child is aged 16-19 years of age and has completed year 12
  • end of the calendar year in which the child turned 19 years of age has been reached
  • child has turned 18 years of age
  • child has completed full-time secondary study

Child age and study circumstances that could affect the rate of FTBB include:

  • Age of youngest child, a higher rate is paid if the youngest child is under 5 years of age
  • Youngest child turns 13 years of age and customer is a member of a couple and is not a grandparent carer
    Note: FTB Part B is not payable to a member of a couple who has a youngest child aged 13 years of age or over, unless the customer is a grandparent carer. For single customers and grandparent carers, FTB Part B can continue to be paid up to the end of the calendar year in which the youngest child turns 18 years of age (child must be in full-time secondary school if 16-18 years of age)
  • Youngest child is over 13 years of age and customer becomes single
  • When a child completes secondary study, FTB Part B is paid up until 31 December, or for 28 days after the course end date if the end date is before November. See Calculating Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part B
  • Child is aged 16 or over and they do not meet the FTB study requirements

3

FTB payment method and/or choice + Read more ...

There are a number of payment choices available to families. Check the FTB Payment Choices (FPC) screen for the customer's choice, and the REXFTBC screen. See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) claim options and payment choices for further information.

The rate of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A is worked out using either Method 1 or Method 2 depending on whether the customer's adjusted taxable income (ATI) is above or below the Part A higher income free area . Check the REXFTBA screen, a change in method used to calculate FTB Part A - Method 1 or Method 2 can alter the rate of payment.

4

Large Family Supplement (LFS) and/or Multiple Birth Allowance + Read more ...

  • LFS is not payable from 1 July 2016. It was paid to customers with 4 or more FTB children for the period 1 July 2015 to 30 June 2016. Before 1 July 2015, LFS was paid to families with 3 or more FTB children
  • If Multiple Birth Allowance (MBA) or LFS rate has changed, check the ages of the children on the Child Selection (CHS) screen to confirm whether the customer is no longer qualified due to the child no longer being an FTB child

The References page contains a link to historical rates.

The Resources page contains a link to the publication FPR006 - Family Assistance Payment rates.

5

Income support + Read more ...

A customer's FTB rate will be automatically adjusted if they or their partner begin or cease to receive an income support payment.

There may also be a change to the rate of FTB Part A if the income support payment customer begins or ceases an employment income nil rate period. See Effect of income support entitlement on Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

Check the REXFTB screen for AUTO reason ISC. See Income support has cancelled

6

Child’s immunisation status + Read more ...

From 1 July 2018, a FTB Part A rate reduction is applied to the rate of FTB Part A for each child that does not meet immunisation requirements.

The child’s immunisation status can be viewed on the Child Immunisation Summary guided procedure. The FTB Part A reduction due to immunisation starts the day after the end of the 63 day grace period, which begins when the child’s non-compliant status is notified.

The reduction will continue until the child meets immunisation requirements.

Note: if the child does not meet immunisation requirements before the end of the 63 day grace period, the FTB Part A rate reduction due to immunisation will be applied to the days in the grace period when FTB is reconciled at the end of the financial year.

If an FTB Part A reduction has been applied to the customer’s rate, it can be viewed on the Assessment Details (ASDT) screen.

To check the reduction in:

Process Direct

  • Enter AX in Super Key
  • Check the Family Tax Benefit service reason (FTB) is displaying in the Service Reason field
  • Select the Expand details tab, the Assessment Details (ASDT) screen will display for the child
  • To close the ASDT screen, select the Expand details tab

Customer First

  • Go to Assessment Explanation (AXFTB) screen
  • Select child/ren and view details on the Assessment Details (ASDT) screen
  • Use <Prev and Next> buttons to scroll through pages and compare details for relevant effect dates

See also: Immunisation requirements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A from 1 July 2018.

7

Income estimate + Read more ...

Check the Income estimate via FAO Income Summary (FISD) screen for changes:

  • Revised income estimate by the customer, or the system may have applied the Automatic uplift
  • Income of the primary earner and secondary earner can affect the rate under the FTB Part B Income Test
  • Customer becomes partnered. Income of the primary earner and secondary earner can affect the rate under the FTB Part B Income Test

8

Change in care + Read more ...

FTB rate can reduce or increase when a:

  • child enters customer's care/custody
  • child leaves customer's care/custody
  • child out of care without consent
  • shared care percentage has changed

The Child in Care (CHC) screen shows the care status of the child. The FTB Shared Care (FSC) screen shows the actual care percentage.

To view the percentage used for the FTB rate in:

Process Direct:

  • use the Super Key to go to the AX/ASDT screen
  • select the Family Tax Benefit service

Customer First:

  • go to the AXFTB screen
  • select the child

Note: if shared care is not being correctly applied when a child is in the care of a parent who has transferred their FTB to their current partner (step parent). See the Resources page for further information relating to this system problem.

9

Maintenance + Read more ...

FTB rate can be affected by a change in child support income:

  • Check details from the REXFTBA,REXFTBM and MNEX screens
  • For more information see Maintenance Income Test (MIT) for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

If the customer has failed the Maintenance Action Test (MAT), the FTB Part A base rate reason will be:

  • in Process Direct, on the AX Summary
  • in Customer First, on the AXFTB screen

Advise customer what action they need to take. See Maintenance Action Test (MAT) for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

Check the maintenance action code for the child on the Maintenance Action Input (MNMA) screen. See Table 2

10

Customer and/or children have been overseas + Read more ...

Check Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen and Immigration Advised Child Movements (CHRSIM) screen by selecting the child from CHS screen and/or check REXFTB screen.

  • For departures on or after 1 July 2016, customers and children who have been temporarily overseas for more than 6 weeks will have their payments cancelled
  • For departures prior to 1 July 2016, customers and children who have been temporarily overseas for more than 6 weeks will have their payments reduced to base rate for FTBA for up to 56 weeks before the payment is cancelled. FTBB if the customer/youngest child has been overseas for more than 6 weeks, then payment is cancelled

11

Rent Assistance + Read more ...

A change in Rent Assistance (RA) can increase or reduce the rate of payment due to any of the following, check the Accommodation History (ACS) screen:

  • rent has not been verified
  • customer is now a homeowner
  • there is a change in the amount of rent the customer pays
  • customer is no longer eligible for RA

Otherwise check the Payment Summary (PS) screen as Customer may receive RA with their primary benefit instead of FTB

12

Other affecting factors to check + Read more ...

  • Customer is receiving Parental Leave Pay (PPL). FTB Part B cannot be paid during a PPL period or on days when the customer is receiving Flexible PPL days
  • A projected overpayment has been identified and the customer's ongoing rate has been reduced under continuous adjustment to offset the potential overpayment
    • Check REXFTBC screen and/or in Customer First, Family Assistance>Family Tax Benefit mandatory continuous adjustment
  • The customer is subject to the non-payment of FTB instalments to zero entitlement customers
    • In Customer First, Select Family Benefits menu>FTB Zero Ent. Preclusion to go to the Zero Entitlement Customer (ZEC) Summary
  • Blended family assessment
    • Check the Blended Families Split FTB Rate (FBSR) screen
  • An FTB Part A rate reduction due to Healthy Start for School (HSFS) requirements has been applied due to a child not meeting HSFS requirements from their fifth birthday. Note: FTB Part A rate reduction due to HSFS applies to child/ren turning 4 on or after 1 July 2018.
    • Check FTB HSFS Reduction Explanation (FHRRE) screen to view the HSFS rate reduction status relevant children to the customer's record

Determining the reason for FTB rate change due to Maintenance Action Test (MAT)

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check recent DOCs + Read more ...

If the action taken to obtain child support has been manually recorded, there should be a DOC on the customer's record. Check Document List (DL) screen.

Is there a DOC that states customer has failed to take reasonable action to obtain child support for a child?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check if FTB Part A rate is limited to base rate for any child + Read more ...

Process Direct:

  • Enter AX in Super Key
  • Check the Family Tax Benefit (FTB) service reason is displaying in the Service Reason field
  • Select the Expand details tab to display the Assessment Details (ASDT) screen for the child
  • To close the ASDT, select the Expand details tab

Customer First:

  • code AXFTB in Nxt: field
  • press [Enter] to go to the Assessment Explanation (AX) screen

Does the Eligible FTB(A): field display Base No Maintenance Act for any child where Claimed: and displays YES?

  • Yes, the customer's FTB Part A rate for that child is limited to the base rate because action to obtain child support has not been taken
  • No, go to Step 6

3

Check/ update action taken to obtain child support + Read more ...

The Maintenance Action workflow can be used to view and update child support action details.

From 1 July 2009, members of both opposite-sex and same-sex couples are recognised as parents. MAT details for children from same-sex relationships may need to be reviewed.

To check/update details manually:

  • Go to Maintenance Entitlement Group Summary (MNGS) screen. Check that all children are correctly grouped according to child support entitlement. See How to group children on a customer's Family Tax Benefit (FTB) record
  • 'S'elect child on MNGS screen and go to the Maintenance Child Details Summary (MNCD) screen. To view further details, 'S'elect Maintenance Action field to go to the Maintenance Action Result (MNMR) screen

Are details correct?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

MAT details not correct + Read more ...

Check for mismatches and fix - see Mutual customer matching with Child Support. If child support entitlement data has not been transferred from Child Support, e.g. customer applied for a child support assessment more than 42 days ago, and maintenance action is NMA, contact the Child Support Unit. Look up local Processing Service details for: Child Support Unit.

To manually update child support action details:

  • code 'M' in the Maintenance Action field on the child's MNCD screen to go to the Maintenance Action Input (MNMA)
  • record MAT code to reflect what action the customer has taken or to show that the customer doesn't need to take action (exempt)
  • correct existing details if the customer had taken reasonable action for that period. Otherwise record new details from the date the customer took reasonable action
  • record details on a DOC and finalise activity on Assessment Results (AR) screen

Procedure ends here.

5

Reasonable action + Read more ...

Customer needs to take reasonable action to obtain child support to be paid more than base FTB Part A rate for child.

  • Advise customer what action they need to take. See Maintenance Action Test (MAT) for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customers to find out what is appropriate action
  • Explain that their FTB Part A rate for the child cannot be increased until they take action. Advise customer that regardless of their personal income it is every parent's responsibility to provide financially for their children
  • Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

6

Check other reasons for the change in FTB rate + Read more ...

To check details of the customer's current FTB rate:

  • type 'REXFTBA' (Part A) or 'REXFTBB' (Part B) in Nxt:, and
  • press [Enter]

The customer's FTB Part A rate may have reduced due to a change in maintenance income.

See Where to find child support information on Centrelink systems
