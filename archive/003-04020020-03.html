Death as a direct result assessment - Service Officers + Read more ...

On the claim form the claimant is asked to provide evidence that substantiates the deceased person died as a direct result of the terrorist act.

Service Officers must examine the evidence that has been supplied, or source other evidence, to determine if there is a direct link between the deceased person's death and the terrorist act. For example, the claimant may provide a police report that states the deceased person was killed when the terrorist bomb exploded in a market place. See the Resources page for the types of evidence that can help in establishing the close family member died as a direct result of the terrorist act.

Evidence for this can come in a number of forms with some sources more reliable than others. Service Officers will need to analyse any evidence provided, or found, to determine how much weight should be given to each piece of evidence and how it supports the facts. Influencing factors to be considered when weighting the evidence include the:

  • nature and integrity of the evidence, for example, an official document versus verbal testimony,
  • source of the evidence, for example, primary versus secondary source of evidence, eye witness, expert account
  • age of the evidence - is it still relevant to the issue being decided

It may be that several pieces of evidence will be needed to determine if the deceased person died as a direct result of the terrorist act.

If the claimant has provided medical evidence that needs an assessment, the claim should be considered by Assessment Services Branch.

If there are difficulties in sourcing the death as a direct result evidence consult with Social Work Services for help. Social workers may be able to help with advice on sourcing evidence, assessing evidence and management of the interactions with the claimant. Contact a social worker as per the instructions in Step 3.

Determine hierarchy of close family members + Read more ...

Complete a family tree of close family members to determine additional eligible claimants who may be invited to submit a secondary victim claim. Record family tree in the AVTOP processing tool.

Before sending invitations to additional close family members, consideration must be given to the payable amount for the initial claimant in line with the hierarchy of close family members table.

Invitations to claim should only be sent to those close family members who may be entitled to a payment.

For example:

  • The partner/spouse of the deceased person lodges a claim as a secondary victim. In their claim they advise that at the time of the terrorist act the deceased person had no children
    In this example, as the partner/spouse is entitled to 100% of AVTOP it would not be appropriate to send an invitation to claim to other parties as, under the hierarchy of close family member rules, they would not be entitled to any portion of the AVTOP.
  • The partner/spouse of the deceased person lodges a claim as a secondary victim. In their claim, they advise that at the time of the terrorist act the deceased person also had a child. At present the child of the deceased person has not lodged a secondary victim claim
    In this example, the child of the deceased person must be invited to claim as they are entitled to 50% of the portion of AVTOP (with partner/spouse entitled to the remaining 50%). If the child of the deceased did not lodge a claim, the partner/spouse would qualify for the full 100% of the payment.
  • Both parents of one child have died as a direct result of the same terrorist act. The legal guardian of the child lodges a secondary victim claim for both parents. However, the maximum payment that can be made to any one secondary victim in relation to a single terrorist act is $75,000.
    In this example, the child of the deceased parents can only receive a maximum of $75,000 and as such will receive $37,500 from each claim. As there is still a proportion of the $75,000 unpaid for both of the child's parent, the parents of the partners/spouses (the child's grandparents) may qualify for a payment according to the hierarchy of close family member rules.

Note: if there are complex family relationships or it is possible that one person could qualify as a secondary victim for more than one deceased person from a single terrorist act, use the Payment Query Template on the Resources page to escalate to the Emergency Management Level 2 Helpdesk for advice.

When appropriate, send the AVTOP SV Invitation reminder to claim letter using the invitation option. The due date on the letter must be a date of no less than 30 days after the notice is dated. Scan and upload a copy of the AVTOP SV Invitation to claim letter to the customer's, and if applicable the nominee's, record. See the Resources page for a link to all AVTOP letters.

For example, if the invitation to apply letter is sent on 12 September the earliest due date is 12 October.

If multiple invitations to apply are being sent, make sure all due date periods are of the same duration.

Review the invitation/s after 14 days and send the AVTOP SV Invitation reminder to Claim letter using the reminder option with the same due date as the initial letter. Scan and upload a copy of the AVTOP SV Invitation reminder to claim letter to the customer's, and if applicable the nominee's, record. See the Resources page for all AVTOP letters.

Deceased persons as primary victims + Read more ...

9

Rate Calculation + Read more ...

To calculate the amount of AVTOP to be paid:

$75,000 - reductions & deductions - any primary victim payment made = AVTOP secondary victim payment.

The AVTOP final payment to each of the eligible claimants must then be calculated according to the hierarchy of close family members table.

Search the AVTOP processing tool to determine if the claimant has received any other secondary victim payments for this declared event. If the secondary victim has received any other secondary victim payments in relation to any other close family members for the same declared event, subtract this payment from their entitlement. This will make sure they do not receive more than $75,000.

Important information + Read more ...

Before rejecting a claim, two genuine attempts must be made to contact the claimant to:

  • Provide the claimant with a full explanation of why their claim is unsuccessful and determine if the claimant can qualify under any other criteria
  • Advise that a letter will be sent outlining the review/appeal process, and
  • Consider if the claimant may be eligible to receive other social security payments and/or if a referral to a social worker or external organisation is needed. The Resources page has a link to the Services Australia website where Service Finder can be located

Approval to reject claim + Read more ...

Note: unsuccessful claims cannot be rejected until approval has been sought from the Emergency Management Level 2 Helpdesk.

Has approval been sought to start claim rejection?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, place claim on hold with the appropriate rejection keyword. AVTOP New Claim keywords are located on the Resources page of Primary victim eligibility and rate calculation for the Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment (AVTOP)
    • Once approval to reject is given, and the held claim becomes due, go to Step 3

Check contacts + Read more ...

Is this the first attempt to contact about rejection?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

First contact attempt + Read more ...

Was the attempt successful?

  • Yes,
    • Complete the new claim activity as 'rejected'
    • Document the claimant's record accordingly
    • Make sure the correct rejection letter is sent, go to Step 6
  • No,
    • Document the claimant's record accordingly
    • Add the AVTRJ1 keyword to the new claim work item and place work item on hold until the following day (the work item will be removed from the inbox and reallocated once off hold)
    • Procedure ends here

5

Second contact attempt + Read more ...

Successful/unsuccessful (the outcome is the same regardless of whether the second attempt is successful or unsuccessful)

  • Complete the new claim activity as rejected
  • Document the claimant's record accordingly
  • Make sure the correct rejection letter is sent, go to Step 6

6

Send rejection letter + Read more ...

Finalise AVTOP on claimant's record.

Document the outcome of the claim and the reason for the decision in the AVTOP processing tool and Customer First. Documentation in Customer First must be in line with GIR standards.

If unsuccessful due to:

  • eligibility requirements, send the AVTOP Reject general letter to the claimant or legal guardian
  • the hierarchy of close family members, send AVTOP reject Apportionment letter to the claimant or legal guardian
  • the deductions send the AVTOP Deduction preclude letter to the claimant or legal guardian

Scan and upload a copy of the AVTOP rejection letter to the customer's, and if applicable nominee's, record.

See the Resources page for a link to all AVTOP letters.
