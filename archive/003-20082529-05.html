Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment - Victoria

Claim form

Period of self-isolation, quarantine or caring that starts:

  • between 9 December 2021 and 17 January 2022 (inclusive), use the Claim for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment (EM252) form
  • before 9 December 2021, (that is, up to and including 8 December 2021), take a claim for this period over the phone using the verbal claim template. If the customer is not able to submit a claim via phone, use the Claim for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment - Victoria form (EM223) form

Scenarios - general

For periods of self-isolation, quarantine or caring that start:

  • from (on or after) 9 December 2021 (ending on 17 January 2022), see Scenarios - from 9 December 2021
  • before 9 December 2021 (that is, up to and including 8 December 2021), see Scenarios - before 9 December 2021

Scenarios - payments customers residing in New South Wales or South Australia are eligible for

See Scenarios – Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster payment, and where a:

  • New South Wales resident tries to claim the Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment, see scenarios about eligibility for either New South Wales or Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment on the Resources page of Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment - New South Wales
  • South Australian resident tries to claim the Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment, see scenarios about eligibility for either South Australia or Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment on the Resources page of Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment – South Australia

Scenarios - Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment

Table 1: Scenarios:

  • specific to the Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • where there was a period in July to September 2020 where customers living in New South Wales or South Australia could claim the Victoria Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment

    Scenario

    Eligibility

    Reason

    Scenario 1

    The South Australian Department of Health informs a customer they must self-isolate for 14 days from 1 August 2020. This is because they are the close contact of a person who tested positive with COVID-19.

    The customer lives and works in South Australia.

    Not eligible

    The customer does not live or work in Victoria.

    The customer is not eligible for the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment – South Australia as their period of self-isolation is before 10 September 2020.

    If the customer’s period of self-isolation occurred from 10 September 2020, they could test their eligibility for the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment - South Australia.

    Scenario 2

    A customer who resides in Melbourne returns home after visiting family in New South Wales. They are required to self-isolate.

    The customer cannot go to their workplace and does not have appropriate leave entitlements.

    Not eligible

    The customer has not been informed to isolate by the Department of Health and Aged Care and Human Services or an ‘agent’ for one of the reasons required.

    Scenario 3

    The customer claimed and received the $450 Coronavirus (COVID-19) Test Isolation Payment while waiting for their results.

    The customer then gets a phone call from Bendigo Health informing them to self-isolate as they tested positive to COVID-19.

    The customer meets all eligibility criteria.

    Eligible

    The customer has been informed to self-isolate by Bendigo Health by phone. This meets the requirements of informed by the Victorian Department of Health and Human Services.

    The $450 Coronavirus (COVID-19) Test Isolation Payment does not stop the customer from claiming the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment (Victoria).

    Scenario 4

    SA Health informs the customer they must self-isolate from 10 September 2020 as they are the close contact of a person who tested positive with COVID-19.

    The customer lives and works in South Australia.

    Not eligible

    The person does not work in Victoria.

    The customer may be eligible for the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment – South Australia, as their period of self-isolation is from 10 September 2020.

    Scenario 5

    A customer, who resides in New South Wales, is told by their General Practitioner (GP) that they:

    • have tested positive with COVID-19, and
    • must self-isolate from 2 September 2020 until well

    The customer works in Victoria.

    Eligible

    The person has been informed by a required authority that they must self-isolate. The customer resides in New South Wales and works in Victoria.

    The customer’s period of self-isolation falls entirely on or before 17 September 2020, before the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment New South Wales started.

    Note: if the 14 day period of self–isolation was in full or part thereof, on or after 17 September 2020, the customer would not be eligible for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Victoria. They could test their eligibility for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment New South Wales.
