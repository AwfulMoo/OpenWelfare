2

Known problems with overpayments calculated by the system + Read more ...

A debt must be raised despite system limitations or incorrect calculations.

The Centrelink system is not calculating pension rates correctly in the following situations:

  • before 1 October 2000 and the rate calculation includes proportional income
  • before 1 July 2002 and the Agreement customer has a non-pension partner.
    The Agreements affected by the error are:
    • Austria
    • Cyprus
    • Ireland
    • Italy
    • Malta
    • Netherlands
    • Portugal
    • Canada
    • Spain

The issue does not affect New Zealand and United Kingdom Agreements.

Do not solely rely upon debt tools (for example, MultiCal) to calculate debts impacted by an Agreement:

  • The system cannot calculate any debt prior to a limiting date
  • The system calculation shows a debt after the end date of the debt. Check the dates, and if needed, change the end the debt to the correct date
  • System calculations do not assess at every reassessment point. This shows a zero debt for this period when there is a debt
  • If the system calculates a zero rate or the customer loses qualification at any time during the debt period, the system will include all payments received after this date in the debt total. This includes where the customer regained entitlement at a later time
  • Where there is a reasonable change in the rate, Service Officers must check the calculations
  • Coding employment income will not provide the correct result when calculating the debt on a cancelled record

3

Identifying incorrect debt calculations + Read more ...

  • Check payments made on a few dates were correct.
    For example:
    • 20 April 2000
    • 20 April 2001
    • 20 April 2002
  • Check the Daily Rates Summary (RATS) screen for any large rate increases. If there are, determine if these should have occurred. Also, check to see if the rate looks right for the income entered

Note: the following screens update to reflect the change once an update has been finalised:

  • Pensions Income and Asset Summary (PIAS)
  • Pensions Rate Calculation (PRC)
  • Factors Affecting Rate (PFAR)
  • Daily Rates Summary (RATS)

The amount actually paid does not always show on these screens.

4

Complete all income sheets for the customer + Read more ...

Record the customer's circumstances, using the correct working life residence (WLR)

See the Resources page for a link to Debt Calculator Tools.

For help, see Using the MultiCal debt calculator

Include income calculated in Step 3 to determine the customer's entitlement at 100 per cent:

  • Manually proportionalise the amount entitled from 1 August 2000 on the 'Customer Entitlement' sheet of the MultiCal print out
  • Write these amounts below the MultiCal 'Fortnightly rate payable' field. (If Italian Agreement, do this for the entire period of the debt). This will check if the customer is rate limited
  • Enter payments received as NIL and adjust for pay cycles as explained below

5

MultiCal and Pay cycles + Read more ...

Take into account the change for pay cycles on 1 July 2000 when calculating entitlement. MultiCal does calculate the additional period correctly, however the Multical must be checked to ensure it includes that period.

To do this, change the customer payment start and end dates for the period spanning 1 July 1999.
For environment I cases, this will be:

  • Select [Next Pay] until reaching the payment starting 9 July 1999 and ending 22 July 1999
  • Change the start date of this period to 1 July 1999 and the end date to 14 July 1999
  • Do not select [Insert]. Select [Next Pay]. Keep selecting until reaching the last payment. At that point the [Next Pay] button will go grey
  • Change the end date of the last payment to the end date of the debt calculation
  • Select [Save] and [Exit]

6

MultiCal and Pay cycles - align the pay periods + Read more ...

Align the pay periods to reflect the period of payment. To do this:

  • Select [Customer Payments]
  • The start date will be the start date of the debt. This is correct so do not change it
  • Change the end date to the last day of the first fortnight. In environment I this will be payday code 4
  • Do not select [Insert]. Select [Next Pay] until reaching the last payment. At that point the [Next Pay] button will go grey
  • Change the end date of the last payment to the end date of the debt calculation
  • Select [Save] and [Exit]

7

Save calculation and consider the proportional rate limiter ceiling rate + Read more ...

There is a ceiling on the rate of Australian pension paid overseas. The pension rate paid to persons outside Australia cannot exceed the notional rate payable to persons in Australia.

This ensures people outside Australia entitled to an income test concession on their contributory foreign pension cannot receive a higher rate of Australian pension than they would if they were in Australia.

This has always been the case under the Agreement with Italy.

This occurs when there is both:

  • a low working life residence, and
  • high foreign pension income produces a rate of pension higher than the 'inside Australia' income tested rate

To save the calculation and consider the limiter ceiling rate:

  • Save the MultiCal case, then select [File] and [New Case]. Check the box next to 'Same Customer' and check all of the remaining boxes. This retains both calculations and is important if the customer appeals the decision
  • To ensure the customer has not been rate limited, enter the customer's circumstances into MultiCal without proportionalising their income. Save this case
  • Compare the proportional amount entitled to the proportional rate limiter ceiling rate for the 2 MultiCal calculations. This shows which rate to pay, as it may fluctuate
  • If the customer is rate limited:
    • add the total amount entitled for the relevant periods where the proportional rate is payable. (Add the MultiCal amounts, not the amounts manually proportionalised) and
    • where the rate limited rate is payable

8

Is the customer's rate affected by the Italian Agreement? + Read more ...

  • Yes:
    • There is a known issue under the revised Italian Agreement from 1 October 2000 to 30 June 2002
    • Disregard any debts which occurred during this period. They are unlikely. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

Step

Action

1

Open the PS tool + Read more ...

See Payment Details Download Sheet (PS Tool) for more information.

See the Resources page for a link to Debt Calculator Tools.

Open the PS Tool spreadsheet to add up the amount received in the period. Make sure the Centrelink system is open. Select [Start], then select [Start Copying].

  • The PS Tool will pick up the CRN of the record. Select the 'PEN' system and enter the period of the debt
  • Once the payments have been downloaded, apportion the start and end periods to align with the period of the debt

2

Check payment and debt details + Read more ...

  • Check the downloaded payments and delete any reissued amounts where needed. Note: reissued payments will be on the Payment Summary (PS) screen. Check for manually issued payments on the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen
  • Delete the $300 Aged Persons Savings Bonus if listed in the downloaded payments
  • Check any arrears payments and ensure they relate to the debt period. Calculate and include payment amounts for any arrears that fall outside the debt period
  • Check the Debt List (OPDL) screen for any raised, waived or written off debts spanning the relevant debt period. Allow for this when determining the amount actually paid. Note: ignore the debt currently being recalculated if appropriate
  • For previously raised or raised and waived debts outside of the current debt period calculation,
    • identify amounts to exclude in the current debt calculation by using a copy of the past debt calculation
    • Note: it may be possible to access the calculation by putting a 'W' next to the debt on OPDL. This opens the debt explanation (ADEX) information for the debt. If ADEX is unavailable, obtain the debt calculations from the customer's file

3

Check the taxation payment summary + Read more ...

Has the customer been issued a payment summary for any or all of the debt period?

  • Yes:
    • Adjust the payment summary and issue the new summary to the customer
    • Adjust all payment summaries for the entire debt period when past issued payment summaries are present
    • Go to Step 4
  • No:
    • When a past issued payment summary is not present, the letter advising of the debt is sufficient. This advice is from the Centrelink Business Manager of Taxation
    • Go to Step 5

4

Tax adjustments + Read more ...

Note: processing is automatic for tax adjustments from 2003. This process uses details recorded on the Component Amount (OPCA) screen.

  • Enter the basic and supplement amount results from MultiCal in the 'Amount Entitled' section of the tax adjustment table. These figures are in the tax adjustment section on the first page of the MultiCal print out
  • Check the proportional percentage is correct. This displays in the 'Inc 1' sheet. Change this figure to '1' if these figures should not be proportionalised
  • Use the PS Tool print out to split the amounts into financial years and add up each financial year. Use basic and supplement amounts. Most of the time, payment summaries do not include other components. Like MultiCal calculations, the Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen displays the start and end dates of each financial year. Arrears payments appear in the financial year paid and not the financial year they relate to. Enter these figures into the 'Amount Received' column
  • Refer to the 'Tax Adjustments' listing for Tax Adjustments
  • Correct the relevant payment summaries on the TXGS screen, by placing a 'C' next to the payment summary. You can amend multiple payment summaries in the one activity:
    • Take the amount of the tax adjustment off the amount in the Taxable Entitle$ field. Record the new amount in this field
    • Record [OVP] in the Amend Rsn field
