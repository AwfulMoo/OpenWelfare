Check that an ACER form is complete

Table 1: how Service Officers check that an Aged Care Entry Record (ACER) form is complete. Note: do not make any mark on the original ACER form unless it is indicated in the procedure.

Step

Action

1

Check form is complete + Read more ...

Check if the AC021 form is complete:

  • Q1. Type of care
  • Q2. Service name
  • Q3. Service ID
  • Q4. Care recipient details
  • Q11. Date of entry
  • Q17. Home Care Package level
  • Declaration - Printed name (person authorised by the aged care service), position held, phone number, signature and date

If any of the above information is missing, the AC021 form is incomplete.

Note:

  • An aged care service cannot submit an ACER form unless the care recipient has entered into care and cannot include a future commencement date
  • The form must be rejected and the aged care service is notified (via email or phone) that future entry dates cannot be entered on the form, see Table 2

Is the AC021 form complete?

  • Yes, go to Table 3
  • No, go to Table 2

Reject an ACER form

Table 2: how Service Officers reject an Aged Care Entry Record (ACER) form in PaNDA.

Step

Action

1

Incomplete section + Read more ...

Do not process an incomplete manual ACER form.

If the ACER form is incomplete:

  • Enter a PaNDA note, include the reason why the ACER form cannot be processed
  • Enter a care recipient note in ACSP to reflect the incomplete form

Contact the service provider by:

  • telephone to advise that the ACER cannot be processed, or
  • email, go to Step 2

2

Send email to the aged care service + Read more ...

Use the below template:

Dear Care Manager,

Thank you for the Aged Care Entry Record (ACER) form received on <insert date of receipt> in regards to <insert care recipient name and ID>.

We are unable to process your ACER form due to the following reason/s:

  • the form is incomplete as missing information <insert what information is missing>
    or
  • the form has a future entry date

As a matter of priority, please resubmit a completed form to aged.care.liaison@servicesaustralia.gov.au

With thanks

Aged Care Payments

Process an ACER form for residential care

Table 3: how Service Officers process an Aged Care Entry Record (ACER) form in the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP) for residential care. Note: do not make any mark on the original ACER form unless it is indicated in the procedure.

Step

Action

1

Check completion and leave details + Read more ...

Check the ACER form is complete, see Table 1.

2

Search for a care recipient record + Read more ...

Search for a care recipient record on the ACSP before entering the ACER to ensure that:

  • the care recipient details are recorded, and
  • there is a valid National Screening and Assessment Form (NSAF) with approval for permanent residential care

To search for:

  • a care recipient record, see Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to search for a care recipient – (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table
  • a valid NSAF with approval for permanent residential care, see Step 2 in the View and process NSAFs in the Aged Care Staff Portal (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table

3

Locate care recipient record + Read more ...

Has the care recipient record been located?

  • Yes,
    • the care recipient record has been located with the appropriate care approval
    • go to Step 4
  • No,
    • contact the aged care service by telephone or email to advise there is no care recipient record or the care recipient does not have a valid NSAF in the system and reject the form, see Table 2
    • Note: if the ACER form includes inaccurate information, reject the ACER form and ask the aged care service to resend the form, see Table 2
    • mark the ACER form as complete in PaNDA and add an appropriate PaNDA note, see Work Optimiser for staff for details
    • procedure ends here

4

Check for existing entry event + Read more ...

  • Check the care recipient’s record for an existing entry event. See Step 9 in the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to search for a care recipient – (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table

If the care recipient has an existing entry event with another aged care service, the ACSP will automatically depart the care recipient from the existing aged care service.

The ACSP uses the date of entry and applies it to the new aged care service as recorded on the ACER form after the form has been registered.

5

Enter new entry event + Read more ...

  • Enter the new entry event in ACSP for the new aged care service. For more information, see Registering events in the Aged Care Staff Portal (Service in Context) table

Action award or settlement indicator on ACER form

Table 4: actions when a care recipient is declared as compensable on the Aged Care Entry Record (ACER) form. Service Australia’s role is to notify the Department of Health and Aged Care who will assess the compensation details for the care recipient and make a decision to apply a compensation payment reduction.

Step

Action

1

Process ACER form + Read more ...

Contact the aged care service to confirm that Question 12 Award or Settlement ticked on the ACER form is:

  • correct, or
  • to avoid unnecessary stoppage of eligible subsidies

Once confirmed the tick on the form is correct:

  • process the ACER form, and
  • escalate this to the Aged Care Programmes Branch

The Aged Care Programmes Branch will:

  • code in the compensation from the ACER form into the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP), see Table 3
  • notify the Department of Health and Aged Care by email that the care recipient has declared as compensable on the ACER form (Question 12 Award or Settlement)

2

Email and add care recipient notes + Read more ...

Send an email to Department of Health and Aged Care Compensation enquiries team

Include the below information in the email:

  • service name and ID
  • care recipient full name and ID
  • date of entry
  • liability type:
    • Workers' Compensation
    • Third Party Insurance
    • Common Law Settlement

Add a care recipient note indicating compensation award or settlement claim and date referred to the Compensation Recovery Team. See Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table.

3

Assessment and advice + Read more ...

Once the Department of Health and Aged Care has received advice from Services Australia, the Department of Health and Aged Care will assess compensation details for the care recipient and make a decision to apply a compensation payment reduction.

The Department of Health and Aged Care Compensation Recovery Team will:

  • apply the compensation payment reduction when determined the compensation payment includes an aged care component
  • manage the compensation in the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP) as appropriate. See Step 3 in the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal for Supplments and subsidies menu tab – (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table
  • advise the aged care service and care recipient of the outcome in writing

Create and maintain entry details of a care recipient in a home care service in ACMPS

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Log on to ACMPS and create an event + Read more ...

Log on to ACMPS:

  • On the Home screen, either select:
    • Event Create option in the Claim Events section, or
    • select the Claim Event option on the left hand side menu and select Event Create in the Create section
  • The Event: New screen displays

2

Add details + Read more ...

Optional fields will display and will vary depending on the type of event selected. The optional fields are:

  • Event ID
  • Online Unique ID
  • CRN Number
  • DVA Reference Number
  • Location Suburb
  • Sighted Current ACCR
  • Unf.Cap. Entry Date (Unfunded Capacity Entry Date)

Enter information in the below mandatory fields:

  • Service: contains the Service ID
    • key manually, or
    • search by selecting the icon to open the search dialog box
  • Care Recipient: contains the Care Recipient ID
    • key manually, or
    • search by selecting the icon to open the search dialog box
  • Type of Event: select Entry from the drop down list
  • Value: select the value from the drop down list
  • Requested Start:
    • the start date of the event can be entered manually, or
    • from the calendar (select icon to display the calendar). Note: the current day’s date is automatically populated in this field
  • Carer Status Code:
    • this field is enabled when the Type of Event field value is selected as Entry
    • select the appropriate value from the drop down list

3

Complete the entry event + Read more ...

On finalising the entry event, either:

  • select Save button
  • select Enter button

The status is updated to Processed upon clearing the validations.

Note:

  • an error message will display when the event fails the validation process and the status is updated to Rejected
  • select the Cancel button to update the status to Deleted
  • if the event being processed by a user, other than a delegate, is outside the 2-year variation limit, it is then forwarded to a delegate for approval/rejection

4

Register location details + Read more ...

On the ACMPS home screen:

  • Select Event Create option
  • Enter information in the mandatory fields below:
    • Service – enter in service id stated on form
    • Care Recipient – enter in care recipient id from form
    • Type of event – select Location Details from the drop down list
    • Requested start – the start date of the location as on form
    • Request end – the end date of the location. Note: when no date is stated, leave as the default date of 31.12.9999
    • Postcode – as stated on the form (if viability supplement is to be claimed correctly then the care recipient’s physical street address is needed)
      Note: a PO Box or service provider address cannot be entered
    • Location Suburb – as stated on the form – select the location from the drop down menu
  • Select Save
