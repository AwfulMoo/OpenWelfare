FAQs for Service Officers

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Question: What is the Home Equity Access Scheme?

Answer: The Scheme is a reverse mortgage like loan for older Australians secured against real estate they own in Australia.

The agency places a registered charge or caveat against the property title to protect the Commonwealth’s interest. You need to repay the loan payments received and the costs and compound interest.

See What is the Home Equity Access Scheme for more details.

2

Question: Is the loan available as a lump sum?

Answer: Yes, from 1 July 2022

See What is the Home Equity Access Scheme for more details.

3

Question: Am I eligible for a Home Equity Access Scheme loan?

Answer: You may be eligible for the Scheme loan if you:

  • are either Age Pension age or the partner of someone who is
  • are receiving or are qualified for Age Pension, Disability Support Pension or Carer Payment
  • are not subject to a bankruptcy or insolvency agreement, and
  • own Australian real estate that is insured

Someone who qualifies for a pension but does not actually receive payment. For example, if their income or assets are too high, can still meet the qualifying pension rule.

Refer the customer to:

  • online tool, Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website, or in
  • their Centrelink online account to see if they might be eligible and estimate how much they can borrow

See Eligibility for the Home Equity Access Scheme for more details.

4

Question: Is my partner eligible for a Home Equity Access Scheme loan?

Answer: Both you and your partner can get a loan under the Scheme but you both need to be eligible and apply for it.

To see whether you or your partner may be eligible go to the online tool, Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website.

See Eligibility for the Home Equity Access Scheme for more details.

5

Question: How much loan can I receive?

Answer:

  • Your loan payment is based on the Maximum Pension Rate and how much pension you get each fortnight
  • The combined fortnightly rate of pension and loan is capped at 150% of your maximum pension rate
  • You can choose the amount of loan per fortnight and the loan payment will automatically adjust to not go over the cap if your pension changes
  • If you don’t receive a pension payment you can get up to 150% of the Maximum Pension Rate as loan payments
  • If you choose to receive your loan as a lump sum advance, it may affect the amount of fortnightly loan payments you receive

The amount of accumulated debt you can build up depends on your age and value of the real estate you offer as security.

To see whether you might be eligible go to:

  • the online tool, Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website, or in
  • your Centrelink online account. This tool will help estimate how much you can borrow

See maximum loan amount (MLA) for more details.

6

Question: Are there any risks involved with the loan?

Answer: We recommend you seek independent legal or financial advice.

7

Question: What is the interest rate for the loan?

Answer:

  • The compound interest rate is currently 3.95% per annum
  • It is applied to the loan balance each fortnight
  • The interest rate is set by the Minister for Social Services

You can forecast a future loan balance:

  • using the Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website, or
  • through your Centrelink online account

See The Scheme loan balance, legal costs and compound interest for more details.

8

Question: How can I apply for the Scheme loan?

Answer:

  • For fortnightly loan payments, you can:
    • apply online, or
    • complete a paper form Home Equity Access Scheme partnered application form (SA310)/Home Equity Access Scheme single application form (SA496) at a Centrelink office, or post it to the Services Australia address included on the first page of the form
  • For an advance payment, you can apply online or complete the Home Equity Access Scheme variation form (SA497)

See Claiming a Home Equity Access Scheme loan for more details.

9

Question: What documents do I have to provide to support my loan application?

Answer: There is a range of information you will need to provide to support your loan application. This includes but is not limited to:

  • proof of identity information
  • a copy of the property title
  • Mortgage agreements, and other encumbrances or guarantees that are secured by your property
  • details of any intended future borrowings. See No Negative Equity Guarantee for more details
  • property rates notice, and
  • proof of insurance

If you have previously provided documents, you may not need to again.

Details of these can be found in the Scheme section of the Services Australia website: Supporting documents.

See Claiming a Home Equity Access Scheme loan for more details.

10

Question: Can I change how much loan I receive each fortnight?

Answer: Yes.

  • Any requests to change the amount can be done online or by completing a Home Equity Access Scheme variation form (SA497) (if you have a partner they need to consent to the request as well)
  • Your loan payments will automatically adjust so your combined pension and loan payments don’t go over 150% of your maximum pension rate

You can forecast a future loan balance:

  • using the Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website, or
  • through your Centrelink online account

See Rate Payable for more details.

11

Question: Are there charges or fees for the loan?

Answer: Yes.

You are responsible for the legal costs with registering and/or removing the charge or caveat against the property title. These costs are added to the loan debt.

Costs start at about $400 and can increase depending on circumstances. We will keep you informed during the application as to the expected costs.

You are not responsible for the valuation costs for the properties offered as security.

See Home Equity Access Scheme loan balance, legal cost and compound interest for more details.

12

Question: Can I stop and start the loan?

Answer: Yes. You can stop and start your loan online or by completing the Home Equity Access Scheme variation form (SA497).

If you have a partner, they need to sign the request as well.

Consent must be provided if a nominee makes the request and/or you have a partner. See Consent required for online requests for more details.

13

Question: How do I repay the loan?

Answer:

  • You don’t need to make regular repayments
  • If you wish to, you can pay the loan in full or in part, at any time
  • The loan needs to be repaid if you sell the property used to secure the loan, or from your estate (unless you wish to secure the loan against another suitable property)
  • Repayments can be made by:
    • BPAY®
    • debit or credit card through the Home Equity Access Scheme summary page in your Centrelink Online Account
  • cheque or money order via post to Home Equity Access Scheme made payable to Collector of Public Money – Services Australia

Repayments may impact the No Negative Equity Guarantee.

See No Negative Equity Guarantee for more details.

14

Question: How often do I receive a loan statement?

Answer: Currently, Services Australia gives an itemised Loan Statement to customers annually.

We will send you an itemised Statement around your birthday. If you are partnered the statement will be sent around the birthday of the younger partner.

View your transaction summary online. Customers can request an itemised statement online from:

  • the Home Equity Access Summary page, Home Equity Access Summary -> view transactions -> request statement, or
  • Request a Document service, Documents and appointments - > request a document -> Home Equity Access Scheme statement

See Home Equity Access Scheme loan balance, legal cost and compound interest for more details.

15

Question: Is the loan taxable?

Answer: No.

16

Question: Does a Home Equity Access Scheme loan affect my pension?

Answer: Taking out a Home Equity Access Scheme loan will not reduce your pension. Your pension might increase if you are paid under the asset test and the Scheme loan is secured against property that is counted in the assets test.

17

Question: I am a current Home Equity Access Scheme customer; can you tell me my current loan balance?

Answer: Yes.

Online:

Customers can view details online about their loan, including their loan balance. These details will show on the Home Equity Access Scheme summary page in the customers Centrelink online account.

Service Officers can go to:

  • Process Direct:
    • Select Search Customers
    • Search using their name and/or CRN in the relevant fields
    • Key ‘PLS’ in Superkey field, select [Enter]
  • Customer First/Customer Record
    • Key ‘PLS’ in Nxt: field, select [Enter]

Both screens display the customers:

  • current loan number
  • a breakdown of the loan
  • any interest charged
  • the costs involved with registering the Charge/Caveat on the real estate used to secure the loan
  • the combined total loan amount

18

Question: What is a No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG)?

Answer:

See No Negative Equity Guarantee for more details.

Table 3: this table provides examples of the fortnightly Scheme loan payments (LON component) and lump sum advance payments (LONA component). See Advance payments for Home Equity Access Scheme loans for more details.

The Maximum Pension Rate (MPR) is the sum of the maximum basic rate of pension, and Pension Supplement, Energy Supplement and Rent Assistance (RA) the customer is eligible for under the Pension Rate Calculator. RA paid with Family Tax Benefit (FTB) is not included in the maximum payment rate for the Scheme purposes.

The maximum Scheme rate is 1.5 times the MPR (150% equivalent).

These amounts may not be current. They are examples only. For current payments, see Rates and thresholds.
