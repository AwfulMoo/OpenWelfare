Customer enters prison + Read more ...

When a customer enters prison, it may not be possible to discuss how a suspension or cancellation of payments may impact on their Income Management or Cashless Debt Card. Their income support payment will be suspended and all Income Management allocations will continue to be paid until income managed funds are exhausted or Income Management ends. 3

Income Management trained Service Officers + Read more ...

Income Management trained Service Officers will explain the impact of suspension/cancellation of payments on Income Management and, where the customer has a BasicsCard, determine if the card is to be suspended or closed. If appropriate, the Service Officer will conduct a Disbursement interview with the customer and advise them how any remaining Income Management funds will be disbursed.

4

Determine if payment is suspended or cancelled + Read more ...

The impact on income support differs depending on whether the customer's income support payment is suspended or cancelled.

If the customer's payment is:

  • suspended, go to Step 5
  • cancelled, go to Step 6

5

Customer's payment is suspended + Read more ...

If a customer's income support payment is suspended, Income Management will continue until the payment is restored. If the income support payment is subsequently cancelled, Income Management will automatically end.

The following action should be taken, when the customer enters prison and their payments are or will be suspended:

  • Priority needs - if the customer's Income Management account has a credit balance, these funds will be available to them for their priority needs. It is highly likely imprisonment will have changed the customer's priority needs, however consideration should be given to customers who may be supporting family members using their income managed funds whilst in prison
  • Income Management expenses - until the customer can be contacted, a customer's Income Management expenses can be stopped when they are admitted to prison. This should occur immediately where the priority need is no longer relevant (for example, regular allocation to BasicsCard), until discussions can be held with the customer. If no action is taken to end Income Management expenses, they will continue to be paid until income managed funds are exhausted or Income Management ends. Where Income Management expenses continue to be paid, recovery of funds may be required following discussions with the customer. For more information see Recall of Income Management funds
  • BasicsCard - if the customer will be imprisoned for less than 13 weeks, the BasicsCard should be suspended upon entry to prison to avoid unauthorised use. If the customer's payment is subsequently restored, the BasicsCard can be reactivated for immediate use by the customer
  • Arrears payments - if a customer is paid an arrears payment, it will be 100% income managed. An Unrestricted Cash payment (UCP) may be payable if the customer requests
  • Payment Nominee: customers may choose to appoint a payment nominee during their period of imprisonment. If appointed, the payment nominee will be responsible for managing a customer's Income Management funds. For more information see Nominee arrangements under Income Management

If required, Service Officers can seek support from a Prison Liaison Officer to confirm admission or release details. See Resources for a link to the Prison Liaison Officer contacts intranet page.

For further information which may assist, see:

  • Admission process for incarcerated customers
  • Effect on Income Management when payment is restored or re-granted
  • Coding Income Management expenses
  • Restricted Direct Payment (RDP)
  • Unrestricted Cash payment (UCP)

Procedure ends here.

6

Customer's income support payment is cancelled + Read more ...

If a customer's income support payment is cancelled, Income Management will automatically end. Customers are notified in writing of Income Management ending and invited to contact Services Australia to discuss how they would like any remaining Income Management funds paid.

The following will occur when a customer's Income Management ends when entering prison:

  • Income Management expenses - Income Management expenses will continue to pay for 14 days. If the customer's payment is not likely to be restored within 14 days, regular expenses should be ended when notification is received of the customer entering prison
  • BasicsCard - as the customer will not be able to use their BasicsCard while in prison, the card should be closed upon entry to prison to avoid any unauthorised use
  • Disbursement of residual funds - incarcerated customers are unable to attend Disbursement interviews. When Income Management automatically ends, any balance in their Income Management Account will be disbursed under the Auto Disbursement rules. Funds are paid to the customer's last known payment destination. It is therefore important payment destination details are correct for Service Reason 'GPY'
  • Payment Nominee - customers may choose to appoint a payment nominee during their period of imprisonment. If appointed, any residual funds will be paid to the payment nominee's nominated bank account. For more information see Nominee arrangements under Income Management

If required, Service Officers can seek support from a Prison Liaison Officer to confirm admission or release details. See Resources for a link to the Prison Liaison Officer contacts intranet page.

Customer leaves prison

Step

Action

1

Prisoners approaching release from a correctional centre + Read more ...

For Cashless Debit Card customers, go to Table 3.

Income Management incarcerated customers may be eligible to claim an income support payment within 21 days prior to their expected release date.

In most cases, Prison Liaison Officers may arrange a pre-release interview with the correctional facility to assist customers with claiming a Crisis Payment (CrP) and Anticipated Payment, if eligible.

Service Officers who are contacted by a customer immediately after leaving prison should assist the customer as per current business process.

See Resources for a link to the Prison Liaison Officer contacts and Centrelink pre-release processes intranet pages.

2

Income Management customer leaving prison + Read more ...

When a customer leaves prison, and their payment is restored or they are granted an income support payment, Income Management may automatically be reinstated, or they may be automatically identified for Income Management due to their circumstances.

  • Customers whose payments were income managed under a compulsory measure prior to entering prison, which are subsequently restored when leaving prison, will automatically have Income Management reinstated. Even where a customer moves out of a declared Income Management area, Income Management may continue depending on the measure. For more information see Customer moves out of an Income Management area
  • Customers granted an income support payment, who move to a declared Income Management area when leaving prison, may be identified for Income Management due to their circumstances. For more information see Identification and eligibility for Income Management

3

Income Management reinstated + Read more ...

If a customer's Income Management has been automatically reinstated on leaving prison, Service Officers should conduct an interview with the customer to:

  • remind them how Income Management will affect their payments
  • review their priority needs and set up any new Income Management deductions
  • reactivate a previously suspended BasicsCard or issue a new or replacement BasicsCard, if required
  • remind customers of the Income Management and BasicsCard self service options available
  • where applicable, discuss an exemption (Disengaged Youth and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient customers only) and exclusions (auto VWPR customers only) from Income Management
  • consider referral to an appropriate specialist as per current business process

4

Prison Release Crisis Payment (CrP) + Read more ...

  • Liquid assets for a CrP: if a customer applies for a Crisis Payment on release from prison, the funds available in a customer’s Income Management account should be taken into consideration as liquid assets when determining whether the customer is in severe financial hardship for eligibility to receive CrP
  • Customer's payment are income managed: if an Income Management customer is granted a CrP, the CrP will not be income managed
  • CrP due to release from prison or psychiatric confinement: a CrP due to release from prison or psychiatric confinement may result in the customer being automatically identified for VWPR Income Management for a 12 month period

5

Prison release customers under the age of 25 moving to a VWPR location + Read more ...

Young people released from prison will automatically be identified for VWPR Income Management if they:

  • are under the age of 25
  • live in a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) Income Management area, and
  • have received a CrP due to prison release within 13 weeks of meeting other eligibility criteria for this measure of Income Management

Prison Liaison Officers and Service Officers should explain to customers the reason for being identified for Income Management is not solely due to them being granted a CrP.

For more information, see:

  • Eligibility for Income Management under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) measure
  • Initial Assessment interview for Vulnerable Welfare Payment
  • Exclusions for automatically triggered Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) Youth Income Management for Service Officers
  • Auto Income Management and suspension of payments when a customer fails to attend Initial Assessment interview

6

Voluntary Income Management + Read more ...

Customers who leave prison and who have, or will be, moving to a declared Income Management location, should be advised of their option to apply for Voluntary Income Management where they meet the eligibility criteria.

The customer may choose to volunteer for Income Management if they believe it will help them manage their finances and assist in meeting their priority needs, and in some cases may act as a harm reduction strategy.

7

Anticipated Payment on release from prison + Read more ...

If customer's payments are income managed when an Anticipated Payment has been granted, the payment will be income managed at the percentage applicable to their measure of Income Management.

2

Customer enters prison + Read more ...

When a CDC participant enters prison, the Cashless Debit Card and account will remain active.

If the participant contacts regarding these funds they should be advised that there is no change in the way they access CDC money.

If possible, participants should be encouraged to cease any direct debit or other regular payments from their CDC account to prevent rejected transfers occurring while imprisoned.

Procedure ends here

3

Customer leaves prison + Read more ...

When a participant leaves prison, they will continue on the program if their payment is restored or re-granted without a gap.

  • If the participant requires a new card, see Temporary Cashless Debit Cards (CDC)
  • If the participant requires access to CDC funds, see Transferring Cashless Debit Card funds
  • If the participant has requested a Housing or Other Expenses limit, see Housing and Other Expense Limits for Cashless Debit Card (CDC) participants

For transaction and card support, the participant can contact the card provider directly. Resources page contains a link.

If the participant is claiming a new payment, and they meet the eligibility criteria, they will be placed on the CDC. See Participation in Cashless Debit Card (CDC).
