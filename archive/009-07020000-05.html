Carer Adjustment Payment

Carer Adjustment Payment (SS454) claim form

The CAP claim form is used by:

  • Services Australia to ensure families are offered all payments and services they may be entitled to
  • Department of Social Services (DSS) to determine eligibility and the amount to be paid

Advise eligibility criteria but do not discourage carers from claiming. If there is any doubt, a CAP claim form should be lodged.

The form contains a number of Stop questions that prompt the carer to call Services Australia.

FAQs - CAP claim process and claim form

Item

General questions and answers

1

Question: What is the correct response if a carer asks about the progress of their claim? + Read more ...

Answer: All CAP claims are scanned onto the carer's record. Wagga Carers Smart Centre (WPH) will forward the CAP claim with additional information to the Department of Social Services (DSS).

Claims are considered on a case by case basis, the decision on CAP claims is made by a senior officer in DSS and their decision is returned to the WPH.

Generally, the claim assessment process takes 12 to 15 weeks, once the claim is lodged with Services Australia.

The WPH contact carers by phone and/or letter with the outcome of the DSS delegate's decision. The letter from Services Australia includes a letter from DSS detailing the reasons for their decision.

Note: if the carer wants to know when their claim will be finalised, send an email to the Seniors, Carers, Nominees and Bereavement Programme Team including the carer's name, phone number and Customer Reference Number (CRN). The Seniors, Carers, Nominees and Bereavement Programme Team or the WPH will contact the carer about the progress of the claim. The carer should never be advised to contact DSS directly. The Resources page contains a link to the email.

2

Question: Is there a fact sheet available? + Read more ...

Answer: Carers should be issued with the Carer Adjustment Payment (SS454) claim form which provides all the details about the payment.

A number of questions in the claim form have prompts for carers to ring Services Australia. Information relating to these specific questions is included below.

3

Question: Can carers apply for CAP if they are receiving a Centrelink payment? + Read more ...

Answer: If carers are receiving CA or Family Tax Benefit only they can apply for CAP.

If they are receiving an income support payment such as CP or JobSeeker Payment (JSP) they may not be eligible for the CAP.

Note: if they have become eligible for an income support payment after the catastrophic event, they may claim CAP for a period after the catastrophic event occurred but before income support was paid.

4

Question: Will carers have reporting obligations if they get CAP + Read more ...

Answer: No, there are no reporting obligations for CAP.

5

Question: How long does the claim process take? + Read more ...

Answer: There is no definite time frame that can be advised to carers who have lodged claims but processing may take 12 to 15 weeks from date of lodgement.

6

Question: How will the carer be notified of the outcome of their claim? + Read more ...

Answer: Carers are contacted by Services Australia about the Department of Social Services (DSS) delegate's decision. This contact involves a phone call if possible. A covering letter advising the outcome of this decision is also sent from the department, together with a letter from DSS about the reasons for their decision.

Note: information about CAP claims, including the DSS delegate's decision, is retained by the department on the carer's paper file. Limited information (including DOCs and grant/rejection details) is retained on the department's system.

7

Question: What is the review process for CAP decisions? + Read more ...

Answer: The current review process for CAP decisions is an interim measure and is described in the Process page.

The review process for these decisions is unique to this payment.

8

Question: Where do staff go for more information or to ask questions? + Read more ...

Answer: If the carer contacts to discuss the length of time in finalising their claim, please send an email to Seniors, Carers, Nominees and Bereavement Programme Team. Include the carer's name, phone number and Customer Reference Number (CRN). The Resources page contains a link to the email.

9

Question: Why do I (and my partner) need to give permission for my information to be given to a third party? + Read more ...

Answer: Eligibility for the CAP is not assessed by Services Australia. If the carer (and their partner) do not agree to the agency providing their information to the Department of Social Services (DSS), the agency will be unable to send the claim form to them to assess their eligibility.

10

Question: Does it matter if I or my partner fill in the form? + Read more ...

Answer: The form should be completed by the person who is in receipt of Carer Allowance (CA) for the child concerned.

11

Question: If I (or my partner) receive an income support payment can I still make a claim for CAP? + Read more ...

Answer: If the carer (or their partner) has become eligible for an income support payment after the catastrophic event, they may still be able to claim CAP for a period after the catastrophic event occurred but before income support was paid.

If the carer (and their partner) have become ineligible for any income support payment after the catastrophic event, they may still be able to claim CAP for a period after income support was stopped.

12

Question: How old does my child need to be to claim CAP? + Read more ...

Answer: If the child was not aged 0-6 at the date of diagnosis and the claim was not lodged within 2 years of the diagnosis CAP may not be available.

13

Question: Can I still continue with this claim for CAP if I have not completed my application for Carer Allowance (CA)? + Read more ...

Answer: Yes. The CAP claim will be held by Services Australia until after the claim for CA has been processed. The carer should return their claim for CA to the department within 14 days of the date it was given to them, or they should contact the department if they are not able to do this.

14

Question: How will my claim be assessed? + Read more ...

Answer: Every application for CAP is considered on a case-by-case basis. The diagnosis of a specific illness, disability or medical condition, including congenital conditions, does not automatically preclude or guarantee eligibility for the payment.

15

Question: If I share the care of my child with other members of our family will I still be able to claim CAP? + Read more ...

Answer: If the child requires full time care but the carer is not providing this full time care on a daily basis, and the carer's partner or other members of the family are assisting, they should provide further details about their circumstances. They must still be in receipt of CA for the child to be eligible for CAP.
