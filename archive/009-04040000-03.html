4

Contact carer + Read more ...

Contact the carer to confirm details in the following situations.

Care receiver getting CA for another adult care receiver

The care receiver cannot qualify for CA for another disabled adult. Before granting, rejecting or cancelling any CA payments, additional information is required from the carer and the care receiver to clarify the circumstances and whether the care receiver has ceased providing care. Where another person is already/still providing care for the care receiver, see Change of Care for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) customers.

When determining the start date for CA, the decision is independent of whether the care receiver has been paid CA, the date CA was cancelled or whether the care receiver is entitled to CA. The start date determination should follow the normal start date procedures that apply for new claims.

Multiple CP claims/care receivers

If a carer lodges a claim for CP for more than one care receiver, or multiple claims for CP for 2 or more carer receivers, it is deemed that the customer has lodged a claim for each care receiver. Each claim must be assessed. If the carer is eligible for more than one care receiver, a determination of who the carer is claiming for must be made.

More than one eligible care receiver

Contact the carer to discuss:

  • CP can be paid for only one care receiver
  • the circumstances of the carer and care receivers
  • the implications of the decision on bereavement provisions
  • which care receiver they want to claim for

Document contact with the carer on the carer's record. If the carer cannot be contacted, a letter must be sent requesting they contact before the claim is finalised.

When the carer advises which care receiver they want to claim for:

  • withdraw the additional claims, and
  • finalise the claim for that care receiver

If the carer cannot be contacted and does not respond to the request to contact Services Australia, reject the claims.

Multiple care receivers:

  • if no care receivers are eligible, reject the claim for each care receiver using the most appropriate reason
  • if only 1 care receiver is eligible, reject the claim for each of the ineligible care receivers before processing the claim for the eligible care receiver
  • create a SOA for each care receiver for action

All claims must be finalised, unless withdrawn.

Streaming of claims + Read more ...

All processing staff are to stream claims before making a referral or placing the claim on hold.

Streaming is the practice of coding of all information that has been provided or is available when the claim is first allocated, including:

  • Identity for carer and care receiver
  • Medical Information
  • Income and Asset details
  • Care Receiver information
  • Any other information that requires coding, provided with the claim

This is to ensure that claims are not being placed on hold unnecessarily, where a decision could be made about the claim.

An example of this is, where it is identified that a claim requires a referral to an external assessment such as a Complex Assessment Officer, but the medical report provided indicates that the customer does not meet medical qualification for the respective payment.

There is sufficient information to make a decision about this claim without proceeding with a referral.

Note: for claims that have been streamed, see Hold to user new claim criteria to determine if claim can be held to user.

Request additional information or documents + Read more ...

Information/documents listed as requested in Tasks do not need to be asked for again.

Where claims received are incomplete or incorrect (missing claim/module information or clarification is needed), processing staff can accept and use verbal information from the customer.

If verbal information is not possible or practical to obtain, send a written request.

For online claims or ACC, issue a written request by selecting > Request documents. See the Process Direct - Request Document table of Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM).

For paper claims, or a SOA new claim, issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter.

The History Summary (HS) screen will also show information/documents requested. If more information is needed to support the carer's claim, the request needs to detail the information originally requested as well as the extra information.

If requested documents have not been provided within 22 days from the date of the original notice, the claim can be finalised (rejected, reason FRC).

Note: if it is clear the claim will be rejected, record the information already provided with the claim including care needs/THP. It is not necessary to request more information (e.g. bank statements) before the claim can be rejected.

Review/update relationships + Read more ...

Relationships must be reviewed/updated before starting to process a claim. These include partner, child and care receiver relationships.

Action required depends on the carer's circumstances, what they have advised on their claim and what is already recorded on their record (confirmed data). If the customer's partner used the Temporary Access Code (TAC) provided at the end of the claim and confirmed the relationship using the 'Partner Confirmation Logon' service on the Services Australia website, this will display as a DMOPD tile and will be available to view in scanned documents.

See Documents required for Centrelink new claims.

In some cases, the claim may need regenerating to allow changes to relationships.

View/update details via the Transaction Summary (TS) screen in the Link Summary (LS) and Marital Status (MS) tables.

Relationship details may only need updating if the tables contain different information to the information provided in the claim.

Note: only delete/edit relationship data if recorded in error. If the relationship data is no longer applicable, for example, the carer has married, add a new line.

To correct a relationship (for example, the relationship is displaying as NRE - Not Required), select:

  • Edit icon to update the status
  • Save

To end or add a new relationship:

  • select above the relevant table
  • in the Create Relationship / Martial Status pop-up, key the event date and the relationship status
  • if becoming partnered:
    • select the Search Criteria icon
    • key mandatory details of Last Name and Date of Birth, or enter the partner's Customer Reference Number (CRN) in the External Type/No field
    • select Go to search for the partner's record
    • from the search results, select the option button to select the CRN
    • update the Partner Permitted to Enquire field
  • Save

If the function to update details in these tables is not available, select > Regenerate claim and then return to update details.

Intention to claim for vulnerable customers + Read more ...

Check the PRECLM screen in Process Direct to review whether intention to claim provisions may apply.

Note: some vulnerable circumstance reasons must be recorded manually. Review the customer's circumstances and identify if a circumstance needs to be recorded manually. To record a circumstance or for more information, see Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.

Note: where an automatic or manual circumstance has been recorded and the vulnerable circumstance date was within 8 weeks of the contact date, Process Direct will automatically adjust the start date if there was a contact within 14 days of the claim submission date. However, when adjusting the start date Process Direct does not take into account:

  • if the customer was qualified on that date (required to fully meet intention to claim provisions)
  • contact date for intent to claim is more than 14 days before claim lodgement
  • backdating provisions apply (in addition to intent to claim provisions)
  • other deemed claim provisions, such as inappropriate claims or incorrect claims

When processing the claim, the start date may need to be adjusted manually if:

  • the start date returned from ISIS is different to what Process Direct displayed on the PNA or CJBA screen and the customer was qualified on a subsequent contact date before the claim was submitted
  • intention to claim provisions apply and the customer is eligible for an extension to lodge up to 13 weeks after the contact date
  • other backdating provisions apply
  • inappropriate or incorrect claim provisions apply

Grant CP and/or CA + Read more ...

Before finalising the claim, view rate details on the Pension Rate Calculator (PRC) and Factors Affecting Rate (PFAR) screens to ensure the expected results are reflected in the provisional data. Both provisional and prior data displays so the changes can be identified.

If the carer is being granted CP and/or CA after a rejected claim, go to Step 8.

Age Pension to CP transfers

If the carer receives Age Pension, lodges a claim for CP and meets the eligibility and payability criteria for CP, contact the carer to explain that any existing qualification and grandfathered status from their existing payment will cease once CP is granted. It is important to discuss the benefits of each payment to determine if CP is still wanted. See Eligibility for Carer Payment (CP) and Transfers to Carer Payment (CP).

JobSeeker Payment (JSP) to CP transfers

If the carer receives JSP, a Benefit Transfer will occur within the new claim activity, cancelling JSP and granting CP for the period of entitlement and paying the calculated amount of arrears for CP (less any JSP received for the same period):

  • ensure the arrears of CP exceed the amount of JSP paid for the period of entitlement. If this does not occur, contact the Carer Helpdesk
  • if CP entitlement CP ceased before the date of claim processing, ensure there is no overpayment from the date CP entitlement was lost (cancellation date) to the date CP claim was processed

Inviting claims

If a customer claims either CP or CA and they will qualify for the other payment, contact the customer to tell them of their eligibility to the other payment. Record a detailed DOC on the customer's record. Although the process is not mandatory, make 1 attempt to contact the customer.

8

Granting CP and/or CA after rejection + Read more ...

If granting payment following a rejection of the claim the Service Officer must be satisfied that the previously supplied information in relation to the carer's income, assets and the care provided to the care receiver is still accurate.

If not, current information must be obtained either verbally or in writing. To determine if new information is required, consider the following factors:

  • the amount of time between the initial claim and the date the claim will be processed
  • if the claim is for CP, CA or a combined claim
  • if the carer is in receipt of another income support payment
  • the income of the customer and their partner (if applicable) at the time of claim and whether these are ongoing
  • the assets of the customer and their partner (if applicable) at the time of claim
  • the likelihood of the customer returning to the workforce (employment or self-employment)
  • the likelihood of the care receiver leaving the carer's care or going into hospital or respite occurring
  • the likelihood of the care receiver's carer score or THP score reducing and if so the impact on the ADAT or DCLAD
  • any relevant factors

Processing + Read more ...

Where the combined CP and CA (child) claim will result in CP (child) granting and CA (child) rejecting and the carer is eligible for CA (auto):

  • do not finalise the combined CP and CA claim
  • cancel the combined claim
  • process the CA claim as an individual claim for CA (child)
  • finalise the rejection of the CA (child) claim. Do not inhibit the rejection letter

On the following day:

  • process the CP (child) component of the claim

  • go back to the ELD screen
