This page contains supplementary information for Complaints Officers in Centrelink and Medicare managing Level 2 complaints received directly from internal and external sources.

Investigate complaint from the Ombudsman, the Minister's Office, Members of Parliament and received via executive correspondence

The information provided in the section is supplementary to the complaints management processes in Level 2 complaints (CLK)(MED). Use the drop-down menu below to locate additional instructions for managing complaints from specific internal and external sources before returning to Level 2 complaints (CLK)(MED).

Customer Liaison Officer (CLO)

This table provides supplementary information for Complaints Officers when investigating and responding to complaints received via a Customer Liaison Officer (CLO).

Step

Action

1

Customer Liaison Officer (CLO) + Read more ...

The CLO is responsible for receiving and managing enquiries and feedback on behalf of Services Australia from the Minister's Office and other related business areas. If the CLO determines the issue is best placed with another business area for response, the issue will be forwarded and responses are required on the day of receipt or within 24 hours if received in the afternoon.

The CLO identifies and forwards complaints requiring response from the Level 2 team to the appropriate positional mailbox. The complaint is allocated to a Complaints Officer.

To manage the complaint:

  • immediately discuss any concerns about the vulnerability of the customer with the Team Leader or Manager
  • create a Customer Feedback Tool entry and include all details of the feedback. Update and maintain this complaint record during the management of the complaint
  • investigate and finalise the complaint
  • notify the customer of the outcome. If unable to contact the customer by phone, issue a finalisation letter
  • finalise the Customer Feedback Tool entry, recording all details of the actions taken, information provided and complaint remedy
  • update the CLO note on the customer's record with the contact and action taken

Return to Level 2 complaints (CLK)(MED) to continue managing the complaint.

Departmental Liaison Officer (DLO)

This table provides supplementary information for Complaints Officers when investigating and responding to complaints received via the Departmental Liaison Officer (DLO).

Step

Action

1

Departmental Liaison Officer (DLO) + Read more ...

Note: this procedure relates only to direct customer contact to the Minister's office.

When a customer contacts the Minister's Office, the DLO can email a request for follow up action to the Level 2 team or via a warm telephony transfer. As the DLO has had direct contact from the customer, they have implied authority to follow up on behalf of the customer.

Once received by the Level 2 Team, the request is allocated to a Complaints Officer. These requests require urgent action and the customer must be contacted on the day of receipt or within 24 hours if received in the afternoon.

To manage the complaint:

  • create a Customer Feedback Tool entry and include all details of the feedback. Update and maintain this complaint record during the management of the complaint
  • fully investigate the customer's record and resolve issues as required
  • contact the customer by phone on day of receipt to acknowledge the correspondence and respond to the concerns raised
  • provide confirmation by email to the DLO that the customer issue has been addressed

Return to Level 2 complaints (CLK)(MED) to continue managing the complaint.

Media, social media and Hank feedback

This table provides supplementary information for Complaints Officers when investigating and responding to complaints received via media, social media or Hank feedback.

Step

Action

1

Media and social media feedback + Read more ...

Media feedback is received by the Level 2 team via email from the Communication Division or Digital Media Section and is allocated to a Complaints Officer for management.

The Resources page contains a link to Services Australia's Media protocols.

When the Communication Division or Digital Media Section request an immediate response:

  • the Complaints Officer contacts the Complaints Officer's manager and provides a verbal response
  • the manager escalates the response
  • the Complaints Officer follows the processes below to manage the complaint, including providing a written response

Collaboration between the Multicultural and Tailored Services Branch and the relevant business area may be required to agree on how the complaint will be managed where the complaint includes issues such as co-location, policy change etc.

Identify any sensitive, high profile or significant service failure issues and escalate to the manager, these include:

  • issues that may have a significant impact on the agency or customer
  • high profile issues including a change in process or policy that is topical or political, for example co-location, emergency management
  • Sensitives issues including:
    • customer vulnerability (family violence, critical health issues, homelessness)
    • current media attention
    • poor management that may affect the agency's reputation, and
    • significant privacy or security incidents, such as complaints about a Senior Executive staff member or a significant staff code of conduct matter
  • an allegation of staff fraud

To manage the complaint:

  • immediately discuss any concerns about the vulnerability of the customer with the team leader or manager
  • create a Customer Feedback Tool entry and include all details of the feedback. Update and maintain this complaint record during the management of the complaint
  • contact the customer:
    • on the same day the media feedback email is received in cases of immediate or swift response, or
    • within 24 hours of the media feedback email being allocated to acknowledge the feedback
  • investigate and finalise the complaint
  • notify the customer of the outcome of the investigation. If unable to contact the customer by phone, issue a finalisation letter
  • finalise the Customer Feedback Tool recording all details of the actions taken, information provided and complaint remedy
  • record all information in the response email template and email to either the Communication Division or Digital Media Section (whichever allocated the complaint)

Go to Step 3

2

Hank feedback + Read more ...

Customers provide feedback and complaints via media sites, social networks and emails directly to the General Manager of Communications.

The Digital Media Section posts a response to the customer on the social media channel asking the customer to email Hank so he can investigate and have their concerns addressed when there is the requirement to arrange for further follow up.

When an email is received in the Hank positional mailbox it is forwarded to the Level 2 team and is allocated to a Complaints Officer.

When the Communication Division or Digital Media Section request an immediate response:

  • the Complaints Officer contacts the Complaints Officer's manager and provides a verbal response
  • the manager escalates the response
  • the Complaints Officer follows the processes below to manage the complaint, including providing a written response

To manage the complaint:

  • Immediately discuss any concerns about the vulnerability of the customer with the team leader or manager
  • Create a Customer Feedback Tool entry and include all details of the feedback. Update and maintain this complaint record during the management of the complaint
  • Contact the customer within 24 hours of the complaint being allocated and advice customer that the feedback is being investigated
  • Investigate and finalise the complaint
  • Notify the customer of the outcome. If unable to contact the customer by phone, issue a finalisation letter
  • Finalise the Customer Feedback Tool entry, recording all details of the actions taken, information provided and complaint remedy
  • Send a response email to the Hank positional mailbox summarising the complaint management and remedy applied

3

Pre-finalisation quality assurance and finalisation + Read more ...

A pre-finalisation check is conducted on all Level 2 complaints from customers referred to the media or Hank.

Complaints Officers:

  • email the manager or designated Quality Assurance checker to request a pre-finalisation quality assurance check on the complaint
  • record the complaint in the Customer Feedback Tool

Return to Level 2 complaints (CLK)(MED) to continue managing the complaint.

This table provides supplementary information for Complaints Officers when investigating and responding to complaints received via a Member of Parliament.

Step

Action

1

Member of Parliament (MP) feedback + Read more ...

An MP or a staff member from their office may contact the agency with a complaint on behalf of a constituent via:

  • their MP contact officer in a local service centre, go to Step 2
  • the Complaints and Feedback line, go to Step 3
  • one of the agency's business lines, go to Step 3

2

How to record a complaint - MP Contact Officer in service centres + Read more ...

The agency provides Contact Officers in service centres for local MPs, Senators or Electorate Offices. They communicate directly with the Local Member or their representative to address any enquiries raised by a constituent.

  • The Contact Officer manages the complaint applying First Contact Resolution.
  • Every contact from an MP, Senator or Electorate Office must be recorded and finalised or escalated in the Customer Feedback Tool.

Note: staff recording a Medicare complaint or feedback access the CFT via the Customer First Front of House Application see the Commence Recording Feedback table in Recording customer complaints and feedback in the Customer Feedback Tool.

In the Customer Details section record:

  • Feedback Channel: drop down menu - select phone or email only as the source of the feedback
  • Date Received: field - select the date the MP/Senator provided the feedback (defaults to today's date)
  • Who is lodging the Feedback: drop down menu - select Member of Parliament or Senator

Can the complaint be resolved locally by the Contact Officer?

  • Yes, refer to Level 1 - Managing customer complaints and feedback to continue managing the complaint
  • No, go to Step 3

For Child Support information, see Parliamentarian complaints regarding Child Support customers.

3

Complaint Escalation + Read more ...

MP/Senator contacts of a technically complex, urgent or sensitive nature may be escalated. This may include complaints with multiple issues, identified vulnerabilities or issues requiring extensive investigation.

Note: MP/Senator Contact Officers (Centrelink and Medicare) can escalate a complaint via the CFT.

Does the complaint meet escalation reason 3 to 8?

  • No, refer to Level 1 - Managing customer complaints and feedback to continue managing the complaint
  • Yes, continue with step 3 process

In the Customer Details section record:

  • Feedback Channel: drop down menu - select phone or email only as the source of the feedback
  • Date Received: field - select the date the MP/Senator provided the feedback (defaults to today's date)
  • Who is lodging the Feedback: drop down menu - select Member of Parliament or Senator
  • Select the appropriate escalation reason in the Customer Feedback Tool entry (only reasons 3-8 should be selected)

In the Customer Feedback section or an escalation management note, record:

  • name of the MP
  • MP office contact details
  • action requested by the MP
  • contact officer response preference

Note: when reviewing a claim issue ensure all claims are complete and correct prior to escalation, see Claims received that are incomplete or incorrect.

For Child Support information, see Parliamentarian complaints regarding Child Support customers.

Refer to Level 2 complaints (CLK)(MED) to continue managing the complaint.

Ministerial feedback

This table provides supplementary information for Complaints Officers when investigating and responding to complaints received via Ministerial feedback.

Step

Action

1

Ministerial feedback + Read more ...

Ministerial correspondence refers to letters or emails sent to the Minister about Services Australia. The correspondence can come from a variety of sources, including:

  • Members of Parliament - Federal and State
  • organisations representing customers or the community
  • the general public, including constituents of Ministers

The Minister's Office may receive contact from a customer (response is via a Departmental Correspondence Brief) or from a local Member of Parliament or Senator (response is via a Ministerial Brief). The Ministerial Correspondence Team sends a package of documents to be completed, including a clearance sheet, brief etc.

Ministerial correspondence brief is received by the Level 2 team via email from the Ministerial Correspondence Team. Ministerial correspondence is allocated to a Complaints Officer.

The Complaints Officer:

  • creates a Customer Feedback Tool entry
  • contacts the customer where possible and provides a full explanation of the situation and apology if appropriate
  • prepares a Draft Correspondence Brief and Correspondence Summary Sheet for the Ministerial Correspondence Team, making sure:
    • the Draft Correspondence Brief and Correspondence Summary Sheet addresses all issues raised in the correspondence
    • the clearance sheet is completed, if required, including relevant background to issues without repeating history outlined in the letter
    • there is no assumed prior knowledge (avoid use of jargon and acronyms)
    • correct names, phone numbers, dates etc. are provided
    • references to relevant legislation are made (if applicable), and
    • copies of relevant letters to the customer are provided (if appropriate)
  • returns the Draft Correspondence Brief and Correspondence Summary Sheet to the Ministerial Correspondence Team on completion of a quality assurance check

The Ministerial Correspondence Team are responsible for completing the Correspondence Brief, preparing a letter for the Minister's signature and obtaining clearance and approvals from appropriate clearance officers.

2

Departmental Brief (customer complaint) + Read more ...

In all cases attempt to resolve the customer's concerns by phone contact. If phone contact is successful and the customer's issues are resolved, a formal written response is not required unless the customer requests this.

Where the customer's concerns have been resolved through phone contact respond to the departmental request by email using the approved template.

Where the customer requests a written response or phone contact was not successful, complete the Draft Correspondence Brief and Correspondence Summary Sheet, including enough detail to support the ministerial correspondence team in preparing the written response.

The proposed response should provide enough details to explain the circumstances surrounding the complaint, identify any issues and concerns and actions taken.

Go to Step 4

3

Ministerial Correspondence Briefing (MP or Senator complaint) + Read more ...

When a Ministerial Correspondence is received a formal written response to the Member of Parliament or Senator is required from the Minister's Office.

Complete the Draft Correspondence Brief and Correspondence Summary Sheet. Ensure enough detail is included to support the Ministerial Correspondence Team in preparing the written response.

If assistance is required in preparing and handling Ministerial Correspondence, refer to the Guide to preparing and handling Ministerial Correspondence. A link is included on the Resources page.

Go to Step 4

4

Writing the brief response + Read more ...

Complete the Draft Correspondence Brief and Correspondence Summary Sheet. Ensure enough detail is included to support the Ministerial Correspondence Team in preparing the written response, including detail explaining the circumstances surrounding the complaint and identifying any issues or concerns and actions taken.

The Resources page contains links to the following resources to help write the response:

  • Plain English Guidelines
  • Preferred Terms for Services Australia
  • The Australian Government Style Manual and Services Australia Writing Style Guide

Prepare draft response to the identified questions

Using the response template provided complete the details required.

Answer the question

When drafting the proposed response:

  • ensure the information provided answers the questions asked
  • do not include unnecessary or irrelevant information
  • provide a holistic response and identify any outstanding issues that may not have been initially identified

Attach any relevant documents

Provide sufficient information to the Minister's Office to carry out their investigation. Relevant evidence should be attached in a PDF format and be clearly identified.

Return to Level 2 complaints (CLK)(MED) to continue managing the complaint.

Ombudsman complaint transfers

This table provides supplementary information for Complaints Officers when investigating and responding to complaints received via the Ombudsman.

Step

Action

1

Ombudsman complaint transfers + Read more ...

When contacted by a complainant the Ombudsman's Office will determine if an investigation is required by their office or make a referral to Services Australia to address the customer's feedback through the usual complaint handling process.

Ombudsman complaint transfers are received by the Level 2 team via email from the Commonwealth Ombudsman and are allocated to the appropriate program for action and response.

2

Allocation of complaint + Read more ...

Ombudsman complaint transfers are received from the Ombudsman's office via email. Upon receipt, the complaints are sent by the Centrelink External Complaints workload manager to the appropriate program for action and response.

3

Vulnerability factors + Read more ...

The Ombudsman may indicate the complainant's circumstances or other risk factors classifies them as vulnerable. This includes:

  • complainant's circumstances, such as financial hardship, geographical isolation, homelessness, imminent medical treatment, most applicants for early release of superannuation
  • risk factors, such as age, disability, balance of power, caring responsibilities, lack of basic skills such as literacy or language difficulties

If the Ombudsman identifies a complainant as vulnerable, see process for complaints from a vulnerable customer

4

Contact customer and record complaint + Read more ...

When an Ombudsman complaint transfer is received:

  • record a Customer Feedback Tool entry for the complaint. If a Level 2 complaint is already in progress, go to Step 5
  • conduct a preliminary review and assessment of the customer's record and contact the customer by phone. The contact attempts must occur on or before the date stated by the Commonwealth Ombudsman Investigation Officer in the complaint transfer email
  • consider the priority and complexity of the issues when determining customer contact and the number of attempts made
  • document all attempts to contact the customer in the Customer Feedback Tool on the dates the attempts are made
  • if phone contact is unsuccessful send a 'referral from Ombudsman complaint acknowledgement letter' to the customer and attach to the Customer Feedback Tool entry
  • record all details in the Customer Feedback Tool. Create an investigation plan to map out actions required to resolve the complaint. Update and maintain this record during the management of the complaint
  • finalise the Customer Feedback Tool entry

5

Manage complaint if Level 2 already in progress + Read more ...

If there is already a Level 2 complaint in progress regarding the same issue:

  • record a new Customer Feedback Tool entry for the complaint. In the new entry document that the issues raised in the Ombudsman's complaint are being addressed in the current Level 2 complaint and record the current complaint identifier number
  • in the existing Customer Feedback Tool record document that the issues raised in the complaint have been received in a complaint transfer and record the identifier number of the new Customer Feedback Tool entry for the complaint transfer and finalise the entry
  • if another Complaints Officer is managing the current complaint, contact the owning Complaints Officer to advise of the complaint transfer
  • finalise the existing complaint

The Complaints Officer managing any Ombudsman complaint transfer will:

  • record a Customer Feedback Tool entry for the complaint
  • conduct a preliminary review and assessment of the customer's record and contact the customer by phone. The contact attempts must occur on or before the date stated by the Commonwealth Ombudsman Investigation Officer in the complaint transfer email
  • consider the priority and complexity of the issues when determining customer contact and the number of attempts made
  • document all attempts to contact the customer in the Customer Feedback Tool
  • if phone contact is unsuccessful send a 'referral from Ombudsman complaint acknowledgement letter' to the customer and attach to the Customer Feedback Tool entry
  • record all details in the Customer Feedback Tool. Create an investigation plan to map out actions required to resolve the complaint. Update and maintain this record during the management of the complaint
  • Finalise the Customer Feedback Tool entry

Note: if the Complaints Officer is unable to contact the complainant during management of the complaint transfer, the Customer Feedback Tool record should not be finalised until the investigation is complete and an outcome letter has been issued to the customer.

Ombudsman section 8 and 12(4) investigations

This table provides supplementary information for Complaints Officers when investigating and responding to complaints received via Ombudsman section 8 and 12(4) referrals.

Step

Action

1

Ombudsman Section 8 and 12(4) investigations + Read more ...

When the Commonwealth Ombudsman's office commences an investigation into a complaint, notice is provided to Services Australia Ombudsman Relationship Management Section (ORMS) under section 8 or section 12(4) of the Ombudsman Act 1976. Upon receipt of the notice, ORMS refer the complaint to the most appropriate line area to prepare a response.

The Commonwealth Ombudsman's office sets out dates in which it requires a formal response to a complaint. If the Ombudsman requests a verbal response, go to Step 2.

To manage the complaint the Complaints Officer:

  • creates a Customer Feedback Tool entry
  • assesses the complaint to ensure their team is responsible and best placed to respond
  • notifies ORMS if the complaint should be referred to another area
  • seeks input from business areas where a response is required (The Commonwealth Ombudsman's office provides a summary of the issues as related to them by the complainant and provides a number of questions for the agency's response)
  • gathers all relevant information to provide a thorough response, checking for relevant information in:
    • agency files (paper and electronic)
    • customer records and files (paper and electronic)
    • Government records
  • prepares a response that explains the circumstances surrounding the complaint and the action taken. When drafting the proposed response, the information provided must answer the questions asked

When responding to the complaint undertake the following actions:

  • Provide sufficient information to the Commonwealth Ombudsman's office to enable them to carry out their investigation. It is particularly helpful if evidence, which is relevant and compelling, is attached
  • Send all communication, including proposed responses and attachments, to ORMS
  • The proposed response needs to be reviewed by ORMS Quality and Clearance Team. ORMS will then manage the clearance and sign-off process by the appropriate SES Officer

If unable to meet any deadline, notify the manager so that if possible, an extension can be sought

Return to Level 2 complaints (CLK)(MED) to continue managing the complaint.

2

Verbal responses to the Ombudsman's office + Read more ...

A verbal response is sometimes requested when the Ombudsman's office is undertaking a section 8 investigation and has identified a customer as vulnerable.

A verbal response is an interim response that occurs prior to the completion of a request for a written response and is required by the Ombudsman's office in 48 hours.

On receipt of the questions requiring verbal response the Complaints Officer:

  • reviews, assesses and investigates the questions requiring verbal response
  • seeks clarification immediately if a question from the Ombudsman's office is not clear. Consults with the manager and if further clarification is required the Complaints Officer contacts the ORMS team
  • investigates and utilises all current and relevant reference material, technical support, customer record information in determining an appropriate response
  • prepares a draft response to the questions and forwards this to the manager

On receipt of the draft responses the manager:

  • reviews and assesses the responses provided for accuracy, correctness, appropriateness and addresses questions asked
  • requests clarification of information provided in the draft response from the Complaints Officer if required
  • refers all suggested changes or additional information for insert to the Complaints Officer for review and amendment

On completion of the verbal response process:

  • ORMS issues a verbal response File Note to the Complaints Officer
  • the Complaints Officer confirms or edits the content for accuracy of the File Note and returns it to the manager
  • the manager forwards the file note to the EL2 Service Manager for clearance to ORMS
  • the Complaints Officer attaches the File Note to the section 8 written response request on receipt of the approved File Note

Return to Level 2 complaints (CLK)(MED) to continue managing the complaint.

Privacy complaints

This table provides supplementary information for Complaints Officers when investigating and responding to complaints about privacy.

Step

Action

1

Complaints with an alleged privacy incident + Read more ...

Failure to report a privacy incident in a timely manner can result in serious consequences. Timely action can reduce the risk of physical harm to customers or other negative consequences.

On day 1 of receiving the privacy complaint, ensure the Privacy Incident - notification form is completed and forwarded to privacy@humanservices.gov.au, from the Escalated complaints mailbox, with a cc to your team leader.

A copy of the Privacy Incident - notification form is located on the Resources page.

All alleged privacy incidents must be referred to the Privacy team for review. The Privacy team will triage the privacy complaint within 24 (working) hours and provide a lexid number and advice on the next steps as soon as practicable to the Escalated Complaints mailbox. The complaints officer will liaise with privacy (via privacy@humanservices.gov.au quoting the lexid number) as to status of the complaint every 5 days and ensure the customer is kept updated.

Privacy complaints which can be resolved within a reasonable timeframe

In these cases, Privacy will liaise with the originating business area to obtain a business contact, for the complaints team to liaise with and determine how the customer will be managed. If there is nothing more you can action on the complaint pending the business area response, change the status of the complaint to "waiting response" and place the complaint on hold.

Privacy complaints which are complex and the Privacy team has advised the review process will be lengthy

The Privacy team will liaise with the business area where the privacy incident originated to ensure you can be provided with a business contact.

Advise the customer that:

  • the agency treats any allegation of privacy incidents seriously
  • the privacy incident has been referred so enquiries can be conducted
  • you will be closing their complaint
  • the business area will be advised of the outcome of the privacy incident complaint and the business area will contact the customer

Confirm how the customer would like to be contacted by the business to be advised of the outcome of their complaint - note details such as telephone number or email address

Finalise the complaint on the CFT.

Complains which present with both a privacy and another complaint issue

If there are additional issues to be investigated manage as per the usual Level 2 complaints (CLK) (MED) process.

Note: if a written response is to be provided to the customer regarding their privacy complaint, the Privacy team will draft or clear any privacy related content in your outcome letter. Forward all draft correspondence containing privacy matters to privacy@humanservices.gov.au (quoting the lexid number), with a cc to your team leader, via the Escalated complaints mailbox for clearance.

Proposed Recording of Administrative Deficiency (PRAD)

This table provides supplementary information for Complaints Officers when investigating and responding to Proposed Recording of Administrative Deficiency (PRAD).

Step

Action

1

Proposed Recording of Administrative Deficiency (PRAD) + Read more ...

The Ombudsman issues a PRAD to Services Australia if they are considering recording an administrative deficiency against the agency. The PRAD seeks a response from the agency to the proposed recording of an administrative deficiency.

The Complaints Officer:

  • refers to the initial response to the Ombudsman Section 8
  • reviews the customer's case
  • obtains relevant files and information
  • reviews relevant process and procedure
  • liaises with relevant stakeholders such as Program Advice, Service Support Officers, specialist business areas
  • prepares the draft response
  • sends the response to Services Australia Ombudsman Relationship Management Section (ORMS) on completion of the approval processes

2

Action the PRAD (Complaints Officer) + Read more ...

To action the PRAD, record the PRAD in the existing complaints record in the Customer Feedback Tool.

3

Investigate PRAD (Complaints Officer) + Read more ...

Review the investigation of the complaint that led to the PRAD to prepare the response.

Investigate by:

  • reviewing the initial response to the Ombudsman Section 8
  • reviewing the customer's record for decisions and referrals made
  • reviewing relevant policy and Operational Blueprint
  • liaising with relevant stakeholders such as Policy teams, Service Support Officers or Privacy Officers

4

Prepare PRAD response (Complaints Officer) + Read more ...

To prepare to respond to the PRAD:

  • consider:
    • the basis of the Ombudsman proposal to record administrative deficiency
    • if there are any differences between the agency's investigation and the facts presented by the Ombudsman in the PRAD
    • any other issues raised by the Ombudsman
    • remedies suggested by the Ombudsman
  • prepare the PRAD response
  • email the draft response to the Complaints Officer's manager
  • document on the Customer Feedback Tool that the draft PRAD has been sent to manager for review

Note: if an extension of time is required, contact Services Australia Ombudsman Relationship Management Section (ORMS) at least 10 days before the due date to negotiate a new due date.

5

Review PRAD (Program Manager) + Read more ...

Review the response letter to ensure the content is correct and addresses the issues raised by the Ombudsman.

Email the response letter:

  • to the Service Manager - External Complaints Resolution, or
  • to the Complaints Officer with feedback if the response requires amendment

6

Approve PRAD (Service Manager) + Read more ...

Review the response letter to ensure the content is correct and addresses the issues raised by the Ombudsman.

Email the response letter:

  • to the relevant Senior Executive Service Officer, if it is approved
  • to the Service Manager - External Complaints Resolution if the response is not approved and requires amendment, provide feedback to the External Complaints Officer

7

Finalise PRAD (Complaints Officer) + Read more ...

To finalise the PRAD, update the Customer Feedback Tool entry with the date the response letter was provided to ORMS.

Return to Level 2 complaints (CLK)(MED) to continue managing the complaint.

Secretarial and executive correspondence

This table provides supplementary information for Complaints Officers when investigating and responding to complaints received via Secretarial or executive correspondence.

Step

Action

1

Secretarial and executive response + Read more ...

A secretarial brief request results from direct contact with the Secretary of the agency or another senior executive correspondence. These are allocated to a Complaints Officer. The Secretary's office sends a package of documents to be completed, including a clearance sheet, brief etc.

In all cases a formal written response to the correspondent is required.

To manage the complaint:

  • immediately discuss any concerns about the vulnerability of the customer with the team leader or manager
  • create a record in the Customer Feedback Tool
  • fully investigate the customer's record and resolve issues as required
  • phone the customer to acknowledge the correspondence and respond to the concerns raised
  • draft a letter of response to the customer addressing the concerns raised and action taken
  • complete the clearance sheet with a summary of the issues raised, detail of the customer's circumstances, action taken, result of contact with the customer and a summary of the points addressed in the written response

Return to Level 2 complaints (CLK)(MED) to continue managing the complaint.

Complaints relating to ATO issues

This table provides supplementary information for Complaints Officers when investigating and responding to complaints received via Ministerial feedback.

Step

Action

1

Complaints relating to ATO issues + Read more ...

Complaints are escalated to Level 2 when they are about Australian Taxation Office (ATO) issues or there is a need to collaborate with the ATO about a complaint for the benefit of mutual customers. When the ATO issue cannot be resolved administratively the complaint is escalated by:

  • Level 1 staff, under escalation reason 5 - Complex, cross program or multiple issues

Liaison with the ATO is managed by the escalated complaints team. This ensures customers are given correct and consistent advice, administrative options are considered and customers are only required to liaise with one agency to have their issues resolved.

2

Explore administrative options + Read more ...

Complaint Officers must attempt to resolve an ATO complaint using administrative options, current complaint handling procedures and protocols and by seeking technical advice where appropriate.

3

How to refer a complaint for ATO liaison + Read more ...

Level 2 Officers request contact with the ATO by forwarding the DHS/ATO Complaint Support Template to ATO liaison complaints.

The Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between Services Australia and the ATO makes provision for urgent enquiries, including sensitive complaints from vulnerable customers. If a request is urgent the Level 2 Officer will forward the template and phone the Complaints Program Manager to request a more immediate response. The Complaints Program Manager will liaise directly with the ATO liaison officer if required.

4

Approval required before ATO contact + Read more ...

If unable to resolve the complaint administratively, the Level 2 Officer must obtain written approval from their Service Support Manager (SSM) and approval from the customer before requesting information from the ATO.

To seek approval from the customer for Services Australia to contact the ATO:

  • Advise the customer Services Australia must contact the ATO to try and resolve the complaint
  • Ask the customer for their permission for Services Australia to liaise with the ATO about the customer's complaint
  • If the customer does not give permission for Services Australia to liaise with the ATO, advise the customer they will need to contact the ATO themselves. The customer can visit the ATO website for more information

If the customer gives permission for Services Australia to liaise with the ATO:

  • advise the customer that liaison with the ATO does not guarantee their complaint will be resolved
  • advise the customer they do not need to contact the ATO and that the ATO will respond to the Services Australia request for information within 5 business days of receiving the request
  • advise the customer that Services Australia intends to resolve complaints in 10 working days and will keep them informed of any delays
  • document in the CFT that the customer has agreed for Services Australia to contact the ATO

Note: comply with Services Australia obligations under the Privacy Act when managing a complaint requiring collaboration and liaison with the ATO.

To seek written approval from the SSM to contact the ATO:

  • Complete the Services Australia/ATO Complaint Support Template. The template must:
    • only contain information about income and or impacts on the ATO. No other issues should be recorded on the template
    • confirm the customer has given their permission for Services Australia to liaise with the ATO about the customer's complaint
    • email the template to the relevant SSM for their approval
  • once approved, forward the completed template to ATO liaison complaints with a CC: to the Complaints Program Manager and the SSM
