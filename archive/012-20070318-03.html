Using the Ahpra Information window

Item

Description

1

Accessing the Ahpra Information window + Read more ...

Only staff who create and/or amend Medicare provider records in the Provider Directory System (PDS) have access to the Ahpra Information window.

To view the Ahpra Information window, access the relevant PDS record. Where the health professional has more than 1 profession, the Ahpra window will only display information received for the profession relevant to the record (stem).

To open the Ahpra information window from within a PDS record:

  • select the health professional’s Unique Identifier (orange hyperlink) at the top of the page

2

Reasons why there may not be an Ahpra Information window for a PDS record + Read more ...

The Ahpra Information window will not be available for a PDS record where:

  • the health profession on the PDS record is not regulated by Ahpra, or
  • the Ahpra record has not received a data feed since the functionality was introduced, or
  • the system was not able to automatically complete the matching/updating process for the Ahpra record and needs a manual fix to resolve. This work is currently done by National Office

3

Registration Details in the Ahpra Information window + Read more ...

  • Profession: will show the profession of the PDS record used to access the window
  • Profession Start Date: will show the date that Ahpra has recorded as the date of first registered of a health professional in this profession within Australia
  • Registration Number: will show the Ahpra registration number which identifies the health professional and their profession

An Ahpra registration record can have 1 or multiple registration lines across the 2 sections within the Registration Details fields.

The health professional can hold Base Registration or Specialist Registration(s) or both at the same time.

Base Registration fields

Each Ahpra registration line in the Base Registration field can be read from left to right on the screen as shown below:

  • Registration Type: will show the kind of registration held with Ahpra, for example: General
  • Registration Date: will show the initial start date of the Registration Type
  • Status: will show the current Ahpra Registration Status for the Registration Type
  • Effective From: will show the start date of the Status

Example:

The Registration Date and Effective From dates may be different due to the medical practitioner holding a different type of Status at different periods of time. For example, a health professional who gained General registration with Ahpra on 20 January 2019 and had their Status last updated as Current on 10 October 2020. This may occur where a health professional has renewed their annual registration during the late period but had no gap in their registration. This scenario would show as below:

  • Registration Type: General
  • Registration Date: 20/01/2019
  • Status: Registered - Current
  • Effective From: 10/10/2020

Multiple registration lines in the Ahpra Information window reflect changes to a health professional’s registration with Ahpra. These changes can be events such as changes to registration type, attainment of specialist registration or gaps in registration periods.

Specialist Registration fields

Each Ahpra registration line in the Specialist Registration fields can be read from left to right on the PDS screen as shown below:

  • Registration Type: will show the type of registration, for example, Specialist
  • Specialty Name: will show the specialty the health professional is registered in, for example, General Practice
  • Registration date: will show the start date for the registration
  • Status: will show the current Ahpra registration status for the registration type

Escalate any issues with interpreting Ahpra registration as per current workflow.

4

Differences between how registration will show in the window and PDS record + Read more ...

A health professional’s Ahpra registration is automatically converted for Medicare purposes when entered into PDS via the automatic data feed from Ahpra. The converted registration recorded in PDS is used for Medicare purposes.

Examples of the type of changes done by the system:

  • Converting an Ahpra registration type and status into a PDS registration status, for example:
    • An Ahpra registration type of General and registration status Registered - Current is recorded with a PDS registration status Current
    • An Ahpra registration type of Provisional and registration status Registered – Current with qualifications from Australia or New Zealand and no limitations, such as additional conditions on their supervised practice, is recorded with a PDS registration status of Intern
    • An Ahpra registration type of Limited and registration status Registered – Current is recorded with a PDS registration status Limited
    • An Ahpra registration type of Non-Practising and registration status Registered – Current is recorded with a PDS registration status Non-Practicing
  • Converting multiple Ahpra registration lines into a single registration line on the PDS record, for example:
    • An Ahpra record with 2 current Ahpra registration type(s) General and Specialist is recorded in PDS as a single registration line with a status of Current
    • An Ahpra record with 2 registration records; 1 with registration type General and registration status Registered – Current and another with registration type Provisional and registration status of Unregistered – Surrendered is recorded in PDS as a single registration line with a status of Current
      Note: this example generally occurs when a health professional has completed their period of supervised practice, ending their Provisional registration, and now has General registration with Ahpra

5

Information available in the window that is not on the Ahpra website + Read more ...

Some information available in the Ahpra Information window is not available on the Ahpra website. This includes:

  • the health professional’s date of birth
  • more information about a health professional’s registration than what shows on the Ahpra website
  • information about health professionals no longer registered with Ahpra, including where Ahpra has notified Services Australia (the agency), the health professional is deceased

6

Supplementary information not displaying on Ahpra website but listed in window + Read more ...

If a supplementary information record, such as conditions, is deleted by Ahpra on their website but is not updated with an end date in their system, it will continue to show up in the Ahpra Information window. Supplementary information will need to be checked on the Ahpra website to ensure it still applies.

Check Ahpra to Search the Register of practitioners:

  • Under Look up a health practitioner
  • Enter details in the below fields:
    • select Health Profession
    • Given name
    • key first name and surname or registration number in Name or Registration Number
  • Select Search
  • Take a print of the Ahpra screen and upload to PaNDA

Where there is a discrepancy in the details in the AHPRA Information window and Ahpra website, contact a Programme Support Officer (PSO).

7

Suppressed Ahpra records + Read more ...

Where a suppressed record is received, the Ahpra Information window will show a message to indicate that the record contains suppressed information. The health professional’s information will not show in the window for most staff.

Where a record is suppressed the health professional’s PDS registration status is still updated by the system. Information in suppressed records must not be disclosed externally or used to add missing information to PDS. There is no suppression flag currently available in PDS.

Where information is needed from a suppressed record, escalate this with a Programme Support Officer (PSO).
