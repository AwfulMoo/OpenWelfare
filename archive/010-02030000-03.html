Eligibility criteria for ABSTUDY

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Check the customer's circumstances + Read more ...

Where an applicant is claiming on behalf of a primary or secondary school student, the student is considered to be the customer for the purposes of this procedure.

Is the customer either:

  • a student who is:
    • enrolled in an approved course, or
    • approved to undertake a Testing and Assessment activity, for either an approved course of study, or the Indigenous Youth Mobility Programme (IYMP) or
  • an Australian Apprentice who has a current Commonwealth registration number in relation to a full-time Australian Apprenticeship?
    • Note: the Australian Apprentice does not need to provide proof of the Commonwealth registration number. Services Australia will obtain the Australian Apprenticeship details from the Department of Education, Skills and Employment
  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ABSTUDY. Check customer's entitlement to other payments. Procedure ends here

2

Does the customer meet the ABSTUDY definition of Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander? + Read more ...

See Aboriginality or Torres Strait Islander status for ABSTUDY purposes

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ABSTUDY. Check entitlement to other payments
    • Procedure ends here

3

Is the customer an Australian citizen? + Read more ...

For details of acceptable evidence, or more information, see Residence requirements for ABSTUDY.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ABSTUDY. Check customer's entitlement to other payments
    • Procedure ends here

4

Is the customer receiving other Government assistance to study or undertake the Australian Apprenticeship + Read more ...

See Not qualified for ABSTUDY if receiving other Government assistance to study or as an Australian Apprentice (apprentice or trainee) for information about what is considered other Government assistance.

Note: the training wage paid to an Australian Apprentice is not considered other Government assistance.

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for ABSTUDY. Check customer's entitlement to other payments
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, the customer may be eligible for ABSTUDY. See Step 1 in Table 2

Identifying the appropriate ABSTUDY Award for the student's circumstances

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Lawful Custody Award and Testing and Assessment Award + Read more ...

  • If the customer is in lawful custody for more than 2 weeks, they may be eligible to receive Lawful Custody Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Lawful Custody Award
  • If the customer is required to undertake a testing and assessment activity to determine suitability for placement in the Indigenous Youth Mobility Programme (IYMP) or admission to certain courses, they may be entitled to Testing and Assessment Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Testing and Assessment Award

2

Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

Is the customer a full-time Australian Apprentice with current registration?

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to Tertiary Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Tertiary Award
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Part time Study + Read more ...

Is the customer studying part time?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Part-time study (continued) + Read more ...

Is the customer studying a post-secondary course or are they studying a secondary course AND they were aged 18 years or older at 1 January in the year of study?

  • Yes, the applicant may be entitled to claim the Part-time Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Part-time Award
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ABSTUDY. Check customer's entitlement to other payments
    • Procedure ends here

5

Level of course of study + Read more ...

If the customer is studying a:

  • Primary course, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • Secondary course, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • Post-secondary or Tertiary course, see Step 1 in Table 5

ABSTUDY Awards for students in primary education

Table 3:

Step

Action

1

Customer 16 years or older + Read more ...

Is the customer 16 year of age or older?

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to Schooling B Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Schooling B Award
    • Note: Family Tax Benefit (FTB) may be claimed by the primary carer of students aged 16-19 where the customer is dependent and living at home while attending full-time secondary study. A check should be performed to identify if FTB may be a more beneficial payment for the claimant's family. See Identifying the most beneficial payment for trainees or Australian Apprentices
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Customer 14 years or older + Read more ...

Is the customer 14 years of age or older at 1 January in the year of study and living at home?

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to Schooling A Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Schooling A Award
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Repeating final year of primary school + Read more ...

Is the customer 15 years of age or under, and:

  • they had already completed the final year of primary school and moved away from home to undertake secondary schooling but instead needs to repeat the final year of primary school at another location that offers both primary and secondary schooling, and
  • they meet one of the conditions for approval to live away from home (for more information, see Away from home rate for ABSTUDY), and do live away from home?

If:

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to Schooling B Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Schooling B Award
  • No, go to Step 4

4

State care or independence + Read more ...

Is the customer?

  • 15 years of age or older, and
  • in State Care, or
  • independent as a result of one of the following circumstances:
    • they are an orphan
    • they have, or have had, a ABSTUDY dependent child
    • currently have the care or custody of another person's dependent child or student
    • they have been in lawful custody for a cumulative period of six months or more
    • they have undergone and completed a traditional initiation ceremony
    • it is unreasonable for them to live at home (UTLAH)
    • their parents cannot exercise their responsibilities because they are in prison, missing, or are mentally incapacitated or living in a nursing home

If:

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to claim the Schooling B Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Schooling B Award
  • No, the customer is not entitled to any ABSTUDY award. Check customer's entitlement to other payments
    • Procedure ends here

ABSTUDY Awards for students in secondary education

Table 4:

Step

Action

1

Non-school secondary studies + Read more ...

Is the customer (of any age)

  • undertaking non-school secondary studies
  • meeting the ABSTUDY progress and duration of assistance rules; and has either:
    • reached the minimum school leaving age as defined by the relevant State/Territory education authority
    • provided evidence that they have been granted an exemption by the State/Territory education authority in order to attend a TAFE or other non-school institution

If:

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to claim the Schooling B Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Schooling B Award
    • Note: Family Tax Benefit (FTB) may be claimed by the primary carer of students aged 16-19. A check should be performed to identify if FTB may be a more beneficial payment for the claimant's family. See Identifying the most beneficial payment for trainees or Australian Apprentices
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Customer 16 years or older + Read more ...

Is the customer 16 year of age or older?

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to Schooling B Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Schooling B Award
    • Note: Family Tax Benefit (FTB) may be claimed by the primary carer of students aged 16-19 where the customer is dependent and living at home while attending full-time secondary study. A check should be performed to identify if FTB may be a more beneficial payment for the claimant's family. See Identifying the most beneficial payment for trainees or Australian Apprentices
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Customer 15 years or younger + Read more ...

Is the customer 15 years of age or younger, and:

  • living at home, or
  • not approved for the living away from home or independent rates?

If:

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to Schooling A Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Schooling A Award
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Customer 15 years or younger (continued) + Read more ...

Is the customer 15 years of age or under, and:

  • they meet one of the conditions for approval to live away from home, and
  • they do live away from home?

If:

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to Schooling B Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Schooling B Award
  • No, go to Step 5

5

State care or independence + Read more ...

Is the customer?

  • 15 years of age or older, and
  • in State Care, or
  • independent as a result of one of the following circumstances:
    • they are an orphan
    • they have, or have had, a ABSTUDY dependent child
    • currently have the care or custody of another person's dependent child or student
    • they have been in lawful custody for a cumulative period of six months or more
    • they have undergone and completed a traditional initiation ceremony
    • it is unreasonable for them to live at home (UTLAH)
    • their parents cannot exercise their responsibilities because they are in prison, missing, or are mentally incapacitated or living in a nursing home

If:

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to Schooling B Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Schooling B Award
  • No, the customer is not entitled to any ABSTUDY award. Check customer's entitlement to other payments
    • Procedure ends here

ABSTUDY Awards for students in tertiary education

Table 5:

Step

Action

1

Masters and Doctorate Studies + Read more ...

Is the customer enrolled on a full-time or concessional study-load basis in an approved Masters degree or Doctorate (PhD) course?

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to Masters and Doctorate Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Masters and Doctorate Award
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Other Tertiary studies + Read more ...

Is the customer:

  • studying on a full-time or concessional study load basis
  • meeting the ABSTUDY progress and duration of assistance rules and has either:
    • reached the minimum school leaving age as defined by the relevant State/Territory education authority
    • provided evidence that they have been granted an exemption by the State/Territory education authority in order to attend a TAFE or other tertiary institution?

If:

  • Yes, the customer may be entitled to Tertiary Award. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Tertiary Award
  • No, the customer is not entitled to any ABSTUDY Award. Check customer's entitlement to other payments
