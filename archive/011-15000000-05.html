A to L - terminology used in Medicare Compensation Recovery

Table 3: This table contains a list of common terminology (A to L) used in Medicare Compensation Recovery. It includes links to relevant Operational Blueprint documents.

Term

Description

advance payment (AP)

A payment which represents a portion of a judgment or settlement amount, where there is no valid notice of past benefits at date of judgment or settlement.

Is received by Services Australia, and both the agency and the injured person (or claimant) have received written notifications that the insurer intends to make that advance payment.

See also: Payments, refunds and debts for Medicare Compensation Recovery.

Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS)

ACMPS is a SAP HANATM database accessed via ESSentials used to confirm if any home care subsidies have been paid on behalf of a specific person. See care costs.

all services taken

See Notice of charge - (Individual).

authorised third party

Either an organisation (such as a solicitor) or an individual (such as a friend or relative) who is authorised to act on behalf of the injured person or claimant on a Third Party Authority form (MO021).

Also may be known as the:

  • authorised person
  • claimant’s authorised solicitor

See also: Authority to release information and acting on a person’s behalf for Medicare Compensation Recovery

Note: an insurer's solicitor or compensation administrator is considered a delegate and/or agent and may act on behalf of the notifiable person/compensation payer.

care costs

Nursing home benefits, residential care subsidies and home care subsidies recoverable under Division 2 of the Health and Other Services (Compensation) Act 1995. Previously referred to as a 'nursing home check'.

See also: Care costs for Medicare Compensation Recovery.

case notes

A public record of information created, sent and received in the course of carrying out business on behalf of the agency.

Case notes can be requested under Freedom of Information Act 1982 provisions.

Where possible, staff should use the case notes macro on Sharepoint or the case notes template to ensure all important information about a process has been included.

claimant

The person seeking compensation. The claimant is either the injured person or someone else such as a parent/guardian or a legal representative. Referred to in the Health and Other Services (Compensation) Act 1995, use 'injured person' instead wherever possible to be specific.

Also may be known as the:

  • authorised representative
  • parent/guardian of an under 14 year old injured person (minor)
  • power of attorney
  • public trustee
  • executor
  • administrator

Client and Service Provider eAnalysis Reporting (CASPER)

CASPER is the Department of Health's data warehouse and reports on data from both the System for the Payment of Aged Residential Care (SPARC) and the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS). See care costs.

compensation payer

The person who is liable to make a payment of compensation. Can be a notifiable person or insurer.

Compensation Case Management System (CCMS)

The StaffwareTM based database system used by the agency to manage Medicare compensation recovery cases, including to register a case, enter details of relevant parties and enter case notes of actions.

completed Medicare history statement

This forms part of the statement by claimant together with a completed declaration regarding care costs.

See also: Statement by claimant for Medicare Compensation Recovery.

Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS)

A database used by the agency to manage Medicare enrolment data, including demographic information such as names, date of birth, gender and addresses.

See also: Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) for Medicare.

declaration

A written statement signed by the injured person or the claimant that something is true.

See also: Declarations for Medicare Compensation Recovery.

history statement

Also known as a Medicare history statement or a 'Request for a notice to claimant'. Previously known as a 'Claims History Statement'.

See also: Medicare history statement request for Medicare Compensation Recovery.

injured person

The individual who has suffered an injury or illness. Also known as the 'compensable person'.

The injured person is the claimant unless the claim is being made on their behalf.

injury

Injury, illness or disease relating to the compensation claim.

insurer

Referred to in the Health and Other Services (Compensation) Act 1995 as the 'compensation payer' or the notifiable person. Note: this may not actually be an 'insurer' in the strict sense in all instances.

legal representative

The person who has been appointed by law to act on the injured person’s behalf (such as an executor, court order or Power of Attorney). Not to be confused with claimant’s solicitor.

M to Z - terminology used in Medicare Compensation Recovery

Table 5: This table contains a list of common terminology (M to Z) used in Medicare Compensation Recovery. It includes links to relevant Operational Blueprint documents.

Term

Description

Medicare Compensation Recovery

A Commonwealth program administered by Services Australia, to recover Medicare benefits, nursing home benefits, residential care subsidies and home care subsidies paid on behalf of a person in relation to an injury/illness where more than $5,000 has been awarded in compensation or where a reimbursement arrangement has been entered into more than 6 months after a compensation claim has been lodged.

This is to prevent double-dipping in compensation cases. That is, so the Commonwealth is reimbursed for subsidies and benefits paid for medical services which were provided to a person (bulk-billed or subsidised) by the government who has been compensated for those services.

Medicare history statement

An itemised list of the services received by an individual that were paid for in whole or part by the Commonwealth.

Also may be known as history statement or a 'Request for a notice to claimant'. Previously known as a 'Claims History Statement'.

See also: Medicare history statement request for Medicare Compensation Recovery.

Notice of past benefits (NoPB)

An interim statement which lists the Medicare benefits, nursing home benefits, residential care subsidies and home care subsidies which the agency believes were paid in respect of treatment for, or as a result of, a compensable injury. A notice of past benefits is not payable until a case settles.

A notice of past benefits takes into account any payments already received for that case. At settlement, a valid notice of past benefits becomes the notice of charge for that case.

See also: Notice of charge – (Individual).

Notice of past benefits - all services taken

All services included in a Medicare history statement are taken as related to an injury or illness because the time to reply has expired. The Statement by claimant was not returned in time or at all. Note: this was previously known as 'deemed'.

Notice to claimant

Referred to in section 17 of the Health and Other Services (Compensation) Act 1995.

Sent to the injured person (or the claimant) to advise of their requirement to repay Medicare benefits, nursing home benefits, residential care subsidies or home care subsidies related to their compensation claim. It contains a Medicare history statement to identify services related to the injury/illness and a declaration for the injured person (or claimant) or authorised third party to complete regarding related care costs.

See also: Medicare history statement request for Medicare Compensation Recovery.

notifiable person

Referred to in the Health and Other Services (Compensation) Act 1995 and is usually an insurance company. Also known as the 'compensation payer' after judgment or settlement.

Section 23A statement

A declaration by an injured person or claimant that:

  • no eligible benefits have been received in relation to the injury of illness, or
  • no further eligible benefits have been received in relation to the injury of illness since the expired notice of past benefits

Solicitor - injured person's

Nominated by the injured person or claimant to send us correspondence relating to their compensation case.

Solicitor - insurer's

Nominated by the insurer to represent them for the case. A third party authority form is not required.

Statement by claimant

Referred to in section 18 of the Health and Other Services (Compensation) Act 1995. This is the returned Medicare history statement and declaration signed by the injured person, claimant or authorised third party. It verifies the Medicare services they believe relate to their case, and whether they have received nursing home benefits, residential care subsidies or home care subsidies as a result of their injury or illness.

See also: Statement by claimant for Medicare Compensation Recovery.

System for the Payment of Aged Residential Care (SPARC)

SPARC is a Medicare mainframe database used to confirm if any nursing home benefits or residential care subsidies have been paid on behalf of a specific person.

See care costs.

third party

Anyone with an interest in the case, other than the injured person (or the claimant) and Services Australia. May also be known as 'claimant's solicitor', 'compensation payer', notifiable person or insurer.

voluntary recovery

Any payment received where there was no intention advised of making an advance payment. May also be known as a voluntary payment.

See also: Payments, refunds and debts for Medicare Compensation Recovery.

work identification number (WIN)

An individual case identifier allocated by the Compensation Case Management System (CCMS) at time of registration.
