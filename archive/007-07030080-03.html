For appropriately trained Families and Child Care Smart Centre staff only.

Customer lodges proof of a child’s birth

Step

Action

1

Check date of receipt + Read more ...

Date of Receipt

Code and process claims using the correct date of receipt. This date can differ from the 'capture date' for confirming the date of a claim which has been scanned. Staff can confirm the correct date in Document Tools in Customer First. This screen displays both the 'date of receipt' and the 'capture date'. The Resources page contains a date of receipt matrix.

2

Check claim status or intention to claim + Read more ...

Check the claim status or intention to claim for both the customer and their partner.

If the customer (or their partner):

  • wants to complete Medicare enrolment only (not claiming payments), go to Step 3
  • lodges FA081 proof of birth (POB) but there is no claim for the child, go to Step 4
  • has had their FTB/PPL claim for the child assessed pending proof of birth, go to Step 6
  • has had their DAP claim for the child assessed pending proof of birth, go to Step 7
  • lodges a pre-birth online or paper claim for FTB/PPL/DAP that has been finalised as 'NEF', go to Step 8
  • lodges a pre-birth claim online or paper claim for FTB/PPL/DAP that is not yet assessed, go to Step 9
  • lodges a Child Care Subsidy (CCS) claim only, go to Step 10
  • lodges a post-birth claim online or a paper claim for FTB/PPL/DAP that is not yet assessed, see Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave payments. Procedure ends here

3

Medicare enrolment only + Read more ...

Customers can enrol a newborn for Medicare online.

Customers can lodge a FA081 to enrol the child in Medicare. This includes the Medicare Safety Net and My Health Record. The FA081 must be complete and signed.

The customer can also provide other acceptable proof of birth and lodge a Medicare Enrolment Application (MS004) form. The Resources page contains a link to this form.

If the completed FA081 indicates they only want to register the newborn for Medicare:

  • Cancel the families scan activity. Ensure the MAENROL DOC remains open. If the original MAENROL DOC closes:
    • create this manually for processing by Medicare
    • ensure the Date of Receipt (DOR) matches the original MAENROL DOC. See Fast Note. Select Auto Text. Use Families > Update > Medicare Enrolment
  • Check if the customer's partner already has a claim for payments. If they have, use the proof of birth to assess the claim. Note: customers claiming PPL/DAP must advise they have applied to register the birth with their state, territory or agency

Despite what the customer states on the form, Centrelink and Medicare can use this proof of birth.

Procedure ends here.

4

No claim for child on record + Read more ...

Check the FA081 to see if the customer wants to claim payments for their newborn child. Check their response to the PPL question and the Medicare-only question.

If the customer lodges a FA081 and is:

  • not FTB current (FTB/CUR or FTB/CZR) and does not have a claim, go to Step 5
  • FTB current (FTB/CUR or FTB/CZR), and
    • wants to claim PPL, go to Step 5
    • does not want to claim PPL (or rejected PPL) and has a complete FA081, reassess FTB. Procedure ends here
    • does not have a pre-birth claim and the FA081 is incomplete or needs more information go to Step 5
    • does not have a pre-birth claim or acceptable POB (FA081 back page), go to Step 5

If the customer is not FTB current (FTB/CUR or FTBCZR) and lodges other acceptable POB, contact to discuss claim options.

Create a manual Q777 letter via the Letter Details (LD) screen. The Resources page contains the instructions and suggested letter text.

Cancel the scanned image. Record details on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

5

Customer claiming payments + Read more ...

Invite the customer to claim payments for the child.

Staff can send a desktop message to tell the customer they will get a phone call. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging.

Call the customer to discuss claim options.

If contact with the customer is successful:

  • Encourage the customer to lodge an online claim. This is the preferred service offer. Ensure customers have 'active' online account access before asking them to claim online
  • If unreasonable for the customer to claim online, issue a Claim for Paid Parental Leave and Family Tax Benefit (FA100)
  • Extra help is available for remote Indigenous customers making a claim
  • Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) is available for families in crisis

If the customer is not available at the time of contact, check the address details and update if needed. If the customer lodges:

  • an FA081: issue a Q776 letter using the FA081 Customer to claim Q776 guided procedure
  • other acceptable POB: issue a Q999 letter. See Resources page for letter text

For more information, see Information requests for FTB, PPL, DAP and CCB lump sum claims.

Complete the families scan activity. Ensure the MAENROL DOC remains open. If the original MAENROL DOC closes:

  • Create this manually for processing by Medicare
  • Ensure that the Date of Receipt (DOR) matches that of the original MAENROL DOC. See Fast Note. Select Auto Text. Use Families > Update > Medicare Enrolment

Check if the customer's partner has a claim for payments. If they have, use the proof of birth for their claim. Note: customers claiming PPL/DAP must advise they have applied to register the birth with their state, territory or agency.

Discuss potential entitlement to another Centrelink payment (e.g. Parenting Payment (PP)) with the customer or send a Q999. See Resources page for letter text.

Record details on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

6

Claim assessed pending proof of birth + Read more ...

FTB/PPL

For customers whose pre-birth claim is pending proof of birth and who:

  • Use the 'Add Newborn' service online or the Express Plus Centrelink App:
    • access these claims through the Search Service Request menu in Customer First, process the Add newborn child activity and do pre-process checks
    • Use the most appropriate Fast Note to record the PPL claim outcome - auto text Families > Claims, and select the relevant claim option
      Note: the ‘Add Newborn Service’ is not a claim. Do not deem any duplicate workflows for the same child as not effective (NEF). Cancel any multiple activities and complete all updates within the first initial ‘Add Newborn Service’
    • if the customer provides the back page of FA081 with Doctor/Midwife section complete, but the customer section at the top of the back page is incomplete, the back page still serves as acceptable proof of birth providing the full serial number is also included
  • Lodge a fully completed paper FA081, use the Add Newborn service through Workspace>Claims Online>Add Newborn and process the activity. Use the Fast Note to record the claim outcome - auto text Families > Claims and select the relevant claim outcome option.
  • Lodge FA081 mostly complete, including the child's name contact the customer by either:
    • getting the missing information by phone (where POB met). Use the Add Newborn service through Workspace>Claims Online>Add Newborn to process the activity. Use Fast Note to record the PPL claim outcome - auto text Families > Claims and select the relevant claim outcome option, or
    • if they have the original FA081, ask the customer to complete the missing questions and upload the complete FA081
  • Lodge incomplete FA081 without the back page and without child's name/details. Contact the customer and advise them:
    • to access the 'Add Newborn' services online or the Express Plus Centrelink App, answer all questions displayed and upload the back page, or
    • if they have the original FA081, complete it in full and upload all pages of the completed FA081 including the back page
  • Lodge/upload the FA081 back page only or other acceptable proof of birth. Contact the customer and advise them:
    • to access the ‘Add Newborn’ services online or the Express Plus Centrelink App and answer all questions displayed, or
    • if they have the original FA081, complete it in full and upload all pages of the completed FA081

If the customer is not available, issue a Q777 about completing their claim online. See approved letter text for Q777 on the Resources page.

For PPL claims

Has the customer provided the requested information within the allowable timeframe?

  • Yes, for PPL claims when processing the Add Newborn service. The system will check the Nominated Start Date (NSD) if it is between:
    • the Expected Date of Birth (EDOB), and
    • Date of Birth (DOB) inclusive, the system updates the NSD to the DOB. This is the earliest possible start date for that child. Do not update in a separate activity if the NSD in the pre-birth claim falls between the EDOB and DOB
  • No, if the Add Newborn workflow can be completed see Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL). If the Add Newborn workflow cannot be completed, the activity must be cancelled and a DOC placed on the customers record with the provided information. The scanned document needs to be updated to Completed. This will allow for the NEF-POB to complete automatically. See Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images.

For FTB claims

If the customer has not provided the requested information with the allowable timeframe, the claim will be automatically NEF.

Customers may need help with:

  • providing proof of a child’s birth
  • using online services

Procedure ends here.

7

DAP + Read more ...

Customers do not need to give any extra information when:

  • their partner is claiming FTB/PPL for the newborn child, and
  • a DAP pre-birth claim is 'pending proof of birth'. The system completes the claim when the agency assesses their partner's PPL/FTB claim/s. Check the Assessment Results (AR) screen to confirm the DAP payment has finalised with the PPL/FTB claim processing. If this payment does not appear see Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims

The Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen for a DAP only customer should not be updated. The incorrect coding of the CHC screen can affect Child Support payments and FTB.

  • for customers whose partner is not claiming FTB/PPL for the newborn child, and
  • a DAP pre-birth claim is 'pending proof of birth' and lodges:
  • a fully completed paper FA081 or birth certificate, see Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims
  • an incomplete FA081, contact the customer to get the missing information. If unable to contact the customer, print the incomplete scanned FA081. Send this with a Q777 (via the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure) to complete the missing questions
  • the back page of the FA081 only, contact the customer. Request they provide the complete FA081 or alternative Proof of birth. If unable to contact customer or they are unable to provide these documents, send a Q777. Send this via the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure asking the relevant questions to complete the customer DAP claim. The Resources page contains examples of Q777 text

Procedure ends here.

8

Pre-birth claim finalised as 'NEF' + Read more ...

If the claim is for:

  • PPL only, status - NEF/PCB:
    • using the pre-birth claim and the FA081 form, the PPL claim can be re-indexed using Assisted Customer Claim (ACC). This will be from the date the agency receives the new information. For more information, see Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
  • FTB only or combined PPL/FTB, status - NEF/PCB
    • the FTB/PPL claim can be re-indexed from the date the agency receives the new information. See Not effective claims or withdrawal of claim for Family Tax Benefit or Stillborn Baby Payment
  • DAP, status - NEF/POB
    • the DAP claim can be re-indexed from the date the agency receives the new information. See Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)

Procedure ends here.

9

Pre-birth claim not yet processed + Read more ...

PPL/FTB

Paper pre-birth claim (excluding PPL or DAP standalone claims):

  • Cancel the pre-birth activity on the Activity List (AL) screen
  • Assess post birth claim using the pre-birth paper claim (FA100) and the FA081/POB
  • Determine the correct Date of Receipt (DOR):
    • DOR = Child’s DOB, proof of birth received less than/equal to 28 days after child’s date of birth
    • DOR = Date proof of birth (POB) received, where more than 28 days after the child’s date of birth
  • Before running Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), complete pre-processing checks and update contact details

Online pre-birth claim or standalone PPL and DAP paper claims:

If the primary claimant is the biological father or partner of the birth mother and they have submitted a pre-birth claim, prior to processing, further checks must be undertaken to determine if the claimant meets the exceptional circumstances criteria, see Step 2 Table 1 Eligibility for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) as a primary claimant.

Attempts to contact the claimant should be made to determine if the claim has been incorrectly made (for example, customer meant to claim DAP instead of PPL).

Before calling, Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) may be used to advise the customer they will be receiving a phone call. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging. If the claim was incorrectly lodged, assist the claimant to withdraw the claim. See Withdrawal of claims.

For all other claims, process the pre-birth claim.

After finalising the pre-birth claim use the Add Newborn service to process the proof of birth. Go to via Workspace> Claims Online>Add Newborn

  • If processing the PPL and DAP pre-birth claim more than 56 days after the nominated start date, or
  • the FTB pre-birth claim more than 56 days after the child’s expected date of birth:
    • the system assesses the claim as NEF-PCB/NEF-POB. As the customer has proof of birth a NEF-PCB/NEF-POB outcome is not appropriate
    • ACC must assess both the pre-birth claim and the proof of birth together

If the customer has lodged other proof of birth contact them to collect all other post birth information. For PPL claims, see Assessing Parental Leave Pay (PPL) claims.

Use the Fast Note to record the claim outcome - auto text Families > Claims and select the relevant claim outcome option.

For PPL claims when processing the Add Newborn service, the system checks the nominated start date. It updates this to the date of birth if it is between the expected date and actual date of birth. This is the earliest possible start date for the child. Do not update if the customer lodges an incomplete FA081, contact them to collect the missing information. If unable to contact the customer print the incomplete FA081. Send this with a Q777 via the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure to complete the missing questions. See Resources page for suggested letter text.

The Resources page contains a date of receipt matrix for all other scenarios.

Record action details on a DOC.

Resubmit the activity, see timeframes.

Procedure ends here.

Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)

For pre-birth DAP claims not yet processed, see Assessing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims.

Once the pre-birth claim has been finalised and the customer:

  • Has a Partner claiming PPL/FTB, they do not need to provide more information for their claim. This this will automatically complete when the agency assesses their partner's PPL/FTB claim/s. Note: check the Assessment Results (AR) screen to confirm the DAP payment has finalised with the PPL/FTB claim processing. If this payment does not appear see Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims
  • Lodges other acceptable proof of birth, lodged a complete paper FA081 or birth certificate. See Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims
  • Lodges an incomplete FA081, contact the customer to get the missing information. If unable to contact the customer print the incomplete scanned FA081 lodged. Send this with a Q777 (via the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure) to complete the missing questions
  • Lodges the back page FA081 only, contact the customer. Ask them to provide the complete FA081 or proof of birth. If unable to contact the customer, or they are unable to provide these documents, send a Q777. Send this via the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure) asking the relevant questions to complete the customer DAP claim. The Resources page contains examples of Q777 text

Staff can send a desktop message to advise the customer they will get a phone call.

Process all paper claims for PPL/FTB/DAP using the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) workflow. This includes pre-birth, post-birth and lump sum claims.

10

CCS only claim + Read more ...

If the customer has submitted combined claims (CCS and FTB/PPL/DAP), the child adding and linking processes must be completed as part of the Families legacy claim process. See Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave payments. Procedure ends here

Customers whose child does not have an existing record and have submitted a standalone CCS claim with acceptable proof of birth, see Helping customers provide proof of a child's birth for family assistance, Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Paid Parental leave scheme claims. For customers providing the following as proof of the child's birth:

  • a completed paper FA081 or birth certificate
  • an incomplete FA081, or
  • the back page of the FA081 only

the child will need to be added in a separate activity. See Linking a child to a customer's record. Note: where the FA081 is used to verify the child's date of birth, the unique serial number must be coded.

Complete the families scan activity. If the customer has lodged a fully completed FA081, ensure the MAENROL DOC remains open.

If the original MAENROL DOC is closed:

  • create this manually for processing by Medicare
  • ensure that the Date of Receipt (DOR) matches the original MAENROL DOC. See Fast Note: Select Auto Text. Use Families > Update > Medicare Enrolment

Procedure ends here.
