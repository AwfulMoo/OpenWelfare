Scenario

Outcome

Reason

A travel agent had a turnover amount of $62,500 and a Total Turnover Value (TTV) of $505,000 in the 2019 calendar year.

Round 1 Payment was granted and payment based on TTV. The business received $14,000.

The business does not lodge a claim but sends an email to Austrade and requests consideration under exceptional circumstances.

The total of payments under Round 1 ($1500) and Round 2 ($7500) if based on turnover be $9000.

Not eligible

Not eligible

The business does not meet the requirement to have received a Round 1 payment that was not based in any part on TTV.

The business is not eligible under exceptional circumstances as the business’ total payment using their turnover amount under Round 1 and Round 2 ($9,000) does not equal more than the amount they received under Round 1 based on TTV ($14,000).

A tour arrangement services provider had a turnover amount of $1,935,000 and a Total Turnover Value (TTV) of $19,505,125 in the 2019 calendar year.

Round 1 Payment was granted and payment based on TTV. The business received $100,000.

The business does not lodge a claim but sends an email to Austrade and requests consideration under exceptional circumstances.

The total of payments under Round 1 ($49,500) and Round 2 ($62,500) if based on turnover be $111,500.

Eligible

Eligible

Although the business does not meet the eligibility requirement to have received a Round 1 payment that was not based in any part on TTV, a favourable determination can be made under exceptional circumstances as:

The business’ total payment using their turnover amount under Round 1 and Round 2 ($115,000) equals more than the amount they received under Round 1 based on TTV ($100,000).

A travel agent started a claim for Round 1 of the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program on 12 March 2021 but never completed all required steps in the application process and did not submit the claim to Services Australia.

They would have met all criteria in Round 1 if they had completed all steps in the online claim process.

Their claim lapsed in Business Hub on 11 May 2021.

They would like to claim for Round 2 of the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program.

Not eligible

The claiming period for Round 1 stopped midnight 13 March 2021.

All businesses that had started claims (not yet submitted) were given a further 90 days after the claim start date to complete all required steps to make it a valid claim.

This business has not met the requirement to complete the online application and therefore the claim for Round 1 is not valid.

Round 2 eligibility requires payment under Round 1. As this business has not lodged a claim under Round 1 and therefore has not received a payment under Round 1, they cannot make a claim under Round 2.

A tour arrangement service provider completed a claim for Round 1 of the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program, submitting their ATO certificate.

They were eligible for a payment of $4,000 based on their turnover of $152,623 in the 2019 calendar year.

They lodge a claim for Round 2 confirming the amount was based on turnover (not TTV) and that they are trading, solvent and have every intention of retaining staff and processing refunds to their customers.

Eligible

The business met the eligibility requirement for Round 1 and have also met all eligibility requirement for Round 2.

They are entitled to a payment of $15,000 in Round 2.

A tour arrangement service provider completed a claim for Round 1 of the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program, submitting their ATO certificate.

They were eligible for a payment of $11,500 based on their turnover of $489,586 in the 2019 calendar year.

They contact on 21 June 2021 to enquire about lodging a claim for Round 2 of the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program.

They stated their Round 1 figure was based on turnover (not TTV) and that they are still trading, solvent and have every intention of retaining staff and processing refunds to their customers

Not eligible

The business met the eligibility requirement for Round 1 and would also met all eligibility requirement for Round 2, however they have not lodged a claim by the required claim closure date of 11:59 (AEST) 12 June 2021.

No claim can be accepted from this business for Round 2.

A tour arrangement service provider completed a claim for Round 1 of the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program, submitting their ATO certificate.

They were eligible for a payment of $26,500 based on their turnover of $1,089,586 in the 2019 calendar year.

They are no longer operating, having let all staff go and being unable to/having no intention of meeting any obligations to process refunds and travel credits to Australian consumers.

They lodge a claim for Round 2 of the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program.

Not eligible

The business does not meet the requirement to:

  • be solvent and intend to continue to operate for the April to June 2021 quarter,
  • make best endeavours to retain its staff; and to meet the business’s obligations to process refunds and travel credits to Australian consumers

Business is not eligible for payment under Round 2.

A hotel derives 90% of their income from tourism. It had a turnover of $1,000,000 in the 2019 calendar year.

The impacts of COVID-19 resulted in cancellation of 90% of their bookings and required refunding to customers.

The hotel lodged a claim for Round 1 of the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program and were rejected as they did not meet the definition of travel agent or tour arrangement services provider.

They make a claim under Round 2.

Not eligible

The business did not meet the eligibility criteria required for Round 1 of the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program.

The business therefore does not meet the eligibility criteria under Round 2, which requires the business to have been eligible for a payment under Round 2.

A travel agent (sole trader) operates their own business and lodged a claim under Round 1 of the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program.

They met all requirements under Round 1 and received a payment of $1,500 based on turnover amount of $68,213.

They make a claim under Round 2.

They advised they are still solvent and will trade for the April to June 2021 quarter.

They have every intention of meeting any obligations to process refunds and travel credits to Australian consumers

Eligible

The business met the eligibility requirement for Round 1 and have also met all eligibility requirement for Round 2.

They are entitled to a payment of $7,500 in Round 2.
