Child support assessment

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Current assessment

Customers can view a summary of the assessment for each child support case:

  • case ID and collection indicator
  • name of the other party
  • child name/s
  • how much the payer has been assessed to pay
  • period of assessment
  • income amount
  • annual, monthly and daily rates

Apply for a new assessment

Customers can apply for a new child support assessment. They can read information about eligibility before starting. They submit the application for Child Support to assess.

Customers can advise the death of a Child Support customer or child. Child Support will confirm the details.

Item

Description

1

Children summary

Customers can view a summary of care details for all of their children.

2

Advise a change of care

Customers can view the current care arrangements they have for each of their children, showing the nights in care and the care percentage.

They can report a change to these arrangements for Child Support to confirm. They can select the frequency and nights, or select days from a calendar. The total nights in care and the care percentage will then display.

Customers can ask to continue a child support assessment for a school student turning 18. Child Support will send a letter to the other party advising them to contact if they disagree.

Related procedure

Table 5

Item

Description

1

How to pay

Parents can use a debit or credit card to make payments for their child support or other debts. They can make payments at any time via the secure Government EasyPay service.

Other ways to make a payment are advised on the How to pay page.

Related procedure

Disbursements of child support

2

Payment overview

Payers can view:

  • Next payment amount and the due date
  • Overdue balance, and
  • Overpaid child support

There are links to make a payment.

Payees can view:

  • Next expected payment - total for all cases and date earliest payment is due
  • Overdue amount - total unpaid child support for all cases
  • Non Agency Payments - total amount for all cases and payment types, with separate view of amount per case
  • Payment received - most recent payment, with separate view of payment details for each case
  • Overpayments and other charges - total amount they have been paid over the assessed amount plus other charges such as consolidated revenue. There are links to make a payment

Select icon at top right to view Payment History.

Related procedure

Disbursements of child support

3

Advise an extraordinary payment

Parents can advise they have made a payment directly to the other parent or received a payment directly from the other parent (non-agency payments).

If a payee notifies, this updates their record.

If a payer notifies, Service Officer action is required.

Related procedure

4

History of extraordinary payments

Parents can view a history of payments made directly to the other parent or received directly from the other parent (non-agency payments).

2

Pay now

Credit or debit card payments

Customers have the option to make payments using a debit or credit card for child support or other debts. They can also add card details through this function.

A card surcharge fee applies for credit card payments. The surcharge fee will display before customer completes the payment. The fee is not subject to GST and is equal to the fee that Child Support incurs from its banker.

Alternate payment methods

There are links to information on how to make child support payments through:

  • employer deductions
  • Post Billpay
  • EFT
  • secure Government EasyPay service
  • BPAY®
  • cheque or money order
  • Centrelink
  • Department of Veterans' Affairs
  • third party or direct payments
  • additional payments for customers living overseas

For Child Support collection cases or an outstanding debt, see Disbursements of child support.

Upload document and Upload history

Table 8

Item

Description

1

Upload document

Customers can upload documents to support changes to their Child Support assessment.

Customers can upload multiple documents for one document type, but need to attach them one at a time. The total size of all files for a document upload must be under 5 MB.

To start the upload, the customer selects Upload a document, followed by the appropriate document type. They then select 1 of the following options:

  • Take photo to capture a new image
  • Upload a file:
    • to choose an existing image from their device's photo album or gallery, or
    • select a document already saved in their online storage device

Accepted file formats are pdf, png, tiff and jpg.

2

Upload history

Upload history will display up to 3 previously uploaded documents. This will be for documents uploaded by the customer through their Child Support online account or the app.

The customer can select View more to see more previously uploaded documents. They can select a document from the list to open and display it in PDF view.

They cannot delete documents from their upload history.
