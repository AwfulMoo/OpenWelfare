The System for the Payment of Aged Residential Care (SPARC) is the legacy aged care payment system for residential care. SPARC is a view only system from 20 August 2022.

To view the legacy SPARC process, see the historical version.

Veterans' Supplement in Flexible Care, Home Care and Residential Aged Care

A veteran:

  • receiving a Commonwealth-subsidised aged care service,
  • whose mental health condition has been accepted by the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) as associated with their service, may attract the Veterans' Supplement

The Veterans’ Supplement is automatically applied for care recipients accessing these types of flexible care:

  • Multi-Purpose Services
  • National Aboriginal and the Torres Strait Islander Flexible Aged Care Program
  • Transition Care Programme

The Veterans’ Supplement provides additional funding to Home Care Package providers. This is for additional costs that may be required to deliver appropriate care to veterans with accepted, service related mental health conditions.

The supplement is available to residential aged care providers who care for veterans with service related mental health conditions. The supplement is designed to ensure a veteran’s mental health condition does not act as a barrier to accessing appropriate care. The supplement is paid directly to the Home Care Package provider on behalf of the care recipient.

Eligibility

The Veterans’ Supplement was introduced on 1 August 2013.

A veteran is eligible who:

  • has a mental health condition accepted by the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) as related to their service, and
  • is receiving Commonwealth subsidised residential care or an eligible Home Care package

Care recipients are not required to undergo any further assessment to establish eligibility for the supplement.

Payment for eligible veterans

Approved providers do not need to submit a claim for the Veterans’ Supplement. The Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) and Services Australia match information to determine eligibility.

A veteran or their legal representative needs to provide consent for information about their eligibility to be given to their aged care provider. Once this occurs, payment to their provider is automatic and backdated to either the date (whichever is the latest):

  • the supplement came into effect
  • of entry into care
  • the care recipient became eligible for the supplement

Timeframes for the Veterans' Supplement file

The Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) send the file to Services Australia Aged Care Programmes Branch Operations team via email each month as required.

Note: the file must be loaded prior to the start of the next claim month.

Leave enough time to ensure that any errors can be dealt with before the next month's run.
