This document outlines how to complete a Claim for ABSTUDY Schooling A (SY018).

On this Page:

When to use this claim form

Customers who already have a Centrelink record (including a CHIld record) can claim Schooling A by lodging a claim using the ABSTUDY Claiming Tool process instead of a paper claim. This process must only be used by the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Call and ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing.

Customers completing a claim through the ABSTUDY Claiming Tool can be offered a verbal declaration. Customers who do not accept a verbal declaration are required to return a signed CDF.

A customer’s acceptance to the verbal declaration is recorded in the Verbal Declaration DOC, and in the Progress of ABSTUDY claim DOC.

Note: on 28 November 2016, a verbal declaration replaced the requirement for the customer to return a signed Customer Declaration Form (CDF). Customers who completed a claim through the ABSTUDY Claiming Tool prior to 28 November 2016 are may still be required to return their signed CDF. See ABSTUDY Claiming Tool process for more information. The student's parent/guardian(s) is normally the applicant for Schooling A.

Customers who are unable to complete a claim through the ABSTUDY Claiming Tool will need to complete the ABSTUDY Schooling A claim (SY018) claim form.

A new claim must be lodged where there is a change of applicant, that is, if the original applicant no longer has care and custody of the student.

Eligibility for ABSTUDY Schooling A

ABSTUDY Schooling A is available for dependent students who are:

  • secondary students aged under 16 years old and living at home, or
  • primary students aged under 16 years old and living at home and are aged 14 years at 1 January of the year for which they are claiming.

Other assistance if not eligible for ABSTUDY Schooling A

Where the student is independent, boarding away from home, or aged 16 years and over, the Claim for ABSTUDY (SY019) should be used.

Where the student is a secondary school student from a remote community who needs to live away from home to attend school, or a student who needs to repeat their final year of primary at another school and has to live away from home, the Claim for ABSTUDY Remote Areas (SY092) should be used.

How to lodge a claim or supporting documents

ABSTUDY claims and supporting documents can be lodged by using any of the following options:

  • Online - registered customers can upload their claim and any supporting documents online. For information about how to lodge online see Submitting your documents online on the Resources page
  • Fax - claims and supporting documents may be accepted by fax. The date the fax is received is considered the date of receipt for processing purposes.
  • Post - claims and supporting documents may be posted using the appropriate reply paid envelope to the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing team.
  • In Person - claims and supporting documents may be lodged in person at the nearest service centre, or in remote areas, with a Services Australia field officer
