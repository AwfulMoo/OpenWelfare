Escalated complaint or feedback from a customer or a business about Centrepay

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Complaint or feedback referred to Level 2 - Escalated Complaints Team + Read more ...

Upon receipt of an escalated Centrepay complaint, the Escalated Complaints Team must send a FYA email from the Customer Feedback Tool to the Deduction and Confirmation Branch – Program Assurance team with clear instructions on the outcome the customer is seeking.

The Escalated Complaints Team will continue to manage customer contact until the complaint is finalised. The Deduction and Confirmation Branch will act as the complaint manager and conduit where the complaint needs to be referred to other areas to be resolved.

For Deduction and Confirmation Branch staff only

When an email is sent to the Deduction and Confirmation Branch – Program Assurance team from the Customer Feedback Tool, it will contain a link with the Feedback ID. This ID can be selected to navigate directly to the complaint or feedback within the Customer Feedback Tool.

The Deduction and Confirmation Branch - Program Assurance staff will review and investigate the complaint or feedback, adding notes as required.

If the complaint or feedback requires action or follow up by another business area, Deduction and Confirmation - Program Assurance staff will create referrals as appropriate. This could include referrals to the:

  • Third Party Program Team for an assurance review
  • Operational Planning Branch
  • Deduction and Confirmation – Centrepay, for policy related matters

2

Recording Notes in the Customer Feedback Tool + Read more ...

For the Deduction and Confirmation Branch only

The Deduction and Confirmation Branch – Program Assurance staff will record notes in the Customer Feedback Tool for complaints related to assurance.

The Deduction and Confirmation – Centrepay staff will record notes if it receives an escalated complaint referral.

To edit or update detail in the Customer Feedback Tool:

  • Select Edit, then go to the Notes tab
  • Enter appropriate information into the Notes field
  • Save all changes to the Feedback

Note: complaints can be accessed by external parties under Freedom Of Information (FOI) so ensure notes are factual, relevant and professional and do not contain sensitive or confidential details such as staff surnames, staff contact details or outcomes of assurance reviews on Centrepay businesses that are the subject of the complaint.

3

Finalising the complaint + Read more ...

The Deduction and Confirmation Branch will add notes to the Customer Feedback Tool and email the outcome to the Escalated Complaints Team.

The Escalated Complaints Team will take action as appropriate for Level 2 complaints to finalise the complaint with the customer and within the Customer Feedback Tool.

Complaint received from a Centrepay customer about a Centrepay approved business

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Centrepay customer complaint received + Read more ...

For the Deduction and Confirmation Branch - Program Assurance only.

A level 2 escalated Centrepay complaint is received via email indicating:

  • a business has taken customer funds without consent and the customer is requesting a refund
  • a business passed on the Centrepay transaction fee and the customer is requesting to be reimbursed
  • any other complaint about a Centrepay approved business

The Deduction and Confirmation Branch – Program Assurance will undertake a desktop review of the complaint within 1 business day.

If the desktop review indicates a referral to Third Party Program Team is required, a referral should be made to this team within 1 business day of receiving the complaint.

Referral to Third Party Program Team:

  • Create an open ACTDOC on the business record with the keyword TPPREF and include:
    • details of the complaint, and
    • the requested resolution process
  • Send an email to Third Party Program Team Technical Support Manager and Program Managers including:
    • details of the complaint
    • the requested resolution process, and
    • a request to allocate the work for immediate action

2

Contact the business + Read more ...

For the Third Party Program Team only

Third Party Programme Team must attempt to contact the business within 1 business day to discuss the issue.

If contact was or wasn’t made this should be followed up with a formal advice via email requesting either:

  • proof of consent, or for the business to return the funds to the customer within 3 business days
  • proof that the business is not passing on (or attempting to recover) the Centrepay fee, or reimburse the customer within 3 business days
  • other action as directed by Deduction and Confirmation Branch – Program Assurance

3

Business response + Read more ...

Has the business satisfactorily resolved the complaint by the due date?

  • Yes, Finalise the complaint as per Table 1. Go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Business has failed to respond + Read more ...

If a business fails to take the appropriate action by the due date, the standard response is partial suspension.

Note: partial suspension may not be appropriate in some cases and both customer and program risk must be assessed.

For the Third Party Program Team

Refer details to Deduction and Confirmation Branch - Program Assurance with a recommendation for partial suspension or alternative.

For the Deduction and Confirmation Branch - Program Assurance

Assess the referral and make a determination on next steps. If a partial suspension is to be applied, seek approval and implement as appropriate.

Has the business satisfactorily resolved the complaint and responded to the partial suspension by the due date?

  • Yes, Finalise the complaint as per Table 1. Remove the partial suspension where appropriate. Go to step 8.
  • No,
    • if the complaint is related to a business taking funds without consent and the customer is seeking to be reimbursed go to Step 5
    • for all other complaints go to Step 6

5

Business has failed to respond to partial suspension + Read more ...

For the Deduction and Confirmation Branch - Program Assurance

Where a complaint is due to a business taking funds without consent, seek approval from EL1 to immediately reimburse the customer for the overpayment amount and raise an invoice against the business to recover the funds.

If approved, send to National Business Gateway Recoveries Team to action this request as per Centrepay overpayment recovery. Go to Step 6.

6

Business has failed to respond to partial suspension + Read more ...

For the Deduction and Confirmation Branch - Program Assurance

Send a show cause letter to the business.

Has the business satisfactorily resolved the complaint and responded to the show cause letter by the due date?

  • Yes, Finalise the complaint as per Table 1 and remove the partial suspension where appropriate. Go to Step 8
  • No, assess whether the business should have its approval to use Centrepay withdrawn. Where appropriate, seek approval and proceed with withdrawal. See Withdrawing a business's approval to use a deduction and confirmation service.

If compliance action has now resolved the complaint, finalise the complaint as per Table 1. Procedure ends here.

If the complaint is not resolved by taking compliance action, go to Step 7.

7

Customer complaint is not resolved by taking compliance action with the business + Read more ...

For the Deduction and Confirmation Branch - Program Assurance

If compliance action has not satisfactorily resolved the customer complaint, a referral may be sent to Deduction and Confirmation – Centrepay.

For the Deduction and Confirmation Branch – Centrepay

Assess and make a determination on the complaint referral.

Procedure ends here.

8

Targeted Assurance Review + Read more ...

If the business was found to be non-compliant during investigation of the complaint, a targeted review must be undertaken to formally breach the business, address broader issues and identify any other areas of non-compliance.

Was the business non-compliant?

  • Yes, code a targeted Centrepay assurance review on the M3RV screen and immediately undertake a targeted assurance review. See Actioning a review for Third Party Organisations (TPOs) for further details
  • No, procedure ends here
