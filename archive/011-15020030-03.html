Request a manual care costs check

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Claimant or injured person has declared ‘yes’ to the care costs question on MO028 + Read more ...

A manual care cost check is required:

  • if the case has settled and the end date of the care costs calculation is after the date of judgment or settlement. The authorised staff member will complete a recalculation
  • if there are special circumstances for the case. For example, the case is about to settle and an urgent issue of the notice of past benefits has been requested
  • after settlement and the case is nearing the end of the 3 month period in which a notice of charge can be issued (nearing the s33D or s24(4) date)
  • if there have been previous care costs included for a different case for the same compensable person
  • to follow up a previous care cost request that was not responded to

Does the case meet one of the above reasons for a manual care cost request?

  • Yes, go to step 2
  • No, request a care cost check in CCMS. See Processing statement by claimant table.

2

Manual care costs check + Read more ...

Use the 'care costs check request' - email template to request a manual care costs check. The Resources page contains a link to the template.

3

Add a case note + Read more ...

Select the Add notes button from the list on the right hand side of the main CCMS screen.

  • The top box shows all notes previously recorded on this case
  • In the blank bottom box write 'Manual care costs check requested'
  • Select Add to save notes. A confirmation box will appear, select 'Yes' to add case note
  • Press [F4] to release the work item, and the case will suspend on CCMS

Determine if case is to be referred to supervisor + Read more ...

Refer case to supervisor?

  • Yes:
    • Select 'Yes' if the claimant’s name has changed in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) or if other information needs to be updated. Examples of 'other information' may be: if a minor has turned 14 years of age and the parent is no longer the claimant; to add or change a case flag; or if a valid notice of past benefits exists and an amendment is required so the 'dates' can be deleted

Class action suit + Read more ...

This field is only used to identify if an advance payment will be made on this case. Is this case a class action suit?

Claimant’s address + Read more ...

Verify claimant's address?

  • Yes, only select 'Yes' if the address details on the case are incorrect. For example, if Services Australia has been advised of a change of address (over the phone or in writing) or if other agency records have been updated such as CDMS or a returned form
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Care costs check result received - issue notice of past benefits + Read more ...

View the care costs check result in the documents folder in CCMS to confirm the amounts to be recovered for Medicare benefits and care costs (nursing home, residential care and home care).

Answer the following questions:

  • Process past nursing, residential or home care details - Select 'Yes', Press [Enter]
  • Enter the amount of past Nursing Home Residential or Home Care costs - Select 'Yes' and enter the amount to be recovered (if any). If the amount is NIL, enter 0.00
  • Produce Notice of Past Benefit - Select 'Yes', Press [Enter]
  • Issue request for Notice of Past Benefit - Select the black horizontal arrow. A result confirming 'Ok' will be returned, Press [Enter]
  • Suspend this work item - Select 'Yes' and Press [Enter]
  • Do not release the work item yet, go to Step 5

Provide a breakdown of the care costs total that was included in the notice of past benefits

Table 3:

Note: do not send copies of screens or reports from online systems as this is a breach of privacy.

Step

Action

1

Access and print the breakdown table + Read more ...

A breakdown table is a summary of the information held in the Commonwealth of Australia's computer systems relating to the period an injured person was in care in relation to the compensable injury.

If further details of care costs are required by a customer and a breakdown table has not already been scanned to the case, request it from the care costs mailbox.

When the table is visible in the documents folder in the Compensation Case Management System (CCMS):

  • print 2 copies of the document
  • if the table has been embedded in an email:
    • use the snipping tool to copy and paste the breakdown table only (not the whole email)
    • paste it into a blank document, and
    • print

Note: do not send the MS Excel spreadsheet file or copies of ACMPS, ACSP or CASPER screens or reports, as this is a breach of privacy as the data is owned by Services Australia and/or the Department of Health and Aged Care, not the individual.

2

Re-print a copy of notice of past benefits or notice of charge + Read more ...

  • Access the Medicare Letters menu in the Fuji Xerox Document Management System [formerly called Hermes Precisa Pty Ltd (HPA) Online Report]. See the Resources page for a link to this system
  • Enter Medicare Logon ID and Password to use this system
  • Search for the case using the CCMS work identification number (WIN). Print a copy of the notice of past benefits or notice of charge
  • Requests from insurer or insurer solicitor - print 'notice of past benefits or notice of charge'. Print 2 copies
  • Requests from injured person or claimant’s solicitor - print 'notice of past benefits or notice of charge- claimant copy'. Print 2 copies

3

Prepare a manual letter + Read more ...

The notice is addressed according to authorities held. For a solicitor request where a third party authority is not held, the notice is addressed to the injured person. See the Resources page for a link to manual letters.

  • Use the z2451 - Document forwarded to you on behalf of the claimant Letter for requests made by the claimant’s solicitor or the insurer’s solicitor
  • Follow the template prompts to open the letter:
    • select the sender
    • enter names, addresses and reference number/s
  • print 2 copies, one to be sent by regular post, the other copy to be scanned to the case)
  • attach the breakdown table printed at Step 1
  • attach the notice of past benefits or notice of charge printed at Step 2

Note: the breakdown is only sent to the person making the request. Copies are not sent to other parties. If the claimant’s solicitor requested the breakdown and the agency does not hold a Third party authority, send the information to the injured person.

4

Add a case note + Read more ...

  • Select the Add notes button from the list on the right hand side of the main CCMS screen
  • The top box shows all notes previously recorded on this case
  • In the blank bottom box write 'Care costs breakdown requested by [insert details of who made the request]. The following documents have been prepared for approval by a Team Leader: [list attachments for example, authority cover letter or claimant cover letter, copy of notice of past benefits or notice of charge (CP007 or CP008) and care costs breakdown document]'
  • Select Add to save notes. A confirmation box will appear, select 'Yes' to add case note
  • Press [F4] to release the work item, and the case will suspend on CCMS

Obtain Team Leader approval + Read more ...

Send the letter and attachments for team leader approval..

Update the case note in CCMS + Read more ...

  • care costs breakdown
  • copy of 'notice of past benefits or charge' or 'notice of past benefits or notice of charge - claimant copy'
  • where applicable, the manual letter 'authority cover letter'
  • the above have been approved by a Team Leader and have been sent

  • write ‘18’ enclosed in a circle at top of the first page of the second copy of the letter and attachments. Attach a copy of the original request (if made by post), and deliver to the scanning room

Refer a request for a review of care costs to Programme Management

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Escalate to Programme Management (PM) email template + Read more ...

  • Access the 'Escalation to Programme Management via TL5 or PSO' - email template from the Resources page
  • Complete and send to the Compensation Team Leaders mailbox for review and discussion with a Team Leader. The supporting documents must be attachedIf a Team Leader agrees with the escalation, the Team Leader will forward the email template and attachments to the Programme Management mailbox for action

2

Add a case note + Read more ...

Select the Add notes button from the list on the right hand side of the main CCMS screen.

  • The top box shows all notes previously recorded on this case
  • In the blank bottom box enter a description about:
    • details of the document/s received that dispute the care costs, and who has submitted the dispute and supporting evidence, and
    • the case will be escalated to PM for a decision
  • Select Add to save notes. A confirmation box will appear, select 'Yes' to add case note
  • Press [F4] to release the work item, and the case will suspend on CCMS

3

Scan copy to case on CCMS + Read more ...

Print a copy of the completed 'Escalation to PM' email template.

Print copies of any/all supporting documents for example, letter from the solicitor, letter from treating medical practitioner.

Write '11' enclosed in a circle in the top right hand corner of the first page and deliver it to the scanning room.

4

Decision by Programme Management and advice sent to Service Delivery + Read more ...

Programme Management may consult Department of Health and Aged Care for complex cases. Programme Management will review the evidence and make a decision to either:

  • reduce the care costs charge (in part or whole), or
  • keep the care costs charge unchanged

Programme Management will then send a reply email to the Compensation Team Leaders mailbox and the requesting officer to advise of the decision.

5

Service Officer receives the escalation outcome and takes further action + Read more ...

Upon receipt of the escalation outcome:

  • print the email to scan to the case (write 11 enclosed in a circle at the top of the document and take it to the scanning room for scanning)
  • where the decision is to not to amend the charge, communicate the decision by telephone to the person who submitted the dispute
  • where the person remains unsatisfied with this decision, advise of their option to submit an appeal to the Ombudsman or the Minister for Services Australia
  • where the decision is to amend the care costs amount, amend the notice of charge by entering case notes then continue with the process steps in Amended notices and reviews for Medicare Compensation Recovery

6

Add a further case note + Read more ...

Select the Add notes button from the list on the right hand side of the main CCMS screen.

  • The top box shows all notes previously recorded on this case
  • In the blank bottom box enter details of the PM decision and the communication with the person who made the request
  • Select Add to save notes. A confirmation box will appear, select 'Yes' to add case note
  • Press [F4] to release the work item, and the case will suspend on CCMS
