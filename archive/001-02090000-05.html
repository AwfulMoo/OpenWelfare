Best practice in SEE for Zone contacts, Team Leaders and Service Centre Managers

Role of Services Australia, Employment Services Providers and SEE providers

Item

Description

1

Services Australia initiated referrals to SEE + Read more ...

Services Australia has a responsibility to promote and encourage participation in SEE to eligible job seekers.

For jobseekers with a new claim:

  • refer eligible job seekers to SEE
  • book a Pre-Training Assessment appointment with the SEE provider, while the job seeker is present, and
  • advise the SEE provider of the job seeker's participation hours to inform an appropriate study schedule

For provider-managed jobseekers with a change of circumstances:

  • run the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI), and
  • consult with the jobseeker's provider before referring the job seeker to SEE

For full details for all job seekers, see Referral to Skills for Education and Employment (SEE).

2

Employment Services Provider initiated referrals to SEE + Read more ...

Employment Services Providers (for example, jobactive, Community Development Program (CDP), Disability Employment Services (DES), Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE), ParentsNext (PN) and Transition to Work (TtW)) can also refer job seekers to SEE.

The provider codes the referral in the Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE) system, which:

  • is sent via an online interface to Services Australia, and
  • creates a referral on the job seeker's record

3

Role of the SEE provider + Read more ...

The provider has a responsibility to:

  • conduct SEE Pre-Training Assessments
  • deliver SEE Program training
  • exit job seekers where attendance or engagement impacts on their ability to complete the SEE Program and refer the job seekers back to Services Australia or their provider
  • advise Services Australia promptly of the results of:
    • pre and post-training assessments
    • participant attendance issues, and
    • all referral outcomes
  • enter details into the DESE system, and
  • commence a job seeker's SEE Program within eight weeks of the date of the Pre-Training Assessment
