7

Applicant disagrees with GST turnover amount precedent set by Austrade + Read more ...

Information to support discussion with the applicant

By using data from the GST exclusive G1 figures in the BAS, the Government can use a pre-existing verifiable data set. This can be validated by the Government.

The Government acknowledges there are various ways that travel agents and tour arrangement service providers can report GST figures in the BAS at G1. This varies among businesses depending on their tax arrangements and business structure.

In some cases, this may create discrepancies in grants between businesses similar in size.

While applicants can exhaust all steps in the review and appeal process, Services Australia:

  • must apply the precedent set by Austrade, and
  • will not forward appeals of this nature to Austrade to consider exceptional circumstances

Tell the applicant

The Guidelines do not allow Services Australia to use a different turnover figure to that on the ATO Certificate. The Guidelines allow Services Australia to refer a decision to Austrade to consider under exceptional circumstances. However, Austrade has already made precedent setting decisions in relation to this. They determined that:

  • no exceptional circumstances exist just because a G1 reporting figure did not represent the total transaction value (TTV), total gross sales or total ticket sales
  • there is no other source of GST turnover amount outside the ATO certificate

If the applicant still does not agree, tell them they can contact the Commonwealth Ombudsman.

Go to Step 9.

Business Activity Statements (BAS) lodged and GST turnover + Read more ...

Disregard the applicant’s information in the User Provided column.

Note: use the ATO certificate to verify these details. Do not use any other document/s to meet this criteria.

In the ATO cert info column, 1 of 4 options may show next to 2019 business activity statements have all been lodged. Note: for annual BAS lodgers, this will show 2018-19 income year.

  • Option 1 - Yes, this means the business’ annual GST turnover was $75,000 or more
    • The system will use the GST turnover amount in the ATO certificate
    • Go to Step 12
  • Option 2 - No, not required to lodge. This means the business’ annual GST turnover was less than $75,000. Therefore the business was not required to be registered for GST and BAS did not need to be lodged
    • The system will use GST turnover amount self-declared by the applicant, if the amount declared was less than $75,000
      Note: if the applicant declared $75,000 or more, the business should have been registered for GST and lodged BAS. The business will not be eligible for this payment
    • Go to Step 12
  • Option 3 - No – did not lodge. This means the business’ annual GST turnover was $75,000 or more. Therefore, they were registered for GST and BAS, they needed to lodge but did not. The claim must be rejected:
    • Select the hand shake icon
    • Make sure the Claim Assessment Summary reflects that the claim is to be rejected as the business needed to lodge BAS
    • Go to Step 12.
  • Option 4 - No, member of a GST reporting group. This means the business is part of a consolidated group/GST reporting group where the individual business had an annual GST turnover equal to or more than $75,000. Therefore, they were registered for GST and BAS were needed but were not lodged
    • Continue assessing the claim until Step 21
