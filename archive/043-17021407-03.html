Eligibility of southbound goods for manufacturer and miner claimants – M Scheme

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Eligibility of new goods + Read more ...

Tasmanian based manufacturers and miners are eligible to claim raw materials and equipment for use in their manufacturing/mining process.

To register:

  • domestic goods, the goods must be manufactured in Australia, or goods that have undergone a manufacturing process on the mainland of Australia before being shipped to Tasmania
  • imported goods, the imported goods must be shipped to Tasmania from a port on the mainland of Australia
  • intrastate goods, the goods must be manufactured on the mainland of Tasmania, or goods that have undergone a manufacturing process on the mainland of Tasmania before being shipped to King Island or the Furneaux Group

Note: the business must be an approved manufacturer/miner before registering new goods.

2

Documentation to add goods/suppliers + Read more ...

Domestic goods

New domestic goods need the following documents:

  • Manufacturers' and Miners' application to register southbound goods form (TAS025), and
  • Manufacturer's Certificate form (TAS027)

If the goods are not supplied by the manufacturer then the following form must also be provided:

  • Supplier's Certificate form (TAS028)

Imported goods

New imported goods need the following documents:

  • Manufacturers' and Miners' application to register southbound goods form (TAS025)

If the claimant indicates on the TAS025, they will provide a Supplier’s Certificate, they must also fill in the:

  • Supplier’s Certificate form (TAS028)

Claimants need to provide other supporting documents with each claim for southbound imported goods.

Intrastate goods

New intrastate goods need the following documents

  • TFES - King Island and Furneaux Group intrastate component Manufacturers' and Miners' Application for approval of goods form (TAS026)
  • Manufacturer's Certificate form (TAS027)

If the goods are not supplied by the manufacturer, the following form is also needed:

  • TFES - King Island and Furneaux Group intrastate component - Supplier's Certificate form (TAS018)

Note: it is the claimant/claims agent’s responsibility to ensure all required forms are completed correctly before submitting.

3

Check the M Scheme listing of registered goods + Read more ...

When a claimant is registered as an eligible southbound Manufacturer or Miner, details of the business are entered on the M Scheme notes.

When southbound goods are registered the details of the goods, their manufacturers and suppliers, are entered on the claimant’s M Scheme.

Before starting an assessment on the eligibility of new southbound goods, or new manufacturers and suppliers, check the claimant’s M Scheme to see if the goods and/or manufacturer/supplier are listed.

In myHSI – TFES General

  • Select Search for Claimants
  • In Search on – Claimant Code
  • Enter the claimant code and select search
  • Select the claimant code and this will bring up the View Claimant Details screen
  • Select the Notes tab
  • In the Category field, select M Scheme from the dropdown menu
  • Select Search
  • Select View to read the content, this displays the goods that have been registered as eligible southbound goods and the manufacturers and suppliers for the goods

To add new:

  • manufacturers and/or suppliers for registered goods, go to Table 2
  • southbound goods, go to Table 3

Add manufacturer and/or supplier for manufacturer and miner claimants – M Scheme

Table 2

Step

Action

1

New manufacturers and/or suppliers for registered goods + Read more ...

Domestic goods

For domestic goods that are already registered and approved, the claimant may wish to add a new Australian manufacturer and/or supplier of the goods.

The new goods must have the same description as the current approved goods.

When adding a new manufacturer and/or supplier of goods that are currently listed as approved goods on the claimant’s M Scheme the claimant/claims agent does not need to provide the TAS025 form.

Adding a new:

  • manufacturer for registered goods:
    • the claimant/claims agent provides a TAS027 completed by the manufacturer of the goods
  • supplier for registered goods:
    • the claimant/claims agent provides a TAS028 completed by the supplier of the goods

Imported goods

Goods that are already registered and approved as domestic goods, may also be eligible as imported goods.

If the goods have already been registered by the claimant and approved as southbound domestic goods, the claimant/claims agent is not required to complete the TAS025 to register the goods as imported goods.

For both Domestic and Imported goods, check if the goods listed on the TAS027 and/or TAS028 are listed on the Claimant’s M Scheme, see Step 3.

Are the goods currently registered, but have a new manufacturer and/or supplier?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Check documentation for adding new manufacturers and/or suppliers of registered goods + Read more ...

Domestic goods

To add a new Australian manufacturer for goods that are already registered, the claimant/claims agent must provide a TAS028 form completed by the Australian manufacturer of the goods.

If the goods are not supplied to the claimant directly from the manufacturer, the claimant/claims agent must also provide a TAS027 as well as the TAS028.

When a TAS027 and/or TAS028 is received to add a new manufacturer and/or supplier for goods that are already registered:

  • Create the activity in the TFES Assessor Tool
    • Select the Work Type code – Add M scheme Manufacturer/Supplier
    • Allocate the activity
  • do not complete the TFES Manufacturing and Mining – Southbound component application for approval of southbound goods template

If the registered goods are coming from a:

  • new Australian manufacturer:
  • check the TAS027 has been completed correctly by the manufacturer of the goods
    • if the goods are not supplied directly from the Australian manufacturer, check the TAS028 has been completed correctly by the supplier of the goods
  • registered manufacturer via a new supplier:
  • check that the TAS028 has been completed correctly by the supplier of the goods

Imported goods

Goods registered as domestic goods may also be claimed as imported goods without undergoing a registration process.

Evidence of no Australian equivalent is provided with each claim. If the claimant cannot provide all shipping details in the supporting documents for their claims, they may provide a TAS028 completed by the supplier, if the imported goods are shipped in the same way each time.

Has the T TAS028 been completed correctly?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, contact the claimant/claims agent to request a new TAS028

Contact by phone, email or both:

  • make 2 genuine attempts to contact
  • if first contact is unsuccessful, make a second attempt within 2 business days
  • if possible, leave a message with your name, contact number and positional email address
  • tell the claimant they have 21 days to provide the information. If information is not received, the new manufacturer and/or supplier will not be added

In myHSI on the Claimant record, record the information needed on the:

  • Claimant Comments tab, and
  • Claim comments (if applicable)

In the TFES Assessor tool:

  • in comments, record the information needed
  • change the activity status to awaiting claimant response

3

Goods are not listed as eligible southbound goods on the Claimant’s M Scheme + Read more ...

If a TAS027 and/or TAS028 is received and the checks of the M Scheme done in step 3 show that the goods are not already registered:

  • complete and send the email template - Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme Application of southbound goods – action required, found in the TFES Support email box under stationery items
  • send the completed email template to the claimant/claims agent

In myHSI – TFES General:

  • upload a copy of the email to the Claimant Documents tab
  • enter details of what has been sent to the claimant in the Claimant Comments, including:
    • TAS027 and/or TAS028 received on <date of receipt>
    • A check of the M-Scheme confirmed goods are not registered
    • Claimant advised by email that they will need to re-lodge the TAS027 and/or TAS028 with a TAS025 so that the southbound goods can be assessed

In the TFES Assessor Tool:

  • enter details of the request in the comments section of the activity
  • complete the activity

Process ends here.

4

Update the claimant M Scheme notes with new manufacturer and/or supplier + Read more ...

If the TAS027 and/or TAS028 have been completed correctly, add the new manufacturer and/or supplier to the claimant record in myHSI

In myHSI – TFES General:

  • Select Search for Claimants
  • In Search on – Claimant Code
  • Enter the claimant code and select search
  • Select the claimant code and this will bring up the View Claimant Details screen
  • Select the Notes tab
  • In the Category field, select M Scheme from the dropdown menu
  • Select Search
  • Select View to locate the registered goods
  • Enter the details of the new manufacturer and/or supplier:
    • Beside the name registered goods
    • Under the existing manufacturers/suppliers that are listed
    • Mark as **pending QA**
    • Select Add

If a TAS028 for imported goods shows a freight cost from overseas to Tasmania without a breakdown of the Bass Strait leg, go to Step 5.

If there is any information about shipping details of the goods on the TAS027 and/or TAS028. E.g. conversions, transport task, FIS rates.

  • Beside the new manufacturer and/or supplier in the M Scheme notes, enter:
    • FIS rate (if applicable), including the date as shown on the TAS027 or TAS028
    • ***see claimant notes*** for any additional information relating to the goods

Enter any additional information about the goods in the Claimant Notes:

  • From the View Claimant Details screen
  • Select the Notes tab
  • Select Add Note
  • Enter the manufacturer or supplier name in the Subject field
  • Enter the additional information in the Note Text, including:
    • FIS rate and date of effect (date on the TAS027 or TAS028)
    • conversions for the goods (e.g. m3 per pallet)
    • Product description or codes
    • Other relevant information about the manufacturer and/or supplier
    • Mark as **pending QA**
    • Select Add

The QA Officer:

  • checks that the goods on the TAS027 and/or TAS028 are already registered and the details added to the M Scheme listing and Claimant Notes are correct
  • sends the QA feedback email with the outcome of the check

If QA outcome is

  • correct, go to Step 7
  • incorrect:
    • the status of the activity will be QA back to Assessor
    • follow the instructions from the QA Officer to correct errors
    • get SSO clarification if needed

5

TAS028 with overseas freight charge + Read more ...

It is preferred that the TAS028 shows the freight charges for the Bass Strait leg of the journey.

If the supplier has completed a TAS028 for southbound imported goods and has provided the freight charges from overseas to Tasmania, ask the claimant if the supplier is able to provide the freight charges from mainland Australia to Tasmania.

If the claimant advises that the supplier is unable to provide the freight charges from mainland Australia to Tasmania, ask the claimant for:

  • A copy of a supplier invoice for the goods, showing the claimant has incurred and paid the freight costs, and
  • A copy of evidence of transhipment, showing the goods are shipped via an Australian mainland port

Has the claimant provided all required evidence with the TAS028?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, contact the claimant/claims agent to request the required evidence

Contact by phone, email or both:

  • Make 2 genuine attempts to contact
  • If first contact is unsuccessful, make a second attempt within 2 business days
  • If possible, leave a message with your name, contact number and positional email address
  • Tell the claimant they have 21 days to provide the information. If information is not received, the new manufacturer and/or supplier will not be added

In myHSI on the Claimant record, record the information needed on the:

  • Claimant Comments tab, and
  • Claim comments (if applicable)

In the TFES Assessor tool:

  • In comments, record the information needed
  • Change the activity status to awaiting claimant response

6

Update the claimant M Scheme notes with freight details + Read more ...

If the TAS028 shows the freight costs from overseas to Tasmania, and the claimant provides:

  • a breakdown of mainland Australia to Tasmania freight cost from the supplier. In the claimant M Scheme notes, beside the approved imported goods listing:
    • enter the mainland Australia to Tasmania freight cost
    • enter the applicable route code using the mainland origin and Tasmanian destination
  • a copy of the supplier invoice for the goods and evidence of transhipment. In the claimants M Scheme notes, beside the approved imported goods listing:
    • enter the overseas to Tasmania freight cost
    • enter the route code using the origin of the Australian port and the destination of the goods as shown on the evidence of transhipment

7

Notify claimant + Read more ...

When the activity has been approved by the QA Officer:

  • Send the email stationery item – Manufacturer or Supplier Added to the claimant/claims agent, confirming:
    • the manufacturer and/or supplier has been added to their M Scheme
    • any conversions or FIS rates have been added to the record (if applicable)
  • Upload a copy of the email to the Claimant Document tab
  • Remove *Pending QA* from the M Scheme entry and Claimant Notes

Step

Action

Add new southbound goods - assess the TAS025 + Read more ...

2

Domestic goods application + Read more ...

Evidence of Australian origin is provided when registering the goods.

Check the claimant/claims agent has:

  • filled in their details correctly
  • listed the goods for registering
  • described how they use the goods in their manufacturing or mining process
  • selected the Domestic Goods boxes and included:
    • the name and address of the Australian manufacturer. This should match the details on TAS027
    • if applicable, the name and address of the Australian supplier (if different to the manufacturer). This should match the details on the TAS028
    • the Manufacturer’s and Miner’s declaration

Consider the eligibility of the goods:

  • Is it a raw material or equipment?
  • Is it part of the claimant’s manufacturing or production process?
  • Is it made/grown on the mainland of Australia
  • Is it an ineligible southbound good?
  • Is it imported, has it undergone a manufacturing process on the mainland of Australia?

Manufacturer’s Certificate (TAS027) – completed by the manufacturer of the goods

Check the Manufacturer has:

  • filled in their details correctly
  • stated where the goods originated/was manufactured
  • filled in details of the mainland Australian process undertaken before coming to Tasmania in the Additional information section if the goods are imported
  • filled in all details if the goods are purchased on a Free into Store (FIS) basis and shipping details and freight paid are not available with each claim
  • filled in the Manufacturer’s declaration

Supplier’s Certificate (TAS028) – filled in by the supplier of the goods if they are different to the manufacturer

Check the supplier has filled in :

  • their details correctly, these should match the details on the TAS027
  • the Purchase details section
  • the Domestic Goods table under the Details of goods. The name of manufacturer should match the TAS027
  • if shipping details and freight paid are not available with each claim, all relevant details about the goods should be entered including any Free into Store (FIS) costs
  • the supplier’s declaration

Is all of the information available to assess the eligibility of the goods?

  • Yes, and
    • the application is also for imported goods go to Step 3
    • there are no other goods on the application, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 6

If the claimant answered No to the question, Are shipping details shown on the invoice or other supporting documents?. and indicates they will provide a TAS028, check they have included the

Supplier’s Certificate (TAS028), with the application

If a Supplier’s Certificate is included check the supplier has filled in:

  • the Purchase details
  • the goods supplied in Details of goods – Imported Goods section
  • the Imported goods supplier’s declaration
  • all relevant details if shipping details and freight paid are not available with each claim

Is all of the information available to assess the eligibility of the goods?

Add goods/supplier for primary production claimants – P Scheme

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Eligibility of new goods + Read more ...

Businesses or individuals engaged in agriculture, forestry or fishing in Tasmania are eligible to claim material inputs, machinery and equipment for use in primary production. The claimant must be the end user of the goods shipped.

To register:

  • domestic goods, the goods must be manufactured in Australia, or goods that have undergone a manufacturing process on the mainland of Australia before being shipped to Tasmania
  • imported goods, the imported goods must be shipped to Tasmania from a port on the mainland of Australia
  • intrastate goods, the goods must be manufactured or produced on the mainland of Tasmania, and shipped to King Island or the Furneaux Group for use in a primary production industry

2

Check goods and/or supplier + Read more ...

Is the claim for southbound domestic goods?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, claim is for southbound imported goods:
    • Southbound imported goods for primary producers are assessed when the claim is lodged
    • Procedure ends here

3

P Scheme domestic goods claim + Read more ...

Check if the goods and/or supplier is listed. Go to myHSI>Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme (TFES) General:

  • on the left hand column select view notes
  • in the Category dropdown menu, select P Scheme
  • next to Search On add the item or supplier name, select Search

Is the goods and/or supplier already listed?

  • Yes,
    • the goods and supplier are listed, assess the claim, see Southbound primary producer claim for TFES (P Scheme)
    • the supplier is listed but not the goods, go to Step 4
  • No, the goods and supplier are not listed, go to Step 4

4

Confirm goods or supplier details + Read more ...

Contact the supplier/manufacturer by phone if a claim is received with goods that are not listed on the claimant's Notes in myHSI or on the P Scheme.

Confirm the following:

  • Where are the goods manufactured?
  • If imported, have any modifications been made on mainland Australia?
  • Is it price equalised?
  • Does the claimant pay freight?
  • What are the freight costs if they are included in the cost of the goods?

Are the P Scheme goods and supplier eligible?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, Reject claim lines for ineligible goods, see Southbound primary producer claim for TFES (P Scheme)

Is the supplier of the goods listed on myHSI P Scheme notes?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

Note: QA officer approval is not needed for new P Scheme goods.

Add goods to existing supplier list + Read more ...

  • upload documentation to the Claimant Documents tab

Goods for suppliers no longer used - if claimant advises in writing that goods for suppliers are no longer used:

  • take a screen shot of the M Scheme Notes and upload the screenshot to Claimant Documents tab in myHSI
  • name the uploaded file ‘deleted M Scheme items’
  • after uploading the screenshot, delete the goods no longer used from M Scheme Notes

Procedure ends here.
