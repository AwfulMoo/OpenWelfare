This document outlines information for staff about releasing information about a deceased person under Public Interest guidelines for the Centrelink program. Every request requires a response.

Calls received about a deceased person

When a person calls and enters the Customer Reference Number (CRN) for a customer that is deceased, a message will display in Services Australia Workspace. This alerts the Service Officer to the sensitivity of the call and to handle the call appropriately. For more information, see Telephone standards.

The caller may be:

  • Authorised to make enquiries about the deceased person’s information, such as the executor or administrator of the estate, with evidence and a request made in writing on the Executor/Administrator Request for information form (SS524)
  • Enquiring about what Bereavement Services are available to them, such as the surviving partner or carer. See About Bereavement services
  • Enquiring about the deceased person’s information but does not have authority, such as a family member or friend. Note: do not provide any information about the deceased person (including whether or not they were a customer), unless the caller has provided sufficient evidence of their authority

Requests for information

Information is frequently requested by executors/administrators after someone has died so the estate can be finalised. These requests should be in writing and preferably made using the Executor/Administrator Request for information form (SS524).

Executors/administrators may ask for information about the deceased person, such as:

  • bank account details
  • declared income and assets
  • if there is any uncontrolled pension or benefit entitlements
  • if Services Australia has a claim against the estate, or
  • if there are outstanding debts or money owing
  • a statement of pension or benefit for current/previous financial years

All requests must have proof that the person asking for the information is the nominated executor/administrator. Executors are usually identified in the will of the deceased person.

Note: it is generally accepted that a Public Trustee or a solicitor is administering an estate when they officially write to the agency and state this.

If a person dies without a will (intestate), the spouse, partner or next of kin should apply to the Probate Office of the Supreme Court for letters of administration. A personal representative (that is, an administrator) will then be appointed by the court to finalise the deceased person's affairs.

This does not apply to small estates (worth less than $10,000). Small estates can be referred to the State or Territory Public Trustees, which have additional powers in dealing with small estates. The Public Trustee usually provides verification of their role at the time of their request for information for:

  • small estates, and
  • court appointed administration of deceased estates

Information must only be disclosed to:

  • the executor as named in the will
  • the Public Trustee
  • a court
  • the administrator of the estate
  • state and territory government departments assessing burial/funeral assistance

Requests for returned payments

An executor of the estate or appropriate person can apply for any of the deceased’s payments that have been returned from the financial institution. See Replacing payment made to a deceased customer where payment was returned from financial institution.

For Centrelink, a Public Interest Release of Information Form must be completed when information is disclosed to a person or authority other than an executor or administrator for this estate.

Recording executor details

Executor details must be recorded on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen. The proof sighted must be recorded on a DOC before Services Australia can issue arrears, complete debt recovery action or issue payment summaries.

Sending a letter about a deceased person

Letters providing information about a deceased person can be prepared by any Service Officer. These must be approved and issued by a staff member with the appropriate delegation level before being issued. The delegation is with a staff member at or above APS4 level.

Proof of executor or administrator status

Proof of executor or administrator status is always required before any information is released or follow-up action is taken to contact or advise the estate on matters such as debts or payments owed. A copy of the deceased person's will should show if there is an executor and identify them.

Information about the executor or administrator (including disputed executor or administrator roles) should be recorded on a DOC on the deceased person's record.

Nominee arrangements and Power of Attorney (POA) cease at the time of death. These should not be relied upon as an authority to receive information about the deceased person. People who were nominee or POA do not automatically become the executor or administrator. A statutory declaration from a person who does not hold an appropriate authority cannot be used as authorisation for access to a deceased person's information. The person should be advised to seek advice from a lawyer, Probate Office or Public Trustee.

Life insurance and superannuation companies can request information to:

  • confirm that a deceased person who held a policy or was a member of a fund was not a customer of Services Australia, or
  • request contact details for a beneficiary

State and territory government departments that provide burial/funeral assistance may request information about assets held by the deceased person. This information will determine eligibility for funeral assistance.

It is extremely important that all requests are actioned urgently.

Note: do not provide a third party with any information (including whether or not the deceased person was a customer) unless they have provided sufficient evidence of their authority. See Disclosing information to third parties.

Executor/Administrator Request for information form (SS524)

The SS524 form is the preferred method to request Centrelink information from Services Australia about a deceased person. The form states what Centrelink information can be provided, to whom and what proof of executor or administrator status is required.

Note: when an executor or administrator makes a request by letter, the request should be accepted, processed and the executor or administrator encouraged in the future to use the SS524.

The Resources page includes a link to the SS524 form.
