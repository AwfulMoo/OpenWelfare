Customers not affected by multiple entitlement exclusions

Customers are able to be suspended on Disability Support Pension (DSP) due to specific 'return to work' reasons and at the same time claim and become current on another social security benefit, such as JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA) or Parenting Payment (PP), if their income is below the income test threshold for that payment. These customers are not affected by multiple entitlement exclusion provisions.

It does not include the non-income support payment types of Exceptional Circumstances Relief Payment (ECRP).

Education and employment examples of multiple entitlement exclusions

Table 1: This table describes the examples of specific education and employment related multiple entitlement exclusions that apply to each payment.

Item

Description

1

ABSTUDY

ABSTUDY is not payable if customer is receiving other Government assistance to study such as Austudy or Youth Allowance (YA).

Students who are receiving assistance under Australian Government income support, such as a social security pension or allowance, may meet the eligibility criteria for some ABSTUDY assistance, for example, Part-time Award. They may not, however, receive ABSTUDY Living Allowance and income support from another government source concurrently.

Note: ABSTUDY customers may hold two ABSTUDY Awards concurrently (apart from a Testing and Assessment Award), provided that only one of the Awards pays Living Allowance or the Pensioner Education Supplement (PES).

2

ABSTUDY PES

ABSTUDY PES is not payable if a customer is receiving other Government assistance to study.

Note: an Australian Apprentice (apprentice or trainee) is not entitled to ABSTUDY PES.

3

Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme

If an applicant or student for AIC receives other Australian Government assistance, it may affect the applicant's eligibility for certain AIC allowances or their rate of the AIC.

4

AIC Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)

Australian Government assistance other than AIC received by an applicant or student may affect AIC PES.

A customer must meet the qualification criteria for AIC PES.

5

Austudy

Austudy is not payable if the customer is receiving one of the following:

  • one of the payments under the prescribed educational scheme
  • one of the payments from Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)
  • a DVA Income Support Supplement (ISS)
  • payments provided under an Australian Government funded employment programme
  • social security benefit or pension
  • Ready Reserve Education Assistance Scheme

6

Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)

PES is not payable if a customer is receiving ABSTUDY PES.

Note: an Australian Apprentice (apprentice or trainee) is not entitled to PES.

Benefit related examples of multiple entitlement exclusions

Table 2: This table describes examples of specific benefit related multiple entitlement exclusions that apply to each payment.

Item

Description

1

JobSeeker Payment (JSP)

JSP is not payable to a customer who is already receiving:

  • social security pension or social security benefit
  • one of the payments from the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)
  • one of the payments under the prescribed educational scheme
  • benefits under an Australian Government funded employment programme

2

Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP)

PPP is not payable to a customer who is already receiving:

  • social security pension or social security benefit
  • one of the payments from DVA
  • one of the payments under the prescribed educational scheme
  • benefits under an Australian Government funded employment programme

3

Partner Allowance (PA)

PA is not payable to a customer who is already receiving:

  • a social security pension or a social security benefit
  • one of the payments from DVA
  • one of the payments under the prescribed educational scheme
  • benefits under an Australian Government funded employment programme

4

Special Benefit (SpB)

To qualify for SpB, a social security pension or a social security benefit must not be payable to the customer.

SpB is not payable to a customer who is already receiving:

  • a social security pension or a social security benefit
  • one of the payments from DVA or a DVA widow pension
  • one of the payments under the prescribed educational scheme
  • benefits under an Australian Government funded employment programme

5

Widow Allowance (WA)

WA is not payable to a customer who is already receiving:

  • a social security pension or a social security benefit
  • one of the payments from DVA or a DVA widow pension
  • one of the payments under the prescribed educational scheme
  • benefits under an Australian Government funded employment programme

6

Youth Allowance (YA)

YA is not payable if customer receiving one of the following:

  • one of the payments under the prescribed educational scheme
  • one of the payments from DVA
  • a DVA Income Support Supplement (ISS)
  • social security benefit or pension
  • Ready Reserve Education Assistance Scheme
  • AIC Scheme
  • Maintenance Allowance for Refugees
  • Adult Migrant Education Programme Living Allowance
  • English as a Second Language Allowance (full-time students)

7

Family Tax Benefit (FTB)

FTB is not payable for a child of any age who is receiving:

  • one of the payments from DVA
  • a social security benefit and pension
  • a payment under an Australian Government Labour Market Program, and
  • for a child 16 years or over
  • receiving one of the payments under the prescribed educational scheme

8

Farm Household Allowance (FHA)

FHA is not payable to a person who is already receiving a social security pension, a Service Pension, Veteran Payment, Income Support Supplement (ISS) or a social security benefit other than FHA.

Note: there are two exemptions to the multiple entitlement exclusion for FHA:

  • The first applies to a woman who has been receiving a pension under the Veterans' Entitlement Act 1986 continuously since before 1 November 1986 and was also receiving a social security benefit before that date. See References for a link to the Act
  • The second applies to a person who has made an application for a payment under the ABSTUDY scheme in certain circumstances, see section 38 of the Farm Household Support Act 2014. See References for a link to the Act

Pensions related examples of multiple entitlement exclusions

Table 3: This table describes examples of specific pension related multiple entitlement exclusions that apply to each payment.

Item

Description

1

Age Pension

Age Pension is not payable to a customer:

  • when another social security pension or social security benefit becomes payable
  • who is receiving a DVA:
    • Service Pension
    • Veteran Payment
    • War Widow(er)'s pension, and
    • an Income Support Supplement (ISS), or who would be eligible for ISS if they claimed one

2

Carer Payment (CP)

CP is not payable to a customer:

  • who is receiving one of the payments from DVA
  • when a social security pension of another type, a social security benefit or a Service Pension or Veteran Payment becomes payable

Note: a customer may be qualified for another payment (such as Age Pension) but it would not be payable until they chose to claim it.

3

Carer Allowance (CA)

Payability of CA is not affected by multiple entitlement exclusions.

4

Disability Support Pension (DSP)

DSP is not payable to a customer:

  • when
    • a social security pension, or
    • a social security benefit (other than DSP) becomes payable
  • who is receiving one of the payments from DVA

5

Parenting Payment Single (PPS)

PPS is not payable to a customer:

  • when
    • a social security pension, or
    • a social security benefit (other than PPS) becomes payable
  • when one of the payments from DVA or a widow pension from DVA becomes payable
