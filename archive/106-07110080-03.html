This is a Family and Domestic Violence Interaction Point. If the customer is with another person, on speaker phone, or already identified with family and domestic violence concerns, continue with the current business conversation. Otherwise, go to the Family and domestic violence procedure to conduct the risk identification and referral process.

On this page:

MOD S Section 1 – Customer to complete

MOD S Section 2 – Verifying your separation

MOD S Section 1 – Customer to complete

Table 1

Item

Question

1

About you + Read more ...

Customers must provide the following details about themselves:

  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Full name
  • Date of birth
  • Safety concerns and whether they would like to be contacted to discuss their support options
    Note: this question set is only in the MODS.2110 version
  • Any change to their phone number
  • Have their address or accommodation details changed?
    Options are:
    • Yes, they must complete the Accommodation Details (MOD AC). Direct the customer to the Forms page on the Services Australia website to download the form. The Resources page contains a link
    • No

2

About your ex-partner + Read more ...

Customers must provide the following details about their most recent ex-partner:

  • Full name
  • Any other names their ex-partner uses (if known)
  • If they live in the same home as their ex-partner
  • If safety concerns:
    • have not been identified and the customer lives in the same home as their ex-partner, both the customer and ex-partner must complete a Relationship details – Separated under one roof (SS293) form. This is because the MOD S does not capture the information required to decide the customer's relationship status
    • have been identified by the customer, only the customer must complete a SS293
    • direct the customer to the Forms page on the Services Australia website to download the form. The Resources page contains a link.
  • Ex-partner's current address (if known)

3

About your relationship + Read more ...

Customers must provide the following details about their relationship:

  • When did they separate from their partner? Customers must list the full date (day, month and year) of their separation because this must be rec recorded on the Marital Status (MS) page
  • Do they think that they will get back together with their ex-partner?
    Options are:
    • Yes, they must advise when they think this may occur
      Pending more information about when they might get back together, decide if an interview is needed
      This can help find out whether there is a true separation/ break down in the relationship
    • No

4

About your children + Read more ...

Customers must provide the following details about their children:

  • Do they have any children or students in their care under 20 years of age?
  • Do they get Family Tax Benefit for the child or children in their care?
    Options are:
    • Yes, direct them to the agency website for more information about Family Tax Benefit (FTB). The Resources page contains a link
    • No
  • Will they share care of any children or has the number of children in their care changed since they separated?
    Options are:
    • Yes, they must complete a Details of your child's care arrangement (FA012) form. Direct the customer to the Forms page on the agency website to download the form. The Resources page contains a link
    • No

5

About your income and assets + Read more ...

Customers must provide the following details about their income and assets:

  • Have they, or are expecting, to receive any money or property as a result of their separation?
    Options are:
    • Yes, they must provide the details. If the customer needs more space, they can attach a separate sheet with more details
    • No
  • Has there been any other changes to their income or assets?
    Options are:
    • Yes, they need to attach a completed Income and assets (MOD iA) form. Direct the customer to the Forms page on the agency website to download the form. The Resources page contains a link
    • No

6

About your payment + Read more ...

Customers must provide details about where they want their payment made:

  • The bank, building society or credit union account must be in their name. A joint account is acceptable
  • They must not include an account used only for funding from the National Disability Insurance Scheme
  • They can nominate an account already provided or update details to a different account

Customers must provide the following:

  • Payments they want directed to a different account or whether they want all payments directed to the new account
  • The name of the bank, building society or credit union
  • Branch number (BSB)
  • Account number (this may not be the customer's card number)
  • The name(s) on the account

7

Privacy notice and declaration + Read more ...

  • Privacy Notice - customers must read the important information about their privacy and personal information
  • Declaration - customers must sign and date this section

MOD S Section 2 – Verifying your separation

Table 2

Item

Question

1

Instructions for the customer + Read more ...

Customers must:

  • Provide their and their ex-partner's full name
  • Give this section to a suitable referee to complete

How to choose a suitable referee is on page 2 of the Notes section of the form.

2

Instructions for the referee + Read more ...

Referees must provide the following details about themselves:

  • Their full name
  • Date of birth
  • Phone number
  • Relationship to the customer
  • To the best of their knowledge has the customer separated from their partner? Options are:
    • Yes
    • No, they must complete the free text section to give details

3

Privacy notice and declaration + Read more ...

  • Privacy Notice – referees should read the important information about their privacy and personal information
  • Declaration – the referee must sign and date this section
