Notes

Travel document number

When entering travel document numbers there must be no spaces between the letters and digits. The format for travel document numbers is as follows:

  • Documents pre-dating the issue of the 'P' series: 1 alphabetic character followed by 7 digits
  • Documents in the 'P' series (commenced issue in June 2014) and subsequent series travel documents: 2 alphabetic characters followed by 7 digits

Date of birth

If the day and/or month of birth is populated with 0s or Xs, escalate to Tier 3 for processing. The system currently does not accept the following data input to the DVS as they should populate the relevant fields with zeroes, for example:

  • 00 000 1940 or XX XXX 1940 to be passed as 00 00 1940
  • 00 MAR 1940 or XX MAR 1940 to be passed as 00 03 1940

Family name

Family name is always listed in the travel document Visual Inspection Zone (VIZ) above the given name/s.

Where an individual has family or given names that are longer than 31 characters (including spaces) the applicant will have recorded, in the Australian travel document, as many full family and given names as possible within the respective fields. All other names will be represented in the field by their initials (without full stops).

For example, a family name of Krishnamurty Don Sariyaarachchige and given name of Sundari Pradeep would be recorded in the passport Machine Readable Zone (MRZ) as:

Krishnamurty>Don>S>>Sundari>Pradeep

Notes

Alternatively, the user can contact the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) directly to resolve any data matching/entitlement issues.

DVS Identity Document samples - Visas

Notes

Passport number

Alpha numeric characters only up to a maximum length of 14. When entering passport numbers there must be no spaces between the letters and or digits.

The passport number is located in the top right hand corner of the Visual Inspection Zone (VIZ) and in the second line of the Machine Readable Zone (MRZ) on all passports. If entering the number as recorded in the MRZ, do not to include the final digit which is a check digit number.

Identifying the data

If DVS does not verify with the name in the MRZ, retry with the name in the VIZ.

Be careful when entering the letter O and the number 0. Numbers in the MRZ are taller and squarer than letters.

Check if the customer has obtained a new passport since they entered the country. If a customer has obtained a new passport and not notified the Department of Home Affairs, the visa cannot be verified.

The place of issue is often printed on the VIZ. If it is a city within Australia then they have had their passport reissued after arrival and likely haven't updated their new passport details with the Department of Home Affairs (see above Failed DVS).

The customer can update their passport details via ImmiAccount (if they have applied for a visa online and have created an ImmiAccount). Alternatively they can complete Form 929 (from the Department of Home Affairs website - see the Resources page for a link) and post the completed form to the nearest Department of Home Affairs office.

Some foreign nationals, such as permanent residents that migrated to Australia before the early 1990s and have not travelled out of Australia since, might not have a verifiable record with the Department of Home Affairs. These individuals should be referred to the Department of Home Affairs to assess their eligibility for an ImmiCard.

Dual nationals

Dual nationals, those with more than one passport, must ensure they present the passport that they entered Australia with in order to successfully match their visa record via the DVS.

Table 7

ImmiCard in the current name/former name.

What cards can be verified?

The Department of Home Affairs is able to verify the following types of ImmiCards:

  • Evidence of Immigration Status (EIS) ImmiCard - Issued to holders of temporary visas subclasses, for example:
    • subclass 050 or 051 Bridging visa E granted to Illegal Maritime Arrivals
    • subclass 070 Bridging (removal pending) visa
    • subclass 449 Humanitarian Stay (temporary) visa holders
    • subclass 785 Temporary Protection visa
    • subclass 786 Temporary (humanitarian concern) visa
    • subclass 790 Safe Haven Enterprise visa
  • Permanent Resident Evidence (PRE) ImmiCard - Issued to Permanent visa holders
  • Residence Determination ImmiCard (RDI) - Issued to immigration detainees accommodated in the community who do not have a visa and who are not Australian citizens
  • Australian Migration Status (AMS) ImmiCard - issued mainly to Refugee and Special Humanitarian Programme (RSHP) visa holders, and a small number of non-RSHP visa holders, who do not have, or cannot obtain, a passport recognised by the Australian Government
  • Australian Migration Status ImmiCards facilitate one way travel and entry to Australia and are also the Commencement of Identity credential to enrol for services and acquire future identity credentials

Foreign passport - no date of birth

If the passport is provided with no day or month of birth, (for example, --/--/yyyy or 00/00/yyyy or xx/xx/yyyy) attempt to pass the passport via Front of House (FOH) using blank spaces in day and month fields and enter the year of birth as per the passport.

If valid, a 'Y' response will be received in FOH. Copy the verification receipt number into the manual override comments. Best practice is to send a screen shot to the email box being used, including the account details for the user, either Registration Authority (RA) number or username in the email.

Document Verification Service (DVS) Match Support for Australian passports, foreign passports and citizenship certificates

There may be instances where data on an identifying document does not match the data held by an issuer, even where the document is valid. These discrepancies can include minor variations in name spellings or formatting conventions. In such cases additional support may be needed to confirm whether a 'non-match' response is in fact legitimate.

With written consent from the individual via email, a copy of the foreign passport can be forwarded to the DVS Manager for review. Once the DVS Manager has reviewed the document and attempts to match the information are still unsuccessful it may be forwarded to the Department of Home Affairs for advice and/or resolution.

Note: for all other types of documents that are sent via DVS, the user needs to be advised to check the information themselves with the issuing agency.

Visa Entitlement Verification Online (VEVO)

VEVO is a tool for visa holders or organisations (with permission) to check the current visa status and conditions of an individual. Visa holders are not required to register in order to use VEVO and can check their visa status themselves and nominate an email address for this information to be forwarded to.

VEVO does not provide evidence of the identity of a person. Organisations wishing to utilise VEVO are required to register by creating an ImmiAccount, the Department of Home Affairs's 'front door' to access online services. The Resources page contains a link to ImmiAccount.
