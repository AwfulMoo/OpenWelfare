Step

Action

1

Issuing BasicsCard + Read more ...

A BasicsCard can be issued by Service Officers at Service Centres, Remote Service Centres and by Remote Servicing Teams (RST) with Centrelink system connectivity and BasicsCard stocks, in a face-to-face interview with the customer.

An income managed customer in a face-to-face interview requests a replacement BasicsCard as their existing BasicsCard has been lost, stolen, damaged or is otherwise unusable.

Confirm Customer Identity

If not already done so, confirm the customer's identification before proceeding with interview. As a result of the risk of issuing funds to third parties in error, it is essential that this step is carried out thoroughly and carefully. Issuing the BasicsCard to a third party in error can result in hardship for the customer and financial loss to Services Australia. If in any doubt, do not issue the BasicsCard and consult a Services Australia social worker, or the Income Management helpdesk for further assistance.

Checks required to ensure the replacement card is to the correct customer/nominee.

Check:

  • the transaction history
  • where/when was the card last used
  • can the person confirm these details - if not why not?
  • has someone else got the original card or is the person getting the replacement card not the customer?
  • is the person where they usually are?

Additional checks when issuing a replacement BasicsCard outside of a customer's usual area.

Check:

  • the person's ID
  • ask additional authentication questions
  • if necessary make contact with someone in the local community to confirm that the customer has travelled into town
  • the transaction history

Payment nominees

If a customer (principal) has a payment nominee, the payment nominee can choose to have the BasicsCard issued in the principal's name.

If the customer/payment nominee:

  • Does not currently have a BasicsCard and is required to be issued a BasicsCard, see BasicsCard offer and issue of initial BasicsCard. Procedure ends here
  • Has an existing BasicsCard and requires funds to be allocated to the BasicsCard, see Changes to income management priority needs. Procedure ends here

If the customer's Income Management ceased more than 60 days ago, advise the customer they are not eligible to receive a replacement BasicsCard. Procedure ends here.

To issue a current BasicsCard customer with a replacement BasicsCard, go to Step 2.

2

Multiple Replacement BasicsCards + Read more ...

Determine if multiple cards have been issued to the customer by checking the BasicsCard action history workflow and the Document List (DL) screen to determine the number of BasicsCards issued.

If multiple cards have been reissued:

  • Check reasons for reissue in the past, ensure that priority needs are being met with current allocation method
  • A referral may be needed to a Specialist (such as an social worker etc) for assistance and discussion with the customer about the number of cards issued, if there is concern a new BasicsCard will not facilitate access to priority needs for the customer

The BasicsCard may not always be the most appropriate option for the customer's circumstances. Discuss with team leader if in any doubt. It also may involve replaying the BasicsCard VOD (available on the BasicsCard Intranet page) in the customer's preferred language, to reinforce the BasicsCard key messages:

  • do not throw your BasicsCard away
  • keep your BasicsCard safe and PIN secret
  • call before you shop (Income Management Line and Freecall BasicsCard Balance Enquiry Line (IVR). See the Resources page for a link to these phone numbers and other options to check the balance)

Under no circumstances should a limit be placed on the number of replacement BasicsCards that can be issued to a customer.

If a customer wants to be issued a BasicsCard but a Service Officer and social worker both believe the request should be refused, the matter must be referred to the Deduction and Confirmation Branch - BasicsCard team. The social worker must forward an email to Deduction and Confirmation Branch - BasicsCard team - the email should outline the customer's situation, the avenues explored to meet the customer's needs and why they recommend that a BasicsCard should not be issued. See contact details when further investigation or action is required in Deduction and Confirmation Branch - BasicsCard team .

3

Nominee requesting reissue? + Read more ...

Is the customer/payment nominee requesting a replacement BasicsCard and the BasicsCard is the most suitable allocation method taking into consideration reasons for multiple BasicsCard replacements?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, advise the customer a replacement cannot be provided and reasons why, for example, no longer entitled, referral to specialist is needed first. Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

4

Provide further information to the customer about the BasicsCard + Read more ...

Advise the customer of the following:

  • How to enquire about balances on the BasicsCard and the importance of having an up to date balance before BasicsCard is used. Current BasicsCard holders are able to contact Freecall BasicsCard Balance Enquiry Line (IVR) (see the Resources page for a link to the phone number), enter their BasicsCard number (16 digit number on the front of the BasicsCard) and their 4 digit Personal Identification Number (PIN) to obtain their BasicsCard balance automatically. This feature is available 24 hours a day, and does not require self service registration
  • Allocations to the BasicsCard can be made 7 days a week during normal business hours and from 8:00am to 5:00pm on weekends
  • Current BasicsCard holders are also able to go to the Services Australia website and access the online BasicsCard Balance Enquiry link under 'Checking balances on your BasicsCard'
  • How to report lost/stolen/damaged BasicsCards and the customer liability for funds lost prior to reporting. Reinforce 24 hour Smart Centre Call for lost/stolen BasicsCards on the Income Management Line. See the Resources page for a link to the phone number
  • Importance of protecting their Personal Identification Number (PIN) and how to change the PIN
  • How to identify a BasicsCard merchant where the BasicsCard can be used. Approved BasicsCard merchants are available on the Services Australia website
  • Discuss priority needs and how they are met through the various allocation methods
  • Procedures to take where a dispute occurs
  • If a replacement BasicsCard is being issued, advise the customer the new BasicsCard will cancel the lost/stolen card

5

Issue replacement BasicsCard + Read more ...

If the Customer/payment nominee has a current BasicsCard, and Service Officer is issuing a replacement BasicsCard in workflow, go to Step 7.

If workflow is not available and manually coding is available, go to Step 6.

6

Workflow not available, manually coding + Read more ...

'S'elect 'Replace (existing) Card' from the BasicsCard task selector (BCTS) screen or type 'BCRC' in the Nxt: field to reissue a card.

Note: this screen should only be used in contingency arrangements as this screen does not offer Thermal Printing of Card Holder's name, and does not link to the Inventory Tracking System (ITS) that is used to track issuing site's BasicsCard stocks.

If this screen is used, ensure that the card number is recorded and reported to the site's Accountable Forms Officer to manually update the status of the BasicsCard in the Inventory Tracking System (ITS) to ensure 'Available' BasicsCard stock level is accurate:

  • enter reason for replacement
  • enter or swipe Replacement Card number
  • get the cardholder to enter their 4 digit Personal Identification Number (PIN). Then get them to reconfirm their 4 digit PIN
  • go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen to finalise the replacement card activity. Document details of replacement card on customer's record

Note: a BasicsCard receipt will automatically print out each time a card is issued or replaced. There is no longer a requirement to sign the receipt and it can be discarded. A system change to disable the automatic print function is currently being considered.

Procedure ends here.

7

Run Replace (existing) card workflow. + Read more ...

An authentication screen displays. If the customer has a Display on Access (DOA) DOC, confirm that they have read the document/s.

Confirm if the customer's record is password protected before they can continue.

Once confirmed, the system will request how the customer has been authenticated. Select the method/s. The customer must be fully authenticated to continue workflow.

Note: DOA DOCs will only appear if they are present on the customer's record.

If a customer has a nominee, a slightly different screen displays. All sections will be greyed out except for the section allowing access to DOA and customer password. Once this is completed, the nominee's name and password must be confirmed to continue workflow. If a nominee cannot recall their password refer to Appointing Nominees.

Once authentication is complete, if the customer has a payment nominee they will be presented with an 'assign card' screen.

An information message will display stating: 'BasicsCard can only be assigned to the customer where the payment nominee has given their permission. Where the customer is assigned the BasicsCard both the payment nominee and the customer must be authenticated'.

The 'assign card' screen will show the Service Officer who the BasicsCard is currently assigned to, and allows the payment nominee to assign the BasicsCard to the principal (customer) if they wish to.

The question 'assign BasicsCard to' will display for the Service Officer. The options presented are 'nominee' or 'customer'.

If the nominee chooses to have the BasicsCard assigned to the customer, they will be presented with the question 'does the nominee consent to assigning the BasicsCard to the customer'.

  • Yes, the continue button will become available
  • No, the Service Officer must go back and select 'nominee' to continue

Note: if the customer does not have a payment nominee the assign card section will not appear.

When replacing current BasicsCards, the workflow will present the Print and Replace BasicsCard screen. This is to allow card holder's names to be thermally printed on the BasicsCard.

Select a reason for the replacement of the card from the Reason for Replacement dropdown list.

Replacement Card Details

This section will display the card holder's names to be thermally printed. This will default to the Legal name(s) recorded. This should only be changed if there is a more suitable name recorded on the customer's/payment nominee's record.

Can thermal printing be used?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in the Issuing the BasicsCard, activation and follow-up table
  • No, select no to Print customer/payment nominee name on BasicsCard and enter the reason for not thermally printing the card. The two options available are:
    • 'BasicsCard previously printed - workflow re-run' and
    • 'Printer not available'
    • The BasicsCard print request will not be sent, and Printer Location and Card Printer ID will not be required. Select Continue. See Step 2 in the Issuing the BasicsCard, activation and follow-up table

Issuing the BasicsCard, activation and follow-up

Table 2: This table describes the step by step process for printing a BasicsCard (if a thermal printer is available) or issuing the card from the card stock, activating the card and completing any follow-up actions required.

Step

Action

1

Thermal printer available + Read more ...

Select Yes for Print Customer/payment nominee name on BasicsCard? to send the print request to the thermal printer.

Radio buttons for the Printer Location? appears:

  • If printing from Service Centre/Remote Service Centre, select Office (LAN) (Thermal Printers (Pebble) located in offices are configured for a LAN connection and linked to the Office Profile of that Site) Select a card printer ID from the Card Printer ID dropdown list. Select Continue. Go to Step 2
  • If printing from a Remote Servicing Trip, select Remote. (Remote Printers (Tattoo) are configured to operate on a USB Connection for use on Laptops in Remote Areas). The Card Printer ID dropdown list will be blank. Enter the IP address (provided by the trip leader)

2

Issue Card + Read more ...

Collect BasicsCard from Thermal Printer (or card stock if Thermal Printing not available).

Note: there is no longer a requirement to complete the BasicsCard Receipt.

Even though the BasicsCard has been printed, it has not been activated on the customer's record. This does not occur until the Issue Card section is completed.

Did the card print successfully and/or swipe successfully?

Note: once card has been activated, it cannot be used for another customer. BasicsCard number can be entered manually when swipe facility is not available.

  • Yes,
    • Swipe card to activate (use the card reader to swipe the new card, the 16 digits card number should then display in the Card Number: field. If the Thermal Printer is not available, collect BasicsCard from stock and enter or swipe card number in the Card Number: field
    • Select Continue
    • The Inventory Tracking System will be updated to show card has been issued
  • No,
    • If card was damaged or misprinted, record 'No' under 'Did the card print successfully and / or swipe successfully?' as this will not activate the BasicsCard
    • Select option for why BasicsCard has not printed/swiped successfully from the drop down box. This will update the card status in the Inventory Tracking System and remove this card number from available stock
    • A reprint card option will present if available where the printing of the BasicsCard failed or was a misprint; this option will update the Inventory Tracking System automatically to adjust available stock. This option will reprint the details previously selected in the workflow

Note: if thermal printing is unavailable, write the customer's full name on the back of the BasicsCard. It is recommended that this is written with a quick drying fine point Permanent Marker (blue or black) rather than a biro as this is more permanent and less likely to rub off. For a payment nominee, write payment nominee's full name followed by '(NOM)'.

A work item will appear on customer records where a hand written card has been issued. This work item will auto complete once a thermally printed card is issued to the customer/nominee at a later contact when a thermal printer is available.

3

Balance on Receipt and EFTPOS terminal + Read more ...

Once the BasicsCard has been activated, the Service Officer will be prompted to offer the option of Balance on Receipt and EFTPOS terminal to the customer/payment nominee. If the customer/payment nominee consents to the offer and 'yes' is selected, then a 'consent statement' will appear.

The customer/payment nominee must read and accept the consent statement in order to 'opt in' to Balance on Receipt and EFTPOS terminal. Once the consent statement is accepted the workflow will continue.

If the customer/payment nominee chooses not to accept the offer then 'no' is selected and the workflow will continue.

Note: the offer of Balance on Receipt and EFTPOS terminal should be made to the individual (customer/payment nominee) who has the BasicsCard issued in their name.

There are a group of national retailers that are BasicsCard Balance on Receipt merchants. These merchants have the functionality to print BasicsCard balances on EFTPOS receipts for customers.

4

Customer/payment nominee must set PIN + Read more ...

The Change or Reset PIN screen allows BasicsCard PINs to be set. PINs must be set whenever a BasicsCard is issued or replaced. Customers/payment nominees can choose to use previous PIN if secure, but they must re-enter PIN when cards are issued.

Note: if workflow is unavailable and manual coding is available, 'S'elect Change or Reset PIN from the BCTS screen or type 'BCPU' in the Nxt: field

Access to the Change or reset PIN screen:

  • Advise the customer/payment nominee to choose a secure 4 digit PIN
  • Provide keypad to customer/payment nominee. Note: If the keypad is unavailable, PIN can be changed by the customer/payment nominee via the keyboard
  • Have the customer/payment nominee change the PIN on the keypad (out of view of Service Officer)
  • Have customer/payment nominee reconfirm PIN
  • Press 'Finalise'
  • If message 'New PIN and re-entered new PIN do not match' is displayed, the PIN update has not been successful. Have the customer/payment nominee enter new PIN and reconfirm new PIN again
  • If message 'PIN successfully updated' is displayed, the PIN has been changed

5

BasicsCard activated + Read more ...

BasicsCard has now been activated on customer's record, and previous BasicsCard has been cancelled (and can no longer be activated). An auto DOC will display on the record.

If a customer's payment nominee decided to have the BasicsCard issued to the principal (customer) this will be included in the text.

BasicsCard VOD will only need to be played if this will assist customer in understanding how to use the BasicsCard and BasicsCard balance options.

Note: when issuing the customer with a replacement card their allocation method will remain the same as previously identified. To update this see, BasicsCard changing daily spend, maximum card balance limits and allocation method.

6

BasicsCard Allocations + Read more ...

If the customer/payment nominee has an existing BasicsCard and requires funds to be allocated to the BasicsCard, see Changes to income management priority needs.
