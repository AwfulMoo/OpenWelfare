5

First advice of change in circumstances + Read more ...

If the customer is advising of the change of circumstances for the first time, the increase in the customer's rate will depend under what law their payment is made.

ABSTUDY Policy

Generally, the date of effect of a favourable determination is the date of the change in circumstances, even if the customer has not notified within 14 days.

Social Security Law

Generally, the date of effect of a favourable determination is the date of notification or date of event, whichever is later. Therefore, the customer will most likely not be eligible for any arrears.

Family assistance law

Generally, the date of effect of a favourable determination is the date from which full effect is given to the decision - full arrears are payable.

However, the date of effect can be no earlier than 1 July of the financial year before the financial year in which the favourable determination is made, for decisions about:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) by instalment. Customers must notify a change in circumstance or event such as adding a child by 30 June of the lodgement year, for example by 30 June 2010 for a change of circumstances which occurred in 2008-09
  • CCB percentage and CCB schooling percentage and entitlement to CCB by fee reduction
  • Child Care Subsidy (CCS) weekly limit of hours. Note: for reviews relating a customer’s CCS Activity Test, jurisdiction and time limit to review depends on whether it is a Customer initiated review of decision or a Secretary initiated review of decision. If the customer is applying for a formal review of a decision relating to the Activity Test, and reconciliation for the relevant financial year has not occurred, there is no jurisdiction for a customer initiated review
  • Any corrective reassessments considered under a secretary initiated review of Child Care Subsidy Activity Test for periods before the start of the previous year may reduce any related debts to nil, but ensure no additional arrears are generated

Paid Parental Leave Guide

Generally, full arrears of PPL can be paid to the customer. However, PPL period and Flexible PPL backdating limits will reduce the time period for which arrears can be paid.

Scan any relevant documents (if necessary) onto the customer’s record. See Scanning Centrelink documents using an MFD and Attaching electronic documents to a Centrelink customer's record using Document Tools in Customer First.

7

Arrears Payment - ROA screen + Read more ...

The Review of Assessment (ROA) screen allows the review of decision date of effect rules to be applied to an assessment. It is a standalone screen. To navigate to the screen:

  • enter 'ROA' in the Next: field, or
  • select the relevant review of assessment summary from the Review of Assessment Summary (ROAS) screen

The ROA screen collects the following information:

  • Start Date of Review of Decision (if applicable)
  • Section of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 the review is based on
  • Source of the error (customer or Services Australia)
  • If the customer was notified of the original decision
  • If a formal review of this decision has been requested within 13 weeks of the customer being given notice of the original decision
  • If, as a Delegate, the user agrees any calculated arrears are payable to the customer (if applicable)

The ROA screen allows the payment of arrears if the customer is entitled to receive them.

If the customer is entitled to arrears this screen cannot calculate, manual calculation of arrears may be needed.

8

Assessment results + Read more ...

Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen. Any arrears payable will be calculated automatically and appear on this screen.

To investigate the arrears further, view the following screens, from the AR screen.

  • Rate Summary (RATS) screen - shows each pay period if there is a rate change, old and new rates, the number of affected pay periods and the amount of the adjustment for each period
  • Assessment Circumstance Explanation (ACE) screen - shows details of any changes in circumstances which have contributed to an adjustment in the customer's or partner's rate for NSS, PEN and PGA systems

Note: verify any Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) arrears with the AIC Processing Team (Office Code LPT) before paying. The Resources page has a link.

Are the arrears (or no arrears) on the AR screen correct?

  • Yes, finalise the activity and record details on a DOC. Tell the customer of the outcome, including any potential tax liability, and explain their review and appeal rights. Procedure ends here
  • No, stop processing and take corrective action. When completing the activity, the Service Officer is making a determination that the result is correct. See Step 2 in Table 2
  • If unsure the customer is entitled to the arrears, go to Step 9

9

Decide if the customer is entitled to arrears + Read more ...

Factors which affect the payment of arrears:

  • a decision about this issue has been previously made
  • if the customer has been notified of the decision in writing
  • how long ago the decision was made
  • if the customer has advised of a change in circumstances
  • correction of a previous assessment (if a debt generated) results in a duplicate arrears amount for the same period. See Step 4 in Table 2 for how to tell the Debt Raising team if arrears are zeroed to offset a corresponding debt
  • there may be system arrears limits to some payments

Check the correct date of effect is used. For more information, see:

  • Dates of effect for review of decision outcomes
  • Customer initiated review of decision
  • Secretary initiated review of decision
  • Changes in circumstances and date of effect for Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS)

Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU)

The system will use arrears to offset a debt if an activity causes an NBU debt due to loss of eligibility. For example, FTB A cancels and then an activity pays NBU arrears. To allow the automated withholdings process to offset arrears, do not suppress the arrears and do not code FMAD.

Withholdings will automatically apply from the NBU payment to the outstanding amount of an existing NBU debt. This displays on the PS and PD screens before the activity is completed. It displays as a withholding for Family Assistance.

This offsetting also occurs when processing multiple activities on one day. The first paying arrears of NBU and then an activity causing a loss of eligibility resulting in a debt. If these activities are on the same day, the offset will occur so the agency does not issue a payment and then raise a debt on the same day.

Parental Leave Pay (PPL)

PPL arrears will offset against any PPL overpayments or income support payment (ISP) overpayments that are generated in the same activity. This only applies to ISP overpayments that occur due to the payment of PPL for the past period.

This offset will occur to avoid the issue of a payment and debt raised on the same day.

4

Adjusting arrears within current activity + Read more ...

Incorrect arrears can be adjusted/zeroed within the current activity. See the Training and Support page for a link to the relevant training.

In Customer First:

  • On the Assessment Results (AR) screen, on the left hand menu select payment type arrears will be paid with
  • Select Rate Details (RATS)
  • Select relevant period on the Daily Rate Component (RAC) and make any changes to the rate:
    • Update the Man Adj $ and Man Adj Rsn fields
    • Generally, use the Man Adj Rsn code of ADJ. Service Officers in Specialist Teams may use other codes

In Customer Record:

  • On the Assessment Results (AR) screen, code 'T' against the relevant line if the incorrect arrears show. Press [Enter]
  • The Daily Rate Summary (RATS) screen is displayed
  • Identify which arrears line(s) are incorrect; code 'R' against the appropriate line(s). Press [Enter]
  • The arrears can now be adjusted:
    • Update the Man Adj $ and Man Adj Rsn fields
    • Generally, use the Man Adj Rsn code of ADJ. Service Officers in Specialist Teams may use other codes

In Process Direct

  • Once ‘assess’ is selected to finalise the activity, an Assessment Results page opens to check the rate of payment and/or arrears being paid
  • If the arrears are incorrect for CCS do not manually adjust the payment. Place the transaction ‘On Hold’ for 7 days, and escalate the record using Roxy or mySupport for investigation. Record Notes with the details of the mySupport request ID and reason for escalation
  • For payments other than CCS, navigate to the ‘Rate Summary’ icon to view the daily rate summary
  • Identify which arrears line(s) are incorrect:
    • Adjust the relevant lines under the Daily Rate Component (RAC)
    • Scroll down to the ‘more options’ button and the ‘apply RAC changes’ button before selecting ‘Finish’

Note:

  • Do not code RAC adjustments for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) arrears within a period of reconciliation and/or re-reconciliation. If arrears are generated for a reconciled period, see Reconciliation of FTB. Adjust FTB arrears for the current financial year or periods that have not been reconciled on the RAC screen if there is a corresponding debt or the customer does not have entitlement
  • For adjustments to Rent Assistance (RA), see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing RA
  • Some payments may have to be adjusted by the One Off Payment (OOP) screen, for example Telephone Allowance (TAL).
  • If arrears have been zeroed to offset a corresponding determined or undetermined debt, tell the Debt Raising team by creating an open work item using the Fast Note. Select:
    • Auto Text use Debts > DMB Raising > Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request
    • Select Confirm
    • Edit auto text, makes sure detailed information is included as to what action is the Debt Raising team needs
    • Select Continue
  • If a reassessment has been completed in Customer First or Customer Record and the customer receives CCS, Service Officers are to investigate the impact of the reassessment on CCS. Access the customer’s record in Process Direct:
    • Select the Child Care icon and tell the customer of any changes to their CCS as per the Entitlement table
    • Open the Weekly Subsidy twisty to check for reassessments that have occurred for periods from the DOV used for the reassessment activity. A reassessment will display as the date of the transaction under the ‘Processed Date’ column. The outcome may be arrears payments to the customer, or potential overpayment(s) (negative amounts)
    • If the CCS percentage has reduced, tell the customer they can increase their CCS withholdings to reduce the risk of an overpayment at reconciliation
    • Even if no potential overpayment has been identified, remind customers they can adjust their withholdings to reduce the likelihood of an overpayment at the end of the financial year.
      Customers can adjust their withholding percentage up to 2 times per financial year through their Centrelink online account.
      They must contact the agency for subsequent adjustments; staff will manually code the change in CCS withholding percentage.
      For more information see CCS withholdings

Tell the customer of the outcome, including any potential tax liability. Explain their review and appeal rights. Record the details on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

2

Incorrect arrears issued in error after an activity has been finalised + Read more ...

Make sure the customer's record is correct before requesting the payment to be stopped. For example:

  • income has been recorded correctly and is reducing the customer's rate correctly
  • if the customer is not entitled to the payment because the record should be suspended (SUS) or cancelled (CAN), make sure the SUS or CAN is coded from the correct date

If a payment already been sent to the Reserve Bank

Generally, a payment cannot be stopped if it is after the payment delivery strip time. The exception is if an arrears payment will reject at the payment delivery strip time as it exceeds the system arrears limits allowed to be issued in 1 payment.

The rejected payment will split into part payments, each of which will be below the system arrears limits. Each payment will deliver separately. This will generally happen the following day. The ICT Service Desk can stop the arrears at that point.

Requests to stop payments

  • Must be made before close of business on the same day the arrears were generated if the amount is below the system arrears limit
  • Can be made later, generally the following day, if the arrears amount is over the system arrears limit. Check the Payment Summary (PS) screen for the status of payments to the customer

  • No, go to Step 3

4

Create a 'Stop Payment' DOC and escalate to ICT Service Desk + Read more ...

'Stop Payment' DOC

Create a 'Stop Payment' DOC on the customer's record explain why they are not entitled to the arrears payment. For audit purposes, the ICT Service Desk needs this DOC.

It must include the following details:

  • Payment type/component
  • Amount to be removed/stopped
  • Period of the payment to be removed/stopped
  • The reason the stop payment is needed. Note: recording 'customer not entitled' as the stop payment reason is not sufficient. The DOC must specify why the customer is not entitled, and
  • The relevant Operational Blueprint file which supports the decision not to pay any unpaid arrears payment (if applicable)

See the Resources page for an example 'Stop Payment' DOC.

Escalate to ICT Service Desk

Escalate directly to the local Service Support Officer (SSO) or Team Leader (TL) for immediate action. See Service Officer role when seeking Technical Peer Support (TPS) in Smart Centres.

Once the SSO or TL is satisfied all action has been taken and the payment must be stopped, they are to escalate to ICT Service Desk using the mySupport webform 'Stop Customer Payment'. Record the relevant Service Desk (SD) number in the 'Stop Payment' DOC.

  • For Child Care Subsidy use the mySupport ‘Child Care Subsidy and Additional Child Care Subsidy Issues’ webform. Enter ‘BLOCK PAYMENT’ under the ‘Please input personalised message’ and 'What function is being attempted when the issue occurred? ’fields
  • For Farm Household Allowance use the mySupport ‘Farm House Allowance (FHA) Issues webform and select 'Block Payment' option under 'What is the issue?' If the arrears are under the system arrears limit:
    • Use the ‘Customer Hardship, Aggression and External Customer Complaints’ option to contact the ‘ICT Service Desk – Urgent Escalation only’ team. The Resources page has a link to mySupport. This will activate processing before the payment strip

Note: the ICT Service Desk will log all instances if incorrect arrears have been paid/stopped. For audit purposes, a list of logons is recorded. The system notifies the Service Manager if staff have 3 stop payments in any 6 month period.

Incorrect arrears used to offset a debt + Read more ...

    • Level 2: Debt recovery referrals
