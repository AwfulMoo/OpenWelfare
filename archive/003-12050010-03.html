For Services Australia social workers only.

If a child is in immediate danger or risk of harm you must act, and a delegate must call 000.

This page has information about child support exemptions that require a social work assessment.

Process of assessing a child support exemption

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer is referred to a social worker as they are unable to take child support action + Read more ...

In some situations, customers may be unable to take action to receive child support. See Maintenance Action Test (MAT) codes for exempt codes assessed by a social worker.

The social worker will interview the customer or assess the evidence provided by the customer. For fear of violence and imposition exemptions, the social worker should firstly explore the benefits of collecting child support and explain collection options to ensure the customer is aware of all alternatives to an exemption.

Does the customer understand the benefits of child support?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Ensure the customer understands the benefits of child support + Read more ...

The social worker should ensure the customer understands that:

  • child support is for the financial benefit of the child and the parent caring for the child
  • child support improves the financial resources for children not living with both parents and can be received until the child turns 18 years old
  • children are entitled to receive support from both parents
  • child support does not have to involve contact between the parents
    • Child Support can act as a mediator and collect on behalf of a parent
    • for example, receiving child support is not linked with access issues and does not require personal contact between the parents
  • the benefits of requesting Child Support Collection, including the opportunity to use the disbursement method if regular Child Support is not being received, and
  • a partial exemption may be granted in private collect cases where a customer is unable to collect their full entitlement, but it is not appropriate for them to transfer collection method to child support

Based on this understanding, does the customer want to apply for an exemption?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, DOC the record with the outcome of the conversation with the customer and/or Child Support. procedure ends here

3

Assessing Eligibility for an exemption + Read more ...

For certain types of exemptions (for example, IVF and PNK), a social work interview is not mandatory. If a social worker is conducting an interview as part of the assessment, the social worker must discuss the following issues:

  • The assessment process, privacy provisions and Freedom of Information (FOI) procedures
  • The customer's requirement to nominate an independent third party to verify their circumstances where possible
  • The customer's requirement to provide requested information such as written verification or a child's birth certificate
  • The reasons why they are unable to collect child support
  • If the customer has a Child Support collect case and is not receiving their full entitlement, discuss whether having their Family Tax Benefit (FTB) assessed using the disbursement method may be appropriate
  • If there is an active child support case, whether a partial or full exemption is more appropriate
  • Tell the customer that a Child Support case can only be ended after a discussion with a Child Support Service Officer:
    • it is important that the customer understands that an active child support case will continue until Child Support has independently confirmed the customer’s wish to end an active case
  • If the customer has an active child support case whether the customer may elect to end their assessment and discharge arrears
  • If the customer asks to end an active case and not discharge arrears, ensure the ongoing risk of arrears collection is fully considered
  • Tell the customer that if the exemption is granted and if their circumstances change, they should contact Services Australia to discuss what child support action is required
  • Ensure the customer is informed of their review and appeal rights

Following the interview with the customer, or receipt of a request for an exemption, register the exemption claim in Social Work Information System (SWIS).

If required, verify the grounds for the exemption, go to Step 4.

4

Verifying grounds for an exemption + Read more ...

The social worker should fully explore with the customer ways of verifying the grounds for an exemption such as advice/verification from:

  • health professionals
  • community agencies
  • legal practitioners
  • police
  • relatives, or
  • friends

Explain to the third party that:

  • the customer has given permission for the contact, and
  • no guarantees can be given that the information that they provide would not be released to the customer

Clearly document any information provided by the third party, in the delegated decision report. Particularly sensitive information may also be documented in SWIS case notes.

If the customer is unable to nominate an independent third party and the social worker considers that there is enough evidence, the exemption may be granted after consultation with a Social Work Support Manager.

Record details of the contact with each independent third party in SWIS.

If social worker is:

  • unable to make an immediate determination due to insufficient information/evidence, go to Step 5
  • able to finalise the exemption go to Step 6

5

Social Worker unable to determine child support exemption due to insufficient information/evidence + Read more ...

  • Inform the customer the claim is pending further information
  • Record the undetermined assessment in a DOC using the Social Work Assessment in Progress Fast Note template. Select Auto text. Use Social Worker > Claims. Include:
    • reason for pending decision
    • what further details are required
    • the advice given to the customer about what is required to finalise the claim and the date this is to be provided. After this time the social worker will need to formally reject the claim, and inform the customer of their review and appeal rights

Note: rejected claims require a SWIS report to be completed. See Recording Child Support Scheme (CSS) exemption referrals in the Social Work Information System (SWIS).

To finalise the exemption, go to Step 6.

6

Finalise the exemption + Read more ...

Using the information provided by the customer, independent third party or verification documents and any additional information:

  • Determine whether the customer's circumstances meet the eligibility criteria for a child support exemption and under which category
  • Ensure the child support exemption assessment supports an intervention in line with the Social Work Practice Standards focus areas of: safety and wellbeing, financial support, education and employment and strengthening communities
  • Consider backdating provisions for Family Tax Benefit:
    • grounds for backdating an exemption are established through an assessment on whether the circumstances existed before the date of claim for the entire backdated period
    • when backdating an exemption before 1 July of the previous financial year, special circumstances for backdating a maintenance action exemption must exist

Backdating an exemption for a customer who was not meeting the MAT may result in an arrears payment. If arrears are to be generated and the customer has an existing debt, consider whether a temporary exclusion from debt recovery is required where exceptional or unforeseen circumstances exist. See:

  • Debt Offsetting during Family Assistance reassessment, reconciliation and claim processing
  • Temporary write off of Centrelink debts

Upon finalising:

  • if rejecting the exemption, go to Step 7
  • if granting the exemption, go to Step 8

7

Rejecting the exemption + Read more ...

If the exemption is denied:

  • complete the report in SWIS and ensure relevant details are in a DOC
  • tell the customer of the decision and of their obligation to take action to obtain child support within 14 days
  • tell the customer of their review and appeal rights

Ensure the appropriate MAT code is updated on the MNGS screen through one of the following processes:

  • Urgent exemption coding
    • Front of House social workers - phone the Access Support Centre (ASC). For the quickest response, call before 10 am or after 2 pm (outside peak times)
    • Smart Centre social workers - use the floor walker model where available, otherwise phone the Access Support Centre
  • Non-urgent exemption coding
    Create a Fast Note: Select Auto text. Use Families > Updates > MTCE exemption coding required. Request an update to the MAT code for the relevant child/ren

Once the coding is complete, the social worker should send the customer a Q888 using the approved text. See Exemptions from seeking child support for FTB customers for approved letter text

Procedure ends here.

8

Granting the exemption + Read more ...

Complete the report in SWIS. In the Decision DOC include the date from which the exemption is to apply, the type of exemption, and the child/ren for which the exemption applies.

If granting a:

  • full exemption and the customer:
    • has an active child support case, go to step 9
    • does not have an active child support case, go to step 13
  • partial exemption and the customer:
    • has an active private collect child support case, go to step 10
    • does not have an active child support case, go to step 11

9

Granting a full exemption for a customer with an active child support case + Read more ...

Tell the customer that their active Child Support case will need to be ended. If the customer does not end their child support case the exemption is of no benefit.

If there is evidence of family and domestic violence, and:

  • the care of the children is shared between the parties to a child support case
    • the receiving parent can elect to end the child support assessments for all the children of the case if the annual rate of child support for both parties is $0 (even if one or more of the children are solely in the other parent’s care).
  • the annual rate of child support is greater than $0 for either party, the receiving parent is only able to end the child support assessment for the children in their care
    • this could result in the receiving parent becoming the paying parent for the children in the other parents care
  • ending the case may result in further family and domestic violence or may result in the receiving parent becoming the paying parent, a partial exemption may be considered but only if the child support case is private collect

Note: if the individual is the net payer, a requirement for them to take reasonable action to get maintenance for the children of the relationship in their care generally does not apply, as they are regarded as having taken reasonable action.
This includes cases where an exemption has been granted for the payee. If on the other hand, the payer’s care of the FTB child increases, or their ATI increases and they subsequently become a potential payee, the requirement to take maintenance action may then apply.
The Level 2 Policy Helpdesk can help if policy advice is needed. See the Resources page for a link

  • If the customer has arrears and is under Child Support collection, ask if they want their arrears recovered or discharged, exploring the risks relating to either options. For example, Child Support will continue to send letters to the non-reciprocal parent, or the likelihood of Child Support being able to recover the arrears through Tax Returns

Note:

  • The end date of an active Child Support case must be after the date a customer has requested to end the case with a Child Support Service Officer. Customers can not request backdating of the child support 'case end' to align with the Family Tax Benefit exemption start date
  • If Child Support are unable to speak to the customer to confirm the request to end the child support case, the child support case will remain active until such time as the customer directly requests to end their child support case with Child Support
  • This can have flow on effects for the customer. If the child support case is:
    • Private collect - their child support case will continue to be assessed under the MIT and have an impact on their rate of Family Tax Benefit
    • Child Support collect - Child Support will continue to attempt to collect child support on the customer's behalf (including any arrears that have accrued). Any amount collected will be assessed under the Maintenance Income Test (MIT) and have an impact on their rate of Family Tax Benefit
  • Go to Step 13

10

Granting a partial exemption for a customer with an active private collect child support case + Read more ...

Partial exemptions are assessed by social workers and can only be granted for:

  • private collect customers who are not receiving their full child support assessment. This can be either ongoing or for a past period due to a retrospective increase in their child support assessment
  • customers that do not have a child support case in place and are receiving voluntary payments (cash or non-cash) from the other parent but they fear if they formally seek child support it will cause the other parent to react violently

The social worker must record a DOC with the amount of child support the customer is/will be privately collecting so that the amount can be recorded. If an amount is not recorded, the full child support entitlement will continue to be used in the MIT. This means the exemption will not benefit the customer.

Tell the customer that the child support case will remain current (Child Support are not advised of partial exemptions).

The customer needs to be aware that Child Support will continue to advise both parties of their full child support liability

Remind the customer to contact Centrelink if the amount privately collected changes.

Go to Step 12.

11

Granting a partial exemption for a customer that does not have an active child support case + Read more ...

A partial exemption from the MAT can be granted in circumstances where a customer does not have an active child support case in place but the other party is voluntarily paying child support to the customer and there is:

  • a fear of violence or other harm
  • cultural considerations to take into account or
  • other exceptional circumstances that make it unreasonable for the individual to formally take maintenance action

The social worker must record a DOC with the amount of child support the customer is collecting so the amount can be recorded.

Remind the customer to contact Centrelink if the amount privately collected changes.

Go to step 12.

12

Coding a partial exemption + Read more ...

Ensure the exemption and maintenance income are coded through one of the following processes:

Urgent exemption coding:

  • Front of House social workers - phone the Access Support Centre (ASC). For the quickest response, call before 10 am or after 2 pm (outside peak times)
  • Smart Centre social workers - use the floor walker model where available, otherwise phone the ASC

Non-urgent exemption coding:

  • Create a Fast Note. Select Auto text. Use Families > Updates > MTCE exemption coding required. Request an update to the MAT code and maintenance income for the relevant child/ren
  • The social worker should send the customer a Q888 using the approved text. See Exemptions from seeking child support for FTB customers for approved letter text.

Go to step 14

13

Coding a full exemption + Read more ...

Ensure the exemption is coded through one of the following processes:

  • Urgent exemption coding
    • Front of House social workers - phone the Access Support Centre (ASC). For the quickest response, call before 10 am or after 2 pm (outside peak times)
    • Smart Centre social workers - use the floor walker model where available, otherwise phone the Access Support Centre
  • Non-urgent exemption coding
    • Create a Fast Note. Select Auto text. Use Families > Updates > MTCE exemption coding required. Request an update to the MAT code for the relevant child/ren
    • Send Q052 to the customer advising the exemption has been granted

Tell the customer of the decision.

If the customer has an active Child Support Case, warm transfer them to Child Support via the Parent Support Team.

If a warm transfer is not possible, social workers must email the Parent Support Team using the ‘Maintenance Exemption Notification to Child Support Program’ template. See the Resources page for the template.

Note: ensure all contact details for the customer are current in Customer First.

Go to Step 14.

14

Determine if a review of exemption is required + Read more ...

The social worker will decide whether a review of an exemption will be required.

Social workers have the discretion to determine the length of the review period, dependent upon the circumstances of a particular case.

If a social worker deems a future review is appropriate, the Service Officer must code the review on the Review Registration (RVR) screen when coding the exemption.

Is a future review of the exemption required?

  • Yes, go to Step 15
  • No, procedure ends here

15

Creating a MAT Exemption Review + Read more ...

When a social worker determines a review is required:

  • determine the date for the review to be created
  • record details of the review requirements in the exemption DOC
  • request the Service Officer coding the exemption also records the manual review
  • record the reasons for the review in the SWIS report

A national layer Work Load Manager (WLM) work item will create when the review is due for action.

Procedure ends here.

Undertaking a review of child support exemption

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Review becomes due for action + Read more ...

When the review becomes due for action, the following steps should be undertaken by the social worker:

  • Contact the customer to assess if an exemption is still required
  • Ensure the customer understands the benefits of child support collection
  • Consider a partial exemption as this may be an alternative option where the customer is able to apply for a Child Support assessment, however is not able to collect all of their possible entitlement

Does the exemption need to continue?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Exemption to continue + Read more ...

If the social worker determines that the exemption is to continue:

  • without the need to schedule a future manual review date, no further action is required, or
  • subject to a further review, the social worker will need to request a manual review is coded by a Service Officer

Procedure ends here.

3

Exemption no longer applies + Read more ...

If the social worker determines that the violence exemption should no longer apply, they must:

  • tell the customer of the decision and of their obligation to take action to obtain child support within 14 days
  • tell the customer of their review and appeal rights
  • complete the report in SWIS and ensure relevant details are in a DOC

Update the MNGS screen with the appropriate MAT code through one of the following processes:

  • Urgent exemption coding
    • Front of House social workers - phone the Access Support Centre (ASC). For the quickest response, call before 10 am or after 2 pm (outside peak times)
    • Smart Centre social workers - use the floor walker model where available, otherwise phone the Access Support Centre
  • Non-urgent exemption coding
    Create a Fast Note. Select Auto text. Use Families > Updates > MTCE exemption coding required. Request an update to the MAT code for the relevant child/ren

Send a free text (Q888) letter to the customer to confirm the decision and the need to take action to obtain child support. See Exemptions from seeking child support for FTB customers for approved letter text.

Note: always notify the customer in writing of the decision to deny the exemption.
