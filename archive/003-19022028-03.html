Additional information for AGDRP/ex-gratia DRP only

To assess major damage from smoke and/or ash for Northern NSW Bushfires. February 2019 a person’s principal place of residence must be reasonably close to the major disaster. The following questions will assist with the assessment of ‘reasonably close’:

  • How far away was your home from the bushfire?
  • Did you have to evacuate?
  • Did the bushfires impact your suburb or the suburb immediately next to your suburb?
  • Was the bushfire within five kilometres of your home?
  • Was the bushfire within ten kilometres of your home?

For customers with a home outside the 10km radius the following questions may help to determine if any major damage was a direct result of the designated bushfire:

  • Your home is quite a large distance away from the fire itself. Were there specific circumstances that led to the interior of your home experiencing smoke and/or ash damage as a direct result of this major disaster?
  • Have you claimed insurance for the damage and been issued with a claim number?
  • How would you describe the circumstances that led to smoke and/or ash damage to the interior of your home?

See the Resources page for maps that will assist in determining if the principal place of residence is ‘reasonably close’ to the bushfires.

Office Locator is to be used to identify which LGA applies for eligibility purposes. Service Officers are to search by suburb name only. Searches are not to be completed on postcodes. See Resources for a link to Office Locator.

Assess eligibility for AGDRP/ex-gratia DRP + Read more ...

See Eligibility for AGDRP and NZ DRP.

For Northern NSW Bushfires, February 2019 the assessment of major damage from smoke and/or ash has been updated. The following questions may be useful to determine if major damage from smoke and/or ash has occurred:

  • Please describe the damage to your home
  • Is the interior or exterior damaged? Note: exterior damage is not eligible
  • Did you have to replace contents?
  • Did you have to repair interior damage?
  • Could the smoke/ash damage be cleaned? Note: customers must have repaired or replaced soft furnishings or electrical appliances.
  • How could you demonstrate the damage occurred? E.g. could you provide a photo or invoice? Note: this information can be forwarded to the department through a customer’s online account via the upload documents service

Elements to be considered when assessing major damage from smoke and/or ash can be found in the Definitions for the adversely affected eligibility criteria table of Assessing eligibility for the Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP) or ex-gratia Disaster Recovery Payment (DRP). The table also provides examples of repair work that needs to be considered when assessing claims.

Note: for ex-gratia DRP a customer must have participated in the Australian taxation system for at least 1 year in the following past 3 financial years 2015-16, 2016-17, 2017-18 or will do so before 21 February 2020.
