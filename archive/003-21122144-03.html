Age eligibility + Read more ...

To be eligible for DRA, a customer must be at least 16 years of age on any day of the major disaster. This means they must have a loss of income as a direct result of the major disaster when they are at least 16 years of age.

Review Current emergency management events for the definition of on any day of the major disaster. The period may range from one day to several weeks from the date of activation.

Was the customer at least 16 years of age on any day of the major disaster?

  • No, the claim should be rejected:
    • If approval has been given to reject claims, process the claim. See Table 3
    • If approval has not been given, see Step 3 in Table 4

Does the customer have dependent children? + Read more ...

If the customer is single and has dependent children, it can affect their rate of payment if DRA is granted. The customer does not need to be the principal carer of the child/ren.

To be paid at the single, with child rate of DRA/NZ DRA the customer must have at least 14% care of the child coded in their care.

Note: system issues currently prevent a customer from recording a child in their care who was 16 years at time of claim lodgement but was under 16 years at time of the disaster. If the customer is single and has a child listed on their record who was under 16 years on any day of the disaster consider whether the customer should be paid at the ‘single with dependent child rate’.

Was the attempt to contact the customer to discuss providing their TFN successful?

  • Yes, if the customer:
    • advised they will provide their TFN within 28 days, go to Step 5
    • refused to provide TFN in writing within 28 days:

      - Tell the customer they can choose to withdraw their claim, or their claim will be rejected
      - Tell the customer why they do not meet requirements for the payment and that a letter will be sent outlining the review/appeal process
      - Annotate the Progress of Claim Note on the DL screen under the Document list/note section with details of the discussion

Request for Information (RFI) for a Tax File Number (TFN) + Read more ...

Select the More Options menu while on the TS screen then Request Documents to send an RFI letter.

  • Benefit: select DRA Disaster Recovery Allowance
  • Authority to request: Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 - Section 63
  • Request: 1st
  • Customer to contact: Call Centre
  • Information to be supplied within. For customers with:
    • an online account, select a date from the calendar 28 days + an additional 2 working days
    • no online account, select a date from the calendar 28 days and Allow extra time for mail delivery
      - see Time allowed to respond
  • Select Next
  • In the search field, key TAX FILE NUMBER to find related options, then select Search
  • Use Select for Tax File Number - Customer to move that item to the Selected Documents list, then select Next
  • Select Generate print preview to review the letter
    • Staff must review the letter to check there are no errors and it is easy to understand.
    • If the letter has any errors and/or is not easy to understand, staff must redo it.
    • To redo the letter, close the print preview
      - Select Close to exit the letter
      - The Close Request box will appear
      - Select OK
      - Go back to the More Options menu then Request Documents to start again. Note: any previously saved options may need to be removed if they were incorrect.
      - Make the updates
      - Select Generate print preview to review the letter again.
      - If any more changes are needed, redo the letter again as per the above steps
  • Options: Defaults to Print Central
  • Staff notes: Add information about the request or ‘Refer to Progress of Claim notes for Claim ID <insert claim ID>
  • Select Finalise to issue the letter
  • Go to Step 5

Place claim on hold + Read more ...

When an RFI has been issued in the claim the system will automatically place the claim On Hold

  • with reason “Customer to Provide Information”, and
  • for the number of days selected in the RFI application.

When information or evidence has been requested verbally, the claim will need to be placed On Hold manually.

Update Claim Status and put claim On Hold manually (verbal requests only)

  • Select Status and edit the status to On Hold
  • Select hold reason Customer to Provide Information
  • Change the On Hold Expiry Date. For customers with:
    • an online account, select a date from the calendar 28 days + an additional 2 working days
    • no online account, select a date from the calendar 28 days and Allow extra time for mail delivery
      - see Time allowed to respond
  • Select Save

Update Progress of Claim Note

  • Annotate the Progress of Claim Note on the DL screen under the Document list/note section with details of attempted customer contact and/or RFI issued and that claim is held pending a TFN
  • Select Save
  • Select Close document

TFN exemption + Read more ...

After contacting the customer, a TFN exemption can be applied if the customer can provide their TFN in writing within 28 days:

  • select the Tax File Number Authorisation from the Task Selector list on the SWE screen
  • record the exemption code DIS in the Exemption Reason field, and
  • select Simulate

Explain TFN exemption

Tell the customer:

  • they can provide their TFN by uploading it online
  • they must provide their TFN in writing within 28 days, otherwise their payment may be cancelled and payments already received will need to be paid back to Services Australia

Make sure the details of exemption and discussion with the customer are recorded in the Claim Finalisation Note.
