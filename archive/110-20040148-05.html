Process Direct - Current Issues

Helping customers complete income stream reviews online

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Question: How long is the One Time Access Code (OTAC) for my income stream review valid for?

Answer:

  • For October reviews, the OTAC within the income stream review letter is valid until the review is submitted online or 31 December the year it was issued
  • For April reviews, the OTAC within the income stream review letter is valid until the review is submitted online or 31 May of the year it was issued

2

Question: Can a third party complete the review on my behalf?

Answer: The OTAC allows your nominee or a third party to complete the income stream review on your and/or your partner's behalf. Your nominee will also get a copy of the letter sent to you containing the OTAC. They will be able to use your Customer Reference Number (CRN) and the OTAC to complete your review online at servicesaustralia.gov.au/incomestreamreviews.

Nominees who are registered for online services can complete the review on your behalf by accessing their Centrelink online account from myGov.

3

Question: I have completed my review online, but I have received a reminder/suspension/cancellation letter?

Answer: In most circumstances, a customer will complete their review between the time a reminder/suspension/cancellation letter is issued and the time the letter is received by the customer.

Service Officers should check the Future Activity List (FAL) screen to determine if the review has been finalised or, if there are any outstanding documents that the customer needs to lodge for the review to be finalised.

4

Question: Why can't I complete my review using a paper form anymore?

Answer: Consider these talking points to promote the benefits of online services, such as:

  • it is the quickest way to update information directly and ensuring customer payments are accurate
  • it provides customers with a faster interaction with a range of other benefits, including immediate access to their information anytime, additional functions available such as advances or income and asset updates, and connections to other services including Medicare
  • using this service enables the agency to devote more resources to complex customer enquiries and vulnerable customers

Determining if account balance increase or decrease is outside the threshold

The system applies a formula that decides if the account balance given by the customer is an increase or decrease outside of the thresholds. In these cases, a Service Officer must investigate the review activity.

The system applies a different formula to the April and October reviews.

Table 2

Item

Description

1

For October reviews

A = (previous 1 July account balance) - (gross annual income in the previous financial year) - (commutations recorded from 1 July to 30 June of previous financial year).

B = A - (Ax30%). This is the minimum threshold.

C = A + (Ax30%). This is the maximum threshold.

Compare the new 1 July balance provided by the customer for the online update.

If the new 1 July balance is lower than B, it is considered a significant decrease.

If the new 1 July balance is higher than C, it is considered a significant increase.

In both scenarios, the review will be referred to a Service Officer for follow up.

If the new 1 July balance falls within B and C it is acceptable and will be updated.

2

Example of a significant decrease/increase for October reviews

Previous 1 July account balance = $20,000

Gross annual income for previous financial year = $7,000

Commutations recorded for previous financial year = $2,000

A = $11,000 ($20,000 - $7,000 - $2,000)

B = $11,000 - ($11,000 x 30%). Therefore B = $7,700. This is the minimum threshold.

C = $11,000 + ($11,000 x 30%). Therefore C = $14,300. This is the maximum threshold.

Therefore, the customer's new 1 July account balance must be between $7,700 and $14,300 to be considered acceptable and will auto-update. If the new 1 July account balance provided by the customer is less than $7,700 or greater than $14,300 it will require further investigation.

3

For April reviews

A = (1 July account balance) - (50% of gross annual income in the current financial year) - (commutations previously recorded and reported during the current financial year).

B = A - (Ax20%). This is the minimum threshold.

C = A + (Ax20%). This is the maximum threshold.

Compare the new account balance provided by the customer for the online update.

If new account balance is lower than B, it is considered a significant decrease.

If the new account balance is higher than C, it is considered a significant increase.

In both scenarios, the review will be referred to a Service Officer for follow up.

If the new account balance falls within B and C it is acceptable and will be updated.

4

Example of a significant decrease/increase for April reviews

1 July account balance = $30,000.

Gross annual income for current financial year = $5,000.

Commutations recorded for current financial year = $1,000.

A = $26,500 ($30,000 - $2,500 - $1,000).

B = $26,500 - ($26,500 x 20%). Therefore B = $21,200. This is the minimum threshold.

C = $26,500 + ($26,500 x 20%). Therefore C = $31,800. This is the maximum threshold.

Therefore, the customer's new current account balance must be between $21,200 and $31,800 to be considered acceptable and will auto-update. If the new current account balance provided by the customer is less than $21,200 or greater than $31,800 it will require further investigation.
