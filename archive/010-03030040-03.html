2

Geographic Isolation Rule 1 + Read more ...

If there is a transport service to the school, or could be made available if requested, the shortest practical route is the distance from the:

  • home to the nearest available transport service, and
  • transport service to the school by the route used by the service (or connecting services)

Where morning and afternoon transport services differ in length, the average of the 2 is taken to be the shortest practical route.

As the applicant has applied under the geographic isolation rules, it must be determined that the principal family home is geographically isolated from the nearest appropriate state school.

Check the information supplied on the Claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (SY040) form, or via evidence supplied with the claim via the AIC Marginal Distance Confirmation (SY069) form.

For verification requirements on when to request this form be completed (The Resources page contains verification requirements on when to request this form be completed (that is, what distances or travel time are marginal distances or time that need checking).

Is the principal family home at least 56 kms from the home to the nearest appropriate state school?

  • Yes, the student is isolated under Rule 1. Code the following fields on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
    • Distance from Home to the Nearest Approp Govt School
    • Distance from Home to School Attended
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Geographic Isolation Rule 2 + Read more ...

Check the information supplied on the Claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (SY040) form, or via evidence supplied with the claim via the AIC Marginal Distance Confirmation (SY069) form. The Resources page contains verification requirements on when to request this form be completed (that is, what distances are marginal distances that need checking).

Is the principal family home at least 16 km from the home to the nearest appropriate state school and distance from the home to transport is at least 4.5 km (or no public/school transport is available)?

Yes, the student is geographically isolated under Rule 2. Code the following fields on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:

  • Distance from Home to the Nearest Approp Govt School
  • Distance from Home to School Attended
  • Distance to Transport
  • Distance travelled via t'port
  • Public t'port available
  • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Geographic Isolation Rule 3 adverse travel conditions or other circumstances + Read more ...

If adverse travel conditions or other circumstances beyond the family's control prevent the student from attending the appropriate state school for at least 20 school days a year, the student may be isolated under Geographic Isolation Rule 3.

A student does not have reasonable access to an appropriate state school if, for at least 20 school days in a year they either:

  • cannot get to school
  • the travel time for the return journey is at least three hours, or
  • the route the student must travel equals or exceeds the distances set down in Rule 1 and Rule 2

Check the evidence supplied to support the claim. If none supplied, it will need to be requested before making an assessment. The Resources page contains the verification requirements for details of evidence needed.

Note: appropriate evidence will be required for applicants that are applying under impassable roads. Impassable roads evidence must show that the road was impassable for at least 20 school days in accordance with AIC Scheme Guidelines 4.2.3.7. The References page contains a link to the AIC Scheme Guidelines.

Does the evidence show the road was impassable for at least 20 school days, or the return travel time exceeds 3 hours?

  • Yes, code the following fields on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
    • Other Reason Isolated: IMP for Impassable Roads or code TIM for excess travel time
    • the number of days in Days per year isolated (or just code 20 days for excessive travel time)
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, code the following field on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
    • Other Reason Isolated: NAP
    • Go to Step 8

5

Parental work requires frequent moves + Read more ...

The applicant has applied because the work/livelihood of a parent of the student requires frequent moves. A claim of this type of claim must be supported with the following evidence:

  • a statement of the family's projected itinerary for the school year for which the AIC allowance is sought
  • a statement of the family's projected itinerary (including locations and dates employed at those locations) over the previous 12 months
  • verification of the family's past and proposed movements, in the form of employers' statements or similar evidence

The Resources page contains verification requirements for details of evidence needed. Note: the evidence supplied by the applicant must show that this travel is a necessary part of earning their livelihood and not merely a preference. Evidence is required to show that their particular trade or skills directly cause their itinerancy.

Does the evidence supplied meet the above bullet points?

  • Yes, the student is deemed isolated and can be paid under AIC Scheme Guidelines 4.4.2.
    The References page contains a link to the AIC Scheme Guidelines.
    Code the following fields on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
    • Nearest Approp Govt School: use the school code of the school the student is attending
    • Deemed isolation: ITN
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, code the following fields on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
    • Nearest Approp Govt School: use the school code of the school the student is attending
    • Deemed isolation: NIT
    • Go to Step 8

Note: Distance from Home is not required when coding EIID with 'ITN' or 'NIT'.

6

Second family home with an eligible sibling + Read more ...

A student may qualify as a Second Home Allowance sibling if they:

  • receive YA or ABSTUDY Living Allowance, and
    • was qualified for AIC in a previous year or earlier in the current year, or
    • has a special need
  • qualify for the Second Home Allowance (under an isolation condition other than this one)

To check if the sibling currently qualifies for AIC under any of the above dot points, find the applicant record and check the Links Summary (LS) screen. Check for a sibling with a Link Type of EIC. Select the EIC sibling and navigate to the EIID screen.

Does the student live in a second family home with an eligible sibling?

  • Yes, the student is deemed isolated and can be paid under AIC Scheme Guidelines 4.4.3. The References page contains a link to the AIC Scheme Guidelines. Code the following in the Deemed isolation field on the EIID screen:
    • SHS (Lives in SH with a SHA sibling), or
    • SHB if the sibling is now on YA/ABSTUDY(ABY), and was either qualified for AIC in the current or previous year, or currently has a special need
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, the student is not deemed isolated. Code on the EIID screen:
    • Deemed isolation: NSO
    • Go to Step 8

7

Retrospective continuity of schooling concession + Read more ...

The retrospective continuity of schooling concession is for students who are not isolated at the start of the year, but have commenced board, are living in a second home or are undertaking study by distance education or other approved education in anticipation of a change of circumstances that make them eligible in the future.

Note: an applicant cannot be paid AIC until the proposed isolation condition is met.

Does the retrospective continuity of schooling concession apply to the student?

  • Yes, and the claim was lodged:
    • before the anticipated change of circumstances, set a review for the date the applicant has indicated that the family will become isolated. The Resources page contains verification requirements for details of evidence needed. Send a Q999 letter to the applicant to request
    • after anticipated change of circumstances, contact the applicant and confirm the living arrangements have occurred. Code the claim as normal. Backdate the claim to the appropriate date on the EIC Claim Details (EICD) screen. Include in the DOC that the applicant is eligible from the earlier determined date as per AIC Scheme Guidelines 4.4.5.2. The References page contains a link to the AIC Scheme Guidelines
  • No, go to Step 8

8

The student is not considered isolated + Read more ...

See Rejecting an Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme claim.

Determining and coding special needs for AIC

Table 2: This table describes how to determine if a student has a disability, other health related condition or special education need, which requires a special program or special facilities that cannot be catered for at a local school, or in the vicinity of the home.

Step

Action

1

Special needs + Read more ...

As indicated on the Claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (SY040) form, the applicant is applying because the student has a disability, other health-related condition or special need. Evidence will be required to support their claim, unless the student is attending a special school or living in a special institution.

Is the student attending a special school or living in a special institution?

  • Yes, evidence is not required. Code the following fields on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
    • Distance from Home to the Nearest Approp Govt School (to the special school/institution in this case)
    • Distance from Home to School Attended
    • Disability/health cond: Y
    • Special Needs Reason: SPS (if special school)
    • Deemed Isolation: DIN (if special institution)
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, evidence will be required. Go to Step 2

2

Special needs evidence + Read more ...

The Assistance for Isolated Children Medical Statement - Student Special Needs (SY099) form. This requires completion when an applicant is applying under the following special needs. The Resources page contains verification requirements for details of evidence needed.

If the student needs to:

  • study from home, go to Step 3
  • access a special facility or a special environment, go to Step 4
  • be removed from a local school environment, go to Step 5

If the applicant is applying under student:

  • needs testing or support for a learning disability, go to Step 6
  • needs learning support, go to Step 7
  • would suffer serious educational disadvantage at local school, go to Step 8

3

Student needs to study from home + Read more ...

Check the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme Guidelines 4.3.5.3, Student needs to study from home. The References page contains a link to the AIC Scheme Guidelines.

The Resources page contains verification requirements for details of evidence needed.

Does the advice of the medical practitioner or specialist on the SY099 support the claim that the student needs to study from home?

  • Yes, code the following fields on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
    • Distance from Home to the Nearest Approp Govt School: 99
    • Distance from Home to School Attended: 99
    • Disability/health cond: Y
    • Special Needs Reason: SPH if the student's health condition is not long term or permanent (this code ensures the applicant is requested to provide renewed evidence when they reapply for AIC for the student), or SPT if the student's health condition is long term or permanent (this code ensures the applicant is not required to provide renewed evidence when they reapply for AIC for the student)
  • No, or the SY099 has not been provided, go to Step 9

4

Student needs to access a special facility or a special environment + Read more ...

Check the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme Guidelines 4.3.5.2, Student needs access a special facility or a special environment. The References page contains a link to the AIC Scheme Guidelines.

The Resources page contains verification requirements for details of evidence needed.

Does the advice of the medical practitioner or specialist on the SY099 support the claim that the student needs access a special facility or a special environment?

  • Yes:
    • If a letter has also been received from the school that offers the special facilities/environment that outlines how the school will aid the management of the condition, code the following fields on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
      Distance from Home to the Nearest Approp Govt School (this will be the distance from home to the school that offers the special facilities or environment)
      Distance from Home to School Attended
      Disability/health cond: Y
      Special Needs Reason: SPF if the student's health condition is not long term or permanent (this code ensures the applicant is requested to provide renewed evidence when they reapply for AIC for the student) or SPT if the student's health condition is long term or permanent (this code ensures the applicant is not required to provide renewed evidence when they reapply for AIC for the student)
    • If a letter has not been received, request that the letter be supplied by the applicant via a Q999 letter
  • No, go to Step 9

5

Student needs to be removed from a local school environment + Read more ...

Check the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme Guidelines 4.3.5.2, Student needs to be removed from a local school environment. The References page contains a link to the AIC Scheme Guidelines.

The Resources page contains verification requirements for details of evidence needed.

If the claim is due to a disability or other health-related condition, does the advice of the medical practitioner or specialist on the SY099 support the claim that the student needs to be removed from a local school environment?

  • Yes:
    • If an appropriate letter also been received from the delegate of the Chief Executive of the state or territory government education authority supporting the removal of the student and the reason(s) for this, code the following fields on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
      Distance from Home to the Nearest Approp Govt School: (code 56 kms)
      Distance from home to School Attended
      Disability/health cond: Y
      Special Needs Reason: SPL if the claim was on the basis of a disability or health-related condition, and the student's disability or health condition is not long term or permanent (this code ensures the applicant is requested to provide renewed evidence when they reapply for AIC for the student) or SPT if the claim was on the basis of a disability or health-related condition, and the student's disability or health condition is long term or permanent (this code ensures the applicant is not requested to provide renewed evidence when they reapply for AIC for the student)
    • If a letter has not been received, request that the letter be supplied by the applicant via a Q999 letter
  • No, go to Step 9

6

Student needs testing or support for a learning disability + Read more ...

Check the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme Guidelines 4.3.5.5, Student needs testing or support for a learning disability. The References page contains a link to the AIC Scheme Guidelines.

The Resources page contains verification requirements for details of evidence needed.

Has an appropriate approval letter been received from the relevant state or territory government education authority and confirmation of the testing being provided from the educational institution?

  • Yes, code the following fields on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
    • Distance from Home to the nearest Approp Govt School: (this will be the distance from home to the school that offers the testing or support)
    • Distance from Home to School Attended
    • Disability/health cond: Y
    • Special Needs Reason: SPR
  • No, request that the letter be supplied by the applicant via a Q999 letter

7

Student needs learning support + Read more ...

Check the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme Guidelines 4.3.5.6, Student needs learning support. The References page contains a link to the AIC Scheme Guidelines.

The Resources page contains verification requirements for details of evidence needed.

Has an appropriate letter also been received from the regional office of the state or territory government education authority (or approved specialist assessment service) and confirmation of the learning support being provided from the educational institution?

  • Yes, code the following fields on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
    • Distance from Home to the Nearest Approp Govt School: (this will be the distance from home to the school that offers the support)
    • Distance from Home to School Attended
    • Disability/health cond: Y
    • Special Needs Reason: SPR
  • No, request that the letter be supplied by the applicant via a Q999 letter

8

Student would suffer serious educational disadvantage at local school + Read more ...

Check the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme Guidelines 4.3.5.7, Student would suffer serious educational disadvantage at local school. The References page contains a link to the AIC Scheme Guidelines.

The Resources page contains verification requirements for details of evidence needed.

Has an appropriate letter been received from the delegate of the chief executive of the state or territory government education authority?

  • Yes, code the following on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:
    • Event date: this is the date the letter supplied by the relevant education authority was signed
    • Distance from Home to the Nearest Approp Govt School: (this will be the distance from home to the school the student is attending)
    • Distance from home to School Attended
    • Disability/health cond: Y
    • Special Needs Reason: SED
  • No, request that the letter be supplied by the applicant via a Q999 letter

9

The student has no special needs + Read more ...

Although the applicant is claiming their student is eligible because of special needs, they do not meet that criteria or evidence supplied does not verify the claim.

Code the following on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen:

  • Distance from Home to the Nearest Approp Govt School
  • Distance from Home to School Attended
  • Disability/health cond: Y
  • Special Needs Reason: NSN

See Rejecting an Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme claim.
