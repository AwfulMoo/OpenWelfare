Assessing if activity requires action

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Check the Participation Summary Screen + Read more ...

Where a JSR Action Item exists:

  • and is indicating a provider appointment needs to be booked, go to Online Diary and check for the message TDI/UPD has been completed. Where this occurs there is No Further Action Required and the Job Seeker Registration (JSR) activity will have completed

Where no JSR Action Item exists:

  • return to the Activity List (AL) screen and if the JSR activity is now completed, there is No Further Action Required

In all other circumstances, the activity requires action. Go to Table 2.

Contacting job seekers to action JSR/ADD, JSR/UPD, JSR/MFU and WAP/MFU activities

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

First outbound attempt to contact job seeker + Read more ...

When it is necessary to contact a job seeker, 2 reasonable and genuine attempts to contact must be made.

If a JSR 1st attempt to contact Fast Note has already been recorded, go to Step 2.

If the contact number recorded on the job seeker's record is a mobile number, Service Officers should create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). After creating the SMS, wait 5 minutes before making the outbound attempt.

Was the contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No:
    • hold the work item to the next calendar day. Record sufficient detail regarding the outbound contact attempt using the JSR 1st attempt to contact Fast Note so subsequent Service Officers can action the second attempt and finalise the activity. Ensure the 'Display on access' box is selected and when prompted check that the Reason field displays 'OTH-Urgent free text message exists'. The Expiry Date field should default to the maximum date allowed (60 days from today). Procedure ends here
    • if the telephone details are not recorded, incorrect or outdated, go to Step 3

2

Second outbound attempt to contact job seeker + Read more ...

Make a second genuine attempt to contact the customer if the first attempt was unsuccessful.

If the contact number, recorded on the job seeker's record, is a mobile number, Service Officers should create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). Following creation of the SMS, wait 5 minutes before making the outbound attempt.

Was the contact successful?

  • Yes, see go to Step 5
  • No:
    • close the JSR 1st attempt to contact Display on Access (DOA) DOC
    • record details of the unsuccessful contact using the JSR 2nd attempt to contact Fast Note, ensuring the 'Display on access' box is selected. When prompted check that the Reason field displays 'OTH-Urgent free text message exists'. The Expiry Date field should default to the maximum date allowed (60 days from today)
    • go to Step 3

3

Issue a Q164 letter after second unsuccessful contact attempt or where no contact details recorded for job seeker + Read more ...

Issue a Q164 letter asking the customer to contact Services Australia by phone within 14 days:

  • after a second unsuccessful outbound contact attempt, or
  • where there are no contact details or incorrect details are recorded (for example, the job seeker's phone number is not registered)

The customer has 14 days to respond after they receive the letter. Add days to allow extra time for mail delivery. Hold the work item until the day after the allowable time has expired.

If the job seeker:

  • contacts because of the suspension, restore payment and action outstanding activities. Go to Step 5
  • fails to contact, go to Step 4

4

Job seeker fails to respond to the Q164 letter + Read more ...

If the job seeker fails to contact by the notification provision date +1 after the Q164 is issued, Service Officers must determine if suspension action can be taken. Before suspending a customer's payment, see Suspension of payments (CLK) for information about factors to consider.

If it is appropriate to manually suspend a customer for Failing to Respond to Correspondence (SUS-FRC):

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen:
    • Key the payment type as the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn field
    • Key the action to be taken in the Action field
    • Key the suspension reason 'FRC' in the Reason field
    • Press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or key the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date field
    • Complete the Source field and the date of receipt in the DOR field
  • Include the following details on a DOC (where relevant):
    • Decision made, payment type and the Act/Guide reference the decision was based on, see Creating documents (Fast Note, Callbacks and ACTDOCs) and Online Document Recording (ODR)
    • Date of effect of the decision
    • Reasons for the decision, including why the person was not qualified or why the payment is not payable, for decisions made under section 80
    • Follow-up action required on contact with the customer
    • If contact with the assessing Service Officer is required before restoration can be done or whether Smart Centre Telephony staff may restore the payment
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity

Where the job seeker's payment has:

  • been suspended for failing to contact:
    • no further action is required by Services Australia
    • hold the work item for 13 weeks from the suspension date. Do not cancel the work item
  • not been suspended as it not appropriate to suspend the job seeker's payment for failing to contact:
    • undertake job seeker contact, and
    • hold the work item as per the Q164 process. Do not cancel the work item

5

Finalise job seeker management JSR/ADD, JSR/UPD and JSR/MFU and WAP/MFU activities + Read more ...

On successful contact with the customer, navigate to the Participation Summary screen to view and action all outstanding job seeker management activities:

  • Check the job seeker's current circumstances to determine whether they need to be connected with a provider or have approved activity or exemption coded. If the job seeker:
    • has an expired approved activity on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen, but are still participating in an approved activity, renew the activity in the job seeker's Job Plan and extend the activity on the AEX screen. See Negotiating Job Plans
    • has a WAP/MFU with keyword 'WAPDHSM' assess whether job seeker can be coded as meeting mutual obligation requirements via one of the following employment related approved activities PTW, SEL, PWS, SSE, SPW, and VPE on AEX screen and update Job Plan if eligible. Note: if job seeker is a Disability Employment Services (DES) job seeker only update AEX screen. See Approved Activities for Job Plans
    • remains eligible for a mutual obligation requirements exemption, see Mutual obligation requirements exemptions
    • qualifies for a deferred referral or extension, go to Deferred referral to Employment Services Providers. Record deferred referrals for customers serving a waiting period who choose not to volunteer for employment services
    • no longer qualifies for registration as a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP), inactivate their JSR via the Looking for Work workflow. This auto-completes the JSR/UPD activity
  • Select the Participation Action Item to make appropriate referrals. Selecting the Action item will direct the Service Officer to the appropriate screens/workflows to complete the job seeker activity. See Participation Summary screen for processing guidelines and actioning issues
  • Discuss the job seeker's mutual obligation requirements including requirements to attend any booked interviews and possible consequences if they fail to attend
  • Ensure and check all job seeker management activities are finalised on the Activity List (AL) screen once all actions are complete as listed on the Participation Summary screen
  • Close any JSR attempt to contact Display on Access (DOA) DOCs

Where a job seeker requires an initial or re-engagement appointment:

  • Ensure the appointment is booked with the provider within the next 2 working days (if available)
  • Tell the job seeker of their appointment details verbally or in writing. Ensure the job seeker receives proper notification and reasonable notice of the appointment and that a DOC is recorded to make sure there is a record of contact with the job seeker, and that verbal notification has been given
  • If booking an appointment through Customer First, Service Officers must record a DOC using the Fast Note template Jobseekers > Update > Provider Appointment Booked

Note: only place job seeker management activities on hold where instructed in this procedure. If job seeker management activities do not finalise, only lodge a mySupport Request as outlined in this procedure.

JSR/ADD and JSR/UPD activities

Table 3: This table describes job seeker management activities generated on customer records. These activities need further action and cannot be cancelled.

Step

Action

1

Job Seeker Registration (JSR/ADD) activities + Read more ...

This activity is created when the job seeker is receiving an income support payment with mutual obligation requirements, but does not have a Job Seeker Registration 'Active' (JSR/ACT) status.

The majority of these job seekers are required to be registered as a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP) job seeker. Job seekers in Community Development Program (CDP) regions will require registration as Remote Fully Eligible (RFE).

Once the job seeker has a JSR/ACT status, the JSR/ADD activity will auto-complete.

Note: certain job seekers can be registered as a No Stream Services Referral Required (NSSRR)/No Remote Referral Required (NRRR) job seeker. For example, principal carers already meeting their requirements who do not want to volunteer for referral to employment services. Complete the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) for these job seekers if they want to access other employment services or if they are NRRR and need an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt).

Special Benefit (SpB) job seekers granted under JobSeeker Payment (JSP) conditions and who have a Temporary Visa (TPV) class, can only be registered as volunteer job seekers.

Do not cancel this activity, unless the corresponding job seeker claim has recently been rejected, in which case it should be cancelled.

2

Job Seeker Registration/Update (JSR/UPD) activities + Read more ...

These activities are created to alert further action is required on a job seeker's record. A To Do Indicator (TDI) may be attached to a JSR/UPD activity and identifies what action is needed. Job Seeker Registration (JSR) activities are displayed on the Activity List (AL) screen. Action outstanding JSR/UPD activities via the Action Item displayed on the Participation Summary screen. Activities are listed in priority order and should be actioned from the highest priority, see Participation Summary screen.

Do not cancel these activities if the job seeker's benefit status line shows they are JSR/ACT or they are registered as a job seeker with the Department of Education, Skills and Employment system. Attempting to cancel the JSR/UPD activity will result in an error.

Complete the relevant workflow to undertake the action appropriate for the job seeker, as indicated by the TDI attached.

If the customer does not require JSR, inactivate their JSR via the Looking for Work workflow. This auto-completes the JSR/UPD activity.

3

JSR/UPD activity with 'JSC' To Do Indicator (TDI) and 'JSCIREQ' keyword + Read more ...

This activity is created when the job seeker is receiving an income support payment with mutual obligation requirements, and has a JSR/ACT status, but has no active JSCI on their record. An Action Item is displayed on the Participation Summary screen showing this requirement.

Once the JSCI workflow is complete, the JSR/UPD activity with 'JSC' TDI will auto-complete, even when the JSCI status is still pending. For example, where an ESAt is triggered.

Note: if the job seeker has a status of commenced/pending/suspended Stream C, or has been referred/commenced in Disability Employment Services (DES), lodge a mySupport Request to cancel the JSR activity. Service Officers cannot update the JSCI.

Stream Services and DES do not apply in CDP regions. The CDP provider in the region will provide all levels of servicing.

4

JSR/UPD activity with 'JSC' TDI and 'FEP' keyword + Read more ...

This activity is created when the job seeker is receiving an income support payment that requires registration as a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP). The Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) must be complete as part of FEP registration. This activity will not appear for job seekers in Community Development Program (CDP) regions.

A job seeker must be registered as a FEP where they are still registered as No Stream Services Referral Required (NSSRR) and not:

  • exempt from mutual obligation requirements, or
  • participating in an approved activity that makes them Services Australia managed

When the Looking for Work workflow is complete, the JSR/UPD activity will complete.

Note: if the job seeker's approved activity has expired on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen, but they are still participating in the activity, they should not be registered as FEP and referred to an Employment Services Provider. Renew the activity in the job seeker's Job Plan and extended the activity on the AEX screen, following the relevant procedure for approval of that activity, for example, part-time work. For job seekers managed by Services Australia, see Negotiating Job Plans.

5

JSR/UPD activity with 'ESA' TDI and 'ESAREFRQ' keyword + Read more ...

This activity is created when a Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) has been run and the job seeker's responses identifies the need for an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) or Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) referral.

The requirement for an ESAt/JCA may also be withdrawn in some circumstances.

To copy an ESAt/JCA or book an appointment, see Actioning a pending Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) initiated Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) activity.

6

JSR/UPD activity with 'ESA' TDI and 'ESATATT' keyword + Read more ...

This activity is created for all job seekers with mutual obligation requirements whose payments are suspended because they failed to attend an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt)/Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) appointment. Payment is suspended with reason:

  • ES1 (failed to attend first cycle ESAt appointment), or
  • ES2 (failed to attend second cycle ESAt appointment)

This activity acts as a flag on the job seeker's record and does not require immediate action. It should not be cancelled, but may be held for a maximum of 13 weeks from date of suspension pending contact from the job seeker.

No action is required until the job seeker contacts.

Once the job seeker contacts Services Australia, action is required via the ESAt Attendance workflow to arrange a new ESAt appointment.

7

JSR/UPD activity with 'ESP' TDI + Read more ...

This activity is created when the job seeker has an active JSR and Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI), but has not been connected with an Employment Services Provider (provider). An Action Item is displayed on the Participation Summary screen. This activity will not appear for job seekers in Community Development Program (CDP) regions.

See Step 5 in Table 2 for action required.

Once an initial appointment with a provider is booked for the job seeker, the activity will complete.

8

Job Seeker JSR/UPD activity with 'ESP' TDI and 'NSUS' keyword + Read more ...

This work item activity ZIAM_JSCI_ESP - ESP Suspension Ended is created to alert Service Officers re-engagement with an Employment Services Provider is required after a Stream Service suspension period has expired. The creation of this activity is dependent on the Stream Service suspension 'Expected End Date' that is advised by the Department of Education, Skills and Employment system.

  • Select the activity on the Activity List (AL) screen and either:
    • 'V'iew activity details on Activity History (AY) screen on the select box on the AL screen, or
    • Select the activity and then put AY in the Next field
  • The AY screen will provide any additional notes and keywords
  • Go to Workspace, then Participation Summary and action the outstanding activity on this page

The Participation Summary screen Activity Type/Exemption Information section provides information on the types of activity and exemptions the job seeker is undertaking. This activity will not appear for job seekers in Community Development Program (CDP) regions.

Note: if the job seeker remains eligible for an alternative activity type/exemption the job seeker's current circumstances must be reviewed and the appropriate process actioned. See Negotiating Job Plans for Services Australia managed job seekers and Mutual obligation requirements exemptions. Finalise any remaining Action Items on the Participation Summary screen.

9

JSR/UPD activity with 'ESA' TDI with 'D26' parameter and 'ESAREFRQ' keyword + Read more ...

This activity is created when a principal carer whose special family circumstances - Family and Domestic Violence (D26) exemption has expired and they:

  • are placed in Stream A or B, or
  • live in a Community Development Program (CDP) region and are Remote Fully Eligible (RFE), or
  • do not have a current deferred Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) referral

The activity will not be created if the job seeker has had an ESAt referral since the exemption end date or an unfinished First Contact Concessions, Carers and Seniors (FCCCS) workflow interview.

Action required:

  • refer the principal carer to an ESAt if they are not eligible for a further exemption
  • go to the Online Diary to book an ESAt appointment using the 'VLNTR' (Volunteer) referral reason. Note: in all circumstances where a customer has experienced family and domestic violence, see Family and domestic violence to identify and offer referrals and consider a social worker referral

10

JSR/UPD with 'RJC' TDI with no parameter + Read more ...

This activity will be created when a job seeker in a Community Development Program (CDP) region requires a referral to a CDP provider. Refer the job seeker to a CDP provider if they meet all of the following:

  • are Remote Fully Eligible (RFE)
  • not linked to an Employment Services Provider, CDP or Disability Employment Services (DES)
  • do not have a deferred referral, and
  • do not have a current exemption from mutual obligation requirements

An Action Item is displayed on the Participation Summary screen about this requirement.

11

JSR/UPD with 'TW5' TDI with no parameter + Read more ...

This activity is created when a job seeker is eligible for a referral to a Transition to Work (TtW) provider and a TtW provider is available in the job seeker's region.

Refer the job seeker to a TtW provider if they meet all of the following:

  • are TtW eligible as determined by the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI)
  • 15 - 24 years of age
  • are an early school leaver or disengaged young person
  • meet residence or visa requirements, and
  • meet the eligibility requirements as determined by the Department of Education, Skills and Employment ICT system

An Action Item is displayed on the Participation Summary screen about this requirement.

JSR/MFU and WAP/MFU activities

Table 4: This table describes Job Seeker Registration/Manual Follow-up (JSR/MFU) and Working Age Participation (WAP/MFU) with 'WAPDSHM' keyword activities that can be completed manually or cancelled (if appropriate).

Activity

Action

1

JSR/MFU with 'AMEP/SEE' keyword + Read more ...

This activity will be created when a job seeker has been identified as benefitting from a referral to an Adult Migrant English Programme (AMEP) or Skills for Education and Employment (SEE) when the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) workflow is run, and the Service Officer confirms that the referral is required. Note: ensure the job seeker meets the eligibility criteria before referring them.

Ensure the job seeker has first tested their eligibility for AMEP before considering referral to SEE.

If the job seeker is a newly arrived migrant, over 18 years of age and does not have functional English, advise them to contact their local AMEP provider.

Record the advice given to the job seeker on a DOC.

For job seekers who are:

  • eligible for SEE and require a referral. Go to Referrals and proceed with the Skills for Education and Employment on-line Eligibility Checklist. If the job seeker was born overseas, check visa and residency details on the Legal Residence (RSLEG) and the Residence Results Display (RSRD) screens before proceeding
  • not interested in proceeding with the referral to distance learning, cancel the MFU. Record a DOC stating the reason why the MFU has been cancelled
  • not eligible for a SEE referral, cancel the MFU. Record a DOC stating the reason why the MFU has been cancelled

2

JSR/MFU with 'DES' keyword + Read more ...

This activity is created to alert that a job seeker receiving an income support payment with mutual obligation requirements requires a referral to a Disability Employment Services (DES) provider when all the following are met:

  • the Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) or Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) report has been accepted and finalised
  • are not residing in a Community Development Program (CDP) region
  • the Assessor's recommendation is for a referral to a DES provider, and
  • the job seeker has not yet been referred to or connected to the recommended DES provider

Use the Online Diary to refer the job seeker to a DES provider.

3

JSR/MFU with 'ADE' keyword + Read more ...

This activity is to alert that a customer with mutual obligation requirements may benefit from a referral to an Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE) Program where the Employment Services Assessment (ESAt)/Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) report recommends a referral.

See the Referring a customer to ADE table of Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE) referrals, for processing instructions on ADE referrals.

4

WAP/MFU with 'WAPDHSM' keyword + Read more ...

This activity is created to prompt Service Officers to update the customer's Job Plan where a customer may be eligible for Part-time Work (PTW), Self-Employment (SEL), Paid Work and Self Employment (Combined) (PWS), Study and Self Employment (Combined) (SSE), Study and Paid Work (Combined) (SPW) or Voluntary and Paid Work (Combined) (VPE) but does not currently have one of those Approved Activities recorded.

If the job seeker is a DES job seeker only the PTW should be coded on the AEX screen as the DES provider will be required to update the Job Plan.

See Approved Activities for Job Plans for more information.
