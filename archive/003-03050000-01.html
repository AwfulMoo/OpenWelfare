All other DRA and NZ DRA claims that do not meet the eligibility criteria can be rejected. See the Process page

This document explains how to record and code Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) and DRA for New Zealand non-protected Special Category (subclass 444) visa holders (NZ DRA) claims. NZ DRA was known as ex-gratia DRA.

Staff not trained for DRA and NZ DRA

Before processing DRA or NZ DRA claims, staff must complete technical training. Line managers will let Service Officers know about training requirements.

If not trained in taking DRA or NZ DRA claims, see Claiming emergency payments.

Processing claims

Fully assess the customer's eligibility before coding the claim.

It will depend on the claim type (DRA or NZ DRA) and if an event has the online claim/Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) channel activated as to where the payment will be processed.

For DRA events activated with:

  • an online claim/ACC, process using Process Direct
  • no online claim/ACC, process using the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow in Customer First

For all NZ DRA events, the way claims are received (online/ACC or verbal claim template) is outlined in the event specific file. All claims are processed using the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow in Customer First.

Only customers who hold a non-protected 444 Visa can be paid NZ DRA. Residency screens must be checked, as customers may not always provide accurate residency details in their claim responses. For more details about the residence rules and check the status, see Table 1 in Australia Residence Rules for New Zealand citizens.

For claims processed in Process Direct and Customer First, payments are always made using the FSP (Flexible Support Payment) subsystem of ISS.

Documenting standards and Fast Notes

This information only applies if the online claim/ACC is not available for the event.

Record all relevant information as per the Online Document Recording (ODR) standards.

For verbal claims, staff must document the discussion with the customer using the DRA/NZ DRA verbal claim template.

The following Fast Notes are in Customer First:

  • DRA/NZ DRA verbal claim for copying the completed DRA/NZ DRA verbal claim template. This becomes the DRA/NZ DRA verbal claim DOC
  • DRA/NZ DRA Granted for successful claims
  • DRA/NZ DRA Rejected for unsuccessful claims
  • Fast Note to record a partner's claim for the event when the partner has other workflows blocking the customer’s DRA or NZ DRA claim
    • EVENT NAME DRA CLM
    • EVENT NAME NZ DRA CLM

Rejecting claims

Only reject unsuccessful claims once there is formal approval to reject claims for the event.

Approval is given by staff communication channels and is published in Current emergency management events.

LGA not declared

Do not reject a claim if all of the following apply:

  • approval has not been given to reject claims
  • the customer is claiming for a Local Government Area (LGA) that has not been declared
  • the customer meets all other eligibility criteria and would receive a payment if their LGA was declared

Payment methods

Customers will receive payments in:

  • their nominated Australian bank account, or
  • their nominee's Australian bank account if they have a payment nominee

If updating a bank account for a current customer, make sure the customer can provide their previous bank account details.

Payments for DRA and NZ DRA are by direct credit and will deliver overnight into the customer's nominated bank account.

Customers with nominee arrangements

A customer may need a third party (a person or organisation) to act on their behalf when claiming DRA or NZ DRA. See Appointing nominees and Obligations of nominees.

A nominee’s identity must be established before an Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf (SS313) form is processed.

If there are concerns about a nominee’s identity or claim information provided by a nominee, submit a Fast Fraud tip-off form.

Claims

  • can be lodged by a correspondence nominee
  • cannot be lodged by a:
    • Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) or Update (PPU) authority or
    • payment nominee

Only a payment nominee can get a payment on a customer’s behalf.

Customers claiming DRA/NZ DRA who have a Payment Nominee arrangement, do not need their claim to be processed in the !FFAP system. Process these claims following standard procedure.

Quality On Line

QOL standards for all events where claims are processed in Customer First is standard. Staff will be notified of any changes for specific events.

Desktop messaging

The DEMC service is available for all emergency payments, including DRA and NZ DRA. Use this service to send an SMS to customers to improve the chances of contacting them.

After claims have been granted

See Managing DRA and NZ DRA after grant for:

  • Coding earnings
  • Actioning POI requirements once DRA or NZ DRA is granted with an exemption
  • Actioning TFN requirements once DRA is granted with an exemption
  • 11 week transition review
  • Transferring to another payment
  • Cancellation of DRA or NZ DRA
  • Restoring DRA or NZ DRA
  • Voluntary surrender
  • If DRA or NZ DRA should not have been paid to a customer

Preventing fraud

For more details, see Emergency payments - preventing fraud.

If staff need help to finalise a claim, they must not call Technical Peer Support unless a customer is on the phone. See Resources for details.
