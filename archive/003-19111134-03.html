This page contains information on assessing claims for Emergency payments from customers affected by the Eastern Queensland Bushfires (Nov 2019). Emergency payments include Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP), Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) and their associated ex-gratia payments.

Additional information for AGDRP/ex-gratia DRP only

To assess major damage from smoke and/or ash for the Eastern Queensland Bushfires (Nov 2019) a person’s principal place of residence must be reasonably close to the major disaster. The following questions will assist with the assessment of ‘reasonably close’:

  • How far away was your home from the bushfire?
  • Did you have to evacuate?
  • Did the bushfires impact your suburb or the suburb immediately next to your suburb?
  • Was the bushfire within five kilometres of your home?
  • Was the bushfire within ten kilometres of your home?

For customers with a home outside the 10km radius the following questions may help to determine if any major damage was a direct result of the designated bushfire:

  • Your home is quite a large distance away from the fire itself. Were there specific circumstances that led to the interior of your home experiencing smoke and/or ash damage as a direct result of this major disaster?
  • Have you claimed insurance for the damage and been issued with a claim number?
  • How would you describe the circumstances that led to smoke and/or ash damage to the interior of your home?

See the Resources page for:

  • a link to live maps that will assist in determining if the principal place of residence is ‘reasonably close’ to the bushfires
  • how to use Google maps to further assist in determining if the principal place of residence is ‘reasonably close’ to the bushfires
  • listing of suburbs within the declared local government areas. Note: Office Locator is not to be used

Where the customer's principal place of residence is outside the 10km burn scar radius, Service Officers can escalate the claim to the Emergency Management Technical Peer Support once the following have been completed and the investigation is documented on the customer's record:

  • All other eligibility criteria has been assessed as met
  • Major damage criteria has been established, the customer has clearly demonstrated how they have met the criteria
  • If there are doubts about the validity of the customer’s claim, ask for evidence before escalating to the Emergency Management Technical Peer Support

If the customer’s principal place of residence is more than 15km from the closest burn scar:

  • ask for evidence to support the major damage assessment
  • once evidence has been provided, refer to the Emergency Management Technical Peer Support for escalation to Emergency Management Level 2 Helpdesk

Step

Action

Disaster determined and basic eligibility + Read more ...

A determination has been made that a major disaster has occurred for the purposes of AGDRP/ex-gratia DRP for the Eastern Queensland Bushfires (Nov 2019).

2

Assess eligibility for AGDRP/ex-gratia DRP + Read more ...

See Eligibility for AGDRP and NZ DRP

For the Eastern Queensland Bushfires (Nov 2019) the assessment of major damage from smoke and/or ash has been updated. The following questions may be useful to determine if major damage from smoke and/or ash has occurred:

  • Please describe the damage to your home.
  • Is the interior or exterior damaged? Note: exterior damage is not eligible
  • Did you have to replace contents?
  • Did you have to repair interior damage? Note: soft furnishings or electrical appliances must need repairing or replacing. These do not need to have already been replaced or repaired for the customer to meet the major damage criteria
  • Could the smoke/ash damage be cleaned?
  • How could you demonstrate the damage occurred? E.g. could you provide a photo or invoice? Note: this information can be forwarded to the agency through a customer’s online account via the upload document service
  • Where was the major asset located on the property?
  • Can the major asset be repaired?
  • Did you own any major assets on your property that were damaged or destroyed? Please describe the damage? Note: a customer must own a major asset or assets and the major asset or assets must be located at the customer’s principal place of residence
  • What do you think is the market value of these assets that were damaged or destroyed?

Major damage does not include cleaning an item or surface from smoke, soot, char or ash.

For the elements to consider when assessing major damage from smoke and/or ash, see definitions and scenarios in Eligibility for AGDRP and NZ DRP.

Note: for ex-gratia DRP a customer must have participated in the Australian taxation system for at least 1 year in the following past 3 financial years 2016-17, 2017-18, 2018-19 or will do so before 12 August 2021.

3

Code claim + Read more ...

See Processing AGDRP and NZ DRP claims in Customer First.

If a customer is not eligible for AGDRP/NZ DRP, claims are still to be recorded and the outcome noted as rejected.

Claims cannot be rejected until formal approval has been provided. Staff will be advised once they are able to reject payments.

Claim should be held with the relevant keyword if formal approval has not been provided.
