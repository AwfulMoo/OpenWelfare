Online task

All carers must be encouraged to submit the CTAT online when possible. The carer is to complete the question set rather than completing a form. The Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance Medical Report - For a person - 16 years or over (SA332A) can be uploaded within the CTAT task.

Carer has not started CTAT + Read more ...

Ask carer to start and submit CTAT online.

If the carer does not have an online account, encourage them to create a myGov account and link to their Centrelink online account.

  • myGov Inbox  > open message > select  CTAT link, or
  • Centrelink online account  > select  Outstanding Tasks  or  Menu  >  Payments and Claims  >  Update circumstances  >  Child to Adult Transfer

5

Carer unable to submit CTAT online + Read more ...

If a carer is unable or unsuitable to submit the CTAT online, they must lodge 2 forms by the due date:

  • Child to adult transfer for Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance (SA432) and
  • Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance Medical Report for a person 16 years or over (SA332A)

Encourage the carer to download forms from Services Australia website. The Resources page contains a link to the forms.

Confirm carer's address and issue SA432 and SA332A forms using Mail Forms Guided Procedure. Create a DOC to record the issuing of forms.

Provide carer with SA432 and SA332A forms. Create a DOC to record the issuing of forms.

Encourage carer to use online services to upload forms, when completed.

Does customer have further questions about the CTAT forms or need help lodging or uploading medical reports?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, remind the carer of the Review Completion Due Date or Payment Guarantee Due Date

Procedure ends here.

5

Temporary absence + Read more ...

Code temporary absence details.

If the care receiver is in a period of temporary absence at the adult transition determination date, eligibility is not impacted. Exceptions to this for CP are if the respite absence quota will be exceeded:

  • before care receiver's 16th birthday (or 18th under terminal care provisions). The payment will cancel when the respite exceeds the quota. The carer will receive a notification letter
  • after care receiver's 16th birthday but before 28 days + Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) from the system date. The transition to adult payment will be granted

There will be a prompt to contact the carer if the child is future assessed but the respite absence quota will exceed:

  • before care receiver's 16th birthday (or 18th birthday under terminal care provisions), and
  • this date is greater than 28 days + EPED from the system date

See Calculating the Adult Disability Assessment Tool (ADAT) score

  • THP score reaches the required level, and

Continue to code all other information for the CTAT, even if the care receiver does not meet the THP or total (ADAT) scores.

9

Date of effect - Payment Guarantee + Read more ...

Check that the correct date of effect has been applied to the customer’s CTAT outcome.

If the carer has been assessed under the Payment Guarantee and:

  • CP and/or CA will continue under adult qualifications, the date of effect for the decision is the later of the date the child turned 16 or the date the CTAT is processed
  • CP and/or CA is cancelled the date of effect for the decision is the date the CTAT is processed

An overpayment will not be created if a carer’s payment has continued under Payment Guarantee and CP and/or CA will not continue under adult qualifications.

If the carer has not been assessed under the Payment Guarantee the date of effect for the decision (for both continuing or cancelling CP and/or CA) is the later date of when:

  • child turns 16, or
  • the CTAT is processed

For carers in receipt of CA HCC only, the HCC will remain valid until the child turns 16 years and 3 months or 18 years if terminal.

10

Conduct Service Offer Interview (SOI) + Read more ...

Before finalising the CTAT, conduct a Service Offer Interview (SOI) for all CTAT outcomes.

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the carer and/or nominee.

Unsuccessful contact attempt

  • If the first contact attempt is unsuccessful but the phone numbers are still current, the Work Item is to be 'Held to User' for a second attempt on the next business day. Record the attempted contact and CTAT outcome details in a Note or DOC on the carer's record.
  • If unsuccessful in attempt to contact, go to Step 11

Successful contact with carer/nominee

Discuss the following with the carer:

  • the outcome of the CTAT (continuation of payment/s under adult provisions or cancellation)
  • date the outcome will take effect from (from CJAR and/or CJAA)
  • CA HCC cancels when CA stops or transitions to adult provisions
  • options for the child to claim:
    • the Ex-Carer Allowance HCC if they are a full time student or a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC)
    • a payment in their own right
  • option to become a nominee for their child, to support them to claim any payments and/or concessions and engage with the agency
  • if the child will be making a claim for a payment or card in their own right, they must complete the Identity Confirmation process

If it is difficult for the child to attend the service centre for Identification Confirmation, consider Alternative Identity provisions. Where appropriate, this can be actioned over the phone.

Additionally, if the child is available to speak with, discuss:

  • creating a myGov account and linking services
  • an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) for Ex-Carer HCC, or offer a warm transfer to the main business line for the ACC to be completed

Note: The CTAT Work Item can be held for 1 week to allow the carer time to purchase any medications using the CA HCC whilst it is still current if:

  • the carer advises they have medications due for their child in the coming days
  • the child has not yet turned 16 years and 3 months

If the carer accepts this, the CTAT Work Item must be 'Held to User' for a maximum of 1 week and a CTAT progress doc created in Notes detailing reason for hold.
