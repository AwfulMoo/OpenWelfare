This page contains information on eligibility and registration for No Stream Services Referral Required (NSSRR)/No Remote Referral Required (NRRR) job seekers.

Eligibility and registration of NSSRR/NRRR job seekers

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Eligibility for NSSRR/NRRR job seeker + Read more ...

NSSRR/NRRR registration is completed as part of the Participation Interview or Looking for Work (LFW) workflow,

Check if the job seeker is eligible for registration as a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP) or Volunteer.

If the job seeker is in a Community Development Program (CDP) region, they will not be eligible for registration as FEP or as a volunteer. The system will determine if the job seeker is Remote Fully Eligible (RFE).

If the job seeker does not meet conditions for registration as a FEP or as a volunteer, NSSRR/NRRR registration is applicable for the following groups:

  • job seekers requiring a Department of Education, Skills and Employment record in order to be referred for an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) or in the case of customers claiming or receiving Disability Support Pension (DSP), referred for a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA)
  • full-time job seekers applying for a supplementary allowance determination
  • full-time students receiving a student payment who are transferring to a job seeker payment, and contact prior to ceasing studies but within four weeks of the Student End Date (SED)
  • the following groups who require a Job Plan but do not choose to volunteer for employment services:
    • principal carers currently meeting their Mutual obligation requirements for principal carers
    • principal carers with long term exemptions from their mutual obligation requirements (greater than 16 weeks)
    • people with a Partial Capacity to Work
    • people who have been assessed as having a capacity of 0-14 hours per week and are considered volunteers for employment services
  • customers in receipt of an income support payment that has no mutual obligation requirements who do not choose to volunteer with employment services and want to apply for ACCS (Transition to Work)
  • New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS) participants presenting to Services Australia after the 39 weeks of participation in the Scheme to claim income support

To proceed with a NSSRR/NRRR registration, go to Step 2.

To review a NSSRR/NRRR registration, go to Step 7.

2

Select appropriate tool for NSSRR/NRRR registration + Read more ...

For customers:

  • who submitted an online claim or a Service Officer has completed the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) workflow, run the Participation Interview
  • currently receiving a payment who require a referral for ESAt, JCA or a Job Plan (including a voluntary Job Plan for voluntary job seekers who want to be referred for ACCS (Transition to Work)), open the Looking for Work workflow

Note: for customers registering as NSSRR/NRRR to access an ESAt/JCA referral or in order to complete a Job Plan registration must be completed prior to the ESAt/JCA referral or Job Plan negotiation.

3

Customer contact + Read more ...

Customer claimed online or via Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) - go to Step 4

Customer contact requires Looking for Work (LFW) to be run - go to Step 5

4

Update Job Seeker Registration screen + Read more ...

Note: if the customer is already an active job seeker and connected with a provider, the registration cannot be changed to NSSRR/NRRR.

The Participation Interview will recognise if the customer is NSSRR/NRRR, however the customer can be assessed for further assistance.

The Participation Interview JSR will present the question 'This customer has been identified as not requiring a referral to Stream Services or Community Development Program (CDP). Do they wish to volunteer for additional assistance?' Select one of the following:

  • No
  • Wishes to be linked to an Employment Services Provider
  • Wishes to be assessed for other assistance (SEE/AMEP)

Note: when the customer is identified as a 'Full Time Student transfer to job seeker within 4 weeks of Student End Date' then the field will be protected and set to 'No'. Conduct the JSCI interview.

Online claim and ACC contact process ends here.

5

Update Job Seeker Registration screen + Read more ...

The Job Seeker Registration screen displays as part of Participation Interview and LFW workflows.

Note: if the customer is already an active job seeker and connected with a provider, the registration cannot be changed to NSSRR/NRRR.

For the question 'Set registration as No Provider Referral Required?' select appropriate reason from the drop down options shown below. Box will pre-populate for DSP new claimants and students contacting within four weeks of the Student End Date.

  • DSP Customer
  • Full-time Student (FTS) within 4 weeks of Student End Date (SED)
  • Principal Carer (PCP) meeting requirements
  • Principal Carer (PCP) with long term exemption
  • Partial Capacity to Work (PCW) meeting requirements
  • PCW 0 - 14 hrs
  • FTS applying for supplementary allowance
  • NEIS participant
  • Special Disability Trust
  • ACCS (Transition to Work)

Complete remaining questions on screen and select Continue.

JSCI Required screen will display.

6

Assessment of requirement for Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) + Read more ...

NSSRR registered job seeker requires a JSCI if the customer wants to access other employment services such as a referral to an Assessor or Disability Employment Services (DES), but no referral to jobactive.

NRRR registered job seeker will only require a JSCI if an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) is required.

If a job seeker does require a JSCI, select Yes option and then select Continue. Conduct the JSCI interview. At the end of the JSCI, advise the job seeker of their Job Seeker Identification Number (JSID).

If a job seeker does not require a JSCI, select No option and then select Continue. Conduct the JSCI interview. At the end of the JSCI, advise the job seeker of their Job Seeker Identification Number (JSID).

Procedure ends here.

7

Review NSSRR/NRRR registration + Read more ...

If a job seeker is no longer eligible for a NSSRR/NRRR registration, such as principal carer not meeting mutual obligation requirements, the NSSRR/NRRR flag must be removed and the job seeker's registration changed to Fully Eligible Participant (FEP)/Remote Fully Eligible (RFE).

  • The NSSRR/NRRR flag will be automatically removed at the SED+1 for those full-time students transferring to a job seeker payment
  • For all other NSSRR/NRRR job seekers, the flag has to be reviewed and removed manually from the Job Seeker Registration screen with the LFW workflow when it is no longer appropriate. When the NSSRR/NRRR registration flag is being removed, ensure that the JSCI is completed as appropriate
  • There is no Issue displayed specifically to review the NSSRR/NRRR registration flag. However, a JSR/UPD activity:
    • for NSSRR with a 'JSC', 'TDI' and 'FEP' keywords
    • for NRRR with a 'JSC', 'TDI' and 'RJC' keywords

will be displayed on the job seeker's record on the Activity List (AL) screen prompting the Service Officer to change the Job Seeker Registration type. For more information on this activity, see Job Seeker Management Activities.
