This page contains information on eligibility and registration for Job Seeker Registration Only (JSRO)/No Remote Referral Required (NRRR) job seekers.

Eligibility and registration of JSRO/NRRR job seekers

Step

Action

1

Eligibility for JSRO/NRRR job seeker + Read more ...

JSRO/NRRR registration is completed as part of the Participation Interview or Job Seeker Registration workflow from the Participation Summary screen.

Check if the job seeker is eligible for registration as a Fully Eligible Participant (FEP) or Volunteer.

If the job seeker is in a Community Development Program (CDP) region, they will not be eligible for registration as FEP or as a volunteer. The system will determine if the job seeker is Remote Fully Eligible (RFE).

If the job seeker does not meet conditions for registration as a FEP or as a volunteer, JSRO/NRRR registration is applicable for the following groups:

  • job seekers requiring a Department of Employment and Workplace Relations record in order to be referred for an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) or in the case of customers claiming or receiving Disability Support Pension (DSP), referred for a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA)
  • full-time job seekers applying for a supplementary allowance determination
  • full-time students receiving a student payment who are transferring to a job seeker payment, and contact prior to ceasing studies but within four weeks of the Student End Date (SED)
  • the following groups who require a Job Plan but do not choose to volunteer for employment services:
    • principal carers currently meeting their Mutual obligation requirements for principal carers
    • principal carers with long term exemptions from their mutual obligation requirements (greater than 16 weeks)
    • people with a Partial Capacity to Work
    • people who have been assessed as having a capacity of 0-14 hours per week and are considered volunteers for employment services
  • customers in receipt of an income support payment that has no mutual obligation requirements who do not choose to volunteer with employment services and want to apply for Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work)
  • New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS)/Self-Employment Assistance participants presenting to Services Australia after the 39 weeks of participation in the Scheme to claim income support

To proceed with a JSRO/NRRR registration, go to Step 2.

To review a JSRO/NRRR registration, go to Step 6.

2

Select appropriate tool for JSRO/NRRR registration + Read more ...

Note: for customers registering as JSRO/NRRR to access an ESAt/JCA referral or in order to complete a Job Plan registration must be completed prior to the ESAt/JCA referral or Job Plan negotiation.

Open Process Direct.

For customers who:

  • submit an online claim or a Service Officer has completed the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) workflow, once in the claim:
    • select > Participation Interview > Job seeker registration & provider referral information > Start job seeker registration
    • go to Step 3
  • currently receive a payment, including a voluntary Job Plan for voluntary job seekers who want to be referred for ACCS (Transition to work):
    • select Customer Summary from the Process Direct Landing page
    • enter the customer's CRN, select Go
    • in the customer's record select > Participation Summary > Job seeker registration & provider referral information > Start job seeker registration
    • go to Step 4

3

Update Job Seeker Registration screen + Read more ...

Note: if the customer is already an active job seeker and connected with a provider, the registration cannot be changed to JSRO/NRRR.

The Participation Interview will recognise if the customer is JSRO/NRRR, however the customer can be assessed for further assistance.

Note: when the customer is identified as a 'Full Time Student transfer to job seeker within 4 weeks of Student End Date' then the field will be protected and set to 'No'. Conduct the Job Seeker Snapshot interview.

Online claim and ACC contact process ends here.

4

Update Job Seeker Registration screen + Read more ...

The Job Seeker Registration screen displays as part of Participation Interview and Job Seeker Registration workflow.

Note: if the customer is already an active job seeker and connected with a provider, the registration cannot be changed to JSRO/NRRR.

For the question 'Set Job Seeker Registration as No Provider Referral Required?' select appropriate reason from the drop down options shown below. Box will pre-populate for DSP new claimants and students contacting within 4 weeks of the Student End Date.

  • DSP Customer
  • Full-time Student (FTS) within 4 weeks of Student End Date (SED)
  • Principal Carer (PCP) meeting requirements
  • Principal Carer (PCP) with long term exemption
  • Partial Capacity to Work (PCW) meeting requirements
  • PCW 0 - 14 hrs
  • FTS applying for supplementary allowance
  • NEIS participant. Note: select this reason for Self-Employment Assistance participant
  • Special Disability Trust
  • ACCS (Transition to Work)

Complete remaining questions on screen and select Continue.

Job Seeker Snapshot Required screen will display.

5

Assessment of requirement for Job Seeker Snapshot + Read more ...

A JSRO registered job seeker requires a Job Seeker Snapshot if the customer wants to access other employment services such as a referral to an Assessor or Disability Employment Services (DES), but no referral to Workforce Australia.

NRRR registered job seekers will only require a Job Seeker Snapshot if an ESAt is required.

If a job seeker does require a Job Seeker Snapshot:

  • Select Yes then select Continue
  • Conduct the Job Seeker Snapshot interview
  • Tell the job seeker their Job Seeker Identification Number (JSID) at the end of the interview

If a job seeker does not require a Job Seeker Snapshot:

  • Select No then select Continue
  • Conduct the Job Seeker Snapshot interview
  • Tell the job seeker their Job Seeker Identification Number (JSID) at the end of the interview

Procedure ends here.

6

Review JSRO/NRRR registration + Read more ...

If a job seeker is no longer eligible for a JSRO/NRRR registration, such as principal carer not meeting mutual obligation requirements, the JSRO/NRRR flag must be removed and the job seeker's registration changed to Fully Eligible Participant (FEP)/Remote Fully Eligible (RFE).

  • The JSRO/NRRR flag will be automatically removed at the SED+1 for those full-time students transferring to a job seeker payment
  • For all other JSRO/NRRR job seekers, the flag has to be reviewed and removed manually from the Job Seeker Registration screen with the Job Seeker Registration workflow when it is no longer appropriate. When the JSRO/NRRR registration flag is being removed, ensure that the Job Seeker Snapshot is completed as appropriate
  • There is no Issue displayed specifically to review the JSRO/NRRR registration flag. However, a JSR/UPD activity:
    • for JSRO with a 'JSC', 'TDI' and 'FEP' keywords
    • for NRRR with a 'JSC', 'TDI' and 'RJC' keywords

will be displayed on the job seeker's record on the Activity List (AL) screen prompting the Service Officer to change the Job Seeker Registration type. For more information on this list, see Job Seeker Management Activities.
