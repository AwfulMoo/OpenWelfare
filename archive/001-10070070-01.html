Failing to attend or satisfactorily participate in a job interview 001-10070070

This document outlines the job seeker compliance policy and procedures that apply when assessing a job seeker's non-attendance at a job interview and situations during a job interview where a job seeker intentionally behaves in a manner that results in an offer of employment not being made.

Requirement to attend and satisfactorily participate in job interviews

Job seekers receiving a participation payment are expected to attend all job interviews their provider has arranged or which they have secured for themselves. When attending a job interview, job seekers are expect to present themselves, speak and behave in a manner that gives them the best opportunity to secure employment.

The Job Seeker Statement included with all Job Plans contains the compulsory requirement to attend all job interviews and do their best to get the job. However, the absence of such a clause in a job seeker's Job Plan, or where a job seeker has no Job Plan, does not remove the requirement for a job seeker with mutual obligation requirements in receipt of a participation payment to attend a job interview and behave in an appropriate manner.

Where the provider arranges the job interview, the provider has responsibility for ensuring the job seeker receives proper notification and reasonable notice. The provider also has responsibility for monitoring the job seeker's attendance and contacting the job seeker where they have been advised of inappropriate behaviour during the job interview.

Where a job seeker fails to attend a job interview or behaves inappropriately this is managed using the relevant job seeker compliance system to ensure that job seekers who are not genuine in their efforts to gain employment are appropriately held to account for their actions.

Note: participation requirements for ParentsNext participants do not include requirements to look for work.

Job seeker unable to attend a job interview

Job seekers who are unable to attend a job interview arranged by their provider are expected to notify their provider prior to the scheduled start time and make alternative arrangements where appropriate. This reflects the same expectations that an employer would have of an employee.

Behaving in an inappropriate manner while at a job interview

Job seekers are expected to behave in an appropriate manner while participating in a job interview.

Intentional inappropriate behaviour or conduct at a job interview that could be expected to result in an offer of employment not being made, may demonstrate the job seeker is not genuine in their efforts to find and undertake suitable work.

This type of behaviour may include:

  • dressing in a way that is clearly inappropriate for the interview
  • displaying an obvious lack of interest in the job on offer, for example, a lack of responsiveness to the employer's questions or explanation about the job on offer
  • using inappropriate language
  • rudeness or verbal abuse, or
  • any other offensive or inappropriate behaviour at the interview

Circumstances that are not within the job seeker’s control cannot be considered deliberate misbehaviour.

  • The relevant job seeker compliance system is used to manage situations where a job seeker behaves inappropriately at a job interview, and it was reasonably foreseeable that this behaviour would result in an offer of employment not being made.

Job interview related compliance event occurring on the weekend

Where the job seeker fails to attend or satisfactorily participate in a job interview that was scheduled for a weekend, the compliance action depends on the job seeker compliance system they are managed under.

  • Under the Targeted Compliance Framework, a mutual obligation failure can be committed on a weekend, in which case the failure event date will be the date the job interview was scheduled.
  • Under the Job Seeker Compliance Model, No Show, No Pay failures relating to attendance or participation in an activity included in the Job Plan cannot occur over a weekend other than where a job seeker has been referred to a job interview on a weekend and they have either not attended, behaved or presented in a manner that resulted in an offer of employment not being made

Managing non-compliance with job interview requirements - Targeted Compliance Framework

Under the Targeted Compliance Framework, when a job seeker does not attend or satisfactorily participate in a job interview a mutual obligation failure has been committed.

Following a mutual obligation failure reported by the job seeker's provider, participation payments are not payable until the job seeker contacts their provider and meets reconnection requirements notified by their provider. Participation payments are cancelled if the reconnection requirement is not met within four weeks.

When providers report that a mutual obligation failure has been committed, the job seeker’s participation payments are automatically suspended. The Services Australia system lifts the payment suspension when the provider electronically notifies the conditions for lifting the payment suspension have been met.

To reflect the serious nature of this type of mutual obligation failure, job seekers in the warning zone will fast track to the next assessment (either a Capability Interview or Capability Assessment) if the provider is not satisfied the job seeker has a valid reason for failing to attend or satisfactorily participate in a job interview.

Job seekers in the financial penalty zone will incur a financial penalty unless:

  • on contact with the job seeker to discuss the failure, their provider closes the Non-Compliance Report (NCR) because they are satisfied the job seeker has a valid reason for not attending the job interview or the behaviour was not within the job seeker’s control, or
  • the job seeker satisfies Services Australia during a compliance investigation that they had reasonable excuse for non-attendance or the behaviour was not within their control. Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Services Officers are responsible for conducting these compliance investigations

Managing non-compliance with job interview requirements - Job Seeker Compliance Model

Under the Job Seeker Compliance Model, failure to attend or satisfactorily participate at a job interview is managed under the No Show, No Pay policy. This is a key component of the Job Seeker Compliance Model and is ‘work-like’ in its approach. It is intended to reinforce the conditionality of participation payments in return for mutual obligation requirements while also reinforcing behaviours generally expected in the workforce, for example, turning up on time, ringing beforehand when unable to attend, and behaving in an appropriate manner while in attendance.

No Show, No Pay failure

A No Show, No Pay failure occurs where a Community Development Program (CDP) job seeker does not attend a job interview, or intentionally behaves in a manner during a job interview that might be expected to result in an offer of employment not being made.

A No Show, No Pay failure is generated for investigation by Services Australia when a provider submits a Participation Report. Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officers are responsible for investigating the incident and determining whether a No Show, No Pay failure has occurred.

Consequence of a No Show, No Pay failure

If applied, a No Show, No Pay failure will result in a No Show, No Pay penalty for the day the non-compliance occurred.

Applied No Show, No Pay failures contribute to a pattern of non-compliance for a Comprehensive Compliance Assessment (CCA). An automatic CCA will be generated based on the number of No Show, No Pay failures applied in a serious failure assessment period. When a decision is made that a CDP job seeker has been persistently non-compliant, a serious failure may be applied.

The Resources page contains the text to be used in the Q062 letter sent to a job seeker who has a No Show, No Pay employment related failure occurring on a weekend.
