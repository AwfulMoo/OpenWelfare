2

Customer contacts about a ParentsNext exemption + Read more ...

An exemption can be extended provided that the customer's circumstances about the exemption remain current. Service Officers must confirm that the customer is still eligible for the exemption. If not, consider whether another exemption type is more appropriate for their circumstances.

If an exemption is extended or a new exemption is required, then the same process will need to be followed as coding the initial exemption period. See relevant process for each exemption type.

If the customer contacts about a ParentsNext exemption or extending an exemption, and:

  • has a future ParentsNext appointment booked with Services Australia, tell them to attend the appointment to discuss their circumstances if possible. If the customer's circumstances are such that they need the appointment rescheduled to a later date. See ParentsNext eligibility and servicing
  • does not have a future ParentsNext appointment booked with Services Australia, and is not yet referred to a ParentsNext provider, book a ParentsNext Services Australia appointment and tell them to discuss their circumstances at the appointment
  • has already been referred to a ParentsNext provider, go to Step 3

3

Circumstances identified during a ParentsNext appointment or for a customer already referred to a provider or where a work item with the keyword PNXEXP has been allocated + Read more ...

The majority of mutual obligation requirements exemptions are also applicable for ParentsNext participants however, there are some circumstances where the exemption type and coding differ for ParentsNext participants. There are two ParentsNext specific exemptions; Temporary Period of Confinement exemption and bereavement exemption.

Both the agency and ParentsNext providers can grant exemptions. A list of all the exemptions that can be applied by the agency and by ParentsNext providers, including the maximum duration for each exemption is available on the Resources page.

Is the Service Officer skilled to action the ParentsNext exemption request?

  • Yes, and the customer advised:
    • they are pregnant, see Step 1 in Table 2
    • a new child under 9 months old has entered their care, see Step 2 in Table 2
    • they have short term caring responsibilities, see Step 3 in Table 2
    • their partner or an immediate family member has passed away, see Step 7 in Table 2
    • they are experiencing family and domestic violence or relationship breakdown, see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances
    • they have a temporary incapacity, see Assessing and coding medical evidence for temporary incapacity exemptions
    • they have a serious illness, see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements for serious illness
    • they have long term caring responsibilities (foster carer, non-parent carer, home schooling, distance education, caring for a child with a disability or illness and the child is not eligible for Carer Payment), see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances
    • they are caring for a large family with 4 or more children who are either 0-15 years old, or who are 16-18 years old inclusive but still completing secondary study, see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances
    • they are experiencing other exceptional circumstances, including homelessness, jury duty, community service order, natural disaster, major disruptions to their home or other major personal crisis which impacts their ability to meet their participation requirements, for example, Indigenous sorry business. See Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances
    • they are travelling overseas, see Exempting a job seeker from their mutual obligation requirements when going overseas
  • No, go to Step 4

Coding ParentsNext specific exemptions or processes

Table 2

Action

1

Customer advised they are pregnant + Read more ...

If the expected date of birth of the child is:

  • within 6 weeks, go to Step 2
  • more than 6 weeks:

    • tell the customer to provide evidence to their provider or recontact with evidence when they are within 6 weeks of the expected date of birth.
      Procedure ends here

2

Temporary Period of Confinement exemption + Read more ...

Pregnant customer's that are within 6 weeks of the expected date of birth or customers that have a new child under 9 months of age enter their care are eligible for a ParentsNext Temporary Period of Confinement (PTC) exemption. The exemption applies until the child turns 9 months (39 weeks) of age.

Evidence of the expected date of birth or the new child entering care must be provided before the PTC exemption can be recorded. Evidence of the expected date of delivery can include a letter from a doctor or registered midwife. It does not need to be a Services Australia medical certificate.

Is the customer able to provide evidence of their child's expected date of birth or the date of new child entering care at the time of the contact?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Short term caring responsibilities + Read more ...

If a ParentsNext participant has short term caring responsibilities, they may be eligible for a Special Family Circumstance exemption that can be applied for up to 16 weeks.

Note: the specific Caring exemption is not available for ParentsNext participants as the maximum duration is limited to 14 weeks (applicable to job seekers).

If the customer has long term caring responsibilities (foster carer, non-parent carer, home schooling), see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances.

The customer must provide evidence of the short term caring responsibilities in the form of a medical certificate or a letter from a treating health professional confirming the caring requirements.

Is the customer able to provide evidence of their caring responsibilities at the time of the appointment or contact?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, and the contact is:
    • During a ParentsNext appointment, go to Step 5
    • Outside of a ParentsNext appointment, go to Step 4

4

Customer not able to provide evidence of their circumstances at contact outside of an appointment + Read more ...

An exemption cannot be recorded for customers who are unable to provide evidence of:

  • their child's expected date of birth
  • a new child under 9 months of age entering their care
  • short term caring responsibilities

If the customer:

  • is not yet connected to a ParentsNext provider, tell them to provide evidence before or at their next Services Australia ParentsNext appointment, procedure ends here
  • is connected to a provider, go to Step 6

5

Customer not able to provide evidence of short term caring during a ParentsNext appointment + Read more ...

As the customer has not yet been referred to a ParentsNext provider, a future ParentsNext appointment must be booked to make sure that evidence of their short term caring responsibilities is received and recorded where appropriate.

  • Tell the customer:
    • they must provide evidence of their short term caring responsibilities before or at their next appointment (they can use the Centrelink Express Plus mobile app to do this)
    • once they provide evidence they will be exempt for the duration provided on the evidence
  • Book a ParentsNext appointment for at least 21 days after the date of the current appointment. For more information on booking appointments, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System
  • Tell the customer of the appointment details and that if they do not provide the required evidence before or at the next appointment they will be referred to a ParentsNext provider
  • Record a Fast Note on the customer's record:
    • Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Parents > Update > ParentsNext Review - Other
    • Delete the irrelevant options
    • record that the customer was advised they must provide evidence of their short term caring responsibilities at or before their next ParentsNext appointment or they will be referred to a provider
    • Complete the DOC

Procedure ends here.

6

Customer already referred to a provider not able to provide evidence of their circumstances + Read more ...

As the customer has already been referred to a ParentsNext provider, a future ParentsNext appointment must not be booked.

Tell the customer:

  • they must provide evidence of their child's expected date of birth (for example, a letter from a doctor or registered midwife) or the new child entering care or the short term caring responsibilities to either their provider or Services Australia before an exemption can be recorded (they can use the Centrelink Express Plus mobile app to do this)
  • once evidence has been received and recorded they will be granted an exemption until the new child is 9 months of age
  • if evidence is not provided they will remain as a compulsory ParentsNext participant and must continue to meet their ParentsNext participation requirements
  • DOC the record to detail the customer was advised they must provide evidence of their circumstances before an exemption can be recorded and that if they do not provide it they must continue to meet their ParentsNext participation requirements

Procedure ends here.

7

Death of a partner or immediate family member + Read more ...

Compulsory ParentsNext participants are eligible for an exemption from participation requirements for up to 16 weeks from the date of their partner's or immediate family member's death. The bereavement exemption must be coded to make sure the customer is able to be exempt for up to 16 weeks.

Note: this exemption differs from the bereavement mutual obligation exemption that is limited to 14 weeks.

Death of a partner

If the Miscellaneous Update Details (NMIS) screen is coded in relation to the partner's death, a mutual obligation exemption will be automatically granted for a maximum of 14 weeks or if the customer is pregnant, to the end of the pregnancy (whichever is longer) where evidence is provided. A manual bereavement exemption must still be coded to make sure the customer can access the maximum 16 weeks available.

If the customer is pregnant and still a compulsory ParentsNext participant when the expected date of birth is reached, the system will automatically apply a ParentsNext temporary period of confinement (PTC) exemption until the child is 9 months of age.

If this is the initial contact after the death of a partner, see Initial contact after someone has died for more information about available services and referrals.

Go to Step 8.

8

Coding the appropriate exemption + Read more ...

Note: Service Officers must not send a referral to the social work team to code an exemption.

To code the Create Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen in Process Direct:

  • Select Customer Summary from the Process Direct Landing page
  • Enter the customer’s CRN, select Go
  • In the customer’s record go to the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen
  • Select Add
  • The AEX screen will display
  • Select the appropriate ParentsNext Activity type:
    • PTC - ParentsNext Temporary Confinement
    • SCI - Special Family Circumstances - for short term caring
    • BVP - Bereavement exemption - for death of partner or immediate family member
  • Complete the Start date and End date, select Save
  • Complete the Receipt Date and Channel fields, select Save
  • Select Assess and address any errors or warnings
  • Finalise the transaction by selecting Assess again, select Finish. Record details of the update on the Finalise screen
  • Select Finalise to complete the transaction
  • If the customer is present at the time the exemption is being applied, tell them:
    • they need to participate once the exemption has ended, (unless they exit the program beforehand) including attending any scheduled appointments with the agency or their provider
    • they must still report fortnightly to Services Australia during the period of their exemption to make sure they receive their Parenting Payment (PP)
  • If the customer:
    • has not yet been referred to a ParentsNext provider, the system will automatically book a new ParentsNext appointment at the end of the exemption where they are still eligible so the customer can be referred to a ParentsNext provider
    • has already been referred to a ParentsNext provider, it will be the responsibility of the ParentsNext provider to re-engage the customer at the end of the exemption period where they are still eligible. The agency does not need to undertake any follow up at the end of the exemption in this situation

Procedure ends here.

2

Retrospective child update + Read more ...

If the child is a newborn child or recently came into the customer's care and is yet to be linked on the customer’s Child in Customer Care (CHS) screen, go to Step 6.

  • Parenting Payment Single (PPS), go to Step 3

3

Customer receiving Parenting Payment Single (PPS) + Read more ...

A qualifying child for a customer receiving PPS for the purposes of ParentsNext is determined by what is recorded on the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen and the Pensions Child Summary (PNKDS) screen.

  • Go to the PNKDS screen
  • Is the child/ren correctly showing as a dependent:
    • Yes, lodge a request for Local Peer Support (LPS) help (phone appointment) or the Technical Support Line (face to face appointment) who will help in determining why the child is not being correctly assessed
    • No, lodge a request for Local Peer Support (LPS) help (phone appointment) or the Technical Support Line (face to face appointment) to request help in updating the PNKDS screen
  • Once the record is updated, go to Step 5

Note: if there is a delay in the update correctly reassessing eligibility for ParentsNext, run the daily driver.

4

Customer receiving Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) + Read more ...

A qualifying child in care for a customer receiving PPP for the purposes of ParentsNext is determined by what is recorded on the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen. Therefore, if a child does not have a PPP entry correctly recorded on the CHOC screen, that child will not be considered in the eligibility determination for ParentsNext.

Update the CHOC screen in Customer First:

  • Navigate to the Child Selection (CHS) screen and 'S'elect the relevant child
  • Go to the CHOC screen
  • Key the following fields on the CHOC screen:
    • Date child's date of birth or date entered care
    • Svc Rsn customer's payment type (PPP)
    • Reason claim
  • Finalise the activity by going to the Assessment Results (AR) screen , if the activity will be sent for QOL include notes to detail the update was made to reassess eligibility for ParentsNext
  • Complete a DOC including the details of why the updates were made

Note: if there is a delay in the update correctly reassessing eligibility for ParentsNext, run the daily driver.

Go to Step 5.

5

Child in care before or after ParentsNext selection date + Read more ...

The date the new child was born or entered care will determine the outcome of updating the child details.

Was the PP child under 9 months of age in care before the ParentsNext selection date?

  • Yes
    • As the youngest child that was in care before selection for ParentsNext is under the minimum age for eligibility the customer does not need to participate in ParentsNext at this time
    • The update to the child details will result in the customer automatically exiting for the reason of Retrospective Child, see Step 1 in Table 5
  • No, the customer is eligible for a Temporary Period of Confinement exemption, see Step 8 in Table 2

6

Evidence of a new born child or child under 9 months recently entered care + Read more ...

It may be evident that there is a new child under 9 months old in care who is yet to be recorded on the customer's record:

  • a Newborn Child Declaration (FAO81) is on the customer's record
  • a DOC is recorded during a previous discussion with the customer

In this scenario, the new child must be recorded before referring the customer to a ParentsNext provider.

If a phone appointment has been scheduled and the customer is not already present, contact must still be attempted as a courtesy. The customer would have received notification of the appointment.

Was outbound phone contact successful or did the customer attend for a face to face appointment?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No,
    • do not suspend the payment
    • record the current ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'completed'
    • do not book a new ParentsNext appointment. There is an auto-review that will check the customer’s eligibility 4 weeks after they were selected. If the customer is still eligible at that time a new ParentsNext appointment will be auto-booked or a review created to manually follow up the status
    • record a DOC on the customers record stating:
      - that there is evidence of a new born child that is yet be recorded on the customers record which may impact on the their eligibility for ParentsNext
      - if the customer makes contact, transfer to the Families main business line to discuss coding of the new born child

Procedure ends here.

7

New child in care that is not recorded on customer’s record + Read more ...

If a customer has provided evidence of a new born on their record or during their phone or face to face appointment they have advised of a new child under 9 months entering their care but the child is not recorded on the CHS screen:

  • check the DOC list to determine if evidence has been received, where there is:
    • a completed FAO81 and no further information is needed, tell the customer nothing further is needed at this time and their record will be updated in due course
    • a DOC detailing further information is needed to update the record, tell the customer what must be provided
    • no evidence or it is unclear on what is needed, transfer or tell the customer to contact the Families line to discuss their circumstances with a families trained Service Officer. Do not tell the customer that this will expedite the process, but rather make sure they are aware of all that is needed to finalise their claim
  • tell the customer if a further ParentsNext appointment is needed they will be notified of the appointment date and time

record the current ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'completed'

  • do not book a new ParentsNext appointment. There is an auto-review process that will check the customer's eligibility 4 weeks after they were selected. If the customer is still eligible at that time a new ParentsNext appointment will be auto-booked or a review created to manually follow up the status
  • record a DOC to detail the customer has a new child in care which is yet to be recorded on their record and which may impact on their eligibility for ParentsNext

Procedure ends here.

8

Current study meets minimum ParentsNext study requirements + Read more ...

As the customer has provided evidence of meeting the minimum ParentsNext study requirements, the study can now be recorded.

Note: if the customer was selected for ParentsNext and they are currently in between semesters of a course, the start date should be coded as the start of the first semester before their selection and not the start of the new semester.

  • Navigate to the ParentsNext Summary page, and:
    • navigate to the Study section and launch the Study Guided Procedure
    • complete all of the fields in the Guided Procedure as per the evidence provided
    • finalise the Guided Procedure
  • Tell the customer:
    • they will be exited from the program due to their current study but may be re-selected, if they are eligible, once their study ends
    • if they are reselected in the future, another appointment will be booked at that time

Is the study for greater than 12 months?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 5

9

Study for greater than 12 months + Read more ...

The study must be reviewed in 12 months to make sure the customer is still studying and that the study continues to meet the minimum ParentsNext study requirements.

Create a manual review:

  • Go to the Review Registration (RVR) screen
  • On the RVR screen, code fields:
    • Service Reason: the customer's payment type (for example, PPP or PPS)
    • Review Reason: NEV - Possible Notifiable Event
    • Due Date: 12 months from the system date
    • Text: Record that the customer must be contacted in 12 months to confirm they are still studying and that the study continues to meet the minimum ParentsNext study requirements
    • Keywords: PNXSTU
    • Source: and Date of Receipt:
    • Press [Enter] or select Continue to accept the review.
    • The review has now been set. It will appear on the customer's FAL screen with the status of REV.
  • Record a closed DOC on the record using the ParentsNext Current Study Fast Note

See Step 1 in Table 5.

Step

Action

1

Customer no longer required to participate + Read more ...

As the customer is not eligible as a compulsory ParentsNext participant, they have the option to elect to register as a voluntary participant.

If the appointment was a phone appointment and the customer is not already present, contact must still be attempted as a courtesy, as they will have received notification of the appointment.

2

Offer customer option to volunteer in ParentsNext + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • that due to their current circumstances they are no longer a compulsory ParentsNext participant at this time
  • this means that they do not need to participate in the program, however they can still choose to volunteer

Tell them that if they volunteer, their provider will:

  • support them to identify and work towards their education and employment goals
  • have access to financial help through the Participation Fund, a flexible pool of funds that can be used to cover costs associated with work or study, such as the cost of training courses, child care, transport, IT equipment and job appropriate clothing

Tell the customer that they can find more information about the program and read stories from participants at the ParentsNext website - https://www.dewr.gov.au/parentsnext

Does the customer wish to volunteer?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, tell the customer if they change their mind about being a ParentsNext volunteer, they can either:
    • contact Services Australia, or
    • talk to a ParentsNext provider to check if they are eligible to participate in the program
      Note: tell them if their circumstances change in the future they may again become eligible for ParentsNext as a compulsory participant and may need to attend another appointment

To find a ParentsNext provider, go to Workforce Australia's website. Record the appointment outcome as 'completed'. Procedure ends here

3

Customer elected to participate voluntarily + Read more ...

As the customer has elected to participate voluntarily, they need to first be registered via the Voluntary Participation guided procedure.

Note: the Volunteer guided procedure will only be selectable if the customer meets the eligibility criteria for voluntary participation.

Register the customer as a voluntary participant.

  • Navigate to the ParentsNext Summary screen in Customer First via the Workspace > Social Services Benefit Management menu
  • Launch the Volunteer guided procedure from the Volunteer button under the Volunteer section of the page and ask the customer the eligibility questions
  • Select the relevant radio button based on the customer's responses to each question
  • Does the customer wish to volunteer? This will automatically default to 'No' if the customer's responses result in them being ineligible, otherwise select 'Yes'
  • Comments field, record relevant notes based on whether the customer was eligible to volunteer or not
  • Select Next
  • Select Finish to finalise the guided procedure

Note: if there is a delay in the update correctly reassessing eligibility for ParentsNext, run the daily driver.

Go to Step 4.

Does the customer have an active job seeker status?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

Activate Job seeker ID + Read more ...

  • On the Job seeker registration & provider referral information screen select the Start Job seeker registration workflow
  • Set job seeker registration to no provider referral required? field, select No
  • Select Next
  • If additional questions present, complete as per the customer’s responses
  • Select Next
  • Do you wish to conduct Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) for this customer? field, select No

  • Check the customer’s Job Seeker ID Status field has been updated to Active

6

Refer a voluntary participant to a ParentsNext provider + Read more ...

Note: E009JW is a generic error code may be encountered within the Start job seeker registration workflow or the Online Diary.

Navigate to the Online Diary. Select Create new referral > SPP-Parents next provider > Create Referral. Workflow will navigate to the List Providers screen:

  • A list of all ParentsNext providers who have appointments available within the next 2 working days will appear on the List Providers screen
  • Select the ParentsNext provider. If multiple providers are available, ask the customer their preferred provider. If a customer is indecisive, service officers must not provide a recommendation
  • From the Available Appointments screen, select a suitable appointment (no earlier than 7 days in the future if an interpreter is needed)
    • If there are no appointments available showing, select more appointments to display more appointments (the date from will need to be updated to the relevant date the search is to be conducted from)
    • In the Reason for choosing more appointments, select the relevant reason. Note: not all reasons are appropriate for ParentsNext
  • When an appointment has been selected, the 'Appointment Detail' screen will display:
    • Confirm details of the appointment with the customer
    • Additional Requirements: field, enter information the provider may need to be aware of, for example, if an interpreter is required
    • Appointment Notification: field, select Face to Face (where the Services Australia appointment was conducted at a service centre) or Phone (where the Services Australia appointment was conducted via phone)
  • Select Book Appointment to finalise the appointment booking process
  • The Appointment Outcome page will display:
    • The details of the appointment
    • Was this appointment created in error? field, this question should be left blank
    • Select Finish
  • From the Provider Appointment screen, read the customer the information contained within the message displayed
