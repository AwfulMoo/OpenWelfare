Examples

Calculations of the Child Disability Assistance Payment

Table 1: This table contains examples of calculations of the Child Disability Assistance Payment.

Example

Description

1

60% care

If a person receives 60% Carer Allowance (CA) (for a child) caring for a child as of 1 July under the shared care arrangements, the amount of the annual payment is $600 ($1,000 x 60%). However if a person providing 60% of the care receives 100% CA because the other carer has not claimed CA then the rate of payment of CDAP will be 100% ($1,000).

2

100% care

If an individual is entitled to 100% CA (for a child) on 1 July for each of 3 child care receivers, the amount of the annual CDAP is $3,000 ($1,000 x 3).

3

Part rate of CA

If a carer receives a part rate of CA (for a child) in the fortnight including 1 July because the child care receiver is attending Education, Training or Treatment (ETT), provided they are not in a shared care arrangement, the amount of the annual payment for carers will be at the full rate. In these cases the annual payment is not paid on a pro rata basis.

FAQ

FAQ examples

Table 2: This table contains information regarding eligibility for Child Disability Assistance Payment.

Item

Description

1

Who is eligible for the payment?

For the purposes of the annual payment to carers, a 'care receiver' is a child that attracts Carer Allowance (CA) (child) including CA (auto) - but not Health Care Card (HCC) only - for the carer on 1 July.

  • An individual is eligible for the annual payment for carers if they were receiving CA (child) on 1 July. If a carer's entitlement was suspended or cancelled for 1 July, and then subsequently restored, they will become eligible for the annual payment
  • The care receiver must be a child under age 16 or extended under the CA (child) provisions. Hence adult care receivers granted as part of 'care provided for a disabled adult and one or two children with a disability or medical condition', and child care receivers over 16 years, are not entitled to Child Disability Assistance Payment (CDAP)
  • HCC only Carer Allowance (child) recipients are not eligible for the annual payment for carers
  • If a HCC only carer is reviewed and found to be eligible for CA (child) payment level, then the CDAP is only payable if the start date of payment (date of effect of the review) is on or before 1 July
  • A person can receive CA (child) because they are caring for two disabled children who have Child Disability Assessment Tool scores of less than 1, and have Treating Health Professional scores of greater than zero. In these cases, the care receivers are counted as one for the purposes of determining the amount of annual payment payable

Note: the system will allow for manual zero of a payment of the CDAP if it is required.

2

Does the carer have to apply for the payment and when will it be paid?

  • No application is required for the CDAP to be paid to eligible customers
  • Where possible, carers who are in receipt of CA (child) on 1 July will be automatically paid the annual payment usually with their first instalment of CA paid after 1 July
  • The instalment will be paid into the same bank account that CA is paid to but will not appear as a separate entry
  • For carers who are eligible in July each year, it is expected that payments will be made automatically until September each year
  • When carers are granted or restored retrospectively after September, there will be a warning for the Service Officer to make the payment manually on the Carer One Off Payment (COOP) screen
  • Manual payment procedures may be required if carer was backdated prior to 1 July and payment has not automatically occurred

3

How much is the payment?

  • The annual payment is $1,000 for eligible recipients of CA (child). The payment may be less than $1,000 if there is a shared care arrangement
  • A CA (child) carer cannot get more than $1,000 for each care receiver, although there may be instances where a care receiver attracts more than $1,000 in total (for example, if there is a change of carer where each carer receives an instalment for 1 July). That is, payment to the second carer is not dependent on recovery of the payment from the first carer

4

Is the payment taxable?

The annual payment for carers is not taxable and is not counted as income for social security or family assistance purposes.

5

What happens when there is a change of care or shared care?

  • Where there is a change of care and retrospective grant on or before 1 July, the new carer will receive the annual payment irrespective of whether the previous carer received the bonus. Note: recovery action should be taken on the other carer if a Carer Allowance (CA) debt is raised for a period including 1 July
  • If an individual receives CA (child) under a shared care arrangement the rate of CA applying on 1 July is used to calculate the rate of CDAP. For example, two carers may each receive 50% of CA and therefore each receive 50% or $500 CDAP or where only one carer has applied for CA and have 50% of the care their rate however will be 100% CA and therefore 100% of CDAP
  • Shared care rate decisions apply only from the date of the decision and are rarely backdated however of the shared care percentage on 1 July is subsequently updated (for example, through review), then the CDAP amount can be updated retrospectively to pay a top up of the annual payment to the carer with the increased percentage. Note: recovery action should be taken on the other carer only if a CA debt is raised for a period including 1 July
  • A system warning and help text will be displayed when the shared care % is changed

6

What happens if the care receiver child dies?

  • CA (child) carers whose child care receiver died within 14 weeks of 1 July (25/3 - 1/7) are also entitled to CDAP for this child. As this bereavement payment is paid in a lump sum when the death is notified, they are not in receipt of an ongoing payment on 1 July but have been paid for a period covering 1 July
  • Payments will be made to the last known account of the person receiving CA in cases where the care receiver has died before payment of CDAP has been made

7

Does a payment nominee receive the payment?

If payment nominee arrangements apply to a CA (child) carer, the CDAP goes to the nominee.

FAQ - continued

FAQ examples - continued

Table 3: This table contains information regarding eligibility for Child Disability Assistance Payment.

Item

Description

1

Can a debt be raised for this payment?

  • Standard debt raising provisions apply to CDAP
  • If a carer is subsequently found to be ineligible for CA (child) and an overpayment of CA is raised for a period including 1 July, the CDAP they received will also be raised as a debt
  • The Debt Management Information System will automatically generate a debt shell activity for a Child Disability Assistance Payment debt
  • Where there is more than one CDAP eligible care receiver the undetermined debt amount will be the total amount for all relevant care receivers. The correct debt amount to be raised will have to be determined by the Service Officer

2

Will CDAP be income managed, in the relevant areas?

The CDAP is 100% income managed in the relevant areas.

3

Do usual review procedures apply?

If a carer has not been paid CDAP, or disagrees with the amount they have received, normal review processes are to be followed.

4

What if the care receiver is absent due to education, training or treatment?

If a carer receives a part rate of CA because the child care receiver is attending education, training or treatment, the amount of CDAP will not be reduced for this reason.

5

Where are the details of the payment?

  • Details of the CDAP will be displayed on the Carer One Off Payment Summary (COOPS) screen
  • This screen will display the date of effect for CDAP, the amount paid, the Customer Reference Number (CRN) of the care receiver who qualified the carer for the payment, and the shared care days/percentage used to calculate the payment if applicable
  • The Payment Summary (PS) screen will show the CDAP as a future payment once the trigger has been run

6

Why didn't the carer get paid?

The carer was advised they would be paid the CDAP.

The PS screen will show the CDAP as a future payment once the trigger has been run. The carer may have been advised that the payment was showing as a future payment.

If Carer Allowance (CA) for this carer receiver is suspended or cancelled after the trigger is run, with a result that the carer does not receive an instalment for 1 July, the CDAP payment will come off the PS screen and the CDAP payment will not be issued.

If the payment is restored and the carer is entitled to the CDAP payment the customer should be paid through the COOP screen.

7

Will the carer receive a letter about the CDAP?

No. From July 2014 there are no letters issued advising of CDAP for 1 July.

However, carers granted or restored retrospectively with an eligibility date including 1 July will have a CDAP paragraph included in their advice letter.
