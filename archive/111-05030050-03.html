Transfer of non-priority calls

Transfer of non-priority calls within the network

This table describes when and how to transfer non-priority calls within the network.

Step

Action

1

A call is answered from a 13 or 1800 number + Read more ...

Can the enquiry be answered by the first Service Officer?

  • Yes, complete the call. Procedure ends here.
  • No, go to Step 2.

2

Check if the call is of an urgent nature + Read more ...

Is the call of an urgent nature, is the caller advising of a death, or are they in a distressed state, or has the Service Officer received a complex query outside their benefit area that they need to transfer to another Service Officer?

  • Yes, transfer the call using priority transfer to another queue. Procedure ends here.
  • No, go to Step 3.

3

Check whether a full authentication check has been done + Read more ...

Has a full authentication check been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Full authentication check to be completed + Read more ...

Record on a DOC that a full authentication check has been completed and whether the caller's identity has (or has not) been confirmed.

The Service Officer who receives the call will not need to repeat the authentication check.

5

National Transfer + Read more ...

The National Transfer is a non-priority transfer to another queue. This option is appropriate when the caller has dialled the wrong queue and the Service Officer does not have adequate knowledge to answer all the customer's enquiries.

Use this option also for callers who are identified as 'surfers' or intentional misusers of a 13 or 1800 number, unless their call is of an urgent nature.

  • A full authentication check is to be undertaken.
  • Record on a DOC that a full authentication check has been completed and whether the caller's identity has (or has not) been confirmed and the reason for the query.
  • Advise the customer of the correct 13 or 1800 number to ring in future.
  • Calls can be transferred to the relevant queue by utilising the Services Australia Workspace Transfer Options list. If no drop down option is available, the National Speed Dial numbers available through Office Locator National Lists can be utilised.
  • The call will go to the middle of a queue (unannounced). Middle of queue transfers take the customer's wait times with the transfer.

The receiving Service Officer is to check the Document List (DL) screen to see if a full authentication check has been completed.
