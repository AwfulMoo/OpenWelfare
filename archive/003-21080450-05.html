Geospatial tool

To assess if a customer lives or works in a relevant area:

  • Open the Queensland Live Feed App
  • Place the address given by the customer in the search box. Residential and employer addresses may need to be checked
  • Select Search (magnifying glass icon)
  • Select COVID-19 Disaster Payment in the Layers menu on the right hand side
  • For relevant areas in:
    • South East Queensland, the address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible to receive a payment
    • Cairns/Yarrabah, the address must lie in the red shaded area to be eligible
  • Continue to assess the claim

Scenarios – eligibility

Scenario

Outcome

Unable to attend work due to exemption from mask wearing

The customer works in a café as a casual for 25 hours per week.

The customer has an exemption from wearing a mask, however, a state public health order mandates that people working in food and beverage outlets must wear a mask.

Their employer tells the customer that they cannot attend work.

Eligible

The customer is eligible for the at least 20 hours or more rate of payment as they have lost 25 hours of work in the relevant period.

The customer’s rate of payment will be $750.

Note: if there was no public health order to mandate mask wearing and this was a condition of employment imposed by the employer, the customer would not be eligible for payment.

Unable to work due to lockdown

The customer works in a café in Brisbane CBD as a casual with on average of 30 hrs per week.

Due to the state public health order for a 'lockdown', the customer has been unable to attend their workplace for the period of the lockdown. The lockdown was for 7 days.

The customer has insufficient appropriate leave to cover the lockdown period.

Eligible

The state public health order for a stay at home 'lockdown' was for 7 days.

The customer was unable to attend work from the first day to the fifth day of the lockdown and would otherwise have worked 30 hours per week.

The customer has insufficient appropriate leave to cover the period for the lockdown.

The customer is paid $750.

Unable to work due to restricted movement (lockdown)

The customer is an essential worker living and working in a declared COVID-19 hotspot.

The customer has 2 children, aged 4 and 7, who ordinarily attend child care while the customer is at work. Due to the lockdown conditions, the customer’s child care centre has closed. The customer is unable to perform their work from home and unable to earn income for the period of the lockdown.

The customer would normally work 7 hour shifts 5 times per week.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer is eligible for the at least 20 hours or more rate of payment, as they as have lost 35 hours of work in the relevant period.

The customer's rate of payment will be $750.

Paid leave for part of restricted movement (lockdown) period

The customer lives in Brisbane and works in a Tourism Centre full time.

Due to the state public health order for a 'lockdown', the customer has been unable to attend their workplace for the period of the lockdown. The lockdown was for 7 days.

The customer has been paid 2 days of special COVID-19 leave by their employer starting from the first day of the lockdown.

The customer is not receiving an income support payment or a state/commonwealth based pandemic payment.

Eligible

The state public health order for a stay at home 'lockdown' was for seven (7) days.

The customer was unable to attend work from the first day to the fifth day of the lockdown. Although they have been paid two (2) days leave from their employer, it is not sufficient to cover the entire period of the lockdown. The customer would have worked more than 20 hours.

The customer is paid $750 - the payment is not pro-rata.

Self employed customer

The customer is a self-employed gardener who works full-time at various homes in their area.

Due to the state public health order for a 'lockdown' the customer has been unable to attend work, unable to work from home and unable to earn an income for the period of the lockdown. The lockdown was for seven (7) days.

The customer is not receiving an income support payment or a state/commonwealth based pandemic payment or a small business payment.

Eligible

The customer is not receiving an income support payment or a state/commonwealth based pandemic payment.

The customer has lost more than 20 hours of work. The customer is paid $750.

Taxi drivers

The customer is a taxi driver.

The customer estimates they have lost 7 hours per day of work because of the reduced demand for taxi driver services during the restricted movement (lockdown).

They would normally work 8 hours per day, 6 days per week.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer is eligible for the at least 20 hours or more rate of payment, as they as have lost 42 hours of work in the relevant period.

The customer's rate of payment will be $750.

Customer is not vaccinated against COVID-19 and their employer does not allow them to attend work

The customer usually works 38 hours per week and lives in a declared COVID-19 hotspot.

The customer has been told by their employer they cannot attend work, as they are not vaccinated against COVID-19.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer is eligible for the at least 20 hours or more rate of payment as they have lost 38 hours of work in the relevant period.

The customer’s rate of payment will be $750.

The customer will receive automated payments for the remaining periods unless the relevant area they live in is no longer deemed a COVID-19 hotspot or there is a change in circumstances.
