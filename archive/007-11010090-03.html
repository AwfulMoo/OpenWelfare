Reconciliation of FTB/CCB for partnered customers

Table 1: This table describes how reconciliation is applied to a FTB/CCB customer who was partnered at any time during the relevant financial year.

Item

Customer's circumstance

1

Customer separated at 30 June of relevant year + Read more ...

This processing applies for the 2002-2003 financial year onwards for FTB/CCB reconciliation and FTB lump sum claims.

The partner is treated as an ex-partner for reconciliation purposes.

New processing may trigger re-reconciliation for previous years. See Item 7.

  • Partial reconciliation will occur from 1 July of the lodgement year if only the ex-partner's actual income details are still needed for reconciliation to occur. See Item 2
  • Interim reconciliation will occur on 1 July of the extended lodgement year if actual adjusted taxable income (AATI) for the ex-partner is still required. See Item 3
  • Final reconciliation will occur if AATI for the ex-partner is received. If the customer advises the ex-partner has received their Tax Notice of Assessment for the relevant year and that income has not been received by the agency, then the FAO Request Actual Income (FRAI) screen will need to be coded on the ex-partner record

See Item 8 in the Debts and other circumstances table.

A non-lodgement exemption is only to be coded for an ex-partner if the customer advises the ex-partner is not required to lodge a tax return for the relevant year. See Recording not required to lodge advice and previous year income.

The customer should not incur a debt for the ex-partner period due to their or the ex-partner's AATI. See Item 8 in the Debts and other circumstances table.

The FTB Reconciliation Type Assessment (FRTA) and CCF Reconciliation Type Assessment (CRTA) screens show the type of reconciliation performed, partner details including the partner type, and the adequate income reason for each person.

  • To view the FRTA screen, 'S'elect the FTB reconciliation result from the FAO Reconciliation & Claim Summary (FRCS) screen, then code 'R' next to a calculation period on the FTB Calculation Result (FACRF) screen
  • To view the CRTA screen, 'S'elect the FTB reconciliation result from the FRCS screen, then code 'R' next to a calculation period on the CCF Calculation Result (FACR) screen

The partial reconciliation will only display in ex-partner periods.

2

Partial reconciliation, customer separated at 30 June of relevant year + Read more ...

The partner is treated as an ex-partner for reconciliation purposes.

Partial FTB/CCB reconciliation can occur from 1 July of the lodgement year as soon as the customer (and current partner) have met their lodgement obligations:

  • For the ex-partner period, estimates as provided during the relevant year are used for the customer and the ex-partner
  • For other periods, adequate income (for example, Actual adjusted taxable income (AATI) is used for the customer (and current partner if applicable)
  • All non AATI circumstances are assessed for the ex-partner period, including actual maintenance income for relevant periods. A debt may be calculated due to maintenance income and/or circumstance changes during the ex-partner period
  • FTB Part A supplement (If entitled) and Part B supplement will be included in the reconciliation result
  • Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS) is assessed using taxable income and will only be paid for a partnered period to eligible persons if partner income is known (reasonable estimate or taxable income details from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO)). A notification only to the ATO that a tax return is not necessary will not cause SIFS to reconcile. Note: for the 2017-18 and later financial years, customers must meet the SIFS grandfathering provisions for payment of SIFS
  • Entitlement to the FTB Part B quarantine period is assessed if the lower income earner returned to work in the relevant year. For 2008-2009 onwards, Part B is subject to the primary income earner limit and the secondary earner income test
  • If the customer received less than their legislative FTB rate due to an FTB payment choice, any extra entitlement up to the legislative rate will be included
  • For 2006-2007 and following years, the customer's annual Child Care Rebate (CCR) entitlement will be calculated when partial reconciliation of CCB occurs
  • Debt offsetting may apply

See the Resources page for an example.

For further information, see:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation or lump sum claim pending
  • Child Care Benefit (CCB) reconciliation or lump sum claim pending

Reconciliation is delayed in some cases due to customer/current partner circumstances

For financial years 2014-15 and earlier, forced reconciliation may have been undertaken on some records.

3

Interim reconciliation, customer separated at 30 June of relevant year + Read more ...

The partner is treated as an ex-partner for reconciliation purposes.

Interim reconciliation will occur immediately after 1 July of the extended lodgement year if only actual adjusted taxable income (AATI) for the ex-partner is still required for final reconciliation to occur:

  • FAO system calculates the customer's FTB/CCB entitlement using the most recent adequate income details, for example, actual income for the customer, most recent estimate for the ex-partner. Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS) is assessed using taxable income and will only be paid for a partnered period to eligible persons if partner income is known (reasonable estimate or taxable income details from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO)). Note: for the 2017-18 and later financial years, customers must meet the SIFS grandfathering provisions for payment of SIFS
  • FAO system compares the above amount with the partial reconciliation result and applies the more favourable income. This is known as the ex-partner income comparison process. A top-up can be paid if the latest result is more favourable. Family assistance debt offsetting may apply
  • A re-reconciliation will occur if adequate income details change after interim reconciliation has occurred. When this happens, the ex-partner income comparison is conducted again and the FAO system again applies the more favourable income. Before 8 December 2008, there were some problems with this process; see Item 8 in the Debts and other circumstances

See the Resources page for an example.

If the ex-partner's income falls into another interim reconciliation category, that is, 300 day income support and no FTB Part B paid, Child Disability Allowance (CDA)/Double Orphan Pension (DOP) saved rate, or low income review, interim reconciliation may occur at any time. For the 2014-15 or earlier financial years, forced reconciliation occurred in the November of the lodgement year.

4

Customer separated at 30 June of lodgement year + Read more ...

This processing applies for the 2002-2003 financial year onwards only for FTB/CCB reconciliation and FTB lump sum claims.

The partner is treated as a previous partner for reconciliation purposes.

FTB/CCB reconciliation will occur using the previous partner's adjusted taxable income (ATI) if known. Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS) is assessed using taxable income and will only be paid for a partnered period to eligible persons if partner income is known (reasonable estimate or taxable income details from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO)). Note: for the 2017-18 and later financial years, customers must meet the SIFS grandfathering provisions for payment of SIFS.

A non-lodgement exemption is only to be coded if the customer advises the previous partner is not required to lodge a tax return for the relevant year. See Recording not required to lodge advice and previous year income.

Interim reconciliation occurs after the end of the lodgement year (from 1 July of the extended lodgement year) if only previous partner actual adjusted taxable income (AATI) is required, using the Most Recent Estimate (MRE) for the previous partner for the relevant year. Debt offsetting may apply.

The customer is not included in the non-lodger process if only the previous partner's actual adjusted taxable income (AATI) is required.

If an FTB lump sum claim for the 2012-2013 or later financial years is pending (awaiting partner income) when the separation is notified, and a Financial Year Estimate (FYE) has not been provided, the claim will remain pending until actual income is received or a not effective claim determination has been made because income has not been confirmed within the allowed time.

If the customer contacts on or after 1 July of the extended lodgement year to advise of a separation that occurred before the end of the lodgement year, and FTB reconciliation has not occurred and only the previous partner's AATI is required, process a 'REA'ssessment activity on the Benefit Action (BA) screen, using 1 July of the relevant financial year as the date of effect (DOE) to trigger reconciliation.

Final reconciliation will occur if the previous partner's AATI is later received. An FTB top-up can be paid if either:

  • the first reconciliation results in a new decision and re-reconciliation occurs within 52 weeks of the customer being notified of that decision, or
  • the first reconciliation does not result in a new decision and re-reconciliation occurs by 30 June of the extended lodgement year

A debt may apply due to the previous partner's AATI.

Australian Taxation Office (ATO) FTB lump sum claims: if the customer claimed FTB as a lump sum through the ATO before 1 July 2009 for any part of the relevant financial year, the separation must also be coded on the ATO Non-lodger Partner Exemption (ATONLPE) screen. See Item 4 in the Debts and other circumstances table.

5

Customer separated during extended lodgement year + Read more ...

This processing applies for the 2002-2003 financial year onwards for FTB/CCB reconciliation and FTB lump sum claims.

From the 2012-2013 and ongoing financial years, if the customer separates from their partner after the end of the lodgement year, reconciliation cannot occur until the previous partner's actual income is obtained.

The non-lodger process will apply if:

  • a customer separates from their partner before the non-lodger debt is raised (September of the extended lodgement year), the non-lodger debt will be immediately written off if the previous partner has not confirmed their income
  • advice of a separation is received after the non-lodger debt has been raised, a temporary write-off of recovery of the non-lodger debt will occur, and any repayments made since the separation will be refunded to the customer
  • the customer re-partners with the previous partner, the write off will cease and recovery of the non-lodger debt will resume

Reconciliation can occur after actual income of both parties becomes available, however, supplements and top-ups will not be payable.

If an FTB lump sum claim for the 2012-2013 and later financial years is pending (awaiting partner income) when the separation is notified, it will remain pending until actual income is received or a not effective claim determination has been made because income has not been confirmed within the allowed time.

For the 2011-2012 and earlier financial years, the partner is treated as a previous partner for reconciliation purposes. FTB/CCB reconciliation will occur using the previous partner's actual adjusted taxable income (AATI) if known.

A non-lodgement exemption is only to be coded if the customer advises the previous partner is not required to lodge a tax return for the relevant year. See Recording not required to lodge advice and previous year income.

  • If the separation is advised before a non-lodger debt is raised, interim reconciliation will occur if only the ex-partner's AATI is needed
  • If the separation occurs or is advised after a non-lodger debt is raised, the debt is cancelled and interim reconciliation will occur if only the ex-partner's AATI is needed

Interim reconciliation uses the Most Recent Estimate (MRE) for the previous partner for the relevant year. Debt offsetting may apply.

If the customer contacts and reconciliation has not occurred and only the previous partner's AATI is required, process a 'REA'ssessment activity on the Benefit Action (BA) screen to trigger reconciliation.

Final reconciliation will occur if the previous partner's AATI is later received. An FTB top-up can be paid if either:

  • the first reconciliation results in a new decision and re-reconciliation occurs within 52 weeks of the customer being notified of that decision
  • the first reconciliation does not result in a new decision and re-reconciliation occurs by 30 June of the extended lodgement year

A debt may apply due to the previous partner's AATI.

ATO FTB lump sum claims: if the customer claimed FTB as a lump sum through the ATO before 1 July 2009 for any part of the relevant financial year, the separation must also be coded on the ATO Non-lodger Partner Exemption (ATONLPE) screen. See Item 4 in the Debts and other circumstances table.

6

Customer separated after extended lodgement year + Read more ...

This process only applies to the 2011-2012 and earlier financial years.

The partner is treated as a previous partner for reconciliation purposes. The previous partner's actual adjusted taxable income (AATI) must be obtained before reconciliation can occur if they are required to lodge a tax return.

If the previous partner is not required to lodge a tax return according to Australian Taxation Office (ATO) rules, reconciliation may occur using their estimated income if a non-lodger exemption is recorded for the previous partner on the customer's record. See Recording not required to lodge advice and previous year income.

The customer is included in the non-lodger process. However, a non-lodgement debt may be written off if caused solely by the previous partner not lodging their tax return. See Item 2 in the Debts and other circumstances table. This process should occur automatically when the separation is coded.

If actual income details are received for the previous partner, the non-lodger debt will be reassessed. However, a top-up can only be paid and the FTB Part A and Part B supplements included in the calculation (if entitled), if the tax return was lodged by 30 June of the extended lodgement year.

Debt offsetting may apply.

ATO FTB lump sum claims: if the person claimed FTB as a lump sum through the ATO before 1 July 2009 for any part of the relevant financial year, the separation must also be coded on the ATO Non-lodger Partner Exemption (ATONLPE) screen. See Item 4 in the Debts and other circumstances table.

7

Changes to system processing - customer separated at 30 June of relevant year + Read more ...

The partner is treated as an ex-partner for reconciliation purposes.

Clause 3A, Schedule 3 of the A New Tax System (Family Assistance) Act 1999 limits the effect of ex-partner income at reconciliation to either a top-up or nil adjustment. This means that ex-partner income cannot cause a debt for any period the customer was with their ex-partner in the financial year that the separation occurred. See the References page.

When family assistance reconciliation first occurred in 2001, the system solution used to apply Clause 3A was to suppress any debts that occurred in an ex-partner period. Although this ensured that ex-partner income could not cause a reconciliation debt, it also meant that other non-income related debts were suppressed (for example debts caused by circumstance changes, maintenance income). There have been a number of changes to the way the system treats ex-partner periods since family assistance reconciliation first commenced.

In the September and December 2005 releases (for FTB) and June 2006 (for CCB), a new process was introduced for 2002-2003 onwards so that only income related debts were prevented in ex-partner periods. The new process was known as the ex-partner period income comparison. See Item 8 in the Debts and other circumstances table. Unfortunately, there were some flaws in this process and in some circumstances it was still possible for a customer to incur an income related debt in an ex-partner period. Changes to correct this process were implemented in the December 2008 release.

The following is a summary of the system changes that have occurred for customers who were separated at 30 June of relevant year (ex-partner):

  • September and December 2005 system releases: ex-partner income comparison process introduced for FTB customers for 2002-2003 onwards
  • 16 June 2007 system release: partial reconciliation introduced and timing of interim reconciliation was changed for FTB customers
  • 10 September 2007 system release: partial reconciliation introduced and timing of interim reconciliation was changed for CCB reduced fees customers (to align with FTB)
  • 8 December 2008 system release: changes introduced to correct the ex-partner income comparison process for FTB and CCB

Re-reconciliation may result in a debt or reduced top-up due to loss of qualification or maintenance income.

Before 8 December 2008 system release, re-reconciliation may have also resulted in a debt due to new income details, but this was not correct processing. See Item 8 in the Debts and other circumstances table.

Debts and other circumstances of an FTB/CCB customer partnered at any time during relevant financial year

Table 2: This table describes debts and other circumstances that affect how reconciliation is applied to an FTB/CCB customer who was partnered at any time during the relevant financial year. Note: all CCB/Child Care Rebate (CCR) references relate to financial years prior to 2018/2019.

Item

Customer's circumstance

1

FTB/CCB processing for 2000-2001 and 2001-2002 + Read more ...

If the ex-partner's actual adjusted taxable income (AATI) is not available, their most recent estimate for the relevant year is used. If there is no estimate recorded, FTB is limited to the base rate for the partnered period. CCB is limited to the minimum rate.

Any negative adjustments for an ex-partner period are suppressed.

2

Non-lodger debts due to separated partner not lodging tax return as required for 2011-2012 and earlier financial years + Read more ...

These rules apply to customers who were partnered to a person during the relevant financial year but separated from that person after the lodgement year.

Amendments have been made to family assistance law that came into effect on 1 January 2006. The amendments ensure that a non-lodger debt that was raised after the end of the lodgement year should be set aside after separation if only the tax return of the previous partner is outstanding.

Setting aside non-lodger debt - customer separates during extended lodgement year

If the customer remains separated from the previous partner when the non-lodger debt is calculated, it will be finalised no debt, and interim reconciliation will occur based on the previous partner's most recent estimate.

If the customer subsequently reconciles with the previous partner, the non-lodger debt will be re-raised if adequate income is still unavailable for the previous partner.

For 2000-2001 there was no extended lodgement year. If a customer separated in 2002-2003 or later, the separated after extended lodgement year process applies. See Item 6 in the Reconciliation of FTB/CCB for partnered customers table.

Writing off non-lodger debt - customer separates after extended lodgement year

If the previous partner has not lodged their tax return by the time of separation, recovery of the non-lodger debt is temporarily written off.

The write off and refund applies from later of:

  • the date of separation
  • the date adequate income becomes available for the customer if it is not available at the time of separation

This process impacts FTB reconciliation for 2000-2001 and later years. (For 2000-2001 there was no extended lodgement year. If the customer separated in 2002-2003 or later, customers are treated the same as customers who separate after the extended lodgement year).

A refund is to be paid to the customer equal to the amount of the non-lodger debt that has been repaid since the later of:

  • the date of separation
  • the date actual income became available for the customer

Note: if the debt has been fully repaid prior to the separation, write-off will not occur.

The write-off and refund of repayments should occur automatically when the separation is coded or actual income becomes available for the customer (whichever occurs later). The debt will now show as 'pending recovery action'.

If reconciliation does not occur automatically, complete an FTB refresh activity on the Benefit Action (BA) screen using the date of effect as 1 July of the relevant financial year to trigger re-reconciliation.

3

Non-lodger debts due to separated partner not lodging tax return as required for 2012-2013 and financial year onwards + Read more ...

These rules apply to customers who were partnered to a person during the relevant financial year but separated from that person after the lodgement year.

If the customer is separated from a previous partner when the non-lodger debt is raised (September of the extended lodgement year), the debt is to be automatically written off for the reason 'Non-lodger Separated Couple' if only the previous partner has not confirmed their income.

If the customer notifies of a separation after a non-lodger debt is raised, the debt is to be automatically written off for the reason 'Non-lodger Separated Couple'.

Any repayments made after the date the debt is written off are to be refunded to the customer.

The write off and refund applies from later of:

  • the date of separation
  • the date adequate income becomes available for the customer if it is not available at the time of separation

If the customer later reconciles with their partner, the write-off will be revoked and the debt will be re-raised if adequate income has still not been received for the partner.

If a non-lodger debt is raised because adequate income for the customer is not available, the non-lodger debt will not be written off in the event of a separation being notified for the relevant financial year.

For further information regarding debt enquiries, see General recovery information.

4

Australian Taxation Office (ATO) FTB lump sum claim, customer separated after the relevant year + Read more ...

This applies to FTB lump sum claims lodged with the ATO before 1 July 2009.

FTB can no longer be claimed through the ATO.

ATO lump sum claim assessments do not recognise separations coded on the Marital Status (MS) screen.

Therefore, some ATO lump sum claimants who separated from their partner after 30 June of the lodgement year (where the previous partner has not lodged their tax return) may be included in the non-lodger process.

If the person contacts and advises that they have separated, the separation must be coded on the ATO Non-lodger Partner Exemption (ATONLPE) screen for the relevant ATO claim year. See Reassessment of Australian Taxation Office (ATO) lump sum Family Tax Benefit (FTB) claims.

5

Customer has current partner and an ex-partner in the relevant financial year + Read more ...

The appropriate income reconciliation rules will be used for each separate partnered period.

The rules are different for FTB and CCS.

For FTB:

  • for the ex-partner period, the rate comparison will occur, and
  • for any period outside the ex-partner period, including the period to 30 June, the combined adjusted taxable income of the customer and their 30 June partner (if applicable) is used to work out the customer's correct rate for that period only

Example:

Mary was partnered to Alan from 1 January - 20 February 2003. Alan is an ex-partner.

Mary has been partnered to Barry since 20 March 2003.

During the rest of the financial year, Mary was single.

Actual adjusted taxable income (AATI) for each person:

  • Mary -$35,000
  • Alan - $30,000
  • Barry - $40,000

Estimates as provided for the ex-partner period:

  • Mary - $40,000
  • Alan - $45,000

At final reconciliation, the appropriate income reconciliation rules are used for each period:

  • 1 July - 31 December 2002 - $35,000
  • 1 January - 20 February 2002 - $65,000 Ex-partner reconciliation rules apply for this period, see Item 2 in the Reconciliation of FTB/CCB for partnered customers table
  • 21 February - 19 March 2003 - $35,000
  • 20 March - 30 June 2003 - $75,000

For Child Care Subsidy (CCS):

  • Due to a change in legislation, CCS has two different reconciliation calculations for ex-partners (including partners who have died) for the 2018/19 year and from the 2019/20 year onwards. See Reconciliation of Child Care Subsidy (CCS) for more details.

6

Partner received income support + Read more ...

If the partner was in receipt of an income support payment during the time they were partnered, the customer's FTB Part A is not income tested for that period. Note: from 1 July 2016, FTB Part A supplement is subject to an income limit. This income limit applies to all customers, including income support customers.

FTB Part B is income tested for the partnered income support period using the income of the lower earner, excluding a quarantine period if the lower earner had a return to work.

For an ex-partner period, special processing rules apply. See Item 2 in the Reconciliation of FTB/CCB for partnered customers table.

For a partnered period, CCB will be paid at the maximum rate for any week the partner was receiving an income support payment.

7

Customer or partner dies within the relevant financial year + Read more ...

For reconciliation to take place when the customer or partner has died during the relevant financial year, the actual adjusted taxable income (AATI) of the late customer or partner needs to be annualised to apply the annual income tests for FTB and CCB for the period they were partnered.

Estimated adjusted taxable income is not annualised by the system as this is already an annual amount as it is an estimate of income for the whole financial year.

The combined AATI of the customer and partner includes the annualised actual income amount for the late customer or partner for the time they were partnered.

Ex-partner processing rules apply for the period the late customer or partner was partnered to the deceased, the same as for separated partners as outlined in the relevant section above.

8

Ex-partner period income comparison + Read more ...

The ex-partner period income comparison is performed whenever an interim or final reconciliation result is calculated for customers who separated from their partner during the relevant financial year.

This comparison is designed to ensure that the customer does not incur an income related overpayment in an ex-partner period.

First ex-partner period income comparison

The FAO system compares the customer's entitlement using income estimates provided during the relevant year with their entitlement using adequate income. For FTB lump sum claims, the system compares the customer's entitlement using the grant income with their entitlement using new (non-grant) income.

The income used is that which results in the most favourable outcome for the customer. This is then known as the favourable income.

The FAO Calculation Period Income Summary (FICS) screen displays the income used as a result of the ex-partner period income comparison process and includes a description of the income that was used. For example:

  • Ex-partner Comparison: Favourable (Adequate)

Subsequent ex-partner period income comparisons

Another comparison will occur every time there is a re-reconciliation.

From 8 December 2008, the FAO system compares the customer's entitlement using the new adequate income with their entitlement using the income deemed most favourable in the previous reconciliation, and again uses the more favourable income.

System limitation prior to 8 December 2008

The system was only able to compare Estimates As Provided with the latest Adequate Income. This meant that in some circumstances the customer was disadvantaged. This occurred most commonly where the customer's and/or ex-partner's Adequate Income has changed.

See the Resources page for an example.

A clean-up of cases where the customer was disadvantaged due to this system limitation was conducted in early 2009. Cases identified before the clean-up were manually adjusted.

Ex-partner period income comparison overrides

In some cases it will be necessary to override the income deemed most favourable in an ex-partner period, for example, where a customer has been disadvantaged due to the system limitation described above.

This is done using the FTB Ex-Partner Period Override (FEXPO) and CCB Ex-Partner Period Override (KEXPO) screens. Access to update these screens is limited to selected network and National Support Office staff, but all staff will be able to view the screens.

If an override has been coded, this will be displayed on the FICS screen as Override.

If income details or an ex-partner period change after an override has been coded, the record will be selected for manual intervention so that the override income can be updated if required.
