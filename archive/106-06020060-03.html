This page contains information on determining and verifying Australian citizenship for Australian Residence purposes.

Determining Australian citizenship

Determining Australian citizenship

Checking immigration/residence screens, country and date of birth

Table 1: This table describes the process and screens to check for determining Australian Citizenship.

Step

Action

1

Customer advises that they and/or their partner (if applicable) are Australian citizens + Read more

This procedure is normally restricted to a service centre Service Officer in relation to a new claim. However, if an activity lifts a residence quarantine, it may be actioned by a Smart Centre Service Officer if the customer's information is available using the datalink with the Department of Home Affairs.

The new claim process will attempt a datalink with the Department of Home Affairs. If successful and the information is available, the datalink will automatically record a customer's grant of Australian citizenship.

Select the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen. Is the Customer Immigration link status: field ACTIVE?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, as field displays INACTIVE status or blank:
    • Code the customer's country of birth in the Country of Birth: field if required
    • Code 'Y'es, 'N'o or 'U'nknown in the Lived or travelled outside Australia since 1 Sept 1994: field
    • If the customer has travel documents, record on this screen
    • Code 'AP' for Australian passports, 'OP' for passports issued by any country other than Australia or 'DI' Documents of Identity issued by Australia which are not Australian passports in the Type: field
    • Record details of the document in the Document Number: field
    • Complete the Country of Issue: field
    • Press [Enter] and a link with the Department of Home Affairs will be activated
    • Take activity to Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise

If the datalink is not successful a message will display at the top of the screen.

If the datalink is successful data will be updated to RSIMME, Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) and Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screens as appropriate.

2

Check RSLEG and RSIMME screens for proof of citizenship details + Read more

Where information is automatically recorded by the Department of Home Affairs datalink, a channel code next to the information will be recorded with 'IMM'. When immigration data has been recorded manually by International Services (CIS) staff, a channel code of 'IMC' is recorded.

  • To check if Australian citizenship details have been recorded, go to the RSLEG screen and check the Country of citizenship: field
  • To check if Australian passport details have been recorded, go to the RSIMME screen and check if the Type: field has been recorded with 'AP'

Note: if a customer and/or their partner does not have a record of Australian citizenship on RSLEG but has a permanent visa or is a protected SCV holder, this can be used for qualification purposes instead of Australian citizenship.

  • If there is an immigration record of the person as an Australian citizen, on RSLEG with a channel code of 'IMM' or 'IMC' Australian citizenship has been confirmed and recorded. Procedure ends here
  • If Australian citizenship is verified by an Australian passport on RSIMME, the date of effect needs to be determined see Step 10 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship table
  • If there is no record of Australian citizenship as above but there is a permanent visa or protected SCV status, go to Step 3
  • If the datalink is not successful for the customer, go to Step 4

3

No record of citizenship but permanent visa or protected SCV status is confirmed + Read more ...

A customer's payment is not affected by whether they and/or their partner are an Australian citizen, unless they are subject to a Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP). A person can receive an exemption from the NARWP if they are an Australian citizen.

Check the list of payments subject to NARWP requirements and if possible identify if the customer has already served the NARWP.

Is the customer potentially not eligible for a payment due to a NARWP?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, if citizenship cannot be proven (for example, when the Australian citizenship cannot be validated edits occur) but a permanent visa can be confirmed, this should be recorded rather than making the customer needlessly produce documents
    • Explain to the customer that their permanent visa or protected SCV status has been recorded rather than their Australian citizenship but this will not affect their ongoing entitlement. Advise them if they want to lodge verification of citizenship they may do so but it isn't needed at this time
    • Record the customer's or partner's citizenship as their country of birth and the relevant visa subclass. For assistance coding the information, see Recording legal residence status
    • Continue with the relevant activity
    • Procedure ends here

4

Residing in Australia prior to 26 January 1949 + Read more ...

Was the person whose citizenship is being coded born before 26 January 1949 and were they residing in Australia or Norfolk Island on 26 January 1949?

  • Yes, the person is automatically an Australian citizen
    • Ensure the Country of Residence (CRES) screen reflects the correct date the customer commenced residing in Australia. Further verification of residence is not required. See Step 10 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship
    • If the person was residing on Norfolk Island on that date, see Step 1 in the Requesting verification of Australian citizenship
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Born in Papua New Guinea (PNG) prior to 16 September 1975 + Read more ...

Was the person whose citizenship is being coded born in Papua New Guinea (PNG) before 16 September 1975?

  • Yes, the customer must provide either a PNG birth certificate, Australian birth certificate, Australian passport or Australian citizenship certificate for the person as proof
    • If any of these have already been recorded on the POI Historical Details (POIH) screen, Identity Summary screen or RSIMME screen, see Step 10 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship table
    • If not already recorded and the customer is providing one of these as proof, see Step 8 in the Requesting verification of Australian citizenship table
    • If not already recorded and the customer cannot provide verification, see Step 1 in the Requesting verification of Australian citizenship table
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Born in Australia prior to 20 August 1986 + Read more ...

Was the person whose citizenship is being coded born in Australia before 20 August 1986?

  • Yes, the person is automatically an Australian citizen. The details of the customer's birth certificate, Australian passport, or Australian citizenship certificate must be recorded
    • If any of these have already been recorded on the POIH screen, Identity Summary screen or RSIMME screen, see Step 10 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship table
    • If not already recorded and the customer is providing one of these as proof (for example a Norfolk Island birth certificate), see Step 8 in the Requesting verification of Australian citizenship table
    • If not already recorded and the customer cannot provide verification, see Step 1 in the Requesting verification of Australian citizenship table
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Born in Australia or Norfolk Island after 20 August 1986 + Read more ...

Was the person whose citizenship is being coded born in Australia after 20 August 1986 and was:

  • at least one of their parents an Australian citizen, permanent visa holder or protected SCV holder, or
  • their parent a New Zealand SCV holder (non-protected) and the child was born between 1 September 1994 and 26 February 2001?
    • Yes, go to Step 8
    • No, go to Step 9

8

Born in Australia or Norfolk Island after 20 August 1986 and meets other criteria + Read more ...

To establish Australian citizenship for Australian Residence purposes the customer will either need to provide an:

  • Australian passport (current or expired); or
  • Australian birth certificate and verification that one of their parents meet the requirements above

Note: if the person was born on Norfolk Island, the customer should provide either an Australian passport or Australian (or Norfolk Island) birth certificate and verification that one of their parents was an Australian citizen or permanent visa holder or official Norfolk Island resident at that time.

  • If they have provided their Australian passport, record the details on the RSIMME screen and refresh the link with the Department of Home Affairs for the Australian citizenship to be accepted. See Step 10 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship table
  • If the customer has provided their Australian or Norfolk Island birth certificate and proof of their parent's residence status at the time of their birth, verification will need to be manually recorded by CIS. See Step 9 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship table

9

Born in Australia after 20 August 1986 and residing in Australia up to age 10 + Read more ...

Was the person whose citizenship is being coded born in Australia after 20 August 1986 and did they clearly reside in Australia up until their 10th birthday?

A person can be an Australian resident if they were born in Australia and have lived here for 10 years regardless of what type of visa is held by the parent. If it is unclear if the child has lived in Australia for the necessary 10 years (e.g. has a long absence), then it is best to obtain confirmation from the Department of Home Affairs.

Note: the same rule applies to a person who was born on Norfolk Island and lived on Norfolk Island and/or Australian until their 10th birthday.

  • Yes, the customer will either need to provide an Australian passport (current or expired) or Australian citizenship certificate or Australian or Norfolk Island birth certificate and verification that they lived in Australia and/or Norfolk Island for 10 years after birth. Verification can be from datalink travel records or school reports etc. The residence for the 10 years must be recorded on the Country of Residence (CRES) screen. See Coding the Country of Residence (CRES) screen for assistance
    • If any of these have already been recorded on the POIH screen, Identity Summary screen or RSIMME screen, see Step 10 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship table
    • If not already recorded and the customer is providing one of these as proof, see Step 8 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship table
    • If not already recorded and the customer cannot provide verification, see Step 1 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship table
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Born in Australia after 20 August 1986 and not residing in Australia up to age 10 + Read more ...

Was the person whose citizenship is being coded born in Australia or Norfolk Island after 20 August 1986 and has not lived in Australia up until their 10th birthday?

  • Yes, the customer must provide either an Australian passport or Australian citizenship certificate as proof. Note: an Australian or Norfolk Island birth certificate is not sufficient evidence and cannot be accepted as proof of citizenship
    • If any of these have already been recorded on the POIH screen, RSIMME screen or Identity Summary Screen. See Step 10 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship table
    • If not already recorded and the customer is providing one of these as proof, see Step 8 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship table
    • If not already recorded and the customer cannot provide verification, see Step 1 in the Requesting verification of Australian citizenship table
  • No, see Step 1 in the Requesting and recording verification of Australian citizenship table

4

Verification of Citizenship is not immediately available + Read more ...

Is the reason for updating citizenship one of the following:

  • as part of the First Contact Concessions, Carers and Seniors (FCCCS) workflow
  • as part of a verbal claim for a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC), or
  • to prevent cancellation for a residence related reason due to an update (for example. Portability interview, Health Care Card reissue)?
    • Yes, verification of citizenship is not immediately required. Continue with the activity and advise customer to provide documents as follow up if necessary. Procedure ends here
    • No, documents must be provided before the claim or update can be finalised
      • If a customer has the documents at home, advise them to provide the documents to the Service Centre. Record contact and any relevant information in a DOC for the Service Centre
      • If the customer does not have the documents at home, record contact and any relevant information in a DOC for the Service Centre
      • Procedure ends here

5

Further methods of verifying citizenship + Read more ...

Centrelink can assist customers to verify their or their partner's citizenship.

It is not necessary for a customer and/or their partner to be Australian citizens for the customer to be residentially qualified for a Centrelink administered payment. A customer may also be the holder of a permanent visa or a protected SCV holder. A customer may also be residentially qualified for Special Benefit, family assistance and concession cards if they have a certain temporary visas that qualify customers for some payments. A customer may also qualify for Parental leave payments, family assistance and concession cards (excluding Ex Carer Allowance Health Care Card (EHC)) if they are a non-protected SCV holder.

If the customer doesn't have any verification, Centrelink can assist a customer to verify both the citizenship and/or permanent visa status for themselves and/or their partner.

A request for verification of citizenship should be sent to CIS.

If the customer states they or their partner (as appropriate) are an Australian citizen, coding the country of citizenship as 'AU' will generally create an edit in the new claim or benefit transfer activity unless an identity confirmation exemption has been recorded or a suitable confirmation document is recorded on the POIH or Identity Summary screens or the datalink has verified Australian citizenship. If the edits occurs after procedures have been followed to this point, contact CIS.

Note: if any of the following applies, it is generally not necessary to record and verify a person's Australian citizenship before granting their claim:

  • the person is a permanent visa or protected SCV holder or
  • the person qualifies for the payment or card as a non-protected SCV holder or has a certain temporary visas that qualify customers for some payments and
  • if the person is claiming a NARWP affected payment, it has already been served or the customer is exempt from the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) without reliance on their Australian citizenship

If none of the above apply, go to Step 6.

6

Request verification of citizenship from CIS + Read more ...

For verification of citizenship:

In Process Direct:

  • go to More Options icon to generate an ‘International Services’, ‘Immigration Datalink investigation Service Request’. See Process Direct navigation and common screens for instructions on creating a third party referral

In Customer First:

  • go to Fast Note and select 'Centrelink International Services'
  • select ‘Residence’
  • select ‘Intl - verify citizenship request'

In Customer Record and Mainframe / ISIS:

  • create a DOC with the summary line Citz to be verified by International Services using the keywords CIS and INVIRN.

Note: do not complete the DOC.

Include the following information on the DOC:

  • PPT: overseas passport number/country of issue if available
  • Name: correct spelling of name and if married include maiden name
  • Alias: all known aliases
  • PAR: if the customer arrived as a child with parents, full names and dates of births of parents

One or both parents' details are also required (from the customer's Australian or Norfolk Island birth certificate) if the customer was born on or after 20 August 1986 to one of the following:

  • an Australian citizen
  • permanent visa holder parent
  • an SCV holder (before 26 February 2001)
  • a protected SCV holder (on or after 26 February 2001)

CIS will investigate and advise if proof of the parent's official Norfolk Island resident status is required.

Once citizenship has been verified, CIS staff will record the Aust Citizenship Verification Ind: field on the Residence Savings (RSS) screen. This screen can only be coded by CIS staff. Hold the new claim or activity until the response has been received from CIS and then continue below.

Has citizenship been verified for the relevant person?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Person is not an Australian citizen or customer cannot provide evidence + Read more ...

  • The relevant person is not to be recorded as an Australian citizen if they were born outside Australia
  • If the person was born outside Australia, record the country of birth in the Country of Citizenship: field on RSLEG screen. If the customer has proof of a permanent residence visa, or RSLEG has a record of a permanent residence visa received from the Department of Home Affairs, use this rather than the Australian citizenship
  • It the person was born on Norfolk Island before 20 August 1986, record their country of citizenship as Australia. If after this date, record the country of citizenship of the customer's parent(s). Do not record the country of citizenship as Norfolk Island
  • If the person's first arrival was before 1 September 1994, accept permanent residence as the Department of Home Affairs records are incomplete, unless there is reason to believe otherwise. Go to the RSLEG screen and record the country of citizenship as the country of birth and code '111' in the Subclass Code: field with 1 September 1994 in the Grant Date: field
  • If the person's first arrival was after 1 September 1994 and no records can be located, the claim should not be granted until the customer provides some proof of legal residence status as the Department of Home Affairs records after 1 September 1994 are very good
  • Record information and reasons for decision on a DOC
  • Continue with the new claim or activity

Procedure ends here.

8

Coding proof of citizenship + Read more

  • An Australian passport must be recorded either on the RSIMME screen or the POI screen as part of Alternative Identity confirmation for the Australian citizenship to be accepted
  • If the Service Officer is adding Australian passport details to the RSIMME screen it is necessary to refresh the link with the Department of Home Affairs to verify the passport via the datalink
  • An Australian citizenship certificate must be recorded on the POI screen as part of Alternative Identity confirmation for the Australian citizenship to be accepted
  • An Australian birth certificate must be recorded on the POI screen as part of Alternative Identity confirmation for the birth certificate to be accepted
  • An Australian passport, citizenship certificate or birth certificate can only be recorded on the Identity Summary screen using the guided procedure. One of these documents cannot be added to the Identity Summary screen if the customer's identity has already been confirmed using a different commencement of identity document or recorded as Transitional

Note: if any of these documents cannot be recorded as above (for example a different commencement or identity document has been coded on they have only been recorded on the Identity Summary screen using the guided procedure, or the customer has provided a Norfolk Island birth certificate and an Australian passport cannot be verified, CIS staff will need to record the verification.

Have any of these documents been recorded?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Australian citizenship needs to be recorded on RSS screen + Read more

Ensure verification document(s) have been scanned to the customer's record.

In Process Direct:

  • go to More Options icon to generate an ‘International Services’, ‘Immigration Datalink investigation Service Request’. See Process Direct navigation and common screens for instructions on creating a third party referral

In Customer First:

  • go to Fast Note and select 'Centrelink International Services'
  • select 'Residence'
  • select 'RSS coding required for AU citizen'

In Customer Record or Mainframe/ISIS:

  • Create a DOC advising the verification provided and requesting that verification to be recorded on the Residence Savings (RSS) screen using the keywords CIS and INVIRN.

Provided citizenship has been verified, CIS staff will record the Aust Citizenship Verification Ind: field on the Residence Savings (RSS) screen. This screen can only be coded by CIS staff. Hold the new claim or activity until the response has been received from CIS and then continue below.

Has citizenship been verified on RSS for the relevant person?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, clarify any further requirements with CIS. Procedure ends here

10

Customer/partner is an Australian citizen and evidence has been provided and recorded + Read more ...

  • Code 'AU' or 'Australia' in the Country of Citizenship: field on the RSLEG screen if not already coded by the Department of Home Affairs datalink
  • Code the relevant date of event for Australian citizenship
  • Refresh the link with the Department of Home Affairs on the RSIMME screen if Australian passport details are not recorded with an 'IMM' channel
  • Finalise activity or continue with new claim (if relevant)
  • Record details on a DOC
