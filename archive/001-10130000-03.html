Identifying Targeted Compliance Framework or Job Seeker Compliance Framework decisions

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Job seeker contacts about a decision relating to non-compliance with compulsory requirements + Read more ...

Process Direct

For customers who:

  • submit an online claim or a Service Officer has completed the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) workflow:
    • when in the claim, key BIPCW in the Super Key field
    • select Enter
    • the Participation Compliance Hub screen will display
  • currently receive a payment:
    • select Customer Summary from the Process Direct landing page
    • enter the customer's CRN, select Go
    • in the customer's record key BIPCW in the Super Key field, select Enter
    • the Participation Compliance Hub screen will display

When a job seeker contacts Services Australia to request a review of a decision, the decision that they do not agree with must be identified so the job seeker can be referred to the appropriate team or organisation.

Decision made by the provider

Job seekers who contact about a decision made by a provider or triggered by the Employment Services System (ESS) that are not made under social security law should be referred to their provider for an explanation. For example, decisions to apply a demerit or the job seeker's Targeted Compliance Framework zone.

Decisions on non-payability are sent to Services Australia electronically. Participation suspensions are automatically applied by the Services Australia system when a non-compliance event is reported.

Providers have responsibility for notifying job seekers of their reconnection requirements, basing them on guidance provided by the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations. They electronically notify Services Australia when the conditions for lifting the payment suspension have been met. The Services Australia system automatically responds to the electronic notification to restore payment, or cancels payment if notification that the conditions have been met is not received within 29 days of the deemed date of notification.

Job seekers who request a review of these decisions are required to talk with their provider about the non-payability decision first. When the provider has explained the decision, completed the initial reconsideration of the decision, and the job seeker is still not satisfied with the outcome, the provider may refer the job seeker to the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations. If the job seeker is still not satisfied with the outcome, Services Australia manage the remainder of the review process.

Decisions made by Services Australia

When an unemployment preclusion period or Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP) has been applied at new claim, a Service Officer with UNPP security resource is responsible for explaining the decision.

If the job seeker subsequently contacts a Service Centre or Smart Centre for a further explanation, refer the job seeker to the Participation Solutions Team (PST) to explain the decision.

The PST is responsible for explaining:

  • Targeted Compliance Framework decisions relating to the application of a penalty for a mutual obligation failure, work refusal failure or an unemployment failure
  • Job Seeker Compliance Framework decisions relating to decisions to apply a No Show, No Pay failure, connection failure, non-attendance failure, reconnection failure, serious failure or a UNPP

For further information on job seeker compliance system decisions, see:

  • Targeted Compliance Framework
  • Job Seeker Compliance Framework

2

Job seeker has contacted Smart Centre Telephony or a service centre about a decision Services Australia made or manages the review process for + Read more ...

Refer the job seeker to the Participation Solutions Team (PST) using the appropriate method below:

  • PST phone if job seeker is in a service centre, or
  • Transfer to PST if job seeker is calling from an external source

Note: all internal transfers to the PST should be conducted via unannounced (cold) transfer.

3

Job seeker has contacted Participation Solution Team (PST) + Read more ...

Where a job seeker requests a review/explanation of a decision made by Services Australia, a decision may be explained by all suitability skilled staff. PST- skilled Service Officers (WNPPD) are the Subject Matter Experts (SME) that undertake a quality check and review of the decision.

PST-skilled Service Officers (PFDEC) must refer the job seeker to a SME after explaining a decision where a quality check and review of decision are required. Transfer to the PST Virtual Support Queue (VSQ) via a warm hand-off.

The PST-skilled Service Officer WNPPD/SME undertakes a quality check and review of the decision, irrespective of where the original decision was made.

The SME is responsible for:

  • establishing the reason why the job seeker is requesting a review of the decision
  • collecting any further information or evidence relevant to the matter
  • updating the job seeker's record if required
  • an explanation of the decision and explaining the review process
  • completing the Internal Review/Explanation script:
    • record site code as PST
    • where the job seeker is requesting a review of decision for more than one failure event incurred on the same date, run the script for each subsequent failure. In the Original Decision field select 'Other' and in the Comments field record that a review has already been recorded for a different failure incurred on the same date
  • if the job seeker is not satisfied with the outcome, refer to an Authorised Review Officer (ARO) for formal review and advise the job seeker they are required to meet all of their compulsory requirements while the review of the decision is being conducted

Note: where the PST-skilled Service Officer (WNPPD) is the original decision maker, the request for a review must be referred to another PST-skilled Service Officer (WNPPD) by transferring the call to the PST Virtual Support Queue (VSQ) via a warm hand-off.

When a decision is varied as a result of a quality check or formal review, the decision is implemented by a PST-skilled Service Officer with a WNPPD security resource. This ICT security resource is required to access the review workflow in the Participation Compliance Hub.

For more information, see Request for an explanation or application for a formal review.

Appealable decisions and review process for Targeted Compliance Framework

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Decisions that are not made under social security law + Read more ...

The following decisions are made by providers or triggered by the Employment Services System (ESS) based on policy provided by the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations:

  • Decision to record a demerit because a valid reason has not been provided
  • The Targeted Compliance Framework zone the job seeker is in
  • The number of demerits accrued within 6 active months
  • When a Capability Interview or Capability Assessment is required

See the Resources page for more information.

2

Decisions made by the provider under social security law + Read more ...

Providers are delegated to make the following decisions under social security law:

  • Non-payability decisions as a result of a mutual obligation or work refusal failure
  • Conditions that must be met (reconnection requirements) before a suspension period ends
  • Decision to restore participation payments following a participation suspension

See the Resources page for more information.

3

Decisions made by Services Australia + Read more ...

Services Australia makes the following Targeted Compliance Framework decisions under social security law:

  • Applying a financial penalty as a result of a mutual obligation failure, including the amount of the penalty and the instalment period this penalty affects
  • Cancelling payment as a result of a mutual obligation, work refusal or unemployment failure, or failure to comply with a reconnection requirement within 29 days. This includes the date of effect of the payment cancellation
  • Application of an unemployment preclusion period or post-cancellation non-payment period, including start date and duration of this period

The outcome of a Capability Assessment is not a decision made under social security law and cannot be appealed. When the job seeker is not satisfied with the outcome of a Capability Assessment on their Targeted Compliance Framework zone, they should be referred to their provider to discuss their requirements.

See the Resources page for more information.

4

Reviewing a mutual obligation failure applied where job seeker accepted financial penalty without a compliance investigation + Read more ...

A decision to accept the financial penalty for a mutual obligation failure as part of finalising reporting, rather than contacting Services Australia for a compliance investigation, does not preclude a job seeker from seeking a decision explanation and requesting a review of this decision.

If, after the decision has been explained, a review of a decision is requested, further available information and/or evidence should be collected and considered.

Review of decision is requested within 13 weeks of mutual obligation failure

PST-skilled Service Officers should take the following action:

  • Key BIPCW in the Super Key field, select Enter
  • The Participation Compliance Hub screen will display
  • Select Compliance Workflow for the mutual obligation failure
  • The Completed Compliance Actions Screen will display
  • Select Review Decision
  • The Review decision workflow will display
  • Complete the appropriate fields, select Next
  • From the Completed/provider compliance actions table, select Compliance Workflow for the mutual obligation failure
  • The Completed Compliance Actions screen will display
  • Select Review Decision to commence the investigation:
    • When the Review Decision link is selected the workflow will automatically change the failure status from 'Applied - job seeker declined investigation (AAP)' to 'Investigation' (INV)
    • Record the failure status reason 'Investigated auto-applied failure' (AAP), create a failure activity on the record and launch the Compliance Investigation workflow
    • The penalty start and end date will continue to display on the record while the failure is under investigation
  • Use the Compliance Investigation workflow to record any additional evidence provided:
    • A message will display the first time the Key Information page accessed advising 'Investigation workflow has been launched to review the decision of a failure applied without investigation'
  • Where there is sufficient evidence to change the decision under review, record the appropriate rejection reason:
    • When finalised, the failure status will update to revoke rather than rejected. The decision text in the workflow will display 'Failure revoked following investigation'
    • After the failure is revoked the financial penalty applied will be automatically removed and the penalty start and end date will no longer display. This action may result in the system automatically adjusting financial penalties for mutual obligation failures in the same failure group
  • If there is not enough evidence to vary the decision, the failure must be applied:
    • When finalised, the failure status will change to 'Applied (APP)'
    • The existing penalty start and end date applied will not change

If there is not enough evidence to vary a decision, or the customer is not satisfied with the new decision, the PST-skilled Service Officer then records the job seeker's review request via the Internal Review/Explanation script and completes a quality check of the decision.

Review of decision is requested after 13 weeks of mutual obligation failure

If the job seeker requests a review more than 13 weeks after notification, the Review Decision must not be selected as this will start the failure investigation and change the failure status to investigation (INV).

5

Adjustment to financial penalties as a result of a mutual obligation failure + Read more ...

When a decision to apply a penalty for a failure is varied, this may impact the penalty applied for other failures within the failure group. A failure group is a sequential series of mutual obligation failures attracting a 1st, 2nd, or 3rd financial penalty. Each financial penalty for a mutual obligation failure in the group is dependent on, or influences, another penalty in the same group.

A new failure group begins each time a job seeker returns to the financial penalty zone after serving a post-cancellation non-payment period.

Financial penalties for other failures that were not part of the failure group will not, however, be adjusted. This policy is in place to protect job seekers from the potential rippling effect that could be caused and issues that would then be driven out of payments retrospectively cancelling.

The job seeker's financial penalties for any subsequent penalties in the group will be adjusted automatically by the system.

Review and appeals decisions for Job Seeker Compliance Framework

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Non-Attendance Reports (NAR), connection, non-attendance, reconnection or No Show No Pay failures + Read more ...

For decisions relating to NARs, connection, non-attendance, reconnection or No Show, No Pay failures, the Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer will consider any additional information and/or evidence and, if applicable, record any changes to the decision using the Review workflow in the Participation Compliance Hub.

The job seeker may request a review of the following decisions:

  • suspend payment following the receipt of a NAR, or generation of a connection, non-attendance failure, reconnection or No Show, No Pay failure
  • maintain a suspension following receipt of a NAR until the job seeker attends a re-engagement appointment with their provider or the provider advises they are no longer required to reconnect
  • apply a connection, non-attendance failure, reconnection or No Show, No Pay failure
  • cancel payment when the job seeker has failed to re-engaged or fails to accept a reconnection requirement following a participation payment suspension
  • cancel payment when the job seeker has not contacted their provider following receipt of a NAR

NARs: when a job seeker contacts Services Australia to request a review of a suspension or cancellation decision resulting from the submission of a NAR, the review request must be accepted. In these instances, the job seeker should also be advised to contact their provider during the review process to have any outstanding issues resolved so payment can be restored as quickly as possible.

2

Serious failures or Unemployment Non-Payment Periods (UNPP) + Read more ...

The job seeker may request a review of the following decisions:

  • a decision to apply a serious failure or UNPP
  • a decision that a job seeker is not eligible for a waiver of a serious failure penalty or UNPP, or
  • the date used to grant a waiver for a serious failure penalty or UNPP

The PST-skilled Service Officer is responsible for:

  • establishing the reason why the job seeker is requesting a review of the decision
  • providing the job seeker with a clear explanation of the decision
  • explaining to the job seeker their possible eligibility for a waiver
  • collecting any additional evidence
  • granting Payment Pending Review (PPR) where appropriate
  • referring the case to a Subject Matter Expert (SME) for a quality check and review of the decision

When enough evidence is provided to revoke the failure, the PST-skilled Service Officer (WNPPD or UNPP) is responsible for updating the failure status and reason.

When the request for review is received by a PST-skilled Service Officer with PFDEC only, they will need to transfer the call to a PST SME, via the appropriate Comprehensive Compliance Assessment (CCA) finalisation queue, who will conduct the quality check and review.

3

Payment Pending Review (PPR) provisions + Read more ...

PPR provisions apply when a job seeker is currently serving a UNPP or serious failure period that is being quality checked by a Subject Matter Expert (SME) or under review by an Authorised Review Officer (ARO) or the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) first review. Payment may also continue during an AAT second review but only if the AAT has issued a stay order against the imposition of the penalty. Job seekers receiving payment under the PPR provisions are required to comply with all mutual obligation requirements. The PST-skilled Service Officer (WNPPD) is responsible for updating the failure status using the Participation Compliance Hub.

For more information, see Payment Pending Review (PPR) of decision to apply a penalty for non-compliance.
