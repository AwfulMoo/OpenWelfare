Identifying a Simplified Billing claim + Read more ...

Simplified Billing claims relate to hospital services which may include surgical, anaesthetic, diagnostic, pathology and other in hospital services.

  • log on to Mainframe
  • key Medicare card number and press [Enter] for example, NHSI, 1234567891, patient name
  • locate the service on the patients history

Simplified Billing claim detailsdisplay above the service details on the patient history screen:

  • the letters SBIL in the item field
  • Minor ID or Registration number in the Provider field
  • the letters F, A, V or W in the P (Payee field):
  • F = fund claim EDI
  • A = billing agent EDI
  • V= ECLIPSE Provider/Fund
  • W= ECLIPSE Billing agent
  • Payee code 8 in the P (Payee) field, displayed with the service line
  • Payee code F in the P (Payee) field, displayed with the service line
  • Claim ID

3

Status of a Claim or Application + Read more ...

Service Officers can help callers enquiring about the status of a claim or application. It is important that a level of assurance is provided to the caller about their claim or application.

This does not include details about internal processes.

For example, the caller does not need to know about digitised electronic workflow systems, fax to email services, scanning solutions or KPIs.

Advise the caller that the agency endeavours to process claims and applications as quickly as possible but it can be impacted by peaks in demand

Confirm with the caller that their claim has been received by the agency by searching the workflow

Do not advise the caller of processing dates.

4

ECLIPSE claim enquiries + Read more ...

ECLIPSE claims can be uniquely identified on the patient history by the claim ID allocated to the services. ECLIPSE claims have a % symbol at the end of the claim ID and the claim IDs are made up of the following characters: NNNNN%.

To access the claim ID, Service Officers must press [F2].

If an ECLIPSE claim is rejected in full, the claim details do not write to the patient history. Claims that are only partially rejected or fully paid will write to the patient history.

Service Officers can resolve enquiries relating to an ECLIPSE claim (claims with a payee code 8) rejection/assessment where the Medicare reason code is a 3 digit number, for example, 179. Where the rejected services are not present on the patient history, Service Officers must ask the caller what reason code was used to reject the claim.

Service Officers may be able to determine why the claim was rejected by referring to the list of reason codes.

Service Officers must warm transfer enquiries for ECLIPSE claims rejected with a 4 digit return code to the eBusiness Service Centre.

When an ECLIPSE claim is rejected in full, the rejected services do not appear on the patient's history. To determine the reason why the service was rejected, Service Officers must ask the caller what the rejected reason code was used to reject the claim. Service Officers may be able to determine why the claim was rejected from the list of Medicare reason codes.

See: Indicators, codes, modifiers and control lines for claims processing in Medicare

If unable to find the reason for rejection, Service Officers must escalate to a Programme Support Officer (PSO).

5

Rejection enquiry + Read more ...

When a call is received in relation to a Simplified Billing claim rejection enquiry:

  • check patient history to determine if and why services were rejected
  • check the specific 3 digit reason code

If the reason code is not indicated on the patient history, Service Officers must ask the caller what reason code was applied to the claim.

Service Officers may be able to determine why the claim was rejected by referring to the list of reason codes.

See: Indicators, codes, modifiers and control lines for claims processing in Medicare

  • determine if the assessment is correct by reading any relevant Text field messages by placing the cursor under the T in the TX field and pressing [PF1]
  • all assessing rules apply and must be followed, for example, multiple operation rule, diagnostic service rules, restrictions, time dependencies

See: General assessing information for Medicare

  • if applicable, request documentation or provide more direction on how to resubmit claims as required

If unable to find the reason for rejection, Service Officers must escalate to a Programme Support Officer (PSO).

6

Simplified Billing Escalation Procedure + Read more ...

If it is determined that the service does not display on the patient history, or Service Officers are unable to resolve the enquiry at the point of contact, the call should be warm transferred to a PSO.

7

Payment Enquiry + Read more ...

If an enquiry is received from a health professional in relation to a payment or a request to adjust a previously paid Simplified Billing or ECLIPSE claim, they are to be directed to the private health insurer or billing agent who lodged the claim.

8

Simplified Billing Forms + Read more ...

Simplified Billing forms are available from the Services Australia website:

  • key Simplified Billing forms into the search box and select the magnifying mirror
  • scroll down and select the required form

Simplified Billing/ECLIPSE Adjustments

Private health insurers, billing agents and health professionals should use the Simplified Billing or ECLIPSE adjustment claim form (HW023) when an adjustment is required to a previously processed claim which needs to be amended with new/altered information. Health professionals must send the completed form to the private health insurer / billing agent to forward to the agency.

Screen shots from a private health insurer or billing agent’s software are not acceptable documentation for the replacement of an account. Service Officers must ensure that the private health insurer or billing agent is using the current HW023 form. Outdated forms must be returned requesting the current form to be completed.

Simplified Billing Late Lodgement

Health professionals, billing agents or private health insurers can complete the Application for late lodgement of a Simplified Billing claim for assigned Medicare benefits form (HW026). Health professionals must send the completed form to the private health insurer / billing agent to forward to the agency. Outdated HW026 forms must be returned requesting the current form be completed.

Simplified Billing Manual Claims batch header form

Private health insurers or billing agents use Simplified Billing manual claims batch header form (HW091) to submit manual Simplified Billing claims for assigned Medicare benefits. Outdated HW026 forms must be returned requesting the current form be completed.

The Resources page has a link to the form.

3

Claim Header + Read more ...

Key claim header details:

  • Minor Customer ID (Minor ID or Registration number)
  • Claim Number is the number allocated by the billing entity to identify the claim

Note: where no claim number is allocated processing Service Officers must return the claim to the private health insurer or billing agent:

Date of Lodgement (DOL) - the date the claim was received by Services Australia - DD MM YY

  • Customer Class:
  • MB for billing agent claims, and
  • MF for private health insurer claims
  • Option - the Option field will default to K, meaning that data can be keyed on the screen:
  • R to reopen already keyed claim, and
  • L to list previous entered claim
  • press [Enter]

The system will validate the header fields. If any errors are found, these will be displayed. If there are no errors, the system will register the claim and unprotect the data entry fields.

4

Key Simplified Billing claim details + Read more ...

From the invoice attached to the claim header, Key:

  • card number - the Medicare number listed on the account. Service Officers must confirm that the Medicare card issue number is correct at date of keying to ensure that services are not automatically rejected by background operator
  • pat - the patient’s first name or IRN
  • item - the item number allocated on the invoice
  • DOS - the date of service listed on the account. This field is also for the referral date
  • no - this field is used to denote the number of fields for radiotherapy and/or the number of patients the health professional has seen when claiming certain consult items, for example, item 33, 40
  • Prov Num - the Servicing Providers Provider number
  • charge - the amount the health professional has charged for the service
  • SCP - the Specimen Collection Point that has to accompany pathology items (for example, SAO1 S055)
  • LSPN - the Location Specific Practice Number that has to accompany diagnostic items (for example LSPN 123456)
  • R3 - an indication of Rule 3 Exemption can be recorded by keying Y in the R3 field
  • SD - self deemed indicator
  • RSN - service operators can force a claim into a PEND status by keying PND in this field
  • press [Enter]

To access LSPN, SCP, SD, RSN or R3 fields, press [F6] from the data entry screen.

If the patient has more than 1 service, the following fields may be duplicated using the INSERT function - Key SHIFT and INSERT:

  • Card number
  • Pat
  • Item
  • DOS
  • Prov Num
  • SCP
  • LSPN
  • SD
  • R3

5

Keying Referral Details + Read more ...

To key a set of referred or requested services, key the service line(s).

In the Item field, key:

  • DI = for Diagnostic services
  • PA = for Pathology services
  • SR = for Specialist consultations
  • in the DOS field, key the referral date
  • in the Prov Num field, key the referring Provider Number

If a health professional has performed more than 1 referred service (pathology or consultations or any other service that requires a referral) on the 1 day and they are listed on the same account, the referral line must state the number of services that were keyed. For example, instead of keying DI - DOS - Provider number, Key DI03 - DOS - Provider number. This is necessary to connect all the relevant item numbers to the referral line. For example, for 2 consultations, key SR02, For 3 pathology services, key PA03.

6

Insert or Delete a Service line + Read more ...

A service line can be inserted or deleted by:

  • inserting ‘I’ in the ‘A’ field and keying [PF6] to insert a line
  • inserting ‘D’ in the ‘A’ field and keying [PF6] to delete a line

Extra data entry fields can be accessed by keying the F6 key from the FEGN data entry screen.

7

Close Simplified Billing claim + Read more ...

Where no errors are found, the system will display <PF3 UPDATE>:

  • key F3 to update the claim
  • key F12 to exit, or
  • key F5 to refresh and return to the FEGN manual data entry screen

Once the claim is closed, the system will assess the claim. Where the system is able to resolve the claim, the claim will be finalised by background operator and will have the background operator number and source code allocated to it. Service Officers can view this using FCEI transaction suite. Where the system is unable to resolve the claim it will return it to pend status.

Service Officers must complete the pend processing using the FAPI transaction.

Table 7: process for Simplified Billing claims submitted for payment over 2 years from the date of service.

Step

Form requirements + Read more ...

Service Officers must ensure all fields on the Application for late lodgement form are complete. 2

Assess Late Lodgement Request + Read more ...

The operator must use the examples below to determine whether the late lodgement meets the criteria to either be accepted or not accepted by the delegate.

Acceptable

  • hospitalisation or serious long term medical condition and an extensive period of recovery for the health professional (however it would not be acceptable if the health professional was still practising during this period)
  • evidence to demonstrate that if a longer period is not allowed, there will be an impact on the ongoing viability of the practice, private health insurer or billing agent (certified copies of bank statements or tax returns)
  • a compliance audit on the health professional, private health insurer or billing agent carried out by the agency that has resulted in the need to resubmit claims

Not acceptable

  • administrative errors, which may include but not limited to:
    • poor book keeping
    • inadequate staffing/resources/training
    • an issue with software, system or computer malfunctions
  • private account has been previously raised and not paid (debt collecting)
  • declined work cover application
  • where no clear reason has been stated
  • where multiple reasons have been stated, therefore it is not clear of the true reason for late lodgement

Granted

If the operator recommends to the delegate that the late lodgement is to be granted, they must confirm the:

  • Application for late lodgement of a Simplified Billing claim for assigned Medicare benefits form (HW026) has been completed in full and signed
  • Invoice/account has been provided
  • Evidence has been provided to demonstrate:
    • the circumstances preventing lodgement within the 2 years, and
    • that if a longer period is not allowed, there will be impact on the ongoing viability of the practice, private health insurer or billing agent (for example, certified copies of bank statements or tax returns)
  • Go to Step 3

A Simplified Billing Manual Claims batch header is not required for manual claims submitted with an Application for late lodgement of Simplified Billing claim form.

Not granted

Where the operator recommends to the delegate that the late lodgement is to be not granted, go to Step 8.

Where an application is approved and an invoice/account is not supplied, or not enough supporting evidence is provided, the application must be returned to the private health insurer or billing agent using the Simplified Billing More information is needed letter template (z2162).

When the application for late lodgement has been received directly from the health professional, the application must be returned using the Simplified Billing cannot be processed letter template (z2160). This advises them their private health insurer or billing agent must lodge the application. It must be returned to the address listed in the Provider Directory System (PDS).

The Resources page has a link to the Simplified Billing letters templates and lists for Private Health Insurers and Approved Billing Agents including their postal addresses.

9

Delegate Assessment + Read more ...

All Simplified Billing late lodgement applications are assessed by the delegate of the Chief Executive Medicare. The delegate (TL5) considers to either:

  • extend the 2 year lodgement period if the acceptable criteria is met and evidence is provided, or
  • decline the late lodgement request if it does not meet the criteria and/or there is insufficient evidence

The delegate is to assess the details given to them and add notes in PaNDA to either approve or decline the late lodgement. This is then re-allocated to the operator in PaNDA for finalisation.

Is the late lodgement approved?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 11

10

  • Can transmit claim, send the Simplified Billing Late Lodgement has <not> been granted letter template (z2161) to the private health insurer or billing agent

Approved Simplified Billing late lodgement claim

Table 8: process for confirming Simplified Billing late lodgement claims after delegate approval.

Step

Action

1

Late Lodgement Claim received + Read more ...

When a late lodgement claim is received with text APPROVED GRANTED DDMMYY:

  • refer to the text with the late lodgement indicating the date of approval
  • using the approval date as a guide, locate the original Late Lodgement Claim file in the workflow by searching for the Medicare card number to locate the letter sent
  • verify the approval by looking at the notes added by the delegate

If the approval:

  • has been confirmed, process the claim. See table 2 Keying a Simplified Billing manual claim

Where a batch header is not provided or the claim is over 7 years old, the claim cannot be processed in mainframe and is required to be paid via manual Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT).
