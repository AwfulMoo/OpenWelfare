5

Partner's death actioned in entitlement period in which death occurred + Read more ...

Note: for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance (YA) customers, suitably skilled Service Officers are to key the Bereavement Payment using the Bereavement Assistance task selector in Process Direct.

In this case, the LBP is worked out for 6 entitlement periods plus the number of days between the date of death and the end of the first period.

LBP = [(CMCR - NR) x 6] plus [(CMCR - NR) x NDEP/14]

  • CMCR is the couple's combined fortnightly member of a couple rate payable had the person not died. For illness separated couples where the survivor receives a pension payment, Partner Allowance or Parenting Payment, the CMCR is the couple's fortnightly member of a couple rate payable had they been living together before the person died. Refer to How to work out the CMCR for an illness separated couple. The CMCR does not include the Coronavirus Supplement if the customer or deceased partner received it prior to the bereavement period
    Note: if the deceased was receiving War Widows Pension (WWP) and Income Support Supplement (ISS), the WWP from the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) is not included in the CMCR part of the calculation. See the Resources page for an example
  • NR is the surviving partner's new fortnightly single rate. If the deceased also received Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and the survivor is expected to be granted FTB, do not include in the survivor's new single rate the Rent Assistance (RA) component. When the survivor's claim for FTB is finalised RA will be paid with FTB rather than the income support payment. The NR also does not include the Coronavirus Supplement, where the survivor received it prior to the bereavement period
  • NDEP is the number of days from and including the date of death until the end of the entitlement period in which the death occurred

To calculate the LBP

  • Work out the difference between the CMCR and NR
  • Multiply the difference by 6 to get the LBP payable for 6 entitlement periods
  • Multiply the difference in the rates by NDEP and divide by 14 to get the LBP payable for the first entitlement period
  • Add the results from steps 2 and 3 to get the total LBP

For an ABSTUDY Living Allowance customer, see Step 1 in Table 3.

For other customers, see Step 1 in Table 4.

6

Partner's death actioned after entitlement period in which death occurred, couple living together before death + Read more ...

Note: for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance (YA) customers, suitably skilled Service Officers are to key the Bereavement Payment using the Bereavement Assistance task selector in Process Direct.

LBP = (CMCR - NR) x (7 - NEPED)

  • CMCR is the couple's combined fortnightly member of a couple rate payable had the person not died. The CMCR does not include the Coronavirus Supplement, where the customer or deceased partner received it prior to the bereavement period
  • NR is the surviving partner's new fortnightly single rate. If the deceased also received Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and the survivor is expected to be granted FTB, do not include in the survivor's new single rate the Rent Assistance (RA) component. When the survivor's claim for FTB is finalised RA will be paid with FTB rather than the income support payment. The NR also does not include the Coronavirus Supplement, where the survivor received it prior to the bereavement period
  • NEPED is the number of entitlement period end days for which the couple was paid at the member of a couple rate after and including the date of death

To calculate the LBP

  • Work out the difference between the 2 rates
  • Multiply the difference in rates by the number of paydays left (7 - NEPED)

For an ABSTUDY Living Allowance customer, see Step 1 in Table 3.

For other customers, see Step 1 in Table 4.

7

Both deaths within 14 weeks, notified at same time + Read more ...

In this case, the LBP that would have been payable to the surviving partner may be paid to an appropriate person (e.g. a relative or executor of the estate). This would occur if the appropriate person has contacted Services Australia to advise they have not received the LBP or not been able to access it from the deceased's bank account.

The LBP is worked out differently from that normally payable to a surviving partner, If both members of the couple are pensioners, the LBP is automatically issued to the surviving partner.

Note: if both members of a couple die at the same time (e.g. in an accident), the younger partner is considered the surviving partner when working out the LBP.

LBP = (CMCR - NR) x (7 - NEPED)

  • CMCR is the couple's combined fortnightly member of a couple rate payable on the payday following the surviving partner's death had the deaths not occurred. The CMCR does not include the Coronavirus Supplement if the customer or deceased partner received it prior to the bereavement period
  • NR is the surviving partner's new fortnightly single rate that would have been payable on the payday after their death. If the deceased also received Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and the survivor is expected to be granted FTB, do not include in the survivor's new single rate the Rent Assistance (RA) component. When the survivor's claim for FTB is finalised RA will be paid with FTB rather than the income support payment. The NR also does not include the Coronavirus Supplement, where the survivor received it prior to the bereavement period
  • NEPED is the number of entitlement period end days from and including the day of the first partner's death up to and including the day before the surviving partner's death

To calculate the LBP

  • Work out the difference between the 2 rates
  • Multiply the difference in rates by the number of paydays left (7 - NEPED)

To work out the LBP as a daily rate:

  • LBP = (CMCR - NR) x (number of days from the later of date of death and date paid to + 1 until the end of the bereavement period)

See the Resources page for an example, and refer to Death of both members of a couple.

For an ABSTUDY Living Allowance customer, see Step 1 in Table 3.

For other customers, see Step 1 in Table 4.

8

Illness separated couple + Read more ...

For pensioners and Parenting Payment customers, the LBP is worked out as if the couple were living together.

This is adjusted by subtracting the difference between:

  • the combined illness separated rates already paid since the date of death, and
  • the combined member of a couple rates which would have been paid, if not illness separated.

Note: other than Parenting Payment, this adjustment does not apply if the survivor receives an allowance or benefit type payment, e.g. JobSeeker Payment (JSP). Refer to item 6 above. For JSP and YA customers, suitably skilled Service Officers are to key the Bereavement Payment using the Bereavement Assistance task selector in Process Direct.

The calculation may result in an overpayment if there is a delay in notifying or recording the death. The overpayment is a recoverable debt.

If the partner's death was actioned after the entitlement period in which death occurred:

LBP = (CMCR - NR) x ( 7 - NEPED) minus (CSR - CMCR) x NEPED

  • CMCR is the couple's combined fortnightly member of a couple rate payable had they been living together before the person died. How to work out the CMCR for an illness separated couple. The CMCR does not include the Coronavirus Supplement, where the customer or deceased partner received it prior to the bereavement period
  • NR is the surviving partner's new fortnightly single rate. If the deceased also received Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and the survivor is expected to be granted FTB, do not include in the survivor's new single rate the Rent Assistance (RA) component. When the survivor's claim for FTB is finalised RA will be paid with FTB rather than the income support payment. The NR also does not include the Coronavirus Supplement, where the survivor received it prior to the bereavement period
  • NEPED is the number of entitlement period end days for which the illness separated member of a couple rate continued after and including the date of death
  • CSR is the combined single rates payable had the person not died. The CSR does not include the Coronavirus Supplement, where the customer or deceased partner received it prior to the bereavement period

To calculate the LBP

  • Work out the difference between the CMCR and NR
  • Multiply the difference in rates by the number of fortnights not paid at the member of a couple rate (7 - NEPED)
  • Work out the difference between the CSR and the CMCR
  • Multiply this difference by the number of fortnights paid at the member of a couple rate (NEPED)
  • Subtract amount obtained in Step 4 from the Step 2 amount.

For an example, see the Resources page.

To work out the LBP as a daily rate:

LBP = (CMCR - NR) x (number of days from the later of date of death and date paid to + 1 until the end of the bereavement period) less (CSR - CMCR) x (number of days from the start of the entitlement period before the date of death until the later of date of death and date paid to + 1).

If the partner's death was actioned within the entitlement period in which death occurred, refer to Item 5 above to calculate LBP. Use the CMCR as the couple's fortnightly member of a couple rate payable had they been living together before the person died. Refer to How to work out the CMCR for an illness separated couple.

For an ABSTUDY Living Allowance customer, see Step 1 in Table 3.

For other customers, see Step 1 in Table 4.

Care receiver LBP calculation

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Death of care receiver + Read more ...

If the carer was getting:

  • Carer Payment (CP), go to Step 2
  • Carer Allowance (CA) for an adult care receiver, go to Step 3
  • CA for a child care receiver, go to Step 4

2

CP customer + Read more ...

First check if the care receiver was partnered. If so, check if the surviving partner is entitled to an LBP. The LBP is worked out differently for a surviving partner. If the non-carer partner is entitled to an LBP, the carer will not be entitled to an LBP as well, but will continue to receive CP for the bereavement period.

If the care receiver was a child with a severe disability or medical condition, or the care receiver child was in a short-term or episodic care situation, the carer may be entitled to an LBP.

Complete the death action in the care receiver's record for the 'ripple' to initiate any lump sum payment to be paid on the carer's record.

If the Carer is entitled to an LBP, the LBP payable to the CP customer is the lesser of:

  • 7 times the partnered maximum basic pension rate
  • 7 times the amount of the actual last instalment of CP paid before the care receiver's death

Generally, the LBP is automatically calculated when a customer is recorded as deceased. A Manual Follow-up (MFU) is created when this is unable to occur with the keywords DEA, *REVIEW and @CAR. Eligibility for bereavement payment needs to be established manually and the activity will need to be completed from the Activity List (AL) screen. See Death of an adult or child care receiver and the effect on CP for information on how to code the LBP manually on the One Off Payment (OOP) screen.

Use the Bereavement payment taxation exemption job aid to calculate if LBP is above the tax free amount and therefore a tax adjustment is required.

See the Resources page for a link to the job aid.

If the carer was receiving Defence Force Income Support Allowance (DFISA), see Step 6 in Table 4.

Procedure ends here.

3

CA customer - deceased adult care receiver + Read more ...

If the carer is in receipt of an income support payment (other than Carer Payment) that does not qualify them for a bereavement payment, CA Bereavement Payment may be payable.

Complete the death action in the care receiver's record for the ripple to identify if the CA (adult) carer is eligible for a bereavement payment for their income support payment.

If the carer is entitled to the CA Bereavement Payment, CA is cancelled from the end of the bereavement rate continuation period.

To calculate the amount of the CA Bereavement Payment:

  • Work out the rate at which CA was payable to the person immediately before the adult care receiver died
  • Work out the number of paydays in the bereavement lump sum period
  • Multiply the rate obtained in Step 1 by the number obtained in Step 2

Check the Carer One Off Payments Summary (COOPS) screen for a record of the CA Bereavement Payment.

If the bereavement payment is not recorded on the COOPS screen, code the payment manually on the One Off Payment (OOP) screen.

See Death of an adult care receiver and the effect on CA for information on the bereavement period and coding the OOP screen.

Procedure ends here.

4

CA customer - deceased child care receiver + Read more ...

  • For CA (auto) customers, there is no lump sum bereavement payment for a CA (auto) child. This is because CA (auto) recipients usually receive CP and may be entitled to a CP lump sum bereavement payment
  • For CA (child) customers, a lump sum bereavement payment of:
  • 14 weeks CA may be payable from the child's date of death, if the child met the definition of a Family Tax Benefit (FTB) child immediately before the date of death
  • 4 weeks CA may be payable from the date of the child's death, if the child did not meet the definition of an FTB child

The bereavement payment is paid at the CA rate that applied immediately before the child died. It is paid as if the child had not died and care continued to be provided.

See Death of a child care receiver and the effect on CA for information on coding the death.

Procedure ends here.

4

Calculate the tax free amount + Read more ...

Use the Bereavement payment taxation exemption job aid to calculate the Tax Free Amount (TFE). The Resources page contains a link to the job aid.

Tax Free Amount = Survivor's Non Taxable Amount if person had not died plus the deceased partner's Gross Amount (taxable and non-taxable) if they had not died.

  • If the LBP is less than the TFE, the entire LBP is exempt from Tax
  • If the LBP is more than the TFE, the difference is taxable and a tax adjustment is required

6

Customer getting JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance + Read more ...

The customer may be eligible for both PBV and LBP, but they can only receive one bereavement payment.

Always issue the LBP to the customer first.

If the PBV will be greater than the LBP, issue a Partner Bereavement Payment application (SA490) inviting the customer to request to be paid PBV. On the form the customer will relinquish their LBP entitlement in writing before they can be paid PBV. The Resources page contains a link to the Partner Bereavement Payment application (SA490).

When the form is processed, an adjustment to the bereavement payments is made by offsetting the LBP already paid with the PBV.

Extra care must be taken to only invite customers who will be paid more PBV than they received of LBP. This is because if PBV is less than LBP, an overpayment may occur.

See Assessing entitlement to a PBV and Assessing entitlement to LBP.
