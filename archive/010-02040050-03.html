7

Unable to live at home + Read more ...

A student or Australian Apprentice may be unable to live at the home of both their natural, adoptive or relationship parents (or if they have only one parent, that parent) because of:

  • extreme family breakdown or similar exceptional circumstances, or
  • no parental concern from one or both parents, see the Resources page of Assessing unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH) for customers claiming or receiving Youth Allowance (YA), Disability Support Pension (DSP), Special Benefit (SpB) or Tertiary Access Payment (TAP) for scenarios, or
  • serious risk to their physical or mental wellbeing due to family and domestic violence, sexual abuse or similar unreasonable circumstance, or
  • their parents being unable to provide a suitable home due to a lack of stable accommodation, and
  • they are not receiving continuous financial or other support directly or indirectly from either a parent or guardian on a long-term basis, see the Resources page of Assessing unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH) for customers claiming or receiving Youth Allowance (YA), Disability Support Pension (DSP), Special Benefit (SpB) or Tertiary Access Payment (TAP) for examples

Is the student or Australian Apprentice unable to live at the home of either their natural, adoptive or relationship parents (or if they have only one parent, that parent) due to one of the above reasons?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Independence status not met + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice is not eligible for ABSTUDY independence under the UTLAH category.

Complete the following tasks:

  • Consider whether the student or Australian Apprentice is:
    • independent on other grounds, see Independence for ABSTUDY customers, or
    • eligible for ABSTUDY away from home, see Away from home rate for ABSTUDY
  • If appropriate, refer the student or Australian Apprentice for other assistance, for example to a Centrelink social worker, or an Indigenous Service Officer
  • If appropriate, consult a Centrelink social worker for consideration of Special Benefit (SpB)

Note: advice can be given over the phone, but the student or Australian Apprentice should be encouraged to test their eligibility, particularly in cases of discretionary decisions.

Record discussion on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

Coding independence details in Process Direct + Read more ...

The independence data will prefill the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen from information provided in the claim.

To view the outcome of the of the social worker's assessment when coding the NIH, view the social worker DOC via the Interactions icon. Information in this DOC will include start/reject reason, start date and consent to contact.

To check this information key 'NIH' in Super Key or select the My Circs icon.

If the information does not prepopulate, or changes are required (for example, an incorrect start date recorded), it must be coded or corrected manually.

Complete the following fields for manual coding:

  • in the Action: field, key 'HOM'
  • in the Start/Reject Reason: field, use to locate the appropriate code. There is a list of ABSTUDY Homeless (HOM) grant and rejection reasons. Select the appropriate code
  • code the later in the Start Date: field of the:
    • ABSTUDY start date, or
    • date the student or Australian Apprentice became independent
  • in the Consent to Contact: field, use to locate the appropriate code
