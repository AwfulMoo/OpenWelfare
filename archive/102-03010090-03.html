Step

Action

1

Change of address to residential aged care + Read more ...

Services Australia can receive advice of a change of address into residential aged care from:

  • a care recipient
  • partner
  • an authorised third party

See Appointing nominees and Accepting information from and disclosing information to Power of Attorney for more information on confirming third party authorisation.

After confirming a permanent change of address into residential aged care updates are needed to the:

  • home and/or postal address
  • contact details, including phone number, and
  • relationship status (if partnered)

See Searching for a customer on the system for help.

Does the care recipient have a Centrelink record?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Care recipient does not have a Centrelink record + Read more ...

If a thorough search for a record fails to produce a valid Centrelink record match:

  • create a new record via the Add Person (AP) screen. See Adding a customer to the system

If the care recipient/third party is unable to give sufficient details to conduct a thorough search:

  • do not add a new record
  • advise the care recipient/third party that Services Australia will make contact with a request to complete an Aged Care – Calculation of your cost of care form

Help these customers by providing:

  • information on the Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC)
  • advice that customers may download this form from the Services Australia website
  • the My Aged Care website details for more information on aged care including fees and charges

Procedure ends here.

3

Care recipients with a current Centrelink record + Read more ...

Ask the person advising the change of address if they have the following details:

  • Name and address of the aged care home
  • Date of entry
  • Information to determine if the care recipient is:
    • in a funded aged care place, or
    • a different kind of aged care placement (for example, Multi-purpose service)
  • If the care recipient has paid any lump sum accommodation costs
  • The address the care recipient wants their mail sent to

Does the care recipient/third party have the information needed?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Checking entry into care via data matching + Read more ...

When a care recipient enters permanent residential care and Aged Care data matching has commenced:

  • System for the Payment of Aged Residential Care (SPARC) receives:
    • Entry details from the provider
    • This includes the aged care home and date of admission
  • When the care recipient is matched, SPARC transmits the details into Customer First via the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS)
  • In Customer First:
    • an RCA/NCL activity is created and contains the details of entry into care
    • use the RCA Institution (RIS) screen to verify entry by checking Admission Date and Care Type: Residential
  • Aged Care staff can access SPARC and confirm any admission into care that have been coded:
    • Contact the Aged Care (RCA) team to confirm details if needed. The Resources page contains links to Office Locator and contact details

Is there a Residential entry on RIS or can an aged care skilled Service Officer supply all the relevant information now?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

5

Change of address into aged care + Read more ...

If the change of address is for:

  • entry into permanent residential aged care, go to Step 6
  • moving from one aged care home to another, see Step 7 in Obtaining and assessing information table
  • respite care, see Temporary change of address to an aged care home for respite care. Procedure ends here
  • a retirement village where the customer will be caring for themselves, see Retirement villages. Procedure ends here
  • an unfunded bed in aged care service (which includes a Multi-purpose service, Unfunded bed in an accredited aged care facility, in hospital awaiting aged care placement), see Rent Assistance (RA) for care receivers. Procedure ends here

6

Is the care recipient partnered? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Care recipient/partner may be separated due to ill health + Read more ...

When a care recipient has permanently entered residential aged care, these accommodation arrangements automatically define a couple as illness separated.

After confirming the date of entry, determine the earliest date of notification that can be used:

  • to code separated due to illness, and
  • to record the Date of Receipt. Note: this may be the date of receipt of an RCA/NCL with a source of AUT

If an RCA/NCL activity is preventing the update of the contact details:

  • cancel the new claim
  • complete updates using the earliest date of notification
  • re-index claim, see Cancelling claim activities and re-indexing claims

To update the MS screen:

  • key 'MS' in Next: field to access new screen for updating
  • Event Date: field, enter the date the care recipient became illness separated
  • Marital Status: field will remain as MAR, REG or DEF
  • PPE Cust and PPE Ptnr: fields, update as required
  • Qualifier Code: field, enter the appropriate illness separated qualifier code:
    • 'ILB' - Illness Separated / Both have left home
    • 'ILC' - Illness Separated / Customer has left home
    • 'ILP' - Illness Separated / Partner has left home
  • Source: field, enter the source of the information
  • Receipt Date: field, enter the date of receipt

Note: after entering the correct 'illness separated' code on the MS screen, the records of the care recipient and partner continue to be linked and correctly display as 'member of a couple'.

Select Continue to go to the Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen.

8

Update address details + Read more ...

From the CDTS screen update the screens including home and postal addresses (if needed) by selecting the Address Details (AD) screen manually.

If the care recipient/third party advises payment of a lump sum accommodation payment, see Aged care fees and charges - accommodation payments for more information on coding.

If care recipient/third party advises that the principal home is sold, see Selling a home.

Is the care recipient in receipt of an income support payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, record details in a Fast Note. Procedure ends here

9

Update the Accommodation (AC) screen + Read more ...

Update the accommodation details even if the care recipient is not paying any rent.

Customer First only accepts the Home ownership codes GFN and GFH if:

  • the care recipient is RCA/CUR, or
  • AC screen is updated within a means assessment activity and the result will be RCA/CUR. For further information on coding AC screen, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA)

If means assessment will be rejected, including when care recipient has now been discharged from care, use HOM or NHO codes

Update these fields on the AC screen:

  • Address Type Used for RA Calc: field, code 'HOM' - Home
  • Event Date: field as date of admission
  • Accommodation details for: field, code 'C' for Customer:
    • Including partnered care recipients as they are now separated due to illness
  • For care recipients currently in a subsidised bed at an approved aged care home Home Ownership:
    • ‘NHO’ = Non homeowner and not RCA/CUR
    • ‘HOM’ = Homeowner and not RCA/CUR
    • ‘GFN' = Non homeowner in Govt funded aged care
    • 'GFH' = Homeowner in Govt funded aged care
  • Type of Rent: is blank unless:
    • the care recipient has a current PINS deduction for Government Rent:
    • The rent type must be coded as GOV
    • An MFU auto creates to follow up coding of the AC screen and end the PINS deduction
  • Sharer Status: EXE – exempt (for NHO only), otherwise blank
  • Reason Home Vacated (for GFH only):
    • INH - In Nursing Home (other than EX5), home exempt for 2 years (for care recipients who entered care after 1 July 2014)
    • EXA - In aged care paying periodic accommodation charge (including Daily Accommodation Contribution) to indefinitely and entry into care prior to 1 January 2017
    • EXP - In aged care paying periodic accommodation bond (or Daily Accommodation Payment) and entry into care prior to 1 January 2017
  • Date Home Vacated (for GFH only): Date the care recipient left the principal home. This may already be updated. For example, if care recipient was previously in respite care or hospital
  • Note: if partnered ensure AC screen is updated for the care recipient only. If the partner remains in the home the 'Reason Home Vacated' and 'Date Home Vacated' are not coded
  • Remaining fields are blank

Was the care recipient paying rent before their change of address?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No,
    • Attach a manual Fast Note with details of the update
    • Finalise via the AR screen
    • Go to Step 4 in Table 2

10

Care recipient was paying rent before change of address + Read more ...

Confirm if the care recipient is liable to pay rent after the address change.

Complete the steps below:

  • If the care recipient is a partnered non-homeowner and the partner is still residing in the home and paying rent:
    • RA entitlement is still applicable for the partner. For more information, see Vacation of principal home due to illness
  • If the care recipient was paying rent for their principal home before moving into residential aged care and they continue to pay rent for the principal home:
    • RA is payable for the first 14 days after admission
    • Pay the RA manually. This ensures the information on the AC screen matches the information on the RIS screen
  • Make a manual payment via the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen
    • An APS5 or above must issue the payment via the PYAJ screen (no MySupport referral required). See Using the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen
  • Attach a manual Fast Note with the update details
  • Finalise via the AR screen

See Step 4 in Table 2.

2

Obtaining information to confirm change of address into aged care home + Read more ...

To confirm the change of address Services Australia must receive admission documentation. This confirms if a member of a couple is separated due to illness.

Before making updates the following information is needed:

  • Evidence from a treating doctor or relevant health professional to confirm vacation of the home due to ill health, and/or
  • Evidence from the accommodation provider that the move has occurred due to ill health
  • Any documentation needed to confirm changes to income and assets

Note: postal address details can be updated before confirming the care recipient has moved into residential care.

Complete the following actions:

  • Update the Address Details (AD) screen with new postal address (if provided)
  • Stop payment of rent assistance and update the Accommodation (AC) screen:
    • Event Date: date of entry advised
    • Type of Rent: NRP – not required to pay
    • Net Rent Amount: delete amount in this field
  • Create and complete a Fast Note stating Services Australia has received advice that the care recipient has moved permanently into residential aged care. Details not yet confirmed. Include the address and date
  • See Requesting information (CLK) and send a request for information to confirm details, which may include:
    • evidence that care recipient has entered residential aged care
    • changes to income and assets that have been advised
    • other supporting evidence that care recipient and partner are now separated due to illness (if needed)

Procedure ends here.

3

Not in receipt of an ISP + Read more ...

Complete the following actions:

  • Update the Address Details (AD) screen with new postal address (if provided)
  • Create and complete a Fast Note stating that Services Australia has received advice that the care recipient has moved permanently into residential aged care. Details are not yet confirmed. Include the address and date

Procedure ends here.

Income and asset updates - small change to asset value thresholds + Read more ...

All the information for income and asset updates is not available + Read more ...

Care recipient/third party can verify the move into residential aged care but cannot give all details of significant changes to income and assets:

  • See Requesting information (CLK) and issue a request for information to confirm details listing:
  • changes to the income and assets advised
  • supporting information needed to confirm changes
  • Procedure ends here

7

Care recipient is moving from one aged care home to another + Read more ...

A new admission into care will create an RCA/NCL activity once admission is received. If RCA/NCL is currently on Activity List (AL), refer to Aged care means assessment - preliminary checks or complete unannounced transfer to Aged Care staff, see Transferring a call with Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone.

To update address details prior to completion of a new means assessment, obtain the following details from the care recipient or their authorised representative:

  • Name and address of facility
  • Date of entry
  • Information to determine if the care recipient is in:
  • a funded aged care place, or
  • a different kind of aged care placement (for example, Multi-purpose service), see Rent Assistance (RA) for care receivers
  • The postal address the care recipient wants their mail sent to
  • Update any other changes as needed. For example, bank accounts. See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services for more details

Procedure ends here.

Changing address based on aged care home notification

Table 3:

Step

Action

1

Care recipient has moved into aged care home + Read more ...

  • Update a residential or postal address based on advice received from a care recipient or an authorised third party
  • Care recipients in receipt of an income support payment (ISP) who move into an aged care home are at risk of mail being returned to Services Australia (if the address is not updated)
  • Aged care systems send aged care letters. The default address is the aged care home

Further action is needed when a care recipient:

  • is in receipt of an ISP, or a concession card, and
  • has not given a new residential or postal address, and
  • has no partner remaining in their formal principal home, and
  • has no nominee, power of attorney or authorised third party acting on their behalf
  • has mail returned from the care recipient's previous address.

Does the care recipient meet the above?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No,
    • Further referral is not needed
    • Procedure ends here

2

Referral to APS5 needed for address update + Read more ...

Delegation to change a Centrelink address based on data exchange only, is limited to staff at the APS5 level or above:

  • Service Officers (if not APS5 or above) must refer the address update to the Service Support Officer (SSO)
  • Use the DIRECT REFERRAL TO SSO web form for Address Update from SPARC Information

See the Resources page for information on accessing the web form

After sending the Direct Referral to SSO web form:

  • complete any outstanding coding including a means assessment
  • add a Fast Note detailing the SSO referral was completed
  • SSO refer to Update address from aged care data exchange

Procedure ends here.
