This document outlines the qualification criteria for Austudy.

On this Page:

Austudy

Austudy is a payment for full-time students and Australian Apprentices 25 years of age or over.

Services Australia administers and delivers Austudy payments on behalf of the Department of Social Services (DSS). The agency's agreement with DSS requires that Austudy claims are processed accurately, with particular attention paid to verification of the customer's age, residence (where applicable) and enrolment with an approved education institution or as an Australian Apprentice. Austudy is an income support payment and cannot be paid concurrently with another income support payment.

Qualifying for Austudy

To qualify for Austudy, a customer must meet all of the following criteria:

  • be of Austudy payment age, and
  • satisfy the residence requirements, and
  • for students, satisfy the Activity test for Austudy, or
  • for Australian Apprentices, undertake a full-time Australian Apprenticeship and have a current Commonwealth Registration Number. Note: an Activity Test does not apply to Australian Apprentices

Austudy payment age criteria

Generally, a student or Australian Apprentice is of Austudy age if they are 25 years of age or more when they apply.

A person is not considered to be Austudy payment age if, at turning 25 have not reached the maximum age for Youth Allowance. See Determining the minimum and maximum age for Youth Allowance.

There is no maximum age for Austudy.

Australian Apprentices

To qualify for Austudy as an Australian Apprentice, a person must have a current (not suspended) Commonwealth Registration Number in relation to a full-time apprenticeship, traineeship or trainee apprenticeship under the scheme 'Australian Apprenticeships'.

The person does not need to provide proof of their status as an Australian Apprentice. Services Australia will confirm the Australian Apprenticeship details with the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations.

Note: Austudy is not payable for any period for which the Australian Apprentice has been paid the Living Away From Home Allowance (LAFHA).
