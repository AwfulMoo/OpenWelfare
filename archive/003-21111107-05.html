Valid (eligible) and invalid (ineligible) impact reasons

For online claims or ACC lodged:

  • on or after 11 March 2022, customers can select from the below impact reasons
  • on or before 10 March 2022, staff can refer to the below impact reasons when reviewing any free text information provided in a customer’s claim

Table 1: Impact reasons and if they meet the adversely affected criteria. That is, if they are a valid (eligible) or invalid (ineligible) impact reason.

If there is no valid impact reason, and any free text information does not meet a valid impact reason, the customer:

  • does not meet the adversely affected eligibility criteria and
  • is not eligible for payment. That is, their claim is to be rejected if formal approval has been provided to reject claims

Staff must always consider free text information to determine if customer contact is needed. See Item 17 below.

For example, a customer may have selected ‘The interior of my home has been exposed to the elements’ however free text information has that rainwater came through a gap in a closed window.

For example, a customer may have selected ‘Clothing, toys, bicycles or gardens were damaged or destroyed’ however free text information has ‘flood waters came in, damaging the inside of my home, including clothing and toys’

These claims are automatically allocated to a Fraud Analyst for initial review before the claim being processed.

Although excluded from automation, the customer may still be eligible for payment once the customer’s circumstances have been manually assessed. These reasons should not be the only focus when assessing eligibility for payment, but service as help only.

Not all exclusion keywords are listed

Service Officers must follow all Process steps to review the claim fully, including any exclusion reason to determine if a customer is eligible or ineligible for the payment.

STP266

Customer's existing Correspondence Nominee (NOC) arrangement is voluntary

Review the customer’s responses to the nominee questions in the claim.

If the customer has indicated in their claim they:

  • want a correspondence nominee, process the claim as usual
  • do not want a correspondence nominee, cancel the existing nominee arrangement

Staff not trained in cancelling nominee arrangements should contact Technical Peer Support for help.

STP267

Customer's NOC was put in place more than 6 months ago

Review the customer’s responses to the nominee questions in the claim.

If the customer has indicated in their claim they:

  • want a correspondence nominee, process the claim as usual
  • do not want a correspondence nominee, attempt to contact the customer to confirm cancellation of the existing nominee arrangement

STP268

Customer has an existing or is appointing a payment nominee

STP269

Customer has advised that their shared care arrangement is less than or equal to 52%

Compare the customer’s recorded care percentage to that in their claim.

Care % reduced, but customer still principal carer:

If the care percentage has reduced, but the customer is still the principal carer (that is, 50% or more care), AGDRP can be paid for the child.

If the customer is in receipt of FTB or CCS, send an ACTDOC (ACTFAO) advising the care percentage of the child should be reviewed (include details of recorded % and declared % as per the Claim ID).

See the Claiming for child/ren step in Table 1 on the Process page.

STP271

STP272

Customer previously granted for same event

If the customer is attempting to claim for children not claimed in the original claim, see Table 6 on the Process page.

STP273

The customer has been outside Australia for more than 13 of the last 19 weeks

Review the customer’s residence and travel history to decide if the customer meets the residence criteria.

See AGDRP / NZ DRP eligibility step in Table 2 on the Process page.

STP276

Customer has not selected any AGDRP eligible home impacted reason

If the customer has selected ‘other’ for damage to home, the free text must be reviewed to decide if the customer’s description falls into an eligible impact category.

If the customer still does not meet any eligible impact reasons, they are not eligible for the payment.

STP277

Customer has not selected any AGDRP eligible asset type

If the customer has selected ‘other’ for damage to major assets, the free text must be reviewed to decide if the customer’s description falls into an eligible impact category.

If the customer still does not meet any eligible impact reasons, they are not eligible for the payment.

STP278

Customer has changed care percentage

Compare the customer’s recorded care percentage to that in their claim.

Care % reduced, but customer still principal carer:

If the care percentage has reduced, but the customer is still the principal carer (i.e. 50% or more care), AGDRP can be paid for the child.

If the customer is in receipt of FTB or CCS, send an ACTDOC (ACTFAO) advising the care percentage of the child should be reviewed (include details of recorded % and declared % as per the Claim ID).

Care % reduced, customer no longer principal carer:

If the care percentage has resulted in them no longer being the principal carer, the customer is not eligible to be paid AGDRP for that child.

Note: they can receive NZ DRP for the child if the child lives with them. See the Claiming for child/ren step in Table 1 on the Process page.

Care % increased, but customer still not principal carer:

Make sure the child is not included when assessing the AGDRP claim, as the customer is not eligible to be paid for the child as they are not the principal carer. If the customer is in receipt of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) or Child Care Subsidy (CCS), an ACTDOC should be sent (ACTFAO) advising the care percentage of the child should be reviewed (include details of recorded % and declared % as per the Claim ID).

Note: they can receive NZ DRP for the child if the child lives with them. See the Claiming for child/ren step in Table 1 on the Process page.

Care % increased, customer claiming they are now principal carer:

Follow the steps in the Process page to complete a care assessment.

STP321

Review the customer’s circumstances.

STP323

If the customer has selected ‘other’ for damage to home or major assets, the free text must be reviewed to decide if the customer’s description falls into an eligible impact category.

If the customer still does not meet any eligible impact reasons, they are not eligible for the payment.

STP324

The customer has confirmed home (HOM) address differs to the address the customer advised they were living at the time of the disaster.

Use the Geospatial tool in the Operational Blueprint for the event to decide if the customer’s address at the time of the disaster is in a declared area.

See the Address during event step in Table 2 on the Process page, when reviewing the customer’s address.

STP325

Review the customer’s previously rejected claim.

Take appropriate steps as per the Claim for same event previously rejected step in Table 1 on the Process page

STP341

Value of combined eligible asset damaged is less than $20,000

If the customer has selected ‘other’ assets, review what has been listed to see if there are items that are included as eligible assets as per Eligibility for AGDRP and NZ DRP Item 8 Table 2.

If not, the customer does not meet this impact criteria.

STP352

Customer already granted for concurrent event

While some customers may genuinely be eligible for two concurrent events, care must be taken to make sure the customer meets all eligibility criteria if claiming for a concurrent event.

See the Claiming for a concurrent event step in Table 1 and take the appropriate action depending on if the customer is eligible or ineligible for their claim for the concurrent event.

Only managed by staff in a specialist team.

Staff must not action these claims.

Help from Technical Peer Support

If staff need help to finalise a claim, they must not call Technical Peer Support (unless the customer is on the phone).

Before seeking help from Technical Peer Support using a Technical Support Request staff must:

  • thoroughly research Operational Blueprint and other available resources
  • try to resolve the issue at a local level, for example, using local MS Teams chats

If:

  • an issue cannot be resolved and further technical assistance is needed, staff will lodge a Technical Support Request
  • staff have not received an outcome for their support request by the end of their shift, the related activity is to be placed on hold using the reason ‘hold to user’

Note: where a customer is on the phone, staff can call Technical Peer Support.
