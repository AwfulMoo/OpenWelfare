ACC

Non-permanent resident adult with Australian citizen child + Read more ...

SpB may be paid to a child:

  • who is an Australian citizen or an Australian permanent resident, and
  • whose custodial parent is a non-permanent resident of Australia and precluded from receiving any social security payment

See SpB for children who are Australian Citizen Children (ACC).

Obtain proof of citizenship or proof of residence to verify the child is an Australian citizen. For example, the child's birth certificate showing the resident parent’s name.

  • Any person born in Australia after 20 August 1986 is an Australian citizen if when they were born at least one of their parents was an Australian citizen or permanent resident of Australia. This applies even if the parent who is an Australian citizen or a permanent resident had separated from the non-resident parent before the child's birth. If neither parent is an Australian citizen or permanent resident visa holder, the person needs to continue to reside in Australia until their tenth birthday when they will automatically acquire citizenship
  • If the child is below school age and the custodial parent can prove they have no means of adequately supporting the child, SpB may be paid to the child
  • SpB can only be paid to children over school age for the period they or their custodial parent are judged to be homeless or at risk of homelessness. The custodial parent must show they have no reasonable means of support and are in genuine hardship
  • In all ACC category cases:
    • The custodial parent or responsible adult should be made the child's correspondence nominee as well as their payment nominee. This is to meet notification provisions and appropriate use of payments
    • The custodial parent or responsible adult needs to complete an Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf form (SS313). For more information, see Appointing nominees
    • The customer must be coded into the child's record
    • Continue to reassess the custodial parent's residence status
  • When the custodial parent has no means of adequately supporting the child, referral to a social worker is strongly recommended to help determine if the child is in genuine hardship
  • SpB can only be granted if the custodial parent has taken all reasonable action to pursue child support from the non-custodial parent. If child support cannot be pursued due to family and domestic violence, a referral to a social worker is mandatory. The social worker should document an exemption reason on the child's record
  • There is a safety net for New Zealand citizens who:
    • are the holders of a Special Category Visa (SCV)
    • arrived in Australia at any time on or after 26 February 2001, and
    • do not meet the definition of an Australian resident for social security purposes
  • These SCV holders may be exempt from the residence requirements and able to qualify for a one-off payment for up to 6 months of either JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA). This is subject to other qualification requirements and applies if just before claiming, they:
    • are the holder of an SCV, and
    • have resided in Australia continuously for at least 10 years at any time on or after 26 February 2001
  • If the New Zealand custodial parent is eligible and receiving the 6 months JSP or YA, the Australian citizen child will not be eligible for SpB. The parent may be eligible for Family Tax Benefit (FTB for the child, whether or not they receive an income support payment
  • FTB is the most appropriate payment for SCV holders. SpB cannot be paid if the custodial parent of the child is receiving FTB to support the child. If the custodial parent is receiving FTB for the child, FTB must be cancelled if a decision is made to pay SpB to a child under this category. This is limited to non-protected SCV holders in certain circumstances
  • Australian citizen children granted SpB are not eligible for Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • If the child is aged 16 or 17 years, Youth Allowance may be payable if the child is not in full-time secondary study

AOS

Assurance of Support (AoS) + Read more ...

All customers under an AoS should be coded under the AOS category.

The customer's and the assurer's circumstances must be investigated to ensure:

  • government intent of the AoS scheme is not undermined, and
  • Services Australia does not grant payment if support is available from an assurer

See The AoS scheme.

Relationship breakdown, including family and domestic violence

If the claim for payment has resulted from a relationship breakdown:

  • If family and domestic violence is not a factor, referral to a social worker is mandatory for exploring the possibility of mediation and/or reconciliation, or
  • If the customer is the victim of family and domestic violence, the assuree customer must be referred to a Services Australia social worker before any contact with the assurer by Services Australia. See Family and domestic violence

CFC

Caring for a child + Read more ...

This category is generally for people who are caring for a child with a medical condition.

The application of SpB provisions for customers with caring responsibilities means they can only be paid SpB if all of the following are met:

  • they do not qualify for Carer Payment (CP), Parenting Payment (PP) or any other income support
  • they have no alternative means of support
  • they are unable to work because of caring responsibilities -the caree (child) needs constant care, and there is no other care available, and
  • the person being cared for does not have sufficient funds to support or partially support the carer

A single principal carer is:

  • not subject to the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) for JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) and Parenting Payment Single (PPS), and
  • not required to have 2 or 4 years qualifying residence for PPS if they became a single principal carer of a dependent child since the start of their current period as an Australian resident

See Item NMN.

There should be very few times when a customer with temporary caring responsibilities needs to be paid SpB.

The customer needs to provide confirmation of the circumstances leading to the claim for SpB. Always obtain evidence of the need for care.

Explore and exhaust all avenues of support before granting the claim.

A social work report is not compulsory but is strongly recommended before making a decision. The social worker can explore options in the community before SpB is considered, and further assess the caring situation if required. For example, determining the carer's personal care or supervision needs, or information about the level of care or supervision and who is providing it.

Customers who are caring for their dependent child(ren) under normal conditions (non-medical) should be coded under category:

  • NMN, or
  • NMP

CIP

Caring for an incapacitated person + Read more ...

It is important to establish all of the following:

  • the customer must provide constant supervision, in the caree's home
  • they are prevented from working because of this
  • they do not qualify for Carer Payment (CP) or any other income support
  • no alternative arrangements for care can be made

The customer needs to provide confirmation of the circumstances leading to the claim for SpB. Always obtain evidence of the need for care.

Carer responsibilities do not change the provisions that a newly arrived resident must meet in order to qualify for SpB.

A social work report is not compulsory but is strongly recommended before making a decision. The social worker can explore options in the community before SpB is considered, and further assess the caring situation if required. For example, determining the carer's personal care or supervision needs, or information about the level of care or supervision and who is providing it.

There should be very few times when a customer with temporary caring responsibilities needs to be paid SpB.

However, SpB may be paid if a customer is providing necessary short term psychological and moral support in a crisis and no one else can provide that support. Closely monitor these cases. Code a reviews within a reasonable period depending on the circumstances, but no longer than 13 weeks.

USY

Under 16 years of age + Read more ...

A social work referral and report is compulsory for this category. See Unsupported under 16 year olds to be immediately referred to a Services Australia social worker.

This category is for when a parent, guardian or someone else has taken on the long term role of parent or guardian, but cannot provide support for the customer who is under 16 years of age. See Assisting a customer aged under 16.

  • If a customer is an Unaccompanied Humanitarian Minor (UHM), referral to a social worker and the Special Benefit Helpdesk is mandatory. See the Resources page for Social Work Service contact details and policy helpdesk query form
  • Full-time students under 16 years of age need to be homeless to qualify for SpB. The definition of SpB homeless person aligns with YA Unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH). A customer can be considered homeless, although living with a parent, if the parent is judged to be homeless
  • Under 16 year olds who are not full-time students can be paid SpB if they are:
    • independent (for example, UTLAH) and
    • unable to receive continuous support from a parent, guardian, or the territory, state or fAustralian government
  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) is the most appropriate payment for under 16 year olds
  • The responsibility for the welfare of under 15 year olds lies with state and territory welfare authorities. Refer the customer to a social worker for referral to a welfare authority to complete a protective assessment. No payment can be made until the welfare authority completes the assessment

Newly arrived residents, non-residential or socially marginalised customers

Table 2

Item

Description

NMA

Newly arrived residents of Age Pension age + Read more ...

This category applies to newly arrived residents of Age Pension age who:

  • have had a change in circumstances beyond their control that has resulted in severe financial hardship and a reduction in available funds, and
  • are exempt from the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) for SpB for other reasons

It is important to establish the customer:

  • cannot receive Age Pension under any International Agreement
  • does not have overseas assets or foreign pension that may affect their SpB

Note:

  • If the SpB applicant has served the NARWP but has less than 10 years qualifying residence, assess under NRA category
  • When the NARWP has ended, the customer(s) should be transferred to NRA category if eligibility for SpB still applies
  • All customers under an Assurance of Support (AoS) should be coded under the AOS category

NMN

Newly Arrived Resident Waiting Period (NARWP) for JobSeeker Payment (JSP)/Youth Allowance (YA) + Read more ...

Establish the customer does not have a qualifying residence exemption for JSP or YA and the NARWP applies. For residence information, see:

  • Residence assessment for customers claiming JJSP
  • Residence assessment for customers claiming Youth Allowance (YA) and/or Tertiary Access Payment (TAP)

This category applies to newly arrived residents of working age who:

  • have had a change in circumstances beyond their control that has resulted in financial hardship and a reduction in available funds, and
  • are exempt from the NARWP for SpB for other reasons

When the only reason a customer does not qualify for JSP or YA is that they do not meet the residence requirement, SpB may be payable under JSP/YA conditions. The customer will need to comply with the mutual obligation requirements for SpB. However, they are not eligible to apply for approved activities that are funded. For example, certain courses, New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS), Work for the Dole (WFD).

Principal carers with dependent child(ren) aged over 6 will have part-time job seeking requirements. They need to seek work of at least 15 hours per week. Code 'JSP' conditions in the Payable Under: field on the SPL Other Details (NSOD) screen.

Code principal carer status on the Principal Carer Circumstances Details (PCCD) screen.

Note: all customers under an Assurance of Support (AoS) should be coded under the AOS category.

NMP

Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) for Parenting Payment (PP)/ Carer Payment (CP) + Read more ...

Establish the customer does not have a qualifying residence exemption for PP/CP) and the NARWP applies. For residence information, see:

  • Residence assessment for customers claiming Parenting Payment (PP)
  • Residence assessment for customers claiming Carer Payment (CP)

Note: if an NMP customer becomes a lone parent after the start of their current period of Australian residence, they may be eligible for a Lone Parent exemption from the NARWP and should test their eligibility for Parenting Payment Single (PPS).

The NMP category applies to newly arrived residents who do not meet residence requirements for PP and CP and who:

  • have had a change in circumstances beyond their control that has resulted in financial hardship and a reduction in available funds, and
  • are exempt from the NARWP for SpB for other reasons

Principal carers who have a youngest child aged under 6 years (partnered or single) may be paid under Parenting Payment conditions. They can be exempted from the mutual obligation requirement for SpB. Leave blank the Payable Under: field on the SPL Other Details (NSOD) screen.

Code principal carer status on the Principal Carer Circumstances Details (PCCD) screen.

Principal carers whose youngest child is aged over 6 years should be coded under the NMN category with JobSeeker Payment Conditions, They will have part-time job seeking requirements and need to seek work of at least 15 hours per week.

Note: all customers under an Assurance of Support (AoS) should be coded under the AOS category.

NRA

Not residentially qualified for Age Pension + Read more ...

This category applies to an applicant of Age Pension age. If they have not served the NARWP, category NMA would apply,

It is important to establish the customer:

  • cannot receive Age Pension under any International Agreements
  • does not have any overseas assets or foreign pension that may affect their SpB

Note:

  • All customers under an Assurance of Support (AoS) should be coded under the AOS category
  • Customers who have been a permanent resident for more than 2 years may still have an AoS in force if they are the holder of visa subclasses 143 or 864. These visas have a mandatory 10 year AoS

NRD

Not residentially qualified for Disability Support Pension (DSP) + Read more ...

This category only applies to customers who do not qualify for DSP for the sole reason that they:

  • do not meet residence requirements for DSP because their disability occurred before they arrived in Australia
  • do not have 10 years qualifying residence, and
  • are not refugees or former refugees

Note: DSP may also be paid under an International Agreement. Check if the customer has lived or worked in an Agreement country that covers DSP. If so, test eligibility before granting SpB.

From 1 July 2006, customers in this category must have a capacity to work of less than 15 hours per week.

  • There is no grandfathering rule for SpB customers in this category
  • The customer must have a current and valid assessment of their work capacity if their medical condition does not meet the manifest eligibility guidelines
  • The Medical Conditions Details (MC) screen must be completed before granting SpB
  • If a claim in this category is granted to a customer whose medical condition is not manifest, a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) must be completed within 28 days of grant, SpB will be cancelled. A new JCA must be completed and coded on the customer's record every 2 years, otherwise SpB will auto suspend FME until a new JCA is completed
  • Customers who have a capacity to work for 15 or more hours per week may be eligible for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) (other) with mutual obligation requirements consistent with their capacity
  • If the customer is not qualified for JSP or YA due to residence and SpB is still the appropriate payment, they should be paid under the NMN category
  • It may be necessary to involve the social worker before finalising a decision. For example, if there are complex circumstances or there is a need to explre community support options
  • If the customer has a Temporary Protection Visa (visa type 695, 070, 785, 786 and 447), assess their SpB claim under the TPD categorye

OLT

Other cases (Long Term) + Read more ...

This category is for applicants likely to need income support for more than 13 weeks. For example, because of illness or other circumstances beyond the customer's control.

  • Establish whether the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) applies to this customer
  • If NARWP does apply, consider assessment of the applicant under category:
    • NMA - newly arrived residents of Age Pension age
    • NMN - NARWP for JSP/YA
    • NMP - NARWP for PA/PP/CP
  • Victim of Trafficking Bridging visa F sub class 060 (BVF) holders are assessed under the OLT or OST categories. If it is clear the applicant only needs SpB for 13 weeks, assess under the OST category. See Victims of trafficking
  • If the applicant is a full-time student, and does not qualify for another payment, SpB can only be paid if they are under 18 years of age or undertaking an approved course
  • Full-time students under 16 years of age must also be homeless for SpB and should be coded under the USY category. For a definition of homeless for SpB, see Section 739 and Subsection 1067A(9) of the Social Security Act 1991. See the References page for links

OST

Other cases (Short Term) + Read more ...

This category is for applicants likely to need income support for less than 13 weeks.

  • If the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) does apply, consider assessment of the applicant under category:
    • NMA - newly arrived residents of Age Pension age
    • NMN - NARWP for JSP/YA
    • NMP - NARWP for PA/PP/CP
  • If the applicant is a full-time student and does not qualify for another payment, SpB can only be paid if they are under 18 years of age or undertaking an approved course
  • Full-time students under 16 years of age must be homeless and should be coded under the USY category

It may be necessary to involve the social worker before finalising a decision. For example, if there are complex circumstances or a need to explore community support options.

A maximum of 13 weeks approval can be entered on the system at any time. At the end of each period, SpB cannot continue until entitlement is re-established.

SOM

Socially marginalised + Read more ...

It is rare for an applicant to be assessed under the SOM category. Service Officers should consult a TPS or escalate to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk if they believe an applicant’s circumstances may reflect this category.

A socially marginalised customer is generally considered to be someone who:

  • was unable to meet the mutual obligation and reporting requirements for JSP due to a medical condition; and
  • was ineligible for DSP, or because of their medical condition refused to claim DSP

See the Resources page for an example.

These factors, however, do not disqualify customers from JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA).Delegates must be aware that if there is any alternative, it is not in the customer's best interest to be paid SpB.

Individual customer needs can often be met through better access to rehabilitation programs, labour force programs or specialist counselling.

If the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) applies, consider assessment of the applicant under category NMN.

SpB must be a last resort after exploring JSP incapacity exemptions and/or eligibility for DSP.

When assessing eligibility for socially marginalised applicants, reports on their condition should be obtained from treating doctors and other professionals who know their circumstances. For example, psychiatrists, psychologists, occupational therapists.

A social work report is mandatory for this category. The social worker must complete an assessment determining:

  • if there are alternatives to granting SpB (for example, if they qualify for DSP)
  • if there is assistance available through external agencies, and
  • whether the person meets the eligibility criteria of SpB

Temporary visa holders

Table 3

Item

Description

Nominated Visa Holders

Nominated Visa Holders + Read more ...

Nominated Visa Holders are:

  • visa subclass 070
  • visa subclass 449
  • visa subclass 785
  • visa subclass 786, and
  • visa subclass 790

Prior to 1 January 2003, Nominated Visa Holders were only granted SpB under the TPV category. For SpB grants from 1 January 2003, the following Temporary visa categories were added.

  • TPA - temporary visa holder not of workforce age
  • TPD - temporary visa holder, medically qualified for DSP, but ineligible due to residence
  • TPP - temporary visa holder with mutual obligation requirements
  • TPV - temporary visa holder of workforce age paid continuously since 31 December 2002

Assess customers in the category that suits their current circumstances. This is described in more detail below.

SpB mutual obligation requirements

If the only reason a customer does not qualify for JSP or YA is that they do not meet the residence requirement, SpB may be payable under JSP/YA conditions. The customer will need to comply with the mutual obligation requirements for SpB.

All Nominated Visa Holders are eligible to be registered as Fully Eligible Participants (FEP) when they need to comply with the SpB Nominated Visa Holder (NVH) mutual obligation requirements.

TPA

Temporary visa holder not of workforce age + Read more ...

This category is for Nominated Visa Holders who are under the Youth Allowance (YA) minimum age, or over Age Pension age.

The minimum age for YA is:

  • 15 years and has achieved minimum school leaving age in the state/territory in which they are enrolled and independent
  • 16 years if not independent

For more information, see Independence for Youth Allowance (YA) customers.

These customers are exempt from SpB mutual obligation requirements.

When a customer reaches minimum age for YA, their category should be changed to TPP category.

TPD

Temporary visa holder medically qualified for Disability Support Pension (DSP) + Read more ...

This category is for Nominated Visa Holders who would qualify for DSP including medically but do not meet residence requirements.

  • Customers in this category must have a capacity to work of less than 15 hours per week
  • There is no grandfathering rule relating to the 1 July 2006 changes about work capacity for SpB customers in this category
  • The customer must have a current and valid assessment of their work capacity if their medical condition is not manifest
  • The Medical Conditions Details (MC) screen must be completed before granting SpB
  • Customers who have a capacity to work for 15 or more hours per week should be paid under JSP conditions with mutual obligation requirements consistent with their capacity. The SpB category should either be TPA, TPP or TPV relevant to the customer's circumstance
  • These customers are exempt from SpB mutual obligation requirements

TPP

Temporary visa holder mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

This category is for Nominated Visa Holders who are of workforce age and are capable of working.

TPP customers are subject to Nominated Visa Holders mutual obligation requirements, based on legislation. Ensure the customer has a Jobseeker status ‘active’ (JSR/ACT) and is connected to an Employment Service Provider.

Some customers may be exempt from Nominated Visa Holder mutual obligation requirements if they have children under 6 years of age or have caring responsibilities. Code an exemption on the AEX screen. See Mutual obligation requirements for SpB.

Generally, the long term available funds test applies to this category. However, a Nominated Visa Holder can be paid under the short term available funds test if:

  • they are unemployed due to a seasonal closure
  • their employer confirms they will be re-employed, and
  • the break is for less than 13 weeks

TPV

Temporary Visa Holder + Read more ...

This category was for temporary visa holders of workforce age paid continuously since before 1 January 2003. They were 'saved' from the SpB Nominated Visa Holder mutual obligation requirements while they remained in continuous receipt of SpB.

There are no longer any SpB customers meeting this eligibility. SpB claims lodged by Nominated Visa Holders must now be assessed under the TPP, TPA or TPD categories.

PPV

Partner Provisional Visa + Read more ...

The PPV was formerly the Spouse Provisional Visa (SPV).

The applicant must be a holder of a PPV (subclass 309 or 820).

Note:

  • A subclass 300 visa is not a PPV. Subclass 300 is a temporary visa, and holders of this visa do not meet the residence qualification for any Social Security payment
  • An Assurance of Support (AoS) is no longer required for PPVs from 1 January 2012. AoS arrangements that started before 1 January 2012 will continue for the AoS period

Newly Arrived Residents Waiting Period (NARWP)

From 1 January 2012, holders of visa subclasses 309, 310, 820 and 826 were not exempt from the NARWP for SpB if they were the family member (usually a partner) of an Australian citizen or permanent resident who has resided continuously in Australia for 2 years. Although the NARWP for PPV starts from the application date, only the physical presence of the visa holder can be counted towards the NARWP.

For PPV holders, the NARWP starts from the later of:

  • the date the customer applies for the PPV (not the grant date), or
  • the date of arrival in Australia

Change in circumstances

If the PPV holder can demonstrate a substantial change in circumstances beyond their control, resulting in financial hardship and a reduction in available funds, they can be exempt from the NARWP to qualify for SpB. They may also be exempt from the NARWP if another qualifying residence exemption reason applies. For example, they are the family member of a refugee or former refugee.

A PPV holder claiming SpB due to a substantial change in circumstances beyond their control must substantiate their claim with documentation. Referral to a social worker is strongly recommended. Social workers can explore the complex change in circumstances and community support options.

If family and domestic violence is present, referral to a social worker is mandatory. See the Family and domestic violence procedure. In these cases, a social worker is to assess and document the customer's situation. Information provided by the social worker should contribute to a decision about qualification for SpB. However, the delegate, not the social worker, makes the final decision about qualification.

The social worker has the delegation to assess Crisis Payment applications in family and domestic violence claims/situations.

If the PPV holder is not eligible for SpB because they cannot demonstrate a substantial change in circumstances beyond their control, consider paying the partner at the single rate under Section 24 if the partner is on income support. See Treatment of a partnered person as single under Section 24 new determinations.

Ensure a review is coded to check the status of the visa and to transfer the customer to another payment when permanent residency is granted.

Principal carers

PPV customers who are principal carers with a youngest child aged:

  • under 6 are exempt from the SpB mutual obligation requirements. Leave blank the Payable Under: field on the SPL Other Details (NSOD) screen
  • over 6 will have partial work capacity. Code 'JSP' condition in the Payable Under: field on the NSOD screen

Code principal carer status on the Principal Carer Circumstances Details (PCCD) screen.

PPV partner on Parenting Payment PP

If the PPV customer has a partner receiving PP for the dependent children, it would not be appropriate to exempt the SpB customer from mutual obligation requirements as well. In this situation, the customer should be subject to the SpB mutual obligation requirements.
