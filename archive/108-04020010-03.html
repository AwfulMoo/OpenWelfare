Assessing net market value of various types of assets

Step

Action

1

Customer advising of a change + Read more ...

When there is a small change to asset values, check if the customer needs to notify.

Is the customer only advising of net market value assets?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check customer has all information required + Read more ...

If there are concerns with the legitimacy of the customer's information, see Verifying income and assets.

Can the customer provide all the details required for this contact? Note: where there are multiple updates and the customer does not have all the required details, Service Officers are to process the updates with complete details and handoff or request all necessary details for the remaining updates.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Who does this work? + Read more ...

All Service Officers can process this work. This work is processed at point of contact for all payments, as per the Income and Assets - who can action.

What needs to be coded or requested:

  • type of asset
  • description
  • total value
  • percentage owned by customer
  • event date

Documentation is only required for motor vehicles with finance or operating leases. All other updates do not require documents. Only request further contact from the customer if they are unable to provide all of the details for this contact.

Can the customer provide all the information?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

If the customer is only able to provide part of the information + Read more ...

  • Tell the customer verbally of all the details they must give
  • Record details on a DOC
  • Set a 14 review for details to be returned. See How to code and action a manual review. If sending a form, use the Mail Forms workflow
  • If the customer asks for the details in writing, send a Request for Information (RFI) letter using the Request for Information guided procedure. Request the details be returned within 14 days
  • Tell the customer that all details are required before any change can be made to make sure they receive correct entitlement
  • Procedure ends here until all details returned

5

Type of asset + Read more ...

Is the asset one of the following types?

  • life insurance
  • livestock (off-farm)
  • household contents and/or personal effects, see Policy for the definition of household contents and personal effects
  • motor vehicles and/or boats and accessories
  • caravans and like vehicles
  • unmounted gems
  • Assurance of Support Bank Guarantee
  • taxi licences and taxi plates
  • fishing and aquaculture licences
  • afforestation/plantation projects
  • other (art collections, leaseholds, etc.)?
    • Yes, go to Step 6
    • No, see:
      • assessing house and curtilage
      • assessment and sale of real estate and timeshare asset
      • superannuation
      • financial Investments
      • for assistance in updating saving accounts, see Coding income and assets

6

Updating assets + Read more ...

Service Officers can update the type of asset, description, date of effect, total value and percentage owned on the Other Assets (OAS) screen in the customer's record, if the asset is one of the following:

  • Home contents
  • Personal effects
  • Life insurance
  • Livestock
  • Other assets
  • Motor vehicles/boats/caravans use the current market value. For motor vehicle values, Service Officers can access the Redbook website. See the Resources page for a link. If the customer has a loan or encumbrances against the motor vehicles/boats/caravans, documents are required only if there is reason to believe the details supplied may not be correct
  • Assets are assessed at their net market value. Net market value is not the replacement value or the amount it is insured for, it is the amount likely to be received by selling the asset. The market value of an asset may be reduced if there is a loan or liability against it. Assets purchased using a Line of Credit account on the principal home do not entitle the customer to reduce the value of the asset. Note: for motor vehicles on finance lease or operating lease, request supporting documentation and set a review for 14 days for the return of the details. If the customer is only advising of a motor vehicle on a finance lease or operating lease and a review is set, this is the end of the procedure until the details are returned

If the customer has become partnered or changes partners, also see Change in relationship status from single to partnered, as more action may be required.

If the customer has separated, also see Change in relationship status from partnered to single, as more action may be required. Go to Step 7.

Under the Australian Accounting Standards there are two types of motor vehicle leases:

  • Finance lease - A customer will own the asset at the end of the lease period. The lease costs that represent the cost of acquiring the asset are not deductible as the payments represent the acquisition of an asset
  • Operating lease - A customer will not own the asset at the end of the lease period. The lease costs are deductible. The value of the asset being leased should be included as an asset only if there is a finance lease. The value will change each year (amortised) on the balance sheet and would reflect the eventual market value less amounts of lease payments still to be made. If this lease is used for a business enterprise it is recorded as part of the business assets

Taxi licence

See Valuation of Real Estate and Other Assets.

7

Coding the Other Assets (OAS) screen + Read more ...

Details of the assets are recorded on the OAS screen.

Code/update OAS screen with - type of asset, description, date of effect, total value, percentage owned.

Note:

  • if the customer and/or their partner no longer have an asset, do not delete the record. Update the balance and ownership to nil.
  • if the customer and/or partner still own the asset but it has a nil value, do not update ownership to zero

If updating an existing asset, select the asset and go to Step 8.

If the asset is new, select Add Asset and record the following details on the OAS screen:

  • Enter the type of asset in the Type: field:
    • LIF = Life insurance policy
    • HPE = Home contents and personal effects
    • VEH = Motor vehicle
    • LIV = Livestock
    • BOA = Boats and accessories
    • CVN = Caravan
    • OTH = Other assets
  • A description of the asset (e.g. Toyota Corolla 2012) in the Description: field
  • The date of event in the Event Date: field

8

Recording the asset value + Read more ...

Record the value of the asset in the Total Asset $: field:

  • Life Insurance - the surrender value is to be recorded. If the customer cannot provide the details the following formula is to be used in calculating the asset value. Number of years policy held x annual premiums paid = value maintained
  • Home contents and personal effects - the reasonable market value
  • Motor vehicle - market value, less any monies owing
  • Livestock - market value
  • Boats and accessories - market value
  • Caravan - market value
  • Other assets - as per the Guide

If the customer also owns the land Water Licences and/or Water Rights, this may be recorded on the Real Estate (RE) screen and included in the value of the property. When the Water Licence/Water Right is attached to exempted Primary Production Land, the value of the Licence/Right is also exempted, e.g. Extended Land Use Test, Assets Test Hardship. For more information, see Assessing house and curtilage. This treatment of water assets is not applicable for Farm Household Allowance customers. See Assessing assets for Farm Household Allowance (FHA) for the treatment of water under the FHA assets test.

If the asset is owned with the customer's partner, or another party, enter the percentage owned by the customer in the % Owned Client: field and the percentage owned by the partner in the % Owned Partner: field. Note: if the asset is no longer owned by the customer update the ownership to '0' and update the balance to nil.

For new claims, when there is no change to an existing other assets value, there is no need to update the date of event in the Event date: field. Otherwise, the date of event is the date the change or notifiable event occurred.

Complete the Source: and DOR: fields as appropriate and press [Enter].

Do the customer's valuations seem realistic?

  • Yes, complete the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen. Record details on a DOC outlining the assets and the value being taken into account or changes to assets made. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Clarify the following with the customer: + Read more ...

  • How the customer arrived at their valuation. Sight any related documents (professional valuations, insurance policy etc.) as this may show items of significant value.
  • The meaning of the term net market value.

If the customer's valuation:

  • Now appears reasonable, or has been varied and the valuation appears reasonable, accept the customer's valuation. Complete the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen. Record details on a DOC outlining the assets and the value being taken into account or changes to assets made
  • Still appears to be understated, see Valuation of real estate and other assets at new claim
