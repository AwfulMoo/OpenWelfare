This page contains information about answering enquiries from service providers, checking a veteran's eligibility, and the business rules for processing Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) optical service claims and relevant coding for Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

Select the provider enquiry or check eligibility process or the relevant processing optical service category from the drop down box below.

Determining eligibility

Checking a DVA card holder's eligibility for DVA optical services

Table 3. This table outlines the steps to follow when the system returns a message to check a DVA card holder's eligibility before paying or rejecting optical services.

Step

Action

1

White card for optical treatment + Read more ...

As optical services are specific to a particular area of the body, providers do not need to specify the condition being treated, as long as the DVA card holder’s accepted disabilities match a condition listed in step 3 of this table.

See Resources page for a link to the DVA website for the ‘Notes for allied health providers’ for more information.

2

View DVA card holder's listed entitlements + Read more ...

Press [PF10] to view their listed entitlements.

A list is displayed of conditions they are eligible to claim.

3

Review card holder's conditions + Read more ...

The following conditions entitle a DVA card holder to optical treatment:

  • ambiopia
  • amblyopia
  • ametropia
  • astigmatism
  • cataracts
  • convergence Insufficiency
  • diabetic retinopathy or similar ocular (Service Officers cannot pay diabetes mellitus on its own - the card holder must have retinopathy or similar eye condition)
  • diplopia
  • drusen
  • ectropion
  • esophoria
  • hypermetropic astigmatism
  • macula lutea
  • macular degeneration
  • maculopathy
  • myopia
  • presbyopia
  • refractive error
  • any other eye related conditions
  • cancer of the eye

Card holders who have accepted disabilities of conjunctivitis, pterygium or blepharitis are restricted to treatment for war caused injuries or diseases. They are not entitled to corrective lenses in respect of those conditions. They are entitled to Plano sunglasses, tints and clip-on sunglasses when prescribed by an ophthalmologist or a DVA registered optometrist.

4

Determine if entitled for listed optical condition + Read more ...

Is the condition recorded on the card holder’s listed entitlements (per Step 2) and a listed optical condition (per step 3)?

  • Yes, key '3L' in the PI field to make payment
  • No,
    • If it is not included on their listed entitlements, go to Step 5
    • If it is not a listed optical condition, go to Step 6
    • If neither applies, go to Step 7

5

Condition not on card holder's listed entitlements + Read more ...

Check for prior financial authorisation.

Check PARS for an approval.

Is there a current PARS approval present or the items claimed?

  • Yes, key '3L' in the PI field to make payment. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Determine if DVA confirmation required - condition not optical + Read more ...

If a condition has been listed on the treatment voucher and matches 1 of the card holder's listed entitlements but is not optical related, DVA confirmation may be required before payment is made.

Email the VAP eligibility query form to the VAP Helpdesk and pend the claim until a response is received. See Resources page for a link to the form. For contact details, see Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

Is eligibility confirmed?

  • Yes, key '3L' in the PI field to make payment. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Reject services + Read more ...

Reject the service with RSN code S 583 if:

  • there is no current PARS approval, or
  • the condition on the voucher does not match any of the card holder's listed entitlements

3

Time restrictions for comprehensive eye examinations + Read more ...

Comprehensive eye examinations can only be paid:

  • once every 36 months for patients under the age of 65
  • once every 12 months for patients 65 years or older

Where it is clinically necessary for an optical service provider to review a patient before the next eligible time period for a comprehensive eye examination, another comprehensive consultation or a subsequent consultation item can be used.

Item 10907 enables a comprehensive initial consultation by another practitioner within the specified time period of a previous comprehensive initial consultation. Refer to the item descriptor and explanatory notes for this item.

For specific information about time and restrictions, check QITI or refer to the current Optical Benefits Schedule. See Resources page for a link to the schedule.
