Card Support for Services Australia staff

Table 1

Card Enquiry

Services Australia Customer Support

Card activations

Services Australia is unable to activate a CDC card on behalf of a participant, however a Service Officer can provide the following support to customers to activate their card by:

  • Advising the participant to sign the back of their card immediately
  • Referring the customer to the card issuer's website
    • The participant will need their CARD ID number (11 digits) and Activation code (6 digits). These details are included in their 'Welcome Pack'
    • Alternatively the participant can contact the card issuer Hotline for support. They will need to complete POI questions with the card issuer

PIN resets

Participants can reset their pin through the App or directly through their online account.

If a participant incorrectly enters their PIN six times, the card will temporarily suspend and will not work. Services Australia is unable to assist participants with PIN resets.

Advise the participant to contact the card issuer Customer Service Centre or go online to unblock their card. See Contact details.

Lost or Stolen Cards

If a participant has lost or had their CDC stolen, advise them to login to their online account and turn OFF their card immediately. They can do this by:

  • Logging into their online CDC account
  • Click 'On/Off' switch

Alternatively the participant can call the card issuer Customer Service Centre to have their card turned off, on or blocked. See Contact details.

If the participant later finds their card, they can log into their online back and turn the card back ON.

If the participant cannot find their card they can login and BLOCK their card by:

  • Logging into their online account
  • Clicking on the 'settings' link
  • Clicking on the 'block card' link
  • Clicking on 'block card' button

Note: blocking a CDC cannot be reversed.

Balance checks

Balance checks are not part of the agency's service offer.

Participants are to be advised balance checks can be conducted through the following options:

  • Logging into their CDC online account
  • Logging into the relevant card issuer mobile app
  • Sending a SMS "BAL XXXX" from their registered mobile (where XXXX is the last four digits of their CDC number) to 0488 112 114 and receiving a reply with their balance.
  • Obtaining a free balance enquiry from any one of the following ATMs:
    • any Westpac, St. George, Bank SA, and Bank of Melbourne ATM;
    • participating DC Payment ATMs in trial areas
      Note: a list of the participating DC Payment ATMs is available here; and any other ATM in Australia that does not charge a fee for balance enquiries (including Commonwealth, NAB and ANZ ATMs).

Frequently asked questions about the CDC

Table 2

Question

Description

1

How will a participant access their money if they have not received, or lose their CDC?

If a participant's payment has been split, and they have not received their CDC, they should obtain a temporary card from a Centrelink Service Centre, Local Partner or eligible Australia Post outlet.

They may request a transfer of funds to their unrestricted account if they are unable to access a temporary card (the card issuer will allow a limited transfer amount per month, see the card issuer's website). Staff may be able to assist the participant by advising of when the payment was sent to the CDC account. Staff cannot contact the card issuer on behalf of the participant.

If a participant loses their card, they will need to contact the card issuer to organise a replacement.

2

How are Urgent Payments, Crisis Payments, Hardship Advances and Disaster Relief payments treated under the CDC?

These payments are paid into the participant's unrestricted bank account by default, unless they ask for it to be directed to their CDC account. Normal processing and assessments should apply when considering to grant a CDC customer an urgent, crisis or hardship payment.

No additional or special consideration should be given to CDC participants when assessing claims for these payments, including if a participant has lost their CDC. Urgent, crisis or hardship payments for a CDC participant must not be approved, if the request is for the purposes of circumventing the CDC to access cash.

See:

  • Immediate payments
  • Eligibility for Crisis Payment (CrP)

3

How are returned payments treated under the CDC?

If the unrestricted portion of the participant's payment is returned, normal procedures apply. See Processing a returned payment.

If the restricted (CDC) portion of the participant's payment is returned, it will be automatically flagged for investigation by the CDC Processing Team and ICT.

4

Are participants charged fees when using their Cashless Debit Card?

The card issuer does not charge fees to CDC participants. Participants can make unlimited purchases on their card and check their CDC balance or transaction history online for free. Lost or stolen cards will be replaced at no cost.

5

How are Advance Payments treated under the CDC?

Advance Payments (excluding hardship advances/anticipated payments) are treated as lump sums, and 100% are directed to the CDC. If the participant subsequently requires access to cash, they can discuss their circumstances with the Cashless Debit Card hotline for consideration of a possible transfer of funds to their regular bank account.

Scenarios - Participant examples for the CDC

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Customers in the CDC area temporarily

Customers will be triggered onto the CDC if their home address (or term address if a full-time student) is in a CDC area. If customers are only in the area temporarily (for example, they are travelling), then their address should be coded as Temporary, to avoid being triggered onto the CDC in error.

Note: care should be taken to ensure the customer has not made an address update solely to avoid the CDC.

If a customer has an incorrect address coded, contact the CDC processing team who will review the customer's circumstances, and if in agreement, will instruct that the customer's address details be corrected. The CDC processing team will then exit the customer from the CDC (if appropriate). Where possible, it is preferred that the customer stays on the phone or in the office in case further information or discussion with the customer is required.

For more information on CDC reviews and appeals, see the Answering customer enquiries about the CDC table on the Process page.

See Item 3 below for when a customer's payment has already been split.

2

Participant advises their address was incorrect and they had physically moved before the CDC determination date

The CDC is portable and participants will remain on the CDC if they move out of the area (unless they move overseas permanently). If a participant advises they physically moved before the CDC determination date, but had not notified Services Australia or the address update had not been completed correctly, then the participant will be eligible for a review of the decision to be placed on the CDC. These cases should be discussed with the CDC processing team, who will review the decision. In some situations, evidence may be required, and the address details will need to be correctly updated.

For more information on CDC reviews and appeals, see the Answering customer enquiries about the CDC table on the Process page.

3

Customer was incorrectly triggered onto CDC, and payment has been split

If a customer was incorrectly triggered onto the CDC, and the customer's payment has already been split to the restricted bank account, the CDC processing team can request for the card issuer to transfer the funds to the customer's unrestricted account.

Customers who have been on the CDC for some time, and who are subsequently exited, will need to contact the card issuer if they are eligible for those funds to be transferred to a different account. Their payment will default back to being sent to the unrestricted bank account.

4

Participant has contacted Services Australia or Local Partner

Participants may contact Services Australia or a Local Partner to discuss the CDC. In some cases, a request may be made to review a participant's circumstances or eligibility for the CDC, or request a social worker well-being assessment for the participant. These requests should always be referred to the CDC Processing team in the first instance or the Social Work Services Branch for action.

Talking points - paying interest to CDC accounts

Table 5

Question

Description

1

When will I get my first payment of interest?

Interest will be credited to Cashless Debit Card accounts on 30 June 2020 and will be back-dated to 1 July 2019.

2

When will interest be paid next?

Answer: From 1 July 2020 onwards, interest will be paid monthly on the last day of the month.

3

Question: How much is the interest rate?

1 per cent per annum.

4

Why did I get asked to provide my Tax File Number?

Typically, the interest earned on accounts is taxable income. Providing a Tax File Number is optional, but if you do not provide a valid Tax File Number or Tax File Number exemption, tax may be withheld at the maximum marginal tax rate (plus the Medicare levy).

5

When do I need to provide my Tax File Number by to avoid withholding tax being deducted?

To avoid tax being withheld from the interest payment on 30 June 2020 and all future interest payments, you will need to provide your Tax File Number, or details of any available exemption, before interest is paid on 30 June 2020. You can update your Tax File Number details from 23 June 2020.

6

How do I update my Tax File Number / Exemption?

From 23 June 2020 you can provide your Tax File Number or advise of any exemption, by logging in to your online CDC account at cdc.indue.com.au, clicking the 'My Details' tab and following the prompts. Alternatively you can contact the card issuer's Customer Service Centre. See Contact details..

7

My account is closed now but why didn't I get any back payment of interest on 30 June, when my CDC account was open for some of the 2019/2020 financial year?

You may still be eligible to receive interest for the 2019/2020 financial year if you had a CDC account open between 1 July 2019 and 30 June 2020. Please contact the card issuer's Customer Service Centre. See Contact details.

8

What information will I need to provide so I can receive the back pay of interest?

The card issuer's Customer Service Centre will ask you to provide details of your external bank account (BSB, account number and account name) where you would like the interest to be paid to, and current contact details (daytime phone number, postal or email address) for the payment summary.

9

How much interest will be back paid to me?

Once your request has been processed, the card issuer will issue you with a statement that shows how much interest you have been paid and any tax that has been withheld.

10

How will the payment be made now my CDC account is closed?

Interest rate for the period 1 July 2019 to 30 June 2020 is 1 per cent per annum.

11

How long will it take to receive the interest payment?

Once the card issuer receives your request, please allow 2-3 business days for the payment to be processed and the funds to be available in your account.

12

Where can I get more information?

You can contact the card issuer's Customer Service Centre for further assistance. See Contact details.

13

Where can I get the updated Conditions of Use?

The Conditions of Use is being updated to reflect these changes. From 23 June 2020, an updated copy of the updated Conditions of Use will be available from: the card issuer's website or contact the card issuer's Customer Service Centre to request a copy. See Contact details.

Talking points - contactless Cashless Debit Cards

Table 6

Question

Description

1

When will contactless cards be available?

Contactless cards will be issued from 29 July 2020.

2

How will I know when contactless cards are available?

There will be a message displayed on the card issuer's website to notify you that contactless cards are available. You will see this message on the homepage and on your login page.

3

How can I tell if my card is contactless?

All contactless cards can be identified by the 'contactless payments' symbol: .

4

How can I get a contactless card?

You can log on to your online account, go to "Settings", and select "Replace damaged card" this means you can continue to use your current card until you activate the new card. Alternatively, you can call the card issuer's Customer Service Centre. See Contact details.

5

My contactless card didn't work, is something wrong with my card?

If you are unable to tap to pay at any store, you can still insert your card, select credit and enter your PIN to finalise your purchase.

6

It asked me to choose Cheque/Savings/Credit and put in my PIN the first time I used my new contactless card, why is that?

For security measures you will be required to enter your PIN the first time you use your contactless card.

7

Will I automatically receive a new contactless card?

Once this functionality is available, all permanent replacement cards issued will be contactless. When your current card expires the next card sent to you will be contactless.

8

The new card I was sent is contactless, do I have the option to turn this off?

Yes. You can turn the contactless feature 'On' or 'Off' if you have a contactless card. You can turn it off by logging into your CDC online account and going to 'Settings' and selecting 'transaction settings', or by calling the card issuer's Customer Service Centre. If you turn the setting 'Off' any contactless transactions will be declined. See Contact details.

9

I received my new card which lets me make contactless payments but I want it to ask for my PIN every time I use it – what can I do?

The tap to pay feature is 'On' by default for new contactless cards but you can turn it off by logging into your CDC online account and going to 'Settings' and selecting 'transaction settings', or by calling the card issuer's Customer Service Centre. See Contact details.

10

If I lose my card and get a new one will I need to turn the contactless setting off again?

No, once the contactless setting is changed, any new cards issued that have contactless capability (indicated by the paywave (contactless payment) symbol), will inherit the same settings.

11

If I turn contactless payments off and change my mind, can I turn it back on?

Yes you can change your contactless settings at any time via the card settings in your online bank account, or by calling the card issuer's Customer Service Centre. See Contact details.

12

I checked online and my contactless setting is 'On', why did it ask me to enter my PIN?

For security reasons you will need to insert your card, select 'Credit' and enter your PIN the first time. After that you can tap to pay for transactions under $100 (limit may be increased from time to time). You will continue to have to enter your PIN for purchases above this amount.

13

I tapped to pay with my card but it was declined with the message 'invalid transaction' – why didn't it work?

If you have turned contactless transactions 'Off' and then attempt to tap to pay, you will get a response 'invalid transaction'. You can turn contactless transactions 'On' through your online account, or by calling the card issuer's Customer Service Centre. See Contact details.

14

My tap to pay transaction didn't work how can I find out why it was declined?

You can set up SMS alerts online to get notified of declined transactions. If you tap to pay, but have turned contactless transactions 'Off', you will receive an SMS 'Declined – Contactless transactions disabled for card'. You can also call the card issuer's Customer Service Centre for assistance. See Contact details.

If you have contactless transactions turned "On" and the transaction was less than $100 and was declined, if you provide the name of the business we will pass on to the Department of Social Services which is responsible for merchant management. You can also call the card issuer's Customer Service Centre for assistance. See Contact details.

15

Why can't I turn the contactless setting on or off through my online account?

You can only turn contactless transactions 'On' or 'Off' if you a have a card with contactless capability (indicated by the paywave (contactless payment) symbol).

Talking points – Additional Card Issuer Traditional Credit Union

Table 7: Traditional Credit Union have a list of FAQs on their website

Question

Description

1

Why has the Government added a second issuer to the Cashless Debit Card program in the Northern Territory?

The addition of a second issuer in the Northern Territory provides participants with a choice when it comes to managing their money on the Cashless Debit Card.

2

Does the Government plan to roll out additional issuers to other Cashless Debit Card sites?

The Australian Government has not made any decisions about engaging any further additional issuers.

3

When will participants have the option to choose the Traditional Credit Union as their preferred card issuer?

New Cashless Debit Card participants will be able to choose the Traditional Credit Union as their card issuer from 6 December 2021.

In certain circumstances, eligible participants in the Northern Territory may change their issuer.

4

What are the key differences between the Traditional Credit Union and Indue Cashless Debit Cards?

The Traditional Credit Union provides participants with in-language services and face-to-face support through an extensive branch network in the Northern Territory.

The Traditional Credit Union also gives participants the option to have their Cashless Debit Card bank account and their everyday banking account with a single financial institution. This service is not offered by Indue.

5

Why did the Government select the Traditional Credit Union as the second card issuer in the Northern Territory?

The Traditional Credit Union has physical branches that offer face-to-face services and has a track record in providing personalised and quality financial advice to Australians living in remote communities in the Northern Territory.

6

Will the payment split change if participants choose to switch to Traditional Credit Union from Indue?

No. Participants who choose to switch card issuers will maintain the same payment split they currently have. In the Northern Territory, the payment split is normally 50 per cent into a nominated bank account and 50 per cent onto the Cashless Debit Card.

7

Will the Cashless Debit Card issued by the Traditional Credit Union look any different to their regular banking cards?

No. The Cashless Debit Card issued by the Traditional Credit Union card is styled in a similar way to their other Traditional Credit Union debit cards.

8

Can participants have their everyday banking account with Traditional Credit Union?

Yes. Participants can choose to have their everyday banking account with the Traditional Credit Union.

9

Where are the Traditional Credit Union branches located?

Traditional Credit Union operates 14 branches across the Northern Territory. More information about branch locations is available on their website: tcu.com.au.

10

Will participants whose Cashless Debit Card is with the Traditional Credit Union be able to transfer money to Cashless Debit Cards issued by Indue?

Yes. Participants who have a Cashless Debit Card can transfer money to other Cashless Debit Card participants, regardless of the issuer.
