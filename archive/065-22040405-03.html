5

Finalise the current claim + Read more ...

Note:

  • Service Officers must escalate to Subject Matter Experts if required for further investigation
  • If a claim submits successfully, a successful message displays
  • An error message displays if there is an issue with the claim finalisation process
  • Tolerance checks are done at the approval stage when an SME approves all Residential claims. See Step 12 in the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal home page table
  • A claim requires refinalisation if tolerance checks are not met and the claim is sent back by a SME
  • Entry events with a Held status will not be processed as part of the current claim’s finalisation process and may result in over occupancy issues
  • Over occupancy applies to all care types except home care
  • For flexible care, these entry events are due to a known timing issue with over lapping periods of care. That is, the care recipient has entered into care of the new aged care service but a departure event has not been submitted by the previous aged care service
  • These entry events are resolved when Services Australia has received the departure event from the previous aged care service
  • If the over occupancy report message and report displays, this will prevent Service Officers from finalising the claim
  • The over occupancy issue must be resolved before the claim can be resubmitted
  • To assist in resolving the over occupancy issue, Service Officers can select the Over occupancy report option to identify:
    • the specific care recipients causing the over occupancy
    • the number of approved places over occupied (the number of care recipients over occupied)
    • total occupancy
    • approved occupancy

On the Current claim screen, Service Officers can finalise the current claim by selecting the Finalise claim button.

A warning message displays:

‘WARNING: The claim may contain outstanding events with a status of being:

  • processed, or
  • held

These may or may not be subsequently accepted and included within this claim for payment. Do you want to finalise the claim for [month year] with these events outstanding?’

Service Officers can select:

  • Yes, select Submit to finalise the claim
    • Go to Step 6 if the claim finalisation process is unsuccessful due to over occupancy issues
    • Procedure ends if the successful receipt message displays
  • No, to cancel the claim finalisation process
    • the Current claim screen will display

Procedure ends here.

  • Over occupied details
    • Over occupied date - Service Officers can select the hyperlinked Over occupied date and the Over occupancy report screen will display
    • Type of places
    • Details
  • Service places over occupancy
    • Places over occupied
    • Total occupancy
    • Approved occupancy
  • Respite over occupancy
    • Respite over occupied
    • Respite approved occupancy

To assist in resolving the over occupancy issue, Service Officers can use the Over occupancy report screen to identify:

  • the specific care recipients causing the over occupancy
  • the number of approved places over occupied (the number of care recipients over occupied)
  • total occupancy
  • approved occupancy

Service Officers must contact the service providers to advise which care recipients or places are over occupied. The service providers must resolve the over occupancy through their current process. Once the over occupancy issue is resolved, the claim must be finalised again.

Service Officers can:

  • select the number of records display from the Show records drop down list (bottom left corner of the screen)
  • select Back to current claim to return to the Current claim screen
  • download the over occupancy report to view in an Excel format

Entry events with a Held status will not be processed as part of the current claim’s finalisation process which may result in over occupancy issues.

Held entry events are due to either a:
