Assess adjustment request

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Assess adjustment request + Read more ...

The following must be completed for an adjustment request to be progressed:

The Simplified Billing or ECLIPSE adjustment claim form (HW023) must be received from the private health insurer or billing agent (not from a provider).

The completed HW023 must include:

  • original Claim ID
  • adjustment Claim ID
  • signature
  • an amended account/invoice. Where the original claim was submitted via ECLIPSE the form must be returned with either a:
    • copy of the ECLIPSE claim transmission (screenshot)
    • statement of benefit from the private health insurer, or
    • letter from the private health insurer

The transmission, statement or letter must be endorsed with changes and include the following information:

  • name of patient
  • date of service
  • provider number
  • amount charged
  • amount paid
  • amount outstanding
  • item number/s

Note: depending on the type of service, other information may need to be provided, for example referral details, equipment numbers, LSPN, service text.

Form incorrectly completed

Adjustment forms must be returned to the private health insurer or billing agent using the More information needed standard letter template (Z2162) in the following circumstances:

  • where any fields on the form are incomplete or incorrect
  • the form is outdated
  • the information provided on the form and the information provided on the account/invoice or screenshot are different
  • the amended account/invoice is not supplied
  • the transmission, statement or letter does not clearly display all the required information
  • a transmission, statement or letter has been supplied when the original claim was submitted using a Batch header form

If the adjustment form is received from a provider instead of a private health insurer or billing agent, the request must be returned to the provider using the Simplified Billing cannot be processed standard letter template (z2160).

For adjustments with a date of lodgement from 1 March 2019 the following forms of signature will be accepted:

  • handwritten signature
  • image of an electronic signature
  • digital image or stamp of the private health insurer or billing agent’s logo

In all instances, the applicant’s full name must be completed to identify the person sending in the adjustment form.

When the information entered on the adjustment claim form is not clear, in order to process the adjustment correctly, and operator can make a phone call to the PHI contact person to confirm the information.

The Resources page contains a link to the Simplified Billing or ECLIPSE adjustment claim form (HW023) and Simplified Billing letter templates.

2

Check patient claims history + Read more ...

In mainframe:

  • view the patients history to confirm the correct original Claim ID and date of lodgement has been provided on the adjustment claim form:
    • key NHSI, Medicare card number and press [Enter]
    • locate the service on history
    • select [F2] to view the Claim ID (CLM ID) and Date of lodgement (DOL)
  • view the claim history to confirm the correct adjustment Claim ID (alpha) has been provided, either by:
    • keying FCEI,Minor ID, or Registration number, Claim ID and pressing [Enter], or
    • keying FHEI,Minor ID, or Registration number, original claim ID, DOL=ddmmyy and pressing [Enter]
  • select [F2] key to view last Claim ID used

Manual adjustments

Note: manual adjustments are required when the adjustment cannot be performed in Mainframe. For example, History has been culled, over 2 years since the date of service.

Table 11

Step

Action

1

Assess adjustment request + Read more ...

The following must be completed for a manual adjustment request to be progressed.

The Simplified Billing or ECLIPSE adjustment claim form (HW023) must be received from either the private health insurer or billing agent (not from a provider). Outdated HW023 forms must be returned requesting the current form be completed.

The completed HW023 must include:

  • Original Claim ID
  • Adjustment Claim ID
  • Signature
  • An amended account/invoice. Where the original claim was submitted via ECLIPSE the form must be returned with either a:
    • copy of the ECLIPSE claim transmission (screenshot)
    • statement of benefit from the private health insurer, or
    • letter from the private health insurer

The transmission, statement or letter must be endorsed with changes and include the following information:

  • name of patient
  • date of service
  • provider number
  • amount charged
  • amount paid
  • amount outstanding
  • item number/s

Note: depending on the type of service, other information may need to be provided. For example, referral details, equipment numbers, LSPN, service text.

Form incorrectly completed:

  • If the adjustment claim form is incomplete, contains incorrect information or the invoice and adjustment claim form differ, the request must be returned to the billing agent or private health insurer using the More information needed standard letter template Z2162
  • If the HW023 is received from a provider instead of a private health insurer or billing agent, the request must be returned to the provider using the Simplified Billing cannot be processed standard letter template (z2160)

When the information entered on the adjustment claim form is not clear, in order to process the adjustment correctly, an operator can make a phone call to the PHI contact person to confirm the information.

The Resources page contains a link to the Simplified Billing or ECLIPSE adjustment claim form (HW023) and the Simplified Billing letter templates.

Manual adjustment - underpayment

Table 12

Step

Action

1

Revised assessment results in an underpayment + Read more ...

A manual Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT) request is required where an adjustment cannot be performed via Mainframe.

Assess revised claim and determine Medicare benefit payable

  • Using QITI and MBS Online, determine the Medicare benefit for the service at the date of service (DOS). Ensure that the inpatient benefit amount (75%) is used to calculate the benefit
  • Apply any rules if necessary (for example, Multiple Operation Rule or Diagnostic Imaging Rule, pathology services and restrictions)
  • Deduct benefit paid on history from revised benefit - the difference is the amount for the manual payment

Note: usually the schedule fee should be used to calculate the difference. However, where the restriction on the patient’s history has been paid as an outpatient service, the Service Officer must use the benefit paid, not the schedule fee, to determine the difference to be paid.
