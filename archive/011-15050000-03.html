Refunding advance payments

Question 53 on the notice of judgment or settlement form (MO022) + Read more ...

Where Question 53 on the Medicare Compensation Recovery Notice of judgment or settlement form (MO022) outlining the refund recipient is completed as:

  • ‘Public Trustee’ or ‘other’ search for the documents on the case. If not on file, contact the compensation payer (or the Public Trustee where relevant) to obtain the document, see Table 3
  • Any other recipient, search for any supporting documents on file authorising a refund to any other third party:
    • If documents have been provided supporting a refund to a third party, escalate to Programme Management.
    • If no document has been provided, refund the injured person.

Subsequent refunds, such as from an amended notice, should also be issued to the injured person or claimant (or an authorised recipient, such as Public Trustee), where indicated on the notice of judgment or settlement form with supporting evidence.

Entitlement to the refund must be established at settlement, and be described in settlement documentation. The refund entitlement must be confirmed prior to finalising the case.

Refunding miscellaneous payments

Refund payment type + Read more ...

Refund according to the type of payment:

  • A payment transferred from one case such as a terminated case, to another for the same injured person
    • Refund to the payer or as per the payer’s instructions on refund recipient Q53. Complete this prior to case closure. (See Refunding notice of charge payments below)
  • A payment made by one compensation payer, however responsibility for the claim has since transferred to another compensation payer
    • Phone the payer to confirm that any refund can be made to the claimant
  • See:
    • Table 2 for Injured person refunds, and
    • Table 3 for Public Trustee refunds

Refunding notice of charge and other payments

Determine the compensation payer + Read more ...

Compensation payer is Insurance Commission of Western Australia (ICWA)

Pay any applicable refund to the refund recipient as nominated on the notice of judgment or settlement form, however supporting documents may be required.

Where the refund recipient Q53 on the Medicare Compensation Recovery Notice of judgment or settlement form (MO022) is:

  • blank –phone ICWA to confirm the refund recipient
  • completed as:
    • ‘Notifiable person’ or ‘Notifiable person’s solicitor’, refund accordingly
    • ‘Injured person’s solicitor’ - refund accordingly if Services Australia have a supporting document, otherwise refund the injured person
    • ‘Public trustee’- refund accordingly if Services Australia have a supporting document, otherwise contact the Public Trustee for the supporting document /matter identification number. See Table 3
    • ‘Other’ or ‘Injured person’s authorised representative’-refund accordingly if Services Australia have a supporting document (such as Last Will and Testament)

Other compensation payers

Pay any applicable refund to the refund recipient as nominated on the notice of judgment or settlement form, however supporting documents may be required.

Where the refund recipient Q53 on the Medicare Compensation Recovery Notice of judgment or settlement form (MO022) is:

  • blank – refund the injured person
  • completed as
  • ‘Notifiable person’ or ‘Notifiable person’s solicitor’, refund accordingly
  • ‘Injured person’s solicitor’ - refund accordingly if Services Australia have a supporting document, otherwise refund the injured person
  • ‘Public trustee’- refund accordingly if Services Australia have a supporting document, otherwise contact the Public Trustee for the supporting document /matter identification number
  • ‘Other’ or ‘Injured person’s authorised representative’-refund accordingly if Services Australia have a supporting document (such as Last Will and Testament)

If unsure escalate to Programme Management.

Where the notice of judgment or settlement has been provided in another format (not the MO022) without a refund instruction, refund the payer.

Rejected EFT payment + Read more ...

Compensation Finance advise Service Delivery if a EFT payment rejects.

A Service Officer (SO5) must:

Compare the MO024 account with the account entered on the ‘Referring a case to compensation Finance’ email template. If the account details:

  • Match, reply to finance 'Checked MO024 - no keying error identified – please re-issue via cheque'
  • Do not match:
    • reply to finance ‘Checked MO024 – keying error identified. Feedback issued. Please re-issue refund to XXXXX XXXXX (being correct account details)’ and include a snip of the MO024 account
    • Send feedback to the Service Officer responsible CDQI- Critical error- Action and respond. Use error code MCR15 Critical – EFT details not included or keyed correctly

    • issue a refund by cheque
  • All other states or territory:

3

Issue a refund using the workflow + Read more ...

To issue the refund follow the process in:

  • Notice of judgment or settlement for Medicare Compensation Recovery, Process page Table 2, or
  • Statement by claimant for Medicare Compensation Recovery, Process page Table 3, or
  • Amended notices and reviews for Medicare Compensation Recovery Tables 1 - 3 if refunding after the case is finalised, as this must be done manually

In the Staffware work flow:

  • Edit claimant and injured person’s fields in CCMS to remove any other party, and enter:
    • the injured person’s name
    • c/- Public Trustee into the address field
    • the matter identification number into the same field

Prepare email template:

  • Go to Referring a case to compensation finance - email template
  • Enter the ‘matter identification number’ as the reference number on the email
  • Complete all relevant fields in the template

Finalise the template:

  • if a Tasmanian or ACT Public Trustee, issue a cheque refund
  • all other state or territory Public Trustees, issue a EFT refund
    • Finance to enter the applicable EFT account
  • Send the email template to the Compensation finance team. The Resources page contains a link
  • Print 1 copy of the email template, and scan to the WIN as backlog
  • Enter a case note
  • Complete all steps in the workflows
