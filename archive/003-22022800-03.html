Contacting a customer who has lodged an ineligible AGDRP/NZ DRP claim - approval to reject all claims has been given

Step

Action

1

Australian Residence + Read more ...

Is the claim ineligible because the customer does not meet the residence requirements for the payment?

  • Yes, the customer does not need to be contacted. Reject the claim
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Declared local government area (LGA) + Read more ...

Does the customer live in a declared LGA for AGDRP or NZ DRP?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Customer does not live in a declared LGA + Read more ...

Different arrangements are in place for customers who do not live in a declared LGA for AGDRP or NZ DRP, and as a direct result of the major disaster, have:

  • been seriously injured
  • a dependent child who has been seriously injured, or
  • an immediate family member who has died, or is missing

Check the claim. Does the claim include one of these events?

  • Yes,
    • A specialist team will make 2 genuine attempts to contact a customer
    • For staff outside the specialist team, see Step 7 in the Assess and finalise claim table for how to allocate these claims to the correct team
  • No, the customer does not need to be contacted. Reject the claim

4

Specialised LGAs + Read more ...

Different arrangements are in place for customers who have claimed for one of the following LGAs:

  • Ballina
  • Byron
  • Clarence Valley
  • Kyogle
  • Lismore
  • Richmond Valley
  • Tweed

The Resources page has a Map of affected areas to assess a customer’s LGA.

Has the customer claimed for one of these LGAs?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Customer has claimed for a specified LGA + Read more ...

Genuine contact attempts must be made before finalising a claim as ineligible when:

  • there is no impact reason in the claim, or it is not an eligible impact reason
    • Note: this information is in the Claim Summary, not the Assessment, or
  • the customer is receiving an income support payment (including zero rate), and the customer record has a different address, or
  • not adversely affected - Principal Place of Residence
    • check the details in the claim. Discuss with the customer if there was any other adverse effect from the disaster, or
  • the impact reason is only major damage to major assets with a combined value less than $20,000 and free text in the claim may indicate eligible assets may or may not have a combined value of $20,000 or more
    • Note: check the details in the claim. Discuss the details with the customer to make sure all major assets have been included, and appropriate values have been assigned

Do any of these criteria apply?

  • Yes:
    • a specialist team will make 2 genuine attempts to contact a customer
    • for staff outside the specialist team, see Step 7 in the Assess and finalise claim table for how to allocate these claims to the correct team
  • No,
    • make one genuine contact attempt to contact the customer
    • reject the claim

6

Other declared LGAs + Read more ...

A genuine contact attempt must be made before finalising a claim as ineligible when a customer has claimed for a declared LGA for AGDRP or NZ DRP that is not a specified LGA at step 4 and:

  • the customer:
    • is receiving an income support payment (including zero rate), and
    • they said they were living at an address different to the address on their record, at the time of the disaster, or
  • the impact reason:
    • is only major damage to major assets, and
    • the total value of eligible assets advised is less than $20,000 but information in the claim, for example in the free text, suggests the combined value may be $20,000 or more

Do either of these criteria apply?

  • Yes:
    • make 1 genuine attempt to contact the customer
    • reject the claim
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Finalise without contact + Read more ...

No contact attempts are needed when a customer has claimed for a declared LGA for AGDRP or NZ DRP that is not a specified LGA at step 4 and:

  • there is no impact reason in the claim, or it is not an eligible impact reason
    • Note: this information is in the Claim Summary not the Assessment
  • the claim is only major damage to major assets and the combined value is less than $20,000, and there is no information in the claim to suggest the combined value of their asset(s) is $20,000 or more

Reject the claim.
