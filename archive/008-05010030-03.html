Starting the online claim + Read more ...

Customers can access the Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website to check if MOB is an appropriate payment for them. See Resources for a link.

To start a claim, they must have a Centrelink online account linked to their myGov account.

To start an online claim for MOB, customers need to:

  • Sign into myGov and access their linked Centrelink online account
  • From the MENU, select Payments and claims > Claims > Make a claim
  • On the Make a Claim page, select Get started in the Disabled, Ill or Injured category
  • Select Apply for Mobility Allowance
  • The Digital Assistant will appear in case they need help or want to ask a question
  • The Check your eligibility for Mobility Allowance page will show. The customer must read the Privacy Notice and answer streaming questions to check they are claiming the most appropriate payment based on their circumstances. For MOB, these Check Eligibility questions include:
    • Do you agree with the Privacy agreement?
    • Are you in Australia right now?
    • Are you either an Australian Citizen, a permanent Australian visa holder or a protected Special Category Visa (SCV) holder?
    • If you were to use public transport, would you need substantial assistance to use it because of a disability, illness or injury?
    • Are you currently receiving a funded package of support from the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS)?
    • Have you been provided a car under the Vehicle Assistance Scheme or the Motor Vehicle Compensation Scheme (MVCS) from the Department of Veterans’ Affairs?
    • Are you undertaking one or more of the following: Gainful Employment, Vocational training or study, Voluntary work, Job search activities, Disability Employment Service?
    • Do you expect your disability, illness or injury to impact your ability to use public transport for more than 12 months?
  • After selecting Claim now, a message box will appear to remind the customer to go to My profile to check and update their details before starting their claim. A My profile and Continue button are available. Selecting the:
    • Continue button opens the Claim navigation page to start their claim
    • My profile button opens the My profile to check their details. The customer can then select Return to your claim to go back to the Claim navigation page to start their claim
  • The Claim navigation page shows:
    • Status of the claim
    • Claim number
    • 4 claim sections that the customer must complete

See Resources for a link to the Services Australia website.

2

Claim progress + Read more ...

The Claim navigation page shows the sections of the claim that the customer needs to complete. They must complete these sections in order, and each section cannot be started until the previous sections are complete:

  • Confirm your basic details
  • Tell us about your situation
  • Tell us about your finances
  • Review your claim and submit

The customer selects Get started at the beginning of each section.

Each section contains modules with relevant questions. The modules are shown as tabs at the top of each page. On each question page, the customer can select:

  • Back to go back to the previous question or module
  • Save and Exit to save the claim at the current stage
  • Next to continue to the next module questions

Customers can select Help to read help text about the questions on the current page. Help text will open in a separate window and can be printed.

When the customer completes each module and reaches the end of the section, they will go back to the Claim navigation page to select Get Started in the next section.

As each section is completed, the customer has the option to go back to a previously completed section to change their answers by selecting Change in that section. They can go directly to the module they want to update by selecting the relevant tab.

To go back to the Claim navigation page after making changes, the customer should select the last module tab and then Next.

The claim does not have to be finished in one session. Customers can select Save and Exit on any of the claim pages to save their answers and come back later (see Item 3).

Customers must answer all mandatory questions before they can submit their claim, see Table 5.

Accessing a started online claim + Read more ...

To access their online claim, customers need to:

  • sign into myGov and select Centrelink
  • select Payments and claims > Claims > My online claims
    • if there are no claims in progress, the Make a Claim page shows
    • if there are incomplete, complete, withdrawn or cancelled claims, the My online claims page will show

If a customer seeks help with their started MOB online claim, the claim details can be viewed in Customer First.

4

Online claim status + Read more ...

Online claim status explanation:

  • Incomplete claims are claims that a customer has started or require additional information from a customer and submission before they are processed. Customers can select Continue claim to go to their claim or Cancel claim to cancel it
  • Submitted claims are claims waiting to be processed and cannot be edited by anyone online. Customers can withdraw their claim by selecting Withdraw from the Available Actions table
  • Draft claims are claims that a customer has started online but not submitted. Service Officers can use Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) to finalise questions and submit these claims in discussion with a customer.
    For more details, see Viewing and processing online and assisted customer claims
  • Cancelled claims are claims that were cancelled by a customer before the claim was submitted. Before the claim is cancelled customers are advised that:
    • If you decide to apply for a payment after cancelling this claim, you will need to complete and submit a new online claim
    • Do you understand that:
      after it is cancelled, this claim cannot be viewed or continued
      this action cannot be undone
  • Completed claims have been processed and are either granted or rejected.
    Note: the rejection reason is not shown on this page. The claim outcome (granted or rejected) must be checked in Workspace as the Social Application (claim workflow) will only show as Approved or Completed
  • Withdrawn claims are claims a customer submitted but then withdrew because they chose not to pursue it. Customers are advised before withdrawing their online claim that:
    • after it is withdrawn, it cannot be reinstated
    • they cannot apply for a formal review of this decision

This will cancel the claim activity and DOC the record. A Manual Follow-up (MFU) may be created if the claim withdrawal could not be completed. See Cancel or withdraw an online claim.

Withdrawing a claim on behalf of a customer

In the Social Application (claim workflow), update the User Status to Withdrawn and select Save. This will cancel the new claim activity and DOC the record. Issue a Q062 letter confirming claim withdrawal. See Withdrawal of claims.

5

What happens when a customer submits an online claim? + Read more ...

When a customer submits an online claim, the system automatically:

  • indexes a new claim activity or creates a Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity on the customer's record. Day one processing includes indexing the claim if necessary
  • creates a DOC which includes documents requested through Next steps
  • uploads customer contact details

Information a customer has given in the online claim can be viewed in the Review claim section.

Messages and notifications

Customers who provide a mobile phone number or email address will be advised they will automatically be subscribed to the Electronic Messaging service unless they have previously declined. They will be asked to nominate a preferred contact method (SMS or email) for notifications.

Customers will be subscribed to get their Centrelink mail online. It will be delivered through their myGov Inbox.

The customer is advised they can opt out of these services at any time.

2

Australian residence + Read more ...

The following information may pre-populate in the online claim if it is already known to Services Australia. A customer is able to change anything that is incorrect.

  • If the country of birth is:
    • Australia for either the customer or their partner, and they are currently living in Australia, they will be asked if they have ever lived outside of Australia
    • not Australia for either the customer or their partner, the Next steps page may advise of the requirement to give proof of residence status (for example, citizenship papers, passports or other documentation)
      The customer will be asked to give details of the date they started living in Australia and the countries they have lived in
  • If the country of citizenship is:
    • not Australia and their country of birth is different to their country of citizenship, they will be asked what date they became a citizen of that country
    • not Australia, and the customer does not have a Department of Home Affairs reference on their Centrelink record, Visa details will be requested on the Visa Details page
    • Australia but the country of birth is not Australia, they will be asked for the date they became an Australian citizen
  • Currently living in a country other than Australia? Use this to determine if the customer is currently living in Australia, otherwise the claim may be rejected.
    Note: for claims lodged from 1 July 2016, customers residing on Norfolk Island are considered to be living in Australia
  • Have they ever lived outside Australia?
    • if they answer Yes, they will be asked for the date they started living in Australia and the Countries lived in page will show. If this date is before 1965, they will be asked for the name of the ship/airline that they (and/or their partner) first arrived in Australia, the place where they first arrived, and the name they were using at the time of arrival
    • customers who have ever lived on Norfolk Island before 1 July 2016 must select Yes. If they have never lived in Australia before this date, they must enter 1 July 2016 as the date they started living in Australia.
      For more information, see Requesting and coding additional residence data for Norfolk Island claims
  • Have they travelled outside Australia including short trips and holidays? If they answer Yes, they will be asked to provide the date they last entered or left Australia, their passport number and the country that issued their passport

Current activity + Read more ...

Add an activity + Read more ...

The customer must select Add, and then choose a work, study or job seeking activity from a dropdown list.

This dropdown list includes a number of options related to work, study or job seeking. The customer is then prompted to give further details in the fields below.

The fields vary depending on the type of activity chosen from the dropdown list. All options require the customer to provide:

  • details of the activity such as the name of the business, employer, organisation or provider
  • address of the business, employer, organisation or provider
  • further information, with the details required varying depending on the type of activity selected. This may include course dates, hours of employment, date commenced employment and a question which asks if the customer is required to travel to and from home to undertake their activities

3

Advance payment + Read more ...

The customer is shown an option to get an advance payment for at least 6 months, if their claim is successful.

If they elect to get an advance payment of MOB, they will not get any fortnightly payment for 6 months.

If the customer indicates they want to get an advance payment, they are asked if they plan to leave Australia within the next 6 months (even for a short holiday).

If the customer selects Yes, they are advised:
To receive a Mobility Allowance Advance Payment you must remain qualified for this payment throughout the next 6 month period. You have advised that you will be travelling overseas in the next 6 months therefore you are not eligible to receive an Advance Payment of Mobility Allowance

Tell us about your finances

MOB is not income or asset tested, but if a customer’s working hours/income vary, their MOB payment may vary between the standard and higher rate. They may have changes from open employment or Supported Wage System (SWS) to supported employment.

Payment destination + Read more ...

Current payment destination details will show if the customer is currently in receipt of a payment. The customer can either select this account or add a new one.

Customers need to provide bank account details, including:

  • branch (BSB) number
  • account number
  • name of account

If the customer requested a nominee to get their payments, they are required to give their own bank account details to make sure they get their payment until their nominee arrangement starts.

Payment destination page in Assisted Customer Claim (ACC)

If the customer advises they do not have a bank account and they have barriers to opening a bank account, a temporary exemption can be granted for 28 days.
Note: the customer will not receive any payments until a payment destination is provided.

The Payment destination page in ACC can be bypassed by selecting the I do not have bank account details checkbox. No account information will be saved and the Service Officer can continue the claim through to submission.

Before selecting the checkbox, consider all of the following:

  • this option must not be selected where the customer has a bank account but is unable to provide the details when ACC is being completed
  • this checkbox must only be selected if the customer does not own a bank account and is unable to provide a payment destination within 14 days because they have barriers in opening a bank account or where no payment destination is provided within a paper claim
  • where this option is selected, the customer must be advised that they will not receive any payments until a payment destination is provided
  • the customer must be advised to provide their bank details as early as possible

For more information, see Payment destination.

Review claim + Read more ...

The Review your claim page contains a summary of all of the information given by a customer so they can check what they have entered is correct.

The customer’s answers are grouped into sections:

  • About me
  • My situation
  • My Finances

Information from My profile will show on the Review your claim page. If customers have not completed all required details, an Update required message will appear in red. They can select the Update button to go to My profile to update these sections before going back to their claim.

If any other information in the claim needs to be changed, the customer can select the Update button next to the answers that require changing. This will take them back to those question pages in the online claim.

If all of the information is correct, the customer must tick the Declaration box that says 'I have read, understood, and accept my obligations'. Then they can select Submit.

2

Next steps + Read more ...

Customers can access their submitted online claim to return to the Next steps page at any time.

This page includes all of the following:

  • confirmation the customer’s claim has been submitted
  • confirmation of the date the claim was submitted and the claim number
  • the tasks the customer needs to complete to finalise their claim, including providing verification documents

Three sections may show:

  • Documents and forms. This section includes:
    • list of supplementary information and supporting documentation required from the customer
    • date these documents are due
    • a link to download or print required forms
    • an option to have the information mailed to them
    • an Upload documents button to use the Upload documents service
    • a link to a printable checklist of documents and forms required
    • a link to the Upload your Centrelink documents online website page that tells them how to upload documents using their Centrelink online account or the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app
  • Medical Report. This section includes:
    • a link to a fillable PDF version of the Medical Report - Mobility Allowance (MA002)
    • the date by which the Medical Report must be returned
    • information about how the Medical Report can be completed and how to return it to Services Australia
  • Additional information. This section includes:
    • the due date for any tasks
    • links to further information and forms on the website about 'Confirming your identity'
    • Services Australia postal address if they want to post original document to confirm their identity
    • information about Essential Medical Equipment Payment Assistance
    • information about how to withdraw the claim

Printing the online claim + Read more ...
