Customer enquires about claiming payments for child + Read more ...

Customers may be eligible for:

  • Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and/ Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
  • FTB only and Newborn Supplement (NBS) and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU)

Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) is paid to fathers or partners.

PPL and DAP are not payable for a child who entered care prior to the process of adoption commencing (referred to as a known adoption).

NBS/NBU is also available for an adopted child of any age who was placed into care as part of the process of adoption. i.e. the child was not in their care prior to the adoption commencing process of adoption (known adoption).

In cases of foster care, customers may be eligible for NBS and NBU under the entrustment to care category. A customer will not be eligible for NBS for a known adoption.

Timeframes for claiming + Read more ...

PPL and DAP

Generally customers must claim PPL and DAP before the first anniversary of the child’s entry into care as part of the adoption process or surrogacy arrangement.

However, to receive the maximum amount they must claim:

  • within the time limits for claiming the maximum PPL period
  • at least 14 days before the child’s first birthday or first anniversary of adoption/surrogacy

Child Care Subsidy (CCS)

CCS can only be backdated a maximum of 28 days from date of claim lodgement. However, the start date must be the Monday of a CCS fortnight. The system determines the start date when the claim is processed.

6

Claim for PPL + Read more ...

  • Customers claiming the PPL period are required to nominate a PPL period start date. This can be the date the child entered care or a later date. It can be backdated to a child's entry into care if an effective claim is lodged within 28 days of the child's entry into care date. See Determining a customer’s Paid Parental Leave (PPL) period start date
  • Customers must verify that the birth mother has relinquished care of the child.
  • For children that enter care on or after 1 July 2020, PPL includes 30 Flexible PPL days. These days:
    • can be accessed before or after the customer returns to work, and
    • must be claimed within 2 years of the date the child entered care
  • The 13 month (392 days) PPL work test period starts prior to the child's expected or actual entry to care date for adoption and surrogacy. If the initial primary carer is eligible for PPL, they can then transfer some/ all of it to a secondary claimant. See Eligibility for PPL as a secondary claimant
  • Customers who lodge an early claim and are eligible for can change the expected entry to care date of their child

PPL is not payable for:

  • adopted children who entered care over the age of 16 years
  • a child entrusted to care under a formal foster care arrangement and the foster parents adopt the child at a later date. The temporary foster parents did not have the child entrusted to their care as part of the adoption process. The initial placement was for foster care purposes and the intention was not adoption at the time of placement
  • a child entrusted to care under a kinship care arrangement, unless:
    • the customer is in the process of adopting the child, or
    • has a Family Court of Australia parentage order for the child
    • These children will otherwise be treated as a child in care as part of a formal foster care arrangement
  • a child that:
    • is initially placed in the individual's care as part of a decision by the relevant state or territory child protection agency, and
    • a court subsequently entrusts the child to the individual's care as part of a permanent care order
  • a known adoption

Note: the adoption process does not need to be finalised for the adoptive parent to meet eligibility for PPL.

For:

  • standalone PPL claims, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • FTB claims, go to Step 7

7

Claim for FTB + Read more ...

A customer may be eligible for both PPL and FTB. NBS/NBU can only be in the assessment if the customer is not eligible for PPL. See Eligibility for Newborn Supplement (NBS) and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU).

NBS/NBU is not payable for a known adoption. Carers who have a child enter care before the age of 1 can still be assessed using the entrustment to care rules. This includes both foster carers and other non-parent carers.

For a known adoption, go to Step 8.

Otherwise, see Step 1, Table 2.

8

Known adoption + Read more ...

The child is in care as part of a known adoption.

PPL or DAP eligibility

The adoptive parent would not be eligible for PPL or DAP if the child was not placed in their care by an authorised party for the purpose of an adoption.

The claim should automatically reject with reason KAD (Known Adoption) if:

  • the child was already entrusted to the customers care, and
  • the adoption is formalising the parental relationship

If the automatic rejection does not occur:

  • cancel the existing claim and re-enter the claim information using the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC)
  • at question ‘Was <child’s name> in your care prior to the adoption process?’ select ‘yes’

This will trigger the claim to automatically reject KAD (Known Adoption).

NBS eligibility

It is essential to establish if the child:

  • has been entrusted to the customer's care by an authorised party as part of the process of adoption, or
  • had already been entrusted to the customer's care before the adoption process

In cases of foster care, the customer may be eligible for NBS and NBU under the entrustment to care category. A customer will not be eligible for NBS under the adoption criteria if a child is a known adoption.

See Eligibility for Newborn Supplement (NBS) and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU).

2

Documentation for adoption/surrogacy + Read more ...

Customers must provide information confirming the child has entered their care as part of the process of adoption or surrogacy. They must supply this before receiving payments.

Documentation to verify

For an adoption, documents include:

  • adoption papers, or
  • other confirmation from an authorised party stating the child has been entrusted to the customer's care as part of the process

For a surrogacy arrangement, documents include:

  • the formal surrogacy agreement or supporting evidence of a surrogacy agreement. If unable to make a determination, contact technical support who may complete an online referral to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk. Note: surrogacy agreement referrals to Level 2 are only required as part of the post-birth claim process

The agency can use confirmation of the adoption or related information as verification of the child's birth.

Kinship care

For kinship care, documents include:

  • Family court documents, such as a parentage order, adoption papers, or
  • other confirmation from an authorised party stating the child has been entrusted to the customer's care as part of the process of adoption

If the customer does not have these documents, the change of care is deemed permanent and assessed as a foster care relationship. The customer will be ineligible for PPL and should be encouraged to test their eligibility to NBS/NBU for the child.

This includes Queensland customers under the Meriba Omasker Kaziw Kazipa (Torres Strait Islander Traditional Child Rearing Practice) Act 2020 passed in Queensland Parliament. This Act provides legal recognition in Queensland of Torres Strait Islander families’ continued use of traditional child rearing practice. This law only applies to Queensland customers. See Resources for examples.

See also Traditional child rearing practices of Torres Strait Islander people.

Create new child record and process claim + Read more ...

Create a new CRN for the child

Code the child's name using the details provided by the adoptive parents and adoption agency. Do not record the birth parents details on the child’s new CRN.

When processing the claim ensure the correct coding is completed on the CHM screen and !CHRE page.

For adoptions, including when a child is placed with foster carers as part of an adoption process and for the purpose of adoption

Code the following fields on the CHM screen with the adoption details:

  • Event Date: code the date the child was adopted or entrusted to the customers care
  • Adopted: Y
  • Adopt outside Australia: Yes only. If child is adopted from Australia leave this blank
  • Date of Arrival: the date that the child entered Australia
  • Adoption type: This is a mandatory field. Options are:
    • Known
    • Local, or
    • Overseas
  • Principal Carer Type: Adoptive Parent
  • On the !CHRE page code:
    • Relationship: Adoptive parent and if the customer has a partner. Select this person's relationship to the child from the drop down box

Proof of birth contains birth mothers name

If the customer has adopted a newborn child and they have not received proof of birth of the child, contact may be required with the relevant adoption agency. The adoption agency may:

  • provide a letter from the hospital, or in isolated cases
  • have the Newborn Child Declaration (FA081) from the hospital

If these documents are provided, DO NOT attach them to the adoptive parent’s record. Refer the case to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk who will engage the New Parent Programme Team to arrange appropriate storage of the document.

Foster Care Health Care Card for children placed with foster carers for the purpose of adoption

Where a customer is claiming PPL for a child entrusted to them by an adoption agency as part of the process of adoption:

  • an override will need to be recorded to enable the foster Care Health Care Card (FST) to issue/continue
  • Refer the case to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk who will engage the New Parent Programme Team to apply the override. Ensure the details of the case are provided, including the customer's CRN
