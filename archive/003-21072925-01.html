This document explains the explanation of decision (review) process for the COVID-19 Disaster Payment and Income support payment customers - COVID-19 Disaster Payment.
Note: Do not use this document if there is an error with an automatic payment.

Review

There is no time limit to request an explanation of a decision.

If a customer does not understand or does not agree with the reason/s for a Services Australia decision, they can ask for an explanation of the decision.

A Suitably Skilled Officer will provide an explanation of the decision.

The Suitably Skilled Officer will:

  • investigate the decision
  • correct any identified errors
  • undertake any reassessment, and
  • contact the customer to explain the decision

No further appeal can be considered by:

  • an Authorised Review Officer (ARO), or
  • the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT)

When a customer has advised of a change in circumstances and the customer should have received the higher rate of payment, (for example, the customer was paid $450 but should have been paid $750), if the customer:

  • has not advised of a change in circumstances within 14 days, a review by a Suitably Skilled Officer is needed
  • has advised of a change in circumstances within 14 days, a review by a Suitability Skilled Officer is not needed

For Abridged customer claims, customers must meet 2 further requirements if advising of a change of circumstances within 14 days. They must:

  • previously have received an automatic payment at the higher rate, and
  • make a declaration of actual hours lost making them eligible for the higher rate

If the abridged customer claim does not meet these 2 requirements, a review is needed.

If, following an explanation of a decision, staff identify a customer has a change in circumstances and automatic payments need to stop, see COVID-19 Disaster Payment - automatic payment and change of circumstances.
