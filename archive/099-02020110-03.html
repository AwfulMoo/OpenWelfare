Undoing a death action

Step

Action

1

Check if death action taken on incorrect record + Read more ...

Has the death action been taken on the incorrect customer record?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Immediately undo death action on incorrect record where identified at time of recording + Read more ...

If it is identified the death action has been taken on the incorrect customer record at the time the death is recorded, take immediate steps to correct the record.

Has it been identified the death action has been taken on an incorrect customer record before any payments were released (that is, on day 1)?

  • Yes:
    • stop payments which may have been triggered by the death action (for example, Bereavement Payment where required)
    • stop any letters or notifications triggered by the incorrect death action
    • action the notification of death on the correct customer record
    • go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Decision to undo a death action + Read more ...

When undoing a death action, record on a DOC:

  • the action taken, and
  • the reason why

Undo the death action according to the circumstances:

  • Where notification is received at a later date that the customer is not deceased and payments are to be restored, only undo the death action if there is no doubt the customer is alive. If in doubt, do not undo the death action, request confirmation to be provided at the customer's service centre
  • Where notification is received that the customer's date of death was incorrect, the death action can be undone provided there is no uncertainty about the correct date of death. If in doubt, request confirmation (for example, the death certificate) before undoing and recoding the death action
  • Where a death action was performed on the wrong customer's record (for example, the partner has been recorded as deceased instead of the customer) action must be taken to ensure payments owed are paid, debts are raised where applicable and the death is recorded on the correct customer's record
  • Where a death action may need to be undone for some activities to be completed. For example, to process a claim that was lodged but not processed before the customer dies, or to update care circumstances for a dependent child

Only staff at the APS5 level or above can undo a death action. For Smart Centre staff, these requests meet the criteria for direct referral to a Senior Support Officer (SSO). They do not need to contact Technical Peer Support (TPS). The action taken must be clearly documented on the record using the Escalated to SSO Fast Note. See the Resources page for a link to the Direct Referral to SSO web form.

Create the Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic Escalation > Escalated to SSO.

To undo the death action:

  • for a child, go to Step 5
  • to correct the date of death of a child, go to Step 8
  • for an adult, go to Step 4

4

Undoing a death action for an adult + Read more ...

From the Name Search (IN) screen, enter the customer's details, using the Customer Reference Number (CRN) or the customer's surname, first initial and date of birth.

While in the customer's record, go to the Death/Executor (DEA) screen and undo the death action by either:

  • keying 'DEA' in the Nxt field on the current screen, or
  • selecting the Death/Executor option from the Customer Details Task Selector (CDTS) screen
  • key 'D' in the Action field to the left of the Date of Death field
  • key 'D' in the Action field to the left of the Date Executor Recorded field. Do this where there are executor details recorded. Note: this action is only required when undoing a death action on a wrong record
  • complete the Source and DOR fields in the screen footer
  • key 'Y' in the Confirm Deletion field in the screen footer to confirm deletion of the death record
  • press [Enter]

Go to Step 6.

5

Undoing a death action for a child + Read more ...

Go to the Child Selection (CHS) screen:

  • select the appropriate child
  • press [Enter]

The Child Task Selector (CHTS) will display:

  • select the Change Child (CCH) screen
  • delete date of death entry in the Death Date field
  • press [Enter]
  • complete the Source and DOR fields in the screen footer
  • press [Enter]

Go to Step 6.

6

Checking activity results + Read more ...

  • Check results on the Activity Results (AR) screen are correct across all impacted payments including any change of benefit rate, see Explaining and actioning negative adjustments on the Assessment Results (AR) screen. If unsure of results consult with the appropriate Level 2 Policy Helpdesk
  • Stop any letters or notifications triggered by the incorrect death action
  • Relationship links are automatically restored on the resurrection of a payment record
  • If the death needs to be recoded, such as after finalisation of a new claim, ensure a duplicate Lump Sum Bereavement Payment is not issued to the survivor

Finalise the activity and record details on a DOC.

Was the death action undone as the death was originally recorded on the wrong customer's record?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No:
    • To correct the date of death for a child, go to Step 8
    • Otherwise, procedure ends here

7

Intertwined Record + Read more ...

Where death action has been coded on the wrong customers record, this results in an intertwined record. A referral to the Data Quality Unit (DQU) is required for all cases, once the corrective action below has been completed.

Intertwined Referral form

Corrective action required

Where a death action has been recorded on the wrong customer's record, Service Officers must consider implications that have occurred as a result and fix all errors. Consider the following actions.

See the Resources page for examples of action required following an incorrect death action.

Executor Details

Delete the executor details when undoing a death action due to the customer not being deceased. For example, the death action was done on the wrong record.

To delete the details:

  • key 'D' in the Action field to the left of the Date Executor Recorded

Review of entitlement

Review the payments made to the deceased (and spouse if applicable) to ensure that on-going rate of payment and/or Bereavement Payments owed are paid.

Note: where a Bereavement Payment has been paid incorrectly to a deceased customer as a result of death action being taken on the incorrect record (such as, the Bereavement Payment should have been paid to the surviving partner) a debt must be raised on the deceased person's record and the Bereavement Payment made to the correct person if entitled.

Possible overpayments

If a Bereavement Payment or income support/other payment has been made incorrectly as a result of the original death action, see whichever is appropriate:

  • Requesting the return of a direct credit, New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) payment made to an incorrect recipient, or
  • Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer

Request for a refund from a financial institution (FI)

If a FI requests a refund on the same day a death action occurs:

  • stop the refund
  • send an urgent request via mySupport

Otherwise, the letter to the FI will issue overnight, and the request cannot be stopped.

The FI will either:

  • return the payment/s, which will need to be reissued, or
  • reject the payment/s, which are then raised as a debt

See Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer.

Returned payments

Any payments returned from the deceased customer's financial institutions must be actioned as they may be owed to the estate of the deceased person. See Replacing direct credit, New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) payments returned from financial institution.

Carer Payment

If the person is a care receiver, the carer's Care Allowance and/or Carer Payment will need to be restored if the payments have cancelled. See Restoration of Carer Payment (CP) and Restoration of Carer Allowance (CA). If the carer's current payments have not cancelled, check to ensure they are current for the care receiver.

Department of Veterans' Affairs customers

If the Reference Numbers (NU) screen shows a Unique Identifier Number (UIN) is linked to the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA), contact the Centrelink DVA Clearances Team (SCS) as the coding will have caused incorrect data exchange with DVA and they will need to be advised of the error.

Letters

  • Stop any letters or notifications triggered by the incorrect death action. Finalise activity and record details on a DOC
  • Issue manual letters (if required) to ensure legal requirements for communications about payments have been communicated appropriately

Procedure ends here.

8

Correcting date of death for a child + Read more ...

To correct an incorrect date of death for a child, go to the Child Selection (CHS) screen:

  • Select the appropriate child
  • Press [Enter]

The Child Task Selector (CHTS) will display.

  • Select the Change Child (CCH) screen
  • Overstrike date of death entry in the Death Date field with the correct date of death
  • Press [Enter]

Check that results on Activity Results (AR) screen are correct across all impacted payments including any change of benefit rate. If unsure of results consult with the appropriate Level 2 Policy Helpdesk.

Stop any letters or notifications triggered by the incorrect death action.

Finalise activity on the AR screen and record details on a DOC.
