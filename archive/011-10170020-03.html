Evidence of immunisation + Read more ...

Generally customers are not required to provide documentary evidence that a child has been immunised.

Advise the customer that the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) assesses and provides the child’s immunisation status. See the Resources page for AIR contact details.

The information held on the AIR for a child is displayed on their immunisation history statement.

If the customer is providing evidence because the information held on the AIR needs to be updated (including evidence of vaccinations received overseas), advise the customer that they will need to take this evidence to their vaccination provider who will update the AIR if required.

Centrelink Service Officers cannot update a child's immunisation status using any evidence provided by the customer. Only a vaccination provider can update a child's immunisation details on the AIR.

If a child is not fully immunised, a customer may lodge evidence for the following reasons:

  • child has commenced a catch up vaccination schedule
  • child has a vaccine exemption due to medical contraindication or natural immunity

If the exemption information is incomplete, see Immunisation medical exemptions in Australian Immunisation Register (AIR).

Action immunisation evidence

Table 2: Process for Centrelink Service Officers when immunisation evidence has been supplied.

Step

Action

1

Determine if the new evidence provided is suitable for the AIR + Read more ...

Examples of suitable evidence include:

  • An Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) - Immunisation medical exemption form (IM011)
  • An Immunisation history form (IM013)
  • An immunisation encounter form (IM018)
  • A letter or medical or community health practice printout which is signed by a recognised vaccination provider that includes details and dates of immunisations. Ensure the immunisation provider number is visible on the letter or printout. Note: from 1 August 2020 Other Immunisation data (ODDs) will be returned to the provider with a letter advising how this data should be lodged. For more information, see Process claims in the Australian Immunisation Register
  • An Immunisation exemption conscientious objection form (IMMU12) signed and dated by a recognised vaccination provider before 1 January 2016

Examples of unsuitable evidence include:

  • The Immunisation history statement which is a record of the existing information held by the AIR and will not result in any updates to the customer's record
  • Child personal health record books and cards. This type of evidence generally does not contain the required immunisation information, or identify the child or vaccination provider
  • A letter or medical or community health practice printout, which is signed by a recognised vaccination provider that is seeking an exemption
  • Overseas immunisation evidence not signed by a recognised vaccination provider. The customer should provide details of the child's immunisation history to a recognised Australian vaccination provider who will check if the child is fully vaccinated according to the Australian vaccination schedule, recommend additional vaccinations if appropriate and complete an Immunisation History form (IM013) and provide this to the AIR

Is the evidence suitable for the AIR and signed by a recognised vaccination provider?

The Resources page contains more details on what evidence is suitable for the AIR.

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Identify type of evidence + Read more ...

What document has the customer provided?

  • Immunisation history statement, go to Step 5
  • Child personal health record book, go to Step 4
  • Overseas immunisation evidence, go to Step 3
  • Evidence for an exemption to be determined by the policy department Secretary - refer to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk for assessment. The Resources page contains a link to the helpdesk

The Resources page contains more detailed information about unstructured evidence that may be provided and correct processes that should be followed.

3

Child immunised overseas + Read more ...

Immunisation evidence can only be recorded on the AIR if it is certified by a current Australian general practitioner or vaccination provider.

The customer should provide details of the child's immunisation history to an Australian vaccination provider, who will check the child is fully vaccinated according to the Australian vaccination schedule, recommend additional vaccinations if appropriate and complete an Immunisation History form (IM013) and provide this to the AIR through the AIR secure site. If the document requires translation the customer may be eligible for assistance from the Department of Social Services free translating service. The Resources page also provides contact details for the free translating service.

If the customer is providing immunisation evidence as part of an Overseas immunisation (OSI), Child Overseas at reconciliation override, see View and update a child's immunisation status and assess or update immunisation exemptions for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

Record details on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

4

Child's personal health record book + Read more ...

A child's personal health record book or any similar document is not suitable immunisation evidence and must not be sent to the AIR.

This evidence cannot be used to update the AIR as they usually do not contain the required immunisation information or sufficiently identify the child or the vaccination provider.

Centrelink Service Officers must not discuss specific vaccinations or vaccination schedules with customers. Refer the customer to their vaccination provider.

Procedure ends here.

5

Immunisation history statement + Read more ...

Immunisation history statements are generated by the AIR and contain the information held for an individual on the AIR. These are not to be referred to the AIR.

Check the immunisation status listed on the statement. The statement includes a date of issue and immunisation status (‘up to date’ or ‘not up to date’). Outstanding vaccinations and due dates will be listed at the bottom.

  • If the child's record is not linked with the AIR, set up a link for the child with the AIR for the relevant payment type. Advise the customer that once the link is set up, the AIR will provide the child's immunisation status
  • If a link has been set up and the immunisation status received is not consistent with the status on the immunisation history statement, see Assisting customers when immunisation status is incorrect
  • If an Immunisation history statement has been lodged as part of a request for CCS restoration, scan using the classification code 'UNSIH'. Go to Step 6
  • If an Immunisation history statement has been lodged because a customer (for a child under 14 years of age) needs to confirm the child is immunised for an Income Management exemption assessment, scan using the classification 'UNSIH'. Go to Step 6
  • If an Immunisation history statement has been lodged as part of an FTB Part A supplement override request, scan using the classification 'UNSIH'. Go to Step 6

Scans classified as UNSIH auto transfer to FCC for assessment. The Resources page contains a link to the Reclassification page for these scans. Do not manually transfer any work items to the AIR.

Otherwise, the immunisation history statement must not be scanned. Return it to the customer and advise that the child's immunisation status will be provided through the AIR link. If they believe that the information held on the AIR and the Immunisation History statement is not correct, they will need to contact their vaccination provider.

Record details on a DOC. The Resources page contain a link to the image of the Immunisation history statement.

Procedure ends here.

6

Scan and upload new evidence + Read more ...

Scan and upload the evidence onto the record of the customer receiving the relevant payment for the child (e.g. FTB).

See Scanning Centrelink documents using an MFD

When scanning immunisation evidence, use the following classifications:

  • IM011, IM013, IMMU11 or IMMU13 when the evidence was lodged on an approved AIR form, (as these forms are barcoded, these should be automatically classified correctly), or
  • UNSIMMU if the manual immunisation evidence needs to be referred to the AIR. This classification code should only be used when the immunisation evidence has been deemed to be suitable for the AIR. See Step 1 above for examples of suitable evidence

Immunisation evidence classified with these classifications will be automatically transferred to the AIR.

  • An AIR Manual Follow-up (MFU) work item will automatically transfer to the AIR processing team for assessment
  • The evidence will appear on the Child Immunisation Summary guided procedure under the Evidence processing section
  • An automatic DOC will be created on the customer's record
  • The evidence will then be validated, assessed (this may take up to 14 days) and updated on the AIR. If this results in a change to the child's immunisation status, the updated status transfers to the Centrelink record (this normally occurs overnight)
  • Do not manually transfer any other activities to AIR (e.g. DOCs, scan MFUs). Do not re-categorise other work items to AIR staff

The Child Immunisation Summary guided procedure, under the Evidence processing section, will display the processing progress of immunisation evidence transferred to the AIR. It may be useful when answering customer enquiries about immunisation status.

  • Awaiting processing. Advise them that evidence has been received that the validation, update and data transfer process may take up to 14 days

'Updated. Check child's immunisation status. If the child is still not up-to-date after all evidence has been recorded, refer the customer to their vaccination provider. Do not discuss specific vaccinations or vaccination schedules with customers

  • Not updated. Advise them the reason the evidence was not updated and discuss suitable action
  • For all enquiries, confirm that a link to the AIR has been set up for the relevant payment type for each child

Record details on a DOC.

For action taken by the AIR specialist or Families and Child Care (FCC) Processing team, see the Immunisation evidence process table.

Update is not required + Read more ...

For AIR specialist team only.

Do not update the AIR if the evidence provided is not appropriate or is already recorded. This may include evidence that is not immunisation related, an Immunisation history statement that has been referred incorrectly, or if the evidence does not meet AIR requirements.

To record that no update to the AIR was made:

  • select Not Updated
  • select the valid reason from the Not Updated Reason drop down menu:
    • AIR Immunisation history statement
    • Child Personal Health record book
    • document is not immunisation related
    • history already recorded
    • insufficient immunisation details
    • other - complete the Comment Box to state why the AIR was not updated
    • overseas provider/document
    • unable to identify child
    • unable to identify immunisation provider

If there are multiple images of evidence repeat this process. If an update is required for a piece of evidence, go to Step 3.

Otherwise, go to Step 5.

6

FCC Processing - no update is required + Read more ...

For Families and Child Care (FCC) Processing staff only.

FCC Processing staff are required to action scanned immunisation history statements (classification code 'UNSIH').

FCC Processing staff will check for outstanding payment issues and if necessary a Families override officer can make an assessment.

Check what action may be required:

  • If the link with the AIR under AIR Link Status is:
    • established (LINKED), continue with other checks
    • not established (NOT LINKED), take steps to set up a link
  • If CCS has been cancelled or child made ineligible:
    • re-grant may be required or the customer may need to reclaim CCS
  • If an FTB Part A rate reduction override is required, see View and update a child's immunisation status and assess or update immunisation exemptions for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
  • If the document has been misclassified, re-upload the document under the correct classification. e.g. if it has been supplied for:
    • Healthy Start for School
    • exemption from Income Management assessment
    • proof of residence
    • proof of identity
      See Scanning Centrelink documents using an MFD
  • If the child is up to date but the updated AIR status was not received until after the end of the allowable timeframe (pre 2018-19 financial year):
    • A reconciliation outcome may need to be manually over-ridden. If the child met (MET) the requirements and the agency was notified before the end of the allowable timeframe. See Immunisation requirements for Family Tax Benefit Part A supplement
  • If the child has not returned to Australia and remains outside of Australia at reconciliation for FTB (for a financial year between 2012-13 and 2017-18):
    • an assessment of evidence for an overseas exemption may be required
    • this does not apply to CCS and FTB from 1 July 2018
  • investigate other problems, e.g.
    • Duplicate Personal Identification Number (PIN)/Supplementary Identification Number (SIN)
    • Duplicate child records or Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • Child's date of birth or spelling of name is incorrect at Medicare or Centrelink
    • Linked to the wrong record on the AIR. Refer to a Families Override Officer to correct a link set up in error
  • if further information is required from the customer, go to Step 7

7

Contact the customer for more information + Read more ...

For Families and Child Care (FCC) Processing staff only.

Contact with the customer may be required to advise that the immunisation evidence has not resulted in a change to the child's status. Advise the customer what further action or information may be required, such as:

  • If they provided a child personal health record book they may need to contact the vaccination provider and request they report the vaccinations to the AIR
  • If they have provided overseas immunisation evidence, they will need to provide proof of the overseas immunisation to their vaccination provider who will complete an IM013 form which can be lodged with the AIR
  • If they have provided an Immunisation history statement for a child history statement, check the date of issue of the statement and the immunisation status. Outstanding vaccinations will be listed at the bottom

The Resources page contain a link to an image of the Immunisation history statement.

Document action taken + Read more ...

For Families and Child Care (FCC) Processing staff only.

The AIR/Centrelink Liaison Team is made up of staff based in Centrelink and also in the AIR in Medicare (staff based in Hobart and Perth). The teams work together on urgent and complex issues affecting the Centrelink/AIR link process and immunisation status data for a child.

Face to Face Service Delivery, Smart Centre and AIR staff + Read more ...

Should this enquiry be escalated?

Before escalation, confirm there is a valid reason for urgency, such as:

  • Customer Relations Unit (CRU) contact

  • Ministerial involvement
  • Emergency declaration is in place

  • when the AIR has not updated a child's record after immunisation evidence was accepted by Services Australia and the customer wants to discuss why the evidence was not accepted

5

AIR liaison referral to Centrelink liaison + Read more ...

AIR liaison

The AIR liaison staff will contact the Centrelink liaison after the following checks have been made:

  • the child is up to date (or has an approved exemption) on the AIR
  • the status has already been transmitted to Centrelink
  • the customer has already contacted Centrelink/AIR more than once to discuss their issue (for example, there is a mismatch with the AIR)
  • there is a reason for urgency (as detailed previously)

Refer the case to Centrelink liaison.

Centrelink liaison

Upon receiving a referral, the liaison staff at Centrelink will:

  • identify the relevant child
  • confirm the child's Medicare details with the customer and the AIR and take appropriate corrective action
  • monitor the record to check the AIR link is correctly set up, and any appropriate follow up action is taken
  • address any Urgent Issues on the record and identify and action any future potential service recovery issues
  • keep a record of contacts by the AIR and customer issues to identify any trends (for example, inappropriate AIR referrals, potential Centrelink system issues)

Members of the liaison team will not be responsible for processing claims or for any resulting reviews and appeals.

See Liaison between AIR and Centrelink staff for Australian Immunisation Register (AIR).

Procedure ends here.

6

Centrelink liaison referral to AIR liaison + Read more ...

When the AIR rejects manual immunisation evidence, the customer should contact the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR), not Centrelink, to discuss why the evidence was not accepted.

Centrelink liaison

Centrelink staff will contact the AIR liaison after the following checks have been made. Referrals are only accepted from the Service Support Officer (SSO) mailboxes.

  • Check:
    • there is a reason for urgency (as detailed previously)
    • the customer has already contacted Centrelink/AIR more than once to discuss their issue
    • that the referral is required to confirm ongoing Child Care Subsidy (CCS) entitlement or rate of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A
  • Refer to AIR liaison: Queries may include:
    • Immunisation status (met/not met) on a particular date (note this may be earlier than the Last Immunisation Update Event (LIUE) date)
    • Date the AIR was notified a child became up to date
    • Urgent evidence processing request
    • Personal details updates (including name and DOB) to resolve linking and data sharing issues
  • Create a DOC on the customer's record
  • Record any inappropriate referrals using the Staff Feedback Tool. The Resources page contains a link to the tool

Centrelink/AIR liaisons should ensure they meet privacy obligations by not requesting or including explicit medical information (e.g. vaccinations given, specific dates) for children over 14 years of age in correspondence and DOCs on the customer record. For more information, see Authentication and release of information for Australian Immunisation Register (AIR). Note: details of individual vaccinations should not be requested.

AIR liaison

Upon receiving a referral, the liaison staff at the AIR will:

  • Confirm the child's immunisation status (‘up to date’ or ‘not up to date’)
  • Arrange to action any outstanding evidence
  • Address any outstanding issues
  • Provide a manual assessment of the individual’s immunisation status on a specified date if this is required to support a payment decision. Details of individual vaccinations should not be provided to Centrelink

Members of the liaison team will not be responsible for processing claims or for any resulting reviews and appeals.
