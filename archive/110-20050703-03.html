3

Categorisation letters issued from 29 October 2021 + Read more ...

Letters were sent to group members notifying them:

  • of the category of the debt/s
  • if eligible to receive a settlement payment based on the category
  • how to register and update payment destination

Note: bank account details are not to be updated manually via the Payment Destination Summary screen (PAS) or in the payment destination workflow. This will NOT update payment destination for the settlement payment. Eligible registered group members must have payment destination updated within the class action settlement transaction.

Eligible group members on a payment or who received a refund

These eligible group members did not need to do anything to receive a settlement payment. This will be paid directly into the bank account already recorded on the group member's Centrelink record. Group members can update their payment destination within the class action settlement transaction by contacting Income Compliance staff on 1800 171 846.

Eligible group members who do not have current payment details

These eligible group members needed to register and update banking details via their outstanding online task, or with the Income Compliance staff via the class action settlement transaction. Group members must have registered by 11 February 2022 or they cannot be paid the settlement. Eligible registered group members can update their payment destination within the class action settlement transaction by contacting Income Compliance staff on 1800 171 846.

7

Queries or disputes + Read more ...

Initial queries were assessed by specialist Income Compliance staff.

Group members had until 10 December 2021 to query or dispute if they thought:

  • the agency had not included all of their eligible debts, or
  • the agency had not taken into account repayments the group member made on a debt, or
  • the debt was based on averaged ATO income information

As the time to query or dispute their debt categorisation ended on 10 December 2021 the categorisation of their debt/s cannot be reviewed and the debt/s remain in the same category/s.

Disputes involved a review of the group members’ categorisation outcome by a specialist officer (Tier 1).

If the group member disagreed with the Tier 1 outcome there was an option to escalate a dispute. This was addressed by the Scheme Assurer (Tier 2). The group member had until 7 January 2021 to dispute a Tier 1 outcome. There is no further course of action for group members if they disagree with the outcome of the Tier 2 dispute. See the Resources page for how to answer questions about how many reviews a group member can have.

Queries and disputes for the class action are handled differently to other complaints. Do not lodge using the ARO/Appeals workflow or as an escalated complaint.

If the group member enquires about Tier 2 disputes:

  • transfer the call using Softphone to Compliance-ClassActionSettlement Line, or
  • give them the Income Compliance line number 1800  171  846

8

Payment eligibility + Read more ...

Group members may be eligible for a settlement payment if they have paid money towards a debt that was based in full, or in part, on averaged ATO income information or if all of the following applies:

  • the debt was based in full or in part on averaged ATO income information
  • the debt was recalculated based on information you gave us, like payslips
  • the group member paid back more than the recalculated amount

Not every group member will be eligible for a settlement payment:

  • under the terms of settlement, we will make payments similar to interest to eligible group members. It is based on the amount of debt repayments you made and for the period you were without the money. Group members who paid back more and were without this money for longer, will get larger payments
  • group members who paid back a small amount or were refunded soon after they made the payment will receive a smaller payment
  • most settlement payments will be between $50 and $300. Some eligible group members who only repaid small amounts may get less than $10
  • Services Australia aims to deliver settlement payments as quickly as possible to eligible group members. The settlement process will take time
  • the agency expects to start making payments by mid-2022. These will be spread out over a number of months
  • there is no need for the group member to call or visit us unless they have been asked to
