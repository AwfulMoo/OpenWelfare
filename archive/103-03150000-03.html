Answering participant enquiries about the CDC

1

Viewing details on the participant record + Read more ...

Participants who are or have recently participated in the CDC will have Income Quarantine 'IQN' displayed on their Benefit Status.

The Debit Card Trial Summary (Q2SU) screen displays:

  • a participant's current CDC status
  • the percentage of funds being restricted
  • the cashless account details (BSB and account number)

To identify a participant's payment split between their standard bank account and the cashless debit card:

  • go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen where it will display Multiple Destinations in the Sent To column for the affected payments
  • view the Payment Details (PD) screen showing a breakdown of the payment

The Debit Card Trial Notes Summary (Q2NS) screen records details keyed by the CDC Processing Team.

2

Activating a cashless debit card + Read more ...

The card issuer will send a CDC with instructions on how to activate the card.

Service Officers may be required to assist participants activate their card. Advise the participant they can activate their card through online banking/mobile app or by phoning the card issuer Customer Service Centre.

Participants can contact the card issuer for further support and assistance, such as:

  • resetting Personal Identification Numbers (PIN)
  • issuing replacement cards
  • assisting participants in setting up expenses, including BPAY and direct credit functions transfers
  • handling any fraudulent or disputed transactions
  • advising if the card issuer has any offices or Local Partners in the area that can assist with card activation or other services on their behalf

3

Complaints + Read more ...

Any complaints in relation to the Services Australia element of the CDC are handled under normal business protocols. See Managing customer complaints and feedback.

Participants should contact the card issuer if the complaint or feedback relates to the cashless debit card or other access to restricted funds.

If a service officer managing a complaint against the CDC needs technical advice or support, they can contact Technical support.

Complaints regarding the policy of the CDC should be directed to the Department of Social Services.

If a participant or third party is not satisfied with the way their concerns have been handled, they can contact the Commonwealth Ombudsman.

See the Resources page for a link to the website, and contact details for the card issuer, DSS and the Commonwealth Ombudsman.

It is not the responsibility of Service Officers to contact DSS or the card issuer on behalf of participants.

4

Centrepay and Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) and other Centrelink deductions and withholdings + Read more ...

Any new or existing Centrepay, RDS or any other Centrelink deductions or withholdings for participants will continue to be paid as per normal processing. All Centrelink deductions and withholdings will reduce the CDC participant's restricted portion of funds initially. If the total deductions/withholdings exceed the restricted percentage for that payment, then the unrestricted funds (cash component) will then be reduced.

Example: If a participant has 90% of their payment going out as deductions or withholdings (for example, Centrepay, Rent Deduction Scheme, Debt Withholdings and advance recoveries), this will be drawn from their restricted portion (80%), and the remaining 10% will be drawn from their unrestricted (cash) portion of funds. In this case, no funds will be paid to the restricted (CDC) account.

There is no change to Centrepay recoveries or processes for Centrepay funds returned by Third Party Organisations. These funds do not need to be compulsorily paid onto the participant's CDC if recovered or returned.

5

Review of Decision - Participant was only in CDC area temporarily or they moved before the CDC location determination date + Read more ...

Customers are triggered onto the CDC when their permanent home address (or term address if a full-time student) is in a CDC location. Some participants may advise they were only in the area temporarily or they moved before the CDC location determination date.

If it is established that the address change is legitimate (for example, the change should have been recorded as a temporary address, rather than a home address) or the address in the Cashless Debit Card location should have been ended before the CDC determination date for that location, then:

  • contact, the CDC Processing Team via Technical support to discuss whether the customer can be exited from the CDC, including if evidence is required, and if so
  • update the address as per normal processes

The CDC Processing Team will assess whether the participant can be exited from the CDC, taking into account address history and ties to the area, and apply correct exit coding to the record (see Ending Cashless Debit Card (CDC) participation).

In circumstances where there is doubt over when the change of address occurred or if the new address details are correct, then the participant may be asked to provide additional evidence.

Note: updating or deleting home address details for the purposes of circumventing the CDC will not automatically exit a participant. Address updates done incorrectly in an effort to avoid the CDC may result in placing the participant at risk of overpayment for additional payments such as Rent Assistance or Remote Area Allowance. Service Officers must not update addresses for the purposes of exiting a CDC participant without an initial discussion with the CDC Processing Team.

6

Reviews and Appeals + Read more ...

Participants may request an explanation or apply for a formal review of the decision to be placed onto the CDC. Under social security law, the following Services Australia elements of the CDC are reviewable:

  • The decision to be exited from the CDC
  • The agency's application of the CDC
  • Payment limit requests
  • Wellbeing determinations

If a participant wants to request an explanation or apply for a formal review of the decision, the usual review and appeal process applies. When recording a request for an explanation or an application for a formal review, use region code 'CDT' and under the Payment type the decision has been made against tab, enter 'IQN' (Income Quarantining). This will correctly refer request for an explanation to the CDC Processing Team, who are the Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) for the CDC. See Request for an explanation or application for a formal review.

If the participant is disputing that, their usual place of residence was not in a CDC area, staff should discuss this with the CDC Processing Team. It may be more appropriate to correct an error before a formal review is considered.

Customers who volunteer to transition from IM to the CDC in the NT cannot request a formal review.

Note: if a participant does not agree with a decision to be placed on the CDC made by the FRC, a State or Territory Child Protection Authority, or a Supporting People at Risk approved referring authority, the referring authority is responsible for reviewing the decision to exit the participant from the CDC program (Cape York or NT participants only).

Cashless debit cards, restricted percentage and split payment calculation

Table 2: This table contains further information about the use of the CDC, the restricted percentage and split payment calculation.

Item

Description

1

Cashless debit card + Read more ...

The card looks and operates like a normal bank card, except that it cannot be used to buy alcohol, gamble or withdraw cash.

The card can be used at most merchants with EFTPOS facilities nationwide, except merchants where the main business is the sale of alcohol or gambling services.

Merchants in CDC areas that are mixed businesses selling alcohol products or gambling services, in addition to other products or services, may be eligible to accept the card if they can demonstrate they can prevent the sale of excluded items. Merchants can contact the relevant card issuer for further information.

If a participant contacts to advise they are unable to make a purchase online or at a merchant that accepts visa and EFTPOS facilities, Service Officers should:

  • Ensure the participant is using the card correctly if at a merchant (correct PIN, available funds)
  • Check the card issuer's website to ensure they are not listed as a blocked merchant

If the merchant is not listed as blocked and the participant has made several attempts to use their card and it appears to be card issuer related not merchant related, a referral can be made to the CDC Processing Team for follow up with DSS and the card issuer.

If the request is urgent (participant is currently at a merchant) the Service Officer can call the CDC Processing Team directly or warm transfer the participant to the CDC Processing team.

The CDC Processing Team can approve Housing and Other Expense Limit increases for expenses such as housing, utilities, education, and medical services that cannot be paid via the CDC. These limit increases will enable the participant to pay these expenses directly from their CDC account. See Housing and Other Expense Limits for Cashless Debit Card (CDC) customers.

Any new or existing Centrepay or Rent Deduction Scheme expenses will be made from the participant's restricted amount initially. The unrestricted (cash) portion will only be reduced if the total deductions and withholdings exceed the restricted percentage for that payment.

2

Restricted percentage and split payment calculation + Read more ...

If an instalment of a restrictable payment is payable to a CDC participant or voluntary participant, the percentage differs depending on their location:

CDC locations of Ceduna, East Kimberley, Goldfields and Bundaberg and Hervey Bay

  • 80% of the gross amount of the restrictable payment is restricted (the restricted portion) and placed on the CDC
  • 20% of the gross amount of the payment is unrestricted (the unrestricted portion) and delivered to the participant's normal bank account

Cape York

  • 60%, 75% or 90% of the gross amount of the restrictable payment is restricted (the restricted portion) and placed on the CDC
  • the remaining 40%, 25% or 10% of the gross amount of the payment is unrestricted (the unrestricted portion) and delivered to the participant's normal bank account
  • The percentage is determined by the FRC

Northern Territory

  • 50% of the gross amount of the restrictable payment is restricted (the restricted portion) and placed on the CDC
  • 50% of the gross amount of the payment is unrestricted (the unrestricted portion) and delivered to the participant's normal bank account

Note: this percentage will be subject to the participant's previous IM measure. If a participant had a higher quarantined percentage, this percentage is applied to the CDC when the participant transitions.

100% of any eligible lump sums and arrears payments (including advance payments) will also be restricted and sent to the participant's CDC.

Crisis Payments, Hardship Advances and Urgent Payments, as well as the Australian Government Disaster Relief Payment (AGDRP) are not restrictable payments, and are paid to the participant's regular bank account by default (unless otherwise requested by the participant).

The restricted and unrestricted portions for instalment (regular) payments is calculated after the income and assets tests are applied for that fortnight.

Any regular withholdings or deductions, including Centrepay, Rent Deduction Scheme, child support, Advance Payment repayments, penalties and tax deductions will continue to be paid as normal, and will initially reduce the restricted portion.

The unrestricted portion (cash component) will only be reduced if the total of all Centrelink deductions exceeds the restricted percentage in the fortnight.

3

Split payments + Read more ...

Participants in the CDC locations of:

  • Ceduna, East Kimberly, Goldfields, Bundaberg and Hervey Bay will have up to 80% of their regular payments sent to their CDC
  • Cape York and NT participants transition to the CDC on the same percentage spilt they were subject to under IM (between 50-80%)

To identify a participant's payment split between their standard bank account and their CDC:

  • go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen where it will display Multiple Destinations in the Sent To column for the affected payments
  • the Payment Details Lodgement (PDL) screen shows a breakdown of the payment

Note: participants located in Ceduna and East Kimberley (Wyndham and Kununurra) can apply to the Local Community Panel in their area to have their restricted welfare amount varied.

If a participant is subsequently exited from the CDC, their CDC account will remain active, however, their income support payments will no longer be split and will be redirected to their default, unrestricted bank account in full. See Resources for scenarios on what happens when a payment is re-granted.

Roles in the CDC

Table 3: This table outlines the different roles in the administration of the CDC.

Item

Description

1

Card Issuer + Read more ...

The card issuer is an Authorised Deposit-Taking Institution (ADI) engaged to provide all CDC and related support services for participants and merchants.

Participants and merchants can contact the relevant card issuer as they are responsible for providing key services including:

  • issuing replacement cards
    • Services Australia can issue Temporary cards
  • helping participants activate their card
  • providing a range of balance checking options, such as:
    • interactive voice response (IVR)
    • mobile phone app
    • Short Message Service (SMS) alerts.
    • online account
    • contacting the card issuer by phone
  • resetting Personal Identification Numbers (PINs)
  • handling any fraudulent or disputed transactions
  • supporting participants to manage direct payments from their CDC account for any approved expenses that cannot be paid via the CDC, including BPAY and direct credit functions
  • assisting local wage earners/ non welfare recipients in the CDC area who want to volunteer to have a CDC
  • undertaking local merchant engagement and compliance activities

Traditional Credit Union have local branches across the Northern Territory to provide face to face assistance to card holders, including issuing temporary cards if required. A link to TCU branch locations can be found on CDC Card Issuer details.

Indue has a range of 'Local Partners' on the ground across some of the Cashless Debit Card locations to provide their face to face assistance to card holders, including issuing temporary cards if required. Details of these 'Local Partners' are published on the card issuer's website. See the Resources page for a link.

2

Local Community Panels + Read more ...

The Local Community Panels in the Ceduna and surrounding regions, and East Kimberley will have the ability to review an individual participant's impacts on the community and instruct Services Australia to adjust the restriction percentage paid to the participant's CDC down to a minimum of 50%.

Details of the Local Community Panels are available on the DSS website, see the Resources page for a link. CDC participants can also contact their nearest Local Partner (details on the card issuer website) for further information and application forms.

Note: there are no Local Community Panels in operation for the Goldfields region, the Bundaberg and Hervey Bay region, the NT or Cape York. Participants in these locations are unable to request their restricted percentage be varied.

See Varying the restricted percentage for the Cashless Debit Card.

3

Department of Social Services (DSS) + Read more ...

DSS is responsible for the policy underpinning the CDC, including merchant and community consultation and engagement.

Merchants can contact DSS as they also offer additional support to participants in conjunction with the card issuer via:

  • email
  • phone, or
  • their website

See Resources page for a link to the website and contact details.

It is not the responsibility of Service Officers to contact DSS on behalf of participants. If staff require support, contact the CDC Processing Team via Technical support.

4

Family Responsibilities Commission (FRC) + Read more ...

The FRC have the option of requesting the Australian Government to manage a customer’s income support and family assistance payments if they believe the customer will benefit from the Cashless Debit Card program. It provides another tool for the FRC to assist with the case management of families.

See Cape York Initiative in Business Hub.

5

Services Australia + Read more ...

In addition to regular CDC business as usual services, Services Australia is responsible for the service delivery of the CDC and the following tasks:

  • identifying, starting and notifying eligible participants for the CDC
  • notifying the card issuer of eligible participants
  • updating any changes of restricted percentages received through authorised Local Community Panels (Ceduna and the East Kimberley only)
  • exiting participants from the CDC when they are no longer eligible
  • payment splitting of a CDC participant's eligible welfare payments
  • advising participants of the amount sent to their CDC and main bank account
  • Issuing temporary Cashless Debit Cards
  • wellbeing exemptions
  • exits via the Exit Application process
  • assessment and facilitation of Housing and Other Expenses Limits. See Housing and Other Expense Limits for Cashless Debit Card (CDC) customers
  • assessment and facilitation of Urgent funds transfers. See Urgent Transfer of funds from a Cashless Debit Card account to an unrestricted account
  • Actioning Business Hub requests generated by the relevant FRC

Services Australia is unable to action any card related enquiries on behalf of participants. Service Officers are however, able to provide support on how they can conduct these enquiries themselves online or direct them to contact the card issuer for assistance as per the Card Support table in Resources.

Service Delivery network:

The CDC Hotline and face-to-face network will be the initial point of contact for the following CDC enquiries:

  • Housing and Other Expenses Limits
  • One-off and Urgent transfer requests
  • Enquiries about Ending Cashless Debit Card (CDC) participation
  • General assistance and support on how the CDC operates
  • Issuing Temporary Cashless Debit Cards (face-to-face only)
  • Card activations
  • PIN resets
  • Lost or Stolen cards

How to guides for Card Support are available in Resources or directly from the card issuer's website.

The CDC Processing Team will:

  • start and exit participants (including voluntary Age Pension customers)
  • code variations of restricted percentages for participants received by authorised Local Community Panels
  • handle any complex queries or issues that cannot be resolved at the first point of contact
  • handle reviews, appeals, complaints and feedback in relation to Services Australia's role with regards to the CDC
  • assess and approve Housing and Other Expenses Limits
  • assess and approve One-off and Urgent transfer requests
  • play a role in Wellbeing Exemption requests and facilitate Social Worker Assessments (if necessary)

Services Australia social workers:

  • can conduct wellbeing assessments if it is determined that participation in the CDC may place the customer's mental, physical or emotional wellbeing at risk
  • refer a wellbeing assessment report to the CDC Processing Team for consideration for a possible exit from the CDC

Income Management Contact Officers (IMCOs):

Action requests generated by the FRC through Business Hub in relation to the CDC including:

  • locate outstanding requests
  • action Mutual Client searches with a 'no' match result
  • action a Personal Information Disclosure (PID)
  • action a Notice to Commence Cashless Debit Card received
  • action a Revocation of Cashless Debit Card received
  • view PIDs that have been submitted by FRC through Business Hub
  • view current expenses and deductions
  • action a referral for Cashless Debit Card

Agents

Participating Agents in the Northern Territory and Cape York have temporary Indue Cashless Debit Cards with PIN Mailers available to hand to participants.

Participating Agents in the Northern Territory also have temporary TCU cards with PIN mailers.

Agents cannot issue temporary cards through the Inventory Tracking System (ITS). If a participant presents to an Agent Site, the Agent hands the participant a temporary card with a matching PIN Mailer and the participant will be required to contact the card issuer to activate the card.
