Allocation of PBV claim + Read more ...

PBV claims are allocated to specialist Service Officers who have the NV_CLK_NCL_Partner Bereavement skill tag. Service Officers can access Workload Management capability and/or work items through:

  • Worklist (Work Optimiser)
  • Inbox (Process Direct)

Bundling

2

Identifying PBV claims + Read more ...

Customers who have applied for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (job seeker) and advised that they are recently widowed, will have the keyword PBVOLC applied to the claim. To check for the keyword:

  • select the Inbox tab from Process Direct
  • key the Customer Reference Number (CRN) and select Go
  • from the Search Results click on the claims work item
  • select the Keyword icon
  • if the keyword PBVOLC is:
    • displayed, see Table 2
    • not displayed, this is not a PBV claim. Procedure ends here

Review claim details + Read more ...

To view supporting documents:

Review death action is complete + Read more ...

On the deceased partner's record, check to see if the notification of death has been confirmed and coded on the record. See Confirmation of the death of an adult or child.

Has the notification of death been coded on the deceased partner's record?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, code notification of death. Go to Step 4

4

Bank accounts and balances verification + Read more ...

Customers claiming PBV may need to supply proof of all bank accounts they hold, including balances as part of the claim.

If the customer has claimed online, do not request proof of bank accounts. The online claim will show this as a Required task.

Customers may need to provide:

  • a bank statement for all accounts held in the customer or/and partner's name, or
  • a copy of digital/online account information and transactions, and
  • evidence of all bank account balances for all accounts held in their name from date last worked plus 1 to the claim submission date

Note: an automatic teller machine (ATM) slip is not an acceptable supporting document. Bank account details may be pre-populated into an online claim and, if the customer has not zeroed off the balance or advised the account is closed, Service Officers can accept verbal confirmation that the account is closed. See Documents screen in the Request for Information guided procedure. For example, if the customer advises they closed an account within the past 12 months, they should provide evidence. If they advise the account was closed more than 12 months ago, Services Officers can accept verbal confirmation.

Supporting evidence + Read more ...

  • Check bank documents are acceptable. See Documents required for Centrelink new claims

  • If the documents supplied are acceptable and all the information has been provided, see Table 3
  • If the documents supplied are not acceptable or more information is required, see Step 3 in Table 4

Assessing PBV claim + Read more ...

If the customer has provided all acceptable documents, process the claim.

For assistance processing, refer to Processing JobSeeker Payment (JSP) claims or Processing Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) claims.

Go to Step 2.

2

Exemption from mutual obligations + Read more ...

Jobseekers are eligible for a bereavement mutual obligation exemption. Partner details are to be recorded on the Miscellaneous Details (NMIS) screen.

  • Update NMIS screen:
    • select Add to add a new line
    • Event date - the date of notification of death
    • Date of Notification - the date the death was notified
    • Notified by - for PBV to be paid, the notification must be recorded as the customer or their correspondence nominee, even if advised by a third party
    • select PBV as the Payment Choice
    • select Save

The Bereavement Period – Partner exemption (BPP) is recorded from the date of death of the partner for 14 weeks.

If the bereaved person is pregnant at the time of the partner’s death, once verification of pregnancy is provided they will have a Bereavement Period - Pregnant Partner (BPE) exemption:

  • for 14 weeks from the date of the partner’s death, or
  • to the end of the pregnancy, whichever is later

Go to Step 3.

3

Unemployment or Unemployment Non-Payment Period Failure + Read more ...

Has an unemployment or unemployment non-payment period failure been generated for investigation?

  • Yes, record a compliance action rejection using Bereavement – Family member (FRB) code. Go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Attempt to contact the customer + Read more ...

Make 1 genuine attempt to contact the customer.

  • Issue a pre-call notification (SMS only) between 8:00 am and 8:00 pm using the customer's local time to let them know a Service Officer will call them today from a private number, see Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging
  • See Calling a customer or returning a customer's call for more information on privacy when making an outbound call to a customer

If contact was:

  • Successful, and
    • able to finalise the claim, go to Step 5
    • unable to finalise the claim, go to Step 8
  • Unsuccessful, and
    • able to finalise the claim, go to Step 6
    • unable to finalise the claim, go to Step 7

5

Claim finalised + Read more ...

If the claim was granted, tell the customer:

  • their rate of payment and payment date
  • about self service options such as enrolling their voiceprint for phone self service, the transactions available in their online account or Express Plus mobile app and how to access tutorials on the agency website. See Access to Centrelink self service - set up, checking, locking and resetting and the Digital Conversation Helper
  • their reporting requirements, including:
    • to use their Centrelink online account or the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app to report
    • to report for the specific dates within the reporting period
    • to report the gross amount of earnings, and
    • to complete any immediate or outstanding reports they must action
    • that they are exempt from any mutual obligation requirements for 14 weeks from the date of the partner's date of death
  • discuss the 14 week ‘bereavement exemption’ from mutual obligation/activity test requirements

Annotate the claim finalisation DOC with the discussion with the customer.

If the claim was rejected, tell the customer:

  • about possible alternate income support payments, and/or
  • their appeal rights

See Table 4.

6

Claim finalised and unsuccessful contact + Read more ...

Annotate the claim finalisation DOC with the attempted contact details.

See Table 4.

7

Unsuccessful contact and claim not finalised + Read more ...

If the contact was unsuccessful:

  • issue a request for information via > Request Documents

Note: documents that have been requested at Next Step in the claim should not be requested again.

8

Successful contact and claim not finalised + Read more ...

If contact was successful:

  • discuss with the customer what is required for the claim to be finalised
  • record the details on the conversation in the PoC DOC

Encourage them to upload the information via the requested task in the online claim on the day, so their claim can be processed as soon as possible. See Upload documents service.

Procedure ends here until documents are returned.

Table 4

Step

Action

1

PBV assessment finalised + Read more ...

After completing the PBV claim, if the outcome is:

  • Eligible for PBV or Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP), go to Step 2

2

Set a future review + Read more ...

As the customer’s eligibility to PBV may change if income is reported, that reduces their JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YAL) to zero rate. Where the initial assessment deems the customer is eligible, the outcome letter must not be issued until after their Entitlement Period End Day (EPED) when eligibility is confirmed.

Create a future review for the letter to be issued via the Review Registration (RVR) screen in Customer First:

  • Service reason - JSP or YAL
  • Review reason - INC
  • Due date - customer’s EPED + 1
  • Source - Internal (INT)
  • Date of receipt - ‘T’oday
  • Notes: PBV outcome letter to be issued
  • Add the keyword PBVLET
    Note:     the keyword must be added to ensure correct allocation
  • Select Continue

Procedure ends here until the review is due.

3

Issue outcome letter + Read more ...

Manually issue the relevant letter to the customer in Customer First:

  • PBV assessment (Q950) letter, where
    • eligible to PBV only, or
    • not eligible for bereavement assistance, or
    • eligible for PBV after assessment of SA490
  • Bereavement (Q150) letter, where
    • eligible to LBP only, or
    • eligible to LBP and PBV, and
    • the estimated LBP is a higher rate, or
    • the estimated PBV is a higher rate. A PBV application form (SA490) must be attached to the Q150

For assistance, see Creating an Online Advice (OLA), including within an existing activity.

Procedure ends here.

Initial checks + Read more ...

4

Eligible for LBP and PBV + Read more ...

If the customer is eligible for LBP and PBV, the comparison calculator will display. Use the bereavement calculator to calculate LBP amount.

If the estimated PBV is a higher rate, make genuine attempts to contact the customer to discuss the 2 bereavement payments, explaining:

  • a PBV application form will need to be completed and lodged
  • if pregnant, evidence will need to be provided
  • a LBP will be paid now and top up paid when the PBV application form is lodged, within 14 weeks of the date of death

To pay LBP:

  • ensure BLS is selected as the Payment Choice
  • select Assess
  • check the LBP amount is correct on the Entitlements (ELD) screen
  • select Finish
  • check the DOC information is correct, select Finalise

Eligible for LBP and PBV + Read more ...

Where the customer is eligible for both the LBP and PBV, in Customer First:

  • Update Miscellaneous Details (NMIS) screen:
    • Event date - the date of notification of death
    • Date of Notification - the date the death was notified
    • Notified by - for PBV to be paid, the notification must be recorded as the customer or their correspondence nominee, even if advised by a third party
    • Expected Date of Confinement - the date the child is due to be born (if applicable)
    • Pregnancy Verified - Yes or No, depending if verification has been provided (if applicable)
    • Bereavement Payment Type - BLS (Bereavement Lump Sum Payment)
    • Enter Source, DOR and Action
    • Press [Enter]
  • Issue the LBP:
    • Go to the One Off Payments (OOP) screen
    • In the Effect Date field, code the date of death
    • In the Payment Type field, code ‘BLS’ (Lump Sum Bereavement Payment)
    • Press [Enter]
    • The Assessment Results (AR) screen is displayed
    • Select Optional Actions>Partner/Ex-Partner>Pensions/Allowances>One Off Payments from the left hand menu
    • Code the LBP amount in the Amount column on the Bereavement Payment line
    • Press [Enter] twice to return to the AR screen
    • Finalise activity
    • Record details on a DOC

If the payment is unable to be issued via OOP, go to Step 8.

If payment has been issued via OOP, go to Step 9.

Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen workaround + Read more ...

If the LBP is unable to be issued via the OOP screen, immediately record the payment through PYAJ, using:

  • reason as SYL (system limitation)
  • component as BRVLS (Bereavement Payment Lump Sum)

Where the customer is eligible for LBP and PBV:

  • Record a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on the customer record to alert a LBP was issued via PYAJ
  • Set the DOA DOC to expire after the 14 week bereavement period

If the LBP is unable to be issued via the OOP screen, immediately record the payment through PYAJ, using:

  • reason as SYL (system limitation)
  • component as BRVLS (Bereavement Payment Lump Sum)

Where the customer is eligible for LBP and PBV:

  • Record a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on the customer record to alert a LBP was issued via PYAJ
  • Set the DOA DOC to expire after the 14 week bereavement period

Contact customer + Read more ...

Make genuine attempts to contact the customer to advise:

  • the assessment outcome:
    • Eligible - the PBV will be paid as a lump sum with their next income support payment, if they are eligible for more than zero rate after reporting on their Entitlement Period End Day (EPED). If the customer’s payment is reduced to zero due to income, they will not receive the PBV

  • requirement to continue fortnightly reporting, including reporting date and channels for newly granted customers
  • a letter will be sent 7 days before the exemption expiry, reminding the customer of the requirement to comply with their current mutual obligation/activity test requirements at the end of the ‘bereavement exemption’

3

Set a future review + Read more ...

As the customer’s eligibility to the PBV may change if income is reported that reduces their JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) to zero rate. Where the initial assessment deems the customer is eligible, the outcome letter must not be issued until after their Entitlement Period End Day (EPED) when eligibility is confirmed.

Create a future review for the letter to be issued via the Review Registration (RVR) screen in Customer First:

  • Service reason - JSP or YAL
  • Review reason - INC
  • Due date - Customer’s EPED + 1
  • Source - Internal (INT)
  • Date of receipt - ‘T’oday
  • Notes: check and confirm PBV eligibility after reporting completed. PBV outcome letter to be issued
  • Add the keyword PBVLET
    Note: the keyword must be added to ensure correct allocation
  • Select Continue

Procedure ends here until the review becomes due.

4

Issue outcome letter + Read more ...

Manually issue the relevant letter to the customer, in Customer First:

  • PBV assessment (Q950) letter, where:
    • eligible to PBV only, or
    • not eligible for bereavement assistance, or
    • eligible for PBV after assessment of SA490
  • Bereavement (Q150) letter, where:
    • eligible to LBP only, or
    • eligible to LBP and PBV, and
    • the estimated LBP is a higher rate, or
    • the estimated PBV is a higher rate. A PBV application form (SA490) must be attached to the Q150

For assistance, see Creating an Online Advice (OLA), including within an existing activity.
