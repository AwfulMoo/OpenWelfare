Social worker UTLAH assessment instructions

Table 1. This table describes how social workers complete an UTLAH assessment.

Step

Action

1

Unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH) assessment needed for a young person under 18 years old + Read more ...

The following steps are needed to complete an UTLAH assessment:

  • conduct an interview with the customer, go to Step 2
  • register the UTLAH assessment, go to Step 3
  • conduct an interview with the customer's parent(s), go to Step 4
  • conduct an interview with an independent third party, go to Step 8
  • determine the customer's UTLAH status, go to Step 9
  • record UTLAH assessment details in the Social work Information System (SWIS) report
  • finalise the UTLAH claim, see Table 3

2

Interview the customer + Read more ...

Interviews for customers aged 16 and 17 years are usually conducted by phone by social workers. Special servicing arrangements are in place to support customers from remote communities in:

  • Zones Western Australia
  • Northern Australia, and
  • Northern Queensland

Interviews for customers under 16 years of age are normally conducted face-to-face. However, they can be conducted over the telephone with customers who live in regional or remote areas or are unable to attend a service centre.

Discuss the following issues with the young person during the interview:

  • options for mediation/reconciliation with parent(s)
  • the assessment process, privacy provisions and Freedom of Information (FOI) procedures
  • the requirement of parental contact at claim or review and the issue of continuous support from a parent
  • the customer's requirement to nominate an independent third party
  • the customer's requirement to provide requested information such as identification and bank account details
  • potential or current nominee (both correspondence and/or payment) or Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) arrangements
  • ways for the young person to maintain links with other relatives and support networks
  • the option of weekly payments
  • the young person's review and appeal rights, and ongoing Services Australia requirements, including regular reviews
  • their safety and any risk of harm to the customer or others, such as siblings
  • in certain situations a referral to the relevant State/Territory Authority by a Youth Protective Assessment or to Reconnect may be needed
  • explore opportunities for social casework
    • to refer a customer for social casework see Social work service referral

Note: if the customer has rejected the offer of a nominee and they are identified as not having the capacity to manage their own affairs, the social worker can appoint a correspondence nominee and/or payment nominee without the young person's consent.

3

Register the UTLAH claim + Read more ...

After completing a UTLAH interview, complete the Social Work Assessment in progress Fast Note in Customer First.

Has the customer provided consent to contact their parent(s)?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No:
    • Explain to the young person how this could affect the UTLAH assessment and explore other options or consult with the Social Work Manager (or a Social Work Support Manager supporting the Over 18 UTLAH Assessment Team for customers 18 years and over)
    • Go to Step 4

4

Make contact with parent(s) + Read more ...

Parental contact is needed in all cases unless:

  • the young person refuses permission
  • there are verified circumstances of family and domestic violence, neglect or abuse
    • this includes all elements of family and domestic violence, including homophobia and transphobia
  • contact with the parent presents a severe risk to the young person or others, such as siblings, or
  • it has been verified that there has been no parental concern for 2 years or more
  • the parent is deceased
  • the parents are living together, one parent is contacted and the other parent is satisfied their input has been represented
  • the parent does not respond to 2 Q465 letters
  • the young person is in State Care

Consultation with an EL2 Social Work Manager must occur on all occasions where a young person’s parent(s) are not contacted because of:

  • the young person refusing permission
  • verified circumstances of family and domestic violence, neglect or abuse being obtained
  • the young person indicating contact with the parent(s) presents a severe risk to them or others, including siblings

If the young person refuses permission, and serious risk circumstances are unable to be established, it may mean their claim cannot be granted.

The serious risk consultation must be documented in the UTLAH report.

Make initial contact with the parent(s) by telephone.

Offer the parent(s) the following options for the interview:

  • telephone interview
  • providing written information to the social worker

Are the parent(s) able to be interviewed?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Issue Q465 Unreasonable To Live At Home (UTLAH) new claim letter to parent(s) + Read more ...

  • If the parent(s) cannot be contacted by telephone, issue a Q465 requesting the parent(s) contact the social worker. Note: if the parents reside together, address the Q465 to both parents. This gives both parents the opportunity to provide information about the family situation
  • Document on the customer's record that the Q465 has been sent to the parent(s)
  • If the parent(s) do not make contact within 7 days of receiving the letter, issue another Q465

Note: the 7 days should start from the date the parent can be considered to have received the letter, taking into consideration the delivery standards for Centrelink and Australia Post. See the Resources page of Letters and customer advices for the delivery standards for Centrelink letters and forms.

Have the parent(s) returned the Q465 or made contact within 7 days of the most recent letter?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Non-return of Q465 + Read more ...

If the parent(s) do not respond to the second letter within 14 days of the date the first letter was received, the UTLAH claim may be assessed without parental contact.

Record details in the Social Work Information System (SWIS) report.

If contact with parents is eventually made, the young person's UTLAH status must be reassessed, taking into account the views of the parent(s).

Go to Step 8.

7

Interview the parent(s) + Read more ...

Discuss the following issues with the parent(s) during the interview:

  • the process for assessing the young person's claim, review procedures, privacy requirements and FOI procedures
  • provide any information the young person has agreed can be released to them
  • their willingness to allow the young person to return home and conditions applicable
  • their perspective of reasons that they young person left home
    • the information they provide about the family situation may need to be explored with the young person before a decision is made
  • previous attempts to resolve family conflict through use of professional or community support services
  • the scope for referral to mediation/counselling services, including Reconnect if appropriate
  • the requirement for third party verification from independent persons
  • verify the date the young person left the parental home and clarify the issue of continuous support
  • advise that they can provide more details about the family situation at any stage and that this would be considered for the young person's continuing entitlement

8

Interviewing independent third parties + Read more ...

Verification from an independent third party for assessment of a UTLAH claim is needed in order to conduct a holistic assessment of the young person's circumstances.

Explain to the independent third party that:

  • the young person and/or the parent(s) have given permission for the contact, and
  • no guarantees can be given that the information they provide will not be released to the young person

Clearly document any information provided by the third party. Written or verbal statements should have:

  • the name, address and telephone number of the party
  • their position and organisation (if applicable)
  • the basis and length of their relationship with the young person, and
  • information about whether they have had contact with parents, siblings or other family members of the young person

If the young person and their parent(s) are unable to nominate an independent third party and the social worker considers that there is enough evidence, the claim may be granted after consultation with a Social Work Manager.

Note: if a referral for a Protective Assessment is made to a State/Territory Welfare Authority, evidence provided from the State/Territory Welfare Authority can serve as third party verification and other independent third parties may not be needed.

Record details of contact with each independent third party in the SWIS report.

9

Assess customer for UTLAH status + Read more ...

Using the information provided during the assessment process:

  • decide if the customer’s circumstances meet the eligibility criteria for the UTLAH rate of Youth Allowance (YA), Special Benefit (SpB), Disability Support Pension (DSP) or ABSTUDY, or exempt the customer from providing parental income for Tertiary Access Payment
  • make sure the UTLAH assessment supports an intervention in line with the Social Work Practice Standards focus areas of:
    • safety and wellbeing
    • financial support
    • education and employment
    • strengthening communities
  • determine eligibility for the UTLAH rate

In situations where parents are separated, there may be other considerations.

Have the parents separated?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3

Situations where parents are separated

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

Establish the parties involved + Read more ...

If parents are separated, the circumstances about each of the 2 parents must be assessed. Parents are referred to as:

  • Parent 1 - the parent the customer normally lives with, or most recently lived with
  • Parent 2 - the other parent

Look at the circumstances about Parent 1 first.

2

Parent the customer normally lives with + Read more ...

Has the assessment found that it is unreasonable for the young person to live at the home of Parent 1?

  • Yes, look at the circumstances about Parent 2, go to Step 3
  • No, UTLAH criteria is not met and the young person is not eligible for UTLAH rate
    • Contact the customer to advise of the likely outcome
    • See Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision

Note: if the decision remains the same, the relevant Fast Note must be completed to advise the processing team the young person does not meet eligibility for UTLAH.

Finalise the UTLAH claim. See Step 1 in Table 3.

3

Other parent + Read more ...

Has the assessment found that it is unreasonable for the young person to live at the home of Parent 2?

  • Yes, UTLAH criteria is met and the customer is eligible for UTLAH rate
    • Finalise the UTLAH claim. See Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Exceptional circumstances + Read more ...

In exceptional circumstances, it may be considered unreasonable for the young person to live with the other parent, although UTLAH reasons are not met. Such exceptional circumstances may include situations where:

  • the other parent does not meet the definition of a parent for the purposes of the parental income test, and
  • either:
    • it has been verified that there has been no parental concern for the young person for at least the past 2 years, or
    • the young person qualifies for the away from home rate of payment based on the home circumstances of the other parent. That is, there is an approved reason for living away from home

In such exceptional circumstances, the young person meets the UTLAH provisions.

5

Applying approved reasons for living away from home + Read more ...

For DSP customers see Assessing payment rates and independence for DSP under 21years.

For YA and SpB customers, has the assessment found that circumstances about Parent 2 meet an approved reason for living away from home?

  • Yes, UTLAH criteria is met and the customer is eligible for UTLAH rate
  • No, UTLAH criteria is not met and the young person is not eligible for UTLAH rate
    • Contact the customer to advise of the likely outcome
    • See Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision

Note: if the decision remains the same, the relevant Fast Note must be completed to advise the processing team the young person does not meet eligibility for UTLAH.

Finalise the UTLAH claim. See Step 1 in Table 3.

Finalise the claim

Table 3:

Step

Action

1

Finalise the UTLAH claim + Read more ...

To finalise a UTLAH claim complete the following:

  • check that all procedures have been followed correctly and there is sufficient verification to support any recommendation to grant or reject the claim
  • clearly outline the reason for granting or rejecting independent status on the basis of UTLAH criteria
  • DOC what nominee or Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) arrangements have been discussed, including current arrangements
  • make sure all confidential material is stored appropriately
  • record all information in the SWIS report, and
  • determine any need for follow-up and/or referral for social casework and/or refer customer to appropriate services if needed
  • if granting the claim, update the customer's payment destination if before grant of UTLAH the payments were received by a parent or guardian
  • if rejecting the claim, refer the application for payment as a dependent back to the processing team
    • discuss any issues that may exist around the relationship between the customer and the parents that may impact on the customer's ability to get Parental Income details
    • consider recommending the away from home rate of Youth Allowance (if applicable)

2

Complete the Fast Note + Read more ...

Complete the relevant Fast Note in Customer First. The Fast Note will automatically transfer to the correct processing team once completed. For:

  • Youth Allowance Student, use UTLAH STUDENT GRANT/REJECT Fast Note
  • Youth Allowance Jobseeker, use UTLAH JOBSEEKER GRANT/REJECT Fast Note
  • Tertiary Access Payment, use the UTLAH TAP GRANT/REJECT Fast Note
  • Disability Support Pension (DSP), use UTLAH DSP GRANT/REJECT Fast Note
  • ABSTUDY, use UTLAH ABSTUDY GRANT/REJECT Fast Note
  • Special Benefit (SpB), use SW recommendation - SpB Under 16 Fast Note

For more detail on creating these Fast Notes using the Auto text function, see Assessing unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH) for customers claiming or receiving Youth Allowance (YA), Disability Support Pension (DSP), Special Benefit (SpB) or Tertiary Access Payment (TAP), (Process page, Table 4).

3

Letter advising customer of UTLAH decision + Read more ...

If the UTLAH decision is to:

  • Reject:
    • Make sure a request to issue a Q168 letter is included in the relevant Fast Note for the service officer processing the claim
    • Procedure ends here
  • Grant:
    • Make sure a request to issues a Q170 letter is included in the relevant Fast Note for the service officer processing the claim
    • If the customer's UTLAH start date is not the same as the grant date of the payment, the Service Officer processing the claim needs to manually issue a Q170 letter. Note this in the UTLAH decision DOC so the Service Officer at claim finalisation can follow it up
