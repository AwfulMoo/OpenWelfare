This page contains information on the restoration of Parenting Payment (PP).

Making a restoration determination for PP

Table 1

Step

Action

1

PP suspended/ cancelled + Read more ...

Customer contacts after PP is suspended or cancelled.

Check customer's record to find out why PP was suspended or cancelled:

  • Check Service Reason Summary (SRS) screen
  • For Parenting Payment Single (PPS), check Pension Status History (PNSH) screen
  • For Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) check the PPP Change History (PGCH) screen
  • If customer has a partner, it may also be necessary to check the partner's record for reasons for suspension/cancellation (it may have been auto action, due to partner's payments being suspended or cancelled)

For information about whether PP can be restored and who can restore payment, see Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Parenting Payment (PP).

2

Contact within 13 weeks + Read more ...

Has customer contacted within 13 weeks of the date the suspension/ cancellation decision was notified, or is payment suspended/cancelled Whereabouts unknown (WUK)?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the customer will need to reclaim. See Claiming PP. Procedure ends here

3

Customers with compulsory mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

Does the customer have compulsory mutual obligation requirements (AEX screen) or ParentsNext requirements (ParentsNext screen)?

  • Yes:
    • mutual obligation requirements, determine if the customer has continued to meet mutual obligation requirements since last paid. In particular, consider whether the customer has continued to meet their mutual obligation requirements during the cancellation period. The customer should be asked about how they have supported themselves since last paid an income support payment, for example, have they worked? Have they used liquid assets? If it cannot be determined that the customer has continued qualification for the period, the PPS should not be restored. Document the decision not to restore the payment and the customer will be required to reclaim payment. If the customer has met their mutual obligation requirements since last paid PPS, go to Step 4
    • ParentsNext requirements, and suspended for reason FDH (failed to attend ParentsNext appointment), see ParentsNext suspension and restoration. If suspended for another reason, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Suspension/cancellation reason + Read more ...

If the customer has a participation-related payment suspension, re-engagement with the provider is required before the suspension can be lifted or reporting finalised. Check the Participation Compliance workflow summary and where the Completed/Provider to Action section shows outstanding compliance actions the provider is responsible for managing. Advise the customer to contact their provider and comply with any reconnection requirements notified by the provider.

If the customer has an outstanding Reporting Statement, see Reporting overview. The customer will need to report before PP can be restored.

If PP suspension/cancellation reason is:

  • C06 (youngest child turning 6 for PPP) and customer has a younger child in care, go to Step 5
  • C08 (youngest child turning 8 for PPS), go to Step 6
  • FRP (failed to report or failed to report employment income), go to Step 7
  • INM (failed to contact after auto income management) should only be restored if customer has had their assessment allocation interview to discuss their expenses and priority needs. See Auto income management and suspension of payments when a customer fails to attend initial assessment interview
  • INV (pending investigation) PP will suspend after cancellation of their partner's JobSeeker Payment (JSP) due to returning to full-time work (JSP CAN-FTE). PP may be restored
  • NTC (customer failed to supply Tax File Number (TFN)). PP may be restored if customer authorises use of TFN already recorded. See Step 1 in the Outcome of restoration determination for PP
  • NTP (partner failed to supply TFN). PP may be restored if partner authorises use of TFN already recorded. See Step 1 in the Outcome of restoration determination for PP
  • O6W (overseas 6 weeks), OA6 (overseas again within 6 weeks), C6W (child overseas 6 weeks), or OAC (child overseas again within 6 weeks), PP can be restored with a gap in payment under certain conditions, see Return to Australia procedures for service centre and Smart Centre staff
  • OAO (overseas again within 13 weeks) or O13 (overseas 13 weeks). Note: this code does not apply for departures after 1 January 2013. PP can be restored with a gap in payment under certain conditions, see Return to Australia procedures for service centre and Smart Centre staff
  • PIJ or PIP (failure to attend Participation interview), see Principal carer of a dependent child fails to attend compulsory participation interview. Procedure ends here
  • Whereabouts unknown (WUK), go to Step 8
  • Other reason not listed above - PP may also be restored if payment was suspended/cancelled correctly, but for a reason that can be restored following necessary customer action and the reason for restoration is reasonable. See Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Parenting Payment (PP)

See Step 1 in the Outcome of restoration determination for PP.

5

PPP child turns 6 - C06 + Read more ...

If PPP has cancelled due to their youngest child turning 6, or has a status of Cancel Next Payment ahead of a child’s 6th birthday (CNP-C06), and the customer has a younger child in their care the following steps will need to be taken:

  • check the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen for each eligible child to ensure a line has been coded for Service Reason ‘PPP’ and Reason ‘CLAIM’
  • for any children where this line is not present, code Service Reason ‘PPP’ with Reason ‘CLAIM’ to ensure each child is assessed as a PP child

PPP will need to be restored, including where the PPP status is CNP. See Step 1 in the Outcome of restoration determination for PP.

6

PPS child turns 8 - C08 + Read more ...

If PPS has cancelled due to their youngest child turning 8, or has a status of Cancel Next Payment ahead of a child’s 8th birthday (CNP-C08), and the customer has a younger child in their care the following steps will need to be taken:

  • check the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen for each eligible child to check if a line exists for Service Reason ‘PPS’ and Reason ‘Other - Not PP Child but qualifies for add IFA' (OTN) or 'Transfer out to partner' (TOP)
  • for any children where an OTN or TOP line is current, code Service Reason ‘PPS’ with Reason ‘End of override' (EOV) to allow the system to assess the child as a PP child

PPS will need to be restored, including where the PPS status is CNP. See Step 1 in the Outcome of restoration determination for PP.

7

FRP - failed to report or report employment income + Read more ...

Note: special circumstances must exist for the payment to be restored, especially if customer contacts towards the end of the 13 week review period. Service Officers should consider the following information:

  • the reasons for the late reporting
  • whether the agency contributed in any way to the delay
  • other mitigating or extenuating factors such as phone problems or illness
  • whether the reasons for the delay are plausible
  • whether the customer belongs to a disadvantaged group such as a migrant with poor English comprehension

Customers with mutual obligation requirements must either lodge their Reporting Statement via self-managed service channels (Online account, Express Plus application or phone) in person or in writing (SU19) before payment can be restored. See Reporting overview. Procedure ends here.

When the customer has employment income:

  • If under Age Pension age - Working Credits which may enable the customer to keep some of their income support payment while they are working
  • If over Age Pension age - and in receipt of a pension (excluding PPS) they may be entitled to the Work Bonus. Note: the Work Bonus is not used in the customer's transitional rules calculation, however the Work Bonus will still be used in the comparison calculations and partners may still benefit by the reduction in total income
  • If the customer's income reduces their fortnightly rate to nil and some of that income is employment income, the customer's payment may remain current at nil rate for up to 6 fortnights and they may retain certain benefits

Has customer contacted within 13 weeks of the date the cancellation decision was notified and can they supply all employment income details for the relevant entitlement periods?

  • Yes, PP can be restored and arrears paid only for those entitlement periods where the customer was eligible. Restore payment and then code employment income via the Earnings and Reporting Tool in the same activity. See Step 1 in the Outcome of restoration determination for PP table
  • No, the customer will need to reclaim PP. See:
    • Initial contact by customers claiming PP
    • Estimating income for family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payments if needed
    • procedure ends here

8

Whereabouts unknown (WUK) + Read more ...

If the customer has continued to be qualified and retained payability for their payment during the period of suspension/cancellation, their payment may be restored with full arrears, even if the customer has contacted more than 13 weeks after the decision to suspend or cancel.

Each customer’s circumstances must be looked at individually to determine whether they could be taken not to have received advice of the decision to suspend/cancel, and whether they retained qualification and payability during the period, and therefore receive arrears upon restoration.

If they were not at their known address or advice of the decision was returned to the agency, the customer is taken not to have received advice of the decision to suspend/cancel.

Ensure the following factors are taken into consideration when deciding whether restoration with full arrears is appropriate:

  • If the customer's address details have not changed, before payment can be restored the customer must provide evidence that they still live at the recorded address. This must be done before the payment can be restored
  • If an address has been incorrectly ended, go to the Address History (ADH) screen and 'D'elete the Address Type Ended line. This will remove the end date from the current address

If the review of the decision to suspend/cancel WUK results in a favourable determination, the date of effect of the restoration is the date of the original decision (date of suspension/cancellation), as per legislation. See Correspondence returned to service centre or Processing Centre ‘Whereabouts unknown’.

Outcome of restoration determination for PP

Table 2

Step

Action

1

PP restored at initial contact + Read more ...

Payment cannot be restored at initial contact if there is any additional information/documentation still required from the customer (for example, modules, proof of address), or if there is any other information that states restoration cannot be actioned at first contact.

If the customer has a participation-related payment suspension providers have responsibility for determining when the conditions for lifting the payment suspension have been met. Check the Participation Compliance workflow summary and where the Completed/Provider to Action section shows outstanding compliance actions the provider is responsible for managing. Advise the customer to contact their provider and comply with any reconnection requirements notified by the provider.

Can PP be restored at initial contact?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Payment cannot be restored until the information/action has been verified + Read more ...

  • Advise customer of the information needed before PP can be restored
  • Issue request for further information if required, for example, Modules, proof of address details
  • If the customer has an outstanding Reporting Statement, see Reporting overview
  • Record details on a DOC with information of advice given to customer
  • Make an 'enquiry' appointment, if appropriate, for customer to supply information. See Booking appointments in the Centrelink appointment system
  • If customer provides information verbally or as requested in writing, go to Step 3

3

Restore PP + Read more ...

Some cancelled records cannot be restored. PP may need to be re-granted. Determine the customer's continuing eligibility for PP since the date of suspension/ cancellation. See Claiming Parenting Payment (PP)

Update any changes in circumstances, such as change of address.

Note: if the customer is under income management (they have INM on the benefit status line) and they have an arrears or lump sum payment entitlement, the Service Officer is not to make any changes to the customer's record to allow this payment to be made. The Service Officer is to contact the Income Management Team to allow income management to be turned on for the arrears payment before it is made.

If the customer's Family Tax Benefit (FTB) is cancelled as a result of the PP being suspended/cancelled, restore FTB first where customer is eligible for Rent Assistance (RA).

When restoring PP under section 131 of the Social Security Administration Act 1999 pending the review of an adverse decision, code PAYPEND as a keyword to the activity or DOC.

Restore PP on BA screen:

  • Svc Rsn: field, code PPP or PPS
  • Action: field, code RES restore
  • Reason: field, the restoration Reason: field is to remain blank
  • Effect Date: field in most cases defaults to the date of suspension/ cancellation
  • Code Source: and DOR: fields

If the customer was suspended or cancelled FRP or has outstanding reports when payment is restored for any other reason, restore payment then report/code employment income via the Earnings and Reporting Tool within the restoration activity.

If the customer is income managed under the Voluntary Income Management measure, and the agreement was in place for more than 13 weeks, the Voluntary Income Management Continuation Assessment screen displays so it can be recorded whether the customer wishes to continue or terminate their VIM agreement. Refer to Effect on Income Management when payment is suspended, on zero rate, cancelled, or an Unemployment Non-Payment Period applied

If partner's payment is also to be restored, code BA screen on partner's record as it is not always automatically restored.

Check Assessment Warnings and Errors (AWE) screen. If presented with error (E235RT) - Manual Report Reason no longer valid-End date on RPRD screen, see Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen for manual coding workaround. Insert today's date as the valid end date on the RPRD screen. See Reporting screens.

Check result on the Assessment Results (AR) screen before finalising activity. If customer is entitled to arrears, check the correct amount is to be paid.

If necessary, see How to manually calculate arrears.

Record the details on a DOC. In Customer First a Fast Note can be recorded by selecting Parents > SUS/CAN/RES > PPP restored or PPS restored.

4

Check other details + Read more ...

The customer may need to have an appointment booked to renegotiate their Job Plan if their circumstances have changed.

For FTB / Child Care Subsidy customers, check that the customer is aware of their payment options and that their annual income estimate is still reasonable. See Estimating income for family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payments.

5

Check for outstanding compliance action + Read more ...

Where the customer has compulsory requirements, check the Participation Compliance workflow summary and where the Outstanding section shows a compliance investigation is outstanding assist the job seeker to contact the Participation Solutions Team.

For more information, see Establishing contact with the job seeker to discuss non-compliance or potential barriers to compliance.
