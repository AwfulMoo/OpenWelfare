Eligibility for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship)

Table 1:

Step

Action

1

Eligibility for Child Care Subsidy (CCS) + Read more ...

To be eligible for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) customers must be eligible for CCS.

Is the customer CCS current?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, and the customer:
    • has not claimed CCS - encourage the customer to lodge a claim for CCS including ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) go to Step 2
    • has claimed CCS, and has been assessed as not eligible. advise the customer they are not eligible for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship). Procedure ends here

2

Temporary Financial Hardship event(s) + Read more ...

To be eligible for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship), the customer (or their partner) must have experienced a Temporary Financial Hardship event(s), which has placed them in financial hardship, is unforeseen, and out of their control.

Note: an event may also apply on the Secretary's own initiative, such as in the case of a natural disaster. The Resources page contains an example of where the secretary may make a determination of ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship).

Is the customer experiencing an event (or multiple events) of Temporary Financial Hardship?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship). Procedure ends here

3

Event(s) occurred within the last 6 months + Read more ...

An event(s) can only be used for the purposes of ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) if it has occurred within 6 months of the application being lodged.

Has the event(s) occurred within the last 6 months?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship). Procedure ends here

4

Reduction in ability to pay child care fees + Read more ...

A financial hardship determination is based on the objective and holistic circumstances that impact on the individual's ability to pay child care fees. What might be a small impact on one family may be a considerable impact on another. Therefore 'hardship' can only be determined by considering all relevant factors (noting that this list is not exhaustive):

  • 'out of pocket' expenses
  • level of debt or savings and how these might impact
  • possible changes (including variability) in income
  • other financial matters relevant to their circumstances - such as:
    • when income might be available, if known,
    • the impact of insurance settlements occurring while the individual is in receipt of ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship)
  • individual circumstances as disclosed by the individual and/ or partner

Has the event(s) caused a significant reduction in the customer's ability to pay child care fees?

  • Yes, the customer meets the eligibility criteria for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) subject to the provision of supporting evidence. See the Resources page for evidence requirements
  • No, the customer is not eligible for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship). Procedure ends here

Applying for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) via online services

Table 2: this table outlines the process for customers to apply for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) via online services. This information will assist service officers in understanding the online application process if customers contact or are applying via a self-service terminal.

Step

Action

1

Online applications for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) + Read more ...

The preferred option for customers to apply for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) is via their Centrelink online account which they can access via their myGov account:

  • For non-current CCS customers, go to Step 2
  • For CCS current customers, go to Step 3

2

Making an online application - non-current CCS customers + Read more ...

Once a customer has accessed their Centrelink online account via myGov, they will:

  • Select the Online Claims tile or the Apply for a Payment > Make a claim menu option and then Start a new claim
  • On the Make a Claim page, select Get Started from the Families category, then select Apply for Child Care Subsidy
  • Answer all questions associated with the CCS claim, such as income estimate, activity test details, child details, child care details
  • If the customer has indicated they are experiencing financial hardship when completing the CCS claim, the ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) questions will be presented as a part of that claim.

Go to Step 4.

3

Making an online application - current CCS customers + Read more ...

When a current CCS customer accesses their Centrelink online account via myGov, they can start a new application for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) by:

  • selecting Child Care Subsidy menu, and
  • the Temporary Financial Hardship Service

A customer who has applied for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) in the past can also:

  • view their history of ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) applications and decisions
  • view current and pending applications
  • withdraw pending applications
  • end a current period of ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship)

Go to Step 4.

4

ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) specific questions + Read more ...

The Temporary Financial Hardship Application collects information including:

  • the events which have caused the temporary financial hardship
  • the date of the event
  • from what date assistance is required
  • how long assistance is required (maximum of 13 weeks)
  • the children for which the additional assistance is to be applied
  • details of loss of income

CCS current customers applying via online services, go to Step 5.

Non-current CCS customers completing a CCS/ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) combined claim, go to Step 6.

5

Submitting a standalone ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) application + Read more ...

When a customer submits their application, they will be presented with a Receipt confirming their application was successfully completed.

Next Steps

Customers will be advised:

  • what evidence they must provide based on the event(s) they have advised has caused their financial hardship
  • to use the Upload documents service to submit their evidence, or submit it in person at a Service Centre

Note: if evidence is not provided within 22 days (14 days + 8 days for postage) of submitting their application, the claim will be taken as not having been made (not effective). The customer will need to submit a new application if they wish to apply for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship). Payment can only be backdated up to 28 days.

Procedure ends here.

6

Submitting a CCS/ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) combined claim + Read more ...

After completing the CCS/ACCS claim, the Next Steps page displays:

  • a list of Required tasks, which will include evidence requirements for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship)
  • the date by which the claim must be submitted before it will expire
  • a link to Upload documents via an Upload button against each Required task

All Required tasks must be provided before the customer can Submit their CCS/ACCS combined claim.

Customers can access their online claim to return to the Next steps page at any time before it is submitted.

Customers have the option to:

  • go Back to the Review your claim page
  • Save the claim

Until all required documentation has been provided, and the claim submitted, it is deemed no claim has been lodged or decision made. This means a customer's CCS and ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) payment can only be backdated from the date the claim is submitted. This is not appealable.

Note: CCS and ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) can only be backdated up to 28 days from the date of claim. While ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) can start on any Monday, it cannot have a start date before CCS.

Started claims will automatically expire after 13 weeks.

Once submitted, all CCS/ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) combined claims are allocated to the ACCS Processing Team for determination.

Procedure ends here.

Staff assisted applications for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship)

Table 3: this table provides information for staff to support customers lodge an application for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship), where an online application has been deemed unsuitable for the customer based on their circumstances.

Step

Action

1

Customer contact + Read more ...

Customers should be advised the preferred method to apply for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) is by logging into myGov and accessing their linked Centrelink Online Account.

Where a customer is unable or unsuitable to complete an online claim:

  • CCS current customers, go to Step 2
  • Non-current CCS customers, go to Step 3

2

Standalone ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) applications for CCS current customers + Read more ...

There is no verbal application process for CCS current customers wishing to apply for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship).

Advise the customer they can lodge an ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) application using their online account by selecting Child Care Subsidy menu and the Temporary Financial Hardship Service.

Customers will be required to provide their evidence within 22 days (14 days + 8 days for postage) of submitting their application, or the claim will be taken as not having been made (not effective).

In limited circumstances, an offline (paper) FA086 Application for Additional Child Care Subsidy (Temporary Financial Hardship) form can be issued to customers. Service Officers must confirm the customer is current on CCS before issuing a FA086. When accepting an FA086, Service Officers must ensure all required evidence is provided with the application. See the Resources page for a link to the FA086.

Procedure ends here.

3

CCS and ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) combined claims for non-current CCS customers + Read more ...

For customers who are not CCS current, Service Officers can access the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) via the desktop icon and enter the customer's Customer Reference Number (CRN).

Before using the ACC workflow to complete a verbal claim, or enter details from non-digital (paper) claims (FA084 Child Care Subsidy claim and FA086 Application for Additional Child Care Subsidy (Temporary Financial Hardship)), Service Officers must update the customer's personal details via the Change in Contact Details workflow in Customer First as these details cannot be updated in the ACC.

If the ACC is started before the Change in Contact Details workflow was run, the ACC can be saved and the updates made outside the claim. Updates made using the Change in Contact Details workflow will appear on the Review and Submit page in the ACC.

If there is a started CCS claim on the Customer Claim Summary, the claim can be:

  • continued by selecting the pencil icon under the Actions column
  • cancelled by selecting the trash icon under the Actions column

'Channel Hopping' within the ACC workflow allows:

  • the customer to start a claim online and have it continued by a Service Officer, or
  • a Service Officer to help a customer with starting a claim, which would then be completed by the customer online (an active Centrelink online account is required)

Go to Step 4.

4

Completing the ACC workflow + Read more ...

Service Officers must read the eligibility message to the customer and ask if they want to be read the full privacy agreement. The customer must agree to the privacy agreement prior to commencing the claim

Service Officers must ask the customer streaming questions on the Check Eligibility screens to check if they may be eligible for CCS.

If answers indicate the customer may be eligible for CCS, select Claim Now when ready to start.

Continue through the remaining question sets. If the customer is unable to answer mandatory questions, the claim workflow can be saved and continued at a later time.

Go to Step 5.

5

ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) specific questions + Read more ...

If the customer advises they are experiencing temporary financial hardship, the ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) question set will display requesting information to support their application including:

  • the events which have caused the temporary financial hardship. Note: one event must be added for the customer to be assessed as eligible for ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship)
  • the date of the event
  • from what date assistance is required
  • how long assistance is required (maximum of 13 weeks)
  • the children for which the additional assistance is to be applied
  • details of loss of income

Go to Step 6

6

Review and submit claim + Read more ...

Check that all information is correct and at least one event has been added on the Review your claim page.

If answers need to be corrected, select the relevant Update button

If personal details need to be corrected, exit the ACC and update using the Change in Contact Details workflow. This information will then populate into the claim

Verbal claim declaration

Does the customer agree to a verbal declaration script being read to meet their signature requirements?

  • Yes, Service Officers must select the my obligations hyperlink (bottom of review page) and read the claim declaration to the customer. Select the 'I have read, understood and accept my obligations' check box. Go to Step 7.
  • No, a Customer Declaration Form (CDF) is required. The CDF must be:
    • printed by selecting the printer icon on the right of the Review and submit page, and
    • issued to the customer to sign and return
  • Advise the customer the claim will not be taken as submitted until the signed CDF is returned, and any supporting documentation (if applicable) is provided
    • Run the Request and Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure to request the claimant return the signed CDF within 14 days
    • Ask customer to sign CDF. Scan CDF to their record
    • Go to Step 7

7

Next Steps + Read more ...

Next Steps will display any documentation or evidence required to support the claim.

Advise the customer of the additional documents/evidence required, encouraging them to lodge these via Upload documents.

Note: if a period of reduced evience is approved where the customer or their partner receive an applicable state government disaster payment that is not viewable on the record they will only need to show proof of this payment. No further evidence will be required to demonstrate financial hardship.

Explain the customer's claim cannot be lodged until the evidence is provided. Where the customer has not agreed to the verbal declaration, they will also need to provide a signed CDF before the ACC can be submitted.

An ACC can be saved and will remain in draft for 13 weeks, after which time it will expire and a new claim (ACC) will need to be initiated.

8

Uploading documents or searching for existing evidence + Read more ...

Encourage customers to access their Centrelink online account or the Centrelink Express Plus mobile app to upload documents and then submit their claim.

On the Next Steps page in the Documents and forms section there is an Upload documents button.

If the customer uploaded or provided documents before starting the ACC, access the customer's record in Process Direct and select Documents .Investigate if documents are available to support the CCS/ACCS claim. Go to Tasks in Process Direct and ensure the required documents have been updated to 'Provided'.

Has required documentation/evidence been provided?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, select Save. An ACC cannot be submitted if required documentation has not been provided. Advise the customer their:
    • date of claim will only take effect from the date they provide their documentation/evidence, and
    • started claim will expire in 13 weeks, after which time they will need to initiate a new claim
    • procedure ends here

9

Submit claim via ACC + Read more ...

If it is determined sufficient evidence is available and accessible to support the CCS/ACCS claim, Service Officers are to Continue the ACC, and select the 'Provided' check box as appropriate.

The ACC can be submitted where the only outstanding information are bank account and/or TFN details. Remind customers to provide their details within 14 days. They can provide this information verbally, or via their online account or the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app as it will display as an outstanding task. If details are not provided within 22 days (14 days + 8 days for postage), their claim will be not effective and they will need to lodge a new claim.

Once a claim is submitted, customers subscribed to Electronic Messaging will receive a Notification confirming the submission and reminded to monitor their claim tracker in their online account or Express Plus Centrelink mobile app.

The Submitted page will display:

  • Claim ID
  • Date submitting for processing
  • Estimated Completion Date (ECD) which can be advised to the customer
  • A 'withdraw claim' option
  • Tabs that link to Notifications, Tasks & Review claim details information

All CCS/ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) combined claims are allocated to the ACCS Processing Team for processing.

Go to Step 10.

10

Exceptional Circumstances + Read more ...

If the customer indicates during the ACC they require:

  • more than 100 hours per fortnight of subsidised child care, see Exceptional circumstances for Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS), and follow the correct referral process. Advise the customer they may be contacted to provide additional evidence in relation to the exceptional circumstances request. Ensure telephone contact details are up to date
  • an ACCS percentage above 120% to cover more or all child care fees, refer the customer to their child care service. The service is responsible for applying to the Department of Education, Skills and Employment for an increased ACCS percentage. If approved, the Department of Education, Skills and Employment will notify Services Australia of the approved percentage and period it will apply

Table 4: this table provides information for staff when a customer contacts notifying of a change in circumstances.

Step

Action

1

Customer contact + Read more ...

Customers must let Services Australia know as soon as possible if their circumstances change as this may affect their eligibility to CCS or ACCS. Notifiable events may include:

  • no longer being in financial hardship, or
  • events which may directly affect their ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) assessment, e.g. becoming partnered

Where a customer contacts notifying of a change in circumstance which will affect the current ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship) assessment, refer to the Additional Child Care Subsidy Processing Team (ACCPT) for reassessment:

  • In Customer First select the Fast Note application and use the Fast Note - auto text Families > Updates > ACCS TFH Update to send a request for reassessment:
    • send to region code FCC or to NAB (Families Remote Smart Centre) for remote customers
    • use the service reason CCP

Note: this update only applies to reducing an existing ACCS TFH assessment. Where customer is seeking an increase in grant period or has a new TFH event, the customer will need to lodge a new ACCS application.

Procedure ends here.
