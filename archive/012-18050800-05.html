Eligibility criteria for allied health professionals

Speciality codes and eligibility criteria for allied health professionals

This table provides information for Service Officers on the speciality codes and eligibility criteria for allied health professionals.

Specialty Code

Health profession

Certificate eligibility criteria

Professional abbreviation

170

Audiologist Note: If an existing stem appears for an Audiologist please check the registration comments field. If comment appears ‘OHS purposes only’ please contact a PSO for assistance. Do not process a provider number on this stem.

Must be either:

a Full Member of Audiology Australia Ltd, who holds a Certificate of Clinical Practice issued by Audiology Australia Ltd, or an Ordinary Member of the Australian College of Audiology AACAud, or Fellow of the Australian College of Audiology (ACAud)

ACAud or ASAAud

423

Dietitian

Must be an Accredited Practicing Dietitian as recognised by Dietitians Australia (DA)

Diet

427

Speech pathologist

Must be a practising member of Speech Pathology Australia

Sppath

428

Diabetes educator

Must be a Credentialed Diabetes Educators (CDEs) as recognised by the Australian Diabetes Educators Association

DiabEd

429

Exercise physiologist

Must be an Accredited Exercise Physiologist as accredited by the Exercise and Sports Science Association (ESSA)

ExPhys

641

Social worker

Must be a member of the Australian Association of Social Workers (AASW), and be certified by the AASW as practicing in accordance with the Practice Standards for Mental Health Social Workers (AASW 2014) with an effective date of certification.

MHSoc

642

Mental health nurse

Must be credentialed as a Mental Health Nurse as recognised by the Australian College of Mental Health Nurses (ACMHN)

MHNse
