Examples: coding medical certificates

Table 1

Example

Scenario

1

Calculating 13 weeks + Read more ...

Jenny lodges a medical certificate stating a temporary incapacity from 10 January 2019 to 26 April 2019 (inclusive).

The medical certificate period covers 15 weeks and exceeds the allowable 13 week period for a non-serious illness.

A Service Officer codes the dates for the allowable 13 week period (91 days) only, from Thursday 10 January 2019 to Wednesday 10 April 2019:

  • Week 1 is 7 days from 10-16 January 2019
  • Week 2 is 7 days from 17-23 January 2019

continuing through to:

  • Week 12 is 7 days from 28 March 2019 to 3 April 2019
  • Week 13 is 7 days from 4-10 April 2019

2

Multiple medical conditions + Read more ...

Barry lodges a medical certificate listing 3 conditions. The Service Officer notes the doctor has stated:

  • asthma - Permanent
  • spinal condition - Permanent
  • leg fracture - Temporary

Note: an exemption cannot be given for a permanent incapacity.

The Service Officer:

  • determines Barry cannot work or otherwise meet his mutual obligation requirements for 8 hours or more per week, and
  • codes all conditions, and
  • grants Barry a temporary incapacity exemption based on the leg fracture

3

Multiple medical conditions - exacerbation/recurring + Read more ...

Susan's medical certificate lists 2 permanent conditions - anxiety and asthma. Normally the asthma does not affect Susan's capacity to work but due to a sudden change in weather, Susan is severely affected and is currently hospitalised. Susan's doctor has noted the current symptoms are an exacerbation of the asthma as follows:

  • anxiety - Permanent
  • asthma - Recurring (temporary exacerbation of permanent condition)

The Service Officer:

  • determines Susan cannot work or otherwise meet her mutual obligation requirements for 8 hours or more per week
  • codes all conditions, and
  • grants an exemption for the temporary exacerbation of Susan's permanent condition of asthma

4

Overlapping dates + Read more ...

David previously lodged a medical certificate for a leg fracture and was granted a temporary incapacity exemption from 13 February 2019 to 14 May 2019. The injury has not improved and on 12 May 2019, David uploads another medical certificate for 10 May 2019 to 8 August 2019 (inclusive).

A Service Officer codes the medical certificate on 16 May 2019 as follows:

  • Date of Event 15 May 2019 (same date as Unfit to work/study from)
  • Unfit to work/study from 15 May 2019 (previous medical certificate end date of 14 May 2019 + 1)
  • Unfit to work/study to 8 August 2019. Note: this date cannot exceed the end date on the current medical certificate therefore the coded period is less than 13 weeks - 15/5/19 to 8/8/19
  • Date of Receipt (if required) 12 May 2019 (date medical certificate was uploaded)

5

Gap in coverage - continuous + Read more ...

Martha was granted a temporary incapacity exemption from 18 April 2019 to 9 May 2019 for shoulder surgery. Martha is still recovering and submits another medical certificate to a Service Officer on 13 May 2019 for the period 13 May 2019 to 13 June 2019.

As there is a gap in coverage from 10 to 12 May 2019, the Service Officer determines Martha's incapacity continued throughout the gap period and codes the medical certificate on 13 May 2019 as follows:

  • Date of Event 10 May 2019 (same date as Unfit to work/study from date)
  • Unfit to work/study from 10 May 2019 (previous medical certificate end date of 9 May 2019 +1)
  • Unfit to work/study to 13 June 2019
  • Date of Receipt (if required) 13 May 2019

6

Gap in coverage - not continuous + Read more ...

Lisa was granted a temporary incapacity exemption from 15 March 2019 to 9 May 2019 for a knee injury. Intensive physiotherapy resulted in the knee no longer causing any problems past the end date of the medical certificate.

Lisa uploads a new medical certificate on 21 May 2019 as she hurt her neck in a car accident on 19 May 2019. The medical certificate covers the period from 19 May 2019 to 12 July 2019.

There is a gap in coverage from 10 to 18 May 2019. Although the new medical certificate was lodged within 14 days, the Service Officer determines Lisa's incapacity did not continue throughout the gap period. The medical certificate is coded on 21 May 2019 as follows:

  • Date of Event 19 May 2019 (same date as Unfit to work/study from date)
  • Unfit to work/study from 19 May 2019 (Unfit to work/study from date)
  • Unfit to work/study to 12 July 2019
  • Date of Receipt (if required) 21 May 2019

7

Incapacity exemption not granted and new medical certificate lodged with different dates and exemption granted + Read more ...

Andrew uploads a medical certificate on 18 March 2019 stating arthritis is a permanent medical condition from 18 March 2019 to 26 April 2019.

The next day, a Service Officer reviews the medical certificate. A current and valid Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) was previously completed for the arthritis and the Assessor determined Andrew had a partial capacity to work (PCW) of 15-22 hours per week.

The Service Officer does not grant the incapacity exemption as the medical certificate and ESAt evidence indicates Andrew is:

  • not suffering from an exacerbation of a permanent condition, and
  • not incapacitated for all work

The Service Officer codes the information contained in the medical certificate on 19 March 2019 as follows:

  • Date of Event 18 March 2019 (same date as Unfit to work/study from)
  • Unfit to work/study from 18 March 2019
  • Unfit to work/study to 26 April 2019
  • Date of Receipt (if required) 18 March 2019
  • Non-Exemption Reasons the Service Officer selects Job seeker is not incapacitated for all work

On 2 April 2019, Andrew lodges a new medical certificate and a Service Officer determines an incapacity exemption can be granted for a temporary back injury. The dates on the medical certificate are from 2 April 2019 to 3 May 2019. The Service Officer must not amend the details coded for the non-granted incapacity exemption when coding the new medical certificate on 5 April 2019. The Service Officer codes the medical certificate as follows:

  • Date of Event 2 April 2019 (same date as Unfit to work/study from)
  • Unfit to work/study from 2 April 2019. Note: overlapping dates do not impact where an incapacity exemption has not been granted for a previous medical certificate
  • Unfit to work/study to 3 May 2019
  • Date of Receipt (if required) 2 April 2019

8

Incapacity exemption not granted and new medical certificate lodged with the same dates and exemption granted + Read more ...

Sam uploads a medical certificate on 8 May 2019 stating anxiety is a permanent medical condition from 8 May 2019 to 9 July 2019.

The Service Officer allocated the medical certificate does not grant the incapacity exemption as the medical condition is permanent.

The Service Officer codes the information contained in the medical certificate on 10 May 2019 as follows:

  • Date of Event 8 May 2019 (same date as Unfit to work/study from)
  • Unfit to work/study from 8 May 2019
  • Unfit to work/study to 9 July 2019
  • Date of Receipt (if required) 8 May 2019
  • Non-Exemption Reasons the Service Officer selects Customer's incapacity for work/study is not temporary

On 14 May 2019, Sam uploads a new medical certificate stating the anxiety is an exacerbation of a permanent condition. The dates on the new medical certificate are the same as the previous medical certificate - 8 May 2019 to 9 July 2019.

The Service Officer must not amend the details coded for the non-granted incapacity exemption. However as the Unfit to work/study from and to dates are the same as those coded for the previous medical certificate, the Date of Event is to be coded as the date the information is being coded (today's date). If not coded with a different Date of Event, the error message E005CQ - This DOE has already been recorded will display.

The Service Officer actions the new medical certificate on 15 May 2019 and codes the following details:

  • Date of Event 15 May 2019 (same date as the date the details are being coded)
  • Unfit to work/study from 8 May 2019. Note: overlapping dates do not impact where an incapacity exemption has not been granted for a previous medical certificate
  • Unfit to work/study to 9 July 2019
  • Date of Receipt (if required) 14 May 2019

9

Incapacity exemption granted and new medical certificate lodged with overlapping dates but incapacity exemption not being granted + Read more ...

Sarah uploads a medical certificate on 8 May 2019 stating back condition as a temporary medical condition from 8 May 2019 to 9 July 2019.

The Service Officer allocated the medical certificate grants the incapacity exemption, as the medical condition is temporary.

The Service Officer codes the information contained in the medical certificate on 10 May as follows:

  • Date of Event 8 May 2019 (same date as Unfit to work/study from)
  • Unfit to work/study from 8 May 2019
  • Unfit to work/study to 9 July 2019
  • Date of Receipt (if required) 8 May 2019

On 1 July 2019, Sarah uploads a new medical certificate stating the back condition has now been diagnosed as permanent and is not an exacerbation/recurring. The dates on the new medical certificate are - 1 July 2019 to 1 September 2019.

The Service Officer determines an exemption cannot be applied, as the condition is now considered permanent.

The Service Officer must not amend the details coded for the granted incapacity exemption. They action the new medical certificate on 3 July 2019 and code the following details:

  • Date of Event 1 July 2019 (same date as Unfit to work/study from)
  • Unfit to work/study from 1 July 2019. Note: overlapping dates do not impact where an incapacity exemption has been granted for a previous medical certificate and a further incapacity exemption is not being applied for the current medical certificate
  • Unfit to work/study to 1 September 2019
  • Date of Receipt (if required) 1 July 2019

As the condition is now permanent, consider referral for an ESAt to assess Sarah's work capacity.

Non-Exemption reasons

Table 2: explanation of reasons in the Choose Non-Exemption Reasons field.

Item

Explanation

1

Job seeker is not incapacitated for all work/participation + Read more ...

Details:

  • the job seeker is able to work, study or participate for 8 hours or more per week
  • their previously reported earnings
  • this determination is based on available medical evidence, including the medical certificate completed by the job seeker's medical practitioner (if applicable) and/or a current and valid assessment of the job seeker's work capacity (Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) or Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) report)

2

Drug and/or alcohol dependency or misuse + Read more ...

Details:

  • the job seeker's incapacity is wholly or mainly caused by their drug and/or alcohol dependency or misuse
  • medical evidence indicates the primary medical condition is drug and/or alcohol dependency or misuse. Finalise the guided procedure and do not apply the exemption due to drug and/or alcohol dependency or misuse, to create an activity on the Activity List (AL) screen to enable a Q549 letter to be sent to the job seeker to advise that an exemption has not been applied:
    • select the activity from the AL screen and finalise to issue the letter
  • the Non-Applied Exemption (NAE) can also be manually recorded via the Medical Conditions (MC) screen if the guided procedure is not available

Note: NAEs can only be updated or deleted if applied in error.

3

Customer's incapacity for work/study/participation is not temporary + Read more ...

Details:

  • the customer's incapacity for work or study or participation is expected to continue for at least the next 2 years. Only use this reason where available medical evidence clearly shows that the customer is expected to be incapacitated for at least the next 2 years
  • this determination is based on available medical evidence, including the medical certificate completed by the customer's medical practitioner (if applicable) and an ESAt report
  • if the job seeker does not have a current and valid ESAt consider referral for an ESAt
  • consider possible entitlement to Disability Support Pension (DSP)

4

Job seeker is no longer considered temporarily incapacitated + Read more ...

Details:

  • the job seeker is now able to undertake work or study at their full capacity as their assessed temporary incapacity period has ended, or their condition has improved sufficiently to participate at a higher level of engagement
  • this determination is based on available medical evidence, including the medical certificate completed by the job seeker's medical practitioner (if applicable) and/or an ESAt or JCA report

5

Customer is able to do their usual work or study + Read more ...

Details:

The customer is able to work or study or participate for 8 hours or more per week. This determination is based on available medical evidence, including the medical certificate completed by the customer's medical practitioner (if applicable) and/or a current and valid ESAt or JCA report.

6

A notable time lag between the date of issue and the date of receipt + Read more ...

Details:

  • The medical evidence provided by the job seeker was issued too long ago and can no longer be considered a current and valid assessment of their medical condition
  • If the job seeker still requires an exemption, advise them they need a new valid medical certificate from their medical practitioner:
  • When deciding to apply this reason, consider if it was reasonable for the job seeker to supply their medical evidence at an earlier date. For example, if the job seeker was comatose and did not have someone to act on their behalf, it would be unreasonable for the job seeker to provide the medical certificate until after they were well enough to do so
