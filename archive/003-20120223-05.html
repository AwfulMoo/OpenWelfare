FAQs from travel agents and tour arrangement service providers

Table 3

Question

Answer

What's the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program?

It's a one-off grant to support travel agents and tour arrangement service providers who have lost income because of the impacts of COVID-19.

It's to help travel agents and tour arrangement service providers stay viable so they can keep paying refunds and credits to Australian consumers unable to travel because of COVID-19.

The program was announced by the Australian Government on 1 December 2020.

You must read the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program Guidelines on the GrantConnect website (grants.gov.au) before you make a claim.

Is my business eligible for the grant?

Your business may be eligible if it had an annual GST turnover of between $50,000 and $20 million in the 2019 calendar year.

All claims for businesses with a GST turnover amount of over $20 million in the 2019 calendar year (or for annual BAS reporters in the 2018-19 Financial Year) will assessed by Austrade as an Exceptional Circumstance Review.

To be eligible, your business must also meet all of the following:

  • lodged a 2018-19 income tax return
  • received JobKeeper Payment in 1 of these fortnights:
    • 28 September to 11 October 2020
    • 12 October to 25 October 2020
  • has an active Australian Business Number (ABN)
  • has an account with an Australian financial institution

You also need to show your business was operating as a travel agent or tour arrangement service provider before 19 March 2020. Evidence can be either:

  • active incorporation or registration with the Australian Federation of Travel Agents (AFTA), Australian Tourism Export Council (ATEC), Council of Australian Tour Operators (CATO), International Air Transport Association (IATA) or another tourism peak industry body
  • an eligible ANZSIC code on your business’ 2018-19 income tax return

If your business had an annual GST turnover of $75,000 or more in the 2019 calendar year you must:

  • be registered for GST
  • have lodged all required Business Activity Statements (BAS) in that year

If you are a member of a GST reporting group, to be eligible for this grant, you must be the reporting member that lodges the BAS on behalf of all group members. All other members of the GST reporting group that do not lodge BAS aren't eligible for the grant.

You don't need to be registered for GST if your business’ turnover was less than $75,000 in the 2019 calendar year.

What's a travel agent or tour arrangement service provider?

A travel agent is an entity that, on a commission basis, mainly operates a business that:

  • sells travel products either directly or on behalf of third parties to consumers, and
  • coordinates reservation bookings, confirmations and payments for travel products either directly or on behalf of third parties for consumers

For the purposes of the grant, a person isn’t a travel agent if they’re only a shareholder of the business.

A tour arrangement service provider is an entity that, on a commission basis, mainly operates a business that:

  • sells travel packages either directly or on behalf of third parties to consumers, and
  • coordinates the booking reservations, confirmations and payments for travel packages either directly, or on behalf of third parties for consumers

For the purposes of the grant, a tour arrangement service provider doesn’t include:

  • a person or entity who operates the day-to-day running of a tour or tourism activity, such as a tour guide
  • a shareholder

What fortnights do I need to be getting JobKeeper Payment to be eligible?

You must be getting JobKeeper Payment under the Coronavirus Economic Response Package (Payments and Benefits) Rules 2020.

You must have received JobKeeper Payment in 1 or both of the following fortnights:

  • 28 September to 11 October 2020
  • 12 October to 25 October 2020

Does ‘GST annual turnover’ mean my total sales or my gross profit?

The GST turnover is the total of your sales for the 2019 calendar year.

For a business:

  • registered for GST, this is the total amount you recorded in all Business Activity Statements for the 2019 calendar year. If you can't provide the exact total turnover for 2019, we'll accept an estimate. We'll verify your turnover with the certificate the ATO gives you
  • not registered for GST and a turnover of under $75,000 in the 2019 calendar year, this is based on your calculation across the 2019 calendar year

My business turnover is less than $75,000, and I’m not registered for GST. What can I do?

If you're not registered for GST, you can declare your 2019 calendar year turnover was between $50,000 to $74,999 when you submit your claim. You'll still need to lodge the ATO certificate and verify your registration with a peak industry body (if you’re registered with one).

If your annual turnover for the 2019 calendar year is less than $50,000, you're not eligible for the grant.

I'm a member of a GST reporting group, am I eligible to make a claim?

Even though you may have an ABN as a member of a GST reporting group, if you don't have BAS reporting obligations you're not eligible for the grant.

However, the reporting member in your GST reporting group that lodges the BAS for the group may apply for the grant on behalf of all group members.

How long do I have to claim?

It's best to get your claim in as soon as possible. You can claim between 14 December 2020 and midnight (AEDT) 13 March 2021, or until the program funds run out.

We can also only accept one claim per ABN.

How will you prioritise claims?

We'll process them in the order we get them.

How will I know when you have stopped taking claims?

We'll carefully monitor funds paid under the program. If program funds run out before 13 March 2021 and you have submitted your application, we’ll let you know.

Information will also be published on our website at servicesaustralia.gov.au/travelsupportprogram and on Business Hub.

Can I submit my claim in one go, from start to finish?

No. You can start the claim now and complete up to and including the 'Review and Hold' step.

You'll need to wait for your ATO certificate of tax information before you can finalise and submit the claim.

How do I claim?

To make a claim you must be an associate or authorised person for your business on the Australian Business Register (ABR). You should check your details on the ABR are up to date before you start your claim.

Go to servicesaustralia.gov.au/travelsupportprogram and go to the How to claim section. You must then follow the steps in order.

Reminders:

  • You must read the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program Guidelines on the GrantConnect website (grants.gov.au) before you make a claim
  • If your business has an existing PRODA account, you need to link it to Business Hub. You don’t need to register again
  • You can't ask for a certificate from the ATO directly and you cannot submit your claim until you have it. We will not accept any other tax documents

What if I don't have access to online services?

You need to make a claim for the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program online on our Business Hub.

If you don't have access to online services, we can give you details of your nearest Services Australia location where you can get support to make your claim online.

What if I can't complete the online application as it will not let me select certain responses?

There are known display issues if you use Internet Explorer as your web browser. We recommend you use Google Chrome to get the best experience when completing your claim in Business Hub.

Do I need to provide anything when I claim?

We'll ask you to verify your business details. You must do this using the certificate of tax information from the ATO.

When you complete the 'Review and Hold' step of a claim, it gives us your consent to pass on your ABN to the ATO. The ATO will then send you a certificate of tax information. This certificate is for the sole purpose of this grant and will verify your business details.

You cannot submit your claim until you have this certificate. When you get it, you need to upload a copy in Business Hub. Use the 'Document upload' step in your claim and submit your claim.

You should keep a copy of your application and any supporting documents.

How will I get the ATO certificate?

We'll pass on your ABN to the ATO once you complete the 'Review and Hold' step of the online claim. The ATO will generate the certificate for your ABN.

The ATO will send it to the mailing address it has for your ABN. This may be your tax agent. If you’re a sole trader and have linked your MyGov account to your ATO business record, the certificate will be sent to your MyGov account.

What if my ATO certificate doesn't arrive, or takes too long to get to me?

It can take up to 5 business days to receive your certificate from the ATO.

If you have not received your certificate after that time and you think the delay is unusually long, you can call the ATO on 13 28 66.

What if the ATO advises they didn't get a request for a certificate for my business

There is currently a known issue causing delays in Services Australia sending ABNs to the ATO. All claims received are date stamped to make sure started claims are included in the total grant funding allocation. This issue is currently under investigation.

Services Australia can check to see if a request has been automatically sent to ATO. If not previously sent and the claim has been submitted, Services Australia will manually request the ATO issue one to you.

How much can I get under the program?

It depends on your annual turnover. Eligible businesses with an annual GST turnover of between $50,000 and $99,999 in the 2019 calendar year can get $1,500.

The grant amount goes up by $2,500 for each $100,000 increase in annual GST turnover or part thereof.

For example, an eligible business with a GST turnover of between:

  • $100,000 and $199,999 will get $4,000
  • $200,000 and $299,999 will get $6,500
  • $500,000 and $599,999 will get $14,000

The maximum amount you can receive is $100,000.

All businesses with an annual turnover of more than $20 million will be assessed by Austrade as an Exceptional Circumstance Review.

How will you pay me?

If the grant is approved it will be transferred as a one-off lump sum into your nominated Australian bank account. It must be the account associated with your ABN.

Can my accountant or tax agent lodge the application for me?

No. Your accountant or tax agent cannot make a claim on behalf of the business unless they are also listed as an associate or registered person in the Australian Business Register.

Once you have completed the 'Review and Hold' step of your application, we'll ask the ATO to send you a certificate of tax information. This certificate is for the sole purpose of this grant. The ATO will send the certificate to the mailing address it has for your ABN. This may be your accountant or tax agent and they should be aware to forward this to you as soon as they receive it.

What if I'm a director/owner of the company but PRODA will not let me register or link my business?

The application must be submitted by someone listed as an associate or registered person on the Australian Business Register (ABR).

To see if someone is an associate or registered person, you can visit https://www.abr.gov.au/checking-your-abr-details-online.

What if I see a different name during my application process to the business I wish to claim for?

Sometimes there are multiple business names recorded under the one Australian Business Number (ABN). When you apply for the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program you choose a business as part of the business registration, linking and starting the claim processes.

You can check these details within the application and record the correct business name in the ‘Organisational Details’ step.

If you get to the end of the claim process and there is another business name (instead of the business you wish to claim for) you should not be concerned as:

  • the verification process that takes place is made under the ABN (not the business name)
  • we will use information received from the ATO (from the certificate) to confirm information relating to the ABN

What if I upload the wrong document?

In assessing your grant application Services Australia verifies the documents uploaded and their content.

If a document has been uploaded in error or is not the document needed to verify your eligibility, Services Australia will send a notification to you (the applicant). It will advise your Business Hub claim has been updated to enable the uploading of the correct document.

If this relates to the ATO issued certificate, on receipt of this certificate (noting in most cases this will be issued by traditional posted mail), you should return to Business Hub and upload at that point.

There is no other process available to lodge documents. Do not attend a service centre, Agent or Access Point. Do not send documents to State communication unless your claim has been finalised already and you are appealing the decision.

What if the GST turnover amount on the ATO certificate is different to my calculation?

If a business is registered for GST, GST turnover details on the certificate of tax information from the ATO will always be used.

If your business needed to submit a BAS in 2019, your business turnover will be calculated as the total of the G1 figures reported in your BAS in the 2019 calendar year.

If your business did not need to submit a BAS in 2019 and did not need to be registered for GST (as your turnover was below $75,000), you can make a self declaration that your business turnover was between $50,000 and $74,999 in the 2019 calendar year.

If your business’ claim is granted and you do not agree with the payment amount, you can lodge an review/appeal.

Services Australia can only make a determination as part of the review/appeal process on the claim (including grant amount paid) based on the figures on the certificate of tax information from the ATO.

Review/appeal rights are explained in the decision letter your business will receive.

What if I put the wrong bank account number in my claim?

Bank account details (as well as other claim information) cannot be updated once the claim has progressed to the ‘Review and Hold’ step.

If a bank account number or any other content needs to be updated, Services Australia will need the correct information from you and will try to update the claim with this information. If this update cannot occur, the claim may need to be cancelled and started again.

What if I disagree with a decision?

If you do not agree with:

  • the initial decision, you can apply for a formal review of the decision by an Authorised Review officer (ARO)
  • the ARO decision, you can ask for the Emergency Management Programme to review the decision. Note the request for this review must be within 29 days of the ARO decision date

What is the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program?

The COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program is a one-off grant to help travel agents and tour arrangement service providers who have lost income because of the impacts of COVID-19.

It is to help travel agents and tour arrangement service providers to stay viable so they can keep paying refunds and credits to Australian consumers unable to travel because of COVID-19.

The program was announced by the Australian Government on 1 December 2020.

An applicant must read the COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program Guidelines on the GrantConnect website (grants.gov.au) before they make a claim.

Is there a way to see the status of a claim?

You can see the status of the claim in the Work Items box in Process Direct.

Search for claims that have a status of submitted.

To view other claim status, change Status to:

  • On Hold
  • Approved
  • Rejected

How do I find a claim if I do not have the CRN?

You can search for organisation records using the Organisation Summary tile from the Home page in Process Direct.

  • Select Organisation Summary
  • Select Search For – Organisation
  • Select ID Type – ABN (Insert ABN in blank field)
  • Select Go (or hard enter)
  • Click anywhere on the Search Results bar
  • Select Transactions
  • Select Applications
  • Select Claim Number (in blue)

What do I do if the ATO Certificate states ‘not required to lodge’ for the BAS, but the applicant has listed their income as over $75,000?

If the ATO Certificate states not required to lodge, you will need to look at the self-declared amount.

If the self-declared amount is:

  • $75,000 or more, then the business should be registered for GST and lodging BAS. If the business is not registered for GST they will not be eligible for this payment
  • $50,000 - $74,999, then the claim can be assessed based on all other criteria and using the applicant’s amount to decide the Payment Amount

ATO certificate states the applicant did not receive JobKeeper payment but the applicant has provided evidence that JobKeeper payment was actually received.

An assessment can only be made on the ATO certificate. Additional documents will not be used to override ATO certificate information.

Refer to Question How can an applicant dispute the ATO certificate.

How can an applicant dispute the ATO certificate?

If the applicant disputes any content that is on their ATO certificate, tell them to contact the ATO on 13 28 66 and discuss their concerns.

Important: if the applicant/third party is advising they dispute the amount recorded on the ATO certificate at ‘GST Turnover amount’, ask them to check their BAS, for the amounts they recorded at the G1 question (exclusive of GST amounts). If they have a different figure than what is on the ATO certificate then they should contact the ATO.

If the ATO confirms that they will be sending an updated certificate they can provide it to Services Australia by:

  • Uploading it by Business Hub account if you have not already uploaded documents and submitted their claim
  • Emailing a copy to State communication if they have already submitted their claim

If the ATO confirms they will not be issuing an updated certificate, the claim must be assessed on the initial certificate.

A decision will be made based on information to hand. Tell applicants that they must either contact Services Australia and:

  • confirm the outcome of ATO contact by phone, or
  • lodge new ATO certificate within 10 business days

Applicant has received a notification advising they have a letter however they are not able to view the letter.

The notification applicants are receiving states ‘to view this letter, please go to the Services Australia website and log in to our business online services’.

Make sure applicants are logging into the Business Hub account (using their individual PRODA account) and not their Centrelink Business Online Services account.

Note: whilst most businesses will receive the decision letter in Business Hub, Individual/Sole Traders will not. For them, it will be available in their myGov account.

How does an applicant upload a new ATO certificate or evidence to support an appeal once a claim has been finalised?

There is no capacity to upload additional information by Business Hub once a claim has been finalised.

If the applicant wants to give more evidence to support their claim, they can email it to State communication.

Do I contact an applicant with a decision after hours? For example, I am processing claims on the evening shift.

No, do not contact applicants to tell them of their claim decision when processing on the night shift. In these cases, give the details on the spreadsheet and a Service Officer on day shift will contact the applicant with an unsuccessful claim outcome.

I am trying to finalise a claim and have received this error message in Process Direct – ‘Application not finalised. Call to ISIS Payment Interface failed. Please try again later.’

Escalate any instances of this error message to Emergency Management (Level 2 Helpdesk) mailbox for further investigation. Include details of the claim and a screenshot of the error message.

Applicant has not received their ATO Certificate yet. What can I do to follow this up?

Ask the applicant if the business lodges their GST annually?

If so, ATO certificates for annual GST lodgers will be issued starting 05 February 2021.

If the business is not an annual GST lodger and they have not received their ATO certificate, tell applicants it can take up to 5 days for them to be issued their certificate from the ATO, as well as any additional time for postage.

If, after that time they have not received the ATO certificate, the applicant can contact the ATO on 13 28 66.

If a business is an annual lodger, which yearly turnover amount do they use?

The turnover amount to be used for annual GST lodgers is their 2018-19 turnover amounts.

Certificates issued to this cohort will have different descriptions for both the BAS and turnover amount fields than all others, as follows:

  • 2018-19 income year business activity statement has been lodged
  • 2018-19 income year GST turnover (G1 on business activity statement – GST exclusive amount) as at 14 December 2020

Process claims as per any other claim:

  • with the responses as per ATO certificate being used with regards to BAS, and
  • amounts being recorded on the ATO verification screen in the workflow

The Process page will help you with these claims.

If the applicant cancels a claim that has been lodged, will this trigger a new ATO certificate to be issued?

No. Only one ATO certificate will be issued per ABN.

If the applicant changes their information after the certificate has generated, a new certificate will not be issued.

Refer to Question How can an applicant dispute the ATO certificate.

I am having trouble finalising/granting an applicant’s claim that shows status as ‘on hold’

You cannot finalise or grant a claim when it is on hold.

The applicant will need to either:

  • Upload any required information and select submit
  • Withdraw/cancel their application (no appeal rights)

What if the applicant disagrees with the decision?

The first step is discuss the decision with the applicant.

If the applicant disagrees with:

  • The initial decision, they can apply for a formal review of the decision by an Authorised Review officer (ARO)
  • The ARO decision, they can ask the Emergency Management Programme to review the decision. Note the request for this review must be within 29 days of the ARO decision date

More details about the review and appeal process is on the Process page.

What should I do if the bank account name is not in the business name?

Services Australia does not have a mechanism to verify if the bank account details is in the business’ name, with the exception that as part of assessing the claim, a Service Officer will check:

  • BSB lookup to confirm Australian bank account using bsb-numbers.com.au/
  • Check the name of the account declared by the applicant – this should be the business name associated with the ABN
  • For individual/sole traders, the name of the bank account may be either the applicant’s personal name or a business name (ABN lookup would confirm if business was an individual/sole trader)
  • For businesses, the name of the bank account should be declared as a business name. In instances where a business, other than individual/sole trader, has a personal name for the bank details, escalate the claim to the Emergency Management (Level 2 Helpdesk) mailbox for advice

What should I do if I identify there is more than 1 claim made for an ABN?

Escalate immediately to the Emergency Management (Level 2 Helpdesk) mailbox for advice.
