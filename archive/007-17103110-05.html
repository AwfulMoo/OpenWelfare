Q888 Request for information - CCS and Additional CCS

Evidence requirements for CCS exemptions

Item

Evidence requirements

1

13 years and under + Read more ...

Statutory declaration recorded on a Commonwealth statutory declaration form, outlining:

  • why it is unreasonable for the child to be unsupervised
  • confirming there is no suitable adult over the age of 18 (including all other support networks, not just the parent/s or carers) available to care for them

Note: for children in this age group, evidence can be supplied showing the child has a disability. This will only impact how long the exemption can be approved for and has no bearing on eligibility.

2

14 and 15 years - with a disability or medical condition + Read more ...

Statutory declaration recorded on a Commonwealth statutory declaration form, outlining:

  • why it is unreasonable for the child to be unsupervised
  • confirming there is no suitable adult over the age of 18 (including all other support networks, not just the parent/s or carers) available to care for them

One of the following, confirming the child's disability:

  • Where the child is a National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) participant, the child's NDIS participant number, or
  • Where the child has a permanent condition and is not an NDIS participant, evidence of the diagnosis by a medical practitioner or registered psychologist. Note: the document does not need to be dated within a certain period and if evidence is already available, the child does not need to be re assessed, or
  • Where the child has a non-permanent condition or is undergoing assessment for a listed condition, documentary evidence of the diagnosis by a medical practitioner or psychologist dated within 24 months

Note: documentary evidence can include letters, certificates, reports, appointment letters and referral for assessments. The evidence should include:

  • condition or disability the child has been diagnosed with, or
  • condition or disability the child is undergoing assessment for, and
  • practitioner or psychologist's signature block with their qualifications

3

16, 17 and 18 years - with a disability or medical condition + Read more ...

Statutory declaration recorded on a Commonwealth statutory declaration form, outlining:

  • why it is unreasonable for the child to be unsupervised
  • confirming there is no suitable adult over the age of 18 (including all other support networks, not just the parent/s or carers) available to care for them

Confirmation of the child's disability:

  • documentary evidence of the diagnosis by a medical practitioner or registered psychologist
  • dated within 24 months, regardless whether permanent or non-permanent conditions

The delegate must also be satisfied that an exceptional circumstance exists to justify CCS or ACCS eligibility, (the medical condition or disability of the child is not sufficient). Customers should make a verbal exception circumstance submission which should outline:

  • the type of exceptional circumstance
  • how access to subsidised care would facilitate them being able to participate in a recognised activity
  • why their child has a legitimate need to continue to access subsidised child care
  • that there are no better care options available to the family (e.g. why they could not instead use NDIS support or another disability program)
  • that the subsidised child care would not be used for respite
  • how long access to CCS is required for (e.g. to the end of the school year)
