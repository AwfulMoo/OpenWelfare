askMBS

The Department of Health are regularly adding, removing and changing MBS items.

Help is available for health professionals who have MBS interpretation questions:

  • AskMBS email advice service assists health professionals understand and comply with MBS requirements
  • Health Professional Education Resources Gateway and Services Australia website have education guides, infographics, and eLearning

Services Australia staff should give general MBS interpretation advice about:

  • patient or health professional eligibility
  • payments, and
  • claiming

See the assessing rules in QITI and explanatory notes on MBS Online when giving advice or processing a claim. Note: information in QITI is for internal use only.

Contact a Programme Support Officer (PSO) for technical support.

If a health professional is asking which item relates to a specific service they will perform or has a complex enquiry, tell them to contact askMBS.

Examples of when Services Australia staff should assist with interpretation

Query: What does it mean in the description for item 30071 when it says ‘as an independent procedure’?

Action/Response: Staff can first look at item description in MBS Online. They could also search that phrase in:

  • MBS Online to find Note TN.8.1

Assist the caller to navigate to information in MBS Note TN.8.1 and/or the Education guide - Billing multiple MBS items on the Services Australia website. If the enquiry changes to be about a specific clinical situation to meet the requirements of an independent procedure refer them to askMBS.

Query: What does it mean when my patient’s claim was rejected with reason 162, and what can I do?

Action/Response: Assist the caller to locate the Education guide - Medicare reason codes and reducing claim rejections on the Services Australia website. Show them the row in the first table for reason code 162. If needed, perform the security check and follow Release of claims information in Medicare. Read text or notations in claims and on patient history.

Examples of when the caller needs to contact askMBS

Query:

  • What mental health item should I bill now that it is a new calendar year?
  • What is this conflicting or unclear information on MBS Online?
  • How am I meant to interpret this legislation?
  • How can I give feedback on factsheets or news articles?

Acceptable text examples – care plans (e.g. Better Access, CDM, Eating disorder treatment and management plans)

Table 5: this table provides information and examples of acceptable text.

Note: these examples and other annotations from health professionals should be assessed on a case by case basis. Health professionals are responsible to ensuring they are claiming correctly and fulfilling the requirements of Medicare items.

Item

Description

1

General practitioner (GP) mental health treatment item during consultation for a separate condition

Example:

A patient sees their GP on 07/06/2015 for a standard consultation (item 23) for a suspected chest infection. The GP recognises that the patient needs a GP mental health treatment plan. The GP:

  • diagnoses the patient with a chest infection and organises relevant treatment (e.g. script for antibiotics and home remedies)
  • creates GP mental health treatment plan (GPMHTP) and fulfils the requirements of item 2701

The GP claims both item 23 and 2701 and states they are unrelated (or to that effect) on the account.

Result:

Both items can be paid (23 and 2701) because the GP has provided sufficient text to show that the 2 attendance items are unrelated.

Reference:

MBS note AN.0.56

2

GPMHTP developed over multiple consultations

Example:

A patient sees their GP on 10/11/2019 and books a standard consultation appointment (would normally be covered under item 23). During the appointment the GP checks that the patient is eligible for and would benefit from a having a GPMHTP in place. The GP begins creating a GPMHTP for the patient, but is already running late so asks the patient to return that afternoon to finish it.

The patient returns later that day and they finish the GPMHTP.

The GP claims 2 consultation items (23 x 2) because the patient attended on 2 separate occasions, and provides the following text:

  • 23 – GPMHTP 10:30
  • 23 – GPMHTP 15:25

Result:

The claim should be rejected because it does not show that the 2 consultations were unrelated (they both say they were for a GPMHTP so are a continuation of an earlier service).

Health professionals should claim the item that best represents the service they provided to that patient on that day. They are responsible for claiming properly. In this scenario the most appropriate item to claim for both consultations on the same day is:

  • one x item 2710 – GPMTHP 15:25 at the completion of the second visit

Note: Service Officers are not to provide suggestions about which items a health professional should claim. They are to provide advice about what information would be required in the claim and eligibility to claim specific items.

Reference:

MBS note AN.0.56

3

Separate consultation during/after consultation for GPMP items

Example:

A patient books an appointment for 22/05/2018 with their GP to review their GP management plan (GPMP) (item 732). During the appointment the patient mentions a rash that has developed on their arm over the past few days.

The GP spends a few minutes assessing the rash and writing a prescription. The remainder of the appointment (and majority of the time) is reviewing the patient’s GPMP.

The GP isn’t sure if they should claim both item 732 and a consultation item for the rash.

Result:

The claim was submitted for both items 732 and 23, e.g.:

  • 732 – GPMP Review 10:25
  • 23 – Rash on arm 10:20

Reject item 23. There is a restriction between the 2 items and no legislative basis for them to be provided on the same day.

Reference:

MBS note AN.0.47

Independent procedure examples

Table 8: this table provides examples of assessment outcomes of independent procedures.

Example

Description

1

On 7/02/2010, Dr Y bills an item 30062 for the removal of an etonogestrel subcutaneous implant on a patient’s left arm and at the same time implants hormone or living tissue in the same arm, through a different incision and bills item 14206.

  • item 30062: etonogestrel subcutaneous implant, removal of, as an independent procedure
  • item 14206: HORMONE OR LIVING TISSUE IMPLANTATION by cannula

On 05/12/1996, Dr Z bills an item 47591 for a knee reconstruction and an item 47966 for the transfer of tendon. The account notates both services were performed on the right knee through separate incisions.

  • item 47966: TENDON OR LIGAMENT, TRANSFER, as an independent procedure

As the health professional has indicated that the service described in independent procedure item 47966 was performed on the same body part described in item 47591, a Medicare benefit is not payable for the independent procedure even though it was performed through a separate incision.

‘Not being a service associated with’ examples

Table 11: this table provides examples of assessment outcomes of where services that are not associated with other services.

Example

Description

1

On 15/08/2016, Dr V performs a diagnostic laparoscopy (item 30390) and immediately performs a laparoscopic appendectomy (item 30572). The description for MBS item 30390 states:

  • not being a service associated with any other laparoscopic procedure

As item 30572 is another laparoscopic procedure, the diagnostic laparoscopy is not payable as it was performed on the same occasion. A Medicare benefit is payable for item 30572 and the claim for item 30390 must be rejected.

2

On 14/12/2018, Dr W performs a diagnostic laparoscopy (item 30390), while a cervical dilation and uterine curettage (item 35640) is performed under the same general anaesthetic. The description for MBS item 30390 states:

  • not being a service associated with any other laparoscopic procedure

A Medicare benefit is payable for both services as the second service is not a laparoscopic procedure.

3

On 23/06/2021, Dr X performs a diagnostic laparoscopy (item 30390) at 9:00 am and a laparoscopic splenectomy (item 31470) is required later that day at 9:00 pm, as a separate occasion of service and under a different anaesthetic. The description for MBS item 30390 states:

  • not being a service associated with any other laparoscopic procedure

Medicare benefits are payable for both laparoscopic procedures as each procedure relates to a separate occasion.

4

On 2/11/2018, Dr Y performs a mastectomy for gynecomastia (item 31525) and liposuction to one regional area (item 45585) during the same occasion. The description for item 31525 states:

  • not being a service associated with a service to which item 45585 applies

A Medicare benefit is payable only for item 45585 as both procedures were performed on the one occasion.

5

On 27/03/2019, Dr Z performs a mastectomy for gynecomastia (item 31525) at 9:00 am and liposuction to one regional area (item 45585) on the same day at 7:00 pm. The description for item 31525 states:

  • not being a service associated with a service to which item 45585 applies

A Medicare benefit is payable for both item 31525 and 45585 as they were performed on separate occasions.

Time dependency examples

Table 14: this table provides examples of assessment outcomes of services that are time dependent.

Example

Description

1

On 14/10/2007, Dr Y bills an item 10970 for providing a speech pathology service under a GP Management Plan, this is the 5th visit for Mr A in the calendar year.

Allied health services are limited to 5 services from any item range 10950 to 10970 in a calendar year.

  • Internal only - a time dependency rule is in place which limits the use of item 55707 to one service every 280 days. These 280 days represent 40 weeks gestation for an average pregnancy length

Item association examples

Table 15: this table provides examples of assessment outcomes of services that have item associations.

Example

Description

1

On 03/08/2020, Dr Y sees the patient, Ms A, for a subsequent specialist attendance and bills an item 133. One of the criteria in the item description for item 133 states:

  • item 132 applied to an attendance claimed in the preceding 12 months

During the assessment of 133 an association rule checks for an item 132, performed by the same health professional on Medicare patient history, or within the same claim. The rule looks for a 132 with a date of service in the previous 12 months of the date for service of item 133.

Ms A had an initial consultant physician attendance with Dr Y 5 months before, on 03/03/2020, and item 132 has been processed on Medicare history. As an item 132 is present on Ms A’s Medicare history within the previous 12 months, item 133 by Dr Y is payable.

2

Dr Z performs a skin flap repair for Mr B and bills an item 45201. The item description for item 45201 states:

  • Muscle, myocutaneous or skin flap, where clinically indicated to repair one surgical excision made in the removal of a malignant or non-malignant skin lesion (only in association with items 31000, 31001, 31002, 31358, 31359, 31360, 31363, 31364, 31369, 31370, 31371, 31373 or 31376) - may be claimed only once per defect

During the assessment of item 45201 an association rule checks for one of the associated skin lesion excision items on Medicare patient history, or within the same claim. The date of service of item 45201 must be the same, or within 3 days date of service of the skin flap.

The account for Mr B has item 45201 and 31369 listed for services performed on 21/08/2020. It is with text notating both items were performed on the left arm and same defect. Both items are payable.
