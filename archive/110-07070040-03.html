Receiving Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) End of Year (EOY) review forms

Table 1: this table outlines the process for Service Officers and AIC Smart Centre staff to complete the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) End of Year (EOY) review forms. Portions of this process are completed by Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme Smart Centre Processing staff only.

Step

Action

1

Receipt of (EOY) review + Read more ...

The completed Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) End of Year (EOY) review form is normally received at the Student Services postal address. The scanned copy of the review form is referred to the relevant AIC Smart Centre. The receipt date of the review is recorded.

2

EOY review form received at service centre + Read more ...

Has the completed form been lodged at, or sent to a service centre rather than sent to the Student Services postal address for the AIC Smart Centre?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, see Step 1 of the Scrutinising AIC EOY Review forms table

3

Review form received at service centre + Read more ...

Check that the form is completed correctly.

Form received in person

If the form is missing the following information return to the customer/applicant for completion:

  • missing questions or documents
  • not signed (including the applicant’s partner’s signature where Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA) is being claimed)

If the form is completed scan onto the student's record. Include the service centre and the receipt date recorded. The scanned copy is then available for processing by the relevant AIC Smart Centre.

Review form received by post or fax

The form is scanned onto the student's record by the service centre and the receipt date recorded. The scanned copy is then available for processing by the relevant AIC Smart Centre.

Lookup local Smart Centre Processing details for: Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) for the area. Record a DOC advising of the action taken. Procedure ends here.

Scrutinising Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) End of Year (EOY) review forms

Table 2: this table describes the process for Service Officers and AIC Smart Centre staff to follow when scrutinising Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) End of Year (EOY) review forms.

Step

Action

1

Is the review form fully complete? + Read more ...

Have all questions been answered, all supporting documents supplied (if needed) and the form signed by the applicant (and the applicant’s partner if Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA) is being claimed)?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No;
    • attempt to call the applicant, or
    • return a photocopy of the form and request the missing information be provided via a Q999 (this includes missing signatures)
    • record details of the request on a DOC
    • procedure ends here

2

Applicant details + Read more ...

Is the person still an approved applicant for AIC payments for the student?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Applicant details (continued) + Read more ...

Does the form indicate the date from which the applicant was no longer an approved applicant?

  • Yes,
    • Do not select the EOY review activity on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen. Navigate to the Benefit Action (BA) screen
    • Code 'EIC' in the SVC RSN field
    • Code 'REJ' in the Action field
    • Code 'LCC' in the reason field, and use the date the EOY review has indicated that the applicant became unapproved
    • Navigate to the AR screen and send any overpayment for investigation. DOC the record and finalise the REJection activity
  • No, attempt to call the applicant to determine the date they became unapproved. If uncontactable by phone, send a request for this information to the applicant via Q999. DOC the record. Procedure ends here

4

Processing an AIC EOY review + Read more ...

The form is returned from the applicant under the following groups:

  • Group 15: no expected changes or supporting documents are required
  • Group 16: expected changes and supporting documents are required as the child moving from primary to secondary
  • Group 17: parental income may be required for Additional Boarding Allowance to be paid

The EOY activity displays on FAL until 1 April. Any EOY reviews processed after 1 April must be indexed as a new claim.

Is the EOY activity being assessed before 1 April?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Assessing the new claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC)(SY040)

5

Access the Future Activity List (FAL) screen in Customer Record + Read more ...

Process the form using the ‘EOY’ activity on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen.

Access the FAL screen by coding 'FAL' in Nxt: field:

  • Select the 'EOY' activity
  • Source: field 'AICEY'
  • DOR: field the date the review was received. Note: do not leave the DOR blank as the system will auto default to the date the review was sent. This affects timeliness standards
  • Confirm changes?: field 'Y'
  • Select [Enter] to register the EOY review activity
  • Select the 'REA' activity from the AL screen to start processing the review
  • Repeat this process for all AIC siblings who have had EOYs lodged and not assessed yet. This makes sure all family members are actioned and assessed on the same day

The system will navigate to the AIC Task Selector (EITS) screen. Select the screens that need updates.

Go to Table 3.

Processing Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) End of Year (EOY) review forms

Table 3: this table describes the process for Service Officers and AIC Smart Centre staff to follow when processing Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) End of Year (EOY) review forms.

Step

Action

1

Home address + Read more ...

Has the principal home address changed?

  • Yes, update the address details on the Address Details (AD) screen. Go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 5

2

Geographical isolation + Read more ...

Is the student paid due to geographical isolation? Note: a student is paid Geographic Isolation if there are distances coded on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 6

3

Geographical isolation and change of address + Read more ...

Students paid geographical isolation must have their eligibility re-established if there has been a change to:

  • their address
  • the distance to the transport pick-up point
  • the distance to the nearest appropriate state school

Has the address of the Principal Family Home or distance to transport or nearest appropriate state school changed?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 9

4

Change to transport + Read more ...

Has the applicant provided details of the:

  • distances from their new address to the nearest government school and the transport type, or
  • new transport and distance details where the address hasn't changed

If:

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, call the applicant to get the distances. If unable to make contact ask for the information using a Q999. If distances are close to geographical isolation rules include a Marginal Distance Confirmation (SY069). See Step 1 in the Resources page of Isolation conditions for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme). The Resources page has a link to the form. Procedure ends here

5

Test geographic isolation + Read more ...

Test the applicant's eligibility for geographical isolation. Noting that when the change of address, transport or distance started can decide when AIC eligibility is affected.

When a change to non-isolated location starts before 1 January, AIC may still be payable until 31 December under Continuity of School concession. See Change of address for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme applicants or students. Update their details on the EIID screen.

Does the new address, transport and/or distance(s) satisfy the geographical isolation rules from 1 January of the new study year?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, update their details on the EIID screen. The system will REJ the customer from the date of change. DOC and finalise the activity. Procedure ends here

6

Student has a special need + Read more ...

A student is paid under special needs because of a health related condition, or has a need to attend a special school/facility. (These are coded on the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen).

Is the student paid because of special needs?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Special needs required documentation + Read more ...

Special needs that are temporary, must be verified each year for eligibility. The form Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Medical Statement – Student Special Needs (SY099) is used. This is completed by the applicant and medical professional.

A new SY099 must be completed when:

  • the condition is temporary, and
  • no duration estimate was provided on the previous SY099 which is dated earlier than the 1 July to which the EOU form relates

Students who have a long term or permanent medical condition or disability is answered on Question 7 by the medical practitioner or specialist listing:

  • the medical diagnosis
  • the expected duration
  • whether the condition is lifelong, chronic or permanent

Students with these special needs will not be re-verified each year. For these types of circumstances, code SPT in the Special Needs Reason: field of the EIC Isolation Details (EIID) screen. This ensures re-verification is not requested.

Does the special need have to be re-verified?

  • Yes, have all the documents been supplied with the EOY review form?
    • Yes, make updates to the EIID screen, then go to Step 9
    • No, request the additional information. Go to step 13
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Student deemed to be isolated + Read more ...

A student can be deemed to be isolated because:

  • their parents occupation require frequent moves
  • they live in a second home with a sibling that meets an Isolation condition (Geographic Isolation or Special Needs), or
  • the student lives in a Special Institution

For students:

  • Whose parent's occupation requires frequent moves, an itinerary of proposed movements in the coming 12 months will need to be supplied with the EOY review form. See Step 5 of Determining and coding isolation criteria for AIC table in Isolation conditions for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme
  • Who live in a second home with a sibling that meets an Isolation condition, check that the student is still deemed to be isolated. See Step 6 of Determining and coding isolation criteria for AIC table in Isolation conditions for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme
  • Who live in a Special Institution, no supporting evidence is needed as the eligibility was established as part of the initial claim processing

Is the student still deemed to be isolated?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, make updates as necessary to the EIID screen, then go to Step 15

9

Study details + Read more ...

Students eligible for Distance Education Allowance may be undertaking home schooling. Depending on their home state, the EOY review process may need a new Home Schooling Registration Certificate if the student:

  • has changed state/territory, or
  • had a break in home schooling

AIC applicants across the country with continuing students doing home schooling, do not need to get a new Home Schooling Registration Certificate for the EOY review process. The student's registration is checked through the Services Australia Student Review (data matching) process during the study year.

See Resources page of Eligibility for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme Distance Education Allowance (DED) for eligibility rules for home schooling.

Will the student meet the approved study requirements? See Approved studies for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme for more information if required.

  • Yes, enter the education details for the following year on a new Education Course Details (EDC) screen, e.g.:
    • primary
    • secondary year, or
    • tertiary course level
  • If they are a tertiary student, include the tertiary end date in the Tertiary End Date: field on the EIC Claim Details (EICD) screen. Go to Step 10
  • No, the student is not eligible for AIC for their new course or study. Enter the education details for the following year on a new EDC screen. For tertiary students, include the tertiary end date in the Tertiary End Date: field on the EICD screen. If the system has not rejected the customer when it is known that the student is eligible, Navigate to the BA screen and code 'EIC' in the SVC RSN field, code 'REJ' in the Action field
    • Code 'NAI' in the reason field for an unapproved institution
    • Code 'NAC' in the reason field for an unapproved course
    • Navigate to the AR screen and send any overpayment for investigation. DOC the record and finalise the 'REJ'ection activity. If the applicant cannot be contacted by phone, send a request for this information to the applicant via Q999. DOC the record
    • Procedure ends here

10

Change in living arrangement + Read more ...

AIC pays for students via 3 main methods:

  • Distance Education Allowance
  • Second Home Allowance
  • Basic Boarding Allowance/Additional Boarding Allowance

Only one of these arrangements can happen at any one time. Care needs to be taken to make sure this has not changed in the EOY review process. This will result in an incorrect payment.

Has the customer swapped living arrangement?

  • Yes, go to the Living Arrangement Details (EILA) screen and enter the new living arrangement and the start date from which this occurred
    Note: when entering a new EILA screen for a student living in a Second Family Home, make sure that the number of other siblings living in the Second Family Home is entered in the No of SHA siblings field
  • No, no update to the Living Arrangement Details (EILA) screen is required. Go to Step 11

11

Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA) + Read more ...

Is the applicant applying for Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA)?

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, go to Step 13

12

ABA claim support documents + Read more ...

Has all the information been supplied in relation to the ABA (Boarding fees and Base Tax Year income details or estimate, or voluntary maintenance received between 1 January 2017 and 31 December 2018 (if applicable))?

  • Yes, code all Base Tax Year details for all relevant parents/guardians on the NHI screen
    • If voluntary maintenance was received between 1 January 2017 and 31 December 2018, for assistance with coding voluntary maintenance, see historical information Step 4 of the Viewing and recording maintenance income details table in Maintenance Income Test (MIT) for ABSTUDY and Youth Allowance (YA)
    • For assistance with coding boarding fees, see Step 5 of the Claim assessment table in Assessing the new claim for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) (SY040)
    • Go to Step 13
  • No, request the additional information. Basic Boarding Allowance can be paid while the assessment for the ABA is being made. Go to Step 13

13

Other factors affecting payment + Read more ...

Where there are changes to the following details, update the respective screens. For:

  • addition or loss of applicant's partner, update the PIT Parent Selection (NPPS) screen
  • changes to siblings, update the details via the PIT Sibling Selection (NPSS) screen. Check the details of the siblings on the Dependent Youth Rate Summary (DYRSUMM) screen
  • changes to income details, update the Household Income and Assets (NHI) screen if necessary

If applicant says they are receiving a payment from another Australian Government agency (i.e. Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) pension) or hold a current Health Care Card (due to receipt of maximum rate of Family Tax Benefit Part A), see Circumstances when the Parental Income Test (PIT) does not apply for information about coding manual PIT exemption reasons on NHI screen.

14

Payment Destination + Read more ...

Payments of AIC can go to the applicant or to a 3rd party nominated by the applicant.

Has the applicant changed the payment destination?

  • Yes, make sure the payment destination is updated via the Payment Instruction (PIPI) screen. If the applicant has instructed that the payment be directed to the school, type 'o' instead of 's' in the default payment section to search for the school. Once found and selected, the school's bank details will be populated
  • No, go to Step 15

15

DOC and finalise the activity + Read more ...

Record a DOC inside the activity before it is finalised. The DOC must include the following details:

  • the outcome of the review
  • the changes made (if any)
  • any extra information requested or supplied
  • details of any supplementary information used to process the activity for example, the date of the AIC Medical Statement - Student Special Needs (SY099) will be required

16

Repeat for all siblings + Read more ...

Repeat this process for all AIC siblings who have had EOY reviews lodged and not assessed yet. This makes sure all family members are actioned and assessed on the same day.
