Claim requirements + Read more ...

Special circumstances – family and domestic violence

Customers who do not meet the DAP work test will be asked in their online claim whether there were any special circumstances that affected their ability to meet the work test. Where a customer has indicated that they were affected by family and domestic violence, a priority keyword (PPLFDV) will be added to the claim work item. When allocated a family and domestic violence claim, the Service Officer must complete a social worker referral.

The customer must provide evidence of employment confirming they would have worked during the work test period. The customer’s employer may not know why the customer was unable to work, and the customer is not expected to tell their employer.

Before sending a social working referral, staff are to check the customer’s employment evidence lodged with their claim to assess if sufficient. For evidence requirements, see Paid Parental Leave scheme Work Test.

If not sufficient, attempt to contact the customer via phone to request the missing claim information.

Before calling, use DEMC to advise the customer they will receive a phone call.

If the customer requires assistance from Social Worker to obtain the relevant employment evidence, include this in the referral doc notes.

A written request for information is to be issued for the employment evidence (even if verbally discussed with customer):

  • Issue a Q777 via Request Documents Q777 guided procedure in Customer First, or
  • Request Documents in Process Direct

To create a Social Worker referral:

  • Run the Social Worker Referral guided procedure in Customer First or Referral in Process Direct
  • Select the ‘Family and Domestic Violence’ and ‘Third Party Verification’ referral option
  • Include the following in Additional Information:
    Customer has been referred for a social work assessment for DAP/PPL FDV special circumstances
    Date of Contact:
    Work Test Period (13mths):
    Referring Officer:
    Login:
    Additional Information (if required): consider any relevant information regarding contact with the customer e.g. most appropriate contact number, availability and any other information including safety and wellbeing concerns. Eg. 'Mrs Smith is available Monday and Tuesday between 9-1:30pm on mobile number 0444 444 444. Customer has advised not to leave a message.'

Complete Fast Note – Families > Claims > PPL/DAP Special Circumstances Work Test.

Annotate progress of claims to refer staff to PPL/DAP Spec Circ work test Fast Note DOC on the record

Place the claim on hold for 21days. When the social worker assessment has been completed they will:

  • take the claim off hold,
  • annotate PPL/DAP Spec Circ work test Fast Note with the outcome, and
  • return it for processing

Proof of birth is required to finalise a pre-birth DAP claim

  • If the customer has uploaded the back page of the FA081 only, contact the customer to request they upload or provide the fully completed FA081 or alternative proof of birth (e.g. birth certificate). If required, print all pages of the incomplete scanned FA081 lodged and issue with Q777 via the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure to ask the customer to complete the missing questions
  • A customer can provide a fully completed FA081 to finalise a pre-birth DAP claim. Customers can upload the completed FA081 via upload documents. If the DAP customer is unable to lodge the fully completed FA081, other acceptable proof of birth needs to be provided

Proof of birth lodged without a claim

  • Where the customer has not lodged a claim and has only lodged the completed FA081, the back page of the FA081 or alternative proof of birth, a claim for payment will need to be completed before the customer can be assessed

Timeframes for return of documents/information

Customers are to provide:

  • Requested documents/information within 14 days (21 days for remote area or overseas customer)
  • Tax File Numbers (TFNs) and/or bank account details within 28 days

Hold the claim for a minimum of 22 days to allow extra time for mail delivery and for customers to respond.

7

Child of multiple birth + Read more ...

DAP can only be paid for 1 child from a multiple birth.

Where the customer has already received DAP for 1 of the children from the multiple birth, the DAP claim for the subsequent child in most cases, should be rejected.

If the child is not from a multiple birth, see Step 1 in Table 2.

In the rare event there is a 'significant delay' between the births of the children, it may be possible for DAP to be paid for more than 1 child. Refer these cases to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk with all relevant evidence for investigation.

Note: Multiple births being treated as separate birthing events does not apply to FTB and does not impact on the rates of Newborn Supplement (NBS), Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) and Multiple Birth Allowance (MBA).

Was there a significant delay between the birth of the first and subsequent child/ren from the same pregnancy?

  • Yes, customers must provide additional evidence to support their claim before the subsequent child can be assessed. A letter from the hospital or midwife where the children were delivered confirming the children were born during separate birthing events and not as part of a multiple birth. Go to Step 8
  • No, DAP can only be paid to 1 child of the multiple birth. If the customer has already received DAP for a child from the same multiple birth, any subsequent claims for other children must be rejected.
    • Go to the P1CS (PPL Claim Summary) screen
    • Select claim
    • Select A (PPL Claim Action)
    • On P1CA screen select:
      • Action: Rejection
      • Reason: Multiple Children Reject
    • Claim outcome will show as REJ/MCR (Multiple Children Reject)
    • Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

Post birth claims (no pre-birth claim lodged) + Read more ...

Check/update child details

  • 'S'elect child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen and press [Enter] to go to the Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen
  • On the CHTS screen the following screens will pre-select:
    • Change child (CCH) screen. Ensure child's name and date of birth are recorded correctly, and the Date of birth verified and Birth Registration fields are correctly completed. Note: where the FA081/FA008 is provided as proof of birth, ensure the Serial Number field is also completed
    • Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM) screen. Ensure the correct relationship to child is entered. Note: do not code the CHM screen if proof of adoption is not provided
    • Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen. Ensure a line exists for the service reason DAP and that the date of event is correct, that is the date of claim

Linking a child with a Centrelink record

The below coding is required when DAP partner is not claiming PPL and/or FTB.

Before linking, check whether the child has an existing record.

  • To use a different DOR other than the current date, start the activity via the FAO Task Selector (FTS) screen, code the Source as 'FA081' and the appropriate DOR
  • From the Paid Parental Leave Claim Summary (P1CS) screen, key 'C' next to the relevant DAP claim and press Continue
  • A FTB reassessment activity will be created on the Activity List (AL) screen. Pick up the activity, then go to the Link Child (LCH) screen to search for the child's record on National Index. Key child's name, sex and date of birth, press [Enter]. If the customer is FTB/CUR and the child needs to be added to the customers FTB record, see Reassessment of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for a current customer where an additional child enters care in the current or previous financial year

Note: if the customer is not claiming FTB for the same child do not code the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen for a DAP. If the workflow has incorrectly updated the CHC screen as part of the new claim, delete the ICC (in customer care) line. In situations where the DAP claim has not auto finalised with their partner's post birth claim, follow the manual coding below.

Linking child to pre-birth DAP claim:

To link the child to the pre-birth DAP claim, complete a reassessment activity:

  • to use a different DOR other than the current date, start the activity via the FAO Task Selector (FTS) screen, code the Source as 'FA081' and the appropriate DOR (do not enter anything in the Activity code field)
  • from the Paid Parental Leave Claim Summary (P1CS) screen, key 'C' next to the relevant DAP claim and press Continue
  • a FTB reassessment activity will be created on the Activity List (AL) screen. Pick the activity up, then go to the Link Child (LCH) screen to search for the child's record on National Index
  • key child's name, sex and date of birth, press [Enter]

Child record:

  • Found, 'S'elect child from the Name List (IL) screen and confirm selection on the Link Confirmation (LC) screen. The Child Selection (CHS) screen will now show with the child name and date of birth
  • Not found:
    • Only add a child to the system after a thorough search has been completed to minimise any risk of duplicating records
    • To add a new child key 'ACHI' in Nxt: field and press [Enter] to go to the Add Child (ACH) screen
    • Key child's name, sex and date of birth
    • Key 'Y' or 'N' in Proof of Birth: field
    • Key 'Y' in the Add Child?: field
    • If adding another child key 'Y' in the Add Another Child?: field
    • Press [Enter]. Add further children if required
    • The CHS screen will now show with the child's name, date of birth

Check/update child details

  • 'S'elect child from the CHS screen and press [Enter] to go to the Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen
  • On the CHTS screen the following screens will be pre-selected:
    • Change child (CCH) screen. Ensure child's name and date of birth are recorded correctly, and the Date of birth verified and Birth Registration fields are correctly completed. Note: where the FA081/FA008 is provided as proof of birth, ensure the Serial Number field is also completed
    • Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM) screen. Ensure the correct relationship to child is entered. Note: do not code the CHM screen if proof of adoption is not provided
    • Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen. Ensure a line exists for the service reason DAP and that the date of event is correct, that is the date of claim

Note: do not code the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen for a DAP customer who is not claiming FTB for the same child. If the workflow has incorrectly updated the CHC screen as part of the new claim, delete the ICC (in customer care) line. The incorrect coding of this screen can affect Child Support payments and FTB.

The child's Customer Reference Number (CRN) will need to be entered into the DAP pre-claim on the Families Early Claim Summary (FECS) screen.

Check the Assessment Results (AR) screen is showing the expected results before finalising the claim.

Error E340CU - DAP claimant advised marital status change

If the DAP customer has advised a change of marital status in the claim the Accommodation Details (AC) screen may require coding. If the AC screen presents with error E340CU; ACR details do not match the customer's relationship status, the following workaround is to be applied.

Current on payment when marital status changed

If the customer or partner were receiving income support payment or FTB on the date the marital status changed, action the marital status and AC coding using the relevant income support payment or FTB coding rules.

To action the change, contact the customer to confirm the details that applied on the date of marital status change. For example, accommodation details, partner FTB income estimate.

If on the date of separation the customer was receiving:

  • income support payment, see Change in relationship status from partnered to single
  • FTB (no income support payment), see Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and/or Child Care Subsidy (CCS) customer separates

If on the date the customer became partnered the customer or partner was receiving:

  • ISP, see Change in relationship status from single to partnered
  • FTB (no ISP, see Family assistance customer becomes partnered

For additional information on coding the AC screen, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA).

Not in receipt of payment when marital status changed

If the customer or partner were not current on a payment on the date the marital status changed, see DAP ACS coding on the Resources page.

Error E704P1 Income for Financial Year ending XXXX appears when the:

  • customer has not provided DAP income details for the relevant financial year, or
  • information has failed to be loaded onto the customer record from the Online Service

Income details need to be collected for the relevant financial year. This is the financial year prior to:

  • the date of claim (for pre-birth claims), or
  • the date of claim/nominated start date, whichever is earlier (for post birth claims)

The claim cannot be assessed until the correct income year has been recorded. Once received, the claim will need to be re-indexed using the ACC to enter the correct financial year income details.

For post-birth/entry to care claims, if eligibility and payability criteria is met, the claim status will finalise as one of the following:

  • DAP/ASS-ASD (awaiting nominated start date)
  • DAP/PAY

For information on coding NEF claims, how to re index previously rejected and manually coding rejections, see Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Dad and Partner Pay (DAP).

Use the Fast Note to record the claim outcome - auto text Families > Claims and select the relevant claim outcome option.

Finalising a DAP claim when customer and/or partner is in receipt of income support payment + Read more ...
