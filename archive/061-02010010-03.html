3

Recording of higher Working Life Residence (WLR) + Read more ...

Customer is entitled to use the higher of their or their former partner's WLR. While the customers are linked, the system will assess the higher WLR but once separated, the WLR needs to be manually recorded to allow correct assessment.

Check the Additional Residence Details (ARD) screen for both the customer and former partner. If the customer has less than their former partner's WLR, they can continue to use their former partner's higher WLR. For example, if the former partner has 120 months WLR and the customer has 82 months WLR, update the customer's record by recording 120 months in the Aust Working Life Residence: field. Note: if both have WLR of more than the maximum, updating the WLR is not necessary.

See the References page for links to the legislation allowing this to occur.

4

Finalise activity + Read more ...

Go to the AR screen to check the effect on the payment. Do not finalise the activity.

If the customer is in receipt of Carer Payment (CP), entitlement will cease from the date of separation if customer is paid under an agreement and granted due to caring for the partner. Note: CP is only payable long term outside Australia under an agreement.

Will the customer or former partner's payment/s cease for a reason other than Whereabouts Unknown (WUK), income or assets?

Customer or former partner's payment will cease + Read more ...

If the customer has become single and lost qualification for CP, check that they do not qualify for another payment type. In this case, they may be able to transfer to an Age Pension without the need for a claim. If a person may qualify for DSP, a claim will have to be lodged to determine qualification prior to grant. Note: do not suggest that the customer claim DSP (which is generally the only other payment available under agreements to a person under Age pension age) unless the customer clearly indicates that they are unable to work or look after themselves financially due to illness.

For more information, go to International Social Security Agreements

2

Dependent child has entered care + Read more ...

Assess whether a child amount can be made.

If a customer is receiving payment under an international social security agreement, they may be entitled to be paid an additional child amount with their agreement pension.

If the customer's relevant agreement includes additional child amounts, the dependent child needs to be added as a dependent. The system will pay the amount with the pension where appropriate.

If the customer is still a resident of Australia and is only temporarily overseas, they may be entitled to a family assistance or Paid Parental Leave scheme payment and a claim will need to be lodged. See:

  • Residence assessment for customers claiming Family Tax Benefit (FTB), Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • Residence assessment for customers claiming Child Care Subsidy (CCS)

Is the customer likely to be entitled to a payment for a dependent child?

  • Yes, and:
    • Customer may be entitled to additional child amount, go to Step 3
    • Customer may be entitled to family assistance or parental leave payment, encourage the customer to complete an online claim for family assistance and PPL/DAP or issue a claim form to the customer. Go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 7

3

Additional child amount + Read more ...

The child must be:

  • under 16 years of age, and
  • wholly or substantially in the care of the customer

Also, there are child residence requirements for the payment of additional child payment.

If the recipient is an Australian resident, the child must be either:

  • an Australian resident, or
  • be living with the adult

If the recipient is not an Australian resident, then the child must:

  • be an Australian resident, or
  • have been an Australian resident, and is living with the recipient outside Australia, or
  • have been living with the adult in Australia, and is living with the recipient outside Australia

Is the child a dependent child of the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 7

4

Add dependent child + Read more ...

    • Code the child's date of birth in the Date of Birth: field. Press [Enter]

  • Details will default in the ACHI screen from the LCH screen
    • If anything is incorrect or missing, update accordingly
    • Code Proof of Birth: field as appropriate. Note: payment will not occur if this is 'N'
    • Ensure Add Child: field is 'Y'

Update dependent child + Read more ...

Dependent child has left care + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving either FTB or additional child payment?

  • Yes, cancel child payment. 'S'elect the child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen. On the Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen, select relevant screens below, including Care Align Details option. Complete updates in the following order:
    • Update the FTB Shared Care (FSC) screen for family assistance: update existing shared care percentage to nil from date left care. Assessment method will be 'PRI' or 'CPP' depending on whether there is a court order or parenting plan
    • On the Shared Care (SSC) screen for children assessed for income support payment purposes under the Social Security Act, update existing shared care percentage to nil from the date left care
    • On the In Customer Care (CHC) screen, code 'LCC' (left customer care) in Care Status: field from the date the child left the customer's care. If there is more than one carer or if in child's customer record, code the carer number for the 'losing' customer in the Carer: field
    • In Customer First update the Care Alignment Details page for the child with a 'Finalised' status

  • No, go to Step 7

Finalise actions + Read more ...

To check if the customer and/or former partner's income or assets have changed on separation, issue an AUS220 - Income & Assets Review. Note: where appropriate, issue the relevant language version. Record a review for 42 days with keyword AUS220.

Separated due to ill health

Table 4: This table describes how to update a customer's record if they are a member of a couple but should be paid the single rate of pension while still retaining the member of a couple income and assets assessment.

Step

Action

1

Customer has indicated that ill health is the reason for separation + Read more ...

Customer is still considered to be a member of a couple but is paid the single rate of pension while still retaining the member of a couple level of permissible and disqualifying income and assets. See Assessment of circumstances for a couple separated due to illness.

Record illness separated status:

  • Go to the Marital Status (MS) screen
    • Record the date the person became separated in the Event Date field. Note: if some details are unknown, use the standard temporary information pending return of the correct details
    • Code 'MAR', 'REG' or 'DEF' in the Marital Status field according to the customer's status (generally the same as currently recorded)
    • Record whether the partner and customer are permitted to enquire in the PPE: fields (generally the same as currently recorded)
    • If the customer has left the home, select Ill Sep/Left Home in the Qualifier Code: field
    • If the partner has left the home, select Ill Sep/Ptnr Left Home in the Qualifier Code: field
    • If both have left the home, select Ill Sep/Both Left Home in the Qualifier Code: field
  • Code the Source: and Date of receipt: fields
  • Select Continue and the Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen will display with Address and Accommodation preselected. Select any other screen from CDTS if required
  • Press [Enter] and the Address Summary (ADS) screen will display. Select the most recent address and press [Enter] and the Address Details (AD) screen will display. Update information for the customer, partner or both according to whom has changed their address. Ensure the date of illness separation is recorded in the Address Start Date: field. Unless both are moving to the same new address, ensure that the Make Partner's address the same as the customer's?: field has No selected. Press [Enter] again

2

Finalise details for an illness separated couple + Read more ...

  • Update Telephone Details (TD) screen if new details have been provided
  • The Accommodation History (ACS) screen will display. Select the Event Date for the change in relationship status and press [Enter]
  • Update any changes in the accommodation for either the customer or the partner.
  • If the customer and partner own their own home, then the person entering care is still considered to be a homeowner. Record 'INH' in the Reason Home Vacated: field and the date of illness separation in the Date Home Vacated: field
  • For more information regarding the assessment of the home, see Vacation of principal home due to illness. Note: while customers outside Australia can have the home exempt for 2 years, they do not qualify for many of the exemptions such as rent assistance or accommodation bonds (unless they relate to care being provided inside Australia). This is because the terms relate to the Age Care Act 1997 which does not apply to nursing homes and similar outside Australia
  • Go to the AR screen and finalise the activity. Do not inhibit the advice
  • Ensure all details have been documented
  • If any details are unknown, issue a Separation Details - Outside Australia (AUS184) form
  • If the customer's income or assets have changed on separation, issue an AUS220 - Income & Assets Review. Note: where appropriate, issue the relevant language version. Record a review for 42 days with keyword AUS220
