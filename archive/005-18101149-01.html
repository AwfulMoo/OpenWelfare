Negotiating Job Plans for newly arrived refugees following the 'Refugee in First 13 Weeks' (REF) exemption 005-18101149

Expand all


This document outlines the negotiation of a Job Plan by a Service Officer for newly arrived refugees with mutual obligation requirements following the end of the 'Refugee in First 13 Weeks' (REF) exemption ending.

Newly arrived refugees

Newly arrived refugees who are in receipt of an income support payment with mutual obligation requirements (for example, JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (other), Parenting Payment Single (with mutual obligation requirements)) Receive the 'Refugee in first 13 Weeks' (REF) mutual obligation requirement exemption for the first 13 weeks from their arrival in Australia. The exemption period is adjusted to 11 weeks and 6 days at the new claim. See Refugee and humanitarian entrant service offer for more information on the changing of the exemption date.

Newly arrived refugees who are eligible for the Better Targeting of Support for Refugees measure remain Centrelink managed for an additional period after their refugee exemption ends.

A Service Officer in the refugee's local service centre is responsible for creating a Job Plan for them at their week 12 post exemption review contact.

Note: as per normal processing, a Job Plan cannot be negotiated for a customer that has a current exemption from their mutual obligation requirements.

Determining eligibility to remain Centrelink managed and the duration of the Job Plan

The Participation Summary page will advise if the refugee is eligible for the Better Targeting of Support for Refugees measure. There are a number of factors that determine this eligibility. See Refugee and humanitarian entrant service offer for more information.

Better Targeting of Support for Refugee measure

Customers eligible for the Better Targeting of Support for Refugees measure remain Centrelink managed until they have been in Australia for 12 months. Their Job Plan needs to last until they have been in Australia for 12 months.

Determining suitable requirements

Refugee Activities

A Centrelink managed newly arrived refugee can fully meet their mutual obligation requirements if they satisfy one of the following:

  • participating in the Humanitarian Settlement Program (HSP)
  • undertaking the Adult Migrant English Program (AMEP)
  • participating in Workforce Australia as a volunteer

Activity referrals by the Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) Assessor

Newly arrived refugees will have an ESAt conducted prior to the end of their REF exemption.

The Assessor can make referrals for the customer but will not complete the Job Plan with the customer. Requirements that the customer have been referred to will be identified in the ESAt report. These requirements can be recorded on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen on the customer's record by the customer's local service centre.

Other Activities

Newly arrived refugees also have the option to participate in any other approved activity to fully meet their requirements. For example:

  • participation in Skills for Education and Employment (SEE), where eligible, or
  • if they are meeting suitable part-time work/voluntary work requirements and are:
    • a principal carer,
    • have been assessed as having a partial capacity to work, or
    • are 60 or over

Negotiating the Job Plan

Whenever possible, newly arrived refugees need to have Refugee Activities added to their record. The system will determine if a new Job Plan needs to be negotiated.

Refugee Activities needs to be added to the customer's record as the compulsory requirement when the customer advises they are participating in:

  • the Humanitarian Settlement Program (HSP)
  • Adult Migrant English Program (AMEP) and/or
  • Workforce Australia

Service Officers must ensure that the start and end dates cover the full Centrelink managed period (for example, until the refugee has been in Australia for 12 months). See Refugee and humanitarian entrant service offer for more information on how long the refugee will remain Centrelink managed.

Note: the refugee does not need to provide evidence that they are participating in these activities.

Ensure Refugee Activities (RAC) is recorded as the compulsory requirement on the customer's record on the Activities and Exemptions page. Once Refugee Activities (RAC) is coded on the AEX screen, the Job Plan will auto populate with the following text 'I will meet my agreed mutual obligation by undertaking approved activities and report my participation in them to Centrelink'. To complete the Job Plan see Step 4 in Table 3 of Negotiating Centrelink Managed Job Plans.

If the customer is not participating in HSP, AMEP or Workforce Australia, discuss a possible referral to one of these. If the customer requests a referral, action referral and ensure that Refugee Activities is added to the customer's record.

If the customer declines a referral to HSP, AMEP or Workforce Australia then another appropriate compulsory requirement will need to be recorded on the AEX screen (for example the requirement referred by their ESAt assessor, Skills for Education and Employment, Part-time work or Voluntary work).

As per normal processing, Service Officers will need to consider the work capacity of the job seeker when negotiating a Job Plan. This is contained in the ESAt report. The system will determine the most appropriate Job Plan Item to be added to the customer's Job Plan.

Quarterly Participation Interviews (QTIs)

Customers who are eligible for QTIs are not eligible for the Better Targeting of Support for Refugees measures. Service Officers must review customer's Activities and Exemptions details in line with QTI arrangements. See Quarterly Participation Interviews for Partial Capacity to work and Temporary Reduced Work Capacity (0-7 or 8-14 hours per week) job seekers, for more information.

TtW, CDP and DES

Refugees who require a referral to Workforce Australia - Transition to Work (TtW), Community Development Program (CDP) or Disability Employment Services (DES) are not eligible for the Better Targeting of Support for Refugee measures. Service Officers must refer refugees to TtW/CDP/DES at their Week 12 post exemption review contact. Service officers do not need to create a Job Plan for refugees who are referred to TtW, CDP or DES.

The Participation Summary page will display an action item if the customer required a referral to TtW, CDP or DES.

See, Refugee humanitarian and protection visa holder service offer for more information on newly arrived refugees eligibility to remain Centrelink Managed.
