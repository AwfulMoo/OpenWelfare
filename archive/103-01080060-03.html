Exemption from Income Management for SpB

Process for exemption from Income Management for certain customers receiving SpB

This table outlines the process for determining an exemption from Income Management for certain customers receiving Special Benefit (SpB). Portions of this process are for the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT) only.

Step

Action

1

Customer on SpB applies for an exemption + Read more ...

Customers receiving SpB are exempt from Income Management if both the following are met:

  • they are not a principal carer of a child under 6 years of age, and
  • are aged 16 years or older

Tell the customer if they wish to test their eligibility, they can be transferred to the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT) to have an interview over the phone. The Resources page contains a link to IMEAT in Locator.

Has the customer requested IMEAT contact them or the transfer to IMEAT was unsuccessful?

  • Yes, send a CALLDOC with the keyword IMEAT to Region Code 'EAT' with details of the request. Ensure the customer's contact details are correct. Procedure ends here
  • No, transfer to IMEAT was successful or customer did not wish to test their eligibility for an exemption, procedure ends here

IMEAT, go to Step 2.

2

Compulsory Income Management exemption workflow + Read more ...

For the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team only

Launch the Compulsory IM exemption workflow.

The IM Exemption Application screen displays.

  • Select today's date under the Exemption Summary History section. This will pre-populate the Exemption Application Details section
  • The Type of Exemption Application: field will default to a value based on the customer's record. If the customer is in receipt of SpB this field will default to Specified Class
  • Enter Source and Date of Receipt
  • Select Continue

3

The Supporting Evidence screen displays + Read more ...

For the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team only

  • Select the relevant supporting evidence for the customer from the Document Type: dropdown list
  • Indicate whether the evidence is Acceptable or Not acceptable
  • Select the Add button to add the supporting evidence
  • The Support Evidence Data for Customer section will populate with the new evidence
  • Select Continue

4

IM Exemption Determination screen + Read more ...

The IM Exemption Determination screen displays a summary of information recorded during the workflow.

For the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team only

Grant, Reject or Incomplete radio button are displayed.

  • Select exemption application as Incomplete, if further evidence is required
  • If all evidence has been provided, Grant or Reject the exemption application
  • Granting of an exemption will display the exemption Start and End dates. The Start date is today's date and the default End date is the date 12 months from the start date
  • If rejecting, select a rejection reason. (No reason is required if granting)
  • Select Continue

Is an exemption being granted?

  • Yes, and the customer is present, go to Step 5
  • Yes, and the customer is not present, go to Step 6
  • No, go to step 6

5

Voluntary Income Management Service Offer + Read more ...

For the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team only

When a customer is exiting compulsory Income Management, explain they can continue to have their payments income managed through Voluntary Income Management.

Record the outcome on the Service Offer screen.

6

Finalise exemption activity + Read more ...

For the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team only

The Manage Expenses screen appears.

Note: if the customer is not transferring to another measure of Income Management, all Income Management expenses should be ended before finalising the activity.

The Income Management Summary screen will display to finalise the activity.

  • Select the Finalise radio button
  • Select Continue

Is the exemption application complete?

  • Yes, a letter is issued to the customer advising the outcome. Record details on a DOC
    • If the customer is present, go to Step 7
    • If the customer is not present, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Finalise interview + Read more ...

When a customer has been contacted to advise their exemption has been granted, further discussions will be required depending on the outcome of the Voluntary service offer.

  • For customers exiting Income Management, offer a Disbursement interview
    • If the customer declines, procedure ends here
    • If the customer accepts the offer, see Disbursement interview for Disengaged Youth and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient Income Management. Procedure ends
  • For customers who want to volunteer for Income Management, see Initial Assessment interview for Voluntary Income Management (VIM). Advise them, if during a period of Voluntary Income Management their exemption ends (for example, they lose qualification for SpB), they will revert to the relevant compulsory measure. Procedure ends here

8

Incomplete Exemption application + Read more ...

For the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team only

An incomplete exemption application that is 28 days old will generate a Work Item for IMEAT to complete the application.

There will be no Issue created in the Centrelink system.

If further evidence/details to an incomplete exemption application are provided, click the Compulsory IM Exemption workflow and select the incomplete exemption application.

Record further evidence/details as required and Reject or Grant the exemption application.
