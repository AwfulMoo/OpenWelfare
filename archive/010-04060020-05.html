Approved free text - Independent - Not Eligible

Table 5: This table provides text for use in an Online Advice (OLA) when requesting evidence for external students undertaking a compulsory component of their course at their institution. Note: each free text paragraph can have 9 lines, has a limit of 74 characters per line and does not line wrap.

Paragraph

Text

Single independent students

Single independent students without dependent children are

generally not eligible for Fares Allowance, except for external

students undertaking a compulsory on campus component of

their course. This is because your permanent home is considered

to be wherever you are living at the time. Other than for short

periods, you cannot be considered to be living away from your

permanent home.

Independent due to age

The qualification criteria for Fares Allowance are contained in

Section 1061ZAAB SSA 1991. One of the requirements is

(subsection 1061ZAAA(1) Subsection (4), which requires the

customer not to be independent when the journey was

made. You were independent due to age from XX/XX/XXXX.

The 'relevant period', according to SSA 1991 is 'the whole or a

part of a study year the person undertakes an approved

tertiary course'. You were independent for the whole of the

academic year for 20XX.

Therefore, you are not eligible for Fares Allowance.

Examples

Examples of assessing Fares Allowance (FAA) eligibility

Table 11: This table contains examples on how to assess eligibility for FAA.

Example

Description

Online student

Aaron is receiving Austudy and is an online student at the University of Queensland in Brisbane.

He lives with his partner in Melbourne. However, he has to attend the University in Brisbane for a compulsory residential unit.

Aaron should submit a claim for Fares Allowance, with evidence of the residential component requirement. Aaron can either submit a claim asking Services Australia to pre-book his travel arrangements or he can submit a claim to have his travel expenses reimbursed after he has travelled.

Dependent student using private transport due to excess luggage

Maikeli is about to move from Dubbo to Sydney to commence the first year of university. Maikeli needs to take 3 large suitcases, bedding, kitchenware and study materials. There is a bus route between Dubbo and Sydney, however the bus company restricts luggage to 1 large suitcase per passenger.

It is not practicable for Maikeli to travel via bus on this occasion, and Maikeli will be entitled to the private vehicle rate of FAA if he decides to travel from Dubbo to Sydney using his car.

Dependent student, living away from home

Sonya is studying at Charles Sturt University in Bathurst, NSW. She is receiving Youth Allowance as a dependent student at the away from home rate because her parent's home is in Townsville, Queensland.

Sonya paid for an economy flight home to see her parents at the end of the study year and had to take a bus to the airport.

Sonya should submit a claim for Fares Allowance for both the cost of the flight and the bus ticket. If Sonya chose to travel by private vehicle instead of the bus, she would only be paid the amount of a bus fare as this is the cheapest form of public transport available.

Combined pre-booked flights and reimbursement of ancillary costs after journey made

Elizabeth is receiving Youth Allowance as a dependent student and lives in Sydney to attend university. Her permanent home is in Perth.

Elizabeth wants to travel home to see her parents at the end-of-year break, returning to university in the new year. Elizabeth can ask Services Australia to book and pay for the most practical mode of transport to travel, for example, return flights from Sydney to Perth.

Elizabeth catches the local bus to and from the airport, which cannot be pre-booked. She will need to submit her bus travel expenses for reimbursement after she has travelled.

1st year Uni student

Kim has completed year 12 in 2021 and will commence a 3 year bachelor of education at Gold Coast University in 2022.

Kim needs to travel from her home in Victoria to the Gold Coast to commence studies in February 2022.

Kim’s Youth Allowance is granted from 1 January 2022 as she is deemed a continuing student due to completing year 12 in 2021.

Kim is eligible for travel from her family home to the university at the start of the study year and return back to her family home at the end of the study year.

Kim will also qualify for a mid year return trip back to the family home once she has been receipt of Youth Allowance for 3 months.

Mid year semester start

Jodie is granted Youth Allowance from 17 July 2022 as she has been accepted in the midyear intake at Uni to commence her nursing degree.

Jodie is entitled to the following travel while studying:

  • In the first year of study (2022):
    • travel from the family home to the Uni to commence studies and return at the end of the study year
    • 1 mid year return trip to the family home once she has been in receipt of Youth Allowance for 3 months
  • subsequent years of study:
    • travel from the family home to the Uni to commence studies and return to the family home at the end of the study year
    • 1 mid year travel from the Uni to the family home and return to the Uni once she has been receiving Youth Allowance for 3 months. As Jodie is a continuing student, the mid year trip can be completed on or after 1 April 2023 as she has been receiving Youth Allowance for 3 months from 1 January
