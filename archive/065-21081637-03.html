5

Assisting the customer with registering online for My Aged Care + Read more ...

To register for My Aged Care advise the customer to visit the My Aged Care website and select the ‘apply for assessment’. See Resources for a link to My Aged Care.

The online application is divided into 3 parts:

  • Eligibility
  • Providing details
  • Arranging assessment

Part A requires details about the customer’s current situation and needs. This helps determine their eligibility for aged care services.

Part B requires the customer’s personal and Medicare card details.

Part C is where an ACAT assessment can be arranged. An assessor will visit the customer in their home to seek further information so they can decide the best aged care option for the customer. An assessor will contact the customer or nominated contact within 3 weeks of submitting their online form to arrange the face-to-face appointment in the home.

Customers can track their application by logging into their My Aged Care online account or calling My Aged Care.

6

My Aged Care online accounts + Read more ...

The My Aged Care online account houses important information about a customer’s assessments, services and interactions.

Customers can do the following in their online account:

  • view and update personal details
  • view and update relationships, including representatives and primary contact person
  • view current support plans
  • view current care approvals
  • view the aged care services they are currently receiving
  • view interactions and communications with My Aged Care
  • view and upload documents
  • view and print information from their record
  • ask for a new support relationship with a person
  • accept or decline any pending request
  • nominate or change a main contact
  • update their email and SMS preferences

Potential Representatives, Representatives and Agents can do the following in their online account:

  • ask for a new support relationship with a person
  • accept or decline any pending request
  • end any pending or active regular representative or agent relationships (excludes authorised relationships)
  • update their email and SMS preferences

Representatives and Agents can use the customer’s online account where they can view and update details depending on their role.

The ‘Relationships’ tile in the online account is called ‘Support Networks’.

The ’Representatives’ page on the MAC website is called ‘My Aged Care Support Roles’.

Does the customer require assistance with navigating their My Aged Care online account?

  • Yes, see how to use your online account link on the Resources page
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Document lodgement + Read more ...

The customer’s ‘Document’ page in their My Aged Care online account allows them to upload important documents to send to My Aged Care. This includes legal and medical documents, or a completed form to appoint a representative.

Customers can also see the documents they have previously uploaded, and whether they have been processed by My Aged Care.

Does the customer need assistance with document lodgement through their My Aged Care online account?

  • Yes, see help with documents on the Resources page
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Searching for aged care service providers + Read more ...

Customers can search for aged care service providers on the My Aged Care website by entering in their postcode and selecting ‘Search’. To define their requirements further they can select the care type required then ‘Search’.

Does the customer need assistance locating aged care service providers in their area?

  • Yes, see find a provider link on the Resources page
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Ending the call + Read more ...

At the end of the call update the customer’s record using Fast Note - select: Auto Text, use Aged Care > Enquiry > My Aged Care GSO interaction > Confirm

Warm transfer to My Aged Care

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Warm transfer to My Aged Care + Read more ...

A warm transfer to My Aged Care from Services Australia may be initiated when a customer:

  • declines My Aged Care digital service offer
  • requests to speak to My Aged Care
  • requires more assistance outside the MACGSO and there is not an ACSO available in their location
  • is unable to travel to a service centre for a face-to-face ACSO appointment

Does the customer meet one of the above criteria to be warm transferred to My Aged Care?

  • Yes,
    • Smart Centre staff, go to Step 3
    • Service centre staff, go to Step 2
  • No, do not warm transfer call

2

Service centre staff + Read more ...

Prior to warm transfer ask the customer for:

  • Aged Care ID (if already registered with My Aged Care)
  • Medicare card number

Depending on the customer’s needs initiate the warm transfer via:

  • self service phone
  • customer interview desk, or
  • customer’s mobile phone

See Aged care - External contacts for warm transfer numbers.

Go to Step 4.

3

Smart Centre Staff + Read more ...

Prior to warm transfer ask the customer for:

  • Aged Care ID (if already registered with My Aged Care)
  • Medicare card number

Place the customer on hold and initiate warm transfer via iWS.

Go to Step 4.

4

My Aged Care Contact Centre + Read more ...

Once connected to My Aged Care Contact Centre, Service Officers must introduce themselves and advise:

  • they are calling from Services Australia
  • the reason for the warm transfer
  • customers Aged Care ID (if already registered with My Aged Care), and
  • Medicare card number

Conference the customer into the call via a 3-way conversation and introduce the customer to the My Aged Care staff member. Service Officers should then leave the call.

5

Record the contact + Read more ...

At the end of the call update the customers record using Fast Note - select: Auto Text, use Aged Care > Enquiry > My Aged Care GSO interaction > Confirm
