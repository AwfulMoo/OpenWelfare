Request duplicate payment notice

Table 1: This table describes how the EBPRP Processing Team can order a duplicate payment advice for a claimant.

Step

Action

1

Obtain claimant's information + Read more ...

If a claimant contacts Services Australia advising they have not received their payment notice, a duplicate statement can be provided.

Conduct a security check and obtain the following information from the claimant:

  • Medicare number
  • Claimant details
  • Postal address
  • Preferred contact number
  • EBPRP claim number (if possible)

2

Email information to EBPRP Program Management + Read more ...

Email the above information to the EBPRP Program Management Team requesting a duplicate payment advice.

The EBPRP Program Management Team will print and send a duplicate payment notice directly to the claimant's postal address.

No further action is required by the EBPRP Processing Team.

Request for EFT trace

Table 2: This table describes how the EBPRP Processing Team handle requests from claimants about unpaid claims payment reimbursements.

Step

Action

1

Enquiry received by Service Officer + Read more ...

If a call is received from a claimant requesting information on their reimbursement, complete a security check and warm transfer the call to the EBPRP Processing Team.

2

EBPRP Processing Team + Read more ...

Ensure the transferring service officer has completed a security check.

Obtain claimant's details:

  • Medicare number
  • Name
  • Contact details
  • Claim ID - if claimant has received the payment advice but no payment in their bank account

3

Email Program Management + Read more ...

Send an email to the EBPRP Program Management Team requesting an EFT trace. The email must include:

  • Claimant's name
  • Medicare number
  • Address
  • Contact details
  • Date the payment was made to claimant's account
  • Claimant's bank account details
  • Date the claim was processed
  • Copy of the claim
  • Screen shot of the claimant's EBPRP screen

4

EFT enquiry outcome + Read more ...

If the claimant supplied incorrect details, the EBPRP Program Management Team will inform EBPRP Processing Team via email.

The agency is under no obligation to pay the claimant. The claimant will be responsible for contacting their own bank and requesting they commence a trace on the funds.

If the agency was at fault, the EBPRP Program Management team will inform the EBPRP Processing Team via email, and the claimant is to be repaid the amount outstanding.

The EBPRP Processing Team will contact the claimant via phone to advise that the claim will be paid into their nominated account.

Note: cash management - the agency is responsible for conducting the EFT trace when it is found that the agencywas at fault.

The EBPRP Program Management Team is to contact the Treasury team and request Accounts Receivable raise an invoice to the incorrectly paid claimant. This will begin the debt recovery process.

Neither Program Management nor Medicare Provider Branch will have any further interaction with the debt recovery process.

Rejected payments from RBA

Table 3: This table describes how Medicare Provider Branch manage rejected payments from the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA).

Step

Action

1

Rejected payment summary email + Read more ...

An email is generated from SAP and sent to the EBPRP Processing Team. The email is titled Common Payment System, CPS Payment Summary.

Access the Common Payment System (CPS) report. Open the PDF for the rejected payment in EBPRP_CPS.

2

Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) details + Read more ...

Using the identified claim ID details, check the details on the claim form match the information contained in CDMS.

If the details do not match:

  • Update the information in CDMS. The EBPRP claimant's claim screen will automatically update
  • If the details match, but there is still a problem with processing the payment, contact the claimant and verify their bank account details

3

Contacting the claimant to confirm details + Read more ...

  • Use identification processes to confirm the identity of the claimant or their legal representative
  • If a certified copy of a POA or other legal documentation is supplied with the claim, forward to the EBPRP Program Management Team
  • Update CDMS with the new information supplied by the claimant
  • Send an email to the Finance team informing that the claimant’s bank details have been updated. Request redirection of the payment. Include in the body of the email:
    • Consumer ID
    • Amount of payment
    • Claimant's name
  • CC the Team Coach and the EBPRP Program Management Team in the email

Finance will respond advising when the master data has been updated for the claimant and the payment will be processed in the next day’s payment run.

See the Resources page for contact details for the Finance team.
