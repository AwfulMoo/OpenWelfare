MCI link set up for receiving income details from the ATO

Table 1

Step

Action

1

TFN consent and data exchange with ATO + Read more ...

A customer's Tax File Number (TFN) and consent to use TFN is recorded for Family Tax Benefit (FTB), Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and/or for a Child Care Benefit (CCB) customer whose CCB for approved care is income tested.

MCI allows for the ATO to transfer actual income details electronically to Centrelink for FTB/CCB/CCS reconciliation.

2

ATO action match + Read more ...

The ATO will match the data provided by Centrelink against their database (using their existing matching criteria).

Data sent to the ATO includes:

  • all names (first name, current family name, maiden name and most recent alias)
  • address
  • date of birth
  • sex
  • TFN
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • client/customer type

Record matching occurs automatically and is linked to new claim and assessment processing tasks that involve:

  • TFN changes,
  • circumstance changes (for example when customer becomes partnered)
  • benefit cancellation/restoration

Note: the new claim or assessment activity needs to be finalised before the MCI link can be established and record matching can occur.

3

ATO will advise Centrelink of the result + Read more ...

Information about record matching is displayed on the following screens:

  • Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) screen. To access the MCI screen, go to the FAO Task Selector (FTS) screen or code 'MCI' in Nxt: field
  • Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) Progress Stage Screen (MCIPS) accessed by coding MCIPS in Nxt field. This screen displays the historic ATO information in relation to the progress of a match transaction for the customer. When a mismatch is received from the ATO it will invoke various processes. This screen will display the progress stage at each of the dates the progress occurred. For example, mismatch, letters, review and manual interventions
  • MCI Transaction History (MTX) screen, accessed by coding 'MTX' in Nxt: field or 's'elect the View transaction history line from the MCI screen

The result on the MCI screen will display the result of the match:

  • Match - a mutual customer has been identified. Go to Step 4
  • ATO pending - the ATO are still investigating. Go to Step 6
  • Mismatch - the Centrelink customer could not be identified by the ATO. A manual follow up activity will be created on the customer's record for a Service Officer to action. If the customer is contacting with a new TFN, TFN provisions for family assistance payments will need to be followed. Once customer is successfully matched, go to Step 4
  • Centrelink Pending - the Centrelink customer's TFN has already been matched with a different Customer Reference Number (CRN). A manual follow up activity (A016F1) with a keyword 'MCICPN' will be created on the customer's record for a Service Officer to action. See Processing Manual Follow-ups (MFU) for Families for instructions on actioning this MFU. Once customer is successfully matched, go to Step 4
  • Error - a manual follow up activity (A014F1) with keyword #MCIERR will be created on the customer's record. Do not action the MFU - refer to Table 2 on the Resources page for further information. Procedure ends here

4

Result is a match + Read more ...

When the ATO returns a Match to Centrelink the MCI screen will display a status of 'match' and there is no further action needed.

This means a mutual customer has been successfully identified and actual adjusted taxable income details will generally be automatically transmitted by the ATO.

Actual adjusted taxable income details will be automatically transferred to Centrelink from the ATO for mutual customers to allow reconciliation of FTB/CCS and CCB and for lump sum FTB/CCB claims to be finalised.

The MCI screen displays whether or not the link with the ATO is open for a financial year. Income details for a financial year will only be automatically transmitted if the link is open and if the customer claimed or received FTB/CCS or CCB for the relevant year.

If the:

  • link with the ATO has closed for the relevant financial year, go to Step 7
  • link for the relevant financial year is open and incorrect income details have been reported by the ATO for the financial year, see Step 1 in the Wrong income details received from the ATO table
  • ATO link is open, the TFN is correct, and MCI status is 'Match', go to Step 5

5

ATO link opened but no income details received + Read more ...

ATO link is open for the relevant financial year but actual income details have not transferred to the customer's record.

Check the Actual Income Required? field on the MCI screen:

  • If it is 'Yes', action an MCI refresh activity. Procedure ends here
  • If it displays 'No' for the person:
    • Check if a NCL/LSC is still being processed. If so, the Actual Income Required? field will stay at 'No' until the claim is finalised. Once the claim is finalised the field will automatically change to 'Yes' and the link will open with the ATO. Procedure ends here
    • Check that the TFNs for the customer and their partner have been recorded correctly as per Requesting a Tax File Number (TFN). MCI requires that the service reasons 'FTB', 'PTR' or 'PAR' have the Auth field as 'Y' for the entire financial year. The TFN Exemption field must be blank. If TFNs have not been recorded correctly - update the previous pages using 'C'orrect in the Action: field. Action an MCI refresh activity. Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here. If TFNs have been recorded correctly - request actual income details on the FAO Request Actual Income (FRAI) screen. Procedure ends here

6

Result is ATO pending + Read more ...

Where further ATO action is required (for example, the customer has more than one TFN), the MCI screen will display a status of 'ATO pending'. There is no further action required by Centrelink for these cases. ATO will update status to a match or mismatch after their investigation is completed.

This could take up to six months (ATO processing).

Advise the customer:

Advise the customer and/or their partner with ATO pending status that generally the reconciliation will not be completed on their record. This is because Centrelink have not received actual income details from the ATO. If the customer contacts because they are expecting a 'top up' payment they should be advised to contact the ATO about the ATO TFN status. The customer can visit the ATO website for more information.

Procedure ends here.

7

Link with ATO closed actual income assessed/amended by ATO + Read more ...

If the ATO amends the customer's taxable income or undertakes an income assessment after the link has closed, the new actual income details cannot be automatically transferred to Centrelink.

Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Families > Enquiries > ATO income details required. Confirm the following with the customer:

  • Whose income details are required (customer and/or partner)
  • The financial year for which income details are required
  • Customer/partner NOA date of issue
  • Customer/partner NOA Reference Number
  • If the TFN has been confirmed or corrected. Note: do not record the TFN on a DOC

The customer should also be asked to confirm income not reported to the ATO, including non-taxable pensions or benefits, tax exempt foreign income and deductible child maintenance expenditure.

If the customer is ‘at risk’ or experiencing hardship, the URGENT keyword should be added.

Note: the Fast Note will automatically add the keyword ACTTAP and will transfer to the ATO Liaison Team (TAP) for action.

The ATO Liaison team will:

  • confirm income details with the ATO and undertake action to record income details and,
  • if the customer has a reconciliation debt outstanding for a financial year for which the MCI link has been ended will refer the debt for temporary write off action

Enquiries relating to processing times should not be made until at least 14 days after providing the information. ACTDOCS and CALLDOCS are not actioned by the ATO Liaison Processing team unless the referral time is outside 14 days.

Procedure ends here.

Wrong income details received from the ATO

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Incorrect actual income details transferred + Read more ...

If an incorrect TFN is recorded for a customer or their partner, the MCI process may result in a match and transfer of actual income details for another person.

Check that the customer has their Notice of Assessment (NOA) for the relevant financial year. As the NOA does not list all ATO income components a check of recorded income should be undertaken.

Do income components and income amounts reported by the customer match information recorded on the FAO Income for Previous Year (FIPY) screen?

  • Yes, correct income details have been transferred. Procedure ends here
  • No, ask the customer to restate their TFN and re-key the TFN on the Tax File Number (TFN) screen. Go to Step 2

2

TFN provided + Read more ...

Is the TFN the same as previously entered?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, updating the TFN will start the MCI process. When the mutual customer is identified, correct income details will be transferred by the ATO to automatically reassess the customer's entitlement. Procedure ends here

Note: there is no need to refresh the record on the MCI screen.

3

TFN is the same as that previously entered + Read more ...

Advise the customer that a specialist team will consult directly with the ATO to confirm the correct income components and income amounts to apply. If the customer wishes to enquire about their NOA, TFN or assessment of taxable income refer them to the ATO website.

Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Families > Enquiries > ATO income does not match and complete the required fields to send a request to the ATO Liaison Team (TAP) for action.

Note: the Fast Note automatically adds the keyword ACTTAP.

The ATO Liaison team will consult with the ATO to ascertain why the income listed on the NOA does not match the income loaded onto the FIPY screen. This may result in the ATO transmitting new income details, or manual record correction occurring.

Procedure ends here.
