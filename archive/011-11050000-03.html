General processing rules

Table 1

Item

Rule/s

1

Initial checks + Read more ...

  • Before creating a new organ donor registration in CDMS, Search for existing organ donor registration or entitlements must be completed. This is to avoid duplication
  • AODR registration forms not signed are to be returned with the appropriate letter
  • AODR registration forms for persons under the age of 16 are to be returned with the appropriate letter. No details are to be updated or comments recorded in CDMS
  • All organ donor registration enquiries must pass a security check before registration and donation decisions are confirmed or accessed
  • Once a date of contact and or decision date has been updated in an AODR registration, it cannot be reversed or backdated

2

Date of Lodgement + Read more ...

The Date of Lodgement (DOL) is the date the donor last contacted to register their donation decision.

The Date of Lodgement is updated with the date when:

  • the registrant visited a Medicare Service Centre - face to face (without a form)
  • the registrant phoned the Donor Register
  • a donor registration form or letter is received

For form processing, update the Date of Lodgement to the date the form was received by Services Australia (PaNDA DOL).

3

Date of Last Contact and signature date discrepancy

+ Read more ...

This is when the donor last contacted Services Australia to amend their registration in any way.

Date of Last Contact must be updated with the date the donor last:

  • visited a Medicare Service Centre - face to face (without a form)
  • telephoned (only for intent registrations – cannot update a consent record via phone)
    • signed and dated a mailed form - letter/ mail received
    • the Date of Last Contact will be updated with the signature date
    • if the form has not been dated/incomplete/invalid, the date the form was received should be used

4

Conflicting donation decision on forms + Read more ...

Contact must be made to confirm their decision where the potential registrant has ticked both:

  • the ALL and selected any individual organs and tissue for donation (Section 9 of current form), and/or
  • ‘be removed from the donor register’ and ‘register my decision not to be a donor’ (or similar wording on older form versions)

If contact cannot be made after one attempt to call, the AODR registration form must be returned with the appropriate letter.

See the Resources page for example scenarios and how to action these.

5

Organs and/or tissue for donation section on forms + Read more ...

Where Organ and tissue decisions are blank (Section 9 of current form) for a ‘Yes’ decision:

  • If the customer has not previously been registered, the form must be returned with the appropriate letter
  • If intent registration exists, process, updating registration to consent. Do not amend organ and tissue for donation decision
  • If the customer is registered as consent, process. As the customer has not indicated a decision, do not amend organ and tissue decision and the Most Recent Donation Decision Date

Where specific organ and tissue decisions are selected without the ‘All’ tick box, enter separate organs/tissue as per form. Organs and/or tissue are in the same order in CDMS as the current form.

Note: the processing of an objection request/form is similar to a new yes registration. However, the Organs and/or Tissues for donation section is deselected to prompt a ‘No’ Donor Decision Type in CDMS.

The Resources page contains:

  • information on processing rules for multiple fields
  • data entry standards, and
  • a link to the Australian Organ Donor Register (AODR) batch record form and contact details

6

Organ donor registration amendments + Read more ...

Organ donor registrations (AODR or Donor Only entitlement) may need updating. This can be upon request by the organ donor or if an error was made when the registration was initially processed.

If the Organ Donor Registrant (ODR) has:

  • an AODR (linked) entitlement, then changes:
    • can be made to their Donation Decision details
    • to their personal details must follow Medicare rules and procedures including changing address in Medicare Group
  • a Donor Only entitlement, then changes:
    • can be made to both Personal and Donation details

7

The ‘How did you find out about us' field + Read more ...

This is an optional field which identifies how the donor registrant found out about the Donor Register.

When updating a donor registration the only time a selection is required from the How did you find out about us drop down field is when either:

  • updating an internet registration
  • if a Service Officer was able to ask the question when talking to the Organ Donor Registrant either in the Medicare Service Centre or over the phone

If the above situations do not apply, the drop-down box is to be left blank and ‘NO’ can be selected when the warning message displays.

8

Record information in Comments field + Read more ...

The information entered into the Comments field is visible to Authorised Medical Personnel (AMP) who have access to the AODR On-Line Enquiry System. Service Officers need to ensure that when an organ donor registrant supplies information to the AODR, only appropriate details are entered into the Comments field.

Comments may also be obtained through Freedom of Information (FOI) requests. Comments must be entered via the AODR comments tab or Medicare Personal Comments tab where no AODR registration exists.

Once a date of contact and or decision date has been updated in an AODR registration, it cannot be reversed or backdated. In this circumstance a comment can be placed to note the original/correct date for this purpose only (no processing comments for normal business):

Example: ‘AODR correct Most Recent Donation Decision (MRDD) date and Date of last contact (DOLLC) for form processed DDMMYYYY is DDMMYYYY- unable to correct.’

Note: entering this comment may be important in the event that an AMP calls or completes an online register check to confirm the consumer’s last decision and decision date with the register.

All comments in relation to AODR must be entered with the prefix of ‘AODR’ per Policy and Medicare agreements to distinguish comments from a Medicare or other comment.

See the Resources page for example scenarios and how to action these.

9

Other minor discrepancies + Read more ...

All minor discrepancies in Medicare enrolment files where AODR have permission to access and/or link to Medicare, are actioned per Medicare policies.

There is legal advice from the Principal Legal Adviser that minor changes to the Medicare enrolment file may be made without supporting documentation.

These amendments cannot change the substance of a person's details only. They correct minor spelling or typographical errors that may have been made by Service Officers in the normal course of their duties. It is acceptable to make these amendments. The agency has an obligation to ensure that the personal information held about customers is up-to-date and accurate.

For more information see Amend a person's Medicare enrolment details and Change legal name on Medicare record.

10

Change address/personal details requests via Donor Registration form + Read more ...

Conditions to update Medicare details

  • Medicare number has to be completed (the number needs to be a valid Medicare number for the Organ Donor Registrant), or
  • Authorisation given to link details to Medicare record (form or phone)
    • If via a form, the form must be signed

Address

If the Organ Donor Registrant has provided an address, the address must be changed under GROUP. This is for any person or dependant on a Medicare card.

No change to existing Donor Only or Medicare Group address is required if:

  • no address has been provided on the form, and
  • sufficient details are provided to match the correct consumer=

See also Amend address in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) for Medicare.

Contact details

  • Update on the customer’s Medicare record;
    • phone number
    • email address and
    • title. Amendments must be made on the Contact Details and Personal Details tabs in ‘Personal’ within CDMS
  • If a contact number has already been supplied in any phone contact field, there is no need to enter it again in another field
  • If a contact number is incomplete, do not enter
  • Where not in CDMS, landlines can be entered in Home or Business phone number fields and mobile numbers must be entered into the Mobile Number field

Note: there may be a prompt to update the ATSI Status and Source when updating registrations linked to consumer records. Update Status to ‘Not Stated’ with the Source as ‘AODR’. See also: Amend a person's Medicare enrolment details

Where AODR registration or Donor Only entitlement is not linked to Medicare

To update the address and contact details on:

  • a Donor Only Entitlement, or
  • AODR registration not linked to Medicare, see Amend organ donor registration if a registration is to then be linked to the registrant’s Medicare record, Amendments for ‘Donor Only’ records are made in the Personal Details tab within the Donor Only Entitlement

Major discrepancy for non AODR processing staff

Table 2: describes how all non AODR TAS trained staff process all major discrepancies relating to new registrations where no Donor Only registration exists.

Step

Action

1

Identified major discrepancy + Read more ...

This is where a major discrepancy in personal details has been identified between an organ donor registration form and either:

  • the linked organ donor entitlement, or
  • consumer record

Where the discrepancy is the surname:

  • refer to Confirm previous name, see Link Donor Only entitlement to consumer record

If the discrepancy still exists or is not resolved, or is a DOB major discrepancy, go to Step 3.

Note: if the Service Officer is proficient in the Medicare Public processing team, follow the current Medicare protocols and policies.

2

Gender major discrepancy + Read more ...

For major discrepancies without new documentation received to the register:

  • process as normal, linking donor only records may be actioned where appropriate
  • enter AODR comments, for example: ‘AODR form received, major gender discrepancy not altered per <comments/Medicare/phone call/PSO>’

For major discrepancies with new documentation attached:

  • process as normal, linking donor only records may be actioned where appropriate
  • enter appropriate AODR comments, for example ‘ AODR form/email received gender discrepancy paperwork escalated’
  • escalate to the AODR PSO Team for action

The Resources page contains a link to the contact details. For assistance, contact the AODR PSO Team. See also Change of sex or gender for Medicare enrolments.

3

Discrepancy still exists + Read more ...

If the discrepancy still exists/is not resolved:

  • enter comments in PaNDA
  • un-assign the work item in PaNDA to raise proficiency for established AODR staff

See Manage work items in PaNDA. The Resources page also contains a link to contact details.

Major discrepancy for AODR TAS processing staff

Table 3: describes how AODR process all major discrepancies relating to new registrations where no Donor Only registration exists.

Step

Action

1

Medicare entitlement match + Read more ...

If:

  • the Medicare number is provided on the registration form, but all personal details have not been provided on the form:
    • use the personal details on the Medicare entitlement for the AODR entitlement registration
  • one or more of the personal details provided on the form varies from those in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS), go to Step 2

2

Personal details + Read more ...

Check for any comments previously entered about any changes in the name and/or details mentioning signed documentation. Update the comments and sighted documentation or escalate to the AODR PSO Team if unsure.

This will prevent staff from sending an AODR letter for a prior processing error.

If the:

  • first name varies, go to Step 3
  • date of birth varies, go to Step 4
  • gender varies, go to Step 5
  • address varies, go to Step 6
  • group and personal name vary, go to Step 7
  • the family name varies, go to Step 8

3

First name + Read more ...

Different spelling including abbreviations to the name in CDMS and other scenarios should be taken as an exact match and the registration processed and linked to the Medicare record in accordance with the Minor discrepancies in Medicare Enrolment File procedures. Record comment in the AODR entitlement comment field 'AODR from received first name also known as <list name>

Note: for Medicare purposes a person can be known by a name other than their legal name. Where a person presents Medicare with documentation that confirms their 'known in the community' name, this name will be changed at 'Group' level only. The person's legal name is still recorded at 'Personal' level the AODR card will issue with the name as it appears in 'Personal' level of CDMS. If the person rings and asks for the name to be changed refer to Change legal name on Medicare record.

4

Date of Birth + Read more ...

Date of birth variations should be taken to be an exact match, where:

  • All other details match that is, first name, last name, gender and address:
    • process the registration as an AODR entitlement linked to Medicare using the date of birth recorded in Medicare, and the date of birth variation is considered to be a 'minor discrepancy'. See also: Amend a person's Medicare enrolment details

Where the discrepancy is not considered a 'Minor discrepancy':

  • send the appropriate AODR letter ‘Date of birth details differ to CDMS’ and finalise Note: where there are 2 people on the one Medicare card:
  • with the same name, and
  • it is obvious that the dates of birth have been switched, attempt one call only. This is to seek clarification from the individual prior to sending the letter

5

Gender + Read more ...

Any gender variation should be processed using the gender recorded on Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) where possible.

Determine if the gender variation between CDMS and the form can be changed without supporting documents. See Amend a person's Medicare enrolment details.

Can the variation be changed?

  • Yes, process the form. If the advice was over the phone, transfer to Medicare Public. Procedure ends here
  • No, and documentation is not attached to the form:
    • process as normal, linking donor only records may be actioned where appropriate
    • enter AODR comments, for example: ‘AODR form received, gender discrepancy not altered per <comments/Medicare/unsuccessful phone call/PSO>’
  • No, and documentation is attached to the form:
    • process as normal, linking donor only records may be actioned where appropriate
    • enter AODR comments, for example ‘ AODR form/email received- gender discrepancy paperwork escalated’
    • escalate to the AODR PSO Team for action

The Resources page contains a link to the contact details. For assistance, contact the AODR PSO Team.

6

Address + Read more ...

If a different address is provided on the form to that which is in Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS):

  • register the individual if required
  • amend the address in Medicare Group

For any group or personal address discrepancies escalate to the AODR PSO Team.

7

Group or personal family name difference + Read more ...

When processing an Organ Donor Registration form where the Medicare number is provided, and all details match but the family name on the form is the same as that recorded in Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) at 'Group' but is not the same as the family name recorded at 'Personal', the following is to apply:

  • Process the form and issue appropriate AODR Letter such as ‘Family Name in Personal different to Family Name in Group’
  • For new registrations, process as per Register new organ donor by Medicare number. Do not create a new Consumer ID with AODR entitlement not linked to Medicare

Where a Donor Only registration exists:

  • Update the Donor Only registration to match the name that is recorded in group
  • Key in the required changes, validate and update
  • Select 'Link to Consumer' and key in the required changes, validate and update
  • Process the registration and issue Letter - Group/Personal names are different
  • Record a comment 'Also known as' (the name recorded on the form and at group)

The new AODR entitlement record reverts back to the surname that is in Personal.

8

Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) and AODR form surname difference + Read more ...

Organ Donor registration forms where the Medicare number is provided and all details match, but the family name recorded in CDMS is not:

  • the same as the family name recorded on the AODR form:
    • process the registration using the family name in CDMS
    • for new registrations, process as per Register new organ donor by Medicare number. Do not create a new Consumer ID with an AODR entitlement not linked to Medicare
  • try one phone attempt if possible and advise:
    • the surname provided on the form is different to their Medicare enrolment
    • the AODR form has been processed and the AODR card will issue with the family name as recorded in Medicare
    • hey should attend a Service Centre or use Medicare online services via myGov or Medicare Express Plus App to update their name as soon as possible
  • If call is unsuccessful or no contact phone details given:
    • issue appropriate AODR Letter ‘such as Name Differs from CDMS to AODR Form’. Enter appropriate AKA and letter comments where applicable, See the Resources page

Birth date discrepancy - donor only records date of birth as 1.1.1900

Table 4: describes how to process a donor registration where a search identifies a Donor Only record which matches with the exception of the date of birth and that donor only record has the date of birth as 1.1.1900 and has the Media Type code VIC TPC.

Step

Action

1

Medicare number supplied + Read more ...

Has the Medicare number or authorisation to link been supplied?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Match date of birth + Read more ...

Is the date of birth on the registration form the same as the one on the existing Medicare record?

  • Yes, update the date of birth on the donor only record and link to consumer record
  • No, see the following procedures:
    • Major discrepancy
    • Minor discrepancies in Medicare Enrolment File

3

Date of birth update + Read more ...

Update the date of birth for the existing donor only record to reflect the date of birth on the registration form.
