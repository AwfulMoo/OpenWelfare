1

Youth Allowance - independent, away from home rate

Sylvia is a full-time student and entitled to a Youth Allowance rate of:

  • $562.80 per fortnight (maximum Independent Away-From-Home rate of Youth Allowance (YA) (Student)
  • plus the Energy Supplement (ES) of $7.00

Sylvia is working and being paid $670 every fortnight. Sylvia's Income Bank balance is zero.

Calculating the amount payable:

  • There is no Income Bank accrual as Sylvia’s employment income is above the income free area of $480.00 per fortnight
  • Sylvia's income is greater than $575.00. Each dollar of income above this value will reduce Sylvia's rate of payment by 60 cents
  • Additionally, as Sylvia's income is greater than $575, the rate reduction of 50 cents for each dollar between $480 and $575 will need to be applied. This is done by adding $47.50 to the calculated rate reduction of income greater than $575

Calculating the affecting income:

  • Affecting income = ($670 - $575) multiplied by 0.60 then add $47.50
    • $104.50 = ($670 - $575) x 0.6) + $47.50 = $104.50

New rate of payment:

  • As a result of earning $670 every fortnight, Sylvia's payment of $562.80 will be reduced by $104.50 every fortnight
  • Sylvia is entitled to receive $458.30 + ES $7.00

2

Youth Allowance, full time Australian Apprentice, single rate

Ben is 27 years old and a single independent full-time Australian Apprentice in receipt of Youth Allowance.

The maximum rate for Ben is:

  • $562.80 Youth Allowance
  • plus the Energy Supplement (ES) $7.00 per fortnight

Ben recently started being paid $850 per fortnight. Ben’s Income Bank balance at the beginning of the fortnight was 266 credits.

Student Income Bank:

  • Ben’s income of $850 is greater than both the income free area and Income Bank balance combined
  • Ben is taken to have drawn down from the Income Bank this fortnight, the difference between the income bank and the ordinary income. The income bank is reduced to zero (266 credits - $850 = - 584 or zero)

Ordinary income:

  • Ben had 266 credits in the Student Income Bank. Ben’s income of $850 is reduced by the Income Bank balance of 266 credits, leaving an affecting income of $584
  • This results in a reduction from the maximum rate by $52.90: (($584 - $575) x 0.6 + $47.50)
  • The rate for the fortnight is $509.90 ($562.80 - $52.90)

Amount payable:

  • Ben’s gross earned income is $850 per fortnight
  • Ben’s income exceeds the income free area and Income Bank Balance combined. The current Income Bank balance of 266 credits is reduced to zero
  • After applying the Income Bank balance, the remaining ordinary income is still greater than $575. Each dollar of income above this value reduces the rate of payment by 60 cents
  • The remaining ordinary income is greater than $575.00. The rate reduction of 50 cents for each dollar between $480 and $575 is applied. This is done by calculating the rate reduction of income greater than $575 and adding $47.50.

Affecting income and Ben's new rate of payment:

  • Remaining ordinary income = (income bank balance - ordinary income)
    • $584 = (266 credits - $850)
  • Affecting income = (remaining ordinary income - $575) multiplied by 0.6, then add $47.50
    • $52.90 = (($584 - $575) x 0.6) + $47.50

New rate of payment:

  • Ben’s payment of $562.80 is reduced by $52.90
  • Ben is entitled to receive $509.90
  • No Income Bank accrual will occur in the coming fortnights as Ben's employment income is over the $480.00 income free area
  • With no Income Bank credits to apply for the following fortnight, and continuing income of $850, Ben’s rate of payment will decrease further to $212.50 + ES $7.00

3

YA student transfers to YA jobseeker then to YA student

Rashid is 19 and defers from a Bachelor of Engineering degree half way through the second year. Rashid attends a Services Australia office to register as a job seeker. At the time of deferral from the course, Rashid's Income Bank balance was 3,400 credits.

As soon as Rashid's activity changes from full-time student to job seeker, the:

  • Student Income Bank balance will be set to zero
  • Working Credit balance will be set 3,400 credits

When Rashid returns to full-time study the following year, any remaining Working Credit balance (up to a maximum of 3,500 credits) will be transferred to the Income Bank.

4

ABSTUDY, full time Australian Apprentice, single rate

Jennifer is 27 years old and a single independent full-time Australian Apprentice in receipt of ABSTUDY.

The maximum rate for Jennifer's circumstances is:

  • $677.20 ABSTUDY per fortnight
  • plus the Energy Supplement (ES) $8.80 per fortnight

Jennifer recently started being paid $850 per fortnight. Jennifer's Income Bank balance at the beginning of the fortnight was 266 credits.

Student Income Bank:

Jennifer's income of $850 is greater than both the income free area and Income Bank balance (266 credits). Therefore, Jennifer is taken to have drawn down from the Income Bank the difference between the income and the $480 personal income lower threshold. This reduces the Income bank to zero (266 credits - $480 = - 214 or zero).

Ordinary income:

  • Jennifer had 266 credits in the Student Income Bank
  • Jennifer's income of $850 is reduced by the Income Bank balance of 266 credits, leaving an affecting income of $584
  • The result is a reduction from the maximum rate by $52.90: (($584 - $575) x 0.6 + $47.50)
  • The rate for the fortnight is $624.30: ($677.20 - $52.90)

Amount payable:

  • Jennifer's gross earned income is $850 per fortnight
  • The current Income Bank balance of 266 credits will reduce to zero, as Jennifer's income exceeds the income free area and Income Bank Balance combined
  • No Income Bank accrual will occur in the following fortnight as Jennifer's employment income is over the $480.00 income free area
  • After applying the balance of the Income Bank, Jennifer's remaining ordinary income is still greater than $575. Each dollar of income above this value reduces Jennifer's rate of payment by 60 cents
  • As Jennifer's remaining ordinary income is greater than $575.00, the rate reduction of 50 cents for each dollar between $480 and $575 is applied. This is done by calculating the rate reduction of income greater than $575 and adding $47.50

Affecting income and Jennifer's new rate of payment:

  • Remaining ordinary income = (income bank balance - ordinary income)
    • $584 = (266 credits - $850)
  • Affecting income = (remaining ordinary income - $575) multiplied by 0.6, then add $47.50
    • $52.90 = (($584 - $575) x 0.6) + $47.50

New rate of payment:

  • As a result of earning $850 this fortnight, the maximum rate of payment of $677.20 is reduced by $52.90
  • Jennifer is entitled to receive $624.30
  • With no Income Bank credits remaining to apply for the following fortnight, and continuing income of $850, Jennifer's rate of payment will decrease further to $601.70

5

ABSTUDY full time student ceases study then recommences

Mike is a full-time student in receipt of ABSTUDY payments. Mike has been on ABSTUDY for some time and has had no employment income to date, so the Student Income Bank balance is 12,000 credits.

Mike then withdraws from the course of study and payments are cancelled from the last day of study. Mike works for 5 weeks, but then decides to return to full-time study. Mike reclaims and is granted ABSTUDY payments from the date study recommences.

As there has been a gap in ABSTUDY entitlement, Mike's Income Bank balance is set to zero from the date ABSTUDY entitlement recommenced. Mike is not entitled to carry over the previous Income Bank balance of 12,000 credits.

If Mike had recommenced studies within 28 days of withdrawing from the previous course, Mike's:

  • entitlement to receive ABSTUDY would have continued through the gap between courses, and
  • Income Bank balance would have continued over this period, and
  • Income Bank and any income from part-time employment would have been taken into account when determining the rate of ABSTUDY for this time

6

ABSTUDY full time student travels overseas temporarily

Sally is a full-time student in receipt of ABSTUDY payments. Sally has been on ABSTUDY for some time and has had no employment income to date. Sally's Student Income Bank balance is 12,000 credits.

Sally travels to England in the mid semester break. While overseas, Sally's ABSTUDY payments are suspended for not meeting the portability rules. As Sally's payments were suspended (and not cancelled), the student income bank balance is restored to 12,000 credits, the amount it was immediately before payments were suspended. Any income that would normally affect Sally's payments is taken into account.
