Qualification assessment for CrP

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Contacting Services Australia + Read more ...

Customers (or someone on their behalf) who contact Services Australia for assistance after experiencing an extreme circumstance may qualify for a Crisis Payment (CrP).

Only one CrP can be granted for the same circumstances, for example:

  • A customer paid CrP under the Social Security Act 1991, then becomes an ABSTUDY customer. The customer cannot claim another CrP under ABSTUDY policy for the same circumstances
  • A customer has received a CrP previously, as an humanitarian entrant, on the same visa. A second or subsequent entry to Australia on the same visa type does not entitle the customer to another CrP

Both members of a couple may qualify for a CrP in their own right for the same event. This is the case unless both members are caring for the same immediate family/household member in quarantine/self-isolation due to COVID-19 requirements.

Check whether the customer has been paid any Crisis Payments, how much, which category and when by navigating to the CRISIS_PS screen using the Superkey field in Process Direct, or by selecting Crisis Payment Summary in Customer First workspace.

Note: when the customer lodges an online claim for an income support payment, they will be asked if they are experiencing a crisis situation and given links to Payment and Service Finder and the link to the Crisis Payment information on the Services Australia website.

In receipt of a benefit + Read more ...

See how to view receipt of ABSTUDY Living Allowance on the system.

3

Customer not qualified for primary payment + Read more ...

To be eligible for a CrP a customer must be in receipt of, or qualified for (and payable) a primary payment for CrP:

  • Assist the customer with Payment Finder and tell them to lodge a claim for the relevant ISP or ABY Living Allowance payment online on the day of contact
  • Tell the customer to claim the relevant CrP (EXV, EXO, PRI or HUM) online after lodging the claim for an ISP or ABY Living Allowance. Note: NHE can be claimed from within the ISP or ABY claim as a combined claim and does not need to be done separately
  • Record the customer’s intent to claim a CrP - EXV using the Crisis Payment - Intent to claim Fast Note. Select Auto text. Use Emergency > Claim > CrP Intent to Claim. When completed, close the Fast Note
  • Identify and offer a referral to an appropriate specialist service if required

Procedure ends here.

Customer experienced or allegedly perpetrated an incident of family and domestic violence + Read more ...

  • No, go to Step 5

Customer left their home because it is unliveable + Read more ...

  • Yes, the customer may qualify for CrP under the other extreme circumstances category. See Table 3
  • No, go to Step 6

Customer due to be released or has already been released from prison or psychiatric confinement + Read more ...

On the customer’s release date, will they/have they spent 14 or more days in prison or psychiatric confinement as a result of being charged with committing an offence?

  • Yes, the customer may qualify for CrP under the release from prison or psychiatric confinement category. See Table 4 or New claim for Crisis Payment prior to the release of a customer in prison or psychiatric confinement
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Customer recently arrived in Australia as a humanitarian entrant + Read more ...

Has the customer recently arrived in Australia with a qualifying humanitarian visa for Crisis Payment?

  • Yes, the customer may qualify for CrP under the humanitarian entrant category. See Table 5
  • No, go to Step 8

For COVID-19 National Health Emergency circumstances only + Read more ...

Is the customer required to quarantine/self-isolate, or care for an immediate family/household member who is required to quarantine/self-isolate?

  • Yes, the customer may qualify for CrP under the national health emergency category. See Table 6
  • No, go to Step 9

Qualifications for CrP Extreme Circumstances family and domestic violence + Read more ...

If the customer is not able to give evidence, a social worker can verbally verify the customer’s circumstances in some instances.

Customer has remained in the home:

If the customer is not able to give evidence, a social worker can verbally verify the customer’s circumstances in some instances.

Eligibility for CrP under other extreme circumstances

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Qualification for CrP for Other Extreme Circumstances + Read more ...

In order to qualify for payment of CrP under other extreme circumstances, a customer must:

  • meet the common criteria for CRP, including severe financial hardship
  • make a claim for CrP within 7 days after the extreme circumstance occurred
  • have left their home due to the extreme circumstance, and it is unreasonable to expect the person to remain in or return to their home
  • have established a new home, or are intending to establish a new home
  • have been in Australia when the extreme circumstance occurred and lodged a claim for CrP within Australia
  • not have been paid more than 4 payments of CrP for extreme circumstances combined (family and domestic violence and/or other extreme circumstances) in any 12 month period

Some common reasons customers may claim CrP-EXO include a house fire, flooding (for example, due a burst water main), structural damage, or where the customer feels unsafe (for example, due to community violence or repeated break-ins/burglaries).

More examples of CrP other extreme circumstances are available on the Resources page.

A customer cannot get a CrP-EXO if they:

  • intentionally brought about the extreme circumstance with a view to getting a payment of CrP
  • were evicted from their home due to not paying their mortgage or rent
  • had to move out because their landlord is selling the property or moving back in
  • decided to move to another home
  • are living in government housing and have applied for a transfer to a different location, but are still living at the current location or intend to return while they await the transfer (such as have not established a new home)

A customer does not qualify to receive a CrP - EXO if a disaster relief payment administered by the local, State or Commonwealth Government for the same extreme circumstance is available.

Note: if the AGDRP or DRP (or any other disaster relief payment) is announced as being available:

  • Crisis Payment is only payable to people who lodged a claim for CrP before the day of the announcement of the disaster relief payment, given they meet all other eligibility conditions
  • Customers who have been granted CrP prior to the announcement could also test their eligibility for a disaster relief payment

Has the customer met all the qualifications for CrP other extreme circumstance?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Customer qualifies for CrP + Read more ...

Note: claims for most Crisis Payments - other extreme circumstances do not require the involvement of a social worker. A referral to a social worker may be appropriate if other assistance and support is required, but this should be done after the claim for CrP-EXO has been made, not as part of the claim. See Step 5 in Table 1 of New claim for Crisis Payment Other Extreme Circumstances.

Procedure ends here.

3

Customer is not qualified for CrP + Read more ...

  • Identify and offer a referral to an appropriate specialist service
  • If there has been a disaster, they may qualify for a Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP)
  • Consider other forms of assistance such as urgent payment or advance payment that may be appropriate

Eligibility for CrP for customers released from prison or psychiatric confinement

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Initial contact + Read more ...

A customer (or someone on their behalf) contacts Services Australia seeking help, as they are due to be, or have recently been, released from prison or psychiatric confinement.

2

In prison or psychiatric confinement for 14 days and charged with committing an offence + Read more ...

To be eligible for a CrP-PRI, the customer must:

  • meet the common criteria for CRP, including severe financial hardship
  • have been in prison or psychiatric confinement for 14 or more days
  • have been charged with committing an offence

For the purposes of CrP, when determining if a customer has been detained for 14 days, the first day of entry into imprisonment/confinement or detention is included in the calculation of days. The day of release is not included.

A customer is not eligible for CrP-PRI if they:

  • were held in an immigration detention centre
  • have been in a mental health facility or psychiatric confinement but have not been charged with committing an offence

Different rules apply when suspending/cancelling payments for customers admitted into custody. See:

  • Confirming prison admission and release dates and
  • Admission process for incarcerated customers

Was the person detained for 14 days or more as a result of being charged with an offence?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Additional qualifications for CrP + Read more ...

In addition to being charged with an offence and being detained for 14 days or more CrP may be payable to the customer if:

  • the common criteria for CrP are met, including severe financial hardship
  • a claim for CrP is made within 7 days after the extreme circumstance occurred

The customer can have been in prison or psychiatric confinement overseas, but must make a claim from within Australia.

Claims for CrP-PRI can also be made up to 21 days before the customer’s expected release date from prison or psychiatric confinement. However, these customers are usually assisted by Prison Liaison Officers who assess the claim while the customer is still in the facility.

Note: only one CrP can be made for the same circumstances e.g. paid CrP under the Social Security Act 1991, then becomes an ABSTUDY customer, they cannot claim another CrP under ABSTUDY policy for the same circumstances.

Has the customer met all the qualifications for CrP - released prisoner?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Customer qualifies for CrP + Read more ...

Where it appears that the customer meets all the qualifications for CrP, a claim should be invited.

See:

  • New claim for Crisis Payment after customer released from prison or psychiatric confinement, or
  • New claim for Crisis Payment prior to the release of a customer in prison or psychiatric confinement

Procedure ends here.

5

Customer is not qualified for CrP + Read more ...

Tell the customer that they do not qualify for CrP, but may still lodge a claim to test their eligibility. Identify and offer a referral to an appropriate specialist service if required. Consider other forms of assistance such as an urgent payment or advance payment that might be appropriate.

Meeting the eligibility criteria + Read more ...

To be eligible for CrP the customer must meet the common criteria, including:

  • making a claim for CrP within 7 days after the extreme circumstance occurred or meets the deemed claim provisions

A customer is not eligible for CrP-HUM if they:

Eligibility for CrP - National Health Emergency

Table 6

Step

Action

1

CrP-NHE due to coronavirus (COVID-19) + Read more ...

CrP-NHE is only payable to customers who, due to coronavirus (COVID-19), are required by the Commonwealth, a state/territory government or health professional to:

  • quarantine/self-isolate, or
  • care for an immediate family member or member of their household while in quarantine/self-isolation

A customer can only be paid one CrP-NHE per quarantine or self-isolation period. For example, if a customer is displaying symptoms of COVID-19 and is awaiting test results when they then need to care for their child who is also displaying symptoms, this is considered to be one event and only one CrP-NHE can be paid.

The following are not eligible for a CrP-NHE:

  • People who are staying home because they have an existing health condition, even if told to do so by a health professional
  • People who are staying home but still able to go out to complete essential activities, such as shopping or work
  • A person who is caring for one of the above people

See:

  • Examples of quarantine or self-isolation, and
  • Qualification definitions

Note: a person cannot qualify for CrP-NHE to care for a person who is in quarantine/self-isolation in a hospital as care is given by hospital staff.

2

Meeting the eligibility criteria + Read more ...

To be eligible for CrP-NHE, the customer must:

  • Meet the common criteria for CrP, including severe financial hardship
  • Contact Services Australia for the purposes of claiming a payment or concession card within 14 days of entering quarantine or self-isolation
  • Lodge a claim for CrP-NHE within 14 days of the contact date
  • Provide evidence of a COVID-19 test notification or result with the date of the test being the event date

Deemed claim provisions apply to any customer who intends to claim CrP-NHE.

Does the customer meet all the criteria for CrP-NHE?

  • Yes, go to New claim for Crisis Payment - National Health Emergency
  • No,
    • tell the customer that they do not qualify for CrP, but may still lodge a claim to test their eligibility
    • identify and offer a referral to an appropriate specialist service if required
    • investigate if the customer is entitled to another payment or product
