This page contains information relating to the Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation overpayments due to child income.

FTB reconciliation overpayments

Overpayment due to child’s income

This table describes the steps to use when identifying that the Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation overpayment was partly or entirely due to a child’s income.

Step

Action

1

Child’s taxable income + Read more ...

Ask the customer: Was the child's adjusted taxable income equal to or above the child income limit for the relevant financial year?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, this is a review of the decision to cancel or reject Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for that child for the relevant financial year. Procedure ends here

If the customer's FTB for the child was deferred on the Child Entitlement Deferment (CHDF) screen rather than cancelled or rejected, their FTB entitlement can be automatically re-reconciled by updating the child's income details on the Child Student/Income Circumstances (CHSI) and CHDF screens. See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) child income.

2

Check if the reconciliation overpayment is due to the child's income + Read more ...

  • Go to the FAO Reconciliation and Calculation Summary (FRCS) screen. Change the Income Year: field to the relevant financial year and 'S'elect the original reconciliation line to display the FTB Calculation Result (FACRF) screen
  • 'S'elect the manual adjustment line on the FACRF screen. The total adjustment amount has been added back into the reconciliation calculation
  • The FTB Total Adjustment Period Display (FTAPD) screen displays a breakdown of the adjustment. Code 'Y' in the GO TO: field
  • The FTB Confirm Reco Amounts Summary (FCRAS) screen will display if a student income overpayment was automatically raised and adjusted during the relevant year
  • From the FCRAS screen, note the start and end dates of the overpayment, any adjustment made and the overpayment ID number. The Amount ($): field shows the original calculated amount of the adjustment

3

Further investigation of overpayment + Read more ...

  • To find the reason the overpayment was finalised, go to the Debt List (OPDL) screen. 'S'elect the overpayment with the ID number noted from the FCRAS screen
  • From the Modify / Assess Debt (OPMA) screen, note the number in the Source Actvy ID (AMR): field
  • Go to the Event Summary (ES) screen. Change the defaulted dates to the dates obtained from the FCRAS screen. 'S'elect the activity with the AMR number already noted from the OPMA screen
  • The History Activity Details (HAY) screen should help explain why the debt was finalised. Note the date of the activity to select the corresponding DOC from the Document List (DL) or the History Document List (HDL) screens
  • Go to the Assessment Explanation (AX) screen and 'S'elect the child to display the Assessment Details (ASDT) screen. This shows the date the system was updated to reflect the customer advised the child's income

If the qualification overpayment was incorrectly finalised (that is the overpayment should have been raised at the time it was detected but was not), the reconciliation result is correct in deducting the qualification debt amount from the customer's FTB entitlement.

Note: the overpayment should have been raised only if the customer had advised at the time that the child's income had already reached the child income limit. If the customer had advised that child's income will be equal to or over the limit by the end of the financial year, but the income had not yet reached the limit, FTB should have been cancelled from the date paid to +1 or deferred.

If the customer's FTB for the child was deferred, their FTB entitlement can be automatically re-reconciled by updating the child's income details on the Child Student/Income Circumstances (CHSI) and CHDF screens. See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) child income.

After checking the system, is it still clear that the overpayment was due to the child's income being equal to or above the child income limit for the relevant financial year?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, see Customer enquires about a reconciliation overpayment for other possible reasons for the overpayment. Procedure ends here

4

Reconciliation inclusions + Read more ...

Part A and Part B supplements and Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS) are included in the reconciliation calculation once adequate income/ not required to lodge details are available.

FTB top-ups, including supplement and SIFS entitlement, are automatically used for debt offsetting.

Advise the customer of the result and record details on a DOC. The minimum standards required for the DOC are as follows (figures used are only for example):

  • Summary line (Sum: field):
    • CUS Pho re: FTB O/P of $1234 for 00/01. 19/03/02
  • Text (Txt: field):
    • FTB reconciliation overpayment of $1234 explained to CUS. (child's name) income exceeded threshold for 20xx/xx
    • Repayment options discussed with CUS
