This page contains information to support decision making about processing a refund to a customer or third party and preparing a submission to create a consolidated revenue debt in situations when a customer has received funds they were not entitled to (for example, mistaken disbursements). It also describes the process involved in finalising a consolidated revenue debt and consideration of waiver provisions.

Action

1

Situations where consolidated revenue funds might be used + Read more ...

Creating a consolidated revenue debt could be appropriate in very limited circumstances only, including:

  • an error made by Services Australia or other Commonwealth agency that led to the customer receiving funds they were not entitled to and amounts need to be refunded to a payer or a third party. For example, mistaken disbursements
  • a decision has been made in accordance with Debt Repayment processes that it is appropriate to ensure the payee continues to receive an ongoing amount for child support

Note: when the management of an overpayment may require the creation of a consolidated revenue debt, the Payee Overpayment Summary letter must be suppressed immediately to ensure that the payee does not get inaccurate information about their debt, see Payment options in Child Support.

2

Mistaken disbursements and refund to third party + Read more ...

If Child Support incorrectly collect money from a third party and disbursed it, a refund of the money to the third party must be made. Funds will be appropriated from consolidated revenue. A consolidated revenue debt will be created on the payee's account. See Refunds Cuba Process Help.

Example: a financial institution actions a section 72A (garnishee) notice and sends funds to Child Support which are disbursed to the payee. The financial institution later advises that the funds were incorrectly deducted from an account that did not belong to the payer. Child Support must refund the amount to the financial institution from consolidated revenue and recover the overpayment from the payee.

For assistance, create an online SSO request, see the Technical support Cuba Process Help.

Contact the third party to advise the money has been refunded.

Go to Step 4.

3

Consolidated revenue funds used for other reasons + Read more ...

In deciding if consolidated revenue funds can be used for other situations other than mistaken disbursement, consider if:

  • it furthers the objects of the Act, such as ensuring the payee will continue to receive periodic payments for child support
  • the interests of the Commonwealth are protected, consider how likely it is that the consolidated revenue debt will be recovered from the payee, for example:
  • the likelihood of recovering a $2,000 debt from a payee with a foreseeable monthly entitlement of $500 is high
  • the likelihood of recovering a $2,000 debt from a payee with a minimum entitlement that ends in six months and the payee has limited financial capacity appears to be low
  • the interests of the payee and payer are taken into account

Note: do not provide any commitment to the payer that monies will be refunded. This cannot be confirmed until a decision is made on creating a consolidated revenue debt.

3

Decide how to manage the consolidated revenue debt + Read more ...

Before preparing a submission for a request of monies from consolidated revenue, decide whether to pay the funds to the payer or credit the funds to the payer's child support account to be used for future child support liabilities.

The funds should be credited to the child support account to pay future child support liabilities when:

  • the payer makes this request
  • the payer's payment history indicates they are unlikely to make payments for future child support liabilities

If the funds will be paid to the payer, see Excess cash on a Child Support customer's account.

Note: clearly document in the submission how the payer should be refunded.

Go to Step 4.

4

Prepare submission for creation of consolidated revenue debt + Read more ...

If a consolidated revenue debt is being created to ensure the payee continues to receive ongoing child support, document in the submission when the next liability will fall due. This will enable the Service Officer, Service Support Officer (SSO), Program Support Manager (PSM) and approver to progress the submission as a priority, minimising the risk of an overpayment being recovered from the ongoing liability.

Prepare submission (Service Officer)

Prepare a submission by:

  • completing the submission to create a consolidated revenue debt
  • create a CSO - potential con rev intray - see Intray management Cuba Process Help
  • copy and paste the submission document into:
    • a SSO online notepad
    • the CSO - potential con rev intray notepad

5

Review submission (SSO) + Read more ...

Consider if the submission is accurate and complete by checking:

  • outstanding actions have been finalised:
    • accurate incomes
    • outstanding non agency payment decisions
    • eligibility has been accurately saved
  • the reasons for making the recommendation have been fully explained in full in the submission
  • the recommendation to create a consolidated revenue debt is appropriate

Note: if unsure or not confident in reviewing a consolidated revenue debt submission, seek PSM support. See Technical support in Child Support.

Complete submission

If the submission is complete:

  • document the submission on the:
    • payer’s Communication window if the submission is to refund the overpayment to the payer prior to recovering from the payee, or
    • payee Communication window if the submission is not to refund the overpayment to the payer
  • update the Technical Support Issue notepad, recording recommendation to create or not create a consolidated revenue debt
  • create a TSO - potential con rev intray
  • copy all notepads from the CSO - potential con rev intray to the TSO - potential con rev intray
  • delete the CSO - potential con rev intray
  • copy the submission from Cuba and paste into a word document
  • email the word document to the PSM
  • add a note in the intray notepad advising the matter has been referred to the PSM

See Intray management Cuba Process Help.

Incomplete submission

If the submission is incomplete:

  • contact the Service Officer to discuss
  • document what action is needed in the Technical Support Issue notepad and the CSO - potential con rev intray
  • request the Service Officer resubmit the submission once the feedback has been actioned

6

Review submission (PSM) + Read more ...

Update the SSO Issue notepad in the Communication window with recommendation to create or not create a consolidated revenue debt. Note: if the submission is to create a consolidated revenue debt of $5,000 or more:

  • request Child Support Program Branch review the submission
  • copy the submission and supporting comments from Program Advice into a word document
  • email the word document to the External Complaints - Child Support

Action the following:

  • Create a Con Rev Submission intray
  • Copy all notepads from the TSO - potential con rev intray into the Con Rev Submission intray
  • Delete the TSO - potential con rev intray
  • Route to the appropriate intray position:
    Region: National Office
    Office:     Client and Community Branch
    Stream/Team:     CR SCR6 CR NAT TM6
    Position:     None

This intray position is monitored daily by External Complaints – Child Support (ExCCS). The ExCCS Administrative Support Officer will regularly allocate these submissions to the appropriate ExCCS Service Support Managers for actioning.

Note: if the consolidated revenue debt submission requires urgent review, email External Complaints - Child Support.

Incomplete submission

If the submission is incomplete:

  • contact the SSO to discuss
  • document what action and/or information is needed in the Technical Support Issue notepad in the Communication window and the TSO - potential con rev intray

7

Submission returned as incomplete (Service Officer) + Read more ...

If the submission is incomplete the Service Officer is to:

  • action the SSO/PSM feedback
  • update the submission notepad in both the Technical Support Issue notepad and the CSO - potential con rev intray notepad

Finalise consolidated revenue debt submission

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Make decision to create a consolidated revenue debt - Authorisations + Read more ...

Submissions are authorised:

  • up to $4,999 by an EL1 – External Complaints – Child Support (ExCCS)
  • $5,000 or more by the National Manager - Customer Service

2

Submission review (EL1 - ExCCS or NM - Customer Service) + Read more ...

Review the submission, the information below sets out the required action for incomplete, refused and approved submissions.

Incomplete:

  • Update the Technical Support Issue notepad in the Communication window recording the reason for the decision, see the Documentation Cuba Process Help
  • Record the reason why the submission is incomplete in the Con Rev Submission intray
  • Route the Con Rev Submission intray back to the PSM and send an email to them requesting they:
    • action any feedback
    • discuss with the SSO
    • resubmit the submission

See:

  • Intray management Cuba Process Help, and
  • Technical support in Child Support

Refused:

  • Update the Technical Support Issue notepad in the Communication window recording the reason for the decision
  • Record the reason why the submission is refused in the Con Rev Submission intray
  • Route the Con Rev Submission intray back to the PSM and send an email to the Child Support Program Branch requesting they provide feedback to the SSO and Service Officer

Approved:

  • Update the Technical Support Issue notepad in the Communication window recording the reason for the decision. Note: clearly document how the payer should be refunded, see Step 4 in Table 1
  • Route the Con Rev Submission intray back to the PSM who will liaise with the SSO to create the consolidated revenue debt

3

Finalise submission (PSM, SSO, Service Officer) + Read more ...

To finalise a submission that is:

  • Incomplete, go to Step 4
  • Refused, go to Step 5
  • Approved and consolidated revenue payment:
    • will be paid to the payer, go to Step 6
    • will not be paid to the payer, go to Step 7

4

Incomplete submission (SSO) + Read more ...

The SSO will:

  • action the updated feedback from the PSM and approver
  • update the submission document in the Technical Support Issue notepad in the Communication window and in the TSO - potential con rev intray
  • send an email to the Child Support Program Branch to advise that the revised submission has been actioned
  • add a note to the intray notepad advising the matter has been referred to the PSM

5

Refused (SSO, Service Officer) + Read more ...

The SSO will:

  • update the submission document in the Technical Support Issue notepad in the Communication window
  • contact the Service Officer to discuss
  • finalise the SSO request, see Technical support in Child Support

The Service Officer will:

  • review the reason for the decision
  • contact the customer to advise of the decision. If the customer requests the decision in writing, create a unique letter. See Letters Cuba Process Help. Note: the customer has no objection rights to the decision to refund or not to refund an overpayment
  • check the Payee Overpayment Summary Suppression indicator. If the indicator is set to:
    • Suppress, change it to Always to allow the POS to issue
    • Standard, do not change as the POS will issue. See Payment options in Child Support
  • take action to recover the overpayment, see Recovery from a payee’s child support entitlement in Step 12 of Debt repayment

6

Approved - consolidated revenue payment will be paid to the payer (PSM, Service Officer, SSO) + Read more ...

The PSM will:

  • document actions required in the Con Rev Submission intray and route it to the Service Officer

The Service Support Officer (SSO) will:

  • reallocate the overpayment to excess cash for the paying parent from the case Reallocate Cash window, see Consolidated Revenue Debt Cuba Process Help. This will create a consolidated revenue debt for the paying parent and excess cash for the same amount for the paying parent
  • navigate to the parent who will owe the consolidated revenue debt. Reassign the consolidated revenue debt to that parent via the Client Con Rev Debt Reassign window
  • check the payee overpayment summary suppression indicator and set it to Suppress to ensure the POS does not issue to the payee. See Payment options in Child Support

The Service Officer will:

  • contact the customers to advise the decision. If a customer requests the decision in writing, create a unique letter. Note: customers do not have objection rights to our decisions to refund or not to refund an overpayment
  • obtain the payer’s bank details during the call. Key the refund to the payer and route the Refund for Approval intray to the SSO
  • take action to recover the consolidated revenue debt from the payee, see Debt repayment
  • record a Special recovery condition for the payee in the Recovery Conditions window to prevent automated collection of TRIPs, see Tax Refund Intercept Process (TRIP) Cuba Process Help

The SSO will:

  • approve the refund
  • ensure the final submission and approval notes are document in the refund notepad
  • finalise the SSO request

For intray assistance see Intray management Cuba Process Help.

7

Approved - consolidated revenue payment will not be paid to the payer (PSM, SSO, Service Officer) + Read more ...

The PSM will:

  • document actions required in the Con Rev Submission intray and route it to the SSO

The SSO will:

  • create a consolidated revenue debt on the payee's account - see Consolidated Revenue Debt Cuba Process Help
  • reassign the consolidated revenue debt to the receiving parent
  • check the payee overpayment summary suppression indicator and set it to Suppress to ensure the POS does not issue to the payee. See Payment options in Child Support
  • ensure the final submission and approval notes are document in the reallocate cash notepad
  • add a special contact detail (other) on the payer's profile reflecting that excess cash should not be refunded
  • document actions required in the Con Rev Submission intray and route to the Service Officer
  • finalise the SSO request and document the decision and outcome on the relevant notepad in the Communication window

For intray assistance see Intray management Cuba Process Help.

The Service Officer will:

  • contact the customer to advise the decision and advise the payer that the funds will be credited to their child support account to be used for future child support liabilities. If a customer requests the decision in writing, create a unique letter. See Letters Cuba Process Help. Note: customers do not have objection rights to the decision to refund or not to refund an overpayment
  • take action to recover the consolidated revenue debt from the payee, see Debt repayment
  • record a Special recovery condition for the payee in the Recovery Conditions window to prevent automated collection of TRIPs - see Tax Refund Intercept Process (TRIP) Cuba Process Help

8

Consider waiver + Read more ...

If a Child Support error contributed to the overpayment, consider if it is appropriate to discuss a waiver of the consolidated revenue debt. Advice can be sought from Child Support - waiver or Child Support Program Branch, who can review the case and consider if there are special circumstance which would suggest the Department’s support of a waiver. If the customer wants more information about the waiver process, the References page has links to policy about waivers.
