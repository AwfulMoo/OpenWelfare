Examples - early claims for Age Pension

Table 1.

Item

Example

1

Customer lodges claim for Age Pension and departs Australia

Robby lodges a claim for Age Pension 13 weeks before turning Age Pension age and then departs Australia for 6 months.

The Age Pension payment will start from the day Robby turns Age Pension age, provided the pension is payable (for example, payable under the income and assets tests).

To be payable in this situation, Robby must also satisfy the specific portability rules for Age Pension. For example, an Age Pension might not be payable if Robby:

  • is affected by former resident rules or had departed Australia permanently before the claims lodged/submitted, unless
  • Robby moves to a country Australia has an International Social Security Agreement with

For further information see the Exception to the lodgement inside Australia rule.

Examples - early claims for ABSTUDY and Youth Allowance

Table 2:

Item

Example

1

Early claim lodgement for Schooling A Award

Mary contacts on 27 September to lodge a claim for Mary's child, John, who will be commencing secondary studies in the new year. John's qualification start date will be 1 January; therefore, the earliest a claim can normally be lodged is 2 October.

Mary advises of going into hospital for a major operation on 28 September and is unsure about the length of confinement. Mary is anxious to submit John's claim due to the uncertainty of the length of the hospital stay. In this instance, discretion can be applied to accept the claim earlier than 13 weeks prior to qualification.

2

Early claim lodgement for ABSTUDY Tertiary Award

Sarah contacts on 20 November to lodge a claim for ABSTUDY.

Sarah will commence a university course which starts on 26 February. Sarah's qualification start date will be 26 February and the earliest date that ABSTUDY can be claimed is 27 November.

Sarah advises of an overseas trip, departing on 22 November until 10 February and will not have access to the internet. As Sarah will not be able to re-contact to lodge a claim until close to the course commencement date, discretion can be applied to accept the claim earlier than 13 weeks. This allows the claim to be taken when Sarah first contacts on 20 November. This will give Sarah time to provide any further supporting documentation for the claim and have the claim finalised prior to commencing studies.

3

Early claim lodgement to confirm ABSTUDY qualification for scholarship purposes

Felicity contacts on 1 August and advises that she has been offered a scholarship to commence secondary studies with a boarding school in the next coming calendar year.

Before the scholarship provider can finalise the offer Felicity must provide evidence of qualification for ABSTUDY Living Allowance.

In this circumstance, discretion can be applied to accept the claim earlier than 13 weeks prior to the qualification date. This makes sure Felicity is not disadvantaged in securing the scholarship on offer.

4

Early claim lodgement to access Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel

Alex will commence secondary studies in the new year. Alex's home address is in a remote location and Alex will be required to attend boarding school. The school requires prospective students to attend an orientation interview prior to acceptance. Alex's parent has received confirmation the orientation interview will be held on 17 July.

Alex's parent has contacted to enquire about travel entitlements for Alex to attend the interview at the boarding school. To qualify for Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel, Alex must meet the eligibility criteria for the Schooling B Award. The earliest a claim could normally be lodged would be 2 October.

In this circumstance, discretion can be applied to accept the claim earlier than 13 weeks prior to qualification in order for Alex to access travel entitlements to attend the interview.

5

Early claim lodgement from Outreach/Remote Servicing Officers

Where outreach/remote servicing teams are visiting communities and a customer lodges a claim with the team, however the customer's qualification start date is, for example, 15 weeks away, it would be appropriate to accept the customer's claim. This will assist in maximising service levels to this customer base and also ensure claim completeness prior to assessment.

6

Early claim lodgement for Youth Allowance or ABSTUDY not accepted

Scenario 1: Scott contacts in June to advise that Scott's child, Matt, is turning 18 in October. Scott is enquiring about Matt's ability to claim Youth Allowance now to ensure the transfer to these payments is smooth. Matt's birthday is on 17 October and the earliest a claim can normally be accepted is 18 July. Matt is currently in Year 12 and living with his parents.

It is not appropriate to apply discretion and take Matt's claim early (prior to 18 July) in this circumstance. There are no extenuating circumstances for Matt to be claiming earlier than 13 weeks prior to qualification. Matt should be advised to lodge a claim within 13 weeks prior to the date of qualification and that this will allow sufficient time for the claim to be finalised for a smooth transition between payments.

Scenario 2: James is commencing university in the new year. James' course commences on 26 February and contacts on 14 November to make a claim for ABSTUDY. James is anxious to have the claim processed due to leaving employment to commence the course and wants to ensure there will be no problems with the payments commencing.

James' qualification start date will be 26 February and the earliest date ABSTUDY can be claimed is 27 November. It is not appropriate to apply discretion and take James' claim this early as there are no extenuating circumstances for James to be claiming more than 13 weeks prior to qualification. James should be advised to re-contact within 13 weeks from qualification, allowing sufficient time for the claim to be processed before commencement of the course.

7

FTB to YA transfers - 1 January start date option

Some YA customers who lodge a claim between 1 October and 31 December have the option to request that their YA payments commence from 1 January, immediately following the date of claim lodgement.

If the customer elects to commence from 1 January the agency can pay the customer from this date as it is within 13 weeks of the claim being lodged. The YA customer’s parent will continue to receive FTB for the YA claimant until YA commences.

Examples - early claims for Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC)

Table 3:

Item

Example

1

Early claim lodgement for eligibility later in the year

Julio contacts on 30 May to lodge a claim for Julio's child, Roberto, as the family will move to an isolated town in late September and Roberto will start secondary studies by distance education in Term 4 of this year. Julio's earliest qualification start date will be 1 October, However, as this will be in the same calendar year as the claim lodgement date, Julio can lodge their claim at any date from 30 May to 31 December.

2

Early claim lodgement for AIC Boarding to confirm a place

Sabrina contacts on 27 September to lodge a claim for Sabrina's child, Tabitha, who will be commencing secondary studies and boarding in the next year. Tabitha's qualification start date will be 1 January. Therefore, the earliest a claim can normally be lodged would be 2 October.

Sabrina advises of needing to confirm Tabitha’s place at the boarding institution by the end of October. Sabrina is anxious to submit Tabitha's claim as boarding costs will be high and they needs to know if AIC Boarding Allowance will be approved before they confirms Tabitha’s place at the boarding institution. In this instance, discretion can be applied to accept the claim earlier than 13 weeks prior to qualification.

3

Early claim lodgement for AIC as overseas

Darren contacts on 20 September to lodge a claim for AIC for Darren's child, Endora.

Darren advises of an overseas trip, departing on 22 September and will not be returning until 10 January. Endora's qualification start date will be 1 January; therefore the earliest a claim can normally be lodged would be 2 October. In these circumstances, as Darren will not be able to re-contact to lodge a claim until January, discretion can be applied to accept the claim earlier than 13 weeks. This will allow the claim to be taken when Darren first contacts on 20 September.

4

Early claim lodgement from Outreach/Remote Servicing Officers

Where outreach/remote servicing teams are visiting communities and a customer lodges a claim with the team, however the customer's qualification start date is, for example, 15 weeks away, it would be appropriate to accept the customer's claim. This will assist in maximising service levels to this customer group and also ensure claim completeness prior to assessment.

5

Early claim lodgement due to special circumstances

Marg contacts on 15 September to lodge a claim for AIC for Marg's child Maggie.

Marg advises Marg is claiming AIC Second Home Allowance as Maggie has special needs and has been offered a place in the new year at a school with a specialised intervention program. They cannot accept the place without assistance with the second home costs. Maggie’s qualification start date will be 1 January; therefore the earliest a claim can normally be lodged would be 2 October. In these circumstances, discretion can be applied to accept the claim earlier than 13 weeks. This will allow the claim to be taken when Marg first contacts on 15 September. This will give Marg time to provide any further supporting documentation for the claim and have the claim finalised prior to accepting the place.

6

Early claim lodgement for AIC not accepted

Scenario 1: Ricky contacts in June to advise that Ricky's child, Matt, is starting distance education in the next year. Ricky is enquiring about the ability to claim AIC now to ensure the claim for this payment goes through before the start of the year. The earliest a claim can normally be accepted is 2 October. Matt is currently in Year 2 and living with their parents.

It is not appropriate to apply discretion and take Ricky's claim early in this circumstance. There are no extenuating circumstances for Ricky to be claiming earlier than 13 weeks prior to qualification. Ricky should be advised to lodge a claim within 13 weeks prior to the date of qualification (that is, 2 October) and that this will allow sufficient time for the claim to be finalised before the start of the year.

Scenario 2: Johnathan contacts on 30 August and is claiming AIC Second Home Allowance for Johnathan's children for the following year. He has not yet set up the second home. Johnathan is anxious to have the claims processed due to anticipated costs for the second home and wants to ensure there will be no problems with the payments commencing.

Johnathan's qualification start date would be 1 January at the earliest and could be later depending on the date that the second home is set up. It is not appropriate to apply discretion and take Johnathan's claim this early as there are no extenuating circumstances for Johnathan to be claiming more than 13 weeks prior to qualification. Johnathan should be advised to re-contact within 13 weeks from qualification allowing sufficient time for the claim to be processed before commencement of the course.

Examples - early claims for Parenting Payment

Table 4:

Item

Example

1

Early claim lodgement for Parenting Payment

Scenario 1: Kim lodges a claim on 1 June for Parenting Payment. Kim does not have any dependent children in care at the time of claim, however is expecting a dependent child (not a newborn) to come into care on 15 July. Kim can lodge an early claim for Parenting Payment as Kim will meet qualification for payment within 13 weeks.

Scenario 2: Jonathan lodges a claim for Parenting Payment on 15 February. Jonathan has not lived in Australia as an Australian resident for at least 104 weeks until 15 April. Jonathan can lodge an early claim as Jonathan will meet qualification for payment within 13 weeks of claiming.

Creating a customised early claim Fast Note

Letter - AIC free text for Q679

Use the Q679 to advise of the rejection and request any further information/evidence as necessary.

Letter Option:

  • Reject claim = 1
  • REJGE1 = 4 for free text as below

‘We cannot pay your AIC claim for [year] for this child as we cannot accept a claim more than 13 weeks before you qualify. To reclaim, please return the enclosed claim after 1 Oct [year].’

Optional sentence

‘Also, further information will be required to assess this claim. Please supply the forms/evidence requested below with your reclaim.’
