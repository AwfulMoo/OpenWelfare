For Prison Liaison Officers (PLO) only

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Referrals to PLO or Prison Servicing Unit for the relevant state or territory + Read more ...

To locate the responsible Service Zone for the prison, refer to the Correctional facilities Intranet page. To find out more about servicing arrangements with a corrections facility, contact the responsible Service Zone.

Referrals to start this process will be provided to the relevant Service Zone by the Debt and Integrity Project Branch via the Face to Face Strategic Support and Coordination Team.

Can the customer be contacted directly by phone?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Direct conversation with the customer + Read more ...

If direct contact with the customer is successful:

  • follow the phone contact requirements
  • confirm they are comfortable with their personal information being disclosed as part of the interaction

Suggested dialogue:
'Could I please confirm with you that this conversation is private and you are happy for me to disclose your personal information?'

Is the customer willing to provide personal information?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, advise the customer (suggested dialogue):
    • 'As this conversation is not private, I will only give you detailed information at your request. We need to refund money that you have paid back on your Income Compliance debt(s). In order to process that refund I need to confirm some information with you'
    • If the customer does not wish to participate in discussion with the PLO:
      'As you do not wish to continue with this conversation, the agency will try to pay the refund into an appropriate account or process the refund after you have been released'
    • Go to Step 9

3

Facilitated interaction with the customer + Read more ...

If contact with the customer is facilitated through corrections staff or a conversation that is not private.

Advise the customer that as the conversation is not private, their personal information will only be disclosed at their request.

Suggested dialogue:

'As this conversation is not private, I will only give you detailed information at your request. We need to refund money that you have paid back on your Income Compliance debt(s). In order to process that refund I need to confirm some information with you.’

Is the customer willing to participate in discussion with the PLO?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • Advise the customer (suggested dialogue):
      'As you do not wish to continue with this conversation, the agency will try to pay the refund into an appropriate account or process the refund after you have been released'
    • Go to Step 9

4

Explain their refund and class action settlement + Read more ...

Suggested dialogue:

'We need to refund money that you have paid back on your Income Compliance Debt(s). We are refunding this money to you because we have changed how we use employment income information from the Australian Taxation Office in Compliance Reviews. We no longer use the averaging of ATO information and will refund repayments made to these debts.

'You may also be eligible to receive a settlement payment from the Income Compliance class action. The settlement payment is in place of interest you could’ve earned on the debt repayments you made.

'Services Australia has identified group members and is categorising each debt. You will be eligible to receive a settlement payment if:

  • your debt was based partially or fully on ATO income information, or
  • our debt was recalculated based on information you gave us, like payslips, and you had paid back more than the recalculated amount

'People who paid us back more, and were without their money for longer, will get larger payments. The amount you will receive depends on when you paid money towards eligible debts, how much you paid, and when you receive your refund. We expect to begin making settlement payments in early 2022.

'As you are receiving a refund, you will likely included in category 2, which means you would be eligible to receive a settlement payment. You may dispute the categorisation of your debt if you do not think it is accurate. Would you like to raise a dispute?'

If asked about other Settlement Distribution Scheme Categories, see Categorisation of group members' debts.

5

Confirm their postal address + Read more ...

The Federal Court of Australia has approved the Income Compliance class action settlement.

Some customers may be eligible to receive a settlement payment. Services Australia will send correspondence through myGov, or by post to the customer’s last known postal address.

Confirm the customer’s postal address details where the agency can send settlement payment correspondence.

Suggested dialogue:

'As you may be eligible to receive a settlement payment, can I confirm your preferred postal address that the agency can send letters to.

'If you are unable to confirm, is there another person you would like us to contact, who may be able to provide postal address details?'

  • Yes, make note of the address details. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Confirm their existing payment destination is correct + Read more ...

Advise the customer:

'As you are not currently on a payment, I need to confirm your bank account details before the refund can be processed. Could you please confirm that the last bank account which you received a Centrelink payment into is still active and the refund can be paid into that account?'

Can the customer confirm and/or update bank account details?

  • Yes, confirm with the customer that the refund will be processed into the nominated account. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Ask the customer if there is someone who could provide the details needed to process their refund + Read more ...

If the customer has a nominee on the record, confirm the arrangement.

Suggested dialogue:

‘Can I confirm if you have a current nominee arrangement and that you are happy for me to contact your nominee to collect this information?'

Is the nominee arrangement still current?

  • Yes, and the customer:
    • consents for the nominee to be contacted, go to Step 9
    • do not give consent for the nominee to be contacted, go to Step 8
  • No, cancel the nominee arrangement. Go to Step 8

8

Nominee arrangement not current or contact not supported + Read more ...

If the customer does not have a nominee on record, check if there is someone else they would like the agency to contact.

Suggested dialogue:

'Is there another person you would like us to contact, who may be able to act on your behalf to provide bank account details?'

If you authorise for the refund to be paid into another person’s account, we will try to contact them to confirm if they agree to act and/or receive the payment on your behalf.’

9

Record the outcome + Read more ...

Record details on a DOC on the customer's record. See the Resources page for the template.

Obtain and record any nominee and authorised contacts as accurately as possible.

Procedure ends here.

To contact their nominee

For staff trained in compliance refunds

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Locate nominee details + Read more ...

In Customer Record, find the nominee and their phone number:

  • go to the Nominee Link Summary (NOLS) screen
  • from NOLS screen, ‘S’elect the nominee to go to the nominee’s record
  • go to the Telephone Summary (TDS) screen

Review nominee status

Check nominee status to confirm the nominee arrangement is current and should not be removed.

2

Contact the nominee + Read more ...

Contact the customer's nominee to:

  • confirm they are still the customer’s nominee and have authority to act on their behalf
  • advise them the person they are a nominee for is entitled to a refund and advise the next steps

Make 3 genuine attempts to contact the nominee on all available numbers. Follow the phone contact requirements.

If unsuccessful, do not leave a voicemail.

Record in a Note all attempts to contact the nominee. Include unsuccessful attempts.

Is contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, after 3 genuine attempts, forward the following details via a Quality Development Officer to DIP.BUSINESS.PROCESS.DESIGN:
    • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • Reason for the referral: Attempted contact to the nominee of the incarcerated customer was unsuccessful
    • Procedure ends here

3

Conversation with nominee + Read more ...

Tell them:

‘Services Australia is contacting you to confirm if you are the nominee for (Customer Name). Can you confirm that you are still the nominee and wish to act on behalf of the customer?’

Does the nominee still have authority to act on behalf of the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • DOC the conversation on the customer’s record
    • Escalate the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via a Quality Development Officer to DIP.BUSINESS.PROCESS.DESIGN
    • Include the escalation reason: Incarcerated customer’s nominee no longer has authority to act on behalf of the customer.
    • Procedure ends here

4

Nominee is still current and has authority to act + Read more ...

Tell them:

‘Centrelink has changed how we use employment income information from the Australian Tax Office (ATO) in Income Compliance reviews.

'(Customer Name) previously had debt/s raised using averaging of ATO information. We no longer do this and will refund the repayments that (Customer Name) has made to these debt/s.

'As (Customer Name) has not received a payment from us in a while, we need you to confirm or update their contact, address and bank account details. We will send you a letter with the next steps.’

Check the nominee's addresses (including postal address), and phone numbers (including mobile number) and record their current contact details using the Change in Contact Details workflow in Customer First.

Can the nominee confirm or provide the customer's bank account details?

  • Yes, see Table 5
  • No, if the customer:
    • has verbally confirmed the refund to be paid to the nominee, go to Step 5
    • has not confirmed the refund to be paid to the nominee:
      DOC the conversation on the customer’s record
      Escalate the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via a Quality Development Officer to DIP.BUSINESS.PROCESS.DESIGN
      Include the escalation reason: Incarcerated customer’s does not authorise nominee to receive refund
      Procedure ends here

5

Customer has verbally confirmed for the refund to be paid to nominee + Read more ...

Confirm if nominee consents to receive refund on behalf of the customer.

Obtain nominee's bank details.

Document conversation and details accurately.

Explain to the nominee: Centrelink will send them a letter confirming they have agreed to receive a payment of behalf of the customer.

To process refund

For staff trained in compliance refunds

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Is the customer on the phone? + Read more ...

Is the customer advising they have not received a refund, or responding to a Request to contact (Q888) letter?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, if a work item indicates the Prison Liaison Officer has:
    • record an authorised person or nominee to receive the refund, go to Step 4
    • obtained updated details, go to Step 5
    • confirmed details, go to Step 6

2

Undertake proof of record ownership + Read more ...

As well as the normal authentication process, ask the customer questions to complete proof of record ownership.

Is the customer’s identity proven beyond doubt?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No,
    • Offer to call the customer back on the phone number on the system
    • Record all details on a DOC

3

Confirm contact and payment destination + Read more ...

Tell the customer the purpose of the call:

Suggested dialogue:

‘Services Australia has raised your Income Compliance debt using averaging of Australian Taxation Office (ATO) income information. We no longer use the Averaging of ATO information to calculate debts. Because of this we a refunding any money you have paid towards the debt. Your refund will be paid into your bank account.’

Confirm the customer’s contact details and payment destination details.

Obtain the new details if they need updating.

Are updates required?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 7

4

Confirm refund be paid to an authorised person and/or nominee + Read more ...

Check that the customer has verbally requested the refund be paid to an authorised person and/or nominee:

  • Check the DOC generated by the PLO to confirm that the customer has requested that their refund be paid to an authorised person or nominee
  • Check the DOC generated by the PLO to confirm that the authorised person and/or nominee has agreed to act on behalf of the customer and accepts the refund to be paid as per customer’s request

Does the DOC confirm authorised person and/or nominee to act on behalf of the customer and receive the refund?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Update the customer’s details + Read more ...

Use Customer Summary to update details:

  • Select Process to go to the Task Selector (TS) screen
  • From the Task Selector
    • select the relevant screens to be updated, or
    • for all tasks, Select all tasks
  • Select Next to go to the relevant screens
    • To add new details, select Add icon, key the required details, Save
    • To edit previously keyed details, select Edit, edit the required details, Save
  • After all updates:
    • select Assess to go to the Entitlement (ELD) screen
    • from the ELD screen, select Finish
    • record the details in the finalisation DOC
    • select Finalise to return to the Income Compliance refund details screen

Are updates required to the payment destination?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Update payment destination + Read more ...

Check the type of update needed:

  • To add new details, select Add icon, key the required details
  • To edit previously keyed details, select Edit, edit the required details
  • To remove any lines, select Delete

Select Save to complete update.

7

Finalise Income Compliance Refund + Read more ...

This will:

  • zero the customer’s debt, and
  • refund any repayments the customer has made

To finalise:

  • Select Finalise to go to the Finalise Income Compliance Refund screen
  • ‘Have you confirmed all the customer's contact details are up to date?’ Y/N
  • ‘Have you confirmed the customer's payment details are up to date?’ Y/N
  • If the customer has a payment nominee, extra questions will display:
    • For voluntary payment nominees
      ‘Does the customer want to receive the refund in their own bank account?’ Y/N
    • For involuntary payment nominees
      ‘Have you informed the customer that the Income Compliance refund will go to their payment nominee?’ Y/N
  • Select Finalise
  • ‘Are you sure you want to finalise this refund? Once you finalise, the refund cannot be undone.’ Yes/No

When finalised:

Record details in a Note. See the Resources page for the template.

The Income Compliance Refund Activity will display a Status of On Hold with the reason Awaiting Further Action. No further action is required.

8

Unable to process refund to the authorised person and/or nominee + Read more ...

The refund cannot be paid to an authorised person and/or nominee either because:

  • the PLO has not recorded the customer’s request for the refund to be paid to an authorised person and/or nominee, or
  • the authorised person and/or nominee does not agree to act on customers behalf

In these cases:

  • Escalate the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via a Quality Development Officer to DIP.BUSINESS.PROCESS.DESIGN
  • Include the escalation reason: Unable to process refund as per Incarcerated customers request
