Review and appeal process

Table 1: This table explains the review and appeal process for Service Officers.

Step

Action

1

Is the Service Officer trained in COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program and have access to Organisation Records in Process Direct and Customer First? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, warm (announced) transfer the call to Technical Peer Support. Procedure ends here

2

Authenticate caller + Read more ...

Authenticate the applicant or third party. See Authenticating an employer.

Can the identity be confirmed?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, no information can be given/acted upon, procedure ends here

3

Request for an explanation of a claim outcome – business’ turnover was more than $20 million + Read more ...

Is the applicant requesting an explanation of claim outcome, as the claim was rejected because of the business’ turnover amount being more than $20 million in the 2019 calendar year (or for annual BAS reporters in the 2018-19 Financial Year)?

  • Yes, tell the applicant
    • Their claim will be referred to Austrade for a recommendation
    • Austrade will decide if the amount on the ATO certificate of tax information is based on Total Transaction Value (TTV). If so, they will reassess each business on a case-by-case basis to decide:
      - eligibility
      - the GST turnover amount to be used to calculate grant entitlement, and
      - the grant amount
    • They will be notified by letter
    • Decision by Austrade will be final. Once Austrade have made a decision, if the applicant still does not agree they can contact the Commonwealth Ombudsman
    • Create an ACTDOC using the Callback/ACTDOC guided procedure:
      Service Reason: Appeals
      Category: Nosub
      Item Name: Manual Action Doc Template
      Extra Details: Over $20 million ref EMP MGNT
      Document Content: Business’ turnover amount in the 2019 calendar year was over $20 million. Claim to be forwarded to Austrade for a recommendation
      Keywords: add CTSPNSO and delete ACTAPL
      In Work Optimiser the item should appear as ZIAM_EIR_CTSP_NSO
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Request for an explanation of a claim outcome – other reasons + Read more ...

Discuss the claim outcome with the caller using the Claim Assessment Summary and uploaded documents in Process Direct and Customer First Direct.

Is the caller satisfied with the discussion?

  • Yes, create a DOC on record outlining the conversation with the caller. Procedure ends here
  • No, create a DOC on record outlining the conversation with the caller. If the caller:
    • does not understand the decision and wants an explanation, go to Step 5
    • does not agree with the decision and wants a formal review, go to Step 7

5

Caller requests an explanation of the decision + Read more ...

If the caller does not understand the decision after a discussion about a claim outcome (other reasons), they can request an explanation which will be provided by a SME:

  • Open Customer Record and search for the record. Then go to Environment B
  • While in the business' CRN (second CRN), launch the Script Selector using the link at top left of screen
  • Key ‘Review’ in the search field, then run the Internal Review/Explanation script to record the request for an explanation
  • Select Add new explanation or review
  • Select Path ‘I am registering a new request for explanation’
  • Customer Circumstances: No (these do not apply to a business)
  • Administrative Details:
    • Source of Request: as per request
    • Date of Decision: date claim finalised
    • Payment Type the Decision has been made against: Ex-gratia One Off Payment
    • Disaster ID: C35 COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program
    • Process Type: Select the most appropriate option from drop down list
    • Decision Maker: logon of original claim decision maker
    • Which Office/Team made the Decision: TCH (regardless of who made original decision)
    • Date Decision Queried: as per request
    • Reason for Contact: Select the most appropriate option from drop down list
    • Special Case Indicator: leave blank
  • Original Decision: Rate or Rejection
  • Reason for Decision: Qualification not met
  • Reason for Explanation: Record the applicants reason for request in free text field
  • Transfer to relevant SME or continue Explanation:
    • Yes if the SME is recording the explanation and ready to finalise
    • No if the Service Officer is referring request for explanation to a SME
  • To which site do you want to transfer the request (if no selected above): TCH
  • Add Keyword: 'CTSPSME'. This must be added to make sure these work items allocate to a Service Officer with the skills to complete these appeals

If the providing the explanation as a SME, go to Step 6.

Otherwise, procedure ends here.

6

Completing the request for an explanation of a claim outcome - other reasons + Read more ...

Open Customer Record, and search for record, then go to Environment B.

To understand and explain the decision, use:

  • Claim Assessment Summary
  • Claim content and uploaded documents in Process Direct (attached to claim)
  • Uploaded documents in Customer First
  • Review precedent decisions as per Assessing and coding COVID-19 CTSP claims

If the applicant/third party is not on the phone, make 2 genuine attempts to contact them to explain the claim outcome decision.

Contact successful (or applicant/third party is on the phone)

Is the person enquiring on behalf of the business satisfied with the explanation?

  • Yes, run the Internal Review/Explanation script to finalise the explanation. Procedure ends here
  • No,
    • The applicant wants to apply for a formal review of the decision
    • For a SME completing the explanation and referring the decision to an ARO, update the CTSP Appeal Tracker spreadsheet with the action taken
    • Go to Step 7

If unable to contact

  • Run the Internal Review/Explanation script to finalise the explanation. The script will issue a Q449 letter advising that contact was unsuccessful and if they would like to talk about the decision they can contact Services Australia
  • When preparing the Q449 letter, select Program, and enter ‘COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program’ in Program Name
  • Procedure ends here

7

The applicant disagrees + Read more ...

The applicant disagrees with:

  • The claim decision because of the business’ turnover amount being over $20 million in the 2019 calendar year (or for annual BAS reporters in the 2018-19 Financial Year), go to Step 8
  • The calculation of payment using GST turnover amount, go to Step 9
  • Any other ATO certification information, go to Step 10
  • The policy, go to Step 11
  • The application of policy/decision, go to Step 12

8

Applicant disagrees – business’ turnover amount was more than $20 million + Read more ...

  • Tell the applicant:
    • Their claim will be referred to Austrade for a recommendation
    • Austrade will decide if the amount on the ATO certificate of tax information is based on Total Transaction Value (TTV). If so, they will reassess each business on a case-by-case basis to decide
      - eligibility
      - the GST turnover amount to be used to calculate grant entitlement, and
      - the grant amount
    • They will be notified by letter
    • The decision by Austrade will be final. Once Austrade have made a decision, if the applicant still does not agree they can contact the Commonwealth Ombudsman
  • Create an ACTDOC using the Callback/ACTDOC guided procedure:
    • Service Reason: Appeals
    • Category: Nosub
    • Item Name: Manual Action Doc Template
    • Extra Details: Over $20 million ref EMP MGNT
    • Document Content: Business’ turnover amount in the 2019 calendar year was over $20 million. Claim to be forwarded to Austrade for a recommendation
    • Keywords: add CTSPNSO and delete ACTAPL
    • In Work Optimiser the item should appear as ZIAM_EIR_CTSP_NSO

Procedure ends here.

9

Applicant disagrees with calculation of payment using GST turnover amount + Read more ...

Tell the applicant:

‘Grant amounts for this program are determined by G1 figures for the 2019 calendar year as at 14 December 2020. We understand that businesses can populate their G1 figure using different methods - some businesses report total turnover, others report gross profit or commission only. Regardless of the method used to populate the figure, the amount that has been entered in your BAS must be used for this grant.
'If you have made changes to your G1 figures after 4 January 2021, the new figures will not be reflected in the certificate issued by the ATO for this program, and are not considered when determining grant amounts for this payment.'

Ask them to check their BAS for the amounts recorded at the G1 question (excluding GST amounts).

Do they get the same figure as on the ATO certificate?

  • Yes, there will be no change to the application of this amount.
    • Any feedback or enquiries about the grant guidelines should be directed to Austrade
    • If the applicant wants to apply for a formal review of the decision, go to Step 13
    • If the applicant does not want to apply for a formal review of the decision, procedure ends here
  • No, Services Australia can only use the amount recorded on the certificate
    • Applicants should contact the ATO on 13 28 66 to ask why a different amount has been used. If they are seeking a review based on a different ATO certificate, they should lodge the updated certificate as part of their review request. See Table 2 for more details
    • Tell the applicant to either contact Services Australia and confirm outcome of ATO contact by phone, or lodge a new ATO Certificate within 10 business days. Otherwise, a review decision will be made based on information to hand
    • If the applicant wants to apply for a formal review of the decision, go to Step 13

10

Applicant disagrees with any other ATO certification information + Read more ...

They should contact the ATO on 13 28 66 to query the information on certificate. If applicant is seeking a review based on different ATO certificate, they should lodge the updated certificate as part of their review request. See Table 2 for more details

Tell the applicant to either contact Services Australia and confirm outcome of ATO contact by phone, or lodge new ATO Certificate within 10 business days. Otherwise, a review decision will be made based on information to hand.

If the applicant wants to apply for a formal review of the decision, go to Step 13.

11

Applicant disagrees with policy, as outlined in the grant guidelines + Read more ...

Any feedback or enquiries about the grant guidelines should be directed to Austrade.

If applicant wants to apply for a formal review of the decision, go to Step 13.

12

Applicant disagrees with application of policy/decision + Read more ...

  • For a referral to ARO, go to Step 13
  • For a referral to Emergency Management Programme (EMP) after an ARO review, go to Step 17
  • If the applicant or EMP is seeking an exceptional circumstances review by Austrade, go to Step 19

  • Customer Circumstances: No (these do not apply to a business)
  • Administrative Details:
    • Source of Request: as per request
    • Date of Decision: date claim finalised
    • Payment Type the Decision has been made against: Ex-gratia One Off Payment
    • Disaster ID: C35 COVID-19 Consumer Travel Support Program
    • Process Type: Select the most appropriate option from the drop down list

    • Which Office/Team made the Decision: TCH (regardless of who made original decision)
    • Date Decision Queried: as per request
    • Reason for Contact: Select the most appropriate option from drop down list
    • Special Case Indicator: leave blank

  • Reason for Decision: Qualification not met

The application for a formal review will allocate to an ARO. Procedure ends here.

14

For information on precedent decisions set by Austrade in relation to ‘exceptional circumstances’, see Assessing and coding COVID-19 CTSP claims.

See Reviews by an Authorised Review Officer (ARO).

Third party or applicant calls about an ARO review + Read more ...

  • If the third party or applicant cannot be warm transferred to the ARO, complete a Callback work item: with the following details:

    • Service Reason: Appeals
    • Officer: ARO
    • Type: Callback
    • Category: generic

    • Extra Details: COVID-19 CSTP ARO callback

17

Applicant has requested Emergency Management Programme (EMP) review the decision + Read more ...

The request for an EMP review must be lodged within 29 days of the ARO decision.

Was the request within 29 days of the ARO decision?

  • Yes,
    • Tell the applicant EMP will not contact them to discuss the review or tell them the outcome. They will be notified by letter
    • Create an ACTDOC using the Callback/ACTDOC guided procedure:
      Service Reason: Appeals
      Category: Nosub
      Item Name: Manual Action Doc Template
      Extra Details: Review [insert APL number] ref EMP MGNT
      Document Content: Caller is appealing ARO’s decision because [record reasons]
      Keywords: add CTSPNSO and delete ACTAPL
      In Work Optimiser the item should appear as ZIAM_EIR_CTSP_NSO
  • No, create a DOC. Note that the applicant has requested EMP review, but as it was requested outside the 29 days from the ARO decision date, no further review avenues are available. Procedure ends here

18

Completing the EMP review + Read more ...

EMP will use the following to complete the appeal:

  • Claim Assessment Summary
  • Claim content and uploaded documents in Process Direct (attached to claim)
  • Uploaded documents in Customer First
  • Explanation of a claim outcome, SME Review Fast Note and ARO DOC on the record

EMP will consider if:

  • there are exceptional circumstances (including businesses with a GST turnover amount of greater than $20 million on their ATO certificate of tax information)*
  • it warrants a referral to Austrade to consider waiving one or more of the eligibility criteria
  • there have been any other claims with similar circumstances previously sent to Austrade and if they are precedent setting. For information on precedent decisions set by Austrade in relation to ‘exceptional circumstances’, see Assessing and coding COVID-19 CTSP claims

EMP will not contact the applicant to discuss the review or tell them the outcome. Only a letter will be issued upon completion.

Should the claim be referred to Austrade?

  • Yes, go to Step 21
  • No,
    • EMP will create a Fast Note to record the review decision:
      Extra Details (Summary) Line: EMP (NSO) Review Completed
    • If the original decision needs changing:
      - EMP will send an email outlining the decision to the Level 2 Helpdesk
      - The Level 2 Helpdesk will assign it to a Service Officer for implementation
    • Update the NSO Review screen in the APL system record
      - Go to the Case List (ACL) screen
      - Select the relevant APL system record
      - Go to the NSO screen

19

Exceptional circumstances review + Read more ...

The applicant or Services Australia can request an exceptional circumstances review if they think that Austrade should consider waiving one or more of the eligibility criteria for a particular business. Note: any decisions made by Austrade may be precedent setting.

Has the applicant specifically asked for a review under the Exceptional Circumstances Review process?

  • Yes, go to Step 20
  • No, go to Step 7 to decide the next steps for the applicant

20

Referral of exceptional circumstances review request to EMP + Read more ...

Refer to the Level 2 helpdesk by completing the Payment Query Template. Give details of why the caller is requesting Exceptional Circumstances Review.

Add a DOC to record advising contact from the caller requesting Exceptional Circumstances Review.

The Level 2 helpdesk will refer the request to EMP by email.

21

Referral of exceptional circumstances review request to Austrade + Read more ...

EMP will complete the escalation email and refer to Austrade by email.

Has a decision been made by Austrade?

  • Yes,
    • EMP will send an email outlining the decision to the Level 2 Helpdesk
    • If the original decision is to be changed, the Level 2 Helpdesk will assign to a Service Officer for implementation
  • No, EMP will contact Austrade to expedite the outcome

Lodging supporting documents

Table 2: This table explains how to provide Services Australia with supporting documents for a claim/review.

Step

Action

1

Does the applicant have documents to provide to support their claim or application for a formal review? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here

2

Has the claim been finalised (grant/reject)? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Is the COVID-19 CTSP claim on hold? + Read more ...

  • Yes,
    • Tell the applicant to use the Upload Documents step in the claim process in Business Hub to upload documents
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Request Further Information + Read more ...

Reset the claim from submitted to on hold to allow documents to be uploaded in Business Hub again:

  • Select the red action button (red button with an up arrow) within the claim activity in Process Direct
  • Select Further Information Required
  • In General Information
    • Authority to Request: COVID-10 Consumer Travel Support Program
    • Information to be supplied within: 28 days
    • Required for: Incomplete Application
    • Staff Notes required: Yes
    • Staff Note: document why the application is incomplete
  • In Available Documents, move the documents that need to be uploaded to Selected Documents by selecting the >> button:
    • ATO Certificate of Tax Information (Business Validation) (ATO certificate) and/or
    • Peak Industry Body document (if applicable)
  • In Additional details box enter:
    • Provider number: Not Applicable
    • Note section: enter a brief description. For example, uploaded wrong document, document is not clear, cannot read the document, copy of ATO certificate previously lodged did not include certificate number in image
  • Select Finish

The claim activity:

  • is automatically put on hold once the Further Information Required process is completed, and
  • will not appear in Process Direct as a ‘submitted claim’ until the documents are uploaded and the claim is submitted again by the applicant

The applicant:

  • will need to go back into Business Hub to upload the required documents to the business’ claim
  • will not be automatically notified this action is needed

5

Lodging supporting documents when the claim is completed + Read more ...

An applicant or third party can send documents to support their application for a formal review to State communication.

The documents will be manually uploaded to the business’ Customer First record that has the decision letter (also known as the second CRN).

Note: Services Australia when assessing eligibility must use the ATO Certificate of Tax Information. Other documents cannot override the ATO certificate.

Has the applicant or third party sent documents to State communication?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, procedure ends here

6

Attaching supporting documents to record + Read more ...

First, save the documents from the email to File Manager.

Open Customer First Direct

Locate the business record. This is the CRN that has the decision letter and payment released on it (if granted). Also known as the ‘second CRN’.

Go to Workspace > Document Tools > Upload document button:

  • Date of Receipt: Date received by email
  • Form Type: CTSP001 (COVID-19 CTSP appeal supporting document)
  • Hand Off For Further Action: No
  • Add Note: Document received by State communication mailbox

Select the Browse button and upload relevant document.
