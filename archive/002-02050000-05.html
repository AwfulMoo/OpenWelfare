Historical asset tests

From 1 September 2018 to 11 June 2020, the farm asset test threshold was $5 million. The FHA assets test for this period included 2 components. Even if a customer qualified for FHA, it was only payable if both of the following assets tests were satisfied:

  • Non-farm Assets Test (Tier 1) included the value of all non-farm and liquid assets. Net values of each asset were used. The net value of a non-farm asset is calculated by deducting the value of any charge or encumbrance over that asset from the gross asset value. The thresholds are the same as the allowance assets thresholds
  • Net Farm Assets Test (Tier 2) included the net value of all farm assets. Calculate the net value of all farm assets by adding up the value of all liabilities related to farm assets and deducting that amount from the total value of all farm assets. The value of Tier 2 assets could not exceed the FHA farm assets value limit

Examples of a water asset held wholly or mainly for the purposes of carrying out a farm enterprise

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Example 1

A farmer runs a cropping enterprise and a forestry planation. Note that forestry activities are not considered a 'farm enterprise' under the Act.

70% of the land is used for the cropping enterprise and 30% for the forestry plantation. The farmer has yearly water allocations and uses the allocation for both sections of the business. 60% of the water is used for the forestry operations and the remaining 40% for the cropping enterprise.

As the water allocation is mainly used for the forestry enterprise (not a farm enterprise as per the FHS definition of a farm enterprise), the water allocation is not seen to be used mainly for the purpose of the farm enterprise and therefore no part of the water asset is captured by the Rule.

2

Example 2

A farmer runs a cropping enterprise and a forestry planation (note that forestry activities are not considered a 'farm enterprise' under the FHS Act.) and uses 70% of the land for the cropping enterprise and 30% for the forestry plantation.

The farmer has yearly water allocations and uses the allocation for both sections of the business (with the usage amounts reflecting the relative proportion of the different activities).

As the water allocation is mainly used for the cropping enterprise (a farm enterprise as per the FHS definition of a farm enterprise), the water allocation is seen to be used mainly for the purpose of the farm enterprise and therefore the entire water asset is captured by the Rule.

3

Example 4

A farmer runs a horticultural enterprise taking up 80% of the land, and small parts of the remaining land is turned into wetlands and income is derived from environment offsets.

The farmer has yearly water allocations and uses the allocation for both activities (with the usage amounts reflecting the relative proportion of the different activities).

As the water allocation is mainly used for the horticultural activities (a farm enterprise as per the FHS definition of a farm enterprise), the water allocation is seen to be used mainly for the purpose of the farm enterprise and therefore all of the water asset is captured by the Rule.

4

Apportionment of a loan secured by multiple assets

A farmer has a loan of $1.8 million secured jointly against:

  • farmland with a gross value of $1.85 million
  • a water entitlement valued at $700,000, and
  • a holiday home valued at $150,000

The combined total value of the security assets is $2.7 million. (That is, $1.85 million + 700,000 + $150,000 = $2.7 million)

To determine how much of the loan relates to each asset, divide the amount of the loan by the value of the secured assets.

(That is, $1.8 million in loans/$2.7 million in secured assets = 0.6667).

Hence, approximately 67% of the value of each asset is held as security for the loan. The value of each asset will be reduced by the amount of the loan that relates to that asset. In this example, the gross value of each asset will be reduced by approximately 67%.

Use the following formula to find the net value of the asset;

Gross asset value - (proportion of the loan that relates to the asset X asset value)

Note: use 4 decimal places

So the net values of the assets in this example are:

Farm land: $1.85 million - (0.67*$1.85 million) = $610,500

Water Asset: $700,000 - (0.67*$700,000) = $231,000

Holiday Home $150,000 - (0.67 * $150,000) = $49,500

5

Sale and Trade of Water

If the ability to sell or trade a water asset is an additional feature of a water asset that is held for carrying out a farm enterprise, the asset would still be captured by the Rule and so its value up to $1.1 million should be disregarded. (That is, the asset is not held wholly or mainly for the purpose of selling and trading, as would be the case if the same asset were held by a water trade company).

To ensure unintended impacts are accounted for when considering whether an asset should be captured by the Rule, the Delegate must apply the following test when administering the Rule:

Is there an identifiable purpose related to the carrying out of a farm enterprise?

If there is no identifiable purpose, which is related to the carrying out of a farm enterprise, the ability to sell or trade can reasonably be seen as a purpose for which the assets are held. In such circumstances, the asset should not be captured under the Rule.

  • The second requirement (a right or entitlement to access, hold, take or use water) is met for all of the assets listed above, due to the nature of the asset
  • The third requirement (used wholly or mainly for the person's farm enterprise) is met for assets 2-4 above - as the use percentages advised indicate the main purpose for holding each is the operation of the farm enterprise

Coding claims lodged before and after 5 April 2017

Where a customer and partner have both lodged a claim for Farm Household Allowance (FHA) and 1 claim is lodged before 5 April 2017 while the other is lodged on or after 5 April 2017, the customer and partner are assessed under the assets test applicable to the date of lodgement of their claims. This means there will be 2 different assets coding applied to the record.
