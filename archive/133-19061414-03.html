1

Eligibility + Read more ...

To use a Medicare online account, customers must be:

  • enrolled in Medicare
  • at least 14 years old
  • eligible customer in Australia

No Power of Attorney (POA) access

POA gives a person the legal right to act on behalf of a customer, but this does not extend to Medicare online accounts. There is no process in place for this access, so there would be no way of knowing who is using the online account.

Third party access to a customer's Medicare online account is a privacy and security breach.

2

Technical requirements + Read more ...

Customers need to use one of the following Internet browsers.

Online services - supported desktop browsers:

  • Google Chrome - 66 or later
  • Firefox - 52 or later
  • Internet Explorer -11
  • Safari - 10 or later
  • Microsoft Edge - 16 or later

Online services - supported mobile browsers:

  • Mobile Safari (iOS) - 10 or later
  • Chrome for Android - 66 or later

They also need to enable Cookies and JavaScript in the browser.

3

Set up access + Read more ...

The customer needs to create a myGov account and link Medicare services.

There are 2 ways customers can link Medicare services to myGov:

  • By confirming details on their Medicare record
  • Using a linking code given to them by a Service Officer in the last 14 days

Run the myGov Staff Linking Application to issue a linking code.

Note: if customers are unable to access myGov due to being locked out or no longer have access, the customer needs their email address released from myGov so they can use it to create a new account. See Release email address information on the Process page of Troubleshooting myGov.

There are 2 account types:

How customers cancel their Medicare online account + Read more ...

The customer needs to:

  • access Medicare services via myGov
  • select My details > Cancel Medicare online account
  • select the reason (common reasons are privacy or security concerns) and select Next
  • select either:
    • I will have to contact you to restart my account. Customers will need to ask a staff member to reactivate it if they want to use it again
    • I would like to restart my account online. Customers can reactivate it at any time by re-linking Medicare services to their myGov account

Go to Step 5.

3

How Services Officers cancel a Medicare online account + Read more ...

Service Officers may cancel a customer's account if requested by a customer.

Perform a security check.

To cancel a Medicare online account:

  • Log in to Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS)
  • Access online services:
    • Select Amend Consumer Details
    • Select Online Services from Program list
  • Customer details:
    • Enter their Medicare card number in ID Number field
    • Select Medicare Card Number from ID Number type drop down box
    • Select Entitlement
  • Complete the appropriate fields to end the online services entitlement
  • Select Validate > Validate > Update

By cancelling a Medicare online account using this method, the customer can re-link via myGov.

If the customer also wants to prevent future access, go to Step 4.

Otherwise, go to Step 5.

4

How Service Officers prevent access to a Medicare online account + Read more ...

Perform a security check.

To prevent future registration or access:

  • Log in to Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS)
  • Access Medicare online services:
    • Select Amend Consumer Details
    • Select Medicare from Program list
  • Customer details:
    • Enter their Medicare card number in ID Number field
    • Select Medicare Card Number > Personal from ID Number type drop down box
  • On the Consumer restrictions screen, select the radio button for the relevant consumer > Consumer Restrictions tab to view the screen
  • On the Add/Amend Restrictions screen, code:
    • Restriction Type: Access to Online Services
    • Action: Prevent Registration
    • Reason: from options listed
    • Channel: Face to face or Phone
  • Confirm amended personal details
  • Select Validate > Validate > Update

If they want to register or access the account in future, the customer will need to ask a Service Officer to enable access. Customers can do this by phone or visiting a service centre.

5

Account cancelled + Read more ...

The customer:

  • will not be able to access Medicare services from their myGov account
  • will not be able to use the Express Plus Medicare mobile app
  • can still access their myGov account
  • will receive Medicare mail by post, not in their myGov Inbox

Express Plus Medicare mobile app

Risks to a customer's privacy have been identified. See Separating Safely - protecting personal details to make sure the customer's personal details are safe before processing.

Set up and access

Item

Action

1

Set up and access + Read more ...

The customer will need to:

  • Create a myGov account and link Medicare services (see Medicare online accounts tab)
  • Download the Express Plus Medicare app from the App Store or Google Play
  • Set up the app:
    • All customers must enter their myGov account sign in details. This registers the app to their myGov account
  • If the customer is not prompted to create a myGov PIN, they already have a PIN for one of the below myGov linked apps:
    • Express Plus Medicare mobile app
    • Express Plus Centrelink mobile app
    • Express Plus Child Support mobile app
    • Workforce Australia app
  • App users may not realise the myGov PIN is a universal PIN for all 4 apps. They may know the PIN by a different name. If they change their myGov PIN, it changes for their other myGov apps
  • Agree to the terms of use for using the app

For more help, see Troubleshooting Medicare self service.

2

Checking access status + Read more ...

The customer must have an active Medicare online account to use the app.

Check the status of a customer's account on the Medicare Online Services screen in CDMS.

See Check online account status table.

3

Managing their PIN and the app + Read more ...

Customers can go to Account settings in their myGov account to manage their PIN and apps.

See Using myGov accounts.

4

Cancel or prevent access + Read more ...

The customer can cancel their Medicare online account at any time. This will prevent access to the app.

They can cancel their account online or a Service Officer can do it for them.

See Cancel account or prevent access table.
