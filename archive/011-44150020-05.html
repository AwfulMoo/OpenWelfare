Alternative Identity Assessment Questions

To authenticate a Medicare record, the system must confirm the following information:

  • Full name
  • Date of birth

The Service Officer should have the caller answer any 4 questions from the following list:

  • Can you please confirm your current address (as listed with Medicare)?
  • Can you please confirm your previous address (as listed with Medicare)? Previous address can be accepted as the current CDMS address. This will count as one correct answer
  • What is your Medicare card number and expiry date?
    • If the card is expired, the previous card number and the expiry date can be accepted as correct answer unless the previous card was reported lost or stolen
  • What is your partner’s full name and date of birth on the Medicare card?
  • What is your child/children’s name and date of birth on Medicare card?
  • What phone number do you have stored with Medicare?
  • Do you have Bank Details stored with Medicare? If so, please provide bank details, e.g. BSB and account number
  • Do you have an email address stored with Medicare and what is it?
  • Have you been listed on a different Medicare card previously?
    • If yes, can you list the full names of another people enrolled on that card?
  • Have you been known by any other name(s) e.g. name at birth, name before marriage, previous married name?
  • What is the name of a Medical practitioner you have attended in the last 6 months?
    • If there is no history and the customer states that they have not been to a doctor in the last 6 months, this is will count as one correct answer
  • What is the name of the last medical practice you attended?
    • This is evident on claims history
  • What is the location or address of the last medical practice you attended?
  • What was the date of the last service you received with a Medical practitioner?
  • What was the total cost of your last visit to a Medical practice (before Medicare benefit)?

If customer is born overseas, correct answer to the following question may be accepted:

  • Country of birth
  • Month/year of moving to Australia

If unable to be authenticated, go to the Unsuccessful authentication of a Medicare record table in Perform public security check for Medicare.

The alternative identity must only be used for the vulnerable customers where customers are unable to provide the identity documents due to the genuine reason. The normal identity requirements must apply to the customers who do not fall into this category.

Service Officers should select questions that fit the customer’s circumstances. For example, if customer is the only person on the card then questions like partner or children’s name must not be asked.

Transfer/copy a person or child to a new Medicare card - My Health Record script

Table 1: This table provides the script to follow when an individual requests to have themselves and/or a child/ren transferred or copied to a new or existing Medicare card. The scripts are designed to remind individuals to consider their My Health Record when family circumstances change.

Do you or your child have a My Health Record?

Advise the individual…

Yes
  • Any existing consents you have set to include Medicare information in your or your child's My Health Record will automatically carry over to your new Medicare card. Consents do not need to be set again
  • Consent to include your child's Medicare information in their My Health Record is only required from 1 authorised representative. With that consent, Medicare information from all cards the child is on will be included in their record, regardless of whether other card holders give consent
  • Anyone who already has access to your child's My Health Record or is granted access in the future may be able to see all the information included in your child's record, including Medicare claiming information and sensitive medical information
  • You can review who has access to your or your child's My Health Record online from the Privacy and Access page
  • If you believe a person is no longer eligible to have access to your child's My Health Record, call the My Health Record helpline for information and assistance

No
  • If you have concerns about your family’s safety, there are steps that can be taken to prevent another person from registering you and your child/ren for a My Health Record, and to protect your information. Even if you opted your child out of My Health Record, or have cancelled their record, another person who meets the eligibility requirements may be able to register them
  • If you have concerns about your safety or believe a person is no longer eligible to have access to your child’s My Health Record, call the My Health Record helpline on 1800 723 471 to discuss the protections and options available

Copy or transfer from one Medicare card to another

Table 2

Action

Detail

1

Transfer to a new Medicare card

When a person transfers to a new Medicare card, they are no longer on the previous Medicare card. For example, a child originally enrolled on their parent’s Medicare card who is 15 years of age or over chooses to have their own card and no longer be on their parent’s Medicare card.

2

Copy to a new Medicare card

When a person is copied to a new Medicare card, they remain active on both their new and existing Medicare cards. For example, a child who attends boarding school can have a card of their own and still be listed on their parent’s Medicare card.

3

Transfer to an existing Medicare card

When a person transfers to an existing Medicare card, they are no longer on the previous Medicare card and become active on the card they transfer to. For example, a couple chooses to be enrolled on the same Medicare card.

4

Copy to an existing Medicare card

When a person is copied to an existing Medicare card, they remain active on both Medicare cards. For example, a parent or a primary carer wants to have a child copied onto their Medicare card.

FAQs for customers inviting someone to their card online

Table 5

Item

Description

1

Question: Why can’t I see the link to Add someone to my card in my Medicare online account or app?

Answer: The link appears for people who are eligible to add someone to their card online. If the link isn’t on your screen, you’ll need to complete an Application to copy or transfer from one Medicare card to another form (MS011).

Service Officers: Check the customer’s circumstances against the eligibility criteria, then suggest updates where appropriate. For example, if the customer is the group contact, they could appoint a new group contact. See Eligibility to invite someone.

2

Question: Do I need consent to invite someone to my card?

Answer: Yes. You can get consent by simply having a conversation with the person you want to invite. You’ll also need their consent to provide their Medicare card details and date of birth online.

3

Question: How many people can I invite?

Answer: There’s no limit on the number of people you can invite but you can only have 9 people. This includes people currently or previously on your card. If someone enters an invite code you’ve given to them and your card exceeds 9 people, they’ll see an error message.

4

Question: Can I give the same invite code to multiple people?

Answer: No. Invite codes are assigned to one person only. If someone tries to redeem an invite code that isn’t assigned to them, they’ll see an error message.

5

Question: Why isn’t the invite code sent automatically to the person I’m inviting?

Answer: We don’t send invite codes to people to protect their privacy. Inviting and accepting an invite should be a mutual agreement between 2 people known to each other. Before you invite someone to your card, you will need their consent.

6

Question: How do I give the invite code to the person I’m inviting?

Answer: You can give the invite code verbally or electronically. You can select the copy icon (located below the code, bottom right corner) to copy the invite code and instructions to your device. You can then send this information using your preferred application such as SMS, Messenger or email.

7

Question: How do I view invites I’ve created?

Answer: You can view the last 10 invites you’ve created online, select My card, then Manage my card invites. We’ll remove invites when they expire, are withdrawn or accepted.

8

Question: Can I stop an invite I’ve created?

Answer: You can stop an invite before it’s accepted online, select My card, then Manage my card invites, then Withdraw invite. If someone accepts your invite and you no longer want to be on a card with them, you or the other person will need to move to another card.

9

Question: How will I know if someone accepts my invite?

Answer: You’ll get a notification on your Medicare online account homepage. If you have your Medicare letter delivery preference set to myGov Inbox, you’ll get a myGov message. You can also check your digital Medicare card to see if someone’s been added to it.

FAQs for customers who have been invited to an existing card online

Table 6

Item

Description

1

Question: What happens if I’ve lost the invite code?

Answer: Contact the person who invited you. They can view their invites online by selecting My card, then Manage my card invites. They can invite you again if the invite code is expired. The invite code disappears when it has expired after 7 days.

2

Question: Can I decline an invite?

Answer: An invite code expires after 7 days. If you don’t redeem the invite code for any reason, we won’t add you to the Medicare card. We won’t notify the person who invited you.

3

Question: Can I accept an invite from someone if I’m waiting for someone else to accept an invite from me?

Answer: No. You’ll need to wait for the person you invited to accept your invite. You can also withdraw the invite you created, select My card, then Manage my card invites, then Withdraw invite.

4

Question: The person who wants to invite me has my correct details but the app tells them the invite can’t be completed online. What can I do?

Answer: The app checks your eligibility to be invited online. If the invite can’t be completed online, you’ll need to complete an Application to copy or transfer from one Medicare card to another form (MS011).

Service Officers: Check the customer’s circumstances against the eligibility criteria, then suggest updates where appropriate. For example, if the only reason for ineligibility is being active on 2 cards, they may want to end their association on one. When the customer is only active on one card, the other person will be able to create an invite code. See Eligibility to be invited.

FAQs for Service Officers

Table 7

Item

Description

1

Question: Can I create, reissue, withdraw or accept an invite code on behalf of a customer?

Answer: No. Invite codes can only be managed by customers. You can:

  • help guide the customer through the online process, see User help guides, or
  • suggest they complete an Application to copy or transfer from one Medicare card to another form (MS011)

2

Question: How can I tell if a copy or transfer to an existing card was completed online?

Answer: The transaction ‘COPYTRANSFERTOEXISTINGCARD’ is recorded in the CDMS in ‘Personal’ and ‘Group’ under the History Summary.

3

Question: Can I process a MS011 form while someone has a pending invite?

Answer: Yes. Customers will see an error message if they try to redeem an invite code for a card they’re already on. If you get a MS011 form and can see that the customer is already on the card, you can check in CDMS if they copied or transferred online. See Question 2.

4

Question: What if a customer has concerns about someone creating an invite code?

Answer: Discuss the invite process and clarify the customer’s concerns.

Discussion points:

  • If someone wants to invite you, they’ll need to know your current Medicare card number, IRN and date of birth. They also need to declare online that they have your consent to provide your details
  • You won’t know about an invite unless someone gives you an invite code
  • If someone gives you an invite code, you don’t need to use it. Invite codes expire after 7 days
  • If you don’t use an invite code, your Medicare card and Medicare online account won’t be impacted
  • We won’t notify the person who invited you if you choose not to accept the invite
  • If you have concerns about someone using your personal information to create an invite code, we can discuss your options to get a new Medicare card and number

For customers with concerns about their safety, see Family and domestic violence.

5

Question: What if a customer advises they’ve been added to someone’s card without their consent?

Answer:

  • See Identity fraud for immediate actions to take
  • See Escalation process for Medicare eligibility if customer has further concerns
  • Send the Consumer ID and any information provided by the customer to the HDM Digital Service Improvements Project team
  • Advise the customer this matter will be investigated urgently

6

Question: What if a customer asks about someone who has been added to their Medicare card without their knowledge?

Answer: There is no change to the policy, anyone 15 years or older on the card can invite others to the card. The customer may need to have a conversation with the people on their card and update their card composition, where required.
