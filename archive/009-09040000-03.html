Managing mail

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Mail received + Read more ...

All mail received through the GPO Box is opened and date stamped by the CAPS Processing Team. The Service Officer rostered on mail duties stamps the mail received.

Internal mail and registered mail are delivered to the CAPS Processing Team and Smart Centre Western Sydney team.

Open internal mail, registered mail, collect faxes and print emails. Date stamp all correspondence.

Note: registered mail must be recorded in the registered mail receipt book.

Is the mail a new CAPS application form?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

CAPS application received + Read more ...

If a new CAPS application is received key a comment on the customer's CDMS record.

If no Medicare card number has been provided:

  • perform an ID number or name search to identify the customer. See Register a customer for Medicare in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS)
  • if customer cannot be identified contact the customer to obtain Medicare Card number.

Note: where the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander/South Sea Islander status field is blank, update the field as per the information provided on the CAPS application before proceeding.

Complete the following:

  • place all applications received for the day in a box with the DOL and total number received recorded on the box and place in the compactus
  • advise the total number of applications received each day to the distribution manager via email to the NSW Mail Distribution Centre. The Resources page contains a link to the Mail distribution centres
  • record application on CAPS WOH spread sheet, located in the CAPS Administration/operator's daily stats.

3

Other Mail (Service Officer rostered on mail duties) + Read more ...

If the mail is return to sender (RTS), go to Step 4

Action all other correspondence or forward to the team leader for relevant action.

4

RTS mail received and actioned by Service Officer rostered on mail duties + Read more ...

Date stamp the RTS mail.

Note: mail received and marked as 'not at this address', 'return to sender' or similar, must be actioned as returned mail.

If the returned mail does not contain a Medicare Card number, see Register a customer for Medicare in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS).

Open the customer record, see Step 1 in Accessing system table.

Review the address details in Group, personal or nominee fields as relevant.

If the address is different to the return mail address, check that the RTS indicator has been removed, if so forward the returned mail to the new address. End of process.

If address is the same

attempt to contact the mail recipient to obtain updated address details.

If contact cannot be made with the returned mail recipient, an attempt should be made to contact an alternative person. For example. if the returned mail recipient is the nominee then an attempt to contact the customer should also be made to obtain updated nominee address details.

Note: if telephone contact is successful and updated address details cannot be obtained then notification must be provided that the CAPS entitlement will be ceased pending further contact from the returned mail recipient.

If new address details are obtained, update the customer record, remove the RTS indicator and forward the returned mail to the new address. End of process.

If no address details are obtained:

  • the CAPS entitlement is to cease, see Ceasing entitlement in Continence Aids Payment Scheme - Customer contact, application, ceasing entitlement and review
  • Set RTS indicator on the customer record, see Step 5 for more information

Record a comment on the customer record, for example:

  • CAPS entitlement ceased due to RTS mail received from customer/nominee address. Unable to contact customer/nominee by telephone. If customer/nominee makes contact to update details, entitlement to be reviewed and may be reinstated. Refer to the CAPS team
  • Telephone contact made with customer/nominee - unable to obtain updated address details for returned mail recipient. Advised CAPS entitlement ceased due to RTS mail received from customer/nominee address. If customer/nominee makes contact to update details, entitlement to be reviewed and may be reinstated. Refer to the CAPS team

See Step 2 in Accessing system for more information on recording comment.

The SSO will respond once the escalation has been completed or if further information is required.

Issuing of letters

Table 6

Step

Action

1

Types of letters + Read more ...

The Continence Aids Payment Scheme (CAPS) issues external letters to customers in relation to their eligibility and payments for the CAPS.

The types of letters that may be issued to a CAPS customer are:

  • system generated letters - Statement of Payment for CAPS
  • standard Letter Template (SLT) - Claim ended or rejected Letter, Bank account and address details required, request for Information, review of decision and overpayment recovery (debt recovery). See public letters located on the Resources page.

System generated letter

The CAPS System generates a Statement of Payment for CAPS which is sent to a CAPS customer or nominee when the payment is sent to their nominated account.

Standard Letter Templates (SLT)

The following letters are prepared and issued to a customer or their legal/authorised representative using the standard letter templates:

  • claim ended/rejected letter - The rejection letter applies to applicants deemed as ineligible and the ended letter is issued to an existing customer who is no longer eligible to participate in the scheme
  • bank account & address details required - where payment is in error due to incorrect bank or address details
  • request for information letter - where an incomplete application is received and information is required in order to determine eligibility or process the application
  • debt recovery letter - where an overpayment(s) have been made to a customer
  • review of decision - where a decision has been reviewed.

2

Issue letter + Read more ...

Open the customer record, see Step 1 in Accessing system table.

Note: a letter cannot be sent to a customer with RTS indicator:

  • a letter can be sent to an alternative person associated with the CAPS (eg. where the customer is the letter recipient and they have a correspondence nominee recorded, the letter could be addressed to the nominee)
  • where an email address has been provided, an email can be sent requesting that they contact the CAPS team. Do not include personal information in the email. Where an email is sent the customer record or application should be noted and the 28 day letter process followed, see Step 4.

For standard letter template:

  • open the template, see public letters located on the Resources page, and complete:
    • the address and signatory tab details as appropriate
    • select reason as appropriate and complete the required fields
  • Note: the operator's P number can be keyed in the signatory name field.
  • select Ok
  • check that all details in the letter are correct
  • where large volumes of letters are required a mail merge can be used.
  • for the bank details letter, attach a copy of the Medicare bank account details collection form (MS013), see Services Australia forms on the Resources page.

3

Finalise letter + Read more ...

Print and check letter. If any details are incorrect update letter and reprint.

Photocopy the letter.

In the detailed CAPS Registration screen include a comment. See Step 2 in Table 1 for more information on recording comment.

Select registration to return to the customer's detailed registration screen:

  • record the correspondence details
  • select the correspondence tab
  • select the relevant letter type
  • key the date the letter is sent
  • select Save
  • select Registration to return to the customer's detailed registration screen

For bank and address details or request for information letters place a copy of the letter sent and other relevant paperwork in the 28 day pigeon hole to access at a later date in line with the 28 day process. Go to Step 4 for 28 day process.

For Claim ended/rejected and review of decision letters place a copy of the letter and any associated paperwork in the operator's daily batch envelope.

Send letter with any relevant paperwork.

Note: Service Officers are not required to sign external letters.

4

28 day letter process + Read more ...

Where letters are sent requesting information to determine eligibility or to process an application or update customer details (for example, updated bank account details, further information required) the letter, requests the customer to respond with the required information within 28 days.

Place letter and associated paperwork in the relevant pigeon hole date. The 28 day period is calculated on 28 days from the date of the letter, for example letter issued on the 1st of January, letter and paperwork placed in pigeon hole of 28th January.

Open the customer record in CDMS or CAPS as required, to check whether the customer's required details have been provided and updated.

For information:

  • received within 28 day period, remove the letter and copied paperwork from the relevant pigeon hole and add to the new information/documentation received from the customer and finalise the application
  • not received within 28 day period, remove all letters from the current days pigeon hole and reject the application

Make an appropriate comment on the customer record as required. See Step 2 in Table 1 for more information on recording comment.

Place any associated paperwork in the operator's daily batch envelope.

Determine if health professionals can complete health report

Table 7

Step

Action

1

Provider number required for health professional + Read more ...

Where an authorised health professional supplies a provider number, the CAPS system will automatically validate the provider details and populate the health professional's details in the health report screen. Where an authorised health professional does not supply a provider number, the health professionals details must be manually entered in the health report screen.

If a general practitioner has not supplied a provider number or the provider number supplied cannot be validated automatically, contact the general practitioner to obtain the provider number. Where confirmation cannot be obtained, manually enter the general practitioner's details in the health report screen.

2

Types of Health professionals + Read more ...

A registered or licensed health professional who is in a position to make an accurate continence assessment of the applicant must complete the health report section of the CAPS application form. Health professions listed on the CAPS application form are:

  • Continence, Registered, Community Nurse
  • General Practitioner
  • Medical Specialist
  • Physiotherapist
  • Occupational Therapist
  • Aboriginal Health Worker
  • Other

Where an 'other' health professional has completed the health report the following health professions are acceptable:

  • Nurse Practitioner
  • Clinical Nurse Consultant
  • Stomal Therapist/Stomal Therapy Nurse
  • Enrolled Nurse
  • Resident/Junior Medical officer
  • Hospital Registrar
  • Psychologist
  • Hospital/Psychiatry Intern.

3

Determine if 'other' health professional can complete health report + Read more ...

To determine if an 'other' health professional that is not on the approved list, can complete the health report:

  • search for health profession on the Ahpra website. See the Resources page for a link
  • check if the health professional is registered, if so then the health report is acceptable and application can be processed
  • check if the health professional is not registered, if not process the application in the CAPS system and record the assessment status as 'Pending'. Record a comment on the customer record, see Step 2 in Accessing system table.
  • request a review of the health professional by escalating matter to the Programme Management team, see Escalations to Service Support table.
  • If the health professional:
  • was approved, action the application as appropriate. See Continence Aids Payment Scheme application table in Continence Aids Payment Scheme - Customer contact, application, ceasing entitlement and review
  • was not approved, issue a Request for information letter to the customer requesting another health report, completed by an authorised health professional. See Letters table.

Making payment enquires in CPS

Table 8

Step

Action

1

Common payment system + Read more ...

Continence Aids Payment Scheme (CAPS) payments are generated in the CAPS system. Successfully prepared payments progress to the Common Payment System (CPS) for payment to the nominated account.

Detailed CAPS payment information is stored in the CPS which may be required to:

  • reprint a statement of payment
  • retrieve a Consumer ID using a bank reference number
  • confirm a CAPS customer's bank account details
  • determine a CAPS customer's payment status
  • generating bulk bank reference details report relating to organisations

2

Obtain bank reference number + Read more ...

A bank reference number is required to perform a search in CPS.

To obtain the bank reference number:

  • open the customer record, see Step 1 in Accessing system table
  • select CAPS Payment Enquiry button, the Bank reference number will display
  • these bank reference numbers are displayed on the CAPS payment statements

3

Obtaining data from Common payment system + Read more ...

The Payment Advice Reprint screen provides payment information. To access the Payment Advice Reprint screen:

  • log on to the Common Payment System (CPS) via SAP
  • double select the SAP Logon icon on your desktop
  • double select CPS
  • log on keying logon details and password
  • key or select the green circle with the tick at the top of the screen
  • key in ZREPRINT in the search menu or double select the Payment Advice Reprint from the Home screen menu. If this option is not visible
    • select on the top tool bar titled SAP Easy Access
    • select Menu
    • select User Menu. Note: if this option is still not visible the Service Officer may need to review your access
  • select bank reference number radio button
  • key bank reference number in first selection box
  • select execute on the top left of screen (displayed as a clock with a green tick)
  • select current button
  • the Payment Advice Reprint screen will display

To view or reprint Statement of Payment, go to Step 4.

To confirm locks on a customer account, go to Step 5.

To check if a payment has been reversed (written back), go to Step 6.

5

View or reprint Statement of Payment + Read more ...

To view or reprint Statement of Payment:

  • highlight the payment details line (select on the square to the left of the details) or click on the select all button
  • select show to view the statement on the screen:
    • select back button (green circle with white arrow) or F3 key to go back if a reprint is required
  • double select download as PDF to reprint the customer's payment statement:
    • a 'Save As' pop up screen will appear. Select the appropriate location and key an appropriate file name and print Statement of payment as needed.

Note: CPS will generate up to 2 payment statements (that is, 1 to the customer or correspondence nominee, and 1 to the payment nominee where appropriate).

6

Locks on a customer account + Read more ...

To confirm locks on a customer account:

  • double select Cont.Acct (Contract account number)
  • select Payments/Taxes tab
  • If a lock is in place it will appear in the Outgoing Payments box, next to Lock:
    • select yellow arrow to display lock(s)

Note: more than one lock can be placed on a payment.

7

Payment has been reversed (written back) + Read more ...

To check if a payment has been reversed (written back):

  • select the arrow in the green circle to go to the Payment Advice Reprint screen
  • select the arrow in the green circle to go back to the SAP Easy Access home screen
  • select ZFPL9 Display Account Balance
  • select the green circle with the tick at the top of the screen
  • payments that have been written back will appear with a status of ST in the DT column

Note: the following codes may display:

  • CR - payment cleared
  • Z4 - payment attempted
  • RL - payment returned (from the RBA)
  • ST - reversal/write back

A '0.00' in the bottom line of the table indicates that there are no CPS open items (payments) to be made to the customer.
