1

PPL eligibility + Read more ...

To be eligible for PPL, a customer must be the primary carer of a PPL child.

The primary claimant can receive up to 18 weeks of PPL. This is 90 Payable days.

Primary claimants can claim the full amount for themselves or choose to:

  • transfer some or all of their PPL period to another person
  • give permission for another person to claim some or all of their Flexible PPL days, if their child is born or entrusted to their primary care on or after 1 July 2020

The other person must claim, have primary care of the child and be an eligible secondary/tertiary claimant.

Most often, the primary claimant will be the birth mother or the initial primary carer of an adopted child. Another person may be eligible as a primary claimant only if exceptional circumstances eligibility criteria are met. For example, the birth mother is incapable of caring for the child.

Note: primary claimants in exceptional circumstances can claim up to the full amount of PPL if they are eligible. Secondary claimants who become the primary carer in exceptional circumstances are only eligible for up to the remainder of the PPL that is not claimed by the primary claimant. It will depend on how much PPL was paid to the primary claimant.

If the customer is:

  • the birth mother of a newborn child or the initial primary carer of an adopted child, see Step 1, Table 2
  • the partner of the birth mother or adoptive parent, the commissioning parent or the child's other legal parent or their partner, go to Step 2
  • another person, go to Step 3

2

Exceptional circumstances - primary claimant - partner or other legal parent + Read more ...

The partner of the birth mother, partner of the initial carer of an adoptive child, commissioning parent, the child's other legal parent or their partner may be eligible as a primary claimant only if:

  • they expect to have primary care of a PPL child for at least 26 weeks, and
  • the child was aged under one year when the person became the primary carer (or within one year of the date the child was placed for adoption), and
  • one of the following applies:
    • the birth mother or the initial primary carer of an adopted child is incapable of caring for the child for at least 26 weeks, or
    • it is considered that the person became the primary carer in special circumstances, it would be unreasonable for the birth mother (or initial adoptive carer) to care for the child, and it is in the interests of the child for the person to care for the child

Does the claimant meet the exceptional circumstances criteria?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, as there are no exceptional circumstances, their claim for PPL will automatically reject Additional Circumstances not Met (ACN). If the customer should be claiming as a secondary claimant, see Eligibility for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) as a secondary or tertiary claimant. Otherwise, see:
    • Eligibility for Family Tax Benefit (FTB), including the Newborn Supplement (NBS) and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU)
    • Eligibility for Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
    • Procedure ends here

3

Exceptional circumstances - primary claimant - other person + Read more ...

A person who is not a parent of the child (or their partner), may be eligible as a primary claimant if:

  • they expect to have primary care of a PPL child for at least 26 weeks, and
  • the child was aged under one year when the person became the primary carer (or within one year of the date the child was placed for adoption), and
  • the child was not placed in their or their partner's care as a result of a decision by a state or territory child protection authority e.g. a formal foster carer arrangement, and:
    • the birth mother (or initial adoptive carer) and their partner are incapable of caring for the child, or
    • it is considered that the person became the primary carer in exceptional circumstances, it would be unreasonable for the birth mother (or initial adoptive carer) to care for the child, and it is in the interests of the child for the person to care for the child

Does the customer meet the exceptional circumstances criteria?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, check:
    • Eligibility for PPL as a secondary or tertiary claimant
    • Eligibility for FTB including Newborn Supplement (NBS)/Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU)
    • Eligibility for Dad and Partner Pay
    • Procedure ends here

4

Exceptional circumstances met + Read more ...

If customer is claiming PPL in exceptional circumstances, evidence to support the circumstance is required as part of their claim. For example, proof of the:

  • surrogacy arrangements, or
  • condition resulting in the birth mother (and partner) being incapable of caring for the child for at least 26 weeks

Note: pre-birth claims for primary claimants in exceptional circumstances will display as ASS-PCB. If the exceptional circumstance criteria is not met, the post birth/add newborn claim activity will finalise with a PPL claim status of REJ-ACN.

If PPL claimants in exceptional circumstances meet all eligibility for PPL including the PPL work and income test, they:

  • can be eligible for PPL as a primary claimant, and
  • claim the full amount of PPL

However, if a customer claims PPL and meets the exceptional circumstances criteria, but have not met the work and income test, they may still be eligible to claim the remainder of the PPL which was not paid to the initial primary claimant. See Eligibility for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) as a secondary or tertiary claimant.

Has the customer met ALL eligibility criteria, meaning they can be paid up to 18 weeks as a primary claimant?

  • Yes:
    • additional coding for Additional Primary Claimant is required by a Families Override Officer. See Exceptional processing of Paid Parental Leave scheme claims
    • when override is finalised, go to Step 1, Table 2
  • No, the customer may still be eligible for the remainder of PPL if an existing eligible PPL primary claimant. See Eligibility for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) as a secondary or tertiary claimant

PPL eligibility - residency care, work and income tests

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Primary care - primary claimants + Read more ...

To receive PPL, a person must be the primary carer of a PPL child (or would have been the primary carer had the child not been stillborn or died).

  • If the birth mother no longer provides care, they are only eligible for PPL in the first 18 weeks after birth if they no longer provide care because the child:
    • is being adopted
    • due to a surrogacy arrangement
    • is removed from care by a state or territory child protection authority, acting under child protection legislation
  • If the birth mother is in prison they can meet the primary carer rule if they gave birth in prison and can care for their child in prison or, if imprisoned after the birth, they continue to care for the child in prison
  • If the primary carer is temporarily unable to care for the child, they may remain eligible if it is due to circumstances beyond their control and the period is expected to be less than 26 weeks
  • If the primary carer loses care of the child, they may remain eligible if:
    • care is lost without legal authority, or
    • care is disputed, and the customer takes reasonable steps to have the child returned to their care. This includes periods before a claim has been made
  • Adoptive and commissioning parents can be eligible for the full 18 weeks of PPL as the primary claimant regardless of any period the birth mother received PPL.

Temporary absences from caring for the child may be permitted, but are limited to the time necessary for respite care or other reasonable purposes

Are the primary care requirements met?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the customer would not be eligible for PPL. Check if NBS/NBU need to be assessed with the customer's (or their partner's) FTB. See Changing claim choice for a newborn or adopted child. Procedure ends here

2

PPL work test + Read more ...

The customer must meet the PPL scheme work test.

The customer may be requested to provide evidence to support the PPL work test.

Does the customer satisfy the PPL work test?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the customer is not eligible for PPL. See Eligibility for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and Eligibility for Newborn Supplement (NBS)/Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU). Procedure ends here

3

PPL income test + Read more ...

The customer must meet the PPL income test. For the PPL income test threshold, see Paid Parental Leave - income limits.

If the customer's actual adjusted taxable income exceeds the income limit in the financial year prior to the date of claim, a second claim may be lodged. For example, if a customer lodged prior to the child's birth in the existing financial year and the baby is born in a new financial year, eligibility can be tested for both financial years.

The customer may be required to provide evidence to support the PPL income test.

For more information, see Paid Parental Leave scheme claims income test and previous financial year income estimates.

Does the customer satisfy the PPL income test?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the customer is not eligible for PPL. Procedure ends here

4

Residence requirements + Read more ...

The customer must meet the Australian residence requirements for the full 18 week payment period.

The customer must be residentially qualified from the PPL child's date of birth or entry to care.

PPL may be paid while the customer is overseas during a temporary absence. See Australian residence requirements for more information.

Does the customer satisfy the residence requirements for PPL?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the customer is not eligible for PPL. Procedure ends here

5

Return to work - primary claimants + Read more ...

The PPL period cannot be paid if the customer has returned to work since they became the primary carer of the child until the end of their PPL period, unless for a permissible purpose, such as a ‘keeping in touch’ (KIT) day.

There are some exceptions that can be applied where the customer has returned to work.

Generally a customer must lodge their PPL claim before they return to work. There are a couple of exceptions to this rule:

  • the customer:
    • lodged an effective claim within 28 days of the child's date of birth (DOB), and
    • they have a nominated start date of the child's DOB or a date before they return to work, or
  • the customer is transferring the full amount of their PPL to another person
  • the customer:
    • meets the PPL extended work test or received COVID-19 Disaster Payment (paid by Services Australia on or after 3 June 2021) during their 10 month qualifying period, and
    • their nominated start date is before their return to work date

See Parental Leave Pay (PPL) customer returns to work for more information about return to work, permissible purposes and exceptions.

Go to Step 6

6

Primary claimant transferring their PPL to another person? + Read more ...

Customers can:

  • transfer some or all of their PPL period to another person, such as their partner or the child’s other parent.
  • give permission for another person to claim some or all of their Flexible PPL days if their child is born or entrusted to their care on or after 1 July 2020

Is the customer transferring some or all of their PPL period to another person?

  • Yes, for a:
    • partial transfer, go to Step 7
    • full transfer, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 10

7

Primary claimant transferring the balance of their PPL period to another person + Read more ...

For a partial transfer of PPL period:

  • the primary claimant must meet the full PPL eligibility criteria for each day of the PPL period between the child’s date of birth or entry into care date and the end of their PPL period (transfer date)
  • the secondary claimant must also lodge an effective claim for PPL and meet the full PPL eligibility criteria for each day beginning on their PPL period start date (transfer date) and ending at their maximum PPL period end date

The primary claimant’s application must be assessed before the secondary claimant's claim can be assessed. This ensures the secondary claimant's entitlement is assessed correctly once their PPL period commences.

When processing the partial transfer of the PPL period, update the Child in Primary Care (!CiPC) screen with the transfer details. To record the child as leaving the customer’s primary care:

  • Date of event: date primary care is being transferred
  • Carer: primary claimant
  • In Primary Care: No
  • Reason: Transfer unused portion of PLP to the partner or Transfer unused portion of PLP to another person

Note: the secondary claimant's PPL period start date is the day after the primary claimant’s PPL period end date. There cannot be a break in the PPL period. For more information on secondary claimants, see Eligibility for PPL as a secondary or tertiary claimant.

Is the primary claimant eligible for PPL?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, reject the PPL claim. Procedure ends here

8

Primary claimant is transferring their full PPL entitlement to another person + Read more ...

Primary claimants are only need to meet limited eligibility criteria when transferring their full PPL period entitlement to a secondary claimant. The limited eligibility criteria includes the:

  • PPL work test
  • PPL income test
  • Australian residency test

In cases where the primary claimant is transferring their full PPL period entitlement to a secondary claimant such as their partner, because they have returned to work, it is essential that both the primary and secondary claimants lodge their claims at the same time.

When processing the full transfer of the PPL period, update the Child in Primary Care (!CiPC) screen with the transfer details. To record the child as leaving the customer’s primary care:

  • Date of event: date primary care is being transferred
  • Carer: primary claimant
  • In Primary Care: No
  • Reason: Transfer full PLP Ent to Another Person or Transfer Full PPL Ent to Partner

The Resources page contains examples of scenarios for these types of cases.

When a primary claimant requested a full transfer to a secondary claimant, the Service Officer must process the secondary claim immediately after processing the primary claim. This applies to both pre-birth claims and post birth claims.

Has the secondary claimant lodged a claim for PPL at the same time?

  • Yes, finalise the primary claimant's claim first and ensure a status of:
    • for pre-birth claims, PPL claim status will be ASS-PCB.After processing secondary claimant's claim, secondary’s status will also display PPL/ASS-PCB
    • for post-birth claims (NSD is a date in the past) a PPL period status of REJ-TFR and PPL claim level status of CZR-FLX
    • for post birth claims (where NSD is in the future), PPL status will be ASS-ASD
    • before assessing the secondary claimant's payment. Go to Step 9
  • No, attempt to contact the customer and advise them there may be a delay in processing their claim as the secondary claimant must submit a claim for PPL before their claim can be finalised. Send letters to both the primary and secondary claimants. See Resources page for letter text.
    If a secondary claimant's claim:
    • has not been lodged, place the claim on 'H'old to await the secondary claimant's claim lodgement
    • has not been lodged within the required timeframe, the primary claimant's claim must be manually made non-effective 'OTH'er (NEF/OTH) on the PPL Claim Summary (P1CS) screen. See Resources for suggested text for the NEF Q999 letters
      To ensure the customer is given the appropriate number of days to respond after the letter is issued, resubmit the activity for:
      Day 22 (14 days + 8 days postage)
      Day 29 (21 days + 8 days postage) for remote or overseas postage, or
      Day 36 (28 days + 8 days postage) for direct credit and/or Tax File Number (TFN) details
      Note: claims that have been deemed NEF because the 'missing claim' was not lodged within the required timeframe can be re-opened when the missing claim has been lodged. See Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) for details on re-opening the claim

Once the proof of birth is received and post birth claim/add child has been completed on the primary claimant’s record and the NSD is:

  • not a future date:
    • if the child was born or entrusted to care on or after 1 July 2020, the primary claimant’s PPL period status will be REJ/TFR and their PPL claim level status will be CZR-FLX
    • if the child was born or entrusted to care before 1 July 2020, the primary claimant’s PPL claim status will be REJ/TFR
    • a post birth claim can then be completed on the secondary claimant’s record, resulting in a PPL claim and PPL period level status of PPL/CUR or PPL/ASS-NSD
  • in the future, the primary and secondary claimants record will finalise PPL/ASS-ASD. When the NSD is reached:
    • if the child was born or entrusted to care on or after 1 July 2020, the primary claimant’s PPL period status will be REJ/TFR and their PPL claim status will be CZR-FLX
    • if the child was born or entrusted to care before 1 July 2020, the primary claimant’s PPL claim status will be REJ/TFR
    • the secondary claimant’s PPL claim and PPL period level status will be PPL/CUR

For information about issuing requests and managing customer tasks, see Information requests for FTB, PPL, DAP and CCB lump sum claims.

9

Nominated start date (NSD) when transferring PPL + Read more ...

The PPL period start date for a secondary claimant is determined by the primary claimant. Secondary claimants cannot nominate a start date for the PPL period.

In their claim, the primary claimant must nominate the start date for the PPL period.

Full transfer: If the primary claimant is transferring all of their PPL period to the secondary claimant, the start date for the secondary claimant depends on the following factors:

Establishing the primary claimant's nominated start date (NSD)

  • If proof of birth and an effective PPL claim are provided by the primary claimant's NSD, the secondary claimant’s PPL start date will be the primary claimant's NSD
  • If proof of birth and an effective PPL claim are provided after the primary claimant's NSD but within 28 days of the birth/entrustment to care, the secondary claimant’s PPL start date will be the primary claimant's NSD
  • If proof of birth and an effective PPL claim are provided after the NSD and more than 28 days after the birth/entrustment to care, the date the proof of birth and/or the date the claim is lodged will be the primary claimant’s NSD and therefore become the secondary claimant’s PPL start date.
    Note: the NSD can be backdated to the child’s date of birth/entrustment to care date if the customer:
    • meets the PPL Extended Work Test (EWT). JobKeeper Payment cannot be paid for the same period the customer is claiming PPL. Confirm with the customer that they did not receive JobKeeper payment during the period they have claimed PPL. It is the customer’s responsibility to advise their employer of the start date and end date of their PPL period (not the dates PPL was received) if these periods overlap
    • received a COVID-19 Disaster Payment (paid by Services Australia on or after 3 June 2021) during their 10 month qualifying period. See Determining a customer’s Paid Parental Leave (PPL) period start date
      Note: Customers who nominate a PPL period start date in the past, will have ‘28D - Backdating the start date’ displayed on the Paid Parental Leave Exemption (P1EX) screen. This code is system generated for advice only. It cannot be applied by a Service Officer
  • If the primary claimant has nominated another NSD in the future which is a later date than the date proof of birth and/or claim is lodged, then the secondary claimants NSD is the future date

Date the child enters the secondary claimant's primary care

  • If this is on the date that would have been the primary claimant's start date, this date is the secondary claimant's start date
  • If this is after the date that would have been the primary claimant's start date, the date the secondary claimant gains primary care is the secondary claimant's start date. In this situation the PPL period for the secondary claimant is reduced by the time between the primary claimant's start date and the date the secondary claimant gains primary care

Note: when processing claims where a reduction of the secondary claimant's PPL period is likely due to the NSD selected by the primary claimant, the Service Officer must attempt contact/s with the primary claimant and suggest changing the nominated start date to avoid the PPL period reduction.

The secondary claimant's claim for PPL should be:

  • finalised at the same time, and
  • the secondary claimant notified of the claim outcome/status

10

Flexible PPL for children born or entrusted to care on or after 1 July 2020 + Read more ...

Primary claimants can give permission for another person to claim some or all of their 30 Flexible PPL days.

This can be done when they claim PPL or later.

If a customer grants permission for another person to claim their Flexible PPL, the secondary claimant needs to submit a claim and be found eligible for PPL. The primary claimant’s claim must be assessed before the secondary claimant’s claim.

The customer can remove permission at any time before the secondary claimant has claimed and nominated dates for the Flexible PPL days.

PPL claim status

The primary claimant’s PPL claim level status will be CZR-FLX until all Flexible PPL days have been claimed by an eligible secondary claimant. Note: this includes where the primary claimant has transferred their full entitlement.

This allows the primary claimant access to remove permission to claim Flexible PPL days up to when they have been claimed by a secondary claimant.

See Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave days.

11

Receipt of other payments + Read more ...

A customer cannot receive JobKeeper Payment for the same period they receive PPL. It is the customer’s responsibility to advise their employer the start date and end date of their PPL period (not the dates PPL was received).

A customer can select a NSD for their PPL period that is after their JobKeeper Payment period only where they have not returned to work. For more information about processing PPL claims for customers receiving JobKeeper Payment, see Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments.

Has the customer (or their partner) been paid NBS for the child with FTB Part A?

Note: FTB Part B is not payable at the same time as PPL. If the customer has already been paid FTB Part B and is now being granted PPL for the same period, the FTB Part B will be a recoverable debt. See Assessing Parental Leave Pay (PPL) claims for more details on FTB Part B.

  • Yes, the customer cannot be paid PPL and NBS for the same child. However, customers can choose to claim PPL after NBS has been paid. If the customer chooses to change their claim choice, the NBS and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) already paid will be a recoverable debt. Go to Step 12
  • No, go to Step 12

12

Proof of birth or entry to care + Read more ...

Advise the customer they must provide proof of the child's birth or verification of the child entering the customer's care if this has not already been given to Services Australia. This includes proof of a stillborn child.

Proof of entry to care for surrogacy

For children who have entered the customer's care as a part of a surrogacy arrangement, the surrogacy agreement should be provided to confirm the surrogacy arrangement between the birth mother and the commissioning parents and include the care arrangements of the child.

The agreement should specify who will care for the child and the date the child will enter the care of the commissioning parent. The document should also establish whether the birth mother has relinquished care of the child.

If a determination cannot be made using the information provided, refer the case to the Service Support Officer (SSO) via the Direct Referral to SSO webform. The SSO may then determine a referral to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk via the Online Form is required.

Before staff complete a referral they must ensure it contains all details of the surrogacy arrangement with any relevant documentation provided by the customer.

Note: surrogacy agreement referrals are only required as part of the post-birth claim process.

The Resources page contains links to the Direct Referral to SSO webform and the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk Online Query form.

Multiple birth

PPL can only be paid for one child in a multiple birth.

Exception: PPL can be paid if there is a significant delay (eg. 5 days or more) between the separate birth events for each child.

In these cases:

  • the customer must provide a letter from the doctor/midwife who attended the births confirming that the children were:
    • of the same pregnancy and
    • born in separate birthing events
  • use the Direct Referral to SSO webform and the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk Online Query form with the letter from the doctor/midwife to refer the case. The Resources page contains links

The separate birthing events could be less than 5 days apart, but this would be confirmed by the doctor/midwife.

Birth registration

To be eligible for a newborn child, parent(s)/guardian(s) must notify the department that they have registered or applied to register the child's birth with the State/Territory Births registry. Customers may notify this verbally.

Exception: This does not apply to stillborn children, adoptions or children born overseas.

Flexible PPL days for children born or entrusted to care on or after 1 July 2020

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Claiming Flexible PPL days + Read more ...

To be eligible for Flexible PPL days a person must:

  • have a child that is born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020
  • meet the eligibility requirements for PPL period, or would have been eligible for a PPL period had they not returned to work or transferred the PPL period to a secondary claimant

Customers are required to choose how and when they want to be paid their Flexible PPL days. Flexible PPL days can be:

  • connected to the PPL period, as a block of up to 6 weeks (30 days). This will give them a continuous block of PPL of up to 18 weeks. Connected days will be paid on the weekdays immediately after their PPL period
  • paid separately at any time after the PPL period and up to the PPL child’s second birthday / anniversary of adoption. These days can be paid on any day of the week, including weekends. The customer must select the dates they want to get these days
  • given to another person to claim, primary claimants can give permission for another person to claim some or all of their Flexible PPL days (such as their partner or the child’s other parent). The other person (secondary claimant) will need to claim, meet eligibility criteria and choose how and when they want to receive these days

For more information about managing and claiming Flexible PPL days, see Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days.

2

Eligibility to claim Flexible PPL on a day + Read more ...

Customers will tell Services Australia when they want to claim Flexible PPL days in their initial claim for PPL or at a later time online, by phone, or in person.

To be eligible to get Flexible PPL on a day the customer must:

  • be the primary carer of the PPL child
  • not be working (they can be on paid or unpaid leave). Note: exemptions apply for work performed for permissible purpose and Keeping in Touch (KIT) days. See Parental Leave Pay (PPL) customer returns to work
  • meet Australian residence requirements
  • have completed the Flexible PPL declaration

Customers cannot be paid Flexible PPL on any day that:

  • they or another person are being paid PPL for the same child
  • they are receiving or have been paid Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) for the child
  • is before or during the PPL period (if they have one)
  • is before the child’s date of birth/adoption
  • is after the child’s second birthday/anniversary of adoption

Customers cannot nominate a date that is more than 42 days in the past, unless they:

  • received a COVID-19 Disaster Payment (paid by Services Australia on or after 3 June 2021) during their 10 month qualifying period, or
  • meet the PPL extended work test

The Paid Parental Leave Exemption (P1EX) screen will display ‘28D - Backdating the start date’'. This code is system generated for advice only. It cannot be applied by a Service Officer. See Paid Parental Leave scheme Work Test.

For more information about claiming Flexible PPL days, see Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days.
