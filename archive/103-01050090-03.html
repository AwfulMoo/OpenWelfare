This page contains the process when an Income Management customer's income support payment is restored or re-granted. Portions of this process are completed by the Income Management helpdesk only.

Reassessment of Income Management

Restoration or re-grant for Income Management customers

Table 1: This table describes the steps when an Income Management customer's payments are restored or re-granted.

Step

Action

1

Restoring a customer's income support payment + Read more ...

When a determination has been made to restore an income managed customer's income support payment, consideration must be given to how this may impact Income Management (for example, automatic reinstatement of Income Management or reviewing priority needs).

Is the Service Officer Income Management trained?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, conduct all non-Income Management business before taking the following action:
    • Cold transfer the call to the Income Management line, Indigenous Services Line (if appropriate) or Multicultural Call (if appropriate). The Resources page contains a link to Locator for National Transfer Numbers. Procedure ends here
    • Seek assistance from a Specialised Service Officer (if available) or Service Support Officer, or contact the Access Support Centre (select Income Management option) for assistance. Go to step 2

2

Determine Income Management measure + Read more ...

Before coding the restoration, check the Income Management Summary screen to identify which measure the customer was eligible for prior to cancellation.

The Income Management Summary screen is located on Workspace in Customer First under the Income Management menu. All information relating to a customer's Income Management is located under the Qualification Details block, including:

  • IM Measure
  • IM Status
  • Community
  • LGA
  • Start Date
  • End Date
  • End Reason
  • Notice/Agreement - If the customer has an 'active' Notice, the Notice will be applied when the customer's payments are restored

Was the customer eligible for Voluntary Income Management (VIM)?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

VIM and restoration of payments + Read more ...

Customers must complete a mandatory period of 13 weeks before being able to exit VIM. Customers can request to exit VIM at any time after this period.

Has the customer been on VIM for more than 13 weeks?

  • Yes, and the customer:
    • is present, see Step 1 in the Updating the Voluntary Income Management Continuation Assessment screen table
    • is not present, Income Management will be automatically reinstated. Go to Step 4
  • No, VIM will be automatically reinstated when the income support payment is restored or re-granted from date paid-to +1. Go to Step 5

4

Voluntary Income Management customer not present + Read more ...

Restore the customer's income support payment.

  • On the Voluntary Income Management Continuation Assessment screen:
    • Customer Present: select the 'No' radio button
    • VIM will be automatically reinstated when the restoration activity is finalised
    • Select Continue
  • Check if customer is entitled to any arrears and that they appear correctly on the AR screen and are being 100% income managed
  • Select Continue
  • The Income Management Summary screen will display. Ensure all details are correct. Investigate, action and tick off any Work Items as appropriate
  • Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page
  • Record details on a DOC
  • The income support payment will be restored from the date of effect and a letter will be generated advising of restoration

Note: a priority needs assessment will be required at next customer contact.

If the customer contacts after their income support payment has been restored and does not wish to remain on VIM, they will need to request termination of their VIM agreement. See Disbursement interview for Voluntary Income Management.

Procedure ends here.

5

Automatic reinstatement of Income Management + Read more ...

When coding the restoration or re-grant of a customer's income support payment:

  • At the Assessment Results (AR) screen, check the following:
    • if the customer is eligible for any arrears
    • any arrears being paid are correct, and
    • the arrears are being 100% income managed
  • Select Continue
  • When the restoration or re-grant activity has been completed and it is within 13 weeks of cancellation, the applicable measure of Income Management will be automatically reinstated if the customer is eligible.
    Note: this does not apply to VWPR Youth customers who are income managed under the UTLAH and SpB categories. Income Management is automatically reinstated if it has been less than 12 months since first commenced, irrespective of when the restoration or re-grant occurs. For IM customers subject to referral measures SPaR, Child Protection or Cape York, IM will automatically reinstate only if payment is restored or re-granted from date paid to +1
  • The Income Management Summary screen will display. Check all details are correct. Investigate, action and 'tick off' any Work Items as appropriate
  • Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page
  • The income support payment will be restored from the date of effect and a letter will be generated advising the customer their payment has been restored

Was the customer's Income Management automatically reinstated?

  • Yes, see Step 2 in the Reinstatement of Income Management, priority needs review and arrears payments table
  • No, see Step 1 in the Reinstatement of Income Management, priority needs review and arrears payments table

Reinstatement of Voluntary Income Management (VIM)

Updating the Voluntary Income Management Continuation Assessment screen

Table 2: This table describes coding on the Voluntary Income Management Continuation screen when restoring or re-granting payment. Portions of this process are to be completed by the Income Management helpdesk only.

Step

Action

1

VIM customer is present + Read more ...

Restore the customer's income support payment.

On the Voluntary Income Management Continuation Assessment screen:

  • Customer present: select the 'Yes' radio button
  • The 'Customer to continue VIM' prompt will be presented

Discuss with the customer whether they would like to continue VIM.

Does the customer wish to continue VIM?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Customer requests to continue VIM + Read more ...

  • On the Voluntary Income Management Continuation Assessment screen:
  • Customer to continue VIM: select the 'Yes' radio button. VIM will be automatically be reinstated when the restoration activity is finalised
  • Select Continue
  • Check if customer is entitled to any arrears and that they appear correctly on the AR screen and are being 100% income managed
  • Select Continue
  • The Income Management Summary screen will display. Check all details are correct. Investigate, action and tick off any Work Items as appropriate
  • Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page
  • Record outcome on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

3

Customer wishes to terminate VIM agreement + Read more ...

  • On the Voluntary Income Management Continuation Assessment screen:
  • Customer to continue VIM: select the 'No' radio button. VIM will not be reinstated when the restoration activity is finalised
  • Select Continue
  • Check the AR screen to confirm arrears are not being 100% income managed
  • Select Continue
  • The Income Management Summary screen will display. Check all details are correct. Investigate, action and tick off any Work Items as appropriate
  • Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page

Service Officers must advise the customer of the following:

  • A 21 day waiting period will apply before being able to enter a new VIM agreement
  • They cannot end a VIM agreement more than 4 times in a 12 month period
  • Their income support payment is no longer income managed and they will need to make other arrangements to continue meeting their priority needs

When the payment restoration activity has been finalised, conduct the disbursement interview as per Disbursement interview for Voluntary Income Management

Procedure ends here.

Reinstatement of Income Management

Reinstatement of Income Management, Priority needs review and arrears payments

Table 3: This table describes the steps for reinstating Income Management under all measures including any manual reinstatement, review of priority needs and options available for arrears payments. Portions of this process are to be completed by the Income Management helpdesk.

Step

Action

1

Income Management is not automatically reinstated + Read more ...

Automatic reinstatement of Income Management will not occur in the following circumstances:

  • Cape York Initiative, Child Protection and Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) measures: the customer no longer has an 'active' Notice and/or there is a break in the customer's income support payment (restoration was not from date paid-to +1)
  • Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients (VWPR) - social worker assessed: The Vulnerable Notice is no longer 'active' and/or payment reinstated outside 13 weeks
  • Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients (VWPR) - UTLAH, Crisis and Special Benefit (SpB):
    • UTLAH and SpB customers who are now a full-time student or apprentice, or their 12 month Income Management period has ended.
    • VWPR crisis customers who are now a full-time student or apprentice.
    • Note: a new engagement period will apply if payment reinstated outside 13 weeks
  • Disengaged Youth (DEY) and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients (LTWPR): more than 13 weeks has lapsed since the customer moved out of the Northern Territory and/or payment reinstated outside 13 weeks
  • Voluntary Income Management (VIM): there is a break in the customer's income support payment from date paid-to +1

If automatic reinstatement does not occur and the customer meets the eligibility criteria for a compulsory measure of Income Management, the applicable ‘Initial Assessment’ workflow must be completed to reinstate Income Management.

Is the customer's Income Management being reinstated?

  • Yes, to reinstate Income Management, see:
    • Initial Assessment interview for Child Protection Income Management (CPIM)
    • Initial Assessment interview for Supporting People at Risk (SPaR)
    • initial Assessment interview for Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients
    • Go to Step 2
  • No, offer the customer a Disbursement interview to discuss how any residual income managed funds will be disbursed, and the options available to continue paying their ongoing expenses. To conduct a Disbursement interview, see:
    • Disbursement interview for Child Protection Income Management
    • Disbursement interview for Disengaged Youth (DEY) and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR) Income Management
    • Disbursement interview for Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients (VWPR) Income Management
    • Disbursement interview for Voluntary Income Management
    • Disbursement interview for Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) Income Management
    • If the customer does not wish to have a Disbursement interview, record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

2

Review expenses and conduct priority needs discussion + Read more ...

When a customer's Income Management is reinstated, Service Officers must:

  • Explain reinstatement of Income Management to the customer and advise them arrears payments will be 100% income managed
  • Advise the customer they can access a portion of their arrears payment as discretionary funds. Funds can be issued via:
    • Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) or Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) card from an arrears payment yet to be stripped, or
    • an Unrestricted Cash Payment, refer to Unrestricted Cash Payments
  • On the Expense Management Summary screen, check the 'Deductions - Partial & Unpaid' hyperlink for any unpaid expenses. Advise the customer of any unpaid expenses and ask if they would like any one off payments made to cover these expenses. Do not make additional expense allocations to cover any missed expense allocations unless instructed by the customer, as the customer may have paid these expenses from their discretionary funds
  • Review the customer's priority needs and confirm existing regular expenses or reinstate expenses as appropriate. See Determining a customer's priority needs for Income Management
  • Record the priority needs discussion via the Priority Needs Expense workflow. Refer to Changes to Income Management expenses
  • If a customer has moved out of a declared Income Management area, explain how this will impact Income Management. For more information, see Customer moves out of an Income Management area
  • Remind customers of their right to request reviews of Income Management decisions
  • Action any outstanding work items. For further information, see Work Items and messages for Income Management
    • Record details on a DOC
  • Confirm the customer's existing regular expenses as recorded under Regular Expenses on the Expense Management Summary screen
  • Check the customer's Regular Expense History on the Expense Management Summary screen for any expenses which may need to be reinstated
  • Do Not make additional expense allocations to cover those expense allocations that were missed during the period of suspension or cancellation, unless instructed by the customer, as the customer may have paid these expenses from their discretionary funds
  • Review the customer's priority needs. See Determining a customer's priority needs for Income Management
  • Record the priority needs discussion via the Priority Needs Expense workflow. See Changes to Income Management expenses
  • Remind customers of their right to request reviews of Income Management decisions
  • For customers income managed under the Disengaged Youth (DEY) or Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR) measure who no longer reside in the Northern Territory, advise customers Income Management will automatically end 13 weeks from the date they moved outside of the declared area
  • Action any outstanding work items. For further information, see Work Items and messages for Income Management
    • Record details on a DOC

Does the customer wish to receive a portion of their arrears payment as discretionary funds?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, procedure ends here

3

Determine method for issuing a portion of arrears + Read more ...

Lump sum arrears of an Income Management customer's income support payment are 100% income managed.

If a customer is in receipt of arrears, funds can be issued equal to the normal amount of discretionary funds they would have received for their last fortnightly payment. The process for issuing these funds will vary depending on if the arrears payment has already been delivered to the Income Management account.

Has the arrears payment been delivered and funds credited to the customer's Income Management account?

  • Yes, issue funds via an Unrestricted Cash Payment (UCP). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Determine amount of arrears payment to be issued and delivery method + Read more ...

Discuss with the customer how much they are requesting as discretionary funds, ensuring the amount does not exceed the normal amount of discretionary funds they would have received for their last fortnightly payment.

Calculate the amount of arrears payable as discretionary funds.

Cape York Initiative customers

  • Immediate payment payable =
    • net fortnightly payment x 0.35 (when income managed at 65%)
    • net fortnightly payment x 0.25 (when income managed at 75%), or
    • net fortnightly payment x 0.10 (when income managed at 90%)

Child Protection and SPaR customers

  • Immediate payment payable =
    • net fortnightly payment x 0.30

DEY, LTWPR, VWPR and VIM customers

  • Immediate payment payable =
    • net fortnightly payment x 0.50

Note: net fortnightly income = gross fortnightly payment less compulsory deductions, that is, amounts deducted for recovery of advance payments and Centrelink debts, payment of financial supplement loans, child support liabilities and tax deductions (including voluntary tax deductions).

Customers can choose a lower amount than the maximum calculation, however they must be advised that any later request for the remaining amount will be assessed under the Unrestricted Cash Payments (UCP) guidelines.

  • Determine the most appropriate method for the immediate payment, ensuring the request meets all usual processing guidelines. For more information see:
    • Immediate payment by payment method Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) and
    • Immediate payment by payment method Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) card
  • Explain to the customer there are no restrictions on how the immediate payment being issued can be spent
  • Go to the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen and confirm the customer's payment destination for Service Reason 'GPY' matches the customer's most recent payment type
  • If there are no details recorded, or the account number is incorrect, select 'GPY' to go to the Payment Destination Details (PAD) screen to update account details. See Changing payment destination
  • Record full details of the immediate payment request on a DOC, including percentage, calculation and delivery method (that is, RTGS or EBT)

Contact the Income Management helpdesk to request coding of the immediate payment. See the Resources page for a link to the Income Management helpdesk.

If it is outside Income Management helpdesk hours or a public holiday, contact the local Income Management Officer. See the Resources page for a link to the Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) intranet page. Procedure ends here.

Income Management helpdesk and staff with appropriate ICT security resource, go to Step 5.

5

Issuing the immediate payment via RTGS or EBT card + Read more ...

Income Management helpdesk staff with appropriate ICT security resource will code the immediate payment as follows:

The activity must be completed in the same payment system as the arrears payments (for example NSS, PEN, FAO).

On the Payment Summary (PS) screen:

  • Select the arrears payment from which a portion of the income managed component is to be paid to the customer via RTGS or EBT by typing 'G' next to the payment line
  • Press [Enter]

On the Issue Payment Select (IPPRE) screen:

  • the system will automatically 'S'elect Immediate/Arrears Payment for 'type of issue' and Default Destination (direct Credit/system Cheque) for 'destination type'
  • Update the 'destination type' field to Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) or EBT as appropriate
  • Press [Enter]

On the Issue Future Payment (IFP) screen, complete the following fields:

  • Issue Amt: the amount of the payment to be delivered
  • Reason: 'URG'
  • Finished (Y/Q/N): key 'Y'
  • EBT Card Num: enter the EBT card number as provided by the service centre
  • EBT Card Issue Date: date of issue
  • Notes: the reason for issue
  • Source: and DOR: fields
  • Press [Enter] to complete the activity
  • Record details of immediate payment on a DOC

When coding has been completed by the Income Management helpdesk and payment has been issued via an EBT card, the service centre Service Officer must ensure the card is activated and all usual processes completed before giving the card and Personal Identification Number (PIN) to the customer. For more information see Immediate payment by payment method Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) card.
