Due to changes in relevant areas deemed a COVID-19 hotspot since August 2021, staff must carefully check which of the 3 impact reasons and relevant area a customer is claiming under.

Customers claiming the COVID-19 Disaster Payment can claim under 1 of 3 impact reasons

  • L - live or work in a relevant area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement that is a deemed a COVID-19 hotspot by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer, or
  • P - were present in a COVID-19 hotspot at the time the restricted movement was declared and are now subject to a second public health order that restricts their movement in the state or territory they are currently in, or
  • I - live or work in a state or territory that has an area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer
    Note: for online claims, this shows as ‘I am impacted by a COVID-19 restricted movement/lockdown, but do not live, work and was not present in a COVID-19 hotspot

For relevant periods 14 and 15, customers will only be able to apply:

  • if they received payment ($750/$450) for relevant period 13 and continue to meet the eligibility criteria
  • under 1 impact reason:
    • F - received the final payment that was released in the relevant period in which the 80% target was met. Note: while this is not 1 of the 3 impact reasons a customer can claim under for earlier relevant periods, for the 2 final relevant periods, it is the only option a customer can claim under. The customer must have previously received a payment for 1 of the 3 impact reasons to receive a payment for the 2 final relevant periods

Geospatial tool

Due to changes in the relevant areas deemed a COVID-19 hotspot for different parts of Victoria since August 2021, staff must use this tool to carefully check the relevant area a customer is claiming under to determine if they live, work or were present in a COVID-19 hotspot area for the relevant period.

Note: for customers outside a COVID-19 hotspot, staff need to check the residential and/or employer addresses provided for the relevant period are in Victoria.

To assess if a customer lives, works or was present in a relevant area:

  • Open the VIC Live Feed App
  • Place the address given by the customer in the search box. Residential, employer and/or present in addresses may need to be checked
  • Select the correct address that populates in the drop down

When using the Victoria geospatial tool:
- blue are areas deemed hotspots
- red are areas not deemed hotspots
Select the layer based on the relevant period the customer is claiming. The colours will help staff work out which impact reason the customer can claim.
The colours do not represent which workflow to use. Go to Check the relevant area so the correct workflow is run below on how to select the correct workflow.

Before all of Victoria was deemed a COVID-19 hotspot (any claim lodged for Relevant Periods 1 and 2):

  • Select only RP 1-2: COVID-19 Disaster Payment August 2021 in the Layers menu on the right hand side. Do not select any other map
  • If claiming for a relevant area in Greater Melbourne/Golden Plains, the address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible to receive a payment

When all of Victoria was deemed a COVID-19 hotspot (any claim lodged for Relevant Periods 3 to 5):

  • Select only RP 3-5: COVID-19 Disaster Payment 22 August 2021 in the Layers menu on the right hand side. Do not select any other map
  • The address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible

When only Greater Melbourne, Greater Shepparton and Ballarat were deemed a COVID-19 hotspot (any claim lodged for Relevant Period 6):

  • Select only RP 6: COVID-19 Disaster Payment September 2021 in the Layers menu on the right hand side. Do not select any other map
  • If claiming for a relevant area in Greater Melbourne or for Greater Shepparton or Ballarat, the address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible to receive a payment

When only Greater Melbourne, Ballarat, Greater Geelong, Mitchell and Surf Coast were deemed a COVID-19 hotspot (any claim lodged for Relevant Period 7):

  • Select only RP 7: COVID-19 Disaster Payment September 2021 in the Layers menu on the right hand side. Do not select any other map
  • If claiming for a relevant area in Greater Melbourne, Ballarat, Greater Geelong, Mitchell or Surf Coast the address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible to receive a payment

When only Greater Melbourne, Greater Geelong, Mitchell, Surf Coast and LaTrobe were deemed a COVID-19 hotspot (any claim lodged for Relevant Period 8):

  • Select only RP 8: COVID-19 Disaster Payment September/October 2021 in the Layers menu on the right hand side. Do not select any other map
  • If claiming for a relevant area in Greater Melbourne, Greater Geelong, Mitchell, Surf Coast or LaTrobe the address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible to receive a payment

When only Greater Melbourne, Greater Shepparton, Mitchell, LaTrobe and Moorabool were deemed a COVID-19 hotspot (any claim lodged for Relevant Period 9):

  • Select only RP 9: COVID-19 Disaster Payment October 2021 in the Layers menu on the right hand side. Do not select any other map
  • If claiming for a relevant area in Greater Melbourne, Greater Shepparton, Mitchell, LaTrobe or Moorabool the address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible to receive a payment

When only Greater Melbourne, Greater Shepparton, Mildura, Mitchell and Moorabool were deemed a COVID-19 hotspot (any claim lodged for Relevant Period 10):

  • Select only RP 10: COVID-19 Disaster Payment October 2021 in the Layers menu on the right hand side. Do not select any other map
  • If claiming for a relevant area in Greater Melbourne, Greater Shepparton, Mildura, Mitchell, or Moorabool the address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible to receive a payment

When only Greater Melbourne and Mildura were deemed a COVID-19 hotspot (any claim lodged for Relevant Period 11):

  • Select only RP 11: COVID-19 Disaster Payment October 2021 in the Layers menu on the right hand side. Do not select any other map
  • If claiming for a relevant area in Greater Melbourne and Mildura the address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible to receive a payment

When only Greater Melbourne was deemed a COVID-19 hotspot (any claim lodged for Relevant Period 12 onwards):

  • Select only RP 12: COVID-19 Disaster Payment October 2021 in the Layers menu on the right hand side. Do not select any other map
  • If claiming for a relevant area in Greater Melbourne the address must lie in the blue shaded area to be eligible to receive a payment

Check the relevant area so the correct workflow is run:

  • Staff must check the relevant area by selecting:
    • the small bright blue box, then

  • The relevant area will show in bold at the top of the pop up box as follows:
    • RP 12 October 2021 map
      -Relevant Area: Greater Melbourne, or
      -Relevant Area: Regional Victoria
    • RP 11 October 2021 map
      -Relevant Area: Greater Melbourne, or
      -Relevant Area: Regional Victoria
    • RP 10 October 2021 map
      -Relevant Area: Greater Melbourne, or
      -Relevant Area: Regional Victoria
    • RP 9 October 2021 map
      -Relevant Area: Greater Melbourne, or
      -Relevant Area: Regional Victoria
    • RP 8 September/October 2021 map
      -Relevant Area: Greater Melbourne, or
      -Relevant Area: Regional Victoria
    • RP 7 September 2021 map
      -Relevant Area: Greater Melbourne, or
      -Relevant Area: Regional Victoria
    • RP 6 September 2021 map
      -Relevant Area: Greater Melbourne, or
      -Relevant Area: Regional Victoria
    • RP 3-5 22 August 2021 map
      - Relevant Area: Greater Melbourne and Golden Plains      , or
      - Relevant Area: Regional Victoria
    • RP 1-2 August 2021 map
      - Relevant Area: Hotspot LGA       (for Greater Melbourne/Golden Plains), or
      - Relevant Area: Other Areas of VIC (for Regional Victoria)
  • The Local Government Area (LGA) where the address is located will show below the Relevant Area
  • To make sure the correct workflow is selected, check the relevant area that shows in the pop up box:
    • For relevant periods 1 to 5 Greater Melbourne and Golden Plains/Hotspot LGA, select the workflow with Greater Melb/Golden Plns
    • For relevant period 6 onwards Greater Melbourne, select the workflow with Greater Melbourne

  • Continue to assess the claim

Scenarios - eligibility

Scenario

Outcome

Unable to work due to restricted movement

The customer works in a café in Melbourne CBD as a casual with on average of 30 hrs per week.

Due to the state public health order for a restricted movement, the customer has been unable to attend their workplace for the period of the restricted movement. The restricted movement was for 7 days.

The customer has insufficient appropriate leave to cover the restricted movement period.

Eligible

The state public health order for a stay at home restricted movement was for 7 days.

The customer was unable to attend work from the first day to the fifth day of the restricted movement and would otherwise have worked 30 hours per week.

The customer has insufficient appropriate leave to cover the period for the restricted movement.

The customer is paid $750.

The customer will receive automated payments for the remaining periods unless there is a change in circumstances.

Once Victoria reached 70% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older:

  • automatic payments stopped, and
  • the customer will lodge a new claim for each relevant period
    • they can use the abridged claim process to lodge their claim

Once Victoria reached 80% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, payments will reduce for 2 weeks before stopping. The customer will receive:

  • Relevant period 14: $450
  • Relevant period 15: $320

Unable to work due to restricted movement

The customer is an essential worker living and working in a declared COVID-19 hotspot.

The customer has 2 children, aged 4 and 7, who ordinarily attend child care while the customer is at work. Due to the restricted movement conditions, the customer’s child care centre has closed. The customer is unable to perform their work from home and unable to earn income for the period of the restricted movement.

The customer would normally work 7 hour shifts 5 times per week.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer is eligible for the at least 20 hours or more rate of payment, as they as have lost 35 hours of work in the relevant period.

The customer's rate of payment will be $750.

Paid leave for part of restricted movement period

Due to the state public health order for a restricted movement, the customer has been unable to attend their workplace for the period of the restricted movement. The restricted movement was 7 days.

The customer has been paid 2 days of special COVID-19 leave by their employer starting from the first day of the restricted movement.

The customer is not receiving an income support payment or a state/commonwealth based pandemic payment.

Eligible

The state public health order for a stay at home restricted movement was for 7 days.

The customer was unable to attend work from the eighth day to the fifth day of the restricted movement. Although they have been paid 2 days leave from their employer, it is not sufficient to cover the entire period of the restricted movement. The customer would have worked more than 20 hours.

The customer is paid $750 - the payment is not pro-rata.

Self-employed customer

The customer is a self-employed gardener who works full-time at various homes in their area.

Due to the state public health order for a restricted movement’, the customer has been unable to attend work, unable to work from home and unable to earn an income for the period of the restricted movement. The restricted movement was for 7 days.

The customer is not receiving an income support payment or a state/commonwealth based pandemic payment or a small business payment.

Eligible

The customer is not receiving an income support payment or a state/commonwealth based pandemic payment.

The customer has lost more than 20 hours of work. The customer is paid $750.

Taxi drivers

The customer is a taxi driver.

The customer estimates they have lost 7 hours per day of work because of the reduced demand for taxi driver services during the restricted movement.

They would normally work 8 hours per day, 6 days per week.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer is eligible for the at least 20 hours or more rate of payment, as they as have lost 42 hours of work in the relevant period.

The customer’s rate of payment will be $750.

The customer will receive automated payments for the remaining periods unless there is a change in circumstances.

Once Victoria reaches 70% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older:

  • the customer will lodge a new claim for each relevant period
    • they can use the abridged claim process to lodge their claim

Lives or works in a state or territory where relevant areas have been released from a state public health order for restricted movement.

A customer works 40 hours per week on a building site in Warrnambool. From 21 August 2021, Warrnambool was declared a relevant area:

  • subject to a state public health order for restricted movement
  • deemed a COVID-19 hotspot

This resulted in the customer being stood down from work pending the end of the restricted movement order.

When the restricted movement order was lifted on 9 September 2021, the customer returned to work on the building site. However, their employer was only able to offer them 18 hours of work per week.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer is eligible:

  • to receive the $750 rate of payment as they have lost 40 hours of work in relevant period 3 and they meet the criteria ‘live or work in a state or territory subject to a state public health order for restricted movement by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer’

The customer will receive $750 for relevant period 3 and receive automatic payments of $750 for relevant periods 4 and 5.

As the restricted movement order stopped on 9 September 2021, the customer’s automatic payments stopped.

From relevant period 6 onwards, the customer is eligible:

  • to receive the $750 rate of payment as they have lost 22 hours of work and they meet the criteria ‘live or work in a state or territory that has an area subject to a state public health order for restricted movement by the Commonwealth Chief Medical Officer’
    • Note: for online claims, this shows as ‘I am impacted by a COVID-19 restricted movement, but do not live, work and was not present in a COVID-19 hotspot area’

To get further payments the customer will need to lodge a new claim and then will receive automatic payments for all subsequent relevant periods, unless they advise of a change of circumstanced.

Note: not all states/territories offer the COVID-19 Disaster Payment for people living or working in relevant areas not deemed a COVID-19 hotspot. Staff must refer to the eligibility for each event.

The customer will receive automated payments for the remaining periods unless there is a change in circumstances.

Once Victoria reached 70% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older:

  • automatic payments stopped, and
  • the customer will lodge a new claim for each relevant period
    • they can use the abridged claim process to lodge their claim

Once Victoria reached 80% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, payments will reduce for 2 weeks before stopping. The customer will receive:

  • Relevant period 14: $450
  • Relevant period 15: $320

Customer is not vaccinated against COVID-19 and their employer does not allow them to attend work

The customer usually works 38 hours per week and lives in a declared COVID-19 hotspot.

The customer has been told by their employer they cannot attend work, as they are not vaccinated against COVID-19.

The customer meets all other eligibility criteria.

The customer’s rate of payment will be $750.

The customer will receive automated payments for the remaining periods unless the relevant area they live in is no longer deemed a COVID-19 hotspot or there is a change in circumstances.

Once Victoria reached 80% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, payments will reduce for 2 weeks before stopping. The customer will receive:

  • Relevant period 14: $450

Unable to attend work due to exemption from mask wearing

The customer works in a café as a casual for 25 hours per week.

The customer has an exemption from wearing a mask however a state public health order mandates that people working in food and beverage outlets must wear a mask.

The customer is told by their employer that they cannot attend work.

Eligible

The customer is eligible for the at least 20 hours or more rate of payment as they have lost 25 hours of work in the relevant period.

The customer’s rate of payment will be $750.

Note: If there was no public health order to mandate mask wearing and this was a condition of employment imposed by the employer, the customer would not be eligible for payment.

Customer was only affected once 70% full vaccination rates reached

A customer works 25 hours a week in a restaurant in Melbourne.

Although the business was impacted by the restricted movement, the customer has been able to continue working their usual hours, as the restaurant has continued to sell take away meals. Other staff have not been able to retain their usual hours of work.

On 22 October 2021, (after Victoria reached 70% full vaccination rates of its population who are aged 16 years and older), the business can no longer operate due to the significant reduction in sales. The restaurant permanently closes.

The customer claims the COVID-19 Disaster Payment and meets all eligibility criteria.

Eligible

The customer:

  • is eligible to receive the $750 rate of payment as they have lost 25 hours of work
  • will need to reapply each week

Once Victoria reached 80% vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and older, payments will reduce for 2 weeks before stopping. The customer will receive:

  • Relevant period 14: $450
  • Relevant period 15: $320
