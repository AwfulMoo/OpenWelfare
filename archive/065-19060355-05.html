Task Selector screens

Table 1: an overview of the screens that display on the Process Direct Task Selector in the Home Equity Access Scheme applications. Screens not listed in the Task Selector can be accessed via the Super Key field.

Item

Description

1

Pensions Assessment Date (PNA)

In Process Direct the Pensions Assessment (PNA) screen displays the customer’s Age Pension age date and the Pensions Assessment Date.

Age Pension age Details

If the customer has reached Age Pension age, the Age Pension Eligibility indicator will display 'Yes'. To be eligible for the Scheme the customer must be Age Pension age or partnered to a person who is Age Pension age.

To view the partner’s Age Pension age details, select to view the Relations menu and select the partner to access their record.

Pensions Assessment (PNA) Date

The PNA date is automatically determined based on the customer’s circumstances, and can be changed if required.

If the customer is current on a pension, no changes need to be made to the PNA date for the purposes of the Scheme application.

If the customer has applied for loan-only loan, the PNA date will default to the date the Scheme application was lodged. For loan-only applications that result in a grant outcome, the PNA date must match the Scheme grant date to ensure pension arrears are not paid. The Scheme grant date is the Effect Date recorded on the HEAS Details (PLS) screen.

2

HEAS Summary (PLSS) screen

The HEAS Summary (PLSS) screen provides a list of current and past loans paid to the customer through the Scheme. The details for each loan can be viewed by selecting the relevant line from the summary to open the HEAS Details (PLS) screen.

Review the information on the PLSS screen before proceeding to add a new loan, noting:

  • if a loan already exists with an outstanding loan balance (for example, a total balance above $0), a new Scheme loan cannot be granted until the existing loan has been settled. See Assessing Home Equity Access Scheme applications for information about how to assess existing loans
  • if an outstanding loan does not exist and the claim was lodged online or via Assisted Customer claim (ACC), a new row for a provisional loan is automatically created. To view and update the details, select Edit
  • if the claim was lodged via the SA310 paper form, a row for a new loan needs to be manually created. Select to add a new row

3

HEAS Details (PLS) screen

The HEAS Details (PLS) screen is mandatory for all Scheme claims. The information recorded on the PLS screen determines the loan assessment outcome.

If the application was submitted online or via ACC, the information the customer entered to the application will be pre-populated. Otherwise, a new loan needs to be added and the data needs to be manually entered. To add a new loan select on the PLSS screen.

Review the details on the PLS screen and make any required updates. For information on the individual fields and how to code the PLS screen see Table 2.

4

Real estate details (REBS)

The Real Estate / Business Details (REBS) screen displays details of the real estate and business assets owned by the customer and (if applicable) their partner. The REBS screen in Process Direct provides view and update access to the following screens:

  • Real Estate/Business Identifying (REBI) screen
  • Real Estate Detail (RE) screen
  • Real Estate Valuation Information (REVI) screen

To view all information for a particular property select Expand.

The information on REBS is pre-populated where the customer has provided details in the online application. The information will have already been reviewed and updated via Customer First as part of the Assessing Home Equity Access Scheme applications process. Review the information entered to the application and ensure that the real estate asset(s) being used as security for the loan are coded correctly. If further updates are required, update the information by adding a new row or amending existing details.

The Centrelink Property ID on the REBI screen for each real estate property offered as security is to be coded in the CLK Loan Property Ids field on the HEAS Details (PLS) screen. If multiple properties have been offered, separate the ID's with a comma.

Note: requests for real estate valuations are coded via the REVI screen, however the request functionality does not yet exist in Process Direct. See Assessing Home Equity Access Scheme applications for information on how to request a valuation for the Scheme applications.

5

Customer Person Detail Summary (CPDS)

The Customer Person Detail Summary (CPDS) screen displays the current and historical person details for the customer, including name, sex and date of birth. Selecting a line on the CPDS screen opens the Customer Person Details (CPD) screen, which displays the full details corresponding to the selected line.

The information on this screen is generally updated when the customer provides their proof of identity documents. If there are any changes required to the customer’s personal details, update the information by adding a new row or amending existing details.

For more information about customer personal details see Aliases and other names (legal, preferred, sex and gender diverse customers, cultural considerations etc.).

6

Marital Status (MS)

The Marital Status (MS) screen is used to record the customer’s relationship status, and details pertaining to the relationship status such as Partner Permission to Enquire (PPE) and qualifiers of the relationship status.

The information on this screen is pre-populated where the customer has provided details in the online application. If any changes are required to the customer’s relationship status, update the information by adding a new row or amending existing details.

For more information about relationship status see Assessment of relationship status – processes, roles and responsibilities.

7

Address details (ADH)

The Address (ADH) screen displays the customer’s current and past addresses and can be used to add, view or change the customer’s address details.

The information on the ADH screen is pre-populated where the customer has provided details in their application. If there are any changes required, update the information by adding a new row or amending existing details.

If the customer has notified via their Scheme application that they have changed address, check if they still live in the same geographically-based environment. If an environment transfer is required, conduct the transfer before processing the Scheme application.

For more information see:

  • Updating address details
  • Environment transfers and relationships

8

Accommodation (ACS)

The Accommodation (ACS) screen is used to record the accommodation details for the customer, such as home ownership details and rent information. The information on the ACS screen is used for determining the customer’s eligibility for Rent Assistance (RA) with their Home Equity Access Scheme qualifying payment.

The information on the ACS screen is pre-populated where the customer has provided details in their application. If changes are required, update the information by adding a new row or amending existing details.

Note: the Scheme application does not gather accommodation information from applicants who advise they are applying for a Scheme-only loan, as Scheme-only customers are not eligible to receive RA. As such, accommodation details are not required to be recorded on the ACS screen for Scheme-only claims.

For more information about the ACS screen see Recording accommodation details.

9

Telephone Details (TDS)

The Telephone Details (TDS) screen displays the customer’s current and past telephone details and can be used to add, view or change the customer’s telephony details.

The information on the TDS screen is pre-populated where the customer has provided details in their application. If there are any changes required, update the information by adding a new row or amending existing details.

For more information see Updating telephone details and/or paying Telephony Allowance (TAL).

10

Sharer Details Summary (SDS)

The Sharer Details Summary (SDS) screen provides access to the following screens:

  • Sharing Details (SDQ)
  • Details of a Sharing Relationship (SHR)
  • Relationship Status Among the Customer, Residence and Sharer (SHD)
  • Living Arrangement (LAO/LAQ)

These screens are used to record whether the customer is sharing accommodation with a person aged 16 years or over who is not an immediate family member. Sharer details are used to determine correct entitlement to Rent Assistance (RA).

The information on the sharer details screens is pre-populated where the customer has provided details in their application. If changes are required, update the information by adding a new row or amending existing details.

Note: accommodation details do not need to be recorded for loan-only claims, as loan-only customers are not eligible to receive RA. Accordingly, the Scheme application does not gather accommodation information from applicants who advise they are applying for a loan-only loan.

For more information about sharer details see The Living Arrangements question set.

11

Regular Period Frequency (RPF)

The Regular Period Frequency (RPF) screen displays the payment frequency for supplements the customer may receive with their Home Equity Access Scheme qualifying payment, including:

  • Energy Supplement (CES)
  • Pension Supplement Minimum (PSMIN)

The screen can be used to change the period frequency for these supplements from a fortnightly payment frequency to a quarterly payment frequency, and vice versa.

Updates to the RPF screen are not required for the Scheme applications, however the functionality is available if the customer requests a change to the frequency of their supplements. Note: loan-only customers are not eligible to receive the Energy Supplement or Pension Supplement Minimum (PSMIN), therefore any update will only be applicable where the customer receives the supplements with their qualifying payment.

For more information about the supplements see:

  • Pension Supplement
  • Energy Supplement for Commonwealth Seniors Health Card holders

12

Residence

To be eligible for the Scheme, applicants must meet the qualifying residence rules applicable to their Home Equity Access Scheme qualifying payment. The customer's qualification is automatically assessed based on the residence information held on the customer’s record and any new information entered to the application.

Check the residence information provided by the customer in their Scheme application and make any required updates. Residence updates must be coded even if the application is to be rejected, in order to correctly assess the customer’s eligibility for the Scheme qualifying payment.

Common residence screens to review and update include:

  • RSIMME - Immigration Enquiry
  • RSLEG - Residence Details
  • CRES - Country of Residence
  • RSIM - Immigration Movements

If the RSIM screen shows customer was outside Australia on the date they lodged the Scheme application, the customer is not eligible for the Scheme as they need to be physically present in Australia when the claim is made.

For information on residence coding, see Residence and Portability screens.

13

Payment Destination Details (PAS)

The Payment Destination Details (PAS) screen is used to record the bank account where the Scheme loan payments will be sent. Payment destination details for the Scheme are recorded against Benefit System PEN – Pensions.

Check the Scheme application to identify which bank account the customer has nominated to receive their loan payments into. Ensure the correct information is recorded on the PAS screen for Benefit System PEN – Pensions.

Note: if the customer receives a pension, the pension and Scheme loan must be paid to the same bank account. Any changes to the payment destination details for Benefit System: PEN – Pensions will be applied to the pension. If a pension recipient has nominated a different account for their Scheme payments, contact the customer before applying the change to advise that the same account must be used for both payments, and confirm which account they wish to proceed with.

For more information see Payment destination.

14

Tax File Number (TFN)

The Tax File Number (TFN) screen is used for recording the customer’s TFN details, TFN authorisation details and TFN exemption information. The Scheme cannot be paid unless the customer has provided a valid TFN and authorisation, or has a valid exemption.

The information on the TFN screen will automatically populate with the customers TFN details if already provided. TFN details for the Scheme are recorded against the Service Reason for the Scheme qualifying payment, for example Age Pension (AGE).

Check the information provided in the Scheme application to ensure the populated details are correct, including that the customer has provided authorisation to use the TFN for the Scheme purposes. If a new provisional entry has been automatically created, check the details for correctness and make edits if required.

If the customer is partnered, switch to the partner’s record and ensure their TFN details have also been updated correctly. Partners of the Scheme applicants are requested to provide a TFN, however it is not mandatory unless they are applying for their own Scheme loan.

If the applicant is unable to provide their partner’s TFN, exemption reason SEC (Secretaries Exemption) may be applied to the partner’s record. Recording of the TFN exemption must be applied by the Customer Details Helpdesk. To request an exemption for the partner, submit a request to the Helpdesk.

For more information about TFN requirements and exemptions see Requesting a Tax File Number (TFN).

15

Office Code (OC)

The Office Code (OC) screen displays the office code(s) for all of a customer’s service reasons applicable as at a particular date. The system automatically assigns an office code based on the customer’s address details, but can be changed if the customer requests to use another office.

For Scheme applicants, the office code is recorded against the qualifying payment type, for example Age Pension. The same office code must be used for the qualifying payment and the Scheme.

Check the OC screen to see if any updates are required. If a new provisional entry has been automatically created, check the details for correctness and make edits required. If the provisional entry is the same as the confirmed data, the provisional data can be deleted.

For more information about office codes, including a link to Office Locator, see Updating address details.

16

Other Contact Details (OCD)

The Other Contact Details (OCD) screen is used to display and update details of a customer’s other contact features. These consist of:

  • Interpreter Language requirements
  • Preferred Written Language
  • Indigenous Identifier

The information on the OCD screen is pre-populated where the customer has provided details in their application. If a new provisional entry has been automatically created, check the details for correctness and make edits if required. If the provisional entry is the same as the confirmed data, the provisional data can be deleted.

Note: the Indigenous Identifier is voluntary and is used to assist government to make decisions about policies, funding and services as well as deliver targeted services. A customer’s status can be updated at any time. Do not ask the customer to provide proof of their Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander status. For more information see About Initial contact and Identity Confirmation.

17

Reporting Regime (RPRD)

The Reporting Regime (RPRD) screen displays whether the customer is required to report for the purposes of their Scheme qualifying payment. The system automatically inserts necessary Report Types and characteristics onto this screen based on Service Profile Manager results and relevant information recorded on other screens.

Updates to the RPRD screen are generally not required for the Scheme applications, however the functionality is available if needed. The RPRD screen enables existing Report Types to be updated or overridden, and new Report Types to be added.

Loan-only customers are not required to report as they do not receive any rate components that are subject to activity testing or the income and asset tests. If a loan-only applicant has a reporting regime in place, investigate what action (if any) needs to be taken. Do not grant loan-only while a reporting arrangement is in effect.

For more information about updating RPRD see Reporting screens.

HEAS Details (PLS) screen

Table 2: fields on the HEAS Details (PLS) screen. The PLS screen is accessed via the HEAS Summary (PLSS) screen.

Field

Description

Loan Number

The Loan Number is a display field that allows distinction between multiple loans. The number is the format ANNNNNNNNN where A is an alphabet character and N is a number 0 to 9.

The Loan Number is automatically generated each time a new loan is created. If an existing loan is selected via the PLSS screen, the details for the applicable Loan Number are displayed for the latest Effect Date.

Effect Date

This field shows the date the loan information was last updated. Details can only be updated on or after the most recent Effect Date. The details for historical Effect Dates are display-only.

The earliest Effect Date is the generally the grant date of the loan.

Note: in Process Direct the Effect Date for a new loan defaults to the date of claim lodgement, and in legacy systems defaults to the system date. If the loan is to be granted from a different date, ensure the correct Effect Date is entered. In Process Direct this requires any pre-populated provisional details to be deleted via the PLSS screen and a new loan added using the correct date.

Current Balance

The Current Balance field shows the total loan balance outstanding as at the displayed Effect Date. The value in this field is the total amount of the Principal, Accrued Interest and Costs, minus any repayments.

When the Current Balance reaches the Maximum Loan Amount, fortnightly loan payments automatically cease to be paid.

Interest Rate

The Interest Rate field displays the interest rate applicable to the loan as at the displayed Effect Date. The interest rate is an annual rate that compounds fortnightly. The Effect Date for the fortnightly update of interest is the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED).

Loan Top up Type

This field is used to record the type of fortnightly loan component the customer has elected to receive. The valid values are:

  • MX2 – this is the most that can be paid. The loan amount paid is the difference between the maximum pension rate x 150%, and the customer’s actual rate. MX2 can only be coded for Effect Dates on or after 1 July 2019
  • MAX – this should only be applied to customer’s who receive less than the maximum rate of pension. The loan amount paid is the difference between the maximum pension rate and the customer’s actual rate. MAX can only be coded for applications lodged prior to 1 July 2019
  • PCT – this loan type allows the customer to elect a percentage that is less than the maximum 150%. A percentage between 1% and 150% can entered. The loan amount paid is the difference between the maximum pension rate x the applicable percentage, and the customer’s actual pension rate. If the elected percentage is less than the actual rate payable, no loan is payable (Note: post-grant this will cause the Scheme payments to stop and require manual restoration). PCT can only be coded for Effect Dates on or after 1 July 2019
  • FIX – for customers who elect to receive a fixed amount of loan per fortnight. The fixed amount plus the customer’s actual pension rate cannot exceed the maximum amount payable (which from 1 July 2019 is the maximum pension rate x 150%)
  • RVM – for customers who elect a combined payment amount each fortnight. The combined amount of pension and loan cannot exceed the amount the customer elects. The loan amount payable is the difference between the customer’s actual pension rate and the RVM amount they set. RVM cannot be coded from 1 July 2019
  • ADV - for customers that request an advance, or are in an advance payment period but do not receive fortnightly loan payments

Percent

This field is used to enter the percentage of the maximum pension rate that the customer would like to receive as a loan per fortnight. The most a person can receive is 150% of the maximum pension rate.

The Percent field is required to be coded when the loan top-up type is MX2, MAX, or PCT. Valid values in the percentage field are values between 1 and 150. The percentage is multiplied by the maximum pension rate to determine the Scheme loan amount to be paid each fortnight.

If the customer receives a pension:

  • the combined amount of pension and loan cannot exceed 150% of the maximum pension rate, therefore the loan amount the customer receives each fortnight is dependent on their pension rate (variable loan amount)
  • If the percentage results in a fortnightly loan amount of $0 or less, the Scheme will reject or cancel. For example, if the customer is a maximum rate pensioner and a percent of 100% or less is entered, the loan amount will be $0

Loan Topup Amt pf $

The Loan Topup Amt field is used to record the amount of loan the customer has requested to receive each fortnight. This field is required to be coded when the loan top-up type is FIX or RVM. The amount is coded as a dollar figure.

The value entered is the amount the customer will be paid as a loan each fortnight. If the customer receives a pension, the combined amount of pension and loan cannot exceed 150% of the maximum pension rate. Where the loan amount will exceed the 150% limit, the system automatically caps the loan amount payable per fortnight.

Loan Amount pf $

This is a display-only field that shows the actual loan component paid to the customer each fortnight as of the displayed Effect Date. No value is displayed in this field for new loan assessments.

Loan Security Value

The Loan Security Value field shows the value of the real estate used as security for the loan. Enter the current market value of the real estate as displayed on the REBI screen. If more than one property is used as security, code the combined current market value of the properties.

Note: if the customer is partnered, only the customer’s share of the current market value must be coded. This is deemed to be 50% of the total value however exceptions may apply.

The current market value must be based on a valuation conducted in the last 12 months. See Assessing Home Equity Access Scheme applications for information on requesting a valuation for the Scheme applications.

The amount recorded in the Loan Security Value field is automatically reduced by any amounts coded in the Deductions and Nominated Amount fields.

See Security for the Home Equity Access Scheme for more information about assessing the loan security value.

Nominated Amount

A Nominated Amount is an amount of money the customer elects to exclude from their Loan Security Value. The value entered to this field is deducted from the amount in the Loan Security Value field when calculating the Maximum Loan Amount (MLA).

Although the Nominated Amount is not included in the calculation of the MLA, the amount is not quarantined i.e. the Scheme debt can still be recovered from it.

See Security for the Home Equity Access Scheme for more information about the Nominated Amount.

Deductions

The Deductions field is used for recording the value of any loans, mortgages or encumbrances against the real estate property being used as security. The value entered to this field is deducted from the amount in the Loan Security Value field when calculating the Maximum Loan Amount (MLA).

Note: if the customer is partnered, only the customer’s share of the deductions must be coded. This is deemed to be 50% of the total deduction amount however exceptions may apply.

If more than one property is used as loan security, code the combined value of deductions applicable to the properties.

See Security for the Home Equity Access Scheme for more information about eligible deductions.

CLK Loan Property Ids

This field is used to identify the real estate property that provides the security for the loan. The property must be recorded on the real estate screens via REBS.

The Centrelink Loan Property ID on the PLS screen must match a Centrelink Property ID for the customer. The Centrelink Property ID is displayed on the Real Estate/Business Identifying (REBI) screen when the property details are recorded.

The Centrelink Loan Property ID field will allow for up to five property ID’s to be recorded, with a comma between them. Each property ID must be coded in the format ANNNNNNNNN.

If the Centrelink Loan Property ID field is blank, the Scheme will reject/cancel.

Note: if the property being used as loan security already exists in Customer First when a Scheme application is lodged, the ID will automatically populate on the PLS screen. Where a new property has been recorded, the ID will automatically populate when Assess is selected in Process Direct. Check the PLS screen prior to finalising the claim to ensure the correct ID has been populated. Manual update may be required if the ID does not populate, or the applicant is using more than one property as loan security.

HEAS Only Indicator

The HEAS Only indicator needs to be coded when a customer applies for a loan and does not receive or claim a Qualifying payment for Home Equity Access Scheme. This may include customers who:

  • are of Age Pension age and qualified for Age Pension, but not payable under the income or assets tests, or they have elected not to claim Age Pension (or any other Scheme qualifying payment)
  • are under Age Pension age and qualified for DSP or Carer Payment, but not payable under the income or assets test

A HEAS Only indicator of 'Y' (Yes) shows the customer is not being assessed under the pensions income or assets test. The only rate component that can be issued when the indicator is 'Y' is the Scheme loan component (LON).

Ensure the HEAS Only indicator is set to 'Y' on the PLS screen for customers who have applied for a PLS-only loan, to suppress the pension rate assessment. Note: for new applications, the pension assessment date on the PNA screen must also match the Effect Date of the HEAS Only indicator to ensure no pension arrears are calculated and paid.

A customer must lodge a full pension claim with income and asset details if they wish to test their eligibility for a payable rate of their Scheme qualifying payment.

The HEAS only indicator cannot be set to 'Y' if the customer receives a payable rate of the qualifying pension as at the applicable Effect Date.

Maximum Loan Amount

This field displays the Maximum Loan Amount (MLA) calculated by the system. When the Current Balance of the loan reaches the MLA, the Scheme loan payments cease to be paid.

For more information about how the MLA is calculated see Security for the Home Equity Access Scheme.

Customer Advised Max Amt $

This field is used for coding the Maximum Loan Amount (MLA) requested by the customer. This field is optional. The amount entered will override the system-calculated MLA (as displayed in the Maximum Loan Amount field) but cannot be more than the amount the system calculates.

Adjusted Real Estate Value $

Do not code anything here. This field should be left blank at grant.

This field requires coding when the loan is settled.

Bankruptcy/Insolvency

This field is used to record whether the customer is bankrupt or subject to a personal insolvency agreement under Part X of the Bankruptcy Act 1966. The Bankruptcy/Insolvency field is mandatory.

Entering 'Y' (Yes) in this field indicates the customer is bankrupt or subject to a personal insolvency agreement. The Scheme loan payments will reject or cancel if the indicator is 'Y'.

A value of 'N' (No) or 'C' (Conversion) in this field indicates the customer is not bankrupt or insolvent.

See Eligibility for the Home Equity Access Scheme for more information about bankruptcy and personal insolvency

Adequate Insurance

This field is used to record whether the customer has adequate and appropriate insurance for the real estate property used as security for the loan. The Adequate Insurance field is mandatory.

Entering 'N' (No) in this field indicates the customer does not have adequate and appropriate insurance. The Scheme loan payments will reject or cancel if the indicator is 'N'.

A value of 'Y' (Yes) or 'C' (Conversion) in this field indicates the customer meets the insurance criteria.

See Eligibility for the Home Equity Access Scheme for more information about the insurance requirements for the Scheme.

Reject/Cease Reason

The Reject/Cease Reason field displays a 3-letter reason code to indicate the reason why the Scheme loan payments have been rejected or cancelled. This field displays automated assessment outcomes and can also be used to manually reject or cancel loan payments.

Values that can be manually entered are:

  • CLR – Voluntary Withdrawal
  • FRC – Failure to return correspondence
  • DIS – Secretary Discretion
  • ARJ - Advance claim rejected

Where a Reject/Cease Reason has been applied, the Ceased Loan Indicator must also be set to 'Y' (Yes) to ensure loan payments are not issued.

For more information about the rejection and cancellation reasons see Home Equity Access Scheme cancellation and rejections.

Ceased Loan Indicator

The Ceased Loan Indicator identifies the status of the loan component.

  • An indicator of 'N' (No) or blank indicates loan payments are payable
  • No loan payments can be paid if the indicator is 'Y' (Yes). Where the Ceased Loan Indicator has been set to 'Y', the Reject/Cease Reason field will also be recorded to indicate the reason loan payments are not payable

The Ceased Loan Indicator does not identify the status of a person’s participation in the scheme, only the status of the payable loan component. The person is considered to be a current Scheme participant until the loan is settled in full (i.e. until the Current Balance becomes zero).

The Ceased Loan Indicator only needs to be coded in a the Scheme application if the:

  • loan is being rejected for a manual rejection reason, or
  • indicator has been previously set to 'Y' and needs to be changed to 'N' to enable the Scheme to be re-granted or restored

If an auto-rejection reason applies, the indicator is set to 'Y' automatically.

For more information about the rejection, cancellation and restoration of the Scheme loans see Home Equity Access Scheme cancellation and rejections.

NNEG Ineligible Reason

Do not code anything here. This field should be left blank at grant.

This field requires coding when the loan is settled.

Recalc

The Recalc field enables staff to manually trigger a recalculation of the Maximum Loan Amount (MLA) for the customer. The recalculation is based on the information on the customer’s record at that point in time. The Recalc field is not required to be coded as part of a Scheme application and does not appear in the Process Direct version of the PLS screen.

Note: the MLA automatically recalculates if any changes are made to the Loan Security Value, Nominated Amount or Deductions fields, and when the customer’s age component increases.

Principal $

This field shows the current accrued balance of payments made as a principal loan component to the customer as at the displayed Effect Date. The Principal field is not required to be coded when processing a Scheme application.

Interest $

This field shows the current balance of interest accrued on the loan as at the displayed Effect Date. The Interest field is not required to be coded when processing a Scheme application.

Cost $

This field shows the current amount outstanding for costs associated with the loan application as at the displayed Effect Date. The Costs field is not required to be coded when processing a Scheme application.

Refund Amt & Receipt Number

For each part of the loan component (Principal, Interest, Costs) there is a corresponding Refund Amount and Receipt Number field. These fields are only used to record refunds of loan components, therefore are not required to be coded when processing a Scheme application.

For more information about coding refunds see Repayment of Home Equity Access Scheme loans.

Pre grant interview

Table 3: information about the Scheme pre-grant interview. The Scheme loans must not be granted until the interview has been conducted and the customer accepts the loan offer.

Step

Action

1

Purpose

Prior to granting the Scheme loan, a verbal offer of loan must be made and an interview conducted with the applicant and partner, if any. The intent of the interview is to discuss the loan available to the applicant once the actual Maximum Loan Amount (MLA) and rate has been determined. The applicant must verbally agree to proceed with the loan. The interview also serves as a control to address the risk of vulnerability or elder abuse, fraud and capacity issues in a way that incorporates and is aligned with best practice for lenders of loans.

The interview provides an opportunity for the Decision Maker (DM) to complete all of the following:

  • inform the applicant of the decision and provide the details of the loan offer
  • ensure the applicant has an understanding of the Home Equity Access Scheme terms and conditions, including the requirements to maintain eligibility criteria and comply with ongoing notification obligations
  • provide information about the customers withdrawal rights and review/appeal rights
  • as far as possible, identify if the customer and/or partner does not understand, or have capacity to understand, the loan
  • identify if there are signs the customer will not be able to comply with the terms/conditions
  • as far as possible, identify indicators of financial elder abuse or fraud
  • reiterate the benefits of seeking independent legal or financial advice before entering into the Scheme
  • answer any questions the applicant may have about the Scheme
  • seek agreement from the applicant and partner (if any) to proceed on the loan offer

The interview should be conducted as close to the date of grant as possible. Typically the application is finalised on the same day the interview is conducted, providing the applicant has agreed to proceed with the loan.

For details of the information to be covered during the Scheme interview, go to Step 2.

2

Interview details

The Scheme pre-grant interview must include discussion of the following information.

  • Details of the loan offer, including:
    • the value of the loan security used in the assessment
    • nominated amount (if applicable)
    • Maximum Loan Amount (MLA) available
    • fortnightly loan payment rate and/or advance payment amount
    • fortnightly pension rate (including if zero)
    • start date for the loan as of the next fortnight
      Note: an offer to backdate the start date should not be made unless the customer specifically requests it
    • projected loan balance information
  • The applicable interest rate and method of compounding interest, noting interest continues to accrue (compounding fortnightly) until the Scheme debt is repaid in full by the customer or their estate. Note: the Scheme interest rate is set by the Minister for the Department of Social Services and is published on the Federal Register of Legislation
  • Taxation status of the loan component (i.e. non-taxable)
  • Costs involved in registering, changing or withdrawing a charge or caveat on the titles of secured real estate. Withdrawal of the caveat/charge occurs at the point of settlement of the loan
  • Liability of the customer, or their estate, for all components of the Scheme loan including principal loan payments, costs and interest
  • The No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG) may apply at settle time. If the NNEG does apply, it guarantees that should a Scheme debt be greater than the value of the property (market value) at the conclusion of the loan, no customer, partner or estate will be required to repay more than the property’s current market value. Exceptions apply for example if a person extends an existing loan, or takes out another loan on the security property after being granted the Scheme, or they misrepresent their circumstances, or they commit fraud. For further details see Settlement of Home Equity Access Scheme loans
  • Procedures for recovery of the loan. See Repayment of Home Equity Access Scheme loans and Settlement of Home Equity Access Scheme loan amounts for supporting information
  • Requirement to:
    • comply with the Home Equity Access Scheme terms and conditions as agreed in the signing of the loan application
    • maintain eligibility for the Scheme, including pension qualification, for as long as the customer wishes to continue receiving loan payments. Loss of eligibility results in the cessation of fortnightly loan payments
  • Notification obligations. See Home Equity Access Scheme reviews for details
  • Right to request an explanation or review of decision. See Request for an explanation, quality check or review
  • The customer’s right to vary their loan arrangements in writing at any time, including stopping or starting their loan payments
  • A written request signed by the customer and, if a member of a couple (MoC), their partner is required to change any of the following:
    • Fortnightly loan payment rate
    • Maximum Loan Amount (MLA)
    • Nominated Amount
    • Stopping or starting loan payments
    • Changes to the loan security
  • Benefit of seeking independent legal or financial advice and talking to any beneficiaries of the estate before entering into the Scheme
  • Once granted:
    • a grant letter will be sent confirming the details of the loan and the customer’s obligations
    • a loan statement is sent at least once per annum. Where both MoC participate in the Scheme, each person receives their own statement
  • How to make repayments, noting they can be made at any time

Obtain verbal agreement from the customer to the loan offer and information discussed before proceeding to grant the loan. If the customer decides not to proceed with the loan, they can choose to withdraw the application. See Assessing Home Equity Access Scheme applications for information on voluntary withdrawal of the Scheme applications.

If there is any doubt regarding the customer’s verbal consent or understanding of the terms and conditions, consider what further action (if any) may be required before granting the loan. This may include speaking with the customer’s partner or correspondence nominee, seeking policy advice, or giving consideration to whether elder abuse may be a factor. For guidance on the assessment of elder abuse see Assessing Home Equity Access Scheme applications.
