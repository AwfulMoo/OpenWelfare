4

Ex-partner advises the separation + Read more ...

Confirm details on the date of separation with the ex-partner but do not unlink the records. DOC the ex-partner’s record using Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Stated Separated from Partner DOV.

If the ex-partner is on an income support payment, see Change in relationship status from partnered to single for immediate updates to be made to the ex-partner’s record.

For all ex-partner contacts, after the contact with the ex-partner is complete: attempt to contact the PPP customer by phone. If contact is:

  • successful, go to Step 5
  • unsuccessful, change Partner Permission to Enquire (PPE) to 'No' and send a Q164 letter requesting the PPP customer contacts the agency, giving the customer 14 days to respond

If the customer does not contact within 14 days and the ex-partner is:

  • not in receipt of, or claiming an income support payment, the customer will remain on PPP until they advise of separation via a MOD S or a Parenting Payment online claim
  • in receipt of an income support payment, the ex-partners record may be unlinked and the customer will need to lodge a new claim for Parenting Payment or another income support payment

If the PPP customer does contact within the required period and has confirmed the separation, the records are only to be unlinked within a Benefit Transfer (BTR) activity if the PPP customer is wishing to transfer to PPS. Unlinking outside a BTR activity will result in cancellation of PPP and a new claim activity will then need to be coded as a workaround to transfer the customer to PPS. This may produce incorrect outcomes for the customer.

Procedure ends here.

5

Vulnerable customer at risk assessment + Read more ...

If a current PPP customer advises that they have separated or confirms advice of separation provided by their ex-partner a vulnerable at risk assessment will need to be made.

Confirm if the customer:

  • indicates that their or their children's safety is at risk due to family and domestic violence, gambling or mental health issues
  • has experienced an extreme circumstance within the past 7 days, for example a house fire

If the response is:

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Risk identification, referral process + Read more ...

Customer has experienced family and domestic violence:

  • See Family and domestic violence procedure to conduct the risk identification and referral process, and to offer suitable services and referrals
  • Offer the customer a referral to a social worker
  • Go to Step 7

Customer has experienced an extreme circumstance:

  • Assess possible entitlement to a Crisis Payment discuss specialist services such as Crisis Payment or exemptions from mutual obligation requirements and child support
  • Offer the customer a referral to a social worker
  • Go to Step 7

7

Immediate updates required + Read more ...

When a customer advises their separation do not update the customer's relationship status at time of contact. This will occur within a benefit transfer activity.

The following must be checked at the first point of contact and updated immediately if relevant:

  • cancel the nominee arrangement. If the customer has their ex-partner appointed as their nominee, ask if they wish to cancel the arrangement. Advise the customer a letter will be automatically issued to the existing nominee, advising them the arrangement has been cancelled. This is particularly important where the customer is affected by family and domestic violence. The request to cancel the arrangement does not have to be in writing
  • Person Permitted to enquire (PPE) authority as required
  • Address and contact details noting a temporary relationship qualifier may need to be coded. For detail see Change in relationship status from partnered to single. Ensure updates do not flow onto the ex-partner’s record by coding 'No' in the Make partner's address the same as the customer's field
  • If the partner has access to the account where the customer’s payment is paid, or the payment is made to a joint account, it may be appropriate to change the customer’s payment destination
  • If the customer has changed their email address, see Adding, changing or removing an email address
  • If the customer believes their ex-partner knows their Self Service PIN or Password they should be advised to access the Self Service Channel to change these details as soon as possible
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and check results (such as debts/arrears) prior to finalising the activity. Record all details on a DOC

If the customer is:

  • also receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and will continue to have care of a child, see Maintenance Action Test (MAT) for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customers
  • has a child attending approved child care and is not CCS current, see Eligibility for Child Care Subsidy (CCS)

Go to Eligibility to transfer PPP to PPS table to determine if customer is eligible for the single rate of payment.
