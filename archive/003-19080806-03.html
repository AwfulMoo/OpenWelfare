Pre-coding checks

Table 1: This table outlines the steps to follow after allocation of a claim for the MH17 Family Support Package, including how to assess a customer’s eligibility to receive an MH17 Family Support Package payment.

Step

Action

1

Claim allocated + Read more ...

Locate the MH17 Family Support Package - Family Hierarchy Spreadsheet.

  • Open the Excel Spreadsheet for the deceased person’s name included in the customer’s claim form
  • Update the spreadsheet
  • Go to Step 2

2

Index the claim + Read more ...

In Customer First go to the Flexible Assistance Payments (!FFAP) screen and complete the relevant fields.

  • Select the Event ID from the drop down box
  • Select ‘grant’ at this point
  • Date affected by event will always be 17 July 2014
  • Source is PER
  • Receipt date is the date the claim form was received by the agency

Go to Step 3.

3

Check claim form and evidence + Read more ...

Current claim forms are EM198.220811 (for a person 16 years and older) and EM199.220811 (for a child under 16 years).

Has the customer provided a current claim form, completed all questions and provided evidence?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to the Requesting evidence table

4

Initial eligibility requirements + Read more ...

To be eligible to receive the MH17 Family Support Package, a customer must be:

  • the family member of an Australian victim, and
  • seeking to observe or participate in the Dutch national prosecution of the individuals responsible for the downing of MH17

Check 'Attachment B of the MH17 Family Support Package - Guidelines for ex-gratia payments to assist Australian MH17 next of kin to participate in the Dutch national prosecution' for a list of the names of the Australian victims. See the References page for a link to the guidelines.

Does the customer meet the initial eligibility requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to the Coding a claim table

5

Family member hierarchy + Read more ...

Up to 4 family members for each Australian victim may be eligible for assistance. The hierarchy is:

1. Partner

2. Child(ren)

3. Parents

4. Siblings

5. Step-parents at the time of the downing (17 July 2014)

6. Step-siblings at the time of the downing (17 July 2014)

7. Other family relationship

Reminder: if only one family member of an Australian MH17 victim submits a claim, and no other known family members are identified as intending to claim, that family member can have a support person accompany them. In all other circumstances, if a family member needs a support person, escalate the claim as a special circumstance.

Does the customer meet the Family member hierarchy assessment?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to the Coding a claim table

6

Identity confirmation requirements + Read more ...

Does the customer meet identity confirmation requirements as per Identity confirmation for the MH17 Family Support Package?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, request identity, see Identity confirmation for the MH17 Family Support Package, then go to the Coding a claim table

7

Special circumstances apply + Read more ...

Do special circumstances apply?

  • Yes, go to the Escalating claims and special circumstances table
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Other Centrelink benefits or allowances + Read more ...

Customers travelling to the Netherlands to attend the Dutch national prosecution should be considered for a Restricted Portability assessment. Note: this applies to any person travelling to the Netherlands to attend the Dutch national prosecution. It is not limited to customers eligible to receive the MH17 Family Support Package.

Is the customer is receiving another Centrelink benefit or allowance?

  • Yes:
    • Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer to discuss the portability of their existing Centrelink benefit or allowance while they are in the Netherlands
    • If the customer is on the phone, warm transfer the customer to International Services or tell the customer to call the Centrelink International Services line
    • Document the customer’s record using the Progress DOC Fast Note in Workspace - Emergency> Claim>MH17
    • Go to the Coding a claim table
  • No, go to the Coding a claim table

Coding a claim

Table 2: This table describes the process for coding an MH17 Family Support Package claim.

Do not grant or reject claims for the MH17 Family Support Package until the end of each claim period and after the Operational Response Branch have confirmed when claim finalisation can start.

See the Resources page for Claims finalisation start dates.

Step

Action

1

Update details from the Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen + Read more ...

Note: if allocated a started claim via Workload Manager (WLM) select it from the Activity List (AL) screen.

If the customer has an existing child (CHI) Customer Reference Number (CRN), convert this to a person (PER) record. See 'Convert child CRN to customer record' in Step 5 in the Indexing a customer's claim on the system - assisted processing table of Indexing a customer's claim on the system.

Go to the CDTS screen. Select the relevant options to update:

  • Address details. See Updating address details
  • Contact details. See Updating telephone details and/or paying telephone allowance
  • Marital status. See Assessment of relationship status - processes, roles and responsibilities
  • Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen - always request immigration data
  • Country of residence (CRES) screen

2

Eligible to receive a payment + Read more ...

After conducting the pre-check is the customer eligible to receive a payment? Note: ensure the customer has provided all relevant evidence as requested in the pre-check table.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to the Rejecting a claim table

3

Flexible Assistance Payments (! FFAP) screen + Read more ...

  • Go to the !FFAP screen
  • Calculate the:
    • travel costs payment (of up to $3,000) based on information provided in the customer’s claim form
    • living allowance payment (of up to $210 per night, capped at $2,100) based on information provided in the customer’s claim form
    • a payment (of up to $1,000), to help with quarantine and/or other increased/unexpected travel expenses because of COVID-19 based on information provided in the customer’s claim form
    • additional assistance payment (of up to $1,000) if the customer has applied and been assessed as eligible for this payment
  • Enter all amounts in the !FFAP screen
  • Go to Step 4

4

Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen + Read more ...

  • Go to the PAS screen
  • Select FSP

Has the customer provided an Australian bank account?

  • Yes, update the Payment Destination Overseas (PAEO) screen
    • See the Resources page for the How to code the Payment Destination Overseas (PAEO) screen task card
    • Go to Step 5
  • No, escalate the claim to the dedicated MH17 Service Support Officer (SSO)
    • Place the claim on hold for 28 days and add the keyword PE to the claim activity
    • For details on how to access the MH17 SSO, speak with a Team Leader
    • Procedure ends here

5

Check claiming period end date + Read more ...

Do not grant or reject claims for the MH17 Family Support Package until the end of each claim period and after the Operational Response Branch have confirmed when claim finalisation can start. See the Resources page for Claims finalisation start dates.

Has the claiming period ended?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, place the claim on hold until the end of claiming period date +1 day. Procedure ends here

6

Claiming period has ended + Read more ...

Note: if allocated a started claim via Workload Manager (WLM) select it from the Activity List (AL) screen.

Review all claim information and coding.

Can the claim be finalised?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No. If the customer needs to provide more details, see the Requesting evidence table. If coding on the customer’s record needs amending, update as appropriate. If there are any system issues, contact the dedicated MH17 Service Support Officer (SSO). For details on how to access the MH17 SSO, speak with a Team Leader

7

Assessment Results (AR) screen + Read more ...

  • Go to the AR screen
  • Review the AR screen
  • Finalise the claim
  • Document the customer’s record using the Grant Fast Note in Workspace - Emergency> Claim>MH17

8

Issue manual Grant letter + Read more ...

  • Create the letter using the MH17 Grant Letter template. See the Resources page for letter template
  • Have draft letter reviewed by an APS6 Team Leader or the APS5 dedicated MH17 Service Support Officer (SSO). For details on how to access the MH17 SSO, speak with a Team Leader. After reviewing the letter, the Team Leader or SSO should annotate the Grant Fast Note on file
  • After the letter has been reviewed and approved by Team Leader or SSO, issue and send the letter locally
  • Upload a copy of the letter to the customer’s record

Procedure ends here.

Rejecting a claim

Table 4: This table describes the process for rejecting an MH17 Family Support Package claim.

Do not grant or reject claims for the MH17 Family Support Package until the end of each claim period and after the Operational Response Branch have confirmed when claim finalisation can start.

See the Resources page for Claims finalisation start dates.

Step

Action

1

Has the claiming period ended? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, place the claim on hold until the end of the claiming period + 1 day

2

Important information + Read more ...

Before rejecting a claim, make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer to:

  • provide the customer with a full explanation of why their claim is unsuccessful
  • tell them that a letter will be sent outlining the review process, and
  • consider if a referral to a social worker or external organisation is required, the Resources page contains a link to the Payment and Service Finder

Go to Step 3.

3

Check contacts + Read more ...

Is this the first attempt to contact the customer regarding rejection?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

First contact attempt + Read more ...

Was the attempt successful?

  • Yes,
    • Go to the !FFAP screen and complete the new claim activity as ‘rejected’

    • Issue a letter using the MH17 Reject manual letter template Issue the letter locally. See Resources for a link to the letter template
      - Reminder: all MH17 letters must be reviewed by an APS6 Team Leader or the APS5 dedicated MH17 Service Support Officer (SSO) before being sent to a customer. Escalating claims and special circumstances

Table 5: This table describes the process to follow when seeking advice from Level 1 or escalating a special circumstances claim to the Level 2 Helpdesk.

Step

Action

1

Collect and document all necessary information/evidence + Read more ...

Assess all other eligibility criteria and collect all relevant information and evidence from the customer for the Level 1 or 2 Helpdesk to give advice or make a decision.

See the:

  • Precoding checks table, and
  • Coding a claim table

2

Place the claim on hold + Read more ...

Place the new claim on hold for 28 days and add the keyword PE to the new claim activity.

Service Officers will contact the dedicated MH17 Service Support Officer (SSO). If by email, they will carbon copy (cc) their Team Leader into the email.

Go to Step 3.

3

Escalation + Read more ...

The dedicated MH17 Service Support Officer (SSO) will complete the Payment Query Template, on the Resources page to escalate issues to Level 2 Helpdesk.

Go to Step 4.

4

Level 2 Helpdesk provides advice + Read more ...

The Level 2 Helpdesk replies with advice or the necessary actions needed.

Go to the Coding a claim table to finalise the claim.
