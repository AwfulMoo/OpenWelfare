This page contains information outlining the arrangements for job seekers who transition between the Targeted Compliance Framework and Job Seeker Compliance Model as a result of a change of address.

Transitioning between the Targeted Compliance Framework and Job Seeker Compliance Model

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Transitioning from the Targeted Compliance Framework to the Job Seeker Compliance Model + Read more ...

Customers who become a declared program participant following a change of address will transition from the Targeted Compliance Framework to the Job Seeker Compliance Model:

  • The Community Development Program (CDP) provider will re-negotiate a Job/Participation Plan
  • Any outstanding compliance investigations that are not required will be automatically finalised
  • Any outstanding payment suspensions relating to the Targeted Compliance Framework are automatically lifted when the customer exits the Jobactive/Disability Employment Services (DES)/ParentsNext provider or the provider decides to cease the compliance action
  • All other compliance action applied under the Targeted Compliance Framework prior to the transfer will remain in place. This includes outstanding penalty investigations, rate reduction penalties, payment cancellations and post cancellation non-payment periods until these have been fully served

Note: it may take a number of hours for a change in address to trigger a transfer to a new provider. The transfer can be delayed if the record is locked by staff or customers.

2

Transitioning from the Job Seeker Compliance Model to the Targeted Compliance Framework + Read more ...

Customers who change address and are no longer a declared program participant transition from the Job Seeker Compliance Model to the Targeted Compliance Framework:

  • The new employment services provider will re-negotiate a Job/Participation Plan. Compliance will be managed under the Targeted Compliance Framework
  • Any outstanding compliance investigations must still be investigated
  • Any outstanding Comprehensive Compliance Assessments (CCAs) should be conducted
  • If a serious failure is investigated and applied and the job seeker is prepared to undertake a Compliance Activity, the serious failure period must be permanently waived. A re-engagement appointment must not be booked, see Waiving a serious failure period
  • Any outstanding non-attendance failure or reconnection failure periods and Job Seeker Compliance Model related payment suspensions will cease when customer is exited from the CDP provider
  • Any serious failure periods will remain in place until the customer contacts Services Australia and a Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer permanently waives the serious failure period or the person fully serves the serious failure period. For further information, see Waiving a serious failure period
  • Any other Job Seeker Compliance Model decisions made prior to the transfer will remain in place, this includes Unemployment Non-Payment Periods (UNPP)
  • Any unrecovered penalties will be recovered from the next available instalment period where there is sufficient entitlement payable after the application of the income and assets test, direct deductions and the deduction of any Targeted Compliance Framework penalties
  • Any serious failures or UNPPs subject to Payment Pending Review, a stay order or pending waiver (Job Seeker Compliance Model serious failures only) can still be actioned by the system or a PST-skilled Service Officer after the customer changes address and provider

Note: it may take a number of hours for a change in address to trigger a transfer to a new provider. The transfer can be delayed if the record is locked by staff or customers.
