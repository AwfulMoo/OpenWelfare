Requesting a refund

For staff responsible for raising and recovering debts only.

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Determine the most appropriate action + Read more ...

Before requesting a refund, check the record to determine if a refund is appropriate. In some circumstances, it may be more appropriate to raise the debt. For example:

  • where the executor has contacted Services Australia to request the debt be raised, or
  • the customer passed away several months ago and it is clear the bank account is closed

If it is more appropriate to:

  • raise the debt, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • proceed with a refund request, go to Step 2

2

Request refund of payment from a financial institution (FI) + Read more ...

Request a refund from the FI where a payment was made after the death of a customer.

If the request for refund relates to:

  • an Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) scheme payment, go to Step 3
  • a Child Care Subsidy (CCS) payment, go to Step 4
  • a group payment made to an organisation, go to Step 5
  • an International FI, go to Step 6
  • a Payment Nominee for the deceased customer, go to Step 7

For all other reviews and/or teams, go to Step 8.

3

Requesting a refund of payment for a deceased AIC customer + Read more ...

The system needs to update before it can send an automatic letter.

If the system is not yet updated:

  • cancel or delete the letter for an incomplete request
  • issue a Q999 letter to the FI. See the Resources page for an example
  • create a future activity for 14 days in the future to check if the payment has been returned, use the keywords:
    • 'Refund' and
    • '3RD'
  • record the details of the request for refund on a DOC

If the payment is not returned:

  • send a second request
  • if payment is not fully returned after the second request, create a debt shell to be investigated

Has the payment been returned by the FI?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

4

Requesting a refund of payment for a deceased CCS customer + Read more ...

Do not recall CCS payments from the Payment Summary (PS) screen.

See Replacing direct credit, New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) payments at a customer's request.

Procedure ends here.

5

Refund from a group payment to an organisation + Read more ...

Record a negative adjustment against an organisation when a customer has:

  • died, and an instalment of their pension has been paid to the organisation after their death, or
  • been underpaid because of a payment being paid incorrectly to an organisation

Record these adjustments at any time during the pension fortnight.

The system will combine and deduct money due to the negative adjustment from the next group payment to the organisation.

Adjustments must be recorded against an organisation when a:

  • customer leaves the organisation before a payment is made, and the organisation still receives the payment
  • customer dies and the money is still paid
  • group payment is made in error, or
  • request for a refund from a group payment to an organisation is required following the death of a customer

For details on coding, see Reissuing a group payment paid to an organisation in error.

Procedure ends here.

6

Refunds from an International FI + Read more ...

  • Send a Q463 letter - recall request from International FI
  • Code a temporary write-off as awaiting estate for 90 days

If the international FI does not respond, recover the debts from the customer's estate.

Procedure ends here.

7

Refunds from a payment nominee's FI + Read more ...

Do not proceed with a request for refund from a payment nominee's FI, recover the debts from the customer's estate.

If the payment nominee was an organisation see Nominee organisations

Procedure ends here.

8

Request a refund from the FI + Read more ...

Do not undertake this process until 4 weeks have passed since the customer's death. An automatic advice is sent to the deceased estate.

When requesting a refund from the FI for all other reviews and/or teams:

  • do not use the Request for Refund (RFR) process to recall part payments. Raise any debt shells for partial payments only
  • request a refund from the FI before raising a debt, if the overpayment includes:
    • at least one full Entitlement Period End Day's (EPED) payment, and
    • an additional partial payment

In the customer's record:

  • go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen
  • select the payment/s to be returned by keying 'F' next to the payment line
  • press [Enter]

The RFR screen shows the refund details of the:

  • payment type
  • amount
  • payment destination

Check the details to ensure the correct payment is being refunded.

  • Make sure the Reason for Refund field defaults to DCD (deceased)
  • Complete the Source and Date of Receipt (DOR) fields
  • Key 'Y' in the Finished? (Y/Q/N) field
    • if the activity is to be Quality On Line (QOL) checked, key 'Q' in the Finished? (Y/Q/N) field
  • Press [Enter]. The system displays the warning 'W030PL. If this is a deceased customer, code the DEA screen first'
  • Press [Enter]. On the PS screen, the payment displays the code RFR

Have any deductions been made from the customer's payment, or does the total overpayment include a partial payment?

  • Yes, if there are any deductions:
    • complete the Debt Investigation Fast Note and attach it to debt shell activity
    • place the activity on hold for 28 days
    • when the activity comes DUE, go to Step 9
  • No, the undetermined debt should be manually finalised 'no debt' (FND). The system will create a new debt shell if FI does not return the payment after 28 days. Procedure ends here

9

Check for returned payment + Read more ...

Does the payment show on the PS screen as returned by the FI?

  • Yes and the payment was:
  • returned in full, procedure ends here
  • returned in part, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Non or partial return of payment from the FI after the death of a customer + Read more ...

If the FI states the payment cannot be returned, the FI will advise if the payment/s:

  • have already been withdrawn and give the name of the person who withdrew the payment (if known)
  • have been redirected to another account and give the BSB, account number and the name of the person who redirected the payment (if known), or
  • have been returned to Services Australia by cheque and give the cheque number
  • cannot be returned for any other reason

Investigate the debt if all or part of the payment is outstanding.

Note: assess the gross amount received by the customer. Any deductions from the gross amount paid are a matter for the estate. Services Australia must recover the gross amount paid after death.

If the FI claims to have returned the payment and the PS screen does not reflect this, see Recalled payments not received by Services Australia.

Did a third party incorrectly receive the payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 4

Can the third party who received the payment or cashed the cheque be identified? + Read more ...

  • No, see Step 1 in Table 4

Raising the debt

For staff responsible for raising and recovering debts only.

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Death action + Read more ...

Has the death been confirmed?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No:
    • Record the executor's name, address and contact details on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen
    • Create an ACTDOC with keyword 'ACTADM' and 'DTH' and allocate it to the local office for action
    • Where possible include the executor reference number, solicitor or Trustee details and any additional information given by the executor

2

Date of death + Read more ...

Go to the Death/Executor (DEA) screen to locate the date of death.

Have 4 weeks passed since the date of the customer's death?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No:
    • If debts exist, temporarily write off the debt(s) using reason code Deceased Awaiting Estate for 4 weeks from the date of death. See Temporary write off of Centrelink debts
    • DOC the temporary write off using the W/O Awaiting Estate Fast Note
    • Finalise the recovery activity on the Activity List (AL) screen
    • Code a manual review to action the debt after the bereavement period

3

Investigate the record + Read more ...

Note: the estate may be due a refund if both the estate and the FI have returned the payment(s). See Refunding debt repayments

Is there an outstanding debt?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No:
    • Advise the executor there are no outstanding debts, and if a debt is found at a later date, a written advice will be issued
    • Confirm the executor's address and contact details
    • DOC the details of this interaction
    • Procedure ends here

4

Action the debt + Read more ...

The system will generate one debt activity for each RFR requested. Only raise and recover one debt with the gross amount paid. All other debt activities relating to the RFR should be manually finalised 'no debt' (FND).

For undetermined debts:

  • advise the executor of the potential debt
  • explain that the agency will investigate the debt and send a debt advice when the debt has been assessed
  • confirm the executor's address and contact details
  • DOC the details of this interaction
  • raise the debt manually. Complete the following:
    • Supress the Account Payable letter on the Account Payable (OPAOD) screen
    • Issue a Q080 letter that captures all debts on record (if possible)
    • Check that the manual advice has been issued on the Key Date (OPKD) screen

For determined debts:

  • advise the executor of the outstanding debt
  • confirm the executor's address and contact details
  • DOC the details of this interaction
  • issue a Q080 letter to the executor that captures all debts on record (if possible)

5

Income Management (IM) customers + Read more ...

Were the customer's payments being income managed at the time of their death and has the debt been raised due to late notification of death?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, take recovery action. See Recovering debts from a deceased customer's estate

6

Late notification of death + Read more ...

Only offset the debt with the funds paid incorrectly due to late notification of death. This means:

  • funds are paid into the IM account after the customer's date of death
  • funds are available in the IM account
  • reason for debt is late notification of death

Are there any funds in the customer's IM account?

  • Yes, send an email to the Income Management Payment and Contact Team (IMPACT). Request that the team offset the debt with the applicable amount of income managed funds. Procedure ends here
  • No, recovery action will proceed. See Recovering debts from a deceased customer's estate. Procedure ends here

Raise the debt + Read more ...

If the full payment amount(s) is returned to the agency, the review created by the RFR will automatically cancel. No further action is necessary.

If the FI and the estate have both returned the payment, the estate may be due a refund. See Refunding debt repayments.

If a deduction has been paid from the customer's payment, or if the FI was unable to return the full payment:

  • action the manual review by raising a debt to recover the difference between the:
    • gross amount of payment, and
    • the actual amount returned
    • see Step 1 in Table 3

Raise the debt using the Debt Action script. The script gives the option of creating a new debt if required.

If a payment was issued to a third party account:

  • Debt must have a Reason: of RFO (Incorrect bank account Third party)
  • Additional Reference No: of the original Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Take recovery action
