This document explains the guidelines for emergency payment claims lodged outside of the time limit.

On this page:

Emergency payments

Emergency payments include:

  • Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP)
  • Disaster Recovery Payment for New Zealand Special Category (subclass 444) visa holders (NZ DRP) - previously known as ex-gratia DRP
  • Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA)
  • Disaster Recovery Allowance for New Zealand Special Category (subclass 444) visa holders (NZ DRA) - previously known as ex-gratia DRA

Claiming emergency payments

Claims for emergency payments must be lodged within 6 months of the date the disaster was declared. See Claiming emergency payments.

If a claim is lodged outside of the allowable claiming period, a decision must be made that there are special circumstance that justify the acceptance of the claim before starting assessment of the claim.

Special circumstances

Special circumstances are generally due to situations that are unusual, uncommon or exceptional and each situation must be assessed on an individual basis. As such, there is no precise, exhaustive or definitive list of what constitutes a special circumstance.

In all situations, consideration must be given to why the customer was unable to lodge their claim within the claiming period and each application must be considered on its merits.

Factors to be considered when making a decision about special circumstances

A holistic view of the individual's circumstances should be applied when making a decision about special circumstances. There may not be just one factor, which makes a situation unusual or 'out of the ordinary' but a combination of factors that apply to the individual. Service Officers should consider:

  • The nature of the event or situation that prevented the customer from lodging their claim within the claiming period. (for example, natural disaster, bereavement, ill health, personal issues, accident)
  • Was the event or situation that delayed the lodgement of their claim outside the customer's control?
  • Was the customer prevented from taking action to lodge their claim before they were impacted by the event/situation? How were they prevented from taking action?
  • When did the event/situation that prevented them from lodging the claim occur?
  • How long was the delay between the event/situation that prevented them from lodging their claim and the actual lodgement of their claim?

Preventing fraud

For more details, see Emergency payments - preventing fraud.
