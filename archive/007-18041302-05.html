Workaround

P24845 problem and workaround

The P24845 system issue may impact in the following scenarios.

  • Where a step parent is claiming Family Tax Benefit (FTB) that has been transferred to them from a biological parent, and the other biological parent also becomes entitled to receive FTB, for example their percentage of care increases to above 35%, and they make a claim
  • Where the biological parent transfers their FTB to a step parent and they later separate. Care details for the child will not be transferred correctly to Child Support if a past period care refresh is requested
  • The care may transfer to the step parent's child support record (with incorrect child relationship details) causing inaccurate child support assessments and impacts to multiple parties

As a result, where FTB is transferred out to partner ('TOP' is coded on the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen - from parent to step parent) and the customer is a Centrelink and Child Support mutual customer, these records are to be referred for investigation. See the Resources page of Assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for further information, including details of the workaround and recording of Display on Access (DOA) DOCs.

Table 5: this table describes how to undertake a manual Benefit Transfer process from PPP to PPS which is only to be completed in limited circumstances and can only be done in Customer First or Customer Record.

Step

Action

1

Manual benefit transfer

The workaround can only be performed in Customer First or Customer Record. After assessing that the customer is eligible for PPS:

On Name Search (IN) screen in Sys: PGA code the following fields:

  • Customer surname, first name and date of birth
  • ServRsn: PPS
  • Act: BTR
  • Source: and DOR: For a BTR the correct Date of Receipt (DOR) refers to notification of changed relationship status, so is the date on which the agency became aware of the change of circumstances
  • Press [Enter]

2

Select customer's record

On the IL screen, 'S'elect the customer's record and press [Enter].

Confirm: 'Y' the selection on the Person Details (PED) screen and press [Enter].

3

Marital Status (MS) screen

On the MS screen update:

  • relationship status
  • date separated
  • PPE details

Press [Enter]

4

Further coding

  • If suspended, restore PPP on the Benefit Action (BA) screen from the date of suspension
  • Within the Benefit Transfer screen flow, update the Customer Details Task Selector (CDTS) screen with any changes to the customer details
Select each PP child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen and check/update child screens via Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen. To add or link a child, see Linking a child to a customer's record

5

Employment income

Record employment income if necessary. If receiving employment income, customer will be required to report fortnightly. If required, update the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen accordingly.

If the customer or the former partner have earnings this must be coded for the period up to and including the day before the customer is linked and coded prior to the BTR activity. If the PPP customer is on two weekly reporting then once the BTR is completed stimulate the final PPP payment.

6

Check results

Check the results on the Assessment Results (AR) screen for correctness. Ensure the transfer date (DOE) is correct prior to completing the activity. See DOE for cancellation of PPP table for examples.

Any outstanding transfer claim should be deleted if the manual work around applied successfully.

A letter will be sent to the customer to advise new rate of payment. Place DOC on the record to advise manual benefit transfer actioned.

Workflows for Parenting Payment (PP)

Table 6: this table describes the most common staff workflows in Customer First that will be presented in the Parenting Payment (PP) Social Application (claim workflow) and the order in which they must be actioned.

Note: other workflows may display based on the customer's circumstances. For example, 'Refer to Complex Assessment Officer' or 'Generate Compliance Action'. Any additional workflows are a trigger that further action may be required. Further processing must be completed in Workspace.

Required Order

Workflow and Function

1st

Manage dependent children

Allows dependent children to be updated, for example link child. The status of this workflow must be Completed to run data load.

If this completes with errors, manually update the status of the workflow to Completed and launch Workspace to complete any linking or unlinking separate to the claim activity before running data load.

Note: in some cases it will be necessary to unlink in a separate activity in Workspace before running the workflow, for example unlink from previous partner before using workflow to link new partner.

2nd

Data load

This workflow automatically takes information from the customer's record and from their online claim and loads it into a new claim activity on the customer's record.

Note: data load will occur automatically upon entry into the Social Application (online claim workflow) unless the Manage Partner, Manage Dependent Child or Participation Interview workflows are present. If one or more of these are present, data load may occur automatically when these workflows are finalised.

Data load should only ever be run once, unless regenerating the claim.

If automatic data load does not occur, launch data load workflow.

Launch data load workflow:

  • Manual Action Required messages may appear when manual updates may be required, for example if information currently on record has not been declared in the claim, such as real estate, businesses, private trusts or companies, shares or managed investments. These messages cannot be viewed again once data load has completed, so they must be addressed by the Service Officer completing the data load. Follow up with the customer if necessary and update the record as required
  • Select Finish to complete the data load
  • The data load workflow status may be Completed with errors. If the status is Complete with errors, this must be manually changed to Completed before running the Finalise claim workflow
  • Click on the grey box to the left of the data load workflow to display errors and warnings

  • Continue processing other staff workflows, excluding Finalise Claim, before completing any manual updates via Workspace.

Any

Plan next Centrelink contact

This workflow is not yet functional. It will ensure the customer's 'next contact' if required is arranged.

Any

Verify employment separation

Allows staff to verify the separation information provided as part of the claim. This workflow can be also be added manually when staff deem it necessary to add a new employment separation.

Any

Identity confirmation

Check the Identity Summary screen, if a customer has an existing Identity Confirmation Indicator (ICI) of 'Confirmed', code 'IC' on the legacy Proof of Identity (POI) screen. Select Continue.

The Activity List (AL) screen will display and the identity confirmation data will have transferred to the customer's claim.

If the ICI displays another status follow up Identity Confirmation with the customer.

Any

Proof of separation

Verification of Separation (PSP) screen coding is no longer mandatory.

Any

Verify accommodation

To be run only if accommodation has been verified within the claim (for example rent certificate or lease provided).

Otherwise, data load will complete appropriate coding on the Accommodation Circumstances (AC) screen.

Any

Verify Australian residence

Allows staff to view residence information to confirm the customer meets residence requirements.

Any

Supply tax file number (for applicant)

To be run if a tax file number or exemption is to be recorded for the customer.

Any

Advise customer about future participation requirements

Customer has indicated their youngest child is approaching 6 years of age. If PPS is granted, contact customer to advise change in participation requirements when their child turns 6 years of age.

Any

Advise customer about future cancellation

Customer has indicated their youngest child is approaching 8 years of age. If PPS is granted, contact customer to advise future cancellation when their child turns 8 years of age.

Last

Finalise claim

Launches the Assessment Results (AR) screen for claim finalisation. This should be run once all other claim updates have been completed.

Note:

Ensure all relevant information included in the online claim has been coded accordingly on the customer's record. Some information will require manual action to complete, for example information relating to income from employment.

Access screens and workflows not available within the Social Application (claim workflow) via Workspace. A claim can be selected from the Activity List (AL) screen to make additional updates within the claim activity.

The status of this workflow must be Completed for the Social Application (claim workflow) status to be Approved.
