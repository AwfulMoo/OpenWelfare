Care subsidy reduction (CSR) to zero

Step

Action

1

Check if care recipient is still in care and get start date + Read more ...

Confirm care details by checking Aged Care Management and Payment System (ACMPS) and the System for the Payment of Aged Residential Care (SPARC):

  • home care recipients:
    • access ACMPS Assessments & Events under Entry Details for the start date
    • check Departure details to see if care recipient is in care
    • there are no pre entry leave provisions
  • residential care recipients:
    • access SPARC Client Profile (screen 2.6)
    • respite care does not apply when deciding the start date
    • the Pre-entry leave date (where relevant) is to be the Start Date
    • any pre-entry can be viewed by expanding the ADM P line

Is this care recipient currently in home or residential care?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 6

2

Period of care that CSR to zero applies + Read more ...

What period of care is the enquiry related to?

  • Current period of care:
    • Care recipient is still in care with the same provider, go to Step 3
  • Previous period of care:
    • Care recipient is no longer in care, go to Step 6
    • Care recipient is now in care with a new service provider, go to Step 6

3

Have fees been set for current care period + Read more ...

For home care:

  • check ACMPS Fees & Charges tab

For residential care:

  • for care recipients assessed under the pre 1 July 2014 assessment rules:
    • check SPARC View Income Testing Review Fees and Refunds (screen 2.15)
  • for care recipients assessed under the post 1 July 2014 assessment rules:
    • check SPARC Means Testing Fees and Refunds (screen 2.30)

Have home care/residential care fees been set for this care recipient?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • further remediation is needed before advice is given
    • see Profiling and manual refresh of aged care means assessment data
    • once fees are set, CSR to zero will be applied automatically
    • for previous periods of care, go to Step 6
    • procedure ends here

4

Has a fee advice been issued for current care period + Read more ...

Check to see if the care recipient has been correctly notified of their care subsidy reduction:

  • ACMPS Communication History for both home care and residential care recipients
  • ACMPS Notes tab – for both home care and residential care
    • For residential care recipients, RC39 letters sent from ACMPS before 14 November 2015 qualify as fee advice as they contain the means tested fee
    • RC39 letters sent after this date do not contain a means tested fee. The care recipient has not been correctly notified of their fees
  • SPARC Income Testing Letter Reprint Facility (screen 2.13) for residential care recipients only
    • Change the Letter Date From field to a date before the start date of care
  • SPARC Client Notes (screen 2.20)
  • Customer First – Document Tools and Document List

Has the care recipient received advice of fees within 6 months of entry into care?

  • Yes,
    • CSR to zero does not apply for the current care period
    • For previous period of care, go to Step 6
    • Procedure ends here
  • No,
    • Go to Step 5

5

Current care period but fee advice not issued + Read more ...

When fees are set and advice issued to the care recipient more than 6 months after entry, the care recipient does not pay any ITF/MTCF from the date of entry until the day before the advice was issued. The care recipient is issued with a Series 9 advice which states:

  • the period of $0 fees, and
  • the correct ongoing fees

These advices commenced production in February 2019. For any CSR to zero period before this a manual calculation may be required.

Action required:

  • Service Officers are to escalate to ICT via MySupport when:
    • fees have been set more than 6 months after entry into care
    • 5 days have elapsed, and
    • the fee advice has not been issued
  • Service Officers are to refer for further investigation when:
    • fees were set more than 6 months after entry into care, and
    • an incorrect advice was issued
    • create a Fast Note using Auto Text – Aged Care > Enquiry > Aged Care Man Adj Request Fees
  • procedure ends here

6

Care recipient discharged before provider receives fee advice + Read more ...

Fee advice must be sent at least 7 days earlier than the discharge date.

Check the following to decide whether a provider has been correctly notified:

  • ACMPS Communication History for both home care and residential care recipients
  • ACMPS Notes tab – for both home care and residential care
  • SPARC Income Testing Letter Reprint Facility (screen 2.13) for residential care recipients only
  • Change the Letter Date From: field to a date prior to the start date of care
  • SPARC Client Notes (screen 2.20)
  • Customer First – Document Tools and Document List

Is the care recipient requesting a letter to confirm that they are eligible for CSR to Zero? (including where a care recipient transfers to another facility, has not passed away and requires a letter)

  • Yes,
    • Service Officers who complete Deceased Manual assessments, go to Step 8
    • to create a work item to refer to Deceased Manual assessment team, go to Step 7
  • No,
    • document outcome of investigation in Customer First and ACMPS or SPARC
    • procedure ends here

7

Create a work item requesting a manual letter be issued + Read more ...

Creation and issue of a manual letter confirming CSR to zero is restricted to staff with the MANADJ or RCADEA skill tag.

  • Create a Fast Note using Auto Text – Aged Care > Enquiry > Aged Care Discharged CR – Man Let Req
  • Procedure ends here

8

Issue manual CSR to zero letters + Read more ...

For Aged Care – Manual deceased assessment staff only

Care recipient is now discharged from care. They require a manual letter advising that they do not have to pay any ITCF/MTCF for their time in care. Details of letter types are available in Aged care letter codes and descriptions.

Select the relevant letter template:

Home care - Manual CSR to zero letter templates

  • HC-9-9D-NM-MAN Nominee
  • HC-9-9D-CR-MAN Care recipient
  • HC-9-9D-SVC-MAN Service

Residential care - Manual CSR to zero letter templates – to be used where the care recipient has departed care and is not deceased

Liable for Accommodation Payment

  • NT Nominee
  • NU Care recipient
  • NV Service

Liable for Accommodation Contribution

  • MT Nominee
  • MU Care recipient
  • MV Service

Residential care - Manual CSR to zero letter templates – to be used where the care recipient departed care and is deceased.

Liable for Accommodation Payment

  • NK Estate
  • NL Service

Liable for Accommodation Contribution

  • MK Estate
  • ML Service

Issue a manual letter for each entry into care where CSR to zero is to be applied:

  • complete the applicable letter templates
  • upload PDF copies to the Customer First record as Form Type: UNS005 – Financial
  • see Attaching electronic documents to a Centrelink customer's record using Document Upload in Customer First for assistance if required
  • send letters

9

Document action taken + Read more ...

In Customer First for home care and residential care

Add a DOC via Auto Text in Fast Note:

  • Category 1: select Aged Care
  • Category 2: select Enquiry
  • Template: select Aged Care – CSR to Zero – Granted
  • Text: Add/edit information as indicated

In ACMPS for home care

Add an ACMPS Note:

  • fee advice issued DD/MM/YY S88(1)(b) applied for periods DD/MM/YY to DD/MM/YY for SVC <insert Service name and ID>, and DD/MM/YY to DD/MM/YY for SVC <insert Service name and ID>

In SPARC for residential care

Add a SPARC Client Note (screen 2.20):

  • leave the RACS ID field blank when the notes refer to multiple services
  • fee advice issued DD/MM/YY S39(1)(b) applied for periods DD/MM/YY to DD/MM/YY for SVC <insert Service name and ID>, and DD/MM/YY to DD/MM/YY for SVC <insert Service name and ID>
    • the Client Notes 180 character limit has been taken into account

Procedure ends here
