This document outlines the processes to follow when documentation is provided or a customer contacts after their claim is rejected due to not supplying documentation in time as requested. This procedure applies to all claims except ABSTUDY, Farm Household Allowance, family assistance and claims for add-on payments and services. It applies regardless of how the claim was lodged.

Requesting documentation

Documents or actions needed for claim assessment may be requested in the following ways:

  • Required Tasks within online claims, see Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM)
  • A claim form may specify that a form, document or other attachment needs to be provided with the claim form
  • Request for information

If the customer does not supply the requested documents or take necessary action within the specified timeframe, their claim may be rejected.

Note: claims must not to be rejected for failing to supply documents requested as Supplementary Tasks. See Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM).

Rejection (REJ) reasons

This process covers the following rejection reasons only:

  • failed to reply to correspondence (FRC)
  • failed to supply documents (FSD)
  • proof of identity (POI)
  • insufficient/no medical evidence for DSP (IME)

For requests from customers to reopen/reassess claims rejected for other reasons, for example Income too high (INC), see:

  • Cancellation, Suspension and Rejection reason codes (CLK), or
  • Request for an explanation or application for a formal review

Claim lodgement

To receive a payment or concession from Services Australia, a customer must lodge a completed claim. See Claim lodgement of Centrelink claims.

When a customer claims a payment or concession from Services Australia, they must provide documentation to verify their circumstances and other details.

A customer in vulnerable circumstances, who has genuine difficulty providing documents, can lodge their claim and then provide any requested documentation. See Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.

If the documentation is not provided, the claim can be rejected.

Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM) aligned claims

Customers lodging claims aligned under CCM must complete all Required Tasks (documents or actions) before being able to submit their online claim, unless it is not reasonable for them to do so. See Claim submission exceptions.

The customer may also be presented with Supplementary Tasks. These documents are not essential for a claim to be submitted and assessed, but help confirm extra assistance, for example, a rent certificate.

Customers will see a message when starting their claim advising them to submit it as soon as possible to be paid from the earliest possible date if they are eligible.

Draft claims will remain available for 13 weeks from the start date, after which they will expire and customers must start another claim. There are exceptions for vulnerable customers.

Extension to lodge requested documents

The timeframe can be extended if the claim is undetermined and there are special circumstances preventing the customer from lodging the requested documents for the claim.

Special circumstances may include:

  • a customer is having practical difficulties in meeting the timeframe because they live in a remote area or overseas
  • the customer is required to obtain the documentation from a third party, or
  • an emergency interfering with customer's plans to lodge within the timeframe. Generally the emergency is unforeseen and for a relatively short term. It includes, but is not limited to, medical or family emergency and recognised natural disasters

See Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.

Application for a formal review of a decision

A customer may apply for a formal review of the decision to reject their claim at any time. If they apply more than 13 weeks after the rejection notice was given, payment or concession may be affected by date of effect rules. See Customer initiated review of decisions.

There is no need for additional evidence or information before an application for a formal review can be made.

If a customer applies for a formal review, record the application. See Request for an explanation or application for a formal review.
