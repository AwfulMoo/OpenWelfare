Calculating the rate of Special Benefit payable

Step

Action

1

Determine the maximum rate of SpB the customer is eligible to be paid + Read more ...

Identify the maximum rate of JobSeeker Payment, Youth Allowance (YA) or Austudy the customer would otherwise receive if the customer was qualified for one of those payments and the payment was payable.

Exception: for Category ACC (Australian Citizen Child), the customer’s maximum rate will vary based on the family unit’s situation.

Is the customer an Australian Citizen Child (ACC)?

  • Yes, see Special Benefit (SpB) for children who are Australian Citizen Children (ACC). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Parental Means Test + Read more ...

When the customer is living with their parents, the rate of SpB payable is subject to the parental means test (as per YA).

With the exception of category ACC (Australian Citizen Child), is the customer considered to be dependent on another person (other than their partner) such as their parent(s)/guardian(s)?

  • Yes, calculate any impacts using the Parental Means Test:
    • any impact from the Parental Means will be coded by Special Benefit processing teams
    • go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Personal income from all sources + Read more ...

Identify the customer's personal income from all sources.

Does the customer's fortnightly income and/or reduction from the Parental Means Test exceed the maximum equivalent fortnightly rate of SpB calculated in Step 1?

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for SpB. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

SpB is reduced by personal income received + Read more ...

Under the Special Benefit income test, all personal income is deducted dollar for dollar from the SpB payment.

Note: income periods of less than a fortnight are calculated on a pro-rata basis.

Does the customer receive any personal income?

  • Yes, calculate the value of any personal income the customer receives
    • The value of any personal income the customer receives is coded on the system in the regular manner by Special Benefit processing teams
    • Go to Step 5
  • No, Go to Step 5

5

In kind or financial support received + Read more ...

With the exception of free board and/or lodgings, the monetary value of any in kind or financial support is deducted dollar for dollar from the SpB payment.

If the person receives any in kind or financial support on a regular basis, the amount of the support is deducted from the rate payable after the personal income test is applied.

Does the customer receive any in kind or financial support?

  • Yes, calculate the value of any in kind or financial support (not including free board and/or lodgings) the customer receives on an ongoing basis
    • The value of any in kind or financial support must be coded on the OINS screen by Special Benefit processing teams as follows:
      - Type: field, 'OTH'
      - Description: field, 'description of the income source'
      - Reference: leave blank
      - Event date: field, the start date of the income source or the start date of the new claim when the income started before the grant
      - Amount $.cc: field, the amount of income
      - Freq: field, 'The appropriate frequency of the income'
    • Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Partnered customers + Read more ...

Any 'excess income' from the partner of a SpB customer will reduce the customer's rate of SpB by a dollar for dollar deduction.

Partner 'excess income' is any income over the point at which the partner is precluded from receiving their own Social Security benefit, (for example, JobSeeker Payment, Special Benefit, Youth Allowance). This cut-off point differs depending on the Social Security benefit the partner is receiving.

Does the customer have a partner with any ‘excess income’?

  • Yes, calculate the value of excess partner income. See the Resources page for examples. Go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Free board and/or lodgings + Read more ...

For free board and/or lodgings the rate of payment is reduced as follows:

  • two-thirds of the rate of SpB payable if both board and lodging are provided, or
  • one-third of the rate of SpB payable if only lodging or only board is provided

Does the customer receive free board and/or lodgings?  

  • Yes,
    • The remaining rate of payment will be impacted by the information on the Accommodation (AC) screen coded by Special Benefit processing teams:
      - Coding the Type of Rent: field with either 'FBL' (free board and lodgings), 'FBD' (free board) or 'FLD' (free lodgings) will allow the system to calculate the reduced rate
    • Note: if the code No Rent Paid (NRP) is used on the customer's record, they will receive the maximum basic rate:
      - NRP should only be used for customers in a temporary situation or who are legitimately homeless to make sure they are not penalised by a rate reduction
      - For example, when someone provides temporary free accommodation while the customer awaits payment of SpB or where they are only staying on someone's couch/floor while they find somewhere else to live
    • Go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Rate of Special Benefit + Read more ...

The result from the above tests and deductions is the customer's rate of SpB payment entitlement for the relevant payment period. See the Resources page for examples.

Procedure ends here.
