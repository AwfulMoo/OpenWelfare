This page contains the process where a payer has failed to pay the initial payment due after registration (Initial Payment Defaulter - IPD), or has defaulted on a payment after a period of compliance (First Time Defaulter - FTD).

On this Page:

Identify and review payment defaults

Customer contact after a payment default

Finalise payment defaulter action

Identify and review payment defaults

Table 1: This table describes the process for identifying and reviewing payment defaults.

Step

Action

1

Payment default - action required + Read more ...

If a payer has not made the first payment they are liable to make or if they have defaulted for the first time on a payment after a period of compliance, the following intrays will be created:

  • Initial Payment Defaulter (IPD) - Payers who have defaulted on the very first payment.
    • Initial Payment Defaulter with employer details (IPDEMEX1)
    • Initial Payment Defaulter without employer details (IPDNOEMP)
  • First Time Defaulter (FTD) - Payers who default on a payment after a period of compliance.
    • First Time Defaulter with employer details (FTDEMEX1)
    • First Time Defaulter without employer details (FTDNOEMP)

2

Review the case + Read more ...

Review the customer’s case including incomes, care, and relevant dependent children. Determine the arrears owing.

Investigate Cuba:

  • Payer Account Summary: Are there arrears owing or has a payment been received since the intray was created which satisfies the debt?
  • Activity Summary: Are there any recent payer contact, changes, or any issues pending? Identify any issues that may indicate the reason for non-payment including unprocessed estimates, unresolved applications for the credit of non- agency payments (NAPS) or Stay Orders etc
  • Case Liability Rate/Income: Check the assessment details, including income, to ensure the accuracy of the liability
  • Employer Relationship: Is the payer paying their child support liability via employer withholding (EW)? If so check if Child Support is still expecting payment from the employer and confirm if there are actually arrears outstanding? Was the customer advised to pay manually prior to payments being received from the employer? For further information refer to 2.1 Verify account balance
  • Stop Withholding: Is there a Stop Withholding indicator in place on Cuba? If so, should it still be in place? A Stop Withholding indicator may be activated when:
    • a court orders a stay of collection
    • a payer's election for Employer Withholding not to apply is allowed in favour of voluntary payments (Form 5), or
    • a decision is made that Employer Withholding would not be an efficient method of collection

Revoke the election for EW not to apply and set up EW (unless special circumstances exist), if the payer:

  • is employed
  • has made an election for EW not to apply, and
  • has defaulted on a payment

3

Verify account balance + Read more ...

Child Support is calculated on an annual and daily rate. Generally a payer’s liability is calculated per calendar month however when a payer is paying via employer deductions, then their liability is calculated based on their pay cycle.

Note: the calculation of liability based on a pay cycle can cause a payer’s account to appear incorrectly to be in debt or credit. The arrears may appear incorrectly on the payer account summary because the liability accrues on the account before the payer is liable for the amounts.

4

Check Payer Account Summary + Read more ...

From the Payer Account Summary window verify the current account balance. This will be the figure with the most recent date next to it. It is also the amount displayed in the Balances box with the words closing as at: xx/xx/xxx. It is not the overdue amount.

5

Check Cuba + Read more ...

Determine if any liability appears on the payer’s account but is not yet due, by checking the Maintenance due next amount and date. This will be any liability that falls due on the following 7th of the month, that is, not due to be received from the employer until the following month.

Example

If it is the 17th of December it will be any liability that will fall due on the 7th of January. If a variation to the account has been actioned it won’t fall due until the 7th day of the month after it appears on a statement.

If employer withholding is in place the employer deductions may have been deducted from the payer’s salary but not yet received.

A discrepancy between the expected deductions and what Child Support actually receive could be due to a number of factors:

  • the customer earned less than the protected earnings amount for that pay cycle
  • the pay cycle is not set up correctly or has changed – it is important to confirm the dates of the pay cycle with the customer or payroll officer
  • the pay dates are incorrect
  • the customer has been on unpaid leave during the pay cycle
  • a delay has resulted from when the link was first established, or
  • if the last payday of the month is close to the end or start of a month it may be received from the employer in a month Child Support are not expecting it. For example, if 5 salary deductions are expected from the employer in December, Child Support may only receive 4 deductions in January and an extra deduction in February because the pay clerk sent the money to Child Support before the last payday in December.

International

Determine if the default has occurred due to exchange rate fluctuations for payers overseas. If this is the only reason for the default, do not contact the customer. Monitor the case in the following month.

Customer contact after a payment default

Table 2: This table describes the customer contact requirements after a payment default has occurred.

Step

Action

1

Contact the payer + Read more ...

Contact the payer as soon as possible before the next payment falls due unless:

  • the customer has an election for employer withholding not to apply, or
  • establishing employer withholding without customer contact is not appropriate

See Contact with Child Support customers

Discuss:

  • reasons for default
  • their commitment to pay the outstanding amount in line with Debt repayment, commencing with a request for payment in full, and an undertaking to make future payments on time, and
  • any other child support issues

Has payer contact been made?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 5

2

Reason for default

+ Read more ...

Discuss the missed payment and establish the reason for default. It is important to identify and attempt to resolve the reason for non-payment to increase the likelihood of compliance in the future. Determine if any non-agency payments have been made.

When working with the payer to resolve their default reasons, be aware of the different Social worker and other service referrals for child support customers available. Customers may miss payments for various reasons.

For assistance in managing payment negotiations, see the Assertive Debt Approach.

3

Obtain commitment for payment

+ Read more ...

Negotiate payment of the outstanding amount in line with Debt repayment.

Explain the benefits of paying child support by establishing periodic payments via BPAY ® or EFT.

Ensure that the payer is aware that if they are unable to meet their child support payments, they must contact Child Support to discuss their circumstances. If a payment is missed enforcement action will occur with no further contact.

4

Resolve any other issues

+ Read more ...

Confirm the regular amount due and if relevant discuss future payment options. If the payer indicates they find it difficult to meet their regular payments, ensure the customer's current financial position is appropriately reflected in the assessment. It may also be beneficial to send the booklet, Me and My Money. See Products and services Cuba Process Help.

Resolve any other issues the payer has and confirm the date of the payer’s next payment.

See the Finalise payment defaulter action table.

5

No payer contact

+ Read more ...

If unable to contact the payer, issue a First Time Defaulter Payer Letter (MX17-1) requesting the payer contact Child Support to discuss their payments, see Letters Cuba Process Help, Create a letter using fill-in fields. This letter is generated from the Letter List window in Cuba.

If the customer is on ‘tracing’, contact the payee for information and discuss if any non-agency payments have been made.

If the customer does not respond to the First Time Defaulter Payer Letter and no payment has been received, take enforcement action. See the following resources:

  • Collection of child support debts through third parties (s72A and s72AC)
  • Employer Withholding (linking) for Child Support customers
  • Centrelink deductions used to collect child support debts

Finalise payment defaulter action

Table 3: This table describes how to finalise a payment default by documenting customer contact and monitoring for payment.

Step

Action

1

Document + Read more ...

Record all attempted and/or successful contact with an IPD/FTD customer in the Payer Collection window, see Documentation Cuba Process Help.

Record any payment arrangements negotiated in the Payment Arrangement window, see Payment arrangements Cuba Process Help.

Document:

  • date and time of the contact made with the customer
  • telephone number contact was made on (ensure customer details are updated where required)
  • reason for non payment
  • date and method agreed upon for this and future payments
  • agreed review date
  • any other issues discussed, and action taken if necessary

See Documenting Child Support information.

2

Intray deletion + Read more ...

The IPD/FTD intray will delete when:

  • a payment arrangement is entered into
  • there is no debt, or
  • the intray is manually deleted

3

Monitor payment

+ Read more ...

If a payment arrangement is recorded and payment is not received by the agreed date, a Payment Arrangement Default intray will generate.

Check the Payer Account Summary window to determine if the payment has been received. If the payment has been received update the IPD/FTD document accordingly and delete the intray.

If the payment has not been received, check the case records for any new information and contact the payer. Consider enforcement action if voluntary payment is no longer appropriate or achievable. See:

  • Collection of child support debts through third parties (s72A and s72AC)
  • Employer Withholding (linking) for Child Support customers
  • Centrelink deductions used to collect child support debts
  • The Child Support Guide 5.2: Administrative enforcement

Explore all appropriate collection action where necessary including appropriate referrals to Intensive Collection Services, utilising the Customer referral guidelines for Child Support staff.
