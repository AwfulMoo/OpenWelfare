Referring inconsistencies in overseas departure and arrival information

Step

Action

1

Service Officer requires further investigation of residence related information + Read more ...

The following cannot be overwritten by a Service Officer:

  • Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen
  • departure and return dates on the Travel Outside Australia Summary (TOAS) screen
  • visa details on the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screens

This is because information has been provided directly from the datalink between Centrelink and the Department of Home Affairs.

If the child does not have their own customer record to check immigration details:

  • go to the Child Selection (CHS) screen
  • 's'elect the relevant child and key either 'CHRSIM', 'CHTOAS' or 'CHRSLEG' in the Nxt: field as required
  • press [Enter]

Appropriate reasons for referral to Centrelink International Services (CIS) using the Investigate Match Questionnaire (IMQ) screen include:

  • The departure or arrival date is missing (common for customers travelling on a cruise), Customers are not generally required to provide evidence of a departure or return date for a cruise. If further details are required, CIS staff will contact the customer directly
  • The customer is disputing the date of departure or arrival supplied by the Immigration Datalink
  • The customer is disputing other Department of Home Affairs details, including visa or citizenship information
  • The Service Officer disputes the information

CIS will follow up this information and correct if needed.

2

Investigate residence related information + Read more ...

Go to the Immigration Link Details (RSIML) and/or child's Immigration Link Details (CHIML) screen.

Click the Report a problem with Immigration data link at the bottom left of the screen and IMQ screen will display.

Note: other fields on this screen cannot be updated by staff outside CIS.

Is the IMQ screen presented?

Note: the IMQ screen presented for a child record through the CHIML screen will be on the customer's record.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, a manual request can be sent. Go to Step 8

3

Investigate Match Questionnaire 'IMQ' + Read more ...

Select one of the following options by clicking on the radio button:

  • 'The customer is disputing the immigration data/thinks we have the wrong person'
  • 'The customer has advised of a past departure or arrival that an active immigration link has not picked up'
  • 'Service Officer has reason to believe that the data returned by immigration does not relate to the customer', or
  • 'Service Officer has reason to believe that the data provided by the immigration link is incomplete'

Ensure all relevant details are provided in the 'Please provide detailed information as to why the customer/you believe the immigration link or the data provided by the immigration link is incorrect' free text box. If the query relates to a child's record, it will be forwarded to CIS using the customer's IMQ screen. Ensure that this box is completed clearly indicating that the query relates to a child and the name and date of birth of the relevant child. The DOC created for CIS will appear on the customer's record.

4

Other names used by the customer + Read more ...

Check the Customer Details (CPD) or Change Child (CCH) screen for alternative names used by the customer or child.

Has the customer or child been known by any other names including checking possible alternative spelling?

  • Yes, include details in the free text box 'Is the customer known by any other names not already recorded on the CPDS or CCH screen? If so, please provide.' Go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Travel details since 1 September 1994 + Read more ...

Has the customer ever lived or travelled overseas since 1 September 1994?

Complete the options Yes, No or Unknown

6

Passport details + Read more ...

Does the customer have a passport?

  • Yes, add the details in the 'Passport Number' and 'Country of Issue' boxes. Go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Complete the Investigate Match Questionnaire (IMQ) + Read more ...

If necessary, add details of any relevant information in the free text box 'Is there any evidence that this information is incorrect? If so, please provide details'.

Click on the Continue option at the bottom right of the screen. A DOC will be created on the customer's record with the appropriate keywords and will be referred to CIS.

Has the relevant DOC been produced on the Activity List (AL) screen?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Manual request to investigate residence related information + Read more ...

  • In Customer First create a DOC on the customer's record with a line 'Res/Port Investigation Req'
  • Add the keyword 'INVIRN'
  • In the DOC, include the reason for the dispute from one of the following:
    • 'The customer is disputing the immigration data/thinks we have the wrong person'
    • 'The customer has advised of a past departure or arrival that an active immigration link has not picked up'
    • 'Service Officer has reason to believe that the data returned by immigration does not relate to the customer'
    • 'Service Officer has reason to believe that the data provided by the immigration link is incomplete'
  • If the query relates to a child's record, ensure that the DOC clearly indicates that the query is related to the child and include both the name and date of birth of the child
  • Record as much detail as possible as to the reason for the referral and why the customer or Service Officer believes the information to be incorrect, including any:
    • alternative names, including alternative spelling, used by the customer
    • details of travel documents, including passports, held by the customer
  • Do not complete the DOC
    • key 'AL' in the Next: field to go to the AL screen. Select the started DOC or activity link to action
    • in Selection Options, select (O), transfer activity to another site/ region. Click on Continue
    • on the Transfer Activity (TA) screen key 'WTW' in the To Regional Office: field. Click on Continue

9

The role of CIS + Read more ...

CIS will:

  • investigate to determine if a discrepancy exists and if so where this has occurred
  • correct any details on the customer's record if necessary
  • annotate the IMQ referral DOC documenting the updates. If more action is needed by the network Service Officer:
    • explain this in the annotation and
    • transfer the DOC back to the originating region
  • if no more updates are needed, finalise the DOC
  • where needed, CIS will escalate cases to CIS Virtual SSO (VSSO) Hub
  • if unable to resolve, CIS VSSO Hub will liaise with the Immigration Datalink team before referring any queries or requests regarding the customer's immigration data to the Department of Home Affairs

If necessary, tell the customer that they will be advised when the outcome is known. They may also be contacted by CIS if further information is required.

CIS contact details are available in Centrelink International Services (CIS) - contact details for staff. However, a phone call is not required unless the customer is experiencing hardship on their return to Australia and the IMQ activity needs to be actioned urgently.
