Examples of Living Away from Home Allowance payability

This table describes two situations which attract the Living Away from Home Allowance.

Item

Description

1

Living Away from Home Allowance for students who repeat the final year of primary level study at another location + Read more ...

A secondary school aged student may be approved for away from home entitlements if they are repeating the final year of primary school at another location, in the following circumstances:

  • the student does not have reasonable access to an appropriate secondary education institution, or
  • the student cannot reasonably be expected to do secondary schooling while living in the permanent home, and
  • the student is doing or intending to do secondary level study. However the secondary school assesses the student needs to repeat the final year of primary school to prepare for a successful transition to secondary study at a later date, and
  • the secondary school the student has enrolled in, or intends to enrol in, also offers the last year of primary study, and
  • the student has enrolled in the final year of primary schooling, and
  • the school is a different school to the one the student attended before, and
  • the student lives away from the permanent home

It is likely that only a boarding school that offers both secondary and primary study will meet these criteria.

Customers that meet these requirements may be eligible for living away from home allowances, even though they are enrolled in primary level study.

Evidence to support away from home rate in this circumstance is a letter from the new school stating that the student:

  • has been assessed as required to repeat the final year of primary study to transition to secondary study at a later date, and
  • the student is now enrolled in primary level study

The school does not need to advise the reasons why it has assessed the student as requiring to repeat the last year of primary school. It does not matter which primary school the student attended before or if the student passed or failed the previous attempt at primary study.

The main thing is that the new school needs them to repeat the final primary year to successfully progress to secondary level study at that school.

2

Prior to January 2022,Living Away from Home Allowance for students who would be in Year 7 primary level study in their home location but are studying at secondary level at another location + Read more ...

Customers who live away from home to attend schools that offer Year 7 as secondary, but have a permanent home in reasonable travelling time/distance of a school that offers Year 7 as primary, should only attract Schooling A benefits (if otherwise eligible), unless the student meets all the requirements listed below as a student who:

  • does secondary level study under a scholarship for a Highly Effective/High Expectations School where the student must live away from home to attend the course, but where they would have been a primary student in their home state, and
  • the school needs to be a different school to the 1 the student attended before, and
  • lives away from the permanent home

Note: students in the above situation who do not have reasonable access to Year 7 at the primary level, may be approved for Additional Assistance at the appropriate Living Allowance rate if the following criteria applies, and where the Department of Social Services determines under exceptional circumstances, on a case by case basis:

  • the student is from a state/territory or region where the final year of primary school is Year 7
  • the student has completed Year 6 and there is no local daily access to Year 7 schooling, and
  • there is no local school available providing Year 7 secondary school courses
