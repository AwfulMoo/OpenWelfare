Reconciliation Not to be reconciled (NREC) + Read more ...

A status of NREC does not mean that a customer will not be reconciled.

NREC status will be activated if there is a reason reconciliation should be delayed for a particular group of customers, or as part of a staggered roll out. The dashboard will not update or refresh as readily during periods of NREC when new data is received or existing data has changed.

  • No, do not escalate the record
    • Advise customer of what information is required before CCS reconciliation can occur

Manual intervention status - Pending ECOM + Read more ...

A pending (ECOM) CCS reconciliation will need to be reviewed by CCS MIV staff if certain conditions are met.

A pending (ECOM) CCS reconciliation result may be placed on hold to:

  • prevent CCS reconciliation debts from being automatically raised during periods when debt raising activities have been paused for customers who have been impacted by a declared major disaster or emergency. The bulk of these MIV activities do not require staff intervention. These activities can be identified by the title 'CCS Disaster Affected' which will appear when the curser is placed over the work item ID hyperlink. Go to Step 7
  • prevent CCS reconciliation in very limited cases where ICT will be required to check the reconciliation outcome. These will not require any staff intervention and will only be able to be completed by ICT. Procedure ends here

Staff can identify the status of the MIV check by the hover text that appears when moving the cursor over the work item ID.

  • For Manual Action indicates MIV checking is yet to be undertaken, and the record is affected by one of the following:
    • No Valid Address
    • provisional arrears are greater than $10,000
    • provisional debts are greater than $10,000

      These can be confirmed by entering the Work Item ID number into the claim ID field in the Process Direct Inbox.
  • Completed indicates MIV checking has already been completed and the final result is pending offsetting or a response from the ATO (wash-up)

7

CCS Disaster Affected Work Item + Read more ...

CCS Disaster Affected Work items are created when reconciliation results in a new debt for customers impacted by a declared major disaster or emergency. These work items will not be allocated to staff for action, and will be paused for a defined period. After this time, the work item will be completed and reassessed automatically. If the reconciliation outcome falls into one of the other MIV categories, a new work item will be created and assessed, as per current process.

Note: a new debt is where a customer has had no previous reconciliation debt instances relating to the relevant CCS year.

CCS reconciliation can be paused the 3 'Manual Action' criteria mentioned in Step 4 above and also for disaster affected customers, where:

  • the outcome is a debt, and
  • the customer’s principal place of residence is within an Local Government Area (LGA), declared affected by a natural disaster or emergency (including pandemics) for at least one day within a prescribed period, or
  • the customer has been granted a disaster payment such as Australia Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP) or Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA)

These activities will present with a work item entitled CCS disaster affected which will ensure the reconciliation debt is not immediately raised, but held for a certain period.

These items will not be allocated to staff for action - they will auto complete after the defined period.

In some cases, the customer may request the ‘CCS Disaster affected’ work item to be completed so that the debt can be raised immediately. Where this request is made, staff are to ensure the customer is fully aware of the consequences as a result of this request, including not being able to access Advance payments.

Note: If the Service Officer believes the debt outcome may not be correct and corrective action is required, any changes in circumstances updates should be actioned within the work item before it is completed. If the updates result in the debt being changed to a nil adjustment or arrears outcome, the work item will auto complete without any further manual intervention.

The following is to be read to the customer and documented on the record:

'Customer has requested that the CCS reconciliation debt amount of $X be raised immediately instead of being held. Customer acknowledges that once the debt is raised, standard debt repayment provisions would apply, whether this is withholdings from ongoing payments, or a voluntary cash arrangement.'

Note: during the debt pause period the below process applies.

Process during the debt pause period

If a customer asks a Service Officer to raise their debt immediately, remind them of the possible impacts including not being able to access Advance payments.

If the customer wishes to proceed to have the debt raised, staff must follow the processes below and tell customers:

  • An Account Payable Notice will issue
  • Debt recovery will remain paused unless the customer specifically asks to enter into a withholdings or payment arrangement
  • Services Australia will not refer debt to an External Collection Agent or accrue interest during the debt pause period

If the customer agrees to these terms and the Service Officer is satisfied the debt outcome is correct, the work item can be actioned, by:

  • conducting a work item search using the work item ID via the inbox
  • selecting Assess, then Finish and Finalise the activity

Once finalised, go to Customer First. Create a Fast Note, select Auto Text. Use Families > Reconciliation > Cus requests Families debt to be raised to record details of the conversation

Procedure ends here.

8

CCS reconciliation - Pending + Read more ...

Timing of CCS reconciliation

CCS reconciliation can only occur from the 29th day following the end of the CCS year at the earliest. Depending on the staggered approach taken it may be several more weeks before an outcome is known.

If a customer and their partner/s have confirmed their incomes and it is:

  • before the 29th day following the end of the CCS year, go to Step 14
  • after the 29th day following the end of the CCS year, advise we cannot balance everyone at the same time. Customers should continue to check their online accounts in the coming weeks and they will be notified of the result once it is known

Note: in some cases a reconciliation activity will show an earlier date than the outcome letter or payment issue dates. This is a result of the staggering process and should not be reported as an issue

Income Confirmation

Staff should discuss income confirmation with the customer to ensure they have done everything they need to. All CCS customers must meet CCS reconciliation requirements by confirming the income they have estimated during the relevant CCS year. This includes confirming income for any partners or ex-partners they may have had during the relevant year.

Income can be confirmed by:

  • lodging an Income Tax Return with the Australian Tax Office (ATO), or
  • advising Services Australia they are not required to lodge an Income Tax Return and providing their actual income, or
  • submitting a non-lodgement advice to the ATO, and advising actual income to Services Australia

Where the instance window status is Pending AMET or Pending PRVS, do not give customers specific information about the potential outcome as any amounts displayed are subject to change.

Note: where reconciliation is pending due to the inaction of an ex-partner, the customer can request an ex-partner most recent estimate (MRE) exception is recorded to meet income confirmation requirements for their ex-partner. See Exceptional circumstances for CCS and ACCS.

Tax Return Status + Read more ...

The Tax Return Status tab displays the current status of the income confirmation requirements for the customer and partner/ex-partner where applicable, including whether income confirmation requirements have been met. Where the customer has a partner/ex-partner it will display the length of the relationship (using CCS Monday as the commencement and CCS Sunday as the end date) under the partner/ex-partner’s name.

The Obligations Met column will display a status of either:

  • Provisional - this means either:
    • no information (i.e. ATI, RNN or not required to lodge details) has been received for the customer, partner or ex-partner; or
    • information has been received for one or more of the customer, partner or ex-partner but has not been processed. This may be because reconciliation has not commenced or because the system has not processed the information yet (this usually occurs within 24 hours)
  • Adequate Income Rule Met - customer and/or partner/ex-partner have met income confirmation requirements
  • Adequate Income Rule Not Met - customer and/or partner/ex-partner have not met income confirmation requirements

The Source column will display information on the income source customer and partner/ex-partner have used to complete income confirmation. This could be:

  • ATI (Actual Taxable Income) – Where the individual has lodged an Income Tax Return with the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) and the income details have been received by the agency.
  • Non Lodger Exemption, where:
    • Family Assistance non-lodgement exemption workflow has been completed and a Not Required to Lodge (NRTL) accepted for either the customer or current partner
    • an ex-partner NRTL has been accepted and recorded on both tables on the EXFNLE screen in Process Direct
  • Not Required to Lodge (ATO), where:
    • a NTRL has not been accepted and customer or current partner have completed a Return Not Needed (RNN) advice with the ATO
    • the customer has lodged a RNN with the ATO but not provided income
    • an ex-partner NRTL has not been accepted but the Non Lodger Exemption table has been recorded on the EXFNLE screen in Process Direct and ex-partner has then completed a Return Not Needed (RNN) with the ATO
    • In all cases income will still need to be recorded. Go to Step 10
  • Exception Recorded - Where the customer has a MRE exception applied due to inaction of a previous/ex-partner
  • Extension- this will be indicated above the Source heading. The extension will be indicated with the end date appearing, where:
    • the customer has been granted an extension, and
    • they have not met the income requirements conditions at either the first or second deadline, and
    • the relevant deadline has passed

Note: where a customer has advised that they have completed their income tax returns and it has been more than 28 days since the customer received their Notice Of Assessment from the ATO, see ATO information not received for family assistance reconciliation or lump sum claim for more information.

Has the customer and partner/ex-partner met the income confirmation requirements for reconciliation to occur?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 10

Reconciliation Outcome + Read more ...

Select the date hyperlink of the completed reconciliation instance, it should default to Reconciliation Outcome tab.

This will display a breakdown of any debts, arrears and withholdings (WHS) included in the reconciliation calculation.

  • Arrears (AUD):
    • Amount of CCS arrears for the year: This is the total amount of top-up payment calculated within the reconciliation activity
    • Arrears used to recover prior year debt in DMIS: This is the total amount of top-up payment that has been used to offset determined CCB/CCR debts that existed prior to reconciliation occurring
    • Arrears cleared for other reasons: This field is not expected to populate as it not expected this should occur. It is the amount of arrears cleared on other arrears
    • Amount of arrears used to recover CCS debts: This is the amount of the top-up that has been used to offset existing CCS debts determined prior to reconciliation
    • Arrears amount paid out to customer: This is the total-to-date of all reconciliation top-up payments made to the customer. Note: this field will contain amounts from any prior reconciliation instance
    • Current state of arrears and potential refund: This is the remaining amount or arrears due to be paid to the customer after all offsets have occurred
    • Amount of arrears offset against CCS debt: This is the arrears amount applied as an offset of the CCS reconciliation debt. This amount is not included in the calculation for total debt amount
  • Withholdings (AUD):
    • Amount of CCS WHS for the year: This is the total amount of withholdings (normally 5% of each fortnightly CCS payment) calculated based on the customer’s reconciled CCS entitlement. Note: where the customer had an initial reconciliation debt and re-reconciliation has resulted in an arrears payment, this field will include any withholdings paid to the debt
    • Amount of WHS used to recover prior year debt in DMIS: This is the total amount of withholdings that has been used to offset historical CCB/CCR debts
    • Amount of WHS cleared for other reasons: This field is not expected to populate as this type of clearing reason should not occur. It is the amount of withholdings cleared for an unexpected reason. Amount of WHS used to recover CCS debts: This is the total amount of withholdings used to offset determined CCS debts that existed prior to reconciliation
    • WHS amount paid out to customer: This is the total-to-date of all reconciliation top-up payments made to the customer. Note: this field will contain amounts from any prior reconciliation instance
    • Current state of WHS and potential refund amount: This is the remaining amount of Withholdings due to be paid to the customer after all offsets have occurred
    • Amount of WHS offset against CCS debt: Withholdings amount applied as offset of CCS reco debt. This amount is not included in the calculation for total debt amount
  • Debts (AUD):
    • Amount of CCS debt for the year: This is the total amount of CCS determined CCS/CCB/CCR debt raised prior to reconciliation occurring, a customer may have a current payment arrangement in place, however the debt may still be offset by reconciliation arrears if applicable
    • Debt total of the reconciliation debt for the financial year: This total includes determined and undetermined debts, therefore may show a figure even if arrears have resulted after balancing income. The arrears will then be used to offset this debt total
    • Reconciliation debt from existing undetermined debts: This is the total amount of undetermined CCS/CCB/CCR debts that have been included in the reconciliation calculation
    • Current outstanding debt amount: This is the total debt amount remaining for the customer to repay after all available offsets have been applied
    • Reconciliation debt recovered by FAO arrears: This is the amount of the customer’s FTB arrears that have been used to offset CCS reconciliation debt
    • Reconciliation debt recovered from Tax Offset: This is the amount of the customer’s tax refund that has been used to offset CCS reconciliation debt
    • Reconciliation debt waived: This is the amount of reconciliation debt that has been automatically waived, generally due to the debt being less than $50
    • Amount of debt written off: This is the amount of debt which has been written off. Note: this will update the Net amount of the debt on the reconciliation tile but will not impact the Subtotal amount on the reconciliation tile.
    • Repayments made against debt: This is the amount of repayments the customer has made against their debt. Note: will update the Net amount of the debt on the reconciliation tile but will not impact the Subtotal amount on the Reconciliation tile
    • Interest accrued against debt: This is the amount of interest accrued against the debt. Note: this will update the Net amount of the debt on the Reconciliation tile but will not impact the Subtotal amount on the Reconciliation tile

Note: due to a known error ACCS (Transition to Work) debts within the 2018/19 reconciliation period require further investigation. See the Resources page.

Tax Return Status + Read more ...

The Tax Return Status tab displays the current status of the income confirmation requirements for the customer and partner/ex-partner where applicable, including whether income confirmation requirements have been met. Where the customer has a partner/ex-partner it will display the length of the relationship (using CCS Monday as the commencement and CCS Sunday as the end date) under the partner/ex-partner’s name.

The Obligations Met column will display a status of either:

  • Provisional - this means either:
    • no information (i.e. ATI, RNN or not required to lodge details) has been received for the customer, partner or ex-partner; or
    • information has been received for one or more of the customer, partner or ex-partner but has not been processed. This may be because reconciliation has not commenced or because the system has not processed the information yet (this usually occurs within 24 hours)
  • Adequate Income Rule Met - customer and/or partner/ex-partner have met income confirmation requirements
  • Adequate Income Rule Not Met - customer and/or partner/ex-partner have not met income confirmation requirements

The Source column will display information on the income source customer and partner/ex-partner have used to complete income confirmation. This could be:

  • ATI (Actual Taxable Income) – Where the individual has lodged an Income Tax Return with the ATO and the income details have been received by the agency.
  • Non Lodger Exemption, where:
    • Family Assistance non-lodgement exemption workflow has been completed and a Not Required to Lodge (NRTL) accepted for either the customer or current partner.
    • an ex-partner NRTL has been accepted and recorded on both tables on the EXFNLE screen in Process Direct
  • Not Required to Lodge (ATO), where:
    • a NTRL has not been accepted and customer or current partner have completed a Return Not Needed (RNN) advice with the ATO
    • the customer has lodged a RNN with the ATO but not provided income
    • an ex-partner NRTL has not been accepted but the Non Lodger Exemption table has been recorded on the EXFNLE screen in Process Direct and ex-partner has then completed a Return Not Needed (RNN) with the ATO
  • Exception Recorded - Where the customer has a MRE exception applied due to inaction of a previous/ex-partner

Note: where a customer has advised that they have completed their income tax returns and it has been more than 28 days since the customer received their Notice Of Assessment from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO), see ATO information not received for family assistance reconciliation or lump sum claim for more information.

The annual cap no longer applies from the 2021-22 CCS year ongoing.

Note: families may still be subject to the annual cap for financial years prior to the 2021-22 financial year.

The ‘Annual Cap Applied’ row will display N/A from the 2021/22 year onwards.
