NEIS Pre-Training/Small Business Training

Table 1

Step

Description

1

Customers undertaking NEIS Pre-Training/Small Business Training + Read more ...

Customers wanting to start a small business or who already run a micro-business can access NEIS Pre-Training/Small Business Training. This accredited training course helps job seekers gain a nationally recognised qualification and is appropriate to starting and/or running a small business. Depending on the type of proposed new business, or existing micro business, the NEIS/Self-Employment provider will refer the job seeker to either a:

  • Certificate III in Entrepreneurship and New Business (BSB30220)
  • Certificate IV in Entrepreneurship and New Business (BSB40320)
  • Micro Business Skill Set (BSBSS00102)
  • New Business Ventures Skill Set (BSBSS00103)
  • Small Business Management Skill Set (BSBSS00104)

During the training, job seekers will develop a Business Plan, which will need to be approved by their NEIS/Self-Employment provider.

When a JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (job seeker) (YA), Parenting Payment Single (PPS) or Special Benefit (SpB) job seeker with mutual obligation requirements, or Disability Support Payment (DSP) job seeker with Compulsory Participation Requirements commences NEIS Pre-Training/Small Business Training, the activity will auto-display on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) page in Process Direct with the code 'NEI'.

To view an approved activity in Process Direct:

  • Key 'AEX' in the Super Key, select Enter
  • The Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) will display

Job seekers will be able to attend the NEIS Pre-Training/Small Business Training activity, prior to the commencement date and record their attendance as part of their points based activity (PBA). Job seekers undertaking NEIS Pre-Training/Small Business Training may be eligible for EdEP. See Education Entry Payment (EdEP) for information on eligibility and coding requirements.

Participation in NEIS Pre-Training/Small Business Training fully satisfies the customer's requirements and the customer will continue to receive their income support payment for the duration of their NEIS Pre-Training/Small Business Training.

2

Voluntary job seekers participating in NEIS Pre-Training/Small Business Training + Read more ...

When a customer in receipt of Age Pension, Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP), PPS (with a child aged under 6), or Carer Payment (CP), commences in NEIS Pre-Training/Small Business Training, the activity will auto-create a Manual Follow-up (MFU) action on the customer's record. Staff should select this activity and cancel it as no action is required.

These customers are not subject to requirements. Their participation in NEIS Pre-Training/Small Business Training will have no impact on their payment eligibility.

NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching and NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance for JSP, YA and SpB job seekers

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Job seekers under JSP conditions - NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching + Read more ...

For JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA) and Special Benefit (SpB) (under JSP conditions) job seekers participating in NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching, the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching activity will auto-display on both the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen and the Other Government Payments (OGP) screen with the code 'EIS', and will include the start date and the expected end date.

JSP, YA and SpB job seekers who are undertaking NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching and eligible for a partial rate of income support payment, will be deemed as fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements. As such, NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participants are managed by Services Australia and will be exited from their employment services provider's caseload, as they will not be required to attend any provider appointments. Service Officers must negotiate a new Job Plan with the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participant.

For information about continuing to pay JSP, YA or SpB as well as NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance, see Table 8.

2

JSP, YA and SpB job seekers in receipt of NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance + Read more ...

For job seekers in receipt of JSP, YA or SpB, commencement in the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching (and payment of NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance by Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE)) will result in the auto-cancellation of their income support payment when the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching activity notification is received from DESE.

If the job seeker was receiving a higher rate of JSP, YA or SpB, they may have an ongoing entitlement to a partial rate of income support payment in addition to NEIS/Self Employment Allowance. Manual restoration of their income support payment and manual coding of their partial rate of income support is required.

For information about continuing to pay JSP, YA or SpB as well as NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance, see Table 8.

JSP, YA and SpB job seekers who receive NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance from DESE, with no partial rate income support payment from Services Australia, may be eligible for a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC). These job seekers are required to lodge a claim for the LIC in order to receive the LIC.

Create a Display On Access DOC using reason code OTH, stating the job seeker is undertaking NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching and fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements.

3

JSP, YA and SpB job seekers - NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching Suspensions + Read more ...

When a JSP, YA or SpB job seeker is suspended from NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching, an advice will be sent from DESE and the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching suspension period will auto-display on the AEX screen with the code 'NES' showing the suspension date.

If a JSP, YA or SpB job seeker attends a service centre to claim an income support payment due to NEIS/Self Employment Allowance suspension, the Service Officer will need to contact the provider to confirm the job seeker's NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching suspension reason. If the suspension is due to a medical condition, and it is confirmed that NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance is suspended and all other eligibility rules are met, Service Officers can grant an income support payment for the period of the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching suspension.

If the suspension is due to other reasons, such as not complying with NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching requirements, Service Officers should reject the claim because the job seeker is receiving a precluding payment and refer the job seeker back to their NEIS/Self-Employment provider.

Service Officers should document any discussions with NEIS/Self-Employment providers and scan any documentation provided by the job seeker, which confirms suspension of participation in NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching.

For information about NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participants claiming income support payments after their NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance ceases, see Table 9.

NEIS/Small Business Coaching and NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance for Parenting Payment job seekers

Table 3

Item

Description

1

PPP job seekers in receipt of NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance + Read more ...

Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) job seekers must change from income support payment to NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance and are not entitled to any partial rate payment from Services Australia. NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance, being paid at the 'single, 22 or over, with no children' rate of JobSeeker Payment (JSP), will always be more than the job seeker's former rate of PPP. The NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance direct deduction amount applied will always reduce the PPP rate to nil.

Therefore, job seekers in receipt of PPP will be auto-cancelled upon commencement in NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching.

PPP job seekers whose income support payment has been cancelled due to transfer to NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance may be eligible for an extended concession card entitlement. These job seekers are required to lodge a claim for the LIC in order to receive the LIC.

2

PPS job seekers in receipt of NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance + Read more ...

Parenting Payment Single (PPS) job seekers can continue to receive their income support payment or elect to receive NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance and a partial rate of their income support payment.

When a PPS job seeker with mutual obligation requirements commences NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching, it will auto-display on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen and the Other Government Payments (OGP) screen with the code 'EIS', and will include the start date and the expected end date.

When a PPS job seeker with no mutual obligation requirements commences the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching, it will auto-display on the OGP screen with the code 'EIS' and will include the start date and the expected end date.

When a PPS job seeker with mutual obligation requirements elects to receive NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance, it will be recorded on the OGP screen. An activity, sent by the Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE), will auto code the OGP screen with the commencement date, amount and payment frequency of NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance. No coding is required by Service Officers.

The job seeker's income support payment will be reduced by the amount of NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance that they receive (direct deduction, dollar for dollar).

Alternatively, PPS job seekers can continue to receive their Services Australia ISP, rather than NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance from DESE. NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance will not be recorded on the OGP screen and no coding is required by Service Officers.

3

PPS job seeker - NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching Suspensions + Read more ...

When a PPS job seeker is suspended during NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching, an advice will be sent from the Department of Education, Skills and Employment and the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching suspension will auto-display on the AEX screen with the code 'EIS' showing the suspension date.

If a PPS job seeker attends a Centrelink Service Centre to claim an income support payment due to NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance suspension, the Service Officer will need to contact the NEIS/Self-Employment Assistance provider to confirm the job seeker's NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching suspension reason. If the suspension is due to a medical condition, and it is confirmed that NEIS /Self-Employment Allowance is suspended and all other eligibility rules are met, Service Officers can grant an income support payment for the period of the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participation suspension.

If the suspension is due to other reasons, such as not complying with NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching program requirements, Service Officers should reject the claim because the job seeker is receiving a precluding payment and refer the job seeker back to their NEIS/Self-Employment Assistance provider.

Service Officer should document any discussions with NEIS/Self-Employment Assistance providers and scan any documentation provided by the job seeker, which confirms suspension of participation in NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching.

For information about NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participants claiming income support payments after their NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance ceases, see Table 9.

Incidental payments and NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching

Table 7

Item

Description

1

Income and Assets - impact on NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance + Read more ...

All job seekers need to be informed that any income support payment (including partial rate) paid by Services Australia is subject to the income and assets test. Income from the business is subject to the income test and may impact partner income support payments. You and/or your partner may need to complete a MOD F when you commence the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching.

Cancellation of income support payments will only occur after the income test has been applied, where the amount of NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance and/or business income received precludes any rate of income support payment.

If the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching applicant has dependents receiving YA, ABSTUDY or AIC, record the parent's NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching details on the Other Government Payments (OGP) screen. If the details are not recorded on the parent's record, dependants will not receive the PIT exempt rate of payment.

Under the personal income test, all income earned in each fortnightly period must be declared. The relevant income free area and taper rates apply.

If the job seeker's income reduces their fortnightly rate to nil and some of that income is employment income, their payment may remain current and will enter an employment income nil rate period during which they may retain certain benefits. Working credits accrue up to a maximum of 1,000 credits for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) job seekers and YA job seekers can accrue up to a maximum of 3,500 credits.

2

NEIS/Self-Employment Rent Assistance (RA) and NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance + Read more ...

This is a fixed rate payment to eligible participants for a period up to a maximum of 26 weeks from the date they first start receiving NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance. It is paid to NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participants who are receiving RA from Services Australia under their current Allowance/Pension at the date they first start receiving basic NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance.

If a NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participant is receiving RA because they receive more than the base rate of Family Tax Benefit (FTB), RA will continue to be paid by Services Australia with their FTB for as long as the job seeker is entitled to RA. It is not limited to 26 weeks. The NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participant's partner may also be entitled to RA from Services Australia for the same period.

NEIS/Self-Employment Assistance RA eligibility is confirmed with the Department of Education, Skills and Employment form - Confirmation of Eligibility for NEIS/Self-Employment Rental Assistance (P1193 INDD). NEIS /Self-Employment Assistance Panel Members will issue this form to prospective participants who then bring it to Services Australia for completion. The NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participant then returns the form to the provider. NEIS/Self-Employment RA is non-taxable income.

3

NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching activities on Activity List (AL) screen + Read more ...

Department of Education, Skills and Employment NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching transactions can sometimes result in an overpayment. Started activities will appear on the AL screen with the keyword 'OGNEIS'. Service Officers must not delete this activity to finalise another activity, as this could lead to duplicate payments of income support and NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance. Service Officers must finalise the started activity before any action can be taken on a job seeker's record. Where required, Service Officers should contact their Subject Matter Expert or Participation Programme Team following local escalation procedures.

Restoring and coding a manual rate of ISP during NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participation and receipt of NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance

Table 8: This table describes the actions involved in restoring payment of JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA) or Special Benefit (SpB) where a NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participant has been auto-cancelled, but is still entitled to a partial rate of payment after the dollar for dollar reduction of the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance, by coding a manual rate. Note: manual coding should only be undertaken by a Service Support Officer.

Item

Description

1

Coding for JSP, YA (other) and SpB customers receiving a higher rate of payment than NEIS/Self-Employment allowance + Read more ...

Manual coding is only be undertaken by a Service Support Officer.

The 'NEI' (Customer is Self Employed under NEIS) cancellation reason ceased being used from 27 April 2020, but may display for some customers prior to this date.

JSP, YA and SpB job seekers have their payment auto-cancelled 'CPP' (Customer on Precluding Payment) upon commencement in NEIS Assistance/Small Business coaching, on the basis that they are now receiving NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance instead. However, some of these job seekers may be entitled to a higher rate of income support than that paid under NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance.

They are:

  • JSP and SpB job seekers who are single with a dependent child
  • JSP, SpB and YA single principal carers with long term (automatic) exemptions
  • JSP and SpB single job seekers aged 60 and over, who have been in receipt of an income support payment for more than 9 continuous months
  • JSP and SpB job seekers who are assessed as either 'Illness Separated', 'Respite Care Couple', or 'Partner in gaol'

These job seekers, if they have no affecting income, should be eligible to continue to receive a partial rate of their income support payment on top of their NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance after the dollar for dollar reduction has been applied.

The partial rate must be calculated manually, taking into account any income being received by the job seeker (including any business income associated with the new business being started under NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching). The partial rate is then set as a manual rate override. Any changes in income, which affect rate, must be manually calculated and set during this partial rate period until the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance ceases.

If these job seekers also receive employment income, they may be eligible for Working Credit.

Note: Service Officers will need to check any Centrepay deductions and, if necessary, discuss with the job seeker any action required for these deductions.

2

Contact the job seeker + Read more ...

If this action was instigated internally by Services Australia (e.g. by actioning a list of NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching commencements who may still be eligible for a partial rate), rather than by the job seeker contacting us, Service Officers must make contact with the job seeker to explain what has happened. Otherwise, they may receive conflicting automatic letters from the agency about the cancellation and then restoration of their payment, which may cause confusion.

The job seeker must also be asked to provide information about any ongoing income and assets, and other circumstances that will factor into a manual rate calculation.

If the contact by phone is not successful, a manual request to contact letter (Q164) should be issued to the job seeker. Regardless of the outcome (contact or no contact, restoration or no restoration), a DOC must be created on the record to explain action taken and decisions made on eligibility.

3

Check date of cancellation + Read more ...

To find cancellation date, check the date on the Allowance/Benefit History (ABH) screen/Pension Status History (PNSH) screen.

4

Cancel job seeker's payment + Read more ...

Cancel the job seeker's payment via the Benefit Activity (BA) screen using the code 'OTH' (other) from 1 day prior to the cancellation date above, then restore the job seeker's payment via the BA screen from the same date.

5

On OGP screen + Read more ...

End-date or zero the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance which displays on the OGP screen with the code 'EIS':

  • if the start date of the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance is prior to the cancellation date, end date the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance from the cancellation date
  • in some circumstances, system limitations will not permit the end dating of the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance. In this case, correct the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance income to zero, with a change reason of 'VER' (Verified)

6

Manual rate override coding on RCO screen + Read more ...

Manual coding is only be undertaken by a Service Support Officer with the appropriate system resource to code manual rate.

After manually calculating the rate of JSP, YA or SpB payable after application of the income test (including a dollar for dollar direct deduction equivalent to the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance rate), set a manual rate on the Rate Component Override (RCO) screen from the date of payment restoration.

To code a manual rate, see NSS system coding for manual rate of payment.

Does the manual rate coded on the RCO screen commence from the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance start date as recorded on the OGP screen?

  • Yes, finalise the activity via Assessment Results (AR)
  • No, on the RCO screen code the start date and end dates for the rate. The rate will be the difference between the rate at which NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance is paid, and the rate of payment the job seeker was in receipt of immediately prior to changing to NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance

Note: NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance will usually run for a maximum period of 39 weeks. Service Officers will need to confirm the start and end dates.

  • Press [Enter]
  • Go the AEX screen and code the activity type as 'NES', with the same dates as the dates on the RCO screen
  • Finalise the activity via AR

Participants are entitled to have their manual rate reassessed in line with the March/September CPI increases. In these cases, create Manual Review for the end of relevant month to reassess the manual rate.

Note: if a manual rate override is set, any earnings declared by the job seeker will not be auto-applied. Any changes to rate will need to be manually calculated and set on the RCO screen each time.

Processing claims and reassessments following cessation of NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance after 39 weeks

Table 9

Step

Action

1

Claims for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (other) following the cessation of the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance after 39 weeks + Read more ...

Where a NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participant receives NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance from their provider, it is paid for the first 39 of the 52 weeks of NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching only. After this time, they can re-test their eligibility for income support payment (if they were cancelled), or have their rate of payment reassessed with no direct deductions due to NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance.

These participants will receive a letter from their NEIS/Self-Employment Assistance provider with details of their final payment of NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance. The letter will tell them to contact Centrelink to determine eligibility to any ongoing payments, including reclaiming income support if they were cancelled. The letter advises the participants to show Centrelink this letter as evidence of the cessation of NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance. Check that the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participant has uploaded this letter to their record.

Is the job seeker claiming or receiving a payment with mutual obligation requirements or Compulsory Participation Requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participants with mutual obligation or Compulsory Participation Requirements + Read more ...

Approved Activity

NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participants who are still receiving a partial rate of JSP, YA, PPS, SpB or DSP will continue to have the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching (NES) display on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen until the end of their 52 weeks NEIS assistance.

These job seekers are still fully meeting their requirements by participating in NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching.

They remain Centrelink managed for the rest of the NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching period.

As part of their reclaim or reassessment, staff must complete a new Job Plan for the rest of this activity period, with the only compulsory requirement being participating in NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching.

Registration and Referral

These job seekers remain ineligible for provider participation until they complete NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching and are exited by their NEIS/Self-Employment Assistance provider. They cannot be referred to a Workforce Australia provider during this period and must be registered as a Job Seeker Registration Only (JSRO) job seeker.

Job seekers can participate in Disability Employment Services (DES) and NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching concurrently, so these job seekers should still be connected to their DES provider. No referral action is necessary.

3

NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance on Other Government Payment (OGP) screen + Read more ...

NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participants still receiving a partial rate of a Pensions System or Parenting System payment, i.e. AGE, PPS, PPP, DSP or CP will have their NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance automatically zeroed from the correct date. This is triggered by an electronic advice received from the Department of Education, Skills and Employment system.

NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participants claiming a Pension, Parenting or JobSeeker System payment will need to have their NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance manually zeroed by staff on the OGP screen. Otherwise, their claim will reject 'CPP' (Customer on Precluding Payment).

The letter from the NEIS/Self-Employment Assistance provider is the only evidence required for staff to manually zero off NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance from the date NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance ceased. Ensure that the letter has been provided by the job seeker and scanned to their record. If necessary, e.g. job seeker has lost their letter, contact the NEIS/Self-Employment Assistance provider to get confirmation of the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance cessation end date.

On the OGP screen, zero the NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance income (which displays on the OGP screen with the code 'EIS') with a date of effect of the last date paid NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance plus 1.

Manual Rate on RCO screen

If a JSP, YAL or SpB job seeker was receiving a partial rate of payment via a manual rate override during NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participation, staff must end the manual rate override from the date payment is being granted or reassessed.

Note: there are no special backdating provisions applying to job seekers claiming or requesting reassessments following NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance cessation.

NEIS Assistance/Small Business Coaching participants whose rate was reduced by NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance, will have this automatically zeroed on the OGP screen at 39 weeks. Service Officers will not have to manually zero NEIS/Self-Employment Allowance for these customers to restore payment back to full rate.

To code or end a manual rate, see NSS system coding for manual rate of payment.
