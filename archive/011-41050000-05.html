Common enquiries

Table 11: this table is to assist Service Officers respond to enquiries from health professionals.

Item

Common enquiry and response

1

Question: can I claim TCAs and GPMP items for the same date of service?

Answer: yes, the 2 items do not restrict with each other and can be claimed for the same day.

2

Question: can I claim 2 review items (e.g. 2x732) for the same date of service?

Answer: yes, you must include text with their claim indicating that one item 732 was reviewing TCAs and the other was to review the GPMP

3

Question: if I visit my patients who are care recipients in residential aged care facilities and bill chronic disease management services, is there anything else I can bill for having to travel from my consulting rooms?

Answer: there are 3 flag fall items. See the factsheets on MBS Online for the items that are available to your profession and for more information.

4

Question: I visit my patients who are care recipients in residential aged care facilities and bill chronic disease management services, can I bill a flag fall item for each patient I see?

Answer: no, the flag fall items are only valid with associated items and are only available for the initial attendance at one residential aged care facility on one occasion.

5

Question: if I see my patients in multiple residential aged care facilities and perform chronic disease management services, can I only bill a flag fall item once?

Answer: you can actually bill a flag fall item for the initial or first patient you provide services to, for each of the residential aged care facilities, per occasion.

Processing FAQs

Table 12

Item

Common processing FAQs

1

Question: do patients need to have a CDM planning item (e.g. 723) on their history for allied health services to be paid?

Answer: (for internal use only): No, they do not need to have the item on their history for their allied health services to be paid.

Note: the claim for allied health services needs to include details of a current referral from a medical practitioner

If Service Officers get an enquiry from a health professional, advise them that the patient must have TCAs in place before they are eligible for allied health services.

2

Question: do patients need to have an assessment for group services (e.g. item 81120) on their history for allied health group treatment services (e.g. item 93285) to be paid?

Answer: For internal use only: No, they do not need to have the assessment item on their history for their group treatment services to be paid.

Note: the claim for group treatment services needs to include details of a current referral from a medical practitioner.

If Service Officers get an enquiry from an allied health professional, advise them that the patient must have an assessment for group services before they are eligible for allied health group services.

3

Question: do patients need to have 8 group services (e.g. item 93613) on their history for additional exercise physiology group treatment service (item 93620) to be paid?

Answer: (for internal use only): No, they do not need to have the assessment item on their history for their group treatment services to be paid.

Note: the claim for group treatment services needs to include details of a current referral from a medical practitioner

If Service Officers get an enquiry from an allied health professional, advise them that the patient must have 8 group services (items 81105, 81115, 81125, 93285, 93613, 93614 or 93615) before they are eligible for additional exercise physiology group services item 93620.
