This page contains the procedure for performing a security check for a Healthcare Provider Identifier - Individual (HPI-I) role making enquiries in relation to the My Health Record system.

Security check for HPI-I

Perform a security check for HPI-I My Health Record

Table 1: This table describes how Service Officers perform a security check for a HPI-I in the My Health Record system.

Step

Action

1

Obtain individual Healthcare Provider Identifier number + Read more ...

Caller is a HPI-I, ask for their individual Healthcare Provider Identifier number or name (part name, minimum 2 alpha characters is acceptable).

Note: in Provider Participation Register (PPR), for individuals, the HPI-I number will display under the HI number heading in the Matched Records table.

2

Search for HPI-I in the PPR + Read more ...

Search for HPI-I in the PPR.

See also: Provider Participation Register (PPR)

3

Confirm HPI-I details + Read more ...

Refer to Individual Details section on the screen.

Ask HPI-I the following details to confirm details displayed on the screen:

  • name (full)
  • date of birth
  • address

Are the HPI-I’s details correctly confirmed?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, details are different, advise caller that the organisation’s RO/OMO will need to contact Tier 2 Healthcare Identifiers Service to confirm the HPI-I’s details. Go to Step 6

4

Confirm HPI-I has an authorised link to an organisation + Read more ...

Is the table List of Organisations the individual has Authorisation Links to displayed?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, advise caller their organisation’s RO/OMO will need to contact Tier 2 My Health Record and select option 2, to ask why their HPI-I’s request for authorised link has not been processed. Go to Step 6

5

Confirm the organisation details + Read more ...

Ask HPI-I for the following organisation details to confirm with the PPR details:

  • Healthcare Provider Identifier - Organisation (HPI-O) number
  • name

Are the HPI-I’s organisation details correctly confirmed?

  • Yes, security check is successful, proceed with the call then go to Step 7. Note: if also processing the procedure to check issues accessing the My Health Record provider portal escalate from Tier 2 to Tier 3/OTS found in content under Escalate provider issues relating to My Health Record, note the Organisation’s HI Status on a piece of paper to refer to in later steps for that procedure
  • No, details are different, advise caller that the supplied details do not match and their organisation’s RO/OMO will need to contact Tier 2 Healthcare Identifiers Service and confirm the organisation’s name and number details to resolve the discrepancy. Go to Step 6

6

Caller did not meet security check requirements + Read more ...

Advise the caller that their request cannot proceed as they did not meet the requirements of the security check. If the caller disagrees with the record details, warm transfer them to the SME (see Tier 1 and 2 provider support) for follow up with Tier 3. Go to Step 7.

7

Record details of the call + Read more ...

Record details of the call in Customer Relationship Management (CRM).

Security check for RO and OMO

Perform a security check for RO and OMO

Table 2: This table describes how Service Officers perform a security check for a Responsible Officer (RO) and an Organisation Maintenance Officer (OMO) in the My Health Record system.

Step

Action

1

Obtain individual Healthcare Provider Identifier number + Read more ...

Caller is a RO/OMO, ask for their individual Healthcare Provider Identifier (HI) number or name (part name, min. 2 alpha characters is acceptable)

Note: in Provider Participation Register (PPR), for individuals, RO and OMO number will display under the HI Number heading in the Matched Records table.

2

Search for RO/OMO in the PPR + Read more ...

Search for RO/OMO in the PPR

See also: Provider Participation Register (PPR)

3

Confirm RO/OMO individual details + Read more ...

Refer to Individual Details section on the screen.

Ask RO/OMO the following details to confirm with the details displayed on the screen:

  • name (full)
  • date of Birth
  • address

Are the RO/OMO details correctly confirmed?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, details are different, advise caller that the organisation’s RO/OMO will need to contact Tier 2 Healthcare Identifiers Service to confirm the RO/OMO details. Go to Step 4

4

Caller did not meet security check requirements + Read more ...

Advise the caller that you cannot proceed with their request as they did not meet the requirements of the security check. If the caller disagrees with our record details, warm transfer them to the SME (see Tier 1 and 2 provider support) for follow up with Tier 3. Go to Step 7.

5

Confirm RO/OMO organisation details, and RO/OMO is authorised officer + Read more ...

Refer to the displayed table List of Organisations the individual has direct links to.

Ask RO/OMO the following organisation details to confirm with the details displayed on the screen:

  • name
  • if their role is RO or OMO

Note: an authorised RO/OMO will have RO or OMO displayed under the field titled Role.

Did the caller correctly confirm the organisation name, and is the Role field populated with RO or OMO?

  • Yes, security check is successful, go to Step 6
  • No, advise caller (RO/OMO) that the supplied organisation details do not match those on the online PPR and they will be warm transferred to Tier 2 Healthcare Identifiers Service to clarify their organisation details. Go to Step 7

6

Record RO/OMO organisation’s registration status + Read more ...

Are you also processing the procedure to check issues accessing the My Health Record provider portal - escalate from Tier 2 to Tier 3/OTS:

  • Yes, select Registrations button. Result: Manage Registrations screen is displayed.
    • Select View Details button for corresponding organisation
    • Write the Organisation’s Registration Indicator, and HI Status on a piece of paper to refer to in later steps for that procedure. Go to Step 7
  • No, proceed with call, go to Step 7.

7

Record details of the call + Read more ...

Record details of the call in Customer Relationship Management (CRM).

Security check for nominated registration representative

Perform security check for nominated registration representative

Table 3: This table describes how Service Officers perform a security check for nominated registration representatives in the My Health Record system.

Step

Action

1

Obtain the organisation number or name + Read more ...

Caller is a nominated registration representative, ask for their organisation’s number or name (part name, min. 2 alpha characters is acceptable)

Note: for organisations in the Provider Participation Register (PPR), Healthcare Provider Identifier - Organisation (HPI-O) number will display under the Organisation Number heading in the Matched Records table.

2

Search for the representative's organisation in the PPR + Read more ...

See also: Provider Participation Register (PPR).

3

Confirm if the organisation is registered + Read more ...

Confirm if organisation is registered. Note: nominated registration representative cannot be given any information once the organisation is registered.

Information about the representative is recorded in Part A of the HW18.1308 (formerly 2978) – Application to register a seed organisation form.

Refer to the Matched Records table.

Does the Reg Indicator display as Registered for the organisation?

  • Yes, the organisation is listed as Registered. Advise the representative that information must only be given to the RO/OMO then go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Confirm nominated registration representative details + Read more ...

Ask the representative for the following details and confirm they are the same on the application form that is being processed:

  • HPI-O number
  • HPI-O name
  • the nominated registration representative’s:
    • first name
    • last name
    • position in organisation
    • day time phone number

Are the representative’s details correctly confirmed?

  • Yes, security check is successful, proceed with call. Go to Step 6
  • No, if incorrect HPI-O name, number or representative details are provided, advise the registration representative that the organisation’s RO/OMO can confirm organisation or representative details by calling Tier 2 Healthcare Identifiers Service. Go to Step 5

5

Caller did not meet security check requirements + Read more ...

Advise the caller that you cannot proceed with their request as they did not meet the requirements of the security check. If the caller disagrees with our record details, warm transfer them to the SME (see Tier 1 and 2 provider support) for follow up with Tier 3. Go to Step 6.

6

Record details of the call + Read more ...

Record details of the call in Customer Relationship Management (CRM).
