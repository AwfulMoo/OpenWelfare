Checking and assessing the AC022 form is complete

Table 1

This process is:

  • applicable to care recipients who were in care on 30 June 2014
  • actioned by Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) / Team Leaders from the Aged Care Payments Team (Aged Care and Allied Health Branch)

Step

Action

1

Check the AC022 form + Read more ...

Care recipients who choose to opt into the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme:

  • must have been provided with written details about the New Arrangements for Aged Care from 1 July 2014, and
  • must have ticked Yes for questions 5 and 6 of the Continuing Care Recipients opting into the New Aged Care Arrangements from 1 July 2014 form (AC022)

Check the responses for questions 5 and 6.

Have both questions 5 and 6 of the AC022 form been ticked Yes?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No:
    • as the care recipient has not received written information from their new aged care service about the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme, their consent to opt in to the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme cannot be accepted
    • go to Step 6

2

Check the AC022 form is complete + Read more ...

Note: the AC022 form is about the care recipient’s (or their nominee’s) consent to opt in to the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme. Do not accept an incomplete form

Check the following is complete on the AC022:

  • care recipient’s personal details
  • care recipient (or their nominee) has signed the form, and
  • is dated with a date before the date of entry into the new service

The form is incomplete if:

  • there are no details in the care recipient’s personal details section
  • it is illegible or unclear
  • any pages are missing
  • the Declaration section is missing the signature and/or the date

Is the form complete?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 6

3

The signing and dating of the form + Read more ...

The care recipient (or their nominee) can complete the AC022 form.

Different processes are in place depending on who signed and dated the form.

Did the care recipient or registered nominee sign and date the form?

  • Yes the care recipient signed the form, go to Step 5
  • No the registered nominee signed the form, go to Step 4

4

Checking the care recipient’s nominee details + Read more ...

If the care recipient’s nominee has completed the AC022 form, check the nominee details match the details recorded in the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS).

See Aged care request for a nominee for the process in locating a care recipient’s nominee details in ACMPS.

Do the nominee details in the Declaration section match the nominee details recorded in ACMPS?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Assessing the form + Read more ...

Assess the form for acceptance.

Accept the AC022 form when the care recipient satisfies the following eligibility criteria:

  • they were receiving an aged care service on 30 June 2014, and
  • they have moved to a new service on or after 1 July 2014, and
  • the date in the Declaration section must be earlier than the care recipient’s entry date into the new service, and
  • the new service type is the same as the previous service type (e.g. moving from home care into home care, or residential care into residential care), and
  • they have not spent more than 28 days outside of care, other than on approved leave

Note: care recipients can also opt in to the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme if:

  • they were receiving an aged care service on 30 June 2014, and
  • moved back to the same service as long as they have departed and entered into care in 28 days

All care recipients will be automatically assessed by the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme if they have:

  • moved from home care into residential care (or vice versa), and/or
  • spent more than 28 days outside of care (not on approved leave)

Has the care recipient satisfied all the criteria?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No,
    • the care recipient is not eligible to opt into the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme
    • the care recipient must be assessed under the pre 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme
    • go to Step 6

6

Returning the AC022 form when the form is incomplete and/or when the eligibility criteria has not been satisfied + Read more ...

To return the form, contact the sender and advise the reason(s) by:

  • phone,
  • email, or
  • mail

Add a care recipient note in the ACSP detailing the action taken. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table.

Add a comment in PaNDA detailing the action taken.

Procedure ends here.

7

Returning the AC022 form when the nominee details not matching + Read more ...

If the nominee details in the Declaration section do not match the nominee details recorded in ACMPS, the form cannot be accepted. This is because the nominee details in the AC0224 form does not match the nominee details in ACMPS and they do not have the authority to make the decision on behalf of the care recipient.

  • To return the form, contact the sender and advise the reason by: phone,
  • email, or
  • mail

Add a care recipient note in the ACSP detailing the action taken. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table.

Add a comment in PaNDA detailing the action taken.

Procedure ends here.

8

Process the AC022 form by care type + Read more ...

There are different processes to follow depending on the care recipient’s care type.

  • For home care recipients, see Table 2
  • For residential care recipients, see Table 3

Procedure ends here.

Processing the AC022 form for home care recipients

Table 2

This process is:

  • applicable to care recipients who were receiving a home care package on 30 June 2014
  • actioned by Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) and/or Team Leaders from the Aged Care Payments Team (Aged Care and Allied Health Branch)

Step

Action

1

Search for the Care Recipient profile + Read more ...

Log on to ACMPS to display the home screen

On the home screen, select

  • Care Recipient Search at the top of the screen, or
  • Manage Partner from the left side menu option
    • Select Manage Care Recipient option from the drop down menu

The Search: Care Recipient screen will display.

On the Search: Care Recipient screen:

  • complete fields to search for the care recipient
  • select Search

The search results will display.

Is there a care recipient record?

  • Yes,
    • select the relevant hyperlinked care recipient record from the search results
    • the Care Recipient Profile screen will display
    • go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

Note:

  • The search fields displayed are not mandatory
  • Search can be done using any single parameter or multiple parameters
  • Select search parameters from the drop down box
  • Copy or remove search rows by clicking the plus (+) or minus (-) icon
  • Select Clear to clear the values in the search fields. New search criteria is retained

2

No care recipient record located + Read more ...

A care recipient has an ACMPS profile when they have an ACMPS ID. The ACMPS ID is a 10 digit ID and starts as ‘04XX XXX XXX’.

If the care recipient’s ACMPS profile cannot be found:

  • contact the aged care service by:
    • phone,
    • email, or
    • mail
  • advise the care recipient cannot be found and return the form to the care recipient or the care recipient’s nominee

Procedure ends here.

3

Check entry details + Read more ...

Opt in details are recorded as part of the care recipient’s entry event.

Check the care recipient profile in ACMPS. Different processes apply depending on whether there is an existing entry event for the new service.

On the Care Recipient Profile screen:

  • select the Assessment & Events tab
  • the Assessment & Events tab will display
  • all entry events are under the Entry and Departure Details section

For care recipients where:

  • there is no entry event for the new service, go to Step 4
  • an Aged care entry record form (AC021) is submitted with the AC022 form, go to Step 5
  • there is an existing entry event for the new service, go to Step 6

4

Contacting the aged care service - no entry event located + Read more ...

Contact the aged care service and advise:

  • the AC022 form is received but there is no entry event for the relevant care recipient
  • request the aged care service to submit the entry event using the:
    • online (Aged Care Provider Portal (ACPP)), or
    • manual (the Aged Care Entry Record form (AC021))
  • the AC022 form cannot be processed until the aged care service has submitted the entry event

Add a care recipient note in the Aged Care Staff Portal detailing the action taken. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table.

Add a comment in PaNDA detailing the action taken.

When the entry event has been submitted, the Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) and/or Team Leaders will decide which process to undertake. Different processes apply depending on how the entry event was submitted.

  • For the online channel:
    • when the entry event is sent via the ACPP, the entry event will flow to ACMPS
    • update the entry event to reflect the care recipient’s decision to opting into the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme
    • go to Step 6
  • For the manual channel:
    • check the Aged Care Entry Record form (AC021) is complete. See Checking that an ACER form is complete table
    • create the entry event to reflect the care recipient’s decision to opting into the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme
    • go to Step 5

5

Create the entry event for the new service + Read more ...

Note: check the AC021 form is complete and valid before doing this step. See Checking that an ACER form is complete table

  • The care recipient's opt in details are recorded in the New Means Testing Arrangements section
  • If the Opt In By Service and the Opt In By Care Recipient fields show Auto opt in and are greyed out, ACMPS has automatically assessed the care recipient under the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme. Both fields are locked for editing.
  • Do not complete the New Means Testing Arrangements section of the entry event as it is not applicable
  • Add a care recipient note in the ACSP detailing the automatic assessment

Create the entry event in ACMPS.

  • From the Home screen in ACMPS:
    • select Event Create from the Create side menu
    • the New Event screen will display

Key the mandatory fields:

  • Service – key in service ID as per the form
  • Care Recipient – key in care recipient ID as per the form
  • Care type – defaults to Home Care. This field is locked for editing
  • Type of event – select Entry from the drop down list
  • Value – select care level from drop down list
  • Requested start – key the entry’s start date
  • Requested End – key the end date if applicable. Otherwise this field defaults to 31.12.9999

Complete the following optional fields if stated on the ACER form:

  • CRN Number
  • DVA Ref Number

Under the New Means Testing Arrangements section:

  • Select Yes from the drop down menu for the Opt In By Service field
  • Select Yes from the drop down menu for the Opt In By Care Recipient field
  • Select Save

The event details will save.

Add a care recipient note in ACSP detailing the action taken. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table.

Procedure ends here.

6

Review existing entry event for the new service + Read more ...

Note:

  • The New Means Testing Arrangements section is where the care recipient’s opt in details are located/recorded
  • If the Opt In By Service and the Opt In By Care Recipient fields both show Auto opt in and are greyed out, ACMPS has automatically assessed the care recipient under the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme. Both fields are locked for editing
  • Do not complete the New Means Testing Arrangements section of the entry event as it is not applicable
  • Create a care recipient note in the ACSP detailing the automatic assessment

 

Update the entry event in ACMPS.

On the home screen, select:

  • Care Recipient Search at the top of the screen or
  • Manage Partner from the left side menu option
    • Select Manage Care Recipient option from the drop down menu

The Search: Care Recipient screen will display.

  • Key Care Recipient ID or search by Last name, First name and DOB
  • Set the death indicator as not set for a current care recipient, or set for a deceased care recipient
  • Select Search
  • The Care Recipient Profile screen will display
  • Select the Assessments & Events tab
  • Select the relevant entry event hyperlink under the Entry and Departure Details tab
  • The Entry event screen will display

The care recipient’s opt in details will display under the New Means Testing Arrangements section. There are different process in place depending on the entry event’s status.

For event status Processed, update the opt in details:

  • select Edit
  • update the relevant fields in New Means Testing Arrangements
  • select Yes from the drop down menu for the Opt In By Service field
  • select Yes from the drop down menu for the Opt In By Care Recipient field
  • select Save to save the event details

For event status Event Claimed, update the opt in details:

  • select Retro claim option
  • update the relevant fields in New Means Testing Arrangements
  • select Yes from the drop down menu for the Opt In By Service field
  • select Yes from the drop down menu for the Opt In By Care Recipient field
  • select Save to save the event details

Add a care recipient note in the ACSP detailing the action taken. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table.

Procedure ends here.

5

Register permanent residential care entry event in the ACSP + Read more ... + Read more ...

Service Officers must be in the care recipient’s profile (Service in Context) screen to register permanent residential entry events.

  • The Care recipient profile (service) screen will display
  • Select the Circumstances tab
  • Select Entry
  • The Entry summary screen will display
  • Select Register entry
    • See the Registering events in the Aged Care Staff Portal (Service in Context) table

Add a care recipient note in the ACSP detailing the action taken:

  • See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table

Procedure ends here.

6

Review an existing held entry event using Event search screen in the ACSP + Read more ...

Different processes apply depending on whether there is a held online entry event or not. These online entry events are held as they contain the care recipient’s decision to opting into the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme.

Check the Event search results screen to confirm if there is a held Permanent entry even for the care recipient.

  • Select the Main menu icon
  • Select Event search under the Aged Care Staff Portal section
  • The Event search displays
  • Complete the fields in the Event search screen to search for the held permanent entry event
    • See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage residential care held events table

Is there a held entry event for the relevant care recipient?

  • Yes:
    • process the held entry event
    • when the held entry event is processed, the entry event’s status changes from Held to Accepted and will reflect the care recipient’s decision to opting into the post 1 July 2014 means assessment scheme
    • procedure ends here
  • No:
    • contact the aged care service about the care recipient’s entry event
    • go to Step 4
