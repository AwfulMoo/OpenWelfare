For staff in the Tasmanian Transport Programs (TTP) Team only

This page contains detailed information of the steps required for a request of extension of time (EOT) for the lodgement of a claim or other timeframes for Tasmanian Freight Equalisation Scheme (TFES) assistance.

Extension of time (EOT) to lodge TFES claim

Claimant process for extending time to lodge a claim

This table demonstrates the process for a claimant to request an extension of time.

Step

Action

1

TFES claim timeframes + Read more ...

Claims must be lodged within six months after the date of shipment as per clause 4.12, of the Ministerial Directions.

An extension of time (EOT) may be requested if the time between the shipment date and the date the TFES claim has been/will be lodged exceeds six months.

Note: further information on the ‘Six Months Claims Period’ can be found on the Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development website under ‘Information Papers’. See the Resources page for a link to Information Papers.

Decision received from Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development

If the claimant sends the EOT directly to the Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development, and a decision has been received from Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development, go to Step 6.

Other timeframes

Claimant or claims agent have 21 days to provide any further information that has been requested by Tasmanian Transport Programs (TTP) Team.

Claims agent must notify claimant of the amount of assistance received within 30 days of the payment being made by the Commonwealth.

No adjustment to the amount of assistance paid may be made if:

  • the claimant or claims agent advises that the intended purpose of goods previously claimed has changed, and
  • the notification is received more than six months after the date of shipment

A ‘round trip’ for sportspersons and entertainers must be completed within six months.

2

Extension of time requests to be in writing + Read more ...

All requests for an EOT must be received in writing.

EOT request is being lodged for:

  • rejected claim, go to Step 3
  • claim not lodged yet, go to Step 4
  • not for a claim, go to Step 4

3

Claim rejected as not received within six months + Read more ...

Claimant has submitted a claim and TTP have rejected it as the claim was not received within six months from date of shipment.

If error was made due to:

  • recording or assessing the claim is identified, this is a review of decision
  • calculating the six month timeframe, this is a review of decision

For assistance calculating the shipment cut-off date, refer to the '6 Month Limit Calculator' found in the TTP Resource Guide.

Go to Step 5.

4

Request for EOT prior to claim lodgement or not for a claim + Read more ...

If the claimant is aware of the time limit they can request an EOT in writing at any time.

If the extension is subsequently refused, there is no need for the claimant to submit a claim or advise of repurposing.

EOT requests may be made by a claimant or claims agent.

All requests must be received in writing and include:

  • details of their request
  • reason as to why they cannot meet the timeframe
  • relevant shipment date, date range, claim ID or circumstance
  • if additional information is required, contact claimant by phone or email and request they provide this

5

Process to lodge an EOT request + Read more ...

Check the claimant record on myHSI to see if the same request has already been sent to Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development.

If the request has not been sent, complete the following actions:

  • record EOT request on the Workflow Tracking spreadsheet found in the TTP Resource Guide using the type 'Review'
  • record EOT request on the Register Database found in the TTP Resource Guide using Activity Type as 'Extension Request'
  • check claimant record on myHSI for last activity date of claim/s, if relevant
  • retrieve rejected claim/s from batch filing, if relevant
  • create electronic claimant file in secure drive located in the TTP Resource Guide if needed
  • check shipments were outside six month timeframe by using the '6 Month Limit Calculator' in the TTP Resource Guide
  • contact claimant for more information if required
  • scan all relevant pages and save in claimant specific folder. Delete scanned documents from scan folder
  • save any relevant electronic documents sent by the claimant to claimant specific folder
  • complete the Review and Policy Information Request template from the TTP Resource Guide and save to claimant folder
  • create a note on the claimant's record in myHSI. Text can be copied from Review and Policy Information Request
  • forward email submission to the Service Support Officer (SSO) or Team Leader with relevant attachments
  • the SSO or Team Leader reviews the email submission and forwards to Programme Team for Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development's consideration
  • When sending email:
    • if the total size of attachments exceeds 15mb, send as a compressed zip folder
    • when documents were received via email, attach as outlook item (not file)
    • send email as 'For Official Use Only'
  • place paperwork in tray

Procedure ends here until a decision is received from the Programme Team by the Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development.

6

Decision received from Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development + Read more ...

Once the decision is received in the TTP Management mailbox, SSO will:

  • review, forward the response and if needed allocate the work to a team member
  • record the response by adding a Note on the claimant file in myHSI with a summary of the decision made. Note: the Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development will also send a copy of the decision directly to the claimant
  • update the Workflow Tracking spreadsheet and the Register Database
  • initiate any required actions and create adjustment activity as required
