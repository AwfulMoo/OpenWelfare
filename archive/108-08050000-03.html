Customer previously paid EdEP + Read more ...

Tell the customer they are not entitled to another EdEP until:

  • 12 months has passed since their most recent EdEP was paid for JSP, PPP , PA or WA customers, or
  • the next calendar year for DSP, PPS, CP or SpB customers

Record a DOC detailing why the customer is not eligible for EdEP and explain the outcome to the customer.

3

Qualifications for EdEP + Read more ...

Customers are eligible for EdEP if:

  • they were on JSP immediately before commencing their course and
  • have been in receipt of any income support payment continuously for at least 12 months

When they claim a student payment they do not need to provide proof of enrolment (POE).

Has the customer been in receipt of an income support payment for 12 months, and:

  • are an Austudy or YA student and were previously on JSP immediately before commencing their course, or
  • a current JSP customer claiming ABSTUDY, Austudy or YA as a student?

  • Yes, see Step 5 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Approved study + Read more ...

To qualify for EdEP, the customer must be actively undertaking either:

  • an approved course of study for Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy and Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), or
  • approved courses of study for ABSTUDY, ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC)

The customer does not need to be getting ABSTUDY, Austudy or YA as a student to qualify for EdEP.

Is the customer undertaking, or intending to undertake study in an approved course?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, they do not qualify for EdEP
    • record a DOC explaining why the customer is not eligible, and
    • tell the customer the outcome
    • procedure ends here

5

Does the customer meet all other qualifications for EdEP? + Read more ...

  • Customers currently receiving JSP, PPP or PA qualify for EdEP if, just before starting an approved course of study:
    • they were getting their current payment, and
    • they have been on an income support payment (which may not be their current payment) continuously for 12 months or longer. See the Resources page for more details and examples of 'one EdEP per 12 months or calendar year'
  • DSP, PPS, CP and SpB customers must qualify for PES paid under the Social Security Act. ABSTUDY PES is not a qualification for EdEP
  • PA customers must undertake a full time course
  • WA customers can undertake either a full-time or part-time course

JSP customers additional information

  • JSP customers start study in a full-time approved course included in their Job Plan as an approved activity. EdEP may be payable on or after the start date of the approved course as specified in the current Job Plan, if all other EdEP requirements are met
  • Principal carers getting JSP may be considered full-time even though they may be studying part-time
  • JSP with a partial capacity to work may be considered full-time even though they may be studying part-time
  • Customers getting PES transferring to JSP who meet all other requirements remain eligible for EdEP
  • EdEP is not payable to activity tested customers who have a participation exemption in place

PPP customers additional information

  • PPP customers starting a course and getting PES who then transfer to JSP, remain eligible for PES while continuing the same course.
  • PPP customers transferring to JSP and continuing the same approved course remain eligible for EdEP if they are getting JSP before the start of their next semester. This rule applies until they complete the same approved course. If they start another course while on their new payment, they can only request payment of EdEP 12 months after their last EdEP was paid
  • PPP customers can undertake either a full-time or part-time course
  • PPS customers transferring to PPP and continuing the same approved course remain eligible for EdEP if they are getting PPP before the start of their next semester and all other qualifying requirements are met. This rule applies until they complete the same approved course. If they start another course while on their new payment, they can only request payment of EdEP 12 months after their last EdEP

Note: EdEP cannot be claimed once the student has completed the course.

Does the customer qualify for EdEP?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, record a DOC detailing why the customer is not eligible and explain the outcome to the customer. If the system is incorrectly paying EdEP to the customer, see Step 4 in Table 2, otherwise, procedure ends here

6

Is the customer on JSP, PPP, PA or WA? + Read more ...

These customers qualify for EdEP if they have:

  • been getting an income support payment for the past 12 months, and were getting JSP, PPP, PA or WA just before starting an approved course, and
  • not received EdEP in the last 12 months

Has the customer been in continuous receipt of an income support payment for the past 12 months and not received EdEP in the last 12 months?

  • Yes, for JSP, PPP, PA or WA customers go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Is the customer on DSP, PPS, JSP (as a single principal carer) SpB or CP and qualified for PES? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, the customer is not eligible for EdEP. Record a DOC detailing why the customer is not eligible and explain the outcome to the customer. Procedure ends here

8

Is the customer on DSP, PPS, JSP (as a single principal carer) SpB or CP and PES and eligible for EdEP? + Read more ...

JSP single principal carers are not automatically eligible for EdEP when PES is granted. These customers must also be:

  • considered full-time (15hrs per week) for mutual obligation purposes, and
  • have their approved course of study included as an approved activity in their Job Plan

If a student contacts and states they are getting PES but have not received EdEP, check if customer has received EdEP in the past 12 months.

Is the customer automatically eligible for EdEP?

  • Yes, if EdEP has:
    • been paid, advise customer when they were paid EdEP. Procedure ends here
    • not been paid, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

9

Customer on DSP, PPS, JSP (as a single principle carer) SpB or CP and PES and eligible for EdEP but not paid + Read more ...

  • Check if PES has been granted and payments have started. The benefit status line will reflect EPF/CUR. Check the customer's record in the Small Benefits System (ISS), under Service Reason EPF, using the PES task selector (ESTS) screen. EdEP will not be delivered until after the first PES payment is made. Where the customer is eligible for payment, and the system had not auto paid the EdEP, go to Step 13
  • If PES has not been claimed, tell the customer to:
    • complete an online claim for PES
    • lodge verbally via Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) or
    • issue a paper Claim for Pensioner Education Supplement (SY004)
    • return the form via Upload documents online, reply paid envelope or their service centre

Record a DOC detailing advice given to customer.

Procedure ends here.

10

Has the customer already completed their course? + Read more ...

  • Yes, EdEP cannot be paid once the student has completed the course. Record a DOC detailing why the customer is not eligible and explain the outcome to the customer. Procedure ends here
  • No, for:
    • customers receiving PA and WA, go to Step 11
    • customers receiving JSP, go to Step 12
    • all other customers, go to Step 13

11

PA and WA customers entitled to EdEP + Read more ...

PA customers

Go to the Miscellaneous Circumstances Details (NMIS) screen and code:

  • Current Circumstances: field, key:
    • FTE if the customer is undertaking full-time study, the EdEP will automatically issue if the customer is qualified
    • PTE if the customer is undertaking part-time study, in this case the student is not eligible for EdEP
  • the date of effect:
    • the date the customer started studying, or
    • today’s date if study has not yet started or the customer is in their 2nd or subsequent year of study
    • procedure ends here

WA customers

Go to the Miscellaneous Circumstances Details (NMIS) screen and code:

  • Current Circumstances: field, key:
    • FTE if the customer is undertaking full-time study, or
    • PTE if the customer is undertaking part time study. The EdEP will automatically issue if the customer is qualified
  • the date of effect:
    • the date the customer started studying, or
    • today’s date if study has not yet started or the customer is in their 2nd or subsequent year of study
    • procedure ends here

12

JSP customers entitled to EdEP + Read more ...

Is the JSP customer undertaking study under the short course guidelines?

  • Yes:
    • provider managed and volunteer job seeker customers, see Step 1 in Table 2
    • Centrelink managed customers, see Step 3 in Table 2
  • No, for JSP customers undertaking study not under the short course guidelines, go to the Miscellaneous Circumstances Details (NMIS) screen and code:
    • Current Circumstances: field, key:
      FTE if the customer is undertaking full-time study
      PTE if the customer is undertaking part-time study
    • the date of effect will be the student start date

If FTE is coded for JSP customers whose course is not approved as a short course, JSP will cancel, reason STU, and EdEP will automatically issue if qualified. The customer no longer qualifies for JSP because of participation in full-time study; they may wish to test their eligibility for a student payment. Customers should use the Payment and Service Finder if they need more help. See Resources for a link.

If PTE is coded, the customer is not eligible for EdEP. See JobSeeker Payment (JSP) customers returning to (or continuing in) part-time study.

Procedure ends here.

13

Customer is entitled to EdEP + Read more ...

If the customer is entitled to EdEP, manually code the OOP screen:

  • type EDP in the Payment type: field
  • the date of effect must be either:
    • the date the customer started studying, or
    • today’s date if study has not yet started or the customer is in their 2nd or subsequent year of study

After the activity is completed, the system will display the correct date on the OOP screen.

Record a DOC and tell the customer the outcome.

Procedure ends here.

Step

Description

1

Provider managed customer is entitled to EdEP

If a job seeker with mutual obligation requirements contacts to request payment of EdEP: + Read more ...

The start date of the full-time approved course must be included in their current Job Plan. Payment must not be issued until on or after the start date of the course.

Go to the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen.

Is the short course approved activity code of SHD (Short Course) displayed?

  • Yes, if the customer’s Job Plan has been updated with the course details the customer is entitled to EdEP, manually code the OOP screen:
    • type EDP in the Payment type: field
    • the date of effect must be either:
      the date the customer started studying, or
      today’s date if study has not yet started or the customer is in their 2nd or subsequent year of study
    • after the activity is completed the system will display the correct date on the OOP screen
    • record a DOC and advise customer of the outcome
    • procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

If a volunteer job seeker contacts to request payment of EdEP: + Read more ...

There will be no activity code SHD on the AEX screen. The customer may have the course as a voluntary activity in their Job Plan. If they do not have written POE, advise the customer they must supply POE for assessment of eligibility to EdEP.

Once POE is provided and the customer is assessed as eligible for EdEP manually code the OOP screen:

  • type EDP in the Payment type: field
  • the date of effect must be either:
    • the date the customer started studying, or
    • today's date if study has not yet started or the customer is in their 2nd or subsequent year of study
  • after the activity is completed the system will display the correct date on the OOP screen
  • record a DOC and add the keyword EDEPPAID
  • advise customer of the outcome
  • procedure ends here

2

Does the customer’s Job Plan include the course details? + Read more ...

  • Yes, manually code the OOP screen before the activity code SHD transaction transfers from the provider:
    • type EDP in the Payment type: field
    • the date of effect must be either:
      the date the customer started studying, or
      today’s date if study has not yet started or the customer is in their 2nd or subsequent year of study
    • after the activity is completed the system will display the correct date on the OOP screen
    • record a DOC and add the keyword EDEPPAID
    • advise customer of the outcome
    • procedure ends here
  • No,
    • tell the job seeker to contact their employment services provider to have their course approved and included in their Job Plan before payment can be made
    • once this is done the job seeker must request EdEP to be paid. No additional proof of enrolment (POE) is needed. Service Officers can check these details in the customers Job Plan
    • procedure ends here

3

Centrelink managed job seeker is entitled to EdEP + Read more ...

Centrelink managed job seeker with mutual obligation requirements contacts to request payment of EdEP.

Has the customer already started the course:

  • Yes, check if Centrelink is permitted to approve a short course for the customer as per Part-time or full-time study as an approved activity for JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
    • If so, check it is an approved course under the short course guidelines. If the course is approved, code the details on the Short Course Details screen. The Short Course Details screen can be accessed through the 'Participation Summary' link in Customer First
    • If eligible, payment of EdEP will be automatically assessed when coding is completed
    • DOC the customer record using the keyword EDEPPAID and include the course details
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, check if Services Australia can approve a short course for the job seeker as per Part-time or full-time study as an approved activity for JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
    • If so, and the job seeker has provided POE, check it is an approved course under the short course guidelines. If the course is approved, update the job seeker's Job Plan and check all other eligibility for EdEP
    • If the agency is permitted to approve the course but the job seeker does not have POE, tell them to return to their provider with POE before payment can be made
    • JSP customers must have the start date and end date of their full time course included as an activity in their current Job Plan
    • Payment must not be issued to JSP customers until on or after the start date of the course
    • Create a future manual review (using the keyword SHDSTA for the course start date), to complete the Short Course Details screen, once the course starts. If eligible, payment of EdEP will be automatically assessed when coding is completed
    • DOC the customer's record using the keyword EDEPPAID and include the course details
    • Procedure ends here

4

Incorrect payment of EdEP + Read more ...

If activity code SHD is coded for a student and the course is not full-time, they may not be entitled to EdEP.

Note: if the system wants to pay EdEP for these students, navigate to the OOP screen while in the activity and record a NIL amount to stop the payment.

Procedure ends here.

5

Is the customer claiming a student payment? + Read more ...

  • Yes, manually code the NMIS screen using the code 'FTE' as part of the transfer activity from JSP to the student payment
  • No:
    • and they are in receipt of a student payment, go to Step 6
    • For all other reasons, tell the customer they are not entitled to EdEP, as they do not meet eligibility requirements. Procedure ends here

6

Is the customer in receipt of student payment and has a participation exemption? + Read more ...

  • Yes,
    • EdEP is not payable to activity tested customers who have a participation exemption in place
    • record a DOC detailing why the customer is not eligible and explain the outcome to the customer
  • No, manually code the OOP screen:
    • type EDP in the Payment type: field
      if the customer is in their first year of study, code the course start date in the Date of Event: field
      if the customer has already completed their first year of study, code the 1st of January of the current year in the Date of Event: field
    • record a DOC, and
    • advise customer of the outcome
