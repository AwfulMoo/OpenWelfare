Registering a customer as a job seeker

Table 1: This table describes the steps and options for registering a customer as a job seeker.

Step

Action

1

Job seekers may contact a Smart Centre Call or a service centre wishing to register for employment services + Read more ...

The following options are available to the customer:

  • for customer's wishing to test their eligibility for income support, go to Step 2
  • aged 15-21 years old and not wishing to test eligibility or not eligible for income support, go to Step 3
  • job seekers 22 or over and not wishing to test eligibility for income support, (including job seekers who have received redundancy payments, have working partners or who are on Leave Without Pay (LWOP) from their regular employment), go to Step 4
  • to register for full employment services only, that is, Fully Eligible Participant (FEP)/ Remote Fully Eligible (RFE). Go to Step 7
  • job seeker who was made redundant from the Tasmanian Forestry Industry, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • job seeker who was made redundant from BlueScope Steel, see Step 4 in Table 2
  • job seeker who was made redundant from an eligible company in the automotive manufacturing industry, see Step 4 in Table 2

2

Customer wishes to test eligibility for income support + Read more ...

Customers claiming JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (Job Seeker) and Parenting Payment are encouraged to complete an online claim. Procedure ends here.

If the customer is not capable of completing an online claim run the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) to test eligibility for income support. Eligible job seekers will be registered with an appropriate provider during the claim process.

Procedure ends here.

3

Job seeker aged 15-21 who does not wish to test eligibility, or is not eligible, for income support + Read more ...

If the job seeker is:

  • not working more than 15 hours per week, and
  • not in full-time education or training, and
  • not eligible for income support due to parental income and assets being too high

they are eligible to be registered as Fully Eligible Participant (FEP). Go to Step 7.

If the customer meets only 1 or 2 of the conditions above, they are eligible for registration as a volunteer. Go to Step 4.

4

Registration for a volunteer + Read more ...

Has the job seeker registered previously?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, and customer is in a remote area, go to Step 6
  • No and customer is not in a remote area,
    • register job seeker and refer to Online Employment Services using the Looking for Work workflow
    • check job seeker has acceptable identity confirmation documents to assist with registration. If registration is done over the phone, advise job seeker to take acceptable identity documentation to their appointment with jobactive
    • advise job seeker of their Job Seeker ID over the phone

Procedure ends here.

5

Customer previously registered as job seeker + Read more ...

Job seekers located in a Department of Education, Skills and Employment designated remote area are registered with a Community Development Programme (CDP) provider and are not streamed. If the record has been inactive for:

  • less than 12 months, advise the job seeker they must re-activate their registration via the self service terminals available at Services Australia, their provider or on the internet at www.jobsearch.gov.au
  • more than 12 months
    • run Looking for Work workflow
    • on the Job Seeker Registration screen, answer 'Yes' to the question 'Does the Job Seeker require Volunteer'
    • refer to a suitable provider and make appointment
    • continue workflow by following the screen flows until finalised

Advise job seeker of their Job Seeker ID number.

Procedure ends here.

6

Customer in remote area registering for volunteer + Read more ...

If the job seeker resides in an identified remote area:

  • issue a Claim for Employment Services Assistance (SU489)
  • ask the job seeker to complete and sign the declaration at the end of the form
  • advise the job seeker that the form contains a privacy statement explaining the reason why their personal information is collected and how it is used
  • advise the job seeker to return the form with at least one form of acceptable identity documentation
  • and if located in a Department of Education, Skills and Employment designated CDP remote area, advise they are not eligible as a volunteer and are not streamed

Procedure ends here.

7

FEP/RFE registration + Read more ...

Use the Participation Interview or standalone Looking for Work workflow to register job seekers.

Procedure ends here.

Registering a redundant customer as a job seeker

Table 2: This table describes the steps and options for registering a customer as a job seeker where they have been made redundant.

Step

Action

1

Job seeker made redundant from the Tasmanian Forestry Industry + Read more ...

To qualify for immediate access to Stream B services, job seekers need to have been employed by an Eligible Tasmania Forest Industry Company. The main employer is Gunns, however, there may be other contractors employed in the industry.

Eligible workers have 6 months from the exit date on their redundancy letter to register for assistance and gain access to this program. Workers do not need to have left the company before receiving assistance under this initiative, but need to have been notified of their redundancy date.

Job seekers who had access to the program and find employment but then lose employment can also regain access to the assistance under this initiative for up to 6 months after their original redundancy date. In this event:

  • request the job seeker to provide a letter of redundancy
  • check if the company the job seeker was working for is an Eligible Tasmanian Forest Industry Company
  • when running the Participation Interview or the Looking for Work (LFW) workflow staff must not select the Tasmanian Forest Industry Redundant Workers checkbox, unless there is documentation that verifies the job seeker has been made redundant, such as a letter of redundancy, from an Eligible Tasmanian Forest Industry Company
  • if the employer was Gunns, and they have provided a letter of redundancy to the Service Centre the job seeker will be eligible for the immediate access to employment services
  • if the employer is not Gunns but a contractor employed in the Tasmanian Forestry Industry, contact the Forestry Industry redundancy contact to confirm if the employer is included in this measure
  • if the job seeker does not have evidence do not select the Tasmanian Forest Industry Redundant Workers checkbox on the Job Seeker Registration (JSR) screen. Job seekers should be advised to discuss their eligibility to the Forestry Industry in Tasmanian Structural Adjustment Programme with their jobactive organisation
  • where the Tasmanian Forest Industry Redundant Workers checkbox has been pre-populated from being selected in a previous job seeker registration, staff should determine whether it is still applicable to the job seeker and uncheck it where necessary to ensure customers receive the correct level of servicing

Has the job seeker supplied a letter of redundancy and the employer is an Eligible Tasmanian Forest Industry Company?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Job seeker has supplied a letter of redundancy from a Tasmanian Forest Industry Company + Read more ...

Run the Participation Interview or, where a claim for income support has not been completed, the Looking for Work (LFW) workflow and register the redundant worker as an FEP job seeker.

On the Job Seeker Registration screen select the Tasmanian Forest Industry Redundant Workers checkbox.

Continue the workflow by following the screen flow until finalised.

Procedure ends here.

3

Job seeker has not supplied a letter of redundancy from a Tasmanian Forest Industry Company + Read more ...

Run the Participation Interview or, where a claim for income support has not been completed, the Looking for Work (LFW) workflow and register the redundant worker as a FEP job seeker.

On the Job Seeker Registration (JSR) screen do not select the Tasmanian Forest Industry Redundant Workers checkbox.

Continue the workflow by following the screen flows until finalised.

Job seekers should be advised to discuss their eligibility to the Forestry Industry in Tasmanian Structural Adjustment Programme with their jobactive organisation.

4

Job seeker who was made redundant from the automotive manufacturing industry + Read more ...

To qualify for immediate access to Stream B Services, workers need to have been employed by an eligible company in the automotive manufacturing industry.

Job seekers must have a letter of redundancy from their employer to be deemed eligible for immediate access to Stream B services. This letter should be provided to their jobactive organisation.

Job seekers wanting further information on assistance available should be directed to either the Department of Education, Skills and Employment website or phone 136 268.
