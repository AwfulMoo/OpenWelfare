Recording a Power of Attorney (POA) on the customer's record

Table 1

Step

Action

1

POA provided + Read more ...

When a POA legal document is provided to the agency, consider any potential risks that may impact on the attorney's ability to:

  • act in the customer's best interests, and
  • handle the customer's affairs responsibly

For Centrelink purposes, while POA can be used to conduct business on the customer’s behalf within the limitations of the legal document, it is preferable for a nominee, PPE or PPU arrangement to be set up and the legal document to be used as supporting evidence.

2

Privacy statement + Read more ...

The privacy statement regarding collection of attorney's personal information must be read to the attorney. See Privacy Statement on the Resources page.

If it cannot be read to them when the legal document was provided, it is to be read to them when they next make contact. When this occurs the DOA DOC is to be annotated to state this has now been done.

3

Proof of identity + Read more ...

  • A photo identification document must be provided except if the POA:
    • has a confirmed identity status on record or
    • is a business
  • Check the Identity Summary on the POA's record:
    • If photo identification is sighted, record details in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard in Process Direct and verification for DVS

Note: if an identity document has been uploaded online, consent does not need to be captured again, as this has been obtained when uploaded.

If a POA does not own an approved photographic identity document, contact the Identity Policy Helpdesk.

Note: photo identification is not required if the POA is a business or an authorised employee.

Make 1 genuine attempt to contact the POA via phone if known.

If POA cannot be contacted, obtain their photo identification when they next make contact. When this occurs, annotate the DOA DOC to state this has now been done.

4

Customer does not have the capacity to make their own decisions + Read more ...

If the customer does not have capacity to make their own decisions the POA must be enduring and medical evidence of inability provided by the attorney.

5

Record details of POA + Read more ...

Record POA on a Display On Access (DOA) DOC on the customer's record.

Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Enquiry > Power of Attorney.

If a POA legal document has been provided, authorising a person or organisation to make decisions on the customer's behalf, code the details immediately.

If the legal document names more than one attorney, state the name, date of birth, phone number, address and relationship to customer for each attorney named in the legal document in the same POA DOA DOC.

Accepting or disclosing information to a Power of Attorney (POA)

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Check what authority is being used + Read more ...

POA documents are often used as supporting evidence to appoint a nominee, PPE or PPU. This procedure is for POA only arrangements.

Check the Nominee (NOLS) screen and Document List (DL) screens for a Display on Access (DOA) DOC for the Power of Attorney. For example, POA DOA DOC, PPE DOA DOC or PPU DOA DOC including level of authority and time frame.

If the attorney is a:

  • Nominee, see Disclosing information to a nominee
  • PPE or PPU, see Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) or Update (PPU)
  • POA only, go to Step 2

2

Confirm proof of identity has been provided + Read more ...

  • A photo identification document must be provided except if the POA has a confirmed identity status on record or is a business
  • Check the Identity Summary on the POA's record:
    • If photo identification is sighted, record details in the Identity Confirmation Dashboard in Process Direct and verification for DVS

If a POA does not own an approved photographic identity document, contact the Identity Policy Helpdesk.

Once provided, record the identification in Process Direct and annotate the POA DOA DOC to state this has now been done.

Note: photo identification is not required if the POA is a business or an authorised employee.

Has photo identification been provided?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No,
    • If POA is a business/authorised employee, photo identification is not required, go to Step 3
    • Otherwise, tell the POA they must provide approved photo identification and a copy of the legal document to their local service centre before any information can be accepted or provided. Procedure ends here

3

Authenticate the POA + Read more ...

See Authenticating a Centrelink customer.

Go to Step 4.

4

Privacy statement + Read more ...

The privacy statement regarding collection of attorney's personal information must be read to the attorney.

Check the POA DOA DOC to see if it was read to the attorney when the arrangement was set up.

Has the privacy statement been read to the attorney?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, read the Privacy Statement to them and annotate the POA DOA DOC. See Privacy Statement on the Resources page

5

Check the attorney has the authority + Read more ...

Every POA is different depending on the customer and the state/territory they reside in. Check the POA DOA DOC and/or legal document for any limitations that would prevent them from accepting or disclosing information or performing an action like signing.

For Centrelink purposes - POA is accepted for financial and/or day to day decision making.

Contact Level 2 Policy Help Desk if there are any concerns about the validity of the POA, that is:

  • incorrect names and/or addresses on the form, or
  • the limitations of the authority

Is there a limitation to this type of action?

  • Yes,
    • Multiple attorneys, go to Step 6
    • The attorney does not have the appropriate authority. Tell the attorney if they want to perform that action they will need the customer to complete and lodge an Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf (SS313) form. DOC the contact. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Multiple attorneys + Read more ...

If there is more than one POA check the POA DOA DOC and/or legal document to see how decisions need to be made.

For joint or majority decision making it is the responsibility of the attorney making contact with the agency to provide written evidence the other attorneys agree.

Has written evidence been provided?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No,
    • If it is not required because the attorney can act severally, go to Step 7
    • The attorney does not have the appropriate authority. Tell the attorney they require written agreement from the other attorneys or they will need the customer to complete and lodge an Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf (SS313) form. DOC the contact. Procedure ends here

7

Action the Attorneys request + Read more ...

If the terms of the POA allow it, a person holding POA can:

  • sign and lodge agency forms (including claim forms) on behalf of the customer
  • tell the agency of changes to the customer's circumstances
  • request information held by the agency on the customer
  • change the customer's payment destination. However, the new account must still be in the customer's name or their name must appear on a joint account. If the person holding a POA wants the customer's payments to go to an account in their name then they need to become a payment nominee
  • in urgent cases, request that the outcome of the customer's aged care means test assessment be faxed through to the aged care service, providing certain criteria are met

DOC the customer record stating the authority used.
