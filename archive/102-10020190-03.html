Job Seeker requests cancellation of their JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) + Read more ...

  • If the customer’s request is to withdraw a claim currently under assessment, or assessed and payment has been granted but not received, see Step 3 in the Rejecting a claim for JSP or YA (job seeker) table

2

Becoming partnered + Read more ...

If the job seeker has requested cancellation of their payment due to becoming partnered, see Change in relationship status from single to partnered.

  • Advise the job seeker to lodge their final Reporting Statement so that they can receive any payment they are entitled to, up to the day before they became partnered
  • Any earnings, other income or changes before the date of cancellation must be reported
  • If the job seeker is on variable reporting (check NSS General Information (NGI) screen), change to standard reporting cycle so that customer may lodge their final Reporting Statement as soon as possible
  • Record a DOC stating the job seeker will provide details requesting the cancellation of their payment on next Reporting Statement

Procedure ends here.

3

Commenced work + Read more ...

Full-time Australian Apprentices may be eligible for ABSTUDY, Austudy or YA. If the customer advises they have become a full-time Australian Apprentice investigate entitlement to one of these payments.

If a job seeker asks for their payment to be cancelled as they have started full-time work, follow procedures outlined in Commencing or returning to work for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and Youth Allowance (job seeker), as:

  • working credits may enable them to keep some of their income support payment while they are working even if it is permanent full-time work
  • if they have commenced casual work (even if it is currently full-time), they can continue to:
    • receive JSP/YA until their working credits are depleted, and
    • lodge their Reporting Statement via self service (online), in person or in writing (SU19) to take advantage of the employment income nil rate period

Procedure ends here.

4

Request to cancel payment prior to EPED + Read more ...

  • If the customer's request to cancel payment is received before the last day of their entitlement period (EPED), cancel the payment from the day the customer request cancellation of their payment. For coding, see Step 3 in Table 2
  • Process/stimulate any outstanding report
  • The customer's entitlement to a Health Care Card (HCC) will change once their payment is cancelled Client Request (CLR)

For more details, see Rejecting a claim for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker).

Procedure ends here.

5

Request to cancel payment to claim Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment (PLDP) + Read more ...

Customers can voluntarily surrender their ISP to claim PLDP.

Make sure the customer understands, by cancelling their ISP, they will not be entitled to any supplementary or add on payments, which may include:

  • Rent Assistance
  • Energy Supplement,
  • Pharmaceutical Allowance,
  • Telephone Allowance
  • Utilities Allowance (available to people who receive youth rates of Disability Support Pension (under 21, no children) on the test days)
  • Approved Program of Work Supplement
  • Language, Literacy and Numeracy Supplement

Tell the customer they:

  • may not be able to use their Concession Card when receiving PLDP
  • may have to serve a waiting period if they reclaim an ISP
  • must provide an income estimate to ensure ongoing Family Tax Benefit (FTB) entitlement
  • may have to update their Child Care Subsidy information, including their activity test details

If the customer decides not to cancel their JSP or YA (job seeker) payment, as a person cannot receive PLDP and an income support payment and they are experiencing financial hardship, a customer may qualify for a Crisis Payment for National Health Emergency (COVID-19).

If the customer decides not to cancel their JSP or YA (job seeker) payment, as a person cannot receive PLDP and an income support payment, procedure ends here.

To qualify for PLDP customers need to prove:

  • they are unable to attend work, and are therefore unable to earn an income
  • they were likely to have worked during the period of self-isolation, quarantine or caring
  • they have insufficient appropriate leave entitlements, including any pandemic sick leave, or will use up this leave during the 7 day period

For eligibility criteria, see Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment

Check the Earning and Reporting Results (EARR) screen. Has the customer been declaring income?

  • Yes, see Step 3 in Table 2
  • No,
    • tell the customer that as they don’t appear to have been reporting employment income they will be unlikely to qualify for PLDP
    • record details on a DOC
    • if customer wishes to proceed with cancellation, go to step 6

6

Receipt of cancellation request + Read more ...

  • If the cancellation request was received via the customer’s Reporting Statement lodged via self service (online or phone), in person or in writing (SU19), go to Step 7
  • If the cancellation request was received in person, by phone or letter, and not via a Reporting Statement go to Step 8
  • If the cancellation request was lodged via a customer’s online account and a work item has been created to manually action the request, go to Step 9

7

Cancellation requested via Reporting Statement + Read more ...

  • If the Reporting Statement is received on or after the entitlement period end date (EPED) process the form as per usual, then cancel the payment with the date of effect (DOE) as the day following the EPED (EPED + 1)
  • If the Reporting Statement is received prior to the EPED cancel the payment on the date of receipt (DOR), then process the report
  • The customers entitlement to a Health Care Card (HCC) will change once their payment is cancelled Client Request (CLR)
  • For coding, see Step 3 in Table 2

8

Cancellation requested in person, by phone or letter (not via Reporting Statement) + Read more ...

  • Tell the job seeker to report on their next EPED so that they can receive any payment they are entitled to (for example, to access working credits, payment to remain current during the employment income nil rate period for up to 6 fortnights if they still qualify, or to be paid up to the day before they lose eligibility)
  • If the job seeker is a statement reporter and they want to cancel their payment immediately, tell them to report employment income immediately after cancellation, online or via Centrelink Express mobile app, to receive any outstanding partial payment. See Table 2, Step 3
  • If payment is manually cancelled and the job seeker is on variable reporting (check NSS General Information (NGI) screen) they will not be entitled to any payment after the 'authorised to date' (that is, the EPED + 1 of the last Reporting Statement they lodged). If they have received any payment after that date, Services Australia must recover it. Therefore it is important that the job seeker lodges a final Reporting Statement to avoid a debt
  • The customer’s entitlement to a Health Care Card (HCC) will change once their payments are cancelled

Does the job seeker intend to lodge a final Reporting Statement via self-managed service channels (Online account, Express Plus application or phone), in person or in writing (SU19)?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 11

9

Manual cancellation following unsuccessful cancellation via online account + Read more ...

Job seekers can cancel their own payment online and have this update automatically applied to their record. When the system has been unable to successfully finalise the cancellation:

  • a Receipt ID will be generated for the customer, and
  • a work item will be created for the cancellation in Work Load Manager (WLM)

For coding, see Step 3 in Table 2.

10

Final reporting lodgement + Read more ...

If a job seeker advises they intend to lodge a final Reporting Statement via self-managed service channels (Online account, Express Plus application or phone), in person or in writing (SU19):

  • Record a DOC to state job seeker will provide details requesting the cancellation of their payment on next Reporting Statement
  • If the job seeker is on variable reporting, change to standard reporting cycle so that the job seeker may lodge a final Reporting Statement as soon as possible, see Reporting requirements for job seekers
  • For advice on entitlements after the job seeker's payment has been cancelled, see Step 4 in Table 2

11

Not intending to lodge final reporting + Read more ...

When the job seeker does not intend to lodge a final Reporting Statement via self-managed service channels (Online account, Express Plus application or phone), in person or in writing (SU19):

Establish whether the job seeker is on variable reporting (if not already done) by checking the NGI screen

Is the job seeker on a variable reporting cycle?

  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

3

Coding voluntary cancellation of JSP + Read more ...

The date of effect can differ depending on the date of event and where the event is reported before the end of the entitlement period in which it occurred.

  • Access the Benefit Action (BA) screen - code 'BA' in Nxt: and press [Enter]
    • In the Svc Rsn field code 'JSP' or 'YAL' as applicable
    • In the Action field code 'CAN'
    • In the Reason field code 'CLR' (withdrawn/voluntary surrender). The job seeker will not be able to access any working credits or employment income nil rate period

Date of effect is:

  • EPED + 1:
    • variable reporter, this will be the EPED + 1 of the last reporting statement they lodged
    • statement reporter and reporting statement received on or after EPED
  • DOR:
    • statement reporter and reporting statement received before EPED
      - in order to receive any outstanding partial payment, tell the customer to report their employment income immediately after cancellation, either online or using the Centrelink Express mobile App
  • DPT + 1:
    • statement reporter not intending to lodge any outstanding reports

For more details, see Rejecting a claim for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) (jobseeker).

When coding a voluntary cancellation of JSP:

  • select CANC CLR - requested by CUS for (PAYMENT TYPE) Fast Note
  • record all relevant details of the cancellation in the template. Clearly document the conversation with the customer and the reason they wish to cancel their payment, following the GIR DOC minimum standards
  • proceed to attach the Fast Note to the CAN activity
  • 'S'elect the CAN activity and complete the Assessment Results (AR) process

Notes:

  • a payment will not be generated until the Reporting Statement is lodged via self-managed service channels (Online account, Express Plus application or phone), in person or in writing (SU19)
  • if a customer is cancelled CLR they will not be eligible for the Youth Bonus Wage Subsidy 26 week suspension period

4

Other considerations + Read more ...

Claiming and CAN CLR

Customers must reclaim if they claimed and were paid another Income Support Payment since cancelling their JSP or YA (job seeker) payment.

Customers claiming JSP or Youth Allowance (job seeker) online will see a reduced question set where payments have cancelled within the last 52 weeks.

If the payment is CAN/CLR and then the customer claimed Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment:

Was the customer paid PLDP?

  • Yes, the customer must lodge a new claim if they need an Income Support Payment
  • No, payment can be restored, see Step 3 in the Restoring payments for JSP and YA customers table

If the customer states they did not request cancellation of their payment in order to claim PLDP and advises there has been fraudulent activity on their record see Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

Appeals and CAN CLR

If a JSP or Youth Allowance (job seeker) payment is REJ or CAN/CLR, the customer has the same appeal and review rights as other customers who have had their payments cancelled regardless of the reason for the cancellation.

For more details about the different types of cancellation reasons, see Cancellation, suspension and rejection reason codes for Newstart System (NSS).

Staff must also consider the following:

  • If the customer has a partner on income support, reassess the partner's entitlement and advise of the effect of the customer's income on their partner's continuing entitlement
  • If the job seeker has a nominee arrangement in place (voluntary or involuntary) contact with the nominee maybe required in some instances before taking action requested by the customer. For more details see Nominees
  • Advise customer of possible entitlement to a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC)
  • If applicable, request and update the estimate of income for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)/Child Care Subsidy (CCS). Estimates may be given over the phone
  • Check if the cancellation reason needs a manual letter. If needed, issue a general cancellation letter Q134 to tell the customer:
    • the reason(s) for cancellation
    • the section(s) of the act used to make the decision, and
    • their review and appeal rights, see Creating a Q134 letter

Procedure ends here.
