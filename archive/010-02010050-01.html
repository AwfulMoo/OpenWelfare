This document outlines interim claim and interim applicant provisions for ABSTUDY.

On this Page:

When to use an interim claim

An interim claim can be used when a student, or the person who would normally apply on behalf of the student cannot due to exceptional circumstances such as serious illness, floods or remote locality. Services Australia must believe, by accepting an interim claim, it is following the wishes of the student or parent/guardian in respect of the student's schooling. The 'interim applicant' is the person who is completing the interim claim.

The interim applicant must:

  • have temporary care of the student
  • be able to make an informed declaration about the student's Aboriginality, and
  • be prepared to accept ABSTUDY conditions as outlined in the ABSTUDY Policy Guidelines

An interim applicant can be a person, an institution or a boarding school.

Institutions may be considered applicants for the purposes of:

  • Away from Base submissions, and
  • interim claims for boarding school students

Interim claims can be accepted from boarding schools only if all the following circumstances apply:

  • the student or person who would normally apply on behalf of the student is cannot submit a claim because of exceptional circumstances, such as serious illness or remote locality
  • the claim is lodged in the student's first term at the school
  • the boarding school accepts responsibility for obtaining a full claim from the applicant
  • the institution accepts responsibility for the student's boarding and tuition fees where a complete claim is not subsequently lodged by the applicant
  • the agency has no reason to believe, by accepting an interim claim it is not following the wishes of the applicant in respect of the student's schooling

Where the above circumstances are met, only ABSTUDY non-income tested allowances and Fares Allowance (FAA) for Terms 1 and 2 can be approved. Where a full claim from the student's parent/guardian has not been received by the end of Term 2, no further FAA is to be approved.

The ABSTUDY interim claim provisions do not apply to Australian Apprentices.

Claim form

There is no specific claim form for interim claims. The interim applicant completes the paper claim form relevant to the payment being claimed. If the student is likely to be eligible for:

  • Schooling A, the Claim for ABSTUDY Schooling A (SY018)
  • Schooling B, the Claim for ABSTUDY (SY019) or the paper Claim for ABSTUDY Remote Areas form (SY092)

Completing an interim claim

Points to note in completing the claim:

  • The minimum information acceptable for an interim claim is both the:
    • student details
    • parent/guardian details
  • The claim must be completed in blue or black pen
  • All relevant questions on the claim form should be answered. Many questions only require a 'Yes' or 'No' tick
  • The claim must be signed by the interim applicant

Note: Service Officers assisting interim applicants to complete a claim must not underline or add comments on any page of the claim. Service Officers must use a separate sheet of paper (attached to the claim), to capture any notations or explanations to questions. No underline or comments of any sort are to be added to a claim that is to be signed by a customer and lodged with Centrelink.

Lodging the interim claim

ABSTUDY paper claims and supporting documents can be lodged using any of the following options:

  • Online - using the Upload documents service
  • Fax - see Processing Service details for: ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY PES
  • Post - via a reply paid envelope or by posting to Services Australia. See Processing Service details for: ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY PES
  • In person - at a service centre, or in remote areas, with a Centrelink field officer

Claim assessment

Where an interim claim is accepted, the delegate may, based on available information, approve only ABSTUDY non-income tested allowances. A full application must be received from the original applicant as soon as possible. If there are no applications made by the original carer by the end of first term then payment will cease.
