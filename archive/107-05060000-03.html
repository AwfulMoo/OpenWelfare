Determining whether to initiate civil debt recovery

Step

Action

1

Review the debt details + Read more ...

Is the debtor's debt total more than $10,000?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, civil recovery action ends here. Debts under $10,000 are not suitable for civil recovery. See Debt recovery for Centrelink

2

Check if it is appropriate and cost effective to pursue civil recovery + Read more ...

Check:

  • if the debtor's home or work address are known (for service of documents)
  • if the agency has confirmed details of the debtor's assets
  • if the debtor's asset/s have enough equity
  • the cost of civil recovery against the value of the debt. Consult the Litigation Team (LT) if costs are unknown

The following searches will help to find asset value and determine the debtor's equity:

  • Title searches (Equifax and RP data)
  • Company and Business searches (Equifax and ABN Lookup)
  • Notices to banks (Q604, Q608)
  • Notices to employers (Q609)
  • Notices to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) (SS320)

If the debtor is hard to find, the below searches may help:

  • On Line Search (OLS)
  • Austrac
  • Immigration
  • IQ Tool

See the Resources page for a link to the Austrac Access and Disclosure document.

To decide if civil recovery should proceed check if:

  • the debt(s) can be proven through supporting document
  • the debtor has lodged an application for a formal review
  • there are any sensitivities or risks with the case

Should civil recovery proceed?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, follow standard recovery procedures. See Debt recovery for Centrelink. Procedure ends here

3

Update the debtor's record + Read more ...

Go to the Add Civil Action (OPCVA) screen.

Update:

  • Action Type - type of civil action being pursued
  • Referral Date - date case referred for civil action
  • Debt Id - Debt ID(s) civil action being pursued. If no debt ID(s) are entered, civil action will apply to all eligible debts
  • Expected Result Date - date on which the result or outcome is expected. Note: cannot be more than 12 months into the future
  • Referral Name - Name of SRT Case Manager
  • Comments - Free text details of any details and/or action taken to date

Note: SRT will case manage civil action cases. This will include managing the application of interest in the preparation of court documents.

Select [Enter].

Adding details of civil recovery will change the status of the debt to 'Determined - Civil Action'.

Record all details on a Display On Access (DOA) document on the debtor's record advising:

  • civil recovery is pending, and
  • no recovery to be actioned

Contacting the debtor about recovery of debt

Step

Action

1

Contact the debtor to discuss debt/s and the debtor's asset value and potential equity + Read more ...

Make reasonable effort to contact the debtor. Was contact successful?

  • Yes,
    • Explain the agency's expectations of recovery of the debt(s) in full because of their identified capacity to repay, or
    • Negotiate an arrangement based on their financial circumstances
    • Go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Has the debtor agreed to an acceptable recovery arrangement? + Read more ...

  • Yes, code recovery arrangement. See Managing recovery and non recovery of Centrelink debts. SRT will case manage recovery to make sure the arrangement is not broken. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

Note: in limited circumstances, an acceptable arrangement may include a Deed of Agreement when the debtor has equity, but is inappropriate to liquidate assets. For example, an older debtor who may not be able to secure a loan against their asset. The Deed of Agreement will secure payment for the agency. LT can approve a submission for a Deed of Agreement if endorsed by the Team Leader.

The Resources page has more details about a Deed of Agreement.

3

Warn the customer of the pending civil recovery action + Read more ...

Send the debtor a pre-litigation warning letter (Q315) before starting civil recovery action.

Record details on Display On Access (DOA) document.

Allow 14 days for the debtor to respond to the Q315.

Note: the Case Manager may contact the debtor within the 14 days

  • to make sure the debtor got the letter, and
  • to talk about repayment options

4

Has the debtor agreed to a suitable payment arrangement in response to the Q315? + Read more ...

  • Yes, code recovery arrangement. See Managing recovery and non recovery of Centrelink debts. SRT will case manage recovery to make sure the arrangement is not broken. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Debt Litigation Submission + Read more ...

The Case Manager will prepare a Debt Litigation Submission and ask LT to send a Letter of Demand (LOD). The LOD gives the debtor 14 days to contact and enter into a suitable repayment arrangement.

The Team Leader will:

  • endorse the Debt Litigation Submission
  • provide feedback to the Case Manager if submission is not approved

Has a LOD been issued by LT?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, follow standard recovery procedures. See Managing recovery and non recovery of Centrelink debts. Procedure ends here

6

Payment arrangement due to the Letter of Demand (LOD) + Read more ...

The Case Manager may contact the debtor within 14 days to make sure they got the LOD and to talk about repayment options.

Has the debtor made a satisfactory payment arrangement due to the Letter of Demand (LOD)?

  • Yes, see Managing recovery and non recovery of Centrelink debts. SRT will case manage recovery to make sure the arrangement is not broken. Procedure ends here
  • No, the Case Manager will prepare a civil action pre-approval to brief for endorsement by the Team Leader. When endorsed, the Team Leader will forward the pre-approval to brief to LT. Go to Step 7

7

Is the Litigation Team (LT) proceeding with civil recovery? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, civil recovery action will stop. LT will provide feedback why civil recovery will not proceed. SRT will also provide feedback to origin of referral if received from an external source. Follow standard recovery procedures. See Managing recovery and non recovery of Centrelink debts. Procedure ends here

8

Case Manager completes Brief for referral to LT + Read more ...

To support the case through the courts, the Case Manager may need to:

  • provide further details
  • liaise with debtor
  • provide instructions to LT
  • give evidence in court
  • report any negotiations with the debtor back to LT

The SRT Case Manager makes decisions with advice from LT and the Legal Service Provider (LSP) engaged in the matter.

During the process, LT will contact SRT to advise civil recovery progress, including prospects of success and future costs. LT may seek further instructions based on new circumstances or information. For example, the debtor lodges a defence to the proceedings.

Once a case has been referred for civil recovery, staff should advise LT immediately if the debtor:

  • contacts Centrelink (including change of address)
  • claims a payment
  • makes a payment

9

Management of the case + Read more ...

Once referred to a Legal Service Provider (LSP), LT and SRT will oversee the management of the case.

This may include:

  • obtaining a judgment against the debtor (summary, default or defended)
  • negotiating a settlement arrangement
  • selling a debtor's asset
  • petitioning for bankruptcy

SRT and LT are responsible for keeping accurate records.
