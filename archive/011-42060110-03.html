Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) ART items

Table 1: this table contains information about Medicare ART items.

Item

Description

13200

ART superovulated treatment cycle proceeding to oocyte retrieval + Read more ...

Item 13200

  • This item is an INITIAL superovulated treatment cycle in a single calendar year
  • This item is payable only once in a calendar year, however, if the initial cycle fails prior to oocyte retrieval, then item 13202 (cancelled superovulated cycle) becomes the initial cycle
  • Items 13209, 13212, 13215 and 13251 may be claimed in the same cycle
  • A restriction between item 13200 and the other global items 13201, 13202, 13203 and 13218 applies for 26 days. Only one item may be billed for the treatment cycle
  • Claims can be overridden with processing indicator PI 45 when the account indicates a ‘short cycle’ and commencement of a new cycle begins

The date of service in respect of treatment covered by item 13200 is deemed to be the first day of the treatment cycle.

13201

ART superovulated treatment cycle proceeding to oocyte retrieval + Read more ...

Item 13201

  • This item is for SUBSEQUENT superovulated treatment cycle in a calendar year
  • This item is payable only if either item 13200 (initial superovulated treatment cycle) or 13202 (cancelled superovulated cycle) has been claimed and paid prior to this item in the same calendar year
  • Item 13209, 13212, 13215 and 13251 may be claimed in the same cycle
  • A restriction between item 13201 and the other global items (including itself) 13200, 13202, 13203 and 13218 applies for 26 days. Only one item may be billed for the treatment cycle.
  • Claims can be overridden with PI 45 when the account indicates a ‘short cycle’ and commencement of a new cycle begins

The date of service in respect of treatment covered by item 13201 is deemed to be the first day of the treatment cycle.

13202

ART superovulated treatment cycle that is cancelled before oocyte retrieval + Read more ...

Item 13202

  • This item is for a superovulated treatment cancelled before oocyte retrieval (egg retrieval)
  • This item can either be an INITIAL or SUBSEQUENT superovulated treatment cycle in a calendar year
  • Item 13200 cannot be paid as a subsequent service to item 13202
  • As item 13202 is for a cancelled cycle that is cancelled on the day of treatment, it should not restrict with other ART services
  • Where item 13202 is restrictive with other global services, the restriction does not apply to services provided after the date of service of the 13202. Therefore a short cycle indication is not required on any accounts for subsequent
  • Item 13209 and 13251 may be claimed in the same cycle
  • A restriction between item 13202 and the other global items (including itself) 13200, 13201, 13203 and 13218 applies for 26 days if they are claimed prior to the date of service of the 13202. Only one item may be billed for the treatment cycle. These claims can be overridden with PI 45 when the account indicates a ‘short cycle’ and commencement of a new cycle begins

The date of service in respect of treatment covered by item 13202 is deemed to be the first day of the treatment cycle.

13203

Ovulation monitoring services, for artificial insemination + Read more ...

Item 13203

  • This item can be claimed regardless of whether the cycle results in artificial insemination, providing the ovulation monitoring services are undertaken for the purposes of artificial insemination
  • Items 13209 and 13221 may be claimed and paid in the same treatment cycle
  • A restriction between item 13203 and the other global items (including itself) 13200, 13201, 13202, 13212, 13215 and 13218 applies for 26 days. Only one item may be billed for the treatment cycle
  • Claims can be overridden with PI 45 when the account indicates a ‘short cycle’ and commencement of a new cycle begins

13207

Embryo biopsy for Pre-implantation Genetic Testing (PGT) + Read more ...

  • Embryo biopsy for providing a sample for PGT, where the patient fulfils the criteria of item 73384
  • Payable per embryo produced in an ART treatment cycle

13209

Planning and Management Services + Read more ...

  • It is expected that 13209 to be billed on the same date of service as the global item
  • Planning and management services only attract a benefit when the patient has been referred and the service is rendered by a specialist
  • This service can also be paid as a stand-alone item where no associated global item has been paid
  • Planning and management services are payable only once in a treatment cycle

13210*

Telehealth Consultation + Read more ...

  • This item is available for an attendance by a specialist via video-conference
  • The attendance covers the services available under item 13209 – Planning and Management
  • Both items are to be listed on the account and Medicare benefits are payable for item 13209 and an additional 50% (for item 13210)
  • Item 13210 only applies to services provided under item 13209

*Item ended 31 December 2021

13212

Oocyte Retrieval + Read more ...

  • Benefits for item 13212 (egg collection) can only be claimed when rendered in association with items 13200 or 13201, whether on history or within the claim
  • Item 13212 is usually billed 10-14 days after the global item and may be claimed the same day as item 13215
  • Where item 13212 is claim more than 30 days after the global item, the account must be annotated with ‘long cycle’ or words to that effect in order to attract a benefit
  • Item 13212 may be claimed in association with an anaesthetic (item 20943)

13215

Transfer of embryos or both ova and sperm + Read more ...

  • Benefits for item 13215 can only be claimed when rendered in association with items 13200, 13201 or 13218, whether on history or within the claim
  • Item 13215 is usually billed 10-20 days after the global item and may be claimed on the same day as item 13212
  • Where item 13215 is claim more than 30 days after the global item, the account must be annotated with ‘long cycle’ or words to that effect in order to attract a benefit
  • Item 13212 may be claimed in association with an anaesthetic (item 20943)

13218

Preparation of frozen or donated embryos or donated oocytes + Read more ...

  • This item is for the preparation only, it does not include the transfer of frozen or donated embryos or donated oocytes to the female reproductive system
  • Items 13209 and 13215 may be claimed in the same treatment cycle
  • Note: if the service is billed as an inpatient service, the clinic should be contacted to confirm
  • A restriction between item 13218 and the other items (including itself) 13200, 13201, 13202, 13203 and 13212 applies for 26 days. Only one item may be billed for the treatment cycle
  • Claims can be overridden with PI 45 when the account indicates a ‘short cycle’ and commencement of a new cycle begins

The date of service in respect of treatment covered by item 13218 is deemed to be the first day of the treatment cycle.

13221

Preparation of semen for the purpose of artificial insemination + Read more ...

  • Item 13221 can only be rendered in conjunction with item 13203 and can be claimed multiple times within a treatment cycle, even on the same day
  • Item 13221 and 13209 may be claimed in the same treatment cycle
  • Where this item is claimed more than 30 days after the item 13203, the account must be annotated with ‘long cycle’ or words to that affect

13241

Open surgical testicular sperm retrieval, unilateral, using operating microscope + Read more ...

  • For the purposes of intracytoplasmic sperm injection for male factor infertility
  • Not being a service associated with a service to which item 13218 or 37604 applies

13251

Intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI) + Read more ...

  • Item 13251 excludes a service to which item 13203 or 13218 applies
  • Items 13200, 13201, 13212, 13215 and 13209 may be claimed in the same treatment cycle

13260

Fertility preservation, processing and cryopreservation of semen + Read more ...

  • This item is eligible under Reciprocal Health Care Agreements (RHCA). See QITI Assessing Ruling for more information

13290

Collection of semen from an eligible patient + Read more ...

  • Patients with spinal injuries or medically induced impotence are eligible

* Item ended 31 December 2021

ART treatment cycles

Table 2: this table describes treatment cycles for the purposes of ART services.

Item

Description

1

ART treatment cycles + Read more ...

A treatment cycle is a series of treatments for the purposes of ART services and is defined as:

beginning on either

  • the day the recipient starts treatment with super ovulatory drugs, or
  • the first day of the recipient’s menstrual cycle

ending on either

  • not more than 30 days after the treatment cycle begins, or
  • the day after the last of the following services is provided:
    • Oocyte retrieval (item 13212)
    • Transfer of embryos or both ova and sperm (item 13215), or
    • Preparation of sperm for the purpose of artificial insemination (item 13221)

A treatment cycle has a maximum of 31 days, including the first day.

2

Short treatment cycle + Read more ...

A treatment cycle is considered a ‘short cycle’ when it is 26 days or less.

Claims received for ART services within the 26 day restrictive period of the previous cycle must be verified by a notation in the text stating 'short cycle' (or wording to that effect).

These items then can be paid with PI 45.

3

Long treatment cycle + Read more ...

A treatment cycles is considered a ‘long cycle’ when it exceeds 31 days.

Treatment cycles generally do not exceed 31 days. However, if items 13212, 13215 or 13221 are claimed more than 30 days after applicable global items, the account must be annotated ‘long cycle’ or wording to that effect in order to attract a benefit.

These items can then be paid with PI 48.

The Medicare assessing system will apply the 26 day restriction automatically when 2 cycles overlap due to a long cycle.

Note: if the date of service for items 13212, 13215 or 13221 is before 1 November 2014 and 6-ASC NOT is returned, reject the service with reason code (RSN) 550.
