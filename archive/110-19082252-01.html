For staff trained in Automation of Income Stream Reviews (AISR).

This document explains how to action a manual automated income stream review for defined benefit products.

Purposes

The automatic update process checks different fields before automatically applying an income stream provider's data. Some of these handoffs are unique to defined benefit income streams.

Differences for defined benefit income streams

Defined benefit income streams can have handoffs due to the following differences in the provider data compared to the customer’s record:

  • Start date
  • Product type
  • Provider name
  • Tax-free component method

A handoff may also be generated when the defined benefit income stream is suspended.

Suspended defined benefit income streams

An income stream provider may suspend the customer's income stream:

  • for failure to confirm proof of life, or
  • because of returned correspondence

If a provider has suspended a customer's income stream, the customer must contact the provider to:

  • restart the payment and
  • get paid any arrears

The customer's income stream is not zeroed off on the system when a suspension occurs.

When the provider pays the arrears to the customer, no coding is needed on the customer's record.

Customer's past fortnightly payments are already adjusted to account for the income from their defined benefit income stream.

Changes in tax-free component method

The provider data may have a different tax-free component method to what is coded on the customer's record.

Apply the tax-free component (TFC) method in the provider data to the customer's record.

Do not change the TFC method to I or F if:

  • the customer's record has a method of S (saved), and
  • the provider data has a method of New with a TFC amount lower than the TFC under the saved method

For more details about TFC methods, see Adding or updating a defined benefit income stream.
