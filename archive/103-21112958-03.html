1

Check initial waiting period + Read more ...

Participants must be within their initial waiting period in order to change their start date.

Check that the participant has commenced on the CDC and the waiting period field on the Q2AS screen displays a number greater than 0 days.

Is the participant currently within their initial waiting period?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No,
    • Check the Payment Summary (PS) screen is displaying ‘multiple desti’ instead of a ‘BSB/Account number’. If yes, the participant is no longer in their initial waiting period and ineligible to change their start date. Procedure ends here
    • If the customer is not on the CDC and is an NT customer, go to Income Management (IM) to Cashless Debit Card (CDC) Transition in the Northern Territory
    • If the customer is not on the CDC but wants to volunteer, go to Cashless Debit Card (CDC) Volunteers

2

Personalised Card + Read more ...

Does the participant have their personalised card?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, remind the participant to check their mail for their personalised card, as it will have their details recorded on it (Name, BSB and Account Number). Go to Step 3

3

Temporary Card + Read more ...

Does the participant have access to a temporary card?

For example, the participant already has their temporary card or is in a face-to-face environment, at a TCU branch or a participating Agent site and can request one.

RISK POINT: If the participant is at a TCU branch or a participating Agent site, confirm they are calling from a location that stocks temporary cards for their selected card issuer.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, access to a temporary card is required to change a waiting period to '0'. See Temporary Cashless Debit Cards (CDC), to help them access a card
    • If the participant is unable to access a card, procedure ends here
    • Otherwise, go to Step 5

4

Card Status + Read more ...

Check whether the participant has activated their personalised or temporary card with the card issuer?

Run the Card Details/History guided procedure in Customer First, check to see if the card status displays 'Active'.

Does the card status display as ‘Active’

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Ability to activate the personalised or temporary card + Read more ...

To reduce a waiting period to ‘0’ days, the participant needs to confirm they will be able to activate their card (temporary or personalised) today.

Tell the participant that they must activate their card to access funds in their CDC account. The card does not have to be activated before their waiting period is reduced.

Has the participant confirmed they will be able to activate their card today?

  • Yes,
    • Service Centre: It is preferred that the customer visits the card issuer's website, app or phone to activate their card, however to activate a customer's temporary Indue card see Table 3 in Temporary Cashless Debit Cards (CDC). Then, go to Step 6
    • Hotline staff, go to Step 6
  • No,
    • If the participant wants to change their waiting period to ‘0’, procedure ends here
    • Otherwise, go to Step 6

6

Residual IM funds + Read more ...

If the participant was previously on Income Management (IM) and is transitioning to the CDC, check the ‘Income Management Summary’ screen to see whether they have a current IM balance.

  • Yes, for customers:
    • previously on Compulsory IM, go to Step 7
    • previously on Voluntary IM, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Table 2

7

Disbursement of IM funds for customers transitioning from IM measures LTWPR and DEY + Read more ...

Remind the participant that IM funds will be held for automatic disbursement once the CDC start date is reached. This also applies if reducing their waiting period to zero days and the CDC start date is the same day.

  • If the IM account has $210 or less, the whole amount will be held. Funds will then be transferred to the CDC account on the next business day
  • If the IM account has more than $210, the system will hold up to $400 with the first instalment of $200 delivered the next business day to the CDC account, the remaining held amount up to $200 will be scheduled for delivery 14 days after the first payment is received. Any remaining IM funds will be held in $200 amounts for disbursement to the CDC account. Disbursements occur in fortnightly instalments until all IM funds are depleted

Example: a customer has $473.04 in their IM account and commences on the CDC today.

  • $400 will be held today, of which $200 will be delivered to their CDC account the next business day. The remaining $200 held will be delivered 14 days after the first payment is received
  • This leaves an available balance of $73.04 in their IM account
  • The participant has the option of:
    • Allowing $73.04 to be automatically disbursed in another fortnight
    • Requesting the $73.04 to be transferred to their CDC account via a UCP after they commence on the CDC
    • Requesting access to the held funds after they commence on the CDC

The participant may want funds transferred to their BasicsCard before reducing their waiting period. They can also request a lump sum transfer to their CDC account once their start date is reached.

Does the participant want IM funds transferred to their BasicsCard?

  • Yes, go to the BasicsCard Summary screen, click on ‘Manage Expenses’ and complete a ‘BasicsCard Immediate Expense’. Go to Table 2
  • No, go to Table 2

8

Participants volunteering after ending their VIM agreement + Read more ...

Participants previously on Voluntary IM may have residual IM funds in their IM account for disbursement.

Advise the participant that remaining IM funds can be transferred to their:

  • BasicsCard for immediate use. Go to the BasicsCard Summary screen, click on ‘Manage Expenses’ and complete a ‘BasicsCard Immediate Expense’ (process before proceeding)(process now)
  • Cashless Debit Card in a lump sum or $200 instalments (See process at Table 2 Step 6).
  • Personal bank account via a lump sum or $200 instalments.

Go to Table 2.

Changing a CDC waiting period

Table 2: this table provides the required information for a staff to change a customer’s initial waiting period and commence CDC earlier.

If any issues occur please contact the TPS or Access Line. If TPS or Access are unable to resolve the issue, TPS or Access are to email the CDC Transition mailbox.

Step

Action

1

CDC summary + Read more ...

To change the participant's initial waiting period, navigate to the Q2AS screen:

  • Waiting Period: change to chosen length in days (must be less than default)
  • Key 'I' in the Action field
  • Press [Enter]

The Q2NT screen will be displayed, where it is mandatory to complete the notes field. Add the date the participant has requested to bring forward their CDC start date.

  • Key ’I’ in the Action field
  • Press [Enter]

The AR screen will be displayed:

  • select Finalise and press [Enter]

This will change the participant's CDC Start date.

If a zero-day waiting period was chosen, the participant will commence on the CDC immediately.

Clear out of the record to allow the CDC trigger to run. Stay out of the record for 3 minutes.

Go to Step 2.

2

Discuss CDC functionality + Read more ...

Discuss CDC functionality and support options with the participant. See Cashless Debit Card (CDC) for more information and support on how participants can:

  • make payments from the CDC
  • locate the card issuer's website and the ‘how to’ tutorials.
  • download the App
  • check their account balance

Go to Step 3.

3

Check start date has changed + Read more ...

Navigate to the Q2AS screen. An information message will display at the top of the screen.

For participants with a zero day waiting period, the information message will state 'Customer is on CDC'.

For participants with a residual waiting period, the information message will state ‘Customer is due to commence on CDC on {DATE}’. Check that the start date is correct.

If the updates have not completed as expected, contact CDC technical support.

  • Voluntary participants (Q2AS screen has a ‘Y’ next to 'Volunteer’), go to Step 7
  • All other participants, go to Step 4

4

Revised Notice of Decision + Read more ...

The participant has already provided consent, and been issued their Notice of Decision letter during their transition contact.

By coding the Q2AS screen again, this automatically generates a Revised Notice of Decision letter with the new CDC start date, which will be seen on the HS screen.

Note: Voluntary CDC participants are not issued a Notice of Decision letter.

Please read to the participant;

  • I am required to inform you that the delegate has issued you a Revised Notice of Decision letter and that you are now a participant of the CDC program. A copy of this Notice will be sent to you.

Face to Face staff Only: the Revised Notice of Decision letter can be printed locally via the HS screen if the participant requests.

Was the participant previously on IM?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Residual IM funds for participants + Read more ...

Does the customer have residual IM funds for disbursement?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 8

6

Disbursement of available IM funds for participants + Read more ...

Was the participant previously on IM, and would like their available IM funds disbursed as a lump sum to the CDC account?

  • Yes, see Access to Income Management (IM) funds for customers starting on the Cashless Debit Card (CDC). Go to Step 8.
  • No, remind the participant that their IM funds will be held in $200 amounts for disbursement to the CDC account. Disbursements occur in fortnightly instalments until all IM funds are depleted. Go to Step 8

7

Disbursement of residual IM funds for Volunteer participants + Read more ...

If the participant has IM residual funds and would like them disbursed to their CDC account:

  • Obtain the participant's CDC account details from:
    • Customer First: Q2SU screen. If they don't display, run the Request Funds Transfer workflow
    • Process Direct: CDC Current Account (CDCAH) screen
  • Go to the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen to update the payment destination recorded for 'GPY'
  • Select 'GPY' to go to the Payment Destination Details (PAD) screen where the payment destination details can be recorded with the participant’s CDC account details, and
  • Finalise the activity
  • Once the GPY has been updated with the participant's CDC or preferred account details, run the ‘Disbursements’ guided procedure
  • At the question ‘Is it likely Income Management will resume within 60 days of IM end? Select ‘No’ radio button, so funds are not held for 60 days before disbursement
  • Select Lump sum disbursement so all remaining funds will pay to the CDC account on the next business day
  • Select ‘Continue’ and finalise the workflow

Note: Income Management funds cannot be disbursed if there is no payment destination recorded for ‘GPY’.

  • For all other disbursements go to Disbursement interview for Voluntary Income Management - Table 2
  • Once the disbursements have been finalised, go to Step 8

8

Finalising the interaction + Read more ...

Advise the participant:

  • their first payment date and amount after commencing on the CDC, and
  • the contact details for the card issuer and CDC Hotline

Provide the participant with their CDC account details. This will assist them to activate a temporary card or access online services when they reach their CDC start date. To access the participant's CDC account details when they have not reached their start date go to:

  • Customer First: run the Request Funds Transfer workflow
    • TCU BSB - 704119
    • Indue BSB - 704772
  • Process Direct: CDC Current Account (CDCAH) screen

Procedure ends here.
