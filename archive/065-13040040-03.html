Check application for oxygen and/or enteral feeding supplement is complete

Note: do not make any mark on the original form unless it is indicated in the below procedure.

Table 1: This table describes how Service Officers check the Application for Oxygen and/or Enteral Feeding Supplement form (AC011) for completion.

Step

Action

1

Check if the form is complete + Read more ...

Service Officers must check the following sections are complete on the ‘Application for Oxygen and/or Enteral Feeding Supplement’ form (AC011):

  • Service name, ID and phone number
  • Care recipient ID and name
  • Declaration, including approved provider/authorised signature and date

Service Officers must check the following items are complete and attached to the AC011 form:

  • For the oxygen supplement, a written and signed certificate from a medical practitioner stating that the care recipient has a continual/ongoing need for the administration of oxygen
  • If the certificate for oxygen supplement states ‘as necessary’ or ‘intermittent' the application cannot be processed
  • For the higher oxygen supplement rate, the application must include monthly costs and copies of tax invoices covering a 3 month period
  • For enteral feeding a written and signed certificate(s) stating that:
    • the care recipient has a medical need for enteral feeding – must have certification from a doctor (not a dietician)
    • the dietary formula is a nutritionally complete formula – must have certification from a doctor or dietician

Note: details of the care recipient’s particular enteral feeding requirements can be in their written certification, care plan or hospital discharge papers, or a dietician’s enteral feeding instructions

  • For the higher enteral feeding supplement rate, the application must include daily costs

Note: where a medical practitioner can be identified via their name and address, it would be sufficient to accept these details on the certificate if the provider number is not supplied.

If Services Australia is not satisfied that the certification about the care recipient’s continual need for oxygen has been made by a medical practitioner (as required by the principles), then Services Australia should ask for the provider number in order to satisfy themselves that the requirement has been met.

The form is incomplete if:

  • it is ineligible or unclear
  • it is missing the declaration page or signature in the declaration
  • supporting evidence has not been provided

Is the form complete with all attachments provided?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Returning an incomplete form + Read more ...

To return an incomplete form, Service Officers must contact the aged care service outlining the reasons via:

  • phone
  • email, or
  • mail. See the Resource page for Using Standard Letter Templates (SLT). To access the letter templates go to letters and electronic messaging:
    • under Medicare, select Provider and Third Party letters
    • select Aged Care

    • complete the template with the necessary information
    • record a note in PaNDA detailing the action taken

Note: once the resubmitted application is received it will be treated as a new application.

Assessment of the oxygen and/or enteral feeding supplement form (AC011)

Table 2: This table describes how Service Officers determine the rate of oxygen and/or enteral feeding supplement.

Step

Action

1

Assessing the supplement payment rates + Read more ...

There are 2 payment rates of the oxygen and/or enteral feeding supplement:

  • The standard oxygen and/or enteral feeding supplement
  • The higher oxygen and/or enteral feeding supplement

The higher oxygen and/or enteral feeding supplement is payable when the cost of administering is 125% or more than the standard rate and tax invoices covering a 3 month period have been accompanied for the higher oxygen rate with the application form.

  • For the higher oxygen,
    • (Concentrator hire + Cylinder delivery cost + Cylinder hire + Kit hire)
  • For the higher enteral feeding,
    • [(mls per day/Tin size) * Tin cost] + Giving set + Flexitainer + Hire of pump cost

Service Officers must manually calculate the payable amount from the application form by using above formula.

2

Higher and standard rate + Read more ...

Are the actual costs greater than or equal to 125% of the standard rate and have tax invoices been included for the higher oxygen supplement?

  • Yes, the higher oxygen and/or enteral feeding supplement is payable
    • Service Officers must manually add up all cost items from the tax invoices (covering a 3 month period) and calculate the daily cost for the higher oxygen supplement for each month
    • The higher enteral feeding supplement costings are required on a daily basis
  • No, the standard oxygen and/or enteral feeding supplement is payable
    • If the total cost is less than the higher rate, the Service Officers must approve standard rate only

Note: the current and historical rates of the oxygen and enteral feeding supplement are published on the Department of Health’s website. The Resources page contains a link.

3

Key standard rate oxygen application + Read more ...

Service Officers must key this standard rate type application by taking below actions.

To key the standard rate of the oxygen in ACMPS:

  • log on to ACMPS
  • select Create tab then Event Create from the left hand side menu. The Event: New screen will display
  • complete Event: New screen with the relevant fields
  • Service – This field attributes to the Service ID and must be keyed in. It can be entered manually, or by search results (To undertake a search, click the field to open the search dialog box)
  • Care Recipient - This field attributes to the Care Recipient ID and must be keyed in. It can be entered manually, or by search results (To undertake a search, click the field to open the search dialog box)
  • Care Type – select ‘Home Care’ from the drop down list
  • Type of Event - select ‘Maintain Oxygen’ from the drop down list
  • Value – select the value of ‘Normal Oxygen’ from the drop down list
  • Requested Start – The start date of the event. It can be entered manually, or selected from the calendar field to display the calendar). By default, the current day’s date is populated in this field. This cannot be a future date.
    • The date on the certificate indicates when the continuous oxygen started.
    • The oxygen start date on the certificate is not necessarily the date which the service has written on the application form. The date cannot be later than the current claim period. In this instance the date on the certificate is always used.
    • If there is no date on the certificate that indicated when the continuous oxygen started but there is a date that the medical practitioner signed the document then the signature date must be used.
  • Requested End – The end date of the event. It can be entered manually, or selected from the calendar field to display the calendar. By default, this field will have the value ’31.12.9999’. This is an optional field.
    • the ‘Requested End Date’ must be after the Entry date
  • select Save tab to save the Event’
  • the event status is updated as ‘Processed’

Note: once approved, the standard oxygen supplement payment continues unless the care recipient's circumstances change.

Procedure ends here.

  • Requested Start – The start date of the event can be entered manually, or selected from the calendar field to display the calendar. By default, the current day’s date is populated in this field. This cannot be a future date.
    • The date on the certificate indicates when the continuous oxygen started.

Review of a rejected decision and reviewing a decision for the higher rate of oxygen

Table 5: This table describes how to reject an application for oxygen supplement and/or enteral feeding supplement and conduct a review on a rejected decision.

Step

Action

1

Reject an application for oxygen and/or enteral feeding supplement + Read more ...

Service Officers must reject the application if the form is:

  • incomplete or unclear
  • missing the signature/declaration
  • supporting evidence has not been provided

Service Officers must:

  • contact the aged care service via telephone
  • formally advise in writing that the application has been rejected
    • the reason for the rejection decision and the review process must be clearly documented in writing
  • return the form to the aged care service

2

Conduct a review of a rejected decision + Read more ...

Services Australia will undertake a review of the decision if requested. The aged care service must provide additional supporting documents with this review application.

An SME or higher who was not involved in the initial decision-making process must conduct this review.

Service Officers can make the following decisions to:

  • uphold the initial decision to reject the application, or
  • overturn the initial decision, process the supplement application

If a review application has been rejected, Service Officers must:

  • refer to a SME if unsure about a decision to reject
  • clearly document the reason for the rejection decision and the appeal process

3

Actions after decision taken + Read more ...

Service Officers must follow the below steps when an application is reviewed and/or rejected:

  • upload all documents in PaNDA of the determination and copies of correspondence (e-mail/fax/letter) forwarded to the aged care service
  • document clear and accurate care recipient notes in the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP)
