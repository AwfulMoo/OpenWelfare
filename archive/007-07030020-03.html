Customer/partner is in receipt of income support payments + Read more ...

PPL is treated as income for customers and/or their partner who are on an income support payments. If an of income support payment customer nominates a PPL period start date in the past, or claims Flexible PPL for a day in the past, they may incur an overpayment for days they were receiving both of income support payment and PPL.

  • Yes, one outbound contact attempt must be made to discuss the potential payment impacts claiming PPL will have on their and/or their partner's income support payment. 4

Receipt of JobKeeper Payment + Read more ...

A customer cannot receive JobKeeper Payment for the same period they are receiving PPL.

A customer can select a NSD for their PPL period that is after their JobKeeper Payment period, only where they have not returned to work.

It is the responsibility of PPL customers who are eligible for JobKeeper Payment to advise their employers the start date and end date of their PPL period (not the dates PPL was received) to ensure they are not overpaid JobKeeper Payment.

If a customer currently receiving PPL advises they are receiving JobKeeper Payment for the same period, advise the customer that they:

  • are not entitled to receive the payments at the same time, and
  • need to advise their employer they are receiving PPL

If the:

  • customer is receiving PPL and JobKeeper Payment and wants to continue to receive PPL:
    • advise the customer of the start and end dates of the PPL period and that they need to advise their employer
    • DOC the details on the customers record. Procedure ends here
  • customer's PPL is not yet current, they may request to change their NSD. See Customer notifies a change of circumstances for Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
  • customer wants to receive JobKeeper Payment instead of PPL and their PPL claim has not yet been determined, the customer can withdraw their claim. See Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL)

Service Officers are not required to contact the customer to discuss PPL entitlement in relation to JobKeeper Payment. JobKeeper Payment does not preclude PPL entitlement, however, PPL does preclude entitlement to JobKeeper Payment for the same period. Customers with queries in relation to JobKeeper Payment entitlement are to be directed to discuss with their employer.

For more information about processing PPL claims, see Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments.

Note: customers in receipt of JobKeeper Payment during their work test period for PPL can count these hours towards the work test. See Paid Parental Leave scheme Work Test.

5

Received Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment + Read more ...

Customers cannot receive Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment/s if they are receiving PPL on any day during the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment relevant period.

For more information on eligibility for Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment, see Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

If a customer is claiming PPL and it is identified that their PPL period will overlap with a Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment, contact the customer.

Tell them they:

  • cannot receive DAP and a Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment at the same time as PPL, and
  • to nominate a future PPL period that does not overlap their Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment period/s

Note: Pandemic Leave Disaster Payments do not count as qualifying work for the PPL or DAP work test.

6

Received Coronavirus (COVID-19) Disaster Payment + Read more ...

A customer cannot receive a COVID-19 Disaster Payment if they are receiving PPL on any day during the COVID-19 Disaster Payment relevant period.

For more information on eligibility for COVID-19 Disaster Payment, see COVID-19 Disaster Payment.

If a customer is claiming PPL as a primary claimant and it is identified that they are, or have been, receiving a COVID-19 Disaster Payment since the date of birth/adoption of their child, contact them to advise:

  • they are not entitled to receive COVID-19 Disaster Payment at the same time as PPL, and
  • if they have been on paid or unpaid leave from their employer for any period before or since the birth of their child, they are not entitled to COVID-19 Disaster Payment for any relevant period where their paid or unpaid leave covers every day of that relevant period

If, after a discussion with the customer it is determined they were eligible for COVID-19 Disaster Payment before or after the date of birth i.e. they were actually due to work during the relevant periods, but they were unable to due to the restricted movement:

  • they could still claim COVID-19 Disaster Payment, and
  • nominate a future PPL period that does not overlap any of their eligible COVID-19 Disaster Payment relevant periods

Note: if a customer is eligible for COVID-19 Disaster Payment, receiving the payment will not count as a return to work unless the customer actually returned to work/duties.

Has the customer received, or are they receiving COVID-19 Disaster Payments when they are not eligible?

  • Yes,
    • If the customer is receiving automatic payments, these will need to stop. Generally, entitlement would stop from either the commencement of any pre-birth leave (paid or unpaid) or the date of birth/adoption of the child, whichever occurs first. See COVID-19 Disaster Payment - automatic payment and change of circumstances
    • For customers who have already received COVID-19 Disaster Payment and were not eligible, a debt will need to be investigated
  • No, go to Step 7

Note: customers in receipt of COVID-19 Disaster Payment during their work test period for PPL can count these hours towards the work test. See Paid Parental Leave scheme Work Test.

Is the customer providing information or documentation for the PPL claim?

WLM business rules will redirect the work item to FIC staff who will process the claim. Procedure ends here
    • Other documentation, see Receipt of claims, forms and documentation for family assistance
  • No, go to Step 8

9

Assessed/pending pre-birth claim + Read more ...

If initial eligibility criteria are met pending proof of birth/entry into care/placement, the claim status will finalise as ASSessed PPL/ASS-PCB (Pending proof of birth/entry into care).

Where the primary claimant is the biological father or partner of the birth mother, pre-birth claims may be finalised as PPL/ASS-PCB. Once proof of birth is provided, the claim may auto reject (REJ) or be made not effective (NEF) due to:

  • additional Circumstances not met (ACN)
  • primary claimant has claimed (PAO)

If the primary claimant is the biological father or partner of the birth mother, advise the claimant to be eligible for PPL once the child is born, they need to meet the exceptional circumstances criteria, see Step 2 in Completing the PPL claim assessment table.

The biological father or partner of the birth mother may be eligible for PPL as a secondary claimant if the primary claimant is transferring their PPL entitlements (full or partial transfer). See Eligibility for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) as a secondary or tertiary claimant.

If the customer is contacting to advise a change in circumstances, see Customer notifies a change of circumstances for Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

Upon lodgement of proof of birth, see Processing proof of a child's birth.

For pre-birth PPL claims, ensure the Tax File Number (TFN) and bank account details have been recorded prior to finalising the claim. If the customer has not provided the TFN and/or bank account details, contact the customer to obtain the information.

Pre-birth/entry claims will be automatically made not effective (NEF) 56 days after the nominated start date of the PPL.

10

Assessed/pending post-birth claim + Read more ...

Claims have been assessed but are awaiting a start date or further action from the employer will have a claim status of:

  • PPL/ASS-ASD if the claim is awaiting nominated start date. This includes claims made by a primary claimant awaiting date of transfer of primary care in cases of full PPL transfer

  • PPL/ASS-AFN if the customer is not eligible for PPL period and the claim is awaiting nomination of first Flexible PPL day
  • PPL/ASS-EIR if the employer registration is still required
  • PPL/ASS-EIA if the employer acceptance is still required

FTB Part B stopped: to avoid an overpayment FTB Part B while their PPL claim status is PPL/ASS-EIR or PPL/ASS-EIA, the customer will not be paid FTB Part B from the child's date of entry into care. Once the employer acceptance is recorded, the customer will be granted PPL from the date of entry into care or will be paid arrears of FTB Part B for this period.

Employers have 14 days to register for the scheme or to accept the determination to provide PPL to the employee. The customer should be encouraged to discuss this with their employer.

If an employer responds to the employer determination and they provide an alternate Australian Business Number (ABN) to the ABN advised by the employee they are, by action, presenting themselves as the nominated employer. See Role of the employer in Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

If a Secretary Initiated review/ACTDOC is on the customer's record from the Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT), advising the employer has informed Services Australia the customer:

  • is not known to the employer, or
  • does not meet the PPL work test, see Role of the employer in Parental Leave Pay (PPL)

Have 14 days passed?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, advise the customer that their employer is still required to take action to accept the employer determination. Encourage the customer to discuss this with their employer. No further action can be taken by PPLEPT until the timeframe has expired. Procedure ends here

11

Assessed/pending post-birth claim more than 14 days + Read more ...

If more than 14 days have passed since the claim was finalised as assessed pending employer action, determine whether PPLEPT have commenced the PPL assisted dispute resolution (ADR) process. PPLEPT will DOC the employee’s record when compliance action has commenced. If compliance action has commenced, advise the customer that PPLEPT are following up with their employer to assist resolve the employer’s outstanding PPL obligations.

Where a Paid Parental Leave MFU is on the AL screen and no DOC has been recorded by PPLEPT to indicate the PPL assisted dispute resolution (ADR) process has been initiated, determine whether an ACTDOC to PPLEPT is appropriate. This may be where:

  • the customer would like to provide additional information to the PPLEPT (including where the employer has indicated they are unwilling to comply), or
  • the customer is experiencing financial hardship and their PLP nominated start date is in the past
  • there is no MFU on the employee’s record and the ADR process has not been started

Where a Paid Parental Leave MFU is on the AL screen and/or there is a DOC recorded by PPLEPT to indicate the PPL ADR process has been initiated, determine whether an ACTDOC to PPLEPT is appropriate. This may be where:

  • the customer would like to provide additional information to the PPLEPT (including where the employer has indicated they are unwilling to comply), or
  • the customer is experiencing financial hardship and their PLP nominated start date is in the past, and
  • it has been more than 21 days since the assisted dispute resolution (ADR) process has been initiated

Does the customer satisfy at least one of the above criteria?

  • Yes, in Customer First send an ACTDOC to the PPLEPT, Region PEP. Use Fast Note (Service reason PPL - Paid Parental Leave) - select Auto text, use Families > Enquiry > Handoff to PPLEPT - CADR. Select Confirm to create a DOC
    Note: Feedback will be provided to service officers sending an inappropriate ACTDOC.
  • No, advise the employee that PPLEPT will commence/continue the PPL assisted dispute resolution (ADR) process. Encourage the customer to discuss this with their employer. Procedure ends here

When the ACTDOC is received at the PPLEPT, see Assisted dispute resolution for employer determination for Parental Leave Pay (PPL)

Procedure ends here.

12

PPL claim benefit status is STA, CUR, REJ or NEF + Read more ...

If the PPL claim benefit status is:

  • STA (started), view assessment details, go to Step 14
  • CUR (current), go to Step 13
  • NEF (not effective), tell the customer the status of their PPL claim and the additional information required to make their claim effective. To re-index the NEF claim, see Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
  • REJ (rejected). If a PPL claim was rejected, NBS and NBU can be assessed with the customer's FTB. Invite the customer to claim FTB if the customer has not already done so. See Claim choice for a newborn or adopted child

If the customer is advising they have registered or applied to register the birth of their newborn child following a rejection BNR (Birth Not Registered), see Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

Procedure ends here.

PPL claim status is CUR + Read more ...

In Process Direct

  • Flexible PPL (P1FLX) will display any Flexible PPL days that have been any Flexible PPL days that have been:
    • connected to the PPL period
    • been claimed separately

In Customer First

  • Go to Workspace > Claim Contacts > Paid Parental Leave claim summary to access the PPL Circs Summary (P1CS) page. This page will display the claim status and component level status for:
  • the PPL period
  • any Flexible PPL days that have been connected to the PPL period
  • any Flexible PPL days that have been claimed separately

For information on these component statuses, see Cancellation, rejection, not effective, assessed and current zero rate codes for Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

Note: Flexible PPL days are only available for children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020.

Tell the customer of the outcome recorded on the new claim DOC on the Document List (DL) screen or Notes.

Procedure ends here.

14

Has the customer made an effective claim? + Read more ...

For Smart Centre Processing staff only.

For a pre-birth/entry claim:

  • A submitted online claim or a paper claim signed by the customer within the family assistance, PPL and DAP allowable early claim period
  • Expected date of birth and primary care
  • Nominated start date
  • Australian residence details - documents may be required
  • PPL work test details - evidence to support the PPL work test may be required
  • PPL income test details - evidence to support the PPL income test may be required
  • Tax File Number (TFN)
  • Bank account details
  • Their employer's Australian Business Number (ABN). In some instances, employers are not required to have an ABN, for example people who are not in a business and employ household workers, such as nannies, cleaners or gardeners. In these instances, Services Australia will deliver PPL to the employee

For a post-birth/entry claim, additional information required:

  • customer's tax file number
  • payment destination details (whether or not an employer determination has been made)
  • proof of the child's birth (if not already provided)

Is the claim effective?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in the Completing the PPL claim assessment table
  • No, go to Step 15

15

Not effective (NEF) claim + Read more ...

For Smart Centre Processing staff only.

Early claim lodged outside of allowable claim period

If a pre-claim is lodged outside the allowable early claim period, see Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

Auto NEF when Proof of Birth is not supplied

Pre-birth claims will be automatically made not effective (NEF) 56 days after the nominated start date of the Parental Leave Pay if the required post birth information is not provided.

Auto NEF-PAC (PPL already claimed by customer)

If a customer's PPL has been cancelled CRQ (customer request) before receiving the full 90 days of PPL, they will not be able to reclaim this payment. Any subsequent claims submitted by the customer will automatically NEF-PAC (PPL already claimed by customer).

Note: If the customer has previously requested their initial PPL claim for the child be cancelled CRQ, they cannot appeal this decision. For more information, see Cancellation of Parental Leave Pay (PPL)

If additional information is required

Contact the customer to obtain the information. Issue written request if necessary. See Information requests for FTB, PPL, DAP and CCB lump sum claims.

If information is not provided as requested, see Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

Once all information required for the claim to be assessed is supplied, see Step 1 in the Completing the PPL claim assessment table.

3

PPL to be delivered by employer + Read more ...

Employers must deliver the PPL period plus any connected Flexible PPL days to their eligible employees. If the employer cannot do this, it will be delivered by Services Australia.

To view employer delivery details,

In Process Direct

  • Use Super Key to go to the PPL Employer Notified Details (P1ERD) screen

In Customer First

  • select Workspace > Claim Contacts > Paid Parental Leave claim summary to access the PPL Claim Summary (P1CS) page
  • Go to the Paid Parental Leave Employer Notified Details (P1ERD) by placing an ‘N’ next to the claim to view the employer details.

If the customer meets the criteria to have their PPL period delivered by their current employer, and the employer has pre-registered for PPL, an acceptance letter will auto-generate to the employer and the claim will have a status of PPL/ASS-EIA awaiting the employer acceptance.

Is the PPL and connected Flexible PPL days to be delivered by the employer?

  • Yes, once acceptance has been received from the employer, the customer's benefit status will be updated to PPL/ASS-PCB (pending proof of birth/entry into care) or PPL/ASS-ASD if the confirmation of birth or entry to care has already been received. The customer's letter will confirm PPL is to be provided by the employer
  • No, if the PPL and Flexible PPL days cannot be delivered by the employer, the payments will be delivered by Services Australia. Note: requests are received from PPL customers specifying their employer(s) as their requested payer when it has been determined Services Australia will be providing their PPL. Although the customer's employer may not be legally required to provide the customer's PPL, they may elect to do so. Encourage the customer to discuss this option with their employer. The employer must register with Services Australia and opt in to provide PPL before the customer submits their claim

For more information about how PPL is delivered to customers by their employer or Services Australia, see Delivery of Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

4

Child left birth mothers care + Read more ...

Birth mothers may still be able to take time off work and be eligible for PPL if:

  • they have given birth as part of a surrogacy arrangement
  • they have given the child up for adoption, or
  • the state/territory have removed their child

PPL is available in these circumstances for maternal recovery purposes.

Note: in this case, the birth mother can only receive PPL within the 18 weeks following the birth of the child. For children that are born on or after 1 July 2020, this includes Flexible PPL days.

Has the PPL child left the birth mothers care within 18 weeks of the child's birth?

  • Yes, see, Claiming payments for children leaving care as part of the process of adoption or a surrogacy arrangement:
    • If the birth mother is not eligible for PPL, check the customer's entitlement to Newborn Supplement (NBS)/Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU)
    • Check the child in care details are correct
  • No, procedure ends here
