Reporting arrangements for national public holidays

This table describes the national public holiday payment and reporting arrangements.

Step

Action

1

Reporting requirement + Read more ...

Customer needs to report their employment income, participation details and any changes to their circumstances to generate their payment.

Is the customer required to report with a Reporting Statement (SU19) form?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Reporting options + Read more ...

Customer needs to report their employment income, mutual obligation requirements and change of circumstances either:

  • using the Reporting option online
  • using the Reporting using phone self service option
  • using the Express Plus application
  • by phone
  • in person

If the customer needs to report early due to a national public holiday, their Reporting Statement will show an early 'Report on this day' date.

Customers need to include information relevant to the full period. This includes estimating employment income paid up to and including their normal Due to Lodge (DTL) date.

When the customer contacts:

  • Check the date in the Report Next Due: field on the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen to make sure that the customer is reporting on the correct date
    Note: see the Holiday processing arrangements information page for the appropriate date of lodgement. The Resources page contains a link to the Holiday processing arrangements page
  • Record employment income as usual, using the Earnings and Reporting workflow in Process Direct

Genuine attempts must be made to transition customers to report via self service options before assisted reporting is completed, unless an Exception applies.

Procedure ends here.

3

Customer needs to submit an SU19 + Read more ...

Does the customer have a Reporting Statement (SU19) form with a Permitted Due to Lodge (PDTL) date?

These forms will be on yellow paper. Check the top of the form for PDTL date. All will have a special message above the Centrelink logo.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, forms should be accepted and processed in the normal way, see Reporting statements for job seekers

Note: customers can still report online or by phone self service during the holiday processing periods. Encourage customers to continue using their regular reporting channel.

4

Submit form before PDTL + Read more ...

Is the customer trying to submit their form before the PDTL date?

Check the top of the form. The PDTL also appears on the NSS General Information (NGI) screen for JSP/YA customers. However, PEN and PGA systems currently do not display this date.

  • Yes, return the form to the customer and advise them to return this on or after the PDTL, unless cancellation is required with a date of effect prior to the PDTL. For cancellation, see Cancellation of payments
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Submitting form on or after PDTL but before DTL + Read more ...

Is the customer lodging their form on the PDTL or after PDTL but before the normal DTL?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Check form + Read more ...

Check the Reporting Statement (SU19) form for change of circumstance information, such as details of income paid. The income details should estimate earnings for the full period of the form.

Process in the normal way, see Reporting statements for job seekers.

7

Report on normal day or first working day after PDTL or DTL + Read more ...

Is the customer reporting on their normal reporting day (or the first available working day after the PDTL or their DTL)?

  • Yes, accept and process the form in the normal way, see Reporting statements for job seekers
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Form submitted late + Read more ...

For customers trying to submit their Reporting Statement (SU19) form three or more days after their usual reporting date, see Early or late lodgement of job seeker reports.
