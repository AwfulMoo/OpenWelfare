Confirming concession card entitlement and issuing interim vouchers

Action

1

Customer asks for a replacement concession card + Read more ...

View entitlement details on the Concession Entitlement Summary (MCCS) screen. This screen shows details of current and historical concession entitlement. Service Officers must check the customer record carefully as there may have been a change in entitlement status that the customer is not aware of or has forgotten. For example, the customer may have returned early from overseas and not advised Services Australia.

Since 1 July 2009, partnered customers have been able to request to have their concession card reissued without their partners name listed. This may be for privacy concerns the customer may have.

The priority concession for the customer will be the first one listed. There will be a blank line between it and the next concession entitlement.

Customers can use the digital wallet in the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app to display a copy of their concession card.

The digital card does not replace the physical concession card. Concession providers decide how to use the digital card.

Partners and children who are eligible dependents of the cardholder will be listed on the Concession Dependent Summary (MCDS) screen.

Note: Concession Card Issue Summary (CCIS) screen displays actual cards issued, not current entitlement.

2

Trouble with accessing concession entitlements through a pharmacy + Read more ...

Is customer experiencing trouble with accessing concession entitlements through a pharmacy?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Ensure residence information is recorded + Read more ...

The customer's residence qualification will be automatically assessed as long as the residence information is correctly recorded.

If the residence information is already recorded from a previous claim, no further information will be necessary. If residence information is incomplete, the datalink with the Department of Home Affairs will need to be activated to obtain the necessary information. For assistance, see Activating the Department of Home Affairs datalink and contingency procedures if datalink is unavailable.

If the customer is experiencing trouble with accessing concessional entitlements through a pharmacy, record their Medicare card number on the Medicare Number Details (MND) screen. See Recording Medicare card numbers for the Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) and Medicare Safety Net for more details. Once the Medicare card number has been recorded, refresh the record go to Step 4. This will retransmit relevant data overnight to Medicare Australia (and subsequently be available to pharmacies).

Note: Social Security Law does not require that a concession card is signed. For example the customer may be unable to sign because of a disability or they may be a child who has not yet developed a signature.

Services Australia only provides the concession cards, not the concessions. It is ultimately the concession provider who determines whether to accept a concession card which has not been signed by the person named on it.

4

Refresh concession entitlement + Read more ...

To refresh, run the Confirm Concession Entitlement guided procedure. The guided procedure will check the Concession Entitlement Summary (MCCS) and the Concession Dependant Summary (MCDS) screens to see if a current or historical entitlement to a concession card exists. It will re-send current or historical details to Medicare Australia and provide the option to issue a confirm concession entitlement letter to confirm the current or historical entitlement selected.

The Confirm Concession Entitlement guided procedure completes a suitable DOC for the selected entitlement, as appropriate.

Manual updates should be done only if the guided procedure is unavailable. See Step 1 in the Manual processing of concession card table.

Note: do not provide customers with a screen dump of the Card Details (CCID) screen when requesting proof of their eligibility for concessions.

Procedure ends here.

5

Replacing priority concession card + Read more ...

For replacing the priority concession card, see How to reissue concession cards.

Note: since 1 July 2009, partnered customers have been able to request their concession card is reissued without their partner’s name listed. This is because of privacy concerns the customer may have.

An interim voucher may be issued to the customer while waiting for their original or replacement card to arrive in the mail.

Is an interim voucher needed?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Interim voucher not required + Read more ...

Issue a replacement card. See How to reissue concession cards.

Record the details on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

7

Interim voucher and/or replacement card required + Read more ...

Interim vouchers should not be issued to a customer in a different name to that recorded on the system.

If a customer requests that an interim voucher be issued because the name stated on their automatically issued concession card is different to the name they use with concession providers, check the Customer Person Detail Summary (CPDS) screen to check which name is recorded as the current legal (LGL) name. The system defaults to display the recorded current LGL name on a concession card. Make any updates before issuing a replacement card or interim voucher.

View entitlement details on the Concession Entitlement Summary (MCCS) screen. The priority concession for the customer will be the first one listed. There will be a blank line between it and the next concession entitlement.

To issue a printed interim card, run the Concession Card Reissue guided procedure.

The printed interim voucher must be signed by the Service Officer and customer and the appropriate card illustration must be date stamped.

If the Concession Card Reissue guided procedure is not available see Step 2 in the Manual processing of concession card table.

Printing workaround:

Some Service Officers have reported that the system generated interim voucher is sometimes not received at the local printer. This anomaly may occur if the user's default printer on ESSentials is different to the printer identified on the User's Profile (OAUP) screen in the Income Security Integrated System (ISIS). If this situation is apparent, take the following action:

  • On the History Summary (HS) screen, locate the letter identified as COMSS055
  • Key 'L' in the field to the left of the identified letter, then press [Enter]
  • Ensure the correct printer ID is stated in the appropriate field
  • Key 'Y' in the FINAL field, then press [Enter]
  • If still unsuccessful, contact mySupport immediately. The Resources page contains a link to mySupport

8

Record issue of interim voucher + Read more ...

If the Concession Card Reissue guided procedure has been used to issue the interim voucher, the action has been recorded on the system and no further action is required for the interim voucher.

Manual processing of concession card

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Manual process to refresh concession entitlement + Read more ...

Updates should be done via the Confirm Concession Entitlement guided procedure and manual updates should only be done if it is not available. See Step 4 in the Confirming concession card entitlement and issuing interim vouchers table.

To manually refresh Concession Entitlements:

Go to the Concession Benefit Action (MCBA) screen and key:

  • Action: RSH
  • Reason: HIC (Health Insurance Commission)
  • Effect date: Today's date
  • Go to Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise

If a concession entitlement refresh has recently been performed and the concession entitled customer is still experiencing difficulties accessing PBS pharmaceuticals, refer the case to the Applications Service Desk via normal protocols.

Note: customers should not be provided with a Screen dump of the Card Details (CCID) screen when requesting proof of their eligibility for concessions.

Procedure ends here.

2

Manual process to issue interim voucher and/or replacement card required + Read more ...

Issue a printed interim card via Concession Card Reissue guided procedure.See Step 7 in the Confirming concession card entitlement and issuing interim vouchers table.

If the Concession Card Reissue guided procedure is unavailable, a manual Confirmation of Concession Card Entitlement (SS055) can be issued. The SS055 is an accountable form completed by hand by a Service Officer. This option should only be used in instances of system failure or declared emergency situations where there is no print facility available to staff. A Service Officer should only issue a SS055 manually if the customer's current concession entitlement has been verified via the Concession Entitlement Summary (MCCS) screen.

3

Manually record details + Read more ...

To manually record the issue of Confirmation of Concession Card Entitlement (SS055):

  • Go to the Concession Card Issue Summary (CCIS) screen
  • Key 'I' next to the concession card required
  • The Concession Card Interim Voucher (CCIV) screen will display. Complete the following fields:
    • Serial no: use the serial number on the SS055 form
    • Valid To: default is 14 days. Can be extended for a maximum period of 3 months from the date of issue (subject to limit of expiry date)
    • Reason for Issue: Use field help ('?') for a list of valid values
  • Complete the Source and DOR fields
  • Print the completed CCIV screen. The printout is to be given to the Accountable Forms Officer as proof of the issue
  • Press [Enter]. The system updates the customer’s record to show the issue of an interim voucher on the CCIS screen. Ensure only the appropriate card illustration is date stamped and all applicable boxes on the form are filled. The 'Date of Grant of concession card' can be found on the MCCS screen. The customer should be made aware of the 'Please note' provisions at the bottom of the SS055 and must sign the SS055 immediately. The Service Officer must then sign the SS055 before issuing the completed form to the customer. There are special requirements for what fields are to be completed for interim vouchers to be issued to custodial parents for use by non-custodial parents. See Concession cards for eligible children temporarily in non-custodial parent's care
  • Finalise the activity and record the details on a DOC

Note: if this process is applied a requested replacement card, may arrive at the same time or even earlier than the interim voucher.

If necessary, issue an auto replacement card via the CCIS screen. See How to reissue concession cards.
