Customer advises they have separated

  • If they advise their relationship status will change in future do not key the change. Offer to issue appropriate forms and do a resubmit DOC for 14 days from the expected date of change. See new postage timeframes for correct resubmit dates
  • If they have their ex-partner appointed as their nominee, ask if they wish to cancel the nominee arrangement

Verification

It is not appropriate to issue a Separation Details (MOD S) or a Separated under one roof (SS293) to FTB/CCS only customers unless:

  • there is any doubt about the separation
  • they are claiming separated under one roof, or
  • there is a possibility they may claim an income support payment in the immediate future

In these cases request verification so that a decision can be made about their relationship status as a member of a couple.

Note: if verification of separation is required, the customer and partner should not be separated on the system until verification is supplied.

The Claiming or not claiming income support table below contains steps for verification of separation.

3

Family and domestic violence present + Read more ...

For vulnerable customers, see the Family and domestic violence procedure to conduct risk identification and offer suitable referrals, such as a referral to a social worker.

Where they indicate that they and/or their children are at risk of family and domestic violence, their relationship status can be updated immediately and verification requested (if required).

Child support

If they have any children from this relationship in their care, discuss initial action to take child support.

Advise them that, if required to do so, failure to take reasonable action to obtain child support within the 13 week (91 day) timeframe can reduce the rate of FTB Part A to base rate.

Refer customers who indicate that they and/or their children are at risk of family and domestic violence to a social worker to discuss eligibility for a maintenance exemption.

Children in their care + Read more ...

Does the customer still have children in their care?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No:
    • If a child has left their care, key child out of care to cancel payments for child see Child leaves customer's care/custody
    • Update relationship status on Marital Status (MS) screen from date of separation and update address if changed
    • Check if the payment was previously made to a joint account. Update the bank details if required, for more information see Changing payment destination
    • Procedure ends here

5

Partner is advising separation + Read more ...

In the partner's record:

If the partner indicates they have not changed address and are still living with the customer, do not update. Issue the Relationship Details - Separated under one roof (SS293) form.

Record the details of the conversation with the customer in a DOC, including details of forms issued. Key a manual review for the return of the form 14 days in the future. Procedure ends here.

If the address has changed, on the Marital Status (MS) screen start a new line and key Event Date: as separation date and key 'SEP' in Marital Status: field. Select Continue. Screen flow will automatically swap from the partner's record to the customer's record after the MS screen is updated.

From the customer's Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen, swap to the partner's record and update the partner's address and any other relevant details such as rent and telephone details.

Finalise activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Record the details on a DOC.

Child/ren in partner's care

If any of the children will be living with the partner after the separation, advise the partner that they can claim FTB/CCS for the child/ren. See Initial contact by customers claiming FTB.

Also consider if eligible for any other income support and other products and services.

Procedure ends here.

6

Customer has child/ren in care + Read more ...

If a child from this relationship is still in the customer's care:

  • Confirm if the care arrangement is going to change or remain the same
  • If a shared care arrangement now exists, an update to the Care Alignment (!CADDS) screen is required to advise of this. It may also be necessary to issue a Details of your shared care arrangements (FA012) form. If their shared care percentage has reduced, their new percentage should be recorded at first contact. See Assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
  • Advise them about initial action to obtain child support. Advise them if required to do so, failure to take reasonable maintenance action within the 13 week (91 day) timeframe may reduce the rate of FTB Part A to base rate. See Maintenance Action Test
  • Obtain details of any maintenance income. Record any of the following maintenance types if necessary:
    • Spousal maintenance. See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customer receives spousal maintenance
    • Private (voluntary) maintenance. See Recording private maintenance income on Centrelink systems
  • If the above information cannot be provided verbally Issue the Details of your maintenance/child support (FA003) form

If a child from a previous relationship is still in the customer’s care:

  • Check the maintenance grouping and maintenance action status on MNGS is correct for previous relationship children. Update if required, see How to group children on a customer's Family Tax Benefit (FTB) record
  • P24845 coding may require updating if the ex-partner was the step parent claiming FTB in relation to the child. See Notification and assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)

If a non-biological child is still in the customer’s care:

  • Check the maintenance grouping and maintenance action status on MNGS is correct. Update if required, see How to group children on a customer's Family Tax Benefit (FTB) record

If a step child is still in the customer’s care:

  • Confirm if the care arrangements are going to change or remain the same
  • From the date of separation, the child/ren are considered non-biological children of the customer, therefore MNGS must be updated to reflect the change. See How to group children on a customer's Family Tax Benefit (FTB) record
  • P24845 coding may be required or may require updating. See Notification and assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit

Check child care details for CCS current customer, for example, children attending care, service providers. If CCS only customer wishes to claim FTB, see Claiming Family Tax Benefit (FTB). If a child has left their care, key child out of care to cancel payments for child. See Child leaves customer's care/custody. Advise them that the new carer may be eligible to claim FTB/CCS for the child.

Do the customer’s responses indicate that they are possibly living as a member of a couple?

4

Process separation for customers not wishing to claim income support + Read more ...

Process separation and any other new details:

Navigate to the Marital Status (MS) screen:

  • enter ‘MS’ in the Next: field to access a new screen for updating
  • Event Date: field, record the date of separation
  • Marital Status: field, select separated marital status
  • PPE Cust and PPE Ptnr: field, update to ‘N’
  • Qualifier Code: field, leave blank
  • Source: field, select relevant source of information
  • Receipt Date: field, date the information was received
  • select Continue to navigate to the Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen
  • update any other changes, including Office Code (OC) screen, by selecting the appropriate screens from the CDTS screen. Note: select ‘S’ for any screen to be updated to ensure only the customer’s record will be updated

If the customer:

  • is updating their address details:
    • on the Address Details (AD) screen update the address. Staff must key ‘No’ in the Make partner the same: field to ensure the address update is not applied to the partner record
    • on the Accommodation (AC) screen: Key 'C' in Accommodation details for: field. An auto ULK code will display in Rent Verification: field and payment of Rent Assistance will stop. If there have been no changes to the rent details, update the Rent Verification: field with the same code that existed prior to ULK. RA can continue. In all other circumstances, re-verify their rent details
    • for more information, see Completing the Accommodation Details (AC) screen and assessing Rent Assistance (RA)
  • is not updating their address details:
    • on the Accommodation (AC) screen: Key ‘C’ in Accommodation details for: field. An auto ULK code will display in Rent Verification: field and payment of Rent Assistance will stop. If there have been no changes to rent details, update the Rent Verification: field with the same code that existed prior to ULK. RA can continue. In all other circumstances, re-verify their rent details
  • has payments made to an account which their former partner has access to, or a joint account, discuss changing their payment destination
  • is caring for a dependent child:
    • Application for child support assessment through Centrelink and/or
    • Record any voluntary private maintenance currently received, or
    • Issue the Details of your maintenance/child support (FA003) form if the information has not been provided
  • has their former partner is appointed as their nominee, ask if they wish to cancel the nominee arrangement
  • has changed their email address, see Adding, changing or removing an email address

Check the current income estimate is reasonable and follow up new estimate/payment choice/work/training/study test if needed. Do not key the partner's income as $0 due to the separation, as the partner's income will cease to be included from the date of separation.

For more information, see Updating income estimates for the current financial year. Ensure the correct date of event is used.

Finalise activity on Assessment Results (AR) screen. Create a Fast Note, select Auto Text. Use Generic > Update > Stated SEPARATED from Partner DOV: to record details of separation.

Confirm that the customer does not wish to claim income support.

Have they confirmed they do not wish to claim?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, they advise they do wish to claim - advise them to lodge a claim online. Go to Step 5

3

PDE to be applied to the record + Read more ...

From the December 2016 release, the 'PDE ' code became a manual code. PDE simulates a DMA period, and when coded on the Maintenance Action (MNMA) screen, results in DMA on the Maintenance Action (MNMR) screen. The PDE MAT code can only be entered by staff with relevant access.

Refer the case to the National ICT Referral Officers (NIRO) via normal escalation procedures to have the 'PDE' MAT code applied. This ensures relevant letters are issued to the customer advising of the requirement to take maintenance action.
