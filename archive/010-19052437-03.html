This page contains additional information to assist with completing the Claim for ABSTUDY (SY019).

Complex sections of the SY019

Item

Description

1

Identity confirmation + Read more ...

For information when Identify Confirmation is required, see Additional procedures exclusive to ABSTUDY apply in some circumstances.

2

Customer details + Read more ...

Australian Apprenticeship

If the customer is an Australian Apprentice, they must provide details of their Australian Apprenticeship. Services Australia will then confirm this information with the Department of Education, Skills and Employment.

Aboriginality

For ABSTUDY purposes, an Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander is someone who:

  • is of Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander descent
  • identifies themselves as an Australian Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander, and
  • is accepted as such by the community in which they live

The declaration on the claim will normally suffice, however for more information, see Aboriginality or Torres Strait Islander status for ABSTUDY purposes.

Born overseas

Customers must be an Australian citizen to qualify for ABSTUDY. Proof of citizenship is only required for customers:

  • born overseas, and
  • who have not provided this proof to Centrelink

See Residence requirements for ABSTUDY.

Name

Customers must provide all names they are currently known by, or have been known by in the past.

Address

The permanent family home address must be provided.

Optional questions

Customers who are under 22 years of age and have a disability preventing them from working up to 30 hours per week may be eligible for the Youth Disability Supplement (YDS).

3

Other government assistance + Read more ...

A customer is generally ineligible for ABSTUDY if they receive other government assistance to study or complete an Australian Apprenticeship. For more information, see Not qualified for ABSTUDY if receiving other assistance to study or as an Australian Apprentice.

Receiving a social security pension or benefit

Students studying a full-time course that is less than 12 months duration, may choose to remain on JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance or Austudy or transfer to ABSTUDY. For further information, see Identifying the most beneficial payment for students and Australian Apprentices.

4

Payment details + Read more ...

ABSTUDY payees

There are specific payee requirements for ABSTUDY payments. See How and to whom ABSTUDY payments are made.

Customers must provide bank account details that are held in their name.

Customers who have genuine barriers in providing bank account details can be granted a 28 day grace period. See Payment destination for more information.

5

Accommodation details + Read more ...

The customer's accommodation and living arrangements must be provided.

Rent Assistance may be payable depending on the customer's circumstances. For further information, see Qualification for payment of Rent Assistance (RA).

Residential costs option

Eligible students that live away from home and who are living in a hostel, or residential college attached to a university may choose to apply for the Residential Costs Option. For further information, see Payment of Residential Costs Option (RCO) for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES).

6

Income and assets details + Read more ...

Income

All assessable ABSTUDY customer income is taken into account for customers claiming ABSTUDY.

Australian Apprentices need to provide details of their employment as an Apprentice under Job 1. If they have a second job, details should be provided under Job 2.

Customers may subtract any maintenance expenditure from their estimated income.

The customer must complete either of the following forms if:

  • they have been self-employed or a subcontractor in the last 12 months - Business details form (Mod F)
  • they own any freehold land or buildings other than the home they live in - Real estate details form (Mod R)
  • in addition to providing statements for superannuation or rollover funds, is receiving a pension from this - Income and Assets form (Mod IA)

The Resources page contains links to these forms.

Assets

All assessable ABSTUDY customer assets are taken into account for customers claiming ABSTUDY.

Customers should not include their principle family home and any surrounding land of 2 hectares or less.

The current market value of the assets must be used to determine its value.

Additional income or assets

If the customer and/or partner earns any income from other sources or owns assets not covered by any questions in the form, an Income and Assets form (Mod IA) should be issued. The Resources page contains a link to this form.

7

Study details + Read more ...

The customer must provide details of their study. The customer can obtain this information from their education institution. Tertiary, Masters and Doctorate level students must also provide details of all non-school study undertaken in the last 10 years.

Secondary student unable to provide school details

A secondary boarding facility may need to conduct an interview or selection exercise prior to accepting a student. Therefore, it may not be possible at the time of claiming to confirm the school the customer will be attending. If this is the case, the claim can be accepted without study details. The customer must tell the agency of their study details as soon as possible.

8

Proof of Enrolment (POE) + Read more ...

Students may be required to provide POE. See Proof of enrolment in a course of study.

9

School Fees Allowance + Read more ...

Normally, School Fees Allowance is paid directly to the school listed in the claim. However, the applicant may choose to pay the school and claim the allowance after providing proof of payment to the school.

10

Scholarships + Read more ...

The customer must supply the details of any scholarships they receive (or expect to receive). For further information, see Assessing scholarship income.

Relocation Scholarship

Dependent students who have to live away from the family home for study, and independent students who are disadvantaged by personal circumstances, may qualify for a Relocation Scholarship.

To determine the rate of Relocation Scholarship, students will be required to provide their family home address. See Relocation Scholarship eligibility.

11

Living away from home + Read more ...

For ABSTUDY, living away from home means the student or apprentice is not living at the permanent family home to undertake their study or apprenticeship. This can include:

  • boarding in another person's home, boarding at a hostel or residential college
  • living alone or sharing a house, flat or caravan with other students or friends

A dependant customer must be living away from home as a result of 1 of the circumstances listed in this section to qualify for the away from home rate of ABSTUDY. The form advises appropriate statements, documents or letters required to support this claim.

Parents of students living in private board accommodation during term must complete all the Away from home details question set. If parents are unable to complete all the required questions, issue the ABSTUDY request for private board provider details form.

12

Independence + Read more ...

A customer may be considered independent as a result of 1 of the circumstances listed in this section.

The form advises appropriate statements, documents or letters required to support this claim. See Independence for ABSTUDY customers.

If the customer in under 18 years of age and is applying under the Unreasonable to Live at Home criteria, an appointment with an agency social worker will be required. See ABSTUDY unreasonable to live at home.

Where a customer does not meet the independent criteria, the parental income test must be applied regardless of receiving parental support or not.

13

Parents / guardians or partner income + Read more ...

If parents/guardians are unable to complete these questions or would prefer to provide their details separately, a MOD JY must be issued. The Resources page contains a link to this form.

Parental Income Test (PIT) exemption

The income test for the customer's parents/guardians is waived when a PIT exemption for ABSTUDY can be applied.

Customers who claim between September and December must supply information for both BASE and current tax years. See Completing Parent(s)/Guardian(s) details for the Base Tax Year and Current Tax Year (MOD JY).

Customer's partner income

Where the customer has a partner, the partner's income must be provided. If the partner information cannot be provided when the claim form is being completed, a Mod P must be issued. The Resources page contains a link to this form.

14

Dependent children / student or Australian Apprentice details + Read more ...

Dependents under 22 years of age

The customer who is applying for ABSTUDY should not be included in this question.

15

Tax File Number (TFN) + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice must make a TFN declaration.

Customers who do not have, or know their TFN, must be given a Tax file number application or enquiry for Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander (NAT1589) form for completion and lodgement within 14 days of lodging their claim.

See Tax File Number (TFN) for ABSTUDY for further information.

16

Permission to enquire + Read more ...

The customer may elect to allow another person or organisation to enquire on their behalf.

Customers may alter who they allow to have access to their record at any time.

Note: if the customer wishes to appoint a correspondence or payment nominee they must complete an Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf form (SS313). The Resources page contains a link to this form.

17

Statement / declaration + Read more ...

The customer and their partner, the customer's parent and/or their partner must sign the declaration in order for their claim to be accepted and determined.

Note: in certain circumstances, only one parent/guardian signature is necessary to assess the customer's entitlement.

Dependent students under 16 years of age are not required to sign the declaration. This is also the case for students turning 16 years of age who are already receiving the living away from home rate of ABSTUDY. There is no requirement to provide a signature (unless they have already) once they become the applicant at 16 years of age.
