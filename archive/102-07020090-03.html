Is the customer continuing studies at a part-time workload? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 3

3

Customer entitlement + Read more ...

The customer may still be entitled to receive Living Allowance or ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) payments if they qualify for a study load concession and are studying a sufficient workload to meet the requirements of that concession.

Consider if any of the following workload concessions apply:

  • Assessing the two-thirds study load concession
  • Assessing the disability study load concession
  • Assessing the pensioner study load concession for ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)

Does the customer meet the requirements to continue receiving benefits under one of these study load concessions?

  • Yes, follow the procedures in the appropriate study load concession file above
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Part-time study + Read more ...

If the ABSTUDY customer is studying part-time, but unable to qualify for a study load concession, their eligibility for the ABSTUDY Part-time Award should be considered.

  • If entitled to the Part-time Award, the Education Course Details (EDC) screen will need to be updated, ensuring the Participation Status field is coded as 'PTS' (part-time studies). See Changing study load from full-time to part-time or concessional study load for Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy or PES. Note: the record will remain ABY/CUR, but only Incidentals Allowance is payable
  • The ABSTUDY Part-time Award pays only a limited range of entitlements, and does not pay Living Allowance or ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) payments. Therefore, the customer's entitlement to other income support payments or benefits should also be investigated

Procedure ends here.

5

Period of incapacity + Read more ...

The appropriate procedure to follow will depend upon whether, because of their incapacity, the customer intends to only be temporarily absent, or is discontinuing studies.

  • If the customer intends to only be temporarily absent from studies, go to Step 6
  • If the customer is discontinuing studies, see Step 7 in Table 2

6

Temporary absence from study + Read more ...

The appropriate procedure to follow for customers who will be temporarily absent from studies because of incapacity depends upon the level of study.

  • For secondary school students, go to Step 7
  • For tertiary or secondary non-school students, see Step 3 in Table 2

7

Secondary school absence + Read more ...

Advise the student that an absence from secondary school is an approved absence (and therefore has no effect on payment) if the absence is due to circumstances beyond the control of the student.

Action

1

Evidence of circumstances + Read more ...

Advise the student they must provide evidence of their approved absence.

Evidence required is:

  • a signed statement from the secondary school explaining the extended absence is approved

Note: an indication by the school in an attendance check that an absence was due to illness or other circumstances beyond the control of the student may generally be accepted as sufficient evidence of an approved absence.

2

Has the student provided appropriate evidence of their circumstances? + Read more ...

  • Yes, complete the following:
    • Advise the student the absence is approved. There is no effect on payment, unless they discontinue study
    • Record details of the student's medical condition on the Medical Conditions (MC) screen. This will need to be updated manually, see the How to access, view and update information table of Disability cluster screens. This allows the system to generate Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA) payments if appropriate
    • Details of the approved absence (including details of the length of the absence and of the evidence supplied) should be recorded on a DOC
    • Advise the customer to notify within 14 days if their circumstances change
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, advise the student the absence is not approved. See Determining Activity Test breaches for unapproved absences for ABSTUDY secondary school students

Procedure ends here.

3

Tertiary or secondary non-schooling student + Read more ...

Advise the tertiary or secondary non-school student that they can remain eligible for payment for up to 8 weeks as long as they remain enrolled by the education institution and provide medical evidence.

Services Australia must be provided with a medical certificate which:

  • states the nature of the condition leading to absence and confirms it prevents the student from studying their full-time or concessional study load study, and
  • confirms the condition is temporary and states the date on which the period of incapacity started and the date the student is expected to be able to resume their full-time or concessional study load study

Has the student remained enrolled and provided the appropriate medical evidence?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, advise the student the absence cannot be approved. The customer is considered to have discontinued study from the date they last studied as a full-time student. Go to Step 7

4

Continuing enrolment and has appropriate medical evidence + Read more ...

  • Advise the student the absence is approved. There is no effect on payment, unless the student discontinues study
  • Code the medical certificate by using the task selector in Process Direct or Medical Certificate guided procedure in Customer First. Complete all relevant fields within the workflow including the following if required:
    • Event Date - use the Unfit to work/study from date recorded on the medical certificate. If the date is in the future, record the Event Date as the date the medical certificate is being recorded. Note: this is not the Date of Receipt unless the date of receipt is today
    • Provider number - Treating Health Professional's Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (Ahpra) or leave blank if not provided
    • Unfit to work/study from - the from date on the new medical certificate
    • Unfit to work/study to - the to date on the medical certificate
    • Date of Receipt (DOR) - the date the medical certificate was initially received/scanned. Note: this field auto populates if the task selector in Process Direct is used. It cannot be changed, and is to be disregarded when QMA/QOL is undertaken
  • Work items assessed and completed in:
    • Process Direct, will automatically be closed
    • Customer First, does not automatically close and the status of the Change of Circumstance activity requires manual completion, see Interaction Records
    • If the task selector is started in Process Direct but not completed, the work item will remain open. Check the work item and update as required, see Interaction Records
  • Details of the approved absence (including details of the length of the absence and of the evidence supplied) should be recorded on a DOC
  • Advise the student to notify within 14 days if their circumstances change
  • Set a Manual Follow-up (MFU) on the Review Registration (RVR) screen for the date on which the student is expected to resume study. The customer should be contacted to determine if they have resumed study

5

Does the student require an extension of their period of incapacity beyond the initial 8 weeks? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, no further action is required. Details of contact with the customer should be recorded on a DOC. Procedure ends here

6

Resumption of study + Read more ...

Advise the student they will be considered to have discontinued study at the end of the period as specified in the medical certificate unless they resume study or receive an extension as detailed below.

Only 1 period of extension of entitlement beyond the first absence may be granted if:

  • the student continues to remain enrolled in the course
  • a further medical certificate is provided containing the same information required for their first period of absence, and
  • a statement is received from the institution's Academic Registrar, or equivalent officer, stating that, at the end of the expected period of incapacity, the student is allowed to resume their full-time or concessional study load study in the course

Students are considered to have stopped study on the last day of the period specified in the second medical certificate if they do not resume full-time or concessional study load study on the following day.

If an incapacity results in an approved absence that extends into an end of year vacation period, and the student intends to continue with full-time or concessional study education in the following year, Living Allowance can continue to be paid until the day before commencement of study in the following year. Subsequent year entitlement will be assessed on the student's circumstances in that year.

Has an extension to the period of approved absence been given?

  • Yes, complete the following:
    • Advise the student this absence is approved, but no further extensions can be approved. There is no effect on payment, unless the student discontinues study
    • Record details of the approved absence (including details of the length of the absence and of the evidence supplied) on a DOC
    • Record details of the student's medical condition on the Medical Conditions (MC) screen. This will need to be updated manually, see the How to access, view and update information table of Disability cluster screens. This allows the system to generate Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA) payments if appropriate
    • Advise the student to notify within 14 days if their circumstances change
    • Set a Manual Follow-up (MFU) on the Review Registration (RVR) screen for the date the student is expected to resume study. The customer should be contacted to determine if they have resumed study
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, the customer is considered to have discontinued studies at the end of the period specified in the first medical certificate. Go to Step 6

7

The student is considered to have discontinued studies + Read more ...

Entitlement to ABSTUDY or ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) ceases from the date they were last considered to be a full-time student.

Complete the following:

  • Advise the customer they are no longer entitled to ABSTUDY/ABSTUDY PES
  • Update the Education Course Details (EDC) screen to reflect the change in study details. If the EDC screen reflects the customer is no longer a student, the system will automatically cancel ABSTUDY payments
  • If necessary, manually cancel ABSTUDY
  • Consider if the customer may be eligible for other income support payments or benefits
  • Record any decision and/or discussion with the student on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

Assessing incapacitated Australian Apprentices

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

An ABSTUDY Australian Apprentice customer who is temporarily incapacitated and unable to continue their apprenticeship is eligible to receive ABSTUDY as long as their Commonwealth Registration Number remains current.

If the Commonwealth Registration Number is suspended or cancelled, there is no entitlement to ABSTUDY as an Australian Apprentice. The status of the registration number can be checked on the Apprenticeship Details (NAPD) screen.

Is there continuing entitlement?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

If there is continuing entitlement + Read more ...

Services Australia must be provided with an original medical certificate (that should be copied then returned to the customer as they may be required to provide a copy to their employer) which:

  • states the nature of the condition leading to absence and confirms it prevents the customer from undertaking their full-time Australian Apprenticeship, and
  • confirms the condition is temporary and the date the period of incapacity started and the date the customer is expected to be able to resume their full-time Australian Apprenticeship. If the incapacity is permanent then investigate if the customer may be eligible for another income support payment, e.g. JobSeeker Payment or Disability Support Pension
  • Code the medical certificate by using the task selector in Process Direct or Medical Certificate guided procedure in Customer First. Complete all relevant fields within the workflow including the following if required:
    • Event Date - use the Unfit to work/study from date recorded on the medical certificate. If the date is in the future, record the Event Date as the date the medical certificate is being recorded. Note: this is not the Date of Receipt unless the date of receipt is today
    • Provider number - Treating Health Professional's Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (Ahpra) or leave blank if not provided
    • Unfit to work/study from - the from date on the new medical certificate
    • Unfit to work/study to - the to date on the medical certificate
    • Date of Receipt (DOR) - the date the medical certificate was initially received/scanned. Note: this field auto populates if the task selector in Process Direct is used. It cannot be changed, and is to be disregarded when QMA/QOL is undertaken
  • Work items assessed and completed in:
    • Process Direct, will automatically be closed
    • Customer First, does not automatically close and the status of the Change of Circumstance activity requires manual completion, see Interaction Records
    • If the task selector is started in Process Direct but not completed, the work item will remain open. Check the work item and update as required, see Interaction Records
  • Update customer's employment income if required, e.g. if no longer being paid wages for period of incapacity
  • If the customer is still being paid income while incapacitated, they must continue to report their employment income via self service
  • Advise the customer to notify immediately if their Commonwealth Registration Number is suspended or cancelled to avoid an overpayment
  • Record details on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

3

If there is no entitlement + Read more ...

  • Cancel ABSTUDY
  • Check the Apprenticeship End Date field on the NAPD screen as the Department of Social Services (DSS) database should automatically transfer information to indicate the date the customer is no longer an Australian Apprentice (the date the Commonwealth Registration Number was cancelled or suspended). Investigate if the customer may be eligible for another Services Australia payment. Also see:
  • For a temporary incapacity:
    • Customers claiming JobSeeker Payment (JSP/Youth Allowance (YA) (Incapacitated) or
    • Youth Allowance (YA) student or Australian Apprentice becomes incapacitated
  • For a long term incapacity - Eligibility for Disability Support Pension (DSP)

Record details on a DOC.
