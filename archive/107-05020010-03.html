1

Debt recovery script + Read more ...

Use the Debt recovery script or the Debt Repayment guided procedure (for Smart Centre and service centre staff) to action a debt.

Use a Fast Note to create:

  • a DOC
  • Callback, or
  • ACTDOC

Only follow the manual process below if the script, guided procedure or Fast Note are unavailable.

2

Negotiate a repayment arrangement + Read more ...

Discuss repayment by cash with the customer as a first option:

  • Agree to an instalment rate
  • Otherwise, discuss the customer's current financial situation
  • Repayment of the debt should be within 6 months, at or above the acceptable cash level

Third party withholdings are:

  • only available if the customer asks for them
  • not the preferred method of withholdings
  • not to be offered by staff as a form of withholdings. This may compromise the other parties privacy

Note: the system automatically raises family assistance reconciliation and/or non-lodger debts. These debts cannot be manually adjusted.

Has the customer requested third party withholdings?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, procedure ends here

3

Third party withholdings + Read more ...

Third party withholdings can be set for customers with:

  • multiple debts
  • outstanding debts for family assistance reconciliation, and/or non-lodger debts

If the third party agrees to withholdings for multiple debts, record the Debt IDs:

  • in the Comments field on the Withholding Instructions (OPWH) screen, and
  • on the summary line of the DOC

Third party withholdings can only be coded for one Debt ID at a time.

Has the customer indicated the third party has given their authority and agreed to a rate for the third party withholdings?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, see Arranging cash repayments to recover debts. Procedure ends here

4

Third party confirmation and authority + Read more ...

Contact the third party. Confirm their identity before discussing the debt.

Discuss the amount they have agreed to be withheld from their payment.

Third party withholdings can only be applied at point of contact, if the third party provides verbal authority.

Third party authority

  • Staff can accept verbal authority from the third party. It must be clearly documented.
  • If the third party does not want to provide verbal confirmation:
    • send a third party withholding authority (Q589) letter
    • do not wait for the return of the Q589 - negotiate an alternative cash repayment arrangement with the customer
    • if the signed authority is returned (signed by the third party), third party withholdings can be revisited

If the third party:

  • agrees (verbal authority provided) to have the instalment rate for the total amount owing on the customer's debt withheld from their payment, go to Step 6
  • agrees (verbal authority provided) to a lesser withholding amount being deducted from their payment, go to Step 5
  • has not given / provided authority (verbal or written) for any amount to be withheld from their payment, negotiate a cash arrangement with the customer to repay the debt. Procedure ends here.

5

Third party has agreed to a lesser withholding + Read more ...

Accept the amount the third party has agreed to have deducted from their payments.

When the third party does not agree to pay the instalment rate, the customer must pay the balance.

Speak to the customer and ask which payment method the customer would prefer. A cash instalment by:

  • payment card
  • direct debit from their financial institution account
  • cheque or money order

Tell the customer that if they do not accept any of the above, Services Australia will look at other methods of recovery.

Note: For more information on ceasing a payment withholding, see Coding and recording an arrangement table in Procedures for arranging withholdings to recover debts.

6

Coding third party withholdings + Read more ...

  • To code third party withholdings, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • If there are no third party withholdings to be coded, see Step 4 in Table 2
  • If the difference between the third party withholding amount and the instalment rate is to be coded, see Step 4 in Table 2

Coding third party withholdings for debt recovery

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Code the third party withholdings + Read more ...

Third party withholdings are coded on the customer's record.

  • Details required for coding the third party withholding are the:
    • Customer Reference Number (CRN) of the third party
    • payment type of the third party
    • Debt ID of the customer's debt
  • On the Activity List (AL) screen, 'S'elect the DMN/RCV No Arrangement activity and press [Enter]
    • Note: if there is no DMN/RCV activity, type 'OPAL' in the Nxt: field and press [Enter]
  • In the Create new arrangement type: field, type 'WHS' and press [Enter]

Is the third party withholdings for more than one Debt ID?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Multiple debt IDs + Read more ...

Apply the third party withholdings to the debt ID to be repaid first, according to the repayment hierarchy.

To view the order in which repayments will be applied:

  • go to the Debt List (OPDL) screen
  • key 'ILY' in the Select View: field

This will show a hierarchy of the debts. There will be:

  • a number on the far right side of each debt, and
  • the order in which the debts will be repaid
  • On the OPWH screen, complete the following fields:
    • Withholding Type: third party payment type
    • Third Party Withholding CRN:
    • Withholding ID:
    • Non Standard Amount $: (if necessary)
    • Comments: record all relevant Debt IDs in this field, as third party withholdings can only be coded for one Debt ID at a time
  • A letter is issued to tell the third party of the withholding amount.
  • Go to the Assessment Finalisation (OPAF) screen:
    • Type 'Y' in the Finished?: field. Press [Enter]
    • Record all details on a DOC including reference to the Act and Debt IDs
    • Code a manual review on the Review Registration (RVR) screen:
      - set the review for the date the debt ID is to be repaid
      - include details of all Debt IDs the third party agreed to repay
    • If a future instalment rate review was discussed, code a manual review using the Review Registration (RVR) screen

3

Single Debt ID + Read more ...

  • On the OPWH screen, complete the following fields:
    • Withholding Type: third party payment type
    • Third Party Withholding CRN:
    • Withholding ID:
    • Non Standard Amount $: field (if necessary)
    • Comments: ensure all relevant Debt IDs are recorded in this field
  • A letter is issued to tell the third party of the withholding amount
  • Go to the Assessment Finalisation (OPAF) screen:
    • Type 'Y' in the Finished?: field. Press [Enter]
  • Record all details on a DOC including the appropriate Act reference and debt ID
  • If a future instalment rate review was discussed, code a manual review using the Review Registration (RVR) screen

4

Code the customer's repayment arrangement + Read more ...

Ask the customer which payment method they would prefer:

  • Cash instalments by payment card, cheque or money order - see Arranging cash repayments to recover debts
  • Cash instalments by direct debit from their financial institution - see Voluntary deduction (direct debit) arrangements to recover debts
