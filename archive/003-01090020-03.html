This page provides information on the role of the Team Leader in the disaster recovery centres created to provide information or services to meet the short, medium and longer-term assistance needs of people affected by a disaster.

Team Leader responsibilities

This table contains information on the role of Team Leader during an emergency.

Item

Description

1

Workplace Health aspects + Read more ...

Team Leaders must have access to:

  • debriefing - either regular (e.g. daily or at the end of the shift) or when requested
  • the Employee Assistance Program (EAP). The Resources page contains a link to information on EAP
  • regular breaks (that is, Staff must have a meal break of at least 30 minutes after five hours of continuous duty)
  • at least eight consecutive hours off duty plus reasonable travelling time between the time they cease duty and the time they next commence duty

The Resources page contains a link to the Safety Reference Centre for further information.

2

Liaison Point + Read more ...

The Team Leader is the liaison point between Services Australia and other agencies at the Evacuation/Recovery Centre.

Their responsibilities as a liaison point include:

  • to be the contact point for Evacuation/Recovery Centre Coordinators, Zone Office enquiries and other agencies within the Evacuation Centre
  • provide daily updates on the level of assistance provided and an overview of the types of assistance being sought
  • follow-up with Zone Office issues/items raised by staff as required
  • advise Zone Office of:
    • any home visits that may be required
    • hours worked (especially overtime) for ESSentials processing
    • any shifts that need backup staff due to frontline absences or if there is a greater need for assistance than anticipated
  • refer to Zone Office any enquiries from the media, Local Members or Federal Government Ministers
  • attend Evacuation Centre meetings to:
    • discuss issues
    • assist in identifying those customer groups/communities that have not yet contacted/accessed the Evacuation/Recovery Centre
  • contribute to discussions on the decision to 'pull out' of the Evacuation/Recovery Centre

3

Manage staff and information + Read more ...

The Team Leader is the manager of staff at the site and must ensure all relevant information is made available to staff.

Their responsibilities in this area include:

  • disseminate information to on-site staff
  • conduct team briefings at the start/end of a shift
  • ensure staff who are commencing their shift have been made aware of the current issues and have access to the information point
  • ensure staff understand how to deal with paper claims and Proof of Identity (POI) and the need to fax or scan them (using whatever method is available at the site)
  • monitor staff for Distress Symptoms
  • prioritise the work for those on site
  • ensure staff are aware of all the agencies/services available at the centre
  • ensure staff observe sensitivities, for example, noise / jokes

4

Monitor Workplace Health issues + Read more ...

The Team Leader must ensure that correct Workplace Health) practices are followed.

Their responsibilities in this area include:

  • Ensure staff sign in and out at the start and end of each shift
  • Deal with and promptly refer / resolve any issues raised by staff
  • Identify and report existing and potential hazards in the work area
  • Regularly provide accurate information about identified hazards
  • Reporting incidents where a staff member incurs an injury or disease as a result of Services Australia operations, regardless of whether time off work is or is not required. This includes during travel to or from the workplace and during meal breaks

5

Provide handover + Read more ...

The Team Leader must provide a handover to the next Team Leader / senior staff member at the end of their shift including providing details about:

  • Centre Information
    • Personally introduce staff to Centre Coordinators
    • Advise on the facilities available (Tea Room, Interview Room, Car Parking etc.)
    • Provide timetables for meetings/phone hookups that they are required to attend
    • Advise of location of First Aid Officer at centre.
  • Staff
    • Personally introduce to staff on site
    • Details of roster - who is working what hours / who is due in next
    • Discuss structure (who is doing what)
    • Discuss operation (what to do for unplanned absence or higher demand than expected)
    • Current Workplace Health issues.
  • Workload
    • Current policy/procedures
    • When are the busiest periods
    • Plans to address this workload
    • Arrangements for staff debriefing
    • Overview of what information is provided to staff on a daily basis
    • How to handle information dissemination
  • Zone Contacts
    • The name and role of the key individuals they need to contact within their area and how regularly they should be contacted
