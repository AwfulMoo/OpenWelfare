Administrative requirements for NWC assessments

Table 1: For Centrelink International Services (CIS) staff only.

Step

Action

1

Customer contacts + Read more ...

A customer contacts to test their eligibility under the NWC provisions. If you do not have the customer on the phone you will need to contact them after the following eligibility checks.

Note: in all cases, the Portability Script - Departures and Returns must be used.

To receive indefinite portability under the NWC provisions, a customer must have ‘No future work capacity'. If they are working and reporting employment income it is possible that they will not meet the NWC provisions. They may also not be entitled to DSP. See Commencing or returning to work or self-employment Disability Support Pension (DSP).

If the customer is legally blind, earnings is not a factor. See Income and assets tests for blind customers.

Complete checks before issuing forms, go to Step 2.

2

Check if the customer is a former resident + Read more ...

A former resident has no portability for 24 months from the date of returning to reside in Australia. A NWC assessment will not override this. The customer can undergo a NWC assessment while they are still a former resident, however any NWC decision would only apply for departures after the former resident provision has expired.

On the Residence Savings (RSS) screen, check the Assessed Former Resident Ind field.

Is the customer a former resident?

  • Yes, and are:
    • leaving within 3 months of former residence ending, go to Step 3
    • going to an agreement country that covers DSP, go to Step 6
    • not going to an agreement country or leaving within 3 months of former residence ending. Customer is not payable overseas, finalise script and annotate the portability DOC with the reason the customer is not payable. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Check if customer has already met NWC provisions + Read more ...

Customers who have already had a portability assessment and met the provisions of indefinite portability under the NWC rules do not need a new assessment. The assessment is valid for all future travel while DSP is current.

Check the following screens:

  • Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen > Portability Reason field. If NWC:
    • is coded, on the Additional Residence Details (ARD) screen > Proportional Portability Savings field, key ‘Y’ if payable indefinitely and needing proportional savings
    • not coded, go to Step 4
  • Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen, check where the Continuing Inability To Work (CITW) occurred as this affects rate of payment. If CITW occurred:
    • in Australia, proportional savings apply. Remove Australian Working Life Residence (AWLR) from the ARD screen
    • outside Australia, check PDI screen and update ARD screen with current AWLR from date of departure from Australia
    • refer to the CIS PORT Medical Decision DOC, portability interview DOC and MC/PDI coding for more confirmation
  • finalise portability script, including:
    • issuing script generated XOB101 letter, and
    • DOC outcome
  • procedure ends here

4

Check if the customer has left Australia + Read more ...

Generally, customers must be in Australia to apply for indefinite portability under NWC provisions (and undergo a portability assessment). Customers who are overseas must return to Australia to apply.

Customers who leave Australia after completing the medical assessment and interview may contact about the assessment outcome, check Document List (DL) to provide the customer with the status/outcome.

If the customer is in or travelling to an agreement country that covers DSP, they may be able to have a severely disabled assessment depending on sufficient medical evidence being available.

Note: if the customer has not undergone a medical assessment in the past 2 years, issue an AUS109 form.

Complete the following checks:

  • Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen if departed from Australia
  • DL for severely disabled/NWC documents

Is the customer in Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Check if the customer is manifestly medically eligible for DSP + Read more ...

If the customer has been granted DSP by meeting one of the current manifest eligibility conditions, they do not need to undergo an NWC assessment as they automatically meet the requirements. There must be a valid code on the Medical Conditions (MC) screen.

If the customer has one of the following codes in the Manifest Code field on the MC screen, a JCA is not required. Run the Portability Script - Departures and Returns, code RSCD with ‘NWC’, code ARD with proportional savings if needed:

  • TRM
  • BLI
  • INT
  • NHM
  • HV4
  • DVA/TPI (Disability Compensation Payment at Special Rate (Totally and Permanently Incapacitated (TPI))

Procedure ends here.

Note: if the Manifest Code field displays the discontinued manifest code MNF, a NWC assessment is required. Go to Step 7.

If MNF is recorded for a customer who is DSP/DSB (Blind), a NWC assessment is not necessary, however for indefinite portability a SA013 form is required. Send a SA013 form to be completed and returned for assessment. Procedure ends here.

If customer does not have a manifest code on MC screen, go to Step 7.

6

Check if the customer is in an agreement country that covers DSP + Read more ...

If the customer has departed and travelled to an agreement country that covers DSP, a severely disabled assessment may take place under agreement provisions.

Contact the customer before the severely disabled assessment and referral for a JCA (medical review of entitlement) file assessment.

Run the Portability Script - Departures and Returns and to the question ‘Would you like us to carry out a new assessment of your medical condition?’, select ‘Yes’ to create an MFU that will flow to the IDOs to action. Procedure ends here.

See Disability Support Pension (DSP) severely disabled assessments for International Agreements.

7

Confirm customer wants to proceed with NWC assessment + Read more ...

Ask the customer the following question:

  • ‘By agreeing to do this review, you are agreeing to a reassessment of your DSP under the current impairment tables which were introduced in 2012. There is a possibility that your payment may be cancelled as a result of this reassessment of DSP. Do you still wish to proceed with the reassessment?’

Tell the customer:

  • if they do not return the forms, the assessment cannot proceed
  • they must have the JCA before they leave Australia

If customer confirms they want to proceed with NWC assessment:

  • run the Portability Script - Departures and Returns
  • to the question, ‘Would you like us to carry out a new assessment of your medical condition?’, select ‘Yes’

IDO Admin specialist officer?

  • Yes, see Table 2
  • No, an MFU will generate and allocate to IDO Admin skilled staff

Procedure ends here.

Issue forms for NWC assessment

Table 2: For International Disability Officers (IDO) Admin specialist staff only.

Step

Action

1

Issue NWC forms and letter + Read more ...

Create the relevant Q999 letter for the customer via the Letter Details (LD) screen.

Include AUS142 and AUS222 forms and 2 reply paid envelopes.

Note: bilingual forms are available for AUS142 and AUS222.

Add customer information to forms and send with the Q999 letter.

Complete any open work items relating to NWC.

Go to Step 2.

2

Requesting eMIFE + Read more ...

If the customer was granted DSP before 2010, electronic Medical Information File Envelope (eMIFE) must be requested and scanned to the customer’s record. This is needed for a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) to be completed.

Check Document Tools to see if medical information is already scanned.

Is the medical information scanned to the customer’s record?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No:
    • request eMIFE using mySupport
    • record the SD number (to be included in the started DOC in Step 3)
    • go to Step 3

3

Document the NWC progress in Customer Record + Read more ...

In Customer Record, run the International Doc Creation Script > NWC Progress DOC > Option 1 and answer all relevant information > Complete.

Annotate DOC with SD number and complete any open work items relating to NWC.

No further action required until forms are returned.

Procedure ends here.

Returned forms for NWC assessment

Table 3: For IDO Admin specialist staff only.

Step

Action

1

NWC forms have been returned + Read more ...

Check AUS142 and AUS222 for completeness.

Note: for AUS222, Section B must be signed, stamped and dated by a treating medical professional.

Ensure all medical and other relevant information has been scanned and added to the electronic MIFE (eMIFE).

To view eMIFE information, select Documents icon. If documents are scanned incorrectly and need to be separated or reclassified, complete the following in Customer First:

  • in Quick Links, select Document Tools
  • see Step 7 in Table 1 of Scanning Centrelink medical/sensitive documents using an MFD to scan documents correctly

Are forms fully complete?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the customer has completed and returned only one form for:
    • AUS222. If Section A and B is complete, the assessment can proceed. Although an AUS142 is preferable, it is not essential for assessment. Go to Step 2
    • AUS142. The NWC assessment cannot continue until the AUS222 form has been received. DOC the record with this information. Complete all open work items within skill set. Procedure ends here until AUS222 is received

2

Check forms for completeness + Read more ...

Check the AUS222 form > Section B > Questions 2 to 4 about the customer’s current medical conditions.

Do the forms indicate the customer is manifestly medically eligible?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Forms indicate customer is manifestly medically eligible + Read more ...

If the scanned forms have UNS006 classification, change to AUS222 or UNS008.

In Document Tools, the eMIFE indicator check box must be ticked for Job Capacity Assessors to see the scan. See ‘Update’ option of the scan.

In Customer Record, rerun the NWC Progress DOC and select the option that customer has a manifest condition.

Annotate the NWC Progress DOC to state:

  • forms have been returned
  • forms have been checked for completeness, and
  • Document Tools has been checked and scans show correct classification

Leave the NWC Progress DOC open. Complete all other open work items within skill set and close all scanned images that relate to the NWC assessment. The NWC Progress DOC will allocate to the IDOs for manifest assessment.

Procedure ends here.

4

Forms indicate customer is not manifestly medically eligible + Read more ...

Check recent travel on Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) and refresh Immigration Datalink on Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) if required.

Is the customer in Australia?

  • Yes:
    • in Customer Record, rerun the International Doc Creation Script > NWC Progress DOC
    • select the forms that have been returned. Ensure the forms do not indicate that medical conditions are manifest > Complete
    • at the end of the script, when prompted to refer the customer for a JCA, select ‘Yes’
    • place the open NWC Progress DOC on hold for 6 weeks
    • complete all open work items within skill set and close all scanned images that relate to the NWC assessment
    • go to Step 5
  • No:
    • the customer cannot be referred for a JCA and cannot continue the NWC assessment at this time
    • issue an appropriate Q999 letter to the customer
    • annotate the NWC Progress DOC stating the customer is not in Australia and that JCA appointment will not be booked until the customer returns to Australia and contacts to advise they would like to continue with the NWC assessment. Close the current NWC Progress DOC. A new NWC Progress DOC will be run if the customer returns to Australia and requests to undergo the NWC assessment process
    • complete all open work items within skill set and close all scanned images that relate to the NWC assessment
    • procedure ends here

5

Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) referral is required + Read more ...

Requests for a JCA must be made using the Streaming and Progress DOC Script in Customer Record. In the following fields:

  • Work team, key Internationals
  • Leave User details, select Next
  • Date of Claim, key today’s date
  • select the relevant priority number:
    • Priority 1, urgent if customer is travelling within one week. Send an email to Assessment Services asking to book the JCA urgently as customer is travelling within a week. Include date of travel in the email
    • Priority 3 for all other non-urgent cases
  • Reason for Referral:
    • Portability Specific Medical Assessment for DSP granted after 1 July 2006
    • Portability Specific Medical Assessment Referral for DSP granted prior to 1 July 2006
  • check the tick box Customer has been assessed as payable under Income and Asset provisions
  • for the question ‘Has the customer resided in Australia for 10 years or do they have a Qualifying Residence Exemption (QRE)?’, select Yes
  • in the Special Needs field:
    • copy and paste the following text:

Please advise where the inability to work occurred as this may affect rate of pension, for portability purposes
Face to face interview is required
Medical code: Physical or Non–Physical
Interpreter required for this interview [Yes/No] [If yes, specify language]

  • include any other information that will assist Assessment Services to book the appointment (such as access issues or anything else that would affect the customer's ability to attend)
  • finalise script. This must be an open work item for allocation purposes
  • remove keyword JAPPT3 and add JAPPT2
  • assign a priority code of portability (immediate departures) or portability (not immediate departures) to assist Assessment Services with streaming referrals
  • procedure ends here

A DOC will be created requesting Assessment Services arrange a JCA. Assessment Services are also responsible for rescheduling all JCA appointments.

Note: if the customer has left Australia after the referral was made, Assessment Services Triage will advise CIS by Fast Note that no JCA appointment will be made. Issue a Q999 letter to the customer. Annotate the NWC Progress DOC stating that the customer is not in Australia and that JCA appointment will not be booked until customer returns to Australia and contacts to advise they would like to continue with the NWC assessment. Close the current NWC Progress DOC. A new NWC Progress DOC will be run if the customer returns to Australia and requests to undergo the NWC assessment process.

Check report and finalise portability decision

Table 4: For IDO Admin specialist staff only.

Step

Action

1

View the JCA report + Read more ...

When a report is received via Workload Manager, select the relevant ESAt/JCA report.

Alternatively, in Customer First on the customer’s home page:

  • select Referrals menu > Employment Services or Job Capacity Assessment referral information to access the JCA report
  • select Show Report to view and enable Accept or Return options

Print the JCA report before accepting.

The Report Complete screen can also be accessed by selecting the started NSS/JCA activity on the Activity List (AL) screen.

Note: do not select and complete the activity via the Assessment Results (AR) screen to accept or return the report. If this is done, the report cannot be accepted or returned on the Report Complete screen. This will leave the report with a submitted status preventing any new ESAt/JCA referrals from being recorded.

If customer does not attend the JCA appointment:

  • the assessor will finalise as Unable to Complete (UTC)
  • no further action will be taken until the customer contacts and requests a new appointment
  • CIS IDO Admin staff will:
    • annotate the NWC Progress DOC as ‘NWC assessment has not been conducted. Customer is subject to general portability provisions’. In Customer First, notes from the Job Capacity Assessor in the Online Diary will explain why the report could not be completed. Add these notes to the annotation
    • close the NWC Progress DOC
    • select report on AL screen, go to AR to close the referral system
    • procedure ends here

CIS IDOs can only accept JCA reports for referral reasons DSPMR and DSPME.

2

Check that the report is complete and consistent + Read more ...

Check the report to ensure it has been completed with sufficient information to determine income support eligibility and/or portability specific criteria have been assessed correctly. Action the JCA report appropriately.

Note: the Continuing Inability To Work (CITW)/Blind residence information is included in the JCA report but is not visible to Centrelink. When a report is accepted, it will automatically populate the Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen.

3

CIS IDO to action the JCA report + Read more ...

Check the JCA report and determine whether to:

  • accept, or
  • return (reject)

Is the report being returned?

  • Yes, see Step 4 in Inconsistencies in a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) report
  • No, go to Step 4

4

CIS IDO accepts JCA report + Read more ...

The IDO will phone the customer with the outcome of the NWC assessment. If the customer has a departure date, the IDO will run the Portability Script - Departures and Returns to issue an XOB101 letter.

Check the following screens:

  • Medical Conditions Details (MC) screen. Ensure the date in the Event Date field matches the date of assessment for the JCA report
  • Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen. Ensure the date in both the Event Date fields for the Portability Specific Medical Outcome and CITW/Blind Residence fields, match the date of assessment for the JCA report

If the customer is assessed as meeting NWC criteria and has left Australia after the JCA assessment and before the acceptance of the report, correct the dates on MC and PDI screens to the date immediately before the departure. Otherwise, the NWC assessment will not take effect.

5

No Future Work Capacity + Read more ...

The DSP Portability section of the JCA report is only created when:

  • the customer is assessed as having a 'severe impairment', and
  • FDTS work capacity is in the 0-7 hours per week bandwidth

Is the customer assessed as meeting the eligibility criteria for indefinite portability (a severe impairment and nil work capacity)?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 6

6

DSP qualification criteria + Read more ...

Does the customer meet the qualification criteria for DSP after assessment?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, see Cancellation of Disability Support Pension (DSP). Procedure ends here

7

Ineligible for indefinite portability + Read more ...

Customer is not eligible for indefinite portability under the ‘No future work capacity' portability provisions.

However, if they are going to an Agreement country that covers DSP and are assessed as severely disabled, they may be eligible for long term payment.

Is the customer travelling to an agreement country that covers DSP?

  • Yes, see Disability Support Pension (DSP) severely disabled assessments for International Agreements. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Eligible for indefinite portability + Read more ...

The customer is assessed as eligible for indefinite portability under the ‘No future work capacity' portability provisions.

In the Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen, or if the customer has already departed Australia, the Travel Outside Australia Details (TOAD) screen:

  • record the date of assessment in the Event Date field
  • record 'NWC' in the Portability Reason field
  • go to the Additional Residence Details (ARD) screen

If the Continuing Inability to Work (CITW) or permanent blindness occurred while the customer:

  • was an Australian resident:
    • in the Date of Event field, key the date of assessment from the JCA report in the top block, and
    • in the Proportional Portability Savings field, key 'Y'
  • was not an Australian resident:
    • in the Date of Event field, key the date of assessment from the JCA report, and
    • in the Proportional Portability Savings field, key 'N'

If the customer is assessed as meeting NWC criteria and has left Australia after the JCA assessment and before the acceptance of the report, correct the dates on the MC and PDI screens to a date immediately before the departure. Otherwise, the NWC assessment will not take effect.

Go to Step 9.

9

Finalise decision + Read more ...

When the NWC assessment has been completed, the IDO will:

  • phone the customer with the outcome of the NWC assessment. If unable to speak to the customer, issue the relevant Q999 letter
  • if the customer has a date of departure, the IDO will run the Portability Script - Departures and Returns to issue a XOB101 letter. If the customer does not have a date of departure, a letter will not be issued until the customer advises of a departure date
  • run the NWC Medical Assessment DOC in the International DOC Creation Script and record the details of the decision, including:
    • the assessment outcome in relation to the portability specific medical criteria
    • if the customer is considered to be manifestly eligible for DSP
    • if the customer's condition occurred while a resident of Australia or not, and
    • how this decision was made

If the script is not available, see DSP customer going overseas.
