This page contains information to help Service Officers when customers make initial contact about a Centrelink decision. A separate procedure applies if the customer has already requested an explanation, quality check or formal review of the decision.

On this page:

Service Officer contact guide

Customer has further information

Customer has lodged an Explanation or formal review of a decision form (SS351 or SY054)

Service Officer contact guide

Table 1: scripting to help Service Officers have a conversation with a customer about a decision and their internal review options.

Step

Action

1

Customer contact about a decision that has been made + Read more ...

  • Service Officers making an outbound call to discuss an unfavourable decision with a customer, go to Step 8
  • If the customer has lodged a new Explanation or formal review of a decision (SS351) or ABSTUDY and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Explanation or formal review of a decision (SY054) form or Service Officer has been allocated a form work item, see Table 3
  • Service Officers taking an inbound call from a customer, go to Step 2

2

Check why the customer has contacted + Read more ...

After checking why the customer has contacted, ask them what they would like to do or know about the decision.

Ask appropriate questions to understand the customer’s enquiry so their concerns can be addressed or referred to the relevant business area. See Resources for examples of referrals to the relevant business area.

If the customer asks about:

  • an SS351 (Explanation or formal review of a decision) or SY054 (ABSTUDY and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Explanation or formal review of a decision) form that has been lodged, see Table 3
  • a decision made by Compliance Risk Branch (CRB), warm transfer them to a CRB Service Officer who will continue with this process. See Procedures for non-compliance staff in CRB interventions. CRB decisions include:
    • Online Compliance Intervention (OCI)
    • Employment Income Confirmation (EIC)
    • Check and Update Past Income (CUPI)
  • a decision made under the Targeted Compliance Framework or Job Seeker Compliance Model, see Non-compliance with compulsory requirements - review and appeals
  • a family assistance care decision, see Internal review process for care decisions
  • a Cashless Debit Card exit decision, see Exit applications from the Cashless Debit Card (CDC)
  • a Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment (PLDP) decision, see Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment - internal review process
  • a COVID-19 Disaster Payment decision, see COVID-19 Disaster Payment – review process
  • a decision relating to changed, missing or cancelled child care attendance data, go to Step 3

For all other decisions, go to Step 4.

3

Decision relates to changed, missing or cancelled child care attendance data + Read more ...

This is data that a child care provider has given to the Department of Education.

  • If it relates to legacy Child Care Benefit (CCB) or Child Care Rebate (CCR)
    • If the customer has not been able to resolve the issue with the child care provider, refer to the Child Care Management System (CCMS) Support Team to check the data. See Customer queries CCMS information
    • If the CCMS Support Team has finalised a referred enquiry, and the customer would like to discuss the decision, go to Step 6
  • If it relates to Child Care Subsidy (CCS) or Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS), see CCS and ACCS - Viewing attendance reports

If the customer is enquiring about a different child care decision, go to Step 4.

4

Service Officer checks they can discuss the decision + Read more ...

Review the customer's record to gather more information to discuss the decision with the customer.

Service Officers should use the support resources available to them if the matter is complex or if they need support to understand the decision and give a satisfactory response to the customer.

If the Service Officer is:

  • not in Smart Centre Operations or a Service Delivery Partner team, go to Step 8
  • in Smart Centre Operations or a Service Delivery Partner team and:
    • suitably skilled to discuss the decision, go to Step 8
    • not suitably skilled to discuss the decision:
      - For decisions about debts (excluding Family Tax Benefit and Child Care Subsidy reconciliation), a compliance review, an intelligence investigation, a compensation or random review, go to Step 9
      - For all other decisions, go to Step 5

5

Service Officer not suitably skilled to discuss decision + Read more ...

If the customer:

  • has not specifically asked for a review (or appeal), go to Step 6
  • has specifically asked for a review (or appeal), confirm if they want to apply for a formal review:
    • Tell the customer
      ‘A formal review will be completed by an Authorised Review Officer. The Authorised Review Officer is an independent officer who will review the decision taking into account any additional information you provide. The Authorised Review Officer will attempt to contact you to discuss the review. We aim to finalise a formal review within 49 days’
    • If the customer still wants to apply for a formal review, record the application for a formal review. Procedure ends here
    • If the customer does not want to apply for a formal review, refer the enquiry to a suitably skilled Service Officer. Go to Step 6

6

Refer to a suitably skilled Service Officer + Read more ...

If a suitably skilled officer has been allocated a Decision Enquiry work item, go to Step 7.

Use the Decision Enquiry Fast Note in Customer First to refer to a suitably skilled Service Officer who will discuss the decision with the customer and give the customer their internal review options if needed.

  • Access the Fast Note workflow
  • Select Auto Text option and complete the fields as below
    • Category 1:Generic
    • Category 2: Decision enquiry
    • Template: Select the option relating to the payment and type of decision the customer wants to discuss. This is important to make sure the enquiry can be assigned to an appropriate Service Officer.
      See Resources for a list of Fast Note options relevant to payment and decision type
  • Select Confirm
  • The Text field will prefill with standard text. Update this field with extra details including:
    • Date of decision
    • Service type
    • The type of decision the customer is enquiring about
  • Select Continue to create an open ACTDOC

Tell the customer they will be contacted within the next few days by a Service Officer who can talk to them about the decision and their options. Procedure ends here.

7

Suitably skilled Officer has been allocated a Decision Enquiry work item + Read more ...

Make genuine attempts to contact the customer to discuss the decision

If contact with the customer is:

  • successful, go to Step 8
  • unsuccessful after the second attempt, ensure all relevant information about attempts to contact have been recorded and complete the work item. Procedure ends here

8

Discuss decision + Read more ...

Discuss the decision with the customer and answer any questions.

If any errors are identified:

  • Service Officers who do not have the skills to undertake a reassessment, see Step 5 in Table 2
  • Services Officers with the appropriate skills are to undertake a reassessment and correct any errors

After discussing the decision and taking any appropriate action:

  • If the customer has given or wants to give more information about a recent decision, see Table 2
  • If the customer does not understand or does not agree with the decision, provide the customer with their options, go to Step 9
  • If the customer would like to make a complaint, go to Step 10
  • If no further action is needed after discussing the decision, go to Step 11

9

Give the customer internal review options + Read more ...

The customer may not be aware of their options, or may not be sure which option to choose.

Give the customer more details about the explanation and formal review process.

Note: Service Officers not undertaking the SME role when discussing a decision must still offer the customer the option to request an explanation, even if they are recognised as a SME. For example, if they are not scheduled as a SME at the time they are discussing the decision with the customer.

Tell the customer:

  • 'We want to help you understand what your different options are so you can choose the best option for you. I’ll explain them to you now’
  • 'If you don't understand or would like more information about the decision, you can ask for an explanation which means I can arrange for a skilled officer to contact you. They will explain the decision, answer any questions you have, consider any additional information, and may be able to address your concerns without the need for a formal review of the decision. We aim to contact you within 14 days’
  • If priority indicated: 'Based on your circumstances, you should be contacted sooner'
  • 'If you don't agree with the decision, you can apply for a formal review. Your application will be sent to an Authorised Review Officer, an independent officer who will review the decision taking into account any additional information you provide. The Authorised Review Officer will attempt to contact you to discuss the review. We aim to finalise a formal review within 49 days'
  • If priority indicated: 'Based on your circumstances, your review should be completed sooner'
  • ‘It's your choice to either request an explanation or apply for a formal review of the decision’
  • 'If you choose an explanation now, you can change your mind and apply for a formal review of the decision at any time'
  • 'An application for formal review should be made within (time frame based on the payment/decision, for example, 13 weeks) of us telling you about the decision. If you apply for a formal review after this time, a review might not be able to be done or, if the decision is changed you may only get your entitlement from the date you apply for the review’
  • Family assistance customers only: ‘If you are also a Child Support customer, you need to apply for a formal review within 28 days of being notified of the decision. Otherwise any changes that may affect your Child Support assessment may only take effect from the date you applied for the formal review'

If the customer:

  • wants to request an explanation, record the request for an explanation. Procedure ends here
  • wants to apply for a formal review, record the application for a formal review. Procedure ends here
  • is satisfied after discussing the decision and no further action is needed, go to Step 11

10

The customer would like to make a complaint + Read more ...

Refer to Managing customer complaints and feedback.

Advise the customer:

  • 'I can help you by recording your complaint. If you don’t want to lodge a complaint now, you can also send us your complaint through your online account'
  • 'Separate to your complaint, if you don't understand or would like more information about the decision, you can ask for an explanation which means I can arrange for a skilled officer to contact you. They will explain the decision, answer any questions you have, consider any additional information, and may be able to address your concerns without the need for a formal review of the decision. We aim to contact you within 14 days'
  • If priority indicated: 'Based on your circumstances, you should be contacted sooner'
  • 'If you don’t agree with the decision, you can apply for a formal review. Your application will be sent to an Authorised Review Officer, an independent officer who will review the decision taking into account any additional information you provide. The Authorised Review Officer will attempt to contact you to discuss the review. We aim to finalise a formal review within 49 days’
  • If priority indicated: 'Based on your circumstances, your review should be completed sooner’
  • 'If you choose an explanation now, you can change your mind and apply for a formal review of the decision at any time’
  • 'An application for formal review should be made within (time frame based on the payment/decision, for example, 13 weeks) of us telling you about the decision. If you apply for a formal review after this time, a review might not be able to be done or if the decision is changed, you may only get your entitlement from the date you apply for the review'
  • Family assistance customers only: 'If you are also a Child Support customer, you need to apply for a formal review within 28 days of being notified of the decision. Otherwise any changes that may affect your Child Support assessment may only take effect from the date you applied for the formal review'
  • Is there anything else I can help you with in regards to this decision?'

If the customer:

  • wants to make a complaint now, record the complaint
  • wants to request an explanation, record the request for an explanation. Procedure ends here
  • wants to apply for a formal review, record the application for a formal review. Procedure ends here
  • is satisfied after discussing the decision and no further action is needed, go to Step 11

11

No further action is needed + Read more ...

  • Customer Service Delivery Group (CSDG) Service Officers
    (For example: Service Centres/ Face to Face, Smart Centres Operations, Service Delivery Partners)
    Document the discussion with the customer, run the Internal Review/Explanation script and select Document decision discussion
  • Non-CSDG Service Officers
    (For example: Intelligence and Investigations, Compliance and Debt Operations, Debt Programme and Compensation, or Appeals and Reviews teams)
    Document the discussion with the customer. Do not select Document decision discussion in the Internal Review/Explanation script

Procedure ends here.

Customer has further information

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Customer has further information + Read more ...

The customer has given or wants to give further information relating to a recent decision.

If the customer has already requested an explanation or applied for a formal review of the decision:

  • check the customer's record to confirm receipt of any documents
  • upload further information if needed
  • the SME or ARO will consider the information
  • Procedure ends here

If the customer has not already requested an explanation or applied for a formal review and has:

  • provided information not yet actioned, go to Step 2
  • information to provide, go to Step 6

2

Customer has provided further information related to a recent decision, not yet actioned + Read more ...

Check the customer's record to confirm receipt of any documents.

Make sure any documents have been added/scanned to the customer’s record and can be read.

If the documents or details:

  • relate to a claim rejected for reason FRC, FSD, POI, IME/OTH (DSP insufficient medical evidence), see Requested documentation not provided resulting in rejection of new claim. Procedure ends here
  • relate to any other decision:
    • If the Service Officer is skilled and scheduled duties allow them to action the information, go to Step 3
    • If the Service Officer is not skilled or scheduled duties do not allow them to action the information, go to Step 5

3

Service Officer can action the information + Read more ...

  • Reassess the decision using the new information
  • Document the reassessment on a DOC or Note on the customer’s record
  • Discuss the new decision with the customer

Is the customer satisfied with the outcome?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Give the customer internal review options + Read more ...

If the customer:

  • wants to request an explanation, record the request for an explanation. Procedure ends here
  • wants to apply for a formal review, record the application for a formal review. Procedure ends here
  • is satisfied after discussing the decision and no further action is needed, See Step 11 in Table 1

5

Service Officer cannot action any further information, or cannot correct any identified errors + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • 'I will arrange for a skilled officer to consider the information and advise you of the outcome. However, there are other options available if you don't understand or don't agree with the decision, such as requesting an explanation or applying for a formal review'
  • 'If you don't understand or would like more information about the decision, I can arrange for a skilled officer to contact you. They will explain the decision, answer any questions you have, consider any additional information, and may be able to address your concerns without the need for a formal review of the decision. We aim to contact you within 14 days'

Your application will be sent to an Authorised Review Officer, an independent officer who will review the decision taking into account any additional information you provide. The Authorised Review Officer will attempt to contact you to discuss the review. We aim to finalise a formal review within 49 days’
  • If priority indicated: 'Based on your circumstances, your review should be completed sooner’
  • 'The lodgement of documents is not considered to be a request for an explanation or an application for a formal review. You must specify that you would like to request an explanation or apply for a formal review'
  • 'It’s your choice to either request an explanation or apply for a formal review of the decision’
  • 'If you choose an explanation now, you can change your mind and apply for a formal review of the decision at any time’
  • 'An application for formal review should be made within (time frame based on the payment/decision, for example, 13 weeks) of us telling you about the decision. Otherwise any changes that may affect your Child Support assessment may only take effect from the date you applied for the formal review'

If the customer:

  • wants to request an explanation, record the request for an explanation. Procedure ends here
  • wants to apply for a formal review, record the application for a formal review. Procedure ends here
  • does not want to request an explanation or apply for a formal review, create an ACTDOC to refer details to the relevant business area. Use Operational Blueprint to find the appropriate Fast Note workflow or Callback/ACTDOC guided procedure. Procedure ends here
  • is satisfied after discussing the decision and no further action is needed, See Step 11 in Table 1

6

Customer has lodged an Explanation or formal review of a decision form (SS351 or SY054)

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Customer has lodged an explanation or formal review of a decision form + Read more ...

  • To request an explanation or apply for a formal review of a decision, a customer may upload or lodge an
    • Explanation or formal review of a decision (SS351) form, or
    • ABSTUDY and Assistance for isolated Children (AIC) Explanation or formal review of decision (SY054) form
  • If the customer is contacting about a SS351/SY054 form they have already lodged, go to Step 2
  • If the customer is lodging a new SS351/SY054 form or a Service Officer has been allocated a form work item, see Request for an explanation or application for a formal review

2

Check if review form has been processed + Read more ...

Check the customer’s record to see if the request for explanation or application for formal review of the decision has been recorded. Check the:

  • DL screen for DOCs relating to the request or application
  • ACL screen in the Appeal (APL) system to see if there is an APL record for the explanation or formal review

If the review form has been processed and the request for explanation or formal review has been recorded:

  • the request/application is in progress, tell the customer that their request/application has been received and will be allocated to a skilled officer/Authorised Review Officer to be completed
  • the request/application has been finalised, tell the customer about the outcome of their request/application based on the information recorded on the customers record

If the review form has not been processed, record the customer’s request for an explanation or application for a formal review. See Request for an explanation or application for a formal review.

Procedure ends here.
