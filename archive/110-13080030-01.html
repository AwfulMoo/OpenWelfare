This document outlines how Compliance Officers assess and finalise an RSI.

Assessing an RSI

After gathering all the evidence, calculate the customer's entitlement as soon as possible.

When making an unfavourable decision, speak to the customer:

  • explain the decision
  • give them a chance to provide more information or evidence relevant to the decision
  • advise their review rights, and record an application for a formal review of the decision if required

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer. If contact is unsuccessful, send a Pre-adverse decision letter to the customer and/or the affected party to advise them how the outcome was determined.

Finalising an RSI

The intervention is finalised after:

  • the conversation with the customer
  • delivering the education message, and
  • calculating any changes to the customer's and/or the affected party’s entitlement

The outcome will be finalised if the customer does not respond to the pre adverse decision letter or fails to give more information.

Before finalising, check that all activities completed as part of the RSI are correctly attributed to the review in the Integrated Review System (IRS).
