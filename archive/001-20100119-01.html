Altered processes and mutual obligation requirements for job seekers due to coronavirus (COVID-19) response 001-20100119

This document outlines how the coronavirus (COVID-19) response starting in March 2020, has impacted mutual obligation, participation requirements and job seeker compliance processes. Service Officers must check Operational Messages for any current altered arrangements.

Altered Requirements

In response to COVID-19 restrictions the Government made temporary changes to mutual obligation requirements for affected job seekers and ParentsNext participants (hereafter referred to as job seekers). When this has occurred, specific rules applied to mutual obligation requirements and job seeker compliance based on geographical location, such as: State / Territory and/or by postcode/LGA/CDP region. Generally the Government applies these contingency arrangements as a result of a public health order issued by the relevant State or Territory.

For Community Development Program (CDP) job seekers, reduced or altered mutual obligation requirements may also be implemented at a local level by the CDP provider for their CDP region. This can occur in circumstances where the community or CDP region is impacted by COVID-19 and where localised restrictions may be introduced by community or regional authorities, without a public health order being issued by the Territory or State Government.

Some locations were affected by multiple COVID-19 outbreaks. This means that mutual obligation requirements and job seeker compliance arrangements may have changed multiple times for specific locations.

Information regarding changes to mutual obligation requirements and/or employment servicing in specific communities and CDP regions due to COVID-19 can be obtained from a range of sources:

  • ask the job seeker to provide details about local COVID-19 management measures
  • contact the job seeker's CDP provider
  • check media and internal messaging
  • access and utilise the knowledge of Indigenous Service Officers (ISO)
  • check the Indigenous Community Events page

The Resources page contains an intranet link to the Indigenous Community Events page.

Some locations were affected by multiple COVID-19 outbreaks. This means that mutual obligation requirements and job seeker compliance arrangements may have changed multiple times for specific locations.

These arrangements may apply to customers in receipt of:

  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
  • Youth Allowance (YA) (other)
  • Parenting Payment (PP) for a single customer (when their youngest child turns 6)
  • Parenting Payment and are a compulsory ParentsNext participant
  • Special Benefit (SpB) (Nominated Visa Holders)

If a job seeker changed address, the relevant mutual obligation requirements and job seeker compliance arrangements for their new location are automatically applied.

Note: in addition, other COVID-19 related measures remain in place for all job seekers, regardless of geographical location (e.g. exemptions from mutual obligation requirements due to COVID-19 impacts).

The Resources page contains exemption codes, links to external websites for further coronavirus (COVID-19) details and a link to the Indigenous Community Events page which contains details of CDP regions where servicing is impacted by COVID-19 restrictions.
