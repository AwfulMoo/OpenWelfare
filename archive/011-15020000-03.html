2

Determine the source of the TAC telephone enquiry + Read more ...

If the telephone enquiry is:

  • from a Medicare Service Officer in response to a system flag while processing a Medicare patient claim, go to Step 3
  • from a Medicare Service Officer, the injured person or claimant solicitor requesting a Medicare history statement, go to Step 5
  • not related to a TAC claim, determine the nature of the enquiry and provide relevant advice accordingly, procedure ends here

Note: if a call is received relating to more complex TAC matters or if Compensation Recovery Service Officers are unsure whether the case should be registered, contact a PSO for assistance by calling the PSO hotline.

3

Compensation Recovery Service Officer + Read more ...

Note: ask the Medicare Service Officer to identify themselves by their operator number (Universal Log In).

Using the National Compensation Recovery Information (NCRI) mainframe screen, request the following information to identify the injured person for the Medicare patient claim:

  • Request the injured person’s Medicare card number
  • Enter the Medicare card number into the mainframe screen by typing NCRI, followed by the Medicare card number, Press [Enter]
  • Confirm the injured person’s name, date of birth and address
  • If more than one person is listed on the Medicare card, place the cursor under the injured person’s name, Press [F5]
  • Press [F2] to access the case notes for details of any actions taken since the date of injury or illness. Press [F8] to traverse more pages if there are detailed case notes
  • Press [F2] again to return to the home page of the NCRI screen, go to Step 3

4

Update NCRI Screen + Read more ...

  • change the COMP STATUS to NOCLAIM (if required)
  • change the COMP TYPE from TAC to MVA, Press [Enter]
  • the NCRI screen will prompt to update the record at the top control line - type U in the control line, Press [Enter]

Note: do not provide a reason code to the Medicare Service Officer to pay the account once the NCRI screen has been updated

  • request the Medicare Service Officer to process the Medicare patient claim again to ensure no further problems are encountered
  • once the account has been successfully paid, update the NCRI screen with case notes advising of comp status and comp type changed

Procedure ends here.

5

Injured person or claimant solicitor requesting a Medicare history statement for a TAC claim + Read more ...

If the enquiry is from a Medicare Service Officer with the injured person or claimant on the line, request the Service Officer to transfer the call and continue with this Step.

Does the injured person live In Victoria and the accident happened in Victoria?

Note: if the injured person lives in Victoria and the accident happened in Victoria, the claim is likely to be TAC Medical Excess only. If the injured person lives in another State or Territory, the claim is likely to be proceeding under common law.

  • Yes, advise the injured person:
    • TAC Medicare history statements are no longer processed by Medicare Compensation Recovery
    • Medicare claims information for the last 3 years can be obtained through their myGov account at my.gov.au - advise they may be required to register if they do not have a myGov account, or
    • if they require a Medicare history statement that is more than 3 years old, to complete a 'Request for Medicare claims information form' (MS031) which is available from the Services Australia website at servicesaustralia.gov.au
  • No, the claim is likely proceeding under common law. Request the injured person to submit a request for a Medicare history statement form (MO026) or to contact TAC and advise them to provide notification of settlement if the claim has been finalised under common law

Note: further information on accessing personal information online including Medicare claims information can be found on Services Australia website. The Resources page has a link to the site.

Case escalation

How to escalate a case

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Prior to case escalation + Read more ...

Note: before escalating a case to Programme Management, the background of the case must be thoroughly reviewed. Make note of the sequence of events and whether or not legislation and policy have been correctly applied.

If after the case review the Service Officer determines:

  • The case is notifiable, continue processing the case
    • before releasing the work item, go to Step 4 to add a case note
    • procedure ends here
  • A decision cannot be made and assistance is required, contact a PSO, go to Step 2

2

Obtain advice from a Programme Support Officer (PSO) + Read more ...

  • Contact a PSO by calling the PSO hotline for clarification of procedures and policy or to review the case. In some instances a PSO may not be available so the Service Officer must then email the Team Leader for further advice
  • Service Officers should suspend the case overnight and keep a record of the Work Identification Number (WIN). This will assist with returning to the case at this step once a response is received from the Team Leader. Before releasing the work item, go to Step 4 to add a case note
  • Procedure ends here

If after the review by the PSO or Team Leader they determine:

  • a decision cannot be made and further assistance is required, the case must be escalated to Programme Management for consideration, go to Step 3

3

Escalate to Programme Management + Read more ...

Note: all escalations must be sent to the Team Leader mailbox using the Escalation to Programme Management via TL5 or PSO email template and will be referred to Programme Management for consideration. The Resources page contains a link to the email template - Escalation to Programme Management via TL5 or PSO.

  • Service Officers must ensure the following information is included in the escalation template:
    • WIN and injured person’s name in the subject header
    • Service Officer's logon ID and Team Leader (TL) logon ID and the date escalation sent to TL
    • reason for escalation (tick appropriate box)
    • injured person’s details and whether injured person has other active cases
    • whether case has previously been escalated to Programme Management
    • which Operational Blueprint procedures were reviewed prior to sending the escalation
    • background of the case and what has happened to date
    • an explanation of the issue/s, why the case is being escalated and any specific question/s the Service Officer has for Programme Management
    • Service Officer’s recommendation
  • Attach any relevant documents from the case to the escalation template
  • Email the escalation to Programme Management - via TL5 or PSO email template to Comp Team Leaders
  • Suspend the case overnight. Before releasing the work item go to Step 4 to add a case note

4

Add a case note + Read more ...

If the Case Notes screen has not opened automatically, select the Add notes button from the list on the right hand side of the main CCMS screen.

  • The top box shows all notes previously recorded on this case
  • In the blank bottom box, record the actions taken, information requested and from whom and reasons for a decision. Use standard case notes where possible
  • Select the Add button to save notes. A confirmation box will appear, select 'Yes' to add case note
  • Press [F4] to release the work item, and
    • if this is the end of the process the case will suspend, or
    • if further actions are required in other Compensation Case Management System (CCMS) work queues, follow through on case until further actions are completed
