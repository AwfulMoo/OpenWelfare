This page contains information on the Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA), which provides financial assistance for eligible customers to help purchase prescription medicines.

Eligibility and process for payment of PhA

Step

Action

1

Check eligibility + Read more ...

Check if customer is automatically eligible for Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA).

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Check customer is receiving PhA + Read more ...

  • Check the Payment Details (PD) screen to ensure that customer is receiving PhA. No coding is required if the customer is automatically eligible for PhA
  • PhA details will show on the Rate Explanation (NREX) and PD screens

Is the customer receiving PhA?

  • Yes, no further action is required. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Is the customer receiving a Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) payment? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Has the customer provided a medical certificate? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, ask the customer to provide a medical certificate to consider eligibility for PhA, go to Step 5

5

Coding of medical certificate + Read more ...

When the medical certificate has been supplied, update the details on the customer's record.

If the customer is temporarily incapacitated for work and provides a medical certificate, update their medical details on the Medical Condition Details (MC) screen:

  • select the Medical Details option from the NSS Task Selector (NTS) screen or the ABSTUDY Task Selector (EYTS) screen, or
  • type 'MC' in the Nxt: field on the current screen

The Medical Conditions Summary (MCSS) screen will display. Press [Enter] to record details on a new MC screen.

Complete the following fields with the information in the medical certificate:

  • Event Date:
  • Assigning Professional:
  • Name: of the assigning professional
  • Telephone: of the assigning professional
  • Unfit to Work From: and to: insert relevant dates
  • Incap Exempt: This field is used to signal to the system whether the medical certificate can be used to authorise an exemption from Mutual Obligation Requirements and pay PhA. Valid values are 'Y' or 'N'

Record a DOC including medical details and dates unfit for work and complete activity.

After assessment, the PhA amount will be shown on the NREX screen.

Advise the customer of the outcome.

Procedure ends here.

6

DVA customers + Read more ...

A DVA customer may be entitlement to PhA or Veterans Supplement at PhA rate (VSP) or Veterans Supplement at the higher rate (VSH).

Couples where one partner is eligible for VSP/VSH

In the case of couples, where one partner is eligible for VSP/VSH from DVA and his or her partner would otherwise be eligible for the income support payment:

  • DVA will pay their customer Veterans Supplement at the high rate (VSH) and PhA will not be paid with an income support payment administered by Services Australia
  • The exception to this is where the DVA customer is in receipt of a Service Pension or Veteran Payment (or a combination of Service Pension or Veteran Payment and some other DVA pension) and his or her partner is receiving a pension from Services Australia. Each department and agency will pay their customer half the combined rate of Pension Supplement

Receiving partial payment of income support payment or service pension

If a single or partnered customer receives a partial payment of any of the following payments:

  • Carer Service Pension
  • Invalidity Service Pension
  • Service Pension
  • Veteran Payment, or
  • a partial income support payment

PhA will not be paid with the income support payment as these payments include VSP/VSH/Pension Supplement already from DVA.

Repatriation Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme

DVA Repatriation Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (RPBS) card holders are not entitled to PhA from Services Australia.

A DVA customer does not have to receive a DVA payment to be an RPBS card holder.

Customers separated due to illness or respite care

Customers will receive the single rate of PhA each if they are paid the single rate of pension or allowance because they are:

  • separated due to ill health, or
  • in respite care

7

Coding Veterans Supplement for DVA customer + Read more ...

On the Veterans Affairs Pension (DVA) screen, code the following fields with information on what rate of PhA customer is receiving from DVA:

  • Type: code either one of the following:
    • 'VSP' for Veteran Supplement at the PhA rate
    • 'VSH' for Veteran Supplement at the high rate, which includes TAL and PhA components
  • DVA Reference: DVA reference number
  • Event Date: date of grant of new claim
  • Fortnightly Amount $.c: will default once [enter] is pressed
  • Exempt Pymt Ind: will default to 'Y'
  • status code of ON/OFF

The Veterans Affairs Pension Summary (DVAS) screen will display. 'Not income' displays in the Amount: field.

Note: the ClientID: field is a display only field that contains the DVA Unique Identifier which is a number that is allocated by DVA to customers who receive a DVA Disability Pension and also receive an income support payment from Services Australia. This number, (unlike the DVA reference number) will not change if the customer moves to a new state.

Advise the customer of the outcome.

Record details on a DOC.
