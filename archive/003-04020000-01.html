Claiming Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment (AVTOP) 003-04020000

This document outlines the claiming process for AVTOP.

Claiming

Claims should be made in one of the following ways:

  • an AVTOP claim form (which can be obtained via servicesaustralia.gov.au/help in an emergency), or
  • contacting the AVTOP processing team and speaking with an operator

Claiming period for AVTOP

A primary victim has 2 years from the date of the declaration by the Prime Minister to make a claim

A secondary victim has 12 months from the date of death of a close family member, where the close family member died as a direct result of, and within 2 years of, the day the declared terrorist act occurred

If a claim is lodged outside of the allowable claiming period, Service Officers should refer the claim to the Emergency Management Level 2 Helpdesk, for consideration. A decision must be made to accept the claim prior to any assessment of the claim being undertaken.

When referring the claim by email, please include the reason the claimant lodged the claim outside of the claiming period.

Assessment of a child under 16 years

A child aged under 16 years may be eligible for the AVTOP as a primary or secondary victim. The claim must be lodged by a legal guardian.

Assessment of a person 16 years and over

A person 16 years and over may be eligible for the AVTOP as a primary or secondary victim. The claim must be lodged in their own right.

Making an AVTOP claim

Claims should be made in one of the following ways:

  • Phone via the dedicated Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Hotline, or
  • Post

For contact details, see Emergency payments - claiming - AVTOP.

Ensure all required documents are submitted with the completed claim form. For example, identity documents (where required) and medical evidence of injuries sustained as a direct result of the terrorist act.

Identity requirements for an AVTOP claim

Different levels of identity must be provided, depending on the age of the claimant:

  • Child Under 16 years:
    • 1 Commencement of Identity document, or
    • sufficient identity documents to satisfy the Service Officer of the child's identity (for a list of documents see Confirming your Identity (SS231))
  • Over 16 years:
    • 1 Commencement of Identity document
    • 1 Primary Use in Community document, and
    • 1 Secondary Use in Community document

Note: an identity document cannot be used multiple times. For example, a document used as Commencement of Identity, cannot be used again as a Primary or Secondary Use in Community document. For a list of documents see Confirming your identity (SS231).

For claimants unable to send original documents, claimants or legal guardians can send certified copies (this procedure is only available to AVTOP claimants).

Certified true copies must be annotated as follows:

  • Certified True Copy of Original Document
    • Signed....................................................
    • Name.....................................................
    • Position..................................................
    • Date.......................................................
