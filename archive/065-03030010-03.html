Current payment suspended or cancelled + Read more ...

If the customer or their partner's payments have been suspended or cancelled due to non-lodgement of the transfer, payments can be restored without loss of payment if the transfer to Age Pension is submitted with required documents within 13 weeks of the date last paid.

6

Check if the customer will be disadvantaged + Read more ...

Customers who are eligible to transfer to Age Pension are provided with details on the disadvantages and advantages using data from their record. See the Resources page for information about Age Pension versus current payment to compare payment options.

Note: Widow Allowance (WA) and Partner Allowance (PA) will cease on 1 January 2022. Customers will need to transfer to Age Pension to continue receiving payment after this date.

If the customer's decision to transfer to Age Pension would disadvantage them make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer:

  • If customer contact is successful: advise the advantages and disadvantages of transferring. Confirm their decision to transfer to Age Pension and DOC the record
  • If customer contact is unsuccessful: DOC the record with details of that attempts and process the transfer

The customer would be disadvantaged by transferring to Age Pension in the following scenarios, however, they may still prefer to access the advantages of Age Pension.

Carer Supplement

  • This disadvantage applies to all customers receiving Carer Payment (CP). If transferred to Age Pension, they would no longer be eligible for an annual Carer Supplement in respect of CP. If they receive Carer Allowance (CA) they will continue to get an annual Carer Supplement in respect of CA.

Bereavement assistance

This disadvantage applies to all CP recipients if they are caring for someone who is not their partner and they transfer to Age Pension:

  • Customer will no longer eligible for a CP Lump Sum Bereavement Payment. This may be payable if the care receiver is a child, is single, or is partnered and the care receiver's partner (who is not the carer) is not getting an income support payment.
  • If the customer gets CP (XWP), they may be eligible for a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (as above) if they only get one payment level Carer Allowance and the care receiver dies.
  • If the customer is only caring for their partner, they may still be eligible for a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment if they transfer to Age Pension

Delaying the transfer

  • If the customer still elects to transfer, they may prefer to delay it until their first Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) after 1 July if Carer Supplement is due to be paid. This includes when they are in a 14 week bereavement period or in a 14 week continuation period after the care receiver has been admitted to an institution. There is no need to delay it if CP will stop before 1 July for any reason e.g. due to respite or the maximum portability period.

Transferring from Disability Support Pension (DSP)

Customers in receipt of Incentive Allowance (IA) will lose eligibility to IA if they transfer to Age Pension, see the Pensions Rate Calculation (PRC) screen.

Customers in receipt of IA who indicate they wish to transfer to Age Pension must be contacted to advise that:

  • IA will cancel, and
  • they may be eligible for Rent Assistance (RA) if they are renting, and
  • if single, paying rent and sharing accommodation, they may wish to consider remaining on DSP and claim RA instead of continuing to receive IA as RA may be a higher amount, see Sharer Status field on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen

Customers whose partner is paid CP under the NZ agreement should be contacted as CP will cease if the DSP customer is transferred to Age Pension. The agreement country code is on the partner's ARD screen.

Check if the customer wishes to stay on DSP and not transfer to Age Pension at this time. See the Resources page for information about Age Pension versus current payment.

If customer wants to change their decision about transferring to Age Pension, continue with applicable checks ensuring that change of decision is recorded in finalisation DOC along with any other relevant details.

Customers who are blind + Read more ...

Permanent blindness

If transferring from Disability Support Pension (DSP) to Age Pension, the customer's Blind status will transfer automatically.

For customers who want to be assessed as permanently blind, see Transfer to Age Pension (Blind).

Income and assets

Customers entitled to Age Pension (Blind) or DSP (Blind) do not need to disclose their income and assets unless:

  • they are claiming Rent Assistance (RA), or
  • their partner is on an income support payment and the customer needs to disclose their income and assets to correctly assess their partner's rate of payment

Customers not subject to the Means Test can disclose their income and assets if they wish.

For more information, see Income and assets tests for blind customers

Go to Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen and check the Blind - Income & Assets Exempt field. Is the customer subject to the Means Test?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Income and assets + Read more ...

Superannuation

For customers of Age Pension age, superannuation products must be declared and details of any changes to income and assets advised.

For example, details of approved deposit fund, deferred annuity or superannuation fund may need to be provided.

Superannuation is assessable at Age Pension age whether the customer transfers to Age Pension or not.

If their partner is over pension age and is not already on an income support payment, details of the partner's superannuation will also be requested.

Home Property Extended Curtilage

If the customer's record indicates their home property is on more than 2 hectares they will have been asked home property questions.

If not previously recorded on the Real Estate Business Details (REBS) screen and the customer's home property exceeds 2 hectares - issue Module R, as the question set will not be included in their online transfer or ACC.

  • If customer is eligible for the Extended Curtilage concession, apply updates to REBS including Curtilage Type field from the later Date of Event of APA or DOR
  • Due to a system limitation, if the Date of Event is more than 30 days in the future the REBS coding cannot be applied. In this instance, the TTA activity must be Held until a date within 30 days of Age Pension age

For information about recording real estate details and recording details for a property valuation, see Assessing and coding the Real estate details Mod R.

9

Indigenous customers + Read more ...

Check the Indigenous indicator on the Other Contact Details (OCD) screen.

Identified Indigenous customers may be excluded from the automatic transfer to Age Pension if they may be disadvantaged.

However, if the customer submits an online transfer, or via ACC, it must be processed manually. If they will be disadvantaged by transferring to Age Pension, they must be contacted to discuss this.

For more information, see the Resources page for information about Age Pension versus current payment.

Identity Confirmation and proof of age

These requirements are deemed to have been met for Indigenous customers already receiving income support payments who are transferring to Age Pension.

If there are gaps in their Identity Confirmation from their previous payment, the system will automatically update their Identity Confirmation requirements when the transfer to Age Pension activity is processed.

Proof of age is not required even if it has not been confirmed previously.

For information about recording real estate details and recording details for a property valuation, see Assessing and coding the Real estate details Mod R.

If assessed for Age Pension (Blind) + Read more ...

On the Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen, code the following fields:

  • Event Date: the date the customer requested the transfer to Age Pension (Blind) or the date of eligibility, whichever is later
  • Code 'I'nsert in Action field to update other blind fields
  • Blind Ind: code 'Y' if permanent blindness criteria were met, otherwise leave blank
  • Severely Disabled Code: key 'Yes' or leave blank
  • Blind - Income & Assets Exempt: fields must be coded. Code 'Y' for both fields if income and assets do not need to be declared. Blind - Income & Assets Exempt: should be coded as 'N' if customer has a partner on payment who is not blind or if RA is claimed
  • For a non-review customer, the Auto Form Exclusion Code: field should be coded as 'BLI', to stop the issue of a medical review if appropriate. Otherwise, it is left blank. Note: the customer may be able to get medical assistance that could change their Blind status for social security purposes. A medical review will be needed for possible future changes if they should seek medical help, e.g. they may have cataracts but choose not to have any medical assistance even though this may help their sight. In these cases do not code 'BLI'
  • Means not Disclosed Ind: key 'N' if the customer disclosed their income and asset details. If income and assets were not disclosed, code 'Y' - payments will be limited to the base rate and supplement
  • CITW/Blind Residence: to indicate whether the permanent blindness occurred while the customer was a resident of Australia (AUS) or another country (OVE)

Transferring from working age payments - existing problem

Customers who have transferred to Age Pension from working age payments are not always paid their final payment due in the entitlement period in which Age Pension starts. Often the payment is cancelled for not lodging (CAN-DNL) but in many cases there was no requirement to lodge in that fortnight.

To ensure the correct entitlement is received, check the customer's record after the first payment of Age Pension. A manual review can be set up as a reminder.

If the payment cannot be issued it should be reported to the ICT Service Desk.

Concession Cards

If the customer retains entitlement to the Pensioner Concession Card, a new card is not issued unless the customer requests one. An exception to this is when a transferring from mainstream pension to Blind Pension a new card will be issued as it will be slightly different.

Any new card issued after the transfer is processed will show the primary payment at time of issue. This includes cards sent annually one month before the current card expires.

Centrepay deductions

Centrepay deductions may only transfer to Age Pension if the customer is transferring from a payment within the PEN system. If they do not automatically transfer as part of the new claim process, discuss with the customer whether they would like the existing Centrepay deductions set up when the transfer is completed. If so, manually code once the transfer is finalised.
