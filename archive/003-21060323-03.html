Customer does not have a record + Read more ...

Consider the following when trying to locate a customer record:

  • For a person born in Australia (or migrated to Australia) after approximately
    • 1959, Historical Family Allowance (HFA) records may exist. For help, see:
      Gathering information from internal sources
      Online Search (OLS) facility
      If they do not have access to OLS, Service Officers should speak with their Team Leader
    • 1988, links to a parent should exist, for example, family assistance, child care payments, maternity payments
  • The person could have been linked as a partner or nominee to a person who previously received a payment from Services Australia
  • Complete a SMS/Email (SMEM) search:
    • A SMEM search can help to identify an existing record or if a customer has multiple records:
      - Key SMEM in the Next field
      - Key the customer’s mobile phone number in the SMS number field
      - Press Enter and the results will show
  • If, after completing a SMEM search, there are concerns about a customer’s identity, see Identity Fraud

If extensive checks and searches return no existing customer record, staff must make sure they are satisfied the caller is who they say they are and the information is genuine before adding a customer to the system.

Create the record in the correct environment. This avoids the need to complete an Inter-environment change of address (ICoA).

Note: if the record is created in the incorrect environment and a claim is granted, the customer will not receive a payment. Where this has occurred, contact the Technical Peer Support who must contact the Level 2 Helpdesk using the payment query template.

  • The Inter-Environment Change of Address Log Information (ITLG) screen shows when environment transfers were done.
  • The Payment Summary (PS) screen will not show a payment has released.
  • Running the workflow again will not fix the error.

Check the customer's identity by completing an Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) update. If the customer has a passport and has travelled or lives outside Australia, the RSIMME update will help to complete PoRO.

Residence requirements + Read more ...

The system does not automatically assess a customer's residence status.

To avoid granting payments incorrectly, make sure all residence data is coded and manually check the customer meets the visa requirements.

Residence checks must be completed before running:

  • ACC, as the details checked or coded will assist in pre-filling residence information in an ACC
  • the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow if using the verbal claim template process, as these details must not be updated in the workflow
    • do not run ACC or the workflow yet

Residence checks needed

  • Check the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen for citizenship, visa and/or New Zealand residence status details
    • For NZ citizens check the Visa (Sub) Class code for a 444 visa
    • Check the New Zealand residence status
      - Grandfathered (GRF) is a protected Special Category Visa (SCV) holder and should claim online if they have access to online services, as an Australian resident
      - Transitional (TRA) is a protected SCV holder and should claim online if they have access to online services, as an Australian resident
      - NOT is a non-protected SCV holder and should claim by phone as a non-Australian resident
  • Check the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen for country of birth or travel documents
  • Check the Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen for travel details
  • Details on the RSLEG, RSIMME and RSIM screens may be refreshed by RSIMME update
  • For Australian residents (through legal permanent residence and residing in Australia) - make sure the Country of Residence (CRES) screen is coded. Note: the CRES screen does not always need to be coded with an Australian entry for non-Australian residents (temporary visa holders)
  • Asking the customer the last time they travelled overseas is a good way to confirm any passport details. This will help to complete PoRO
  • If there are no residence details on the RSLEG screen, see Recording legal residence status to complete residence coding on the record
  • Check the Travel Outside Australia Summary (TOAS) screen to make sure the customer is in Australia. Note: If a customer’s first movement is an arrival in Australia, it is not displayed on this screen. See the RSIM screen instead
    • If the customer is not in Australia at the time of claiming, they are not eligible to receive a payment. Tell them their claim cannot proceed

If the customer is an Australian resident (through legal permanent residence and residing in Australia), tell them to submit a claim online. They can visit the Centrelink online account website for information on how to claim. If the customer advises they have no access to online services, continue with the phone claim.

A phone claim can continue where the customer is:

  • a non-Australian Resident with an eligible visa
  • an Australian Resident with no access to online services

See Resources for eligible visa information.

Note: if a customer is an Australian Resident, when completing the relevant verbal claim template, staff must document why the:

  • customer's claim is being taken over the phone, and
  • customer cannot lodge an online claim

Special circumstances

When a customer advises of special circumstances that led to being unable to lodge a claim within the claiming period, a decision maker will consider various factors.

While each situation must be assessed on an individual basis, there must be something ‘out of the ordinary’ that caused the customer to miss the claiming period. Simply forgetting to lodge a claim would not be a sufficient reason. However, if there are unusual circumstances that led to a customer forgetting to lodge a claim, these can be considered.

Special circumstances must also be considered where, for any state or territory event where automatic payments apply:

PPL/NCL or PPL/ASS + Read more ...

Pre-birth claims

As the child has not been born yet and the PPL start date is in the future, contact the customer to advise:

  • they are eligible to receive a COVID-19 Disaster Payment until either the child is born or they start any leave that will cover a period before and up to the birth of the child, whichever is the earliest date
    • Note: it is important to consider any unpaid leave the customer may take in the period before the birth of the child. For unpaid leave, while the customer may have lost income, they would not meet the ‘would have worked’ criteria. That is, if a person is on unpaid leave, they would not have worked even if the restricted movement (lockdown) did not occur
  • if they are still receiving the COVID-19 Disaster Payment
    • once they commence any leave associated with the birth of the child (or any other leave), they cannot receive any COVID-19 Disaster Payments during relevant periods where the leave covers every day in the relevant period, and
    • once their PPL period starts, they cannot receive any COVID-19 Disaster Payments until the PPL period ends
  • to cancel any future automatic payments by advising of a change in circumstances via their Centrelink online account or by contacting the agency
  • if this action is not taken, they will be overpaid

Go to Step 6.

Post-birth claims

Discuss the customer's situation -

  • Has the customer returned to work since the birth of the child?
    • If the customer has returned to work in the period between the birth of the child and the end of their PPL period they are no longer eligible for PPL (unless an allowable exception reason applies) and may be eligible for a COVID-19 Disaster Payment
    • If the customer's income is affected by a state public health order for restricted movement they are eligible for a COVID-19 Disaster Payment for relevant periods where no payment overlap occurs
  • Is the customer still on the leave they started relating to the birth of a child and has not returned to work at any time since the birth of the child?
    • If the customer is still on their planned leave relating to the birth of their child, and this leave covers the entire relevant period, they are not eligible for a COVID-19 Disaster Payment.
      For definitions of leave entitlements affecting the COVID-19 Disaster Payment see:
      - New South Wales see Insufficient appropriate leave or pandemic related leave entitlements
      - Victoria (August 2021) see Leave entitlements
      - Australian Capital Territory see Leave entitlements
      - Northern Territory see Leave entitlements
      - Queensland see Leave entitlements
      - South Australia see Leave entitlements
      - Victoria (July 2021) see Leave entitlements

Note: the receipt of the COVID-19 Disaster Payment in the period between the birth of the child and the commencement of PPL is not considered to have returned to work, as they have not performed paid work.
