This page contains information about the relevant matters that must be considered by Participation Solution Team (PST)-skilled Service Officers during a work refusal failure investigation and how to finalise this investigation.

On this page:

Relevant matters for work refusal failure investigations

Making a work refusal failure decision

Relevant matters for work refusal failure investigations

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Focus of the compliance investigation + Read more ...

The compliance investigation focuses on all of the relevant facts relating to the work that the job seeker has refused and their reasons for refusing the work. These facts inform the decision on whether a work refusal failure has been committed.

The Service Officer must ensure all of the evidence required to make a good decision is identified and appropriately considered by:

  • providing a clear explanation of the work refusal failure that is under investigation, the specific matters that need to be considered to reach a decision and the potential outcomes of the decision
  • identifying the facts relevant to the non-compliance event
  • giving the job seeker the opportunity to respond to any adverse claim that have been made against them and to disclose any further information they would like considered
  • making contact with the provider or employer to assist in establishing the facts, where evidence provided by the job seeker contradicts the reported non-compliance event
  • consultation with a specialist officer, PST-skilled social worker or Indigenous Cultural Consultant maybe appropriate to establish the impact of job seeker circumstances on suitability of work or reason for refusal. See Accessing a PST-skilled social worker for information on when consultation with a PST-skilled social worker may be required or appropriate

For more information, see Refusing or failing to accept a suitable job offer.

2

Suitable work + Read more ...

A work refusal failure is only a failure under social security law if the work was suitable for the job seeker based on their circumstances at the time they refused the work. Suitability of work is based on the conditions of the position that were offered and consideration of the job seeker's circumstances such as their skills, experience, qualifications and medical history.

If the work refusal failure was reported by an Employment Services Provider, the suitability is expected to have already been considered by the provider. The PST-skilled Service Officer generally assumes that work was suitable for the job seeker based on the report from the provider unless further information provided during the investigation requires further investigation to substantiate the provider's assessment of suitability of employment. If the PST-skilled Service Officer determines that the work was unsuitable at the time the work was refused, a failure has not been committed and it should be rejected.

Job seekers who have an assessed partial capacity to work, or are a principal carer parent, are not required to accept a job offer if it involves working more than 15 hours per week.

For more information, see Suitable/unsuitable work.

3

Refusing work + Read more ...

A work refusal includes:

  • refusing an offer of suitable work
  • failing to accept an offer or suitable work, or
  • accepting an offer of suitable work, but failing to start work as planned

4

Assessing reasonable excuse + Read more ...

If work was considered suitable, the job seeker's reasons for the refusal must be considered to determine if a work refusal failure has been committed.

Where a reasonable excuse is:

  • established, a work refusal failure has not been committed
  • not established, a work refusal failure has been committed. The decision to apply a work refusal failure results in cancellation of the job seeker's participation payment and a post-cancellation non-payment period applies

All factors that significantly and directly affected the decision to refuse work are relevant matters. It is generally not reasonable for a job seeker to refuse suitable work for factors within their control.

For more information, see Assessing reasonable excuse for non-compliance with mutual obligation requirements.

Making a work refusal failure decision

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Recording the compliance investigation + Read more ...

All compliance investigations relating to work refusal failures must be recorded in the Compliance investigation workflow.

PST-skilled Service Officers must record:

  • any relevant facts identified relating to the suitability of work that were not already considered by the provider
  • any relevant facts relating to the circumstances associated with the work refusal
  • the reasons provided by the job seeker for refusing work
  • any evidence collected that supports the facts

For more information about:

  • evidence to support facts and action required when supplementary evidence is required, see Gathering information and weighing evidence to establish facts for a compliance investigation
  • recording facts and the evidence that supports the facts during a compliance investigation, see Participation Compliance workflow

2

Making the decision + Read more ...

A work refusal failure has not been committed if:

  • the customer was a ParentsNext participant at the time the work refusal occurred
  • the customer was not in receipt of a participation payment, or
  • work was not considered suitable work for the job seeker

If a job seeker was in receipt of payment and refused, failed to accept, or failed to start suitable work without a reasonable excuse, a work refusal failure has been committed. Payment is cancelled and a post-cancellation non-payment period will apply.

The PST-skilled Service Officer is required to make an evidence based decision that applies work refusal failure legislation and policy in a firm but fair manner.

The rationale used to apply the legislation to the facts that have been established must be recorded in the Compliance Investigation workflow.

If a work refusal failure has:

  • not been committed as work was unsuitable or the failure is otherwise invalid, go to Step 3
  • not been committed as the job seeker has established reasonable excuse, go to Step 4
  • been committed as a reasonable excuse has not been established, go to Step 6

3

Work refusal has not been committed + Read more ...

If a work refusal failure has not been committed, an appropriate failure rejection reason must be selected that shows why the failure was invalid and free text sent to the provider where appropriate.

For more information, see Rejecting compliance action.

If job seeker circumstances are identified, or are currently recorded but no longer impacting, these must be updated.

Go to Step 5.

4

Reasonable excuse established for work refusal + Read more ...

If a work refusal failure has been reported, consideration must be given to the job seeker's reasons for this failure.

Identify the main reason the job seeker refused suitable work and record this in the Compliance Investigation workflow.

Collect enough evidence to establish the facts on which this decision is based.

In the Compliance Investigation workflow, a decision not to apply a work refusal failure as a result of reasonable excuse would generally not be appropriate if the following responses are recorded:

  • Is this an acceptable reason that warrants further investigation: No
  • Does the job seeker's excuse indicate it had a direct and significant impact on the failure: No
  • Does the job seeker's excuse indicate the failure could have been avoided by the job seeker taking appropriate action? Yes

An appropriate failure rejection reason must be selected, the Compliance Investigation workflow will only present options relevant to the primary job seeker excuse recorded. Free text must be sent to the provider where appropriate.

If reasonable excuse is established, a financial penalty is not applied.

5

Advise job seeker of the decision not to apply a work refusal failure + Read more ...

It is important to discuss the reason for the decision with the job seeker in a way that consistently reinforces expectations that they:

  • actively seek suitable work
  • accept all offers of suitable work
  • take personal responsibility for working with their provider to find suitable work

Talking points displayed in the Compliance Investigation workflow should be used to ensure consistent messages are provided.

Note: if unable to verbally advise the decision and/or review and appeal rights, the PST-skilled Service Officers should finalise the decision in the workflow. Finalising the decision will issue a letter to the job seeker advising of the adverse decision and review and appeal rights.

Go to Step 8.

6

Reasonable excuse not established + Read more ...

If a work refusal failure has been reported, the job seeker's reasons for committing this failure must be considered.

Identify the main reason the job seeker refused suitable work and record this in the Compliance Investigation workflow. Matters that did not significantly and directly impact the job seeker's work refusal cannot be considered when assessing reasonable excuse.

An assessment of prior contact is not undertaken as part of establishing reasonable excuse. However, attempts to make contact with the provider or employer prior to the work refusal may help establish intent at the time of the failure.

Generally, matters within the job seeker's control would not be considered reasonable excuse. For more information, see Assessing reasonable excuse for non-compliance with mutual obligation requirements.

If reasonable excuse is not established, payment is cancelled and a post-cancellation non-payment period applies. These financial consequences reinforce the expectation that participation payments are only available to those who are genuine in their efforts to move into paid work.

For more information about non-compliance cancellation date of effects and the length of the post-cancellation non-payment period, see Targeted Compliance Framework financial penalties and payment cancellations.

7

Advise job seeker of the decision to apply a work refusal failure and their appeal rights + Read more ...

A decision to apply a work refusal failure, and the consequences of the decision (payment cancellation and a post cancellation non-payment period) should be verbally explained to the job seeker at the end of the compliance investigation.

Talking points presented by the Compliance Investigation workflow must be used to ensure consistent messages are communicated regarding:

  • the decision
  • how the payment cancellation will affect their participation payment, along with concession cards and other payments they are also receiving
  • their appeal rights, for further information see Non-compliance with compulsory requirements - review and appeals

If unable to verbally advise the decision and/or review and appeal rights, the PST-skilled Service Officers should finalise the decision in the workflow. Finalising the decision will issue a letter to the job seeker advising of the adverse decision and review and appeal rights.

8

Job seeker circumstances impacting compliance or crisis intervention required + Read more ...

The job seeker Circumstances impacting Compliance workflow must be updated to:

  • flag new job seeker circumstances identified during the investigation that will impact on a temporary or ongoing basis, or
  • add an end date to any job seeker circumstances that are no longer affecting

The Circumstances impacting Compliance workflow is accessed via links in the Compliance Investigation workflow.

For more information, see Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirement.

Once the compliance action is complete, if the job seeker requires immediate social work crisis intervention, a general referral may be appropriate in certain circumstances. See Social work service referral.
