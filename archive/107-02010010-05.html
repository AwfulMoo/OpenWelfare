Pursuable and non pursuable debts - Payments and debt recovery

This table describes various scenarios and payment types where debts may or may not be raised.

Item

Description

1

Excess payment - Non pursuable debts + Read more ...

Some excess payments are not legally recoverable debts. For example:

Pension Bonus Scheme (PBS)

  • If the customer was entitled to the payment at the time it was made, the debt is, not legally recoverable. These debts can be recoverable if the customer has given fraudulent or misleading information

Incentive Allowance (IA)

  • Payments to Disability Support Pension (DSP) customers made after an IA qualifying activity is completed are not legally recoverable. This is because the customers were not told to advise of changes in their IA status

Payment of Special Benefit (SpB) to minors

  • Where the parent(s) are not nominees, SpB may be paid to a child or children SpB in their own right. This mainly applies where the parent(s) are not residentially qualified for a payment. These are not legally recoverable debts. The customers (in this case, the child/ren) cannot be expected to understand the notification obligations

Youth Allowance (YA) - Payments based on parental income estimates

Identity fraud and debts

Debts may be raised as a result of incorrect income details used in the calculation of the customer's entitlement.

Where incorrect income details are due to a customer's compromised identity, the debt is an 'excess payment - no legal debt'. See Australian Tax Office (ATO) identity fraud and debts for further details.

2

Recoverable debt - Legally pursuable debts + Read more ...

A recoverable debt is an amount that is legally due and payable to the Commonwealth.

Staff raise debts under the:

  • Social Security Act 1991
  • Student Assistance Act 1973
  • Paid Parental Leave Act 2010
  • Child Care Act 1972, or
  • Family Assistance (Administration) Act 1999

Note: see Legislation for specific information about recoverable amounts.

Some examples of recoverable amounts are:

  • a payment to which a person was not entitled made under one of the Acts
  • an Advance payment which was not recovered while the customer was receiving payments
  • an Advance payment made under the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 because of severe financial hardship
  • a payment to a child care service in respect of a child who was no longer in attendance at that service
  • one of the below payments made to an individual or approved child care service which they were not entitled to:
    • family assistance
    • child care payments
  • a debt arising out of an Assurance of Support (AoS)

3

Finalised 'no debt' (FND) - Lack of information on FND activities + Read more ...

Finalise undetermined debts in DMIS by entering:

  • zero in the amount field, and
  • a reason for zeroing the debt

Where a debt is incorrectly determined as a debt and not coded as FND, zero the debt amount on the Debt Amount (OPDA) screen. A list of reasons for zeroing debts is available on OPDA.

Do not determine FTB debts which are due to changes in estimated income or maintenance income until:

  • actual income or maintenance income is known, and
  • FTB reconciliation has occurred

This does not include debts created due to adding a partner income estimate as part of a relationship status change.

FTB undetermined debts can be actioned using the FND codes.

Apply real estate reviews prospectively. Debts will accrue if a person has deliberately misrepresented the details of the real estate. This does not include a failure to advise of the increasing value in real estate holdings. The References page has a link to the Guide.

If identity fraud is the cause of a debt being raised, finalise the debt with the reason code NLD (no legal debt). This will ensure recovery does not continue, pending the outcome of the investigation.

4

Non legally recoverable debts + Read more ...

Example of a debt not legally recoverable:

  • A newly granted customer has a notifiable event occur. They have not received a 'recipient obligation notice' yet. As a result, the customer:
    • is unaware of their obligation to notify within 14 days of any change in circumstances, and
    • cannot be expected to comply with the notice

This may result in an excess payment, but no recoverable debt.

Note: where a customer lodges an online claim, they complete a declaration prior to submitting it. This declaration meets the 'recipient obligation notice' in accordance with the Act. There are no grounds to 'no debt' an excess payment prior to grant notice.

The effect of Section 99 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999

There may be no recoverable debt when a customer gets an over payment. This could be due to:

  • a change of circumstances which results in a rate reduction and
  • the customer notifies of the change correctly and
  • a lower rate is payable. The rate is not applied until after the customer is paid for the instalment period in which the change occurs (the event instalment period) and
  • the change is actioned in the next instalment period after the event instalment period. There is an excess payment but no debt for the event instalment period

ABSTUDY customers

If the customer notifies a change of circumstance within 14 days, reassess them from the date of the change. A debt for ABSTUDY:

  • may need to be raised, and
  • is not considered to be a non-recoverable debt

See General 14 day notification provisions for further information.

See also:

  • Notification provisions for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • Youth Allowance - payments based on parental income estimates
  • Youth Allowance - increase in income from 1 October

5

Youth Allowance (YA) based on parental income estimates debts + Read more ...

YA entitlement can be reassessed when an estimate differs from the verified (actual) parental income. Legislation does not support raising a debt, unless there is a failure to notify.

If an excess payment (greater than $50) is identified when updating the verified (actual) parental income within the reassessment activity, select 'INV' or 'CAL' to create an undetermined debt DMN/NDB activity. Using the Debt Action script, finalise the DMN/NDB activity as 'no debt':

  • use FND reason code excess payment EXP - no legal debt
  • complete DOC to record the FND decision

See Actioning negative adjustments on the Assessment Results (AR) screen and Finalised 'no debt' (FND) or 'zeroing' debts that are not legally recoverable.

Failure to notify

Debts for failure to notify are raised:

  • under section 1223(1) of the Social Security Act 1991
  • from the 'Date of Issue' on the Notice of Assessment (NOA)

The below provisions provide a 'date of effect' as the day on which the event or change of circumstances occur:

  • Cancellation provisions in sections 93 and 99 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999
  • Rate reduction provisions in sections 94 and 100 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999

Notes:

  • There is no legally recoverable debt prior to the first period that includes 1st January where:
    • the DMN/NDB activity was created due to the customer failing to provide verified (actual) parental income, and
    • the application of the 125% rules backdates the YA rate reduction to 1 October
  • A notification obligation exists for 7 days after the 'Date of Issue' on the Notice of Assessment (NOA). This timeframe covers postage time and expires after 21 days (7 + 14). It does not apply for YA customers who complete the declaration lodging an online claim

6

Youth Allowance (YA) - increase in income from 1 October, combined parental income exceeds 125% + Read more ...

The tax year following the base tax year is used from the first period that includes 1 October when combined parental income exceeds both:

  • 125% of the base tax year, and
  • 125% of the parental income free area

There is no legislation supporting raising a debt for these excess payments.

On the system, the coding of income that meets the increase criteria after 1 October is an excess payment.

There is no legally recoverable debt if:

  • the DMN/NDB activity is generated due to applying the tax year following 1 October, and
  • the verified (actual) parental income was lodged and coded before 31 December

Using the Debt Action script, finalise the DMN/NDB activity as 'no debt':

  • use FND reason code excess payment EXP - no legal debt
  • complete DOC to record the FND decision

See Actioning negative adjustments on the Assessment Results (AR) screen and Finalised 'no debt' (FND) or 'zeroing' debts that are not legally recoverable.

Notes:

If the customer lodges income details:

  • before 31 December but updates are not actioned until after 1 January, the system will still backdate the reassessment to 1 October. A recoverable debt my occur depending on the application of the notification rules for the customer, which can only be from the first day of the period that includes January 1. That is, when the base tax year changes
  • after 1 January and the customer did not provide income details within notification provisions, there is a legally recoverable debt from the:
    • date of the Notice of Assessment (NOA) (if after 1 January), or
    • the first day of the period that includes 1 January (if the date of the NOA is prior to 1 January)

Where this occurs, finalise the debt as a 'no debt'. Use FND reason code excess payment EXP - no legal debt.

Raise a manual debt for the recoverable component. See Create a new debt record manually on the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS).
