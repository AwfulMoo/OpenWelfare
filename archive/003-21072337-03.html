Phone contact - interpreter + Read more ...

If a customer needs an interpreter, staff should:

  • stay on the line with the customer
  • not place the customer into the queue

4

Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) or nominee contacts + Read more ...

Changes cannot be advised by a Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) or payment nominee. Unless the customer is there, can be authenticated, and gives permission for the PPE or nominee to act on their behalf, this authority will only apply to this contact.

A correspondence nominee or Person Permitted to Update (PPU) status can advise of a change in circumstances for a customer.

Before disclosing or updating any customer information check the customer's record to see if they have given the authority and what type of authority has been given:

  • For PPE/PPU -
    • if the PPE/PPU is the customer's partner check PPE/PPU authority on the Marital Status (MS) screen
    • if the PPE/PPU is not the customer's partner check Display on Access DOCs for the PPE/PPU authority
  • For Nominees - check the Nominee Link Summary (NOLS) screen for any nominee (payment or correspondence) arrangement

Do not disclose or update any information if:

  • the PPE or nominee's details cannot be confirmed as per information on the customer's record
  • there are concerns about the validity of the information provided by the third party

Record the contact:

  • Add a DOC to the customer's record by running the Fast Note workflow:
  • Location: Customer First > Workspace > Documents and Correspondence > Fast Note
  • Service Reason: select EXG - Emergency Exgratia
  • Document Completion: select Yes, otherwise the Fast Note will not close and will create a new work item
  • Extra Details to enter a title for the DOC. Type in this field COVID-19 DP PPE/NOM Query
  • Clearly document what was discussed and the person's name and contact details
  • If information could not be disclosed/updated, document why

If the person is a PPE, payment nominee, or correspondence nominee making an enquiry, procedure ends here

If the person is a correspondence nominee or a PPU, and wants to advise of a change of circumstances on behalf of a customer, and there are no concerns about the validity of information provided by the nominee, go to Step 5.

7

Change of circumstances + Read more ...

Confirm the customer has had one of the following changes in circumstance:

  • change to employment
  • any increase or decrease in hours of work compared to the last relevant period claimed (that would result in changing them from losing less than 20 to more than 20 hours of work or vice versa)
    • Note: where a state or territory has reached 80% full vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and over, the payment amount of the COVID-19 Disaster Payment changes. Check the Operational Blueprint for the event for payment amounts during these relevant periods
  • change in bank account details
    • see Payment returned because of incorrect bank details to decide if a customer has had a payment returned
  • any change in residency
  • changes in where the customer lives or works
  • no longer subject to a second public health order as a result of being present in a COVID-19 hotspot
  • receiving any of the following:
    • Parental Leave Pay
    • Dad and Partner Pay
    • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment,
    • any state based pandemic payment
    • payments for pandemic related leave entitlements from their employer
    • an income support payment, for any days in the relevant period for the COVID-19 Disaster Payment

Has one or more of the customer's circumstances above changed?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Check if a change needs recording for another payment or service + Read more ...

Is the customer advising a change in circumstance that needs to be recorded for the purposes of another payment?

  • Yes, complete that task or transfer the call to the appropriate business line. Procedure ends here
  • No, procedure ends here

9

Stop receiving automatic payments + Read more ...

Does the customer want to stop their automatic payments?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

10

Run the Fast Note and advise the customer + Read more ...

Run the Fast Note workflow:

  • Location: Customer First > Workspace > Documents and Correspondence > Fast Note
  • Service Reason: select CLI – Client from the drop down
    • Do not leave the Service Reason as 'CCD – Customer Contact Details' or any other claim type, or the auto payment will not be stopped
  • Document Completion: make sure this is set to Yes
    • Do not change this to No, or the auto payment will not be stopped
  • Select Auto text
  • From the following 3 dropdown boxes select:
    • Category 1: Emergency
    • Category 2: Claim
    • Template: COVID19 Disaster Payment CoC update
  • Select Confirm once the text in the Fast Note drops into the text box
  • Complete only the option relevant for the customer and remove the option that does not apply:
    • Change in circumstances advised by the customer (therefore auto payments need to stop)
      - complete questions under Option 1
    • Customer requests to stop auto payments - complete questions under Option 2

The system will apply the change based on the keyword already attached to the Fast Note. Staff do not need to take further action to stop future automatic payments.

Tell the customer:
'The system will update overnight and stop any future payments from the next day. This may result in an overpayment and you may be contacted at a later date to discuss this. You will need to manually lodge a new claim for any future relevant period.'

Note: lodging a change in circumstances does not affect the next claim a customer lodges. If staff are allocated an online claim lodged by a customer who has just lodged a change in circumstances, they will assess the claim as they would any other online claim. Future automatic payments will change to align with information in the new online claim.

11

Has an automatic payment been issued incorrectly? + Read more ...

An incorrect issue of an automatic payment occurs when:

  • a customer advises of a change in circumstances, that means they:
    • are not entitled to the payment but the automatic payment has already been issued, or
    • received the incorrect rate of automatic payment
  • staff identify online claim details do not match a:
    • payment amount received, for example, a customer should have been paid $750 (20 hours or more) but was paid $450 (less than 20 hours), or
    • state or territory claimed, for example, a claim should have been paid for Victoria (August 2021) but was paid for New South Wales, or
    • relevant area claimed, for example, a customer is paid for New South Wales Greater Sydney relevant period 3 but should have been paid for Sydney relevant period 3

In Customer First, check the customer's Document List (DL) screen.

If a customer has already received an automatic payment, the DOC titled CUS CLM EXG FSP, will show AUTO as the Source.

Check the claim to make sure:

  • it matches:
    • payment amount received
    • state or territory claimed
    • relevant area claimed
  • the customer was eligible for the event the system automatically paid them for

Has the customer received an automatic payment and should not have, or received the incorrect rate of automatic payment (this includes where claim details do not match a payment amount received, state or territory claimed or relevant area claimed)?

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, procedure ends here.

12

Automatic payment was issued incorrectly + Read more ...

An automatic payment was issued incorrectly:

  • the customer must request an explanation of the decision, if they:
    • did not advise of a change in circumstances within 14 days of the change occurring, and
    • should have been paid the higher rate of COVID-19 Disaster Payment, for example, the customer was paid $450 but should have been paid $750
    • see COVID-19 Disaster Payment - review process
  • for any other reason from Step 11, see Table 2 (noting that the COVID19 Disaster Payment CoC Update Fast Note does not need to be completed again if it was completed in Step 10 above)

Do not follow this table if a:

Follow this table when there is an error with an automatic payment.

Errors with an automatic payment occur when a customer has already received an automatic payment and:

  • staff identify online claim details do not match a
    • payment amount received, for example, a customer should have been paid $750 (20 hours or more) but was paid $450 (less than 20 hours), or
    • state or territory, for example, a claim should have been paid for Victoria (August 2021) but was paid for New South Wales, or
    • relevant area claimed, for example, a customer is paid for New South Wales Greater Sydney relevant period 3 but should have been paid for Sydney relevant period 3
      Note: see Locating an online claim in Assessing COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims or Locating details in an online claim in Assessing income support customers - COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims for information on how to determine the above points
  • after a customer has advised of a change in circumstances, an automatic payment should not have been issued or they received the incorrect rate of automatic payment

    • Document Completion: make sure this is set to Yes
      - Do not       change this to No, or the auto payment will not be stopped
    • Select Auto text

      Category 1: Emergency
      Category 2: Claim
      Template: COVID19 Disaster Payment CoC update
    • Select Confirm once the text in the Fast Note drops into the text box
  • Complete only the option relevant to the customer and remove the option that does not apply:

6

Customer received the correct amount but was paid for the wrong event or relevant period + Read more ...

For example, customer was paid the:

  • $750 rate for relevant period 6 for a relevant area in Sydney, but should have been paid the $750 rate for relevant period 6 for a relevant area in Greater Sydney
    • Relevant periods for Sydney and Greater Sydney do not align, even though the customer was paid the correct rate of payment
  • $200 income support payment rate for relevant period 1 Australian Capital Territory, but should have been paid the $200 income support payment rate for relevant period 2 Victoria (August 2021)

Contact Technical Peer Support who will complete a payment query template.

Note: the Level 2 Helpdesk will determine if any automatic payments need to stop.

  • Select Save (top left), or the update to Completed will not occur
