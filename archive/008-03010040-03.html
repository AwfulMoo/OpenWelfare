Transferring to DSP (Blind)

Step

Action

1

Permanent blindness + Read more ...

Does the customer meet the definition of permanent blindness?

If unsure, contact Disability Technical Peer Support (TPS) line for advice.

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, customer cannot transfer from their current benefit to DSP (Blind). Procedure ends here

2

Is the customer current on DSP? + Read more

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the customer cannot transfer from their current benefit to DSP (Blind). They will need to apply for DSP on the basis of permanent blindness. They will be subject to normal eligibility criteria, but without a continuing inability to work (CITW) requirement. Procedure ends here

3

Ophthalmologist report or an Association for the Blind report + Read more

Does the customer have a medical report from an Optometrist that refers to an Ophthalmologist report, or an Association for the Blind report that refers to an Ophthalmologist report?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, issue the Request for Ophthalmologist Report (SA013) form. Record a manual follow-up for its return. Procedure ends here

4

Assessment of medical papers + Read more

On receipt of the medical form or report, does the customer qualify for DSP (Blind)?

For help, see Assessing permanent blindness.

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the customer cannot transfer from DSP to DSP (Blind). Advise them via a Free Text (Q999) letter and record on a DOC why their claim was rejected. Procedure ends here

5

Customer qualifies for DSP (Blind) + Read more

A customer does not need to disclose their income and assets if they are in receipt of DSP (Blind), except for the following reasons:

  • They wish to receive more than the basic pension and the pension supplement. Note: the DSP rate for customers who are blind is not affected by the income and assets tests unless they are claiming Rent Assistance (RA)
  • They have a partner who is on or applying for an income support payment (other than Blind). Details of customer’s income and assets are needed to determine their partner’s eligibility

6

Transfer to DSP (Blind) + Read more

Action the transfer.

While in the customer's record, go to the Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen to update the Blind status. Code the following fields:

  • Event Date: (for CITW/Blind Residence) is the date the customer requested the transfer to Blind or the date of eligibility, whichever is later
  • CITW/Blind Residence:
  • 'AUS' if a continuing inability to work while in Australia
  • 'OVE' if a continuing inability to work while overseas
  • Event Date: (for the other blind status fields) is also the date the customer requested the transfer to Blind or the date of eligibility, whichever is later
  • Blind Ind: 'Y'es
  • Blind - Income & Assets Exempt:
  • 'Y'es, for income and assets not declared. The pension will be limited to the basic rate plus supplement
  • 'N'o, for income and assets declared. If the customer wishes to claim an additional payment, for example Rent Assistance, or if they have a partner also on a payment who is not blind, then income and assets need to be declared. Check the Accommodation (AC) screen has current information recorded and obtain verification of rent details if required. The customer will receive income and asset reviews just like any other pensioner
  • Source: and DOR:
  • Action: 'I'
  • Severely Disabled Code: 'Y'es

7

Code the MC screen + Read more

Code 'BLI' in the Manifest code field. The Event date should be the same as the one coded on the PDI screen.

  • This will stop the issue of a medical review
  • Some customers may be able to get medical assistance that could possibly change the status of 'blind' for social security purposes. For example, the customer may have cataracts but choose not to have any medical assistance even though this may help their sight. In these cases, do not code 'BLI'

Finalise activity and record the details on a DOC.

The Assessment Results (AR) screen may not indicate the transfer to DSP (Blind) has occurred. When the activity is finalised the Benefit Status Line will show DSB/CUR.

Advise the customer they can choose to receive their letters from Centrelink in an alternative format.
