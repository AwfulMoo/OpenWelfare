General alternative assistance options + Read more ...

Suggestions for alternative assistance options to explore with customers include but are not limited to:

  • Assess for weekly payments
  • Make a Centrepay arrangement to make regular repayments
  • Referral to a welfare agency. Note: welfare agencies have limited capacity and resources. Consider other options first
  • Referral to Employment Service Provider for costs associated with looking for or commencing employment (including attending job interviews)
  • Explore whether the doctor/local hospital/pharmacy can supply medication/treatment for free, supply samples or allow the customer to pay the account on payday
  • Referral to a state or local authority
  • Negotiation with the creditor to extend due date of account and/or repayments
  • Hire of equipment, for example refrigerator, rather than purchase
  • Fares Allowance (FAA) for Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy and Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) students
  • Referral to a financial/budget counsellor
  • Referral to a Community Engagement Officer (where appropriate)
  • Referral to help build financial capability
  • Advance payment. Take care due to the:
    • limited number of advances some customers can access, and
    • minimum amount of an advance may be more than the customer needs
  • Crisis Payment
  • Change of payday code to better suit budget requirements or offset (where appropriate). Caution must be taken as this may also cause hardship. A change of payday code should not be used to issue an urgent payment when the customer is not eligible
  • Check that customer is receiving their correct entitlements and if they may be entitled to other payments/concessions

2

Referral to a social worker + Read more ...

A referral to a social worker can be considered for customers presenting with highly complex needs in line with the social work priority groups, including:

  • customers at risk of suicide or self-harm
  • young people without adequate support
  • customers experiencing family and domestic violence including assessment for a Crisis Payment due to family and domestic violence

Repeated requests for urgent payments may indicate that a customer is having difficulty managing their finances. Consider referring the customer to an Authorised Weekly Payment Officer (AWPO) or an external financial counselling or budgeting support service.

Consider any social worker recommendations on the customer record. However, the delegation to grant or reject urgent payments remains with the Service Officer. They must ensure they are following the correct procedures in approving any urgent payment.

To reduce the risk of the customer having unrealistic expectations, be clear about why they are being referred and the role of the social worker. A social worker is unable to change the urgent payment decision. They offer support and assistance for customers presenting with highly complex needs.

If a social worker referral is appropriate:

  • refer to customer to the Social Work Line via a warm handoff
  • if unable to contact a social worker, complete a Fast Note. Select Auto text. Use Emergency > Urgent Payment > Referral to SWO due to UP assessment

For more information, see:

  • Social work service referral
  • Referral to external support services

4

Locating external assistance information + Read more ...

If the Payment and Service Finder states the agency requires a Welfare Agency Referral (with the customer's permission), the Service Officer should make 2 attempts to contact the agency and enquire if the referral details can be provided:

  • verbally
  • by fax, or
  • via the customer

If unable to contact the agency advise the customer to attend the nearest service centre to obtain a referral form before attending the agency.

Alternative assistance based on types of payment

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Advance payments + Read more ...

Urgent payment is not available for advance payments of an income support payment or Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

These advance payments should be made into the customer's regular payment account via direct credit only. This will take 1 to 2 working days.

Alternative assistance options include referral to a welfare agency. Note: welfare agencies have limited capacity and resources. Consider other options first.

2

Family Tax Benefit (FTB) + Read more ...

FTB is not an eligible payment for an urgent payment, except if making a payment due to Centrelink delay.

Alternative assistance options include:

  • Referral to a welfare agency. Note: welfare agencies have limited capacity and resources. Consider other options first
  • Changing their regular payday to better suit budget requirements
  • Offsetting Parenting Payment and FTB paydays, resulting in a payday each week
  • Testing eligibility for Carer Allowance and Carer Payment for babies with special needs

3

Fares Allowance (tertiary students) + Read more ...

Urgent payment is not available for Fares Allowance (tertiary student) payments.

Payment for Fares Allowance (tertiary student) payments must be made into the customer's payment account via the system. This will take 1 to 2 working days.

Alternative assistance options include referral to a welfare agency. Note: welfare agencies have limited capacity and resources. Consider other options first.

4

Lump sum arrears + Read more ...

Urgent payment is not available for lump sum arrears payments.

Payment of lump sum arrears must be made into the customer's payment account via the system. This will take 1 to 2 working days.

Alternative assistance options include referral to a welfare agency. Note: welfare agencies have limited capacity and resources. Consider other options first.

5

Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA) advance + Read more ...

Urgent payment is available for PhA advance payments if:

  • alternative assistance options have been exhausted, and
  • the customer has not received 2 urgent payments in the previous 12 months, and
  • the medical need is life threatening, or
  • in the case of an emergency

Where urgent payment is not available, PhA advance payments are made into the customer's payment account via the system. This takes 1 to 2 working days.

Alternative assistance options include:

  • Negotiating with the pharmacy to extend due date of account and/or repayments
  • Investigating whether the customer can hire the equipment until their next payday
  • Referral to a welfare agency. Note: welfare agencies have limited capacity and resources. Consider other options first

6

Weekly payments + Read more ...

Urgent payment is not available for customers receiving weekly payments.

Consider other alternative assistance options, as per the customer’s circumstances.

Baby needs including baby formula, nappies and baby capsule + Read more ...

Urgent payment is available if the baby is born with special needs or prematurely and:

  • alternative assistance options have been exhausted, and
  • the customer has not received 2 urgent payments in the previous 12 months

Alternative assistance options include:

  • negotiating with the pharmacy to extend the due date of the account and/or repayments
  • hiring baby capsules until the customer can purchase or until it is no longer required
  • testing eligibility for Carer Allowance and Carer Payment for babies with special needs

2

Bond and utility connection fees + Read more ...

Urgent payment is available for these costs in exceptional circumstances where:

  • alternative assistance options have been exhausted, and
  • the customer has not received 2 payments in the previous 12 months

Alternative assistance options include:

  • Referral to State Housing to test eligibility for Bond Assistance
  • Negotiating with the utility company to include the cost of connection fee in the first account
  • Paying future utility fees via Centrepay
  • The customer can arrange with their bank for future utility costs to be direct debited from their account

3

Car repairs, maintenance, registration, licence or petrol costs + Read more ...

Urgent payment is available if:

  • alternative assistance options have been exhausted, and
  • the customer has not received 2 urgent payments in the previous 12 months, and
  • is not eligible for a Special Employment Advance, and
  • the customer:
    • has no access to public transport
    • requires the car for work or to start a confirmed job

Alternative assistance options include:

  • Negotiating repayments with the business or an extension to the account due date.
  • Referral to a welfare agency. Note: welfare agencies have limited capacity and resources. Consider other options first
  • Offsetting Parenting Payment and Family Tax Benefit paydays, resulting in a payday each week
  • Changing their regular payday to better suit budget requirements
  • For Indigenous customers in New South Wales (NSW) attending a funeral in NSW (only), see the Resources page for a link to the Transport for NSW, Transport for Aboriginal communities page

4

Cost associated with looking for accommodation including moving expenses + Read more ...

Urgent payment is available if:

  • alternative assistance options have been exhausted, and
  • the customer has not received 2 urgent payments in the previous 12 months, and
  • the customer:
    • was evicted without due legal notice, or
    • had to move due to an exceptional circumstance (e.g. house became uninhabitable)

Alternative assistance options include:

  • Testing eligibility for Crisis Payment
  • If evicted illegally, referral to tenant’s advice service
  • Referral to a welfare agency. Note: Welfare agencies have limited capacity and resources. Consider other options first
  • Referral to temporary accommodation
  • Referral to State Housing Department that may provide assistance with bond, moving costs etc.
  • Changing their regular payday to better suit budget requirements

If it is a regular request:

  • Referral to financial/budget counsellor
  • Discuss weekly payments with the customer

5

Eviction due to failure to pay rent or where due legal notice was given + Read more ...

Urgent payment is available if:

  • alternative assistance options have been exhausted, and
  • the customer has not received 2 urgent payments in the previous 12 months, and
  • eviction was beyond the customer's control, or
  • the customer was evicted without due legal notice

Alternative assistance options include:

  • Referral to Tenancy Advice Service or Tenants' Union.
  • Negotiating with the landlord or bank to extend the due date or stop the eviction
  • Referral to Temporary Accommodation
  • Changing their regular payday to better suit budget requirements

If it is a regular request:

  • Refer to financial/budget counsellor
  • Refer to Weekly Payments Officer or discuss weekly payments with the customer if the staff member is trained in this

Regular accommodation costs such as rent, mortgage payments or rent arrears + Read more ...

Urgent payment is available for these costs in exceptional circumstances if:

  • alternative assistance options have been exhausted, and
  • the customer has not received 2 urgent payments in the previous 12 months

An example of exceptional circumstances is where the customer has used funds that were reserved for rental costs due to an exceptional and unforeseen circumstance such as a medical emergency and no longer have the funds to pay the rent.

Alternative assistance options include:

  • Test entitlement to Rent Assistance
  • Negotiating with landlord, real estate agency or bank to extend due date
  • Referral to State Housing Department to see if any assistance is available
  • Changing their regular payday to better suit budget requirements
  • Offsetting Parenting Payment and Family Tax Benefit paydays, resulting in a payday each week
  • Referral to a welfare agency. Note: welfare agencies have limited capacity and resources. Consider other options first
  • Paying future rent via Centrepay
  • The customer can arrange with their bank for future rent to be direct debited from their account

If it is a regular request:

  • Refer to financial/budget counsellor
  • Refer to Weekly Payments Officer or discuss weekly payments with the customer if the staff member is trained in this

Purchase fridge or other white goods or furniture + Read more ...

Urgent payment is available for these expenses if:

  • alternative assistance options have been exhausted, and
  • the customer has not received 2 urgent payments in the previous 12 months, and
  • the expenses are:
  • required for medical needs, or
  • due to unforeseen circumstance (e.g. customer has ceased working)

Urgent payment is not available if the customer is upgrading white goods.

Alternative assistance options include:

  • Suggesting the customer investigate purchase of second-hand goods through local welfare agencies etc.
  • Negotiating with the business to extend the due date and/or repayments
  • Investigating hiring the goods until the customer can purchase the item
  • Offsetting Parenting Payment and Family Tax Benefit paydays, resulting in a payday each week
  • Referral to No Interest Loans (NILs), see Helping customers to build their financial capability

If it is a regular request, refer to financial/budget counsellor.

15

Stolen or lost wallet or purse + Read more ...

Urgent payment is available for these costs if:

  • alternative assistance options have been exhausted, and
  • the customer has not received 2 urgent payments in the previous 12 months, and
  • the urgent payment will not cause or intensify financial hardship

Discuss with the customer how much money was stolen/lost and what they had intended to use it for:

  • food and general living expenses - refer to a welfare agency
  • mortgage payments - negotiate with the landlord or bank to extend due date
  • bill payments - negotiate with creditor to extend the due date

Alternative assistance options include offsetting Parenting Payment and Family Tax Benefit paydays, resulting in a payday each week.

If it is a regular request:

  • Refer to financial/budget counsellor
  • Refer to Weekly Payments Officer or discuss weekly payments with the customer if the staff member is trained in this

Urgent payment is not available for regular veterinary expenses + Read more ...

This includes

  • vaccinations
  • regular medication
  • birth of puppies

Alternative assistance options include:

  • Negotiating with the veterinary practice to enter a payment schedule
  • If the practice uses Centrepay, entering a payment arrangement

If the request is due to an emergency situation, where without veterinary intervention the pet's life would be threatened, this request may be considered.
