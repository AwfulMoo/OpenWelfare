This document outlines information about the Paraplegic and Quadriplegic (ParaQuad) program. The program provides certain medicines free of charge to members of authorised associations.

On this Page:

Administration of ParaQuad

The Department of Health and Aged Care is responsible for the legislative arrangement for the Paraplegic and Quadriplegic Program including the approval of authorised associations and listing of drugs and medicinal preparations. Services Australia has responsibility for the registration of authorised associations and the processing and payment of claims lodged by the authorised associations.

Authorised (ParaQuad) associations are permitted to supply certain pharmaceutical items listed in Schedule 1 of Special Arrangement which are also on the Schedule of Pharmaceutical Benefits to their members. Currently 3 associations are listed in the Special Arrangement, 2 of which participate in the :

  • Paraplegic and Quadriplegic Association of NSW, and
  • Independence Australia (previously known as The Paraplegic Association of Victoria (ParaQuad Victoria))

The Resources page provides contact details for the associations.

Program overview

The ParaQuad Program provides access to certain medicines (enemas and aperients) on the Schedule of Pharmaceutical Benefits free of charge to members of Authorised associations.

Under the program, a Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) prescription is not required and there is no patient co-payment. The medication supply is for a maximum per month that is slightly higher than that permitted for general supply on the PBS.

Authorised associations purchase medications directly from manufacturers and distribute to their members mostly by post. Authorised associations submit claims to Services Australia for the cost of the medicine. A 2.75% handling fee is paid on the total value of the claim. GST is not payable on any items listed on the ParaQuad schedule. GST is only payable on the 2.75% handling fee.

Legislation

The Paraplegic and Quadriplegic Program is legislated under a special arrangement made under subsection 100(1) of the National Health Act 1953 - National Health (Paraplegic and Quadriplegic Program) Special Arrangements 2010. See the References page for a link.

Eligible persons

A person is eligible to receive a pharmaceutical benefit from an authorised association if the person:

  • is an eligible person within the meaning of the Health Insurance Act 1973; and
  • has paraplegia or quadriplegia; and
  • is a member of the association

ParaQuad claims processing

Processing of ParaQuad and Stoma Appliance Scheme (SAS) claims are the same. The ParaQuad program uses the same application forms as SAS and can be ordered from The Camerons Group. The Resources page provides a link.

For information on processing Stoma Appliance Scheme claims see Stoma Appliance Scheme (SAS).
