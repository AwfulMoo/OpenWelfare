Notification of the death of a single pensioner

Step

Action

1

Confirm if death has previously been notified + Read more ...

A Service Officer may either receive:

  • a work item if the death has previously been notified but unable to be finalised at first point, or
  • multiple contacts from different parties notifying the death

Has the death action been taken on the deceased customer's record?

  • Yes, no further action is required. Procedure ends here
  • No, and:
    • this is the initial advice received via phone or lodgement of an Advice of death form (SA116), go to Step 2
    • the death has not been confirmed yet and an additional person has contacted to notify of the death, every effort should be made to confirm the death at this contact, go to Step 2
    • any outstanding activities which were preventing the death action have now been finalised, go to Step 7
    • a Death Notified but not confirmed - Adult or Child work item has been received, contact the funeral director or the hospital where the deceased passed away to confirm the death. If this is not an option and contact with relatives is proposed, social worker involvement may be necessary. Once the death has been confirmed, go to Step 7

2

Notification of the death of a customer + Read more ...

When a person contacts Services Australia to advise of the death of a customer, staff must offer:

  • a social worker referral in the first instance or at any time the person shows signs of distress during the contact
  • the pamphlet 'Needing help after someone has died?' (HD001) if the person is attending a service centre, and/or direct them to 'Death of a loved one' on the Services Australia website. See Resources page for the link

Consider and respect the following cultural issues when being advised of the death of an Indigenous customer.

  • The name of the deceased person cannot be mentioned by immediate family members. This means they may have to ask other relatives/friends to call on their behalf or attend the interview with the customer
  • It may also be offensive to the person for the Service Officer to say the name of the deceased customer. If unsure, sensitively ask the person if it is all right to use the deceased customer's name. If the person is not comfortable with this, avoid using the deceased customer's name wherever possible. See Cultural Considerations when someone dies

Does the customer's record indicate they were income managed, as shown by INM on the benefit status line?

  • Yes, follow process to update record for death of an income managed customer, then return to this procedure and go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Confirm the death of the deceased + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure in the deceased customer’s record to determine if death can be confirmed.

Confirmation can be accepted where the deceased customer's full name and date of death is provided along with 2 of the following:

  • deceased customer's last address in full
  • date of birth
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)

Confirmation of death also requires the following information about the informant:

  • name
  • address
  • phone number

Confirm if the person notifying of the death is the executor of the estate. If the informant is not the executor, but knows the executor details, obtain:

  • the name and address of the executor, and
  • the name of the funeral director or hospital

Nominee arrangements and Power of Attorney (POA) stop at the time of death. Any correspondence will be sent to the executor or administrator of an estate. This includes the payment summary and possible debt recovery action.

If the executor or administrator details are incorrectly recorded or not recorded at all when details have been provided, this will impact the agency's ability to finalise any outstanding matters.

If executor details are not known when notifying the death, leave blank the executor details fields in the Notification of Death guided procedure. The details can be recorded later on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen after completing the death action.

Can death be confirmed at first contact?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Death cannot be confirmed + Read more ...

Using the Notification of Death guided procedure obtain and record all relevant information from the informant including their own name, address, contact number and relationship to the deceased, and if known, details of the executor, the name of the funeral director, and if applicable the name of the hospital.

The guided procedure will not code the death, but will generate a DOC on the record which is an open work item to be allocated to a Service Officer with the required skill set to confirm the death.

If the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item. Use Fast Note. Select Auto Text use > Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED.

The guided procedure presents a suspension option if the death is unable to be confirmed:

  • use the reason code 'OTH' (Other) the date of effect should be the unconfirmed date of death or date paid to plus one (DPT+1), whichever is the later
  • ensure the DOC meets Online Document Recording (ODR) standards for significant decisions and including what action is required to confirm the death
  • do not issue a suspension letter. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension
  • if the guided procedure is not available or the suspension option is not presented, suspend the deceased customer’s payments manually

When the death is confirmed, go to Step 5.

5

Outstanding activities + Read more ...

A death action cannot be processed with activities on the Activity List (AL) screen. The Notification of death guided procedure will prompt if there is an activity on the AL screen.

Note: if a deceased customer’s record was suspended while the death was being confirmed and their death has now been confirmed, manually check for started activities on the AL screen.

Where possible ensure any activities are completed and run the daily driver (DDR):

  • if there is a 'STA' debt activity on the AL screen, create an open work item for region XWA advising them of the death and requesting the debt activity be completed. Use Fast Note. Select Auto Text use > Debt > DMB Raising > Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request. See Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer
  • if there is an RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM activity on the AL screen, add the keyword RCAPRI. The RCA team will action the activity and complete the death action
  • for other outstanding activities, immediately contact the team or Service Officer who needs to complete the outstanding activity - if unsure who to contact, refer to existing escalation channels

Have any started activities been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

When it is not possible to complete an activity + Read more ...

If there is an outstanding activity that the Service Officer is unable to complete, suspend the deceased customer's payment if not already suspended.

The suspension option is not available in the Notification of death guided procedure if there is an activity preventing the death action:

  • manually suspend using the reason 'OTH' (other) pending completion of the outstanding activity
  • the suspension date of effect should be the later of:
    • date of death, or
    • date paid to plus one (DPT + 1)
  • record a DOC on the customer’s record, ensuring that it meets Online Document Recording (ODR) minimum standards for significant decisions and including what action must occur so the death can be actioned
  • as the customer’s death has been confirmed, no letter must be sent. It is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension

If an open DOC/work item regarding the death notification has not already been created, either:

  • run the Notification of death guided procedure to generate an open work item for the relevant staff to complete the death action once the outstanding activities have been completed, or
  • if the guided procedure is not available create an open work item using Fast Note. Select Auto Text use > Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED

When activity is completed, go to Step 7.

7

Check if restoration is needed + Read more ...

Is payment suspended?

  • Yes, restore payment if suspended:
    • run the daily driver (DDR) if finalised activities are still displaying on the AL screen
    • single pensioners are entitled to the final payment for the entitlement period in which they died
    • make sure that no arrears are issued other than the final payment, unless it has already been paid
    • inhibit the restoration advice on the AR screen. It is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Was the deceased customer due to report + Read more ...

Was the deceased customer due to report, or lodge a reporting statement?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 11

9

Deceased customer was due to report + Read more ...

Are the deceased customer's employment income details for the reporting period in which they passed away known, or can the person notifying of the death provide the customer's employment income details for the last reporting period?

  • Yes:
    • Using the Earnings and Reporting tool, process the reporting statement. Note: the date of lodgement on the Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen may need to be changed first to the day before the date of death. For more information see Changing Entitlement Period End Dates (EPED)
    • Go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Stimulate the payment + Read more ...

Stimulate the payment using the Earnings and reporting workflow tool or manually coding ‘Y’ on the Report Results (RR) screen.

Take the deceased customer off reporting if possible, by updating the Reporting Regime Details (RPRD) screen.

11

Check if child out of care needs coding + Read more ...

Was the customer receiving any of the following payments:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) instalments
  • Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • Ancillary benefits for a Regular Care Child
  • Paid Parental Leave (PPL)
  • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
    • Yes, code the child out of care. See Step 5 and 6 in Notification of death of family assistance or Paid Parental Leave scheme customer table. If the activity submits to QOL, request an urgent QOL check so the death action can be finalised
      Once completed, continue with this process
    • No, go to Step 12

12

Record death action on the system + Read more ...

To record the death:

  • use the Notification of Death guided procedure
  • include details of the executor of the estate, if known

Effect on deceased customer's payment:

  • A final payment will be made that covers the full entitlement period in which the customer died
  • If the customer's date of death is the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED), there is no further payment after the EPED
  • Any payment made after the first EPED in which the death occurred is an overpayment

Will the death be actioned before the first EPED after death?

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No, the death will be actioned after the first EPED after death, go to Step 14

13

Death actioned before the first EPED after death + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure to complete the following coding on the record. If the guided procedure is not available manually code the death action:

  • finalise activity via the Assessment Result (AR) screen
  • record the details of the bereavement on a DOC, including how notification was received and from whom (the guided procedure will automatically include this in the doc)
  • check the AL screen on the deceased customer’s record for any RCA/MFU activity for income support payment to be cancelled and cancel this MFU
  • consider the following:
    • identify any service providers to be advised of the death
    • offer social worker referral if appropriate

Procedure ends here.

14

Death actioned after the first EPED after death + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure to complete the following coding on the record. If the guided procedure is not available manually code the death action.

After recording the Death/Executor (DEA) screen the Assessment Result (AR) screen will display.

A negative adjustment may be displayed if payments were made after the customer's death to which the estate is not entitled.

Finalise activity via the AR screen:

  • record the details of the bereavement on a DOC, including how notification was received and from whom
  • check the AL screen on the deceased customer’s record for any RCA/MFU activity for income support payment to be cancelled and cancel this MFU
  • consider the following:
    • identify any service providers to be advised of the death
    • offer social worker referral if appropriate
