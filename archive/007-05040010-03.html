For Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) staff only

Confirm the current employer details and any notifiable events using the Employer Details page in Customer First and discuss the change with the employer.

Note: a resignation, termination of employment or return to work can be advised up to 30 days in advance, but a system limitation will not currently allow coding of a future date on the employee record. Staff should continue to accept any advance information (up to 30 days) provided by the employer, and complete any follow up to enable the change. Record details on a DOC, and code a future review for the date of the termination, or resignation, or return to work. When the review becomes due, update the change in circumstances using the PPL circumstances guided procedure.

If the employer:

  • is advising that their employee has resigned or been terminated and the date is in the past or within the next 30 days, go to Step 7

The workflow will allow changes to the employer details required for the delivery of Parental Leave Pay to an employee. These changes can be made before and after the start of the PPL period. Go to Step 15.

5

Employer advises employee has returned to work or performed work on a day they received PPL + Read more ...

For Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) staff only

Check whether the customer’s return to work details have already been notified and updated.

If the return to work has already been notified and updated, and the return to work date was:

  • before the end of the customer’s PPL period, their PPL period will cancel (reason: RTW) from the return to work date. Any connected Flexible PPL days will be returned to the customer’s balance of unclaimed Flexible PPL days
  • during the customer’s connected Flexible PPL days, the day scheduled on the return to work date will be rejected (reason: NWF) and any subsequent Flexible PPL days will be rejected

The customer’s:

  • PPL claim status will be cancelled (reason: RTW), if their child was born or adopted before 1 July 2020
  • PPL period status will be cancelled or rejected (reason: RTW), if their child was born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020

Flexible PPL days can be accessed before and after the customer has returned to work. However, they must not be performing paid work on the days they want to access Flexible PPL. If the customer returns to work or performs work on a Flexible PPL day, the day will:

  • be rejected (reason WOF), and
  • returned to their balance of unclaimed days

Note: employers are only required to deliver Flexible PPL days that are connected to the customer’s PPL period.

See Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days.

Has the return to work already been recorded?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

Discuss the circumstances + Read more ...

For Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) staff only

Obtain the following information from the employer:

  • employee's name
  • date of return to work
  • date of days the employee performed paid work
  • reason for the return to work - i.e. was it for an allowable exception reason

Employers can advise a customer has returned to work for an allowable reason.

Employers advising a return to work change of circumstance are presented with:

  • Return to Work for allowable reason field and asked to select a reason from the dropdown list:
    • Does not apply
    • Health/Emergency/Other Essential Worker
    • Defence Force/Law Enforcement Worker
    • Court Ordered Summons

Employers are not presented with all exception reasons in the dropdown list due to maintaining the employee’s privacy for sensitive circumstances. See Parental Leave Pay (PPL) customer returns to work.

This may have been advised via Business Hub, CBOS, verbally or in writing.

If evidence has been received from the employer, attach the evidence to the employer's record.

Keeping in touch

Employees can access up to 10 keeping in touch (KIT) days that allow them to transition back to employment. These days can be accessed:

  • before the customer returns to work for the first time following their child’s birth or adoption, and
  • during the PPL period or any Flexible PPL days

For more information about what qualifies as a KIT day, see Parental Leave Pay customer returns to work.

Discuss with employer the details surrounding the work performed, including if KIT discussions have occurred between the employer and the employee.

Both the employee and the employer must agree on KIT days and either party can refuse to participate. KIT days allow employees to perform a day of regular duties, up to ten (10 days) for the purpose of transitioning back to employment.

This can include:

  • performing regular duties
  • training
  • refreshing their skills, or
  • to become familiar with the workplace or role

It does not include performing regular duties for other purposes, such as filling in for an absent staff member or to earn additional income.

Note: in the instance where an employee suggests to perform a KIT day, it should not be performed within 14 days after the day the child was born. If the employer requests the employee perform a KIT day, this must not be within 42 days after the date of birth of the child.

Following discussion of the employee’s circumstances, ask the employer to confirm they have incorrectly advised that the employee has returned to work or performed work.

Has the employer confirmed they incorrectly advised that the employee has returned to work or performed work (for example, KIT and work for permissible purpose occurring)?

  • Yes, go to Step 1 in the Evidence provided incorrectly table
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Contact the employee + Read more ...

For Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) staff only

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the employee using all contact numbers.

If contact is:

  • unsuccessful, run the PPL circumstances guided procedure and update the return to work as advised by the employer:
    • If the employer has advised that the customer has/will perform work on day, update via the Flexible guided procedure within the PPL Circumstances guided procedure
    • Any Flexible PPL days scheduled after the return to work date (including those connected to the end of their PPL period) do not automatically reject when the return to work is recorded. This is because Flexible PPL days can be claimed before and after a return to work. Check these days to ensure the customer is not working on the scheduled days
    • Use the PPL circumstances guided procedure to update the return to work. See Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days
    • DOC the attempts made and advice from employer. Procedure ends here
  • successful, discuss if return to work has occurred:
    • If employee confirms the advice by the employer, run the PPL circumstances guided procedure to record the change. For running the PPL circumstances guided procedure, go to Step 14
    • If the employee disagrees with the advice from the employer (including date of return to work or that an exception applies), follow up action is required. Discuss why the employee does not agree and request the employee to provide evidence within 14 days. Go to Step 8

8

Employee disagrees with the advice from the employer + Read more ...

For Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) staff only

  • The PPLEPT must contact the employer to discuss and request evidence if not previously provided
  • If the employer is unable to be contacted, send a Q999 to request evidence

Evidence could include:

  • For termination, a termination letter
  • For return to work:
    • exchange of emails confirming return to work, or work
    • KIT arrangements
    • timesheets/attendance record, or
    • payslips

For example, an employer and employee might have an exchange of emails to agree on:

  • using a KIT day
  • what paid work activity will be undertaken
  • the timing and duration of the paid work

Note: employers do not need to keep formal records of KIT activities. However, it is considered best practice to have a record of the agreed arrangements in place before the KIT activity occurs.

Creating a task in Process Direct to enable the employer to upload documents in Business Hub + Read more ...

For employers to upload a document in Business Hub after a task must be initiated by the Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) staff must:

  • select the Business Hub Request Documents tile
  • search for the organisation. Note: CRN (CRN must be entered as 10 consecutive characters with no spaces, for example, 408XXXXXXA)
  • select the organisation
  • select Claimants of PPL
  • select the relevant employee from the column on the left hand side
  • select Tasks
  • select Request evidence for employee’s return to work or termination
  • update the Authority to request: Paid Parental Leave Act 2010
  • update the Information to be supplied: 14 days
  • update the Required for : Incomplete Application
  • select:
    • Termination
    • Resignation, or
    • Return to work
  • Select right arrow
  • select Add as no additional details are required
  • select Finish

Note: evidence is not required from either the employer or employee when the customer has returned to work for an allowable exception reason. Evidence can only be requested if the Service Officer is not satisfied with the information provided.

After the task is initiated:

  • an outstanding task is generated for the employer in Business Hub
  • an email notification is sent to the employer advising that the organisation can provide the requested documents through the Upload Requested Documents tile in Business Hub

Reviewing Evidence + Read more ...

Review the evidence, if it is determined that the employee has:

  • been terminated or has resigned and the employer has advised more than 30 days in advance, go to Step 13
  • accessed a KIT day:
    • DOC both the employee and employer records with the decision
    • The employee will continue to receive PPL payments
    • If there is an active REA activity on the employee’s record cancel the REA update in Process Direct. Procedure ends here
  • exception return to work:
    • Run the PPL circumstances guided procedure to update the return to work exception
    • Contact the employer to advise they will need to continue delivering PPL to their employee
    • Go to Step 14
  • returned to work:
    • Contact and advise the employee. If contact is unsuccessful, run the PPL circumstances guided procedure to update the return to work, and
    • update the return to work as advised by the employer
    • Go to Step 14

14

New return to work date or amend previously recorded details + Read more ...

Launching the PPL circumstances guided procedure

To add a new return to work date or amend previously recorded details:

  • Select PPL circumstances guided procedure
  • Next on the Claim Summary page > Work Details tab> Next
  • Select Work Details > Next
  • On the Task Selector page select both Work Details and Flexible PPL
  • Launch Staff Task
  • Select Work Details.
  • On the PPL Return to Work Summary, select New
    • If advising they are returning to work, record the date in the Return to work field. If the customer has returned to work for an allowable exception reason, select the relevant reason from the Return to work Reason drop down list
    • If advising they are no longer returning to work (a return to work date is currently recorded), leave the Return to Work date field blank
    • If advising a new return to work date (from a date previously advised), record the new date in the Return to Work date field
  • Select Finish

The return to work exception reason ‘Essential worker responding to state, territory or national emergency’ can only be recorded if the customer returned to work on or after 12 March 2020.

For claims requiring a return to work exception prior to 12 March 2020, code the Paid Parental Leave Exemption (P1EX) screen using the exception reason as DWO - Disregard Work. The P1EX screen is not to be used for coding the DWO exception after 12 March 2020. See Parental Leave Pay (PPL) customer returns to work.

To complete change of circumstances tasks, Go to Step 15.

15

Completing change of circumstances tasks + Read more ...

  • The Activity Details screen will display the Staff Tasks based on the selections made on the Task Selector. If a relevant task is not displayed, select Back to return to the Task Selector
  • Select the hyperlink for each task. Once inside the task, use the New button to update the employee’s circumstances. To update a field, select and type the changed details or use the calendar function
  • If necessary, manually change the status of workflows to Completed, Required or Not Required
    Note: this should only be done if the status of the staff workflow is preventing the claim from being processed. Select the Icon in the Change Status column in Staff Workflows. This will change the status to Completed, Required, or Not required
  • Once all updates are made, to complete the PPL circumstances guided procedure, run the Finalisation GP guided procedure from Staff Tasks
  • If the Finalisation GP guided procedure does not appear in the workflow, update the status to Completed, then click Save to ensure the workflow is completed
  • If the activity has not completed after completing the Finalisation GP guided procedure, select the activity from the Activity List (AL) screen and finalise on the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Advise the employee of the change of circumstances outcome
  • If the employee’s PPL entitlement has been reassessed and a debt is identified, the debt shell will automatically allocate to the appropriate debt team for investigation
  • DOC both the employee and employer records with the:
    • contact attempts,
    • return to work advice from the employer or employee, and
    • return to work outcome, including debt if identified
  • The employee will be issued with a letter advising of the outcome. A copy of the letter will also be issued to the employer if required

Using the Fast Note > auto template Families > updates > Change of circs > PPL/DAP, record all details of the update.

16

Payment delivery change + Read more ...

For Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) staff only

If the employer notifies a change in circumstance that no longer requires them to deliver PPL to an employee, for example the business is or has ceased trading, a transfer of payment responsibility needs to be actioned and the agency will commence payments directly to the employee.

Revocation or transfer of the employer role can be updated retrospectively; in cases where PPL has cancelled and it has been determined the employer has not/cannot deliver PPL funds to an employee. Overpayments of PPL may occur in these circumstances, and apportioning of debts must be investigated.
