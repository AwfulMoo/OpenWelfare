There are no handoffs for CrP-NHE. All enquiries/claims must be actioned at first point of contact engaging Main Business Line TPS support that aligns with the customer’s payment type as needed.

For multiple and complex issues that need a professional assessment, refer to a social worker after completing action required. For example family and domestic violence.

For verbal claims for CrP-NHE where the customer is calling from a service centre, flag with a Team Leader immediately. The service centre can be advised of the health risk and take appropriate action.

4

Prevented from claiming CrP-NHE online after 2 payments in the last 6 months + Read more ...

Customers are not entitled to more than 2 CrP-NHE payments in 6 months. The system checks payments in the 6 months before their latest claim attempt and prevents the customer from claiming in this circumstance.

Check the customer was granted CrP-NHE within 6 months of the date of the contact to claim or date claim submitted for CrP-NHE:

  • In Process Direct, go to the CRISIS_PS screen
  • in Customer First, run the Crisis Payment Summary workflow

See the Resources page for examples of how to calculate the rolling 6 month period.

Has the customer received 2 CrP-NHE payments in the preceding 6 months?

  • Yes, tell the customer they are not eligible for a CrP-NHE at this time
    • Record the customer’s intent to claim CrP-NHE using the Crisis Payment - Intent to claim Fast Note. Select Auto text. Use Emergency > Claim > CrP Intent to Claim. When completed, close the Fast Note
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Prevented from claiming CrP-NHE after grant or rejection in the last 7 days + Read more ...

A customer may have a genuine need to claim multiple times in 7 days, but the system prevents a customer claiming online more than once if they have claimed in the last 7 days.

Check if customer was granted or rejected CrP-NHE within 7 days of the date of the contact to claim:

  • In Process Direct, go to the CRISIS_PS screen
  • in Customer First, run the Crisis Payment Summary workflow

If the customer tried to claim for:

  • the same event of a rejected claim, follow Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process. Procedure ends here
  • the same event of a granted claim, advise the customer only one payment is available for the same event. Procedure ends here
  • a different event of the granted or rejected claim but within 7 days, go to Step 6
  • a date of test on or before 30 June 2022 and the actual date of the test was on or between 1 July 2022 and 19 July 2022, go to Step 6

Review evidence + Read more ...

The National Health Emergency - Crisis Payment Questionnaire collects the details for the Crisis Payment circumstances.

For an Online Claim or ACC, the questionnaire will include the information provided in the claim.

Open and examine the evidence the customer has uploaded via Documents.

COVID-19 test evidence + Read more ...

Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) test for COVID-19:

The evidence of the COVID-19 PCR test must show the test date of the customer or the person they are caring for and who performed the test. The evidence may be:

  • a screen shot of a text message from a:
    • pathology service
    • state health service, or
    • doctor’s surgery advising that the test was done or showing a test result and the test date
  • scanned document or photo of an attendance certificate from a:
    • pathology service
    • state health service

The date of the PCR test must show in the evidence uploaded by the customer. This is to make sure the:

  • claim is being lodged within 14 days of the test and the requirement to isolate (or within 28 days if the customer has lodged an intent to claim)
  • test or result date is in relation to the isolation event being claimed

Rapid antigen test (RAT) for COVID-19:

For claims lodged on or after 13 January 2022, the evidence must be notification the customer has reported the positive RAT to their state or territory health authority when the test was taken on or after 13 January 2022.

With the exception of customers from Western Australia, the positive RAT result date must be given in the evidence. This is to make sure the claim is being lodged within 14 days of the positive RAT and the requirement to isolate (or within 28 days if the customer has lodged an intent to claim).

As advised in the notification from the state or territory health authority, based on the customer's circumstances the test date will be either:

  • the date the customer tested positive to COVID-19, or
  • the date 7 days prior to the isolation end date

Note: evidence of reporting a positive RAT from customers in Western Australia will not have a test date. The self-declaration provided by the customer in the claim must be accepted for these customers.

It is not sufficient for a person to give a notice that they need to self-isolate or quarantine. For example, close contact notification. The person or the person they are caring for must give evidence that they have been tested for COVID-19 or reported the positive RAT result to their state or territory health authority.

Check the customer’s record for any recent granted CrP-NHE claims and uploaded evidence of COVID-19 test. Make sure the same evidence is not being used for another claim using a different isolation date.

Evidence of bank balance + Read more ...

If the customer is required to give evidence of their bank balance, see, Table 2 Item 7 on the Resources page of Documents required for Centrelink new claims - Bank accounts and balances, for acceptable documents.

Acceptable evidence + Read more ...

The evidence is acceptable if it is:

  • a COVID-19 test (either notification of the test or result and includes the test provider) showing the date of test corresponding to test date in the NHE questionnaire
  • notification the customer has reported the positive RAT to their state or territory health authority and the test was taken between 13 January 2022, and
  • documents which support financial hardship

Note: notification the customer has reported the positive RAT to their state or territory health authority is preferable to a picture of a RAT, but is not mandatory until 7 March 2022, as all states and territories had not implemented a positive RAT result registry previously.

Unacceptable evidence + Read more ...

The claim will need to be manually rejected if any of the following apply:

  • proof of vaccination
  • it is not a COVID-19 test (notification or result)
  • the evidence does not include the header of the SMS or email to show where the message was sent from
  • it is a RAT with a negative result
  • it is a positive RAT where the date of event is before the 13 January 2022
  • it is clearly not related to the customer or person they are caring for, or
  • it is not a bank statement or a digital copy of the customer’s account information and transactions. Note: an ATM slip is not acceptable

Contacting customers with illegible evidence + Read more ...

Only contact the customer if the evidence is not legible:

  • COVID-19 PCR test notification or outcome (positive or negative) or positive RAT result, or
  • bank statement as proof of financial hardship

A customer must contact within 14 days of the date the quarantine or self-isolation started. This will be from the date of the COVID-19 test. After the initial contact, the customer must lodge the claim within 14 days. See Table 1 on the Resources page for what 'contact' means for intent to claim and deemed claims.

Payment of Crisis Payment + Read more ...

Payment should be made immediately using the New Payments Platform (NPP) if available for the customer. If NPP is unavailable, use Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS)

If the claim is to be granted and selected for Quality Management Application (QMA) follow urgent QMA processes.

Staff MUST manually code the immediate payment for Crisis Payment in Customer First by selecting reason CRI. Do not use the immediate payment script for Crisis Payments. (See Table 3 in Immediate payment by NPP).

Note: if the immediate payment is not coded manually via NPP or RTGS, the Crisis Payment will default to Direct Credit. It can take several days to reach the customer’s account.

For Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customers:

Approval of the customer's Social Service Plan (SSP) must occur before paying the Crisis Payment. The system will approve this within 15 minutes. To check if the SSP has been approved, navigate to SSP.

Once approved, pay the Crisis Payment using RTGS. Otherwise, the payment will deliver by direct credit the following business day.
