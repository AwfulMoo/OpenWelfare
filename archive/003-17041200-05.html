Question and answers from claimants

Table 3: This table contains questions that claimants may ask about 2017 Past Overseas Incidents.

Item

Description

1

Why have these particular acts been declared by the Prime Minister?

  • Decisions about whether a particular incident should be declared under the Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment (AVTOP) scheme are a matter for the Prime Minister
  • The Australian Government does not comment on incidents it is considering for declaration
  • Before making a declaration, the Prime Minister must be satisfied that the incident is a ‘terrorist act’ within the definition in subsection 100.1(1) of the Criminal Code
  • Once satisfied that an incident is a terrorist act, the Prime Minister has broad discretion in deciding whether to make a declaration
  • To ensure consistency in decision making, the Prime Minister has identified ‘declaration criteria’ to which he may have regard when considering whether to declare an incident for the AVTOP scheme
  • The declaration criteria are available on the AVTOP page of the Australian Government’s DisasterAssist website (www.disasterassist.gov.au/)
  • All declared terrorist acts have been of national significance to Australia, due to the involvement of Australians, Australian interests or the interests of our allies, and resulting in the injury or death of Australian residents
  • Once an incident has been declared by the Prime Minister, Services Australia (the agency) is responsible for assessing claims and making payments to eligible victims

2

Does this scheme apply to victims of terrorist acts that have occurred in Australia?

  • The AVTOP scheme only applies to overseas terrorist acts
  • The AVTOP scheme was designed to complement the state and territory schemes for victims of domestic crime - including terrorism - and match the maximum amount available under some of those schemes
  • See your State or Territory’s Victims of Crime website for further information

3

Will payments under this scheme affect victims’ other entitlements?

  • This payment is not designed to replace any existing payments or benefits
  • A payment under this scheme will not affect insurance or workers compensation
  • Victims will not be required to reimburse the Government for Medicare benefits received in relation to their injuries
  • Payments under the scheme will not be subject to income tax and will not be treated as income for social security purposes. However, if invested, such payments could generate income which would be relevant for the purposes of the Social Security income test and income tax
  • Payments under the scheme will not be treated as damages or compensation under Commonwealth law

4

How can a claim be made?

See the Services Australia’s Help in an emergency website for information on how to make a claim for the Australian Victim of Terrorism Overseas Payment.

The website also provides information on eligibility criteria and a link to the current claim forms.

5

A claimant no longer has evidence of their travel. Can they still make a claim?

  • Advise the claimant to provide as much detail as they can regarding their travel dates, the name of the city or town they visited, and the name and/or address of the accommodation they stayed in for example, specific names of hotels, address of relatives or friends the claimant stayed with at the time
  • Assist the claimant by conducting a Google search to confirm the address of accommodation details they have provided, and checking the claimant’s Residence and Portability screens in Customer First. See Residence and Portability screens in Operational Blueprint.
    Note: the Residence and Portability screens will show when the claimant left and returned to the country but will not provide more specific details about where they travelled to
  • If a claimant has provided information of their experience to the media, conducting a Google search may be useful in verifying the claimant’s evidence

6

A claimant’s medical practitioner or specialist is no longer available. How can they provide evidence of their previous injury/injuries?

  • Discuss with the claimant if their current medical practitioner or specialist can assist with providing information that will support their claim
  • If the claimant has kept personal records of medical information, advise them to provide this to the agency. This evidence may be sufficient for the purposes of an assessment
  • If a claimant has provided information of their experience to the media, conducting a Google search may be useful in verifying the claimant’s evidence
  • A last option is for the agency’s Assessment Services and Smart Centres Coordination Branch to conduct a specialist assessment with the claimant
  • While the Assessment Services and Smart Centres Coordination Branch could undertake a specialist assessment in some instances, this may only provide evidence regarding current symptoms and impacts of the event. In some AVTOP cases, the impacts from a previous period may have resolved over time. In these instances it is critical to have access to evidence in relation to the previous impacts rather than just current functioning
