Cancelling a Service Update or Review in IRS

Step

Action

1

Cancelling a Service Update or Review + Read more ...

A Service Update or Review can be cancelled in IRS when deciding not to review the customer's circumstances.

Examples of why a review may not be needed are:

  • the review information is incorrect
  • examination of the customer's record shows no discrepancy
  • the customer has been reviewed within the previous 12 weeks for the same or similar circumstances
  • preliminary examination of the customer's record indicates that the information has already been provided
  • no triggers have been identified when undertaking system checks to determine if the review should be cancelled or continued

Note: Veda Advantage Database (VEDA) and Online Search (OLS) facility for Medicare Australia (HIC) and Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) checks will only provide a trigger to determine whether the review should be cancelled or continued.

2

Service Update and Review status + Read more ...

Only Service Update or Reviews with a primary status of Registered (REG) or Started (STA) can be cancelled in the IRS. Note: generally, a started Compliance or Intelligence and Investigations Branch review should be progressed to finalisation, unless the review should not have been started in the first place. For example, information was already provided.

Is the review status REG or STA?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the Service Update or Review cannot be cancelled in IRS. Procedure ends here

3

Is this an Intelligence and Investigations Branch review? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Intelligence and Investigations Branch review + Read more ...

These reviews have separate security that prevents a started review from being cancelled unless the action is performed by staff who have authority to cancel a started review.

Reason to cancel a review:

  • If there is not enough information to assess the customer's entitlement as not enough customer and/or third party checks have been conducted, then the review must be cancelled
  • If there is not enough information to assess the customer's entitlement, then cancel the review. For example, a contact is returned for a member of a couple review and the information provided indicates that the customer may be in receipt of an incorrect rate. The review is not actioned again for 9 months at which time it is determined that the information is old and the review will not continue. As there is not enough information to identify that the customer is receiving a correct rate, and it is determined that the investigation to establish the correct rate will not continue, the review must be cancelled rather than finalised with a calculated result

5

Correct authority and other factors + Read more ...

A review must not be cancelled and the review information re-loaded as a new review. Once a review is cancelled, the information within that review is deemed to have been actioned.

A Review which has been referred for Surveillance action (Review Method is SURveillance) may not be cancelled unless the Surveillance Status is OSR (Optical Surveillance Registered).

Does the Service Officer have the correct authority to cancel the review in IRS?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, liaise with staff who have the authority. Compliance Officers are to request Quality Development Officer (QDO) to contact the Compliance Management Centre (CMC) via the Customer Compliance Service Desk Online Enquiry Form

6

Action to cancel or finalise a review + Read more ...

Determine if Service Update or the Review should be cancelled or finalised.

A Service Update or Review in IRS is to be finalised if:

  • There is enough information to assess that the customer is receiving correct entitlement
  • There is not enough information to be able to correctly assess the customer's entitlement after all avenues of investigation have been exhausted, then the review can be finalised with a calculated result. This is because there is no evidence to prove that the customer is in receipt of an incorrect rate therefore the current rate is accepted as a correct rate

Does either of these conditions apply to the customer?

  • Yes, the review is to be finalised and not cancelled. See Finalising and attributing actions to a Service Update or Review in IRS. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Not a valid match (NVM) + Read more ...

Is the cancellation reason 'Not a Valid Match' (NVM)?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Cancelling a review NVM + Read more ...

Only use the Cancel Review Reason NVM when the match information does not relate to the customer.

Approval must be obtained before cancelling the review.

Forward requests to the Customer Compliance Service Desk via the Online Enquiry Form. Include details of the Customer Reference Number (CRN), Review ID and reasons why the match is not valid.

Cancelling a review using this code will delete the match data from the record.

Procedure ends here until response from the Customer Compliance Service Desk.

  • When approval is obtained, go to Step 9.
  • If not given approval, the Service Update or Review cannot be cancelled in IRS. Procedure ends here

9

Cancel a Review + Read more ...

In the Integrated Review System (IRS), select the appropriate review from the Review Summary (IRRS) or Review Enquiry (IRRE) screen by placing a 'C' next to the review and pressing [Enter].

The Cancel Review (IRCR) screen will display.

10

Select appropriate Cancel Review Reason + Read more ...

In the IRCR screen, select the appropriate Cancel Review Reason by using field help ('?') in the Cancel Reason field.

Some Cancel Review Reason codes are only valid for particular Review Types and Review Reasons. The Resources page contains a listing of appropriate Cancel Review Reasons by Review Type.

  • Enter the code in the Cancel Reason field. Note: if the review is to be combined with another, CMB must be used. For help, see Combining a Review in IRS.
  • If required, amend the Review Region
  • Key 'Y' in the Cancel Review (Y/N/Q) field
  • Press [Enter]
  • Fully DOC the reason for cancelling the review in IRS

Once a Review Status displays as cancelled, no further actions can be attributed to the Service Update or Review. Any activities attributed at the time of cancellation will be automatically unattributed. All other information is retained as part of the Review History.
