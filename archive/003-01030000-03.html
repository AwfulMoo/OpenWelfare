Check if customer has received AGDRP/NZ DRP or DRA/NZ DRA for another active event + Read more ...

When reviewing the Payment Summary (PS) screen to decide if a customer has already been paid for the same event, also check to see if the customer has been issued a Flexible Support Payment (FSP) for another active event.

For example, a customer may have been granted AGDRP for a Queensland event, and is now claiming for an active New South Wales event.

While some customers may be eligible for both payments (having been forced to relocate from one location to the other), these would not be common.

Further discussion with the customer is needed to decide their circumstances. If there are any concerns about the validity of their changed address, evidence should be requested.

Concerns about identity or the validity of the claim + Read more ...

Note: fraud concerns may have already been identified in Table 1 or Table 3.

When completing an ACC, staff may suspect the customer is acting fraudulently if they:

  • have had claims previously rejected or withdrawn, there are inconsistencies in the information provided for the ACC, and more evidence has been requested
  • have a history of withdrawing claims for AGDRP/NZ DRP, DRA/NZ DRA and/or different emergency payments, including the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • have a DOA DOC relating to concerns about fraud
  • have debt ACTDOCs completed on record
  • have or want to change their address to one within a declared area just after a disaster
  • receive regular payments and want to change their payment destination to an account they have not used before (referred to as ‘payment hijacking’)
  • give inconsistent responses to how they were adversely affected by a disaster or how their income was impacted
  • have contact phone numbers that are currently linked to other customers
  • have AGDRP/NZ DRP claim/s for the same event previously rejected as they did not meet the definition of ‘adversely impacted’ and their new claim is giving different impact reasons
  • have DRA/NZ DRA claims for the same event previously rejected as they did not have income below the AWOTE or the reduction in income was not as a direct result of the disaster event and they are now giving different income amounts to those previously declared or changing how their income was impacted by the disaster
  • have claim/s previously rejected due to the validity of the claim or the customer’s identity and no further evidence has been provided
