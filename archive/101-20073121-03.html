For Income Management Contact Officers (IMCOs) only.

This page contains information to assist the Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO), when a request is received from the Family responsibilities Commission via the Business Hub, to commence a customer on the Cashless Debit Card program.

On this page:

Processing request for the Cashless Debit Card from Family Responsibilities Commission

Table 1: This table describes how an IMCO is to action a request from the Family Responsibilities Commission to commence the Cashless Debit Card program for a mutual customer.

Step

Action

1

Request received + Read more ...

Requests are submitted by the referring authority via the Business Hub.

The Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) can view all requests sent by a referring authority by viewing the Third Party Information Summary Organisation (TPISO) screen in Customer First.

Navigate to the TPISO screen.

Note: the TPISO screen must only be viewed in Environment B. If an attempt is made to view via any other system or environment, the information displayed may be incomplete or incorrect.

Searching for requests + Read more ...

Searching the TPISO screen - B Environment

The TPISO screen shows all recent requests submitted by the organisation through the Business Hub.

To search for requests, complete the following fields:

  • Application Type: Select Welfare Payment Reform
  • Business Function: Select Cape York
  • Information Type: Select the desired search function
  • Status: Select the desired search function, that is, Pending, Processed, Unprocessed etc

or:

  • Organisation CRN: Input the Organisation Customer Reference Number (CRN) for the referring authority
  • Date Range: Input desired date range

Click Search

Actioning Cape York Cashless Debit Card requests + Read more ...

To view any request, click on the hypertext under the Information Type heading, to see detailed information.

  • To action an unprocessed Mutual Client search, go to Step 4
  • To action an unprocessed Personal Information Disclosure (PID) eligibility check, See Step 1 in the Personal Information Disclosure via Business Hub table
  • To action an unprocessed or auto processed Notice of Commencement of Cashless Debit Card, See Step1 in the Notice To Commence Cashless Debit Card table
  • To action an unprocessed or auto processed Revocation of Cashless Debit Card, See Step 1 in the Notice to Revoke Cashless Debit Card table

4

Undertake a Find Mutual Client Search + Read more ...

In some cases when the referring authority undertakes a Mutual Client search it results in a 'no' match. The referring authority will then refer it to Services Australia for follow up.

The IMCO will need to investigate these requests and provide a response to the referring authority.

Mutual Client searches that have returned a 'no' match will display as 'unprocessed'.

To view all unprocessed Mutual Client searches submitted by the referring authority complete the following:

Navigate to the TPISO screen.

Code the following fields:

  • Information Type: 'Find Mutual Client'
  • Date Range: set to preferred period of time
  • Status: 'Unprocessed'
  • Click Search and a list of results will be displayed for this period

Go to Step 5.

5

Actioning an unprocessed Mutual Client Search + Read more ...

Click on the hyperlink Find Mutual Client text under the Information Type column for the unmatched customer. This will load the Find Mutual Client screen.

This screen displays the information entered into the Business Hub by the referring authority to locate a customer. Take note of the Mutual Client Summary details on the screen.

Conduct an Index Search in Customer First for this person, for example, Name, Date of Birth (DOB).

Has the customer been identified via the Index Search?

  • No, go to Step 6
  • Yes, go to Step 7

6

Advice outcome sent to the referring authority - No match + Read more ...

In the Record Outcome section of the Find Mutual Client screen:

  • select 'no' as the response to the question 'Did you uniquely match the Client'?
  • add any additional notes
  • tick the Declaration box and press Submit

An 'Advice Outcome' will be automatically sent to the referring authority via their daily reports, indicating the client has not been matched to Services Australia.

Procedure ends here.

7

Advice outcome sent to the referring authority - match + Read more ...

In the Record Outcome section of the Find Mutual Client screen:

  • select 'yes' as the response to the question 'Did you uniquely match the Client'?
  • type in the CRN, and provide any additional notes.
  • tick the Declaration box and press Submit

An 'Advice Outcome" will be automatically sent to the referring authority via their daily reports, stating that the details provided have been matched to Services Australia.

The IMCO will then need to complete a Personal Information Disclosure to be sent to the referring authority via the Business Hub.

A Personal Information Disclosure will provide the referring authority with information that will assist in determining if the Cashless Debit Card program is suitable for the customer.

  • Date Range: set to preferred period of time
  • Status: 'unprocessed'

The Personal Information Disclosure screen will be displayed.

3

Manual check of eligibility + Read more ...

Take note of the customer details found on the Personal Information Disclosure screen and navigate to locate the customer record.

Complete a manual check of Cape York Cashless Debit Card eligibility. Identify if the customer meets eligibility criteria under the Cape York Initiative for example, person or partner in receipt of a category P welfare payment, has an excluded Nominee arrangement, have or are residing in a Cape York area and there is a Cape York commencement notice.

For further information regarding eligibility, see Eligibility for Cashless Debit Card.

4

Confirm eligibility results + Read more ...

  • Include information supporting the eligibility outcome in the ‘Notes’ free text box
  • Complete the activity

An 'Advice Outcome' will be automatically sent to the referring authority via their daily reports advising of the customer's actual eligibility.

Procedure ends here.

Notice to Commence Cashless Debit Card

Table 3: This table describes how an Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) is to action a notice to commence Cashless Debit Card

Step

Action

1

Actioning a Notice to Commence Cashless Debit Card + Read more ...

On receipt of a Notice to Commence Income Management or the Cashless Debit Card the system will automatically create an 'inactive' notice on the customer record and place an automatic DOC on the record stating a notice has been received. When this occurs, the Status of the notice in the Business Hub will set to 'auto processing'.

If Auto Processing fails and an 'inactive' notice cannot be created, the Status of the notice in the Business Hub will be set to 'unprocessed' and a reason for rejection will be displayed advising why an 'inactive' notice could not be created.

In circumstances where Auto Processing fails, manual processing must be completed:

  • search the TPISO screen for an 'unprocessed' Notice to Commence Cashless Debit Card
  • launch the Cashless Debit Card (Cape York) Notice Details Screen and record an 'inactive' notice on the Cape York Notice Details screen
  • Update the status of the Notice to Commence Income Management or the Cashless Debit Card via the TPISO screen from 'unprocessed' to ‘pending'
  • Identify if the customer meets eligibility under the Cape York Initiative. If the customer is not eligible for Cape York Cashless Debit Card, update the status of the Notice to Commence Cashless Debit Card via the TPISO screen from 'unprocessed' to 'processed'

Go to Step 2.

2

Record outcome of the Cashless Debit Card decision + Read more ...

Do not record outcomes until a formal decision about the Cashless Debit Card has been made (i.e. customer has been switched on or a decision has been made not to apply Cashless Debit Card for customer, for example, not eligible).

Once the decision has been made, select the 'Yes' or 'No' option for the question 'Switch on?'.

Access the TPISO screen again and select the relevant notice from the list of search results. This will display the Commence CDC for Cape York screen where the Record Outcomes section of the notice is to be completed.

Note:

  • the Third Party Information Summary (TPIS) screen can be accessed from the customer's record. Any activity that appears on the TPISO screen that is relevant to a specific customer will be displayed on that customer's TPIS screen
  • If the participant has been on CDC in the NT previously and TCU was their card issuer of choice, TCU will be defaulted as the card issuer when the customer is switched on to CDC in Cape York
  • The following message will displayed on the CY Notice Screen: ‘The customer’s current card issuer is Traditional Credit Union’ to indicate the participant is with TCU and will remain with TCU unless they elect to switch card issuers

Has the customer been placed on the Cashless Debit Card program?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 9

3

Customer has been placed on the Cashless Debit Card program + Read more ...

The customer's Cashless Debit Card status will be automatically updated on the TPISO screen, and the notice changed from 'unprocessed' to 'pending' or 'processed'.

This automated processing will send an advice outcome to the referring authority via the daily report advising of the change in Cashless Debit Card status.

If the automated process is not successful the 'unprocessed' notice will need to be manually updated on the TPISO screen.

  • Scroll to the bottom of the Notice to the Record outcome section
  • Select 'Yes' for question 'Has the Client been switched on to Cashless Debit Card'?
  • Go to Step 4

Note: this sends a Cashless Debit Card Commencement advice outcome to the referring authority advising of the decision to switch the customer on to the Cashless Debit Card program..

4

Cashless Debit Card start date + Read more ...

Where a customer has a New/Continuation Cape York CDC Notice applied after the original CDC end date, a new 28 day waiting period is applied. If the participant still has access to an active CDC, their start date can be brought forward.

The FRC will ask the participant if they have an active card in their possession when they are notifying them of their New/Continuation notice and will relay this information to Services Australia to support bringing forward their start date.

If the participant does not have an active card in their possession, the FRC will advise the participant to attend their preferred Agent site or service centre to get a temporary card. The customer can then request to bring forward their start date by contacting a service centre or the CDC Hotline.

For further information on changing a CDC start date, see Cashless Debit Card - changing initial waiting periods.

For further information on temporary CDCs, see Temporary Cashless Debit Cards (CDC).

Procedure ends here.

5

Customer has not been placed on to the Cashless Debit Card program + Read more ...

Select the 'unprocessed' notice from the TPISO screen.

Scroll to the bottom of the notice to the Record outcome section.

Answer the question to 'Has the Client been switched on to Cashless Debit Card under the Cape York Initiative'?

If 'No' is selected, a sub question appears 'Why has no action been taken to switch the Client on to the Cashless Debit Card under the Cape York Initiative'?

Select the most appropriate reason from drop down list:

  • not receiving a trigger payment
  • excluded Nominee
  • other

Click Submit

This sends an advice outcome to the referring authority via the daily report advising that the customer was not switched on to the Cashless Debit Card program and the selected reason.

Procedure ends here.

A Continuation Notice + Read more ...

A referring authority may lodge a new Notice to Commence Income Management or the Cashless Debit Card to extend the period of the Cashless Debit Card program beyond the current notice expiry date.

A Continuation notice will be automatically created when a customer with an active notice receives a new Notice to Commence Income Management or the Cashless Debit Card within 60 days prior to the current notice's end date.

The continuation notice will have an inactive status as there can only be one active notice on a customer's record at any time.

When the customer's current notice expires, the continuation notice will automatically become active and Cashless Debit Card will continue uninterrupted.

A message will display under Description on the TPISO screen indicating the results of the automated processing, including where the automated process was unsuccessful in which case manual processing is required.

Was the automated processing of the continuation notice successful?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, manually record the continuation notice. Go to Step 2

2

Manually coding Commencement/Continuation Notice + Read more ...

In workflow open the Cashless Debit Card - Notice Details Cape York screen.

On the Notice screen complete the following fields:

  • FRC Reference Number: enter the FRC reference number
  • Type of Notice: select appropriate option
  • Receipt Date: will default to today's Date, do not change
  • Start Date: will default and is protected, do not change
  • Period of Notice: select 3, 6, 9 or 12 months (this information will be provided on the notice)
  • End date: will be calculated from period of notice
  • Status: Inactive
  • Percentage: select appropriate percentage from the dropdown list
  • State/Territory: select appropriate option from the dropdown list
  • Organisation/Office: select Family Responsibilities Commission
  • Contact: select appropriate contact from dropdown list

Click 'Continue'

Finalise the assessment.

This will place an automated DOC on the participant's record.

In the 'Annotated Document Text' field, include any additional information provided on the notice.

Note: if the case worker provided information in the notice that refers to reasons why the customer was referred for the CDC, the IMCO should contact the case worker to request the notice be re-sent without this information.

Click 'Finalise' to complete the activity.

The customer's Cashless Debit Card status will be automatically updated on the TPISO screen, and the notice changed from 'unprocessed' to 'processed'. An advice outcome will be automatically sent to the referring authority via the daily report.

Notice to Revoke the Cashless Debit Card

Table 5: This table describes how an Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) is to action a request to revoke the Cashless Debit Card.

Step

Action

1

Actioning a request for Revocation of Cashless Debit Card + Read more ...

When a referring authority lodges a Revocation of Income Management or Cashless Debit Card notice (Revoke notice) via the Business Hub, the system will automatically send the details to Customer First and the Status of the notice will be set to 'auto processing'. Depending on the individual circumstances, the system will perform one of the following actions:

  • automatically revoke any inactive notice/s on the customer's record
  • automatically create a 28 day review and Work Item for any currently active notice
  • create a Work Item for any revoke notices that are received for a currently active notice that is ending in less than or equal to 28 days

If the Auto Processing fails, the revoke notice will be set to 'unprocessed' and the rejection reason will be displayed advising why the notice could not be revoked.

In circumstances where the revoke notice is not automatically processed, the revoke notice must be manually recorded:

  • search the TPISO screen for unprocessed Revocation of Income Management or Cashless Debit Card notices, and select the relevant notice from the search results
  • Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) will run the Cape York Notice Details workflow and update the 'active' notice to 'revoked'. This will end the customer's Cape York Cashless Debit Card.

Revoking the customer's notice via the Cape York Notice Details workflow will automatically update the Status of the notice on the TPISO screen to 'processed'. Was the automated processing of the revoke notice successful?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, manually update the revoke notice in the Business Hub. Go to Step 2

2

Manually updating the revoke notice + Read more ...

Access the Cape York Notice Details workflow and select the current active notice. Select Revoke and complete the workflow.

No action should be taken to record the outcome of a Revocation notice until a formal decision has been made about revocation of Cashless Debit Card (that is, the revocation has been coded on the customer's record).

Search the TPISO screen for unprocessed revocations of Cashless Debit Card. Select the relevant notice from the search results.

Scroll down to the Record Outcome section of the notice and answer the question 'Has the client's Cashless Debit Card been revoked?' with 'Yes'.

Tick the Declaration.

This will send an advice outcome to the referring authority advising that the Cashless Debit Card has been revoked.

Procedure ends here.
