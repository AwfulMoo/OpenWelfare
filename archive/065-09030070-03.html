Processing MFUs

The information below explains the required action for each aged care Manual Follow-up (MFU).

Match the 2 line information message on the Activity List (AL) screen with the list of MFUs below. For ease of access, the information is divided into the action that has produced the MFU, that is, entries and discharges.

Note: while references to DSS (Department of Social Security) and DHA (the former Department of Health and Aged Care and Ageing) in steps 1 to 4 of Table 2 are out of date, they reflect the text that currently appears in the automatically created MFUs.

Aged care MFUs after entry into care

Table 1

Item

Description

1

I006 Aged care home resident's AC screen has been updated

I007 RA has been paid past date of admission - check for overpayment + Read more ...

Services Australia are advised that this customer has entered permanent residential care. Rent Assistance is not payable from date of admission. The exception to this is if the resident continued to pay rent on their former home in which case their eligibility to Rent Assistance would cease on the 15th day of admission into the aged care home.

When the Accommodation Circumstances (AC) screen was automatically updated, the system recognised that Rent Assistance has been paid past the date of admission.

Check RCA Institution Summary (RIS) screen and Payment Summary (PS) screen to see if there is an overpayment and raise a debt if required. See Rent Assistance (RA) for care receivers for further information.

2

I006 Aged care home resident's AC screen has been updated

I008 Check if current government rent deduction should cease + Read more ...

Services Australia are advised that this customer has entered an aged care home. However, a government rent deduction is still in place. Check if the rent deduction should cease by contacting the customer or nominee by phone.

If unable to contact the customer:

  • create a manual Free Text (Q999) letter including standard text for customers in an aged care home who still have a government rent deduction in place
  • finalise the manual follow up (MFU)
  • record details on a DOC
  • if address has not been updated refer to Changing address based on aged care home notification

Note: this customer is not eligible for Rent Assistance.

3

I006 Aged care home resident's AC screen has been updated

I009 Resident may be illness separated. Review partner's RA + Read more ...

This MFU is created when a care recipient enters Residential Care and is:

  • in receipt of an income support payment or CSHC
  • a non-homeowner, and
  • a member of couple

Action is required to update the relationship status to illness-separated and if appropriate, restore any Rent Assistance that was being paid to the partner who remains in the principal home. When updating this information, the date of receipt to be used will be the earliest date of notification that a care recipient has entered care. If there is no earlier notification recorded, these updates can be completed in the MFU activity.

Additional checks may be required for:

  • Carer Payment/Carer Allowance where the care recipient was previously the carer:
    • cancel Carer Payment/Carer Allowance if this has not previously been cancelled
    • see Carer Payment (CP) carer is absent or Carer Allowance (CA) carer is absent for assistance if required
  • Carer Payment/Carer Allowance where the care recipient was a care receiver:
    • determine whether coding has been completed to reflect that the care recipient has entered permanent residential care
    • complete coding if this has not previously been done
    • see Care receiver permanently enters or temporarily/permanently leaves an institution for assistance if required. Note: Carer Payment (XWP) does not continue for 14 weeks when a care receiver is permanently admitted to an institution. CP (XWP) is automatically cancelled when the carer's only or last payment level Carer Allowance is cancelled. For more information see Transfer from Wife Pension (WP) to Carer Payment (CP)
  • Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC):
    • where a member of a couple is assessed as illness separated, CSHC updates are required to ensure the correct income test thresholds are applied
    • see Changes in a customer's circumstances and effect on concession card entitlement for assistance

See Change of address to an aged care home for further information on completing updates.

4

RCA Online Claim Submitted DD MTH YEAR Claim ID + Read more ...

NCL could not be recorded, manual action required.

An online, or Assisted customer claim (ACC) has been submitted but an RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM was unable to be created as there was an existing means assessment (usually an entry event from User *RC) with a status of registered, started or held.

Action required:

  • View the MFU activity
  • Copy the following text: RCA Online Claim Submitted dd mth yyyy Claim ID 0000000

Return to Activity List (AL) screen and locate the auto RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM activity:

  • Select 'V' to view the RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM
  • Paste the copied text onto the notes line
  • Add keyword:
    • INDOCRCA - for an online claim
    • INDACRCA - for an assisted customer claim
  • On the same screen remove the resubmit date

Finalise the MFU.

Aged care MFUs after discharge from care

Table 2

Item

Description

1

I012 DSS have advised that aged care resident died on (date)

I013 Record income details and cancel RCA after record is current + Read more ...

  • Care recipient has commenced home or residential care
  • RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM is currently outstanding
  • Discharge from care has been coded in the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS) or Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP) and care recipient is now deceased
  • Discharge event was not able to be finalised due to started RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM

See Aged care means assessment - preliminary checks to action the manual follow up.

2

I012 DSS have advised that aged care resident died on (date)

I014 Check if income support should be cancelled and means assessment finalised + Read more ...

  • Care recipient has commenced home or residential care
  • RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM is currently outstanding
  • Discharge from care has been coded in ACMPS or ACSP and care recipient is now deceased
  • Discharge event was not able to be finalised due to started RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM

See Aged care means assessment - preliminary checks to action the manual follow up.

If ACMPS or ACSP state that a care recipient has departed home care, further confirmation is required prior to actioning. Check Document List (DL) for other notifications received or website search. See Confirmation of the death of an adult or child for resources.

If ACMPS or ACSP state that a care recipient has departed residential care and is:

  • deceased, see Table 3 for the steps to complete the Notification of Death guided procedure using data match
  • not deceased, but has been discharged for a different reason, correct the discharge details on RCIRC screen and create a Fast Note to record information on discharge

3

I012 DSS have advised that aged care resident died on (date)

I022 RCA auto cancelled. Check if income support should be cancelled + Read more ...

Information has been transmitted to Customer First from ACMPS or ACSP to state that a care recipient has died. Confirm entry and discharge details currently coded in:

  • ACMPS - Assessments & Events tab > Entry Details - for Home Care
  • ACSP – Care recipient - Care recipient summary screen. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to search for a care recipient – (CRiC) table for information

See Death of an income support customer or their partner for more information.

If ACMPS or ACSP state that a care recipient has departed home care, further confirmation is required prior to actioning. Check Document List (DL) for other notifications received or website search. See Confirmation of the death of an adult or child for resources.

Confirm that there have not been any corrections made to departure details. If ACMPS or ACSP state that a care recipient has departed residential care and is:

  • deceased, see Table 3 for the steps to complete the Notification of Death guided procedure using data match
  • not deceased and is still in home or residential care, complete an RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM to restore aged care (RCA) status, and delete discharge details on RCIRC screen
  • not deceased, but has been discharged for a different reason, correct the discharge details on RCIRC screen and create a Fast Note to record information on discharge

4

I015 DHA advised the resident was discharged on (date) for reason (reason)

I013 Record income details and cancel RCA after the record is current + Read more ...

  • Care recipient has commenced home or residential care
  • RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM is currently outstanding
  • Discharge from care has been coded in ACMPS or ACSP
  • Discharge event was not able to be finalised due to started RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM

When an entry event is received in Customer First, an automatic (AUT) discharge is coded on RCIRC screen to allow the new entry event to be populated. If a provider subsequently records a discharge, and an RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM is currently started, an MFU will be created to follow up discharge details.

Compare the existing discharge details on RCIRC. If the MFU contains a discharge date that is:

  • earlier than or the same as the discharge currently on RCIRC, finalise the MFU via Activity Result (AR) screen activity as no further action is required
  • later than the discharge currently on RCIRC, further investigation is required. Check entry details for home and residential care. Has the care recipient entered care with a new provider?
    • Yes, ACMPS contains an overlapping discharge date, complete the 'MPB email template - notification of overlapping date' and send to Aged Care Payments (MPB), see the Resources page for the email template
    • No, ACMPS has had a correction made to a discharge date, index an RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM, delete the current discharge details, and code the corrected discharge details

See Aged care means assessment - preliminary checks to action the manual follow up

5

I032RC - Aged Care have advised that customer has not been discharged + Read more ...

When a discharge is coded, RCIRC will be updated and Aged Care (RCA) status will be cancelled based on discharge reason. If discharge is subsequently deleted or corrected:

  • Aged Care (RCA) status must be reinstated if care recipient is still in care, or
  • If discharge date is changed, there may be no effect on Aged Care (RCA) status

Action:

  • Check RCIRC screen to confirm current care details. Also confirm deleted information
  • Confirm entry details currently coded in ACSP:
    • Care recipient - Care recipient summary screen. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to search for a care recipient – (CRiC) table for information
    • Care recipient - Event summary
  • If care recipient is currently in home or residential care and aged care (RCA) assessment status has been cancelled, index an RCA/HOM or RCA/NCL using the entry date as date of receipt (limited to 12 months in past) and finalise based on existing circumstances. Note: if the entry date is more than 12 months ago then use the DOR (Date of Receipt) 12 months in the past
  • Complete Fast Note with action that has been completed
  • Finalise the MFU

Completing Notification of Death guided procedure using data match

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Review record + Read more ...

Use V to view the date of receipt of the MFU activity as this will be needed to complete Notification of Death guided procedure.

If possible, complete activities and run the daily driver (DDR). If there is:

  • a current Quarterly Review or Manual Adjustment work item, add keyword RCADEA with notes that care recipient now deceased. Work item and death action will then be completed
  • any other started activity that cannot be completed, see Step 4 in the Advice of death of a customer who is a member of a pension couple table

Once all started activities have been completed, go to Step 2.

2

Confirm care recipient's details in aged care system + Read more ...

Details have been transmitted to Customer First. Confirm that no subsequent changes have been made. Discharge/departure details are located in:

  • Customer First - the RIS and RCIRC screens
    • Check Discharge Date and Discharge Rsn fields
    • Check Document List (DL) for any other notification received
  • ACSP:
    • Care recipient - Care recipient summary screen to view entry and departure dates. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to search for a care recipient – (CRiC) table for information

Once details have been confirmed, use V to view the date of receipt of the MFU activity. Once date of receipt has been noted, cancel the activity from AL screen.

3

Process the death action + Read more ...

Run the Notification of Death guided procedure to code the death action:

  • Complete the details within the guided procedure:
    • Who contacted: Non-Government Agency
    • Contact Method: External
    • Channel: Electronic Data Transfer
  • Complete the contact details section with the following:
    • First Name: First part of Aged Care service provider
    • Surname: Rest of Aged Care service provider
    • Postal Address Line 1: Aged Care system
    • Line 2: blank
    • Town/Suburb: Data Match
    • Country: Australia
    • State: Australian Capital Territory
    • Postcode: 0000
    • Phone Number: 00 00000000
  • Confirm details of deceased by checking the boxes for:
    • Name
    • CRN
    • Date of Birth
    • Age
  • Code relevant dates:
    • Date of notification: enter MFU date of receipt
    • Date of Death
    • DOR will default and must not be changed

4

Add free text to DOC + Read more ...

Insert free text referring to the notification type:

  • Aged Care MFU received

Once completed the document will display all contact details entered and free text.

Does this care recipient have a surviving partner?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, procedure ends here

5

Check if follow up action is required for surviving partner + Read more ...

If care recipient has a surviving partner, check if an update is required due to them being:

  • RCA/CUR and currently in residential care, or
  • in receipt of an income support payment

See Death of one member of a pensioner couple. Once surviving partner's record has been checked, complete any open Fast Notes or work items.
