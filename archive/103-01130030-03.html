Coding an IM Notice and booking interview

Table 1: This table describes how to code a Child Protection Authority notice received via Unified Government Gateway (UGG) and booking an Initial Assessment interview.

Step

Action

1

Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) receives Notice to Commence Income Management via the Unified Government Gateway (UGG) + Read more ...

The IMCO can view all requests sent by a State or Territory Child Protection Authority by viewing the Third Party Information Summary Organisation (TPISO) screen in Environment B.

Key 'TPISO' in next field.

Complete the below fields:

  • Application Type, select 'Welfare Payment Reform'
  • Business Function, select 'Child Protection'
  • Information Type, select 'Child Protection Send Notice for Income Management'
  • Key the date range in search (the IMCO should be checking this on a regular basis throughout each working day)
  • Status, for the purposes of searching a new notice which has not been auto-processed by the system, select 'unprocessed'
  • Expand the 'extra search components' twisty, and select the relevant state to be searched
  • Select 'Search'

This will display any unprocessed notices that have been received.

Review the details advised in the notice, taking note of relevant information such as the duration to be income managed and the priority needs as ranked by the case worker.

Note: the system will attempt to auto-update any new Notice to Commence Income Management, change the status to pending and record an inactive notice on the customer's record. When automated processing has occurred, no further coding of the notice is required by the IMCO. IMCOs can view the status of automated processing by viewing the messaging under Description on the TPISO screen. (For example, Auto process successful, Outcome pending).

  • If customer already has an Active Notice on their record, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • Otherwise, go to Step 2

2

Confirm customer still meets the requirements for Child Protection Income Management + Read more ...

(See Legislation page Section 123UC of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999). It is important to thoroughly check the eligibility as the customer's circumstances may have changed since the case worker performed the eligibility check and sent the Notice to Commence Income Management.

When a customer does not meet the requirements for Child Protection Income Management, the notice must still be coded on the customer's record as it will be held for the period of the Notice. If in that time the customer meets the requirements, the notice will become Active and Income Management will commence. If the notice expires without having become active, the notice will auto-convert to lapsed.

If automated processing of the notice has not occurred, the IMCO is required to code the Notice in Centrelink system.

Go to Step 3.

3

Coding the Income Management Notice + Read more ...

Open the Child Protection Assessment workflow.

On the Notice screen complete the following fields:

Notice:

  • Receipt Date: will default to today's Date, do not change
  • Period of Notice: select 3, 6, 9 or 12 months. (This information will be provided on the Notice)
  • Status: Select Active or Inactive as appropriate from the drop down menu
  • Start Date: will default and is protected, do not change
  • End date: will be calculated from period of notice (field is protected and cannot be changed) and only displays when the Notice status is Active

Referring Organisation Details:

  • State/Territory: Select the applicable state/territory from the drop down menu
  • Organisation/Office: A list of organisations/offices will display based on the state/territory selected. Select appropriate referring authority from the drop down menu
  • Contact: A list of contacts will display based on the Organisation/Office selected. Select appropriate contact from dropdown list
  • Expense Priorities: replicate priority needs ranked by the case worker in the Notice received

Select 'Continue'.

4

Service Offer screen + Read more ...

Is the customer present?

  • Select 'No' if the customer is not present
  • Select 'Yes' if the customer is present and discuss available service offers. (Note: there would be limited circumstances in which the IMCO would be conducting the initial assessment interview). See Initial Assessment interview for Child Protection Income Management

Is customer eligible for the 20% Unrestricted Cash Payment (UCP)? Refer to the advice provided on the notice.

Select 'Yes' or 'No' depending on the information provided in the notice.

Select 'Continue'.

5

Finalise the assessment + Read more ...

Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page.

The workflow is completed.

Annotate the auto generated DOC with any information required under the guidelines for adding a DOC to a customer's record.

Note: If the case worker provided information in the notice that refers to reasons the customer was referred for Income Management, the IMCO should have contacted the case worker to request the notice be re-sent without this information.

Is the customer eligible to be income managed?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, see Step 9 in Table 2

6

Does customer live in a location that is serviced by the Remote Servicing Team (RST)? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Customer lives in a location that is serviced by the Remote Servicing Team (RST) + Read more ...

Contact the zone RST Co-ordinator to confirm details of the next trip to the customer's community.

Once this discussion has occurred, reconfirm the details via In Confidence Secure email to the remote trip Coordinator to ensure the customer's details are included on the trip intent for the next planned visit to the community. (This process may vary slightly between zones).

The RST will then be responsible for conducting the Initial Assessment interview. See Initial Assessment interview for Child Protection Income Management.

8

Book appointment + Read more ...

See Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System to book appointments using the Customer First guided procedure.

Book the next available Initial Assessment appointment as follows:

  • Appointment Type field, select IM initial (INT) Interview
  • Appointment Channel field, select Face to Face, or another channel as appropriate
  • Service Reason field, select Cape York (CY) Income Management

Note: if the wrong service reason is selected, an incorrect letter will be generated on the customer's record.

Procedure ends here.

Continuation Notice or Notice from a new Authority

Table 2: This table describes how to code a new or continuation notice and finalise an assessment.

Step

Action

1

Continuation of a Notice + Read more ...

A State or Territory Child Protection Authority may lodge a new Notice to Commence Income Management to extend the period of Income Management beyond the current notice expiry date, or a notice may be received from a different Child Protection Authority.

Is the notice from the same Child Protection Authority who lodged the current active notice?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Automatic Continuation notice + Read more ...

A Continuation notice will auto-create when a customer with an active notice receives a continuation notification within 60 days of the current notice's end date.

The notice will have an inactive status as there can only be one active notice on a Child Protection customer's record at any time.

When the customer's current notice expires, the Continuation notice will automatically become active and Income Management will continue uninterrupted.

A message will display under Description on the TPISO screen indicating the results of the auto-processing, including where the automated process was unsuccessful in which case manual processing is required.

Was the automated processing of the continuation notice successful?

  • Yes, book an appointment for the customer to discuss the extension of Income Management and review their circumstances. Procedure ends here
  • No, manually record the continuation notice, go to Step 4

3

Notice from a new Child Protection Authority + Read more ...

If a new Notice to Commence Income Management is received from a different Child Protection Authority than the existing active notice, an inactive notice will auto-create on the customer's record (subject to usual business rules being satisfied).

A message will display under Description on the TPISO screen indicating the results of the automated processing, including where the automated process was unsuccessful and manual processing is required.

Note: if the notice includes an Unrestricted Cash Payment (UCP) service offer, the customer will be identified as eligible for the service offer in the automated process.

If the automated process is not successful IMCOs must manually create an inactive notice on the customer's record using the Child Protection Assessment workflow.

Was the automated processing of the new authority's notice successful?

  • Yes, the customer will be sent a letter advising Income Management will be continuing under a new authority and they will need to contact Services Australia to arrange an interview. Procedure ends here
  • No, manually record the notice, go to Step 4

4

Coding a new or continuation notice if automation process not successful + Read more ...

Manually record the notice on the customer's record via the Child Protection Assessment workflow:

Notice Section:

Receipt Date: Date Notice received

Period of Notice: select 3, 6, 9 or 12 months. (This information will be provided on the Notice)

  • Status: Inactive
  • Start Date: Will Default and is protected

Referring Organisation Details:

State/Territory: Select the applicable state/territory from the drop down menu

Organisation/Office: A list of organisations/offices will display based on the state/territory selected. Select appropriate referring authority from the drop down menu

Contact: A list of contacts will display based on the Organisation/Office selected. Select appropriate contact from dropdown list

Expense Priorities:

Priority Needs: Check if the ranked priorities have changed since the last notice. Make any required changes to reflect the most recent Notice

Note: when the customer's current notice expires or is revoked, the continuation notice will automatically become active and Income Management will continue uninterrupted.

Select Continue. This will navigate to the IM Decision screen.

5

IM Decision screen + Read more ...

Add required information in the Interview/Reassessment section.

  • Reason for reassessment: Other
  • All other fields to remain unchanged.
  • Select Continue to navigate to the Expense Management screen

6

Service Offer screen + Read more ...

Is the customer present?

  • Select 'No' if the customer is not present
  • Select 'Yes' if the customer is present and discuss available service offers (there would be limited circumstances in which the IMCO would be conducting the Initial Assessment interview). See Initial Assessment interview for Child Protection Income Management

Note: If 'Yes' is selected, the Expense Management Summary screen will display. Expenses are only to be adjusted after discussion with the customer. Do not make any changes to expenses without discussing with the customer.

Is customer eligible for the 20% Unrestricted Cash Payment (UCP)? (refer to the advice provided on the notice).

  • Select 'Yes' or 'No' depending on the information provided in the notice
  • Select 'Continue'

7

Finalise the assessment + Read more ...

Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page.

The workflow is completed.

Annotate the auto generated DOC with any additional information such as:

  • date of Notice
  • case manager details
  • any additional information that has been provided by the case manager should be recorded
  • any information required under the guidelines for adding a DOC to a customer's record

Any updates via the Child Protection Assessment workflow will send advice to the Child Protection Authority confirming the notice has been processed.

Procedure ends here.

8

Advise Child Protection Authority Outcome of Notice + Read more ...

Coding of the notice on the customer's record will auto-send information back to the relevant Child Protection Authority advising the customer's Income Management status.

If automation was unsuccessful, the following steps will need to be actioned:

IMCO to:

  • navigate to the TPISO screen in Janus
  • select the customer's notice from the TPISO screen
  • in the 'Record Outcomes' section at the bottom of the notice record the following:
  • is the client eligible to be income managed?
    • No - select no, go to step 9
    • Yes - select 'yes'
  • has the client been switched onto IM under the child Protection Measure?
  • Select 'no'
  • has a notice been coded and an initial interview been scheduled for the client?
  • Select 'yes'
  • code any additional information in the notes section including the date the Initial Assessment appointment has been confirmed, or the week the RST will be visiting the customer's community
  • declaration by the processor:
  • Tick the box 'I confirm that I have completed the actions required to change this request to Pending'
  • select Submit

This will auto-send advice to the relevant Child Protection Authority via the daily reports advising the notice has been recorded and the customer's Income Management status.

Procedure ends here.

9

Customer is not eligible + Read more ...

If the customer is not eligible to be income managed under Child Protection Income Management:

Why has no action been taken to switch the client on to Income Management under the Child Protection initiative?

  • Select the appropriate reason from the dropdown menu
  • Include any further relevant information in the notes
  • Complete the declaration:
    • Tick the box 'I confirm that I have completed the actions required to finalise this request' and submit

This will auto-send advice to the Child Protection Authority via the daily reports advising the notice has been recorded and that the customer is not eligible for Income Management. On receipt of this advice, the case worker may decide to revoke the notice, however this action should not be recorded unless the Revocation notice is received via the UGG.
