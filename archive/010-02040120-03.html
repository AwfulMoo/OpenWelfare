Determining independence when an ABSTUDY customer has returned to live in an Indigenous Australian community

Table 1.

Step

Action

1

Returned to live in an Indigenous Australian community + Read more ...

Is the student or Australian Apprentice:

  • 16 years of age or over, and
  • returned to live in an Indigenous Australian community after being adopted or fostered by a non-Indigenous family for more than 2 years

They are deemed to have returned to live in an Indigenous Australian community if they return to live with family, extended family, or become part of the local Indigenous community.

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Documentation and evidence + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice must provide evidence to support their claim for independence due to returning to live in an Indigenous Australian community after being adopted or fostered.

Evidence may include a statement from:

  • an Indigenous Australian community organisation
  • a well-known and respected person of the Indigenous Australian community, or
  • the relevant state or territory authority

The statement must confirm the student or Australian Apprentice:

  • was adopted or fostered into a non-Indigenous family, or
  • were subject to removal from their biological family by government, religious or other similar organisations, and
  • placed with a non-Indigenous family for a period of 2 years or more, and
  • have returned to live within an Indigenous Australian community

Go to Step 4.

3

Independence status not met + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice is not eligible for grant of independent status for ABSTUDY on the basis they do not meet the criteria of returning to live in an Indigenous Australian community after being adopted or fostered.

  • Consider whether they are independent on other grounds. See Independence for ABSTUDY
  • If appropriate, refer them for other assistance, e.g. social worker or Indigenous Service Officer

Go to Step 6.

4

Procedure when customer contacts + Read more ...

If the student or Australian Apprentice has contacted through:

  • a service centre, go to Step 5
  • Smart Centre Call, go to Step 6
  • the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Services team, see Step 1 in Table 2

5

Scanning documentation + Read more ...

Ensure the claim/form and all supporting documents are scanned to the student or Australian Apprentice's record.

6

Record details of the decision + Read more ...

Record details of the decision or discussion on a DOC.

Coding independence when an ABSTUDY customer has returned to live in an Indigenous Australian community

Table 2. For ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing staff only.

Step

Action

1

Assessing eligibility + Read more ...

Check the evidence provided to determine if the student or Australian Apprentice meets eligibility for independence due to returning to live in an Indigenous Australian community after being adopted or fostered.

Is there enough evidence to support granting independence due to returning to live in an Indigenous Australian community after being adopted or fostered?

  • Yes, if processed in:
    • Customer First, go to Step 6
    • Process Direct, go to Step 2
  • No, if processed in:
    • Customer First, go to Step 7
    • Process Direct, go to Step 5

2

Coding independence details in Process Direct + Read more ...

The independence data prepopulates the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen from information provided in the online claim.

Check the information on the NIH screen.

Key 'NIH' in Super Key or select the My Circs icon.

If the information has not pre-filled or needs changing, code manually. For example, an incorrect start date has been recorded.

If manual coding is required, complete these fields:

  • Action: code 'IND'
  • Start/Reject Reason: code 'PAC'
  • Start Date: code the later of:
    • ABSTUDY start date
    • date the student or Australian Apprentice became independent

3

Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

Is the customer an Australian Apprentice?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Relocation scholarship eligibility + Read more ...

For qualifying students, the Regional Location Details (RLD) screen pre-fills information provided in the online claim. Check the information and dates on the RLD screen are correct.

For information on coding the RLD screen, see Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding.

Go to Step 8.

5

Deleting independence details in Process Direct + Read more ...

If the student or Australian Apprentice does not meet the eligibility for independence due to returning to live in an Indigenous Australian community being adopted or fostered, delete the pre-filled information from the NIH screen.

To delete an entry:

  • key 'NIH' in Super Key or select the My Circs icon
  • select the Delete icon to the left of the line where start/reject reason PAC is coded
  • select Yes to confirm deletion

Go to Step 8.

6

Coding independence details in Customer First + Read more ...

Student or Australian Apprentice meets eligibility for independence due to returning to live in an Indigenous Australian community being adopted or fostered.

Complete the following fields on the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen:

  • Action: code 'IND'
  • Start/Reject Reason: code 'PAC'
  • Start Date: code the later of:
    • ABSTUDY start date
    • date the student or Australian Apprentice became independent

If the student is studying an approved scholarship course, code 'Y' in the To update regional classification details, go to RLD field.

For more information on coding the RLD screen, see Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding.

7

Record details in a DOC + Read more ...

Record details of the decision in a DOC.

Indicate the grounds for granting or rejecting the claim for independence due to returning to live in an Indigenous Australian community.

Procedure ends here.

8

Record notes of the decision in Process Direct + Read more ...

Record details of the decision in Notes or Document annotations.

Indicate the grounds for granting or rejecting the claim for independence due to returning to live in an Indigenous Australian community.
