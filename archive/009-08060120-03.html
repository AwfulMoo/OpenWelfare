Carer neglect tip-off review process - SCCRT

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Review referred to SCCRT + Read more ...

A carer neglect referral will be sent to a Smart Centre Critical Response Team (SCCRT) Officer via email from Business Integrity.

The SCCRT Officer will conduct a basic examination of the tip-off and identify:

  • who the tip-off relates to (for example, carer)
  • third parties mentioned (for example, care receiver)
  • the Customer Reference Number (CRN) of the person the tip-off relates to

Is the tip-off about a person receiving CP and/or CA?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, SCCRT Officer to contact the Business Integrity referring officer and advise the tip-off does not relate to a person receiving CP and/or CA. Procedure ends here

2

SCCRT check the tip-off for information relating to the allegation + Read more ...

SCCRT Officer will examine the information provided in the tip-off and determine what actions are required. They should consider:

  • the source of the allegation
  • the medical condition(s) of the carer receiver and whether this would impact on their capacity to confirm the care they receive
  • the age of the carer and the care receiver
  • if similar allegations have been made previously
  • any other relevant information
  • if further information is required to investigate the tip-off
  • if a referral to a Social Worker is required

The SCCRT Officer may determine further information is required to investigate the tip-off where:

  • there is not enough information on the carer and/or care receiver's record to refute the information contained within the tip-off, or
  • the information provided in the tip-off cannot be validated against information previously provided by the carer and/or care receiver for example, portability

Additional information may include but is not limited to:

  • details provided on the Carer Allowance questionnaire - Carer not living with the person for whom care is being provided (SA381) form. The Resources page contains a link to this form
  • evidence of residential address for the carer and/or care receiver
  • verification of current activity details, study timetable and/or hours, employment hours
  • details of services or other people involved in caring for the care receiver
  • a manual medical review. See Reviews of Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA) for more information

3

SCCRT recommendation and referral + Read more ...

Once the SCCRT Officer has finalised the initial investigation and determined what actions should take place to fully investigate the tip-off, a referral with recommendations will be prepared.

The recommendation will detail the findings of the initial examination of the record and what action must take place to fully review/investigate the tip-off by the Carer Processing Team. The referral must be sent via email to the Workload Management Team. They will then allocate this tip-off review to the most appropriate Carer Processing Team

Procedure ends here for SCCRT.

Carer neglect tip-off review process - Carer Processing Team

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Carer neglect tip-off review allocated to Carer Processing Team + Read more ...

When a carer neglect tip-off is received by a Carer Processing Service Officer there will be actions recommended by the Smart Centre Critical Response Team (SCCRT). The recommendations may include but are not limited to the following:

  • Manually issue a medical review and/or
  • Refer to a Social Worker and/or
  • Phone interview with carer
  • Phone interview with care receiver
  • Request other information

2

Is a manually issued medical report required? + Read more ...

  • Yes, issue the forms via:
    • Request for Information guided procedure
    • Carer Processing Service Officer must create an open DOC detailing the information/forms, referrals, etc. that have been requested. The DOC must be held to the Processing Officer on the customer record with date of expected return. The DOC should not include any details of the allegation. Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Is a Social Work assessment required? + Read more ...

Social workers can provide information that contributes to decisions about qualification. They do not make the decision regarding qualification, they provide assistance in determining CP and/or CA care situations. Social workers can provide information to verify:

  • the care receiver's personal care or supervision needs in the home
  • the level of care or supervision actually being provided by the carer
  • whether any other person assists with the care of the care receiver, and if so whether the CP claimant/recipient is personally providing constant care
  • whether the carer has the physical and/or emotional capacity to deal with the care receiver's care needs, if the carer is under 18 years of age, or 80 years of age or older
  • where there are concerns about the capacity of the carer to provide an appropriate level of care, regardless of their age, for example the carer's own health/mental health needs

If:

  • Yes, create a referral using the Refer to Social Work Guided Procedure. From the care receiver's record, select CSAT Processing Team - Service Zone Referral. To maintain the care receiver's privacy and safety, the referral should not be run from the carer's record. Select the zone where the customer and care receiver live. The referral must not include any details of the allegation. The referral should include clear details of the reason a social work assessment is required. A note can also be included for the social worker to contact the referring officer for further information. For referrals, see Social Worker assessment of carer situations for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) claims
    • Once the social worker report is finalised, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Social worker report finalised + Read more ...

Has further information been requested by the Carer Processing Service Officer? For example, medical review forms, verification documents

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

Note: where the social worker is unable to contact the carer and/or care receiver, a recommendation is to be made based on the available evidence on the record.

5

Check if information and/or medical review forms have been returned + Read more ...

Has the carer returned all of the requested information and/or forms?

  • Yes, record all information.
    • If this results in the cancellation of CP and/or CA, procedure ends here.
    • If this does not result in the cancellation of CP and/or CA, a telephone interview with the carer is mandatory. If telephone contact is unable to be made with the carer, an Invitation to attend interview (Q001) letter must be sent asking the carer to contact the Carer Processing Service Officer for a telephone interview at a set date and time. Following the interview with the carer, phone contact may also be required with the care receiver or third party (for example, treating health professional) to gather further information about the care situation. Go to Step 6
  • No, suspend the CP and/or CA 'failed to reply to correspondence' using the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow. Procedure ends here

6

Phone interview + Read more ...

The Carer Processing Service Officer is required to contact the carer for a telephone interview. Contact with the care receiver or third party may also be required to determine whether the care receiver's condition or circumstances have changed.

Due to the nature of the contact, information obtained must be relevant to the investigation being undertaken and to confirm the carer still qualifies for CP and/or CA.

Discuss and update (where required) the carer and care receiver circumstances including:

  • contact details of the carer and care receiver
  • the address details of the carer and care receiver. For CA, if the carer and care receiver are no longer residing together, issue a Carer Allowance Questionnaire (SA381) form. See Eligibility for Carer Allowance (CA) (adult) when caring in a private home not shared by the adult and carer for further information
  • any temporary absences (for example, hospitalisation, respite). If the carer indicates that there has been any absences, these details must be coded on the Absences Detail (ABSN) screen. For further information, see Absences for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA)
  • temporary cessation of care (respite) and hospitalisation
  • changes to the care receiver's medical condition or the amount of care required/provided. If the medical condition has improved significantly or the amount of care has reduced, consider sending a manual medical review if this has not already been requested in Step 2
  • involvement in any paid or voluntary work, study or training (for CP carers only). Update the details on the Carer Payment Activity Summary (CPAS) screen to record the absence. See Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self employment, voluntary work, education or training activities for further information

Note: where there is more than one care receiver, a discussion should take place regarding all care receivers.

Once the phone interview(s) have been completed go to Step 7

7

Finalise investigation + Read more ...

The Carer Processing Service Officer is to determine ongoing eligibility for CP and/or CA based on the information received both in writing and verbally. All information is to be recorded on the carer and/or care receiver's records.

Finalise the investigation by:

  • updating both the carer and care receiver's records with the information received in writing and verbally
  • creating a comprehensive DOC on the carer's record outlining the updates that have been made to the record(s) and the information received from the carer. Take care when recording any information provided by the care receiver and/or third party as this may be of a sensitive nature and should not be recorded on the carer’s record. Information provided by the care receiver and/or third party may be better recorded on the care receiver's record
  • including the outcome of the investigation and the decision in the DOC

Does the carer continue to meet the qualification of CP and/or CA?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No,
    • Contact the carer to discuss the outcome
    • Explain qualification requirements and the review and appeal rights
    • See Carer cancellation and suspension workflow for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) for more information
