Actioning Service Updates and Reviews

Calculating Review Results and Restoration Processing in the IRS

This table describes the process for calculating a Review Result and applying Restoration Processing for a Service Update or Review in the Integrated Review System (IRS).

Step

Action

1

Finalising a Service Update or Review + Read more ...

Once a Service Update or Review has been finalised, no further updates can be made to the Service Update or Review in the Integrated Review System (IRS) unless that action is to:

  • manually attribute a debt to a finalised Service Update or Host Review
  • amend a Random Sample Surveys (RSS)
  • automatically attribute a restoration, grant or re-grant action to a review as part of Restoration Processing, or
  • automatically attribute a second consecutive 'CAN'cellation action if a 'CAN'cellation action is already attributed to the review and the review is in the Applicable Restoration Period (ARP)

The Review Result of the review is displayed on the Review Results (IRRR) screen. The Results Component (IRRC) screen displays the results of individual payment components in addition to providing the activity numbers which caused the rate variation.

The ARP end dates for each payment component are located on the IRRR screen. The ARP end date is removed once the end date has been reached.

The Gross Result is visible by typing 'G' in the Result Type: field on the IRRR or IRRC screens.

Note: an Earned Income Intervention (EII) has separate rules applied to identify the Result Reason code for a PRT (Present Rate to Continue) result. See the Resources page for a list of Review Result codes and Result Reason codes used in IRS.

2

Determine if the Service Update or Review is subject to Restoration Processing + Read more ...

Is the Service Update or Review subject to Restoration Processing?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Service Update or Review is not subject to Restoration Processing + Read more ...

A 'CAN'cellation, 'SUS'pension or 'REJ'ection action is not attributed to the review, and no payment components were stopped as part the Service Update or Review.

The Review Status of the review will have 'COM'pleted directly after the review was 'FIN'alised in IRS.

The Gross and Net Results are the same.

Procedure ends here.

4

Service Update or Review is subject to Restoration Processing + Read more ...

Did Restoration Processing end before the review was finalised?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Restoration Processing has not ended + Read more ...

The review will complete 42 days after the date the action to 'CAN'cel, 'SUS'pend, 'REJ'ect or stop the customer’s payment was processed (unless the Date of Effect of the action is in the future, then that date is used as the start date).

Restoration Processing is invoked and IRS will automatically attribute any action to restore, re-grant or grant the customer an alternative payment if that action occurs with the Applicable Restoration Period (ARP). The 'COM'pleted Review Result will include these actions and adjust the Review Result accordingly.

The gross and net results will reflect different 'FIN'alised and 'COM'pleted dates.

The gross and net results will only be the same if the customer's payment is not restored, re-granted or another alternative payment is granted before the ARP ends.

Procedure ends here.

6

Restoration Processing has ended + Read more ...

The review will be 'COM'pleted directly after the review is 'FIN'alised in IRS.

The gross and net dates will be the same.
