Contact applicant to objection

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Initial contact applicant + Read more ...

If a payer objects to a decision to accept an application for assessment on grounds other than disputed parentage they may apply for Child Support to suspend payments pending the objection decision. See Suspending disbursements of child support and the References page for links to the Child Support Guide.

Within 2 business days of receiving the objection intray, make 1 outbound contact attempt to the applicant on all available numbers. Send a pre-call SMS to help contact the customer.

If the 1st contact attempt in unsuccessful, make 2 additional attempts on all numbers, within 2 business days of receiving the objection intray at different times of the day, before issuing letters.

If the customer has multiple cases, they must lodge a separate objection for each case upon which they are objecting to a decision.

Does the objection relate to issues other than care percentage decisions with notification dates on or after 1 July 2010 (3 March 2011 for WA ex-nups)?

  • Yes. go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Care objection + Read more

Encourage the customer (including non-mutual customers) to lodge their objection with the agency that made the original decision.

Exception: If the customer is only objecting to the revocation date of the care decision (as opposed to the date of event), Child Support must make the objection decision, even if the original care percentage decision was made by Family Assistance.

If Family Assistance made the original care percentage decision, explain to the customer how the decision affects their child support assessment. If the customer requires further explanation of how the decision was made, refer the customer to Family Assistance once all Child Support related matters have been resolved.

3

Care objections - Non-mutual customers + Read more

If the customer is not a mutual customer and the original decision was made by Family Assistance:

  • warm transfer the customer to Family Assistance
  • provide the Family Assistance officer with the details of both parties to the assessment, the child/ren involved and all the care decisions the customer would like to object to

Family Assistance will need to confirm that Child Support has conducted an identity check for the non-mutual customer and locate the mutual customer in ISIS (Centrelink customer data base) before they can take the call.

If a non-mutual customer contacts Family Assistance to lodge a review to a care decision that affects them, Family Assistance are unable to confirm the customer's identity. In this situation, Family Assistance must conference call Child Support. Child Support will:

  • conduct an identity check for the non-mutual customer
  • confirm the Identity Confirmation has been successfully conducted with Family Assistance
  • provide Family Assistance with the mutual customer details and details of the relevant care decision

When this has occurred, Family Assistance will proceed with the review for the non-mutual customer.

If the customer is a non-mutual customer, Child Support must record some basic information about the objection in Cuba. See Objections Cuba Process Help.

4

Care objections - Duplicate applications + Read more ...

If the customer is only objecting to the revocation date of the care percentage decision (as opposed to the date of event), Child Support must make the objection decision. As the customer is objecting to the particulars of the assessment, not a care percentage decision, they will need to object in writing.

If Centrelink are conducting a review by an Authorised Review Officer (ARO) or a Subject Matter Expert (SME) for the same decision, see Step 2 in the Taking care objection by phone.

5

Customer contact + Read more ...

Use the Objection First Contact macro to document the discussion with the customer, including:

  • if there is no reasonable basis to conclude that the original decision may be wrong:
    • manage their expectations about the objection decision.
      Note: while it is appropriate to manage the customer's expectations about the objection, ensure the customer is aware all relevant information will be considered before making the decision
    • obtain further information about the reasons for their objection
    • determine what they are trying to achieve with their objection
    • if, after discussion with the applicant, there remains no reasonable basis to conclude that the original decision may be wrong, advise the customer that their objection is likely to be disallowed
  • their concerns about the original decision and what they hope to achieve by lodging an objection
  • clarification of information previously discussed with the decision maker or other Service Officer
  • confirmation of their intention to lodge an objection
  • the objections process, including Child Support open exchange of information policy. See Open exchange of information for Child Support customers
  • administrative options available to resolve their concerns
  • the applicant may withdraw their objection at any time before the decision has been finalised
  • any request for additional information to help determine whether an objection can be made, including extension of time or clarification of what decision is being objected against
  • evidence requested to establish the claims including clear timeframes for providing evidence
  • possible outcomes, including potential contrary or adverse decision
  • ongoing liability must continue to be paid while the objection is in progress
  • minimum collection activity if a debt exists, using the Collection and debt macro

Debt repayment negotiations must occur when an objection is in progress. The outcome of the objection cannot be pre-empted.

Note: ensure the customer is aware that information gathered may not be exchanged with the other party during the objections process, however all relevant information may be exchanged if the matter progresses to the AAT. See Open Exchange of Information for child support customers for further information.

Offensive contact and FDV content

If there is any offensive content:

  • No call attempts are required
  • Send a letter to the applicant advising the objection cannot be accepted because it contains offensive content

Policy advice is required before making any decision about whether the objection should be treated as improperly made.

If it is determined, an objection contains offensive information or has FDV content concerns, encourage the customer to withdraw the objection and resubmit it without the offending content.

If the applicant withdraws their objection, explain to the applicant:

  • it is their choice to withdraw the objection
  • they cannot request an Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) review of a withdrawn objection as no decision has been made
  • the other party may lodge their own objection. If the other parent has already lodged their own objection and does not want to withdraw, the objection will continue
  • there is a cooling off period of 48 hours before finalising the application as 'withdrawn' in Cuba
  • the onus is on the applicant to contact the Objections Service Officer within the 48 hour cooling off period if they decide to change their decision about withdrawing their objection
  • they can object at any time, however if the applicant objects outside the 28 day timeframe (90 days for customers in reciprocating jurisdictions) they will need to request an extension of time for objection to proceed
  • if the objection is about a care percentage decision (including date of event of a care change), the applicant can object at any time, however, if the respondent objects outside the 28 day timeframe (90 days for customers in reciprocating jurisdictions) any change to the care percentage will only apply to the assessment from the date the objection was lodged, unless there were special circumstances that impacted on the respondent's ability to lodge an objection earlier
  • they will received a copy of the application (response or objection etc) and documents

If the customer refuses to withdraw the objection, tell the customer their application cannot be accepted because it is not properly made.

The timeframes for determining if an objection has been received in time should be based on where the applicant lives, not the postal address. Where the applicant lives will not always match a person’s Residency as recorded in Cuba Residency window.

Where the Objections Officer forms the view that a contrary decision will be made, the likely outcome of the objection should not be discussed with the customer at any stage of the process and the option of a withdrawal should not be proactively offered to the applicant as this may result in an unfair assessment being left unchanged.

Create a notepad in the Objections Maintenance window and paste in the document created by the Objection First Contact macro with the subject line 'Applicant First Contact'. See Create or pend a new note of Documentation Cuba Process Help.

After this contact has occurred and the objection is to proceed, lock the Respondent to own position in Cuba.

Invalid objections

If the objection is invalid, including where no response to an invitation for an EOT has been received, send a letter to the applicant to advise the objection is invalid and the reason why, phone contact is not required.

6

Part 6A objections + Read more ...

For Part 6A objections, use the financial circumstances worksheet to record the financial information. If the applicant cannot provide the information during the initial call, arrange a time to call back.

Note: financial information does not need to be obtained if it has recently been supplied by the customer.

7

Further evidence requested + Read more ...

If further evidence has been requested from the applicant:

  • create an Obj Reply Req intray with a due date of either the date the information is due, or the following day. See Intray management Cuba Process Help
  • document any additional information requested from the applicant and timeframes for returning evidence in a notepad in the Objection Maintenance window with the subject heading 'Information required from applicant'. See Documenting Child Support information in Cuba

Contact the applicant on the Obj Reply Req intray due date if the evidence requested has not been provided. Ask if they intend to provide further evidence. See Contact with Child Support customers. Make 2 phone contact attempts to the applicant across the day.

If the applicant advises they have additional information, ask the applicant to provide the information within the shortest possible timeframe based on their preferred communication channel.

If the applicant:

  • cannot be contacted and further information is required, go to Step 8
  • is continuing with the objection or they cannot be contacted and no further information is required, see Step 3 in Send acknowledgement letters table
  • is objecting to a decision about remission of a penalty payable by either the payer or an employer (contact with the respondent is not required), see Send acknowledgment letters
  • did not intend to lodge an objection, see Step 4 in Finalise and process objection decision of Make decision and finalise Child Support objection
  • no longer wants to object, see Step 5 in Finalise and process objection decision of Make decision and finalise Child Support objection.

8

Contact unsuccessful + Read more ...

Create a new notepad in the Objection Maintenance window with the subject heading 'Information required from applicant' and specify what information is required if:

  • further information is required to establish if an objection has been lodged
  • there are concerns about the customer's intention to object
  • the objection was lodged out of time and requires an extension of time application

Write to the applicant, go to Step 2 in Send acknowledgement letters.

If the customer does not contact Child Support in response to the letter and:

  • the Objections Service Officer cannot be satisfied that an objection has been lodged, go to Make decision and finalise Child Support objection, Finalise objection Step 4, or
  • the objection cannot proceed (for example it was lodged out of time without an EOT), go to Make decision and finalise Child Support objection, Finalise objection Step 5

If customer contact is unsuccessful and there are concerns about exchanging the information in the objection, the objection (including Open Exchange of information) must proceed if:

  • the applicant has recently been informed about the open exchange requirements and the option of self-editing
  • the Objections Service Officer is satisfied that an objection has been lodged and is properly made

Go to Contact respondent table.

If there are concerns about exchanging the information and the applicant has not been informed about the open exchange requirements:

  • in the objection record create a new notepad in the Objection Maintenance window with the subject heading 'Information required from applicant'
  • document that information is recorded in the objection that the customer may wish to edit before the information is exchanged

Write to the applicant again. Go to Step 2 in Send acknowledgement letters.

If the customer does not contact Child Support in response to the letter and the Objections Service Officer is satisfied that an objection has been lodged and is properly made, the objection (including Open Exchange of Information) has to proceed.

Go to Contact respondent table.

Note: after this contact has occurred and the objection is to proceed, lock the Respondent to own position in Cuba.

Contact respondent to objection

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Contact respondent + Read more ...

Contact the respondent/s to the objection to provide them with the opportunity to respond. Make at least 1 attempt to contact the respondent within 1 business day of successfully contacting the applicant. Send a pre-call SMS to help contact the customer.

If the 1st contact attempt in unsuccessful, make 2 additional attempts on all numbers, within 2 business days of receiving the objection intray at different times of the day, before issuing letters. See Contact with Child Support customers.

Note: there may be more than 1 respondent, for example in a case involving a non-parent carer. Write to the respondent if the first contact attempt is unsuccessful then continue contact attempts by phone, go to Step 3 in Send acknowledgement letters table.

If there is no reasonable basis to conclude that the decision may be wrong advise the respondent:

  • the objection is likely to be disallowed
  • that all relevant information will be considered before making the objection decision
  • if further relevant information or evidence becomes available which may increase the likelihood of the objection being allowed, the respondent will be provided with an opportunity to comment

2

Document + Read more ...

Use the Objection First Contact macro to document the discussion, including:

  • that an objection has been received
  • the decision the objection relates to
  • the grounds for the objection
  • any concerns the respondent may have with the decision
  • the applicant may withdraw their objection at any time before the decision has been finalised
  • if the respondent wants to ensure an objection decision is made, they may consider lodging a separate objection to the same decision. This will enable the full merit review to continue even if the applicant elects to withdraw their objection. They can object at any time, however, if they object outside the 28-day timeframe (90 days for customers in reciprocating jurisdictions) they will need to request an extension of time for the objection to proceed. If the objection is about a care percentage decision and they object outside the 28/90 day timeframe any change to the care percentage will only apply to the assessment from the date the objection was lodged unless there were special circumstances
  • administrative options available to address the respondent's concerns
  • the objection process, including open exchange of information. See Open exchange of information for Child Support customers
  • evidence required to establish any claims of the applicant and/or respondent
  • encouraging a verbal or written response in the shortest possible timeframe advising customer of option to complete the Responding to an Objection form (CS4243). The Resources page contains a link to this form
  • if the customer indicates difficulties in providing a timely response, advise they have a maximum of 28 days from the date they receive a copy of the objection to provide their response (90 days if 1 of the customers is living in a reciprocating jurisdiction)
  • possible outcomes of the objection, including potential contrary or adverse decision
  • Minimum Collection Activity if appropriate. See Common and specialised activities for Child Support staff

3

Part 6A objections + Read more ...

For Part 6A objections, use the financial circumstances worksheet to record the financial information. If the respondent cannot provide the information during the initial call, arrange a time to call back.

Note: financial information does not need to be obtained if it has been recently supplied by the customer.

4

Evidence + Read more ...

If the respondent advises they will not be providing further response or evidence, ask them to confirm this and seek their agreement to proceed with finalising the objection without waiting for the 28 day response period.

Note: if a decision is made within 28 days and the respondent subsequently provides additional evidence within this time, consideration should be given to making the initial decision invalid and making a new decision. Discuss options with the Team Leader. See Correcting errors on Child Support cases. Create a notepad in the Objections Maintenance window. Paste in the document created by the Objection First Contact macro with the subject line 'Respondent First Contact'. See Create or pend a new note of Documentation Cuba Process Help.

The timeframes for determining if an objection has been received in time should be based on where the applicant lives, not the postal address. Where the applicant lives will not always match a person’s Residency recorded in Cuba Residency window.

5

Further evidence required + Read more

If further evidence has been requested from the respondent:

  • create an Obj Reply Req intray with a due date of either the date the information is due, or the following day. See Intray management Cuba Process Help
  • create a new notepad in the Objection Maintenance window with the subject heading 'Information required from respondent' and document any additional information requested and expected timeframes for return of the evidence from the respondent

Contact the respondent on the Obj Reply Req intray due date if the evidence requested has not been provided. Ask if they intend to provide further evidence. Make 2 phone contact attempts to the respondent across the day.

 

If the respondent advises they have additional information, ask the respondent to provide the information within a suitable timeframe based on their preferred delivery channel.

Go to Send acknowledgement letters table.

Send acknowledgement letters to applicant and respondent

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Send acknowledgement letters + Read more

Ensure any documents forwarded are reviewed and edited if required in accordance with open exchange of information. See Open exchange of information for Child Support customers.

Do not send acknowledgement letters for care objections taken over the phone if:

  • the applicant has formally acknowledged the objection has been taken over the phone
  • the respondent has been contacted and confirmed they have been provided a right of reply

2

Write to applicant + Read more ...

After successfully or unsuccessfully contacting the applicant, undertake the following:

  • Use the Objection letters macro to generate either 'acknowledgement of objection' (no extension of time) text or the 'acknowledgement of objection' (with extension of time) text
  • If contact has been unsuccessful and/ or additional information is required, use the UQ50.02 - Request call re-Objection grounds or EOT - Applicant letter to request the required information
  • Paste the 'acknowledgement of objection' and if required the 'request call re grounds or EOT – applicant text into an MX01 -Unique letter. See Letters Cuba Process Help
  • Update the Objection Maintenance window notepad to reflect the letters sent. See Documentation Cuba Process Help, View and update a note

If evidence has been requested from the applicant, create an Obj Reply Req intray with a due date of either the date the information is due, or the following day. See Intray management Cuba Process Help. If there is no response by that date call the applicant to determine if they are sending the information.

If the applicant advises they have additional information, ask the applicant to provide the information within a suitable timeframe based on their preferred delivery channel.

3

Write to respondent + Read more ...

After successfully contacting the respondent or after 2 phone contact attempts across the day:

  • use the Objection letters macro to generate either 'acknowledgement of objection' (no extension of time) text or the 'acknowledgement of objection' (with extension of time) text
  • paste the 'acknowledgement of objection' respondent text into an MX01 - Unique letter
  • attach to the letter:
    • a copy of the objection letter in accordance with open exchange of information
    • any additional relevant information provided with the objection
    • a summary of additional claims or reasons discussed with the applicant, if applicable
    • update the Objection Maintenance window notepad to reflect the letters sent

If evidence has been requested from the respondent create an Obj Reply Req intray with a due date of either the date the information is due, or the following day. If there has been no successful contact by that date call the respondent to determine if they are sending the information.

If the respondent advises they have additional information, ask the respondent to provide the information within the shortest possible timeframe based on their preferred delivery channel.

Note: use Child Support online where possible to ensure the respondent receives the objection as quickly as possible. See CSAOnline secure message Cuba Process Help.
