Changing or correcting course of study and/or educational institution for ABSTUDY students 102-07020150

  • Background
  • Process Summary
  • Process

Expand all


This document outlines how a change of course of study will affect an ABSTUDY student's payment and how to process the necessary changes.

On this Page:

Study requirements

An ABSTUDY or ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) student needs to be studying a full-time or concessional study load, with the exception of students who qualify for the Part-time Award, in an approved course to qualify for their payment. An ABSTUDY PES student must also be receiving 1 of the qualifying payments for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and ABSTUDY PES.

Change of course

If a student tells Services Australia they are changing courses, find out if:

  • the new course and institution are approved,
  • they are still within their reasonable time and
  • they have not exceeded limits of assistance

If the student is changing to an unapproved course or institution, they will only be eligible for the agency's administered payments for students if they are undertaking an approved Indigenous special course.

Note: if an ABSTUDY student discontinues and then subsequently starts full-time or concessional study-load study, in either the same course or a different course, they remain entitled to ABSTUDY Living Allowance if the period between is no greater than 28 days.

Where an ABSTUDY student completes a course, then starts another course, code new study details.

Where a student was approved to live away from home and course needs to be ended, see Cessation of the ABSTUDY away from home rate for additional coding that may be required.

Where a student starts a new course and is approved to live away from home, see Coding away from home rate for ABSTUDY for additional coding requirements.

Online accounts

Students registered online can view their personal information including study details by selecting My Details > Personal and contact details > Manage study details menu option on their Centrelink online account or on their mobile device using the Express Plus applications.

Students may notify the change to their study details by updating their details online if the student provides a change to their study details, the record may generate a DOC with details of the change for follow up action to be undertaken by a Service Officer.

Grandfathered ABSTUDY PES

Students who receive ABSTUDY PES and transferred to JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA), will stop being eligible for ABSTUDY PES if they defer or withdraw before the end of their course if they:

  • are in the Disability Support Pension (DSP) transition group and assessed as having a partial capacity to work, or
  • stopped receiving Parenting Payment Single (PPS) due to their youngest child turning 8 years of age

If they subsequently resume the same course, they are still not eligible for ABSTUDY PES except for ABSTUDY PES students who resume the same course within 28 days.

Changing courses and potential debts

Changing details of the course including Student Start or Student End date on the Education Course History (EDCH) screen can also affect entitlement for Relocation Scholarship (RS). See Scholarship study requirements.
