This page contains information on the procedure to request deletions of digital images from Centrelink customer records. A deletion might be required when documents (for example, letters or faxes) have been scanned or faxed in error, or inappropriate documents have been uploaded online and require deletion.

Deleting scanned, faxed or uploaded images

Step

Action

1

Deleting images + Read more ...

Images must be deleted from a customer record when:

  • incorrect information has been attached or uploaded
  • the document or documents contain inappropriate or offensive content

Failure to request deletion of inappropriate images from customer records may result in a breach of privacy.

If the image is:

  • attached to an incorrect record, go to Moving and/or updating Centrelink digital images via Document Tools
  • inappropriate, offensive, or threatening to staff, customers, or a site, go to Step 2

2

Image is inappropriate, offensive or threatening to staff, customers or a site + Read more ...

All threatening or offensive documents must be treated in the same way as other forms of customer aggression.

Service Officers:

  • Notify a Team Leader/Manager immediately
  • If an image contains threats of suicide, self-harm or concern for the customer's wellbeing, see Customers talking about suicide or self-harm
  • When advised by your Team Leader/Manager or technical support:
    • Complete an Enterprise Content Records Management (ECRM) Deletion Request, the Resources page has a link
    • ECRM will permanently delete the image

If the image is viewed during manual classification escalate the document to Classification Support for deletion. Do not attach the image to the customer record.

If the deletion request form is not available email the following information to ECRM Support:

  • Full Document ID
  • Reason for the deletion, e.g. 'This is not related to Centrelink business.’

Team Leaders/Managers:

  • Assess content containing threats as per emergency plans and local response guidelines if required. A link to the guidelines is located on the Resources page
  • Requesting the deletion of an image may not be sufficient action if the threatening or offensive information is potentially relating to illegal acts
  • The image may need to be printed and follow up contact with customer required. For the steps required across each service brand, see Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression
  • Delete the image after an assessment is made and relevant follow up completed

If support is required after viewing an inappropriate or offensive image, go to Step 3

3

Accessing support after viewing an inappropriate or offensive image + Read more ...

Staff affected by an inappropriate or offensive image should speak to a Team Leader or manager who is able to provide critical incident reporting and support to staff.

The Employee Assistance Program (EAP) intranet page provides information to support employees, including contact details for the EAP in each state. The Resources page contains a link to the EAP homepage.

The Safety Reference Centre supports the Work Health and Safety Policy and Guidelines. The intention is to provide a self-service function for all managers, employees, Health and Safety representatives and members of Health and Safety committees.
