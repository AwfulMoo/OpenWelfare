Pre 2010-11 FTB Reco Summary Screen + Read more ...

  • ‘S’elect the appropriate reconciliation/Lump Sum Claim result from the FRCS screen to go to the FTB Calculation Result (FACRF) screen
  • The FACRF screen contains information for each of the calculation periods:
    • Period begin - column displays start date of period being assessed
    • Days - column displays number of days within the calculation period
    • Legisl - column displays legislative entitlement amount for the period
    • MCFF - column displays More Choice for Families options
    • Paid - column displays amount of FTB previously paid
    • Reco - column displays FTB entitlement amount within the period
    • Difference - column displays difference between the previous assessment and new assessment. It may help identify the source of the overpayment if it is only applying for certain periods. This usually means a change of circumstance has occurred to change the assessment result, not income
    • Sub Total line - displays the sub total for the full financial year for each column
  • Input 'B' at the sub total line will link to the FTB Component Calculation Result (FCCR) screen. This screen displays each component of FTB assessed in the financial year being viewed. This includes FTB Part A supplement prior to 1 July 2018, FTB Part B and supplements. 'S'elect the supplement line to display the FTB Component Calculation Result Summary (FCCRS) screen which breaks down the FTB Part A and Part B supplements and SIFS components and will display the amount that has been included in the reconciliation or Lump sum claim period
  • 'S'electing a period from the FACRF screen will link to the FTB Calculation Period Assessment (FCPA) screen, which displays further information and more options to choose from
  • FTB (A) status - displays if FTB Part A is current
  • 'S'electing FTB (A) status line, links to the FTB Calculation Period Assessment Part A (FCPAA) screen - this screen displays information used in the calculation of FTB Part A for the calculation period including: blended family details, income support status, rent assistance assessment details, relationship details (including partner details), income used in the assessment process and maintenance income used in the assessment
  • 'S'electing the FTB income field links to the FTB Calculation Period Income Summary (FICS) screen which displays income components and the source of the income (that is, AIN - Actual income provided by the ATO; MRE - Most recent estimate of income; FYE - Financial year estimate of income)
  • Entering 'G' in the Maintenance Income field links to the FTB Financial Yr Maint Expln (FYME) screen which provides additional information relating to the assessment of maintenance income
  • Enter/Continue to FCPA screen
  • FTB (B) status - displays if FTB Part B is current. 'S'electing this field will display additional information about the secondary income earner
  • FTB (B) secondary earner - displays whose income is being used for FTB Part B. 'S'electing this field will display information about any Part B quarantine assessments relating to a return to work in the relevant period
  • Child details display - selecting this field will link to the FTB Child Eligibility Summary (FCES) screen which displays the children linked to the record. Child eligibility details, including summary of immunisation requirements, shared care, child deferral, maintenance action test are displayed by 's'electing the child record. *No updates can be made from this screen. This is a view only screen
  • Child count display - selecting this field will link to FCEC screen. This screen displays the FTB components and number of children linked to each component. This is a view only screen

Newborn Supplement (NBS), Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU), Stillborn Baby Payment (SBP), Schoolkids Bonus + Read more ...

Lump sum claim

As NBS is a component of FTB Part A, eligibility for this payment will be automatically assessed when the lump sum claim is processed. It can be used in debt offsetting or may be included in the top-up. If NBS is to be paid then the NBU will be paid. Note: NBU can be used to offset any previous NBU debt.

Reconciliation

If reconciliation results in a reduced rate or nil rate of FTB Part A, and NBS has been paid, the overpayment may also include NBS, e.g. if paid above base rate but only entitled to tapered base rate or nil entitlement.

Debt offsetting may occur if eligible for FTB Part A and NBS in the new financial year. See Debt offsetting during family assistance reassessment, reconciliation and claim processing.

Note: NBU can be used to offset any previous NBU debt.

NBU is a one-off payment which is not included as part of the FTB Part A calculation. As a result, it is not included in the reconciliation process but can be impacted if qualification to NBS is gained or lost as a result of reconciliation. NBU will automatically be raised as a qualification debt.

If reconciliation results in nil entitlement to NBS for the relevant financial year, they may be eligible to receive NBU/NBS in the following financial year if they qualify for FTB Part A prior to the child turning 1 (for parent and entrustment category) or within 52 weeks for adoptions.

See Eligibility for Newborn Supplement (NBS) and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU).

Stillborn Baby Payment (SBP)

If SBP is paid under the FTB Part A eligibility test, it is subject to the FTB reconciliation process.

If reconciliation results in nil FTB Part A entitlement for the day that SBP was paid, the SBP will be raised as a debt. If entitled to FTB Part A in the following financial year and eligible for a day within 52 weeks of the delivery date of the stillborn child, the SBP debt will be offset with any SBP entitlement paid in that period.

SBP can be used to offset any automatically created determined, undetermined or waived (less than $50) SBP debts and any existing family assistance debts that do not have a current recovery arrangement in place.

Schoolkids Bonus

Schoolkids Bonus ceased from 31 December 2016. The final instalment was payable for the 30 June 2016 bonus test day.

Notification requirements had to be met by 30 June 2017 to receive a Schoolkids Bonus payment for the final bonus test days on 1 January and 30 June 2016 due to FTB entitlement for a child.

However, Schoolkids Bonus payments and debts may still result from FTB reconciliation for the 2012-13 through to 2015-16 financial years if FTB entitlement is reassessed for those years.

For 2014-15 and 2015-16 an income test applied. Schoolkids Bonus was only payable if the adjusted taxable actual income was $100,000 or less for the relevant financial year. This means that payments and debts may result due to actual income details being notified for these years.

For more information, see Schoolkids Bonus.

5

Immunisation and Healthy Start for School (HSFS) + Read more ...

From 2018-19 onwards, an FTB Part A rate reduction is applied to the maximum rate of FTB Part A that can be paid for a child that does not meet immunisation or HSFS (if applicable) requirements.

At reconciliation:

  • The FTB Part A rate reduction due to immunisation is applied to any FTB immunisation grace period days for children that did not meet the immunisation requirements before the end of the FTB immunisation grace period (FTB instalment periods only)
  • If a customer is serving an FTB Part A immunisation grace period on 30 June, FTB reconciliation will not occur until the child meets the immunisation requirements or the FTB immunisation grace period ends (whichever occurs first).
  • For FTB lump sum claims, the child's immunisation status is checked and used to determine the rate of FTB Part A for the entire claim period. If the child's immunisation status is unknown, the claim will remain pending.

Information about the child's immunisation and health check status can be viewed on the FTB Child Eligibility Details (FCED) screen which is accessed by 's'electing the child from the FTB Child Eligibility Summary (FCES) screen. Generic RRD and Reason fields display whether an FTB Part A rate reduction has been applied and the reason (immunisation or HSFS)

For previous financial years, customers had until 30 June of the lodgement year (unless a special circumstances extension has been granted) to meet the following requirements for payment of the FTB Part A supplement:

  • immunisation (for financial years between 2012-13 and 2017-18)
  • Healthy Start for School (for financial years between 2011-12 and 2017-18) requirements (if applicable)

6

Unable to unpack and discuss the reconciliation result + Read more ...

Service Officers not trained in reconciliation are to transfer reconciliation enquiries (involving child support, changes in care, special adjustments etc.) to the Families and Parenting queue.

All trained Service Officers should be able to answer basic enquiries about reconciliation.

If a trained Service Officer is unable to resolve the enquiry:

  • Smart Centre Service Officers should escalate the call to a Families and Parenting Technical Peer Support (TPS) staff member/floorwalker.
  • Service Centre Service Officers should escalate to a Technical Peer Support (TPS) staff member via Access Support Centre
  • Service Delivery Partner Staff should escalate to a floorwalker/follow local Technical Support Model for assistance

Technical Peer Support (TPS) staff/floorwalker unable to unpack the result will advise the Service Officer to escalate the enquiry using the Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Families > Reco > FTB reco escalation for an appropriately skilled Service Officer to contact the customer to discuss the complex reconciliation enquiry. The Fast Note must include:

  • the logon of the TPS/floorwalker consulted
  • full details about the query

Note: the automatic inclusion of keyword 'EXPFTB' will ensure the activity is automatically allocated to the appropriately skilled Service Officer, removing the need to 'transfer' the Fast Note to a specific region. Unless directed, reconciliation enquiries must not be referred to the Manual Intervention (MIV) team, escalated to Service Support Officers or referred to the customer's local service centre.

System issues

The only reconciliation issues to be escalated to a Service Support Officer are system issues. Service Officers and TPSs must thoroughly investigate the record before escalating. If unsure about the type of income used in reconciliation, see Previous partners and Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and Child Care Benefit (CCB) reconciliation.

Tell the customer they will be contacted within the next few days by a Service Officer who will discuss the decision.

For Service Officers, procedure ends here.

Finalise the enquiry

Record what action or discussion has been undertaken in a DOC.

The minimum information to be recorded on a DOC for enquiries about a top-up received as a result of reconciliation is:

  • detail what the enquiry was about and the results discussed which should include whether the Part A and Part B supplements and SIFS were included, and the amounts paid. If the Part A or Part B supplements or SIFS were not included, detail the reason why
  • include any additional information about the reconciliation result which was discussed, e.g. any amounts used for offsetting or debt recovery

If no further action is needed after discussing the decision, procedure ends here.

If the customer does not understand or does not agree with the decision, provide the customer with their internal review options.
