I101Q1 Customer needs to be switched onto NIM

NIM assessment required for customer

Table 1: This table details the Manual Follow-up (MFU) activities created, and the associated actions for users, when a Nominee Income Management (NIM) assessment is required for a customer. Note: in this table any references to principal have the same meaning as customer.

Step

Action

1

Payment nominee on IM. Customer needs to be switched onto NIM + Read more ...

This MFU will present to the appropriately skill tagged staff in Workload Management and is created when the principal is not on NIM and their payment nominee is on an eligible Income Management measure that qualifies them for NIM.

2

Confirm appropriateness of the nominee arrangement + Read more ...

Confirm the appropriateness of the nominee arrangement before assessing the principal's eligibility for NIM.

Is the payment nominee income managed under:

  • Child Protection
  • Supporting People at Risk
  • Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient
    • Yes, the payment nominee arrangement must be reviewed to determine it is in the best interests of the principal. Go to Step 3
    • No, go to Step 4

3

Review of payment nominee relationship + Read more ...

If there is no DOCument on the customer's record confirming a recent review has been conducted, or there are concerns about the arrangement, contact an Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) or social worker to arrange a review. This investigation is to occur before applying NIM or sending out invitation to contact letters.

Has the review determined the arrangement is no longer appropriate?

  • Yes, the arrangement should be ended and details of action taken DOCumented on the record. For more information, see Cancelling a nominee arrangement. Procedure ends here
  • No, the review confirmed the arrangement is to remain in place. The IMCO/social worker who conducted the review must DOC the record before NBG assesses the customer's eligibility for NIM. Go to Step 4

Note: if the IMCO/social worker requires additional time to follow up the arrangement, hold the MFU for 3 days pending IMCO advice. DOC the action taken on the customer's record.

4

Is the customer a CDC participant? + Read more ...

Go to the Debit Card Trial Assessment Summary (Q2AS) screen in Customer First and check the Debit Card Trial Assessment History for the customer's CDC status.

If the customer has commenced on the CDC, the Debit Card Trial Assessment History displays with the customer's CDC start date.

Is the customer a CDC participant?

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for NIM. Place the work item on hold for 7 days and email INCOME.MANAGEMENT.HELPDESK with the customer's CRN and ask for the 'I101Q1. Customer needs to be switched on to NIM' MFU to be removed. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Eligibility for Nominee Income Management + Read more ...

Assess customer's eligibility for NIM:

  • Customer is not eligible for NIM (for example, nominee arrangement has ended or the payment nominee is no longer income managed), go to Step 6
  • Customer is eligible for Income Management in their own right under another measure, go to Step 7
  • Customer is eligible for NIM, go to Step 9
  • Customer is re-identified for NIM within 13 weeks after it has ended, go to Step 13

6

Customer not eligible for Nominee Income Management + Read more ...

If it is determined the customer is not eligible for NIM:

  • Record the details of investigation in a DOC on the principal's record
  • Finalise the MFU, and if necessary manually tick off the Work Item

Note: the principal does not have to be in receipt of an income support payment to be eligible for NIM. See Nominee Income Management customer not receiving payments for more information.

Procedure ends here.

7

Customer income managed in their own right under another measure + Read more ...

Check the Income Management summary screen to confirm the customer's payments are currently being income managed (excluding NIM).

Has Income Management commenced?

  • Yes:
    • Run the nominee IM Assessment workflow which will automatically apply NIM in the background
    • Create a DOA DOC on the customer and payment nominee's record for this to be discussed at their next contact
    • Finalise the MFU and, if necessary, manually tick off the Work Item
    • Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • Navigate to the Check IM Eligibility screen to see if the customer has been manually coded as ineligible. If so, refer the CRN to an Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) for the ineligibility coding to be removed
    • Check the History Summary (HS) screen to confirm an invitation to contact letter has been issued for the other qualifying Income Management measure. If no letter has been issued, refer the CRN to the Income Management Contact Officers (IMCO) who will issue the invitation to contact letter
    • Place the MFU on hold for 28 (for urban customers) or 56 (for remote customers) days and issue the following Online Advice letters (OLA):
      • Create a DOA DOC on the principal's and payment nominee's record for NIM to be discussed at their next contact
      • When the MFU matures, reconfirm the customer's eligibility for NIM, and whether the other qualifying measure has commenced
    • Go to Step 8

8

Customer income managed and other measure commenced + Read more ...

Has the other qualifying IM measure commenced?

  • Yes:
    • Run the nominee IM Assessment workflow which will automatically apply NIM in the background
    • Annotate the DOA DOC previously created to advise NIM has been applied in the background
    • Finalise the MFU and, if necessary, manually tick off the Work Item
    • No letters are issued for these updates
    • Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • Apply NIM via the nominee IM Assessment workflow
    • Annotate the DOA DOC previously created to advise NIM has commenced and an Initial Assessment interview is required
    • No letters are issued for these updates, reasonable attempts are to be made to contact the customer and/or nominee via phone to advise them NIM has commenced. All contact attempts are to be DOCumented on the customer's record
    • Finalise the MFU and, if necessary, manually tick off the Work Item
    • Procedure ends here

9

Customer eligible for Nominee Income Management + Read more ...

Once the customer's eligibility for NIM has been confirmed and they are not income managed in their own right:

  • Create a DOA DOC on the principal and payment nominee's record for an Initial Assessment interview to be conducted with the payment nominee (and if possible the principal) at their next contact
  • Place the MFU on hold for 28 (for urban customers) or 56 (for remote customers) days
  • Issue the following Online Advice (OLA) letters:
    • Q746 - invitation letter to payment nominee
    • Q748 - Customer invitation to contact letter
  • Go to Step 10

10

Attendance at Initial Assessment interview + Read more ...

When the MFU matures, check the record to confirm whether an Initial Assessment interview has been conducted within the engagement period.

Has the payment nominee attended an Initial Assessment interview?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 12

11

NIM applied + Read more ...

Has NIM been applied?

  • Yes:
    • Issue the following Online Advice (OLA) letters:
      • Q745 - Income Management on advice to payment nominee
      • Q749 - Income Management on advice to customer
    • If an ACTDOC was sent to Region Code IMT with the keyword ACTIMT requesting OLAs be sent, complete the DOC
    • Finalise the MFU, and, if necessary, manually tick off the Work Item
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 12

12

Commencing Nominee Income Management + Read more ...

NIM is to be commenced if a payment nominee has not attended an Initial Assessment interview or they have not contacted within their engagement period.

  • Apply NIM via the nominee IM Assessment workflow
  • Annotate the DOA DOC previously created to advise NIM has been applied and an Initial Assessment interview is still required
  • Issue the following Online Advice (OLA) letters:
    • Q745 - Income Management on advice to payment nominee
    • Q749 - Income Management on advice to customer
  • Finalise the MFU and, if necessary, manually tick off the Work Item once NIM has been applied

Procedure ends here.

13

Customer is re-identified for NIM within 13 weeks of it ending + Read more ...

When a customer's NIM is ended as they are no longer eligible, and they become eligible within 13 weeks of it ending, NIM is to be reinstated without any new engagement period.

Has it been less than 13 weeks since NIM ended?

  • Yes:
    • Apply NIM via the nominee IM Assessment workflow
    • Create a DOA DOC on the customer and payment nominee's records to discuss reinstatement of NIM at their next contact
    • Reasonable attempts are to be made to contact the customer and/or nominee via phone to advise them NIM has been reinstated. All contact attempts are to be DOCumented on the customer's record
    • No letters are to be issued
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, the usual engagement period applies. Follow this process for customers identified for NIM

  • they have a new payment nominee who is not on an eligible Income Management measure, go to Step 3

Issue Online Advice (OLA) letters + Read more ...

NIM ended and will not be reinstated:

  • Issue the following Online Advice (OLA) letters:
    • Q794 - Income Management end letter to payment nominee
    • Q793 - Income Management end letter to customer
  • If an ACTDOC was sent to Region Code IMT with the keyword ACTIMT requesting OLAs be sent due to NIM being ended, complete the DOC

Procedure ends here.

4

Customer has a new payment nominee who is not on an eligible Income Management measure + Read more ...

When the customer appoints a new payment nominee, it is important action is taken as soon as possible to ensure the previous payment nominee does not have access to the customer's income managed funds.

  • If the BasicsCard was issued in the name of the payment nominee, the BasicsCard is to be closed immediately. See BasicsCard close an account for further information. Any remaining funds will be returned to the customer's Income Management account
  • Go to the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen to confirm the payment destination. Check the 'GPY' payment destination is that of the new nominee. To update, select 'GPY' to go to the Payment Destination Details (PAD) screen where details can be updated
  • End NIM via the nominee IM Assessment workflow
  • The disbursements workflow will automatically present. Proceed through this workflow, selecting customer is not likely to become eligible again within 60 days
  • If the customer's current Income Management account balance will not meet existing regular expenses, end the expenses on the Expense Management Summary Screen
  • Record details of decision and action taken in a DOC on the customer's record
  • Issue the following Online Advice (OLA) letters:
    • Q794 - Income Management end letter to payment nominee
    • Q793 - Income Management end letter to customer

If an ACTDOC was sent to Region Code IMT with the keyword ACTIMT requesting OLAs be sent due to NIM being ended, complete the DOC.

  • Finalise the MFU and, if necessary, manually tick off the Work Item

Nominee Income Management customer not receiving payments

Action required for NIM customer not receiving payments

Table 5: This table details the action required for customers who have been profiled for NIM and are not receiving an income support payment. Note: in this process, any references to 'principal' have the same meaning as 'customer'.

Step

Action

1

NIM customer not receiving payments + Read more ...

NIM is still applied (in the background) where a principal is not in receipt of an income support payment.

If the principal has a payment granted or restored, their payments will be automatically income managed from the date of grant.

Is the customer no longer receiving a payment and has a BasicsCard and/or funds in their Income Management account?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here

2

NIM customer no longer receiving a payment and they have a BasicsCard and/or Income Management funds + Read more ...

  • NIM will remain in place even when a customer is no longer in receipt of an income support payment
  • If a customer no longer receives a payment but has an active BasicsCard and/or funds in their Income Management account, create the following reviews on the customer's record:
    • 14 days from the date the customer's payment was cancelled. On maturity of this review, cancel any regular expenses
    • 60 days from when the customer's payment was cancelled. On maturity of this review, close the BasicsCard to recall any funds to the Income Management account and issue any residual funds as a Restricted Direct Payment (RDP). If the balance of their Income Management account is $210 or more, this will need to be disbursed via regular RDPs

Note: RDPs are required as there is no functionality to issue Unrestricted Cash Payments (UCP) for Nominee Income Management customers.

Q745 - Issued to Payment Nominee - Nominee Income Management commenced letter + Read more ...

An Online Advice (OLA) is to be issued to the payment nominee when a customer for whom they are the payment nominee commences Nominee Income Management.

Note: a Q745 letter is not required when:

  • the customer is income managed in their own right and NIM is only applied in the background, or
  • NIM has been reinstated within 13 weeks of it ending

Obtain information for letter:

  • payment nominee's name
  • customer's name
  • the start date for Income Management. Note: the date is to be entered in full (for example, 01 January 2017)
  • whether the payment nominee has attended an Initial Assessment interview or Income Management is being applied as no contact was made within the engagement period
  • Income Management percentage (in round figures, e.g. 50)
  • details of any current regular Income Management expenses (payee, amount, frequency), and
  • BasicsCard details (if active, whether in the name of the customer or payment nominee)

Create letter:

  • Go to the payment nominee's record
  • Navigate to the OLA Accept Service Reasons (OASR) screen
  • In the Service Reason: field, key 'INM'
  • In the Advice Letter Code: field, key 'Q745' and press [Enter]
  • The Letter Display (LDT) screen will display

Quality assurance and finalise letter:

  • Using the information obtained complete the required fields in the letter by selecting each appropriate option, then press [Continue/Enter]. Navigate through the letter by using the [Previous/Next] page buttons until all fields are completed.
  • Print a draft version of the letter via EMAILME for quality checking:
    • While still on the LDD screen, key 'DRAFT' in the Command: field and press [Enter]
    • The LDT screen will display. The system will prompt if any required fields are not completed. On the LDT screen key 'HS' in the Nxt: field. Press [Enter]
    • The History Summary (HS) screen will display with letter status (ST) of DR
    • The draft letter will be sent via email. Once received provide this to a quality checking officer for a quality assurance check
  • When the letter has passed quality assurance checking, key 'FIN' (finalise) in the Command: field and press [Enter] to finalise the letter
  • Refer to Creating an online advice (OLA) for further assistance with finalising or cancelling letters

Procedure ends here.

Q749 - Issued to customer - Nominee Income Management has commenced + Read more ...

  • NIM has commenced
  • the customer is transferring to NIM as they are no longer income managed in their own right, or
