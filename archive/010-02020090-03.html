This page contains the steps to assess ABSTUDY Incidentals Additional Allowance (SY031).

ABSTUDY form SY031

Assessing Form m (SY031)

This table describes the steps involved in assessing the ABSTUDY Additional Incidentals Allowance claim (SY031).

Step

Action

1

Check claim form and record lodgement + Read more ...

Ensure that the ABSTUDY Additional Incidentals Allowance Claim Form m (SY031) has been checked - and is scanned onto the customer's record.

2

ABSTUDY awards + Read more ...

Is the student qualified for one of the following ABSTUDY awards?

  • Tertiary Award
  • Masters and Doctorate Award

Note: this means the student either is currently receiving ABSTUDY Living Allowance, ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) or they are current but on a nil rate due to means testing.

  • Yes, go to Step 3.
  • No, the student is not eligible to receive Additional Incidentals Allowance. Advise the student of the decision and their rights of review of decision and appeal, and record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here.

3

Determining essential course costs + Read more ...

Check the claim has been certified by the Head of School/Faculty of the education institution confirming that the items listed are essential for all students studying the course.

Determine whether or not each item listed on page 4 of the claim is considered to be an essential course cost.

Course costs that are considered essential include:

  • Education institution fees - including union, sports, library, administration, amenities, laboratory fees or levies charged by an approved education institution, and
  • Textbooks and equipment - including clinical instruments, books, published articles, stationery and other equipment items,

Course costs not considered essential include:

  • tuition or course fees charged by the institution, including the flying time and associated fees charged for aviation courses, and
  • items which would be expected to be provided by the education institution such as musical instruments, cameras, videos, sewing machines, typewriters or computers.

Add up the amounts of the essential course costs that have been determined are approved essential course costs.

4

Total amount of approved essential course costs + Read more ...

Is the total amount of the approved essential course costs more than the Additional Incidentals prescribed amount relevant to the length of the course being undertaken by the student?

Note: the claim may include necessary items which have not yet been purchased, although the evidence of expenditure, such as receipts, is required to cover the bulk of the expenditure.

  • Yes, go to Step 5.
  • No, the student is not eligible to receive Additional Incidentals Allowance. Advise the student of the decision and their rights of review of decision and appeal, and record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here.

5

Course details + Read more ...

Access the student's record in the ISS system and ensure the following fields on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen have been completed correctly, as the Prescribed amount for Additional Incidentals Allowance is determined by the duration of the student's enrolment:

  • Inst Course Start Date: field - ensure that the correct start date of enrolment is recorded
  • Inst Course End Date: field - ensure the correct course end date is recorded. Check the student's proof of enrolment for these details

Note: for more information on coding this screen, see Coding education details.

6

Determining additional incidentals amount payable + Read more ...

Determine the amount of Additional Incidentals Allowance payable:

  • Subtract the Additional Incidentals prescribed amount from the total amount of the approved items.
  • The remaining amount will be the amount payable.

Record the above calculation in the 'Office use only' box on the form.

7

Updating EPSP screen + Read more ...

Record the amount payable on the Supplementary Education Payments (EPSP) screen by completing the following fields:

  • Event Date: field - record either the processing date or the date the relevant course began.
  • Payment Type: field - code 'AIN'
  • Reason: field - leave blank
  • Amount Claimed: field - optional field
  • Amount Paid: field - record the amount which is to be paid to the customer
  • Payee: field - code 'CUS' (customer)
  • Claim Details: field - record details which are relevant to the customer's situation relating to the payment they are claiming. Note: this is an optional free text field.
  • Frequency: field - record details to reflect the frequency the customer's requested payment is to be paid. This is usually 'ONE' (one off payment) for this particular allowance.

Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

8

Record DOC and create a Manual Follow-up + Read more ...

Record any items that have not been purchased yet on a DOC.

Create a manual follow-up for the return of the receipts for these items. The receipts should be provided within three weeks of the payment being made, otherwise the payment for these items will be regarded as an overpayment.

When the MFU matures:

All receipts have been provided and the total amount matches the payment issued, finalise the MFU.

Partial or no receipts provided, calculate the amount of Additional Incidentals paid to the customer to match the receipts. Any Additional Incidentals paid where no receipts have not been provided will need to be recovered.

Select the EPSS screen and complete the following fields:

  • Amount Paid: field – will be the amount of Additional Incidentals that receipts have been provided for. For example, Additional Incidentals paid for $1025, receipts received total $750, therefore Amount Paid field correct to $750.00 and overpayment raised of $275.00.

Complete the Source, DOR and Action as ‘Correct’ continue to the AR screen. An overpayment will be raised and sent for Investigation. Record a DOC, procedure ends here.

9

Storage of receipts and DOC + Read more ...

Store the receipts provided with the claim on the customer's file, and record outcome on a DOC.
