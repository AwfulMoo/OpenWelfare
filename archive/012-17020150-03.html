This page contains information about checking a veteran's eligibility, how to process footwear modification and repair items and the business rules for processing DVA podiatry service claims and relevant coding for Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

Determining eligibility and assessing claims for white card holders

Verifying DVA health card holders eligibility when system returns 2-CHK-ELIG

This table describes the steps to follow to verify a DVA health card holders eligibility before paying or rejecting podiatry services when the system returns '2-CHK-ELIG'

Step

Action

1

White card for podiatry treatment + Read more ...

As podiatry services are specific to a particular area of the body, providers do not need to specify the condition being treated, as long as the DVA card holder’s accepted disabilities match a condition listed in step 3 of this table.

Provisional Access to Medical Treatment (PAMT)

For Veterans with PAMT eligibility, provider must specify 1 or more of the 20 PAMT conditions listed. For more information see Patient eligibility for Veterans’ Affairs Processing (VAP).

See Resources page for links to the DVA website for more information.

2

View DVA card holder's listed entitlement + Read more ...

Press [PF10] to view their listed entitlements.

A list is displayed of conditions they are eligible to claim.

3

Review card holder's conditions + Read more ...

The following conditions entitle a DVA card holder to podiatry treatment:

  • calcaneus
  • callosities
  • cancer
  • cervical/neck
  • corns
  • calluses
  • claw toes
  • diabetes
  • hallux valgus
  • hallex
  • metatarsals/metatarsus
  • os calcis
  • pes
  • pes planus
  • plantar warts
  • tinea pedis
  • talipes
  • peripheral vascular disease
  • ankle related injury or disability
  • foot related injury or disability
  • leg related injury or disability
  • knee related injury or disability
  • back related injury or disability
  • hip related injury or disability
  • skin diseases or conditions

4

Determine if entitled for listed podiatry condition + Read more ...

Is the condition recorded on the card holder's listed entitlements (per Step 2) and a listed podiatry condition (per Step 3)?

  • Yes, key '3L' in the PI field to make payment
  • No,
    • If it is not included on their listed entitlements, go to Step 5
    • If it is not a listed podiatry condition, go to Step 6
    • If neither applies, go to Step 7

5

Condition not on card holder's listed entitlements + Read more ...

Requires Prior Financial Authorisation. Check PARS for an approval.

Is there a current PARS approval present or the items claimed?

  • Yes, key '3L' in the PI field to make payment. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7.

6

Determine if DVA confirmation required - condition not podiatry + Read more ...

If a condition has been listed on the treatment voucher and matches one of the card holder's listed entitlements but is not podiatry related, DVA confirmation may be required before payment is made.

Email the VAP eligibility query form to the VAP Helpdesk and pend the claim until a response is received. See Resources page for a link to the form. For contact details, see Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP).

Is eligibility confirmed?

  • Yes, key '3L' in the PI field to make payment. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Reject services + Read more ...

Reject the service with RSN code S 583 if:

  • there is no current PARS approval, or
  • the condition recorded under [PF10] does not match any of the card holder's listed entitlements

Footwear modifications and repairs F611-12 and F615

Process footwear modifications and repairs

This table describes how Service Officers process Medical Grade Footwear (MGF) items F611, F612 and F615 for VAP.

Step

Action

1

Check and process amount claimed for MGF + Read more ...

Is the amount claimed lower than or equal to $100, excluding GST?

  • Yes, override with PI 18 and select [PF9] to confirm the fee
  • No, go to Step 2

See also: Footwear Modifications and Repairs fee schedule (DVA website). The Resources page contains a link to the DVA website.

2

Check for prior approval + Read more ...

Check for prior financial authorisation where the amount claimed is greater than $100.

Requires Prior Financial Authorisation. Check PARS for an approval.

Is there a current PARS approval present or the item claimed?

  • Yes, override with PI 18 and select [PF9] to confirm the fee
  • No, reject the item with reason RSN code S 279

Note: select [PF1] within the claim, to display the maximum amount claimable both with and without prior financial approval.

Consultation items F004-999

Actions required for podiatry consultation items

This table describes the rules, restrictions and actions required for processing items F004-F999.

Item

Description and resolution

F004, F024

Initial consultation - rooms, in-home + Read more ...

After 1 October 2019

Rules

  • Limit of 1 service in 14 days from any provider
  • Limit of 12 services in 365 days from any provider

Restrictions

If an initial consultation restricts with:

  • itself or another podiatry initial consultation item, reject with RSN code S 160
  • any other item, reject the item with the lower fee with RSN code S 159

Before 1 October 2019

Rules

  • Initial consultation limit of 2 in 365 days
  • Only 1 consult item can be claimed per day and cannot be claimed on the same day as an initial and subsequent consultation for the same patient

Restrictions

  • Itself or another initial consultation, reject with RSN code S 160
  • Any other item, reject item with lower fee with RSN code S 159

Note: item F004 (initial podiatry consultation) covers a footwear assessment and a consultation. Only 1 initial consultation and 1 initial footwear assessment item can be claimed per referral. An initial consultation and an initial footwear assessment item cannot be claimed on the same day.

F029, F065

Initial consultation - low-care residential aged care service, private hospital + Read more ...

Item

Description and resolution

F201-F222 and F265-F268

Complete orthoses + Read more ...

Complete orthoses items

Rules

  • Only one pair of orthoses should be provided at any one time. DVA expects entitled persons to transfer the orthoses between their various shoes as necessary
  • Complete orthoses items (F201-F222 and F265-F268) cover the complete initial device including plain covers, posts, dispensing and follow up consultations
  • Only 1 each of F222 or F266, or 2 each of F221 or F265 can be provided within a 365 day period. Additional provision of these devices requires prior financial authorisation from DVA

  • Any item, Check PARS for an approval. Is Prior Financial Authorisation present?

Actions required for miscellaneous podiatry items

This table describes the rules, restrictions and actions required for processing items F984-F999.

Item

Description and resolution

F984, F985

Non-DVA schedule services - taxable, GST-free + Read more ...

Non-DVA schedule podiatry items, excluding GST

Requires Prior Financial Authorisation. Check PARS for an approval.

Is Prior Financial Authorisation present?

  • Yes, pay service with PI18
  • No, reject service with RSN code S 279
  • Not covered by the current approval, reject service with RSN code S 283

F986, F987

Consumables clinically required - during consult/treatment, for treatment after consultation + Read more ...

Rules

  • DVA expects each Orthomechanical item claimed is either customised or custom-built for the patient. Standard, unmodified orthoses should not be claimed as orthomechanical items, these should be provided using either the appropriate consumables item (F986 or F987) or through DVA’s Rehabilitation Appliances Program (RAP). The Resources page has a link to Notes for Dental and Allied Health Providers available on the Department of Veterans' Affairs website.

Restrictions

If a provider claims an amount for a single F986/F987 that exceeds the maximum fee, excluding GST:

  • pay the maximum fee allowable and apply RSN 288

If a provider claims multiple F986/F987 items, check that the total amount claimed excluding GST does not exceed the daily fee limit. If the daily fee limit is:

  • exceeded, reduce the amount of the items to the daily limit, insert RSN 288, and reject any additional consumable services with RSN code S 160
  • not exceeded, pay the items with PI 45

F998

Postage + Read more ...

Rules

  • When items are required to be posted to the veteran, Item F998 is used to claim for postage
  • No referral or prior approval is required for this item
  • Postage item should be exclusive of GST, not exceeding the maximum amount payable. DVA will automatically add GST to the amount claimed

No item restrictions apply.

F470, F546, F547, F548

Surgical podiatry - avulsion of nail plate, nail edge avulsion and matrix sterilisation - single edge/total nail/each additional edge + Read more ...

Rules

  • Items F470, F546 & F547 include 2 follow up consultations and/or dressings

Restrictions

  • Any item, reject item with lower fee with RSN code S 159

F990

Podiatry - report or service specifically requested by DVA + Read more ...

Rules

  • Benefit claimed for F990 is excluding GST
  • Providers may use item number F990 only when DVA contacts them directly to request that they provide a:
    • written report; or
    • consultation or assessment to eligible veterans or war widow/ers, either separately or in conjunction with a written report

For example, this may occur when DVA requires a second opinion concerning treatment for a veteran. DVA will give financial authorisation and advise the fee at the time of the request, according to the relevant schedule items. The kilometre allowance is included in the fee, and is not to be claimed in addition to the fee.

Note: this item does not cover the supply of clinical notes, care plans or other information requested by DVA as part of monitoring activities, as these are provided free-of-charge under DVA requirements.

Restrictions

Requires Prior Financial Authorisation. Check PARS for an approval.

Is Prior Financial Authorisation present?

  • Yes, pay service with PI18
  • No, reject service with RSN code S 279
  • Not covered by the current approval, reject service with RSN code S 283

F991

Allied Treatment Cycle Report + Read more ...

Provision of report to GP on outcomes of Treatment Cycle related to the referral.

Rules

  • An initial consultation item in the allied fee schedule has been claimed
  • Any number from 1 to 11 subsequent consultation items from the same fee schedule have been claimed
  • Only 1 report item is payable for each initial consultation
  • The provider must have claimed 1 initial and 1 subsequent consultation
  • Cannot be paid twice on the same day
  • Limit of 1 report item payable within 14 days
  • Limit of 12 report items payable within 12 months

Restrictions

  • Associated consult not claimed, reject with RSN code S 748
  • If the item restricts with itself or any other item, reject item. Use lower fee with RSN code S 159
