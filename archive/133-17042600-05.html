Provider Digital Access (PRODA)

Verifying an account

A user must have at least 3 documents. One must be a Commencement of Identity (CoI) document, and 1 must be a Primary Use in Community (PUiC) document.

Birth certificate jurisdictional variations - information required

+ For TAS, VIC and WA birth certificates with a change of name registered, the new name is the name that will be validated against. The original name recorded in the body of the certificate will not validate.

# QLD birth certificates issued before 1 July 1974 do not show the child’s family name. Users need to enter the father’s family name, or if not recorded, the mother’s family name. If there is a marginal/record history notation on the certificate to the affect that the child has assumed another family name, that family name must be entered.

## A hyphen or an empty name field will be accepted for NSW birth certificates.

### For QLD birth certificates submit numerical data only for registration numbers.

* Date of registration may be different to the date of birth.

Change of name certificate jurisdictional variations - information required

+ A hyphen in the family name field cannot be verified for single name ACT change of name certificates.

++ For TAS, VIC, and WA birth certificates with a change of name registered, the new name is the name that will be validated against. The original name recorded in the body of the certificate will not validate.

# VIC change of name certificates dated from 31 October 1986 to 26 June 2000 can only be verified if the change of name certificate was acquired after 26 June 2000.

## A hyphen or an empty name field will be accepted for NSW marriage certificates
