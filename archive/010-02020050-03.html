2

ABSTUDY Claiming Tool + Read more ...

Applicants completing a claim through the ABSTUDY Claiming Tool can be offered a verbal declaration.

Applicants who do not accept or have not been offered a verbal declaration are required to return a signed Customer Declaration Form (CDF).

An applicant's acceptance to a verbal declaration is recorded on the Verbal Declaration DOC, and on the Progress of ABSTUDY claim DOC.

If the Verbal Declaration DOC:

  • is not recorded, the CDF must be signed and returned
  • records an inappropriate applicant has accepted the verbal declaration, reject the claim

Note: on 28 November 2016, a verbal declaration replaced the requirement for the applicant to return a signed CDF.

3

Checking the new claim (NCL) activity + Read more ...

The ABSTUDY NCL activity must be checked to ensure the correct Source is recorded and the Claim Type and Verbal Declaration keywords have been added.

If the ABSTUDY NCL activity has already been indexed

To check the Source:

  • Select the started ABSTUDY NCL activity from the Activity List (AL) screen
  • Check the Source is recorded as 'SCT'. If an incorrect Source is recorded, the NCL activity will need to be cancelled and the claim will need to be reindexed using the correct Source of 'SCT'

To check the keywords:

  • Select the ABSTUDY NCL activity from the AL screen
  • From the Selection Options screen, select Keywords
  • Check the appropriate claim type keyword has been added:
    • 'ABYNCL' - ABSTUDY Living Allowance
    • 'BOARDER' - ABSTUDY Secondary Boarding
  • Check the appropriate Verbal Declaration keyword has been added:
    • 'VERBNIL' - if the applicant accepted a verbal declaration and was not required to provide any supporting documents before their ABSTUDY claim can be assessed
    • 'VERBDOC' - if the applicant accepted a verbal declaration and was required to provide supporting documents before their ABSTUDY claim can be assessed
    • 'VERBNO' - if the applicant did not accept a verbal declaration

If the Claim Type and Verbal Declaration keywords have not been added:

  • Select the ABSTUDY NCL activity from the AL screen
  • From the Selection Options screen, select (O). Transfer the activity to another site/region
  • Add the appropriate Claim Type keyword:
    • 'ABYNCL' - ABSTUDY Living Allowance
    • 'BOARDER' - ABSTUDY Secondary Boarding
  • Add the appropriate Verbal Declaration keyword:
    • 'VERBNIL'
    • 'VERBDOC'
    • 'VERBNO'

If no ABSTUDY NCL activity is on the AL screen

Index a NCL activity. Ensure the correct Source of 'SCT' is coded when indexing the NCL. For more information, see Indexing a customer's claim on the system.

Select the ABSTUDY NCL activity from the AL screen:

  • From the Selection Options screen, select (O). Transfer the activity to 'ABS'
  • Add the appropriate Claim Type keyword:
    • 'ABYNCL' - ABSTUDY Living Allowance
    • 'BOARDER' - ABSTUDY Secondary Boarding
    • 'ABAPP' - ABSTUDY Apprentice or Trainee
  • Add the appropriate Verbal Declaration keyword:
    • 'VERBNIL'
    • 'VERBDOC'
    • 'VERBNO'

8

Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

To be qualified for income support as an Australian Apprentice, a person must have a current Commonwealth registration number in relation to a full-time Australian Apprenticeship as administered by the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations. Services Australia will obtain the Australian Apprenticeship details from the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations before the Australian Apprentice's claim for ABSTUDY can be approved.

If the customer indicates they are an Apprentice or Trainee, but the Australian Apprenticeship details are not yet current:

  • record the details in the Progress of ABSTUDY Claim DOC
  • contact the customer and advise payments cannot occur until confirmation of their Australian Apprenticeship status is received
  • place the claim on hold for 14 days

The Apprentice claim can continue to be re-held for up to 13 weeks while waiting for the details to be confirmed. Where the details are not confirmed after 13 weeks, reject the claim NAT (Not an Apprentice).

Is the customer an Australian Apprentice?

  • Yes, and they are:
    • eligible for ABSTUDY, go to Step 1 in Table 2
    • not eligible or the Apprentice claim has been held for 13 weeks, go to Step 9
  • No, and they are a Student, go to Step 1 in Table 2

3

Eligibility for Youth Disability Supplement (YDS) + Read more ...

If the customer is under 22 years of age and has a partial capacity to work as assessed by an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt), they will be entitled to Youth Disability Supplement (YDS). The customer must provide evidence (for example, a medical certificate) of their impairment for the assessment to be undertaken.

If the customer has had an ESAt in the last 2 years, which stated they have a partial capacity to work, YDS will pay automatically when ABSTUDY is granted.

Is the customer under 22 years of age and has indicated they have a physical, intellectual or psychological impairment, which prevents them from working for up to 30 hours per week, and the impairment is expected to last for more than 2 years?

  • Yes, and if evidence is provided, refer to an ESAt. If evidence is not supplied, advise the customer it must be provided for an assessment of YDS entitlement.
    Note: if there is a current ESAt on the customer's record stating a partial capacity to work, there is no need to re-refer for an ESAt. Go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

Coding for students + Read more ...

  • Education Payments Start Date (EPDC) - the date of commencement for payment will be automatically calculated by the system however in some circumstances this may need to be manually adjusted. See Calculating the start date for ABSTUDY

Student Start-up Loan (SSL)

If the customer has accepted an SSL, this will need to be coded within the new claim activity. SSL details can be viewed and updated from the Student Start-up Loan Summary (SSLS) screen in Process Direct or !SSLS in Customer First.

See Student Start-up Loan (SSL) for coding details.

Claim lodged to access Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel

Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel is available if:

  • a secondary school or boarding facility requires prospective students to attend interviews or other selection procedures prior to acceptance
  • the student is intending to study in an approved tertiary course of at least one year's duration at the study location, and has completed secondary studies in the previous year

In order to claim this type of travel, a full ABSTUDY assessment needs to be made for the period the student is intending to study.

A secondary boarding facility may need to conduct an interview or selection exercise in their own right and it may not be possible at the time of claiming to confirm the school they are attending. In these cases, special coding instructions are required. See Coding away from home rate for ABSTUDY.

6

Coding for Australian Apprentices + Read more ...

For Australian Apprentices, the mandatory screens for coding details of their Australian Apprenticeship are:

  • Education Course Details (EDC) - insert an EDC screen with a Participation Status of 'APT' (Apprentice/Trainee) from the date the customer became an Australian Apprentice
  • Apprenticeship Details (NAPD) screen. Australian Apprenticeship details need to be coded on the Apprenticeship Details (NAPD) screen
  • Education Payments Start Date (EPDC) - the date of commencement for payment will be automatically calculated by the system. However, in some circumstances, this may need to be manually adjusted. See Calculating the start date for ABSTUDY
  • the Apprentice employer details must be coded on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen

Prior to finalising a claim for an Australian Apprentice, the customer should be contacted to confirm their income for each fortnight since the date they became qualified as an Australian Apprentice.

All Australian Apprentices in receipt of Living Allowance are required to report their income fortnightly. The system will automatically apply the 2-weekly reporting profile to these recipients.

Australian Apprentices who have an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) on their record in the last 2 years, stating a partial capacity to work, may be required to undergo another ESAt to determine if it is still valid.

Until another ESAt is undertaken, Youth Disability Supplement (YDS) will be paid automatically when ABSTUDY is granted. Payment of YDS cannot be stopped manually.

To refer the customer for another ESAt, see Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) Referral.

Service Officers should use 'Change of Circumstances' as the reason code for the ESAt referral.

If the Australian Apprentice is:

  • 22 years of age or over, go to Step 7
  • under 22 years of age, go to Step 8

Customers under 22 years of age + Read more ...

For customers under 22 years of age, the other mandatory screens are:

  • Select Product (SVP) - for more information to update this screen, see Coding income and assets. Note: there is no requirement for account balances where ABSTUDY payments go to the parent/guardians on behalf of the student
  • Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) - complete if the customer is considered to be:
    • independent for ABSTUDY, or
    • a homeless ABSTUDY customer, or
    • qualified for the away from home rate of ABSTUDY
  • Relocation Location Details (RLD) screen - to be coded for students:
    • claiming independence under self supporting regional or remote students, or
    • students studying an approved scholarship course who are independent and disadvantaged by personal circumstances, or dependent and must live away from home

If the customer has not been coded as 'IND' or 'HOM' on the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen:

  • Check the PIT Parent Selection (NPPS) screen. Ensure parents are linked on the customer's record and that the parent's/guardian's Customer Reference Number(s) (CRNs) and Tax File Number(s) (TFNs) are recorded
  • Check the Relationship Summary (NRS) screen - will display a summary of the family's details
  • Check the details of the siblings using the Family Pool Summary guided procedure, or on the Family Pool Summary (NSFP) screen or the Dependent Youth Rate Summary (!DYRSUMM) screen. This screen displays both Active Sibling Information (those siblings that are part of the family pool) and Other Sibling Information (those siblings not considered to be in the family pool). If the sibling information is not correct, swap to the sibling's record to make changes
  • Check the Household Income and Assets (NHI) screen - ensure the parent(s)/guardian(s) details are correctly recorded. If the parent(s)/guardian(s) income information has not been supplied and no Parental Income Test (PIT) exemption applies, make 2 genuine attempts to contact the parent to request the information verbally. Where they provide an estimate, see Assessing and coding parental income for ABSTUDY, Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme and Youth Allowance (YA)
  • Where the parents have completed the MOD JY or cannot be contacted, for coding, see Applying the Parental income Test (PIT) for ABSTUDY and Youth Allowance (YA). Do not reject the claim for failing to provide parental income information, as payment of non-means tested allowances/benefits can still occur for the relevant award
  • If the customer is dependent, code the appropriate CRN on the Payee/Debtor (EYPY) screen. To determine the appropriate payee/debtor, see How and to whom ABSTUDY payments are made.
    Note: the payee/debtor will be the student, Australian Apprentice, or parent/guardian. This screen should not be coded with school/hostel, private board provider or payment nominee's details
  • If the customer is a secondary boarding student in a boarding school or hostel, the School Fees and Boarding Details (EYSB) screen will need to be manually selected from the ABSTUDY Task Selector (EYTS) screen and coded so that the Group 2 School Fees Allowance (SFA) can be paid. See Coding away from home rate for ABSTUDY for coding instructions
  • If the customer is boarding privately, the Supplementary Education Payments (EPSP) screen must be coded for payment of school fees. See Coding away from home rate for ABSTUDY for coding instructions

For claims processed in Customer First:

  • Manually select any other screens on the EYTS screen that have a 'Y' next to them, to confirm that the details on these screens are still current

  • Mandatory screens will be selected

Note: manually select any other screens required to be updated.

Finalise claim + Read more ...

Before finalising a claim for an ABSTUDY customer who has advised they have variable employment income, contact them to confirm the income they were paid for each fortnight since the date of commencement of ABSTUDY. See Recording and correcting employment income details.

If the customer cannot be contacted, DOC the contact attempt within the new claim grant DOC.

ABSTUDY students have access to report their employment income via Centrelink online account up to 6 months after their reporting date providing:

  • the student's benefit status is 'CUR'rent
  • the student is registered for online services
  • there are no other reasons preventing the student from reporting online (for example, SSAF exclusions), and
  • the student is reporting within 22 days of the date of grant of their new claim

ABSTUDY students reporting using any other self service channel (Interactive Voice Response (IVR) or Express Plus App) will not have access to reporting for previous reports greater than 13 days after their report date.

If the ABSTUDY customer has transferred from another payment such as JobSeeker Payment (JSP), ensure any Centrepay deductions from the previous benefit have transferred to ABSTUDY. See Starting, changing and cancelling a Centrepay deduction at the request of a customer.

The Energy Supplement will be paid if the recipient is eligible. If a frequency change is requested, check/update Energy Supplement payment frequency option.

Note: ABSTUDY Masters and Doctorate can only receive the Energy Supplement quarterly in arrears.

  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen if the claim is in Customer First or select Assess to go to Entitlements (ELD) screen in Process Direct
  • Check and finalise the activity
  • Record details of the claim on a DOC
  • Expire the 'Progress of ABSTUDY claim' Display on Access DOC
  • Complete any scanned documents actioned as a part of the claim
  • If the customer has advised that they have a dependent child under 19 years of age in their care and they or their partner are not in receipt of Family Tax Benefit (that is, FTB/CUR or FTB/CZR), see Eligibility for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for individuals. Issue a Q888 letter inviting the customer to check their eligibility for family assistance. See the Resources page for standard text to be included
  • Register a future Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) claim unless the customer is receiving ABSTUDY Living Allowance or has indicated an immediate need for a Health Care Card (HCC) within the ABSTUDY claim form, or lodged a Claim for a Health Care Card (SS050). See Processing Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) claims

    • Student Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • Date of Birth

  • School email address is to be obtained from the Education Institution Summary available by accessing the Reference Data Enquiry screen. Key CODES in the Nxt: field in Customer First:
    • Complete the Search Field 1: by either selecting 'code' or 'name'
    • Complete the Search Text 1: by typing in the code number or name of school, press [Enter]

  • Send the Boarding claim outcome to the Third Party Organisation:

    • Select the Local Print button for schools that do not have an email, and post directly to the school and include a reply paid envelope
    • Record details on a DOC in the student's record
  • Upload a copy of the email to the customer's record using Form Type UNS010
  • Annotate the grant DOC to state that an email has been issued to the institution

13

Email has failed + Read more ...

If the Service Officer receives an email advising the email has failed, due to an error:

  • contact the school to investigate
  • check if the email address has been recorded correctly. If not, or a new email address is provided, update the Reference Data Facility (RDF) and resend the email
    • record a DOC on the school's record noting the time the email was issued
    • procedure ends here
  • where the email address is recorded correctly send Fast Note Action Request for investigation

If the email continually fails, print a copy of the email, post directly to the school and include a reply paid envelope. Record a DOC on the school's record noting the action taken.
