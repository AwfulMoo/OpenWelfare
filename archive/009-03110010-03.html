This page contains the process steps for change of circumstance which may result in cancellation or suspension or CP and/or CA.

Change of circumstance

Table 1

Step

Action

1

A change in circumstances may result in cancellation of CP and/or CA + Read more ...

Service Officers must be mindful of permission to enquire rules and not release information if the person contacting has no permission to enquire.

Service Officers must not:

  • send Fast Notes to CD1 relating to information provided by the carer, or allegations received from third parties or care receivers
  • record allegations the carer is no longer providing care on the carer or care receiver’s record
  • record allegations of family and domestic violence within the carer/care receiver relationship on the carer or care receiver's record

This information is accessible under Freedom of Information (FOI) and places the reporter and the care receiver at risk.

2

A change in circumstances to be investigated + Read more ...

If Service Officers:

  • receive allegations, from any party, relating to carer neglect within the carer/care receiver relationship, or staff suspect there is. See Carer Payment and Carer Allowance carer neglect tip-off reviews. Procedure ends here
  • receive allegations, from any party, relating to potential fraud, or staff suspect there is. See Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption. Procedure ends here
  • identify correspondence has been returned from the carer or care receiver’s address. See Correspondence returned to Centrelink 'Whereabouts unknown'. Procedure ends here
  • are allocated a Manual Follow Up (MFU) for a returned payment. See Replacing direct credit, New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) payments returned from financial institution. Procedure ends here
  • are allocated a work item 'Update your carer details' see Step 1 in Table 3
  • are advised of a change in circumstances and no FDV risks identified
    • if advised by the care receiver or a third party, see Accepting information from and disclosing information to a care receiver or third party. Procedure ends here
    • if advised by the carer, go to Step 3

3

Update the carer’s change of circumstance + Read more ...

If the carer:

  • advises they (the carer) are in gaol or psychiatric confinement, see Carer Allowance (CA) carer is absent and Carer Payment (CP) carer is absent. Procedure ends here
  • advises they are undertaking paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities. See Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities. Procedure ends here
  • advises the care receiver has permanently entered an institution see Care receiver permanently enters or temporarily/permanently leaves an institution. Procedure ends here
  • reports the care receiver has died, see Confirmation of the death of an adult or child. Procedure ends here
  • advises they have separated from their partner, see Change in relationship status from partnered to single. Procedure ends here
  • advises a change of address, see Change of address for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA). Procedure ends here
  • advises the they have ceased to provide care to the care receiver, or the care receiver has left care of the carer, go to Step 4
  • requests to cancel CP and/or CA and does not wish to provide any additional information, see Step 1 in Table 2

4

Change of care/care receiver has left care + Read more ...

Once the circumstance is keyed, the system will auto-cancel CP and/or CA for the carer.

To update a change of care/care receiver leaving care for:

  • CP (with or without CA), go to Step 5
  • CA only, go to Step 7

5

Coding change of care/care receiver has left care for Carer Payment + Read more ...

Access the Carer Receiver Task Selector (CETS) screen:

  • For an adult, or child care receiver with adult Centrelink record:
    • in the care receiver's record
    • go to the Care Receiver Summary (CRS) screen
    • 'S'elect 'Self' and press [Enter]
    • go to Step 6

For a child care receiver (under 16 years of age) with a child record:

  • in the carer's record
  • go to the CRS screen
  • 'S'elect the child leaving care, press [Enter]
  • Go to Step 6

6

Coding change of care/care receiver has left care for Carer Payment continued + Read more ...

Once the CETS screen is displayed for the relevant care receiver, take the following action to update CP:

  • 'S'elect the Care & Institution Details field, press [Enter] to display the CRCI screen
  • Key details in the:
    • Date of Event (DOV) field. This is usually the first 24 hour period in which the care ceased. Date last cared for + 1
    • Carer Number/CRN field: carer's CRN
    • Carer Is field: relationship to the carer
    • Constant Care field. In this case, key 'N'
    • Permanently entered institution field: key ‘N’
    • Source and DOR fields as appropriate, press [Enter]
  • select Add
  • go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • The RIP Consequences (RMC) screen will display showing the ripple effect that will happen. Press [Enter], record the cancellation details on a DOC on the carer’s record, and finalise the activity on the AR screen
  • key DDR in the Next field and press [Enter]

Note: no change will be noticeable on the care receiver's record. It may take some time for the system to reflect the CP cancellation on the carer's record. Exit the carer's record and allow any ripple activities to complete.

Does CA also need to be updated?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Coding change of care/care receiver has left care for Carer Allowance continued + Read more ...

  • Access to the Carer Receiver Task Selector (CETS) screen
  • For an adult, or child care receiver with adult Centrelink record
    • in the care receiver's record
    • go to the Care Receiver Summary (CRS) screen
    • 'S'elect 'Self' and press [Enter]
  • For a child care receiver (under 16 years of age) with a child record:
    • in the carer's record
    • go to the CRS screen
    • 'S'elect the child leaving care, press [Enter]
  • ’S'elect the Care Details field, press [Enter] to display the Care Details (CDCR) screen
  • Key the following fields:
    • Effect Date: This is the date the payment will be cancelled from. Consider and apply the General 14 day notification provisions
    • Carer CRN: select from the dropdown list,
    • Relationship to Carer Receiver field: select from the dropdown list
    • Does the Carer Live Separately field: Yes
    • Paid Relevant Minimum Wage: Y or N depending on the circumstances
    • Provides Daily Care field: No
    • Hours of care per week as verified by Service Officer: 0
    • Hours of care per week as advised by Customer: 0
    • Source and Date of Receipt fields
  • select Add
  • key 'AR' in the Next field and select [Enter] or continue

8

Finalise Activity + Read more ...

Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen. A message will display stating that the updates were applied successfully.

Run the Daily Driver (DDR) to clear the Activity List (AL) screen.

  • Key 'DDR' in the Next field and select [Enter]
  • Go to the carer's record
    • Check the system - Carer Payment (PEN) or Carer Allowance (ISS)
    • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen
    • Service Reason: 'CAR' or 'CDA'
    • Action: 'REA' Reassess
    • Effect Date: Date of Event (DOV) of cancellation
    • Code Source and Date of Receipt (DOR)
    • Key 'AR' in the Next field: and select [Enter] or Continue
    • If the cancellation activity results in an overpayment, the system will navigate to the Assessment Consequences (ASC) screen. Code the Debt Action: field with 'INV' if further investigation is required
    • Finalise the activity at the Assessment Results (AR) screen
    • Record details on a DOC on the carer's record

If the care receiver is a child and the carer is receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB), check whether FTB is still payable. For more details see Child leaves customer's care/custody.

If the carer and care receiver are partners, they may be entitled to be assessed as being separated from their partner due to ill health

Carer requests cancellation of CP and/or CA

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Carer requests cancellation of CP and/or CA + Read more ...

If the carer has requested to cancel CP and/or CA without providing other details, cancel CP and/or CA at the carer level using the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow.

Note: staff can cancel both CP and CA in the same activity, if applicable.

To cancel CP and/or CA:

  • select the Carer cancellation and suspension workflow
  • to cancel CA at the carer level:
    • Reason: from the dropdown box, select CLR: Withdrawn/Voluntary Surrender
    • Date of Effect: This is usually the date the carer requests cancellation (see Resources for example)
  • to cancel CP at the carer level:
    • Reason: from the dropdown box, select CLR: Withdrawn/Voluntary Surrender
    • Date of Effect: This is usually the date the carer requests cancellation
  • Code Source and Receipt Date
  • Select Continue
  • The carer cancellation and suspension workflow will automatically create a DOC using the Notepad function. The DOC will include:
    • suspension or cancellation reason
    • DOE
    • Act Reference
  • Check the DOC and record any additional notes, if needed
  • Select continue
  • Review the AR screen and finalise the activity

Cancellation/Suspension DOC will only display once the activity has been approved in the Quality Online Tool (QOL) tool. Any activities selected for QOL should be released, unless another error is identified.

If the workflow is unavailable or not working go to Step 2.

2

Manual coding - carer requests cancellation of CP and/or CA + Read more ...

In the carer’s record:

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen. In the following fields:
    • Svc Rsn, key 'CAR'
    • Action, key 'CAN'
    • Reason, key 'CLR'
    • Effect Date: This is usually the date the carer requests the cancellation (see Resources for example)
  • Press [Enter]
  • Go to the AR screen and finalise the activity
  • DOC the cancellation on the carer’s record

Action

1

Manual cancellation for CA using Process Direct + Read more ...

If the carer has not completed the 'Update your caring details' online task within 14 days a work item is allocated to Service Officer’s via Process Direct.

Is the carer’s CA current?

Yes,

  • Manually cancel CA 'failed to reply to correspondence' (FRC) in Process Direct
  • Select the work item Caring Detail Review for Carer Allowance. The Customer and Claim information details screen will display
  • Select then select Update DOR to display the Update Date of Receipt screen
  • change the Receipt Date to:
    • Day 15, if it is within the current entitlement period
    • Date paid to + 1, if carer has been paid since day 15
  • select Update > Process
  • from the Task Selector screen, select CJBA - Carers Benefit Action and select Next to display the Care Receiver Benefit Action screen
  • select Add to display Create Care Receiver Benefit Action popup and update
    • Care Receiver Name: If there is more than one care receiver, select the correct care receiver’s name
    • Action: CAN - Cancel
    • Reason: FRC - Failed to supply requested information
    • Date of Effect: Receipt Date
  • select Save
  • a provisional cancellation activity will appear on the Care Receiver Benefit Action (CJBA) screen. Select Next
  • select Assess
  • the carer’s entitlement to CA is displayed on the ELD screen
  • check the expected outcome has occurred. Select Finish
  • a pop up window will display with prepopulated text, including the Social Security Act reference. Add additional comments regarding the decision in Notes. The comments will appear in Notes tab
  • select Finish. The work item Carer Allowance Questionnaire Review status will change to completed
  • Procedure ends here

No, go to Step 2.

2

Cancel task in Process Direct + Read more ...

If the carer’s CA has already been cancelled and the work item no longer needs to be updated, staff will need to cancel the task.

In Process Direct, select Inbox:

  • Main Category: Online Intervention
  • CRN: Customers CRN
  • select Go
  • from the Work item list, select the open Carer Allowance Questionnaire Review
  • select Status > > Cancelled > Reason, select appropriate code > Save
