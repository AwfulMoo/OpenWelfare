This document explains grants and ex-gratia payments and explains who is responsible for developing the guidelines for these payments.

Grants

The Australian Government makes grants to individuals, families and businesses following specific major disasters or events that warrant a special payment. Amounts paid and eligibility criteria are defined by the minister(s) whose portfolio(s) have been affected by those major disasters/events. Grants were previously known as ex gratia payments.

The most commonly known grants are:

  • Disaster Recovery Payment for New Zealand Special Category (subclass 444) visa holders (NZ DRP). This was known as ex-gratia DRP
  • Disaster Recovery Allowance for New Zealand Special Category (subclass 444) visa holders (NZ DRA). This was known as ex-gratia DRA

These payments are very similar to the Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP) and Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) but are targeted to New Zealand non-protected Special Category visa (subclass 444) holders.

For more details on:

  • NZ DRP, see AGDRP and NZ DRP
  • NZ DRA, see DRA and NZ DRA

Other examples of grants include:

  • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • MH17 Family Support Package

Legislation

Grants (previously known as ex-gratia payments) are administered under the Financial Framework (Supplementary Powers) Act 1997.

As grants are not covered under relevant legislation, customers cannot request a review by the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT). The References page contains a link to the Financial Framework (Supplementary Powers) Act 1997.

Eligibility criteria, review and appeal rights

These details vary, depending on the type of grant. See:

  • Eligibility for AGDRP and NZ DRP
  • Assessing DRA and NZ DRA claims
  • Eligibility and coding MH17 Family Support Package claims
  • Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Claims in Customer First

Note: grants cannot be reviewed by the Administrative Appeals Tribunals.
