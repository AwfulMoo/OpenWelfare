Assessment of income cancellation periods

Step

Action

1

Reconciliation assessment not for full financial year + Read more ...

If a customer contacts before the end of the relevant lodgement year advising they have not been assessed for the full financial year,

Check reconciliation result (Calculation Status: on FRCS screen may be Pending, Completed or otherwise)

The Reconciliation and Claim period Summary (RCPS) screen shows which periods have been included in reconciliation or lump sum claim calculations as well as showing non-claim periods.

To get to the RCPS screen from the FAO Reconciliation and Claim Period Summary (FRCS) screen, code 'Y' in the Go to Reconciliation & Claim Period Summary Screen field.

Is there a non-payment period either CAN-INC (cancelled due to income) or CAN-NIE (cancelled due to no instalment entitlement) for the previous financial year?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, advise the customer of the reconciliation result. For help, see Customer enquires about Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation or lump sum claim. Procedure ends here

2

Identify the reason for the income cancellation + Read more ...

Identifying the reason for the income cancellation will determine how a CAN-INC or CAN-NIE period in the previous financial year can be assessed. Depending on the circumstances, a customer may:

  • need to lodge a lump sum claim or
  • verbally request the assessment

FTB can cancel due to income because:

  • an Uplift 1 or Uplift 2 estimate is applied for a customer or their partner that causes the family income estimate to exceed the FTB income threshold
  • of a change in circumstances, for example shared care reduction, child left care, child finished or left secondary school, change to marital status
  • customer or partner provided a family income estimate that exceeded the FTB income threshold (prior to 1 July 2020)

From 1 July 2020, when a customer or partner provides a family income estimate that exceeds the FTB income threshold, their FTB will remain current at a zero rate.

Determine the reason for the CAN-INC or CAN-NIE period:

  • Go to the DS List (XDS) screen
  • Find the activity management reference (AMR) number of the CAN-INC activity
  • Go to the FAO Taxable Income (FTI) screen
  • Locate the AMR and check the source of the estimate

If:

  • the source of the estimate for either customer or partner is CUS or PTR provided go to Step 3
  • the sole source of the estimate for both customer and partner (or just customer if not partnered) is an automatic uplift 1 or 2 (UP1 or UP2), go to Step 6
  • a change of circumstances occurred causing the existing family income estimate to exceed the FTB income threshold go to Step 6

3

Customer or partner provided estimate + Read more ...

The customer can verbally request the assessment of the CAN-INC or CAN-NIE period. A request for payment for an outstanding income cancellation period must be made by the end of the relevant lodgement year. If the customer is requesting:

  • assessment of the income cancellation period in the previous financial year only, go to Step 4
  • assessment of the income cancellation period in the previous financial year and restoration of current financial year fortnightly FTB payments, see Restoration of Family Tax Benefit (FTB). Procedure ends here

4

Assessment of CAN-INC or CAN-NIE for previous financial year + Read more ...

Have actual income details been received for the customer and partner for the previous financial year?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, advise the customer:
    • the income cancellation period cannot be assessed until actual income details are received
    • income must be confirmed by 30 June
    • contact again after actual income is received to request the assessment. Procedure ends here

5

Code the FTB Instalment Period Override (FIPO) screen + Read more ...

To assess FTB for the CAN-INC or CAN-NIE period:

  • Confirm the dates of the income cancellation non-payment period
  • Go to the FTB Instalment Period Override (FIPO) screen
  • Code the start and end dates of the non-claimed period
  • Code the instalment override reason of 'SYS' = ‘System limitation’. Press [Enter]
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen. Do not finalise the activity
  • On the left hand side menu, select the Family Tax Benefit twisty
  • Select the FAO Reconciliation and Claim Summary (FRCS) link
  • Check that a new assessment line is displaying on the FRCS screen (This will show a re-reconciliation result of either arrears, overpayment or nil adjustment)
  • Select ‘Assessment Results’ on the left hand side menu to go back to the AR screen
  • Check that the results are correct before finalising the activity
  • Payment of arrears for the income cancellation non-payment period must take into consideration the normal rules of reconciliation
  • Use Fast Note. Select Auto text > Families > Reconciliation > FTB CAN INC/NIE period reassessed to DOC the activity

Note: a lump sum claim is not required in this circumstance. However, if a lump sum claim is lodged this will be considered a request for a review of the CAN-INC or CAN-NIE income cancellation non-payment period.

6

Lump sum claim required + Read more ...

To be assessed for any income cancellation period(s) in the previous financial year advise the customer:

  • lodge a lump sum claim, preferably online and
  • confirm income by lodging a tax return or advising non-lodgement before the end of the lodgement year

This may be extended if special circumstances exist that have contributed to the customer not lodging within the allowable time.

Note: if customer lodged a claim for instalments in the current year, the system will assess the previous year automatically without the need to lodge a separate lump sum claim.
