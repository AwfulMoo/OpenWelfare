Incorrect workflow used by staff

Do not follow this process if:

  • the customer lodged a claim for the incorrect event/relevant period and a decision has not been made
    • those claims should be rejected
  • a customer's payment rate needs to change following an explanation of a decision (review) process
    • see COVID-19 Disaster Payment - review process
  • the customer wants to return a payment they have already received
    • see COVID-19 Disaster Payment claim or automatic payment - customer calls to enquire

If there is an error with an automatic payment, see COVID-19 Disaster Payment - automatic payment and change of circumstances. This tells staff when to follow certain steps in this process.

Table 1: process to follow when the incorrect workflow was run by staff or the customer was not eligible to receive a payment.

Step

Action

1

Incorrect workflow run by staff or customer not eligible? + Read more ...

Do not run another workflow to correct any claim already paid

  • If the incorrect workflow was run by staff, go to Step 2
  • If the correct workflow was run by staff but the customer was not eligible for a payment, go to Step 5

2

Incorrect workflow run by staff + Read more ...

If the customer received the correct amount, go to Step 3.

If the customer received an amount:

  • less than they were entitled to, go to Step 4
  • more than they were entitled to, go to Step 5

3

Customer received the correct amount but was paid for the wrong event or relevant period + Read more ...

For example, customer was paid the:

  • $750 rate for relevant period 6 for a relevant area in Sydney, but should have been paid the $750 rate for relevant period 6 for a relevant area in Greater Sydney.
    • Relevant periods for Sydney and Greater Sydney do not align, even though the customer was paid the correct rate of payment.
  • $200 income support payment rate for relevant period 1 Australian Capital Territory, but should have been paid the $200 income support payment rate for relevant period 2 Victoria (August 2021)

Contact Technical Peer Support.

Technical Peer Support must confirm the customer’s circumstances. If appropriate, they will complete a payment query template.

Note: the Level 2 Helpdesk will determine if any automatic payments need to stop.

4

Customer received less than they were entitled to + Read more ...

For example:

  • customer paid $375 for less than 20 hours worked
  • online claim shows customer:
    • lost 20 hours or more work, and
    • should be paid $600

Note: where a state or territory has reached 80% full vaccination of its population who are aged 16 years and over, the payment amount of the COVID-19 Disaster Payment changes. Check the operational blueprint for the event for payment amounts during these relevant periods.

Contact Technical Peer Support who will complete a payment query template.

Note: staff with PYAJ access must not issue payments via PYAJ unless given approval to do so by the Level 2 Helpdesk.

If automatic payments:

  • need to stop, go to Step 6
  • do not need to stop, procedure ends here

5

Customer received more than they were entitled to or were not eligible for a payment + Read more ...

For example:

  • customer paid $600 for 20 hours or more
  • claim shows customer:
    • has lost less than 20 hours of work, and
    • should only have been paid $375, or
  • customer should not have received a payment

Staff must:

  • Create a minimum standards ACTDOC using the Callback/ACTDOC guided procedure
    • To find the Callback/ACTDOC guided procedure, type Callback/ACTDOC in the free text field under Search Workspace
  • Select Service Reason Emergency Exgratia
  • Under Category select Debt Raising
  • Select Generate Document Content
  • In Document Content add:
    • Customer's CRN and name
    • Customer has been paid: $ (enter amount)
    • For relevant period: (enter relevant period number and dates)
    • State or territory claimed: (enter name of the state or territory)
    • LGA: (enter LGA)
    • Event Code: (enter event code)
    • Customer only entitled to: $ (enter amount - if the customer is not entitled to any amount enter $0 and that the customer was not eligible to receive a payment)
    • Raise a debt for: $ (enter amount)
      Note: if a customer has been incorrectly paid for more than 1 relevant period, the 1 ACTDOC can include:
      - all relevant period numbers and dates, and
      - the debt to be raised for each relevant period
      Staff do not need to complete a separate ACTDOC for each relevant period a customer was incorrectly paid
  • Select Finish at the top left corner of the screen
  • Go to the Activity List (AL) screen
  • Select the ACTDOC activity
  • Select O Transfer the activity to another site or region
  • Select Continue
  • For Income support payment customers - COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims ($200 rate):
    • In Regional Office enter: CVD
    • In New Keywords add keyword: CDPDET
  • For COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims (not the $200 rate):
    • In Regional Office enter: TEP
    • In New Keywords add keyword: CDPDET
  • Select Continue
  • Select OK to Are you sure you want to update the activity?
  • Return to the AL screen to make sure the correct code shows under Region
  • Note: staff do not need to take any further action to have the ACTDOC actioned. If a customer is on the phone, tell them they may be contacted at a later date to discuss this further

6

Automatic payments need to stop + Read more ...

If remaining on automatic payments means the customer will continue to be overpaid, complete the COVID19 Disaster Payment CoC Update Fast Note

  • Location: Customer First > Workspace > Documents and Correspondence > Fast Note
  • Service Reason: select CLI - Client from the drop down
    • Do not leave the Service Reason as 'CCD - Customer Contact Details' or any other claim type, or the auto payment will not be stopped
  • Document Completion: make sure this is set to Yes
    • Do not change this to No, or the auto payment will not be stopped
  • Select Auto Text, use Emergency > Claim > COVID19 Disaster Payment CoC Update
  • Select Confirm once the text in the Fast Note drops into the text box
  • Complete only the option relevant for the customer and remove the option that does not apply:
    • Change in circumstances advised by the customer has caused the incorrect rate (therefore auto payments need to stop) - complete questions under Option 1
    • Customer requests to stop auto payments - complete questions under Option 2
    • If the customer has not contacted but staff have identified auto payments need to stop - complete Option 3
  • Add to the Fast Note:
  • 'This is because the customer lodged an online claim, but their automated payment should not have been made. Subsequent payments would be incorrect if left on automated payment'
  • The system will apply the change based on the keyword already attached to the Fast Note. Staff do not need to take further action to stop future automatic payments
  • Procedure ends here

Payment returned because of incorrect bank details

Table 2: process for staff to follow when they are allocated a manual follow up (MFU) as a payment has been returned because of incorrect bank details.

Step

Action

1

Contact the customer + Read more ...

A payment returned by a financial institution shows as RTN in the customer’s Payment Summary (PS) screen.

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer.

Is customer contact successful?

  • Yes,
    • Ask for the correct bank details
    • Cancel the MFU
    • Add a DOC to the customer’s record by running the Fast Note workflow:
      - Location: Customer First > Workspace > Documents and Correspondence > Fast Note
      - Service Reason:       select EXG - Emergency Exgratia
      - Document Completion:       select Yes, otherwise the Fast Note will not close and will create a new work item
      - Extra Details to enter a title for the DOC. Type in this field COVID-19 DP payment details
      - Clearly document what was discussed with the customer
    • See Replacing direct credit, New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) payments returned from financial institution
    • To make sure the customer’s ongoing payment goes to their new bank account, automatic payments will need to stop. See Run the Fast Note and advise the customer to run the COVID19 Disaster Payment CoC update Fast Note. Tell the customer they will need to lodge a new claim to continue receiving a COVID-19 Disaster Payment
    • Procedure ends here
  • No,
    • Cancel the MFU
    • Do not issue a letter to the customer
    • Add a DOC to the customer’s record by running the Fast Note workflow:
      - Location: Customer First > Workspace > Documents and Correspondence > Fast Note
      - Service Reason:       select EXG - Emergency Exgratia
      - Document Completion:       select Yes, otherwise the Fast Note will not close and will create a new work item
      - Extra Details to enter a title for the DOC. Type in this field COVID-19 DP payment details
      - clearly document the 2 contact attempts and information needed from the customer
    • To make sure no further payments are sent to the wrong bank account, automatic payments will need to stop. See Run the Fast Note and advise the customer to run the COVID19 Disaster Payment CoC update Fast Note
    • If the customer calls about a RTN payment, staff will follow Step 1 in COVID-19 Disaster Payment claim or automatic payment - customer calls to enquire
    • Procedure ends here
