3

Locating the ATO Data Categorisation Task (DCT) review + Read more ...

An Integrated Review System (IRS) review type DCT was loaded for most completed online or manual compliance income interventions where a debt is identified.

The DCT review will help find the original review in the Integrated Review Summary (IRRS) screen that needs remediation. These may be:

  • Manual Pay As You Go (PAYG)
  • Online Compliance Intervention (OCI)
  • Employment Income Confirmation (EIC)
  • Check Update Past Income (CUPI)

The DCT review activity is in IRRS and can be identified by a line showing:

  • IRS/DCT/ADC/COM -000
  • ATO Data Categorisation

Once found, key 'M' next to the DCT review to display the Match Data (IRMD) screen. The IRMD screen will display the following details:

  • Original Review ID:
    • reflects the ID number of the original corresponding income compliance review in IRRS
    • make a note of the number
  • Online Interaction Activity ID:
    • displays the review ID that is linked to the original Online Income Compliance Integrated Review (ZIRV) in Customer First. Take note of the number
    • If blank, find the match data using the Original Review ID in IRRS, either via Customer Record or Customer First

If a DCT review is not identified, confirm the attribute CMPEIIRFND is on the OPAT screen for each debt to be remediated,

The customer may have multiple DCT activities. When these exist, all interventions need to be taken into account and assessed in one activity. Staff will need to complete all actions relating to the customer. This may include:

  • reviewing multiple Activity Reference Numbers (AMR)
  • reviewing multiple debts and debt periods
  • reviewing multiple compliance interventions or reviews, and
  • removing income from multiple employers

Note: make note of all information gathered. It will help with remediation.

Locate the review type + Read more ...

The income compliance review on the IRS will determine the service used to action the customerâ€™s income compliance intervention. Locate the review type to work out where to find information about the review on the customerâ€™s record.

The customer may have had multiple income compliance interventions. Assess each one to determine the income averaging used to calculate the debt.

Remediation will occur on debts that have been set back to zero for an income compliance review loaded from 1 July 2015 (2015-16), where ATO averaging has been used.

To locate the original EII review/s:

  • go to the IRRS screen
  • locate the Original Review Id obtained from the DCT review. This identifies the EII Compliance review, an EII Compliance review will display IRS/EII/EMP
  • 'S'elect the review to go to the Review Details (IRRD) screen

The IRRD screen will display the review method and review origin, This will help determine the review type.

  • The review method will display as:
    • PAYG Manual Compliance Interventions
      Phone (PHO)
      Mail (MAI)
      File (FIL)
    • PAYG Online Compliance Interventions
      ONL
      ONM
  • The review origins will display as:
    • PAYG Manual Compliance Interventions
      PYG
    • Enhanced Data Matching (EDM). EDM is income provided by the customer to the ATO via their Income Tax Return. This information is then given to Centrelink
    • PAYG Online Compliance interventions
      PYG

Is the intervention a PAYG Manual Compliance Intervention?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, it is a PAYG Online Compliance Intervention, go to Step 6

Review record actions and notes + Read more ...

Staff must undertake a full check of the customerâ€™s record to determine if there is any sign that income averaging was used.

There are a number of ways to check what income was used to calculate the customerâ€™s debt outcome. Check:

  • Notes in the integrated review
  • DOCs on the Document List (DL) screen
  • Employment Income Details (EANS) screen
  • CUPI, EIC and OCI Customer and staff receipts
  • Archive and Culling Engine (ACE)
  • Document Tools

Documents

The Integrated Review Action (IRRA) screen displays all relevant dates from the start date to the completion date of the review. This screen can be used as a reference for the review date range when searching for DOCs.

Relevant DOCs will have titles such as:

  • review finalised
  • debt raised
  • reassessment
  • appeal
  • debt ID
  • DMN Payments requested

If the DOC no longer appears on DL, use ACE to conduct an â€˜ODR searchâ€™ for historical DOC/s relating to the review.

It will also be helpful to note the Activity Management Reference (AMR) and any notes or Documents on the customer record, when checking these screens. Customers may have seasonal work, real estate, business income or other Income. Capturing this information will help when having to investigate these details later.

Receipt data

The receipt data displays the information the customer input via the online workflow and/or the information staff updated on behalf of a customer.

Note: these notes will help with the investigation for remediation. However, details may have changed if the debt has been reassessed or new information provided.

  • For OCI reviews, the receipt generated may only display in the Attachments tab located within the COC Interaction screen which is accessed via the Related Transactions tab
  • For EIC reviews, receipt data can be accessed via the Attachments tab in the Integrated Review
  • For CUPI reviews, the receipt data is presented in 2 attachments
    • Customer Receipt
    • Staff Receipt

The receipt can be accessed from Customer First or Process Direct.

Notes in the integrated review

Using Customer First or Process Direct, check Notes to determine if there is evidence income averaging was applied for part or all of the debt period.

No system generated DOCs are available when the customer used the online platform. The Receipt (EIC) or Customer Receipt (CUPI) provides the details on how the income was applied.

Debt investigation + Read more ...

Staff need to identify the debt period to identify what earnings need to be removed as part of the income compliance intervention. This will help identify what the subsequent impacts were (if any) during this period.

There are a number of ways to check details relating to the debt/s:

  • Debt List (OPDL) screen. Select the Debt ID for the debt that was raised using averaged income
  • Debt Amount (OPDA) screen. This screen will outline any adjustments made to the debt amount as a result of a reassessment. If the debt was Finalised no Debt (FND), it will show the date changed and when the debt was reduced to $0.00
  • The General Information (OPGI) screen will show the debt ID and the date the debt was raised
  • Modify/Assess Debt (OPMA) screen. This screen shows debt dates and the activity AMR from the original earnings update
    • There will be debts that have no AMR, when the Source of Debt: is MANUAL:IR
    • In these cases, MultiCal was used to generate a debt and no pre-existing debt shell was used

For more information on debt screens, see Debt Management and Information System (DMIS) screens.

Identifying subsequent impacts

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Earnings + Read more ...

Check the earnings updates on the Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen to help determine if match data (income averaging) was applied.

Using Customer Record or Customer First, check the EANS screen to see if there is a regular pattern of recorded income for any or all matched employers within the match period.

A regular pattern of income (e.g. $1921.79 per fortnight for multiple fortnights) can highlight that income averaging of ATO data has been used.

Note: sometimes the customer may have earned the same amount each fortnight. The receipt and/or notes should indicate if payslips or bank statements were used and the customer earned the same amount each fortnight.

Entitlement period start and end dates + Read more ...

Knowing the following periods that were updated using income averaging of ATO data may help identify the impacts:

  • first Entitlement Period State Date (EPSD)
  • first Entitlement Period End Date (EPED)

To find these, check employer details on the Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen.

If income averaging was applied for a period before grant, use the first EPSD of the period the customer was on payments.

Other employment related income + Read more ...

This can include but is not limited to:

  • Other Income screen (OINS). This may include leave and termination payments (for ABSTUDY customers only), salary continuance or Income protection payments from superannuation and royalty and commission payments

Subsequent impacts + Read more ...

When averaged income was applied to customer records, there may have been some subsequent impacts.

Viewing the original AMR via Event Summary (ES) screen will help identify these impacts. Replaying these activities will give staff details on what interactions and impacts occurred when averaged income was applied.

If activities have been archived, staff can retrieve these from the Archive and Culling Engine (ACE). See the Resources page for a link.

Note: archived activities can be sent back to the system to view in Customer Record.

As part of remediation, the agency will be removing all income averaging of ATO data and fixing any subsequent impacts to the customer, their partner (at the time), their children or siblings.

If an impact has been identified, staff are to complete the extra steps and processes.

Identify the following subsequent impacts before removing income averaged ATO data processes:

  • employment income credit impacts
  • dependent Youth Allowance or ABSTUDY
  • reconciliation for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) or Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • customer with dependent child/ren getting an income support payment
  • customer has a compensation preclusion period
  • other qualification entitlement impacts
  • a request for an explanation or application for a formal review has been received
  • subsequent income related debt
    • undetermined debt
    • possible underpayment
  • limiting date impacts

5

Employment income credit impacts + Read more ...

Depending on which payments the customer received during the review period, working credits need to be checked as they may need to be updated.

When investigating these records take note of Activity Management Numbers (AMR) relevant to the updates. The AMR numbers will help when investigating the customer records.

Check the limiting date on the LMTD screen to see if it will affect the outcome. Be in the correct system for the payment type. There may be different limiting dates in each system. Make sure to check the dates against the relevant EPSD as this will determine if the limiting can be moved or not.

If the limiting date has a reason of BLK or REM recorded, this limiting date may be moved to let the system to correctly reassess the customer's entitlement. All other limiting date reasons are deemed a hard limiting date and cannot be moved

There are different system limitations for moving a BLK or REM limiting date in the PEN and NSS systems:

  • for all NSS and current PEN customers, it is a soft limiting date and can be moved
  • for non-current PEN customers, it is a hard limiting date and cannot be moved

This includes:

  • Working Credit
    • Customers eligible for working credits
    • Screen: Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS), W/C Manual Override (WCRC)
    • See Working Credit
  • Income Bank
    • For students receiving Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy or ABSTUDY
    • Screen: Income Bank (NIB) or EYIB for ABSTUDY
    • See Personal Income Test and Income Bank for ABSTUDY, Austudy and YA students and Australian Apprentices
  • Work Bonus
    • For customers who were Age Pension age receiving the following payments:
      Age Pension
      Bereavement Allowance (BVA)
      Carer Payment (CP)
      Disability Support Pension (DSP)
    • Screen: Eligible Income Assessment (EIA)
    • See Work Bonus and balance for pensioners of age pension age

6

Dependent Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY + Read more ...

The Parental Income Test (PIT) may be applied to the combined parental income of dependent YA, ABSTUDY (ABY) and Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) customers.

A child is considered dependent if:

  • they are wholly or substantially dependent on the parent(s)/guardian(s) (this does not include paying child support), and
  • the child receives (or their parent/guardian qualifies on their behalf for) one of these qualifying payments:
    • Youth Allowance (YA)
    • ABSTUDY Living Allowance
    • ABSTUDY Group 2 School Fees (means tested component)
    • AIC Scheme Additional Boarding Allowance (ABA)
    • FTB child or regular care child

Check if the customer was getting YA or ABSTUDY via the XBS screen.

Check if they were dependent via the Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH) screen. The customer will need to have:

  • received these payments during the identified review period, and
  • been impacted by the application of income averaged ATO data

Check if the customer had any siblings in their entitlement calculation via the Family Pool Summary (NSFP) screen.

See:

  • Determining a dependent child for inclusion in the family pool for the PIT
  • Raising Family Pool debts
  • Changes which may affect the Parental Income Test (PIT)

Other qualification entitlement impacts + Read more ...

There may have been changes to the customer's record as a result of applying the averaged income details, but they did not occur at the same time.

These subsequent debts may have occurred after the review was finalised. This could include:

  • Marital status impacts where the partner had impacts, so partner records will need to be fixed or corrected
    • Other debts were raised and not linked to an IRS Review, as a reassessment on a cancelled record may have happened later. For example. the customer may not have had a dependent child for the compliance period and lost qualification to some or part of payments
  • The customer lost qualification to Rent Assistance (RA) for a period covered by the compliance period after the debt was raised

If staff see anything that may have affected the customerâ€™s qualification entitlement, they should remediate the record.

12

Subsequent income related debt + Read more ...

Check if the customer had subsequent income related debt/s or a reduction in payment due to working credit/income bank/work bonus balances.

Review the Debt List (OPDL) screen and review all debts.

A common scenario is where the Working Credit balance was refreshed at a later date and this created a debt that was raised and not included in the original compliance review.

The same AMR number attached to a series of fortnightly employment income updates of the same amount would also indicate that PAYG income averaging had been applied.

If there were related debt/s, these debts may need to be reassessed and potential over/underpayment identified.
