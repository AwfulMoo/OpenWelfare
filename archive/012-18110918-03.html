Assessing a request to recognise additional training in non-directive PSC

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Notice of additional training in non-directive PSC received + Read more ...

Determine which organisation the notification of training in non-directive PSC was received from.

  • RACGP: - notifications of registration are received via email from the RACGP with the subject line 'RACGP - Confirmation of completion of pregnancy counselling support activity - MBS Item No.4001 - [date]'
  • ACRRM: - notifications of registration are received via email from ACRRM with the subject line 'Non-Directive Pregnancy Support (medical practitioner's name)'

If the request comes from a source other than the RACGP or ACRRM, re-categorise the application to National Office Escalation in PaNDA and contact a Programme Support Officer (PSO) who will escalate the notification to a Programme Officer (PO) to obtain confirmation of eligibility.

2

Confirm medical practitioner's details in PDS + Read more ...

  • Open PDS:
  • Select:
    • Provider
    • View
  • Search:
    • Detailed Search tab, by name

Note: using Detailed Search will ensure all records for the medical practitioner are located.

Does the medical practitioner's name and provider number (if provided) match the request?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the information does not match, contact a PSO for assistance

Note: if an exact match is found and multiple stems exist then select one of the provider numbers. All unsynchronised linked stems must be updated individually.

3

Check Specialty Codes + Read more ...

Scroll to the bottom of the Provider Stem record to view the specialty codes for the medical practitioner.

Check if the PDS stem has any of the following specialty codes current:

  • 132 FRACGP (RACGP Fellow)
  • 532 FACRRM (ACCRM Fellow)

If either of the above specialty codes are recorded at stem level, they will need to be updated. See Recognition or removal of general practitioners (GP)

If the medical practitioner is a general practitioner and the specialty codes 130, 530 or 540 are current, then check:

  • Notification received from RACGP - processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - vocationally registered - RACGP (647)
  • Notification received from ACRRM - processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - vocationally registered- ACRRM (648)

If the medical practitioner is listed as a specialist or consultant physician, (specialty codes 1-99, 802-808 or 898-899) and is not also a general practitioner, no further action is required. In PaNDA:

  • Add a Note stating medical practitioner is not eligible as a specialist/consultant physician

  • Save

Assess which code(s) to add: + Read more ...

  • If the medical practitioner is a general practitioner (130, 132, 532) the VR specialty code needs to be added, go to:
    • Processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - vocationally registered - notification from RACGP (647), or
    • Processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - vocationally registered - notification from ACRRM (648)
  • If the medical practitioner is eligible to receive non-VR benefits then the non-VR specialty code needs to be added, go to:
    • Processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - non vocationally registered - notification from RACGP (662), or
    • Processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - non vocationally registered - notification from ACRRM (663)
  • If the medical practitioner is eligible to receive VR benefits then the VR specialty code needs to be added, go to:
    • Processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - vocationally registered - notification from RACGP (647), or
    • Processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - vocationally registered - notification from ACRRM (648)
  • If the medical practitioner is eligible to receive both non-VR and VR benefits then both specialty codes will need to be added, go to:
    • Processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - non vocationally registered - notification from RACGP (662), or
    • Processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - non vocationally registered - notification from ACRRM (663)
  • Once the steps in this table have been completed go to:
    • Processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - vocationally registered - notification from RACGP (647), or
    • Processing a medical practitioner's additional training in non-directive PSC - vocationally registered - notification from ACRRM (648)

Note: medical practitioners who have multiple approved 3GA placements are often eligible for both non-VR and VR benefits depending on the programs they are participating in. It is important to correctly assess eligibility and complete the correct processing table(s).
