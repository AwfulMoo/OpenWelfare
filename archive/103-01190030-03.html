Preparing for Initial Assessment interview

Table 1: This table outlines the steps required before proceeding with the Initial Assessment interview for Voluntary Income Management.

Step

Action

1

Is the customer eligible for Voluntary Income Management (VIM)? + Read more ...

Customers may be eligible for VIM if they:

  • receive a Category H welfare payment
  • reside in a declared Income Management area, and
  • do not have an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes

For more information, see Voluntary Income Management eligibility.

Note: customers income managed under the Voluntary Income Management measure are required to complete a mandatory period of 13 weeks before being able to terminate their VIM agreement.

Service Officers not trained in Income Management - cold transfer customer to the Income Management line, Indigenous Services line (if appropriate), or Multilingual Call (if appropriate). See the Resources page for a link to the National Transfer numbers. Procedure ends here.

Income Management trained Service Officers go to Step 2.

2

Initial Assessment Interview + Read more ...

Customers must attend a face to face Initial Assessment interview before commencing Voluntary Income Management.

This interview is to:

  • explain the terms and conditions of Voluntary Income Management
  • explain how Income Management works
  • determine how income managed funds can be best used to meet the priority needs of the customer and their family, and
  • provide information about other services which may assist them, including Centrepay

If the customer requires an interpreter, only continue with the Initial Assessment when an interpreter is present. See Booking an interpreter for an appointment.

Go to Step 4.

Go to Step 3.

3

Service Officers not trained in Income Management + Read more ...

Initial Assessment interviews are only to be conducted by Service Officers trained in Income Management.

Is the Service Officer Income Management trained?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, follow this process and if required:
    • Seek assistance from a specialised Service Officer (if available) or a Service Support Officer, or
    • Contact the Access Support Centre (select 'Income Management' option) for assistance to respond to customer enquiries. See Resources page for a link to Office Locator

4

Confirm Income Management status + Read more ...

Is the customer eligible for Voluntary Income Management?

  • Yes, check if the customer is being income managed under a compulsory measure via the Income Management Summary page:
    • If currently income managed, go to Step 5
    • If not income managed, go to Step 6
  • No, see Step 5 in Table 5

5

Customer currently income managed + Read more ...

Customers who are currently on a compulsory measure of Income Management are not eligible for Voluntary Income Management. They can choose to continue Income Management through Voluntary Income Management when the compulsory measure ends.

Ensure the customer's compulsory measure of Income Management has ended before continuing with the following process to place the customer onto Voluntary Income Management.

See Overview and hierarchy of Income Management for further information.

Has compulsory Income Management ended?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, advise the customer Voluntary Income Management cannot commence until after their current measure has ended. Offer to book an Initial Assessment interview, and advise them of the date, time and location of their appointment. Record details in a DOC. Procedure ends here

6

Determine if Voluntary Income Management is suitable for the customer + Read more ...

To determine if Voluntary Income Management is appropriate, advise the customer:

  • how Income Management works
  • when they are exited from an agreement, a 21 day waiting period will apply from the date they are exited, before any new agreement can be entered into, and
  • they can only apply for Voluntary Income Management four times in any 12 month period

Consider the customer's net payment when discussing options with the customer.

If the customer is receiving an insufficient amount of income support payment, Voluntary Income Management may not benefit the customer. For example, a customer may only receive income managed funds of $50 due to earnings and the only expense that can be covered is electricity. In this case, Centrepay would be a better choice for the customer.

Discuss alternative options with the customer about managing their money. For example:

  • Have they considered Centrepay deductions?
  • Have they considered a payment nominee?
  • Have they considered participating in Financial Wellbeing and Capability Activities or financial counselling?
  • Advise the customer they can visit servicesaustralia.gov.au/managingmoney for useful information and resources

7

Provide information to customer + Read more ...

Before progressing with the interview, provide the customer with an overview of a Voluntary Income Management Agreement, including:

  • the mandatory 13 week period of Voluntary Income Management
  • how they can request to end Income Management any time after 13 weeks, or
  • the agency can end Income Management if they lose eligibility or it is no longer feasible
  • review any payment nominee arrangement to determine if it is still in the customer's best interests. See Reviewing nominee arrangements

Does the customer wish to participate in Voluntary Income Management?

  • Yes,
    • Advise the customer they must attend a face-to-face interview
    • If they have a payment nominee, advise the customer their payment nominee must be present at the interview
    • Book an Initial Assessment interview at the customer's Service Centre
    • If the customer lives in a remote area, advise the customer of the date and location of the next visit, if available. Send an ACTDOC to the customer's Region Code advising of the customer's request to enter into a Voluntary Income Management Agreement. Arrangements will be made to contact the customer prior to the next Remote Servicing Team visit, or the customer's details will be added to the trip list for the next visit to that location
    • Procedure ends here
    • If the customer's compulsory measure of Income Management has not ended, book an Initial Assessment interview for a date after Income Management has ended. Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here
    • If advice is received that a customer living in a remote area wishes to enter into a Voluntary Income Management Agreement, attempt to contact the customer to advise of the next visit. In addition, contact the relevant Remote Servicing Team and request the customer's details be add to the trip list for the next scheduled visit
    • If the customer wishes to enter into a Voluntary Income Management Agreement, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, record details on a DOC. If applicable, code Centrepay and/or if requested, provide the customer with contact details of their nearest Financial Wellbeing and Capacity Activity provider. Procedure ends here

Customers identified for or referred for Income Management should have their payment nominee relationship reviewed. For more information, see Nominee arrangements under Income Management.

Note: Having a correspondence nominee (NOC) only does not exclude a customer from Income Management.

3

Does the customer have an excluded payment nominee? + Read more ...

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for Income Management if they have an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes. Go to Step 4
  • No, advise the customer their payment nominee must be present for the Initial Assessment interview as they are responsible for managing their funds. Go to Step 5

4

Excluded payment nominee + Read more ...

  • Advise the customer their payments will not be income managed due to their payment nominee arrangement. However, if the arrangement changes, they may become eligible for Income Management
  • Create a DOC on the customer's record with the following information:
    • 'Customer has an excluded payment nominee and is not eligible for Income Management'
  • Advise the customer their payments will not be income managed due to their payment nominee arrangement. However, if the arrangement changes, they may become eligible for Income Management

Procedure ends here.

5

Is the payment nominee present? + Read more ...

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, book another appointment at a suitable time for the payment nominee as it is mandatory for the payment nominee to attend an Initial Assessment interview. Although it is not compulsory for the customer to attend, it is preferred to ensure they are aware of how Income Management will affect them.
    Procedure ends here

Conducting the Initial Assessment interview

Table 3: This table contains steps to conduct the Initial Assessment interview.

Step

Action

1

Conduct Initial Assessment interview + Read more ...

During the interview, the following must be covered:

  • Explain Income Management and how delivery of their payments will change
  • Explain what happens if the customer moves out of an Income Management area
  • Explain how income managed money is accessed. For more information, see Coding Income Management expenses
  • Explain what income managed money can be used for that is, School Meals Program (if applicable), food, rent/housing, utilities, clothing, medical needs. See Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management
  • Explain what goods cannot be purchased from income managed funds that is, prohibited goods and services - alcohol, home brew concentrate, home brew kits, tobacco products, pornographic material or gambling
  • Explain how money can be allocated to their BasicsCard:
    • Residual Payment method (default), or
    • they choose the Partial Payment method, and
    • Immediate transfers (where Income Management funds are available)
  • If applicable, discuss converting existing Centrepay deductions to an income managed expense. Explain the impact of Income Management on any ongoing Centrepay deductions
  • Review and discuss any existing Rent Deduction Scheme payment and whether the customer would like to pay their State Housing Authority (SHA) rent from:
    • their income managed funds
    • their discretionary funds, or
    • a combination of both
  • Explain how money can be accessed for Income Management customers accessing funds while travelling
  • Explain how customers can contact Services Australia, including self service, in regards to Income Management and BasicsCard. See Income Management and BasicsCard self service options. Offer to register the customer for self-service
  • Explain how common changes in circumstances may affect Income Management and the importance of notifying the agency of any changes in their expenses
  • Advise customer of impact on expense payments if income support payments are reduced due to earning from employment or other income and the priority needs payment order when there are insufficient funds to cover an expense
  • Explain how lump sum payments such as Advances, arrears and Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation payments are 100% income managed
  • Explain what happens if they are identified for a higher measure of Income Management and how this may impact ongoing eligibility for Voluntary Income Management. See Overview and hierarchy of Income Management
  • When Income Management ends, all Income Management expenses will stop. They must make arrangements to continue paying these expenses themselves, or contact the agency to discuss alternative payment options, such as Centrepay
  • Advise the customer of their right to review Income Management decisions

Note: if during the interview, the Service Officer identifies vulnerability factors (on the record or verbally), offer a social worker referral as part of normal business processes.

2

Discuss with customer how Priority Needs will be met + Read more ...

Regular expense allocations will be established to meet their priority needs and those of their family.

Discuss with the customer what ongoing expenses they have (for example, rent, food, utility bills etc.). If the expense meets a priority need, this can be paid from their income managed funds.

For more information, see Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management.

3

Voluntary IM Assessment workflow + Read more ...

Launch the Voluntary IM Assessment workflow.

Enter the following information on the Voluntary Agreement Details page:

Agreement

  • Receipt Date: field will display as today's date. This should only be changed where the Income Management interview was held on a previous date

Note: although the interview may be coded for a previous date the date of effect will be the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) following the assessment being completed.

  • Start date: field is protected and defaults to the day the workflow is being completed
  • Status: Select 'Active' from the drop down list
  • Customer referred by: Select the appropriate option from the drop down list:
  • Non State authority
  • Not referred
  • State authority
  • If 'Non State authority' is selected, select the Organisation/office from the drop down list:
  • Financial Management Program Services
  • Housing Authorities
  • Welfare Authorities
  • If 'State authority' is selected, select State/territory and Organisation/office from the drop down menu

Northern Territory:

  • DCF Head Office
  • DCF Katherine

Western Australia:

  • Department for Child Protection

Payment nominee details

This information will only present where the customer is a payment nominee for another person.

  • Principal/s: This will default with the names of any persons for whom the customer is a payment nominee
  • Customer will continue acting in each principal's best interests/spending payments for their benefit:
  • Customer agrees? Select Yes or No radio button as appropriate
  • If Yes is selected, explain that the principal may become eligible for Income Management in their own right
  • If No is selected, refer to reviewing nominee arrangements. Customers are not prevented from entering into a Voluntary Income Management Agreement where they no longer wish to act as a payment nominee, however Service Officers must ensure all appropriate action is taken where the arrangement is to be cancelled

For more information, see Nominee arrangements under Income Management.

Select Continue.

4

Continue workflow: Voluntary Agreement Details - Interpreter details + Read more ...

Interpreter Requirements

Details of the interpreter requirements must be recorded for interview purposes.

Where no information is recorded on the Other Contact Details (OCD) screen, the Service Officer must exit the assessment workflow and update the OCD screen before proceeding with the interview. See Booking an interpreter for an appointment.

Customer's Spoken Language: This will default to the interpreter language recorded on the OCD screen.

Interpreter Required: This will default to:

  • Yes if the OCD screen indicates an interpreter is required
  • No if interpreter language is recorded as English, and cannot be changed. If it is determined an interpreter is needed for the interview, the Service Officer must exit the workflow and update the OCD screen as appropriate

Is an interpreter being used?: This will only present if the customer's language is not English. Select Yes, No, Not Available or Not Required from the dropdown list as appropriate.

Reason: If an interpreter is not available, not being used or not required, the reason the assessment continued is to be selected from the applicable dropdown list.

  • If Yes is selected, no further information is required. Select Continue and go to Step 5
  • If No is selected, select the applicable reason from the dropdown menu:
    • Appointment not booked
    • Conflict of interest
    • Customer declined interpreter
    • Customer wishes to use agent or family member
    • Deaf or hearing impairment
    • DHS does not have an interpreter who speaks the customer's language
  • If Not available is selected, select the applicable reason from the dropdown menu:
    • Interpreter did not attend interview
    • Interpreter service for that language not available
    • Same sex interpreter not available
  • If Not required is selected, select the applicable reason from the dropdown menu:
    • No contact required
    • Speaks and understands English
  • More information: if an interpreter is not available, not being used or not required, additional information can be recorded to the standard reasons selected above. For example, where 'Customer wishes to use agent or family member' is selected, the agent or family member's details can be recorded in this field

It is not recommended the interview proceed without an interpreter.

5

Continue workflow: Voluntary Agreement Details - Income Management Agreement + Read more ...

Issue the customer with a Voluntary Income Management fact sheet. The Resources page contains a link.

Has the customer been issued with the VIM fact sheet? Select Yes to confirm. Note: the workflow cannot continue until the customer has been issued the fact sheet and this is confirmed by the Service Officer.

Read the statement to the customer to confirm they will be entering into a Voluntary Income Management Agreement.

Terms of Agreement

Read all the terms and conditions of entering into a Voluntary Income Management Agreement to the customer and confirm they understand by selecting the Yes option to Do you understand?

Has Voluntary Income Management been explained to the customer?

Select Yes to confirm this has been explained to the customer.

Does the customer accept the terms and conditions and wishes to continue with Voluntary Income Management?

  • Yes, select Continue:
    • For customers referred by a state authority, go to Step 6
    • For customers not referred by a state authority, go to Step 7
  • No, as the customer has answered no to any of the terms and conditions, do not proceed with the Voluntary IM assessment workflow. See Step 6 in Table 5

6

Continue workflow: Consent details screen + Read more ...

Record consent details if required.

The Consent Details screen will only present where the customer has been referred to Voluntary Income Management by a State authority.

Are details of whether the customer has provided consent (or not) to share information with a state authority been recorded?

  • Yes, enter the following information on the Consent Details page:
    • The Date: field is the consent start date. This will default to today's date when recording a new consent
    • The Organisation will default to the organisation/office on the Voluntary Agreement details page
    • Select the Yes or No radio button for Does the customer consent to exchange information with organisation?
    • The referring agency will only be notified when the customer commences Voluntary Income Management, exits Voluntary Income Management, or withdraws their consent
    • Go to Step 7
  • No, select Continue. Go to Step 7

7

Continue workflow: Income Management Initial interview + Read more ...

Interview Details

The Logon ID Interview, Date and Channel will default to today's date and details of the Service Officer completing the workflow. Fields should only be changed where the Income Management assessment was completed on a previous date or by a different Service Officer.

Note: although the interview may be coded for a previous date, the date of commencement of the Agreement will be the date the workflow is completed.

Privacy Notice and Third Party Disclosure

The Privacy Notice and Third Party Disclosure information must be read to the customer.

Confirm that the customer has been read the Privacy Notice and Third Party Disclosure Information.

  • Yes is selected, select Continue. The IM Decision screen will be presented. Go to Step 8
  • No is selected, the workflow will not continue. Do not proceed with the Voluntary IM assessment workflow. See Step 6 in Table 5

8

Continue workflow: IM Decision screen + Read more ...

No updates are required unless the customer is ineligible.

Selecting an ineligibility reason will result in Income Management ceasing immediately.

No selection in the IM Ineligibility Reasons section means that Income Management will only end when the customer is no longer eligible or requests to exit Voluntary Income Management.

Select Continue. See Step 1 in Table 4.

Initial Assessment interview - Priority needs and expenses

Table 4: This table outlines the steps for recording the priority needs discussion and expenses in the Initial Assessment interview.

Step

Action

1

Continue workflow: Expense Management Summary screen + Read more ...

This page is a summary of the customer's payments and deductions and used to:

  • commence new Income Management expenses (regular or one-off)
  • vary or cease existing Income Management expenses
  • cease or vary existing Centrepay (voluntary) deductions
  • access the Priority Needs Expenses screen

2

Centrepay and Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) + Read more ...

Does the customer have any Centrepay deductions or deductions to a State Housing Authority (SHA)?

Check the Voluntary Deduction for All Benefits on the Expense Management Summary screen to display deductions being paid from the customer's gross discretionary funds.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Changing Centrepay/ Rent Deduction Scheme payments to Income Management expenses + Read more ...

Centrepay:

Discuss with the customer whether current Centrepay deductions should be:

  • converted to an Income Management expense
  • maintained as they are
  • varied, or
  • ceased

Search the Income Management Third Party Organisation (TPO) using Office Locator to determine the preferred payment methods for the TPO. Check for any payment notes that might impact converting the deduction from Centrepay to Income Management.

To change a Centrepay deduction to be paid in full or partially from income managed funds:

  • obtain any additional information, such as account details, from the Centrepay deduction to be cancelled or varied
  • action Centrepay deduction as appropriate:
    • cease the deduction where it will be paid in full from income managed funds, or
    • vary the deduction as discussed with the customer. For more information, see Starting, changing and cancelling a Centrepay deduction at the request of a customer
  • code new Income Management expense

Rent Deduction Scheme:

Discuss with the customer whether they would like their rent to continue being paid from discretionary funds, changed to an Income Management expense or a combination of both. For more information, see Updating an existing Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) deduction for a customer who has commenced Income Management.

4

Continue workflow: Code expenses + Read more ...

As per the priority needs discussion with the customer during the interview, set up the regular expenses to be paid from Income Management funds.

If there are offset payments note the EPED (as shown on Expense Management Summary screen) as offset payments affect how expenses are coded. The next EPED date will be the start date for each expense allocation.

For new expenses:

  • Select Add regular expenses from the Please select the expense you wish to add: dropdown list.
  • For details on how to code expenses, see Coding Income Management expenses
  • For details on how to code School Meals Program expenses, see Payment of School Meals Program (SMP) expenses from Income Management funds or Centrepay

For customers who wish to pay their expenses via BasicsCard, and choose the Partial Payment method, regular expenses must be set up once the assessment is finalised.

When all expenses have been added, select the Priority Needs hyperlink.

5

Continue workflow: Priority Needs Discussion Result + Read more ...

The results of the priority needs discussion must be recorded on the Priority Needs Expenses screen when conducting an Initial Assessment interview.

As this would be the first discussion being held for a customer commencing Income Management, an answer of Yes or No must be given to each of the Priority Needs types. If priority needs have been recorded previously, two questions will display:

  • Is a discussion being held? Select Yes as a discussion must be recorded as part of an Initial Assessment interview
  • Are there changes? Select Yes or No as appropriate. If the answer is Yes then a change must be made to either the priority expense/s need/s and/or how the expense/s is/are being met

Priority Needs Expense Category

Customer Response: This field records how the customer will or will not meet the priority need. The field appears only after Yes or No is selected against the priority expense type, and appears against each expense type field when selected. When the field appears, a payment method type must be selected.

  • Yes, select the most appropriate value
  • No, select the appropriate value:
    • Not applicable to customer
    • Not met

If this is not the first discussion, Service Officers can view historical discussions under the Priority Needs Discussion History block. Past discussions can be selected and viewed but the details are protected.

Select Continue to be directed back to the Expense Management Summary screen.

Select Continue. See Step 1 in Table 5.

Finalising the Initial Assessment interview process

Table 5: This table contains the steps for finalising an Initial Assessment interview.

Step

Action

1

Finalise workflow: Income Management Summary screen + Read more ...

If there are Work Items that need to be actioned, these will be displayed on the Income Management Summary screen in the Existing Work Items section.

If Work Items cannot be actioned within the workflow, investigate and action while the customer is present, once the workflow has been finalised. If the customer is not present, the Work Items should be left for a Service Officer to action at the next customer contact. For more information on Work Items, see Voluntary Income Management (VIM) Work Items.

Review the Income Management Summary screen to confirm:

  • Voluntary Income Management has been correctly applied ('Qualification Details' block), and
  • payments and expenses are correctly reflected in the 'Fortnightly (14 days) balance details' block

Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page.

The workflow is complete. A DOC will be automatically generated on the customer's record when the activity is finalised. If required, annotate the DOC with any additional information as per the guidelines for adding a DOC to a customer's record.

Advise the customer no letter is issued to confirm they have been placed on Voluntary Income Management, however a copy of the DOC can be printed for their records.

Note: if the activity was started but not finalised on the same day, the date of the interview will be recorded in the DOC, but the timestamp recorded will be the date the activity is finalised.

2

Does the customer require a BasicsCard? + Read more ...

  • Yes:
    • Issue the BasicsCard. See BasicsCard offer and issue of initial BasicsCard
    • Explain how funds will be transferred to the BasicsCard:
      • The Residual Payment method (default), or
      • They choose the Partial Payment method. Set up any regular expenses to the BasicsCard as required. See Coding Income Management expenses
    • Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Does the customer require any specialist referrals, e.g. social worker, Multicultural Service Officer? + Read more ...

  • Yes, refer customer to appropriate specialist as per current business process. For more information, see Specialist officers and other internal supports for customers. Go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Finalise interview + Read more ...

Provide customer with details of expense allocations and any other relevant information about Income Management.

Advise the customer they should contact the agency if they have any problems with their allocations or wish to change them.

Procedure ends here.

5

Voluntary IM Assessment workflow - customer ineligible for Voluntary Income Management + Read more ...

If a customer has requested to enter into Voluntary Income Management and has been determined to be ineligible, this must be recorded.

Launch the Voluntary IM Assessment workflow and complete the Voluntary Agreement Details screen:

  • Receipt date: will default to today's date
  • Start date and End date: these will default to today's date and are protected
  • Status: select Ineligible from the dropdown menu
  • Customer referred by: only complete where the customer was referred to the agency for Voluntary Income Management by a third party organisation
  • Agreement Ineligibility Reasons: select the applicable ineligibility reason from the dropdown menu

Select Continue and finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page.

Advise the customer why they are not eligible for Voluntary Income Management and how they can request a reviews of Income Management decisions.

Record details in a DOC.

If appropriate, refer customer to a specialist as per current business process. For more information, see Specialist officers and other internal supports for customers.

Procedure ends here.

6

Workflow ends: cancel out of the workflow + Read more ...

  • If 'No' has been selected for any of the terms and conditions on the Voluntary Agreement Details screen, an error message will display and the workflow cannot continue
  • Cancel the workflow and cancel the activity on the Activity List (AL) screen (if it exists)
  • Clearly record on a DOC why the customer has not agreed to the Terms and Conditions, and/or the Privacy Notice and Third Party Disclosure information
