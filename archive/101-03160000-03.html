Identifying and actioning ParentsNext work item

For ParentsNext processing Service Officers only.

Table 1

Step

Action

1

ParentsNext work item presents to a Service Officer for action + Read more ...

If the work item is:

  • for a scheduled Services Australia ParentsNext appointment, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • not a scheduled Services Australia appointment, go to Step 2

2

Non ParentsNext Appointment work item to be actioned + Read more ...

For work items allocated as ParentsNext REV and Manual Follow-ups (MFU), check the work item attributes for the associated keyword and the text in the review DOC or MFU activity to determine the action needed:

  • PNXOTH, this keyword is recorded for any situation where a customer's circumstances require a future review, investigate the 'Notes' within the review work item on the Document List (DL) to determine what action is needed. Procedure ends here
  • PNXAOA - a customer was overseas at the time of their scheduled ParentsNext appointment with no expected date of return to Australia. Go to Step 3
  • PNXAPPT - a ParentsNext appointment must be manually booked. Go to Step 5
  • PNXEXP - the customer may be eligible for an exemption. See ParentsNext exits and exemptions
  • PNXSTU – study for greater than 12 months duration to be reviewed. Go to Step 7
  • PNXJ2R – leave with a job to return to for greater than 12 months duration to be reviewed. Go to Step 7

3

PNXAOA Keyword - customer overseas at the time of their scheduled ParentsNext appointment + Read more ...

If the customer has returned to Australia and is still eligible for ParentsNext, they must attend a ParentsNext appointment with Services Australia.

Navigate to the ParentsNext Summary page.

Is the customer still eligible as a compulsory participant?

  • Yes, if the customer has:
    • returned to Australia, book a ParentsNext appointment. For guidance, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System. Procedure ends here
    • not returned to Australia, go to Step 4
  • No, finalise the work item. Procedure ends here

4

Customer has not returned to Australia + Read more ...

Go to the Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) and/or Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen.

Has the expected return to Australia date now been recorded?

  • Yes, book a ParentsNext appointment for 7 days after the expected date of return to Australia. See Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System. Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • place the activity on hold for a further 28 days by checking the Held field
    • when the review matures the customer's circumstances will be reviewed to determine if they have returned to Australia or have a return to Australia date recorded
    • make sure the keyword PNXAOA remains in the review activity. Procedure ends here

5

PNXAPPT Keyword - ParentsNext appointment not auto-booked + Read more ...

The auto-booking of the appointment has failed. If the customer is still eligible for ParentsNext:

  • an appointment must be manually booked. The work item has key words to detail what appointment to book
  • take note of the work item creation date (this is needed if a customer is showing as ineligible)

Navigate to the ParentsNext Summary page to make sure there has not been a change of circumstances, which has resulted in the customer exiting ParentsNext after the creation of the work item.

Is the customer still eligible as a compulsory participant?

  • Yes, go to step 6:
  • No, and then:
    • ParentsNext Summary page is blank. See Step 1 in Table 4
    • most recent exit/ineligible date is before the work item creation date. See Step 1 in Table 4
    • most recent exit/ineligible date is after the work item creation date. Finalise the work item. Procedure ends here

6

Manually book a ParentsNext appointment + Read more ...

Manually book a ParentsNext appointment via the customer's relevant channel.

  • If the customer:
    • Has a current contact number, book a phone appointment
    • Does not have a current contact number, book a face to face appointment at the customer's local service centre
  • Do not suppress the appointment notification letter otherwise the customer will not receive the required notification
  • For guidance, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System. Procedure ends here

7

Study or leave with a job to return to greater than 12 months - Contact customer + Read more ...

The customer previously provided evidence of study or leave with a job to return to of greater than 12 months in duration. A review must be undertaken by contacting the customer to confirm they are still studying or on leave with a job to return to.

Has the customer previously been sent a Q164 – Request to contact letter to review their Current Study more than 12 months or Leave more than 12 months with a job to return to?

  • Yes - Go to Step 9
  • No - Attempt to contact the customer:
    • Go to Workspace > Self Service > Electronic Messaging and online letters to determine if the customer is registered for Electronic Messaging via SMS
    • If the customer is registered, Service Officers should create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Electronic Messaging (EM)
    • Select the document description of 'Pre-call notification'
    • After creating the SMS, wait 5 minutes before making the outbound call attempt
    • If unable to contact the customer on the first attempt, try again approximately 15 minutes later. (Two genuine contact attempts should be made to the customer)
    • Sufficient detail about the outbound contact attempts must be included in a DOC

Was the customer successfully contacted by phone?

  • Yes, and the review was for:
    • Study - Go to Step 10
    • Leave with a job to return to – Go to Step 11
  • No, Go to Step 8

8

Issue a Q164 letter after unsuccessful contact attempts or where no contact details are recorded + Read more ...

  • Issue a Q164 letter asking the customer to contact Services Australia by phone within 14 days:
    • after a second unsuccessful outbound contact attempt, or
    • where there are no contact details or incorrect details are recorded (for example, the customers phone number is not registered)
  • The customer has 14 days to respond after they receive the letter. Add days to allow extra time for mail delivery
  • Add notes to the work item (current study or leave with a job to return to review) to detail a Q164 was sent as the customer could not be contacted
  • Place the review work item on HOLD for 15 days (or longer if additional time was given for the customer to respond)

Procedure ends here.

9

Customer previously sent Q164 – Request to contact letter + Read more ...

As previous attempts to contact the customer to review their study/leave have been unsuccessful, they were sent a Q164 to contact.

Check the customer's record to determine if contact has been made as a result of receiving the request to contact letter:

  • Document List (DL) screen
  • Scanned images within the Document tools menu of Customer First Workspace

Has the customer responded to the Q164 – Request to contact letter?

  • Yes:
    • Navigate to the ParentsNext Summary page to check the study/leave start and end dates. For:
      - Study – select the twisty at the Study section
      - Leave – select the twisty at the Leave With a Job To Return To section
  • If there are no changes to the study/leave, complete the review. Procedure ends here
    • If a change has occurred to the study/leave, for:
      - Study – Go to Step 10
      - leave with a job to return to – Go to Step 11
  • No:
  • Suspend customers Parenting Payment Failed to Reply to Correspondence – See Suspension of Parenting Payment (PP)

10

Review study greater than 12 months + Read more ...

Is the customer still studying?

  • Yes, and the study end date is still correct:
    • Tell the customer if they stop studying they must contact to advise
    • Complete the review
    • DOC the record with details of the contact
  • Yes, and the study end date has changed since it was recorded:
    • Go to the ParentsNext Summary page
    • From the Study section select the existing study recorded
    • Within the interaction, select the Staff Workflow tab and click into the Study workflow
    • Update the end date as appropriate
    • Go to the Review screen and finalise the GP
    • Tell the customer they may be selected for ParentsNext in the future when their study concludes
    • DOC the record with details
    • If the study end date is more than 12 months in the future create a manual review for the reason NEV (Possible Notifiable event) and include the keyword PNXSTU. Record the customer must be contacted in 12 months to confirm they are still studying and that the study continues to meet the minimum ParentsNext study requirements
  • No,
    • Go to the ParentsNext Summary page
    • From the Study section select the existing study recorded
    • Within the interaction, select the Staff Workflow tab and click into the Study workflow
    • Update the end date with the system date
    • Go to the Review screen and finalise the GP
    • Tell the customer as they are no longer studying they may be selected for ParentsNext in the future
    • Complete the review
    • DOC the record with details

Procedure ends here.

11

Review leave with a job to return to greater than 12 months + Read more ...

Is the customer still on leave with a job to return to?

  • Yes, and the leave end date is still correct:
    • Tell the customer if their leave ends they must contact to advise
    • Complete the review
    • DOC the record with details of the contact
    • Yes, and the leave end date has changed since it was recorded:
    • Go to the ParentsNext Summary page
    • From the Leave with a job to return to section select the existing leave recorded
    • Within the interaction, select the Staff Workflow tab and click into the Leave with a job to return to workflow
    • Update the end date with the system date
    • Go to the Review screen and finalise the GP
    • Tell the customer they may be selected for ParentsNext in the future when their leave concludes
    • DOC the record with details
    • If the leave end date is more than 12 months in the future create a manual review for the reason NEV (Possible Notifiable event) and include the keyword PNXJ2R. Record the customer must be contacted in 12 months to confirm they are still on leave with a job to return to
  • No,
    • Go to the ParentsNext Summary page
    • From the Leave with a job to return to section select the existing leave recorded
    • Within the interaction, select the Staff Workflow tab and click into the Leave with a job to return to workflow
    • Update the end date as appropriate
    • Go to the Review screen and finalise the GP
    • Tell the customer as they are no longer on leave with a job to return to they may be selected for ParentsNext in the future
    • Complete the review
    • DOC the record with details

Procedure ends here.

For ParentsNext processing Service Officers only.

Action

ParentsNext appointment due - Review customer's record to confirm eligibility status + Read more ...

  • Select Ready in the Customer First inbox
  • 'Pick up' the appointment
  • Change the Status field to In Process
  • Select Save

This makes sure the work item is not allocated to another Service Officer.

As circumstances can change resulting in a customer exiting ParentsNext after the appointment was booked but before the scheduled day of the appointment, review the customer's record to make sure they are still eligible as a compulsory ParentsNext participant before starting the phone or face to face appointment.

Navigate to the ParentsNext Summary page.

Note: if the youngest child has reached 5 years and 6 months of age after the customer's selection for ParentsNext, they are still eligible and must be referred to a provider.

Is the customer eligible as a compulsory participant?

  • Yes, and the work item is for the
    • First outbound contact attempt for a phone appointment or it is a face to face appointment, go to Step 2
    • Second outbound attempt for a phone appointment, see Step 4 in Table 6
  • No, and
    • there is an ineligible reason displayed, see Step 1 in Table 3
    • the ParentsNext Summary page is blank (the Customer Summary page will display a status of Never been a ParentsNext participant), see Step 1 in Table 4

2

Customer eligible for ParentsNext + Read more ...

Customers may have been required to attend a second ParentsNext appointment to provide verification of their circumstances after having attended a previous ParentsNext appointment. Review the record to determine if this is the first or a subsequent follow up appointment.

There may be notes included in the appointment work item, otherwise go to the Document List (DL) screen to determine if there is a DOC recorded about a previous appointment. If it is:

  • the first ParentsNext appointment, go to Step 3
  • a second or subsequent ParentsNext and the customer needs to provide verification of:
    • highest level of education achieved before selection in ParentsNext, see Step 6 in Table 5
    • expected date of confinement, see Step 7 in Table 5
    • maternity/paternity leave, see Step 8 in Table 5
    • leave with a job to return to, see Step 8 in Table 5
    • current study, see Step 9 in Table 5

3

Check child details + Read more ...

ParentsNext selection is based on the information recorded about PP children in care at the time of the eligibility assessment. If the child and related care details are not correctly recorded or not up to date at the time of the eligibility assessment, customers may be incorrectly selected for ParentsNext where they have a child in care under 9 months of age. See ParentsNext eligibility and servicing.

To confirm the age of the youngest child in the customer's care go to the Child Selection (CHS) screen.

Does the customer appear to have a child under 9 months of age in care?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Appears customer has a child under 9 months in care on CHS + Read more ...

There can be circumstances where one family unit has chosen to record some of the children in the care of one customer and others in the care of the other customer. In these cases it must be determined if the younger child with no PP entry displaying on the CHS screen line is actually recorded as being in the ParentsNext participants care.

  • 'S'elect child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen
  • On the Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen 'S'elect In Customer Care (CHC) screen and Shared Care (SSA) SSC screen

Is the youngest PP child under 9 months old recorded as being in the ParentsNext participant's care and has a shared care percentage of 55% or greater?

  • Yes, the child's details need updating. See ParentsNext exits and exemptions
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Review customer's circumstances + Read more ...

As there may be circumstances that could impact the outcome of the ParentsNext appointment, or the customer's ability to participate in ParentsNext, review the customer's record before contacting them or starting a face to face appointment.

If:

  • PP is Suspended for failing to attend a ParentsNext appointment (FDH), PP should only remain suspended where the customer has missed more than 2 previous ParentsNext appointments within this ParentsNext eligibility cycle. The appointment must still be conducted and when the customer attends their scheduled appointment PP will be restored. See Step 1 in Table 6
  • PP is Suspended for a non-ParentsNext reason, if the suspension is:
    • due to imprisonment (IMP) see Step 3 in Table 3
    • for any other reason, go to Step 6
  • the youngest PP child has reached 5 years and 6 months of age since the customer was selected, the customer is still eligible and needs to participate, see Step 1 in Table 6
  • the customer is currently overseas, go to Step 8
  • there is evidence of a new born child (for example, a FAO81) who is not yet recorded on the Child Selection (CHS) screen on the customer's record, see Step 4 in Table 3
  • none of the above apply, see Step 1 in Table 6

6

PP is suspended for a non-ParentsNext reason + Read more ...

Investigate the reason for the suspension to determine if the suspension can be resolved and the appointment can still be conducted.

See Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Parenting Payment (PP) for a list of suspension codes and whether PP can be restored.

Attempt to contact the customer. Has contact with the customer been successful?

  • Yes, and the suspension:
    • can be resolved, go to Step 7
    • cannot be resolved, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

7

Current suspension can be resolved + Read more ...

Make any required updates about the suspension and restore payment:

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen
  • Svc Rsn field, PPP or PPS
  • Action field, Restore
  • Reason field, leave blank
  • Effect Date field, select [Enter] for the date of effect to default to the date of suspension
  • Source field, select relevant source
  • DOR field, date the information was recorded
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity
  • DOC the record

See Step 1 in Table 7.

8

Customer overseas at time of ParentsNext appointment + Read more ...

If it is identified the customer is overseas at the time of their scheduled appointment, their expected date of return to Australia must be determined.

Go to the Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) and/or Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen.

Has the return to Australia date been recorded?

  • Yes:
    • Book a ParentsNext appointment for 7 days after the expected date of return to Australia. For guidance, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System
    • Record the ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'completed'
  • No:
    • Record the ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'completed'
    • Generate the Fast Note – select Auto text, use Parents > Update > ParentsNext Travel Overseas, ensuring the Fast Note remains open and is placed on hold for 28 days. This will allow the customer's record to be reviewed in the future to determine if they have returned to Australia or have a return date recorded. Once the 28 days have expired and the Fast Note comes off hold, see ParentsNext eligibility and servicing

For more details on creating a Fast Note, see Creating, reviewing and deleting documents (including Fast Notes, and DOA DOCs).

9

Current suspension cannot be resolved + Read more ...

Tell the customer of the suspension reason and action needed for payment to be restored:

  • Record the ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'completed'
  • Book a ParentsNext appointment for 14 days' time, by which point the suspension should be resolved. For guidance, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System. Procedure ends here

10

Payment currently suspended - contact unsuccessful + Read more ...

Record the ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'completed':

  • Book a ParentsNext appointment for 14 days' time, by which time the suspension should be resolved. For guidance, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System
  • Record a DOC to detail an outbound contact attempt about the scheduled ParentsNext appointment was unsuccessful
  • As the payment has already been suspended, PP cannot be further suspended for failing to attend the ParentsNext appointment. Procedure ends here

Action required where customer is no longer eligible for ParentsNext

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Customer no longer eligible for ParentsNext + Read more ...

There can be various reasons why a customer is no longer eligible for ParentsNext at the time of their scheduled appointment including the cancellation of their Parenting Payment (PP). ParentsNext Service Officers do not need to investigate or resolve the cancellation of payment, unless the cancellation is for the reason of Failed to Report (FRP).

There is a known ICT issue where ParentsNext eligibility information fails to replicate to the:

  • ParentsNext Summary page, and
  • Customer Summary page

This failure results in a ParentsNext appointment being correctly booked for a compulsory ParentsNext participant however, the record incorrectly displays as ineligible when the appointment falls due. To make sure the customer is no longer eligible Service Officers must check the most recent exit/ineligible date occurred after the appointment creation date.

Is the most recent exit/ineligible date after the appointment creation date?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 4

2

Customer is no longer eligible as a compulsory ParentsNext participant + Read more ...

As the customer is no longer eligible for ParentsNext at the time of their scheduled appointment they do not need to participate as a compulsory participant but they do have the option to participate voluntarily.

If the appointment was a phone appointment and the customer is not already present, contact must still be attempted as a courtesy, as they will have received notification of the appointment.

Was the customer already present via phone or was the outbound phone contact successful or did the customer attend for their face to face appointment?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No:
    • Do not suspend the payment
    • Record the appointment outcome as 'completed'
    • DOC the record to detail the customer is no longer eligible for ParentsNext at this time (and if it was a phone appointment, that an outbound contact was attempted). Procedure ends here

3

Customer imprisoned at time of scheduled ParentsNext appointment + Read more ...

If a customer's PP is suspended due to imprisonment (IMP) at the time of the scheduled ParentsNext appointment, the record must be investigated to determine if a release date is recorded. See Confirming prison admission and release dates.

Note: Service Officers do not need to make any contact with either the customer's Prison Liaison Officer or the prison/institute directly. Only interrogation of the record is needed.

Is a release date recorded on the customer's record?

  • Yes:
    • Book a ParentsNext appointment for 14 days after the release date. For guidance, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System
    • Record the current ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'completed'
    • DOC the record to detail the customer was imprisoned at the time of the appointment and a new appointment has been booked, procedure ends here
  • No:
    • Do not book a future ParentsNext appointment. There is an auto-review that will check the customer's eligibility 4 weeks after they were selected. If the customer is still eligible at that time a new ParentsNext appointment will be automatically booked or a review created to manually follow up the status
    • Record the appointment outcome as 'completed'
    • DOC the record to detail the customer was imprisoned at the time of the appointment but a new appointment was not booked as there is no release date recorded, procedure ends here

4

Evidence of a new born child on customer's record + Read more ...

It may become evident either from the presence of a Newborn Child Declaration (FAO81) on the customer's record or a DOC during a discussion with the customer that there is a new child under 9 months old in care who has not yet been recorded on the customer's record.

In this scenario, the new child must be recorded before referring the customer to a ParentsNext provider.

If the appointment was a phone appointment and the customer is not already present, contact must still be attempted as a courtesy, as they will have received notification of the appointment.

Was the outbound phone contact successful or did the customer attend for their face to face appointment:

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No:
    • Do not suspend payment
    • Record the current ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'completed'
    • Do not book a new ParentsNext appointment. There is an auto-review that will check the customer's eligibility 4 weeks after they were selected. If the customer is still eligible at that time a new ParentsNext appointment will be auto-booked or a review created to manually follow up the status
    • Record a DOC on the customer's record stating:
      - there is evidence of a newborn child that is yet to be recorded on the customer's record. This can impact the customer's eligibility for ParentsNext
      - if the customer makes contact, transfer to the Families main business line to discuss coding of the new born child

Procedure ends here.

5

New Child in care that is not recorded on customer's record + Read more ...

If a customer has either provided evidence of a new born on their record or during their phone or face to face appointment they have advised of a new child under 9 months entering their care but the child is not recorded on the CHS screen:

  • Check the DOC list to determine if evidence has been received, where there is:
    • a completed FAO81 and no more information is needed, tell the customer nothing further is needed at this time and their record will be updated in due course
    • a DOC detailing more information is needed to update the record, tell the customer what must be provided
    • no evidence or it is unclear on what is needed, transfer or tell the customer to contact the Families main business line to discuss their circumstances with a families trained Service Officer. Do not tell the customer that this will expedite the process, but rather make sure they are aware of all that is needed to finalise their claim
  • Tell the customer if a further ParentsNext appointment is needed they will be notified of the appointment date and time
  • Record the current ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'completed'
  • Do not book a new ParentsNext appointment. There is an auto-review that will check the customer's eligibility 4 weeks after they were selected. If the customer is still eligible at that time a new ParentsNext appointment will be auto-booked or a review created to manually follow up the status
  • Record a DOC to detail the customer has a new child in care which is yet to be recorded on their record and which may impact on their eligibility for ParentsNext

Procedure ends here.

6

Offer customer option to volunteer in ParentsNext + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • That due to their current circumstances they are no longer a compulsory ParentsNext participant at this time
  • This means that they do not need to participate in the program. However, they can still choose to volunteer

Tell them that if they volunteer, their provider will:

  • Support them to identify and work towards their education and employment goals
  • Have access to financial help through the Participation Fund, a flexible pool of funds that can be used to cover costs associated with work or study, such as the cost of training courses, child care, transport, IT equipment and job appropriate clothing

Tell the customer that they can find more information about the program and read stories from participants at the ParentsNext website.

Does the customer want to volunteer?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, tell the customer if they change their mind they can either direct register with a ParentsNext provider or recontact Centrelink. Procedure ends here

7

Customer elected to participate voluntarily + Read more ...

As the customer has elected to participate voluntarily, they need to first be registered via the Volunteer Guided Procedure (GP).

Note: the Volunteer GP will only be selectable if the customer meets the eligibility criteria for voluntary participation.

Register the customer as a voluntary participant:

  • Go to the ParentsNext Summary screen via the Workspace > Social Services Benefit Management menu
  • Launch the Volunteer GP from the Volunteer button under the Volunteer section of the page and ask the customer the eligibility questions
  • Select the relevant radio button based on the customer's responses to each question
  • Does the customer wish to volunteer? This will automatically default to 'No' if the customer's responses result in them being ineligible, otherwise select 'Yes'
  • Comments field, record relevant notes based on whether the customer was eligible to volunteer or not
  • Go to the Review screen and finalise the GP

Note: if there is a delay in the update correctly reassessing eligibility for ParentsNext, run the daily driver.

See Step 1 of Table 8

Data replication failure and escalation process

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Data replication failure + Read more ...

There is a known SAP data replication issue where ParentsNext eligibility information fails to replicate to the ParentsNext Summary page and the Customer Summary page.

This issue impacts the accuracy of information displayed on the customer's record as follows:

If the customer has:

  • Never been selected for ParentsNext:
    • the ParentsNext Summary page will be blank, and
    • the Customer Summary page will display as 'never been a ParentsNext participant'
  • Previously been selected for ParentsNext, exited and then later, selected again:
    • the ParentsNext Summary page will display the most recent entry with an exit/ineligible date from before the failed appointment work item or appointment creation date
    • the Customer Summary page will display with a status of ineligible for ParentsNext

The failure results in a compulsory ParentsNext participant having:

  • a ParentsNext appointment correctly booked, but it appears they are ineligible, or
  • a failed appointment work item correctly created, but it appears they are ineligible

If the issue was identified:

  • during a scheduled ParentsNext appointment, go to Step 2
  • on allocation of a failed appointment work item, place the work item on Hold for a further 7 days by checking the Held field, go to Step 3

2

ParentsNext appointment + Read more ...

The customer will have received notification of their appointment. If it is a phone appointment, attempt to contact them:

Was the outbound phone contact successful or did the customer attend for their face to face appointment?

  • Yes, tell the customer that due to current technical difficulties the agency is unable to complete the appointment at this time. Once the issue has been resolved, a new appointment will be booked. They will receive notification of the new date and time
  • No, do not suspend the payment

Record the appointment outcome as completed regardless of whether contact with the customer was made, or not.

3

Escalate the customer record + Read more ...

The customer record must be escalated for corrective action:

  • Send an email to the National Team:
    • Include the impacted Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • Send the email as High Importance and sensitive
    • Record in the subject line 'SAP data replication issue'
  • Create a Display on Access (DOA) DOC to record:
    • whether contact was made with the customer or not (this would be for a phone appointment)
    • If contact was made or the customer attended for a face to face appointment, record details of the conversation
    • that the customer was advised a new appointment will be booked once the issue is resolved
    • that the CRN is impacted by a known ICT issue due to a data replication failure. It has been escalated to the ParentsNext Business Team for action

Procedure ends here.

Verification of circumstances

For ParentsNext processing Service Officers only.

Table 5

Step

Action

1

Customer has achieved Year 12 or equivalent + Read more ...

If a customer is selected for ParentsNext and they achieved Year 12 or equivalent before their selection and they have been receiving income support for less than 4 years, they do not need to participate in ParentsNext.

Evidence of the highest level of education achieved before selection for ParentsNext must be provided before customer's record can be updated.

Evidence of education obtained from overseas is acceptable. See the Resources page for examples of overseas qualifications acceptable for ParentsNext.

Is the customer able to provide verification they achieved the required level of education before selection for ParentsNext at the time of the appointment?

  • Yes, See ParentsNext exits and exemptions
  • No, go to Step 5

2

Customer pregnant and within 6 weeks of due date + Read more ...

Customers that are within 6 weeks of their expected date of confinement are eligible for a ParentsNext Temporary Period of Confinement (PTC) exemption.

Evidence of the expected date of confinement (for example, a letter from a doctor or registered midwife) must be provided before the PTC exemption can be recorded.

Is the customer able to provide evidence of their expected date of confinement at the time of the appointment?

  • Yes, See ParentsNext exits and exemptions
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Customer is currently studying + Read more ...

If a customer is studying but not receiving Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), the study will not generally be recorded on their Centrelink record before selection into ParentsNext, as there is currently no requirement for them to advise of these details.

Customers who have been selected for ParentsNext and advise they are currently studying must provide evidence of the study meeting the minimum ParentsNext study requirements before their record can be updated.

The evidence may include:

  • the institution's enrolment advice to the student
  • an enrolment fee receipt
  • Mainstream Enrolments Via the Internet (MEVI) receipts - (for TAFE courses only, and can only be accepted if the nominal course hours are on the receipt)
