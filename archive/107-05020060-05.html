Direct debit confirmation letter

This letter:

  • is sent when a direct debit (voluntary deduction) is coded and all fields are completed on the Direct Debit (OPVD) screen
  • advises the customer to make sure there is enough money in their account to cover each payment. If funds are not available, their financial institution (FI) may charge a fee
  • includes customer rights and responsibilities and details of the direct debit arrangement:
    • name of the financial institution
    • account number/name
    • deduction commencement date and rate

The letter will display details of recoverable debts of any age that meet all of the following criteria:

  • the Outstanding Balance is greater than $0
  • the debt does not have a status of Finalised - Written Off (FWO)
  • where the debt has a status of Determined - Written Off (DWO) the write-off reason is one of:
    • DDE Debtor Deceased - Awaiting Estate
    • DOR Debtor Overseas
    • NCE Not Cost Effective
    • ORA Outcome of Review/Appeal
    • PRI In Prison
    • STH Short Term Hardship
    • TAX Refer for Possible Tax Garnishee, or
    • WUN Whereabouts Unknown
  • the Authority is not Other Government Department (OGD)
  • the AOS Protection indicator is not Y
  • The CRN balance which is displayed both in the Pay Information and the Schedule will be the total outstanding balance of all debts that meet the criteria for inclusion in the Schedule

Note: this letter may not include details of all debts. For example, it does not include details of debts that are at an external collection agent.

Customer claim process

Customers can lodge a claim for a refund of FI fees or charges they incur from an incorrect Centrelink direct debit. Centrelink will accept the claim for any direct debits wrongly initiated by Centrelink. If a claim is accepted, Centrelink will:

  • make every attempt to resolve the dispute within five (5) business days
  • advise the customer of the outcome
  • refund the customer or arrange for payment of expenses if such a decision is made

If a customer contacts their FI to lodge a claim, the FI should refer the customer back to Centrelink. If the FI does accept the claim the FI will forward the claim to the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA). The RBA:

  • may request Centrelink to produce evidence of the direct debit authority (for example, a copy of the direct debit confirmation letter)
  • must respond to the FI advising whether the claim is accepted or refused within five (5) business days
  • will advise the customer of the outcome of the claim
  • will notify Centrelink of the outcome of any claim it handles

If the claim is successful the RBA will request Centrelink to make a refund and/or to arrange for payment of expenses if such a decision is made.

If the FI does not receive a response from the RBA within the specified period:

  • the FI may refund the customer the disputed amount and advise the RBA
  • the RBA will advise Centrelink that it will draw on Centrelink's RBA account to reimburse the FI
  • the RBA will provide Centrelink (in writing), customer details to refund the money

Example

Text

Refund claim accepted / supported

Services Australia accept the claim for a total of $___.__ for DD/MM/YR $___.__ [include all dates and amounts included in DDR claim, where appropriate]. Please find attached completed DDR claim.

The arrangement was [choose an option]

  • set up by the customer via their online services on DD/MM/YYY. Services Australia have been unable to contact the customer
  • not set up by the customer

Further direct debit deductions have been ceased.

Please contact me for further information if required.

Refund claim refused / not supported

Services Australia are refusing the claim. The authority form [this is the Direct Debit Confirmation letter. It is only to be included where customer has given permission for a copy to be sent to the RBA] and the completed DDR claim are attached.

The customer set up the direct debit arrangement themselves via online services on DD/MM/YYYY.

Please let me know if you require any further assistance.

Cessation of direct debt supported / ceased

Services Australia ceased the customers' payment arrangement on DD/MM/YYYY as [choose an option]:

  • the debt has been fully recovered
  • per the customer request
  • the customer is not required to make repayments toward their debt at this time

No further action is required.

Please let me know if you require any further assistance.

Cessation of direct debit not supported / not ceased

Services Australia contacted the customer on DD/MM/YYY. The customer has acknowledged the direct debit arrangement accepted on DD/MM/YYY. The customer has indicated they want the arrangement to continue.

The customer has been advised to contact their financial institution to withdraw their cancellation request.

No further action is required.

Please let me know if you require any further assistance.
