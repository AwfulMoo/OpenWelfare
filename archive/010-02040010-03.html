Determining ABSTUDY independence when a customer is currently or previously married or in a registered relationship

Table1.

Step

Action

1

Relationship status + Read more ...

Determine the relationship status of the student or Australian Apprentice.

Are they currently, or have they previously been:

  • legally married
  • married under Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander Lore, or
  • in a registered relationship?
  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 4

2

Independence status + Read more ...

Check if the student or Australian Apprentice was assessed as independent under an ABSTUDY permanent independent criteria for a previous claim.

To check, in Customer First go to the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen.

Have they previously been assessed as independent under an ABSTUDY permanent independent status?

  • Yes, no further action is required to assess independence. However, their relationship status may need updating. See Member of a Couple (MoC) and Separated under one roof (SUOR) assessments. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Supporting documentation + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice may be required to provide evidence to support their claim for independence due to currently or previously married, or in a registered relationship.

To determine if further evidence is required, in Customer First, go to the Marital Status (MS) screen.

Does the student or Australian Apprentice's MS screen have MAR, REG or DIV recorded in the Marital Status field?

  • Yes, no further evidence will be required. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, further evidence will be required to support their independence claim. Evidence must include:
    • an original or certified copy of their marriage certificate
    • legal documentation confirming marriage, or previous marriage
    • a statement confirming the marriage within the community, signed by a recognised Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander elder responsible for cultural ceremonies, or
    • evidence confirming the relationship between the person and another person is registered under a law of a State or Territory prescribed for the purposes of section 22B of the Acts Interpretation Act 1901 as a kind of relationship prescribed for the purposes of that section and they are not living separately from the other person on a permanent or indefinite basis

Go to Step 5.

4

Independence status not met + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice is not eligible for grant of independent status for ABSTUDY on the grounds of currently or previously married or in a registered relationship.

  • Consider whether the student or Australian Apprentice is independent on other grounds. See Independence for ABSTUDY
  • If appropriate, refer them for other assistance, e.g. social worker or Indigenous Service Officer

Go to Step 7.

5

Procedure when customer contacts + Read more ...

If the student or Australian Apprentice has contacted through:

  • a service centre, go to Step 6
  • Smart Centre Call go to Step 7
  • the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Services teams, see Step 1 in Table 2

6

Scanning supporting documentation + Read more ...

Ensure the claim/form and all supporting documents are scanned to the student or Australian Apprentice's record to allow for processing by the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Services team.

Procedure ends here.

7

Record details of the discussion + Read more ...

Record details of the discussion in a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

Coding ABSTUDY independence when a customer is currently or previously married or in a registered relationship

Table 2. For ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing staff only.

Step

Action

1

Assessing eligibility + Read more ...

Check the evidence provided to determine if the student or Australian Apprentice meets eligibility for independence due to currently or previously married or in a registered relationship.

If the Marital Status (MS) screen is coded as Married (MAR) or Registered Relationship (REG), they are automatically assessed as independent. The Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen does not need to be coded with the independence details.

Is there enough evidence to support granting independence due to currently or previously married or in a registered relationship?

  • Yes, if processed in:
    • Customer First, go to Step 4
    • Process Direct, go to Step 2
  • No, if processed in:
    • Customer First, go to Step 5
    • Process Direct, go to Step 3

2

Coding independence details in Process Direct + Read more ...

The independence data will pre-fill the NIH screen from information in the claim.

Check the NIH screen via Super Key or by selecting the My Circs icon.

If the information has not pre-filled or needs to be changed, code manually. For example, an incorrect start date has been recorded.

If manual coding is required complete the following fields:

  • Action: code 'IND'
  • Start/Reject Reason: code 'PBM'
  • Start Date: code the later of :
    • ABSTUDY start date
    • date the student or Australian Apprentice became independent

Link the student or Australian Apprentice and their partner to ensure the correct ABSTUDY rate is paid. See Change in relationship status from single to partnered.

Go to Step 6.

3

Deleting independence details in Process Direct + Read more ...

If the student or Australian Apprentice does not meet the eligibility for independence due to currently or previously married or in a registered relationship, the pre-filled information must be deleted from the NIH screen.

To delete an entry:

  • go to the NIH screen via Super Key or by selecting the My Circs icon
  • select the Delete icon to the left of the line where start/reject reason PBM is coded
  • select Yes to confirm deletion

Go to Step 6.

4

Coding independence details in Customer First + Read more ...

Student or Australian Apprentice meets eligibility for independence due to currently or previously married, or in a registered relationship.

Complete the following fields on the NIH screen:

  • Action: code 'IND'
  • Start/Reject Reason: code 'PBM'
  • Start Date: code the later of
    • :ABSTUDY start date
    • date the student or Australian Apprentice became independent

Link the student or Australian Apprentice and their partner to ensure the correct ABSTUDY rate is paid. See Change in relationship status from single to partnered.

5

Record decision in Customer First + Read more ...

Record details of the decision in a DOC. The information must indicate the grounds on which the claim for independence has been granted or rejected, and if rejected, the reasons for the rejection.

Has the claim for independence been granted?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, procedure ends here

6

Record decision in Process Direct + Read more ...

Record details of the decision in Notes or Document annotations. The information must indicate the grounds on which the claim for independence has been granted or rejected, and if rejected, the reasons for the rejection.

Has the claim for independence been granted?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, procedure ends here

7

Determine if a current Child Support assessment exists + Read more ...

To check if an assessment exists for the student or Australian Apprentice, see Maintenance Income Test (MIT) for ABSTUDY and Youth Allowance (YA).

Is there a current Child Support assessment for the student or Australian Apprentice?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, procedure ends here

8

Terminating event for Child Support send ACTDOC to CSJ + Read more ...

Create an ACTDOC with the following text:

'Student or Australian Apprentice has been granted ABSTUDY and is independent based on currently or previously married, or in a registered relationship and is no longer in the care of a parent/guardian from {date}. This is a terminating event for maintenance purposes. Please update Cuba to reflect the event for the young person, if the parent(s)/guardian(s) of the young person is a mutual Centrelink/Child Support customer'

Send the ACTDOC to region code CSJ.
