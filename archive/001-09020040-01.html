Mutual obligation requirements for Youth Allowance job seekers under 22 years of age who have not completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification 001-09020040

This document explains the mutual obligation requirements for Youth Allowance (YA) job seekers under 22 years of age who have not completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification.

Definition of Early School Leaver (ESL)

Youth Allowance (YA) job seekers under 22 years of age, who have not completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification are identified as Early School Leavers (ESL).

Identifying ESLs

Job seekers can be identified as an ESL via the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen. All YA job seeker claims will require verification action and coding on the NES screen to specify if the job seeker has evidence they have completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification. The verification can only be actioned by a Services Australia Service Officer.

If a customer transfers from YA (full-time student) to YA (job seeker) and has not completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification, they will be subject to the ESL requirements from their date of transfer.

Job seekers who completed a Certificate II in 2015 or earlier

YA students who completed a Certificate II in 2015 or earlier, and who are current and continue on payment when they transfer to YA job seeker, will not be identified as an ESL.

If there has been a break in payment for 13 weeks or more and the job seeker previously had a Certificate II keyed as Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification, it will no longer remove them from the ESL cohort.

Mutual obligation requirements for ESLs

ESLs are required to undertake approved activities and/or job search or a combination of these to meet their mutual obligation requirements until they turn 22 years of age or attain Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification. For provider managed job seekers, this is:

  • full-time education or training
  • a combination of part-time education or training and part-time work for a total of 25 hours per week (15 hours for Principal Carer Parents and those with a Partial Capacity to Work of 15 to 29 hours per week), or
  • other approved activities for 25 hours per week (15 hours for Principal Carer Parents and those with a Partial Capacity to Work of 15 to 29 hours per week)
  • in Community Development Program (CDP) regions activities such as study and training are voluntary. For all CDP job seekers, including ESL:
    • CDP providers consider each job seeker's assessed capacity and individual circumstances when negotiating Job Plans
    • minimum hourly requirements do not apply, and
    • there are no specific requirements for CDP job seekers based on their age group or participation category

It is the responsibility of the Employment Services Provider to discuss mutual obligation requirements with the job seeker and negotiate a Job Plan with appropriate activities.

Activities that result in an ESL being managed by Services Australia

ESLs who have one of the following approved activities will be managed by Services Australia (the agency):

  • Quarterly Participation Interview (QTI) (job seekers with partial capacity to work (PCW) or temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC) of 0-14 hours per week)
  • Job seekers with partial capacity to work (PCW) or temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC) of 15-29 hours per week undertaking paid work, study or self-employment, or a combination of the 3, for at least 15 hours per week
  • Principal carers undertaking paid work, study or self-employment, or a combination of the 3, for at least 15 hours per week
  • Job seekers participating in NEIS whose NEIS Allowance has ceased after 39 weeks after commencing the programme
  • Job seekers participating in Youth Activities

    Referrals to Employment Service Providers

    ESLs are eligible for referral to either Transition to Work (TtW), jobactive, Disability Employment Services (DES) or the Community Development Program (CDP) for job seekers who live in a Department of Education, Skills and Employment designated remote area.

    Note: except for eligible job seekers in CDP regions, ESLs identified as eligible must be referred to Transition to Work (where available) and not be referred to jobactive.

    Verification of Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification

    Youth Allowance job seekers who advise that they have completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification are required to provide evidence of their qualification to Services Australia. If evidence is not provided, the job seeker will be considered an ESL. Note: ensure qualifications are recorded correctly.

    Check if the job seeker has provided verification of completing Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification and the details are confirmed via the Education Course History (EDCH) screen. If so, ensure the Y12/Equiv Verified field on the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen is updated with an appropriate code. For example, 'OTH' (other evidence as appropriate).

    Updating education level on the JSCI

    When the ESLs education qualification level has been updated via the JSCI to state that they have now completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification, the Employment Services Provider will notify the agency. A Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity will be generated on the job seeker's record to prompt a Service Officer to follow-up and obtain proof of the qualification from the ESL in order for this to be keyed on the NES screen.

    Reminders to provide verification of Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification

    A Short Message Service (SMS) and/or e-mail reminder will be sent to ESLs advising them to provide their educational qualifications for verification to the agency as soon as possible. A reminder will also be sent if the ESL uses the self-service facilities.

    Proof of studies provided to Employment Services Provider

    Early School Leavers can provide original documentation that proves Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification to their provider. The provider will sight the original documentation and fax a copy to the National Business Gateway (NBG). Upon receipt of the fax, NBG staff will take appropriate action including updating the NES screen.

    The latest information relating to the Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification verification on the NES screen will be automatically transmitted to the Employment Services System.

    Verification of Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification at new claim

    Job seekers have 28 days after claim to provide verification they have completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification. If verification has not been provided by the end of the 28 day period, a Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity will be displayed on the job seeker's record on the Activity List (AL) screen. In addition, an Action Item will be displayed.

    Both the MFU and Action Item are reminders to Service Officers to take follow-up actions regarding the verification of the job seeker's qualification. If verification is not provided at the next contact (generally when the job seeker lodges their next Reporting Statement), the Service Officer must tell the job seeker they will be identified as an ESL and the Service Officer will need to update the JSCI and NES screen. It will be the responsibility of the Employment Services Provider to discuss mutual obligation requirements with the job seeker and to negotiate a Job Plan with appropriate activities.

    Referral to Employment Services Provider

    Some YA job seekers may be exempt from RapidConnect at the initial claim stage due to potential eligibility for Skills for Education and Employment (SEE) or Adult Migrant English Program (AMEP). These job seekers are however still required to be referred immediately to an Employment Services Provider with a note in the Online Diary that they would benefit from participating in the relevant programme.

    ESLs who are under 18 years of age

    As a system requirement, all job seekers under 18 years of age who have not completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) and are a new customer are required to have a study exemption keyed on the NES screen to qualify for YA, even when they are undertaking approved activities to satisfy mutual obligation requirements.

    However, Youth Allowance will no longer be cancelled when the exemption expires. The exemption does not have to be recorded again while the job seeker remains on payment.

    The Resources page contains a list of study exemptions that can be granted for YA job seekers under 18 years of age who have not completed Year 12 or an equivalent level of education (Certificate III or higher) qualification and links to the Services Australia website.
