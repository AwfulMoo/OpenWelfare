Pre-birth/entry into care claim for FTB, PPL or DAP

Step

Action

1

Customer enquires about progress of early claim for FTB, PPL or DAP + Read more ...

If an early claim has not been lodged, advise the customer how they can lodge an early claim. See Initial contact for an early claim for family assistance and Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

An early claim is awaiting assessment

This is indicated by a claim activity on the Activity List (AL) page/screen with keyword PRECLM.

To check, go to the AL page and code 'K' next to the claim activity.

Activity List (AL) page example - early claim activity

Is the customer's early claim awaiting assessment?

  • Yes, check the progress DOC on the customer's record and advise customer of outcome. Procedure ends here
  • No, the early claim may have been processed. Go to Step 2

2

Check claim status + Read more ...

An assessed early claim is indicated by a benefit status of DAP/PND (pending) or PPL/ASS (assessed).

Select Families Benefits > Families Summary.

Families Summary page example - early claim assessed, awaiting proof of birth

View Early Claim Information details. The Benefit Status and Benefit List fields display payments the customer has claimed. These can include FTB, PPL or DAP.

For example:

  • Early Claim pending proof of birth/ entry into care:
    • FTB/PND-POB
    • DAP/PND-POB or DAP/PND - POP
    • PPL/ASS - PCB

Claims that have been assessed but are awaiting a future start date or further action from the employer will have a claim status of:

  • PPL assessed pending post birth claims codes include:
    • PPL/ASS-EIR (PPL Assessed - Employer registration required)
    • PPL/ASS-EIA (PPL Assessed - Employer acceptance required)
    • PPL/ASS-ASD (PPL Assessed - Awaiting nominated start date)
    • DAP/ASS-ASD (DAP Assessed - Awaiting nominated start date)

For more information on post birth claim assessed status, see Step 9 in the PPL Claims table of Assessing Parental Leave Pay (PPL) claims.

Does the early claim have a status of DAP/PND or PPL/ASS?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Check circumstances + Read more …

PPL claims

Select Guided Procedures > Paid Parental Leave circumstances.

Paid Parental Leave circumstances page example - early claim

This provides a summary of the customer's PPL circumstances.

Claim details

  • PPL claim dates
  • Tax Rate
  • Employer Details
  • Work History
  • PPL income
  • Termination/Resignation
  • Primary Care
  • Return to Work

It identifies the expected date of birth and whether the customer will be the primary carer.

For PPL/ASS-PCB claims, advise the customer following the birth of their child that they may be able to use the Express Plus Centrelink App or go online to provide their child's proof of birth.

Note: due to PPL Pre-birth claim automation, where the primary claimant is the biological father or partner of the birth mother, claims finalised as part of automation may assess as PPL/ASS-PCB and then the claim may auto reject (REJ) or be made non effective (NEF) due to:

  • Additional circumstances not met (ACN)
  • Primary claimant has claimed (PAO)

Attempt to contact the claimant to determine if the claim has been incorrectly lodged (for example, customer meant to claim DAP instead of PPL).

Before calling, Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) may be used to advise the customer they will be receiving a phone call. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging. If the claim was incorrectly lodged, assist the claimant to withdraw the claim. See Withdrawal of claims.

If the primary claimant is the biological father or partner of birth mother, advise the claimant that they need to meet the exceptional circumstances criteria to be eligible for PPL, see Table 2, Step 1 Eligibility for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) as a primary claimant.

The biological father or partner of birth mother may be eligible for PPL as a secondary claimant if the primary claimant is transferring their PPL entitlements (full or partial transfer).

DAP claims

Advise the customer the claim is pending (PND) awaiting either proof of birth/entry into care or assessed (ASS) awaiting their nominated start date.

Select Guided Procedures > Dad and Partner Pay circumstances

Dad and Partner Pay circumstances page example – early claim

This provides a summary of:

  • Claim details
  • Claim dates
  • Tax rate
  • Work history
  • DAP income
  • Advice child not in care
  • Advice of work or paid leave

For information about what to do on lodgement of proof of birth or documentation to support a DAP claim, see Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims.

Early claims will be automatically issued with a reminder letter 21 days after the customer’s DAP period start date if proof of birth or proof the child came into care has not been provided. The claim will be made not effective (NEF) 56 days after the DAP period nominated start date if the required information is not provided.

Family assistance

Families Summary page example - early claim assessed, pending proof of birth

All relevant information for FTB early claims will display in Early Claim Information on the Families Summary page. Information will also display in the new claim DOC on the Activity List (AL) page. Procedure ends here.

Does the customer wish to report a change in circumstances for PPL or DAP?

  • Yes, see Customer notifies a change of circumstances for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) or Change in circumstances for Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • No, procedure ends here

4

Has the early claim been rejected or made not effective? + Read more ...

This is indicated by a benefit status of REJ (rejected) or NEF (not effective) on the Families Summary page. For example:

  • FTB/WDR-POB (FTB Withdrawn/Voluntary Surrender)
  • PPL/REJ-CLR (PPL Rejected - Withdrawn/Voluntary Surrender)
  • DAP/REJ-CLR (DAP Rejected - Withdrawn/Voluntary

For a full list of codes, see:

  • Cancellation, rejection, not effective, assessed and current zero rate codes for Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
  • Rejection and not effective codes for Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • Cancellation, rejection and Current Zero Rate codes for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
  • Families Summary page example - early claim withdrawn

Claims lodged outside of allowable early claim period

If the claim was lodged outside the allowable early claim period, see:

  • Not effective claim for PPL
  • Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • Not effective claims or withdrawal of claim for Family Tax Benefit or Stillborn Baby Payment
