Conduct interview with assuree + Read more ...

Contact the assuree to conduct a phone interview.

Has the assuree advised that contacting the assurer/s will endanger them?

  • Yes, refer the assuree to a social worker. Go to Step 3

    • Discuss why they are no longer receiving support by the assurer/s. Advise that support could be in the form of money, accommodation, food or any other assistance
    • Advise if they are being paid an AoS recoverable payment, the assurer/s will have an AoS debt raised
    • Offer to invite assurer/s to conversation, go to Step 4

Discussing the assurer's obligations + Read more ...

6

Unable to contact or assurer/s decline discussion + Read more ...

If the Service Officer is unable to contact the assurer, or joint assurers then issue an Assurance of Support MOD AR.

The assurer/s has 14 days to respond to the request from the date of receipt of the notice.

  • On the assurers record, issue the MOD AR:
    • Customer First>Workspace>Mail Forms>Search Forms>Claim>MOD AR
  • DOC the assuree record and hold the claim for 21 days for the return of the MOD AR

If the 21 days expires and the MOD AR is:

  • returned or an interview has been arranged. Process the claim. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • not returned and the assurer/s has not contacted, process the claim. DOC that the assurer/s is not willing to provide support due to failure to respond. See Step 1 in Table 2

Conducting assurer/s interview to determine if assuree is granted payment

Table 2

Step

Action

1

At assurer/s interview (either by phone or in person) + Read more ...

Make sure that the assurer/s has completed the Assurance of Support (MOD AR) form. If not completed, assist the assurer's to complete the MOD AR

  • Tell the assurer/s that any AoS recoverable payment paid to the assuree will become a debt. This debt is legally required to be paid in full to the Australian Government. It should also be made clear that approximately every 12 months Services Australia will recover AoS debts from the term deposit under the bank guarantee they lodged. If there is no AoS bank guarantee and term deposit, or this has been depleted, the assurer/s are required to repay any outstanding debt amount. For arrangements with joint-assurers, the debt will not be split between each assurer
  • Tell the assurer/s that if the assuree is granted a recoverable payment, both assurer/s and assuree are required to submit a review form every 3 months. See Resources for AoS review forms
  • If more than one assurer, an interview must be completed with each assurer

2

Providing adequate support + Read more ...

Ask the assurer/s if they are willing and able to provide adequate support to the assuree.

Note: even if the assurer/s states that they are willing to support the assuree, they may not be able to provide adequate support. Examine the circumstances thoroughly.

Is the assurer/s willing and able to provide adequate support to the assuree?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Record outcome of the interview + Read more ...

Record a DOC advising outcome of AoS interview and details of information provided to assurer/s and assuree about implications of granting an AoS recoverable payment to the assuree.

To complete claim processing see Step 1 in Table 3

4

Is the assuree refusing the assurer's support? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, reject the claim. Procedure ends here

5

Reasonable reason to refuse support + Read more ...

Some instances where it would be reasonable for the assuree to refuse support are where:

  • an assuree has not been provided with a room that they can access as required on a continuing or indefinite basis
  • the assuree cannot rely on a reasonable regular pattern of support, or
  • a social worker interview may be required to investigate allegations of abuse

If the Service Officer is unsure, clearly DOC the record with information on the support the assurer/s indicate they provide and discuss with your SSO before making a decision. See Resources page for more scenarios.

Is it reasonable for the assuree to refuse support?

  • Yes, to complete claim processing see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, reject the claim. Procedure ends here.

Processing the claim when assuree can be granted payment

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Eligibility for payment + Read more ...

A customer can be granted a payment providing other qualification and payability conditions are met. If the assuree is claiming an AoS recoverable payment, the Service Officer must determine if the assurer/s is unable to provide support for the assuree. AoS interviews still need to be conducted with both the assuree and the assurer/s.

Note: for arrangements where there are joint-assurers, interviews must be conducted with all assurers. Do not delay the assuree's claim if contact with an assurer/s cannot be made.

Record information about the AoS interviews and decision on a DOC.

  • For a claim for an AoS recoverable payment, go to Step 2
  • For a claim for a non-recoverable payment, go to Step 4

2

Processing a claim for an AoS recoverable payment + Read more ...

Automatically rejecting AoS in Process Direct

  • Select Assess
  • The Entitlements (ELD) screen displays Status REJ, Reason AOS-AOS IN FORCE FOR CUSTOMER
  • Do not finalise claim. Go to Step 3

3

Update the AOCL screen + Read more ...

To update the AOCL screen for:

  • Youth Allowance (YA) and Austudy (student or apprentice) and Youth Allowance (job seeker) claims:
    • ensure all claim coding (apart from AOCL) is complete in Process Direct
    • refer the claim to ROXY, advising within the referral that AOCL coding is required
    • DOC the record
    • finalise the claim in Customer First
    • navigate to the Status screen in Process Direct and set the claim status to Complete
      Note: as student and apprentice claims and Youth Allowance (job seeker) are not accessible in Customer Record / Customer First, these claims will be unlocked for Income Security Integrated System (ISIS) processing.
  • all other claims:
    • ensure all claim coding (apart from AOCL) is complete in Process Direct
    • in Customer First, select the NCL activity from the Activity List (AL) screen
    • navigate to the AOCL screen and populate all relevant fields
    • finalise the claim in Customer First
    • navigate to the Status screen in Process Direct and set the claim status to Complete

Reviews are required every 3 months to reassess the assurer/s and assuree's circumstances. See Assurance of Support (AoS) Reviews to action a review.

Go to Step 5.

4

Processing a claim for a non-recoverable payment + Read more ...

If the assuree is claiming a non recoverable payment (that is a payment not listed as an AoS recovered payment), process as normal as no debt will occur to the assurer/s.

If the status is AOE/ACC and a start date has not been recorded, submit a request via Fast Note use Generic > Supervisor Approved Claim Escalation > Assurance of Support > AoS Immediate processing to request to the AoS Team to update the AoS start date.

5

Considerations when granting new claim - Letter to assurer/s and assuree + Read more ...

When an AoS recoverable payment is granted on the AoS record, an auto letter will be sent to advise the assurer/s and assuree about the grant and their AoS liability.

Note: if the social worker has recommended that contact with the assurer/s be delayed, make sure the National AoS Team has inhibited the automatic advice.

6

At end of AoS period + Read more ...

When the AoS period expires the National AoS Team follows up any debt to be raised and contacts the assurer/s about the AoS bank guarantee and term deposit.

Processing the claim when assuree is to be rejected a payment

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Not eligible for payment + Read more ...

The customer's payment must be rejected where the assurer/s is willing and able to provide support and there is insufficient reason why the assuree should not accept the support being provided. The Service Officer must determine that the assurer/s is able to provide support for the assuree and the assuree does not have a valid reason for not accepting this support. The AoS interviews still need to be conducted with both the assuree and the assurer/s.

Note: for arrangements where they are joint assurers, interviews must be conducted with all assurers. However, the assuree's claim should not be delayed if contact with an assurer cannot be made.

Record information about the AoS interviews and decision on a DOC.

 

2

Streamline Rejection for an AoS recoverable payment in Process Direct + Read more ...

An AoS start date must be coded on the Assuree Details (AOAE) screen on the assuree's record before the NCL can be coded.

To automatically reject AoS in Process Direct:

  • Select Assess
  • The Entitlements (ELD) screen displays REJ, Reason AOS-AOS IN FORCE FOR CUSTOMER
  • Do not finalise claim. Go to Step 3

Consideration when rejecting new claim - letter to assurer/s and assuree + Read more ...
