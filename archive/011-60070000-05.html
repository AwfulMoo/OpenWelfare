FAQ from health professionals and customers

Item

Question and Answer

How do I register a PRODA account?

I can send you some educational material that steps you through the process. I can send a detailed User Guide and Quick Reference Guide for associations, which will assist you.

I can send you some educational material that steps you through the process. I can send a detailed User Guide and a Quick Reference Guides for associations, which will assist you.

Service Officer - see the Registering as an organisation table for step-by-step instructions on how to register an organisation account in PRODA:

3

How can I submit a claim through Health Professional Online Services (HPOS)?

You need to have an individual PRODA account and have registered the association into PRODA before you can submit a claim through HPOS.

I can send you some educational material that steps you through the process. I can send a detailed User Guide for ostomy associations and some Quick Reference Guides for ostomy associations, which will assist you.

  • Log in to your individual PRODA account
  • Select My Programs
  • Select the Stoma Appliance Scheme and Paraplegic & Quadriplegic Program tile
  • Select Browse to choose claim file
  • Click Submit to upload file
  • Claim file is instantly validated before it is passed through to the system for full automated assessment
  • Claim paperwork must be submitted to Services Australia for associations using software version 1.0. Once received, the claim will be processed and paid within 15 days.
  • No claim paperwork is required to be submitted for associations using software version 2.0. Claim is processed and paid within 7 days.

4

How can I order ostomy stationery?

You need to have an individual PRODA account and have registered the association into PRODA before you can order ostomy stationery through HPOS.

I can send you some educational material that steps you through the process. I can send a detailed User Guide for ostomy associations and some Quick Reference Guides for ostomy associations, which will assist you.

  • Log in to your individual PRODA account
  • Select My Programs
  • Select the PBS Stationery Online Portal tile
  • Select your association and confirm the registered delivery details
  • Select the ostomy products and quantity required
  • Select Checkout
  • Confirm your order
  • Order is processed and dispatched. Tracking information is updated in PRODA
  • Order is received

5

I am unable to upload my file via Health Professional Online Services (HPOS)

You will need to review and correct the file. When submitting claim files via HPOS, the file is instantly validated to ensure all aspects of the file formats are included. Contact your software provider to assist.

6

I’m having trouble linking my organisation to HPOS or I keep receiving an error message when I try to link my organisation to HPOS

Service Officer - Escalate calls about Provider Digital Access (PRODA) / Health Professional Online Services (HPOS) organisation linking issues or complaints and feedback to the Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) - Helpdesk.

7

Why am I receiving three payment statements instead of one?

Each payment statement has a specific purpose:

  • Payment Advice – provides a summary of the claim payments you can expect to receive in your nominated bank account and allows for a quick review to check the total reimbursement is within the expected amount of your claim
  • Recipient Created Tax Invoice – provides GST applicable information to assist with your financial reporting and reconciling payments
  • Reconciliation Statement – assists with reconciling payments. Items are listed individually and detail the supplies paid, rejected, or adjusted by Services Australia

8

Why do I have multiple PBS Payment ID’s on my statement? I don’t understand what they mean?

All PBS Payment ID’s will correspond on each payment statement. If you look at page 2 of the Reconciliation Statement, this provides a breakdown of the items paid, rejected or adjusted by Services Australia.

The first PBS Payment ID noted on the statement represents the automated claim assessment outcomes of the electronic claim load and lists serial numbers, item codes, amount paid and any associated reason codes for the item.

Any subsequent PBS Payment ID’s represent adjustments made following claim assessment review by us. The relevant reason codes assigned to the claim/s and their explanation are noted on page 3 of the statement.

Service Officer - if there is a particular item to be discussed, go to next question.

9

Why has this item been rejected?

The rejection codes and explanation are noted on page 3 of the Reconciliation Statement.

Service Officer - retrieve the item record in the system through Query Ostomy Item screen. Key in the association’s approval ID, relevant claim period number and other supporting information such as patient identifier or serial number.

Discuss the concerns regarding the error. Use the Stoma and ParaQuad Reason Codes and fix instructions as guide to assist the enquiry. Advise the association of the correct claiming requirements and to resubmit the item in their next claim if necessary.

Note: if looking at the payment lines for the item, the payment amount noted on the PBS Claims Processing System does not include GST.

10

Why is there a W or warning reason code on my statement?

Warning codes do not affect payment.

All reason codes provide valuable feedback to reconcile claims. They also help association’s to improve payment integrity and capture useful information for managing the Scheme.

11

I’m a customer or calling on behalf of a customer needing stoma appliances/products. Can I order products through Services Australia?

You need to become a member of an approved Stoma Association. You and your referring stomal therapy nurse or medical practitioner will need to complete a Stoma Appliance Scheme application form (PB049) (located on the Stoma Appliance Scheme page of the Services Australia website) and submit to your nominated Stoma Association. Your Stoma Association will register you and provide you with the required products.

There are currently 21 associations across Australia. You can find a list of the approved associations on the Department of Health and Aged Care website or Australian Council of Stoma Associations.

Further information about the Scheme, refer to the Stoma Appliance Scheme webpage on the Services Australia website.

12

What are the products available under the program?

Your stomal therapy nurse or medical practitioner will suggest the suitable products for your condition. The Department of Health and Aged Care approves the products available for the Scheme.

Please see Services Australia's Stoma Appliance Scheme webpage for more information including a link to the SAS schedule.

Stoma Appliance Scheme or ParaQuad program forms

Table 2: This table describes the different forms used for the Stoma Appliance Scheme or ParaQuad program. These forms are located on the Stoma Appliance Scheme page of the Services Australia website.

Form number

Form name

Form used for

PB049

Stoma Appliance Scheme application form

SAS:

This form is used to register new members to the SAS and is also used to register new ParaQuad members.

The applicant (or their authorised representative), their referring medical practitioner or stomal therapy nurse must complete and sign the form.

Once completed the applicant sends to their nominated stoma association for registration and supply of products.

Associations using software version 1.0 must forward the completed applications forms to Services Australia as part of their monthly claim.

Associations using software version 2.0 need to retain the form under normal record keeping practices and audit purposes.

ParaQuad:

PB049 form can also be used to register new ParaQuad members. The same form completion requirements and submission processes applies for ParaQuad associations using software versions 1.0 or 2.0.

PB050

Stoma Appliance Scheme application for additional supplies for clinical and other reasons form

This form is used to obtain additional supplies for SAS members if the monthly allocation does not meet their need. The form can be used for all additional supply types including: clinical, holiday, remote and Norfolk Island. There are different sections on the form, which must be completed according to the type of additional supply required.

The member (or their authorised representative), stomal therapy nurse (or medical practitioner) must complete and sign the form before it is forwarded to the nominated stoma association. If the supply required is more than 4 times over the scheduled quantity then approval must be obtained from the Department of Health and Aged Care. The formal approval must also be provided to the stoma association.

The stoma association need to complete Part 4 of the form.

Associations using software version 1.0 must forward the completed form to Services Australia as part of their monthly claim.

Associations using software version 2.0 need to retain the form under normal record keeping practices and audit purposes.
