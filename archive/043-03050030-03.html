This page contains information on the employment status of a customer who advises they are in receipt of commission or contract income. They may be employed, self-employed or unemployed, and it is sometimes not easy to determine if the income received is either commission or contract income.

Customers already in receipt of an income support payment + Read more ...

For customers currently in receipt of payment who advise they are starting or have started employment on a contract basis or are now receiving commission income, determine if the customer is self-employed or an employee. Ask the customer if they consider themselves self-employed or an employee.

If it is unclear, use the Decision Support Tool.

  • characteristics of self-employment, or
  • characteristics of an employee

If the customer is a minister of religion, they are not self-employed and cannot claim business deductions from their income. However, any reimbursement of expenses, for example books, phone and petrol, is disregarded when calculating income. See Assessing income and assets for ministers of religion.

If unable to determine whether the customer is an employee or self-employed, ask the customer to supply documentation for use in determining the characteristics of the arrangement.

2

For customers who are considered to be self-employed + Read more ...

If the customer's contract for services is part of their business, treat the contract income as business income.

If the customer's contract for services is a one-off occurrence and income is received as a lump sum, the Net contract income is treated as a remunerative lump sum and held as income for up to 52 weeks from the date the lump sum is paid. The lump sum is assessed as income for a period into the future equal to the length of time for which the work was done.

If the customer receives commission income, the net commissions are treated as business income.

Commission or contract payments received by self-employed customers may be reduced by the costs of obtaining the income as for businesses. To assess allowable deductions, see Business deductions.

For commission workers considered to be self-employed, go to Step 7.

For contract workers considered to be self-employed, go to Step 8.

3

For customers considered to be employees + Read more ...

If contract payments are received by employees:

  • periodically, treat the contract income as employment income
  • as a lump sum, treat the contract income as a remunerative lump sum that can be held as income for up to 52 weeks

If commission income is received by employees:

  • periodically, as a wage or retainer, treat the commission income as employment income
  • as a lump sum, it is coded as employment income and assessed from the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) of the period in which it is paid for the period it represents, up to a maximum of 52 weeks. For example, commission income paid for work done over April, May and June (1 April to 30 June) is assessed for 91 days, frequency would be LOP

The table on the Resources page explains how to assess commission income for employees after 7 December 2020.

No deductions can be made for expenses.

Note: when an employee, such as a taxi driver, has takings including Goods and Services Tax (GST), the amount of GST is not considered income if the amount is remitted to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO). This is similar to the treatment of business income where GST is forwarded to the ATO.

4

Code the assessed income + Read more ...

If the customer receives more than one commission or contract payment in the year from the same employer, and where these payments are assessed as remunerative lump sums on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen, each paid income event can be coded under the same employer.

For customers regarded as:

  • employees who are commission workers, go to Step 5
  • employees who are contract workers, go to Step 6
  • commission workers who are self-employed, go to Step 7
  • contract workers who are self-employed, go to Step 8

5

Customers who are commission workers and regarded as employees + Read more ...

For customers who are commission workers and regarded as employees, use the Earnings and Reporting workflow to add new, edit, or update income details.

If the employee is paid an advance of a future commission, a review of their contract should determine if the advance is a repayable loan - no income is assessed if it is a loan.

If the Earnings and Reporting workflow is not available, code manually on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen.

If employer details are already recorded, record multiple instances of new commission income against the same employer.

If employer details are not already recorded, add new employer and code the following fields:

  • Type: key 'SAC' (sales commission)
  • Description: key the source of the commission (free text)

The following fields must also be recorded for each new instance of commission:

  • Event Date: key the date the commission income was paid (more than one event date can be coded)
  • Amount $: key the amount of commission income paid
  • Freq: if the income paid is for a period covering a fortnight or less, the frequency will be IOP and the customer will be a statement reporter. If the period is more than the duration of their reporting period:
    • use the frequency code of LOP, and
    • code the start date and end dates of the period the commission income is paid for
    • if the period is greater than 52 weeks or unknown (this may require Service Officer speaking with the customer to determine the correct period), code with frequency of LOP with start and end dates. For periods of more than 52 weeks, the system will automatically cease assessment of the income after 52 weeks
  • Hrs f/n: key if hours are known. If unknown, for payments which have a mandatory requirement to code this field, code one hour. For all other payments, leave blank

Record a DOC on the customer's record.

Procedure ends here.

6

Customers who are contract workers and regarded as employees + Read more ...

For customers who are contract workers and regarded as employees, use the Earnings and Reporting workflow to add new, edit or update income details.

Check to ensure any employment income coded previously on EAN using an ongoing assessment code, for example, MTH, ANN. etc. have been removed at the end of their assessment period.

If the Earnings and Reporting workflow is not available, add new employer, code the following fields on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen:

  • Type: key EAR - Earnings from employment
  • Employer Name/Description: key the source of the income
  • Event Date: key the date the income was paid (more than one event date can be coded)
  • Amount $: key the amount of income assessed
  • Freq: key the income paid is for a period covering a fortnight or less, the frequency will be IOP. If the period is more than the duration of their reporting period: use the frequency code of LOP, and code the start date and end dates of the period for which the commission income is paid
  • If the customer is assessed as an employee and eligible for Working Credit, apply the Working Credit rules to the total ordinary income before applying the Income Test
  • If the customer is a pensioner, over Age Pension age and eligible for the Work Bonus, the Work Bonus will automatically be applied to their employment income

Record a DOC on the customer's record.

Procedure ends here.

7

Customers who are commission workers or receive royalties and regarded as self-employed + Read more ...

The date of event (DOV) is the date a change of circumstance or notifiable event actually occurred.

The Other Income Summary (OINS) screen will only assess a single amount of income from each particular employer or other income source. Each new date and amount recorded overrides the previously recorded amount. As lump sums can be apportioned up to 52 weeks, each time an individual lump sum is received, it must be added as a new employer with a unique Employer Name/Description to remain on OINS for the full relevant period.

Check to ensure any old income coded previously on OIN using an ongoing assessment code, for example, MTH, ANN, etc. have been removed at the end of the assessment period.

Add new other income. Code the following fields on the OIN screen:

  • Type: key 'ROC' (Royalty or Commission)
  • Description: key the source of the commission business or royalty income - free text. If the customer receives lump sums from a number of contracts with the same company within a 12 month period, a unique Description needs to be used for each instance of contract income. For example, code as Porter's Homes 12/09/16, Porter's Homes 26/09/16, Porter's Homes 10/10/16, etc., using the date of event of the payment
  • Event Date: key the date the commission was paid
  • Amount $: key the net commission/royalty paid (gross amount received less allowable expenses incurred)
  • Freq: key 'ANN'

Go to Step 9.

8

Customers who are contract workers and regarded as self-employed + Read more ...

The date of event (DOV) is the date a change of circumstance or notifiable event actually occurred.

The Other Income Summary (OINS) screen will only assess a single amount of income from each particular employer or other income source. Each new date and amount recorded overrides the previously recorded amount. As lump sums can be apportioned up to 52 weeks, each time an individual lump sum is received, it must be added as a new employer with a unique Employer Name/Description to remain on OINS for the full relevant period.

Code the details in either:

  • Process Direct:
    • Use Customer Summary to run the Self-Employment workflow to add or update details. Select the Self-employment workflow from the Task Selector (START)
  • Customer Record:
    • Use the Self Employment script to update income and assets

The workflow or script will not update personal financial assets. See Income and expenses of a business.

If script is not available and:

  • the contract for services is part of the customer's normal business, treat income as business income. Net income will be coded on the Business Details (BUS) screen. See Income and expenses of a business. Procedure ends here
  • income is a one off occurrence and the income is received as a lump sum, treat the net amount as income. Add new other income, coding the following fields on the OIN screen:
    • Type: key 'OTH' (other income)
    • Description: key the source of the income. (If the customer receives lump sums from a number of contracts with the same company within a 12 month period, a unique Description needs to be used for each instance of contract income for example code as George's Services 12/09/16, George's Services 2, George's Services 3, etc. using the date of event of the payment)
    • Event Date: key the date the income was entitled to be received
    • Amount $: convert an annual rate of income for the net lump sum amount (income less allowable expenses) of the contractor income. For example if the net income was $3,000 for 3 months of work, an amount of $12,000 would be recorded
    • Freq: key 'ANN'
  • go to Step 9

9

Set a review to code off income - only for income recorded on OINS + Read more ...

If commission is to be held for a lesser period, a review needs to be coded to remove the commission income from the customer's record.

After coding the commission or contract details on the OIN screen, code a manual review to remove that income assessment. For example if the commission was received on 1 May 2020 and the commission is to be assessed for 6 weeks, the review should be set for 12 June 2020 to zero off the income at the end of the 6 week period.

Check any income coded previously using an ongoing assessment code, for example MTH, ANN, etc. on OIN has been reduced to $0 from the end of its assessment period. To set a manual review, see How to code and action a manual review.

Record a Note or DOC.

If the customer or partner receives Family Tax Benefit (FTB) run the Family Income and Choices workflow to record the customer's new estimate, if required, after completing the income update.

For Child Care Subsidy (CCS) customers, see Changes in circumstances and date of effect for Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS).
