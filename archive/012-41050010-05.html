Video consultations for Telehealth

Types of video consultations

This table describes the types of Telehealth video consultations.

Type

Description

Additional information

Unsupported

Only the patient and specialist or consultant are involved in the video consultation.

Patients may be accompanied by a carer, family member etc., however, this person is not acting in a medical capacity and this is considered an unsupported consultation.

Supported

Both the patient and a supporting health professional, who works for and on behalf of a general practitioner (GP) or non-vocationally recognised medical practitioner (non-VR MP), are present during the video consultation with the specialist or consultant.

Support services can be billed by the following health professionals who have a provider number in a Telehealth eligible area:

  • medical practitioners (including locums)
  • non-VR MP
  • practice nurses and Aboriginal health workers (providing services on behalf of a medical practitioner)
  • nurse practitioners
  • midwives
  • another specialist
  • When the supporting practitioner is also a specialist, then:
    • Restrictions on eligible Telehealth areas apply.
    • They can only bill a medical practitioner MBS Telehealth item number. Only 1 Telehealth specialist service is payable.
  • Only 1 MBS Telehealth item number is payable for the supporting practitioner service per patient episode even when 2 or more practitioners provided clinical support during the consultation.
  • A supporting practitioner does not need to be present the entire consultation, only as long as is clinically relevant.

See also: Claims for Telehealth video consultations in Medicare.

Health professionals conducting Telehealth consultations

Health professional’s requirements for conducting Telehealth consultations

This table describes the requirements for a health professional conducting Telehealth consultations.

An eligible…

is a…

Practice nurse

registered or enrolled nurse who is employed by, or whose services are otherwise retained by a medical practitioner or their practice. This includes a health service in relation to which a direction made under subsections 19(2) of the Health Insurance Act 1973 applies.

Aboriginal health worker

person who:

  • is employed by, or whose services are otherwise retained by, a medical practitioner, a general practice or a health service in relation to which a direction made under subsection 19(2) of the Health Insurance Act 1973 applies; and
  • holds a Certificate III in Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander Health Worker Primary Health Care (Clinical) or other appropriate qualification; or
  • is registered, and holds a current registration issued by a state or territory regulatory authority, as an Aboriginal health worker
