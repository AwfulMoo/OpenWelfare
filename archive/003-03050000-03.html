Customer does not have a record + Read more ...

Consider the following when trying to find a customer record:

  • For a person born in Australia (or migrated to Australia) after approximately
    • 1959, Historical Family Allowance (HFA) records may exist. For help, see:
      Gathering information from internal sources, or
      Online Search (OLS) facility. If they do not have access to OLS, Service Officers should speak with their team leader
    • 1988, links to a parent should exist, for example, family assistance, child care payments, maternity payments
  • The person could have been linked as a partner or nominee to a person who received a payment
  • Complete a SMS/Email (SMEM) search:
    • A SMEM search can help to identify an existing record or if a customer has multiple records:
    • Key SMEM in the Next field, then press [enter]
    • Key the customer’s mobile phone number in the SMS number field
    • Select Continue

  • If after completing a SMEM search, there are concerns about a customer’s identity as the search results indicate the mobile number is being used to create multiple records, see Identity Fraud

If extensive checks and searches return no existing customer record, staff must make sure they are satisfied, the caller is who they say they are and the information is genuine before adding a customer to the system.

Add the customer to the system if not able to find a customer record after an extensive search.

Create the record in the correct environment. This avoids the need to complete an Inter-environment change of address (ICoA).

Check the customer’s identity by completing an Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) update. If the customer has a passport and has travelled or lives outside Australia, the RSIMME update will help to complete PoRO.

7

Residence requirements + Read more ...

The system does not assess a customer’s residence status.

To avoid granting payments incorrectly, staff must make sure all residence data is coded. Manually check the customer meets the residence requirements for DRA or NZ DRA before running the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow.

  • Check if the customer is a legal permanent Australian resident or holds a visa that qualifies them for DRA. Visas, and in some circumstances Australian citizenship, will show on the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen. To check if a visa is a permanent visa, see Social Security Guide, Chapter 9.2, Visa subclasses and payment eligibility for visas issued after 01/9/1994
  • Customers may have 2 current visas showing on the RSLEG screen, for example a 444 visa and a 010 visa
  • Staff must check the Residence Results Explanation screen (RSREX) to check if the customer meets the residence criteria. See DRA additional eligibility and NZ DRA additional eligibility in Eligibility for DRA and NZ DRA
  • To do this:
    • in the Next field key RSREX and press [Enter]
    • RSREX screen will show which visa is current
  • Note: for NZ citizens (Visa Sub Class Code will be 444):
    • Staff must carefully check the New Zealand Residence Status:
      - Grandfathered (GRF) is a protected Special Category Visa (SCV) holder and should apply for DRA as an Australian resident
      - Transitional (TRA) is a protected SCV holder and should apply for DRA as an Australian resident
      - Not Entitled (NOT) is a non-protected SCV holder and should apply for NZ DRA
  • Travel documents, such as passports, will show on the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen
  • If a customer has travelled since 1 September 1994, movement information should show on the Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen
  • A customer may have a successful immigration link but no movements if they have a passport but have not travelled since 1 September 1994
  • Details on the RSLEG, RSIMME and RSIM screens may be refreshed by completing a RSIMME update
  • Make sure the Country of Residence (CRES) screen is coded with the country a customer is currently living in. See Assessing if a customer is an Australian resident
  • Asking the customer the last time they travelled overseas is a good way to confirm any passport details. This will help to complete PoRO
  • If there are no residence details on the RSLEG screen, see Recording legal residence status

Check the claim is valid and genuine + Read more ...

There may be warning signs that a call may be fraudulent. It may be an odd feeling or inconsistencies in what the customer says.

Some warning signs include if the customer:

  • has trouble answering basic Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO) questions
  • wants to change recently updated contact details such as address (for example, recently moving into an LGA for this emergency event)
    • check the Address History (ADH) screen and past DOCs for recent address updates
  • wants to change a payment destination
  • insists on an urgent payment
  • has an inconsistent story about how they have been adversely affected
  • has contact phone numbers that are currently linked to other customers

Are there concerns the customer’s claim or identity may not be valid or genuine?

  • Yes, go to Step 15
  • No, go to Step 17
