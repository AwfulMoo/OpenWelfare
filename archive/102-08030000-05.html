Commencing or returning to self-employment

This table below contains an example relating to a customer wanting to commence or return to self-employment.

Item

Example

1

Customer wants to become self employed

Francis is in receipt of JobSeeker Payment (JSP), is regarded as a computer whiz and has a good idea for setting up a computer consultancy business. Francis wants to know about the assistance that is available. An appointment should be booked with a Service Officer for Francis to discuss available options.

Things to discuss with Francis

  • Discuss with Francis the availability of the New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS) and the possibility that such a program can provide the skills required to set up a business
  • Consider the availability of short courses if Francis is interested in skills upgrading
  • Advise Francis to remain on JSP and operate a business at the same time Activity Test must still be satisfied
  • Explain to Francis the effect any business income will have on JSP
  • Explain working credits to Francis and how employment income is treated as Francis may have income other than from self-employment
  • Advise Francis to declare any income from the business once operational
  • Offer information on state based small business organisations
  • If Francis does not want to be on JSP any longer, advise that the payments will be cancelled

Actions to take for Francis

  • If Francis wants to participate in New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS)
  • If Francis' self-employment is not an approved activity, do the following:
    • Apply the mutual obligation requirements
    • Reclassify Francis's JSCI score. See Conducting a JSCI interview
    • Assess Francis' business income once operational
    • Explain to Francis how the income test works, see JobSeeker Payment and Youth Allowance job seeker Income Test
    • If Francis no longer wishes to remain on JSP, then cancel the payment
