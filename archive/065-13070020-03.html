Transferring a service in aged care

Table 3: This table describes the process for transferring a service from one approved provider to another approved provider.

Step

Action

1

Email advice received by Services Australia + Read more ...

The Department of Health notifies Services Australia of services that are transferring in the coming month.

Email notification is sent through to the Aged Care liaison inbox.

2

Checking the claim history + Read more ...

Service Officers must check the claim history of the aged care service that is to be transferred. This service must be up to date with their claims.

For example, if the transfer date is 1 June 2019, the aged care service is up to date with their claiming when the service has submitted the May 2019 claim.

Is the aged care service up to date with their claims?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, contact the aged care service and request to lodge the remaining claims for the transfer process to continue. Procedure ends here

3

Process last monthly claim + Read more ...

To process the last monthly claim in:

  • SPARC - see Process a claim form table in Residential Care monthly claim form
  • ACMPS - see Process claim summary table in Home Care monthly claim form

4

Suspend monthly advance + Read more ...

Suspend the future monthly advance for the service that is due to be transferred. This ensures the correct payment of subsidies to the approved provider who is operating the service.

To suspend the advance in:

SPARC - see Suspend an auto advance table in Advances in the System for the Payment of Aged Care Residential Care (SPARC)

ACMPS - see Suspend and unsuspend advances table in Advances in the Aged Care Management Payments System (ACMPS)

5

Check for updated provider details + Read more ...

The Department of Health sends updated approved provider details via the National Approved Provider System (NAPS).

This information flows through to SPARC and/or ACMPS.

Check SPARC and/or ACMPS daily to ensure that the new approved provider details have been uploaded in the data exchange.

Has the new provider details been updated in SPARC and/or ACMPS?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, Service Officers will need to ensure the escalation process is actioned appropriately prior to forwarding queries to the Aged Care Support positional mailbox. See Team Leader for the escalation process. Procedure ends here

6

Provider Statement and update bank account details + Read more ...

The new approved provider must:

  • complete the Aged Care Provider Statement, and
  • advise the bank account details

These details are received via PaNDA (email, post and fax).

To update the bank account details in:

  • SPARC - see Add bank account details in SPARC table in Bank account details for aged care services
  • ACMPS - see Maintain banking details in ACMPS table in Bank account details for aged care services

7

Lift suspension on advance + Read more ...

Lift the suspension (e.g. unsuspend the advance suspension) on the advance and pay any outstanding advances.

To unsuspend advances in:

  • SPARC - see Unsuspend an advance table in Advances in the System for the Payment of Aged Care Residential Care (SPARC)
  • ACMPS - see Suspend and unsuspend advances table in Advances in the Aged Care Management Payments System (ACMPS)

To pay any outstanding advances in:

  • SPARC - see Certification of an individual service advance table in Advances in the System for the Payment of Aged Care Residential Care (SPARC)
  • ACMPS - see Verify and approve an advance table in Advances in the Aged Care Management Payments System (ACMPS)

8

Reply to the Department of Health when processed + Read more ...

Reply the Department of Health when all actions have been processed.

Closing an aged care service in SPARC

Table 6: This table describes how Team Leaders and Subject Matter Experts close an aged care service in the System for the Payment of Aged Residential Care (SPARC).

Step

Action

1

Advice sent to Service Australia + Read more ...

The Department of Health notifies Services Australia of services that are closing in the coming month.

Email notification is sent through to the Aged Care liaison inbox.

2

Check the claim history + Read more ...

Service Officers must check the claiming history of the closing aged care service. This service must be up to date with their claims.

For example, if the closure date is 1 June 2019, the aged care service is up to date with their claiming when the service has submitted the May 2019 claim.

Is the aged care service up to date with their claims?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, contact the aged care service and request to lodge the remaining claims for the closure process to continue. Procedure ends here

3

Suspend next advance + Read more ...

Suspend the future monthly advance for the closing service. This ensures the correct payment of subsidies to the approved provider who is operating the service.

To suspend advances, see Suspend an auto advance table in Advances in the System for the Payment of Aged Care Residential Care (SPARC).

4

Process the last monthly claim + Read more ...

The last monthly claim for the closing service is to be processed and approved, i.e. certified.

To process the last monthly claim - see Process a claim form table in Residential Care monthly claim form

5

Check amounts held + Read more ...

Once the last claim has been processed and approved, calculate (if any) the held over amount. That is, any held over amount from the second last claim are added to the last claim’s held over amount. This is the final recoverable amount.

For example, 1 July 2019 is the closure date:

  • May - $10 000
  • June - $13 000
  • July - the final recoverable amount is $23 000

When the final recoverable amount is known, contact the aged care provider to confirm their preferred debt recovery method.

Is there a held over amount?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

The debt recovery method + Read more ...

The aged care provider can choose for the held over amount to be recovered:

  • against an ongoing service via a manual adjustment, or
  • as an invoice via the Debt Advice Notice (DAN)

To confirm the aged care provider’s preferred debt recovery method, see Aged Care debt recovery process.

7

Advise the Department of Health + Read more ...

Advise the Department of Health that the last claim has been certified and the service may be closed.

The Department of Health will update aged care services’ approved places to zero (0) via the National Provider System (NAPS). This information will flow through to SPARC.

View service details + Read more ...

Note: while Service Officers can use the Service Search screen to view residential services, Service Officers must not use this screen to edit residential care services data. Service Officers must continue to use SPARC until all functionality of the Service Search screen are fully functional for residential care purposes. If a Service Officer edits any residential services in ACMPS the changes will be lost overnight once SPARC is refreshed.

To view service details:

  • Log on to ACMPS
  • Select Service Search. The Search: Service screen will display
  • Key the relevant service details
    • Service ID
    • Care Type - select the drop down button to display the various options (Home Care, Residential Care, Short Term Restorative Care or Transition Care)
  • Select Search
  • The Service Details are displayed
  • Select the below tab to display the below tab's relevant information:
    • Identification Numbers
    • Indicators
    • Status Details
    • Service Places Details
    • Addresses
    • Address Types
    • Relationships
    • Contact Person
    • Key Personnel
    • Active Care Recipients
    • Communication History
    • Bank details
    • Advance Suspension Details
    • Sanction Details
    • Workforce Supplement Details
    • Provider Statement for Service
    • Notes
    • Notes Log
    • Change History

Note:

  • Service Places Details are only applicable to a flexible care service
  • A flexible care service can either be a Short Term Restorative Care or Transition Care service
