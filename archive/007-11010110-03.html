This page contains information about the non-lodger or late lodger process which applies if a customer's correct Family Tax Benefit (FTB) rate cannot be calculated because actual income details needed for the customer or their current partner have not provided by the end of the allowable period.

2

Potential non-lodgers and non-lodger debts for FTB + Read more ...

Potential non-lodgers

Potential non-lodger debts are calculated in early July following the end of the lodgement year. The potential non-lodger status will remain in place until the outstanding lodger confirms their income allowing for reconciliation or until a non-lodger debt is raised, whichever comes first.

A Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) qualification debt will be raised in July when the potential non-lodger debt is calculated if a customer had been paid NBU during the non-lodger financial year.

Note: if a customer is identified as a potential non-lodger in July, and a special circumstances income lodgement extension is granted before the non-lodger debt raising in late October, the non-lodger debt will not be raised until the special circumstances extension period has lapsed, which will extend the period of time the status is 'potential non-lodger'.

Potential non-lodgers after previously been reconciled

If the FAO Reconciliation & Claim Summary (FRCS) screen for the relevant financial year displays the most recent calculation as 'potential non-lodger' and the previous reconciliation result was a debt, the reconciliation debt may show as over-recovered on the Debt List (OPDL) screen.

If an FTB reconciliation debt has been over-recovered due to the customer being identified as a potential non-lodger, see Step 8 in the Checking for receipt of actual income details and actions table.

Non-lodger debt

A non-lodger debt is automatically raised for all FTB (including Newborn Supplement (NBS) or Stillborn Baby Payment (SBP)) paid for the relevant financial year if actual income details are not received by the last Monday of October following the end of the lodgement year.

The raising of FTB non-lodger debts for the 2019-20 financial year was deferred until 19 January 2022. This was due to the agency’s pause on debt raising for customers impacted by COVID-19 lockdowns.

Recovery action will start 28 days from the date the account payable letter is sent, unless an extension is applied due to the agency’s pause on debt recovery for customers impacted by COVID-19 lockdowns or natural disasters.

Sunset Clause

FTB instalments will immediately cancel under the Sunset Clause if the non-lodger debt is the third or subsequent non-lodger assessment.

Prohibition

Prohibition of FTB Instalment Payments (PIP) may apply if the customer or their partner has an outstanding non-lodger debt status.

A 75 day grace period starts on the date the relevant non-lodger debt is raised, before PIP commences.

PIP applies until all relevant non-lodger debts are resolved or repaid.

Income received

If actual income details are later received, reconciliation will occur and the reconciliation result will replace the non-lodger debt.

Late lodgers

If tax returns are required and are lodged after the end of the allowable period, the late lodger process applies. See Step 4 in Checking for receipt of actual income details and actions table.

3

Is the customer or their partner required to lodge a tax return? + Read more ...

Ensure they are aware of their obligations to check their circumstances with the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) and the consequences of an incorrect assessment.

Services Australia may use payroll information from the ATO to work out if customers and/or partners may be required to lodge a tax return. This only occurs if the employer reports to the ATO through Single Touch Payroll (STP).

The customer and/or partner will need to lodge a tax return or seek an ATO exemption from lodgement, if payroll information shows income:

  • has tax withheld
  • is over the income free threshold
  • includes reportable fringe benefits
  • includes net investment loss
  • includes reportable superannuation contributions
  • includes exempt foreign income
  • includes child support received or paid

Any attempt to advise non-lodgement of a tax return using the 'Advise non-lodgement of tax return' service, will result in a 'not accepted' outcome. The receipt page will provide the reason/s for this decision and direct the customer to lodge a tax return or seek an ATO exemption from lodgement. Customers are advised not to attempt another update as the advice on non-lodgement will likely remain as 'not accepted'.

If further advice is needed about whether they are required to lodge a tax return, the customer should be advised to visit the ATO website for more information.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Awaiting expected actual income details from ATO + Read more ...

Have expected tax returns for:

  • 2012-13 or a later financial year been lodged by the end of the lodgement year or by the special circumstances extension date?
  • 2011-12 or an earlier financial year been lodged with the ATO by the end of the extended lodgement year?
    • Yes, reconciliation of FTB will occur once taxable income details are transferred by the ATO. Reconciliation could result in a top-up payment, overpayment or no change. See Step 1 in Checking for receipt of actual income details and actions table
    • No, go to Step 7

5

Not required to lodge a tax return + Read more ...

To receive an FTB top-up including Part A and Part B supplements for the:

  • 2012-13 or a later year, the customer must advise by the end of the allowable period (by 30 June 2020 for 2018-19) that they and/or their partner are not required to lodge a tax return
  • 2011-12 or an earlier year, the customer had to advise by the end of the extended lodgement year (by 30 June 2014 for 2011-12) that they and/or their partner were not required to lodge a tax return

Is the customer advising they and/or their partner are not required to lodge a tax return after the allowable period?

Note: if they previously advised they were not required to lodge or were treated as a non-lodger and then lodge a tax return, they may be classed as a late lodger. See Re-reconciliation of Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, see Recording not required to lodge advice and previous year income. Procedure ends here

6

Not required to lodge a tax return and advises after allowable period + Read more ...

If the customer provides 'not required to lodge advice' after the allowable period they are considered a late notifier.

  • From the 2015-16 year, FTB supplements, including SIFS will not be included in the reconciliation calculation and top ups will not be payable

Note: from 2016-17 financial year the customer will not be considered a 'late notifier' for Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS) purposes, and SIFS will be payable if the customer is otherwise eligible.

  • For financial years 2012-13 to 2014-15, FTB supplements will be included in reconciliation calculations and may be used to reduce an overpayment within the reconciliation assessment. If the overall reconciliation result is a top-up (which may include supplements), this will be automatically inhibited. Date of effect limitation rules prevent payment.
  • For financial years 2011-12 and earlier, FTB supplements will be included in reconciliation calculations and may be used to reduce an overpayment within the reconciliation assessment. If the overall reconciliation result is a top-up (which may include supplements), the amount must be manually inhibited. Run the Family Assistance Office non lodgement exemption workflow to record not required to lodge advice and previous year income. Note: it may be necessary to run the workflow in both the customer and partner record if:
    • both were FTB current during any period of the financial year, or
    • if they were paid FTB as a blended family

Note: if the customer deferred some or all of their entitlement during the relevant financial year due to payment choices (MCFF) or prohibition (PIP), the late notifier determination will make the following calculations:

  • if a negative result is calculated between the legislative entitlement based on estimates and the actual entitlement, the amount deferred during the MCFF/PIP period (if eligible) will be offset or reduce the negative result and may result in a negative, positive or nil reconciliation outcome
  • if a positive result is calculated between the legislative entitlement based on estimates and the actual entitlement, the amount payable will be equal to the lesser of the:
    • legislative entitlement based on estimates minus the amount paid, or
    • actual entitlement minus the amount paid

If the activity is presenting the FAMIV error screen, do not complete the activity.

Note: only Manual Intervention Team (MIV) staff are to action activities that contain the FAMIV error screen. When Family Tax Benefit (FTB) / Child Care Benefit (CCB) reconciliation or lump sum claim activities have been stopped for manual intervention, Service Officers should not contact Technical Peer Support (TPS).

Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and select Do not finalise.

Place the activity on hold with a resubmit date of 7 days.

  • Do not complete the activity if the system wants to pay a top-up as well as the supplements. These activities require manual intervention and need to be transferred to 'MIV' for coding on the FAO Reconciliation Manual Adjustment (FRMA) screen
  • Once the workflow has recorded a DOC on the record, 'O'ut the activity to region code 'MIV'
  • Use Fast Note. Select Auto text > Families > Reconciliation > ACTDOC Manual Correction on FRMA include all the following details:
    • Financial year requiring correction
    • CRN and name of customer/partner not required to lodge
    • Service Reason (FTB/CCF/CCR)
    • Date and AMR of the reconciliation result that needs changing
  • If the customer is 'at risk' or experiencing hardship, the URGENT keyword should also be added
  • Annotate the Non Lodgement Exemption DOC with: 'Arrears to be suppressed as outside allowable timeframe as per Family Assistance Guide 6.4.3.20'. The References page contains links to the Guide
  • The MIV team will check the activity result, make any required updates and contact the original Service Officer via MS Teams or email requesting the activity be finalised

Procedure ends here.

7

Has a tax lodgement extension been granted by the ATO to allow lodgement after lodgement year? + Read more ...

To receive full FTB entitlements for a financial year, tax returns must be lodged by the end of the allowable period for an FTB top-up including supplements to be paid.

Has an extension been granted by the ATO to lodge a tax return after the end of the lodgement year?

If the ATO grants the customer or their partner a lodgement extension or deferral, the ‘new’ lodgement date is not reported to Services Australia.

  • Yes, the ATO decision will not be sufficient for Services Australia to automatically grant a special circumstances income lodgement extension. Customers will need to discuss their circumstances with the agency and request an extension of time to meet income confirmation requirements for family assistance purposes. Go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Special circumstances extension + Read more ...

From the 2012-13 financial year, a special circumstances income lodgement extension may be granted, extending the income lodgement date.

If an income lodgement extension is granted before the non-lodger debt is raised, debt processing will be delayed until after the extension date.

If the non-lodger debt has been raised before a special circumstances income lodgement extension is granted, repayments will continue to be withheld until actual income details have been received.

9

Tax return required + Read more ...

Advise the customer that tax returns must be lodged with the ATO by the end of the allowable period for the supplements to be included and for a full top-up to be paid, if entitled.

A non-lodger debt will be raised if actual income is not received by the last Monday of October following the end of the lodgement year.

The Sunset Clause or Prohibition of FTB Instalment Payments (PIP) may apply if the customer or their partner has an outstanding non-lodger debt status.

Timeframe for ATO link

The link with the ATO will remain open until any outstanding non-lodger debts have been finalised.

If actual income details are provided for a financial year where the link has ended, the customer will need to provide documentation. See information about 'link with ATO ended' in Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) process for family assistance customers.

Procedure ends here.

10

Previous partner and non-lodger debts + Read more ...

If a customer separates from their partner and the cause of an existing non-lodger debt is solely the result of the partner’s income being outstanding, the non-lodger debt will not be recoverable.

3

Check Tax File Number (TFN) and ATO match (MCI) details + Read more ...

Some customers may be selected for the non-lodger mailout or an non-lodger debt because they have no (or an incorrect) TFN recorded, preventing the transfer of ATO income details. These could be Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) mismatch cases.

Is the customer advising their or their partner's TFN?

  • Yes, record the TFN details
  • No, check if TFN details are recorded. If
    • they have previously provided TFN details and the Status on the customer and/or partner records is not ‘match’ on the MCI screen, resolve the issue preventing the match. See Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) mismatch cases
    • not already provided and the customer cannot advise details, explain TFN provisions. See Requesting a Tax File Number (TFN)
    • TFN details are not provided as requested, cancel FTB

Once TFN details have been checked or updated, check that an MCI link with the ATO exists. See Australian Taxation Office (ATO) information not received for family assistance reconciliation or lump sum claim. Procedure ends here.

4

Notice of Assessment received but reconciliation not finalised + Read more ...

When family assistance payments are reconciled, information about arrears or debts for the relevant financial year and outstanding FTB/CCS debt amounts for prior financial years are reported to the ATO.

The reconciliation result will remain parked until an ATO response transaction is received. This usually occurs within 28 days of reconciliation occurring.

See Australian Taxation Office (ATO) information not received for family assistance reconciliation or lump sum claim.

Procedure ends here.

5

Late lodgers + Read more ...

If a customer or their partner is required to lodge a tax return and lodges after the end of the allowable period:

  • The non-lodger debt will be set aside
  • FTB will be calculated using actual income details. If actual entitlement is lower than the amount they were paid based on their estimated income, a reconciliation debt will be raised
  • If they received less than their legislative rate of FTB due to a payment choice or prohibition applied during the year, they will be entitled to the lesser of their entitlement based on the estimate and their actual entitlement based on their actual adjusted taxable income (AATI). This is not a top-up
  • FTB lump sum claims for the 2012-13 or later financial years will be made not effective
  • FTB lump sum claims for the 2011-12 or earlier financial years will be paid the lower of the rate calculated based on estimated income and the rate based on their AATI
  • Return to work (FTB Part B quarantine) assistance will not be included
  • Generally Part A and Part B supplements cannot be paid. If they were paid during interim reconciliation, the total amount paid is compared with entitlement based on actual income, and the lesser amount paid. Depending on the circumstances, a debt may be raised for supplements paid
    • FTB supplements can be paid to late lodgers in some circumstances. For example if a customer re-partners after the lodgement year / extended lodgement year (for 2011-12 or earlier)
    • Cases can be referred to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk if it is unclear whether FTB supplements have been paid correctly
    • Requests to the MIV team to have the reconciliation result and the debt amended in these cases via a FAO Reconciliation Manual Adjustment (FRMA) screen adjustment must be referred with approval from the Families and Child Care Level 2 Policy Helpdesk
    • If actual entitlement is less than the amount paid during the year, any deferred amount will be used to offset the excess FTB paid
  • If it is identified that the supplements have been paid incorrectly (or an activity is attempting to pay them incorrectly), these are to be referred directly to the MIV team using Fast Note - select Auto text, use Families > Reconciliation > ACTDOC Manual Correction on FRMA action to raise them as a debt or suppress payment

6

Late lodger - date of tax lodgement disputed + Read more ...

If the customer says the tax return was lodged by the end of the allowable period but the FIPY screen shows it was lodged later, advise their details to family assistance Level 1 Policy Support, (e.g. Families Senior Support Officer (SSO). They will forward details to the ATO Liaison Processing Team via email. The Resources page contains a link to Office Locator for contact details for the ATO Liaison Team (TAP).

Note: providing financial statements to a tax agent or accountant prior to the end of the allowable period does not constitute lodgement of tax returns. It is the responsibility of the individual to ensure their tax agent/accountant lodges tax returns with the ATO within the allowed period.

If the ATO confirms that the tax return was lodged by 30 June, the ATO Liaison Processing Team will advise how to pay the top-up.

7

FTB Maximum Deferral Amount (FMDA) screen + Read more ...

Once the late lodger or late notifier FTB reconciliation completes, the FMDA screen displays the FTB entitlement the customer chose to defer until after the end of the relevant financial year and/or amounts unpaid due to Prohibition (PIP).

Instalment amounts unpaid due to More Choice for Families (MCFF) or Prohibition (PIP) may be paid in late lodger or late notifier reconciliation and can be used for debt offsetting. The maximum amount payable is the lower of the amount calculated based on the estimate and the amount calculated based on the actual income. This is not a top-up.

'S'elect a period to view the FTB Deferred Amounts Details (FDAD) screen.

8

FTB Late Lodger Top-up Summary (FLLTS) screen + Read more ...

Displays:

  • Total calculated top-up before any adjustments are made, that is the amount they would have been entitled to had they not been a late lodger or late notifier
  • More Choice for Families (MCFF) and PIP payable top-up: The top-up amount that relates to the amount deferred during the relevant financial year under a payment choice, prohibition and Mandatory Continuous Adjustment (MCA) (this amount may be payable in late lodger/notifier reconciliation, eligibility is subject to actual income assessment and less the amount deferred under MCA which is not payable, see #RECLLM)
  • SIFS payable top-up: relates to a Single Income Family Supplement (SIFS) amount payable
  • Inhibited amount: relates to the amount unpaid due to late lodgement or late notification

For information on inhibiting suppressed supplements and top-ups, see FTB reconciliation

9

Manual Intervention (MIV) activity - #RECLLM + Read more ...

From June 2014, if a late lodger/late notifier assessment is made for the customer or their partner for the 2011-12 and later financial years, manual intervention is required if the customer had Mandatory Continuous Adjustment (MCA) deductions in the relevant year.

The reconciliation activity will be automatically transferred to MIV for investigation.

Manual intervention is necessary as reconciliation calculations cannot distinguish between MCA deductions, More Choice for Families (MCFF) deductions and prohibition (PIP) deductions when determining if a late lodger is eligible for a payment.

MIV staff will be required to manually check the result to ensure MCA deductions are adjusted and not paid as a top-up.

10

Potential non-lodger requests over-recovered debt to be refunded + Read more ...

A potential non-lodger assessment can result in over-recovery of an existing FTB debt.

  • If the customer asks to be refunded the over-recovered amount, advise them: they have another outstanding debt, the over-recovered amount may be transferred to the debt without their consent
  • If they do not have any other outstanding debts, the over-recovered amount is held until either the non-lodger debt is raised, or reconciliation is finalised
