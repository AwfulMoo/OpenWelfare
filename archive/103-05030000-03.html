7

System and Other limitations + Read more ...

Lodgement/reporting date/EPED cannot be changed where the customer:

  • Has been linked to their partner in the same fortnight
    • Generally when customers become members of a couple their payments are aligned. For exceptions to this, see the  Resources  page
  • Has a Benefit Status code of 'Cancel next pay'
  • Is paid by a Group Payment Schedule
  • Is overseas long term or permanently, and paid by International Services (CIS)
    • Due to complexities in overseas payments, all customers are paid on the same date
  • Is calling on their EPED
  • Requests the change the same day they report earnings to stimulate payment
    • Requests made on EPED/Reporting day can be recorded on a Fast Note for staff to action
  • Suspended customers should be restored prior to any change to EPED date

Other limitations

Other limitations include:

  • The customer's local service centre may have limitations on EPED codes - check Office Locator/region code/office notes/EPED for any EPED codes to avoid
    • If the customer has requested a code that is listed, negotiate another day (if possible)
  • If the customer is going overseas and must report employment income prior to departure, the EPED must not be changed for this reason. For more details on how the customer should report income, see Recording and correcting employment income details

Will a system limitation prevent the changing of lodgement/reporting date/EPED?

  • Yes,
    • the customer has contacted on their EPED/Reporting day to request a change, See Step 1 in Table 2
    • for all other limitations, advise customer that the request will be rejected and they will need to recontact in the future if they still require the change and record the decision on a DOC. Consider whether the customer may be eligible for weekly payments Procedure ends here
  • No, system limitations do not exist, see Step 1 Table 2

3

Effect of changing the payday code + Read more ...

Explain the effect of changing the payday code. For example, the entitlement period may be for a reduced period (less than 14 days) and their first payment after the change of payday code may be reduced accordingly.

The first payment after the change of payday code will be paid as arrears. For customers with an outstanding debt, the system will use arrears to recover the debt, reducing the payment further.

Important:

Ensure the customer acknowledges and understands the changes and considers options such a requesting the change at the start of an entitlement period to reduce the initial arrears payment, or waiting until the debt has been repaid.

Remember, the EPED is not the customer’s payday. The customer’s EPED will either be 1 or 2 days prior to the payday, depending on the payment type.

If a person is a member of a couple and Person Permitted to Enquire is N:

  • If possible, speak to the partner before making the change. If this is not possible, the change can still be made.
  • Service Officers must advise the customer:
    • this will change their partner's delivery day also
    • their partner will be notified of the change in writing
    • if their partner enquires about the change, they will be informed of the reason and its effect
    • no other information can be provided to them about their partner's payments

4

Customer requests to change their EPED and contacted on their current EPED + Read more ...

Due to system limitations, the EPED cannot be updated on the EPED/Reporting day. The request can be recorded on a Fast Note where:

  • the Service Officer has thoroughly explained the effects on the customers’ payment, and
  • the customer understands and acknowledges the change

The Fast Note workflow can be accessed in Customer First via:

  • Workspace > Documents and Correspondence or
  • Type Fast Note in the Search field on the Workspace page

Use the Auto Text option:

  • Category 1: Generic
  • Category 2: Update
  • Template Name: CUS request EPED change on current EPED

Service Officers must:

  • ensure the customers requested EPED code is recorded in the DOC
  • Hold the Fast Note for action the following business day

Advise the customer that their request will be actioned the following business day.

Note: statement reporters will be sent an updated Reporting Statement with their new reporting dates included. Notification reporters will be able to see their new payday and the amended payment amount in their online account once the change has been applied to the system.

  • Coding change to EPED for other acceptable reasons, go to Step 5
  • Otherwise, procedure ends here

5

Customer still wishes to change their EPED + Read more ...

Navigate to the payment system relevant to customer’s payment (for example, NSS system for customers on Jobseeker Payment).

Use the Change Entitlement Period workflow to process the change in EPED. If the workflow is unavailable see Step 1 in Table 3.

  • Open the Change Entitlement Period workflow
  • Change Date of Effect to today's date. This will automatically update to the customer's entitlement period start date (date paid to +1)
  • Select the New Entitlement Period End Day code from the drop down box
    • Resources contains a link to the Centrelink Payments Calendar
  • The New Delivery Day will automatically default to the corresponding payday
  • Select the Delivery Day Change Reason:
    • 'CUS' for 'Customer Request', or
    • if the date has been changed to meet operational demand 'ORQ' for 'Office requirement' should be selected
  • If the customer is receiving family payments, double orphan pension (DOP) or Carer Allowance, do they want this payment to be paid on the same week as their income support payment or offset to the alternate week? Select 'Yes' to pay on alternate week or 'No' if FTB to be paid in the same week in the Offset Family Payment (if appropriate) See, Offsetting Family Tax Benefit, Double Orphan Pension and Carer Allowance for more information
  • Complete Source and DOR (Date of Receipt) fields
  • Press Continue to be taken to the Assessment Result (AR) screen and finalise the activity
  • Any immediate payment that will be issued to the customer will show on the AR screen
  • The change to the EPED may not show on the customer's record immediately. There may be a Future Activity created on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen for when this will occur
  • Manually record details on a DOC
  • If a system fault prevents the workflow from running correctly, the change of lodgement/reporting date/EPED can be completed manually, see Step 10 in Table 3

Note: when offsetting payments, an immediate payment of any entitlement accrued to the date of the change may be issued. This amount should not be paid as an urgent payment to the customer.

6

Provide information + Read more ...

Advise customer of the new EPED, interim payment details, next full payday and new lodgement/reporting date.

Note: if the customer is in receipt of weekly payments, advise the customer of their new interim payment, and Week 1 and Week 2 payments in the following full fortnight, using the weekly payment entitlement calculations.

  • for a customer granted weekly payments prior to 8 December 2012, or
  • for a customer granted weekly payments or transitioned to the new payment delivery model after 8 December 2012

If the customer has employment income to report for the period, make sure that they only include income paid in the reduced period. See Recording and correcting employment income details

7

Effect of EPEDC change on Income Management + Read more ...

If a customer's payments are being income managed, any regular Income Management expenses must be updated to align with the new EPEDC.

Are the customer's payments subject to Income Management?

  • Yes, and Service Officer is not trained in Income Management:
    • Transfer the customer to Income Management for assessment
    • Procedure ends here
  • Yes, and Service Officer is trained in Income Management:
    • check any regular expenses on the Expense Management Summary screen, and
    • update relevant expenses to align with the new EPEDC see Coding Income Management expenses
  • No, procedure ends here

Manual coding + Read more ...

The following manual coding is only to be used if the Change Entitlement Period workflow is not available.

Navigate to the payment system relevant to customer’s payment (for example, NSS system for customers on Jobseeker Payment).

Determine the new EPED: from the day that the customer wants to receive their payment, count back 1 or 2 days, depending on the payment's delivery time (for example, Age Pension is 2 day delivery, JobSeeker is 1 day delivery). Then refer to the Centrelink Payments Calendar to identify the payday code for that day (A, B, C, D, E, 2, 3, 4, 5 ,6). Resources contains a link to the Payments Calendar.

Where a Service Officer is allocated a work item, via Fast Note ‘CUS request EPED change on current EPED’, new requested EPED will be recorded in DOC.

Code the new payday code on the Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen:

  • In the relevant system, enter RAD in the NXT: field
  • Enter the payday code change in the Entitlement Period End Day Code: field
  • Code the Delivery Day Change Reason: field with the reason 'CUS' (Customer Request).

Manually record details on a DOC.
