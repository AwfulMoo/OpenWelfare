This page contains information on how to help a person complete a combined partner claim for Age Pension.

Select the relevant tab:

  • Self service tab explains how to help a person make an online claim
  • Assisted tab explains how to check a person's circumstances to ensure a new claim is required, complete an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) with them, and lodgement of claims and documentation

1

Check access to online account + Read more ...

An online claim will expire after 13 weeks if it has not been submitted. The customer must then submit a new claim. In some cases the claim can be assessed from the original start date, see Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM). If further assistance is required, for example, there has been a system error, refer to the Retirements Helpdesk

Check the customer and their partner are registered with an active Centrelink online account and subscribed to electronic messaging.

Note: Nominees cannot lodge an Age Pension combined partner online claim.

If they do not have an active account they must confirm their identity at their local service centre to register for a Centrelink online account.

Advise the customer how they and/or their partner can create a myGov account and link Centrelink services. This will also enable them to receive Centrelink mail online through the myGov Inbox.

Service centre staff can help customers with their online claim through self service terminals. Note: Service centre staff must remind customers to submit their claim once all required tasks are completed. Alternatively, if the customer is unable to access their online account, the Service Officer can scan and upload the documents for them.

Customers can access the Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website to check if Age Pension is an appropriate payment for them, and to start their claim.

Record the claim contact in a DOC and advise the customer to start and submit a claim as soon as possible to be paid from the earliest possible date. Advise the customer if they are having difficulty submitting the claim to contact the agency for assistance.

Update My profile + Read more ...

On the My profile page, information from the customer’s record is pre-populated. Customers need to select Update Section for any sections such as Address or Relationship details that require an update. Note: accommodation details are covered within the claim questions.

For each part of My profile updated, they need to select Save.

Claim progress + Read more ...

The Claim navigation page will display:

  • Status of the claim
  • Claim number
  • Advises the customer to submit their claim to be paid from the earliest possible date
  • Sections of the claim that need to be completed

The sections of the claim must be completed in order and the next section cannot be started until the previous section is complete.

The sections are accessed by selecting the Start button.

Each section contains modules with relevant questions. The modules are displayed as tabs at the top of each page. On each question page, there are options to select:

  • Back to go back to the previous question or module
  • Save to save the claim at the current stage
  • Next to progress forward with the module questions

Select Help to access help text about the questions on the current page. Help text will open in a separate window and can be printed.

When each module is completed and the end of the section reached, the Claim navigation page will display to select the next section.

As a section is completed, there is an option to select Edit to go back to a previously completed section to change the answers. To go directly to a module, select the relevant tab.

To return to the Claim navigation page after making changes, select the last module tab and select Next.

The claim does not need to be completed in one session. Select Save and Exit on the Claim navigation page at any time and the responses will be automatically saved, to continue with the claim later.

All compulsory questions must be answered before the claim can be submitted.

9

Age Pension combined partner claim option + Read more ...

If the customer is eligible, they will be presented with an option to claim 'just for myself' or 'for both myself and your Partner' as an Age Pension combined partner claim.

The option to submit a combined partner claim will only appear where the following criteria is met:

  • It is not a Nominee (Correspondence or Third Party Organisation) claiming on behalf of customer
  • Customer does not indicate that they are in crisis (domestic violence, humanitarian visa entrant, natural disaster and recent gaol release) in the claim
  • The partner already is linked in the system
  • When asked about the current relationship status, customer must not change their answer within the claim. And must not be single, married but unable to live together, de facto but unable to live together or in a registered relationship and unable to live together
  • Both customer and partner are subscribed to electronic messaging
  • Partner would be eligible to lodge an early claim for Age Pension
  • Partner is not already receiving an Income Support Payment
  • In the claim the customer must answer yes to the question 'Can you provide accurate information regarding your partner's circumstances to support your claim?'
  • Customer must not be applying as an Age Pension Blind claimant
  • Partner must not be receiving Disability Blind Pension or wanting to claim as an Age Pension Blind claimant
  • Neither customer nor partner are registered for Pension Bonus Scheme (PDB/REG)
  • Customer must not try to submit a claim more than 12 weeks after starting, as enough time for partner to complete the partner tasks needs to be allowed before the claim will automatically cancel online due to time lapse
  • Partner must have a payment destination account in their name either jointly or solely
  • Partner must not have draft Age Pension claim

The partner must complete the claim task within 3 days of being notified, otherwise the claim will revert back to an individual Age Pension claim for the customer only.

Has the criteria for completing a combined partner online claim been met?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, see Claiming Age Pension. Procedure ends here

Partner Task + Read more ...

The partner will receive a notification via either SMS or email to advise that the customer would like to submit a claim on behalf of both customer and partner.

The partner will need to:

  • Sign into myGov and access their linked Centrelink online account
  • Select Task for Age Pension combined partner claim on the home page
  • Give permission for their partner to claim on their behalf
  • Answer questions about their preferred payment destination and if they would like any tax deductions
  • Review the claim that the customer completed on the partner behalf (no changes are allowed to be made)
  • Accept and declare the information to be true and correct

Next steps + Read more ...

This page advises the customer and partner of any further information or tasks required for their claim to be submitted.

To submit their claim, the customer or partner must answer all mandatory questions and provide all their required documents, including identity documents if there is no Identity Confirmation Indicator (ICI) recorded. Exceptions apply for some vulnerable customers.

To be assessed from the earliest possible date, customer or their partner need to provide all required information and supporting documents and submit the claim as soon as possible, however only the customer will be able to submit the Age Pension combined claim. The date the claim has been submitted will generally determine the claim start date. Customers and their partner can access their saved claim for 13 weeks. After 13 weeks the claim will expire.

Documents and forms section includes:

  • A list of supplementary information and supporting documentation required from the customer
  • How many days they have to submit their claim
  • A link to download or print required forms
  • An Upload documents button to use the Upload documents service

Additional information section includes:

  • Links to further information and brochures such as 'Confirming your identity'
  • Information about subscription to online letters and electronic messaging

If the required tasks are not complete, e,g required documents are not uploaded, the claim cannot be submitted (some exceptions apply). Once the relevant tasks are completed, the status changes from required or supplementary to Done. If the task needs changing the customer can Edit it. Once all required tasks are completed the button will change to Submit. The customer can then submit the claim and will get a notification if they have successfully submitted it.

Customers will get an estimated completion date that the claim will be processed.

Customers can select:

  • Home to go back to their online account homepage
  • View claim history to see the history of the claim, including any documents or tasks they’ve completed
  • View claim answers to see the information they’ve provided

Customers can print their claim information now or any time later.

Customers can access their submitted online claim to return to the Next steps page at any time.

Once a claim has been submitted, the customer cannot update the online claim details. They will need to contact Services Australia if they want to provide information in relation to their claim.

Customers and partners can withdraw their online claim once submitted. If the customer or the partner chooses to withdraw their claim this will also withdraw their partner’s claim.

Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) + Read more ...

ACC may be used by Service Officers when a customer is unable or unwilling to claim online. ACC replicates the questions in the online claim. ACC must only be used when the customer is available to answer the questions in ACC. If a customer is unable or unwilling to claim online or via ACC, running the First Contact Concessions, Carers and Seniors (FCCCS) workflow will send out the correct forms. If both members of a couple want to claim payment, FCCCS should be run for both.

Correspondence nominees may be offered ACC when they want to apply for Age Pension on behalf of their principals. The option to complete a combined partner claim via ACC is only available if the couple is eligible to lodge a combined partner claim and where the nominee is appointed to act for both members of the couple. If they decline ACC, running FCCCS will send out the correct forms.

ACC will identify any documents required for the claim. The documents can be uploaded by a Service Officer or the customer.

If the required documents include a form, for example, Business Details form (MOD F), this can be printed by the customer from the Services Australia Website, or issued by a Service Officer.

Advise the customer the documents, including supporting documents required in a form, must be provided before the claim can be submitted.

For information about completing ACC with the customer, see Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) table.

  • If the claim is to be submitted under a claim submission exception, tell the customer they must meet the requirements within 14 days of submission or the claim may be rejected

On the Next Steps page in the Documents and forms section there is an Upload documents button.

If the customer or partner uploaded or provided documents before an online claim or ACC is started they would generally be scanned to the customer's or partner's record. Once the online claim or ACC is present the document displays as Required on the Next Steps page.

The customer or partner can either:

  • Re-upload the document from the Next Steps page
  • Request the Request & Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure be run to change the document status from Required to Provided
  • Note: where documents are provided before starting the claim, ensure they meet the document requirements.

To submit via ACC + Read more ...

To submit the claim, all mandatory questions must be answered, and all required documents must be provided, including verbal declaration or CDF signed by the customer (where applicable) and identity documents where there is no Identity Confirmation Indicator (ICI) recorded. Exceptions will be made for some vulnerable customers.

Once all Required tasks are completed, or to be submitted under a claim submission exception, the Save button will change to Submit so the claim can be submitted. The customer or partner can submit the claim once all mandatory documents have been uploaded.

The Submitted page will display:

  • Claim ID
  • Estimated Completion Date (ECD) which can be advised to the customer
  • A 'withdraw claim' option
  • Tabs that link to Notifications, Tasks & Review claim details information
