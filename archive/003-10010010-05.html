Examples of urgent payment requests

Item

Description

1

Funeral can be verified

The customer lives in Tennant Creek and is obligated to attend a funeral in Alice Springs, 4 hours away. The customer has their own car and somewhere to stay, but needs money for fuel.

The customer has already received 3 urgent payments recently to attend funerals for extended family members.

The customer can provide details of the deceased and information on the location, date and time of the funeral. The funeral is in Alice Springs on Wednesday of the next week.

In this case, the Indigenous Funeral Notices page on the intranet is used to confirm the funeral.

Key things to consider:

  • This may be the third or fourth funeral the customer needs to attend in a short period due to kinship and obligations
  • Depending on the customer’s cultural obligation, they may need to attend sorry business before the funeral date
  • Take care when requesting information. Mentioning the name of a deceased person may cause distress or offence in some Aboriginal communities
  • If the funeral details are not available via the Indigenous Funeral Notices page, see the Process page for how to verify the funeral
  • The customer may also want an urgent payment to travel to the funeral or contribute to funeral costs
  • Paying this urgent payment may leave the customer in financial hardship and they may be unable to meet their regular expenses at the end of the fortnight. The Service Officer should deny the urgent payment if making the payment will intensify the customer’s hardship

2

Funeral cannot be verified

The customer is staying in Adelaide but wants to attend a funeral in Ceduna.

The customer has provided the name of the deceased but no details of the funeral can be found on:

  • Obits
  • Ryerson Index
  • Indigenous Funeral Notices page

Contact has been made with the Indigenous Agent in Yalata. They do not have any details of a funeral for a person with the name supplied.

As the funeral cannot be verified, the request must be denied.

3

Customer cannot meet the costs or travel to the funeral in time

The customer has requested an urgent payment to attend the funeral of a cousin. On investigation, the customer advises they were raised by their aunt and the cousin was like a brother, so would fit the requirement to be immediate family.

The customer is living in Brisbane and the funeral is at 10am the next day in Broome. The customer is contacting at 11am on a Monday.

Customer has available funds of $150 for this entitlement period.

By using Rome2Rio:

  • Air travel from Brisbane to Broome would cost between $369 and $1,021. Flights leave each day at 6 am and arrive at 11:40 am, or 6:30 am and arrive at 3:25 pm. The customer would not arrive until after the funeral
  • By train and bus, travel would take 5 days
  • By car, travel would take over 46 hours and cost over $525

All options would:

  • cost more than the available funds
  • not get the customer to the funeral until after it has taken place

In this case, the Service Officer should deny the urgent payment as it is not possible for the customer to attend the funeral.
