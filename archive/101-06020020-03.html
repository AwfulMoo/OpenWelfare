HCC for FTB customers - Customer eligibility

This table describes the circumstances where a Health Care Card (HCC) for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) may, or may not, automatically issue to a customer.

Step

Action

1

Income support payment status + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving an eligible income support payment?

Note: customers receiving FTB, who have recently had their income support payment cancelled, must update their FTB estimate within 21 days from the date the income support payments stop. The customer's continued entitlement to an FTB HCC will be reassessed based on their current estimate.

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the customer is not entitled to an automatic income support payment HCC. Go to Step 2

2

FTB entitlement + Read more ...

Note: customers receiving FTB, who have recently had their income support payment cancelled, must update their FTB estimate within 21 days from the date the income support payments stop. Continued entitlement to an FTB HCC will be reassessed based on the customer's current estimate.

If a customer has care of a foster child, see Health Care Cards (HCC) for foster children.

Is the customer:

  • Entitled to receive the maximum legislative rate of FTB Part A (see Resources) via fortnightly instalments, irrespective of whether or not the customer opts to receive a lower amount of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A under More Choice for Families (MCFF) options, or
  • Eligible for FTB Part A at the maximum rate except that they only have care of a Regular Care Child?
    • Yes, go to Step 5
    • No, go to Step 3

3

FTB Part A reduced + Read more ...

Check the REXFTBA screen for any maintenance income reduction.

Is the customer's FTB Part A reduced from the maximum rate due to the Maintenance Income Test (MIT)?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Customer receiving a reduced rate of FTB + Read more ...

Check the AXFTB screen to view child eligibility details.

Is the customer's FTB Part A reduced from the maximum rate due to the failure of the Maintenance Action Test (MAT) for all children?

Note: if only some of a customer's children fail the MAT, the customer may continue to receive a HCC - for all children including the ones who have failed the MAT.

  • Yes, the customer’s FTB (A) rate will be reduced to base rate for all eligible FTB children for reason 'Base No Maintenance Act', and the customer’s existing HCC may be cancelled (check MCCS for any other eligible concession): Submit a Level 2 Policy Helpdesk Enquiry (see Resources) including the following details for a manual override to be coded:
    • CSAQuery: select dropdown 'Families, Chidcare & Parenting'
    • CSAEnquiry: select dropdown 'Concessions'
    • Service/Payment Type: select dropdown 'Card - Health Care Card (HCC)'
    • CRN: Insert relevant Customer Reference Number
    • Reference Check: Insert 'Health Care Card (HCC) 101-06020000'
    • Enquiry Description: Insert 'FTB HCC Manual override MAT'
    • Fill out all additional fields and click 'Save'
    • DOC the customer record to advise a referral for manual override has been requested
  • No, the FTB rate is reduced due to the income test for FTB Part A, the customer is not entitled to an automatic HCC. The customer can test their eligibility for a Low Income Health Care Card

5

Residential status + Read more ...

The customer is qualified for an automatic Health Care Card if they are residentially qualified and their (and their partners) verified residential details are coded on the Legal Residence Information (RSLEG) and Country of Residence (CRES) screens.

Check Concession Entitlement Summary (MCCS) screen for current entitlement start date. Card Issue Summary (CCIS) screen displays actual cards issued, not current entitlement.

6

Reissuing the card + Read more ...

Reissue concession card to the customer.

Customers can use the digital wallet in the Express Plus Centrelink app or the myGov app wallet. See the link on the Resources page
It allows customers to present their concession card using their smart device. It is at the discretion of the concession provider whether they accept the digital card so it is important that customers have their physical card with them.

7

Interim Confirmation of Concession Entitlement + Read more ...

Does the customer urgently require an Interim Confirmation of Concession Entitlement (SS055)?

  • Yes, see Confirming concession card entitlement and issuing interim vouchers
  • No, complete action for customer and record the outcome on a DOC
