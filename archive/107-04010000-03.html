Check the debt. See:

2

Customer contact + Read more ...

Does the customer circumstances meet one or more of the following criteria?

  • a single high value debt of more than $5,000
  • 3 debts with a value of $5,000 or more
  • 4 or more debts, or
  • if the customer has identified vulnerabilities including, but not limited to:
    • customers under 16 years of age
    • customers over 80 years of age
    • homelessness
    • mental illness
    • family and domestic violence, or
    • low literacy or numeracy skills

Yes, and there is at least one contact number recorded, go to Step 3

Yes, and:

  • the customer is in receipt of a payment, however has no contact numbers recorded, or
  • is not in receipt of a payment, but has been in receipt of a payment within the last 12 months, and
  • has no contact numbers listed, go to Step 4

Yes, and:

  • the customer is no longer in receipt of a payment, and
  • has not been in receipt of a payment within the last 12 months, and
  • has no current contact number recorded:
    • See Safeguarding customer privacy when raising and recovering debts to identify, confirm and update (if required) address details for the customer. Then go to Step 5

No, and:

  • the customer is in receipt of a payment or
  • the customer is not in receipt of a payment however has been in receipt of a payment within the last 12 months and
  • the address is not end dated on the system, go to Step 5

No, and:

  • the customer is not in receipt of a payment, and
  • has not been in receipt of a payment within the last 12 months, and/or
  • the customers address is end dated:
    • See Safeguarding customer privacy when raising and recovering debts to identify, confirm and update (if required) address details for the customer. Then go to Step 5

3

Contact the customer + Read more ...

Before initiating contact with a customer who meets the above criteria, send a pre-call SMS prior to every contact attempt if the customer has:

  • a telephone number on record, and
  • has subscribed to receive SMS messages via Electronic Messaging. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging

Make 3 genuine attempts over 2 days to contact the customer prior to finalising the activity.

Use Interpreter Services for all customers who require an interpreter. See Interpreter Services for more information about this service.

DOC the outcome of each contact attempt on the customer's record. For the first attempt use Fast Note. Select Auto Text use Debts > DMB Raising > Debt Investigation-Debt staff only. Annotate the Fast Note with further attempts.

Consider sending a Debt Raising – Unsuccessful Contact SMS if:

  • the customer is subscribed to receive SMS messages via Electronic Messaging, and
  • 3 genuine attempts to contact the customer have been unsuccessful

Go to Step 5.

4

Send a general request to contact letter + Read more ...

Send the customer a Q164 Request to contact letter. See Creating an Online Advice (OLA), including from within an existing activity.

Hold the debt activity(s) with a due date of 15 days from the date the request to contact letter is issued in line with current business processes for holding debt.

Debt and Compensation staff

Debt and Compensation staff will need to also run the Debt Investigation – Debt staff only Fast Note and attach it to the debt(s). In Customer First:

  • use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Debts > DMB Raising > Debt Investigation – Debt staff only
  • run the Fast Note, include all relevant details of the debt investigation and link/attach to the relevant debt

Note: where there are multiple debts, a separate Fast Note must be run and attached to each debt.

When the customer makes contact or the activity becomes due, go to Step 5.

5

Raise the debt + Read more ...

See:

  • Raising debts of Advance Payments
  • Paid Parental Leave (PPL) and Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • Raising Assurance of Support (AoS) debts
  • Child care debts
  • Debt arising from Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation
  • International Social Security Agreements
  • Specific cause of debt raising
  • Raising debts for students and Australian Apprentices
  • Raising ISP debts for customers granted PPL and DAP

Go to Step 6.

6

Check if recovery fee is required + Read more ...

Check if the debt is a working age payment. Customer contact may be needed to determine if the recovery fee should be applied. See Recovery Fee applied to debts resulting from incorrect declaration of income.

  • Yes, assess and apply recovery fee where appropriate. Go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Record recovery fee decision + Read more ...

Record the decision on a DOC.

8

Ensure the debt is recorded on DMIS + Read more ...

  • Action the undetermined debt if the debt is on the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS)
  • If a debt is recorded on the host system, but not yet on DMIS
    • Action the negative adjustment, then
    • Action the undetermined debt on DMIS
  • If there is no debt, create a new debt record manually on DMIS

9

Record the details + Read more ...

Record a DOC for all completed debts. The DOC must contain:

  • details of the debt
    • the decision itself
    • the Act/Guide reference upon which it is based
    • the reason for making the decision, including detail of information viewed when making the decision, and
    • the reason waiver is not appropriate
  • 10% recovery fee (where applicable)
  • evidence and calculation sheets used to determine the debt

For more information, see:

  • Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts, and
  • Recording reasons for decisions under Getting it Right (GIR)

Mark documents placed on file in relation to a debt 'Overpayment - Do Not Destroy'. See Storing documents.

See Step 1 in Table 2.

Step

Action

3

Check if tax details need adjusting + Read more ...

Make tax adjustments of the customer's tax details, if required. See the Resources page for further information.

Avoid common tax adjustment errors:

  • Use the correct benefit type for the entire debt period
    • Raise separate debts if the customer received more than one payment type
    • Action individual tax adjustments via the Component Amount (OPCA) screen
  • Check multiple debts are not raised for the same period and benefit type. This prevents the tax system using the total amount to adjust the Payment summary
    • Examine the debt(s) when a previous debt is manually deducted from a system based calculation
    • Offset the part of the debts which overlap
  • Check component(s) breakdown against overpayment calculations on OPCA. Ensure the correct payment components are used
  • Check that the OPCA reflects the payment's correct breakdown of periods for financial year in OPCA. Update the component amounts if they are incorrect
  • For payments where the entitlement falls within one financial year, but is paid in the following financial year, code the OPCA screen using the tax year dates in which the payment was paid. The OPCA screen has tax logic and will generally determine this

For compensation charge debts:

  • Check the OPCA screen
  • Make sure the debt is correctly apportioned by component and the financial year when the Compensation Affected Payments (CAPs) being recovered were received
  • Check the delivery dates on the Payment Summary (PS) screen to determine payments made by tax year
  • Incorporate the arrears payment into the financial year the payment was made. See Processing Manual Follow-up (MFU) tax activities for compensation debts

QOL check required + Read more ...

Complete Quality On Line (QOL).

6

Customer is deceased + Read more ...

Start the 'request for refund' process if the customer is deceased.
