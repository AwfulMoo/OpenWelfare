Actioning and correcting the negative amount(s) on the payment summary

Step

Action

1

How to identify a compensation debt + Read more ...

A compensation debt (with a reason code CMA, CMC, CMM or CMP) has been identified on the Debt List (OPDL) screen.

For staff outside the compensation recovery team (CRT):

Refer all compensation MFU tax activities to the Compensation Recovery Team (CRT) to complete.

  • Complete TAXMFU Referral Fast Note
  • Attach to the TAX MFU
  • Re-categorised to CRT in Work Load Manager (WLM)

For CRT, in the customer's record:

  • Navigate to Tax Detailed web page:
    • On the Activity Search (AYS) screen select the activity
    • Go to the Tax Payment Summary (TXGS) screen and check the line for a negative payment. The TXGS screen is the summary of all payment summaries calculated including those issued and not issues
    • Select the relevant line, press [Enter]
    • The TXGD screen will show the details of the calculated Payment Summary for the specific financial year
    • Note the period and total of the negative amount
    • Go to the Tax YTD Totals (TXYTD) screen
    • Enter the period of the financial year, all benefits paid and 'Y' in the Message field
    • The TXYTD screen will launch the Tax Detailed Explanation web page for periods after 01 July 2003. This screen shows the tax calculation for the period entered
  • Click on the Period in the Payment Summary Information block. This will show the Calculation Summary block
  • Click on the Debt Adjustments and note the Debt ID and the total amount of the debt

2

Determine the periods and component amounts for the debt + Read more ...

Compensation debts are due to:

  • lump sum (preclusion period), or
  • periodic weekly payments

Go to the Compensation Management System (CMS) to determine reason for the debt.

Go to OPDL screen, key 's' against the relevant Debt ID and then go to the Component Amount (OPCA) screen.

Check the periods on the screen. Compensation debts do not always have the period broken into financial years. Calculate this information using the:

  • ADEX calculations, or
  • reviewing CMS, or
  • PS Tool, or
  • by checking for a DOC detailing the compensation breakup

3

Is the compensation debt amount correct? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

The debt amount is less than or greater than the total amount the customer received + Read more ...

If part of the debt is due to partner’s entitlement, go to Step 7.

Once the correct charge amount is determined update the Debt Amount (OPDA) screen.

Amending the debt will recalculate and send a corrected payment summary, if the debt period is within the last 5 years. Periods more than 5 years ago may require manual adjustment.

If the MFU tax activity is within the last 5 years and was due to:

  • End of Financial Year processing, the payment summary will become available for request by the customer; Customers can request Payment Summaries through a range of self service options
  • Retrospective changes, the amended payment summary will automatically issue via the customers preferred correspondence channel

Where the amendment results in:

  • under-recovery:
    • consider raising the additional debt
    • CRT will refer the debt to the Decision Maker (DM) for further investigation and correction. See Periodic compensation payments and Lump sum compensation payments (CLK)
  • over-recovery,
    • refund the amount either to the compensation payer or to the customer. See Transfers and refunds.

Record details of action on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

5

Update of Debt Components + Read more ...

On the OPCA screen manually amend the details of the debt.

CMS apportions components into financial years.

Occasionally these apportionments may be incorrect.

Correcting each financial year totals often corrects the payment summary discrepancy.

If the customer has multiple periods of entitlement within the same financial year (as per Tax Year To Date (TXYTD) screen). Record this as separate periods on OPCA:

  • Break down compensation debt amount into financial years (by date received, rather than entitlement period)
  • Ensure debt period and relevant components are recorded correctly
  • On the OPCA screen before any updates can be made, change the debt status to 'Determined'
  • Update amounts noting if the debt has been repaid

Once OPCA update is complete, check payment summary via TXYTD.

Payment summaries will auto generate once triggers complete. Original payment summaries will then be available for issue to or accessed by the customer. Amended payment summaries will issue to the customer via their preferred channel.

Record details of action on a DOC.

Does the payment summary still show a negative amount?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, record details on a DOC and complete MFU tax activity. Procedure ends here

6

Debt period includes advances, benefit transfers or arrears + Read more ...

If debt amount and components are correct, the negative amount will be due to other reasons. For further information see:

  • General information on Manual Follow-up (MFU) tax activities
  • Processing Manual Follow-ups (MFU) tax activities for Advance recoveries
  • Processing Manual Follow-up (MFU) tax activities for benefit transfers

Procedure ends here.

7

Debt cannot be amended due to inclusion of the partner's entitlement on the customer's benefit + Read more ...

Where the debt details cannot be amended due to inclusion of the partner's entitlement on the customer's benefit by using the figures supplied by the DM.

Correct the Tax Payment Summary:

  • Go to the TXGS screen, key 'C' against the financial year to be corrected and press [Enter]:
  • On the TXGD screen, correct the amount with the calculated total using reason code 'ADM'
  • Complete the tax activity
  • Send the corrected Payment Summary
  • Customers are able to request an amended Payment Summary through a range of self service options
  • From the TXGS screen, key 'I' against the relevant debt to send the corrected payment summary
  • Complete the MFU tax activity
  • Record details of action on a DOC

Were the debt details able to be updated?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No,
    • The case should be referred to an Operational Leader for further investigation
    • If required, the Operational Leader will escalate to either Business Demand and Capacity Planning or ICT
    • Procedure ends here

8

Is part of the debt for the partner's entitlement? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, procedure ends here

9

If part of the debt is for the partner's entitlement + Read more ...

Where part of the debt is for the partner's entitlement. Manually correct the financial years covered by the debt:

  • Go to the TXGS screen, key 'C' against the financial year to be corrected and press [Enter]
  • On the TXGD screen, correct the amount with the calculated total using reason code 'ADM'
  • Complete the tax activity

10

Send the updated Payment Summary + Read more ...

  • From the TXGS screen:
    • Key 'I' against the line to be issued
    • Key 'Y' at Issue Group Cert/Statement, press [Enter]
    • Complete the MFU activity
  • Customers are able to request the amended Payment Summary through a range of self service options
  • Record details of action on a DOC
