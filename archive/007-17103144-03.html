Customers may request access to additional hours of subsidised child care (increased Activity Test result) for Child Care Subsidy (CCS) or Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) because they have exceptional circumstances, and:

  • it would be unreasonable for them and/or their partner to satisfy the activity test or care for their children

  • customer request

  • a re-application following the expiry of an exceptional circumstance

2

Exceptional circumstance scenarios + Read more ...

Exceptional circumstances include, but not limited to, situations where the customer, (and/or their partner) or the child are:

  • affected by family and domestic violence
  • experiencing serious illness or medical condition or hospitalisation preventing a customer (or partner) from working or caring for their child
  • experiencing serious mental health issues that prevent the customer (or partner) working or caring for the child
  • participating in a treatment or rehabilitation program to address substance abuse issues that prevents the individual working or caring for the child
  • impacted by significant trauma
  • experiencing short-term incapacity
  • attending a funeral or bereavement service or the resolution of an estate where long distance travel is required
  • responding to an emergency in their capacity as an employee (for example, SES during fires)
  • complying with a compulsory obligation imposed by the court (this covers undertaking community service, being on jury duty or being a witness)

Note: exceptional circumstances do not cover a customer's situation where their child care services charges more than 10 hours per session, or where usual work/travel hours total more than 100 hours per fortnight.

The Resources page has scenarios that may or may not be considered an exceptional circumstance for an increase in subsidised hours.

When seeking an exceptional circumstance Activity Test result, the customer is to advise:

  • the impact of the circumstance(s) on the customer and/or their partner's capacity to meet the Activity Test
  • the impact of the circumstance on the customer and/or their partner's capacity to care for their or their partner's child/ren
  • how the circumstances impact the child/ren
  • how access to subsidised child care would assist in the circumstances
  • why the child/ren cannot reasonably be left alone, and that no one over 18 years or above is able to provide suitable care
  • how long they expect the circumstance(s) to last
  • how many hours of subsidised child care they believe they need to access per fortnight to assist in their circumstances

Is the customer, (their partner) or the child subject to an exceptional circumstance listed above?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

4

Evidence required to support exceptional circumstance Activity Test result + Read more ...

In addition to providing information about their exceptional circumstances, customers are required to provide evidence to support their application for an exceptional circumstances Activity Test result. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • court documents
  • medical certificate. Note: a standard medical certificate (including Centrelink medical certificates) advising an individual 'is unable to work' is insufficient. A medical certificate:
    • should confirm the customer's diagnosis (where a serious illness), and
    • must include the practitioner's confirmation the customer is not able to engage in any recognised activities (not just work), and are unable to safely care for the child/ren
  • death certificate
  • declaration of natural disaster
  • signed statement from social worker, counsellor, doctor, or other party outlining:
    • the customer's circumstances
    • confirmation they are not able to engage in any activity (not just work), and
    • the customer is unable to safely care for the child/ren
  • receipts and invoices of medical and funeral expenses
  • fire report, police report or insurance report
  • any other evidence that could explain their circumstances
  • Commonwealth Statutory Declaration with all relevant information where documentation cannot be provided (for example, due to an emergency situation)

If the customer uses In Home Care (IHC), the customer is also required to provide their Family Management Plan developed by the IHC Agency (not their IHC service).

Note: evidence must outline that the customer is unable to participate in any recognised activities and provide a safe level of care for the child/ren due to those circumstance(s). If the customer requests the assessment be backdated up to the eligible 28 days (to the beginning of the CCS fortnight before the CCS fortnight in which the request is made), the evidence must cover this period.

ACCS subsidy percentage + Read more ...

The following customers receive a subsidy of 100% of the fee charged, up to 120% of the hourly rate cap:

  • ACCS (Grandparent)
  • ACCS (Temporary Financial Hardship), or
  • ACCS (Child Wellbeing)

The intent of this higher subsidy is to provide additional child care fee assistance, not provide free child care. It is expected the additional subsidy will cover the cost of fees for most families.

Where the ACCS subsidy does not cover the full cost of fees, and the customer is experiencing exceptional circumstances, they can request an increase in the subsidy above 120%.

Note: there is no subsidy increase available for CCS and ACCS (Transition to Work).

Special circumstances extension + Read more ...

Relevant year income requirements have not been met and a customer requests a special circumstances extension, or the available information provided suggests that there are special circumstances that prevented the customer from meeting this requirement.

Are they requesting an extension or have they provided sufficient information to support an extension to confirm their income?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, a special circumstances extension will not apply, they must meet the one year timeframe for the requirement. Record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

2

Special circumstances extension for the 1st or 2nd Deadline + Read more ...

If special circumstances apply, the extension options available will depend on the timing of the request with respect to the relevant deadline. The extension end date should be decided after discussing the circumstances with the customer.

Note: on 30 June 2020, the Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DESE) announced that the 1st deadline date for 2018-19 financial year would be extended until 31 March 2021. This allowed customers who had not met their CCS reconciliation requirements for 2018-19 to retain their CCS entitlement until 5 April 2021. The 2nd deadline for the 2018-19 remained 1 July 2021.

Customer may contact:

  • before the 1st deadline if they anticipate not meeting income confirmation requirements in time due to special circumstances and requests a 1st deadline extension in advance
  • after the 1st deadline has already passed and the 0% has already taken effect
  • after the 1st deadline: reconciliation requirements have now been met but there is a gap in entitlement at zero rate
  • before the 2nd deadline with either:
    • no 1st deadline extension applied previously
    • 1st deadline previously granted which has since expired, or
    • 1st deadline still in effect

Determine the end date for the special circumstances extension

The end date should be the soonest reasonable date the customer will be able to meet the requirement/s and must be an appropriate period of time to meet that requirement. This will depend on the circumstances affecting the customer. For example, it may be sufficient to only extend an income confirmation timeframe by a few months to give the customer time to lodge an income tax return.

The Resources page contains questions and answers including points to consider in making a decision on suitable timeframes for a special circumstances extension.

If the customer:

  • is applying for a special circumstance extension for the 1st deadline or has previously been granted a 1st deadline extension and is now applying to extend this further, go to Step 3
  • has previously been granted a 1st deadline extension and is now applying to extend this further, go to Step 5
  • is applying for a 2nd deadline extension, see Table 6

Continue to code the extension + Read more ...

Ex-partner Most Recent Estimate (MRE) exception + Read more ...

CCS reconciliation requires all previous ex-partners income. If a customer is unable to meet income requirements by the 1st deadline only due to the inaction of an ex-partner, the customer can request a Most Recent Estimate (MRE) exception. This allows the balancing process to complete using the most recent estimate of the ex-partner's income from the 1st deadline.

Discuss the ex-partner's income and known circumstances with the customer to establish a reasonable income estimate. Investigation should not include browsing ex-partner records. Only information mentioned in conversation, or contained in the CCS customer's record (such as activity test details or previous estimates provided during the partnered period) should be used to establish income and likelihood of tax lodgement.

  • If the MRE exception is being requested before the 1st Deadline, advise the customer that the balancing process will not commence until after the 1st Deadline. Also advise the customer that if the ex/previous-partner lodges a tax return in the meantime and all other requirements have been met, reconciliation will be triggered using the actual annual taxable income (ATI)
    Note: even though the 2018/19 1st deadline has been extended until 31 March 2021, MRE Exceptions for that year will still be triggered from 1 July 2020 when this coding is completed
  • If the 1st Deadline has already passed and the customer's CCS is being affected by 1st deadline penalties, applying the MRE Exception will resume CCS entitlement based on customers circumstances from the start of the next CCS fortnight. It will also and trigger relevant year reconciliation if all other requirements are met. The customer will not receive arrears for the period already assessed at 0
    Advise the customer that once reconciliation occurs using the ex-partner MRE and the ex-partner subsequently lodges an Income Tax Return, a new assessment will be untaken and re-reconciliation will occur using the actual income details and this could result in a top up, a nil payment, or an overpayment

Go to Step 2.

Document the customer record in Process Direct (PD) + Read more ...

Document customer's record by selecting Notes in Customer Summary. Document information must include text:

'Customer states ex-partner will not lodge income tax return because [reason]. Customer is aware that reconciliation will not occur until the 1st deadline unless ex-partner lodges ITR before this date. Customer has also been advised that if the reconciliation outcome is calculated on MRE and the ex-partner later lodges an ITR, CCS for the relevant year will be re-reconciled and the result could be either a nil adjustment, debt or arrears.'

Upon completing the MRE Exception coding, advise the customer that reconciliation will not occur until whichever is the later of:

  • the ex-partner meets the income confirmation requirements by some other means
  • the 1st deadline date has passed, or
  • the next CCS Monday after the MRE Exception has been coded, if it is applied after the 1st Deadline has already passed

Note: if this coding is completed before the 1st deadline, the CCS Reconciliation Dashboard will still display in 'Tax Return Status' that the ex-partners tax details are still 'Not Met'. This is because the ex-partner has until the 1st deadline to meet the reconciliation income conditions.

Where an MRE exception is applied and the ex-partner subsequently lodges a tax return, CCS will be re-reconciled based on the new income received, which could result in an arrears, nil adjustment or overpayment result. A MRE exception cannot override actual income received from the ex-partner.

Procedure ends here.
