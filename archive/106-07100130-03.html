1

Advice of real estate + Read more ...

A customer advises:

  • their home property is on more than one title
  • their home property is more than two hectares (five acres)
  • their home property includes a business premises and that premises is used primarily or solely for business purposes
  • they own any real estate other than their home property in Australia or overseas
  • they have entered aged care on or after 1 January 2017 and are renting their former principal home

Has the customer updated their real estate details online?

  • Yes, a Mod R is not required. See Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services. Procedure ends here.
  • No, follow the steps below

Issue a Real estate details (Mod R) to obtain details of the real estate.

Also issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter to ask for completion of the Mod R and a copy of a rates or valuation notice. The rates or valuation notice will show the legal title of the property.

If required, issue the Rural Property Questionnaire (Q454).

The Q454 is used in conjunction with the Mod R for medium to large farm properties. It collects more details from the customer for the approved valuer, to allow them to more correctly value the property. Generally, if the property is operated as a primary production enterprise, issue the Q454 in addition to the Mod R. The Q454 is not needed for smaller properties such as hobby farms with minimal farm improvements or bush blocks. Where the whole property is exempt because it is the principal home, or it is an exempt farm asset, do not issue a Q454 for that property.

If all details of new real estate are available at the first point of contact update the customer's record. For more details, see:

Note: if a customer borrows money to purchase a property other than their own home, and the loan is secured by a mortgage over their home or any other assets, the debt cannot be offset against the value of the investment property. See the Legislation links on the References page.

Coding overseas real estate + Read more ...

2

The REBI screen + Read more ...

Note: new real estate should only be linked to a business where it is unavoidable. For example - a non-primary production business where there is a liability over all business assets (real estate and other) and the value of the net business excluding the real estate are negative. If the real estate is linked to a business, code the business details first.

Complete the following fields on the REBI screen using the Real Estate Update script.

The REBI screen is used to record the type, description and address details of a business or real estate in Australia. Real estate information must be recorded if the customer's relationship status has changed since the Real Estate (RE) screen was last updated.

  • Real Estate/Business Type: for a business, code the business type; for real estate code the appropriate type of real estate
  • Description: enter a description for the business or real estate, that is, Company name
  • Principal Home: enter 'Y' for yes, if the real estate is the customer's principal home or 'N' for no if not the principal home. Note: if a business is being updated coding of this field is not required
  • Sold/Gifted/Ceased: update if sold, gifted/transferred, no longer assessable or business has ceased trading, enter valid code using field help '?'
  • Authority to inspect: 'Yes' or 'No'. It is important that this field is coded as it will help the Services Australia contracted third party valuer to obtain access to the property including when a full inspection is required
  • Land Titles Reference: enter the land title reference (the rates notice will contain this information). If the property has multiple land title references, record each individual reference. Note:
    • if the property is to be assessed under the extended curtilage provisions, complete the Other Circumstances: field on the Real Estate Valuation Information (REVI) screen to show which title the principal home is on
    • if the property is being used as security for the Home Equity Access Scheme, the free text value recorded in the Land Titles Reference field(s) will appear in correspondence to the customer. Changes should be made only by the Home Equity Access Scheme National team (region code PLS)
  • Multiple Titles: this field is for information only. This field is used for real estate only, not for business details. Three options are available 'Y' for yes, 'N' for no. Blank is the default. Leave blank if unknown

Note: code 'N' for no, where the customer's property has a single title document, even if it is made up of multiple adjoining lots. Where a customer's property is on multiple titles, and they are all adjoining, they are to be recorded as one real estate record. The Multiple Title indicator should be set to 'Y' for yes in these cases. More details on multiple lots on the one title document can be found in Real estate.

  • Address: Town/Suburb: Postcode: State: Country: code the address details. The customer must uniquely identify the location of any real estate or business site they own or have an interest in. For more details on a real estate address, see Address recording for real estate
  • Source: DOR: Action: code appropriate source, date of receipt and action code

Note: A system error E607Q may be present if changes are attempted to Real Estate Business Summary (REBS) screen and no updates have been made since the customer’s relationship status had changed. This will happen if the customer is single and the real estate is still recorded as partly or fully owned by the partner.

If this error is presented when attempting to apply changes to the REBS screen:

  • screen dump all, including historical pages, on the Real Estate (RE) screen, relating to the impacted property or business
  • delete the relevant entry in REBS and in the same activity recode all relevant information including historical entries and the correct % ownership
  • look at ex-partner’s record to check if property is still being assessed and if updates are needed. Do not use the information supplied by the customer to update the ex-partner’s record

The script will flow to the Business Detail (BUS) screen or the Real Estate (RE) screen depending on the Type: entered on the REBI screen.

Was the BUS screen or the RE screen presented?

  • for BUS screen, go to Step 3
  • for RE screen, go to Step 4

3

BUS screen + Read more ...

Note: if full details of the business are not known, complete as much as possible to allow the link to the real estate to be made, for example, Module F and Income Tax Return has not been supplied.

Important: Real estate should not be linked to a business unless advised and assessed by a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO). Linking real estate to a business will not allow the real estate to be included in the risk based approach to real estate assessment.

Update business details by coding the following fields:

  • Event Date: appropriate event date
  • Link New Real Estate?: 'Y' for yes
  • Valuation Source: the appropriate source
  • Net Assets Customer $: the business net value for customer
  • Net Assets Partner $: the business net value for partner
  • Income p.a. $: income profit of business per annum
  • % Income (C/P): percentage of income
  • Income Earned (C/P)?: if income is earned by customer and/or partner

See Assets and liabilities of a business for full details on how to determine business income and assets for a business.

4

The Real Estate (RE) screen + Read more ...

Note: if the real estate is linked to a business the income, deductions and asset fields will not be presented.

Update real estate details by coding the following fields:

  • RAF Code: used to identify that the income and asset value of the farm is exempt from the gifting provisions under the Retirement Assistance for Farmers (RAF) Scheme. The income and assets will be included in the Net Income/Net Assets calculated on this screen but will not be included in the Total Income/Asset calculation
  • Bus Link: displays the business Ref Id if the real estate is owned by a business
  • Curtilage Type: Three options are available:
    • GRF - grandfathered, for customers whose curtilage is assessed under the grandfathered savings provision for real estate, grandfather's concessions for real estate prior to 1 January 2007. Note: existing customers with curtilage recorded as at 31 December 2006 were automatically assessed as curtilage type GRF. Ensure that a new assessment of curtilage is undertaken and the curtilage type is updated to the appropriate code
    • EXT - extended, where the extended curtilage concession applies, that is, customer satisfies the extended land use test
    • PLU - for customers assessed under the single title rule from 1 January 2007
    • Leave field blank - for customers to whom curtilage does not apply, that is, it is not the principal home
    • For more detail on house and curtilage assessments, see Assessing house and curtilage
  • Event Date: the date the change in circumstance occurred, for example, the date the property was rented out or purchased
  • Estimate Value $: the estimate value of the real estate (if owner is a business, value as listed on the balance sheet). This should include the value of any assessable Water Rights related to the property
  • Actual Valuation Date: enter the date of the valuation
  • Current Market Value $: the amount should represent the current value of the whole item of real estate
    • It should include the value of any assessable Water Rights related to the property
      Note: the assessment of water assets for Farm Household Allowance (FHA) customers is different than the assessment under social security policy, see Assessing assets for Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
    • It should equal the house and curtilage value. For the property to be fully exempt under the extended curtilage provisions, the value of other farm assets, including sheds, should be recorded as Other Assets and not included in the whole property valuation
    • Property values shown on council rates notices are for calculating council rates, fire and ambulance services or other taxes. They do not always indicate the current market value of a property
    • A current market valuation shows how much a property might sell for in the current market
  • Asset Valuation Source: is the source of the real estate valuation
    • For new properties, the valuation source will be CLI for client estimate
    • if the property has previously been recorded with a valuation source code of NVE, VAL, ONS, EST or IDX, do not update the source code to CLI, unless the customer advises significant improvement/damage to the property
    • If the property has previously been recorded and has a source code other than NVE, VAL, ONS, EST or IDX, update the source code to CLI
  • Current House & Curt. $: enter the value of the principal home and surrounding curtilage applicable
    • For all new claims from 1 January 2007 the assets test assessment for the principal home and curtilage has been amended to a single title rule
    • Where the private land use test applies, the amount of house and curtilage is the lesser of two hectares or the area of the one title on which the home is situated
    • Where the extended land use test applies, the amount of house and curtilage is the value of all of the land on the same title as the home
    • Only one item of real estate with a home and curtilage amount can be coded for each customer

For more detail on house and curtilage assessments, see the Resources page and Assessing house and curtilage

  • House Contents $: code the value of house contents that belong to the customer
    • Note: the system does not apportion house contents based on the asset ownership percentage coded. If the customer is single, 100% of the value coded in this field is applied to the customer. (If they are partnered, 50% is applied to each partner)
    • If this property is the customer's home do not record house contents here. Household contents and personal effects are recorded on the Other Assets (OAS) screen
  • Other Assets $: if there are any other assets related to this real estate that belong to the customer, code their value. The system does not apportion other assets based on the asset ownership percentage coded
  • Livestock $: if there is any livestock related to this real estate that belongs to the customer, code the value of the livestock. The system does not apportion the value of livestock based on the asset ownership percentage coded
  • Mortgage/Loan $: if there are mortgages or loans on the real estate, code the value of the mortgages or loans. Note: if the property is not a principal home and is used as security for a loan payment under the Home Equity Access Scheme (the Scheme), a Complex Assessment Officer will update this field with the Scheme debt balance
  • Former Principal home for RCA purposes: only relevant to residential aged care (RCA) customers. Indicates the real estate was the customer's former principal home and may affect the RCA asset assessment. It will continue to be assessed in the Income Support Payment asset test assessment

5

More field information for the RE screen + Read more ...

  • Net Income $: net income derived from the property. Note: customers who enter aged care on or after 1 January 2017 will have the net rental income from their former principal home assessed as ordinary income. The former principal home will be exempt from the assets test for two years after the customer vacates it to enter a care situation
  • Net Assets $: net monetary value of the assets
  • % Income Owned: the customer's percentage of the income derived from the property
  • Gross Annual Income $: the total income derived from the property
  • Income Valuation Source: the source on which the valuation figures are based
  • Annual Interest $: the interest amount paid (in whole dollars) for a loan or mortgage. This is usually obtained from the rental property schedule of the business, a personal ITR or a profit and loss type statement. Interest payments on a mortgage for the property concerned are allowed as an additional deduction from the amount of income to be assessed
  • Allowable Income Dedns $ pa: the allowable deductions from the gross income, not including the interest paid for loans or mortgages. Details are usually obtained from the rental property schedule of the business, a personal ITR or a profit and loss type statement. If expenses are expected to be higher, ask for proof
  • Valuers Job Number: used to record the agency's contracted third party valuer's reference number for their valuation if the property was valued by them
  • Valuers Property Number: the number assigned to the property by the agency's third party valuer when it was first valued by them

How the system derives the net asset value of real estate.

6

Real Estate Valuation Information (REVI) screen + Read more ...

The REVI screen records details about the property that is required in order for a valuation to be done, such as property size, number of buildings.

All property information available should be recorded on the REVI screen, including land area, number of rooms, improvements, purchase details if available, authority to inspect and details of person to be contacted for an inspection.

The Purchase Ind: field records who purchased the property. 'Y' should be recorded if the customer and/or their partner purchased the property.

The Improvements: field allows free text. Use this field to record any more details provided by the customer, for example, 'property has water damage'. Providing all available information will enable a successful and accurate valuation.

Important: more details or instructions can also be recorded on the Other Circumstances: field. If the property is to be assessed under the extended curtilage provisions, complete the Other Circumstances: field on the REVI screen to show which title the principal home is on.

Note: assets such as marina berths, car park spaces should be recorded as real estate type OTH.

Self-contained flats and other dwellings on the same title as the principal home that are currently rented (other than to a near relative), should be recorded as real estate type SCF. See the Detail section of Assessing house and curtilage.

Valuation

See Valuation of real estate and other assets for information about when a valuation is required.

8

Income details + Read more ...

  • Using the Real Estate Update script code the percentage interest the customer and/or the customer's partner has in the income value of the real estate in the % Income Owned: field
  • Enter the valuation source of income in the Income Valuation Source: field
  • Code the gross annual income for the real estate in the Gross Annual Income $: field
  • Code the interest on any mortgages or loans associated with the real estate in the Annual Interest $: field
  • Code any allowable deduction in the Allowable Income Dedns $ pa: field

Note: unless there is an amount in the Mortgage/Loan: field, the Interest Expense: field cannot be recorded. Where interest expense is an allowable deduction but the mortgage is not an allowable deduction, an amount of $1 must be recorded in the Mortgage/Loan: field.

See Loans and Liabilities against assets for more details about allowable liabilities and apportionment.
