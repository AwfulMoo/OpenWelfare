Information and services available via HPOS

Table 1

Services

Information available

Australian Immunisation Register (AIR)

Record vaccination information, view individual’s vaccination history, record a vaccination exemption, access AIR Reports, view AIR payments, update provider’s contact details. Program is only available to GPs and allied health professionals who have registered for access.

Centrelink forms

GPs can access, complete and transmit the Medical Report Disability Support Pension and Medical Certificate forms to Centrelink.

Delegate facility

Allows a health professional to nominate a delegate to act on their behalf (including practice staff with their own PRODA account to undertake administrative tasks.

Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA) Patient Treatment reports

Receive Patient Treatment Reports for veterans in their care who are enrolled in the Coordinated Veterans' Care (CVC) Program.

DVA Webclaim

Health professionals can use DVA Webclaim to submit Medical and allied health services for the treatment of eligible DVA customers. Private Hospitals and Community Nursing facilities cannot lodge services through DVA Webclaim.

My Health Record system – organisation registration

Register organisations for the My Health Record system and maintain details. Establish a list of individual healthcare providers who are authorised to access the My Health Record Provider Portal. Authorise contracted service providers to interact with the My Health Record system on the organisations behalf.

Find a patient

Confirm and search patient Medicare card details. (includes Concessional entitlement verification)

Healthcare Identifiers (HI) Service

The Healthcare Identifiers (HI) Service in HPOS can be used to:

  • maintain healthcare identifier details
  • search for healthcare providers and organisations in the HI Service, and
  • maintain links to providers working at the organisation, to display in the Healthcare Provider Directory

What can be performed in the HI Service through HPOS is tailored for the individual roles of either a Responsible Officer, Organisation Maintenance Officer or Individual Healthcare Provider.

MBS items online checker

The MBS items online checker enables the:

  • viewing and checking of patient eligibility based on their MBS history
  • checking of providers eligibility for claiming MBS items, and
  • checking of the claiming conditions for MBS items

This service helps to make informed treatment decisions and avoid rejected claims.

Medicare Bulk Bill Webclaim

Medicare Bulk Bill Webclaim is an internet based claim channel that is accessed via the Health Professional Online Services (HPOS) portal on the Services Australia website.

Medicare Patient Claim Webclaim

Medicare Patient Claim Webclaim is an online claiming channel through HPOS where Medicare claims are submitted on behalf of patients at the point of service. This provides real time assessment and reduces the need for patients to obtain additional information from the provider to receive their Medicare benefit. For example, as part of submitting the claim, the provider can inform Services Australia that a service was not aftercare.

My details

View provider personal details including provider number details, banking details and add a new or reopen a closed provider number (location).

Practice Incentives Program (PIP)

Practice owners and authorised contacts with a PRODA account can now:

  • register for the PIP
  • update their practice details and apply for additional incentives under the PIP
  • access information about the PIP
  • view their PIP statements
  • receive broadcasts

Workforce Incentive Program (WIP) - Practice Stream

Apply for the WIP – Practice Stream, view statements, letters and broadcasts.

Prescription Shopping Information Program

Information, patient summary reports and FAQs.

After contacting the Prescription Shopping Information Service, if the patient met the programme’s criteria, providers can access a report listing medicines supplied to the patient in the last 3 months.

Rural Incentives Programs

General Practice Rural Incentives Program (GPRIP) including the GP Component, Registrars Component and the Rural Relocation Incentive Grant (RRIG), the Rural Procedural Grants Program (RPGP) and the HECS Reimbursement Scheme information and services. Apply for the GPRIP, RRIG and HECS and view statements.

Workforce Incentive Program (WIP) - Doctor Stream

Provider can view status, service and payment history and update bank account details.

HPOS Messages

Secure email facility to receive statements, letters and notifications online for:

  • 90 day pay doctor cheque scheme EFT payments
  • Australian Immunisation Register
  • DVA Coordinated Veterans' Care Program Patient Treatment Report
  • DVA Webclaim EFT statement, recipient credited tax invoice statement and zero benefit statement
  • Medicare bulk bill – Medicare Bulk Bill Payment Report (Statement)
  • Midwives Professional Indemnity (MPIS)
  • National Bowel Cancer Screening Program – Payment advice and tax invoice
  • Rural incentives programs
  • Track and Scale - scaling details and location s19AB exemption details

HPOS Messages - Form upload

Health professionals and their delegates will be able to securely upload select forms via a new 'form upload' facility in HPOS. The service will be for the submission of scanned and/or photographed Health Professional forms and accompanying documents. The service will also provide a receipt and the capability for Services Australia Service Officers to securely reply to the health professional via email.

Closing the Gap PBS Co-payment Register

PBS Prescribers, Aboriginal Health Practitioners and their delegates are able to register an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander patient for the Closing the Gap (PBS) co-payment program. Pharmacies can search to see if a patient is registered for the Closing the Gap (PBS) co-payment program for PBS claiming purposes.

Item

Description

1

Question: Who can access HPOS?

Answer: All health professionals and their delegates as long as each person has their own PRODA account.

A PKI site certificate can also be used for limited services such as Find a patient and Messaging.

2

= make this page a favourite page for easy access

 = legal information

= contact us/help information

= privacy information

Top left hand corner of the screen:

= menu

3

Question: Can HI service Organisation Maintenance Officers OMO NASH PKI certificates be renewed online within HPOS?

Answer: We no longer issue NASH PKI certificates for individual healthcare providers. Existing NASH PKI certificates will still be able to access the My Health Record read-only National Provider Portal (NPP) until they expire (2 year expiry date). A Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account is needed to log on to NPP if the provider doesn’t have an existing certificate.

Organisation Maintenance Officers (OMO) can request, new or changes to, the following PKI certificates:

  • National Authentication Service for Health (NASH) PKI certificates for healthcare provider organisations
  • link existing Services Australia PRODA accounts for lower level OMOs

A request these certificates under the new Certificates tab when creating or amending network organisations and amending OMO records.

4

Question: Will the re-establishment of delegates be required for accessing HPOS with PRODA?

Answer: Yes, all delegate functions are authentication based

5

Question: How are tasks performed on behalf of the provider as a delegate?

Answer: Select 'Select Provider' from top right hand corner and chose the provider to act on behalf of.

6

Question: Where is the HPOS logon page?

Answer: From the Services Australia homepage:

  • select myGov sign in
  • select HPOS under Health Professionals

For help with HPOS access issues, customers can call eBusiness.

7

Question: Will there be a demonstration model or way for Service Officers, BDOs and users to see the system?

Answer: Unfortunately not, at this stage. Services Australia will provide screen shots for staff.

Health professionals can access step-by-step guides for each service in HPOS from the Health professional's website.

8

Question: Is there an eLearning Module available?

Answer: Yes there is an eLearning module for health professionals outlining the user interface changes on the Services Australia website

9

Question: Where can required services be found?

Answer: The HPOS interface is arranged under broad headings, which are:

  • Messages
  • Find a patient
  • Items
  • Claims
  • Reports
  • My programs
  • My details
  • Info

Select one of these quick links to be taken to the services you need within HPOS.

10

Question: What are the most used services within HPOS and how are these services now found?

Answer: The 3 most used services in HPOS are:

  • Find a patient - found by selecting the 'Find a patient' title on the HPOS homepage.
  • MBS items online checker - found by selecting the 'items' tile on the HPOS homepage, and then MBS items online checker
  • Medicare Bulk Bill reports - found by selecting 'reports' on the HPOS homepage

11

Question: Where can 'View patient care plan history' and 'Multiple Details Request be found?'

Answer: View Patient Care Plan History and Multiple Details Request is accessible from the left hand menu only under 'Find a patient'.

12

Question: Where can help or information on the services within HPOS be found?

Answer: Select the 'info' tile from the homepage which navigates to the HPOS page on the Services Australia health professional's website. This site has a user guide/instructions for each service available in HPOS.

13

Question: Where is the Medicare Bulk Bill Webclaim service found within HPOS?

Answer: On the HPOS home page:

  • select Claims tile
  • select 'Make a new claim' or 'View saved claims'
  • select 'Medicare Bulk Bill Webclaim'

14

Question: How does a health professional get a PRODA account?

Answer: Information is available on the Services Australia website and searching PRODA.

15

Question: What support is available to customers when applying for a PKI certificate?

Answer: Information is available on the Services Australia website. Customers can contact eBusiness.

16

Question: Can a health professional delegate access to another individual to act on their behalf?

Answer: Yes, health professionals with an individual PRODA account can delegate access to another person (who has their own individual PRODA account) to do tasks on their behalf within HPOS.
