Aged Care Provider Portal (ACPP) frequently asked questions and answers

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Question: What is the ACPP?

Answer: the ACPP is a single point of entry for online services available to all aged care services.

ACPP makes it easier and quicker for aged care services to perform secure online transactions with Services Australia.

2

Question: Who can use the ACPP?

Answer: ‘A users’ (authorised users from approved aged care services) can access ACPP if they have:

  • a Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account and
  • their service linked through PRODA

Their service can be a:

  • residential care service
  • home care service
  • flexible care service

3

Question: How can ‘A users’ access the ACPP?

‘A users’ (authorised users from approved aged care services) can access the ACPP via 2 methods:

  • The PRODA webpage
  • The Services Australia website
    • Go to the Services Australia website
    • Select the myGov Sign in on the right hand side
    • Select PRODA under Health professionals
    • Select Log on
    • The PRODA Login screen will display
    • Key in the username in the Username field
    • Key in the password in the Password field
    • Select Login

4

Question:- How do I create a PRODA account?

Answer: To create a PRODA account, 3 actions are required to complete the registration process:

  • Create account - provide personal details, create an username and password and verify their email address
  • Verify documents - verify 3 different identity documents
  • Match existing services - select the Aged Care Provider Portal tile to complete matching process for their existing services

Staff can follow the steps outlined in the Helping users create a Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account to support the ‘A user’.

5

Question: Where do I go to for support and more information about PRODA?

Answer: For support and more information about PRODA, staff can refer to:

  • Operational Blueprints:
    • Provider Digital Access (PRODA)
    • Helping users create a Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account
    • Managing a Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account
  • the Services Australia website

6

Question: What can I do with ACPP?

Answer: Aged care services can use ACPP to:

  • access online claiming. This allows ‘A users’ to:
    • submit a monthly claim for the aged care service
    • submit an invoice amount for home care purposes only
    • submit and correct care recipients’ circumstances events
    • register Viability events and register, delete and/or return Commonwealth (CW) unspent amount (for home care purposes only)
    • register care recipients’ Aged Care Funding Instrument (ACFI) assessment details (for residential care purposes only)
    • register Oxygen Supplement and the Enteral Feeding Supplement events and upload the Application for the Oxygen or Enteral Feeding Supplement (AC011) form and supporting evidence (for residential care purposes only)
    • view a care recipient’s leave balance including respite care (for residential care purposes only)
    • register care setting days and bulk events (for flexible care purposes only)
  • access, view and download care recipients’ personal details and payment information history
  • access and view a care recipient’s home care account (for home care purposes only)
  • access, view and download their services’ payment statement details
  • switch between services (if authorised)
  • manage and request registration of service user details (if authorised)
  • manage and register service contact details (if authorised)

7

Question: What happened to the Terms and conditions?

Answer: Users will need to accept the terms and conditions when:

  • they initially log on to ACPP and
  • there are changes to the terms and conditions

8

Question: Where can I find the Terms and conditions?

Answer: ‘A users’ can view the Terms and conditions by selecting the Terms and conditions hyperlink in the grey banner of the bottom of every ACPP screens.

9

Question: Why can’t I see my service?

If ‘A users’ cannot see their service, it could be due to the:

  • ‘A user’ is not associated with the service
  • service has not been granted access to the ACPP yet e.g. timing issue

10

Question: Can I view the letters that have been sent to a care recipient or an aged care service?

Answer: No, this functionality is not available.

11

Question: Can I submit aged care forms via ACPP for flexible care and home care?

Answer: No, this functionality is not available. Aged care forms will still need to be:

  • emailed
  • faxed, or
  • posted to Services Australia

See Help me find a form for health professionals for a list of forms for aged care services.

12

Question: Can I submit aged care forms via ACPP for residential care?

Answer: Yes. Residential care services can only submit the Application for the Oxygen or Enteral Feeding Supplement (AC011) form and supporting evidence (tax invoices and medical certificates).

For all other forms, this functionality is not available. All other aged care forms for residential care will still need to be:

  • emailed
  • faxed, or
  • posted to Services Australia

See Help me find a form for health professionals for a list of forms for aged care services.

13

Question: Why am I seeing the departed care recipient error message?

Answer: If ‘A users’ are attempting to view the specific details for a departed care recipient, the message ‘This care recipient was admitted into one of your services previously’ will display.

14

Question: Why am I seeing the 2 year limit error message?

Answer: If ‘A users’ are attempting to update an event outside the 2 year limit, an error message will display.

‘A users’ will need to send an email request and supporting evidence to the Aged Care Payments Team. See the 2 year variation rule in the Aged Care Provider Portal for the process.

15

Question: Why am I seeing the 70 day limit error message? (home care only)

Answer: If ‘A users’ are attempting to update an event for a home care recipient more than 70 days after the care recipient’s departure, an error message will display.

No changes can be made for home care recipients more than 70 days after departure.

If the ‘A user’ believes there has been a serious error made by Services Australia which needs to be fixed, they will need to send an email request and supporting evidence to the Aged Care Payments Team. See Managing departures for more information.

FAQs - ACFI questions and answers

Table 2: this table contains questions and answers associated with the Aged Care Funding Instrument (ACFI).

Item

Question and answers

1

Question: What is a CNR?

Answer: CNR equates to the term 'care needs reassessed'.

A service may submit an ACFI with appraisal type 'CNR' when a care recipient’s care needs have been reassessed:

  • six months after discharge from hospital leave
  • when Resident Classification Scale (RCS) has expired
  • more than 12 months after the last appraisal was performed

2

Question: What is TRA and how do I use it?

Answer: TRA equates to the term 'transfer from another facility'.

A service may submit an ACFI with appraisal type 'TRA' when a recipient's care needs have been reassessed following transfer of a care recipient within 28 days.

3

Question: How do I convert the 'Reason for this application' section into an appropriate appraisal code type?

Answer: The service should use the ACFI Appraisal Pack when completing the ACFI web form as this information is the same.

See the Department of Health and Aged Care website, Aged Care Funding Instrument User Guide.

4

Question: How can I look up to see whether a new resident to my facility has had an ACFI appraisal completed by another service?

Answer: Aged care services are unable to view ACFIs submitted by other services due to privacy reasons. The service may call the previous service for the information.

PDF version of payment statements – residential aged care services

A PDF version of a payment statement for a residential aged care service is not currently available through the Aged Care Provider Portal (ACPP).

If a residential aged care service requires a PDF version of a payment statement, the aged care service provider:

  • must open the relevant page from the payment statement/summary for the appropriate claim month from the service landing page
  • press the control and P buttons on the keyboard (Ctrl + P)
  • under the ‘printer’ dropdown (for Microsoft Edge) or ‘destination’ dropdown (for Google Chrome, select ‘save as PDF’
  • select the ‘save’ button at the bottom of the popup screen
  • save the relevant page of the payment statement on the desktop or in a folder that is easy to access

Repeat the above steps for any other pages of the payment statement.

PDF version of Aged Care Funding Instrument (ACFI) – residential aged care services

A PDF version of an Aged Care Funding Instrument (ACFI) for a residential care recipient is not currently available through the Aged Care Provider Portal (ACPP).

If a residential aged care service requires a PDF version of a care recipient’s ACFI, the aged care service provider:

  • must open the relevant page from the ACFI summary screen for the relevant care recipient in ACPP
  • press the control and P buttons on the keyboard (Ctrl + P)
  • under the ‘printer’ dropdown (for Microsoft Edge) or ‘destination’ dropdown (for Google Chrome, select ‘save as PDF’
  • select the ‘save’ button at the bottom of the popup screen
  • save the relevant page of the ACFI on the desktop or in a folder that is easy to access

Repeat the above steps for any other pages of the ACFI and/or for other care recipients.
