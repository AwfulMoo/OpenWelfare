Frequently Asked Questions

Item

Response

1

Question: Will I need to access the database to process an application for CD?

Answer: Yes. Access the database to confirm patient has been recorded, then key in data for initial applications, initial grandfather applications and first continuing applications.

You will also need to:

  • key a line in the database for change, medical break, recommencement and demonstration of response application
  • update the next assessment date for applications, including subsequent continuing

2

Question: Where do I process Initial and Continuing CD applications?

Answer: Process all authority prescriptions through the Online PBS Authorities system. You will need to enter a line into the CD database for initial, first continuing, change, medical break, recommencement and demonstration of response applications.

3

Question: I can see on the patients ‘display patient history’ screen in the Online PBS Authorities system that this medicine was approved yesterday. Do I reject the prescription for being too soon?

Answer: Check the application is not replacing a top-up or lost/replacement prescriptions. If so, follow instructions for top-ups or lost/replacement prescriptions. Otherwise reject the application if the patient's dispensing indicates 'reject too soon'

4

Question: The prescriber calls and asks for a 1-month approval due to infection. Can this be approved?

Answer: Check if the patient has any outstanding repeats. If there are repeats available, tell the prescriber to use them first then reassess response.

If no repeats are available, advise to complete a continuing application and document the reason. Escalate to a PA for assessment.

5

Question: Which drugs require a patient weight?

Answer: infliximab i.v.

6

Question: The patient’s Medicare number is different to what is on the database – do I need to update?

Answer: OPA will advise if the Medicare number is current. Check RPQN (Mainframe) for current number if OPA advises the number is not valid and update on database.

7

Question: Why does the restriction and item code have date tabs?

Answer: This indicates if there has been a ‘first of the month’ schedule update for the program. You need to key a prescription under the item code and restriction from the date of prescribing.

8

Question: What dates do I key into a ‘too soon’ rejection letter?

Answer: Key the ‘supply start’ as either the date of approval or the date of first dispensing (whichever is greater). Key the ‘supply end’ date as the Next Assessment Date.

9

Question: The prescriber has requested quantity/repeats higher than the restriction allows – can I reduce the number for approval?

Answer: Yes, quantity and repeats can be reduced, but not increased. An ‘approved with changes’ letter should be returned to the prescriber in these cases.

10

Question: I have an application form but no prescription, what should I do?

Answer: Check if the application is to record a demonstration of response. If so, record the application ‘demonstration of response’. If not, a reject letter may be required ‘prescription not received’.

11

Question: Does a patient have to be passing their current drug to change?

Answer: No.

12

Question: The prescriber has not provided the hospital number or name. Can I copy the previous one?

Answer: Yes if same prescriber as previously.

13

Question: Can I tell the prescriber their hospital provider number if they only know the name?

Answer: No, you can use the hospital provider number for approval and inform the prescriber that this information is published on the Department of Health and Aged Care and Aged Care’s website.

Home/For Health Professionals/Public and private healthcare/Hospitals/Current Listing of Commonwealth declared hospitals

14

Question: Prescriber wants to replace a lost prescription, should I check dispensing history?

Answer: Yes, to determine and confirm how many repeats are outstanding.

15

Question: The prescriber states the patient had a reaction to the drug, is this considered a failure to the current drug?

Answer: This does not count towards the patients failures.
