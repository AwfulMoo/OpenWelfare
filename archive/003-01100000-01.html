Raising emergency payment debts and grant/ex-gratia overpayments 003-01100000

  • Background

  • Resources
Expand all


This document explains how potential debts can be raised for emergency payments and grants (previously known as ex-gratia payments). These include Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP), Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA), Disaster Recovery Payment for New Zealand Special Category (subclass 444) visa holders (NZ DRP) and Disaster Recovery Allowance for New Zealand Special Category (subclass 444) visa holders (NZ DRA).

Grants

From October 2020, the term 'ex-gratia payments' changed to 'grants'.

Grants include:

  • NZ DRP - previously known as ex-gratia DRP
  • NZ DRA - previously known as ex-gratia DRA
  • Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment
  • MH17 Family Support Package

Relevant legislation

AGDRP and DRA are paid under the Social Security Act 1991.

Grants/ex-gratia payments are paid under the Financial Framework (Supplementary Powers) Act 1997.

When to raise a debt or overpayment

A debt is raised for Social Security Act 1991 payments as per general procedures for raising debts.

For AGDRP, a debt is raised where a customer was not eligible to receive a payment.

For DRA, a debt is raised where a customer

  • was not eligible to receive a payment, and/or
  • continued to receive a payment when they were no longer eligible to receive a payment

Under similar circumstances, an overpayment is raised for Financial Framework (Supplementary Powers) Act 1997 payments. These are grants/ex-gratia payments as listed above.

Overpayments are:

  • raised as outlined in the specific policy guidelines (often known as Operational Instructions), and
  • recovered under the Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013. This replaced the Financial Management Accountability Act 1997 from 1 July 2014

Systems

AGDRP and grants/ex-gratia payments

  • From December 2011, AGDRP and one-off grants/ex-gratia payments are paid in the Flexible Support Payment (FSP) system
  • Before December 2011, the Emergency Recovery Payment (ERP) and Emergency (EMG) systems were used to deliver these payments

DRA and NZ DRA

DRA and NZ DRA (previously paid and known as ex-gratia DRA) were implemented in October 2013 and have always been paid in the FSP system.

Request for a formal review

AGDRP or DRA debts

Customers wishing to appeal an AGDRP or DRA debt decision have access to the standard review and appeals process.

Grants/ex-gratia payments

These are not appealable and cannot be reviewed by the Administrative Appeals Tribunal.

For more information on the appeal options for current grants, see the Review and appeals section of:

  • Eligibility for AGDRP and NZ DRP
  • Assessing DRA and NZ DRA claims
  • Eligibility and coding the MH17 Family Support Package
  • Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Claims in Customer First

If the customer disagrees with the decision, they can contact the Commonwealth Ombudsman and/or request a waiver of debt through the Department of Finance and Deregulation.
