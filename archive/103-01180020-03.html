For Services Australia social workers only

This page contains information to assist social workers in determining customer eligibility for Income Management as a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR).

VMPR IM eligibility

Determining and recording eligibility for IM as a VWPR by a social worker

Step

Action

1

Vulnerability determination + Read more ...

This process is not relevant for customers who are auto triggered for the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient Income Management measure.

An assessment of vulnerability should be completed at a face-to-face interview.

If this is not possible, the interview can be conducted by telephone.

If neither of these options are available, a determination can be made through a file assessment.

Where a reasonable effort has been made and the delegate is unable to contact a person, the delegate can make an assessment of the customer's circumstances using relevant documents and information in the delegate's possession.

Relevant documents and information could include:

  • Income Management allocations
  • immediate payments
  • payday changes
  • prison incarceration
  • carer information
  • whether the person has, or is, a nominee
  • previous social worker case notes
  • Participation compliance notes
  • medical information

2

Indicators of vulnerability as per policy + Read more ...

  • Financial hardship
    • A person is defined as experiencing financial hardship where they are unable to purchase goods, access services or engage in activities to meet their priority needs, due to a lack of financial resources. The receipt of income support is not, by itself, sufficient evidence that a person is experiencing financial hardship. There must be some additional consideration such as lack of skills or an inability to manage limited resources, which are contributing to a person's financial difficulties
  • Financial exploitation
    • Financial exploitation occurs when a person is subject to undue pressure, harassment, family and domestic violence, deception or exploitation for financial resources by another person or people, including other family members and community members
  • Failure to undertake reasonable self care
    • A failure to undertake reasonable self-care applies if a person is engaged in conduct that threatens their physical or mental wellbeing and the Secretary is satisfied the person has not taken sufficient steps to address the conduct. This conduct may include for example, substance abuse issues or problem gambling
  • Homelessness or risk of homelessness
  • A person may be considered to be homeless or at risk of homelessness where the person does not have, or is at risk of not having access to safe, secure and adequate housing. Examples of these circumstances include:
    • where the person is at risk of eviction from accommodation
    • where a person is sleeping outdoors (sleeping rough) or is sleeping in an abandoned or untenanted building (squatting)
    • where a person lives in, or moves between, temporary accommodation with friends or family, or temporary accommodation provided by a charitable organisation
    • where the person's accommodation damages, or is likely to damage their health
    • where the person's accommodation threatens or is likely to threaten their safety, and
    • where the person's accommodation does not provide the person with access to a reasonable level of personal amenities

Is the customer experiencing one of these indicators of vulnerability?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, Income Management under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) measure is not appropriate. Offer alternative assistance. See Recording Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) assessments in the Social Work Information System (SWIS)

3

Priority needs assessment + Read more ...

Is the customer meeting their priority needs and the priority needs of their partner and/or children or other dependents?

Explore and document what supports are already in place for the customer and the services the customer is engaged with.

Third parties may be contacted if required, preferably with the customer's consent.

If customer has a payment nominee who is not income managed, then they are excluded from being eligible for Income Management, however consider if this arrangement is still suitable and in the best interest of the customer.

  • Yes, Income Management under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) measure is not appropriate. Consider other interventions as appropriate
    • Complete Social Work Information System (SWIS) case notes. See Recording Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) assessments in the Social Work Information System (SWIS) and DOC determination
    • Note: the 'IM Vulnerability not applied' does not require completion
    • If a well-being review/follow up is required, go to Step 9 otherwise, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Is Income Management an appropriate option + Read more ...

Would Income Management be an appropriate support for the customer to meet their responsibilities, to build and maintain self-care and to manage their money?

Examples of other services and mechanisms available include: weekly payments; Rent Deduction Scheme; Centrepay; Nominee Arrangements; Guardianship; financial counselling etc.

At this point of the assessment, Voluntary Income Management is not permitted to be an alternative option under the policy.

If a customer meets the eligibility for Income Management under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) measure, then this is the only form of Income Management able to be implemented under the Overview and hierarchy of income management measures.

  • Yes, the customer can be considered for Income Management under the VWPR measure. Go to Step 5
  • No, consider other interventions as appropriate. Complete a SWIS IM 'Vulnerability not applied' report and DOC. See Recording Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) assessments in the Social Work Information System (SWIS). APS5/6 social workers seek delegation from EL1 social worker (via the QDM process). If a well-being review/follow up is required, go to Step 9, otherwise procedure ends here

5

Record assessment outcome + Read more ...

The assessment outcome must be recorded as follows:

  • Complete SWIS IM 'Vulnerability apply decision' report and DOC. See Recording Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) assessments in the Social Work Information System (SWIS). APS5/6 social workers seek delegation from EL1 social workers (via the QDM process)
  • Tell the customer of outcome and discuss possible pathways out of Income Management, including offers of referral to Financial Management Program Services
  • Tell the customer of their right to apply for a formal review of the determination

APS5/6 social workers - Is a well-being review/follow up required?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, procedure ends here
    • Delegated social workers finalise the 'IM Vulnerability' SWIS report and have the ICT Security Portal access required to complete the Vulnerable Notice workflow. Go to Step 6

6

Launch the Vulnerable Notice workflow + Read more ...

Launch the Vulnerable Notice workflow by selecting the Income Management menu and clicking on Vulnerable Notice.

Did the Interpreter Requirements screen present?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Continue workflow: Interpreter requirements + Read more ...

The Interpreter Requirements screen will present if the customer does not have their interpreter language recorded as English on the Other Contact Details (OCD) screen.

If there is no language recorded on the OCD screen users must exit the workflow and update the OCD screen before the Vulnerable Notice workflow can proceed.

The Interpreter Requirements screen will present each time a new VWPR Notice is added or a VWPR Notice is being reviewed.

Record interpreter details as follows:

Is the customer present: Select Yes or No as appropriate.

Customer's Spoken Language: This will default to the interpreter language recorded on the OCD screen.

Interpreter Required: This will default to Yes.

Is an interpreter being used? Select Yes, No, Not Required or Not Available as appropriate from the drop down menu.

  • If Yes is selected, no other information is required, select 'Continue' and go to Step 8
  • If No is selected, select the applicable reason from the drop down menu:
    • Appointment not booked
    • Conflict of interest
    • Customer declined an interpreter
    • Customer wishes to use agent or family member
    • Deaf or hearing impaired
    • DHS does not have an interpreter who speaks the customer's language
  • If Not available is selected, select the applicable reason from the drop down menu:
    • Interpreter did not attend interview
    • Interpreter service for that language not available
    • Same sex interpreter not available
  • If Not required is selected, select the applicable reason from the drop down menu:
    • No contact required
    • Speaks and understands English

More information: if an interpreter is not available, not being used or not required, additional information can be recorded in addition to the standard reasons selected above. For example, where 'Customer wishes to use agent or family member' is selected, the agent or family member's details can be recorded in this field.

Select Continue.

8

Continue workflow: Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients (VWPR) Notice Details + Read more ...

Complete the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) Notice Details. Users can also refer to the 'Help Text' within the workflow when creating a VWPR Initial Notice.

Initial notice details

Social worker report finalised: Select Yes or No as appropriate.

  • If no is selected, a review will appear on the social worker's AL screen in seven days (keyword INMVWSWR)

Referred by: Select where the referral for a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient assessment was received from the drop down menu:

  • CSA
  • Social worker
  • State housing
  • Community agency
  • Other

This should match the selection in the SWIS 'IM Vulnerability Applied' report.

Type of assessment: Select the method which best suits how the customer's vulnerability was assessed from the drop down menu:

  • Face to face
  • File review
  • Telephone.

Start date: This will default to today's date and is protected.

End date: This will default to 12 months less one day from the VWPR Notice start date and is protected.

Indicators of Vulnerability

This is the reason why the customer is vulnerable or at risk:

  • Financial hardship
  • Financial exploitation
  • Failure to undertake reasonable self-care
  • Homeless or risk of homelessness

Select Yes or No to each indicator as they apply to the customer. At least one of the indicators must be Yes or the workflow cannot proceed.

The indicators may change over the life of the VWPR Notice, but can only be varied after there has been a review of the customer's circumstances.

Priority Needs

Social workers can rank the priority needs in order of relevance for the customer's circumstances. Only those which are relevant need to be ranked and at least one priority need must be selected:

  • School nutrition
  • Food
  • Housing
  • Utilities
  • Clothing and footwear
  • Medical expenses
  • Other

Additional information about the customer's expenses can be recorded in the 'Expense Details' section.

Information provided in this section will be viewable by the Service Officer completing the Income Management Initial Assessment interview. Service Officers will take this ranking into consideration when setting up regular expense allocations but are not required to comply with the ranking.

VWPR eligibility reviews

The following VWPR eligibility reviews can be selected:

  • 3 month
  • 6 month
  • Other (select if a review is required other than at the 3 or 6 stage of a notice. A date must be entered if this review is selected)
  • Pre-exit (mandatory). The default date is 35 days prior to the Notice but can be changed to an earlier date if required (for example, if a customer is transient or lives remotely and will require more time to contact)

If any of the above are selected, a review event will be sent to the social worker who created the VWPR Notice:

  • 3 months from the start date of the VWPR Notice (keyword INMVW3M)
  • 6 months from the start date of the VWPR Notice (keyword INMVW6M)
  • On the date entered for the 'Other' review (keyword INMVWOTH)
  • 35 days (or a date entered by the social worker) before the VWPR Notice end date (keyword INMVWPRE)

These reviews will be listed on the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) Review List and Details screen.

Select Continue and finalise the VWPR Notice via the Income Management Summary screen.

A letter will be generated and sent to the customer requesting they contact Services Australia to arrange an Initial Assessment interview.

A Work Item I053Q1 and Issue 5300 will display on the customer's record stating an Income Management assessment needs to be completed. It will also show the date the customer will automatically commence Income Management as a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient if they do not contact Services Australia. See Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients (VWPR) Income Management Work Items.

A Service Officer will action the flagged Work Item/Issue and conduct the Initial Assessment interview.

9

Consider the need for a wellbeing review + Read more ...

Consider whether the customer may benefit from further follow up following the assessment. This may be particularly useful for those who are to be income managed for the first time.

A Manual Follow Up review can be scheduled within the 'IM Vulnerability Applied' and 'IM Vulnerability Not Applied' SWIS reports.

Entering a date in the 'wellbeing review' field will schedule an activity that will appear on the Activity List (AL) on the date requested, to follow up with the customer.

The keyword for this activity is SWSVWPR and it will go to the Region Office of the social worker who finalised the report.
