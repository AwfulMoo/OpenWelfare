Service delivery arrangements for prisoners approaching release from a correctional centre + Read more ...

Customers may lodge a claim for an income support payment up to 21 days before their expected release date. The payment will be issued on the customer’s next Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) following release.

Service centres near correctional centres have nominated staff (Prison Liaison Officers) to liaise with the prison authorities. Local service arrangements for delivery of CrP should include:

  • The agreed method is for the correctional centre to advise Services Australia of the names of customers in prison due for release within the next 21 days. The Release Notification Ledger (SU517) may be used for this purpose, if the prison doesn't already have a preferred format
  • A method of obtaining applications and other forms from customers, such as whether a Service Officer personally interviews customers (this can be done via phone), or forms are delivered/received via prison welfare officers

For information and links to forms, see the Resources page.

2

Establish Identity Confirmation for prison release customers + Read more ...

Alternative Identity Confirmation process

If the customer has insufficient identity documentation, use the alternative Identity Confirmation process using the Personal Crisis (PC) code. See Alternative Identity.

Note: do not use the Identity Review Period (IRP) code unless they can definitely provide identity documentation within 28 days.

Types of Proof of Identity (POI) prison release customers may have

Prison release customers may not be aware of what documents are approved identity documents. In addition to common identity documents, ask if they have any of the following:

  • Justice or other lease agreement (LA). Prisoners living in supported accommodation with a local prison or a justice health agency that has a housing strategy for prisoners, can use their 'lease agreement' (LA)
  • Discharge or Release Certificates with their photo and/or signature (PA)
  • Qualifications completed while in custody educational or trade certificates, educational reports or references or student cards

Prison release certificates with photo or signature are approved identity documents that count towards establishing the customer's identity. Certificates that do not meet these criteria can only be used towards Alternative Identity.

When the customer provides identity documents, take care to check it is acceptable Identity Confirmation to help prevent fraud. The customer's identity must be satisfied before the claim can proceed.

If the customer has any names/aliases or alternative identity information not recorded on their Centrelink record, update the Customer Person Details Summary (CPDS) screen. Correctional Services departments are a trusted third party data source.

For how to code the details, see Aliases and other names (legal, preferred, sex and gender diverse customers, cultural considerations etc.).

3

Assess new claim or restoration for a primary payment + Read more ...

To be eligible for Crisis Payment (CrP) a person must be qualified for a social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance and the payment is payable.

  • Upon receipt of the completed claim forms, assess the customer's qualification for a new claim for a primary payment or a restoration of primary payment
  • If the customer qualifies, grant the payment with the date of commencement being the expected date of release
  • Record grant or reject decision and any action taken on a DOC. Ensure the advice letter is correctly addressed or delivered to the customer

Disability Support Pension (DSP)

  • Customers may have their record suspended due to imprisonment (SUS-IMP) for up to 2 years. In these cases a new claim is not required. Instead, restore the record from the date of release
    If the payment is to be restored and the customer is partnered, check the payment rate and Marital Status (MS) screen to ensure a relationship qualifier code: 'GAC' (In Gaol) is not present. If present, see Assessment of circumstances for customers with partner in prison for further instruction
  • The Resources page contains links to contact details for release of medical information, the form to request information for Queensland and an Authority for release of information generic fax template if required by the prison/corrective services
  • Medical records can assist with DSP claims, and help the customer's doctor to assess them for ongoing medication. Usually prisoners are released with up to 3 days medication. It may be difficult for a new doctor to assess some illnesses or medical conditions, for example schizophrenia, in such a short period

Parenting Payment (PP)

  • PP should generally not be granted with a future start date for parents about to be released from prison. This is because the customer will not ordinarily have a dependent child in their care whilst they are in custody
  • However, if the customer has the child with them in custody, and the child remains with them when released, PP can be restored or granted upon the customers release from prison
  • In the majority of cases JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) should be assessed for these customers before their release from prison
  • PP backdating rules allow a claim to be backdated to the date the child came into the customer's care, if the claim for PP is made within 28 days of the child entering care
  • If the child does come into the customer's care and the customer qualifies for PP on the day of release, the customer should receive a 'top up' of the difference between PP and the payment they were granted (JSP or YA) prior to their release. The difference in rates of CrP should also be topped up

Is the customer qualified for a social security pension, social security benefit or ABSTUDY Living Allowance (and this is payable)?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, customer is not eligible for CrP. Offer other assistance if available

Customer does not want to pursue claim + Read more ...

A claim for CrP - PRI is not pursued where the customer identified they have incorrectly enquired about CrP.

6

Checking customer’s relationship status + Read more ...

Check the customer’s relationship status. Due to system limitations, CrPs for partnered customers will need to be processed post release. Claims processed pre-release will result in an incorrect rate of payment.

Prison Liaison staff are to tell the customer they must attend a service centre on the day of release to lodge and process their claim. Customers who have entered into a new relationship after they were imprisoned are required to verify their relationship status via a Partner Details (Mod P) form.

If a prisoner indicates under parole they need to attend a rehabilitation program for a set period or has restrictions on where they can live, consider a single rate of payment under section 24 provisions. See References page for a link to Guide to Social Security Law, 2.2.5.50, Discretion to treat a Person as Not Being a Member of a Couple for a Special reason.

Does the customer require a change to their relationship status?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Code a Manual review + Read more ...

Code a future manual review to ensure the Marital Status (MS) screen is updated and that relationship details have been verified to ensure the correct rate of payment.

On the Review Registration (RVR) screen code:

  • The RevRsn: field as 'REL'
  • Timeframe for review: 14 days after the date of release
  • Text: Partner released from prison on XX/XX/XXXX. Check for return of Mod P for verification of relationship status. If applicable, link record to partner's CRN. Ensure correct coding on MS screen to reflect relationship status

The purpose of the review is to ensure the customer and partner records are linked from the date that the customer is released from prison and the correct CrP, Anticipated Payment and partnered rate of payment is made from that date.

Create a DOC on the record indicating that a Mod P must be issued when customer attends the service centre post-release. DOC that a manual review has already been coded for the return of the Mod P.

Advise the customer to attend a service centre once they are released.

Procedure ends here.

Start Assisted Customer Claim + Read more ...

Open the ACC via the Assisted Customer Claim desktop icon.

  • Enter the customer’s CRN
  • Select Make a new claim
  • Read the privacy message to the customer and ask if they want to hear the full privacy agreement. Read this to the customer if requested. The customer must agree to the privacy agreement prior to commencing the claim
  • Select Crisis Payment
  • Select Apply for Crisis Payment for Release from Prison or Psychiatric confinement
  • Read the questions to the customer and select their answer
  • Ask the customer if they can provide evidence of their bank account balance and the extreme circumstance for example Prison release documentation (if they can’t, the claim can still be submitted and a provided within 14 days)
  • A screenshot of the customer’s bank balance from their mobile banking app is acceptable if it shows the customer’s first and/or last name
  • If a pre-release customer cannot provide evidence of their bank account balance while imprisoned, Prison Liaison Officers can use their discretion to decide if they agree that the customer is in severe financial hardship. The customer should be advised to provide evidence within 14 days. If, due to information on the customer’s record or during conversation with the customer, the Prison Liaison Officer believes that the customer may not be in severe financial hardship, then the customer should be advised to claim CrP - PRI once they are released. They should be advised to bring evidence of their bank balance so that the claim can be assessed
  • If the customer can provide evidence (bank account, extreme circumstance), ask them if they are able to upload it to their Centrelink Online account
  • If the customer can’t provide any required proof/evidence at this time, advise them that they have 14 days to upload it to their Centrelink Online account (preferred option). If customers are unable to upload these to their Centrelink Online account they can also be provided in a Service Centre or sent in via post
  • No manual review is required for evidence and no follow up is required if the customer isn’t able to, or doesn’t supply evidence within 14 days of being released. This only applies to pre-release claims being assessed by Prison Liaison Officers. Evidence is required for post-release claims

Check that all information is correct on the Review your claim page.

  • If answers need to be corrected, select the relevant Update button
  • If personal details need to be corrected, exit the ACC and update using the Change in Contact Details workflow. This information will then populate into the claim
  • Go back into the ACC

Verbal claim declaration

Select my obligations hyperlink (bottom of review page) and read the claim declaration to the customer.

  • Select the ‘I have read, understood and accept my obligations’ check box
  • Record the following information on the customer’s record via Note in Process Direct: Verbal signature declaration script read and accepted by customer/nominee. CDF not required’
  • Select Submit

Advise the customer:

  • A service officer will process their claim within 2 business days
  • They will receive a letter with the outcome of their claim in their Centrelink Online account if they have one, or via post if they don’t

‘SR598 - Customer may not be in Australia’

Confirm eligibility criteria - customer in Prison for 14 days or more + Read more ...

The customer must have:

  • spent 14 or more days in prison or psychiatric confinement.
  • been charged with committing an offence

Note: customers who have been in psychiatric confinement but have not been charged with committing an offence are not eligible for CrP - PRI.

To identify the customer’s admission and release date:

  • Check the ABH or PNSH screen and check the imprisonment code and date of event
  • Check DOCs on the customer’s record. The prison admission script generates a doc as part of the prison admission review which will include an admission date
  • Check the customer’s release documentation for an admission and release date
  • If the prison admission date cannot be identified on the customer’s record, the period in custody may be able to be confirmed by contacting the Prison Liaison Officer for that prison, or the prison/institution directly
  • Check that the customer has not already received a CrP in the 14 days preceding the event date on the CRISIS_PS screen

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, customer is not eligible, reject the claim
    • Update the Manual rejection field to L14 - Period of imprisonment is less than fourteen (14) days

Payment of CrP + Read more ...

Payment should be made via the New Payments Platform (NPP).

If the claim is selected to go to QMA, add 'Crisis Payment should be paid via NPP - QMO to action after grant' to the DOC.

If NPP is unavailable, payment should be made by Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS).

Direct credit can also be used if NPP and RTGS are not available.

For Farm Household Assistance (FHA) customers:

Approval of the customer's Social Service Plan (SSP) must occur before paying the CrP. The system will auto approve this within 15 minutes. To check if the SSP has been approved, see Navigate to SSP. Once approved, pay the CrP via RTGS. Otherwise, the payment will deliver via direct credit the following business day.

Changes to release date

If the prison advises of a new release date and the CrP has not been finalised:

  • If the new release date is unknown or more than 21 days after the original claim was made, reject the claim and a new claim will be required with the amended release date. DOC the record accordingly
  • If the new release date is within 21 days of the original claim, change the ‘expected release date’ field in PD to the new release date

If the prison advises of a new release date and the CrP has already been finalised and paid:

  • A debt will need to be raised and a new claim will be required for the amended release date. See Raising Subsection 1223AA debts for prepayments, including prison release payments, Crisis Payment (CrP) and Anticipated Payment

Description

1

Hardship advance or anticipated payment + Read more ...

If a customer has been released from prison or psychiatric confinement and is in severe financial hardship, it may be appropriate to offer the customer a hardship advance of the first instalment of their regular payment. See Making a decision about a hardship advance or anticipated payment.

2

Defer debt recovery for 28 days + Read more ...

Customers who are in prison should have the recovery of their debts deferred until release. Upon release, customers typically need further deferment until they can negotiate a debt repayment arrangement.

If a customer who has been released from prison has an outstanding debt; Prison Liaison Officers (PLO) with access to the Debt Recovery script can temporarily write-off the debt for 28 days. This allows the customer to receive full entitlement for 2 pay periods and minimises hardship. See Prison Release under Debt Recovery general information.

PLOs can tell the customer that there are a number of repayment options to repay their debt. The PLO can negotiate for a preferred payment option on the customer's behalf. If a PLO is not available prior to release, the customer can use their Centrelink online account or contact the Debt Management Branch to organise repayment.

See:

  • Identifying and managing debt activities on the system
  • Temporary write-off of debts

3

Reduction or suspension of advance payment recovery + Read more ...

If the customer has been released from prison and meets the financial hardship criteria for CrP they are eligible for a reduction or suspension in recovery of an existing advance payment for 4 weeks. See Changes to the repayment rate of an advance payment.

4

Give a temporary exemption from mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

Prison release customers generally find it difficult to look for work immediately after their release as many need to sort out basic personal needs such as travelling home, permanent accommodation, food, clothing and medical assessments. See Exemptions from mutual obligation requirements for prison release customers.

5

Centrepay offer + Read more ...

Offer Centrepay.

Customers released from prison usually have a higher than average debt and Centrepay may assist with budgeting.

Tell the customer that Centrepay is a free service that allows for voluntary deductions from customer's Centrelink payments to cover bills such as electricity, gas, telephone, water, court fines, some chemist methadone programs, whitegoods and education fees. See Centrepay.

6

Update circumstances on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen + Read more ...

Information recorded on the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen alerts Service Officers and Employment Service Providers to job seekers who may have a higher risk of non-compliance, such as released prisoners.

See Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirement.

If the customer was not in prison for at least 14 days, check if other categories should be recorded, for example drug and alcohol dependency, homelessness; lack of literacy and numeracy skills; psychiatric problems or mental illness. See Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirement.
