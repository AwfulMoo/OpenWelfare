This page contains information in relation to recording additional information in the Participation Compliance workflow for Employment Services Providers and ParentsNext providers.

Explaining why a financial penalty has not been applied to providers

Item

Description

1

When should further information about a rejection reason be recorded? + Read more ...

Section 202 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 provides Services Australia with the authority to disclose information to Employment Services Providers and ParentsNext providers when information will affect the:

  • provider's capacity to perform their duties
  • provider's capacity to deliver efficient and effective service to the job seeker, or
  • job seeker's capacity to access services to which they are eligible

The Service Officer is expected to record relevant free text information for providers in the Participation Compliance workflow, when:

  • a non-compliance event is invalid due to insufficient evidence, insufficient documentation or an incorrect incident date
  • a decision is made to reject a financial penalty due to an inappropriate requirement or when work did not meet the minimum standards and conditions and was unsuitable. The provider is responsible for assessing whether a requirement is suitable prior to reporting a non-compliance event. However, in cases when a decision is made not to apply a financial penalty for these reasons, the additional information will assist the providers understanding and effectiveness of the service they provide in the future
  • the rejection reason code and/or the auto text generated based on the rejection reason code does not adequately describe the rejection reason. In these cases additional information needs to be included to assist the provider to appropriately service the job seeker
  • the provider may need to review how they engage with the job seeker to reduce the likelihood of future non-compliance. If the job seeker's circumstances suggest that future compliance action may also be rejected unless a change in servicing is undertaken, for example, use of an interpreter, this feedback should be given to the provider so appropriate action can be taken, or
  • the rejection reason indicates the provider may need to review the job seeker's compulsory requirements or Job Plan. Providing further clarifying information may assist the provider to determine what action to take in this regard

2

When is it not appropriate to record further information about a rejection reason? + Read more ...

It is not appropriate for the Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer to insert information that is not relevant to the:

  • provider's capacity to perform their duties
  • provider's capacity to deliver an efficient and effective service to the job seeker, or
  • job seeker's capacity to meet requirements or access services they are eligible for

It is also not appropriate for the following to be recorded:

  • information that does not specifically relate to the job seeker and the non-compliance event under consideration
  • information that is not professional in nature, is biased or cannot be supported
  • information that is inaccurate or misleading, or
  • sensitive personal information, for example, sensitive medical conditions, relationship history or personal financial history

3

How do I write free text information? + Read more ...

Free text comments should:

  • only include relevant information that will enable the provider to deliver effective services to the job seeker
  • be succinct
  • be written in a professional manner, and
  • be free from jargon

Free text should not include special characters (for example, '<', '>', ':', '&' or ','). These characters will not transition appropriately to the Employment Services System (ESS) which may affect the meaning of the text content.

Free text should also not include abbreviations or acronyms. Providers may not be familiar with them and this may cause unnecessary confusion and additional contacts from a provider to clarify.

4

Disclosing personal information without the job seeker's permission + Read more ...

The confidentiality provisions in section 202 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 enable the Service Officer to disclose information regarding a job seeker to the provider when disclosure is determined as reasonably necessary to assist the provider to deliver a more efficient, effective or appropriate service to the job seeker or to allow the job seeker to access the services for which they may be eligible.

Personal information that Services Australia discloses to a provider in order for them to perform their duties is considered protected information. What the provider does with this information is protected by the Social Security (Administration) Act, 1999. This means it would be an offence for the provider to use or disclose free text information relating to a rejection reason for a purpose not covered under section 202 of the Social Security (Administration) Act, 1999.

While the PST-skilled Service Officer is not required to obtain the job seeker's express consent to release information to the provider it may sometimes be appropriate to:

  • discuss the benefits of the provider being aware of the personal circumstance with the job seeker, and
  • determine the level of information to be disclosed to their provider

The personal information recorded by the Service Officer to be transmitted to the provider should be succinct and focussed on how the provider could continue to support the job seeker. See the Resources page for an example.

When a job seeker is adamant they do not wish any personal information to be transmitted to their provider, exercise judgment over whether or not the provision of the information is necessary for the provider to deliver effective services. When sensitive personal information is not needed in order for the provider to deliver effective services, the specifics of the personal issue should not be disclosed. General advice relating to the reason for not applying the financial penalty may still need to be provided. The PST-skilled Service Officer should explain to the job seeker the rationale for disclosing information to their provider.

Consideration should also be given to updating the Job Seeker Classification Instrument (JSCI) and/or reviewing other suitable referrals. See the Resources page for an example.

5

Recording free text if the rejection reason has already been discussed with the provider + Read more ...

A provider may be aware a financial penalty will not be applied when:

  • the provider has contacted to withdraw a Participation Report, or
  • the PST-skilled Service Officer has contacted the provider to discuss the financial penalty

In these cases there is still an expectation the PST-skilled Service Officer will record a succinct summary of any identified issues that affect the job seeker's capacity to meet requirements or access provider services or that will assist the provider in effective and efficient servicing if this is available. For example, relevant information may be available when:

  • a compliance investigation commenced prior to provider contact
  • job seeker contact has not occurred, however the PST-skilled Service Officer has identified relevant information in the system whilst finalising the compliance action

If there is no additional information that will impact the job seeker's capacity to meet requirements or access provider services or assist the provider to deliver an effective and efficient service, free text information is not required.
