Current year FTB arrears generated + Read more ...

Check the reason for current year arrears being generated. This can be done by going to the Rate Summary (RATS) screen by keying a ‘T’ next to the result on the Assessment Results (AR) screen) and/or by viewing the Payment Summary (PS) screen.

Are the arrears being paid due to Prohibition (PIP) previously being applied to customer’s record?

(Payment Summary (PS) will display as follows if this was the case

Example only: FAO 4 JUL 16 0.00 0.00 Regular 30 JUN 11 PIP Prohibited)

  • Yes, and the customer is currently in receipt of FTB:
    • the arrears should be paid
    • note all information required to document the customer’s record in regards to the FTB reconciliation result/s and the current year arrears payable
    • finalise the activity
    • document the record using the #ATOVER Fast Note template. Include the amount payable, and relevant period of any current year arrears, and the reason the arrears are payable. Procedure ends here
  • Yes, and FTB has cancelled due to income (CAN/INC):
    • the arrears generated due to Prohibited (PIP) being lifted should be zeroed out via the Period Rates Summary (RATS)/Period Rate Components (RAC) screen. This is done by keying a ‘T’ next to the arrears result on the AR screen, and then an ‘R’ next to the period where the arrears are being generated. Key the amount of $0.00 and reason of Adjustment (ADJ) for all periods where arrears are being generated
    • note all information required to document the customer’s record in regards to the FTB reconciliation result/s and the current year arrears payable
    • finalise the activity
    • document the record using the #ATOVER Fast Note template. Include the amount and relevant period of any current year arrears, and the reason the arrears have been zeroed. Procedure ends here
  • No, arrears may not be payable:
    • if the arrears are related to income changes or maintenance income, they must be zeroed out via the RATS/RAC screen. This is done by keying a ‘T’ next to the arrears result on the AR screen, and then an ‘R’ next to the period where the arrears are being generated. Key the amount of $0.00 and reason of Adjustment (ADJ) for all periods where arrears are being generated
    • document the record using the #ATOVER Fast Note template. Include the amount of current year arrears that were zeroed, the relevant period of these arrears, and the reason the arrears were zeroed (for example, ‘arrears of $123.45 for period 12/12/18 – 20/12/18 were zeroed as they relate to maintenance income changes’). Procedure ends here

Arrears generated for other payment/s + Read more ...

Is the period the arrears being generated for the Income Support Payment more than 13 weeks ago?

  • Yes,
    • arrears are to be zeroed out via the Period Rates Summary (RATS)/Period Rate Components (RAC) screen (see Step 5 for instructions). Reason to be included when Rate assessment explanation screen is produced is ‘Arrears not payable as outside allowable time frame’
    • finalise the activity
    • document the record using the #ATOVER Fast Note template. Include the amount and relevant period of any current year arrears, and the reason the arrears were zeroed. Procedure ends here
  • No,
    • contact the Access Support Centre (select appropriate payment stream) for assistance with determining if the arrears generated are correct. The Resources page contains a link to the contact details
    • take corrective action if required
    • finalise the activity
    • document the record using the #ATOVER Fast Note template. Include the amount, and relevant period of any current year arrears, and the reason the arrears are/are not payable. Procedure ends here

Current year FTB debt generated + Read more ...

Check the reason for the current year debt being calculated. Is the debt qualification related (for example resulting from a change of care, shared care, child education details, changes to rent or overseas absence)?

  • Yes,
    • if the debt amount is less than $50, this can be waived (WVD) on the ASC screen (key ‘Z’ in the Go to field on the AR screen to navigate to ASC). If a qualification debt has been waived, also include the Act reference in the DOC (Section 99 of the Family Assistance and Administration Act The Reference page contains a link to the legislation)
    • if the debt amount is more than $50, the debt must be sent for investigation (key ‘Z’ in the Go to field on the AR screen to navigate to ASC, and code INV on the ASC screen)
  • No,
    • as the debt is related to income or maintenance income, this can be zeroed out via the Period Rates Summary (RATS)/Period Rate Components (RAC) screen (see Step 5 for details) to be included in reconciliation

Document the record using the #ATOVER Fast Note template. Include the amount, and relevant period of any current year arrears/debts, and the reason the arrears are/are not payable.

Procedure ends here.

Debt generated for other payment/s + Read more ...

Is the debt being generated for a period in the past (that is there is no change to the customer’s current Income Support Payment (ISP) entitlement and the activity is not cancelling/rejecting ISP from date of grant)?

  • Yes,
    • the debt is to be sent for investigation (INV) via the Assessment Consequences (ASC) screen. The debt shell will be automatically mapped to the relevant debt team once the activity has been finalised
  • No,
    • contact the Access Support Centre (select appropriate payment stream) for assistance with checking the result, to determine if debt/cancellation or rejection is correct. The Resources page contains a link to the contact details

Document the record using the #ATOVER Fast Note template. Include the amount, and relevant period of any current year arrears/debts, and action taken, for example debt sent for investigation).

Procedure ends here.

Newborn Supplement (NBS) and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) offsetting being applied + Read more ...

Follow the below process if the NBS/NBU start date and eligibility period is being altered (Period Rate Components (RAC) offsetting occurs so that the customer is not paid a duplicate amount of NBS/NBU).

The NBS period may be altered for the following reasons:

  • Eligibility start date is found to be an earlier date

Example: a child is born on 1 March 2016 and the customer claims NBS. The income estimate for the 2015/2016 period is above the income threshold, however income is below the threshold for the 2016/2017 financial year. The NBS start date is 1 July 2016 and the customer is paid the 16/17 rate for 91 days.

When 2015/2016 reconciliation is finalised the income is found to be less than the estimate, and the customer meets the 2015/2016 FTB A income test. The NBS start date then moves back to the child’s DOB 1 March 2016. The NBS is now included in the 2015/2016 reconciliation assessment.

The amended NBS period falls into the 2015/2016 period.

The NBS rate is now paid at the 15/16 rate for 91 days.

Example of NBS offsetting:

  • 2015/2016 NBS adjustment: +523.25
  • 2016/2017 NBS adjustment: -532.35
  • Difference: -9.10

The above offsetting will occur within Period Rates Summary (RATS)/Period Rate Components (RAC) and the overall NBS result on AR will be an overpayment of -$9.10

  • Eligibility period is moved to a later date

Example: a child is born 1 April 2016 and the customer claims NBS. The 2015/2016 income estimate meets income test and NBS is paid from the child’s DOB 1 April 2016.

The NBS is paid at the 15/16 rate per day for 91 days.

When 2015/2016 reconciliation occurs, it is found that the actual income precludes entitlement, however the estimate in the following financial year meets the FTB A income test requirement and therefore the NBS start date is moved to the later date of 1 July 2016.

The NBS is now paid at the 16/17 rate per day for 91 days.

NBS offsetting

  • 2015/2016 NBS adjustment: -523.25
  • 2016/2017 NBS adjustment: +532.35
  • Difference: +9.10

The above offsetting will occur within RATS/RAC and the overall NBS result on AR will be an arrears payment of $9.10.

Check the offsetting for correctness and note the reason for the change.

See Eligibility for Newborn Supplement (NBS) and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) for examples of the NBS eligibility period being amended.

Go to Step 10.

  • Key ‘O’ beside the ‘reco completed’ line. This opens the FTB Withholding for Debt Recovery (FWDC) screen, which displays a breakdown of the reconciliation amounts, including supplement amounts, and amount of any components withheld to repay existing debts. Make a note of these amounts. Press [Enter] twice to return to the FRCS screen
  • From 10 June 2019, FTB top-ups, supplements, lump sum claim amounts and instalment arrears can be used to recover any family assistance, social security, student assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme payment debts.     • The Debt Offset Summary (OPOS) screen displays a list of each offsetting occurrence that exists for a particular debt in context
  • 'S'elect the reconciliation line. This opens the FTB Calculation Result (FACRF) screen, which displays the total amounts paid and entitled for each FTB component for each reconciliation calculation period. Scroll up/down to view all periods. This also shows if the customer had a More Choice for Families (MCFF) payment choice in place (along with the FTB Payment Choices (FPC) screen). This may assist in explaining why the large arrears have been generated
  • On the FACRF screen, 'S'elect the earliest reconciliation line available (normally 1 July of the financial year being reconciled). This could be a different date if customer only commenced receiving payments part way through the financial year). This opens the FTB Calculation Period Assessment (FCPA) screen, which displays:
    • calculation periods
    • child count display selector
    • FTB A & B status
    • Income amount selector (select the FTB (A) Status to navigate to the FTB Calculation Period Assessment Part A (FCPAA) screen)
  • 'S'elect the FTB income field, which opens the FICS screen. This will tell what type of income has been used (AIN, EST, EAP or MRE). Note the amount/s and type for both the customer and partner. Press [Enter] to go back to the FCPAA screen
  • 'S'elect ‘Child Details Display’, which opens to the FTB Child Eligibility Summary (FCES) screen. This screen displays details of child/ren and whether there is a blend, shared care, maintenance action met etc. Ensure the details are correct. Press [Enter] twice to go back to the FCPA screen

5

Check the Auto/man/special adjustment details + Read more ...

  • ‘S’elect the manual/auto/special adjustment line. The FTB Total Adjustment Period Display (FTAPD) screen will display
  • Key a ‘Y’ in the Go to FCRAS field at the bottom of the FTAPD screen

Continue checking the manual/auto/special adjustment + Read more ...

  • If there is a debt ID - check the details through via the Debt List (OPDL) screen, by selecting the debt and typing Modify/Assess Debt (OPMA) screen in the Nxt field before pressing [Enter]. The OPMA screen provides the Source Activity ID (AMR) of the source activity

7

Continue checking the reconciliation result + Read more ...

  • ‘S’elect the FTB income field and note the type of income/amount for both the customer and partner:
    • AIN - Actual Taxable Income
    • MRE - Most recent estimate
    • FYE - Financial year estimate
  • Hit [Enter] 3 times to return to the FACRF screen
  • Key a ‘B’ beside a Period line on the FACRF screen and Page Up/Page down to see a breakdown of payments issued, that is, FTB (A) or (B), LFS or RA. This may assist in determining which payment has been overpaid
  • Key a ‘K’ beside a period line on the (FACRF) screen to navigate to the FAO Data Changed (FACHK) screen. This may also provide an indication as to what has changed between the previous reconciliation result and the current reconciliation outcome
  • For further details about child eligibility, ‘S’elect a line from the FACRF screen and ‘S’elect the ‘Child Details Display’ for further details about child eligibility

Go to Step 8.

8

Continue checking the reconciliation result + Read more ...

  • If the estimated income is less than the actual income used in reconciliation, this will be the most likely cause of the large debt
  • If a child has left care, and there were periods of payment made between the date child left care and the date of notification, this may have caused the debt
  • Maintenance action changes are other possible causes of debts
  • If a MCFF Payment Choice has been elected this can be seen on the FTB Calculation Result (FACRF) screen
  • The type of Payment Choice can be checked via the FTB Payment Choices (FPC) screen

Go to Step 9.

9

Check and finalise the activity + Read more ...

  • Once a determination has been made that the debt is correct, and the reason/s for the debt established, navigate to the AR screen
  • Finalise the activity by keying a ‘Y’ (Yes) in the Finished field
  • Document the record using the #LGEDBT Fast Note template in Customer First
  • Check for and complete any associated Customer First SAP Interaction Record/s
