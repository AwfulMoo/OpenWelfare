1

Staff must check they are in the correct customer record + Read more ...

Due to the number of claims assessed and processed by staff, staff must check they are in the correct customer record before.

  • launching the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow, and
  • finalising a customer's claim

This will make sure that information is not incorrectly transferred/entered from one customer record to another, and prevents intertwined records. If staff identify an intertwined record, see Intertwined Centrelink records.

Are staff in the correct customer record?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No,
    • do not continue processing the claim
    • if updates have been made to, for example, payment destination details or address details, make sure those updates belong to the correct customer record - if not, revert details to the correct details for the customer
    • procedure ends here for this customer record

2

Check if customer has a payment nominee arrangement + Read more ...

If nominee arrangements were checked as part of assessing a phone claim, go to Step 3

To check go to the Nominee Link Summary (NOLS) screen. If the customer has:

  • no nominee go to Step 3
  • a correspondence nominee - code NOC, go to Step 3
  • a payment nominee - code NOP. When staff
    • run the workflow they will not be able to finalise the workflow
    • get to the Disaster Details screen, they will note 'the customer's claim could not be finalised as they have a payment nominee arrangement in place, and
    • get to the Claim Decision screen, they will select Follow-up and add the keyword NOM to the claim
    • go to Step 3

3

Dad and Partner Pay (DAP), Parental Paid Leave (PPL) or pending, assessed or suspended income support payment (ISP) + Read more ...

When assessing the COVID-19 Disaster Payment claim, did staff note that a DAP/PPL customer would need to be contacted by a DAP/PPL trained staff or a customer with a pending, assessed or suspended ISP would need to be contacted by an ISP trained staff?

  • Yes,
    • any DAP/PPL/ISP claim will need to be cancelled
      - go to the Activity List (AL) screen and cancel the pending DAP/PPL/ISP claim. This claim will be re-indexed later by trained DAP/PPL/ISP staff
    • when staff not trained in DAP/PPL/ISP get to the coding instructions below for the:
      - Disaster Details screen, note the claim will be directed to a DAP/PPL/ISP trained staff member
      - Claim Decision screen, select claim for Follow-up and add the keyword PPLDAPCP (for DAP/PPL customers) or ISPNCL (for customers with a pending, assessed or suspended ISP claim)
    • go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

Launch workflow - Customer Claims screen + Read more ...

In the customer's record in Customer First, launch the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow.

If the customer has a claim in progress, the Customer Claims screen will show:

  • claim ID
  • event details
  • date claim lodged

  • available actions

Note: a started claim should never be picked up from the Activity List (AL) screen, as this will not allow the workflow to complete. Does the customer have a claim in progress?

  • Yes, select the claim and go to Step 7
  • No, select the New Claim button, go to Step 5

Privacy message + Read more ...

All customers must read/hear and acknowledge the COVID-19 declaration, privacy and acknowledgement statements. This must be done before running the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow.

Phone claims

  • For COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims, staff with access to the Automated Messages icon in Services Australia Workspace can play recorded statements otherwise the statements are in the relevant verbal claim template to read to the customer. See Table 1 Phone claims - pre-claim checks in Assessing COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims.
  • For Income support payment customers - COVID-19 Disaster Payment claims, the statements are in the relevant ISP COVID-19 Disaster Pymt VERBL CLM Fast Note.

Do not read the privacy message on this screen as it does not meet requirements for the payment.

Online claims

  • Customers read and acknowledge the privacy statement as part of submitting their online claim.

For all customers:

  • Select The customer has read/been read the privacy notice
  • Select The customer wishes to continue
  • For phone claims only, make sure the relevant COVID-19 Disaster Pymt Verbal Claim / ISP COVID-19 Disaster Pymt VERBL CLM Fast Note shows the customer agrees to the privacy message

6

Disaster Payment screen + Read more ...

For online claims, staff may need to return to the customer's online claim details to make sure they know:

  • which event and payment type to select
  • the date the customer lodged their online claim with Services Australia

Staff must select the correct claim event and payment type (as per the staff members assessment of the customer's online or phone claim details) to make sure the customer receives the correct amount and correct letter.

For customers receiving Home Equity Access Scheme (the Scheme) payments, select the income support payment (ISP) customers workflow for the customer’s relevant area and relevant period. All claims where a customer is receiving the Scheme will be:

  • selected for follow-up when staff get to the Claim Decision screen (using the keyword ‘PE’)
  • escalated to Technical Peer Support, who will complete a payment query template
    • Note: the Level 2 Helpdesk will make a decision on eligibility. If it is decided the customer is not receiving an ISP, specialist staff will cancel the workflow and run the correct one

Workflows are only available when a claiming period for a relevant period starts.

See Event codes and workflows for event codes, workflow names, payment rates and claiming periods

If the customer is eligible to receive a payment, they will receive the full rate of $500 or $325/$600 or $375/$750 or $450 or $200, depending on:

  • if the hours lost as a result of the restricted movement/lockdown was more than or less than 20 hours in the 'relevant period'
  • the event/relevant period the customer is claiming for
  • if they are in receipt of an income support payment

Staff must not select any Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment workflow.

When the correct workflow has been selected:

  • key the date the claim was received (date of receipt):
    • for phone claims, the date the customer called Services Australia to lodge the claim
    • for online clams, the date the online claim was lodged with Services Australia
  • record the source of claim:
    • Phone for phone claims
    • Online for online claims
  • select Continue

9

Disaster Details screen + Read more ...

Effect on customer

  • Select the check box next to Income affected as a result of the event
  • Enter details in the free text box as to how the customer has 'verified their eligibility', or if the customer is not eligible why they do not meet the eligibility requirements. Document:
    • if claimed under the wrong event
    • has advised they have been unable to work and therefore, have not been able to earn an income
    • results of any geospatial check
    • has or has not already received a payment for the same 'relevant period'
    • if the customer is or is not receiving an income support payment (and whether this is being paid at the nil rate)
    • if the customer has reported income
    • is or is not an Australian resident or eligible visa holder. If an eligible visa holder, record the customer's visa number
    • has met the 'unable to work and therefore unable to earn an income' criteria depending on the event and relevant period the customer has claimed for
    • can or cannot access leave entitlements that cover the entire 'relevant period'
    • if the liquid assets test was met (for relevant period 1 Sydney and Greater Sydney claims only)
    • if the customer is not eligible, why they are not eligible
    • is eligible, how they have met the eligibility criteria
    • if a claim is to be selected for follow-up, for example, if:
      - the customer's claim cannot be finalised as a payment nominee arrangement is in place
      - a DAP/PPL/ISP trained staff member needs to contact the customer to discuss their COVID-19 Disaster Payment claim
      - a payment query template has been escalated to the Level 2 Helpdesk with reasons why, for example, customer is receiving the Home Equity Access Scheme
      - PLDPCDP keyword is to be applied
    • Note: do not use characters such as #,/,@. This can give errors at the end of the workflow

For the question 'date affected by the event', record the first date of the relevant period the customer is claiming for the following events:

  • Tasmania
  • Northern Territory
  • Australian Capital Territory
  • Victoria (August 2021)
  • Queensland
  • South Australia
  • Victoria (July 2021)
  • New South Wales
  • Income support payment customers - Northern Territory
  • Income support payment customers - Australian Capital Territory
  • Income support payment customers - Victoria (August 2021)
  • Income support payment customers - Queensland
  • Income support payment customers - NSW
  • Income support payment customers – Tasmania

For Victoria (4-10 June 2021):

Customer advised the relevant period they are claiming for, started:

  • 3 June 2021 or earlier, calculate if the 7 days from the start date covers any period between 4-10 June 2021 inclusive. If:
    • Yes, record 4 June 2021 in 'date affected by the event'
    • No (the period ends before 4 June), record the date provided by the customer in 'date affected by the event'. The customer is not eligible to receive a payment
  • between 4-10 June 2021 inclusive, record 4 June 2021 in 'date affected by the event'
  • 11 June or later, record the date noting that a future date cannot be recorded

For online claims, customers must not be called to:

  • confirm the 'date affected by the event', unless advised to by the Level 2 Helpdesk
  • tell them of a claim decision

Social Worker contact needed

Online claims - always select 'No'.

Phone claims:

  • only select 'Yes' where the customer has asked for a social worker to contact them
  • selecting 'Yes' in this screen will create a SWO referral DOC
  • After a claim is finalised, this will map to a social worker to complete and action
  • if a customer's claim is not being finalised today the SWO referral DOC will not map to a social worker to complete. This will only happen after the claim is finalised
  • if a customer needs a more immediate referral to a social worker, see Social work service referral for contact details to the social worker phone queue using Services Australia Workspace.

13

Claim Decision screen + Read more ...

All online claims that do not meet eligibility requirements can be rejected, except when the customer has a pending income support payment

Select from 3 options below:

  • Grant if the customer meets all eligibility criteria
    • Staff must not contact a customer to advise their claim has been granted
    • customers will receive a decision letter
    • go to Step 15
  • Follow-up:
    • if 1 payment has already been issued today under the Flexible Support Payment (FSP)
    • if a payment query has been escalated to the Level 2 Helpdesk using the payment query template, for example, customer is receiving the Home Equity Access Scheme
    • if the customer has a pending Income Support Payment for any COVID-19 Disaster Payment event at the higher rate, not the Income support payment customers – COVID-19 Disaster Payment ($200 payment)
    • if the customer was paid Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment up to 14 days before the COVID-19 Disaster Payment relevant period started however the last day of self-isolation/quarantine is at least 1 day before the relevant period claimed
    • when the customer has a payment nominee arrangement in place
      - do not select phone claims for follow-up that do not meet the eligibility criteria. These claims are not processed in the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow
    • claims where the customer's ISP status is pending, ASS (assessed) or SUS (suspended)
    • claims where DAP/PPL trained staff need to contact the customer
    • select Unable to complete claim from the drop down box
    • select Exit
    • go to Step 14
  • Reject, for all online claims where the customer does not meet the eligibility criteria
    • select Continue
    • staff must not contact a customer to advise their claim has been rejected
    • customers will receive a decision letter
    • do not reject phone claims that do not meet the eligibility criteria. These are not processed in the Disaster Recovery Payment Claims workflow
    • go to Step 16

Claim selected for 'follow-up' + Read more ...

Complete the following:

  • For phone claims (including ACC) complete activities under Online and phone claims
  • For online claims complete and ACC phone claims activities under both Online and phone claim and Additional activity for online and ACC phone claims only

Online and phone claims

  • go to the Activity List (AL) screen in Customer First
  • select the Emergency Exgratia (EXG) claim activity
  • select (H) Hold/resubmit for action on a future date
  • select Continue
  • select the Keyword twisty
  • add keywords by typing/entering them in uppercase
    • customer's ISP status is pending, ASS (assessed) or SUS (suspended), add keyword 'ISPNCL', or
    • DAP/PPL trained staff need to call the customer, add keyword 'PPLDAPCP', or
    • payment query escalated to the Level 2 Helpdesk (including customers receiving the Home Equity Access Scheme), add keyword ‘PE’, or
    • paid Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment up to 14 days prior to relevant period, add keyword ‘PLDPCDP', or
    • payment nominee, add keyword 'NOM'
      - PLDPCDP and NOM claims are not held
      - unassign PLDPCDP and NOM claims by selecting the work item from the inbox and by choosing Unassign
      -Reallocation Reason for NOM claims, select ‘Second Contact Attempt Required’ from the drop down box
      -Reallocation Reason for PLDPCDP claims, select ‘Service Support Referral’ from the drop down box.
      - this will return PLDPCDP and NOM claims to the allocation pool to be reallocated to a trained staff member
    • Note: where 1 payment has already been issued today under the FSP - no keyword is needed
  • In the Resubmit date field for:
    • Claims where 1 payment has already been issued today under the FSP hold the claim for 1 day
    • for 'ISPNCL' and 'PPLDAPCP' keywords hold the claim for 28 days by entering +28 in the Resubmit date field
    • for ‘PE’ keyword hold the claim for 14 days by entering +14 in the Resubmit date field
  • select Continue
  • on the AL screen, cancel any Manual Follow Up (MFU) generated by the workflow. Do not cancel the claim activity
  • add a DOC to the customer's record by running the Fast Note workflow. This is so there is a DOC on the customer's record if they call about the status of a pending claim:
    • Location: Customer First > Workspace > Documents and Correspondence > Fast Note
    • Service Reason: select 'EXG - Emergency Exgratia' from the drop down
    • Document Completion: Select 'Yes' otherwise the Fast Note will not close and will create a new work item
    • Extra Details to enter a title for the DOC. Type in this field 'COVID-19 DP Not Finalised'
    • Clearly document:
      - claim ID (copy/paste the claim ID from the SOA
      - event customer is claiming for
      - relevant period the customer is claiming for
      - why the claim cannot be finalised
    • Select Continue

Additional activity for online and ACC phone claims only

  • return to the open Social Application (SOA) in Customer First. If the SOA has closed, select it from the Inbox
  • select the Keywords in Inbox tab. Add keywords by typing/entering them in uppercase in the Keyword field under the existing COVDP keyword:
    • customer's ISP status is pending, ASS (assessed) or SUS (suspended), add keyword 'ISPNCL', or
    • DAP/PPL trained staff need to call the customer, add keyword 'PPLDAPCP', or
    • payment query escalated to the Level 2 Helpdesk, (including customers receiving the Home Equity Access Scheme) add keyword ‘PE’, or
    • paid Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment up to 14 days prior to relevant period, add keyword ‘PLDPCDP’
    • payment nominee, add keyword 'NOM'
    • Note: where 1 payment has already been issued today under the FSP - no keyword is needed
    • do not delete the COVDP keyword
    • delete the CTAP keyword by clicking on the grey box on the left of the CTAP keyword and clicking on the trash can
  • select Save
  • a SOA does not need to be held, Workload Management will remove this from inboxes in due course
  • do not change the status of the SOA

Procedure ends here until claim re-assigned.
