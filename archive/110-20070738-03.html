Specialist services available + Read more ...

There are a number of specialist officers that help customers directly or support staff in servicing customers. The role and function of specialists varies according to the customer groups they service.

Depending on the customer's situation, there are 3 specialist services below which may help customers receiving a refund.

Financial Information Service (FIS) Officers

FIS is a free education and expert information service provided by the government, and:

  • helps people make informed decisions about investment and financial issues by helping them understand their financial affairs their options
  • provides a service by phone, appointment and through seminars

FIS does not provide financial planning, financial advice or financial counselling.

See: The FIS Officer.

Social Work

Social workers are available to assess peopleâs needs, offer short term counselling/support and provide information and referrals to specialised services.

Social workers focus on helping customers with complex needs. This may include:

  • customers at risk of suicide or self-harm
  • young people without adequate support
  • customers who are experiencing family and domestic violence

See: Social work service referral.

Indigenous Service Officers (ISO)

Assistance ISOs can provide:

  • cultural support and advice for Service Officers interacting with Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander customers if needed
  • local Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander community resource information
  • promotion of payments and services to Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander customers, communities and organisations
  • promotion and support of online services (myGov)

Assistance ISOs cannot provide:

  • advise customers about how to invest their money
  • make decisions about government payments or entitlements

See: ISO.
