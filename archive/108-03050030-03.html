Calculating frozen rate pensions for Department of Veterans' Affairs War Widow(er) pensioners 108-03050030

This page contains information for calculating the entitlements for Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) War Widow(er)s who are also receiving a relevant income support payment granted before 20 March 1995.

On this page:

Claim received

Rate of payment

Claim received

Table 1: This table describes the action required when a claim is received for a social security payment or benefit and the customer is also receiving Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) War Widow(er)'s Pension (WWP).

Step

Action

1

New claim submitted by a WWP recipient + Read more ...

Note: do not update the Veterans' Affairs Pension (DVA) screen on a current War Widow(er)'s record. This may cause frozen rate pension customers to lose their saved pre 20 March 1995 status, which cannot be restored.

To ensure the correct DVA income is recorded on the customer's record, Service Officers can run the DVA Clearance/Advice Request Fast Note in Customer First Workspace. Use Fast Note - Generic > Enquiry > DVA Clearance/Advice Request.

For urgent requests only, contact the DVA Clearance Team by phone. Do not refer customers to this number.

A WWP recipient cannot be granted a social security income support payment (unless they have no entitlement to a DVA Income Support Supplement (ISS)). In any circumstance they should be encouraged to apply for ISS through DVA if they have not already done so.

Advise the customer to contact DVA regarding eligibility for any additional assistance and record details on a DOC.

If customer has already lodged a claim for a social security income support payment, reject the claim and record details on a DOC. For assistance with rejecting claim, see Rejecting a Centrelink claim.

Note: there may be limited circumstances where a pension may be granted if the claimant is not eligible for ISS.

Rate of payment

Table 2: This table describes how to calculate the rate of payment after application of the income and assets test.

Step

Action

1

Calculating the pension rate + Read more ...

This process is only applicable for customers who were granted Age Pension, Carer Payment, Disability Support Pension or Wife Pension and DVA War Widow(er)'s Pension prior to 20 March 1995.

All income and asset details must be coded on the customer's record, and their partner's if they have one. The system will automatically calculate the customer's (and their partner's) entitlement.

Note: the war widow(er)'s Centrelink pension amount will be the lower of the frozen rate, the income tested rate or the assets tested rate.

To manually check a customer's entitlements, determine the amount payable under the frozen rate as well as the income and assets tests.

2

Determine if the customer is a homeowner + Read more ...

If the customer is a homeowner, the value of assessable assets allowed before there is any effect on the rate of pension is less than for non-homeowners.

For assistance, see Determining homeowners and non-homeowners.

3

Rate 1 (Assets Test) + Read more ...

To calculate Rate 1 (Assets Test) determine the customer's assessable assets amount (or if they have a partner the combined assets) by checking Pensions Income and Asset Summary (PIAS) screen for total assets.

Is the value of the customer's assets (or the combined assets) over the pensions assets test limit?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, Rate 1 (Assets Test) will be the maximum rate of the basic Pension. Include Pension Supplement, Energy Supplement and Rent Assistance (RA) if applicable. Go to Step 7

4

Determine excess asset value: + Read more ...

  • Single War Widow(er)'s excess assets = total assessable assets - pensions assets test limit
  • Partnered War Widow(er)'s excess assets = total combined assessable assets - total combined pensions assets test limit

5

Determine pension reduction amount + Read more ...

Pension reduction amount = Excess assets (rounded down to nearest $250) x 19.50 then divided by 250.

Divide this result by 26 to obtain the fortnightly pension reduction amount.

To convert to a daily amount, divide result by 364 (not 365).

6

Pension amount payable under the Assets Test (Rate 1) + Read more ...

Determine the amount payable for the fortnight under the Assets Test. The maximum fortnightly pension rate includes Pension Supplement, Energy Supplement and Rent Assistance (RA) if applicable (only if RA is not paid by DVA):

  • Single war widow(er)s
    • Pension amount payable = maximum fortnightly rate - fortnightly pension reduction amount
  • Partnered war widow(er)s
    • Combined pension amount payable per fortnight = maximum combined fortnightly rate - fortnightly pension reduction amount
    • Pension amount payable per fortnight to each partner = Combined amount payable per fortnight divided by 2

Now calculate the customer's rate under the income test.

7

Rate 2 (Income test) + Read more ...

To calculate Rate 2 using the current income test, determine the customer's assessable income (or if they have a partner their combined income) by checking the Pensions Income and Assets Summary (PIAS) and Employment Income Summary (EANS) screens. Note: WWP is treated as income for social security purposes.

When the customer, and/or their partner, has employment income:

  • if over Age Pension age - the customer may be entitled to the Work Bonus. The work bonus deduction is made before the income test is applied
  • if under Age Pension age - Working Credit may enable the customer to keep some of their income support payment while they are working. For partnered customers, if one party is entitled to Working Credit and the other is entitled to the Work Bonus calculate the Work Bonus deduction first

Is the assessable income less than or equal to the pension income free area? For the pension income free area amount, see the Pension - income free area link on the Rates and thresholds page.

  • No, if assessable income is greater than the income free area, go to Step 8
  • Yes, Rate 2 (income test) will be the maximum rate of pension. Include Pension Supplement, Energy Supplement and Rent Assistance (RA) if applicable, (only if RA is not paid by DVA). Go to Step 10

8

Affecting income + Read more ...

Determine the affecting income for the fortnight. For the pension income free area amount, see the Pension - income free area link on the Rates and thresholds page:

  • Single customer
    • affecting income = (individual assessable fortnightly income - pension income free area) x 0.50
  • Partnered customer
    • divide the total assessable income by 2 to obtain the individual's assessable income
    • affecting income = (individual assessable fortnightly income - half of partnered pension income free area limit) x 0.50

Note: if the customer is currently paid under the transitional rules for pension customers, the transitional rules rate calculation must be compared to the rate payable under the current income test. If the customer is still entitled to a higher rate under the transitional rules, the transitional rule rate will be their Rate 2.

9

Pension amount payable under the income test (Rate 2) + Read more ...

  • For single war widow(er)s
    • determine the maximum fortnightly single rate payable, including Pension Supplement, Energy Supplement and Rent Assistance (RA) if applicable (only if RA is not paid by DVA)
    • pension amount payable = maximum fortnightly rate - direct deduction income amount - affecting income (calculated in step 8)
  • For partnered war widow(er)s
    • determine the maximum fortnightly partnered rate payable for the fortnight under the income test, including Pension Supplement, Energy Supplement and Rent Assistance (RA) if applicable (only if RA is not paid by DVA)
    • individual pension amount payable = maximum fortnightly rate - direct deduction income amount - affecting income (calculated in step 8)

Note: for periods before 1 January 2022, if the customer's rate is affected by the income test and they or their partner are receiving an Adjusted Disability Pension from DVA, they may also be entitled to receive Defence Force Income Support Allowance (DFISA).

10

Continuous receipt of WWP since 1 November 1986 + Read more ...

Is the customer female and have they been in continuous receipt of a WWP since 1 November 1986?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, Maximum Frozen Rate of $124.90 applies. Go to Step 12

11

Compare rates + Read more ...

Compare the social security amount being paid to the customer prior to 1 November 1986 with the frozen rate of $124.90.

Maximum Frozen Rate = the greater of these 2 amounts.

12

Compare Maximum Frozen Rate with rates calculated under the Assets Test and Income Tests + Read more ...

The customer's entitlement to a social security income support payment will be the lower of:

  • the maximum frozen rate
  • the assets tested rate (Rate 1), or
  • the income tested rate (Rate 2)

Record information regarding customer's entitlement and any advice given to customer on a DOC.
