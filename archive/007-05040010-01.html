This document outlines the change of circumstances process that applies to employers who have registered to provide Parental Leave Pay (PPL) to an employee.

Employer advises of a change

Employers can advise up to 30 days in advance of an:

  • employee resignation/termination of employment, and
  • return to work - including for an allowable exception reason

Once the assessment of the employees PPL claim is complete employers can notify a change in circumstances via:

  • Centrelink Business Online Services (CBOS) or
  • Business Hub, or
  • If they are not registered, the Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT)

If the change of circumstance means the employee is not eligible for PPL contact the employee to confirm this prior to cancellation action or transfer of paymaster role.

The PPL circumstances option for registered employers

Centrelink Business Online Services (CBOS)

The PPL circumstances option is available to CBOS registered employers on the Services Australia website, and also allows the Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) to process employer advised change of circumstances.

Employers and the PPLEPT will be able to:

  • View and update current PPL employer details on the employer record, such as:
    • bank details and
    • authorised users
    • manually update current employer details
  • Add/update employer Australian Business Number (ABN) and business name
  • Verify and validate newly entered employer ABN through Australian Business Register (ABR) web service
  • Record payment delivery changes (PPLEPT only)
  • View historical information

Business Hub

Business Hub allows employers to advise of an employee’s change in circumstances online.

An employer

  • selects this Providing Leave Pay for existing claimants tile
  • completes the required fields, and submits the information

The Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) will action the work item.

The PPLEPT can undertake a revocation/transfer of the employer role in circumstances that no longer require the employer to deliver PPL to an employee.

The Resources page contains contact details for the Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT).
