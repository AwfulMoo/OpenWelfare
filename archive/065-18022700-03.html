This page contains processes for the release of financial information to service providers and information regarding signature requirements for Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care (means assessment) forms.

On this page:

Financial disclosure to service providers

Signature requirements for the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care forms

Financial disclosure to service providers

Table 1: This table describes the processes of recording a care recipient’s consent to release financial information to a service provider, recording the withdrawal of previously provided consent and actioning requests for the release of financial information to service providers.

Note: portions of this process can only be completed by Aged Care Programmes Branch staff.

Step

Action

1

Receiving forms containing consent + Read more ...

Forms sent to Services Australia

The following forms containing consent for the release of financial information to a service provider may be received by Services Australia:

  • Aged Care Financial Disclosure Consent form (SA480)
    • See the Resources page for a link to the SA480 form
  • Versions 1711 (only) of:
    • Aged Care Fees Income Assessment form (SA456.1711)
    • Permanent Residential Aged Care – Request for a Combined Assets and Income Assessment form (SA457.1711)

Scan the received form to the Customer First record as in Scanning Centrelink documents using an MFD. Note: do not select Scan to Store

Forms sent to the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)

The following forms containing consent for the release of financial information to a service provider may be received by DVA:

  • Versions 1711 (only) of:
    • Aged Care Fees Income Assessment form (SA456.1711)
    • Permanent Residential Aged Care – Request for a Combined Assets and Income Assessment form (SA457.1711)

DVA does not receive the SA480 form.

DVA staff will email the consent details contained in the SA456/SA457 to the Aged Care Programmes Branch.

Aged Care Programmes Branch staff are responsible for scanning and creating a DOC for any consent forwarded by DVA to the Aged Care Programmes Branch

The process undertaken is the same as the previously described process (where the forms were sent to Services Australia)

2

Recording consent received via version 1711 of an SA456 or SA457 form + Read more ...

Aged care skill tagged staff undertake the following action:

  • Check that the SA456 or SA457 is completed and signed by the care recipient (or nominee/authorised representative). Consent for financial disclosure to service providers is located in the Declaration section of the forms

Note: Financial Disclosure consent was only able to be requested on version 1711 of either form.

Has the care recipient provided financial disclosure consent?

  • Yes, form is signed and ‘Yes’ is ticked:
    • Create a Fast Note. Select Auto Text > Aged Care > Enquiry > AC Fin Disclosure Consent–Smart Ctr Use. Ensure the 'Display on access' box is ticked and change the DOA DOC type from OTH to PPE
    • Add details required in the Fast Note
    • Check the ‘Display on access’ box underneath the Text: box and ‘Confirm’
    • Change the Reason from OTH to PPE
    • Delete the Expiry Date and ‘Confirm’
    • Add ACMPS Note (Text Type ‘Correspondence’) by going to Notes tab, clicking on New, and changing text type to Correspondence. Copy the content from the DOC created by the Fast Note into the ACMPS Note
    • Click on Back and then Save
    • Where the care recipient does not have an ACMPS record, annotate the ‘Financial Disclosure Consent’ DOC indicating that no ACMPS record exists to create a Note
  • No, procedure ends here

Note:

  • At this stage, consent for financial disclosure to service providers must be provided in writing
  • Financial disclosure consent does not apply where a care recipient is means not disclosed

3

Recording consent received via an SA480 form + Read more ...

Aged care skill tagged staff will undertake the following action:

Check the SA480 has been completed and signed by the care recipient (or nominee/authorised representative.

Has the care recipient provided financial disclosure consent?

  • Yes, the form is signed and ‘Yes’ is ticked:
    • Complete the Customer First AC Fin Disclosure Consent – Smart Ctr Use Fast Note to record the details. Ensure the 'Display on access' box is ticked and update the DOA DOC type to PPE from the default OTH
    • Finalise the work item including the Notes 'Aged Care financial disclosure consent provided'
    • Add ACMPS Note (Text Type ‘Correspondence’) by going to Notes tab, selecting New, and changing text type to Correspondence. Copy the content from the DOC created by the Fast Note into the ACMPS Note
    • Select Back and Save
    • Where the care recipient does not have an ACMPS record annotate the 'Financial Disclosure Consent' DOC indicating that no AMCPS record exists to create a Note
    • Complete the work item
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, the form is signed and 'No' is ticked:
    • Do not record any consent details
    • Update the work item Notes in Customer First with 'Aged Care financial disclosure consent not provided'
    • Complete the work item
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, the form is not signed:
    • Update the work item in Customer First with 'Aged Care financial disclosure consent not provided'
    • Complete the work item
    • Procedure ends here

4

Recording the withdrawal of previously provided consent + Read more ...

Where a care recipient (or nominee/authorised representative) wishes to withdraw their consent to the release of financial information to a service provider, the following process applies.

Service Centre Service Officers:

  • Create an ACTDOC
    • Service Reason 'Residential Care Assessments'
    • Office 'RCA'
    • add the keyword FINDIS
  • Include the name and contact details of the person withdrawing the consent

For more information about creating an ACTDOC, see Creating documents (Fast Note, Callbacks and ACTDOCs)

Aged care skill tagged staff:

  • In Customer First
    • Annotate the Display on Access DOC with details of the revocation, including the name and contact details of the person withdrawing the consent
    • When annotation is completed, 'X' the Display On Access DOC to expire the DOC from the date the revocation is updated
  • In ACMPS
    • Add ACMPS Note (Text Type ‘Correspondence’) by going to Notes tab, clicking on New, and changing text type to Correspondence. Copy the details of revoking of consent from the DOC created by the Fast Note into the ACMPS Note
    • Select Back and Save

5

Receiving service provider requests for financial information + Read more ...

A service provider can only request a care recipient's financial information via email. All request emails must be sent by the service provider to the Aged Care Programmes Branch

Financial information will not be released verbally

  • Where a service provider or a care recipient (or nominee/authorised representative) makes a verbal request for a care recipient’s financial information to be released to the service provider, they must be advised that the request needs to be emailed by the service provider

Where consent is provided, the service provider will be sent a letter containing the financial information.

  • This will usually occur within 28 days of their request

6

Release of financial information to service providers + Read more ...

For Aged Care Programmes Branch staff only.

  • Complete a security check to ensure the request was sent by the service provider. This may include:
    • checking that the email address the request was sent from is connected to the service provider making the request
    • searching Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS) or Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP) systems for the name of the person sending the request
    • check the care recipient record for the presence of the Service/RACS ID
  • Where it is not possible to establish whether the request has come from a service provider connected to the care recipient, a reply email will be sent to the service provider requesting further information
  • Check ACMPS Notes to determine whether consent has been received
    • Where an ACMPS record did not exist when consent was being recorded, the consent will have been recorded in Customer First instead
    • If it is expected that consent was given but it is not recorded in ACMPS, check Customer First Document List (DL)
    • If consent was received, copy the content into ACMPS Notes

Has the care recipient (or nominee/authorised representative) given consent for release of financial disclosure details to the provider?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Where there is no consent for financial disclosure to the service provider + Read more ...

For Aged Care Programmes Branch staff only.

If there is no consent recorded send the following rejection email back to the service provider:

Dear [name of provider]

Our records shows that [care recipient’s name] has not given consent for their financial information to be released. As a result we are unable to approve your request.

You may wish to discuss this with [care recipient’s name].

Yours faithfully

[name of person sending the email]

Complete ACMPS Notes stating:

'A request was received by [insert service name and ID].

The release of financial information was rejected as consent was not authorised by the care recipient as at [DD/MM/YY].'

Procedure ends here

8

Gathering the financial information to be released + Read more ...

For Aged Care Programmes Branch staff only.

In ACMPS Means Testing Data Tab:

  • Scroll down to the Means Tested Assessments field and select the means assessment with a Received On date closest to the date that the financial information is being requested for.
    • Several means assessment data sets may require checking to locate the details required

Within the applicable means assessment, information to be released to the service provider can be located as follows:

  • For Residential Care requests:
    • Income Information: The Total Amount Details tab contains a breakdown between Income Support and Non-Income Support amounts
    • Assets Information: The Asset Details tab contains a breakdown of assets
  • For Home Care Requests:
    • Income Information: The Total Amount Details tab contains a breakdown between Income Support and Non-Income Support amounts.
      Note: Asset information must not be released to providers for Home Care recipients (even if this information appears in the means assessment)

9

Letter to the service provider + Read more ...

For Aged Care Programmes Branch staff only.

Populate the requested information in the Financial Disclosure to Service Providers letter template

Insert the date the information has been requested for into the 'This information is correct as at <DD Month YYYY>' field in the template

Save the letter in pdf format and upload to the Customer First record as a 'UNS005 form type'.

Print and mail the letter as per current procedures.

10

Documenting the release of financial information + Read more ...

For Aged Care Programmes Branch staff only.

Customer First DOC

In the Customer First record use the AC Fin Disclosure Request Actioned Fast Note to record the details of the request including:

  • who the request was received from (include the service provider name and ID)
  • the date of effect
  • Assets and Income sourced from ACMPS MTA (MTA number to be inserted)
  • Income and Assets summary letter issued to (insert the service provider address)
  • Details of the form type (UNS005) and date uploaded to the Customer First record

ACMPS Notes

Add ACMPS Note (Text Type ‘Correspondence’):

  • Go to Notes tab
  • Select New, and change text type to Correspondence
  • Copy the content from the DOC created by the Fast Note into the ACMPS Note
  • Select Back and Save

11

Where the care recipient believes incorrect financial information was released to the service provider + Read more ...

If a care recipient contacts Services Australia advising that the financial details that were released to the service provider were incorrect, advise the recipient that the information released was based on the information that they have previously provided.

If the details are incorrect for that particular period or have changed since, a reassessment or update may be required. Refer to Aged care reviews – reassessments and updates.

12

Where the service provider makes telephone contact as they believe that they have been given incorrect financial information + Read more ...

Service providers do not have implied authority.

  • They should be advised that due to privacy regulations, Services Australia staff can only talk to the care recipient. See the Resources page for a link to the Privacy and Secrecy page

Advise the service provider to discuss the financial details provided directly with the care recipient (or nominee/authorised representative).

Signature requirements for the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care forms

Table 2: This table outlines the signature requirements for the Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care forms.

Step

Action

1

Form is signed and the signatory is a person other than the care recipient + Read more ...

Where a person other than the care recipient has signed the form, they must also provide a name and contact details to aged care skill tagged staff to determine whether that person is an authorised third party.

Where the signatory is an authorised third party

A means assessment can be accepted where a signatory is already recorded in:

  • Customer First as a current correspondence nominee
  • ACMPS as an aged care nominee, or
  • Customer First as a Fast Note showing documentation has been provided authorising them to act on the care recipient’s behalf

Ensure that all authorised parties have signed the form when a document authorises more than one person jointly.

Where the signatory is not an authorised third party

Where a signatory has not previously provided documentation that grants them authority to sign on behalf of the care recipient, they are requested to do so in the form.

A person can apply for a calculation of your cost of care on behalf of someone else if:

  • they hold a power of attorney or guardianship order
  • a letter from a doctor, nurse or similar health professional is provided stating that the care recipient is unable to sign the form
  • the application is made by the Director if Nursing at the aged care home where the care recipient is a resident

Where the care recipient is deceased:

  • only the executor of the estate or person holding a letter of administration can sign/act on their behalf
  • Any previous power of attorney or guardianship orders are no longer valid

Where acceptable documentation is received with the form:

  • the form can be accepted
  • in circumstances where a document authorises more than 1 person jointly, all authorised parties must have signed the form

Where acceptable documentation has not been provided with the form:

  • the form cannot be accepted
  • there is no authority to contact the signatory as they are not an authorised third party:
    • an Aged Care Request for Further Information letter must be sent to the care recipient asking for either their signature, or 1 of the previously listed documents that authorises the third party to sign on their behalf
    • See the Resources page for link to the Request for Further Information letter templates
  • complete a Progress of Claim DOC with action taken

2

Form is signed however the care recipient or authorised third party has not signed the signature block in the ‘Declaration’ section + Read more ...

The care recipient or authorised third party may have provided a signature in another signature block, or elsewhere on the form (for example, in the Contact Person/Nominee details sections or the back page of the form). In this case, the form can be accepted without further action.

3

Form is not signed + Read more ...

Where the care recipient or authorised third party has not signed the form, an attempt must be made to contact the care recipient or authorised third party by phone to:

  • confirm they have requested the assessment
  • confirm the details within the form

Where phone contact is able to be made, if the aged care skill tagged officer is satisfied that the information lodged is sufficient for the assessment to be completed correctly:

  • the assessment may be completed
  • they must DOC the record with action taken

Where the Aged Care skill tagged officer:

  • is unable to make phone contact, and/or
  • has any concerns about the identity of the person completing the form, and/or
  • has any concerns about the completeness or accuracy of the information provided;

the following action must be undertaken:

  • send an aged care request for Information letter asking for the outstanding information or signature to be provided
    • See the Resources page for the link to the Request for Further Information letter templates
  • DOC the record with action taken
