This document outlines information for Service Officers who are responsible for coding a claim, or a review of the away from home rate for dependent ABSTUDY customers.

On this page:

Applying for the away from home rate

ABSTUDY claimants can apply for away from home rate in the initial claim by:

  • using the ABSTUDY Claiming Tool (secondary students only)
  • online claim (tertiary students and Australian Apprentices only)
  • Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) (tertiary students and Australian Apprentices only), or
  • completing the Claim for ABSTUDY (SY019)

Customers can also apply for the away from home rate while in receipt of ABSTUDY allowances by providing details of the home and/or study circumstances in a statement (verbal or written).

Cessation of the away from home rate

If a customer ceases to qualify for the ABSTUDY away from home rate, see Cessation of the ABSTUDY away from home rate.

Secondary students impacted by COVID-19

During the COVID-19 pandemic which occurred in Australia from March 2020, some boarding providers closed and others had reduced capacity due to social distancing requirements within the board facility. Additionally, some students may not be able to return to their private board arrangements. Students who returned to the permanent home and did not return to their boarding arrangements in Term 2, 3 or 4 in 2020 or Terms 3 or 4 of 2021 may continue to be entitled to the away from home rate.

Continuing students

Students remain entitled to the away from home rate in Term 2, 3 and 4 in 2020 or Term 3 and 4 of 2021 if:

  • they were approved for boarding entitlements in the term immediately prior
  • their board provider holds a boarding place for them
  • the student intends to return to board as soon as possible, and
  • the school/board provider continues to support the student study remotely using other methods, such as online

Continuing students who changed board providers

Entitlement to the away from home rate for continuing students who commenced with a new board provider in Term 2 in 2020 or Term 3 of 2021 was dependant on their study mode and enrolment or acceptance date, regardless of whether they qualified in Term 1 2020 or Term 2 2021. The following students remained entitled:

  • students who attended in person, or
  • students studying remotely and who were accepted to board before the end of Term 1 in 2020 or Term 2 of 2021 to commence in Term 2 in 2020 or Terms 3 of 2021, and
  • continue to meet an away from home criteria for Term 2 in 2020 or Term 3 of 2021

Students with a new boarding provider, who were accepted to board after the start of Term 2 in 2020 or Term 3 of 2021 were not entitled to the away from home rate for Term 2 in 2020 or Term 3 of 2021 where they did not attend in person.

Continuing students who started with a new boarding provider in Term 3 or 4 in 2020 or Term 4 of 2021 were only entitled if they physically attended the new boarding provider during the term they commenced.

New Claims

For ABSTUDY boarding claims granted from 1 April 2020, a student will be entitled to the away from home rate in Term 2 if they were:

  • accepted to board before the end of Term 1 and will study either in person or online during Term 2, or
  • accepted to board after the start of Term 2 and will physically attend at the boarding provider during Term 2

For ABSTUDY boarding claims granted from 1 July 2021, a student will be entitled to the away from home rate in Term 3 if they were:

  • accepted to board before the end of Term 2 and will study either in person or online during Term 3, or
  • accepted to board after the start of Term 3 and will physically attend at the boarding provider during Term 3

For ABSTUDY boarding claims granted from 1 July 2020 for Term 3 or 1 October 2020 or 2021 for Term 4, the student is entitled to the away from home rate only where they physically attended the boarding provider during the term they commenced.

Where students with new claims were entitled to the away from home rate, claims should be assessed as per Assessing a claim for ABSTUDY Schooling B Award, Tertiary Award and Masters and Doctorate Award. In Term 2, 3 and 4 of 2020 the Payment on Advice process did not occur, these claims are granted from:

  • 1 April 2020 for Term 2,
  • 1 July 2020 for Term 3, or
  • 1 October for Term 4

There was no change for students who continued to board and study in person.

Tertiary students impacted by COVID-19

During the COVID-19 pandemic, which occurred in Australia from March 2020, some students were required to leave their on campus term accommodation. Many students also transitioned to online study as face-to-face teaching was unavailable.

Students who were receiving the away from home rate, and temporarily transitioned to online learning as a result of COVID-19 remain eligible for the away from home rate. Tertiary students must return to face-to-face study as soon as it becomes available to continue to be eligible for the away from home rate.

Where a student had to move out of education provider accommodation (such as on campus) and are, or were temporarily staying with their parents, they will remain eligible for the away from home rate providing they:

  • remain enrolled in their course of study, and
  • intend to return to their campus accommodation as soon as the education provider allows them to return

This includes students who:

  • had their accommodation arrangement cancelled by the education provider and have moved their belongings out of that accommodation
  • were approved for Residential Cost Option (RCO)

Note: the away from home rate of ABSTUDY Living Allowance can be paid for any period that the education provider does not charge a residential fee, rather than the reduced rate of Living Allowance.

Coding requirements

The payment of away from home rate needs to be recorded on the customer's Services Australia record including:

  • the criteria for away from home rate approval
  • the customer's living arrangements
  • the payee
  • any board and school fee charges

If all of these are correctly recorded on the customer's record, the correct rate of payment and correct payee will result. The full assessment and coding for ABSTUDY entitlements is covered separately.

Secondary, secondary non-schooling and tertiary students

The coding required for a secondary school student varies slightly in respect of students boarding at hostels, boarding schools, or in an approved ABSTUDY Homestay arrangement, and students boarding privately. Some coding specific to tertiary, secondary non-schooling students and Australian Apprentices is also different to secondary school students. This reflects key entitlement differences between secondary, secondary non-schooling and tertiary students, with additional allowances available to secondary students.

Note: ABSTUDY secondary school students approved for the away from home rate are entitled to receive the full package of away from home allowances for the full period of eligibility for away from home rate. This differs from ABSTUDY tertiary, secondary non-schooling students and Australian Apprentices, and particular coding is needed to ensure full entitlement is paid.

Claim lodged for the purpose of attending an interview

Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel is available if:

  • a secondary school or boarding facility requires prospective students to attend interviews or other selection procedures prior to acceptance
  • the student is intending to study in an approved tertiary course of at least 1 year's duration at the study location, and has completed secondary studies in the previous year

In order to claim this type of travel, a full ABSTUDY assessment needs to be made for the period the student is intending to study.

A secondary boarding facility may need to conduct an interview or selection process in their own right and it may not be possible at the time of claiming to confirm the school they are attending. The Process page contains special coding instructions for these cases.

Relocation Scholarships

Dependent students living away from home studying an approved scholarship course may also be qualified to receive Relocation Scholarship, see Relocation Scholarship (RS) eligibility.

Note: ABSTUDY Students in state care (not receiving Foster Care Allowance) are to be assessed the same way as ABSTUDY dependent students who are living away from home for the purposes of Relocation Scholarship.
