This page contains the process for Service Officers to validate and record payments against the Compensation Case Management System (CCMS) and National Compensation Recovery Information (NCRI) mainframe screen. It also outlines how to change the allocation of payments and request missing documentation from a compensation payer.

Table 1: This table describes the process for Service Officers to validate a payment for an existing case (WIN) in the Compensation Case Management System (CCMS). Payments may be received individually or as part of a multiple receipt so each payment must be validated as part of this process.

Step

Action

1

CCMS Applications folder + Read more ...

Payment receipts that have a WIN starting with 2, 3 and 4 are New South Wales (NSW) cases and payment receipts that have a WIN starting with 5, 6 and 7 are for Queensland (QLD). Select the appropriate 'State' application to launch below.

Open the CCMS Applications folder and double click on the following icons to launch the required programs:

  • Component Request Broker (CRB) for QLD or NSW according to the WIN on the payment receipt. The CRB opens a console window with a title beginning with 'HIC NT4 Component Request Broker'. This also starts a number of other programs which must be running for all applications that link to the CRB to function correctly
  • Case Enquiry
  • Staffware Process Client and enter the following information in the pop-up window:
    • User - enter P Number (PXXXXX)
    • Password - enter LAN password
    • Server - select either the NSW CCMS (NSYDAP01) for WIN starting with 2, 3 and 4 or QLD CCMS (QBNEAP01) for WIN starting with 5, 6 and 7 and select OK
  • Medicare Mainframe, go to Step 2

2

Case Enquiry screen + Read more ...

Access the Case Enquiry screen:

  • select 'New Search' in the Operations box
  • enter 'WIN' or 'WXXXXXXXXX' (without the W) into the Work Item Number Search Criteria box, press [Enter]

The screen will be rebuilt with case information including injured person’s name, compensation payer, date of injury, case notes, case progress and the document view folder, go to Step 3.

3

Mandatory checks in Case Enquiry screen + Read more ...

Compare details on the payment receipt to the case information in the Case Enquiry screen by completing all mandatory checks. To ensure the correct case record is open, check:

  • injured person’s name
  • injured person’s date of birth (if supplied on payment receipt)
  • compensation payer details, and
  • date of injury (if supplied on payment receipt), go to Step 4

4

Medicare mainframe screens + Read more ...

Using the National Compensation Recovery Information (NCRI) mainframe screen, check the injured person’s record for any important information:

  • Enter the Medicare card number into the mainframe screen by typing NCRI, followed by the Medicare card number, Press [Enter]
  • Confirm the injured person’s name and date of birth (if supplied) from the payment receipt
  • If more than one person is listed on the Medicare card, place the cursor under the injured person’s name, Press [F5]
  • Press [F2] to access the case notes for details of any actions taken. Press [F8] to traverse more pages if there are detailed case notes
  • Press [F2] again to return to the home page of the NCRI screen, take note of any information (where applicable) to compare with the case details in Case Enquiry Screen, continue processing at this work step

Using the National Compensation Accounts Reconciliation (NCAR) mainframe screen, check the payment is allocated correctly to the WIN:

  • Enter the WIN (without the alpha) in the WIN Command Line, Press [Enter]. A system message is returned in the Command Line of the mainframe screen 'WIN FOUND' with the following information:
    • WIN, Account Number, Merlin Amount, Complexity, Create Date, Process Date and Operator

Note: if a system message is returned in the Command Line of the mainframe screen ‘TOO COMPLEX’, further investigation is required as there may be more than 1 payment allocated to WIN. Access SAPFI to investigate.

Take note of the information (where applicable) to compare with the case details in Case Enquiry screen, go to Step 5.

5

Document view folder in Case Enquiry screen + Read more ...

Service Officers must look for a notice of settlement (NtcSettl) and/or the most recent notice of past benefits or notice of charge (CP007M). In the 'Case Documents' window, all correspondence issued and received is listed by line number in date order.

Is there a 'NtcSettl' listed in Case Documents window?

  • Yes, to view the 'NtcSettl' line in the Case Documents window, double click on the 'NtcSettl' line in the view folder. The 'HIC Generic Image Viewer' is automatically launched providing a scanned image of the document, check for:
    • the total amount of compensation awarded (including legal costs)
    • whether there has been an apportionment of liability
    • whether the compensation payer intends to pay an advance payment, and
    • the advance payment amount if nominated - the advance payment amount must be 10% of the total amount of compensation and must be paid within 28 days of the date of settlement. For more information see, Payments, refunds and debt for Medicare Compensation Recovery
  • Select the 'CP007M' line (if it has been issued) in the Case Documents window by double clicking on the corresponding line number in the view folder. The 'HIC Generic Image Viewer' is re-launched providing an image of the document, check for:
    • notice of past benefits or notice of charge amount
    • take note of the information obtained above (where applicable) to compare with the payment receipt, go to Step 6
  • No, issue a z2277 - More information is needed to process a payment letter template. Print 2 copies of the letter and scan 1 copy to the case as backlog. The Resources page contains a link to the letter template, go to Step 6

6

Compare payment amount with notice of settlement or notice of past benefits or charge + Read more ...

If the notice of settlement indicates an apportionment of liability applies and the percentage of apportionment has been entered on the notice to support this, the payment received can be accepted if the notice of charge amount (when reduced by the apportionment percentage) matches the payment received. For more information see, Legal and requests for information for Medicare Compensation Recovery.

Does the payment receipt equal the advance payment amount or the notice of past benefit or notice of charge amount?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, and if the payment does not match the advance payment or notice of past benefits or charge, telephone the compensation payer to query the payment discrepancy, and ask:
    • What the payment received is for
    • Why the payment does not equal the 10% advance payment or notice of charge amount? For example, have expenses been deducted from the payment made or has there been an apportionment of liability, and
    • Advise the compensation payer that further amounts may be owed and request the shortfall or the evidence required to support the reduced payment (if applicable), go to Step 7

7

Case progress + Read more ...

Check the case progress against the WIN in the Case Enquiry screen 'Results' window. If the case is showing as:

  • ACTIVE, continue processing at this work step
  • PENDED, go to Step 8
  • CLOSED, go to Step 9

ACTIVE cases

Access the Staffware Process Client and search for the WIN:

  • select the 'Find Work Item' icon on the command line or press [F3]
  • enter the WIN (without alpha) in the 'Case Description' field, press [Enter]
  • double click on the WIN that is now highlighted in the Staffware Process Client

Scroll to the bottom of the work form and answer the following questions in order:

  • Refer case to Supervisor? - Select No and press [Enter]
  • Is this case a class action suit? – Select Yes and press [Enter].
    • select APO if the payment is a valid or intended advance payment, and press [Enter]
    • select NON APO if the payment is an invalid advance payment, press [Enter], or
    • select NOCH if the payment is for a notice of charge and press [Enter]
  • Verify claimant’s address? - Select No and press [Enter]
  • Process settlement or judgment or Adhoc work form? - Select No and Press [Enter]

Note: if a notice of settlement is on file and the case is within 28 days of the deadline to finalise the case under s33K or s24(4), do not suspend the case. Process and finalise the case accordingly.

  • Suspend work item? - Select Yes, a 'suspend until' date must be entered
    • If a notice of settlement is on file and not nearing a deadline, enter the suspend date as tomorrow's date and press [Enter], or
    • If a notice of settlement is not on file, enter the suspend date as 28 days from today’s date and press [Enter]. Do not release the work form yet, go to Step 10 to add a case note

Procedure ends here.

8

Pended cases + Read more ...

Access the Case Enquiry screen

  • select 'New Search' in the Operations box of the Case Enquiry screen
  • enter 'WIN' or 'WXXXXXXXXX' (without the W) into the Work Item Number Search Criteria box, Press [Enter]

The screen will be rebuilt with case information including injured person’s name, compensation payer, date of injury, case notes, case progress and the document view folder.

Answer the following questions in the Wake-Up command box:

  • Case is to be? In the drop down menu - select 'Activate'
  • New Wake Step? In the drop down menu - select 'Adhoc enquiry or data update' and select Update. The case notes box automatically launches and a case note must be added before finishing this task

Keep a record of the case – it is the Service Officer’s responsibility to flag the case appropriately the next business day.

Note: if the flag cannot be completed the next business day, complete the ‘Follow up work to be actioned’ email template. Send the template to a Team Leader who will allocate the template to a Service Officer to flag the case the next day. The Resources page contains a link to the email template.

Go to Step 10

9

Closed cases + Read more ...

Access the Case Enquiry screen

  • select 'New Search' in the Operations box of the Case Enquiry screen
  • enter 'WIN' or 'WXXXXXXXXX' (without the W) into the Work Item Number Search Criteria box, Press [Enter]

The screen will be rebuilt with case information including injured person’s name, compensation payer, date of injury, case notes, case progress and the document view folder. Select the Case notes command box in the Operations window.

Review the case and ensure that the payment receipt equals the advance payment amount or notice of charge amount and that the payment has been reconciled or matches the notice of charge or reduced notice of charge amount (where an apportionment has been applied).

Have all debts or refunds been reconciled on the closed case?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, if there has been an apportionment of liability accepted and the notice of charge has been paid in full or the case been closed as a non-advance payment and the payment is an advance payment, a refund is required, go to Step 10

10

Scan or file payment receipt + Read more ...

Is there a refund to be processed on a closed case?

  • Yes, further action is required to reconcile the debt or refund
    • Single payment receipts are to be scanned to the WIN with scanning code 15 (ad-hoc) so the case is activated into the Matched 2 Closed work step, or
    • Multiple payment receipts are not to be scanned to the case. Return the completed multi receipt back to your Team Leader once all payments have been processed. Complete 'Follow up work to be actioned' email template and send the template to your Team Leader who will allocate the case to a Service Officer for a refund to be actioned the next day. The Resources page contains a link to the email template, go to Step 11
  • No,
    • Single payment receipts are to be scanned to the WIN with scanning code 18 (backlog), or
    • Multiple payment receipts are not to be scanned to the case. Return the completed multi receipt back to your Team Leader once all payments have been processed

11

Add a case note + Read more ...

Enter the details of anyone contacted by telephone during the workflow and the details of the conversation.

If the Case Notes screen has not already populated, select the Add notes button from the list on the right hand side of the main CCMS Staffware Client or Case Enquiry screen.

  • The top box shows all notes previously recorded on this case
  • In the blank bottom box, record the actions taken, information requested and from whom and reasons for a decision. Use standard case notes where possible
  • Case notes are to include the details of the payment received for the case and whether it is a:
    • valid advance payment amount
    • non-advance payment (including invalid advance payment) amount
    • notice of charge amount
    • any other part payment amount
  • Enter (9000:__________FDS:__________$Amount:__________)

Select 'Add' to save notes. A confirmation box will appear, select 'Yes' to add case note

Payment received - No existing Work Identification Number (WIN)

Validating payment - No existing WIN

Table 2: This table describes the process for Service Officers to validate a payment when it is banked to a Medicare Number, 8600 number or 8700 number where there is no existing case (WIN) in the Compensation Case Management System (CCMS). Payments may be received individually or as part of a multiple receipt so each payment must be validated as part of this process.

Step

Action

1

CCMS Applications folder + Read more ...

Open the CCMS Applications folder and double click on the following icons to launch the required program:

  • Medicare mainframe, log on to the Medicare mainframe by typing:
    • 'CESN' in the command line at the top of the screen, Press [Enter]
    • 'OPERATOR' - enter (P Number) without the 'P'
    • 'PASSWORD' - enter the random generated number from RSA SecurID token, Press [Enter]
    • enter 'OPOQ' in the command line at the top of the screen, Press [Enter]
    • place the cursor under the correct 'Source Office Code' for Service Officers designated team and enter 'S' for select, Press [Enter], go to Step 2

2

Verify injured persons information + Read more ...

Is there a Medicare Number for the injured person on the payment receipt?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No - payment is banked to an 8600XXXX or 8700XXXX number.
    Search in Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS)
    Search CDMS to confirm if the injured person is enrolled in Medicare and eligible to receive Medicare benefits. A detailed name search, followed by an address search (if known) must be performed to attempt to locate the Medicare card enrolment details. See: Register a customer for Medicare in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS).
    Was the search successful?
    • Yes, Medicare card details located, go to Step 5
    • No, go to Step 3

3

Contact the compensation payer + Read more ...

Contact the compensation payer to confirm the correct spelling of the name, address and date of birth of the injured person and to obtain any further information that is relevant to the payment received.

4

Perform a secondary search + Read more ...

Perform a secondary search in CDMS using the correct details or further information now provided by the compensation payer (if applicable).

Was the secondary search successful and a Medicare Number obtained?

  • Yes, Medicare card details located, go to Step 5
  • No,
    • place a post-it-note on the payment receipt confirming Service Officer’s operator number (Pxxxxx), first and secondary searches already performed in CDMS and that the injured person is not entitled to claim Medicare benefits
    • place the payment receipt in the overseas tray on the desk of the Service Officer allocated the work, consult Team Leader if assistance is required. The responsible Service officer will process the payment receipt
    • procedure ends here

5

Search by Medicare number on the receipt + Read more ...

Using the National Compensation Recovery Information (NCRI) mainframe screen, enter the following information to access the injured person's record:

  • enter the Medicare card number into the mainframe screen by typing NCRI, followed by the Medicare card number, Press [Enter]
  • confirm the injured person’s name against the name on the payment receipt
  • if more than one person is listed on the Medicare card, place the cursor under the injured person’s name, press [F5], go to Step 6

6

Contact compensation payer + Read more ...

Telephone the compensation payer to request further information regarding the payment to determine whether the case has settled and whether the payment is for:

  • an advance payment amount which should be 10% of the total amount of compensation awarded (including legal costs), or
  • a voluntary recovery for Medicare services already paid by Services Australia

Has the case has settled?

  • Yes, request the compensation payer to send a notice of judgment or settlement to the agency and issue a z2277 - More information is needed to process a payment letter. Attach all single receipts to the z2277 letter and return both the letter and receipt to the compensation payer. The Resources page contains a link to this letter, go to Step 7
  • No, request details of the Medicare services being repaid by the compensation payer including the item numbers, dates of service and the provider’s name. If the Service Officer is unable to contact the compensation payer by telephone and obtain this information, issue a z2277 - More information is needed to process a payment letter. Attach all single receipts to the z2277 letter and return both the letter and receipt to the compensation payer. The Resources page contains a link to this letter, go to Step 7

Note: if payment is part of a multi receipt, do not attach it to the z2277 letter. Return the completed multi receipt to a Team Leader once all payments have been processed.

7

Process in NCRI screen + Read more ...

Enter the following information from the payment receipt into the applicable fields:

  • ADDRESSEE TYPE - enter 'insurer'
  • NAME - enter 'compensation payer name'
  • ADDR - enter 'address' of the compensation payer from payment receipt
  • P/C - enter 'postcode' of compensation payer from payment receipt (if known)
  • PHONE – enter compensation payer claim/reference number from payment receipt
  • COMP STATUS - enter 'noclaim'
  • COMP TYPE - enter 1 of the following from the payment receipt:
    • workers, public, common, mva or unknown (if not known)
  • ACCIDENT DT - enter the 'date of injury' in DD/MM/YY format if it is listed on the payment receipt. If unknown, enter 'date of processing' as a dummy date
  • SETTLEMENT DT - leave this field blank
  • CLOSED DT - leave this field blank
  • AUTH DT - leave this field blank
  • INJURY/COND - enter 'nature of injury or illness' if it is listed on the payment receipt. If unknown, leave this field blank
  • THIS CONTACT - enter 'today’s date' in DD/MM/YY format, then 'mc' and the word 'receipt', Press [Enter]
  • The NCRI screen will prompt you to update the record at the top control line - type U in the control line, Press [Enter], go to Step 8

Note: if the COMP STATUS field has previously been filled update it to ‘noclaim’. Do not enter any other text in the COMP STATUS field.

8

Add a case note + Read more ...

Service Officers must record details of all actions taken on the NCRI screen. Press [F2] to access the case notes command lines.

If the case has settled

Enter the following compensation payer information in the 'COMMENTS' field:

  • 9000XXXX account number from the payment receipt
  • $Amount of the advance payment
  • Date of receipt of the advance payment
  • Confirmation the z2277 - More information is needed to process a payment – letter has been issued, press [Enter]
  • The NCRI screen will prompt you to update the record at the top control line - type U in the control line, press [Enter]. Use standard case notes where possible, go to Step 9

If the payment is a voluntary recovery for Medicare services already paid by the agency

Enter the following compensation payer information in the 'COMMENTS' field:

  • 9000XXXX account number from the payment receipt
  • $Amount of each Medicare service being repaid including the item number, date of service and the provider’s name (if known)
  • Date of receipt of the voluntary recovery
  • Confirmation the z2277 More information is needed to process a payment letter has been issued (if applicable), press [Enter]
  • The NCRI screen will prompt you to update the record at the top control line - type U in the control line, press [Enter]. Use standard case notes where possible, go to Step 9

9

Save z2277 - More information is needed to process a payment letter (if applicable) + Read more ...

  • Save z2277 - More information is needed to process a payment letter at CompRec\Finance\CP069 for MC Card, 86 & 87 Numbers. Select the appropriate folder by year and month
  • The format for saving is z2277 – (Enter Medicare Care Number) and (enter claimant name)
