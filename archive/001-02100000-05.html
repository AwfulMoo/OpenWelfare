The Department of Employment and Workplace Relations (DEWR) Workforce Australia helpline will assist with general Self-Employment Assistance enquiries and advise the job seeker of any local Self-Employment Assistance providers near them. This phone number is located in Department of Employment and Workplace Relations.

Self-Employment Assistance Helpdesk: where conflicting information about Self-Employment Assistance participation or Allowance is received, contact the Participation Programmes Team.

Job seekers wishing to participate in Self-Employment Assistance are to be referred by Workforce Australia, Disability Employment Services or ParentsNext providers. Services Australia staff are not to refer customers directly to a Self-Employment Assistance provider.

Item

Description

1

Coding for JSP, YA (other) and SpB customers receiving a higher rate of payment than Self-Employment Allowance

Manual coding is only be undertaken by a Service Support Officer.

The 'NEI' (Customer is Self Employed under NEIS) cancellation reason ceased being used from 27 April 2020, but may display for some customers prior to this date.

Prior to 3 December 2022, JSP, YA and SpB job seekers may have had their payment auto-cancelled 'CPP' (Customer on Precluding Payment) upon commencement in Small Business coaching, on the basis that they were receiving Self-Employment Allowance instead. However, some of these job seekers were entitled to a higher rate of income support than that paid under Self-Employment Allowance.

They are:

  • JSP and SpB job seekers who are single with a dependent child
  • JSP, SpB and YA single principal carers with long term (automatic) exemptions
  • JSP and SpB single job seekers aged 60 and over, who have been in receipt of an income support payment for more than 9 continuous months
  • JSP and SpB job seekers who are assessed as either 'Illness Separated', 'Respite Care Couple', or 'Partner in gaol'

These job seekers, if they had no affecting income, were eligible to continue to receive a partial rate of their income support payment on top of their Self-Employment Allowance after the dollar-for-dollar reduction was applied.

The partial rate was calculated manually, taking into account any income received by the job seeker (including any business income associated with the new business being started under Small Business Coaching). The partial rate was then set as a manual rate override. Any changes in income, which affected rate, were manually calculated and set during this partial rate period until the Self-Employment Allowance ceased.

If these job seekers also received employment income, they may also have been eligible for Working Credit.

These customers will remain on a manual rate until their Self-Employment Allowance End Date.

The Self-Employment Allowance End Date will show on the Rate Component Override (RCO) screen. Once this date has passed the system will automatically recalculate their income support payment rate as per their existing assessment rules.

To end a manual rate, see NSS system coding for manual rate of payment.
