This document outlines content relevant when a customer contacts Services Australia intending to claim a payment, or concession card (including some health care cards).

Claim enquiries

When a customer, their nominee or a third party advises they intend to claim a payment or concession card, advise them to submit a claim with all the supporting documents requested to be assessed from the earliest possible date.

When the customer is unable to submit their claim due to vulnerable circumstances, see Intent to claim and vulnerable customers.

If a customer, their nominee or a third party contacts Services Australia about making a claim before the customer is qualified:

  • check if the customer can lodge a complete claim under the early claim provisions, or
  • advise them to submit a claim when they become qualified

Note: early claim provisions do not apply to concession card claims.

Claim lodgement

For further information on lodging a claim, select the appropriate link below:

  • a social security payment or concession card
  • ABSTUDY
  • Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme
  • Family Assistance

Financial Hardship

Different procedures apply when a customer contacts Services Australia stating they are in financial hardship and requests urgent processing of a new claim. See:

  • ABSTUDY and student claims
  • Disability Support Pension
  • Farm Household Allowance
  • Request for urgent processing of a new claim when experiencing financial hardship
  • Other income support payments
  • Immediate claim and priority processing
  • Double Orphan Pension
