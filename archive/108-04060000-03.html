Assessing income of customers providing child care

Step

Action

1

Customer advises child care business income + Read more ...

Customer advises receipt of business income from child care.

Where a customer operates a business providing child care, the income from child care is income from self-employment, it is not employment income. It is used when calculating the accrual of Working Credits. However, the customer is unable to use Working Credits to offset this income.

Some customers may have an ABN and be required to lodge Business Activity Statements (BAS). The BAS will not replace income tax returns for determining social security income support entitlement, as they do not contain the necessary information.

If the customer is operating the child care business through a:

  • Sole trader or partnership structure, follow this procedure. For information about assessing income and assets, see:
    • Sole traders
    • Partnerships
  • Private trust or private company, the case must be referred to a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO). CAOs see:
    • Assessment of income from trusts and companies
    • Assessment of assets for trusts and companies
    • Procedure ends here.

Determine the gross amount of income received. If the customer can provide documentary evidence to substantiate the amount claimed for associated costs, a deduction should be allowed for these items.

Note: if the customer's tax return does not reflect current income, they should supply a revised profit and loss statement (SU580). Some changes which might have occurred include:

  • the number of children being cared for has changed
  • the level of care has changed

Has the customer provided the business tax return and profit and loss statement, or interim profit and loss statement?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Calculate income from child care + Read more ...

Calculate the net income from child care using documentation provided.

  • Deduct the amount of associated costs from the gross amount to get the net income from child care
  • Go to Step 7

3

Can customer give evidence + Read more ...

Can the customer provide the business tax return and financial statements, or interim profit and loss statement within 14 days?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Calculate income from evidence provided + Read more ...

  • Calculate the income using the figures provided. Subtract the allowable associated expenses from the gross amount to determine the net business income. For more information about allowable expenses, see Business deductions
  • Go to Step 7

5

Financial statements, tax return or profit and loss statement not available + Read more ...

In the unlikely event the customer cannot provide a profit and loss statement, use the following formula to calculate the business percentage of household costs for the period of child care provision.

A x B x C = D where:

  • A = days worked in a year/ 365
  • B = hours worked in a day / 24
  • C = % of home used for child care (expressed as a figure divided by 100)
  • D = business percentage to be applied to total annual household costs

If the period of the child care provision under assessment is less than a full year, the days worked must be annualised, by dividing the number of days in the period and multiplying by 365.

The Resources page contains links to business assessment tools and an example of using the formula to calculate deductions against child care income when an income tax return or a profit and loss statement is not available.

6

Apply business percentage + Read more ...

Apply the business percentage to total household costs for the period:

  • Estimate total household costs for the period of child care provision (for example, two weeks)
  • Multiply this by the business percentage D calculated at Step 5
  • Subtract the resulting amount from the gross income for the same period (declared by customer at Step 1). This will be the net income from child care

For an example, see the Resources page.

7

Code Display on Access DOC + Read more ...

This may already have been done. It will alert Service Officers to code future business income correctly.

Code 'DOC' in Nxt: field then press [Enter] and code:

  • 'NSE' (Non standard earnings assessment) in the Msg: field
  • a future date in the Expiry: field (for example, 12 months in the future, or whenever the work is expected to cease)
  • in the txt: field of the DOC, include the following details:
    • the advice given to customer about notification obligations
    • details of how income from self-employment is to be calculated (for example, from tax return, from profit and loss statement, or by using the formula)

8

Update Families estimate and finalise activity + Read more ...

Check if the customer needs to revise their current annual income estimate for Family Tax Benefit (FTB), Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and advise payment choices available to help reduce the risk of overpayment. See Updating income estimates for the current financial year.

Finalise activity

  • Record the details in a DOC
  • Finalise activity via Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Advise customer of outcome, including notification obligations
