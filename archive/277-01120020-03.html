Notification received - payer deceased

Step

Action

1

Notification of death + Read more ...

If the customer is Indigenous obtain permission before mentioning the deceased by name. Check the indicators on the Customer window for an Indigenous indicator.

Deceased payers are managed within the Deceased Customer Management (DCM) Team. If advised of a payer’s death, warm transfer the call to the DCM Team using Interaction Workspace (iWS) Softphone.

Child Support can be notified of a customer’s death by:

  • Centrelink, go to Step 2
  • the payee or a third party, go to Step 3

2

Centrelink notification + Read more ...

When Centrelink have been informed of a mutual customer’s death the information is automatically uploaded to the Deceased Client window and an intray is generated.

Note: Centrelink notification alone is not sufficient proof of a customer’s death, DCM Service Officers must obtain additional proof. Go to Step 4.

3

Notification from payee or third party + Read more ...

When a payee or third party notifies Child Support of a payer’s death, obtain the following information:

  • name and contact details of the caller, if a third party
  • sufficient detail to identify the deceased, such as name, address and date of birth
  • date of death
  • country/state/town the death occurred in
  • if the payer had a will
  • name and contact details of the executor or person who will be finalising the affairs of the deceased
  • the name of the funeral director
  • where the children are residing, and
  • if the caller does not know these details, try to obtain contact details of another person who may

Note: if the caller is a third party do not disclose any information about either parent or the case to the caller. Any information they provide, however, can be taken and recorded.

If the caller is upset, check if there is someone available that the caller would prefer Child Support to speak with or whether they would like to continue the call at a later date. If the caller wishes to continue the call at a later date document the conversation on the Deceased Client window and offer to transfer them to the Family Relationship Advice Line or Social worker and other service referrals for child support customers. See Products and services Cuba process help for information. Go to Step 4.

If the caller is the payee and wishes to continue the call, advise the following:

  • the case will be ended, from the day before the date of death, once the death has been confirmed
  • any child support debt or overpayment on the case and if these are likely to change
  • options available for addressing child support debt or overpayment. See Step 5 in Table 2 for information, and
  • support services are available and, if appropriate, offer to transfer them to the Family Relationship Advice Line or Social worker and other service referrals for child support customers. See Products and services Cuba process help

4

Immediate action required + Read more ...

When notification of death is received the following steps are required for all cases until proof of death is received and the case finalised:

  • lock the deceased customer, and any associated customers
  • record the death in the Deceased Client window. See Deceased Customer Management Cuba process help for more information
  • do not review or reconcile any estimates. If a review or reconciliation is underway suspend activity until a case management strategy is decided
  • add a Sensitive Issue Indicator to the deceased customer and all associated customers, see Sensitive issue management Cuba process help, Record a sensitive issue, and
  • if notification of death is received via Centrelink exchange of information or through the business (for a customer who uses Centrelink services) and more information is required, check Customer First for the required information

If the case is Collect or Private Collect with arrears the following action is also required:

  • suspend payer statements, in the Payment Options window
  • place all payments on hold
  • suspend the accrual of late payment penalties (LPP)
  • suspend all collection action (stopping any EW and Centrelink deductions)

See the Deceased Customer Management Key Performance Indicator Checklist.

Is either the payee or payer involved in an International case? International cases can be identified by an 'INT' indicator on either of the parent's customer screens.

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

International case + Read more ...

Go to the Case screen to see what type of application the case is based on.

If the case type is ASSESSMENT, action the deceased notification in the usual way. Go to Step 6.

If the case type is INTERL CO/AGR or INTERL ASSMNT, contact the Overseas Team.

  • Create an INTERNATIONAL STAKEHOLDER FOLLOW UP intray
  • Reset the intray due date to 2 business days from today's date - instead of 14 days
  • Ensure the Service Officer's contact details are added to the intray notepad with the reason for contacting the Overseas Team, for example, DCM International case – INTERL CO/AGR or INTERL ASSMENT, and route to NO S001 OVERSEAS TM - None

An Overseas Service Officer will work collaboratively with the Child Support Service Officer and undertake any necessary liaison with the Overseas Authority. For example, the Overseas Authority may assist Child Support in establishing proof of death and whether or not there is a will.

The Overseas Authority must be consulted on whether collection of any arrears is required and the date the case should be ended.

The DCM officer will be kept informed and all actions will be recorded on the customer’s Cuba record.

Procedure ends here.

6

Obtain proof of death + Read more ...

Proof of death must be obtained before processing the terminating event. The following can be used as proof of death:

  • External Searches Guide – search by the State the customer died in
  • funeral notice from a newspaper – citing the customer's name, date of birth, date of death or other identifying detail
  • confirmation from the public trustee, coroner's office, hospital or police
  • death certificate from the Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM), and
  • confirmation from the Department of Foreign Affairs (if the person died overseas)

Note: due to the cost involved in obtaining a death certificate from BDM, this request should only be pursued if no other proof can be obtained. BDM take a minimum of 6 weeks (12 weeks where there is a Coroner’s enquiry) to produce a death certificate.

Proof of death should generally be in writing. If unable to obtain written evidence, or unable to obtain any proof of death but believe the customer is deceased, escalate the query, via a Service Support Officer, to a site Program Support Manager to assist in the decision making process. See Technical support in Child Support.

In the event an Indigenous customer passed away in a remote location, the community manager or elder may be able to assist. Contact the local Indigenous Relations Officer who will draw on their networks to progress the matter.

Centrelink notification alone is not sufficient to process a customer’s death. Additional proof must be obtained.

Once the proof of death has been obtained, go to Table 2.

For International Cases (New Zealand In), to update Cuba, go to Step 7.

  • CSID

  • Date of death
  • Proof of death document
  • Testacy searches: Y/N

For the following searches, dates of contact are required:

  • public trustee, Y/N
  • probate searches, Y/N
  • contact with next of kin, Y/N
  • is there a will, Y/N
  • testacy, has an executor been identified, Y/N/NA
  • finalised estate, Y/N/NA
  • intestate; letters of administration, Y/N/NA

If no collection avenue is identified, refer to CAT for withdrawal.

Route the intray to:

  • Region: INTERNATIONAL
  • Office: INTERNATIONAL HOBART
  • Stream/Team: MC S003 CENT AUTH TM 1
  • Position: NZ INFO REQUEST

Save the notepad from the ‘Return to NZ – Customer Deceased’ Intray into the Case Screen with the title – Return to NZ – Customer Deceased.

If the Return meets the criteria, this is the end of the process for the DCM Service Officer.

If the Customer Deceased Return has all required details, the International Central Authority (CA) Team will proceed with the Withdrawal of the case and return to NZ.

  • The Central Authority (CA) Service Officer will check the details to ensure all details have been provided to return the case as Customer Deceased
  • The CA Service Officer will process the withdrawal
  • The CA Service Officer will advise NZIR of the Return by completing the ‘Customer Deceased’ tab on the ‘Returns to NZ’ spreadsheet

Process death + Read more ...

Once proof of death has been obtained process the death in the Deceased Client window. See Deceased Customer Management Cuba process help.

Before running Eligibility:

  • update care records; if the payee now has care of the children, change the care to reflect this
  • check for an A18 application and if so, change the last day of secondary education to the day/date immediately prior to the date of death

Customer contact + Read more ...

Each call must be approached in a sensitive and considerate manner. Every customer contact will differ. Gauge the situation at the time of the call. If unsure how to proceed, speak to Team Leader or Service Support Officer.

Before contacting the payee determine:

  • if there has been previous contact regarding the death, go to Step 3, or
  • if there has been no previous contact regarding the death, go to Step 4

3

Previous contact + Read more ...

If Child Support has already spoken to the payee about the death, documenting the conversation, confirm the following:

  • executor details if not already obtained
  • the case has ended and the date it ended from
  • that letters will be issued confirming the case has been ended
  • any amount the payer owes in child support. Explain:
    • the collection process if the payee requests collection of unpaid child support, and
    • the options available for addressing the arrears
  • any overpayment the payee owes the payer, for more information see Child support overpayments and other payee debt
  • go to Step 5

4

No previous contact + Read more ...

The payee may not be aware of the payer’s death. Approach this conversation with extreme caution.

If Child Support has not had any contact with the payee since the payer’s death allow a reasonable amount of time (14 days from date of death) before contacting the payee.

Before contacting the payee check previous communications to see if there are any records indicating that the customers were in contact with each other, that is, is the payee likely to be aware of the payer’s death?

Contact the payee and advise that Child Support have been notified of the death of the payer and their child support case has ended.

If the payee is upset, check if there is someone available that the payee would prefer Child Support to speak with or if they would like to continue the call at a later date. If the payee wishes to continue the call at a later date document the conversation in the Deceased Client window and offer to transfer them to the Family Relationship Advice Line. Once contact has been documented, go to Step 5.

If the payee wishes to continue the call, advise:

  • the case has ended, from the day before the date of death
  • any arrears on the case and if these are likely to change
  • the options available for addressing the arrears, for information go to Step 5, and
  • the support services available and, if appropriate, offer to transfer them to the Family Relationship Advice Line or Social worker and other service referrals for child support customers See also Products and services Cuba process help

5

End case + Read more ...

Before ending the case and issuing letters consider if the case is:

  • Private Collect (no arrears), go to Step 6
  • Collect or Private Collect with child support debt or overpayment, go to Step 7

6

Private Collect (no arrears) + Read more ...

If the case is Private Collect and there is no debt or other outstanding issues, such as consolidated revenue debt, excess cash or overpayments use the DCM letters macro to issue a letter to the payee advising that the case has been ended. In these circumstances there is no need to locate or contact the executor for the payer’s estate.

International case

Do not issue the payee letter until the Central Authority Service Officer has been consulted. They will assist to determine whether the letter should be sent to the payee or whether the Central Authority Team will need to write to the overseas jurisdiction.

If the case is Private Collect but has a consolidated revenue debt, excess cash or overpayment, go to Step 7.

7

Collect or Private Collect with arrears + Read more ...

Use the DCM letters macro to issue a letter to the payee advising the case has been ended and detailing any outstanding issues, except where the payer is a payer in a NZ 'In' case.

Only send letters to an executor when Child Support has sighted proof of executorship and has recorded them as a customer representative, see Customer Representative Process Help. Use the DCM letters macro to issue a letter to the executor advising that the case has been ended and detailing any outstanding issues.

If the payer owed a debt, is owed overpaid child support or there are other outstanding issues:

  • check the notes in the Deceased Client window to determine if the payer had a will and is:
  • testate - died with a valid will, go to Table 3
  • intestate - died without a valid will, go to Table 4

Child support debt with a testate payer + Read more ...

When a testate payer has a debt the options are as follows:

  • Collection, go to Step 3
  • Non - collection, including:
  • opt out and discharge
  • waiver
  • non-pursuit, or
  • obsolete entry, go to Step 5

3

Collection + Read more ...

The estate must have an executor, administrator or executor de son tort to collect any arrears. If there is no executor, administrator or executor de son tort, go to Table 4.

Upon death a person’s assets cease to belong to them. Any assets become held in trust as part of their estate, which is administered by an executor.

The usual administrative enforcement options (such as TRIPs and s72 notices) only apply to funds held for, or on behalf of, a child support debtor. Because the assets no longer belong to a child support debtor (the payer) normal enforcement options must not be used post death. As such Child Support cannot collect from bank accounts, superannuation, life insurance policies, etc. because the funds/assets are not being held in the payer’s name. Notices cannot be issued against an executor or estate because they are not a child support debtor.

For information about collecting from a payer's estate, go to Step 4.

4

Collection from a payer’s estate + Read more ...

To collect from a payer's estate:

1. Contact the executor, administrator or executor de son tort

executor or administrator

Call the executor or administrator and if Child Support has not spoken to them already, advise:

  • that there is a child support debt that needs to be paid out of the estate
  • they are required to lodge any outstanding tax returns for the payer, which may change the debt owed to Child Support
  • try to gauge an expected timeframe for payment, and
  • advise they can apply for a waiver on the payer’s behalf

Executor de son tort

Call the executor de son tort and, if Child Support has not spoken to them already, advise:

  • that there is a child support debt that needs to be paid out of the estate, and
  • try to gauge an expected timeframe for payment

Note: an executor de son tort is not authorised or required to lodge tax returns for the estate and cannot waive any debts owing to the estate. They can only be asked to pay the debt if they distribute the estate. They cannot act as executor for any other purpose.

2. Issue proof of debt notice

Once the executor or administrator has been spoken to:

  • issue a proof of debt notice, and
  • use the DCM letters macro to attach a letter detailing the arrears and any outstanding tax returns

Once the executor de son tort has been spoken to:

  • using the executor do son tort letter prepare a letter detailing the arrears for local printing and issue

3. Create intray and monitor for payment

Create an intray to monitor for payment over a 60 day period. If payment is received use the DCM letters macro to issue a letter for the executor and the payee advising that the arrears have been paid and that the case has been finalised.

Note: any holds previously placed on the case will have to be removed before the funds can be disbursed to the payee.

If no payment is received recontact the executor and ask if any funds are likely to be available. If funds are likely to become available create a subsequent intray and continue to monitor for payment.

If funds are not likely to become available:

  • ensure the executor is aware of the option to apply for a waiver
  • recontact the payee to explain the situation and discuss the possibility of opt out and discharge, and
  • consider non-pursuit

If unable to collect the arrears the debt must still be addressed prior to finalising and unlocking the case, using 1 of the options in Step 2.

Estates can take a long time to be finalised, sometimes several years.

5

Non-collection of debt + Read more ...

Opt out and discharge

When contacting the payee about opting out and discharging the arrears, see Collection of child support payments privately and/or discharging arrears.

Note: Centrelink have advised that there is no effect on Family Tax Benefit if the arrears are discharged after death, that is, there is no reconciliation, however advise the payee to contact Centrelink to discuss their individual circumstances.

Waiver

For a waiver application to be considered by the Department of Finance the executor must complete the representative authority form and provide a copy of the will (or letters of administration). See Waivers for Child Support customers for more information on how to progress a waiver application.

Non-pursuit

Non-pursuit should only be considered if unable to address the debt in any other way. If no collection avenues are available, see Non-pursuit of individual child support debts.

Obsolete entry

An entry associated with a testate payer is only eligible for deletion from the Child Support Register in accordance with section 41 of the Child Support (Registration and Collection) Act 1988 if the payer’s estate has been finalised with no avenue for further collection. See Obsolete Child Support entries.

6

Overpayment + Read more ...

If the payer has overpaid child support to the payee, check if the overpayment is recoverable. For more information, see Child support overpayments and other payee debt.

If the overpayment is recoverable, contact the executor. Discuss if they want to make an election for Child Support not to recover the overpayment.

Explain:

  • such an election means the payee does not have to repay the overpaid monies
  • the estate will no longer be entitled to the amount, and
  • the election cannot be reversed and the overpayment cannot later be reclaimed later

If the executor has requested collection of overpayment go to Step 7.

If the overpayment is not a debt due to the Commonwealth (not a recoverable overpayment) follow the process in Information for payers about child support overpayments.

Election for an overpayment not to be recovered

If the executor has elected for the overpayment not to be recovered:

  • contact the payee
  • advise that the amount is no longer owed
  • create unique letters to both the payee and executor advising that the overpayment has been waived and the case has been finalised

For more information, see Overpayments Cuba process help.

7

Collection from payee + Read more ...

If the executor requests collection, advise that Child Support will attempt to recover the overpayment and reimburse monies to the estate as they are collected from the payee.

Contact the payee to advise how the overpayment occurred and they are required to repay the amount. Note: the payee may apply to the Department of Finance for a waiver of the overpayment. For more information, see Waivers for Child Support customers.

To process collection of the overpayment with the payee, see Debt repayment

Document all calls and decisions made. If a payment arrangement has been made, See Payment arrangement Cuba process help.

Once monies are received from the payee they should be disbursed to the estate, via a bank account provided by the executor, or by cheque.

If Child Support cannot collect the overpayment non-pursuit should be considered. See Non-pursuit of individual child support debts

8

Consolidated revenue debt + Read more ...

If the payer has a consolidated revenue debt, follow the process detailed in Step 2.

The executor, or Child Support on behalf of the executor, can apply for a waiver of the consolidated revenue debt. See Waivers for Child Support customers.

9

Excess cash + Read more ...

If the payer has excess cash on their account, check that there are no arrears owed to the payee. If the payee is owed child support remove any holds placed on the case and disburse the funds.

If there is no debt contact the executor and make arrangements to refund the excess cash to the estate, either via a bank account provided by the executor or by cheque.

Intestate payer - collection options

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Finalising outstanding issues + Read more ...

If the payer is intestate there are limited avenues available to finalise any outstanding child support issues.

If no executor details have been provided attempt to locate an executor before pursuing the options for an intestate payer.

To locate an executor:

  • contact the payer’s next of kin, family or surviving parent and ask if they are aware of an executor
  • contact Centrelink to find out who reported the death and if they know who the executor is, and
  • contact the public trustee in the state where the deceased resided, unless the deceased resided in Western Australia or Tasmania, where a fee is charged

If an executor is located from the above searches, go to Table 3.

If no executor is located, go to Step 2.

2

Child support debt + Read more ...

If the payer has debt outstanding on their account and is intestate the options for addressing arrears are:

  • opt out and discharge, go to Step 3
  • letters of administration, go to Step 4
  • public trustee, go to Step 5
  • non-pursuit or waiver, go to Step 6
  • obsolete entry, go to Step 7

If there are no arrears, but the case has 1 or more of the following issues:

  • overpayment of child support owed to the payer
  • consolidated revenue debt
  • excess cash
  • go to Step 8

3

Opt out and discharge + Read more ...

Contact the payee and explain that (for intestate payers):

  • no collection options available
  • will be unable to collect the arrears, and
  • debt will not be pursued, unless someone applies for letters of administration

If the payee wishes to opt out and discharge the arrears see Collection of child support payments privately and/or discharging arrears.

Note: Centrelink have advised that there is no effect on the payee’s Family Tax Benefit if the arrears are discharged after death, that is, there is no reconciliation. However, advise the payee to contact Centrelink to discuss their individual circumstances.

4

Letters of administration + Read more ...

The payer’s family, or other party, can apply for letters of administration to administer the payer’s estate. Letters of administration are dealt with in the Supreme courts, so the costs and processes may vary state by state. However, generally applying for letters of administration is a costly process; the cost may be greater than the value of the estate, resulting in it not being worthwhile to apply for letters of administration.

If there is a family member or other party considering applying for letters of administration create an intray and monitor for 60 days. If letters of administration are received treat the administrator in the same manner as an executor and follow the process detailed in Step 3 in Table 3.

If Child Support does not receive letters of administration in that time, contact the person who was considering applying and check if that is still their intention.

If no-one is going to apply for letters of administration:

  • opt out and discharge, go to Step 3
  • non-pursuit, go to Step 6

The arrears must be addressed before unlocking the customers.

Note: administrators perform a similar role to that of an executor but distribute the assets of the deceased according to intestacy laws.

5

Public trustee + Read more ...

If the payer has significant assets their family may be able to approach the public trustee to administer the estate. The requirements of the public trustee will vary from state to state, however generally the value of the assets must be reasonably significant and the public trustee charge according to the value of the estate.

If the estate is taken on by the public trustee treat them in the same manner as an executor and follow the process detailed in Step 3 in Table 3.

Note: Child Support cannot refer a deceased customer’s estate to the public trustee.

Different legislation applies to Indigenous estates. If the customer is Indigenous contact the relevant public trustee to determine their requirements for accepting a referral to administer the estate.

If the payer’s estate is not going to be referred to the public trustee go to:

  • opt out and discharge, go to Step 3, or
  • non-pursuit, go to Step 6

The arrears must be addressed before unlocking the customers.

6

Non-pursuit or waiver + Read more ...

Non-pursuit

Non-pursuit should only be considered if unable to address the debt in any other way.

Waiver

Currently Child Support cannot apply for a waiver on behalf of an intestate payer. In cases where there are no other options for addressing the debt, non-pursue the debt.

See Non-pursuit of individual child support debts.

7

Obsolete entry + Read more ...

An entry associated with an intestate payer is only eligible for deletion from the Child Support Register in accordance with section 41 of the Child Support (Registration and Collection) Act 1988 if:

  • the payer has no known assets, and
  • the case has been ended for 2 or more years

See Obsolete Child Support entries.

8

No arrears on case - other issues + Read more ...

Review the following, if the case has any of the below issues.

Overpayment

If the payer has overpaid child support to the payee and the payer is intestate, do not take collection action to recover the overpayment. Child Support has no means to refund the overpayment to an intestate payer.

To ensure automatic collection does not occur, remove the overpayment in the system using the Gift functionality.

Note: the payee may apply to the Department of Finance for a waiver of the overpayment. For more information, see Waivers for Child Support customers.

For more information, see Overpayments Cuba process help.

If letters of administration are subsequently obtained follow the process for testate payers at Step 6 in Table 3.

Consolidated revenue debt

If the payer is intestate there are no options for collection, unless the payer’s family apply for letters of administration or refer the estate to the public trustee. In this instance move to not pursue the debt. See Non-pursuit of individual child support debts.

Excess cash

If the payer is intestate there is no action that can be taken in relation to the excess cash, it will remain as excess cash on the payer’s account.
