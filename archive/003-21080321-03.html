This page has the process to record the outcome of an urgent payment request on the customer's record and issue payment if approved. Staff must first follow the relevant procedure to consider the request and make a decision to approve or deny it.

Urgent payment requests + Read more ...

The script provides the general information below about urgent payment eligibility. Some of this is out-of-date:

  • The title of 003-10010000 has been updated:

Payment Check + Read more ...

The script will determine the following:

  • Check there are enough net funds in the next due payment before continuing
  • Consider any deductions before determining what amount can be issued

Select Next.

Verifying the customer’s identification + Read more ...

Has the customer established their identity?

For help, see Authenticating a Centrelink customer.

  • Select either Yes or No
  • Select Next

If the customer has not established their identity, the script will record that the payment is to be denied.

6

Action to be taken + Read more ...

The script gives these options:

  • Assess request
  • Deny the payment
  • Internally refer the request (do not select this option)
  • Offer Alternative Assistance (the urgent payment will be recorded as denied)
  • Customer does not agree with an Urgent Payment decision

Select Assess request.

Complete Source and Date of receipt fields.

Select Continue.

7

Urgent payment approval + Read more ...

The script gives 2 options as the reason for request:

  • Exceptional and unforeseen circumstances (limited to $200 or less)
  • Centrelink/Departmental error (limited to $1000 or less)

If Exceptional and unforeseen circumstances, the amount to be issued is the lowest of:

  • the amount requested by the customer
  • the available funds, or
  • the maximum amount of $200

No payments over $200 are to be issued.

Select Exceptional and unforeseen circumstances (this is recorded in the DOC only, and is not the payment reason code), or Centrelink/Departmental error.

Select Continue.

As part of the approval process, provide extra details to be included in the DOC:

  • why the payment has been approved
  • any alternative assistance offered and why it was refused by the customer

Once completed, select Continue.

8

Select method of payment + Read more ...

On this screen:

  • Code the amount to be paid to the customer. The script will advise the maximum amount available, but the Service Officer must code the amount to be paid, taking into account the maximum urgent payment limit
  • Choose the payment method to issue the payment

Payment is made by direct credit, New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) or Electronic Benefit Transfer (EBT) card.

Only use an EBT card when direct credit, NPP and RTGS are not suitable payment methods.

Agents and Remote Service Centres do not have access to EBT cards. Due to the remote location of these sites, the method of payment is limited to direct credit, NPP or RTGS. Do not refer a customer to these sites to collect a payment.

If the customer's regular payments are made to a payment nominee or parent, the urgent payment must also be made into the regular payment account by direct credit, NPP or RTGS, unless the third party agrees for payment to be made directly to the customer (only for voluntary arrangements).

If the payment will be issued by:

  • Direct credit, NPP or RTGS, go to Step 9
  • EBT card
    • Conditional approval is to be given. Go to Step 10
    • Go to Step 11

9

Issue urgent payment via direct credit or Priority Direct Credit (NPP or RTGS) + Read more ...

New Payments Platform (NPP) is the preferred method of delivery for urgent payments.

Payment by Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) or direct credit will delay the customer's access to the payment by 1 to 2 business days.

NPP

If the customer’s account is reachable via the NPP

  • the payment will deliver to the customer’s account immediately, regardless of the time or day of request
  • but the payment fails to deliver, it will automatically re-issue via RTGS if available, or via direct credit

If the account is not reachable via NPP, RTGS will be available.

Payments should only be made by NPP or RTGS when one regular payment has been made to the account.

RTGS

Opening hours are weekdays 8:00 am - 3:30 pm (Victoria/NSW time), excluding national public holidays observed on the same day in both Sydney and Melbourne.

If the RTGS activity is completed after 3:30 pm (AEST) (including QOL), the payment will not issue until the next working day, from 9am. If payment is to the smaller banks and credit unions, it may take up to 2 working days to go into the customer's account.

When offering RTGS, make sure the customer understands their bank may charge a fee for processing the payment, and timeframes vary between banks. Services Australia does not have details of timeframes or fees. The customer will need to negotiate any fee with their bank.

Direct credit

The expected delivery date will be:

  • 1 working day for payments made via the Newstart System (NSS)
  • 2 working days for payment made via all other systems

Overdrawn accounts

If the customer advises the account is overdrawn, it is their choice whether or not payment is made to the account. Tell the customer that:

  • the bank may retain some or all of the overdrawn amount; and
  • it will take longer for the payment to be available as manual processing may be required by the bank

Select how the payment will be issued:

  • New Payments Platform (if available)
  • Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) - this option will only display if NPP is not available
  • Direct Credit - will need to record why RTGS was not chosen as the payment method

Select Next.

Choose from the drop down menu, then select Next.

Go to Step 12.

10

Conditional approval for urgent payment to be made via EBT + Read more ...

Only issue EBT cards in denominations of $20 or $50, as these are the amounts available at ATMs.

Check the Office Locator page of the service centre the customer will be attending. Check for any general notes for the service centre. See the Resources page for a link.

Do not activate EBT cards before the customer attends the service centre.

Rural Agency/Remote Service Centres do not have access to EBT cards. Do not refer the customer or their representative to these sites to collect a payment. As these are remote sites, payment has to be by direct credit or NPP.

Tell the customer:

  • to attend the service centre on the day of approval during the preferred walk-in times (as per Office Locator) and present at reception or to the Customer Liaison Officer (CLO)
  • if they do not attend the service centre on the day urgent payment is approved the customer will need to reapply for urgent payment
  • they need to provide identity documents including at least one photo identity document, and/or answer questions from details on their customer record to verify Proof of Record Ownership. See Authenticating a Centrelink customer
  • if they cannot confirm their identity or establish Proof of Record Ownership, the EBT card will not be issued

Service Officers in:

  • a Participation Solutions Team or Processing Team, go to Step 11
  • any other team, go to Step 12

11

Create DOC - Participation Solutions Team and Processing Teams + Read more ...

Participation Solutions Team and Processing Teams cannot process an EBT request using the Immediate Payment requests script.

Create a DOC to advise the service centre the customer will be attending to collect the EBT card. The DOC should include:

  • Reason: select the correct reason
  • Explanation: for example,
    • For Except & unforeseen: Customer needs funds as fridge has broken down and needs to be replaced. Customer needs $200. Cust understands they are eligible for up to 2 urgent payments in a 12 month period. Cust knows payment will be reduced on normal delivery date and says will have enough money to meet regular expenses
    • For dept delay: Customer did not attend interview but DOC on record advises customer contacted to advise they have hospital appointment at same time and could not attend. Interview was not rebooked by staff member which cause payment to be suspended
  • Why RTGS (or other payment method) is not being used
  • amount to be paid, for details on payment limitations, see Immediate payment by payment method EBT card
  • name and logon of approving officer

The DOC can be finalised.

The service centre needs to run the Immediate Payment requests script to issue the EBT to the customer.

Procedure ends here.

12

Record payment details + Read more ...

Determine the payment reason code.

The Immediate Payment requests script will pause on the Issue Future Payment (IFP) screen for the Service Officer to enter the correct reason:

  • EXC - Exceptional and Unforeseen (first 2 payments)
  • ECI - Extraordinary Circumstances (third or subsequent payment)
  • DEP - Centrelink delay or Holiday Processing
  • FNL - Funeral

Use the correct code: an incorrect code will stop the customer from being able to use self service. If the payment is for any reason other than the 4 above, e.g. reissuing a rejected payment, check Issue Future Payment (IFP) screen codes.

After entering the correct code, press [Enter], then select OK.

The script will create a DOC and will pause for the Service Officer to add more details. The DOC must include the urgent payment category and a detailed report of the:

  • Reason: select the correct reason
  • Alternative assistance: was this discussed and outcome. Include this in the explanation
  • Explanation: for example:
    • For Except & unforeseen: Customer requires funds as fridge has broken down and needs to be replaced. Customer requires $200. Cust understands they are eligible for up to 2 urgent payments in a 12 month period. Cust knows payment will be reduced on normal delivery date and says will have enough money to meet regular expenses. Alternative assistance was offered but refused as customer needed funds
    • For dept delay: Customer did not attend interview but DOC on record advises customer contacted to advise they have hospital appointment at same time and could not attend. Interview was not rebooked by staff member, which caused payment to be suspended. Alternative assistance was offered but refused
    • For Funeral: Funeral verified. Cust needs money for petrol to travel from Adelaide to Mt Gambier for funeral of aunt. Rome2Rio indicates travel costs will be $45. Customer has available funds of $215. Confirmed no other expenses will be required for funeral. Urgent payment granted for $45. Customer has confirmed they will be able to meet regular expenses due to reduced rate. Customer has been advised they will need to contact again if they need funds for return trip. Alternative assistance was offered but refused
  • Details of the staff member from whom approval was sought (when granting more than 2 urgent payments in 12 months)
  • Documents or verifiable evidence provided by the customer (if it was required)
  • Amount approved and how this was decided
  • Method of payment
  • Reason, if paid by EBT, why direct credit and/or RTGS was not considered appropriate

If conditional approval is given, the script will transfer the DOC to the customer's nearest service centre.

The script has not been updated and will ask 'Has the customer made 10 or more urgent payment requests in the last six months'. Answer No to this question.

Tell the customer the expected delivery time/date of the payment.

If the payment has been issued by:

  • NPP, the script will advise the Service Officer to wait 1 minute to check the activity has been completed, to make sure the payment has been issued
  • EBT, make sure correct issuing steps are followed as per Immediate payment by payment method EBT card

If the payment has been selected for Quality On Line (QOL), go to Step 13.

Otherwise, procedure ends here.

13

Activity selected by QOL + Read more ...

QOL checking will not apply to payments delivered via the NPP.

The activity must be checked while the customer is still in contact (face-to-face or phone call). This is regardless of the payment method.

  • The customer contact does not stop until the Payment Summary (PS) screen shows that the payment has been issued
  • A Service Officer must not stop contact with the customer until after the urgent QOL check has been requested and completed. If the activity is not passed, the Service Officer must either correct the activity or tell the customer they are not eligible for the payment

For an RTGS payment to be paid to the customer, the activity must be finalised (including QOL completion) before 3.30pm Canberra, Sydney or Melbourne time. Otherwise, the payment will not be delivered until the next business day.

See the Resources page for information on the role of QOL checkers.

Using the script to deny a request for urgent payment

Table 3: how to run the Immediate Payment requests script to record the decision.

Step

Action

1

Run Immediate Payment requests script + Read more ...

The customer is not eligible for an urgent payment.

Farmer Assistance hotline staff only: Record the details of the rejection in a DOC.

Status Resolution Support Service customers - Record details of the rejection using the Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Multicultural > SRSS Urgent Payment Outcome

Run the Immediate Payment requests script to record the details of the denied payment. Make sure there is enough detail for other Service Officers to explain the decision without contacting the decision maker.

The script has not been updated and will ask 'Has the customer made 10 or more urgent payment requests in the last 6 months'. Answer No to this question.

If the script is unavailable, record details of the decision in a DOC.

2

Action to be taken + Read more ...

The script gives these options:

  • Assess request
  • Deny the payment
  • Internally refer the request (do not select this option)
  • Offer Alternative Assistance (the urgent payment will be recorded as denied)
  • Customer does not agree with an Urgent Payment decision

Select Deny the payment.

Complete Source and Date of receipt fields.

Select Continue.

3

Reason payment denied + Read more ...

The script gives these options for denying the urgent payment:

  • Urgent request already granted today
  • Urgent request already denied today
  • Does not meet ‘exceptional and unforeseen circumstances’ criteria

Select the correct option.

Select Continue.

If rejecting the customer because of insufficient accrued funds, choose the does not meet ‘exceptional and unforeseen circumstances’ option.

4

Record details of decision to deny payment + Read more ...

On the Reason Urgent Payment not Approved screen, DOC the details of the decision to deny an urgent payment on the customer's record.

The DOC must explain the reason the request was denied, including all details so the decision can be explained if the customer disagrees. Note: the reason section must include enough detail to allow the next Service Officer to understand why it was denied.

If the customer requests a reconsideration of the decision, see Customer disagrees with an urgent payment decision.

5

Does the customer need to be referred for alternative assistance? + Read more ...

If the customer has been denied an urgent payment, they should be offered alternative assistance.

The script gives the following options:

  • Yes
  • No
  • Has already been referred

Select the correct option

Select Continue.

A DOC will be created on the customer’s record.

6

Consider referral to social worker + Read more ...

Consider a referral to a social worker for customers presenting with highly complex needs, in line with the social work priority groups. These groups include:

  • customers at risk of suicide or self-harm
  • young people without adequate support
  • customers experiencing family and domestic violence. Note: social workers are the decision maker for Crisis Payments due to family and domestic violence

Refer the customer to a social worker if they display suicidal or possible self-harming behaviours. See Customers talking about suicide or self-harm.

See the Resources page for FAQs and information about roles.

If a social work referral is appropriate:

  • Refer the customer to the Social Work Line via a warm handoff. Select option 2 'if calling from Centrelink'
  • If unable to contact a social worker, complete a Fast Note. Select Auto Text, use Emergency > Urgent Payment > Referral to SWO due to UP assessment

For help, see Social work service referral.

Customer attends service centre to collect urgent payment

Table 4: how to use the Immediate Payment requests script and issue the urgent payment to the customer.

Step

Action

1

Confirm the customer is the person approved for the urgent payment + Read more ...

Before issuing an urgent payment, authenticate the customer. Ask questions to verify Proof of Record Ownership (PORO).

Sight any identity documents the customer has, including at least 1 photo identity document if possible.

If the customer presents without identity documents, ask more authentication questions that can be cross-checked with the customer's record. Do not limit questions to details a customer's friends and relatives would know such as their date of birth or how many children are in the family. The questions must be searching, but not so personal as to be intrusive. If appropriate, explain to the customer why they are being asked these questions.

If the customer cannot confirm details and the Service Officer is not satisfied they are speaking with the customer do not release any information. Do not run the Immediate Payment requests script. See Intertwined Centrelink records.

Has the customer been authenticated and PORO verified?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, deny the urgent payment request, procedure ends here

2

Update ACTDOC + Read more ...

There will be an ACTDOC on the customer's record with the urgent payment approval details.

If the urgent payment was approved by a Service Officer in a Processing Team or Participation Solutions Team (PST), they cannot run the script to approve the urgent payment. Service centre staff will need to run the Immediate Payment requests script to record the approval reasons and urgent payment details.

Annotate the DOC to state the identity documents have been sighted and/or PORO verified.

3

Issue payment to customer + Read more ...

Launch the Immediate Payment requests script. The script will display:

  • the amount of the urgent payment
  • method of payment
  • any evidence the customer has been asked to provide

Run the script to issue the payment to the customer:

  • Do not change the amount and method of payment without recording a reconsideration of the decision. See Customer disagrees with an urgent payment decision
  • For payments issued via EBT card, the script will generate a Record of Electronic Benefits Transfer card issue (SS292) form. This form must be completed before issuing the payment

If the customer disagrees with the decision, the method of payment or the amount, see Customer disagrees with an urgent payment decision. The original decision is only to be changed if:

  • new information is provided, or
  • the decision did not comply with the guidelines

3

When issuing an EBT card + Read more ...

Give the customer the How to use your EBT card factsheet. See the Resources page for a link.

Tell the customer:

  • For security reasons, do not keep the EBT card and PIN number together
  • The EBT card can only be used at ATMs, not via EFTPOS facilities
  • Make one withdrawal, immediately, for the full amount
  • Answer 'YES' if the ATM asks them to pay a transaction fee to withdraw the money. They will not be charged a fee and the amount available will not reduce
  • Bend the card in half and place in a bin, once they complete the withdrawal

Is the SRSS customer eligible for an urgent payment? + Read more ...

Only SRSS customers who have not received their regular entitlement because of a Centrelink delay or error and are in severe financial hardship are eligible for an immediate payment.
