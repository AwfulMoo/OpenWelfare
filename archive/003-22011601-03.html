Do not follow this procedure for self-isolation, quarantine or caring periods, or second or subsequent claim periods that start on or before 17 January 2022. See Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Claims in Customer First.

Customers can claim online again from 8am Wednesday 20 July 2022. If a customer calls, tell them to visit https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/pandemic-leave-disaster-payment for how to claim online. There are different claiming periods depending on the self-isolation, quarantine or caring period claimed. See the Resources page.

If a customer has lodged a claim online or using ACC that has not yet been finalised, they cannot lodge another one. If a customer calls to ask why they cannot lodge more than 1 claim, tell them:
‘You can lodge your next claim once you have received confirmation from Services Australia on the claim outcome of your previous claim.’

A correspondence nominee can claim payments for a customer.

      - the PPE, PPU or nominee's details cannot be confirmed as per information on the customer's record

Different circumstances to previous withdrawn claims + Read more ...

If a customer advises different details to their previously withdrawn claim/s, and is advising different details for the purposes of completing an ACC, staff must determine:

  • the reason for the changed information
  • if evidence is needed to assess the claim
  • if the claim should be rejected

Staff must:

  • Ask the customer why they had to withdraw and re-lodge their claim/s. For example, were they trying to add new information or having difficulty with the online claim?
  • Ask more probing and open-ended questions to verify the details they are providing in their new claim and what they declared in their previous claims
    • Do not:
      - give the customer answers to questions
      - prompt them for correct answers, or
      - include details they can use to frame their responses
  • Record the outcome of the discussion in a DOC with the summary heading ‘To be read for PLDP claim’ to help staff finalise the ACC claim (if run) and it does not meet the rules for straight through processing
    • Include in the DOC the impact reason and 7 day self-isolation, quarantine or caring period claimed
    • Do not use the term ‘fraud’ in the DOC
  • If the customer’s change in circumstances sound valid and no more evidence is needed to support their current claim:
    • include reasons why in the ‘To be read for PLDP claim’ DOC
    • keep assessing the claim. Go to Step 12
  • If there are irregularities in the customer’s information, and there are concerns about the validity of the claim, request the customer upload documents via the Upload Documents Service so their claim can be assessed
    • For evidence, customers are asked to keep to support their claim as Services Australia may ask for it later, see Evidence in the Background page of Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment claims in Process Direct
    • Note: statutory declarations will not be accepted as evidence to support a claim
  • If the customer advises they will upload the necessary documents (subject to all other pre-checks being met)
    • Tell the customer, if they meet all other eligibility criteria for payment, their claim will not be assessed until they submit the requested evidence. They should do this within 7 days, otherwise they will receive notification from the agency their claim is ineligible
    • Encourage the customer to upload documents via the Upload Documents Service the same day to minimise delays in processing their claim
    • Include in the ‘To be read for PLDP claim’ DOC evidence the customer was asked to provide
    • If the customer meets all other pre-claim checks, later steps will tell staff to run ACC, then immediately place the claim On Hold for 7 days
    • Keep assessing the claim. Go to Step 12
  • If the customer advises they will not provide any evidence and there is no valid reason for not providing evidence
    • Tell the customer: ‘Your claim cannot be granted as eligibility cannot be established’
    • Do not use the term ‘fraud’ when speaking with the customer or when updating their record
    • Include in the ‘To be read for PLDP claim’ DOC why the claim is being rejected
    • Later steps will tell staff to:
      - immediately select the claim for processing
      - reject the claim at the conclusion of the ACC, and
      - complete a DOA DOC
    • Go to Table 2

Residence and identity requirements + Read more ...

Make sure all residence data is coded and manually check the customer meets the visa requirements.

Residence checks needed + Read more ...

To avoid granting payments incorrectly, review all screens to make sure a customer meets the residence requirements. That is:

  • an Australian resident (through legal permanent residence and residing in Australia). Note: to check if a visa is permanent, see Social Security Guide, 9.2, or
  • the holder of a visa class permitted to work in Australia

The customer must also be in Australia

  • at the time of their claim and,
  • for the full period of the self-isolation, quarantine or caring period they are claiming

In Customer First:

  • Check the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen for citizenship, visa and/or New Zealand residence status details
    • For NZ citizens check the Visa (Sub) Class code for a 444 visa
    • Check the New Zealand residence status
      - Grandfathered (GRF) is a protected Special Category Visa (SCV) holder and should claim as an Australian resident
      - Transitional (TRA) is a protected SCV holder and should claim as an Australian resident
      - NOT is a non-protected SCV holder and should claim as a non-Australian resident
  • Check the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen for country of birth or travel documents
  • Check the arrival and departure dates for the customer on the Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen to make sure the customer was in Australia for the full period of the self-isolation, quarantine or caring period they are claiming
  • Details on the RSLEG, RSIMME and RSIM screens must be refreshed by running a RSIMME update
  • For Australian residents (through legal permanent residence and residing in Australia) - make sure the Country of Residence (CRES) screen is coded. Note: the CRES screen does not always need to be coded with an Australian entry for non-Australian residents (temporary visa holders)
  • Asking the customer the last time they travelled overseas is a good way to confirm any passport details. This will help to complete PoRO
  • If there are no residence details on the RSLEG screen, see Recording legal residence status to complete residence coding on the record
  • Check the Travel Outside Australia Summary (TOAS) screen to make sure the customer is in Australia at the time of their claim. Note: if a customer’s first movement is an arrival in Australia, it is not displayed on this screen. See the RSIM screen instead
  • If the customer is a non-Australian resident, check they have an eligible visa
  • If the customer is an Australian resident with a visa, to check if the visa is a permanent resident visa, see Social Security Guide, 9.2, Visa subclasses & payment eligibility for visas issued after 1/9/94)

If the customer does not meet the residence requirements, they do not meet the eligibility criteria. Go to Step 26.

Confirm eligibility + Read more ...

Verbally confirm all eligibility criteria before commencing the ACC.

Points to check:

  • The 7 day period they are claiming starts on or after 18 January 2022. If it starts on or before 17 January 2022, see Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Claims in Customer First
  • They are claiming within 14 days from and including the first day of their 7 day claiming period. If not, see Late claims. Note: this should have been identified at the Taking Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment claims step
  • They are unable to work (including unable to work from home) and therefore, unable to earn an income due to the reason they are claiming
  • They were likely to have worked at least 8 hours or one full day during the period of self-isolation, quarantine or caring they are claiming
  • The customer has liquid assets of less than $10,000 on the first day of their self-isolation, quarantine or caring period
  • They have not received or applied for, on any day of the 7 day period they are claiming, the
    • ACT COVID-19 Hardship Payment
    • NSW Test and Isolate support payment
    • South Australian COVID-19 Cluster Isolation Payment
    • Tasmanian Test and Isolate Grant
    • Tasmanian Pandemic Isolation Grant Assistance
    • Victorian COVID-19 Test Isolation Payment
    • Victorian Sick Pay Guarantee
    • Western Australian COVID-19 Test Isolation Payment
  • They do not have any appropriate leave entitlements that will cover the full 7 day period they are claiming.
  • If the customer is applying for a self-isolation, quarantine or caring period that is an extension of a previous period claimed, discuss with them the reasons why. They may be eligible for a second or subsequent payment
  • Reminder for self-isolation, quarantine or caring periods that start on or after 18 January 2022 only if claimed under a:
    • Close contact reason, they can only claim 1 x 7 day payment even if the self-isolation, quarantine or caring period is greater than 7 days. A new period can only be claimed if the customer can claim under a new impact reason. That is, they:
      - or the person in their care tests positive with COVID-19,
      - start caring for a different person (who is a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19 or who tests positive with COVID-19), or
      - or the person in their care becomes a close contact of a different person who tests positive with COVID
    • Positive test reason, if assessing a claim where a customer has been paid for a 7 day period immediately before for the same reason impact reason of testing positive or is caring for the same person who tested positive, they cannot self-declare the same evidence from the previous claim. They must have new evidence available of a
      - medical certificate from an appropriate health professional that states they are still need to isolate in the current period claimed due to the effects of COVID-19. See Positive test result
  • If a customer is claiming they are a close contact as they usually live in the same household as a positive COVID-19 case during their infectious period, they must have the same address. Note: this is not a requirement if the customer is claiming they are a close contact as they have spent more than 4 hours in the same household as person who has tested positive with COVID-19 during their infectious period. See Close contact
  • For claims lodged on or after 27 April 2022, customers can make a self-declaration that they meet either the Australian Government definition of close contact or the close contact definition in their state or territory. See Close contact. Staff do not need to:
    • check which definition is met, or
    • refer to state or territory close contact definitions, or
    • find out if a customer meets a state or territory close contact definition
  • For claims lodged on or after 8 May 2022, options to select in the claim around the need to self-isolate for close contact impact reasons have been updated to include a person’s
    • state or territory government requires them to isolate, or
    • employer has directed them not to attend their workplace even though their state or territory government does not require them to self-isolate.
      - See Close contact
  • Staff do not need to
    • check state or territory government rules in relation to requirements to self-isolate or quarantine
    • find out if a customer should or should not self-isolate or quarantine
      - under their state or territory government rules, or
      - due to being directed by their employer not to attend work

If any of the above criteria are not met, they do not meet the eligibility criteria. Go to Step 26.

  • Tell the customer:

    - You will receive a letter from Services Australia, or an SMS directing you to complete an online task. The letter or online task will request that you provide this evidence by a specified due date otherwise your claim will be ineligible (Note: customers will be given 14 days plus more time to respond as per agency standards to provide the evidence)
    - Any claims already assessed and paid will also be recoverable
    - The letter or online task will outline how to provide this evidence

A disaster is a serious disruption to the functioning of a community. Examples of disaster events are:

  • Floods
  • Bushfires
  • Cyclones
  • Severe Storms

One of the following must have been activated:

  • Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment (AGDRP) or Disaster Recovery Payment for New Zealand Special Category (subclass 444) visa holders (NZ DRP)
  • Disaster Recovery Allowance (DRA) or Disaster Recovery Allowance for New Zealand Special Category (subclass 444) visa holders (NZ DRA)

See Current emergency events.
