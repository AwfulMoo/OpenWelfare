This page contains the process staff use when a one-off servicing variation to a Managed Service Plan (MSP) is being considered due to exceptional circumstances and how it is recorded in the Customer Incident Management System (CIMS).

1

Consider alternative service options + Read more ...

Consider other service options before making a recommendation for a one-off servicing variation, including:

  • conducting business over the phone where the customer has a face-to-face service restriction
  • customer utilising a third party nominee
  • customer using online services
  • contact through a trusted source, for example Social Worker or welfare group
  • Alternative Identity Confirmation
  • consultation with the appropriate business owner to determine the provision of a one-off exemption to identified process or procedure
  • an early review of the MSP

Child Support customer considerations:

The 'contact restricted to writing correspondence only' restriction and the 'contact via an authorised representative only' restriction will not apply when the customer is involved in the COA process. For COA applications, the PS Service Officer should:

  • liaise with the COA team and /or COA Service Officer when an application is received
  • consult their team leader to establish clear parameters and expectations
  • advise the customer of these parameters and expectations in writing via a MX0-1 Unique Letter, see Letters Cuba Process Help, create a unique letter, and
  • ensure all other information relevant to the COA process is sent in the usual manner

For more information see Change of assessment in special circumstances (CS).

Consideration for a one off variation may also be necessary in the following:

  • income reconciliation
  • Capacity to Pay
  • lodging verbal objections, or
  • other high impact decision making processes

National Redress Scheme survivor/nominee considerations

If the customer is a survivor or nominee who needs to have their identity confirmed for the National Redress Scheme, they are required to attend in person to a service centre. Consideration for a one-off variation may be necessary in the following situations for the National Redress Scheme:

  • proof of identity requirements for survivor and or nominee
  • provision of identity documents
  • provision and signing of statutory declaration

Is an alternative service option available?

  • Yes, record the details on the customer record and in CIMS. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

One Main Contact (OMC)/ Personalised Services Officer (PSO)

2

Recommend one-off servicing variation + Read more ...

Record in the MSP in CIMS:

  • the reason for the request to approve a one-off servicing variation
  • alternative servicing arrangements considered and why these are not appropriate
  • consideration of risks and the strategies employed to minimise these risks
  • proposed one-off servicing arrangement

Note:

  • do not advise the customer of a possible one-off variation prior to approval from the delegate
  • for customers attending for National Redress Scheme, record in CIMS as provision of documents to confirm identity. Do not mention National Redress Scheme in CIMS or on the customer's Centrelink record

See Task Card: Recording and Approving a One-Off Servicing Variation to a Managed Service Plan (MSP) in Exceptional Circumstances on the Customer Incident Management System page.

OMC/PSO

3

Assess recommendation for one-off servicing variation + Read more ...

Assess the one-off variation recommendation. Consider:

  • Does the customer's business require them to be physically present in an office?
  • Have all other options been considered?
  • Would a review of the MSP as a whole be more appropriate?

Is the recommendation for a one-off servicing variation approved?

  • Yes, approve the recommendation in CIMS. See Task Card: Recording and Approving a One-Off Servicing Variation to a Managed Service Plan (MSP) in Exceptional Circumstances on the Customer Incident Management System page
  • No, contact the OMC and/or PS Officer to discuss other arrangements

Approver

Table 2: this table describes the process staff follow after a recommendation for a one-off servicing variation in exceptional circumstances has been approved and the Managed Service Plan (MSP) in the Customer Incident Management System (CIMS) is updated.

Step

Action

Role

1

Phone customer to advise details of one-off variation + Read more ...

Phone the customer and explain the one-off variation to the servicing arrangement in the MSP. Explain to the customer:

  • the reason for the appointment
  • how the one-off variation works
  • what the contact arrangements are
  • the specific date and time the customer is to attend the location
  • the documentation or information the customer will be required to provide at the appointment
  • the requirement for the customer to contact the One Main Contact (OMC) and/or Personalised Services (PS) Officer if unable to attend this appointment

Advise the customer and confirm their understanding of the following:

  • the customer will receive a letter confirming the agreed date and time
  • if the customer misses this appointment without advising the department, the one-off variation will lapse
  • the customer's current MSP arrangements otherwise remain in place and the customer should continue to follow the contact arrangements in the MSP
  • if the customer displays aggression or counterproductive behaviour during the appointment, the appointment will be ended immediately and, if in a face-to-face environment, the customer will be asked to leave the premises

Document this contact in the MSP in the Customer Incident Management System (CIMS).

Note: for customers attending for National Redress Scheme, record in CIMS as provision of documents to confirm identity. Do not mention the National Redress Scheme in CIMS or on the customer's Centrelink record.

OMC/PSO

2

Send one-off variation letter + Read more ...

Send the One-off Variation of Restrictions letter to the customer. The letter template is available on the Resources page.

Attach the letter to the MSP record in CIMS.

Note: for customers attending for National Redress Scheme, record in the letter as provision of documents to confirm identity. Do not mention the National Redress Scheme in the letter.

OMC/PSO

3

Consider safety and security when customer attends appointment + Read more ...

If the one-off variation to the MSP requires the customer to attend a service centre:

  • consider the safest location for the interview
  • do not use an interview room

See the Resources page for a link to HSI page Security for Service Centre Managers.

Advise the following staff of the one-off variation interview arrangements:

  • Customer Liaison Officer
  • designated interviewing officer
  • leadership team
  • security guard
  • any other staff as considered necessary

When the customer arrives at the service centre:

  • acknowledge the customer has arrived
  • ensure a timely notification and referral to the designated officer
  • guide the customer to the identified interview location

Note: the warning message in CIMS will identify that a one-off variation is in place.

OMC/PSO, Service Centre Manager

4

Record outcome of appointment + Read more ...

At the conclusion of the one-off servicing arrangement appointment:

  • Update the customer record to advise the customer's attendance and any action taken
  • Check CIMS to ensure the MSP displays as it did prior to the one-off servicing variation being recorded
  • For the National Redress Scheme, note only that the appointment has been attended. Do not mention the National Redress Scheme in the Centrelink record
