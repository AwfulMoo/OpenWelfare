Letter review process

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Identify deceased parent, carer or child and identify relevant letters + Read more ...

Before determining if the letter is okay to be released, identify who is the deceased parent, carer or child and if they relate to the case for which the letter has generated.

  • Open the customer via the letter intray. Go to the customers letter list and record the case number the letter has generated on
  • Go to all associated customers letter lists to find any other letters with a pending status and Deceased Client letter intray. All letters will need to be reviewed at the same time
  • Go to the Case window. Check all customers/children to find who is deceased
  • A deceased child will have the notation DEC in the Care window and may have an indicator on the parent or carers Customer window
  • If there is no case number associated with the letter, check all associated customers to find out which person(s) or child is/are deceased
  • To auto search for a customer or child within the letter text, open the letter as a PDF document and select CTRL+ F. The search function is case sensitive, so add the name exactly as in Cuba

Does the pended letter relate to a case where someone is deceased?

  • Yes, and the:
    • other parent/carer in the case is deceased, go to Step 2
    • letter is for an executor or an administrator where a parent or carer is deceased, go to Step 3
    • letter names a deceased child or relevant dependent, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

2

Issue letter to a customer where the other parent/carer in the case is deceased + Read more ...

Services Australia has a legislative requirement to provide information about a change to an assessment in writing to the associated customer of a deceased parent or carer. All letters should be released unless the content is incorrect or insensitive.

In some instances it may be appropriate to call the customer to discuss the letter content before releasing it.

For example, if a deceased person is mentioned in a letter, contact with the customer by phone may be appropriate, before making a decision to release, hold or delete the letter.

If it is appropriate to release the letter without making contact, go to Step 5.

If customer contact is required:

  • DCM Service officers - Contact the letter recipient
  • Non DCM service officers:
    • document what is required in the Deceased client letter intray and
    • route the intray as per the Deceased Customer Referral Guidelines

Note: if the customer is already locked to a Service Officer in DCM, advise the Service Officer that contact is required, after initial review of the letter has taken place.

Once contact or attempts are made, and it is appropriate to release the letter, go to Step 5.

3

Issue of a letter to an executor or an administrator where a parent or carer is deceased + Read more ...

When an executor is recorded for a deceased parent or carer in the customer representative window and the mail option is set to 'mail to executor’ in the Deceased Client window, all letters generated will be addressed to the executor.

It is important for letters to be issued to executors so they have visibility of any outstanding issues, such as debt which needs to be addressed by the estate.

These letters should be reviewed in the same way as Step 2.

Note: the mail option will default as 'mail to estate' in the Deceased Client window when a customer is updated as deceased. If this is the 'mail to' status letters will not issue.

Go to Step 5.

4

Letter names a deceased child or relevant dependent + Read more ...

Letters which name a child who is deceased should be reviewed with increased sensitivity. Contact with the letter recipient is required in most instances, unless there is clear documentation in the child’s Deceased Client window that the customer has agreed to receive letters without contact.

The APS 5 Service Officers in DCM (in most circumstances) will make the contact.

Other DCM Service Officers may make contact with the support of their Team Leader.

Non DCM service officers must refer the customer to the DCM team via the Deceased Customer Referral Guidelines.

Once contact or attempts are made, and it is appropriate to release the letter, go to Step 5.

5

Releasing the letter + Read more ...

Once you have determined the letter is to be released to the customer:

  • open the Cuba Letter window and set the letter status to 'reviewed', and
  • delete the Deceased Client letter intray

If the pended letter text is not appropriate, consider deleting the letter and issuing one with unique text. See Letters for Child Support customers.

Note: if an associated customer is on tracing and a letter has generated for review, attempt to update the address before changing the status of the letter. See Customer location (Tracing).

End Assessment letters

Table 2: for DCM staff only.

Step

Action

1

Issue of End Assessment letters by DCM + Read more ...

Cuba does not automatically produce an end assessment letter when the terminating event is death.

The Deceased Customer End Assessment letters macro enables staff in DCM to produce appropriate letter text used in a unique letter, when a letter is requested confirming the end of the case. The letter will name the deceased person.

Issue an end assessment letter once proof of death has been confirmed, and:

  • a case is ending due to the death and/or the case ended before the death and there is debt that requires follow up
  • successful phone contact has been made with the customer or executor to confirm the death of the person, and
  • the parent, carer or executor has agreed to receive the end assessment letter

Do not issue an End Assessment letter if:

  • the person has died after a case has ended and there is no debt, or
  • the parent, carer or executor asked not to receive the letter

Before sending an End assessment letter, ask a peer to review the letter text for any errors.

If a Notice of Assessment letter (NOA- 01) or other notice of assessment letter is generated , print it locally and sent together with the end assessment letter.

When phone attempts to discuss the death with the associated customers are unsuccessful, do not issue an end assessment letter. Instead issue a pre-approved 'case' preset letter action text CAL. See Letters Cuba process help.

Note: if a CAL is issued, it should not mention the parent, carer or child is deceased.
