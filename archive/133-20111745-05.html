FAQs from health professionals about a patient care plan history search

Table 1:

Item

Description

Question: What items appear when I use the care plan search?

Answer: Health Professional Online Services (HPOS) displays items that are part of:

  • General Practitioner Management Plans
  • Team Care Arrangements
  • Multidisciplinary Care Plans
  • General Practitioner Mental Health Treatment Plans

Question: Why can’t I access specific details about my patients care plan?

Answer: This resource is not intended to provide detailed clinical information. It is available for checking whether a care plan exists to assess whether additional treatment is clinically appropriate. Patients may share relevant clinical details with health professionals using their My Health Record, or getting a previous health professional to send their records over.

Question: How do I access the care plan checker?

Answer: You need to have access to Health Professional Online Services (HPOS). Instructions are available on the services Australia website.

Question: Can my practice manager access this information on my behalf?

Answer: No. Delegates are not able to access this service on your behalf. Due to patient privacy, only the treating health professional can access this information.

FAQs from customers about care plans

Table 2:

Item

Description

1

Question: I am on a care plan; can a GP other than my regular GP check the details?

Answer: No, they can only see whether you are on a plan or not. For further information, the health professional who has created the care plan will be able to provide more information. Alternatively, you can allow different health professionals access to your clinical records using My Health Record.

Note: Service Officers, see Medicare online account - Care plan search history screen for more information about the care plan search history screen.

FAQs from health professionals about MBS Items Online Checker

Table 3:

Item

Description

1

Question: What items are available on the items online checker?

Answer: The MBS Items Online Checker displays a full list of the items available. Select the relevant item range tab to see if your item number is listed. The list is updated regularly.

Note: Service Officers, see the screenshot of the MBS Items Online Checker screen in HPOS.

2

Question: Why would I use the Item checker?

Answer: The MBS Items Online Checker enables health professionals to check eligibility to claim an item for a patient.

3

Question: What will happen when:

  • a patient has seen another doctor who has provided the same service on an earlier date, and
  • Medicare has not received and assessed the claim, and/or
  • the previous claim is pending and not yet in the patient’s history?

Answer: The MBS Item Online Checker will return a ‘Yes’ response.

4

Question: Can I use the MBS Online Item Checker without my provider numbers and/or the patients Medicare information?

Answer: Without this information, the system cannot determine if the search has returned the correct response.

5

Question: If I receive a 'Yes' answer, is this telling me that the patient is eligible to bill?

Answer: Yes. The patient is eligible at the time of checking the item, however it is important to bill the item close to the time that the check was undertaken.

6

Question: I am searching 5 items at a time, why are the result through the MBS Online Item Checker returning incorrect results?

Answer: It is best to search for 1 item number at a time. This is because some numbers restrict with each other, which may show incorrect results.

7

Question: Can I check items well in advance of the service being provided?

Answer: No, it is important to verify the patient eligibility using the MBS Online Item Checker close to the date of the service. If the check is done too far in advance, there is a risk of other claims being lodged and paid.

8

Question: Can I check a patient’s eligibility for the Child Dental Benefits Schedule (CDBS)?

Answer: Yes, however, restrictions that apply to a particular tooth cannot be checked through this service.
