Calculate the care percentage + Read more ...

2

Care other than in nights + Read more ...

Where the care that is actually occurring or outlined in the customers' care arrangement, provides for care other than in nights (such as hourly care). For more information, References has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.2.1 Basics of care: Care other than in nights.

3

One-off block of 100% care + Read more ...

Where a customer or carer unexpectedly and temporarily provides 100% care of a child, the Registrar may recognise that the person has 100% care. This is the case even though they are not expected to continue to have that level of care.

In these situations, the Registrar will:

  • determine the care over a short care period related to the unexpected circumstance, and
  • make a one-off block of 100% care determination

The Registrar will not review the care again once a one-off block of 100% care determination is made. However, when the care arrangements return to the usual pattern or a new pattern is established, either carer may request a new care percentage determination.

The period of unexpected care will generally be at least 4 weeks in length in order for the Registrar to make such a determination. However shorter periods can be considered, especially where there is a possibility the period may be extended. For more information, References has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.2.2 Care Determination and Changes in Care.

To record a one-off block of 100% care determination in Cuba:

  • add a new care record
  • select the care type 'Actual' and care percentage of 100% to the person who has unexpectedly gained 100% care
  • ensure the 'Block of 100% Care' indicator is selected. For more information see Care Cuba Process Help - Add a new care record

Determine the care percentage + Read more ...

Determine the care percentage over the care period from the date of event
This is date the actual care of the child changed.

Do the customers have a written care arrangement in place?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Actual care + Read more ...

If the customers do not have a written care arrangement, calculate the care percentage based on the care that is actually occurring.

Use the care calculator to determine the care percentage based on the actual care that is most likely to be occurring, even if the customers:

  • provide their own calculations
  • agree on a certain number of nights or a care percentage

Add a new care record and select the care type ‘Actual’, see Care Cuba Process Help, Add a new care record

If a customer disagrees with the details of the reported care change, go to Step 8.

6

Written care arrangement + Read more ...

A written care arrangement is a court order, parenting plan or written agreement that outlines a pattern of care for a child and may include a percentage of care. For more information, References has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.2.1 Basics of care: Agreements and orders regarding care.

Note: if a customer/s advises that they have a written care arrangement but do not provide a copy of the arrangement. Make a decision (to reject or accept). Calculate the care percentage based on the care that is actually occurring, go to Step 5.

Interpretation and implementation guidance

If required, seek help from a Service Support Officer (SSO), see Technical support in Child Support.

International customers

International customers may submit a court order concerning care that was made outside Australia. Only treat and document the court order as a care arrangement if it is registered under section 70G of the Family Law Act 1975 (Cth), see Documenting Child Support information.

Only use information within the court order to determine a care percentage if the parents are complying with the order. Request a copy of the care arrangement documents, scan and record this in the Care window notepad, see Correspondence received in Child Support.

Written care arrangement is being complied with

If the customers are complying with the provisions of the written care arrangement, make a care determination and record the care type as court order, parenting plan or written agreement in Cuba, see Step 1 in Table 2.

Written care arrangement is not being complied with

If the customers are not complying with the provisions of the written care arrangement and:

  • both customers agree to an alternate actual care situation - make an 'Actual' care determination in Cuba and record the details of the care arrangement in a Care window notepad, see Step 1 in Table 2, or
  • the care is disputed and the customer with reduced care is taking reasonable action to have the written care arrangement complied with, go to Step 9. References has a link to Child Support Guide 2.2.4: Disputed Care Arrangements

7

Below regular care decision + Read more ...

Below regular care decisions apply where a customer satisfies the following:

  • assessed to have at least regular care (at least 14%) of the child during a care period
  • will have no care or a pattern of care that is less than regular in the care period despite the child being made available to them
  • the other customer or non-parent carer has notified Child Support within a reasonable time of the change

References has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.2.3: Below Regular Care Determinations.

8

Disagreed care + Read more ...

If at any point a customer disagrees with the details of the reported change in care, refer them to the Integrated Care Team as per the Integrated Care referral guidelines.

Before referring the case to Integrated Care:

  • Gather all relevant information from the customers about the new care pattern
    • the date the care changed, and
    • details which are, or are likely to be disagreed
  • If the customer is on the phone, ask them to provide evidence to support their care change. Ask the customer to upload their evidence via Child Support self service. Tell the customer an Integrated Care Service Officer will call them to discuss the disagreed process.

Exception - referrals to the Integrated Care Team

Service Officer in specialised areas like Personalised Services, Complaints, New Customers (if there is a pending registration) or a RACs officer are not required to refer the customer to the Integrated Care Team.

9

Disputed care + Read more ...

Refer the customer to the Integrated Care Team in accordance with the Customer Referral Guidelines to manage the care change if at any point:

  • it is identified that the existing written care arrangement has ceased being followed and the care change is disputed
  • a decision to apply actual care in special circumstances is being considered
  • an interim care determination has already been made and there is a change in care before the end of the maximum interim period. To identify the maximum interim period of a decision, see the relevant care decision notepad

Undertake the following activities before referring the case to the Integrated Care Team:

  • Gather information from the customer regarding:
    • the new care pattern
    • date the care changed
    • details of the current written care arrangement. Including its made on date, and
    • any reasonable action that is being taken by the person with reduced care
  • Advise the customer to send any further evidence available to support their claim within 14 days. This can include a copy of the current written care arrangement if it is not yet recorded in Cuba

Check and process the care outcome or change in care decision

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Change in care percentage but not the cost percentage + Read more ...

Services Australia has the discretion not to change the existing care decision if the reported change in care:

  • would result in a change to the care percentage, but
  • would not result in a change to the cost percentage

Make a formal care decision for all care change notifications even if there would be no change to the cost percentage.

The agency will make a new care percentage decision despite the fact this will not change the cost percentage if:

  • the reported care change is due to a written care arrangement (court order, parenting plan or written care agreement) not previously recorded in Cuba. This is mandatory. Services Australia will always make a new care decision to reflect a previously unrecorded written care arrangement, or
  • the reported care change is due to a written care arrangement ceasing to be followed, or
  • either customer has expressed a desire to have the care arrangement reflected despite there being no change to the cost percentage, or
  • the reported care change does not require any further investigation to make the decision. That is, all the information needed to make a decision is available at the initial point of contact with the notifier or respondent, or
  • the particular circumstances of the case warrant the making of a new care percentage decision.

Go to Step 2.

Refusal decision to be made

If none of the above exist, consider whether contact with the other carer should occur prior to finalising the refusal decision.

Contact may be necessary if:

  • The notifying customer indicates the other carer will disagree with the care change
  • The other party is the one whose care has increased
  • Any other circumstances of the case that make it suitable to do so

In these circumstances, make one genuine attempt to contact the other party before proceeding with the refusal decision.

If the decision made is to not make a new care percentage decision (to ‘refuse’ the reported care change) under the discretion allowed in s54H or s54HA, the pending care record should be rejected and the reason for that decision documented.

Note: where there is an indication of family violence, consider deleting the refusal letter for the other carer.

The customers have the right to object to the decision over the phone or in writing. See Step 3 in Table 3 for more information

2

Pre-decision Contact + Read more ...

Consider whether pre-decision contact is required with each party to the care decision (unless the care decision will not affect the cost percentage).

Pre-decision contact is necessary if:

  • the care change will affect the cost percentage, and
  • no successful phone contact has occurred throughout the care process and the information in the initial notification was unclear or incomplete. For example Care % is not consistent with reason given for change, or
  • there is evidence or information being used to make the decision that has not been discussed with the customer, or
  • a decision is being made which will have an adverse impact for a customer that Services Australia has not been able to contact

3

Care change decision + Read more ...

Ensure the 'Pending' care record in Cuba is updated to reflect the correct final care determination for each change in care notification received. Inaccurate care information, as well as unnecessarily deleting and rekeying Child Support care records may impact on a customer's Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

See the Notification table in Notification of change in child support care (post 1 July 2010), Notification to check the pended care record reflects the correct:

  • date of event
  • date of notification
  • percentage of care
  • date of effect - this is generally auto-populated by the system using the 28 day rule, but be alert and check the outcome of the decisions. Note: for WA Ex-nuptial children, if the change was late notified after 14 May 2019, the system needs to be enhanced to treat these customers correctly. For more information see WA Child Support ex-nuptial cases Process page Table 3
  • care type
  • 'additional information' indicators are checked appropriately for the decision type

Check the decision:

  • has considered all relevant information provided by both customers
  • has been made by an appropriately authorised or delegated officer, see Delegations and authorisations
  • includes references to relevant sections of the legislation
  • different sections of legislation may apply to each of the parties involved in the decision as well as the circumstances of the case

Consider the following, if:

  • a care change is loaded in error or the customer incorrectly advises that a change has occurred, for example customer incorrectly pended care via CSAonline or the change is for a future date, delete the pended care record.
    To ensure care data transfers to Centrelink correctly the care record that is to be applied must be accepted prior to deleting pended care records. Document the reason for deleting the record To delete a care record, follow the process in Care Cuba Process Help
  • a change in care is incorrectly loaded as a new change in care, instead of an objection to a previous care decision, delete the pended care record
  • a customer indicates they no longer wish to pursue a change in care, but believes a change did occur, a decision must still be made. A customer cannot elect to withdraw notification of a change in care as it is a notification, not an application. Where the notifying customer withdraws their participation in the process, make a decision based on the information available. If insufficient evidence is available it would be appropriate to reject the change, not to delete it

Whenever a decision is being made to reject, refuse or accept a care change notification, letters are issued and customers have the right to object

If help is required, see Technical support in Child Support.

Objection rights

When Child Support or Centrelink have made a care decision, an objection to that decision may be lodged by phone or in writing. For a verbal care percentage objection see Customer referral guidelines - Objections

If a customer raises multiple issues in an objection, only the part that is an objection to a care percentage decision is able to be taken over the phone. They may need to lodge both written and verbal objections.

Seek help if required, see Technical support.

4

Document the care decision + Read more ...

Save the output document from the Change in care macro in the Care window notepad.

5

Assessment needs to be started/restarted + Read more ...

When running eligibility to give effect to the care change, review the pre-confirmation results.

If the warning message Terminating event, should case be restarted? Is child in pye/pay care? displays, identify if 1 of the following is required:

  • Manual creation of a reverse case
  • Restart/add a new registration to a reverse case, or
  • Restart/add a new registration to an existing assessment

Create or restart a case or add a new registration if the customer indicated they want the child to be assessed. They may have advised during the change of care notification or subsequently.

Check all notepads associated with the care change and recently added to the case.

If a customer has asked to reapply for an assessment, the assessment will restart from the date they answered the question.

As the child support assessment will be active until eligibility is run following the acceptance of the care change, the child support assessment can be restarted by the owning officer without the need for a referral to New Customers.

Follow the process for creating a reverse case with the following exceptions:

  • Assessment will begin on the day the application was made
  • Customers must be contacted to identify what collection method
  • Child Support periods will not be aligned
  • Formula departures. Most formula departures, for example change of assessment, agreement, court order, will not survive a terminating event. The only exceptions are a non-periodic payments clause in an agreement that does not affect the annual rate, and lump sum crediting provisions in a binding agreement. These will not apply again until the payee named in the agreement again becomes an eligible carer
  • Estimates. If there was an estimate of income in place for either customer before the terminating event, contact the estimating customer to discuss their income. If the estimate:
    • was in place prior to the terminating event and the case is restarted within the same financial year and the customer does not revoke the estimate, the estimate will be used as that customer’s income in the restarted assessment
    • election was made during a period when the case had ended, after the change in care occurred, ask the customer if they would like to lodge a new estimate election

6

Process care decision + Read more ...

Open the pending Cuba care record and process the care decision to reflect 'Accepted' or 'Refused', see Care Cuba Process Help, Process a care decision.

Review and check the Pre-confirmation Results window, see Eligibility Cuba Process Help. Ensure all the changes that will take effect once Eligibility is saved, are correct.

Note: for WA Ex-nuptial children, if the change was late notified after 14 May 2019, the system needs to be enhanced to treat these customers correctly. For more information see WA Child Support ex-nuptial cases Process page Table 3.

If help on the information displayed is required, seek technical support.

If all the changes displayed in the Pre-confirmation Results window are correct, save Eligibility to process the care decision.

Once eligibility is saved and the care decision is applied, check that the child support assessment is updated accurately via the case liability rates window. Cuba will transfer the care percentage to Centrelink for mutual customers via data exchange to be applied to the customer’s FTB and other income support payments.

If an assessment has been terminated, go to Step 5.

If the care change causes a reverse entitlement, check if a formula assessment is appropriate or if a Court Order (CO), Registrar Varied Assessment (RVA), Agreement etc. has to be put back in place. See Managing reverse child support cases to ensure the accuracy of the reverse case, particularly in relation to collection, has been verified.

7

Letters + Read more ...

Letters and assessment notices are sent to customers notifying them of changes. One of the following letters will generate:

  • MEA1-9 Change of Care
  • MEA1-6 Letter to advise payer and payee that the agency has rejected/refused to make a change to the care percentage of the child
  • MEA1-4 Letter to advise payee that a terminating event has occurred
  • MEA1-5 Letter to advise payer that a terminating event has occurred
  • MAC1-3 Letter to advise multiple decisions have been made on case but no change to the annual rate has resulted
  • MAC1-4 Letter to advise multiple decisions have been made on case which have resulted in a change to the annual rate
  • NOA-01 CS Notice of Assessment and Covering Page. Sometimes the system:
    • suppresses the Cuba letters, and
    • issues a NOA-01 which consolidates the decision letters

Check any letters generated thoroughly to ensure they reflect the care decision and care percentages made, see Letters Cuba Process Help, View or preview a letter.

Note: for WA Ex-nuptial children, if the change was late notified after 14 May 2019, the system needs to be enhanced to treat these customers correctly. For more information see WA Child Support ex-nuptial cases Process page Table 3.

Where the customers’ roles have changed, see Reverse child support cases.

Case review and post-decision issues management

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Manage post-decision issues + Read more ...

Once the care decision has been finalised, review the case to identify if the change has resulted in:

  • a payee overpayment, contact the payee to discuss repayment options, see Contact with Child Support customers and Child support overpayments and other payee debt
  • a retrospective liability increase for the payer, discuss repayment options with the payer
  • changes to prescribed NAPs, contact both customers to discuss the impact, see Non Agency Payments (NAPs)
  • the customers’ roles changing, see Reverse child support cases
  • the care being suspended and an application to extend the child support assessment past the child's 18 birthday is pending, see Eighteen year old children extending a child support assessment
  • the assessment/case being terminated, see Terminating events (Stage 2)
  • an agreement being suspended, transfer the customer to the Agreements and Court Orders team to discuss the impact on their agreement

2

Determine whether post -decision contact is necessary with each party to the care decision + Read more ...

Post-decision contact is necessary if:

  • the care change will affect the cost percentage, and
  • pre- decision contact was not attempted, or
  • the customer is unaware of an adverse decision, or
  • a debt or overpayment has been created and there is no payment arrangement in place, or
  • there are other outstanding issues which need to be discussed

3

Customer disagrees with care decision + Read more ...

If a customer disagrees with the care decision, discuss the reasons why and try to resolve the issue by explaining how the care decision was made. Determine appropriate referral options, see Customer referral guidelines for Child Support staff, for the customer including:

  • a referral to an external service provider
  • a referral to another section within Child Support
  • a transfer to Centrelink
  • a referral to the Complaints process
  • lodgement of an objection on a care decision or application for a Centrelink review

If the customer contacts Child Support about the change in care letters they received advising the care percentage on their assessment is not in line with the care decision. This may indicate a keying error has occurred. Seek technical support about whether an error has occurred that needs correcting, see Correcting errors on Child Support cases.

If a customer wants to pursue an objection or apply for a review of the care decision, they can do so by notifying Child Support or by notifying Centrelink where 1 or more carers receive family assistance. This applies to all customers including non-mutual customers.

Objections to care decisions may be lodged either verbally or in writing.

Check whether the decision was made by Child Support, Centrelink or the Integrated Care Team.

Transfer customers requesting an objection or review of a decision made by Integrated Care to the Integrated Care Team. The Integrated Care Team will discuss the decision with the customer to decide which program to lodge their objection or review with.

4

Care decision made by Child Support + Read more ...

If the customer still wants to pursue an objection to the care decision transfer the customer to the Objection team.

If the customer advises of:

  • a change in the care since the original decision was made, treat this as a new care notification. See Notification of change in child support care (post 1 July 2010). Progress the new care decision as required and transfer the customer to the Objection team if the customer wants to object
  • additional information on the same date of the original decision that will change the outcome. It may be possible to reconsider the decision because a decision becomes final when it is communicated in writing to the relevant parties. Seek technical support, see Technical support in Child Support

Document discussion in the Care window notepad, see Documenting Child Support information.

5

Care decision made by Centrelink + Read more ...

If Centrelink made the original care decision, provide an explanation of the care decision to Child Support customers (mutual or non-mutual) impacted by the decision.

Any Child Support customer (mutual or non-mutual) impacted by a Centrelink care decision can:

  • lodge an objection
  • initiate a Centrelink review, or
  • participate in a review process that may have already started

Tell the customer the program who made the care decision is generally best placed to review it.

Example

A non-mutual customer may not understand the relationship between Child Support and Centrelink. Explain this so they can understand why they can speak with Centrelink about care. A non-mutual customer can seek a review with Centrelink.

If the customer asks why the care percentages recorded in the Centrelink decision and Centrelink assessment are different, transfer the customer to Centrelink to determine why this has occurred. It may be a keying error.

6

Customer has lodged a review + Read more ...

Ask the customer if they have already lodged a review with Centrelink. If the customer has lodged a review, open the External Information Care window to check:

  • the Appeal Related indicator has a status of ‘Y’, or
  • that a review/objection is in progress

Open the Objections window to check if there is a Child Support objection in progress. If there is no objection in progress, the review is being managed by Centrelink.

If the Appeal Related indicator has a status of ‘Y’ and the customer has not lodged a review with Centrelink, the other party may have initiated this.

Tell the customer that Centrelink is managing this process and once a decision is made, they will advise Child Support of the outcome.

Finalise any child support issues and transfer the customer to Centrelink to discuss the review in progress.

Note: Centrelink may make a formal decision for their customer only. Determine whether the customer is currently a mutual customer to help manage the customer’s options.

Document discussion in the Care window notepad.

7

Customer wants to lodge a review + Read more ...

If the customer wishes to pursue their review rights to the Centrelink decision with:

  • Centrelink, finalise any child support issues and transfer the customer to Centrelink, or
  • Child Support, transfer the customer to the Objections Team

See Customer referral guidelines for Child Support staff for transfer information, and document discussion in the Care window notepad.
