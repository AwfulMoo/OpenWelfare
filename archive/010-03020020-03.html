This page contains the steps to take to determine whether a parent/guardian or an organisation or an institution can be the applicant for a student under the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme.

On this page:

Possible applicants for the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme

Determining eligibility criteria for a person other than a parent

Possible applicants for the Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme

Step

Action

Applicant + Read more ...

Is the applicant an organisation?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 4

Student accommodation details + Read more ...

Does the student live with their parents/guardians in the organisation or the institution and the parent has at least some responsibility for the student?

  • Yes, the parent is the approved applicant. Check the other eligibility criteria for AIC. Procedure ends here
  • No go to Step 3

3

Approved applicant for an organisation or institution + Read more ...

An organisation or institution may be the approved applicant if:

  • it is a non-government body and the following conditions are met:
    • the student boards with organisation during school term and their permanent home address satisfies one of the geographical isolation rules
    • the student normally lives with the organisation / institution (including school vacations) but needs to board away to access an appropriate school due to the geographic isolation of the organisation / institution
    • the organisation is a special institution where the student resides because of the nature of a disability or other health related condition

An organisation who does not have primary responsibility for a student living continuously at the institution (due to a disability or other health-related condition), is not the applicant. The applicant would be the parent with whom the student would normally live, but for the need to board. To determine the applicant in this case, go to Step 6.

Does the organisation have full responsibility for the student?

  • Yes, the organisation has primary care of the student and is a:
    • non-government organisation, it is an approved applicant. Check the other eligibility criteria for AIC. Procedure ends here
    • government organisation, it is not an approved applicant. However, to determine if there is another approved applicant, go to Step 5
  • No, see Step 3 in the Determining eligibility criteria for a person other than a parent table

4

Is the applicant in prison, lawful custody or a psychiatric institution? + Read more ...

  • Yes, the applicant is not an approved applicant. However, to determine if another person may be an approved applicant, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Is the student in State authorised foster care? + Read more ...

  • Yes, the foster parent is an approved applicant. Check the other eligibility criteria for AIC. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Is the student not in the care of a parent/guardian or the State? + Read more ...

  • Yes, the student should apply for Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY if an Indigenous student. Advise the student how to claim YA online or claim ABSTUDY by phone. Issue relevant paper form to the student if appropriate. See:
    • Initial contact with students or Australian Apprentices claiming Youth Allowance (YA), or
    • Claiming ABSTUDY
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Are the parents or guardians of the student divorced or separated? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, either parent/guardian may be an approved applicant. Check the other eligibility criteria for AIC. Procedure ends here.

8

Does the student normally live with one of their parents/guardians? + Read more ...

  • Yes, the parent/guardian the student normally lives with is the approved applicant. If the student normally lives with one of the parents and his/her partner, either that parent or their partner may be the approved applicant. Check the other eligibility criteria for AIC. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Does the student live with each parent/guardian at different times? + Read more ...

  • Yes, consider the following factors:
    • the approved applicant is the parent who has legal custody of the student, or
    • if there is no legal recognition of who has care of the student, the -approved applicant is the parent:
      • with whom the student normally lives and has lived for the last 12 months or lives with during holidays
      • who is responsible for the care (provides food and shelter) of the student
      • who contributes regularly to the student's living and education costs
      • who receives Family Tax Benefit, holds a Medicare card or receives other allowances for the student, or
    • if neither parent has sole responsibility for the care of the student, the parent who has principal financial responsibility for the student is the approved applicant
  • No, in cases where the parents are equally sharing the care of the student and the student spends an equal amount of time living with each parent, the approved applicant can be either parent or either parent's partner. See Step 1 in the Determining eligibility criteria for a person other than a parent table

Determining eligibility criteria for a person other than a parent

Can the approved applicant be determined? + Read more ...

  • Yes, check the other eligibility criteria for AIC. Procedure ends here

Is the applicant neither the parent/guardian of the student nor the parent's partner? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Primary or joint responsibility for the student's care + Read more ...

Where the form is lodged by a person other than a parent or parent's partner, consider:

  • does the person have prime (or joint) responsibility for the student's care?; and
  • does the person support the student wholly or substantially?; and
  • is the arrangement bona fide?

If the parent (or parent's partner) is supporting the student in the other person's home or directly or indirectly providing financial assistance to the student, the person is not regarded as an approved applicant.

Can the approved applicant be determined?

  • Yes, check the other eligibility criteria for AIC. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Request for further information + Read more ...

If it cannot be determined who is the approved applicant on the basis of the information provided, request further information to explain the student's and applicant's circumstances and advise the customer to lodge a claim form if one has not already been lodged.

If a claim form has been lodged, go to Step 5.

Contact the appropriate AIC Smart Centre Processing for further assistance if necessary. Look up Processing details for: Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC).

Procedure ends here.

5

Document and notify decision + Read more ...

Claim has been lodged:

  • if the determination cannot be made that the applicant has primary care of the student, the application should be rejected at the end of the assessment
  • if there is more information required to determine primary care, suppress the automatic letter via the Assessment Results (AR) screen and issue Request for information (Q760) to the applicant. The claim can then be reassessed when the additional information is provided
  • assess the AIC claim
  • record details of decision to grant or reject the claim including the relevant policy references on a DOC using a keyword of AIC
