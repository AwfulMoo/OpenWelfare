      Select Workflow >Debts >DMB Raising ACTion DOC request

If the customer is, or has been, receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB), Child care Subsidy (CCS), Parental Leave Pay (PPL) or Dad and partner Pay (DAP), refer to the families Bereavement Processing team for action.

  • Yes, go to step 2
  • No:

    • place the intervention on hold (HLD) for 7 days Inline waiting for the activities to be cleared

Selecting the IRS intervention + Read more ...

Before any updates are made on the customer's record ensure the IRS intervention has a status of 'STA' (started) and that you are in the 'Review Image'.

Is a referral required to the Families Bereavement Processing team?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to step 6

3

Referral to Families Bereavement processing team + Read more ...

If the customer, either current or non-current, has been receiving FTB, CCS, PPL or DAP:

  • send a Fast Note – Auto Text – FAMILIES > UPDATES > RGO Bereavement to the Families Bereavement Processing team to action the customer's record, including:
    • coding off the child records
    • following up any overpayment
    • actioning any bereavement payment entitlements
  • place the intervention on hold (HLD) for 14 days pending outcomes from the Families Bereavement Processing team

Is the customer also receiving an income support payment (ISP), for example, JobSeeker, Carer, Age?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, procedure ends here until Families Bereavement Processing team actions are completed, then go to Step 5

4

Customer is receiving an income support payment (ISP) + Read more ...

If the customer is receiving an ISP, manually suspend the payment pending the Families Bereavement Processing team's actions:

  • use the reason code 'OTH' (Other) and suspend from the date of death or date paid to plus one (DPT+1), whichever is later
    Note: Do not send a Q134 letter. It is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the suspension
  • ensure the DOC:
    • meets Online Document Recording (ODR) standards for significant decisions, and
    • includes what must occur for the death action to be completed
  • procedure ends here until the Families Bereavement Processing team actions are completed, then go to Step 5

5

Action is completed by the Families Bereavement Processing Team + Read more ...

  • If customer was not receiving an ISP:
    • Check the record shows the customer is deceased
    • If not showing as deceased, go to Step 6
    • If showing as deceased, go to Step 9
  • If customer was receiving an ISP that is suspended OTH:
    • Check the record shows the customer is deceased
    • If not showing as deceased, go to Step 6
    • If showing as deceased, go to Step 7

6

Actioning an adult death match + Read more ...

Update the customer record to reflect the correct date of death.

Run the RGO Review script to code death action. If the script is not available, use the guided procedure. If the Notification of Death guided procedure is not available manually code the death action.

Work around for partnered customers

When recording a death, the Notification handler (NOHL) is incorrectly using the date of notification as the date to increase the survivor's rate to the single rate. The workaround for this is to record the date of receipt (DOR) as the later of date of death or date paid to + 1, whichever is the later.

Home Equity Access Scheme

Customers who are over Age Pension age, or partnered to a person who is over Age Pension age, may have an outstanding loan under the Home Equity Access Scheme (the Scheme).

This loan is usually repaid from a customer's estate after their death, or if partnered, the surviving partner's death.

If a benefit type code of PLS appears on a customer's Benefit Status Line, check the Loan Details (PLS) screen of the deceased customer.

If the customer has a Scheme loan balance greater than zero, check that a PLS manual follow up review has been automatically generated and assigned to the Home Equity Access Scheme National team (region code PLS) with activity notes “I012PH – PLS customer has passed away”.

If the PLS review has not been created send an ACTDOC to the PLS team (region code PLS) with keyword PLSDEA.

7

Bereavement payment + Read more ...

The Compliance Officer (CO) will need to make sure the relevant bereavement payment is made.

Calculate the correct amount of bereavement payment referring to the following procedures, if required:

  • Financial assistance after a death occurs
  • Calculation of Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP)
  • Calculation of a Partner Bereavement Payment (PBV)
  • 28 Day rule for bereavement
  • Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP)

Check partner's entitlement to financial assistance after a death.

Following calculation of entitlement and comparison with payments already made is a bereavement payment to be paid?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Issue bereavement payment + Read more ...

The bereavement payment should be paid via the Assessment Result (AR) screen when coding the death action.

If this is not possible code the bereavement payment on the One Off Payments (OOP) screen in the appropriate payment system:

  • enter the date of effect in the Effect Date column
  • code 'BLS' (Bereavement Lump Sum) in the Payment Type column
  • complete the Source: and DOR: fields
  • press [Enter]. Annotate the intervention DOC making sure all relevant information is included in the DOC

9

Check payments made after death + Read more ...

Check the Payment Summary (PS) screen for payments made after the date of death.

If payments were made to a deceased person's account, a refund can be sought from the account into which the payment was made.

Determine if any of the payments made after the person's death are regarded as excess payments.

Have excess payments been detected?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 11

10

Excess payments detected + Read more ...

Place the intervention and associated debt shell on ‘HLD’ (Hold) for 28 days.

Arrange for a refund of payments from the customer's financial institution, see the Requesting a Refund table in Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer.

Note: due to a current system fault, conduct a check to ensure that no-one was receiving a Carer Allowance and/or Carer Payment for the deceased customer. To do this check the Link Summary (LS) screen for a carer link end dated with the date of death of the customer. The Compliance Officer (CO) will need to assess ongoing qualification to Carer Allowance (CA) and/or Carer Payment (CP) for the carer.

See:

  • Death of adult or child care receiver and the effect on Carer Payment
  • Death of an adult care receiver and the effect on Carer Allowance
  • Death of a child care receiver and the effect on Carer Allowance

11

Check for partner record + Read more ...

Is there a partner record for the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, see Finalising RGOs

12

Partner record exists + Read more ...

Where there is a partner, check for any superannuation, investments or savings details held in the customer's name or in joint names with the customer's partner as these will impact on the surviving partner's entitlements.

Check the Future Activity List (FAL) screen for a bereavement statement activity:

  • If there is an activity on FAL no further action is required. See Finalising RGOs
  • If there is no activity on the FAL code a manual review to contact the surviving partner 16 weeks after the date of death regarding changes in circumstances resulting from death of customer. This is considered a program review and will be conducted by the customer's service centre

Is the date of death 16 weeks in the past (or more) and the customer payment is still current as death has not been notified?

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No, see Finalising RGOs

13

Date of death 16 weeks in the past (or more) and the customer payment is still current + Read more ...

  • Contact the surviving partner by phone regarding any changes to their circumstances as a result of the distribution of the estate income and assets
  • This contact can be with the assistance of a social worker, if preferred. If a social worker was not involved in contacting the partner, offer social work contact
  • If the customer advises that the estate has been settled and resulted in a change to their income and assets but our records have not been updated send customer details to Customer Compliance service desk for follow up with the program area

See the 'Finalisation process for adult, or child or care giver death matching' table in Finalising RGOs.

Initiation and assessment process for child death matching

Table 2: This table describes the steps to be taken to initiate and assess child death matches.

Step

Action

1

Initiate the child intervention + Read more ...

Ensure the initial compliance checks have been completed.

Initiate the intervention using the manual process.

2

Actioning a Child Death Match + Read more ...

All actions on the death of a child for which Family Tax Benefit, Child Care Subsidy, Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) is being paid, are to be referred to the Families Bereavement Processing Team for action.

All action for non-current child records that is, child records that no one is receiving a payment for are to be processed by the RGO Customer Compliance Team.

Is the action for a non-current child record?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Referring to Families Bereavement Processing Team + Read more ...

Send a Fast Note – Auto Text – FAMILIES > UPDATES > RGO Bereavement to the Families Bereavement Processing team to action the customer's record.

Integrated Review System (IRS) action.

  • Place the intervention on HLD for 14 days pending outcomes from the Families Bereavement Processing team
  • When action is completed by the Families Bereavement Processing Team, go to Finalising RGOs

4

Action for non-current child records + Read more ...

The record will need to be updated to ensure the record is ended. Update the customer record to reflect the correct date of death.

Run the RGO review script to code death action. If the script is not available, use the guided procedure. If the guided procedure is not available manually code the death action. As the record is for a non-current child record no bereavement action is required.

Once the record has been actioned the intervention will need to be finalised, see Finalising RGOs.

Initiation and assessment Care Giver death matching

Table 3: This table describes the steps to be taken to initiate and assess Care Giver Death Matches.

Step

Action

1

Initiate the Care Giver intervention + Read more ...

Ensure the initial compliance checks have been completed. If there is any evidence to show that changes to the customer’s record were requested after the date of death, refer the case to the Quality Development Officer for further investigation.

  • Initiate the intervention using the RGO Review script
  • Issue a Registrar-General's Office (RGO) Death Data-matching Care Giver (Q079) letter to the customer via the Letter Display (LD) screen. Allow 21 days before taking further action
  • Update the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen to record details of the contact and the expected response date. See Coding the Contact screen in the Integrated Review System (IRS)

Has the Care Giver contacted within the 21 days?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 4

2

Care Giver Contact + Read more ...

Before proceeding be aware of the procedures to refer a customer to a Business Integrity (BI) social worker, see Social workers role in Payment and Integrity.

  • Explain to the care giver that Services Australia routinely matches records with government departments including state and territory Registrar-General's Office (RGO)
  • Advise that information received indicates that the person they were caring for has recently passed away and Services Australia wishes to discuss alternative assistance and possible entitlement to bereavement payment
  • Be aware that in many cases the care giver will still have an entitlement to payment at this stage
  • Discuss options with the care giver for alternative assistance and any bereavement payment and advise that their Carer Payment (CP) or Carer Allowance (CA) will be reassessed

Does the Care Giver dispute the match data/death?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Care Giver disputes the match data/death + Read more ...

If the care giver disputes the match data/death, advise the care giver that further enquiries will be made:

  • the intervention will need to be referred to the Quality Development Officer (QDO) for vetting
  • do not reassess the care giver payment/s
  • do not proceed further until the QDO and Service Desk has investigated
  • record details of the contact on a DOC and the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen

Refer the intervention to a Quality Development Officer for further investigation. Procedure ends here.

4

Selecting the IRS intervention + Read more ...

Before any updates are made on the customer's record ensure the IRS intervention has a status of 'STA' (started) and that you are in the 'Review Image'.

5

Actioning a Care Giver Death Match + Read more ...

Discuss the case with a social worker if necessary, see Social workers role in Payment and Integrity.

A death action cannot be processed with activities on the Activity List (AL) screen. Where possible ensure any activities are completed.

If it is not possible to complete an activity attempt a phone call and send an ACTDOC (if required) to the service centre advising of the death and requesting completion of the activity.

If there is a 'STA' (Started) debt activity on the AL screen, attempt a phone call and send an ACTDOC (if required) to the debt team advising of the death and requesting completion of debt activity.

In these instances the customer's record should be manually suspended pending processing of the death.

Update IRCD/IRCS screen to place the intervention on Hold (HLD) for 7 days.

Update the deceased's customer record to reflect the death.

Run the RGO script to code death action. If the script is not available, use the guided procedure. If the guided procedure is not available manually code the death action.

6

Check Bereavement Payment + Read more ...

Qualification for bereavement assistance depends on whether the carer receives CP or CA.

Note: the Compliance Officer (CO) will need to assess ongoing qualification to Carer Allowance and/or Carer Payment for the carer.

See:

  • Death of adult or child care receiver and the effect on Carer Payment
  • Death of an adult care receiver and the effect on Carer Allowance
  • Death of a child care receiver and the effect on Carer Allowance

See Finalising RGOs.
