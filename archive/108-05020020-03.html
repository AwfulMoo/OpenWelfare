Type of lump sum + Read more ...

Exempt lump sum

Certain types of lump sums are exempt from income test assessment.

For a lump sum to be exempt it:

  • must be unlikely to be repeated
  • could not reasonably be expected to be received or anticipated, and
  • does not represent receipt of money for services rendered directly or indirectly

See Exempt lump sums.

All other lump sums are one of 2 types:

Remunerative lump sum:

  • a form of wage or amount paid for a service provided
  • generally assessable if paid after claiming an income support payment. For more information, see Item 3

Non-remunerative lump sum:

  • a 'profit' but is not remuneration nor match the characteristics of the excluded lump sums. See Item 4

Non-remunerative lump sums + Read more ...

If the non-remunerative lump sum is from another country, see Item 8 in Table 2.

For disability lump sum benefits from superannuation, see Item 10 Table 2.

Non-remunerative lump sums are assessed if the customer is claiming or receiving:

  • ABSTUDY and the lump sum was paid on or after the date of claim
  • another income support payment and the lump sum was paid:
    • after the date of claim, or
    • within 52 weeks before the customer's or partner's date of claim

Non-remunerative lump sums:

  • distributions from private trusts and private companies are keyed by a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) on the TAC system. The information 'ripples' through to the customer's or partner's record
  • all other lump sums, (except foreign pensions) must be keyed on the customer's or partner's Other Income (OIN) screen. See Item 4 in Table 2

Assessing and coding lump sums

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Lump sums to be treated as employment income + Read more ...

Employment income is a remunerative lump sum because it is for a service provided:

  • Key the employment income using the Earnings and Reporting workflow
  • Key amounts on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen
  • These amounts are to be apportioned over a period for which the lump sum represents, up to 52 weeks and are income tested
  • The event date is the date the person was paid the lump sum

If a lump sum is received prior to 7 December 2020, it may be appropriate to record it using a continuous income frequency. The continuous income must be ceased or converted to IOP/LOP within the same activity in which it is recorded, see Resources of Recording and correcting employment income details.

  • Convert the lump sum amount to a fortnightly (2WE) figure, this can be done by dividing the total amount by the number of days the assessment period represents and multiplying the daily amount by 14
  • The date the lump sum was received will be the Date of Event
  • Record details on the EAN screen

2

Keying lump sums for an existing employer group + Read more ...

The Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen can assess multiple income amounts at one time. Each time a new amount is recorded on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen, the amounts previously recorded for the same employer (if coded as IOP or LOP) will remain to be assessed for the duration the payment(s) cover, as well as assess the new amount coded.

3

Lump sum incorrectly keyed + Read more ...

If the lump sum is incorrectly keyed on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen:

  • update the information to zero ('0') on the EAPP screen manually or via the Earnings and Reporting workflow. See Recording and correcting employment income details

Note: use the same activity and date of event for both updates on EAPP and the Other Income (OIN) screen.

Update the correct information on OINS, see Item 4.

4

Keying the Other Income (OIN) screen + Read more ...

For more information, see Verifying and recording other income on the Other Income OIN screen.

Go to the OIN screen, complete the Type: field and key the following fields:

  • Description: the source of the non-remunerative lump sum
  • Reference: income reference number, if known
  • Event Date: date the person was paid the lump sum
  • Amount $: amount of other income paid
  • Freq: The amount will be assessed over a 52 week period. Key the frequency as 'ANN'

Finalise activity and record lump sum details on a DOC.

Review activity

The system automatically creates a FRD review for 52 weeks' time on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen.

Update the Notes: field in review to specify the review is for removal of lump sum details.

The system sends out a letter advising the customer and/or their partner of the new rate in most instances.

Note: for customers receiving family assistance, check the annual income estimate is reasonable and request a revised estimate, if necessary.

5

Back pay for a period of employment + Read more ...

If the customer or their partner receive back pay of employment income, ensure the following details are available:

  • date the back pay was received
  • gross amount received
    • If the back pay includes an interest amount, record it separately as a non-remunerative lump sum
  • period which the back pay covers
    • If the back pay spans multiple pay periods which aren’t consecutive, determine the total number of pay periods where back pay is relevant. For example, when bulk remediation is done and back pay is reviewed for a period of 12 months. If only 2 fortnightly pay periods have back pay, the back pay period is 4 weeks, not 12 months
    • Clearly document calculations and dates on the customer’s record

Record back pay on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen using the Employment Type code ‘Earnings from Employment (EAR). Back pay can be recorded under the same employer as usual wages, however it is important to record it as a separate entry.

Assess the back pay:

  • from the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) of the period in which it was paid
  • for the same period of time for which the income was paid, up to a maximum of 52 weeks

Note: From 7 December 2020 when back pay is received for individual pay periods, do not correct the pay periods. If the back pay was received prior to 7 December 2020 see Resources for an example of how to code.

If the back pay is for:

  • a pay period equal to or less than the number of days in the customer's entitlement period, record the back pay using the frequency code 'IOP'
  • a pay period greater than the customer's entitlement period, record the back pay using the frequency code 'LOP'

The Start Date and End Date fields must include the dates of the period which the back pay covers.

If the back pay spans multiple pay periods which aren’t consecutive:

  • code the End Date as the date the back pay was received
  • count backwards from the End Date by the number of relevant pay periods to determine the Start Date. For example, if the end date is 1 May 2022 and the back pay period is 26 weeks, the start date is 1 November 2021

The system automatically ceases the assessment of income once the applicable time ends.

For assistance with coding see Recording and correcting employment income details.

6

Bonus type lump sums + Read more ...

For bonus type lump sums, such as Aged Care Workforce Bonus Payment, record as Employment Type EAR - Earnings from Employment on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen

  • Assess the bonus as income for the period it represents, up to 52 weeks
  • The date of event is the date the person was paid the lump sum
  • It is assessed the same way as commissions received by employees
  • If the frequency code 'LOP' is used, the system will automatically remove the income once the required time has passed

If a lump sum is received prior to 7 December 2020, it may be appropriate to record it using a continuous income frequency. The continuous income must be ceased or converted to IOP/LOP within the same activity in which it is recorded, see Resources of Recording and correcting employment income details.

  • Convert the lump sum amount to a fortnightly (2WE) figure. This can be done by dividing the total amount by the number of days the assessment period represents and multiplying the daily amount by 14
  • The date the lump sum was received will be the Date of Event
  • Record details on the EAN screen

9

Other foreign income lump sum amounts + Read more ...

Some foreign lump sums are exempt lump sums:

  • Examples are, bereavement or funeral grants
  • Refunds of contributions or other withdrawals or commutations are not assessable where the amount consists entirely of a return of the customer's contributions

Note: most overseas compulsory contribution schemes include contributions from the person's employer, making them assessable.

Exempt foreign lump sums are coded on the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen.

Navigate to the FID screen. Key:

  • Country of Payment: As required
  • Type: 'LMP'
  • Reference/Description: As required
  • Event Date: Date the lump sum was received (as determined above)
  • Currency: Source currency
  • Income Amt: Lump sum amount
  • Frequency: 'ONE'
  • Asset Amt: As required
  • Rural Exempt: Not applicable

If the foreign lump sum is not exempt, it is:

  • treated as a non-remunerative lump sum, and
  • assessed for 52 weeks from the date is was paid

Assessable foreign lump sums are keyed on the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen:

  • record in the source currency
  • use the exchange rate applicable on the date the lump sum amount was paid
  • Exchange rates are available on the Foreign Exchange Summary (RDFXS) screen
  • Where the date the customer received the lump sum is unknown, use:
    • the month of issue of the notice of grant (NOG), or
    • the following month if the notice is issued within 5 days of the end of the month and it is in the customer's favour

Navigate to the FID screen. Key:

  • Country of Payment: As required
  • Type: 'LMP'
  • Reference/Description: As required
  • Event Date: Date the lump sum was received (as determined above)
  • Currency: Source currency
  • Income Amt: Lump sum amount
  • Frequency: 'ANN'
  • Asset Amt: As required
  • Rural Exempt: Not applicable

10

Disability benefits from superannuation + Read more ...

Total and Permanent Disability Benefit (TPD)

Usually paid as a lump sum payment but may also be paid as an invalidity lifetime pension from a defined benefit fund.

Lump sum payment is not assessed as income. See:

  • Assessing withdrawals from superannuation

The Defence Force Retirement Death Benefit Scheme (DFRDBS) can be accessed early if the member is medically discharged. Arrears of such a payment are not a TPD benefit, they are a non-remunerative lump sum and are assessed as income for 52 weeks from the date the person was notified of the arrears payment.

Lump sum arrears of an invalidity lifetime pension prior to commencing regular ongoing payments are assessed as income from the income stream:

  • the payments are assessed as income only from the date the person was notified by their superannuation fund about the approval of their Invalidity or TPD pension
  • it is not the date they applied for the Invalidity or TPD benefit
  • see Adding or updating a defined benefit income stream

Salary Continuance or Income Protection payments

Usually paid as regular ongoing payments; may also be paid a lump sum equivalent to monthly payments for a set period.

Lump sum arrears prior to commencing regular ongoing payments are assessed from the date of receipt for a period of not more than 52 weeks. See Verifying and recording other income on the Other Income (OIN) screen.
