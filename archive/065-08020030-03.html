Initiating recovery of the outstanding Scheme loan amounts

Step

Action

1

Is the secured real estate in the process of being sold? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 6.
  • No, go to Step 2.

2

Have changes that will affect payment of the Scheme been identified or reported by the customer? + Read more ...

  • Yes, review the customer’s eligibility for the Scheme to determine whether loan payments should continue. Advise the customer if the outcome is unfavourable. See Home Equity Access Scheme reviews. Procedure ends here.
  • No, go to Step 3.

3

Is the customer deceased? + Read more ...

  • Yes, and the customer:
    • was partnered, go to Step 4
    • was not partnered, commence recovery action immediately. Send the deceased estate letter to the executor, the executor’s legal representative or address to the estate of the deceased at the last known address. Include Executor/Administrator Request for information form (SS524). This form asks for a copy of the will
    • Process direct issues the letter via Superkey HEASL
    • DOC all actions
  • No, and:
    • the delegate has determined that the outstanding loan amount is now recoverable. Start recovery action, send the settlement letter to the customer. DOC all actions, and place on hold for 4 weeks. If the customer has not responded within 4 weeks, send a second request and hold for a further 4 weeks. If the customer has not responded to the second request go to Step 9
    • the delegate has determined that the outstanding loan amount is not recoverable. Procedure ends here

4

Is the surviving partner of Age Pension age? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Does the surviving partner have use and enjoyment of the secured real estate? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 7
    • No, issue a deceased estate letter after the 14 week bereavement period has ended, and create an MFU and hold for 90 daysIf the surviving partner contacts within 90 days to arrange repayment of the outstanding loan amount see Settlement of Home Equity Access Scheme loan amounts. Procedure ends here
    • If the surviving partner does not contact within 90 days to arrange repayment of the outstanding, DOC all actions then go to Step 9

6

Real estate being sold + Read more ...

Does the customer want to nominate another property as security for the loan?

  • Yes, see Assessing Home Equity Access Scheme applications. Procedure ends here.
  • No, see Settlement of Home Equity Access Scheme loan amounts. Procedure ends here.

7

Surviving partner of Age Pension age and has use and enjoyment of the secured real estate + Read more ...

The outstanding loan amount of the deceased customer is not recoverable until after the surviving partner is deceased or the secured property is sold, whichever occurs first.

The surviving partner is issued with a deceased estate letter, after the 14 week bereavement period has ended. Details include:

  • the deceased customer had a Home Equity Access Scheme loan with Service Australia
  • the outstanding loan balance, including costs and charges
  • interest accrued
  • how they can settle the loan if they wish to finalise the loan

If the surviving partner does not wish to settle, an annual deceased loan statement is issued. See Home Equity Access Scheme reviews. Process direct issues both the deceased estate letter and the deceased loan statement via Superkey HEASL.

Procedure ends here.

8

Surviving partner under Age Pension age + Read more ...

Is the secured real estate the surviving partner's principal residence?

  • Yes,
    • after the 14 week bereavement period has ended send the deceased estate letter to the surviving partner, to start the recovery process of the outstanding loan amount. Process direct issues the letter via Superkey HEASL
    • Note: the delegate has discretion to delay or phase recovery in unusual or specific circumstances on a case-by-case basis. See the Resources page for examples of when the delegate my exercise discretion
    • if unique circumstances exist, email the Older Australians Business Processing team with details of any unusual or special circumstances
  • No, the outstanding loan amount of the deceased customer is now recoverable. Start recovery action after the 14 week bereavement period has ended. Send the deceased estate letter to the surviving partner, to start the recovery process of the outstanding loan amount. Process direct issues the letter via Superkey HEASL. DOC actions and reason for recovery
  • Code a Manual Follow-Up (MFU) for 90 days to follow up response

9

Check for MFU 90 days after advice sent + Read more ...

Has the customer or executor of the estate (if customer is deceased) contacted and arranged for the repayment of the outstanding loan amount?

  • Yes, see Settlement of Home Equity Access Scheme loan amounts. Procedure ends here.
  • No, send an email advice from the Scheme. Retire mailbox to Debt Taskforce via their mailbox HIGH.VALUE.DEBT, with the following details:
    • Customer's Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • the Scheme outstanding loan amount (principal, interest, legal costs breakdown)
    • real estate ID
    • real estate asset value, mortgage and loan amounts
    • copy of advice from AGS regarding caveat/charge details
    • copy of advice sent to customer/executor requesting repayment of loan

Advice received from Debt Taskforce

On receipt of advice from Debt Taskforce regarding recovery arrangements and/or amounts of principal, interest and legal costs repaid, action repayments.

See Repayment of Home Equity Access Scheme loans, for ESSentials accounts and centralised cost centre numbers for each component of repayment.
