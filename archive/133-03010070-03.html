Overview of the family assistance and/or PPL online claim process

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Claim choice + Read more ...

Refer customers to the Payment Finder on the Services Australia website. This will check what the most appropriate service offer is. For example:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
  • Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
  • Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
  • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • A combination of Family Tax Benefit and Parental Leave Pay

Customers can choose whether to claim PPL or to have Newborn Supplement and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBS/NBU). The system will assess this with their FTB claim.

Early claims for FTB, PPL or DAP will not present with questions for Child Care Subsidy (CCS).

Customers cannot lodge a second PPL claim if they have a PPL claim ASSessed:

  • waiting proof of birth or
  • entry into care

Customers will have to wait until the current pre-birth PPL claim is processed. Customers should use the Add Newborn service to submit the child’s proof of birth to finalise the pre-birth claim.

Combined claims

Families claims can include:

  • FTB (current year) + PPL
  • FTB (current year) + CCS (current year)
  • FTB (current year) + FTB (previous year)
  • FTB (current year) + FTB (previous year) + PPL
  • FTB (current year) + CCS (current year) + PPL
  • FTB (current year) + FTB (previous year) + CCS (current year), and
  • FTB (current year) + FTB (previous year) + CCS (current year) + PPL

DAP is lodged as a standalone claim.

For help with CCS claims, see Claiming Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Additional Child Care Subsidy (CCS).

2

Start the online claim + Read more ...

For online claims

Customers need a Centrelink online account to claim:

  • Family Tax Benefit
  • Parental Leave Pay
  • Dad and Partner Pay

This includes claims for a stillborn child.

On the Make a Claim page, select Get Started from the Families category, then select Apply for Family Assistance (including Paid Parental Leave).

After selecting Claim now, a message will display. This will remind the customer to check My profile. Customers can review and update their details before starting their claim. Customers must make sure their personal details are correct. Select:

  • Continue will take the customer to the Claim navigation page to start their claim
  • My profile will allow the customer to review and update their details. Selecting Return to your claim will take the customer to the Claim navigation page to start their claim

Customers can:

  • access a started online claim
  • view their current online claim/s status

For Assisted Customer Claim (ACC)

Complete an ACC with the customer and a verbal signature declaration for:

  • families in crisis who are:
    • experiencing difficulty in understanding, or
    • complying with claim requirements, or
    • who need a speedy outcome to meet immediate financial needs
  • remote Indigenous customers who have language or literacy barriers affecting their capacity to use forms or online claims. Transfer the customer to the Indigenous Services Queue
  • the customer is unable or unsuitable to complete an online claim, or
  • a correspondence nominee is applying on behalf of their principal

Customers can submit their claim online or with a staff member via ACC after:

  • answering all compulsory questions, and
  • providing the documents to submit their claim

3

Review and submitting the claim + Read more ...

For online claims

If all of the information is correct, the customer must tick the box I have read, understood, and accept my obligations before they can select the Next button.

For online claims, go to Step 6

For ACC

  • Check all information is correct on the Review your claim page. If mandatory information is missing, a red Update required message displays
  • If this does display, exit ACC and update using the Change in Contact Details Workflow
  • Return to the claim in ACC

Does the customer agree to make a verbal signature declaration?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Verbal signature declaration + Read more ...

Read the declaration to the customer. Tick the box on the Review your claim page if they accept. This will confirm that the Service Officer has read the declaration script and that the customer accepts it.

Once a verbal declaration completed and ACC submitted a DOC confirming the verbal declaration has been accepted will be displayed in:

  • Customer First Super Case
  • the Customer Claim Summary

Go to Step 6.

5

Family Declaration Form (FDF) + Read more ...

If the customer declines the verbal signature declaration. Print the FDF via the Customer Declaration page.

If necessary, the FDF can be re-printed: locate claim in ACC, select View then Print. For help, see the Resources page.

  • If customer is in a Service Centre:
    • Ask the customer to sign the FDF. Scan signed FDF to their record
    • Record on Progress of claim DOC that FDF has been signed and scanned to record
    • For a PPL claim, ensure claim signature requirements are met
  • If customer is at an agent's office:
    • Fax the printed FDF to the agent
    • Record details on the Progress of claim DOC. Include that the FDF has been faxed and the name of the agent
    • Advise the agent to get the customer's signature and fax the completed FDF to the Service Centre
  • Otherwise:
    • Ensure all contact numbers and details are current
    • Mail/fax the FDF to the customer using the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure, including approved Free Text
    • Request any other outstanding information
    • The review DOC created by the guided procedure can be altered to become the Progress of claim DOC. Record any additional information and then close the DOC
    • When the signed FDF is returned, submit the ACC for processing
    • Record details on Progress of claim DOC

When the customer signs and returns the FDF, submit the claim for processing.

6

Actioning claim with status of 'Submission Failed' + Read more ...

Sometimes an error may occur between claim submission and the claim activity generating. This can be due to conflicting activities on the customer or partner record, or record locking when the claim submitted.

Claims that have failed to submit may show a status of Submission Failed in the General Information section at the top of the Social Application. An MFU may also generate when this occurs. Finalise this after regenerating the claim.

  • To action a claim with a status of Submission Failed:
    • update the claim status by selecting the User Status tab, select Set New Status and change to In Process
    • select Regenerate Claim at top
  • Another way is through the Scheduled Actions tab. To add this tab, select the Personalise (pencil) icon at top right and add it to Tile 3 - Staff Workflows
    • The NCL Activity Creation will display with a status of Incorrect and an Execute button. Note: if this does not appear, select Schedule New Actions to trigger this line
    • Select Execute in the Actions column in the same row as the active NCL Activity creation
    • Select Save at top left

This NCL Activity Creation entry status will show as Processed with a green status symbol. The claim status will now show as In Process or Submitted with errors.

If the status remains as Submission Failed, the claim activity could not create. Return to Workspace to check the record. Ensure there are no other activities preventing the claim activity from creating. Action as per existing processes.

See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions

7

Finalising the claim + Read more ...

Customers cannot update claim details after they submit it. They will need to contact the agency to provide information in relation to their claim. They can withdraw their claim online.

When the online claim is submitted, the system will:

  • index a new claim (NCL) activity or create a Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity on the customer's record
  • create a DOC
  • upload customer contact details

Information the customer provides in the online claim is viewable in the Review Claim section of the customer's online claim.

Customers may receive messages and notifications.

8

Next steps + Read more ...

This page displays a list of Required and Supplementary tasks.

For online claims

Customers can access their unsubmitted online claim to return to the Next steps page at any time.

For ACC

  • Advise the customer of the documents and forms they need. This will appear on the task list
  • Explain to the customer options available for uploading their documents online

Book an interpreter appointment if the customer needs an interpreter to help complete their claim.

Customers can provide proof of a child's birth by uploading the Doctor/Midwife declaration. This is available on the back page of the Newborn Child Declaration (FA081).

For a pre-birth claim, customers can:

  • access their Centrelink online account and select the Add Newborn service or
  • use the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app and select the Add Child service

Customers submitting a post-birth claim can use Upload documents service to upload proof of birth.

Income and payment choices - FTB

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Income + Read more ...

Early FTB claims

The customer does not have to provide income details until the child is born or enters care.

For FTB claims only

Current FTB/CCS customers will not need to provide these details. They may return to the entry page and select the Update Family Income Estimate option.

Annual income estimate

Customers must provide a reasonable estimate of their (and their partner's) Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) for the full financial year.

Customers will need to provide a separate estimate for each financial year they are claiming.

Claims for the current financial year require a separate estimate for the next financial year if:

  • it is between 1 April and 30 June, or
  • the claim starts before 30 June but the customer does not submit it until the following financial year

Taxable income includes any taxable government payments. Customers who don't need to lodge a tax return must still include any taxable income they expect to receive for the year.

For all other ATI components:

  • A request for an estimated amount will occur if the customer indicates they will receive other components
  • There will be no request when the customer indicates they expect it will be nil

Customers lodging a tax return for the previous year

Actual ATI details will transfer from the ATO. Customers will not need to provide estimates for these ATI components.

Stillborn Baby Payment (SBP)

To meet the SBP income test:

  • the customer's combined income estimate for the 6 months beginning on the date of the stillbirth must not exceed the SBP income limit, or
  • the 6 month income limit is exceeded but they are eligible for FTB Part A for another child within 52 weeks of the stillbirth.

2

FTB payment choices for current financial year + Read more ...

A pre-populated 'auto-selected' option displays based on their circumstances.

Customers who accept the payment choice displayed are less likely to incur a debt. At the end of the financial year, the system will use the customer's actual income to work out their correct FTB entitlement. This will occur during the reconciliation process. Customers will receive any extra entitlement when FTB reconciliation occurs.

Customers can select an alternative payment choice. If they do, a warning of a possible overpayment will display.

See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) claim options and payment choices.

Table 4

1

Immunisation + Read more ...

Immunisation requirements apply for any child under 20 years of age for:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) claims
  • Child Care Subsidy (CCS) claims
  • Child Care Benefit (CCB) lump sum for approved care (2017-18 financial year) claims

If an individual is not linked with the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR), customers must provide the child's Medicare number. This enables their immunisation status to be checked with AIR.

If the child is:

  • not yet enrolled for Medicare, they will need to:
    • take action to enrol the child, and
    • provide the Medicare details to the agency
  • not eligible for Medicare enrolment, the child’s record must be linked with the AIR

Customers do not have to provide documentary evidence of a child's immunisation status as part of their claim. The AIR gives it to the agency.

See also:

  • Immunisation requirements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A from 1 July 2018
  • Immunisation requirements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A supplement for financial years 2012-13 to 2017-18
  • Immunisation requirements for Child Care Subsidy (CCS)

2

Return to work - FTB + Read more ...

FTB claims - partnered customers

Parents who return to work in the financial year for the first time may receive FTB Part B free of the income test for the FTB Part B quarantine period.

Eligibility for this assistance is calculated during FTB reconciliation.

See Recording return to work details and explaining eligibility for the FTB Part B quarantine.

The customer will be asked to advise:

  • the child they were caring for before the return to work. That is the child most recently into care, usually the youngest
  • if they previously worked, the date they ceased work
  • the date they returned to work
  • if the work is at least 10 hours per week averaged over four weeks; if not, they can nominate this as a return to work but may choose to wait until later
  • if they are self-employed
  • employer details (unless self-employed)
  • details of any:
    • paid leave
    • work related insurance, or
    • self-employment income

Before eligibility for assistance can be calculated

Customers who are self-employed or who nominate a return to work must verify details via a statement after the end of the financial year.

SBP and NBS/NBU information

Table 5

Item

Description

1

Stillborn Baby Payment (SBP) + Read more ...

Customers claiming for a stillborn can claim PPL or SBP.

For SBP claims

To determine the rate of payment, the customer will be asked questions about:

  • the stillborn child/ren and
  • the family circumstances

Details include:

  • customer's relationship to child
  • whether or not the child was from a multiple birth
  • if a multiple birth, were any other children stillborn
  • family income estimate for the 6 month period beginning from the date of delivery. They may be asked to supply evidence of the estimate

From 1 January 2021 there is a single rate of SBP. The single rate is paid for each stillborn child delivered on or after 1 January 2021.

2

Newborn Supplement (NBS)/Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) + Read more ...

If a customer has claimed Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for a child, they will be automatically assessed for NBS/NBU unless they are eligible for PPL for the child.

To determine the appropriate rate of payment, the customer will be asked details about the family circumstances.

Details include:

  • If the customer has indicated the child is under one year of age:
    • For non-parent carers, will the child be in their care for a continuous period of at least 13 weeks?
    • Birth registration requirements question
    • Relationship to child
    • Is the child from a multiple birth or multiple entrustment to care?
    • For parents, has the birth mother given birth to any other children (excluding stillbirth)
    • For entrustment to care, - has the customer or partner previously had a child aged under one entrusted to their care?
  • For adoptions, has the customer or partner previously adopted a child?
  • If a second or subsequent child passes away on or after 1 January 2021 and before their first birthday, a top-up of NBS may be paid. The top-up is the difference between the lower and higher rate
  • If advice of a child’s death is part of an FTB claim, and the lower NBS rate has not previously been paid for the child, the higher rate of NBS will be paid for the eligible NBS period

PPL specific information

Table 6

Item

Description

1

PPL work test + Read more ...

PPL customers must meet the PPL work test. The customer:

  • must enter their work history details and
  • may need to provide suitable evidence to support the PPL work test

See Paid Parental Leave scheme Work Test.

Exceptions apply in some circumstances and suitable evidence may be required.

Note: previous periods of PPL and/or Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) in the 13 months before the birth or adoption of a second or subsequent child will count as ‘qualifying work’ towards the work test for the new PPL or DAP claim.

2

PPL employer details + Read more ...

PPL customers who indicate they will be employed from the child's date of birth or date they were entrusted to care as part of an adoption process to the end of the PPL period will be requested to provide the following:

  • Employer details (name, mailing address, phone number and Australian Business Number (ABN))
    • The employer may have more than one ABN. Customers must check with the employer which ABN is preferred for Parental Leave Pay purposes
  • Employee identification number (where applicable)
  • The date they commenced employment with their employer
  • Other names(s) the customer is known by with their employer, for example, their maiden name

The ABN will automatically match with the Australian Business Register (ABR). If a match is found the business name and/or trading name will display for the customer to confirm.

3

PPL income test + Read more ...

The customer must meet the PPL income test in the previous financial year. This is the financial year:

  • before the date of an early claim, or
  • the date of the child's birth/entry into care for a post-birth claim

The customer can lodge more than one claim to test eligibility. For example, where a customer lodges an early claim and the child is born in a new financial year. These customers can lodge another claim and provide the new income details.

The income limit will index annually in line with the Consumer Price Index.

Authenticated customers when lodging an online claim, will not require proof of income if:

  • the ATO has the ATI and
  • is already on the customer's record

4

Return to work - PPL + Read more ...

Generally a customer must lodge their PPL claim before they return to work to be entitled to the PPL period. There are a couple of exceptions to this rule:

  • they return to work for a permissible purpose
  • the customer:
    • lodged an effective claim within 28 days of the child's date of birth (DOB), and
    • has a nominated start date of the child's DOB or a date before they return to work, or
  • the customer is transferring the full amount of their PPL to another person

If the customer is not entitled to the PPL period due to returning to work before claiming and their child’s birth or adoption is:

  • before 1 July 2020, the claim for PPL will be rejected
  • on or after 1 July 2020, the PPL period component will be rejected. They may be entitled to Flexible PPL days

See Parental Leave Pay (PPL) customer returns to work.

Flexible PPL days can be claimed after the customer has returned to work. However they cannot be paid on days that the customer is working. See Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days.

5

Flexible PPL days + Read more ...

For children that are born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020, PPL includes 30 Flexible PPL days.

The customer will tell us how and when they want to claim these days. They can:

  • connect them to the end of their PPL period to create a continuous block of PPL of up to 18 weeks
  • claim them separately up to 2 years after their child’s birth or adoption
  • give permission for another person to claim them

For more information, see Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days.

Note: Flexible PPL changes were implemented in the claim from 14 September 2020. Additional processing arrangements will apply for PPL claims lodged before this date. See Reassessment of claims for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) due to Flexible Paid Parental Leave changes.

DAP specific information

Table 7

Item

Description

1

Work Test for DAP + Read more ...

Customers must meet the DAP work test. The customer will need to enter their work details. Customers may need to provide evidence to support the Paid Parental Leave scheme work test.

Customers need to nominate a DAP period start date.

This date can be one of the following:

  • expected date of birth
  • actual date of birth
  • date the child enters care as part of the adoption process
  • date chosen by the customer

Note: the maximum DAP period is the 14 days beginning from the nominated start date. Customers not eligible on one of the days during the 14 days, will not be eligible:

  • for that day, and
  • any remaining days of the maximum period

2

DAP options + Read more ...

The customer needs to advise that the child will be/was in their care:

  • on the first day of their nominated DAP period start date and
  • for each day during the 14 day period

More than one person may be caring for the same child on a particular day. For example, the child's birth mother and the DAP customer.

The customer must not be working or taking paid leave during the DAP period.

If the customer advises that they have:

  • worked, or
  • received paid leave

on a day during the DAP period, they may cease to be eligible:

  • from that day, and
  • each remaining day in the DAP period

3

Income test questions + Read more ...

The customer must meet the DAP income test in the previous financial year.

If the adjusted taxable income is between $135,000 and the relevant financial year income limit:

The What You Must Do page will ask the customer to provide evidence of income. This will appear when the customer submits their claim.

The claim will reject if the adjusted taxable income exceeds the income test in the financial year prior to the:

  • date of claim, or
  • nominated start date (whichever is earlier)

The customer may want to lodge another claim if:

  • their income is under the income limit for the financial year before the date they lodge their new claim or
  • their nominated start date (whichever is earlier)

For example, a claim lodged in a previous financial year to the customer's nominated start date. The system would base the income assessment on the financial year prior to the date of claim. If the income was over the income limit the customer can:

  • lodge another claim in the next financial year
  • with a future nominated start date

The system will assess the income on the different financial year.

The income limit will index annually in line with the Consumer Price Index from 1 July 2015.

Previous partner (FTB only) + Read more ...

Customers who indicate they have a previous partner must provide the previous partner's details. This will calculate the correct rate for that partnered period.

Previous partner details include:

  • name
  • date of birth, if known
  • country of birth, if known
  • current home address, if known
