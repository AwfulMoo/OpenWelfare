Action required and assessing income upon ceasing employment

Step

Action

1

Customer advises they (or their partner) have ceased working + Read more ...

If, in the previous 12 months, a customer left work or was terminated from a job, check if employment income and/or ceasing work was previously declared. If employment income/ceasing work was not previously advised, request an Employment Separation Certificate (SU1) for customers claiming or receiving a payment where a failure can apply which must be investigated or if an income maintenance period (IMP) is to apply.

This procedure does not cover customers who have ceased self-employment.

Note: if the customer is in the Youth Bonus Wage Subsidy (YBWS) 26 week suspension period, they may be eligible to have their payments restored if employment has ceased through no fault of their own. Customers ceasing employment under the YBWS initiative must have the Verify Employment Separation Guided Procedure completed to finalise the action.

If the customer or their partner is receiving a payment:

  • with mutual obligation requirements, ask them to supply an SU1 and advise them to include details of any termination or redundancy payments on their next Reporting Statement
  • with no mutual obligation requirements, they must provide details of any termination or redundancy payments when they are received

Has a lump sum termination payment been received?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Lump sum termination payment received + Read more ...

The customer or their partner received a lump sum termination payment:

  • The person who is receiving JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Partner Allowance (PA), Widow Allowance (WA), Parenting Payment (PP), Disability Support Pension (DSP) (except customers who are permanently blind), Youth Allowance (YA) or Austudy may have an IMP imposed
  • The payment may also be subject to the deeming provisions, depending on the customer’s use or investment of the amount. Update termination/leave payment on customer’s record as required
  • The person who is receiving Age Pension, Carer Payment (CP), Special Benefit (SpB) or DSP (Blind only) are not IMP affected. For more information on how to treat a lump sum termination payment, see:
    • Leave and termination payments paid by an employer
    • Treatment of lump sums
  • ABSTUDY customers who receive a lump sum termination payment (remuneration related) including leave entitlements on or after the date of grant, the lump sum is apportioned over a 12 month period in 52 weekly amounts. Lump sum termination payments received prior to the date of grant are not taken into account. See Treatment of lump sums
  • If the customer is a statement reporter and contacts on their Entitlement Period End Day (EPED) and is unable to advise of their lump sum termination payment, do not update the Report Results (RR) screen and stimulate their payment. Advise customer to contact again with lump sum termination payment details
  • DSP is subject to IMP from 20/09/2006 only
  • Other pension customers are not subject to the IMP but must have their financial circumstances updated
  • If the person is receiving another payment, the termination payment is not treated as income, but what they do with it is assessable for example, if they put it in a term deposit the term deposit is an assessable asset and income will be assessed under the deeming provisions

Note: if the pensioner received the lump sum termination or redundancy payment and their partner is in receipt of a payment subject to an IMP, the details must still be coded for correct assessment of partner’s payment.

Update any assets purchased or financial investments made with the lump sum.

3

How is the employment income currently recorded? + Read more ...

  • If employment income is recorded as income in 1 period (IOP) on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen, go to Step 4
  • If employment income is assessed as continuing employment income on the EAN screen, go to Step 5

4

Employment income currently recorded as income in one period (IOP) + Read more ...

Note: if it is not the customer’s reporting day, advise them to provide details when they next report. Procedure ends here until their reporting day.

Record the customer has ceased work on a DOC, including any employment income in the current fortnight and/or they are to receive any termination payments.

Record all employment details via the Earnings and Reporting workflow. Note: if the workflow is unavailable, manually update all details on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen. For help updating employment income, see Recording and correcting employment income details.

If the customer has ceased working and has not been paid any income during this period and the previous income was coded as IOP, do not code zero income on the EAPP screen.

For more information about taking a customer ‘OFF’ reporting employment income reporting, see Reporting screens.

If it is the customer’s normal reporting day, code the employment income for this period. For customers receiving Carer Payment (CP), ensure the hours worked are updated on the Carer Payment Activity Details (CPAD) screen. For help with coding see Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities. Go to Step 6.

For more information about selecting the correct date of event (DOV), see Determining the Date of Event for employment income.

5

Employment income currently recorded as continuous + Read more ...

Record all employment details via the Earnings and Reporting workflow. If the workflow is unavailable, manually update all details on the EAPP screen. For help updating employment income, see Recording and correcting employment income details.

For more information about selecting the correct date of event (DOV), see Determining the Date of Event for employment income.

The Centrelink system uses a notification handler (often referred to as NOHL) to calculate a customer’s payments. NOHL uses the date the information was provided (such as date of event (DOV) and date of notification) to calculate the date of effect (DOE) of the payment rate change. The Review of Assessment (ROA) screen assists with the process of determining the correct date of effect from which to apply a change in entitlement.

For customers receiving Carer Payment (CP), ensure the hours worked are updated on the Carer Payment Activity Details (CPAD) screen. For help with coding see Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities.

6

Finalise activity + Read more ...

The activity will finalise within the workflow/screen flow.

  • Ensure all details are recorded on a DOC
  • Advise customer of outcome
  • Service Officers at the service centre or Smart Centre can override the Service Profiler to change the customer to a notification reporter if:
    • the customer is a statement reporter, and
    • they do not have any reporting obligations related to their mutual obligation requirements, and
    • they are able to provide Paid Leave, redundancy or termination payment details

If the customer cannot provide Paid Leave, Redundancy or Termination payment details, the Service Profiler will determine the reporting regime. If however, the customer asks to be taken off reporting, only the Service Officer at the Service Centre can manually override the Service Profiler. See Reporting screens for further information.

Note: Service Officers must always advise a customer of new reporting requirements and notification obligations.

If an alert DOC has been coded for this employment, move it to the normal document list by entering an expiry date of today’s date:

  • Check if the customer needs to update their income estimate for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) or Child Care Subsidy (CCS). Note: their estimate must include taxable income support payments and tax-free pension/benefit amounts, employment income and any other components of adjusted taxable income (ATI) received or expected for the whole financial year for both members of the couple. If either member of the couple is eligible for income support payments, the expected payments must also be included in the estimate for family assistance
  • Check if the customer’s entitlement for CCS will be affected
