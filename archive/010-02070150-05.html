This page contains information about term-in-advance payments prior to 1 January 2019 and scenarios of changes to study that affect entitlement.

Term-in-advance payments prior to 1 January 2019

Before 1 January 2019, ABSTUDY payments delivered to boarding schools, or hostels which are a signatory to the Standard Hostel Agreement, were delivered as a lump sum term-in-advance payment. An ABSTUDY payment that was delivered term-in-advance, comprised the total calculated payments, taking into account the student's circumstances and relevant means tests, for the relevant school term entitlement period. It was delivered in a lump sum on a designated payment date of the fourth Thursday of the school term. A term-in-advance payment processed after the fourth Thursday was still delivered, but as a lump sum payment. The school term entitlement periods were:

  • 1 January to 31 March
  • 1 April to 30 June
  • 1 July to 30 September
  • 1 October to 31 December

Changes to study that affect entitlement after 1 January 2019

Scenarios of how changes to study affect entitlement after 1 January 2019

This table describes scenarios relating to changes to study that affect entitlement after 1 January 2019.

Scenario

Description

1

Student discontinues study part way through a term at a boarding school or hostel

Mary is approved for away from home entitlements to board at Canberra Girls Grammar School in 2019. It is May’s first time away from home and she becomes homesick and discontinues study at the end of Week 6 and returns home.

Canberra Girls Grammar School has been paid an ABSTUDY boarding instalment on behalf of Mary. The first instalment was delivered in Week 4. Canberra Girls Grammar School is only entitled to keep boarding entitlements up to the last day of study for Mary, which is the last day in Week 6 of the school term.

Boarding allowances payable up to the end of Week 6 will be paid to Canberra Girls Grammar School as an immediate arrears payment for the period from the first day of boarding instalment period 2, up to and including the last day in Week 6 of term.

2

Student discontinues part way through a term at a boarding school which has a no refund policy

Jasmine is approved for away from home entitlements to board at Presbyterian Ladies College (PLC) in 2019. Jasmine discontinued study on 20 May – part way through Term 2.

PLC was paid the first ABSTUDY boarding instalment for Term 2 on 19 May, which is Jasmine’s entitlements calculated up to 14 May. Upon reassessment of Jasmine’s last day of attendance, ABSTUDY boarding allowances are paid as an immediate arrears to PLC for 6 days in boarding instalment 2 (from 15 May to 20 May) up to the last day Jasmine attended at school.

PLC has a no refund policy and provides an invoice to Centrelink for the outstanding fees remaining for Term 2. No further ABSTUDY is payable to PLC as ABSTUDY away from home rate is only payable up to the last day a student attends at the school.

3

Student discontinued study at the end of the school term

Mabel completed Term 3 at Iona Presentation College and returned home to Derby for the vacation. Mabel advised Centrelink that she would not be returning to Iona for Term 4.

As Mabel completed the school term at Iona for Term 3, she is entitled to away from home rate up to the end of the relevant term entitlement period. For Term 3 that is 30 September.

Changes to study that affect entitlement before 1 January 2019

Scenarios of how changes to study affect entitlement before 1 January 2019

This table describes scenarios relating to changes to study that affect entitlement before 1 January 2019.

Scenario

Description

1

Student leaves boarding accommodation prior to 1 January 2019 to return home

Isaac is approved for the away from home rate and arrives at Myera Hostel on 26 January 2018, prior to school commencing in Term 1. Myera Hostel is a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement. Isaac becomes homesick and discontinues study in the fourth week of Term 1. Isaac is deemed to be no longer eligible for the away from home rate from the date Isaac discontinued at the hostel and returned home.

As Myera Hostel are a signatory to the ABSTUDY Hostel Agreement, and Isaac is entitled to payments from the commencement of the term entitlement period (as studies commenced by the third Friday), the Hostel is entitled to retain ABSTUDY allowances paid term-in-advance for Isaac up until the sixth Friday after the first day of school for the term. An overpayment amount will occur for Myera Hostel for entitlements received from the day after the sixth Friday until the end of the term entitlement period.

Isaac cannot access alternate ABSTUDY allowances for any period the hostel retains ABSTUDY away from home allowances. Therefore, Isaac can only begin (if entitled) to receive ABSTUDY allowances again from the day after the sixth Friday of the term.

2

Student is expelled from school prior to 1 January 2019

Dianne is boarding at Gibley College and is approved for the away from home rate. Payments are directed to Gibley College via the term-in-advance method. Dianne is expelled and returns home after 3 days of Term 3.

Gibley College do not have an enrolment policy that determines that full term fees are retained if a student ceases study. Gibley College are required to repay all term-in-advance entitlements received after the day Dianne returns home and loses eligibility to the away from home rate.

3

Student is an approved boarder and leaves school prior to 1 January 2019

Pedro has been an approved boarder at St Patrick's College for 3 years and is completing Year 12. Pedro's level of ABSTUDY entitlement exceeds the board fee charged by St Patrick's College and therefore Pedro has access to a residual amount of ABSTUDY paid fortnightly. Pedro is offered an AFL Cadetship and decides to leave school 2 weeks into Term 3.

St Patrick's College has an enrolment policy that allows them to retain payments for a term for a student who discontinues. St Patrick's College is entitled to retain the ABSTUDY term-in-advance payment for the entire term.

Pedro is deemed to have discontinued study and is no longer eligible for the away from home rate from the date of discontinuation. Pedro advised Services Australia immediately and was not paid another fortnightly entitlement after the date of discontinuation and has incurred no overpayment.

Pedro cannot access alternate ABSTUDY allowances for any period the Boarding School retains ABSTUDY away from home allowances. Therefore, Pedro can only begin (if entitled) to receive ABSTUDY allowances again from the day after the end of the term entitlement period.

4

Student leaves hostel accommodation - commenced studies after the third Friday of Term 1 prior to 1 January 2019

Jarrah gained approval for the away from home rate to attend Cardiff High School. Jarrah began residing at the Hostel on a Tuesday in Week 4 of Term 1. Jarrah became extremely home sick, and after several attempts at assistance to remain at the boarding school, Jarrah's parents decided to bring Jarrah home. Jarrah returned home only after 2 weeks of boarding at the hostel.

Jarrah commenced studies at Cardiff High School after the third Friday, and was only eligible for payments from the date studies commenced and not from 1 January (commencement of Term 1 entitlement period).

Jarrah left the hostel within 6 weeks from the date of first residence at the hostel, therefore, the hostel is entitled to keep boarding allowances for the period from the start date of Living Allowance up until the sixth Friday after that date. This would be the Friday of Week 9, 6 Fridays from the date of first residence.

Jarrah cannot access alternate ABSTUDY allowances for any period the hostel retains ABSTUDY away from home allowances. Therefore, Jarrah can only begin (if entitled) to receive ABSTUDY allowances again from a date after the Friday of Week 9 in Term 1.
