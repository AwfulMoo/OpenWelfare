How to enrol a customer who presents at a service centre

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Confirm customer’s identity + Read more ...

Ask the customer for their Customer Reference Number (CRN) or their Customer Access Number (CAN) to confirm the customer’s identity using photo ID. Crosscheck the following items against previously recorded information on the (Identity Confirmation Summary screen in Customer First:

  • the photo ID serial number
  • address
  • date of birth

Service Officers must be satisfied they are creating a PIN with the right person.

2

Read privacy statement + Read more ...

Read out the Privacy Statement and Self Service Conditions of Use. Ask for the customer's consent to continue.

The Privacy Statement and the Conditions of Use are available on the Resources page.

3

Enter details in Customer First to create a PIN + Read more ...

  • Go to Workspace>Self Service>Manage Self Service Access (AMSS)
  • Select phone self service
  • Select the FastTrack process

Write down the PIN and give it to the customer.

Ask the customer to read out the number.

Confirm this against the number on the screen.

Advise the customer all words in the common passphrase must be repeated as per the IVR prompt. Some customers may want to practice the passphrase “In Australia my voice identifies me" prior to creating their voiceprint.

4

Customer calls self service line + Read more ...

Ask the customer to call the Self Service line on 136 240 to finalise the enrolment using either

  • the Self Service phones in the service centre
  • their own mobile phone

The customer should be encouraged to call while still in the service centre so they have staff available in case of any problems.

Note: some customers may have a different CAN to their CRN. The Service Officer must advise the customer to use their CAN when calling the self service line and prompted for their CRN.

Procedure ends here.

How to enrol a customer who calls a smart centre

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Before taking the call + Read more ...

Before the Service Officer has answered the call, the customer has:

  • provided their CAN or CRN (Customers must use their CAN if it is different to their CRN)
  • heard the Privacy Statement and Conditions of Use through the IVR - Customers Consent
  • recorded their Voiceprint by repeating the Common Pass Phrase (CPP) of ‘In Australia my voice identifies me’ 3 times

2

When the call connects with a voiceprint recorded + Read more ...

If the Case Information window displays:

  • green, the voiceprint has already been recorded and authenticated. Procedure ends here
  • amber, the customer has recorded a voiceprint but it needs to be authenticated
  • red, the customer did not record a voiceprint or there is a problem with authentication

For example, the following amber icon and the green 'Confirm Voiceprint' button display because the caller has recorded a provisional voiceprint in the Smart IVR. The customer's Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO) needs to be finalised for the customer's voiceprint enrolment to be successful.

A green 'Confirm Voiceprint' button will appear when the customer's voiceprint has been successful:

See Authentication status for more icon examples.

Get the customer’s consent to finalise the enrolment. Suggested wording:

'You have successfully recorded your voiceprint with us. I can finalise your voiceprint registration for you now.'

Does the customer want to register their voiceprint? (Ensure record ownership and that this is not a partner, parent of a child record, nominee or caller on a deceased record)

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No,
    • Authenticate the customer
    • Record Fast Note - Voiceprint Recorded: Declined Enrol (customer chose not to enrol)
    • The provisional voiceprint will delete from the system within 24 hours. It cannot be accessed once the call ends
    • Continue with the customer enquiry

Note: If the customer says they do not want to enrol their voiceprint or do not want to be offered the voiceprint option in future, see Table 5

3

Confirming the customer’s identity + Read more ...

Click Confirm Voiceprint button to open the Manage Self Service Access (AMSS) workflow

Before starting the voiceprint registration workflow, confirm the customer's identity to make sure the correct customer record is used. Check the customer's:

  • name
  • address
  • date of birth
  • CRN

Select the tick box after confirming their identity.

Go to Step 4

Note: Service Officers no longer need to read the Privacy Statement or Conditions of Use, as the customer has already heard this in the IVR.

4

Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO) + Read more ...

Additional questions will display from the customer’s Centrelink record.

Select the Yes radio button in the Confirmed column for each correct answer.

Has customer completed the required PoRO questions?

Note: do not use receipt number from the current call if using "Receipt number issued within the last eight weeks”.

Yes,

  • Select the Continue button when PoRO is confirmed
  • Go to Step 5

No,

  • If the customer is unable to answer any of the questions initially presented, select the Ask more questions button. It displays a full list of the customer’s previously recorded identity confirmation items.
  • If the customer is unable to answer enough questions to satisfy PoRO, go to Step 6

5

Enrolment successful + Read more ...

Advise the customer their account is now secured with their voiceprint.

On future contacts, the customer can use their voiceprint for authentication in the IVR. They must:

  • enter their CAN/CRN (customers must use their CAN if it is different to their CRN)
  • say the common passphrase once

Note: for future authentication, the IVR may ask customers to repeat an alternative passphrase instead of the standard common passphrase “In Australia my voice identifies me.” This is part of the expected system design.

Advise the customer:

  • The system will ask extra security questions before providing service if the voiceprint:
    • does not match, or
    • is not used on a phone call
  • The voiceprint allows customers to use the agency's 24 hour phone self service options

Procedure ends here.

6

Service Officer is not satisfied they are talking to the customer + Read more ...

Do not complete the enrolment process. The voiceprint has not been finalised and will be deleted from the system within 24 hours. Once the call ends, it is not accessible.

  • If the enrolment is completed, delete the voiceprint
  • Advise the caller the voiceprint is only for the record owner to use
  • Record a Fast Note, see Step 1 in Table 4

Procedure ends here.

Customer does not want to enrol their voiceprint

Table 5

Item

Action

1

Customer does not want to enrol + Read more ...

Customers are encouraged to record their voiceprint Common Pass Phrase (CPP) in the Interactive Voice Response (IVR). Customers may record their CPP but not wish to enrol. The IVR tells customers enrolment is optional.

Some customers may present to a Service Officer and decide not to enrol. While the agency prefers voiceprint as an authentication method for phone contact, Service Officers cannot demand a customer use it.

Do not finalise the voiceprint enrolment in Manage Self Service Access (AMSS).

Advise the customer:

  • The passphrase audio has not been stored. This is because they did not complete their enrolment
  • Enrolling their voiceprint is fast and easy, and provides extra security for their record
  • Using a voiceprint means they can use phone self service applications to save time
  • They will get the opportunity to enrol in the IVR each time they call

If a customer does not want to enrol for a voiceprint:

  • create a Fast Note and record why they do not want to enrol, and
  • authenticate the customer

2

Customer does not want to be asked to enrol their voiceprint each time they call + Read more ...

Advise the customer:

  • The agency has a duty of care and must offer the highest level of security to customers
  • Voiceprints are the preferred method of authentication for phone services
  • The system is not able to stop the offer for specific customers
  • To skip the offer, they can say ‘Do not do this’ when asked to say ‘In Australia, my voice identifies me’

If the customer wishes to complain about the use of voice biometrics, go to Table 8

Managing voiceprints - failed voiceprints or suspensions

Table 6

Item

Description

1

Problems recording Voiceprint + Read more ...

The IVR will transfer the caller to a Service Officer if they are not able to record a CPP. The IVR will prompt them to enrol the next time they call with only a CAN or their CRN (Customers must use their CAN if it is different to their CRN).

Reasons why this may happen include:

  • A customer may have a different Customer Access Number (CAN) to their Customer Reference Number (CRN). The customer must use their CAN when prompted in the Smart IVR to say their CRN
  • No input - the customer does not speak as prompted, or they begin to speak too early or too late
  • Mismatched audio - the system does not identify the customer as who they say they are, for example the system detects a recording of a customer's voice is being played, multiple speakers are speaking (excluding the Service Officer), or the customer is not saying the correct Common Passphrase
  • Inconclusive audio - the technology cannot check the vocal sample spoken by the customer. This includes when the customers speech is too soft, too loud, too short or too long. Inconclusive audio may also be caused by excessive background noise. It is recommended enrolment be completed in a quiet location
  • The previous Service Officer may have provided the customer with an incorrect PIN number (Accidently wrote down a wrong digit). Or the customer may have written down a wrong digit
  • The customer must repeat all words in the common passphrase as per the IVR prompt

2

Caller asks to stop using their voiceprint + Read more ...

Proceed with caution. Complete the proof of record ownership (PoRO) to confirm the customer's identity.

Advise the customer:

  • A voiceprint is the most secure way customers can deal with Centrelink over the phone. If they do not have a voiceprint they will be offered this service each time they call
  • Deleting will remove all voiceprints Telstra has stored for the customer (on behalf of Centrelink)
  • If they have a PIN they can continue using it
  • If they want a PIN:
    • they must complete the proof of record ownership (PoRO) to confirm their identity
    • when they enter their PIN they will be asked to enrol their voiceprint

3

Customer frequently fails voiceprint + Read more ...

Reasons why this may happen include:

  • poor sound quality, for example, due to background noise, poor phone line
  • the customer must repeat all words in the common passphrase as per the IVR prompt
  • Also, go to Step 1, Problems recording a Voiceprint

The customer may have to re-record their voiceprint if reporting their voiceprint often fails and they show as:

  • 'Authentication: Failed' in the Authentication tab
  • Case Information window is red
  • Authentication displays 'Type: Voice'

Make sure it is the customer on the phone. See Step 8 in the Authenticating a customer table in Authenticating a Centrelink customer.

  • Delete the voiceprint
  • Give the customer a PIN. See the Registration for self service using Manage Self Service AMSS table in Access Management for Self Service (AMSS) - Staff-assisted access for phone self service and nominees online
  • The PIN lets the customer re-record and enrol a voiceprint the next time they call
  • Offer to transfer them to the Centrelink phone self service line to complete the enrolment
  • Do not conference call while customers record their CPP. Transfer and disconnect
  • Advise the customer to record their voiceprint in a quiet location

4

Suspended voiceprint + Read more ...

The system suspends a customer's voiceprint after 5 failed call attempts in a row to match their voice. This may be due to:

  • repeated fraudulent attempts
  • problems with the voiceprint recording
  • the caller’s audio quality
  • some customers may have a different CAN to their CRN. In this case, their CAN must be used

The IVR will advise the customer: ‘Sorry, your access to self service has been suspended’.

If they still want to use a voiceprint, help the customer with a new enrolment:

  • Make sure it is the customer on the phone. See Step 8 in the Authenticating a customer table in Authenticating a Centrelink customer
  • delete the voiceprint
  • Give the customer a PIN. See Step 2 in the Registration for self service using AMSS table in Access Management for Self Service (AMSS) - Staff-assisted access for phone self service and nominees online
  • The PIN lets the customer re-record and enrol a voiceprint the next time they call
  • Offer to transfer them to the Centrelink phone self service line to complete the enrolment
  • Do not conference call while they record their CPP. Transfer them and disconnect
  • Advise the customer to record their voiceprint in a quiet location
  • Some customers may have a different CAN to their CRN. The Service Officer must advise the customer to use their CAN when calling the self service line and Smart IVR when prompted for their CRN

Managing voiceprints - deleting voiceprint

Table 7

Step

Action

1

Delete voiceprint + Read more ...

To be completed by the Service Officer.

Proceed with caution.

Before deleting a voiceprint, make sure:

  • it is the customer requesting the deletion, or
  • it is the customer and their voiceprint is not working, or
  • the voiceprint is not the voice of the customer

See Authenticating a Centrelink customer to make sure it is the correct person on the phone.

Go to the Voice Biometrics Summary screen in Customer First.

  • Select the row for the voiceprint from the Credential Type field. Note: If you are unable to select Delete, see below for the security role required.
  • Select a deletion reason:
    • customer request
    • unauthorised use (no consent)
    • unauthorised use (consent)

If the option to delete is not available, please obtain Team Leader approval and request the additional skill tag required to delete a voiceprint. Request access in ESS through Access Management. The security role is ZC.CF.CSO.VOICE.BIOMETRICS. Once this is approved, please delete the voiceprint.
