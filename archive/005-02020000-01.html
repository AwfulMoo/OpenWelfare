This document outlines which payments and concession cards are generally subject to a Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP) and/or a Qualifying Residence Period.

Qualifying Periods of Residence prior to Grant

Qualifying Periods of Residence prior to Grant

  • For most payments to qualify and be paid, a person must:

  • Meet a minimum accumulated qualifying residence period; or
  • Serve a Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP); or
Have an exemption from 1 or both of the qualifying residence periods

Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP)

Customers who have recently arrived to live in Australia and are claiming a payment or concession are required to have been an Australian resident and physically in Australia for the required duration of the NARWP, either in 1 period, or in a number of periods before the payment or concession is payable. Note: the period of residence required for the NARWP does not need to have occurred immediately before claiming.

Temporary absences from Australia in the waiting period do not restart the NARWP but the days that a customer is overseas do not count towards meeting the NARWP requirements. For example, a customer claiming JobSeeker Payment may have a 2 or 4 year NARWP. If they leave Australia temporarily for 3 weeks the NARWP period does not recommence, but the absence extends the NARWP end date by 3 weeks.

The requirement to have been in Australia for a period or periods totalling the required duration of the NARWP, allows for broken periods of residence in Australia to count if the person continued to be an Australian resident. For example, a customer who has returned to live in Australia after several years living overseas may have previously lived in Australia as an Australian resident. The period of former Australian residence would count towards any current NARWP requirements.

Note: the NARWP only needs to be served once for the applicable payment.

Payments and concession cards subject to the waiting period

  • Austudy
  • Carer Allowance (CA)
  • Carer Payment (CP)
  • Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC)
  • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • Ex-Carer Allowance (child) Health Care Card (EHC)
  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) - Part A only
  • Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
  • Low Income Health Care Card (LIC)
  • Mobility Allowance (MOB)
  • Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
  • Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP)
  • Parenting Payment Single (PPS)
  • Partner Allowance (PA) (new claims ceased 20 September 2003)
  • Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
  • Special Benefit (SpB)
  • Youth Allowance (YA)

NARWP changes from 1 January 2019

Payments subject to an increase to existing waiting period

From 1 January 2019, the 2 year (104 week) NARWP increased to a 4 year (208 week) NARWP for any claimant who is granted a permanent resident visa or temporary visa providing qualification for payment on or after 1 January 2019 for the following payments/benefits:

  • Austudy
  • Commonwealth Seniors Health Care Card (CSHC)
  • Ex-Carer Allowance (child) Health Care Card (EHC)
  • Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
  • Low Income Card (LIC)
  • Mobility Allowance (MOB)
  • Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
  • Special Benefit (SpB)
  • Youth Allowance (YAL)

Payments subject to a waiting period from 1 January 2019

A NARWP was introduced for any claimant who is granted a permanent resident visa or temporary visa providing qualification for payment on or after 1 January 2019 for the following payments/benefits:

  • 4 year (208 week) NARWP
    • Carer Adjustment Payment (CAP)
    • Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP)
    • Parenting Payment Single (PPS)
  • 2 year (104 week) NARWP
    • Parental Leave Pay (PPL)
    • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
  • 1 year (52 week) NARWP
    • Carer Allowance (CA)
    • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A

Payments not subject to change from 1 January 2019

  • Carer Payment (CP) - remains as 2 year NARWP
  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part B - no NARWP

Changes explained

This means a customer may have to serve either a 1 year, 2 year or 4 year NARWP depending on the grant date of the first Australian visa that allows qualification for the payment or concession the customer is claiming.

If the visa grant is:

  • before 1 January 2019, the customer is required to serve a NARWP of 2 years (104 weeks) or nil for those payments that had no NARWP
  • on or after 1 January 2019, the customer is required to serve a NARWP applicable to the payment or concession they are claiming (as detailed above)

Note: for SpB only, the above determination is based on the application date of the 309 or 820 visa, rather than the visa grant date.

People who hold a visa subclass 115, 835, 117 or 837 subclass will not be affected by the increase to the NARWP or introduction of the NARWP.

No NARWP applies to Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A, Parental Leave Pay (PPL), Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) and Carer Allowance (CA) claimants whose first permanent resident visa or temporary qualifying visa (060, 070, 309, 820, 785, 786, 790 or 449) was granted before 1 January 2019.

For PPL and DAP, the NARWP does not apply if the child's date of birth or entry to care is before 1 July 2019, even if the qualifying visa is granted post 1 January 2019.

For LIC, the NARWP will generally be 2 years (104 weeks) if the person:

  • holds a temporary partner visa (subclasses 309 and 820), or
  • they hold or have held a special category visa 444 or visa subclass 115, 117, 835 or 837

If the holder of a SCV or temporary partner visa (subclasses 309 and 820) is granted a permanent visa on or after 1 January 2019, they will become subject to a 4 year (208 week) NARWP for the LIC.

The claimant will need to serve the 2 year qualifying period of residence concurrently with the 4 year NARWP for Parenting Payment (PPP and PPS).

Start date for the NARWP

The NARWP generally starts on the day the person:

  • first became an Australian resident; and
  • was present in Australia

For Special Benefit, Family Tax Benefit, Parental Leave Pay, Dad and Partner Pay and Low Income Health Care Card claimed by a person who holds a 309 or 820 Partner Provisional visa, the NARWP starts on the day the person:

  • lodges an application for the relevant visa; and
  • is present in Australia

Exemptions from the NARWP

There are a number of exemptions from the NARWP. There are also payments and concession cards available to a person serving a NARWP.

Some exemptions from the NARWP are common across all affected payments and concessions. For example, when the person is an Australian citizen. However, some payments and concessions have specific exemptions, for example certain visa holders can have an exemption to the Carer Payment (CP) or Carer Allowance (CA) NARWP, but would have to serve the NARWP for any other payment or concession.

Some temporary visas are NARWP exempt for specific payments only. For example, visa subclass 060 and 070 holders would qualify for Low Income Card and Special Benefit but would need to be granted a permanent visa and meet relevant NARWP requirements, or qualifying residence periods for any other payment or concession.

Customers whose affected payment was claimed within 2 years prior to 1 January 2017 and who were entitled to either of the following exemptions are entitled to the 2017 NARWP Exemption grandfathered status:

  • NARWP exemption as a family member of an Australian citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • Qualifying Residence exemption as the holder of a visa subclass 100, 110, 801, 814, 832 or 833

This status is displayed on the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) screen. Updates, if required for reasons of 'Appeal', 'Policy Decision' or 'System error or omission', can be made by authorised staff in International Services on the Grandfathering Override (GFD) screen.

For more information about exemptions from the NARWP, see Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period and Qualifying Residence Exemptions.

NARWP rules for New Zealand citizens

The NARWP savings provisions for New Zealand citizens determine the length of the NARWP, how it is calculated and who it applies to. The provisions take into account when the person became an Australian resident and the payment they apply for.

A New Zealand citizen arriving in Australia using a New Zealand passport is generally issued a Special Category Visa (SCV). For more information on the residence status, see Australian Residence Rules for New Zealand citizens.

Protected SCV holders qualify as Australian residents and can access the full range of Centrelink payments provided they are currently living in Australia and satisfy the relevant qualification criteria and waiting periods such as the NARWP. Past periods of presence as an Australian resident count towards the applicable waiting period.

Non-protected SCV holders do not qualify as an Australian resident and will need to apply for and be granted a permanent resident visa before any periods of presence as an Australian resident can be counted towards the NARWP for all payments and concessions except Commonwealth Seniors Health Care Card (CSHC) and Low Income Health Care Card (LIC). Periods of presence as an Australian resident prior to 26 February 2001 can be counted towards the NARWP if the customer now has a permanent visa.

Non-protected SCV holders do not require a permanent visa in the following situations:

  • One-off payment of either JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance (YA) for a non-protected SCV holder under the New Zealand 10 year residence exemption
  • Parental leave payments, family assistance and concession cards (excluding Ex Carer Allowance Health Care Card (EHC).

Qualifying Residence Period

A qualifying residence period is a period of accumulated qualifying Australian residence required to qualify for some payments. The period may be 2 or 10 years depending on the payment.

Payments subject to a qualifying residence period

  • Age Pension
  • Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • Parenting Payment (PP)

Start date for qualifying residence

The qualifying residence period commences when the customer is present in Australia as an Australian resident. The length of the qualifying residence period is either 2 years (104 weeks) or 10 years depending on the payment/allowance.

Serving the qualifying residence period

To satisfy the residence criteria generally the customer needs to have been an Australian resident physically present in Australia for the prescribed period of time, depending on the claim lodged. Temporary absences from Australia during the period of residence are treated differently depending on the payment. For a 2 year qualifying residence period, periods outside Australia will not count towards satisfying the requirements. For a 10 year qualifying Australian residence period, short periods outside Australia can be included as long as the customer continued to be an Australian resident during the absence.

Exemptions from the qualifying residence period

There are a number of exemptions from the qualifying residence requirements. Exemptions for the qualifying residence period are not necessarily the same as the exemptions from the NARWP. The qualifying residence exemptions are also different depending on the payment being claimed.

For more information about exemptions from the qualifying residence period, see Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period and Qualifying Residence Exemptions.
