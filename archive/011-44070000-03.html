Application lodged for TPV (subclass 785) or SHEV (subclass 790)

Table 1: This table describes how to extend Medicare eligibility for an unauthorised maritime arrival (UMA) who has applied for a TPV. Note: this means they have only applied for either of these visas but the visas have not yet been granted.

Step

Action

1

Extend Medicare eligibility + Read more ...

To extend the UMA’s Medicare eligibility past the previous Ministerial order date of 31/12/2017, Service Officers must:

  • have a completed Medicare Enrolment Application form, and
  • have the UMA’s existing Medicare card number
    • provided by the UMA or via search in CDMS, and
  • have proof of identity, such as:
  • Immicard, and
  • confirm the UMA has a valid visa and their visa expiry date
  • provided by UMA by way of correspondence from the Department of Home Affairs, or
  • search VEVO, or
  • UMA Daily Spreadsheet (if you do not have access to this, contact HSDD Eligibility on 150038)

Note: VEVO will confirm what visa they hold and the validity date. When they have applied for a TPV or SHEV and it is not yet granted, VEVO will show the current BVE-050 lodgement and grant date only. VEVO will not confirm that the UMA has applied for a TPV or SHEV.

Determine entitlement type + Read more ...

To determine what entitlement type to use to extend the UMA’s enrolment, Service Officers must perform an immigration data link and Medicare record search:

Search returns data link match + Read more ...

  • this is identified by the 'Immigration Clients' tab being shaded in green
  • select the Immigration Clients tab to return the search results

Where the immigration data link search returns a match with a dash (-) in the ETM field and the client status field does not say 'Granted', Service Officers must:

  • not link this immigration data link to the UMA's Medicare record
  • advise the UMA to resubmit their application once their application TPV or SHEV has been granted

The UMA will retain the ‘Entitlement type’ of an UMA until the TPV or SHEV is granted.

Note: the UMA must produce evidence of their valid BVE-050 and the expiry date by way of:

  • VEVO

Search does not return data link match + Read more ...

8

Visa grant date is in excess of 12 months ago + Read more ...

Where the UMA has an open ended BVE-050, they can continue to be enrolled for periods of 12 months at a time from the anniversary of the most recently granted BVE-050.

This would be the same date every year, based on when the BVE-050 was granted.

Go to Step 10

9

Eligibility period from visa grant date only allows for an extension of less than 3 months from the current entitlement end date + Read more ...

Where an eligibility extension of 12 months from the grant date (or anniversary of the grant date) only gives a new eligibility period of less than 3 month from the date the card currently expires, a further 12 month period entitlement period can be given.

Therefore, instead of issuing a card for a period of less than three months, an additional 12 months can be given - giving an eligibility period of anywhere between 12 and 15 months (providing the IUMA’s BVE-050 is open ended).

Go to Step 10

Immigration status remains UMA, and 'lodged' immigration data link has not been linked to the Medicare record + Read more ...

Result:

  • the UMA's current entitlement type of UMA does not change
  • the UMA is eligible for the correct entitlement period (as per BVE-050 expiry date or 12 months from the visa grant date if open ended)
  • the eligibility period will no longer be limited by a fixed Ministerial Order
  • UMA to resubmit once their application for a TPV or SHEV has been granted

Future changes to their immigration status will not be automatically updated. Applicants will need to reapply for a new Medicare card when it expires or if their TPV or SHEV is granted.

Subsequent TPV or SHEV application lodged

Table 4: This table describes how to extend the eligibility period for the holder of a TPV or SHEV who has lodged an application for a subsequent TPV or SHEV.

Step

Action

1

Extend eligibility + Read more ...

Service Officers must confirm if the applicant is the holder of a TPV or SHEV at the time of lodging their subsequent application for a TPV or SHEV.

If they have lodged their subsequent TPV or SHEV before the current TPV or SHEV has expired, VEVO will indicate the current TPV or SHEV is open ended (meaning no visa expiry date).

If VEVO indicates their TPV or SHEV has a defined visa expiry date (which has expired or is close to expiring), then they have either:

  • not applied for a subsequent TPV or SHEV prior to the current visa expiring, or
  • their application has been refused, and have not appealed the decision, or did appeal but the refusal has been upheld

If the applicant has lodged an appeal against the decision to refuse their subsequent TPV or SHEV, the VEVO visa expiry date will be ‘open ended’.

Where an applicant has applied for the subsequent TPV or SHEV before the current TPV or SHEV has expired, they will need to request an extension to their Medicare eligibility and provide:

  • a completed Medicare Enrolment Application form
  • evidence from the Department of Home Affairs that a subsequent TPV or SHEV has been lodged prior to the expiry of the current TPV or SHEV (or a VEVO check can be conducted)
  • proof of identity, such as their immicard or passport

Service Officers must check VEVO to confirm if the current TPV or SHEV is open ended (meaning there is no ‘visa expiry date’ field included in the VEVO information).

Evidence sighted must be documented in the ‘Documents’ tab in CDMS

Note: where the applicant has not provided correspondence from Home Affairs regarding their subsequent visa application, VEVO confirmation is sufficient evidence, providing the applicant’s current TPV or SHEV is indicated as being open ended with no expiry date.

2

Extension of period + Read more ...

If all requirements outlined in step 1 are met, Service Officers can extend the applicant’s Medicare eligibility for 12 months.

Comment must be added to CDMS which states:

  • VEVO sighted on [dd/mm/yy] which confirms current [TPV or SHEV] open ended with no expiry date - application for subsequent [TPV or SHEV] lodged. Eligibility extended for 12 months

Where the 12 months extension period has expired:

Where the application for a subsequent TPV or SHEV remains in progress (therefore an outcome has not yet been determined), a further 6 month extension can be given to their Medicare eligibility.

The applicant will need to resubmit all required documentation as outlined in Step 1

Service Officers must check VEVO to confirm the current TPV or SHEV is still open ended with no expiry date.

Comment must be added to CDMS which states:

  • VEVO sighted on [dd/mm/yy] which confirms subsequent application still in progress. Eligibility extended for 6 months

3

How to extend TPV or SHEV eligibility in CDMS + Read more ...

Where the applicant has provided all required documentation, and it has been confirmed via VEVO that their existing TPV or SHEV is open ended with no expiry date, their eligibility can be extended by 12 months in the first instance.

Access the Amend Medicare Entitlement Details screen in CDMS:

  • select the ‘Eligibility Status’ radio button associated with the ‘Ministerial Order (785 Visa – Temp Protection)’ or ‘Ministerial Order (790 Visa – Safe Haven Enterprise)’ entitlement type
  • select the Amend tab
  • extend the eligibility period for 12 months by amending the ‘Entitlement End Date’ field
  • Validate and update amendment

Record a comment in the CDMS record as outlined in Step 2 above.

Note: the same process applies where a further 6 month extension is required (as described above in Step 2). Service Officers must first confirm via VEVO that the current TPV or SHEV is open ended with no expiry date.

Subsequent TPV or SHEV application granted

Table 5: This table describes how to extend the eligibility period for the holder of a TPV or SHEV who has been granted a subsequent TPV or SHEV.

Step

Action

1

Extend eligibility + Read more ...

Service Officers must confirm via VEVO if the applicant is the holder of a TPV or SHEV with an expiry date in the future. A granted TPV is valid for 3 years and a granted SHEV is valid for 5 years. The visa start and expiry date of the subsequent TPV or SHEV will be outlined on VEVO once it has been granted.

Where an applicant has been granted the subsequent TPV or SHEV they will need to request an extension to their Medicare eligibility and provide:

  • a completed Medicare Enrolment Application form
  • evidence from the Department of Home Affairs that a TPV or SHEV has been granted (or a VEVO check can be conducted)
  • proof of identity, such as their immicard or passport

Service Officers must check VEVO to confirm if the applicant now holds a current TPV or SHEV which has an expiry date in the future.

Evidence sighted must be recorded in the Documents tab in CDMS.

Note: where the applicant has not provided correspondence from Home Affairs regarding their TPV or SHEV application, VEVO confirmation is sufficient evidence, providing the applicants current TPV or SHEV is indicated as being valid (meaning the expiry date is in the future).

If VEVO shows that the TPV or SHEV is open ended with no expiry date, this indicates that the application process has not yet been finalised. Refer to Table 4 above.

2

Extension period + Read more ...

If all requirements outlined in Step 1 are met, Service Officers can extend the applicant’s Medicare eligibility for the duration of their new TPV or SHEV.

Where there is an existing comment in CDMS which indicates a subsequent TPV or SHEV has been lodged, an update to that comment must be added which states:

  • VEVO sighted on [dd/mm/yy] which confirms subsequent [TPV or SHEV] granted. Eligibility extended to visa expiry date

3

How to extend granted TPV or SHEV eligibility in CDMS + Read more ...

Where the applicant has provided all required documentation, and VEVO has confirmed that their new TPV or SHEV has been granted, their eligibility can be extended for the duration of their visa.

There should be an immigration data link in CDMS for the granted TPV or SHEV. Link this to the Medicare record.

Access the Amend Medicare Entitlement Details screen in CDMS:

  • A new ‘Entitlement Details’ must be entered to reflect the new TPV or SHEV which has been granted. This will be either the ‘Ministerial Order (785 Visa – Temp Protection)’ or ‘Ministerial Order (790 Visa – Safe Haven Enterprise)’ entitlement type
  • Key the ‘Country of Relevance’
  • Entitlement Start Date (date the new TPV or SHEV was granted)
  • Entitlement End Date (date visa expires)
  • Entitlement End Reason – ‘entitlement ceased’
  • Validate and update amendment

Access the Amend Medicare Card Details screen:

  • amend the ‘Group Eligibility Start Date’ – to the date the original TPV or SHEV was granted
  • amend the ‘Group Eligibility End Date’ – to the expiry date of the new TPV or SHEV
  • record a comment in the CDMS record as outlined in Step 2 above

Ensure all other required details are updated (address, contact details etc.) and finalise transaction.

Step

Action

1

Extend Medicare eligibility + Read more ...

To extend an UMA's Medicare eligibility past the previous Ministerial order date of 31/12/2017, Service Officers must:

  • have a completed Medicare Enrolment Application form, and
  • have the UMA’s existing Medicare card number
    • provided by the UMA or via search in CDMS, and
  • confirm the UMA has a valid BVE-050 either with or without a visa expiry date
