This page contains the processes to follow to complete income compliance debt remediation on a customer's record. Follow this process in sequence after completing the initial assessment.

Select the required process from the drop down box below.

Removing ATO averaged income

Removing the averaged Australian Taxation Office (ATO) income

Step

Action

1

Establish the entitlement period start and end dates + Read more ...

Using information from the remediation investigation, remediate the employer/s identified from the review data.

Identify the first Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) that was updated when income was applied within the Income Compliance Review. To do this:

  • check employer details on the Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen
  • determine the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD)
  • use the EPSD for the Review of Assessment (ROA) screen and any reassessment updates

2

View the Limiting Date (LMTD) screen for limiting date impacts + Read more ...

Check the limiting date on the LMTD screen. Be in the correct system to view the relevant limiting dates for that payment type. There may be different limiting dates in each system.

NSS:NGI

PEN:PNGI

PGA:PGGI

Limiting date has a reason of Bulk Update (BLK)

If there are limiting date impacts and the reason is BLK in the required system, some of these limiting dates can be moved. APS5 staff with access to change limiting dates can move the limiting date.

Moving the limiting date to an earlier date will let the reassessment occur before the BLK limiting date. This should adjust income credits and identify underpayments. In this case, the system can complete the remediation.

The limiting date cannot be moved for any other reason, e.g. DOC, LNK, ULK, NCL or BTR.

If the following occurs, refer to staff with access to change limiting dates:

  • NSS and PGA system BLK limiting dates can be moved even if the customer is not current
  • PEN system BLK limiting dates cannot be moved unless the customer is current in the PEN system

Limiting date has a reason of Remediation Update (REM)

If there are limiting date impacts in the required system with the reason REM, manual action is needed when making updates to the customer’s record that will interact with the recorded averaged income information.

The REM limiting date will be the debt end date plus one day of the averaged debt. Staff need to remediate the customer’s record, returning it to its pre-averaged state, from the first EPSD of the coding of the averaged income review period.

A limiting date that is after the debt end date may prevent a correct result, Staff will need to complete a manual calculation.

3

Verified income + Read more ...

There may be scenarios where verified income is identified on the customer's record.

Verified income can be identified as one or more of the following:

  • verified income coded on the customer's record within the review period
  • verified documents have been found on the record and the information has not been updated, and/or
  • the debt has been finalised ‘no debt' and the customer has provided payslips or verification of income for the period of the original review

Verbal payslip information and bank statements are not considered verified income for recoding information back onto the customer’s record.

Note: when checking the customer's record and removing ATO averaged income, make sure to remove all coding on EAN in the same activity for the period of the review.

4

Removing ATO averaged income + Read more ...

To remove ATO averaged income, delete or correct the affected entries for each employer listed in the match period for the review.

This includes deleting the zero income line or re-recording a zero income line that was updated as part of the original income compliance review and which could impact the customer’s ongoing rate. This will be a different Activity Management Reference (AMR) from the ATO averaged income coding.

Start remediation of earnings using:

  • Date of receipt: the date the customer was issued the pre-refund notice. For customers who have not received a pre-refund letter, this is the date the customer’s debt was reduced to zero FND
  • Source: INT (internal) - staff will need to establish the EPSD
  • System: NSS/EAN or PEN/EAN or PGA/EAN, Start the activity in the correct payment system. Check the system and benefit type the customer was current on during the review period

When a customer has not declared earnings in the match period:

  • select the employer from EANS and update from EAN screen
  • key 'D' next to affected entries and confirm delete
  • recode all non-matched employers that were deleted as part of the CUPI process

If there are no other earnings for this employer outside the match period:

  • delete this employer from the EANS screen
  • key ‘D’ next to the employer and confirm the deletion.

When a customer has declared income in match period:

  • select the employer from EANS and update from EAN screen
  • key 'C' next to affected entries and correct to what the customer had originally declared
  • key 'CSO' in the Change Reason field
  • key 'NVE' in the Verified field

Check and correct the Actual Earnings Details (EAAD) screen. The ATO match data will need to be deleted if updated as part of the review, e.g. same AMR. Only verified payslip details should remain coded on this screen. If verified payslip details remain coded on EAAD, document that EANS will be updated in the next activity.

Did the customer have Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE) employer/s coded within the review period?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE) employer + Read more ...

Check if the customer has employment coded with an ADE employer and their employment income reported through the Employer Electronic Reporting (EER) process.

If the ATO averaged income was coded as an ADE employer, delete the affected entries for each match listed in the match period by the relevant AMR. Then re-code all original EER coding.

If the ATO averaged income coded is:

  • only for an ADE employer, start remediation by using
    • Date of receipt: the date the customer was issued the pre-refund notice. For customers who have not received a pre-refund letter, this is the date the customer’s debt was reduced to zero FND
    • Source: INT (internal)
    • System: NSS/EAN or PEN/EAN or PGA/EAN. Start the activity in the correct payment system. Check the system and benefit type the customer was current on during the review period
  • both earnings and ADE employer/s, make sure to use the same activity (AMR) to update all entries

To remove ATO averaged income for ADE employers:

  • select the ADE employer from EANS and update from EAN screen
  • key 'D' next to affected entries and confirm delete
  • re-code all original EER channel type income using:
    • original Event Date
    • ‘CSO’ in the Change Reason field
    • ‘FVE’ in the Verified field

Check the Actual Earnings Details (EAAD) screen. The ATO match data will need to be deleted if updated as part of the review, e.g. same AMR. If there was previous EER channel coding on EANS, this should be verified on EAAD. If it was deleted as part of the ATO averaged income update, make sure the EER periods are re-coded on EAAD.

Did the customer have Other Income coded within the review period?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Removing Other Income + Read more ...

If the ATO averaged income was coded as Other Income, delete or correct the affected entries for each match that was listed in the match period by the relevant AMR.

Examples of Other Income (not a complete list):

  • Other Income (OINS)
  • Income Maintenance Period Summary (IMPS)
  • Real Estate Income (REBS)
  • Managed Investments (MIS)
  • Income Stream (SUPS)
  • Seasonal Work (SWPS)

Note: if the customer has averaged income coded as earnings and other income, make sure to use the same activity (AMR) to update all entries.

  • If the ATO averaged income is only Other Income, start remediation by using:
    • Date of receipt: the date the customer was issued the pre-refund notice. For customers who have not received a pre-refund letter, this is the date the customer’s debt was reduced to zero FND
    • Source: INT (internal)
    • System: NSS/EAN or PEN/EAN or PGA/EAN. Start the activity in the correct payment system. Check the system and benefit type the customer was current on during the review period
  • When the customer has not declared any previous Other Income in the same period as the match period, in Customer Record:
    • select the entry from the relevant screen
    • key 'D' next to affected entries and confirm delete
    • if there are no other entries for this payer outside the match period, delete this payer from the relevant screen
    • key ‘D’ next to the payer and confirm the deletion
  • When the customer has previously declared Other Income in the match period, in Customer Record:
    • select the entry from the relevant screen
    • key 'C' next to affected entries and correct to what the customer had originally declared
    • key 'CSO' in the Change Reason field and 'NVE' in the Verified field

7

Limiting date + Read more ...

Was a limiting date identified which impacts remediation?

  • Yes, a manual calculation may be needed, See the relevant process on this page, e.g. Working Credits or Income Bank
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Reassessment and coding of the Review of Assessment (ROA) screen + Read more ...

After remediating all earnings, complete an ROA in the remediation activity to reassess back to the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) of when the income was applied to the record.

Note:

  • If ROA is not completed at this time, a future reassessment may generate these arrears which could be incorrectly paid to the customer causing a duplicate payment and another debt
  • If ROA does not generate arrears a reassessment should be coded on Benefit Action (BA) screen within the same activity using the event date as the EPSD

For remediation activities related to income compliance averaging, these dates will be specific to the Minister decision that led to the agency reducing the identified debts to $0.

The decision to reduce the debt and refund the customer was made under section 126 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 or section 303 of the Student Assistance Act 1973.

For entitlement periods not within the review of decision period, standard Notification Handler rules will be applied. It is possible that arrears may be calculated for these periods.

Coding the ROA screen

The date of receipt for all decisions will be the date the customer was issued the pre-refund notice, or the date the debt was Finalised - no Debt (FND).

  • Only applies to payments administered under the Social Security Act
  • Section of SSAA Review: 126
  • Source of error: Centrelink
  • Customer notified of Original Decision: No
  • Start Date for Review of Decision: use the Entitlement Period Start Dates (EPSD). This allows for both notification and statement reporters in the cohort.
    • Use the first EPSD of the EPED first used when income was applied within the Income Compliance Review, or
    • If income was applied for a period before grant, use the first EPSD of the period the customer was on payments
    • If a customer has been granted during the entitlement period, use the GRANT date if ATO averaging income information has been applied over the period
  • Review Request within 13 weeks: check if the original income compliance review has been completed in the last 13 weeks
  • Delegate: Yes

Has the correction to earnings adjusted the income credits back to pre-ATO averaged income and do the arrears generated equal the debt amount?

  • Yes, arrears need to be suppressed to zero and the activity finalised, go to Step 10
  • No, and:
    • the income credit balance has not adjusted back to a pre-ATO averaged income state, see the relevant process on this page for manual calculations, e.g. Working Credits or Income Bank impacts
    • arrears generated do not match the debt amount, go to Step 9

9

Identified underpayment /not paid + Read more ...

In most cases, arrears will not be payable to the customer as the activity needing remediation will not have occurred within 13 weeks before the date of the original decision to apply the averaged income.

If an update to a customer’s record has created arrears payments, make a decision under section 109(4) or 109(5) of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 to decide if these are payable.

If the system calculates arrears as the exact amount of the related Income Compliance Intervention debt, arrears can be zeroed out on the Assessment Result (AR) screen and there is no subsequent impact.

After deleting the ATO averaged income and coding the ROA screen:

  • go to the AR screen
  • any arrears payable will display, as calculated by the system

If ROA does not generate arrears:

  • Code a reassessment on BA screen in the same activity using the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) as the event date
  • The action reason on BA may vary depending on the payment type and benefit status
    • If current on any payment type, use the BA screen to code REA
    • For cancelled PEN and PGA records, use the BA screen to code a Reassessment of a Cancelled Record (RCR).

To investigate the arrears further, view the following screens from the AR screen. To show the result in ADEX, key 'W' in the box at the AR screen:

  • Rate Summary (RATS) screen shows
    • each pay period where there is a rate change
    • old and new rates
    • number of affected pay periods
    • amount of the adjustment for each period
  • Assessment Circumstance Explanation (ACE) screen shows details of any changes in circumstances that contributed to an adjustment in the customer's or partner's rate for NSS, PEN and PGA systems

Are the arrears on the AR screen accurate and payable?

  • Yes, and there was:
    • verified income, go to Step 11
    • no verified income, procedure ends here
  • No, and arrears need to be adjusted, go to Step 10

10

Adjusting arrears + Read more ...

In most cases, arrears will not be payable to the customer. If an update to a customer’s record has created arrears payments outside the debt period, make a decision under section 109(4) or 109(5) of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 if these are payable to the customer.

If the arrears do not match the income compliance debt or if the decision is to pay the arrears generated, the case must be referred to a Quality Development Officer (QDO) for advice on how to progress the activity.

If arrears are present, ensure adjustments are made only on income support payment arrears. If there are arrears for Families or Student payments, these will need to be assessed separately. If needing more help to action Families or Student arrears, refer to a QDO. See relevant training.

If the customer is not entitled to arrears or if arrears need to be suppressed, in Customer Record:

  • Select the debt remediation activity from AL
  • Progress the activity to the AR screen
  • Key ‘T’ in the box on the arrears line
  • Press [Enter]
  • On RATS screen, identify which arrears line/s are incorrect and:
    • Key ‘R’ next to the arrears to be suppressed
    • Enter the adjusted amount in the Man Adj $ field
    • Enter 'ADJ' in the Adj Rsn field
    • Move through each screen by using [F11]
    • After coding all adjustments, press [Enter] to return to AR

Document the Remediation Outcome Fast Note with details of any updates to the customer's record and investigation.

After adjusting arrears and getting the expected outcome on AR, finalise and complete the activity.

11

Code all verified income in a new activity + Read more ...

If there is verified income on the customer's record:

  • Code all verified income to the customers record and complete activity
  • If there are negative adjustments, make sure an undetermined debt shell is generated
  • Use the Customer declared and verified earnings Fast Note to handoff this work item, attaching the Fast Note to the undetermined debt shell

Note: if coding the verified income generates a small debt (e.g. under $50), the system will waive it. In this case there is no need for an undetermined debt shell or Fast Note. The case is fully remediated and no further action is needed.

If the system does not create an undetermined debt shell, one can be created manually. See Create a new debt record manually on DMIS.

Transfer debt activity

Go to the Activity List (AL) screen. Select the DMN/NDB activity and go to the TA screen. Update the fields:

  • To Regional Office: (region of processing team)
  • Keyword: (to aid the WLM Classification of the activity)
    • Add keyword 'DQRXXX', where XXX is the region code generating the debt
    • Add a further keyword relating to the debt benefit type, e.g. 'AGE' for Age Pension

Transfer using Fast Note template. Select Auto text > Debts > DMBRaising.

Working credit impacts

How to remediate a customer record that has working credit impacts

Step

Action

1

Entitlement period start and end dates + Read more ...

Information from the remediation investigation should have identified the employer/s and/or other income from the review data.

Identify the first Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) that was updated when income was applied within the Income Compliance Review. To do this:

  • check employer details on the Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen
  • determine the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD)
  • use the EPSD for the Review of Assessment (ROA) screen and any reassessment updates

2

View the Limiting Date (LMTD) screen for limiting date impacts + Read more ...

Check the limiting date on the LMTD screen. Be in the correct system to view the relevant limiting dates for that payment type. There may be different limiting dates in each system.

Limiting date has a reason of Bulk Update (BLK)

If there are limiting date impacts and the reason is BLK in the required system, some of these limiting dates can be moved. Staff with access at the APS5 level can move the limiting date.

Moving the limiting date to an earlier date will let the reassessment occur before the BLK limiting date. This should adjust income credits and identify underpayments. In this case, the system can complete the remediation.

The limiting date cannot be moved for any other reason, e.g. DOC, LNK, ULK, NCL or BTR.

If the following occurs, refer to staff with access to change limiting dates:

  • NSS and PGA system BLK limiting dates can be moved even if the customer is not current
  • PEN system BLK limiting dates cannot be moved unless the customer is current in the PEN system

Limiting date has a reason of Remediation Update (REM)

If there are limiting date impacts in the required system with the reason REM, manual action is needed when making updates to the customer’s record that will interact with the recorded averaged income information.

The REM limiting date will be the debt end date plus one day of the averaged debt. Staff need to remediate the customer’s record, returning it to its pre-averaged state, from the first EPSD of the coding of the averaged income review period.

Is the limiting date before the first EPSD?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, and:
    • the customer is current in the PEN system and has a limiting date in the PEN system with reason BLK or REM, or the customer has a limiting date in NSA system with reason BLK or REM, go to Step 3
    • the limiting date cannot be changed, go to Step 6

3

Refer to staff with access to change limiting dates + Read more ...

An APS5 staff member with suitable access can change the limiting dates with the reason BLK or REM in some instances. See the Resources page for how to move limiting dates.

Moving the limiting date to an earlier date will allow the system to automatically reassess and make updates to the customer record.

Note: limiting dates should be moved as far back as possible, which is the day after the earlier limiting date in that system. Once the BLK is moved it cannot be moved again.

Moving the limiting date to before the ATO averaged income was applied will allow the working credit balance to reassess when taking the remediation action.

Once the limiting date has been moved, go to Step 4.

4

Check the Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS) screen + Read more ...

The WCBS screen allows staff to view working credits and the relevant accrual and depletion. Select any line on WCBS to view more details. For more information, see Working Credit.

Go to the WCBS screen and check the AMR number where the ATO averaged income was applied.

Check for impacts to the partner’s working credits. The impact that a partner's income has on a recipient's working credit depletion depends on whether the couple is an allowance couple or a pensioner couple. Refer to Social Security Guide reference 3.1.11.30 Working credit depletion for further details. See References for a link.

If a Youth Allowance (YA) customer has received YA as both a student and a jobseeker, check both the Income Bank (NIB) and Working Credit (WCBS) screens. Check all subsequent impacts to identify income bank as its own impact.

5

Coding ROA screen + Read more ...

Make sure all relevant income has been removed from the customer's record. Then complete an ROA. This is to avoid incorrect arrears in future reassessments, causing a duplicate payment and potential extra debts.

Coding the ROA screen:

  • Only applies to payments administered under the Social Security Act
  • Section of SSAA Review: 126
  • Source of error: Centrelink
  • Customer notified of Original Decision: No
  • Start Date for Review of Decision: use the Entitlement Period Start Dates (EPSD). This allows for both notification and statement reporters in the cohort.
    • Use the first EPSD of the EPED used when income was applied within the Income Compliance Review, or
    • If income averaging was applied for a period before grant, use the first EPSD of the period the customer was on payments
    • If a customer has been granted during the entitlement period, use the GRANT date if ATO averaged income information has been applied over the period
  • Review Request within 13 weeks: check if the original income compliance review has been completed in the last 13 weeks
  • Delegate: Yes

If ROA does not generate arrears, code a reassessment on BA in same activity:

  • Use the EPSD as the event date
  • Code Action reason
    • REA for any customers current on payments
    • RCR for cancelled PEN and PGA records

Check the WCBS screen to see that the working credit balance has been adjusted back to pre-ATO averaged income balances.

To investigate the arrears further via an ADEX, on the AR screen:

  • Key 'W' in the box on the arrears line
  • Press [Enter]
  • Open the ADEX via the HTTP address populated

To investigate the arrears further via Rate Summary (RATS), on the AR screen:

  • Key ‘T’ in the box on the arrears line
  • Press [Enter]
  • The RATS screen will display:
    • Service Reason
    • Entitlement period/s
    • Old and new rates
    • Days in the relevant pay period
    • Adjustment amounts for each period

Have the working credits correctly adjusted and do arrears equal the debt amount?

  • Yes, arrears need to be suppressed and remediation finalised, go to Step 6
  • No, and:
    • arrears generated do not equal the debt amount, go to Step 6
    • working credit balance is not auto-adjusting back to pre-ATO averaged income state, go to Step 7

6

Adjusting arrears + Read more ...

In most cases, arrears will not be payable to the customer. If an update to a customer’s record has created arrears payments outside the debt period, make a decision under section 109(4) or 109(5) of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 if these are payable to the customer.

If the arrears do not match the income compliance debt or if the decision is to pay the arrears generated, the case must be referred to a Quality Development Officer (QDO) for advice on how to progress the activity.

If arrears are present, ensure adjustments are made only on income support payment arrears. If there are arrears for Families or Student payments, these will need to be assessed separately. If needing more help to action Families or Student arrears, refer to a QDO. See relevant training.

If the customer is not entitled to arrears or if arrears need to be suppressed, in Customer Record:

  • Select the debt remediation activity from AL
  • Progress the activity to the AR screen
  • Key ‘T’ in the box on the arrears line
  • Press [Enter]
  • On RATS screen, identify which arrears line/s are incorrect and:
    • Key ‘R’ next to the arrears to be suppressed
    • Enter the adjusted amount in the Man Adj $ field
    • Enter 'ADJ' in the Adj Rsn field
    • Move through each screen by using [F11]
    • After coding all adjustments, press [Enter] to return to AR

Document the Remediation Outcome Fast Note with details of any updates to the customer record and investigation.

After adjusting arrears and getting the expected outcome on AR, finalise and complete the activity.

7

Code all verified income + Read more ...

If there is verified income on the customer's record:

  • Code all verified income to the customers record and complete activity
  • If there are negative adjustments, make sure an undetermined debt shell is generated
  • Use the Customer declared and verified earnings Fast Note to handoff this work item, attaching the Fast Note to the undetermined debt shell

Note: if coding the verified income generates a small debt (e.g. under $50), the system will waive it. In this case there is no need for an undetermined debt shell or Fast Note. The case is fully remediated and no further action is needed.

If the system does not create an undetermined debt shell, one can be created manually. See Create a new debt record manually on DMIS.

Transfer debt activity

Go to the Activity List (AL) screen. Select the DMN/NDB activity and go to the TA screen. Update the fields:

  • To Regional Office: (region of processing team)
  • Keyword: (to aid the WLM Classification of the activity)
    • Add keyword 'DQRXXX', where XXX is the region code generating the debt
    • Add a further keyword relating to the debt benefit type, e.g. 'AGE' for Age Pension

Transfer using Fast Note template. Select Auto text > Debts > DMBRaising.

8

Checks before a manual calculation + Read more ...

Check the WCBS screen for any other income affecting the customer’s entitlement to working credits. Select any line on WCBS to view more details. For more information, see Working Credit.

If the customer was getting a student payment, also check the Income Bank (NIB) screen to make sure the income bank is calculating correctly.

If the working credit or income bank balance is not auto-adjusting, complete the following checks before starting a manual calculation of the customer’s entitlement.

Does the customer have other income that prevents the accrual of any working credits for all periods of averaged income?

  • Yes, and there are no further impacts:
    • there is no need for a manual calculation
    • DOC the record
    • go to Step 10
  • No, and:
    • the customer had no income recorded after the review period, and a break in payments of 12 months or more, there is no need for a manual calculation. DOC the record. Go to Step 10
    • a manual calculation is needed, go to Step 9

9

Complete manual calculation + Read more ...

If staff cannot determine what the working credit balance should be, a manual calculation using Multical is needed. This is to check if the customer has been paid the correct rate of payment. The calculation needs to cover the period from the start of the remediated income until after the hard limiting date. This will allow the correct calculation of working credits.

Staff may need to correct the customer’s working credit balance to allow the system to make further assessments to identify other impacts. For more information on amending working credit balances, see Working Credit.

Check the customer record to see if:

  • the customer has a subsequent income related debt raised for the same payment type after the review, and
  • the debt was raised before the limiting date

If there is a subsequent debt, the manual calculation will start from the EPSD of the averaged income until the end date of the subsequent debt. If the subsequent debt needs amending, see Changing account details on debts raised in the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS).

Consider impacts to the customer’s rate after the debt period where incorrect working credit balance was applied due to coding of income averaged ATO data. The customer may have reported earnings with an event date after the debt period.

If having coding issues with MultiCal, see the Resources page for the Multical Problems guide. For more help, check with a QDO.

All MultiCal debt calculations must be saved to FileNet and printed, scanned and uploaded to the customer's record. See Steps 12 and 13 in Using the MultiCal debt calculator.

Make sure all ATO averaged income has been removed.

SME quality check

Table 1: Process for when a customer has an inflight Subject Matter Expert (SME) quality check for income averaged ATO debt. Note: this process applies to SME quality checks requested before 15 May 2021. For information about the internal review process from 15 May 2021, see Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process.

Step

Action

1

Pending review of decision on customer's record + Read more ...

Staff will need to action pending SME quality checks that relate to ATO income averaged debts that were finalised as ‘no debt’ under the income compliance refund process.

As a result of the income compliance refunds process, a review under the relevant legislation resulted in a fully favourable decision to reduce the ATO income averaged debt to zero (finalised ‘no debt’).

This means that the decision the customer was seeking to review has been changed.

A letter has been issued to the customer explaining the outcome of the income compliance refund process under the relevant legislation.

As the customer requested a review of the debt decision and is expecting an outcome, staff need to contact the customer to let them know of the change to the decision.

2

Check APL system + Read more ...

Check the APL system to confirm if the review of decision is at the SME quality check (ODM) or Authorised Review Officer (ARO) level.

In Customer Record:

  • Sys field: key ‘APL’ and press [Enter]
  • Next: field: key ‘ACL’ and press [Enter]
  • The level column will show
    • ODM for a SME quality check
    • ARO for an ARO review

If there is a:

  • pending SME quality check identified, go to Step 3
  • pending ARO review identified, see Table 2
  • no outstanding review, or the level is not ODM or ARO, procedure ends here

3

Confirm pending SME quality check relates to income compliance debt/s + Read more ...

First confirm that the SME quality check is linked to an income compliance debt that has been reduced to zero as part of the Income Compliance Refund process.

Go to:

  • Document List (DL) screen to locate the 'DMN Payments requested' DOC
  • Note the relevant Debt ID/s which have been reduced to zero and/or refunded
  • Debt List (OPDL) screen to select the relevant debt
  • General Information (OPGI) screen of the relevant debt. Note the date the debt was raised
  • DL screen and locate the relevant APL DOC 'APL AXXXXX Request Pending'
    • Note the APL ID
    • The APL DOC will be on or after the date the debt was raised
    • Select the DOC and note the relevant debt ID and date debt raised
    • DL screen for any other DOCs relating to the same APL ID or any other DOCs that indicate the SME quality check has been completed

There may be times where the APL DOC does not appear exactly as above, but if staff are aware that a pending SME quality check exists for the debt/s, this should be actioned.

If the SME quality check relates to multiple debts and across multiple review types, refer to a Quality Development Officer (QDO) for clarification. Only after exhausting all avenues are staff to send an enquiry to the Service Desk.

Is there a pending SME quality check?

  • Yes, self assign the work item/DOC, go to Step 4
  • No, procedure ends here

6

Issue Q888 letter to explain the debt review outcome + Read more ...

Issue a Q888 letter to a customer when:

  • the customer has requested a review of a debt that has been reduced to a zero balance as a result of the income compliance refund process, and
  • 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer by phone have been unsuccessful

This letter explains the outcome of the debt review.

Letter quality

All staff are responsible for making sure that letters created manually promote a high focus on delivering quality outcomes. Letters must be checked for correctness. This includes but is not limited to:

  • correct formatting
  • spelling errors
  • missing words
  • punctuation applied correctly

See the Resources page for the Q888 letter template.

Finalise the SME quality check + Read more ...

Staff must update the APL system to finalise the SME quality check:

In Customer Record:

  • Sys field: key ‘APL’ and press [Enter]
  • Next: field: key ‘ACL’ and press [Enter]
  • ‘S’elect the relevant APL record
  • Go to the Modify Reconsideration (MR) screen and code these details:
    • New Info Received: N
    • Date Finalised: XX/XX/XXXX <Today’s date>
    • Outcome: SAS
    • Comments: Decision set aside as part of the Income Compliance Refund process

Manually close the APL DOC after updating the APL system.

Close or complete any other outstanding items relating to the Income Compliance debts that have been reduced to zero or refunded. For example, scanned images.
