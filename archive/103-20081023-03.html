Wellbeing exemption requests

Table 1: This table contains information to assist Service Officers with the correct referral pathway for a Wellbeing Exemption Assessment

Step

Action

1

Risk assessment + Read more ...

Is the CDC participant talking about suicide, self-harm or harm to others?

  • Yes, see Customers talking about suicide or self-harm. Procedure ends here.
  • No, go to Step 2

2

CDC impacting mental, physical or emotional wellbeing + Read more ...

A Wellbeing Exemption should not be offered up front, as it is a participant-initiated process.

A Wellbeing Exemption is only to be considered if the participant presents at risk or with concerns that the CDC is impacting their mental, physical or emotional wellbeing.

If a participant is not at risk and wants to end participation in the CDC see Exit Applications from the Cashless Debit Card (CDC).

Does the information provided by the participant indicate they may benefit from a Wellbeing Exemption assessment?

  • Yes,
    • warm transfer the participant to the Cashless Debit Card Hotline
    • For CDC Hotline staff, go to Step 3
  • No, DOC the participant's record with the reason
    • If the participant is not at risk and wants to end participation in the CDC, see Exit Applications for the Cashless Debit Card
    • Procedure ends here

3

Determine if a referral is required + Read more ...

For Cashless Debit Card Hotline Staff only

Confirm the participant has been switched onto the CDC programme correctly.

Discuss how and why the CDC is negatively impacting the participant's physical, mental or emotional wellbeing and the reason they want to exit the program. Ensure the participant understands how the CDC works and can effectively be used.

Confirm participant’s card issuer on the Payment Details Lodgement (PDL) Screen by checking the BSB for the restricted account.

  • Traditional Credit Union participants' BSB will be 704 119
  • Indue participants' BSB will be 704 772

Obtain the following card and banking-related information from CDC screens via Workspace or by contacting the card issuer. Information will form part of the Guided Procedure referral:

  • Has the participant:
    • an active card and uses it regularly
    • had any replacement CDCs
    • had a high volume of reoccurring transactions in the past 30 days
    • had their online account set up
    • had a current or previous Housing or Other Expenses Limit
    • conducted internal transfers
    • used their $200.00 other expenses limit regularly
    • currently or previously had any regular BPAY or Direct Debit set up from their account
  • Identify and resolve any card functionality issues they may be experiencing. Provide information and support on how they can access their CDC funds, including flexible allocation options for CDC, using the information obtained from the card issuer. If appropriate, this may include one-off or urgent transfers via the card issuer
  • Explain how they can change deductions, including amount and frequency, to meet their ongoing needs
  • Offer Financial Wellbeing and Capability Activities that can assist them in developing their financial skills

Service Officers can also provide contact details and website information for the card issuer. See Resources.

Is a Wellbeing Exemption assessment referral required?

  • No, go to Step 4
  • Yes, go to Step 5

4

Referral not required + Read more ...

If the participant is satisfied with card functionality support, but disagrees with being placed on the CDC, advise the participant:

  • why they have been placed on the Cashless Debit Card (CDC)
  • if they want to end participation in the CDC,a 'Cashless Debit Card exist application' (SS526) with supporting evidence will need to be lodged, see Exit Applications from the Cashless Debit Card
  • of the review and appeal process

Record details of discussion on a DOC.

Does the participant wish to review or appeal the decision to be placed on the CDC?

  • Yes, see Request for an explanation or application for a formal review
  • No, procedure ends here

5

Triaging Wellbeing Exemption requests + Read more ...

Discuss the participant's concerns in line with the Wellbeing Assessment - Preliminary Questions, taking into consideration the impacts the CDC may be having. For example, whether the participant:

  • has health issues impacting their understanding of the CDC
  • has an inability to understand the functionality and associated processes of the CDC
  • presents with an advocate or community agent representative concerned about the impact of the CDC on the participant's wellbeing

Considering the discussion, is a Wellbeing Exemption assessment referral still appropriate?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, DOC the participant's record with the reason
    • If the participant is not at risk and wants to end participation in the CDC, see Exit Applications from the Cashless Debit Card
    • Procedure ends here

6

Refer to the CDC Social Worker + Read more ...

Where the participant’s request has been reviewed, card functionality issues addressed, and the Service Officer is satisfied that the CDC may be impacting on the participant's mental, physical or emotional wellbeing, refer the request to social work for assessment.

Run the Guided Procedure Workspace>Guided Procedures> Social Work Referral.

  • Referral Reason: Cashless Debit Card
  • Referral Details: Select the participant’s CDC Declared Area
  • Additional Information: Wellbeing Assessment

Use the Social Work Referral template from Resources in the notes field of the guided procedure, ensuring all information obtained as part of Step 2 is included.

DOC the participant's record using Fast Note - Select Auto Text, use Generic > Cashless Debit Card > Wellbeing Exemption Request.

Note: the participant will remain on the Cashless Debit Card until a full assessment and delegate decision has been made.

For CDC Hotline, procedure ends here.

Wellbeing Exemption Assessments

Table 3: This table explains the process undertaken by Social Workers to complete the CDC Wellbeing Exemption Assessment

For CDC trained Social Workers only.

Step

Action

1

Referral received + Read more ...

CDC Wellbeing Exemption referral is received via WLM to undertake an assessment.

Once allocated annotate the Wellbeing Exemption Request DOC that the referral has been received.

2

Undertake Wellbeing Exemption Assessment + Read more ...

  • Following the initial interview with the customer manually create a DOC on the customer’s record to indicate that a wellbeing assessment is in progress
  • Title the DOC SW contact - CDC wellbeing assessment
  • Annotate this DOC as required during the assessment process with significant progress updates such as:
    • Date and time of unsuccessful contact attempts
    • Participant interview completion date
    • Pending third party interview dates and times

3

Complete Wellbeing Exemption Assessment report + Read more ...

To complete the report using the:

  • Social Work Recording Application (SWRA) in Process Direct, go to Step 4
  • Social Work Information System (SWIS) in Customer First, go to Step 11

4

Recording the CDC wellbeing assessment report using the SWRA in Process Direct + Read more ...

  • Select the Social Work Recording Application on the Task Selector screen
  • On the Reports and Case Notes Summary screen select New
  • Assessment type: from the drop down list select Report - General (SW) - Cashless Debit Card
  • Select Next

5

Complete consent to record and share page + Read more ...

On the Consent to Record and Share screen:

  • Complete all of the mandatory fields
  • Add third party contacts where required

6

Recording the Referral Record screen + Read more ...

Some fields on the Referral Record screen will prepopulate. Review all fields and make appropriate changes.

  • Customer Status: select the most appropriate option from the drop down box
  • Interpreter used?: Y or N
  • Cultural Background: will pre populate based on information currently recorded on the customer's record and can be updated as required
  • Referral allocated through Workload Manager?: Y or N
  • Select Referral Source:
    • If the referral is received from Workload Manager (WLM) from the CDC Processing team select DHS and Other
    • If the referral is received from the Social Work Services (SWS) National Team Branch select Specialist/Professionals- Social Worker
    • Other referral source categories can be selected as applicable
  • Customer currently on Income Management?: select yes or no. If yes, select the relevant measure from the drop down list
  • Referral Issues: will prepopulate based on the assessment type selected and cannot be changed. Referral issues will be:
    • Cashless Debit Card Wellbeing Assessment
    • Health Issues
    • Mental Health Issues
  • Select Next to go to the Contact Record screen

7

Recording the Contact Record screen + Read more ...

  • Area and Location fields will prepopulate based on the user profile and can be updated as required
  • Contact date: will prepopulate with today's date and can be updated as required
  • Contact type: will prepopulate to- File/Paper Assessment and cannot be changed
  • Entitlement Decision: This will be left blank.
  • Social Work Intervention: will prepopulate to Advice- DHS Services and Other Assistance
  • Outcome of Intervention: will prepopulate to Assessment Completed and Information Collected

8

Recording the CDC wellbeing assessment report + Read more ...

All CDC wellbeing exemption assessments must have a report completed and transferred to SWIS.

  • Complete all sections on the General Social Work Report screen
  • Recommendation section within report: select No Recommendation Required. Note: the headings listed are designed to assist with report writing. The use of these heading is not mandatory and they can be deleted or changed as appropriate.
  • Complete the DOC as identified in the Report Template and below
  • DOC TEXT:
    SW phone interview undertaken [insert date/s] with customer in respect to their request to be exited from the Cashless Debit Card (CDC).
    Third party: yes/no
    General Report recorded in SWIS, and forwarded to the delegate for consideration
    Doc by SW [insert logon]

Social workers must have their work cleared by a delegated CDC SWSM before proceeding. If no delegated CDC SWSMs are available email report to the National Team for clearance. When no further edits are required select 'Submit' for the report to be transferred into SWIS Customer First

As Social Work Services are not the decision maker for the CDC Wellbeing exemption, the report must be sent to the decision making delegates, Welfare Quarantining Programme Management, by the delegated CDC SWSM. If a delegated CDC SWSM is not available, email the National Team and they will send the report.

Note: social workers can save reports whilst awaiting the delegation decision by selecting the save and exit function on the General Social Work Report screen.

9

Social Work Support Managers record the delegation + Read more ...

Social Work Support Managers recording the delegation of the report for non-delegated social workers must add a contact in SWIS Customer First.

  • Select the referral created by the SWRA
  • Select Create a new contact
    • Contact Type: field select File/Paper Assessment
    • Entitlement Decision: field leave blank
    • Social Work Intervention: field select Delegating SW Assessments
    • Outcome of Intervention: field select Assessment Completed
  • Annotate the DOC with a note that the assessment has been delegated

10

Recording additional contacts + Read more ...

Recording a Wellbeing Exemption determination

Table 5: This table explains the process to record decisions related to Wellbeing Exemption determinations.

For the Cashless Debit Card Processing Team only.

Step

Action

1

Wellbeing Assessment determination received + Read more ...

Wellbeing Exemption determinations are received by the CDC Processing Team via WLM for coding. The completed determination with the delegate’s decision can be viewed on the participant’s record.

Go to Workspace > DOC tools, and search for the form code IQNDEC – CDC Wellbeing Assessment Decision.

Open the document to view the delegate’s determination. Only staff with the ISP WELLBEING_USERS access can view this document.

Is the determination to Exempt the participant from the CDC?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Record exemption + Read more ...

  • Go to Workspace > Cashless Debit Card > Decision Details and select Wellbeing Assessment
  • Decision Type will default to Wellbeing assessment
  • Select Approved to Wellbeing Impacted due to Cashless Debit Card
  • Under Additional information select Add mandatory notes. See Resources for Approved Wellbeing Outcome annotation standard text
  • Processing Declaration select and Continue to complete the activity
  • An approval letter will be generated and issued to the participant advising of the outcome
  • Create a new closed Fast Note - select Auto Text, Generic > Cashless Debit Card > CDC Wellbeing Outcome

Contact the participant to confirm BSB and Account details to which any residual CDC will be transferred.

Email the card issuer to disburse residual CDC funds and close account. See email templates on the Resources page of Ending Cashless Debit Card (CDC) participation.

Procedure ends here.

Note: if pre-emptively coding an existing Restricted Access participant in a CDC location, contact the National Restricted Access Team to seek temporary access to the participant’s record. The Resources page contains a link to National Restricted Access team contact details.

3

Record rejection + Read more ...

  • Go to Workspace > Cashless Debit Card > Decision Details and select Wellbeing Assessment
  • Decision Type will default to Wellbeing assessment
  • Select Reject to Wellbeing Impacted due to Cashless Debit Card
  • Under Additional information select Add mandatory notes. See Resources for Rejected Wellbeing Outcome annotation standard text
  • Processing Declaration select and Continue to complete the activity
  • A rejection letter will be generated and issued to the participant advising of the outcome
  • Create a new closed Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Generic > Cashless Debit Card > CDC Wellbeing Outcome

Procedure ends here.

Note: if pre-emptively coding an existing Restricted Access participant in a CDC location, contact the National Restricted Access Team to seek temporary access to the participant’s record. The Resources page contains a link to National Restricted Access team contact details.

Review and Appeals

Table 6: This table explains how to record details of a CDC wellbeing exemption appeal requested by the participant.

Step

Action

1

Reviews and Appeals + Read more ...

Participants may request an explanation, or apply for a formal review, of the decision to not be exempt from the CDC at any time.

If a participant wishes to request and explanation or apply for a formal review of the decision, the usual review and appeal process applies.

Select the following options:

  • Payment Type: Income Quarantining/IQN
  • Process Type: OTH
  • Which office/Unit made the Decision: CDT
  • Reason for Contact: Disagree with legislation/POL
  • Decision Type: IQA/Application/Admin
  • Reason for Decision: Wellbeing Exit Assessment (WEA)
  • Transfer Request: CDT

This will correctly refer to request for an explanation to appropriately skilled CDC processing staff for action. See Request for an explanation or application for a formal review.
