Age of customer + Read more ...

Check if the student or Australian Apprentice:

3

Criteria for away from home rate + Read more ...

Does the student or Australian Apprentice meet a criteria for the away from home rate?

The away from home (AFH) rate is determined by assessing why a customer is living away from the permanent home. When assessing this for ABSTUDY customers in state care, the 'Permanent Home' is defined as:

  • For a student in state care who has a foster carer, the permanent home is the place where the foster carer normally resides
  • For a student in state care who does not have a foster carer, the permanent home is the place where they normally reside

A customer may be placed into the care of a boarding school. In these cases, they do not have any place that they ‘normally reside’. These vulnerable customers are most likely placed with respite carers who may change every school holidays. These customers would be eligible for the AFH rate under ‘Home Conditions’ as they do not have a place where they ‘normally reside’. As the department still accepts ongoing responsibility for them, they are ineligible for the independent criteria ‘Unreasonable to Live at Home’. Note: do not assess the AFH rate from the place that the child was removed from before being placed into state care.

  • Yes and they meet the provisions for either:
    • Schooling B Award
    • Tertiary Award, or
    • Masters and Doctorate Award, go to Step 4
  • No, the customer may be eligible for:
    • ABSTUDY School Term Allowance
    • ABSTUDY School Fees Allowance (SFA) at home rate (Group 1), or
    • ABSTUDY School Fees Allowance (SFA) (Group 2)
    • if eligible for the allowances above, the student is to be assessed for ABSTUDY Schooling A Award. See Assessing the Claim for ABSTUDY Schooling A
    • ask the customer to provide supporting evidence. Go to Step 9

Student in state care + Read more ...

Is the student or Australian Apprentice currently in state care?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Customer is in state care and is eligible to be paid Living Allowance + Read more ...

Does the student's or Australian Apprentice's carer receive a foster care or similar allowance? This includes cases where the carer is an organisation, or Departmental Accommodation as they have been placed directly into this type of accommodation service, for example, a student has been placed into a boarding school by a government department.

  • Yes, the customer may be eligible for the at home rate of Living Allowance. See ABSTUDY qualification for the at home (standard) rate. Ask the customer to provide supporting evidence. Go to Step 9
  • No, the customer may qualify for the away from home rate of Living Allowance. See Assessing ABSTUDY student in care for the away from home. Procedure ends here

6

Student or Australian Apprentice further criteria + Read more ...

Check if the student or Australian Apprentice:

  • has left the carer's home, and
  • is living in a government funded or subsidised refuge or residential care facility, and
  • the state/territory welfare department has not accepted ongoing responsibility for the customer?

Does the student or Australian Apprentice meet the above criteria?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Student or Australian Apprentice stopped situation due to age + Read more ...

  • Has the student or Australian Apprentice stopped being in one of the following situations only because of their age?
    • they are not living with a parent, and
    • they are in the guardianship, care or custody of a court, a government minister or department of an Australian, state or territory government department

      or
    • there is a current direction from a court, government minister or department placing the student or Australian Apprentice in the guardianship, care or custody of someone who is not their parent. For example, the state/territory welfare department no longer accepts responsibility for the customer because of their age
  • Yes, see Step 7 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Not Eligible for Independence + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice is not eligible for grant of independent status for ABSTUDY based on being, or having been in state care. Complete the following tasks:

  • advise the customer of the decision (including reasons for the decision). Explain review and appeal rights to the customer
  • consider whether the customer is independent on other grounds. See Independence for ABSTUDY customers
  • consider whether the customer is eligible for the away from home rate. See Away from home rate for ABSTUDY
  • if appropriate, refer the customer for other assistance, e.g. social worker, Indigenous Service Officer

Note: while general advice based on information provided by the customer can be given over the phone, encourage the customer to test their eligibility, particularly in cases of discretionary decisions.

See Step 8 in Table 2.

9

Supporting evidence + Read more ...

Ask the student or Australian Apprentice to provide evidence of their current circumstances from the state/territory welfare department.

Evidence can be provided from the state/territory welfare department or other suitable source such as Courts, and should address the following points from the state care criteria:

  • the student or Australian Apprentice is not living with a parent, and
  • they are in the guardianship, care or custody of a court, a government minister or department of an Australian, state or territory government, or
  • there is a current direction from a court, government minister or department placing the student or Australian Apprentice in the guardianship, care or custody of someone who is not the student's or Australian Apprentice's parent, or
  • the student or Australian Apprentice stopped being in one of the above situations only because of their age

Supporting evidence may not be required for Schooling A Award if Formal Foster Care is coded on the guardian’s Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM) screen

Supporting evidence will be required in the future if the student is transferring from Schooling A to Schooling B. This is because the correct rate of payment needs to be assessed

Customer contact + Read more ...

If the student or Australian Apprentice has contacted through a service centre, scan the claim/form and all supporting documents.

All scannable document exceptions will be recorded in a DOC, showing where and when the document was received, what action was taken with the document and where it was sent. Go to Step 11.

If the student or Australian Apprentice has contacted through a service centre, refer to the appropriate ABSTUDY Smart Centre. Look up local Smart Centre processing details for: ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY PES. Go to Step 11.

If the contact has been received by the ABSTUDY smart centre processing team, scan the claim/form and all supporting documents.

Record all scannable document exceptions in a DOC. State where and when the document was received, action taken on it, and where it was sent.

Collecting evidence and coding independence details

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Claiming Independence + Read more ...

  • If the student or Australian Apprentice is applying with their ABSTUDY Claim (SY019), ensure questions on the student Circumstances or Australian Apprentice circumstances are completed
  • If the student or Australian Apprentice has lodged their claim form (SY019) and not completed the above, send the customer an ABSTUDY Additional Information Form (SY023) and ask her/him to complete the relevant questions regarding their circumstances
  • If the student or Australian Apprentice is applying for independent status after the grant of ABSTUDY, either:
    • verbally obtain the required information from the customer, or
    • ask the customer to complete an ABSTUDY Change of Circumstances/Enquiry Sheet (SY053), indicating they are claiming independent status because they have been in state care

Note: these forms should be sent out by Smart Centre staff or given to the student over the counter at a service centre.

2

Supporting evidence + Read more ...

Ask the student or Australian Apprentice to provide evidence of their current circumstances from the state/territory welfare department.

Evidence can be provided from the state/territory welfare department or other suitable source such as courts and should address the following points from the state care criteria:

  • they are not living with a parent, and
  • they are in the guardianship, care or custody of a court, a government minister or department of an Australian, or a state or territory government, or
  • there is a current direction from a court, government minister or department placing the student or Australian Apprentice in the guardianship, care or custody of someone who is not the student's or Australian Apprentice's parent, or
  • the student or Australian Apprentice stopped being in one of the above situations only because of their age

If the student or Australian Apprentice has contacted through a service centre, refer to the appropriate ABSTUDY Smart Centre. Look up local Smart Centre processing details for: ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY PES. Go to Step 8.

If the contact has been received by the ABSTUDY smart centre processing team, scan the claim/form and all supporting documents.

Record all scannable document exceptions in a DOC. State where and when the document was received, action taken on it, and where it was sent.

4

Updating independence + Read more ...

Update ABSTUDY independence details on the customer's record by completing the following fields on the NIH screen in the ISS system:

  • in the Action: field, code 'HOM'
  • in the Start Date: field, code the date of grant of independence status
  • in the Start/Reject Reason: field, code 'HOE'
  • in the Consent to Contact: field, code 'CNG'

Is the student undertaking an approved scholarship course?

  • Yes, code 'Y' in the field, To update regional classification details, go to the Regional Location Details (RLD) screen, go to Step 5
  • No, for all other students, go to Step 6

Note: the End Reason and End Date fields do not need to be completed.

5

Regional Location Details (RLD) screen + Read more ...

For information on coding the RLD screen, go to the Process tab of Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding.

6

Independence for under 18 years old + Read more ...

For under 18 year olds who are granted the independent status due to homelessness (UTLAH), make sure the student or Australian Apprentice has provided their own bank account details and that these details are recorded on the system. This will avoid difficulties that are likely to arise if the ABSTUDY payments are incorrectly directed to the parent(s).

Ensure sensitive information (report from state/territory welfare department) is stored in a sealed envelope on the customer's file.
Note: these papers are destroyed when the customer turns 22.

Go to Step 8.

7

ABSTUDY customer no longer responsibility of government minister or department, or court order only because of their age + Read more ...

For ABSTUDY purposes, a student or Australian Apprentice who has met the qualification for state care and has ceased care only because of their age can qualify for entitlements available to students or Australian Apprentices who are still formally in state care.

See Assessing ABSTUDY student in state care for the away from home rate.

8

Recording decisions + Read more ...

Record details of decision or discussion on a DOC.
