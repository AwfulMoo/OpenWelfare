QMO prerequisites table

Requirement

Minimum Standards

Additional information

Training
  • Completed all QMA training as listed in the QMO Accreditation Procedure

Confirm the staff member has completed the QMA training packages and the content is understood.

Proficiency
  • Achieved the required proficiency/accuracy Level - see QMA Staff Proficiency Policy

Check data reports for the work type they are applying to check. Note: QOL Stat reports may also need to be checked.

Communication
  • Identifies and manages emotions
  • Writes fairly and briefly
  • Uses plain English
  • Communicates based on facts

Review written material of the staff member to ensure their communication meets the minimum standards, for example, Plain English Policy.

Time Management
  • Knows when and how to prioritise workloads
  • Achieves results within timeframes and can negotiate new deadlines
  • Assess how complex and lengthy work items are handled.
  • Check the work item is completed within timeliness standards.
  • View the number of work items returned to the pool and know why
  • Ensure the required standards are met.
    		•

    Continuous Improvement Focus
    • Demonstrates a commitment to service quality and continuous improvement
    • Applies sound problem-solving skills
    • Demonstrates decision making skills
    • Provides feedback to improve processes

    Look at the processing of the work type and the complexity in the outcomes achieved.

    Review the outcomes to see if problem solving skills are applied before referring to Service Support Officers (SSO) or Technical Peer Support (TPS).

    Ensure the required standards are met

    Giving and Receiving Feedback
    • Open to receive feedback
    • Provides effective feedback

    Check the feedback on QMA sent to the staff member to see they have applied the knowledge gained for future work.

    Check QMA disputes and ensure the dispute is supported.

    Business Knowledge
    • Demonstrates knowledge of the programmes delivered
    • Demonstrates good practice in work processes
    • Proficient in the use of tools and resources
    • Demonstrates ability to gather facts and apply appropriate legislation and policy
    • Demonstrates knowledge of the agency’s Operational Privacy Policy and all agency guidelines

    Ensure the staff member:

    • has business knowledge
    • can research, and
    • can use tools and resources such as Operational Blueprint and SharePoint pages to resolve knowledge gaps

    Review the use of support, for example, SSO and TPS, ensure they are referred only when necessary and without multiple contacts for the same topic / issue.

    Ensure the required standards are met.
