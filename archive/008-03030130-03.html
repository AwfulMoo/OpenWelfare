3

Compare Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) and Disability Medical Assessment (DMA) reports + Read more ...

Compare the DMA report to the JCA report. Ensure all medical conditions are assessed in the JCA report, are considered and recorded in the DMA report. Conditions assessed as customer or recipient reported or other in the JCA report do not have to be considered by the GCD, but may be assessed if medical evidence is provided or obtained during the course of the assessment.

In some cases, the GCD

  • disagrees with the JCA about the presence of a medical condition.
  • determines the customer is manifestly medically eligible. GCD must record the manifest indictor in the report. They need to mark the condition as permanent and verified by medical evidence. It is not mandatory to mark the condition as fully diagnosed, treated and stabilised (FDTS) or assign Impairment Rating(s)

The GCD must outline this in the section titled Does your report differ from the JCA? In these cases, it is considered that the medical condition has been assessed.

The GCD:

  • must not provide an alternative diagnosis to that documented in the medical evidence, or a provisional diagnosis of a medical condition, and
  • may determine the customer has additional medical conditions or the medical condition/s selected by the JCA are incorrect. Check this is clearly explained in the DMA report

Medical conditions assessed separately in the JCA report must be assessed separately in the DMA report. If a GCD has chosen to combine or separate out medical conditions as documented in the JCA report, check this is clearly explained in the DMA report.

Contributing GCD assessments

For some DMA referrals, multiple GCDs may be involved in assessing the customer's medical conditions. The customer will have their DMA assessment conducted by the Primary GCD. A Contributing GCD completes the assessment of other conditions as needed.

The Primary GCD is assigned based on the medical condition with the highest functional impact/combined functional impact as indicated in the DMA referral. If their nature is:

  • physical, a Medical Practitioner is assigned
  • psychological, a Clinical Psychologist is assigned
  • split between physical and psychological, a Medical Practitioner is assigned

If the Primary GCD can assess the secondary conditions they can do so, but a Contributing GCD is compulsory if the customer has 2 primary conditions that attract 20 points each on different tables. For example, 20 points on a physical table and 20 points on a psychological table.

The Primary GCD may consider obtaining a Contributing GCD assessment if:

  • they feel the secondary conditions are out of scope of practice. For example, if a DMA has primary physical conditions and significant psychological secondary conditions such as intellectual disability or mental health conditions
  • FDTS, or impairment rating recommendations for the primary conditions are significantly different to the JCA or SA471 (Disability Medical Assessment during appeal)

Where multiple GCDs have been involved in the DMA assessment (assessing different medical conditions), the PDF version of the DMA report contains information from each GCD. The Primary GCD's complete assessment is followed by the Contributing GCD's complete assessment.

The report must be returned if a GCD indicates:

  • they cannot assess some medical conditions, even if the primary medical condition attracts 20 points or more. Return the report for the additional conditions to be assessed, before it is used to process a claim or undertake a review of decision
  • that 'assessment of secondary medical conditions are not relevant for the purposes of this assessment' (or similar wording), and there are secondary medical conditions which have not been assessed
  • the customer meets manifest criteria, but the relevant indicator is not displayed in the DMA report

Does the DMA report consider all medical conditions supported by medical evidence and assessed in the JCA report?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, return the DMA report, go to Step 9

If unsure, go to Step 8 to hold DMA report and refer to SSO.

4

Independent view of customer's conditions + Read more ...

The GCD must show they have formed an independent view on all medical conditions the customer has at the time of the assessment. This must include at least all conditions assessed in the JCA report which are supported by medical evidence. The GCD must not rely solely on the JCA report to form their opinion or advise they are not qualified to complete an assessment of particular medical conditions. If text in the DMA report substantially relies on or copies the text in the JCA report, the DMA report must be returned.

To show an independent view of all medical conditions, the report should contain enough medical information and justification of recommendations for the Delegate to feel confident the recommendations are evidence-based and comply with legislation. The Delegate should not have to refer to medical evidence (including the JCA or SA463) to understand DMA recommendations.

Has the GCD formed an independent view of the customer's medical conditions?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, return the DMA report, go to Step 9

If unsure, go to Step 8 to hold DMA report and refer to SSO.

5

Check DMA report for double counting + Read more ...

If a customer has multiple conditions rated on the same Impairment Table, the combined functional impact must be assigned against 1 of the medical conditions. The second or subsequent time that Impairment Table is used, an impairment rating of zero must be applied.

If a customer has 2 or more medical conditions with a common impairment, only one relevant table should be applied and a single impairment rating assigned to reflect the combined impairment. It would be inappropriate to assign a separate impairment rating for each medical condition as this would result in the same impairment being assessed more than once (double counting).

Does the same Impairment Table appear more than once in the DMA report and have ratings greater than zero been applied more than once against the medical conditions or has the same impairment being assessed more than once?

  • Yes, return the DMA report, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 6

If unsure, go to Step 8 to hold DMA report and refer to SSO.

6

Treating Health Professional (THP) contacts + Read more ...

Sometimes GCDs need to contact a customer's THP to verify medical evidence. In limited cases, a DMA can be completed with the customer's THP, for example when the person is in prison, psychiatric confinement or deceased.

If THP contact is needed, a GCD must document the following.

For successful contacts:

  • the date they contacted the THP
  • the name of the person they spoke with, and
  • all relevant information provided by the THP

For unsuccessful contacts, in the additional details section of the DMA report:

  • the dates (2 different business days) on which they tried to contact
  • which THP they tried to contact, and
  • what information they needed from the THP

If this information is not included, return the report for the information to be added.

Refer all submitted DMA reports conducted without contact with the customer or the THP to the GCD Liaison Officer for advice before acceptance. In the referral, explain why the DMA report should or should not be accepted. The GCD Liaison Officer will provide advice on how to proceed.

Note: in certain circumstances, DMA appointments are booked to be completed with a THP as a directive from Services Australia. To identify these cases, check the referral note in the DMA referral. If the GCD has been unable to contact the THP, the GCD will record this in the additional comments section in the DMA report. Do not refer these cases to the GCD Liaison Officer.

If contact with a THP is required or the assessment was completed with the THP, has the GCD outlined THP contact/attempted contact in the DMA report?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, return the DMA report, go to Step 9

If unsure, go to Step 8, to hold DMA report and refer to SSO.

7

Justification of findings in the DMA report + Read more ...

GCDs are required to demonstrate a thorough and independent assessment of the customer's medical conditions and to justify their findings in the DMA report.

The DMA cannot rely primarily on the findings in the JCA to justify their recommendations for either primary or secondary conditions when:

  • there is not enough information or justification in the report for the delegate to be confident a thorough and independent assessment has been made, or
  • justification is ambiguous or unclear, or
  • there are concerns about the justification for FDTS status, impairment rating or Impairment Table used

Are there concerns with the justification in the DMA report?

  • Yes, return the DMA report, go to Step 9
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3

If unsure, go to Step 8.

Delegates must indicate all return reasons when returning the DMA report to the GCD. Delegates must not stop at 1 reason if there are multiple reasons.

Delegates must return the report if there are any quality issues, whether the DMA report recommends grant or rejection.

  • select > DSP Claim Summary

When returning a DMA report:

  • give enough detail in the referral notes to explain the reason for return. Without these notes, the GCD will not know why the report has been returned and what needs clarifying. As the notes are saved on the customer's record, make sure they are clear, concise, factual and written in a professional manner
  • do not use abbreviations other than Sonic, GCD, THP, JCA and DMA when explaining the reasons
  • minimise the need for further returns by carefully checking the report to ensure all required amendments for all medical conditions are included in the first return

If a report has been returned multiple times, see Step 1 in Table 5.

Before accepting a DMA report that was returned and re-submitted, delegates must refer to the Notes section of the DMA referral summary screen to ensure the GCD has addressed all quality issues raised.

Check the DMA report is satisfactory before accepting it, regardless of whether it does or does not support medical eligibility for DSP. With auto-finalisation of automatic grants of claims, any errors in DMA reports may result in incorrect claim outcomes for the customer. Both primary and secondary medical conditions must be completed to a satisfactory standard. Once a DMA referral is in a status of finalised it cannot be referred back to the GCD for amendment.

When quality issues have been identified with a DMA report after it has been accepted, Delegates are to discuss with an SSO before returning the report. An SSO may contact the GCD Contract Management Team for advice if required, see Resources. Go to Step 8 to hold DMA and refer to SSO.

Contributing GCD assessments

  • is physical in nature, a Medical Practitioner is assigned as the Primary GCD
  • are psychological in nature, a Clinical Psychologist is assigned as the Primary GCD
  • are split between physical and psychological, a Medical Practitioner is assigned as the Primary GCD

If the Primary GCD can assess the secondary conditions they can do so, but a Contributing GCD is compulsory if the customer has 2 primary conditions that attract 20 points each on different tables, for example, 20 points on a physical table and 20 points on a Psychological table.

Check DMA report for double counting + Read more ...

If a customer has multiple conditions rated on the same Impairment Table, the combined functional impact must be assigned against 1 of the medical conditions. The second or subsequent time that Impairment Table is used, an impairment rating of zero must be applied.

If a customer has 2 or more medical conditions with a common impairment, only one relevant table should be applied and a single impairment rating assigned to reflect the combined impairment.
