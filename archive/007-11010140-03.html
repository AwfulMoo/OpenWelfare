Cancellation of FTB due to the Sunset Clause

1

Check FTB customer's record + Read more ...

If FTB has been cancelled due to the Sunset Clause, the status will be CAN-L3Y, CAN-N30 (manual override), CAN-N3P (partner non-lodger) or CAN-N3Y).

To view PIP information:

  • Select Family Assistance > Prohibition of Family Tax Benefit > Assessment Explanation
  • Check reconciliation outcomes on the FAO Reconciliation & Claim Summary (FRCS) screen for the relevant financial year/s. Note: if reconciliation has been completed advise outcome and record details on a DOC
  • Check Debt List (OPDL) screen for outstanding non-lodger debts
  • Check DOCs detailing PIP information and discuss with the customer
  • Check letters issued on the History Summary (HS) screen

2

Tax return lodged or not required to lodge + Read more ...

Is the FTB customer advising a tax return has been lodged or that they are not required to lodge, for any of the relevant years?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Sunset Clause + Read more ...

FTB cannot be restored if it is cancelled due to the Sunset Clause.

The customer can re-claim FTB instalments only if adequate income (either ATO income or not required to lodge advise) details are available.

FTB can be paid from:

  • the date the last outstanding tax return is lodged; this date is found in the Date of Lodgement field on the Families Income Previous Year (FIPY) screen, or
  • the date the customer last advised they were not required to lodge a tax return for an applicable year: this date is found on the AMR next to the applicable income update on the FAO Income for Previous Year (FIPY) screen (or the AMR on the FAO Non-Lodgement Exemption (FNLE) screen where non-lodgement advice was lodged with the ATO), and
    • FTB reconciliation has completed for all outstanding years, or
    • all outstanding non-lodger debts are fully repaid

If the customer advises:

  • a tax return has been lodged, but actual income details have not yet been transferred from the ATO, see Australian Taxation Office (ATO) information not received for family assistance reconciliation or lump sum claim
  • they (or their partner) are not required to lodge a tax return for one of the outstanding years as per advice from the tax office or an accountant:
    • Run Family Assistance Office non lodgement exemption workflow to record non-lodgement exemption
    • Check or update the income estimate for the relevant year. See Recording not required to lodge advice and previous year income

Go to Step 4.

Special circumstances + Read more ...

Deferral requests under the Sunset Clause will be rare as only a small number of customers will be eligible. See Resources for possible Sunset Clause deferral reasons.

Families SSO Assessment + Read more ...

For Families Service Support Officers (SSO) only

  • A decision about the Sunset Special Circumstance deferral should be made within 24 hours of receiving the initial referral
  • Annotate the DOC with the decision for example, type of deferral granted or not granted and reason

Is the decision to grant the deferral?

7

Deferral granted + Read more ...

For Families Service Support Officers (SSO) only

Send an email to the Families Reconciliation team (NFP).

Record 'Sunset Special Circ. Request' in the subject line and in the email include:

  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Type of deferral requested, for example family and domestic violence
  • Period of deferral (start and end dates)
  • Evidence provided

Families Reconciliation Team SSO process go to Step 8

8

Families Reconciliation Team process + Read more ...

For Families Reconciliation Team (NFP) Service Support Officer (SSO) only

  • Record a deferral on the PIP Override (!PIPOVR) screen including the following information:
    • Start date (date deferral is approved from)
    • End date (date deferral is approved up to)
  • Select ‘Remove Cancellation’ override type
  • Repeat steps for ‘Remove Prohibition’ override type
  • Add reason for deferment in Activity Notes
  • Complete this activity via Assessment Result (AR) screen
  • Email requesting SSO to advise the coding has been done
  • Procedure ends here

9

Email received from Families Reconciliation Team + Read more ...

When Families Reconciliation Team responds to request:

  • Reinstate FTB via a restoration activity on the Benefit Action (BA) screen (if FTB cancelled in the previous financial year, a dummy FTB NCL will be required to commence FTB from the deferral start date)
  • Annotate the Sunset Special Circ. Request DOC with the following information:
    • Type of deferral requested: family and domestic violence/severe hardship/severe illness (remove as required)
    • Evidence provided
    • Period of deferral (start and end dates)
    • Outcome of deferral (restoration of FTB and details of arrears paid)

Customers are expected to resolve their non-lodger circumstances by the end of the deferral period by:

  • lodging all outstanding tax returns
  • advising they or their partner are not required to lodge, or
  • by repaying all outstanding non-lodger debts

If they fail to do so and their special circumstances no longer apply, their FTB will be cancelled due to the Sunset Clause.

Procedure ends here.

10

Deferral rejected + Read more ...

For Families Support Officers (SSO) only

  • Run the Deferral Exemption workflow, and complete all relevant fields:
    • Notification Date: Date the customer requests the deferral
    • Activity Note: Record relevant information
    • Rejection Reason: 'CNM' (criteria not met)
    • Source and DOR: Record relevant information
  • A letter will be issued outlining the decision and:
    • requirements for PIP to be lifted so that FTB can be re-claimed
    • review and appeal rights. This only applies to rejection of the deferral or decisions leading up to the application of PIP
  • Consider referring the customer to other support options, for example referral to a social worker

11

Advise the customer + Read more ...

FTB cannot be restored if cancelled due to the Sunset Clause. FTB must be re-claimed. See Initial contact by customers claiming payments for families.

Note: if the customer separates from their partner and FTB was cancelled due to their partner's outstanding tax returns, they can re-claim FTB. FTB arrears will be paid from the date of separation.

If the customer partners, or reconciles with their ex-partner, who has had 3 or more outstanding tax returns, and at least 1 non-lodger debt is outstanding, FTB will automatically cancel due to the Sunset Clause once their relationship details are updated.

  • FTB can be re-claimed as fortnightly payments if:
    • all outstanding tax returns are lodged and actual income has been received from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) and FTB reconciliation has occurred for all relevant financial years. (check Act Inc DOL: field on the FTB Status Override (FIPY) screen for customer or partner actual lodgement date) or
    • not required to lodge details are provided and FTB reconciliation has occurred for relevant financial years, or
    • all debts with an outstanding non-lodger debt status are fully repaid
  • The instalment claim must be granted no earlier than the date the income was confirmed (check Act Inc DOL: field on the FIPY screen) or all non-lodger debts were repaid. Any sunset arrears prior to this date in the current and immediate previous financial year cannot be paid until:
    • the customer has lodged an FTB lump sum claim. See Resources for an example and coding requirements, and
    • the customer's (and partner's if applicable) Actual Adjusted Taxable Income (AATI) is confirmed.
      Note: customers must lodge a lump sum claim and confirm income by the end of the lodgement year
  • Any periods that are paying FTB arrears prior to the date AATI is confirmed / non-lodger debts are repaid, must be overridden on the FTB status override (FYSO) screen as:
    • Status: CAN
    • Reason: OTH (other)
  • Any previous financial year lump sum assessments that are generating prior to a FTB lump sum claim being lodged need to be overridden on FYSO as a separate line to the current financial year:
    • Status: CAN
    • Reason: OTH (other)
  • For information on coding estimate for when FTB is claimed after the Sunset Clause has been applied, see Date of event (DOV) for family assistance income estimates
  • For manual processing of FTB new claims and the treatment of incorrect arrears, see Manual processing of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) claims

Lump sum claim lodged

  • If an FTB lump sum claim is lodged after the end of the financial year, coding which prevents FTB assessment for the applicable period on the FYSO screen must be deleted
  • Actual income details will be needed for the claim to be determined and any arrears paid, if entitled
