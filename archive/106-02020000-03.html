Review of pre-release interview scheduled by State and Territory Corrective Services

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Check if the customer has an existing CRN + Read more ...

Does the customer have an existing Centrelink Customer Reference Number (CRN)?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, add the customer to the system and document the customer’s record. Go to Step 2

2

Review customer’s CRN + Read more ...

Action before pre-release phone interview.

  • Check the corrective services list of upcoming known releases. Confirm the claim date is within 21 days of the known release
  • Check if the customer:
    • is on a managed service plan
    • is partnered
    • is income managed
    • has dependent children
    • is getting Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • has a Child Support case
    • has an outstanding Centrelink debt

3

Managed Service Plan (MSP) + Read more ...

Does the customer have an MSP?

If the interaction escalates into an incident of counterproductive behaviour or customer aggression, record an incident in CIMS. See Customer aggression - Reporting and recording incidents.

  • Yes, follow these steps:
    • Check the MSP Restrictions Page in the Customer Incident Management System (CIMS) for any service channel restrictions. Prioritise servicing customers with service channel restrictions
    • Before interacting with customer, contact the One Main Contact (OMC) Employee Responsible (ER) or Personalised Services (PS) to discuss issues or concerns relating to the MSP
    • Where channel restrictions limit face-to-face servicing and/or phone contact, liaise with the OMC/Personalised Services Service Officer (PSSO) to check the suitability of phone contact with the customer before their release. In the customer’s MSP record, document the contact with OMC/PSSO. For access, see Requesting Access to CIMS if phone contact with the customer is possible
    • Notify the customer before their release of their MSP restrictions, obligations, and options for contacting the agency when they are released
    • Advise the customer they will get an MSP letter. Go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Schedule Pre-Release Interview + Read more ...

Using the prison’s upcoming release list, schedule pre-release interviews with customers who have a known release date.

Some State and Territory Corrective Services will schedule appointments using local arrangements.

Step

Action

1

Authenticating a customer + Read more ...

Is the interview being completed by phone?

  • Yes, authenticate the customer. Go to Step 2
  • No, for paper claim authenticate the customer, scan the claim to Centrelink CRN:
    • Prison Pre-release Claim (SU674)
    • Request for restoration of Disability Support Pension or Age Pension Claim for Crisis Payment and Anticipated Payment (SU695)

2

Identity Confirmation Status + Read more ...

Does the customer have a Confirmed Identity Confirmation Status?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No,
    • for paper claim (SU674, SU695), create a JobSeeker Payment (JSP) Social Online Application (SOA) shell in Process Direct. Place claim on hold (Held to User) until expected date of release. Go to Step 16
    • update the customer’s Identity Confirmation Status using the Identity Dashboard workflow in Process Direct. See Identity Confirmation. Consider Alternative Identity processes for these customers only if the customer is present and attending an appointment

Review customer’s CRN + Read more ...

Action before pre-release phone interview:

  • Check if the customer:

    • is partnered
    • is income managed
    • has dependent children
    • is getting Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • has a Child Support case
    • has an outstanding Centrelink debt

4

Check the customer's income support payment (ISP) history and status + Read more ...

Does the customer have a suspended ISP payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Suspended Payment Status + Read more ...

See if payment is restorable based on their payment type and suspension reason, see Cancellation, Suspension and Rejection reason codes .

Is payment restorable?

  • Yes,
    • Advise the customer to contact Services Australia upon release to initiate:
      - restoration of payment, and
      - application for Crisis Payment from the earliest possible date
    • Go to Step 16
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Start Claim Lodgement Process + Read more ...

Is the interview being completed by phone?

  • Yes,
    • Complete the SU674, making sure to advise customer of verbal claim declaration. See the Resources page for Verbal Claim Declaration
    • Scan the SU674 to customer's record. See Scanning Centrelink documents using an MFD
    • Go to Step 7
  • No, paper claim (SU674) received. Make sure the paper claim is scanned to customer’s record. Go to Step 7

7

Obtain consent for prison trust account + Read more ...

For phone interview claims, request the customer’s consent to obtain their prison trust and phone account balance.

Does customer give consent?

  • Yes, request the prison trust and phone account balances from the correct State and Territory Corrective Services. Issue a manual QSS40 letter from the Letter Display (LD) screen in Customer First. Go to Step 8
  • No, advise the customer they will need to give this evidence and will also be asked to provide current income and asset details. Tell the customer this may delay the assessment of their claim. See Verifying income and assets. Go to Step 8

8

Create SOA for appropriate ISP + Read more ...

Create a JSP SOA shell in Process Direct. See Step 4 in the Locate or search for work table in Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions.

Note: JSP SOA shell creation access is restricted to identified users. Prison Liaison Officers can obtain access. See Resources page for further information.

9

Check relationship status + Read more ...

Is the customer single?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No and the partnership is:
    • recorded and their partner is on ISP, there is no need for more relationship information. Go to Step 10
    • recorded and their partner is not on ISP, tell the customer their relationship details need to be confirmed. Request a Partner details - if you live in Australia (MOD P) in the next step. Go to Step 10
    • not recorded, tell the customer their relationship details need to be confirmed. Request a MOD P in the next step. Go to Step 10

10

Check if more information is required + Read more ...

Is the customer required to provide more information?

  • Yes,
    • Request the information by selecting in Process Direct
    • Place the claim on hold (Hold to User) for 23 days using Work Optimiser
    • Claims placed on hold for a Request for Information will need confirmation of the customer’s release date:
      If they have been released, consider rejection of the new claim
      If they have not been released, place the activity back on hold
    • Go to Step 16
  • No, go to Step 11

11

JSP processing + Read more ...

Make sure the Participation Interview workflow is completed before processing the claim.

Has a SOA claim been created?

  • Yes, use the Participation Requirements Workflow (OPR) in Customer First to complete the Participation Interview.
    In Customer First the OPR is accessed by the following:
    • In Workspace search for the First Contact Concessions, Carers and Seniors (FCCCS) workflow
    • On the Frist Contact History screen make sure the Online Participation Requirements (OPR) is selected
    • Select Start New Workflow to commence the Participation Interview. The OPR will flow through all the appropriate screens and recommendations
  • No, access the workflow by selecting in Process Direct

For help, see Processing Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) claims and Processing Jobseeker Payment (JSP) claims.

Make sure the job seeker has a mutual obligation exemption for Major Personal Circumstance (MPC) for 2 weeks from the date their release is recorded. See Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances.

Apply the REM TFN exemption to help vulnerable youth leaving detention who are unable to provide their TFN details with their claim. The customer has 28 days to provide a TFN or complete an Application for TFN after their release from detention. See Requesting a Tax File Number (TFN) for coding instructions.

12

Customer has an outstanding Centrelink Debt + Read more ...

If the customer has an existing debt that has not been written off, record the customer’s period of imprisonment. See Temporary write off of Centrelink debts and Interest Charge (IC) for Centrelink debtors no longer receiving payments.

A customer after release is eligible for a further 28 days deferral of recovery. See Item 22 in the E to P debt recovery information and scenarios table of General Centrelink debt recovery information.

13

Child support + Read more ...

Is there a current child support case in place?

  • Yes, tell the customer that child support contact is needed to make sure an accurate assessment of their income is assessed. Go to Step 14
  • No, go to Step 14

14

Is the customer claiming hardship advance payment? + Read more ...

  • No, go to Step 15

Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) is not required with the completion of a paper claim (SU674) scanned to customer’s record. Go to Step 16
  • No, go to Step 16

Confirmation of expected date of release + Read more ...

Prison Liaison Officers (PLO) are to confirm the expected release date. See Confirming prison admission and release dates.

Create a DOC on the record advising the expected release date.

Hold DOC to user with maturity date of the expected date of release.
