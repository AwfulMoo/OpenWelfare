5

Medical Condition + Read more ...

Confirm if the customer has a medical condition during the pre-release interview.

If a pre-release customer has a medical condition, it may be appropriate to have this verified by the prison medical practitioner prior to their release. This medical evidence can be used to support the completion of an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) if one is required. A link to the Verification of Medical Conditions form (SU684) is available on the Resources page.

If, at the time of their release, a pre-release customer is temporarily incapacitated for all work of at least 8 hours per week due to an illness or injury, they may be eligible for an exemption from mutual obligation requirements. A valid medical certificate will need to be provided to the Agency for assessment. A link to the Centrelink Medical Certificate (SU415) is available on the Resources page, see Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) overview.

Claim lodgement + Read more ...

If the paper Claim for Prison Pre-release SU674 form has been submitted, PLOs must complete an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) with the information contained in the SU674 during the pre-release service.

When assessing the JSP or YAL (Jobseeker) claim via ACC, all mandatory questions must be answered and all required documents must be provided.

Customers claiming a Jobseeker payment are required to book a New Claim Appointment prior to submitting their claim.

When completing an ACC PLOs must:

  • mark the book appointment task as ‘mark as booked’ via the ACC
  • conduct a Participation Interview and assess the claim

See Claiming JobSeeker Payment (JSP) to assess the claim.

13

JSP processing + Read more ...

A JSP or YAL (Jobseeker) claim cannot be granted unless the Participation Interview has been completed and a RapidConnect determination made. The participation interview must be conducted and run in Process Direct.

The Participation Interview codes a RapidConnect status on the Job Seeker RapidConnect (JMRC) screen to determine the customer's start date of payments.

If error E543NM – ‘Participation Workflow has not been completed’ appears this means a Rapid Connect determination has not been made and a Participation Interview has not been completed.

The Participation Interview is launched via More Options from the claim activity in Process Direct.

For help, see Processing Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) claims and Processing Jobseeker Payment (JSP) claims.

Make sure the job seeker has a mutual obligation exemption for Major Personal Circumstance (MPC) for 2 weeks from the date their release is recorded. See Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances.

For vulnerable youth leaving detention, apply the REM Tax File Number (TFN) exemption for those customers who are unable to provide their TFN details with their claim. The customer has 28 days to provide a TFN or complete an Application for TFN after their release from detention. See Requesting a Tax File Number (TFN) for coding instructions.

If a pre-release customer has medical conditions, it may be appropriate to have these verified by the prison medical practitioner prior to their release. This medical evidence can be used to support the completion of an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) if one is required. A link to the Verification of Medical Conditions form (SU684) is available on the Resources page.

If, at the time of their release, a pre-release customer is temporarily incapacitated for all work of at least 8 hours per week due to an illness or injury, they may be eligible for an exemption from mutual obligation requirements. A valid medical certificate will need to be provided for assessment. A link to the Centrelink Medical Certificate (SU415) is available on the Resources page, see Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) overview.

Claim Lodgement + Read more ...

A customer or their nominee may make a claim for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (job seeker) payment.

To help the customer or their nominee make a claim, either:

  • refer the customer to claim online or self service terminals, procedure ends here, or
  • complete an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) with the customer.

Jobseeker Online Claim appointment

Service Officers skilled in Participation Interview and assessing Jobseeker claims must:

  • mark the book appointment task as 'mark as booked' via the ACC
  • conduct a Participation Interview and assess the claim

Where Service Officers are not skilled in Participation Interview and assessing Jobseeker claims they must:

mark the book appointment task as 'mark as booked' via ACC, and

book a Jobseeker Online Claim Appointment via the Appointments tab in Process Direct

To book a Jobseeker Online Claim appointment see table 1 of Job seeker online claim appointment.

Once all Required tasks are completed, the Submit button is displayed so the claim can be submitted.

  • Submitted date
  • Claim ID
  • Estimated Completion Date (ECD) which can be advised to the customer
